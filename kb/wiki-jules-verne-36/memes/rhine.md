## Overview

The Rhine is the great river of the German lands. Seen from the car of a balloon, the Rhine, which is so large, seemed an unrolled ribbon. (DA-001-149)

## In the Narrative

In [[In Search of the Castaways]] the Rhine stands for what cannot be stopped and for a people's river. It would be as impossible to check the man as to stop the Rhine at the Falls of Schaffhausen. (SC-007-014) The Waikato is to the Maories what the Rhine is to the Germans and the Danube to the Slavs. (SC-054-024)

## Geography & Features

The Rhine is counted among the great rivers of the world, named with the [[Amazon]], the [[Elbe]], and the Loire as bringing their waters to the ocean. (TTLU-032-002) In a diplomat's talk of frontiers, the common frontier, the Rhine, it was said, serves very well. (YEAR-001-041)

## Connections

The Rhine is the German river set beside the [[Elbe]] in the roll of the world's great streams. (TTLU-032-002) It marks the western frontier of the German lands. (YEAR-001-041)
