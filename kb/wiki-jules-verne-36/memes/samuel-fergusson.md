# Samuel Ferguson

## Overview

He is a seasoned voyager from his youth, for "Samuel Ferguson, then twenty-two years of age, had already made his voyage around the world" (FWB-001-030).

He is the son of a sailor, since "Ferguson's father, a brave and worthy captain in the English Navy, had associated his son with him, from the young man's earliest years" (FWB-001-027).

## Role in the Story

He is the author of the great design, "the daring project of Dr. Samuel Ferguson, whose fine explorations our readers have frequently had the opportunity of appreciating" (FWB-002-005).

He is the head of the venture, for "if Ferguson was the head and Kennedy the arm, Joe was to be the right hand of the expedition" (FWB-006-006).

He personally drives the outfitting, since he "energetically pushed the preparations for his departure, and in person superintended the construction of his balloon" (FWB-005-002).

At the launch he rises with his companions, when "Ferguson, Kennedy, and Joe, waved a last good-by to their friends" (FWB-011-041).

## Character & Traits

He is celebrated for boldness of mind, honoured for "the energetic character of Dr. Ferguson, and the heart, thrice panoplied in bronze, that could conceive and undertake such an enterprise" (FWB-002-013).

He is restless where his friend is calm, "a restless spirit personified in Ferguson; perfect calmness typified in Kennedy--such was the contrast" (FWB-003-009).

He meets every objection with resolve, replying "as for difficulties, they were made to be overcome; as for risks and dangers, who can flatter himself that he is to escape them?" (FWB-003-057).

He is an inventive mind, who "by an ingenious arrangement, combined the advantages of two balloons, without incurring their inconveniences" (FWB-007-010).

He is prudent aloft, for "Dr. Ferguson prudently kept her above the reach of the barbarian arrows" (FWB-012-038).

His hardy frame never fails him, since "notwithstanding fatigues of every description, and in all climates, Ferguson's constitution continued marvellously sound" (FWB-001-033).

## Relationships

- [[Dick Kennedy]] — the Scottish sportsman and dearest friend who shares his cabin and reluctantly joins the crossing, honoured beside him as "the no less courageous Kennedy, his daring companion" (FWB-008-010).
- Joe — his devoted servant, the "right hand of the expedition," in whose hands he "put himself entirely" for the details of daily life (FWB-006-006).

## Appearances

- [[Five Weeks in a Balloon]] — the tale of the balloon crossing of Africa that he conceives and commands as "the intrepid Ferguson, the glory of England" (FWB-008-010).
