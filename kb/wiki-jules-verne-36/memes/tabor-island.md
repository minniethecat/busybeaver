# Tabor Island

## Overview

Tabor Island is a small, almost arid rock in the South Pacific, lying at 153° west longitude and 37° 11' south latitude. (MI-034-116) In *The Mysterious Island* it proves to be the nearest neighbor of [[Lincoln Island]], and its role in the plot links the colonists to the wider Vernean world through the marooned convict Ayrton.

## In the Narrative

The sailor Pencroft conceived the idea of a voyage of discovery to Tabor Island, though Cyrus Harding could not approve of a trip made simply from curiosity, judging there was nothing to be found on the desert rock. (MI-033-009) They sailed there in the "Bonadventure," the boat the settlers had built for the purpose.

On the island they found a castaway who had reverted to a savage, wild state. Brought aboard, the castaway of Tabor Island manifested a wish to run away when first set on land. (MI-037-032) A document recovered in a bottle had already told the colonists that the man was a skilled navigator, writing in English and giving the island's position to a second of approximation. (MI-035-006)

The castaway was Ayrton, the reformed accomplice of the Glenarvan expedition. Harding understood how Ayrton could point out the island's exact situation, since the events preceding his marooning had made it known to him. (MI-040-007) Lord Glenarvan had promised to return and take Ayrton off Tabor Island once his crimes were expiated. (MI-041-016)

## Geography & Features

Tabor Island is described as a desert and almost arid rock, small enough that a single castaway could subsist only by hunting the game of its scanty forest. (MI-033-009) Its isolation in the Pacific is precisely fixed at 153° W longitude, 37° 11' S latitude, making it Lincoln Island's only close neighbor. (MI-034-116)

The island had no settlement, harbor, or permanent structure beyond the rude hut in which Ayrton had lived during his long solitude. Its very barrenness is what nearly drove its lone inhabitant back into a feral existence before rescue came.
