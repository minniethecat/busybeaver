# M. Fridrikssen

## Overview

He is a Reykjavik scholar and a warm friend, for "M. Fridrikssen, professor of natural sciences at the school of Rejkiavik, was a delightful man, and his friendship became very precious to me" (JCE-009-031).

He receives the travellers under his roof, where the narrator "returned to M. Fridrikssen's house" and found his uncle "already in his host's company" (JCE-009-053).

## Role in the Story

He is a gracious host, "kind enough not to pursue the subject" of his guest's embarrassment and hoping they would not leave "until you have seen some of its mineralogical wealth" (JCE-010-037).

He names the gateway of the descent, answering simply "Snæfell" when the fatal volcano is sought (JCE-010-047).

He unriddles the silent stranger, explaining "that this tranquil personage was only a hunter of the eider duck, whose under plumage constitutes the chief wealth of the island" (JCE-011-006).

He furnishes the expedition its guide, since Hans Bjelke "came recommended by M. Fridrikssen" (JCE-011-009).

## Character & Traits

He is a polyglot of learning, carrying on the talk in Latin, mixing it "with Latin for my benefit" (JCE-010-003).

He is proud of his island's letters, boasting "we possess eight thousand volumes, many of them valuable and scarce, works in the old Scandinavian language" (JCE-010-006).

He is generous in spirit, regretting "that my engagements will not allow me to absent myself, or I would have accompanied you myself with both pleasure and profit" (JCE-010-051).

He is a classicist to the last, at parting treating the narrator "to a line of Virgil eminently applicable to such uncertain wanderers as we were likely to be" (JCE-011-042).

He wins deep affection, for their last evening "was spent in intimate conversation with M. Fridrikssen, with whom I felt the liveliest sympathy" (JCE-011-040).

## Relationships

- [[Professor Lidenbrock]] — his guest, whose membership of "about a hundred learned societies" he accepted with a grace that "evidently touched M. Fridrikssen" (JCE-010-011).
- [[Hans Bjelke]] — the eider-hunter he recommends as the expedition's guide (JCE-011-009).
- [[Axel]] — the nephew to whom his friendship "became very precious" (JCE-009-031).
