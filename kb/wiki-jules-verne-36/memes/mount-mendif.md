# Mount Mendif

## Overview

Mount Mendif is the African peak that rises across the Victoria's path in the Adamova country of *Five Weeks in a Balloon*. It stands among the landmarks the narrator gathers in surveying the region: "The Kingdom of Adamova... The Atlantika Mountains.--The River Benoue.--The City of Yola.--The Bagele.--Mount Mendif." (FWB-029-001)

## In the Narrative

The mountain first appears as a distant marker of the balloon's progress: "that evening the balloon halted about forty miles from Yola, and ahead of it, but in the distance, rose the two sharp cones of Mount Mendif." (FWB-029-043)

The winds then carry the Victoria straight at it against the doctor's wishes. "In spite of all he could do, the doctor bore directly to the northeast, toward Mount Mendif, which was lost in the midst of environing clouds." (FWB-029-048) The drift brings them full upon it: "by three o'clock the Victoria was directly in front of Mount Mendif." (FWB-029-050)

## Geography & Features

The peak is distinguished by its twin sharp cones and by the vast bird-colonies that whiten its flanks. "Immense accumulations of bird-guano gave the sides of Mount Mendif the appearance of calcareous rocks, and there was enough of the deposit there to manure all the lands in the United Kingdom." (FWB-029-051)

Lying some forty miles from the city of Yola in the Kingdom of Adamova, it serves the crossing as a towering navigational fix on the run northeastward.
