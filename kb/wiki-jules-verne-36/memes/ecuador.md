## Overview

Ecuador is the equatorial South American republic whose frontier the Amazon defines in *Eight Hundred Leagues on the Amazon* — the village of Iquitos lies on that portion of the great river whose bed separates Peru from the republic of Ecuador. (EHLA-003-001)

## In the Narrative

It is one of the rival claimants to the river's source. Peru, Ecuador, and Colombia have for years disputed the honor of the Amazon's glorious paternity. (EHLA-005-019)

Its highlands start the waters on their course — the river's waters come from afar, from the mountains of the republic of Ecuador, which start them on a course that no falls break until two hundred and ten leagues from its junction with the main stream. (EHLA-016-070)

## Geography & Features

Its border with Peru is drawn by latitude: the Amazon is comprised, from the point where it turns to the east on the frontiers of Ecuador and Peru, between the second and fourth parallels of south latitude. (EHLA-008-007)

Its rivers feed the greatest of all — the Amazon's affluents form the highways which penetrate Bolivia, Peru, Ecuador, New Grenada, Venezuela, and the four Guianas. (EHLA-005-018)
