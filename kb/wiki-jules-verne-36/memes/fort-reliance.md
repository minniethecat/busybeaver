# Fort Reliance

## Overview

Fort Reliance is the Hudson's Bay Company post on the Great Slave Lake from which the Arctic expedition of *The Fur Country* departs. Situated on the lake's north-east extremity, about three hundred miles from the Chesterfield inlet of Hudson's Bay, it is the last outpost of settled company life before the journey into the far north. (FC-004-002)

## In the Narrative

The novel opens at Fort Reliance on the evening of the 17th March 1859, with a fête given by its commander, Captain Craventy. (FC-001-002) Among the guests newly arrived is Paulina Barnett, a laureate of the Royal Society and seasoned traveller, whose scheme of exploration has brought her to the fort. (FC-001-027)

From Fort Reliance, Lieutenant Jasper Hobson leads his detachment out toward the northern coast to found a new factory at [[Cape Bathurst]]. Two hundred miles had been traversed since the expedition left Fort Reliance when the party pressed on across the frozen wilderness. (FC-006-002)

The fort remains the colonists' link to the inhabited world throughout the story. When [[Victoria Island]] later drifts helplessly at sea, the settlers count on a relief convoy making its way back toward the Great Slave Lake and Fort Reliance, and mourn the failure of the expected detachment to arrive. (FC-022-058)

## Geography & Features

Fort Reliance stands at 61° 47' north latitude, on the north-east extremity of the Great Slave Lake, a region of ferocious winters. (FC-001-029) On the night of the opening fête the cold outside stood at twenty-four degrees Fahrenheit below zero, and the fort's stove consumed a hundredweight of coal to keep the guests warm. (FC-001-029)

The post supported a garrison and a civil service of company employees, and drew traders from neighbouring forts of the Great Slave Lake district. (FC-001-023) As a Hudson's Bay factory it served as a hub for the fur trade and a staging point for expeditions probing the Arctic coast.
