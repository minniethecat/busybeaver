# The Death of Martin Paz

## Overview

The Death of Martin Paz is the tragic climax of *[[the-pearl-of-lima|The Pearl of Lima]]*—at the foaming cataract of the Madeira the young Indian lassoes the canoe bearing the condemned Sarah, holds it back from the abyss, and is pierced by an arrow, falling into the boat to be engulfed with her in the vortex.

The setting was a deadly waterfall. Above the village the Madeira plunges from a height of more than a hundred feet, a foaming cataract which breaks against sharp rocks (PL-009-047).

## What Happens

Sarah was condemned to the falls. It was in the midst of this foaming tempest that the unfortunate young girl was destined to die, exposed at the first rays of the sun in a bark canoe (PL-009-048).

Then her rescuer appeared. Suddenly a man appeared on the opposite shore—it was Martin Paz, with Don Vegal and the Indian Liberta beside him (PL-009-056).

His lasso caught the canoe. Standing on a rock, Martin Paz balanced his lasso, and the long leathern thong unfolded and surrounded the canoe just as it was about to be precipitated (PL-009-060).

He held it from the abyss. Martin Paz redoubled his efforts; the canoe remained suspended over the abyss, the current unable to prevail against the strength of the young Indian (PL-009-064).

## Significance

An arrow ended him. Suddenly an arrow whistled through the air and pierced the heart of Martin Paz; he fell forward into the bark of the victim and, re-descending the current, was engulfed with Sarah in the vortex of the cataract (PL-009-065).

The tribe exulted. A yell of triumph was heard above the sound of the torrent (PL-009-066).

Their union was eternal. Martin Paz and Sarah, in their brief and final re-union, were betrothed for eternity (PL-009-071).

## Participants & Setting

- Martin Paz, the proud young Indian, dies at the cataract of the Madeira attempting to save Sarah, the girl the vengeful tribe had condemned to be sent over the falls in a bark canoe.
- Don Vegal, the Spanish noble who had sheltered Martin Paz, and the faithful Indian Liberta stand with him on the shore as the rescue fails.
- The tribe's yell of triumph closes the tragedy, and Don Vegal returns to Lima to die of grief; chronicled in *[[the-pearl-of-lima|The Pearl of Lima]]*.
