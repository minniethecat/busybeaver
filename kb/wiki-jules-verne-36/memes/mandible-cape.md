# Mandible Cape

## Overview

Mandible Cape is the pair of fictional headlands framing the northern shore of Lincoln Island's Union Bay, named by the colonists for their resemblance to a set of jaws. Pencroft proposes "naming the two parts of the jaws Mandible Cape," (MI-011-066) split into "North Mandible Cape and South Mandible Cape." (MI-011-068)

The twin capes become a familiar landmark of the settlers' domain.

## In the Narrative

The colonists explore them early — "they had explored as far as the point of Mandible Cape, seven miles distant from the Chimneys." (MI-015-005) From Granite House the horizon "was closed by the two Mandible Capes on the north, and Claw Cape on the south." (MI-019-040)

The capes bound the bay where a stranded whale thrashes: the cetacean "made rapid furrows across it from Mandible Cape to Claw Cape." (MI-032-034) The *Bonadventure* rounds them on her coasting voyages — "weathering the North Mandible Cape, entered the strait." (MI-042-005)

## Geography & Features

The two capes form the northern jaw of Union Bay, opposite Claw Cape to the south. A full survey of the shore "from the mouth of Falls River to the Mandible Capes" is only slowly completed. (MI-041-034)

The *Bonadventure* runs "rapidly towards North Mandible Cape, for the wind was right astern," (MI-041-052) the headland marking the turn of the island's northeastern coast.
