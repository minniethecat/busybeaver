# Prospect Heights

## Overview

Prospect Heights is the elevated plateau that crowns the high granite precipice of [[Lincoln Island]] in *The Mysterious Island*. The colonists gave it its name because, standing above the shore, the gaze from it could embrace the whole of the vast bay below. (MI-011-074) It sits directly above [[The Chimneys]] and became the cultivated heart of the settlement.

## In the Narrative

Cyrus Harding used the commanding elevation of Prospect Heights for survey work, resolving to make his observations from the plateau because of its height above the level of the sea. (MI-013-063) The engineer fixed the points of the compass roughly by the sun, placing Union Bay and Prospect Heights to the east of the island. (MI-011-077)

As the colony matured, the plateau was turned into productive ground. The settlers gave assiduous care to cultivating the wild plants transplanted from the forest onto Prospect Heights. (MI-030-038) A poultry-yard was established on the heights. (MI-033-073)

Harding later resolved to turn the plateau into a kitchen-garden, defended by its deep belt of creeks, and to move the livestock down to the unprotected meadows that had no need of guarding against quadrupeds. (MI-038-077)

## Geography & Features

The plateau rises above the granite precipice that overlooks the mouth of the Mercy and the Chimneys. (MI-011-074) By diverting the stream, the engineer contrived to make Prospect Heights "a regular island, being surrounded with water on all sides, and only communicating with the rest of our domain by the bridge." (MI-029-021)

This deliberate moating turned the plateau into a natural fortress: the deep belt of creeks kept out marauding animals and hostile intruders alike, while the single bridge could be raised for defense. (MI-020-027) The elevation gave the settlers both fertile garden soil and an unobstructed watch over Union Bay.
