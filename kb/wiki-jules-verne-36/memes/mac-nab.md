# Mac-Nab

## Overview

He is the expedition's builder, introduced when "Hobson and Mac-Nab the carpenter went to choose the site of the principal house on the plateau at the foot of Cape Bathurst" (FC-013-005).

He turns his hand to every fitting, for "the carpenter Mac-Nab constructed a most substantial table, around which were ranged fixed benches" (FC-014-002).

## Role in the Story

He commands the fort's construction gang, since the work "was the business of Mac-Nab and his men; and while the carpenters were at work" the foragers gathered supplies (FC-013-013).

He raises the fort's defences, undertaking "to erect a wooden sentry-box commanding the coast-line, from which a watch could be kept without any danger" (FC-014-011).

He labours without pause, for "Mac-Nab and his subordinates set to work zealously, and completed their task in a few days" (FC-019-003).

He is charged with the great escape-boat, when "Mac-Nab was ordered to commence the construction of a huge boat" for a reconnaissance of the coast (FC-026-003).

Beside his men "Petersen, Belcher, Garry, Pond, and Hope," he "worked zealously at the construction of a boat, a difficult task, likely to occupy them for several months" (FC-028-006).

## Character & Traits

He is a rapid and practical craftsman, who "was able to proceed very rapidly without endangering the safety of the building" (FC-013-014).

He is also a keen sportsman, for "Sabine and Mac-Nab might many a time have shot a very valuable animal on this excursion" had the Lieutenant not forbidden it (FC-011-019).

He feels each setback keenly, and "poor Mac-Nab was in despair; he had not reckoned upon such a contingency when he constructed the roof" (FC-021-025).

He is counted among the brave, called together with "Long, Mac-Nab, and Rae the blacksmith, as the bravest men in his party" (FC-021-032).

Yet loyalty is tempered by duty to his family, for when he "volunteered for the perilous service," the Sergeant "reminded the other two that they were married" (FC-021-039).

## Relationships

- [[Jaspar Hobson]] — the Lieutenant who directs his labours and orders the building of the boat by which the colony hopes to escape (FC-026-003).
- Mrs Mac-Nab — his wife, whose safe delivery of "a fine healthy boy" fills "the head carpenter" with pride (FC-017-027).

## Appearances

- [[The Fur Country]] — the Arctic tale in which he builds Fort Hope and the boat that carries the marooned colony to safety, working "zealously" to the last (FC-028-006).
