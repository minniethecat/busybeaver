# Detroit River

## Overview

The Detroit River is the strait linking the lower and upper Great Lakes, a pivotal escape route in the chase of *The Master of the World*. Geographically it binds Lake Erie to the greater lakes: "It is joined on the northwest, by means of the Detroit River, with the still greater lakes to the westward, and receives their waters." (MW-011-022)

## In the Narrative

The pursuers reckon the river among the *Terror*'s possible bolt-holes. The machine "could retrace its road by the Detroit River which would lead it to Lake Huron and the Great Lakes above." (MW-012-061) They wonder whether its captain had already slipped away — "Had not the Captain reascended the Detroit River, and entered Lake Huron, or even Lake Superior beyond?" (MW-013-008)

When the *Terror* does not take that road, the mystery deepens: "Since the 'Terror' had not retreated by the Detroit River, down which she had descended from the upper lakes, how was she to escape?" (MW-013-035)

## Geography & Features

In the final pursuit the river is the sole outlet, a race the destroyers cannot win: "The only escape was by the Detroit River, and the 'Terror' was constantly leaving that farther behind." (MW-014-004) It threads between [[Lake Erie]] and the upper lakes as the artery of the whole chase.
