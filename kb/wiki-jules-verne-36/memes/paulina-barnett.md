# Paulina Barnett

## Overview

Paulina Barnett is "no ordinary woman"; she is "Paulina Barnett, a laureate of the Royal Society" whose name, "the rival of the Pfeiffers, Tinnis, and Haimaires of Hull, has been several times honourably mentioned at the meetings" of the geographers, her journeys reaching up the Brahmaputra "as far as the mountains of Thibet" and across "an unknown corner of New Holland" (FC-001-027).

Into the polar enterprise she carries the breadth of a life spent travelling — where a companion complains of the cold she recalls "the tropical heat of India" and marvels at "the difference between these Polar regions and the green prairies of Australia" (FC-005-023).

## Role in the Story

She is the guest of honour whose presence sets the expedition in motion: "the lady traveller was to join the expedition of Jaspar Hobson for the exploration of the north" (FC-001-029).

Her curiosity is active, not ornamental — eager to "now see the Lieutenant at work" (FC-002-011), she makes herself "well informed as to the ulterior projects of the celebrated Company" (FC-002-048).

When Fort Hope's peninsula breaks free and drifts as an ice-island, her fortitude carries the colony. Through the long ordeal "Mrs Barnett was invaluable in setting an example of constant activity: always brave, she kept herself awake, and encouraged others" (FC-020-019).

On New Year's Day Hobson "complimented her on the courage and good temper with which she endured" the peril (FC-020-020). Even as the danger deepened her "courage was unabated" (FC-021-060).

## Character & Traits

Her courage is matched by a democratic warmth: "Mrs Barnett shared everything with her companions, never holding herself aloof or remaining shut up in her cabin, but working zealously amongst the others" (FC-018-010). That generosity can turn to something near heroism — rather than wound a young native's feelings she "showed superhuman courage" (FC-019-048).

She reads character shrewdly too, telling the Lieutenant she knows him well enough to assert "that on occasion you would be ready to devote body and soul to science" (FC-007-024).

## Relationships

- [[Jaspar Hobson]] — the Hudson's Bay lieutenant she accompanies, whose enthusiasms she indulges, liking "to hear his raptures" (FC-006-018).
- Madge — her faithful maid and lifelong companion, who rides beside her as "Mrs Barnett and her faithful Madge" (FC-004-064).
- [[The Fur Country]] — the novel whose northern expedition and ice-island drift she shares.
