# Great Bear Lake

## Overview

Great Bear Lake is the vast subarctic lake of northern Canada, the great inland water the Hudson's Bay expedition must skirt on its way to the polar coast in *The Fur Country*. Jaspar Hobson's mission is "to explore the northern shores of the Great Bear Lake, and to found a" new fort beyond it. (FC-001-024)

## In the Narrative

The lake is the last settled waypoint before the far north. A post on its shore "served as a resting-place for the men taking the convoys of furs from the Great Bear Lake, some three hundred miles further to the north-west." (FC-006-003) Hobson presses on toward its far side, "most anxious to reach Fort Confidence, built on the northern extremity of the Great Bear Lake." (FC-008-003)

At the Indian encampment "situated at the north-west angle of the Great Bear Lake" (FC-008-039) he gathers his last intelligence before striking for the coast.

## Geography & Features

The lake is an inland sea in its own right: "The Great Bear Lake is quite a Mediterranean Sea, extending over several degrees of latitude and longitude." (FC-008-004) The country beyond is forbidding — "the district between the Great Bear Lake and Cape Bathurst was very difficult to cross, being hilly and intersected by streams" (FC-008-041) — and its waters are dangerous, for "the storms on the Great Bear Lake are often terrible." (FC-009-006)
