# Stony Hill

## Overview

Stony Hill is the barren Floridian rise chosen by the Gun Club as the site to cast and launch its giant Columbiad cannon in *From the Earth to the Moon*. When Barbicane asks its name the Floridians answer simply, "It is called Stony Hill." (FEM-013-044)

Its "barren and rocky nature" strikes Barbicane as offering "every condition favourable to our enterprise," and stamping his foot on the ground he declares "it is from this very spot, the summit of Stony Hill, that our projectile will start for the regions of the solar world!" (FEM-013-047)

## In the Narrative

The choice transforms the region. Nearby Tampa Town is "enormously benefited by this enterprise of the Gun Club," drawing both workmen and "the influx of curious idlers" from every corner of the globe. (FEM-014-005) Barbicane lays "the first sleepers of a railway fifteen miles long that was destined to unite Stony Hill and Tampa Town." (FEM-014-006)

Barbicane arrives with his detachment on the 1st of November, and "the very next day a small town of workmen's houses rose round Stony Hill." (FEM-014-009) On the 4th, fifty workmen begin to dig "a circular hole sixty feet wide" at the summit. (FEM-014-016) For eight months "Barbicane did not leave Stony Hill for a minute," guarding both the boring and the health of his men. (FEM-014-027)

## Geography & Features

The hilltop becomes a scene of ceaseless industry. By night "the noise of the pickaxe on the rock, the blasting and the machines" trace "a circle of terror round Stony Hill, which the herds of buffaloes and the detachments of Seminoles never dared to pass." (FEM-014-022) The molten metal is carried up "by railway to Stony Hill," where the enormous mass is delivered by mid-January. (FEM-015-006)

After the casting "an immense column of smoke was still soaring towards the sky, and the ground burnt the soles of the feet" within 200 feet of the summit. (FEM-016-004) Once cooled, Barbicane and his engineer take their place on "the cast-iron surface which covered the summit of Stony Hill." (FEM-016-008)

The enclosure opens to the public on the 23rd of September, and the crowds make Tampa Town to Stony Hill "a perfect procession, or, better still, a pilgrimage." (FEM-016-020)

The launch itself is staged here: at the climax "the multitude round Stony Hill heartily united their shouts to those of the ten revellers hidden from sight in the depths of the gigantic Columbiad." (FEM-016-025) It is "the Columbiad of Stony Hill" whose projectile is at last perceived departing for the Moon. (FEM-028-006)
