# Dream Bay

## Overview

Dream Bay is the large bight on the shore of the Pacific island where Godfrey Morgan is cast away, a name of his own coining. Godfrey walks "along that part of the shore which formed the beach of the large bight to which he had given the name of Dream Bay." (GM-014-013)

## In the Narrative

The bay is where fortune washes up: a mysterious chest lands there, and Godfrey weighs "transporting it from the bottom of Dream Bay to Will Tree," but "its weight forbade it." (GM-014-018)

After a meal inland he is drawn back to it — "Godfrey wished after the meal to get back at once to Dream Bay" — and the pair set "off... for Dream Bay by the shortest road." (GM-014-046)

## Geography & Features

The bay anchors the island's geography between two landmarks: "It was nothing but a vast desert from Flag Point right away beyond the curve of Dream Bay." (GM-017-024)

At its far end Godfrey gathers shellfish, "at the extreme end of Dream Bay, when they perceived out at sea an innumerable quantity of small moving islets which the rising tide was bringing gently to shore." (GM-018-056)

Its sweep frames the island's fiery climax, when "a huge glare lighted up... the whole of the coast from Flag Point to the southern cape of Dream Bay." (GM-021-098)
