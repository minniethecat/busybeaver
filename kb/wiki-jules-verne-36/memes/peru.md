## Overview

Peru is the Andean republic from whose forests the waters of the Upper Amazon issue, where Torres is first found wandering after passing the frontier. (EHLA-001-008) The village of Iquitos sits on the left bank of the great river, on that reach where its bed separates Peru from the republic of Ecuador. (EHLA-003-001)

It was to Peru, at the village of Iquitos, that Joam Dacosta fled and lived the years of his youth in exile. (EHLA-024-018) There he took refuge for so many years, safe within the frontiers of Peru. (EHLA-037-008)

## In the Narrative

Once Joam Dacosta crossed into Brazilian territory he became more at the mercy of his enemy than while he remained within the frontiers of Peru. (EHLA-022-023)

The story's imperial geography turns on the boundary with the country: it remained only to define the frontier between the new empire and that of its neighbour, Peru. (EHLA-012-007) If Brazil wished to extend to the Rio Napo in the west, Peru attempted to reach eight degrees further, as far as the Lake of Ega. (EHLA-012-008)

## Geography & Features

There seems little doubt that the Amazon rises in Peru, in the district of Huaraco, in the department of Tarma, starting from the Lake of Lauricocha. (EHLA-005-020) The river is called the Marañon in its journey through Colombia and Peru up to the Brazilian frontier. (EHLA-005-022)

Peru stands among the affluent highways of the great river, which penetrate Bolivia, Peru, Ecuador, New Grenada, Venezuela, and the four Guianas. (EHLA-005-018) Its life colours the tale: golden sols of Peru among Torres's coins (EHLA-001-019), and the liquor known as *chica* in Peru. (EHLA-001-023)
