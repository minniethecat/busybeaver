# Cape Bernouilli

## Overview

Cape Bernouilli is the point on the South Australian coast where the thirty-seventh parallel — the line of latitude on which the wrecked *Britannia* is sought — first touches the continent in *In Search of the Castaways*. "The 37th parallel touches this continent at Cape Bernouilli, and leaves it at Twofold Bay." (SC-027-067)

## In the Narrative

The cape is the westward landfall of the search, opposite the South American cape where the parallel departs that continent: the 196 degrees "which separate Australia from America, or, more correctly, Cape Bernouilli from Cape Corrientes." (SC-028-001) After debate, Glenarvan and John Mangles resolve "to sail round the Australian coast, stopping at Cape Bernouilli, and continuing their route south." (SC-032-006)

The *Duncan* makes it on schedule: "On the 20th of December, they arrived off Cape Bernouilli, which terminates Lacepede Bay, and yet not a vestige of the Britannia had been discovered." (SC-032-010)

## Geography & Features

From the cape the overland party sets out eastward across the continent; "it is scarcely sixty-two miles from Cape Bernouilli to the frontiers of Victoria." (SC-035-003) It stands with Twofold Bay as one of the two poles of the Australian search, remembered later in bitterness: "They remembered how full of well-warranted hope they had been at Cape Bernouilli, and how cruelly disappointed at Twofold Bay!" (SC-046-068)
