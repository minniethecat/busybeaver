## Overview

Vanikoro is a Pacific island to which Captain Dumont d'Urville gave the name "Island of the Search". The Nautilus lay right in front of the little harbour of Vana, in latitude 16 degrees 4' south and longitude 164 degrees east. (TTLU-019-033) During the night of December 27-28 the Nautilus left the waterways of Vanikoro behind with extraordinary speed. (TTLU-020-002)

## In the Narrative

Vanikoro is bound to the loss of the La Pérouse expedition, whose ships met the unknown reefs of the island. (TTLU-019-066) A native boatman claimed that during a stay there years before he had seen two Europeans from ships wrecked on the reefs. (TTLU-019-043) It was said the castaways built a third ship on Vanikoro Island, whose fate remains unknown. (TTLU-019-061)

## Geography & Features

The search for La Pérouse drew ship after ship to the reef-girt island. The Hope and the Search passed right by Vanikoro without stopping, on a voyage that cost the lives of Rear Admiral d'Entrecasteaux and several of his men. (TTLU-019-042) Later Dillon reached it, trying to recover rubble of the shipwreck. (TTLU-019-044)

## Connections

Vanikoro became a fixed point of French exploration: the Search dropped anchor before it on July 7, 1827, in the same harbour of Vana. (TTLU-019-046) The Astrolabe raised the island on February 12 and sailed along its reefs. (TTLU-019-052) Fearing d'Urville was unaware of Dillon's work, the government sent the sloop Bayonnaise there. (TTLU-019-059)
