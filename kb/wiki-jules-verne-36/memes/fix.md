# Detective Fix

> **Note:** Inline PN citations for this page use a 4-character work code (`AWED`); they render as plain text pending RFC-0003. The underlying PN data is valid.

## Overview

Fix is the police detective who mistakes [[phileas-fogg|Phileas Fogg]] for the robber of the Bank of England and shadows him across the world. Carrying the card "Fix, Detective" (AWED-005-014), he is one of the detectives dispatched to intercept the suspected thief (AWED-006-004), and his single-minded pursuit supplies the novel's chief obstacle.

## Role in the Story

Convinced of Fogg's guilt, Fix bends the entire journey toward an arrest. He telegraphs London to "send without delay warrant of arrest to Bombay" (AWED-005-013), and when the paper fails to arrive he vows to hold his quarry until he can get a warrant to arrest him from London (AWED-007-009). His scheming repeatedly delays Fogg, yet by keeping close to his suspect he ironically helps the voyage forward.

## Character & Traits

Fix is defined by professional tenacity and fixed conviction. He scrutinizes every crowd according to habit (AWED-006-016), reading suspects with a policeman's reflex, and once he has settled on Fogg's guilt no evidence dislodges him. His error — pursuing an innocent man with the zeal owed to a criminal — makes him an antagonist by misjudgement rather than malice.
