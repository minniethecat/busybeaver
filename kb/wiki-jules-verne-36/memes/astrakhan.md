## Overview

Astrakhan is the old Russian city at the north end of the [[Caspian Sea]]. (RC-014-009) Once styled the Star of the Desert, it has sunk from the first rank to the fifth or sixth, keeping its old walls, ancient towers, mosques, and a cathedral with five gilded domes. (RC-014-009)

## In the Narrative

Astrakhan figures in the imperial style of Russia. The Tsar's ukase, recited in [[Michael Strogoff]], names him Emperor and Autocrat of all the Russias, Czar of Kazan and Astrakhan. (MS-005-053) Its name is borne too by the astrakhan fur, worn as a Persian's cap. (MS-005-019)

## Geography & Features

Astrakhan commands rich fisheries near the mouth of the great river. The best drag-net catches (carp, bream, salmon, pike, and prized sterlets) were sent alive to Astrakhan, [[Moscow]], and Petersburg. (RC-013-060) Following the valley of the [[Volga]], the aeronef covered the twelve hundred miles from Astrakhan to Moscow in ten hours. (RC-014-011)

## Connections

Astrakhan is the Caspian landfall noted from the aeronef in [[The Clipper of the Clouds]], the last town seen at the north end of the [[Caspian Sea]]. (RC-014-009) From there the airship followed the [[Volga]] the twelve hundred miles up to Moscow. (RC-014-011)
