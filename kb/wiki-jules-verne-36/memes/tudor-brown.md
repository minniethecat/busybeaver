# Tudor Brown

## Overview

He announces himself by a small American calling card bearing the name "Mr. Tudor Brown, on board the 'Albatross'" (WC-010-002), a name whose incongruity the doctor at once remarks — "the singular person who answered to the feudal name of Tudor, and the plebeian name of Brown" (WC-010-008).

His person is as artificial as his name: "a man about fifty years of age," his forehead ringed with "little ringlets, of a carroty color... made of curled silk," his hooked nose "surmounted with an enormous pair of gold spectacles" (WC-010-009).

## Role in the Story

He first intrudes on Erik's friends by producing forged proof, presenting "a paper stamped with a notarial seal" (WC-010-044) — "a certified account of the death of Patrick O'Donoghan," though the man "appeared to be living" (WC-012-003).

He buys his way aboard the rescue voyage, endowing it "on condition that he is received as a passenger" (WC-011-064), so that Erik's party is forced to carry the very man they suspect — for they reason he is "a factor quite as important as Patrick O'Donoghan himself" (WC-012-038).

His course ends on the Arctic ice, where he "shot Patrick O'Donoghan through the heart" (WC-019-076) to silence the secret of Erik's birth, and an instant later "received a bullet in his forehead, and fell forward on his face" (WC-019-077).

## Character & Traits

He is a study in cold, evasive hostility, sitting apart "with his hat on his head" and manifesting "not the least inclination to enter into any relations with his neighbors" (WC-012-044).

His malice is quick and cruel: when Otto's dog seizes him by the legs, he draws his revolver and, "white with rage and terror, insisted that the dog's brains should be blown out" (WC-012-053).

His whole existence resists inquiry — inquiries at Edinburgh found that "nobody knew Mr. Tudor Brown, which he thought looked suspicious" (WC-010-077).

## Relationships

- Patrick O'Donoghan — the man who holds the secret of Erik's parentage, whom he first declares dead and finally murders to keep silent.
- [[Erik]] — the young sailor whose search for his origins he schemes throughout to thwart.

## Appearances

- [[The Waif of the Cynthia]] — as the mysterious antagonist who dogs the Alaska's polar voyage, hoping "a storm... coming to interpose... to permit Tudor Brown to escape" (WC-018-015), before his violent end on the ice.
