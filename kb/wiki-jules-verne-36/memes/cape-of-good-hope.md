# Cape of Good Hope

## Overview

The Cape of Good Hope is the southern promontory of Africa, invoked in *Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea* as one of the world-girdling capes the Nautilus might round. The narrator wonders at the vessel's designs: "Would he double the Cape of Good Hope, then Cape Horn, and push on to the Antarctic pole?" (TTLU-023-008)

## In the Narrative

Conseil and Ned Land debate the submarine's route by way of the cape. Conseil predicts "the Nautilus will go back down to the Indian Ocean, perhaps through Mozambique Channel... and then make for the Cape of Good Hope." (TTLU-028-012) Ned Land is aghast at the pace required "if it's to double the Cape of Good Hope, circle around Africa, and lie in the open Mediterranean by the day after tomorrow." (TTLU-028-088), demanding "what's this talk about doubling the Cape of Good Hope?" (TTLU-028-089)

## Geography & Features

The cape marks the meeting of two oceans and the great sailing route round Africa. Ships are seen "no doubt going as far as Cape São Roque to find favorable winds for doubling the Cape of Good Hope" (TTLU-033-016), and vessels "laden for the East Indies, were heading toward the Cape of Good Hope." (TTLU-035-015) The narrator reasons about a wounded animal's improbable path, "unless it had cleared the equator and doubled Cape Horn or the Cape of Good Hope." (TTLU-036-047)
