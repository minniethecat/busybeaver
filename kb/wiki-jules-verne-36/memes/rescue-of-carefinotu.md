# The Rescue of Carefinotu

## Overview

The Rescue of Carefinotu is the episode of *[[Godfrey Morgan]]* in which Godfrey, aided by Professor Tartlet, snatches a bound prisoner from a band of cannibals about to roast him on the beach of Phina Island, felling their chief with a musket-shot and scattering the rest to their canoe (GM-017-059) (GM-017-067). The freed victim becomes Godfrey's companion, afterwards named Carefinotu.

## What Happens

A vessel manned by savages was seen approaching the island, and Godfrey followed the proa through his glass as it turned the point of the promontory and ran in towards the mouth of the river (GM-016-031) (GM-016-036). Fearing discovery, he shut the door of Will Tree closely and hid within with Tartlet through a night of alternating fear and hope (GM-016-042).

At dawn Godfrey went out to reconnoitre, and creeping to the bank he saw a large fire of wood lighted on the beach among the rocks (GM-017-051). Ten of the savages tended the fire and drove stakes into the ground to rig up a spit, while a twelfth savage lay stretched and tightly bound to a post (GM-017-053) (GM-017-055).

Godfrey recognised at once the fate in store for the wretched man: the spit was for him, and Tartlet had not been mistaken in calling these folk cannibals (GM-017-056).

## Significance

Resolving to act as the hero of Defoe had done, Godfrey determined not to permit the massacre of the prisoner (GM-017-059). When the chief gestured and three of his companions unloosed the captive and forced him towards the fire, Godfrey uttered a cry followed by a report, and the chief, mortally wounded, fell to the ground (GM-017-064) (GM-017-067).

At the sight of the two white men the savages who held the prisoner instantly released him (GM-017-068). Then a second report was heard—it was Tartlet, who, firing with his eyes shut, by pure chance brought down a second savage close to the chief (GM-017-071) (GM-017-072).

The rout at once began; the savages seized their two wounded, rushed to the proa, and drove it out of the creek (GM-017-073). Godfrey had no thought of pursuing them, for they had saved the victim (GM-017-074).

## Participants & Setting

- Godfrey Morgan, the young castaway, who resolves to rescue the prisoner in imitation of Robinson Crusoe and fells the cannibal chief with his first shot (GM-017-059) (GM-017-067).

- Professor Tartlet, the clumsy dancing-master, whose musket—fired blindly with his eyes shut—delivers a second lucky shot that helps put the savages to flight (GM-017-071).

- The band of twelve savages, who land in a proa, kindle a fire on the beach, and prepare a spit to roast their bound captive (GM-016-031) (GM-017-053).

- The bound prisoner, the twelfth savage tied to a post and destined for the spit, delivered by Godfrey's intervention and afterwards Godfrey's companion Carefinotu (GM-017-055) (GM-017-074).
