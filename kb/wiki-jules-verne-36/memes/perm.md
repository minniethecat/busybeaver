## Overview

Perm is a real Russian town and province on the western slope of the Urals, the threshold at which Michael Strogoff's road passes from European Russia into Siberia. It stands first among the stations after Nijni-Novgorod on the boat route: the office of the company whose boats plied between Nijni-Novgorod and Perm sets him on his way. (MS-006-004)

## In the Narrative

The Tsar's order names Perm early in the chain of telegraph stations to receive Ivan Ogareff's description — "Nijni-Novgorod, Perm, Ekaterenburg, Kasirnov, Tioumen, Ishim, Omsk, Tomsk." (MS-002-025) Ogareff himself is traced to the province: "In the province of Perm." (MS-003-027)

From here the traitor's route runs east — after leaving Perm, Ivan Ogareff crossed the Ural mountains, entered Siberia, and penetrated the Kirghiz steppes. (MS-003-042) The loyal answer marches the same way: the regiments from Perm and Nijni-Novgorod, and the Cossacks from the frontier, are advancing toward the Ural Mountains. (MS-003-044)

## Geography & Features

Set on the Kama in the Ural foothills, Perm is the pivot between the Volga basin and Siberia — river-port to the west, mountain road to the east.
