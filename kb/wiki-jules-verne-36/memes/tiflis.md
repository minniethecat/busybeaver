## Overview

Tiflis is the chief city of Transcaucasia and the gateway from which the great Asian journey begins. It was there, on the 13th of May, that the correspondent found the telegram redirecting his travels. (ASC-001-005) The station at Tiflis is the junction of three lines of railway, ending west at Poti on the Black Sea and east at Baku on the Caspian. (ASC-001-019)

## In the Narrative

The reporter meant to spend three weeks visiting the Georgian provinces before the wire sent him east. (ASC-001-008) Guide-books boast of doing Paris in two days and Rome in three; it would be curious, he thought, if he could not do Tiflis in a half day. (ASC-001-035)

## Geography & Features

Tiflis is a city of citadels, belfries and thermal springs. Its famous baths draw thermal waters that reach sixty degrees centigrade. (ASC-001-045) From here the railway skirts the carriage road to the Caspian, running through Elisabethpol and Aliat to Baku along the valley of the Koura. (ASC-002-003)

## Connections

Tiflis links the [[Black Sea]] port of Poti with the [[Caspian Sea]] terminus at Baku by a single trunk line. (ASC-001-019) It opens the transcontinental route of [[The Adventures of a Special Correspondent]]. (ASC-001-005)
