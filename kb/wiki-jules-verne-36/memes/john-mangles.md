# John Mangles

## Overview

John Mangles is the young captain of the yacht *Duncan*, introduced simply as "John Mangles, the captain" (SC-001-003). A capable commander, when the vessel is fitted out for her long voyage "John Mangles had only to attend to her interior arrangements" (SC-005-003).

He takes a part from the very first: examining the document drawn from the shark, he recognizes at once, "This is written in German" (SC-002-021), helping to piece together the message that sets the search in motion.

## Role in the Story

Mangles' seamanship is proven in the great storm off the coast. Trusting his weather-glass, "he remained on deck the whole night... and took every precaution that prudence could suggest" (SC-031-013), and through the worst of it "never left his post, not even to take food" (SC-031-043).

When the yacht is driven toward the rocks he "did not lose a second in extricating his ship from the peril in which she was placed" (SC-031-039). Brought safely through, he earns Lord Glenarvan's plain gratitude: "Thank you, John" (SC-032-002).

## Character & Traits

He is above all "a prudent captain" (SC-026-017); in a crisis his authority is unmistakable, for their situation "must indeed be desperate for John Mangles to speak in such authoritative language" (SC-031-036). Yet he is gentle with the young — praising Robert, he says, "No need to blush... Your conduct has been worthy of your name" (SC-027-006).

## Relationships

- [[Mary Grant]] — Captain Grant's daughter, to whom Mangles grows "particularly attentive" through the voyage (SC-027-024, SC-028-007).
- [[Lord Glenarvan]] — owner of the *Duncan*, who entrusts his yacht and passengers to Mangles and thanks him by name (SC-032-002).
- Robert Grant — Mary's brother, whom Mangles encourages and embraces after his feat of daring (SC-027-006).

## Appearances

- [[In Search of the Castaways]] — as captain of the *Duncan* throughout the search for Captain Grant.
