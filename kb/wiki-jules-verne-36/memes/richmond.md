## Overview

Richmond is the Confederate capital of Virginia from which the heroes of *[[The Mysterious Island]]* make their famous escape. Verne tells how, "on the 20th of March, escaped from Richmond, besieged by the troops of General Ulysses Grant, they found themselves seven thousand miles from the capital of Virginia" (MI-002-002), the city the starting point of their whole adventure.

It is a place of captivity for the Northern prisoners. When several officers "fell into the power of the enemy and were detained" (MI-002-004), the engineer and his companions "had both been carried to Richmond" (MI-002-009), the city holding them fast within its lines.

## In the Narrative

Richmond is a fortress-town under close guard. Though the prisoners were allowed to wander the streets, "Richmond was so strictly guarded, that escape appeared impossible" (MI-002-010), the besieged city sealing every road against flight.

Their deliverance comes at last by air. A captive balloon, Verne writes, "inflated on the great square of Richmond, was ready to depart on the first abatement of the wind" (MI-002-018), and the storm that seizes it carries the party from the city out over the ocean toward [[Lincoln Island]].

## Geography & Features

Richmond stands as the prize Grant's army could not easily take. It is recorded that in "the coups de main by which General Grant attempted, though in vain, to possess himself of Richmond" (MI-002-004) the city held out, and only later "had doubtless soon fallen into the hands of General Grant" (MI-030-042).

The city is also a seat of letters and government. In *An Antarctic Mystery* it is noted that Poe's romance "was published at Richmond" (AM-005-001), while in *[[The Blockade Runners]]* the death-warrant comes when "the reply from the Richmond cabinet had arrived by a courier" (BR-008-067), the city commanding the Confederacy's affairs.

## Connections
