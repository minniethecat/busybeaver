# Dick Kennedy

## Overview

He is bound to the doctor by deep affection, for "Dick Kennedy and Samuel Ferguson lived with one and the same heart" (FWB-003-003).

He is a Scot to the core, "a Scotchman, in the full acceptation of the word--open, resolute, and headstrong" (FWB-003-004).

## Role in the Story

He is the strong arm of the venture, since "if Ferguson was the head and Kennedy the arm, Joe was to be the right hand of the expedition" (FWB-006-006).

Though he opposes the scheme, loyalty carries him along, and he vows to "go with you up to the last moment, to prevent Samuel even then from being guilty of such an act of folly" (FWB-006-023).

At the launch he rises with his friends, when "Ferguson, Kennedy, and Joe, waved a last good-by to their friends" (FWB-011-041).

## Character & Traits

He is the calm foil to the restless doctor, "perfect calmness typified in Kennedy--such was the contrast" (FWB-003-009).

At the first proposal he is dumbstruck, for "Kennedy stood speechless with amazement" (FWB-003-041).

Yet even he softens before the vision, since "the stubborn Kennedy began to feel moved, and yet the spectacle... gave him the vertigo" (FWB-003-067).

He is a keen sportsman above all, who "descried some hares and quails that asked nothing better than to get a good shot from his fowling-piece" (FWB-012-039).

His stubbornness surfaces in small things too, declaring, "I am not going to let myself be weighed" (FWB-006-033).

## Relationships

- Samuel Ferguson — the doctor and dearest friend whose expedition he joins, sharing his very cabin, for "two state-rooms, comfortably fitted up, were ready for the reception of Dr. Ferguson and his friend Kennedy" (FWB-008-007).
- Joe — Ferguson's servant and the "right hand" beside whom he serves as the expedition's arm (FWB-006-006).

## Appearances

- [[Five Weeks in a Balloon]] — the tale in which he crosses Africa by balloon at Ferguson's side, honoured as "the no less courageous Kennedy, his daring companion" (FWB-008-010).
