# Engineer Serko

## Overview

He first appears aboard the schooner as a jesting confederate, sending Captain Spade off on the abduction with "Good luck, Spade... and don't make more noise about it than if you were a gallant carrying off his lady-love" (FF-003-047).

His history is bound to the pirate chief's: among the Australian gold-diggers "were Captain Spade and Engineer Serko, two outcasts, whom a certain community of ideas and character soon bound together in close friendship" (FF-010-048).

## Role in the Story

The submarine that makes Ker Karraje invincible is his conception — "well versed in his profession, and... a clever mechanic to boot, and who had made a special study of submarine craft, [he] proposed to Ker Karraje that they should construct one" (FF-010-061), the vessel then "constructed from a model and under the personal supervision of Engineer Serko" (FF-010-064).

He is the brain of the whole enterprise, "the most formidable and resolute of his accomplices" (FF-010-065), who fitted out Back Cup itself, having "installed an electric power house" within the mountain (FF-010-081).

His steady task is to break the inventor: Thomas Roch "is daily sounded in regard to his discoveries, especially by Engineer Serko" (FF-011-016), and the narrator clings to the hope that "Engineer Serko's efforts to acquire it will remain futile" (FF-013-006).

## Character & Traits

His menace wears a smile. Pressed by the narrator, he answers only "'Of business, Mr. Hart, of business,'... with a smile. 'Our engines are now completed, and when the fine weather returns we shall resume offensive operations'" (FF-014-027).

He is vain of his own ingenuity — "very proud of his handiwork,--and also very positive that the prisoner of Back Cup will never be able to disclose the secret" (FF-010-077).

Yet he guards his master's own secrets closely; the narrator listens "in the hope that Engineer Serko will give away a part of the secret, but in vain" (FF-012-049).

## Relationships

- [[Ker Karraje]] — the pirate chief whose invincible fleet he engineers and whose schemes he directs.
- [[Thomas Roch]] — the inventor he cajoles and pressures for the secret of the fulgurator.
- Captain Spade — his fellow outcast from the goldfields, bound to him in long friendship.
