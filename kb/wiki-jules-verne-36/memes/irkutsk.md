# Irkutsk

## Overview

Irkutsk is the distant capital of Eastern Siberia and the destination of the courier's desperate ride in *Michael Strogoff*. Verne fixes its remoteness plainly: it is "the far capital of Eastern Siberia." (MS-003-003) From it the Grand Duke governs the eastern provinces, and to it Michael Strogoff must carry the Czar's warning before the Tartar invasion closes the road.

## In the Narrative

The city is the pivot of the whole imperial crisis. The Czar asks whether "your majesty's brother, his Highness the Grand Duke, is now isolated in the government of Irkutsk," cut off from Moscow. (MS-003-045) The traitor's design is to reach the city under false colours: "when the Tartars have invested Irkutsk, he will betray the town, and with it my brother, whose life he seeks." (MS-003-048)

Every route bends toward it. Orders go out that "the troops of the provinces of Amoor and Irkutsk... received orders to march immediately upon Irkutsk," (MS-002-019) even as the telegraph is severed and the young Livonian Nadia finds "all the routes to Siberia had just been pitilessly closed to her" on her own way there. (MS-007-010)

## Geography & Features

The city sits at the eastern end of a vast courier network. A branch line runs onward from it: "from Irkutsk there is a branch to Kiatka, on the Mongolian frontier; and from thence... the post conveys the dispatches to Pekin in a fortnight." (MS-003-005)

Its sheer distance defines the drama. Before the telegraph reached the eastern frontier, the fastest couriers took "eighteen days to get from Moscow to Irkutsk," (MS-005-001) the measure of the ground Strogoff must cover ahead of the enemy.
