# Professor Aronnax

> **Note:** Inline PN citations for this page use a 4-character work code (`TTLU`); they render as plain text pending RFC-0003. The underlying PN data is valid.

## Overview

Professor Pierre Aronnax is the first-person narrator of *[[twenty-thousand-leagues|Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Seas]]* and an Assistant Professor at the Paris Museum of Natural History (TTLU-002-002). A recognized authority on natural history, he is publicly named "the honorable Pierre Aronnax, Professor at the Paris Museum" when the press debates the mysterious sea creature (TTLU-002-012).

His scientific curiosity draws him into the voyage that makes him a captive aboard the *[[nautilus|Nautilus]]*.

## Role in the Story

Invited to join the frigate *Abraham Lincoln* on its hunt for the supposed monster, Aronnax sets aside his exhaustion and collections and accepts at once (TTLU-003-003). When the frigate strikes the *[[nautilus|Nautilus]]*, he goes overboard (TTLU-007-004) and survives only because his manservant [[conseil|Conseil]] seizes hold of him in the water (TTLU-007-012).

Taken aboard the submarine, he is recognized by [[captain-nemo|Captain Nemo]], who declares that sheer chance has placed the specialist in natural history in his presence (TTLU-010-006). Nemo tells him he will be free aboard the vessel only in exchange for conditions that bind him as a permanent guest (TTLU-010-025), and addresses his captive throughout as "Professor Aronnax" (TTLU-010-015).

## Character & Traits

Aronnax is first of all a man of science: he keeps at his side a seasoned specialist in biological classification, [[conseil|Conseil]], and shares his enthusiasm for cataloguing the natural world (TTLU-003-008). Even when threatened, he clings to his professorial dignity (TTLU-002-025).

His naturalist's eye frames the whole narrative, turning captivity aboard the *[[nautilus|Nautilus]]* into a scientific expedition through the deep.

## Relationships

- [[conseil|Conseil]] — his devoted manservant and fellow classifier, who saves him when he falls overboard.
- [[ned-land|Ned Land]] — the Canadian harpooner and fellow captive aboard the [[Nautilus]].
- [[captain-nemo|Captain Nemo]] — his captor and host, whose secrets he strives to understand.
- Held aboard the [[Nautilus]] as a permanent guest for the length of the voyage.
