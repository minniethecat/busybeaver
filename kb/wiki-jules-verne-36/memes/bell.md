# Bell

## Overview

He is the carpenter of the brig Forward, reckoned among the loyal hands, for "the harpooner, Simpson, and the carpenter, Bell, were sure men, faithful to duty and discipline" (ACH-005-041).

He is chosen for the polar march, his fellows being "Bell the carpenter and Simpson" (ACH-028-013).

## Role in the Story

When mutiny flares he helps subdue the ringleader, and "Johnson and Bell disarmed Pen, who no longer made any resistance, and placed him in the hold" (ACH-022-021).

On the overland journey he takes the lead, "sounding the ice with his iron-tipped stick" (ACH-029-002), and later "took the necessary precautions to assure the solidity of the construction" of their snow shelter (ACH-032-008).

## Character & Traits

He is the doctor's deliverer in the cold, seizing "a handful of snow, and began to rub his companion's face with all his might" (ACH-030-009), then telling him wryly "if you've got a nose left, you owe it to me" (ACH-030-013).

He is not spared the trials of the ice, being one of the men "struck with snow blindness" in spring (ACH-010-012), and he tends the failing team when "some of them had begun to eat their leather harness" (ACH-031-013).

Under long suffering his spirit hardens, "the poor fellow's heart was hardened by his own suffering, and he did not answer" (ACH-031-018).

## Relationships

- [[Captain Hatteras]] — the commander he guides across the ice, for "Bell led the way; Hatteras marched in a straight line behind him" (ACH-029-025).

- [[Dr Clawbonny]] — the doctor whose frozen face he saves, who answers "thanks, Bell... I'll do the same for you in case of need" (ACH-030-016).

- [[Johnson]] — the boatswain beside whom he keeps watch, whose silent reverie "Bell did not interrupt" (ACH-027-010).

## Appearances

- [[The Adventures of Captain Hatteras]] — the Arctic expedition of the Forward, on whose polar dash "Bell started the first; the others followed" (ACH-028-014).
