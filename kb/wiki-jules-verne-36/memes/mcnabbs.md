# Major McNabbs

## Overview

Major McNabbs is a cousin of [[Lord Glenarvan]], aboard the *Duncan* from the outset as "one of his cousins, Major McNabbs" (SC-001-002). When the ship's company is enumerated for the great voyage, the narrator notes that "to complete the roll of passengers, we must name Major McNabbs" (SC-005-011).

## Role in the Story

The Major's keen, unhurried eye serves the party well. Where "anyone else but McNabbs might have passed the hut a hundred times... without suspecting its existence" (SC-013-001), he alone marks it out as a refuge. A steady hunter, he provides for his companions, answering a query only "By this," as he holds up the animal he has killed (SC-013-040).

Paganel counts him among the natural chiefs of the expedition — "the Major, who would not yield his place to anybody" (SC-010-049).

## Character & Traits

McNabbs is defined by his imperturbability. The excitement of a stranger forms "a strong contrast to the Major's placidity" (SC-006-034); where another would have smiled at a ludicrous sight, "McNabbs never moved a muscle of his face" (SC-006-036).

He watches a drenching flood "with the utmost indifference" (SC-008-036), and his counsel is as dry as his temper: "'I should wait,' said the Major, just as if he had said, 'I should not wait'" (SC-008-063). Between remarks he is generally "absorbed in his cigar" (SC-006-030).

## Relationships

- [[Lord Glenarvan]] — his cousin and the expedition's leader, who trusts his judgment ("Our friend McNabbs is right") (SC-001-002).
- [[Paganel]] — the geographer to whom the Major is attached "with all his apparent coldness" (SC-014-006).
- [[Mary Grant]] — of whom the Major approves early: "That Mary Grant must be a brave girl" (SC-004-015).

## Appearances

- [[In Search of the Castaways]] — as Glenarvan's cousin and steadfast companion throughout the search.
