# Island of Ljakow

## Overview

The Island of Ljakow is one of the New Siberian islands in the Arctic Ocean, the goal of a tense sea-chase in *The Waif of the Cynthia*. When a passenger asks to be put ashore there, the crew comply: "We therefore acceded without regret to his request to be landed on the large Island of Ljakow, as we were following the southern coast." (WC-016-029)

## In the Narrative

The island becomes the object of a race against the villain Tudor Brown. "The next day at dawn Erik had the 'Alaska' steering for the island of Ljakow" (WC-016-038), the crew anxiously wondering, "Would they be able to reach the Island of Ljakow before him?" (WC-016-040)

Their rival's purpose is plain, and they "felt sure that he would embrace the first opportunity to leave the Island of Ljakow." (WC-017-006) The passenger who lands there means to overwinter, "collecting, during the summer months, all the ivory that he could find." (WC-016-031)

## Geography & Features

Ljakow is a large, low Arctic island off the Siberian coast, reached by following its southern shore. The *Alaska*'s approach is hindered by the polar weather — "a tempestuous snow storm followed by dense fogs which sensibly retarded the progress of the 'Alaska.'" (WC-016-045)
