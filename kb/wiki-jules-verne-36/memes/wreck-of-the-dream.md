# The Wreck of the Dream

## Overview

The Wreck of the Dream is the foundering of the steam yacht *Dream* on a Pacific reef, the disaster that strands Godfrey Morgan and his tutor Tartlet as castaways and sets in motion the robinsonade of *[[godfrey-morgan|Godfrey Morgan]]*.

Godfrey survives the night clinging to a bare rock, working out on reflection that the wreck had taken place at the highest tide of the new moon (GM-008-004).

## What Happens

The catastrophe erupts without warning in the fog. Almost immediately a fearful cry was heard on deck, "We are sinking!" and Godfrey rushes from his cabin into the chaos (GM-007-095).

The cause is a hidden reef. The *Dream*, which had doubtless struck on a rock, was sensibly foundering as the water crept up toward the level of the deck (GM-007-101).

Captain Turcott orders the passengers overboard toward the dimly seen coast, crying that there is not a moment to lose and that they are only half a cable from the shore (GM-007-104).

Only afterward does Godfrey grasp the how of it: it was only too certain that the *Dream* had been driven out of her route during the storm of the preceding days (GM-008-005).

## Significance

The loss is charged to human error. Captain Turcott's reconnaissance of the reported reefs would have certainly prevented the catastrophe if it had only been pushed far enough (GM-008-006).

The apparent human cost is total. Godfrey fears the whole ship's company is gone, wondering whether they had all been sucked in by the terrible whirlpool which the ship had drawn round herself as she sank (GM-008-012).

Yet the wreck is a fiction. Uncle Kolderup later reveals that though Godfrey thought she sank for good, the captain brought her up and steamed away once he and Tartlet had got safely to land (GM-022-022).

## Participants & Setting

- The steam yacht *Dream*, foundered on a Pacific reef with Captain Turcott and his crew aboard.
- Casting Godfrey Morgan and his dancing-master Tartlet ashore as involuntary castaways.
- Secretly staged at the direction of Godfrey's uncle William W. Kolderup; chronicled in *[[godfrey-morgan|Godfrey Morgan]]*.
