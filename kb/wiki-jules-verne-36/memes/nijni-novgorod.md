## Overview

Nijni-Novgorod is a real Russian city at the confluence of the Volga and Oka, the western threshold of the great courier road that carries Michael Strogoff toward Irkutsk. Two towns, separated by the stream of the Volga, together compose the place. (MS-006-003)

## In the Narrative

At the time of the annual fair the city's fixed population of thirty to thirty-five thousand swelled past three hundred thousand. (MS-006-002) Strogoff goes to the office of the company whose boats plied between Nijni-Novgorod and Perm, seeking passage east. (MS-006-004) With hours still to pass he wanders the crowded streets; it seemed to him an age. (MS-006-032)

The fair itself is held somewhat outside the town. (MS-006-034) Later the journalists Blount and Alcide recognize the travelling players, remarking, "These are the Tsiganes of Nijni-Novgorod." (MS-024-012)

The Tsar's order names the city first among the telegraph stations to receive Ivan Ogareff's description: "Let a description of him be immediately dispatched to Nijni-Novgorod, Perm, Ekaterenburg..." (MS-002-025)

## Geography & Features

The two towns straddle the Volga, the great river forming the artery of trade that made the fair the largest in the empire. From here boats descended toward Perm and the Ural crossing beyond.
