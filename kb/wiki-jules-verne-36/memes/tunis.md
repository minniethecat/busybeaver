## Overview

Tunis is a city and coastal region of North Africa. In the Vernean corpus it appears chiefly in *Off on a Comet*, where the passage of the comet Gallia tears away the Mediterranean shore and the Bay of Tunis.

It figures also as a waypoint of the African explorers in *Five Weeks in a Balloon* and is overflown by the Albatross in *Robur the Conqueror*.

## In the Narrative

After the catastrophe of *Off on a Comet*, the surveyors find the coast utterly changed: "from Cape Matafuz to Tunis it had all gone, as though it had never been." (OC-012-003)

The schooner *Dobryna*, rounding the point where Cape Blanc had stood, is "now stemming the waters of what once had been the Bay of Tunis." (OC-012-005) Soon she traverses the shallows covering the peninsula "which had separated the Bay of Tunis" from the open sea. (OC-013-001)

## Geography & Features

Of the old city almost nothing survives: there was "no trace of Tunis, except one solitary rock, which was crowned by an ancient tomb of one of the kings of France." (OC-015-033) Following the new shoreline, the party passes "through what had been Tunis, across the province of Constantine, away to the oasis of Ziban." (OC-018-027)

In *Robur the Conqueror* the Albatross, in a single day, "traversed Tunis from Cape Bon to Cape Carthage." (RC-015-004)

## Connections

Tunis is bound above all to *Off on a Comet*, whose Bay of Tunis is among the Mediterranean coasts swept away by the comet Gallia. (OC-012-005)

It links to *Five Weeks in a Balloon* through the earlier explorers Richardson, Barth, and Overweg, who "arrived at Tunis and Tripoli" on their way toward Fezzan. (FWB-004-007)
