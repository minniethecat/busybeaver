## Overview

Canada is the great northern fur-trade dominion of *The Fur Country*, where French agents made Canada their headquarters and ventured on hazardous but most lucrative expeditions. (FC-002-019) The conquest of Canada, however, somewhat lessened the danger of the Company's exposed position. (FC-002-020)

## In the Narrative

The land's divided allegiance shadows the enterprise. The rival trappers are the descendants of the masters of Canada, from the days when it belonged to France (FC-012-007), and during the ninety-four years of French supremacy in Canada, French agents always proved themselves superior. (FC-012-011)

Lieutenant Hobson is uneasy at the thought of rivals in the north-western districts of English America. (FC-012-004)

## Geography & Features

Its trading geography is precisely drawn: the Company's principal factories lie on James Bay, at the mouth of the Severn, and towards the frontiers of Upper Canada, on Lakes Athapeskow, Winnipeg, and Superior. (FC-002-023) In 1837 a commission of the Colonial Secretary decided it necessary to annex to Canada all the disputed territory. (FC-002-047)

Even its lesser creatures bear its name — the polecats and pine-martens called "Canada-martens" frequent the shores of the Arctic Ocean. (FC-016-019)
