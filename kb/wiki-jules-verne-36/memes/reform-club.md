## Overview

The Reform Club is the London gentlemen's club on Pall Mall where [[phileas-fogg|Phileas Fogg]] spends his ordered days and where, in *[[around-the-world-in-eighty-days|Around the World in Eighty Days]]*, he wagers his fortune that he can circle the globe in eighty days. He is among its most noticeable members. (AWED-001-002)

## In the Narrative

The wager is struck in the club's card room over a game of whist, and its terms fix the very place and moment of settlement:

> London in this very room of the Reform Club, on Saturday, the 21st of Dec... (AWED-003-063)

Having made the bet, Fogg leaves the Reform Club to begin his journey. (AWED-004-002) The whole novel is a race back to this room, and at its climax a great crowd collects in Pall Mall and the neighbouring streets to see whether he will arrive in time. (AWED-036-006)

## Geography & Features

The club is an imposing edifice in Pall Mall, costly and grand, the heart of Fogg's rigidly regular London life. (AWED-003-002) Its news and gossip drive the plot: the Reform Club is especially agitated as the wager's outcome hangs in doubt. (AWED-003-013)
