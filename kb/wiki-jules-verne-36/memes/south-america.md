## Overview

South America is the coast thrown as an immense barrier between the Pacific and the Atlantic, from Cape Horn to the shores of Columbia. (DSCF-009-053) Across its interior sweep practically inexhaustible forests (EHLA-006-011), and the Amazon waters the largest empire of the continent as though it were the Pacific Ocean itself flowing into the Atlantic. (EHLA-005-012)

## In the Narrative

English and American ships are seldom absent from its Pacific side, ascending from Cape Horn toward the equator and the continent's extreme point. (DSCF-011-004) Cast ashore, Dick Sand's party dares not enter the country without knowing to what province of South America it belongs. (DSCF-014-052) Harris assures them, to their cost, that they stand on the sea-coast of South America. (DSCF-015-043)

## Geography & Features

Its rubber-yielding trees — the ficus, the castilloa, the syphonia elastica — abound in the provinces of South America. (DSCF-016-045)

Luminous insects, glowing from two spots at the base of the corselet, are very numerous there. (DSCF-016-105) The vast district crossed by the equator gave its name to Brazil (EHLA-012-001), today the largest state of the continent, headed by the artist-king Dom Pedro. (EHLA-012-002)
