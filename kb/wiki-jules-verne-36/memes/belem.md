## Overview

Belem, in the province of Pará, stands near the mouth of the Amazon, eight hundred leagues downstream from the little Peruvian village where the raft begins its voyage. (EHLA-010-005)

It is the great commercial city of the lower river, its shops stocked with the latest novelties from Belem and Rio de Janeiro. (EHLA-012-055)

## In the Narrative

Belem is the goal of the journey. Joam Garral every year sends rafts of wood down to Manaos and Belem (EHLA-004-015), and the marriage of his daughter Minha gives the family a natural reason to accompany her there, where she is to live with her husband. (EHLA-004-020)

The young merchant is entrusted with a few products of the country to dispose of on others' account at Manaos and Belem. (EHLA-014-034) The family would rather hold the wedding at Belem than at Iquitos. (EHLA-004-045)

## Geography & Features

Belem lies in Pará, the seaward province of the Amazon basin; at the age of twelve Benito is sent there to be educated under able professors. (EHLA-003-031)

During his first years of residence at Belem, Benito makes the acquaintance of Manoel Valdez, far from the family fazenda upstream. (EHLA-003-032)
