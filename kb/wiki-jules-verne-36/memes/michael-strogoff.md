## Overview

*Michael Strogoff* is Jules Verne's romance of loyalty and endurance across the vastness of Siberia. Its hero "belonged to the special corps of the Czar's couriers, ranking as an officer among those picked men," a hardy Siberian born at Omsk to the huntsman Peter Strogoff and his mother Marfa (MS-004-032, MS-004-035).

He is summoned to the imperial library as "Michael Strogoff, the courier" (MS-004-029).

## Plot Summary

When a Tartar revolt cuts the telegraph and threatens Siberia, the Czar charges Strogoff: "Here is a letter which I charge thee... to deliver into the hands of the Grand Duke, and to no other but him" (MS-004-059). The journey lies across "a rebellious country, invaded by Tartars," and he is warned to "beware of the traitor, Ivan Ogareff" (MS-004-063, MS-004-065).

On the road Strogoff takes as companion the young Nadia, daughter of an exiled captain, passing her off as his sister to travel to Irkutsk (MS-007-034, MS-007-035). Captured by the Emir Feofar-Khan, he is condemned; "Look while you may," cries the Emir, before a scorching blade is passed before his eyes and he is left for blind (MS-024-002, MS-025-002).

Yet the blade's heat was quenched by his tears, and "he might make use of his supposed blindness for the accomplishment of his designs" (MS-034-003). Having secretly read the traitor's plans, he presses on to Irkutsk, reaches the Grand Duke, and the siege is broken with Ogareff dead (MS-034-005, MS-034-015).

## Main Characters

- Michael Strogoff — the courier of the Czar entrusted with the letter to Irkutsk
- Nadia — the exile's daughter who travels with him as his supposed sister
- Ivan Ogareff — the Russian traitor allied with the Tartar invaders
- Feofar-Khan — the Emir who condemns Strogoff to be blinded

## Key Places & Technology

- Irkutsk — the besieged Siberian city Strogoff must reach with the Czar's letter
- The Imperial letter — the message whose delivery may save the Grand Duke's life

## Themes

Verne fashions a patriotic adventure of duty pushed to the breaking point, driving his courier through invasion, capture, and blindness real or feigned. Beneath the geography of Siberia runs a study of devotion — to the Czar, to a mother, and to Nadia — that redeems the hardships of the road.
