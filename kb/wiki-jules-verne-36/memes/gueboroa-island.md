# Gueboroa Island

## Overview

Gueboroa Island is the coral-ringed island in the Torres Strait, off the coast of Papua, where the Nautilus grounds during her dangerous passage through the narrows in *Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea*. Aronnax sights it close aboard: "Two miles to starboard lay Gueboroa Island, its coastline curving north to west like an immense arm." (TTLU-020-037)

## In the Narrative

The island offers the prisoners a rare footfall on land. The Nautilus's skiff "had just run gently aground on a sandy strand, after successfully clearing the ring of coral that surrounds Gueboroa Island." (TTLU-020-073) Ashore, Aronnax botanizes among "the breadfruit tree, which is quite abundant on Gueboroa Island." (TTLU-021-023)

Ned Land forages so successfully that the party resolves to go back: "We decided to return to Gueboroa Island." (TTLU-021-069) When the Papuan islanders grow hostile, Aronnax still pleads for them: "'I don't want my visit to Gueboroa Island to cost the life of a single one of these unfortunate people!'" (TTLU-022-093)

## Geography & Features

The island lies within the treacherous coral maze of the Torres Strait, where "Dumont d'Urville's two sloops of war had gone aground." (TTLU-020-033) Its coast is fringed by the reef that briefly traps the Nautilus, and its interior yields breadfruit and game to the foraging Canadian.
