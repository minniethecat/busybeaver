## Overview

Tasmania is the English island lying south of [[Australia]], a recurring southern waymark in Verne's ocean tales. It is counted plainly among the British possessions of the southern seas—"New Zealand, belonging to the English; Tasmania, belonging to the English" (SC-038-063)—in the geography lessons of *[[In Search of the Castaways]]*.

The island is also a byword for the penal settlements. It is noted that "the province of Victoria, Southern Australia, Queensland, and even Tasmania, have agreed to expel convicts from their territories" (SC-033-085), the island standing with the mainland colonies in their common war on the convict class.

## In the Narrative

Tasmania marks the far southern reach of the whaling flotillas. The Pilgrim was sent each season "in the quarters of Tasmania or of Cape Horn" (DSCF-001-004), the island naming one of the two great extremities toward which the whalers pushed, alongside [[Cape Horn]].

It is likewise a haven at the season's turn. In *An Antarctic Mystery* the sealing captain, "on the approach of the bad season, he returned to Hobart Town, in Tasmania" (AM-026-019), the island's port serving as the refuge to which the southern voyager withdrew before the ice closed in.

## Geography & Features

Tasmania stands among the southernmost points of the inhabited world. Reckoning the winds, it is observed that "the nearest land, ending in the points of the Cape of Good Hope, Patagonia, and Tasmania" (AM-013-017) shapes the atmospheric currents of the far south, the island being one of three great capes of the southern hemisphere.

Its forests share the character of the temperate world. The trees found on a distant shore "belonged to the species which abound in the temperate zone of America and Tasmania" (MI-012-038), the island lending its name to a whole class of southern temperate growth.

## Connections

Tasmania is the English island south of [[Australia]] in *[[In Search of the Castaways]]*, a convict colony beside the mainland provinces, a whaling extremity paired with [[Cape Horn]], and a southern refuge in the sealing voyages, kin to [[New Zealand]] among the British possessions of the south.
