## Overview

The Isthmus of Panama is the narrow neck of land joining North and South America, and in Verne's tales the great shortcut between the Atlantic and the Pacific. It carries the trans-oceanic traffic of [[Dick Sand: A Captain at Fifteen]], whose vessels of the Golden Age line "ply between Melbourne and the Isthmus of Panama" (DSCF-001-016).

The isthmus is repeatedly invoked as the crossing point of the New World. A steamer is described as "about sailing for the Isthmus of Panama" (DSCF-038-004), the narrow land the hinge on which Pacific and Atlantic navigation turns.

## In the Narrative

For Verne's voyagers the isthmus is a matter of routes and reckonings. In [[The Waif of the Cynthia]] a captain weighs his passages — "I am not thinking of Panama, nor of Cape Horn, nor of the Cape of Good Hope" (WC-015-013) — setting the way of Panama beside the two great capes as a rival path between the oceans.

Its traffic feeds the lines of steam. In *Godfrey Morgan* it is said that "the Panama, California and British Columbia Company... would have to take him to Europe" (GM-005-011), the isthmus knit into the web of transoceanic companies.

## Geography & Features

The isthmus lies athwart the great mountain spine of the Americas. In [[From the Earth to the Moon]] Verne traces the range that "follows the west coast of South America under the name of the Andes or Cordilleras, crosses the Isthmus of Panama" (FEM-024-020), the chain threading the narrow land.

Its waters are rich as well as strategic. [[Captain Nemo]] numbers "the Gulf of Panama" among the world's pearl fisheries (TTLU-026-011), and driftwood is borne "towards the Pacific by some river of the Isthmus of Panama" (ACH-006-020).

## Connections

The Isthmus of Panama is the land bridge and ocean shortcut linking the Atlantic to the [[Pacific Ocean]], set beside [[Cape Horn]] and the [[Cape of Good Hope]] as a route between the seas in [[The Waif of the Cynthia]]. It is crossed by the [[Andes]] cordillera in [[From the Earth to the Moon]] and carries the trans-Pacific traffic of [[Dick Sand: A Captain at Fifteen]].
