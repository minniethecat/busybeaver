## Overview

*Off on a Comet* is Jules Verne's boldest cosmic fantasy, sweeping a piece of the Earth into interplanetary space. Its hero is "Captain Hector Servadac, Staff Officer," a French soldier of thirty stationed on the Algerian coast near Mostaganem (OC-002-009, OC-003-009).

His sole companion is his devoted orderly Ben Zoof, a son of the hill of Montmartre in Paris (OC-003-016, OC-003-017).

## Plot Summary

A violent shock convulses the world: the laws of gravity are disturbed, and Servadac finds his body grown so light that he and Ben Zoof "ran like hares and leaped like chamois" (OC-006-047, OC-006-058). A comet has grazed the Earth and torn away a fragment of its surface.

The captain, Ben Zoof, and a scattering of other survivors are carried off on this new little world, which they come to call Gallia (OC-016-051, OC-017-001). Among them is the astronomer Professor Palmyrin Rosette, Servadac's old tutor, who claims the comet as his own discovery and charts its orbit (OC-027-008, OC-027-014).

As Gallia swings out toward the frozen depths and back again, the colonists endure cold and dwindling resources until the comet at last regrazes the Earth. Escaping by balloon at the moment of contact, the wanderers find themselves once more "in Algeria; and not far from Mostaganem" (OC-044-032, OC-045-002).

## Main Characters

- Captain Hector Servadac — the French officer carried into space aboard Gallia
- Ben Zoof — Servadac's faithful orderly, forever praising his native Montmartre
- Professor Palmyrin Rosette — the irascible astronomer who names and studies the comet

## Key Places & Technology

- Gallia — the comet-borne fragment of Earth on which the survivors live
- The balloon — the fragile craft by which the wanderers regain the Earth

## Themes

Verne stretches his scientific romance to a planetary scale, using the comet Gallia as a laboratory for astronomy, reduced gravity, and human adaptability. Beneath the speculation runs his familiar comedy of character — Servadac's gallantry, Rosette's monomania, and Ben Zoof's unquenchable love of Montmartre.
