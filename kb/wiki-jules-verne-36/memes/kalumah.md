# Kalumah

## Overview

Kalumah first appears as a young native drawn to the expedition's chief lady, "the name of the young girl was Kalumah, and she seemed to have taken a great fancy to Mrs Barnett" (FC-019-043).

She is one of the Esquimaux of the far northern coast, recognised at a later meeting as "the young Esquimaux girl Kalumah" (FC-031-087).

## Role in the Story

Through the depths of the polar winter she comes daily to the fort to sit by her friend, and "when Mrs Barnett read aloud, Kalumah listened with great attention" (FC-019-053).

When spring returns she keeps her word and seeks them out, for "the month of May having come round, Kalumah set out to fulfil her pledge" (FC-032-015).

A presentiment later drives her out into the storm, when "a kind of presentiment led Kalumah to venture down to the beach, and, braving the wind and rain" she launched her kayak (FC-032-029).

## Character & Traits

Her resolve is unshakeable, and once set on her course "Kalumah did not doubt or hesitate a moment" (FC-032-033).

Her endurance is extraordinary; when at last recovered it is found that "Kalumah had then been seventy hours at sea since she embarked" (FC-032-051).

Her love for her lost friends is plain, for "Kalumah's tears flowed fast at the loss of those whom she had come so far to see" (FC-032-019).

## Relationships

- [[Paulina Barnett]] — the lady she cherishes above all, whom she was loath to leave, being "sorry to part with Mrs Barnett" (FC-019-075), and to whom on her rescue she poured out her thanks, "seated on the sand between Mrs Barnett and Madge" (FC-032-013).
- [[Jaspar Hobson]] — the lieutenant amazed by her reappearance, for one wonders "what must Lieutenant Hobson have thought when he saw her leaning on Mrs Barnett" (FC-033-004).

## Appearances

- [[The Fur Country]] — the tale of the drifting settlement, at whose close she is welcomed back with joy, when "Mrs Mac-Nab, Mrs Rae and Mrs Joliffe overwhelmed her with caresses" (FC-033-002).
