## Overview

Charleston is the South Carolina port that lies at the heart of *[[The Blockade Runners]]*, besieged and blockaded during the Civil War. The daring captain resolves on "going straight to Charleston; no English boat has yet been able to penetrate into the harbour, except the Bermuda" (BR-001-052), the port standing as the hardest prize on the Confederate coast.

It is a city choked with the cotton it cannot ship. Uncle Vincent observes that "Charleston is overwhelmed with cotton; they are even burning it to get rid of it" (BR-001-053), the blockade having dammed up the wealth of the South within the town.

## In the Narrative

Charleston is the city where the war itself began. A prisoner's daughter recalls that her father "was at Charleston when the first shot of the Civil War was fired, and the troops of the Union driven from Fort Sumter by the Confederates" (BR-003-067), and a general affirms "it was at Charleston that the first shot of separation was fired" (BR-007-014).

The whole voyage of the Dolphin bends toward it. The runner must pass "the Federal fleets and forcing the blockade of Charleston" (BR-004-032), threading the guns of the forts to reach the quays where "the Dolphin, on arriving at the Charleston quay, had been saluted by the cheers of a large crowd" (BR-007-002).

## Geography & Features

Charleston stands on a long estuary between two rivers. Verne describes how "the town of Charleston is situated on the banks of an estuary seven miles long and two broad, called Charleston Harbour" (BR-006-014), and how, past the fort, "the town of Charleston appeared, lying between Ashley and Cooper Rivers" (BR-006-034).

Its harbour is guarded by the celebrated island fortress. Running the channels, the Dolphin passes near [[Fort Sumter]], and Verne notes the fort "so celebrated in the civil war, is situated three miles and a half from Charleston" (BR-006-031), the pentagonal stronghold commanding the entrance to the bay.

## Connections

Charleston is the blockaded South Carolina port at the centre of *[[The Blockade Runners]]*, the city where the Civil War's first shot was fired at [[Fort Sumter]], a cotton-choked harbour whose blockade the Dolphin runs, ranked with [[New Orleans]] among the Confederate ports.
