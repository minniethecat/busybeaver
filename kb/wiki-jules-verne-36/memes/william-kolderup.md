# William W. Kolderup

## Overview

He is introduced simply as a magnate of San Francisco, "It was William W. Kolderup, of San Francisco" (GM-001-053).

His fortune is boundless, for he is "a man extraordinarily rich, who counted dollars by the million as other men do by the thousand" (GM-002-002).

## Role in the Story

His will prevails at the great auction, where "Spencer Island fell for four millions of dollars to William W. Kolderup, of San Francisco" (GM-002-081).

It is he who secretly stages his nephew's whole ordeal, admitting that "the _Dream_ was quietly sunk by means of her water ballast, according to the instructions I had given" (GM-022-022).

Yet his contrivance is nearly undone by real perils, for "without the arrival of William W. Kolderup" the winter and genuine wild beasts would have destroyed the castaways (GM-022-064).

## Character & Traits

His commercial reach spans the globe, for he keeps "2000 branch offices scattered" across the world (GM-002-006).

His scheme extends even to counterfeit savages, which he laughingly explains were "stuffed with straw, and landed before you saw them with Jup Brass and his companions" (GM-022-032).

## Relationships

- Godfrey — his nephew and ward, whom "his uncle was thinking of binding" in the chains of marriage (GM-003-030).
- Phina — his goddaughter, for "Phina Hollaney was the goddaughter of William W. Kolderup" (GM-003-014), a match which "William W. Kolderup desired" (GM-003-025).
- [[Carefinotu]] — the hired player he set upon the island, who addresses him at the unmasking, "Yes, Mr. Kolderup" (GM-022-049).
