# Ephrinell

## Overview

He announces himself with a salesman's flourish as "Fulk Ephrinell, of the firm of Strong, Bulbul & Co., of New York City, New York, U.S.A." (ASC-002-027).

A seasoned globe-trotter, "Ephrinell, as may be supposed, has been everywhere--and even farther, as he observes" (ASC-002-030).

## Role in the Story

His trade fills the baggage van, and the narrator finds him "gathering up the odontological treasures of the forty-second case" of artificial teeth (ASC-003-060).

So bulky is his stock that "the front van is already full of Ephrinell's baggage" as the train is loaded (ASC-005-028).

At the frontier his affairs pass without a hitch, for "Ephrinell and Miss Bluett went through like a posted letter" at the custom-house (ASC-016-033).

## Character & Traits

His dry practicality disappoints the narrator, who reflects that "Ephrinell would not be quite the traveling companion I had dreamed of" (ASC-002-041).

He and his Englishwoman are cut from one cloth, "an understanding between these two perfectly Anglo-Saxon natures" (ASC-004-034).

Even at leisure the pair "talk of brokerages and prices current" rather than romance (ASC-004-053).

His courtship is a merger, and the narrator learns "that as soon as they reach Pekin, Miss Bluett will become Mrs. Ephrinell" (ASC-006-054).

## Relationships

- [[Claudius Bombarnac]] — the special correspondent who enrolls him among his cast, noting "I have already secured Ephrinell, and perhaps that charming Englishwoman" (ASC-004-005).
- [[Caterna]] — a fellow passenger at table, for at breakfast "gather all the passengers whom the sea has not affected: ...Ephrinell, Miss Bluett, Monsieur Caterna" (ASC-004-087).

## Appearances

- [[The Adventures of a Special Correspondent]] — as the American teeth-merchant whose commercial romance runs the length of the line, "all the time absorbed in their commercial tête-à-tête" with Miss Bluett (ASC-017-003).
