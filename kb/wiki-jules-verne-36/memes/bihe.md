## Overview

Bihe is a slave-trade market town of the Benguela interior in *Dick Sand: A Captain at Fifteen*, named among the important markets of the western slave-trade alongside Cassange and Kazounde. (DSCF-019-049) It holds one of the trader Alvez's factories. (DSCF-027-006)

## In the Narrative

Bihe belongs to Alvez's network of slave-factories: his principal establishment lay at Kazounde, a second at Bihe, and a third at Cassange, in Benguela. (DSCF-027-006) In the traffic of the region a man might be sought at the Bihe market. (DSCF-020-070) When ruin spread through the country, Bihe was not spared, numbering among the places where Alvez owned factories. (DSCF-032-025)

## Geography & Features

Bihe lies in the province of Benguela, in the interior of the western slave-trade country. (DSCF-027-006) It was reached over a route of seven hundred miles, along which the trader Alvez guided Lieutenant Cameron and his caravan to his own establishment at Bihe. (DSCF-027-014)

## Connections

Bihe belongs to *Dick Sand: A Captain at Fifteen* as one of the western slave-trade's important markets, with Cassange and Kazounde. (DSCF-019-049) It was one of the two other factories, with Cassange, that Alvez need not leave Kazounde to visit. (DSCF-033-015) From Bihe came Major Coimbra, whose son appeared alongside Alvez and was reckoned the greatest scamp in the province. (DSCF-027-032)
