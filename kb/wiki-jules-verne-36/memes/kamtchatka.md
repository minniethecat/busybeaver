## Overview

Kamtchatka is the remote Asiatic peninsula that lends its name to the sea and the current dominating the northern voyage of *[[The Fur Country]]*. Its waters are prized hunting grounds, for the trackers must "pursue it on the shores of the Kamtchatka Sea, and in the islands of the Behring Archipelago" (FC-008-022) in search of valuable furs.

The region is famous above all for its otters. The narrative notes that magnificent otters "scarcely inferior in value to those from Kamtchatka itself" (FC-008-024) are taken in these northern seas, marking the peninsula as the standard against which all such pelts are measured.

## In the Narrative

Kamtchatka's chief role is as the source of a dangerous current. Among the northern seas "one is called the Kamtchatka Current" (FC-025-025), and it becomes the unseen antagonist that seizes the drifting ice-island and threatens to bear it away.

The peril mounts as the island nears it. Hobson fears "our ice-field would soon meet the Kamtchatka current, and be carried by it to the northwest" (FC-029-012), and at the crisis "the island was finally in the grasp of the Kamtchatka Current, and was drifting towards the unknown latitudes where the large icebergs come into being" (FC-033-026).

## Geography & Features

The peninsula lies at the far northeast of Asia. The Kamtchatka Current "runs along the coast of Asia" (FC-040-015), sweeping past its shores, and the land marks a limit of the fur-bearing north, being reached only by hunters who follow the game "as far as Kamtchatka" (FC-016-019).

Its faunal range is set beside the world's other cold coasts. The peninsula is grouped with places where rare pelts are found, "in Jan Mayen's Land, the Aleutian Isles, Kamtchatka, Russian America, and Iceland" (FC-015-025). Escape overland toward it is even weighed as a desperate hope, for the castaways consider "venturing across the frozen ice fields we might perhaps reach either Russian America or Kamtchatka" (FC-033-013).

## Connections

Kamtchatka gives its name to the current that menaces [[Victoria Island]] and its castaways in *[[The Fur Country]]*, driving them past [[Cape Bathurst]] toward the Arctic. It lies across the northern seas from the [[Aleutian Islands]] and the [[Behring Strait]], at the eastern edge of [[Siberia]].
