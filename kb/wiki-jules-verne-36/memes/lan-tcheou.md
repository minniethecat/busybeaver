## Overview

Lan-Tcheou is an important city of western China on the Grand Transasiatic railway, seven hundred kilometres east of Sou-Tcheou. In the Vernean corpus it belongs to *The Adventures of a Special Correspondent*, where it is a halt on Claudius Bombarnac's journey — the town at which the imperial treasure van draws a governor's attention and the travellers break their run to visit. (ASC-023-003)

## In the Narrative

The train covers the "seven hundred kilometres" that separate it from Sou-Tcheou and reaches Lan-Tcheou "in the morning—26th of May, 7 A.M." (ASC-023-036) There the local governor, acting on an order from Pekin, has telegraphed the arrival of the imperial treasure and waits to learn whether to forward it or "keep it provisionally at Lan-Tcheou." (ASC-023-042)

Indifferent to the treasure's fate, the narrator declares that "our business is to visit Lan-Tcheou," (ASC-023-048) and on the platform, just as the departure signal sounds, Caterna asks his wife what was the most curious thing she had seen in the city. (ASC-023-070)

## Geography & Features

Lan-Tcheou marks a bend in the Great Wall: after "descending to the southeast toward Lan-Tcheou," the wall turns to the northeast, covering the provinces north of Pekin. (ASC-022-072) On leaving the city the railway "crosses a well-cultivated country, watered by numerous streams, and hilly enough to necessitate frequent curves." (ASC-024-001)

## Connections

Lan-Tcheou is bound to *The Adventures of a Special Correspondent* as the Grand Transasiatic city where the escort of the Son of Heaven's treasure pauses under the governor's eye. (ASC-023-042) It is the town the narrator sets out "to visit," (ASC-023-048) and the station from which he sends the telegram that is answered only after he reaches the Chinese capital. (ASC-027-062)
