# Caribbean Sea

## Overview

The Caribbean Sea is the clear tropical water whose depths the *Nautilus* traverses in *Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea*, prized by Aronnax for the astonishing transparency of its waters. He marvels that "in certain parts of the Caribbean Sea, you can see the sandy bottom with startling distinctness as deep as 145 meters down." (TTLU-014-049)

## In the Narrative

The sea becomes one of the voyage's great galleries of marine life. Cruising it, Aronnax finds "through the panels opening into these Caribbean waters ten meters below the surface of the waves... so many fascinating exhibits to describe in my daily notes!" (TTLU-042-009)

Even the ship's slow drift lets him study its creatures at the windows: "through the windows of our almost motionless Nautilus, I could see nothing among these long filaments other than the chief articulates of the division Brachyura." (TTLU-042-013) Its corals and sponges swell the vessel's collections. (TTLU-011-034)

## Geography & Features

The sea lies beside the warm gulf that feeds the great current. A sea-creature the *Nautilus* meets is placed by its shunning of these waters — it "obviously didn't want to frequent the waves of the Gulf of Mexico or the Caribbean Sea." (TTLU-042-002)

Its brilliant clarity and shallow sandy floors set it apart from the darker oceans the submarine crosses, a bright tropical basin flanking the Gulf Stream's birthplace.
