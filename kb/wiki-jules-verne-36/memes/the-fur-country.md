## Overview

*The Fur Country* is Jules Verne's Arctic drama of a fur-trading outpost set adrift on the ice. It opens at Fort Reliance, where "Captain Craventy gave a fête" in honour of the celebrated traveller Mrs Paulina Barnett (FC-001-002, FC-001-029).

The Company entrusts Lieutenant Jaspar Hobson, "a man of forty years of age," with founding a new fort on the shores of the Arctic Ocean (FC-001-024, FC-002-011).

## Plot Summary

Hobson leads his party — Sergeant Long and Mrs Barnett among them — north to Cape Bathurst, chosen because it lies beyond the seventieth parallel as the Company requires (FC-011-009, FC-012-052). There they raise the fort and settle in for the long polar winter (FC-012-021).

But the ground beneath them is treacherous. An earthquake snaps the isthmus that joins "the peninsula of Victoria" to the mainland, and the settlers learn their cape "is nothing more than a vast sheet of ice welded" to the shore (FC-024-002, FC-024-027).

Their fort has become a floating island over which "we have absolutely no control," carried off by the ocean currents toward Bering Strait (FC-025-014, FC-025-025). The colonists can only drift with the melting ice and struggle to endure until it bears them within reach of rescue.

## Main Characters

- Lieutenant Jaspar Hobson — the Company officer who founds and commands the fort
- Mrs Paulina Barnett — the intrepid lady traveller who joins the expedition
- Sergeant Long — Hobson's steady second, "the type of a good soldier"

## Key Places & Technology

- Cape Bathurst — the Arctic headland, beyond 70° N, where the fort is raised
- The peninsula of Victoria — the "floating island" of ice that carries the colony out to sea

## Themes

Verne turns solid ground itself into the story's central menace: a fort built in good faith on what proves to be a raft of ice. The novel blends meticulous polar geography with a slow-building dread, and finds its heroism less in conquest than in the patient endurance of Hobson and Barnett against a wandering, melting world.
