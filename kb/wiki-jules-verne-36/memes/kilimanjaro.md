## Overview

Kilimanjaro is the great East African mountain into whose flank the Baltimore Gun Club bores a colossal cannon in Topsy Turvy, meaning to fire a shot that will shift the Earth's axis. The enormous cannon had been erected in the flank of Kilimanjaro. (TT-019-038)

## In the Narrative

The whole world awaits the firing. Thanks to the journals, every one knew the precise hour at which the shooting would take place. (TT-019-002) The moment is fixed to midnight at the mountain's meridian: just as the twenty-fourth minute after five o'clock, corresponding with midnight at Kilimanjaro, was reached, the calculation turns. (TT-019-015)

Afterward the deed is confirmed — "The cannon was fired off yesterday evening at midnight exactly by the device bored in the southern part of Kilimanjaro." (TT-019-035) Yet at first no one knew what had happened at Kilimanjaro. (TT-019-032) Later the engineers are pressed: "Did your shaft at Kilimanjaro really have a diameter of twenty-seven metres?" (TT-020-008)

## Geography & Features

The highest massif of East Africa, Kilimanjaro offers the Gun Club a mountain vast enough to swallow a cannon-shaft twenty-seven metres across, its southern flank chosen for the axis-shifting shot.
