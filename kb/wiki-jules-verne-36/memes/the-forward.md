# The Forward

## Overview

The Forward is the brig built for [[Captain Hatteras]]'s secret expedition toward the North Pole in *[[the-adventures-of-captain-hatteras|The Adventures of Captain Hatteras]]*. A newspaper notice announces that "the brig _Forward_, Captain K. Z----, Richard Shandon mate, will start from New Prince's Docks for an unknown destination" (ACH-001-002).

Though she "might easily have been confounded with the other brigs in the port," connoisseurs spot her peculiarities: "a vessel of a hundred and seventy tons, charged with a screw and steam-engine of a hundred and twenty horse-power" (ACH-001-004).

## Design & Operation

The Forward is purpose-built to survive polar ice. Her hull is armoured against pressure: "her ribs were built of teak-wood... remarkable for its extreme hardness, and were, besides, plated with iron," so that she was "destined to resist enormous pressure" (ACH-001-046).

Her bow is a weapon against the pack: "her steel prow, cast in the workshop of R. Hawthorn, of Newcastle," carries "a steel cutter as sharp as a razor and capable of cutting a three-decker in two" (ACH-001-047, ACH-001-008). A "wide helm-port" lets the crew "take off the helm when they like," a manoeuvre "often necessary when you have ice to deal with" (ACH-001-012).

Power comes from a compact engine "of a hundred and twenty horse-power, with oscillating cylinders," driving her "fourteen knots an hour with her screw" (ACH-001-049, ACH-001-009). She stows coal and provisions "enough for five or six years" (ACH-001-014) and a hold of ice tools — "gigantic saws and powerful instruments... blasting cylinders" (ACH-001-052).

## Role in the Story

The Forward carries her crew deep into the Arctic, "breaking up the ice with her steel prow" as she forces a passage north (ACH-016-036). To seasoned sailors her purpose is plain: she "was prepared to face the ice regions" (ACH-001-048).

The voyage ends in mutiny and fire. After the crew turn on Hatteras, the abandoned brig burns: "For two days the _Forward_ struggled with the flames" before the ice swallowed her wreck (ACH-034-021).

## Science & Speculation

Verne frames the Forward against the real history of Arctic exploration, invoking the Franklin search, McClintock's 1859 return, and McClure's North-West passage as the backdrop to Hatteras's obsession (ACH-001-048).

Her engineering — iron-plated teak ribs, a cast-steel ram, a removable helm, antiscorbutic stores — is drawn straight from mid-nineteenth-century ice-navigation practice rather than invented, making the Forward one of Verne's most realistic vehicles.
