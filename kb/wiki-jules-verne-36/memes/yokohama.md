## Overview

Yokohama is the principal Japanese port, the point on Phileas Fogg's circuit where the route from Hong Kong meets the Pacific crossing to San Francisco. (AWED-017-012)

Reaching it, Fogg has traversed exactly one half of the terrestrial globe by the ninth day after leaving the port. (AWED-024-011)

## In the Narrative

The steamer for Yokohama governs Fogg's schedule out of Hong Kong; a delay of twenty hours, making him too late for the boat, would almost inevitably cost him the wager. (AWED-018-003) When the Carnatic sails early, Fogg is twenty-four hours behind-hand and the Yokohama steamer is missed. (AWED-018-007)

Fogg presses on by other means. Fix fears he might jump from one boat to another and get under way again for Yokohama before anything can be done. (AWED-016-012)

## Geography & Features

Yokohama is a working harbour of the Pacific Mail Steamship Company, whose steamer the General Grant departs it for San Francisco. (AWED-024-008)

Its roadstead is busy with signalling: the captain of the Yokohama steamer espies the Tankadere's flag at half-mast and directs his course toward her. (AWED-024-002)
