# Nell

## Overview

Nell is the strange young girl discovered in the depths of New Aberfoyle, unaccustomed to the company of others: she "uttered these few words like one unused to speak much" (UC-012-010).

Her origin is the story's central secret. Only late does she confess it: "I am the granddaughter of old Silfax," she says, adding, "I never knew a mother till the day I came here" (UC-017-027).

## Role in the Story

Taken into the Ford cottage, "by the time Nell had been a fortnight in the cottage, she had become a most intelligent and zealous assistant to old Madge" (UC-012-034), and her rescue soon made her a marvel: "Nell became a fashionable wonder without knowing it" (UC-012-022).

Yet a mystery clung to her — "some secret existed in connection with the place, which she could have explained" (UC-012-028).

She proves to be the unseen guardian who once kept the trapped explorers alive; Harry reveals to her that "those men were James Starr, my father, and myself, Nell!" (UC-012-076), when they lay "at the point of death... but for a kind and charitable being--an angel perhaps" (UC-012-074).

At last she names the author of every attack: recounting how, "as soon as my grandfather saw that you were fairly inside the gallery leading to New Aberfoyle, he stopped up the" passage (UC-017-033).

She warns that against Silfax resistance is vain, for "my grandfather is everywhere and nowhere" (UC-017-038). Her story closes at the altar, where the minister asks "Harry, will you take Nell to be your wife" (UC-018-015).

## Character & Traits

Born in darkness, her eyes cannot yet bear the day: "this imperfect light suited Nell, to whose eyes a glare was very unpleasant" (UC-012-049).

She loves the world she was born to as much as the one she is learning, saying "darkness is beautiful as well as light" (UC-012-057). Beneath her timidity runs a grave selflessness — "for me indeed it is well, whatever may happen," she answers earnestly, "for others--who can tell?" (UC-012-069).

## Relationships

- [[James Starr]] — the engineer whose life she secretly saved in the trapped gallery, revealed to be one of "those men" (UC-012-076).
- Harry Ford — the young miner who loves her and whom she weds; the marriage that "malevolence would be disarmed" only when "she was his wife" (UC-016-052).
- [[The Underground City]] — the novel whose buried romance and mystery both centre on her hidden origin.
