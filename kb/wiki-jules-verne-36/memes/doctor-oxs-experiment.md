## Overview

*Doctor Ox's Experiment* is Jules Verne's satirical fantasy set in the drowsy Flemish town of Quiquendone, which "you will not succeed" in finding "on any map of Flanders" (DOSE-001-002). Its 2,393 placid souls have lived undisturbed "for some eight or nine hundred years," so phlegmatic that the burgomaster takes over an hour to smoke a single pipe (DOSE-001-002, DOSE-003-002).

Into this sleepy place comes the mysterious Doctor Ox, offering to light the whole town at his own expense.

## Plot Summary

"Doctor Ox had arrived at Quiquendone five months before, accompanied by his assistant... Gédéon Ygène," proposing to illuminate the streets not by ordinary means but by "oxyhydric gas" (DOSE-004-006, DOSE-003-042). His apparatus decomposes water so that "the oxygen passed off at one end; the hydrogen... at the other" (DOSE-004-010).

But the lighting is a pretext. In secret the doctor "saturated, first the public buildings, then the private dwellings, finally the streets of Quiquendone, with pure oxygen," for "one who lives in an air saturated with oxygen grows excited, frantic, burns!" (DOSE-017-002, DOSE-017-003).

To Ox and Ygène the townsfolk are merely "subjects... for our experiment," and success would let them "reform the world" (DOSE-004-022, DOSE-004-031).

The dulled town flares to life. Passions kindle everywhere — even the languid courtship of Frantz Niklausse and Suzel Van Tricasse, who had reckoned to marry only "in five or six years" (DOSE-006-006, DOSE-006-028). At last the roused citizens march to make war upon the neighbouring town of Virgamen.

The experiment ends by accident. "A formidable explosion resounded" when "a communication had been established between the reservoir which contained the oxygen and that which enclosed the hydrogen," and in the tumult "Doctor Ox and his assistant Ygène had disappeared" (DOSE-015-002, DOSE-015-006, DOSE-015-007).

At once "Quiquendone immediately became the peaceable, phlegmatic, and Flemish town it formerly was" (DOSE-016-002).

## Main Characters

- Doctor Ox — the scientist who secretly floods the town with oxygen to test his theory
- Gédéon Ygène — his gaunt assistant, who obeys him blindly
- Van Tricasse — the placid burgomaster of Quiquendone
- Frantz Niklausse and Suzel — the slow-courting young lovers roused by the gas

## Key Places & Technology

- Quiquendone — the sleepy Flemish town chosen as the doctor's laboratory
- Doctor Ox's gas-works — the oxyhydric apparatus that saturates the town with oxygen

## Themes

Verne plays a chemist's joke on human nature, asking whether "virtue, courage, talent, wit" are "only a question of oxygen" (DOSE-017-007). The satire mocks both leaden provincial torpor and the reckless scientist who would remake mankind by tampering with the very air it breathes.
