# Zinca Klork

## Overview

Her nationality is written in her name, for "this Zinca Klork--her name showed it--ought to be a Roumanian" (ASC-004-017).

She is imagined as a beauty awaiting her lover's arrival, "the most lovely of Roumanians, is expecting you at Pekin" (ASC-010-057).

## Role in the Story

She is the destination of Kinko's strange journey, her home inscribed on his case as "Mademoiselle Zinca Klork, Avenue Cha-Coua, Pekin, Petchili, China" (ASC-004-016).

She waits out the long transit in dread, "pretty Zinca Klork, devoured by anxiety in her house in the Avenue Cha-Coua" (ASC-022-060).

When she believes her lover lost, grief overwhelms her, so that "the unhappy Zinca falls on to a chair... her tears roll down like rain on an autumn night" (ASC-026-085).

At the sound of him below, "Zinca jumps up, springs to the window, opens it, and we look out" (ASC-026-090).

## Character & Traits

She meets the narrator's arrival with a start, "evidently much surprised at seeing a stranger in her doorway" (ASC-026-044).

Her devotion turns to bold appeal for Kinko's rescue, pleading, "Gentlemen... do save him before he is sentenced" (ASC-027-018).

Her cause is blessed by the story's kindly fortune, for "divine intervention in favor of Kinko and Zinca Klork was manifested in all its plenitude" (ASC-013-104).

## Relationships

- [[Kinko]] — the stowaway lover she awaits and at last weds, for the narrator's "No. 11 was married to Zinca Klork with great ceremony" (ASC-027-059).
- [[Claudius Bombarnac]] — the narrator who seeks her out, reading "the name of Madame Zinca Klork on a door" (ASC-026-041).

## Appearances

- [[The Adventures of a Special Correspondent]] — as the consignee of Kinko's disguised freight, the supposed recipient of "glass goods exported to Miss Zinca Klork, Avenue Cha-Coua, Pekin, China" (ASC-007-057).
