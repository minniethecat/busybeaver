# The Firing of the Kilimanjaro Cannon

## Overview

The Firing of the Kilimanjaro Cannon is the climactic discharge of Barbicane & Co.'s colossal gun in the African Wamasai, an attempt to jolt the earth onto a new axis that gives *[[topsy-turvy|Topsy-Turvy]]* its central spectacle.

The weapon was no ordinary gun. It was not a cannon, nor even a mortar, that Barbicane and Nicholl intended to make, but simply a gallery bored in the massive rock of [[Kilimanjaro]]—a shaft of a mine (TT-017-008).

## What Happens

President Barbicane named the aim plainly. Asked by the Sultan what all these operations were for, he replied that it was a work which would change the face of the earth and bring the greatest glory upon the greatest of Eastern kings (TT-017-013).

The firing was fixed for a precise instant. On the evening of the 22d of September, twelve hours after the sun had passed the meridian of Kilimanjaro—that is, at midnight—Captain Nicholl was to touch off the terrible cannon (TT-018-002).

The onlookers kept their distance. With great precaution the Sultan, his court, and the crowd had all taken a position three kilometres from the shaft bored in Kilimanjaro, so that they would have nothing to fear from the concussion of the air (TT-018-005).

At the appointed second the order came. "Fire," ordered President Barbicane (TT-018-011).

The shot answered instantly. Nicholl pressed the button, and a terrible detonation followed whose echoes spread to the furthest corners of the province of Wamasai, with a sharp whistle and a rush of air from the deflagration of the two thousand tons of melimelonite (TT-018-012).

## Significance

The distant report confirmed the deed and its cost. A cablegram from the consul at Zanzibar announced that the cannon had been fired off at midnight exactly by the device bored in the southern part of Kilimanjaro, whole provinces destroyed by the concussion of the air and the ocean agitated as far as the Mozambique channel (TT-019-035).

Yet the great object was not attained. Nothing changed: a few minutes after sunrise the disc inclined away towards the Australian hemisphere, and nothing was changed in its apparent course (TT-019-021).

The verdict was total failure. The trial which Barbicane & Co. had made had entirely failed (TT-019-040).

## Participants & Setting

- Fired by President Impey Barbicane and Captain Nicholl of the Gun Club, from a mine-shaft gun bored into the southern flank of [[Kilimanjaro]] in the Wamasai of Central Africa.
- Charged with two thousand tons of melimelonite and a projectile launched to shift the earth's axis, per the calculations of J. T. Maston; witnessed by Sultan Bali-Bali and his people.
- The shot succeeded mechanically but failed astronomically—the axis unmoved—leaving only regional devastation; chronicled in *[[topsy-turvy|Topsy-Turvy]]*.
