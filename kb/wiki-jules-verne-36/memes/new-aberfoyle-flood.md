# The Flooding of New Aberfoyle

## Overview

The Flooding of New Aberfoyle is the sudden inundation that nearly drowns the subterranean colony of Coal Town, a disaster later proved to be a deliberate act of sabotage and one of the mysterious blows struck against the mine in *[[the-underground-city|The Underground City]]*.

At its onset the peril seems total. Cries arose from all parts of Coal Town, which was threatened by a sudden inundation, and the inhabitants fled for safety to the top of the schist rocks bordering the lake (UC-016-006).

## What Happens

The miners fear the worst. It was feared that the sea had burst into the colliery, in which case the entire excavation would be completely flooded and not a single inhabitant of New Aberfoyle would escape death (UC-016-007).

Simon Ford halts the panic. He shouts that if the town is to be overwhelmed the floods will rush faster than any man can flee, then judges from the water that the danger is over (UC-016-008).

His reading proves right. The sudden influx of water had rushed to the very lowest bed of the vast mine, and its only ultimate effect was to raise the level of Loch Malcolm a few feet, leaving Coal Town uninjured (UC-016-011).

The cause is at first a mystery. Simon and his men could not decide whether this inundation was owing to a subterranean sheet of water, until the local papers explained the marvellous phenomenon of Loch Katrine (UC-016-012).

## Significance

The truth is sabotage. Resolving to satisfy themselves, James Starr, together with Simon and Harry, returned to the scene of the disaster (UC-016-025).

Proceeding by boat to examine the natural pillars on which the basin of the loch had rested, they discovered that the massive columns had been undermined by blasting, the blackened traces of explosion proving the fall had been premeditated by man (UC-016-026).

The narrowness of the escape appals them. "It is impossible to doubt it," said James Starr, reflecting on what might have happened had the sea, instead of a little loch, been let in upon the mine (UC-016-027).

## Participants & Setting

- The sudden inundation of Coal Town beneath New Aberfoyle, when Loch Katrine drained through pillars secretly undermined by blasting.
- Its panic stayed by the old overman Simon Ford and its sabotage exposed by the engineer James Starr with Harry Ford.
- One of the covert attacks on the colony chronicled in *[[the-underground-city|The Underground City]]*.
