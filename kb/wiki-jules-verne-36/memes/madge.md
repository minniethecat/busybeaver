# Madge

## Overview

Madge enters the story as the traveller's attendant, for "the celebrated traveller was accompanied by a servant named Madge" (FC-001-028).

Their bond outgrows service, since "Paulina looked upon Madge as an elder sister, and Madge treated Paulina as her daughter" (FC-001-028).

## Role in the Story

She follows her mistress into the Arctic, riding at her side, "the sledge provided for Mrs Barnett and her faithful Madge" (FC-004-064).

When the exploring party set out to verify a report, "Lieutenant Hobson, Mrs Barnett, Madge, and a few others at once went to ascertain the truth" (FC-028-003).

## Character & Traits

She is the plainer, steadier soul of the pair, "more matter of fact than her mistress," who disguised from herself "neither the dangers of an expedition to the Arctic Ocean" (FC-005-031).

Her memory yields to feeling, for she confesses, "I have not the gift of remembering like you" (FC-005-024).

She is a patient angler too, "the faithful Madge, another worthy disciple of Isaak Walton" (FC-014-019).

Her courage has its limit at the native hut, where "Madge could not stand it, and hurried out at once" (FC-019-048), though moments before "she bravely entered the narrow tunnel in imitation of her guide" (FC-019-047).

Above all she keeps faith, answering the winter's threat with "her usual unshaken confidence" (FC-031-034), and vowing of disaster, "God would never permit that" (FC-025-032).

## Relationships

- [[Paulina Barnett]] — her mistress and dearest friend, whom she tends through peril, watching by the sick bed until dawn, for "Mrs Barnett and Madge watched by him until the next morning" (FC-021-063), and joining her lone reconnaissance, since "Madge agreed at once to Mrs Barnett's proposal" (FC-031-028).

## Appearances

- [[The Fur Country]] — the expedition tale through which she shares every hardship at Paulina's side.
