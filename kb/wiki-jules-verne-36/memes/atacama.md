## Overview

The Atacama is the desert of South America, a region apart between the Andes and the Pacific Ocean and one of the most vast deserts of the continent. (DSCF-017-015) In Dick Sand it is the false destination by which the traitor Harris lures the shipwrecked party inland, though their true landfall lies in Africa.

## In the Narrative

Questioned about the country, Harris answers that he lives in the south on the Chilian frontier but is bound at present for Atacama, in the northeast. (DSCF-015-060) He proposes that if the castaways follow him they will be well received, and the means of transport to gain the town of Atacama will not fail them. (DSCF-015-066)

Wary of the plan, Dick Sand asks why, instead of travelling a hundred and twenty miles through the Desert of Atacama, they should not follow the coast. (DSCF-015-097)

Harris only repeats that at the Farm of San Felice they will be furnished the means of gaining the town. (DSCF-015-105) Long after, he confesses that for the first three days he made his victims take this province for the Desert of Atacama, which he had once visited. (DSCF-020-011)

## Geography & Features

Harris paints it as a land able to stand apart from the plateau, unknown country if it did not form one of South America's most vast deserts, set between the Andes and the Pacific Ocean. (DSCF-017-015) Its absence of caoutchouc trees and quinquinas betrays the fraud: the ground is neither the plateau of Atacama nor the Bolivian pampa. (DSCF-018-109)
