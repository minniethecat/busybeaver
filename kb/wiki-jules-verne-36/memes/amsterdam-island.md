# Amsterdam Island

## Overview

Amsterdam Island is the real Indian Ocean island lying on the 37th parallel that Glenarvan's searchers consider as they trace Captain Grant's course. It is "the only group of islands found on this route, the Amsterdam Isles." (SC-027-064)

A lonely landmark, its "high cone of which in clear weather is visible fifty miles off." (SC-029-005)

## In the Narrative

The searchers debate the island as a possible refuge: Paganel asks "wouldn't it be advisable to stop a day or two at the Tristan d'Acunha Isles and the Amsterdam?" (SC-027-084) Yet "after a close survey, the Amsterdam Isles were rejected in their turn." (SC-027-065)

The *Duncan* sights it on the long passage east — "between the Cape and Amsterdam Island there is a distance of 2,900 miles... only a ten day's voyage," (SC-029-002) and Paganel keeps up "a discussion on the advantages and disadvantages of Isolation" until the ship draws near. (SC-029-022)

## Geography & Features

The island sits at "37 degrees 47 minutes latitude and 77 degrees 24 minutes longitude." (SC-029-005) It is a haven for wildlife and stray castaways alike: "like Tristan d'Acunha, Amsterdam Island is equally rich in seals and Robinsons." (SC-029-007)

It is in fact one of a close pair — "to the north is Amsterdam Island, and to the south St." Paul. (SC-029-023)
