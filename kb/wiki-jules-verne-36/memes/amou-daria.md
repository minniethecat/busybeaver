## Overview

The Amou-Daria is the Oxus of the Ancients, the rival of the Indus and the Ganges. (ASC-010-064) It is the great river of Central Asia, running down from the highlands toward the old bed that once fed the [[Aral Sea]].

## In the Narrative

In [[The Adventures of a Special Correspondent]] the traveller counts on seeing it. If he cannot visit the van, he says, he shall at least see the Amou-Daria. (ASC-010-063) A large number of boats, most of them laden with petroleum, pass up and down the Amou-Daria, and it frequently happens that these become fire-ships. (ASC-010-071)

## Geography & Features

An affluent of the Amou-Daria, the Zarafchane, the river that rolls with gold, winds up to the valley of the Sogd, that fertile oasis on which stands the city of Samarkand. (ASC-010-072) The Grand Trans-Asian line crosses the Syr Daria by a bridge like that over the Amou-Daria, but of less importance. (ASC-013-013)

## Connections

The Amou-Daria drains the country below the [[Pamir]], its old course once running to the [[Aral Sea]]. (ASC-010-064) It carries the petroleum boats of Turkestan up and down its stream. (ASC-010-071)
