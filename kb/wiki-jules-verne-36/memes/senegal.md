## Overview

Senegal is the West African region and French colony that forms the western goal of the balloon voyage in [[Five Weeks in a Balloon]]. Lying at the Atlantic edge of the continent — the coast where "the distance from Zanzibar to the coast of Senegal is only thirty-five hundred--say four thousand miles" (FWB-008-022) — it is the settled country toward which Dr. Ferguson steers across Africa.

## In the Narrative

For the aeronauts Senegal means rescue and journey's end. Verne warns that "as far as Senegal, this part of Africa is marked down as dangerous" (FWB-041-003), yet it is home to "the French settlers, who occupy the colony of Senegal" (FWB-041-022).

Ferguson takes his bearings and finds himself "scarcely twenty-five miles from Senegal" (FWB-042-002); the sinking balloon is at last saved by an expedition "sent by the governor of Senegal" (FWB-044-002). Earlier travellers had likewise "arrived in Senegal, penetrated to the Fonta-Jallon, visited the Foullah and Mandingo populations" (FWB-038-037), and to a castaway it promised "fellow-countrymen at Senegal" (SC-008-031).

## Geography & Features

The region is defined by the Senegal River. It marks a great watershed, for the aeronauts "would have to cross the chain which separates the basin of the Niger from the basin of the Senegal" (FWB-041-002). Where the river falls, "with a breadth of two thousand feet, the Senegal precipitates itself for a height of one hundred and fifty, with a thundering reverberation" (FWB-043-067) — the cataracts of Gouina.

Upstream on the river stands "the post of Medina" (FWB-044-010). Verne ranks the Senegal among the world's great arteries, naming it beside "Amazon, Plata, Orinoco, Niger, Senegal, Elbe, Loire, and Rhine" (TTLU-032-002).

## Connections

Senegal is the West African goal of Dr. Ferguson's aerial crossing in [[Five Weeks in a Balloon]], the French colony that closes his traverse of [[Africa]]. Its river basin adjoins that of the Niger and drains toward the bay of [[Cape Verde]], while far inland lies fabled [[Timbuctoo]], another waypoint of the same voyage. Here, among the cataracts of Gouina, the exhausted Victoria came down at last among countrymen.
