## Overview

Singapore is a coaling stop on Phileas Fogg's passage across the Indian Ocean and a link in Detective Fix's chain of pursuit. The island of Singapore is not imposing in aspect, for there are no mountains; yet its appearance is not without attractions. (AWED-017-009) The Rangoon puts in here on her way to Hong Kong.

## In the Narrative

For Fix the port is a telegraph office, not a landfall. All the detective's hopes were now centred on Hong Kong, for the steamer's stay at Singapore would be too brief to enable him to take any steps there. (AWED-016-008) Still, the wire serves — the steamer stopped at Singapore, whence there is a telegraphic line to Hong Kong. (AWED-016-013)

The stop is a matter of hours. The Rangoon weighed anchor at Singapore the next day at four a.m., to receive coal, having gained half a day on the prescribed time. (AWED-017-007) From here the sea road runs on — Singapore is distant some thirteen hundred miles from the island of Hong Kong. (AWED-017-012)

## Geography & Features

A low, forested British island port at the tip of the Malay Peninsula, Singapore is a coaling and telegraph station on the Calcutta–Hong Kong mail run — thirteen hundred miles of sea short of Fix's hoped-for arrest.
