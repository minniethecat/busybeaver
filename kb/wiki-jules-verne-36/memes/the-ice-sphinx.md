# The Ice Sphinx

## Overview

The Ice Sphinx is the closing event of *[[An Antarctic Mystery]]*, in which the reunited survivors of the *Jane* and the *Halbrane* discover that a colossal Antarctic mound is a natural magnet, learn the fate of the lost Arthur Pym, and witness the death of the half-breed Dirk Peters (AM-025-056).

## What Happens

As the *Paracuta* neared the western coast, the grapnel that had belonged to the *Halbrane* flew out of its socket as though drawn by an irresistible power, straining its rope to breaking point (AM-025-032). When the boatswain tried to cut it free, the knife was snatched from his hand, the rope broke, and the grapnel shot off toward a great sphinx-shaped mound (AM-025-034).

At the same moment every article of iron or steel on board—cooking utensils, arms, and the knives torn from their pockets—took flight in the same direction, while the boat drove ashore (AM-025-035).

On the beach the crews found the wreck of the boat Hearne had stolen, with all its ironwork gone, down to the hinges of the rudder (AM-025-040). Three corpses lay nearby—Hearne, Martin Holt, and one of the Falklands men (AM-025-043).

## Significance

Approaching the dusky mound, the narrator grasped the truth and cried out that it was a loadstone, a magnet of prodigious power of attraction (AM-025-054). The Antarctic Sphinx was simply a colossal magnet, whose pull had torn the iron from the *Halbrane's* boat and would have destroyed any iron-built vessel that came near (AM-025-056).

At the base they found their arms, utensils, and grapnel adhering to the monster's sides, fixed too high and too fast to be recovered (AM-025-075).

Dirk Peters, silent since leaving the places where no trace of Pym had been found, stretched his body toward the sphinx as though turned to iron and drawn by the magnet (AM-025-072). At the back of the monster's right paw he found an almost naked corpse, preserved rigid by the polar cold, a rusted musket-barrel held across it by a cross-belt (AM-025-082) (AM-025-084).

"Pym—my poor Pym!" groaned the half-breed (AM-025-085). As he tried to rise and kiss the body his knees failed, his faithful heart broke, and he fell back dead (AM-025-086). The story was easy to read: after their separation Pym had drifted into the monster's zone and, unable to cast off his slung gun, was seized by the magnetic fluid and hurled against the fatal loadstone Sphinx (AM-025-087).

## Participants & Setting

- Dirk Peters, the half-breed who had shared Arthur Pym's original voyage, rides the *Paracuta* northward in obstinate silence and dies of a broken heart at the foot of the sphinx on finding his companion's preserved corpse (AM-025-088).

- Arthur Pym, lost since his boat separated from the others years before, is revealed to have been drawn to the magnetic mound and killed, his body fixed six feet above the ground on the monster's flank (AM-025-083).

- Jeorling, the narrator, and the reunited crews of the *Jane* and the *Halbrane* land on what they name the Land of the Sphinx, witness the magnetic catastrophe, and bury Peters beside Pym on the shore of the Antarctic mystery.
