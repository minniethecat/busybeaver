## Overview

Saint Paul de Loanda is the capital of the Portuguese colony on the west coast of Africa in *Dick Sand: A Captain at Fifteen*. (DSCF-019-032) A port and administrative seat, it stands roughly four hundred miles from the interior slave-market of Kazounde. (DSCF-025-036)

## In the Narrative

Loanda shadows the story as the seat of colonial authority: it holds the penitentiaries of the colony (DSCF-020-033), and one of the trader Alvez's agents is an escaped prisoner from the Loanda penitentiary. (DSCF-020-076) In the end he is recognised, conducted to Saint Paul de Loanda, and condemned to finish his days in one of its penitentiaries. (DSCF-037-030)

## Geography & Features

Saint Paul de Loanda lies on the west coast of Africa, the capital of a colony that once set off from the kingdom of Portugal. (DSCF-019-032) The distance from Loanda to the interior city does not exceed four hundred miles, leaving some two hundred and fifty miles between it and the camp on the Coanza. (DSCF-025-036)

## Connections

Saint Paul de Loanda belongs to *Dick Sand: A Captain at Fifteen* as the coastal goal of journeys across the continent — the object of crossing Africa obliquely from the Cape. (DSCF-032-037) From Loanda, David Livingstone set out toward the Zaïre by way of San Salvador (DSCF-019-054), and left the city on the 24th of September. (DSCF-032-046)
