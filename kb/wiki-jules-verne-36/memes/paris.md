## Overview

Paris is the French capital, home ground of Passepartout, who was a true Parisian of Paris. (AWED-002-007) It ranks among the cities a guide-book claims one can "do" in two days, alongside Rome and London. (ASC-001-035)

## In the Narrative

Two first-class tickets for Paris were speedily purchased as Fogg set out from London on his wager. (AWED-004-032) His itinerary recorded reaching Paris on Thursday, October 3rd, at 7.20 a.m., and leaving at 8.40 a.m. (AWED-007-031) All that Passepartout saw of Paris lay between twenty past seven and twenty to nine that morning. (AWED-008-012)

## Geography & Features

The Boulevard des Italiens is to Paris what Broadway is to New York and Regent Street to London. (AWED-025-006) The city was a magnet for education and amusement, where a family of distinction might send a son to spend some years. (ASC-009-003)

## Connections

Passepartout had hoped they might go only as far as Paris, to see the city once more. (AWED-004-020) Paris is the first Continental stop after [[London]] on the eastward circuit of [[Around the World in Eighty Days]]. (AWED-007-031)
