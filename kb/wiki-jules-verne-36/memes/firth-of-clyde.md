## Overview

The Firth of Clyde is a broad sea inlet on the west coast of Scotland, the waters where Lord Glenarvan's newly built yacht Duncan makes her first trials. (SC-001-003)

It is here, entering and leaving the firth, that the voyage in search of Captain Grant both begins and ends.

## In the Narrative

The Duncan, newly built, makes a trial trip a few miles outside the Firth of Clyde. (SC-001-003) On that trial within the firth she reaches seventeen miles an hour, a higher speed than any vessel had yet attained. (SC-005-003)

As the yacht sets out, a sailor informs the captain that they are about to enter the Firth of Clyde, and asks for his orders. (SC-002-054) At the journey's close, after crossing the Irish Sea, the Duncan reaches the Firth of Clyde once more on the 10th of May. (SC-066-006)

## Geography & Features

Irvine, a little seaport of Renfrew of nearly seven thousand inhabitants, lies in a sharp bend of the Scottish coast near the mouth of the Firth of Clyde. (UC-009-006)

Beneath these waters run the workings of the Aberfoyle mine: above the coal seam lies the Firth of Clyde, and beyond it extend Loch Lomond and Loch Katrine. (UC-010-028)
