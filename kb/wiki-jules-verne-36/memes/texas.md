## Overview

Texas is the great southern state of the American Union, and in [[From the Earth to the Moon]] one of the two candidates to host the Gun Club's giant cannon. When the launch site was debated, the projectors had "at our disposition all the southern part of Texas and Florida" (FEM-011-015), and it was resolved that the Columbiad "should be cast either on the soil of Texas or on that of Florida" (FEM-011-016).

## In the Narrative

The choice set Texas and Florida at bitter rivalry. Texas "came up proudly with its twenty-six counties" (FEM-011-022) and "bragged of its 33,000 inhabitants" (FEM-011-023), retorting sharply that Florida need not envy its fevers (FEM-011-024).

The Texicans pressed their pride of history, recalling the state that "drove out the Mexicans on the 2nd of March, 1836" and made itself independent before joining the Union (FEM-011-039). Yet the Gun Club chose Florida, and ever after "the rivalry was so great between Texas and Florida" that the Texican pretensions were set aside (FEM-016-019).

## Geography & Features

Texas is a settled land where, Verne observes, "towns are more numerous and more important" (FEM-011-019) than in its rival. Its Gulf coast offered a decisive argument — "the coast of Texas offers Galveston Bay, which is fourteen leagues round" (FEM-011-027), a harbour vast enough to shelter a fleet.

The state fronts the Gulf of Mexico, its shore traced by the great meridian that, "skirting Texas, off which it cuts an angle... continues its direction over Mexico" (FEM-011-017). In the sweep of the Union it stands as a southern bound, one restless traveller ranging "from Oregon to Florida, from Maine to Texas" (MW-007-010).

## Connections

Texas is the southern rival of [[Florida]] for the casting-ground of the [[Columbiad]], the great cannon of the [[Gun Club]] in [[From the Earth to the Moon]]. Its Galveston shore opens upon the [[Gulf of Mexico]], and the Columbiad's meridian runs on from it toward [[Mexico]]. Passed over in favour of the Floridan peninsula, Texas remains the Union's proud southern giant, twenty-six counties strong.
