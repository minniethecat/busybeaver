## Overview

Quebec is the old Canadian city on the St. Lawrence from which the harpooner of *[[Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea]]* springs. "The harpooner's family originated in Quebec, and they were already a line of bold fishermen back in the days when this town still belonged to France" (TTLU-004-011), a lineage that made [[Ned Land]] the master whaler he became.

The city is the fixed point of his loyalty. Recalling the great river in the Nautilus, he bursts out that "Lawrence is my own river, the river running by Quebec, my hometown" (TTLU-043-021), so that Quebec stands behind every longing he feels for dry land and open water.

## In the Narrative

Quebec is a waypoint on the flight of the [[Albatross]] in *[[Robur the Conqueror]]*. As the airship crosses Canada the aeronauts note it slipping away, one saying "that town we are leaving behind is Quebec" (RC-008-017), and another marvels "but we only left Quebec two hours ago!" (RC-008-036) at the machine's tremendous speed.

The city also anchors the sky-monster's course. When the strange aerial body reappears, it is logged "first in Canada, over the country between Ottawa and Quebec" (RC-021-017), the two Canadian cities together fixing its northern path across the dominion.

## Geography & Features

Historically Quebec is a cornerstone of the northern fur trade. Verne records how "three years after the taking of Quebec, 1776, the fur trade received a new impulse" (FC-002-020) in *[[The Fur Country]]*, dating the commerce of the wilderness from the city's change of hands.

It also serves as a marker of latitude in the far south. In reckoning the drift of the ice it is noted that a current would draw the castaways "to the height of the fiftieth parallel, which, in the southern hemisphere, is that of Paris or Quebec" (AM-010-027), the Canadian city standing as the northern twin of that antarctic line.

## Connections

Quebec is the St. Lawrence city that gave [[Ned Land]] his birth and his loyalty in *[[Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea]]*, a waypoint overflown by the [[Albatross]] in *[[Robur the Conqueror]]* beside [[Montreal]], and an early seat of the fur trade of *[[The Fur Country]]*.
