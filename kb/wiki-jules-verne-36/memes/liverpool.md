## Overview

Liverpool is the last port of Phileas Fogg's circuit and the scene of his apparent ruin. Having arrived at Liverpool at twenty minutes before twelve on the 21st of December, he had till a quarter before nine that evening to reach the Reform Club — the journey from Liverpool to London was six hours. (AWED-034-007) The margin should have been enough.

## In the Narrative

The port is the goal of the Atlantic leg. A railway train from San Francisco to New York, and a transatlantic steamer from New York to Liverpool, would bring the travellers to the end of this impossible journey. (AWED-024-010) To reach it, Fogg buys a vessel outright — "Will you carry me and three other persons to Liverpool?" (AWED-032-024)

Liverpool is also the detectives' net. When the Bank robbery breaks, picked detectives hasten off to Liverpool, Glasgow, Havre, Suez, and other ports. (AWED-003-012) At the Reform Club the wager turns on it — "What time did the last train arrive from Liverpool?" asks Thomas Flanagan. (AWED-036-009)

## Geography & Features

A great Atlantic port on England's west coast, Liverpool is the gateway between the American crossing and London — six rail hours from the Reform Club, and the place where Fix's warrant finally closes on Fogg.
