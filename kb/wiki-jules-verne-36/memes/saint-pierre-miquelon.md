## Overview

Saint-Pierre-Miquelon is the station from which the fishing schooner Viking set sail upon her last voyage. It was here that the final letter of that voyage was dated, "Saint-Pierre-Miquelon, March 17th, 1862." (TN-001-081) The Viking had left Saint-Pierre-Miquelon on the very date named in Ole Kamp's last letter. (TN-011-072)

## In the Narrative

The islands became the fixed anchor of the whole mystery that followed the ship. It was held beyond doubt that on the third of June, about a month after the departure from Saint-Pierre-Miquelon, the lottery ticket was picked up two hundred miles south-west of [[Iceland]]. (TN-013-027)

It was likewise an incontestable fact that between the sailing of the Viking from Saint-Pierre-Miquelon and the discovery of the bottle by a Danish vessel, constant gales had prevailed. (TN-013-046)

## Geography & Features

Saint-Pierre-Miquelon served as the last mooring and departure point of the Viking before she was lost, and every reckoning of her course was measured from the day she cleared the station. (TN-013-046) It was from here, too, that the young sailor's last letter had been written, before he was at length found and carried home. (TN-020-016)

## Connections
