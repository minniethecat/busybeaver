## Overview

Dublin is the Irish capital and a link in the swiftest mail route across the Atlantic. Mails landed at Queenstown are carried to Dublin by express trains always held in readiness, and from Dublin sent on to [[Liverpool]] by the most rapid boats, gaining twelve hours on the Atlantic steamers. (AWED-033-060)

## In the Narrative

Dublin is where Phileas Fogg's pursuit turns toward home. The party boarded a train at half-past one, and at dawn of day they were in Dublin, losing no time in embarking on a fast steamer that cut through the waves rather than riding over them. (AWED-033-063) The city also bred Major Hobson, an Irishman from Dublin. (FC-001-024)

## Geography & Features

Dublin's place on the map made it a natural reference for geographers. In a fanciful reckoning a new zero meridian would pass through Dublin in [[Ireland]], Paris in France, and Palermo in Sicily. (TT-015-015)

## Connections

Among the principal cities of the world Dublin stands with New York, [[London]], [[Edinburgh]], and [[Paris]]. (TT-015-024) On the mail road of [[Around the World in Eighty Days]] it is one swift stage from [[Liverpool]], the fast boats gaining twelve hours on the Atlantic steamers. (AWED-033-060)
