## Overview

The United States is the vast North American republic that stretches "from ocean to ocean," the phrase Americans apply to the great trunk railway crossing its entire width. (AWED-026-002) Phileas Fogg allots seven days to cross the United States by rail on his circuit of the globe. (AWED-005-005)

## In the Narrative

During the Federal war a new and influential club was established at Baltimore, in the United States. (FEM-001-002) Impey Barbicane became from that day one of the greatest citizens of the United States, hailed as a Washington of science. (FEM-003-013) When the Gun Club proposed its lunar shot, one man alone out of all the United States protested against it. (FEM-010-005)

## Geography & Features

The republic is reckoned ten times as large as France, and at the announcement of the moon-shot the United States of America cheered with a single voice of twenty-five million hearts. (FEM-003-009) A hunted criminal once traversed three quarters of the globe expressly to gain the United States and its American continent. (AWED-021-011)

## Connections

Its great artery is the [[Mississippi]], which the Americans would call the king of rivers. (FEM-024-018) Fogg crosses United States soil aboard the "great trunk line" that runs the country's full width. (AWED-026-002) Verne stages much of [[From the Earth to the Moon]] here, and part of [[Around the World in Eighty Days]] passes across the republic. (AWED-005-005)
