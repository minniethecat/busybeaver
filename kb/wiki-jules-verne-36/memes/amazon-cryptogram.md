# The Amazon Cryptogram

> **Note:** Inline PN citations for this page use a 4-character work code (`EHLA`); they render as plain text pending RFC-0003. The underlying PN data is valid.

## Overview

The Amazon Cryptogram is the enciphered document whose decipherment alone can prove the innocence of Joam Dacosta, and the analytical struggle to read it is the climax of *[[eight-hundred-leagues-on-the-amazon|Eight Hundred Leagues on the Amazon]]*.

Everything turns on a paper the dead adventurer Torres carried. Torres had declared that the proof of the innocence of the convict of Tijuco actually existed, and that the document had been written by the very hand of the author of the attack (EHLA-027-002).

Once the paper reaches the bench, its nature is plain. Judge Jarriquez unfolded it and turned it over to examine back and front, both covered with writing, and pronounced, "A document it really is!" (EHLA-031-057).

## What Happens

But the writing yields no sense. The judge realises at once that this document is a cryptogram (EHLA-031-061), an enciphered text that hides its meaning behind meaningless characters.

The challenge fires the magistrate's mind. At the thought that the document might contain the justification of Joam Dacosta, his instinct as an analyst was aroused, for here before his eyes was a cryptogram (EHLA-032-022).

He attacks it methodically. Reasoning that the gist lay in its final lines, he was right to bring all his resources to bear in the first place on the last paragraph of the cryptogram (EHLA-032-027).

Yet the text resists, an unbroken block without spaces. He rails that the writer did not separate the words, and tries forcing likely terms--"diamond," "Tijuco," "Dacosta"--against the enciphered mass (EHLA-034-012).

## Significance

The stakes could not be higher. As Manoel protests, an innocent man has been branded as guilty and is to lose his life and his honour while the magistrate holds in his hands the material proof of his innocence (EHLA-033-078).

When the truth is at last wrung from the cipher, the reversal is total: a complete change takes place in public opinion, and to anger succeeds pity for Joam Dacosta (EHLA-034-001). The cryptogram thus stands as the pivot on which a condemned man's life is saved.

## Participants & Setting

- The enciphered confession that proves the innocence of Joam Dacosta, deciphered by Judge Jarriquez at Manaos.
- Turning on the document carried by the adventurer Torres and defended by Dacosta's son Benito and Manoel Valdez.
- Chronicled in *[[eight-hundred-leagues-on-the-amazon|Eight Hundred Leagues on the Amazon]]*.
