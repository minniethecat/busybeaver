## Overview

*Five Weeks in a Balloon* was Jules Verne's first novel and the founding work of his *Voyages Extraordinaires*. It opens on "the 14th of January, 1862, at the session of the Royal Geographical Society... London," where the explorer Dr. Samuel Ferguson announces his design to cross Africa by air (FWB-001-002).

His scheme, the Society hears, will "complete and link together the notions, as yet disjointed, which the world entertains of Africa" (FWB-001-005).

## Plot Summary

Ferguson proposes to traverse the unmapped continent by balloon, "from east to west," settling the geography of its great lakes (FWB-003-071). He is joined by his headstrong Scottish friend, the hunter Dick Kennedy, and by his devoted servant Joe, "the right hand of the expedition" (FWB-003-004, FWB-006-006).

The doctor's great invention is a balloon that can "rise and descend at will, without losing ballast or gas," managed by a heating cylinder that expands or cools the gas (FWB-010-002, FWB-012-008). Christened the *Victoria*, it lifts off from Zanzibar (FWB-011-039, FWB-011-001).

Over five weeks the three drift westward across Africa, surviving storms, hostile tribes, thirst, and a dwindling gas supply, until the *Victoria* carries them at last toward the continent's western coast.

## Main Characters

- Dr. Samuel Ferguson — the intrepid explorer who conceives the aerial crossing
- Dick Kennedy — his skeptical Scottish friend, a keen sportsman
- Joe — Ferguson's nimble, faithful servant

## Key Places & Technology

- The *Victoria* — [[victoria-balloon|Ferguson's balloon]], able to rise and fall without spending gas or ballast
- Zanzibar — the island starting-point of the trans-African voyage

## Themes

Verne's debut fuses breathless adventure with a scrupulous digest of real African exploration, turning the balloon into an instrument of pure discovery. It sets Ferguson's serene faith in science against Kennedy's doubt and Joe's cheerful daring, sketching the trio of temperaments that would recur throughout the author's work.
