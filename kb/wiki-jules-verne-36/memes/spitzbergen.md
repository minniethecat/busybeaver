## Overview

Spitzbergen is the high-Arctic archipelago that serves as a northern threshold in Verne's polar tales. When the destination of the Forward is guessed at in *[[The Adventures of Captain Hatteras]]*, the very first surmise pairs it with its neighbour: "To Spitzbergen or Greenland?" (ACH-004-016), marking it as one of the two great gateways to the ice.

It stands near the limit of the charted world. Verne places it in the roll of northern discoveries, recording "Spitzbergen, by Willoughby, in 1596" (ACH-012-054) among the feats of the early navigators who pushed toward the pole.

## In the Narrative

Spitzbergen marks the far edge reached by earlier expeditions. The narrative recalls how "in 1817 the Neptune got up to the north of Spitzbergen, as far as the eighty-second degree" (ACH-013-017), a record of northing against which Hatteras measures his own ambition, and it laments "the cruise of the Farewell... that got lost in the Spitzbergen seas!" (ACH-023-018).

The archipelago also anchors later mysteries. In *[[Robur the Conqueror]]* strange lights are logged when "the observatory of Kautokeino at Finmark, in Norway, and... that of Isfjord at Spitzbergen" (RC-001-028) report the same phenomenon, and "in Europe not a doubt was thrown on this observation of the stations in Finmark and Spitzbergen" (RC-001-029).

## Geography & Features

Spitzbergen lies far out in the northern ocean, separated from Greenland by open sea. It is noted that across "the extent of five hundred leagues, which separates Greenland from Spitzbergen, no land has been found" (WAI-008-004), a measure of the empty Arctic waste around it.

It sits at the top of the great ocean currents and voyages. The Gulf Stream, after crossing the Atlantic, "heads toward Spitzbergen" (TTLU-035-003), and the Nautilus's own wanderings are wondered at in the same breath: "did it call at the capes of Spitzbergen or the shores of Novaya Zemlya?" (TTLU-046-009). Even from the crater's mouth in *A Journey to the Center of the Earth* the far isle is invoked, "the Esk in Jan Mayen Island, not far from Spitzbergen!" (JCE-043-026).

## Connections

Spitzbergen is a northern gateway of the polar voyages, paired with [[Greenland]] as a threshold to the ice in *[[The Adventures of Captain Hatteras]]* and marking the far reach of the [[Gulf Stream]]. Its observatory at Isfjord logs the mystery that opens *[[Robur the Conqueror]]*, and it lies on the northern edge of the seas above [[Norway]] and [[Iceland]].
