# The Destruction of the Terror

## Overview

The Destruction of the Terror is the storm-death of [[Robur]]'s all-conquering machine [[the-terror|the Terror]], the catastrophe that ends the inventor's defiant career over the [[gulf-of-mexico|Gulf of Mexico]].

A tempest gathers as the machine hovers above the sea. A storm was approaching from the northwest, and vivid lightning darted amid the dense, black clouds (MW-017-054).

Robur meets it with contempt. With an eye more burning than ever, impassive before this frightful storm, the captain looked it full in the face, as if to defy it, knowing that he had nothing to fear (MW-017-057).

## What Happens

Instead of fleeing, he drives straight into the heart of it. With one hand on the helm, the other on the speed regulators while the great wings beat furiously, he headed his machine toward the very center of the storm, where the electric flashes were leaping from cloud to cloud (MW-017-064).

The Terror climbs into the lightning. The air-ship soared upward into the heart of the sky, amid a thousand lightning flashes, surrounded and shaken by the bursts of thunder (MW-017-063).

Then the bolt finds her. Struck by the lightning in the very middle of her powerful batteries, the air-ship spread out on all sides and went to pieces (MW-017-068).

The ruin plunges into the waves. With her wings fallen, her screws broken, and bolt after bolt of the lightning darting amid her ruins, the Terror fell from the height of more than a thousand feet into the ocean beneath (MW-017-069).

## Significance

The machine's maker does not survive her. Robur the Conqueror and his two companions had ended their adventurous careers in the waters of the Gulf (MW-018-004), while the captive narrator alone was plucked from the sea, his helpless body entangled in the wreckage.

Only that survivor lives to tell it. Rescued and restored, he recounted the pursuit of the destroyers on the lake, the soaring of the Terror from amid Niagara Falls, the halt within the Great Eyrie, and the catastrophe during the storm (MW-018-008). With the Terror lost, the secret of the world's most powerful machine perishes with its inventor.

## Participants & Setting

- The destruction by lightning of the flying machine the Terror, and the death of [[Robur]] and his two companions, over the Gulf of Mexico.
- Witnessed by the sole survivor, the police inspector Strock, who alone is rescued from the wreck.
- Chronicled in *[[the-master-of-the-world|Master of the World]]*.
