# Cape Bathurst

## Overview

Cape Bathurst is the Arctic promontory on the North American coast that Lieutenant Jasper Hobson chooses as the site of a new Hudson's Bay Company factory in *The Fur Country*. The coast juts north-westward from the gulf, ending in Cape Bathurst, which lies above the seventieth parallel. (FC-010-008) It becomes the setting for the novel's central catastrophe.

## In the Narrative

The Hudson's Bay expedition set out to take the shortest route to Cape Bathurst on the North American coast, seeking new fur-trapping grounds beyond the seventieth degree of latitude. (FC-005-008) On the 5th June, about three o'clock in the afternoon, the party at last halted at the extremity of Cape Bathurst. (FC-012-021)

Hobson judged the extremity of the cape a most favourable site for the fort, with a lagoon behind them and the sea open for four or five months of the warm season, giving access to the highway of Behring Strait for provisions and trade. (FC-012-020) There he founded the factory he named Fort Hope.

The choice proved disastrous. Fort Hope had unknowingly been built upon ice, not solid land. (FC-024-003) The earthquake of the 8th January broke the connecting-link between cape and continent, and the whole peninsula was set adrift as [[Victoria Island]]. (FC-026-061)

## Geography & Features

Cape Bathurst forms the north-western termination of a gulf bounded on the north by Cape Krusenstern, rising above the seventieth parallel into the Arctic Ocean. (FC-010-008) Behind the chosen fort-site lay a lagoon whose water proved unexpectedly sweet rather than brackish, and before it the sea opened seasonally toward Behring Strait. (FC-012-020)

What the settlers took for a rocky headland was in fact a mass of frozen ground: the floe forming its foundation had been built up in the calm waters along the North American shore, disguised beneath soil and vegetation. (FC-027-017) Its hidden nature made it a promontory that could, and did, float away.
