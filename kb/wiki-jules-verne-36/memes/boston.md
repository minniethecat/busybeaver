## Overview

Boston is the great New England seaport that recurs as an American setting across several of Verne's tales. In *Master of the World* it is one of the coastal cities thrown into alarm by an unidentified craft prowling offshore, and its harbour launches pursuit against the "moving body" (MW-005-030) that no one can catch.

The city stands throughout as a shorthand for Yankee enterprise and reform. Its citizens appear as missionaries, journalists, and merchants of the northern states, and it is counted among "the large towns of the Union, New York, Boston, Albany, Washington, Richmond" (FEM-003-009) that share in the national excitement.

## In the Narrative

In *Master of the World* Boston is a nerve-centre of the hunt for the mysterious machine. Reports reach the narrator "from Boston, on a subject of which the evening papers had just begun to apprise their readers" (MW-005-028), and its harbour is one of many from which pursuers set out: "from Providence, from Boston, from Portsmouth, and from Portland motor boats and steam launches had repeatedly attempted to approach this moving body" (MW-005-030).

The city even sends warships. "One day, even, a fast United States gun boat went out from Boston, if not to pursue the monster, at least to send after it a few cannon shot" (MW-005-033), and the narrator ranks it among the great enigmas, musing that "after the mystery of Great Eyrie, comes that of Milwaukee and Boston" (MW-006-008).

## Geography & Features

Boston is a northern port bound up with the politics of the Union. In *The Blockade Runners* it is the home of the fervent abolitionist Halliburtt, described as "a journalist from Boston, a violent Abolitionist with the confounded spirit of Lincoln" (BR-007-022), and the marriage that crowns that tale unites "James Playfair, of the firm of Vincent Playfair & Co., of Glasgow, with Miss Jenny Halliburtt, of Boston" (BR-010-006).

The city's reach extends across the world in the persons of its wandering citizens. In *The Adventures of a Special Correspondent* one meets "the Reverend Nathaniel Morse, of Boston, one of those honest Bible distributors, a Yankee missionary, in the garb of a merchant" (ASC-016-035), a Bostonian carrying New England's zeal into far Asia.

## Connections

Boston is a New England seaport in *Master of the World*, ranked beside [[The Great Eyrie]] among the story's mysteries, and home to characters reaching from *The Blockade Runners* to distant Asia. It stands with [[New York]] among "the large towns of the Union" (FEM-003-009) caught up in the excitement of the great American enterprises.
