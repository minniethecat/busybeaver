# Washburn Bay

## Overview

Washburn Bay is a deep inlet of the northern Canadian coast, one of the landmarks charted by Lieutenant Jaspar Hobson's fur-trade survey in *The Fur Country*. The expedition first rounds it early in its march: "On the 4th July the travellers skirted round another deep bay called Washburn Bay, and reached the furthest point of a little lake." (FC-012-019)

## In the Narrative

The bay becomes a stage on Hobson's later coastal exploration. He plans his halt there — "Hobson intended to encamp for the night at Washburn Bay" (FC-027-006) — reaching it after an uneventful march: "No fresh incident marked the exploration of the short distance between Walruses' Bay and Washburn Bay." (FC-027-007)

From its shore the party takes stock of the land: "A halt was made at Washburn Bay, and the explorers were able to enjoy an extended view on the south." (FC-027-008)

## Geography & Features

The bay marks a limit of the drifting island's ruined coast. Searching for lost companions, Kalumah "explored the coast as far as the further side of Washburn Bay, but without finding any sign of those she sought." (FC-032-022) It lies along the same stretch as [[Cape Bathurst]], on the shifting margin of the north.
