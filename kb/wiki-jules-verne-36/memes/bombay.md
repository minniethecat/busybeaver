## Overview

Bombay is the Indian port at which Phileas Fogg first sets foot on the subcontinent, the gateway between his sea passage from Suez and the railway east to Calcutta. Like Calcutta and Singapore, it betrayed everywhere the evidence of English supremacy. (AWED-019-003)

## In the Narrative

Fogg's itinerary allots thirteen days from Suez to Bombay by steamer, then three from Bombay to Calcutta by rail. (AWED-003-029) He crosses the Arabian Sea aboard the Mongolia, which plied regularly between Brindisi and Bombay via the Suez Canal. (AWED-006-003) At Suez, Passepartout confirms the route: "And does she go from Suez directly to Bombay?" (AWED-006-022)

Bombay is also where the law reaches for Fogg. Detective Fix wires London's warrant of arrest to the city. (AWED-005-013) The document that will dog the travellers had followed him from Bombay, and had come by the Carnatic. (AWED-024-017)

## Geography & Features

A principal steamer terminus of British India, Bombay opens onto the Arabian Sea and anchors the western end of the Great Indian Peninsula Railway that Fogg rides toward Allahabad and Calcutta.
