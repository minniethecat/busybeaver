Sophie Lengholm's confession is the emotional culmination of her character arc. In the aftermath of the rescue operation at Romney Marsh, she finally reveals to Detective Inspector Labar everything she knows about the conspiracy.

## The Confession

Limp, hysterical, and half-paralysed by her own emotions, Mrs. Gertstein takes the place of Sophie Lengholm in the interview room [029-001]. The confession does not last long, for Sophie literally flings herself before the detective, desperate to unburden herself of the secrets she has carried [029-001]. The weight of her complicity — knowing more than she had revealed during her captivity — has become unbearable.

## What She Reveals

Sophie's confession fills critical gaps in Labar's understanding of the conspiracy. She provides details that corroborate the evidence gathered from the banknote trace, the forged cheque, and the testimony of other witnesses. Her firsthand knowledge of the gang's operations during her captivity proves invaluable to the prosecution.

## Impact

The confession transforms Sophie from a marginal figure — a woman caught between fear and conscience — into a key witness for the Crown. Her testimony will form part of the case presented at the Old Bailey trial [030-001], helping to secure the convictions that bring down Larry Hughes's criminal enterprise.
