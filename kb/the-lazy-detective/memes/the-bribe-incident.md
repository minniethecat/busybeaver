# The Bribe Incident

The novel opens with a moment of startling abruptness: a car passing Detective Inspector Labar on the street, a blank envelope tossed from its window, and inside — a hundred-pound Bank of England note. It is a bribe, and an amateurish one:

> It was a bribe of course. But the touch of melodrama was amateurish and clumsy. The most illiterate crook in London should have known that a hundred pound note was ridiculously easy to trace. [[001-026]]

## Significance

Labar immediately recognizes the bribe's significance. The amateurish execution tells him that the briber is not a professional criminal but someone desperate — someone who does not understand how traceable money is. The woman in the car is Penelope Noelson ([[penelope-noelson]]), and the bribe becomes the thread that leads Labar into the entire conspiracy.
