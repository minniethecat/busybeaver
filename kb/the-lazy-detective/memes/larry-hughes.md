# Larry Hughes

Larry Hughes is the antagonist of *The Lazy Detective* — a criminal of unusual sophistication who operates not through violence but through knowledge, leverage, and charm. He is responsible for the Streetly House jewel theft and uses blackmail ([[blackmail]]) to maintain control over those who might expose him.

## Character

Hughes is not a crude criminal but a figure of unsettling elegance. He moves easily between social worlds, cultivated enough to charm Gertstein's circle and ruthless enough to destroy anyone who threatens his position. His power derives from what he knows about others — and his willingness to use that knowledge.

## Role in the Novel

### The Jewel Theft

Hughes is the architect of the Streetly House theft. The scale and precision of the operation — a quarter-million pounds of jewels and objets d'art — reflect his organizational skill:

> The men who could plan or carry out such an undertaking were few. [[002-016]]

### The Blackmailer

Beyond the theft, Hughes is a blackmailer. He has extracted payment from Mrs. Gertstein to keep silent about her forgery ([[forgery]]). Penelope Noelson ([[penelope-noelson]]) confronts him directly:

> "You are the blackmailer. You are the man she has been buying silence from. You are the man who wrecked her life." [[007-039]]

### The Confrontation

Hughes and Labar engage in a battle of wits across the novel. Hughes believes himself untouchable — his crimes are perfectly planned, his leverage over others is absolute. Labar's challenge is to find the flaw in the perfect criminal's design.
