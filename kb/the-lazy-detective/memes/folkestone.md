# Folkestone

Folkestone is a coastal town in Kent whose principal significance in the novel lies in the "Folkestone—Rye road"（[[021-023]]）, a transport route used by the criminal gang to move between the coast and their [[romney-marshes|Romney Marshes]] hideout. The road is first mentioned in the intelligence gathered by the constabulary: five cars matching the gang's description had been seen on it, and a shepherd on the marshes reported a large vehicle — "which the police officer believed might have been a Rolls Royce"（[[021-023]]）— using the route. The road thus becomes one of the early leads that draws [[detective-inspector-labar|Labar]] to the area.

## In the Novel

### The Road as Evidence

The Folkestone—Rye road functions in the narrative as a thread of physical evidence. In the open, sparsely populated marshland, a car travelling a specific route multiple times leaves a trail of sightings — constables, shepherds, the occasional motorist. Labar's method is to gather these fragments and triangulate: the frequency of sightings on this particular road suggests a pattern, and the pattern points to a destination.

### Local Assumptions

A local officer, seeing Labar's rough travelling clothes and his stated intention of walking across the marshes, assumes his destination: "'Reckon you'll be making for Folkestone. 'Tis a tidy walk'"（[[021-036]]）. The assumption is revealing: in a region where the settlements are sparse and the distances between them significant, a stranger on foot is naturally assumed to be heading towards the nearest sizeable town. Labar's cover identity — a naturalist walking between towns — is plausible precisely because the geography makes it the only reasonable explanation for his presence.
