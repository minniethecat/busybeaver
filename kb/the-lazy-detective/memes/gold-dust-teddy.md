# Gold Dust Teddy

Gold Dust Teddy is a professional burglar whose rare skill set makes him a person
of interest in the [[streetly-house-theft|Streetly House jewel robbery]]. A
middle-aged career criminal with the appearance of a respectable citizen,
Teddy operates in the shadow economy of [[larry-hughes|Larry Hughes]]'s criminal
network. [[detective-inspector-labar|Detective Inspector Labar]] identifies him
early as a potential lever into the larger conspiracy and deploys a subtle
strategy of pressure and surveillance rather than immediate arrest.

## Criminal Profile

Teddy belongs to an elite class of criminal: the master burglar. According
to police intelligence, he was one of the few men in London capable of
executing a heist on the scale of the Streetly House robbery:

> It told of one, Gold Dust Teddy, who had left his little suburban house on the Monday, and had been absent all night. Teddy was one of the few men who had the craftsmanship to execute a great burglary. [006-007]

Unlike the street-level thugs in Hughes's employ, Teddy possessed genuine
technical skill. His tools and methods were those of a specialist, and his
criminal record reflected a career built on expertise rather than brute force.

## Appearance and Demeanour

In a genre where criminals are often grotesques, Teddy is strikingly ordinary.
His respectability is a professional asset — it allows him to move through
society without arousing suspicion:

> Teddy did not look a Bill Sikes. He would have passed any normal scrutiny as a respectable middle-class citizen. He wore a collar and tie, and there were distinct traces of a crease in his trousers. His cleanshaven face was hard, but not in the least forbidding, except that the puffy eyes betrayed something of sottishness. [008-015]

## Interrogation by Labar

Labar's interrogation of Teddy is a masterclass in psychological pressure.
Rather than charging him outright, Labar employs a calibrated mix of threat
and conciliation, aiming to crack Teddy's loyalty to Hughes:

> "Bluffing, am I? Don't you believe it, son. I know all about Larry and the others. You think that Larry will help you out of this mess. He won't. He's on his way out of London, and he's leaving you and the others to hold the baby." [008-027]

Labar's strategy is to sow doubt — to make Teddy question whether Hughes will
protect him or sacrifice him. The inspector's approach reflects the practical
limits of British policing (the "Third Degree" being prohibited) and the
necessity of psychological manipulation in its place.

> He was determined to break Teddy down, and since the Third Degree is frowned upon by British law, there was only this way open to him. [008-028]

## The Bait Strategy

Rather than charging Teddy immediately, Labar releases him — but not out of
generosity. The detective's plan is to use Teddy as unwitting bait, following
his movements in the hope that he will lead the police to the higher echelons
of the conspiracy:

> "Don't you worry about Teddy. We'll get our hooks on him when we want. I'm using him as bait. What I want you to do is to watch when the big fish bites." [008-042]

Two ambitious young detectives, Down and Heath, are assigned to shadow Teddy's
movements. The surveillance continues for days, with Teddy maintaining a
"normal, half-drunken existence" while the police wait for him to make contact
with the gang's leadership [009-006].

## Role in the Wider Conspiracy

Teddy's exact role in the Streetly House robbery remains ambiguous throughout
much of the narrative. Labar believes him to be one of Larry Hughes's "tools"
[006-010], and his name recurs in the investigation alongside that of
[[billy-bungey|Billy Bungey]] and [[mrs-gertstein|Mrs. Gertstein]]:

> "Stebbins may help us to get at Billy Bungey. There is Mrs. Gertstein. There is Gold Dust Teddy. So far we're to the good." [021-004]

## Arrest and Fate

When the net finally closes on the Hughes gang, Teddy is among those marked
for arrest. His capture is entrusted to Detective Sergeant Down, the same
officer who had shadowed him throughout the investigation:

> Among those who were to be arrested and definitely charged was Gold Dust Teddy. Detective Sergeant Down to whom was entrusted the execution of this mission, received his orders with satisfaction. The absence of Teddy was likely to make a difference in the statistics of crime. [030-034]
