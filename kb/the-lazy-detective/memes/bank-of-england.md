The Bank of England is the central bank of the United Kingdom. It is a financial institution that issues currency and maintains records of banknote serial numbers, enabling law enforcement to trace the movement of money.

## In the Novel

The Bank of England appears as a key source of financial intelligence in the investigation. When Detective Inspector Labar receives a hundred-pound note in a blank envelope [001-024], he traces its origin through official channels. A subordinate reports having made inquiries at the Bank of England, which reveals that note No. K002947 was part of a series issued to the Midland Bank a week prior [004-016]. The ten consecutively numbered notes — K002946 to K002955 — were paid to honour a cheque drawn by Mr. S. Gertstein of Streetly House [004-016].

Labar anticipates that the remaining notes from the cashier's cheque would eventually return to the Bank of England through normal circulation, potentially allowing investigators to trace them back through every hand they had passed through — though he acknowledges this process would likely take weeks [012-004].
