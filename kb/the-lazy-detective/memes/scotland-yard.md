# Scotland Yard

Scotland Yard, the headquarters of London's Metropolitan Police, is the institutional anchor of *The Lazy Detective*. It is the place where Labar's career hangs in the balance, where his superiors judge his performance, and where the bureaucratic machinery of criminal justice operates — sometimes smoothly, sometimes not.

## In the Novel

### The Yard's Judgment

The novel opens with Labar being called to account at the Yard. Chief Constable Winter ([[chief-constable-winter]]) does not mince words about Labar's performance:

> "You're lazy — damned lazy. If things don't clear up in your division in the next month or so you can count on something happening." [[001-001]]

The Yard is not merely a building but a system of judgment — a place where careers are made and broken, and where Labar must prove his worth.

### The Bureaucratic Machine

Throughout the investigation, the Yard functions as a resource and a constraint. Labar can call on its fingerprint records, its criminal databases, its network of informants — but he must also navigate its politics and satisfy its expectations. The Yard is both tool and taskmaster.
