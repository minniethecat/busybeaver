# Public Prosecutor

The Public Prosecutor represents the institutional bridge between police
investigation and criminal trial in [[the-lazy-detective|The Lazy Detective]].
While the office does not appear as a dramatic presence in the narrative,
its institutional weight is felt throughout: every piece of evidence gathered
by [[detective-inspector-labar|Detective Inspector Labar]] is collected with
the knowledge that it must ultimately satisfy the prosecutor's standards for
bringing charges, and every suspect's fate turns on the decision to prosecute.

## Institutional Role

The Public Prosecutor's function in the novel's legal world is threefold:

1. **Charging decision**: Determining whether the evidence gathered by police is sufficient to bring formal charges
2. **Case preparation**: Organising the evidentiary record for presentation at trial
3. **Crown representation**: Appearing on behalf of the state in criminal proceedings

## Relationship to Police Investigation

Labar's investigation operates with the prosecutor's requirements as an
implicit constraint. Evidence must be gathered in legally admissible form;
confessions must be voluntary; witness testimony must be credible and
corroborated. The prohibition on the "Third Degree" in British policing
[008-028] is not merely a matter of police regulation but a legal
requirement — evidence obtained by coercion would be rejected by
the prosecutor and ruled inadmissible at trial.

## The Trial Context

The novel's climax involves the trial of those arrested in connection with
the [[streetly-house-theft|Streetly House robbery]]. The trial at the
[[old-bailey|Old Bailey]] represents the culmination of the investigative
process, where the evidence assembled by Labar is tested in open court.
The [[treasury-counsel|Treasury Counsel]] appears as the Crown's advocate,
embodying the prosecutorial function in the courtroom:

> With the shrewd suavity of an Old Bailey lawyer he examined and cross-examined. [003-002]
