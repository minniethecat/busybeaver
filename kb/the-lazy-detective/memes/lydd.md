# Lydd

Lydd is a small town on the Kent coast, situated a few miles from the [[romney-marshes|Romney Marshes]], that serves as the primary logistical base for the large-scale police operation against the criminal gang. Unlike [[rye|Rye]], which [[detective-inspector-labar|Labar]] uses as a discreet personal base, Lydd functions as the overt police staging ground where officers assemble, prisoners are held, and the administrative apparatus of the manhunt is coordinated.

## In the Novel

### The Staging Ground

Labar identifies a point "some few miles from Lydd"（[[021-030]]） as the location of a weather-beaten inn — the nearest habitation to the gang's marshland hideout. The town's position on the landward edge of the marsh makes it the natural marshalling point. When the time comes for a coordinated sweep, "a body of police were to start from Lydd to patrol the shore as far as Dungeness"（[[024-024]]）, creating a cordon that would cut off any escape by sea.

Labar mobilises quickly from Lydd: "Once he had put things in train he hired a car, and with Malone and a couple of local officers he started for the marsh"（[[024-024]]）. The town provides the infrastructure — vehicles, local constables, communications — that transforms intelligence into action.

### The Prisoner Hub

Following the arrests, Lydd becomes the central holding location. Labar arranges for "motor cars... from various points by which the prisoners already gathered in might be escorted to Lydd, where they were to remain for the time guarded by a strong force of police"（[[028-022]]）. The emphasis on a "strong force of police" reflects the scale of the operation and the number of individuals taken into custody. "The two heads of the C.I.D. accompanied Labar to Lydd where a list of the prisoners by name had been made out"（[[028-029]]）, underscoring the town's role as the administrative centre for processing the arrested gang members.

### Coordination Hub

During the operation, Labar uses Lydd as a communications relay. When checking on the progress of the search cordon, he asks: "'Do you know whether the men from Lydd have got to this part of the coast yet?'"（[[027-014]]）. The question reveals the operation's dependency on Lydd as the origin point for the police line advancing across the marshland — a chain of constables spaced across miles of difficult terrain, their progress tracked and coordinated from the town.
