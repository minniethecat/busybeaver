# Streetly House

Streetly House is the residence of financier Solly Gertstein ([[solly-gertstein]]) and the scene of the central crime in *The Lazy Detective*. Located just off Park Lane in one of London's most exclusive districts, it is described as "that imposing and historic residence" — a building whose very location announces the wealth and status of its owner.

## The Theft

The crime that opens the investigation is a clean sweep of Streetly House:

> "They've made a clean sweep of Streetly House. Old Gertstein's foaming at the mouth. Quarter of a million of pounds worth of jewels and curios melted away as clean as a conjuring trick." [[001-030]]

The scale is staggering, and the precision of the operation suggests professional criminals ([[larry-hughes|Larry Hughes]]) rather than opportunistic thieves.

## As a Crime Scene

Bill Malone's ([[bill-malone]]) examination reveals the absence of forensic evidence — "not so much as a blighting fingerprint" [[002-015]] — which is itself the most significant finding. The professional criminals have left nothing behind. Streetly House becomes less a scene to be examined than a puzzle to be solved: how could so much be taken while leaving so little?
