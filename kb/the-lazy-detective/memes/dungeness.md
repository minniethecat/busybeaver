# Dungeness

Dungeness is a shingle headland on the Kent coast that marks the seaward limit of the police cordon during the manhunt for the criminal gang. Extending into the English Channel south of the [[romney-marshes|Romney Marshes]], the headland forms a natural boundary for the search operation: a body of police starting from [[lydd|Lydd]] patrols "the shore as far as Dungeness"（[[024-024]]）, creating a line that prevents escape by sea. The location's exposed, barren character — a vast expanse of shingle with sparse vegetation — makes concealment difficult and renders it a logical terminus for a containment sweep.

## In the Novel

### The Coastal Cordon

The police strategy for capturing the gang relies on cutting off all avenues of escape. While the marshland interior is searched on foot, the coastline must be sealed to prevent a seaward exit. Dungeness serves as the cordon's endpoint: officers patrol from Lydd eastward along the shore to the headland, ensuring that no boat can land or depart undetected.

[[detective-inspector-labar|Labar]]'s concern about maritime escape is well-founded. A suspicious yacht "had been picked up by H.M. destroyer *Hawk* off Dungeness"（[[028-004]]）, confirming that the gang had arranged — or attempted to arrange — a seaborne extraction. The headland, with its proximity to the Channel shipping lanes, represents both a vulnerability in the police perimeter and a potential escape route for the quarry.

### The Naturalist's Route

Earlier in the investigation, Labar uses Dungeness as a pretext for his movements. Posing as a naturalist, he tells a local police officer: "'I thought of walking across towards Dungeness'"（[[021-039]]）. The headland's reputation as a site of ecological interest — its unique shingle flora and bird life — provides cover for a detective who needs a reason to traverse open ground where every stranger is noted. Labar waves aside warnings about the danger of the marsh and "since one road was like another for his purpose set off across the nearest marsh track in the general direction of Dungeness"（[[021-048]]）.

### The Elusive Boat

From Dungeness, Labar scans the coastline: "His gaze swung to the beach. Far as he could see that was open. There was no sign of the motor boat"（[[025-045]]）. The motor boat represents the gang's escape plan — a vessel that could spirit [[larry-hughes|Larry Hughes]] away from the marsh and across the Channel before the police cordon could close. That the boat is "labouring on the other side of Dungeness with engine trouble"（[[025-045]]） is a stroke of fortune that ultimately aids the police, though Labar cannot know this at the time.
