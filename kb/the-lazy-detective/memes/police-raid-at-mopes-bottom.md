# Police Raid at Mope's Bottom

The police raid on [[mopes-bottom|Mope's Bottom]] is the climactic
enforcement operation of [[the-lazy-detective|The Lazy Detective]]. It
represents the culmination of [[detective-inspector-labar|Detective Inspector Labar]]'s
investigation into the [[streetly-house-theft|Streetly House jewel robbery]]
and the moment at which the [[larry-hughes|Larry Hughes]] criminal network
is decisively broken. The operation brings together multiple police
jurisdictions and specialist units in a coordinated assault on the gang's
marshland refuge.

## Prelude

Labar locates Mope's Bottom through a combination of investigative deduction
and local knowledge. The approach is complicated by the difficult marsh
terrain and the need for surprise. A local constable familiar with the
district is recruited as guide, along with a farmer and shepherd who
volunteer their knowledge of the area [024-025].

The operation's planning reflects the logistical challenge of mounting
a raid on a remote location. The [[flying-squad|Flying Squad]] is mobilised,
uniformed constables from the Kent County Constabulary are coordinated,
and the [[criminal-investigation-department|C.I.D.]] provides investigative
leadership.

## The Ambush

Labar reaches the house first — and walks into an ambush. Hughes and
[[billy-bungey|Billy Bungey]] emerge from a secret panel, pistols drawn,
and the detective is taken prisoner [024-046]. During his captivity,
Bungey presses for Labar's execution [025-035], while Hughes appears
more circumspect.

## The Escape and Counter-Attack

Labar frees himself from his bonds while his captors are distracted [026-021].
In a dramatic reversal, he turns on the remaining gang members and escorts
them back to Mope's Bottom as prisoners:

> "I'm going to take you people back to Mope's Bottom," he said. "You will keep well in front of me and if anyone looks back he will be turned into something deader than a pillar of salt. Now march." [026-036]

## The Tunnel Pursuit

Meanwhile, Hughes and Bungey have fled through the secret tunnel. The
discovery of this escape route triggers a secondary operation:

> As Labar and Moreland pulled themselves out of the tunnel they were confronted by a bunch of plainclothes men and uniformed police with three dejected prisoners in their midst. [027-058]

A hurried council of war is held [027-076], and the search of the marsh
begins. Most of the gang is in custody, but Hughes and Bungey remain at
large.

## Outcome

The raid succeeds in its primary objective: the dismantling of the gang's
infrastructure and the capture of its key members. However, the escape
of Hughes represents an incomplete victory:

| Outcome | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Gang members captured | All except Hughes and Bungey |
| Billy Bungey | Killed by Kent constable [029-013] |
| Larry Hughes | Escaped, later recaptured [030-005] |
| Evidence recovered | Systematic search of tunnel and premises [028-004] |
