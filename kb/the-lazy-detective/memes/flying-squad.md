# Flying Squad

The Flying Squad is a specialist unit within [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]]'s
[[criminal-investigation-department|Criminal Investigation Department]],
tasked with rapid response to serious crime and the pursuit of professional
criminals. In [[the-lazy-detective|The Lazy Detective]], the Squad forms
part of the broader institutional framework within which
[[detective-inspector-labar|Detective Inspector Labar]] operates — a resource
for mobile operations and armed interventions beyond the capacity of divisional
detectives.

## Role in the Novel

The Flying Squad appears in the narrative primarily as background
infrastructure — the operational arm of the Yard that can be mobilised when
an investigation escalates from intelligence-gathering to enforcement.
Its mention signals the seriousness with which the authorities regard
[[larry-hughes|Larry Hughes]]'s criminal network:

> Labar's original plan had been to get in touch with the nearest considerable town where there was any reasonable reserve of police, and have assistance sent out, while he would have also asked for steps to be taken to notify all the police forces within a big area to keep a lookout for Larry. [015-037]

The coordination between the Flying Squad, local constabularies, and the
C.I.D. reflects the multi-layered nature of British policing — a system
in which specialist units supplement divisional resources for major operations.

## Historical Context

The Flying Squad was established at Scotland Yard in 1919 as a mobile
detective unit equipped with motor vehicles — a response to the increasing
mobility of professional criminals in the post-World War I era. By the time
of the novel's events, the Squad had become a recognised component of
Metropolitan Police operations, particularly in cases involving organised
theft, robbery, and the activities of career criminals like the Hughes gang.
