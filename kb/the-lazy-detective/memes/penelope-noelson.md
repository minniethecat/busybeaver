# Penelope Noelson

Penelope Noelson is the enigmatic figure at the heart of *The Lazy Detective* — the woman who tosses a hundred-pound bribe from a passing car and becomes, over the course of the investigation, the key to unraveling the entire conspiracy. She is simultaneously suspect ([[suspect]]), witness ([[witness]]), and victim — a figure whose loyalties and motives Labar must painstakingly decode.

## Character

Penelope is introduced through action rather than description. Labar's first glimpse of her is a momentary flash from a moving car, but he is certain of what he saw:

> Momentary though his glimpse of the girl in the car had been, he had no doubt that it was Penelope Noelson. He did not make that kind of mistake. [[004-004]]

She is resourceful and desperate in equal measure. Her attempts to bribe Labar, to destroy evidence, and to protect herself reveal a woman pushed to extremes by circumstances she cannot control.

## Role in the Novel

### The Bribe

Penelope's first act is the bribe — the hundred-pound note that sets the investigation in motion. It is amateurish, as Labar immediately recognizes, and this amateurishness tells him something important: she is not a professional criminal but someone who has been drawn into deeper waters than she can navigate.

### Desperation and Resistance

Labar confronts her with a litany of her attempts:

> "She only tried to bribe me yesterday. She only changed a forged cheque on the Midland Bank. She only tried to sandbag me last night." [[005-034]]

Each attempt is more desperate than the last, and each fails. Yet Penelope persists — driven by loyalties and fears that Labar only gradually understands.

### The Witness

Ultimately, Penelope's greatest value is as a witness. She knows who planned the theft, who drove the forgery, and who has been extracting blackmail payments. The question is whether Labar can persuade her to testify before she is silenced.
