The pursuit and ambush at Romney Marsh is one of the most dramatic sequences in the narrative. Detective Inspector Labar tracks Larry Hughes into the desolate Romney Marshes, only to be ambushed and taken prisoner by Hughes's gang.

## The Pursuit

Labar's search for Larry Hughes leads him into Romney Marsh, a desolate and treacherous landscape. Although the marsh pursuit appears spectacular, the most challenging part is the intensive investigation that precedes it [021-001]. Labar regrets the lack of a telephone, acutely aware of how the conveniences of civilisation are missed once absent [022-001]. His common sense tells him to return for reinforcements from the Kent Constabulary or even to wire Scotland Yard, but he has no idea how long it would take to walk out of the marsh — and he cannot afford to let Hughes slip away [022-002].

## The Ambush

Labar falls into an ambush. He knows himself to be in the tightest corner of his life, certain that at the first false move, he will be dealt with ruthlessly [025-001]. The gang has been waiting for him, and his position is hopeless.

## Aftermath

Labar's capture sets the stage for the police rescue operation. The local constabulary, led by the retired military officer who heads the Kent County Constabulary, mobilises alongside reinforcements [027-011]. The marsh becomes the site of a coordinated police action involving no fewer than three police forces — the Metropolitan Police, the Kent Constabulary, and the Sussex Police [024-022].
