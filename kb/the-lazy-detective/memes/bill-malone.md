# Bill Malone

Detective Sergeant Bill Malone is Detective Inspector Labar's right hand in *The Lazy Detective* — a seasoned officer whose bluff, energetic manner provides a constant counterpoint to Labar's indolent refinement. Where Labar insinuates, Malone blusters; where Labar observes, Malone searches. The partnership works because each supplies what the other lacks.

## Character

Malone is introduced in a burst of physical energy — "a heavy hand fell with mathematical accuracy between his shoulder blades" — and his boisterous manner is a source of constant, affectionate irritation to Labar:

> "One of these days, Moreland, someone's going to slap you hard on the wrist, slog you on the jaw, and kick you where it hurts most. You're too boisterous for the society of gentlemen." [[001-006]]

Malone presumes on years of association to speak frankly. He is Labar's conscience and, at times, his goad — the voice that reminds the lazy detective that work must actually be done.

## Role in the Novel

### The Investigator on the Ground

While Labar conducts the strategic dimension of the investigation, Malone does the legwork. It is Malone who examines the crime scene and reports the absence of forensic evidence:

> "Not so much as a blighting fingerprint." [[002-015]]

It is Malone who traces the hundred-pound note, who interviews the bank officials, who pounds the pavement. Without Malone, Labar's brilliant deductions would lack their factual foundation.

### The Loyal Second

Malone's loyalty to Labar is absolute. When the investigation threatens to go badly, his concern is as much for Labar's career as for the case:

> "Nice lookout for us, an' the Yard not throwing any flowers our way as it is." [[001-031]]
