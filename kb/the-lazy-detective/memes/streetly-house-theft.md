# Streetly House Theft

The theft at Streetly House ([[streetly-house]]), the Park Lane residence of financier Solly Gertstein ([[solly-gertstein]]), is the catalytic event of *The Lazy Detective*. A quarter-million pounds' worth of jewels and objets d'art vanish in a single night, stolen with a precision that suggests professional criminals rather than opportunists.

## The Crime

Bill Malone reports the theft with grim satisfaction — this is a case that will make or break careers:

> "They've made a clean sweep of Streetly House. Quarter of a million of pounds worth of jewels and curios melted away as clean as a conjuring trick." [[001-030]]

The absence of forensic evidence — "not so much as a blighting fingerprint" [[002-015]] — confirms that the thieves are professionals who understand and neutralize scientific detection.

## The Investigation

The theft launches Labar's investigation, which gradually reveals that it is not an isolated crime but the visible surface of a deeper network of forgery ([[forgery]]), blackmail ([[blackmail]]), and concealment. The jewels are what was taken; the truth is what Labar must recover.
