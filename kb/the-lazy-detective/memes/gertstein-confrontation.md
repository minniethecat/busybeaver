The Gertstein confrontation marks a critical escalation in Detective Inspector Labar's investigation. Labar directly confronts Solly Gertstein, the criminal mastermind behind the Streetly House theft and the forged cheque scheme, as the net tightens around the conspiracy.

## Gertstein Under Pressure

No one can tell with certainty how a great disaster will affect a man. Gertstein, chewing a cold cigar and with hands thrust deep in his trousers pockets, strides with rolling gait about the room [017-001]. The usually composed criminal financier is rattled — the carefully constructed edifice of his scheme is crumbling under Labar's relentless pressure.

## The Confrontation

Labar presses his advantage. The confrontation is not merely an interrogation — it is a battle of wills between two formidable adversaries who understand the stakes. Gertstein, a man accustomed to controlling every situation, finds himself cornered. The inspector's methodical approach has stripped away the layers of protection Gertstein had built around himself.

## The Arrest of Stebbins

No charge is made against Stebbins that night, and inquiries from the newspapers — anxious to know more about the developing case — go unanswered [018-001]. Labar is methodically building his case, ensuring that when the arrests come, the evidence will be unassailable.

## Aftermath

The confrontation sets in motion the final phase of the investigation. Gertstein's composure has been cracked, and the information Labar extracts — directly and indirectly — leads to the discovery of the wider conspiracy and ultimately the pursuit across Romney Marsh.
