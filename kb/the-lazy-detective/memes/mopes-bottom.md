# Mope's Bottom

Mope's Bottom is a lonely house deep in the marshlands of Kent, serving as
the ultimate refuge and operational redoubt of [[larry-hughes|Larry Hughes]]'s
criminal gang. Remote, surrounded by difficult terrain, and accessible only
by winding tracks, it represents the gang's last line of defence against
[[detective-inspector-labar|Detective Inspector Labar]]'s investigation.
The location becomes the setting for the novel's climactic sequence: Labar's
ambush and imprisonment, his escape through a secret tunnel, and the
coordinated police raid that brings down the conspiracy.

## Location and Geography

Mope's Bottom is situated on the Kentish marshes, a landscape of isolation
and concealment. The terrain itself serves the gang's purposes — unfamiliar
to outsiders, difficult to navigate, and affording natural cover. Local
knowledge is required even to locate the house:

> From somewhere the local inspector who accompanied him, routed out a constable who was said to know the district, and a farmer and a shepherd picked up on the way volunteered their services. Both these latter agreed that the house for which search was being made could be none other than Mope's Bottom. [024-025]

The approach is by rough track, nearly impassable by motor car, forcing
the police to proceed with caution:

> For a mile or so they pushed the car along one of the rough tracks of the marsh. As the looker explained, the detour was even more extensive than if they had gone on foot. [024-032]

## Strategic Significance

The choice of Mope's Bottom as a hideout reflects Hughes's operational
sophistication. Its advantages are multiple:

| Feature | Tactical Value |
|---------|---------------|
| Remote location | Limits police presence and response time |
| Marsh terrain | Difficult for outsiders to navigate |
| Secret tunnel | Provides escape route to the sea |
| Limited approaches | Easy to spot approaching threats |
| Local obscurity | Not known beyond the immediate district |

## The Secret Tunnel

The house conceals a hidden tunnel — accessed through a secret panel — that
runs from the building to the marsh edge, providing an escape route in case
of police approach. It is through this tunnel that Hughes and
[[billy-bungey|Billy Bungey]] attempt to flee:

> As he was led through the open panel, with Larry and Billy Bungey on each side of him, one of them slid it into place behind. For a second they were in impenetrable blackness. Then someone switched on an electric torch and Labar gathered that he was in a narrow tunnel which widened as they advanced. [025-008]

## Labar's Captivity

Mope's Bottom is where Labar experiences his most dangerous moments.
Ambushed by Hughes and Bungey emerging from the secret panel, he is
bound and held prisoner while the gang debates his fate:

> Before an open panel in the woodwork stood Larry Hughes and Billy Bungey each with a levelled pistol in his hand. [024-046]

Bungey advocates for killing the detective, and the threat of execution
is genuine. Labar's escape — freeing himself from his bonds and turning
the tables on his captors — is the novel's most dramatic sequence [026-021].

## The Police Raid

The raid on Mope's Bottom represents the culmination of the investigation.
Coordinated between [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]]'s [[flying-squad|Flying Squad]],
local Kent constables, and the [[criminal-investigation-department|C.I.D.]],
the operation captures most of the gang. Only Hughes and Bungey initially
escape:

> So far as the detectives could tell every one of the gang who had been at Mope's Bottom, except Larry and Billy Bungey, was safely in custody. [028-001]

After the raid, Labar orders a systematic search of the premises, including
the tunnel, to gather evidence and ensure no confederates remain hidden
[028-004].
