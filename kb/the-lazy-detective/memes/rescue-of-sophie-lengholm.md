The rescue of Sophie Lengholm is the turning point in the Romney Marsh sequence. After Detective Inspector Labar is captured by Larry Hughes's gang, a large-scale police operation converges on the marsh hideout and frees the captives.

## The Underground Chamber

Labar and Sophie Lengholm are held in an underground chamber as they await the return of Larry Hughes. Time passes slowly in the darkness [026-001]. Labar can hear muffled sounds from above and strains to interpret the movements of the gang members who guard them.

## The Rescue Operation

Explanations are deferred until the prisoners can be dealt with. Half a dozen stalwart constables, each armed with a shotgun, take charge of the captives, who are subjected to a swift and methodical search [027-001]. The rescue is the culmination of the coordinated effort between the Metropolitan Police, the Kent Constabulary, and the Sussex Police [024-022].

Sophie Lengholm is freed from her confinement. The toll of her captivity is evident — she is shaken but alive, having endured the gang's threats and the grim conditions of the hideout.

## Aftermath

The rescue clears the way for the arrest of the remaining conspirators. The underground hideout is secured, and the police begin the process of gathering evidence for the prosecution. Labar, though battered by his ordeal in the marsh, resumes command of the investigation.
