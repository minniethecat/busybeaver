The abduction of Sophie Lengholm is a key criminal event in the narrative. Sophie Lengholm is taken by Larry Hughes's gang and held captive at a remote, walled property, where she is joined by Penelope Noelson, who is also confined against her will.

## The Captivity

Penelope Noelson endures days of confinement at the walled property. She comes to know every inch of the walled garden, gazing through the iron bars of the gate [019-001]. At night, a great Alsatian wolfhound patrols the grounds, and her door is locked from the outside [019-002]. She sees strange men about the house — associates of Larry Hughes — who have long interviews with him, though they never approach her [019-003].

Larry Hughes treats Penelope with punctilious politeness, but she glimpses his savage and indomitable will beneath the surface [019-004]. Her horror grows at her own impotence in the face of an unknown destiny, wondering whether the police are searching for her [019-005].

## Sophie's Role

Sophie Lengholm meets Penelope's inquiries with grim assurances that the men will return shortly. Penelope knows she is lying. Sophie twists under the pressure of her own complicity, caught between fear of the gang and the weight of what she knows [019-006].

## Larry Hughes's Control

Larry Hughes maintains an air of urbane menace. He appears silently, offering gold cigarettes, treating his captives as "allies in a common cause — our own safety" [020-002]. He frames Detective Inspector Labar's investigation as "harsh and brutal methods" designed to force them all into opposition [020-003]. The abduction is a strategic move in Hughes's effort to evade justice.
