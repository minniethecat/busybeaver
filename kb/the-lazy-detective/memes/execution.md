# Execution

In [[the-lazy-detective|The Lazy Detective]], execution is the hinge between
planning and action. [[detective-inspector-labar|Detective Inspector Labar]]'s
investigative method is celebrated for its intellectual component — the
[[deduction|deduction]], the [[observation|observation]], the patient assembly
of [[evidence|evidence]] — but the novel is equally attentive to the moment when
thought must become deed: when a plan must be put into execution, a warrant
served, an arrest made.

## The Operational Sense

The novel's most common use of "execution" is operational: the carrying out of
a planned action. Labar is characterised as "not the man to waste time in putting
any project into execution" [020-046]. This is execution as follow-through — the
quality that distinguishes the effective detective from the merely clever one.
Ideas are cheap; execution is what counts.

The language of "mission" attaches to police work throughout the novel. When
[[gold-dust-teddy|Gold Dust Teddy]] is to be arrested, the task is framed in
military terms:

> Detective Sergeant Down to whom was entrusted the execution of this mission,
> received his orders with satisfaction. [030-034]

"Mission," "entrusted," "orders" — the vocabulary imports the hierarchy and
discipline of military operations into police work. Execution is what subordinates
do; conception is what leaders do. The novel's police force is an organisation
designed to translate strategic intent into tactical action.

## The Deadly Sense

Execution carries its darkest meaning — the taking of life — in the novel's
moments of lethal confrontation. When Labar is cornered in a confined space with
armed adversaries, his capacity for deadly force becomes the sole guarantee of
his authority:

> He had convinced them, for the time, that he would stand at nothing, and in
> that confined space even a bad shot could not fail to wreck deadly execution. [026-045]

"Deadly execution" — the phrase compresses the operational and the lethal into a
single expression. Labar is not a natural killer; the narrator has already noted
that he is "a rotten shot" [015-006]. But the threat of execution, like the threat
of arrest, is a tool that can be deployed without being discharged. The credibility
of the threat is what matters.

## The Gap Between Plan and Act

The novel's treatment of execution also registers the inevitable gap between
intention and outcome. Plans are executed imperfectly; missions go awry. The
elaborate arrangements for the Mope's Bottom raid are compromised by fog, by
the absence of a telephone, by the sheer friction of translating a plan into
action across difficult terrain. Execution, in the end, is always a negotiation
between the ideal of the plan and the resistance of reality.
