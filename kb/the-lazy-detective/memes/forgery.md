# Forgery

Forgery, the act of falsifying a document or signature with intent to defraud, serves as a pivotal plot mechanism in *The Lazy Detective*. A forged cheque on the Midland Bank becomes the thread that unravels the web of crimes surrounding the Streetly House jewel theft, revealing the desperation of its perpetrator and the reach of those who exploit it.

## In the Novel

### The Forged Cheque

The discovery of a forged cheque at the Midland Bank connects the seemingly unrelated strands of the investigation. Gertstein himself could offer little insight:

> Gertstein had been able to throw no light on the forgery, save that a cheque form was missing from his book. [[009-002]]

The missing cheque form and the forged signature point toward someone with access to Gertstein's private affairs — narrowing the suspect pool considerably.

### Mrs. Gertstein's Involvement

Labar eventually concludes that Mrs. Gertstein is the author of the forgery:

> Labar had made up his mind that Mrs. Gertstein was the author of the forgery, and her identification would have been an important link in the evidence. [[012-001]]

The forgery is framed not as a coldly calculated crime but as an act of desperation. Larry Hughes uses knowledge of the forgery to control Penelope Noelson:

> "You are the man who wrecked her life, who has driven her to forgery, and worse." [[007-039]]

### Forgery in the Criminal Ecosystem

The novel places forgery within a taxonomy of organized crime. Professional criminals who finance operations keep their distance from the actual execution:

> They will organise and finance a burglary, a forgery, or a holdup, but they keep well in the background. [[001-017]]

### Legal Implications

The bank's liability hangs on the question of whether the cheque is indeed a forgery. Malone's investigation reveals the bank manager's caution:

> "You see if it is a forgery the bank will be liable. Didn't want me to bring away the cheques at first." [[004-038]]
