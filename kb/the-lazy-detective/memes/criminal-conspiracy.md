# Criminal Conspiracy

The plot of [[the-lazy-detective|The Lazy Detective]] is structured
around a criminal conspiracy — the planned and coordinated robbery of
[[streetly-house|Streetly House]], the residence of millionaire
[[solly-gertstein|Solly Gertstein]]. The conspiracy, orchestrated by
[[larry-hughes|Larry Hughes]], exemplifies the classic features of
organised criminal enterprise: hierarchical structure, compartmentalised
information, specialised roles, and a mastermind insulated from direct
participation.

## Structure of the Conspiracy

The Streetly House conspiracy follows a cell structure designed to
protect its leadership:

> No hint was then given as to the time or method of the robbery. All instructions would reach Stebbins either by letter addressed to him at an accommodation address, or through Billy Bungey. It was pointed out to him that he must on no account seek out the latter unless sent for. [018-033]

This operational security — one-way communication, intermediaries,
accommodation addresses — is characteristic of sophisticated criminal
conspiracies. Each participant knows only what they need to know, and
no single arrest can compromise the entire organisation.

## The Conspirators

The conspiracy involves a diverse cast of participants:

- **Larry Hughes**: Mastermind and financier. Plans the operation and manages its proceeds.
- **[[billy-bungey|Billy Bungey]]**: Recruiter and enforcer. Identifies and handles lower-level operatives.
- **[[gold-dust-teddy|Gold Dust Teddy]]**: Specialist burglar. One of the few men in London with the skill to execute the robbery.
- **Stebbins**: Inside man. Placed within Streetly House to provide intelligence and access.
- **Tom and others**: Support operatives. Provide manpower for the hideout and escape.

## Legal Framework

At common law, conspiracy is an agreement between two or more persons
to commit an unlawful act. The crime is complete upon agreement — no
overt act is required (though the Streetly House conspiracy certainly
involved many). The doctrine allows prosecutors to charge all
participants for the full scope of the criminal enterprise, not merely
their individual contributions.

## Detection and Collapse

The conspiracy unravels through a combination of [[detective-inspector-labar|Labar]]'s
investigative persistence and internal weaknesses. The careful
compartmentalisation that protects Hughes also means that lower-level
operatives, once identified, can be leveraged against the leadership.
[[gold-dust-teddy|Teddy]]'s surveillance, Stebbins's confession, and
[[penelope-noelson|Penelope Noelson]]'s flight each represent a breach
in the conspiracy's information security.
