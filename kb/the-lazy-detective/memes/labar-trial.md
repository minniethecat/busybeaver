# Labar's Trial

The trial at the Old Bailey ([[old-bailey]]) is the novel's climactic event — the moment when Detective Inspector Labar's ([[detective-inspector-labar]]) entire investigation is transformed into testimony and subjected to the formal procedures of criminal justice:

> Labar took his stand in the witness box for examination at the hands of Treasury Counsel. His evidence began with the events of that morning when he had received the hundred-pound note. [[030-073]]

The trial is the institutional endpoint toward which the entire novel has been moving. Everything Labar has discovered — the forgery, the blackmail, the jewel theft — must now be proved in open court.
