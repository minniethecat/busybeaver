The interrogation of Penelope Noelson is a central investigative sequence in the early chapters. Convinced that she is the key to the mystery, Detective Inspector Labar subjects her to a methodical and psychologically acute questioning.

## Labar's Conviction

It is with the conviction that Penelope Noelson is the key to the mystery that Labar makes his way back to town [006-001]. The hints and inconsistencies in her account have not escaped his trained eye. He knows that she is holding something back — something that could unlock the entire case.

## The Interrogation

Labar's approach is patient and layered. He does not accuse her directly. Instead, he allows her to tell her story, probing gently at the edges where her narrative frays. His voice is soothing, velvety, as he asks whether she is certain that a jury would believe the witnesses she proposes [008-025]. The question hangs in the air, and Penelope understands its implication — she is being offered a choice between cooperation and complicity.

## What Emerges

The interrogation yields fragments rather than a confession. Penelope's relationship with Larry Hughes, her knowledge of the Streetly House theft, and her connection to Solly Gertstein all come under scrutiny. Labar pieces together enough to justify his growing certainty that she is entangled in something far larger than she will admit.

## Aftermath

Although Labar would have preferred to tumble into the fastest motor car he could find and engage in swift pursuit, he restrains himself [008-001]. The interrogation has given him threads to follow, and patience — his greatest asset — will serve him better than haste.
