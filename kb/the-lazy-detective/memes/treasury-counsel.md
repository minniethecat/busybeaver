# Treasury Counsel

Treasury Counsel represents the Crown at the Old Bailey trial that concludes *The Lazy Detective*. As the examining barrister, he is the figure through whom Labar's investigation is transformed into admissible evidence and presented to the jury ([[jury]]).

## Role

The examination of Labar follows a careful structure:

> Labar took his stand in the witness box for examination at the hands of Treasury Counsel. His evidence began with the events of that morning when he had received the hundred-pound note. [[030-073]]

Treasury Counsel must build the narrative of the crime from Labar's testimony — selecting what to emphasize, what to omit, and how to present the chain of evidence in terms the jury can follow. The prosecutor is not merely a mouthpiece but an active shaper of the story the jury hears.
