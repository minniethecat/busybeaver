# Treasury

The Treasury, through its counsel, represents the Crown in the criminal prosecution at the Old Bailey ([[old-bailey]]) trial that concludes *The Lazy Detective*. Treasury Counsel examines Labar as a witness, transforming his investigation into the formal evidence presented to the jury ([[jury]]).

The Treasury's role in the novel is procedural rather than personal — it represents the institutional machinery of justice, the apparatus that takes the detective's work and presents it in the formal language of the law.
