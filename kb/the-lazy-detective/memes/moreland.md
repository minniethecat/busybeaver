# Moreland

Moreland is a minor but memorable presence in *The Lazy Detective* — a detective whose enthusiastic back-slapping manner provides comic relief and character contrast. Labar's reaction to his greeting is typical:

> "One of these days, Moreland, someone's going to slap you hard on the wrist, slog you on the jaw, and kick you where it hurts most. You're too boisterous for the society of gentlemen." [[001-006]]

He represents the physical, energetic side of police work — the opposite of Labar's languid intellectualism. His limited appearances in the novel serve to highlight by contrast what makes Labar unusual.
