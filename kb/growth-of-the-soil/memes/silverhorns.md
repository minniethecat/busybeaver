# Silverhorns

Silverhorns is the calf of Goldenhorns — the second-generation cow at Sellanraa whose arrival marks the farm's transition from mere survival to genuine growth and reproduction.

## 定义

Silverhorns is the first calf born at Sellanraa, daughter of Goldenhorns. While Goldenhorns was brought to the farm by Oline, Silverhorns represents something new: an animal born on the land itself. Her name, given by Isak without hesitation, signals that the farm has achieved a self-sustaining cycle — the land now produces life, not just sustains it (003-019).

## 在小说中的表现

### The First Birth at Sellanraa

Goldenhorns' calving of Silverhorns is treated as a momentous event — the first birth on Isak's land, proof that the wilderness can become a place of generation, not just subsistence. The calf's arrival transforms the homestead from a shelter in the wild into a living, growing place (004-043):

> Isak heard a sudden shout; Inger stood on the door-slab with the child in her arms, pointing over to the bull and the pretty little cow Silverhorns — they were making love.

### Fertility and Multiplication

Silverhorns herself soon calves, and the farm's livestock begins to multiply naturally. What started with a single goat and one cow has become a herd. This multiplication is the biological counterpart to Isak's cultivation of the soil — both are forms of growth that measure the farm's success (004-059):

> And now pretty Silverhorns had calved, the sheep had lambs, the goats had kids, the young stock fairly swarmed about the place.

## 主题意义

Silverhorns embodies the theme of generational continuity. Where Goldenhorns was a gift from outside (brought by Oline), Silverhorns is a product of the land itself. She represents the moment when the pioneer's labor begins to yield not just crops but life — the farm becomes a place where things are born, not merely maintained. This mirrors the human story at Sellanraa, where Eleseus and Sivert represent the second generation.
