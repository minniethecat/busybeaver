# Barn

The barn is the most ambitious building project at Sellanraa after the house itself. Its construction spans multiple chapters, its scale draws comment from neighbours, and its interior serves as the gathering place for some of the novel's most consequential social scenes.

## 定义

In the pioneer's building hierarchy, the barn marks a transition from subsistence shelters to proper farm infrastructure. A barn with a threshing-floor means grain can be processed on site; a barn with a bridge means hay can be stored overhead. Isak builds his barn in stages — first the basic structure, then the threshing-floor, then the paint — each addition a milestone in the farm's development.

## 在小说中的表现

### Planning and Construction

Isak conceives the barn early: "a big barn with a threshing-floor inside" (004-059) appears as a distant dream. By Chapter V, the dream becomes necessity: "how could he have threshed it without a barn and threshing-floor?" (005-001). The question is rhetorical — without a barn, the harvest is useless. Construction proceeds across seasons: Isak gets "a spell of roofing on the new barn, and get the south wall at least" (007-002) done between other tasks. The barn's completion is stated with Hamsun's characteristic brevity: "The barn was finished; a great big place" (007-055).

### A Painted Barn

The most telling detail comes in Chapter X: Isak "painted it red" (010-059). A painted barn is not a necessity — it is a statement. Red paint costs money and time, and a pioneer who paints his barn is a pioneer who no longer worries about bare survival. The red barn becomes a landmark, visible from a distance, announcing that Sellanraa is a proper farm.

### The Barn as Social Space

The barn's function extends beyond agriculture. During the mining rights meeting in Chapter XIII, the men "were given shelter in the big barn" (013-017), transforming it into a makeshift inn. Later, after the meeting disperses, the last stragglers linger in the barn, reluctant to leave. Geissler, the novel's most enigmatic figure, leaves behind "a bundle and a cap I left in your barn" (013-084) — a small gesture that makes the barn a repository of unfinished business.
