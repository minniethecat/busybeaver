# Leopoldine

The daughter of [[isak|Isak]] and [[inger|Inger]], named after the day of her birth, whose quiet presence at [[sellanraa|Sellanraa]] represents the domestic continuity that sustains the pioneer family across generations.

## 角色概要

Leopoldine is born after [[inger|Inger]]'s return to [[sellanraa|Sellanraa]]. Her naming — "after the day she was born" — reflects the simple, practical culture of the farm, where even a child's name is drawn from the rhythm of days and seasons rather than family tradition or ambition. She is the youngest of [[isak|Isak]] and [[inger|Inger]]'s children, and her presence completes the family circle at [[sellanraa|Sellanraa]] (PN=009-062).

## 叙事弧线

### 归来与团聚

When [[inger|Inger]] returns from her absence, she brings Leopoldine with her. "This is Leopoldine; she's stood the voyage much better than I did," [[inger|Inger]] announces, introducing the child to [[isak|Isak]]. This moment of family reunion — father meeting daughter for the first time — is one of the novel's tender domestic scenes (PN=011-008, PN=011-009).

### 农场上的童年

Leopoldine grows up in the warmth of the [[sellanraa|Sellanraa]] household. She runs about in the heather, falls asleep in the cart during journeys, and wakes "thoroughly rested." Her childhood is presented as idyllic — the natural life that Hamsun idealizes. [[isak|Isak]], the gruff pioneer, shows a particular tenderness toward his daughter: "it was a pretty sight and a pleasure to see little Leopoldine coming up so trustingly to him" (PN=011-035, PN=011-121, PN=012-003).

### 成长与传承

As Leopoldine grows, she begins to take on the rhythms of the household. She learns crochet work, helps with domestic tasks, and is noted for her growing capability. "A few years more, and Leopoldine'll be big enough to do this and that," [[inger|Inger]] observes, marking the passage of time and the continuity of domestic knowledge from mother to daughter (PN=012-010, PN=014-015).

## 角色功能

Leopoldine is the novel's emblem of domestic continuity. Unlike her brothers — [[eleseus|Eleseus]] who leaves for the city and [[sivert|Sivert]] who inherits the farm — Leopoldine represents the quiet, often overlooked work of maintaining the household. Her presence is a constant throughout the later chapters, a reminder that pioneering requires not only the breaking of new ground but the nurturing of new life.
