# Family

Family in Growth of the Soil is the fundamental unit of survival, meaning, and continuity. Isak and Inger's marriage, their children's divergent paths, and the question of who will inherit Sellanraa together form the novel's human core — the story that unfolds alongside the growth of crops and livestock.

## 定义

The family at Sellanraa is not a sentimental construction but a working partnership. Isak and Inger marry because the homestead needs a woman's labor as much as a man's. Their marriage, initially practical, deepens over decades of shared work, loss, and rebuilding. The novel treats this evolution — from partnership to love forged in labor — as the highest form of human relationship.

## 在小说中的表现

### Isak and Inger

Their marriage begins awkwardly: Inger arrives with a hare-lip, Isak cannot believe his luck in finding a woman willing to stay. But the bond they form through shared work — building, farming, bearing and losing children — becomes the novel's emotional anchor. Inger's imprisonment in Bergen tests the marriage but does not break it; when she returns, she and Isak rebuild their life together, older and marked by experience but fundamentally united (015-XXX).

### Children and Divergence

The four children — Eleseus, Sivert, Leopoldine, and the unnamed infant — represent different possibilities of pioneer life. Eleseus leaves for the city, Sivert stays on the land, Leopoldine marries into the community. The unnamed infant, killed by Inger, haunts the family as a silent presence — the child who never had a chance to choose between city and land.

### Inheritance

The question of who will take over Sellanraa is the family's ultimate test. Sivert, who has worked the land alongside Isak, is the natural heir — not because of legal right but because of the labor he has invested. Eleseus, the educated firstborn, has forfeited his claim by leaving. The novel's resolution — Sivert stays, Eleseus departs — is both a family story and a moral argument: inheritance belongs to those who have labored for it.

### Generational Change

Each generation at Sellanraa transforms the homestead. Isak cleared the first fields, built the first cabin. Sivert will inherit not just the farm but the responsibility to maintain and extend it. The novel ends with the sense that this cycle will continue — the soil will keep growing, the family will keep working, generation after generation. This is Hamsun's answer to modern anxiety about "legacy": legacy is not a name or a fortune but a piece of land and the willingness to work it.

## 主题意义

Family is the bridge between the individual pioneer and the community. Through family, Isak's solitary labor becomes a shared project; through family, Sellanraa acquires a future beyond Isak's lifetime. The novel's title — Growth of the Soil — refers to crops and animals, but it also refers to the growth of human life rooted in a particular place, sustained by family bonds across generations.
