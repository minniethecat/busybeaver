# Work

Work in Growth of the Soil is not what you do to live — it is what living is. Hamsun's novel is one of literature's most sustained celebrations of physical labor, presenting work not as burden or necessity but as the fundamental human activity through which individuals create value, meaning, and identity.

## 定义

The novel distinguishes between two kinds of work. There is the work of the body — Isak's plowing, building, ditching; Inger's milking, cheese-making, weaving. And there is the work of transactions — Geissler's mining negotiations, the village store's commerce, Eleseus's clerical duties. The first is honored; the second is tolerated at best. This is not a romantic preference but a structural commitment: work that transforms the material world directly is real; work that merely rearranges ownership is secondary.

## 在小说中的表现

### Isak as Worker

Isak is defined entirely by his work. The novel introduces him not with a description of his appearance or personality but with an account of what he does: he walks, he searches, he clears, he builds. His identity is his labor. When he stops working — which is almost never — the narrative has nothing to say about him. Isak at rest is invisible; Isak at work is the center of the novel's world.

### The Rhythm of Labor

Work in the novel has a specific rhythm tied to seasons, daylight, and the body's capacities. There are no deadlines, no quotas, no performance reviews — only the natural pace of tasks that must be done when conditions require them. This rhythm is the novel's deepest argument against modern industrial work, which imposes artificial schedules on activities that have their own natural timing.

### Work and Gender

The division of labor at Sellanraa follows traditional gender lines but the novel treats both sides with equal seriousness. Isak's heavy outdoor labor is no more honored than Inger's domestic production. Together, their complementary forms of work create the self-sufficient household that is the novel's ideal.

### Failed Work

The novel also shows failed or inadequate work: Brede Olsen's half-hearted farming, Eleseus's store that has no customers. These failures are not moral condemnations of the characters but demonstrations of a structural truth: work that is not fully committed — work that is done for show or for quick returns — cannot sustain life in the wilderness. The land knows the difference.

## 主题意义

Hamsun's philosophy of work can be summarized as: the goal of work is not to finish working. Isak never talks about retirement, never imagines a life of leisure. His satisfaction comes not from the completion of tasks but from the ongoing process of doing them. This is the novel's most radical claim — and its most challenging one for readers shaped by a culture that treats work as a cost to be minimized.
