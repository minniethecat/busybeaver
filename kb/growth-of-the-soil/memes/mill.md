# Mill (Grain)

The grain mill at Sellanraa is Isak's first major water-powered construction — a small but functional mill built across the river that allows the farm to process its own corn into flour, marking a transition from manual labor to mechanical aid.

## 定义

A water-powered grain mill, constructed by Isak across the river at Sellanraa. It uses the force of a waterfall to turn a grinding mechanism, converting harvested grain into flour. Before the mill, Isak had to carry his corn to an external mill [007-065]; the new mill brings this process under his own control. Geissler describes it as "a mill for dwarfs, for trollfolk, but strong and useful for a man's work" [010-052].

## 在小说中的表现

### 正面描写

The mill is built before the sawmill and serves as proof of concept for harnessing the river's power. When Geissler visits Sellanraa, he notices it immediately: "Built a mill of your own, have you? grind your own corn? Excellent" [010-004]. The mill is modest in scale — "it was small enough, and roughly built" [010-052] — but its significance lies in what it represents: self-sufficiency and the gradual accumulation of infrastructure on the homestead.

### 对比与张力

The mill contrasts with the earlier practice of carrying corn to a distant mill for grinding [007-065]. It also foreshadows the more ambitious sawmill project, with Isak carefully observing the river's falls and planning sequential improvements: first grinding, then sawing [010-052]. The mill's humble construction ("for dwarfs, for trollfolk") is characteristic of Isak's practical, unhurried approach to building — functional before beautiful.

## 主题意义

The mill embodies the novel's theme of incremental progress through labor. Each addition to the farm — the turf hut, the mill, the sawmill, the new house — represents a layer of civilization laid upon the wilderness. The mill specifically connects the agricultural cycle (sowing, harvesting) to the domestic sphere (baking, eating), closing the loop of self-sufficiency [007-065].

## 关键引文
