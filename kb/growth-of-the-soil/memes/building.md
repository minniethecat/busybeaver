# Building

Building in Growth of the Soil is not merely construction — it is the pioneer's signature on the wilderness, the transformation of raw timber and turf into a home, a cowshed, a barn, a mill. Isak builds continuously across the novel, each new structure marking a stage in Sellanraa's growth and his own growing mastery of the land.

## 定义

In Hamsun's novel, building is the central activity of the pioneer. Isak arrives with nothing and builds everything — first a turf hut for shelter, then a proper timber house, a cowshed, a barn, a mill, a sawmill. Each building project is described with painstaking technical detail: notching baulks, fitting joints, driving half-inch nails "as gentle as building dolls' houses" (004-056). Building is both necessity and art; it is how the pioneer roots himself in the soil.

## 在小说中的表现

### The First Hut

When the novel opens, Isak's first priority is shelter. He builds a turf hut — the most primitive form of dwelling, made from the very earth he stands on. This hut is the seed from which Sellanraa grows. The narrator notes that he went about "building with timber all by himself" (002-048), a solitary giant in the wilderness.

### Continuous Expansion

Building never stops at Sellanraa. When Oline first visits, she observes Isak at work: "'Seems like you're building,' says Inger. 'What?'" (002-047). Later, Geissler too notices: "And you're building again, I see. Now what'll that be?" (003-064). Each new structure answers a need — more children, more animals, more ambition. Isak "went about building, notching his baulks and fitting them" (002-061), the rhythm of his labor matching the rhythm of the seasons.

### Beyond the House

Isak's building extends beyond the dwelling. He builds a mill across the river, a sawmill for processing timber, and a cowshed — though the neighbors doubt him: "it's never a cowshed you're building, surely?" (002-052). He proves them wrong. The buildings accumulate: "Isak was building again — what was he building now?" (004-055). The answer is always more rooms, more storage, more infrastructure for a growing homestead.
