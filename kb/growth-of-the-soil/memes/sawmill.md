# Sawmill

The sawmill at Sellanraa represents one of Isak's most ambitious building projects — a steam-powered circular saw that transforms his ability to process timber and marks a significant step in the farm's technological development.

## 定义

The sawmill is a mechanized timber-cutting installation powered by a river fall, using a large circular saw blade. In the context of Sellanraa, it represents the transition from hand-tool subsistence to machine-assisted production. Isak orders the circular blade and fittings from Trondhjem, transports them to the farm on winter roads, and constructs the framework himself [012-030].

## 在小说中的表现

### 正面描写

Isak conceives of the sawmill as a natural extension of the farm's water-powered infrastructure, having already built a grain mill on the same river. Geissler notices the second waterfall during a visit and Isak explains it "was to turn a saw, if so be God gave him health" [010-052]. The project takes over a year to complete: the foundation posts are set by Isak with his sons' help [012-083], but the building is not roofed in before autumn. "No, the sawmill did not get roofed in that autumn, but that could not be helped—after all, 'twas not a matter of life or death" [012-090]. By the following season, "between seasons, he got his sawmill roofed in, and could set to work putting up the machine parts" [013-014].

### 对比与张力

The sawmill project highlights the tension between ambition and physical limitation. Isak, ordinarily indefatigable, finds himself "beginning to feel tired in the evenings now" [012-084]. The project competes with the endless demands of farming — hay, corn, potatoes — and Isak's weariness is a rare glimpse of human frailty in a character defined by relentless labor. The mill and sawmill together also mark the shift from pure subsistence to surplus production on the farm.

## 主题意义

The sawmill embodies the novel's broader theme of gradual technological progress in the wilderness. Where the first hut was built by hand from turf [001-014], the sawmill represents a new phase of settlement — one where machinery, long-distance trade (ordering parts from Trondhjem), and intergenerational cooperation (the sons helping) combine to transform the homestead [012-083].

## 关键引文

> "Last winter, when the roads were hard, he had carted up the big circular blade and the fittings, ordered from Trondhjem through the village store. The parts were lying in one of the sheds now, well smeared with oil to keep off the rust. He had brought up some of the beams too, for the framework; he could begin building when he pleased, but he put it off." [012-030]
