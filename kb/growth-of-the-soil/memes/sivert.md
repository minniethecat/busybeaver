# Sivert

The younger son of [[isak|Isak]] and [[inger|Inger]], who remains at [[sellanraa|Sellanraa]] to work the land — the true heir to his father's agricultural life and the character through whom Hamsun affirms the continuity of the pioneer spirit.

## 角色概要

Sivert is the second son born at [[sellanraa|Sellanraa]]. Unlike his elder brother [[eleseus|Eleseus]], Sivert is robust and practical from the start: "[[eleseus|Eleseus]] was inclined to be ailing sometimes, which was not the way of Sivert." [[isak|Isak]] himself would rather have called him something else, but defers to [[inger|Inger]]'s naming once again (PN=004-057). From childhood, Sivert shows an affinity for the farm and its work that marks him as [[isak|Isak]]'s true successor.

## 叙事弧线

### 童年与性格形成

Sivert grows up in the rhythms of farm life. When visitors come, he is the child who receives coins and attention with quiet acceptance. His father involves him in the work of the farm early: "a hand with this, and little Sivert helps with that" (PN=005-043, PN=011-033).

### 计数与学习

A revealing scene shows [[isak|Isak]] teaching both sons to count — a small domestic moment that foreshadows their divergent paths. Sivert participates in these lessons alongside [[eleseus|Eleseus]], but the practical, grounded intelligence he displays will remain tied to the farm rather than the office (PN=008-052).

### 母亲的离开与归来

When [[inger|Inger]] is away, Sivert waits and watches; when she returns, he cries openly — "little Sivert cried more openly, could not help it" — showing an emotional directness that contrasts with [[eleseus|Eleseus]]'s more reserved response. His open grief reveals a character who feels deeply but acts practically (PN=009-060, PN=009-064).

### 农场的继承人

As the novel progresses, Sivert increasingly takes on the role of [[isak|Isak]]'s successor. Where [[eleseus|Eleseus]] leaves for the town, Sivert stays. He represents the generational continuity that the novel values above all else: the son who will continue to work the land after the father is gone.

## 角色功能

Sivert is the novel's symbol of continuity and inheritance. If [[isak|Isak]] is the archetypal pioneer, Sivert is the next generation — proof that the values of agricultural life can be passed down. His contrast with [[eleseus|Eleseus]] makes the novel's moral argument explicit: staying on the land is not a failure of ambition but a fulfillment of purpose.
