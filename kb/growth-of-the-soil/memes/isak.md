# Isak

The protagonist of *Growth of the Soil*, a man of few words and steady purpose who transforms a patch of Norwegian wilderness into the thriving farmstead of [[sellanraa|Sellanraa]].

## 角色定位

Isak is the novel's central figure and moral center. He is introduced in the opening lines as a lone figure walking through the wilderness, searching for a place to settle. Over the course of the novel, he establishes a farm, marries, raises a family, and becomes a pillar of the rural community (PN=001-001, PN=001-005). Hamsun presents him not as an intellectual but as a man of instinctive wisdom whose every action is guided by his relationship to the soil.

## 主要经历

### Arrival and Settlement

Isak arrives alone, carrying his tools. He builds a turf hut, clears the land, and begins cultivating the soil. His first visitor is a wandering Lapp who asks if he knows of a woman — a question that foreshadows [[inger|Inger]]'s arrival (PN=001-001, PN=001-011, PN=001-014). From the very first pages, Isak's identity is defined by work: he is always in motion, always improving, never idle.

### Marriage to Inger

[[inger|Inger]] arrives in response to an advertisement, and she and Isak form a partnership that grows into a marriage. Together they build [[sellanraa|Sellanraa]] into a prosperous farm (PN=001-039, PN=001-047, PN=002-039). Their partnership is practical and mutual — Isak provides the land and the buildings, Inger provides the domestic labor and the human warmth that transforms a shelter into a home.

### The Copper Mining Episode

When [[mining-in-the-wilderness|mining operations]] begin near [[sellanraa|Sellanraa]], Isak resists the lure of quick money, preferring the steady work of [[tillage-of-the-soil|farming]]. The novel presents his choice as morally correct (PN=008-002, PN=009-002). While others are seduced by the prospect of sudden wealth, Isak continues to dig ditches and break new ground, understanding that mining is temporary but the land is permanent.

## 性格与主题意义

### The Silent Man

Isak speaks little but acts deliberately. His silence is not ignorance but concentration — he is focused entirely on the work before him. Hamsun presents this quality as a virtue, in contrast to the empty talk of townspeople and officials (PN=001-017). When Isak does speak, it is to practical purpose: asking for a woman to help, negotiating a price, giving a simple instruction.

### The Man of the Soil

Isak's identity is inseparable from his land. He eventually takes "[[sellanraa|Sellanraa]]" as his surname, symbolizing the complete merger of person and place, labor and identity (PN=010-017). His physical description reinforces this merger — he is described with an iron beard and a rugged body, as though he were a natural feature of the landscape rather than a man standing apart from it.
