# Breidablik

A farm established near [[sellanraa|Sellanraa]] by [[brede-olsen|Brede Olsen]], representing a contrasting model of pioneer settlement — one that values appearances and social standing over the patient [[tillage-of-the-soil|cultivation]] of the soil. The name "Breidablik" means "broad view" or "wide outlook," an ironic choice given Brede's narrow vision as a settler.

## 地理位置

Breidablik is located near [[sellanraa|Sellanraa]], close enough that the two farms interact regularly. Its proximity creates a natural comparison between [[isak|Isak]]'s and [[brede-olsen|Brede]]'s approaches to pioneer life. The farm sits on less favorable land than Sellanraa, and the road down from Breidablik is described as difficult terrain.

> "The road down from Breidablik, the last part of the way, is none too good." (PN=012-019)

## 所有者

[[brede-olsen|Brede Olsen]], a man of different character than [[isak|Isak]] — more concerned with status and appearances, less dedicated to the soil itself. Brede was originally the Lensmand's assistant before attempting to become a settler. His naming of the farm reveals his ambitions:

> "Breidablik, he calls it. There's wide moorland enough, but no doubt he'll find a use for it all." (PN=011-050)

## 日常生活

Life at Breidablik lacks the steady rhythm of [[sellanraa|Sellanraa]]. The household is less self-sufficient, relying more on purchased goods and outside help. Even small luxuries mark the difference in values:

> "Nils, he gets coffee; down at Breidablik, too, they've coffee." (PN=009-074)

The children at Breidablik are described as having amenities that the Sellanraa children lack:

> "Even the children at Breidablik, it seemed, had each a pair of ski." (PN=013-001)

## 在小说中的角色

Breidablik serves as [[sellanraa|Sellanraa]]'s foil. Where [[isak|Isak]] builds slowly and permanently, [[brede-olsen|Brede]] seeks shortcuts. The contrast reinforces the novel's central argument about the dignity of agricultural labor.

> "To start house in the wilds. Breidablik, he called the place; and it was no great things to look at." (PN=008-079)

When [[brede-olsen|Brede]] eventually fails as a settler, the farm becomes a symbol of what happens when a man tries to work the land without the proper temperament. The farm passes to other hands, completing its arc from ambitious settlement to cautionary tale.

## 关键引文

> "Brede, at Breidablik." (PN=010-026)

This terse identification — Brede defined entirely by his farm's name — captures the novel's philosophy: a man is what his relationship to the land makes him.
