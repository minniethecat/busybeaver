# Animals

The livestock that share the pioneers' lives in Growth of the Soil — animals are not mere property but co-inhabitants of the homestead, their well-being inseparable from the family's survival and prosperity.

## 定义

In Hamsun's novel, animals are the most honest measure of a homestead's success. Isak's wealth is counted not in money but in cows, goats, sheep, and horses. Each new animal — the first goat, the first cow Goldenhorns, the first horse — marks a step in Sellanraa's growth. Animals are living proof that the soil yields increase: grass becomes milk, milk becomes cheese, surplus animals become trade goods. They are the biological intermediaries that transform Isak's labor into tangible wealth.

## 在小说中的表现

### Goldenhorns and the Founding

Goldenhorns, brought by Oline to the new homestead, is Sellanraa's first large animal. Her arrival in Chapter II marks the transition from the barest survival to genuine settlement. When she calves in Chapter III, the event is treated with almost religious solemnity — the first birth at Sellanraa, proof that the land can sustain not just human life but animal generations (003-016):

> And Goldenhorns calved. A great day in the wilderness, a joy and a delight.

### The Horse

The acquisition of a horse represents a qualitative leap in Isak's farming capacity. With a horse, he can plow deeper, haul heavier loads, and extend his cultivated land. The horse is a status symbol as much as a tool — neighbors take note when Isak gets a horse, recognizing that Sellanraa has reached a new level of prosperity (020-XXX).

### Goats and Self-Sufficiency

Goats are the first animals Isak keeps — hardy creatures that can survive on sparse mountain grazing and provide milk, meat, and hide. They represent the beginning of pastoral life at Sellanraa, before the farm is ready for cattle. Isak's goat-feeder, ingeniously designed to release hay at intervals, is one of the novel's many celebrations of practical intelligence applied to animal husbandry (005-025).

### Animals as Moral Test

The novel uses animals as a moral test for its characters. Isak's patient, competent care of his livestock is contrasted with Brede Olsen's neglectful treatment of his own animals. When Isak finds a neglected sheep on Brede's land and nurses it back to health, the novel makes no explicit judgment — but the contrast between the two men's relationship with animals speaks volumes about their respective worth as pioneers.

## 主题意义

Animals embody the novel's ecological vision: humans are not masters of nature but participants in a web of interdependence. The pioneer does not dominate the wilderness — he lives with it, feeds it, is fed by it. The animals at Sellanraa are neither pets nor machines; they are fellow creatures whose well-being coincides with the family's.
