# Copper Mine

The copper mine on Isak's mountain land is a recurring plot element in *Growth of the Soil* — a mineral deposit discovered early in the settlement, sold to Geissler, and later sold onward to Swedish investors. It serves as a foil to the agricultural life, representing the speculative, extractive economy that stands in contrast to Isak's patient cultivation of the soil.

## 地理定位

The copper-bearing stones are found on the mountain above Sellanraa, on a hillside Isak crosses while taking an unfamiliar path to fish. The location is on Isak's own land, though he has no knowledge of its mineral value at the time of discovery [002-010].

## 建立与发展

### 开辟

Isak first encounters the heavy stones by chance during his first year on the land: "Grey rock and brown, and strewed about with bits of heavy stone, heavy as copper or lead. There might be many things in those heavy stones; gold or silver, like as not—he had no knowledge of such things, and did not care" [002-010]. He picks up a couple of the stones and brings them home, where they end up serving as weights for mouse-traps in the larder. It is Geissler who later identifies them: "Copper," he says, after examining the stones with mining experts [008-006].

### 演变

Geissler buys the mining rights from Isak [010-002] and sends samples to experts for analysis: "If there's a decent percentage of copper, you'll be a rich man" [008-030]. The mine changes hands again when Geissler sells to Swedish millionaires, generating significant income [018-045]. Throughout, Isak remains detached from the mining enterprise — his wealth comes from the land transaction, not from mining operations, and he continues farming as before [011-100].

## 叙事功能

The copper mine introduces the theme of speculative wealth versus earned wealth. Isak's riches from the mine sale are passive and almost accidental — in stark contrast to the deliberate, painstaking labor that produces everything else on the farm. The mine also serves as a plot mechanism for Geissler's periodic returns to Sellanraa, each visit tied to some development in the mining business [015-057], [017-055].
