# Harvest

Harvest is the culmination of the agricultural year in Growth of the Soil — the moment when months of plowing, sowing, and tending are rewarded with the grain and hay that will sustain the homestead through winter.

## 定义

In the novel, harvest refers to the gathering of crops — primarily corn (barley) and hay — that constitutes the peak of summer labor at Sellanraa. Harvest is not merely an agricultural event but a moral one: the yield measures the quality of the year's work and the land's response to that work. A failed harvest threatens survival; a good harvest confirms the pioneer's righteousness (005-001):

> Indeed, it almost looked like a providence that the corn harvest had failed — for how could he have threshed it without a barn and threshing-floor?

## 在小说中的表现

### The Rhythm of Intense Labor

Harvest-time is the most demanding period of the year. Isak works from dawn to dusk, mowing, gathering, carting, and stacking. The harvest demands every able body — even Inger helps in the fields despite her domestic duties (013-052). The intensity of harvest labor is presented not as hardship to be pitied but as the natural, even sacred, rhythm of life on the land.

### Harvest as Providence

Isak's relationship to the harvest is framed in religious terms. He sees the success or failure of his crops as connected to a higher order. At one point, Hamsun writes that Isak "had seen God with his own eyes, one night in harvest-time, in the woods" (013-055) — a rare moment of explicit religious experience, significantly set during harvest, the season when the land's response to human labor is most visible.

## 主题意义

Harvest is the book's most concrete expression of the relationship between work and reward. Unlike the speculative economy of copper and trade, harvest yields are directly proportional to labor invested. The harvest scene is also where the community dimension of farming is most visible — neighbors help each other, and the abundance or scarcity of the harvest affects everyone in the settlement.
