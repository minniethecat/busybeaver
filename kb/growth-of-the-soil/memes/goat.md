# Goat

Goats are the first animals Isak brings to the wilderness, arriving before the cow, before the horse, before any neighbour. They provide milk, cheese, and the sound of bells on the hillside — one of the few comforts in the pioneer's early solitude.

## 定义

In the novel's economy, the goat is the foundational animal. Hamsun gives Isak three goats at the very start: "came leading three goats in a leash. He was proud of his goats" (001-007). Where the cow represents settled prosperity and the horse represents status, the goat represents beginnings — hardy, undemanding, and productive from the first day.

## 在小说中的表现

### The First Livestock

Goats appear on Isak's very first journey into the wild. After securing his hut and clearing ground, he returns from the village "leading three goats in a leash" (001-007). The detail is characteristic of Hamsun — not "he bought goats," but the physical image of a man leading them on a rope, visibly proud. Inger, when she arrives, immediately takes over the goats' care: "milked the goats, and scoured pots and things" (001-047), establishing the domestic routine that defines Sellanraa's early days.

### Multiplication and Prosperity

The goats multiply quickly. By Chapter I: "the two she-goats had each had twins, making seven in all" (001-028). The numbers are noted matter-of-factly, the way Isak himself would record them. By Chapter III, the farm has grown to include goats "that were many already" (002-040), and by spring they are "thriving, their heavy udders dragging" (004-003). The progression from three to thriving herd mirrors the farm's own growth.

### Milk and Cheese

Goats' milk is a staple at Sellanraa. Isak offers it to visitors: "some of his goats' milk to drink" (001-046). Inger makes cheese from it: "she made cheese now and then from the goats' milk" (002-069). The surplus becomes trade goods — Isak drives down to the village with "Inger's goats' milk cheeses" (004-045), converting the goats' productivity into goods the farm cannot produce. This cycle — goat to milk to cheese to trade — is the novel's model of self-sufficient economy in miniature.
