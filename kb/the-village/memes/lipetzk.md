# Lipetzk

**Lipetzk** (also Lipetsk) is a village in the Oryol Governorate where [[tikhon-krasoff]] worked in a candle shop during his early years and where several minor characters in the novel originate or reside.

## Location and Geography

Lipetzk lies in the Oryol region, within the broader trading and agricultural network that connects the villages and towns of the novel. The exact distance from Durnovka is not specified, but the village is mentioned alongside [[eletz]] as one of the commercial centers where Tikhon sought work after his release from prison [026-001]. [[akim]] is described as coming from "over Lipetzk way," suggesting the village anchors a broader rural district [032-013].

## Role in the Narrative

Lipetzk appears in the novel as a secondary way-station in the itinerant economy of the countryside. After Tikhon Krasoff's release from prison, he found work in Lipetzk as an employee in a candle shop, part of the patchwork of odd jobs that sustained him before he accumulated capital [026-001].

The village also provides a point of origin for two minor figures. Pankoff, a little old man described as a gardener in Lipetzk, is cited by Kuzma when he speculates about who might teach peasants the skills of orchard-keeping [032-012]. Kuzma interrupts himself to note that Akim — the peasant laborer he encounters at Kazakovo — comes from "over Lipetzk way," reinforcing Lipetzk's role as part of the local geography of peasant life [032-013].

## Key Passages

> After his release from prison, he had carried on the same trade in Eletz, had worked in a candle shop in Lipetzk, had been a clerk on Kasatkin's staff. [026-001]

> Off yonder in our village of Lipetzk there's a little old man, Pankoff, a gardener. [032-012]

> Why, doesn't Akim come from over Lipetzk way? interrupted Kuzma. [032-013]
