# The Schoolhouse

The **schoolhouse** is a whitewashed building standing on the pasture land beyond the village of [[durnovka]]. It gleams in the morning light and, together with a solitary windmill, forms the visual landmark visible from the porch of the manor house. [[kuzma-krasoff]] visits it during his stay at the estate.

## Location and Geography

The schoolhouse stands on a promontory of pasture land beyond Durnovka village. From the porch of the Durnovka manor house, two landmarks dominate the view across the pasture: a single windmill and the schoolhouse, the latter described as gleaming white against the landscape [034-012] [035-001]. The building is reached by a dirt path leading out of the village across open grazing land.

## Role in the Narrative

The schoolhouse appears in Part II as part of Kuzma Krasoff's exploration of the village world. On a fresh, sunny morning after his arrival at Durnovka, Kuzma wanders out to the village and makes his way to the schoolhouse. The visit is brief but significant: the whitewashed building represents one of the few institutional structures in the village landscape, standing in contrast to the pothouse and the threshing-floor as a marker of the civilizing impulse that the novel treats with deep ambivalence.

The morning of Kuzma's visit is described with luminous freshness: the sun rises to the left, behind the Durnovka orchard, and the schoolhouse gleams on the pasture beyond — a moment of visual clarity framed against the broader squalor of village life [035-001]. Kuzma feels "freshened up, sunburnt" as he wanders toward it [035-004].

## Key Passages
