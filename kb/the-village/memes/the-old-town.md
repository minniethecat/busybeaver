# The Old Town

The Old Town is the unnamed provincial center that serves as the commercial and administrative hub of the novel's rural world. It is here that the annual fair draws peasants, merchants, Gypsies, Jews, and gentry into a temporary suspension of normal hierarchies — and where the brutal logic of rural commerce is most nakedly displayed.

## Role in the Narrative

The Old Town appears primarily in the early chapters, during the fair that Tikhon attends. Its geography maps social stratification: the bazaar (commerce), the cathedral (religion), the river and tanyards (industry and pollution), and the Black Suburb (poverty). Tikhon's journey homeward through these zones is a descent through the layers of Russian society.

## Key Passages
