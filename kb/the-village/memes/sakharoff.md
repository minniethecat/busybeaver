## The Highway Encounter

Sakharoff first appears on the highway near Durnovka, strolling with a gun over his shoulder. He is the postmaster at Ulianovka, known for two things: his passion for ordering free price-lists through the mail — catalogues of guns, seeds, and musical instruments — and his brutal treatment of the local peasants.

> "When you pass in a letter, your hands and feet fairly shake!" — the peasants' saying about Sakharoff [014-002]

Tikhon Ilitch spots him and privately mocks him: "A fool of an old man. He slumps along through the mud like an elephant." Yet outwardly Tikhon greets Sakharoff with friendly deference, revealing the performative respect demanded by the postmaster's official position.

## The Post-Office Visit

Kuzma encounters Sakharoff at his post-office in Ulianovka during a bitter winter journey. The post-office is described as a place of profound dreariness — low-ceilinged, smelling of mildew and sealing-wax, with a ragged postman pounding stamps.

Sakharoff is portrayed as a figure of grotesque rage:

> "Grumpy Sakharoff, who resembled a gorilla, was roaring at the peasants, raging because it had not occurred to Kuzma to send him half a dozen fowls or, at least, a pood of flour" [041-005]

After rummaging through his closet, Sakharoff announces with finality: "Nothing for you." The scene reveals how rural officials expected bribes in kind — chickens, flour — and how failure to provide them resulted in petty vengeance. Sakharoff is not a grand villain but a small, mean functionary whose power over communication and commerce makes him a daily torment to the villagers.

## Significance

Sakharoff is a minor character but a significant one in Bunin's portrait of rural Russia. He represents the layer of petty officialdom — the postmaster, the clerk, the local administrator — that stood between the peasantry and the state. His combination of ludicrous vanity (the catalogues) and casual cruelty (the roaring, the bribe-extraction) embodies the corruption and coarseness of provincial life. He is neither evil nor exceptional — merely a man whose small authority has made him monstrous.
