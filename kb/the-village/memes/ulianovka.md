Ulianovka is a village situated along the highway near Durnovka, notable as the site of the local post-office and a waypoint for travellers on the Vorgol road. It lies on the route connecting the Krasoff estate to the broader region, and its inhabitants include several minor but vividly drawn figures whose paths intersect with the Krasoff brothers.

## Location & Geography

Ulianovka sits on the metalled highway running southward from Vorgol, bordered by a bare forest from which travellers sometimes emerge onto the road [014-011]. The village is close enough to Durnovka that peasants regularly travel between the two, and its post-office serves as the postal hub for the surrounding district [041-005]. The railway line from the southeast intersects the highway near a watchtower visible from the Krasoff property, with Ulianovka lying along this route [008-001].

## Role in the Narrative

Ulianovka appears at several key moments in the novel, often as a point of transit or communication. Tikhon Ilitch encounters travellers and news from Ulianovka along the highway, where he stops peasants driving to the village [008-001]. The village is also associated with political ferment: the deacon's son, a student at the Theological Seminary, declares himself a Social-Democrat — a fact that irritates Tikhon Ilitch amid the broader revolutionary unrest [007-005].

The post-office at Ulianovka, run by the brutish Sakharoff, serves as a minor but telling setting for Kuzma's isolation during his winter sojourn at Durnovka. His visit to collect mail ends in disappointment and cold indifference [041-005]. Rodka, the sullen young peasant who becomes entangled in the Durnovka conflicts, originally hails from Ulianovka [010-001].
