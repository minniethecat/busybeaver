# Eletz

**Eletz** (also Yelets) is a real trading town in the Oryol region, serving as a commercial way-station for [[tikhon-krasoff]]'s early working life and a geographical reference point for several anecdotes that characters recount in the novel.

## Location and Geography

Eletz is a district town in the Oryol Governorate, situated within the trading network that connects Durnovka to larger commercial centers. The town serves as a regional hub for drovers, merchants, and small traders who move goods through the countryside. The precise distance from Durnovka is not stated, but the frequent references to travel between Eletz and other locations suggest it lies within a few days' journey by cart or on foot.

## Role in the Narrative

Eletz functions primarily as a waypoint in the economic geography of Tikhon Krasoff's early career. As a young man, Tikhon spent a week working as an assistant to a drover near Eletz before heading to Moscow on his employer's business [025-001]. Later in life, after his release from prison, he continued similar trade in Eletz, working in a candle shop and as a clerk for the grain merchant Kasatkin [026-001].

The town also surfaces in the darker anecdotes that circulate among the characters. A laborer who had been employed at a distillery near Eletz fell into the malt-kiln, was dragged out by his fellows, and died the following day — a story that Kuzma recalls with grim detail [037-001]. Akim mentions staying at the house of a priest near Eletz when he was ill, and reflects that he might now be on his feet if he had remained there [037-022]. In one of the novel's most violent episodes, Deniska recounts what happened at the farm of the Bykoff brothers near Eletz — an incident involving the ruthless beatings of laborers [045-010].

## Key Passages

> Tikhon Ilitch spent a week as assistant to a drover near Eletz, went to Moscow on his employer's business, then went to another employer... [025-001]

> After his release from prison, he had carried on the same trade in Eletz, had worked in a candle shop in Lipetzk, had been a clerk on Kasatkin's staff. [026-001]

> A laborer had entered a distillery near Eletz, fallen into the malt-kiln while shoveling grain, been dragged out by his comrades... and had died on the following day. [037-001]

> Had you heard what happened near Eletz? At the farm of the Bykoff brothers? A laborer there... [045-010]
