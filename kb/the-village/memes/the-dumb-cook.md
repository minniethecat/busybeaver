# The Dumb Cook

The dumb cook is the novel's most silent character — literally and figuratively. She cannot speak, and Bunin gives her no thoughts, no feelings, no name. She exists as a body that serves, a body that is used, a body that bears a child. Tikhon's rationale — "A dumb woman can't betray anything with her tongue" (002-002) — reveals the convenience of her disability: her silence guarantees his impunity.

## Biography / Role in the Novel

The cook lives with Tikhon for a long time, bears his child, and remains unnamed throughout. She is the most extreme example of Bunin's treatment of female characters as sites of suffering rather than agents of action. Her muteness is not a character trait but a narrative condition: she cannot speak because the world she inhabits has no language for her experience.
