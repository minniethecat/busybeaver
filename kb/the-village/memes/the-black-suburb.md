# The Black Suburb

The Black Suburb (*Chornaya Sloboda*) is the poorest quarter of the Old Town, lying beyond the river and the tanyards. Its name comes from the black soot and industrial filth that coat its buildings — a permanent marker of the poverty that defines its inhabitants. In Tikhon's journey homeward, the Black Suburb is the final zone before the countryside: the last and lowest stratum of urban society.

## Role in the Narrative

Bunin maps social class onto urban geography with precision. The bazaar and cathedral occupy the town center; the river and tanyards form an industrial belt; and beyond them lies the Black Suburb, where the poorest laborers live. This spatial ordering makes visible the hierarchies that structure the novel's social world.
