# The Cemetery

The cemetery on the edge of Durnovka is one of the novel's few spaces of genuine peace. When Tikhon visits the family graves, he encounters inscriptions that "spoke touchingly and peacefully of rest, repose; of tenderness to the dear departed" (005-016). In a novel where the living offer each other little tenderness, the dead provide what the living cannot.

## Role in the Narrative

The cemetery scene is a turning point for Tikhon — a moment when the hard-nosed merchant is moved by the memory of his dead relatives. The cemetery is a threshold between the present and the past, the living and the dead, commerce and contemplation.

## Key Passages

> "He walked to the far corner of the cemetery where all his relatives were buried — father, mother, the sister who had died when a little girl."
> — (005-016)
