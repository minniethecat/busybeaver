# Ivanushka

Ivanushka belongs to the ancestral past of the Krasoff family — the generation that lived and died as serfs, whose experiences shaped the world Tikhon and Kuzma inherited. The name itself — the diminutive, affectionate form of Ivan — suggests both intimacy and diminishment, the way the serf is at once a member of the family and less than fully human.

## Biography / Role in the Novel

Ivanushka is referenced in connection with the Krasoff family history, part of the ancestral burden that the brothers carry. Like the great-grandfather hunted by Durnovo's wolfhounds, Ivanushka represents the generation for whom serfdom was not a memory but a lived reality — and whose suffering is the unspoken substrate of the novel's action.
