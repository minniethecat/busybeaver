# The Bathhouse

The **bathhouse** is an old structure in the orchard of the [[durnovka|Durnovka estate]] where [[kuzma-krasoff]] spends a night during his spring visit to the manor. It stands at the farthest end of the orchard, surrounded by apple trees, and serves as a place of nocturnal refuge and sensory immersion.

## Location and Geography

The bathhouse lies deep within the garden of the Durnovka estate, situated at the very end of the orchard where the farthest apple trees stand [032-025]. It is described as old and somewhat dilapidated — a wooden building that has long since lost its original purpose as a steam-bath and now serves as a makeshift sleeping quarters for visitors. The orchard path leading to it passes through overgrown fruit trees, making it a secluded spot well removed from the main house.

## Role in the Narrative

When Kuzma Krasoff arrives at Durnovka in Part II, the superintendent arranges for him to sleep not in the manor house but in the garden: "You will spend the night in the garden, in the old bathhouse," the superintendent announces upon Kuzma's arrival [031-001].

That night, returning from his excursion to the village after midnight, Kuzma makes his way through the pitch-dark orchard. The path is barely discernible: he finds his way to the bathhouse "by the sense of feeling alone" [032-027]. Once inside, the darkness is so profound that he cannot see his own hand. As he lies there, the eastern sky begins to flush with a faint light, and slowly the shape of the apple trees framing the bathhouse resolves against it — the low-hanging canopy of boughs, the grey vault of foliage, all gradually taking form as dawn approaches [032-025]. The passage is one of the novel's most atmospherically rendered moments of sensory perception.

## Key Passages

> You will spend the night in the garden, in the old bathhouse, said the superintendent. [031-001]

> It grew faintly flushed in the east, and in the air, in the garden, all was motionless; the apple trees, the farthest of which were visible, where stood the bathhouse, and suddenly inundated everything with their grey vault of foliage. [032-025]

> Kuzma had found his way to the bathhouse by the sense of feeling alone. [032-027]
