# Balashkin

Balashkin is a minor but significant figure in Kuzma Krasoff's intellectual world — one of the educated Russians who debate the "peasant question" from a distance, without living among the peasants or sharing their suffering. He represents the intelligentsia's abstract engagement with the very realities that Kuzma experiences directly.

## Biography / Role in the Novel

Balashkin appears in conversations with Kuzma, offering theories about the Russian peasantry that Kuzma's own experience both confirms and complicates. His function in the novel is to dramatize the gap between intellectual understanding and lived experience — a gap that Kuzma, the self-educated peasant, inhabits painfully.
