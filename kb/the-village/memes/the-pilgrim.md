# The Pilgrim

The pilgrim (*strannik*) who appears in *The Village* belongs to a distinctively Russian tradition: the religious wanderer who has renounced fixed habitation for a life of perpetual movement and prayer. In a novel where most characters are trapped — by poverty, by land, by family, by fate — the pilgrim represents the possibility of freedom through renunciation.
