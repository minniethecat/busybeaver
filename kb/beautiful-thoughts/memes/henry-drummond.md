# Henry Drummond

## Biography

Henry Drummond (1851–1897) was a Scottish evangelist, biologist, writer, and lecturer. He served as professor of natural science at the Free Church College in Glasgow and was a key figure in the Moody and Sankey revival movement. His unique contribution was bridging the gap between science and religion—arguing that spiritual laws parallel natural laws.

## Connection to Beautiful Thoughts

*Beautiful Thoughts* (1898) is a posthumous collection of brief selections from Drummond's writings, arranged as daily readings by Elizabeth Cureton. The readings distill Drummond's key ideas from his major works into daily devotional form.

## Key Works

- *Natural Law in the Spiritual World* (1883)
- *The Greatest Thing in the World* (1890)
- *Pax Vobiscum* (1890)
- *The Programme of Christianity* (1891)
- *The Changed Life* (1891)

## Key Ideas

Drummond's central thesis is that the spiritual world operates according to laws as consistent as those of the natural world. Key concepts include:

- **Love** as the supreme law of the spiritual world
- **Growth** as the natural process of spiritual development
- **Environment** as essential for spiritual life
- **Correspondence** between organism and environment
