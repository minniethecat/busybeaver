## Overview

Dr. Kenneth is the local physician of [[Gimmerton]], who appears intermittently throughout *Wuthering Heights* to tend to the various illnesses and injuries suffered by the residents of both [[Wuthering-Heights]] and [[Thrushcross-Grange]]. Though a minor character, his medical pronouncements often carry narrative weight — his warnings about the severity of illnesses, his blunt assessments of patients' conditions, and his practical interventions serve as objective markers of the physical toll that emotional turmoil exacts upon the characters. He is one of the few figures in the novel who moves freely between the two estates, unentangled in their feuds.

## Biography

Dr. Kenneth is first mentioned during Catherine Earnshaw's stay at Thrushcross Grange, where he tends to her after she is bitten by the Lintons' bulldog. He treats her fever and prescribes quiet and rest, directing his instructions through Nelly (008-004/008-010).

His most significant medical role comes during Catherine's final illness. When called to examine her, Dr. Kenneth immediately pronounces her condition dangerous — "as soon as he saw her, he pronounced her in a state of incipient brain fever" (009-124). His diagnosis of delirium proves accurate, and he issues "serious threats of a fit" that keep those around her cautious (009-126). Despite his best efforts, Catherine never fully recovers before her death in childbirth.

Dr. Kenneth later attends to the younger Catherine, whom Nelly mentions almost gave up hope for: "the terrible intimation of Kenneth that I need not expect to be with her long" (010-001). He also treats Hindley during his decline and is present at key moments of crisis, such as when he "was fortunately just issuing" from his house when urgently needed (012-062).

## Relationships

**Nelly Dean** — Dr. Kenneth communicates primarily through Nelly, who relays his medical instructions to the households. He speaks to her with professional candor, and his warnings carry weight within the narrative.

**Catherine Earnshaw** — His most famous patient. Dr. Kenneth diagnoses her brain fever and correctly assesses the danger she is in, though his medical expertise can do little against the self-destructive passion consuming her.

**Hindley Earnshaw** — Dr. Kenneth attends to Hindley during his alcoholic decline, witnessing firsthand the physical destruction wrought by grief and drink.

**Heathcliff** — When Catherine is ill, Heathcliff interrogates Nelly about the doctor's opinion, revealing his desperate concern. Later, Nelly recalls having "just crammed Kenneth, head-downmost, in the Black-horse marsh" — an odd remark suggesting some form of professional rivalry or dark humor about the doctor's limitations (009-002).
