## Overview

Linton Heathcliff is the only child of [[Heathcliff]] and [[Isabella Linton]], born after his mother fled her abusive marriage and raised in the south of England, far from [[Wuthering-Heights]]. He embodies the worst qualities of both his parents: his mother's physical frailty and his father's capacity for selfish manipulation, without the redeeming passion or courage of either. Brought to Yorkshire as a teenager after his mother's death, Linton becomes his father's most pliable instrument — a weak, sickly boy whose marriage to the younger [[Catherine-Linton]] completes Heathcliff's legal claim to [[Thrushcross-Grange]]. His brief, unhappy life and lingering death represent the terminal decay of the Heathcliff-Linton line.

## Biography

Linton is born in the south of England after Isabella flees her catastrophic marriage to Heathcliff. She raises him alone, and he grows up as a pampered, delicate child, cosseted by his mother and unused to hardship. Isabella dies when Linton is about thirteen, at which point Heathcliff — Linton's legal father — claims him and brings him to Wuthering Heights.

At the Heights, Linton is utterly out of place. He is weak, peevish, and terrified of his father. Heathcliff regards him with contempt — as nothing more than a legal instrument. "Linton Heathcliff: answers to documents forwarded" (021-098) captures his status: he exists not as a son but as a claim to property. Heathcliff forces him to correspond with and woo the younger Catherine Linton, exploiting Linton's fear and Catherine's compassion.

The scheme culminates in Linton's forced marriage to Cathy: "Catherine, Mrs. Linton Heathcliff now, was suffered to stay at the Heights only on sufferance" (028-036). The marriage gives Heathcliff legal claim to Thrushcross Grange upon the death of [[Edgar Linton]]. Linton himself dies shortly after — weak, unloved, and unmourned — having served his father's purpose. As Nelly observes with weary resignation after his death, even a friendship between herself and the widowed Cathy would have been "for Mrs. Linton Heathcliff, had she and I struck up an alliance" (031-040) — the name "Linton Heathcliff" now a mark of Cathy's captivity rather than a living presence.

## Relationships

**Heathcliff** — Linton's father, who views him with undisguised contempt. Heathcliff uses Linton purely as a tool to secure his claim to Thrushcross Grange, showing no paternal affection whatsoever. Linton, in turn, is terrified of his father and too weak to resist his commands.

**Isabella Linton** — Linton's mother, who raised him alone in the south. Her death severs Linton's only nurturing bond and delivers him into Heathcliff's hands. Isabella's legacy to her son was her frailty — but not, tragically, her courage to flee.

**Catherine Linton (younger)** — Linton's cousin and wife. Cathy initially pities Linton and is drawn to him out of compassion, but his cowardly complicity in Heathcliff's scheme destroys any genuine affection. Their marriage is a legal fiction, not a union.

**Edgar Linton** — Linton's uncle, who attempts to protect him after Isabella's death but is outmaneuvered by Heathcliff. Edgar sees in Linton a remnant of his lost sister and tries, unsuccessfully, to claim guardianship.

**Nelly Dean** — Nelly observes Linton with a mixture of pity and exasperation, recognizing his weakness but also his moral failure in betraying Cathy's trust.
