# Themes and Concepts

This page lists the major themes, motifs, symbols, and conceptual frameworks
that literary scholarship has identified in *Wuthering Heights* (1847).
Emily Brontë's novel is dense with recurring ideas: the destructive nature of
passionate love, the tension between civilization and wildness, social class
and inheritance, revenge and its corrosive effects, and the supernatural
permeating the natural world.

## Notes

- Concepts range from major literary themes (love, revenge, death) to specific Gothic motifs (ghosts, dreams, weather).
- Each concept page traces the theme's development across multiple chapters with direct textual evidence.
