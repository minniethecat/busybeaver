# Social Order

The social world of *Wuthering Heights* (1847) is rigidly stratified yet permeable
at its edges. The Earnshaws and Lintons occupy the gentry tier of Yorkshire society;
their servants — Nelly Dean, Joseph, Zillah — mediate between upstairs and downstairs.
Heathcliff, the Liverpool foundling, enters as an outsider and exits as the master of
both estates, his life a violent traversal of every rung on the social ladder.

## Notes

Categories are not mutually exclusive — a character may appear in multiple sections.
The novel's social taxonomy is itself a subject of critical debate: Heathcliff is
simultaneously outsider, servant (briefly), gentry landowner, and revenant.
