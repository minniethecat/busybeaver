## Overview

Mr. Green is a lawyer in [[Gimmerton]] who appears in the final chapters of *Wuthering Heights* as a legal facilitator of [[Heathcliff]]'s complete takeover of the Earnshaw-Linton estates. Though he appears only briefly and speaks few lines, his role is structurally significant: he represents the legal machinery that enables Heathcliff's final consolidation of power over [[Thrushcross-Grange]], demonstrating how the law can be wielded as an instrument of revenge.

## Biography

Mr. Green is first mentioned when a servant is sent to fetch him on an urgent matter concerning [[Edgar-Linton]]'s will. The servant returns to report that "Mr. Green, the lawyer, was out when he arrived at his house, and he had to wait for his coming in" (028-029). This delay proves consequential — by the time Mr. Green arrives, Edgar has already died and Heathcliff has begun to assert control.

When Mr. Green finally appears, he finds the situation at the Grange already settled in Heathcliff's favor. Rather than contesting the new order, "Mr. Green took upon himself to order everything" (028-036), effectively ratifying Heathcliff's authority. His professional detachment in the face of the family's tragedy underscores the cold efficiency with which legal institutions can serve those with power.

In the novel's closing chapter, [[Nelly-Dean]] reflects on the final settlement: Heathcliff dismisses any concern about Mr. Green's legal work, saying "never mind Mr. Green" when the topic of repentance and justice is raised (034-053).
