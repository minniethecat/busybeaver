# Gothic and Supernatural Index

*Wuthering Heights* (1847) is a novel saturated with the Gothic inheritance.
Catherine's ghost claws at Lockwood's window in Chapter 3; Heathcliff digs up her
grave; characters are haunted by visions, dreams, and the literal return of the dead.
This index aggregates every wiki page tagged with `gothic`, `supernatural`, `ghosts`,
or `horror`, providing a map of the novel's dark romantic architecture.

## Notes

The Gothic in *Wuthering Heights* is not decorative but structural: ghosts, graves,
and haunted houses externalise the psychological violence of passion, grief, and
revenge. Emily Brontë transmutes the Gothic machinery of Ann Radcliffe and M. G. Lewis
into a Yorkshire vernacular — the supernatural is never quite confirmed, never quite
denied, suspended in the testimony of unreliable narrators.
