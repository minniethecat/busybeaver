# Family Hub

The dynastic structure of *Wuthering Heights* (1847) spans two generations across three
intertwined families. The Earnshaws of Wuthering Heights, the Lintons of Thrushcross Grange,
and the Heathcliff line — created when the foundling Heathcliff is brought into the Earnshaw
household — form a closed genealogical system that drives the novel's drama of inheritance,
revenge, and resolution.

## Notes

Family boundaries in *Wuthering Heights* are fluid — marriages cross the two houses,
and Heathcliff's acquisition of both properties merges the families into a single
patrimonial structure by the novel's end. Characters may appear in multiple sections
above if tagged with more than one family affiliation.
