# Places and Locations

This page lists all significant locations in *Wuthering Heights* (1847).
The novel's geography is starkly bipolar: the wild moorland farmhouse of Wuthering Heights
and the cultivated valley estate of Thrushcross Grange define the emotional and social
landscape of the story, with Gimmerton village and the surrounding moors completing the setting.

## Notes

- The Yorkshire moors form the atmospheric and symbolic backdrop of the entire novel.
- Some locations (e.g., individual rooms within Wuthering Heights) are described within their parent location's page rather than having separate entries.
