## Overview

Zillah is the housekeeper at [[Wuthering-Heights]] during the period of [[Heathcliff]]'s mastery, replacing [[Nelly Dean]] in that role after Nelly moves to [[Thrushcross-Grange]]. Unlike Nelly, Zillah is a largely unsympathetic, matter-of-fact presence — she does her work, minds her own business, and shows little inclination to intervene in the cruelties and dramas unfolding around her. She serves as a narrative device: her detachment highlights the harsh, loveless atmosphere of the Heights under Heathcliff's rule, contrasting starkly with the warmth and moral engagement that Nelly brought to the household.

## Biography

Zillah first appears when Lockwood, stranded by a snowstorm, is brought into Wuthering Heights for the night. She is described as "the stout housewife" who leads him to his chamber (002-088) and, at Heathcliff's instruction, provides him with a glass of brandy (002-091). Her manner is brusque but efficient — she does what is required and nothing more.

When Lockwood returns the next morning, Zillah is already at work: "the females were already astir; Zillah urging flakes of flame up the chimney" (003-060). She is one of the five members of the household Lockwood names: "Heathcliff, Earnshaw, Zillah, Joseph and I" (002-070) — a bare, unhomely enumeration that captures the coldness of life at the Heights.

In the later chapters, Zillah appears as a minor but consistent presence. During the younger [[Catherine-Linton]]'s forced residence at Wuthering Heights, Zillah shows occasional flashes of sympathy — fetching assistance when Cathy is frightened — but largely maintains her policy of non-involvement. Catherine complains of her unreliability: "Zillah is constantly gadding off to Gimmerton" (023-014), and at another point cries out for help, "called for Zillah, as loud as I could" (024-040), only for Zillah to eventually respond with characteristic slowness.

## Relationships

**Heathcliff** — Zillah's master. She obeys his orders without question, including the unenviable task of hosting his unwelcome guests. She neither fears nor admires him; she simply works for him.

**Nelly Dean** — Zillah replaces Nelly as housekeeper at Wuthering Heights after Nelly moves to Thrushcross Grange. The contrast between the two women — Nelly's moral engagement versus Zillah's pragmatic detachment — underscores the degradation of life at the Heights. Where Nelly would intervene, Zillah looks away.

**Catherine Linton (younger)** — Zillah is one of the few women in Cathy's confined world at the Heights, but offers little in the way of comfort or solidarity. Cathy's complaints about Zillah's absences reflect her isolation.

**Joseph** — Zillah's fellow servant at the Heights, equally unyielding but far more religiously fanatical. Together they form the unhomely domestic staff of Heathcliff's household.
