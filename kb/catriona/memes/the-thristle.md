# The Thristle

**_The Thristle_** is a merchant vessel mentioned early in the novel _Catriona_. She is captained by Andie Scougal and is engaged for the purpose of transporting Alan Breck Stewart to France, where he can escape the legal consequences of the Appin Murder.

## Role in the Novel

The _Thristle_ appears in the background of the novel's opening chapters as part of the network of ships that facilitate Alan Breck's evasion of the authorities. When discussing potential vessels for Alan's escape, Rob Macgregor (Robin Oig) mentions the ship to David Balfour:

> "There'll be Andie Scougal, in the _Thristle_," replied Rob. "I saw Hoseason the other day, but it seems he's wanting the ship. Then there'll be Tam Stobo; but I'm none so sure of Tam."[^1]

This passage reveals the informal maritime network on which Jacobite fugitives relied. Ship captains like Andie Scougal, Hoseason, and Tam Stobo formed a web of potential transport, with varying degrees of reliability. Rob's caution about Tam Stobo — "I've seen him colloguing with some gey queer acquaintances" — suggests that the choice of vessel could be a matter of life and death for a wanted man like Alan Breck.

## Connection to Andie Scougal

The _Thristle_'s captain, Andie Scougal, is also known as "Black Andie" — a sailor who later reappears in the novel as the keeper of David Balfour's prison on the Bass Rock. The dual role of ship captains in the novel (as both merchants and potential accomplices to fugitives) reflects the historical reality of 18th-century Scottish coastal life, where the line between legitimate trade and clandestine activity was often blurred.

## Maritime Context

The _Thristle_ belongs to the category of merchant vessels operating out of the Firth of Forth in the mid-18th century. Leith, the port of Edinburgh, was a busy commercial harbour with regular traffic to the Low Countries, France, and Scandinavia. Ships like the _Thristle_ were typically small sailing vessels — brigs or snows — capable of crossing the North Sea. The name "Thristle" (a Scots variant of "thistle") connects the vessel to Scottish national identity, the thistle being the national emblem of Scotland.

[^1]: Catriona, ch-02: "There'll be Andie Scougal, in the *Thristle*," replied Rob. "I saw Hoseason the other day, but it seems he's wanting the ship. Then there'll be Tam Stobo; but I'm none so sure of Tam. I've seen him colloguing with some gey queer acquaintances; and if was anybody important, I would give Tam the go-by."
