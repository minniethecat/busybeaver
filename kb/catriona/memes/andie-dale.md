# Andie Dale

**Andie Dale** (also called **Black Andie**) is the keeper of the Bass Rock in the novel _Catriona_. He is a sailor turned warden — or "Prefect," as David Balfour jocularly calls him — of the isolated island prison in the Firth of Forth.

## Role as Bass Rock Keeper

When David is seized and transported to the Bass Rock, he finds Andie Dale serving as the island's sole resident warden. Andie is "at once the shepherd and the gamekeeper of that small and rich estate."[^1] His duties include tending the dozen or so sheep that graze on the slopes of the rock and managing the vast colony of solan geese, from which "an extraordinary income is derived." The young geese are sold as a delicacy — "as much as two shillings a-piece being a common price, and paid willingly by epicures" — while the grown birds provide oil and feathers.[^1]

Andie's tenure on the Bass is familial: his father, Tam Dale, had served as a soldier in the garrison of the old fortress before him. This history gives Andie an intimate knowledge of the rock, "down to the names of private soldiers."[^2]

## Relationship with David Balfour

Despite being David's jailer, Andie develops a genuine rapport with his prisoner. David describes him as "gifted besides with a natural genius for narration, so that the people seemed to speak and the things to be done before your face."[^2] Andie tells David the history of the Bass, including the harrowing tale of his father Tam Dale's service there during the days of the Covenanters' persecution, and the supernatural story of Peden the Prophet.

David sets out deliberately to win Andie's goodwill, and succeeds. He reflects: "I could not honestly deny but what I liked him; I soon saw that he liked me; and indeed, from the first I had set myself out to capture his good-will."[^2]

When David attempts to bribe Andie with a paper from the British Linen Company to secure his release, Andie is tempted but honest. He admits the arrangement looks "unco underhand" and that, were the authorities not "guid sound Whigs and true-blue Presbyterians," he "would has seen them ayont Jordan and Jeroozlem" before taking part in it.[^3] Ultimately, Andie levelly tells David that there will be no harm to David from remaining on the Bass, no harm to the country from "just ae mair Hielantman hangit," but considerable harm to Andie himself if he lets David go. He concludes: "the plain fact is that I think ye'll just have to bide here wi' Andie an' the solans."[^4]

## Connection to Alan Breck's Escape

Andie Dale also appears earlier in the novel as the captain of the ship _Thristle_. Rob Macgregor mentions him as a potential transport for Alan Breck: "There'll be Andie Scougal, in the _Thristle_."[^5] This dual identity — merchant captain and Bass Rock keeper — places Andie within the network of maritime operators who could either assist or endanger Jacobite fugitives.
