# Cluny Macpherson

**Cluny Macpherson** (Ewen MacPherson of Cluny, 1706–1764) was the chief of Clan Macpherson and a prominent Jacobite leader who appears in the background of both _Kidnapped_ and _Catriona_. He is most notable in Stevenson's narrative for having sheltered Alan Breck Stewart and David Balfour in his famous hiding-place, known as Cluny's Cage, on the slopes of Ben Alder.

## Historical Role

Cluny Macpherson fought on the Jacobite side during the Rising of 1745, leading his clan at the Battle of Prestonpans and at Falkirk. After the defeat at Culloden in 1746, he became one of the most famous Jacobite fugitives, evading capture for nine years in the Highlands. His most celebrated hiding-place was "Cluny's Cage" — a cleverly concealed shelter on Ben Alder, constructed of wood and camouflaged with heather and moss, where he lived for much of this period.

Despite a reward of £1,000 on his head, Cluny was never betrayed by his clansmen. He eventually escaped to France in 1755, where he died in exile in 1764.

## In Catriona

In _Catriona_, Cluny appears only through references. David Balfour recalls a financial debt: "The next thing is the bit money I am owing to Cluny... It was two pounds five shillings and three-halfpence farthing sterling."[^1] This small sum — borrowed during David and Alan's flight through the Highlands in _Kidnapped_ — speaks to the role Cluny played in supporting fugitives during the years of persecution after Culloden.

Later in the novel, James More mentions "Cluny's Treasure" — a reference to the money that Cluny had hidden during his years as a fugitive, and which was a source of ongoing intrigue among the Jacobite community in exile.[^2] The treasure was both a practical resource and a symbol of the continuing Jacobite cause, though in James More's hands the reference takes on a characteristically mercenary cast.

## Connection to Alan Breck

Cluny's relationship with Alan Breck Stewart was one of mutual respect among Jacobite fugitives. In _Kidnapped_, the episode at Cluny's Cage on Ben Alder is a pivotal moment — the last safe haven before Alan and David make their final dash to the Lowlands and to David's recovery of his inheritance. Cluny's hospitality, his skill at the card-table (which leads to Alan losing all his money), and his dignified endurance of hardship create a memorable portrait of the Jacobite chief in exile.

[^2]: Catriona, ch-29: "The plain fact is that it is Cluny's Treasure."
