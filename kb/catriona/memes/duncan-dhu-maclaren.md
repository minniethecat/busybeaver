# Duncan Dhu Maclaren

Duncan Dhu Maclaren is a Highland ally of David Balfour and Alan Breck Stewart, mentioned in the early chapters of Robert Louis Stevenson's *Catriona* as part of the network of friends and contacts that sustains the Jacobite fugitives. He represents the Highland world beyond the novel's immediate action — a reminder that the events in Edinburgh are embedded in a larger geography of clan loyalty and mutual obligation.

## Role in the Story

Duncan Dhu first appears in David's account of his Highland contacts: "a very honest, kind man called Duncan Dhu Maclaren" (001-019). This brief mention establishes him as part of the social network that David and Alan navigated in *Kidnapped* — a network that continues to matter in *Catriona* even though the novel's action has shifted from the heather to the courtroom.

Duncan Dhu is typical of Stevenson's Highland minor characters: mentioned briefly, but carrying significant thematic weight. His name — "Dhu" meaning "black" or "dark" in Gaelic — is a traditional Highland epithet, and his presence in David's narrative reminds the reader that the legal drama unfolding in Edinburgh is only one surface of a much larger story, one that extends into the glens and villages where men like Duncan Dhu still live by the old codes.

## Biography

### Background

Duncan Dhu Maclaren is a Highland clansman of the Maclaren family, living in the district of Balquhidder in Perthshire. He is known to David Balfour from their shared experiences during the events of *Kidnapped*, when David and Alan Breck passed through Maclaren country during their flight through the Highlands.

### Key Actions

- He provides hospitality and assistance to David and Alan during their flight through the Highlands in *Kidnapped*, contributing to the network of Highland allies who make their survival possible.

- His name is invoked by David in Edinburgh as a credential — proof that David is not merely a Lowland outsider but a man who has earned the trust of Highland clansmen.

### Fate

Duncan Dhu's fate is not addressed in *Catriona*. He remains in the Highlands, a representative of the world David has left behind but to which Alan Breck still belongs.
