Neil Roy Macgregor, also known as Neil of the Tom or Neil Duncanson, is James More Macgregor's
red-headed Highland retainer. He appears throughout the novel as a messenger, escort, and
enforcer for James More, bridging the Edinburgh legal world with the Highlands' Jacobite
underworld.

## Role in the Story

Neil serves as the principal physical link between David Balfour and the Highland Jacobite
network. He is James More's most trusted servant and courier, carrying messages between
Edinburgh and the Highlands, and is entrusted with the task of escorting Catriona on her
travels. His presence signals the intrusion of Highland clan politics into the Lowland
legal proceedings — whenever the "red head of Neil" appears, trouble follows
(008-028).

Beyond his practical function as messenger, Neil embodies the theme of divided loyalties
that pervades the novel. He is outwardly obsequious to David, James More, and Catriona,
but his true allegiance is ambiguous — he reports to James More while serving Catriona,
and his errands in Edinburgh hint at machinations that affect all parties (010-058).

## Biography

### Background

Neil is introduced as "Neil, son of Duncan" — one of James More Macgregor's men
(001-041). He is a Macgregor clansman, which places him among the proscribed Highland
clan whose name was banned following the 1745 Rising. His epithet "of the Tom" (008-028)
indicates a specific farmstead or dwelling in the Highlands.

### Key Actions

When Catriona first appears at David's door seeking help, Neil is sent away with money
and instructions to wait — a sign of his subordinate but trusted role (001-042).

Neil emerges as the key intermediary after James More's arrest. He takes Catriona from
the hands of her father's enemies and escorts her to safety, while simultaneously
maintaining communication with James More in prison. David encounters him repeatedly in
Edinburgh in the company of suspicious Highland recruits, and Neil's obsequious manner
masks a watchful intelligence (010-060, 010-063).

During the journey northward, Neil serves as guide and translator. He is "the only one"
among the Highland party capable of conversing in English, positioning him as the
cultural bridge between David's Lowland sensibilities and the Gaelic-speaking Highlands
(015-001). At the journey's end, Neil's role shifts from escort to potential threat
as his split loyalties become evident (013-054).

### Fate

Neil's ultimate fate is left ambiguous. He fades from the narrative as David and
Catriona escape the Jacobite orbit, his last appearances showing him among the
Highland party that collected at the journey's terminus (015-001, 015-002). His
utility as James More's instrument ends when James More's own schemes unravel.

  absolute loyalty, though Neil is shrewd enough to serve his own interests alongside
- **[[catriona-drummond]]** — Neil is assigned as Catriona's escort and protector
  during her travels; their relationship is one of duty rather than affection
- **[[david-balfour]]** — David mistrusts Neil from first encounter, seeing him as

  hostility
- **[[simon-fraser-of-lovat]]** — Fellow Jacobite operative; Neil and Simon Fraser
  are both "James More's men" and coordinate their efforts (013-046)

## Historical Note

Neil Roy Macgregor is a composite fictional character of a type familiar in
eighteenth-century Highland society: the trusted retainer of a clan chief, operating
in the gray zone between servant, messenger, and armed follower. The proscription of
the Macgregor name (following the 1603 Battle of Glen Fruin and renewed after 1745)
meant that men like Neil lived perpetually outside the law, their status defined by
personal loyalty rather than legal standing.

Stevenson's portrait captures the duality of the Highland retainer: Neil is both
comically red-headed and genuinely dangerous, both "obsequious" (010-063) and capable
of violence. His mastery of both Gaelic and English makes him indispensable to the
Jacobite communication network that drives much of the novel's plot machinery.
