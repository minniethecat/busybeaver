# Mr. Rankeillor

**Mr. Rankeillor** (full name: **Mr. Hope of Rankeillor**) is a Writer (solicitor) in Queensferry, who serves as the legal agent for the Shaws estate and appears as a trusted ally of David Balfour in both _Kidnapped_ and _Catriona_.

## Role in David's Affairs

Rankeillor is first introduced in _Kidnapped_ as the lawyer who helps David Balfour reclaim his inheritance from his uncle, Ebenezer Balfour. He is a shrewd, practical man of business who is nevertheless capable of recognising justice and acting upon it. In _Catriona_, Rankeillor continues to serve as David's legal representative and adviser.

David's first act upon arriving in Edinburgh is to visit Rankeillor's son, and he carries with him the reputation that Rankeillor has given him. Rankeillor's letter of recommendation proves crucial — when David approaches Mr. Balfour of Pilrig for assistance, the laird reads Rankeillor's letter and immediately places himself "obligingly at my disposal."[^1] Mr. Balfour notes: "I have Rankeillor's word for it, and I count that a warran-dice against all deadly."[^2]

## Connection to the Grant Family

Rankeillor is also connected to the Grant family, knowing Miss Grant (Barbara Grant) and maintaining correspondence with her. When David is imprisoned on the Bass Rock, Miss Grant dispatches a horseman to Rankeillor at Queensferry, knowing him to be "the doer for Shaws," and receives back a letter "presenting, in the most favourable view, my character and prospects."[^3] This reveals Rankeillor's quiet but effective hand working behind the scenes of the novel's action.

## Host to Prestongrange

Later in the novel, when David returns to Scotland with the Lord Advocate (Prestongrange), they visit Rankeillor at Queensferry: "Rankeillor gave us a good welcome, being indeed out of the body to receive so great a visitor. Here the Advocate was so unaffectedly good as to go quite fully over my affairs, sitting perhaps two hours with the Writer in his study."[^4] The meeting at Rankeillor's house underscores his position as a respected figure in the legal community, capable of hosting a meeting between the Lord Advocate and a young laird.

## Final Communication

At the novel's conclusion, Rankeillor writes to David in Leyden with the news of Ebenezer Balfour's death and David's full accession to the Shaws estate. His letter is described as being, characteristically, "wholly in the business view" — a fitting final note for the practical, loyal lawyer who first set David on the path to reclaiming his birthright.[^5]
