Zedekiah Scadder is an [[america|America]]n land speculator and agent for the [[eden|Eden]] Land Corporation who sells [[martin-chuzzlewit-jr|Martin]] Chuzzlewit a plot in the [[deception|fraud]]ulent settlement of [[eden|Eden]]. He is one of the most sinister of Dickens's [[america|America]]n characters, directly responsible for [[martin-chuzzlewit-jr|Martin]]'s ruinous investment (023-001).

## Overview

Scadder is described as a gaunt, cadaverous figure—"a lank, cadaverous man" who chews tobacco constantly. His appearance reflects the moral decay of his profession: he sells worthless swampland to unsuspecting emigrants with the practiced ease of a [[trust|confidence]] man (023-002).

## Biography

### The Eden Scheme

Scadder operates out of a shabby office where he displays a grandly deceptive map of [[eden|Eden]], showing broad avenues, public buildings, and commercial establishments that exist only on paper. He uses high-pressure salesmanship and patriotic rhetoric about [[america|America]]n progress to convince [[martin-chuzzlewit-jr|Martin]] to purchase land in what is actually a disease-ridden swamp (023-005).

### The Transaction

[[martin-chuzzlewit-jr|Martin]], eager to establish himself in [[america|America]], falls for Scadder's pitch and purchases a plot in [[eden|Eden]]. The transaction represents the climax of [[martin-chuzzlewit-jr|Martin]]'s [[america|America]]n disillusionment—Scadder's [[deception|fraud]] is emblematic of the gap between [[america|America]]n rhetoric and reality that Dickens exposes throughout the novel (023-008).

## Character Analysis

Scadder embodies the predatory capitalism that Dickens saw as a dark underside of [[america|America]]n expansion. His cadaverous appearance suggests death—he profits from the sickness and death of those who settle in the swampland he sells. Unlike the comic [[america|America]]n caricatures ([[elijah-pogram]], [[mrs-hominy]]), Scadder is genuinely sinister: his [[deception|fraud]] has real, devastating consequences for his victims.

> "You air a tongue-y person, Gen'ral. For you talk too much, and that's fact," said Scadder. "You speak a-larming well in public, but you didn't ought to go ahead so fast in private. Now!"
