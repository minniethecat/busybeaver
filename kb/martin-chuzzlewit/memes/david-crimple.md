# David Crimple

The Secretary and resident Director of the [[anglo-bengalee|Anglo-Bengalee Disinterested Loan and Life Assurance Company]], a [[deception|fraud]]ulent financial enterprise whose very name — altered from "Crimp" to "Crimple" to avoid "an awkward construction" — announces its commitment to cosmetic respectability over genuine substance.

## Overview

David Crimple appears as the administrative face of [[montague-tigg|Montague Tigg]]'s grand financial swindle. His original surname, Crimp, "was susceptible of an awkward construction and might be misrepresented"(027-002), so he added a syllable — a small linguistic [[deception|fraud]] that mirrors the larger [[deception|fraud]] of the Anglo-Bengalee itself. Where [[montague-tigg|Tigg]] provides the charismatic front, Crimple provides the bureaucratic apparatus: the circulars, the letterhead, the official notices in which "one David Crimple, Esquire, Secretary and resident Director, takes the liberty of inviting your attention to the accompanying statement of the advantages offered by the [[anglo-bengalee|Anglo-Bengalee Disinterested Loan and Life Assurance Company]]"(027-001).

## Biography

### Background & Family

Crimple's background is deliberately vague — he exists as a function of the Anglo-Bengalee rather than as a person with a history. His name change from Crimp to Crimple is the one biographical detail Dickens provides, and it is entirely characteristic: a modification that changes nothing of substance while creating an appearance of refinement.

### Role in the Plot

Crimple's most extended appearance comes during a scene at the Anglo-Bengalee offices, where his leg becomes the subject of a comic medical demonstration by [[doctor-jobling|Doctor Jobling]]: "In Mr. Crimple's leg, you'll observe... where Mr. Crimple's knee fits into the socket, here, there is — that is to say, between the bone and the socket — a certain quantity of animal oil"(027-015). Crimple's leg, appropriated as a prop for Jobling's professional performance, reduces him to a piece of office furniture — which is, in a sense, what he is. He is the Anglo-Bengalee's furniture: present, functional, and wholly subordinate to the enterprise's larger design.

[[anglo-bengalee|The Anglo-Bengalee]] offices, described in one of Dickens's most exuberant catalogues of commercial display, are Crimple's natural habitat: "newly plastered, newly painted, newly papered, newly countered, newly floor-clothed, newly tabled, newly chaired, newly fitted up in every way, with goods that were substantial and expensive, and designed (like the company) to last"(027-001). The parenthetical "like the company" drips with irony — the Anglo-Bengalee is designed to *appear* lasting, and Crimple is part of that appearance.

### Fate & Resolution

Like the Anglo-Bengalee itself, Crimple vanishes when the [[deception|fraud]] collapses. His fate is not individually resolved because he is not an individual — he is a function of the company, and when the company falls, the function ceases.

## Character Analysis

David Crimple is a minor character who illustrates a major theme: the relationship between language and [[deception|fraud]] in commercial enterprise. His name change from Crimp to Crimple is a miniature version of the Anglo-Bengalee's entire operation — take something plain and possibly incriminating, add a decorative flourish, and present it as respectable. "Disinterested" in the company's name performs the same function as the "-le" in "Crimple": a linguistic ornament that signals virtue while obscuring truth.

Crimple is also a study in administrative anonymity. [[montague-tigg|Tigg]] is the face of the [[deception|fraud]]; Crimple is its paperwork. The circulars, the letterhead, the official notices — these are Crimple's domain, and Dickens understands that [[deception|fraud]] at scale requires not just charismatic frontmen but diligent bureaucrats who manage the documentation. Crimple's crime is not passion but process.

His reduction to a medical prop — Jobling demonstrating on his leg while Crimple stands silent — completes the portrait. In the Anglo-Bengalee's hierarchy, Crimple is a limb, not a brain. He administers the [[deception|fraud]] without originating it, a functionary whose moral [[guilt|culpability]] is diluted by his own insignificance.
