# Kingsgate Street

Kingsgate Street, High Holborn, is the London address of Mrs. [[sarah-gamp|Sarah Gamp]], the novel's disreputable midwife and layer-out of the dead. More than a geographical location, Kingsgate Street is Gamp's operational headquarters — the place from which she dispatches herself to deathbeds across the city, and the neighborhood whose "circles" generate the gossip and professional intelligence on which her trade depends.

## Mrs. Gamp's Headquarters

The street is introduced early in Gamp's narrative: "her residence in Kingsgate Street, High Holborn" (019-003). From this modest address, Gamp conducts a thriving practice in what she calls "the nursing [[commerce|business]]." The location — between the respectable Holborn thoroughfare and the less salubrious alleys behind it — captures Gamp's own ambiguous social position: indispensable to the households that employ her, yet never quite respectable.

Kingsgate Street is also the locus of Gamp's professional network. "Certain whispers current in the Kingsgate Street circles" (019-022) keep Gamp informed of potential clients. When her services are needed urgently, the instruction is always the same:

> "Send a boy to Kingsgate Street," she says, "and snap her up at any price, for Mrs. Gamp is worth her weight and more in goldian guineas."
>
> (025-042)

## Narrative Function

The street serves practical plot functions as well. It is from Kingsgate Street that Gamp dispatches for her night-watches, from Kingsgate Street that she retrieves "a bundle of robes" (025-057) for her professional appearances, and to Kingsgate Street that [[bailey|Bailey Junior]] escorts her after a long night's work (026-073). The repeated returns to this address give Gamp's movements a kind of rhythm, a professional circuit that always brings her back to the same modest doorstep.
