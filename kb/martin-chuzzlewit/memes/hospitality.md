## Overview

Hospitality in *Martin Chuzzlewit* operates both as a moral index and as a structural device, bringing disparate characters together under one roof. The Blue Dragon Inn, presided over by the good-humoured Mrs. Lupin, serves as the novel's primary site of genuine welcome — a counterpoint to the false hospitality of Pecksniff's household.

## The Blue Dragon

Mrs. Lupin's inn embodies the ideal of English hospitality: warm, unpretentious, and generously given. (003-005) The sign of the Blue Dragon — a mythical creature that "had been, in his distincter days" courteous and considerate — sets the tone:

> He was a courteous and considerate dragon, too; or had been in his distincter days; for in the midst of his rampant feebleness, he kept one of his forepaws near his nose, as though he would say, "Don't mind me — it's only my fun"; while he held out the other in polite and hospitable entreaty. (Chapter III)

The Dragon becomes a refuge for Tom Pinch, Martin Chuzzlewit, Mark Tapley, and others in need of shelter, food, and kindness. (031-010)

## Pecksniff's False Hospitality

Mr. Pecksniff's welcome is the antithesis of Mrs. Lupin's. (002-030) His offers of shelter and food come with strings attached — moral lectures, designs on inheritance, and surveillance of his guests. His hospitality serves his self-interest, transforming a virtue into an instrument of manipulation.

## The American Contrast

Martin's experiences in America offer a satirical mirror of hospitality. The Norris family's effusive welcome is performative rather than genuine, while Mr. Bevan's quiet kindness represents the true article. (017-035) The Watertoast Association's grandiose reception of Martin parodies the excesses of American boosterism. (021-001)

## Hospitality as Moral Measure

Throughout the novel, how characters receive strangers — and how they behave as guests — serves as a reliable indicator of moral character: (003-010)

- **Mrs. Lupin**: Genuine care without expectation
- **Tom Pinch**: Grateful acceptance, generous hosting of others in turn  
- **Pecksniff**: Calculated welcome with hidden agenda
- **Jonas Chuzzlewit**: Exploitative, treating hospitality as weakness
