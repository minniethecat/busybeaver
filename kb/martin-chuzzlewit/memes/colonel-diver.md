Colonel Diver is an [[america|America]]n newspaper editor and one of the journalistic figures whom [[martin-chuzzlewit-jr|Martin]] Chuzzlewit encounters during his travels in the [[america|United States]]. He is associated with the sensationalist press culture that Dickens satirizes throughout the [[america|American chapters]] (021-001).

## Overview

Diver is introduced among the circle of [[america|America]]n editors and journalists who surround figures like [[elijah-pogram]]. His editorial practice exemplifies the jingoistic, sensation-driven journalism that Dickens saw as corrupting [[america|America]]n public discourse. His military title—likely [[honour|honor]]ific—adds to the satirical portrait of [[america|America]]n self-importance (021-002).

## Biography

### The Rowdy Journal

Diver is editor of the *[[the-rowdy-journal|Rowdy Journal]]*, alongside figures like [[mr-la-fayette-kettle]] and [[mr-jefferson-brick]]. The paper's name itself signals Dickens's critique: it is "rowdy" rather than respectable, privileging noise and sensation over truth and analysis (021-003).

### Public Life

Diver attends the public functions, levees, and receptions that form the social backdrop of [[martin-chuzzlewit-jr|Martin]]'s [[america|America]]n experience. He moves comfortably in the world of patriotic rhetoric and journalistic bombast (034-001).

## Character Analysis

Colonel Diver represents the type of editor who, in Dickens's view, debases the press by making it an instrument of jingoism rather than enlightenment. His title and his profession combine to create a figure of pompous self-importance.

> "My name is Colonel Diver, sir. I am the Editor of the New York Rowdy Journal."
