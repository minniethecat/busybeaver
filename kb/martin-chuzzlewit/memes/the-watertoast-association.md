# The Watertoast Association

The Watertoast Association is a fictional American civic organization that appears in the later American chapters of *Martin Chuzzlewit*. Named after its hometown—a place whose name combines the watery insubstantiality of "water" with the specious warmth of "toast"—the Association is Dickens's instrument for satirizing the rhetorical inflation and moral vacuity of American public institutions.

## Overview

The Watertoast Association operates according to the logic of American civic boosterism that Dickens had observed during his 1842 tour: any gathering of citizens becomes an occasion for grandiloquent speeches, resolutions, and toasts, regardless of whether any actual good is accomplished. (021-001) The Association's proceedings follow a ritual of mutual congratulation—members praise one another's republican virtue, pass resolutions of no practical consequence, and pronounce themselves the moral leaders of the age.

## Satirical Function

The Watertoast episodes extend the novel's critique of American rhetoric from journalism (the [[the-rowdy-journal|Rowdy Journal]]) to civic life. (021-005) Where the *Rowdy Journal* is vicious, the Watertoast Association is vacuous: both are distortions of the democratic ideal, but of opposite kinds. The Association's members speak the language of liberty and enlightenment while practicing exclusion and self-congratulation.

The Watertoast name itself carries Dickens's satirical judgment. "Water" suggests dilution—principles watered down to platitudes. "Toast" suggests the ceremonial drinking that is the Association's real activity: moral posturing over brandy. The Association produces words, not deeds; resolutions, not reforms. (021-020)

## Key Scenes

- Meeting proceedings: The Association convenes to debate and pass resolutions on matters of supposed national importance (021-025)
- Speeches and toasts: Members deliver elaborate speeches full of references to "freedom," "enlightenment," and "the eyes of the world" while demonstrating none of the qualities they celebrate (021-030)

## Quotations

The Watertoast Association's rhetoric is magnificent in its vacuity — a torrent of words signifying nothing:

> "Sir, I address you on behalf of the Watertoast Association of United Sympathisers. It is founded, sir, in the great republic of America! and now holds its breath, and swells the blue veins in its forehead nigh to bursting, as it watches, sir, with feverish intensity and sympathetic ardour, your noble efforts in the cause of Freedom."
>
