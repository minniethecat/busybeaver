Mr. La Fayette Kettle is an [[america|America]]n newspaper editor whom [[martin-chuzzlewit-jr|Martin]] Chuzzlewit meets during his [[america|America]]n travels. He is part of the journalistic circle that Dickens satirizes in the novel, alongside figures like [[colonel-diver]] and [[mr-jefferson-brick]] (021-001).

## Overview

Kettle represents the sensationalist press culture that Dickens critiques in the [[america|American chapters]]. His name—associating the [[america|America]]n revolutionary hero Lafayette with the domestic "kettle"—suggests the domestication and trivialization of revolutionary ideals in contemporary [[america|America]]n journalism (021-002).

## Biography

### The Rowdy Journal Circle

Kettle moves in the same journalistic circles as other [[america|America]]n editors and correspondents. He attends the [[the-watertoast-association|Watertoast]] levee and contributes to the atmosphere of patriotic bombast and journalistic sensationalism that characterizes the [[america|America]]n social scenes in the novel (034-001).

## Character Analysis

Like the other [[america|America]]n journalists in the novel, Kettle embodies the transformation of the press from an instrument of democratic accountability into a vehicle for sensationalism and patriotic self-congratulation. His name and his professional conduct suggest the bathos of [[america|America]]n cultural aspiration.
