# Anthony Chuzzlewit

Anthony Chuzzlewit is the brother of [[martin-chuzzlewit-sr|Old Martin Chuzzlewit]] and the father of [[jonas-chuzzlewit|Jonas Chuzzlewit]], a partner in the Manchester warehousing firm *Anthony Chuzzlewit and Son*. A lifelong miser consumed by [[wealth|avarice]] and suspicion, he exemplifies the corrosive effects of [[commerce|money]]-love in Dickens's moral universe—training his son [[jonas-chuzzlewit|Jonas]] in cunning and [[envy|covetousness]], only to die unmourned at his counting-house desk.

## Biography

### Background and Family

Anthony Chuzzlewit belongs to the first generation of the Chuzzlewit family whose quarrels over Old [[martin-chuzzlewit-jr|Martin]]'s fortune drive much of the novel's plot. Unlike his brother [[martin-chuzzlewit-jr|Martin]], whose wealth is the object of the family's scheming, Anthony has built his own modest fortune through [[commerce|trade]]. His firm, *Anthony Chuzzlewit and Son*, is lodged in "a dim, dirty, smoky, tumbledown, rotten old house" behind the Post Office in [[london|London]]—a physical expression of the moral squalor within (011-001). He appears at the family gathering at [[pecksniff-house|Mr. Pecksniff's House]] in Chapter IV alongside [[jonas-chuzzlewit|Jonas]], both "winking their red eyes, side by side, and whispering to each other softly" (004-022).

### Role in the Plot

Anthony's principal dramatic function is as the architect of [[jonas-chuzzlewit|Jonas]]'s character. Dickens makes the causal chain explicit:

> It was strange enough that Anthony Chuzzlewit, himself so old a man, should take a pleasure in these gibings of his estimable son at the expense of the poor shadow at their table. But he did, unquestionably. ... For the same reason that young man's coarse allusions, even to himself, filled him with a stealthy glee; causing him to rub his hands and chuckle covertly, as if he said in his sleeve, "_I_ taught him. _I_ trained him. This is the heir of my bringing-up. Sly, cunning, and covetous, he'll not squander my money. I worked for this; I hoped for this; it has been the great end and aim of my life."
>
> (011-042)

This passage is the moral centre of Anthony's characterization: he has deliberately cultivated [[jonas-chuzzlewit|Jonas]]'s worst qualities, mistaking cunning for prudence and cruelty for strength. His partnership with [[jonas-chuzzlewit|Jonas]] is less a commercial arrangement than a conspiracy of mutual suspicion—father and son watch each other as warily as they watch the world.

### Death

Anthony dies at his desk in Chapter XI, collapsing suddenly while the ancient clerk [[chuffey|Mr. Chuffey]] is beside him:

> a scream from Chuffey, rendered a hundred times more loud and formidable by his silent habits, made the house ring from roof to cellar; and, looking round, they saw Anthony Chuzzlewit extended on the floor, with the old clerk upon his knees beside him.
>
> (011-218)

His funeral is a grimly comic set piece: [[sarah-gamp|Mrs. Gamp]] lays out the body with professional relish; the undertaker [[mr-mould|mr. mould]] preens; and the whole household feasts "like a Ghoul" (011-236). Only Chuffey genuinely grieves. [[jonas-chuzzlewit|Jonas]]'s response—calculating how soon he can claim the inheritance—completes the circle Anthony himself drew: the father trained the son too well.

## Character Analysis

Anthony Chuzzlewit is a Dickensian **miser-type**, kin to Ebenezer Scrooge but bleaker still: Scrooge is redeemed; Anthony dies as he lived, his last act having been to corrupt his son. He represents what [[martin-chuzzlewit-sr|Old Martin]] might have become had he not, through the novel's arc, learned the insufficiency of wealth and suspicion.

The key dramatic irony of Anthony's character is that his "great end and aim"—[[jonas-chuzzlewit|Jonas]]—becomes the very agent of the Chuzzlewit family's darkest crimes, culminating in [[jonas-murders-tigg|the murder of Montague Tigg]]. Anthony's pride in having "trained" [[jonas-chuzzlewit|Jonas]] in selfishness is the novel's most pointed condemnation of a father's moral abdication.

## Key Relationships

- [[jonas-chuzzlewit|Jonas Chuzzlewit]] — son, [[commerce|business]] partner, and the corrupted product of Anthony's "training" in cunning (011-042)
- [[martin-chuzzlewit-sr|Martin Chuzzlewit Sr.]] — brother; the two represent contrasting fates of wealth: [[martin-chuzzlewit-jr|Martin]] learns, Anthony dies in his miserly shell
- [[chuffey|Mr. Chuffey]] — ancient clerk, the only person who genuinely mourns Anthony's death (011-236)
- [[seth-pecksniff|Seth Pecksniff]] — encounters Anthony at the family gathering and prays for him "who has done me an [[justice|injustice]]" after Anthony refuses to be drawn into [[seth-pecksniff|Pecksniff]]'s schemes (004-030)
