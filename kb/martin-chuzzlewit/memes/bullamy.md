# Bullamy

Bullamy is the porter, doorkeeper, and general functionary at the [[anglo-bengalee|Anglo-Bengalee Disinterested Loan and Life Assurance Company]], the fraudulent financial enterprise run by [[montague-tigg|Montague Tigg]]. In the novel's satire of commercial puffery, Bullamy represents the lower rung of the swindle: the man who clears his throat obsequiously, bustles about on command, and ultimately flees with whatever he can carry when the scheme collapses.

## Role at the Anglo-Bengalee

Bullamy's function is nominally that of a porter but practically that of a stage-manager for Tigg's performances. When visitors arrive, it is Bullamy who announces them, presents cards, and clears tables on command (027-054, 027-089). His voice is invariably deferential; his movements are described as bustling. He is the human machinery of the Anglo-Bengalee's front-of-house illusion — the man whose very presence suggests a legitimate business with proper hierarchies.

Tigg uses Bullamy as an instrument of his own theatricality. The repeated bark of "Bullamy!" punctuates the office scenes, each summons followed by some menial task that Tigg elevates — through sheer rhetorical inflation — into an act of great significance. Bullamy, for his part, plays his role without apparent irony: "Bullamy cleared his throat, and bustled about, and shook his head" (027-054).

## The Collapse

When the Anglo-Bengalee scheme finally collapses, Bullamy does not go down with the ship. In the novel's final chapters, the narrative notes "the flight of Crimple and Bullamy with the plunder" (051-007). Unlike [[david-crimple|Crimple]], who was a knowing participant in the fraud from the beginning, Bullamy's exact degree of complicity is left ambiguous. He is, at minimum, a man who knew when to run and what to take with him.
