Mr. Jefferson Brick is an [[america|America]]n war correspondent and newspaperman whom [[martin-chuzzlewit-jr|Martin]] Chuzzlewit encounters during his travels. He is a caricature of the sensationalist, jingoistic journalism that Dickens associated with the [[america|America]]n press of the 1840s.

## Overview

Jefferson Brick is introduced as one of the notable [[america|America]]n figures in the circle of [[elijah-pogram]]. He represents the aggressive, patriotic press corps that Dickens satirizes throughout the [[america|American chapters]] of the novel. His profession as a "war correspondent" reflects the bellicose rhetoric that Dickens observed in [[america|America]]n newspapers (021-001). Colonel Diver introduces him with theatrical pride: "Our War Correspondent, sir. Mr. Jefferson Brick!" (016-058) — an introduction that assumes immediate recognition, and when Martin confesses ignorance, Diver is incredulous: "You have heard of Jefferson Brick, I see, sir" (016-061).

## Biography

### The Rowdy Journal

Brick is associated with the *[[the-rowdy-journal|Rowdy Journal]]*, a newspaper that embodies the sensationalism of [[america|America]]n journalism. His writing style is described as inflammatory and designed to provoke conflict rather than inform. He is part of the same professional circle as [[colonel-diver]], another editor (021-002). Diver boasts that "the name of Jefferson Brick" makes "every country in Europe quail" (016-065), a hyperbolic claim that satirises the American press's inflated sense of its own international importance.

### Public Figure

Brick moves in the same circles as the [[the-watertoast-association|Watertoast Association]] and its associated politicians and public figures. He attends the levee where [[elijah-pogram]] and [[mrs-hominy]] have their celebrated exchange, representing the journalistic interest in such public spectacles (034-001).

## Character Analysis

Jefferson Brick embodies the type of journalist that Dickens saw as corrupting [[america|America]]n public discourse—more interested in sensation and jingoism than in truth. His very name suggests the brittle, constructed nature of his patriotic posturing.
