General Choke is an [[america|America]]n military figure and land speculator encountered by [[martin-chuzzlewit-jr|Martin]] Chuzzlewit during his [[america|America]]n travels. He is associated with the [[eden|Eden]] Land Corporation and its [[deception|fraud]]ulent land sales (023-001).

## Overview

General Choke is one of the figures involved in promoting the [[eden|Eden]] settlement. Like [[zedekiah-scadder]], he profits from selling worthless swampland to unsuspecting emigrants under the guise of [[america|America]]n progress and development. His military title, like those of other Dickensian [[america|America]]n figures, is likely [[honour|honor]]ific—a [[comedy|satire]] of the proliferation of self-aggrandizing titles in [[america|America]]n society (023-002).

## Biography

### The Eden Enterprise

Choke is part of the network of speculators who promote and sell land in the [[deception|fraud]]ulent [[eden|Eden]] settlement. Alongside Scadder and other agents, he uses patriotic rhetoric about [[america|America]]n expansion to [[deception|defraud]] emigrants, contributing to [[martin-chuzzlewit-jr|Martin]]'s ruinous investment (023-003).

## Character Analysis

General Choke embodies the intersection of military pretension and commercial [[deception|fraud]] in Dickens's [[america|America]]n [[comedy|satire]]. His title—"General"—suggests authority and [[honour|honor]], but his actions reveal the corruption beneath the patriotic [[appearance|surface]]. He is a minor but telling figure in the novel's critique of [[america|America]]n land speculation (021-054).
