# Chevy Slyme

Chevy Slyme is a distant Chuzzlewit relation and the inseparable companion of [[montague-tigg|Montague Tigg]], with whom he forms one of Dickens's great comic double acts in *[[martin-chuzzlewit-jr|Martin]] Chuzzlewit*. Introduced in Chapter IV as the man who is "always waiting round the corner," Slyme embodies the type of the genteel sponger: a man of presumed good family who has sunk into permanent dependence on others' purses, yet maintains an elaborate pretense of injured dignity.

## Biography

### Background and Family

Slyme's exact connection to the Chuzzlewit family is established early: he is present at the [[pecksniff-house|family gathering]] in Chapter IV, and [[montague-tigg|Tigg]] refers to him as "my friend Slyme" and "my adopted brother" (004-012). He has apparent standing as "Chevy Slyme, Esquire," but his circumstances are evidently reduced: he and [[montague-tigg|Tigg]] are perpetually cadging small loans and living by their wits on the margins of the family's inheritance-hunt.

### The Man Around the Corner

Slyme's defining comic attribute is established by [[montague-tigg|Tigg]] in a bravura speech to [[seth-pecksniff|Mr. Pecksniff]]:

> "Sir, the peculiarity of my friend Slyme is, that he is always waiting round the corner. He is perpetually round the corner, sir. He is round the corner at this instant. ... that is a remarkably curious and interesting trait in Mr. Slyme's character; and whenever Slyme's life comes to be written, that trait must be thoroughly worked out by his biographer, or society will not be satisfied."
>
> (004-009)

This "waiting round the corner" is both a physical habit and a moral description: Slyme perpetually hovers at the edge of schemes, never quite committing, always available to profit but never exposed to primary risk. His movements are described as "shambling" and his glances as "sneaking"—Dickens's physical shorthand for moral evasion (004-022).

### The Half Moon and Seven Stars

Slyme and [[montague-tigg|Tigg]] set up operations at the Half Moon and Seven Stars, an obscure alehouse where they receive intelligence about [[martin-chuzzlewit-sr|Old Martin Chuzzlewit]]'s movements and coordinate with other family members. It is Slyme who announces the arrival of [[anthony-chuzzlewit|Anthony Chuzzlewit]] and [[jonas-chuzzlewit|Jonas]]: "'I saw 'em not five minutes ago, when I was waiting round the corner'" (004-017). The phrase has become his signature, repeated with variations throughout the novel.

## Character Analysis

Slyme belongs to a recognizable Dickensian category: the **genteel parasite**. Unlike [[montague-tigg|Tigg]], who possesses a kind of exuberant, creative dishonesty that eventually propels him (briefly) into the role of company director, Slyme has no ambition beyond immediate gratification. His "great abilities," Dickens notes with devastating irony, "seemed one and all to point towards the sneaking quarter of the moral compass" (004-010).

The comic relationship between Slyme and [[montague-tigg|Tigg]] is one of symbiotic [[deception|deceit]]. [[montague-tigg|Tigg]] praises Slyme in wild hyperbole—"the highest-minded, the most independent-spirited, most original, spiritual, classical, talented, the most thoroughly Shakspearian, if not Miltonic, and at the same time the most disgustingly-unappreciated dog I know" (004-007)—while simultaneously exploiting him as the front man for their joint begging operations. Slyme, for his part, provides [[montague-tigg|Tigg]] with the thin veneer of family connection that makes their schemes possible.

## Key Relationships

- [[montague-tigg|Montague Tigg]] — inseparable companion, spokesperson, and exploiter; together they form the novel's most sustained comic portrait of parasitic friendship (004-005 through 004-022)
- [[seth-pecksniff|Seth Pecksniff]] — target of their opening scheme for a crown-piece loan; [[seth-pecksniff|Pecksniff]]'s refusal is one of his few moments of resisting a fellow [[deception|fraud]]ster
- [[anthony-chuzzlewit|Anthony Chuzzlewit]] — Slyme reports Anthony's arrival at the family gathering, positioning himself as a source of intelligence
- [[jonas-chuzzlewit|Jonas Chuzzlewit]] — recognized as a fellow schemer though operating at a far more dangerous level
