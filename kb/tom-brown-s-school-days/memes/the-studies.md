# The Studies

The private rooms of [[schoolhouse|the Schoolhouse]]'s senior boys — the intimate spaces where [[fagging|fags]] served, [[praepostor|praepostors]] governed, friends talked, and the real work of character formation occurred.

## Overview

- **Category**: School interior / private room
- **Location**: [[schoolhouse|Schoolhouse]], [[rugby-school|Rugby School]]
- **Part of**: [[schoolhouse|Schoolhouse]]
- **Chapters**: 005–018

(005-029)

## Geography & Description

The studies lined [[schoolhouse|the Schoolhouse]] passages, varying in desirability by position. The study at the fire end, occupied by Jones the praepostor, was the prize — rigged with "an iron rod and green baize curtain across the passage, which he draws at night, and sits there with his door open; so he gets all the fire" (005-029). Each study was a small kingdom: the praepostor's desk and chair, the [[fagging|fag]]'s corner, the shared fire, the whispered conversations that constituted [[the-great-school|the school]]'s hidden curriculum.

(005-029)

## Role in the Narrative

[[tom-brown|Tom]] and [[arthur|Arthur]]'s shared study was the novel's most important room — the space where [[arthur|Arthur]]'s gentle influence transformed [[tom-brown|Tom]]'s soul. The study was also the unit of political organization: from his study, a praepostor governed his [[fagging|fags]]; from their shared study, [[tom-brown|Tom]] and [[harry-east|East]] planned [[war-of-independence|the rebellion]]; in [[diggs-the-mucker|Diggs]]'s study, the persecuted younger boys found shelter. The studies were where [[schoolhouse|the Schoolhouse]]'s real life was lived.
