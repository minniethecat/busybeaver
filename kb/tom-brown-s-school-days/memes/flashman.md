# Flashman

The school's arch-villain — a sixth-form praepostor whose combination of physical cruelty, moral cowardice, and systematic [[bullying-at-rugby|bullying]] made him the living antithesis of everything [[rugby-school|Rugby School]] was meant to produce.

## Profile

- **Full name**: Flashman (first name not given in the novel)
- **Age/Form**: Sixth Form (senior boy, praepostor)
- **House**: [[schoolhouse|Schoolhouse]]
- **Role**: [[praepostor|Praepostor]] (corrupt), bully, [[sneaking|sneak]], coward
- **Family**: Not specified; later became the anti-hero of George MacDonald Fraser's "Flashman" series
- **First appearance**: Chapter VI — mentioned in [[old-brooke|Brooke]]'s speech (006-030); directly appears in Chapter VIII
- **Last appearance**: Chapter IX — expelled after being caught drunk (009-030)

## Character & Personality

Flashman is defined by the vices that are the inversion of every [[rugby-school|Rugby]] virtue. Where the ideal boy is courageous, he is a coward; where the ideal boy is [[honour-at-rugby|honour]]able, he is a [[sneaking|sneak]]; where the ideal boy protects the weak, he torments them. His physical strength and sixth-form status give him power, which he uses not to govern but to terrorize. His cowardice is revealed in his final disgrace — caught drunk and expelled, he proves that the bully's courage is only ever directed at those who cannot fight back.

## Role in the Narrative

### The Corrupt Praepostor

Flashman represents the failure mode of the [[the-doctor|Arnold]]ian system. When the [[the-forms|sixth form]] is strong, [[the-great-school|the school]] thrives; when a Flashman occupies a [[the-studies|praepostor's study]], the system of [[fagging|fag]]ging becomes indistinguishable from [[bullying-at-rugby|bullying]], and the moral order of the house collapses. His tenure is the novel's darkest period — the time when [[tom-brown|Tom]] and [[harry-east|East]] learn that authority can be not merely imperfect but wicked.

(008-016)

(009-003)

### The Fagging Rebellion

Flashman's persecution of [[tom-brown|Tom]] and [[harry-east|East]] — "sly blows and taunts" that persist even after open confrontation — drives the [[fagging|fag]]ging [[fagging-rebellion|rebellion]]. He is the cause that forces [[tom-brown|Tom]] into moral action, and his hatred pursues the rebels even after his power is checked. His final disgrace — caught drunk, exposed as a coward, and expelled — is the novel's judgment on the type he represents.

## Significance

Flashman is the novel's negative pole — the demonstration, by inversion, of everything [[rugby-school|Rugby]] valued. He is courageous only toward the weak, [[honour-at-rugby|honour]]able only when observed, loyal to no one. His existence is the novel's concession that the [[the-doctor|Arnold]]ian system could fail, and that the same institutions that produced [[old-brooke|Old Brooke]] could also produce a Flashman. His expulsion is the system's self-correction, but the evil he did "lives after him" (009-030) — a sober reminder that character, once formed, is not easily reformed.
