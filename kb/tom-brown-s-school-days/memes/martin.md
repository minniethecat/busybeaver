# Martin

The kindly old guard of the [[the-stage-coach|Tally-ho]] [[the-stage-coach|coach]] who delivered [[tom-brown|Tom]] from London to [[rugby-school|Rugby]] (004-029) — a brief but vivid portrait of the decent English working man whose warmth and competence formed the background of [[tom-brown|Tom]]'s world.

## Profile

- **Full name**: Martin (surname not given)
- **Role**: Guard on the [[the-stage-coach|Tally-ho]] [[the-stage-coach|coach]]
- **First appearance**: Chapter IV (004-001)
- **Last appearance**: Chapter IV (004-029)

## Character & Personality

Martin is the model of the faithful old servant — competent, kindly, and possessed of a quiet dignity. He muffles [[tom-brown|Tom]]'s feet in straw against the cold, shares his silence companionably, and delivers the boy safely to his destination. He represents the stable, decent England that [[tom-brown|Tom]] is leaving — the world of hierarchy and mutual obligation that the [[squire-brown|Squire]] believes in and that [[rugby-school|Rugby]] will, in its way, both preserve and transform.

(004-029)

## Role in the Narrative

Martin appears only in the [[the-stage-coach|coach]] journey of Chapter IV, but his brief presence is significant: he is [[tom-brown|Tom]]'s last human connection to the old world before [[rugby-school|Rugby]] claims him. His kindness — practical, wordless, reliable — is the final gift of home before the trials of school begin.
