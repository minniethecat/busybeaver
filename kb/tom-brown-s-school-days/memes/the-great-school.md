The vast assembly [[the-hall|hall]] and schoolroom where the daily ritual of calling-over was performed — the architectural embodiment of the school's hierarchical order.

## Geography & Description

The great school was a long, high room where the entire school assembled for calling-over. The master mounted the high desk by the door; the [[praepostor|praepostor of the week]] stood beside him; the other [[praepostor|praepostors]] marched the aisles with their canes. The [[the-forms|sixth form]] stood nearest the door, the [[the-forms|upper school]] behind them, the [[the-forms|lower school]] at the far end — a spatial arrangement that encoded the school's entire hierarchy in the positions of standing boys.

## Role in the Narrative

The great school was the theater of the school's daily ritual. At calling-over, the entire community assembled in strict order of seniority, each boy answering "here" as his name was called. It was also the site of floggings — public punishments that reinforced the boundary between order and disorder. The room was, in effect, the school made visible: a structured community in which every boy knew his place.
