## Overview

[[old-brooke|Old Brooke]]'s address to [[schoolhouse|the Schoolhouse]] after the [[football-at-rugby|football]] match — the novel's moral manifesto, naming [[bullying-at-rugby|bullying]] as the house's central problem and pledging reform (006-030).

## Key Participants

[[old-brooke|Old Brooke]] (speaker), the assembled [[schoolhouse|Schoolhouse]] boys, [[tom-brown|Tom Brown]] and [[harry-east|Harry East]] (listeners)

## Significance

This event shaped the moral and social world of [[rugby-school|Rugby School]] as depicted in the novel, contributing to the formation of character that was [[the-great-school|the school]]'s central mission (006-030).
