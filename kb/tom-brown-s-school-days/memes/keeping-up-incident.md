# The Keeping-Up Incident

[[tom-brown|Tom]]'s first-night experience of the [[keeping-up|blanket-tossing]] ritual — interrupted by the praepostor's arrival, a mild version of the tradition that could easily become [[bullying-at-rugby|bullying]]

## Overview

[[tom-brown|Tom]]'s first-night experience of the [[keeping-up|blanket-tossing]] ritual — interrupted by the praepostor's arrival, a mild version of the tradition that could easily become [[bullying-at-rugby|bullying]] (006-080).

## Key Participants

[[tom-brown|Tom Brown]] (the victim), the older boys (the tossers), the praepostor (who ended it)

## Significance

This event shaped the moral and social world of [[rugby-school|Rugby School]] as depicted in the novel, contributing to the formation of character that was [[the-great-school|the school]]'s central mission (006-080).
