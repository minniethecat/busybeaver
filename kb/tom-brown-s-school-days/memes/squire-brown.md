# Squire Brown

[[tom-brown|Tom Brown]]'s father — a true-blue Tory squire of the old school whose parting words to his son ("never tell a lie, and never be afraid of what you think is right") provide the moral foundation on which [[rugby-school|Rugby]] builds.

## Profile

- **Full name**: Squire Brown (first name not given)
- **Role**: Father, [[vale-of-white-horse|Berkshire]] country squire, Tory [[gentleman-ideal|gentleman]]
- **Family**: [[mrs-brown|Mrs. Brown]] (wife), [[tom-brown|Tom]] (eldest son), several younger brothers "still under petticoat government"
- **First appearance**: Chapter I (001-001)
- **Last appearance**: Chapter IV — accompanies [[tom-brown|Tom]] to London en route to [[rugby-school|Rugby]] (004-003)

## Character & Personality

Squire Brown is the model of the old English country [[gentleman-ideal|gentleman]] (001-001) — warm-hearted, principled, conservative in the best sense. He believes in the established order, loves his family, and trusts that the public school system will make a man of his spirited eldest son. His parting advice to [[tom-brown|Tom]] — simple, direct, unforgettable — is the novel's moral baseline (004-003): the home-grown virtue that [[rugby-school|Rugby]] will refine but not replace (001-001).

## Significance

The Squire represents the foundation on which [[rugby-school|Rugby]] builds. [[tom-brown|Tom]] arrives at school already possessing courage, honesty, and a sense of justice — gifts from his father. [[rugby-school|Rugby]]'s work is not to create these virtues from nothing but to elevate and spiritualize them, transforming the Squire's Tory integrity into [[the-doctor|the Doctor]]'s [[gentleman-ideal|Christian gentleman]]liness.
