# The Stage Coach

The Tally-ho coach that carried [[tom-brown|Tom]] from London to [[rugby-school|Rugby]] — a liminal space suspended between the boy's old life in [[vale-of-white-horse|Berkshire]] and his new life at school.

## Overview

- **Category**: Transport / transitional space
- **Location**: London to [[rugby-school|Rugby]] route, via St. Albans
- **Chapters**: 004

(004-001)

## Description

The Tally-ho was a Birmingham coach that deposited passengers at [[rugby-school|Rugby]]. Its guard, [[martin|Martin]], muffled [[tom-brown|Tom]]'s feet in straw against the cold and shared the journey in companionable silence. The coach was filled with the paraphernalia of travel — parcels, hampers of game, the bustle of inn yards — and marked the physical and symbolic passage from the world of [[vale-of-white-horse|the Vale]] to the world of [[the-great-school|the school]].

(004-029)

(004-022)

## Role in the Narrative

The coach journey of Chapter IV is the novel's transitional passage — the space between two worlds. [[tom-brown|Tom]], "half-frozen" but enjoying the ride, is suspended between the boy he was and [[the-great-school|the school]]boy he will become. [[martin|Martin]]'s kindness is the last gift of the old world; [[rugby-school|Rugby]], waiting at the journey's end, is the new.
