# The Dormitory

The large shared bedroom where the younger [[schoolhouse|Schoolhouse]] boys slept — a space of enforced intimacy where [[friendship]]s deepened and the unofficial life of [[the-great-school|the school]] continued after the masters withdrew.

## Overview

- **Category**: School interior / sleeping quarters
- **Location**: [[schoolhouse|Schoolhouse]], [[rugby-school|Rugby School]]
- **Part of**: [[schoolhouse|Schoolhouse]]
- **Chapters**: 005–018

(007-008)

## Description

The dormitory was a long room lined with beds, each boy's narrow territory within the larger community. The praepostor of the room maintained order from behind his curtains, calling for hot water in the morning and silencing noise at night. After lights-out, the whispered conversations that constituted [[the-great-school|the school]]'s emotional life — confessions, plans, fears, and jokes — filled the darkness.

(007-008)

(007-009)

## Role in the Narrative

[[tom-brown|Tom]]'s first morning in the dormitory — "half doubtful as to where exactly in the universe he was, but conscious that he had made a step in life" (007-004) — is the novel's acknowledgment that the dormitory was where a boy's school life began and ended each day. It was the space of maximum vulnerability and maximum intimacy.
