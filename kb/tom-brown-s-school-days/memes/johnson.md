# Johnson

Johnson is a [[schoolhouse|Schoolhouse]] cricketer who plays a prominent role in the [[mary|Mary]]lebone match described in Chapter 17, "[[toms-last-match|Tom Brown's Last Match]]." He is noted both as a bowler — taking crucial wickets — and as a steady, determined batsman who helps secure [[schoolhouse|the Schoolhouse]] victory.

## Profile

- **Full name**: Johnson (given name never specified)
- **Age**: Upper-school boy, likely 16–18 during the cricket season
- **House**: [[schoolhouse|Schoolhouse]]
- **Role**: Cricketer for [[schoolhouse|the Schoolhouse]] Eleven; bowler and batsman
- **Family**: Not mentioned
- **First appearance**: Chapter 9 (009-037) — mentioned as a potential [[fagging|fag]]-master
- **Last appearance**: Chapter 17 (017-078) — carries his bat at the [[the-close|close]] of the [[mary|Mary]]lebone match

## Character

### Early Mention

Johnson first appears in the narrative in Chapter 9, when a senior boy, searching for a [[fagging|fag]] to assign, runs through possibilities: "Well, let's see. There's Willis, Johnson. No, that won't do. Yes, I have it. There's [[harry-east|young East]]; I'll give you him" (009-037). This glancing reference suggests Johnson was a known figure in the [[the-forms|upper school]] even before the cricket scenes of Part II.

### The Marylebone Match

Johnson's defining appearance comes in the climactic cricket match of Chapter 17. As a bowler, he takes a crucial wicket against the [[mary|Mary]]lebone eleven, drawing the captain's praise: "Oh, well bowled! well bowled, Johnson!" (017-014). [[arthur|Arthur]], watching from the sidelines, drops his bat and claps furiously, and [[tom-brown|Tom]]'s shout of "Bravo, Johnson!" is "heard at [[rugby-chapel|the chapel]]" (017-030).

Johnson's bowling is described with technical precision: "such a ball, three-quarters length, and coming straight for his leg bail. Nothing but that turn of the wrist could have saved him, and he drew it away to leg for a safe one" (017-032).

### Batting Under Pressure

When [[schoolhouse-match|the match]] reaches its tense final phase, Johnson proves himself as a batsman as well. With [[rugby-school|the School]] needing runs and wickets falling, Johnson's batting is described as "worthy the occasion": "He makes here a two, and there a one, managing to keep the ball" (017-076). [[tom-brown|Tom]]'s assessment — "[[arthur|Arthur]] is the steadiest, and Johnson is the best bat left" (017-073) — confirms Johnson's standing in the eleven. At the [[the-close|close]] of play, "Winter and Johnson carry out their bats" (017-078), having remained not-out — the highest accolade for a batsman in a hard-fought match.
