# Warner

One of [[schoolhouse|the Schoolhouse]] [[praepostor|praepostors]] who, with [[old-brooke|Brooke]], governed the house during its period of strongest moral order (008-016) — a secondary figure whose quiet competence reinforced the system [[old-brooke|Brooke]] led.

## Profile

- **Full name**: Warner (first name not given)
- **Form**: Sixth Form
- **House**: [[schoolhouse|Schoolhouse]]
- **Role**: [[praepostor|Praepostor]]
- **First appearance**: Chapter VI (006-001)
- **Last appearance**: Chapter VIII — departed with [[old-brooke|Brooke]] (008-016)

## Character & Personality

Warner is a supporting figure in the house's moral economy — not as charismatic as [[old-brooke|Brooke]], not as corrupt as [[flashman|Flashman]], but reliable and just. He represents the depth of the [[the-forms|sixth form]]'s strength during [[schoolhouse|the Schoolhouse]]'s golden age: it was not dependent on a single great man but sustained by a cadre of decent ones. His departure, alongside [[old-brooke|Brooke]]'s, is part of the general decline that follows.

(008-016)

## Role in the Narrative

Warner appears primarily as [[old-brooke|Brooke]]'s colleague in governance. His presence assures the reader that the house's order is institutional (008-016), not personal — that the system produces not one good praepostor but a bench of them. His departure signals the end of an era: after [[old-brooke|Brooke]] and Warner leave, the [[the-forms|sixth form]] is thinner, weaker, and more vulnerable to corruption.

(008-016)
