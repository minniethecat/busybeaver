# Slogger Williams

The strong, physically imposing boy (014-004) whom [[tom-brown|Tom Brown]] fought and defeated in the novel's climactic contest — less a developed character than a necessary foil, the Goliath to [[tom-brown|Tom]]'s David.

## Profile

- **Full name**: Williams, called "Slogger" for his physical, forceful style
- **House**: Not [[schoolhouse|Schoolhouse]] (opposing house)
- **Role**: [[tom-brown|Tom]]'s opponent in the climactic fight
- **First appearance**: Chapter XIV (014-001)
- **Last appearance**: Chapter XIV (014-004)

## Character & Personality

Slogger Williams is the novel's purest example of the antagonist who is not evil — merely strong, proud, and opposed to [[tom-brown|Tom]]. Unlike [[flashman|Flashman]], he [[the-fight|fights]] fairly; unlike the [[sneaking|sneak]]s, he stands openly. His defeat by [[tom-brown|Tom]] is not a moral victory over wickedness but a demonstration that courage, skill, and righteousness can overcome superior physical force.

(014-004)

## Role in the Narrative

Williams exists to provide [[tom-brown|Tom]] with his great physical trial — the fight that proves his courage (014-004) in the most visible arena [[the-great-school|the school]] offers. The fight is [[honour-at-rugby|honor]]able on both sides, and Williams's defeat is without shame: he is beaten by a better fighter [[the-fight|fighting]] for a better cause.

(014-004)
