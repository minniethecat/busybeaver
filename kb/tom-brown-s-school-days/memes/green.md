# Green

The leader of the "fast set" in [[schoolhouse|the Schoolhouse]] — the alternative social orbit that drew [[harry-east|Harry East]] away from [[tom-brown|Tom Brown]]'s influence, representing the path of easy sociability that led not to ruin but to mediocrity.

## Profile

- **Full name**: Green (first name not given)
- **House**: [[schoolhouse|Schoolhouse]]
- **Role**: Leader of a fast set of boys
- **First appearance**: Mentioned in Chapter XVI as the leader of the set [[harry-east|East]] joined (016-068)

## Character & Personality

Green is not a villain like [[flashman|Flashman]] — he is something more common and, in the novel's view, almost as dangerous: the leader of boys who are content to be merely decent. His set offers easy [[friendship|fellowship]] without moral demand, good cheer without spiritual c[[the-hall|hall]]enge (016-068). [[harry-east|East]]'s admission — "I was living mostly with Green's set. You know the sort. They all went in" — is a confession of the path not taken, the influence that might have been [[arthur|Arthur]]'s but was Green's instead (016-068).

(016-068)

## Role in the Narrative

Green appears only as a name, but his function is significant: he represents the gravitational pull of the ordinary (016-068), the social force that keeps boys from rising to the moral heights the novel celebrates (016-068). [[harry-east|East]]'s tragedy is not that he became wicked but that he remained ordinary (016-068) — and Green's set was the vehicle of that ordinariness.

(016-068)
