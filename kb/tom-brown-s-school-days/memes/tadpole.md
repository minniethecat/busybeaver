# Tadpole

A junior [[schoolhouse|Schoolhouse]] boy — one of the anonymous crowd of [[fagging|fags]] whose collective experience constitutes the novel's portrait of life at the bottom of [[the-great-school|the school]] hierarchy.

## Profile

- **Full name**: Known only as "Tadpole" or "the Tadpole"
- **House**: [[schoolhouse|Schoolhouse]]
- **Role**: Fag, junior boy
- **First appearance**: Chapter VII — hailed [[tom-brown|Tom]] for hare-and-hounds (007-028)

## Character & Personality

Tadpole is the representative junior boy — spirited, mischievous, good-natured, and entirely without the depth or complexity of a [[tom-brown|Tom Brown]]. He exists to populate the novel's world, to provide the background of ordinary boyhood against which the drama of [[tom-brown|Tom]]'s moral education unfolds. His presence reminds us that most boys were neither heroes nor villains — they were simply boys, enduring [[fagging|fag]]ging, enjoying hare-and-hounds, and making their way through school as best they could.

(007-028)

## Role in the Narrative

Tadpole appears in the junior-school scenes — hare-and-hounds, [[fagging|fag]]ging duties, the general bustle of lower-school life (007-028). He is one of the "young Ishmaelites" who share [[tom-brown|Tom]] and [[harry-east|East]]'s persecution, and his presence fills out the portrait of the community that [[war-of-independence|the rebellion]] aimed to protect (009-032).
