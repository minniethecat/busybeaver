# Diggs

The rough, eccentric older boy — familiarly called "the Mucker" — who emerged as an unlikely protector and counsellor to [[tom-brown|Tom]] and [[harry-east|East]] during the darkest days of their persecution, embodying the novel's conviction that goodness can appear in unexpected [[the-forms|forms]].

## Profile

- **Full name**: Diggs, familiarly called "the Mucker"
- **House**: [[schoolhouse|Schoolhouse]]
- **Role**: Older boy, protector of younger boys under persecution
- **First appearance**: Chapter VIII (008-057)
- **Last appearance**: Chapter IX (009-032)

## Character & Personality

Diggs is "a very queer specimen of boyhood" (008-057) — rough, unrefined, physically imposing, and utterly unlike the elegant ideal of the [[gentleman-ideal|Christian gentleman]]. Yet he possesses what the novel values most: an instinctive decency and a willingness to protect the weak. He advises [[tom-brown|Tom]] and [[harry-east|East]] in their struggle against [[flashman|Flashman]], provides a safe harbor when they are persecuted, and asks nothing in return. He is the novel's reminder that virtue does not require polish — that a rough heart can be as true as a refined one.

(008-057)

## Role in the Narrative

Diggs appears at the crisis point of the [[fagging|fag]]ging [[fagging-rebellion|rebellion]], when [[tom-brown|Tom]] and [[harry-east|East]] are isolated and persecuted. He advises them, shelters them, and helps them navigate the treacherous politics of the house. His presence assures the reader — and the boys — that they are not alone, that even in a house corrupted by [[flashman|Flashman]] there are older boys who remember what [[old-brooke|Brooke]] stood for.

(008-057)
