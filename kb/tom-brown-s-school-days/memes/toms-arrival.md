# Tom's Arrival at Rugby

[[tom-brown|Tom Brown]]'s first arrival at [[rugby-school|Rugby School]] by [[the-stage-coach|coach]] — the moment of transition from the old world of [[vale-of-white-horse|Berkshire]] to the new world of public school. This event opens the novel's central narrative, carrying [[tom-brown|Tom]] across the threshold from childhood to the institution that will form him.

> Tom's heart beat quick as he passed the great schoolfield or close, with its noble elms, in which several games at football were going on, and tried to take in at once the long line of gray buildings, beginning with the chapel, and ending with the Schoolhouse, the residence of the headmaster, where the great flag was lazily waving from the highest round tower.
>
> (005-003)

## Overview

[[tom-brown|Tom]]'s arrival at [[rugby-school|Rugby]] spans Chapters IV and V. The journey begins with his departure from [[brown-family-home|the Brown family home]] in [[vale-of-white-horse|Berkshire]], continues on [[the-stage-coach|the stage coach]] with [[martin|the guard]] [[martin|Martin]], and culminates in his first glimpse of [[the-great-school|the school]] — a moment of awe and apprehension that every [[rugby-school|Rugby]] boy experienced. That evening, kneeling in his attic room, [[tom-brown|Tom]] prays "that, come what might, he might never bring shame or sorrow on the dear folk at home" (004-015).

## Key Participants

- **[[tom-brown|Tom Brown]]** — the new boy, eleven years old, leaving home for the first time
- **[[squire-brown|Squire Brown]]** — [[squire-brown|Tom's father]], whose parting words echo throughout the novel
- **[[martin|Martin]]** — [[martin|the guard]] on the [[the-stage-coach|stage]] [[the-stage-coach|coach]], [[tom-brown|Tom]]'s first guide to [[rugby-school|Rugby]]
- **[[schoolhouse|The Schoolhouse]]** boys — [[tom-brown|Tom]]'s first glimpse of school society

## Significance

[[tom-brown|Tom]]'s arrival is the novel's archetypal scene of [[keeping-up|initiation]] — the moment when a boy leaves the protected world of family for the forming world of school. The physical journey from [[vale-of-white-horse|the Vale]] of White Horse to [[rugby-school|Rugby]] maps a moral journey from innocence to experience. The first sight of [[rugby-chapel|the chapel]] and [[schoolhouse|Schoolhouse]], and [[tom-brown|Tom]]'s private prayer that night, establish the novel's coordinates: public institution and private conscience, architecture and soul.
