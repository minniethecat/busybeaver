# Minnie Hershey

Minnie Hershey is Gabrielle Leggett's personal servant — a small, wiry mulatto whose
fierce devotion to her mistress is weaponized by the Haldorns. Through a campaign of
drugged visions and religious terror, she is made to believe Doctor Riese is Satan and
kills him on the Temple altar.

## Role in the Novel

Minnie is the novel's most exploited character — a working-class woman of color whose
race and class make her doubly vulnerable to the Haldorns' manipulation. Her role in
Riese's murder is not voluntary; she is a tool wielded by Joseph Haldorn and Owen
Fitzstephan. The Op's recounting of her exploitation serves as the novel's strongest
indictment of the Temple's evil.

## Biography

### Background

Minnie came to San Francisco from Winchester, Virginia, four or five years before the
novel's events (002-034). She had been with the Leggett household for nearly two years
when the diamond theft occurred — Mrs. Leggett vouched that "I'm sure she had nothing
to do with it. She's been with us for nearly two years and I can vouch for her honesty"
(001-049).

When the Op first interviews her, she is described as "a small, wiry mulatto girl with
the straight black hair and brown features of an Indian. She was very polite and very
insistent that she had nothing to do with the theft of the diamonds" (001-050). She
lives in a Negro neighborhood in San Francisco with a man called Rhino Tingley (002-034).

### In the Novel

After the diamond theft, Minnie quits the Leggett household in protest at being
suspected: "She wouldn't, she said tearfully, work anywhere where anybody thought she
had stolen anything. She was just as honest as anybody else, and more than some, and
just as much entitled to respect" (001-069).

When Gabrielle flees to the Temple of the Holy Grail, she takes "the mulatto, Minnie
Hershey, with her as maid" (009-008). At the Temple, the Haldorns "had cut loose on
Minnie from the very beginning. She was a mulatto, and therefore susceptible to that
sort of game" (012-027). Using the Temple's apparatus of drugged gas and projected
illusions, they "chucked visions and voices at the poor girl until she was dizzy."

The culmination of their manipulation came when they drugged Doctor Riese and placed
him on the altar, then "ghosted her into thinking that he was Satan." Minnie killed
him with a large dagger — "this is serious: they did this cold-bloodedly, deliberately"
(012-027). The Op emphasizes the premeditated nature of the crime: the Haldorns had
methodically conditioned Minnie to be their killer.

In the Temple's final crisis, Minnie remained loyal to Gabrielle, "throwing herself
down in front of Gabrielle Leggett" when danger threatened (011-086), crying out about
"that devil" who had come after her mistress again (011-087).
