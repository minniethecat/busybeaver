# Continental Detective Agency

A San Francisco-based detective agency run by the figure known only as "the Old Man," employing the Continental Op and serving as the institutional framework for the novel's investigative work.

## Overview

The Continental Detective Agency is the unnamed narrator's employer, a professional investigative firm headquartered in San Francisco. The Op identifies himself by it early in the case: "I'm not a police detective — Continental Agency — for the insurance company" (001-014). Over the course of the novel, the agency handles four successive clients connected to Gabrielle Leggett: first the insurance company's diamond theft claim, then the Leggett murder investigation, then the Riese murder at the Temple, and finally Hubert Collinson's request that the Op "get to the bottom of it" (015-004).

## Key Members

- **The Old Man** — the agency's head, based in San Francisco. He assigns the Op to cases, offers wry non-committal support, and intervenes with the district attorney when needed (015-029, 015-057)
- **[[continental-op]]** — the agency's operative and the novel's narrator, dispatched to Quesada to investigate the Leggett diamond theft

## In the Novel

### Activities

The agency's involvement proceeds in stages. The Op is first assigned to recover eight diamonds stolen from the Leggett household, insured by the General American Indemnity Company. After the Leggett suicide-murder case concludes, the recovery of the Halstead and Beauchamp diamonds allows the agency to write "Discontinued" at the bottom of the Leggett record (008-025). But the agency is drawn back in when attorney Andrews, acting as Guardian for Gabrielle Leggett, hires the Op to go undercover at the Temple of the Holy Grail (009-011). Later, Hubert Collinson becomes "the fourth client for whom the agency had been concerned with Gabrielle's affairs" (015-004).

### Narrative Role

The agency functions as the institutional anchor for the Op's investigation, providing resources, backup operatives, and the professional infrastructure that distinguishes his work from both police procedure and amateur detection. Telegrams come "to the agency" (013-004), the Op returns "to the agency" between field operations (004-001, 004-009, 015-054), and the Old Man's authority overrides local law enforcement when necessary.
