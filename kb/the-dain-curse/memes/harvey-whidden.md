# Harvey Whidden

**Harvey Whidden** is a resident of Quesada and the lover of [[Daisy Cotton]], the marshal's wife. His involvement in the events of the novel escalates from a witness sighting to the revelation that he murdered [[Eric Collinson]] and kidnapped [[Gabrielle Leggett|Gabrielle Collinson]].

## In the Novel

Whidden is described as a "big-boned man of thirty-five or so" with heavy features and voice, who "moved and spoke deliberately" (014-239). He lives in a small house in the Quesada area.

### The Witness

Whidden was the only person to see the mysterious man in the Chrysler with Gabrielle Collinson on the night of Eric Collinson's disappearance. He reported seeing a man with her as the car passed his property (014-263), a detail that contradicted the initial assumption that Gabrielle had acted alone.

### The Murderer Revealed

It eventually emerges that Whidden himself was Collinson's murderer and Gabrielle's kidnapper. As revealed in [[Daisy Cotton]]'s written confession:

> "Harvey Whidden came last night... said husband after him... frame him for Collinson trouble... he wasn't here that night... tried to kidnap her Thursday night... husband nearly caught him... came in office after Collinson sent telegram and saw it... followed him and killed him... went to San Francisco, drinking whiskey... decided to go through with kidnapping anyway..." (021-766)

Whidden had seen the telegram [[Eric Collinson]] sent to the [[Continental Op]], followed Collinson to the cliff road, and killed him. He then went through with the kidnapping, holding Gabrielle at an abandoned bootleggers' hideout below Dull Point.

### Death

When the rescue party found Gabrielle, Whidden appeared at the far end of the cove with a rifle, yelling in anger and astonishment. He was shot dead by the authorities—three bullets in his chest, one in his head (022-828).

## Key Passages

> "As suddenly as if he had sprung out of the air, Harvey Whidden appeared in the far end of the v, standing in the sand, a rifle in his hands. Anger and utter astonishment were mixed in his heavy face." (022-822)

> "Whidden went over backwards, his feet flying in the air. He was dead when we got to him—three bullets in his chest, one in his head." (022-828)
