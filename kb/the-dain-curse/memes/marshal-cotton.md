# Marshal Dick Cotton

**Dick Cotton** is the town marshal of Quesada, described as "a pompous, unintelligent man in his forties" (014-290). He becomes involved in the investigation of [[Eric Collinson]]'s death and the disappearance of [[Gabrielle Leggett|Gabrielle Collinson]].

## In the Novel

Marshal Cotton initially voted with the other local officials—[[Sheriff Feeney]], [[District Attorney Vernon]], and Deputy [[Ben Rolly]]—that Gabrielle had killed her husband. He had been in San Francisco at the time of the murder (014-290).

### Framing Whidden

Cotton attempted to frame [[Harvey Whidden]] for the murder by planting evidence—a grocer's bill and tax receipt—in Whidden's house. The [[Continental Op]] discovered the frame-up and prevented it from succeeding (018-571). Cotton's motive was personal: Whidden was his wife's lover.

> "The marshal's wife, a frail youngish woman with a weak pretty face and nice shy manners, who worked in the telegraph office..." (014-292)

[[Daisy Cotton]] later confessed that Whidden had forced her to provide a false alibi, threatening to kill her if she didn't cooperate (021-766).

### Downfall

When Cotton's attempted frame-up was exposed by the Op, the marshal fled. The search shifted from Whidden to Cotton, though Whidden's actual guilt was confirmed when he appeared at the hideout with a rifle and was killed by the authorities.

## Key Passages

> "The marshal's Dick Cotton," he told me; "but he went up to the city last night." (013-976)

> "Sheriff Feeney... and district attorney Vernon... and Marshal Dick Cotton—a pompous, unintelligent man in his forties—returned from San Francisco, he added his vote to the others." (014-290)
