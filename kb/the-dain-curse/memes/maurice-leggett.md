# Maurice Leggett

Maurice Pierre de Mayenne is the man born in Fecamp, France, who would later become
Edgar Leggett. An artist turned fugitive, his wrongful conviction for his wife Lily's
murder and his escape from Devil's Island form the hidden history that drives the
entire plot of The Dain Curse.

## Role in the Novel

Maurice never appears in the novel's present action under this name — he has been
Edgar Leggett for years. But his past as Maurice de Mayenne is the secret whose
revelation destroys him. The blackmailers Upton and Ruppert tracked him from Devil's
Island; Alice Dain used his fugitive status as leverage; and his confession letter,
signed "Maurice de Mayenne" (006-034), is the document around which the Op's
investigation turns.

## Biography

### Background

Maurice Pierre de Mayenne was born in Fecamp, France, on March 6, 1883. He received
the bulk of his education in England. In 1903 he went to Paris to study painting, and
there, four years later, met the Dain sisters — Alice and Lily — orphaned daughters of
a British naval officer (006-024).

Maurice married Lily in 1908. A daughter, Gabrielle, was born in 1909. The household
was complicated: Alice lived with them, and by her own account Maurice "was intimate
enough with both" sisters (007-040).

In 1913, Lily was shot and killed. The five-year-old Gabrielle was the instrument —
trained by Alice to retrieve Maurice's pistol and point it at her sleeping mother —
but Maurice was convicted of the murder and sent to Devil's Island, the French penal
colony in French Guiana (007-043).

### Escape and Transformation

Maurice escaped from Devil's Island and made his way through Central America and
Mexico, pursued by criminals Upton and Ruppert who eventually traced him to Mexico
City (006-032). In 1920 he arrived in San Francisco, assumed the name Edgar Leggett,
and built a legitimate life as a scientist: "I came to the United States, to San
Francisco, changed my name once more — to Edgar Leggett — and began making a new place
for myself in the world" (006-030).

His color experiments attracted commercial interest from jeweler Collinson, who "thought
what a wonderful thing it would be if the same method could be applied to diamonds"
(002-028).

### The Past Returns

Maurice believed he had escaped his history: "believing that Edgar Leggett could never
now be connected with Maurice de Mayenne" (006-030). He was wrong. In August 1923,
Alice and Gabrielle appeared at his door, having traced him. Upton and Ruppert soon
followed, demanding blackmail. The weight of the past, combined with Alice's
manipulations, crushed him within months.

His final act was to write a confession assuming responsibility for everything —
including crimes he did not commit — and to shoot himself at his laboratory table. He
signed the letter with his true name: "Maurice de Mayenne" (006-034).

The Op later told Gabrielle: "Stop thinking about your Dain blood and think about the
Mayenne blood in you. Where do you suppose you got your toughness, except from him?
It's the same toughness that carried him through Devil's Island, Central America, and
Mexico, and kept him standing up till the end" (019-071).
