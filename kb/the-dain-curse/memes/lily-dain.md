# Lily Dain

Lily Dain is the murdered mother who exists only in the novel's backstory, yet her death
is the foundational event upon which every subsequent crime is built. Killed by her
sister Alice in Paris in 1913, Lily is the absent center around which the Dain curse
narrative revolves.

## Role in the Novel

Lily never appears in the novel's present action, but her murder in 1913 is the
originating trauma. The false narrative that her husband Maurice killed her sent him to
Devil's Island. The truth — that her sister Alice murdered her — is the secret Alice
guarded with escalating violence for over a decade. Lily's death is the crime that
generates all subsequent crimes.

## Biography

### Background

Lily Dain was one of two orphaned daughters of a British naval officer, raised with her
sister Alice. The sisters were "true sisters, inseparable, hating one another
poisonously" (007-040). In 1907, while living in Paris, the sisters met Maurice Pierre
de Mayenne, a young art student. Both became "intimate enough with both" sisters, though
"Maurice, he wished to marry neither of us — why should he?" (007-040).

Lily married Maurice in 1908, and their daughter Gabrielle was born in 1909. Alice lived
with them — "from the first Gabrielle had more love for me than for her mother" — and
began systematically turning the child against Lily (007-041).

### Death and Aftermath

In 1913, Alice trained five-year-old Gabrielle to play a game: climbing onto a chair,
retrieving a pistol from a locked drawer, and pointing it at Alice as she lay pretending
to sleep. Alice practiced this game with the pistol unloaded. One afternoon, when Lily
was napping after taking aspirin, Alice unlocked the drawer without unloading the pistol
and "told the child she might play the game with her mother" (007-043).

The pistol discharged. Lily died. Maurice was convicted of the murder and sent to
Devil's Island. Alice raised Gabrielle, systematically erasing the child's memory of the
event: "we had succeeded in quite emptying her mind of the last trace of memory"
(007-037).

The truth remained buried until Alice herself confessed it during her rage in the
laboratory: "I taught her to kill her mother. Do you understand? I taught her, trained
her, drilled her, rehearsed her" (007-040). Fitzstephan later suggested that Alice may
have killed Lily herself and lied to hurt Gabrielle, but the Op noted "he didn't have any
evidence to support what was after all only his guess" (023-029).
