# San Francisco

San Francisco of the early 1920s is the urban stage for [[the-dain-curse|*The Dain Curse*]].
The novel's geography — from the elite residences of Nob Hill to the financial district on
Montgomery Street, from the [[the-temple-of-the-holy-grail|Temple of the Holy Grail]] on
Pacific Avenue to the cliff roads winding toward [[quesada|Quesada]] — maps a city of sharp
contrasts, where old money, religious fraud, and violent crime occupy the same streets. The
[[continental-op|Continental Op]]'s investigation crisscrosses San Francisco repeatedly,
each location acquiring the weight of the events that unfold there.

## Overview

The novel is set in San Francisco in the early 1920s. The city provides the backdrop for
a story that moves between elite residences, financial district offices, seedy
neighborhoods, and the headquarters of a fashionable cult. San Francisco's cultural climate
of the era is specifically referenced: cults come and go in California, and the Temple of
the Holy Grail chose San Francisco because it "held less competition than Los Angeles"
(012-008).

### Arrivals

Several key characters arrive in San Francisco from elsewhere, each bringing part of the
Dain family's tangled history with them:

- **[[edgar-leggett|Edgar Leggett]]** arrived in 1920, having changed his name from
  Maurice de Mayenne, and began developing his color experiments (006-030). In 1923,
  believing himself safely anonymous, he sent for Alice and Gabrielle to join him.
- **[[eric-collinson|Eric Collinson]]** had been in San Francisco for "a little more than
  a year" when the Op meets him (003-008).
- **[[minnie-hershey|Minnie Hershey]]** had come to San Francisco from Winchester,
  Virginia, four or five years before the events of the novel (002-034).
- **[[continental-op|The Op]]** works for the San Francisco branch of the Continental
  Detective Agency, run by the Old Man.

### Cultural Context

The novel references the broader California phenomenon of religious cults rising and
falling in popularity, with San Francisco specifically chosen by the Haldorns because it
"held less competition than Los Angeles" (012-008). This cultural detail anchors the
[[the-temple-of-the-holy-grail|Temple of the Holy Grail]] in a specifically Californian
context of spiritual experimentation — and exploitation. As the Op notes dryly: "Half of
them are still willing to string along with Aaronia Haldorn" even after the fraud is
exposed (012-016).
