# Joseph Haldorn

Joseph Haldorn is the charismatic and ultimately deranged co-leader of the Temple of the
Holy Grail, a fashionable San Francisco cult. Together with his wife Aaronia, he
transformed a theatrical career into a profitable religious enterprise — until his
obsession with Gabrielle Leggett destroyed everything.

## Role in the Novel

Haldorn is the antagonist of the novel's middle act, the Temple sequence. He exploits
Gabrielle's vulnerability after her father's death, using the cult's apparatus of
visions, drugged gas, and manufactured miracles to control her. When his obsession
crosses into murder, the Temple collapses into violence.

## Biography

### Background

Joseph and Aaronia Haldorn were "just pretty good" actors who, inspired by a former
troupe colleague who had "chucked the stage for the pulpit and had made a go of it,"
decided to create their own religion (012-006). The cult was actually the brainchild of
Owen Fitzstephan, who "organized it, financed it, and brought it to San Francisco"
while keeping his involvement secret (023-005). Aaronia was Fitzstephan's mistress;
Joseph was "a puppet, in the family as in the Temple" (023-006).

The Temple maintained a dignified public face. Eric Collinson admitted the services were
"quite as dignified, as beautiful even, as either Episcopalian or Catholic services"
(005-041). Behind the scenes, however, the Haldorns used concealed pipes to pump
perfumed gas into victims' rooms and projected illuminated "spooks" — steam illusions —
to manufacture religious visions (012-011).

### In the Novel

Described at first meeting as "tall, built like a statue" with "thick, long, white, and
glossy" hair and beard, a "healthily pink" face and "white even-toothed smile" (009-053),
Haldorn presented a commanding presence. The Op felt the pull of his charisma: "I
actually believed that for some reason he was happy" (009-055).

Haldorn's interest in Gabrielle Leggett was not purely financial. Aaronia discovered
"that her husband's interest in the girl wasn't altogether financial" and "that he had
gone off center, was a dangerous maniac" (012-029). His success in manipulating his
followers had gone to his head: "Going around hypnotized all the time, what brains he
had — not a whole lot to start with — had become completely scrambled" (012-029).

When Doctor Riese overheard Haldorn's instructions to the Fink brothers and confronted
him, Haldorn "locked Riese up — a prisoner" (012-026). He then drugged Riese, placed him
on the altar, and used the vision-and-voice apparatus to convince Minnie Hershey that
Riese was Satan, inducing her to kill him (012-027). He then used the same trick to
"send Gabrielle down to see the corpse on the altar step," intending to "tie her to him
by playing his divinity against her curse" (012-030).

In the final Temple confrontation, Haldorn went fully berserk, tying Aaronia to the altar
step and attacking the Op. He died during the chaos of the cathedral showdown (012-055).
