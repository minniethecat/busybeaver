# Gabrielle Leggett

Gabrielle Leggett (née Dain) is the novel's central female character and the human
face of the Dain curse. The daughter of Lily Dain — murdered by her sister Alice in
Paris in 1913 — Gabrielle was raised by her mother's killer, systematically addicted
to morphine, and conditioned to believe she carries a hereditary curse that dooms
everyone close to her.

## Role in the Novel

Gabrielle is both victim and catalyst. She does not drive the plot through action
but through the effect she has on others — her father, her fiancé, her stepmother,
and the various men who try to control or protect her. The Continental Op's
investigation is, in large part, an effort to free Gabrielle from the psychological
prison Alice Dain has built around her.

## Biography

### Background

Gabrielle was an infant when her mother Lily Dain was murdered in Paris. Her father,
Maurice Leggett (or Edgar — the novel's paternity claims are ambiguous), was framed
for the murder while Alice Dain raised the child. Alice systematically erased
Gabrielle's memory of the event: "we had succeeded in quite emptying her mind of the
last trace of memory" (007-037). She was then addicted to morphine as a control
mechanism.

### In the Novel

When the novel opens, Gabrielle is a young woman living in the Leggett household in
San Francisco, engaged to Eric Collinson. She is described as psychologically fragile
and morphine-dependent. After the diamond theft investigation begins, she witnesses
the murder of a man in Golden Gate Avenue and flees to the Temple of the Holy Grail.
At the Temple, she becomes the focus of Joseph Haldorn's manipulation — he plans to
"tie her to him by playing his divinity against her curse" (012-030).

Alice Dain's curse speech is directed at Gabrielle: "you're cursed with the same
black soul and rotten blood that she and I and all the Dains have had" (007-048).
Gabrielle's response — screaming and collapsing — demonstrates how thoroughly the
curse narrative has been internalized. She believes she killed Riese and "ought to
be hanged for it" (012-030).

After the deaths of both her father and stepmother, Gabrielle marries Eric Collinson,
but the curse follows her. When Collinson is killed, she attempts suicide "to put an
end to the curse by shooting herself" (017-059). By the novel's end, the Op has
established that Gabrielle is a victim of systematic manipulation, not a vessel of
supernatural evil.

  systematically destroyed her mind (007-037)
