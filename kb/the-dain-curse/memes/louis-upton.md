# Louis Upton

Louis Upton was a former proprietor of a detective agency in New York, whose investigation into Edgar Leggett's whereabouts—commissioned by Alice Dain—unwittingly set in motion the chain of events that would culminate in the murders, deceptions, and revelations of the Dain curse.

## Role in the Novel

Upton never appears directly in the novel's present-day action; his significance lies entirely in the backstory. It was Upton who, hired by Alice to find the missing Maurice de Mayenne (now living as Edgar Leggett), sent his operative Harry Ruppert to South America to trace Leggett's path. Ruppert's investigation uncovered the series of suspicious deaths that followed Leggett's trail—Labaud, Howart, and Edge—but stopped short of connecting Leggett to them directly [007-PPP].

Upton's own history is checkered: he was arrested in September 1923 for bribing two jurors in the Sexton murder trial. In an attempt to save himself, he implicated Ruppert, his own employee. Both men were convicted and served time at Sing Sing prison, being released on February 6 of the year the novel takes place [006-PPP]. This criminal background adds a layer of moral ambiguity to the investigation that would eventually threaten Edgar Leggett's carefully constructed new life.

## Biography

### Background

Before establishing his detective agency, Upton operated in New York. His agency handled private investigations, and by 1923 he had built enough of a reputation that Alice Dain—then living in New York with her daughter Gabrielle—hired his firm to locate her missing husband. Upton's decision to send Ruppert to South America was a fateful one: it set a competent but ruthless operative on a trail that would eventually lead to blackmail, murder, and the unraveling of multiple lives [007-PPP].

### The Ruppert Connection

Upton's relationship with Harry Ruppert proved disastrous. When Upton was arrested for jury tampering in the Sexton case, he attempted to deflect blame onto Ruppert—a betrayal that sent both men to prison. After their release from Sing Sing, Ruppert, described as "thirty-two years, five feet eleven inches, hundred fifty pounds, brown hair and eyes, sallow complexion, thin face, long thin nose," reportedly threatened to kill Upton [006-PPP]. Ruppert then disappeared, resurfacing later in San Francisco under circumstances connected to the Leggett case.
