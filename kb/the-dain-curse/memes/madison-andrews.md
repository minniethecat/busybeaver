# Madison Andrews

Madison Andrews is Edgar Leggett's attorney who becomes Gabrielle's guardian and the
executor of the Leggett estate. A tall, gaunt man of sixty with a reputation for
scandal, Andrews is a study in compromised authority — a hardheaded lawyer who keeps
making questionable decisions about his ward's welfare.

## Role in the Novel

Andrews is the institutional gatekeeper. He controls access to Gabrielle, manages her
money, and makes the decisions that determine where she goes and who protects her. His
decisions are consistently poor: he sends Gabrielle to the Temple, hires the Op too
late, and later conspires with Aaronia Haldorn. He is never revealed as a criminal
mastermind, but his weakness and poor judgment make him a useful tool for those who
are.

## Biography

### Background

Andrews is described as "a tall gaunt man of sixty with ragged white hair, eyebrows, and
mustache that exaggerated the ruddiness of his bony hard-muscled face. He wore his
clothes loose, chewed tobacco, and had twice in the past ten years been publicly named
corespondent in divorce suits" (009-001). He had been Leggett's attorney and became the
executor of the estate after Leggett's death.

### In the Novel

Following the Leggett-Ruppert inquests, Andrews took charge of Gabrielle: "She was with
Madison Andrews, who had been Leggett's attorney and was now his estate's executor"
(008-024). When Doctor Riese recommended sending Gabrielle to the Temple, Andrews
agreed — "over Collinson's protests" — arguing that the Haldorns had been "Edgar
Leggett's friends" (009-008).

When Collinson demanded Gabrielle be removed from the Temple, "he and Andrews had a
swell row." Andrews "thought that the girl had simply suffered a relapse from which she
would most speedily recover." But "it wouldn't look so good for him, the hardheaded
lawyer, letting his ward go to such a place, if anything happened to her" (009-010). His
compromise — letting her stay with the Op as guard — came too late to prevent Riese's
murder.

After the Temple affair, Andrews hired the Op again to investigate Eric Collinson's death
in Quesada. He "offered a thousand-dollar reward for information leading to discovery of
the girl's whereabouts" (015-026) when Gabrielle vanished.

Andrews's most compromising behavior came late in the novel: he repeatedly visited
Aaronia Haldorn at the Jeffries home in San Mateo (020-013). The Op learned that Andrews
"had been to San Mateo to see Aaronia Haldorn again" (021-037). When the Op confronted
Aaronia about Andrews, she admitted that "Mr. Andrews thinks you suspect him" (021-093).
The Op recognized what was happening: "The old boy's woman-crazy: he'd be duck-soup for
a woman like you" (021-103) — Aaronia was manipulating Andrews to create confusion and
cover for Fitzstephan.

Andrews ultimately "surrendered his letters testamentary" and was replaced as
administrator of the estate by Walter Fielding (023-030).
