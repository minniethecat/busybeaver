# Laurence Collinson

Laurence Collinson is the brother of [[eric-collinson|Eric Collinson]] and son of [[hubert-collinson|Hubert Collinson]]. He appears in the later stages of the case and features in the novel's closing scene, where his quiet disapproval of the Continental Op provides a note of understated tension.

## In the Novel

Laurence accompanied his father Hubert down from San Francisco when Eric was arrested [015-042]. When the Continental Op came to Hubert Collinson's office to report his findings, Hubert was out, and Laurence heard the Op's story instead [015-052].

By the novel's closing chapter, Laurence had become a steady presence in [[gabrielle-leggett|Gabrielle Leggett]]'s life. She, Laurence, and the Op lunched together regularly. Gabrielle told the Op she would never undergo another morphine cure, teasing that he had "enjoyed himself awfully" during the previous one — a remark Laurence laughed at, though the Op suspected his amusement was only surface-deep [023-018].

The Op had the impression that Laurence thought he wasn't a refining influence on Gabrielle [023-022], a quiet note of friction between the detective and the family at the novel's close.
