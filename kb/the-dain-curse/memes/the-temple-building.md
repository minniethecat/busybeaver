# The Temple Building

The physical headquarters of the Temple of the Holy Grail, a religious cult led by Joseph Haldorn. Housed in a converted apartment building on Pacific Avenue in San Francisco, it was the site of multiple key events including the discovery of Doctor Riese's body at the altar.

## Overview

From the street, the Temple of the Holy Grail looked like what it had originally been: a six-story yellow brick apartment building. Its exterior revealed nothing of its new function as a religious institution (005-046). Collinson described it as "the fashionable one just now" among California's many ephemeral cults, noting that the right sort of people attended, including Mrs. Payson Laurence, the Ralph Colemans, and Mrs. Livingston Rodman (005-039).

## In the Novel

### Key Scenes

The Continental Op and Eric Collinson visit the Temple to find Gabrielle Leggett, who had gone to stay there on Doctor Riese's recommendation. As they approach, the Op cautions Collinson to control his breathing (005-049). They are greeted by a small boy of twelve or thirteen with big dark eyes who examines them from the doorway (005-058).

Aaronia Haldorn, Joseph's wife, receives them in a reception room where she raises a blind to let in afternoon sunlight (005-067). The building interior includes white rooms with white-papered walls, white-painted ceiling, white-enameled furniture and fixtures, and white felt on the floor, giving it the appearance of a hospital rather than a residence (005-108).

The building's chapel contains a wide altar of brilliant white, crystal, and silver (010-046). It is at this altar, on the lowest of three steps, that Doctor Riese's body is found with four stab wounds to his chest (010-047). The chapel walls are described as white, smooth, and unbroken except for two identical doors on opposite sides, with four straight whitewashed walls undecorated (010-067).

The dining room is paneled and furnished in dark walnut, with seating for ten at the table (009-052). The building was scientifically equipped for eavesdropping, allowing the Haldorns to overhear confessions made in private rooms (012-032).

## Description

The Temple building was converted from a six-story yellow brick apartment building to serve as the headquarters of the Temple of the Holy Grail cult. Its interior was extensively remodeled: rooms were furnished entirely in white with white-enameled fixtures and white felt flooring, creating a stark, hospital-like atmosphere (005-108). The chapel featured a brilliant white, crystal, and silver altar (010-046). The contrast between the mundane exterior and the austere interior reflected the cult's dual nature as both a fashionable social institution and a secretive religious organization.
