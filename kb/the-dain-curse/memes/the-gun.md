# The Gun

A .38 automatic pistol discovered on the floor of Eric Collinson's bedroom in the
Quesada house, with "an empty shell close to it, another under a chair across the
room" (013-039), constituting key evidence in the investigation of Collinson's
suspicious death.

## Overview

When the Op arrives at the Collinsons' house in Quesada, he finds Eric Collinson's
body in the water below a cliff and returns to the house to search the scene. In Eric
Collinson's bedroom he discovers: "a .38 automatic pistol lay in the center of the
floor. There was an empty shell close to it, another under a chair across the room,
and a faint odor of burnt gunpowder in the air. In one corner of the ceiling was a
hole that a .38 bullet could have made" (013-039). The bedclothes were smooth and
undisturbed, suggesting no struggle occurred.

## Narrative Role

### Plot Significance

The gun is the central piece of evidence around which the mystery of Eric Collinson's
death revolves. The Op shows it to Rolly along with the other evidence — "the gun on
Collinson's floor, the hole in the ceiling, and the empty shells on the floor"
(014-001) — and notes that the shell positions have been altered: one shell had been
moved from near the gun to under a chair (014-002). Rolly observes the puzzle of "two
shots and one hole" (014-008), questioning whether the second bullet exited through a
window. The pistol was later identified as Collinson's own (015-002), and no
fingerprints were found on it, leading the Op to doubt the accident theory and suspect
foul play (015-009).
