# Tom Fink

**Tom Fink** is a stage mechanic and former illusionist's assistant who served as the technical operator of the [[Temple of the Holy Grail]]'s fraudulent apparatus. A small, fidgety man with a "half-whisper" manner of speaking, Fink was responsible for constructing the concealed rooms, cabinets, and trick mechanisms that enabled [[Joseph Haldorn]] and [[Aaronia Haldorn]] to simulate supernatural phenomena.

## In the Novel

Fink was brought to San Francisco from the East Coast by the Haldorns, for whom he had previously worked on stage magicians' and illusionists' acts. He was "in charge of the mechanical end" of their operations (001-762). His wife, described as "a big village-smith of a woman," also worked for the Temple (001-762).

### Remodeling the Temple

When the Haldorns acquired the apartment building from [[Mrs. Rodman]], Fink supervised the remodeling. He converted scattered kitchen spaces into concealed rooms and cabinets, and adapted gas pipes, water pipes, and electrical wiring for the Temple's illusions (001-764).

### Role in the Murders

During the night of [[Joseph Haldorn]]'s killing spree, Fink and his wife participated in the attempted murder of the [[Continental Op]]. After the detective was gassed in [[Minnie Hershel]]'s room, the Finks attacked him in the darkened corridor. The Op fought them off and escaped (001-836).

Fink later refused to cooperate with authorities, maintaining he heard noises but knew nothing of the murders (001-856).

### The Explosion

Fink survived the explosion at the Op's hotel room. He had come to share information about the case—having been persuaded by the district attorney to turn state's evidence—when a bomb detonated. Fink was carried backward by the blast and struck his head on a doorframe, sustaining a cracked skull. He was taken to the county hospital (003-975).

## Key Passages

> "With them they brought a little fellow named Tom Fink who had at one time or another been in charge of the mechanical end of most of the well-known stage magicians' and illusionists' acts; and Fink's wife, a big village-smith of a woman." (001-762)

> "The stage mechanic Fink was in charge of the remodeling, and did a neat job." (001-764)

> "Fink won't talk. He's a mechanic, yes; and he put in his trick-machinery for the Haldorns and operated it; but he doesn't know what happened last night. He heard a lot of noises, but it was none of his business to go poking his nose out to see what it was." (001-856)
