# Mary Nunez

Mary Nunez is a Mexican woman who serves as a maid in the Carter (later
Collinson) household. She is one of the few people who had regular contact with
[[gabrielle-leggett|Gabrielle Leggett]] during her marriage to
[[eric-collinson|Eric Collinson]], and her testimony — and its gaps — become
important to the [[continental-op|Continental Op]]'s investigation.

## Role in the Novel

Mary Nunez appears in two distinct phases of the story. In the first, during the
investigation of Collinson's death, she is a witness whom the Op interviews —
and whose story he distrusts. In the second, after Gabrielle is moved to the
house in the cove, she becomes a fiercely protective attendant to her mistress.
Her fierce loyalty to Gabrielle, expressed in snarling Mex-Spanish when she
thinks the Op is hurting her, provides one of the novel's rare moments of
genuine human warmth amid the Gothic machinery of the plot.

## Biography

### Background

Mary Nunez is Mexican and lives in a settlement near the Carter household. She
has a husband who was "sent over" (apparently imprisoned), an experience that
the Op believes has made her wary of becoming entangled with the law. This
background — a working-class immigrant woman with reason to distrust authority
— informs her cautious, occasionally deceptive testimony.

### In the Novel

The Op first encounters Mary Nunez when interviewing potential witnesses to the
Collinson case. Rolly tells him she is the only person who regularly spoke with
Gabrielle: "I don't guess there's anybody around here that's talked to her,
except of course Mary Nunez" (014).

When interviewed, Mary Nunez insists she was kept home by chills on the morning
of the discovery — a claim the Op immediately doubts. He theorizes that she
actually went to the Collinson house, found the bloody evidence, and fled,
fabricating the illness to avoid involvement: "having had enough of that sort of
trouble when her husband was sent over" (014). Despite his suspicions, her
alibi holds: "She had a flock of Mexican witnesses to back it up" (016).

Later, when Gabrielle is moved to the house in the cove, Mary Nunez rejoins her
as a maid. She arrives at half-past seven in the morning, driven by
[[mickey-linehan|Mickey Linehan]] (021). During Gabrielle's night terrors, Mary
Nunez patrols the hallway — "watching over her mistress" — and when Gabrielle
screams, she comes "snarling and spitting at me in Mex-Spanish," convinced the
Op is harming her charge (022). Her loyalty is fierce and instinctive, a
striking contrast to the calculating relationships that dominate the novel.

## Relationships

- **[[gabrielle-leggett|Gabrielle Leggett]]** — Employer and protectee. Mary
  Nunez is fiercely, almost maternally protective of Gabrielle.
- **[[continental-op|The Continental Op]]** — Antagonist. Mary Nunez views the
  Op with suspicion, distrusting his treatment of Gabrielle and lying to him
  during the initial investigation.
- **[[ben-rolly|Ben Rolly]]** — Acquaintance. Rolly knows her by name and is
  familiar enough to ask why she isn't at work.
