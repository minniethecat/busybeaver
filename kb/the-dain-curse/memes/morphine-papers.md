# Morphine Papers

The small folded paper packages — bindles — containing morphine, discovered as
physical evidence at multiple locations throughout the investigation of the Leggett
case, and later used by the Continental Op in his treatment of Gabrielle Leggett's
addiction.

## Overview

The morphine papers are described as "a small piece of thick white paper that had been
folded" with "white powder clung to one crease" (013-040). The Op identifies the
substance by taste: "I touched it with the end of my tongue — morphine" (013-040).
These bindles serve as recurring physical evidence throughout the later chapters of
the novel, linking the crime scenes at Quesada to Gabrielle's ongoing addiction. The
Op later obtains medical morphine in "bindles" (021-003), and the folded paper format
becomes a consistent marker of her dependency.

## Narrative Role

### Plot Significance

The morphine paper discovered on Gabrielle Collinson's dressing table (013-040) is one
of the key pieces of evidence the Op shows to Rolly when reconstructing the events
surrounding Eric Collinson's death (014-001). Along with the bloodstained towels,
dress, and gun, the paper helps establish the physical sequence of events. Later, the
Op uses morphine bindles as part of his strategy to manage Gabrielle's withdrawal
symptoms — a calculated clinical decision that contrasts with the criminality of her
prior use (021-003). The final twist involves the Op's confession that "for two days
the folded papers had held powdered sugar instead of the original morphine" (022-112),
demonstrating his protective manipulation of Gabrielle during her withdrawal.

### Associated Characters

- **[[Gabrielle Leggett]]** — the morphine user whose addiction is evidenced by the papers; receives bindles from the Op during withdrawal treatment (021-003)
- **[[The Continental Op]]** — discovers the morphine paper at the Quesada crime scene (013-040); obtains and dispenses morphine bindles to Gabrielle (021-003); secretly replaces them with powdered sugar (022-112)
- **[[Mary Nunez]]** — steals eight bindles from the Op's pocket (022-047), but later returns them (022-108)

## Appearances

- **013-040**: First discovery — a folded paper with morphine traces on Gabrielle's dressing table in Quesada
- **014-001**: The Op shows the evidence to Rolly — "the paper that had held morphine"
- **014-009**: Rolly examines "the morphine paper from the dressing-table"
- **021-003**: The Op takes ten grains of morphine in bindles to Gabrielle — she "held her hands out eagerly"
- **021-009**: A crumpled white paper ball — "all that remained of one bindle"
- **022-047**: Eight bindles stolen from the Op's pocket by Mary Nunez
- **022-108**: Mary Nunez returns the eight stolen bindles
- **022-112**: The Op reveals the bindles contained powdered sugar, not morphine
