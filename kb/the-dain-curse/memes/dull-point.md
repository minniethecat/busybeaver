# Dull Point

Dull Point is a blunt promontory on the California coast, south of the town of Quesada. The waters and rocky inlets below the point conceal a bootlegger's hiding-place that becomes critical to the investigation when a wanted man takes refuge there.

## Overview

Dull Point is a coastal landmark defined by its rugged shoreline of high, sharp rocks at the water's edge. Below the point lies a hidden cleft in the rocks, accessible only by boat, which serves as a secluded hiding-place. The location is sufficiently remote and well-concealed that even locals are uncertain of its existence.

## In the Novel

### Key Scenes

The hiding-place below Dull Point becomes crucial when Harvey Whidden, a man wanted for his involvement in the murder at the Collinson house, is hidden there. Mrs. Whidden initially provides a false alibi for her husband, claiming he was home on the night of the murder, but the truth emerges: "Whidden came last night … said husband after him … frame him for Collinson trouble … I hid him in garret …" (017-024).

When the Continental Op learns the truth, he demands immediate action: "Below Dull Point! What are we waiting for?" (017-035). The party rushes to the waterfront, where they engage a green and white motor boat run by a pink-cheeked, towheaded youngster called Tim, who claims he can find any hiding-place below Dull Point (017-037).

The search along the coastline is methodical and tense. Half an hour from the dock, the boat rounds Dull Point and slows, moving close to the rocks. The searchers strain their eyes under the noon sun, examining every cleft and inlet (017-038). The third cleft they investigate seems as hopeless as the others, but now that Dull Point is some distance behind them, they cannot pass up anything (017-039).

Later, Mrs. Collinson is described as having "been in bed since we brought her back from Dull Point yesterday" (018-082), indicating the emotional toll of the events at the point.

Fitzstephan — the master manipulator — is revealed to have joined the expedition: "He went with us to Whidden's hiding-place below Dull Point. He knew Whidden, knew the dull man well" (023-021).

## Description

Dull Point is described as a blunt promontory with high, sharp rocks rising from the water's edge. The area below the point features a series of clefts and inlets in the rocky shoreline, each appearing forbidding and inaccessible at first glance. The searchers must examine each one, running the boat as close as possible to the rocks to peer inside. The setting sun adds urgency to the search, with the danger of being caught on the water after dark increasing the tension. The landscape is characterized by its harshness: jagged rocks, pounding surf, and hidden fissures that could conceal anything — or anyone.
