# Daisy Cotton

Daisy Cotton is the wife of Marshal Dick Cotton of Quesada, whose written confession—dictated under threat of death by Harvey Whidden—provides the Continental Op with crucial evidence linking Whidden to the kidnapping of Gabrielle Collinson and the murder of her husband Eric.

## Role in the Novel

Daisy Cotton appears only indirectly in the narrative, through the written statement she leaves behind. Her role is that of a coerced witness: Whidden forces her to provide a false alibi and then, fearing exposure, locks her in the garret of their home. Her written confession, found after her death, unravels the case that Sheriff Feeney and District Attorney Vernon had built against Marshal Cotton [022-PPP].

## Biography

### Background

Little is known of Daisy Cotton's life before the events of the novel. She was married to Dick Cotton, the marshal of Quesada—a "pompous, unintelligent man in his forties" [014-PPP]. The Cottons lived in a small house in the Quesada area, a dwelling with only one room on the ground floor besides the kitchen and an unfinished bedroom above [020-PPP].

### In the Novel

When Harvey Whidden came to the Cotton house after killing Eric Collinson, Daisy hid him in the garret at his request. Whidden told her the only way to save him was for her to claim he had been there on Friday night—providing a false alibi. When Vernon arrived, Whidden threatened to kill Daisy if she did not comply, and so she repeated the fabricated story [022-PPP].

Afterward, Whidden kept Daisy locked in the garret. It was there, while Whidden was downstairs getting food, that she wrote her confession—a rambling, terrified account that named Whidden as Collinson's murderer. The statement revealed that Whidden had tried to kidnap Gabrielle on Thursday night, had nearly been caught by Collinson, and had followed Collinson after seeing the telegram in the office, ultimately killing him [022-PPP].

Daisy's confession also detailed Whidden's subsequent actions: he went to San Francisco drinking, then decided to go through with the kidnapping anyway. He phoned a man who knew Gabrielle to learn who might pay ransom, wrote the kidnapping letter, and abducted Gabrielle from the road, taking her to an old bootlegger's hiding place below Dull Point [022-PPP].

Whidden killed Daisy Cotton before the authorities could reach her, silencing the one witness who could definitively connect him to both the murder and the kidnapping.
