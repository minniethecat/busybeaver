# Alice Dain Leggett

Alice Dain Leggett is the novel's most explicitly villainous character — a woman who
murdered her sister, framed an innocent man, raised her niece as a psychological
prisoner, and killed anyone who threatened her secrets. Her confession in the
laboratory is one of the novel's most dramatic set pieces, revealing the full extent
of her crimes with a chilling combination of pride and madness.

## Role in the Novel

Alice is the architect of the Dain curse as a practical reality. While others debate
whether the curse is supernatural or psychological, Alice has been actively
constructing it for decades — murdering her sister, erasing Gabrielle's memory,
addicting her to morphine, and killing to protect her secrets. She is the curse made
flesh: not a supernatural force but a calculating woman who uses the language of
curses to describe her own crimes.

## Biography

### Background

Alice Dain was one of two sisters in the Dain family. In Paris in 1913, she arranged
the murder of her sister Lily Dain, making it appear that Lily's infant daughter
Gabrielle had killed her own mother. She then framed Lily's husband Maurice Leggett
for the crime. After Leggett's conviction and imprisonment, Alice raised Gabrielle as
her own child, systematically erasing the girl's memory of the murder.

### In the Novel

By the time of the novel's events, Alice is living as Edgar Leggett's wife in San
Francisco, having maintained her false identity for years. When the diamond theft
investigation threatens to expose her past, she begins killing again — Ruppert,
and others who come too close to the truth.

Her confession in the laboratory (chapter 7) is the novel's centerpiece of villainous
revelation. She admits to her sister's murder, the memory erasure, the drug
addiction, and her manipulation of everyone around her. Her curse speech to Gabrielle
— delivered with "mad hatred" and "savage triumph" (007-046–007-048) — is one of the
most powerful scenes in hard-boiled fiction.

Alice dies in a confrontation with the Continental Op on the stairs, shot during a
struggle. Her death is ambiguous — the Op may have killed her deliberately or
accidentally — but it represents the violent end of the curse's human architect.
