# The Cliff Road

The cliff road is a rugged coastal path near the town of Quesada, running along the Pacific Ocean. It is the setting for one of the novel's most vivid sequences: the Continental Op's nighttime journey to a remote house and his discovery of a body at the bottom of the cliff.

## Overview

The cliff road is not truly a road but a rocky path that climbs along the side of a rocky ledge above the Pacific Ocean. It leads from the town of Quesada to a brown shingled house on a little hill, approximately three miles distant. The path's isolation and danger make it a fitting setting for the violence that the Op discovers there.

## In the Novel

### Key Scenes

The hotel clerk in Quesada gives the Op directions: go down the street to the end, take the fork on the ocean side, and follow along the cliff. The clerk warns that it is not really a road but more of a path, and that it would be impossible to find at night without knowing the country (013-021).

Walking the path, the Op describes it as rocky, climbing along the side of a rocky ledge that became increasingly steep. The Pacific Ocean crashes below. At one point, rounding a corner where the cliff is steepest — straight up and down for a hundred yards — the Op notices a small ragged hole in the path's outer rim, about six inches across, with fresh loose earth piled beside it (013-026).

Looking down from this point, he sees a bush twenty feet below on a stunted tree, with fresh brown earth on it (013-027). Further investigation reveals a man's feet and legs in black shoes and dark trousers, lying on a water-smoothed boulder, the legs disappearing into the ocean (013-028). The Op descends via a crooked ravine that cuts diagonally through the cliff, wading waist-deep into the Pacific to retrieve the body (013-029).

The body is later identified as connected to the Leggett case. The physical difficulty of the recovery — the Op is "a middle-aged fat man" who must climb down a ravine, wade through surf, and haul the body back up — underscores the grim determination required of the detective's work (013-028).

## Description

The cliff road is characterized by its physical danger and isolation. The path narrows from a dirt road to a rocky track barely distinguishable from the cliff face. At its steepest, the cliff falls straight down for a hundred yards to the ocean below. The setting is defined by the contrast between the narrow, precarious path above and the churning Pacific below, with boulders at the water's edge where the sea pounds against the rock. The natural violence of the landscape mirrors the human violence the Op finds there.
