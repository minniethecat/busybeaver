# The Murder of Doctor Riese

Doctor Riese, [[gabrielle-leggett|Gabrielle Leggett]]'s physician, is murdered on the
altar steps of the [[the-temple-of-the-holy-grail|Temple of the Holy Grail]], his body
arranged in an orderly position with a dagger wound in the chest. The murder is the
centrepiece of [[joseph-haldorn|Joseph Haldorn]]'s scheme — executed on
[[owen-fitzstephan|Owen Fitzstephan]]'s orders — to bind Gabrielle to the Temple by making
her believe she committed the killing under the influence of the
[[the-dain-curse|Dain curse]]. It is the most psychologically elaborate murder in the novel,
involving drugged victims, hypnotized killers, manufactured evidence, and a disappearing
corpse.

## Overview

The murder of Doctor Riese is the central event of the Temple sequence. Riese had become a
threat to the Haldorns because he was beginning to understand the truth about Gabrielle's
treatment at the Temple — her relapses, her drugged state, the nature of the "visions" she
was being subjected to. Joseph Haldorn, acting on Fitzstephan's orders, had Riese drugged
and brought to the altar chamber, where he was killed. The body was placed on the white
altar steps with a ritual dagger, and Gabrielle was then sent down to "discover" the
corpse — a psychological manipulation designed to tie her to Haldorn by making her believe
the curse had driven her to murder (012-027).

## Circumstances

### The Discovery

The murder took place in the innermost chamber of the Temple — a white-walled room
containing a brilliant altar of white crystal and silver (010-046). The Op, having gained
access to the Temple as a covert observer, discovered Riese's body:

> On the lowest of the three altar steps Doctor Riese lay dead on his back.
>
> (010-047)

There was a pool of blood on the white floor and a dagger nearby. The Op took the dagger
as evidence and instructed [[eric-collinson|Eric Collinson]] to guard Gabrielle. But when
he returned to the chamber:

> I didn't find Riese's body on the altar steps. It was nowhere in sight. The dagger was
> gone. Every trace of blood, except where the pool on the white floor had left a faintly
> yellow stain, was gone. Somebody had been tidying up.
>
> (010-100)

The disappearing corpse — a Gothic touch in a novel already thick with Gothic atmosphere —
became one of the Op's most disorienting moments. He was subsequently attacked by the
Finks, the Temple's mechanical operators, in a dark corridor (012-045).

### The Method

Joseph Haldorn's method was a refinement of the Temple's standard fraud technique:

| Stage | Action | Tool |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1. Selection | Chose Minnie Hershey as the instrument | Prior psychological conditioning through "visions and voices" |
| 2. Incapacitation | Drugged Riese and brought him to the altar chamber | Likely the perfumed gas used in Temple "visions" |
| 3. Execution | Made Minnie stab Riese under hypnotic influence | [[the-dagger|Ritual dagger]] |
| 4. Staging | Arranged body on altar steps in "orderly position" | White altar as theatrical backdrop |
| 5. Implication | Sent Gabrielle to "discover" the corpse | "Vision-and-voice trick" (gas + steam projections + piped voices) |
| 6. Cleanup | Removed body, cleaned blood, hid dagger | Temple staff (the Finks) |

### Key Participants

- **Doctor Riese** — Gabrielle's physician, killed because he knew too much about the
  Temple's true nature and could not be silenced any other way (012-027)
- **[[joseph-haldorn|Joseph Haldorn]]** — Temple leader who orchestrated the murder to
  bind Gabrielle to him through shared guilt (012-030)
- **[[aaronia-haldorn|Aaronia Haldorn]]** — Joseph's wife, who claimed no prior knowledge
  but later manufactured evidence to frame Gabrielle (012-032)
- **[[minnie-hershey|Minnie Hershey]]** — Former Leggett servant, drugged and manipulated
  by the Haldorns; made to stab Riese under hypnotic influence (012-027)
- **[[continental-op|The Continental Op]]** — Discovered Riese's body and was later
  attacked when he returned to investigate (010-099)
- **[[gabrielle-leggett|Gabrielle Leggett]]** — Sent by Joseph to see the corpse, made to
  believe she had committed the murder (012-030)
- **[[owen-fitzstephan|Owen Fitzstephan]]** — The true architect; ordered the murder
  because Riese "could not be kept quiet" (023-013)

## Investigation

The Op quickly determined that Gabrielle had not killed Riese. The body was lying in an
"orderly position," clearly indicating Riese had been drugged before being killed — the
work of someone methodical, not a terrified girl. The altar chamber door, which the Op
believed was kept locked, had been open, and Gabrielle knew nothing about the key (012-031).

Aaronia Haldorn, having discovered that her husband Joseph had "gone off center" and become
a dangerous maniac, began cooperating with the Op. She admitted manufacturing evidence —
she took Gabrielle's dressing-gown, retrieved the bloody dagger from beside Riese's body,
and planted both to corroborate the confession the Haldorns had extracted from the drugged
girl (012-032).

The full truth emerged later: Fitzstephan had engineered Riese's murder because Riese
could not be kept quiet. Once Fitzstephan's connection to the Temple became known, the
truth about his role in the Leggett killings would surface. He used Joseph and Minnie as
his "easily handled tools" (023-013).

## Aftermath

Riese's murder deepened the mystery and further entangled Gabrielle in apparent guilt.
The body's disappearance and the manufactured evidence made her appear complicit. Joseph
Haldorn died soon after in the Temple confrontation, and the official blame for Riese's
murder was placed on him as a "supposed madman" (022-078).

The murder also had a strategic consequence: it woke [[aaronia-haldorn|Aaronia Haldorn]]
to the full scope of Fitzstephan's intentions. Her cooperation with the Op, beginning with
the admission about the manufactured evidence, would eventually help unravel the entire
conspiracy.
