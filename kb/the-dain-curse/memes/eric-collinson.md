# Eric Collinson

Eric Collinson is Gabrielle Leggett's fiancé — a sincere but ineffectual young man
whose love for Gabrielle is one of the novel's few genuinely positive relationships
but whose inability to understand the forces arrayed against her leads to his death.

## Role in the Novel

Collinson serves as a measure of the curse's destructive power. He is innocent,
devoted, and well-intentioned — and these qualities do not save him. His death
demonstrates that the curse, whether supernatural or psychological, kills
indiscriminately: it destroys not only the guilty (Alice Dain) but also the innocent
who love Gabrielle.

## Biography

### Background

Little is revealed about Collinson's life before the novel. He is engaged to
Gabrielle Leggett and appears to come from a respectable background.

### In the Novel

Collinson appears early in the investigation as a concerned fiancé. He is present
during Alice Dain's curse speech and is horrified by it: "'Stop!' Eric Collinson
gasped. 'Make her stop'" (007-049). His reaction — protective but helpless —
establishes his character: he loves Gabrielle but lacks the resources to protect her.

Against the advice of others, Collinson marries Gabrielle after the deaths of her
parents, carrying her off while "she was too rattled to know what she was doing"
(014-071). His insistence on the marriage, despite Gabrielle's fears about the curse,
is both romantic and tragically misguided.

Collinson is killed — shot in his own home — becoming another victim of the
conspiracy. His death drives Gabrielle to attempt suicide, and his body becomes
evidence in the Op's investigation. The bullet hole in his ceiling and the empty
shells on his floor (014-001) are among the novel's most concrete pieces of physical
evidence.
