# Doctor Riese

Doctor Riese is Gabrielle Leggett's physician, brought into the case to provide medical
oversight during her stay at the Temple of the Holy Grail. His professional diligence
in checking on his patient leads him to overhear Joseph Haldorn's criminal plans —
with fatal consequences.

## Role in the Novel

Riese is the novel's most tragic bystander. He is not involved in any of the conspiracies
that entangle the Leggett household; he is simply a doctor doing his job. His murder on
the Temple altar is the clearest demonstration that the Haldorns will kill anyone who
threatens their operation. His death also triggers Eric Collinson's desperate dash to the
Temple, which precipitates the climactic confrontation.

## Biography

### Background

Riese is a San Francisco physician with a gray, dandified appearance — "the gray-faced
dandified man by the table" (007-013). He was present at the Leggett house when Leggett's
body was discovered, which is where the Continental Op first encountered him. His medical
authority is respected by both Madison Andrews and Eric Collinson.

### In the Novel

When Gabrielle's mental health deteriorated after her father's death, Riese recommended
sending her to the Temple of the Holy Grail for a short stay: "He said that letting her
go to the Temple for a short stay would do more to restore her to mental health than
anything else we could do" (009-008). This recommendation was made in good faith — Riese
had no knowledge of the Temple's true nature.

Collinson demanded daily reports on Gabrielle. When Riese made his last visit and told
Collinson the truth about what he saw, "Collinson hit the ceiling. He wanted the girl
taken away from the Temple immediately: the Haldorns were preparing to murder her"
(009-009).

The truth was worse than Collinson knew. Riese "went back to see Joseph before he came
up to the girl's room, and managed to overhear Joseph giving instructions to the Finks"
(012-026). Instead of leaving quietly, Riese "was foolish enough to let Joseph know he
had overheard him. Joseph locked Riese up — a prisoner" (012-026).

Haldorn then drugged Riese and had him placed on the altar. Using the Temple's
apparatus of gas and projected illusions, the Haldorns manipulated Minnie Hershey into
believing Riese was Satan, and she killed him with a large dagger. The Op describes the
crime in detail: "They drugged him and put him on the altar. They ghosted her into
thinking that he was Satan" (012-027).

When the Op discovered the body, "Doctor Riese lay dead on his back" on "the lowest of
the three altar steps" (010-047). By the time the Op returned to the scene, the body
had been removed and the blood cleaned — "Riese's body on the altar steps. It was
nowhere in sight. The dagger was gone. Every trace of blood, except where the pool on
the white floor had left a faintly yellow stain, was gone" (010-100).

Gabrielle later claimed responsibility for the murder in her drugged confession, but
the Op immediately knew she was lying: "As soon as I saw Riese's body I knew she hadn't
killed him. He was lying in an orderly position. It was plain he had been doped before
being killed" (012-031).
