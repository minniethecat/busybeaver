# The Murder of Eric Collinson

Eric Collinson, Gabrielle's devoted husband, is murdered on a cliff path in the village of Quesada, pushed to his death by Harvey Whidden on the orders of Owen Fitzstephan. The killing represents Fitzstephan's most brazen act in his campaign to eliminate everyone standing between him and Gabrielle.

## Overview

After marrying Gabrielle and taking her to the quiet coastal village of Quesada to recover from the Temple ordeal, Eric Collinson becomes the next target of Fitzstephan's obsession. Fitzstephan, having already arranged the deaths of Leggett, Alice, and Riese, now viewed Gabrielle as "his property, bought with the deaths he had caused. Each death had increased her price, her value to him" (023-014). When Eric carried her off and married her, "Fitzstephan hadn't hesitated. Eric was to be killed" (023-014).

## Details

### Circumstances

Collinson had taken Gabrielle to Quesada — a recommendation that had come through Fitzstephan, who had spent a couple of months there finishing a novel and knew its isolation well (015-044). Fitzstephan had even given Collinson a card to a local real estate dealer named Rolly.

The murder occurred on a Friday night, between eight and nine o'clock, on a cliff path above the ocean (015-002). Collinson's body was found the next morning by the Op, who had been summoned to Quesada by a telegram Collinson had sent after a previous attempt on his life.

### Key Participants

- **Eric Collinson** — The victim; Gabrielle's husband, pushed off a cliff (013-031)
- **Harvey Whidden** — Deputy marshal of Quesada, hired by Fitzstephan to kill Collinson for a thousand dollars (023-016)
- **[[owen-fitzstephan|Owen Fitzstephan]]** — Commissioned the murder; refused to pay Whidden afterward (022-080)
- **Gabrielle Leggett (Collinson)** — Eric's wife; discovered his body at daylight and attempted suicide (017-059)
- **[[continental-op|The Continental Op]]** — Collinson had wired him for help; arrived too late to save him (013-031)
- **Mary Nunez** — Housekeeper who testified about the Collinson household's unhappiness (014-059)

### Sequence of Events

Whidden made a first attempt on Thursday night but bungled it. The attempt frightened Collinson sufficiently that he sent a telegram to the Continental Op, asking for help. Whidden saw the wire in the telegraph office and realized he had to go through with the killing to save himself (023-017).

On Friday night, Whidden fortified himself with whiskey, followed Collinson onto the cliff path, and shoved him off. Collinson fell to the rocks below. When the Op found his body in the water the next morning, bones showed through flesh and clothing on his shattered back, and the back of his head was crushed (013-031).

Whidden then took some of Gabrielle's belongings — her comb and brush — and walked to a nearby town to mail a ransom note, attempting to frame the murder as a kidnapping gone wrong. He later kidnapped Gabrielle herself, holding her for the money Fitzstephan had refused to pay (023-018).

## Investigation

The investigation in Quesada was led by Sheriff Feeney and District Attorney Vernon from the county seat. They initially concluded that Gabrielle herself had killed her husband (015-001). This theory was supported by the absence of defensive wounds on Collinson and the testimony of locals.

The Op, however, believed Gabrielle was innocent and pursued alternative leads. He focused on the inconsistencies in the kidnapping narrative and the suspicious behavior of Whidden, the deputy marshal. Cotton, a local man who became a suspect, was eventually cleared when the truth about Whidden emerged.

Mary Nunez, the Collinsons' housekeeper, testified that Mrs. Collinson "didn't seem happy — wasn't happy" (014-060). She had speculated that Mrs. Carter (Gabrielle's alias) had married beneath her and regretted it.

The Op cornered Whidden at Dull Point, where he was holding Gabrielle captive in a cave. In the ensuing gunfight, Whidden was killed. Gabrielle was rescued, and she recounted her story: she had found Eric's body at daylight, then tried twice to shoot herself but lacked the courage (017-059).

## Aftermath

Collinson's murder was the incident that brought the Op to Quesada and ultimately led to the unraveling of Fitzstephan's entire scheme. Fitzstephan's refusal to pay Whidden — "you had him killed — hired Whidden and then didn't pay him" (022-080) — caused Whidden to kidnap Gabrielle, which in turn exposed the connection between Fitzstephan and the killings.

The Collinson family — Hubert Collinson, the wealthy lumber magnate, and his son Laurence — arrived to claim Eric's body and offered substantial rewards for the arrest of his murderer (015-026). The Op assured them that Eric's murder was "tied up with Gabrielle's troubles" and that he would get to the bottom of it (021-020).
