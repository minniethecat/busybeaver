# Jack Santos

Jack Santos is a reporter for a San Francisco newspaper who tracks the unfolding events of the Dain curse case in Quesada. His appearances at critical moments in the investigation illustrate the public and press interest in the sensational crimes.

## Role in the Novel

Santos serves as the representative of the outside world's interest in the Quesada case. He first appears emerging from the telegraph office, asking the Continental Op for news [019-PPP]. Later, he visits the Op's hotel room in pajamas, bathrobe, and slippers, demonstrating both his informality and his relentless pursuit of information [020-PPP]. His presence reminds readers—and the characters—that the murders, kidnapping, and cult activities have become public spectacle.

Santos also asks pointed questions during key moments, such as inquiring about Madison Andrews's reason for coming to Quesada [022-PPP]. His questions often mirror those the reader might ask, and his presence adds a layer of journalistic scrutiny to the investigation.

## Biography

### Background

Santos works as a reporter for a San Francisco newspaper. His assignment to the Quesada case reflects the newsworthiness of the events: a double murder, a kidnapping, a mysterious cult, and a cache of stolen diamonds. As a working journalist, he navigates between cooperation with and skepticism toward the authorities.

### In the Novel

Santos appears at several points during the Quesada phase of the investigation. He approaches the Op for information after filing his stories at the telegraph office, and later visits the Op's room for informal briefings. His role, while not central to the plot's resolution, highlights the media dimension of the case and the pressure that public attention places on the investigation.
