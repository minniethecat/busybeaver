# Gabrielle's Jewelry

Personal jewelry belonging to Gabrielle Leggett, including a string of pearls and a
topaz brooch purchased by Edgar Leggett from Halstead and Beauchamp, which Gabrielle
turned over to Minnie Hershey to finance her refuge at the Temple of the Holy Grail.

## Overview

The jewelry is identified through the investigation when Rhino Tingley, Minnie
Hershey's partner, is caught "trying to unload some jewelry" in a hock-shop (005-005).
The jeweler Halstead positively identifies "two pieces — a string of pearls and a
topaz brooch — as articles Leggett had purchased there for his daughter" (005-007).
The Op instructs the police to check if Minnie is "wearing an emerald ring" or if
other Leggett jewelry is present in her flat (005-008). Fitzstephan later explains the
full story: "After seeing Ruppert murdered, she beat it to the Haldorns' with what
money she had and her jewelry, turning the jewelry over to Minnie Hershey to raise
money on" (008-016).

## Narrative Role

### Plot Significance

The jewelry serves as a crucial link in the chain of evidence connecting Gabrielle to
Ruppert's murder and her subsequent flight to the Temple. When Minnie Hershey's
partner is caught pawning the jewelry, it provides the investigation with its first
tangible connection between the Leggett household and the Temple of the Holy Grail.
Minnie "bought a couple of pieces for herself — her man had picked himself up a lot of
dough in a crap game a night or two before: the police checked that — and sent the man
out to peddle the rest" (008-016). The jewelry thus functions as both financial
instrument (payment for sanctuary) and forensic evidence (linking Gabrielle to the
Temple through Minnie).
