# The Black Velvet Gown

A black velvet dress belonging to Gabrielle Leggett (later Collinson), found in her
Quesada bedroom closet along with other bloodstained items, and examined as physical
evidence in the investigation of Eric Collinson's death.

## Overview

The gown is first encountered as "a black satin dress, a once-white handkerchief, and
a pair of black suede slippers, all wet and muddy" found on the floor of Gabrielle's
closet in the Quesada house, the handkerchief "also wet with blood" (013-040). When
Rolly examines the dress more closely in the bedroom, it is described as a "black
velvet gown" with "some torn places in it — down near the bottom — but no
bullet-holes" (014-009). The shift in description between "black satin dress" and
"black velvet gown" may reflect two different garments or a variation in the Op's
initial observation versus Rolly's closer examination.

## Narrative Role

### Plot Significance

The black velvet gown is part of a constellation of physical evidence linking
Gabrielle to the night of Eric Collinson's death. Alongside the bloodstained towels,
the morphine paper, and the muddy slippers, the gown suggests Gabrielle was outdoors
and involved in some physical struggle or accident. The absence of bullet-holes in the
gown — despite the gun having been fired in the adjacent room — is significant: it
suggests she was not shot at, or that the shots were not directed at her. The torn
places near the bottom of the dress suggest contact with rough terrain, consistent with
the outdoor cliffside location where Eric Collinson's body was found.
