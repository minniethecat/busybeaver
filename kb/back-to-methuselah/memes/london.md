# London

The city where [[george-bernard-shaw|Bernard Shaw]] spent most of his adult life and the implied setting of the Barnabas brothers' scientific project in Parts II and III. London represents the center of English intellectual and political life — the place where ideas must prove themselves against sophisticated scepticism.

## Location in the Text

The political and scientific world of Parts II and III is recognizably Edwardian London, with its clubs, its Parliament, its scientific societies, and its class structure. The Barnabas brothers operate within this world while trying to transcend it.

## Significance

London represents the test of practicality for [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]]. It is not enough for the doctrine to be philosophically sound — it must survive contact with London's politicians, journalists, and professional sceptics. The Barnabas brothers' success in this environment is the proof that [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]] is not mere speculation.
