# French Academy

The French Academy of Sciences (Académie des sciences), founded 1666 by Louis XIV, is [[france|France]]'s preeminent scientific institution. [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] invokes it as the arena where the pre-[[darwinian|Darwinian]] debate over species transformation played out.

## Overview

The Academy was the center of [[europe|Europe]]an natural science in the 18th and early 19th centuries. Its members dominated the life sciences before [[charles-darwin|Darwin]], and its debates shaped the intellectual climate [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] critiques throughout the Preface to *[[back-to-methuselah|Back to Methuselah]]*.

[[jean-baptiste-lamarck|Lamarck]] was a member of the Academy. So were [[georges-cuvier|Cuvier]] and Saint-Hilaire. The Academy's public disputes over transformism prefigured the [[darwinian|Darwinian]] controversy that [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] argues still distorts evolutionary thought.

## Shaw's Usage

[[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] invokes the French Academy to anchor his argument that the scientific establishment has consistently resisted [[jean-baptiste-lamarck|Lamarck]]ian insight in favor of mechanistic, selection-based theories. For [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]], the Academy was not a neutral venue but a battleground.

### The Cuvier-Saint-Hilaire Debate (1830)

The famous 1830 debate marked a turning point. [[georges-cuvier|Cuvier]] argued for catastrophic extinctions and the fixity of species; Saint-Hilaire argued for transformation and structural unity across organisms.

> ...out of the open rupture in the French Academy between Cuvier and St. Hilaire... (P01-033)

[[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] treats this rupture as symbolic. [[georges-cuvier|Cuvier]]'s triumph, he argues, closed the door on [[lamarckism|Lamarckian evolution]] and set science on a path that would later crystallize into [[charles-darwin|Darwin]]ism.

### Lamarck's Reception

[[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] contends that the Academy's treatment of [[jean-baptiste-lamarck|Lamarck]] was emblematic of the establishment's failure:

> ...pond full of amoebas into the French Academy. Though Lamarck's way, the wa... (P01-047)

[[jean-baptiste-lamarck|Lamarck]]'s evolutionary framework was dismissed rather than refined. [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] returns to this point repeatedly: the Academy's rejection of transformism was, in his view, less about evidence and more about intellectual fashion.

## Historical Context

The Academy was the institutional home of the three figures central to [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s evolutionary argument: [[jean-baptiste-lamarck|Lamarck]] (elected 1779), [[georges-cuvier|Cuvier]] (elected 1795), and Saint-Hilaire (elected 1807). The 1830 debate was not merely academic — it defined the terms on which biology would develop for the next century.

[[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s interest is not antiquarian. He uses the Academy to argue that institutional science has a habit of canonizing the wrong ideas, a pattern he sees continuing in his own day with [[darwinian|neo-Darwinism]].

## Key Figures

- [[jean-baptiste-lamarck|Lamarck]] — Academy member whose concept of inherited acquired characteristics [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] champions

- [[georges-cuvier|Cuvier]] — champion of catastrophism and species fixity; victor in the 1830 debate

- [[etienne-geoffroy-saint-hilaire|Saint-Hilaire]] — advocate of transformism and structural homology across species
