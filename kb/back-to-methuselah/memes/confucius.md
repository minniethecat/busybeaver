# Confucius

The Chinese philosopher (551–479 BC) who appears among the historical figures conjured by the Oracle in Part IV of *[[back-to-methuselah|Back to Methuselah]]*. Confucius represents the Eastern wisdom tradition — a parallel to the Western philosophical tradition that [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]] both draws upon and, in his view, supersedes.

## Dramatis Personae

Confucius appears briefly but significantly in Part IV. His presence among the conjured figures makes an important point: [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]] is not a Western doctrine but a universal one. The [[life-force|Life Force]] operates across all cultures and civilizations, and the wisdom traditions of China are as much a part of its history as those of [[europe|Europe]].

## Role in the Play

### Part IV: Tragedy of an Elderly Gentleman

Confucius is summoned by the Oracle alongside other historical figures. His manner — dignified, philosophical, measured — contrasts sharply with [[napoleon|Napoleon]]'s bluster. Where [[napoleon|Napoleon]] asserts his destiny, Confucius offers wisdom.

## Key Dialogue / Speeches

Confucius's appearance is brief but his presence carries weight — he represents a tradition of ethical and philosophical thought that runs parallel to the Western tradition [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] primarily engages.

## Relationships

- [[the-oracle]] — The being who summons Confucius
- [[napoleon]] — The contrasting historical figure — Eastern wisdom vs. Western [[free-will|will]] to power

## Themes

Confucius represents the universality of the evolutionary impulse. The wisdom that the [[life-force|Life Force]] produces is not confined to any one culture — it emerges wherever human beings have had the time and peace to think deeply about existence.

## Real-World References

Confucius (Kong Qiu, 551–479 BC) was the most influential philosopher in Chinese history. His teachings on ethics, governance, and the cultivation of virtue shaped East Asian civilization for two millennia. [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s inclusion of Confucius alongside Western figures asserts the global scope of his evolutionary vision.
