# Faust

Johann Wolfgang von Goethe's two-part dramatic poem about the scholar who makes a pact with the devil for unlimited knowledge and experience. Goethe's *Faust* represents the German Romantic tradition — the striving, infinitizing impulse — that parallels [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]].

## Shaw's Context

Goethe, like [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]], was both a creative artist and a scientific thinker (Goethe developed a theory of colors and studied morphology). The Faustian drive for infinite experience is a secular version of the [[life-force|Life Force]] — the impulse that cannot rest in mere existence but must always strive for more.
