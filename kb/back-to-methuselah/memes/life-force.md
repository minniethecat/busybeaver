# Life Force

The immanent creative drive that [[george-bernard-shaw|Bernard Shaw]] posits as the true engine of evolution. In [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s philosophy, the Life Force (or [[creative-evolution|Élan Vital]]) is not a passive mechanism but an active, purposive impulse constantly striving toward higher forms of consciousness and being.

## Definition

The Life Force is the immanent, creative power driving evolution forward. It is not omnipotent but works "by the method of trial and error" — hence the world is full of its failed experiments. [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] borrows from Henri Bergson's concept of the [[vitalism|élan vital]] but gives it dramatic form: the Life Force is what makes the serpent in [[garden-of-eden|Eden]] suggest conception, what makes inventors invent, and what ultimately drives humanity toward longer life and greater mind.

## Shaw's Exposition

### In the Preface

[[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] traces the intellectual genealogy of the Life Force, distinguishing it from mechanistic views of evolution. He notes that "when we are turning in weary disgust and disillusion from [[neo-darwinism|Neo-Darwinism]] and Mechanism to [[vitalism|Vitalism]] and [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]], it is difficult to imagine how this new departure of [[charles-darwin|Darwin]]'s could possibly have appealed to his contemporaries" (P01-035).

He presents [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]] — the method of the Life Force — as "the religion of [[vitalism|metaphysical Vitalism]]" that "has been gaining the definite adherence of the masses" (P01-084). The Life Force transforms evolution from a blind mechanical process into something with direction and purpose.

### In the Play

In Part IV (*Tragedy of an [[the-elderly-gentleman|Elderly Gentleman]]*), the [[longevity|long-lived]] characters explicitly discuss the Life Force. [[the-he-ancient|The He-Ancient]] explains that earlier humans "could not fix and conduct the Life Force. It was like a wooden magnet or a lightning conductor made of silk: it could not carry the current" (008-006).

The body itself is described as something "handmade to receive the sacred Life Force. There is one person of the kind in every generation" (008-010) — a vessel for the vital impulse to work through.

In a moment of awe, the character Ecrasia calls the [[longevity|long-lived]] beings "the noblest projections of the Life Force" (008-013), acknowledging that extended life and heightened consciousness are what the Life Force has been working toward all along.

## Key Passages

> Creative Evolution is already a religion, and is indeed now unmistakeably the religion of the twentieth century, newly arisen from the ashes of pseudo-Christianity, of mere scepticism, and of the soulless affirmations and blind negations of the Mechanists and Neo-Darwinians. (P01-093)

> Unfortunately neither parent, Lamarck or Butler, can be said to have made a religion of Creative Evolution as the method of the Life Force. (P01-084)

> They could not fix and conduct the Life Force. It was like a wooden magnet or a lightning conductor made of silk: it could not carry the current. (008-006)

## Intellectual Context

The concept of a vital force running through all living things has a long history in Western thought. [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s immediate sources include:

- **Henri Bergson's [[creative-evolution|Élan Vital]]**: The French philosopher's concept of a creative impulse driving evolution, developed in *[[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]]* (1907), provided philosophical vocabulary that [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] adopted for dramatic purposes.

- **[[jean-baptiste-lamarck|Lamarck]]'s [[lamarckism|inheritance of acquired characteristics]]**: [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] championed [[jean-baptiste-lamarck|Lamarck]] because his theory allowed for [[free-will|will]] and effort to shape evolution — organisms striving to adapt pass those improvements to offspring. This aligned with the idea of a purposive Life Force, whereas [[darwinian|Darwinian]] natural selection offered only passive filtering.

- **[[samuel-butler|Samuel Butler]]'s anti-[[charles-darwin|Darwin]]ism**: [[samuel-butler|Butler]] argued that [[charles-darwin|Darwin]] had eliminated mind and purpose from the universe. [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] took up [[samuel-butler|Butler]]'s cause, insisting that evolution must be driven by something more than accident.

## Dramatic Expression

The Life Force is dramatized from the very first scene of *[[back-to-methuselah|Back to Methuselah]]*. In the [[garden-of-eden|Garden of Eden]], [[the-serpent|the Serpent]] whispers to [[eve|Eve]] the possibility of birth — of creating new life rather than merely existing. This is the Life Force's first manifestation: the impulse to create, to make more, to become more.

Throughout the five-play cycle, the Life Force works through successive generations: from the biological awakening in [[garden-of-eden|Eden]], through the intellectual formulation by the Barnabas brothers, to the practical achievement of [[immortality|extended lifespan]], and finally to the transfigured beings of Part V who have transcended bodily limitations entirely. Each leap forward is a victory of the Life Force over the inertia of matter.
