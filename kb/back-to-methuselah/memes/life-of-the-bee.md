# The Life of the Bee

Maurice Maeterlinck's study of bee society (1901), representing the tradition of literary natural history that bridged scientific observation and philosophical reflection — a mode that [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] adopted and transformed in his own evolutionary drama.

## Shaw's Context

Maeterlinck, like [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]], was a playwright who wrote seriously about nature. His work on bees, termites, and flowers demonstrated that literary skill and scientific curiosity could coexist — a principle [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] applied in dramatizing [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]].
