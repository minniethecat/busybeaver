# The Communist Manifesto

Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels's *Manifest der Kommunistischen Partei* (1848) — the foundational text of modern socialism. [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] references Marx in the Preface as one of the forces that reshaped the nineteenth-century intellectual landscape, noting that "Karl Marx had proclaimed in his Communist Manifesto" economic and political truths that made traditional religious narratives seem irrelevant (P01-071).

## Shaw's Reading

[[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s Fabian socialism was influenced by Marx but distinct from revolutionary Marxism. In the Preface, Marx appears alongside [[charles-darwin|Darwin]] as a figure whose ideas demolished old certainties — [[charles-darwin|Darwin]] discredited the [[garden-of-eden|Garden of Eden]] as biology, Marx discredited it as [[political-science|politics]] — without providing a replacement adequate to human spiritual needs.
