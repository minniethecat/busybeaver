# Europe

The continent whose intellectual history and political catastrophe form the backdrop to [[george-bernard-shaw|Bernard Shaw]]'s Preface to *[[back-to-methuselah|Back to Methuselah]]*.

Europe is the stage on which the drama of [[charles-darwin|Darwin]]ism, political failure, and the need for [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]] plays out — an arena of ideas and wars that demonstrated, for [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]], the bankruptcy of mechanistic thinking.

## Location in the Text

[[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s Preface is European in scope, tracing intellectual developments across the continent: French [[lamarckism|Lamarckism]], English [[charles-darwin|Darwin]]ism, German mechanism, and the First World War.

The war demonstrated the collapse of what [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] called the Infidel Half Century — and made the case for [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]] urgent.

> Neo-Darwinism in politics had produced a European catastrophe of a magnitude so appalling, and a scope so unpredictable, that as I write these lines in 1920, it is still far from certain whether our civilization will survive it. (P01-007)

> Within thirteen years of her death Europe became a hell. (P01-004)

The play dramatizes this crisis: [[eve|Eve]] and [[adam|Adam]] represent the creative and conservative impulses whose conflict plays out across European history, while [[darwinian|Darwinian]] fatalism enables the catastrophe.

## Europe in the Preface

[[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] traces the intellectual trajectory of Europe through several phases:

| Period | Movement | [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s Assessment |
|--------|----------|-------------------|
| 1789–1848 | Revolutionary idealism | Noble but impractical |
| 1848–1914 | Infidel Half Century | Loss of religious purpose |
| 1859–1900 | [[darwinian|Darwinian]] ascendancy | Mechanism replaces [[free-will|will]] |
| 1900–1914 | Neo-[[darwinian|Darwinian]] [[political-science|politics]] | Drift toward catastrophe |
| 1914–1918 | Great War | The bill comes due |
| 1918–1920 | "Peace" | Ongoing economic warfare |

The arc is clear: intellectual error becomes political disaster. [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s diagnosis is that Europe lost its creative [[free-will|will]] under [[darwinian|Darwinian]] orthodoxy and drifted into a war that should have been prevented by intelligent political action.

## The War as Demonstration

For [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]], the First World War was not an accident but the logical result of Neo-[[darwinian|Darwinian]] thinking applied to [[political-science|politics]]. If [[circumstantial-selection|Circumstantial Selection]] is nature's law, then nations are justified in competing ruthlessly.

> At the present moment one half of Europe, having knocked the other half down, is trying to kick it to death, and may succeed: a procedure which is, logically, sound Neo-Darwinism. (P01-008)

This is [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] at his most polemical: the war is not a failure of policy but a failure of philosophy. Europe's statesmen, lacking [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]]'s purposeful intelligence, could only drift toward catastrophe.

## Europe in the Play

The dramatic action reflects this European crisis. In Part II, [[conrad-barnabas|Conrad Barnabas]] declares:

"I shall clear out. It was hard enough to stand the party politicians before the war; but now that they have managed to half kill Europe between them, I can't be civil to them."

[[franklyn-barnabas|Franklyn Barnabas]] tells the politicians they "[[free-will|will]] go down to posterity as one of a European group of immature statesmen and monarchs who succeeded in all-but-wrecking the civilization of Europe."

[[england|England]] is the specific national setting, but the crisis is continental. The Barnabas brothers' gospel of [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]] is [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s answer to a European problem.

## Intellectual Geography

Europe in [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s vision is a map of intellectual traditions:

- **[[france|France]]** — Source of [[lamarckism|Lamarckism]], the evolutionary theory of purposeful striving that [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] championed
- **[[england|England]]** — Home of [[charles-darwin|Darwin]]ism and the political establishment [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] attacked
- **Germany** — Center of mechanistic philosophy and militarist ambition
- **Russia** — The eastern frontier where Materialist Communism arose as an alternative to drift

This geography matters because [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s argument is that [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]] must triumph across all of Europe — not merely in one nation. The [[life-force|Life Force]] operates universally, and the error [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] diagnoses is continental in scope.

## Legacy

Europe in 1921 was a continent in ruins, governed, in [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s view, by men without principles. His proposal — extend the human lifespan to three centuries — was deliberately provocative.

But the diagnosis was serious: European civilization could not survive without a philosophy that replaced mechanistic fatalism with creative [[free-will|will]]. [[eve|Eve]]'s bold choice, [[adam|Adam]]'s reluctant courage, and the Barnabas brothers' scientific dedication are [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s models for the European transformation he believed necessary.
