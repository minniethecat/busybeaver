# Pygmalion (Shaw)

[[george-bernard-shaw|Bernard Shaw]]'s 1913 play about Professor Henry Higgins's transformation of the flower girl Eliza Doolittle through speech training. *[[pygmalion|Pygmalion]]* treats social transformation — the making of a lady from a flower girl — as a small-scale model of the evolutionary transformation that [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]] effects on a cosmic scale.

## Relationship to Back to Methuselah

The mythological [[pygmalion|Pygmalion]] appears as a conjured figure in Part IV, and the theme of creative transformation runs through both plays. Where *[[pygmalion|Pygmalion]]* shows a human creator transforming a human subject, *[[back-to-methuselah|Back to Methuselah]]* shows the [[life-force|Life Force]] transforming the human species.
