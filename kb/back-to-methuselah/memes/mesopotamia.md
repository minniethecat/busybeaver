# Mesopotamia

The region between the Tigris and Euphrates rivers, referenced in the play's sweeping temporal scope as one of the locations where early human civilization emerged. Mesopotamia represents the deep historical background against which [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]] operates — the place where human society first developed the complexity that would eventually produce the evolutionary crisis [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] addresses.

## Location in the Text

The play's time-span reaches from the mythic [[garden-of-eden|Garden of Eden]] to the far future. Mesopotamia, as the historical location of the earliest cities and written records, marks the transition from myth to history — the point at which human evolution becomes documented and deliberate.

## Significance

Mesopotamia represents the scale of [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]]'s ambition. The [[life-force|Life Force]] has been working not for decades or centuries but for millennia, from the first cities of the Fertile Crescent to the [[longevity|long-lived]] beings of Part V. Understanding [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]] requires this deep temporal perspective.
