# Shaw's London

The Edwardian and interwar [[london|London]] where [[george-bernard-shaw|Bernard Shaw]] lived, wrote, and agitated — the specific historical context in which *[[back-to-methuselah|Back to Methuselah]]* was conceived and first performed. This [[london|London]] is not merely a geographical location but a historical moment: the aftermath of the First World War, the decline of Victorian certainties, and the beginning of the modern era.

## Location in the Text

The Preface, written in the 1920s and revised in the 1940s, reflects the [[london|London]] of its composition — a city still reckoning with the meaning of the war, still absorbing the implications of [[charles-darwin|Darwin]]'s revolution, and still searching for a faith adequate to the twentieth century.

## Significance

Shaw's London is the immediate audience for [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]] — the sophisticated, sceptical, war-weary public that [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] must persuade. The play is designed for this specific audience, in this specific place, at this specific historical moment.
