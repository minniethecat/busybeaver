# Baghdad

The capital of the Abbasid Caliphate and a major center of medieval science and philosophy. Baghdad appears in the play's historical scope as a representative of the continuity of civilization — one of the cities where human knowledge was preserved and advanced during the centuries when [[europe|Europe]] was in relative decline.

## Location in the Text

The play's characters, particularly the [[longevity|long-lived]] beings of later Parts, reference the sweep of human history that includes cities like Baghdad — centers of learning and civilization that rose, flourished, and declined across the centuries of the short-lived era.

## Significance

Baghdad represents the pre-modern history that [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]]'s timeline encompasses. The [[life-force|Life Force]] worked through Baghdad's scholars as it worked through the Barnabas brothers — the tools and vocabulary changed, but the creative impulse is the same.
