# The Tempest

[[william-shakespeare|Shakespeare]]'s late romance about Prospero, the exiled duke-magician who renounces his art. The play's themes — magic as knowledge, transformation as goal, renunciation as wisdom — parallel [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s depiction of the Ancients in Part V, who have transcended not magic but the body itself.

## Shaw's Relationship

[[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s late plays, like [[william-shakespeare|Shakespeare]]'s late romances, move beyond the realistic toward the visionary. The Ancients of Part V, who have renounced bodily life for pure contemplation, echo Prospero's renunciation of his magic — both represent the culmination of a creative life's work.
