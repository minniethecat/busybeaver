# Jarrowfields University

**Jarrowfields University** is the fictional academic institution where [[conrad-barnabas|Conrad Barnabas]] holds the chair of Biology. Mentioned only once in [[Back to Methuselah]], it functions as a marker of institutional scientific authority — the establishment from within which Conrad launches his critique of [[darwinian|Darwinian]] orthodoxy.

## Location

Jarrowfields is a fictional university; [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] gives no geographical details beyond the name. The name itself — combining "Jarrow" (evoking the industrial northeast of [[england|England]], associated with the Venerable Bede and later with the Jarrow March of 1936) and "fields" (suggesting pastoral or agricultural land) — may be [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s composite of the Victorian "redbrick" universities that emerged alongside the ancient institutions of Oxford and Cambridge.

## Role in the Play

### Part II: The Gospel of the Brothers Barnabas

[[conrad-barnabas|Conrad Barnabas]] is introduced as "Professor of Biology at Jarrowfields University" (003-006). The credential serves to establish his scientific bona fides — he is not a crank or an amateur, but a recognized figure within the academic establishment. His critique of [[neo-darwinism|Neo-Darwinism]] carries institutional weight.

The fact that Conrad holds a chair at Jarrowfields rather than at Oxford or Cambridge may be significant. [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] consistently positioned his intellectual protagonists on the margins of the establishment — close enough to understand its workings, distant enough to see its flaws. Conrad's redbrick affiliation signals both competence and independence.

## Significance

Jarrowfields University is one of several institutional markers [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] uses to ground his characters in professional reality. Conrad's professorship makes his biological arguments credible; [[franklyn-barnabas|Franklyn]]'s former clerical career makes his moral arguments credible. Together, the brothers represent the convergence of science and religion that [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] saw as necessary for [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]] to be accepted.

The university's fictional status allows [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] to avoid tying his argument to any real institution's reputation or controversies, while still claiming the authority of academic science. It is a rhetorical device as much as a setting.

## Notable Events

- [[conrad-barnabas|Conrad Barnabas]] teaches biology at Jarrowfields, presumably using his position to critique [[darwinian|Darwinian]] mechanism from within the scientific establishment
- The Barnabas brothers' collaboration — Conrad's biology, [[franklyn-barnabas|Franklyn]]'s theology — represents the interdisciplinary synthesis [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] advocated
