# Conrad Barnabas

[[franklyn-barnabas|Franklyn Barnabas]]'s brother and scientific collaborator in Parts II and III of *[[back-to-methuselah|Back to Methuselah]]*. While [[franklyn-barnabas|Franklyn]] provides the theoretical framework, Conrad provides the practical implementation — the laboratory work, the persistence, and the [[free-will|will]]ingness to dedicate a life to proving what his brother has theorized.

## Dramatis Personae

Conrad is the quieter, more practical counterweight to [[franklyn-barnabas|Franklyn]]'s visionary intellect. Where [[franklyn-barnabas|Franklyn]] soars into philosophical speculation, Conrad keeps his feet on the ground. Together, they represent the complete scientific temperament: theory and practice working in tandem.

## Role in the Play

### Part II: The Gospel of the Brothers Barnabas

Conrad provides the scientific credibility that grounds [[franklyn-barnabas|Franklyn]]'s speculations. He is the one who can answer the practical objection — "how?" — when [[franklyn-barnabas|Franklyn]] has answered the philosophical question — "why?"

### Part III: The Thing Happens

By Part III, Conrad's practical work has yielded results. The extension of human life is no longer theoretical — it has been achieved in at least one case. Conrad's quiet persistence has borne fruit.

## Key Dialogue / Speeches

Conrad's most important contributions are in his exchanges with sceptics who doubt the practicality of the brothers' project.

## Relationships

## Themes

Conrad embodies the virtue of persistence — the quality that turns visionary theory into practical achievement. If [[franklyn-barnabas|Franklyn]] is the prophet of [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]], Conrad is its engineer.
