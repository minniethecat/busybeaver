# Lubin

The "immovably sceptical" politician of Parts II and III. Lubin represents the intelligent, worldly resistance to visionary ideas — the kind of person who dismisses [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]] as a "fairy tale" until its success makes denial impossible.

## Dramatis Personae

Lubin is the dramatic foil to the Barnabas brothers. His scepticism is not unreasonable — it is the scepticism of experience, of a man who has seen many schemes fail. But it is also the scepticism of limited vision, unable to imagine possibilities beyond the familiar.

## Role in the Play

### Part II: The Gospel of the Brothers Barnabas

Lubin's primary dramatic function is to ask the hard questions: "And what does Science say to this fairy tale, Doctor Barnabas?" (003-014). His resistance forces the Barnabas brothers to articulate their ideas with precision.

### Part III: The Thing Happens

When the Archbishop achieves extended life, Lubin's worldview collapses — and his conversion from sceptic to believer is one of the play's most satisfying dramatic arcs.

## Key Dialogue / Speeches
