# Savvy Barnabas

Cynthia "Savvy" Barnabas is the daughter of [[franklyn-barnabas|Franklyn Barnabas]] in [[part-ii|Part II: The Gospel of the Brothers Barnabas]]. Quick-witted, irreverent, and politically informed, she serves as a foil to the male politicians and intellectuals — refusing to be "a goody goody wife while you men monopolize the conversation" (003-011-r189). Her uncle [[conrad-barnabas|Conrad Barnabas]] explains her nickname: "usually called Savvy, short for Savage" (003-006-r55).

## Dramatis Personae

Shaw lists her as: "Cynthia 'Savvy' Barnabas" (P02-008).

## Role in the Play

### Part II: The Gospel of the Brothers Barnabas

Savvy enters the play by taking charge of [[william-haslam|William Haslam]] with easy confidence: "I usually call Mr. Haslam Bill, short for William" (003-006-r56). When her father notes Haslam has an engagement, she replies: "I know. I'm the engagement" (003-006-r60). Her frank pursuit of the young rector is one of Shaw's most charming romantic subplots.

But Savvy is far more than a love interest. She is the most politically articulate member of the younger generation in Part II. When [[lubin|Lubin]] tries to charm her with small talk about books and plays, she refuses: "I am not going to sit here mumchance like an old-fashioned goody goody wife while you men monopolize the conversation and pay out the very ghastliest exploded drivel as the latest thing in politics" (003-011-r189). She then delivers a concise summary of modern evolutionary science — "Just the regular orthodox science of today" — that leaves the politicians floundering.

She and Haslam share a comedic chemistry, exploding into "shrieks of merriment" at the absurdity of the political discussion (003-014-r6, r15). Together they represent the generation that will outgrow the short-lived politicians — literally, once they discover the longevity theory.

## Key Dialogue / Speeches

> On being introduced to Haslam: "Rather. Sit down, Bill." And moments later: "I know. I'm the engagement." (003-006-r58, r60)

> "I am not giving you my own ideas, Mr. Lubin, but just the regular orthodox science of today. Only the most awful old fossils think that Socialism is bad economics and that Darwin invented Evolution. Ask Papa. Ask Uncle. Ask the first person you meet in the street." (003-011-r189)

## Relationships

- [[franklyn-barnabas|Franklyn Barnabas]] — Her father, whose intellectual seriousness she inherits
- [[conrad-barnabas|Conrad Barnabas]] — Her uncle, who nicknamed her "Savvy, short for Savage"
- [[william-haslam|William Haslam]] — Her romantic interest, the new rector
- [[lubin|Lubin]] — The politician whose condescension she effortlessly punctures

## Themes

Savvy embodies the generational argument at the heart of [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]]: the young already understand what the old refuse to accept. She knows the science, she sees through the politics, and she is ready for the future in a way that the short-lived establishment cannot be. Her romance with Haslam is simultaneously a genuine love story and a symbolic union — the intelligent young woman and the honest young parson who will together outlive their era.

Her nickname — "Savvy, short for Savage" — carries a double meaning: she is both shrewd ("savvy") and untamed ("savage"), a representative of the Life Force's restless push toward something better.
