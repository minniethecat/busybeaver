# Philosophie Zoologique

[[jean-baptiste-lamarck|Jean-Baptiste Lamarck]]'s *Philosophie Zoologique* (1809) — the first comprehensive theory of evolution, predating [[charles-darwin|Darwin]]'s *Origin of Species* by fifty years. [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] championed [[jean-baptiste-lamarck|Lamarck]]'s mechanism of acquired characteristics because it made [[free-will|will]] and effort into evolutionary forces.

## Shaw's Reading

[[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] explicitly directs his reader's attention to [[jean-baptiste-lamarck|Lamarck]]: "Let us fix the [[lamarckism|Lamarckian evolution]]ary process well in our minds" (P01-024). He traces the lineage from [[jean-baptiste-lamarck|Lamarck]] through Buffon to his own [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]], presenting it as a tradition superior to the [[darwinian|Darwinian]] one.

## Relationship to Back to Methuselah

The play dramatizes [[lamarckism|Lamarckian evolution]]: characters [[free-will|will]] themselves to live longer, and this acquired capacity is passed on. The entire arc of the play — from the [[garden-of-eden|Garden of Eden]] to the disembodied Ancients — is [[lamarckism|Lamarckism]] made dramatic.
