# Back to Methuselah

[[george-bernard-shaw|Bernard Shaw]]'s own five-play cycle --- *Back to Methuselah* considered
reflexively as a work of literature. [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] presents it as a deliberate,
mature revision of his earlier dramatic treatment of
[[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]] in
[[man-and-superman|Man and Superman]].

## The Five-Part Structure

[[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s cycle spans from the [[garden-of-eden|Garden of Eden]] to the year 31,920 AD,
dramatizing the entire arc of [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]] across five distinct
eras:

| Part | Title | Setting | Key Theme |
|------|-------|---------|-----------|
| Part I | In the Beginning | [[garden-of-eden|Garden of Eden]], 4004 BC | [[eve|Eve]] and [[adam|Adam]] choose birth, death, and creative life over immortal stasis |
| Part II | The Gospel of the Brothers Barnabas | [[england|England]], post-WWI | The intellectual case for extending human lifespan through [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]] |
| Part III | The Thing Happens | [[england|England]], 2170 AD | The first long-livers emerge in governance, exposing the inadequacy of short-lived administrators |
| Part IV | Tragedy of an [[the-elderly-gentleman|Elderly Gentleman]] | [[europe|Europe]] / [[ireland|Ireland]], 3000 AD | A mortal visitor confronts the [[longevity|long-lived]] civilization; tragedy of generational displacement |
| Part V | As Far as Thought Can Reach | 31,920 AD | The Ancients have transcended bodily form --- evolution completes its creative arc |

Each part advances the dramatic argument that [[free-will|will]], intelligence, and
creative impulse --- not passive [[circumstantial-selection|Circumstantial
Selection]] --- drive evolutionary progress.

## Shaw's Self-Assessment

[[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] describes the work's genesis with characteristic self-deprecation
that masks genuine ambition:

> I now find myself inspired to make a second legend of Creative Evolution
> without distractions and embellishments. My sands are running out; the
> exuberance of 1901 has aged into the garrulity of 1930. (P01-102)

The word "garrulity" is typical [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] --- disarmingly modest,
strategically disarming. But the ambition is unmistakable: this is to be
the definitive dramatic statement of [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]].

He further distinguishes *Back to Methuselah* from its predecessor by
the scale of its philosophical reach, presenting the cycle not merely as
theatre but as scripture for the modern age:

> Here, then, is a stone that we have left unturned, and that may be worth
> turning. To make the suggestion more entertaining than it would be to most
> people in the form of a biological treatise, I have written *Back to
> Methuselah* as a contribution to the modern Bible. (P01-017)

This self-conception --- a "modern Bible" --- signals [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s conviction
that the work transcends literary categories. It is drama, philosophy,
and prophecy simultaneously.

## Place in Shaw's Oeuvre

*Back to Methuselah* occupies a unique position in [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s work ---
neither a conventional play (it is essentially unperformable at full
length) nor a philosophical treatise (it is genuinely dramatic), but
something between the two.

It is [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s most ambitious attempt to unite theatre and philosophy. The
cycle responds directly to [[origin-of-species|Origin of Species]] by
restoring purpose and [[free-will|will]] to evolution, pushing back against the
[[darwinian|Darwinian]] worldview that eliminated creative intelligence
from the process.

No other work in the [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] canon attempts so vast a temporal canvas ---
from the [[garden-of-eden|Garden of Eden]] to the thirtieth millennium --- or so bold a
philosophical argument: that humanity can and must [[free-will|will]] itself beyond
its natural limits.
