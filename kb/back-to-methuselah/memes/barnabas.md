# Barnabas

The Barnabas family are central figures in [[george-bernard-shaw|Bernard Shaw]]'s *[[back-to-methuselah|Back to Methuselah]]*, appearing primarily in [[Part-II|Part II]] and referenced throughout the cycle. Their biological research into [[longevity|longevity]] provides the scientific foundation for the play's central premise.

## Family Members

- [[conrad-barnabas|Conrad Barnabas]] — The elder brother, a biologist who theorizes that human longevity can be extended through [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]]
- [[franklyn-barnabas|Franklyn Barnabas]] — The younger brother, also a biologist, who collaborates with Conrad on their longevity research
- [[savvy-barnabas|Cynthia "Savvy" Barnabas]] — The Barnabas sister, who serves as housekeeper and brings practical common sense to the brothers' theoretical discussions

## Role in the Play

In [[Part-II|Part II: The Gospel of the Brothers Barnabas]], Conrad and Franklyn expound their theory that humans can live for three hundred years if they cultivate the [[life-force|Life Force]] through creative will. Their ideas set the intellectual framework for the entire Pentateuch.

By [[Part-III|Part III: The Thing Happens]] (AD 2170), the Barnabas brothers' theories have been realized — but the political consequences prove more complicated than they anticipated.
