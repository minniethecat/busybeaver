# Burge-Lubin

The President in Part III (*[[Part-III|The Thing Happens]]*) of [[george-bernard-shaw|Bernard Shaw]]'s *[[back-to-methuselah|Back to Methuselah]]*. His name fuses the surnames of the two rival politicians from Part II — [[joyce-burge|Joyce Burge]] and [[lubin|Lubin]] — suggesting that by the year 2170, the political class has blended into a single type: the breezy, shallow, short-lived administrator.

## Dramatis Personae

> Burge-Lubin, the President (P02-011)

The stage direction introduces him as a figure of authority, but the play rapidly undercuts that authority. Shaw's point is that short-lived politicians, however confident their manner, lack the perspective to govern wisely.

## Role in the Play

### Part III: The Thing Happens

Burge-Lubin governs with "breezy assertion" and "shallow jollity" — exactly the kind of leader the long-livers regard as a child playing at adult responsibilities. When confronted with the Archbishop's miraculous longevity and the [[conrad-barnabas|Barnabas]] brothers' biological discoveries, his bluster collapses into panic.

His interaction with [[barnabas|Barnabas]] in the government office reveals the central dramatic irony of Part III: the people in power are the least equipped to understand what is happening.

> Burge-Lubin | Hallo, Barnabas! | | Barnabas | Without looking up… (004-006)

## Relationships

- [[joyce-burge|Joyce Burge]] — The Liberal politician whose name he inherits
- [[lubin|Lubin]] — The Conservative politician whose name fuses with Burge's
- [[mrs-lutestring|Mrs Lutestring]] — The former parlormaid, now a long-liver, whom he regards with suspicion
- [[barnabas|Barnabas]] — The Accountant General, whose scientific detachment baffles him

## Themes

Burge-Lubin represents the inadequacy of conventional short-lived political leadership. His hyphenated name is Shaw's comic gesture toward the convergence of political parties — by 2170, Liberal and Conservative have become indistinguishable flavors of the same shallow governance.
