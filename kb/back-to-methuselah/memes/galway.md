# Galway

A location in the west of [[ireland|Ireland]] referenced in [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s autobiographical passages in the Preface. Galway represents the provincial Irish world — the world outside [[dublin|Dublin]] — that formed part of [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s early imaginative landscape.

## Location in the Text

[[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] mentions Galway in passing, part of the Irish geography that shaped his upbringing. The specific reference locates his family history within the broader social and geographical landscape of nineteenth-century [[ireland|Ireland]].

## Significance

Galway, along with other Irish locations, represents the rooted, local world that [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]] transcends. [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s journey from provincial [[ireland|Ireland]] to metropolitan [[london|London]] is itself a small-scale version of the evolutionary movement from the local to the universal.
