# Don Juan (Mozart)

[[mozart|Mozart]]'s opera *Don Giovanni* (1787), whose central legend [[george-bernard-shaw|Bernard Shaw]] adapted for his first dramatic treatment of [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]] in *[[man-and-superman|Man and Superman]]* (1903). [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] later abandoned the Don Juan legend in favor of the [[garden-of-eden|Garden of Eden]] story, judging that the erotic associations of Don Juan distracted from the philosophical argument.

## Shaw's Use

[[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] describes his choice: "I took the legend of Don Juan in its [[mozart|Mozart]]ian form and made it a dramatic parable of [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]]" (P01-101). The Don Juan in Hell dream sequence — the philosophical core of *[[man-and-superman|Man and Superman]]* — uses the Don's legendary defiance of conventional morality as a vehicle for evolutionary argument.

## Relationship to Back to Methuselah

[[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] "abandon[s] the legend of Don Juan with its erotic associations, and go[es] back to the legend of the [[garden-of-eden|Garden of Eden]]" (P01-102). The shift from Don Juan to [[garden-of-eden|Eden]] represents a shift from the erotic to the cosmic, from the personal to the universal.
