# Charles Darwin

The English naturalist (1809–1882) whose theory of evolution by natural selection transformed biology. [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] carefully distinguished Darwin from the "[[darwinian|Darwinians]]" throughout the Preface, treating the man with respect even while attacking the philosophical uses to which his theory had been put.

## Dramatis Personae

Darwin appears in the Preface as a towering intellectual presence. [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] distinguishes "the view of Evolution taken by the [[darwinian|Darwinians]] (though not altogether by Darwin himself)" from [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]] (P01-018). The parenthetical qualification is crucial — [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] honors Darwin's achievement while rejecting Darwinism as a comprehensive philosophy.

## Key Dialogue / Speeches

[[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] notes that "Darwin could not read [[william-shakespeare|Shakespeare]]" (P01-018) — not as criticism but as evidence that even the greatest scientific minds have limitations.
