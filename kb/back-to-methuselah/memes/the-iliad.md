# The Iliad

Homer's epic of the Trojan War — the foundational text of Western literature and a representative of the heroic tradition that [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s comic, philosophical drama deliberately rejects. Where Homer celebrates martial glory, [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] celebrates evolutionary wisdom.

## Shaw's Reference

While not directly discussed in the Preface, the Iliad represents the kind of traditional literature that [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s contemporaries envied: "One of them expressed to me his envy of the ancient Greek playwrights because the Athenians asked them, not for some 'new and original' disguise of the half-dozen threadbare plots of the modern theatre, but for the deepest lesson they could draw from the familiar and sacred legends of their country" (P01-099).
