# The Odyssey

Homer's epic of Odysseus's ten-year journey home from Troy — the archetypal story of a long journey through trials toward a destination. [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]]'s *[[back-to-methuselah|Back to Methuselah]]* inverts this structure: the journey is not through space but through time, and the destination is not home but transformation.

## Shaw's Context

The epic tradition that Homer represents provides the narrative scale against which [[george-bernard-shaw|Shaw]] measures his own ambition. *[[back-to-methuselah|Back to Methuselah]]* spans tens of thousands of years — a journey not of one man but of the entire human species.
