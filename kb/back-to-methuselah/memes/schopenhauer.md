# Arthur Schopenhauer

The German philosopher [[schopenhauer|Arthur Schopenhauer]] (1788–1860) appears in [[george-bernard-shaw|Bernard Shaw]]'s preface to *[[back-to-methuselah|Back to Methuselah]]* as a key figure in the intellectual genealogy of [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]].

Shaw mentions Schopenhauer twice, in two distinct contexts: once as an example of how historical figures are misremembered by popular reputation, and once as a serious philosophical predecessor to the evolutionary metaphysics Shaw himself champions.

## Role in Shaw's Argument

### The Metaphysical Complement to Lamarck

Shaw's primary use of Schopenhauer is to establish the philosophical lineage of [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]]:

> In 1819 Schopenhauer published his treatise on The World as Will, which is the metaphysical complement to Lamarck's natural history, as it demonstrates that the driving force behind Evolution is a will-to-live, and to live, as Christ said long before, more abundantly. (P01-032)

For Shaw, Schopenhauer's concept of the Will provides the metaphysical half of the evolutionary equation: [[lamarckism|Lamarck]] described the *mechanism* of evolution (the inheritance of acquired characteristics through willed effort), while Schopenhauer described its *motive force* — the blind, striving will-to-live that impels all organisms upward.

This pairing of Schopenhauer and Lamarck is central to Shaw's argument that [[creative-evolution|Creative Evolution]] was not a new idea in the twentieth century, but had been anticipated by thinkers across the nineteenth.

### The Misattribution Critique

Shaw's other reference to Schopenhauer is more caustic. In a passage mocking the historical amnesia of the "pre-Darwinian age", Shaw writes:

> Schopenhauer was credited with inventing the distinction between the Covenant of Grace and the Covenant of Works which troubled Cromwell on his deathbed. (P01-004)

The point is ironic: Schopenhauer, like [[nietzsche|Nietzsche]] and [[richard-wagner|Wagner]] in the same passage, was treated by fashionable opinion as having originated ideas that were in fact centuries old. Shaw's larger argument is that the nineteenth century's sense of its own novelty was largely an illusion — and by extension, that [[charles-darwin|Darwin]]'s supposed originality should be viewed with similar skepticism.

## Significance for Creative Evolution

Schopenhauer's importance to Shaw's project in *[[back-to-methuselah|Back to Methuselah]]* lies in his concept of the Will. In Shaw's reading, Schopenhauer demonstrated that evolution is not a mechanical sorting process (as the [[neo-darwinism|Neo-Darwinists]] would have it) but the expression of an inner drive — a [[life-force|Life Force]] that wills organisms toward greater complexity, consciousness, and longevity.

This reading of Schopenhauer places him in a lineage that runs from the ancient philosophers through [[lamarckism|Lamarck]] and [[samuel-butler|Samuel Butler]] to Shaw himself. The "will-to-live" of Schopenhauer becomes, in Shaw's hands, the [[life-force|Life Force]] — a purposeful, creative energy that drives evolution forward.
