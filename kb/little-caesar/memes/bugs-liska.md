# Bugs Liska

Bugs Liska. A gangster and lieutenant to North Side gang leader Steve Gollancz, who appears alongside Joe Massara in a police lineup following the Courriere holdup investigation.

## Role in the Novel

Bugs Liska appears briefly during the police investigation of the Courriere holdup. As Steve Gollancz's lieutenant, he represents the North Side gang's presence in the Chicago underworld. His brief interactions with Joe Massara in the police station holding room provide a glimpse of the gangland code — casual defiance in the face of arrest, confidence that powerful connections will secure release ([041-025]–[041-062]).

## Character Profile

- **Full Name**: Unknown (known only as "Bugs" Liska)
- **Aliases**: Bugs
- **Affiliation**: Steve Gollancz's gang (North Side)
- **Role**: Lieutenant
- **First Appearance**: Police lineup, Chapter IV ([041-025])

## Biography

Bugs Liska is the lieutenant of Steve Gollancz, a North Side gang leader. Steve Gollancz had previously been arrested when police, investigating the Casa Alvarado robbery, mistakenly identified him as a suspect — "Steve and his bunch had just tapped a bank and Steve thought they had the goods on him" ([017-007]).

Liska himself is arrested and placed in a police lineup at the same time Joe Massara is brought in for the Courriere holdup investigation. In the holding room, Joe sees Liska among a dozen prisoners and they exchange a glance ([041-025]). As the prisoners are herded into the identification room, Liska, standing in front of Joe in line, whispers to him ([041-029]). When the peroxide blonde identifies Joe as the shooter, Liska observes: "Jesus, it's your funeral, huh?" ([041-046]).

Later, back in the big holding room, Liska tells Joe: "Joe, it sure looks tough for you" ([041-051]). When Joe protests that they cannot prove anything, a police sergeant strikes Joe. Liska's casual response draws the sergeant's attention. The sergeant laughs at Liska's defiance. Liska remains confident: he will not be held long "cause Steve's gonna spring me" — Steve Gollancz will secure his release ([041-060]). The sergeant, familiar with Liska, tells the turnkey: "Lock the dago up; you plant yourself over there in a chair, Bugs" ([041-062]).

## Key Scenes

**The Police Lineup** ([041-025]–[041-046]): Liska is among a dozen prisoners brought into the identification room. Standing in front of Joe Massara in line, he watches as the peroxide blonde identifies Joe as the Courriere shooter. His whispered comment — "Jesus, it's your funeral, huh?" — captures the gangster's fatalistic view of police encounters.

**"Steve's Gonna Spring Me"** ([041-051]–[041-062]): Back in the holding room, Liska offers Joe a grim assessment of his situation. When a sergeant strikes Joe, Liska's casual toughness amuses the sergeant, who addresses him familiarly as "Bugs." Liska's confidence that his boss Steve Gollancz will secure his release illustrates the patronage networks that defined Chicago gangland.

## Significance

Bugs Liska is a minor figure whose brief appearance illuminates three aspects of the novel's gangland world: the routine nature of police lineups for career criminals, the expectation that gang leaders would bail out their men, and the fatalistic camaraderie among detainees facing the law together. His confidence that "Steve's gonna spring me" contrasts with Joe Massara's genuine fear, underlining Joe's position as an outsider to the hardened gangster mentality.
