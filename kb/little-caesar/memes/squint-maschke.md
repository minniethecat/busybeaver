# Squint Maschke

Squint Maschke. A minor gangster in Little Arnie's orbit who, after Arnie's fall, attempted to join Rico Bandello's organization — only to be given twenty-four hours to leave Chicago.

## Role in the Novel

Squint Maschke appears briefly in the catalog of Little Arnie's displaced henchmen after Rico smashes Arnie's gang. His attempt to switch sides and serve Rico, met with a cold rejection, illustrates the ruthless consolidation of power that follows a gangland victory: Rico does not absorb the defeated, he purges them ([035-008]).

- **First Appearance**: Mentioned after Arnie's fall, Part IV ([035-008])

## Biography

Squint Maschke was a minor figure associated with Little Arnie's gang. When Rico Bandello destroyed Arnie's outfit, Maschke initially went into "a short exile." He then made the miscalculation of offering his services to Rico — a gesture of submission and self-preservation common in gangland power transitions. Rico's response was unforgiving: he gave Maschke "twenty-four hours to make a second disappearance" ([035-008]). Maschke's fate after this second exile is not recorded, but his ejection was part of the thorough dismantling of Arnie's network.

The same passage notes that Jew Mike fled to the South Side, Kid Burg moved to Cicero, and with the fall of Arnie's three lieutenants, "the last vestiges of his rule vanished" ([035-008]).

## Key Scenes

**The Purge** ([035-008]): In the aftermath of Arnie's destruction, his former associates scatter or attempt to ingratiate themselves with the victor. Squint Maschke, after a brief exile, makes the fatal error of offering Rico his services. Rico's response — twenty-four hours to disappear — signals that there will be no reconciliation, no absorption of the old guard. The new regime is absolute.

## Significance

Squint Maschke's half-sentence fate encapsulates the lot of the minor gangster in Burnett's world. When the boss falls, the underlings scatter or try to switch allegiances. Maschke's failed overture to Rico demonstrates the new leader's strategy of total replacement rather than co-option — a contrast to the more porous gang structures that preceded Rico's consolidation of power. He is a minor figure, but his ejection marks the final erasure of Arnie's influence in Chicago.
