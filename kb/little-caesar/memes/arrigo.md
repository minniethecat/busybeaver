# Arrigo

Arrigo. A minor associate in [[rico-bandello|Rico Bandello's]] network who provides the fugitive gangster with an army shirt during his flight from Chicago — a small act of assistance in the constellation of favors and loyalties that sustain a criminal on the run.

## Role in the Novel

Arrigo appears in a single sentence: Rico, preparing his escape, wears "a plain, dark suit and an army shirt Arrigo had given him." The mention is fleeting but significant — it demonstrates that even at his lowest point, Rico has a network of allies willing to provide practical help. The army shirt is a fugitive's uniform, chosen for anonymity and utility rather than style — a marked contrast to Rico's earlier sartorial vanity. [046-001]

## Significance

Arrigo represents the invisible support network that sustains every gangster — the dozens of minor associates whose names appear once and vanish, but whose small contributions (a shirt, a car, an alibi, a warning) make the gangster's career possible. The name "Arrigo" — Italian, working-class — places him in the ethnic community that is the novel's social foundation, one of the many neighborhood figures who exist at the boundary between the legitimate and criminal worlds.
