## Role in the Novel

Ma Magdalena is a fence (dealer in stolen goods) who operates out of a
fruit store in Little Italy. Her son Arrigo sits half-asleep by the
oranges while she conducts her real business in a back office
[012-001].

She is one of the few people Rico genuinely trusts. When he needs to
liquidate stolen goods, he goes to her [012-009]. When he is on the run
from the police with a price on his head, he tells Otero: "We got to get
to Ma Magdalena's. She's got most of my jack and a good hideout"
[043-062]. She lets him in at the alley door without hesitation
[044-001].

## Character Profile

Ma Magdalena is shrewd and unsentimental. She runs a legitimate
storefront while operating as a criminal fence — a classic Prohibition-era
dual identity. She finds Rico's lack of interest in women amusing,
making "a clucking noise and shook her head" when he denies buying
a ring for a woman, saying "You are cold, Rico. Don't like wine.
Don't like women. A very cold man" [012-020].

Despite her criminal role, she shows a maternal side — bringing Rico
coffee and newspapers while he hides in her back room [044-026], and
smiling broadly at his remarks [044-017].

## Key Relationships

- **Rico**: Her most important client. She holds his money and provides
  him a safe hideout, acting as both banker and protector [043-062].
- **Arrigo**: Her son, who helps run the fruit store front [012-001].
