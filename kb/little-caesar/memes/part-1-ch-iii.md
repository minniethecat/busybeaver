### III

(004-001)[[otero|Otero]] lay looking out the window at the electric sign across the Street.

(004-010)> Dancing

(004-011)Snow was falling past the windows. Otero lay smoking a big twenty-five cent cigar and singing softly to himself. He always sang when he was with [[seal-skin|Seal Skin]].

(004-012)“Some snow,” said Otero.

(004-013)“Yeah, some snow,” said Seal Skin, who was sitting with her feet on the window sill, smoking one of Otero’s cigars.

(004-014)“Heavy like cotton,” said Otero.

(004-015)“Yeah,” said Seal Skin.

(004-016)“Where I used to be it never snowed.”

(004-017)“Didn’t it?”

(004-018)“No, it never snowed.”

(004-019)Seal Skin blew out a cloud of smoke.

(004-020)“How come you ever left Mexico, Ramón?”

(004-021)“Well, I don’t know.” Otero scratched his head. “I just left.”

(004-022)“Was they after you?”

(004-023)“No, I just left.”

(004-024)Otero got up and put his arms around Seal Skin.

(004-025)“Some girl,” he said.

(004-026)Seal Skin gave him a push.

(004-027)“Wait’ll I finish this.”

(004-028)“Sure, sure,” said Otero, smiling and patting her on the shoulder.

(004-029)“Look,” said Seal Skin, “you’re a good guy, Ramón. But dumb. How come you hang after [[rico-bandello|Rico]]?”

(004-030)“Rico is a great man.”

(004-031)Seal Skin laughed out loud.

(004-032)“Yeah? Great, but careless. He’ll never die of old age.”

(004-033)Otero didn’t understand.

(004-034)“What you say?”

(004-035)“They’ll fill him full of lead. He’s too cocky.”

(004-036)Otero shook his head.

(004-037)“No, they’ll never get Rico.”

(004-038)“They get ’em all.”

(004-039)“No,” said Otero, “they’ll never get Rico. Once I say to him. ‘Look, you must be careful.’ But he say, ‘Not me, they’ll never get Rico.’ ”

(004-040)Seal Skin opened the window and flung her cigar down into the street. A gust of cold air rushed into the overheated room.

(004-041)“Listen,” she said, “that’s bunk. Rico’s no different from anybody else. You stick with Rico long enough and you’ll have a swell funeral. Why don’t you get into the beer racket? That’s safe.”

(004-042)“I go with Rico,” said Otero. “What do I care? I have no people. Once I had a brother but they shot him.”

(004-043)“The cops?”

(004-044)“No, the *rurales*. He was with Villa.”

(004-045)“Who the hell’s Villa?”

(004-046)“Villa was a great man, like Rico.”

(004-047)Seal Skin got up and took a drink from a bottle on the bureau. Then she said:

(004-048)“Let’s hit the hay, Ramón.”

(004-049)“Sure, sure,” said Otero.
