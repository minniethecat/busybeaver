# Young Chiggi

Young Chiggi. The son of Chiggi — the Toledo bar owner who staked a young Rico Bandello and urged him toward Chicago — Young Chiggi runs his own joint in Toledo, where Rico visits years later to reconnect with his old mentor's circle.

## Role in the Novel

Young Chiggi appears briefly in a late scene when Rico, flush with success, returns to Toledo seeking Chiggi's old associates. He serves as a bridge figure — the second generation of the Toledo underworld that launched Rico's career, now on the verge of following Rico's path to a bigger city ([050-029]–[050-032]).

## Character Profile

- **Full Name**: Unknown (known only as "Young Chiggi")
- **Aliases**: Young Chiggi
- **Affiliation**: Independent (Toledo)
- **Role**: Bar owner, son of Chiggi
- **First Appearance**: Rico's visit to Toledo, Part VII ([050-029])

## Biography

Young Chiggi is the son of the original Chiggi, a Toledo bar owner who was a pivotal figure in Rico Bandello's early life. The elder Chiggi had advised the young Rico that he had "big town stuff" in him and urged him to leave Toledo for Chicago, even staking him money — though Rico blew the stake on a woman before eventually hitting the rails with Otero ([038-048]).

By the time Rico returns to Toledo years later, the elder Chiggi is in federal prison in Atlanta. Rico visits Chiggi's old place to find it padlocked by the Federal Authorities ([050-009]). A fruit-store owner next door tells him that Chiggi's son — Young Chiggi — runs a place a couple of blocks away ([050-027]).

Young Chiggi is portrayed as "a dressed-up wop" who thought himself better than his father: "He wouldn't even wait on a customer, but sat all day in the back of his joint reading the Police Gazette or playing solitaire" ([050-029]). Things are going well for him, and he is considering selling out and moving to Chicago or Detroit — mirroring his father's advice to Rico years earlier ([050-029]).

When Rico visits, Young Chiggi does not even look up from his solitaire game until Rico speaks ([050-031]–[050-032]).

## Key Scenes

**The Return to Toledo** ([050-009]–[050-032]): Rico, now successful in Chicago, walks to Chiggi's old place only to find it padlocked by federal authorities. A neighbor directs him to Young Chiggi's bar. When Rico arrives, Young Chiggi is absorbed in solitaire, indifferent to the customer — a portrait of the second-generation Toledo underworld figure, casually prosperous and considering his own move to the big city.

## Significance

Young Chiggi is a minor figure who represents generational continuity in the novel's criminal world. The elder Chiggi launched Rico toward Chicago; his son, running the family bar in Toledo, is on the verge of following the same trajectory. The brief encounter underscores the novel's theme of ambition as a cyclical force — each generation of small-time operators breeds the next wave of big-city aspirants.
