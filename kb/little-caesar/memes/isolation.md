Power in *Little Caesar* is a solvent that dissolves every human connection — the higher Rico Bandello rises, the more completely isolated he becomes.

## Definition

Isolation in Burnett's novel is the psychological cost of gangster ambition. Unlike the communal world of Little Italy where the novel begins, Rico's ascent strips away friends, allies, lovers, and finally even enemies. His isolation is both practical (he trusts no one, and with good reason) and existential (he has no identity outside his role as boss).

## In the Novel

Rico's trajectory traces a steady movement from community to solitude:

- **The card game opening** — Rico begins surrounded by colleagues at Palermo Club, part of Vettori's crew, embedded in a web of relationships [002-002]–[002-046]. He is already alienated (he "hated Joe" [002-014]) but not yet isolated.

- **After the takeover** — Once Rico displaces Vettori and then Little Arnie, his only companions are subordinates. The novel explicitly notes: "Rico and Joe felt queer alone together. They were silent" [002-110] — even with his oldest friend, Rico cannot connect.

- **The fugitive** — After Joe's betrayal, Rico is completely alone, hiding in Ma Magdalena's boarding house [043-046]. His isolation is now literal: a wanted man who can trust no one, cut off from the organization he built.

- **Final exile** — Rico flees Chicago and dies in another city, anonymous, stripped of his empire, his reputation meaningless. His final thoughts are memories of "Montana and Little Arnie and the Big Boy" [048-002] — the only community he has left is the ghosts of gangsters who fell before him.

## Thematic Function

Burnett anticipates a central theme of the gangster genre: the boss at the top is the most vulnerable man in the organization. Rico's isolation is not a punishment for his crimes but the structural consequence of his success — the same force that elevated him ensures he will die alone. This theme would become central to later gangster films like *Scarface* (1932) and *The Godfather* (1972), but Burnett established it in 1929 with stark economy.
