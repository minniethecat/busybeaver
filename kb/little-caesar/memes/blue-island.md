# Blue Island

**Blue Island, Illinois** appears in *Little Caesar* as a geographic marker on [[rico-bandello|Rico Bandello's]] escape route from Chicago — a suburb where a motorcycle policeman waves to him, unaware that he is a wanted fugitive, in a moment that captures the mixture of luck and nerve that defines Rico's flight.

## Overview

Blue Island is a working-class suburb south of Chicago, founded in the nineteenth century and named for the blue-tinged glacial ridge on which it sits. In Burnett's novel, it appears in a single sentence — "A motor cop out in Blue Island had waved to him" — that encapsulates the fugitive's paradox: Rico is simultaneously the most wanted man in Chicago and anonymous enough to pass unnoticed through the suburbs. [046-001]

## Significance in the Story

The Blue Island moment is brief and almost comic: a policeman waves at the very man the entire Chicago police force is hunting. It is Rico's last piece of good luck, the final moment when his nerve and his anonymity hold, before the long decline that ends in a Hammond gutter. The geographic trajectory — from Chicago's center to Blue Island to Hammond — maps Rico's descent from kingpin to fugitive.
