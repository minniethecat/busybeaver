# Arnold Worch

Arnold Worch. A North Side underworld figure referenced in a newspaper clipping — one of the many names that flicker through *Little Caesar's* news-media background, establishing the density and reach of Chicago's criminal environment beyond the novel's central cast.

## Role in the Novel

Arnold Worch appears only in a newspaper item, part of the background texture of crime reporting that Burnett uses to establish the public dimension of the gangster world. The newspapers in *Little Caesar* function almost as a secondary character — they shape public perception, create the celebrity that destroys Rico, and document the larger criminal landscape. Worch is one name in that landscape, a reminder that the novel's central drama is only one thread in a much larger fabric. [SCN28 R26]

## Significance

Worch is a marginal figure whose significance lies less in any specific action than in his very existence as a name in the newspaper — the media ecosystem that both enables and ultimately destroys the gangster's career. In Burnett's novel, the press is the gangster's necessary enemy: it creates the celebrity that feeds Rico's ego but also the visibility that makes him traceable. Worch is one pixel in that larger picture.
