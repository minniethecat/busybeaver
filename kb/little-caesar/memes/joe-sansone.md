## Role in the Novel

Joe Sansone is a core member of Sam Vettori's gang. At the banquet
thrown for Rico, he is listed alongside Killer Pepi and Ottavio Vettori
as "the most important men in the gang next to Sam Vettori" [026-057].

He is a former lightweight boxer, described as a gunman, and appears at
key moments throughout the novel — guarding doors, participating in
raids, and serving as one of Rico's trusted men [032-004].

## Character Profile

Sansone is physically small — Killer Pepi mockingly calls him "the
half-pint" [026-061] — but he is tough and professional. Unlike
Ottavio Vettori, he is not impressed by Rico's flashy clothes and
jewelry: "Joe Sansone was not impressed; he knew better" [026-059].

He has a certain education or pretension: at the banquet, he corrects
someone's pronunciation to "Apéritif" [026-014], suggesting he has
picked up some refinement beyond the typical gangster.

## Key Relationships

- **Sam Vettori**: His boss and the gang leader.
- **Rico**: Initially one of Rico's rivals for status within the gang,
  but later serves as one of Rico's armed escorts [034-007].
- **Killer Pepi**: Fellow lieutenant, with whom he shares top status.
- **Ottavio Vettori**: Fellow lieutenant, though portrayed as rivals
  for Rico's favor [032-004].
