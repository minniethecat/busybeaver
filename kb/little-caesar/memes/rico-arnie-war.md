Rico's dismantling of Little Arnie's gambling operation is the novel's most sustained depiction of gangster territorial conquest — a campaign of intimidation, violence, and absorption.

## Background

Little Arnie ran the biggest gambling joint on the North Side and had been a fixture of Chicago's underworld since arriving from New York, where "his reputation had got so bad... that no one would do business with him" [035-006]. He controlled the neutral zone between the Vettori/Montana territories — a lucrative buffer that Rico, newly in command of Vettori's crew, coveted.

Arnie, "fearing the worst, committed blunder after blunder; first, he made advances to Rico, then, growing frightened, he tried to fight him" [035-007]. This vacillation sealed his fate.

## The Conflict

Arnie's fatal move was hiring Detroit gunmen to assassinate Rico — a double-cross Rico anticipated. "He'll only double-cross me once," Rico said coldly [026-080]. When the hit failed, Rico used it as justification for all-out war.

Rico confronted Arnie directly: "you been double-crossing me for two months. In the second place, you hire these bums here to pop me" [034-059]. Arnie's desperate denial — "I wouldn't double-cross you" [034-061] — was useless. Rico had already decided.

Joe Peeper, Arnie's lieutenant, tried to distance himself: "I didn't know nothing about them Detroit bums" [034-096]. But in Rico's world, guilt was collective.

## Outcome

Arnie was killed, his organization dissolved, and his territory absorbed. Rico emerged as the undisputed master of the neutral zone, controlling gambling operations that had previously been independent. The conquest demonstrated that the old order — Arnie, Vettori, the generation of cautious gangsters — could not withstand the new.

## Significance

The Arnie war is Rico's coming-of-age as a gangster leader. It proves his maxim that a rival "will only double-cross me once" — ruthless preemption as business strategy. But it also plants the seeds of his later downfall: Arnie's fate mirrors what will eventually happen to Rico himself.
