# Chicago Red

Bill "Chicago Red" Hackett. A physically imposing gangster in the alcohol racket who initially clashes with Rico Bandello but later falls under his command.

## Role in the Novel

Chicago Red appears late in the novel as one of the alcohol runners working with Young Chiggi. He represents the street-level muscle that Rico must manage during his post-Chicago exile — a man whose physical dominance and personal grudges pose a constant management challenge for the more calculating Rico ([188-001]–[190-001]).

## Character Profile

- **Full Name**: Bill Hackett
- **Aliases**: Chicago Red, Red Hackett
- **Affiliation**: Chiggi's gang (alcohol racket)
- **Role**: Alcohol runner, enforcer
- **First Appearance**: Late novel ([188-001])

## Biography

Chicago Red is a former Chicago gangster who left the city "under a cloud" — there was a rumour he had "got in bad with a South Side gang" and fled to avoid being killed ([190-001]). He settles into the alcohol racket as a partner with Young Chiggi.

Physically, Red is enormous: over six feet tall, weighing about two hundred pounds, with "muscles like a wrestler, a bull neck, and enormous hairy hands" ([190-001]). He takes an immediate dislike to Rico and "never missed an opportunity of bullying him" ([190-001]).

However, under Rico's guidance, Chiggi's business prospers. Chicago Red, "impressed by Rico's reputation, carried out his orders and never argued" ([196-001]). Despite this surface compliance, Rico fears both Red and Chiggi — Red is a dangerous drunk who "would boast about his former connection with Steve Gollancz," threatening Rico's concealed identity as Cesare Bandello ([198-005]).

Eventually, having accumulated enough money, Red announces his departure: "Old Red's going back to the big burg... I'm going back and show 'em who Red Hackett is" ([210-001]).

## Key Scenes

**Bullying Rico** ([190-001]): Red takes an immediate dislike to Rico upon his arrival and exploits his physical advantage to intimidate him.

**Following orders** ([196-001]): As Rico's reputation and results become undeniable, Red falls in line, executing Rico's directives in the alcohol business.

**Drunken boasting** ([198-005]): When drunk, Red brags about his connection to Steve Gollancz — a serious security risk for Rico, who is hiding his true identity.

## Significance

Chicago Red exemplifies the type of gangster Rico must learn to manage through reputation rather than physical force. Red's physical superiority and initial hostility highlight Rico's reliance on strategic thinking and reputation. His eventual compliance demonstrates the gangland principle that results command respect, while his drunken indiscretion represents the constant threat of exposure that haunts Rico's exile.
