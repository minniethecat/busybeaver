# Casa Alvarado Robbery

The **Casa Alvarado Robbery** is a pivotal heist in W.R. Burnett's *Little Caesar* — a lucrative score that demonstrates [[rico-bandello|Rico Bandello]]'s operational superiority over [[vettori-gang|Sam Vettori]] and bankrolls the gang's expansion, but whose aftermath exposes the greed and disloyalty eating at the crew from within. Where the [[courriere-holdup]] was Vettori's plan that went wrong, the Casa Alvarado job is Rico's — and the difference between the two operations measures his ascent from lieutenant to de facto leader.

## Summary

The [[casa-alvarado|Casa Alvarado]], an apartment building distinguished by its "huge electric sign" [009-054] on a deserted Chicago street, enters the gang's consciousness during planning for the Courriere holdup [002-035]. After the Courriere job's chaotic outcome, Rico identifies the Casa Alvarado as a high-value target and organizes the crew for a second major score. Unlike the Courriere operation, the Casa Alvarado robbery succeeds: the gang nets significant cash — Otero alone receives "over a grand and a half" [029-023] — and Rico consolidates his reputation as the gang's most capable operator. The robbery becomes a touchstone in Rico's memory, counted among "all the steps which had led to his own rise and fall" [048-012].

## Timeline

1. **Identification**: Joe Massara raises the Casa Alvarado as a potential target during Courriere planning [002-035]
2. **Execution**: Rico leads the crew to the Casa Alvarado; the heist succeeds without the chaos that marred the Courriere job [009-054]
3. **Aftermath**: The gang splits the proceeds; Otero burns through his share, exposing the crew's financial indiscipline [029-015] [029-023]

## Consequences

The robbery's financial success further erodes Vettori's authority: Rico has now planned and executed a clean score without the boss, proving he no longer needs Vettori's guidance. But the heist also reveals the gang's internal rot — [[otero|Otero]]'s immediate squandering of his share [029-015] shows that the crew cannot retain wealth, a weakness that will contribute to their eventual disintegration. Rico, looking back at the end, counts the Casa Alvarado robbery among the crucial steps of his trajectory [048-012].

## Significance

The Casa Alvarado Robbery functions as a structural mirror to the [[courriere-holdup]]: one fails, one succeeds; one is Vettori's plan, one is Rico's. Together they trace Rico's rise and the gang's shift in leadership. But the robbery also introduces a theme that runs through Burnett's novel — the gangster's inability to convert criminal success into lasting power. Otero's immediate dissipation of a year's wages [029-023] is the rule, not the exception, in Rico's world.
