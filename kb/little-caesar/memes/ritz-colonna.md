# Ritz Colonna

Ritz Colonna. The loyal lieutenant to North Side crime boss Pete Montana, a former prizefighter who serves as Montana's enforcer and right-hand man.

## Role in the Novel

Ritz Colonna functions as Pete Montana's shadow — always present, always watching, and fiercely protective of his boss's interests. His role in the narrative is secondary to Montana's, but he embodies the street-level muscle of the established gang hierarchy that Rico Bandello is disrupting ([044-001]).

## Character Profile

- **Full Name**: Ritz Colonna
- **Aliases**: Ritz
- **Affiliation**: Pete Montana's North Side gang
- **Role**: Lieutenant, enforcer
- **First Appearance**: Chapter 2 ([044-001])

## Biography

Ritz Colonna accompanies Pete Montana to the pivotal territory negotiation with Rico. He is described as "once a ham prizefighter," now "a small, bull-necked man with a battered, dark face" wearing shabby clothes and an old cap ([044-001]).

During the meeting, Colonna voices open hostility toward Little Arnie, saying he was "just aching to bump that bird off" ([044-013]). He initially suspects Rico of attempting to muscle in on Montana's territory, admitting later: "I had the wrong steer" ([046-009]).

After Montana cedes Kiketown to Rico, Ritz offers his hand to Rico as they leave — a gesture of acceptance from the old guard ([046-012]).

The Big Boy later warns Rico that after Montana's fall, "Ritz Colonna and a couple of other lowdown bums is gonna make a rush" — Colonna will fight to defend his boss's legacy ([060-018]).

## Key Scenes

**Territory meeting with Rico** ([044-001]–[046-012]): Colonna sits silently for much of the meeting, interjecting only to express his eagerness to eliminate Little Arnie. He follows Montana's lead, echoing his chief's words.

> "He wasn't no good," said Colonna; "I was just aching to bump that bird off." ([044-013])

**Admitting error** ([046-009]): When Montana reveals that Colonna had suspected Rico of encroaching on North Side territory, Ritz concedes his mistake — a rare moment of humility from a gangland enforcer.

## Significance

Ritz Colonna represents the old-guard enforcer archetype: physically imposing, unquestioningly loyal, and ultimately disposable when his boss falls. His presence at Montana's side reinforces Montana's status, while his shabby appearance and the Big Boy's dismissive assessment ("lowdown bum") underscore the gap between the established North Side gang and the rising power Rico represents.
