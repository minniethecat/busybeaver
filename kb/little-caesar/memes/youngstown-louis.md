# Youngstown Louis

Youngstown Louis. The street alias of Luigi De Angelo, a minor figure in the Hammond underworld who operates out of [[sansotta|Sansotta's]] card room — a small-time gambler whose nickname, linking him to [[youngstown|Youngstown, Ohio]], creates an implicit connection to [[rico-bandello|Rico Bandello's]] own origins.

## Role in the Novel

Youngstown Louis is one of the background presences in Sansotta's establishment during Rico's period of hiding in Hammond. His nickname is significant: in the novel's symbolic geography, "Youngstown" represents the working-class immigrant origins that Rico has spent his career trying to transcend. Louis, content to remain a small-time card-room figure, is what Rico might have become had he never left Ohio — a mirror of diminished possibilities. [SCN28 R26]

## Significance

Youngstown Louis is a minor figure whose importance is almost entirely symbolic. He shares a hometown with the novel's protagonist but none of the ambition — a man content with the small stakes of a Hammond card room. In Burnett's taxonomy of gangster types, Louis is the one who never rose, never fell, and never mattered: the vast majority of the underworld, the human material out of which a "Little Caesar" occasionally, and briefly, emerges.
