# Francis Wood

Francis Wood. The owner of the [[casa-alvarado|Casa Alvarado]], an "up and up place" that becomes the target of [[vettori-gang|Sam Vettori's gang]] after Scabby provides intelligence that the club is "lousy with jack" and "they've never been tapped."

## Role in the Novel

Francis Wood never appears directly in the novel — he is mentioned only once, in the planning session for the Casa Alvarado robbery, when Joe Massara identifies him as the owner. Joe's familiarity with the establishment ("I nearly got an engagement there once") is what qualifies him for the inside-man role. The fact that Wood operates through an agent rather than personally — "it was all done through an agent" — means Joe can enter the club unrecognized, a detail that makes the robbery possible. ## Biography

Almost nothing is known of Francis Wood beyond his ownership of the Casa Alvarado and his use of agents for booking talent — a common practice in the entertainment industry that inadvertently provides the gang with their inside advantage. His club is described as "one of Francis Wood's joints" by Joe, suggesting he may own multiple establishments. The manager, described derisively by Vettori as "a goddamn bohunk" with "an ounce of fight in him," is the only Casa Alvarado employee who appears directly. [001-023]

## Significance

Francis Wood is a minor but representative figure — the legitimate businessman whose enterprise exists in the same urban ecosystem as the gangsters who prey upon it. His absence from the narrative (he never appears, never speaks, never learns he has been victimized on the page) is itself significant: in Burnett's world, the victims of crime are not characters but conditions, the background against which the gangsters' drama plays out.
