# Blondy Belle

Blondy Belle. "The swellest woman in Little Italy" — a handsome, bold Italian woman who moves between the most powerful men in Chicago's underworld, first as Little Arnie's companion and then as Rico Bandello's consort during his rise.

## Role in the Novel

Blondy functions as a marker of status in the novel's gangster hierarchy. Her transfer from Little Arnie to Rico signals Rico's ascension — in the world Burnett depicts, a woman of Blondy's stature is both a prize and a symbol of power. She is present at every major gang function, seated at Rico's left at the banquet table, her position reflecting his rank. ([026-044], [026-093])

## Character Profile

- **Full Name**: Blondy Belle
- **Aliases**: Blondy
- **Affiliation**: Rico Bandello (formerly Little Arnie)
- **Role**: Gang consort, social fixture of Little Italy's underworld elite
- **First Appearance**: Rico's gang banquet, Part IV ([026-044])

## Biography

Blondy Belle enters the narrative at Rico's gang banquet, where she arrives on his arm — "talking to Blondy Belle, the swellest woman in Little Italy." Burnett describes her as handsome, bold, and aquiline, a striking physical presence that commands attention in any room. Her complexion is noted as olive, marking her Italian heritage. ([026-044])

She had previously been Little Arnie's woman "for a long time." Her transfer of loyalty to Rico is both personal and political — it signals that Rico has surpassed Arnie in the gang hierarchy. When the Big Boy asks "How did Little Arnie take it?", Blondy answers simply: "He took it standing up." The exchange is cool and transactional, underlining the world's harsh pragmatism. ([026-053], [026-055])

At the banquet, Blondy sits at Rico's left — the position of honor — while the Big Boy sits at his right. The seating arrangement is a precise map of power, and Blondy's place in it is unambiguous. Throughout Rico's reign, she remains in his inner circle. When his empire collapses and Otero helps him escape through a window, Blondy screams — a rare moment of raw emotion in a character defined by composure. ([026-093], [043-058])

## Key Scenes

Blondy's entrance at the gang banquet establishes her status:

> Rico came in slowly, talking to Blondy Belle, the swellest woman in Little Italy. She was a handsome Italian, bold and aquiline. ([026-044])

When asked about her former man's reaction to losing her:

> "He took it standing up," said Blondy Belle. ([026-055])

## Significance

Blondy Belle is the novel's most visible female presence in the gangster hierarchy — not a victim or a bystander but a participant who understands and leverages the world's rules. Her movement between powerful men mirrors the gangsters' own territorial shifts, making her a living barometer of underworld status. In Burnett's unsentimental universe, Blondy's pragmatism is not a moral failing but a survival strategy — and one she executes with more dignity than many of the men around her.
