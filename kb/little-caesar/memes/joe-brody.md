# Joe Brody

Joe Brody. A minor figure in the extended criminal landscape of *Little Caesar* — one of many names that populate Burnett's Chicago, establishing the breadth and density of the underworld beyond the novel's central characters.

## Role in the Novel

Brody is a background presence, a name that appears without elaboration, part of the social fabric of the gangster world that Burnett renders with documentary precision. His specific actions are less important than his existence: the novel's Chicago is not inhabited by a dozen major gangsters but by hundreds of minor ones, each with a name, a territory, and a role in the criminal economy.

## Significance

Names like Joe Brody represent the novel's commitment to world-building through accumulation rather than exposition. Burnett does not explain the gangster world — he populates it, and the sheer density of named figures creates the sense of a fully realized criminal ecosystem.
