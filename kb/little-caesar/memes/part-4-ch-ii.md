### II

(026-001)The gang gave a banquet for [[rico-bandello|Rico]] in one of [[sam-vettori|Sam Vettori]]’s big back rooms. The table was fifteen feet long and was covered by a fine white cloth. Red, green and white streamers hung from the chandeliers and Italian and American flags were crossed at intervals along the walls. At eleven o’clock the notables began to arrive. [[killer-pepi|Killer Pepi]] in a blue suit and a brown derby, with his woman, Blue Jay, on his arm. [[joe-sansone|Joe Sansone]], gunman and ex-lightweight, in a tuxedo, followed by his shadow Kid Bean, a Sicilian, dark as a negro. Then [[ottavio-vettori|Ottavio Vettori]], Sam’s cousin, not yet twenty-one, already famous as a gunman and spoken of as a potential gang chief. Then [[otero|Otero]], [[blackie-avezzano|Blackie Avezzano]] and Bat Carillo, all with their women. They stood about stiffly, a little uncomfortable in their fine clothes, and tried to make conversation. The men, like all specialists, talked shop. Ottavio Vettori declared that the police were a bunch of bums. Killer Pepi agreed that they were. [[joe-massara|Joe]] Sansone said that the Federal men were just as bad, only smarter and crookeder. Killer Pepi agreed that they were. Ottavio Vettori didn’t agree. He said that the Federal men were dumber and harder to fix. This brought on an argument.

(026-002)When Sam Vettori came in the men were all shouting.

(026-003)“What the hell!” said Sam, “ain’t this a fine way to act at a banquet? You act like a bunch of gas house micks. Cut the chatter.”

(026-004)Ottavio made a noise like a goat.

(026-005)“Baa! Baa!”

(026-006)Everybody laughed. Otero took out a quart bottle of whiskey, drank from it and passed it to [[seal-skin|Seal Skin]]; she drank and passed it to Ottavio. The bottle circled the room and returned empty.

(026-007)“You sure came prepared, you birds,” said Sam. “Did any of you guys bring a lunch?”

(026-008)“Baa! Baa!” bellowed Ottavio.

(026-009)“My God, ain’t that cute!” said Killer Pepi’s girl.

(026-010)“Hell, that ain’t nothing,” said Pepi, “listen.” Pepi put three fingers in his mouth and blew a blast that made their eardrums ring.

(026-011)“Lord,” said Ottavio, “the cops! Baa! Baa!”

(026-012)Three waiters came in, each carrying two quarts of whiskey. They put the bottles on the table and went out.

(026-013)“That’s an appetizer,” said Sam.

(026-014)“Apéritif,” Joe Sansone corrected.

(026-015)Ottavio slapped him on the back.

(026-016)“What’s that, little Joe? What the hell lingo is that?”

(026-017)Joe pushed him away.

(026-018)“You dumb birds don’t know nothing. Swell people don’t say ‘appetizer’; they say ’apéritif.’ ”

(026-019)“The hell they do! Well, I expect you know all about it. You used to be a bellboy at the Blackstone.”

(026-020)Everybody laughed. Killer Pepi blew a blast on his fingers. His girl looked at him admiringly.

(026-021)“How the hell you ever learn to do that?”

(026-022)“Aw, that ain’t nothing.”

(026-023)“Say, Sam,” said Carillo, “when do we eat?”

(026-024)“When the boss gets here,” said Pepi.

(026-025)“Well, he better step on it because I’m so hungry I could eat dynamite,” said Ottavio.

(026-026)“Keep your shirt on,” said Pepi.

(026-027)“Haven’t got an old soup sandwich in your pocket, have you?” asked Ottavio.

(026-028)Everybody laughed. Ottavio was the recognized wit of the Vettori gang. All that he had to do to get a laugh was to open his mouth.

(026-029)Sam Vettori took one of the quarts from the table and sent it round the room. It came back empty.

(026-030)“What the hell you suppose is keeping Rico?” asked Carillo. “Keep your shirt on,” said Pepi.

(026-031)“I go see,” said Otero.

(026-032)As he went out, the [[big-boy|Big Boy]] came in. He had on a big racoon coat and his derby was on the side of his head. Sam Vettori rushed over and shook hands with him.

(026-033)“What the hell you doing here?” he demanded.

(026-034)“Me. I came to see the fun. Things are looking up, Sam. Things sure to God are looking up. I think we got ’em whipped.”

(026-035)Sam Vettori smiled broadly and poured the Big Boy a drink. Well, well! If the [[courtney|Courtney]] business blew over he was sitting pretty. All things considered, he hadn’t done so bad. Time after time he had seen old gang leaders go down before younger men. But here he was hanging on, getting a 50–50 split, and taking no chances. Rico was the goods. Goddamn him and all his kind, but he was the goods.

(026-036)“Yeah,” said the Big Boy, “you got the Old Man on the run and [[flaherty|Flaherty]]’s about ready to do the Dutch Act. It’s gonna blow over, Sam. You heard me speak. It’s gonna blow over. I want to see Rico.”

(026-037)“He ain’t showed yet,” said Sam.

(026-038)“Damn smart boy,” said the Big Boy.

(026-039)Sam smiled.

(026-040)“Yeah,” he said, pouring the Big Boy another drink, “damn smart kid. He’s young yet, but I can show him the ropes.”

(026-041)The Big Boy didn’t say anything. He just looked at Vettori. Otero came running in, followed by two waiters, one of whom was carrying a big ulster and a derby; the other was carrying a woman’s fur coat.

(026-042)“Here he comes,” cried Otero.

(026-043)Kid Bean, who had collected a crowd in the middle of the room, and was walking on his hands to amuse them (he had once been an acrobat), jumped hastily to his feet and backed up against the wall. The crowd followed him. Killer Pepi said:

(026-044)“All right now. Everybody yell like hell when he comes in.” Rico came in slowly, talking to [[blondy-belle|Blondy Belle]], the swellest woman in [[little-italy|Little Italy]]. She was a handsome Italian, bold and aquiline. Her complexion and eyes were dark, but her hair, naturally black, was blondined, and this gave her an incongruous and a somewhat formidable appearance.

(026-045)Rico was greeted by an uproar, pierced by Killer Pepi’s shrill whistle. The Big Boy went to meet Rico and shook hands with him. Sam Vettori smiled and nodded, very affably, then went out to get things started. The Big Boy said to Blondy Belle:

(026-046)“Got yourself a regular man, did you?”

(026-047)Blondy took hold of Rico’s arm.

(026-048)“Surest thing you know.”

(026-049)The Big Boy laughed.

(026-050)“What’d you do with Little Arnie?”

(026-051)Rico took out a cigar and bit off the end.

(026-052)“She ditched him,” he said.

(026-053)The Big Boy meditated. Blondy Belle had been Little Arnie’s woman for a long time. Little Arnie ran the biggest gambling joint on the [[north-side|North Side]], but he had been slipping for a year or more. He wasn’t right; nobody could trust him.

(026-054)“How did Little Arnie take it?” asked the Big Boy.

(026-055)“He took it standing up,” said Blondy Belle.

(026-056)“Well, what could he do?” said Rico.

(026-057)Killer Pepi, Ottavio Vettori and Joe Sansone, as the most important men in the gang next to Sam Vettori, came over to shake hands with Rico.

(026-058)“A million dollars ain’t in it with you,” said Pepi, looking his boss over.

(026-059)Rico was wearing a loud striped suit and a purple tie. He still had on his gloves, yellow kid, of which he was very proud, and his diamond horseshoe pin had been replaced by a big ruby surrounded by little diamonds. Ottavio envied him his gloves. But Joe Sansone was not impressed; he knew better.

(026-060)“Yes sir, boss, you sure are lit up,” said Ottavio.

(026-061)“Here’s the half-pint,” said Killer Pepi, pushing Joe Sansone forward.

(026-062)Joe shook hands with Rico.

(026-063)“Yes sir,” said Ottavio, “the half-pint’s a good boy, but he and Gentleman Joe’re too swell for us.”

(026-064)Rico looked around the room.

(026-065)“Joe Massara here?”

(026-066)“Ain’t seen him,” said Pepi.

(026-067)“He won’t be here,” said Joe Sansone; “he’s busy.”

(026-068)Rico didn’t say anything. Blondy took hold of his arm.

(026-069)“I want a drink.”

(026-070)Rico looked at Pepi.

(026-071)“Get her a drink,” he said.

(026-072)The Big Boy took Rico aside and said:

(026-073)“I want to see you a minute, Rico.”

(026-074)Rico said:

(026-075)“Listen, if you see Joe Massara tomorrow you tell him to look me up. I got something to say to that bird.”

(026-076)“I’ll be seeing him maybe,” said the Big Boy. “I got a date with his boss tomorrow morning. There’s a square guy, Rico. [[devoss|DeVoss]] is a square guy all right. Never have to nudge him for dough.” Rico seemed in a bad humour.

(026-077)“They tell me you lined up something good,” said the Big Boy. Rico nodded.

(026-078)“Yeah, it’s gonna be a money maker. Little Arnie wised me up. I’m gonna give him a split. That’s the game now. Sam never had sense enough to get in on it.”

(026-079)“Little Arnie, eh? That guy’d double-cross his grandmother.”

(026-080)“He’ll only double-cross me once,” said Rico.

(026-081)“I believe you,” said the Big Boy; then, putting his hand on Rico’s shoulder, he went on: “Funny for you to split with Arnie. How about Blondy?”

(026-082)“Arnie don’t give a damn. He’s all shot to pieces. He can’t do a woman no good.”

(026-083)“No wonder,” said the Big Boy, “with a woman like that.” Rico grinned.

(026-084)“Ain’t she a bearcat!” he said; then his face clouded. “Wonder what the hell Joe Massara’s game is?”

(026-085)The Big Boy looked at Rico for a moment.

(026-086)“That little hunky dancer over at DeVoss’s has got him down. They tell me he’s going straight.”

(026-087)Rico laughed unpleasantly.

(026-088)“Yeah? Well, I’ll have to go over and give that bird an earful.”

(026-089)“Better stay out of that end of town, Rico.”

(026-090)“To hell with that.”

(026-091)Sam Vettori came in, followed by three waiters bringing the soup.

(026-092)“All right,” said Sam, “we’re all set.”

(026-093)Rico took his place at the head of the table. The Big Boy sat on his right and Blondy Belle on his left. The gunmen and their women arranged themselves according to rank. Blackie Avezzano sat at the foot of the table.
