## Role in the Novel

Ottavio Vettori is Sam Vettori's cousin, "not yet twenty-one, already
famous as a gunman and spoken of as the coming head of Chicago's underworld"
[026-057]. He is one of the three top lieutenants in the Vettori gang,
alongside Killer Pepi and Joe Sansone.

His most dramatic moment comes when he shoots a rival, making headlines:
"This was the work of Ottavio Vettori. The underworld was convulsed and
thousands of extra copies of the paper were sold. Rico and Ottavio Vettori
had become famous overnight" [035-003].

## Character Profile

Ottavio is "daring enough and cool in a tight place, he could shoot
straight and he feared nothing, but he was light-minded, dissipated"
[032-004]. He is brave but lacks the cunning and self-discipline that
make Rico a leader rather than just another gunman.

He has a sense of humor — during a photograph session, he leaps into
the air crying "My God, I'm shot" and falls across the table, making
everyone laugh [027-028]. He envies Rico's flashy yellow kid gloves
[026-059].

## Key Relationships

- **Sam Vettori**: His cousin and boss.
- **Rico**: Serves as one of Rico's armed escorts and enforcers; his
  shooting of a rival brings both men fame [035-003].
- **Bat Carillo**: Fellow gang member, often seen alongside him
  [033-080].
