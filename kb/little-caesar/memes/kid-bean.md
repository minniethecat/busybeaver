# Kid Bean

Kid Bean. A minor but colorful member of [[vettori-gang|Sam Vettori's gang]], known for his hand-walking party trick at the banquet for Rico, and one of the casualties of Rico's ruthless consolidation of power.

## Role in the Novel

Kid Bean's most vivid moment comes at the [[banquet-for-rico|Banquet for Rico]], where he "collected a crowd in the middle of the room and was walking on his hands to amuse them" — a brief, almost comic image of gangster conviviality before the evening's darker turn. He also quarrels with [[killer-pepi|Killer Pepi]] during the toasts, a flare-up of the internal tensions that plague Vettori's crew. His eventual fate — killed as Rico consolidates control — is handled offstage, one more name on the list of those who didn't survive the transition. [026-025]

## Biography

Kid Bean is a gang regular, present at the banquet and treated as one of the crew's "notables." His hand-walking performance at the banquet is the kind of rough, physical humor that defines the gang's social life — a world of crude jokes, drinking contests, and displays of masculine bravado. But beneath the comedy is tension: his quarrel with Killer Pepi during the toasts reveals the simmering rivalries that Rico will later exploit. [026-025] [027-005]

**The hand-walking** — Kid Bean performs for the gathered gang members, walking on his hands in the middle of the room, a moment of coarse gangland festivity. [026-025]

**The quarrel** — During the toasts after Rico's speech, Kid Bean and Killer Pepi exchange words, the brief flash of hostility foreshadowing the violence to come. [027-005]

## Significance

Kid Bean is a representative figure: the rank-and-file gangster who doesn't make decisions but serves as the material on which leaders' ambitions are built and broken. His death is one of the novel's many offstage casualties, the human cost of the gangster's rise that Burnett registers without sentimentality.
