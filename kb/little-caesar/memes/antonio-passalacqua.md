# Antonio Passalacqua

Antonio Passalacqua. A name from the extended background of Chicago's criminal justice apparatus in *Little Caesar* — one of the many figures whose existence is registered in passing, establishing the density of the underworld beyond the novel's central cast.

## Role in the Novel

Antonio Passalacqua appears as a name — possibly in a police document, a newspaper item, or an investigator's report — one thread in the legal and criminal web that surrounds the novel's main action. His function is atmospheric: Burnett's Chicago is not populated solely by major gangsters but by a dense network of minor figures whose names fill police blotters and court dockets.

## Significance

Like many of the novel's named-but-never-seen figures, Passalacqua represents the breadth of the gangster ecosystem — the hundreds of minor criminals whose arrests, trials, and sentences create the background noise against which the major dramas play out. His name, with its distinctively Italian cadence, also contributes to the novel's careful mapping of ethnic identity in Chicago's underworld.
