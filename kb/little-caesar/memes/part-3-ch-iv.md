### IV

(020-001)Vettori stared at [[rico-bandello|Rico]], who said nothing.

(020-002)“Crazy! Crazy!” said [[blackie-avezzano|Blackie]]. “I tell him, be a man, be a man. But he say, I got to, I got to.”

(020-003)Rico hastily put on his overcoat.

(020-004)“Well, I guess that’s it,” said [[sam-vettori|Sam Vettori]].

(020-005)“Yeah,” said Rico, “that’s it. Now get yourself a can, Sam, and let’s go. We ain’t got any time to waste.”

(020-006)Vettori rubbed both hands over his face.

(020-007)“Not me,” he said.

(020-008)Rico looked at him.

(020-009)“Take Blackie,” said Vettori.

(020-010)Blackie implored them with his eyes.

(020-011)“Blackie’s no good,” said Rico.

(020-012)“No,” said Blackie, “I no good.”

(020-013)Carillo put his head in the door.

(020-014)“Reilley’s downstairs, boss.”

(020-015)“Take Carillo,” said Vettori.

(020-016)Carillo stared at them suspiciously. Rico leapt across the room and grabbed him by the arm.

(020-017)“Listen, Bat, can you drive a can?”

(020-018)“Sure.”

(020-019)“Will you let her out when I office you?”

(020-020)“Sure.”

(020-021)“All right, let’s go.”

(020-022)“Take that black roadster, Carillo,” said Vettori, “but for God’s sake don’t smash it up.”

(020-023)Carillo ran out leaving the door open. Rico walked over and closed the door, then he said:

(020-024)“Sam, you ain’t got any more guts than Tony. Now listen, get down there and talk turkey to Reilley. Get that! By God, I guess I got to boss this job myself.”

(020-025)Vettori looked at Rico with hatred. But he said:

(020-026)“All right, Rico, you’re the boss now.”

(020-027)Rico went out. Blackie said:

(020-028)“Goodbye Tony!”

(020-029)Carillo was waiting with the black roadster in the alleyway. Rico jumped in and the roadster leapt away. Carillo took a turn on two wheels.

(020-030)“It’s a cinch he went the shortest cut,” said Rico.

(020-031)“Sure,” said Carillo, “I know what I’m doing.”

(020-032)“All right,” said Rico, “do it.”

(020-033)The wind had risen and it began to snow, big, heavy flakes which sailed past the street lights. In a few minutes the ground was covered.

(020-034)Carillo took the shortest cut and Rico, holding his big automatic on the seat beside him, sat straining his eyes. But there was no sign of Tony.

(020-035)“If we miss him, I’ll kick hell out of Blackie,” said Rico.

(020-036)“Keep your shirt on, boss,” said Carillo.

(020-037)The tall spires of St. Dominick’s rose before them at the end of the block. The street was deserted. Carillo drove slowly now, hugging the curb. In a moment he pointed:

(020-038)“There’s a guy.”

(020-039)Rico leaned forward.

(020-040)“Take it easy, Bat,” he said, “I think it’s Tony.”

(020-041)“Tony,” called Rico.

(020-042)“Yeah?” came Tony’s voice. “Who is it?”

(020-043)Rico fired. A long spur of flame shot out in the darkness. Rico emptied his gun. Tony fell without a sound.

(020-044)“All right now, Bat,” said Rico, “let her out.”
