### III

(011-001)[[joe-massara|Joe]] handed [[rico-bandello|Rico]] a sheet of paper full of figures. Rico read: 9,331.75.

(011-002)“All right,” said Rico, “split it five ways and we’ll make up [[scabby|Scabby]]’s split between us.”

(011-003)[[otero|Otero]] sat with his chair tipped back against the wall, smoking a cigarette with his eyes closed. Vettori was playing solitaire and swearing softly to himself.

(011-004)Joe looked at his watch.

(011-005)“Quarter till. I got to beat it. Say, [[sam-vettori|Sam]], call Carillo and let him get me a cab, will you?”

(011-006)Sam heaved himself to his feet and called Carillo. In a moment the bouncer put his flattened face in the door.

(011-007)“Three dicks downstairs, boss.”

(011-008)“Who are they?” asked Vettori.

(011-009)“[[flaherty|Flaherty]] and two guys I don’t know, boss. They want to see you.”

(011-010)Vettori stood looking at the floor. Carillo jumped in and shut the door.

(011-011)“Christ,” he said, “they’re coming up.”

(011-012)Rico leapt to his feet, ran across the room and opened a panel in the wall.

(011-013)“Come on, Joe,” he said, “you can slip out the back way. Stay where you are, Otero, and go right on smoking. Send Joe’s cab around in the alley, Bat.”

(011-014)Vettori looked at Rico.

(011-015)“You suppose they know something, Rico?”

(011-016)“Not unless they picked Tony up. You don’t know nothing, Sam, see? I’ll be right here listening, and if there’s any trouble, why, it’ll be tough on the dicks.”

(011-017)Vettori scooped up the money, wrapped his coat around it, and handed it to Rico. Joe went through the panel, followed by Rico. There was a knock at the door.

(011-018)Vettori nodded and Carillo opened the door. Two plainclothes men stepped in and stood looking around the room. One was tall and burly in a huge ulster; the other was short and very young. They both had their right hands in their overcoat pockets.

(011-019)“All right, Carillo,” said Vettori, “go ahead. That’s all.”

(011-020)“Wait a minute,” said the burly one, “tell Flaherty we’ll be down in a couple of minutes, for him to wait.”

(011-021)“Sure, sure,” said Carillo.

(011-022)He went out closing the door softly.

(011-023)“Well,” said Vettori, “you want to see me?”

(011-024)“Yeah,” said the burly one, who did all the talking, “we want to see you, Vettori.”

(011-025)“Well, here I am!”

(011-026)Otero opened his eyes long enough to look at them, then closed them again and went on smoking.

(011-027)“Vettori,” said the detective, “we want some information.”

(011-028)“Well?”

(011-029)Vettori sat down at the table and began to shuffle the cards.

(011-030)“There’s a big Cadillac draped around a pole a couple of blocks down the street, and we just wondered if you knew anything about it.”

(011-031)Vettori began to lay out a game of solitaire.

(011-032)“How should I know anything about it? Ain’t it got no licence plates on it?”

(011-033)“Sure, but they’re phoney.”

(011-034)“Yeah?”

(011-035)“Yeah. It was stolen about eight o’clock tonight on the [[north-side|North Side]] and we got a pretty good description of the guy that stole it.”

(011-036)“Well,” said Vettori, “I got a good business. What the hell’d I be doing stealing automobiles.”

(011-037)He laughed and shook his head.

(011-038)“Oh, you got me wrong,” said the detective with elaborate innocence. “You see, it’s piled up right straight down the street from here and I thought maybe it was some of the guys from your joint, see? I mean some of the young guys that come here to dance.”

(011-039)“Well,” said Vettori, “how would I know?”

(011-040)The detective took out a cigar and began to chew on it.

(011-041)“Wasn’t there nobody in it?” asked Otero.

(011-042)“Yeah,” said the detective, “one guy. But he beat it.”

(011-043)“I don’t know nothing about it,” said Vettori.

(011-044)“Well, no harm in asking,” said the detective. “Come on, Mike, let’s get going. I guess Vettori don’t know nothing about it.”

(011-045)The two of them walked slowly to the door. The big one turned.

(011-046)“Say, Vettori,” he said, “did you hear the news?”

(011-047)Vettori looked up.

(011-048)“What news?”

(011-049)“Why, some bastard bumped Cap. [[courtney|Courtney]] off over at the [[casa-alvarado|Casa Alvarado]].”

(011-050)“Yeah?” said Vettori, “some guys are sure careless with the lead. That’s a tough break.”

(011-051)The young detective opened the door and they started out.

(011-052)“Ain’t it?” said the big one. “Well, so long.”

(011-053)As soon as the door closed, Vettori went over and shot the bolt, then peeped out through the shutter. Rico came out of his hiding place.

(011-054)“Well,” said Vettori, glancing at Rico, “things ain’t going so good.”

(011-055)Rico shrugged.

(011-056)“They don’t know nothing. Just feeling around. Listen, Sam, where’s your guts? We got to stick together on this.”

(011-057)“I know,” said Vettori, falling back into his chair, “but I never seen things break so tough.”

(011-058)Rico held out a roll of bills.

(011-059)“Here’s your split, Sam.”

(011-060)Vettori took the bills and stuffed them into his pocket. Rico handed Otero his. Otero got up and put on his overcoat.

(011-061)“I think I go see my woman,” he said.

(011-062)When he had gone, Rico went over and sat down beside Vettori.

(011-063)“Listen, Sam,” he said, “I been taking orders too long. We’re done. Get the idea? But we got to see this through. We get a break and we’ll come clean. Only we got to shoot straight. See what I mean? I got a rope around my neck right now and they can only hang you once. If anybody gets yellow and squeals, my gun’s gonna speak its piece.”

(011-064)“That’s OK with me,” said Sam.

(011-065)They sat silent. Downstairs the jazz band was playing and the saxophone was sending vibrations along the floor. Vettori laid out another game of solitaire.

(011-066)“Funny for Tony to crash,” he said.

(011-067)“He lost his nerve,” said Rico.

(011-068)“You suppose he’ll show?”

(011-069)“Not till tomorrow if he’s got any sense. I’ll leave his split with you.”
