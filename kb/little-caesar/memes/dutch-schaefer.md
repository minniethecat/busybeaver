# Dutch Schaefer

Dutch Schaefer. A minor figure referenced in the extended criminal landscape of *Little Caesar* — his ethnically marked nickname ("Dutch," i.e., Deutsch/German) distinguishing him in a novel dominated by Italian and Irish characters, a reminder that Chicago's underworld was not monolithic but a patchwork of ethnic criminal traditions.

## Role in the Novel

Dutch appears in the novel's background texture, part of the network of minor criminals whose names establish the diversity of Chicago's underworld. His Germanic nickname is significant: in a novel where almost every gangster is Italian or Irish, "Dutch" signals a different ethnic track through the criminal world — the German and Eastern European bootleggers and stickup men who operated alongside (and sometimes in competition with) the Italian-dominated crews.

## Significance

Dutch Schaefer's presence, however fleeting, is part of Burnett's documentary commitment to depicting Chicago's criminal world as it actually was: ethnically diverse, with Germans, Poles, Jews, Greeks, and others operating in parallel (and sometimes overlapping) with the Italian-dominated syndicates that would eventually consolidate power. The name is a historical footnote rendered in fiction.
