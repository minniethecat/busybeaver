## Role in the Novel

Seal Skin is the woman in Otero's life, appearing primarily in the early
chapters. She is comfortable and familiar with the gang's world, smoking
Otero's cigars and sharing his apartment while snow falls outside
[004-011].

She questions Rico's dominance over Otero, asking him bluntly: "How come
you hang after Rico?" [004-029]. Her realism contrasts with Otero's
romanticized devotion to the gang leader.

## Character Profile

Seal Skin is practical and down-to-earth. When Otero is drunk and
belligerent, she stays up all night watching him, "dead for sleep, but
she didn't shut her eyes for fear Otero would do some crazy thing like
shooting out the window at the street light" [014-002].

She has a sharp sense of humor — when Otero dances drunkenly, stamping
his heels, "Seal Skin nearly fell out of her chair laughing" [014-005].
Despite her affection for Otero, she does not worship him and is not
afraid to push him away or criticize his choices [004-026].

## Key Relationships

- **Otero (The Greek)**: Her partner. She tries to sober him up and keep
  him out of trouble, but is often frustrated by his idolization of Rico
  [014-001].
- **Rico**: She is skeptical of Rico's hold over Otero and questions his
  judgment, showing a rare willingness to criticize the gang's leader
  [004-029].
