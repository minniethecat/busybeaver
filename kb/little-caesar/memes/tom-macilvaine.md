# Tom MacIlvaine

Tom MacIlvaine. A minor figure in the extended social world of *Little Caesar* — one of the many names that populate the borderland between Chicago's criminal and legal systems.

## Role in the Novel

MacIlvaine appears as a background name, part of the institutional landscape — law enforcement, journalism, or the legal profession — that surrounds and constrains the novel's gangster protagonists. His Irish surname places him in the ethnic tradition of Chicago's police and political establishments, the institutional counterweight to the Italian-dominated criminal world.

## Significance

Names like MacIlvaine represent the novel's attention to the full ecosystem of urban crime — not just the gangsters but the police, lawyers, journalists, and politicians whose careers intersect with (and often depend upon) the criminal world. The Irish names scattered through the novel's institutional background are as carefully chosen as the Italian names in its foreground: they map the ethnic division of labor in 1920s Chicago's criminal-legal complex.
