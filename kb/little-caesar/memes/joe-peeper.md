## Role in the Novel

Joe Peeper is one of Little Arnie's men who becomes a key informant for
Rico. Blondy Belle identifies him as the weak link: "hand Arnie's boy,
Joe Peeper, some dough and he'll spill the news. Joe hates Arnie"
[028-048].

Rico cultivates Peeper as an inside source, and when Rico confronts
Little Arnie about cheating him on the gambling revenue, Peeper
delivers the damning testimony: "The books're crooked, Rico; he's been
gypping you out of half your split every week" [034-067].

## Character Profile

Peeper is motivated by resentment — he turns on Arnie because, as he
says, "you thought you could bat me around and make me like it"
[034-087]. He is not a principled man but a pragmatic one, switching
allegiance when it benefits him.

After helping Rico expose Arnie, Peeper follows Rico's men out and
nervously seeks reassurance: "Honest to God, Rico, I didn't know
nothing about them Detroit bums. I didn't know what Arnie was up to.
Lord, you know I wouldn't double-cross you after all you done for me"
[034-096].

## Key Relationships

- **Little Arnie**: His boss, whom he betrays out of resentment for
  mistreatment [034-087].
- **Rico**: The man he sells information to, seeking protection and
  favor in return [034-096].
- **Blondy Belle**: Identifies Peeper as Rico's potential inside man
  [028-048].
