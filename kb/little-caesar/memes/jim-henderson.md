# Jim Henderson

Jim Henderson. A name from the extended background of *Little Caesar's* Chicago — one of the institutional figures whose existence helps establish the novel's carefully constructed urban realism.

## Role in the Novel

Henderson is part of the documentary texture of Burnett's narrative method — a name that appears without elaboration, contributing to the sense that the novel's events are taking place in a fully populated city whose institutions (police, courts, newspapers, city hall) operate continuously in the background. His Anglo-Scottish name places him in the ethnic mainstream of Chicago's professional classes, distinct from both the Italian gangsters and the Irish police.

## Significance

The accumulation of minor names in *Little Caesar* — Henderson, MacIlvaine, Brody, Schaefer — is Burnett's technique for creating fictional density without narrative sprawl. Each name implies a biography, a network of relationships, a role in the city's social machinery. The result is a novel that feels not like a story but like a document, a report from a world that exists independently of its telling.
