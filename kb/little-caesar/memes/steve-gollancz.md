# Steve Gollancz

Steve Gollancz. A Chicago gang leader and bank robber whose crew operates independently of the novel's main gang factions. He is notable for being wrongly arrested during the Courriere holdup investigation.

## Role in the Novel

Steve Gollancz appears primarily through references by other characters rather than direct action. He represents the broader criminal ecosystem of Chicago — the numerous independent operators whose paths intersect with the police investigation into Rico's gang ([022-022]).

## Character Profile

- **Full Name**: Steve Gollancz
- **Aliases**: —
- **Affiliation**: Independent gang (bank robbery crew)
- **Role**: Gang leader, bank robber
- **First Appearance**: Referenced in Chapter 1 ([022-022])

## Biography

Steve Gollancz first enters the narrative through a story of mistaken identity. After the Courriere holdup, the manager of Wood's jewelry store identified the robbers as Poles, leading police to arrest Gollancz and his crew. The irony was that Gollancz and his men "had just tapped a bank" — they were guilty of a different crime entirely. The narrator describes it as "funny as hell" ([022-022]).

Gollancz's lieutenant is Bugs Liska, who later appears in a holding room alongside Joe Massara after Joe's arrest ([104-001]).

Later in the novel, Chicago Red reveals a past connection to Gollancz. When drunk, Red boasts about his former association, and Gollancz had previously described Rico to Red as "a big fellow" — leading to Red's surprise at Rico's small physical stature ([198-002]).

## Key Scenes

**Mistaken arrest** ([022-022]): Gollancz and his crew are arrested for the Courriere holdup based on the jewelry store manager's misidentification. The police soon realize their error — Gollancz had been robbing a bank, not a jewelry store.

**Reputation in the underworld** ([198-002]): Chicago Red's drunken boasting about his Gollancz connection reveals Gollancz's standing in Chicago's criminal hierarchy and his awareness of Rico Bandello.

## Significance

Steve Gollancz serves as a narrative device illustrating the crowded, competitive nature of Chicago's criminal underworld. His mistaken arrest provides comic relief while also demonstrating police methodology. His name carries weight among other gangsters, as shown by Chicago Red's boasting, establishing him as a known quantity in the gangland ecosystem even though he never appears directly in the main action.
