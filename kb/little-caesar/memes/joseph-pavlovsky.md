# Joseph Pavlovsky

Joseph Pavlovsky. Little Arnie's doorman who drove the getaway car in the attempted killing of Rico Bandello, then fled to Hammond, Indiana, where he opened a speakeasy on Arnie's money.

## Role in the Novel

Joseph Pavlovsky is one of Little Arnie's henchmen who participates in the botched attempt on Rico's life and later resurfaces when Rico encounters him by chance. His post-exile success as a beer-racket operator in Hammond illustrates how gangland violence displaced minor figures into new territories, spreading the Chicago underworld's influence outward ([035-004], [049-019]–[049-026]).

## Character Profile

- **Full Name**: Joseph Pavlovsky
- **Aliases**: None recorded
- **Affiliation**: Little Arnie's gang
- **Role**: Doorman, getaway driver
- **First Appearance**: Mentioned in aftermath of the shooting attempt, Part IV ([035-004])

## Biography

Joseph Pavlovsky served as Little Arnie's doorman and was tasked with driving the car during the attempted killing of Rico Bandello. When the attempt failed and Arnie fled Chicago, several of his henchmen closely connected to the shooting followed him into exile. Pavlovsky went to Hammond, Indiana, where, using money Arnie had given him, he opened a speakeasy ([035-004]). He was accompanied by Pippy Coke, one of the Detroit gunmen who had done the shooting, and two croupiers who had been shadowing Rico ([035-004]).

Later, Rico encounters Pavlovsky by chance and recognizes him: "It was Little Arnie's doorman, Joseph Pavlovsky, one of the guys he had chased" ([049-019]). Pavlovsky greets Rico without hostility, telling him he has been in Hammond ever since Rico "gave us the rush" ([049-020]). He boasts that he has done well in the beer racket and cleaned up, planning to return to Chicago the following month ([049-022]). Pavlovsky offers Rico a compliment — "You sure pulled one on 'em, Rico; you always was a smart boy" — but Rico brushes him off and leaves ([049-025]–[049-026]).

## Key Scenes

**The Failed Shooting and Exile** ([035-004]): After Little Arnie's attempt on Rico's life fails, Pavlovsky drives the getaway car and then follows Arnie into exile, settling in Hammond where he uses Arnie's money to open a speakeasy.

**The Chance Encounter** ([049-019]–[049-026]): Rico unexpectedly runs into Pavlovsky and immediately recognizes him. Pavlovsky greets him without resentment, proudly recounting his success in the Hammond beer racket and his plan to return to Chicago. His friendly demeanor toward the man who destroyed his boss's gang illustrates the pragmatic, unsentimental loyalties of Burnett's underworld.

## Significance

Joseph Pavlovsky represents the diaspora of minor gangsters displaced by gang violence. His trajectory — from doorman and driver to independent speakeasy operator — mirrors in miniature the upward mobility that the novel's protagonist pursues on a grander scale. His casual friendliness toward Rico, the man who smashed his outfit, underscores the novel's vision of gangland as a world where personal loyalties are situational and survival is its own victory.
