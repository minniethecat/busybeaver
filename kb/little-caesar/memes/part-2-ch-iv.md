### IV

(012-001)[[rico-bandello|Rico]] went over to see [[ma-magdalena|Ma Magdalena]], the fence. Her fruit store was still open and her son Arrigo was sitting half-asleep beside a pile of oranges.

(012-002)“Hello,” he said.

(012-003)“Where’s Ma?” asked Rico.

(012-004)Arrigo pulled a cord which rang a bell in the rooms beyond the store. Ma, leaning on her stick, came out into the store. Seeing Rico, she said:

(012-005)“Oh, it’s you! Well, well! Come back. Come back.”

(012-006)“Can I come too, Ma?” said Arrigo.

(012-007)“You stay and mind the store, you lazy loafer,” said Ma, shaking her stick at him.

(012-008)Arrigo sat down once more by the pile of oranges.

(012-009)Rico followed Ma Magdalena back into her little office. She pulled up a chair for him and he sat down, then she got out a bottle.

(012-010)“You talk, I drink,” she said, sitting down beside him and pouring herself a drink.

(012-011)Rico took out his split, peeled off a few bills and handed her the rest.

(012-012)“Plant it,” he said.

(012-013)She took the roll, counted it, and put it down inside her dress.

(012-014)“Had a big New Year’s Eve, did you?”

(012-015)“Yeah,” said Rico, “plenty big. There’ll be lots of fun tomorrow.”

(012-016)“Well, well,” said Ma, “that’s the way it goes.”

(012-017)She poured herself another glass of wine, then she reached over and touched Rico with her stick.

(012-018)“Look, Rico, you ain’t got a nice little girl who wants a big diamond ring, have you?”

(012-019)“Me, buy a diamond ring for a skirt?”

(012-020)Ma Magdalena made a clucking noise and shook her head.

(012-021)“You are cold, Rico. Don’t like wine. Don’t like women. You are no good, Rico.”

(012-022)Rico smiled.

(012-023)“Me, I like women once in a while, but I ain’t putting out no diamond rings.”

(012-024)Leaving Ma Magdalena’s, Rico went in the direction of [[sicily-pete|Sicily Pete]]’s.

(012-025)At Sicily Pete’s the mechanical piano was playing. Three men, all Italians, and two girls, both Americans, were sitting at a front table. They were drunk. They played with their food, spilled their coffee, and banged on the plates with their knives. Pete stood behind the counter, scowling.

(012-026)When Rico came in he said:

(012-027)“Hello, my friend, where have you been keeping yourself?”

(012-028)“I haven’t been around lately. Got some noisy birds, ain’t you?” Pete shrugged his shoulders.

(012-029)“Yes, the fools. They drink gin. That is no drink for an Italian.” Rico took out his cigarettes and offered Pete one. They stood smoking. One of the girls pulled up her dress and fixed her garter. Rico smiled.

(012-030)“Get an eyeful of that, Pete.”

(012-031)“Yes, yes,” said Pete, “that’s all I get, an eyeful. Every night I stand here while other people have a good time.”

(012-032)The girl looked up at Rico and he winked at her. She said to one of the men:

(012-033)“Look at that smarty over there. He thinks he’s cute.”

(012-034)The man looked foggily at Rico. Pete put his hand on Rico’s arm.

(012-035)“My friend, don’t start no trouble, please. That’s all we have around here, trouble. With one thing and another, I think I go back to Italy.”

(012-036)Rico turned his back on the girl.

(012-037)“OK,” he said.

(012-038)While Pete was getting Rico a cup of coffee, a newsboy came in:

(012-039)“**Extra! Extra!** All about the big holdup.”

(012-040)Rico bought a paper and glanced at the three-inch headlines.

(012-041)> Thugs kill Captain [[courtney|Courtney]]

(012-042)> in [[casa-alvarado-robbery|Casa Alvarado holdup]]

(012-043)Rico showed Pete the paper.

(012-044)“Another killing,” he said.

(012-045)“Yes,” said Pete, “kill, kill, that’s all they do. I wish to God I was back in Sicily. The Mafia, what is that? That is a kindergarten.”

(012-046)One of the Italians bought a paper and started to read the account of the holdup aloud. All the people round the table stopped eating to listen. Rico sipped his coffee and watched them.
