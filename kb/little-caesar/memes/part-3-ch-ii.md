### II

(018-001)[[About]] nine o’clock Carillo put his head in the door. Downstairs the jazz band had just started to play.

(018-002)“Well?” demanded Vettori, getting to his feet.

(018-003)“[[blackie-avezzano|Blackie]] wants to see you,” said Carillo.

(018-004)“All right.”

(018-005)Carillo went out.

(018-006)“What you suppose he wants?” said Vettori.

(018-007)[[rico-bandello|Rico]], who was sitting with his chair tipped back against the wall reading a magazine, shook his head without looking up or answering. He was deep in the reading of a story about a rich society girl who fell in love with a bootlegger. Rico read everything he could find that had anything to do with society. He was fascinated by a stratum of existence which seemed so remote and unreal to him.

(018-008)Blackie Avezzano, who managed [[sam-vettori|Sam]]’s garage, came in and shut the door behind him. He was small and bowlegged, and he was so dark that he had been taken for a mulatto many times.

(018-009)Vettori impatiently exclaimed:

(018-010)“Well, what’s on your mind, Blackie?”

(018-011)Rico went on reading his magazine. Blackie sat down at the table and seemed to be making an effort to collect his thoughts.

(018-012)“All right, spit it out,” said Vettori.

(018-013)Blackie couldn’t speak very good English, but as Rico didn’t know a word of Italian and Vettori preferred to speak English, he did the best he could.

(018-014)“Tony, he took sick. Listen, I tell you, Tony he no know what. He took sick. I see him, listen, I tell you, what-you-say, he no got his guts. The Madre she send me call the doctore. Listen, he say, Tony, what-you-say, you been a drink. Now listen, you cut out a drink. That’s all. Tony he no drink. What a hell! One bottle of beer he can no drink. He no got his guts, that’s all.”

(018-015)Vettori looked at Rico, who went on reading.

(018-016)“Rico,” he said.

(018-017)“I heard him,” said Rico, “I ain’t deaf.”

(018-018)Blackie got up and stood twisting his cap in his hand. Vettori took out his billfold and handed Blackie a ten.

(018-019)“Blackie,” he said, “keep your eyes open, understand?”

(018-020)“All right,” said Blackie, “I watch, see, I know. Tony no good. All right, I watch.”

(018-021)When he had gone Rico said:

(018-022)“Well, that’s that.”

(018-023)“We can’t take no chances,” said Vettori.

(018-024)“I’ll give him till tomorrow,” said Rico; “he can’t go far wrong with Blackie watching him. After that if he don’t settle down there won’t be no more Tony.”
