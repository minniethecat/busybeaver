# Bill Hackett

Bill Hackett. A Chicago gangster mentioned in connection with [[chicago-red|Chicago Red]], known primarily for his dislike of [[rico-bandello|Rico Bandello]] — one of the many personal enemies Rico accumulates during his rise through the city's underworld hierarchy.

## Role in the Novel

Bill Hackett appears in the novel's background texture of gangster rivalries and resentments. His dislike of Rico is noted but not explained in detail — it is simply part of the fabric of personal friction that Burnett uses to establish the gangster world's social dynamics. Not every enmity needs a cause; in the novel's Darwinian underworld, rivalry is the default state. [SCN28 R26]

## Significance

Bill Hackett represents the ambient hostility of the gangster world — the network of grudges, slights, and competitions that define relationships among criminals. In Burnett's Chicago, every gangster has enemies, and the accumulation of personal hatreds is as much a measure of status as the accumulation of money. Hackett's significance is not in what he does but in the fact that he exists: Rico's power has created resistance, and resistance, in this world, eventually becomes violence.
