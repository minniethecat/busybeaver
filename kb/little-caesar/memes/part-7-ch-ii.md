### II

(047-001)[[rico-bandello|Rico]] went up the alley at the side of Sansotta’s place and knocked at the back door. It was a long time before somebody came and took a look at him through the shutter. A voice with a marked Italian accent said:

(047-002)“Who are you?”

(047-003)“Where’s Sansotta?” asked Rico.

(047-004)“What do you care?”

(047-005)“Listen, buddy,” said Rico, “don’t get all het up. I’m right. Go tell Sansotta that Cesare wants him.”

(047-006)In a few minutes the door opened and a hand motioned for Rico to come in. The hall was dark and Rico stumbled going up the stairs. The lookout took hold of his arm.

(047-007)“The boss’s up in his room. I’ll take you up. Where you from, buddy?”

(047-008)“Youngstown,” said Rico.

(047-009)“Where’s that?”

(047-010)“Over east.”

(047-011)The lookout led Rico down a long, dark hallway and to a door at the end of it. Lights showed over a transom. The lookout knocked three times and the door was opened. Rico went in.

(047-012)“Well,” said Sansotta, locking the door, “here you are.”

(047-013)“Yeah,” said Rico.

(047-014)Sansotta was a small, bowlegged Italian with a dark, scarred face. He had on a striped suit, brown and red, and a stiff collar the points of which were so high that his chin rested on them. There was a big diamond stud in his shirtfront.

(047-015)“You must’ve got a break,” said Sansotta.

(047-016)Rico explained how he had got away.

(047-017)“Pretty nifty,” said Sansotta; “I got to hand it to you on that, Cesare.”

(047-018)“Yeah,” said Rico, “it was a good idea.”

(047-019)Sansotta went over to a table, opened a drawer and took out a handbill which he gave to Rico. Rico smiled.

(047-020)“Raised the ante, did they? Last I heard it was five grand.”

(047-021)Rico read the handbill over and over and stared at the Bertillon pictures.

(047-022)“Them pictures don’t look like me,” he said.

(047-023)Sansotta pursed his lips and scrutinized them.

(047-024)“Not since you got the tickler off. No, and you look thinner in them pictures. How long ago was they taken?”

(047-025)“[[About]] seven years ago.”

(047-026)The handbill read:

(047-027)> Wanted for murder: Cesare Bandello, known as Rico, Age: 29. Height: 5 ft. 5 in. Weight: 125. Complexion: pale. Hair: black and wavy. Eyes: light, grey or blue. His face is thin and he walks with one foot slightly turned in. Does not take up with strangers. Solitary type, morose and dangerous. Reward: $5,000, offered by management of [[casa-alvarado|Casa Alvarado]]. $2,000, offered by City of Chicago, for capture dead or alive.

(047-028)“Well,” said Sansotta, “where you headed for?”

(047-029)“I’m gonna stick around here for a while,” said Rico.

(047-030)“Yeah?” said Sansotta; “pretty close to trouble, ain’t it?”

(047-031)“I don’t know,” said Rico, “they ain’t got any idea which way I went. I got a big stake and I don’t have to worry none.”

(047-032)“You sure went up fast over in the big burg,” said Sansotta, looking at Rico with a sort of awe.

(047-033)“Yeah,” said Rico, “and the hell of it was, I was just getting started. Everything was on the up and up when one of the gang turned softie. Ain’t that hell?”

(047-034)Rico had been very much elated over his escape from Chicago, so elated in fact that he had forgotten all about his troubles; but, now that the excitement of the escape had passed, the thought of how much he had lost struck him full force. He felt resentful.

(047-035)“Yeah,” said Sansotta, “that’s the way it goes. It’s a tough game. They picked up two of my men last night.”

(047-036)“That so?” said Rico, paying no attention.

(047-037)Sansotta got up.

(047-038)“Well, Cesare,” he said, “I got business or I’d stick around and chin with you. Want to stay here with me till things blow over?”

(047-039)“Yeah,” said Rico.
