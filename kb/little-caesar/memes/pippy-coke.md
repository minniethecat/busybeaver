# Pippy Coke

Pippy Coke. A Detroit gunman brought in by Little Arnie for the attempted killing of Rico Bandello, who afterward fled to Hammond, Indiana, alongside Joseph Pavlovsky.

## Role in the Novel

Pippy Coke is a peripheral figure in the novel's gang-war arc. He is one of two Detroit gunmen recruited by Little Arnie to carry out the shooting of Rico Bandello. When the attempt fails, he follows the outbound tide of Arnie's henchmen into exile in Hammond, a minor casualty of the territorial war that Rico wins decisively ([035-004]).

## Character Profile

- **Full Name**: Pippy Coke
- **Aliases**: None recorded
- **Affiliation**: Little Arnie's gang (hired gun)
- **Role**: Gunman
- **First Appearance**: Mentioned in the aftermath of the shooting, Part IV ([035-004])

## Biography

Pippy Coke was one of two Detroit gunmen brought to Chicago by Little Arnie to kill Rico Bandello. The attempt failed, Arnie fled the city, and Coke, along with Joseph Pavlovsky — the doorman who had driven the getaway car — went to Hammond, Indiana ([035-004]). They were followed by two croupiers who had been shadowing Rico. Coke's subsequent fate is not recorded in the novel; he vanishes into the Hammond exile community alongside Arnie's other displaced henchmen.

## Key Scenes

**The Exodus to Hammond** ([035-004]): After Little Arnie's attempted killing of Rico fails, Arnie's henchmen scatter. Pippy Coke, "who with the two Detroit gunmen had done the shooting," goes with Pavlovsky to Hammond — a single sentence that captures the dispersal of a defeated gang.

## Significance

Pippy Coke is barely more than a name in the novel, but his presence serves a structural purpose: he is evidence of the external muscle Arnie imported for his war with Rico, and his exile to Hammond shows how gang violence radiated outward from Chicago into the surrounding industrial towns. Like Squint Maschke and other minor figures swept aside by Rico's ascent, Coke illustrates the collateral damage of underworld power struggles.
