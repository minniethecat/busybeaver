## Overview

The [[duchess-of-crowborough|Duchess of Crowborough]]'s charity bazaar, scheduled for the 16th of the month, serves as an unexpected catalyst in the deteriorating relationship between [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]] and [[julie-le-breton|Julie Le Breton]]. What begins as a well-intentioned charitable event — [[duchess-of-crowborough|the Duchess]] eager to harness [[julie-le-breton|Julie]]'s talents for organization and entertainment — becomes a battleground for control. [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]] perceives [[duchess-of-crowborough|the Duchess]]'s request for [[julie-le-breton|Julie]]'s help as yet another encroachment on her authority, and her absolute refusal to allow [[julie-le-breton|Julie]] to participate marks a critical escalation in the quarrel that will ultimately drive [[julie-le-breton|Julie]] from [[bruton-street|Bruton Street]].

## Background

### The Duchess's Plans

The [[duchess-of-crowborough|Duchess of Crowborough]], young and energetic, has undertaken the organization of a large charity bazaar. She recognizes [[julie-le-breton|Julie Le Breton]]'s exceptional social gifts — her fluency in French, her organizational inventiveness, her ability to draw people together — and is determined to secure her assistance:

> "Just think, there is my big bazaar on the 16th. You don't know how clever Julie is at such things. I want to make her recite — her French is too beautiful! And then she has such inventiveness, such a head! Everything goes if she takes it in hand." [003-082]

The Duchess's enthusiasm is genuine — she sees [[julie-le-breton|Julie]] as indispensable to the bazaar's success. But her request sets in motion a chain of events she has not anticipated.

### Julie's Position

[[julie-le-breton|Julie]], caught between her loyalty to [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]] and [[duchess-of-crowborough|the Duchess]]'s friendship, navigates carefully. She understands that any commitment to the bazaar must be approved by [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]], whose jealousy has been mounting:

> The Duchess, a little sobered by the turn events had taken and the darkened prospects of her bazaar, protested in vain against this sudden departure. [003-100]

## The Conflict

### Lady Henry's Refusal

When [[duchess-of-crowborough|the Duchess]] approaches [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]] to request [[julie-le-breton|Julie]]'s help, the response is explosive. [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]] perceives the request as yet another attempt to appropriate her companion:

> "She came, of course, to beg and protest. She offered me her valuable services for all sorts of superfluous things that I didn't want — if only I would spare her Julie for this ridiculous bazaar. So then my back was put up again, and I told her a few home truths about the way in which she had made mischief and forced Julie into a totally false position." [006-030]

The word "ridiculous" encapsulates [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]]'s contempt — she dismisses [[duchess-of-crowborough|the Duchess]]'s charity as beneath notice, and the request for [[julie-le-breton|Julie]]'s involvement as impertinence. Her rage escalates to an absolute prohibition:

> "Well, I'll try again. I'll try to hold my tongue," she granted him, sullenly. "But, understand, she shan't go to that bazaar!" [006-065]

### Sir Wilfrid's Mediation

[[sir-wilfrid-bury|Sir Wilfrid Bury]] attempts to mediate, recognizing the bazaar as a pretext in a larger struggle. He urges [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]] to be strategic rather than confrontational:

> "Suppose, as a first step," he smiled at his companion, "you tell Lady Henry about the bazaar?" [004-082]

### A Tactical Concession

In a rare moment of tactical retreat, [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]] grants permission — but her language reveals it as a calculated move rather than genuine generosity:

> "Oh, it mayn't answer," she said, as their hands dropped. "But we may as well have one more trial. And, mademoiselle, I shall be delighted that you should assist the Duchess with her bazaar." [006-109]

[[julie-le-breton|Julie]] relays the news to [[duchess-of-crowborough|the Duchess]] with careful diplomacy:

> "You're quite mistaken," said Julie, smiling. "Lady Henry says I may help you with the bazaar." [007-025]

## Outcome

The bazaar episode reveals the fundamental shift in power relations. [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]]'s authority, once absolute, is now exercised through grudging concessions rather than confident command. The Duchess, by persisting in her request, has demonstrated that she will not be cowed — and that [[julie-le-breton|Julie]] has allies outside [[bruton-street|Bruton Street]].

The bazaar itself is never depicted in the novel. Its significance lies not in the event but in the conflict it generates — a proxy battle in the war for control over [[julie-le-breton|Julie]]'s life and loyalties.

## Significance

The bazaar controversy crystallizes the novel's exploration of dependence and control. [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]]'s prohibition — "she shan't go to that bazaar!" — is the language of ownership, not employment. The episode exposes the uncomfortable truth that [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]] regards [[julie-le-breton|Julie]] not as a paid companion but as a possession, and that any independent social activity threatens the power structure of [[bruton-street|Bruton Street]].
