# Marriott Dalrymple

## Role
was the man for whom [[lady-rose-delaney|Lady Rose Delaney]] abandoned her husband and her social position. A traveller, painter, and dreamer with 'fine, long-drawn features bronzed by the sun of the East' (002-009), he brought with him 'the reputation of having plotted and fought for most of the lost causes of our generation.' His 'free and generous nature, his independence of money and convention, his passion for the things of the mind' (002-009) made him irresistible to the unhappily married Lady Rose. After her death, he fades from the narrative — a figure more significant in his consequences than his presence.

## Biography
appears only in retrospect in [[ch02|Chapter 2]], where his character and his relationship with Lady Rose are established through [[sir-wilfrid-bury|Sir Wilfrid Bury]]'s recollections. The description of his courtship of Lady Rose — 'nothing in his view of life predisposed him to put what probably appeared to him a mere legality before the happiness of two people meant for each other' (002-009) — encapsulates the romantic idealism that is both his gift and his limitation.
