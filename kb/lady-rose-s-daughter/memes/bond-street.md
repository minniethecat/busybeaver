# Bond Street

## Setting

Bond Street is a renowned shopping thoroughfare in Mayfair, London, appearing in the novel as a setting for incidental movement and reflection. It is mentioned in [[ch03]] when [[jacob-delafield|Jacob Delafield]] "walked towards Bond Street, pondering the situation of the two women — the impotent jealousy and rancor with which Lady Henry was devoured, the domestic slavery contrasted with the social power of Mademoiselle Le Breton" (003-005). The street reappears briefly in [[ch11]] when [[jacob-delafield|Jacob Delafield]] and a companion "walked to Bond Street together" (011-078).

## Description

The novel does not describe Bond Street in detail, treating it as a known landmark rather than a developed setting. Its significance is functional: Bond Street is a destination for walking and thinking. For [[jacob-delafield|Jacob Delafield]], the walk towards Bond Street provides a physical context for his internal deliberation about [[julie-le-breton|Julie Le Breton]]'s position — caught between [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]]'s "impotent jealousy" and her own "social power" (003-005). The street belongs to the masculine, public sphere of London that contrasts with the enclosed feminine space of [[bruton-street|Bruton Street]].

## Significance

Bond Street functions as a background space of masculine freedom and public life. In a novel dominated by interior scenes — drawing-rooms, libraries, boudoirs — the walk towards Bond Street represents a brief release into the open air, where men like [[jacob-delafield|Jacob Delafield]] can think and move unobserved. The two mentions (chapters 3 and 11) frame the novel's central crisis: the first walk occurs early, as Jacob weighs the dynamics of [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]]'s household, while the later walk occurs as the narrative moves toward resolution.

## Associated Characters

**[[jacob-delafield|Jacob Delafield]]** is the character most closely associated with Bond Street, using it as a route for solitary walks and reflection about [[julie-le-breton|Julie Le Breton]].
