# Cureton Street

## Location

Cureton Street is a modest street in London, situated near [[heribert-street|Heribert Street]]. It lies in a quiet residential district, described as having a mews behind it that belonged to houses "long since pulled down" [011-003]. The street's character is unpretentious — a contrast to the grand squares and fashionable addresses that dominate the novel's London geography.

## Significance

Cureton Street appears in the novel primarily through a contested housing plan. The "little place at the back of Cureton Street where Cousin Mary Leicester lives" [010-042] becomes a point of disagreement between the [[duke-of-chudleigh|Duke of Chudleigh]] and other characters. The Duke's reaction — "'Cureton Street!' said [[duke-of-chudleigh|the Duke]], almost at the top of his voice" [010-044] — reveals his snobbery about unfashionable addresses. The street thus serves as a measure of social aspiration and prejudice.

## Description

Cureton Street is part of a network of modest London streets in the vicinity of [[heribert-street|Heribert Street]]. A former mews behind the street has been redeveloped, with the old stable buildings pulled down [011-003]. The area retains a slightly old-fashioned, quiet character, attracting residents who value its seclusion and relative affordability. The "Cureton Street plan" that [[duke-of-chudleigh|the Duke]] objects to involves housing arrangements for a character connected to the [[lady-mary-leicester|Lady Mary Leicester]] household.

## Associated Characters

- **[[lady-mary-leicester|Lady Mary Leicester]]** — Her cousin lives in Cureton Street, making the street part of the extended Leicester-Delafield family geography.
- **[[duke-of-chudleigh|Duke of Chudleigh]]** — Vehemently opposes the Cureton Street housing plan, his outrage revealing his class prejudices [010-044][010-048].
- **[[jacob-delafield|Jacob Delafield]]** — Walks through Cureton Street on his way to [[heribert-street|Heribert Street]], connecting the two locations in the novel's mental map of London [016-057].
- **[[julie-le-breton|Julie Le Breton]]** — A character walks "in a blind haste to Cureton Street" [014-093] in a moment of emotional urgency, suggesting the street holds personal significance.
