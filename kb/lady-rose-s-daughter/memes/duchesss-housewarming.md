## Overview

[[julie-le-breton|Julie Le Breton]]'s housewarming party — her first attempt at independent entertaining after establishing herself in new lodgings — becomes a humbling demonstration of her altered social position. Of the sixty people invited, many do not respond at all, and of those who do, half decline. The party that does take place is small, strained, and a world away from the glittering Wednesday receptions [[julie-le-breton|Julie]] once presided over at [[bruton-street|Bruton Street]]. Yet it also represents [[julie-le-breton|Julie]]'s determination to build a life on her own terms, however reduced the circumstances.

## Background

### New Lodgings

After her dismissal from [[bruton-street|Bruton Street]] and the period of exile that followed, [[julie-le-breton|Julie]] establishes herself in modest accommodations in [[heribert-street|Heribert Street]]. The move represents her first step toward independence — a life no longer propped up by [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]]'s patronage or [[duchess-of-crowborough|the Duchess]]'s hospitality.

The Duchess, ever [[julie-le-breton|Julie]]'s champion, urges her to hold a housewarming:

> "Evelyn has made me send out cards for a little housewarming. She said I must." [016-092]

### The Social Climate

The timing is inauspicious. [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]]'s influence remains formidable, and the rumors about [[julie-le-breton|Julie]]'s conduct — her relationship with [[captain-warkworth|Captain Warkworth]], her supposed manipulation of [[bruton-street|Lady Henry's house]]hold — have spread through London society. The [[chatton-house|Chatton House]] party has already demonstrated that former acquaintances are willing to cut [[julie-le-breton|Julie]] publicly.

[[julie-le-breton|Julie]] herself is apprehensive:

> "She made me go to that smart party at Chatton House the other night. It was a great mistake. People turned their backs on me. And this, too, will be a mistake — and a failure." [016-092]

## The Party

### The Guest List

[[julie-le-breton|Julie]], following [[duchess-of-crowborough|the Duchess]]'s advice, sends invitations to approximately sixty people — "whom I knew well" [017-158]. The responses are devastating:

> "I have been here a month — the critical month. Now was the moment to stand by me, or throw me over — n'est-ce pas? This is my first party, my housewarming. I gave a fortnight's notice; I asked about sixty people, whom I knew well. Some did not answer at all. Of the rest, half declined — rather curtly, in many instances. And of those who accepted, not all came." [017-158]

### The Evening

The party that materializes is a subdued affair. The guests who do attend are [[julie-le-breton|Julie]]'s loyal few — [[duchess-of-crowborough|the Duchess]], [[jacob-delafield|Jacob Delafield]], [[dr-meredith|Dr. Meredith]], and a handful of others who have refused to abandon her. The gathering has a defiant quality, a small circle closing ranks against the larger world's hostility:

> The little gathering had taken its note — artificially gay. [017-161]

### Jacob's Presence

[[jacob-delafield|Jacob Delafield]]'s attendance carries particular significance. By appearing at [[heribert-street|Julie's house]]warming, he signals publicly that his regard for her has not been altered by the scandal. The gesture is not lost on the other guests or, eventually, on [[julie-le-breton|Julie]] herself.

## Aftermath

The housewarming, despite its disappointments, serves its purpose. It draws a line under the period of social uncertainty and establishes [[julie-le-breton|Julie]]'s new life in visible form. The guests who attended become the nucleus of a smaller but more genuine social circle — friends rather than acquaintances, loyalty tested and proven.

## Significance

The housewarming is the novel's most concentrated depiction of social arithmetic — the cold calculation by which London society determines who is "in" and who is "out." The sixty invitations and the tally of responses — silence, curt refusal, reluctant acceptance — are a ledger of social capital. [[julie-le-breton|Julie]] reads the numbers and understands her position with painful clarity.

Yet the episode is not merely about loss. The housewarming also marks the beginning of [[julie-le-breton|Julie]]'s reconstruction — the deliberate building of a life that does not depend on [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]]'s drawing-room. The smallness of the gathering is also its honesty: those who come are there for [[julie-le-breton|Julie]] herself, not for the social advantage of being seen at [[bruton-street|Bruton Street]].
