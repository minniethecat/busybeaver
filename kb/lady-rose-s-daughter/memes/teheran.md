# Teheran

## Location

Teheran (modern-day Tehran) is the capital of Persia (modern-day Iran). In the Edwardian period, it was a significant post for British diplomats engaged in the "Great Game" of Anglo-Russian competition for influence in Central Asia. The city represents the distant, masculine world of empire and diplomacy that stands in contrast to the drawing-rooms of London.

## Significance

Teheran functions in the novel as a destination for characters whose lives are shaped by imperial service. [[sir-wilfrid-bury|Sir Wilfrid Bury]]'s diplomatic career has been centered on Persia [001-024], and his experience there gives him a worldliness that sets him apart from the purely domestic characters. [[captain-warkworth|Captain Warkworth]]'s posting to Teheran [005-019] is a major plot point — his departure for Persia removes him from [[julie-le-breton|Julie]]'s orbit and creates the conditions for the novel's romantic resolution.

## Description

Teheran is never directly depicted in the novel — it exists as a name on maps, a destination in dispatches, and a topic of conversation among those with imperial connections. The British diplomatic presence there is part of the wider network of embassies, legations, and consulates that sustained Britain's global influence. For characters like Sir Wilfrid, Teheran represents a career of service and adventure; for [[captain-warkworth|Captain Warkworth]], it represents duty and departure.

## Associated Characters

- **[[sir-wilfrid-bury|Sir Wilfrid Bury]]** — A senior diplomat with extensive experience in Persia. His postings to Teheran have shaped his career and given him a perspective wider than that of London society.
- **[[captain-warkworth|Captain Warkworth]]** — Posted to Teheran, a development that removes him from the London social scene and affects his relationship with [[julie-le-breton|Julie Le Breton]].
