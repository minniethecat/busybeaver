# Hatton

Hatton is [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]]'s butler at her [[bruton-street|Bruton Street]] establishment. He appears in several chapters — particularly [[ch02|Chapter II]], when [[julie-le-breton|Julie Le Breton]] hosts an impromptu gathering during [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]]'s illness — as a figure of quiet competence and professional discretion.

## Role in the Household

As butler, Hatton manages the front-of-house operations: receiving guests, managing the footmen, overseeing the Wednesday evening receptions, and — crucially — mediating between [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]]'s imperious demands and the practical realities of the household. When [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]] is ill and her Wednesday reception must be cancelled, it is Hatton who conveys the news to arriving guests with the appropriate blend of regret and discretion.

## The Impromptu Gathering

Hatton's most significant appearance comes in [[ch02|Chapter II]], when [[julie-le-breton|Julie Le Breton]] — seizing the opportunity of [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]]'s illness — asks him to admit selected guests to the library rather than turning everyone away. The scene reveals Hatton's delicate position: he must balance loyalty to his mistress with accommodation of [[julie-le-breton|Julie]]'s semi-authorized request, all while maintaining the appearance that nothing unusual is happening.

His exchange with [[julie-le-breton|Julie]] is a masterclass in servant diplomacy:

> "If the Duchess of Crowborough comes tonight, I should very much like to see her… Do you think it could possibly disturb Lady Henry if you were to show her into the library for twenty minutes?"

Hatton considers, then agrees — with the careful qualification that "I should, of course, warn her grace that her ladyship was ill." He further arranges to close certain doors, have refreshments ready "but I don't suppose anybody will ask for it," and generally manage the quiet subversion of his mistress's orders with professional thoroughness.

## Significance

Hatton represents the servant class that enables the social world of the novel to function — invisible, competent, and possessed of a shrewd understanding of the human dynamics they serve. His willingness to assist [[julie-le-breton|Julie]]'s scheme suggests a quiet sympathy for her position, or at least a recognition that her authority in the household, though unofficial, is real.

He works alongside Dixon, [[lady-henry|Lady Henry]]'s personal maid, forming part of the small domestic staff that maintains the [[bruton-street|Bruton Street]] establishment.
