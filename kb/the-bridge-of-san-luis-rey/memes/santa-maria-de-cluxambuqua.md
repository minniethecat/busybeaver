# Santa María de Cluxambuqua

## Overview

Santa María de Cluxambuqua is a shrine town in the Peruvian Andes, perched high among the peaks and accessible from [[lima|Lima]] by crossing the [[bridge-of-san-luis-rey|Bridge of San Luis Rey]]. The ground on which the shrine stands was considered holy through three successive religions — pre-Incan, Incan, and Christian — making it one of the most sacred sites in the region (002-059). The town is noted for the healing waters of its springs, which were sought out by figures as eminent as the [[archbishop-of-lima|Archbishop of Lima]] (004-047).

## In the Novel

### The Marquesa's Pilgrimage

[[marquesa-de-montemayor|The Marquesa de Montemayor]] made a pilgrimage to the shrine of Santa María de Cluxambuqua in the anxious weeks before her daughter Doña Clara was to give birth. The journey, undertaken as "the supreme rite of Peruvian households looking forward to this event," required crossing the bridge and ascending into the hills. The town itself is described as a place of tranquil beauty — "a city of large-girdled women, a tranquil town, slow-moving and slow-smiling, a city of crystal air, cold as the springs that fed its many fountains; a city of bells, soft and musical, and tuned to carry on with one another the happiest quarrels" (002-059).

It was during this pilgrimage, at an inn in Cluxambuqua, that the Marquesa underwent a profound spiritual change. While staying there, she read [[pepita|Pepita]]'s unfinished letter to [[abbess-madre-maria|the Abbess]] and was moved to write her famous Letter LVI — known as her "Second Corinthians" — containing the immortal paragraph beginning "Of the thousands of persons we meet in a lifetime, my child..." (002-083). The experience led her to whisper, "Let me live now. Let me begin again" — just two days before she and Pepita would perish crossing the bridge on their return to Lima (002-084).

### The Viceroy's Watering Place

Not far from Santa María de Cluxambuqua, [[viceroy-don-andres|the Viceroy Don Andrés]] built a fashionable resort modeled on the French spa at Vichy, complete with a pagoda, drawing-rooms, a theatre, a bullfighting arena, and French gardens (004-052). The [[archbishop-of-lima|Archbishop of Lima]] also visited, finding relief from the torments of kidney stones by drinking the water from the springs of Santa María de Cluxambuqua (004-047).
