# Hope

## Definition

Hope in *The Bridge of San Luis Rey* is presented with characteristic ambivalence: it is the force that sustains lives of service and the potential delusion that distorts them. The [[abbess-madre-maria|Abbess]] works in hope of "an age when women could be organized to protect women" (002-050)—a hope that the narrator, looking back from the twentieth century, calls "the whole folly of her hope." Yet it is precisely this hope that saves her from despair and gives her life its shape. Wilder treats hope not as naivete but as necessary obstinacy—the refusal to accept the world as it is.

## In the Novel

**The Abbess Madre María del Pilar** is hope's embodiment. She "hurled herself against the obstinacy of her time in her desire to attach a little dignity to women" (002-050). Her hope is described as a "folly," yet the novel's treatment is not ironic but tender: "Such persons are raised up in every age; they obstinately insist on transporting their grains of wheat and they derive a certain exhilaration from the sneers of the bystanders." The Abbess's hope is the novel's moral center—unrealistic, undeterrable, sacred.

**Pepita** represents hope in its nascent form. "The beginnings of hope and affection that [[pepita|Pepita]] had so lately come to feel" (002-053) are disrupted by her death on the bridge. Her hope is undeveloped, a seedling that never grows—and the Abbess's grief is partly grief for a hope cut short.

**Brother Juniper's hope** is theological: he hopes to prove that divine pattern can be demonstrated empirically. His hope is sincere, disciplined, and ultimately misguided—the novel does not condemn his hope so much as the arrogance of thinking it could be validated through data.

The novel ends with hope unresolved. The Abbess continues her work; [[camila-perichole|Camila]] continues her slow recovery; [[condesa-dona-clara|Doña Clara]] continues her regret. Hope is neither confirmed nor refuted—it simply persists, as the Abbess persists, because the alternative is not to live at all.

## Related Characters

- **Pepita** — hope cut short, a life barely begun
- **Brother Juniper** — hope that mistakes itself for certainty

## Related Concepts

- **Love** — hope and love sustain each other
