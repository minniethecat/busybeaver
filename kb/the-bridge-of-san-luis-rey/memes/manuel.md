# Manuel

## Overview

Manuel is the twin who falls. Where [[esteban|Esteban]]'s love is complete and undivided, Manuel's is captured by the [[camila-perichole|Perichole]]—"it was not the first time that Manuel had been fascinated by a woman, but it was the first time that his will and imagination had been thus overwhelmed" (003-009). His obsession introduces the fatal asymmetry into the brothers' bond: he experiences "that crazy loss of one's self, that neglect of everything but one's dramatic thoughts about the beloved," while Esteban watches from the outside. When Manuel dies of an infected wound, Esteban is left with a grief compounded by the knowledge that he was not enough.

## Biography

Found as an infant with his twin Esteban, Manuel is raised by the [[abbess-madre-maria|Abbess]] and grows up in the brothers' characteristic silence. After working as scribes together, they become laborers—ferrymen, fruit-pickers, teamsters. The Perichole's entrance into Manuel's life is catastrophic: he drifts outside her window at night, "his knuckles between his teeth, and listened to his loud heartbeats" (003-009). His wound—sustained in a brawl, or perhaps from his own despair—becomes infected, and he dies calling his brother's name.

## Relationships

- **[[esteban|Esteban]]** — his twin; the defining relationship, broken asymmetrically
- **[[camila-perichole|Camila Perichole]]** — the object of his obsession
- **The [[abbess-madre-maria|Abbess Madre Maria del Pilar]]** — his childhood guardian

## Themes

Manuel represents the vulnerability of [[love|love]]—how even the deepest bond can be disrupted by a new attachment that seems, to the one left behind, inexplicable. His story is the novel's most compressed tragedy: love arrives, displaces what was already there, and then destroys the vessel it entered.
