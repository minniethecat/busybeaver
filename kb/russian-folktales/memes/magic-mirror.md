# Magic Mirror

The magic mirror appears across multiple Russian tales as an instrument of supernatural vision. In *Vasilísa the Fair*, the Tsarévna "saw the evil thing that had been done, through her magic mirror; and she quickly summoned her carriage and drove to the sea" (030-062). The mirror collapses distance, making its possessor effectively omniscient within the tale's geography.

The mirror is a feminine magical artifact — used by the Tsarévna, the wise woman, the witch — and represents a mode of power (vision, knowledge, surveillance) distinct from the masculine mode (strength, combat, quest).
