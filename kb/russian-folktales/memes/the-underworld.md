# The Underworld

The underworld in Russian folklore is not the Christian Hell but a chthonic realm of magic, treasure, and testing. Ralston's Notes state plainly: "The underworld is the home of magic" (078-001). To descend is not to be damned but to enter the source of supernatural power.

In tales such as *The Realms of Copper, Silver and Gold*, the hero descends through three colored kingdoms — copper, silver, gold — each more wondrous than the last, each ruled by a captive princess. The descent structure maps the folk cosmos vertically: the ordinary world above, the magical realms below.
