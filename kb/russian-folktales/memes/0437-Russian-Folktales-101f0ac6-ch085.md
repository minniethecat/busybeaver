# Glossary

[079-001]Aspen

[079-002]Always associated with [[magic-in-russian-folklore|magic]]. Its trembling leaves give it a weird appearance.

[079-003][[baba-yaga|Bába Yagá]]

[079-004]Russian witch, also Yagá-Búra.

[079-005]Babushka

[079-006]The grandmother.

[079-007]Bárkhat

[079-008]This word also means velvet.

[079-009]Bátyushka

[079-010]Father in a general sense, meaning anybody older. Otéts is father, meaning the relationship of father and son.

[079-011]Birds’ milk

[079-012]The Russian folktale expression for asking for the moon.

[079-013]Boyárs

[079-014]This may be translated earls, but in the Russian social scale it only meant the bigger men, the seigneurs.

[079-015]Boyáryni

[079-016]Countesses, feminine plural of boyár.

[079-017]Chúdo-Yúda

[079-018]The Old Man of the Sea. This is a very clear loan from the Homeric Proteus.

[079-019]Dyádka

[079-020]Uncle. A term of respect.

[079-021]Egórushko Zalyót

[079-022]Means George the Bold Flier.

[079-023]Fatá

[079-024]A long silken glove.

[079-025]Gúsli

[079-026]A musical instrument, something like a zither with seven strings.

[079-027]Iváshko Zapéchnik

[079-028]Iván, who is always sitting behind the stove.

[079-029]Iváshechko

[079-030]A diminutive form of Iván.

[079-031]Iváshko

[079-032]A diminutive form of Iván.

[079-033]Izbá

[079-034]Hut.

[079-035]Kaftán

[079-036]A peasant’s overcoat, made very long.

[079-037]Khvalýnsk

[079-038]The old name of the Caspian. Vide Vazúza and Vólga.

[079-039]Korolévich

[079-040]King’s son. Koról, king.

[079-041]Korolévna

[079-042]King’s wife.

[079-043]Ksálavy

[079-044]Mythical birds, the meaning of which is entirely unknown.

[079-045]Mikháilo Ivánovich

[079-046]The popular name for the bear.

[079-047]Mísha Kosolápy

[079-048]Dmítri, the Bandy-legged.

[079-049]Morévna

[079-050]Of the sea.

[079-051]Nikíta

[079-052]From the Greek *Νικήτης*, conquer.

[079-053]Pope

[079-054]Village [[the-church-in-russian-folklore|priest]].

[079-055]Pud

[079-056]A Russian weight. Thirty-six pounds avoirdupois.

[079-057]Sarafán

[079-058]A short sleeveless jacket, generally embroidered, worn over the bodice or the blouse.

[079-059]Sazhén

[079-060]A length of seven feet.

[079-061]Sebézh

[079-062]A city in the Vítebsk province, bordering on Poland. The Poles and the Mussulmen are all called infidels, Saracens or Busormany.

[079-063]Shúba

[079-064]A fur mantle.

[079-065]Stárosta

[079-066]Mayor of a town.

[079-067]Teléga

[079-068]A peasant’s cart without springs.

[079-069]Tsarévich

[079-070]Tsar’s son.

[079-071]Tyátya

[079-072]Daddy.

[079-073]Tsarévna

[079-074]Tsar’s wife.

[079-075]Úkaz

[079-076]Imperial edict.

[079-077]Ványa

[079-078]A diminutive form of Iván.

[079-079]Vertodúb

[079-080]The oak-turner, a gigantic figure.

[079-081]Vertogór

[079-082]The mountain-turner; a gigantic figure.

[079-083]Vóron Vóronovich

[079-084]Crow Crowson.

[079-085]Zamorýshek

[079-086]This name is freely translated Benjamin, the last-born son of an old man.
