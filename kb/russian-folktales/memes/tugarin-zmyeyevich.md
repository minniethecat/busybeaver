# Túgarin Zmyéyevich

Túgarin Zmyéyevich is a winged pagan champion in the Kíev cycle of Russian byliny, whose patronymic — "Zmyéyevich," meaning "Son of the Serpent" — marks him as the offspring of a dragon. He confronts [[alyosha-popovich|Alyósha Popóvich]] at the court of [[prince-vladimir|Prince Vladímir]] and is ultimately slain in a duel in the open field (037-011, 037-022, 078-033).

## Identity

- **Role**: villain
- **Epithets**: "the Serpent's Son" (Zmyéyevich), "the Strong Man" (078-033)
- **Relations**: Son of a dragon (Zmyéy); adversary of the bogatyr [[alyosha-popovich|Alyósha Popóvich]] and his servant Marýshko Paránov (037-005, 037-014)

## Key Appearances

| tale | role | PN ref |
|------|------|--------|
| Alyósha Popóvich | Primary antagonist | [037-011]–[037-026] |

Túgarin appears in the bylina of Alyósha Popóvich, one of the three great bogatyri of the Kíev cycle. He arrives uninvited at Prince Vladímir's white stone palace and almost immediately comes into conflict with Alyósha (037-011).

## Traits and Attributes

Túgarin possesses **wings** that allow him to fly through the open sky — a physical trait that aligns him with dragon-kind rather than ordinary men (037-018, 037-020). He is armed with a **steel knife**, which he hurls at Alyósha during their first encounter in the palace; the knife misses, and Alyósha mockingly thanks him for the gift before promising to use it to carve out Túgarin's heart (037-013, 037-016).

His great size and power are conveyed through simile: when he and Alyósha meet in battle, it is "like two mountains falling on each other" (037-022). Despite his might, he is vulnerable to divine intervention — Alyósha prays to the Mother of God for a "thick gritty rain" to dampen Túgarin's light wings, causing him to fall to earth (037-021).

## Narrative Function

Túgarin Zmyéyevich serves as the **dragon-slayer's adversary** in a Christianized variant of the combat myth. Alyósha Popóvich's defeat of the pagan Serpent's Son mirrors the archetypal struggle between Christian order and pagan chaos, a theme central to the Kíev cycle.

Structurally, the duel unfolds in two stages: first, an indoor confrontation at Prince Vladímir's feast, where Túgarin's thrown knife is divinely redirected; second, the climactic outdoor battle, where Alyósha's prayer brings rain to ground the flying foe. Alyósha kills Túgarin with his own weapon and presents the severed head to Prince Vladímir — who, in a moment of dramatic irony, initially mistakes the approaching horseman for Túgarin bringing Alyósha's head (037-023, 037-024).

## Folkloric Context

The name "Zmyéyevich" connects Túgarin to the Slavic dragon (*zmyéy* or *zmey*), a recurring adversary in Russian folklore that also appears in tales of [[dobrynya-nikitich|Dobrýnya Nikítich]]'s battle with the dragon and in the broader Indo-European dragon-slaying tradition. Túgarin represents a transitional form between the fully zoomorphic dragon and the humanoid adversary — he flies on wings but wields a knife, attends feasts but is unmistakably monstrous.

The notes to Afanasyev's collection identify him simply as "Túgarin Zmyéyevich, the strong man, the Serpent's Son" (078-033), underscoring that his dragon lineage is his defining characteristic.
