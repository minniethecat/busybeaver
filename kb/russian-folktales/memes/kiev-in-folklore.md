# Kíev in Folklore

Kíev functions in the Russian folktale as the center of civilization — the court from which heroes like [[ivan-tsarevich]] depart and to which they return. It is the seat of [[prince-vladimir]], the gathering place of the [[bogatyr]] warriors such as [[ilya-muromets]], and the standard against which the wildness of [[the-thrice-tenth-realm]] is measured.

The Introduction identifies the bogatyrí as figures who "are decidedly demigods; others more decisively human" (075-004), and Kíev is where these semi-divine warriors converge. When a dragon "appeared near Kíev" demanding tribute, it is the city's bogatyr who must respond — the threat to the civilized center activating the hero's duty.
