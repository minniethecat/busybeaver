# The Self-Playing Gusli

The *gusli-samogudy* (self-playing psaltery) is a distinctively Russian magical instrument — a stringed psaltery that plays without human hands and compels supernatural effects from its audience. It appears in multiple tales as a tool of enchantment, capable of making demons dance, giants sleep, or the dead to stir.

## Music as Magic

The gusli embodies the folk concept of music as magical force — not merely beautiful sound but operative power that acts upon the listener's body and will. The Introduction notes that the third category of Russian folk record includes "magical incantations and popular melodies" (P02-005), suggesting that the magical instrument in the tale reflects actual ritual practice in which music and incantation were inseparable.
