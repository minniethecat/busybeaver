# Bryánsk Forest

The Bryánsk forest occupies a unique position in Russian folk geography — neither fully of this world nor fully of the thrice-nine realm, but a liminal zone where the natural bleeds into the supernatural. "Bryánsk in the Province of Orél contains wonderful woods which were in ancient times impenetrable, and became the legendary home of magic, and of weird happenings" (075-005).

A liminal forest between the ordinary world and the thrice-nine realm. "Bryánsk contains wonderful woods which were in ancient times impenetrable, and became the legendary home of magic, and of weird happenings" (075-005). The aspen tree — "always associated in Russian folklore with magic and wizardry" (075-005) — grows here. In *The Soldier and Death*, the soldier hangs Death herself "on the bitter aspen, on the very top twig" (022-062) in this forest.
