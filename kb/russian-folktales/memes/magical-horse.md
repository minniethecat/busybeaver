# Magical Horse

The [[magical-horse|magical horse]] is central to the Russian folktale arsenal and an indispensable companion on [[the-heros-quest]]. Bestowed upon the worthy hero — most famously [[ivan-tsarevich]] — this steed embodies the supernatural aid that pervades [[magic-in-russian-folklore]]. Kept restrained by "twelve locks" and "twelve chains" (072-021), its power is so immense that it requires physical containment. When the worthy hero approaches, "the magical horse stood there as though he were made of stone, and never pricked an ear" (072-025) — submission marking the true rider.

The Introduction identifies such magical animals among the common "incidents of transformations, magic, witches, the valorous youngest son" (P02-024), placing the magical horse firmly within the shared Indo-European narrative tradition.
