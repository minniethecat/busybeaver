# Ilyá Múromets

Ilyá Múromets is the greatest of the Russian *bogatyrí* — the epic knights who defend Prince Vladímir's court at Kíev against dragon, robber, and invading Tatar. His story bridges the folktale and the *bylína* (epic song), combining supernatural origin with historical resonance.

## The Bogatýr

The Introduction defines the bogatýr as a distinctively Russian figure: "He is a person of magical power and gigantic stature and prowess. Some of the bogatyrí are decidedly demigods; others more decisively human" (075-004). Ralston notes that "the derivation of the word has been very much in dispute. The characteristic thing to note is that the word is only found in Russian, and in no other Slavonic language" (075-004). Ilyá embodies this hybrid nature — born of peasant stock, granted supernatural strength by wandering pilgrims, serving a historical prince in a legendary court.

## Origin and Power

Ilyá was crippled for the first thirty years of his life, unable to walk, until "wandering pilgrims" (*kalíki*) came to his house and gave him a miraculous drink that conferred superhuman strength. "From the famous city of Múrom, out of the village of Karachárovo, the valiant, doughty youth Ilyá Múromets, the son of Iván, set out far into the open fields" — so begins his *bylína*, situating him precisely in the Russian geography while marking him for legendary destiny.

## The Nightingale Robber

Ilyá's most famous exploit is the defeat of the Nightingale Robber (*Solovéy-Razbóynik*), a monstrous being whose whistle could level forests and kill ordinary men. This tale appears in the Afanasyev collection under the title *The Story of the Brave and Doughty Knight Ilyá Múromets and the Nightingale Robber*.

## Folkloric Context

The Introduction classifies the bogatýr tales alongside the magical folktales. The Slav peoples "conserved primitive Aryan customs almost up to the middle of the nineteenth century; and then these were industriously and conscientiously compiled" (P02-026). Ilyá Múromets represents the point where pagan demigod, Christian saint (he was later canonized as Saint Ilya of Murom), and historical warrior converge.
