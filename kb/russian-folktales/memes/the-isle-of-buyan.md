# The Isle of Buyán

Buyán is the mythical island at the heart of Russian folk cosmology. Ralston's Notes describe it as "a kind of fairy hill like the Tír n'an óg of the Irish folktales, the land of youth" (078-008), suggesting an Indo-European parallel to the Celtic otherworld. "Most probably the mythical Isle of Buyán is the reminiscence of the Isle of Rügen" — the last stronghold of Slavic paganism on the Baltic coast (078-008).

## The Soul-Oak

It is on Buyán that the oak grows, beneath which the chest is buried, inside which the hare, duck, egg, and needle — Koshchéy's external soul — are contained. The island is thus not merely a location but the axis of the folktale cosmos, where the most powerful magical secret is hidden.
