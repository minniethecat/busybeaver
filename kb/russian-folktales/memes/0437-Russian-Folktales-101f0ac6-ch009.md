# A Tale of the Dead

## A Tale of the Dead

[003-001]Once a carpenter was going home late at night from a strange village: he had been at a jolly feast at a friend’s house. As he came back an old friend met him who had died some ten years before.

[003-002]“How do you do?”

[003-003]“How do you do?” said the walker, and he forgot that his friend had long ago taken the long road.

[003-004]“Come along with me: let us have a cup together once more.”

[003-005]“Let us go.”

[003-006]“I am so glad to have met you again, let us toast the occasion.”

[003-007]So they went into an *izbá*,5 and they had a drink and a talk. “Well, goodbye; time I went home!”

[003-008]“Stay, where are you going? Come and stay the night with me.”

[003-009]“No, brother, do not ask me: it is no good. I have business at home tomorrow and must be there early.”

[003-010]“Well, goodbye.”

[003-011]“But why should you go on foot? Better come on my horse, and he will gallop along gaily.”

[003-012]“Thank you very much.”

[003-013]So he sat on the horse, and the horse galloped away like a whirlwind.

[003-014]Suddenly the cock crowed: it was a very terrible sight! Graves all around, and under the wayfarer a gravestone!
