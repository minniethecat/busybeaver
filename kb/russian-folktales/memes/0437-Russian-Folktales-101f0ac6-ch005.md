# The Pronunciation of Russian Words

## The Pronunciation of Russian Words

[P03-001]Every Russian word has *one* strongly accented syllable, which is marked with an acute accent. The vowels are to be sounded as in Italian.

[P03-002]- *Ch* to be sounded as in English.

[P03-003]- *G* always hard, as in “*g*ive,” “*g*ot”: never as in “gem.”

[P03-004]- *J* always as in English.

[P03-005]- *Kh* like German *ch*, or Scotch *ch* in “lo*ch*.”

[P03-006]- *L* when hard (e.g. before *a*, *o*, *u*) something like *ll* in “pu*ll*”; when soft (e.g. before *e*, *i*) like *l* in French “vi*l*.”

[P03-007]- *S* always hard, as in “*s*o.”

[P03-008]- *V* as in English: at the end of words as *f*.

[P03-009]- *Y* consonantly, as in English “*y*et”; as a vowel like *i* in “w*i*ll.”

[P03-010]- *Z* always as in English.

[P03-011]- *Zh* like *s* in lei*s*ure, or French *j*.
