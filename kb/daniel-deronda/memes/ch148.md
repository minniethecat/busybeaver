[148-001]But something more precious than gold and gems came to [[Daniel-Deronda|Deronda]] from the

[148-002]neighborhood of [[diplow-hall|Diplow]] on the morning of his marriage. It was a letter

[148-003]containing these words:

[148-004]Do not think of me sorrowfully on your wedding-day. I have remembered

[148-005]your words—that I may live to be one of the best of women, who

[148-006]make others glad that they were born. I do not yet see how that can

[148-007]be, but you know better than I. If it ever comes true, it will be

[148-008]because you helped me. I only thought of myself, and I made you

[148-009]grieve. It hurts me now to think of your grief. You must not grieve

[148-010]any more for me. It is better—it shall be better with me because I

[148-011]have known you.

[148-012]GWENDOLEN GRANDCOURT.

[148-013]The preparations for the departure of all three to the East began at

[148-014]once; for Deronda could not deny [[Mordecai|Ezra]]’s wish that they should set out

[148-015]on the voyage forthwith, so that he might go with them, instead of

[148-016]detaining them to watch over him. He had no belief that Ezra’s life

[148-017]would last through the voyage, for there were symptoms which seemed to

[148-018]show that the last stage of his malady had set in. But Ezra himself had

[148-019]said, “Never mind where I die, so that I am with you.”

[148-020]He did not set out with them. One morning early he said to Deronda, “Do

[148-021]not quit me to-day. I shall die before it is ended.”

[148-022]He chose to be dressed and sit up in his easy chair as usual, Deronda

[148-023]and [[Mirah-Lapidoth|Mirah]] on each side of him, and for some hours he was unusually

[148-024]silent, not even making the effort to speak, but looking at them

[148-025]occasionally with eyes full of some restful meaning, as if to assure

[148-026]them that while this remnant of breathing-time was difficult, he felt

[148-027]an ocean of peace beneath him.

[148-028]It was not till late in the afternoon, when the light was falling, that

[148-029]he took a hand of each in his and said, looking at Deronda, “Death is

[148-030]coming to me as the divine kiss which is both parting and

[148-031]reunion—which takes me from your bodily eyes and gives me full

[148-032]presence in your soul. Where thou goest, Daniel, I shall go. Is it not

[148-033]begun? Have I not breathed my soul into you? We shall live together.”

[148-034]He paused, and Deronda waited, thinking that there might be another

[148-035]word for him. But slowly and with effort Ezra, pressing on their hands,

[148-036]raised himself and uttered in Hebrew the confession of the divine

[148-037]Unity, which long for generations has been on the lips of the dying

[148-038]Israelite.

[148-039]He sank back gently into his chair, and did not speak again. But it was

[148-040]some hours before he had ceased to breathe, with Mirah’s and Deronda’s

[148-041]arms around him.

[148-042]“Nothing is here for tears, nothing to wail

[148-043]Or knock the breast; no weakness, no contempt,

[148-044]Dispraise or blame; nothing but well and fair,

[148-045]And what may quiet us in a death so noble.”
