# Jewish Identity & Heritage

*Daniel Deronda* (1876) is George Eliot's most sustained engagement with Jewish identity, culture, and nationalism. The novel traces Daniel Deronda's gradual discovery of his Jewish ancestry and his eventual embrace of a Zionist calling, while painting a rich portrait of Anglo-Jewish life through the Cohen family, the visionary Mordecai, and the singer Mirah Lapidoth.
