# The Diamonds Delivery

Grandcourt, now married to Gwendolen, demands that his former mistress Lydia Glasher hand over the family diamonds that he had previously given her, intending to bestow them upon his new wife — an act of calculated cruelty that transforms Lydia's resentment into active vengeance.

## Context

Before his marriage to Gwendolen, Grandcourt had conducted a long liaison with Lydia Glasher, who left her husband for him and bore his children. As a mark of his commitment, Grandcourt had given Lydia his mother's diamonds [061-090, 061-092]. Lydia treasured these diamonds not only for their value but as the symbol of her claim upon Grandcourt. When Grandcourt chose to marry Gwendolen instead, the diamonds became the focus of an unresolved conflict between past and present.

## The Event

Grandcourt, with characteristic coldness, informs Lydia that he requires the diamonds back. He intends to give them to Gwendolen, asserting his legal right to the jewels while ignoring the moral obligation they represent [062-120]. When Lydia protests, Grandcourt is immovable: the diamonds must be delivered before a specified deadline [062-154]. The demand is delivered not as a request but as an ultimatum — the diamonds that Lydia had worn as a mark of her favored status are to adorn the woman who has displaced her [062-131].

Lydia, humiliated and enraged, complies — but not without extracting her revenge. She sends the diamonds to Gwendolen along with a letter revealing their history, ensuring that Gwendolen will know she wears a cursed inheritance [062-174]. When Gwendolen receives the diamonds, the accompanying message transforms them from a gift into an accusation [063-032, 063-055].

## Participants

- **Henleigh Grandcourt**: The perpetrator; his action reveals the full extent of his moral vacancy — he treats both women with the same instrumental cruelty
- **Lydia Glasher**: The victim turned avenger; her compliance masks a determination to destroy Gwendolen's peace of mind
- **Gwendolen Harleth**: The unwitting recipient; the diamonds become the physical token of her guilty knowledge

## Consequences

The delivery of the diamonds with Lydia's letter is the pivotal event in Gwendolen's moral education. She can no longer pretend ignorance of Grandcourt's past or her own complicity in Lydia's displacement. The diamonds become a weight she cannot escape — a constant reminder that her marriage is built on another woman's ruin. The knowledge she gains through this event will haunt her until Grandcourt's death and beyond, shaping her confession to Deronda and her eventual moral awakening.

## Thematic Significance

The diamonds crystallize the novel's treatment of inheritance, guilt, and the inescapable past. What should be a gift becomes a curse; what should signify love becomes evidence of betrayal. The event demonstrates how the sins of the powerful — Grandcourt's casual cruelty — ripple outward to poison the lives of both the woman he abandons and the woman he claims.

## Key Passages

> He must ask for the diamonds which he had promised to Gwendolen [062-127]
