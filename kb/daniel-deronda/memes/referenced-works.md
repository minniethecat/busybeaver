# Referenced Works

This page catalogues the literary, musical, and artistic works that George Eliot weaves into *Daniel Deronda* (1876). These references range from English classics (Shakespeare's plays, Milton's *Paradise Lost*) to Continental music (Schubert's compositions, opera) and Jewish cultural texts (Hebrew melodies). Each referenced work enriches the novel's thematic texture and character development.

## About This Index

Referenced works are listed alphabetically. They include literary texts quoted or discussed by characters, musical pieces performed at social gatherings (notably by Klesmer and Mirah), and works that serve as intertextual touchstones for the novel's treatment of art, morality, and cultural inheritance.
