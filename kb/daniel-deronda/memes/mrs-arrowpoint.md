# Mrs. Arrowpoint

Mrs. Arrowpoint is the mistress of [[Quetcham|Quetcham Hall]], the mother of [[Catherine-Arrowpoint]], and a figure of literary pretension in Wessex county society. Her social vigilance and maternal ambitions make her a foil to both [[Gwendolen-Harleth]] and her own daughter.

## Social Character

Mrs. Arrowpoint is a woman of "well-meaning" [007-070] disposition but sharp social instincts. The narrator notes that she "was ridiculous" but not therefore less formidable [007-102]. She observes Gwendolen with a mixture of admiration and suspicion, detecting the younger woman's "double and satirical" nature [007-080] and remaining "on her guard against her" [007-080].

Her literary pretensions are among her defining traits: she is a woman with "sheets in manuscript" [007-108] to her credit, a detail that Gwendolen's sharper perceptions immediately file away. The narrator's portrayal of her as "the Lady of Quetcham" [007-075] captures both her status and her self-regard.

## Role in the Marriage Plot

When [[Catherine-Arrowpoint]] declares her intention to marry [[Herr-Klesmer]], Mrs. Arrowpoint's reaction is more visceral than her husband's. She exclaims, "who in their senses ever thought it would come to this?" [022-177], and demands that Mr. Arrowpoint intervene: "Mr. Arrowpoint, _will_ you sit by and hear this without speaking?" [022-177].

Her opposition is rooted in social ambition and a horror of mésalliance. Yet the narrator's treatment of her is characteristic of Eliot's irony: her objections, though socially predictable, are rendered with enough energy to be dramatically compelling even as they are gently satirized.

## Relationship with Gwendolen

Mrs. Arrowpoint's interactions with Gwendolen are a study in mutual wariness. She extends hospitality as social duty requires, but privately confides that Gwendolen "is less agreeable" on further acquaintance [007-075]. Her suspicion of Gwendolen's sincerity — "this girl is double and satirical" [007-080] — proves prescient, though her own motives are no less mixed.
