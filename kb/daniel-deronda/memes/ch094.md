[094-001]“That’s it, then!” said Cohen, slapping his knee. “He’s been expecting

[094-002]you, and it’s taken hold of him. I suppose he talks about his learning

[094-003]to you. It’s uncommonly kind of _you_, sir; for I don’t suppose there’s

[094-004]much to be got out of it, else it wouldn’t have left him where he is.

[094-005]But there’s the shop.” Cohen hurried out, and Jacob, who had been

[094-006]listening inconveniently near to [[Daniel-Deronda|Deronda]]’s elbow, said to him with

[094-007]obliging familiarity, “I’ll call [[Mordecai]] for you, if you like.”

[094-008]“No, Jacob,” said his mother; “open the door for the gentleman, and let

[094-009]him go in himself Hush! Don’t make a noise.”

[094-010]Skillful Jacob seemed to enter into the play, and turned the handle of

[094-011]the door as noiselessly as possible, while Deronda went behind him and

[094-012]stood on the threshold. The small room was lit only by a dying fire and

[094-013]one candle with a shade over it. On the board fixed under the window,

[094-014]various objects of jewelry were scattered: some books were heaped in

[094-015]the corner beyond them. Mordecai was seated on a high chair at the

[094-016]board with his back to the door, his hands resting on each other and on

[094-017]the board, a watch propped on a stand before him. He was in a state of

[094-018]expectation as sickening as that of a prisoner listening for the

[094-019]delayed deliverance—when he heard Deronda’s voice saying, “I am come

[094-020]for you. Are you ready?”

[094-021]Immediately he turned without speaking, seized his furred cap which lay

[094-022]near, and moved to join Deronda. It was but a moment before they were

[094-023]both in the sitting-room, and Jacob, noticing the change in his

[094-024]friend’s air and expression, seized him by the arm and said, “See my

[094-025]cup and ball!” sending the ball up close to Mordecai’s face, as

[094-026]something likely to cheer a convalescent. It was a sign of the relieved

[094-027]tension in Mordecai’s mind that he could smile and say, “Fine, fine!”

[094-028]“You have forgotten your greatcoat and comforter,” said young Mrs.

[094-029]Cohen, and he went back into the workroom and got them.

[094-030]“He’s come to life again, do you see?” said Cohen, who had

[094-031]re-entered—speaking in an undertone. “I told you so: I’m mostly

[094-032]right.” Then in his usual voice, “Well, sir, we mustn’t detain you now,

[094-033]I suppose; but I hope this isn’t the last time we shall see you.”

[094-034]“Shall you come again?” said Jacob, advancing. “See, I can catch the

[094-035]ball; I’ll bet I catch it without stopping, if you come again.”

[094-036]“He has clever hands,” said Deronda, looking at the grandmother. “Which

[094-037]side of the family does he get them from?”

[094-038]But the grandmother only nodded towards her son, who said promptly, “My

[094-039]side. My wife’s family are not in that line. But bless your soul! ours

[094-040]is a sort of cleverness as good as gutta percha; you can twist it which

[094-041]way you like. There’s nothing some old gentlemen won’t do if you set

[094-042]’em to it.” Here Cohen winked down at Jacob’s back, but it was doubtful

[094-043]whether this judicious allusiveness answered its purpose, for its

[094-044]subject gave a nasal whinnying laugh and stamped about [[music|singing]], “Old

[094-045]gentlemen, old gentlemen,” in chiming cadence.

[094-046]Deronda thought, “I shall never know anything decisive about these

[094-047]people until I ask Cohen pointblank whether he lost a sister named

[094-048][[Mirah-Lapidoth|Mirah]] when she was six years old.” The decisive moment did not yet seem

[094-049]easy for him to face. Still his first sense of repulsion at the

[094-050]commonness of these people was beginning to be tempered with kindlier

[094-051]feeling. However unrefined their airs and speech might be, he was

[094-052]forced to admit some moral refinement in their treatment of the

[094-053]consumptive workman, whose mental distinction impressed them chiefly as

[094-054]a harmless, silent raving.

[094-055]“The Cohens seem to have an affection for you,” said Deronda, as soon

[094-056]as he and Mordecai were off the doorstep.

[094-057]“And I for them,” was the immediate answer. “They have the heart of the

[094-058]Israelite within them, though they are as the horse and the mule,

[094-059]without understanding beyond the narrow path they tread.”

[094-060]“I have caused you some uneasiness, I fear,” said Deronda, “by my

[094-061]slowness in fulfilling my promise. I wished to come yesterday, but I

[094-062]found it impossible.”

[094-063]“Yes—yes, I trusted you. But it is true I have been uneasy, for the

[094-064]spirit of my youth has been stirred within me, and this body is not

[094-065]strong enough to bear the beating of its wings. I am as a man bound and

[094-066]imprisoned through long years: behold him brought to speech of his

[094-067]fellow and his limbs set free: he weeps, he totters, the joy within him

[094-068]threatens to break and overthrow the tabernacle of flesh.”

[094-069]“You must not speak too much in this evening air,” said Deronda,

[094-070]feeling Mordecai’s words of reliance like so many cords binding him

[094-071]painfully. “Cover your mouth with the woolen scarf. We are going to the

[094-072]_[[hand-and-banner|Hand and Banner]]_, I suppose, and shall be in private there?”

[094-073]“No, that is my trouble that you did not come yesterday. For this is

[094-074]the evening of the club I spoke of, and we might not have any minutes

[094-075]alone until late, when all the rest are gone. Perhaps we had better

[094-076]seek another place. But I am used to that only. In new places the outer

[094-077]world presses on me and narrows the inward vision. And the people there

[094-078]are familiar with my face.”

[094-079]“I don’t mind the club if I am allowed to go in,” said Deronda. “It is

[094-080]enough that you like this place best. If we have not enough time I will

[094-081]come again. What sort of club is it?”

[094-082]“It is called ‘The Philosophers.’ They are few—like the cedars of

[094-083]Lebanon—poor men given to thought. But none so poor as I am: and

[094-084]sometimes visitors of higher worldly rank have been brought. We are

[094-085]allowed to introduce a friend, who is interested in our topics. Each

[094-086]orders beer or some other kind of drink, in payment for the room. Most

[094-087]of them smoke. I have gone when I could, for there are other men of my

[094-088]race who come, and sometimes I have broken silence. I have pleased

[094-089]myself with a faint likeness between these poor philosophers and the

[094-090]Masters who handed down the thought of our race—the great

[094-091]Transmitters, who labored with their hands for scant bread, but

[094-092]preserved and enlarged for us the [[inheritance|heritage]] of memory, and saved the

[094-093]soul of Israel alive as a seed among the tombs. The heart pleases

[094-094]itself with faint resemblances.”

[094-095]“I shall be very glad to go and sit among them, if that will suit you.

[094-096]It is a sort of meeting I should like to join in,” said Deronda, not

[094-097]without relief in the prospect of an interval before he went through

[094-098]the strain of his next private conversation with Mordecai.

[094-099]In three minutes they had opened the glazed door with the red curtain,

[094-100]and were in the little parlor, hardly much more than fifteen feet

[094-101]square, where the gaslight shone through a slight haze of smoke on what

[094-102]to Deronda was a new and striking scene. Half-a-dozen men of various

[094-103]ages, from between twenty and thirty to fifty, all shabbily dressed,

[094-104]most of them with clay pipes in their mouths, were listening with a

[094-105]look of concentrated intelligence to a man in a pepper-and-salt dress,

[094-106]with blonde hair, short nose, broad forehead and general breadth, who,

[094-107]holding his pipe slightly uplifted in the left hand, and beating his

[094-108]knee with the right, was just finishing a quotation from Shelley (the

[094-109]comparison of the avalanche in his “Prometheus Unbound”)

[094-110]“As thought by thought is piled, till some great truth

[094-111]Is loosened, and the nations echo round.”

[094-112]The entrance of the new-comers broke the fixity of attention, and

[094-113]called for re-arrangement of seats in the too narrow semicircle round

[094-114]the fire-place and the table holding the glasses, spare pipes and

[094-115]tobacco. This was the soberest of clubs; but sobriety is no reason why

[094-116]smoking and “taking something” should be less imperiously needed as a

[094-117]means of getting a decent status in company and debate. Mordecai was

[094-118]received with welcoming voices which had a slight cadence of [[sympathy|compassion]]

[094-119]in them, but naturally all glances passed immediately to his companion.

[094-120]“I have brought a friend who is interested in our subjects,” said

[094-121]Mordecai. “He has traveled and studied much.”

[094-122]“Is the gentlemen anonymous? Is he a Great Unknown?” said the

[094-123]broad-chested quoter of Shelley, with a humorous air.

[094-124]“My name is Daniel Deronda. I am unknown, but not in any sense great.”

[094-125]The smile breaking over the stranger’s grave face as he said this was

[094-126]so agreeable that there was a general indistinct murmur, equivalent to

[094-127]a “Hear, hear,” and the broad man said,

[094-128]“You recommend the name, sir, and are welcome. Here, Mordecai, come to

[094-129]this corner against me,” he added, evidently wishing to give the

[094-130]coziest place to the one who most needed it.

[094-131]Deronda was well satisfied to get a seat on the opposite side, where

[094-132]his general survey of the party easily included Mordecai, who remained

[094-133]an eminently striking object in this group of sharply-characterized

[094-134]figures, more than one of whom, even to Daniel’s little exercised

[094-135]discrimination, seemed probably of Jewish descent.

[094-136]In fact pure English blood (if leech or lancet can furnish us with the

[094-137]precise product) did not declare itself predominantly in the party at

[094-138]present assembled. Miller, the broad man, an exceptional second-hand

[094-139]bookseller who knew the insides of books, had at least grand-parents

[094-140]who called themselves German, and possibly far-away ancestors who

[094-141]denied themselves to be Jews; Buchan, the saddler, was Scotch; Pash,

[094-142]the watchmaker, was a small, dark, vivacious, triple-baked Jew; Gideon,

[094-143]the optical instrument maker, was a Jew of the red-haired,

[094-144]generous-featured type easily passing for Englishmen of unusually

[094-145]cordial manners: and Croop, the dark-eyed shoemaker, was probably more

[094-146]Celtic than he knew. Only three would have been discernable everywhere

[094-147]as Englishmen: the wood-inlayer Goodwin, well-built, open-faced,

[094-148]pleasant-voiced; the florid laboratory assistant Marrables; and Lily,

[094-149]the pale, neat-faced copying-clerk, whose light-brown hair was set up

[094-150]in a small parallelogram above his well-filled forehead, and whose

[094-151]shirt, taken with an otherwise seedy costume, had a freshness that

[094-152]might be called insular, and perhaps even something narrower.

[094-153]Certainly a company select of the select among poor men, being drawn

[094-154]together by a taste not prevalent even among the privileged heirs of

[094-155]learning and its institutions; and not likely to amuse any gentleman in

[094-156]search of crime or low comedy as the ground of interest in people whose

[094-157]weekly income is only divisible into shillings. Deronda, even if he had

[094-158]not been more than usually inclined to gravity under the influence of

[094-159]what was pending between him and Mordecai, would not have set himself

[094-160]to find food for laughter in the various shades of departure from the

[094-161]tone of polished society sure to be observable in the air and talk of

[094-162]these men who had probably snatched knowledge as most of us snatch

[094-163]indulgences, making the utmost of scant opportunity. He looked around

[094-164]him with the quiet air of respect habitual to him among equals, ordered

[094-165]whisky and water, and offered the contents of his cigar-case, which,

[094-166]characteristically enough, he always carried and hardly ever used for

[094-167]his own behoof, having reasons for not smoking himself, but liking to

[094-168]indulge others. Perhaps it was his weakness to be afraid of seeming

[094-169]straight-laced, and turning himself into a sort of diagram instead of a

[094-170]growth which can exercise the guiding attraction of fellowship. That he

[094-171]made a decidedly winning impression on the company was proved by their

[094-172]showing themselves no less at ease than before, and desirous of quickly

[094-173]resuming their interrupted talk.

[094-174]“This is what I call one of our touch-and-go nights, sir,” said Miller,

[094-175]who was implicitly accepted as a sort of moderator—on addressing

[094-176]Deronda by way of explanation, and nodding toward each person whose

[094-177]name he mentioned. “Sometimes we stick pretty close to the point. But

[094-178]to-night our friend Pash, there, brought up the law of progress; and we

[094-179]got on statistics; then Lily, there, saying we knew well enough before

[094-180]counting that in the same state of society the same sort of things

[094-181]would happen, and it was no more wonder that quantities should remain

[094-182]the same, than that qualities should remain the same, for in relation

[094-183]to society numbers are qualities—the number of drunkards is a quality

[094-184]in society—the numbers are an index to the qualities, and give us no

[094-185]instruction, only setting us to consider the causes of difference

[094-186]between different social states—Lily saying this, we went off on the

[094-187]causes of social change, and when you came in I was going upon the

[094-188]power of ideas, which I hold to be the main transforming cause.”
