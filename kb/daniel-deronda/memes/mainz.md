# Mainz

Mainz is the German banking center where Deronda's inheritance from his grandfather is held — a city that appears only briefly in the novel's closing movement but carries immense symbolic weight as the financial foundation of his Jewish mission.

## Description

Mainz enters the narrative through the practical mechanism of a banking house: the Princess Halm-Eberstein directs Deronda to "the banking-house in Mainz, where you are to go for your" inheritance (116-084). The city itself is not described — it exists in the novel primarily as a financial destination — but its function is crucial: the Mainz bank holds the material resources that will fund Deronda's work. The journey to Mainz, undertaken as part of Deronda's larger movement toward Jewish identity, links economic inheritance to cultural inheritance, suggesting that both are necessary for the mission he is about to undertake.

## Narrative Function

Mainz serves as the financial pivot of Deronda's transformation. Throughout the novel, questions of inheritance — who gets what, and why — drive the English plot (Diplow, Ryelands, Grandcourt's will). Mainz represents an alternative model: inheritance in service of a cause rather than personal enrichment. The money Deronda receives there is not British landed property but continental financial capital, channeled through Jewish banking networks, and destined to support the national revival Mordecai envisions.

## Associated Characters

- **Daniel Deronda**: Travels to Mainz to claim his inheritance, which will fund his Jewish mission (PN 116-084)
- **Mordecai**: Does not travel to Mainz but his vision gives Deronda's financial inheritance its purpose; the money from Mainz will support their shared cause (PN 124-082)

## Symbolic Significance

Mainz embodies the novel's vision of inheritance as purposeful transmission rather than passive receipt. Unlike the English estates that cause conflict and suffering, the Mainz inheritance arrives cleanly, designated for a specific mission. The city's location in Germany — the heart of Reform Judaism and Haskalah — further aligns financial inheritance with cultural rebirth. That the money comes through a "banking-house" also acknowledges the historical role of Jewish financial networks in European economic development.

## Key Scenes

- The Princess's direction to Deronda: "the banking-house in Mainz, where you are to go for your" inheritance, linking financial disclosure to identity revelation (PN 116-084)
- Deronda's journey to Mainz with "wishes" — the material and spiritual converge as he moves toward his destiny (PN 124-107)
