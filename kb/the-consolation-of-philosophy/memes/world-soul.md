## Definition & Context

The World Soul (*Anima Mundi*) is the principle of life, motion, and rational order that pervades the entire cosmos. Drawing directly on [[plato|Plato]]'s [[timaeus|Timaeus]], [[boethius|Boethius]] describes the World Soul in the great hymn of Book III (3.M9) as emanating from the Divine Mind and animating all things. The World Soul is the intermediary between the eternal realm of [[divine-intellect|Intellect]] and the temporal realm of matter — it receives the Forms from above and impresses them upon the material world below.

> "Thou dost unite the soul to the body, creating a living being from these two natures" (012-041)

## Role in the Consolation

The World Soul hymn (3.M9) is the poetic and philosophical centre of the *Consolation*. After two books of dismantling false goods, Philosophy turns to the positive task of describing the true structure of reality, and she begins with cosmology — the ordered universe that is the visible expression of divine [[providence|Providence]]. The World Soul is the mechanism by which that order is transmitted from the eternal to the temporal, from the One to the many.
