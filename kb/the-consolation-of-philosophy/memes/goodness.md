## Definition & Context

Goodness (*bonitas*) is the fundamental metaphysical and ethical concept of the *Consolation* — the attribute of God, the goal of [[desire|desire]], and the ontological ground of all [[being|being]]. [[lady-philosophy|Philosophy]]'s central argument in Book III is that [[happiness|happiness]] (*beatitudo*) is identical with the supreme Good, and the supreme Good is identical with God. "Since happiness is nothing else but the enjoyment of the supreme Good, and the supreme Good is God, it follows that true happiness is the enjoyment of God" (010-184).

The doctrine of the convertibility of being and goodness — everything that exists is good insofar as it exists, and evil is a privation rather than a positive reality — is the metaphysical foundation for Philosophy's resolution of the [[problem-of-evil|problem of evil]] in Book IV.
