## Definition & Context

Perfection (*perfectio*) is the condition of being complete, lacking nothing, fully actualised. It is the metaphysical concept that bridges the *Consolation*'s arguments about [[happiness|happiness]] and [[God|God]]: happiness is the perfection of human nature, and God is the being whose perfection is absolute and self-sufficient. [[lady-philosophy|Philosophy]] argues that "in what manner this perfection of felicity is built up" can be demonstrated by analysing the false goods into the one true Good (010-184).

> "I will next show in what manner this perfection of felicity is built up" (010-184)

## Key Passages

On the building of perfect happiness:

> "I will next show in what manner this perfection of felicity is built up. And first, observe that whatever is in any respect imperfect must necessarily derive from something perfect" (010-184, 015-022)

On human imperfection:

> "These things are, perchance, due to the imperfection of human nature" (003-012)
