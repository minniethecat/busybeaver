## Definition & Context

Hierarchy is a concept that plays a supporting role in [[boethius|Boethius]]'s philosophical argument. It belongs to the Neoplatonic metaphysical framework that undergirds the *Consolation* — the hierarchical vision of reality in which all things derive from and return to the divine [[good|Good]].

The concept contributes to [[lady-philosophy|Philosophy]]'s systematic exposition of the structure of reality, the nature of the soul, and the path to [[happiness|true happiness]].

## Role in the Consolation

Hierarchy functions within Philosophy's broader argument about the relationship between the temporal and the eternal, the mutable and the immutable. The *Consolation* draws on this concept to illuminate the nature of [[providence|Providence]], the structure of [[order|cosmic order]], and the soul's capacity to ascend from the particular to the universal through the exercise of [[reason|reason]].
