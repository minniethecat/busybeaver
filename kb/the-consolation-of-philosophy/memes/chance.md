## Definition & Context

Chance (*casus*, *fortuna*) is the appearance of randomness — events that seem to occur without cause or purpose. The *Consolation* systematically eliminates chance as a genuine metaphysical category: what appears to be chance is actually [[providence|Providence]] operating through the chain of [[fate|Fate]], invisible to those who cannot see the whole. [[lady-philosophy|Philosophy]]'s definition is famous: chance is "an unexpected event brought about by causes concurring in things done for some purpose" (017-057).

> "If anyone defines chance as an event produced by random motion without any chain of causation, I assert that chance is nothing at all" (017-050)

## Key Passages

On the definition of chance:

> "Chance is an unexpected event brought about by causes concurring in things done for some purpose" (017-057)

On the illusion of randomness:

> "If anyone defines chance as an event produced by random motion without any chain of causation, I assert that chance is nothing at all" (017-050)
