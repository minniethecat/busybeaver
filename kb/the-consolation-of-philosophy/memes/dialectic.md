## Definition & Context

Dialectic (*dialectica*) is the art of logical reasoning — the method by which philosophy proceeds from accepted premises to necessary conclusions through question and answer. The *Consolation* is a dialectical work: it proceeds by [[lady-philosophy|Philosophy]] questioning [[boethius|Boethius]], exposing contradictions in his beliefs, and leading him through a chain of reasoning to conclusions he had forgotten but never truly denied.

Boethius was the author of logical commentaries and translations that made him the primary transmitter of Aristotelian logic to the Latin Middle Ages. His treatise *De Topicis Differentiis* was a standard textbook for centuries. The *Consolation* demonstrates dialectic in action — not as a technical discipline but as a therapeutic method, the medicine that cures the soul's sickness through the power of rational argument.
