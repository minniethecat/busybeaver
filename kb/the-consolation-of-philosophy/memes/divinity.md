## Definition & Context

Divinity is a concept that plays a supporting role in [[boethius|Boethius]]'s philosophical argument. It belongs to the Neoplatonic metaphysical framework that undergirds the *Consolation* and was transmitted to the Latin Middle Ages largely through Boethius's translations and commentaries.

## Role in the Consolation

The concept contributes to [[lady-philosophy|Philosophy]]'s comprehensive philosophical vision — her diagnosis of Boethius's sickness and her prescription of a cure. Understanding this aspect of the philosophical framework is essential to grasping the full scope of the *Consolation*'s argument about [[providence|Providence]], [[fate|Fate]], [[free-will|free will]], and [[happiness|true happiness]].
