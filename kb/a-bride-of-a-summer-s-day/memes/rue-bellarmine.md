# Rue Bellarmine

The Rue Bellarmine is a Paris street in *A [[ida-culvers|Bride]] of a Summer's Day*, notable as the location of the Church of the [[carmelite-friars|Carmelite Friars]] and the residence of [[father-baldwin|Father Baldwin]], the English priest who discovers [[ida-culvers|Ida Culvers]]'s diamond brooch in the offertory bag.

## Location Type

The Rue Bellarmine is a Parisian street in the vicinity of the Palais de Justice. It houses the Carmelite Friars church — where English Catholic expatriates worship — and the adjoining residence of [[father-baldwin|Father Baldwin]], who officiates there.

## Description

The Rue Bellarmine presents a stark moral contrast to the [[rue-vervien|Rue Vervien]] in the novel's Parisian geography. Where the Rue Vervien is the haunt of gamblers and adventurers, the Rue Bellarmine is the seat of religious integrity and institutional trust.

It is to this address that [[lord-george-culvers|Lord Culvers]] and [[clive-redway|Clive Redway]] direct their coachman after [[sefton-culvers|Sefton Culvers]]'s "extraordinary" refusal to identify [[ida-culvers|Ida]]'s brooch (014-010). At Father Baldwin's residence, the brooch is identified and the Paris investigation formally begins.

The street's name — evoking the Counter-Reformation cardinal Robert Bellarmine — suggests doctrinal rigour and moral seriousness, qualities embodied by [[father-baldwin|Father Baldwin]]'s conscientious handling of the brooch.
