# Abruzzi

The Abruzzi (modern Abruzzo) is a mountainous region in south-central Italy, referenced in *A Bride of a Summer's Day* as a region bordering [[calabria|Calabria]].

## Role in the Novel

The region is first mentioned in Chapter XII when a waiter in [[paris|Paris]] identifies the dialect of an Italian organ-boy as belonging to "the mountain dialects of Calabria or the Abruzzi" (012-028). Later, in Chapter XVIII, it is revealed that [[sefton-culvers|Sefton Culvers]], after his brother officer fell ill in [[naples|Naples]], continued his travels alone through the region before diverging into [[calabria|Calabria]] and eventually reaching [[la-sila|La Sila]] (018-002). The Abruzzi thus forms part of the geographic backdrop to Sefton's Italian journey, a period during which he assumed the false identity "Xardez" and became entangled with the household of [[francesca-xardez|Francesca Xardez]].
