# Marchese da Nava

The Marchese da Nava is the chief landowner in [[alta-lauria|Alta Lauria]], a mountain hamlet in [[calabria|Calabria]], and the widowed father of [[violante]]. He appears in Chapter XVIII of *A Bride of a Summer's Day*.

## Role in the Novel

The Marchese provides hospitality to [[sefton-culvers|Sefton Culvers]] when the Englishman arrives in Alta Lauria, and it is within the Marchese's household that Sefton encounters the healer [[francesca-xardez|Francesca Xardez]] and her nurse-child [[violante|Violante]], setting in motion the events that lead to his secret marriage and the eventual crisis of the plot.

## Character

He was a widower "close upon seventy years of age" when Sefton visited Alta Lauria. Late in life he had married a peasant girl, who died leaving him with one child, Violante (018-007). Although his large estates consisted largely of exhausted mines, hill pastureland, and mountains, and he was a man of "ungovernable temper" with an ill-arranged household, the devotion of the peasantry to him was unswerving (018-007).

> This Marchese da Nava was a widower, and a man close upon sixty years of age; but in spite of his years he was still strong and vigorous, and unsparing of his strength in his endeavors to crush the brigandage which had become a terror in the district.
>
> (018-007)
