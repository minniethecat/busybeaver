# Aunt Sefton

Aunt Sefton was the aged, widowed mother of [[sefton-culvers|Sefton Culvers]] and sister-in-law of [[lord-george-culvers|Lord George Culvers]]. She lived in Devon, where her son's body was brought for burial after his death in Italy. (024-003)(024-009)

## Ida's Devotion

After Sefton's death, [[ida-culvers|Ida Culvers]] passed nearly two full years in Devon with her husband's aged mother, "ministering to her as a daughter might, and doing her utmost to render her closing days days of peace, if not of happiness." (024-003)

Ida considered her obligations to Sefton's desolate mother binding until death released her. [[juliet-culvers|Juliet Culvers]] remonstrated in vain — Ida held to her purpose, and it was not until the old woman passed away that Juliet found her opportunity for "plain speaking" about Ida's future. (024-015)

Juliet later referred to her as "Aunt Sefton," noting that her death need not prevent "a quiet, a very quiet wedding." (024-037)
