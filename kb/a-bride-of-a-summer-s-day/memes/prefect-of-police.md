## Role in the Investigation

> They had returned from their interview with the Prefect of Police, and now sat in Clive's sitting-room at his hotel, trying to "face the worst and act for the best." That interview had been a long and painful one, and the two men had come away from it fully convinced that they had acted the part of imbeciles in allowing a fortnight of precious time to slip away without making an effort to track the missing girl.
>
> (014-003)

The Prefect of Police in [[paris|Paris]] was brought into the investigation of [[ida-culvers|Ida Culvers]]'s disappearance after the discovery of her damaged diamond brooch in the offertory bag at the [[carmelite-friars|Carmelite Friars Church]] in the [[paris|Rue Bellarmine]]. (013-016)

## The Interview

[[clive-redway|Clive Redway]] and [[lord-george-culvers|Lord Culvers]] met with the Prefect at his office in Paris. Although the Prefect preferred to speak in his own tongue, he possessed a perfect command of colloquial English, so no interpreter was needed. (014-006)

During the interview, Clive and Lord Culvers related the full story: [[ida-wedding|Ida's marriage]] to [[sefton-culvers|Captain Sefton Culvers]], her subsequent disappearance after [[ida-wedding|the wedding]], and the history of her engagement. They also presented the damaged brooch for the Prefect's inspection. (014-005)(014-011)

The Prefect declined to offer any immediate opinion on Ida's disappearance, stating that he could not form one until he had given the case his most careful consideration in all its aspects. (014-008)

## Key Observations

The Prefect questioned Clive and Lord Culvers closely on several points:

- **[[sefton-culvers|Captain Culvers]]'s resignation**: He asked whether any reason other than enfeebled health could account for Captain Culvers having resigned his commission. (014-014)
- **The advertisement**: He requested and took down in writing the text of the advertisement offering a reward for information [[About|about]] the brooch. (014-017)

## Assessment and Recommendations

The Prefect advised caution, warning that the disappearance of the lady and the finding of the brooch should be treated as distinct matters, to be "classified and treated as things apart." He cautioned against allowing "side issues" to become entangled with the main facts of the case. (014-019)

He was not prepared to rule out the possibility that Captain Culvers's wife had "fallen into bad hands" and been robbed — or worse. (014-019)

In conclusion, the Prefect requested permission to communicate with the English police, so that "the highest professional skill in both countries" could be brought to bear on what he regarded as a matter "beginning to assume a most serious aspect." (014-020)

## The Detective

Following the interview, the Prefect assigned a detective to attend at the café where Clive Redway was to meet a person claiming knowledge of the brooch's whereabouts. (014-044)

## Later Involvement

As the situation developed, those involved in the search for Ida concluded that "the Prefect of Police, not a doubt, must be told of the situation." (018-035)
