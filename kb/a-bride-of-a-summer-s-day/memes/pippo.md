# Pippo

Pippo is the youngest son of [[francesca-xardez|Francesca Xardez]] and foster-brother to [[violante|Violante da Nava]]. Originally "Pippo the little goatherd" in the mountains of Alta Lauria, he is brought to England by his family as part of their pursuit of [[sefton-culvers|Captain Sefton Culvers]]. (018-009)(019-014)

## Role in the Plot

Francesca used the family's pooled resources to buy Pippo a barrel-organ, with which it was hoped he could earn money as a street musician in England. Pippo and his organ became useful to Francesca's schemes, as he could loiter in public places without attracting suspicion — allowing him to watch Captain Culvers's movements and deliver messages. (019-038)(019-040)

During the critical moment when Francesca's party tracked the Culvers family to Hastings, Pippo touched his mother's shoulder to alert her that Sefton had been spotted. He was then despatched to verify the captain's whereabouts and later carried Ida's hurried note from [[saint-mary-star-of-the-sea|Saint Mary, Star of the Sea]] church to [[glynde-lodge|Glynde Lodge]] for delivery to Lord George Culvers. (019-043)(019-054)(019-055)

After the action moved to the Continent, Pippo and his brother [[giorno|Giorno]] remained in Paris, keeping a watchful eye on Captain Culvers while Ida travelled onward toward Italy to find the dying Violante. (020-024)


> Then it was that Pippo and his organ had become useful. Francesca had but to indicate to him that information was wanted as to the whereabouts of certain persons, and Pippo had but to go forth with his organ and his monkey through the streets where those persons were likely to be found.
>
> (019-040)

## Family

Pippo is the younger brother of Giorno the vinedresser and the son of Francesca Xardez. Through his mother's foster-care of Violante da Nava, he is connected to the [[vendetta]] narrative that drives the novel's climax in southern Italy. (018-009)(019-014)
