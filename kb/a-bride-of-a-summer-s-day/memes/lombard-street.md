# Lombard Street

Lombard Street is the address of the Redway family firm in the City of London in *A [[ida-culvers|Bride]] of a Summer's Day*. It is the setting for the pivotal consultation between [[clive-redway|Clive Redway]] and his father [[joshua-redway|Joshua Redway]] about [[ida-culvers|Ida Culvers]]'s disappearance.

## Location Type

Lombard Street is a real thoroughfare in the City of London, historically the centre of British banking and finance. In the novel, the Redway offices there represent the world of commerce, clear-headed analysis, and financial power that stands in contrast to the aristocratic Culvers family's emotional turbulence.

## Description

> They were seated in a private room, opening off the offices of the firm in Lombard Street; a room in which big loans had been negotiated, and contracts of worldwide importance had been signed and sealed; a room, too, to which others beside Clive were in the habit of resorting in times of perplexity for wisdom and counsel from the successful financier.
>
> (004-003)

The Redway offices are situated in "a private room, opening off the offices of the firm in Lombard Street; a room in which big loans had been negotiated, and contracts of worldwide importance had been signed and sealed; a room, too, to which others beside Clive were in the habit of resorting in times of perplexity for wisdom and counsel from the successful financier" (004-015).

This description establishes Lombard Street as a seat of competence and worldly wisdom — a place where problems are analysed rather than merely agonised over. It is here that [[joshua-redway|Joshua Redway]] brings his "great reputation for shrewdness and clear-headedness" (004-016) to bear on the mystery of [[ida-culvers|Ida]]'s disappearance.
