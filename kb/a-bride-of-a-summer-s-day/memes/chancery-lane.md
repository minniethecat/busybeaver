# Chancery Lane

Chancery Lane is a street in [[london|London]], appearing in *A Bride of a Summer's Day* as the professional address of Messrs. [[hunt-and-locke|Hunt and Locke]], solicitors.

## Role in the Novel

The firm first appears in Chapter V when the family places a newspaper advertisement seeking information about the [[violante-death|missing diamond brooch]]: "Information to be given to Messrs. Hunt and Locke, Chancery Lane, London" (005-004). In Chapter X, [[clive-redway|Clive Redway]] receives a letter "from Messrs. Hunt and Locke, of Chancery Lane" containing a report from Father [[father-baldwin|Baldwin]] of the [[carmelite-friars|Carmelite Friars]] in Paris about the brooch's provenance (010-003). Hunt and Locke thus serve as the professional intermediary through which information about the brooch investigation flows between England and France, connecting the novel's [[london|London]] and [[paris|Paris]] settings.
