# All Saints Church, Hastings

All Saints Church is the church in [[hastings|Hastings]] where [[ida-culvers|Ida Culvers]] chose to be married to [[sefton-culvers|Sefton Culvers]] in *A [[ida-culvers|Bride]] of a Summer's Day*. Its bells open the novel and its churchyard holds the grave of [[ida-culvers|Ida]]'s mother, making it the novel's founding site of memory, obligation, and disrupted ritual.

## Location Type

All Saints is a fictional parish church in the seaside [[london|town]] of [[hastings|Hastings]], Sussex. Its tower, bells, and churchyard—reached by "a flight of steep steps" from the pavement (002-002)—are precisely mapped in the novel's opening pages, establishing a spatial intimacy that grounds the drama to come.

## Description

The novel's first sentence is the crash of bells from All Saints tower (001-001), an auditory signature that anchors the entire opening chapter.

The opening of the novel establishes All Saints as the novel's sonic landmark:

> Crash went the bells from All Saints tower; "One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight," jangled the bells of old Saint Clement's in response; and then All Saints fired off its volley again.
>
> (001-001)

The churchyard, where [[ida-culvers|Ida]]'s mother "lies buried" (001-011), is the emotional heart of the location: [[ida-culvers|Ida]]'s choice of [[hastings|Hastings]] for her wedding is driven entirely by the desire to be near her mother's grave.

Before the ceremony, she instructs the coachman to "drive first to All Saints churchyard—I want to lay these flowers on mamma's grave" (001-018), a gesture of filial piety that is among the last things she does before vanishing.

The churchyard is also the scene of the discovery that [[ida-culvers|Ida]] is missing—[[ida-wedding|the wedding]] guests search among the graves for a [[ida-culvers|bride]] who has disappeared between the church and the grave.

## Key Events

- **The bells**: The novel opens: "Crash went the bells from All Saints tower" (001-001)
- **[[ida-culvers|Ida]]'s visit to her mother's grave**: Before [[ida-wedding|the wedding]], [[ida-culvers|Ida]] takes flowers to the churchyard (001-018)
- **[[ida-wedding|The wedding]] that wasn't**: The ceremony is set at All Saints but never takes place
- **The search**: The churchyard becomes the site of the initial search for the missing [[ida-culvers|bride]]

## Narrative Function

All Saints Church is the novel's symbolic threshold—the place where the expected narrative (marriage, social ritual, the joining of families) is ruptured and an entirely different story begins. Its bells mark time, its churchyard holds the dead, and its altar stands empty. The church is both a monument to what should have happened and a portal to everything that happens instead.
