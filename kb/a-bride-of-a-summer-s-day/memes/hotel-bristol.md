# Hôtel Bristol

The Hôtel Bristol is a Paris hotel in *A [[ida-culvers|Bride]] of a Summer's Day* that serves as the base of operations for the English party during the Paris investigation.

## Role in the Story

The Hôtel Bristol is first mentioned when [[clive-redway|Clive Redway]] writes to [[juliet-culvers|Juliet Culvers]] from his continental journey, saying that "mindful of a certain suggestion of hers, he intended to break his return journey at Paris, and would be glad if she would send to him at the Hôtel Bristol the present address of her cousin, Captain Culvers" (009-002).

The letter's postscript locates the hotel at the intersection of romance and investigation:

> There was added a postscript to the letter, in which Clive said that, mindful of a certain suggestion of hers, he intended to break his return journey at Paris, and would be glad if she would send to him at the Hôtel Bristol the present address of her cousin, Captain Culvers.
>
> (008-007)

It subsequently becomes the headquarters of the English investigation party: [[lord-george-culvers|Lord Culvers]] stays there upon his arrival in Paris, and it is at the Hôtel Bristol that [[john-skinner|John Skinner]] presents himself to offer his information about [[sefton-culvers|Sefton Culvers]]'s schemes (017-008).

## Function

The Hôtel Bristol represents English respectability transplanted to Paris — a neutral, orderly space from which the investigation into [[sefton-culvers|Sefton Culvers]]'s chaotic Parisian life can be conducted. It is the safe base to which the English party retreats after confronting the morally compromised world of the [[rue-vervien|Rue Vervien]].
