# Clive Redway's Search for Ida

Clive Redway's search for [[ida-culvers|Ida Culvers]] in Paris is the investigative engine of the second half of *A [[ida-culvers|Bride]] of a Summer's Day*. Working from fragmentary clues — a recovered brooch, a suspicious address, a dying woman's summons — [[clive-redway|Clive]] pieces together the truth of [[sefton-culvers|Sefton Culvers]]'s scheme.

## Background

[[clive-redway|Clive Redway]] has been searching for [[ida-culvers|Ida]] since her disappearance from [[all-saints-hastings|All Saints Church]]. His pursuit has taken him across Europe, and his return journey brings him to [[paris|Paris]], where he stays at the [[hotel-bristol|Hôtel Bristol]] (009-002). The discovery of [[ida-culvers|Ida]]'s diamond brooch at the [[carmelite-friars|Carmelite Friars]] church redirects the search to the French capital.

Lord Culvers comes to recognise that Clive's investigative zeal may have a personal motive:

> It was beginning to dawn upon him that Clive was throwing, not too much energy, but energy of not quite the right kind into his search for Ida.
>
> (008-007)

## Key Developments

### The Rue Vervien Stakeout

Learning that [[sefton-culvers|Sefton Culvers]] lodges at No. 15 [[rue-vervien|Rue Vervien]] — a house kept by the Russian [[ivanoff|Ivanoff]] and known as "the resort of all sorts of shady people" (012-015) — [[clive-redway|Clive]] stakes out the street. He observes an Italian [[organ-boy|organ-boy]] with a monkey grinding a melody outside No. 15 (013-022) — the same figure who had delivered [[ida-culvers|Ida]]'s note at [[glynde-lodge|Glynde Lodge]]. The coincidence reinforces his conviction that [[sefton-culvers|Sefton]] is at the centre of the mystery.

### The Rue Corot Summons

While waiting for [[lord-george-culvers|Lord Culvers]]'s arrival, [[clive-redway|Clive]] receives a message that "a poor penitent, lying at the point of death at No. 11, Rue Corot, has a story to tell that may interest Lord Culvers" (015-012). He goes to the [[rue-corot|Rue Corot]] and finds [[marie-schira|Marie Schira]], a Parisian actress dying of burns, who possesses knowledge of [[sefton-culvers|Sefton]]'s plans.

### The Skinner Revelations

Although [[marie-schira|Marie Schira]] dies before she can speak, her brother [[john-skinner|John Skinner]] — motivated by financial reward — reveals what she knew: the full scope of [[sefton-culvers|Sefton Culvers]]'s scheme to claim [[ida-culvers|Ida]]'s fortune through the clause of reversion in the [[marriage-settlement|marriage settlement]] (017-033).

## Significance

[[clive-redway|Clive]]'s search transforms him from a frustrated lover into an active agent of justice. His methodical pursuit of clues — from the [[rue-vervien|Rue Vervien]] to the [[rue-corot|Rue Corot]], from the [[organ-boy|organ-boy]] to [[john-skinner|John Skinner]] — provides the novel's detective plot with its momentum and ultimately leads to the exposure of [[sefton-culvers|Sefton]]'s crimes.
