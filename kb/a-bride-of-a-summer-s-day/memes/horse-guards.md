# Horse Guards

Horse Guards is the British Army's administrative headquarters in [[london|London]], referenced in *A Bride of a Summer's Day* as the office where [[sefton-culvers|Sefton Culvers]] formally resigned his military commission.

The resignation is mentioned in Chapter I: "he sent in his papers to the Horse Guards." (001-061) The event is recalled again in Chapter XIV, where it is noted that Sefton "had considered it advisable to send in his papers to the Horse Guards" before leaving England. (014-013)

## Key Quotations

> "It has never been clearly explained why he sent in his papers to the Horse Guards. Your father knew of his debts, no doubt. All the world knew of them; but debts, though bad enough, are scarcely enough to condemn a man utterly."
>
> (001-061)
