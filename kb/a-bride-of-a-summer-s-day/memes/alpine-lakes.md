# Alpine Lakes

The Alpine lakes and mountains are mentioned in *A Bride of a Summer's Day* as the planned honeymoon destination for [[ida-culvers|Ida]] and [[sefton-culvers|Sefton Culvers]].

In Chapter VIII, after the [[summer-wedding|wedding]], the narrative notes that the couple anticipated "their felicity among the Alpine lakes and mountains." (008-004) This plan, however, would soon be disrupted by the events that follow.

In Chapter XIV, a minor character — an acquaintance of Clive's who is "a member of the Alpine Club" — is mentioned as being "en route for the Swiss frontier." (014-049)

## Key Quotations

> Wild and unreasonable as Juliet's proposal had at first seemed to him, he was at heart uncommonly glad to get away from the embarrassing enquiries of a large circle of friends as to the bride and bridegroom, who were supposed to be at that moment at the height of their felicity among the Alpine lakes and mountains.
>
> (008-004)

> This chance acquaintance was a member of the Alpine Club, en route for the Swiss mountains, and was eager to detail to Clive a new line of road that he had mapped out for himself.
>
> (014-049)
