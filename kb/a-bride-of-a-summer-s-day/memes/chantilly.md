# Chantilly

Chantilly is a town in northern France, famous for its racecourse, referenced in *A Bride of a Summer's Day* in the backstory of [[marie-schira|Marie Schira]].

In Chapter XV, John Skinner recounts how Captain [[sefton-culvers|Sefton Culvers]] first encountered the young Englishwoman Mary Skinner (later Marie Schira) "on the racecourse at Chantilly" and bestowed upon her the French version of her first name. (015-059)

## Key Quotations

> John, in his somewhat vagrant career, had lighted upon her on the racecourse at Chantilly with her first name Frenchified, and the family patronymic of Skinner Italianised into Schira.
>
> (015-059)
