# Saint Mary, Star of the Sea

The Church of Saint Mary, Star of the Sea, is a Catholic church in [[hastings|Hastings]] that serves as the staging point for [[ida-culvers|Ida Culvers]]'s escape after [[ida-wedding|her wedding]] in *A [[ida-culvers|Bride]] of a Summer's Day*. It is here — not at [[all-saints-hastings|All Saints]] churchyard nor at [[glynde-lodge|Glynde Lodge]] — that the escape plan transitions from flight to communication.

## Location and Setting

The church is reached "along by-streets" from the back entrance of [[all-saints-hastings|All Saints]] churchyard (019-054). Its location, off the main thoroughfare where [[sefton-culvers|Sefton Culvers]] waited in the carriage, made it a discreet rendezvous point — close enough to reach on foot, distant enough to escape detection. The church is likely a fictionalised version of the real Our Lady Star of the Sea Catholic church in Hastings (St. Mary Star of the Sea).

## Role in the Escape

> Ida and Francesca, leaving the churchyard by its back entrance, had made their way along by-streets to the church of "Saint Mary, Star of the Sea," whither Pippo had directions to follow them.
>
> (019-054)

After slipping away from [[all-saints-hastings|All Saints]] churchyard through its back entrance while the wedding guests searched for the missing bride, [[ida-culvers|Ida]] and [[francesca-xardez|Francesca Xardez]] made their way to Saint Mary, Star of the Sea. [[pippo|Pippo]], [[francesca-xardez|Francesca]]'s young brother, was despatched to verify that [[sefton-culvers|Sefton Culvers]] still waited in the street below, then followed them to the church with instructions (019-054).

It was inside this church that [[ida-culvers|Ida]] "tearing a leaf from her notebook, had written the hurried line to her father which Pippo had carried to [[glynde-lodge|Glynde Lodge]]" (019-055). This note — the only communication from [[ida-culvers|Ida]] on her wedding day — was the catalyst that transformed the wedding celebration into a crisis of disappearance.

From Saint Mary, Star of the Sea, the escape party planned the next stage: [[ida-culvers|Ida]] would travel alone from [[hastings|Hastings]] station (not [[ore|Saint Leonard's]]) on an excursion train to London, then onward to Italy.

## Narrative Function

Saint Mary, Star of the Sea, occupies a pivotal structural position in the novel's opening movement. It is the liminal space between the public ceremony of [[all-saints-hastings|All Saints]] and the private journey that follows — a Catholic church in a predominantly Anglican narrative, a sanctuary that enables not worship but flight. The name itself, "Star of the Sea" (*Stella Maris*), is an ancient Marian title invoking guidance and protection for travellers, making it an apt backdrop for the moment [[ida-culvers|Ida]] commits herself to an unknown journey.

## Significance

Together with [[all-saints-hastings|All Saints]], [[glynde-lodge|Glynde Lodge]], and [[hastings|Hastings]] station, Saint Mary Star of the Sea completes the geography of Hastings in the novel's opening chapters — a mapped route that traces [[ida-culvers|Ida]]'s passage from bride to fugitive in the space of a single afternoon.
