## Character

Joshua Redway was the present representative of the Redway family, who in late years had made their mark as financiers and bankers. He was a man of great reputation in the commercial world.

The setting of the Redway firm's inner sanctum conveys the financier's authority:

> They were seated in a private room, opening off the offices of the firm in Lombard Street; a room in which big loans had been negotiated, and contracts of worldwide importance had been signed and sealed; a room, too, to which others beside Clive were in the habit of resorting in times of perplexity for wisdom and counsel from the successful financier.
>
> (004-002) (001-030)(004-004)

## The Redway Empire

The Redway family's banking and mercantile operations spanned [[london|London]], Liverpool, and New York, making them one of the wealthy "merchant-prince" families of the era. (001-031)

## Role in the Investigation

When [[clive-redway|Clive Redway]], his son, became concerned [[About|about]] the disappearance of [[ida-culvers|Ida Culvers]], he consulted with Joshua Redway. Following this consultation, an advertisement offering a large reward for information about Ida's missing diamond brooch was published in the London newspapers and the principal Continental papers. (005-001)(005-006)

Joshua Redway's wealth and business connections provided the resources necessary to conduct a far-reaching investigation into Ida's disappearance, complementing the efforts of the [[paris|Paris]] Prefect of Police]] and the firm of [[hunt-and-locke|Messrs. Hunt and Locke]].
