# Théâtre Beaumarchais

The Théâtre Beaumarchais is a Paris theatre in *A [[ida-culvers|Bride]] of a Summer's Day* where [[marie-schira|Marie Schira]] performed as an actress of "low comedy parts" and where she sustained the burns that led to her death.

## Location Type

The Théâtre Beaumarchais is described as an "inferior theatre" (017-005) in the unfashionable quarter of the Porte Saint Martin, in close vicinity to the [[rue-corot|Rue Corot]]. Its name evokes Pierre Beaumarchais, the eighteenth-century French playwright — a connection that lends a certain ironic grandeur to its shabby reality.

## Description

The theatre is the scene of the accident that sets the novel's final revelations in motion. [[marie-schira|Marie Schira]], "while dressing in her tiny dressing-room behind the scenes at the Théâtre Beaumarchais, had set her gauze sleeve on fire with the candles on her table, and, before assistance could be procured, had been so severely burned that her life was despaired of" (015-017). The accident happened three nights before [[clive-redway|Clive Redway]]'s visit to the Rue Corot.

The theatre represents the precariousness of the performer's life — the "gauze sleeve" and "candles on her table" suggesting a world of cheap materials and dangerous proximity to flame. The irony is theatrical: a woman who made her living playing comic roles dies in her dressing-room, her costume becoming her shroud.


> The Rue Corot lies in the unfashionable quarter of the Porte Saint Martin, in close vicinity to the Théâtre Beaumarchais. It is a narrow and somewhat noisy thoroughfare of tall seven-storied houses that are let and sublet to all sorts and conditions of men.
>
> (015-013)
