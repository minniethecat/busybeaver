# Palais de Justice

The Palais de Justice is the Paris law courts in *A [[ida-culvers|Bride]] of a Summer's Day*, where [[lord-george-culvers|Lord Culvers]] and [[clive-redway|Clive Redway]] formally report [[ida-culvers|Ida Culvers]]'s disappearance to the [[prefect-of-police|Prefect of Police]].

## Location Type

The Palais de Justice is a real building on the Île de la Cité in Paris, housing the city's principal law courts and, in the nineteenth century, the offices of the Paris Prefect of Police. Its imposing architecture and the gravity of its function make it the novel's symbolic seat of official authority.

## Description

[[lord-george-culvers|Lord Culvers]] orders the coachman "to drive at once to the Palais de Justice" after identifying [[ida-culvers|Ida]]'s brooch at the [[rue-bellarmine|Rue Bellarmine]] (014-012). The transition from the priest's quiet residence to the bustling law courts marks the formalisation of the search: what had been a family's private anxiety now becomes a matter of police record.

At the Palais de Justice, [[lord-george-culvers|Lord Culvers]]'s "narrative, in all its minute detail" is "taken down in writing by an official" (014-016). The [[prefect-of-police|Prefect of Police]] questions him about [[sefton-culvers|Sefton Culvers]]'s career, his resignation from the Army, and the circumstances of the brooch's discovery.


> "I suppose," Clive said, savagely, "you feel bound to look up Culvers, otherwise I should say don't lose a minute in going to the house of this priest, see the brooch, and drive straight away to the Palais de Justice."
>
> (013-011)
