## Background

> As for Violante, she was simply the darling and the idol of these rude mountaineers. Ill-trained, and all but uneducated, she had grown up among them half-princess, half-peasant, related on her father's side to some of the noblest houses in Italy, and owning on her mother's side to near relatives among the poorest and most debased of the vinedressers and shepherds of Alta Lauria.
>
> (018-009)

[[violante|Violante]] was the nurse-child of [[francesca-xardez|Francesca Xardez]], who had cared for her since infancy. Her mother had died many years before, leaving her father with this one child. She was described as "simply the darling and plaything" of [[alta-lauria|Alta Lauria]], the remote mountain hamlet in Calabria. (018-006)(018-007)(018-009)

When [[sefton-culvers|Captain Sefton Culvers]] came to stay at Alta Lauria during his travels through Italy, Violante — "young, beautiful, half-educated, and penniless" — attracted his attention. Before his departure, he made her an offer of marriage, promising to return within a year. (018-012)(018-013)

## The Abandonment

A year passed, and Violante's friends — meaning Sefton and his family — made no sign. Sefton, who had since married [[ida-culvers|Ida Culvers]] in England, wrote a letter to Violante confessing his hasty marriage and offering her a "solatium" — a financial settlement in lieu of the promised marriage. [[francesca-xardez|Francesca Xardez]], Violante's devoted protector, intercepted the letter and destroyed it before Violante could see it, hoping to spare her the pain. (018-016)

## The Death

When [[ida-culvers|Ida Culvers]] — who had herself fled her marriage to Sefton — arrived at Alta Lauria seeking refuge, she found that Violante was already dying. A doctor hastily summoned from Cosenza pronounced that she could not live more than a week. (020-020)

Violante had been overwhelmed with grief upon learning the details of Sefton's marriage and betrayal. She had given Ida every penny she possessed to fund Ida's journey, though she herself was gravely ill. (019-036)(019-038)

## Aftermath

Ida carefully sealed a letter to Sefton with Violante's ring, to render it the more impressive, and sent it through the post to [[paris|Paris]], where associates of the group kept watch on Sefton's movements. (020-024)

Violante's death represented the final human cost of [[sefton-culvers|Sefton Culvers]]'s irresponsibility: his false promise of marriage to a vulnerable young woman, his abandonment of her for an advantageous English match, and the fatal consequence of his betrayal.
