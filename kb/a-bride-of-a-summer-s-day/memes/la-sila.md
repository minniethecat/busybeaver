# La Sila

La Sila is the mountainous region of Calabria in southern Italy that forms the dramatic landscape of the novel's climactic chapters in *A [[ida-culvers|Bride]] of a Summer's Day*. Its remote pasture tablelands, towering crags, and treacherous passes provide the setting for the mountain journey that brings the narrative toward its resolution.

## Location Type

La Sila is a real mountain plateau in Calabria, southern Italy—a region of forests, pasture tablelands, and rugged peaks. In the novel, it is presented as a sublime and dangerous landscape, far removed from the cultivated English countryside of [[dering-hall|Dering Hall]] and [[devon|Devon]].

## Description

The novel renders La Sila in language that shifts between the sublime and the threatening. Its ridges "towered" in front of travellers, "crag over crag, spire over spire" (022-020), while its "pasture tablelands" in summer serve as "the summer pasture-ground of countless flocks" (023-002). The region is accessed not by rail but by mule-track, guided by men like [[ditta-capelli|Ditta Capelli]] and his brother Andrea, "the two best mule-men the district could supply" (022-013).

La Sila is also the location of [[alta-lauria|Alta Lauria]], the mountain hamlet where [[sefton-culvers|Sefton Culvers]] took refuge and where his daughter [[violante|Violante]] was raised. The mountain landscape thus encompasses both the novel's most dramatic physical journey and its deepest moral reckonings.


> The inn stood on rocks a little above the small cluster of houses dignified by the title of village. Below these the valley lay in depths of purple gloom; straight in front towered the ridges of La Sila, crag over crag, spire over spire, till they lost themselves in the clouds.
>
> (022-020)

## Narrative Function

La Sila represents the novel's geographical and symbolic extreme—the furthest point from [[dering-hall|Dering Hall]] in every sense. Its crags and tablelands are the terrain of truth, where the evasions and performances of English society cannot survive. [[mountain-journey-through-la-sila|The mountain journey through La Sila]] is both a physical ordeal and a moral pilgrimage: to reach [[alta-lauria|Alta Lauria]], one must first cross the mountains that guard it.
