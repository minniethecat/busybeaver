# Ore

Ore is a village near [[hastings|Hastings]], Sussex, mentioned in the opening chapters of *A [[ida-culvers|Bride]] of a Summer's Day* as a geographic waypoint locating [[glynde-lodge|Glynde Lodge]].

## Location Type

Ore is a real village in East Sussex, situated on the road between [[hastings|Hastings]] and the inland country. In the novel, [[glynde-lodge|Glynde Lodge]] is described as standing "in a few acres of land abutting on the high-road between Ore and Hastings" (001-010). This precise geographic placement — between the seaside town and the inland village — situates the wedding events in a recognisably real English landscape.

## Description

Ore serves a purely geographic function in the novel, anchoring [[glynde-lodge|Glynde Lodge]] in the actual topography of Sussex. The detail that the Lodge stands on the road "between Ore and Hastings" creates a liminal quality: the wedding party is neither in the town nor in the country, but in a transitional zone that mirrors the transitional nature of the wedding day itself — a threshold between one state and another that is never crossed.

The road between Ore and Hastings is also where [[clive-redway|Clive Redway]] tramps in his unsuccessful search for the Italian [[organ-boy|organ-boy]] who delivered [[ida-culvers|Ida]]'s note (003-017).
