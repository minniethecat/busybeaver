# Giorno

Giorno is the eldest son of [[francesca-xardez|Francesca Xardez]] and foster-brother to [[violante|Violante da Nava]]. A vinedresser by trade in the mountains of Alta Lauria, he accompanied his mother and younger brother [[pippo|Pippo]] to England as part of the family's mission to confront [[sefton-culvers|Captain Sefton Culvers]]. (018-009)(019-014)

## The Vendetta Threat

Giorno's passage to England was paid for by his fellow vinedressers, pooling their resources to send one of their own across the sea. During the family's low-voiced discussions in England, it was Giorno's deep bass voice that delivered the vendetta threat against Sefton Culvers: "let him look out — that's all." (019-038)(019-042)(019-043)

His presence embodied the Calabrian code of honour that Francesca's party brought with them — a sense of justice that existed outside English law and threatened Sefton with violent retribution if Violante were to die. (019-043)

## Surveillance in Paris

After the pursuit moved from England to the Continent, Giorno and Pippo remained in Paris, keeping a watchful eye on Captain Culvers's movements while Francesca and [[ida-culvers|Ida Culvers]] travelled onward toward Italy. Information was transmitted through the post to Giorno during this surveillance. (020-024)


> Giorno was saying at the moment that Clive entered, "Let him look out—that's all," as Francesca Xardez and her two sons sat in low-voiced talk in their cottage on the mountain-side.
>
> (019-043)

## Family

Giorno is the elder brother of Pippo the goatherd and the son of Francesca Xardez. Though he and his brother share the same mission, Giorno's role is the more menacing — where Pippo acts as messenger and spy, Giorno represents the physical threat of vendetta. (018-009)(019-014)
