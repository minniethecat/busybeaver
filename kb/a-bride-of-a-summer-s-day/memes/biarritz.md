# Biarritz

Biarritz is a fashionable seaside resort on the southwestern coast of France, referenced in *A Bride of a Summer's Day* in connection with [[ida-culvers|Ida Culvers]]'s past.

In Chapter XVII, [[juliet-culvers|Juliet Culvers]] recalls:

> When Ida went off to Biarritz two years ago, we agreed on a certain cipher, so that we might correspond without anyone being the wiser.
>
> (017-005)

This secret correspondence between the twins becomes significant later in the novel's plot — the cipher system established during this visit enables the sisters to maintain clandestine communication throughout the story, contributing to the novel's themes of [[twin-bond|twin intimacy]] and [[disguise-and-deception|disguise]].

## Significance in the Novel

Biarritz serves as the origin point for the secret cipher correspondence between the twin sisters. The visit occurs two years before the novel's main events, establishing the pre-existing pact of secrecy that allows Ida and Juliet to communicate without detection during the central crisis of the plot (017-005). As a fashionable continental resort beyond the reach of English social surveillance, Biarritz represents a space of freedom and confidentiality for the sisters — a contrast to the constrained social world of [[dering-hall|Dering Hall]] and [[london|London]].
