## Location

Glynde Lodge stood on the high-road between Ore and Hastings, on the Sussex coast. (001-033)

## Role in the Wedding

The house was lent to [[lord-george-culvers|Lord Culvers]] for the celebrations surrounding the marriage of his daughter [[ida-culvers|Ida Culvers]] to [[sefton-culvers|Captain Sefton Culvers]]. It was at the garden gates of Glynde Lodge that [[juliet-culvers|Juliet Culvers]] said goodbye to her lover, [[clive-redway|Clive Redway]], on the morning of the wedding.

The narrative establishes Glynde Lodge with precise economy:

> Glynde Lodge, the house that had been lent to Lord Culvers for the wedding, was small and unpretending, and stood in a few acres of land abutting on the high-road between Ore and Hastings.
>
> (001-033) (001-033)(002-020)

## The Glynde Family

The Lodge was the home of Mrs. Glynde and her son [[arthur-glynde|Arthur Glynde]], a young man of poetic tendencies who was a general favourite in the neighbourhood. Mrs. Glynde was known for her lively and somewhat excitable temperament. (007-012)(009-015)(009-041)

## Servants

Mrs. Glynde's servants remained at the Lodge during the wedding preparations, attending to the needs of the [[culvers-family|Culvers family]] and their guests. (002-042)
