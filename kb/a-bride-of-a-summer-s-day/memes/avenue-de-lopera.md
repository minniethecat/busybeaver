# Avenue de l'Opéra

The Avenue de l'Opéra is a Parisian avenue in *A [[ida-culvers|Bride]] of a Summer's Day*, serving as the geographic waypoint from which [[clive-redway|Clive Redway]] navigates into the hidden streets of the Parisian demimonde.

## Role in the Story

Seeking the [[rue-vervien|Rue Vervien]], [[clive-redway|Clive Redway]] "was directed down the Avenue de l'Opéra, and thence into a narrow street lined on either side with cafés and restaurants. Off this street at right angles led the Rue Vervien" (013-017).

## Function

The Avenue de l'Opéra is the novel's threshold between public and hidden Paris. One of Haussmann's great boulevards — wide, straight, monumental — it represents the official city of spectacle and order. From it, [[clive-redway|Clive]] turns into increasingly narrow streets, each turn taking him deeper into the private Paris of the [[rue-vervien|Rue Vervien]] and [[ivanoff|Ivanoff]]'s boarding-house. The geographic descent mirrors the moral descent: from the open Avenue to the "quiet, old-fashioned thoroughfare" (013-018) where crimes are planned in shadow.
