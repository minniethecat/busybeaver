# Stacy

Stacy was Juliet Culvers's maid for many years before the events of the novel. Though she never appears on-stage, she serves as Juliet's personal benchmark of loyalty and trustworthiness. (007-053)

## Juliet's Loyalty Standard

When Juliet enlists Arthur Glynde to place a secret advertisement in the newspapers, she compares him to Stacy — explaining that he must be as discreet and devoted as her former maid. Juliet tells him that in his goddess's eyes, he "only stood on a level with Stacy, the maid." (007-054)(007-069)

Juliet's memorable description of her former maid captures the quality she prizes above all others:

> "Stacy was true as steel, and" — this with a little amused, upward glance — "pliable as whalebone."
>
> (007-070)
