# Clive and Juliet's Engagement

The engagement between [[clive-redway|Clive Redway]] and [[juliet-culvers|Juliet Culvers]] is the romantic framework of *A [[ida-culvers|Bride]] of a Summer's Day*. Announced before the novel's opening, it is tested by the crisis of [[ida-culvers|Ida Culvers]]'s disappearance and complicated by the competing claims of [[arthur-glynde|Arthur Glynde]].

Juliet's irreverent philosophy of courtship captures the comic undertow of the novel's romantic plot:

> "I should adore, positively adore, Clive — not just like him, as I do now — if only everyone in the house would run him down, and tell me I'm throwing myself away on him!"
>
> (002-020)

## Background

[[clive-redway|Clive Redway]] and [[juliet-culvers|Juliet]] are engaged at the novel's opening. [[clive-redway|Clive]] has just returned from "six months' absence at the Cape" (002-004) and arrives at [[glynde-lodge|Glynde Lodge]] on [[ida-wedding|Ida's wedding day]]. His reunion with [[juliet-culvers|Juliet]] is immediately overshadowed by [[ida-culvers|Ida]]'s disappearance.

## Development

The engagement is strained by [[juliet-culvers|Juliet]]'s spirited independence and [[clive-redway|Clive]]'s absorption in the search for [[ida-culvers|Ida]]. [[juliet-culvers|Juliet]] chafes at the passive role expected of an engaged woman, while [[clive-redway|Clive]]'s single-minded pursuit of the truth about [[sefton-culvers|Sefton Culvers]] leaves little emotional energy for courtship.

The engagement is also shadowed by [[arthur-glynde|Arthur Glynde]]'s quiet devotion to [[juliet-culvers|Juliet]] and [[mrs-glynde|Mrs. Glynde]]'s determined matchmaking on her son's behalf. [[mrs-glynde|Mrs. Glynde]] explicitly probes [[juliet-culvers|Juliet]] about the duration of her engagement: "Till when do you mean to be engaged to Clive Redway? Can you fix a date for the ending of your engagement?" (007-031).

## Resolution

The crisis of [[ida-culvers|Ida]]'s disappearance ultimately strengthens the engagement. [[clive-redway|Clive]]'s loyalty to the Culvers family, his determination in the Paris investigation, and his eventual success in uncovering [[sefton-culvers|Sefton]]'s scheme demonstrate the steadfastness that [[juliet-culvers|Juliet]] values. The engagement survives its trials, and the novel closes with [[juliet-culvers|Juliet]] being urged to "fix your wedding-day" (024-080) — now with [[arthur-glynde|Arthur Glynde]] rather than [[clive-redway|Clive]], in a late reversal that rewards patient devotion.
