# Devon

Devon is the English county where [[ida-culvers|Ida Culvers]] spent two years of secluded widowhood, living with [[sefton-culvers|Sefton Culvers]]'s aged mother after his death in *A [[ida-culvers|Bride]] of a Summer's Day*. It represents the phase of [[ida-culvers|Ida]]'s life between the catastrophe of her marriage and [[sefton-culvers-death|the resolution]] of the novel's close.

The narrative frames Ida's Devonshire years as an act of filial expiation:

> And the whole of those two years, with but brief interludes, Ida had passed in Devon with Sefton's aged mother, ministering to her as a daughter might, and doing her utmost to render her closing days days of peace, if not of happiness.
>
> (024-003)

## Location Type

Devon is a real county in southwest England, characterised in the Victorian period by its rural landscapes, coastal villages, and distance from [[london|London]] society. In the novel, it serves as a place of withdrawal—a deliberate retreat from the world that knew [[ida-culvers|Ida]] as a [[ida-culvers|bride]] and a scandal.

## Description

The novel's treatment of Devon is deliberately sparse, reflecting the obscurity of [[ida-culvers|Ida]]'s life there. She passed her widowhood in Devon "with Sefton's aged mother, minding the poultry and the dairy" (024-003)—a life of rural domesticity that stands in stark contrast to the drama of her wedding day and the passion of her Italian exile. The county is presented as a place of suspension: [[ida-culvers|Ida]] is neither fully hidden nor fully present, existing in a kind of social limbo.

## Key Events

- **Retreat to Devon**: After [[sefton-culvers-death|Sefton's death]], [[ida-culvers|Ida]] goes to Devon to live with his mother (003-068)
- **Two years of widowhood**: [[ida-culvers|Ida]] spends the intervening years in rural seclusion, described as "minding the poultry and the dairy" (024-003)
- **A life of constraint**: The narrative notes that someone "had sent her down into Devon to lead a life from which she shrank with every instinct of her being" (024-007)
- **Final departure**: Sefton's body was taken "straight to his home in Devon" (024-009), and [[ida-culvers|Ida]] eventually leaves

## Narrative Function

Devon functions as a narrative ellipsis—the place where time passes and the novel's central action is suspended. The two years [[ida-culvers|Ida]] spends there are largely un-narrated, a gap that corresponds to her social disappearance. When the novel's final chapters return [[ida-culvers|Ida]] to visibility, Devon is left behind as the site of a life that was never fully lived: a holding pattern between disaster and resolution.
