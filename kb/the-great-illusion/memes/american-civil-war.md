# American Civil War

The 1861–1865 conflict Angell references in discussions of whether war can impose social institutions on a conquered population.

## Background

An event in modern European and world history whose economic dimensions inform Angell's argument about the changing relationship between military force and national prosperity.

## Significance in the Book

Angell references this event as part of his cumulative historical case that military victory does not produce the economic benefits that imperialist ideology assumes. The conditions that made conquest profitable in earlier eras — simple economies, physical wealth — no longer obtain.

Relevant passages anchor this event in the book's argumentative structure (P05-006).
