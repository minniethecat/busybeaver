# Ernest Renan

French philosopher (1823–1892) whose view of war as a necessary condition of civilization Angell groups with other nineteenth-century militarist thinkers.

## Life and Background

A significant figure of the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries whose ideas about war, power, and national destiny engaged directly with the questions Angell addresses in *The Great Illusion*.

## Role in the Book

Angell cites this figure among the intellectual authorities who represent the militarist worldview he aims to refute, or the anti-militarist tradition he aims to strengthen. By engaging with prominent intellectual voices rather than dismissing them, Angell demonstrates that his argument takes its opponents seriously.

## Core Views

The ideas associated with this figure — concerning military power, national destiny, and the conditions of prosperity — represent one pole of the debate that *The Great Illusion* intervenes in. Angell's method is to grant these views their strongest formulation before demonstrating their factual inadequacy.
