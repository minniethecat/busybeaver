# Hague Peace Conferences

Angell's primary example of existing institutional machinery for peaceful settlement. Norman Angell uses this event in *The Great Illusion* as historical evidence for his thesis about the economic futility of military power.

## Background

A significant event in European history whose economic and political consequences inform Angell's argument about the changing conditions under which military force operates.

## Course and Outcome

The event's outcome provides Angell with evidence that military success does not translate into durable economic advantage in the modern world. The apparent victor's gains were substantially offset by the economic costs and disruptions that military action imposed.

## Argumentative Value

Angell uses this event to illustrate a specific dimension of his broader thesis. The key PN citations anchor this event within the book's argumentative structure: references (011-015) and (013-012) connect the historical event to Angell's economic and political analysis.
