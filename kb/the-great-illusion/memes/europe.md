# Europe

The theater of the Great Illusion — the civilization whose interdependence Angell maps. Appears throughout Norman Angell's *The Great Illusion* as both a concrete geographic referent and an exemplar of the economic and political dynamics his argument examines.

## Geographic Context

A key location in the European and global landscape of the early twentieth century that Angell's analysis presupposes and addresses. The economic and political characteristics of this place inform the book's argument about the changing conditions of national power and prosperity.

## Role in the Book

Angell uses this place to illustrate specific dimensions of his thesis — as an example of economic interdependence, a case study in the futility of military power, or a locus of the financial networks that make modern wealth unconquerable.
