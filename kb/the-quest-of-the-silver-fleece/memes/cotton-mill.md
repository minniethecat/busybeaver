# The Cotton Mill

The cotton mill in *The Quest of the Silver Fleece* is the industrial destination of the cotton grown on the [[colonel-cresswell|Cresswell]] plantation. "A thousand cotton-mills in this country" (010-109) form the manufacturing infrastructure that processes Southern raw material into Northern [[economics|wealth]]—and the target of the novel's central financial conspiracy.

## Industrial Scale

Du Bois presents the cotton mill as an instrument of massive economic power. "Half of the" cotton mills (010-109) are [[power|control]]led by a small group of financiers—a concentration that the [[colonel-cresswell|Cresswell]]-[[john-taylor|Taylor]] scheme seeks to exploit. The "selling of stock in various cotton-mills and other industrial enterprises" (006-046) is the mechanism by which Northern investors profit from Southern cotton. The dream of [[power|control]]—"[[power|control]] of the cotton-mills of America" (006-050)—reveals the ambition that drives the novel's antagonists.

## The New Mill

The most dramatic mill moment comes when [[zora|Zora]] "cried joyfully, 'It's the new cotton-mill!'" (035-001). The new mill represents industrial development coming to the South—potentially bringing jobs and economic independence. But the novel is skeptical: who will own this mill, who will [[labor|work]] in it, and who will profit? The new mill is a test case for whether Southern industrialization will liberate or further exploit black labor.

## Economic Chain

The cotton mill is the middle link in the novel's economic chain: field → gin → mill → market → bank. Each link extracts value. The mill transforms raw cotton into cloth, adding the value of manufacturing—but that value flows north, not to the farmers who grew the cotton. The entire [[colonel-cresswell|Cresswell]]-[[john-taylor|Taylor]] scheme—to "corner the cotton market" (010-109) by [[power|control]]ling the mills—is an attempt to capture this manufacturing value for Southern white interests.
