# Sheriff

The sheriff in *The Quest of the Silver Fleece* embodies local law enforcement as a tool of the plantation power structure. Far from being a neutral arbiter of justice, the sheriff serves the interests of landowners like Colonel Cresswell in maintaining control over Black labor.

## Role in the Power Structure

The sheriff appears first as an armed enforcer: "Down the courthouse steps the sheriff, with Winchester on shoulder" (004-089), a visual that equates law enforcement with armed intimidation. The sheriff is not an independent official but works in concert with the economic elite — summoned alongside "the local member of the State Legislature" when collective action is needed against Black workers (026-001).

## The Election and Its Consequences

The sheriff's election is depicted as a corrupt process where "seventy-two votes cast but the sheriff added a cipher," openly manipulating the count without consequence (026-009). When Colton becomes "the new sheriff," he immediately aligns with the planters: Cresswell declares "I'm going to set the sheriff behind these lazy niggers" (035-018). Colton discourages a labor strike and personally enforces labor contracts — "He refuses to work," the sheriff announces, functioning as an extension of Cresswell's authority (035-047, 035-055).

## Related Concepts

The sheriff's office illustrates how law enforcement, the [[plantation]] economy, and racial hierarchy are interwoven in the post-Reconstruction South. The position serves to enforce not abstract law but the specific interests of the landed class.
