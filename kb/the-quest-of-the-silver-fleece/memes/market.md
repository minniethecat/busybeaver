# The Market

The market in *The Quest of the Silver Fleece* operates at two scales: the "peculiar spectacle of the market place" (017-031) in [[toomsville|Toomsville]] and the abstract "cotton market" (006-003) manipulated from Wall Street. Together they form the pricing mechanism that translates black labor into white [[economics|wealth]].

## The Physical Market

The [[toomsville|Toomsville]] market square is a site of social theater. Its "peculiar spectacle" (017-031) was "of unusual interest" (017-031)—the market is where the community gathers to witness the public performance of [[economics|commerce]]. Farmers bring their cotton, merchants bid, and the price is set. But this apparent transparency masks the larger forces at [[labor|work]].

## The Financial Market

The real market operates from [[new-york|New York]]. The novel describes how speculators "successfully manipulated the cotton market" (006-003) so that "black men who made" the cotton received the smallest share of its value. "Cautious fliers on the market" (006-010) describes the speculative dimension—cotton becomes not a crop but a financial instrument. "Cornering the cotton market here means the whip-hand of the" producers (010-110)—market [[power|control]] is coercive power.

## Cotton Pricing

Market prices determine the lives of every character connected to cotton. "Estimated the current market prices according to the Montgomery" reports (036-044) is how farmers learn what their year's labor is worth. The gap between the market price and what reaches the farmer's hands—after the store, the bank, and the [[cotton-mill|mill]] take their cuts—is the measure of [[greed|exploitation]] the novel anatomizes.
