# The Hotel

The hotel in *The Quest of the Silver Fleece* is a space of metropolitan luxury and social exclusion. "His apartment in the [[new-york|New York]] hotel" (015-001) establishes it as a residence for the [[economics|wealth]]y, while "the Fifth Avenue front of the hotel" (023-006) locates it in the most prestigious corridor of American urban life.

## Social Space

The hotel is where the novel's elite characters perform their status. "Gradually the inarticulate question" (023-006) that forms on the hotel's facade suggests that the building itself prompts social questioning—who belongs here, and who does not. "The hotel employees think her perfect" (023-039) reveals the service [[economics|economy]] that sustains the hotel's luxury: black labor, invisible and deferential, maintaining the comfort of white guests.

## Exclusion

The hotel is a space of [[race|racial]] boundary-policing. The observation that "if she stepped into this hotel to rest, even with full purse" (029-036) she would be turned away—the conditional construction leaves the outcome ominously implicit. The hotel's exclusion is not about ability to pay but about [[race|racial]] belonging: the "full purse" is not enough to overcome the color line at the door.

## Symbolic Function

The hotel stands in structural opposition to the cabin. Where the cabin "crouches" (001-005), the hotel rises on Fifth Avenue. The distance between them—geographic, economic, [[race|racial]]—is the distance that the novel's characters must travel, and the distance that most cannot.
