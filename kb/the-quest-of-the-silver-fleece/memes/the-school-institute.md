# The School at Tooms County

As an organization, the [[the-school|school]] that [[mary-taylor|Mary Taylor]] founds must navigate the impossible contradictions of its position: funded by white philanthropy, staffed by Northern [[philanthropy|reform]]ers, serving black children, and operating within a white supremacist [[society|social order]].

## Governance and Funding

The [[the-school|school]] is dependent on Northern philanthropy for its budget—a structural vulnerability that shapes every decision. [[mrs-grey|Mrs. Grey]]'s foundation provides funds but also demands [[power|influence]] over curriculum and personnel. The [[the-school|school]]'s [[politics|governance]] is thus split between its on-site staff and its distant funders, a di[[dream|vision]] that generates constant tension (015-020)(015-032).

## Faculty and Students

[[mary-taylor|Mary Taylor]] leads the [[the-school|school]] with [[miss-smith|Miss Smith]] as her experienced assistant. The students range from young children [[education|learning]] their letters to older students like [[bles-alwyn|Bles]] and [[zora|Zora]] pursuing more advanced study. The [[the-school|school]] serves multiple educational levels with minimal resources.

## Curriculum Politics

What the [[the-school|school]] teaches is a matter of political contestation. Industrial education (training for manual labor) versus liberal education (cultivation of critical thinking) is debated not as pedagogy but as politics—a decision about what kind of future black children are being prepared for.
