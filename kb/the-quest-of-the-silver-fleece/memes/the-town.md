# The Town

The unnamed town that serves as the commercial hub of [[tooms-county|Tooms County]] is a study in the spatial organization of Southern [[race|racial]] hierarchy. Its streets, buildings, and public spaces encode the [[society|social order]] in brick and dust.

## The Commercial Center

The town is where cotton is weighed, graded, bought, and shipped. [[cotton|Cotton]] warehouses dominate the skyline, their contents representing the accumulated labor of thousands of black hands (004-075). The [[store|stores]] where tenants must buy supplies on [[debt|credit]], the bank where land loans are negotiated, the lawyer's office where contracts are drawn—all are located here.

## Spatial Segregation

The town's geography maps the [[race|racial]] order: white businesses on the main street, black laborers in the alleys and loading docks, separate entrances, separate spaces, separate worlds occupying the same few acres. The physical proximity of black and white in the town makes the social distance between them all the more visible.

## The Cotton Warehouse

The warehouse is the town's central institution—the place where the cotton grown on the surrounding plantations is aggregated, processed, and shipped north. Its chimneys smoke beyond the town limits, a constant reminder of the industrial dimension of the cotton [[economics|economy]] (004-076).
