# The Parlor

The parlor in *The Quest of the Silver Fleece* is the inner sanctum of [[economics|wealth]]. "Some exquisite little parlor with its green and gold" (005-086) is the imagined destination of social [[ambition|aspiration]], while "the ladies were in the parlor, where the female Easterlys" (006-048) held court—the parlor is where elite women perform the rituals of their class.

## Domestic Luxury

The parlor is defined by its dec[[speech|oration]] and its exclusion. The "green and gold" (005-086) color scheme signals [[economics|wealth]] through aesthetic refinement rather than mere expense—this is old money, not new. The parlor is "exquisite" and "little" (005-086), suggesting that its value lies in taste rather than scale. It is a curated space, arranged for display and [[power|control]]led encounter.

## Social Performance

"A stir in the parlor indicated departure" (006-073)—the parlor is where the smallest social signals carry weight. The ladies who occupy it are identified by their family names ("the female Easterlys," 006-048), their identities subsumed into dynastic affiliation. The parlor is the stage on which elite womanhood is enacted: visiting, receiving, departing—a choreography of leisure that masks the economic power these women wield through inheritance and [[family|marriage]].

## Structural Opposition

The parlor is the Northern counterpart to the Southern cabin, but where the cabin is crowded, dark, and defined by lack, the parlor is spacious, luminous, and defined by abundance. The distance between the "one-room cabin" and the "exquisite little parlor" is the distance the novel measures—not in miles but in the distribution of the nation's [[economics|wealth]].
