# Mrs. Grey

A [[economics|wealth]]y Northern philanthropist whose interest in Negro education masks a determination to [[power|control]] it—her schemes represent the political [[economics|economy]] of white-[[power|control]]led black [[education|schooling]].

## Character Overview

A [[economics|wealth]]y Northern philanthropist whose interest in Negro education masks a determination to [[power|control]] it—her schemes represent the political [[economics|economy]] of white-[[power|control]]led black [[education|schooling]].

## Key Relationships

Mrs. Grey embodies the Northern philanthropic establishment's approach to Negro education: well-funded, well-intentioned, and fundamentally committed to white [[power|control]] of black institutions. Her 'education schemes' are the mechanism through which Northern [[wealth|capital]] shapes Southern black [[education|schooling]] to serve white interests (015-020)(015-032).

## Key Scenes

- With [[mary-taylor|Mary Taylor]]: Mrs. Grey represents the philanthropic power structure that [[mary-taylor|Mary]] must learn to navigate—and ultimately resist.

## Character Arc

Mrs. Grey's machinations around the [[the-school|school]] bill reveal how philanthropy functions as politics by other means. Funding is never just funding; it is [[power|control]] over curriculum, over personnel, over the very purpose of black education.
