# Loyalty

Loyalty in *The Quest of the Silver Fleece* is presented as a value claimed by multiple, often conflicting, [[duty|obligation]]s. Characters must choose between personal loyalty, political allegiance, and [[race|racial]] solidarity—and the novel shows that these choices are seldom clean.

## Personal Loyalty

[[emma|Emma]] is described as possessing "loyalty and pluck" for "one she loves she would give her life" (020-040). This personal devotion is the novel's most straightforward loyalty—the bond between individuals, unmediated by institutions.

## Political Loyalty

[[miss-wynn|Miss Wynn]] is said to have been "bribed to remain loyal to the Republicans by a six thousand dollar appointment" (026-073). Here loyalty is reduced to a transaction, exposing the cynicism beneath political allegiance in the [[washington-dc|Washington]] sections of the novel. The language of loyalty in politics is a mask for patronage.

## The Tension

The novel does not resolve the conflict between personal and political loyalty. The servant's humility that [[emma|Emma]]'s loyalty is paired with (020-040) hints at the cost: loyalty to individuals in an unequal society can reinforce the very hierarchies that political [[betrayal|disloyalty]] might challenge.
