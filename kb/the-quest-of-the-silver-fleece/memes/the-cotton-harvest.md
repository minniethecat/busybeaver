# The Cotton Harvest

The cotton harvest is the novel's great seasonal event—the weeks when the bolls burst white and every available hand is needed in the [[field|fields]]. It is the climax of the agricultural year and the moment when the economic realities of the cotton system are most nakedly visible.

## The Picking Season

When the cotton bolls open, the [[field|fields]] fill with pickers—men, women, and children bending over the rows from dawn to dusk. The [[labor|work]] is brutal, the pay minimal, the urgency absolute. [[cotton|Cotton]] must be picked before rain or frost damages the crop (005-053)(005-059).

## Zora in the Fields

[[zora|Zora]] picks cotton to fund her education, her body performing the labor that the cotton [[economics|economy]] demands while her mind reaches toward a different future. Her presence in the harvest [[field|fields]] embodies the tension between the demands of survival and the [[ambition|aspiration]] toward something more.

## The Settlement

After the harvest comes the [[village|settlement]]—the annual accounting when debts are tallied, cotton is sold, and farmers learn whether they have earned a surplus or sunk deeper into debt. The [[village|settlement]] is where the abstractions of the cotton [[economics|economy]] become the concrete fates of individual families.
