# The Big House

The [[colonel-cresswell|Cresswell]] mansion—"the Big House" in the parlance of the plantation—is the architectural expression of planter power. Its columns, its wide verandas, its furnished rooms, and its carefully maintained grounds all declare: this family rules this land.

## Architecture of Authority

The Big House is built in the style of the Southern plantation mansion, its neoclassical columns invoking the [[power|authority]] of ancient Greece and Rome. The architecture makes a claim: the [[colonel-cresswell|Cresswell]]s are not merely [[economics|wealth]]y but civilized, not merely powerful but legitimate heirs of Western tradition.

## Inside the Big House

The interior of the house—its parlors, dining rooms, and bedrooms—is the setting for the intimate politics of the white elite. Here, [[colonel-cresswell|Colonel Cresswell]] receives guests, negotiates business, and performs the rituals of Southern gentility. Black servants move through these rooms invisibly, privy to every secret.

## The View from the Veranda

From the Big House veranda, the [[colonel-cresswell|Cresswell]] plantation spreads out like a kingdom: the cotton [[field|fields]] stretching to the horizon, the tenant cabins in the distance, the town beyond. The view enacts the planter's gaze—the land and its people laid out for inspection and [[power|control]].
