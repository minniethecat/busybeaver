# Cresswell Plantation

The largest plantation in [[tooms-county|Tooms County]]—a system of land, labor, and power organized around cotton cultivation and [[race|racial]] hierarchy.

## Description

The [[colonel-cresswell|Cresswell]] plantation is not merely a farm but an entire social system. Its [[field|fields]] stretch across the best land in the county, [[labor|work]]ed by black [[the-tenant-farmers|tenant farmers]] whose labor en[[wealth|riches]] the [[colonel-cresswell|Cresswell]] family while keeping the [[labor|workers]] themselves in perpetual debt (004-054)(004-082)(004-083).

## Key Aspects

- [[the-big-house|The Big House]]: The [[colonel-cresswell|Cresswell]] mansion, seat of white [[power|authority]], visible from every corner of the plantation.

## Geography

- [[the-tenant-cabins|The Tenant Cabins]]: Where black families live, their homes as precarious as their economic position.

## Social Significance

- [[the-cotton-fields|The Cotton Fields]]: The vast acreage where the plantation's [[economics|wealth]] is produced by black hands.

## Historical Context

- The Commissary: Where tenants must buy supplies on [[debt|credit]], the first link in the chain of debt.
