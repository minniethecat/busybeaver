# The Bedroom

The bedroom in *The Quest of the Silver Fleece* is the space of private selfhood. When characters retreat to their bedrooms, the novel gains access to interior states—physical and psychological—that are hidden from public view. "She climbed to her bedroom and stared at the stars" (003-054): the bedroom is where the day's performances end and the unguarded self emerges.

## Miss Smith's Room

[[miss-smith|Miss Smith]]'s bedroom is a space of labor and vulnerability. "S[[cabin|hut]] up in her bedroom, went laboriously over her accounts" (008-016)—the bedroom is not only for rest but for the solitary [[labor|work]] of running the [[the-school|school]], the administrative labor that no one else sees. When "she was helping [[miss-smith|Miss Smith]] in the bedroom" (011-014), the bedroom becomes a sickroom, revealing the physical toll that the [[the-school|school]] project exacts on its founder.

## Zora's Stars

The most lyrical bedroom moment belongs to [[zora|Zora]]. "She climbed to her bedroom and stared at the stars" (003-054)—the bedroom window frames the night sky, connecting the confined domestic space to the vastness beyond. The bedroom is where [[zora|Zora]] dreams, where her ambition takes shape in solitude before it can be expressed in public.

## Privacy and Revelation

The bedroom is the novel's space of maximum privacy and therefore maximum vulnerability. Characters who are composed and strategic in the parlor or the office are found in their bedrooms exhausted, ill, or quietly longing. The contrast between public performance and private truth is one of the novel's central concerns, and the bedroom is where that contrast is most sharply drawn.
