# Mrs. Grey's Philanthropic Foundation

The foundation that [[mrs-grey|Mrs. Grey]] [[power|control]]s is the organizational form through which Northern philanthropy intervenes in Southern black education. It is structured as a [[philanthropy|charity]] but functions as a mechanism of [[power|control]].

## Organizational Form

The foundation is a classic [[progress|Progress]]ive-era philanthropic institution: funded by Northern [[economics|wealth]], governed by a white board, ostensibly dedicated to Negro [[progress|uplift]]. Its organizational form disguises its political function—the foundation presents itself as apolitical benevolence even as it shapes educational policy to serve white interests (015-020)(015-032).

## Funding as Control

The foundation's power lies in its [[power|control]] of funding. [[school|School]]s that depend on its grants must accept its conditions—conditions that shape curriculum, personnel, and the very purpose of black education. The foundation does not need to issue orders; it simply needs to set the terms on which funding is available.

## Historical Parallels

Du Bois had direct experience with the major philanthropic foundations of his era—the Rosenwald Fund, the Peabody [[education|Education]] Fund, the [[john-taylor|John]] F. Slater Fund—all of which funded black education while maintaining white [[power|control]]. [[mrs-grey|Mrs. Grey]]'s foundation is a fictional composite of these real institutions and their real contradictions.
