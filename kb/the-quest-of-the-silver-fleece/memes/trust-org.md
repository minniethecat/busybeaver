# The Cotton Trust

The cotton trust in *The Quest of the Silver Fleece* is the financial instrument through which the novel's antagonists seek to monopolize the cotton [[economics|economy]]. Modeled on "the steel trust" (006-060), the cotton trust would [[power|control]] every stage of production—"a vast trust of cotton manufacturing covering" (006-048) the entire industry from raw fiber to finished cloth.

## The Trust Vision

The trust is described in explicitly monopolistic terms. "The biggest trust next to steel" (006-050) is the stated ambition—a combination that would rival the great industrial consolidations of the Gilded Age. The question "why not bring them into the trust?" (006-054) reveals the logic of expansion: every independent producer is a potential acquisition, every competitor a future subsidiary. The trust's [[dream|vision]] is total [[power|control]] of the cotton market.

## Raw Material Control

The trust's architects study the steel trust's model. "The steel trust owns its iron mines" (006-060)—vertical integration from raw material to finished product. The cotton trust would need to [[power|control]] not just the [[cotton-mill|mill]]s but the cotton [[field|fields]] themselves. "Our raw material is" (006-061) the key vulnerability: cotton is grown by thousands of farmers, not mined from a few sites, making monopolization more difficult. The trust's scheme to overcome this difficulty—through debt, through land acquisition, through political [[power|control]]—is the novel's economic plot.

## Critique of Monopoly

Du Bois presents the trust as an instrument of extraction. The [[economics|wealth]] that the trust concentrates is [[economics|wealth]] created by black labor in the [[field|fields]], now appropriated by white [[wealth|capital]] in the boardrooms. "[[john-taylor|Taylor]]'s larger trust as chimerical" (010-099) suggests both the ambition and the potential impossibility of the scheme—the trust may be a fantasy, but the attempt to create it inflicts real damage on those whose labor it seeks to [[power|control]].
