# The Office

The office in *The Quest of the Silver Fleece* is a space of institutional power. Whether at [[miss-smith|Miss Smith]]'s [[the-school|school]], on Wall Street, or in [[colonel-cresswell|Colonel Cresswell]]'s plantation headquarters, the office is where decisions are made that shape the lives of those who [[labor|work]] in the [[field|fields]], study in the classrooms, and worship in [[religion|the church]]es.

## The School Office

[[miss-smith|Miss Smith]]'s [[the-school|school]] office is the administrative heart of the educational project. "[[mary-taylor|Mary Taylor]] had charge of the office that morning" (008-016) indicates its daily function. When [[miss-smith|Miss Smith]] enters, "that lady entered the office" (008-047) and the atmosphere shifts—the office is where [[power|authority]] resides and where grievances are adjudicated. [[bles-alwyn|Bles]] is directed to "go to the office" (002-013) upon arrival, establishing it as the threshold through which all must pass.

## Wall Street

The novel's urban offices represent a different scale of power. "In the rich Wall Street offices of Grey and Easterly, Brokers" (006-003), the cotton that black hands pick in [[alabama|Alabama]] becomes a financial instrument. The "clerk in her late husband's office" (006-013) reveals the gendered and precarious nature of office employment. The [[colonel-cresswell|Colonel]]'s [[new-york|New York]] office, where "the older one perturbed" lingers (010-141), links rural plantation power to urban finance.

## Power Relations

The office is where the novel's power relations become institutionalized. [[mary-taylor|Miss Taylor]] "returned to the office, and threw [[mary-taylor|Miss Taylor]]'s complaint" (011-012)—the office receives, processes, and disposes of human concerns. The [[colonel-cresswell|Colonel]] "left for his office" (003-006) with a "'Humph!'"—the office is the destination of those who hold power and the space from which they exercise it.
