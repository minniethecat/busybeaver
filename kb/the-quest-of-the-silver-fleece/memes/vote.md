# The Negro Vote

The Negro vote in *The Quest of the Silver Fleece* is a political force that the novel's elite characters seek to [[power|control]]. "A bolt in the Northern Negro vote" (022-016) can shift elections, and "a hundred thousand or more black voters in pivotal Northern States" (025-011) are a bloc to be managed, not a constituency to be served.

## Political Calculus

The black vote is treated throughout the novel as an instrument rather than an expression of democratic citizenship. "We may have to give them a" concession (022-016) is the language of political transaction—votes in exchange for minimal recognition. The calculation that "in a close election the Negroes of [[new-york|New York]], Ohio" (025-011) could decide the outcome reduces black political participation to a variable in white electoral strategy.

## The School Bill Context

The vote is central to the novel's political plot. The [[the-school|school]] bill that would fund [[miss-smith|Miss Smith]]'s educational project depends on legislative votes that, in turn, depend on black electoral support. The connection between the [[the-school|school]] in [[alabama|Alabama]] and the ballot box in Ohio makes visible the national scope of [[race|racial]] politics: a black child's education in [[tooms-county|Tooms County]] is determined by a black voter's ballot in Cleveland.

## Containment

The novel reveals how black voting power is contained. "Certain socialistic votes in the House" (006-019) are dismissed as aberrations. The black vote is acknowledged only when it is needed, ignored when it is not, and suppressed when it threatens. "One can advise a black man to vote the Democratic ticket?" (024-060)—the rhetorical question exposes the absurdity of asking black voters to support the party of white supremacy.
