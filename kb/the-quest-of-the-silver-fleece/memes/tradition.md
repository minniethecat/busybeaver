# Tradition

Tradition in [[tooms-county|Tooms County]] is a force that [[labor|work]]s almost entirely against the novel's protagonists. When "bewilderment and outraged traditions" (035-033) greet [[zora|Zora]]'s heresies, the novel shows tradition not as a benign inheritance but as an active agent of social [[power|control]].

## Tradition vs. Progress

Every [[philanthropy|reform]] [[mary-taylor|Mary Taylor]] attempts collides with Southern tradition—traditions of deference, of [[race|racial]] hierarchy, of what can and cannot be said. The novel does not sentimentalize tradition; it understands it as the codification of past power relations, a way of making the present answerable to the dead.

## The Weight of Custom

"Lying customary in this community" (007-048)—the novel links tradition to deception. What is "customary" is what goes unquestioned, and what goes unquestioned in [[tooms-county|Tooms County]] is a [[race|racial]] order built on violence. Tradition is the name that power gives to its own longevity.
