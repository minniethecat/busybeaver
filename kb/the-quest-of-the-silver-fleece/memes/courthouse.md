# The Courthouse

The courthouse in *The Quest of the Silver Fleece* dominates the [[toomsville|Toomsville]] square. It is "an old square brick" building (004-087) to which the other buildings on the square seem to be "doing obeisance" (004-089)—the architectural expression of legal [[power|authority]] in the county.

## Architecture of Power

Du Bois renders the courthouse as a building that commands deference. The other structures on the square appear to bow "as if doing obeisance to the courthouse" (004-089), a metaphor that makes explicit what architecture implies: the law sits at the top of the [[class|social hierarchy]]. "Down the courthouse steps" (004-089) come the officials whose decisions shape county life—judges, clerks, sheriffs—the men who ad[[preacher|minister]] the legal order.

## Zora's Intervention

The courthouse becomes a site of dramatic action when [[zora|Zora]] is found "crowding into the village courthouse to see if she could not help" (035-039). Her presence in this space of white male [[power|authority]] is itself a challenge. She enters not as a defendant or supplicant but as someone seeking to exercise power on behalf of others—a transformation of the courthouse's function from instrument of [[power|control]] to potential site of justice.

## Symbolic Function

The courthouse is the institutional counterpart to [[religion|the church]]. Where [[religion|the church]] ad[[preacher|minister]]s moral order, the courthouse ad[[preacher|minister]]s legal order—and in the Jim Crow South, the legal order is white order. The "obeisance" that other buildings make to the courthouse mirrors the deference that black citizens must show to white law.
