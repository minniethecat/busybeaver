# The Black Church of Tooms County

The black [[the-church|church]] is the only major institution in [[tooms-county|Tooms County]] that is organized, led, and [[power|control]]led by black people themselves. Its organizational autonomy makes it unique—and uniquely important—in the social landscape of the cotton South.

## Organizational Autonomy

Unlike the [[the-school|school]] (dependent on white funding) or the plantation (owned and [[power|control]]led by whites), [[religion|the church]] belongs to its congregation. It elects its own [[preacher|pastor]], manages its own finances, and determines its own priorities. This autonomy is its defining institutional characteristic (022-108)(022-110).

## Leadership Structure

[[johnson|Brother Johnson]] serves as [[preacher|pastor]]—a position of genuine [[power|authority]] within the black community. The deacons and elders form a leadership structure that mirrors, in some ways, the political organization that black Southerners were denied in the formal political system.

## Social Functions

Beyond worship, [[religion|the church]] provides mutual aid, dispute resolution, education, and [[community|collective]] organizing. It is the closest thing the black community has to a self-governing institution—and as such, it is watched carefully by the white power structure.
