# The Company

The company in *The Quest of the Silver Fleece* is the corporate form through which business is conducted. Whether it is the [[colonel-cresswell|Cresswell]] plantation enterprise, the cotton factoring companies, or the investment vehicles that channel Northern [[wealth|capital]] southward, the company is the legal structure that organizes economic activity and limits liability while concentrating profit.

## The Corporate Form

Du Bois is attentive to the legal form of the company. The company is distinct from the individuals who own it—it can hold property, sign contracts, sue and be sued. This separation of the company from its owners is both its strength (it survives the death of any individual) and its moral hazard (it shields owners from the consequences of its actions). The cotton [[cotton-mill|mill]] accident—"particularly distressing accident to a little girl" (037-006)—is a company liability, not a personal one, which means the "damage-suits" (037-005) target a legal entity rather than the individuals whose decisions caused the harm.

## Company and Community

The company's [[love|relationship]] to the community is fundamentally extractive. The company takes cotton from the [[field|fields]], labor from the cabins, and profit from both, returning little. "The pleasure of Mr. [[colonel-cresswell|Cresswell]]'s company" (012-008)—the pun on "company" as both business entity and social presence—captures the dual nature of corporate power: the company is simultaneously an economic institution and a social force, shaping not just markets but manners.

## Exclusion

The company is an instrument of white economic power from which black Southerners are systematically excluded. Black farmers cannot form companies; black laborers cannot own shares. The company is the organizational form of the color line in the economic sphere—a legal structure that channels the value created by black labor into white hands. There is no "[[zora|Zora]] and [[bles-alwyn|Bles]], [[cotton|Cotton]] Factors" in the novel's world.
