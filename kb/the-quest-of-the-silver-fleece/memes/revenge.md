# Revenge

Revenge in *The Quest of the Silver Fleece* appears as a double-edged response to [[race|racial]] wrong. The novel treats it as a natural reaction to [[justice|injustice]]—yet one that threatens to consume those who pursue it more than those it targets.

## Vengeance as Specter

The [[the-swamp|swamp]] is described with imagery of "vengeance" rising "before them like a spectral vengeance" (007-133), associating retribution with the untamed landscape itself. This figurative use suggests that the demand for justice hangs over the entire [[society|social order]] like a haunting.

## Plotting and Power

When wronged, [[colonel-cresswell|Colonel Cresswell]] is "furious and began to plot vengeance" (024-004), framing revenge as the recourse of the powerful when their interests are threatened. Later, the appeal to "red vengeance" is made to "all the State, all the nation" (037-021), showing how the language of retribution can escalate personal grievance into [[community|collective]] mobilization.

## Ambivalence

The novel does not simply condemn revenge. The drive to avenge wrongs is entangled with the drive for justice itself. But the spectrum from [[colonel-cresswell|Cresswell]]'s petty plotting to the apocalyptic call for "red vengeance" (037-021) suggests that revenge, untransformed, merely reproduces the violence it answers.
