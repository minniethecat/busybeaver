# Courage

"He laughed in the bold courage of day" (001-011)—the novel's first mention of courage links it to light, to visibility, to the refusal to hide. Throughout *The Quest of the Silver Fleece*, courage takes many forms, but it is always the decision to act rather than submit.

## Courage to Act

[[zora|Zora]]'s courage is the novel's most sustained example. "Since you came, but I hadn't the courage," she tells [[miss-smith|Miss Smith]] (032-035), acknowledging the gap between knowing what must be done and finding the strength to do it. Her trajectory is one of courage learned, not innate—a quality that grows through the demands the world makes on her.

## Courage and Discouragement

The novel gives nearly as much attention to discouragement as to courage. "The League too discouraged to repeat the dose" (010-120) records how structural opposition wears down [[community|collective]] will. [[mary-taylor|Mary Taylor]] "hastened to encourage" (013-025) those around her, understanding that courage is a resource that must be replenished. The novel treats courage not as an individual virtue but as something produced—or destroyed—by community.

## False Courage and True

Not every bold act in the novel is courageous. Bluster and violence, the "courage" of the mob, are the opposite: they act from safety rather than risk. The novel distinguishes the courage that protects privilege from the courage that challenges it.
