# Mr. Easterly

A Northern [[wealth|capital]]ist and [[john-taylor|John Taylor]]'s business partner whose interests in Southern cotton reveal the flow of [[wealth|capital]] from North to South—and the [[greed|exploitation]] that follows.

## Character Overview

A Northern [[wealth|capital]]ist and [[john-taylor|John Taylor]]'s business partner whose interests in Southern cotton reveal the flow of [[wealth|capital]] from North to South—and the [[greed|exploitation]] that follows.

## Key Relationships

Easterly is the silent partner behind [[john-taylor|John Taylor]]'s schemes. He represents the established Northern [[wealth|capital]] that sees in the post-Reconstruction South a field for investment and extraction (006-046).

## Key Scenes

- With [[john-taylor|John Taylor]]: Easterly is [[john-taylor|John]]'s mentor in [[wealth|capital]]ism—the established player to [[john-taylor|John]]'s ambitious upstart.

## Character Arc

Easterly's presence in the novel connects [[tooms-county|Tooms County]] to the broader currents of American [[wealth|capital]]ism. The cotton that [[zora|Zora]] picks, that [[colonel-cresswell|Cresswell]] sells, that [[john-taylor|John Taylor]] dreams of manufacturing—it all flows toward men like Easterly, who profit without ever setting foot in an [[alabama|Alabama]] cotton field.
