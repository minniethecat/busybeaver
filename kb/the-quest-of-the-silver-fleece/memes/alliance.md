# The Political Alliance

The alliance in *The Quest of the Silver Fleece* is the organizational form of elite cooperation. "He was anxious for an alliance" (010-167)—the alliance is what powerful men seek, the combination of resources and interests that amplifies individual power into [[community|collective]] force. The novel's central alliance joins [[colonel-cresswell|Cresswell]] land to [[john-taylor|Taylor]] [[wealth|capital]], Southern agriculture to Northern finance.

## Marriage as Alliance

The double wedding at [[colonel-cresswell|Cresswell]] Oaks is an alliance formalized as [[family|marriage]]. [[mary-taylor|Mary Taylor]] marries [[harry-cresswell|Harry Cresswell]], and the "alliance" (010-167) that [[colonel-cresswell|the Colonel]] was anxious for is consummated. "It was a mésalliance of a sort that pricked him" (020-003)—the French term suggests the class anxieties that attend the alliance. Northern money may be necessary, but it is not quite respectable; the alliance brings power at the cost of social discomfort.

## Economic Logic

The alliance has a clear economic logic. Southern land produces cotton; Northern [[wealth|capital]] finances its production and sale. The alliance joins these complementary resources, creating an integrated economic machine. The goal is to "bring to the alliance as much independent economic" (035-073) power as possible—each partner brings resources, and the combined entity is stronger than either alone. The alliance is the organizational form of monopoly in embryo.

## Exclusion

Like all the novel's organizations, the alliance is defined as much by who it excludes as who it includes. Black farmers, black [[labor|workers]], and black communities have no place in the [[colonel-cresswell|Cresswell]]-[[john-taylor|Taylor]] alliance except as its objects—labor to be managed, consumers to be indebted, voters to be delivered. The alliance is the organizational expression of the color line in its economic dimension.
