# The School Board

The [[the-school|school]] board in *The Quest of the Silver Fleece* is the governing body that oversees [[miss-smith|Miss Smith]]'s educational project. "Of course, it would be a board of friends of the [[the-school|school]]" (012-009)—the board is presented as benign, a circle of supporters, but the novel reveals how [[politics|governance]] structures can channel and constrain the educational mission they exist to support.

## Composition and Power

The board's composition determines its character. A "board of friends" (012-009) sounds congenial, but who counts as a friend of a Negro [[the-school|school]] in Jim Crow [[alabama|Alabama]]? The board includes people whose interests may not align with the [[the-school|school]]'s mission—political patrons, financial supporters, representatives of the white community whose approval the [[the-school|school]] needs to survive. The board is where the [[the-school|school]]'s radical potential is moderated by the necessity of institutional survival.

## Governance and Constraint

The board appears most dramatically around the "appointment of the new school-board" (022-074). Appointments to the board are political acts—each new member shifts the balance of power, potentially redirecting the [[the-school|school]]'s resources and mission. The board that governs can also be the board that [[power|control]]s; the "friends" who oversee the [[the-school|school]] may become the gatekeepers who limit its reach.

## Institutional Mediation

Du Bois presents the board as one of the novel's key sites of institutional mediation. Between the teacher in the classroom and the politician in [[the-state-legislature|the legislature]] stands the board—translating educational need into administrative language, filtering political pressure into institutional policy. The board is where the [[the-school|school]]'s dependency on white political support is made structural, built into the [[politics|governance]] of the institution itself.
