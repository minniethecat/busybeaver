# The Alabama State Legislature

The state legislature appears in the novel primarily through its actions (or inactions) on education funding. It is the organizational embodiment of the political system that sustains—rather than challenges—the [[race|racial]] and economic order of the cotton South.

## Composition and Power

In the post-disfranchisement South, state legislatures were composed entirely of white men elected by white voters. Black Southerners had no representation and no recourse. The legislature's formal [[duty|responsibility]] for public policy masked its actual function: protecting the interests of the white landowning class.

## The Education Bill

The legislature's treatment of the [[the-school|school]] bill is a case study in how political organizations serve economic interests. The bill's fate is determined not by the merits of black education but by the balance of forces among planters, philanthropists, and politicians—all of whom are white (027-053).

## Law and Legitimacy

The legislature gives legal form to the [[race|racial]] order. Its statutes—on contracts, on debt, on education, on voting—create the legal frame[[labor|work]] within which the cotton [[economics|economy]] operates. Law is not neutral; it is an instrument of power, and the legislature is where that instrument is forged.
