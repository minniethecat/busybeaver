# The Executive Committee

The committee in *The Quest of the Silver Fleece* is the organizational form through which power is coordinated. "Form a big committee of Northern business men like" (015-032) is [[john-taylor|Taylor]]'s strategy—the committee brings together the interests that will shape Southern development, concentrating decision-making in a small group of [[economics|wealth]]y white men.

## Taylor's Committee

[[mary-taylor|Mary Taylor]] proposes the committee as an instrument of coordinated action. "We'll get this committee which [[john-taylor|Taylor]] suggests appointed" (015-039)—the passive construction ("get...appointed") reveals how committees are formed: not elected but assembled, not representative but curated. "Who'll be the committee?" (015-040) is the key question—membership determines outcomes, and the membership is predetermined by [[economics|wealth]] and connection.

## The Farmers' League

The "executive committee of the Farmers' League" (018-051) represents the Southern agricultural interest. Its members are "the big landlords who composed the committee" (018-052)—the committee is not a democratic body but an oligarchy, the organizational form of planter power. "Whereupon [[harry-cresswell|Harry Cresswell]] in" (018-052) acts—the committee's decisions are immediate and consequential, translating discussion directly into action.

## Committee as Governance

Du Bois presents the committee as the real form of [[politics|governance]] in the novel's world, displacing formal democratic institutions. The committee makes decisions that [[the-state-legislature|the legislature]] merely ratifies; the committee allocates resources that the market merely distributes. [[mary-taylor|Mary Taylor]], "alone of the committee, took her commission so seriously" (016-011)—the irony is that her seriousness about the committee's [[labor|work]] only makes her a more effective instrument of the interests the committee serves.
