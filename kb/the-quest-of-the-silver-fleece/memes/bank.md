# The Bank

The bank in *The Quest of the Silver Fleece* is the financial [[courage|nerve]] center of the cotton [[economics|economy]]. "The banks and wholesale houses" (006-064) are presented as the second pillar of economic [[power|control]]—after [[the-cotton-trust|the cotton monopoly]] itself—through which [[cresswell-plantation-org|the plantation system]] is financed and Northern [[wealth|capital]] extracts Southern [[economics|wealth]].

## Financial Power

Du Bois renders the bank as an instrument of concentrated economic force. "Ten banks [[power|control]] these eight thousand" cotton [[cotton-mill|mill]]s (010-114), a statistic that reveals the scale of financial consolidation. The novel maps the chain of [[power|control]]: banks finance the [[cotton-mill|mill]]s, [[cotton-mill|mill]]s buy the cotton, and black farmers grow the cotton at the bottom of this pyramid. The bank is where the value created by black labor is captured and redirected.

## Toomsville

The "[[toomsville|Toomsville]] bank" (012-164) is the local node of this financial net[[labor|work]]. When characters "drew up at the [[toomsville|Toomsville]] bank" (012-164), they enter the space where cotton becomes [[wealth|capital]]. "Business at the bank will keep me until after lunch" (010-020) is the casual language of those who move comfortably within this world of finance—a world from which black farmers are excluded.

## Strategic Ambition

The bank is central to the [[colonel-cresswell|Cresswell]]-[[john-taylor|Taylor]] scheme. Their plan to "invest in the South in bank stock and industries" (010-099) reveals the ambition to [[power|control]] not just land and labor but the financial infrastructure itself. "If we can get the banks, wholesale merchants, and big planters" (010-116), the monopoly is complete. The counterargument—"you couldn't get the banks and the big merchants behind" it (010-124)—exposes the limits of even [[colonel-cresswell|Cresswell]]'s power.
