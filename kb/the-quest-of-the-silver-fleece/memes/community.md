# Community

Community in *The Quest of the Silver Fleece* exists as both a present reality and a future ideal. The novel maps the actual communities of [[tooms-county|Tooms County]]—the tenant quarters, the [[the-school|school]], [[religion|the church]]—while reaching toward [[zora|Zora]]'s [[dream|vision]] of "the beginning of a free community" (032-059).

## The Actual Community

[[mary-taylor|Mary Taylor]] studies "the community, and particularly the [[colonel-cresswell|Cresswell]]s" (007-002) upon her arrival, discovering how thoroughly one family dominates local life. She also discovers "lying [[tradition|custom]]ary in this community" (007-048)—habits of evasion and self-protection that the color line has made second nature. The community the novel depicts is damaged by the forces it must survive.

## The Forces of Evil

"To the forces of evil in this community," a character reflects, [[elspeth|Elspeth]]'s [[power|influence]] "has been such that he can direct" them (016-056)—and later acknowledges she "has meant evil for this community, and for me" (019-057). The novel insists that community can be a vector of harm as readily as of good.

## The Free Community

[[zora|Zora]]'s purchase of [[the-swamp-land|the swamp]] land is aimed at creating something new. "We can when once the community learns that it pays" (036-020)—her pragmatic [[dream|vision]] links community to economic self-sufficiency, refusing the separation of material and moral life that the white power structure depends on.
