# Easterly's Investment Syndicate

Easterly's syndicate is the organizational mechanism through which Northern [[wealth|capital]] enters the Southern cotton [[economics|economy]]. It pools investment from Northern sources and deploys it in the South—buying [[cotton-mill|mill]]s, financing planters, speculating on cotton futures.

## Organizational Form

The syndicate is deliberately opaque—a private investment group rather than a public corp[[speech|oration]], its operations shielded from scrutiny. It is the organizational form of [[wealth|capital]] that prefers not to be seen. Easterly operates through intermediaries and behind the scenes (006-046).

## Capital Flow

The syndicate channels [[wealth|capital]] from North to South, from industry to agriculture, from the future markets of [[new-york|New York]] to the cotton [[field|fields]] of [[alabama|Alabama]]. It profits from the spread between what cotton costs to grow and what it sells for—a spread maintained by the [[greed|exploitation]] of black labor.

## The Invisible Hand

The syndicate's members never see the [[field|fields]] where their [[wealth|capital]] [[labor|work]]s. They never meet the pickers whose labor generates their returns. This distance is not incidental but essential: the organizational form of finance [[wealth|capital]] depends on rendering the human reality of production invisible.
