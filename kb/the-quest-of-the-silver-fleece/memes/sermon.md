# The Sermon

The sermon in *The Quest of the Silver Fleece* is the central event of black religious life. Whether delivered from the pulpit of the "little [[the-church|church]]" (027-001) or at the outdoor revival, the sermon is where sacred text is interpreted through the lens of lived experience and where the preacher's voice becomes a [[community|collective]] possession.

## The Preacher's Voice

Du Bois pays careful attention to the sermon as oral performance. "Under the sermons and ceremonies she had always" (027-004) sat, absorbing not just doctrine but the texture of the preacher's voice, the rhythm of call and response, the [[community|collective]] breath of [[the-black-church-org|the congregation]]. The preacher who yields to "say just a word before the sermont" (033-078) before launching into full delivery performs the ritual of reluctance that precedes inspired speech—the sermon is not prepared but received, the preacher an instrument rather than an author.

## Sacred and Social

The sermon bridges sacred text and social reality. The preacher interprets scripture through the experience of black Southern life, making biblical narratives speak to the conditions of plantation existence. The sermon is where the promise of deliverance—from Egypt, from Babylon, from sin—is made to resonate with the specific oppression of Jim Crow [[alabama|Alabama]]. The congregation hears not just about ancient Israel but about themselves.

## Community Formation

The sermon is an event of community formation. Gathered to hear the Word, [[the-black-church-org|the congregation]] becomes a [[community|collective]] body—breathing together, responding together, constituted as a "[[the-church|church]]" in the theological sense through the act of shared listening. The sermon creates the community it addresses, and the community's response—amens, shouts, silence—shapes the sermon in turn.
