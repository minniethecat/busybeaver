# The Political Party

The political party in *The Quest of the Silver Fleece* is the organization through which electoral democracy operates—and through which it is subverted. The Democratic and Republican parties compete for power, but their competition over black votes reveals a deeper consensus: black interests are to be managed, not served.

## Party as Machine

Du Bois renders the party as a machine for managing populations. "On the eve of the great party [[tradition|convention]]s" (024-065), the political class assembles to select candidates and distribute patronage. The [[tradition|convention]] is where the party's internal democracy is performed and contained—delegates speak, votes are cast, but the outcomes are determined in advance by the same net[[labor|work]]s of [[economics|wealth]] and [[power|influence]] that structure every other institution in the novel.

## The Black Vote

The party's [[love|relationship]] to black voters is purely instrumental. The question "one can advise a black man to vote the Democratic ticket?" (024-060) is rhetorical—the answer is obviously no, but the fact that the question must be asked reveals the party's need for black votes in close elections. The party wants black electoral support without black [[politics|political power]]—votes without representation, ballots without [[power|influence]].

## Party and Policy

The party is where political principle is subordinated to electoral calculation. The [[the-school|school]] bill, the cotton trust, the administration of justice—all pass through the party's filter, where they are evaluated not by their justice but by their electoral consequences. The party transforms political questions into organizational questions: not "is this right?" but "will this help us win?"
