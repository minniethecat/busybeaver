# Carlomagno

Frankish emperor — Mill mentions Charlemagne alongside Akbar as an example of a benevolent despot who could legitimately govern 'barbarians' before they reached the stage of development where liberty becomes both possible and necessary

## Vita e contesto storico

A historical figure whose life and work intersect with Mill's argument in *Sulla Libertà*. Mill draws on this figure to illustrate his broader claims about liberty, authority, and human development.

## Ruolo in *Sulla Libertà*

Mill references this figure within his broader argumentative framework. As with all historical figures in *Sulla Libertà*, the primary significance lies not in biographical detail but in the argumentative function — what this person's life, death, or teachings demonstrate about the nature of liberty, the dangers of authority, or the conditions of human progress.
