# Verità

*Verità* (truth) plays a central epistemic role in Mill's argument for liberty of thought and discussion. Mill's famous Chapter II defense of free speech is built not on the right to express truth but on the **utility of falsehood** — the idea that truth requires constant challenge to remain living and vital.

## Definizione

Mill does not offer a metaphysical definition of truth but an epistemological one grounded in human fallibility. The justification for free discussion rests on the premise that no one — not even the majority, not even the wisest — can be certain of possessing complete truth:

> "La specie umana non è infallibile; le sue verità non sono, per la maggior parte, se non delle mezze verità; l'unanimità delle opinioni non è desiderabile, a meno ch'essa non risulti dal confronto più libero e completo delle opinioni contrarie." — *Sulla Libertà*, (003-002)

## Ruolo nell'argomentazione di Mill

Truth functions as the **epistemic anchor** of Mill's argument for freedom of thought and discussion:

**The fallibilist case for free discussion**: Mill's most innovative argument in Chapter II is that suppressing an opinion is wrong *even if the suppressed opinion is certainly false*. He gives three cases:
1. The suppressed opinion may be true — to suppress it is to assume infallibility.
2. Even if false, the suppressed opinion may contain a portion of truth — most received opinions are only partial truths.
3. Even if the received opinion is the complete truth, it will be held as "dead dogma" rather than "living truth" unless it is vigorously contested.

This third argument is Mill's most distinctive contribution — truth without challenge becomes prejudice:

> "Le migliori credenze e le pratiche migliori hanno una eccessiva tendenza a degenerare in qualcosa di macchinale." — *Sulla Libertà*, (003-023)

**Truth as progressive**: Mill's conception of truth is dynamic rather than static. Truth is not a fixed possession but something that grows through contestation. The collision of opposing opinions is the engine of truth's development.

**Half-truths**: A key Millian insight is that most received opinions are "mezze verità" (half-truths). The suppressed opinion often contains the neglected half. Free discussion allows the full truth to emerge from the partial truths of competing views.

## Citazioni chiave

> "Se l'opinione è vera, gli uomini sono privati dell'occasione di cambiare l'errore per la verità; se è falsa, essi perdono un beneficio quasi altrettanto grande: la percezione più chiara e l'impressione più viva della verità, risultante dalla sua collisione con l'errore." — *Sulla Libertà*, (002-003)
