# Sviluppo

In *Sulla Libertà*, *sviluppo* (development) is not primarily economic or institutional — it is the growth of the human person. Mill places individual development at the center of his moral vision, arguing that the free unfolding of human capacities is both the ultimate justification of liberty and the measure of a society's worth.

## Definizione

Mill inherits and radicalizes Wilhelm von Humboldt's conception of human development. Humboldt appears in the essay's epigraph and recurs throughout as the authority on what development means:

> "Il grande principio dominante, a cui si riconducono tutti gli argomenti svolti in queste pagine, è l'importanza essenziale ed assoluta dello sviluppo umano in tutta la ricchezza della sua varietà." — *Sulla Libertà*, (P03-011, citando Humboldt)

Development, in this sense, is not the acquisition of external goods but the cultivation of internal powers — perception, judgment, feeling, discrimination, and moral preference. It requires diversity because human beings develop along different trajectories; a uniform path of development is a contradiction in terms.

Mill frames development as the criterion by which social arrangements should be judged:

> "Se si considerasse il libero sviluppo dell'individualità come uno dei principali beni essenziali... non vi sarebbe pericolo che la libertà fosse sottovalutata." — *Sulla Libertà*, (003-004)

## Ruolo nell'argomentazione di Mill

Development is the normative foundation of Mill's entire argument. Its role can be mapped across three claims:

**Development as the purpose of liberty**. Liberty is not valuable for its own sake, but because it is the necessary condition of human development. Without freedom of thought and action, the capacities that constitute a fully human life cannot grow:

> "In ragione dello sviluppo della sua individualità, ogni persona diventa più valutabile a sè stessa, ed è perciò capace di essere più valutabile agli altri." — *Sulla Libertà*, (003-016)

**Development requires diversity of conditions**. Since individuals differ, a uniform social environment cannot develop all equally. Different "condizioni pel loro sviluppo intellettuale" (conditions for their intellectual development) are required (003-035). Mill calls these differing conditions "experiments in living" — each person trying out a mode of life suited to their own character and capacities.

**Individuality as the engine of collective development**. Mill closes the loop between individual and social development. Individual development is not opposed to collective progress — it is its precondition:

> "L'individualità è sinonimo di sviluppo, e... solamente la coltura dell'individualità produce, o può produrre, degli esseri umani ben sviluppati." — *Sulla Libertà*, (003-019)

**Development as the answer to stagnation**. Mill's historical argument is that societies stagnate when they suppress individual development. China, in his analysis, became stationary because custom replaced individual initiative. Europe advanced because diversity of character and opinion generated the friction that drives progress.
