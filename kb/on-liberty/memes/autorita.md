# Autorità

In *Sulla Libertà*, *autorità* (authority) is the permanent antagonist of liberty. The entire book is framed around the historical and ongoing struggle between these two forces. Mill traces authority's evolution from despotic rule to the more insidious modern form of social tyranny.

## Definizione

The book opens by defining the central tension:

> "La lotta tra libertà ed autorità è la nota caratteristica di quelle epoche storiche che ci divengono a prima giunta familiari nelle storie greca, romana ed inglese." — *Sulla Libertà*, (001-002)

Historically, authority meant the power of rulers over subjects — "il potere dei governanti come necessario, ma anche come altamente pericoloso" (the power of rulers as necessary but also highly dangerous). The aim of patriots was to set limits on this power:

> "Per questo, scopo dei patrioti era di assegnare dei limiti al potere che i governanti dovessero esercitare sulla collettività: questo essi intendevano per libertà." — *Sulla Libertà*, (001-003)

But Mill's critical insight is that in democratic ages, authority shifts form — from the tyranny of rulers to the tyranny of the majority:

> "La società può eseguire, ed eseguisce essa stessa, i suoi proprî decreti; e, se ne emana di cattivi, o se ne emana a proposito di cose in cui non dovrebbe entrare, essa esercita una tirannia sociale più formidabile di qualunque oppressione legale." — *Sulla Libertà*, (001-006)

## Ruolo nell'argomentazione di Mill

Authority in Mill's argument serves as the **problem** that liberty is the **solution** to. His analysis operates on several levels:

**Historical evolution**: Authority originally meant the power of one ruler, tribe, or caste over the governed — power derived from conquest or succession, not consent. The struggle against this form of authority produced constitutional checks and eventually democratic accountability. But this evolution did not solve the problem — it merely transformed it.

**The democracy paradox**: When rulers are elected and accountable to the people, authority becomes the power of the majority over the minority. "Volontà del popolo significa, praticamente, volontà della parte più numerosa ed attiva del popolo — della maggioranza insomma" (the will of the people means, practically, the will of the most numerous and active part). The people may wish to oppress a part of itself:

> "Di conseguenza, il popolo può desiderar di opprimere una parte di sè stesso, e le precauzioni sono, a questo riguardo, utili altrettanto che contro qualunque altro abuso di potere." — *Sulla Libertà*, (001-005)

**Social vs. political authority**: Beyond formal political power, Mill identifies a more pervasive form of authority — the power of society to enforce its opinions and customs through moral coercion rather than law. This social authority "penetra ben più addentro nei particolari della vita ed incatena l'anima stessa" (penetrates much more deeply into the details of life and chains the soul itself).

**The harm principle as boundary**: Mill's entire argument is an effort to establish where the legitimate boundary of authority lies — not whether authority should exist (it must, for social order), but *which* exercises of authority are justified and *which* constitute illegitimate encroachments on individual liberty.
