# Civil Liberties Concepts

This page indexes concepts related to civil liberties and constitutional protections
of individual rights as addressed in The Federalist Papers. While the original
Constitution did not include a Bill of Rights — a point of sharp Anti-Federalist
criticism — Publius argued at length that the structure of the proposed government
itself provided the surest safeguard of liberty. These entries cover the specific
rights, protections, and guarantees discussed in the ratification debates.
