# Executive Power Concepts

This page indexes concepts related to executive authority, the design of the presidency,
and the theory of energetic administration developed in The Federalist Papers. Alexander
Hamilton's essays on the executive (Nos. 67–77) provide the most sustained treatment of
these themes, arguing for unity, duration, adequate provision, and competent powers in
the executive branch.
