## Definition

Philosophy (*la philosophie*) in the late [[rome|Roma]]n world was both a discipline of rational inquiry and a way of life. By the 4th century, the classical schools — Platonism, Aristotelianism, Stoicism, Epicureanism — had been synthesized into an eclectic [[hell|Hell]]enistic culture.

In *[[thais|Thaïs]]*, philosophy represents the urbane, skeptical alternative to [[paphnuce|Paphnuce]]'s desert faith.

## In the Novel

Philosophy enters the novel through [[nicias|Nicias]], [[paphnuce|Paphnuce]]'s childhood friend turned [[alexandrie|Alexandria]]n *philosophe*. [[nicias|Nicias]] greets [[paphnuce|Paphnuce]] with the amused tolerance of a man who has "étudié la philosophie à [[athens|Athènes]] et à [[alexandrie|Alexandrie]]" — studied philosophy at [[athens|Athens]] and [[alexandrie|Alexandria]] (001-085) — and has concluded that nothing is worth fervent belief.

The banquet scene (Part II) is philosophy's showcase. [[dorion|Dorion]], [[cotta|Cotta]], [[eucrite|Eucrite]], and [[zenothemis|Zénothémis]] debate the nature of virtue and the relationship between beauty and truth. [[nicias|Nicias]] dismisses [[paphnuce|Paphnuce]]'s Christianity as one more sect among many, while [[eucrite|Eucrite]] defends a neo-Platonic theism.

The conversation is erudite, witty, and completely ineffectual. [[nicias|Nicias]] himself confesses "J'aime beaucoup la philosophie et je l'étudie à mes heures de loisir" — I love philosophy and study it in my leisure hours (002-280). Philosophy, as France presents it, is a beautiful mirror that reflects everything and changes nothing.

[[paphnuce|Paphnuce]] himself, in his moment of deepest crisis, attempts philosophy as a last resort: "il essaya de la philosophie et construisit un raisonnement" — he tried philosophy and constructed a reasoning (003-020). It fails him. Neither faith nor philosophy can fully account for the mystery of human desire and divine grace.

## Philosophical Context

The 4th-century [[alexandrie|Alexandria]]n intellectual milieu was shaped by the legacy of Philo, Plotinus, and the Catechetical School. Neo-Platonism dominated philosophical discourse, emphasizing the ascent of the soul toward the One.

France, writing in the aftermath of Renan and the 19th-century crisis of faith, uses this historical setting to stage a perennial tension between rational inquiry and revealed truth.
