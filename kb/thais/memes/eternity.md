## Definition

Eternity (*l'éternité*) in Christian eschatology is the timeless state of existence after death, in which the soul experiences either beatitude in the presence of God or damnation in separation from Him. In *[[thais|Thaïs]]*, eternity functions as the ultimate referent that gives weight to every earthly decision — what one does in time determines one's fate in eternity — yet the novel complicates this by suggesting that eternity may be found, or lost, in a single moment.

## In the Novel

Eternity is introduced early as a threat. [[paphnuce|Paphnuce]] warns [[thais|Thaïs]] that if she does not repent, "les démons la tourmenteront durant l'éternité" (001-021) — the demons will torment her for eternity. The prospect of endless suffering is the lever he uses to pry her from her life of pleasure. He asks her bluntly: "ne désires-tu pas vivre dans l'éternité?" (001-065) — do you not desire to live in eternity? The question is rhetorical in his mind; for him, eternity is the only thing worth living for.

Yet the novel introduces a counter-current. [[nicias|Nicias]], the Epicurean, describes the philosopher's truth as a perfume that "embaume et parfume pour l'éternité tout ce qu'il pénètre" (002-192) — embalms and perfumes for eternity whatever it penetrates. This is not the Christian eternity of the anchorites but a philosophical eternity, achieved through wisdom and pleasure rather than renunciation.

The novel's most devastating moment on this theme comes at the end, when [[paphnuce|Paphnuce]] realizes that "l'éternité bienheureuse était dans un seul baiser" (003-268) — blessed eternity was in a single kiss. The ascetic who traded all earthly pleasures for the promise of eternal reward discovers that eternity was available in the very thing he renounced. His final cry is not one of hope but of rage: he wishes to exhale "l'éternité de rage qui est en moi" (003-274) — the eternity of rage that is in me. Eternity, for him, has become not beatitude but infinite fury.

## Key Passages

> "...les démons la tourmenteront durant l'éternité." [001-021]

> *"...the demons will torment her for eternity."*

> "...ne désires-tu pas vivre dans l'éternité? Mais, dis-moi, n'habites-tu pas dans la mort?" [001-065]

> *"...do you not desire to live in eternity? But tell me, do you not dwell in death?"*

> *"How did I not feel that blessed eternity was in a single kiss?"*

> *"...so as to exhale the eternity of rage that is in me."*
