The Sutton family are neighbors and friends of the Tellwrights in Bursley. The family consists of Alderman Sutton, a potter's agent, and Mrs. Sutton, who appears in scenes of domestic social life throughout the novel.

## Members

- **[[alderman-sutton|Alderman Sutton]]** — A potter's agent and civic figure in Bursley
- **[[mrs-sutton|Mrs. Sutton]]** — His wife, a prominent woman in Bursley Wesleyan society

## Role in the Novel

The Suttons represent the respectable middle class of the Five Towns — comfortable but not wealthy, integrated into the Wesleyan community, and concerned with proper social observance. They serve as a benchmark of normal Bursley domesticity against which the more complex Tellwright household can be measured.

Mrs. Sutton appears in several key social scenes, including the sewing meeting at the chapel and various domestic gatherings. Her relationship with the Tellwright sisters, particularly Agnes, adds texture to the novel's portrait of neighborhood and community life.
