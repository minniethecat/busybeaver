## Overview

Bethesda Chapel is a large Wesleyan Methodist place of worship in Hanbridge, one of the Five Towns. It serves as a central hub of Wesleyan activity in the district, with Ephraim Tellwright among its prominent lay preachers and financial officers. The chapel is described as "the big Bethesda chapel," indicating its size and importance within the Wesleyan circuit [225-P225].

## Role in the Wesleyan Circuit

Bethesda Chapel was part of the broader Wesleyan Methodist circuit that spanned the Five Towns. Ephraim Tellwright served as circuit treasurer for many years and regularly preached and taught at the chapel. His religious activities at Bethesda, however, were performed "as routine, without skill and without enthusiasm," motivated more by the social standing they conferred within the society than by spiritual conviction [225-P225].

The chapel hosted prayer-meetings where Tellwright "grew garrulous with God," and anniversary services that involved the collection and counting of contributions — a financial aspect of chapel life that Tellwright, with his methodical nature, particularly excelled at [225-P225].

## Historical Context

Bethesda Chapel belonged to the Wesleyan Methodist tradition, distinct from the Primitive Methodist movement that had seceded from Wesleyanism in 1808 at Bursley. Tellwright's father had been involved in the conflicts preceding that secession, representing the "Old Guard of Methodism" committed to "pure doctrine" [225-P225].
