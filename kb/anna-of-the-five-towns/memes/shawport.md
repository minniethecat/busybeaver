Shawport is a district of the Five Towns notable as the site of the railway station from which the Sunday-School Treat excursion train departs in Chapter 9 of *Anna of the Five Towns*.

## Role in the Novel

The Sunday-School Treat begins with the gathering of children and teachers at Shawport station, where a special train is hired to transport the 250 participants to their excursion destination (009-118). The station scene provides a lively opening to one of the novel's major set-piece events.

Shawport also appears in Chapter 10, when Anna and Mynors begin their journey to the Isle of Man. The station serves as a point of departure — a liminal space marking transitions between the familiar world of the Five Towns and the wider world beyond.

## Context

In the railway age, stations like Shawport were the gateways through which the industrial population of the Potteries accessed holiday destinations. The special excursion trains organized for Sunday-School treats and works outings were a distinctive feature of Victorian working-class and lower-middle-class culture, representing one of the few opportunities for travel and leisure available to the district's inhabitants.
