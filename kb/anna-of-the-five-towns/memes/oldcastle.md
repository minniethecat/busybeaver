## Overview

Oldcastle is a town near the Five Towns in Staffordshire. It lies outside the "five contiguous towns" but is connected to the region through family and commercial ties. Anna Tellwright's maternal grandfather was a wood merchant at Oldcastle, and it was from Oldcastle that her mother came when she married Ephraim Tellwright [223-P223].

## Family Connections

Ephraim Tellwright, at the age of thirty-five, "suddenly married, without any perceptible public wooing, the daughter of a wood merchant at Oldcastle" [223-P223]. Shortly after the marriage, Anna's mother inherited a sum of eighteen thousand pounds from her father — a significant fortune that augmented Tellwright's already considerable wealth. Anna's mother is described as "a rosy-cheeked, very unassuming and simple woman, who smiled easily and talked with difficulty" [223-P223].

## Geography

Oldcastle is situated near Hanbridge, with the small settlement of Pireford lying "between Hanbridge and Oldcastle" [223-P223]. Oldcastle Street in Bursley — mentioned during the Suttons' departure for the Isle of Man — likely took its name from the road leading towards Oldcastle [1937-P1937].
