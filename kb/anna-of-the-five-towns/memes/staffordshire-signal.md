The Staffordshire Signal (commonly referred to as "The Signal") is the local newspaper serving the Five Towns district. It appears throughout *Anna of the Five Towns*, functioning as the primary source of commercial news, market reports, and community announcements for the potteries region.

## Role in the Novel

The Signal first appears in the opening chapters as Ephraim Tellwright's source for financial intelligence. He "read the Signal daily, and knew the commercial history of every stock and share quoted therein" (001-048). The newspaper's market reports directly inform Tellwright's investment decisions and, by extension, his management of Anna's inheritance.

Throughout the novel, the Signal serves multiple narrative functions:

- **Economic intelligence** — publishing pottery market reports, stock prices, and commercial notices
- **Social register** — reporting on community events such as the school treat and revival meetings
- **Legal notices** — publishing official announcements including the inquest verdict on Titus Price
- **Plot catalyst** — its reports on land and property transactions influence character actions

## Significance

The Signal represents the penetration of modern commercial journalism into the industrial Midlands. Its presence in the novel underscores the interconnectedness of the Five Towns economy and the importance of information flow in a rapidly industrializing society. For Tellwright, the newspaper is an essential business tool; for the wider community, it is the chronicle of shared civic life.
