The North Staffordshire Railway is one of the principal companies in which Anna Tellwright holds shares through her inheritance from her maternal grandfather. It appears as part of the detailed enumeration of Anna's financial holdings when Ephraim Tellwright reveals the extent of her fortune.

## Holdings

Anna inherits stock in the North Staffordshire Railway as part of her portfolio, alongside shares in other regional enterprises including the Five Towns Waterworks, Norris's Brewery, and the Toft End Colliery and Brickworks (003-039). The railway stock represents a stable, infrastructure-based investment typical of Victorian prudent portfolio management.

## Historical Basis

The company is based on the historical North Staffordshire Railway (NSR), which operated from 1845 to 1923, serving the Potteries region with its hub at Stoke-on-Trent. The NSR was informally known as \"The Knotty\" due to the knot-shaped Staffordshire emblem and the complex network of lines connecting the industrial towns. Its inclusion in the novel reflects Arnold Bennett's careful attention to the economic realities of the region he chronicled.
