# Men's Bible Class

A weekly Bible-study class for men, held in the Wesleyan lecture-hall on Sunday afternoons and conducted by Henry Mynors. The Bible-class is introduced early in the novel as one of the institutions that structure the religious and social life of Bursley's Methodist community.

## Role in the Community

The Bible-class is a distinct organisation from the Sunday-school proper — it serves adult men rather than children, and its purpose is doctrinal instruction and religious fellowship rather than basic literacy and moral education. Mynors's role as "conductor of the men's Bible-class held in the lecture-hall on Sunday afternoons" [001-004] is listed among his credentials as a leading figure in the chapel community.

## Henry Mynors as Conductor

Mynors's conductorship of the Bible-class is one of several positions — along with his role as Sunday-school superintendent and his prominence in chapel financial affairs — that establish him as a model of Wesleyan lay leadership. The Bible-class represents the serious, adult side of Mynors's religious commitment, complementing his more publicly visible role in the Sunday-school and his practical work on chapel finances.

For Mynors, the Bible-class is both a genuine expression of religious conviction and a platform for social influence. It brings him into regular contact with the men of the congregation, reinforcing his position as a figure of authority and respectability within the Wesleyan community.
