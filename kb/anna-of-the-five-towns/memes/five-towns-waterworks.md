The Five Towns Waterworks is the utility company responsible for supplying water to the Potteries district. It is one of the enterprises in which Anna Tellwright holds shares through her inheritance from her maternal grandfather.

## Holdings

Ephraim Tellwright enumerates Anna's Waterworks shares alongside her other investments: the North Staffordshire Railway, Norris's Brewery, and the Toft End Colliery and Brickworks (003-041). As a public utility, the Waterworks represents a conservative, yield-focused investment typical of the prudent Victorian portfolio management practiced by Anna's grandfather.

## Context

In late 19th century industrial England, municipal and regional water companies were essential infrastructure enterprises. The Five Towns, with their dense concentration of pottery manufactories requiring large quantities of water for clay preparation, would have been particularly dependent on reliable water supply. The Waterworks thus represents not merely a financial asset but a stake in the fundamental infrastructure of the Potteries economy.
