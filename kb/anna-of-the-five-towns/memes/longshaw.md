## Overview

Longshaw is one of the five contiguous towns of the Staffordshire Potteries. It sits at the eastern end of the "single winding thoroughfare some eight miles in length" that unites the Five Towns in the valley [139-P139]. Like its sister towns, Longshaw is an industrial community, marked by the "vaporous poison" of ovens and chimneys that Bennett describes as characteristic of the region [139-P139].

## Industry

Longshaw is associated with china manufacture. At the betrothal tea for Anna Tellwright and Henry Mynors at the Sutton household, the table "glittered with silver, glass, and Longshaw china" — an indication that the town was known for its ceramic production, consistent with the broader Potteries industry [2923-P2923].

## In the Novel

Longshaw appears only in passing references. Its primary significance is as part of the geographic backdrop of the Five Towns and as the source of the fine china used at the Suttons' celebratory tea.
