The Toft End Colliery and Brickworks is an industrial enterprise combining coal mining and brick manufacturing in the Five Towns district. It is one of the most significant holdings in Anna Tellwright's inheritance portfolio.

## Holdings and Description

Ephraim Tellwright details Anna's investment in the company alongside her other inherited shares (003-035). The dual nature of the enterprise — both extractive (colliery) and manufacturing (brickworks) — reflects the vertically integrated industrial economy of the Potteries, where coal from local mines directly fueled the kilns and furnaces of the district's manufacturing concerns.

## Industrial Context

Collieries and brickworks were fundamental to the Potteries economy. Coal powered the pottery kilns; bricks built the expanding towns. The Toft End operation, combining both functions, represents the kind of integrated industrial enterprise that characterized the rapidly developing Staffordshire coalfield in the late 19th century.
