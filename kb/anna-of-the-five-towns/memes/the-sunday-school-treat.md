The Sunday-School Treat is the annual excursion for the children of the Bursley Wesleyan Sunday-School, a major event in the social calendar of the Five Towns Methodist community. The treat occupies most of Chapter 9 and serves as the backdrop for crucial narrative developments.

## The Excursion

The treat involves a special train hired to transport some 250 children and their teachers from Shawport station to an excursion destination. The day includes games, food, and festivities, with the entire Sunday-School community participating in the organization and supervision.

## Narrative Function

The Sunday-School Treat serves multiple narrative purposes:

- **Social Panorama** — The event brings together all strata of Bursley Wesleyan society, from the wealthy superintendent Henry Mynors to the humblest Sunday-School scholar, offering a comprehensive portrait of the community
- **Romantic Development** — Anna and Mynors's relationship advances amid the pastoral setting of the treat, with their interactions observed and commented upon by the community
- **Dramatic Exit** — The treat provides the occasion for the dramatic departure of Titus Price and his son Willie, whose financial troubles have reached a crisis point

The contrast between the festive, innocent atmosphere of the children's treat and the dark undercurrent of the Prices' ruin exemplifies Bennett's skill at layering social comedy with genuine pathos.
