The Wesleyan Methodist Society of Bursley is the central religious institution in *Anna of the Five Towns*. It encompasses the chapel, Sunday-school, Bible classes, and the broader community of Wesleyan Methodists in the town.

## Role in the Novel

The Society is the primary social and religious framework for most of the novel's characters. Henry Mynors serves as the morning superintendent of the Sunday-school and conductor of the men's Bible-class. Ephraim Tellwright is a former local preacher within the Society. The chapel on Duck Bank serves as the Society's physical home.

The Society's activities — Sunday services, class meetings, revival services, sewing meetings, the annual school treat — structure the social calendar of the novel's characters. Through the Society, Bennett portrays the central role of Methodism in shaping the moral, social, and communal life of the Victorian Potteries.
