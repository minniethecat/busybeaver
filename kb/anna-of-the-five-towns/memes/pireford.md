## Overview

Pireford is a small village situated "between Hanbridge and Oldcastle" in Staffordshire, near the Five Towns. It was the residence of Ephraim Tellwright and his wife during their early married life, before Anna was born. The couple "lived narrowly in a small house up at Pireford" [223-P223].

## The Tellwright Residence

Ephraim Tellwright and his wife chose Pireford as their marital home, living there in relative seclusion. "They visited no one, and were never seen together except on Sundays," reflecting Tellwright's reclusive and miserly character [223-P223]. It was here that Anna's mother spent her married life, described as living "a servile life of satisfaction and content" despite the couple's substantial combined fortune of thirty-eight thousand pounds [223-P223].

The Tellwrights later moved, as Anna was born elsewhere, and Mrs. Tellwright died of erysipelas when Anna was five years old.

## Geography

Pireford lies on the road between Hanbridge (a Five Towns centre) and Oldcastle (a nearby market town). Its position outside the main Five Towns conurbation is consistent with Tellwright's preference for isolation.
