# Toft End

A "mean and struggling suburb" of Bursley [012-081], located on the heights above the coalpits about half a mile east of Bleakridge [003-099]. Toft End is closely associated with the Price family — Titus Price's house is here — and it provides the setting for one of the novel's most emotionally charged scenes in Chapter 12.

## Location and Character

Toft End lies on the eastern fringe of Bursley, accessed by a road that "climbed up to Toft End, a mean annex of the town about half a mile east of Bleakridge" [003-099]. It is a struggling working-class suburb, "flung up the hillside" in a manner that suggests hasty and unplanned development [012-081]. The area is defined by its proximity to the coalpits, with the Toft End Colliery and Brickworks Limited being a major local employer [003-035].

## Significance in the Novel

### The Price Family Connection

Titus Price's house is located in Toft End. The house "belonged to Mr. Price, but was mortgaged up to the hilt" [012-070] — a detail that encapsulates the Prices' financial precariousness. After the embezzlement scandal breaks and Titus Price's suicide, Anna makes a determined visit to the Price house at Toft End, walking "as if walking in her sleep" [012-080].

### Industrial Landscape

Toft End represents the industrial reality of the Five Towns — not the genteel respectability of Hillport or the commercial bustle of central Bursley, but the working-class margin where collieries and brickworks dominate the landscape and define the lives of their inhabitants. It is one of the few places in the novel where Bennett takes the reader into a distinctly working-class environment.

## Notable Events

- Anna visits the Price house at Toft End after the embezzlement scandal, walking up alone in a state of near-dissociation [012-080].
- The house, though owned by Price, is revealed to be deeply mortgaged — a concrete symbol of the family's hidden financial ruin [012-070].
