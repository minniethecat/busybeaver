# Bursley Wesleyan Chapel

The Wesleyan Chapel in Bursley is a central place of Methodist worship, situated on the hill leading up to the churchyard.

## Overview

The Bursley Wesleyan Chapel serves as a focal point for the Methodist community in the Five Towns. Methodism is woven deeply into the social and moral fabric of Bursley life, and the chapel features in key moments of Anna Tellwright's experience[PN=010-088].

## Location

The chapel is located on the hill leading to the churchyard in Bursley[PN=010-088]. This positioning places it prominently in the town's landscape, linking the commercial lower town with the higher ground of the churchyard.

## Significance in the Novel

The chapel represents the institutional face of Wesleyan Methodism in the Five Towns. It is attended by characters from across the social spectrum and serves as a venue for moral instruction and community gathering. The decision that Anna and other characters "should attend the Wesleyan Chapel on the hill" reflects the social expectation and moral imperative of chapel attendance that prevails in the Potteries[PN=010-088].
