## Overview

Knype is one of the five contiguous towns that make up the Potteries district of Staffordshire. It lies along the "single winding thoroughfare some eight miles in length" that connects all Five Towns through the valley [139-P139]. Like its neighbours, Knype is a manufacturing town, characterised by the "sombre, hard-featured, uncouth" aspect typical of the industrial Potteries landscape [139-P139].

## Geography

Knype sits in the valley between Hanbridge and Longshaw, forming part of the continuous urban sprawl that Bennett describes as "inundating the valley like a succession of great lakes" [139-P139]. The town is smaller than Hanbridge (the largest of the Five Towns) and less historically significant than Bursley (the "mother" town).

## In the Novel

Unlike Bursley and Hanbridge, Knype is not a primary setting for the novel's events. It is mentioned only in the opening descriptive passage (Chapter 1) as part of Bennett's atmospheric introduction to the Five Towns, establishing the industrial landscape that shapes the characters' lives.
