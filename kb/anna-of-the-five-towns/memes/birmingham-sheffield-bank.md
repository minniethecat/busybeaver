The Birmingham, Sheffield & District Bank is a regional banking institution serving the industrial Midlands and Yorkshire. It is the bank chosen by Ephraim Tellwright for Anna to open an account to manage her inheritance of fifty thousand pounds.

## Role in the Novel

After Anna comes of age and her fortune transfers to her control, Tellwright takes her to the bank to establish her account (003-083). The scene underscores Anna's transition from financial dependence to formal economic agency, though in practice her father continues to manage her affairs.

The bank represents the formal financial infrastructure of Victorian England — the institutional machinery through which the industrial wealth of the Potteries was stored, transferred, and multiplied. For Anna, the visit to the bank is an initiation into the adult world of commerce, though she remains largely passive in her own financial affairs.
