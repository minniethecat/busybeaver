Mrs. Banks is the wife of the Reverend Banks, the Wesleyan minister in Bursley. She is noted for her independent fortune, her striking violet eyes, and her influential position within the Wesleyan social hierarchy.

## Character and Position

Mrs. Banks is described as \"a lady of independent fortune\" who married the minister not out of necessity but choice. Her wealth gives her a degree of independence unusual for a minister's wife, and she exercises considerable social influence within the Bursley Wesleyan community (007-052).

## Role in the Novel

Mrs. Banks appears in several key social scenes, including the sewing meeting at the chapel and various gatherings that define the social world of Bursley Methodism. She serves as a foil to Anna — both are women of means within the Wesleyan community, but Mrs. Banks has the confidence and social ease that Anna lacks.

Her presence highlights the complex intersection of wealth, piety, and social status in Victorian Methodist culture. As a woman of independent means married to a clergyman, she occupies a distinctive position that reflects the growing role of wealthy laywomen in the organizational life of Nonconformist congregations.
