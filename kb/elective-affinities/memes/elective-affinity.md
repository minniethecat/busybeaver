# Elective Affinity

## Definition

**Elective Affinity** (*Wahlverwandtschaft*) is the central metaphor of Goethe's novel.
The term originates in chemistry, where it describes the tendency of chemical substances
to selectively combine with or separate from one another. Goethe extends this scientific
concept into the human sphere, using it to explore how people are drawn together and
driven apart by forces beyond rational control.

The chemical analogy was well-known in Goethe's time: certain substances, when brought
into contact, abandon their existing partners to form new combinations — a phenomenon
chemists called "elective affinity" because the substances appeared to *choose* their
preferred partners. (004-044)

## In the Novel

The term is introduced in [[Chapter-4]] during an evening conversation among the four
central characters — Eduard, Charlotte, the Hauptmann (Captain), and Ottilie. The
Hauptmann explains the chemistry of affinities, and the characters gradually recognize
themselves in the metaphor: Eduard as A, bound to Charlotte (B); the Captain as C; and
Ottilie as D. When C enters the scene, A is drawn away from B — just as one chemical
substance displaces another. (004-056)

Charlotte herself observes that the language of "choice" and "elective affinity" may be
too generous when applied to human relationships, since chemical reactions follow
necessity rather than will. (004-049) Yet the metaphor proves prophetic: the four-way
reconfiguration it describes unfolds exactly as the chemistry predicts.

## Chemical Metaphor

The double displacement reaction — `AB + CD → AC + BD` — serves as the novel's
structural template. In chemical terms, the Hauptmann explains:

> Bring a piece of limestone into dilute sulfuric acid: the acid seizes the lime
> and appears with it as gypsum, while the delicate, airy acid escapes. Here a
> separation has occurred, a new composition has arisen, and one feels justified
> in using the word *Wahlverwandtschaft*, because it truly appears as if one
> relationship is preferred to another, one chosen over another.
> (004-044)

The metaphor is not merely decorative — it determines the plot. Each character is a
chemical "element" with specific affinities, and the novel traces what happens when
these elements are brought into proximity. (004-051)

## Key Passages

> Charlotte reflected: "The human being is elevated so many levels above those
> elements, and if he has been somewhat generous with the fine words *Wahl* and
> *Wahlverwandtschaft*, he does well to return into himself and consider the
> value of such expressions on this occasion."
> (004-049)

> "In this letting-go and seizing, in this fleeing and seeking, one truly
> believes one sees a higher determination; one credits such beings with a kind
> of willing and choosing, and holds the term *Wahlverwandtschaften* to be
> completely justified."
> (004-051)

> Charlotte said: "I consider it fortunate that we have come together so
> completely today, and that these natural and elective affinities have
> accelerated a confidential communication among us."
> (004-058)
