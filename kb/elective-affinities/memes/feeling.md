# Feeling

## Definition

**Feeling** (*Gefühl*) is the novel's most pervasive inner faculty — the immediate,
pre-rational awareness through which characters register attraction, foreboding,
triumph, guilt, and belonging. Unlike [[Neigung|inclination]], which names a
specific directional pull, *Gefühl* is the broader medium of affective
experience: it is what Charlotte consults before making a decision, what Ottilie
rests upon when reason fails her, and what Eduard surrenders to when passion
overwhelms restraint. Goethe treats feeling not as a romantic indulgence but as
an epistemological problem — characters feel truly but interpret poorly, and the
novel's tragedy unfolds in the gap between authentic feeling and faulty
judgment.

## In the Novel

The word *Gefühl* appears from the novel's opening pages, where Eduard invokes
feeling as a counterweight to rational planning:

> „daß diesem Vorhaben mein Gefühl widerspricht, daß eine Ahnung
> mir sagt, es werde nicht gut tun"
> (001-036)

Eduard's feeling opposes Charlotte's plan to invite the Captain and Ottilie —
and his feeling proves prescient, yet he overrules it. This pattern recurs: the
novel's characters feel accurately but act against their own affective
knowledge.

Charlotte consults her inner feeling after the child's death and finds it a
stabilizing force:

> Sie half Charlotten ihr inneres Gefühl hinweg. Sie war sich ihres
> ernsten Vorsatzes bewußt
> (013-017)

Ottilie's moral purity is expressed through feeling rather than reasoning. When
accused, she is sustained by the feeling of her innocence:

> Ottilie, getragen durch das Gefühl ihrer Unschuld, auf dem Wege
> zu dem erwünschtesten Glück, lebt nur für Eduard.
> (013-020)

Eduard's surrender to passion is likewise narrated as a feeling:

> daß er sich im stillen dem Gefühl seiner Leidenschaft ganz
> überließ
> (018-003)

The novel's domestic world is also textured by feeling — the pleasant feeling of
doing something for another, the domestic feeling disturbed by intrusion, the
painful feeling of ignorance:

> Bei dem angenehmen Gefühle, daß sie für ihn etwas tue
> (012-007)

> doch ward dieses häusliche Gefühl einigermaßen gestört
> (009-026)

> peinlich war ihr das Gefühl der Unwissenheit, das ihr aufstieg
> (017-021)

## Chemical Metaphor

In the novel's central chemical analogy, feeling corresponds to the *sensible
quality* of an element — the perceptible sign that an elective affinity is
active. Just as a chemist observes a precipitate forming and infers a chemical
bond, Goethe's characters register feeling as the phenomenological evidence of
attraction. But the analogy also contains a warning: the chemist does not trust
appearance alone but confirms through experiment. The characters, by contrast,
treat feeling as self-validating — and are repeatedly deceived.

## Related Characters

- [[Eduard]] — the character most governed by feeling and least capable of
  interrogating it; his feelings are genuine but his interpretations are
  catastrophic
- [[Charlotte]] — consults feeling but subordinates it to duty; her inner
  feeling is a resource she draws upon without surrendering to it
- [[Ottilie]] — her feeling of innocence becomes the foundation of her moral
  authority; she is the only character whose feeling and action never diverge
- [[der Hauptmann|the Captain]] — notably *absent* from the discourse of
  feeling; his rationality is the novel's counterpoint to Eduard's
  impulsiveness
