## Definition

The journey (*Reise*) in *Elective Affinities* functions as a structural and symbolic motif. In the narrative economy of the novel, journeys mark transitions between states — departures signal the breaking of an existing configuration, while arrivals introduce new energies.

Goethe employs the journey not merely as a plot device for moving characters through space, but as a metaphor for the psychological and relational transformations that define the novel's central drama.

## In the Novel

The novel's plot is set in motion by a journey not taken — the Captain, invited to the estate, arrives instead of remaining at a distance. This arrival disrupts the equilibrium between Eduard and Charlotte.

The Count and Baroness visit, bringing worldly perspectives on marriage. Eduard departs after growing tension, removing himself to a military encampment. Ottilie's planned journey back to the pension is repeatedly deferred.

The journey motif reaches its symbolic peak in Eduard's return after the tragedy of the child's death. The journey outward and the journey inward form a dialectical pair mirroring the chemical metaphor of attraction and repulsion.

Journeys in the novel are also sites of decision. The *Spaziergang* (walk, promenade) functions as a miniature journey. These smaller movements condense the larger pattern: every departure carries the seed of transformation.
