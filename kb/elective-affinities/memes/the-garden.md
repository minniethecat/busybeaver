# The Garden

## Description

**The Garden** (*Garten*) is a significant location in Goethe's
*Elective Affinities*. Situated within the country estate of Eduard and
Charlotte, the garden serves as both physical setting and symbolic space for
key developments in the narrative. (001-033), (017-015), (017-019), (017-020)

## In the Novel

The garden appears across multiple chapters as the characters
undertake estate improvements, engage in conversation, and experience
pivotal emotional moments. The physical transformation of the garden mirrors
the relational changes among the four central characters.

## Significance

The novel uses the garden to embody Goethe's interest in the relationship
between human design and natural forces — a theme that parallels the
chemical metaphor of elective affinities.
