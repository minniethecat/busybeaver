# The Baptism

## Overview

The **Baptism** (*Taufe*) of the child in Goethe's *Elective Affinities*
is a ceremonial event that takes place after the child's birth. The baptism
serves as a moment of symbolic naming: the child receives the name *Otto*,
derived from both parents' names, binding the four central characters in a
shared identity that the novel's tragic logic will not sustain.

## Context

The baptism occurs in the novel's second half, following [[edwards-departure|Eduard's
departure]] and during the period of separation between the
central characters. The child, whose conception occurred during a moment of
imaginative infidelity, carries the doubled name of both fathers — a biological
echo of the crossed affections that define the novel's emotional geometry.

## Description

The baptism is arranged with [[Mittler]] serving as godparent:

> Mittler sollten das Kind als Taufzeugen halten. Der alte Geistliche, der es
> getauft hatte, stand noch immer an der Seite.
> (026-018)

The old clergyman who performed the baptism remains present — a figure of
religious tradition amid the surrounding emotional turbulence. The ceremony is
conducted without delay, as the narrative presses forward:

> Die Taufe sollte nicht lange aufgeschoben werden.
> (026-015)

The child is given the name *Otto*, drawn from the shared name of both Eduard
and the Captain:

> hatte man ihm dann in der Taufe ihren beiderseitigen Namen Otto gegeben.
> (031-002)

The name *Otto* — common to both [[Eduard]] and [[The Captain]] — makes the
child a living synthesis of the two men whose relationships with [[Charlotte]]
and [[Ottilie]] form the novel's central geometry.

## Consequences

The baptism marks the child's formal entry into the community, but the
ceremony's symbolic weight lies in the naming. The shared name *Otto* encodes
the crossed affections that produced the child and foreshadows the tragedy to
come. The double naming — two fathers, one name — is the novel's genetic code
in miniature.
