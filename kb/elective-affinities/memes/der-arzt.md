## Overview

Der Arzt is the physician who appears at critical moments in the novel, attending to illness and death. Though his appearances are brief, his presence marks the novel's most consequential scenes.

## Biography

The doctor is first referenced in Chapter 4, when Charlotte remarks on the limits of medical intervention, noting that a famous doctor has done more harm than good (004-010). The doctor himself appears later, attending to the characters in moments of crisis.

In Chapter 24, during the preparations for the child's birthday celebration, the doctor moves among the guests, playing his role in the social-medical scene (024-001). His most significant appearance comes in Chapter 36, where he attends the dying [[Ottilie]]. He works alongside Nanny, examining the pale, heavenly childlike face of the unconscious girl (036-017). When Nanny is overcome with emotion, the doctor scolds her harshly, demanding practical assistance rather than displays of grief (036-021). Later, he is summoned again for a final observation of Ottilie's condition (036-043).

## Relationships

- **[[Ottilie]]** — The doctor attends her in her final illness, examining her with clinical detachment (036-017).
- **[[Nanny]]** — The doctor scolds Nanny for her emotional outburst during the crisis, demanding practical help (036-021).
- **[[Eduard]]** — The doctor is called by Eduard for the final observation (036-043).

## Role in the Novel

The doctor represents the rational, scientific perspective in the novel's emotional landscape. Where other characters are swept up in passion, grief, and renunciation, the doctor maintains clinical composure — scolding Nanny for her tears, observing Ottilie's condition with professional detachment. His presence at the deathbed underscores the novel's movement from the "chemical" experimentation of human relationships toward their inescapable physical consequences.
