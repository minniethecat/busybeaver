## Definition

**Guilt** (*Schuld*) in *Elective Affinities* is not a simple matter of
wrongdoing and punishment.

Goethe distributes guilt across all four central characters with deliberate
asymmetry: Charlotte bears a quiet, acknowledged guilt; Eduard deflects it;
the Captain absents himself; and Ottilie transforms it into a radical ethical
stance.

The German *Schuld* carries the double meaning of "guilt" and "debt."
This resonance allows the novel to depict guilt as a relational obligation
that can never be fully discharged.

## In the Novel

From the opening chapters, guilt is framed as something to be avoided rather
than confronted:

> … wir uns dabei Verdienst oder Schuld sonderlich zurechnen dürfen.
> (002-039)

This disavowal establishes the novel's moral epistemology: guilt is real but
diffused, impossible to isolate in any single agent.

Charlotte is the character most honestly engaged with her own culpability:

> Charlotte war nicht ganz ohne Schuld, nicht ganz rein von allem Um…
> (010-029)

After the child's death, her internal accounting intensifies. She entertains
the thought that she alone bears all guilt:

> … zu denken, daß sie allein alle Schuld habe, und ohne sich durch die…
> (024-005)

She feels herself Eduard's debtor. The German word *Schuldnerin* is rooted
in the same *Schuld*:

> … und fühlte sich als seine Schuldnerin. Nicht wohl konnte sie i…
> (024-015)

Eduard's relationship to guilt is more evasive. He reframes it as cosmic
debt rather than finite fault:

> Es ist eine unendliche Schuld, die nur durch die Ewigkeit a…
> (009-045)

The word *unendliche* (infinite) is key: by elevating guilt into the
infinite, Eduard releases himself from the duty of earthly reckoning.

Ottilie's final speech contains the novel's most compressed ethical
statement:

> … können, da ich unschuldig die Schuld an allem bin! Durch meine Zud…
> (030-017)

She declares herself innocent yet simultaneously responsible for everything.
This paradox is the moral climax of the novel: total responsibility claimed
alongside total innocence, dissolving ordinary blame into sainthood.
