## Definition

**Image** (*Bild*) in *Elective Affinities* encompasses the portrait, the
landscape view, the living tableau, and the mental picture. The German *Bild*
is richer than its English counterpart: it carries the root of *Bildung*
(formation, education, culture), linking the making of images to the making of
selves.

Goethe uses images as thresholds between presence and absence. An image holds
the trace of what is no longer there; it makes the absent present, but only as
representation. This ambiguous power — the power to preserve and the power to
substitute — runs through every appearance of the term.

## In the Novel

The novel's first reference to *Bild* is architectural and aesthetic:

> …und Fenster die verschiedenen Bilder, welche die Landschaft glei…
> (001-009)

The windows frame the various pictures — *die verschiedenen Bilder* — which the
landscape resembles. The landscape is already an image, already framed. Nature
is perceived through the conventions of pictorial representation.

Ottilie carries the most charged image in the novel — a miniature portrait of
her father:

> …iniaturbild tragen. Es ist das Bild Ihres Vaters, des braven Mann…
> (007-012)

The miniature portrait — *Miniaturbild* — is a physical object, small enough to
wear. But its meaning exceeds its size: it connects Ottilie to her dead father,
to her past, to her identity.

The friend's response to the portrait marks a threshold of intimacy:

> Der Freund wagte nicht, das Bild an seine Lippen zu drücken, a…
> (007-013)

The friend dared not press the image to his lips — *das Bild an seine Lippen zu
drücken*. The image is a surrogate for the person, and the gesture of kissing
it is an act of displaced affection.

Later, at a moment of crisis, Ottilie offers her father's portrait to Charlotte:

> …e Kette vom Halse, an der das Bild ihres Vaters gehangen hatte, …
> (009-015)

The chain from her neck on which her father's picture had hung — *das Bild ihres
Vaters*. The release of the picture is a release of identity, a gesture of
self-renunciation.

The mental image haunts as powerfully as the physical one. Eduard, separated
from Ottilie, is visited by her image in dream:

> … jetzt erst erscheint mir ihr Bild im Traum, als wenn sie mir sa…
> (018-009)

Her image appears to me in dream — *ihr Bild im Traum*. The dream image is
more vivid than the waking recollection, but it is unstable:

> …r sogleich verändert sich ihr Bild, ihr schönes, rundes, himmlis…
> (018-010)

Immediately her image changes — *verändert sich ihr Bild*. The mental image
eludes fixation; it shifts and transforms, refusing to settle into a stable
representation.

The novel's most extended meditation on images comes through the living
tableaux (*tableaux vivants*) staged by Luciane and the architect. These are
images made of living bodies:

> …n, die aber, weil sie auf die Bilder selbst durchgezeichnet ware…
> (020-006)

The sheets traced over the pictures themselves — *auf die Bilder selbst*. The
living tableau is an image of an image, a representation at two removes.

The architect reflects on the ontological status of the image:

> …ins ist von der Bedeutung des Bildes. Die Unterhaltung mit einem…
> (020-015)

The meaning of the image — *die Bedeutung des Bildes* — is that it preserves
what a living conversation cannot. The image endures; the person passes.

A portrait, the architect continues, is more than a likeness:

> …rtigen Menschen als mit einem Bilde. Er braucht nicht zu spreche…
> (020-016)

One can converse with a worthy person as with an image — *als mit einem Bilde*.
The image does not need to speak. Its silence is its virtue.

The living tableaux culminate in Ottilie's appearance as the Virgin in a
Nativity scene, a moment the novel frames with the language of formation:

> …men, wie es gegeben wird, ist Bildung.
> (022-039)

What is taken as it is given is formation — *Bildung*. The word is the
root of *Bild*. To receive an image is to be formed by it.

The novel's final reflection on images comes through the mirror:

> …piegel, in welchem jeder sein Bild zeigt.
> (023-052)

A mirror in which each person shows their image — *sein Bild zeigt*. The mirror
image is the most honest and the most evanescent of all images: it shows what
is, and it vanishes when the viewer turns away.
