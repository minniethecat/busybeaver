# Duty

## Definition

**Duty** (*Pflicht*) is the counterforce to [[Neigung|inclination]] in Goethe's
*Elective Affinities*. Where inclination draws characters toward one another
with the force of a natural law, duty pulls them back toward social order,
marital obligation, and moral responsibility. The novel does not present duty
as merely repressive — it is also the structure that holds lives together — but
it traces with precision the moment when duty becomes hollow, when it is
performed without conviction and therefore ceases to protect anyone.

## In the Novel

Duty is most strongly associated with [[Charlotte]], who embodies the
conscientious performance of social and domestic obligations:

> als daß sie jeden Tag ihre Pflicht tat.
> (026-013)

Charlotte's daily performance of duty — managing the estate, maintaining
appearances, honoring her marriage — is the stabilizing force in the novel's
first half. Yet Goethe shows that duty performed without love becomes a form of
endurance rather than virtue:

> Er fühlte sich von allen Pflichten losgesprochen.
> (013-011)

[[Eduard]]'s declaration that he feels "released from all duties" marks the
novel's ethical fault line. For Eduard, the discovery of elective affinity
invalidates earlier commitments — the new bond cancels the old. For Charlotte,
duty persists regardless of feeling. This divergence is the novel's central
moral argument, and Goethe refuses to resolve it in either character's favor.

The novel also explores duty in its social dimension — the obligations of
hospitality, of professional role, of religious observance:

> versah also die doppelte Pflicht eines guten Kapellmeisters
> (002-043)

> es ward ihnen angenehmen Pflicht gemacht, wenigstens jeden Sonntag
> (009-025)

These smaller duties form the fabric of ordered life, and the novel's tragedy
is in part the unraveling of that fabric when inclination overwhelms it.

## Key Passages

> Er fühlte sich von allen Pflichten losgesprochen.
> (013-011)

> als daß sie jeden Tag ihre Pflicht tat.
> (026-013)

> was Verhältnisse, was Pflicht anzuraten und zu gebieten, ja was unabänderlich
> zu sein schien.
> (028-038)

## Related Characters

- [[Charlotte]] — the novel's primary embodiment of duty; her conscientiousness is both her strength and her limitation
- [[Eduard]] — rejects duty in favor of inclination; his "release from all duties" is the novel's ethical crisis
- [[Ottilie]] — transforms duty into *Entsagung* (renunciation), a saintly self-denial that exceeds mere obligation
- [[Mittler]] — the mediator who enforces social duty with comic rigidity
