## Definition

**Chance** (*Zufall*) in *Elective Affinities* is never merely random. Goethe
systematically blurs the boundary between accident and fate, between what
"falls to" a character (*zu-fallen*) and what is destined. The novel's
characters constantly wrestle with the question: was this *Zufall* or was it
meant?

The German *Zufall* literally means "that which falls to one," from *zu*
(to) + *Fall* (fall, case). This etymology matters: a *Zufall* is not an
abstract probability but a concrete event that befalls a specific person.
The novel's treatment of chance is deeply anti-statistical — it is interested
in how particular accidents shape particular lives.

## In the Novel

The novel's most symbolically charged *Zufall* occurs at the foundation-stone
ceremony. A glass engraved with the entwined initials E and O — made for Eduard
in his youth — is thrown into the air and caught by a workman:

> Dort hinauf flog das Glas und wurde von einem aufgefangen, der diesen Zufall als ein glückliches Zeichen für sich ansah.
> (009-020)

The workman immediately reinterprets chance as omen. This is the novel's
paradigm for how characters process *Zufall*: the random becomes meaningful
the moment it is received.

In the aphoristic interlude of Chapter 22, the narrator reflects on how chance
governs social life:

> Wir befinden uns nicht leicht in großer Gesellschaft, ohne zu denken, der Zufall, der so viele zusammenbringt, solle uns auch unsre Freunde herbeiführen.
> (022-036)

We project our wishes onto chance, expecting it to serve us. The aphorism is
gentle but devastating: chance has no intentions, yet we cannot stop treating it
as if it did.

Luciane's charitable interventions are described with wry detachment — her
medical treatments succeed or fail purely by chance:

> …eine solche Kur, wie sich vermuten läßt, gelang oder mißlang, wie es der Zufall herbeiführte.
> (024-001)

The word *herbeiführte* (brought about, conducted hither) is precisely chosen:
chance *conducts* outcomes, like a director moving actors on a stage.

The narrator later reflects on the productive potential of misfortune. A broken
wheel on a journey can lead to the most delightful connections:

> Der Reisende bricht unterwegs zu seinem höchsten Verdruß ein Rad und gelangt durch diesen unangenehmen Zufall zu den erfreulichsten Bekanntschaften und Verbindungen, die auf sein ganzes Leben Einfluß haben.
> (028-003)

This is Goethe's deepest statement on chance: it is the unexpected detour that
delivers more than the intended route. *Das Schicksal gewährt uns unsre
Wünsche, aber auf seine Weise* — fate grants our wishes, but in its own way.

Eduard's long speech in Chapter 30 contains the novel's most explicit
repudiation of chance as a governing principle. To rely on chance would be
criminal self-deception:

> …daß der Zufall uns leiten und begünstigen solle, so wäre dies ein sträflicher Selbstbetrug.
> (030-017)

He rejects passivity — waiting for *Zufall* to resolve matters — in favor of
deliberate action. Yet his "deliberate action" is itself driven by forces he
does not fully understand.

The novel's final treatment of chance is Charlotte's anguished reinterpretation
of the child's death. What seemed like the most terrible accident becomes, in
her accounting, something Ottilie might have been the instrument of:

> …was sie ihm als Werkzeug des wunderbarsten Zufalls geraubt hat?
> (032-011)

*Wunderbarsten Zufalls* — the most wondrous chance. The superlative is
unsettling. Charlotte reframes the accident not as meaningless but as
miraculously meaningful, a *Zufall* so extraordinary it becomes a kind of
revelation.

## Related Characters

- [[Eduard]] — treats chance as something to be overruled by will; his
  rejection of *Zufall* as a governing principle is the prelude to his most
  consequential decision
- [[Charlotte]] — the character who most thoroughly reinterprets chance as
  fate, reframing the child's accidental death as a meaningful event
- [[Ottilie]] — the character whose life is most shaped by *Zufall* and who
  is most silent about what it means
- [[Luciane]] — the character who treats chance with cheerful indifference;
  her charitable interventions succeed or fail by *Zufall* and she barely
  notices
