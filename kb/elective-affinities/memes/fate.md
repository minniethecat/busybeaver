# Fate

## Definition

**Fate** (*Schicksal*) is a central thematic element in Goethe's
*Elective Affinities*. The novel examines this concept through the lives of
its four principal characters — Eduard, Charlotte, the Captain, and Ottilie —
showing how fate shapes, constrains, and transforms human relationships. (001-031), (010-006), (012-019), (012-021)

## In the Novel

The characters' discussions in [[Chapter-4]] establish the chemical metaphor
that frames the entire work, while later chapters show the consequences when
abstract principles collide with human desires. The novel portrays fate not
as a simple moral absolute but as a force interacting with individual will,
social convention, and natural impulse.
