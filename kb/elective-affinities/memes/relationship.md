## Definition

*Verhältnis* — translated as relationship, proportion, or relation — is arguably the master concept of Goethe's "Elective Affinities." The term bridges the novel's two domains: in chemistry, it denotes the proportional relation between elements in a compound; in social life, it names the bonds between persons.

The semantic range of *Verhältnis* allows Goethe to move seamlessly between scientific and human registers. A chemical *Verhältnis* is governed by laws of affinity and proportion; a human *Verhältnis* aspires to similar stability but is vulnerable to the same forces of displacement.

The title of the novel — *Die Wahlverwandtschaften* — names the elective character that distinguishes certain affinities from mere mechanical relations.

## In the Novel

From the opening pages, *Verhältnis* sets the terms of the drama. Eduard complains that "none of the relationships suits him" (001-024), and the narrator notes that "a hundred other relationships spring from it" (001-030). The word recurs throughout, each occurrence layering chemical, social, and emotional meaning.

The Captain's chemical demonstration in Chapter 4 provides the theoretical key. Using letter-notation to "express the relationship" (004-055) between substances, he shows how elements combine, separate, and recombine — a model that maps directly onto the four main characters.

Charlotte recognizes the danger early, observing that "such new relationships can be fruitful in happiness or in disaster" (002-039). Her insight proves prophetic as the novel traces the dissolution and reformation of relationships with the precision of a chemical equation.
