## Overview

The Monkeys' Farewell is the grand celebration held by [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] of [[africa-expedition|Africa]] to honor [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]] before his departure from the [[land-of-the-monkeys|Land of the Monkeys]]. It marks the conclusion of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s mission to cure [[chee-chee|the monkey]] sickness and the beginning of his return [[travel-and-exploration|journey]] home. The event is immortalized by a massive stone monument that still marks the spot.

## The Party

"when the packing was finished and everything was ready to start, [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] gave a grand party for [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]], and all the animals of the jungle came" ((010-029)). The feast was lavish: "they had pineapples and mangoes and honey and all sorts of good things to eat and drink" ((010-029)).

## The Doctor's Speech

After the meal, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] rose to address the assembled animals. His speech was characteristically humble and practical: "My friends: I am not clever at speaking long words after dinner, like some men; and I have just eaten many fruits and much honey" ((010-031)).

His advice was veterinarian in nature — reflecting his deep care for the animals' wellbeing: "remember never to let the flies settle on your food before you eat it; and do not sleep on the ground when the rains are coming" ((010-031)). He concluded simply: "I hope you will all live happily ever after" ((010-031)).

## The Monkeys' Response

The monkeys were deeply moved. "When [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] stopped speaking and sat down, all [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] clapped their hands a long time and said to one another, 'Let it be remembered always among our people that he sat and ate with us, here, under the trees. For surely he is the Greatest of Men!'" ((010-032)).

## The Stone Monument

The Grand Gorilla, possessed of "the strength of seven horses in his hairy arms," performed the most dramatic act of the farewell: he "rolled a great rock up to the head of the table and said, 'This stone for all time shall mark the spot'" ((010-033)–(010-034)).

> And the Grand Gorilla, who had the strength of seven horses in his hairy arms, rolled a great rock up to the head of the table and said, "This stone for all time shall mark the spot."
>
> (010-033) (010-034)

The monument endures in monkey lore: "And even to this day, in the heart of the jungle, that stone still is there. And monkey-mothers, passing through the forest with their families, still point down at it from the branches and whisper to their children, 'Sh! There it is — look — where the Good White Man sat and ate food with us in the Year of the Great Sickness!'" ((010-035)).

## Aftermath

After the party, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] and his pets departed for the seashore, accompanied by "all [[chee-chee|the monkeys]]" who "went with him as far as the edge of their country, carrying his trunk and bags, to see him off" ((010-036)).
