# Too-Too the Listener

## Summary

Aboard the pirates' captured [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]], the animals discovered a locked door that none could open. [[too-too|Too-Too the owl]], using his astonishingly acute hearing, listened through the keyhole and described in precise detail the movements of a person inside — from a hand going into a pocket to a tear falling on a sleeve. His extraordinary listening revealed a frightened little boy, the pirate captain's nephew, imprisoned in the rum-room.

## Background: Too-Too's Keen Ears

[[too-too|Too-Too]]'s exceptional hearing had been demonstrated before. On a cold December night back in [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]], it was [[too-too|Too-Too]] who first detected the sound of someone running outside — [[chee-chee|the monkey]] [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] bringing news of [[chee-chee|the monkey]]s' sickness in [[africa-expedition|Africa]] (004-001, 004-002). His ears, attuned to frequencies and volumes imperceptible to other creatures, made him [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s most reliable sentinel.

## The Locked Door

After [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] and his pets set sail in the pirates' swift [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] with three red sails, they explored their new vessel and found it luxuriously appointed — silk beds, silver dishes, and a great store of plundered treasures (016-004, 016-005). Amid their discoveries, they came upon a small, mysterious door that would not open.

[[jip-the-dog|Jip]] peered through the keyhole but saw nothing — something had been stood against the wall inside (016-007). While the others puzzled over how to enter, [[too-too|Too-Too the owl]] suddenly spoke up.

> "Sh! — Listen! — I do believe there's someone in there!" (016-009)

The Doctor heard nothing and was skeptical, but [[too-too|Too-Too]] insisted. Pressing his ear to the door, [[too-too|the owl]] began to describe, in astonishing detail, what he could hear on the other side:

> "I hear the noise of someone putting his hand in his pocket," said the owl. (016-014)

The Doctor protested that such an action made hardly any sound, but [[too-too|Too-Too]] explained that "almost everything makes *some* noise — if your ears are only sharp enough to catch it. Bats can hear a mole walking in his tunnel underground" (016-016). The owl's auditory world was richer and more detailed than any human could comprehend.

## Reading the Prisoner

Lifted to the keyhole, [[too-too|Too-Too]] continued his remarkable acoustic diagnosis. He could tell the prisoner was rubbing his face with his left hand — a small hand against a small face. When the prisoner pushed his hair back, [[too-too|Too-Too]] knew it was a man: women's long hair made "quite a different sound" when pushed back (016-021, 016-022, 016-023). Each observation was a deduction from the faintest of noises.

Then came the most delicate observation of all. After listening long and hard, [[too-too|Too-Too]] looked up and announced:

> "The man in there is unhappy. He weeps. He has taken care not to blubber or sniffle, lest we should find out that he is crying. But I heard — quite distinctly — the sound of a tear falling on his sleeve." (016-026)

When [[gub-gub|Gub-Gub]] skeptically suggested it might have been a drop of water from the ceiling, [[too-too|Too-Too]] dismissed the idea with contempt — a drop of water would have made "ten times as much noise" (016-028). The distinction between a tear and a water drop, inaudible to any other ear, was unmistakable to [[too-too|the owl]].

## Rescue

The Doctor, moved by the report of an unhappy prisoner, called for an axe and chopped through the door (016-029, 017-001). Inside the dark, barrel-filled rum-room, they found a frightened little boy — the son of a [[the-fisherman|fisherman]], the pirate captain's nephew — who had been captured with his uncle while fishing in a little boat and left aboard when the pirates fled (017-003, 017-011).

The boy was trembling in the darkness, uncertain whether his rescuers were friend or foe. Seeing [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s kind face, he asked, "You aren't one of the pirates, are you?" When [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] laughed warmly, the boy smiled and took his hand, saying, "You laugh like a friend — not like a pirate" (017-007, 017-009). That single laugh, genuine and warm, told the boy everything he needed to know about the man who had freed him.

The boy explained that pirates had captured both him and his uncle two days earlier, sinking their little fishing boat. When the pirates fled [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s party, they had left him locked in the rum-room with only barrels for company (017-011, 017-012).

## Outcome

[[too-too|Too-Too]]'s extraordinary hearing not only saved the boy from his dark prison but also set in motion the search for the boy's missing uncle — a [[the-fisherman|fisherman]] lost on some distant shore — which would become the next great challenge for [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s party. The episode stands as the most celebrated demonstration of [[too-too|Too-Too]]'s auditory prowess, proving that the quietest sound — a tear on a sleeve — can reveal the greatest truths when heard by the right ears.
