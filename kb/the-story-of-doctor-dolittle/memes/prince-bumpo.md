# Prince Bumpo

> The Prince of Jolliginki is not like his father. While the King imprisons Doctor Dolittle, Prince Bumpo secretly dreams of fairy-tales — of princes who kiss sleeping princesses, of white knights and heroic rescues. In the darkness of the prison, he becomes Dolittle's unlikely ally.

## Role in the Story

Prince Bumpo is the son of [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]] and [[queen-ermintrude|Queen Ermintrude]] of [[jolliginki|Jolliginki]]. He is first mentioned as being away fishing on the day of [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]]'s arrival at the palace — a detail that underscores his distance from his father's suspicious and autocratic rule. (006-002)

Unlike his father, who sees [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]] as a foreign threat to be imprisoned, Bumpo is fascinated by [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s powers. He secretly visits Dolittle in the prison by moonlight and strikes a bargain: he will help Dolittle and his animals escape if [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] can use his [[medicine-and-magic|medicine]] to transform Bumpo into a fairy-tale prince — a white prince in shining armor who might one day awaken a sleeping princess with a kiss. Dolittle agrees, though he privately doubts that his medical skills can achieve so fantastical a result. (012-007)(012-009)

> She heard Prince Bumpo snoring in his bedroom at the back of the palace. (006-019)

## Dreams of a White Prince

Prince Bumpo's deepest longing is not for political power or [[money-and-wealth|wealth]], but for the romantic transformation promised by European fairy-tales. He yearns to become a *white prince* — a figure out of the storybooks he has somehow acquired in this remote [[africa-expedition|Africa]]n kingdom. Walking alone in the palace garden at night, he murmurs to himself:

> "If I were only a white prince!" said he, with a dreamy, far-away look in his eyes. (011-025)

This wish is the engine of the entire escape plot. When [[polynesia|Polynesia]] and the other animals overhear Bumpo's soliloquy in the garden, they recognize an opportunity: here is a man who can be persuaded to help them, not through [[money-and-wealth|gold]] or threats, but through the promise of a fairy-tale makeover. (011-021)(011-032)

The Doctor, ever the pragmatist, is momentarily taken aback by the request. Changing a man's skin color is not, strictly speaking, a medical procedure — it belongs more to the realm of [[medicine-and-magic|magic]] than [[medicine-and-magic|medicine]]. Yet Dolittle, rifling through his little black bag of bottles and powders, finds a solution: a concoction that, when applied, will turn Bumpo's face as white as snow. The transformation is performed in the dead of night, and Bumpo, overjoyed, hands over the keys to the prison. (012-011)(012-024)

> "White Man, I am an unhappy prince. Years ago I went in search of a Sleeping Beauty, but I could not find one, because I am a black prince." (012-008)

## Key Appearances

Prince Bumpo appears primarily in chapters 6 and 11–12. He is mentioned first as being away fishing when Dolittle arrives at [[jolliginki|Jolliginki]] (006-002), and his snoring is heard from the back of the palace (006-019). His most significant role comes in chapter 12, when he visits Dolittle in the prison camp by moonlight and negotiates the escape bargain that becomes one of the book's most memorable subplots.

Bumpo's character represents the tension between the [[jolliginki|Jolliginki]] royal court's hostility toward outsiders and the genuine curiosity and romantic idealism of its younger generation. His love of fairy-tales — specifically, the European fairy-tale motif of the prince who kisses a sleeping princess awake — adds a layer of gentle humor to the narrative.
