# The Rock

> "Jip! Jip! I see a great, great rock in front of us — look — way out there where the sky and the water meet. See the sun shine on it — like gold!" (019-024)

## Description

The Rock is a large, barren rock formation in the ocean, described as being as large as a big field. It has no trees, no grass, nothing growing on it — it is as smooth and bare as the back of a tortoise. There is no house on the rock, but it contains a hole or cave where a person can take shelter. (019-027)

The rock is located far out at sea, so far that even from the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]]'s mast, it only appears where the sky and water meet. It is isolated and devoid of life — not even a gull, starfish, or seaweed can be found on its surface. (019-029) The party aboard [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]]'s [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] sails completely around it, calling out until their voices grow hoarse, but hears only the hollow echo of their own shouts bouncing off the bare stone. (019-030)(019-031)

## Role in the Story

The [[barbary-coast|Barbary]] Dragon — [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]], the notorious pirate — used this rock as a prison. When the red-haired [[the-fisherman|fisherman]] refused to join his pirate crew, the Dragon put him ashore on this desolate rock and left him there to die. The [[the-fisherman|fisherman]] survived for four days with no food and no water, sustaining himself on nothing but snuff from his snuffbox and sleeping in a hole in the rock because there was no house. (020-004)

> "For four days I have had nothing to eat or drink. I have lived on snuff." (020-006)

The discovery of the [[the-fisherman|fisherman]] is one of the most dramatic moments in the novel. [[too-too-the-listener|Too-Too the owl]], stationed at the masthead, first spots the rock glowing [[money-and-wealth|gold]]en in the sunset. (019-024) [[jip-the-dog|Jip the dog]], following the faint scent of [[black-rappee|Black Rappee]] snuff carried on the West wind, confirms that this is their destination. The party searches the entire circumference, finding no one, until at last the [[the-fisherman|fisherman]] emerges from his hole — weak, starving, but alive.

## The Fisherman's Ordeal

The red-haired [[the-fisherman|fisherman]]'s four days on the rock were a test of endurance. With no spring for fresh water, no vegetation for food, and no shelter beyond a hollow in the stone, he subsisted on the one comfort he had left: a snuffbox of [[black-rappee|Black Rappee]]. It was this very snuff — its distinctive scent drifting across miles of open ocean — that enabled [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] to track him. (019-008)(019-011)

When rescued, the [[the-fisherman|fisherman]] is emaciated but grateful. He and his sister later offer [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] a handsome reward, but Dolittle — characteristically — refuses payment, accepting only the gift of a watch. (020-017) The [[the-fisherman|fisherman]]'s rescue demonstrates the moral economy of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s world: a life saved is its own reward.

## Discovery

[[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]], guided by [[jip-the-dog|Jip]]'s extraordinary sense of smell, sailed to the rock following the scent of [[black-rappee|Black Rappee]] snuff carried by the West wind. (019-008)(019-011) The owl [[too-too-the-listener|Too-Too]] first spotted the rock from the mast, describing it as shining like [[money-and-wealth|gold]] in the sunset. (019-024)

After searching the entire circumference of the rock and finding no visible sign of life, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] and his animals called out until their voices were hoarse, but only echoes returned. (019-030)(019-031)

The [[the-fisherman|fisherman]] was eventually found in a hole on the rock, from which he had been unable to escape. (020-004)

## Significance

The Rock represents the isolation and cruelty of the [[barbary-coast|Barbary Dragon]]'s piracy. It also serves as the setting for one of [[jip-the-dog|Jip]]'s greatest feats of tracking — locating a missing person by smell alone across the vast ocean — demonstrating the extraordinary capabilities of [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]]'s animal companions. The rescue also marks the defeat of [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]] and the liberation of his prisoners, closing one of the novel's most perilous episodes.
