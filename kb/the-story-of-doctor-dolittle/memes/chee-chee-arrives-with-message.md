## Overview

[[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]]'s arrival with a message from [[africa-expedition|Africa]] is the catalytic event that launches [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]]'s great [[voyage-and-sailing|voyage]]. On a bitter winter night, [[chee-chee|the monkey]] rushes in with news delivered by a swallow: a terrible sickness is devastating [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] of [[africa-expedition|Africa]], and they beg [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] to come and save them.

## A Cold December Night

The event occurs during "a very cold" winter, on a December night when the animals are gathered around the kitchen fire and [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] is reading aloud from books he had written in animal-language ((004-001)). The domestic scene is interrupted by [[too-too|Too-Too the owl]]: "Sh! What's that noise outside?" ((004-002)).

## The Arrival

"The door flew open and [[chee-chee|the monkey]], [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]], ran in, badly out of breath" ((004-003)). [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] delivers the dire news: "Doctor! I've just had a message from a cousin of mine in [[africa-expedition|Africa]]. There is a terrible sickness among [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] out there. They are all catching it — and they are dying in hundreds. They have heard of you, and beg you to come to [[africa-expedition|Africa]] to stop the sickness" ((004-004)).

## The Swallow Messenger

The message was brought by "a swallow" who had [[travel-and-exploration|traveled]] from [[africa-expedition|Africa]] despite the winter cold — "[[the-swallow-messenger|the swallows]] flew South six weeks ago" ((004-006)–(004-007)). The swallow is initially "all huddled and shivering" but warms up on the edge of the mantelpiece and tells the full story ((004-008)).

## Immediate Consequences

The Doctor is immediately willing to go: "I would gladly go to [[africa-expedition|Africa]] — especially in this bitter weather" ((004-010)). However, the practical obstacle of [[money-and-wealth|money]] arises immediately. When [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] retrieves the [[money-and-wealth|money-box]], it is empty — the last twopence had been spent "on a rattle for that badger's baby when he was teething" ((004-012)–(004-014)).

Undeterred, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] resolves to borrow a boat from a sailor whose baby he had cured of measles, setting in train the preparations for the great [[travel-and-exploration|journey]] ((004-015)–(004-016)).

## Significance

This event is the inciting incident of the [[africa-expedition|Africa]] storyline. It introduces [[the-swallow-messenger|the swallow]] messenger system that [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] will use throughout the book, establishes [[chee-chee|the monkeys]]' faith in [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s abilities, and sets the entire plot in motion. The image of [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] bursting through the door on a cold night, carrying news from another continent, captures the [[medicine-and-magic|magical]] interconnectedness that defines [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s world.
