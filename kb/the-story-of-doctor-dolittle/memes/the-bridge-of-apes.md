# The Bridge of Apes

> The King's army is closing in. Doctor Dolittle and his animals are trapped at the edge of a deep gorge — the Land of the Monkeys on one side, the soldiers on the other. Then Chee-Chee shouts a command, and the impossible happens: a living bridge of monkey bodies stretches across the chasm.

## Summary

The Bridge of Apes is the climactic set-piece of the [[africa-expedition|African voyage]]. Having escaped from [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]] of [[jolliginki|Jolliginki]]'s prison, [[john-dolittle|John Dolittle]] and his animals race toward the safety of monkey territory. But a deep river gorge blocks their path. As [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]]'s soldiers close in, [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] form a living bridge — bodies linked from tree to tree across the ravine — and Dolittle crosses to safety just as the pursuers arrive.

## Context

After being imprisoned by [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]] of [[jolliginki|Jolliginki]] (who distrusts the foreign "[[medicine-and-magic|medicine]]-man") and briefly escaping through trickery, Dolittle and his animals are pursued by [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]]'s army. The [[land-of-the-monkeys|Land of the Monkeys]] lies beyond a deep river gorge. With the soldiers approaching, there is no time to find a normal crossing point.

> Chee-Chee looked down at the river and cried: "Boys — a bridge! Quick! — Make a bridge!" (007-031)

## Narrative

The monkeys respond instantly to [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]]'s command. Hundreds of them link hands and feet, forming a living chain from one side of the gorge to the other. Dolittle and his animals cross this bridge — a terrifying passage at dizzying height:

> John Dolittle was the last to cross. And just as he was getting to the other side, the King's men came rushing up. (007-036)

The bridge is pulled across to [[chee-chee|the monkey]] side the moment Dolittle reaches safety, stranding the pursuers on the far bank. The escape is complete — and [[chee-chee|the monkeys]]' loyalty to the animal-doctor who came to heal them is proven in the most dramatic fashion possible.

## Outcome & Consequences

The Bridge of Apes saves Dolittle's life and allows him to reach the [[land-of-the-monkeys|Land of the Monkeys]], where he treats the disease that had been devastating [[chee-chee|the monkey]] population. The event cements the bond between Dolittle and [[chee-chee|the monkeys]], who later gift him the pushmi-pullyu in gratitude. It also represents the moment when the animal kingdom collectively repays Dolittle's kindness — not with words but with bodies, stretched across a chasm to save the one man who had crossed an ocean to save them.
