# Black Rappee

Black Rappee is a dark variety of snuff tobacco — a finely ground nasal tobacco popular in the 19th century. In [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] Dolittle narrative, it serves a dual role: as [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s everyday habit and as the crucial olfactory signature that enables one of the book's most remarkable tracking feats.

## Doctor Dolittle's Snuff

The Doctor is a habitual snuff-taker. In the [[the-fisherman|fisherman]]'s cave on the rock, after the man realizes who has come to rescue him, "he gave [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] a pinch of snuff" — a gesture of friend[[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] and relief ((020-003)). Earlier, when [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] first begins tracking, he specifically asks about the uncle's habits, knowing that distinctive personal scents would be his key to identification: "Has your uncle any peculiar marks or brand on him?... Has he any peculiar smells?" ((018-028)).

## The Key to Jip's Tracking

Black Rappee plays its most dramatic role in the search for the missing [[the-fisherman|fisherman]]. After the eagles fail, [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] asks the boy if his uncle had anything distinctive about him. When the boy produces a red handkerchief, [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] immediately identifies its scent:

> "Snuff, by Jingo! — Black Rappee snuff. Don't you smell it? His uncle took snuff — Ask him, Doctor." ((018-029))

The boy confirms his uncle was an enthusiastic snuff-taker: "Yes. My uncle took a lot of snuff" ((018-030)). [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] declares the man "as good as found" and promises to locate him within a week — despite having the entire ocean to search. When [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] protests that it is dark, [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] replies that he "needed no light to track Black Rappee snuff" and declares simply: "Follow that man who smells of Black Rappee snuff" ((018-033)). Darkness, which blinded the eagles, was no obstacle to a nose.

The tracking method is methodical. [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] goes to the bow of the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] and smells the wind itself, working through each compass direction systematically. At first, the Southeast wind disappoints: "I smell no snuff as yet" ((019-003)). Even when the wind shifts, the scent proves elusive — [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] "could not catch the smell of snuff" ((019-004)).

## Across the Ocean by Scent

[[jip-the-dog|Jip]]'s method is to smell the wind itself. The Southeast wind carries the scent of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s own garden in [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]] — which brings tears to [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s eyes — but not a trace of Black Rappee. When the wind shifts to the West, [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] finally catches the scent: "I smell it!... Black Rappee! I'd know it anywhere!" ((019-000)). The confirmation is swift: [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] can tell "the man is still taking Black Rappee snuff" ((019-006)).

Even when the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] encounters regions with competing smells — parsnips and other strong food odors — [[jip-the-dog|Jip]]'s focus never wavers. At one point he dismisses a particularly strong competing scent: "No parsnips whatever. And no snuff — plenty of pipes and cigarettes, but no snuff" ((018-044)). His dedication to the single scent is absolute.

When the search reaches land, the scent trail leads directly to the marooned [[the-fisherman|fisherman]]:

> Jip describes the smell in the West wind: "it smells of nothing but snuff. Come upstairs and start the ship to the West" ((019-009))

Even with the overwhelming concentration of snuff in the air, [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] maintains his certainty: "I can smell no other smell in the West wind but snuff" ((019-016)). He reasons that "if the man were dead, the smell would have stopped" — an intuitive understanding of scent decay that guides the entire rescue.

## The Snuffbox

The final confirmation of the scent trail comes on the rock itself. The Doctor finds the [[the-fisherman|fisherman]] and confirms: [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] "picked it up. It was an enormous snuffbox. And it was full of Black Rappee" ((019-044)). The snuffbox — enormous and full — vindicates [[jip-the-dog|Jip]]'s extraordinary tracking across the open ocean.

## Survival on Snuff

In a grimly comic detail, the [[the-fisherman|fisherman]] reveals that during his four days marooned on the rock with no food or water, "I have lived on snuff" ((020-006)) — making Black Rappee not just the key to finding him, but the thing that sustained him through his ordeal.

## Significance

Black Rappee is a narrative device that connects characters across distance and time. It links [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s English gentleman habits to the [[africa-expedition|Africa]]n [[travel-and-exploration|adventure]], provides the sensory thread for [[jip-the-dog|Jip]]'s tracking, and serves as both a symbol of civilization (the snuff) and of survival against the odds. Most importantly, it demonstrates the book's recurring theme that the so-called "lesser" senses — dismissed by the eagles' aerial vision — can triumph where more obvious methods fail. A humble pinch of snuff becomes the thread that spans an ocean.
