# Polynesia Teaches Animal Language

The discovery of animal language is the single most transformative event in [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle's]] life. What begins as an ordinary rainy afternoon in [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s house on the edge of [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]] becomes the moment that redefines his entire career and purpose.

## The Revelation

One afternoon, while [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] was busy writing in a book, [[polynesia|Polynesia]] sat in the window looking out at the leaves blowing about in the garden. Presently she laughed aloud. When [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] asked what was funny, [[polynesia|Polynesia]] casually delivered a revelation that would change everything (002-003).

> "Did you know that animals can talk?" (002-004)

The Doctor was skeptical at first. [[polynesia|Polynesia]] explained that she, as a parrot, could speak "two languages — people's language and bird-language" (002-005). To demonstrate, she said: "Ka-ka oi-ee, fee-fee?" — which meant "Is the porridge hot yet?" in bird-language (002-006).

## The First Lesson

Convinced that animals indeed had a language of their own, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] became eager to learn. "And all that afternoon, while it was raining, [[polynesia|Polynesia]] sat on the kitchen table giving him bird words to put down in the book" (002-009). This afternoon session marked the beginning of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s study of animal languages.

[[polynesia|Polynesia]] also taught [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] that [[animal-language|animal communication]] went far beyond spoken words: "They talk with their ears, with their feet, with their tails — with everything. Sometimes they don't want to make a noise" (002-012). She demonstrated by pointing out how [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] [[jip-the-dog|the dog]] was twitching one side of his nose — a form of silent speech.

## The Transformation

After a period of study, "with [[polynesia|the parrot]]'s help, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] got to learn the language of the animals so well that he could talk to them himself and understand everything they said" (002-014). This led to a momentous decision: "Then he gave up being a people's doctor altogether" (002-014).

## Consequences

The ability to speak with animals transformed [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s practice. When sick animals were brought to him, "as soon as they found that he could talk their language, they told him where the pain was and how they felt, and of course it was easy for him to cure them" (002-041). News spread rapidly through the animal kingdom, and creatures from far and wide flocked to the little house with the big garden.

This event is the origin of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s unique gift and the foundation upon which all his subsequent [[travel-and-exploration|adventure]]s — including the [[voyage-and-sailing|voyage]] to [[africa-expedition|Africa]] to save [[chee-chee|the monkey]]s — are built.
