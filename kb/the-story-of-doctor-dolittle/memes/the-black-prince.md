# The Black Prince

## Summary

[[prince-bumpo|Prince Bumpo]], the son of [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]] of [[jolliginki|Jolliginki]], discovered European fairy tales and became obsessed with the story of the Sleeping Beauty. Under the moonlight in the palace garden, he read aloud and dreamed of being transformed, not knowing that [[polynesia|Polynesia]] was watching and would later use this knowledge to secure [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s freedom from the palace dungeon (011-021)(011-030).

## Context

[[prince-bumpo|Prince Bumpo]] had returned to his father's palace in [[jolliginki|Jolliginki]] after [[travel-and-exploration|travel]]ing the world in search of the Sleeping Beauty. He had been educated by a European tutor and had developed a taste for romantic literature. Unbeknownst to the prince, [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]] and his animals were at that very moment imprisoned in the palace dungeon, condemned to scrub [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]]'s kitchen floor for the rest of their lives (011-021)(012-008).

## Narrative

"Look! There's [[prince-bumpo|Prince Bumpo]] coming into the garden!" whispered [[polynesia|Polynesia]] from their hiding place (011-021). The prince entered the moonlit garden carrying "a book of fairy tales under his arm" and sat down upon a stone bench, completely absorbed (011-022).

> "He read aloud the story of the Sleeping Beauty, sighing over the romantic passages, entirely unaware that he had an audience (011-022).

[[polynesia|Polynesia]], "keeping very still so [[prince-bumpo|Bumpo]] couldn't see her," listened carefully (011-030). When [[prince-bumpo|Bumpo]] cried out in anguish — "Who can I be turned into a white prince?" — [[polynesia|the parrot]] saw her opportunity. She spoke in a disguised voice, pretending to be the Fairy-Queen from the story, and directed [[prince-bumpo|Bumpo]] to visit [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] in secret that very night (011-031)(011-032).

The ruse worked perfectly. [[prince-bumpo|Bumpo]], "clasping his hands in joy," believed he had been visited by a real fairy and resolved to follow her instructions (011-031).

That night, [[prince-bumpo|Bumpo]] came secretly to [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] in the prison dungeon and poured out his story:

> "Years ago I went in search of The Sleeping Beauty, whom I had read of in a book. And having traveled through the world many days, I at last found her and kissed the lady very gently to awaken her — as the book said I should. 'Tis true indeed that she awoke. But when she saw my face she cried out, 'Oh, he's black!' And she ran away and wouldn't marry me — but went to sleep again somewhere else." (012-008)

The Doctor, ever resourceful, saw the opportunity. After much negotiation — [[prince-bumpo|Bumpo]] must first prepare a [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] with provisions, and promise to free all the animals — [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] mixed a concoction from his [[medicine-and-magic|medicine]] bag, murmuring about "liberated chlorine on animal-pigment — perhaps zinc-ointment, as a temporary measure, spread thick" (012-006)(012-015).

[[prince-bumpo|Bumpo]] dipped his face into the basin and held it there so long that "[[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] seemed to get dreadfully anxious and fidgety, standing first on one leg and then on the other" (012-019). When [[prince-bumpo|the Prince]] finally lifted his face, "all the animals cried out in surprise: for [[prince-bumpo|the Prince]]'s face had turned as white as snow, and his eyes, which had been mud-colored, were a manly gray!" (012-020)(012-021).

## Participants

- **[[prince-bumpo|Prince Bumpo]]** — the king's son, enchanted by fairy tales and desperate to become a white prince
- **[[polynesia|Polynesia]]** — [[polynesia|the parrot]] who impersonates the Fairy-Queen in the garden, orchestrating the entire scheme
- **[[john-dolittle|John Dolittle]]** — imprisoned in the dungeon, who seizes the opportunity to bargain for freedom

## Outcome & Consequences

[[prince-bumpo|Bumpo]] kept his promise. Taking "a bunch of copper keys from his pocket," he unlocked the prison doors and [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] and all his animals ran to the waiting [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] (012-024). [[prince-bumpo|Bumpo]] stood in the moonlight, "smiling after them happily, his big face shining like polished ivory in the light of the moon" (012-024).

The Doctor, however, was troubled. He admitted to his companions that the [[medicine-and-magic|medicine]] "will never last. Most likely he will be as black as ever when he wakes up in the morning" (012-026). [[dab-dab|Dab-Dab]] was unsympathetic — "Serve him right, if he does turn black again! I hope it's a dark black" — but [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]], characteristically generous, noted that [[prince-bumpo|Bumpo]] was not to blame for his father's cruelty: "Still, he had a good heart — romantic, of course — but a good heart. After all, 'handsome is as handsome does'" (012-028)(012-031).

The episode secured [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s escape from [[jolliginki|Jolliginki]] and set the stage for [[the-swallow-messenger|the swallow]]s' escort that would guide the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] homeward across the moonlit sea (012-033)(012-043).
