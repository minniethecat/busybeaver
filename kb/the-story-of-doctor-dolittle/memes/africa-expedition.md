# Africa (Expedition Destination)

> A message arrives from a cousin in Africa: a terrible sickness is killing the monkeys. John Dolittle borrows a boat, packs his medicine-bag, and sails south — into a continent of jungles, kingdoms, and creatures who have never met an animal-doctor before.

## Description

Africa in *The Story of [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]]* is not a single location but a [[travel-and-exploration|journey]]. It encompasses the [[voyage-and-sailing|sea voyage]] from England, the barbary coast, [[jolliginki|the land of the Jolliginki]], the deep jungle where [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] live, and the rocky coastlines where pirates prowl. It is depicted as both dangerous and wondrous — a place where fever strikes, kings imprison, and monkeys build living bridges across gorges.

> "There is a terrible sickness among the monkeys in Africa. Hundreds and hundreds of them are dying." (004-004)

## The Voyage

The [[travel-and-exploration|expedition]] begins in haste. [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]] borrows a boat from a sailor living near [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]] — a sailor who cannot accompany him because of a broken leg — and sets off with his chosen crew: [[jip-the-dog|Jip the dog]], [[dab-dab|Dab-Dab the duck]], [[gub-gub|Gub-Gub the pig]], [[too-too-the-listener|Too-Too the owl]], and, of course, [[polynesia|Polynesia the parrot]]. (004-018) [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] pulls up the [[voyage-and-sailing|anchor]] with his clever hands, and the [[voyage-and-sailing|voyage]] begins. (004-038)

The Atlantic crossing is eventful. [[polynesia|Polynesia]] navigates, reading the stars and the color of the water with the ease of a bird who has crossed oceans before. [[too-too-the-listener|Too-Too]], stationed at the mast, scans the horizon. Dolphins appear alongside the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]], offering gossip and directions, while [[the-swallows-escort|swallows]] fly ahead to announce [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s coming. (005-007)(005-025) The [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] itself is old and battered — one storm reduces its sails to ribbons — but Dolittle, ever resourceful, fashions new sails from blankets. (005-022)

As the coast of Africa rises on the horizon, [[polynesia|Polynesia]] — who was born there nearly two centuries ago — sighs with recognition: "Dear old Africa! It's good to get back. Just the same old palm-trees; same old red earth; same old black ants." (005-025) For [[polynesia|the parrot]], this is a homecoming. For Dolittle, it is the beginning of his greatest [[travel-and-exploration|adventure]].

> "Dear old Africa!" sighed Polynesia. "It's good to get back." (005-025)

## Location & Geography

The [[travel-and-exploration|expedition]] [[travel-and-exploration|travels]] from England southward across the Atlantic, arriving somewhere on the west coast of Africa. The party passes through [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the Kingdom]] of [[jolliginki|Jolliginki]], [[travel-and-exploration|travels]] inland through dense jungle, and eventually reaches [[chee-chee|the monkeys]]' territory — a region with deep gorges and rocky cliffs that only [[chee-chee|the monkeys]]' bridge can cross.

## Story Significance

Africa transforms Dolittle from a shabby [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]] doctor into a figure of legend. It is in Africa that he cures [[chee-chee|the monkey]] epidemic, receives the two-headed pushmi-pullyu as a gift, and is called a "wizard" and "[[medicine-and-magic|medicine]]-man." The African [[travel-and-exploration|expedition]] occupies the central portion of the book (chapters 4–18) and contains all of its major set-pieces.
