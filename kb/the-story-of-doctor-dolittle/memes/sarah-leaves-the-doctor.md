# Sarah Leaves the Doctor

## Summary

When a crocodile came to live in [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]]'s house, his sister [[sarah-dolittle|Sarah]] could bear no more. After a final heated argument — in which [[sarah-dolittle|Sarah]] declared she would leave and go get married — [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] calmly accepted her decision. [[sarah-dolittle|Sarah]] packed up her things and departed, leaving [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] alone with his animal family [003-007–014].

## Context

The Doctor's household had long been a source of friction between brother and sister. [[sarah-dolittle|Sarah Dolittle]] served as housekeeper and watched with growing dismay as her brother filled the house with animals — rabbits in the pantry, white mice in the piano, a squirrel in the linen closet, and a hedgehog in the cellar (001-004). She complained that the animals made the house untidy and drove away paying patients. An old lady with rheumatism, having sat upon the sleeping hedgehog on the sofa, "never came to see him any more" (001-005).

The crisis came when [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] acquired a crocodile. The creature had been given to him by a [[travel-and-exploration|traveling]] circus, where it had been unhappy, and [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] — unable to refuse any animal in distress — took it in on the promise that it would not bite anyone (003-010)(003-011).

## Narrative

[[sarah-dolittle|Sarah]] confronted her brother and demanded that he "send that creature away," insisting that farmers and old ladies were now afraid to bring their animals for treatment "just as we were beginning to be a little better off" (003-007)(003-008). When [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] gently corrected her — "It isn't an alligator, it's a crocodile" — she was unmoved (003-009).

> "I don't care what you call it," said his sister. "It's a nasty thing to find under the bed. I won't have it in the house." (003-010)

The Doctor protested that the crocodile had promised not to bite anyone and that he did not like the circus any more than the crocodile did. He had "no [[money-and-wealth|money]] to send him back to [[africa-expedition|Africa]] where he comes from" and the creature seemed content simply "to lie at the bottom of the pond in the garden" (003-011).

[[sarah-dolittle|Sarah]] was unyielding. "I tell you I *will not* have him around," she said. "He eats the linoleum. If you don't send him away this minute I'll — I'll go and get married!" (003-012)

The Doctor's response was characteristically calm. He simply said: "All right, go and get married. It can't be helped." He took down his hat and went out into the garden (003-013).

## Participants

- **[[sarah-dolittle|Sarah Dolittle]]** — [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s long-suffering sister and housekeeper, who finally reached her breaking point
- **[[john-dolittle|John Dolittle]]** — [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]], who valued the crocodile's welfare over domestic harmony
- **[[the-crocodile|The Crocodile]]** — the immediate cause of the rupture, a gentle creature who merely ate the linoleum

## Outcome & Consequences

[[sarah-dolittle|Sarah Dolittle]] packed up her things and left the house on the edge of [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby-on-the-Marsh]], ending her long tenure as [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s housekeeper (003-014). The Doctor was left "all alone with his animal family" — but he was not truly alone. He had [[dab-dab|Dab-Dab the duck]], [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] [[jip-the-dog|the dog]], [[gub-gub|Gub-Gub the pig]], [[polynesia|Polynesia the parrot]], [[too-too|Too-Too the owl]], the old lame horse, and all the other creatures he had taken in (003-014).

[[sarah-dolittle|Sarah]]'s departure marks a decisive turning point in the story. With his sister gone, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] was free to live entirely on his own terms — surrounded by animals, speaking their languages, and caring for them without domestic interference. But it also meant the loss of the last human presence in his household and the end of any pretense of a conventional [[medicine-and-magic|medical practice]] in [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]].

The Doctor's parting observation about [[sarah-dolittle|Sarah]] — "an excellent woman — in some ways" — was a recollection he would voice weeks later, when the animals' vegetable stall was failing and winter was approaching (003-024). Yet he never expressed regret about his choice. The crocodile stayed, and [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s life with animals continued.
