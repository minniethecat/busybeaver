# John Dolittle

> John Dolittle, M.D. — the genteel English doctor who abandons his human practice when he learns to speak with animals — embarks on a voyage to Africa to save the monkeys, armed with nothing more than a medicine-bag and the friendship of creatures who trust him.

## Role in the Story

John Dolittle is the protagonist of *The Story of Doctor Dolittle*. He begins as a respected people's doctor in [[west-country|the West Country of England]], but his love of animals transforms his life. After [[polynesia|Polynesia]] [[polynesia|the parrot]] teaches him animal language, he transitions from a human physician to the world's foremost animal-doctor — a change that costs him his [[money-and-wealth|wealth]] and social standing but wins him fame across the animal kingdom.

His defining trait is a gentle kindness toward all living creatures. He accumulates an extraordinary household of pets — [[money-and-wealth|gold]]fish, rabbits, white mice, a squirrel, a hedgehog, a cow with a calf, an old lame horse, chickens, pigeons, two lambs, and many others — which drives away his human patients and eventually his sister [[sarah-dolittle|Sarah]]. (001-004)(001-007) Yet he never wavers in his devotion to animals, preferring poverty and ridicule to turning away a creature in need.

> …there was a doctor; and his name was Dolittle—John Dolittle, M.D. (001-001)

## Character and Personality

John Dolittle is defined by his boundless compassion for animals. When presented with a wounded creature — whether a [[chee-chee|monkey]] from [[africa-expedition|Africa]] or a stray hedgehog from the [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]] hedgerows — he never hesitates to offer help, regardless of the cost to himself. This generosity of spirit is both his greatest strength and the source of his worldly troubles: his human patients abandon him, his savings dwindle, and even his sister deserts him in exasperation. (001-006)(001-007)(001-013)

Despite his misfortunes, the Doctor maintains a cheerful, unpretentious nature. He lives simply in his little house with its large garden in [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby-on-the-Marsh]], wearing the same shabby top-hat and carrying his little black [[medicine-and-magic|medicine]]-bag wherever he goes. He speaks to animals as naturally as he speaks to people, and his face lights up at the sight of any new creature. (002-039) [[the-cats-meatman|The Cat's-meatman]], who supplies meat for his many pets, is one of the few humans who understands and supports his unusual vocation.

> "Oh, there are plenty of animal-doctors," said John Dolittle, putting the flowerpots outside. (002-009)

What distinguishes Dolittle from other kind-hearted men is his intellectual curiosity. Learning the language of animals is not a parlor trick for him — it is a genuine scholarly pursuit. He studies parrot-talk, dog-language, and the speech of eagles with the same discipline he once applied to human [[medicine-and-magic|medicine]]. By the end of his apprentice[[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] under [[polynesia|Polynesia]], he can converse fluently with creatures from land, sea, and air — a skill that transforms him from village eccentric into an internationally celebrated figure in the animal world. (002-018)(002-024)(002-044)

## To Learn Animal Language

The turning point of Dolittle's life comes when [[polynesia|Polynesia the parrot]] — who has lived with him for many years — reveals that animals have their own languages and offers to teach him. Starting with the simplest sounds of the alphabet of parrot-talk, the Doctor applies himself with the same diligence he once gave to his medical textbooks. [[polynesia|Polynesia]] corrects his pronunciation: *"That isn't a cough. That was regular parrot-language."* (002-018)

Within a short time, Dolittle has acquired enough vocabulary to converse with birds and beasts alike. [[jip-the-dog|Jip the dog]] becomes his confidant in dog-language, and even the horses and cows in the [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]] lanes understand that here is a man who actually listens. (002-020)(002-024) This new ability marks the end of his human [[medicine-and-magic|medical practice]] but the beginning of a far greater calling — one that will carry him across the sea to [[africa-expedition|Africa]].

> You ought to be an animal-doctor. Give the silly people up — if they haven't brains enough to see you're the best doctor in the world. (002-008)

## Key Appearances

John Dolittle appears in every chapter of the book. His most significant moments span chapters 1–21, from his introduction as a people's doctor with too many pets, through his [[voyage-and-sailing|voyage]] to [[africa-expedition|Africa]], his imprisonment and escape from [[jolliginki|Jolliginki]], to his triumphant return to [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]] with the famous pushmi-pullyu.
