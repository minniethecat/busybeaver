# Doctor's Garden

The Doctor's garden, attached to his small house on the edge of [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby-on-the-Marsh]], is a large, welcoming space that becomes the de facto waiting room and sanctuary for animals from the surrounding countryside. Though the house itself is small, the garden is described as "very large" with "a wide lawn and stone seats and weeping-willows hanging over" (001-005).

> The house he lived in, on the edge of the town, was quite small; but his garden was very large and had a wide lawn and stone seats and weeping-willows hanging over.
>
> (001-005)

## A Sanctuary for Animals

As word spread that there was a doctor in the little house with the big garden who truly understood animals, creatures of all kinds began arriving for treatment. The garden was "nearly always crowded with animals trying to get in to see him" (002-007). The influx was so great that [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] had to install separate doors for different species — "Horses" over the front door, "Cows" over the side door, "Sheep" on the kitchen door, and even a tiny mouse-tunnel into the cellar (002-008).

## The Market Stall

When [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] fell into poverty after [[sarah-dolittle|Sarah Dolittle]] left, the animals took matters into their own hands. They made "a vegetable and flower stall outside the garden-gate" and sold radishes and roses to passersby on the road (003-005). The garden thus became not only a sanctuary but also a source of sustenance for the household.

## A Place of Learning

It was in this garden, with [[polynesia|Polynesia]] looking out at the leaves blowing about, that [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s [[travel-and-exploration|journey]] into animal language began. [[polynesia|Polynesia]] sat in the window observing the garden and the creatures within it, and it was here she first revealed that animals could talk — a discovery that would change [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s life forever (002-003).

## Significance

The garden represents [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s connection to the natural world and his role as a healer who transcends the boundary between human and animal. It is a microcosm of the harmony [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] creates wherever he goes — a place where creatures of all sizes and species gather peacefully.
