# Ben Ali

Ben Ali, who styles himself the Barbary Dragon, is the pirate antagonist [[doctor-dolittle-m-d|Doctor Dolittle]] encounters on the return [[voyage-and-sailing|voyage]] from [[africa-expedition|Africa]]. His reign of terror along the [[barbary-coast|Barbary Coast]] is ended not by naval engagement but by [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s unique approach to justice — converting a feared pirate into a peaceful farmer.

## The Barbary Coast

The Doctor's [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] must pass through dangerous waters:

> …the ship had to pass the coast of Barbary. This coast is the seashore of the Great Desert. (013-001)

The [[barbary-coast|Barbary]] pirates are a constant threat — swift [[voyage-and-sailing|sailing]] [[voyage-and-sailing|ships]] that pursue merchant vessels:

> …they would come out in their fast sailing-ships and chase it. (013-002)

## The Encounter

The pirates intercept [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]], and Ben Ali reveals both his reputation and his temper:

> …no sailor to beat Ben Ali, the Barbary Dragon. (015-005)

His anger is theatrical and volatile:

> The Barbary Dragon turned pale with anger… (015-037)

## Defeat and Transformation

The Doctor's victory over Ben Ali is characteristically unconventional. Rather than killing or imprisoning the pirate, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] offers redemption — a path from violence to honest work:

> …he took the ship away from The Barbary Dragon — and made him into a farmer. (020-019)

The Doctor's final words to Ben Ali encapsulate the theme of transformation:

> …you should never more call yourself 'The Dragon of Barbary.' Now go and be a good farmer. (015-041)

## A Reformed Man's Testimony

After his transformation, Ben Ali's story spreads, becoming part of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s growing legend:

> And the man told how the Barbary Dragon had put him on to this new way of life… (020-004)

The pirate turned farmer becomes a living example of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s philosophy — that even the most fearsome antagonist can be reformed by decency and second chances.

## The Pirate's Moral Choice

Ben Ali's post-defeat behavior reveals a complexity unexpected in a children's villain:

> …stealing was no work for a good fisherman to do. (017-011)

This line, while spoken by [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] about the pirates' former captives, reflects the moral universe Ben Ali must now inhabit — one where honest labor replaces predation.

## Significance

Ben Ali's arc serves as a microcosm of the book's moral vision:

1. **Antagonist redeemed**: A feared pirate becomes a peaceful farmer
2. **Power inverted**: The Doctor defeats not by violence but by moral authority
3. **Legend amplified**: Ben Ali's transformation contributes to [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s worldwide reputation
