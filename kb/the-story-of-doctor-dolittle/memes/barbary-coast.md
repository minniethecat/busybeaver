# Barbary Coast

The Barbary Coast is the treacherous stretch of North [[africa-expedition|African]] shoreline that [[doctor-dolittle-m-d|Doctor Dolittle]]'s [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] must navigate on the return [[voyage-and-sailing|voyage]] from [[africa-expedition|Africa]]. Home to [[ben-ali|Ben Ali, the Barbary Dragon]] and his pirate fleet, this coast represents the perils of the sea — a place where [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s peaceful mission confronts organized violence.

## Geographic Description

The coast is described in terms that emphasize its stark geography:

> …the coast of Barbary. This coast is the seashore of the Great Desert. (013-001)

The image is striking — desert meeting sea, a harsh landscape producing harsh inhabitants. The Barbary pirates are contextualized as products of their environment:

> …they are surely the pirates of Barbary. (013-016)

## The Terrain and the Desert

The Barbary Coast is defined by its position at the edge of the Great Desert — a frontier where the inhabited world gives way to sand and desolation. This geographic reality shapes everything about the region: the pirates who dwell there, the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]]s that dare its waters, and the fear it inspires in sailors who must pass by (013-001).

The coast's isolation makes it a natural haven for pirates. No kingdom polices these shores; no navy patrols the desert edge. The pirates operate with impunity, returning to their strongholds after each raid, "singing songs and feeling proud of themselves" (013-002).

## The Pirate Threat

The pirates of Barbary are swift, aggressive, and joyfully predatory:

> …they would come out in their fast sailing-ships and chase it. When they caught it, they would sink the ship and sail back to Barbary singing songs and feeling proud of themselves. (013-002)

## The Chase

When [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]]'s [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] sails past the Barbary Coast, [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]]'s pirate vessel gives chase. [[dab-dab|Dab-Dab the duck]] is the first to spot the pursuing sail, and [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s crew must rely on every resource at their disposal — swallows to summon wind, sharks to intimidate the pirates — to escape (013-003, 013-021, 013-025).

The chase becomes a test of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s unconventional command. He does not fight with cannon or cutlass but with animal allies, turning the very elements of the sea against the pirates. The swallows, two thousand strong, pull the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] faster than any sail could manage, while the sharks — summoned by [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s authority over the animal world — surround [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]]'s vessel (013-021, 013-025).

## Site of Redemption

The Barbary Coast becomes the site of one of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s most remarkable achievements — the transformation of [[ben-ali|the Barbary Dragon]] from feared pirate to peaceful farmer:

> …he took the ship away from The Barbary Dragon — and made him into a farmer. (020-019)

The Doctor converts the coast's greatest terror into a productive inhabitant. [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]], once "the most terrible pirate that ever sailed the Seven Seas," becomes a farmer — an absurd but genuine transformation that deprives the Barbary Coast of its most feared predator (020-019).

The coast itself is not redeemed — it remains a dangerous place — but one of its terrors is neutralized, suggesting that even the most threatening environments can yield to [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s approach.

## The Aftermath

In the wake of [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]]'s transformation, the people of the Barbary Coast react with a mixture of disbelief and gratitude. When [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] approaches a coastal village, a man mistakes him for [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]] returning and punches him in the dark — only to discover the truth (020-002). Later, the villages send [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] a token of thanks, recognizing that the man who rid them of the pirate is no threat but a benefactor (020-025).

## Significance

The Barbary Coast functions as the story's maritime antagonist — a geographic embodiment of danger that tests [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s courage and philosophy. Unlike [[jolliginki|Jolliginki]], which is political, Barbary is elemental — wind, water, and human predation combined.
