# The Crocodile Incident

## Summary

When a [[travel-and-exploration|travel]]ing circus came to [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]], a crocodile suffering from a severe toothache escaped and sought out [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]] for treatment. The Doctor, true to his compassionate nature, took the creature in. But the crocodile's presence terrified the townspeople, causing [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s human patients to abandon him and deepening his financial troubles. His sister [[sarah-dolittle|Sarah Dolittle]] was furious, and the incident marked a turning point in [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s descent from respected physician to "animal doctor."

## The Circus Escape

A circus had come to the little town of [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby-on-the-Marsh]]. Among its animals was a crocodile suffering from a bad toothache. In pain and desperation, the creature escaped from the circus and made its way to [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]]'s house, instinctively seeking the one man who could understand and help it.

> The circus came to Puddleby, the crocodile who had a bad toothache escaped and came to the Doctor's house. (003-005)

## Aftermath

The arrival of a full-grown crocodile in [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s house proved catastrophic for his [[medicine-and-magic|medical practice]]. The townspeople, already uneasy about the growing menagerie of animals in [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s care, were now thoroughly alarmed. The farmers refused to bring their animals to him, and his human patients stopped coming altogether.

> The farmers wouldn't believe the Doctor could cure their animals because of the crocodile. (003-006)

When his sister [[sarah-dolittle|Sarah]] confronted him about the creature, demanding he send it away, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s response was simple and characteristic: "It's a crocodile" (003-009). He saw only a creature in distress; she saw the final ruin of their household's reputation.

## Outcome

The crocodile stayed. It became one of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s permanent animal companions, [[travel-and-exploration|travel]]ing with him on his great [[travel-and-exploration|journey]] to [[africa-expedition|Africa]] and enjoying the hot tropical sun (005-002). The incident crystallized [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s choice between human society and the animal world — a choice he had already made in his heart long before the crocodile arrived.
