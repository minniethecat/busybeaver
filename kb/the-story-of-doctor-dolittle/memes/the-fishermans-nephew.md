## Overview

The Fisherman's Nephew is a boy of about eight years old who is kidnapped by [[barbary-coast|Barbary]] pirates along with his uncle. [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]] discovers him locked in the rum-room of the captured pirate [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]], and the search for his missing uncle becomes the driving plot of chapters 16 through 20 — the rescue sequence that concludes [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s return [[travel-and-exploration|journey]] from [[africa-expedition|Africa]].

## Discovery in the Rum-Room

After [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] and his animals escape the [[barbary-coast|Barbary]] pirates, they find a locked room on the captured [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]]. While the animals search for the key and discover the pirates' stolen treasures — Kashmiri shawls, Jamaican tobacco, carved ivory boxes — it is [[too-too|Too-Too the owl]] who detects someone inside: "I hear the noise of someone putting his hand in his pocket" (016-009–016-014).

[[too-too|Too-Too]] listens at the keyhole and makes a remarkable series of deductions: the person inside is "rubbing his face with his left hand," has "a small hand and a small face," and is weeping — [[too-too|the owl]] can hear "the sound of a tear falling on his sleeve" (016-021–016-026).

## The Boy's Story

After [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] chops through the door, he finds "a little boy, about eight years old, crying bitterly" seated in the middle of the pirates' rum-room floor (017-003). The boy initially fears he has encountered more pirates, but when [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] "threw back his head and laughed long and loud, the little boy smiled too and came and took his hand" (017-008).

> "You laugh like a friend," he said — "not like a pirate. Could you tell me where my uncle is?"
>
> (017-009)

The boy explains that he and his uncle were fishing in their little boat when the pirates captured them. The pirates wanted his uncle — "clever at [[voyage-and-sailing|sailing]] a [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] in all weathers" — to join them, but he refused because "killing people and stealing was no work for a good [[the-fisherman|fisherman]]" (017-011). [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]] threatened to throw him into the sea, and the next day the uncle was gone.

## The Search

The boy's grief and fear drive [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] to launch a full search operation. The boy provides the crucial description: his uncle has "red hair — very red hair, and the picture of an [[voyage-and-sailing|anchor]] tattooed on his arm. He was a strong man, a kind uncle and the best sailor in the South Atlantic" (017-023).

During the search, the boy rides on the two-headed pushmi-pullyu (017-000). He provides [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] with a [[money-and-wealth|gold]] ring that belonged to his uncle, which [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] uses to pick up the scent (018-024). He also gives [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] the uncle's snuff-stained handkerchief, which proves essential for tracking across the sea (018-000).

## Rescue and Reunion

When [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] finally tracks the [[the-fisherman|fisherman]] to the rock where he was marooned, the boy's joy is overwhelming. "When the animals and the little boy saw [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] and [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] coming back to the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] with a red-headed man, they began to cheer and yell and dance about the boat" (020-009). The swallows joined in, "whistling at the top of their voices — thousands and millions of them" (020-009).

Back at the [[the-fisherman|fisherman]]'s town, the boy's mother — "who was also the man's sister" — had been "sitting on a hill for twenty days, watching the sea and waiting for them to return" (020-014).
