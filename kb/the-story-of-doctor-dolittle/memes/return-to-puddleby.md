# Return to Puddleby

## Summary

In June, when "the hollyhocks were in full bloom," [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]] came back to [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby-on-the-Marsh]] — a rich man after his [[travel-and-exploration|traveling]] circus tour with the pushmi-pullyu. The old lame horse in the stable was glad to see him, and [[the-swallow-messenger|the swallows]] had already built their nests under the eaves of his house. His first acts upon returning were to repay his debts and share his [[money-and-wealth|wealth]] with those who had helped him (021-008)(021-009)(021-011).

## Context

The Doctor had been away for many months. He had sailed to [[africa-expedition|Africa]] to cure [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] of a terrible sickness, been imprisoned by [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]] of [[jolliginki|Jolliginki]], escaped with the help of [[prince-bumpo|Prince Bumpo]], captured the legendary pushmi-pullyu, survived a pirate chase, and finally sailed home in a borrowed [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] (021-001).

But he did not go straight home. First, "he went [[travel-and-exploration|traveling]] through the land with the pushmi-pullyu in a gipsy-wagon," stopping at country fairs and charging sixpence for admission to see the marvelous two-headed creature. So many people came that "very soon [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] was able to give up being a showman," having earned enough to settle all his obligations (021-002)(021-007).

## Narrative

### The Arrival

The Doctor's return came at the height of summer. "March winds had come and gone; April's showers were over; May's buds had opened into flower; and the June sun was shining" when he finally walked down the streets of [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]] (021-001). The townspeople who had once dismissed him as a crank now beheld a man transformed — not by [[medicine-and-magic|medicine]], but by [[travel-and-exploration|adventure]].

The Doctor went directly to his little house on the edge of town. The old lame horse, twenty-five years of age, was in the stable and was "glad to see him" (021-009). The swallows, faithful [[travel-and-exploration|travelers]] themselves, had already anticipated his return, building their nests under the eaves of the roof (021-009). The animals who had remained behind — those who had not accompanied him to [[africa-expedition|Africa]] — welcomed him home.

### Paying Debts

The Doctor immediately set about settling his accounts. He went to see the sailor who had lent him the boat for the [[africa-expedition|African]] [[travel-and-exploration|journey]] — the same boat that had been wrecked on the coast and could not be returned. The Doctor did not merely repay the debt; he "bought two new [[voyage-and-sailing|ships]] for him and a rubber doll for his baby" (021-011).

He also paid the grocer for the food that had been borrowed for the [[travel-and-exploration|journey]] — provisions that [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]], perpetually short of [[money-and-wealth|money]], had taken on credit before departing (021-011). In a world where [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] had once been unable to pay the fishmonger and the butcher, he now discharged every obligation with generosity.

### Wealth

The Doctor's financial situation had been transformed as dramatically as [[prince-bumpo|Prince Bumpo]]'s face. Even after filling his old [[money-and-wealth|money-box]] on the dresser-shelf, "he still had a lot of [[money-and-wealth|money]] left; and he had to get three more [[money-and-wealth|money-boxes]], just as big, to put the rest in" (021-012).

In [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s household — where [[money-and-wealth|money]] had once been so scarce that the animals went hungry in the snow — this was an almost miraculous reversal. The pushmi-pullyu, the shy creature who had wept at being looked at, had earned [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] his fortune.

## Participants

- **[[john-dolittle|John Dolittle]]** — the returning hero, richer in [[money-and-wealth|wealth]] and experience
- **The [[pushmi-pullyu|Pushmi-Pullyu]]** — the two-headed wonder whose exhibition funded [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s new prosperity
- **[[dab-dab|Dab-Dab]]** — [[dab-dab|the duck]] who managed the household's return to normalcy
- **[[jip-the-dog|Jip the Dog]]** — who "went and showed his [[money-and-wealth|golden]] collar to [[the-collie-next-door|the conceited collie]] next-door" before running around the garden "like a crazy thing" (021-010)

## Outcome & Consequences

The Doctor's return to [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]] marks the conclusion of his great [[africa-expedition|African]] [[travel-and-exploration|adventure]]. The pattern of his life resumed — but on new, more secure terms. When winter came again and "the snow flew against the kitchen-window," [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] and his animals would "sit round the big, warm kitchen-fire" telling stories of their [[travel-and-exploration|travels]], with plenty of food and warmth for all (021-015).

The Doctor had been changed by his [[travel-and-exploration|journey]]. He had discovered the pushmi-pullyu, cured a monkey plague, escaped from prison, outwitted pirates, and returned [[money-and-wealth|wealthy]] — yet he remained the same gentle man who preferred animals to "the best people." His reflection on [[money-and-wealth|wealth]] was characteristically ambivalent:

> "Money," he said, "is a terrible nuisance. But it's nice not to have to worry." (021-013)

[[dab-dab|Dab-Dab]], toasting muffins for his tea, could only agree (021-014).
