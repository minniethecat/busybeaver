# Jip Tracks the Lost Man

## Summary

When six eagles scoured half the world but failed to locate the [[the-fisherman|lost fisherman]] — the pirate captain's brother — [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] [[jip-the-dog|the dog]] declared he could do better. Using a red handkerchief scented with [[black-rappee|Black Rappee]] snuff, [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] tracked the man's scent across the open ocean, guiding the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] by nose alone until they found him trapped in a cave on a barren rock.

## Background: Jip's Extraordinary Nose

[[jip-the-dog|Jip]]'s sense of smell was legendary among [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s household. Earlier in the [[voyage-and-sailing|voyage]], while napping on deck, he had detected the scent of roast beef cooking on a [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] ten miles away — in his sleep (013-005, 013-011). More remarkably, he had smelled "bad men" and "trouble" from that same distance, growling in his sleep about "six bad scoundrels fighting against one brave man" (013-014). This preternatural olfactory ability, surprising even to [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]], would prove decisive when the party needed to find a single lost man on an island somewhere in the vast ocean.

## The Eagles' Failure

After the little boy told them his uncle had been captured by pirates, [[dab-dab|Dab-Dab]] suggested enlisting eagles — "no living creature can see better than an eagle" (018-003). Six eagles of different species answered [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s call: Black, Bald, Fish, Golden, Eagle-Vulture, and White-tailed Sea Eagle — each twice the height of a boy (018-005).

The eagles searched "all the seas and all the countries and all the islands and all the cities and all the villages in this half of the world" but returned empty-handed. They had spotted three red hairs in Gibraltar, but those belonged to a cat, not a man (018-015). Despite their peerless vision, the eagles had been defeated by the sheer scale of the search — there were simply too many places a single man might be.

[[jip-the-dog|Jip]] was unimpressed. "They're just conceited," he declared. "They may have very good eyesight and all that; but when you ask them to find a man for you, they can't do it" (018-019). The dog understood that finding a person required a sense more refined than sight — it required smell.

## The Snuff Clue

[[jip-the-dog|Jip]] approached [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] with a practical suggestion: ask the boy for something belonging to his uncle. The boy showed them a [[money-and-wealth|gold]] ring, but [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] found it carried no useful scent — metal held nothing a dog could track. Then the boy produced a large red handkerchief.

> As soon as the boy pulled it out, Jip shouted, "Snuff, by Jingo! — Black Rappee snuff. Don't you smell it? His uncle took snuff — Ask him, Doctor." (018-029)

The boy confirmed his uncle was an enthusiastic snuff-taker. [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] declared the man "as good as found" and promised to locate him within a week. When [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] protested it was dark, [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] replied that he needed no light to track [[black-rappee|Black Rappee]] snuff (018-033). Darkness, which blinded the eagles, was no obstacle to a nose.

## Reading the Wind

[[jip-the-dog|Jip]] went to the bow of the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] and began to read the oncoming wind, cataloguing its contents with astonishing precision: "Tar; Spanish onions; kerosene oil; wet raincoats; crushed laurel-leaves; rubber burning; lace-curtains hanging out to dry; and foxes — hundreds of 'em — cubs" (018-037).

When [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] expressed amazement, [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] dismissed these as "the easy smells — the strong ones. Any mongrel could smell those with a cold in the head" (018-039). He then closed his eyes and reached deeper into the wind, whispering a poetic inventory of more delicate scents:

> "Bricks — old yellow bricks, crumbling with age in a garden-wall; the sweet breath of young cows standing in a mountain-stream; the lead roof of a dovecote — or perhaps a granary — with the midday sun on it; black kid gloves lying in a bureau-drawer of walnut-wood..." (018-042)

But no snuff. [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] announced they must wait for the wind to change. The Southeast wind carried the scent of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s own garden in [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]] — which brought tears to [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s eyes — but not a trace of [[black-rappee|Black Rappee]].

## The West Wind

For three days they waited. The South wind brought nothing. The East wind at three o'clock the next afternoon was equally barren. But [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] assured the boy: "when the wind changes to the West, I'll find his uncle even though he be in China — so long as he is still taking [[black-rappee|Black Rappee]] snuff" (019-006).

On Friday morning, as a fine rainy mist lay upon the sea, the West wind arrived — soft, warm, and wet. [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] rushed to the deck, poked his nose into the air, and became "most frightfully excited" (019-008).

> "Doctor! I've got it! The wind's from the West and it smells of nothing but snuff. Come upstairs and start the ship — quick!" (019-009)

The Doctor asked if [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] could tell how far away it was. "It's a good long way," [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] admitted, "but we'll get there" (019-011).

## Guiding by Nose

All that morning [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] stood at the bow, sniffing the wind and pointing the way. The animals and the little boy watched in wonder as [[jip-the-dog|the dog]] steered the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] by scent alone (019-012). The smell grew stronger by the hour — the man was taking snuff "in large pinches," [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] reported, noting the complete absence of food smells. The [[the-fisherman|fisherman]] had nothing to eat or drink (019-016).

The Doctor worried the snuff might blow away, but [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] reassured him: snuff is strong stuff, especially [[black-rappee|Black Rappee]]. When [[gub-gub|Gub-Gub]] asked why the fish didn't smell the snuff in the water, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] explained that smell doesn't [[travel-and-exploration|travel]] well through water — but carried on the air, it was a different matter entirely.

At last land appeared: a great rock shining like [[money-and-wealth|gold]] in the sun. [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] insisted the scent stopped there. Leaping onto the rock, he zigzagged across its surface — "Snuff here! Snuff there!" — [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] running breathlessly behind, until he sat down before a deep hole (019-037, 019-038).

## The Discovery

The Doctor climbed down into the dark passage and emerged in a tiny rock chamber. There lay the [[the-fisherman|fisherman]] — and beside him, an enormous snuffbox full of [[black-rappee|Black Rappee]] (019-044). The man had survived on nothing but snuff for days, trapped in that desolate cave with no food or water.

[[jip-the-dog|Jip]]'s nose had crossed the ocean to find a single man, guided by nothing more than the scent of tobacco powder on a handkerchief. Where the eagles' peerless vision had failed, a dog's sense of smell had triumphed.
