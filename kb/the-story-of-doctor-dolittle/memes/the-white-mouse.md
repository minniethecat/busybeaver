## Overview

The White Mouse is one of the smaller members of [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]]'s household. Originally one of the white mice living in the piano, one particular mouse stows away to [[africa-expedition|Africa]] in [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s high hat, survives a [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]]wreck, and returns home to be placed in the new piano — a complete [[travel-and-exploration|journey]] narrative in miniature.

## Life in Puddleby

The white mice are among [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s earliest pets, mentioned in the catalogue of his household: "white mice lived in the piano" (001-000). [[polynesia|Polynesia]] personally ensured the mouse's comfort during the [[voyage-and-sailing|voyage]] preparations: "[[polynesia|Polynesia]] had found a place in the trunk where the white mouse could [[travel-and-exploration|travel]] comfortably" (005-033).

## The Stowaway

When [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] sets sail for [[africa-expedition|Africa]], a white mouse secretly hides in the baggage. During the [[voyage-and-sailing|voyage]], the mouse emerges: "the white mouse, who was a great stowaway and had hidden in the baggage, came out and nearly got drowned when a big wave came over the side" (005-000). The mouse survives the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]]wreck by sheltering inside [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s high hat. When [[the-storm-at-sea|the storm]] is at its worst, "the white mouse, who was inside [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s high hat, said it was the worst storm he had ever been in" (012-000). After the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]]wreck, [[dab-dab|Dab-Dab]] finds the mouse inside the floating hat:

> "I didn't want to be left behind," said the mouse. "I wanted to see what Africa was like — I have relatives there. So I hid in the baggage and was brought on to the ship with the hardtack. When the ship sank I was terribly frightened — because I cannot swim far. I swam as long as I could, but I soon got all exhausted and thought I was going to sink. And then, just at that moment, the old man's hat came floating by; and I got into it because I did not want to be drowned."
>
> (005-029)

After the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]]wreck, the mouse is counted among the survivors alongside the other animals: "the pushmi-pullyu, the white mouse, [[gub-gub|Gub-Gub]], [[dab-dab|Dab-Dab]], [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] and [[too-too|the owl]] [[too-too|Too-Too]]" (012-033).

## At Sea

During the return [[travel-and-exploration|journey]], when the animals discuss how to find the missing [[the-fisherman|fisherman]], the white mouse chimes in with a practical suggestion — recalling past ingenuity:

> "If we only had Polynesia with us. She would soon think of some way. Do you remember how she got us all out of prison — the second time? My, but she was a clever one!" (018-018)

This comment reveals two things: the mouse's memory for past [[travel-and-exploration|adventure]]s, and the reassuring presence of even the smallest animal in [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s deliberative councils. Every creature, regardless of size, has a voice.

## The Mouse's Perspective on Survival

The white mouse also serves as a wry commentator on hard[[voyage-and-sailing|ship]]. When the animals endure difficulties at sea, the mouse's perspective — shaped by his experience of surviving a [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]]wreck inside a hat — provides a measure of how dangerous each situation truly is. His earlier declaration about "the worst storm he had ever been in" becomes a running reference point for every subsequent tempest.

## Home Again

Upon the return to [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]], the mouse is put back into place: "the white mouse was put back into the new piano" (021-000). The new piano, purchased with [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s recovered treasure [[money-and-wealth|money]], represents both continuity and renewal — the same musical instrument, but new, just as [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s household has been through transformation and returned to its essential nature.

## Significance

The White Mouse's story is a miniature parallel to [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s own [[travel-and-exploration|journey]] — a small creature who ventures beyond the familiar, survives through circumstances beyond its control, and returns home to resume its place. The image of the mouse riding out a [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]]wreck inside [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s high hat captures the whimsical scale of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s world, where a piano-dwelling mouse can become an [[travel-and-exploration|adventure]]r without ever intending to be one. The mouse's voice in the animal councils also demonstrates [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s democratic approach: every creature, from the mighty pushmi-pullyu to the tiny white mouse, has equal standing in his company.
