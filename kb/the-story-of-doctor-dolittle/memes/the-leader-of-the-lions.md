# The Leader of the Lions

## Summary

While [[travel-and-exploration|traveling]] through [[africa-expedition|the African jungle]], [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]] encounters a pride of lions. The leader of the lions initially refuses to help, but after [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] treats the lion's sick cub, the lion leader becomes a powerful ally, persuading other animals to assist [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] in return (008-008)(008-017).

## Context

As [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] and his animal companions [[travel-and-exploration|journeyed]] deeper into [[africa-expedition|Africa]], they needed the help of many jungle animals. The lions, being the kings of the jungle, were a crucial group to win over. However, the lion leader was proud and uncooperative. The other animals, including "the lions and the leopards and the antelopes," were watching to see how [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] would handle this challenge (008-005)(008-011).

## Narrative

When [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]] first met the lion, the great beast "looked very terrible" and pawed the ground, snarling at him rudely (008-008)(008-011). The Doctor, undaunted, spoke calmly to the lion, pointing out that someday the lions might fall sick and need his help (008-009).

> "The day may come when the lions get sick. And if you don't help me now, don't expect me to help you then" (008-009).

The lion scoffed, claiming arrogantly:

> "The lions are never in trouble — they only make trouble" (008-010).

The Doctor's opportunity came when a lioness mother had a sick cub. Despite his earlier hostility, the lion leader came to [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] for help. After [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] successfully treated the cub, the lion told his wife proudly of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s skill (008-017). The lion then visited the neighboring den where another mother-lion lived and "told her all about it" — convincing her to trust [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] as well (008-020).

Though the lioness later rebuked her husband sharply, calling him "empty-headed" and grouping him with "those stupid leopards," [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s reputation among the big cats was already growing (008-019).

- **The Lion Leader** — proud king of the jungle who becomes an ally after [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] saves his cub
- **The Lioness** — mother of the sick cub, who later becomes [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s outspoken advocate

## Outcome & Consequences

After [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] saved the cub, the lion leader remarked ruefully on the difficulty of finding reliable help: "Animals don't seem to want to work any more" (008-023). His change of heart became a turning point: his endorsement convinced many other jungle animals to trust the strange human doctor. From then on, "all the lions and the leopards and the antelopes" came to respect and assist [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] in his mission (008-024)(008-023).

## Significance

The encounter with the lion leader established a recurring pattern in [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s [[africa-expedition|African]] [[travel-and-exploration|journey]]: his medical skill proves to be a universal currency that wins over even the most hostile creatures. The lion, as the self-proclaimed king of beasts, represented the ultimate test of this principle — and his conversion opened the door for cooperation from the entire jungle ecosystem.
