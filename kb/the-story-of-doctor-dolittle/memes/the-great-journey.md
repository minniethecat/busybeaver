# The Great Journey

## Summary

After receiving a message from [[africa-expedition|Africa]] that [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] are dying of a terrible sickness, [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]] decides to make the [[voyage-and-sailing|voyage]]. He borrows a boat from a sailor whose baby he once treated, and after extensive preparation guided by [[polynesia|Polynesia's]] seafaring experience, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] and his animals set sail for [[africa-expedition|Africa]]. Six weeks of [[voyage-and-sailing|sailing]] follow, during which they encounter flying fish, porpoises, and a [[the-storm-at-sea|great storm]] that [[voyage-and-sailing|shipwrecks]] them on the [[africa-expedition|African]] coast.

## Context

The [[travel-and-exploration|journey]] is prompted by a message brought by a swallow from [[africa-expedition|Africa]]: [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] are suffering from a devastating illness. The Doctor, though poor and living simply in [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]], resolves to go and help them. This is the first major [[travel-and-exploration|expedition]] in the story and the first time the animals [[travel-and-exploration|travel]] together as a crew.

## Narrative

### The Call to Adventure

One December night, as [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] read aloud to his animals by the kitchen fire, [[too-too|the owl]] [[too-too|Too-Too]] heard a noise outside (004-001). The monkey [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] burst in, badly out of breath, with urgent news: [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] of [[africa-expedition|Africa]] were dying in hundreds from a terrible sickness and begged [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] to come (004-004).

The messenger was a swallow — a bird that should have flown South six weeks earlier — huddled and shivering on the rain-butt. Brought inside to warm by the fire, she confirmed the desperate situation (004-008).

> "I would gladly go to Africa — especially in this bitter weather. But I'm afraid we haven't money enough to buy the tickets." (004-010)

The [[money-and-wealth|money-box]] was empty. The Doctor had spent his last twopence on a rattle for a badger's baby (004-014). Undeterred, he resolved to borrow a boat from a sailor whose baby he had once cured of measles.

### Securing the Ship

> The Doctor went down to the seashore. And when he came back he told the animals it was all right — the sailor was going to lend them the boat. (004-016)

The crocodile, monkey, and parrot were overjoyed — they were returning to [[africa-expedition|Africa]], their true home. The Doctor announced he could only take three of his [[africa-expedition|African]] animals plus [[jip-the-dog|Jip]], [[dab-dab|Dab-Dab]], [[gub-gub|Gub-Gub]], and [[too-too|Too-Too]]. The dormice, water-voles, and bats would remain behind, sleeping through the winter (004-018).

### Preparation and Supplies

[[polynesia|Polynesia]], who had been on long [[voyage-and-sailing|sea voyages]] before, rattled off the essentials: pilot-bread ("hard tack"), beef in cans, an [[voyage-and-sailing|anchor]] ("you can't stop if you haven't got an [[voyage-and-sailing|anchor]]"), a bell to tell the time, and "a whole lot of rope — it always comes in handy on [[voyage-and-sailing|voyages]]" (004-020, 004-022, 004-024).

The Doctor sent the sailor to the grocer for supplies on credit. They packed up the house, turned off the water so the pipes wouldn't freeze, put up the shutters, and left the key with [[the-old-lame-horse|the old horse]] in the stable (004-028).

[[the-cats-meatman|The Cat's-meatman]] arrived to see them off with a present: a large suet-pudding, because "you couldn't get suet-puddings in foreign parts" (004-029). [[gub-gub|Gub-Gub]], discovering that [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] beds were shelves called "bunks," declared himself too excited to sleep (004-032, 004-033).

### The Voyage

For six whole weeks they sailed on and on, following [[the-swallow-messenger|the swallow]] who flew ahead to show the way. At night she carried a tiny lantern so they would not lose her in the dark; passing [[voyage-and-sailing|ships]] thought the light was a shooting star (005-001).

As they sailed south, the weather grew warmer. [[polynesia|Polynesia]], [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]], and the crocodile basked in the sun, while [[gub-gub|the pig]], dog, and owl sat in the shade drinking lemonade with their tongues hanging out (005-003). [[dab-dab|Dab-Dab]] cooled herself by swimming behind the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]], diving under to catch herrings on Tuesdays and Fridays when everyone ate fish to conserve the beef (005-004).

Near the Equator, flying fish approached to ask if this was [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]]'s [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]]. When [[polynesia|Polynesia]] confirmed it, they reported [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] were growing worried — only fifty-five miles remained to the coast of [[africa-expedition|Africa]] (005-005).

> A whole school of porpoises came dancing through the waves; and they too asked Polynesia if this was the ship of the famous doctor. (005-006)

When [[polynesia|Polynesia]] mentioned they had run short of onions, the porpoises sped to a nearby island where wild onions grew, returning with nets full of them dragged through the waves (005-007, 005-009).

### The Shipwreck

On the last evening, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] called for his telescope: "Our [[travel-and-exploration|journey]] is nearly ended. Very soon we should be able to see the shores of [[africa-expedition|Africa]]" (005-011). But darkness fell — and with it came a [[the-storm-at-sea|great storm]]. Thunder crashed, lightning split the sky, and the waves rose so high they splashed over the boat (005-013).

Suddenly there was a bang. The [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] had struck a rock. [[dab-dab|Dab-Dab]] dived down and confirmed the worst: a big hole in the bottom, water rushing in, the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] sinking fast (005-017).

> "We must have run into Africa," said the Doctor. "Dear me, dear me! — Well — we must all swim to land." (005-018)

[[polynesia|Polynesia]]'s foresight proved invaluable. She had [[dab-dab|Dab-Dab]] fly a rope to the shore and tie it to a palm tree, creating a lifeline. Those who could not swim — [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] and [[gub-gub|Gub-Gub]] — climbed along the rope to safety, dragging [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s trunk and handbag with them (005-020, 005-021). The [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] was beaten to pieces on the rocks.

### Arrival in Africa

They sheltered in a dry cave high in the cliffs until morning. When the sun came out, [[polynesia|Polynesia]] sighed with joy: "Dear old [[africa-expedition|Africa]]! It's good to get back. Just think — it'll be a hundred and sixty-nine years tomorrow since I was here! And it hasn't changed a bit!" (005-025). The others noticed she had tears in her eyes.

The Doctor discovered his high hat had blown into the sea during [[the-storm-at-sea|the storm]]. [[dab-dab|Dab-Dab]] retrieved it — and found one of the white mice, very frightened, sitting inside. The mouse had stowed away in the baggage, unable to bear being left behind. "I wanted to see what [[africa-expedition|Africa]] was like — I have relatives there" (005-030).

> "That's what you call a 'stowaway,'" said the parrot. (005-032)

Before they could settle, [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] heard footsteps. A black man emerged from the jungle and demanded they come before [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]] of the [[jolliginki|Jolliginki]], who ruled all the surrounding lands. The Doctor introduced himself: "My name is [[john-dolittle|John Dolittle]] — [[doctor-dolittle-m-d|M.D.]] I have been asked to come to [[africa-expedition|Africa]] to cure [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] who are sick" (005-036, 005-037). The great [[travel-and-exploration|journey]] had reached its destination — but a new set of challenges was about to begin.

## Thematic Significance

The Great Journey is more than a plot mechanism to move characters from England to [[africa-expedition|Africa]]. It is a microcosm of the book's deepest concerns: the nature of leader[[voyage-and-sailing|ship]], the value of reciprocity, and the idea that a good [[travel-and-exploration|journey]] transforms its [[travel-and-exploration|travelers]].

**leader[[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] without hierarchy.** During the [[voyage-and-sailing|voyage]], [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] is nominally the captain, but [[polynesia]] handles navigation, [[dab-dab]] procures food, and [[the-swallow-messenger|the swallow]] guides from above. No one issues commands — each contributes what they can. This distributed model of leader[[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] mirrors [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s philosophy that every creature, regardless of species, has something valuable to offer. The [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] becomes a floating demonstration of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s world — cooperative, multi-species, and effective precisely because it is not organized around a single authority.

**The economy of kindness.** The Doctor has no [[money-and-wealth|money]] for the [[voyage-and-sailing|voyage]]. He obtains a [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] because he once cured a sailor's baby. He gets supplies on credit. Flying fish and porpoises provide intelligence and onions because [[polynesia]] can speak their language and because [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s reputation precedes him. The entire [[travel-and-exploration|journey]] operates on the currency of past good deeds — a radical alternative to the cash economy that failed [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] in [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]]. The message is clear: kindness, invested wisely, yields returns that [[money-and-wealth|money]] cannot buy.

**The [[voyage-and-sailing|shipwreck]] as initiation.** The storm that destroys the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] on the final night is not a punishment but a threshold. Arriving safely and simply would have been too easy — [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s party must earn [[africa-expedition|Africa]] by surviving its waters. The [[voyage-and-sailing|shipwreck]] strips them of their vessel (they cannot return the way they came) and of their remaining supplies, forcing them to rely entirely on what [[africa-expedition|Africa]] provides and on their own resourcefulness. The old [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]], like [[sarah-dolittle|Sarah]]'s departure, is something that must be lost for the real story to begin.
