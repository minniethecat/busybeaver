# Finding the Pushmi-Pullyu

## Summary

After [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]] cured [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] of a terrible sickness, they gathered to decide on a magnificent farewell gift for the man who had saved their lives. Following [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]]'s advice, they resolved to capture the pushmi-pullyu — the rarest animal in [[africa-expedition|the African jungles]], a two-headed creature that no White Man had ever seen. The monkeys organized a great hunt through the forest, surrounding the shy animal in a circle of linked hands. After three days of patient reasoning and persuasion, the creature agreed to meet [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]], and upon seeing his kind face, consented to [[travel-and-exploration|travel]] with him back to England. The pushmi-pullyu later proved its worth during the return [[voyage-and-sailing|voyage]], defending the party against pirates with its sharpened horns and delighting children with its gentle nature along the way.

## Context

After curing [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] of a devastating sickness, [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]] slept for three days without stirring. When he awoke, his first thought was of his debts in [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]] — he had borrowed the boat and provisions for the [[africa-expedition|African]] [[travel-and-exploration|journey]] and owed the sailor a new vessel (009-002). The monkeys, who had assumed he would stay forever, were stunned by the news of his departure. They convened a great council to decide how to honor the man who had saved their lives.

## The Monkeys' Council

When [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] learned that [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] must return to [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]] to pay his debts — he had borrowed the boat and provisions for the [[africa-expedition|African]] [[travel-and-exploration|journey]] and owed the sailor a new vessel — they gathered in the jungle to deliberate on a parting gift (009-025). [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]], the great [[travel-and-exploration|traveler]] who had [[travel-and-exploration|journeyed]] to the [[land-of-the-white-men|Land of the White Men]] and back, advised them against giving fruit or coconuts: these were too heavy to carry, would spoil quickly, and [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] would surely find more before he reached his own country (009-027).

[[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] suggested instead a rare animal, one that could not be found in the menageries of the White Men. The monkeys eagerly proposed various creatures — an iguana, a [[money-and-wealth|golden]]-and-scarlet bird-of-paradise, an okapi — but each time [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] shook his head. The White Men, he explained, already possessed specimens of each (009-029, 009-033).

Then a small monkey spoke up from the back of the council: "Have they a pushmi-pullyu?" (009-034). The question hung in the air. [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] responded with certainty:

> "No. No White Man has ever seen a pushmi-pullyu. Let us give him that." (009-036)

The decision was unanimous. The monkeys understood instinctively that the pushmi-pullyu would be the most extraordinary gift they could offer — a creature of legend, wholly unknown to the outside world, lightweight enough to transport across the sea, and invaluable as both a scientific curiosity and a source of wonder.

## The Great Hunt

The monkeys dispersed through the forest in search of the fabled creature, fanning out across miles of jungle. The hunt proved extraordinarily difficult, for the pushmi-pullyu is notoriously elusive — with a head at each end of its body, it can watch in both directions simultaneously, making it nearly impossible to approach from any angle (010-001). 

After [[travel-and-exploration|traveling]] a great distance, one of [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] discovered peculiar footprints along the grassy bank of a river — tracks unlike any other animal's, where each print appeared to go in both directions at once (010-002). The tracks confirmed that a pushmi-pullyu had recently passed that way, and [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] followed the trail with mounting excitement.

The tracks led them to a place where the grass grew unusually high and thick — perfect cover for a shy creature hiding from the world. Certain that the pushmi-pullyu was concealed within, [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] formed a great circle around the tall grass, joining hands to create an unbroken ring of bodies (010-003). Slowly they closed inward. The pushmi-pullyu, hearing their approach, tried desperately to break through the circle, dashing first one way and then the other, but found itself unable to escape the coordinated encirclement (010-004).

The capture required the effort of the entire monkey troop. No single monkey, however swift, could have outmaneuvered a creature that could see in both directions; only collective action — linking hands to deny every avenue of escape — could succeed.

## Three Days of Persuasion

The captured creature was terrified. When [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] explained their purpose — that they wished it to [[travel-and-exploration|travel]] with [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] to the [[land-of-the-white-men|Land of the White Men]] — the pushmi-pullyu shook both its heads in vigorous refusal. 

> "You know how shy I am — I hate being stared at," it said, nearly in tears. "I have been so shy from my earliest childhood." (010-008)

The animal's reluctance was deeply felt. It had spent its entire life hidden in the deepest recesses of the jungle, far from any prying eyes. The prospect of being exhibited before crowds of strange White Men was nothing short of horrifying.

But [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] did not give up. For three full days and nights they stayed with the creature, reasoning with it and pleading their case. They explained that [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] was not an ordinary man — he was a kind man, gentle of heart, who spoke the language of all animals and would never dream of caging it in a cruel menagerie (010-007). They described how [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] had come all the way from England and worked day and night to save [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] from a plague that would have killed every last one of them (010-009).

The turning point came when [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] described [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s ability to speak animal languages. The pushmi-pullyu, whose own speech was so unusual — it conversed with only one mouth while keeping the other free for eating — was intrigued by the idea of a human who could understand it without translation (010-028).

At last, after three full days, the pushmi-pullyu agreed to come and see what kind of man [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] was first — a trial meeting, not a binding commitment (010-010). Even in consent, the creature was cautious.

## Meeting the Doctor

When [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] proudly led the strange creature into [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s grass house, the reaction was immediate astonishment. [[john-dolittle|John Dolittle]], absorbed in his notebook, looked up at the two-headed beast and asked simply: "What in the world is it?" (010-014). 

[[dab-dab|Dab-Dab the duck]], ever practical, circled the creature with an expression of bewilderment and blurted out: "How does it make up its mind?" (010-015). And [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] [[jip-the-dog|the dog]], with characteristic dryness, remarked: "It doesn't look to me as though it had any" (010-016).

[[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]], beaming with pride, declared the animal to be "the rarest animal of [[africa-expedition|the African jungles]], the only two-headed beast in the world," and assured [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] that exhibiting it among the White Men would make his fortune — bringing in more [[money-and-wealth|money]] than [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] had ever seen (010-017). The Doctor, despite his constant financial troubles, protested mildly that he did not care about [[money-and-wealth|money]]. But [[dab-dab|Dab-Dab]], remembering the leaky boat they had borrowed and the angry sailor waiting for repayment, reminded him firmly of their poverty in [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]] (010-019, 010-021).

The pushmi-pullyu, observing this exchange in silence, studied [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s face. What it saw decided everything. The Doctor's eyes were kind, his expression gentle, his manner unhurried. The shy creature recognized at once that this was a man to be trusted — a man utterly unlike the hunters and collectors who would have caged it for profit (010-023). The pushmi-pullyu agreed to go with him to England.

This moment — in which the most timid creature in the jungle saw the truth of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s character in a single glance — is among the most revealing in the entire story. Every animal that met [[john-dolittle|John Dolittle]] came to the same conclusion: here was a man who would never betray their trust.

## The Gift Ceremony

The presentation of the pushmi-pullyu was the culmination of [[chee-chee|the monkeys]]' farewell to [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]. Hundreds of monkeys gathered to say goodbye, and the air was filled with their chattering voices, all speaking at once in a confusion of gratitude and sorrow. The pushmi-pullyu stood among them, no longer trying to hide, having accepted its new role in [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s company.

The monkeys had kept their promise — they had given [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] the most extraordinary gift in all of [[africa-expedition|Africa]], something no other man possessed. The pushmi-pullyu, once the most hidden creature in the jungle, was now [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s companion.

## The Pushmi-Pullyu's Lineage

The Doctor, ever the naturalist, immediately reached for his well-worn copy of Buffon's natural history to verify what [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] had told him. But the pushmi-pullyu itself supplied the genealogy:

> "Yes," said the pushmi-pullyu — "to the Abyssinian Gazelles and the Asiatic Chamois — on my mother's side. My father's great-grandfather was the last of the Unicorns." (010-025)

The lineage was astonishing: ordinary gazelles and mountain chamois on the maternal side, but on the paternal side — the last of the unicorns. The pushmi-pullyu thus embodied both the natural and the legendary, a living bridge between documented zoology and the creatures of myth that still inhabited the deeper reaches of the [[africa-expedition|African]] continent.

When [[dab-dab|Dab-Dab]] observed that the pushmi-pullyu spoke only through one of its mouths, the creature explained its custom with characteristic dignity: it always kept the other mouth free for eating, so it could converse without being rude. "Our people have always been very polite," it added (010-028). This practical arrangement — one head for talking, one for eating — was not merely a biological curiosity but a mark of its refined lineage.

## Later Adventures

The pushmi-pullyu's story did not end with its capture. During the long [[voyage-and-sailing|voyage]] home and the [[travel-and-exploration|adventures]] that followed, the once-shy creature revealed unexpected depths of courage and gentleness.

### The Return Journey

On the homeward [[voyage-and-sailing|voyage]], the pushmi-pullyu [[travel-and-exploration|traveled]] aboard the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] with [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s growing company of animals. When storms drove the vessel off course and the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] was wrecked upon the coast, the pushmi-pullyu shared in the party's hardship. It welcomed the arrival of [[the-swallow-messenger|the swallows]] — two thousand strong — who came to pull the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] free from its rocky trap, understanding that without their aid, the [[travel-and-exploration|journey]] might never continue (014-022).

### The Pirate Encounter

The pushmi-pullyu's most dramatic transformation came during the encounter with the [[barbary-coast|Barbary]] pirates. When the pirate [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] bore down upon them with hostile intent, the animal that had once wept at the thought of being looked at now prepared for battle. Frightened but resolute, it lowered both heads and began to sharpen its horns against the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]]'s rail — ready to fight if the pirates attempted to board (015-012).

This moment marks a profound change in the creature's character. The pushmi-pullyu, once defined entirely by its shyness, had found something worth defending. The Doctor's kindness had transformed it from a creature in hiding to a creature willing to stand and fight.

### A Friend to Children

During [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s subsequent [[travel-and-exploration|travels]], the pushmi-pullyu revealed its gentlest side. Passing through a village on the [[travel-and-exploration|journey]] home, the creature allowed a small boy to climb upon its back — and the child clapped his hands with pure joy at the strange and wonderful ride (017-020). Later, other children gathered, and the pushmi-pullyu, though still shy, submitted to their delighted curiosity (017-036).

The Doctor, watching from a distance, whispered to his companions not to let the pushmi-pullyu see them observing — for the shy creature was, at heart, still the same modest animal of the deep jungle, uncomfortable with attention even when giving joy to others.

## Outcome

The capture of the pushmi-pullyu stands as one of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s most celebrated achievements as a naturalist. He brought back from [[africa-expedition|Africa]] a creature of legend — the last descendant of the unicorns — that no European had ever beheld. The animal's exhibition in England indeed brought in the [[money-and-wealth|money]] [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] needed to pay his debts, as [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] had predicted.

But beyond its monetary value, the pushmi-pullyu's story reveals the central pattern of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s relationships with animals. Where others would have captured and caged, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] waited and persuaded. Where others would have seen only a curiosity to exploit, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] saw a shy creature deserving of dignity. And in return, he earned not merely a specimen for exhibition but a loyal companion — one who would sharpen its horns to defend him against pirates, and temper its deepest shyness to bring joy to village children.

The pushmi-pullyu, the animal that saw both ways at once, chose to go forward with [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] — and in doing so, showed that the rarest quality in the world is not two-headedness, but the trust that kindness inspires.
