# The Cat's-meatman

The Cat's-meatman is a minor but pivotal figure in the story of [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]]. He is [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s last remaining human patient in [[doctors-garden|Puddleby]], and it is his casual suggestion — "Why don't you give up being a people's doctor, and be an animal-doctor?" — that sets the entire narrative in motion.

## The Last Patient

By the time [[matthew-mugg|the Cat's-meatman]] enters the story, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s practice has dwindled to almost nothing. His house is overrun with animals, and his human patients have abandoned him:

> Till at last he had no one left — except the Cat's-meatman, who didn't mind any kind of animals. (001-010)

The Cat's-meatman is not [[money-and-wealth|wealth]]y — "he only got sick once a year — at Christmas-time, when he used to give [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] sixpence for a bottle of [[medicine-and-magic|medicine]]" ((001-010)) — but his loyalty is absolute. Where other patients fled from the [[the-white-mouse|white mice]] in the piano and the hedgehog on the sofa, [[matthew-mugg|the Cat's-meatman]], accustomed to animals through his trade, remained unfazed.

## The Fateful Stomachache

The turning point comes when [[matthew-mugg|the Cat's-meatman]] visits with a stomachache:

> It happened one day that the Doctor was sitting in his kitchen talking with the Cat's-meatman who had come to see him with a stomachache. (002-001)

In the course of their conversation, [[matthew-mugg|the Cat's-meatman]] makes his historic suggestion:

> "Why don't you give up being a people's doctor, and be an animal-doctor?" asked the Cat's-meatman. (002-002)

His reasoning is practical and prescient:

> "You see, Doctor," the Cat's-meatman went on, "you know all about animals — much more than what these here vets do. That book you wrote — about cats, why, it's wonderful!" (002-004)

## Theodosia

The Cat's-meatman reveals that he himself cannot read, but his wife **[[theodosia|Theodosia]]** is a scholar who read [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s cat book aloud to him ((002-004)). This detail connects [[matthew-mugg|the Cat's-meatman]] to a broader domestic world — his enthusiasm for [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s work comes not from direct reading but from his wife's mediation, suggesting a household where [[animal-language|animal knowledge]] is valued.

## A Dubious Business Proposal

The Cat's-meatman's suggestion is not entirely altruistic. He proposes a scheme:

> "Just a little something to make them droopy-like was what I had reference to. But as you say, maybe it ain't quite fair on the animals." (002-006)

He suggests that if animals did not get sick naturally, he could "put something in the meat I sell 'em to make 'em sick" ((002-004)) — ensuring a steady supply of patients. [[john-dolittle|The Doctor]] firmly rejects this idea, and [[matthew-mugg|the Cat's-meatman]], to his credit, accepts the rebuke and drops the scheme.

## Spreading the Word

After [[polynesia|Polynesia]] convinces [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] to follow [[matthew-mugg|the Cat's-meatman]]'s advice, [[matthew-mugg|the Cat's-meatman]] becomes [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s first promoter:

> As soon as the Cat's-meatman had told everyone that John Dolittle was going to become an animal-doctor, old ladies began to bring him their pet pugs and poodles who had eaten too much cake; and farmers came many miles to show him sick cows and sheep. (002-025)

This single sentence marks the transition between [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s two careers. The Cat's-meatman, a humble tradesman, becomes the unwitting herald of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s worldwide fame.

## Significance

The Cat's-meatman embodies the story's democratic spirit. A barely literate meat-seller, not a learned colleague or a fellow physician, is the one who perceives [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s true calling and articulates it. His suggestion is not born of vision or idealism but of simple observation — he sees that [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] is better with animals than with people, and says so. In a story filled with talking animals and fantastical [[voyage-and-sailing|voyage]]s, [[matthew-mugg|the Cat's-meatman]] represents the grounded, practical wisdom of the ordinary person who sees what others overlook.
