# The Monkey Sickness

## Summary

Upon reaching [[africa-expedition|Africa]], [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]] found [[chee-chee|the monkey]] population in the grip of a terrible epidemic. Hundreds and thousands of monkeys — gorillas, orangutans, chimpanzees, and dog-faced baboons — were sick and dying. The Doctor became "dreadfully, awfully busy," working day and night to cure them. (008-001) His success earned him the undying gratitude of [[chee-chee|the monkey]] nation and led directly to their extraordinary farewell gift: the [[pushmi-pullyu|pushmi-pullyu]], the rarest animal of all.

## The Scale of the Crisis

The sickness was far worse than [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] had imagined. Everywhere he looked, monkeys of every kind were suffering. The epidemic affected not one tribe but the entire simian population of the region — gorillas, orangutans, chimpanzees, and dog-faced baboons alike, all struck down by the same terrible illness. (008-001)

> John Dolittle now became dreadfully, awfully busy. He found hundreds and thousands of monkeys sick — gorillas, orangutans, chimpanzees, dog-faced baboons — all with the same terrible illness. (008-001)

## Triage and Treatment

The Doctor's first act upon arriving in the jungle was systematic and medical: he separated the sick monkeys from the well ones to prevent further contagion. (008-002) This is classic epidemic management — isolation of the infected, protection of the healthy — applied not in a hospital ward but in the canopy of [[africa-expedition|the African jungle]].

He then set to work on individual treatment, moving from patient to patient with his little black [[medicine-and-magic|medicine]]-bag. The work was overwhelming in its sheer volume. So many monkeys were sick that there were not enough well ones to do the nursing. Faced with a staffing crisis in his jungle hospital, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] sent messages to the other animals — lions, leopards, and antelopes — enlisting them as volunteer nurses. (008-005)

> But so many were sick, there were not enough well ones to do the nursing. So he sent messages to the other animals, like the lions and the leopards and the antelopes, to come and help with the nursing. (008-005)

This cross-species collaboration is one of the most striking episodes in the book. Predators and prey — lions and antelopes — set aside their natural enmity to serve together under [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s direction, a testament to the authority he commands in the animal world.

The Doctor's reputation preceded him. The monkeys had already heard — through the gossip network of birds and migrating animals — how the strange little man from England could cure any kind of sickness, and how kind he was. Most importantly, he was the only human in the world who could speak their language, so terrified monkeys who might have fled from any other human came willingly to receive treatment. (008-001)(002-044)

The work was exhausting but effective. One by one, [[chee-chee|the monkey]]s recovered under his care. Word spread through the jungle of the miraculous doctor who had crossed an ocean in winter because creatures in need had asked for his help.

## Outcome

The curing of [[chee-chee|the monkey]] sickness was one of [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]]'s greatest medical achievements. It demonstrated not only his skill as a physician but his extraordinary compassion — he had crossed an ocean in winter because creatures in need had asked for his help. The monkeys, once recovered, would not forget what he had done.

Their gratitude took the form of the rarest gift the jungle could offer. After much debate among [[chee-chee|the monkey]] elders, they resolved to give [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] a [[pushmi-pullyu|pushmi-pullyu]] — a creature so rare that most animals had never seen one, a [[pushmi-pullyu|two-headed gazelle]]-unicorn that had never been captured by any man. (010-002)(011-002) The gift of the pushmi-pullyu, which would later make [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s fortune when he exhibited it in England, can be traced directly back to this epidemic: [[chee-chee|the monkey]]s paying the debt of their salvation.
