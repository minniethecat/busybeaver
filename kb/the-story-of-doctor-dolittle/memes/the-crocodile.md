## Overview

The Crocodile is a recurring animal character who arrives at [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s house after escaping from the circus with a bad toothache. Despite his fierce appearance, he is described as "as gentle as a kitten" to everyone in the household. His presence in the garden fishpond becomes a pivotal point of conflict: the old ladies and farmers grow afraid to bring their animals to [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]], and [[sarah-dolittle|Sarah Dolittle]] leaves the household over the dispute.

> "It isn't an alligator," said the Doctor—"it's a crocodile."
>
> (003-009)

## Arrival at Puddleby

When the circus came to [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]], the crocodile escaped at night and came into [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s garden seeking relief from a toothache. The Doctor spoke to him in crocodile-language, treated his tooth, and the crocodile was so impressed by the house — with different places for different kinds of animals — that he asked to stay. He promised not to eat the fish if he could sleep in the fishpond at the bottom of the garden ((003-005)).

When the circus-men came to take him back, the crocodile "got so wild and savage that he frightened them away" — yet to everyone in the house he was "always as gentle as a kitten" ((003-005)).

## Conflict with Sarah

The crocodile's presence had serious consequences. The old ladies "grew afraid to send their lap-dogs to [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]] because of the crocodile," and the farmers wouldn't believe he would not eat the lambs and sick calves they brought for treatment ((003-006)). When [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] tried to send him back to the circus, the crocodile "wept such big tears, and begged so hard to be allowed to stay, that [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] hadn't the heart to turn him out" ((003-006)).

[[sarah-dolittle|Sarah Dolittle]] issued an ultimatum: either the crocodile goes, or she leaves. She complained that the creature "eats the linoleum" and called him a "nasty thing to find under the bed" ((003-008)–(003-012)). The Doctor famously corrected her — "It isn't an alligator, it's a crocodile" — and ultimately chose the crocodile over his sister. [[sarah-dolittle|Sarah]] packed up and left, leaving [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] "all alone with his animal family" ((003-013)–(003-014)).

## The Africa Journey

The crocodile accompanied [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] on the [[voyage-and-sailing|voyage]] to [[africa-expedition|Africa]]. He was "happy because he was going back to his native land" and enjoyed the [[voyage-and-sailing|voyage]] so much that he "did not want to go below at all, but stayed on deck all day" ((004-000)). During the [[travel-and-exploration|journey]], he at times showed annoyance with [[polynesia|Polynesia]]'s storytelling, grumbling at her in the rigging ((006-000)).

When the [[travel-and-exploration|journey]] home began, the crocodile chose to remain in [[africa-expedition|Africa]] — "he found that he liked [[africa-expedition|Africa]] better than England after all" — eventually returning to the river mud where he was last heard "grunting happily" upon [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s return home ((012-000), (021-000)).

## Personality

Despite being a crocodile — an animal most people fear — the Crocodile is gentle, emotional, and deeply loyal to [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]. He weeps easily, refuses to leave when first asked, and enjoys simple pleasures like lying in the fishpond. His dual nature — fierce when threatened, gentle with friends — mirrors the book's theme that animals are misunderstood when humans cannot speak their language.
