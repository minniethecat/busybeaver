# Doctor Dolittle, M.D.

> The title "M.D." represents John Dolittle's formal medical training — the foundation upon which his later career as an animal-doctor was built. Though he eventually abandons his human practice, his medical knowledge proves essential in his adventures.

## Definition

**Doctor Dolittle, M.D.** refers to [[john-dolittle|John Dolittle]]'s credential as a Doctor of Medicine — a formally trained physician who initially treated human patients in [[west-country|the West Country of England]]. The "M.D." title follows him throughout the book even after he transitions to treating animals, and it commands respect (or suspicion) wherever he [[travel-and-exploration|travels]].

## In the Story

At the story's opening, Dolittle is established as a people's doctor living in [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby-on-the-Marsh]]. His house is filled with animals, and local patients begin avoiding him:

> …there was a doctor; and his name was Dolittle — John Dolittle, M.D. (001-001)

The animals themselves recognize the value of his medical training. It is [[polynesia|Polynesia]] [[polynesia|the parrot]] who first suggests he redirect his skills from people to animals:

> "Oh, there are plenty of animal-doctors," said John Dolittle, putting his flower-pots in the sunshine. (002-009)

But [[polynesia|Polynesia]] corrects him — there are *veterinary surgeons* who treat farm animals, but no one who treats household pets with genuine medical expertise. Dolittle's M.D. training sets him apart.

## The Credential Abroad

When Dolittle arrives in [[africa-expedition|Africa]], his M.D. credential undergoes a curious transformation. To the [[king-of-the-jolliginki|King of the Jolliginki]] and his court, he is not a doctor of [[medicine-and-magic|medicine]] but a "[[medicine-and-magic|medicine-man]]" — a figure of power to be feared and contained. The King imprisons him not despite his medical skill but because of it: he fears Dolittle will make his people sick, just as he claims [[chee-chee|the monkey]]s got sick. (006-005)(006-029)

To [[chee-chee|the monkey]]s, however, his credential is understood differently. They have heard through the animal gossip network that there is a doctor in England who can speak their language and cure any sickness — a claim that only makes sense if the doctor in question has genuine medical training. (002-044)(008-001) They do not care about diplomas or Latin prescriptions; they care about results. And Dolittle delivers: he separates the sick from the well, administers treatment, and saves a species. (008-002)

The M.D. thus functions as a flexible symbol. In England, it marks Dolittle as a gentleman and a professional — until his animals drive away his patients. In [[africa-expedition|Africa]], it marks him as a "wizard" — powerful, potentially dangerous, and worthy of both fear and gratitude. The credential itself is never questioned; only the audience changes.

## Significance

Dolittle's M.D. credential is significant because it represents the *legitimacy* of his practice. He is not a folk healer or a charlatan — he is a formally trained physician who happens to have learned animal languages. This combination of scientific training and communicative ability makes him uniquely effective.

The M.D. title also creates dramatic tension: the respectable [[west-country|West Country]] doctor becomes a shabby animal-doctor whom polite society shuns, then finally a celebrated figure whose medical expertise saves the lives of creatures across continents. The credential never changes — only the patients do.
