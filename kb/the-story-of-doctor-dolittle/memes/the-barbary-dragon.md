# The Barbary Dragon

## Summary

While [[voyage-and-sailing|sailing]] along the [[barbary-coast|Barbary Coast]], [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]] and his animals encountered the pirate [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] of [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]], the self-styled "[[ben-ali|Barbary Dragon]]" — described as the most terrible pirate that ever sailed the seven seas. With the help of sharks, swallows, and his own unflappable courage, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] subdued the pirate without violence and transformed him into a peaceful farmer (015-004, 015-037, 020-019).

## Context

The [[barbary-coast|Barbary Coast]] was infamous as the hunting ground of pirates who "used to wait for sailors to be [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]]wrecked on their shores" and would plunder wrecked vessels (013-001, 013-002). After commandeering the pirates' fast [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] at the [[canary-islands|Canary Islands]], [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] and his animals found themselves pursued across the sea by [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]]'s fleet (014-027).

## Narrative

[[ben-ali|Ben Ali]] was a figure of genuine menace. He "called himself '[[ben-ali|Ben Ali]], The Dragon'" and shook his fist at any vessel that crossed his path (015-004). Sailors throughout the region knew him as "the most terrible pirate that ever sailed the seven seas" (020-019). When he spotted [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]], he prepared to attack with confidence born of a long career in terror.

But [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s animal allies changed the equation entirely. [[polynesia|Polynesia]] [[polynesia|the parrot]] flew toward the pirate [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]], calling out mockingly, while [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] "went to the front of the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] and began barking and calling [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]] bad names in dog-language" (015-012). More decisively, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] summoned the sharks — creatures who owed him no loyalty but respected his authority over the animal world.

A shark chased [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]] from his own vessel across to [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]], where [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] confronted him directly:

> "Listen, Ben Ali," said John Dolittle, leaning over the side. "You have been a very bad man; and I understand that you have killed many people. These good sharks here have just offered to eat you up for me — and 'twould indeed be a good thing if the seas were rid of you. But if you will promise to do as I tell you, I will let you go in safety."
>
> (015-032)

When [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]] asked — bewildered — what he was supposed to do instead to earn a living, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s answer was characteristically eccentric: he suggested farming birdseed. [[the-barbary-dragon|The Barbary Dragon]] "turned pale with anger" and muttered in disbelief: "Thunder and Lightning! … Birdseed!" (015-035, 015-037, 015-039).

Yet [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] was immovable. [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]], for the first time in his career, faced an opponent he could neither intimidate nor outfight. He surrendered and accepted his new calling.

## The Battle of Wills

The confrontation on [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] is not fought with weapons but with authority. [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]], accustomed to intimidating sailors with cannon and cutlass, finds himself facing a small, round man with no weapons at all — and a shark at his side. The Doctor's tone is not angry but absolute: "Listen, [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]]. You have been a very bad man; and I understand that you have killed many people" (015-032).

The shark, speaking on behalf of all the sharks of the sea, confirms [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s authority: "We know these pirates to be a bad lot — especially [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]]" (015-029). The shark then does something no pirate has ever seen: it takes orders from a man. "Please make [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]] swim over here that I may talk to him," [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] says, and the shark obeys (015-030).

This moment represents the complete inversion of [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]]'s power. The man who commanded a fleet of pirates, who shook his fist at every [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] on the sea, is now swimming from his own vessel at the command of a shark and a doctor. [[the-barbary-dragon|The Barbary Dragon]] has been reduced to a swimmer in the open water, awaiting the judgment of a man who does not raise his voice.

## The Absurd Sentence

The Doctor's sentence — farming birdseed — is one of the book's great comedic moments, but it is also philosophically serious. [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]] expects execution or imprisonment. He gets birdseed. The absurdity is the point: [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] diminishes piracy not by demonizing it but by making it ridiculous. A man who farms birdseed cannot also be the terror of the seven seas.

[[ben-ali|Ben Ali]]'s horrified response — "Thunder and Lightning! … Birdseed!" — captures the collision between his self-image and [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s dismissal of everything he thought he was (015-039). The Dragon was always a performance; [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] sees through it to the man beneath, and the man beneath is going to farm birdseed.

## The Fisherman's Testimony

The [[the-fisherman|fisherman]] later confirms the transformation from the victim's perspective. When relating his story, he notes that [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]] threatened to throw him into the sea for refusing to join the pirates, adding: "Then the leader, [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]], got very angry and gnashed his teeth" (017-011). The [[the-fisherman|fisherman]]'s quiet refusal and [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]]'s tooth-gnashing rage are two sides of the same coin — integrity unmoved by terror, and terror unmanned by integrity.

The [[the-fisherman|fisherman]]'s testimony also reveals [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]]'s method: capture sailors and force them into piracy, killing those who refuse. This systematic violence makes [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s nonviolent intervention all the more remarkable. He breaks the cycle without repeating it.

- **[[ben-ali|Ben Ali]]** — [[ben-ali|the Barbary Dragon]], fearsome pirate captain who meets his match in [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s unconventional methods
- **The Sharks** — called by [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] to assist in subduing the pirate

## Outcome & Consequences

[[ben-ali|Ben Ali]]'s transformation was genuine. Later reports confirmed that [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] "took the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] away from [[the-barbary-dragon|The Barbary Dragon]] — and made him into a farmer" (020-019). The people of the [[barbary-coast|Barbary Coast]], initially terrified when they saw [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] approaching — mistaking it for [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]]'s return — eventually sent [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] a token of gratitude for ridding the seas of the dreaded pirate (020-002, 020-025).

The episode illustrates [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s philosophy of reform over punishment. Rather than destroying [[ben-ali|Ben Ali]], he redirected him — converting a force of destruction into something productive, however absurd the specific calling (birdseed farming) might have seemed to the former pirate.
