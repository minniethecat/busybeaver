# Before the King

The audience before the [[king-of-the-jolliginki|King of the Jolliginki]] is the pivot point of [[doctor-dolittle-m-d|Doctor Dolittle]]'s [[africa-expedition|African]] chapters — the moment when [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s mission of mercy collides with royal suspicion, and a simple passage through a kingdom becomes a political crisis. What follows is imprisonment, ingenuity, and an escape that reshapes the kingdom itself.

## The Summons

The Doctor and his animals are intercepted at the border and brought before [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]]:

> "You must all come before the King," said the black man. (005-037)

The [[king-of-the-jolliginki|King of the Jolliginki]] is described as living in a palace with his Queen, Ermintrude, and their son, [[prince-bumpo|Prince Bumpo]]. (006-002) The summons carries the weight of absolute authority — in [[jolliginki|Jolliginki]], [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]]'s word is law, and refusal is not an option. The Doctor, whose entire mission is built on voluntary compassion, now faces compulsion.

## The King's Grievance

The King's hostility toward [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]] is not personal but historical. When [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] requests permission to [[travel-and-exploration|travel]] through his lands to reach the sick monkeys, [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]] refuses with a bitter recollection:

> "Many years ago a white man came to these shores," said the King. "And I was very kind to him. But after he had dug holes in the ground and found gold, he went away secretly — without even saying thank you." (006-004)

This grievance — a white visitor who exploited the kingdom's hospitality and resources — has hardened into a standing policy: no white man shall pass through [[jolliginki|Jolliginki]] again. The Doctor, arriving with his [[medicine-and-magic|medicine]]-bag and his animals, is judged not by his own actions but by the sins of his predecessor. The irony is sharp: Dolittle, the least exploitative white man imaginable, pays the price for colonial greed.

## The Palace

The audience takes place in [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]]'s palace, a setting that emphasizes the power differential:

> …the Doctor and the rest were taken before the King. (011-010)

The palace serves as both court and prison — the same space where [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]] dispenses justice becomes the site of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s captivity. The architecture of power is literalized: mud walls, royal authority, and no exit. At night, when the moon shines through the palm leaves, [[polynesia|Polynesia]] — who has hidden herself during the audience — creeps through the palace gathering intelligence for the escape. (006-018)

## The King's Judgment

The King's decision is swift and absolute. He does not negotiate or deliberate — he imprisons. [[polynesia|Polynesia]], watching from the shadows, delivers her assessment:

> "Foolish King!" answered Polynesia. "Have you forgotten that I can make you and all your people sick like the monkeys?" (006-029)

The judgment sets in motion the central conflict of chapters 5 through 12:

- **Imprisonment**: The Doctor and his animals are confined within the palace grounds
- **[[polynesia|Polynesia]]'s gambit**: The parrot hides and begins plotting, observing guard rotations and palace layouts
- **[[prince-bumpo|Prince Bumpo]]'s sympathy**: The [[prince-bumpo|King's son]], overhearing the animals' distress, is moved to intervene — setting up the bargain that will free them all

The King, meanwhile, underestimates his prisoners. He sees a shabby English doctor with a handful of pets; he does not see a network of animal intelligence that spans continents and speaks a dozen languages. His confidence in his mud walls and armed guards is, in retrospect, tragically misplaced.

## Escape and Aftermath

The audience before [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]] leads directly to [[escape-from-prison|the Doctor's escape from prison]], one of the book's most dramatic sequences. [[polynesia|Polynesia]]'s hidden observation during the audience gives the animals the intelligence they need to outmaneuver [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]]. What begins as a formal audience ends as a contest of wits between royal power and animal ingenuity — a contest [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]], for all his authority, cannot win.

> John Dolittle was the last to cross. And just as he was getting to the other side, the King's men came rushing up. (007-036)

## The Second Audience

The Doctor faces [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]] a second time after the escape attempt — a confrontation that tests whether the balance of power has shifted. By this point, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] has proven his medical prowess by curing [[chee-chee|the monkey]]s and has gained the loyalty of creatures across the jungle. The King, once absolute in his authority, now faces a man whose reputation has grown beyond anything his mud walls can contain.

The audience before [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]] thus becomes a recurring motif — each appearance marking a new stage in the power struggle between authority and compassion, suspicion and trust.

## Significance

The audience before [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]] serves as:

1. **The inciting incident**: It transforms the [[africa-expedition|African]] [[travel-and-exploration|adventure]] from a [[travel-and-exploration|journey]] into a crisis
2. **A study in power**: Absolute monarchy versus [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s moral authority — and [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s moral authority proves stronger
3. **A reckoning with colonial history**: The King's grievance against a previous white visitor makes Dolittle, unfairly, the inheritor of colonial guilt
4. **A test of loyalty**: The animals must stand together or fall separately
5. **[[prince-bumpo|Prince Bumpo]]'s awakening**: The prince begins his transformation from observer to ally, moved by the injustice he witnesses
