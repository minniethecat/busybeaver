# Escape from Prison

> Locked in the King of Jolliginki's strongest prison, with double guards and double locks, John Dolittle seems trapped. But the King's own son — a prince who dreams of fairy-tales — slips into the prison by moonlight and offers a bargain that changes everything.

## Summary

[[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]] and his animals are imprisoned by [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]] of [[jolliginki|Jolliginki]], who considers the foreign doctor a threat. After a brief first escape (using the animals' abilities to trick the guards), they are recaptured and locked in a stronger prison. [[prince-bumpo|Prince Bumpo]], [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]]'s son, secretly visits Dolittle by night and offers to help him escape — on the condition that Dolittle use his "[[medicine-and-magic|magical]]" [[medicine-and-magic|medicine]] to transform [[prince-bumpo|Bumpo]] into a fairy-tale prince.

## Context

Upon arriving in [[africa-expedition|Africa]], Dolittle passes through [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the Kingdom]] of [[jolliginki|Jolliginki]]. The King is suspicious of outsiders and orders Dolittle locked up:

> "Lock them up in my strongest prison." (006-005)

The animals briefly escape by tricking the guards, but they are recaptured. The King doubles the locks (011-011). Dolittle's situation seems hopeless — until [[polynesia|Polynesia]] spots [[prince-bumpo|Prince Bumpo]] in the garden.

> "Sh! — Look! There's Prince Bumpo coming into the garden! He mustn't see you." (011-021)

## Narrative

[[prince-bumpo|Prince Bumpo]] visits Dolittle in the prison camp by night. He has heard stories of Sleeping Beauty — of princes who kiss sleeping princesses awake — and he dreams of being such a prince. But he believes his current appearance is unworthy. He asks Dolittle to transform him:

> "Prince Bumpo," said the Doctor, looking thoughtfully at the bottles in his medicine-bag, "supposing I made your hair the color of a real prince?" (012-009)

Dolittle agrees — using his [[medicine-and-magic|medicines]] to change [[prince-bumpo|Bumpo]]'s appearance — and in return, [[prince-bumpo|Bumpo]] unlocks the prison doors. Under cover of darkness, Dolittle and all his animals slip away, racing toward [[chee-chee|the monkey]] territory and the Bridge of Apes that awaits them.

## Outcome & Consequences

The escape allows Dolittle to reach the [[land-of-the-monkeys|Land of the Monkeys]] — and, shortly afterward, to cross the Bridge of Apes to safety. The bargain with [[prince-bumpo|Bumpo]] introduces one of the book's most memorable comic subplots: a [[jolliginki|Jolliginki]] prince who now believes he looks like a European fairy-tale hero. [[prince-bumpo|Bumpo]]'s transformation, and Dolittle's slightly guilty relief at having performed it, provide a note of gentle satire about cultural exchange and the power of stories to cross borders.
