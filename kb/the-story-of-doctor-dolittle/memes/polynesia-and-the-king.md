# Polynesia and the King

## Summary

Imprisoned in a stone dungeon by [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]] of the [[jolliginki|Jolliginki]], [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]] and his animals are freed by [[polynesia|Polynesia the parrot]], who sneaks into [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]]'s bedroom and impersonates [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s voice, frightening [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]] into releasing them (006-018)(006-031).

## Context

When [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] and his animals arrived in [[africa-expedition|Africa]] seeking to help the sick monkeys, [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]] of the [[jolliginki|Jolliginki]] refused them passage through his lands. His reason was rooted in bitter history:

> "Many years ago a white man came to these shores; and I was very kind to him. But after he had dug holes in the ground to get the gold, and killed all the elephants to get their ivory tusks, he went away secretly in his ship — without so much as saying 'Thank you.' Never again shall a white man travel through the lands of Jolliginki."
>
> (006-004)

The King's grievance was not baseless. It was colonialism distilled to its ugliest elements — exploitation, extraction, and abandonment — and [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] paid the penalty for a crime he did not commit. The King ordered them all "shut up in a stone dungeon" with only one small window high up in the wall, and the door was "strong and thick" (006-005)(006-006).

## Narrative

While the animals grew sad in the dark prison — [[gub-gub|Gub-Gub the pig]] even began to cry — [[polynesia|Polynesia]] was not trapped with them. She had hidden in the pocket of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s coat tail, and only revealed herself once they were locked away (006-013). When the crocodile grumbled that she must have "sneaked off into the jungle as soon as her friends got into trouble," she retorted: "I'm not that kind of a bird. You see, I'm small enough to get through the bars of that window; and I was afraid they would put me in a cage instead. So while [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]] was busy talking, I hid in [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s pocket — and here I am! That's what you call a 'ruse'" (006-015).

### The Escape Route

[[polynesia|Polynesia]]'s choice of the pantry window was not accidental — it had been broken "by a tennis ball the week before" and never repaired (006-019). This small detail of domestic negligence in a mud palace turned out to be the key to [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s freedom. The window, invisible to the guards because it was inside the palace, gave [[polynesia|Polynesia]] a way in and out that no one was watching.

That night, when the moon shone through the palm trees and all [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]]'s men were asleep, "[[polynesia|the parrot]] slipped out through the bars of the prison window" (006-018). She flew to the palace, entered through the broken pantry window (damaged by a tennis ball the week before), crept up the stairs, and hid under [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]]'s bed (006-019).

[[polynesia|Polynesia]] began coughing — "just the way [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]] used to cough" — for she could mimic anyone (006-022). When [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]] woke, thinking it was [[queen-ermintrude|Queen Ermintrude]], she spoke in [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s voice, declaring herself "[[john-dolittle|John Dolittle, M.D.]] — the most wonderful man on earth" (006-029). She warned [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]] that if he did not release them, she would "raise my little finger — and you shall be struck dead" (006-029).

The terrified King ran to the prison and unlocked the door himself, crying "Doctor, it shall be as you say. Do not raise your little finger, please!" (006-031).

[[gub-gub|Gub-Gub]]'s earlier dismissal — "Oh, what can you do? You're only a bird!" (006-011) — was answered by [[polynesia|Polynesia]]'s performance. Her retort was telling: "I can talk like a man — and I know these darkies" (006-012). The second clause reveals [[polynesia|Polynesia]]'s worldly experience; she had spent over a century observing humans across continents, and understood their fears, their superstitions, and their predictable responses to a voice in the dark.

### The Trick Unraveled

The deception was discovered when the Queen, returning from a dance at her cousin's, saw [[polynesia|Polynesia]] escaping through the pantry window and realized "[[polynesia|the parrot]] getting out through the broken glass" had tricked them all (006-033). The King, "dreadfully angry," hurried back to the prison — but was too late. "The door stood open. The dungeon was empty. The Doctor and all his animals were gone" (006-034)(006-035).

The aftermath was fierce. [[queen-ermintrude|Queen Ermintrude]] "had never in her life seen her husband so terrible as he got that night. He gnashed his teeth with rage. He called everybody a fool. He threw his toothbrush at the palace cat" (007-001). He woke the entire army, and even sent the Queen — "tired from dancing in a pair of tight shoes" — into the jungle to hunt for [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] (007-001).

## Participants

- **[[polynesia|Polynesia]]** — the clever parrot who impersonates [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]
- **[[john-dolittle|John Dolittle]]** — the imprisoned Doctor
- **The [[king-of-the-jolliginki|King of the Jolliginki]]** — the superstitious ruler tricked by [[polynesia|the parrot]]'s voice
- **[[prince-bumpo|Prince Bumpo]]** — [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]]'s son, mentioned as away fishing

## Outcome & Consequences

The trick succeeded, but it also deepened [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]]'s fury and set the stage for [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s second capture. The Doctor and his animals escaped into the jungle, guided by [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]], and eventually reached the [[land-of-the-monkeys|Land of the Monkeys]]. But upon their return [[travel-and-exploration|journey]] through [[jolliginki|Jolliginki]], they would be captured again — and this time, no voice in the dark would free them (011-001).

[[polynesia|Polynesia]]'s performance revealed [[polynesia|the parrot]] as [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s most strategically valuable companion. She could do what none of the others could: she could enter human society undetected, speak its language, and manipulate its fears. In a world where [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] was constantly at the mercy of human authorities who did not understand him, [[polynesia|Polynesia]] was his secret weapon — a bird who could pass as a man, and a man who was terrifying indeed.

## The Power of Voice

The trick worked because [[polynesia|Polynesia]] understood a fundamental truth about [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]]'s worldview: he believed in [[medicine-and-magic|magic]]al power. The Doctor was a "[[medicine-and-magic|medicine]]-man" to the [[jolliginki|Jolliginki]], and the idea that such a man could make himself invisible and strike people dead with a raised finger was not absurd to [[king-of-the-jolliginki|the King]] — it was consistent with everything he understood about white men's powers (006-010).

[[polynesia|Polynesia]] weaponized [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s reputation, not his actual abilities. The Doctor could not make himself invisible; he could not strike people dead. But he had cured animals, and animals had told other animals, and the stories had grown. The King was not being tricked by a parrot so much as by the legend that had formed around [[john-dolittle|John Dolittle]] without his knowledge or consent — a legend that [[polynesia|Polynesia]], who knew [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] intimately, was uniquely positioned to exploit.
