# The Rats' Warning

## Summary

While [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] rested in a bay of the [[canary-islands|Canary Islands]], a whole company of rats began abandoning the vessel. One big black rat stepped forward to deliver a formal warning: the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]]'s timbers were rotten and it would sink before the following night. This eerie but accurate prediction from the humblest of creatures gave [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] crucial advance notice of his vessel's fate (014-004)(014-012).

## Context

After escaping from [[africa-expedition|Africa]], [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] was being pulled through the sea by swallows. The labor was exhausting and [[the-swallow-messenger|the swallow]]s needed rest, so they towed the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] into a beautiful bay of the [[canary-islands|Canary Islands]] — a place with "a very beautiful, high, green mountain in the middle of it" (014-001)(014-002). The Doctor and his animals disembarked to search for fresh water and stretch their legs (014-003).

## Narrative

As the party prepared to go ashore, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] noticed "a whole lot of rats were coming up from downstairs and leaving the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] as well." [[jip-the-dog|Jip]] immediately started to chase them — "because chasing rats had always been his favorite game" — but [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] held him back (014-004).

One big black rat stepped forward timidly along the rail, watching [[jip-the-dog|the dog]] out of the corner of his eye. After coughing nervously and cleaning his whiskers, he addressed [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] directly (014-005):

> "You have heard that rats always leave a sinking ship?" (014-008)

The Doctor acknowledged he had been told so. The rat explained that people "always speak of it with a sneer — as though it were something disgraceful. But you can't blame us, can you? After all, who WOULD stay on a sinking [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]], if he could get off it?" (014-010).

Then came the warning itself — delivered with the precision of a professional inspection:

> "This is a bad ship you have here. It isn't safe. The sides aren't strong enough. Its boards are rotten. Before tomorrow night it will sink to the bottom of the sea." (014-012)

When [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] asked how the rat could know this, the answer was both charming and uncanny:

> "The tips of our tails get that tingly feeling — like when your foot's asleep. This morning, at six o'clock, while I was getting breakfast, my tail suddenly began to tingle." (014-014)

The rat had consulted his aunt — "the long, piebald rat, rather skinny, who came to see you in [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]] last Spring with jaundice" — and her tail was tingling too. That confirmed it: the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] had less than two days (014-014).

The Doctor thanked the rat warmly: "Very considerate of you — very! Give my regards to your aunt. I remember her perfectly" (014-015). The rats then departed to find a new home on the island.

## Outcome & Consequences

The rats' prophecy was fulfilled with terrible speed. While [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] was exploring the [[canary-islands|Canary Islands]] and receiving a warm welcome from the canaries, two swallows arrived with shocking news: pirates had boarded the abandoned [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] (014-024). The Doctor seized the opportunity to commandeer the pirates' own vessel — a much faster [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] — and escape, leaving his doomed original [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] behind (014-027)(014-028).

The episode demonstrates a core theme of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s worldview: even the humblest and most despised creatures possess knowledge that the powerful lack. The rat — a being most people would chase away with a broom — delivered a warning that was as precise and reliable as any naval engineer's report. The Doctor's courtesy toward him ("Very considerate of you — very!") reflects his consistent belief that all creatures deserve respect and a hearing.
