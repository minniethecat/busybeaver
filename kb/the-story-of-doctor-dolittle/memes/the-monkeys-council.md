# The Monkeys' Council

## Summary

When [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] learned that [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]] planned to return to England, they held a great council to decide what to give him. Led by [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee the monkey]], they discussed various gifts before settling on the pushmi-pullyu — an animal no white man had ever seen (009-026)(009-036).

## Context

After [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] had cured the terrible sickness among [[chee-chee|the monkeys]], he "went to bed and slept for three days without even turning over" . [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] stood guard outside his door, "keeping everybody away till he woke up" (009-001). When [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] finally awoke, his first words were that "he must now go back to [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]]" (009-002).

The monkeys "were very surprised at this; for they had thought that he was going to stay with them forever" (009-002). That night, all [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] gathered in the jungle to discuss his departure. The scale of the assembly was immense — all those thousands of monkeys who had been cured, sitting together in the darkness under the [[africa-expedition|African]] sky, debating what to do about the man who had saved them.

## Narrative

The Chief Chimpanzee rose and asked why the good man was leaving, but none could answer. The Grand Gorilla proposed asking [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] to stay, suggesting they build him "a new house and a bigger bed" and promise him "plenty of monkey-servants to work for him and to make life pleasant for him" (009-007).

But [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]], "the great Traveler," rose to speak. All the others whispered, "Sh! Look! [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]], the great Traveler, is about to speak!" (009-010). [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] had seen the [[land-of-the-white-men|Land of the White Men]]; he had [[travel-and-exploration|traveled]] with an organ-grinder through European cities. He understood, as the jungle monkeys could not, the constraints that bound [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]:

"My friends, I am afraid it is useless to ask [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] to stay. He owes [[money-and-wealth|money]] in [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]]; and he says he must go back and pay for the house he lives in" (009-010).

When [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] asked "What is [[money-and-wealth|money]]?" [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] explained that in the [[land-of-the-white-men|Land of the White Men]] "you could get nothing without [[money-and-wealth|money]]; you could do nothing without it — that it was almost impossible to live without [[money-and-wealth|money]]" (009-011)(009-012).

The monkeys were incredulous. "But can you not even eat and drink without paying?" they asked. [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] shook his head. Even he, when he was with [[the-italian-organ-grinder|the organ-grinder]], "had been made to ask the children for [[money-and-wealth|money]]" (009-014).

The Chief Chimpanzee turned to the Oldest Orangutan and said, "Cousin, surely these Men be strange creatures! Who would wish to live in such a land? My gracious, how paltry!" (009-016).

The monkeys sat "quite still upon the ground and thinking hard" (009-018). The idea of [[money-and-wealth|money]] — an invisible debt that compelled a man to leave paradise — was the most alien concept they had ever encountered.

### The Gift Debate

The Biggest Baboon broke the silence, proposing they give [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] "a fine present to take with him, so that he may know we are grateful for all that he has done for us" (009-020). A "little, tiny red monkey who was sitting up in a tree" shouted his agreement, and then "they all cried out, making a great noise, 'Yes, yes. Let us give him the finest present a White Man ever had!'" (009-022)(009-023).

The debate that followed revealed [[chee-chee|the monkeys]]' limited understanding of the White Man's world. They proposed "fifty bags of coconuts" and "a hundred bunches of bananas — at least he shall not have to buy his fruit in the Land Where You Pay to Eat!" (009-024). But [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] said these "would be too heavy to carry so far and would go bad before half was eaten" (009-025).

Then [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] made the suggestion that would change everything: "If you want to please him, give him an animal. You may be sure he will be kind to it. Give him some rare animal they have not got in the menageries" (009-026).

When [[chee-chee|the monkeys]] asked what "menageries" were, [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] explained they were "places in the [[land-of-the-white-men|Land of the White Men]], where animals were put in cages for people to come and look at" (009-028). The monkeys were "very shocked and said to one another, 'These Men are like thoughtless young ones — stupid and easily amused. Sh! It is a prison he means'" (009-029).

They considered the iguana — but [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] said "Yes, there is one in the London Zoo" (009-032). They proposed the okapi — "Yes. In Belgium, where my organ-grinder took me five years ago, they had an okapi in a big city they call Antwerp" (009-034).

Finally one monkey asked: "Have they a pushmi-pullyu?" [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] replied, "No. No White Man has ever seen a pushmi-pullyu. Let us give him that" (009-035)(009-036).

## Participants

- **[[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]]** — [[chee-chee|the monkey]] [[travel-and-exploration|traveler]] whose knowledge of the White Man's world guides the council's decision
- **Chief Chimpanzee** — who first questions [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s departure
- **Grand Gorilla** — who proposes asking [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] to stay
- **Oldest Orangutan** — who receives the Chief Chimpanzee's remark about the strangeness of Men
- **Biggest Baboon** — who proposes the gift
- **Tiny Red Monkey** — the first to second the motion from the treetops
- **Major of the Marmosettes** — who asks about the iguana
- **[[john-dolittle|John Dolittle]]** — the absent recipient of [[chee-chee|the monkeys]]' gratitude, sleeping behind a guarded door

## Outcome & Consequences

The council's decision to give the pushmi-pullyu as a gift directly led to [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] acquiring the rarest animal in existence, which would later prove crucial as a source of income during the circus tour (021-002). The council also showed [[chee-chee|the monkeys]]' remarkable capacity for organized deliberation and gratitude.

The gift also completed an economic circle. The Doctor had come to [[africa-expedition|Africa]] with nothing — having borrowed a boat and food on credit — and was returning with an asset more valuable than anything he had ever owned. The monkeys, who had no [[money-and-wealth|money]], had given him something that would, paradoxically, solve his [[money-and-wealth|money]] problems forever.

## The Gift as Culture Clash

The council scene is a sustained exercise in cross-cultural misunderstanding — and cross-cultural generosity. The monkeys, who have no concept of [[money-and-wealth|money]], struggle to comprehend a world where food must be purchased. They propose gifts based on their own values (food, hospitality) and are gently corrected by [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]], who serves as translator between worlds.

The pushmi-pullyu is a perfect gift precisely because it bridges both worlds. It is rare enough to command attention in the [[land-of-the-white-men|Land of the White Men]]; it is a living creature, not an object, fitting [[chee-chee|the monkeys]]' understanding of what has value; and [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]]'s insistence that [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] "will be kind to it" is both true and necessary — an acknowledgment that the gift carries moral responsibility (009-026).
