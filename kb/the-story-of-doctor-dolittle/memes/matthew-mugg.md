# Matthew Mugg

Matthew Mugg, known throughout [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]] as the Cat's-meatman, is one of the few humans in the town who never turns his back on [[doctor-dolittle-m-d|Doctor Dolittle]]. While others see [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s growing household of animals as eccentric or disreputable, Matthew Mugg sees an opportunity — and ultimately becomes a crucial link in [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s transformation into an animal physician.

## Role in the Story

[[the-cats-meatman|The Cat's-meatman]] is introduced early as a regular figure in the Dolittle household. Even when most of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s human acquaintances have drifted away, Mugg continues his visits:

> …no one left — except the Cat's-meatman, who didn't mind any kind of animals. (001-010)

His acceptance of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s unconventional household sets him apart from the judgmental townspeople and [[sarah-dolittle|Sarah Dolittle]].

## The Catalyst for Animal Medicine

It is Matthew Mugg, conversing with [[polynesia]] [[polynesia|the parrot]] in [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s kitchen, who provides the pivotal suggestion that redirects [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s career:

> "You see, Doctor," the Cat's-meatman went on, "you know all about animals…" (002-004)

> When the Cat's-meatman had gone the parrot flew onto the table… (002-007)

## His Wife's Ailment

Mugg brings a personal concern — his wife's illness — to [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]:

> …asked the Cat's-meatman. "Just a little something wrong with the missus…" (002-006)

This moment humanizes Mugg further: he is not merely a tradesman but a husband seeking help, and his trust in [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s abilities (even in animal [[medicine-and-magic|medicine]]) reflects genuine faith.

## Loyalty and Farewell

When [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] departs for [[africa-expedition|Africa]], Mugg is there at the dockside:

> The Cat's-meatman was there to see them off; (004-029)

His presence at this moment — when [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] leaves everything familiar behind — affirms a quiet but steadfast loyalty. Among all the shopkeepers and townspeople of [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]], Matthew Mugg shows up.

## Significance

Matthew Mugg serves as a narrative bridge between the human world of [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]] and the animal world [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] is entering. He is:

- **The catalyst**: His kitchen conversation with [[polynesia|Polynesia]] triggers [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s career change
- **The witness**: He spreads word of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s new practice
- **The loyalist**: He remains when others abandon [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]], and sees him off to [[africa-expedition|Africa]]
