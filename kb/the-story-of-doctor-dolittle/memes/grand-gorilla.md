# Grand Gorilla

The Grand Gorilla was a powerful leader among the primates of the African jungle, renowned for his immense physical strength — said to equal that of seven horses (010-033). He participated in both the [[the-monkeys-council|Monkeys' Council]] and the Doctor's farewell feast.

## At the Council

When [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]] announced his intention to return to [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby]], the Grand Gorilla rose at [[the-monkeys-council|the Monkeys' Council]] to propose a solution. He suggested that the monkeys build the Doctor a new house with a bigger bed and provide him with monkey-servants, hoping that comfort and honor might persuade him to remain in Africa (009-006, 009-007).

## The Memorial Stone

At the grand farewell party held for the Doctor, after the feast of pineapples and mangoes, the Grand Gorilla performed an act of lasting significance:

> And the Grand Gorilla, who had the strength of seven horses in his hairy arms, rolled a great rock up to the head of the table and said, "This stone for all time shall mark the spot."
>
> (010-033, 010-034)

The stone remained in the heart of the jungle as a monument, and generations of monkey-mothers would point to it, telling their children of the Good White Man who sat and ate with them in the Year of the Great Sickness (010-035).
