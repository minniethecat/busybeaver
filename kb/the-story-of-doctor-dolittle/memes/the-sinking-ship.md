# The Sinking Ship

## Summary

During the final leg of the [[voyage-and-sailing|voyage]] to [[africa-expedition|Africa]], [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]]'s [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] struck a rock in a [[the-storm-at-sea|great storm]] and began taking on water rapidly. With the help of his animal companions — particularly [[polynesia|Polynesia]] [[polynesia|the parrot]] and [[dab-dab|Dab-Dab]] [[dab-dab|the duck]] — [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] organized a resourceful evacuation, guiding everyone safely to the [[africa-expedition|African]] shore without a single life lost (005-014)(005-021).

## Context

The Doctor had set sail from [[puddleby-on-the-marsh|Puddleby-on-the-Marsh]] with a full company of animals, bound for [[africa-expedition|Africa]] to cure the sick monkeys. For six weeks they sailed "on and on, over the rolling sea," following [[the-swallow-messenger|the swallow]] who flew before the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] to show them the way, carrying a tiny lantern at night so they should not lose her in the dark (005-001).

As they approached the Equator, the weather grew warmer. [[polynesia|Polynesia]], [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]], and the crocodile "enjoyed the hot sun no end" and ran about laughing, eager to sight land (005-002). Flying-fishes came to greet the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] and confirmed they were "only fifty-five miles now to the coast of [[africa-expedition|Africa]]" (005-005). Porpoises danced through the waves and, learning of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s mission, fetched wild onions from a nearby island "in big nets made of seaweed" to replenish the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]]'s stores (005-006)(005-009).

## Narrative

As the sun set on the [[voyage-and-sailing|voyage]]'s final evening, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] called for his telescope: "Our [[travel-and-exploration|journey]] is nearly ended. Very soon we should be able to see the shores of [[africa-expedition|Africa]]" (005-011). But darkness fell and a violent storm erupted:

> "The wind howled; the rain came down in torrents; and the waves got so high they splashed right over the boat" (005-013).

The disaster struck without warning:

> "Presently there was a big BANG! The ship stopped and rolled over on its side" (005-014).

[[polynesia|Polynesia]] immediately diagnosed the situation: "I'm not sure, but I think we're [[voyage-and-sailing|shipwrecked]]. Tell [[dab-dab|the duck]] to get out and see" (005-016). [[dab-dab|Dab-Dab the duck]] dived beneath the waves and surfaced with the grim assessment: they had struck a rock, there was "a big hole in the bottom of the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]]," water was pouring in, and they were sinking fast (005-017).

The Doctor, recognizing they must have "run into [[africa-expedition|Africa]]" itself, declared calmly: "We must all swim to land" (005-018). But [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee the monkey]] and [[gub-gub|Gub-Gub]] [[gub-gub|the pig]] could not swim (005-019). It was [[polynesia|Polynesia]] who devised the ingenious solution: she ordered [[dab-dab|Dab-Dab]] to fly one end of the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]]'s rope to the shore and tie it to a palm-tree, creating "what you call a 'lifeline'" (005-020).

The evacuation was a model of animal teamwork. Those who could swim or fly made their own way to land; [[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]] and [[gub-gub|Gub-Gub]] climbed along the rope to safety, carrying [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s trunk and handbag with them (005-021). When morning came, they emerged from their cave shelter to dry themselves on the sandy beach, and [[polynesia|Polynesia]] sighed with emotion at the sight of her homeland after a hundred and sixty-nine years away (005-023)(005-025).

## Participants

- **[[john-dolittle|John Dolittle]]** — [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] who coordinated the evacuation with calm resolve
- **[[polynesia|Polynesia]]** — [[polynesia|the parrot]] who diagnosed the [[voyage-and-sailing|shipwreck]] and devised the rope-lifeline solution
- **[[dab-dab|Dab-Dab]]** — [[dab-dab|the duck]] who dove to inspect the damage, retrieved [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s hat, and rescued a stowaway mouse
- **[[chee-chee|Chee-Chee]]** — [[chee-chee|the monkey]] who served as lookout but could not swim
- **[[gub-gub|Gub-Gub]]** — [[gub-gub|the pig]] who also could not swim and required the lifeline

## Outcome & Consequences

The [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] was destroyed — "the rough sea beat it to pieces on the rocks and the timbers floated away" (005-022). The Doctor lost his high hat to the waves, though [[dab-dab|Dab-Dab]] recovered it the next morning (005-027). Inside the hat, she discovered a stowaway white mouse who had hidden in the baggage because he "wanted to see what [[africa-expedition|Africa]] was like" and had relatives there (005-029)(005-030).

The [[voyage-and-sailing|shipwreck]] left [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] and his animals stranded on the [[africa-expedition|African]] coast with no vessel to return home. Yet this misfortune became the beginning of their [[africa-expedition|African]] [[travel-and-exploration|adventures]]. The loss of the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] meant there was no turning back — [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] was now fully committed to reaching [[chee-chee|the monkey]] kingdom and completing his mission of mercy.

## Significance

The [[voyage-and-sailing|shipwreck]] episode is one of the earliest demonstrations of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s unflappable temperament in crisis. While others might panic, his immediate response is calm, practical problem-solving. The episode also showcases the animals not merely as companions but as essential participants — [[polynesia|Polynesia]]'s quick thinking and [[dab-dab|Dab-Dab]]'s diving skills are directly responsible for saving lives. The stowaway mouse, hiding in the baggage out of curiosity, adds a comic grace note to an otherwise perilous scene.
