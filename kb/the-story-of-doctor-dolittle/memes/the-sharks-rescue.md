# The Sharks' Rescue

## Summary

During [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s return [[voyage-and-sailing|voyage]] from [[africa-expedition|Africa]], his [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] escaped from the [[barbary-coast|Barbary]] pirates but then became becalmed. The swallows, who had been towing the vessel, grew exhausted from the hard labor. At this critical moment, a school of sharks appeared — not as a threat, but as unexpected allies. The sharks, grateful for [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s kindness to animals, offered to pull the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] onward, saving the party from being stranded at sea.

## The Swallows' Exhaustion

Dragging a [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] through the sea is hard work. After two or three hours, [[the-swallow-messenger|the swallows]] began to tire, their wings aching and their breath growing short. The Doctor's [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]], which had just escaped the pirates, now faced a different danger — being left motionless on the open ocean.

Without wind to fill the sails, and with [[the-swallow-messenger|the swallows]] nearing the limits of their endurance, the situation grew desperate. The Doctor and his animals watched the exhausted birds with growing concern.

## The Sharks Arrive

Suddenly, a cry went up from one of the animals. Dark fins were cutting through the water toward them.

> "The sharks! The sharks are coming!" (015-024)

At first the cry was one of terror — sharks were known to be dangerous creatures. But these sharks were different. They had heard of [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] and his remarkable kindness to animals of every kind. Rather than threatening the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]], they made an extraordinary offer: they would use their great strength to pull the vessel through the water themselves.

## Gratitude and Farewell

The sharks proved powerful and willing helpers. With their assistance, the [[voyage-and-sailing|ship]] made swift progress. When at last they reached safer waters, [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]] thanked them warmly for their kindness.

> Having thanked the sharks again for their kindness, the Doctor and his pets set off once more on their journey home. (016-001)

## Outcome

The sharks' rescue demonstrated a recurring theme in [[john-dolittle|Doctor Dolittle]]'s [[travel-and-exploration|adventures]]: his kindness to animals was returned in unexpected ways. Creatures that other men feared — sharks, pirates, kings — became allies when they recognized his gentle nature. The incident also showed that in [[john-dolittle|the Doctor]]'s world, the line between "dangerous beast" and "loyal friend" was drawn not by species, but by character.
