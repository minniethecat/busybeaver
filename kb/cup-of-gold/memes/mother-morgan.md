# Mother Morgan

## Role

Mother Morgan is Henry Morgan's mother and the sole practical force in a household dominated by dreamers and visionaries. While her husband [[old-robert-morgan|Old Robert]] gazed into the fire and her mother-in-law [[gwenliana|Gwenliana]] practiced folk divination, Mother Morgan managed the farm, the meals, and the thatch. She represents the novel's grounding in domestic reality — the earth-bound, material life that Henry must leave behind to pursue his destiny on the sea.

## Biography

Mother Morgan was "too busy with the day itself to be bothered with the foolishness of abstractions" [001-011]. In her view, "someone in the family had to be practical or the thatch would blow away — and what could you expect of a pack of dreamers like Robert and Gwenliana and her son Henry?" [001-011]. She bore the burden of holding the household together while the others drifted in their visions.

She had little patience for Gwenliana's prophecies and magical pretensions. The "play of claque to a simpleton was a harsh tax on the convictions of Mother Morgan" [001-009], yet she tolerated it as part of the family fabric she maintained. All the family "loved Mother Morgan and feared her and got in her way" [001-012] — a testament to her formidable, loving presence.

When news arrived that fifteen-year-old Henry was to be sent to the Indies, Mother Morgan's response was immediate and fierce: "He's only a little boy. He can't be going to the Indies" [002-007]. Her maternal protectiveness clashed with the inevitability of Henry's departure, and she dismissed Gwenliana's prophetic consolations as "Nonsense!" [002-019].

Years later, when Henry returned to Wales, Mother Morgan "jumped from her seat and went angrily to Henry" [005-001] — her anger masking the relief and love of a mother who had long feared her son lost to the distant world.

## Relationships

- **[[henry-morgan|Sir Henry Morgan]]** — Son. Mother Morgan's fierce practicality shaped Henry's early life; her anger at his departure and her joy at his return bracket his transformation from farm boy to buccaneer.
- **[[old-robert-morgan|Old Robert Morgan]]** — Husband. She shared his life but not his dreaminess; together they formed the complementary poles of Henry's Welsh childhood.
- **[[gwenliana|Gwenliana]]** — Mother-in-law. Mother Morgan resented the time and energy wasted on Gwenliana's magic but maintained the household around her.
