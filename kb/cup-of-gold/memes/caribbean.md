## Overview

The Caribbean in *Cup of Gold* is more than a geographic setting — it is the theater of transformation where European identities are unmade and remade. Henry Morgan arrives in the Indies as an indentured servant and rises to become its most feared buccaneer and eventual governor. The Caribbean's geography — its hidden coves, tropical diseases, volatile weather, and strategic chokepoints — shapes every phase of his career.

## In the Novel

The novel presents the Caribbean as a world apart from Europe, governed by different rules. The "green crown of the Indies" [007-004] glows on the horizon as a promise of wealth and liberation, but the reality is brutal: fever, slavery, and violent death await most who arrive. The Caribbean is where England's unwanted — younger sons, convicts, religious dissenters — are sent to be consumed by the colonial machine [008-005].

The buccaneers thrive in this environment precisely because they adapt to its lawlessness. The Caribbean's geography enables their operations: the labyrinthine coastline of Darien, the hidden anchorages of [[tortuga|Tortuga]], the strategic port of Port Royal — each location serves a tactical purpose in the buccaneer economy of raid and retreat.

The novel contrasts the European perception of the Caribbean (an exotic paradise yielding gold) with its reality (a graveyard of ambition where even the successful are hollowed out). Morgan's death in Port Royal — isolated, haunted, and despised — is the Caribbean's final judgment on its most famous son.

## Key Locations Within

- **[[port-royal|Port Royal]], [[jamaica|Jamaica]]** — Morgan's base of operations and eventual seat as Lieutenant-Governor
- **Darien** — the wild jungle isthmus crossed during the Panama campaign
- **Tortuga** — the buccaneer haven and early headquarters of the [[free-brotherhood-of-the-coast|Free Brotherhood]]
- **Barbados** — Morgan's place of indentured servitude under James Flower
- **[[hispaniola|Hispaniola]]** — the buccaneers' original hunting grounds

## Related Characters

- **[[henry-morgan|Henry Morgan]]** — the Caribbean is the only world in which his rise is possible
- **The Buccaneers** — collectively, they are creatures of the Caribbean, impossible in Europe
- **[[edward-mansveldt|Edward Mansveldt]]** — his dream of a buccaneer republic imagines the Caribbean as a new nation rather than a colonial periphery
