# Captain Sawkins

## Role

Captain Sawkins is a buccaneer captain serving under Henry Morgan during the Panama campaign. Described as a short, serious man whose eyes "burned with the fury of a Puritan prophet," he represents the disciplined, morally intense strain of buccaneer that contrasts with the casual cruelty of figures like L'Ollonais.

## Biography

Captain Sawkins is introduced among Morgan's assembled captains during the council of war before Panama. He is "the short, serious Captain Sawkins, whose eyes burned with the fury of a Puritan prophet" `[010-052]`. Unlike many buccaneers driven by greed or bloodlust, Sawkins is animated by a fierce moral intensity — the buccaneer cause is, for him, a kind of holy war.

When the captains are seated and the council begins, Sawkins speaks out with characteristic directness `[010-057]`. His training has made him "abhor disorder" — he questions the departure from buccaneer custom, demanding to know why Morgan's plan deviates from established practice `[010-058]`. He begins to object when Panama is named as the target, but Morgan cuts him off `[010-062]`. After hearing the full plan, Sawkins concedes: "It is dangerous — dangerous, but good" `[010-065]`.

During the buccaneer celebration following the sack of Panama, Captain Sawkins stands apart from the revelry. He "glowered at the dancers" `[010-356]`, his Puritan severity refusing to join in the debauchery. In this moment, Sawkins embodies the contradiction at the heart of the buccaneer enterprise — holy violence pursued by men who believe themselves instruments of divine justice, yet whose methods are indistinguishable from the worst atrocities.

## Historical Basis

Historical records mention a Captain Sawkins (or Sawkins) among the buccaneers of Morgan's era, though details of his career are sparse. Steinbeck's characterization — the Puritan buccaneer with burning eyes — draws on the historical reality that many English privateers framed their activities in religious terms, viewing Spanish Catholics as legitimate targets of Protestant holy war.
