# Edward Mansveldt

## Role

Edward Mansveldt was a Dutch buccaneer admiral and one of the most celebrated figures on the Spanish Main during Henry Morgan's early career. Operating from the pirate haven of [[tortuga|Tortuga]], Mansveldt dreamed of establishing a permanent buccaneer republic — an independent state of freebooters that would transcend the loose, opportunistic alliances that characterized Caribbean piracy. His execution by the Spaniards elevated him to martyrdom and passed the torch of leadership to Morgan, who would go on to realize many of Mansveldt's ambitions.

## Biography

Mansveldt's reputation preceded him throughout the Caribbean. When the young Henry Morgan first spoke of going a-buccaneering, veteran sailors invoked Mansveldt's name with reverence: "Take Mansveldt; there's a master for you — a real master. But Mansveldt is in Tortuga" [008-347].

Henry Morgan sailed to Tortuga specifically to join Mansveldt's fleet. Upon arriving with his plunder from the [[ganymede|Ganymede]], Henry presented himself to the Dutchman. "His little eyes glittered as he looked at this plunder" [009-029], and Mansveldt immediately recognized Morgan's quality, regarding him "with admiration" [009-034]. The Dutchman's approval thrilled Henry: "The name of Mansveldt was mighty on the seas, and Henry flushed with pleasure" [009-036].

Mansveldt's career ended tragically. His ship was wrecked near Havana, and the Spaniards captured and executed him on the garotte [009-022]. His death became a rallying point for the buccaneer brotherhood and left a leadership vacuum that Morgan — along with other captains like [[bras-de-fer|Bras de Fer]] and [[l-ollonais|L'Ollonais]] — would eventually fill.

## Historical Basis

The historical Edward Mansveldt (also spelled Mansfield or Mansvelt) was indeed a Dutch buccaneer active in the 1660s. He led significant raids against Spanish settlements in Cuba and Central America, and some accounts credit him with the early conception of a buccaneer stronghold — a vision partially realized when Morgan later captured and fortified [[port-royal|Port Royal]]. Mansveldt's execution by the Spanish in 1666 created the opening for Morgan's rise to leadership of the buccaneer fleet.

## Relationships

- **[[henry-morgan|Sir Henry Morgan]]** — Protégé and successor. Mansveldt recognized the young Welshman's strategic brilliance and welcomed him into the buccaneer elite.
- **[[bras-de-fer|Bras de Fer]]** — Fellow buccaneer captain; an older contemporary who survived Mansveldt.
- **[[l-ollonais|L'Ollonais]]** — Another fearsome buccaneer of Mansveldt's era, later killed by Indians of Darien.
