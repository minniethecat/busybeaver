# Alderman Tubber

An alderman of [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] noted for his jovial manner — "jocular Mr. Tubber" — who appears among [[casterbridge|the town]]'s civic figures at moments of public significance.

## Appearances

Tubber first appears in the magistrates' court, where his physical gesture marks a moment of tension:

> "Henchard," said Alderman Tubber, applying his thumb to the coin. [017-154]

Described as "jocular Mr. Tubber" [017-163], he represents the lighter side of [[casterbridge|the town]]'s civic character — a contrast to the gravity of [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s tragic trajectory.

He reappears later among the assembled civic figures — "Dr. Bath, Lawyer Long, Alderman Tubber, and several" [043-168] — during the council proceedings that follow the novel's climactic events.
