# Farfrae's Arrival

[[donald-farfrae|Donald Farfrae]]'s arrival in [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] is the novel's pivotal turning point — the moment when [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s world acquires its counterweight and his tragedy acquires its mechanism. [[donald-farfrae|Farfrae]] is "pas[[guilt-and-redemption|sin]]g through" on his way to America when [[michael-henchard|Henchard]], impressed by the young man's competence and charm, persuades him to stay (009-005).

## Narrative Context

Farfrae's arrival introduces the novel's central contrast: [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s [[temper-and-impulse|impulsive]], emotional [[power-and-authority|authority]] versus [[donald-farfrae|Farfrae]]'s methodical, modern competence. "[[donald-farfrae|Farfrae]]'s character was just the reverse of [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s" (019-002) — and it is this reversal that [[ambition-and-will|will]] [[ambition-and-will|drive]] the plot through rivalry, reversal, and eventual [[farfrae-becomes-mayor|transfer of power]].

## Event Details

- **Setting**: [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]], the [[the-kings-arms|King's Arms]] inn (009-005)
- **Catalyst**: [[donald-farfrae|Farfrae]]'s skill at reviving a failing batch of corn
- **Action**: [[michael-henchard|Henchard]], impressed and lonely, offers [[donald-farfrae|Farfrae]] a position as his manager
- **[[donald-farfrae|Farfrae]]'s decision**: "Done," said [[donald-farfrae|Donald Farfrae]] (010-090) — a [[guilt-and-redemption|sin]]gle word that seals both men's [[fate-and-determinism|fate]]s

## Consequences

Farfrae's arrival sets in motion the novel's central dynamic: the master-pupil relationship that becomes a rivalry, the transfer of commercial and political [[gender-and-power|power]] from the old order to the new, and the romantic triangle involving [[lucetta-templeman|Lucetta]]. Without [[donald-farfrae|Farfrae]], [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s character flaws would have had no foil and no catalyst.
