# Section 57

(056-001) “Dear me—is that so!” said [[donald-farfrae|Farfrae]].

(056-002) As for Elizabeth, she said nothing.

(056-003) “Upon the head of his bed he pinned a piece of paper, with some writing

(056-004) upon it,” continued [[abel-whittle|Abel Whittle]]. “But not being a man o’ letters, I

(056-005) can’t read writing; so I don’t know what it is. I can get it and show

(056-006) ye.”

(056-007) They stood in silence while he ran into the cottage; returning in a

(056-008) moment with a crumpled scrap of paper. On it there was pencilled as

(056-009) follows:—

(056-010) MICHAEL HENCHARD’S WILL.

(056-011) “That [[elizabeth-jane-newson|Elizabeth-Jane]] Farfrae be not told of my death, or made to grieve

(056-012) on account of me.

(056-013) “& that I be not bury’d in consecrated ground.

(056-014) “& that no sexton be asked to toll the bell.

(056-015) “& that nobody is wished to see my dead body.

(056-016) “& that no murners walk behind me at my funeral.

(056-017) “& that no flours be planted on my grave.

(056-018) “& that no man remember me.

(056-019) “To this I put my name.

(056-020) “MICHAEL HENCHARD.”

(056-021) “What are we to do?” said Donald, when he had handed the paper to her.

(056-022) She could not answer distinctly. “O Donald!” she cried at last through

(056-023) her tears, “what bitterness lies there! O I would not have minded so

(056-024) much if it had not been for my unkindness at that last parting!... But

(056-025) there’s no altering—so it must be.”

(056-026) What [[michael-henchard|Henchard]] had written in the anguish of his dying was respected as

(056-027) far as practicable by Elizabeth-Jane, though less from a sense of the

(056-028) sacredness of last words, as such, than from her independent knowledge

(056-029) that the man who wrote them meant what he said. She knew the directions

(056-030) to be a piece of the same stuff that his whole life was made of, and

(056-031) hence were not to be tampered with to give herself a mournful pleasure,

(056-032) or her husband credit for large-heartedness.

(056-033) All was over at last, even her regrets for having misunderstood him on

(056-034) his last visit, for not having searched him out sooner, though these

(056-035) were deep and sharp for a good while. From this time forward

(056-036) Elizabeth-Jane found herself in a latitude of calm weather, kindly and

(056-037) grateful in itself, and doubly so after the Capharnaum in which some of

(056-038) her preceding years had been spent. As the lively and sparkling

(056-039) emotions of her early married life cohered into an equable serenity,

(056-040) the finer movements of her nature found scope in discovering to the

(056-041) narrow-lived ones around her the secret (as she had once learnt it) of

(056-042) making limited opportunities endurable; which she deemed to consist in

(056-043) the cunning enlargement, by a species of microscopic treatment, of

(056-044) those minute forms of satisfaction that offer themselves to everybody

(056-045) not in positive pain; which, thus handled, have much of the same

(056-046) inspiring effect upon life as wider interests cursorily embraced.

(056-047) Her teaching had a reflex action upon herself, insomuch that she

(056-048) thought she could perceive no great personal difference between being

(056-049) respected in the nether parts of [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] and glorified at the

(056-050) uppermost end of the social world. Her position was, indeed, to a

(056-051) marked degree one that, in the common phrase, afforded much to be

(056-052) thankful for. That she was not demonstratively thankful was no fault of

(056-053) hers. Her experience had been of a kind to teach her, rightly or

(056-054) wrongly, that the doubtful [[reputation-and-social-standing|honour]] of a brief transit through a sorry

(056-055) world hardly called for effusiveness, even when the path was suddenly

(056-056) irradiated at some half-way point by daybeams rich as hers. But her

(056-057) strong sense that neither she nor any human being deserved less than

(056-058) was given, did not blind her to the fact that there were others

(056-059) receiving less who had deserved much more. And in being forced to [[class-and-social-order|class]]

(056-060) herself among the fortunate she did not cease to wonder at the

(056-061) persistence of the unforeseen, when the one to whom such unbroken

(056-062) tranquility had been accorded in the adult stage was she whose youth

(056-063) had seemed to teach that happiness was but the occasional episode in a

(056-064) general drama of pain.
