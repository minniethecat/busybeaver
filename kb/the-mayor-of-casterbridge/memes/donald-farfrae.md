# Donald Farfrae

The foil to [[michael-henchard|Michael Henchard]] and the novel's embodiment of modern competence. Donald Farfrae arrives in [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] as a young Scotchman pas[[guilt-and-redemption|sin]]g through on his way to America and becomes, through a combination of skill, [[temper-and-impulse|temper]]ament, and circumstance, [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s rival and eventual successor. His presence in the novel is the catalyst that transforms [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s hidden past into public catastrophe.

## Character Overview

Farfrae's "character was just the reverse of [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s" — where [[michael-henchard|Henchard]] is [[temper-and-impulse|impulsive]], Farfrae is methodical; where [[michael-henchard|Henchard]] rules through personality, Farfrae succeeds through competence; where [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s emotions destroy him, Farfrae's restraint protects him. He is not, however, simply virtuous: his success comes partly at [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s expense, and his decency has limits.

> "Done," said Donald Farfrae. [010-090]

His modernity — scientific farming methods, the horse-drill, the seed-sower, efficient book-keeping — represents the [[tradition-and-change|new commercial order]] displacing [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s patriarchal world. He is not cruel, but he is relentless; his very competence is [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s undoing.

## Role in the Novel

Farfrae functions as both antagonist and yardstick. His steady ascent — from traveller to manager to independent merchant to [[mayor-of-casterbridge|mayor]] — provides the plot's counter-movement to [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s decline. He wins [[lucetta-templeman|Lucetta]]'s love, [[casterbridge|the town]]'s respect, and the [[power-and-authority|mayor]]'s office, but Hardy is careful to show that he does not seek to destroy [[michael-henchard|Henchard]] — he simply succeeds where [[michael-henchard|Henchard]] fails.

> "Donald Farfrae was overhauling bins and samples with great care, for his first business as a master corn-factor." [010-169]

## Character Analysis

Farfrae is a [[character-as-fate|study in temperament]] — not passionate, not brilliant, but steady, competent, and likeable. His songs ("I've seen the smiling Of fortune beguiling") charm [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]]; his bu[[guilt-and-redemption|sin]]ess methods win its trade. He is the future arriving before anyone is ready for it. Yet Hardy's irony is that Farfrae's success requires [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s destruction: the same qualities that make Farfrae a better bu[[guilt-and-redemption|sin]]essman — prudence, restraint, calculation — are what make him a lesser man in the grand tragic sense. [[michael-henchard|Henchard]] loses everything and is magnificent; Farfrae wins everything and is merely competent.
