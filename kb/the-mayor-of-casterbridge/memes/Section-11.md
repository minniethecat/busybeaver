# Section 11

(010-001) Thus [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] was in most respects but the pole, focus, or

(010-002) nerve-knot of the surrounding country life; differing from the many

(010-003) manufacturing towns which are as foreign bodies set down, like boulders

(010-004) on a plain, in a green world with which they have nothing in common.

(010-005) Casterbridge lived by [[rural-economics|agriculture]] at one remove further from the

(010-006) fountainhead than the adjoining villages—no more. The townsfolk

(010-007) understood every fluctuation in the rustic’s condition, for it affected

(010-008) their receipts as much as the labourer’s; they entered into the

(010-009) troubles and joys which moved the aristocratic families ten miles

(010-010) round—for the same reason. And even at the dinner-parties of the

(010-011) professional families the subjects of discussion were corn,

(010-012) cattle-disease, sowing and reaping, fencing and planting; while

(010-013) politics were viewed by them less from their own standpoint of

(010-014) burgesses with rights and privileges than from the standpoint of their

(010-015) country neighbours.

(010-016) All the venerable contrivances and confusions which delighted the eye

(010-017) by their quaintness, and in a measure reasonableness, in this rare old

(010-018) [[the-market-place|market]]-town, were metropolitan novelties to the unpractised eyes of

(010-019) [[elizabeth-jane-newson|Elizabeth-Jane]], fresh from netting fish-seines in a [[budmouth|seaside]] cottage.

(010-020) Very little inquiry was necessary to guide her footsteps. [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]’s

(010-023) passage to the end of the garden—nearly a quarter of a mile off.

(010-024) Mr. Henchard was not in the house, but in the store-yard. She was

(010-025) conducted into the mossy garden, and through a door in the wall, which

(010-026) was studded with rusty nails speaking of generations of fruit-trees

(010-027) that had been trained there. The door opened upon the yard, and here

(010-028) she was left to find him as she could. It was a place flanked by

(010-031) of the yard were wooden granaries on stone staddles, to which access

(010-032) was given by Flemish ladders, and a store-house several floors high.

(010-033) Wherever the doors of these places were open, a closely packed throng

(010-034) of bursting wheat-sacks could be seen standing inside, with the air of

(010-035) awaiting a famine that would not come.

(010-036) She wandered about this place, uncomfortably conscious of the impending

(010-037) interview, till she was quite weary of searching; she ventured to

(010-038) inquire of a boy in what quarter Mr. Henchard could be found. He

(010-039) directed her to an office which she had not seen before, and knocking

(010-040) at the door she was answered by a cry of “Come in.”

(010-041) Elizabeth turned the handle; and there stood before her, bending over

(010-042) some sample-bags on a table, not the corn-merchant, but the young

(010-043) Scotchman Mr. [[donald-farfrae|Farfrae]]—in the act of pouring some grains of wheat from

(010-044) one hand to the other. His hat hung on a peg behind him, and the roses

(010-045) of his carpet-bag glowed from the corner of the room.

(010-046) Having toned her feelings and arranged words on her lips for Mr.

(010-047) Henchard, and for him alone, she was for the moment confounded.

(010-048) “Yes, what it is?” said the Scotchman, like a man who permanently ruled

(010-049) there.

(010-050) She said she wanted to see Mr. Henchard.

(010-055) man’s presence we may briefly explain how he came there.

(010-063) the bottom of the scarp. It was by this path the Scotchman had to

(010-064) descend.

(010-065) “Well, here’s success to ’ee,” said Henchard, holding out his right

(010-066) hand and leaning with his left upon the wicket which protected the

(010-068) nipped and wishes defeated. “I shall often think of this time, and of

(010-069) how you came at the very moment to throw a light upon my difficulty.”

(010-080) The young man’s hand remained steady in Henchard’s for a moment or two.

(010-085) any one go against it? No; I’ll not go to America; I’ll stay and be

(010-088) grasp.

(010-089) “Done,” said Henchard.

(010-090) “Done,” said Donald Farfrae.
