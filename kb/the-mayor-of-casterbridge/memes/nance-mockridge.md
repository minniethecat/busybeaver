# Nance Mockridge

One of the [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] townspeople through whom Hardy provides a ground-level view of the novel's events. Nance Mockridge belongs to the community of ordinary observers — sharp-tongued, morally conventional, and collectively [[gender-and-power|power]]ful — whose judgments shape the [[appearance-and-reality|reputation]] economy that governs [[casterbridge|the town]].

## Role in the Novel

Mockridge and her fellow townspeople function as a kind of Greek chorus — commenting on, judging, and occasionally intervening in the lives of their social superiors. Their perspective provides the novel with its sense of [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] as a community of watchers, where private acts are always already public knowledge.
