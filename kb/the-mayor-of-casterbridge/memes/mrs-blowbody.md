# Mrs. Blowbody

A lady of [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] described as "a lady of [[casterbridge|the town]]" who appears among the crowd during the [[the-royal-visit|royal visit]] — the public occasion that becomes the stage for [[michael-henchard|Michael Henchard]]'s most humiliating exposure.

## Character Overview

Mrs. Blowbody belongs to the circle of [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] townspeople who form the novel's collective witness. She appears at the moment of public spectacle, her presence part of the crowd before whom the drama of [[appearance-and-reality|reputation]] and disgrace is enacted.

> "Her husband's old patron!" said Mrs. Blowbody, a lady of the town. [044-078]

Her exclamation at the [[the-royal-visit|royal visit]] captures [[casterbridge|the town]]'s awareness of the shifting [[gender-and-power|power]] dynamics between [[michael-henchard|Henchard]] and [[donald-farfrae|Donald Farfrae]] — the old patron displaced by the new.

## Role in the Skimmington Aftermath

Blowbody reappears in the investigation following the skimmington-ride. Referred to simply as "Blowbody" [046-170], the figure appears to be a constable or official involved in tracking down the perpetrators:

> "Well, what's the row?" said Blowbody. "Got their names — hey?" [046-172]

Directed to patrol "round by the Old Walk" [046-174], Blowbody participates in the official attempt to restore order — an attempt that, like much of the novel's institutional [[power-and-authority|authority]], proves ineffective against the deeper social forces at work.

## Significance

Mrs. Blowbody is one of Hardy's functional minor characters — a figure whose individual identity is less important than her representative function. Like [[mother-cuxsom|Mother Cuxsom]] and [[nance-mockridge|Nance Mockridge]], she embodies [[casterbridge|the town]]'s female [[reputation-and-social-standing|public opinion]]: observant, judgmental, and inescapable.
