# Mr. Joy

A [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] auctioneer whose professional role places him at the intersection of commerce and community. Mr. Joy is one of [[casterbridge|the town]]'s minor functionaries — the people who keep the economic machinery running and whose professional routines constitute the background against which the central characters' dramas unfold.

## Role in the Novel

Joy appears in connection with [[henchards-bankruptcy|Henchard's bankruptcy]] and the auction of his possessions — a scene in which the professional functionary must preside over the dissolution of a former [[power-and-authority|mayor]]'s estate. The encounter dramatizes the impersonality of economic processes: Joy does his job, and his job is to dismantle [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s life, piece by piece.
