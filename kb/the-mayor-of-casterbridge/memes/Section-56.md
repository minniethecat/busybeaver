# Section 56

(055-001) Then, before she could collect her thoughts, [[michael-henchard|Henchard]] went out from her

(055-002) rooms, and departed from the house by the back way as he had come; and

(055-003) she saw him no more.

(055-004) XLV.

(055-005) It was about a month after the day which closed as in the last chapter.

(055-006) [[elizabeth-jane-newson|Elizabeth-Jane]] had grown accustomed to the novelty of her situation,

(055-009) he had been in the habit of doing for some time.

(055-056) live on as one of his own worst accusers. She went out, looked at the

(055-057) cage, buried the starved little singer, and from that hour her heart

(055-058) softened towards the self-alienated man.

(055-066) former friend had done, and he was therefore not the least indisposed
