# The Three Mariners

The modest inn where [[donald-farfrae|Donald Farfrae]] takes his first lodging in [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] — a humbler alternative to the [[the-kings-arms|King's Arms]] that signals his practical, unpretentious character. The inn functions as a node in the novel's social geography, a place where commercial and personal relationships intersect (009-069).

## Description

The Three Mariners is presented as a respectable but unpretentious establishment, suitable for a travelling Scotchman of modest means. Its contrast with the [[the-kings-arms|King's Arms]] — where [[michael-henchard|Henchard]] conducts his bu[[guilt-and-redemption|sin]]ess — maps onto the social and [[temper-and-impulse|temper]]amental differences between the two men.

## Narrative Function

The inn is where [[donald-farfrae|Farfrae]] is first established as an independent presence in [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]], separate from [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]. It represents the alternative path — modest, steady, and ultimately more sustainable — that [[donald-farfrae|Farfrae]] embodies throughout the novel.
