# Mellstock

A village in the rural hinterland of [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] — one of the [[tradition-and-change|tradition]]al agricultural communities that feed the [[the-market-place|market]] town's economy and population. Mellstock represents the older, slower world that [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]]'s commercial energy both depends upon and disrupts (009-157).

## Description

Mellstock appears in the novel as a point of origin — people "hailed from Mellstock" (009-157) — rather than a fully developed setting. It represents the rural background against which [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]]'s urbanity defines itself, and the reservoir of [[tradition-and-change|tradition]]al life that [[tradition-and-change|modernisation]] is transforming.

## Narrative Function

As part of Hardy's [[nature-and-landscape|Wessex]] geography, Mellstock connects The Mayor of [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] to the broader fictional [[nature-and-landscape|landscape]] of Hardy's novels. It functions as a reminder that [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]], for all its urban pretensions, is surrounded by and dependent upon the agricultural villages whose produce it trades.
