# Section 2

(001-001) The conversation took a high turn, as it often does on such occasions.

(001-002) The ruin of good men by bad wives, and, more particularly, the

(001-003) frustration of many a promising youth’s high aims and hopes and the

(001-005) theme.

(001-007) contemplative bitterness that was well-nigh resentful. “I married at

(001-008) eighteen, like the fool that I was; and this is the consequence o’t.”

(001-015) wished to ease her arms. The man continued—

(001-016) “I haven’t more than fifteen shillings in the world, and yet I am a

(001-017) good experienced hand in my line. I’d challenge England to beat me in

(001-049) his former conviction, and said harshly—

(001-127) “I say so,” replied [[richard-newson|the sailor]].

(001-128) “Saying is one thing, and paying is another. Where’s the money?”
