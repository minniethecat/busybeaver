## Role in the Novel

Dr. Chalkfield is a minor figure in the civic life of Casterbridge, mentioned only in connection with his official role as a councilman and mayor. His two appearances in the narrative are defined by absence and death — in each case, his removal from the scene creates an opening for a more central character.

### Mayor and Magistrate

Chalkfield served as Mayor of Casterbridge for the year. In his capacity as mayor, he would ordinarily preside over the town magistrates' court. His absence from a court session in Chapter 34 creates the circumstance in which Michael Henchard, as a Justice of the Peace, takes the "big chair" and adjudicates a case — notably the trial of the furmity woman, whose testimony later proves devastating to Henchard [034-011].

### Death and Succession

In Chapter 41, Alderman Vatt brings news of Chalkfield's death to Donald Farfrae and Lucetta:

> "You've heard, I suppose of poor Doctor Chalkfield's death? Yes — died this afternoon at five," said Mr. Vatt. Chalkfield was the Councilman who had succeeded to the Mayoralty in the preceding November. [041-005]

Vatt notes that Chalkfield had been ailing for some days and that his family was "well provided for." The death carries immediate political significance: it creates a vacancy on the town council, and Vatt arrives at Farfrae's home with the private intention of nominating Farfrae to fill the seat. This nomination becomes a pivotal step in Farfrae's rise within Casterbridge, setting the stage for his eventual election as mayor and the corresponding displacement of Henchard from civic power [041-006].

## Narrative Function

Chalkfield serves a structural role in the novel's plot machinery. His absence from court enables the furmity woman's trial scene, which plants the seed of Henchard's eventual public disgrace. His death, in turn, opens the council seat that accelerates Farfrae's political ascent. In both cases, Chalkfield's removal from the scene — whether temporary or permanent — facilitates the forward movement of the central characters' arcs.
