# Solomon Longways

A [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] townsperson who embodies the community's collective memory and judgment. Longways is one of the many ordinary residents through whom Hardy creates the texture of provincial life — the network of watchers and talkers whose opinions constitute public [[appearance-and-reality|reputation]].

## Role in the Novel

Longways appears as a source of local information and commentary, part of the choral infrastructure through which [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]]'s social world is rendered. His observations help establish [[casterbridge|the town]]'s perspective on the [[the-rivalry|Henchard-Farfrae rivalry]] and the other events that convulse the community. Like the other minor townspeople, his function is to remind the reader that [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] is not a stage for a few central characters but a living community with its own memory and judgment.
