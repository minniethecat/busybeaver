# The Skimmity-Ride

The novel's most devastating event — a [[tradition-and-change|tradition]]al rough-music ritual in which effigies of [[michael-henchard|Henchard]] and [[lucetta-templeman|Lucetta]] are paraded through [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] on a donkey, expo[[guilt-and-redemption|sin]]g their past relationship to public view. The skimmity-ride is the fatal collision of [[mixen-lane|Mixen Lane]]'s underworld with [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]]'s respectable surface, and its victim is [[lucetta-templeman|Lucetta]] (035-012).

## Narrative Context

The skimmity-ride represents the return of the repressed — the forces that respectable society excludes (the poor of [[mixen-lane|Mixen Lane]], the suppressed knowledge of sexual transgression) erupting into visibility. [[lucetta-templeman|Lucetta]], whose entire existence depends on the maintenance of appearances, cannot survive this exposure. "What a scandal!" (040-026) — [[casterbridge|the town]]'s reaction is both prurient and lethal.

## Event Details

- **Setting**: [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] streets, past [[high-place-hall|High-Place Hall]] (035-012)
- **Perpetrators**: The residents of [[mixen-lane|Mixen Lane]], acting on their own sense of rough justice
- **Method**: A [[tradition-and-change|tradition]]al skimmity-ride — effigies on a donkey, accompanied by cacophonous noise (the "rough music")
- **Target**: [[lucetta-templeman|Lucetta]] and [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s past affair

## Consequences

[[lucetta-templeman|Lucetta]], already pregnant and fragile, collapses upon witnes[[guilt-and-redemption|sin]]g the spectacle and dies shortly after. Her death is the novel's most direct indictment of the cruelty of [[reputation-and-social-standing|public opinion]] and the lethal [[gender-and-power|power]] of shame in a society built on appearances.
