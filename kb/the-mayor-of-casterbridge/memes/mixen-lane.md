# Mixen Lane

The dark underside of [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] — a quarter where "rural servants too rebellious to" conform (042-066) live alongside criminals and outcasts. Mixen Lane is [[casterbridge|the town]]'s shadow, and from its squalor emerges the [[the-skimmity-ride|skimmity-ride]] that destroys [[lucetta-templeman|Lucetta Templeman]].

## Description

Hardy portrays Mixen Lane as a community outside the community — a place where [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]]'s [[class-and-social-order|social order]] breaks down and its repressed elements gather. It is the source of [[casterbridge|the town]]'s moral pollution and also, in a darker sense, of its moral judgment: the [[the-skimmity-ride|skimmity-ride]] that punishes [[lucetta-templeman|Lucetta]]'s sexual transgression originates here.

## Narrative Function

Mixen Lane functions as [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]]'s unconscious — the place where desires and resentments that cannot be expressed in respectable society find their outlet. The [[the-skimmity-ride|skimmity-ride]] is Mixen Lane's judgment on the respectable world above it, and its cruelty is inseparable from its marginality. Hardy shows that a society that creates such places cannot escape their consequences.
