# Section 55

(054-001) To intrude as little of his personality as possible upon a gay event

(054-002) with which that personality could show nothing in keeping, he decided

(054-003) not to make his [[appearance-and-reality|appearance]] till evening—when stiffness would have worn

(054-004) off, and a gentle wish to let bygones be bygones would exercise its

(054-005) sway in all hearts.

(054-006) He started on foot, two mornings before St. Martin’s-tide, allowing

(054-007) himself about sixteen miles to perform for each of the three days’

(054-009) Melchester and Shottsford, of any importance along his course, and at

(054-010) the latter he stopped on the second night, not only to rest, but to

(054-018) would not now offend her, he proceeded to the more interesting

(054-054) It was the hill near which he had waited to meet Farfrae, almost two

(054-057) Farfrae had another wife—and, as Henchard knew, a better one. He only
