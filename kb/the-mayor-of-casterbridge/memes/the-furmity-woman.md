# The Furmity-Woman

An apparently minor figure whose two appearances in the novel — at its beginning and at its turning point — make her the agent of [[fate-and-determinism|fate]]. The furmity-woman is both witness to [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s crime and the instrument of its exposure, a figure from the novel's underworld who brings the suppressed past crashing into the respectable present.

## Role in the Novel

At [[the-wife-auction|Weydon-Priors fair]], she serves the furmity that intoxicates [[michael-henchard|Henchard]] and enables [[susan-henchard|the wife]]-auction. Two decades later, arrested for a petty crime in [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]], she recognizes [[michael-henchard|Henchard]] in the courtroom and exposes his past to the magistrates. Her testimony destroys the [[appearance-and-reality|reputation]] [[michael-henchard|Henchard]] spent twenty years building. She is Hardy's embodiment of the return of the repressed — the past that cannot be escaped because it is embedded in the social fabric.
