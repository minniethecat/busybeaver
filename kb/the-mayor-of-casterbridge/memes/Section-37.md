# Section 37

(036-001) It was still early in the evening when she hastily put on her things

(036-002) and went out. In a few minutes, knowing the ground, she had found a

(036-003) suitable lodging, and arranged to enter it that night. Returning and

(036-005) in a plain one, packing up the other to keep as her best; for she would

(036-006) have to be very economical now. She wrote a note to leave for [[lucetta-templeman|Lucetta]],

(036-007) who was closely shut up in the drawing-room with [[donald-farfrae|Farfrae]]; and then

(036-008) [[elizabeth-jane-newson|Elizabeth-Jane]] called a man with a wheel-barrow; and seeing her boxes

(036-009) put into it she trotted off down the street to her rooms. They were in

(036-010) the street in which [[michael-henchard|Henchard]] lived, and almost opposite his door.

(036-011) Here she sat down and considered the means of subsistence. The little

(036-012) annual sum settled on her by her stepfather would keep body and soul

(036-013) together. A wonderful skill in netting of all sorts—acquired in

(036-014) childhood by making seines in [[richard-newson|Newson]]’s home—might serve her in good

(036-015) stead; and her studies, which were pursued unremittingly, might serve

(036-016) her in still better.

(036-018) [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]]; had been discussed noisily on kerbstones, confidentially

(036-019) behind counters, and jovially at the [[the-three-mariners|Three Mariners]]. Whether Farfrae

(036-020) would sell his business and set up for a gentleman on his wife’s money,

(036-021) or whether he would show independence enough to stick to his trade in

(036-025) in four-and-twenty hours there was not a person in Casterbridge who

(036-039) to descend rapidly on the other side. It was strange how soon he sank

(036-064) felt quite tearful, and when she heard that he was present in the hotel

(036-065) she wished to go in and see him, but was advised not to intrude that

(036-066) day.

(036-076) “Gentlemen,” he said, “over and above the assets that we’ve been

(036-173) Two bridges stood near the lower part of Casterbridge town. The first,

(036-174) of weather-stained brick, was immediately at the end of High Street,

(036-176) low-lying Durnover lanes; so that the precincts of [[the-bridge|the bridge]] formed

(036-177) the merging point of respectability and indigence. The second bridge,

(036-187) each joint; since it had been no uncommon thing for desperate men to
