# Mother Cuxsom

An elderly [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] woman whose role — tending to the dying [[susan-henchard|Susan Henchard]] — places her at the intersection of domestic care and community knowledge. Mother Cuxsom represents the informal female networks that operate beneath the novel's more visible male-dominated public sphere.

## Role in the Novel

Cuxsom's presence at Susan's deathbed establishes her as part of the community's care infrastructure — the [[gender-and-power|women]] who attend births, nurse the sick, and lay out the dead. Her knowledge of the [[michael-henchard|Henchard]] household's secrets makes her one of the many keepers of the community's suppressed knowledge. She is a minor figure in the plot but a significant element in the novel's social texture.
