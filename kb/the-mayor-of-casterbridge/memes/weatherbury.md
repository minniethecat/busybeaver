# Weatherbury

One of the [[nature-and-landscape|Wessex]] villages that feed [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]]'s population and economy. Weatherbury appears alongside [[mellstock|Mellstock]] as a place of origin — a reminder that [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]]'s urban character is built on the labour and produce of the surrounding countryside (009-157).

## Description

Weatherbury is mentioned rather than described — a name in a list of villages that together constitute the rural world from which [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] draws its people and its trade. Its presence in the novel, however brief, is typical of Hardy's technique of building [[nature-and-landscape|Wessex]] through accumulation of place-names.

## Narrative Function

Weatherbury functions as a node in Hardy's fictional geography, connecting The Mayor of [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] to the broader [[nature-and-landscape|Wessex]] universe. Its mention alongside [[mellstock|Mellstock]] creates a sense of [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] as a hub in a network of rural communities — a town defined as much by its relations to its hinterland as by its internal life.
