# Section 24

(023-001) XXI.

(023-002) As a maxim glibly repeated from childhood remains practically unmarked

(023-003) till some mature experience enforces it, so did this [[high-place-hall|High-Place Hall]]

(023-004) now for the first time really show itself to [[elizabeth-jane-newson|Elizabeth-Jane]], though her

(023-005) ears had heard its name on a hundred occasions.

(023-006) Her mind dwelt upon nothing else but the stranger, and the house, and

(023-007) her own chance of living there, all the rest of the day. In the

(023-008) afternoon she had occasion to pay a few bills in [[casterbridge|the town]] and do a

(023-009) little shopping when she learnt that what was a new discovery to

(023-010) herself had become a common topic about the streets. High-Place Hall

(023-011) was undergoing repair; a lady was coming there to live shortly; all the

(023-012) shop-people knew it, and had already discounted the chance of her being

(023-013) a customer.

(023-014) Elizabeth-Jane could, however, add a capping touch to information so

(023-015) new to her in the bulk. The lady, she said, had arrived that day.

(023-016) When the lamps were lighted, and it was yet not so dark as to render

(023-017) chimneys, attics, and roofs invisible, Elizabeth, almost with a lover’s

(023-018) feeling, thought she would like to look at the outside of High-Place

(023-019) Hall. She went up the street in that direction.

(023-020) The Hall, with its grey _façade_ and parapet, was the only residence of

(023-021) its sort so near the centre of the town. It had, in the first place,

(023-022) the characteristics of a country mansion—birds’ nests in its chimneys,

(023-023) damp nooks where fungi grew and irregularities of surface direct from

(023-024) Nature’s trowel. At night the forms of passengers were patterned by the

(023-025) lamps in black shadows upon the pale walls.

(023-026) This evening motes of straw lay around, and other signs of the premises

(023-027) having been in that lawless condition which accompanies the entry of a

(023-028) new tenant. The house was entirely of stone, and formed an example of

(023-029) dignity without great size. It was not altogether aristocratic, still

(023-030) less consequential, yet the old-fashioned stranger instinctively said

(023-031) “Blood built it, and Wealth enjoys it” however vague his opinions of

(023-032) those accessories might be.

(023-033) Yet as regards the enjoying it the stranger would have been wrong, for

(023-034) until this very evening, when the new lady had arrived, the house had

(023-035) been empty for a year or two while before that interval its occupancy

(023-036) had been irregular. The reason of its unpopularity was soon made

(023-037) manifest. Some of its rooms overlooked the [[the-market-place|market-place]]; and such a

(023-038) prospect from such a house was not considered desirable or seemly by

(023-039) its would-be occupiers.

(023-040) Elizabeth’s eyes sought the upper rooms, and saw lights there. The lady

(023-041) had obviously arrived. The impression that this woman of comparatively

(023-042) practised manner had made upon the studious girl’s mind was so deep

(023-043) that she enjoyed standing under an opposite archway merely to think

(023-044) that the charming lady was inside the confronting walls, and to wonder

(023-045) what she was doing. Her admiration for the architecture of that front

(023-046) was entirely on account of the inmate it screened. Though for that

(023-047) matter the architecture deserved admiration, or at least study, on its

(023-048) own account. It was Palladian, and like most architecture erected since

(023-049) the Gothic age was a compilation rather than a design. But its

(023-050) reasonableness made it impressive. It was not rich, but rich enough. A

(023-051) timely consciousness of the ultimate vanity of human architecture, no

(023-052) less than of other human things, had prevented artistic superfluity.

(023-053) Men had still quite recently been going in and out with parcels and

(023-054) packing-cases, rendering the door and hall within like a public

(023-055) thoroughfare. Elizabeth trotted through the open door in the dusk, but

(023-056) becoming alarmed at her own temerity she went quickly out again by

(023-057) another which stood open in the lofty wall of the back court. To her

(023-058) surprise she found herself in one of the little-used alleys of the

(023-059) town. Looking round at the door which had given her egress, by the

(023-060) light of the solitary lamp fixed in the alley, she saw that it was

(023-061) arched and old—older even than the house itself. The door was studded,

(023-062) and the keystone of the arch was a mask. Originally the mask had

(023-063) exhibited a comic leer, as could still be discerned; but generations of

(023-064) Casterbridge boys had thrown stones at the mask, aiming at its open

(023-065) mouth; and the blows thereon had chipped off the lips and jaws as if

(023-066) they had been eaten away by disease. The [[appearance-and-reality|appearance]] was so ghastly by

(023-067) the weakly lamp-glimmer that she could not bear to look at it—the first

(023-068) unpleasant feature of her visit.

(023-069) The position of the queer old door and the odd presence of the leering

(023-070) mask suggested one thing above all others as appertaining to the

(023-071) mansion’s past history—intrigue. By the alley it had been possible to

(023-072) come unseen from all sorts of quarters in the town—the old play-house,

(023-073) the old bull-stake, the old cock-pit, the pool wherein nameless infants

(023-074) had been used to disappear. High-Place Hall could boast of its

(023-075) conveniences undoubtedly.

(023-076) She turned to come away in the nearest direction homeward, which was

(023-077) down the alley, but hearing footsteps approaching in that quarter, and

(023-078) having no great wish to be found in such a place at such a time she

(023-079) quickly retreated. There being no other way out she stood behind a

(023-080) brick pier till the intruder should have gone his ways.

(023-081) Had she watched she would have been surprised. She would have seen that

(023-082) the pedestrian on coming up made straight for the arched doorway: that

(023-083) as he paused with his hand upon the latch the lamplight fell upon the

(023-084) face of [[michael-henchard|Henchard]].

(023-085) But Elizabeth-Jane clung so closely to her nook that she discerned

(023-086) nothing of this. Henchard passed in, as ignorant of her presence as she

(023-087) was ignorant of his identity, and disappeared in the darkness.

(023-088) Elizabeth came out a second time into the alley, and made the best of

(023-089) her way home.

(023-090) Henchard’s chiding, by begetting in her a nervous fear of doing

(023-091) anything definable as unladylike, had operated thus curiously in

(023-092) keeping them unknown to each other at a critical moment. Much might

(023-093) have resulted from recognition—at the least a query on either side in

(023-094) one and the selfsame form: What could he or she possibly be doing

(023-095) there?

(023-096) Henchard, whatever his business at the lady’s house, reached his own

(023-097) home only a few minutes later than Elizabeth-Jane. Her plan was to

(023-099) day had urged her to the course. But its execution depended upon his

(023-102) something worse. Absolute indifference had taken the place of

(023-104) departure, even more than [[temper-and-impulse|hot temper]] could have done.

(023-105) “Father, have you any objection to my going away?” she asked.

(023-106) “Going away! No—none whatever. Where are you going?”

(023-115) “You don’t object?”

(023-126) “Then I’ll see about it this very day.” He seemed relieved to get her

(023-133) the boot-room where her pattens had hung ever since her apotheosis;

(023-135) she had done in old times. Thus mounted, and with cloak and umbrella,

(023-137) not there, to call at the house.

(023-142) the projection of the thatch she saw a figure. The young lady had come.

(023-145) strongest minds. Here, in a churchyard old as civilization, in the

(023-147) seen elsewhere: there might be some devilry about her presence.

(023-155) “Your father is willing?”

(023-156) “Yes.”

(023-157) “Then come along.”
