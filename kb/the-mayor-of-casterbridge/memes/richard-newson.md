# Richard Newson

The sailor whose bid of five guineas at [[the-wife-auction|Weydon-Priors fair]] [[tradition-and-change|change]]s every life in the novel. Richard Newson is an essentially decent man — kind to Susan, proud of [[elizabeth-jane-newson|Elizabeth-Jane]], and entirely unaware of the havoc his return to [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] [[ambition-and-will|will]] cause (052-117).

## Role in the Novel

Newson appears briefly at the novel's beginning and end, but his influence is decisive. His purchase of Susan at [[weydon-priors|the fair]] removes her from [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s life and gives [[elizabeth-jane-newson|Elizabeth-Jane]] a stable childhood. His return, two decades later, to claim his daughter precipitates [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s final crisis. Newson's "[[pride]] in what she had grown up to be" (052-117) contrasts with [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s inability to sustain loving relationships.
