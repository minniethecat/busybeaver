# Christopher Coney

An elderly [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] resident whose attachment to the [[tradition-and-change|old ways]] embodies the [[tradition-and-change|tradition]]al world that [[donald-farfrae|Farfrae]]'s modern methods are displacing. Coney is a voice from the past — sceptical of [[tradition-and-change|change]], loyal to established customs, and representative of the generation that is being left behind.

## Role in the Novel

Coney appears as a spokesperson for the [[tradition-and-change|tradition]]al values that [[michael-henchard|Henchard]] also represents, but without [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s [[ambition-and-will|ambition]] or destructive energy. His quiet conservatism provides a baseline against which both [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s turbulent [[ambition-and-will|will]] and [[donald-farfrae|Farfrae]]'s modern efficiency can be measured. He is part of the novel's rich texture of minor characters who collectively constitute [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]]'s social world.
