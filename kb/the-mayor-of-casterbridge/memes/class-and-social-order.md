# Class and Social Order

The Mayor of [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] maps the class structure of a provincial [[the-market-place|market]] town with ethnographic precision. [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s trajectory — from itinerant hay-trusser to [[power-and-authority|mayor]] to bankrupt labourer — traces the full arc of Victorian social mobility in both directions. Class is simultaneously rigid and permeable: it can be climbed, but the climb leaves scars, and the fall is absolute.

## Overview

[[casterbridge|Casterbridge]]'s social order is a hierarchy of occupation and property: the [[power-and-authority|mayor]] and town council at the top, merchants and tradesmen in the middle, agricultural labourers at the bottom. [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s rise from labourer to [[power-and-authority|mayor]] is exceptional — a feat of [[ambition-and-will|will]] and luck — but his fall is depicted as a return to his natural level, suggesting the era's ambivalence about social mobility.

## Manifestation in the Novel

- **[[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s rise**: From "hay-trusser" to "corn-factor and [[power-and-authority|mayor]]" — a trajectory of extraordinary upward mobility [[ambition-and-will|drive]]n by [[ambition-and-will|ambition]] and abstinence (009-093).
- **The [[power-and-authority|mayor]]'s position**: [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s "natural [[pride]] as Mayor of" [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] (009-032) shows how class identity is internalized — his [[pride]] is specifically the [[pride]] of achieved status.
- **[[donald-farfrae|Farfrae]]'s ascent**: [[donald-farfrae|Farfrae]]'s rise from Scottish stranger to [[power-and-authority|mayor]] represents a different model of mobility — gradual, merited, and socially accepted — in contrast to [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s volatile trajectory.
- **The "moneyed class"**: Hardy notes "all the moneyed class" (028-045) with ironic distance, showing how economic [[gender-and-power|power]] translates into social position.
- **[[elizabeth-jane-newson|Elizabeth-Jane]]'s liminality**: As a working woman of uncertain parentage, [[elizabeth-jane-newson|Elizabeth-Jane]] occupies the precarious space between classes — "a common workwoman of such a character as hers!" (021-152).

## Literary Analysis

### Class as Moral Framework

Hardy uses class not merely as background but as a moral testing ground. [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s treatment of others varies with their social position; his generosity to [[donald-farfrae|Farfrae]] early on contrasts with his contempt for those below him. Class reveals character — and character, in turn, determines class trajectory.

### The Naturalist View of Social Hierarchy

True to Naturalist principles, Hardy presents the class system as an [[nature-and-landscape|environment]] that shapes its inhabitants as surely as [[nature-and-landscape|Wessex]] [[nature-and-landscape|weather]] shapes the [[nature-and-landscape|landscape]]. Characters do not transcend their class so much as they are expressed through it.
