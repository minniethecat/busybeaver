# Section 49

(048-001) “Yes,” said [[michael-henchard|Henchard]] shortly, though little dreaming of [[joshua-jopp|Jopp]]’s

(048-002) complicity in the night’s harlequinade, and raising his eyes just

(048-003) sufficiently to observe that Jopp’s face was lined with anxiety.

(048-004) “Somebody has called for you,” continued Jopp, when Henchard was

(048-005) shutting himself into his own apartment. “A kind of traveller, or

(048-006) sea-captain of some sort.”

(048-007) “Oh?—who could he be?”

(048-008) “He seemed a well-be-doing man—had grey hair and a broadish face; but

(048-009) he gave no name, and no message.”

(048-010) “Nor do I gi’e him any attention.” And, saying this, Henchard closed

(048-011) his door.

(048-013) two hours of Henchard’s estimate. Among the other urgent reasons for

(048-015) a second physician; and when at length Farfrae did come back he was in

(048-016) a state bordering on distraction at his misconception of Henchard’s

(048-065) instant or two. Recognizing him, she said, “Because they may knock as

(048-068) Henchard went home. The morning having now fully broke he lit his fire,

(048-069) and sat abstractedly beside it. He had not sat there long when a gentle

(048-070) footstep approached the house and entered the passage, a finger tapping

(048-071) lightly at the door. Henchard’s face brightened, for he knew the

(048-072) motions to be Elizabeth’s. She came into his room, looking wan and sad.

(048-073) “Have you heard?” she asked. “Mrs. Farfrae! She is—dead! Yes,

(048-074) indeed—about an hour ago!”

(048-075) “I know it,” said Henchard. “I have but lately come in from there. It

(048-076) is so very good of ’ee, Elizabeth, to come and tell me. You must be so

(048-077) tired out, too, with sitting up. Now do you bide here with me this

(048-078) morning. You can go and rest in the other room; and I will call ’ee

(048-079) when breakfast is ready.”

(048-080) To please him, and herself—for his recent kindliness was winning a

(048-081) surprised gratitude from the lonely girl—she did as he bade her, and

(048-082) lay down on a sort of couch which Henchard had rigged up out of a

(048-083) settle in the adjoining room. She could hear him moving about in his

(048-084) preparations; but her mind ran most strongly on Lucetta, whose death in

(048-085) such fulness of life and amid such cheerful hopes of maternity was

(048-086) appallingly unexpected. Presently she fell asleep.

(048-087) Meanwhile her stepfather in the outer room had set the breakfast in

(048-088) readiness; but finding that she dozed he would not call her; he waited

(048-089) on, looking into the fire and keeping the kettle boiling with

(048-090) house-wifely care, as if it were an [[reputation-and-social-standing|honour]] to have her in his house. In

(048-091) truth, a great [[tradition-and-change|change]] had come over him with regard to her, and he was

(048-092) developing the dream of a future lit by her filial presence, as though

(048-093) that way alone could happiness lie.

(048-094) He was disturbed by another knock at the door, and rose to open it,

(048-095) rather deprecating a call from anybody just then. A stoutly built man

(048-096) stood on the doorstep, with an alien, unfamiliar air about his figure

(048-098) cosmopolitan experience. It was the man who had asked the way at

(048-099) Peter’s Finger. Henchard nodded, and looked inquiry.

(048-100) “Good morning, good morning,” said the stranger with profuse

(048-101) heartiness. “Is it Mr. Henchard I am talking to?”

(048-102) “My name is Henchard.”

(048-103) “Then I’ve caught ’ee at home—that’s right. Morning’s the time for

(048-104) business, says I. Can I have a few words with you?”

(048-105) “By all means,” Henchard answered, showing the way in.

(048-106) “You may remember me?” said his visitor, seating himself.

(048-107) Henchard observed him indifferently, and shook his head.

(048-108) “Well—perhaps you may not. My name is [[richard-newson|Newson]].”

(048-109) Henchard’s face and eyes seemed to die. The other did not notice it. “I

(048-111) “I make no doubt of that. Well, the fact is, I’ve been looking for ’ee

(048-113) Casterbridge on my way to Falmouth, and when I got there, they told me

(048-163) “Dead!” he said, in a low voice. “Then what’s the use of my money to

(048-184) herself to be, had been stimulated by the unexpected coming of Newson

(048-185) to a greedy exclusiveness in relation to her; so that the sudden

(048-186) prospect of her loss had caused him to speak mad lies like a child, in

(048-187) pure mockery of consequences. He had expected questions to close in

(048-190) departure could be but momentary; he would learn all by inquiries in

(048-191) the town; and return to curse him, and carry his last treasure away!
