# The Carrier's Rest

An inn that serves the rural carriers who bring agricultural produce into [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] — a liminal space where [[casterbridge|the town]]'s commercial life meets its rural origins. The Carrier's Rest represents the transport infrastructure that makes the [[the-market-place|market]] town possible (009-093).

## Description

The Carrier's Rest is a functional establishment rather than a social venue — a place where rural carriers stable their horses and conduct their bu[[guilt-and-redemption|sin]]ess. It represents the practical infrastructure of the agricultural economy: the wagons, the roads, the overnight stops that connect farm to [[the-market-place|market]].

## Narrative Function

As a transit space, the Carrier's Rest embodies the flow of goods and people between [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] and its hinterland. It is a reminder that [[casterbridge|the town]]'s prosperity depends on connections that extend far beyond its boundaries — connections that are material (roads, wagons, carriers) before they are social or political.
