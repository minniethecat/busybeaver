# Constable Stubberd

[[casterbridge|Casterbridge]]'s constable — a minor official whose encounters with [[michael-henchard|Henchard]] and other characters provide a perspective on the operations of law and [[power-and-authority|authority]] in the [[the-market-place|market]] town. Stubberd represents official [[gender-and-power|power]] at its most local and least glamorous.

## Role in the Novel

Stubberd appears in scenes involving [[casterbridge|the town]]'s legal apparatus — the magistrates' court, the enforcement of public order — providing a ground-level view of how [[power-and-authority|authority]] functions in [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]]. His presence reminds the reader that [[casterbridge|the town]] is not merely a stage for personal drama but a governed community with laws, officials, and institutional [[gender-and-power|power]]. His encounters with [[michael-henchard|Henchard]] illuminate the gap between [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s personal [[power-and-authority|authority]] (waning) and official [[power-and-authority|authority]] (which [[michael-henchard|Henchard]] can no longer command).
