# Abel Whittle

A labourer whose minor role provides one of the novel's sharpest contrasts between [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s and [[donald-farfrae|Farfrae]]'s modes of [[power-and-authority|authority]]. Whittle's chronic lateness provokes [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s tyrannical rage and [[donald-farfrae|Farfrae]]'s pragmatic accommodation — a small-scale demonstration of the larger pattern that determines both men's [[fate-and-determinism|fate]]s.

## Role in the Novel

Whittle appears briefly but memorably: [[michael-henchard|Henchard]], enraged by his lateness, forces him to work without his trousers — an act of [[pride|humiliation]] that alienates the workforce and damages [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s [[appearance-and-reality|reputation]]. [[donald-farfrae|Farfrae]]'s gentler treatment of the same man demonstrates the alternative approach to [[power-and-authority|authority]] that [[ambition-and-will|will]] ultimately prevail.
