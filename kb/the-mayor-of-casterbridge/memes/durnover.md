# Durnover

The quarter of [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]] most directly connected to its agricultural hinterland — home to the corn ex[[tradition-and-change|change]] and the rhythms of rural commerce that underpin [[casterbridge|the town]]'s prosperity. Durnover represents the economic foundation upon which [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s fortune was built and lost (009-093).

## Description

Durnover is where [[casterbridge|Casterbridge]]'s identity as an agricultural [[the-market-place|market]] town is most visible. The corn ex[[tradition-and-change|change]], the granaries, and the commercial offices that line its streets are the physical infrastructure of [[michael-henchard|Henchard]]'s world. When he loses his bu[[guilt-and-redemption|sin]]ess, he loses Durnover.

## Narrative Function

Durnover embodies the economic dimension of the novel's social world. It is the place of work, transaction, and [[the-rivalry|competition]] — the arena where [[michael-henchard|Henchard]] and [[donald-farfrae|Farfrae]]'s rivalry plays out in its most material form. Its contrast with the residential and social spaces of [[casterbridge|the town]] maps the division between [[appearance-and-reality|public and private]] life.
