# Life

**Life** in [[A Book of Strife in the Form of The Diary of an Old Soul]] is understood primarily in spiritual terms — not biological existence but the divine life that flows from [[god|God]] through [[christ]] into the soul. [[george-macdonald|George MacDonald]] draws a consistent contrast between mere existence and the abundant life found in union with [[god|God]].

## Christ as Life

[[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] identifies [[christ|Christ]] as the very substance of true life, echoing the Johannine "I am the way, the truth, and the life":

> "Who said, 'I am the truth, the life, the way.'" [002-009]

> "Thou art my life, O Christ, and nothing else will do." [002-010]

The second line is particularly emphatic: nothing less than [[christ|Christ]] himself can satisfy the soul's need for life. This is not [[christ|Christ]] as dispenser of life but [[christ|Christ]] as life itself.

## Life That Is Divine

The poet yearns for a life transformed from the merely human into the divine:

> "Unworthy is my life till all divine." [001-004]

This aspiration is not self-improvement but radical transformation — the infusion of divine life into the human frame. [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] prays for [[god|God]] to [[wind|breath]]e life into what is otherwise a lifeless form:

> "Wilt thou not breathe thy life into the toy?" [004-002]

## The Inheritance of Life

True life is an inheritance that awaits full realization:

> "Into the truth, my life's inheritance." [008-001]

The life the soul seeks is already secured — it is an inheritance — but must be entered into through alignment with truth.

## Life and Weariness

[[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] is honest about the soul's fatigue with its own limited life, distinguishing it sharply from [[god|God]]'s inexhaustible life:

> "I AM a little weary of my life— / Not thy life, blessed Father!" [012-001]

The [[weary|weariness]] of the natural self is the preface to receiving divine life. The poet's dissatisfaction with his own life drives him toward the life that only [[god|God]] can give.
