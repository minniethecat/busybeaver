## Definition

In [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s [[worship|devotion]]al poetry, *self* carries a central theological paradox: the self that insists on its own autonomy is the "bastard self" that must [[death|die]], yet there is also a "holy self" — the true self known and held by [[god|God]]. Selfhood is not abolished but re-centered: the individual ego is displaced by the in[[tent|dwelling]] [[christ|Christ-self]], and only in that displacement does the person become fully real. Self-denial is thus not self-annihilation but self-discovery through divine love.

## Key Passages

> "The worst power of an evil mood is this — / It makes the bastard self seem in the right, / Self, self the end, the goal of human bliss. / But if the Christ-self in us be the might / Of saving God, why should I spend my force / With a dark thing to reason of the light?" (002-002)

[[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] contrasts the "bastard self" — the ego that sets itself as life's goal — with the "[[christ|Christ-self]]," the in[[tent|dwelling]] presence of [[god|God]] that is the true centre of identity.

> "It is thyself, and neither this nor that, / Nor anything, told, taught, or dreamed of thee, / That keeps us live." (008-008)

What sustains life is not concepts about [[god|God]] but [[god|God]]'s own self, immediately present.

> "More life I need ere I myself can be. / Sometimes, when the eternal tide ebbs low, / A moment weary of my life I grow — / Weary of my existence' self, I mean." (008-017)

Exhaustion with the self-as-it-is becomes the [[door|threshold]] of turning toward [[god|God]].

> "My holy self, thy pure ideal, lies / Calm in thy bosom, which it cannot leave; / My self unholy, no ideal, hies / Hither and thither, gathering store to grieve." (012-020)

The "holy self" is [[god|God]]'s own idea of the person, held safely in the [[mind|divine mind]] while the [[earth|earthly]] self wanders.

> "We all are lonely, Maker — each a soul / Shut in by itself, a sundered atom of thee. / No two yet loved themselves into a whole." (012-026)

The isolated self is an atom of the [[sun|divine sun]]dered from its source; love alone reunites what is divided.

## Related Concepts

The self in [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s [[mind|thought]] is inseparable from [[soul]], [[sin]], and [[obedience]]. The "bastard self" correlates with [[will]] untransformed, while the true self emerges through [[love]] and finds its being in [[god]]. The [[weary|weariness]] of self (008-017) intersects with the spiritual state explored in [[weary]], and self-surrender is the path to [[life]] that is truly life.
