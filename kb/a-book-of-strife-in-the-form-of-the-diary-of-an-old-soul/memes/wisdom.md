Wisdom in [[george-macdonald|George MacDonald]]'s "A Book of [[strife|Strife]] in the Form of The Diary of an Old [[soul|Soul]]" (1880) is radically redefined. It is not the accumulation of knowledge but the honest confession of its limits. The diary traces a downward arc: from the love of wisdom-as-book to the recognition that all human wisdom is at best "half a dream," and that only love qualifies as true knowledge.

## My Book of Wisdom

The diary opens its treatment of wisdom in February with an affectionate image:

> My book of wisdom, loved of all the best;
> Oh, bring it to my chamber blest. (002-012)

The "book of wisdom" is cherished — "loved of all the best." But the request that it be brought "to my chamber blest" hints that the book belongs to a private, [[worship|devotion]]al space rather than a public, disputative one. Wisdom, for [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]], is not a weapon for argument but a companion for prayer.

## I Have Not Knowledge, Wisdom, Insight, Thought

October brings the diary's most radical statement on wisdom:

> I have not knowledge, wisdom, insight, thought,
> Nor understanding, fit to justify
> Thee or thy ways. (010-003)

The speaker disclaims all [[mind|intellect]]ual possession. "Knowledge, wisdom, insight, [[mind|thought]], nor understanding" — the five terms exhaust the lexicon of [[mind|intellect]]ual attainment, and the speaker claims none of them. The purpose of this disavowal is theological: no [[mind|human mind]] can "justify" [[god|God]] or [[god|God]]'s ways. Wisdom begins with the renunciation of theodicy.

## My Highest Wisdom Half Is But a Dream

The confession continues, sharpening the point:

> My highest wisdom half is but a dream;
> My love runs on like a stream
> That has no source, nor end, yet must I deem
> It is thine own. (010-005)

"Highest wisdom" — the very best human thinking — is "half a dream," at best incomplete, at worst illusory. Against this half-wisdom, [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] sets love — "my love runs on like a stream" — which, though it seems sourceless and endless, is claimed as [[god|God]]'s own. The contrast is stark: wisdom is a half-dream; love, even when it cannot trace its own origin, is real because it participates in [[god|God]].

## Only Love's Knowledge Is the Primal Light

In July, the diary crystallises the priority of love over knowledge:

> Only love's knowledge is the primal light.
> Question all else; love alone is bright. (007-027)

"[[love|Love]]'s knowledge" is a category that collapses the distinction between knowing and loving. It is "primal light" — not derived from prior principles but itself the source of illumination. Everything else may be questioned; love alone is self-authenticating, "bright" by its own nature. This is the foundation of [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s epistemology: we know truly only what we love, and we love truly only what [[god|God]] has given us to love.

## Trust in Knowledge

December's warning completes the arc:

> Who trusts in knowledge all its good shall miss. (012-018)

[[trust|Trust]] misplaced in knowledge is self-defeating: the very "good" that knowledge might yield is lost by [[trust|trusting]] in knowledge itself. Knowledge is a gift to be received, not a foundation to be [[faith|trust]]ed. The one who leans on knowledge finds it gives way; the one who receives knowledge as gift finds it bears [[harvest|fruit]].
