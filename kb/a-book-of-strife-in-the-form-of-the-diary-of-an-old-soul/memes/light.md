# Light

**Light** in [[A Book of Strife in the Form of The Diary of an Old Soul]] is the dominant metaphor for [[presence|divine presence]], truth, and the illuminating work of [[christ]] in the soul. [[george-macdonald|George MacDonald]] draws on the rich biblical tradition of light as [[god|God]]'s first creation and [[christ|Christ]] as "the light of the world," weaving it into the fabric of his [[worship|devotion]]al address.

## Christ as the Light

[[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] identifies [[christ|Christ]] as the source of all true illumination. The soul sees only by participating in [[christ|Christ]]'s own light:

> "Hear in thy light, of which I am a spark." [008-006]

The image of the soul as a "spark" of divine light is characteristic — the soul has no light of its own but bears a derivative [[fire|flame]] kindled from [[god|God]]. This preserves both the transcendence of [[god|God]] and the genuine participation of the creature.

## Light and Darkness

The poet does not flee from darkness but ac[[wisdom|knowledge]]s it as the context in which light is sought:

> "With a dark thing to reason of the light." [002-008]

[[darkness|Darkness]] is not the enemy of light but its backdrop. [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] refuses to pretend that the soul does not experience darkness; instead, he brings his darkness into the presence of the one who is light.

## The Promise of Light

Light is both a present reality and a future promise. The poet [[faith|trust]]s that [[christ|Christ]] will give light even when he cannot yet see:

> "And Christ will give thee light. I do not know / What sleep is, what is death, or what is light." [011-007]

The admission of ignorance is honest: the poet does not claim to understand the nature of light, only its source. [[trust|Trust]] in the [[gift|giver]] precedes comprehension of the gift.

## Light as Life

[[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] connects light with life itself, echoing the Johannine theme that in [[christ|Christ]] "was life, and the life was the light of men":

> "Be thou, O Life eternal, in me light." [002-020]

Light is not mere information or [[mind|intellect]]ual clarity; it is life-[[gift|giving]] presence. To be illuminated is to be made alive.
