The **way** is a central metaphor in George MacDonald's _A Book of Strife in the Form of The Diary of an Old Soul_, representing the spiritual path by which the soul journeys toward God. It encompasses both the divine direction and the human act of walking in obedience.

## The Way as Divine Path

MacDonald presents the way as divinely ordained, the only route to true blessedness. The soul does not choose its own path but follows the way God has established:

> Thou know'st no other way to bliss the highest
> Than love. (004-008)

## Walking in the World

The way is set in contrast to worldly paths. The soul must choose between the "loud world's busy way" and the quiet path of devotion:

> Not walk the loud world's busy way,
> Yet in thy palace-porch sit. (008-021)

## The Way of Suffering

The way often passes through suffering and loss. MacDonald's speaker acknowledges that pain and stripping away are part of the divine way:

> The hand away that stripped me for the rod. (004-015)
