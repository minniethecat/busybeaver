# Christ

## Biography

Christ is the central figure of [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s [[worship|devotion]]al poetry — not a
distant historical personage but a living, in[[tent|dwelling]] presence. [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]
portrays Christ as the "High Priest" whose glow rises behind the unopened
door (001-002), the "Christ-self" within each believer (002-002), and the
risen Lord who walks crowned rather than lying in the tomb (006-005).
In the poems, Christ is simultaneously the object of faith, the model of
obe[[death|die]]nce, and the inner power that makes obe[[death|die]]nce possible.

## Connection to the Work

"A Book of [[strife|Strife]]" is addressed to Christ throughout — nearly every poem
is a prayer. The speaker w[[peace|rest]]les with doubt, [[weary|weariness]], and the failure
of his own will, and at every turn turns to Christ as the answer. Christ
appears as the way to the Father (002-003), the fellow worker (006-005),
the for[[gift|giver]] (006-028), and the elder brother (006). [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s
Christology here is deeply incarnational: Christ is not merely an example
to follow but a living presence who transforms the believer from within.
