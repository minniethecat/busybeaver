## Definition

In *A Book of [[strife|Strife]]*, infinity is not a mathematical abstraction but the living atmosphere of [[god|God]]. The soul "[[wind|breath]]es only in thy infinite soul" (001-005) — infinity is the element in which spiritual life is possible.

[[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] uses spatial images: "infinite reaches bathing many a coast" (003-009), "the infinite zenith of my soul" (001-022). Yet infinity is not merely vast space; it is personal and filling: [[god|God]] fills the soul with "an infinite overflow" (001-025).

The soul's journey toward [[god|God]] is an approach across "infinitely closer" distances (004-027), and [[faith|trust]] itself should be "grandly, infinitely" expansive (008-029). The infinite presses close — as near as the [[peace|rest]] to which all loves return (011-030).

## Key Passages

> "My soul breathes only in thy infinite soul; / I breathe, I think, I live, I love, I will." (001-005)

[[god|God]]'s soul is the infinite atmosphere the human soul inhales. Every act of the soul draws its possibility from this infinite source.

> "Across the infinite zenith of my soul." (001-022)

[[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] places infinity within the soul. The soul participates in infinity; it has its own infinite dimension.

> "Filling me with an infinite overflow." (001-025)

[[divine|Divine]] fullness exceeds containment. The soul is filled not to capacity but beyond it, continuously spilling over.

> "How close, how infinitely closer yet / Must I come to thee." (004-027)

No approach to [[god|God]] is final. Each step closer reveals an infinitely closer distance yet to travel.

> "Trust right largely, grandly, infinitely, / Daring the splendour of the Father's peace." (008-029)

[[trust|Trust]] should match its object — if [[god|God]] is infinite, [[faith|trust]] must stretch toward infinity. [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] urges a [[faith|trust]] that dares.

## Related Concepts

The infinite is the native element of [[god]] and the medium in which [[soul]] lives. It connects to [[trust]] as the appropriately boundless response to an infinite [[god|God]], to [[eternal]] as infinity in time, to [[death]] as the hair's-breadth distance from infinite loss, and to [[love]] as the infinite overflow that fills the soul.
