## Definition

In *A Book of [[strife|Strife]]*, [[wind|breath]] is the central metaphor for the life-[[gift|giving]] presence of [[god|God]] in the human soul. Just as "thy fishes [[wind|breath]]e but where thy [[water|waters]] roll" (001-005), so the soul [[wind|breath]]es only in [[god|God]]'s infinite being.

[[divine|Divine]] [[wind|breath]] animates, fills, and transforms: it "wakes beauty, love, and [[joy|bliss]]" (005-006), s[[water|well]]s the soul's small song into a wing (001-012), and blows on the soul's [[death|dying]] fires to rekindle them (011-008).

To be without [[god|God]]'s [[wind|breath]] is to be "[[thirst|athirst]] and empty" (005-021); to be filled with it is to [[wind|breath]]e in "the God-breathed spirit air" (005-012). Breath thus unites creation, inspiration, and sanctification into a single vivid image.

## Key Passages

> "My soul breathes only in thy infinite soul; / I breathe, I think, I live, I love, I will." (001-005)

The soul's [[wind|breath]] is drawn from [[god|God]]'s own infinite being — not metaphorically but essentially. All functions of the soul depend on this sustaining inflow.

> "Oh! swell it with thy breath; make it a wing, — / To sweep through all the mystery of thing." (001-012)

The small song of the human soul, when filled with divine breath, becomes capable of flight — [[wind|breath]] transforms limitation into transcendence.

> "Thy breath wakes beauty, love, and bliss among / The trees, and brook, and moss." (005-006)

[[divine|Divine]] [[wind|breath]] is the [[wake|awakening]] force in creation — a present, in[[tent|dwelling]] animation that makes the natural world alive with beauty and love.

> "Lord, with thy breath blow on my being's fires, / Until they flame to thee." (011-008)

The soul's fires, banked and [[death|dying]], need the wind of [[god|God]]'s [[wind|breath]] to blaze again. This is an explicit prayer for rekindling — [[wind|breath]] as revival.

## Related Concepts

Breath is inseparable from [[life]] as its animating principle, from [[spirit]] as the [[holy|Holy]] [[spirit|Spirit]]'s mode of in[[tent|dwelling]], and from [[soul]] as the recipient of divine breath. It connects to [[fire]] through the image of [[wind|breath]] blowing on [[death|dying]] embers, to [[wind]] as the [[spirit|Spirit]]'s movement, to [[grace]] as unearned animation, and to [[infinite]] as the source from which the soul draws [[wind|breath]].
