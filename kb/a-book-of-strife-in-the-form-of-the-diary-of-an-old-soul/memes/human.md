## Definition

In [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s [[worship|devotion]]al poetry, the *human* is an ambivalent category: humanity is at once "a human clod" (002-017) — fallen, selfish, earthbound — and the [[clay|vessel]] intended for divine in[[tent|dwelling]]. The human face will one day "shine" as a window for eternal light (004-012), and the human heart, though "[[earth|dust]]" when [[god|God]] seems absent (008-029), is also the [[tent|dwelling]]-place of [[god|God]]'s in[[tent|dwelling]] life. [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] never condemns the human as essentially worthless; rather, he sees it as incomplete — a creature that, left to itself, collapses into selfishness, but when inhabited by the [[christ|Christ-self]], becomes the locus of the divine image. The "perfect human face" of [[christ|Christ]] (008-025) is the pattern toward which all humanity tends.

## Key Passages

> "Each human face will shine my window for / Thy light to enter me." (004-012)

The human face — each one — is destined to become a window through which divine light passes into the beholder. Humanity is not opaque but potentially translucent.

> "Lord, I have fallen again — a human clod! / Selfish I was, and heedless of thy will." (002-017)

The speaker confesses the "human clod" — the heaviness, [[self|the self]]ishness, the earthward pull that is native to unaided humanity.

> "And shining from thy perfect human face, / Will sink into me like the silent light." (008-025)

[[christ|Christ]] bears the "perfect human face" — the fulfilled humanity that sinks into the beholder not as demand but as light.

> "Faith is the human shadow of thy might." (006-009)

[[faith|Faith]] is described as a human shadow cast by divine might — derivative, dependent, yet real, the silhouette [[god|God]]'s power makes on a human soul.

> "I am less than a man, with more than human cries — / An unshaped thing in search of what to be." (003-019)

The speaker's anguish is "more than human" yet he is "less than a man" — a creature whose intensity of [[desire|longing]] exceeds its form, an incompleteness crying toward completion.

> "The human thought of the eternal mind, / Washes many a coast." (003-009)

Human [[mind|thought]] is the tide-wash of the eternal mind — it originates beyond itself, moving like water across the shores of [[conscious|consciousness]].

## Related Concepts

The human condition intersects with [[sin]] and [[self]] — especially the distinction between the "bastard self" and the [[christ|Christ-self]]. The transformation of the human is accomplished through [[love]], [[grace]], and the in[[tent|dwelling]] of [[god]]. The yearning of the human heart finds its object in [[bliss]] and its [[peace|rest]] in [[home]].
