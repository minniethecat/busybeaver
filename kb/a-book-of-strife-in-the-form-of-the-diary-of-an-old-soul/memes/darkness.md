# Darkness

**Darkness** in [[A Book of Strife in the Form of The Diary of an Old Soul]] is the spiritual condition of separation from [[god|God]] — a state [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] explores with unflinching honesty while insisting that darkness belongs to the human soul, never to [[god|God]]. It is the context in which [[desire|longing]] for [[light]] is born and faith is tested.

## Darkness Belongs to the Soul, Not to God

[[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] is emphatic that darkness has its origin in the human condition, not in the divine nature:

> "Not thine, my Lord, the darkness all is mine." [007-003]

> "For God is light — in him no darkness is at all." [002-023]

This clear distinction protects [[god|God]]'s character while taking full [[duty|responsibility]] for the soul's experience of obscurity. The darkness is real but it is not ultimate.

## The Experience of Deepening Darkness

The poet does not minimise the se[[truth|verity]] of spiritual darkness. It is a "dire deepening" that presses upon the soul:

> "The darkness dire deepening with moan and mirth." [007-002]

[[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] gives voice to the soul's disorientation in darkness without rushing to resolve it. The darkness must be felt before it can be understood.

## Darkness as the Womb of Light

In a characteristically paradoxical move, [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] envisions darkness as the very context from which light emerges:

> "Born in the darkness, what fair-flashing gem / Would seem a sickly flake of falling snow!" [012-004]

> "Though on his darkness he should found a school." [006-013]

The image of a gem born in darkness suggests that what is formed in obscurity may shine with peculiar brilliance when brought into light. Darkness is not merely to be endured — it can become a place of formation.

## Waiting Through Darkness

The proper response to darkness is not panic but patient waiting:

> "If we might sit until the darkness go, / Possess our souls in patience." [011-004]

This patience is not passivity but the active possession of one's soul — a deliberate holding of oneself in [[faith|trust]] while darkness lasts.
