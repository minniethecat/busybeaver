## Definition

In [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s theology, *perfection* belongs first to [[god|God]]: [[god|God]] loves perfectly, and that perfect love is both the standard and the power that perfects the human soul. Perfection is not a human achievement but a divine gift worked gradually — the soul is "hewn in the rough" and shaped toward completion. The perfection of love in the creature is a response to and participation in the perfection of love in the Creator. [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] holds perfection and process together: the perfect end is certain, but the way is slow and often painful.

## Key Passages

> "If more can be and less, in love's perfect zone! / My fancy shrinks from least of all thy harms, / But do thy will with me — I am thine own." (004-001)

The "perfect zone" of love is [[god|God]]'s own sphere, where even harm serves love's purposes.

> "Thou lovest perfectly — that is thy bliss: / We must love like thee, or our being miss." (004-008)

[[god|God]]'s perfect love is both the pattern and the necessity: to love perfectly is to fulfill one's being.

> "To love the perfect love, is primal, mere / Necessity; and he who holds it not / Is as a beast that knows not why he is." (004-009)

[[love|Love]] of perfect love is not optional but constitutive of [[human|humanity]].

> "Thou hast me, statue-like, hewn in the rough, / Meaning at last to shape me perfectly." (012-014)

Perfection is a sculpting process — the raw block is already in the artist's hands.

> "Ideal son, thou hast left the perfect home, / Ideal brother, to seek thy brothers come!" (012-021)

[[christ|Christ]] leaves the "perfect home" — the divine sphere — to seek the imperfect, making perfection accessible.

> "Love's perfect will can be nor sore nor small, / For him who doth the loved one's voice fulfil." (002-023)

The perfect will of love transforms duty into joy for the [[obedience|obedient]] heart.

## Related Concepts

Perfection is the horizon toward which [[love]], [[obedience]], and [[joy]] tend. It stands in tension with the present state of [[sin]] and [[will]], and is realised through the work of [[god]] in [[christ|Christ]]. The process of perfecting relates to [[soul]]-making and intersects with [[trust]] in the divine craftsman. The perfected state correlates with [[bliss]] — the beatific fullness that is the soul's [[home|true home]].
