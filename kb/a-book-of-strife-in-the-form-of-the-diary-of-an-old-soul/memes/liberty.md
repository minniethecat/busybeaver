**Liberty** in MacDonald's poetry is not political freedom but spiritual liberation — the [[soul]]'s release from bondage to sin, self, and the [[world]]. It is found paradoxically through [[surrender]] to [[god|God]].

## True Freedom

MacDonald presents liberty as the fruit of divine [[grace]], not human effort. The [[soul]] freed by God walks in the open:

> The soul set free upon the eternal ground. (004-018)

## Liberty Through Obedience

Paradoxically, true liberty comes through [[obedience]]. To bind oneself to God's [[law]] is to be freed from every lesser bondage. This reflects the Christian paradox that service to God is perfect freedom.

## Liberty and Love

Liberty is inseparable from [[love]]. The soul freed by God loves freely, without compulsion. [[love|Love]] is both the means and the end of spiritual liberty.
