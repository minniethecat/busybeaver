# Will

**Will** in [[A Book of Strife in the Form of The Diary of an Old Soul]] is the central faculty of human agency that must be yielded to divine will. [[george-macdonald|George MacDonald]] explores will as both the seat of human resistance and the instrument of union with [[god|God]], tracing a movement from [[strife|struggle]] through surrender to creative participation in the divine purpose.

## The Surrender of Will

The fundamental imperative in [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s treatment of will is surrender — the yielding of human will to [[god|God]]'s will. This is not presented as a one-time decision but as an ongoing labour:

> "Thy will be done. I yield up everything." [001-007]

> "Help me to yield my will, in labour even." [002-014]

The surrender is a conscious act that must be renewed. [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] frames the question of salvation itself in terms of will-alignment:

> "'Tis—shall thy will be done for me?—or mine." [002-016]

## Human Will and Divine Will in Tension

[[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] does not depict surrender as effortless. The human will resists, and the poet gives voice to this tension with striking honesty:

> "Would that my will did sweep full swing with thine!" [001-009]

> "My will to seek thee—only to fear the more." [001-011]

The gap between human and divine will is ac[[wisdom|knowledge]]d as a source of [[pain|suffering]], yet [[pain|suffering]] itself becomes the context in which will is purified.

## Will as Creative Participation

Beyond mere submission, [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] envisions the transformed will as participating in [[god|God]]'s own creative activity. The yielding of will is not annihilation but adoption into a higher mode of agency:

> "Then give us making will, adopting thine." [003-029]

> "To will to die is one with highest life, / The mightiest act that to Will's hand doth lie." [003-030]

The paradox is central: the death of [[self|the self]]-will is the birth of a truer, more powerful will that shares in the divine nature.

## Will and Love

For [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]], will cannot be separated from love. The [[perfect|perfection]] of will is found in its union with perfect love:

> "Love's perfect will can be nor sore nor small." [002-017]

> "The loves that love, the wills that will the same." [004-025]

The alignment of will with [[god|God]] is at root an alignment of love — to love what [[god|God]] loves is to will what [[god|God]] wills.
