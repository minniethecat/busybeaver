## Definition

In [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s [[worship|devotion]]al poetry, *[[joy|bliss]]* is not mere happiness or pleasure but the intrinsic blessedness of [[god|God]]'s own life — "in thee lies every [[joy|bliss]] / Whose shadow men go hunting everywhere" (004-005). [[earth|Earth]]ly joys are [[shadow|shadows]] cast by divine bliss; humans chase the [[shadow|shadows]] while the substance waits in [[god|God]]. Bliss is the atmosphere of the [[presence|divine presence]]: the speaker experiences "calm [[joy|bliss]]" even in meeting [[god|God]]'s "troubled [[mind|thought]]" (001-011), because [[joy|bliss]] is not the absence of trouble but the presence of [[god|God]]. [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] describes a "[[perfect|perfectness]] of [[joy|bliss]] beyond compare" (004-029) that lies at the end of the spiritual journey, and a state in which the soul is "one with the [[joy|bliss]] of the eternal mind" (012-009). Bliss, in this vision, is participatory — it cannot be possessed; it can only be entered, and to enter it is to love [[god|God]]: "Thou know'st no other way to [[joy|bliss]] the highest / Than loving thee" (004-008).

## Key Passages

> "In thee lies every bliss / Whose shadow men go hunting everywhere." (004-005)

All human pursuits of happiness are hunting [[shadow|shadows]]; the substance of [[joy|bliss]] resides in [[god|God]] alone.

> "Thou know'st no other way to bliss the highest / Than loving thee, O Lord, the only bliss!" (004-008)

The "highest [[joy|bliss]]" has only one path — loving [[god|God]] — and [[god|God]] himself is that [[joy|bliss]]. The way and the destination are one.

> "A perfectness of bliss beyond compare! / A time when I shall know that I am thine." (004-029)

Bliss is consummated in the [[wisdom|knowledge]] of be[[desire|longing]] — "I am thine" — not in any acquisition or achievement.

> "Not less my spirit with calm bliss doth glow, / Meeting thee only in thy troubled thought." (001-011)

Bliss is not contradicted by meeting [[god|God]]'s "troubled [[mind|thought]]"; the soul can glow with calm [[joy|bliss]] even as it shares the divine burden.

> "One with the bliss of the eternal mind; / And all the pulses of thy heart my own." (012-009)

The ultimate state is union: the soul shares the very [[joy|bliss]] of the eternal mind, and the pulses of [[god|God]]'s heart become the soul's own heartbeat.

> "What makes thy being a bliss shall then make mine / For I shall see thee as thou art, and be." (012-010)

Bliss is participatory — what constitutes [[god|God]]'s own blessedness will constitute the soul's, because vision transforms the seer.

## Related Concepts

Bliss is the substance of which [[joy]] is the shadow, and the fulfillment of every [[desire]]. It is inseparable from [[love]] — to love is to enter [[joy|bliss]] — and is the destination to which [[home]] draws the soul. The self that sets itself as "the goal of human [[joy|bliss]]" (002-002) misses [[joy|bliss]] entirely; blessedness is found only in the displacement of [[self]] by the [[christ|Christ-self]].
