**Might** in MacDonald's _A Book of Strife_ refers primarily to divine power — God's sustaining and creative energy that upholds the soul. It stands in contrast to human frailty and is always exercised in the service of [[love]].

## Divine Might

MacDonald presents might as an attribute of God that opens toward the eternal. It is not brute force but creative, life-giving power:

> Open to the eternal morning's might. (004-012)

## Might and Weakness

Divine might is paradoxically revealed through human weakness. The speaker acknowledges that true strength comes not from self but from God:

> 'Tis not strength, nor youth,
> That content me. (012-006)

## Might and Ripeness

Spiritual growth involves the paradox of losing might to gain ripeness:

> Ripeness must always come with loss of might. (012-003)
