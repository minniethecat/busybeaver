The **cup** in MacDonald's poetry is a multivalent symbol: it can be the cup of [[suffering]] that the [[soul]] must drink, the cup of [[blessing]] that overflows with divine [[grace]], or the vessel of the self that God fills.

## The Cup of Suffering

Like the biblical cup that [[christ|Christ]] prayed would pass, MacDonald's cup represents the portion of suffering allotted to each [[soul]]. To drink it is to accept the divine will:

> The cup my Father gives me to drink. (008-009)

## The Cup as Self

The [[soul]] itself is a cup — an empty vessel awaiting divine filling. The [[heart]] must be emptied of self before it can receive God. This aligns with the broader theme of [[emptiness]] as prerequisite for divine indwelling.
