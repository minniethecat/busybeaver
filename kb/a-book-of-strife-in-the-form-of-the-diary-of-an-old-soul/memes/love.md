Love is the central theological theme of [[george-macdonald|George MacDonald]]'s "A Book of [[strife|Strife]] in the Form of The Diary of an Old [[soul|Soul]]" (1880). Across its 366 seven-line stanzas, the word "love" appears over 120 times, making it the most pervasive concept in the work after the divine names themselves. [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] presents love not as mere sentiment but as the active, purifying power of [[god|God]] that transforms the [[heart]] and draws the soul toward its source. In [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s theology, love is simultaneously the nature of [[lord|the Lord]], the method of redemption, and the ultimate destination of the spiritual life.

## Definition in MacDonald's Thought

For [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]], love is not an attribute that [[god|God]] *has* — it is what [[god|God]] *is*. The opening of the June section establishes this explicitly:

> But, like a virtuous medicine, self-diffused / Through all men's hearts thy love shall sink and float; / Till every feeling false, and thought unwise, / Selfish and cold, shall take the golden glow (006-004)

[[divine|Divine]] love is "self-diffused" — it spreads outward without diminishment. It works like medicine, healing the diseased [[heart]] from within. This conception draws on the [[christ|Christ]]ian theological tradition of *caritas* (charity) as [[god|God]]'s essential nature, but [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] gives it a distinctively personal, experiential cast through the diary form.

Love also functions as the standard against which all other virtues are measured. Anger purified becomes love (006-025); [[righteousness|justice]] without love corrupts into revenge (006-022); [[mother|motherhood]] without love becomes possessive jealousy. Love is the ontological [[earth|ground]]: "Only love's [[wisdom|knowledge]] is the primal light" (007-027).

## Key Passages

The diary returns to love as its central refrain. Several passages mark key [[growth|development]]s in the speaker's [[wisdom|understanding]]:

> Life-giving love rots to devouring fire; / Justice corrupts to despicable revenge; / Motherhood chokes in the dam's jealous mire; / Hunger for growth turns to the lust of mammon (006-022)

This stanza catalogues the perversion of goods when separated from their source in divine love. Each virtue becomes its opposite vice. The pattern is diagnostic: wherever [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] observes corruption, he traces it to love's absence.

> The love of thee will set all notions right. / Right save by love no thought can be or may; / Only love's knowledge is the primal light. / Questions keep doubts, doubts breed despair and blight (007-027)

Here love is epistemologically primary — the foundation of all true [[wisdom|knowledge]]. Intellectual questions, unresolved, breed despair; love alone resolves them. This passage anchors [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s anti-doctrinal bent: right thinking flows from right loving, not the reverse.

> But of thy love, my God, one glimpse alone / Can make me absolutely confident-- / With faith, hope, joy, in love responsive grown (010-004)

A single glimpse of divine love produces more certainty than all argument. The speaker's confidence is "responsive" — it arises in answer to love received, not earned.

## The Ascent Through Love

[[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] traces a spiritual [[rise|ascent]] in which love progressively refines the soul:

| Stage | PN Source | Description |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Reception | 006-004 | Love enters the heart as healing medicine |
| Purification | 006-025 | Anger and selfishness burned away, leaving pure love |
| Transformation | 007-027 | Love becomes the organ of true [[wisdom|knowledge]] |
| Union | 010-004 | The soul grows "responsive" to divine love |
| Overflow | 012-002 | Love flows outward to others as patience and endurance |

This five-stage pattern mirrors the classical mystical [[rise|ascent]] (purgation → illumination → union) but inflected through [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s distinctive idiom of father-child intimacy. The soul does not climb to [[god|God]]; love descends into the soul and carries it upward.

## Related Concepts

Love in the *Diary of an Old [[soul|Soul]]* is inseparable from several other theological concepts:

- **[[heart]]** — The organ that receives and responds to love; love's [[tent|dwelling]]-place in the human person. [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] treats heart and love as correlative: heart is the *capacity*, love is the *content*.
- **[[lord|Lord]]** — The personal source of love. The speaker's addresses to "[[christ|Lord]]" are almost always [[prayer|petition]]s for more love, purer love, or deliverance from love's perversions.
- **[[father|Father]]** — Love's relational mode. [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s preference for "Father" over more distant divine titles emphasizes love as familial intimacy rather than abstract principle.
- **[[obedience]]** — Love's practical expression. To love [[god|God]] is to will what [[god|God]] wills; obe[[death|die]]nce is love made actionable.
- **[[faith]]** — Love's cognitive counterpart. [[faith|Faith]] is [[faith|belief]] *in* love even when love is not felt.
