# Holy

**Holiness** in [[A Book of Strife in the Form of The Diary of an Old Soul]] is both the essential character of [[god|God]] and the transformative goal of the human soul. [[george-macdonald|George MacDonald]] presents holiness not as sterile separation but as the atmosphere of divine life into which the believer is drawn through [[christ|Christ]].

## Holiness as Divine Demand and Gift

[[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] frames holiness as something [[god|God]] both requires and bestows. The demand for love is itself a demand for holiness, coming from the one who is holy:

> "Thou dost demand our love, holy Lord Christ." [004-008]

Yet holiness is not merely a standard imposed from without. It is the native element of [[god|God]]'s own life, into which the soul is invited.

## The Danger of Unholy Holiness

With characteristic psychological honesty, [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] warns of the corruption that can hide within religious practice:

> "In holy things may be unholy greed." [008-007]

This [[wisdom|insight]] — that the very sphere of holiness can become a site of spiritual d[[wrath|anger]] — reflects [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s suspicion of religious formalism and his insistence on inward transformation over outward observance.

## Holiness as Human Destiny

The poet envisions holiness as the true identity of the redeemed self, not an alien imposition but the recovery of what [[self|the self]] was always meant to be:

> "My holy self, thy pure ideal, lies / Calm in thy thought." [012-020]

Holiness is the realisation of [[god|God]]'s "pure ideal" for each person — a destiny already conceived in the [[mind|divine mind]] and progressively unfolded through the spiritual life.

## The Community of the Holy

Holiness is not individualistic in [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s vision. It creates a fellow[[vessel|ship]]:

> "Oh brothers! sisters holy! hearts divine!" [001-006]

The holy are not a separate caste but a family bound by shared participation in the divine nature. This communal dimension of holiness reflects [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s vision of a redeemed [[human|humanity]] living in mutual recognition.
