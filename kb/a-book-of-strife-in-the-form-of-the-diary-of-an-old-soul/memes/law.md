**Law** in MacDonald's poetry is not external regulation but the inner moral order that flows from God's nature. It is fulfilled through [[love]] and [[obedience]], not mere compliance.

## Law and Obedience

The divine law is honored through human obedience. MacDonald presents this relationship as mutual — God honors the obedient soul:

> Thou honourest his obedience, he thy law. (012-024)

## Law as Expression of Love

The law is not opposed to [[love]] but is its structured expression. To obey God's law is to walk in love. This understanding places law within the context of relationship rather than legalism.

## Law and the Way

The divine law guides the [[soul]] along the [[way]]. It provides direction when feelings fail and [[darkness]] obscures the path forward.
