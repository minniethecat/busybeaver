## Definition

In [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s poetry, "mind" operates at two levels: the human mind as a receptive channel for divine [[light]] and [[truth]], and the divine Mind as the infinite source from which all finite [[wisdom|understanding]] flows. The human mind is not an autonomous reasoning faculty but a conduit: "through all the channels of my mind; / The sun of thought, across the farthest mind" (004-023). [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s epistemology is participatory — to know is to receive, and the mind's health depends on its openness to the "eternal mind" (003-009) rather than its capacity for independent intellection.

## Key Passages

The speaker's most striking image of mental illumination depicts thought as solar: "The sun of thought, across the farthest mind" (004-023). Here the mind is not the generator of thought but its landscape — thought travels across it like [[sun|sunlight]] across terrain. This reverses the modern assumption of mind as originating agent; for [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]], the mind is first a receiver.

The ontological priority of divine Mind appears in: "The best is only in thy perfect mind" (003-007). [[human|Human]] minds possess only fragments and [[shadow|shadows]]; completeness resides in [[god|God]]'s mind alone. This is echoed in: "human thought of the eternal mind, / Pulsed by a living tide" (003-009), where human thinking is a pulse driven by a tide originating beyond itself.

The speaker's aspiration is toward mental union with [[god|God]]: "see him all in all, the lifting mind" (001-009), and "In one thy oneness us together bind; / Clearing, growing, heart and mind" (001-018). [[spirit|Spirit]]ual growth involves the progressive clarification and expansion of the mind, not its abandonment. Yet the mind can also become a barrier when it turns self-referential: "To fade into an image of my mind; / I, dreamer, cover, hide thee" (006-007), where the mind's own images obscure rather than reveal [[god|God]].

The speaker explicitly subordinates his mind to divine mastery: "[[christ|Lord]], make me mind thee" (003-025), a deliberate wordplay where "mind" shifts from noun to verb — to mind [[god|God]] is to hold [[god|God]] in [[conscious|awareness]]. The most daring line in the sequence extends this: "Thy mind, my master, I will dare explore" (003-030), where the human mind ventures into the divine mind as into a territory.

The union of will and intellect appears in: "[[duty|Duty]] alive, self-willed, in me, / [[christ|Christ]], with my deepest mind" (007-030), where the deepest mind is the site of [[will]]'s alignment with [[christ|Christ]] rather than of abstract reasoning.

## Related Concepts

Mind's receptive character connects to [[wisdom]] as divine gift versus human acquisition. The "sun of thought" ties mind directly to [[sun]] and [[light]] as sources of illumination. The mind's capacity for self-deception links to [[dream]] and [[sin]], while its clarifying function connects to [[truth]]. The "eternal mind" is the source of [[life]], [[love]], and [[grace]]. Mind's integration with will in moral action connects to [[duty]] and [[obedience]].
