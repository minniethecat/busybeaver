## Definition

In *A Book of [[strife|Strife]]*, [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] employs maternal imagery to describe both the soul's origin in [[god|God]] and the quality of divine love. [[god|God]] is not only Father; the divine care carries a mother's tenderness — folding arms, watchful gaze, a lap the child instinctively seeks (001-006). The earth itself is "mother earth" from which the soul is "new-born again" (001-029), and even fire yields "flowers from their mother's prison" (007-021). The mother-child bond serves as [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s deepest metaphor for the soul's relation[[vessel|ship]] with [[god|God]]: a love that holds, nurtures, and from which the soul may stray but to which it always returns.

## Key Passages

> "To every human heart a mother-twin. / A mother angel, see! folding each wing" (001-006 – 001-007)

[[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] introduces the mother-image early: every heart has a "mother-twin" — an angelic maternal presence that mirrors [[god|God]]'s own nurturing care. The angel folds its wings around the soul as a mother folds her arms.

> "The child that, weary of his mother's petting, / Runs out the moment" (008-023)

Even the child who grows weary of maternal affection runs only for a moment; the impulse is always to return. This captures the soul's [[peace|rest]]lessness and its inevitable homecoming.

> "As, hungering for his mother's face and eyes, / The child through darkness" (012-015)

The child in darkness hungers not for [[bread|food]] but for the mother's *face* — presence, recognition, love. [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] uses this to describe the soul's [[desire|longing]] for [[god|God]]'s countenance.

> "Will leave his mother's lap, run down the stair" (002-026)

The soul's journey away from [[god|God]] is figured as a child leaving the safety of the mother's lap — a descent into distance and d[[wrath|anger]], yet always with the possibility of return.

## Related Concepts

The divine motherhood complements [[father]] as a second parental image for [[god|God]]'s care. It connects to [[love]] as nurturing affection, to [[home]] as the place of return, to [[birth]] as spiritual origin, and to [[trust]] as the child's instinctive posture toward the divine parent. The mother metaphor also links to [[grace]] as unearned tenderness and [[life]] as the gift of maternal origin.
