To **walk** in MacDonald's poetry is to live the daily spiritual life — the steady, step-by-step progress of the [[soul]] toward [[god|God]]. It is not dramatic spiritual experience but faithful persistence along the [[way]].

## Walking with God

The walk is a metaphor for ongoing relationship. MacDonald's speaker walks not alone but in the divine presence:

> I walk with Him upon the eternal floor. (004-017)

## The Daily Walk

The spiritual walk is not about great leaps but about daily [[faithfulness]]. Each step matters. The walk encompasses ordinary [[obedience]], quiet [[prayer]], and steady [[love]].

## Walking vs. Running

MacDonald's walk contrasts with the restless haste of the [[world]]. The [[soul]] that walks with God moves at a divine pace — unhurried, steady, and sure. This aligns with [[patience]] and [[waiting]] as spiritual virtues.
