# God

**God** in [[A Book of Strife in the Form of The Diary of an Old Soul]] is the personal, loving Father to whom the poet addresses every stanza. [[george-macdonald|George MacDonald]]'s God is not an abstract philosophical principle but a living presence — the source of life, the object of [[faith|trust]], and the agent of the soul's transformation.

## God as Father

[[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s primary mode of address is "Father," reflecting an intimate, familial [[wisdom|understanding]] of the divine. God is not distant or indifferent but actively engaged in the soul's growth:

> "Ah, me, my God! in thee lies every bliss." [004-005]

The fatherhood of God is inseparable from his love, and the poet's relation[[vessel|ship]] with God is framed in terms of [[faith|trust]] and dependence rather than fear or transaction.

## Trust in God as the Highest Action

For [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]], [[trust|trust in God]] is not passive resignation but the highest form of human activity:

> "To trust in God is action's highest kind." [008-007]

This reframing of [[faith|trust]] as action is central to [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s theology. The soul that [[faith|trust]]s is not idle; it is exercising the most fundamental of spiritual faculties.

## God as All in All

The ultimate horizon of [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s vision is the complete in[[tent|dwelling]] of God in all things:

> "A moment more, and God is all in all." [009-002]

This echoes the Pauline vision of 1 Corinthians 15:28 and reflects [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s hope for the full realization of [[presence|divine presence]] — not the annihilation of [[self|the self]] but its fulfilment in God.

## The Living God

God is not an idea but a living reality whose presence transforms from within:

> "Comes from the living God at very heart." [009-005]

The poet experiences God not as a doctrine to be affirmed but as a life to be received. This living quality distinguishes [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s God from theological abstraction.
