## Definition

In [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s [[worship|devotion]]al poetry, *[[faith|trust]]* is not a static doctrinal stance but a dynamic act of self-surrender that is paradoxically the "highest kind" of action (008-005). Trust is the soul's response to the Fatherhood of [[god|God]]: to [[faith|trust]] is to lean into divine care, to receive rather than to grasp. [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] treats [[faith|trust]] as gain and growth — not a one-time decision but a deepening orientation of the heart that, like a root, draws life from unseeable depths (008-009). Crucially, [[faith|trust]] coexists with doubt; it is forged not in certainty's daylight but in the darkness where [[doubt|mistrust]] is "possible only, but its opposite true" (008-030).

## Key Passages

> "To trust in God is action's highest kind; / Who trusts in God, his heart goes life throughout." (008-005)

Trust redefines what action means: the highest deed is not doing but entrusting, and the one who [[faith|trust]]s carries life through every chamber of the heart.

> "Nor trust the living root beneath the sod; / To trust is gain and growth, not mere repose." (008-021)

[[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] distinguishes [[faith|trust]] from passive [[peace|rest]]. Trust grows like a root — unseen, alive, deepening. It is the opposite of hoarding surface [[harvest|fruit]].

> "Then most I trust when most I would receive. / I must have perfect being, Lord, in thee." (008-016)

Trust and receptivity are inseparable: the moment of deepest need is the moment of deepest [[faith|trust]], because only [[god|God]] possesses the fullness the soul lacks.

> "Thou art the father of thy people; thou / Wilt not forsake; help me, dull of heart, to trust in thee." (004-020)

Trust is [[earth|ground]]ed in the character of [[god|God]] as Father. The speaker confesses a "dull heart" and asks for help even to [[faith|trust]] — [[faith|trust]] itself is a gift.

> "Sometimes it seems pure natural to trust, / And trust right largely, grandly, infinitely." (008-029)

Trust is not always a strain; there are moments when [[faith|trust]] feels as natural as [[wind|breath]]ing, generous and unbounded.

> "Against my fears, my doubts, my ignorance, / I trust in thee, O father of my Lord!" (010-022)

Trust is not the absence of fear, doubt, or ignorance but the act that stands against them, rooted in personal relation[[vessel|ship]] and filial address.

## Related Concepts

Trust is the inward form of [[faith]] and the outward posture of [[obedience]]. It stands opposite to [[doubt]] yet learns to live with it, and is the lived expression of the soul's relation[[vessel|ship]] to [[god]] as Father ([[father]]). Trust grows in the soil of [[humility]] and yields the [[harvest|fruit]] of [[peace]].
