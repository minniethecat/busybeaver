**Strength** in MacDonald's _A Book of Strife_ is understood primarily as divine strength — a gift from God that sustains the [[soul]] through trials. Human strength is insufficient; only what comes from above endures.

## Strength Beyond Self

The speaker confesses that natural strength and youth cannot bring contentment. True satisfaction comes only from [[god|God]]:

> 'Tis not strength, nor youth,
> That content me. (012-006)

## Strength in Weakness

MacDonald echoes the Pauline paradox that divine [[power]] is perfected in human [[weakness]]. The soul that acknowledges its frailty becomes the vessel of God's [[might]].

## Strength and Endurance

Spiritual strength is not about heroic acts but about enduring [[suffering]] and [[waiting]] with faith. It sustains the soul through the long journey of the [[way]].
