## Definition

In *A Book of [[strife|Strife]]*, patience is a spiritual virtue of extraordinary depth — far more than passive waiting. [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] describes it as "strong patience" (012-001), "perfect patience" (012-002), and "patience forceful" (005-021).

It is the soul's capacity to endure without fleeing and to wait without demanding: "neither to start nor cower" (003-023). Patience is active [[calm|stillness]] — the soul holds its [[earth|ground]] under pressure, [[trust|trusting]] that [[god|God]]'s refining work requires time.

It is both a gift received ("make me all patience," 004-013) and a discipline practiced. In its fullest expression, patience participates in [[god|God]]'s own long-[[pain|suffering]] toward [[human|humanity]].

## Key Passages

> "Make me all patience and all diligence; / Patience, that I may never faint or cower." (004-013)

Patience and diligence are paired — the one inward, the other outward. True patience is the steady companion of earnest effort.

> "I shall endure / With perfect patience — willing beyond my ken / Beyond my power." (012-002)

Patience reaches beyond the soul's natural capacity. [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] describes a patience aligned with [[god|God]]'s will so completely that it becomes supernatural.

> "Be in us patience — neither to start nor cower." (003-023)

This is the essence of [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s patience: the middle way between impulsive flight and fearful collapse. Patience is poised, centered, unmoved.

> "And now, with patience forceful, hard, lie still, / Submitted to the process." (005-021)

[[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] uses the striking phrase "patience forceful, hard." Patience is not soft acceptance but a muscular, willed [[calm|stillness]] under [[god|God]]'s refining hand.

## Related Concepts

Patience is the inward discipline that enables [[trust]] and sustains [[faith]]. It is the soul's posture under [[pain]] and [[suffering]], the [[calm|stillness]] that allows [[grace]] to do its refining work. Patience is the opposite of [[wrath]] and the companion of [[peace]].
