Silence in [[george-macdonald|George MacDonald]]'s "A Book of [[strife|Strife]] in the Form of The Diary of an Old [[soul|Soul]]" is not absence but presence — the "[[god|God]]like silence of a loving care" (002-022) into which all [[earth|earthly]] noise eventually subsides. The speaker's noisy "strife and blare" are not opposed to this silence but drawn into it, as the [[peace|rest]]less surface of a [[soul]] yields to the depth where [[God]] speaks without sound. Silence is the native element of the spirit, the condition in which the soul hears what the clamour of self-concern obscures.

## The Godlike Silence

The speaker contrasts the noise of human life — "strife and blare" — with the divine silence that receives it. This silence is not indifferent; it is the silence of "a loving care" (002-022), the kind of attention that does not need to speak to be present. The movement from noise to silence is the movement from self-assertion to [[trust]]. What the soul most needs is not an answer but a presence, and the silence of [[god|God]] is that presence at its pu[[peace|rest]].

> Into the Godlike silence of a loving care. (002-022)

## The Plunge Out of View

The soul must sometimes "plunge into a silence out of view" (007-013), disappearing from its own [[vision|sight]] and the [[vision|sight]] of others. This is the silence of relinquishment — the willingness to cease being the centre of one's own story. The speaker's imagery of plunging suggests a decisive act, a letting-go that is not passive but chosen. In the plunge, the soul [[faith|trust]]s that the silence into which it falls is held by the same [[love]] that frames all sound.

> And plunge into a silence out of view — / Whence not a cry, not a complaining word. (007-013)

## The Soul's Nest

Silence is described as "the soul's nest" (007-023), a place of safety and [[peace|rest]] where the soul is held as long as it "has [[power|strength]] to be." The nest image combines vulnerability with protection — a nest is a place of beginnings, of fledging, of being fed. [[god|God]]-silence is not the silence of the [[emptiness|void]] but the silence of the brooding [[Spirit]], the warmth that hatches new [[life]].

> In such God-silence, the soul's nest, so long / As it has strength to be, lies quietly. (007-023)
