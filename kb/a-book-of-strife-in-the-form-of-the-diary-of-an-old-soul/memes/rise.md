## Definition

*Rising* is one of [[george-macdonald|MacDonald]]'s most insistent verbs. It names a triple movement: the soul's moral effort to rise from sin and lethargy, [[christ|Christ]]'s own resurrection as the [[earth|ground]] of all rising, and the cosmic ascent of all creation toward its source. Rising is never purely human effort — it is always a response to a prior divine call ("rise up from the dead, / And [[christ|Christ]] will give thee light") and a participation in [[christ|Christ]]'s own risen life. The [[morning|dawn]] image ("a dim aurora rises in my east") anchors rising in the daily rhythm of hope.

## Key Passages

> "O Love divine, rise up in me and move me — / I follow surely when thou first dost move. / To love the perfect love, is primal, mere / Necessity." (004-009)

Rising begins not with human initiative but with divine love rising within the soul; the human response follows.

> "Destroy my darkness, rise my perfect joy; / Love primal, the live coal of every night, / Flame out, scare the ill thoughts with ravishing light." (007-003)

[[darkness|Darkness]] is destroyed not by negation but by the rising of joy — a positive displacement.

> "Wake, thou that sleepest; rise up from the dead, / And Christ will give thee light." (011-005)

[[george-macdonald|MacDonald]] echoes Ephesians 5:14 directly: rising from spiritual death is both command and promise.

> "Then shall I rest within thy holy vast, / One with the bliss of the eternal mind; / And all creation rise in me created new." (012-009)

The individual rising expands into cosmic renewal — all creation rises in the perfected soul.

> "A dim aurora rises in my east, / Beyond the line of jagged questions hoar." (001-002)

The [[morning|dawn]] image recurs as the poem's opening metaphor of hope: light rises even before the questions are answered.

## Related Concepts

Rising is the counter-motion to [[sin]]'s downward pull and [[death]]'s finality. It is closely tied to [[wake]] (the [[wake|awakening]] that precedes rising), [[life]] (the goal of rising), and [[light]] (the [[morning|dawn]] that accompanies it). The call to rise echoes through [[obedience]] and is empowered by [[love]]. False rising — self-effort without grace — collapses into the [[weary|weariness]] described in [[weary]].
