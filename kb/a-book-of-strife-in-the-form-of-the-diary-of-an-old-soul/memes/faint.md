**Faint** in MacDonald's poetry describes the spiritual weariness that overtakes the [[soul]] on its journey toward God. It is not sin but the natural exhaustion of finite beings striving toward the infinite.

## The Experience of Faintness

The speaker repeatedly confesses to faintness — the draining of spiritual energy that leaves the [[soul]] unable to continue in its own [[strength]]:

> I faint, I fail, I cannot seek thy face. (012-010)

## Faintness and Dependence

Faintness serves a spiritual purpose: it drives the [[soul]] to depend entirely on [[god|God]]. When human [[might]] and [[strength]] fail, divine [[power]] is perfected. Faintness is thus the threshold of [[grace]].

## Faintness and the Way

On the long [[way]] toward God, faintness is inevitable. It is not a sign of failure but a marker of authentic spiritual striving. The [[soul]] that never grows faint has never truly exerted itself toward the divine.
