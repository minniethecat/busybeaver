## Overview

Derby is a [[urban|manufacturing town]] in the East Midlands of England, historically a centre of silk-throwing (mechanised silk spinning) and framework-knitting. By the 1840s, it was one of the principal industrial centres outside [[lancashire|Lancashire]], grouped by Engels with [[leicester|Leicester]] and [[nottingham|Nottingham]] as towns where [[housing|working-class housing]] was uniformly substandard.

## Engels's Account

### Housing Conditions

Engels places Derby among the [[urban|factory town]]s with the worst housing:

> "In Leicester, Derby, and Sheffield, it is no better." [002-024]

This follows his account of [[nottingham|Nottingham]]'s 7,000–8,000 back-to-back houses without through ventilation, implying similar building practices in Derby. The town's older industrial quarters inherited the narrow courts and alleyways characteristic of the pre-regulation building era.

### Silk and Hosiery Industries

Derby's principal industries were silk-throwing — a [[factory-system|factory]]-based process using water and later [[steam-engine|steam power]] — and the domestic framework-knitting trade. Engels draws on the [[childrens-employment-commission-report|Children's Employment Commission]]:

> "The framework-knitters of Nottingham, Derby, and Leicester. Touching these workers, the report of the Children's Employment Commission asserts that their condition is in the highest degree deplorable." [007-002]

The Commission found that framework-knitters' children began working at very young ages, often in cramped domestic workshops where moral and physical conditions were equally degrading.

### Silk Mills

Derby's silk mills, among the earliest factories in England (Lombe's Mill on the River Derwent dated from the early eighteenth century), employed large numbers of women and children. While Engels does not describe the Derby silk mills in detail, his general analysis of [[factory-system|factory]] conditions — long hours, low wages, physical deterioration — applied to silk-throwing as to cotton-spinning.
