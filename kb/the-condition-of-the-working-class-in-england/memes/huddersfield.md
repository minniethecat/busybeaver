## Overview

Huddersfield is a [[urban|manufacturing town]] in the West Riding of [[yorkshire|Yorkshire]], situated on the River Colne between [[leeds|Leeds]] and [[manchester|Manchester]]. During the 1840s, it was one of the principal centres of the woollen textile industry, specialising in the production of hard-spun yarn and woollen cloth from short wool. Its position in the Colne valley placed it within the dense industrial belt stretching from [[leeds|Leeds]] westward toward the [[lancashire|Lancashire]] border.

## Engels's Account

### Woollen Industry

Engels describes the regional [[division-of-labour|specialisation]] of [[yorkshire|Yorkshire]]'s wool district:

> "The principal centre of this industry is the West Riding of Yorkshire, where, especially at Bradford, long English wool is converted into worsted yarns, etc.; while in the other cities, Leeds, Halifax, Huddersfield, etc., short wool is converted into hard-spun yarn and cloth." [P04-011]

Huddersfield thus occupied a distinct niche in the wool supply chain — processing short-staple wool into hard-twisted yarn and finished cloth, in contrast to [[bradford|Bradford]]'s focus on long-wool worsted production.

### Factory Concentration

Huddersfield had a significant concentration of woollen mills:

> "more than 60 in the West Riding of Yorkshire, Leeds, and its environs, 25 in Huddersfield" [P04-014]

With 25 mills, Huddersfield was a substantial centre of woollen [[manufacture|manufacturing]], ranking among the larger concentrations in the West Riding.

### Child Labour

The town also appears in the [[childrens-employment-commission-report|Children's Employment Commission]] evidence that Engels reproduces, documenting child labour in the textile districts. A specific case from the Huddersfield area involves a manufacturer at:

> "Meltham Mills, near Huddersfield, in Yorkshire, which refers to a manufacturer there" [006-057]

This reference connects Huddersfield to the broader pattern of [[child-labour|child employment]] in [[yorkshire|Yorkshire]] textile mills that the Commission documented.
