# Nassau William Senior

## Life

Nassau William Senior (1790–1864) was a British economist who held the first Drummond Professorship of Political Economy at Oxford University, a chair established in 1825. He was a leading figure in the classical school of political economy alongside [[thomas-malthus|Thomas Malthus]], [[david-ricardo|David Ricardo]], and [[adam-smith|Adam Smith]]. Senior served on several government commissions, including the [[poor-law-commission|Poor Law Commission]] of 1832, which drafted the [[poor-law|Poor Law]] Amendment Act of 1834. His economic writings emphasised abstinence as the source of capital formation and defended the existing distribution of property.

In 1837, responding to the growing agitation for [[factory-system|factory]] reform, Senior published *[[letters-on-the-factory-act|Letters on the Factory Act to the Rt. Hon. the President of the Board of Trade]] (Chas. Poulett Thomson, Esq.)*, a pamphlet arguing that the restriction of [[working-day|working hours]] would destroy manufacturers' profits and harm workers themselves. Engels lists this work in his bibliography. (012-002)

## Role in Engels's Account

Engels does not engage extensively with Senior's arguments in the body of the text, but Senior's presence in the apparatus of the book is significant. Engels lists Senior alongside other authorities — [[archibald-alison|Archibald Alison]], [[james-kay-shuttleworth|Dr. Kay]], [[peter-gaskell|P. Gaskell]], and the various parliamentary commission reports — that constitute the evidential foundation of his indictment. (012-002, 013-002)

Senior's *[[letters-on-the-factory-act|Letters on the Factory Act]]* represented the [[manufacture|manufacturing]] interest's principal intellectual defence against legislative interference with the working day. Senior famously advanced the "last hour" argument — the claim that manufacturers' entire profit was earned in the final hour of the working day, so that any reduction of hours would eliminate profit and destroy industry. This argument, widely circulated in parliamentary debates over the [[factory-act|Factory Acts]], exemplified the kind of bourgeois political economy that Engels saw as systematically hostile to working-class interests.

Engels's treatment of Senior is implicitly critical, placing him in the bibliography among the class of "bourgeois witnesses" whose data Engels uses while rejecting their conclusions. The citation "Nassau W. Senior. [[letters-on-the-factory-act|Letters on the Factory Act]]... 1837, p. 24" appears at PN=013-002, in a note appended to Engels's discussion of [[crowding|court dwellings]] in [[manchester|Manchester]]. (013-002)

## Context

Senior's economic arguments were deployed against the [[factory-act|Ten Hours' Bill]], the same legislative effort that figures such as [[lord-ashley|Lord Ashley]], [[richard-oastler|Richard Oastler]], and [[michael-sadler|Michael Sadler]] championed in Parliament. The contest between Senior's political economy and the [[factory-system|factory]] reformers' humanitarian case constituted one of the central political battles of the 1830s and 1840s. Engels's book, appearing in 1845, entered this debate on the side of the reformers, treating the economists' arguments as rationalisations of class interest.

Senior's prominence in the classical school makes his inclusion in Engels's bibliography a deliberate gesture — Engels was signalling his engagement with, and rejection of, the most authoritative economic doctrine of his day. The same gesture appears in Engels's citations of [[adam-smith|Adam Smith]] and [[thomas-malthus|Malthus]], whose theoretical frameworks Engels both employs and subverts.

## Legacy

Senior's "last hour" argument was empirically refuted by the successful operation of the [[factory-act|Factory Act of 1847]] (the [[ten-hours-bill|Ten Hours Act]]), which demonstrated that manufacturers could remain profitable with a shortened working day. His broader reputation as a political economist survived, however, and he remains a recognised figure in the history of classical economics. Engels's brief but pointed inclusion of Senior in his source apparatus ensured that Senior's role as a defender of unfettered [[capital|industrial capital]]ism would be remembered in the socialist tradition.
