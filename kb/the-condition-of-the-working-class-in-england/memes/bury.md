## Overview

Bury is a [[urban|manufacturing town]] in [[lancashire|Lancashire]], approximately eight miles north of [[manchester|Manchester]] on the River Irwell. With a population of about 25,000 in the 1840s, it was one of the smaller but significant cotton-spinning centres in the [[manchester|Manchester]] district. Bury appears in Engels's account notably as the site of a disastrous boiler explosion at the mill of Bently & White, a case study in the industrial hazards faced by [[factory-system|factory]] workers.

## Engels's Account

### The Bently & White Boiler Explosion

The most detailed coverage Engels gives Bury concerns a fatal industrial accident:

> "the boiler of Bently & White, at Bury, in Lancashire, exploded and produced considerable damage to the mill and serious or fatal injuries to the operatives" [008-015]

This explosion exemplifies the routine industrial dangers Engels catalogues — boiler explosions were a recurring hazard in [[steam-engine|steam-power]]ed factories, where pressure vessels were poorly maintained and safety valves often tampered with to increase power. The incident at Bury was part of a broader pattern: Engels documents multiple boiler explosions across [[lancashire|Lancashire]] in his section on "Single Branches of Industry," using them to illustrate the systematic disregard for worker safety.

### Industrial Character

Bury belonged to the first ring of satellite towns around [[manchester|Manchester]]:

> "Hence Bolton, Preston, Wigan, Bury, Rochdale, Middleton, Heywood, Oldham, Ashton, Stalybridge, Stockport, etc., with their innumerable 'respectable' retail dealers" [002-031]

Like its neighbours, Bury's economy was dominated by cotton-spinning, with mills clustered along the Irwell and its tributary streams.

### Crime Statistics

During the period of industrial conflict and distress, Bury's crime reports included:

> "in Bury, one theft" [005-043]

Engels compiles such crime statistics from multiple towns to demonstrate the connection between economic deprivation and [[crime|criminality]] — not as moral failing, but as a structural consequence of the industrial system.
