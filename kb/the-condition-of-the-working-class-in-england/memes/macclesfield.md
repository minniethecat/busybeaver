## Overview

Macclesfield is a [[urban|manufacturing town]] in Cheshire, situated on the River Bollin at the edge of the Peak District. During the 1840s, it was the primary centre of the English silk-throwing industry — the mechanised spinning of raw silk into thread for weaving. Engels mentions it briefly among the industrial centres outside the cotton and woollen districts, but the conditions of Macclesfield's silk workers — especially the extensive employment of children — are captured in the broader documentary record he draws upon.

## Engels's Account

### Silk Industry

Engels identifies Macclesfield as the chief silk-throwing centre:

> "the silk industry, whose chief centres are Macclesfield and Congleton" [P04-010]

Silk-throwing was one of the earliest [[factory-system|factory]]-based industries in England, predating cotton-spinning. The Lombe brothers' silk mill in [[derby|Derby]] (1719) was the prototype, and Macclesfield's mills replicated this model. By the 1840s, Macclesfield housed numerous multi-storey silk mills powered by both water and steam.

### Child Labour

While Engels does not describe Macclesfield's conditions in separate detail, his extensive citations from the [[childrens-employment-commission-report|Children's Employment Commission]] applied to the silk district as to others. Silk mills were notorious for employing children as young as seven or eight as "piecers" and "doffers," working twelve to fourteen hours in hot, humid conditions necessary for the silk fibres. The Commission found that silk workers' children suffered the same physical deterioration — stunted growth, spinal deformities, respiratory disease — documented in cotton and woollen mills.

### Commercial Organisation

Macclesfield's silk trade was organised through a [[cottage-system|putting-out system]] for weaving and a [[factory-system|factory]] system for throwing, creating a mixed economy of domestic workshops and large mills. The town's dependence on a single industry made it acutely vulnerable to trade fluctuations, as documented in Engels's accounts of crisis conditions throughout the industrial districts.
