## Overview

Yorkshire is England's largest historic county, with the West Riding serving as the primary centre of the woollen textile industry during the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]]. Alongside [[lancashire|Lancashire]], Yorkshire formed the industrial heartland that Engels studied in *The Condition of the Working Class in England*. Its [[urban|factory town]]s — [[leeds|Leeds]], [[bradford|Bradford]], [[halifax|Halifax]], [[huddersfield|Huddersfield]] — developed around the processing of wool and worsted yarns. The county also contained significant [[coal|coal mining]] districts.

## Engels's Account

### The Woollen Industry

Engels documents the explosive growth of wool [[manufacture|manufacturing]] in Yorkshire's West Riding, paralleling the development he traces in [[lancashire|Lancashire]]'s cotton industry:

> "In 1738 there were 75,000 pieces of woollen cloth produced in the West Riding of Yorkshire; in 1817 there were 490,000 pieces, and so rapid was the extension of the industry that in 1834, 450,000 more pieces were produced than in 1825." [P04-011]

He describes the regional [[division-of-labour|specialisation]] within the county:

> "The principal centre of this industry is the West Riding of Yorkshire, where, especially at Bradford, long English wool is converted into worsted yarns, etc.; while in the other cities, Leeds, Halifax, Huddersfield, etc., short wool is converted into hard-spun yarn and cloth." [P04-011]

### Industrial Geography

Engels describes the West Riding as part of a continuous industrial belt with South [[lancashire|Lancashire]]:

> "This thickly peopled stretch of West Yorkshire and South Lancashire, with its numerous factory towns" [002-028]

Despite its industrial character, Engels acknowledges that certain Yorkshire [[urban|factory town]]s had a more pleasant setting than [[manchester|Manchester]]:

> "all the factory towns of Yorkshire and Lancashire, by reason of its charming situation" [002-029]

This passage contrasts the geographic advantages of some Yorkshire towns with the severely polluted environment of [[manchester|Manchester]]'s central districts.

### Working-Class Conditions

The Yorkshire [[proletariat|working class]] shared many afflictions with [[lancashire|Lancashire]]. Engels notes that medical evidence from Yorkshire was equally damning:

> "though this district rivals Yorkshire in the number of cripples" [006-019]

On mining accidents, Engels observes:

> "they are so frequent that in Yorkshire and Lancashire, as in Northumberland and Durham, hardly a week passes without an accident" [009-007]

The [[childrens-employment-commission-report|Children's Employment Commission]]'s reports from the Cheshire, [[lancashire|Lancashire]], and Yorkshire districts — "including a number of Yorkshire towns" [005-013] — provided Engels with extensive evidence of child labour abuses.
