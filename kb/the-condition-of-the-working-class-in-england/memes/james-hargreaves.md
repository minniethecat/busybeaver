## Overview

James Hargreaves (c. 1720–1778) was an English weaver from Standhill, near Blackburn, in North Lancashire, who invented the **spinning jenny** in 1764 (P04-006). Engels identifies this invention as the starting point of the Industrial Revolution's transformation of the English working class.

## The Spinning Jenny (1764)

The spinning jenny was the first mechanical invention to radically alter the conditions of English textile workers. Engels describes it as follows:

> The first invention which gave rise to a radical change in the state of the English workers was the jenny, invented in the year 1764 by a weaver, James Hargreaves, of Standhill, near Blackburn, in North Lancashire.
>
> (P04-006)

The machine was hand-operated and carried sixteen or eighteen spindles, all managed by a single worker — a dramatic improvement over the single-spindle spinning wheel. This increase in productivity led to a surplus of yarn, which in turn lowered the cost of woven goods and raised demand for weavers (P04-006).

## Social Consequences

Engels traces profound social changes directly to Hargreaves' invention. The abundance of cheap yarn increased demand for woven goods, causing weavers' wages to rise sharply. A family of four adults and two children could earn four pounds sterling per week with eight hours of daily work (P04-006).

This prosperity accelerated the disappearance of the farming-weaver class. Weavers abandoned agriculture to work full-time at the loom, becoming wage-dependent proletarians. Engels notes:

> By degrees the class of farming weavers wholly disappeared, and was merged in the newly arising class of weavers who lived wholly upon wages, had no property whatever, not even the pretended property of a holding, and so became workingmen, proletarians.
>
> (P04-006)

The jenny also disrupted the traditional household economy, as spinning moved from women using single-spindle wheels to men operating the multi-spindle jenny, which required greater physical strength.

## Legacy

The spinning jenny was the rough prototype for Samuel Crompton's later spinning mule (1785), which combined features of the jenny with Richard Arkwright's water-powered throstle (P04-008). Hargreaves' invention thus inaugurated the cascade of mechanical innovations that established the factory system in English cotton spinning.
