## Overview

Halifax is a [[urban|manufacturing town]] in the West Riding of [[yorkshire|Yorkshire]], situated on the River Hebble in the Calder valley approximately seven miles southwest of [[bradford|Bradford]]. During the 1840s, it was a major centre of the woollen and worsted textile industry. Its location on the direct transport route between [[manchester|Manchester]] and [[leeds|Leeds]] — via the [[rochdale|Rochdale]] [[canal|Canal]] and later the [[lancashire|Lancashire]] and [[yorkshire|Yorkshire]] [[railway|Railway]] — made it a key node in the regional industrial network.

## Engels's Account

### Woollen Industry

Engels places Halifax among the principal woollen [[manufacture|manufacturing]] centres of the West Riding:

> "The principal centre of this industry is the West Riding of Yorkshire, where, especially at Bradford, long English wool is converted into worsted yarns, etc.; while in the other cities, Leeds, Halifax, Huddersfield, etc., short wool is converted into hard-spun yarn and cloth." [P04-011]

Halifax specialised, like [[huddersfield|Huddersfield]] and [[leeds|Leeds]], in the processing of short-staple wool into hard-spun yarn and finished cloth — the heavier, more durable woollen fabrics distinct from the lighter worsted products of [[bradford|Bradford]].

### Transport Hub

Halifax sat on the cross-Pennine transport corridor:

> "via Manchester and Halifax, and via Leicester, Derby, and Sheffield" [P04-020]

This passage indicates Halifax's position on one of the key trunk routes linking [[manchester|Manchester]] (and [[liverpool|Liverpool]], the Atlantic port) to the industrial towns of [[yorkshire|Yorkshire]] and beyond to the East Midlands. The town's location facilitated both the inward shipment of raw wool and coal and the outward distribution of finished textiles.

### Industrial Character

Halifax was a classic West Riding textile town — dominated by mills, dye-works, and the crowded housing of the operatives who worked them. While Engels does not provide a separate physical description of Halifax's streets, his general characterisation of [[yorkshire|Yorkshire]] [[urban|factory town]]s as sharing the same patterns of [[crowding|overcrowding]], inadequate [[sanitary|sanitation]], and high mortality as their [[lancashire|Lancashire]] counterparts applies to Halifax as much as to [[leeds|Leeds]] or [[bradford|Bradford]].
