## Overview

Rochdale is a [[urban|manufacturing town]] in [[lancashire|Lancashire]], situated in the valley of the River Roch approximately ten miles north of [[manchester|Manchester]]. With a population of about 75,000 in the 1840s [P04-010], it was one of the larger satellite towns of the [[manchester|Manchester]] cotton district. In addition to cotton-spinning, Rochdale was a centre of flannel production, lying in the transitional zone between the cotton districts of [[lancashire|Lancashire]] and the woollen districts of the West Riding of [[yorkshire|Yorkshire]] [P04-011].

## Engels's Account

Engels lists Rochdale among the [[urban|factory town]]s of the [[manchester|Manchester]] orbit:

> "Hence Bolton, Preston, Wigan, Bury, Rochdale, Middleton, Heywood, Oldham, Ashton, Stalybridge, Stockport, etc., with their innumerable 'respectable' retail dealers" [002-031]

The quotation marks around "respectable" are characteristically ironic — Engels implies that these dealers profited from the poverty of the [[proletariat|working class]].

### Industrial Character

The town's position between cotton and woollen districts gave it a mixed industrial character:

> "Then come the adjacent part of Lancashire, the region of Rochdale, where in addition to the cotton industry much flannel is produced" [P04-011]

### Working-Class Diet

During trade depressions, Rochdale's operatives suffered extreme dietary deprivation. The [[childrens-employment-commission-report|Children's Employment Commission]] documented:

> "hundreds of operatives in Ashton-under-Lyne, Oldham, and Rochdale had nothing to eat but potatoes, and those of the worst description" [006-058]

### Labour Unrest

Rochdale was affected by the power-loom riots and machine-breaking episodes that swept [[lancashire|Lancashire]]:

> "the power-loom weavers of Rochdale, who had been thrown out of employment by the introduction of these machines, destroyed a mill at Middleton" [008-015]

This passage documents the Luddite-like destruction of machinery by [[hand-loom-weaver|hand-loom weavers]] whose livelihoods were eliminated by [[machinery|mechanisation]] — a key dynamic in Engels's analysis of technological unemployment.
