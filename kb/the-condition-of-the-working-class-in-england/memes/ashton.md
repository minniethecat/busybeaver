## Overview

Ashton-under-Lyne is a [[urban|manufacturing town]] in [[lancashire|Lancashire]], approximately six miles northeast of [[stockport|Stockport]] and part of the wider [[manchester|Manchester]] industrial district. With a population of about 40,000 in the 1840s (including the adjacent town of [[stalybridge|Stalybridge]]) [P04-010], it was one of the newer [[urban|factory town]]s of the region. Engels describes it as a relatively recent development among the [[cotton-industry|Lancashire cotton]] towns, and it features in his accounts of both working-class living conditions and the labour unrest of 1842.

## Engels's Account

### Urban Geography

Engels notes that Ashton is situated on the River Tame and describes its physical setting:

> "A few miles northeast of Stockport is Ashton-under-Lyne, one of the newest factory towns in this region. It lies on the slope of a hill, between the canal and the River Tame, and is, on the whole, built on a more regular plan, so that it makes a somewhat more pleasant impression than most of the other factory towns." [002-034]

This relatively favourable impression was reinforced by the view approaching the town:

> "In coming over the hill from Ashton, the traveller has, at the top, a splendid view over the valley of the Tame, with Manchester in the background." [002-035]

### Labour Movement

Ashton was the site of significant industrial conflict. Engels records that the manufacturer Thomas Ashton of [[hyde|Hyde]] played a role in the labour movement:

> "violent labour movement, young Ashton, a manufacturer in Hyde, near Ashton-under-Lyne" [008-015]

During the general strike of August 1842, Ashton was a focal point of workers' mobilisation:

> "August 8th they proceeded to Ashton and Hyde five thousand strong" [008-029]

The crowd of 5,000 workers marching from surrounding districts to Ashton underscores its importance as a centre of working-class collective action.

### Working Conditions

Ashton's operatives shared the hardships common to all [[lancashire|Lancashire]] mill towns. The [[childrens-employment-commission-report|Children's Employment Commission]] found severe dietary deprivation among [[factory-system|factory]] workers:

> "hundreds of operatives in Ashton-under-Lyne, Oldham, and Rochdale had nothing to eat but potatoes, and those of the worst description" [006-058]

### Crime and Social Disorder

[[crime|Crime]] statistics for Ashton during the industrial conflicts of 1842–1843 include:

> "in Ashton, two thefts, one burglary, one robbery" [005-043]

The Sunday trading and [[drunkenness|drink]]ing documented through [[manchester|Manchester]] Guardian court reports (Ashton Sessions, June 26, 1844) record fourteen dealers fined for violations [002-064].
