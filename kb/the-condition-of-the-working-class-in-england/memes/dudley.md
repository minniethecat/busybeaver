## Overview

Dudley is an industrial town in the Black Country, historically straddling the Staffordshire–Worcestershire border. During the 1840s, it was a major centre of ironworking, [[coal|coal mining]], and nail-making. Engels cites it as having higher mortality than [[birmingham|Birmingham]] despite being "only a few miles distant," reflecting the severe occupational and environmental hazards of the Black Country's metal trades.

## Engels's Account

### Mortality

Engels uses Dudley as a mortality comparison:

> "Birmingham shows even during the reign of an epidemic a far smaller mortality than, for instance, Wolverhampton, Dudley, and Bilston, only a few miles distant." [002-024]

The proximity of Dudley to [[birmingham|Birmingham]] — "only a few miles distant" — makes the mortality differential particularly striking as evidence that industrial conditions, not geography or climate, determined [[mortality|death rate]]s.

### Black Country Iron District

Dudley belongs to the group of towns Engels surveys in the Staffordshire iron district:

> "In Wolverhampton, Willenhall, Bilston, Sedgeley, Wednesfield, Darlaston, Dudley, Walsall, Wednesbury, etc., there are, therefore, fewer factories, but chiefly single forges, where the small masters work alone, or with one or more apprentices, who serve them until reaching the twenty-first year." [007-010]

The conditions Engels documents for the district — apprentices fed tainted meat, crowded into filthy hovels, developing physical deformities from heavy forge work — applied to Dudley's nail-makers, chain-makers, and ironworkers as much as to any other Black Country town.

### Nail-Making

The nail trade was particularly concentrated in the Dudley area. Nailers' wages, as Engels records for the wider district, were among the most miserable in England: 5¾ pence for 1,200 nails, each requiring 12 hammer blows [007-010]. Dudley's nail-makers shared the occupational diseases — ruptures, spinal deformities, "hind-leg" — that the [[childrens-employment-commission-report|Children's Employment Commission]] documented throughout the Black Country.
