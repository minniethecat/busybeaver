## Overview

The power-loom riots were a series of attacks on textile machinery in [[lancashire|Lancashire]] and neighbouring districts during the early decades of the nineteenth century. Hand-loom weavers, whose livelihoods were destroyed by the introduction of power-driven looms, smashed the [[machinery|machines]] they blamed for their immiseration. Engels treats these early machine-breaking disturbances as a primitive form of working-class resistance — a stage workers passed through before developing more sophisticated forms of trade union and political organisation.

## Engels's Account

### Machine-Breaking as Primitive Resistance

Engels places the power-loom riots in the context of the historical development of working-class consciousness. Unlike later strikes and political movements that targeted the capitalist system itself, the early machine-breakers directed their violence at the physical instruments of their displacement:

> "In the beginning, the workers opposed the machines themselves, not yet understanding that it was not the machine but the social use made of it that was their enemy." [008-015]

This passage reflects Engels's [[karl-marx|Marx]]ist analysis: the workers' initial response was misdirected at the technology rather than at the relations of production that determined how technology was deployed. The transition from machine-breaking to trade unionism and [[chartism|Chartism]] represented, in Engels's view, a progression toward political maturity.

### The 1826 Disturbances

The most extensive wave of power-loom attacks occurred in 1826, when [[hand-loom-weaver|hand-loom weavers]] across [[lancashire|Lancashire]] rose against the mills. Power-looms were smashed in [[blackburn|Blackburn]], Accrington, Burnley, and other cotton towns. The authorities responded with military force, and dozens of weavers were transported or executed.

### Historical Significance

Engels treats the power-loom riots as more than historical background — they were the first evidence that industrialisation would not proceed without resistance from those it dispossessed. The riots prefigured the more organised and politically conscious movements of the 1830s and 1840s, including [[chartism|Chartism]] and the [[labour-movement|trade union movement]].
