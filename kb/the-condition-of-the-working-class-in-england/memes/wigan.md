## Overview

Wigan is an industrial town in [[lancashire|Lancashire]], situated approximately halfway between [[manchester|Manchester]] and [[liverpool|Liverpool]] on the River Douglas. During the 1840s, it had a mixed industrial economy combining cotton-spinning with extensive [[coal|coal mining]]. The town lay on important transport routes — the [[leeds|Leeds]] and [[liverpool|Liverpool]] [[canal|Canal]] and the railway connecting [[manchester|Manchester]] to the west coast — which facilitated its integration into the regional industrial economy.

## Engels's Account

### Transport Connections

Wigan appears in Engels's account primarily as a node in the regional transport network that bound the [[lancashire|Lancashire]] industrial district together:

> "via Wigan, and via Manchester and Bolton; also with Leeds (via Manchester and Halifax, and via Leicester, Derby, and Sheffield)" [P04-020]

This brief reference establishes Wigan's position on the canal and railway routes connecting [[manchester|Manchester]] to [[liverpool|Liverpool]] and the wider industrial network. The town's location on these arteries made it a natural site for both textile [[manufacture|manufacturing]] and coal distribution.

### Commercial Character

Engels lists Wigan among the [[manchester|Manchester]] satellite towns with their commercial infrastructure:

> "Hence Bolton, Preston, Wigan, Bury, Rochdale, Middleton, Heywood, Oldham, Ashton, Stalybridge, Stockport, etc., with their innumerable 'respectable' retail dealers" [002-031]

The quotation marks around "respectable" convey Engels's characteristic irony — these retailers may have maintained external appearances of respectability while profiting from the desperate consumption needs of impoverished workers.

### Coal Mining

While Engels does not describe Wigan's coal-mining industry in specific detail, the town was one of the most important centres of the [[lancashire|Lancashire]] coalfield. The mining accidents, strikes, and union organisation that Engels documents in [[lancashire|Lancashire]] mining districts applied equally to the Wigan area, which was surrounded by [[coal|collieries]]. His accounts of mining conditions — frequent accidents, the use of women and children underground, and the formation of miners' unions — describe the industrial environment in which Wigan's [[proletariat|working class]] lived.
