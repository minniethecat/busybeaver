# The Flat in Down Street

## Overview

Down Street is a short street in Mayfair, London, running between Piccadilly and Hertford Street. In *The Powerhouse*, it is the location of [[sir-edward-leithen]]'s flat, which serves as his home, his refuge, and ultimately the site of a dramatic siege.

## In *The Powerhouse*

Leithen's flat in Down Street is introduced early in the novel as his residence: "I lived at the time in Down Street" [001-014]. The location places him in the heart of fashionable London, a short walk from both the Park and the Temple. It is from his Down Street doorstep that Leithen first becomes aware of Lumley's surveillance network, noticing watchers at the Piccadilly end and at the Hertford Street opening [003-042].

The flat becomes central to the novel's climax in Chapter IX. When Leithen realises he is being hunted, he retreats to the flat and summons [[chapman]] for reinforcement. The telephone line is cut, his valet Waters disappears, and Leithen declares that "the state of siege had begun" [009-100]. The flat transforms from a gentleman's residence into a fortress, as Leithen and Chapman barricade themselves against Lumley's agents. The ensuing confrontation — with Lumley's men breaking in and a violent struggle in the rooms — makes the Down Street flat the arena for the novel's decisive action.
