# Saronov

Saronov is a Russian operative within [[andrew-lumley|Andrew Lumley]]'s Powerhouse network, dispatched alongside [[tuke|Tuke]] to intercept [[charles-pitt-heron|Charles Pitt-Heron]] during his flight through Central Asia. His capture and confession provide crucial evidence linking Lumley directly to the conspiracy's field operations.

## Description

Saronov is never described in physical terms — he exists in the text almost entirely as a name, a telegram, and a confession. This absence of physical description underscores his function in the novel: he is an operational node in Lumley's network rather than a character in his own right. What little we know of him comes from the Russian police reports tracking his movements through Turkestan and from his eventual confession.

## Role in the Story

Saronov first enters the narrative as a name in a cryptic telegram discovered by Leithen at Lumley's White Lodge: "Suivez a Bokhare Saronov" — "Follow Saronov to Bokhara" (002-020). This message is one of Leithen's first concrete clues to the Powerhouse's international reach, revealing that the conspiracy has agents operating across the European-Asian frontier.

Saronov is later identified by the Russian police as one of two men (alongside Tuke) following the trail of [[tommy-deloraine|Tommy Deloraine]] and Pitt-Heron through Turkestan (005-022). The two operatives had travelled by Samarkand, intending to strike into the hills by a shorter route than the main road from Bokhara — a route that suggests local knowledge and careful planning.

## Key Appearances

- **The Bokhara telegram (002-020)**: Leithen discovers the instruction "Suivez a Bokhare Saronov" in a telegram at Lumley's White Lodge, providing his first glimpse of the Powerhouse's operational reach beyond England. The message indicates that Saronov is a known quantity to the conspirators — someone whose movements and instructions are communicated by name.
- **Central Asian pursuit (005-022)**: The Russian police report that two men — Tuke and Saronov — are following the trail of Deloraine and Pitt-Heron through Turkestan. They had gone by Samarkand, intending to cut off the fugitives by a shorter mountain route.
- **Capture and confession (008-030)**: Saronov is felled by [[tommy-deloraine|Tommy Deloraine]] during the pursuit and taken into custody. His confession provides crucial evidence of the Powerhouse's operations, directly implicating Lumley. Leithen refers to him as "your creature, Saronov" when confronting Lumley at the novel's climax (008-030), exposing Lumley's direct personal control over the network's field agents.

## Significance

Saronov represents the operational arm of the Powerhouse — a willing agent who pursues targets across continents. Unlike the philosophical conspirators of London drawing rooms (Lumley, Pavia, and their circle), Saronov is a man of action who operates in the remote borderlands where civilised law does not reach. His presence in the narrative demonstrates that the Powerhouse is not merely a discussion club for subversive ideas but a functioning organization with field agents, secure communications, and the capacity for transcontinental pursuit.

His confession is a pivotal plot point: it transforms the case against Lumley from circumstantial to direct, providing the link between the London mastermind and the Central Asian operation that Leithen needs to make his final confrontation stick.

## Notable Quotes

Lumley's reaction when Leithen names Saronov is telling: "A mistake, James," he said. "You can go" (008-030). The dismissal of his butler James signals that Lumley recognizes the gravity of Leithen having Saronov's testimony — and needs privacy for what follows.
