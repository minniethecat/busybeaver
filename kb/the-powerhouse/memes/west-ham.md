# West Ham

## Overview

West Ham is a district in East London, located north of the River Thames in what was historically part of Essex. In *The Powerhouse*, it appears not as a setting for action but as the subject of a distinctly unglamorous legal brief on [[edward-leithen]]'s desk -- a symbol of the mundane professional life that the novel's adventure disrupts.

## In the Novel

West Ham features in two parallel scenes that bookend the narrative, both set in Leithen's chambers at [[fountain-court]] in the [[the-temple|Temple]].

In the opening chapter (Ch. I, PN 1), [[tommy-deloraine]] visits Leithen on a hot May morning to announce his sudden departure in pursuit of the vanished [[charles-pitt-heron]]. Tommy, in wild spirits at the prospect of travel, picks up a brief from Leithen's crowded desk:

> "It was about a new drainage scheme in West Ham. He tossed it down and looked at me pityingly. 'Poor old beggar!' he said. 'To spend your days on such work when the world is chockful of amusing things. Life goes roaring by and you only hear the echo in your stuffy rooms.'" (Ch. I)

Tommy's contempt for the West Ham drainage brief establishes the contrast between the two friends: Tommy the adventurer, Leithen the sedentary lawyer. It also sets up the central irony of the novel -- that it is Leithen, not Tommy, who will experience the real adventure from his chambers in London.

The brief reappears in the novel's closing chapter (Ch. X, PN 10). After all the drama -- the Powerhouse exposed, Lumley dead, civilization saved -- Leithen is back at his desk:

> "The sun was shining through my Temple chambers, much as it had done when he started. So far as I remember the West Ham brief which had aroused his contempt was still adorning my table."

The unchanged presence of the West Ham brief signals the restoration of normalcy. The great conspiracy has been defeated, and Leithen returns to his quiet work -- the mundane legal business of drains and sewers that, in its own unglamorous way, represents the civilization Lumley sought to destroy.

## Historical Context

The County Borough of West Ham was a rapidly growing industrial and working-class district in the Edwardian period. Formed in 1886, it encompassed Stratford, Canning Town, and Plaistow, and was a centre of manufacturing, docks, and railways. Sanitation and drainage were pressing issues for such densely populated industrial areas -- hence the existence of legal briefs concerning drainage schemes. The mundane subject matter of Leithen's work reflects the reality that much of a junior barrister's practice consisted of unexciting but essential public works cases.
