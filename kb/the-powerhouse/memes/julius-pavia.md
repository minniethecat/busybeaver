# Julius Pavia

Julius Pavia is a retired East India merchant who enters the narrative as a financial associate of [[charles-pitt-heron|Charles Pitt-Heron]] and an "esteemed client" of a London law firm with ties to the Powerhouse conspiracy.

## Role in the Story

Pavia appears only indirectly — his name surfaces during [[edward-leithen|Leithen]]'s investigation into Pitt-Heron's disappearance. A house on the edge of a northern heath is revealed to be "the property of a Mr. Julius Pavia, a retired East India merchant" (002-002). The solicitor who provides this information identifies Pavia as "an esteemed client of ours," noting that both Pavia and Pitt-Heron are "attracted by the same line of business" (002-009). This connection hints at the financial dimension of [[andrew-lumley|Andrew Lumley]]'s Powerhouse network.

## Relationships

- **[[charles-pitt-heron]]** — Financial associate; the two conducted "closely connected financial operations" (002-009)
- **[[andrew-lumley]]** — Likely connected through the Powerhouse financial network

## Significance

Pavia is one of the shadowy figures on the periphery of the Powerhouse conspiracy. He never appears in person, but his name serves as an early clue that Pitt-Heron's disappearance is not merely personal — it is entangled with international financial interests. The retired East India merchant represents the respectable facade behind which the Powerhouse operates.
