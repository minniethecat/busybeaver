# Langham Place

## Overview

Langham Place is a short street in the West End of London, connecting Portland Place to Regent Street. It is best known for All Souls Church at its northern end and Broadcasting House (later the BBC headquarters) at its southern end. In *The Powerhouse*, it serves as a geographical marker locating the shadowy restaurant in Antioch Street.

## In *The Powerhouse*

Langham Place is mentioned in Chapter VI as Leithen walks towards the dinner meeting that will become a deadly trap. He walks "up Regent Street towards Oxford Circus" and notes that "Antioch Street, as I had learned from the Directory, was in the area between Langham Place and Tottenham Court Road" (PN 706). This brief reference places the action in the ambiguous borderland between the fashionable West End and the more modest streets of Bloomsbury -- a fitting location for a restaurant whose respectable exterior conceals a criminal purpose. The proximity to both respectability and danger mirrors the novel's central theme of hidden menace behind civilised facades.

## Historical Context

Langham Place was laid out in the 18th century and named after Sir James Langham. Its most famous landmark, All Souls Church, was designed by John Nash and consecrated in 1824. In the Edwardian period, the area was a mix of professional offices, medical institutions, and residential properties, close to the shopping districts of Regent Street and Oxford Street. Today, Langham Place is dominated by the BBC's Broadcasting House, which opened in 1932.
