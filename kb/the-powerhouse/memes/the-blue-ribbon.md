# The Blue Ribbon

## Overview

The blue ribbon of the Order of the Garter is a visual symbol that appears during the dinner party in Chapter VIII of *The Powerhouse*. Observed by [[sir-edward-leithen]], it represents the established order of British honours and the complacent civilisation that [[andrew-lumley]] has infiltrated.

## In *The Powerhouse*

During the dinner at Lumley's house, Leithen studies the scene with a detective's attention. His eye catches "the blue ribbon bulging out on Lord Morecambe's breast as he leaned forward to question him" [008-050]. The image is striking: a decoration of the highest order — the Order of the Garter, Britain's most prestigious chivalric honour — worn by a peer of the realm at a dinner hosted by the most dangerous man in England.

The blue ribbon is a marker of everything the Powerhouse despises and seeks to undermine: inherited privilege, institutional prestige, the formulas of social hierarchy. That Lord Morecambe wears it while leaning in to listen to Lumley dramatises the blindness of the establishment to the threat in its midst. Leithen sees what nobody else does: behind Lumley's mild spectacles and soft voice lurks a "light wild" intelligence. The ribbon is a symbol of the civilisation that Lumley has learned to mimic, and that his iconoclasm aims to swallow.
