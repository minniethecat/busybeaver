# Chapman

Chapman, nicknamed "Chipmunk" by [[tommy-deloraine|Tommy Deloraine]], is a Labour Member of Parliament who serves as a political ally and source of intelligence on the Powerhouse's infiltration of organised labour. He speaks with a rich Yorkshire accent and shares a Yorkshire constituency with Tommy.

## Role in the Story

Chapman first appears at Tommy Deloraine's farewell dinner party, where Tommy teases him mercilessly but with genuine affection. Tommy declares that Chapman "should have been a lumberman in California" and calls him "a dashed bad politician" (001-009). Despite the jibes, the two men "lived adjacent in Yorkshire, and on platforms abused each other like pickpockets" (001-008), making their public adversarial relationship a running joke.

When [[edward-leithen|Leithen]] needs intelligence on the Powerhouse's reach, he turns to Chapman. The Labour MP provides crucial information about the organisation's penetration of the labour movement. Chapman "adored Tommy Deloraine" (005-011), making him a willing and valuable source.

- **[[ch05|Chapter V]]** — Chapman provides details about the Powerhouse's labour connections through his trade union contacts (005-010–005-011)

## Background

Chapman is a Labour MP from Yorkshire, Tommy Deloraine's neighbouring constituency. Their relationship is one of public political opposition and private friendship — they "abused each other like pickpockets" on platforms (001-008). Chapman speaks with "rich Yorkshire" accent and has deep connections in the trade union movement, which makes him a valuable source of political intelligence.

## Relationships

- **[[tommy-deloraine]]** — Political ally and friend; Tommy calls him "Chipmunk" and the two maintain a public facade of opposition
- **[[edward-leithen]]** — Leithen approaches Chapman for intelligence on the Powerhouse's labour connections
- **[[andrew-lumley]]** — Chapman describes Lumley as "a cold-blooded, sneering devil, a sort of Parnell" (005-012), reflecting the Labour movement's hostility to Lumley's methods

## Significance

Chapman represents the legitimate political world that the Powerhouse seeks to subvert. His intelligence on the organisation's penetration of labour unions provides Leithen with a crucial piece of the puzzle. As a character, he embodies the theme of friendship transcending political divides — his loyalty to Tommy Deloraine matters more than party allegiance.

## Notable Quotes

> "A cold-blooded, sneering devil," as Chapman put it, "a sort of Parnell. He's a mystery man, and nobody knows exactly where he comes from." (005-012)
