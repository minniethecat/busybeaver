# The Temple

The Temple is the historic legal district in central London where [[edward-leithen|Leithen]] keeps his chambers as a practising barrister. Throughout the novel, the Temple serves as Leithen's professional anchor, the place he returns to between investigations and the setting for several key communications.

## Leithen's Chambers

Leithen's professional life centres on the Temple. He describes his routine: "Then I went down to the Temple, fixed some consultations, and spent a quiet evening in my rooms" [004-022]. His daily walk takes him "down to the Temple by way of Pall Mall and the Embankment" [004-050], a route through the heart of official London that later becomes shadowed by Powerhouse surveillance.

## A Place of Work and Refuge

The Temple remains Leithen's base of operations even as the conspiracy unfolds. "At four o'clock I left the Temple and walked to the Embassy" [004-054], he notes during a critical phase of his investigation. Working late becomes a habit: "I was working late at the Temple next day, and it was nearly seven before I got up to go home" [006-002].

The Temple also features in the coordination of the final confrontation. Leithen instructs his clerk that "I would not be at the Temple that day" [007-006] as he prepares for the decisive encounter with the Powerhouse.

## A Nighttime Discovery

Leithen's return from the Temple one evening yields a crucial development: "One night, on my way home from the Temple, I saw in the bills of the evening papers the announcement of the arrest of a Labour Member" [005-022] — [[chapman|Chapman]], who had assaulted a suspected Powerhouse agent in Jermyn Street.
