# Marble Arch Station

## Overview

Marble Arch Station (referred to in the novel simply as "the Marble Arch Station") is a London Underground station at the western end of Oxford Street, adjacent to the Marble Arch monument and the northeast corner of Hyde Park. Opened in 1900 as part of the Central London Railway, it was one of the early Tube stations serving the West End.

## In *The Powerhouse*

Marble Arch Station features in Chapter IV, when Leithen attempts to shake off the men shadowing him through London. He devises a clever stratagem: "I walked up Park Lane, for it seemed to me that by taking the Tube at the Marble Arch Station I could bring matters to the proof" (PN 541). Noticing that a certain carriage in the 9:30 train stops opposite the exit at Chancery Lane Station, he hurries up the passage to catch an earlier lift, reaching the street before other passengers. From behind a pillar-box, he watches his pursuer emerge "breathless" from the stairs, confirming that the espionage is no figment of his imagination (PN 543-544).

## Historical Context

The Marble Arch itself was designed by John Nash in 1827 as the ceremonial entrance to Buckingham Palace, but was relocated to Cumberland Gate in 1851 when the palace was extended. By the time of *The Powerhouse* (set around 1913-1914), the Tube station had been operational for about a decade and was a familiar landmark for West End commuters and shoppers.
