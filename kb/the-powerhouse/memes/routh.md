# Routh

Routh is a solicitor whom [[edward-leithen|Leithen]] consults during his investigation into the Powerhouse. He provides crucial information about the organisation's inner workings, but his own identity is revealed to be duplicitous — he was formerly Tuke, a butler in the [[charles-pitt-heron|Pitt-Heron]] household, and one of the Powerhouse's own operatives.

## Role in the Story

Leithen approaches Routh seeking information about the Powerhouse's financial operations. Routh is described as speaking "with a strong North-country accent" (004-025) and initially presents himself as a cooperative informant. Leithen offers him a cigar and questions him about Pitt-Heron's business affairs; Routh is "very willing to speak" (004-028).

The revelation of Routh's true identity is one of the novel's key twists: "Tuke's real name was Routh, and it was Tuke who had gone" (004-038) — the solicitor and the Pitt-Heron butler are the same person. This discovery reveals how deeply the Powerhouse has infiltrated the Pitt-Heron household.

## Key Appearances

- **[[ch04|Chapter IV]]** — Leithen consults Routh as a solicitor, unaware of his true identity (004-025–004-031)
- **[[ch04|Chapter IV]]** — Leithen realises "the man I knew was called Routh" (004-033), recognizing him from an earlier context
- **[[ch04|Chapter IV]]** — The revelation that Routh was Tuke the butler (004-037–004-038)

## Background

Routh presents himself as a solicitor with knowledge of the Powerhouse's operations. His North-country accent marks his regional origins. The discovery that he was formerly employed as "Tuke, the butler, and one Routh who was a groom or footman" (004-037) in the Pitt-Heron household reveals the layered surveillance the Powerhouse maintained over its associates.

## Relationships

- **[[edward-leithen]]** — Leithen consults him as an informant, then discovers his duplicity
- **[[charles-pitt-heron]]** — Formerly Pitt-Heron's butler under the name Tuke
- **[[the-powerhouse-org]]** — A Powerhouse operative embedded in the Pitt-Heron household

## Significance

Routh illustrates the Powerhouse's methodology: placing operatives in trusted domestic positions to monitor targets. His double identity — solicitor and butler — reveals the organisation's reach across social classes and its ability to operate in both professional and domestic spheres. For Leithen, the Routh revelation is a turning point: it demonstrates that the conspiracy is not abstract but has penetrated every layer of the Pitt-Heron affair.
