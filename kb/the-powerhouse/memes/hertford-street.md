# Hertford Street

## Overview

Hertford Street is a street in Mayfair, London, running between Park Lane and Curzon Street. In the Edwardian era it was a quiet residential address, close to the grand houses of Park Lane and the diplomatic quarter towards Belgrave Square. It was named after the Marquess of Hertford.

## In *The Powerhouse*

Hertford Street appears in Chapter IV during Leithen's growing realization that he is under surveillance. On the morning after his first suspicion of being shadowed, he leaves his flat in Down Street and confirms his fears: "There seemed to be nobody in Down Street as I emerged from my flat, but I had not walked five yards before, turning back, I saw a man enter from the Piccadilly end, while another moved across the Hertford Street opening" (PN 539). This passage marks the exact moment Leithen moves from uneasy suspicion to near-certainty. The "Hertford Street opening" -- where one watcher crosses to cover a potential escape route -- demonstrates the systematic nature of Lumley's surveillance: every junction, every side street, every possible path is covered.

## Historical Context

Hertford Street was developed in the 18th century and had long been associated with prosperous Londoners. It was a side street off Park Lane in one direction and Curzon Street in the other, placing it in the heart of Mayfair. At the time of the novel, it was a typical residential Mayfair street, with tall Georgian townhouses, many converted into flats. Its proximity to Down Street (where Leithen lived) and to Piccadilly made it a natural surveillance point.
