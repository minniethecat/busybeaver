# Government Whip

## Overview

The Government Whip is a parliamentary official responsible for maintaining party discipline in the House of Commons. In the opening chapter of *The Powerhouse*, the Whip appears as a symbol of the ordinary, institutional world that Leithen inhabits — a world about to be shattered by his encounter with the Powerhouse.

## In *The Powerhouse*

The novel opens with Leithen and [[tommy-deloraine]] emerging from the House of Commons after a dull debate. The narrator observes: "The contrast between the frowsy place and the cheerful world outside would have impressed even the soul of a Government Whip" [001-001]. This is the only mention of the Whip in the novel, but it serves an important narrative function: it anchors the story in the tedium of parliamentary routine before propelling both characters into adventure.

The Government Whip represents everything the Powerhouse stands against — the established order, the slow machinery of democratic governance, the "compact" of civilisation that Lumley despises. Tommy Deloraine, a reluctant MP who later succeeds to his family seat in Yorkshire, embodies the instinct to flee this parliamentary world; Leithen, by contrast, is at home in it. The Whip's unspoken presence in the opening paragraph signals that this is a novel about the tension between institutional order and the lawless energy that threatens it.

Later, when [[chapman]] urges a "speech in Parliament" [009-023] to expose Lumley, he invokes the same parliamentary world the Whip represents — but Leithen, having seen the true nature of the Powerhouse, understands that such institutional remedies are inadequate to the threat.
