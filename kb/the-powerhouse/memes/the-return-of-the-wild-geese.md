# Return of the Wild Geese

## Overview

"Return of the Wild Geese" is the title of Chapter IX, the final chapter of *The Powerhouse*. The "Wild Geese" are [[tommy-deloraine]] and the Powerhouse operatives who have been chasing [[charles-pitt-heron]] across Central Asia — and whose pursuit ends with the collapse of the conspiracy.

## In *The Powerhouse*

The chapter opens the morning after Leithen's climactic confrontation with [[andrew-lumley]] at the Powerhouse. Leithen reads in *The Times* of Lumley's death — the collapse of the conspiracy that had threatened Pitt-Heron's life. The "Wild Geese" are those who have been abroad in pursuit: Tommy, who set out on his "wild-goose chase" [001-001] to rescue his friend; and the Powerhouse agents — Tuke (Routh), the super-butler, and Saronov — who had been sent to eliminate Pitt-Heron.

The resolution is carefully managed. Leithen, [[chapman]], and [[felix]] must convince [[charles-pitt-heron]] to return without revealing that they know about his connection to Lumley. Felix arranges for news of Lumley's death to appear in a Russian newspaper that Charles cannot avoid seeing: "The device was successful. Calling at Portman Square a few days later I learned from Ethel Pitt-Heron's glowing face that her troubles were over. That same evening a cable to me from Tommy announced the return of the wanderers" [009-036].

The chapter title echoes the novel's opening chapter, "Beginning of the Wild-Goose Chase," completing the narrative arc. What began as a seemingly quixotic pursuit across Central Asia ends in a quiet London resolution, with the Powerhouse destroyed and its secrets buried.
