# Edgeley Street

## Overview

Edgeley Street is a small street in the vicinity of Edgware Road and Connaught Mews in London. It forms part of the intricate network of lanes and byways through which Leithen escapes his pursuers in Chapter VII.

## In *The Powerhouse*

Edgeley Street is mentioned at a critical escape moment in Chapter VII. After discovering that his chauffeur has been replaced by an impostor, Leithen dives into Levison's picture-shop on the Edgware Road. The proprietor, Mr. Levison, tells him of a back exit: "Why, yes; there is the door into the lane which runs from Edgeley Street into Connaught Mews" (PN 949). This back-door knowledge proves crucial -- Leithen escapes into the lane, reaches a "narrow alley," and threads his way through "various cross streets" until he emerges on the Bayswater Road (PN 953-954). Edgeley Street is one link in the chain of obscure London geography that enables his survival.

## Historical Context

Edgeley Street was typical of the modest side streets that ran off London's great arterial roads, serving as service routes, mews entrances, and access to the rear of commercial premises. In the Edwardian era, the area north of the Edgware Road was a mix of shops, workshops, and residential terraces, with a dense network of back lanes providing essential infrastructure. Streets like Edgeley were part of the hidden geography of London -- known to tradesmen, servants, and those who, like Levison, worked in the lesser trades of the city.
