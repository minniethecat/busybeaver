# The Carlton Club

## Overview

The Carlton Club is one of London's most prestigious gentlemen's clubs, historically associated with the Conservative Party. Founded in 1832 by Tory peers and MPs, it occupied premises on Pall Mall and was a centre of Conservative political networking and social life for the British establishment.

## In *The Powerhouse*

The Carlton Club appears only once in the novel, but it serves a significant function: when the authorities investigate the background of a German spy arrested at Plymouth, [[andrew-lumley]] is identified as "a rich bachelor, a member of the Athenaeum and Carlton Clubs, and a dweller in the Albany" (PN 193). This detail, which Leithen reads in the legal papers sent to him by the Solicitor-General's office, establishes Lumley as a figure of the highest social respectability. Membership in both the intellectual Athenaeum and the political Carlton places him squarely within the British establishment -- the very establishment he secretly conspires to destroy. The Carlton Club thus functions as a piece of the facade Lumley constructs to mask his radical ambitions.

## Historical Context

By the Edwardian period, the Carlton Club was the undisputed headquarters of Conservative political organisation. Its members included Cabinet ministers, party leaders, and influential backbenchers. The club was known for its elegant architecture and its role as a venue for political deal-making. Its mention alongside the Athenaeum -- the more intellectual and literary club -- signals Lumley's careful cultivation of an identity that spans both politics and culture.
