# Belgrave Square

Belgrave Square is the location of the Embassy where [[felix|Monsieur Felix]], a diplomat and ally of [[edward-leithen|Leithen]], works. It serves as a crucial safe haven and intelligence hub during Leithen's struggle against the Powerhouse conspiracy.

## Felix's Embassy

Leithen first seeks out Belgrave Square upon returning from a fishing trip, having formulated a plan to involve his diplomatic friend in the investigation. "I reached London about eleven in the morning, and went straight to Belgrave Square" [004-012]. In the "little library off the big secretaries' room," he finds Felix, "a sunburnt sportsman fresh from a Norwegian salmon river," and enlists his help.

The Embassy becomes Leithen's operational base in the later stages of the affair. Felix provides resources, diplomatic cover, and access to international intelligence networks that prove essential in tracking [[charles-pitt-heron|Charles Pitt-Heron]]'s movements across Europe and ultimately unmasking [[andrew-lumley|Andrew Lumley]].

## The Final Dash

During the climactic chase sequence, Belgrave Square represents safety. After escaping through [[hyde-park|Hyde Park]] and fleeing down [[grosvenor-place|Grosvenor Place]], Leithen's sole objective is to reach the Embassy. "My goal was the Embassy in Belgrave Square, but I was convinced that if I approached it directly I should share the fate of Chapman" [007-017].

The distance proves agonising: "There was now less than a quarter of a mile between me and Belgrave Square, but I saw that it would be a hard course to cover" [007-031]. The Powerhouse network has deployed its full resources to intercept him before he reaches the diplomatic sanctuary.

After the crisis resolves, Leithen departs Belgrave Square in calmer circumstances: "I left Belgrave Square about a quarter to eight and retraced my steps along the route which for me that afternoon had been so full of tremors" [008-002].
