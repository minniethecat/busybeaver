# Bayswater Road

## Overview

Bayswater Road is a major road forming the northern boundary of Hyde Park and Kensington Gardens. It runs from Marble Arch westward through Notting Hill towards Shepherd's Bush, offering views across the park on its southern side. In *The Powerhouse*, it marks Leithen's re-emergence into the open after his escape from the fake chauffeur.

## In *The Powerhouse*

Bayswater Road appears in Chapter VII after Leithen has escaped from the impostor chauffeur by diving into Levison's picture-shop. Having exited "into the lane which runs from Edgeley Street into Connaught Mews," Leithen makes his way through back streets and "came out on the Bayswater Road pretty far west" (PN 954). From here, he resolves to "strike southeast across the Park" towards Belgrave Square and the safety of the Embassy. The Bayswater Road represents a momentary respite from pursuit -- an open public space where, in daylight, a man might feel temporarily safe before descending back into the danger-filled streets of Mayfair.

## Historical Context

Bayswater Road forms part of the A402 and runs along the line of the old Uxbridge Road. In the Edwardian era, the northern side was developed with stuccoed terraces and mansion blocks, while the southern side opened onto Hyde Park. The area was popular with middle-class professionals and was less fashionable than Mayfair but more respectable than the districts further west. The "pretty far west" reference suggests Leithen emerged well into residential Bayswater, a quiet area where he could plan his next move unobserved.
