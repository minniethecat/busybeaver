# House of Commons

## Overview

The House of Commons is the elected lower chamber of the Parliament of the United Kingdom. In the Edwardian period depicted in *The Powerhouse*, it was the primary legislative body of the British Empire, dominated by the Liberal and Conservative parties with a growing Labour presence. It met in the Palace of Westminster, in a chamber rebuilt after the 1834 fire in Gothic Revival style.

## In *The Powerhouse*

The novel opens at the House of Commons, when [[edward-leithen]] and [[tommy-deloraine]] emerge from the building on an afternoon "early in May" (PN 64). Leithen had recently entered Parliament "by an accident at a by-election" and found himself busy with both his legal practice and his parliamentary duties (PN 64). Tommy, still sitting "for the family seat in Yorkshire," is in "a powerful bad temper" about the dull debate inside, contrasting "the frowsy place" with the "cheerful world outside" (PN 64-65).

The Labour member [[chapman]] is a third MP in the novel. After Chapman is attacked in the street, Leithen notes that "for a Member of Parliament to be robbed in broad daylight next door to the House of Commons upset the foundations of his faith" (PN 1161). Chapman himself, when recovering, demands "a speech in Parliament by himself on this last outrage of Capitalism" (PN 1163). The House thus serves as both a physical setting and a symbol of the established order that the Powerhouse conspirators seek to undermine.

## Historical Context

The Edwardian House of Commons was in a period of transition. The Parliament Act of 1911 would soon curtail the powers of the House of Lords, and the Labour Party was emerging as a significant third force. The "Mother of Parliaments" (as Leithen calls it at PN 70) was the hub of British political life, its members drawn largely from the aristocracy and the professional classes -- men like Leithen (a barrister) and Tommy (a country gentleman).
