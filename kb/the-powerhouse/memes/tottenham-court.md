# Tottenham Court Road

## Overview

Tottenham Court Road is a major road in central London, running from Euston Road in the north to St Giles Circus (where it meets Oxford Street and Charing Cross Road) in the south. For much of its history, it was known for furniture shops, second-hand dealers, and a slightly raffish character distinct from the polished West End. In *The Powerhouse*, it provides the eastern boundary of the area where Leithen walks into a trap.

## In *The Powerhouse*

Tottenham Court Road is mentioned in Chapter VI as Leithen navigates towards the dinner that will become an ambush. Heading to Rapaccini's restaurant, he notes: "Antioch Street, as I had learned from the Directory, was in the area between Langham Place and Tottenham Court Road" (PN 706). This geographical bracket places the restaurant in an ambiguous zone -- not quite the fashionable West End of Langham Place, not quite the commercial bustle of Tottenham Court Road, but somewhere in between, in a district of "boardinghouses and architects' offices, with a few antiquity shops and a picture-cleaner's" (PN 706). The location is perfectly chosen for its obscurity: a restaurant that can be at once "respectable" and a criminal front.

## Historical Context

Tottenham Court Road takes its name from the manor of Tottenham Court, which was originally a rural estate. By the 19th century, it had developed into a commercial street known for furniture and cabinet-making, and later for electrical goods and electronics. Heal's furniture store (established 1810) and Maple & Co. were among its famous businesses. At the time of the novel, it was a diverse thoroughfare that formed the eastern edge of the area being developed for professional and medical uses around Portland Place and Harley Street.
