# South London

## Overview

South London refers to the districts of the capital lying south of the River Thames. In *The Powerhouse*, it encompasses locations pivotal to the early stages of the mystery: the County Court where Leithen first meets the super-butler Tuke, and the White Lodge in Blackheath where Lumley stores his art collection under the alias of Pavia.

## In *The Powerhouse*

South London becomes Leithen's gateway into the conspiracy in Chapter II. He travels there reluctantly for "an ordinary taxicab accident" case in a County Court (PN 165). There he encounters Tuke, Pavia's butler, whose "lines of ruthlessness" around his mouth and intense intelligence make a deep impression (PN 168-169). Later, driven south across the river again with Jenkinson to view the Wedgwood plaques, Leithen realizes they are heading "through South London" towards Blackheath (PN 214). He reflects that Lumley "probably passed under an alias in South London" (PN 439), using the suburban anonymity of the area to mask his operations.

## Historical Context

In the Edwardian era, South London was a patchwork of industrial districts, working-class neighbourhoods, and genteel suburbs like Blackheath and Dulwich. It was connected to the City and West End by a growing network of bridges, trams, and railways. The area had a distinct identity from the wealthier North and West London and was seen as a place where one might live quietly without attracting the attention of Society.
