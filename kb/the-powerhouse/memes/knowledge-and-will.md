# Knowledge and Will

## Overview

The phrase "the marriage of knowledge and will" encapsulates the philosophical engine behind the Powerhouse conspiracy. It represents the uneasy synthesis of scientific discovery (knowledge) and ruthless ambition (will) that Andrew Lumley believes is the key to remaking the world.

## In *The Powerhouse*

The concept originates with a German Nietzschean professor whom Leithen once met in the Tyrol, who declared: "The pity is that the reformers do not know, and those who know are too idle to reform. Some day there will come the marriage of knowledge and will, and then the world will march" [003-038].

Lumley echoes this idea during his long monologue at [[high-ashes|High Ashes]], arguing that civilisation's enemies fail because they are "parochial" — the ones who possess destructive knowledge lack the will to deploy it, and the fanatics with will lack true knowledge. He describes two types of "extra-social" minds: the artists who discover new knowledge for its own sake, and the idealists who want to reshape society. "If I were to hazard a guess," he says, "I should say that it took both types to bring about results, for the second find the knowledge and the first the will to use it" [003-035].

The [[the-powerhouse-org|Powerhouse]] is Lumley's attempt to engineer this marriage — bringing together scientists, financiers, and men of action into a secret organisation capable of wielding hidden knowledge against the existing compact of civilisation. For Leithen, a barrister who describes himself as "the cement of civilisation" [003-033], this vision represents the ultimate threat to everything he values.
