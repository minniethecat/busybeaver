The "East India merchant" is the professional identity of [[julius-pavia|Julius Pavia]], a retired trader whose wealth and international connections make him a valuable asset to [[andrew-lumley|Andrew Lumley]]'s Powerhouse network.

## In the Novel

When Leithen first investigates Pavia, he notes that "a retired East India merchant did not suggest anything wildly speculative" [002-015]. This assumption proves misleading — Pavia's commercial background provides the financial resources and global reach that the Powerhouse exploits.

Pavia's East India connection represents the respectable, established face of imperial commerce. His wealth — derived from cotton mills, Liverpool ground rents, and Eastern trade — funds the Powerhouse's operations, and his network of agents ([[tuke|Tuke]], [[routh|Routh]]) provides its operational muscle.

## Significance

The East India trade in the novel functions as a bridge between legitimate commerce and covert conspiracy. Pavia's position as a retired merchant allows him to operate without suspicion, his servants and agents moving freely through the channels of imperial trade. The juxtaposition of respectable commercial identity with subversive activity is characteristic of Lumley's method — hiding the extraordinary within the ordinary.
