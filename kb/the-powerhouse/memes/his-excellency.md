"His Excellency" is the unnamed Ambassador who presides over the Russian Embassy in Belgrave Square, London. Although he never appears directly in the narrative, his presence and authority are crucial to the novel's climax.

## Role in the Narrative

When [[edward-leithen|Leithen]] flees from [[andrew-lumley|Andrew Lumley]]'s agents through the streets of London, he seeks refuge at the Russian Embassy [007-045]. Knowing that an embassy is privileged ground which the police cannot enter, Leithen invokes his acquaintance with "His Excellency" to gain admission through the back door.

Leithen appeals to the Ambassador's authority to overrule a suspicious footman and demands to be taken before him. The Ambassador's chef, Monsieur Alphonse, recognizes Leithen as someone who has dined at "His Excellency's table," and this recognition helps secure Leithen's safety.

## Connection to Felix

The Ambassador is the patron of [[felix|M. Felix]], who serves at the Embassy and is Leithen's direct contact there. Through Felix, Leithen has been coordinating with the Russian authorities to track [[tommy-deloraine|Tommy Deloraine]], [[charles-pitt-heron|Charles Pitt-Heron]], and the Powerhouse operatives pursuing them through Central Asia.

## Significance

The Ambassador represents the reach of international law and diplomacy — the "civilised" world's counter to the stateless conspiracy of Lumley's Powerhouse. His embassy serves as literal sanctuary, a space where the rules of ordinary pursuit do not apply, and Leithen's appeal to diplomatic privilege is what ultimately saves him from capture.
