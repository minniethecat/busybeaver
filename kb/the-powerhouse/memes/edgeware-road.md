# Edgware Road

## Overview

Edgware Road (now commonly spelled Edgware Road) is one of London's principal arterial roads, running from Marble Arch northwest through Maida Vale and Kilburn towards Edgware. The southern stretch forms part of the A5, the historic Roman road of Watling Street. It is a major thoroughfare serving the northwestern suburbs.

## In *The Powerhouse*

The Edgware Road serves as the setting for one of Leithen's most dramatic moments in Chapter VII. After discovering that his chauffeur Stagg has been replaced by an impostor, Leithen waits until the car slows near "the point where the Marylebone Road joins it" (PN 939). Seizing the moment when traffic halts for a policeman, he opens the car door and makes a desperate escape: "the car slowed down in obedience to a policeman's uplifted hand, and very gently I opened the door on the left side" (PN 940). He dives between vehicles and disappears into a picture-shop, leaving the fake chauffeur unknowingly driving on. The busy junction provided the perfect cover for a man fleeing for his life.

## Historical Context

Edgware Road is one of London's oldest roads, following the line of the Roman Watling Street. By the Edwardian era, it was a bustling commercial and residential artery, lined with shops, pubs, and terraced houses. The junction with Marylebone Road was (and remains) one of London's busiest traffic intersections, making Leithen's escape both plausible and perilous. The area around Edgware Road was historically known for its Arab and Middle Eastern communities, a character that developed later in the 20th century.
