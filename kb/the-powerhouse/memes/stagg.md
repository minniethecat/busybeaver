# Stagg

Stagg is a chauffeur employed by the Powerhouse network who is assigned to drive [[edward-leithen|Sir Edward Leithen]] during the investigation. Described as "a sombre and silent man" (003-011), Stagg proves competent and resourceful, and his quiet professionalism eventually marks him as more ally than adversary.

## Role in the Story

Stagg enters the narrative when Leithen is given "a car and a chauffeur called Stagg" (003-004). Initially, Leithen views him as a Powerhouse asset — a minder rather than a helper — and looks forward to "trying his mettle" against him (003-004).

Stagg's competence quickly becomes apparent. When their car is forced off the road by "a heavy carrier's cart," Stagg "clapped on the brakes, but they were too late" (003-012). In the aftermath, he proves resourceful: he "borrowed a bicycle somehow" to continue the pursuit (003-013). His reliability shifts Leithen's perception from suspicion to grudging respect.

In later chapters, Stagg becomes a fixture of Leithen's London movements, calling "for me at two o'clock precisely" (007-013). His punctuality and competence mark him as a professional rather than a conspirator.

## Relationships

- **[[edward-leithen]]** — Chauffeur to Leithen; their relationship evolves from mutual suspicion to professional trust
- **[[the-powerhouse-org]]** — Employed by the Powerhouse, but his loyalty appears to be professional rather than ideological

## Significance

Stagg represents the ordinary people caught in the Powerhouse's machinery — employed by the organisation but not necessarily committed to its goals. His silence and competence make him an ambiguous figure: is he watching Leithen or simply doing his job? The novel leaves the question open, and in doing so, reinforces the theme that in a world of conspiracies, even the most ordinary interactions are tinged with uncertainty.
