# Ethel Pitt-Heron

Ethel Pitt-Heron is the wife of [[charles-pitt-heron|Charles Pitt-Heron]] and cousin of [[tommy-deloraine|Tommy Deloraine]]. Her distress at her husband's sudden disappearance is the emotional catalyst that sends Tommy on his pursuit to Moscow and back. The narrator, [[edward-leithen|Leithen]], describes her as "that adorable child unequalled in the world" (001-042) and admits she is "the only person that ever captured my stony heart" (001-016).

## Role in the Story

Ethel's role is primarily emotional: she is the reason Tommy Deloraine abandons his Venezuelan bird-nesting expedition to pursue Pitt-Heron. When Charles fails to return home, she sends for Tommy "in terrible straits" (001-024). Her distress humanizes the stakes of the conspiracy plot — the Powerhouse is not merely an intellectual puzzle but a force that threatens real lives and marriages.

Later, when Leithen visits her, he finds her "in grave distress" (003-005), and the novel ends with the brief, satisfying note that Leithen learned "from Ethel Pitt-Heron's glowing face that her troubles were at an end" (009-010).

## Key Appearances

- **[[ch01|Chapter I]]** — Tommy reveals that Ethel sent for him when Charles disappeared (001-024)
- **[[ch03|Chapter III]]** — Leithen visits Ethel and finds her "in grave distress" (003-005)
- **[[ch09|Chapter IX]]** — Leithen sees Ethel's "glowing face" confirming the resolution (009-010)

## Relationships

- **[[charles-pitt-heron]]** — Her husband, whose disappearance causes her distress
- **[[tommy-deloraine]]** — Her cousin, who acts to protect her interests
- **[[edward-leithen]]** — The narrator, who once had feelings for her (001-016)

## Significance

Ethel Pitt-Heron is not an active agent in the plot — she is the figure in distress around whom the male protagonists organize their rescue mission. Yet her presence serves a crucial narrative function: she grounds the conspiracy thriller in domestic emotion, reminding the reader and the characters that the stakes are human, not merely political.
