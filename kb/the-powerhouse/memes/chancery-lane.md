# Chancery Lane

## Overview

Chancery Lane is a historic street in the City of London, running between Fleet Street and High Holborn. For centuries it has been at the heart of the English legal profession, giving its name to the Court of Chancery and housing the Law Society, Lincoln's Inn, and numerous barristers' chambers and solicitors' offices.

## In *The Powerhouse*

Chancery Lane appears in Chapter IV as the location of the Tube station Leithen uses to confirm he is being followed (PN 541). More significantly, the area is Leithen's professional home ground -- his chambers are in [[fountain-court|Fountain Court]] in the Temple, a short walk away. When he returns from Chancery Lane Station to his chambers, he passes through a legal landscape that represents everything Lumley seeks to undermine: the rule of law, civil procedure, and the ordered administration of justice. The proximity of Chancery Lane to Leithen's chambers underscores the contrast between the quiet legal world and the international conspiracy he stumbles into.

## Historical Context

Chancery Lane takes its name from the Court of Chancery, which historically sat in Westminster but had offices nearby. From the 13th century onwards, the area developed as the administrative hub of English law, centred around Lincoln's Inn and later the Law Society. The Public Record Office once stood at the northern end of Chancery Lane (the building is now part of King's College London). By Buchan's time, the lane was a well-established metonym for the legal profession.
