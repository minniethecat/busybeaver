# Bill Docker

Bill Docker is a minor character in *The Powerhouse* who appears in two distinct contexts: first as a criminal defendant represented by [[edward-leithen|Edward Leithen]], and later in a violent confrontation at a restaurant.

## The Trial

Bill Docker was the accused in a burglary case. Although criminal law was not Leithen's usual province, he took the case occasionally to maintain his skills in handling witnesses. The evidence against Docker was strong, but Leithen believed his client had been the victim of a shabby trick played by his associates. Leithen mounted a vigorous defence and secured a verdict of "Not guilty" (006-029):

> A certain Bill Docker was the accused, a gentleman who bore a bad reputation in the eyes of the police. The evidence against him was strong, but it was more or less an ugly business, and my sympathies were with the accused, for though he may very well have been guilty, yet he had been the victim of a shabby trick. (006-029)

Docker expressed his gratitude to Leithen after the acquittal, asking in a hoarse whisper how Leithen had "squared the old bird" — meaning the judge.

## The Restaurant Confrontation

Docker reappears later in the narrative when Leithen encounters him at a restaurant. Leithen greets him and reminds him of the favour he once did him, appealing to Docker's sense of loyalty (006-048):

> "Mr. Docker," I said. "I once did you a good turn. But for me you might be doing time now instead of drinking champagne like a gentleman." (006-048)

Docker affirms his loyalty: "The man who says Bill Docker would go back on a pal is a liar" (006-048).

The scene culminates in a physical struggle, during which Docker is described as lying senseless among the wreckage he created, "like Samson among the ruins of Gaza."

## Character

Bill Docker is portrayed as a rough but not entirely dishonourable man — a criminal with a code of loyalty. His gratitude toward Leithen for the legal rescue creates a debt that Leithen later calls upon.
