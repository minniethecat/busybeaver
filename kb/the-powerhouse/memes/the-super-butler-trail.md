# The Super-Butler Trail

## Overview

"The Super-Butler Trail" refers to Chapter IV of *The Powerhouse*, titled "I Follow the Trail of the Super-Butler," in which [[sir-edward-leithen]] methodically tracks the movements and identity of Tuke — the tall, thin-faced butler he first encountered in a County Court case — as a way into the Powerhouse conspiracy.

## In *The Powerhouse*

Leithen begins his investigation by recognising Tuke as a critical lead. Having encountered the super-butler at the Blackheath house of [[julius-pavia]], Leithen decides Lumley himself is too formidable to approach directly: "I thought I might have a better chance with the super-butler. As it turned out I hit his trail almost at once" [004-090]. The trail leads Leithen through London, uncovering a network of false identities and shadowy connections.

Through careful detective work, Leithen and [[chapman]] identify Tuke's real name as Routh — a disgraced trade union official who had embezzled funds and used legal loopholes to escape prosecution. Chapman describes Routh as "the leader of the left wing of the movement" with "that gift of half-scientific, half-philosophic jargon which is dear at all times to the hearts of the half-baked" [004-110]. The trail demonstrates Leithen's methodical approach and the novel's grounding in realistic investigative procedure, even as the conspiracy it uncovers grows ever more fantastic.
