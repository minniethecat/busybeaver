# Monsieur Felix

"Monsieur Felix" is the pseudonym [[edward-leithen|Sir Edward Leithen]] gives to his friend, a diplomat at an unnamed Embassy. A "grave young man, slightly my senior, learned, discreet, and ambitious, but with an engaging boyishness cropping up now and then under the official gold lace" (ch05), Felix becomes Leithen's most valuable institutional ally in the Powerhouse investigation, using diplomatic cables and international police contacts to track [[tommy-deloraine|Tommy Deloraine]] and [[charles-pitt-heron|Charles Pitt-Heron]] across Central Asia.

## Role in the Story

Felix is Leithen's conduit to official power. When Tommy and Pitt-Heron vanish into the wilds of Central Asia, it is Felix who cables the Russian authorities in cipher, tracks their movements through Bokhara and the Hissar range, and later alerts the frontier police to the presence of the Powerhouse assassins pursuing them. His assistance transforms the investigation from a personal quest into an operation with international reach.

Leithen and Felix's friendship was forged on "a dry-fly stream in Hampshire" (ch05) and deepened through legal work Leithen did for the Embassy. Their intimacy is marked "by the use of Christian names and by dining frequently together" (ch05). This personal bond makes Felix willing to bend diplomatic protocol — Leithen notes that he is "by no means certain that the part he played in this tale was strictly in accordance with official etiquette" (ch05).

- **[[ch05|Chapter V]]** — Leithen enlists Felix's help; Felix sends ciphered cables to Bokhara (ch05)
- **[[ch06|Chapter VI]]** — Felix provides an hour of his time to review the full story; cables again to warn of Tuke/Routh and Saronov (ch06)
- **[[ch08|Chapter VIII]]** — Felix telephones Leithen with news that the frontier police have intercepted the assassins (008-XXX)
- **[[ch09|Chapter IX]]** — Felix is among those Leithen attempts to contact during the state of siege; Leithen receives a copy of the formal statement intended for Felix (ch09)

## Background

Felix works at an unnamed Embassy in London, stationed in Belgrave Square. He has recently returned from a Norwegian salmon river, "a sunburnt sportsman" (ch05), when Leithen first approaches him. He has access to diplomatic cipher communications and the authority to request assistance from foreign police forces — resources that prove decisive in the investigation.

## Relationships

- **[[edward-leithen]]** — Close friend and ally; Leithen assists him with legal matters, Felix returns the favour with diplomatic resources
- **[[tommy-deloraine]]** — Felix tracks Tommy's movements through Central Asia via diplomatic channels
- **[[charles-pitt-heron]]** — Felix's cables help locate Pitt-Heron and prevent his assassination

## Significance

Felix represents the legitimate international order that stands in opposition to the Powerhouse's conspiratorial network. While [[andrew-lumley|Andrew Lumley]] operates through shadow networks and secret organisations, Felix works through embassies, police forces, and diplomatic protocols. His presence in the story assures the reader — and Leithen — that official power, however bureaucratic, can be mobilised against the forces of chaos. The tension between Felix's discretion as a diplomat and his willingness to act as Leithen's ally reflects the novel's broader theme of personal loyalty transcending institutional constraints.
