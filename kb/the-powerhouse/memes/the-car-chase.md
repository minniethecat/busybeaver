# The Car Chase

The car chase is a pursuit sequence in [[ch03|Chapter III]] during which [[edward-leithen|Sir Edward Leithen]] and his chauffeur [[stagg|Stagg]] are deliberately forced off the road, revealing the Powerhouse's willingness to use physical intimidation to obstruct the investigation into [[charles-pitt-heron|Charles Pitt-Heron]]'s disappearance.

## Summary

Leithen, having been given "a car and a chauffeur called Stagg" (003-004), sets out to follow Pitt-Heron's trail. A heavy carrier's cart forces their vehicle off the road: "Stagg clapped on the brakes, but they were too late" (003-012). The crash is no accident — it is the Powerhouse's opening move to slow Leithen's pursuit. Stagg, undeterred, "borrowed a bicycle somehow" and continues the chase (003-013), demonstrating the resourcefulness that would later mark him as more ally than adversary.

## Context

The car chase occurs early in Leithen's investigation, shortly after [[tommy-deloraine|Tommy Deloraine]] departs for Central Asia. It is the first moment when the Powerhouse's methods become physically threatening rather than merely mysterious. Before this point, the conspiracy had been a matter of strange behaviour and financial irregularities; the deliberate crash makes it clear that Leithen is dealing with an organisation willing to use force.

## Participants

- **[[edward-leithen]]** — Passenger, in pursuit of Pitt-Heron's trail
- **[[stagg]]** — Chauffeur, who responds to the crash with quick thinking and resourcefulness

## Location

- The roads outside London, near the beginning of Leithen's cross-country pursuit

## Chronology

- **Preceded by**: [[pitt-herons-disappearance]] — Leithen begins his investigation
- **Followed by**: Leithen's continued pursuit and eventual meeting with informants

## Significance

The car chase marks the transition from mystery to active conflict. The Powerhouse moves from the shadows into direct action, and Leithen's role shifts from curious observer to targeted adversary. Stagg's behaviour during the crash — competent, calm, and resourceful — introduces the ambiguity that surrounds many of the Powerhouse's employees: are they servants of the conspiracy or simply professionals doing their jobs?
