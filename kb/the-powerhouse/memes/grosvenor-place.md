# Grosvenor Place

Grosvenor Place is a London thoroughfare that features prominently in two critical episodes of the Powerhouse narrative: [[andrew-lumley|Andrew Lumley]]'s threatening confrontation with [[edward-leithen|Leithen]], and Leithen's desperate flight from the Powerhouse network.

## Lumley's Warning

Leithen first visits Grosvenor Place in the company of Lumley himself. "I am going to make a call in Grosvenor Place," Lumley tells him, "and shall beg in return the pleasure of your company" [004-064]. The walk is not a social courtesy but a calculated prelude to intimidation.

At a house in Grosvenor Place, Lumley delivers his ultimatum: "We stopped at a house in Grosvenor Place, and he relinquished my arm. 'Mr. Leithen,' he said, 'a word from one who wishes you no ill. You are a friend of Pitt-Heron, but where he goes you cannot follow. Take my advice and keep out of his affairs. You will do no good to him, and you may bring yourself into serious danger'" [004-069]. The mask of benevolent sophistication drops, revealing "something implacable and deadly" beneath.

## The Chase

Grosvenor Place becomes the scene of Leithen's most perilous moment. After discovering the full extent of the Powerhouse conspiracy and escaping through Bayswater and [[hyde-park|Hyde Park]], Leithen finds himself hunted on Grosvenor Place. A group of workmen, a pavement artist, and even a policeman move to intercept him, all apparently under Lumley's direction.

"I took to my heels and ran for my life down Grosvenor Place" [007-036]. In the blistering June heat, Leithen sprints the length of the street, pursued by the full resources of the Powerhouse organisation. His destination is the [[belgrave-square|Embassy in Belgrave Square]], less than a quarter-mile away, where safety awaits.
