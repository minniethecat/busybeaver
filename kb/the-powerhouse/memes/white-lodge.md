# White Lodge

The White Lodge is a suburban mansion occupied by [[julius-pavia|Julius Pavia]], a shadowy figure connected to the Powerhouse conspiracy. Described as "one of those small, square, late-Georgian mansions which you see all around London—once a country-house among fields, now only a villa in a pretentious garden" [002-033], the house serves as a front for Pavia's operations and is filled with an extraordinary collection of art treasures.

## Discovery

[[edward-leithen|Leithen]] first learns of the White Lodge while investigating Pavia's background. Consulting the directory, he finds Pavia listed "as the occupier of a suburban mansion called the White Lodge" [002-006]. The discovery fuels Leithen's growing suspicion that Pavia is not merely a retired East India merchant but something more sinister.

Leithen's acquaintance Jenkinson, an art expert, independently mentions the White Lodge as the source of rare collectibles. When asked the name of the house, Jenkinson confirms: "The White Lodge" [002-029]. This corroboration strengthens Leithen's suspicion that Pavia, [[andrew-lumley|Lumley]], and [[charles-pitt-heron|Charles Pitt-Heron]] are interconnected.

## The Visit

Leithen and Jenkinson visit the White Lodge "about half-past three" [002-033] under the pretext of examining Japanese plaques. The interior proves staggering: "The hall was crowded with Japanese armour and lacquer cabinets. One room was lined from floor to ceiling with good pictures, mostly seventeenth-century Dutch, and had enough Chippendale chairs to accommodate a public meeting" [002-033].

The visit confirms Leithen's suspicions about the connection between Pavia and Lumley. The butler Tuke, whom Leithen had previously encountered in a County Court case, opens the door for the visitors, further linking the household to the Powerhouse network.

## Later References

During the investigation, Leithen is advised "to write to the White Lodge" [004-037] as a means of contacting Pavia, indicating that the mansion functions as a communication point within the conspiracy's network.
