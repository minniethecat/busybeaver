# Cannon Street

## Overview

Cannon Street is a major thoroughfare in the City of London, running from St Paul's Churchyard to Monument. Historically a centre of commerce, it was known for its proximity to the financial district and Cannon Street Station, a key railway terminus. In *The Powerhouse*, it provides the setting for a minor but significant clue.

## In *The Powerhouse*

Cannon Street is mentioned in Chapter IV as the location of a "bucket-shop" -- a disreputable brokerage firm -- where the clerk Routh works (PN 487). During a fraud prosecution at the Old Bailey, Leithen examines Routh as a witness and notices his face: "His jaw looked as if it had been made in two pieces which did not fit, and he had little, bright protuberant eyes" (PN 488). Recognizing the family resemblance, Leithen interrogates the clerk privately and discovers he is the brother of Tuke (alias Routh), the super-butler who has been dispatched to hunt Pitt-Heron in Central Asia. The Cannon Street bucket-shop clerk thus unwittingly gives Leithen the name and history of one of Lumley's key operatives.

## Historical Context

Cannon Street Station, opened in 1866, was a major London terminus and the headquarters of the South Eastern Railway. The street itself was heavily bombed during the Blitz and has since undergone extensive redevelopment. In the pre-war period of the novel, it typified the bustling commercial City where fortunes could be made and lost in speculative ventures.
