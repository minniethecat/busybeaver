# The Legion of Honour

## Overview

The Legion of Honour (Legion d'Honneur) is France's highest order of merit, established by Napoleon Bonaparte in 1802. In *The Powerhouse*, it appears among the foreign decorations worn by [[andrew-lumley]] at his dinner party, adding to the impression of a cultivated, internationally respected gentleman.

## In *The Powerhouse*

Leithen observes that Lumley "was wearing some foreign orders, including the Legion of Honour" [008-050] as he presided over the dinner table, charming his guests. The detail is carefully placed: Lumley has not merely acquired British respectability but has garnered recognition from foreign governments as well. The Legion of Honour signals that Lumley has been operating successfully on the international stage long before Leithen began to suspect him.

The decoration is part of Lumley's masterful persona. He is simultaneously the genial host, the scholarly conversationalist, and a decorated figure of the international establishment — all while running a conspiracy that aims to destroy that very establishment. The foreign orders, like his mild spectacles and soft voice, are protective colouring. They make it almost impossible for anyone at the table to see what Leithen alone perceives: that Lumley is not a pillar of civilisation but its most formidable enemy.
