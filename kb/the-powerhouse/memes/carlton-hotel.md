# Carlton Hotel

## Overview

The Carlton Hotel was one of London's most prestigious luxury hotels, located on the corner of Haymarket and Pall Mall. Opened in 1899, it was the epitome of Edwardian elegance, frequented by politicians, aristocrats, and wealthy international travellers. Designed by the architect C.J. Phipps, it competed with the Ritz and Claridge's for the custom of high society.

## In *The Powerhouse*

The Carlton Hotel appears in Chapter I in a revealing nightmare Leithen experiences after Tommy Deloraine's departure for Russia. He dreams of "coercing Tommy and Pitt-Heron in a Russian fortress which faded away into the Carlton Hotel" (PN 145). This surreal transition -- from a Russian fortress to a London luxury hotel -- mirrors the novel's central tension between the savage "extra-social" world Lumley describes and the civilized surface of Edwardian society. The Carlton, as a haunt of the political and social elite, represents the "compact" of civilization that Lumley despises and seeks to undermine.

## Historical Context

The Carlton Hotel was badly damaged during the Blitz in 1940 and was eventually demolished in the late 1950s. Before its decline, it was noted for its luxurious Palm Court restaurant and its reputation as a gathering place for Conservative politicians. Cesar Ritz was involved in its early management, bringing the same standards that made the Ritz Hotel famous. Its location at the corner of Haymarket and Pall Mall placed it at the heart of London's clubland.
