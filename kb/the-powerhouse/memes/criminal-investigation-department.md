# Criminal Investigation Department

## Overview

The Criminal Investigation Department (CID) was the detective branch of the Metropolitan Police, based at Scotland Yard. By the Edwardian period it had become Britain's foremost agency for investigating serious crime, counter-espionage, and political conspiracy. Its officers, typically plainclothes detectives, handled everything from murder to anarchist plots.

## In *The Powerhouse*

The CID, represented by the character [[macgillivray]], serves as Leithen's institutional link to the forces of law and order. When [[edward-leithen]] needs to investigate the Powerhouse, he contacts Macgillivray, who "had been at the Bar . . . and was now one of the heads of the Criminal Investigation Department" (PN 515). Leithen presumes "on our old acquaintance" when asking for confidential information (PN 515).

Macgillivray is initially sceptical about the Powerhouse, laughing "out loud" at Leithen's question about a secret organisation by that name (PN 517). However, Leithen continues to work through him: after the confrontation at the restaurant, Leithen writes "a chit for Macgillivray which I asked one of the constables to take to Scotland Yard" (PN 857). He later sends [[chapman]] to deliver a crucial document to Macgillivray at Scotland Yard (PN 908). When Chapman disappears, Leithen rings "up Scotland Yard, but they had no news of him" (PN 912). The CID thus functions as Leithen's official backstop -- an institution he can call upon but whose methods are too slow and public for the delicate work of hunting the Powerhouse.

The novel ends with a note that "Scotland Yard is not in the habit of publishing its information" (PN 1186), implying that Macgillivray has kept the secrets of the affair.

## Historical Context

The CID was established in 1878 following a corruption scandal in the earlier Detective Branch. By the early 20th century it had grown into a professional detective force, though it was still small by modern standards. Counter-espionage was in its infancy -- the British Secret Service Bureau (later MI5 and MI6) was only founded in 1909. This explains why Leithen, a private citizen and MP, effectively conducts his own intelligence operation against the Powerhouse with only limited official support.
