# Whitehall

## Overview

Whitehall is the principal thoroughfare in the City of Westminster running from Trafalgar Square to Parliament Square. It is synonymous with the British government, housing the principal offices of the civil service including the [[the-treasury]], the [[the-foreign-office]], and the [[the-admiralty]].

## In *The Powerhouse*

Whitehall represents the official machinery of the British state in Buchan's novel. When [[edward-leithen]] walks from the House of Commons through Parliament Square, he passes through this district — the physical embodiment of [[the-compact]] and [[civilisation]] that [[andrew-lumley]] seeks to undermine. [[scotland-yard]], where Leithen consults with Macgillivray, lies just off Whitehall, symbolizing the overlap between political and police power.

The novel's geopolitical stakes play out against this backdrop: the [[the-russian-embassy]] represents foreign power intruding upon the heart of British government, while the network of Whitehall departments forms the defensive machinery of the state.

## Historical Context

In the Edwardian period (circa 1910s), Whitehall was already the established center of British imperial administration. The Foreign Office building (completed 1868), the Treasury, and the Admiralty all faced Whitehall, forming the architectural expression of British global power.
