# Chancery Lane Station

## Overview

Chancery Lane Station is a London Underground station on the Central line, situated at the junction of High Holborn and Gray's Inn Road. Opened in 1900, it serves the legal district of Holborn, close to Lincoln's Inn, Gray's Inn, and the Royal Courts of Justice. It was named after the nearby Chancery Lane, historically the heart of the English legal profession.

## In *The Powerhouse*

Chancery Lane Station plays a pivotal role in Chapter IV, when Leithen confirms he is being watched. After boarding the Tube at [[arch-station|Marble Arch Station]], he exploits his knowledge that a particular carriage stops "exactly opposite the exit at the Chancery Lane Station" (PN 541). By dashing up the passage to catch the lift of an earlier train, Leithen reaches the street ahead of fellow passengers. Concealed behind a pillar-box, he observes his tracker emerge breathless from the stairs, thus proving Lumley's agents are on his trail (PN 543-544). The station serves as the site where Leithen transforms from a man with mere suspicions into one with hard evidence of a conspiracy against him.

## Historical Context

Chancery Lane Station opened on 30 July 1900 as part of the Central London Railway (now the Central line). Its proximity to London's Inns of Court and the Law Courts made it a natural thoroughfare for barristers and solicitors, including the fictional Leithen, whose chambers were in the nearby Temple. The station was originally designed by the architect Harry Bell Measures.
