# Tuke

Tuke is a butler in the employ of [[julius-pavia|Julius Pavia]], operating as part of [[andrew-lumley|Andrew Lumley]]'s Powerhouse network. A tall, thin-faced man of unflappable courtesy, he serves as one of Leithen's earliest concrete leads in tracing the conspiracy — and later as one of the agents dispatched across Central Asia in pursuit of [[charles-pitt-heron|Charles Pitt-Heron]].

## Description

Tuke is described as a tall man with a very long, thin face and a jaw whose two parts seem scarcely to fit together (002-008). He is noted for his bland, unshakeable politeness — a quality that makes him effective both as a servant and as a covert operative, since it provides no purchase for suspicion or inquiry. Leithen takes an intense and unreasoning dislike to the man during their first encounter, noting his steady, impenetrable courtesy.

## Role in the Story

Tuke serves as a **connective tissue character** — one of the minor operatives whose movements allow Leithen to trace the shape and reach of Lumley's organization. He first appears as a witness in a seemingly trivial taxicab accident case, but Leithen's instinctive aversion to him proves justified when Tuke resurfaces as one of the agents dispatched to intercept Pitt-Heron in Central Asia.

Tuke's two appearances — first in a South London courtroom, then in Central Asia — demonstrate the geographical range of the Powerhouse and the versatility of its operatives. A man who can pass as a respectable butler in London can also pursue a fugitive across Turkestan.

## Key Appearances

- **County Court, South London (002-008)**: Tuke appears as a witness for the defence in a taxicab accident case on the 21st of May, representing his absent master, Mr. Pavia. Leithen, who is present on unrelated legal business, takes an immediate and unreasoning dislike to him, noting his tall frame, thin face, and impenetrable courtesy. This chance encounter plants the first seed of Leithen's suspicion about Pavia's household.
- **Central Asian pursuit (005-022)**: Tuke is later identified by the Russian police as one of two men (alongside [[saronov|Saronov]]) following the trail of [[tommy-deloraine|Tommy Deloraine]] and Pitt-Heron through Turkestan. The two had gone by Samarkand, intending to strike into the hills by a shorter route than the main road from Bokhara.

## Significance

Tuke represents the operational versatility of [[the-powerhouse-org|the Powerhouse]]. He moves between the drawing rooms of suburban London and the caravan routes of Central Asia with the same bland competence, embodying Lumley's insight that the most effective operatives are those who attract no attention. His role as a domestic servant is the perfect cover: a butler is trained to be invisible, to anticipate needs without being seen, to move through rooms without leaving a trace. These are precisely the skills required of a covert operative. Tuke is, in essence, a figure of the "servant as spy" — a trope that Buchan uses to suggest that the conspiracy's reach extends into the most intimate and trusted spaces of upper-class life.

## Notable Quotes

Leithen's visceral reaction to Tuke captures the man's unsettling quality without specifying anything actionable: Leithen could not identify what was wrong with the man — only that every instinct warned him something was (002-008).
