# Tommy's Journey to Bokhara

## Overview

Tommy's Journey to Bokhara is the opening action of John Buchan's *The Powerhouse*, in which [[tommy-deloraine|Tommy Deloraine]], a restless [[member-of-parliament|Member of Parliament]], volunteers to travel to Central Asia in search of his vanished friend [[charles-pitt-heron|Charles Pitt-Heron]].

## Context

The journey is set in motion by [[ethel-pitt-heron|Ethel Pitt-Heron]]'s appeal to Leithen at the beginning of the novel. Her husband Charles had suddenly fled London, leaving only a brief note saying he was "going away for a little" (PN Ch. I). When Leithen recruited Tommy, the MP for Joggleberry, Tommy had been complaining about Parliament: "What a juggins I am to be mouldering here! Joggleberry is the celestial limit... And the frowst on those back benches!" (PN 68). He called the House of Commons a "Damned monkey-house" and declared he must "get off for a bit" (PN 71--72).

Tommy traveled via Moscow, using the Transcaspian railway. Leithen calculated "it would have taken him seven or eight days to get from Moscow by the Transcaspian" (PN 447). However, by the time he reached Moscow, Pitt-Heron had already moved on. Ethel received "a very dispirited letter, for he had had no luck. Nobody in Moscow had seen or heard of any wandering Englishman remotely like Charles, and Tommy, after playing the private detective for three weeks, was nearly at the end of his tether and spoke of returning home" (PN 245).

## Significance

Tommy's journey represents the loyal friendship that drives the novel's positive action. While Lumley represents organized, extra-social intelligence, Tommy represents the equally powerful force of personal loyalty. Leithen describes him as a man of action: "whatever his failings in politics, I knew no one I would rather have with me to go tiger-shooting" (PN 537).

The journey's significance deepens when Leithen discovers Lumley's telegram and realizes Pitt-Heron is being hunted. On Leithen's instructions, Ethel wired Tommy: "Go on to Bokhara. Have information you will meet him there" (PN 247). This transformed Tommy's search-and-rescue into a race against Lumley's agent, creating a parallel chase across Central Asia that mirrored the conflict unfolding in London.

Tommy's initial journey also establishes the geographical scope of the novel. From the drawing rooms of Mayfair to the wilds of Turkestan, the Powerhouse conspiracy operates on a global scale. Tommy's transformation from bored parliamentarian to adventurer echoes the novel's theme of hidden capabilities emerging under pressure.
