# Oxford Street

## Overview

Oxford Street is one of London's most famous streets, running for approximately one and a half miles from Marble Arch in the west to St Giles Circus in the east. A major shopping destination since the 19th century, it was home to many of London's great department stores. In *The Powerhouse*, it is a street where the ordinary and the sinister coexist.

## In *The Powerhouse*

Oxford Street appears at a tense moment in Chapter VI, when Leithen and Chapman are walking home after the Antioch Street affair. Once they enter Oxford Street, Leithen becomes aware "that there were people on those pavements who knew all about me" (PN 863). He notices shadowy figures at "the mouth of one of those little dark side-alleys which run up into mews and small dingy courts" and finds himself "being skilfully edged away from Chapman into the shadow" (PN 863). The street's familiar commercial bustle -- shopgirls, promenading clerks, all the "flotsam of a London summer" (PN 861) -- conceals Lumley's operatives, turning the everyday shopping street into a landscape of hidden menace.

## Historical Context

Oxford Street follows the route of the Roman road Via Trinobantina. By the Edwardian period, it had become London's premier shopping destination, home to Selfridges (opened 1909), John Lewis (opened 1864), and numerous smaller retailers. The street's development into a retail hub accelerated with the opening of the Central London Railway stations at Marble Arch, Bond Street, and Oxford Circus. The "little dark side-alleys" Leithen notices were characteristic of the West End's older fabric, where grand commercial frontages hid a warren of mews and courtyards behind.
