# Park Lane

## Overview

Park Lane is a major road in central London running along the eastern edge of [[hyde-park]], dividing the park from the wealthy district of Mayfair. In *The Powerhouse*, it features in two critical episodes: Leithen's counter-surveillance manoeuvre and his desperate flight from [[andrew-lumley]]'s agents.

## In the Novel

**The Marble Arch Manoeuvre (Ch. V).** After learning that he is being followed, Leithen devises a plan to identify his tracker using Park Lane and the Tube. He explains:

> "I walked up Park Lane, for it seemed to me that by taking the Tube at the Marble Arch Station I could bring matters to the proof. I have a knack of observing small irrelevant details, and I happened to have noticed that a certain carriage in the train which left Marble Arch about 9:30 stopped exactly opposite the exit at the Chancery Lane Station, and by hurrying up the passage one could just catch the lift which served an earlier train and so reach the street before any of the other travellers." (Ch. V, PN 5)

The plan succeeds: Leithen performs the manoeuvre, conceals himself behind a pillar-box, and watches his breathless pursuer emerge -- confirming that "the espionage was no figment of my brain." Walking up Park Lane thus becomes Leithen's first proactive step in the counter-espionage game.

**The Piccadilly Escape (Ch. IX).** Later in the novel, after a tense encounter in [[belgrave-square]], Leithen again traverses Park Lane -- this time as part of a desperate escape from Lumley's operatives, who are closing in on him through the streets of Mayfair:

> "I emerged in Park Lane and walked down it to Hamilton Place." (Ch. IX, PN 9)

Shortly afterward, he narrowly avoids being struck by a car that swerves at him, dodges "a bus and missing a motor bicycle by a hair's breadth," and reaches the railings of [[green-park]]. The sequence transforms Park Lane from a pleasant morning walk into a gauntlet of danger.

**The Car Journey (Ch. VIII).** Park Lane also appears as a transit route when Leithen, accompanied by [[stagg]], makes a circuit out of London:

> "We went up Park Lane into the Edgeware Road, my instructions to Stagg being to make a circuit by Harrow and Brentford." (Ch. VIII)

## Historical Context

Park Lane in the Edwardian period was already established as one of London's most prestigious addresses. The grand mansions on its eastern side -- such as Dorchester House, Brook House, and Grosvenor House -- were homes to some of Britain's wealthiest families. The road's position between Hyde Park and Mayfair made it a natural thoroughfare for both the fashionable and the pursued. Its broad pavements and clear sightlines, which Leithen exploits for counter-surveillance, also made it a convenient route for motor traffic -- the increasing presence of which is a notable feature of Buchan's London.
