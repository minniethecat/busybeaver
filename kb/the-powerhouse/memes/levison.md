# Mr. Levison

Mr. Levison is a minor character in *The Powerhouse*, a shopkeeper and amateur artist who briefly aids [[edward-leithen|Edward Leithen]] during his desperate flight from [[andrew-lumley|Lumley]]'s operatives in [[leithens-siege|the siege sequence]].

## Role in the Narrative

When Leithen needs to escape from pursuers, he seeks out Mr. Levison's shop. He asks Levison directly whether there is a back door (007-025):

> "Mr. Levison," I said, "have you a back door?" (007-025)

Levison leads Leithen through a yard filled with the debris of picture frames and unlocks a door in the wall, giving him access to a narrow alley. As Leithen slips away, he instructs Levison to deny having seen him (007-028):

> "If anyone inquires, you have not seen me here, remember," I said, and Mr. Levison nodded. (007-028)

## Character

Mr. Levison is described as "an artist in his small way" who "liked the scent of a mystery." He is portrayed as a man of modest talents who finds quiet excitement in being drawn, even briefly, into intrigue.
