# Honour

The concept of honour in *The Powerhouse* represents the traditional reward of the scientific discoverer — the public recognition and prestige that accompanies a contribution to human knowledge. Andrew Lumley's antagonist Sir Edward Leithen invokes honour to argue that true scientific discovery requires publicity, and the novel itself probes whether conventional honour has any hold on a mind like Lumley's.

## Definition

In the novel's terms, honour is paired with "glory" as the natural reward of the discoverer. Leithen articulates this as an axiom of scientific civilisation: "the first thing a discoverer does is to make his discovery public. He wants the honour and glory, and he wants money for it" (003-053). This view treats honour as a social mechanism — the recognition by one's peers that validates a contribution. Lumley, however, operates entirely outside this economy of recognition. The question of whether a man immune to honour can be negotiated with becomes central to the novel's climax.

## Origin in the Text

The word first appears in Leithen's summary of [[charles-pitt-heron|Charles Pitt-Heron]]'s motives for dropping out of sight. Leithen remarks that Pitt-Heron had "all the honour and glory" (002-005) available to a young man of his station, yet had fled — implying that honour is insufficient to bind a man facing existential terror.

The concept re-emerges during the dinner party at [[andrew-lumley|Lumley]]'s house, when Leithen presses his host on why a genuine "powerhouse" of scientific knowledge would remain hidden. Lumley himself wears the outward trappings of honour — "some foreign orders, including the Legion of Honour" (005-018) — yet his public decorations are a mask, an ironic performance for the society he despises.

## Key Passages

> "But surely," I said, "the first thing a discoverer does is to make his discovery public. He wants the honour and glory, and he wants money for it. It becomes part of the world's knowledge, and everything is readjusted to meet it." (003-053)

> He was wearing some foreign orders, including the Legion of Honour, and I could hear in the pause of conversation echoes of his soft, rich voice. (005-018)
