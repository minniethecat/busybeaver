# Parliament Square

## Overview

Parliament Square is a garden square at the northwest corner of the Palace of Westminster in London. Flanked by the Houses of Parliament, Westminster Abbey, and government buildings, it is the symbolic heart of British political life. In *The Powerhouse*, it sets the scene for the very beginning of the adventure.

## In *The Powerhouse*

Parliament Square appears in the opening lines of Chapter I, establishing the contrast that drives the entire narrative. Leithen describes emerging from the House of Commons with Tommy Deloraine on "an afternoon, early in May": "there was greenery in Parliament Square and bits of gay colour, and a light wind was blowing up from the river" (PN 64). He contrasts this with the "dull debate" inside and the "frowsy place" with "its moth-eaten old museum" atmosphere (PN 64, 69). The square's spring freshness -- greenery, colour, river breeze -- represents the world of action and adventure that Tommy craves, while the Commons chamber stands for the stifling routine that both men, in different ways, are about to escape.

## Historical Context

Parliament Square was laid out in the 1860s as part of Sir Charles Barry's redesign of the area around the newly built Palace of Westminster. By the Edwardian period, it was a formal garden square, bordered by the Houses of Parliament, Westminster Abbey, St Margaret's Church, and the Middlesex Guildhall. It was (and remains) a focal point for political demonstrations and a space where the daily rhythms of Parliament -- members arriving, departing, taking the air between debates -- played out against a backdrop of grand Victorian Gothic architecture.
