# The Foreign Office

## Overview

The British Foreign Office is the government department responsible for the United Kingdom's diplomatic relations. In *The Powerhouse*, it forms part of the institutional background against which the novel's international intrigue unfolds.

## In *The Powerhouse*

The Foreign Office is not a direct setting in the novel but is an implicit presence throughout the diplomatic dimension of the plot. [[charles-pitt-heron]]'s disappearance involves a high-powered passport obtained through the Russian Embassy, and the pursuit crosses international borders — Moscow, Bokhara, and the Hissar range. [[felix]], Leithen's contact at the Embassy (most likely [[the-russian-embassy]]), moves comfortably in this diplomatic world, cabling Russian police and coordinating with officials.

The Foreign Office represents the official, institutional approach to international affairs that the [[the-powerhouse-org|Powerhouse]] conspirators circumvent. Lumley's organisation operates entirely outside diplomatic channels — its agents cross borders under false identities, and its assassination plots are carried out far from any embassy's protection. The novel contrasts the slow, proper machinery of Foreign Office diplomacy with the swift, lawless action of the Powerhouse network.

Leithen, himself a Member of Parliament and a figure of the establishment, instinctively works through official channels — the Embassy, [[scotland-yard]] — but the novel demonstrates that these institutions are insufficient against the Powerhouse's methods. It takes Leithen's personal courage, not Foreign Office cables, to save Pitt-Heron.
