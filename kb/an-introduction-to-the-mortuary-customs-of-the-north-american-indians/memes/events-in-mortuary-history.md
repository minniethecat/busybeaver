The mortuary customs documented in Yarrow's 1880 monograph did not exist in isolation. They were observed, recorded, and transformed through major historical events — from early Spanish expeditions that first encountered elaborate burial mounds, to the forced relocations of the 19th century that disrupted traditional funerary practices across the continent. This page indexes all documented historical events that intersect with Native American mortuary customs.

## Chronological Context

The events documented here span from the 16th-century Spanish entradas through the 19th-century reservation era. The earliest European accounts of Native American burial practices come from expedition chronicles, while later observations were made by military officers and ethnographers attached to government surveys and removals. Each event page links to the tribes and burial practices affected by or documented during that historical moment.
