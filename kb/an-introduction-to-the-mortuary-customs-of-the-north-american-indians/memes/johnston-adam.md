# Adam Johnston

## Overview

Adam Johnston was a nineteenth-century contributor to [[schoolcraft-henry-rowe|Henry Rowe Schoolcraft]]'s monumental *Historical and Statistical Information Respecting the Indian Tribes of the United States* (1851–1857), the most ambitious ethnographic compilation undertaken by the United States government before the founding of the Bureau of American Ethnology. Johnston's contribution on the Bonaks, or root-diggers, was singled out by Yarrow for its record of a [[cremation]] origin-myth — one of the few accounts in the 1880 monograph that addresses not merely the mechanics but the mythological rationale for the practice of [[cremation|burning]] the dead (010-005).

## Contributions

Johnston's principal contribution to Yarrow's chapter on [[cremation]] is the record of a Bonak myth explaining the origin of corpse-[[cremation|burning]]. According to Johnston's account, the first Indians were coyotes. When one of their number died, the body became filled with small animal-like spirits that crawled over the corpse and then assumed the shapes of deer, elk, antelope, and other creatures. A great many of these spirits, however, took wings and began flying toward the moon. The original coyote-Indians, fearing that the earth would become depopulated if spirits continued to escape, decreed that henceforth the bodies of the dead must be burned. The practice of [[cremation]] was thus instituted — according to this tradition — as a measure to contain the spirit population within the terrestrial realm (010-005).

The account holds particular ethnographic interest because it represents a rare instance in which an Indigenous informant's own mythological explanation for a mortuary practice was recorded verbatim. Johnston appears to have transcribed the narrative directly rather than summarizing or paraphrasing, preserving the characteristic flatness of mythic narration — the matter-of-fact register in which coyotes, animal spirits, and lunar flight are recounted without rhetorical embellishment (010-005).

## Accounts Provided

Johnston, through [[schoolcraft-henry-rowe|Schoolcraft]]'s 1854 volume (Part IV, page 224), contributed the Bonak [[cremation]] origin-myth to the documentary record available to Yarrow. No additional accounts from Johnston appear in the 1880 monograph.
