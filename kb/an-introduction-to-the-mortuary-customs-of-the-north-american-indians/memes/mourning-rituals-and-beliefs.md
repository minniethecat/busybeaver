Mortuary customs among Native American tribes were never purely technical — they were embedded in elaborate systems of mourning, ritual performance, and supernatural belief. Widows wept for prescribed periods, professional mourners were hired, food was offered to the dead, and ghosts were feared or propitiated. This page aggregates all pages documenting the ritual and belief dimensions of Native American deathways.

## About This Index

The boundaries between "mourning," "ritual," and "superstition" in Yarrow's sources are often blurry — a widow's self-mutilation is simultaneously grief, ritual obligation, and spirit-appeasement. Pages may appear in multiple query blocks above where their content spans these categories.
