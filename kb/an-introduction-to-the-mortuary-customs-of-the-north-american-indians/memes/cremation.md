# Cremation

## Overview

Cremation was among the common modes of disposing of the dead among North American Indians, documented across tribes from [[california|California]] to the Great Lakes region. Yarrow (1880) collected numerous accounts from military officers, physicians, and explorers describing the practice.

## Tribal Practices

### California Tribes
The [[nishinam|Nishinam]] of [[california|California]] practiced cremation as described by different correspondents, with accounts varying in detail regarding the ceremony and its significance (010-001)(010-003).

### Chinooks
Among the [[chinook|Chinook]] people of the Pacific Northwest, cremation was the primary mode of burial, distinct from the inhumation practices of neighboring tribes (010-004).

## Variations

Different tribes practiced cremation with varying rituals — some burned the dead with their possessions, others combined cremation with scaffold burial or canoe burial. The practice often involved ceremonial feasting and [[mourning]] rituals (010-006).
