# Caleb Atwater

## Overview

Caleb Atwater (1778–1867) was an American antiquarian, lawyer, and postmaster of Circleville, [[ohio|Ohio]]. His archaeological investigations of the burial mounds and earthworks of the [[ohio|Ohio Valley]], published in the *Transactions of the American Antiquarian Society* (1820), provided some of the earliest systematic documentation of prehistoric Native American mortuary structures in the region (004-005).

## Contributions

Atwater's description of the burial mounds of [[ohio|Ohio]] was the primary source for Yarrow's chapter on mound burial. Atwater excavated a tumulus near the center of a round fortification, finding it to be about 10 feet in height and several rods in diameter. His work documented the stratified nature of these mounds — often containing multiple burials at different levels — and helped establish that the mounds were constructed over extended periods with successive [[inhumation|interment]]s. His observations were cited as authoritative by later researchers including Foster and [[schoolcraft-henry-rowe|Schoolcraft]] (004-005)(004-006).

## Accounts Provided

Atwater provided detailed measurements and descriptions of mound structures in the [[ohio|Ohio Valley]], noting the presence of grave goods, the orientation of bodies, and the evidence of secondary burials within the mounds. His work was among the first to recognize that the mounds represented deliberate mortuary architecture rather than natural formations, laying groundwork for the study of the [[mound-builders]] (004-005).
