# Hubert H. Bancroft

## Overview

Hubert Howe Bancroft (1832–1918) was an American historian and ethnologist whose monumental *Native Races of the Pacific States* (1874–1876) provided Yarrow with extensive documentation of mortuary customs among the indigenous peoples of western North America, Central America, and the Pacific coast. His work bridged the gap between military reports and systematic ethnology (016-003)(017-001).

## Contributions

Bancroft's *Native Races* was among the most frequently cited secondary sources in Yarrow's survey. His documentation of canoe burial among the Indians of the Mosquito Coast — where the corpse was wrapped in cloth and placed in one half of a pitpan (dugout canoe) cut in two — was a key reference for the chapter on superterrene and aerial burial. Bancroft also documented the aquatic burial practices of the [[Itzas]] of Guatemala, who cast their dead into Lake Peter, and the burial customs of tribes from [[alaska|Alaska]] to Central America, providing Yarrow with a hemispheric context for North American practices (016-003)(017-001)(017-002).

## Accounts Provided

Bancroft's work compiled accounts from Spanish missionaries, early explorers, and military reports, synthesizing them into a comprehensive survey of indigenous customs. For Yarrow, Bancroft served as a crucial source for customs of tribes in regions not directly visited by the U.S. Army medical officers and Indian agents who provided most of the primary data (016-003).
