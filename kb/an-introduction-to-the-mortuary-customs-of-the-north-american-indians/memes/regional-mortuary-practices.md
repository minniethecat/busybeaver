Yarrow's 1880 survey documents striking regional patterns in Native American mortuary customs. The mound-building cultures of the Southeast differed fundamentally from the scaffold burials of the Great Plains, the cave interments of the Southwest, and the canoe burials of the Pacific Northwest. This page organizes all documented tribes, persons, and practices by geographic culture area, allowing readers to explore the regional logic of indigenous deathways.
