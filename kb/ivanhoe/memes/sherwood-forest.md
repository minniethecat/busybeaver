# Sherwood Forest

Sherwood Forest（舍伍德森林）是诺丁汉郡著名的森林，在小说的义勇军情节线中作为罗宾汉（Locksley）及其部下活动的自然场景出现。

## 地理位置与描述

> “Let your grace never trust me else,” answered the master of the spies. “One is from Hexamshire; he is wont to trace the Tynedale and Teviotdale thieves, as a bloodhound follows the slot of a hurt deer. The other is Yorkshire bred, and has twanged his bowstring right oft in merry Sherwood; he knows each glade and dingle, copse and high-wood, betwixt this and Richmond.”（034-071）

> “Call me no longer Locksley, my Liege, but know me under the name, which, I fear, fame hath blown too widely not to have reached even your royal ears—I am Robin Hood of Sherwood Forest.” [561](3192617847664551881_82-h-57.htm.html#linknote-561)（040-120）

Sherwood Forest 是英格兰中部一片广阔的皇家森林，在12世纪是 outlaw（逃犯和 outlaw）的传统藏身之处。小说中，罗宾汉及其"快活伙伴"（Merry Men）以此地为基地。

## 历史/现实依据

> The royal forest of Sherwood, in which the famous outlaw Robin Hood and his Merry Men traditionally operated, covers a vast tract of country in Nottinghamshire.（034-071）

舍伍德森林在中世纪英格兰是皇家狩猎林（royal forest），受特别法律保护。罗宾汉传说与此地紧密关联，反映了中世纪英格兰 outlaw 文化的真实一面。
