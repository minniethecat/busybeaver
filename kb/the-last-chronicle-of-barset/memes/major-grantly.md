## Character

Major Henry Grantly is the son of Archdeacon Grantly of Plumstead and one of the central romantic figures in *The Last Chronicle of Barset*. A widowed army officer living at Cosby Lodge, he falls in love with Grace Crawley, the daughter of the disgraced clergyman Josiah Crawley (002-095). His determination to marry Grace despite his family's opposition and the scandal surrounding her father forms one of the novel's principal plot lines.

## Relationships

Grantly's relationship with Grace Crawley places him in direct conflict with his father, Archdeacon Grantly, and his stepmother, Mrs. Grantly, who regard the match as socially disastrous. His loyalty to Grace — maintained even as her father faces trial for theft — demonstrates a moral courage that transcends the social calculations of his class. The Grantly family's eventual acceptance of Grace marks one of the novel's key resolutions.

## Significance

Major Grantly represents the younger generation's challenge to the rigid social hierarchies of Barsetshire. His willingness to marry beneath his station — and to defy his formidable father — dramatises Trollope's theme of personal integrity over social convention. His military background and widower status also distinguish him from the clerical figures who dominate the series, offering a secular perspective on the questions of honour and reputation that pervade the novel.
