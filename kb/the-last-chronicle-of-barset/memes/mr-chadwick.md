## Character

Mr. Chadwick is the ecclesiastical secretary or man of business at the Bishop's Palace in Barchester, serving as the administrative intermediary between Bishop Proudie, Mrs. Proudie, and the diocesan clergy. He is a functionary rather than a policymaker — his role is to execute instructions, not to shape them — but Trollope sketches him with enough individuality to suggest a man who understands the awkwardness of his position. "But, my dear, Mr. Chadwick can do nothing," the bishop tells Mrs. Proudie, trying to shield his secretary from her demands (062-163). When Mrs. Proudie, in her frustration at Dr. Tempest's deliberate slowness, insists on summoning Chadwick directly, "in her anger she did" write to him, demanding his presence (062-164).

## Biography

Chadwick operates in the shadow of the Proudies, carrying out the administrative work of the diocese under the bishop's — and more often Mrs. Proudie's — direction. His role in the Crawley affair is to facilitate the ecclesiastical inquiry that Mrs. Proudie is determined to pursue. "Then I will see Mr. Chadwick," Mrs. Proudie declares when the bishop proves uncooperative, and she writes "a note to Mr. Chadwick, begging him to come over" (062-165). Chadwick's position is unenviable: he must serve his ecclesiastical masters while maintaining working relationships with the diocesan clergy who are the targets of episcopal discipline. His brief appearances in the novel suggest a man who navigates these tensions with professional discretion.

## Significance

Mr. Chadwick is a minor but telling figure in the novel's anatomy of ecclesiastical power. He represents the permanent administration that persists beneath the comings and goings of bishops — the clerks and secretaries who actually write the letters, keep the records, and implement (or quietly delay) the policies handed down from above. His reluctance to be drawn into Mrs. Proudie's campaign against Crawley, and the bishop's protective "Mr. Chadwick can do nothing," suggest that even the palace functionaries recognize the injustice of the proceedings they are asked to facilitate. In this, Chadwick is one of many small figures of institutional resistance that collectively slow the Proudies' pursuit of Crawley.
