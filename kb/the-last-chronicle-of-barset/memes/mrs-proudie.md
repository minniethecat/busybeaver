## Character

Mrs. Proudie is one of the most memorable figures in the Barsetshire novels — the Bishop's wife who effectively rules the diocese through her husband. Her forceful personality and determination to control church appointments make her a formidable antagonist. She features prominently in *The Last Chronicle of Barset*, where her clash with Josiah Crawley over his fate exemplifies her characteristic blend of moral certainty and personal vindictiveness.

## Significance

Mrs. Proudie represents the unofficial power structures within the Victorian Church of England. Though barred by gender from holding ecclesiastical office, she exercises immense influence through her husband. Her campaign against Crawley — motivated by her conviction of his guilt and her determination to purify the clergy — dramatises the tension between institutional authority and individual justice. Trollope uses her to critique the conflation of personal ambition with moral crusading in church governance.
