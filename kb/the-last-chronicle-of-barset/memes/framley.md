## Overview

Framley is a significant estate and parish in Barsetshire, centred on Framley Hall, the seat of the Lufton family (008-059). Lady Lufton, the matriarch of Framley, is one of the most influential figures in the county, exercising considerable sway over ecclesiastical appointments and social affairs. The parish and its great house represent the established order of rural Victorian society — hierarchical, interconnected, and resistant to change.

## Significance

Framley functions in the Barsetshire novels as a symbol of traditional landed authority. Unlike the ecclesiastical centre of Barchester or the impoverished parish of Hogglestock, Framley embodies the stable, paternalistic ideal of English country life. Lady Lufton's influence from Framley Hall extends across the county, and her interventions in the Crawley affair illustrate how personal relationships among the gentry shape institutional outcomes. The estate's appearance across multiple novels in the series — it gives its name to the fourth Barsetshire novel, *Framley Parsonage* — marks it as one of Trollope's most fully realised settings.
