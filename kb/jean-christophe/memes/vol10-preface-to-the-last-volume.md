# Preface to the Last Volume of Jean-Christophe

### Preface to the Last Volume of *Jean-Christophe*

[037-001]I have written the tragedy of a generation which is nearing its end. I have sought to conceal neither its vices nor its virtues, its profound sadness, its chaotic pride, its heroic efforts, its despondency beneath the overwhelming burden of a superhuman task, the burden of the whole world, the reconstruction of the world’s morality, its esthetic principles, its [[religion|faith]], the forging of a new humanity.—Such we have been.

[037-002]You young men, you men of today, march over us, trample us under your feet, and press onward. Be ye greater and happier than we. For myself, I bid the soul that was mine farewell. I cast it from me like an empty shell. Life is a succession of deaths and resurrections. We must die, [[jean-christophe-krafft|Christophe]], to be born again.

[037-003]Romain Rolland.

[037-004]October, 1912.
