# Dorcas Society

## Overview

The Dorcas Society is the charitable sewing circle that represents organized female piety in Baslehurst. Named for the biblical Dorcas (Tabitha), who was "full of good works and almsdeeds," the society brings together the town's respectable women to make garments for the poor. For Mrs. Prime, the Dorcas meetings are a spiritual duty and a social credential—proof that her religion is practical as well as doctrinal. The society is part of the novel's quiet mapping of female public life: in a world where women cannot vote, hold office, or conduct business, charitable work provides one of the few legitimate arenas for female organization and influence.

## In the Novel

### Female Public Space

The Dorcas Society occupies an intermediate space between the domestic and the public. It is not a home (like Bragg's End) or a social event (like the ball) or a religious service (like church). It is women meeting to do practical good, and in doing so, creating a network of mutual observation and influence that parallels the masculine networks of the inn and the counting-house.

### Mrs. Prime's Arena

For Mrs. Prime, the Dorcas Society is where her piety can be exercised in company with like-minded women. It reinforces her identity as a serious Christian and provides a context in which her severity is valued rather than resented. The meetings also offer opportunities for conversations about Rachel's spiritual state—charitable work and sisterly surveillance go hand in hand.
