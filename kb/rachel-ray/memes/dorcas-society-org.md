# The Dorcas Society

## Overview

The Dorcas Society is the charitable sewing circle that represents organized female philanthropy in Baslehurst. Named for the biblical Dorcas (Tabitha), who was "full of good works and almsdeeds," the society channels female piety into practical action. For Mrs. Prime, the Dorcas meetings are more than charity—they are an arena for female influence and a validation of her religious identity. In a world where women cannot hold office, practice professions, or participate in formal politics, the Dorcas Society provides one of the few legitimate spaces for female organization. Trollope treats this with his characteristic even-handedness: the society does genuine good, and it also provides a context in which female surveillance and social control operate under the cover of charitable purpose.

## In the Novel

### Female Organization

The Dorcas Society is entirely female—organized by women, attended by women, serving women (and their families). It represents a sphere of female activity parallel to the male institutions of the inn, the counting-house, and the brewery. Its meetings are occasions for work, conversation, and the exchange of information that flows through Baslehurst's female networks.

### Mrs. Prime's Arena

For Mrs. Prime, the Dorcas Society is where her severity is valued rather than resented. Among like-minded women, her piety is a credential, not a burden. The meetings also provide a context for conversations about Rachel's spiritual state—charitable work and sisterly concern intertwine.
