# Exeter

## Overview

Exeter is the regional capital that hovers in the background of Baslehurst life—a place of lawyers, courts, and larger commercial opportunities that represents the wider world beyond the provincial town. Mr. Ray practiced law there before his death; legal business concerning the brewery dispute may be transacted there; and the city's existence reminds the reader that Baslehurst, for all its self-contained drama, is part of a larger social and economic geography.

## In the Novel

### The Legal and Commercial Center

Exeter appears in the novel primarily as a point of reference. When legal matters require a higher authority than Mr. Comfort's informal mediation, Exeter's courts and lawyers are invoked. The city's commercial infrastructure—banks, legal offices, wholesale suppliers—forms the invisible network that supports Baslehurst's visible economy.

### Mr. Ray's Legacy

Rachel's father was "a steady, sober, hardworking, earnest man, well esteemed in the world"(001-004), a lawyer in Exeter whose death removed the family from the city to the cottage at Bragg's End. The move from Exeter to Baslehurst is a move from professional urban life to provincial domesticity, and Rachel's eventual marriage to Luke Rowan—a London man with commercial ambitions—may represent a return to the wider world from which her mother retreated.
