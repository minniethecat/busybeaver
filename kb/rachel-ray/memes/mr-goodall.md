## Overview

Mr. Goodall is a financial advisor based in the Close at Baslehurst, who assists Mrs. Ray in managing her financial affairs following the death of her husband. He advises on the sale of the family's small property holdings and the reinvestment of the proceeds, serving as a practical link between the domestic world of Bragg's End and the commercial machinery of the town.

## Biography

Mr. Goodall operates from an office in the Close in Baslehurst, where he receives clients and conducts financial business. After Mr. Ray's death, he advises the widowed Mrs. Ray on the disposition of her "little estate" [021-021], recommending sale and reinvestment.

When Mrs. Ray visits his office, she brings her daughter Rachel, who observes the transaction with the critical eye of youth. Goodall is businesslike and direct: he tells Mrs. Ray that they "have got our money" [021-065] after completing the sale, treating the matter as a straightforward commercial transaction rather than the emotional milestone it represents for the Ray family.

Mrs. Ray wishes Goodall "could make it ten" [021-041]—a slightly better rate on her investment—suggesting the modest scale of the Rays' financial world and Goodall's position as the gatekeeper of what little prosperity they possess.

## Relationships

### Mrs. Ray

Goodall serves as Mrs. Ray's trusted financial adviser. She relies on his guidance for major decisions—selling property, investing proceeds—and defers to his professional judgment. Their relationship is formal and transactional, conducted through office visits and correspondence.

### Rachel Ray

Rachel accompanies her mother to Goodall's office and observes the proceedings with a mixture of curiosity and suspicion. She presses her mother about who else she saw during the visit [021-042], sensing that financial business in the Close is never purely financial.

## Key Scenes

- **Advising the Sale** (Chapter 21) — Goodall recommends that Mrs. Ray "part with her little estate" [021-021], setting the financial plot in motion.
- **The Office Visit** (Chapter 21) — Mrs. Ray and Rachel visit Goodall's office in the Close to sign papers. Goodall is described as efficiently concluding the business [021-065].
- **Mrs. Ray's Second Visit** (Chapter 21) — Mrs. Ray sees Goodall and also someone else—a fact Rachel's questioning reveals [021-068], hinting at the social connections that intersect with financial dealings in Baslehurst.

## Character Analysis

Mr. Goodall is one of several minor professional figures—alongside Mr. Honyman the lawyer and Mr. Sharpit the attorney—who populate the commercial infrastructure of Baslehurst. As a financial advisor, he represents the world of practical calculation that stands in contrast to the novel's romantic plot. His office in the Close, the administrative and ecclesiastical centre of the town, signals his respectability and his integration into the established order. Goodall's role is entirely functional: he exists to facilitate Mrs. Ray's financial decisions, yet in doing so he anchors the domestic story in the material realities of Victorian provincial life.
