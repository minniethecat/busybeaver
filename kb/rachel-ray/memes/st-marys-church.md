# St. Mary's Church

## Overview

St. Mary's Church—the parish church of Baslehurst—represents the institutional face of moderate Anglicanism in Trollope's novel. Under the pastoral care of Mr. Comfort (vicar) and with Mr. Prong (curate) providing the Evangelical alternative, the church is the spiritual institution that structures Baslehurst's religious life. Its services, its sermons, and its social authority form the background against which Mrs. Prime's private piety and the Ray family's domestic religion are measured. Trollope, who had complex feelings about the Church of England—he admired its moderation while recognizing its complacency—presents St. Mary's as a functional institution that serves its parish adequately if not heroically.

## In the Novel

### Moderate Anglicanism

Mr. Comfort's ministry represents the Anglican via media—a Christianity that is serious without being severe, pastoral without being intrusive. His advice to Mrs. Ray about Rachel's courtship ("he asked after Luke Rowan's worldly means"(005-042)) is characteristically practical: marriage is a good thing, young people should marry, and financial prudence is a Christian virtue.

### The Curate's Challenge

Mr. Prong's presence as curate introduces a theological tension within the parish. His Evangelical severity appeals to Mrs. Prime and others of similar inclination, creating an alternative spiritual pole within the institutional church. This internal diversity—Anglican and Evangelical worshipping under the same roof—reflects the actual state of the Victorian Church of England.

### Social Function

St. Mary's is as much a social institution as a spiritual one. The Sunday service assembles the town in a ritual of communal visibility; baptisms, marriages, and funerals mark the passages of provincial life; and the churchyard provides the setting for Rachel and Luke's first private conversation.
