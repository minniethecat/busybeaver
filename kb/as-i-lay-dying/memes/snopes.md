# Snopes

The **Snopes** family is a recurring presence across Faulkner's Yoknapatawpha fiction. In *As I Lay Dying*, the name appears in connection with the horses the Bundrens use for their journey — descendants of Texas ponies that Flem Snopes brought into the county.

## The Snopes Horses

Jewel's spotted horse is identified early in the novel as "one of them Snopes horses" (ch022):

> It was a descendant of those Texas ponies Flem Snopes brought here twenty-five years ago and auctioned off for two dollars a head.

The Snopes horses are wild, untamed creatures — fitting mounts for Jewel, whose fierce independence mirrors their own. The horse's lineage through the Snopes trade connects Jewel's private act of acquisition to the broader economic world of Yoknapatawpha county.

## Anse's Trade with Snopes

When the Bundrens need a new team after the river crossing, Anse turns to Snopes:

> "You might try Snopes," I said. "He's got three-four span. Maybe one of them would suit you."

The subsequent negotiation becomes a minor comic set-piece. Anse, who has never demonstrated any competence in practical matters, trades Jewel's horse — acquired through months of secret labor — for a team of mules. He returns boasting that he "beat Snopes in a trade," though the mules are of dubious quality:

> "Snopes must have fed them on sawdust," Darl observes.

## Faulknerian Context

The Snopes name carries weight across Faulkner's work. Flem Snopes is the central figure of the "Snopes trilogy" (*The Hamlet*, *The Town*, *The Mansion*), representing the rise of amoral commercialism in the post-Reconstruction South. In *As I Lay Dying*, the brief appearance of the Snopes name anchors the novel within this larger fictional universe and introduces the theme of economic exchange as a moral transaction.
