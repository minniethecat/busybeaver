## Reading the Rankings

Faulkner's Yoknapatawpha County is rendered through the perceptual immediacy of his narrators rather than through omniscient description. A place's reference count reflects not just physical presence but the degree to which it saturates characters' consciousness — the Bundren farm, the flooded river, and the Jefferson road are as much psychological landscapes as physical ones.
