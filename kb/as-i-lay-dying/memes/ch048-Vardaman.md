# Vardaman

## Vardaman

[044-001]Now there are seven of them, in little tall black circles.

[044-002]“Look, [[darl-bundren|Darl]],” I say; “see?”

[044-003]He looks up. We watch them in little tall black circles of not-moving.

[044-004]“Yesterday there were just four,” I say.

[044-005]There were more than four on the barn.

[044-006]“Do you know what I would do if he tries to light on the wagon again?” I say.

[044-007]“What would you do?” Darl says.

[044-008]“I wouldn’t let him light on her,” I say. “I wouldn’t let him light on [[cash-bundren|Cash]], either.”

[044-009]Cash is sick. He is sick on [[addies-coffin|the box]]. But my [[motherhood|mother]] is a fish.

[044-010]“We got to get some medicine in Mottson,” [[anse-bundren|pa]] says. “I reckon we’ll just have to.”

[044-011]“How do you feel, Cash?” Darl says.

[044-012]“It don’t bother none,” Cash says.

[044-013]“Do you want it propped a little higher?” Darl says.

[044-014]Cash has a broken leg. He has had two broken legs. He lies on the box with a quilt rolled under his head and a piece of wood under his knee.

[044-015]“I reckon we ought to left him at Armstid’s,” pa says.

[044-016]I haven’t got a broken leg and pa hasn’t and Darl hasn’t and “It’s just the bumps,” Cash says. “It kind of grinds together a little on a bump. I don’t bother none.” [[jewel-bundren|Jewel]] *has gone away. He and his horse went away one supper time.*

[044-017]“It’s because she wouldn’t have us beholden,” pa says. “ ’Fore [[religion-and-faith|God]], I do the best that ere a man.” *Is it because Jewel’s mother is a horse, Darl? I said.*

[044-018]“Maybe I can draw the ropes a little tighter,” Darl says. *That’s why Jewel and I were both in the shed and she was in the wagon because [[jewels-horse|the horse]] lives in the barn and I had to keep on running the bustard away from*

[044-019]“If you just would,” Cash says. And Dewey Dell hasn’t got a broken leg and I haven’t. Cash is my brother.

[044-020]We stop. When Darl loosens the rope Cash begins to sweat again. His teeth look out.

[044-021]“Hurt?” Darl says.

[044-022]“I reckon you better put it back,” Cash says.

[044-023]Darl puts the rope back, pulling hard. Cash’s teeth look out.

[044-024]“Hurt?” Darl says.

[044-025]“It don’t bother none,” Cash says.

[044-026]“Do you want pa to drive slower?” Darl says.

[044-027]“No,” Cash says. “Ain’t no time to hang back. It don’t bother none.”

[044-028]“We’ll have to get some medicine at Mottson,” pa says. “I reckon we’ll have to.”

[044-029]“Tell him to go on,” Cash says. We go on. Dewey Dell leans back and wipes Cash’s face. Cash is my brother. *But Jewel’s mother is a horse. My mother is a fish. Darl says that when we come to the [[water-and-river|water]] again I might see her and Dewey Dell said, She’s in the box; how could she have got out? She got out through the holes I bored, into the water I said, and when we come to the water again I am going to see her. My mother is not in the box. My mother does not smell like that. My mother is a fish.*

[044-030]“Those cakes will be in fine shape by the time we get to [[jefferson|Jefferson]],” Darl says.

[044-031]Dewey Dell does not look around.

[044-032]“You better try to sell them in Mottson,” Darl says.

[044-033]“When will we get to Mottson, Darl?” I say.

[044-034]“Tomorrow,” Darl says. “If this team don’t tack to pieces. Snopes must have fed them on sawdust.”

[044-035]“Why did he feed them on sawdust, Darl?” I say.

[044-036]“Look,” Darl says. “See?”

[044-037]Now there are nine of them, tall in little tall black circles.

[044-038]When we come to the foot of the hill pa stops and Darl and Dewey Dell and I get out. Cash can’t walk because he has a broken leg. “Come up, mules,” pa says. The mules walk hard; the wagon creaks. Darl and Dewey Dell and I walk behind the wagon, up the hill. When we come to the top of the hill pa stops and we get back into the wagon.

[044-039]Now there are ten of them, tall in little tall black circles on the sky.
