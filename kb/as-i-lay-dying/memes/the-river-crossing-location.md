# The River Crossing

The [[water-and-river|flood]]ed [[water-and-river|river]] ford is the [[the-journey|journey]]'s crisis point — the place where the abstract imperatives of [[family-duty|duty]] and [[family-duty|family obligation]] meet the brute physical reality of [[water-and-river|water]] in [[water-and-river|flood]], and where the [[the-journey|journey]] nearly ends in disaster.

## Description

[[darl-bundren|Darl]]'s narration of the river is characteristically precise and hallucinatory: "the still surface of the [[water-and-river|water]] like a tremendous completely effortless piece of magic" (023-001). The [[water-and-river|river]] is swollen by days of [[water-and-river|rain]], its ford obliterated, its currents strong enough to sweep away [[the-mule-team|mules]] and [[the-bundren-wagon|wagon]] alike. Faulkner's description emphasizes the [[water-and-river|water]]'s [[anse-bundren|pa]]radoxical nature — still on the surface, lethal beneath — as a correlative for the [[the-journey|journey]] itself, which appears purposeful but teems with hidden dangers.

## Role in the Novel

The [[the-river-crossing-event|river crossing]] is the novel's central symbolic event. It functions as a trial by [[water-and-river|water]] that reveals each character's essential nature: [[jewel-bundren|Jewel]]'s furious courage, [[darl-bundren|Darl]]'s detached observation, [[cash-bundren|Cash]]'s stoic endurance, [[anse-bundren|Anse]]'s [[anse-bundren|pa]]ssive self-preservation. The [[water-and-river|crossing]] nearly kills [[cash-bundren|Cash]] (his leg is broken), drowns the [[the-mule-team|mules]], and almost sweeps away [[addies-coffin|the coffin]] — the entire mission hangs on [[jewel-bundren|Jewel]]'s physical intervention. The [[water-and-river|crossing]] transforms the [[the-journey|journey]] from a difficult [[family-duty|duty]] into a near-tragedy, rai[[sin-and-judgment|sin]]g the [[the-journey|quest]]ion of whether the [[family-duty|promise]] to [[addie-bundren|Addie]] is worth the destruction it causes.

## Key Scenes

The [[water-and-river|crossing]] itself is narrated primarily by [[darl-bundren|Darl]] (023) with supplementary perspectives from [[cash-bundren|Cash]] and [[vernon-tull|Tull]]. The sequence — the [[the-bundren-wagon|wagon]] tipping, the [[the-mule-team|mules]] [[water-and-river|drowning]], [[cash-bundren|Cash]] clinging to [[addies-coffin|the coffin]] with his [[cashs-broken-leg|broken leg]], [[jewel-bundren|Jewel]] diving into the current to save [[addie-bundren|Addie]]'s [[the-body|body]] — is one of the most viscerally rendered action sequences in American literature. The aftermath, with [[cash-bundren|Cash]] lying on the far bank, his leg "swollen like a dead horse's leg" and the family regrouping around their diminished resources, marks the [[the-journey|journey]]'s point of no return.
