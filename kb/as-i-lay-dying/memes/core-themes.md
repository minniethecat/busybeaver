# Core Themes

This page ranks thematic concepts in *As I Lay Dying* by total reference count, mapping which ideas Faulkner returns to most persistently across the novel's fifty-nine chapters and fifteen narrators.

## Reading the Rankings

High reference counts indicate themes that recur across multiple chapters and narrators — ideas that structure the novel's polyphonic consciousness rather than belonging to any single character's perspective. Death, identity, family obligation, and the failure of language are among the concerns that Faulkner's narrators circle obsessively.
