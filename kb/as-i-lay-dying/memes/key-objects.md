## Reading the Rankings

In Faulkner's world, objects are never merely functional. The coffin that Cash builds with fastidious care, the wagon that carries it, the tools that define a man's identity — these become sites where the novel's deepest concerns (mortality, labor, family obligation, the inadequacy of language) take material form.
