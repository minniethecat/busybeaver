# The Cow

The Bundren family's dairy cow, whose distressed lowing and swollen udder become a recurring presence in the novel's first half, [[anse-bundren|pa]]rticularly in [[dewey-dell-bundren|Dewey Dell Bundren]]'s consciousness. The cow serves as [[dewey-dell-bundren|Dewey Dell]]'s animal double — a female [[the-body|body]] defined by its reproductive and productive functions, whose distress is expressed physically rather than verbally.

## Description and Role

The cow is a standard dairy animal on the Bundren farm. After [[addies-death|Addie's death]], the milking duties fall to [[dewey-dell-bundren|Dewey Dell]] — a detail that [[darl-bundren|Darl]] notes in his narration — and the cow's swollen, unmilked udder becomes a source of physical urgency that [[anse-bundren|pa]]rallels [[dewey-dell-bundren|Dewey Dell]]'s own bodily condition. In [[dewey-dell-bundren|Dewey Dell]]'s monologue (chapter 7), the cow's need and her own pregnancy blur into a [[sin-and-judgment|sin]]gle sensation of bodily pressure and entrapment:

> "The cow is lowing, her head turned toward the door, the slow warm milk rising in her, moaning. It is not her udder that aches. It is the whole sun-plunge, the body suspended in a warm sea of light. I am a little tub of guts and there is room for something else very small laying quiet and wait" (007-001).

## The Cow as Double

The cow functions as [[dewey-dell-bundren|Dewey Dell]]'s animal double in several registers:

- **The reproductive [[the-body|body]]**: Both [[dewey-dell-bundren|Dewey Dell]] and the cow are defined by what their bodies contain — milk in one, a fetus in the other — and both experience this containment as a claim upon them that cannot be refused

- **Expression without [[language-and-meaning|language]]**: The cow's lowing is the novel's purest instance of suffering expressed without the mediation of [[language-and-meaning|language]] — a form of [[language-and-meaning|communication]] that [[dewey-dell-bundren|Dewey Dell]]'s own narrative voice, with its resistance to abstraction, aspires to

- **Domestic economy**: The cow, like [[dewey-dell-bundren|Dewey Dell]], exists within the Bundren household as a productive [[the-body|body]] — its milk feeds the family, its reproduction maintains the herd — and this economic framing of the female [[the-body|body]] is one of the novel's implicit critiques

## Faulkner's Animal Consciousness

The cow is [[anse-bundren|pa]]rt of the novel's larger [[anse-bundren|pa]]ttern of animal presence — the [[the-mule-team|mules]], [[jewel-bundren|Jewel]]'s horse, [[vardaman-bundren|Vardaman]]'s fish, the [[the-buzzards|buzzards]] — through which Faulkner explores forms of consciousness that exist outside [[language-and-meaning|language]]. Unlike [[jewel-bundren|Jewel]]'s horse, which is individualized through its relationship with a specific human, the cow remains anonymous and generic, defined entirely by its biological function. This anonymity is the point: the cow represents the condition of being a [[the-body|body]] in the world without the compensatory fiction of a self.
