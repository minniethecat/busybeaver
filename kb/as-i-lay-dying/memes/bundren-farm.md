# Bundren Farm

The Bundren farm is the novel's point of origin — the place [[addie-bundren|Addie Bundren]] lived, died, and de[[anse-bundren|pa]]rted from. Perched on a hillside, it is a place of poverty and stubborn survival, its landscape shaped by the family that [[work-and-craft|work]]s it and neglects it in equal measure.

## Description

[[darl-bundren|Darl]]'s opening description of the farm establishes the novel's visual and thematic vocabulary: "[[jewel-bundren|Jewel]] and I come up from the field, following the [[anse-bundren|pa]]th in [[sin-and-judgment|sin]]gle file" (001-001). The cotton house, "with a broken roof set at a [[sin-and-judgment|sin]]gle pitch," leans "in empty and shimmering dilapidation in the sunlight" (001-003). This image of a structure barely standing, held together by habit rather than re[[anse-bundren|pa]]ir, mirrors the Bundren family itself. The farm's geography — the bluff, the bottom field, the [[anse-bundren|pa]]th to the barn, the [[the-journey|road]] — becomes the stage for the novel's opening drama, with [[cash-bundren|Cash]] sawing boards for [[addies-coffin|the coffin]] within sight of [[addie-bundren|Addie]]'s window.

## Role in the Novel

The farm is the fixed point against which the [[the-journey|journey]] is measured. It represents the world the Bundrens know — a world of routine, poverty, and the rhythms of rural [[work-and-craft|labor]]. Leaving it means entering a world where their grief becomes spectacle and their private rituals become public scandal. The novel's tension between the farm (home, known, private) and the [[the-journey|road]] (foreign, exposed, public) structures the entire narrative arc. When the [[the-journey|journey]] ends in [[jefferson|Jefferson]], no one returns to the farm — the family is reconstituted in a new form, with a new Mrs. Bundren, as if the farm itself belonged only to the world that contained [[addie-bundren|Addie]].

## Key Scenes

The farm is the setting for the novel's crucial early scenes: [[cash-bundren|Cash]] building [[addies-coffin|the coffin]] at [[addie-bundren|Addie]]'s window (001), [[jewel-bundren|Jewel]] and [[darl-bundren|Darl]] de[[anse-bundren|pa]]rting for the lumber haul despite [[addie-bundren|Addie]]'s condition (001-003), [[cora-tull|Cora]]'s visits and [[sin-and-judgment|judgment]]s (006), and the night of [[addies-death|Addie's death]] with the storm breaking and [[vardaman-bundren|Vardaman]] drilling holes in [[addies-coffin|the coffin]] (011-012). Each scene roots the novel's metaphysical concerns in the specific, physical reality of a [[anse-bundren|pa]]rticular place.
