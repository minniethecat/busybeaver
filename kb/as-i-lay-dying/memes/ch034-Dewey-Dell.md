# Dewey Dell

## Dewey Dell

[030-001]The signboard comes in sight. It is looking out at the [[the-journey|road]] now, because it can wait. New Hope. 3 mi. it will say. New Hope. 3 mi. New Hope. 3 mi. And then the road will begin, curving away into the trees, empty with waiting, saying New Hope three miles.

[030-002]I heard that my [[motherhood|mother]] is dead. I wish I had time to let her die. I wish I had time to wish I had. It is because in the wild and outraged earth too soon too soon too soon. It’s not that I wouldn’t and will not it’s that it is too soon too soon too soon.

[030-003]Now it begins to say it. New Hope three miles. New Hope three miles. *That’s what they mean by the womb of time: the agony and the despair of spreading bones, the hard girdle in which lie the outraged entrails of events.* [[cash-bundren|Cash]]’s head turns slowly as we approach, his pale, empty, sad, composed and questioning face following the red and empty curve; beside the back wheel [[jewel-bundren|Jewel]] sits [[jewels-horse|the horse]], gazing straight ahead.

[030-004]The land runs out of [[darl-bundren|Darl]]’s eyes; they swim to pinpoints. They begin at my feet and rise along my body to my face, and then my dress is gone: I sit naked on the seat above the unhurrying mules, above the travail. *Suppose I tell him to turn. He will do what I say. Don’t you know he will do what I say?* Once I waked with a black void rushing under me. I could not see. I saw Vardaman rise and go to the window and strike the knife into [[vardamans-fish|the fish]], the blood gushing, hissing like steam but I could not see. *He’ll do as I say. He always does. I can persuade him to anything. You know I can. Suppose I say Turn here.* That was when I died that time. *Suppose I do. We’ll go to New Hope. We won’t have to go to [[jefferson|town]].* I rose and took the knife from the streaming fish still hissing and I killed Darl.

[030-005]*When I used to sleep with Vardaman I had a nightmare once I thought I was awake but I couldn’t see and couldn’t feel I couldn’t feel the bed under me and I couldn’t think what I was I couldn’t think of my name I couldn’t even think I am a girl I couldn’t even think I nor even think I want to wake up nor remember what was opposite to awake so I could do that I knew that something was passing but I couldn’t even think of time then all of a sudden I knew that something was it was wind blowing over me it was like the wind came and blew me back from where it was I was not blowing the room and Vardaman asleep and all of them back under me again and going on like a piece of cool silk dragging across my naked legs.*

[030-006]It blows cool out of the pines, a sad steady sound. New Hope. Was 3 mi. Was 3 mi. I believe in [[religion-and-faith|God]] I believe in God.

[030-007]“Why didn’t we go to New Hope, [[anse-bundren|pa]]?” Vardaman says. “Mr. Samson said we was, but we done passed the road.”

[030-008]Darl says, “Look, Jewel.” But he is not looking at me. He is looking at the sky. The buzzard is as still as if he were nailed to it.

[030-009]We turn into Tull’s lane. We pass the barn and go on, the wheels whispering in the mud, passing the green rows of cotton in the wild earth, and Vernon little across the field behind the plough. He lifts his hand as we pass and stands there looking after us for a long while.

[030-010]“Look, Jewel,” Darl says. Jewel sits on his horse like they were both made out of wood, looking straight ahead.

[030-011]I believe in God, God. God, I believe in God.
