## Reading the Rankings

Faulkner's animals are never merely decorative. Vardaman's famous equation of his mother with a fish, the buzzards that circle the wagon bearing Addie's decomposing body, the mules that drown in the flooded river — these animal presences articulate what the human characters cannot say directly about death, transformation, and the precariousness of being.
