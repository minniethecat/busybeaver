# Minor Characters

Beyond the Bundren family and the narrating circle, Faulkner populates *As I Lay Dying* with a constellation of minor characters — farmers, townspeople, doctors, and passersby — whose brief appearances create the social texture of Yoknapatawpha County and provide external vantage points on the Bundrens' grotesque journey.

## The Chorus Effect

Minor characters function as a Greek chorus in Faulkner's rural Mississippi: [[samson|Samson]] and [[armstid|Armstid]] offer farmers' hospitality and incredulity; [[moseley|Moseley]] the druggist registers urban disgust at Dewey Dell's predicament; [[peabody|Doctor Peabody]] brings professional authority that collapses before the Bundrens' chaotic reality. Each encounter underscores the gap between social convention and the family's single-minded, increasingly grotesque determination to reach [[jefferson|Jefferson]].
