# Dewey Dell

## Dewey Dell

[058-001]When he saw the money I said, “It’s not my money, it doesn’t belong to me.”

[058-002]“Whose is it, then?”

[058-003]“It’s Cora Tull’s money. It’s Mrs. Tull’s. I sold the cakes for it.”

[058-004]“Ten dollars for two cakes?”

[058-005]“Don’t you touch it. It’s not mine.”

[058-006]“You never had them cakes. It’s a lie. It was them Sunday clothes you had in that package.”

[058-007]“Don’t you touch it! If you take it you are a thief.”

[058-008]“My own daughter accuses me of being a thief. My own daughter.”

[058-009]“Pa. Pa.”

[058-010]“I have fed you and sheltered you. I give you love and care, yet my own daughter, the daughter of my dead wife, calls me a thief over her [[motherhood|mother]]’s grave.”

[058-011]“It’s not mine, I tell you. If it was, [[religion-and-faith|God]] knows you could have it.”

[058-012]“Where did you get ten dollars?”

[058-013]“Pa. Pa.”

[058-014]“You won’t tell me. Did you come by it so shameful you dare not?”

[058-015]“It’s not mine, I tell you. Can’t you understand it’s not mine?”

[058-016]“It’s not like I wouldn’t pay it back. But she calls her own father a thief.”

[058-017]“I can’t, I tell you. I tell you it’s not my money. God knows you could have it.”

[058-018]“I wouldn’t take it. My own born daughter that has et my food for seventeen years, begrudges me the loan of ten dollars.”

[058-019]“It’s not mine. I can’t.”

[058-020]“Whose is it, then?”

[058-021]“It was give to me. To buy something with.”

[058-022]“To buy what with?”

[058-023]“Pa. Pa.”

[058-024]“It’s just a loan. God knows, I hate for my blooden children to reproach me. But I give them what was mine without stint. Cheerful I give them, without stint. And now they deny me. [[addie-bundren|Addie]]. It was lucky for you you died, Addie.”

[058-025]“Pa. Pa.”

[058-026]“God knows it is.”

[058-027]He took the money and went out.
