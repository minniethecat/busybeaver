# The Mule Team

The [[anse-bundren|pa]]ir of mules that pull the [[the-bundren-wagon|Bundren wagon]] from [[bundren-farm|the farm]] to [[the-river-crossing-event|the flood]]ed [[the-river-crossing-location|Yoknapatawpha River]], where they drown during [[the-river-crossing-location|the ford]] [[water-and-river|crossing]]. Their [[death-and-dying|death]] — narrated by [[darl-bundren|Darl]] with hallucinatory precision — is the [[the-journey|journey]]'s first major catastrophe and precipitates [[anse-bundren|Anse]]'s transactional purchase of a replacement team with money obtained by selling [[jewel-bundren|Jewel]]'s horse.

## Description and Role

The mules are farm animals of the standard [[jefferson|Mississippi]] type: sturdy, [[anse-bundren|pa]]tient, essential to the subsistence economy of the Bundren farm. They are not individualized — unlike [[jewel-bundren|Jewel]]'s horse, they have no names and no emotional relationship to any character — and this anonymity is thematically significant. The mules represent [[work-and-craft|labor]] without identity, the animal infrastructure of rural poverty that is consumed by human necessity without ceremony or grief.

When the [[the-bundren-wagon|wagon]] overturns in [[the-river-crossing-location|the river]], the mules are trapped in their traces and drown. [[darl-bundren|Darl]]'s narration of their [[death-and-dying|death]] is one of the novel's most extraordinary [[anse-bundren|pa]]ssages:

> "The mules stand in the water, their heads gone, their bodies beginning to roll as the current takes them, the wagon tilting farther, the box sliding, sliding off, the mules rolling, their legs stiff, their backs coming up out of the water, their heads gone, the mules rolling" (032-003).

The repetition of "their heads gone" and "rolling" creates a hypnotic, almost incantatory effect that registers both the horror of the event and [[darl-bundren|Darl]]'s estranged perception of it.

## Symbolic Meaning

The mule team's [[water-and-river|drowning]] serves several symbolic functions:

- **The cost of the [[the-journey|journey]]**: The mules are the first life to be sacrificed for the [[the-journey|journey]]'s continuation — their [[death-and-dying|death]] is the cost of proceeding

- **[[anse-bundren|Anse]]'s transactional logic**: [[anse-bundren|Anse]] immediately replaces the mules by selling [[jewel-bundren|Jewel]]'s horse, treating the loss as an accounting problem rather than a tragedy — a transaction that mirrors his eventual replacement of [[addie-bundren|Addie]] with the new Mrs. Bundren

- **The [[water-and-river|river]] as consumption**: The [[water-and-river|river]] that swallows the mules is the same [[water-and-river|river]] that nearly takes [[addie-bundren|Addie]]'s coffin and [[cashs-broken-leg|Cash's leg]] — nature as an indiscriminate consumer of bodies, animal and human

## The Replacement Team

The new mule team, purchased in [[mottson|Mottson]] with [[jewel-bundren|Jewel]]'s horse money, pulls the [[the-bundren-wagon|wagon]] for the remainder of the [[the-journey|journey]]. Their arrival marks a narrative shift: the original team, associated with [[bundren-farm|the farm]] and the family's pre-[[the-journey|journey]] life, is gone; the new team, purchased through sacrifice ([[jewel-bundren|Jewel]]'s horse), belongs to the [[the-journey|journey]] itself.
