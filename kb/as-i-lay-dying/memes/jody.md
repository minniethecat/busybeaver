# Jody

A clerk at the [[jefferson|Jefferson]] drugstore where Skeet Macgowan [[work-and-craft|work]]s, appearing in Macgowan's [[sin-and-judgment|sin]]gle narrative chapter (ch055) of William Faulkner's _As I Lay Dying_. Jody functions as witness, reluctant accomplice, and moral counterweight to Macgowan's predatory scheme against [[dewey-dell-bundren|Dewey Dell Bundren]].

## Role in the Novel

Jody appears exclusively in Macgowan's chapter, which narrates [[dewey-dell-bundren|Dewey Dell]]'s attempt to obtain an abortion at the [[jefferson|Jefferson]] drugstore. Jody is the first clerk to encounter her; he initially directs her away ("Tell her all the doctors have gone to Memphis"), then — after Macgowan takes an interest — becomes an unwilling lookout. His role oscillates between [[anse-bundren|pa]]ssive complicity and quiet resistance. [055-001] [055-013]

## At the Drugstore

Jody's introduction establishes the drugstore's hierarchy: he is subordinate to Macgowan but not entirely powerless. When Macgowan initially dismisses the "country woman," Jody adds the qualifying detail that shifts Macgowan's interest: "She looks pretty good for a country girl" [055-001]. This remark, casual but consequential, sets the entire exploitative encounter in motion.

When Macgowan orders Jody to act as lookout for the store owner, Jody's reluctance is immediate and persistent:

> "You better lay off of that," Jody says. "He'll fire your stern out of here so quick you can't wink." [055-013]

Jody's concern is framed as practical rather than moral — fear of being fired, not objection to the scheme itself. Yet his repeated interventions ("What you doing to her, Skeet?" [055-042]) carry an undertow of unease that Macgowan deflects with mock-professional [[language-and-meaning|language]]: "It wouldn't be ethical. You go on up there and watch" [055-044].

After [[dewey-dell-bundren|Dewey Dell]] leaves, Jody's [[the-journey|quest]]ion — "Didn't you get it?" [055-077] — is ambiguous, hovering between prurient curiosity and genuine concern. Macgowan's evasive answer preserves the plausible deniability that the drugstore's masculine economy requires.

## Narrative Function

Jody's presence enables Macgowan's chapter to dramatize complicity as a spectrum rather than a binary. He does not actively harm [[dewey-dell-bundren|Dewey Dell]], but his "watching" and his half-hearted objections enable the harm to proceed. His character illustrates how the [[jefferson|Jefferson]] community's young men [[anse-bundren|pa]]rtici[[anse-bundren|pa]]te in — without individually originating — the exploitation of rural women who arrive in [[jefferson|town]] unprotected.
