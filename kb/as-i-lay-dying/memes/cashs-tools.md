# Cash's Tools

[[cash-bundren|Cash Bundren]]'s tools — his adze, saw, plane, and rule — are more than implements. They are the medium through which he engages with the world, the vocabulary with which he expresses a [[love|devotion]] he cannot articulate, and the measure of his worth as a person.

## Description

The tools are described through their use rather than their appearance: the adze shaping coffin boards, the saw cutting lumber to length, the plane smoothing surfaces, the rule ensuring precision. [[cash-bundren|Cash]] handles them with the automatic expertise of a master [[work-and-craft|craft]]sman — each motion economical, each cut deliberate. When the tools are nearly lost in [[the-river-crossing-location|the river]] [[water-and-river|crossing]], [[anse-bundren|Anse]]'s concern for them ("Where's my team?") contrasts with [[cash-bundren|Cash]]'s stoic silence: his tools are his livelihood and his identity, but he will not complain about their loss.

## Symbolic Significance

In a novel dominated by [[language-and-meaning|language]] — by characters speaking, interpreting, justifying, and misunderstanding — [[cash-bundren|Cash]]'s tools represent an alternative mode of [[language-and-meaning|meaning]]-making. Where [[language-and-meaning|words]] fail (as [[addie-bundren|Addie]] insists they always do), [[work-and-craft|craft]] succeeds. The coffin, made with these tools, is a more honest expression of grief than any verbal eulogy could be. The tools em[[the-body|body]] an ethic of attention, precision, and care that the novel presents as morally superior to the verbal performances of [[cora-tull|Cora]]'s piety or [[anse-bundren|Anse]]'s self-justification.

## Appearances in the Text

### First Appearance

The tools appear in the novel's opening scene: "[[cash-bundren|Cash]] is sawing a little. He sets the saw and rests the board on the plank he has been u[[sin-and-judgment|sin]]g for a [[work-and-craft|work]]-bench" (001-001). The rhythm of [[cash-bundren|Cash]]'s [[work-and-craft|work]] — the saw, the adze, the plane — provides the sonic backdrop for the novel's opening movement, the sound of [[work-and-craft|craft]] against the silence of approaching [[death-and-dying|death]].

### Key Scenes

During [[the-river-crossing-location|the river]] [[water-and-river|crossing]], [[cash-bundren|Cash]]'s tools are submerged and nearly lost. His concern, characteristically, is not for the tools' monetary value but for their precision: [[water-and-river|water]] will rust the saw, warp the plane. The tools' near-loss [[anse-bundren|pa]]rallels [[addies-coffin|the coffin]]'s near-loss — both are extensions of [[cash-bundren|Cash]]'s identity, threatened by the same [[water-and-river|flood]].
