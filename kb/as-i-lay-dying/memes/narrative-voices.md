# Narrative Voices

*As I Lay Dying* is narrated by fifteen different characters across fifty-nine monologue chapters — one of the most ambitious experiments in polyphonic narration in modernist literature. This page indexes every character who takes a turn as narrator, ranked by total reference count as a proxy for their narrative weight.

## Reading the Polyphony

Faulkner distributes narrative authority unevenly: [[darl-bundren|Darl Bundren]] narrates nineteen chapters — nearly a third of the novel — while [[addie-bundren|Addie Bundren]] speaks only once, from beyond the grave. Between these extremes, characters like [[vardaman-bundren|Vardaman]] and [[cash-bundren|Cash]] claim their own linguistic registers, and even peripheral figures like [[samson|Samson]] and [[armstid|Armstid]] are granted a single chapter that refracts the Bundren family's journey through an outsider's gaze. The cumulative effect is a world constituted by competing consciousnesses, where no single perspective can claim final authority.
