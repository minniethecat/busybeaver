The destroyed bridge is the proximate cause of the Bundren family's most dangerous trial in *As I Lay Dying*. When the family reaches the usual crossing point on their journey to Jefferson, they discover the bridge has been washed away by floodwaters, forcing them to ford the swollen river directly.

## Discovery and Warnings

The bridge's destruction is reported early. Darl notes in his opening chapter: "Down to the bridge" [008-014], already establishing the crossing as a point of concern. The local farmers warn the Bundrens about the danger. Armstid tells them "That bridge is old. I wouldn't monkey with it" [020-003], and reports that "the bridge has gone" [020-026].

Quick explains the severity: "That ere bridge" has been there "a long time" [020-029], making its destruction more significant. Houston, ever sardonic, remarks on Anse: "I knowed it that won't cross no more bridges" [020-035], suggesting the bridge's loss is almost providential punishment for Anse's character.

## Consequences

Without the bridge, the Bundrens face a choice: take the long way around by Samson's bridge — "It'll take you a day to get there" [020-074] — or ford the river directly. Anse, stubborn as always, chooses the ford. The decision proves nearly fatal.

The aftermath reverberates through the community. When others hear the news, they are shocked: "They must not a heard about the bridge" [029-007]. The destruction of "Tull's bridge" [029-009] and the question of whether "there's ere a bridge between here and Mouth of Ish" [029-010] emphasize how thoroughly the flooding has isolated the region.

The locals try to warn the Bundrens about both bridges: "The bridge at Tull's is gone, too" [029-012]. The message is clear — there is no safe crossing. But the Bundrens press on regardless.

## Symbolic Meaning

The destroyed bridge represents the collapse of civilized order — the infrastructure that connects communities has been swept away, leaving only the raw confrontation with nature. It also symbolizes the Bundrens' isolation: cut off from normal routes, they must take a path that nearly destroys them.
