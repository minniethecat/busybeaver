# Cash's Broken Leg

[[cash-bundren|Cash Bundren]] suffers a broken leg during [[the-river-crossing-location|the river]] [[water-and-river|crossing]] — a leg already broken once before the novel begins, when he fell from a church roof. The leg's progressive deterioration across the remainder of the [[the-journey|journey]], culminating in [[anse-bundren|Anse]]'s decision to set it in concrete, embodies the novel's dark logic of physical sacrifice in the name of [[family-duty|family obligation]].

## Sequence of Events

**First injury (pre-novel)**: [[cash-bundren|Cash]] falls from the roof of a church he is helping to build. His leg is set by [[peabody|Doctor Peabody]] and is healing — but not fully healed — when [[addie-bundren|Addie]] dies.

**Re-break ([[the-river-crossing-event|river crossing]], ch031–032)**: During [[the-river-crossing-location|the ford]] of [[the-river-crossing-event|the flood]]ed [[the-river-crossing-location|Yoknapatawpha River]], the [[the-bundren-wagon|wagon]] overturns. [[cash-bundren|Cash]], gripping his [[cashs-tools|carpenter's tools]] even as the [[water-and-river|water]] takes him, is thrown against the [[the-bundren-wagon|wagon]] or [[the-river-crossing-location|the river]]bed. His leg is re-broken; he is pulled from the [[water-and-river|water]] by [[jewel-bundren|Jewel]] and laid on the far bank. [[darl-bundren|Darl]]'s narration captures the moment with clinical precision: "[[cash-bundren|Cash]] is trying to tell us something. He is trying to talk, but he can only mouth at us, his face all twisted with the [[anse-bundren|pa]]in" (033).

**The cement cast (ch039–040)**: Money is scarce and the family's priorities are perversely ordered. [[anse-bundren|Anse]] — who has just sold [[jewel-bundren|Jewel]]'s horse to buy a new [[the-mule-team|mule team]] — decides to set Cash's leg with cement, reasoning that it will "hold it solid." The cement hardens around Cash's leg, becoming a rigid, heavy, and ultimately dangerous enclosure. [[cash-bundren|Cash]], in character, says only: "It never bothered me none" (040).

**Aftermath**: By the time the family reaches [[jefferson|Jefferson]], Cash's leg has deteriorated. The cement has prevented proper circulation; [[cash-bundren|Cash]] can no longer walk and must be carried. At the novel's end, he rides atop the [[the-bundren-wagon|wagon]] with the new Mrs. Bundren, his leg still encased in the grotesque cast — a silent monument to the [[the-journey|journey]]'s cost.

## Thematic Analysis

[[cash-bundren|Cash]]'s broken leg is the novel's most explicit convergence of the [[the-body|body]], [[work-and-craft|work]], and [[family-duty|family obligation]]. [[cash-bundren|Cash]] is the family's [[work-and-craft|craft]]sman — he built [[addie-bundren|Addie]]'s coffin on her [[death-and-dying|death]]bed, planing each board carefully — and his broken [[the-body|body]] is the price of that role. The cement cast, applied by [[anse-bundren|Anse]] with the same rough pragmatism that characterizes all his decisions, literalizes the Bundrens' relationship to suffering: acknowledge it just enough to continue the [[the-journey|journey]], never enough to properly address it.

Critics have read the cement cast as a symbol of the [[the-journey|journey]]'s futility — a leg "fixed" in a way that guarantees permanent damage — and as Faulkner's dark joke about [[anse-bundren|Anse]]'s character: the man who refuses to [[anse-bundren|pa]]y for a doctor will pour concrete on his son's broken leg and call it medicine.

## Key Passages

> "They had to prise the top of the box up to get his leg in. But he is lying on his back now. He is looking up at the sky and the top of the barn" (040-001).

> "It never bothered me none," Cash says. He looks at the concrete on his leg. "It's just a little stiff" (040-003).
