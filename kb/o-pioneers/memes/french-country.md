## Overview

French Country — The region settled by French immigrants near the Divide, representing one of the ethnic enclaves of the Nebraska prairie.

## In the Novel

French Country appears prominently in the narrative of *O Pioneers!*, shaping the course of events and revealing character. The novel's treatment of this place illuminates Cather's larger themes of pioneering, identity, and the American frontier.

## Significance

French Country contributes to the rich tapestry of *O Pioneers!*, demonstrating Cather's ability to weave individual stories into a larger vision of life on the Nebraska prairie.
