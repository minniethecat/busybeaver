## Overview

Carl Linstrum's Departure — Carl Linstrum's decision to leave the Divide after his family's failure at farming, separating him from Alexandra for sixteen years.

## In the Novel

Carl Linstrum's Departure appears prominently in the narrative of *O Pioneers!*, shaping the course of events and revealing character. The novel's treatment of this event illuminates Cather's larger themes of pioneering, identity, and the American frontier.

## Significance

Carl Linstrum's Departure contributes to the rich tapestry of *O Pioneers!*, demonstrating Cather's ability to weave individual stories into a larger vision of life on the Nebraska prairie.
