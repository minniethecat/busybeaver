## Overview

Old World vs. New World — The tension between European heritage and American identity experienced by immigrant characters in O Pioneers!.

## In the Novel

Old World vs. New World appears prominently in the narrative of *O Pioneers!*, shaping the course of events and revealing character. The novel's treatment of this concept illuminates Cather's larger themes of pioneering, identity, and the American frontier.

## Significance

Old World vs. New World contributes to the rich tapestry of *O Pioneers!*, demonstrating Cather's ability to weave individual stories into a larger vision of life on the Nebraska prairie.
