[017-001]Carl came into the sitting-room while Alexandra was lighting the lamp. She

[017-002]looked up at him as she adjusted the shade. His sharp shoulders stooped as if

[017-003]he were very tired, his face was pale, and there were bluish shadows under his

[017-004]dark eyes. His anger had burned itself out and left him sick and disgusted.

[017-005]“You have seen Lou and Oscar?” Alexandra asked.

[017-006]“Yes.” His eyes avoided hers.

[017-007]Alexandra took a deep breath. “And now you are going away. I thought so.”

[017-008]Carl threw himself into a chair and pushed the dark lock back from his forehead

[017-009]with his white, nervous hand. “What a hopeless position you are in, Alexandra!”

[017-010]he exclaimed feverishly. “It is your fate to be always surrounded by little

[017-011]men. And I am no better than the rest. I am too little to face the criticism of

[017-012]even such men as Lou and Oscar. Yes, I am going away; to-morrow. I cannot even

[017-013]ask you to give me a promise until I have something to offer you. I thought,

[017-014]perhaps, I could do that; but I find I can’t.”

[017-015]“What good comes of offering people things they don’t need?” Alexandra asked

[017-016]sadly. “I don’t need money. But I have needed you for a great many years. I

[017-017]wonder why I have been permitted to prosper, if it is only to take my friends

[017-018]away from me.”

[017-019]“I don’t deceive myself,” Carl said frankly. “I know that I am going away on my

[017-020]own account. I must make the usual effort. I must have something to show for

[017-021]myself. To take what you would give me, I should have to be either a very large

[017-022]man or a very small one, and I am only in the middle class.”

[017-023]Alexandra sighed. “I have a feeling that if you go away, you will not come

[017-024]back. Something will happen to one of us, or to both. People have to snatch at

[017-025]happiness when they can, in this world. It is always easier to lose than to

[017-026]find. What I have is yours, if you care enough about me to take it.”

[017-027]Carl rose and looked up at the picture of [[john-bergson|John Bergson]]. “But I can’t, my dear,

[017-028]I can’t! I will go North at once. Instead of idling about in California all

[017-029]winter, I shall be getting my bearings up there. I won’t waste another week. Be

[017-030]patient with me, Alexandra. Give me a year!”

[017-031]“As you will,” said Alexandra wearily. “All at once, in a single day, I lose

[017-032]everything; and I do not know why. Emil, too, is going away.” Carl was still

[017-033]studying John Bergson’s face and Alexandra’s eyes followed his. “Yes,” she

[017-034]said, “if he could have seen all that would come of the task he gave me, he

[017-035]would have been sorry. I hope he does not see me now. I hope that he is among

[017-036]the old people of his blood and country, and that tidings do not reach him from

[017-037]the New World.”
