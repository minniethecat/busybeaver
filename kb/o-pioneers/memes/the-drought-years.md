## Overview

The Drought Years — The period of severe drought that tests the settlers of the Divide — Alexandra's decisive response transforms the Bergson fortunes.

## In the Novel

The Drought Years appears prominently in the narrative of *O Pioneers!*, shaping the course of events and revealing character. The novel's treatment of this event illuminates Cather's larger themes of pioneering, identity, and the American frontier.

## Significance

The Drought Years contributes to the rich tapestry of *O Pioneers!*, demonstrating Cather's ability to weave individual stories into a larger vision of life on the Nebraska prairie.
