## Overview

Mrs. Hiller is an elderly neighbor who lives near the Shabata farm on the Divide. Though she appears only briefly, her proximity to [[marie-shabata|Marie Shabata]] and [[frank-shabata|Frank Shabata]] makes her an important witness to the tensions in their household.

In a farming community where neighbors are few and far between, Mrs. Hiller's presence provides one of the few outside perspectives on the Shabatas' troubled marriage.

## Biography

Mrs. Hiller lived near the Shabata farm, close enough that Marie considered her the nearest neighbor. Marie mentioned her to Frank during one of their quarrels: "I'll speak to Mrs. Hiller again. But, really, she" (011-172), indicating that Mrs. Hiller was a regular point of contact.

Marie visited Mrs. Hiller to show her a new dress: "On your way home, show it to Mrs. Hiller. She's crazy about embroidery" (018-066). These small exchanges reveal the fabric of women's neighborly relations on the isolated Divide.

When Marie went missing on the day of the tragedy, Alexandra's first thought was: "I thought she was going to Mrs. Hiller's, and I telephoned" (028-019). Mrs. Hiller's home was the natural place to check — she was Marie's closest female neighbor and confidante.

After the tragedy, Alexandra visited Mrs. Hiller: "She had gone over to Mrs. Hiller's to leave a little present" (030-004). Carl Linstrum and Alexandra "came out of Mrs. Hiller's garden" (030-024) together, the visit marking a moment of community healing.

## Significance

Mrs. Hiller represents the quiet presence of community on the sparsely settled Divide. In a landscape where families lived miles apart, neighborly visits sustained the social fabric. Marie's visits to Mrs. Hiller, Alexandra's gift-giving — these small gestures formed the web of mutual care that held the community together.

After the tragedy, Mrs. Hiller's home becomes a place of healing. Alexandra's visit there with Carl suggests the gradual restoration of normal human connections in the wake of violence. Mrs. Hiller, like [[mr-schwartz|Mr. Schwartz]], belongs to the older generation whose steady presence offers continuity amid upheaval.
