# Shanghai

## Overview

Shanghai appears in _Futility_ as part of the geography of Russian exile. As a major port city in the Far East, it was one of the way stations for Russians fleeing the Revolution, along with other cities like Harbin and Vladivostok.

## Exile Geography

The novel's characters move through a network of Far Eastern cities—Vladivostok, Shanghai, Peking, Tokyo—that constituted the Pacific rim of the Russian diaspora. The three sisters are initially carried away "to Shanghai" (002-002) in the narrator's imagination, a destination that represents the farthest reach of exile. Zina's people make money by importing "fashionable 'Parisian' hats from Peking and Shanghai and selling them at great profit in Vladivostok" (026-039), a detail that captures the improvised economy of the exile community. The narrator's final departure takes him "towards Singapore, Hong Kong, Shanghai" (033-050), the string of port cities marking his retreat from Russia and the family he leaves behind. Shanghai represents one node in this network, a place of temporary refuge and uncertain futures.
