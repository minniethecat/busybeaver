# Magda Nikolaevna

## Overview

Magda Nikolaevna is the mother of the three sisters—[[sonia|Sonia]], [[nina|Nina]], and [[vera|Vera]]—and a member of the sprawling family entourage that follows [[nikolai-vasilievich|Nikolai Vasilievich]] through revolutionary Petrograd and into Siberian exile. She is a widow who travels with [[cecedek|Cecedek]], the family's financial patron and hanger-on.

## Character

Magda Nikolaevna is described as "a beautiful woman enough, but so delicate and with such an elaborate concoction of accessories by way of dress that the chief sensation yielded from touching her was that of being entangled in web-like fabrics" (021-015). She "laughed" teasingly when asking the narrator whether he is a law student (009-039), displaying a playful, slightly ironic personality. Despite her delicate exterior, she endures the hardship of revolution and displacement alongside her daughters.

## Role in the Family

As matriarch of the three sisters, Magda Nikolaevna occupies a position of quiet authority. When [[andrei-andreiech|Andrei Andreiech]] complained of the narrator's attentions, he did so through her—Cecedek was dispatched to relay her prohibition against hampering her daughters' happiness (012-021). She also enjoys the company of [[fanny-ivanovna|Fanny Ivanovna]], with whom she socializes at the Admiral's dinner party (026-005) and in the shared living quarters in Vladivostok.
