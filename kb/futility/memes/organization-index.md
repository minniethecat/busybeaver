## Institutions and Power

The organizations in *Futility* represent the structures of power — armies, governments, embassies — that the novel's characters navigate, resist, or serve. From the Bolsheviks consolidating their revolution to the fading authority of the White Army and the distant concern of the British Embassy, these institutions embody the impersonal forces against which individual agency proves futile.
