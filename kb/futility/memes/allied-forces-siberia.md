## Overview

The Allied Forces in Siberia were the international military contingents deployed to Russia's Far East during the [[russian-civil-war|Russian Civil War]], nominally to rescue the Czechoslovak Legion and reopen the Eastern Front against Germany, but increasingly drawn into the anti-Bolshevik cause. In William Gerhardie's *Futility*, the British component of this force — represented by [[admiral-butt|Admiral Butt]], [[sir-hugo|Sir Hugo]], and the narrator himself — provides the institutional framework for the novel's Siberian sections.

## Composition

The Allied force in Siberia was a multinational patchwork: British, Japanese, American, French, Italian, and Czechoslovak contingents, each with its own objectives and command structure. The novel registers this diversity through the interactions of its British and Russian characters, while the Japanese presence — the largest contingent — is felt only at the periphery (020-069). Gerhardie's focus on the British experience reflects his own service in the British military mission, which operated "under vague instructions from some Allied War Office" (019-004).

## Mission and Ambiguity

What exactly the Allied forces were supposed to accomplish in Siberia remains, in the novel as in history, unclear. The narrator and his colleagues attend conferences, write dispatches, and maintain the forms of military bureaucracy without ever articulating a coherent objective (021-025). This ambiguity is Gerhardie's subject: the Allied Intervention was, in his treatment, an exercise in organized futility — vast resources deployed for purposes that no one could convincingly state.

## Social Function

In the novel, the Allied forces function less as a military organization than as a social one. The British officers provide a framework of official sociability — dinners, dances, thé dansants — that structures the characters' lives in [[omsk|Omsk]] (020-069). The military mission is, in effect, a club with uniforms, and its true purpose may simply be to give Englishmen something to do in Siberia while history unfolds around them.
