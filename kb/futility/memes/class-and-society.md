# Class and Society in Futility

The world of *Futility* is one in which social hierarchies have collapsed but not disappeared. The Bursanovs are aristocrats without money, living in a "large, luxurious flat in the Mohovaya" (004-001) while dependent on Nikolai Vasilievich's irregular support. Revolution has swept away the old order, but the old order's habits — pride of family, horror of disgrace, the performance of cultivation — persist, creating a permanent comic dissonance.

## Overview

Fanny Ivanovna's refrain — "I belong to a very proud family indeed. My father and all my brothers were officers in the German Guards" (006-022) — is both a claim to status and an acknowledgment of its loss. The pride is real, but it is a pride that no longer corresponds to any social reality. The family's position is maintained through performance: the flat, the piano, the talk of composers and writers, the attendance at the theatre. Class has become a matter of style rather than substance.

## Displaced Aristocracy

The novel is populated by displaced aristocrats: Colonel Olenin "now out of work" playing cards, a "brother officer, also out of work" (035-003). The Revolution has created a class of people whose skills — military command, social grace, the bearing of authority — are suddenly valueless. They fill their days with cards, with visits, with conversations about "love among monkeys" (014-002) at the British Embassy, where the diplomatic world offers a temporary refuge from the world outside.

## The Performance of Cultivation

The narrator's desperate recitation of intellectual names — "Turgenev, Goethe, Dostoevski, Chekhov, Flaubert, Shakespeare and Tolstoy" followed by "Wagner, Scriabin, Debussy and Richard Strauss" (036-005) — is a class performance that fails. The old markers of cultivation no longer impress. The novel registers this failure without nostalgia: the old world is gone, and its cultural currency is worthless.

## Key Passage

> It was on one of those long, happy evenings which it had now become my custom to spend regularly at their large, luxurious flat in the Mohovaya in St. Petersburg, that I was further initiated into the domestic affairs of the Bursanov family. (004-001)
