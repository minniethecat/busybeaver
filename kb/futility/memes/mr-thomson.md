# Mr Thomson

## Overview

Mr Thomson is Nikolai Vasilievich's consulting engineer, a British professional employed at the goldmines in Siberia. His departure and subsequent letter requesting reinstatement serve to illustrate the disrupted economic order in the wake of the Russian Revolution.

## The Goldmines

Thomson appears in the narrative through his association with Nikolai Vasilievich's mining operations. When the Czecho-Slovak troops recaptured the mines from the Bolsheviks and the miners subsequently killed the manager, "Mr. Thomson, his consulting-engineer, despairing of the situation, had returned to England" (019-060). This departure symbolizes the collapse of the economic enterprise that sustained the extended family.

## The Letter

Thomson reappears through a letter "from an obscure address in Scotland" (020-045), stating that "the afterwar conditions prevailing in the west of Europe had frankly disappointed him" and soliciting reinstatement. Fanny Ivanovna describes him as "such a nice man" and worries about "his wife and children" (020-044). The letter generates discussion about the mines' future and the English military intervention, but the three sisters "looked as if they didn't care a hang about Mr. Thomson, the English, the mines or anybody else"—a characteristic moment of emotional deflection.
