# Madame Olenin

## Overview

Madame Olenin is a peripheral figure in _Futility_, connected to the central family through Uncle Kostia. She appears primarily in domestic scenes that establish the extended family network of Russian exiles in Vladivostok.

## Family Role

Madame Olenin is identified as a relative of Uncle Kostia, part of the sprawling family network that surrounds Nikolai Vasilievich. Kniaz, the family hanger-on, is noted to "probably settle at his sister's, the Olenins" (020-029), indicating her role as a host for displaced relatives. She is the mother of Olya Olenin, described as "stout and round, like a football" (021-016), and of an infant daughter christened Fanny after Fanny Ivanovna, who serves as godmother: "the little girl I christened. Madame Olenin's little daughter. She's just three weeks old today. A dear little thing" (025-005).

## Domestic Presence

In her few appearances, Madame Olenin is depicted in maternal postures: "a suckling in a jumper at her breast, stood in the doorway and watched" as the younger characters dance. She also appears as a host, providing space for family gatherings during the exile period. Her home becomes one of the temporary refuges where the displaced Russian community maintains its social rituals.

## Narrative Function

As a minor character, Madame Olenin contributes to the novel's texture of crowded domestic life, where the central drama of the three sisters plays out against a backdrop of relatives, dependents, and hangers-on. Her presence reinforces the theme of family as both sustenance and burden.
