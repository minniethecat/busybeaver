## Overview

Oscar Wilde appears in William Gerhardie's *Futility* through a single, telling moment: [[zina|Zina]] turns to the narrator and says, "Your Oscar Wilde" [010-012]. The possessive pronoun — "your" — marks Wilde as the property of the English-speaking world, a cultural commodity that Zina can deploy in conversation but that belongs, finally, to the narrator's tradition. This brief reference opens onto the novel's broader engagement with literary culture, wit, and the relationship between aestheticism and lived experience.

## Zina's Quotation

Zina's reference to Wilde is characteristically specific and slightly off-center: she quotes Wilde on the subject of hearing too much — a Wildean epigram about the dangers of excessive knowledge. The epigram is pure Wilde: paradoxical, witty, and ethically unserious. That Zina, a young Russian woman with no apparent literary education, can quote Wilde suggests the reach of his reputation and the way his wit had become common cultural currency.

## Wilde and Gerhardie

Gerhardie's relationship to Wilde is oblique but significant. Both writers deploy irony as a primary mode; both treat serious subjects with apparent lightness; both understand that the epigram can carry as much weight as the essay. Gerhardie's narrative voice — detached, amused, unwilling to moralize — owes something to Wilde's example, even if Gerhardie's range of emotional sympathy is broader and his irony less brittle.

## The Cosmopolitan Literary Culture

Wilde's presence in the novel, like that of [[ibsen-in-futility|Ibsen]] and [[three-sisters|Chekhov]], is evidence of the cosmopolitan literary culture that the novel's characters inhabit. A British narrator, a Russian woman, a French opera, a Norwegian playwright, an Irish wit — the cultural references cross national boundaries as freely as the characters cross [[siberia|Siberia]]. This cosmopolitanism is itself a subject of the novel: the world that made such cultural mobility possible is the world the [[russian-revolution-1917|revolution]] is destroying.
