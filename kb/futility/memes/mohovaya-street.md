# Mohovaya Street

## Overview

Mohovaya Street (Моховая улица) is a street in central Petrograd (now St. Petersburg) that appears in _Futility_ as one of the locations associated with the characters' life before the Revolution. The street runs parallel to the Neva River near the Summer Garden.

## Narrative Context

In the novel, Mohovaya Street is the location of the family's "large, luxurious flat" (004-001) where the narrator is first initiated into the household's domestic complexities. The flat becomes a recurring point of reference — Nikolai Vasilievich's relatives invoke "these millions, this house in the Mohovaya" (007-005) as shorthand for the wealth and stability they have lost, while the narrator's uncle demands: "Why won't his wife give him a divorce? She wants his goldmines in Siberia and the house in the Mohovaya" (007-009). Mohovaya Street is part of the pre-revolutionary Petrograd geography—the world of comfortable apartments, social visits, and domestic routines that the Revolution destroys. Its mention evokes the lost world of the characters' former lives.
