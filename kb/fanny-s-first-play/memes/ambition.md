## Definition

Ambition in Fanny's First Play takes surprising forms. The Gilbey-Knox world is ostensibly ambitious — the families maintain their social position through business success and careful respectability — but their ambition is purely defensive

## Key Passages

 to stay where they are, to avoid scandal, to maintain appearances. Genuine ambition belongs to the young and the outsiders. Fanny O'Dowda's ambition to be a playwright, Margaret's ambition to live honestly, Dora's ambition to rise through charm and wit — these are ambitions for something new, not for the preservation of something old. Duvallet's comic ambition is to be more English than the English — to absorb what he sees as English freedom and bring it back to France.
