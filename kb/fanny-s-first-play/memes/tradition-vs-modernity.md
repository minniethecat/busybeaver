## Definition

## Key Passages

The Count's costume is a manifesto for tradition against modernity. He has built his life as a refuge from the nineteenth century — Venice, not England; Cimarosa, not Wagner; the gondola, not the motor-car. His daughter's play, with its vulgar modern subject matter, shatters this refuge. The critics, too, are caught between tradition and modernity
