## Setting

The Adelphi is a district in the City of Westminster, London, located between the Strand and the River Thames. In the Edwardian period, it was known for its theatres, hotels, and proximity to the political and cultural heart of London. The Adelphi Theatre itself — a major West End venue — gave the district its theatrical reputation, but the venue named in Shaw's Preface is the Little Theatre in the Adelphi, a smaller house located on John Street (now John Adam Street).

The *raison d'etre* of this page is the final line of Shaw's Preface: "*Fanny's First Play* was performed for the first time at the Little Theatre in the Adelphi, London, on the afternoon of Wednesday, April 19th 1911" [P01-005]. This single sentence anchors the play — with all its meta-theatrical playfulness — in a specific time and place.

## In the Play

The Adelphi does not appear in the dramatic text itself. Its entire presence in the corpus is the Preface's performance note. Yet this absence is thematically significant. The play-within-the-play takes place entirely in the fictional spaces of Denmark Hill (the Gilbey home), the Knox home, and the framing device of Florence Towers. The real London — the London of the Little Theatre, the Adelphi, the Strand — stands outside the fiction, a reminder that the play is a made thing, a performance enacted before a live audience in a specific venue.

## Dramatic Function

The Adelphi serves as the outermost layer of the play's Russian-doll structure of theatrical frames. The innermost layer is the domestic drama of the Gilbeys and Knoxes. Around that is the Induction/Epilogue frame at Florence Towers, where critics debate the play they have just seen. Outside even that is the real-world performance at the Little Theatre, where Shaw's audience watches actors pretending to be critics watching a play by a fictional young woman. The Adelphi is the anchor of the real — the historical fact that holds the fictions in place.

## Real-World Context

The Little Theatre opened in 1910 on John Street, Adelphi, designed as an intimate venue for new and experimental drama. It was associated with the repertory movement championed by [[granville-barker|Harley Granville Barker]] and others. Shaw's choice of this venue for the premiere of *Fanny's First Play* was appropriate: a small, modern theatre for a play that was itself a meta-theatrical experiment.

The afternoon performance time (not the traditional evening premiere) is notable. Matinees were often used for try-out performances or for plays expected to generate controversy — either way, the scheduling suggests Shaw's awareness that this was not a conventional first night.

## Key Passages

- **The performance note**: "*Fanny's First Play* was performed for the first time at the Little Theatre in the Adelphi, London, on the afternoon of Wednesday, April 19th 1911" [P01-005].
- **The Preface's meta-theatrical framing**: "The concealment of the authorship, if a *secret de Polichinelle* can be said to involve concealment, was a necessary part of the play" [P01-004].
