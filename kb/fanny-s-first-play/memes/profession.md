## Key Passages

The play's characters are defined by their professions — or their lack of them. The critics are professional judges of art; the Gilbeys and Knoxes are shopkeepers whose business success funds their respectability; Juggins is a footman whose professionalism conceals his true self. Shaw, who wrote extensively about professions and professionalism, uses occupational identity as a lens for character. Trotter's diplomatic profession shapes his critical style — everything is negotiation, nothing is confrontation. The Gilbeys' shopkeeping shapes their relationship to class — they are ''in trade'' and anxious about it. Juggins's profession is the most interesting
