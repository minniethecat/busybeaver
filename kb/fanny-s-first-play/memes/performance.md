## Definition

Performance is both the subject and the method of *Fanny's First Play*. The play is about a play being performed; the Induction and Epilogue frame that performance within another performance; and the social world depicted is itself a theatre of performed identities. Shaw's meta-theatrical structure — a play about critics judging a play about respectable society — turns performance from a theme into a method of inquiry.

## The Theatrical Performance

The inner play is an amateur production staged at [[florence-towers|Florence Towers]] for an invited audience of four critics. The [[count-odowda|Count]] has arranged everything — the actors, the costumes, the setting — with the meticulousness of a professional impresario, though he insists on amateur status. The performance itself is never shown to the real audience: we hear only the critics' reactions. This withholding is deliberate — Shaw is interested not in the play-as-performed but in the play-as-judged.

[[fanny-odowda|Fanny]]'s direction of the rehearsal reveals her as a proto-feminist auteur. She has chosen actors "perfectly suited to their parts" (001-037), and she has managed the difficult "gentleman … called the producer" who "hadn't read the play" but eventually "found out what it was all about" and cooperated. The performance is her creation, though her authorship is concealed.

## Social Performance

The Gilbeys and Knoxes are performers in the drama of respectability. [[gilbey|Mr. Gilbey]] plays the role of Outraged Father; [[mrs-gilbey|Mrs. Gilbey]] plays the role of Anxious Mother; [[knox|Joseph Knox]] plays the role of Moral Authority. Each performance is convincing enough — until it is challenged. When [[margaret-knox|Margaret]] refuses to play her assigned role, the entire production collapses.

The critics, too, are performers. [[trotter|Trotter]] plays the Fastidious Aesthete; [[vaughan|Vaughan]] and [[gunn|Gunn]] play the Serious Critics; [[flawner-bannal|Bannal]] plays the Man in the Street. Their verdict on the play is partly a verdict on their own performances — a revelation of the roles they have chosen to inhabit.

## Key Passages

- **Fanny's direction**: "She tells me that the actors you sent down are perfectly suited to their parts" — the invisible director (001-037).
- **The Count's horror**: "No costumes! Do you mean to say it is a modern play?" — the performance of tradition (001-041).
- **The critic's role**: The verdict scene in the Epilogue where each critic performs his judgment.
