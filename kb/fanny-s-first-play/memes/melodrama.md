## Definition

The Victorian and Edwardian stage was dominated by melodrama — plays built on sensation, moral polarization, and the well-made plot. Shaw's career as a critic and playwright was, in large part, a campaign against melodrama and its assumptions. Fanny's First Play is an anti-melodrama. It has a prison scene but no villain; a reconciliation but no moral; a daughter's rebellion but no punishment. Gunn, the journalist-critic, represents the melodramatic sensibility
