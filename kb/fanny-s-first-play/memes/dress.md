## Definition

Dress in *Fanny's First Play* is never merely decorative. Every costume choice is a social and philosophical statement. [[count-odowda|Count O'Dowda]] dresses in eighteenth-century Venetian style to declare his rejection of the modern world. [[juggins|Juggins]] wears "grandiose Spanish livery" (001-043) that marks his servant status while its grandeur comically exceeds his role. The critics arrive in evening dress and diplomatic uniform — their clothing identifying their professional types before they speak a word.

## In the Play

The Induction opens with costume as its first signal. [[juggins|Juggins]] enters "in grandiose Spanish livery" (001-043), a costume that immediately establishes the theatrical frame: this is a performance, and everyone is in costume. [[cecil-savoyard|Savoyard]] arrives "in evening dress and a fur-lined overcoat" (001-045) — the modern man confronting the Count's anachronistic world.

The [[count-odowda|Count]]'s costume is his philosophy made visible. He tells Savoyard: "I wear this costume because — well, perhaps I had better explain the position" (001-056). His dress is a manifesto: the eighteenth century against the nineteenth, beauty against vulgarity, Venice against England.

[[fanny-odowda|Fanny]] appears "in a dress synchronous with her father's" (001-122). This sartorial alignment suggests her initial position within her father's aesthetic world — a world she will challenge with her play. The critics arrive in their own professional costumes: [[trotter|Trotter]] "wears a diplomatic dress, with sword and three-cornered hat" (001-127), marking him as the establishment figure he is.

## Key Passages

- **Juggins's livery**: "A footman in grandiose Spanish livery enters before the curtain" (001-043).
- **The Count's costume philosophy**: "I wear this costume because... I had better explain the position" (001-056).
- **Fanny's synchronous dress**: "A girl of nineteen in a dress synchronous with her father's" (001-122).
- **The Count's aesthetic manifesto**: "I have never had anything less than the very best that life has produced" (001-074).
