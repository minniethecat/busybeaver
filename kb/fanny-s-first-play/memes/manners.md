## Definition

Manners in *Fanny's First Play* are both a social performance and a class marker. Shaw uses them to expose the gap between external propriety and internal truth. The play's characters navigate a world where "good manners" can mean genuine courtesy, class-bound etiquette, or strategic manipulation — and the play delights in showing when these meanings collide.

## In the Play

The Induction is a parade of manners. [[trotter|Trotter]]'s diplomatic polish, [[vaughan|Vaughan]]'s intellectual reserve, [[gunn|Gunn]]'s journalistic bluntness, and [[flawner-bannal|Bannal]]'s cheerful vulgarity each represent a different code of social conduct — and each code imposes different constraints on what can be said and thought about art.

[[dora-delaney|Dora Delaney]] is the play's great virtuoso of manners. Her encounter with the policeman in Jamaica Square is a masterclass in social performance: "I put on my best company manners and stepped up to the copper. 'If you please, sir,' says I, 'can you direct me to Carrickmines Square?' I was so genteel, and talked so sweet, that he fell to it like a bird" (002-318). Dora deploys manners as a weapon, disarming authority through a performance of deference before delivering the knock-out blow.

[[juggins|Juggins]] the footman complicates the manners question further. [[bobby-gilbey|Bobby]] observes: "Dora says your name can't be Juggins, and that you have the manners of a gentleman. I always thought you hadn't any manners. Anyhow, your manners are different from the manners of a gentleman in my set" (003-530). Juggins's manners are unplaceable — they don't fit any class category, suggesting an authentic self beneath the livery.

## Key Passages

- **Dora's genteel performance**: "I was so genteel, and talked so sweet, that he fell to it like a bird" (002-318).
- **Bobby on Juggins's manners**: "Your manners are different from the manners of a gentleman in my set" (003-530).
- **Dora and Rudolph**: "Rudolph will teach me high-class manners. I call it quite a happy ending" (005-968).
