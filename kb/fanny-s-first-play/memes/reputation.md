## Definition

Reputation is what the parents have instead of religion, instead of morality, instead of love. When Knox says Margaret "had a happy, respectable home — everything —" (003-409), the "everything" is reputation

## Key Passages

 the good opinion of neighbors, the standing in the community, the appearance of propriety. The play's action consists largely of reputation being lost and the characters discovering what remains. Margaret discovers that without reputation she still has a self. Bobby discovers that without reputation he is free of his engagement. The parents, clinging to a reputation that can no longer be maintained, are the play's true losers.
