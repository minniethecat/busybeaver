## Definition

## Key Passages

Fanny's First Play is structured around a generational conflict that is also a moral one. The young characters — Fanny, Margaret, Bobby, Dora — are vital, honest, and capable of growth. The old characters — the Count, the parents, the critics — are fixed, self-deceiving, and incapable of learning. Shaw's sympathies are clearly with the young, but his treatment of age is not simply dismissive. The Count's farewell speech — ''The world has passed me by and left me'' (005-988) — has genuine pathos. Age, in this play, is not a biological fact but a moral condition
