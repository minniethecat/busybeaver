## Definition

Scandal is the specter that haunts the Gilbey-Knox world. When Margaret is imprisoned, her parents' first thought is not for her safety but for their reputation

## Key Passages

 "When a girl runs away from home like that, people know what to think of her and her parents" (003-408). Scandal, in this world, is worse than wrongdoing — it is wrongdoing made visible. The play's comic resolution depends on the young characters' discovery that scandal, once faced, loses its power. Margaret declares she will "tell everybody" about her prison experience
