## Definition

The Gilbey-Knox parents are terrified of failure — failure of their businesses, failure of their marriages, failure of their parenting. This fear drives their every action and paralyzes their moral judgment. The young characters, by contrast, have already "failed" by the time the play begins — Bobby has been away for a fortnight doing who-knows-what, Margaret has been in prison — and they discover that failure, survived, is liberating. Margaret's declaration that she is "not sorry" (003-467) is the play's moral center
