## Definition

The Count's Venice is a monument to chosen solitude — an "earthly paradise" built to exclude the modern world. His solitude is aesthetic and aristocratic

## Key Passages

 he has chosen to be alone with beauty. Margaret's solitude in prison is the opposite — forced, degrading, shared with "other poor wretches" — yet it produces a more genuine independence than the Count's willed isolation. The play's deepest solitude belongs to characters who can no longer share their families' moral framework
