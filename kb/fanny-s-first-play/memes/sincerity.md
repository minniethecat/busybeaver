## Definition

Sincerity is the play's quiet moral standard. Margaret's refusal to apologize to the policeman — "I wasn't sorry" (003-467) — is an act of radical sincerity

## Key Passages

 she will not say what she does not feel, even when convention and self-interest demand it. Dora's sincerity is comic but no less real — she admits she's "a regular pig" about table manners (004-895) with the same cheerfulness with which she admits everything about herself. The parents, by contrast, cannot be sincere because sincerity would require them to acknowledge the gap between their professed values and their actual lives. Knox's confession that he has no real religion is the closest any parent comes to sincerity — and it is devastating.
