## Definition

Margaret Knox's most radical claim is that her night of adventure was innocent. "I can't imagine anything more innocent and more happy," she tells Bobby (004-619). This is not naivety but a philosophical position

## Key Passages

 innocence is not the absence of experience but the absence of guilt. Margaret has experienced — dancing, champagne, prison, violence — but she refuses to experience shame. Her innocence is post-lapsarian
