## Overview

The forbidden song episode dramatizes the collision between the world of adult propriety and the reckless, unself-conscious world of childhood. Martin's innocent imitation of his older friend Johan becomes a public scandal, teaching him that offenses are judged by the reaction they provoke.

## The Evening with Johan

On summer evenings, Martin's best friend was Johan, the bridge-tender's assistant. Johan told stories of ghosts and murders that left both boys excited and frightened. To drive off his fear, Johan would strike up a jolly song to the tune of "There was an old woman by Konham Square": "I go to my death wherever I go, *killivillivippombom*!" [009-071]. Martin joined in.

## The Scandal

When they reached the bridge-tender's house, Johan fell silent — but Martin sang on at the top of his voice. Old Moberg, who was drinking toddy on his porch, was deeply offended: he was terrified of death and heard Martin "singing a funeral hymn to the tune of an insolent street song" [009-079]. Moberg turned blue in the face and declared in a trembling voice: "And this child is supposed to come of respectable people! These are strange times, I may say" [009-081].

## The Aftermath

Martin's parents had heard everything from the veranda. Summoned inside, Martin was confronted by his father, who "explained to Martin how dreadfully he had behaved" [009-091]. Martin cried and was sent to bed. His mother cried too during prayers. The narrator notes: "For children's offenses, like those of adults, are judged more according to the scandal they have aroused than according to their inner nature, and Martin's misdeed had caused a terrible scandal" [009-091].
