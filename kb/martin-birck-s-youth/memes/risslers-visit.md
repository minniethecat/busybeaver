## Overview

Henrik Rissler, Martin's closest friend from boyhood, visits him in Stockholm after finishing his preliminary degree at Uppsala. Over wine and cigarettes in Martin's room, the two men discuss the transience of feeling, the philosophy of love, and Rissler's confession of his engagement to Sigrid Tesch. The visit is one of the novel's richest intellectual exchanges, touching on Nietzsche, romantic irony, and the ethics of attachment.

## The Arrival

Henrik shades his eyes from the sun and observes: "Here everything is the same. Here time has stood still" [024-001]. Martin counters by pointing out the new factory chimney opposite — "especially in the evening as a silhouette" when one can forget its purpose and it becomes "a pillar tower built by some Chaldaean prince and priest" [024-002]. Henrik retorts: "One forgets the purpose, then first it becomes beautiful" [024-003].

## The Conversation: Growth and Melancholy

Henrik reflects on the pain of outgrowing old associations — "to read over a book one has been fond of, or hear an opera into which one has formerly been able to put everything imaginable and a bit more — and sit empty-handed" [024-006]. Martin names the plaintiff: "one's old self, and that is dead and buried" [024-007].

## The Philosophy of Love

Turning to love, Henrik confides his deepest fear: "I have never even in my imagination begun the game otherwise than with the thought: may she be the first to tire, and not I!" [024-009]. He recounts seeing an old woman on the street who reminded him of the girl he loves, and the chill of imagining her "withered, discarded, a little comic" [024-011]. He would rather live "as wretched and exhausted as she" than know her abandoned [024-012].

## The Confession

Finally, Henrik reveals the woman's identity: "It is Sigrid Tesch" [024-013]. The chapter closes with Martin envisioning her — "a young and supple figure, with dark abundant hair and delicate regular features" — a woman who had sometimes passed through his own "twilight thoughts" with "a pallid dream smile" [024-014].
