## Overview

Tullman is a minor figure among Martin Birck's schoolmates, defined entirely by his pairing with Planius. The two are mentioned together in every appearance, forming a composite portrait of the conventional, unreflective student type against which Martin's interior complexity is measured.

At the graduation celebration, "Planius and Tullman presented the same docile countenances with which they had listened in class to the exposition of the instructor" [016-041]. Their passive absorption of whatever authority places before them — whether a classroom lecture or punch-fueled philosophy — marks them as members of the conformist majority that Martin instinctively resists.
