## Overview

The Long Row is a tenement building diagonally across from Martin Birck's home, described as "a low, dirt-gray range of houses" where the poorest residents of the neighborhood live [002-009]. It serves as a constant visual and social reminder of class difference in Martin's childhood world — a shadowy counterpoint to the comparative prosperity of the stone house.

## Description in the Text

Söderberg introduces the Long Row through Martin's perspective, filtered through adult commentary:

> Long Row, diagonally across from it, lay in shadow; a low, dirt-gray range of houses. Only really poor people lived there, Martin's mother said. Only scum, said Mrs. Lundgren. [002-009]

The building is associated with darkness and shadow, in contrast to the sunlit stone house. A "flock of chickens trailed out from the yard of Long Row" [002-010], suggesting a semi-rural poverty that distinguishes its residents from the more established households.

## Narrative Function

The Long Row functions primarily as a symbol of social stratification, made concrete through physical space. Its proximity to Martin's home — it is always visible, always present — means that class difference is an inescapable fact of his daily experience. The bully Franz, who appears in Martin's nightmare "making an ugly face with his thick, raw-looking lips" [001-001], is from the Long Row, and his aggression carries the threat of class resentment.

The contrast between the stone house and the Long Row also prefigures one of Söderberg's recurring themes: the arbitrariness and fragility of social position. Martin's family is only one step removed from the Long Row; their respectability is maintained through effort and convention, not intrinsic superiority.

## Associated Characters

- **Franz** — A bully from the Long Row who spits on Martin's jacket and calls him "stuck-up."
- **Mrs. Lundgren** — Anxious to distinguish herself from the Long Row residents.
- **Martin's mother** — More measured in her judgment but equally aware of the social divide.
