## Overview

The Swedish Royal Family appears in the novel not as individual personalities but as a ceremonial presence — the public performers of a seasonal ritual that organizes Stockholm's civic life. "Spring at last, real spring. It came first when the Royal Family drove out to the big park with their plumed and golden equipage" [009-001]. The royal procession is the signal that winter has ended: the city dresses in bright colors, the Life Guards stand at attention, and the crowds follow the carriages to Djurgården.

For the young Martin, the Royal Family belongs to the pageantry of public life — distant, golden, and benign. "The king had long since driven home to his castle" by the time Martin's day darkens into exhaustion and the encounter with the Punch and Judy theater. The royal presence frames the day's emotional arc: from the exhilaration of the procession to the bewildering violence of the puppet show to the drunken scuffles of the journey home.
