## Overview

Martin Birck's graduation party takes place at a restaurant terrace on a spring evening. Having received his certificate with the words "Graduated with honor" — a result that surprised him given his academic apathy during his final terms — Martin joins his classmates for the traditional celebration. The scene is one of the novel's most extended and carefully orchestrated set-pieces, bringing together nearly every named schoolmate and establishing the social dynamics that will persist into adult life.

The party unfolds in three movements: the formal dinner with punch and speeches, where the old classroom groupings reassert themselves; the dispersal into the Stockholm night, where the white student caps gleam in the darkness; and the encounter with streetwalkers, where each young man reveals his character through action rather than words. The pale girl with black eyes whom Martin is too hesitant to approach becomes the object of his sleepless romantic reverie — and is claimed instead by the unreflective Planius.
