[014-001] Home was quieter and more empty than before. [[maria-birck|Maria]], [[martin-birck|Martin]]'s sister, had been married a year ago to a doctor who lived far away in the country, and grandmother was no longer there.

[014-002] In the evening Martin and his companions were to have a supper at [[hasselbacken|Hasselbacken]]. Martin's father gave him five crowns to offer to the joy of [[youth-in-martin-birck|youth]], and his mother took him aside and said: "Martin, Martin, you must promise me to be careful tonight and not be led into any foolishness. Don't make a point of emptying your glass every [[time-in-martin-birck|time]] anyone drinks a toast with you, or you'll lose your head. The best thing would be just to pretend you drank. And I must tell you, Martin, that there is a class of dreadful women who do nothing else but try to lead young men to their destruction. You must beware of them especially. Dear Martin, if I only knew you had given yourself to the Lord and had your thoughts fixed on Him, I shouldn't be anxious about you; but I know you don't do that. Their very breath is poisonous; if you only stand on [[martin-bircks-neighborhood|the street]] and talk to such a woman, you may catch the most frightful diseases that no doctor in the world can cure."

[014-003] "Mother dear," said Martin, "you're always getting off on that."

[014-004] He took up his white cap, said goodbye and went.

[014-005] His mother followed him with troubled eyes, and when he was gone, sat down in a dark corner and wept. For she knew she was going to lose him as mothers always lose their sons.
