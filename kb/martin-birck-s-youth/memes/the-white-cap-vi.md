[018-001] When one asks a young man who has just passed his [[education-in-martin-birck|school]] examinations, "What do you intend to be?" he cannot answer, "A poet." People would turn away their heads and put their hands over their mouths. He may answer, a lawyer or a painter or a musician, for a man can train himself for all these fields at some public institution, and even in one's apprenticeship one has a modest place in the community, a [[work-in-martin-birck|profession]] to follow, one already *is *something; a student at the university, or a pupil in the art school or the conservatory. It is not much, but still it is always a sop to throw to indiscreet questioners, and a conceivable [[time-in-martin-birck|future]] to point to in the case of these more kindly disposed. But he who is to become a poet is nothing but a mockery before [[faith-in-martin-birck|God]] and man until he is recognized and famous. He must therefore during all his long prentice years hang a false sign over his door and pretend to be busy at something that people consider respectable.

[018-002] This [[martin-birck|Martin]] realized, he found it perfectly natural and not to be altered, and so when his father asked him what he was to be, he answered not that he meant to become a poet but that he should like to work as an extra in a government office. His father was pleased with this answer, perceiving in it a sign that his son would be as sensible and happy as himself. He had feared that Martin might want to go to [[uppsala|Uppsala]] and study [[art-in-martin-birck|aesthetics]] and he felt within himself that he could not have refused, but he trembled at all the outlay and trouble there would be for a poor father of a family to keep a son at the university. He was therefore delighted with the reply and had nothing to remark except that Martin ought to try to enter not one office but as many as possible. That evening he invited his son to go to Blanch's café to hear the [[music-in-martin-birck|music]] and drink toddy.

[018-003] But the very next day he put the affair in motion, speaking with his acquaintances in various departments and helping Martin to write applications.
