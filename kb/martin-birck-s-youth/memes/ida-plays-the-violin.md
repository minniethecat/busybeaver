## Overview

Ida's violin performance and the subsequent Catholic ritual are among the most vivid episodes of Martin's childhood visits to the Dupont household. The scene reveals Ida's theatrical imagination and exposes Martin to a religious world utterly foreign to his Lutheran upbringing.

## Ida's Violin

After sharing wine and the peepshow box, Ida took down an old violin from the wall and began to play. "It sounded very queer the way Ida played the violin" [003-073]. She danced and swayed while wearing a white veil, and Martin "held his ears, laughed, sung, and screamed" [003-073]. The chaotic performance was interrupted when Martin recalled his mother's warning that Ida Dupont had fleas, sending a chill down his back.

## The Catholic Ritual

The scene shifted to the sleeping alcove, where a madonna stood behind half-burned wax candles beneath a crucifix. Ida, swathed in pink tulle with gold spangles, lit the candles and a pastille of incense. "In long blue clouds the incense curled from under the curtain of the alcove, and the air grew heavy with a strong spicy fragrance" [003-087]. She fell to her knees before the madonna, her dark-red plaits glowing "like bright copper in the candlelight" [003-093], and made strange gestures. When Martin asked what she was doing, she whispered solemnly: "That is our religion" [003-083].
