# Rouen

**Rouen** is a historic city in Normandy, [[france|France]], on the River Seine. During the First World War, it became a crucial logistical hub for the [[british-army|British Expeditionary Force]] — a staging post for troops heading to the front and a major center for military hospitals treating the [[casualty|wounded]].

## In the Narrative

Holmes first passed through Rouen on his way to the front, after the cross-Channel journey from [[england|England]]:

> "…time we reached Rouen, twenty-four hours later, we…" [002-006]

The city reappears in the narrative under darker circumstances. After being [[casualty|wounded]], Holmes notes the fate of comrades who passed through Rouen's hospitals:

> "…Green died a few days later at Rouen, and the Sergeant Major lost…" [014-032]

Holmes himself was treated at one of Rouen's many military hospitals:

> "…General Hospital Number 12 in Rouen. I was…" [014-140]

Rouen thus represents the full arc of a soldier's journey — the initial transit toward danger, and later (for those who survived) a way station on the road to recovery or, for those less fortunate, a final destination.

## Rouen's Wartime Role

During WWI, Rouen was a vital component of the British logistical system:

- **Base depot**: Supplies, ammunition, and reinforcements flowed through Rouen to the front
- **[[hospital|Hospital]] center**: Multiple general hospitals and stationary hospitals ringed the city, treating [[casualty|casualties]] evacuated from the front by ambulance train
- **Transit camp**: Troops arriving from [[england|England]] passed through Rouen's camps and depots before entraining for their units
- **Rest and recuperation**: Convalescent depots allowed recovering soldiers to regain strength before returning to duty

By 1918, the Rouen area contained over 20 hospitals with tens of thousands of beds, making it one of the largest medical complexes on the [[france|Western Front]].
