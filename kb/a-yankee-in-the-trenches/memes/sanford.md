# Sanford

## Overview

Sanford was one of [[r-derby-holmes|R. Derby Holmes]]'s comrades in the [[trench|trenches]] — a fellow soldier whose presence in the narrative, though brief, carries the weight of the war's casual violence. Holmes mentions him in passing, but the context leaves no doubt about the bond between men who shared the [[trench|trenches]].

## Role in the Narrative

Sanford appears in the memoir as one of the familiar names in Holmes's platoon — a man whose company was part of the fabric of [[trench-life|daily trench life]]. The shared experience of the front created relationships of unusual intensity, and Holmes's references to Sanford, while brief, reflect the deep mutual reliance that characterized trench friendships.
