# Wellsie

## Overview

Wellsie — also called "Wells" — was one of Holmes's closest comrades in the [[trench|trenches]], a cheerful cockney private whose humor and fatalism embodied the [[tommy|British soldier]]'s spirit. He reappears throughout the memoir, from the early days of [[trench-life|trench routine]] to the final offensive, a constant presence in Holmes's war.

## Role in the Narrative

Wellsie is introduced in the context of [[trench-rations|rations]], when Holmes is detailed for the hated duty and Wellsie, with characteristic cockney humor, dismisses his complaints:

> "Garn," says Wellsie. "Wot's the diff if yer gets it 'ere or there. If ye clicks, I'll draw yer fags from Blighty and say a prayer for yer soul. On yer way."
> — (007-261)

Holmes's assessment is immediate and affectionate:

> Cheerful beggar, Wellsie. He was doing me a favor and didn't know it.
> — (007-264)

The ration party detail — the "favor" Wellsie unknowingly did — turned out to be the assignment that removed Holmes from the front line at a critical moment. Wellsie's casual intervention, treated as nothing at the time, may have saved Holmes's life.

When Holmes returns to the battalion after hospital leave, Wellsie is the first to greet him:

> I sure was glad when old Wellsie hopped out and grabbed me. "Gawd lumme, Darby," he said. "Hi sye, an' me thinkin' as 'ow you was back in Blighty."
> — (012-347)

On learning they were going back into [[battle-of-the-somme|the Somme]], Wellsie voiced the collective exasperation of the men:

> "Gawd lumme," says Wellsie, "'ave we got to fight th' 'ole blinkin' war. Is it right? I awsks yer. Is it?"
> — (013-003)

In the hours before the final offensive, Wellsie brought the news — delivering the heaviest tidings with his usual sigh. He had been sent to check on a summons for [[platoon|platoon command]]ers and returned with the orders:

> Wellsie sank dejectedly down beside me. "Darby," he sighed hopelessly, "wot th' blinkin' 'ell do you think 'appened?"
> — (013-190)

They were [[over-the-top|going over the top]] in the first wave.
