# Battle of the Somme

## Overview

The Battle of the Somme, launched on July 1, 1916, was one of the defining [[battle|battles]] of World War I — and one of the bloodiest military ope[[trench-rations|rations]] in human history. By the time it ended in November 1916, over a million men had been [[casualty|killed]] or [[casualty|wounded]]. [[r-derby-holmes|R. Derby Holmes]] participated in the Somme offensive and his account provides a soldier's-eye view of the battle's grim realities.

## In the Memoir

Holmes's chapter on the Somme is one of the memoir's most intense sections. He describes the preparatory [[artillery|bombardment]], the tension of waiting to go [[over-the-top|over the top]], and the chaos of the advance. The battle is rendered not as a strategic overview but as a sequence of visceral moments: the whistle blow, the scramble up the ladders, the sprint across [[no-mans-land|no man's land]], and the sudden, shocking impact of a bullet.

Holmes was [[casualty|wounded]] during the Somme and evacuated to hospital — a [[blighty-wound|Blighty wound]] that, for a time, took him out of the war. His description of the battlefield and its aftermath is unsparing.
