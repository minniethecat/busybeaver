# Le Havre

**Le Havre** is a major port city on the Normandy coast of [[france|France]], at the mouth of the River Seine. During the First World War, it served as one of the principal disem[[estaminet|bar]]kation points for British and Commonwealth forces arriving from [[england|England]] — the gateway to the [[france|Western Front]] for [[hun]]dreds of thousands of soldiers.

## In the Narrative

Holmes crossed the Channel from [[england|England]] to [[france|France]] through Le Havre, joining the immense flow of troops making the same journey:

> "…put aboard the transport for Havre. The next day we…" [001-223]

The brief mention underscores the matter-of-fact nature of the transit — a soldier's journey reduced to a few words about a transport ship and a [[france|French]] port. Holmes later mentions a comrade who served in a different capacity there:

> "…Sergeant Page was away in Havre as instructor for a…" [013-178]

## Gateway to the Front

Le Havre's wartime significance extended far beyond Holmes's brief passage:

- **Primary base port**: Along with Boulogne and [[rouen|Rouen]], Le Havre was one of the [[british-army|British Expeditionary Force]]'s main supply ports
- **Troop movements**: Tens of thousands of reinforcements disem[[estaminet|bar]]ked monthly at Le Havre's harbor
- **[[hospital|Hospital]] facilities**: The port had medical facilities for [[casualty|wounded]] evacuated from the front
- **Supply chain**: Food, ammunition, equipment, and mail all funneled through ports like Le Havre

For most [[tommy|British soldier]]s of the Great War, Le Havre meant the moment the war became real — the first sight of [[france|France]], the first step toward the [[trench|trenches]].
