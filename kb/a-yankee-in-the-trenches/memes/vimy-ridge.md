# Vimy Ridge

## Overview

The [[battle|Battle]] of [[vimy|Vimy Ridge]] in April 1917 was a meticulously planned Canadian Corps offensive that succeeded where previous [[france|French]] and British attempts had failed. The capture of the heavily fortified ridge was a significant tactical victory and became a foundational moment in Canadian national memory. [[r-derby-holmes|R. Derby Holmes]] fought at [[vimy|Vimy Ridge]] and records the experience in his memoir.

## In the Memoir

Holmes's account of [[vimy|Vimy Ridge]] focuses on the preparation and precision that distinguished this battle from the more chaotic offensives he had experienced. The Canadian Corps employed innovative tactics — [[barrage|creeping barrage]]s, detailed maps issued to every soldier, rehearsed advances — that made the assault more effective and less costly than [[battle-of-the-somme|the Somme]].

The chapter conveys a sense of professional competence: these were soldiers who knew what they were doing, executing a plan that made sense. Holmes's tone is noticeably different from his [[somme|Somme]] account — less desperate, more controlled.
