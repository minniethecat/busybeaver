# Mr. May

## Overview

Mr. May — referred to formally as [[lieutenant-may|Lieutenant May]] — was a British officer in Holmes's battalion, remembered by the author in superlative terms. On the night of a [[raid|trench raid]], Holmes was assigned to select the men and May was placed in direct command. In a handful of paragraphs, Holmes sketches a portrait of quiet courage.

## Role in the Narrative

On a night when Holmes was ordered to organize a [[grenade|bomb]]ing party, [[lieutenant-may|Lieutenant May]] was announced as the officer in charge:

> That very night I was called upon to take charge of a bombing party of twelve men. A lieutenant, Mr. May, one of the bravest men I ever knew, was to be of the party and in direct command.
> — (012-028)

The assessment — "one of the bravest men I ever knew" — is one of the highest compliments in the memoir. It comes without elaboration: Holmes does not list May's exploits or describe specific acts of heroism. The statement stands alone because it is complete.

[[captain-green|Captain Green]] briefed both Holmes and [[lieutenant-may|Lieutenant May]] together, outlining the plan:

> Take your twelve men out there at ten o'clock and _take that trench_! You will take only bombs with you. You and Mr. May will have revolvers.
> — (012-044)

May appears only in this single episode — the briefing and the [[trench-raid|raid]] — but the impression he left on Holmes was permanent enough to earn that single, unqualified sentence of praise.
