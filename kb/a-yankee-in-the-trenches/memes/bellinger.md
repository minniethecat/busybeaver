# Bellinger

## Overview

Bellinger was a [[tommy|British soldier]] who accompanied Holmes on a nerve-wracking [[patrol|night patrol]] near the German lines. Though he appears only in a single episode, the [[patrol]] sequence is one of the memoir's most vivid passages — and Bellinger's caution, which Holmes initially resisted, likely saved both their lives.

## The Night Patrol

Holmes introduces Bellinger matter-of-factly — he had volunteered for [[patrol]] duty and was paired with a single companion. The [[patrol]]'s objective was reconnaissance near [[grenay|Bully-Grenay]], but the two men quickly became disoriented in the darkness of [[no-mans-land|No Man's Land]]:

> I went over with one man, a fellow named Bellinger. We got out and started to crawl. All we knew was that the left sector was two hundred yards away.
> (006-152)

Machine-gun bullets cracked overhead as they advanced. When German [[very-light|flare]]s illuminated the ground, the two men froze, pressing themselves into the earth:

> I tried to lie still and burrow into the dirt at the same time. Nothing happened. Presently the lights died, and Bellinger gave me a poke in the ribs. We started to crawfish.
> (006-165)

The poke in the ribs is the only direct communication between the two men during the [[patrol]] — an entire conversation compressed into a single physical gesture. In the absolute silence required by [[night-patrol|night reconnaissance]], touch replaced speech.

## The Argument

After they were spotted and a fusillade of [[machine-gun|machine-gun fire]] ripped the air above them, the two men faced a life-or-death decision — move now or wait:

> I was all for getting out and away immediately. Bellinger wanted to wait a while. We argued for as much as five minutes, I should think, and then the lights having gone out, I had my way and we started to crawfish again.
> (006-174)

Why they were not seen — and [[casualty|killed]] — Holmes could not explain. "Why we weren't seen I don't know, but we had gone all of one [[hun]]dred yards before they spotted us" (006-165). Bellinger's instinct for caution, which Holmes initially resisted, likely saved both men by preventing a panicked retreat under the [[very-light|flare]]s.
