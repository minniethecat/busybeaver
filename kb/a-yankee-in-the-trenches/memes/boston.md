# Boston

**Boston, Massachusetts**, was the [[blighty|home]]town of [[r-derby-holmes|R. Derby Holmes]] and the American city where his journey to the [[france|Western Front]] began. A bastion of New [[england|England]] patriotism and a major port, Boston was where the author enlisted and em[[estaminet|bar]]ked for [[england|England]].

## In the Narrative

Holmes was brought to Boston as an infant and considered it [[blighty|home]]:

> "…to Boston as a baby and have lived here…" [001-030]

The recruitment office that launched his war journey was on Boston's waterfront:

> "…office on Commercial Street, Boston, where they…" [001-056]

One of the most colorful characters Holmes met had a vivid — if geographically confused — imagination about Boston:

> "…the redskins scalped people on Boston Common. After that he used to…" [004-077]

When asked where he was from, Holmes answered simply:

> "I answered, 'Boston.'" [008-299]

Reflecting after his return, Holmes described his [[blighty|home]]town with the affection of a veteran looking back:

> "…sober and staid old Boston." [015-179]

A crowded troop train in [[france|France]] reminded him of [[blighty|home]]:

> "…It was a kind of sardine or Boston Elevated effect, and by the…" [002-005]

## Boston in the Great War

While the United States did not enter WWI until April 1917, Boston was a center of pro-Allied sentiment and recruitment activity long before. Key aspects of Boston's role:

- **British recruitment**: The office on Commercial Street was one of many venues where British authorities recruited horse tenders, drivers, and other specialists in the neutral United States
- **Port of em[[estaminet|bar]]kation**: Volunteers and later American troops departed from Boston's harbor
- **Irish-American tensions**: Boston's large Irish-American population had complex feelings about [[battle|fight]]ing alongside Britain
- **New [[england|England]] patriotism**: As Holmes notes, there was "a patriot in New [[england|England]]" eager to join the [[battle|fight]] [001-059]
