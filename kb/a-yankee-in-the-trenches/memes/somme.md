# Somme

The **Somme** ([sɔm]) is a river and region in northern [[france|France]] that became one of the most iconic and devastating battlegrounds of the First World War. For [[r-derby-holmes|R. Derby Holmes]] and his comrades, "[[battle-of-the-somme|the Somme]]" meant the front-line [[trench|trenches]] where they faced mud, shellfire, and the relentless attrition of [[trench|trench warfare]].

## In the Narrative

Holmes opens his memoir with a reference to [[battle-of-the-somme|the Somme]], immediately anchoring his story in the geography of the [[france|Western Front]]:

> "Once, on the Somme in the fall of 1916, when I had been over a month and had been gassed and patched up…" [001-001]

The Somme appears throughout the narrative as a constant backdrop — the place where the author experienced his most harrowing moments. In Chapter VIII, he describes being in the middle of a [[estaminet|bar]]rage:

> "It was on the Somme. We were in the middle of a barrage…" [008-129]

Later, Holmes describes the widespread devastation that characterized [[battle-of-the-somme|the Somme]] sector during the [[big-push|Big Push]] of 1916:

> "…the Somme district was battered and smashed beyond description…" [009-133]

Distances and movement were particularly challenging in this sector due to the intense [[battle|fight]]ing and destroyed infrastructure:

> "Distances were great on the Somme, as the Big Push was in full swing…" [012-065]

The author departed and later returned to [[battle-of-the-somme|the Somme]], underscoring the sector's centrality to his war experience:

> "I rejoined on the Somme again just as they were going into the line…" [012-343]

After experiencing the relative quiet of the Ypres sector, Holmes reflects on the contrast:

> "…paradise now compared with the Somme." [013-009]

## Key Events on the Somme

- **Gas attack and wounding** (Fall 1916): Holmes was [[gas-warfare|gas]]sed and sent to recover, the incident that opens the memoir [001-001]
- **[[trench|Trench]] holding and [[estaminet|bar]]rages**: Multiple chapters describe the daily reality of life under shellfire in Somme [[trench|trenches]] [008-129, 008-314]
- **The [[big-push|Big Push]]**: The Somme Offensive of 1916, referenced as "the [[big-push|Big Push]]," created extreme conditions for the soldiers holding the line [012-065, 016-159]
- **Final return and the tank attack**: Holmes rejoined his unit on [[battle-of-the-somme|the Somme]] just before the tank assault in which he was severely [[casualty|wounded]] [012-343]

## Military Context

The Somme sector was the site of the **[[battle-of-the-somme|Battle of the Somme]]** (July–November 1916), one of the largest [[battle|battles]] of the First World War with over one million [[casualty|casualties]]. The [[british-army|British Army]] launched a major offensive along a 25-mile front, hoping to break through German lines. The battle has become synonymous with the horrors of [[trench|trench warfare]] — mud, [[machine-gun|machine guns]], [[barrage|artillery barrage]]s, and staggering loss of life for minimal territorial gain.

While Holmes does not describe specific set-piece [[battle|battles]] of [[battle-of-the-somme|the Somme]] offensive, the conditions he records — constant [[shell|shelling]], destroyed villages, churned earth, and the grinding everyday reality of trench holding — are characteristic of [[battle-of-the-somme|the Somme]] sector during and after the [[big-push|Big Push]].
