## Overview

Lieutenant May was a [[british-army|British Army]] officer in the [[queens-royal-west-surrey|Queen's Royal West Surrey Regiment]] who appears in the memoir as the leader of a dangerous night [[grenade|bomb]]ing [[trench-raid|raid]] during [[battle-of-the-somme|the Somme]] campaign. Holmes describes him with deep respect as "one of the bravest men I ever knew" (003-017).

## Role in the Narrative

Lieutenant May was placed in direct command of the [[grenade|bomb]]ing [[trench-raid|raid]] party, with Holmes selecting the men. [[captain-green|Captain Green]] briefed them both on the mission — to capture a German [[mine-explosion|sap]] or small trench beyond [[high-wood|High Wood]]:

> Captain Green had me up along with Lieutenant May early in the evening, and as nearly as I can remember these were his instructions: "Just beyond High Wood and to the left there is a sap or small trench leading out to a crater."
> (003-017)

## The Night Raid

Lieutenant May led the party [[over-the-top|over the top]] in strict silence, navigating a thousand yards of open ground to reach the objective:

> Lieutenant May took us out over the top on schedule time, and we started for the position to be taken. We walked erect but in the strictest silence for about a thousand yards.
> (003-018)

During the [[trench-raid|raid]], when the [[germans|Germans]] counter-attacked with [[grenade|bomb]]s, Lieutenant May directed the [[battle|fight]]ing — a "seesaw game" of rushing and being rushed in the narrow trench. Despite receiving a wound, he continued to lead:

> After we had lost three men and Lieutenant May had got a slight wound, we got desperate and got out of the trench and rushed them for further orders.
> (003-021)

## Aftermath

When the position was finally secured, Holmes counted the [[casualty|casualties]]. Lieutenant May was among the [[casualty|wounded]] and was sent back with an escort:

> After the row was over we counted noses and found four dead and three slightly wounded, including Lieutenant May. I detailed two men to take the wounded and the Lieutenant back.
> (003-022)

Lieutenant May promised to return with reinforcements, leaving Holmes and the remaining men to consolidate the captured position.

## Character

Holmes' portrait of Lieutenant May is one of quiet courage and professional competence. Unlike some officers who earned the soldiers' contempt, May led from the front and shared the dangers. His wounding during the [[trench-raid|raid]] exemplified the high casualty rate among junior officers on the [[france|Western Front]].
