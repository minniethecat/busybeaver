# Nap

The **nap** or **napper** was the [[tommy|British Tommy]]'s word for his head — the most precious and vulnerable part of his anatomy in the [[trench|trenches]]. "Keep your napper down!" was the first and most important lesson a new arrival learned.

## Usage in the Narrative

Holmes records the warning [[estaminet|bar]]ked at new recruits:

> "'Keep your nappers down, you rooks. Don't look…'" [002-073]

The veteran knew the consequences of carelessness:

> "…you stick the old 'napper' up in daylight." [002-080]

A new arrival from [[england|England]] was derided:

> "…of any young whipper-snapper just out from Blighty with…" [002-123]

Even seasoned soldiers joked about the risk:

> "…give me bloomin' napper to stroll down the Strand…" [002-154]

The glossary entry formalized the term:

> "…the top without exposing the napper." [016-254]

## Life Below the Parapet

The napper was the soldier's most exposed body part. The [[trench|trenches]] were designed so that a man standing upright was protected from direct fire — but the moment he raised his head above the parapet, he invited a [[sniper]]'s bullet. German [[sniper|snipers]] were known for their patience and accuracy. "Keep your napper down" was not casual advice but a survival imperative.
