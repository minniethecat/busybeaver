## Overview

The Souchez area, named for the Souchez River, was part of the [[vimy|Vimy Ridge]] sector on the [[france|Western Front]]. Located in the Pas-de-Calais region of northern [[france|France]], it was the scene of heavy [[battle|fight]]ing throughout the war and the location of trench systems where Holmes's battalion served.

## In the Memoir

Holmes mentions the Souchez sector in the context of movement between positions. The area was characterized by the same devastated landscape common to the [[france|Western Front]] — [[crater|shell craters]], ruined villages, and an elaborate network of [[trench|trenches]] and communication ways.

The terrain here was flat and open in places, making daylight movement impossible. All troop rotations, ration deliveries, and [[patrol]]s had to be conducted under cover of darkness.

## Tactical Significance

The Souchez sector lay between the larger battlefields of Artois and [[battle-of-the-somme|the Somme]]. Holding this line was essential for the [[british-army|British Army]] to maintain the integrity of the [[france|Western Front]], even when no major offensives were underway in the immediate area.
