# Hand Grenade

The **[[mills-bomb|hand grenade]]** — most famously the British **Mills bomb** — was one of the transformative weapons of the First World War. It gave the individual infantryman the ability to deliver high-explosive force by hand, clearing enemy positions without exposing himself to fire. In the confined geometry of the trench system, grenades were often more effective than [[rifle|rifles]].

## In the Narrative

Holmes mentions Mills bombs frequently — they were central to [[raid|trench raid]]ing:

> "of 'Mills' bombs and 'Stokeses.'" [003-082]

He describes using Mills bombs in close [[battle|combat]]:

> "out two or three Millses and away we went." [003-120]

A comrade used them decisively:

> "Blofeld chucked in two or three Millses and away we went." [003-118]

[[patrol|Patrol]]s carried a standard load:

> "patrols. He carries two or three Mills bombs and a pistol, but not…" [007-046]

In a tense moment, Holmes prepared to throw:

> "and they hadn't heard me. I got out a Mills, pulled the pin, and…" [007-132]

## The Mills Bomb

The **Mills bomb** (Number 5) was the standard British [[mills-bomb|hand grenade]], introduced in 1915. It featured:

- **Serrated cast-iron body** that fragmented into lethal shrapnel on detonation
- **"Pineapple" shape** for grip and fragmentation pattern
- **Time fuse**: 4–7 seconds after releasing the lever
- **Lethal radius**: Approximately 30 yards (27 meters)
- **Safety pin** with ring, pulled just before throwing

Before 1914, grenades were considered specialist weapons. The [[trench|trench warfare]] of the [[france|Western Front]] changed everything — grenades became standard infantry issue. Bombers (grenadiers) were specialized assault troops who led trench-clearing ope[[trench-rations|rations]], throwing grenades into each successive bay before rushing in with [[pistol|revolver]]s and [[bayonet|bayonets]].
