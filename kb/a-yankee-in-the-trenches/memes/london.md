# London

**London** was the beating heart of the British Empire during the First World War — the capital city where soldiers arrived, trained, went on leave, and returned to heal. For [[r-derby-holmes|R. Derby Holmes]], an American who crossed the Atlantic to [[battle|fight]], London was his first sight of [[england|England]] and later his refuge as a [[casualty|wounded]] man.

## In the Narrative

Holmes and his fellow horse tenders sailed from [[boston|Boston]] bound for London:

> "We landed in London on Saturday night about nine-thirty…" [001-088]

Their initial accommodations were humble — a reminder that even the imperial capital had its rough edges:

> "…were quartered was a typical London doss house." [001-096]

During his brief time before shipping to [[france|France]], Holmes explored the city:

> "…the day seeing the sights of London, and…" [001-145]

A popular soldiers' song of the period captured the longing for [[blighty|home]] and the capital:

> "Put me on the tryne for London town…" [002-136]

Holmes had personal connections that made London meaningful:

> "…a supply. I had friends in London…" [005-144]

After being severely [[casualty|wounded]] by a tank attack, London became the destination Holmes most desired:

> "…me I could go to a London hospital as I had asked, for…" [015-046]

He arrived as a casualty, welcomed by the city's wartime hospitality:

> "I arrived in London on Tuesday and was taken in a…" [015-050]

Holmes was deeply moved by London's treatment of its [[casualty|wounded]] soldiers:

> "London is good to the wounded men. The…" [015-078]

## Wartime London

During WWI, London was transformed by the demands of total war:

- **Recruitment and training**: Thousands of volunteers and conscripts passed through London's [[estaminet|bar]]racks and training facilities
- **Military hospitals**: London's many hospitals — converted hotels, schools, and purpose-built facilities — treated tens of thousands of [[casualty|wounded]] soldiers
- **Zeppelin [[trench-raid|raid]]s**: The city endured German air [[trench-raid|raid]]s, bringing the war to the civilian population
- **Imperial hub**: Soldiers from across the Empire — Canadians, Australians, New Zealanders, Indians — passed through London

## A Soldier's London

For the ordinary [[tommy|Tommy]], London meant different things at different stages: a first bewildering taste of military life for the raw recruit; a few precious days of leave with friends and family; or, for the [[casualty|wounded]] like Holmes, a city that "is good to the [[casualty|wounded]] men" — offering care, comfort, and a first step back toward normal life.
