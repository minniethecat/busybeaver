## Overview

Mametz was a small village on [[battle-of-the-somme|the Somme]] battlefield, one of the objectives captured by British forces in the opening days of the great offensive of July 1916. Located near the boundary between the British and [[france|French]] sectors, the village and the nearby Mametz Wood became the scene of fierce [[battle|fight]]ing during the [[battle-of-the-somme|Battle of the Somme]].

## Strategic Context

The capture of Mametz on July 1, 1916, was one of the few successes of the first day of [[battle-of-the-somme|the Somme]] offensive — a day otherwise remembered for the catastrophic [[casualty|losses]] suffered by the [[british-army|British Army]]. The village was taken by the 7th Division, but [[battle|fight]]ing continued in the surrounding woods and fields for weeks afterward.

## In the Memoir

Holmes arrived on [[battle-of-the-somme|the Somme]] front after the initial captures, when the battlefield had already been transformed into the devastated landscape he describes. While he does not mention Mametz by name in detail, the village was part of the operational landscape through which he moved during the later stages of the offensive.
