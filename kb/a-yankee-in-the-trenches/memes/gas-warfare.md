# Gas Warfare

## Overview

Gas warfare was one of the most terrifying innovations of World War I. Unlike bullets or [[shell|shells]], gas was invisible, indiscriminate, and produced lingering, agonizing effects. By the time [[r-derby-holmes|R. Derby Holmes]] arrived at the front, both sides had integrated gas into their tactical repertoire, and every soldier carried protective equipment as a matter of routine.

Holmes's training included preparation for [[gas-weapon|gas attack]]s from the outset. During his basic instruction in [[england|England]], he was drilled in:

> the various forms of gas and protection from it
> — (001-227)

## Types of Gas

Holmes encountered at least two distinct types of gas during his service:

**[[gas-weapon|Poison Gas]]**: The lethal variety, deployed via canisters and released to drift across [[no-mans-land|no man's land]] toward the enemy. During a [[trench-raid|raid]] preparation, Holmes observed the gas apparatus up close:

> This was the poison gas ready for release over the top
> — (003-015)

The effectiveness of gas depended heavily on wind conditions. Holmes describes a [[trench-raid|raid]] where a fifteen-mile wind carried the gas toward the German lines, prompting a fellow soldier to mutter:

> "May it finish the blighters."
> — (003-045)

**Tear Gas**: A newer, non-lethal but incapacitating agent that Holmes first encountered later in the war:

> This delectable vapor came over in shells, comparatively harmless in themselves, but which loosed a gas
> — (009-039)–(009-040)

The tear gas was nonetheless effective at disorienting soldiers and forcing them to don protective equipment that severely limited visibility and breathing.

## Protective Equipment

The standard [[gas-mask|gas helmet]] was Holmes's constant companion — and his constant complaint. Every inspection included verification of gas equipment:

> Inspection of ammo and gas masks. One hour drill.
> — (005-139)

When moving into position, each soldier carried:

> rounds of ammo, a tin hat, two gas helmets, and a lot of
> — (008-072)

The [[gas-helmet|gas mask]] was particularly despised. Holmes called it "the bane of my existence." The goggles provided for tear gas proved nearly useless in practice, forcing soldiers to fall back on the full [[gas-mask|gas helmet]]:

> Goggles provided for this gas were nearly useless, and we all resorted to the regular gas helmet.
> — (009-045)–(009-046)
