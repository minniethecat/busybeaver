## Overview

Lens was a significant mining town in the Pas-de-Calais region of northern [[france|France]], located just behind the German lines opposite the British positions at [[grenay|Bully-Grenay]]. Before the war it was a center of coal [[mine-explosion|mining]]; during the war it became a German-held strongpoint and a reference point for British troops in the sector.

## In the Memoir

Holmes mentions Lens only briefly, as a geographical marker. [[grenay|Bully-Grenay]] is described as being:

> ...the little village of Bully-Grenay, less than three miles behind the lines. This is directly opposite Lens.
> (004-053)

The proximity to Lens placed Holmes's battalion in one of the most heavily contested sectors of the [[france|Western Front]]. The [[mine-explosion|mining]] infrastructure of the region — pitheads, slag heaps, and tunnel networks — became integrated into the trench systems of both sides.

## Mining and Warfare

The coal mines around Lens added a unique dimension to the [[battle|fight]]ing. Underground galleries, originally dug for coal, were expanded by military engineers for [[mine-explosion|mining]] ope[[trench-rations|rations]] — the digging of tunnels beneath enemy lines to place explosive charges, a tactic Holmes would encounter firsthand in the mine explosions on the front.
