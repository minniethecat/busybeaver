# Persuader

The **persuader** — also called a persuader stick or trench club — was a brutal close-[[billets|quarters]] weapon carried by [[tommy|British soldier]]s on night [[raid|trench raid]]s. Unlike the rifle and bayonet, the persuader was designed for silent killing: a heavy bludgeon that could crush a man's skull without alerting the entire trench line.

## In the Narrative

Holmes describes the persuader as standard [[raid|raiding]] equipment, along with other specialized close-[[battle|combat]] weapons:

> "served with persuader sticks or knuckle knives, and…" [003-031]

He provides a precise description of the weapon:

> "The persuader is a short, heavy bludgeon with…" [003-033]

In [[battle|action]], the persuader was deployed decisively. Holmes describes his comrade [[germans|Jerry]] in [[battle|combat]]:

> "…and unlimber his persuader on a huge German who had just…" [003-117]

The weapon was considered essential emergency equipment:

> "…in extreme emergency. Also a persuader stick or a…" [007-042]

A single solid blow was usually enough:

> "…get in one good lick with the persuader. He will retreat…" [007-057]

The persuader could be lost in the chaos of [[battle|combat]]:

> "missed him with the persuader and lost my grip on it, leaving…" [007-136]

Even when disarmed, the instinct was to recover it:

> "…staggered back groping for the persuader." [007-147]

## Design and Purpose

The persuader was typically a short wooden club, about 18–24 inches long, weighted with lead or iron at the striking end. Some had nails or studs driven into the head. The design prioritized:

- **Silence**: Unlike a [[revolver|pistol]] shot, a bludgeon made no noise to alert the enemy trench
- **Lethality**: A single blow to the head was usually fatal
- **Portability**: Short enough to be carried on a belt or tucked into webbing
- **Simplicity**: Many persuaders were handmade by unit armorers or the soldiers themselves
