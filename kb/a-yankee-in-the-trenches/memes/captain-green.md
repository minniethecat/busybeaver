## Overview

Captain Green was a [[british-army|British Army]] officer who served as Holmes's company commander on the [[france|Western Front]]. His appearances in the memoir trace a grim arc from command authority through difficult decisions to a fatal wounding — embodying the heavy toll the war took on junior officers.

## The Crucifixion Incident

Green's most morally complex moment came when Holmes reported a shocking discovery — a Canadian sergeant found crucified on a [[estaminet|bar]]n door by German forces:

> I told Captain Green of the occurrence when I got back to our own trenches, and he ordered me to keep silent, which I did. It was feared that if the story got about the men would be so enraged that they would kill the first German prisoners they got their hands on.
> (007-040)

This order to suppress the truth reflected the brutal calculus of command — maintaining [[trench-discipline|discipline]] and preventing reprisal killings, even at the cost of burying an atrocity.

## Promotion to Bomber

After the successful tank assault, Green summoned Holmes with mixed news — a "promotion" to the battalion [[grenade|bomb]]ers (the "Suicide Club"):

> It must have been two days after we went over the top with the tanks that Captain Green had me up and told me that I was promoted. At least that was what he called it. I differed with him, but didn't say so.
> (008-045)

Holmes "accepted the decision cheerfully — like a man being sentenced to be electrocuted — and managed to convey the impression to Captain Green that I was greatly elated" (008-045). The exchange reveals the dark humor and fatalism of soldiers assigned to the most dangerous duties.

## The Night Raid Briefing

Green personally briefed Holmes and [[lieutenant-may|Lieutenant May]] for the critical night [[grenade|bomb]]ing [[trench-raid|raid]] near [[high-wood|High Wood]]:

> Captain Green had me up along with Lieutenant May early in the evening, and as nearly as I can remember these were his instructions: "Just beyond High Wood and to the left there is a sap or small trench leading out to a crater."
> (003-017)

## Death

Captain Green was mortally [[casualty|wounded]] during a [[artillery|bombardment]], struck down alongside the [[sergeant-major|Sergeant Major]]:

> Presently one burst a little behind me, and down went Captain Green and the Sergeant Major with whom he had been talking. Captain Green died a few days later at Rouen, and the Sergeant Major died before they got him to the dressing station.
> (010-055)

His death at [[rouen|Rouen]] — far behind the lines in a [[hospital|base hospital]] — underscores how even officers removed from the front lines were never truly safe.
