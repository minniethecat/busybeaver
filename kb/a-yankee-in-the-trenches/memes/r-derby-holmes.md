## Overview

R. Derby Holmes is the author and first-person narrator of *A Yankee in the [[trench|Trench]]es*, a WWI memoir published after the war. An American from [[boston|Boston]], Massachusetts, Holmes volunteered for the [[british-army|British Army]] roughly a year and a half into the war, serving as an enlisted soldier (later promoted to Corporal) on the [[france|Western Front]], primarily in [[battle-of-the-somme|the Somme]] sector. His middle name "Derby" earned him the nickname "Darby the Yank" among his British comrades (001-004).

## Role in the Narrative

Holmes is the central figure of the memoir, recounting his experiences from enlistment through [[trench|trench warfare]], night [[trench-raid|raid]]s, [[gas-weapon|gas attack]]s, and eventual wounding. As an American [[battle|fight]]ing in a British regiment, he provides a unique outsider-insider perspective on trench life, British military culture, and the camaraderie of soldiers.

> "Hi sye, Yank. Wot th' bloody 'ell are you in this bloomin' row for? Ayen't there no trouble t' 'ome?" And for the life of me I couldn't answer.
> (001-004)

Holmes was motivated not by abstract patriotism but by a sense of adventure and the desire to witness history firsthand — "with so much history in the making, any unattached young man would be missing it if he did not take a part in the big game" (001-004).

## Key Appearances

Holmes appears throughout all 16 chapters as the first-person narrator. Key episodes include:

- **Enlistment and training**: Journey from [[boston|Boston]] to [[england|England]], joining the [[queens-royal-west-surrey|Queen's Royal West Surrey Regiment]] (001-005)
- **First trench experience**: Learning [[trench-life|trench routine]] and slang from Corporal [[wellsie|Wells]] (002-012)
- **The [[trench-raid|raid]]**: Participating in a successful night [[grenade|bomb]]ing [[trench-raid|raid]] under [[lieutenant-may|Lieutenant May]], earning commendation (003-020)
- **Rest in [[billet|billets]]**: Recovering behind the lines after the [[trench-raid|raid]] (004-025)
- **Night [[patrol]]**: Reconnaissance mission with [[bellinger|Bellinger]] near the German listening post (005-030)
- **Mine explosion**: Witnessing and surviving the German mine explosion at the Pimple (006-035)
- **Gas attack**: Enduring a [[gas-weapon|gas attack]] at [[grenay|Bully-Grenay]] (007-040)
- **The tank attack**: Watching [[tank|tanks]] in [[battle|action]] for the first time on [[battle-of-the-somme|the Somme]] (008-045)
- **Wounding and evacuation**: Being [[casualty|wounded]] and carried back by [[stretcher-bearer|stretcher bearers]] (010-055)

## The "Yank" Identity

Holmes' American identity was a constant source of both ribbing and curiosity from his British comrades. They discovered his middle name was Derby and "christened me 'Darby the Yank'" (001-004). Throughout the narrative he is addressed as "Yank" — a term of both affection and mild foreignness. His attempts to explain American life ([[boston|Boston]], skyscrapers, "beans") provided comic relief amid the grimness of [[trench|trench warfare]].

> They discovered that my middle name was Derby, and they christened me "Darby the Yank." Darby I remained as long as I was with them.
> (001-004)
