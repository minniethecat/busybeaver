# Revolver

The **[[pistol|revolver]]** was the standard British officer's [[pistol|sidearm]] and an essential weapon for [[raid|trench raid]]ing parties. In the confined space of a trench, where a long rifle was unwieldy, the [[pistol|revolver]] provided stopping power at arm's length — the last resort when stealth failed and the [[battle|fight]]ing went loud.

## In the Narrative

Holmes describes the desperate moment when [[germans|Jerry]]'s [[pistol|revolver]] ran dry:

> "…fired the last shot from his revolver and, believe…" [003-141]

A [[france|French]] civilian had cause to use one in a dramatic scene:

> "…just then mon beau-fils sees a revolver that hangs by the…" [004-133]

The weapon was seized and used:

> "…he leaps forward and grabs the revolver. And there he shoots the…" [004-135]

In the aftermath of a trench assault, [[pistol|revolver]]s were standard:

> "have revolvers. After taking the trench, company…" [012-045]

## The Webley Revolver

The standard British service [[pistol|revolver]] was the **[[pistol|Webley]] Mark VI**, a .455 caliber six-shot [[pistol|revolver]] adopted in 1915. It featured:

- **Six-round cylinder** loaded with .455 [[pistol|Webley]] cartridges
- **Double-[[battle|action]] trigger** for rapid fire at close range
- **Top-break design** for quick reloading
- **Effective range**: Approximately 50 yards (45 meters)

The [[pistol|Webley]] was renowned for its reliability in the mud and filth of the [[trench|trenches]] — conditions that often jammed [[pistol|automatic pistol]]s. Its heavy .455 round had formidable stopping power, making it lethal at trench-[[battle|fight]]ing distances. Officers purchased their own [[pistol|sidearm]]s; enlisted men in [[raiding-party|raiding parties]] were issued [[pistol|revolver]]s for specific missions.
