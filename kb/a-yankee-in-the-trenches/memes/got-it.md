# Got It

To **"get it"** or to have **"got it"** was the simplest and most direct of the [[tommy|British Tommy]]'s death euphemisms. Unlike "[[click]] it" with its mechanical metaphor or "going west" with its poetic overtones, "got it" just meant what it said: you'd been hit.

## Usage in the Narrative

Holmes describes his own first wound with the term:

> "I got it at the end of ten days, and was…" [001-248]

Troops who needed watching had it coming:

> "…they needed watching, and they got it from…" [011-057]

A soldier's boots being taken from a dead man was matter-of-fact:

> "…bloomin' boots of 'im that's got it." [012-196]

## The Matter-of-Factness of Death

"Got it" encapsulates the fatalism of [[trench|trench warfare]] more than any other slang term. There is no poetry, no metaphor — just a statement of fact. You got it or you didn't, and there was little you could do to control which. The brevity of the phrase matched the speed with which death could come — a [[sniper]]'s bullet, a shell fragment, a burst of [[machine-gun|machine-gun fire]]. One moment you were there, the next you had "got it."
