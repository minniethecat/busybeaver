# British Army

## Overview

The British Army of World War I was a mass citizen army — vastly expanded from its pre-war professional cadre by volunteers and, later, conscripts. By 1918, over five million men had served. [[r-derby-holmes|R. Derby Holmes]], an American, volunteered to join this army in 1916 and served in its ranks through multiple campaigns on the [[france|Western Front]].

## In the Memoir

Holmes's memoir is, among other things, a portrait of the British Army as experienced by a rank-and-file soldier. He records its [[trench-discipline|discipline]], its slang, its [[trench-rations|rations]], its class divisions, and its extraordinary capacity for endurance. The [[tommy|British soldier]] — "[[tommy|Tommy]]" — emerges from these pages as resilient, irreverent, and profoundly human.

Holmes, as an American outsider, was particularly well-positioned to observe the character of the [[tommy|British soldier]]. His account is affectionate but clear-eyed, acknowledging both the army's strengths and its absurdities.
