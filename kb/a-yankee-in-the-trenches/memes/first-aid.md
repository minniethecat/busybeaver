## Overview

First aid on the [[france|Western Front]] was primitive by modern standards but could mean the difference between life and death. Every soldier carried an emergency field dressing — a sterile bandage in a waterproof packet — and was trained in its basic use. More seriously [[casualty|wounded]] men depended on [[stretcher-bearer|stretcher bearers]] to carry them to [[dressing-station|dressing stations]] behind the lines, and from there to casualty clearing stations and [[hospital|base hospital]]s.

## In the Memoir

Holmes witnessed and participated in improvised first aid multiple times. After one comrade was [[casualty|wounded]] in the arm:

> Private X got out his own emergency bandage and fixed the arm.
> (007-279)

The field dressing was a simple but vital piece of [[equipment|kit]] — a pad of gauze and a bandage that could be applied under fire to stop bleeding until the [[casualty|wounded]] man could be evacuated.

## Stretcher Bearers

Stretcher bearers were the unsung heroes of the [[trench|trenches]]. Working under fire, often in [[no-mans-land|No Man's Land]], they retrieved the [[casualty|wounded]] and carried them to safety. Holmes describes one cockney stretcher bearer calling out to him:

> "Ring, a cockney stretcher bearer shot this question at me."
> (001-003)

The job was dangerous and emotionally taxing — bearers witnessed the worst of the war's human cost at close range.

## Dressing Stations

Behind the front line, [[dressing-station|regimental aid post]]s and [[dressing-station|dressing stations]] provided the first organized medical care. Here, wounds could be properly cleaned and dressed, and triage decisions made — who could return to duty and who needed evacuation to a [[hospital|base hospital]].

## A Soldier's Hope

For many soldiers, a wound was a paradoxical hope — a "[[blighty-wound|Blighty wound]]," serious enough to be sent [[blighty|home]] to [[england|England]] ("[[blighty|Blighty]]") but not so serious as to cause permanent disability. Holmes himself was [[casualty|wounded]] and sent to [[blighty|Blighty]]:

> The time to crystallize until I had been wounded and sent to Blighty.
> (001-006)
