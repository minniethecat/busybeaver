# Army Service Corps

## Overview

The Army Service Corps (A.S.C.) was the logistical backbone of the [[british-army|British Army]] during World War I. Responsible for the procurement, storage, and distribution of food, ammunition, equipment, and fuel, the A.S.C. operated the supply chain that kept the front-line infantry fed and [[battle|fight]]ing. By the war's end, the Corps had grown to over 300,000 men and was operating everything from horse-drawn supply wagons to motor lorries, field bakeries, and supply dumps. It was renamed the Royal Army Service Corps in 1918 in recognition of its essential service.

## In the Memoir

The A.S.C. appears in Holmes's memoir only once, and then through the lens of soldiers' humor. During the distribution of [[trench-rations|rations]], a soldier complains about the quality of the bread:

> "Lookee! Do yer call that a loaf o' bread? Looks like the A.S.C. (Army Service Corps) been using it fer a piller."
> — (005-063)

The joke — that the bread was so stale and flat it must have been used as a pillow — is typical of the sardonic relationship between front-line infantry and the support services. The infantryman, living in mud and under fire, took a certain grim pleasure in disparaging the efforts of those who supplied him from the relative safety of the rear. The complaint was unjust — the A.S.C. operated under the same difficult conditions and shellfire — but it was a cherished part of trench culture.

That Holmes felt it necessary to spell out the abbreviation in parentheses suggests that "A.S.C." was common military shorthand his American readers might not know, but the soldiers themselves used it familiarly and often irreverently.

## Role in the War

While Holmes's memoir gives the A.S.C. only a passing mention, the Corps was fundamental to every aspect of [[trench|trench warfare]]. Food had to be transported from Channel ports to railheads, from railheads to divisional dumps, and from dumps forward to the front lines — each leg of the journey more exposed and dangerous than the last. The "ration party" that Holmes himself served on (007-253) was the final link in a supply chain that began with the A.S.C.
