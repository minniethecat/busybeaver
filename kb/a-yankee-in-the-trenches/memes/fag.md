# Fag

## Overview

"Fag" was the [[tommy|British soldier]]'s word for a cigarette — and cigarettes were, by universal agreement, the most reliable comfort in the [[trench|trenches]]. The word derived from British public-school slang (where a "fag" was a junior boy who performed servant-like duties for a senior), but in the army it meant one thing only: a smoke. Government-issued fags and privately-sent cartons from [[blighty|home]] sustained morale through long watches, [[artillery|bombardment]]s, and the grinding monotony of [[daily-life-in-the-trenches|trench routine]].

## In the Memoir

Holmes, himself a heavy smoker, mentions fags repeatedly — often in the context of small comforts that made trench life bearable. After a successful [[trench-raid|raid]] or a hard day's work, the reward was often described in terms of smoking:

> high-grade "fags", a real bath, a good stiff rum ration, and
> — (003-152)

The progression is telling: fags first, then bath, then [[rum-ration|rum]]. The cigarette was the immediate, portable pleasure — no need for hot water or a quartermaster's issue.

## The Government Issue

Smoking was so central to army life that it was officially supplied:

> Tommy is a great smoker. He gets a fag issue from the government
> — (005-141)

The government fag issue was a recognition that morale depended on small comforts. Holmes, who received private supplies from [[blighty|home]], became a one-man tobacconist for his section:

> who sent me about five hundred fags a week, and I was consequently
> — (005-145)

Five [[hun]]dred fags a week made Holmes a popular man. Cigarettes were currency in the [[trench|trenches]] — traded for favors, shared to build camaraderie, given freely to men who had run out.

## The Ritual of Smoking

Holmes captures the compulsive quality of trench smoking with a vivid image:

> positively green. They smoked fag after fag, lighting the new ones
> — (010-053)

Smoking "fag after fag" was the response to fear — chain-smoking as a way to keep the hands busy and the mind occupied while waiting for a [[artillery|bombardment]] or an attack. The phrase "positively green" suggests men sick with anxiety, the fag their only comfort.

> crazy for a fag
> — (011-030)

To be "crazy for a fag" was to be desperate for the small ritual of lighting up, inhaling, and finding, for a few moments, something normal in a world gone mad.

## Fags and the Enemy

Even [[germans|German soldier]]s recognized the value of a good cigarette:

> buy English or American fags. And believe me, they liked them. Any
> — (011-101)

English and American cigarettes were prized by both sides. The fag crossed lines — a small reminder that, beneath the uniforms, the men on both sides shared the same needs and the same fears.

## Tommy's Favorite

> Tommy has his favorite in "fags" like anybody else. He likes above
> — (005-148)

Like any smoker, [[tommy|Tommy]] had preferences — brands, types, countries of origin. The government issue might be whatever was available, but the private parcels from [[blighty|Blighty]] brought the good stuff.
