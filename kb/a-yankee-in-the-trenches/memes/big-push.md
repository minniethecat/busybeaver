## Overview

The "Big Push" was the soldiers' term for the major Allied offensive on [[battle-of-the-somme|the Somme]] in the summer and fall of 1916. Beginning on July 1, 1916, it was one of the largest [[battle|battles]] of WWI and the defining military event of Holmes' experience on the [[france|Western Front]]. The offensive saw the first use of [[tank|tanks]] in warfare and resulted in over a million [[casualty|casualties]] on all sides.

## In the Memoir

Holmes arrived on [[battle-of-the-somme|the Somme]] front while the Big Push was in full swing. The landscape he describes is one of utter devastation — trench systems demolished, communication lines destroyed, and the ground churned into a maze of shell holes:

> The big push had been on all summer, and the whole of the Somme district was battered and smashed. Going up from Mill Street there were no communication trenches.
> (001-008)

The rapid advance during the offensive meant that established trench systems had been obliterated, forcing troops to navigate between shell holes and isolated strongpoints:

> Trench systems had been demolished, and in many places there were only shell holes and isolated trenches.
> (003-018)

## Tank Debut

Holmes witnessed one of the first uses of [[tank|tanks]] in warfare during the Big Push. He describes watching the [[tank|tanks]] advance on Eaucort Abbaye, which "had been razed with the aid of the [[tank|tanks]]" (003-018). The [[tank|tanks]], still a experimental weapon, made a strong impression:

> In front of this trench was Eaucort Abbaye, which had been razed with the aid of the tanks. We had watched this battle from the rear from the slight elevation of Mill Street.
> (003-018)

## Impact on Trench Warfare

The Big Push fundamentally altered the nature of [[trench|trench warfare]] on [[battle-of-the-somme|the Somme]]. With [[communication-trench|communication trenches]] destroyed, troops moved above ground at night. The distances between lines became vast, with forward positions often isolated. The offensive's intensity is reflected in the memoir's glossary, which defines "Big push" simply as "The [[battle|battles]] of [[battle-of-the-somme|the Somme]]" (016-090).
