## Overview

The [[revolver|pistol]] or revolver was the standard sidearm of officers and senior NCOs in the [[british-army|British Army]] during World War I. Unlike the rifle, which was designed for aimed fire at distance, the [[revolver|pistol]] was a close-[[billets|quarters]] weapon — used in trench [[battle|fight]]ing, on [[patrol]]s, and as a last resort when the enemy was at arm's length.

## In the Memoir

Holmes describes the [[revolver|pistol]] in several contexts. During the [[raid|trench raid]] briefing, [[lieutenant-may|Lieutenant May]] specifies who carries what:

> The officers and senior NCOs have revolvers.
> (012-034)

The [[revolver|pistol]] was a weapon of authority and last resort. [[lieutenant-blofeld|Blofeld]] fires the final shot of one [[battle|engagement]]:

> Blofeld fired the last shot from his revolver.
> (004-047)

## Patrol Weapon

For [[patrol|patrol work]], the [[revolver|pistol]] was preferred over the rifle — it was lighter, easier to handle in confined spaces, and could be fired one-handed. Holmes describes the [[patrol]]man's [[equipment|kit]]:

> He carries two or three Mills bombs and a pistol, but not a rifle.
> (007-279)

## Close-Quarter Combat

In the tight confines of a trench or [[mine-explosion|sap]], a rifle was often too long to bring to bear quickly. The [[revolver|pistol]], by contrast, could be drawn and fired in an instant. Holmes witnessed one tense moment when a man snatched a [[revolver|pistol]] from its holster:

> ...leap at Mad Harry and snatch his pistol from the holster.
> (007-279)

He also describes a German officer captured while:

> ...with an automatic pistol balanced on his finger, smiling, pulling the trigger.
> (010-075)

## The Webley Revolver

The standard British sidearm was the Webley Mark VI revolver, a .455 caliber six-shot weapon known for its stopping power and reliability in the mud and filth of the [[trench|trenches]]. Officers purchased their own, and the weapon became a symbol of rank and authority.
