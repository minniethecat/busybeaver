# Hazlehurst Manor

Hazlehurst Manor, also known as Treverton Manor, is the ancestral estate of the Treverton family and the principal domestic setting of *The Cloven Foot*. Located under the shadow of Dartmoor in a quiet Devonshire village, the manor is where the novel opens with the dying Jasper Treverton and closes with the resolution of the inheritance plot and the fate of its characters.

## Location

Hazlehurst Manor is situated near the village of Hazlehurst in Devonshire, "a far away nook under the shadow of Dartmoor" (001-004). The manor stands within a drive of the adjacent market town of Beechampton (002-006, 003-002). The estate is approached through gates leading into a winding carriage-drive, bordered by park-like grounds. The landscape around the manor is described as a mix of lane, glade, and moor, with the "rough sequestered lane" that leads toward Dartmoor and ultimately connects to the remote parish of Camelot (001-007, 020-043). The estate grounds include large, old-fashioned gardens "laid out in prim terraces" with clipped hedges and gravel walks (001-103).

## Description

The manor is a substantial red-brick Queen Anne mansion, described on the novel's first night as:

> "a massive stone mansion with many long narrow windows, and a massive carved stone portico" (001-029)

The interior is characterized by spacious rooms with high ceilings and period features. The entrance hall has a "broad staircase with massive oak bannisters" (001-034). Key rooms include:

- **The dining room**: an apartment "half-panelled with dark and light wood, after the manner of the Queen Anne period," with oak carving and gold panelling, six long windows, and a fireplace at each end (001-064, 002-005). Its walls are hung with Dutch cabinet pictures (001-064).

- **The study**: a small apartment adjoining the invalid's room, used by Laura Malcolm as her sitting room. It contains an old cabinet "so full of secret drawers, and mysterious boxes and recesses" (020-030). Here John Treverton first meets Laura Malcolm (001-065, 001-090).

- **The library**: "much larger than the book-room" at Mr. Clare's house, though rarely used by the new master (022-071, 032-014). It becomes a place for private conferences later in the novel.

- **The book-room**: a small room with a sunny southern aspect, "not large enough for a library," used as an informal sitting room (007-079, 022-071).

- **Laura's dressing room**: containing a dressing case with a secret drawer where she locks away important letters (012-062).

The manor is furnished with curious old cabinets of carved ebony, inlaid with ivory, and the long narrow windows are draped with dark red velvet (001-064, 001-079).

## Significance in the Novel

### Plot Function

Hazlehurst Manor is the novel's narrative fulcrum. The story opens here with the dying Jasper Treverton summoning his heir [[John-Treverton]] to receive a poisoned inheritance — a fortune conditional on marrying Laura Malcolm within one year (001-048, 002-005). The reading of the will takes place in the manor's dining room (002-005). Throughout the novel, the manor is where Laura Malcolm resides as its mistress, where John Treverton courts her and where their halcyon early married life unfolds before scandal descends. The final resolution of the inheritance plot and the restoration of John Treverton's identity are tied to this house.

The manor also serves as a barrier to intimacy: its spacious rooms and separate wings accommodate the formal, guarded relationship between John and Laura. After their marriage, the manor witnesses both their brief happiness and the unravelling of trust when John's past is exposed.

### Thematic Role

Hazlehurst Manor represents the nexus of domestic ideal and hidden secrets — the quintessential sensation-novel country house. Its "closely-shuttered windows" that "showed no ray of light" when viewed from the lane at night (003-042) symbolize the darkness concealed within respectability. The manor's ancestry — generations of Trevertons buried in the nearby churchyard (002-004) — embodies the weight of inheritance and family legacy that drives the plot. The house's old cabinets "full of secret drawers" (020-030) mirror the novel's central theme of concealed truth beneath a polished surface.

### Key Rooms / Areas

- **The oak-panelled study**: Where John Treverton first encounters Laura Malcolm; the room adjoins the invalid's chamber and serves as Laura's private domain during Jasper Treverton's illness.
- **The dining room**: Site of the will reading and the formal dinners that mark the novel's social rituals; its Queen Anne grandeur underscores the weight of inheritance.
- **The library**: Used for private conferences; later in the novel it becomes a room for confrontations and revelations.
- **The gardens**: "Large, and laid out in prim terraces" (001-103); John Treverton walks them on his first morning, and they are the backdrop to his earliest conversations with Laura.
- **Laura's dressing room**: Contains a dressing case with a secret drawer where she locks away letters; emblematic of the secrets the house holds.
