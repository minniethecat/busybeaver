# Foreign Policy & Cold War Index

Goldwater devoted the longest chapter of *The Conscience of a Conservative* — Chapter 10, "The Soviet Menace" — to foreign policy, arguing that the struggle against Communism must be the organizing principle of American engagement with the world. ## Key Themes

The foreign policy pages in this wiki cluster around several recurring Goldwater themes:

- **Anti-Communism as organizing principle**: Goldwater insisted that anti-Communism must guide all foreign policy decisions, not merely serve as rhetorical decoration.
- **Discriminating aid**: Foreign aid should go only to governments demonstrably aligned with American interests and the anti-Communist cause.
- **Military strength**: Goldwater advocated for overwhelming military superiority, including nuclear capability, as the foundation of credible deterrence.
- **Critique of bipartisan consensus**: Goldwater attacked both Democratic and Republican administrations for what he saw as a shared failure to confront the Soviet threat with sufficient resolve.
