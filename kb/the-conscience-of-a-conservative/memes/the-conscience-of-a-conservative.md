## Overview

*The Conscience of a Conservative* is a political manifesto published in 1960 under the name of Arizona Senator Barry Goldwater. Ghostwritten by L. Brent Bozell Jr., the book crystallized a set of conservative principles — limited government, fiscal restraint, states' rights, anti-communism, and individual freedom — that would go on to animate the modern conservative movement in the United States. It sold over 3.5 million copies and became a touchstone for American conservatism.

## Publication and Authorship

The book was published in 1960 by Victor Publishing Company, a small press in Shepherdsville, Kentucky. While credited to Goldwater, the manuscript was primarily drafted by L. Brent Bozell Jr., Goldwater's Senate speechwriter and brother-in-law of William F. Buckley Jr. Goldwater provided the political vision and anecdotes from his Senate experience; Bozell shaped them into a systematic argument (001-001).

## Core Arguments (001-001)

Goldwater opens with the observation that "so many people today with Conservative instincts feel compelled to apologize for them" (001-001). The book sets out to demonstrate the connection "between Conservative principles so widely espoused, and Conservative action, so generally neglected" (001-001).

The Foreword positions the work not as an effort to "improv[e] on the Conservative philosophy" or to "bring it up to date," but as a restatement of "ancient and tested truths that guided our Republic" (000).

## Structure

The book comprises ten chapters covering the full range of Goldwater's political philosophy:

| Chapter | Title | Core Theme |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| I | The Conscience of a Conservative | Conservative identity and apology |
| II | The Perils of Power | Federal overreach |
| III | States' Rights | Federalism and decentralization |
| IV | And Civil Rights | Integration, law, and federal authority |
| V | Freedom for the Farmer | Agricultural subsidies and economic freedom |
| VI | Freedom for Labor | Union power and right-to-work |
| VII | Taxes and Spending | Fiscal conservatism |
| VIII | The Welfare State | Welfare and individual responsibility |
| IX | Some Notes on Education | Federal vs. local control of schools |
| X | The Soviet Menace | Anti-communism and national defense |

## Political Impact

The book became the intellectual platform for Goldwater's 1964 presidential campaign and shaped the ideological direction of the Republican Party for decades. It articulated a fusion of free-market economics, social traditionalism, and militant anti-communism that prefigured the Reagan coalition.

## Legacy

As of 2026, *The Conscience of a Conservative* remains in print and is widely cited as a seminal text in the American conservative tradition. Its core themes — limited government, individual liberty, fiscal responsibility, and a strong national defense — continue to define conservative political discourse.
