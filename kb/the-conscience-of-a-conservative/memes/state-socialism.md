## Definition

State socialism, as Goldwater uses the term, does not refer to the classic Marxist model of government ownership of the means of production. Instead, it describes a more subtle form of collectivization: the expansion of government power and dependency through welfare programs, regulation, and wealth redistribution. "The currently favored instrument of collectivization is the Welfare State. The collectivists have not abandoned their ultimate goal — to subordinate the individual to the State — but their strategy has changed" (008-009).

## In Conservative Thought

Goldwater considers "Socialism-through-Welfarism" a greater danger than traditional socialism precisely because it is harder to recognize and resist. "Socialism-through-Welfarism poses a far greater danger to freedom than Socialism-through-Nationalization precisely because it is more difficult to combat" (008-010). Unlike outright nationalization, which is visible and alarming, welfare programs appeal to humanitarian sentiment and create constituencies of dependency that resist reform.

The strategy is effective because of "Welfarism's strong emotional appeal to many voters, and the consequent temptations it presents the average politician" (008-011). Politicians find it "hard...to call for a reduction in federal welfare funds" when such proposals are portrayed as lacking compassion (008-011).

## Key Arguments

Goldwater's case against state socialism through welfarism centers on its effects on individual character and freedom. "Consider the consequences to the recipient of welfarism. For one thing, he mortgages himself to the federal government" (008-018). The moral harm extends to both recipients and donors: welfare programs "promote the idea that the government owes the benefits it confers on the individual, and that the individual is entitled, by right, to receive them" (008-018).

> Conservatism is not an economic theory, though it has economic implications. The shoe is precisely on the other foot: it is Socialism that subordinates all other considerations to man's material well-being. It is Socialism that values the individual's welfare above his freedom.
