## Document Themes in Goldwater's Argument

Goldwater's use of these documents reveals a consistent interpretive strategy:

- **The Constitution as a limiting document**: Goldwater read the Constitution primarily as a charter of *restraints* on federal power, not as a grant of authority. The Tenth Amendment, the separation of powers, and the enumerated powers doctrine were, in his view, not legal technicalities but the central architecture of American freedom.
- **The Founding vision**: Goldwater invoked the Founders — Madison, Jefferson, Franklin — as authorities whose original understanding of limited government had been abandoned by both modern parties.
- **20th-century departures**: The New Deal legislation (exemplified by the Social Security Act) represented, for Goldwater, the decisive break with the Founders' constitutionalism. These documents were not merely policy choices but constitutional watersheds.
