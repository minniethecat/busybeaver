## Description

Schleissheim is a palace complex with extensive park grounds located north of Munich. In the narrative, it appears primarily at night — a dark, cold, and isolated location. Von Wenk awakens there on a bench, "feeling giddy and at first unable to recall anything," perceiving "the castle buildings gleaming through the darkness" (006-001). The park's remoteness makes it an ideal site for Mabuse's operatives to dispose of an unconscious victim without witnesses.

## Role in the Narrative

Schleissheim is the site of the first direct physical attack on State Attorney von Wenk. After being lured into a car by Mabuse's chauffeur [[spoerri|Spoerri]], Wenk is rendered unconscious by poison gas and driven "throughout the darkness to Schleissheim," where he is propped up on a park bench and abandoned (005-020)(005-021).

When Wenk awakens, he is freezing, disoriented, and discovers that his watch, purse, and — most critically — his official notebook have been stolen (006-002). The notebook contains addresses, reports, and detailed plans, including the full account of the [[edgar-hull|Hull]] affair. This theft gives [[dr-mabuse|Mabuse]] direct access to Wenk's investigative strategy.

> He rose hastily and moved away, but he was still dazed, and had to jump about to get any warmth into his body. He felt for his watch, but it was not there, and then he went hastily through his pockets. His purse was missing; so, too, were his pocketbook and his official notebook. He had fallen into the hands of thieves.
>
> (006-002)

The incident at Schleissheim becomes a recurring reference point throughout the investigation. Wenk later tells [[countess-dusy-told|Countess Told]]: "Last night I was decoyed into a car and left in the freezing cold lying on a bench in the Schleissheim Park, stupefied by gas" (006-057). Mabuse himself, in a moment of private fury, rages that his operative "let him escape us at Schleissheim" (009-108). In the final act, it is revealed that notes were copied from Wenk's notebook "that night in the Schleissheim Park" (020-075), confirming the lasting strategic damage of this episode.

## Real-World Context

Schloss Schleissheim is a real Baroque palace complex located in Oberschleissheim, approximately 15 kilometers north of central Munich. Built in the early 18th century, the complex includes three palaces (Altes Schloss, Neues Schloss, and Schloss Lustheim) set within extensive formal gardens. In the 1920s, when the novel is set, the area was considerably more remote than today, making it a plausible location for the clandestine disposal of an unconscious man. The palace grounds' nocturnal isolation in the narrative reflects the actual geography of the period.

## Significance

Schleissheim represents the moment when Mabuse's threat transitions from psychological manipulation (the gambling tables, the hypnotic card games) to direct physical violence. The location itself — a cold, dark park bench in winter, with the "castellated buildings gleaming through the darkness" (006-001) — embodies the exposure and vulnerability Wenk experiences when stripped of his official identity (his notebook, his credentials). The episode establishes the stakes of the investigation: Mabuse will not merely outplay his opponents but will physically eliminate them if necessary. That Wenk survives at all is attributed by Mabuse to his operative's "softheartedness" (009-108), a mistake Mabuse vows not to repeat.
