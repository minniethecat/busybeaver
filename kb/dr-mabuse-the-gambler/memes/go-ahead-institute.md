# Go-Ahead Institute

The Go-Ahead Institute is [[dr-mabuse|Dr. Mabuse]]'s most elaborate physical establishment — a gambling venue that combines criminal enterprise with avant-garde aesthetics, mechanical ingenuity with theatrical spectacle. Located in Munich and accessible through a barber shop that serves as its legitimate front, the Institute represents the fullest realization of Mabuse's method: the creation of spaces in which the boundaries between entertainment and exploitation, art and crime, performance and predation become permanently blurred.

## Design and Aesthetics

The Institute's physical design is a collaboration between criminal infrastructure and contemporary art. The croupier's introductory speech explicitly frames the establishment in aesthetic terms:

> "One of the first artists of our day, whose work you are constantly encountering in exhibitions and periodicals, has designed them for the 'Go-ahead,' and placed them here, and we have united art with technique, the strongest product of the age." (007-010)

The commissioning of a recognized contemporary artist to design gambling apparatus is an audacious gesture. It blurs the boundary between the gallery and the gambling den, between the cultural institution and the criminal enterprise. The artist, presumably unaware of the establishment's true nature, has lent their prestige to a front for fraud and manipulation. The "union of art with technique" that the croupier celebrates is, in this context, a union of aesthetics with exploitation — the beautiful surface that conceals the predatory mechanism.

## The Barber Shop Front

The Institute is accessed through a barber shop — a front that is simultaneously mundane and ingenious. The barber shop is the perfect cover: a legitimate business whose daily operations provide cover for the traffic of gamblers entering through the back rooms. Von Wenk's informant provides "a list of all the secret gambling-dens" (004-007) that includes the Institute, but the informant's knowledge has been hard-won; the barber shop front has successfully concealed the gambling operation from casual observation.

## The Garde-Police

The Institute's most notorious feature is the garde-police — a mechanical defense system that, at the press of a button, transforms the gambling hall into a theatre. The mechanism is described with pride by the croupier:

> "What would you do, ladies and gentlemen, if the police were suddenly to intrude upon you and rob you of your money and your freedom on account of your forbidden game? You need have no anxiety on that score. We have hit upon an arrangement which might be called a garde-police." (007-013)

When activated, the garde-police causes the gambling table to sink into the floor and a stage to rise in its place. A nude ballet is performed by a company of dancers while actors dressed as policemen conduct a mock search. The spectacle is designed to confuse genuine police raiders and to provide cover for the escape of the establishment's operators.

Von Wenk, during the [[the-raid-on-go-ahead|police raid]], immediately identifies the mechanism's limitations:

> "This is only an ordinary cinema," said Wenk, turning to Karstens. "The most ordinary kind of cinema. If the police were to come, they would discover the whole trick in ten minutes." (007-017)

## Petits Chevaux

The Institute's primary gambling apparatus consists of petits chevaux — mechanical horse-racing games that simulate the experience of betting on live races. The devices are described as works of art in their own right, designed by the commissioned artist and presented as evidence of the Institute's commitment to the union of art and technique. The petits chevaux represent a transitional technology between the traditional card table and the fully mechanized gambling machines that would become ubiquitous in the later twentieth century.

## The Fake Police Guard

Beyond the theatrical garde-police, the Institute maintains a more practical form of protection: a fake policeman stationed outside the establishment. This false officer, wearing what appears to be a genuine police uniform, deters the real police from investigating further. The presence of a uniformed officer suggests that the establishment is already under police supervision, making additional scrutiny unlikely. The fake policeman is, in its way, more effective than the garde-police — not a spectacular defense but a quietly functional one.

## Significance

The Go-Ahead Institute is the novel's emblematic space — a microcosm of the world Mabuse has created, in which every element of the environment serves his criminal purpose. The art on the walls, the machines on the floor, the dancer on the stage, the policeman at the door — all are components of a single, integrated system designed to extract wealth from its patrons while remaining invisible to the institutions that might shut it down. The Institute demonstrates that Mabuse's criminal genius extends beyond psychology to encompass architecture, design, and the manipulation of physical space itself.
