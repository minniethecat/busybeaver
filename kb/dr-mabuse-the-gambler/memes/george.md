# George

**George** (also known as **George Strümpfli**) is [[dr-mabuse|Dr. Mabuse]]'s chauffeur
and trusted accomplice. He drives the gang's vehicles between their various operational
sites — [[munich|Munich]], the [[schachen-villa]], and [[constance|Constance]] (Konstanz) —
and participates in the logistics of Mabuse's criminal enterprise, including the transport
of counterfeit materials and the secret telephone connection to the Villa Elise.

## Role in the Organization

George serves as more than a driver. He is Mabuse's personal attendant, serving meals
at the Schachen villa, maintaining the pneumatic communication systems, and executing
orders that require physical presence and discretion. His presence in eight chapters
makes him one of the most frequently recurring minor characters in the novel.

| Function | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Driver | Transports Mabuse, Spoerri, and the Countess between operational sites |
| Attendant | Serves supper and drinks at Schachen villa strategy sessions |
| Communications | Maintains the concealed wall telephone and pigeon-post system |
| Logistics | Prepares transport of the chest containing counterfeit notes to Villa Elise |

## The Schachen Villa Evening

George features prominently during the critical evening at Schachen (Chapter V), where
Mabuse, Spoerri, and Pesch plan their next moves. George drives the group, serves
refreshments, and witnesses the escalating tension:

> While Mabuse and George were still standing on the doorstep, Mabuse looked fiercely
> at Spoerri. "Go inside," he said. "George, I'll speak with you later."
>
> (005-096) / (005-097)

The scene includes George serving supper and bringing increasingly large glasses of brandy
as Mabuse's mood darkens:

> George served the supper, brought red wine from the cellar which stood in the
> well-furnished dining-room.
>
> (005-100)

> "George, bring drinks!"
>
> George shuddered, for he knew that this tone and this mood meant something
> uncanny was brewing.
>
> (005-177)

## The Encounter with the Steamroller

During the flight toward Lake Constance, Mabuse encounters an unexpected obstacle — a
steamroller blocking the narrow road. His exchange with George reveals the hierarchical
tension in their relationship:

> "Why did you not tell me the steamroller was here?" he asked George imperiously.
>
> (014-013)

## The Final Chase

In Chapter XVIII, George plays a crucial role in the climactic pursuit. Mabuse instructs
him to prepare the chest of counterfeit materials for transport to the Villa Elise:

> Mabuse, with the Countess, would drive to the Villa Elise, where George, who had
> been instructed by pigeon-post, would be waiting with the chest containing the
> counterfeit notes.
>
> (018-007)

During the police chase, George intervenes to save Mabuse:

> Two hands covered his own, snatched the steering-gear from him, and a wild,
> hoarse, impressive voice whispered, "Doctor, I'm here: it's George."
>
> (018-062)

George's sacrifice in the pursuit is acknowledged tersely:

> George had remained as the victim of the pursuer's vengeance, and he was saved.
>
> (019-007)

> George is taken, but we have escaped.
>
> (019-008)

## The False Identity

George's name — or the name "George" — circulates through the investigation as a
mysterious reference. [[cara|Cara Carozza]] is heard calling out "George" when
arrested, leading to confusion about whether she was referring to her deceased
husband George Strümpfli or to Mabuse's chauffeur:

> "The constable said it was George."
>
> (008-025)

> "My husband's name was George. Could I, in my excitement, have called his name?"
>
> (008-029)

## The Strümpfli Alias

Wenk's investigation into the name "George" leads to a rental record in Frankfurt:

> "George Strümpfli, artist, was born in Basle in 1885, and lived at the address
> indicated from January 1st to December 10th last year."
>
> (009-020)

This is likely a cover identity used by George for Mabuse's operations.
