# Characters of Dr. Mabuse

The world of Jacques and Lang's novel is populated by a carefully structured cast whose relationships form the narrative's skeleton. The characters arrange themselves along a spectrum from the hyper-visible ([[dr-mabuse|Mabuse]], who is everywhere felt but rarely seen) to the nearly invisible (the waiters, clerks, and informants who enable the plot without claiming its attention).

## Central Figures

The novel's dramatic core is the duel between two men:

- **[[dr-mabuse|Dr. Mabuse]]** — Psychoanalyst, master criminal, and master of disguise. The novel's absent center — felt in every scene, visible in very few. His hypnotic powers and psychological penetration make him more than a villain: he is a diagnosis of [[modern-city|modernity]] itself.
- **[[von-wenk|State Prosecutor von Wenk]]** — The investigator who must become something like his quarry to catch him. His arc traces the transformation of a rational legal professional into a man who adopts [[disguise-and-secret-identity|disguise]] and deception in service of justice.

## The Criminal Network

Mabuse's organization depends on accomplices who inhabit the margins between legitimate and criminal worlds:

- **[[spoerri|Spoerri]]** — Mabuse's chauffeur and most trusted operative, a tool whose loyalty is purchased rather than inspired.
- **[[cara|Cara]]** — A dancer and performer at the Bonbonnière, entangled in Mabuse's orbit and in von Wenk's increasingly complicated investigation.
- **[[cara-carozza|Cara Carozza]]** — Dancer at the Bonbonnière, accomplice to Mabuse (alternate/related entry to Cara).
- **[[george|George]]** — An associate within Mabuse's criminal infrastructure.
- **[[pesch|Pesch]]** — A member of Mabuse's network.
- **[[wellmann|Wellmann]]** — An operative in the criminal organization.

## Aristocracy and Society

The novel's social world centers on the Told family and their circle:

- **[[count-told|Count Told]]** — A wealthy aristocrat whose household becomes entangled in Mabuse's schemes.
- **[[countess-dusy-told|Countess Dusy Told]]** — The Count's wife, simultaneously Mabuse's patient and his hostage — the therapeutic relationship perverted into a mechanism of control.

## The Law

Von Wenk's investigation depends on institutional support:

- **[[inspector-schramm|Inspector Schramm]]** — Von Wenk's subordinate, representing the institutional police methods that the prosecutor increasingly transcends.
- **[[karstens|Karstens]]** — A police operative involved in the investigation.

## Victims and Witnesses

- **[[edgar-hull|Edgar Hull]]** — A gambler destroyed by Mabuse at the baccarat table; his ruin is the novel's inciting event.
- **[[balling|Balling]]** — A witness to or participant in the gambling underworld.
- **[[basch|Basch]]** — A character connected to the criminal or investigative threads.
