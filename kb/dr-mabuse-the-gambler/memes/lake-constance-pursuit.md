# The Lake Constance Pursuit

## Summary

The novel's climactic action sequence takes place on Lake Constance, where Mabuse attempts his final escape to Switzerland (014-037). Piloted by his chauffeur Spoerri, the amphibious car carries him across the lake toward the Swiss border — a technological marvel that symbolizes Mabuse's ability to transcend conventional boundaries but also his ultimate vulnerability.

## Context

The pursuit represents the novel's shift from psychological to physical conflict. Throughout the narrative, Mabuse's power has been exercised through hypnosis, disguise, and manipulation — invisible weapons that leave no trace. On the lake, there is nothing to hide behind. The confrontation becomes a test of will and nerve in a space stripped of urban complexity.

## Key Participants

- **Dr. Mabuse**: The fugitive, his disguises now useless against the stark clarity of open water
- **State Prosecutor von Wenk**: The pursuer, who has finally shed his own disguises to confront Mabuse directly
- **Spoerri**: The chauffeur-pilot, whose technical skill is the last instrument of Mabuse's will

## Significance

The lake pursuit is the novel's symbolic turning point — the moment when concealment gives way to exposure, when the invisible becomes visible. Mabuse's flight is also his unmasking. (014-037) The open water, which should offer escape, instead offers clarity.

> During the night Mabuse started for Lake Constance. Just as he was approaching the villa at Schachen, having extinguished his lights, he narrowly missed a collision with the engine of the steamroller which was standing in the road a few yards from the garden entrance.
>
> (014-037)
