# Wangen

**Wangen** (Wangen im Allgäu) is a small town in southern Germany that enters the
narrative as the native place and final location of [[pesch|Pesch]], a minor
accomplice in [[dr-mabuse|Dr. Mabuse]]'s criminal network.

## In the Telephone Network

During the coordinated manhunt for Mabuse's organization, State Prosecutor
[[von-wenk|von Wenk]] included Wangen in the region-wide telephone alert:

> I telephoned the number to Friedrichshafen, Ravensburg, Lindau, Wangen and
> Constance.
>
> (014-064)

This places Wangen within the same Lake Constance regional network as
[[lindau|Lindau]] and [[constance|Constance]], forming an arc of surveillance
around Mabuse's known operational territory.

## Pesch's Arrest and Imprisonment

Wangen becomes significant when [[pesch|Pesch]] — dismissed by Mabuse on
the [[lindau|Lindau]] road — is tracked down and arrested:

> Pesch was arrested, and his first night in prison was spent at Wangen,
> which was his native place.
>
> (015-053)

| Detail | Information |
|--------|------------|
| Prisoner | Pesch |
| Charge | Criminal association with Mabuse's gang |

## The Prison Murder

Pesch never left Wangen alive. He was found dead in his cell under circumstances
that strongly suggested Mabuse's organization had eliminated a potential witness:

> On the spot. The dead man was Pesch. He had been stabbed, and as the wound
> was in his heart, death must have followed instantaneously.
>
> (015-054)

The murder demonstrated Mabuse's reach — even a small-town prison in Wangen
was not beyond the organization's capacity to penetrate. The incident
underscored the danger faced by anyone who might testify against the network:

> A few hours later a telephone message came to the Wangen police.
>
> (015-055)

## Significance

Wangen represents the dark terminus of Mabuse's human supply chain. Minor
accomplices like Pesch, recruited from small towns, were expendable — and when
they became liabilities, the organization reached into their places of origin
to silence them. The prison death at Wangen is the novel's clearest
demonstration that Mabuse's power extended beyond [[munich|Munich]] and
[[constance|Constance]] into the smallest jurisdictions of southern Germany.
