# Spoerri (Georg)

## Role in the Narrative

Spoerri is Mabuse's chauffeur and primary operational subordinate — the man who drives the vehicles, pilots the amphibious car across Lake Constance, and executes the practical logistics of Mabuse's schemes. He is not a partner but a tool: skilled, loyal, and ultimately disposable.

## Biography

Spoerri's background is working-class; his skills are technical rather than intellectual. He drives, he pilots, he maintains the vehicles that enable Mabuse's mobility and escape. His relationship with Mabuse is purely hierarchical — he follows orders without question, and his loyalty is purchased rather than inspired.

## Key Appearances

- **The chauffeur disguise (Ch. IX)**: Mabuse "disguised himself as a chauffeur," blurring his identity with Spoerri's role (009-046)
- **The amphibious car pursuit (Ch. XV–XVI)**: Spoerri pilots the vehicle across Lake Constance in the novel's climactic chase sequence

## Analysis

Spoerri represents the banality of criminal enterprise — the ordinary men who perform the ordinary tasks that make extraordinary crime possible. Without him, Mabuse cannot move, cannot escape, cannot operate. He is the human infrastructure of evil, neither villainous nor innocent, simply useful.

> "It is a mere accident," said Spoerri. "I had to go to Switzerland in a hurry, and down there in the Rhine valley a cowl is more useful than even a genuine frontier pass. The last pass I had is in St. Gallen, and you know that I had to leave there hastily. But I had left the list of securities with Schaffer, and he brought them to me at Altstetten today. It is not safe to send such things by post nowadays."
>
> (005-098)
