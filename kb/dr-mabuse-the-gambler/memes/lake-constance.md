# Lake Constance (Bodensee)

## Description

Lake Constance (Bodensee) is a vast lake on the border between Germany, Switzerland, and Austria. The novel references it as a strategic waypoint in Mabuse's smuggling network, noting operations "as against ten percent by Lake Constance or the Ticino frontier" (005-042). In the narrative, it serves as the setting for the climactic pursuit sequence — a space of open water and natural grandeur that contrasts sharply with the claustrophobic interiors of Munich's gambling dens.

## Role in the Narrative

The lake represents the novel's shift from urban to natural space, from concealment to exposure. Mabuse, fleeing Munich, heads for the Swiss border across the lake — the narrative records that "Mabuse started for Lake Constance" (014-037) as the pursuit entered its decisive phase. The amphibious car, piloted by Spoerri, is the technological marvel that enables the escape attempt, but the lake itself resists human control. The climactic confrontation occurs on the shore, where von Wenk finally penetrates Mabuse's disguise.

> During the night Mabuse started for Lake Constance. Just as he was approaching the villa at Schachen, having extinguished his lights, he narrowly missed a collision with the engine of the steamroller which was standing in the road a few yards from the garden entrance. It was directly in front of him when he applied his brakes, and he therefore did not drive up to the house, but continued along the road for another kilometre, then left the car standing and went back to the house by the shore-path.
>
> (014-037)

## Real-World Context

Lake Constance has been a site of border-crossing and cultural exchange since Roman times. In the 1920s, it marked the boundary between Weimar Germany and neutral Switzerland — a border that was simultaneously a political reality and, for smugglers and fugitives, a permeable membrane.

## Significance

The lake represents nature as the antithesis of Mabuse's urban labyrinth. In the city, he is invisible; on the open water, he is exposed. The shift in setting marks the shift in power — from Mabuse's world of hidden spaces to a world where nothing can be hidden.
