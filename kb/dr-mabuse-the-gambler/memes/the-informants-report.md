# The Informant's Report

The informant's report is the investigation's first major breakthrough — the moment when [[von-wenk|von Wenk]]'s general concern about the gambling epidemic crystallizes into specific intelligence about specific locations. The informant provides "a list of all the secret gambling-dens" (004-007), transforming the investigation from a diffuse anxiety about social conditions into a targeted inquiry into identifiable establishments. The report marks the transition from the investigation's preparatory phase to its operational phase.

> The next day Wenk asked Hull to procure for him a list of all the secret gambling-dens, the addresses of which might be obtained with the help of Cara, who was au courant of such matters. He made Hull promise, however, that he would not speak to the girl of himself in this connection.
>
> (004-007)

## The Informant

The informant is never named or fully characterized — an absence that is itself significant. He exists in the gray zone between the legitimate world and the criminal underworld, motivated by factors — money, fear, personal grievance — that the novel acknowledges without celebrating. His information is valuable but his reliability is uncertain, and von Wenk's dependence on him is both a practical necessity and an ethical compromise. The informant represents the investigation's uncomfortable relationship with the methods it must employ: to catch criminals, the law must deal with those who operate at the margins of criminality.

## The List

The list of gambling dens is the concrete product of the informant's knowledge — a document that maps the geography of Mabuse's network across Munich. Each entry on the list is a potential target for police action; collectively, they reveal the scale and organization of the gambling epidemic. The list transforms Mabuse's network from a rumor into a fact, giving von Wenk something he can act on rather than merely worry about.

## The Transition to Action

The informant's report enables von Wenk to transition from preparation to action. The [[the-raid-on-go-ahead|raid on the Go-Ahead Institute]], the surveillance of other establishments, the arrests that follow — all are made possible by the intelligence the informant provides. The report is the hinge between the novel's exposition and its action, the moment when the investigation begins to move.
