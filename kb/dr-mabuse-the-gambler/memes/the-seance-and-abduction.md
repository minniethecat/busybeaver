# The Séance and Abduction of Countess Told

## Summary

The séance scene dramatizes the perversion of therapeutic practice into criminal predation. Under the guise of scientific inquiry — "studying the effect of hypnosis on a medium" (011-058) — Mabuse conducts a hypnotic experiment on the Countess Told that ends with her disappearance and abduction.

> "I do not believe there could have been any. All my guests have been personally known to me for some time. You know what the occasion of our meeting was, don't you? We were studying the effect of hypnosis on a medium. There were experts, professors, artists of repute, and some personal friends in the company."
>
> (012-052)

## Context

The séance is set within Mabuse's consulting room, a space that should signify healing but has been transformed into a theater of power. The Countess, already drawn to the gambling dens by a compulsion she cannot explain, is now placed in a position of absolute vulnerability — the hypnotic subject, unable to resist.

## Key Participants

- **Dr. Mabuse**: The hypnotist, using his medical authority as cover for criminal operation
- **Countess Dusy Told**: The subject, whose psychological vulnerability makes her a perfect victim
- **Count Told**: The absent presence — the husband whose failure to protect his wife is the scene's unspoken indictment

## Significance

The séance is the novel's darkest depiction of institutional corruption. Medicine, science, and the law (von Wenk investigates but cannot prevent) all fail the Countess. Her abduction is not a dramatic rupture but the logical culmination of a system that treats women as objects to be managed.
