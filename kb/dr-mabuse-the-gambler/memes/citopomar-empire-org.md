# Empire of Citopomar (Organization)

The Empire of [[citopomar|Citopomar]] exists primarily as an organizational fantasy — the planned framework for the absolute sovereignty that [[dr-mabuse|Dr. Mabuse]] intends to establish in the Brazilian forests. It is an organization without territory, a government without subjects, an empire whose only reality is the name Mabuse has given it and the wealth he is accumulating to fund it. But as an organizational concept, Citopomar deserves attention: it represents the ultimate form of the criminal enterprise that Mabuse has been building in Munich.

## The Organizational Vision

Citopomar is envisioned not as a criminal network but as a state — a sovereign entity with territory, subjects, and the monopoly on legitimate violence that defines statehood in the Weberian tradition. The transition from criminal network to state is the ultimate ambition of Mabuse's enterprise: the transformation of illicit power into legitimate authority, of the mastermind into the sovereign, of the gang into the government.

> Four thousand people; it was a whole district. But in Citopomar it would be something very different when he traversed the virgin forests and had the Botocudos and all the other tribes directly under his thumb, and had left this insignificant beggarly little continent behind him! There his word alone would be law. There, in Citopomar, the dream of his boyhood would be fulfilled — a dream which had already begun to be realized on that large and desolate island which lay cradled in the ocean yonder.
>
> (005-050)

The organizational structures that would govern Citopomar are not specified — the empire remains a fantasy, its institutions unbuilt — but the ambition that drives it is clear. Mabuse does not merely want to be rich; he wants to be sovereign. The criminal network is a means to an end, and the end is the absolute authority that only statehood can provide.

## The Colonial Dimension

Citopomar is a colonial fantasy — the imagination of a European establishing dominion over a non-European territory. The choice of Brazil is not accidental: the Brazilian interior, in the European imagination of the early twentieth century, represented a space of radical possibility, a territory where the constraints of European society did not apply and where a man with sufficient resources could create a world of his own design. Citopomar is the colonial project stripped of its civilizing pretext — not a mission to bring light to the darkness but a project of pure dominion.

## The Organizational Irony

The irony of Citopomar as an organization is that it requires the very qualities — stability, legitimacy, institutional continuity — that Mabuse's criminal methods systematically undermine. The empire funded by fraud and manipulation would be an empire built on the same foundations that enabled its funding, and those foundations are inherently unstable. Citopomar is an organizational contradiction: a state that can only be created by methods that make statehood impossible.
