# Karstens

## Role in the Narrative

Karstens is a witness — the man who sees Hull's destruction and tries, unsuccessfully, to intervene. His presence at the gambling scene provides the novel with a moral observer: someone who recognizes what is happening but lacks the power to stop it. He is the reader's surrogate, watching horror unfold from a position of helpless proximity.

## Key Appearances

- **The gambling scene (Ch. I–II)**: Karstens watches Hull's ruin, his concern growing as the losses mount; he warns Hull, "you are off your head" (001-031), and points out the stranger to his friend (001-035)
- **The aftermath (Ch. III)**: He provides von Wenk with a witness account of the evening's events, helping to consult the telephone directory to identify the mysterious Balling (001-037)

## Analysis

Karstens embodies the moral limits of friendship in a world of predatory power. He cares about Hull, genuinely — but caring is not enough. The institutions that might have protected Hull (friendship, club membership, social standing) prove hollow against Mabuse's weaponized psychology.

> "Hull, you are off your head," said Karstens, when the stranger had left the room.
>
> (001-031)
