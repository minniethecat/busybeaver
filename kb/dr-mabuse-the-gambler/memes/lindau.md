# Lindau

**Lindau** is a town situated on the eastern shore of [[lake-constance|Lake Constance]]
(Bodensee), near the borders of Germany, Austria, and Switzerland. In the narrative,
it serves as a transit and rendezvous point for [[dr-mabuse|Dr. Mabuse]]'s gang
operations between [[munich|Munich]] and the [[schachen-villa|Schachen]] area.

## The Lindau Hill Rendezvous

During the nocturnal journey of Chapter V, Mabuse plans a sequence of pickups along
the route to Schachen. Lindau Hill is designated as the meeting point for
[[pesch|Pesch]], one of the accomplices:

> We must be at Schachen, and before that we meet Spoerri at Opfenbach and Pesch
> on the Lindau Hill.
>
> (005-047)

The Lindau Hill meeting is notable for its tension — George, the chauffeur, initially
fails to spot Pesch at the rendezvous, prompting Mabuse's sharp reaction:

> "Not Pesch?"
>
> "I don't see anybody."
>
> "Pesch!" exclaimed Mabuse.
>
> (005-078) / (005-084)

| Opfenbach | Pickup point | Spoerri |
| Lindau Hill | Rendezvous | Pesch |

## The Steamer Connection

Lindau's position on Lake Constance made it accessible by water. Mabuse himself
used the steamer service:

> He often travelled by the steamer to Lindau.
>
> (005-139)

This multi-modal access (road via Lindau Hill, water via steamer) made Lindau a
strategically flexible point in the gang's logistics network.

## In the Investigation

When State Prosecutor [[von-wenk|von Wenk]] coordinated the search for Mabuse's
network, Lindau was included in the regional telephone alert:

> I telephoned the number to Friedrichshafen, Ravensburg, Lindau, Wangen and
> Constance.
>
> (016-024)

This places Lindau within the constellation of Lake Constance towns that formed
the geographical theatre of Mabuse's operations — a ring of locations around
the lake connected by road, water, and telephone.

## Strategic Significance

Lindau's role in the novel reflects the real geography of the Bodensee region.
As a border town with rail and steamer connections, it offered Mabuse's network:

- **Accessibility**: Road and water routes from Munich and Schachen
- **Discretion**: Small-town anonymity away from major city police presence
- **Proximity**: Close to the Swiss and Austrian borders for potential escape
- **Integration**: Part of a coordinated network of Lake Constance towns

The journey pattern — Munich → Opfenbach → Lindau → Schachen — traces a route
that circles the lake, using each stop as a layer of operational security.
