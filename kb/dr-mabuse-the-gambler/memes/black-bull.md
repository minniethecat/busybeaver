## Description

The Black Bull is a tavern in the Lake Constance (Bodensee) border town of Constance. It functions as an informal headquarters for the lower tier of Mabuse's cross-border smuggling operation — the car dealers, boat handlers, and discharged workers who move contraband between Germany and Switzerland. Its clientele includes men looking for quick money, forged travel documents, or a way to vanish when the authorities close in. (009-068)(009-078)

The tavern is situated opposite an iron-foundry, whose grimy windows provide State Prosecutor [[von-wenk|von Wenk]] with a discreet surveillance post during his investigation of the Constance smuggling ring. (009-080)

## Role in the Narrative

The Black Bull first appears when a young car-dealer, one of the Constance smugglers Wenk has cultivated as an informant, directs Wenk to the tavern as the place to find Poldringer — a commercial traveller who is actually Mabuse's local agent Hinrichsen in disguise. "You only need go to the Black Bull and ask for Poldringer," the man says, describing it as the hub of the Constance operation. (009-068)

Later, the same informant elaborates on the Black Bull's function: "They'll look after you properly there, and everything is arranged so that you can fly off as easily as these greenbacks will. Not a trace left behind!" The tavern is not merely a drinking establishment but a node in Mabuse's infrastructure of disappearance — a portal through which men facing arrest can be funnelled into the Foreign Legion recruitment pipeline and erased from German jurisdiction. (009-078)

Wenk positions himself at the iron-foundry opposite the Black Bull to observe the comings and goings of Poldringer and his associates. From this vantage point he witnesses the telephone conversation between Mabuse (calling as "Dr. Dringer") and the Constance operation, gathering intelligence that will eventually lead to the arrest of seven men at Offenburg station. (009-080)(011-011)

When the arrests are made, Poldringer stays behind and is last reported heading to the Black Bull, where he remains under police observation — the tavern having become, by this point in the investigation, a known and watched location. (011-012)

## Real-World Context

The Black Bull is likely a fictional establishment, though it reflects the real ecosystem of smugglers' taverns that flourished in German-Swiss border towns like Konstanz during the hyperinflation and economic chaos of the early Weimar Republic. Border regions became hotbeds of illicit trade — currency speculation, untaxed goods, forged documents — and waterfront taverns provided neutral ground where criminals, informants, and police agents could intersect.

## Significance

The Black Bull represents the mundane, almost bureaucratic infrastructure that underlies Mabuse's criminal empire. While the Doctor himself inhabits the rarefied world of the Excelsior Hotel, the stock exchange, and aristocratic villas, the Black Bull anchors the operational ground floor — the point where high-level schemes (counterfeiting, stock manipulation, hypnotic control) connect to the rough physical work of moving goods, men, and money across borders. Wenk's surveillance of its doorway from a grimy iron-foundry window emblematizes the investigator's method: patient observation of the unglamorous margins in order to trace the threads back to the centre.
