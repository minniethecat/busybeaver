# The Munich Municipal Police

The Munich Municipal Police is the uniformed law enforcement body of the city — distinct from the criminal investigation department led by [[von-wenk|von Wenk]], but essential to the enforcement operations that support his investigative work. The uniformed officers who patrol the streets, who provide the manpower for raids on gambling establishments, and who maintain the public order that crime disrupts are the visible face of the state's commitment to law enforcement. The novel depicts them sparingly but respectfully, acknowledging their contribution to the investigation without making them central to its narrative.

## The Uniformed Officer

The uniformed officer — figures like [[schmied|Sergeant Schmied]], who pursues suspects on a bicycle through dark Munich streets — represents the physical, street-level dimension of police work. Where von Wenk analyzes and strategizes, the uniformed officer patrols and pursues; where the criminal investigation department builds cases, the uniformed police make arrests. The division of labor between investigative and uniformed functions is characteristic of modern policing, and the novel's registration of it reflects the professionalization of police work in the early twentieth century.

## The Raid

The uniformed police are essential to the raid — the tactical operation in which a criminal establishment is entered, secured, and searched. The [[the-raid-on-go-ahead|raid on the Go-Ahead Institute]] requires uniformed officers to secure the premises, to detain the occupants, and to provide the physical presence that makes the state's authority tangible. The raid is the operation in which the investigative work of the criminal department and the enforcement capacity of the uniformed police converge.

## The Limits

The uniformed police have limits that the novel registers. They can secure a building but cannot build a case; they can make an arrest but cannot conduct an investigation; they can enforce the law but cannot adapt it to novel circumstances. The division of labor that makes modern policing efficient also makes it rigid, and the rigidity is a vulnerability that a criminal like Mabuse can exploit. The uniformed officer who follows procedure is an officer who cannot follow Mabuse into the psychological territory where his crimes are actually committed.
