# The Dance Floor

The dance floor of the [[the-bonbonniere|Bonbonnière]] is a space of concentrated attention — the stage on which [[cara-carozza|Cara Carozza]] performs and the focal point toward which the gazes of the establishment's patrons converge. It is a space of visibility and vulnerability, of power and exposure, of art and commerce. The dancer who commands the floor commands the room, but the command is temporary and conditional — sustained only as long as the performance lasts, and dependent on the continued attention of an audience that can withdraw it at any moment.

## The Space of Performance

The dance floor is designed for performance. It is elevated, illuminated, separated from the audience by a boundary that is simultaneously physical (the edge of the floor) and symbolic (the convention that separates performer from spectator). This separation is essential to the performance's function: it marks the dance as something to be watched rather than participated in, as an object of the [[gaze-and-observation|gaze]] rather than an occasion for interaction.

But the separation is also permeable. The dancer on the floor makes eye contact with patrons at the tables; the patrons respond to the performance with attention, applause, and desire that does not remain confined to the aesthetic register. The dance floor is a space of transaction between performer and audience, and the transaction exceeds the terms of artistic appreciation.

## Cara's Floor

For Cara, the dance floor is simultaneously the space of her greatest power and her greatest vulnerability. On the floor, she is an artist in control of her performance — the author of the movement that commands the room's attention. But the floor is also the space where she is most visible to those who would exploit her — the police who track her movements, the master who controls her, the patrons whose desire can become a threat. The floor is the stage of her freedom and the site of her captivity, and the tension between these two conditions is the tension of her existence in the novel.

## The Floor as Arena

The dance floor is also an arena in the novel's larger contest between visibility and invisibility, power and vulnerability, performance and reality. The dancer who commands the floor is visible, powerful, real — but the visibility, power, and reality are bounded by the floor's edges and the performance's duration. When the dance ends, when the dancer leaves the floor, the visibility becomes exposure, the power becomes vulnerability, and the reality becomes a memory of something that no longer exists.
