# Dr. Ebenhügel

Dr. Ebenhügel is a minor but significant figure in Mabuse's financial network, operating from Zürich as a conduit for the movement of securities and currency across the Swiss border.

## Role in the Criminal Network

Ebenhügel functions as Mabuse's Zürich-based financial agent, handling the practical mechanics of the counterfeiting and currency exchange operation. Mabuse instructs his operatives to inform Ebenhügel when fresh securities are deposited, directing him to convert the holdings into Brazilian milreis — a currency that offered a more favorable exchange rate than dollars or Swiss francs. (005-114)

## The Night Rendezvous on Lake Constance

Ebenhügel appears in person during a nocturnal meeting on Lake Constance (Chapter 5), arriving from Zürich by car. He identifies himself to Mabuse at the shore rendezvous point — "It is I, Ebenhügel, Doctor. I have just come from Zürich." (005-160) During this encounter, Ebenhügel reports that a clerk in the Consulate headquarters in Zürich has sent a warning about an investigation originating from the Munich Criminal Investigation Department — confirming that State Attorney von Wenk is on Mabuse's trail. (005-160)(005-161)

Mabuse, unfazed by the threat, declares: "I am constantly in danger, but I've never come to grief yet." (005-161)

During this meeting, Mabuse reassigns Spoerri's duties to Ebenhügel: "Spoerri, you need not go to Zürich. Ebenhügel will take the portfolio with him." (005-174) This marks Ebenhügel as a trusted courier for Mabuse's most sensitive financial instruments.

## Later References

Ebenhügel is mentioned again in later chapters as a contact point in Zürich. When Mabuse's operatives need to exchange securities, they are directed to an address in Zürich with Dr. Ebenhügel, who handles the currency conversion. (018-002) His name alone — "Dr. Ebenhügel, Zürich" (019-006) — functions as a coded reference to Mabuse's Swiss financial infrastructure.

## Significance

Though a background figure, Ebenhügel represents the international dimension of Mabuse's criminal enterprise. His presence in Zürich — a traditional center of discreet banking — underscores the cross-border nature of Mabuse's financial operations, which exploit the jurisdictional gaps between German and Swiss law enforcement.
