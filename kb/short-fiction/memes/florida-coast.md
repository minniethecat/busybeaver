# Florida Coast

## The Opening Scene

"Way of a Rebel" opens with [[laskell|Lieutenant Laskell]] surfacing his one-man submarine "fifty miles off the Florida coast where he had been hiding" (005). The Florida coast is the geographic threshold between Laskell's isolation and his decision to engage with the moral crisis posed by the [[manlin-bill|Manlin Bill]].

## Geographic Specificity

Miller's use of real geographic locations — Florida, [[earth|Earth]], the [[federation|Federation]] — grounds his bureaucratic dystopia in recognizable space. The Federation is not an alien empire but a future version of our own political geography, and the Florida coast is the same Florida coast. The horror is not in the unfamiliar but in the transformation of the familiar.
