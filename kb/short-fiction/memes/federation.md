# Federation (Western Federation of Autonomous States)

## Definition

The Western Federation of Autonomous States is the future North American polity that serves as the setting for "[[conditionally-human|Conditionally Human]]" (001) and "Check and Checkmate" (003). It is an authoritarian bureaucratic state whose name — with its echo of democratic "autonomy" — belies a system of rigid social control exercised through genetic classification, employment assignment, population management, and manufactured political consent.

## Governance

The Federation is nominally democratic: the President is "elected by a popular vote of 603,217,954 to 130, the dissenters casting their negative by announcing that, for the sake of national unity, they refused to participate in any civilized activities during the President's term" (003-036). The lopsided margin — and the dissenters' immediate admission to a camp for conscientious objectors — reveals the election as ceremony rather than contest. Presidents, who have "no real name of their own," are "trained for the executive post since birth" (003-036).

The President governs behind a golden tragedy mask and is "publicly nameless, but also publicly faceless and publicly unknown as an individual" (003-039). This anonymity is ostensibly protective — preventing assassination by making the man indistinguishable from his [[stand-in|Stand-in]] [[stand-in|body-double]]s — but it also dissolves the distinction between ruler and office, between person and institution.

## Social Engineering

The Federation's control extends deep into private life through two mechanisms: employment assignment and reproduction control.

Employment is determined by aptitude testing: "They looked over my aptitude tests and sent me to Bio-Administration. If I don't want to follow my aptitudes, the only choice is common labor. That's the *law*" (001-005). The system treats individual preference as irrelevant; the state's efficient allocation of human resources overrides personal autonomy.

The Federation's population policy is its most invasive feature. Through the class system (see [[class-c-citizen]]), citizens are genetically classified, and reproduction is permitted or forbidden accordingly: "By keeping the population at five billions, the Federation could insure a decent living standard for everybody" (001-052). This Malthusian calculus is presented as benevolent — the Federation provides for all — but its cost is the denial of fundamental human freedoms to those classified as genetically unfit.

## Ideological Decay

The Federation's founding ideals have been hollowed out into ritual. [[stand-in|Tertiary Stand-in]]s quote the Declaration of Independence "in the name of the Federation's rule" (003-076) — the language of revolution now serving an absolutist state. The Federation presents the form of democracy (elections, congress, speeches to "the rabble") while emptying it of substance. It is, in Miller's rendering, what becomes of liberal democracy when procedure outlasts principle.
