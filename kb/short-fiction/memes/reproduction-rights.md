# Reproduction Rights

## The Biological Becomes Political

In the [[federation|Federation]] of Miller's fiction, reproduction is not a right but a privilege granted by the state based on [[genetic-classification|genetic fitness]]. Class-A citizens may reproduce freely. Class-C citizens — those with "defective heredity" (001-007) — are legally barred from having children. The state does not merely regulate reproduction; it owns the decision of who may create the next generation.

## The Substitute Economy

The denial of reproduction rights creates a captive market for substitute children — [[neutroid|neutroids]] and [[mutant-animals|genetically engineered pets]]. [[class-c-citizen|Class-C]] couples "were allowed two lesser units or one neutroid" (001-007), channeling parental instinct toward manufactured substitutes. The word "allowed" is crucial: even the substitute is a permission, not a right.

The system is circular. Denied children, Class-C couples seek substitutes. The substitute industry ([[organization-anthropos|Anthropos, Inc.]]) creates demand for continued classification. The classification system ensures continued demand for substitutes. Each element reinforces the others.

## The Emotional Cost

The emotional cost of controlled reproduction is documented through [[sarah-glubbes|Sarah Glubbes]]. She has bonded with her neutroid as a mother bonds with a child — the system designed the neutroid to elicit exactly this response. But when the neutroid falls ill, the system that created the bond refuses to honor it. The hospital will not treat a neutroid; the doctor "forgot she was class-C" (001-093); the substitute child dies, and the mother who was not allowed to be a mother kills the doctor who would not save her not-child.
