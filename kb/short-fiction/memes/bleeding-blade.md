# Bleeding-Blade

## The Executioner's Tool

The bleeding-blade is the instrument of justice in the Mars colony of "It Takes a Thief." Wielded by the executioner [[slubil|Slubil]], the blade is central to a justice system built on ritual uncertainty: the condemned does not know his sentence until the blade falls.

## The Ritual of Punishment

The execution ritual is designed to maximize psychological torment. [[asir|Asir]] hangs from a post by his wrists, spikes driven through his forearms, while Slubil sits nearby "playing mumblety-peg with the bleeding-blade" (002-011). The executioner is bound by law to wait until the victim requests his fate — the phrase "Ready for me yet, Asir?" becomes a refrain. But the victim is not told what the sentence will be.

The uncertainty is the punishment's core. The blade may deliver death, amputation of a limb or sensory organ, or merely a scar of banishment. "He might lose only an eye or an ear or a finger. But on the other hand, he might lose his life, both arms, or his masculinity" (002-041). The condemned must choose to face an unknown fate or hang for four days — which is itself a death sentence.

## The Scar of Banishment

When the blade finally falls on Asir, it "chopped into the post above his head" (002-093). Slubil's sentence — delivered on behalf of the Council of Senior Kinsmen — is perpetual banishment, marked by a wound on the forehead "raw and rubbed with rust to make a scar" (002-173). The bleeding-blade has marked him as an outcast without killing him.

## Symbolic Function

The bleeding-blade is more than a weapon; it is a symbol of how the Mars colony administers justice through ritual rather than reason. The executioner does not judge; the Council judges in secret; the condemned cannot know the judgment until the blade acts. Every element of the process is opaque — power without accountability, punishment without explanation.

This opacity mirrors the larger theme of Miller's work: systems that control human life without being transparent to the humans they control. The bleeding-blade is the [[class-c-citizen|genetic classification system]] in miniature — a mechanism whose outcomes are final but whose logic is hidden from those it affects.
