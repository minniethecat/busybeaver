# Council of Senior Kinsmen

## The Governing Body

The Council of Senior Kinsmen is the governing authority in the Mars colony village of "It Takes a Thief." Composed of elders like [[welkir|Welkir]], the Council administers justice, manages the [[ritual-economy|ritual economy]], and maintains the social order.

## Secret Justice

The Council's most distinctive feature is its practice of secret judgment. When [[asir|Asir]] is tried for ritual-theft, the Council "judged him in secret, and passed sentence as to what the executioner would do with the knife. But Asir was not informed of their judgment" (002-041). The condemned faces the [[bleeding-blade|bleeding-blade]] without knowing whether the stroke will kill, maim, or merely mark him.

This system of secret justice embodies the opacity that characterizes all of Miller's institutional systems. The Council, like the [[federation|Federation]]'s classification bureaucracy, exercises power without accountability. Its judgments are final but its reasoning is invisible.

## Crisis of Authority

The Council's authority rests on its control of the [[ritual-economy|ritual formulas]] that constitute wealth and knowledge. But Asir's theft — and his synthesis of stolen fragments into actionable knowledge — exposes the Council's fundamental weakness: it possesses the rituals but cannot use them. The Council owns the keys to Mars's survival but has forgotten what the keys unlock.
