# Vaults

The **Vaults** are a sacred underground complex on [[mars|Mars]] in Walter M. Miller Jr.'s "It Takes a Thief," believed to contain the technological relics and knowledge of the ancient Martian civilization. Guarded by priests and the [[artificial-intelligence|robot]]ic sentinel Big Joe, the vaults represent both forbidden knowledge and the potential salvation of a dying world.

## The Sacred Guardians

The vaults are protected by a dual layer of security: [[the-priest|the priest]]s who guard the entrance, and the Sleeper—a [[artificial-intelligence|robot]] called Big Joe—who guards the inner door:

> "Fool! The vaults are sacred! The priests guard the entrance, and the Sleeper guards the inner door. They'll kill you if you try it, and if you linger, the council will kill you tomorrow." [002-082]

This sacred status makes the vaults untouchable to ordinary Martians, their contents shrouded in religious awe and mortal fear.

## Asir's Quest

The protagonist [[asir|Asir]] believes the vaults contain the key to reversing [[mars|Mars]]'s ecological collapse. As a thief who has memorized fragments of ancient knowledge, he is uniquely positioned to understand their value:

> "The vaults," Asir insisted. "The key to the ancients—" [002-056]

His determination to enter the vaults defies both the council's judgment and the lethal reputation of its guardians:

> "But not to the hills. I'm going out to the vault." [002-081]

The vaults thus become a symbol of forbidden enlightenment—the knowledge that could save a world, locked away by fear and tradition.

## The Technological Legacy

What lies within the vaults is never fully revealed, but the implication is clear: the ancients possessed technology that could renew the atmosphere and restore [[mars|Mars]]. The vaults preserve what the current civilization has forgotten—making them not just a physical location but the embodiment of lost human potential.
