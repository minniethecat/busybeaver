# Social Classification

## The Logic of Sorting

Across Miller's fiction, societies classify their members — by [[genetic-classification|genetic fitness]] in the [[federation|Federation]], by [[ritual-economy|ritual ownership]] on [[mars-colony|Mars]], by office and function in the presidential system. Classification is not neutral description but an act of power: to classify is to define who counts and who does not.

## Federation vs. Mars

The Federation classifies by biology (genes, heredity, fitness); the Mars colony classifies by knowledge (ritual ownership, clan membership). Both systems produce the same effect: some lives matter more than others. The Federation's [[class-c-citizen|Class-C]] and Mars's [[asir|ritual-thieves]] are different in kind but identical in function — they are the classified, the excluded, the conditionally human.
