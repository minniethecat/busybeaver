# Literary Naturalism

## The Materialist Method

Miller's fiction is marked by a distinctive literary naturalism — a refusal to separate moral questions from their material conditions. The [[federation|Federation]]'s cruelty is not abstract but physical: bodies classified, reproduction controlled, substitutes manufactured and disposed of. [[old-donegal|Donegal]]'s guilt is not a spiritual condition but a physical one — radiation sickness, the body's record of a working life in space.

## Bodies and Systems

Miller's naturalism treats social systems ([[bureaucracy]], [[genetic-classification]], the [[ritual-economy]]) as material forces, as real in their effects as gravity or radiation. A classification certificate is a piece of paper; its consequence — denial of reproduction, assignment to a manufactured substitute — is written on the body. The moral horror of Miller's fiction is inseparable from its materialism: the system is real because its effects are physical.
