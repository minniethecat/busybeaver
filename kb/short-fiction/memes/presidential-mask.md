# Presidential Mask

## The Hidden Face

The President of the [[federation|Western Federation of Autonomous States]] wears a golden mask at all times. No one sees the President's face. The mask makes the office independent of the officeholder — the President is not a person but a function, and the mask is the function's visible sign.

## The Mask of Tragedy

Miller describes the mask as the "golden mask of tragedy" — a theatrical reference that frames the Presidency as performance. The President is an actor playing a role; the mask signals that what the public sees is not a person but a representation. The tragedy is that the person behind the mask — John Smith XVI — has "no real name of his own" (003-001). The mask consumes the man.

## Depersonalization of Power

The mask is the physical embodiment of Miller's theme of bureaucratic depersonalization. Just as the [[genetic-classification|genetic classification system]] reduces humans to categories, the mask reduces the head of state to a symbol. The [[stand-in|Stand-in]] who finally removes the mask — who kills rather than continue the performance — is rejecting not just a role but the entire system of depersonalized power.
