# Edward

## The Ballad's Echo

"The Ties That Bind" opens with a quotation from the traditional Scottish ballad "Edward, Edward":

> *Why does your brand sae drop wi' blude,*
> *Edward, Edward?*
> *Why does your brand sae drop wi' blude,*
> *And why sae sad gang ye, O?*

The ballad tells of a son who has killed his father — a crime revealed through the mother's questioning. The epigraph signals the story's concern with kin-murder, clan loyalty, and the blood-ties that bind and destroy.

## In the Story

Edward's role in "The Ties That Bind" is informed by the ballad tradition: a figure caught in the impossible logic of clan obligation, where loyalty to kin may require violence against kin.
