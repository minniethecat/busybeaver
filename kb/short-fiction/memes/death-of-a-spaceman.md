# Death of a Spaceman

**"Death of a Spaceman"** is a 1954 science fiction short story by [[walter-m-miller-jr|Walter M. Miller Jr.]], included in the Standard Ebooks *Short Fiction* collection. It tells the story of Old Donegal, an elderly spaceman on his deathbed, and his family's vigil as they await his passing [005-025].

## Plot

Old Donegal is dying, and his family — including his wife Martha and son — gather around him. Father Paulson, a priest, visits to administer last rites and counsel the family. The story unfolds in the intimate space of the Donegal home, interweaving memories of Donegal's spacefaring past with the present reality of his mortality. Martha serves whiskey to the gathered visitors as they confront grief, memory, and the meaning of a life spent partly among the stars [005-025].

## Themes

The story explores mortality, family obligation, the rituals of death, and the enduring human need for spiritual comfort. Donegal's identity as a spaceman shapes how his family and community understand his life and approaching death — the cosmos becomes both backdrop and metaphor for the ultimate unknown [005-025].
