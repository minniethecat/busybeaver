# Anthropos Incorporated

**Anthropos Incorporated** (often referred to as **Anthropos, Inc.**) is a biotechnology corporation in the world of "[[conditionally-human-work|Conditionally Human]]." It pioneered the production of mutant animals and neutroids — quasi-human beings created through evolvotron technology [001-020].

## Operations

Anthropos operated evolvotron manufacturing facilities, including one in New Atlanta, Georgia. The company employed biologists and evolvotron operators to design and produce custom mutant animals for the pet trade and other purposes [001-020]. Notable employees included Delmont, a pioneering evolvotron operator whose work produced unique neutroid specimens such as Peony.

## Mutant Animal Production

The company was responsible for large-scale mutant animal production. Serial numbers tracked batches of mutant animals — for example, the Bermuda-K-99 series covered animals with birth dates during July [001-020]. Anthropos was cautious about making quasi-humans too intelligent, "lest some clod might try to use them for a morally objectionable purpose" [001-020]. The company established limits on intelligence and capability for its products.

## Relationship with the Federation

Anthropos operated under the regulatory framework of the Western Federation of Autonomous States. The Federation's rules required all mutants to be neutered. Anthropos complied with these regulations in its standard production lines, though Delmont's unauthorized work produced at least one fertile neutroid.
