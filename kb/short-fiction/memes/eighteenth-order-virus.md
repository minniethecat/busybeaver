# Eighteenth-Order Virus

## The Disease

Eighteenth-order virus is the disease afflicting [[sarah-glubbes|Sarah Glubbes]]'s neutroid child in "Conditionally Human." The virus is medically significant not for its pathology but for what it reveals about the [[federation|Federation]]'s medical economy: it "can be cured with the proper treatment — in humans" (001-105).

## Treatable — For Humans

The distinction between "treatable in humans" and fatal in [[neutroid|neutroids]] is not biological but economic and legal. Hospitals will not admit neutroids; doctors will not treat them; the treatment exists but is not allocated to beings classified as property. The virus kills not by its virulence but by the system's refusal to extend care across the human/substitute boundary.

This is the hidden architecture of the classification system: medical triage based on genetic status. A Class-C citizen like Mrs. Glubbes can watch her neutroid child die of a disease she knows is curable — a cruelty designed into the system.
