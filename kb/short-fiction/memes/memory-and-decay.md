# Memory and Decay

## The Half-Life of Knowledge

The [[mars-colony|Mars colony]] is built on knowledge that is decaying. The original colonists — the [[ancient-gods|Ancient Gods]] — understood the [[blaze-of-the-winds|Blaze of the Winds]] as technology; their descendants know it only as myth. Technical manuals became [[ritual-economy|ritual chants]]; maintenance procedures became liturgy; the names of scientists (Fermi, Einstein) became the names of gods.

## Generational Loss

Miller documents the stages of decay: first, the loss of the physical books ("The books themselves were no longer in existence"); then, the fragmentation of oral memory ("most men owned scattered phrases, and scattered phrases remained meaningless"); finally, the approach of extinction ("Mars, once dead, was dying again"). Each generation knows less than the last, and the knowledge needed to reverse the decay is itself subject to the decay it might reverse.
