# Political Theater

## The Performance of Power

In "Check and Checkmate," governance has become theater. The President wears a [[presidential-mask|golden mask]]; the Stand-ins are actors trained to replace him; the rabble's votes are counted in the billions but their power is channeled through institutions that render them "really impotent." Politics is a performance whose audience — the rabble — is allowed to vote but not to govern.

## The Script

The President's pronouncements are scripted. Miller emphasizes his "smooth tenor, slightly muffled by the mask" (003) — the voice is pleasant, processed, mediated. The Stand-ins nod dutifully. The scene is a rehearsal, not a deliberation. Governance is the repetition of approved lines.

## When the Performance Breaks

The [[stand-in|Stand-in]]'s act of violence at the story's end is a rupture in the theater — a moment when the performance stops and something real happens. But the rupture is not revolutionary; it is terminal. The system has no mechanism for genuine action, only for its own continuation or termination.
