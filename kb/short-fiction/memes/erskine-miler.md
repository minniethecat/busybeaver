# Erskine Miler

## The Official Voice

Police Chief Erskine Miler appears briefly in "Conditionally Human" as the official voice reporting on the murder of [[doctor-georges|Doctor Georges]]. He is interviewed by reporter [[james-duncan|James Duncan]] outside the Georges residence and provides the factual account of the crime.

## Role in the Narrative

Miler's function in the story is to represent civic authority — the police, the law, the state — in its response to the murder. His report is factual and procedural, but this very factuality is significant: the system that produced the murder (genetic classification, manufactured substitutes, the denial of medical care to [[neutroid|neutroids]]) is the same system Miler represents.

He does not comment on the underlying causes of the crime. His job is to report what happened — "Doctor Georges' death has shocked the community deeply" (001-287) — not to ask why a woman would murder her family physician over a manufactured substitute. The gap between the official account and the moral reality is one of the story's central tensions.
