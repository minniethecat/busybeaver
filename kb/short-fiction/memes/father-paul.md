# Father Paul

Father Paul was the priest who attended Old Donegal in his final hours in "Death of a Spaceman." He administered last rites and provided spiritual counsel to the Donegal family. [PN=004-167]

## Ministry to Donegal

As Donegal lay dying in his home, Father Paul was a constant presence. When Martha and others sought to see the old man, they were told they could not until Father Paul had finished his ministrations. His voice could be heard from the sickroom, asking about the patient and offering prayers. [PN=004-167][PN=004-173]

## Assessment of Providence

Father Paul offered a characteristically blunt spiritual assessment of Donegal's condition. When asked about the old man's spiritual state, he grunted sourly that Providence had taken a particular interest in the case. His gruff demeanor masked a genuine pastoral concern for the dying spaceman and his grieving family. [PN=004-197]

## Final Counsel

After Donegal's death, Father Paul was seen on the terrace talking to someone — a final act of pastoral care for the bereaved household. His presence bookended Donegal's passage, from confession to burial. [PN=004-222]
