# Mars Colony

## A Dying World

The Mars colony in Miller's fiction is a society in decline. Once terraformed by the original colonists — the "Ancient Fathers" who "rode down from the heavens in the Firebirds of the Sun" (002-002) — Mars is now dying again. Its atmosphere, generated by the [[blaze-of-the-winds|Blaze of the Winds]] deep underground, is leaking back into space.

The colony's inhabitants know this. [[asir|Asir]], through his stolen ritual knowledge, understands that "Mars, once dead, was dying again, its air leaking away once more into space. And Man would die with it, unless something were done, and done quickly" (002-121). But knowledge alone is not enough; the colony's social structure prevents action.

## Tribal Society

Martian society has devolved into a tribal structure organized around kinship and ritual. The Council of Senior Kinsmen governs; the [[chief-commoner|Chief Commoner]] adjudicates; the people live in villages separated by "low hills of Mars, sun-washed and gray-green with trees, trees brought down from the heavens by the Ancient Fathers" (002-011).

Technology has become magic. The original colonists' science is remembered only as "ritual chants" — memorized formulas whose meaning has been lost. The [[ritual-economy|ritual economy]] values possession over understanding: owning a chant conveys wealth, but no one can explain what it means or how to apply it.

## The Vaults

Beneath the surface, the original colonists left infrastructure: the [[blaze-of-the-winds|Blaze of the Winds]], the vaults guarded by [[big-joe|Big Joe]], the machinery that could restore the atmosphere. But access to this infrastructure has been ritualized. The twenty-four numbers that control Big Joe are a "holy phrase" owned by specific families; no one has entered the vaults in generations.

This is the colony's tragedy: the means of salvation exist, but the society has forgotten how to use them. The knowledge is stored in the same [[ritual-economy]] that prevents its application — a paradox that Asir, as a thief who can synthesize stolen fragments, may be uniquely positioned to resolve.

## Connection to the Federation

The Mars colony exists in the same universe as the [[federation|Federation]] of "Conditionally Human" and "Check and Checkmate," though the exact relationship is unclear. Both societies share Miller's characteristic concern: the alienation of people from the systems they depend on, whether those systems are genetic classification bureaucracies or the atmospheric machinery of a dying world.
