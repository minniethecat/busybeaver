# Big Bottomless

**Big Bottomless** is the colloquial name for the vast expanse of deep space beyond Earth orbit in "[[the-hoofer|The Hoofer]]." It is where the protagonist, Big Hogey Parker, spent his working life as a spaceman before returning home to Earth [006-030].

## Description

From Big Bottomless, Earth appeared as "a fat moon with fuzzy mold growing on it" — a distant, almost unrecognizable orb. The space station rose in the west, "floating out in Big Bottomless," where Parker's gang worked: Nichols, Guerrera, Lavrenti, and Farran [006-030]. The region represented the professional world of spacemen, in stark contrast to the domestic life on Earth that Parker struggled to reintegrate into.

## Cultural Significance

For the spaceman protagonist, Big Bottomless represented freedom, camaraderie, and purpose. Returning from Big Bottomless was disorienting — the phrase "when a man was just back from Big Bottomless" served as an explanation and excuse for Parker's strange behavior and difficulty readjusting to terrestrial life [006-030]. Parker's longing for Big Bottomless manifested in his inability to connect with his family, culminating in his staring out at the space station and the deep beyond it.
