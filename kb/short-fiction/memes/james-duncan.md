# James Duncan

## The Reporter

James Duncan is a radio reporter for a Sherman II station, reporting from a mobile unit outside the home of the murdered [[doctor-georges|Doctor Hiram Georges]]. His broadcast provides [[norris|Norris]] — and the reader — with the factual account of the murder.

## Broadcast Style

Duncan's reporting is professional and formulaic: "This is James Duncan, ladies and gentlemen, speaking to you from our mobile unit in front of the late Doctor Hiram Georges' residence just west of Sherman II" (001-287). His interview with Police Chief [[erskine-miler|Erskine Miler]] provides the official version of events — a version that Norris, listening at home, recognizes as incomplete.

## Narrative Function

Like [[erskine-miler|Chief Miler]], Duncan represents the institutional voice that reports events without questioning their causes. The news report tells what happened but not why — a distinction that matters deeply in a story about systemic injustice. The news can report a murder; it cannot report the system of [[genetic-classification]] that produced the conditions for murder.
