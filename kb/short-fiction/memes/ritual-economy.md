# Ritual Economy

## Knowledge as Currency

In the Mars colony of "It Takes a Thief," wealth is measured not in material goods but in memorized ritual formulas — fragments of scientific and technical knowledge passed down from the original colonists, the "Ancient Fathers." These ritual chants are the currency of the society: they are "spent" in transactions, traded for wives, and hoarded as capital.

The system is described through [[asir|Asir]]'s experience as a thief. A ritual-thief does not steal physical objects but "the strength of their souls" (002-027) — the memorized chants that constitute a family's wealth and social standing. The thief memorizes the chants by overhearing transactions, accumulating knowledge that belongs to others.

## How It Works

The economy is documented in the story through Asir's meditation on his trade:

> "A thief, if successful, frequently became endowed with wisdom, for he memorized more wealth than a score of honest men. Quotations from the ancient gods — Fermi, Einstein, Elgermann, Hanser and the rest — most men owned scattered phrases, and scattered phrases remained meaningless. But a thief memorized all transactions that he overheard, and the countless phrases could be fitted together into meaningful ideas." (002-045)

This passage reveals the economy's fundamental tension: fragmented knowledge is merely currency, but aggregated knowledge becomes power. A ritual formula in isolation might buy a wife; assembled into a coherent body, the same formulas reveal that Mars is dying and how it might be saved.

## The Ritual-Thief

A ritual-thief occupies a paradoxical position in the society. The thief is a criminal — Asir is hung by his wrists and threatened with execution — but also a potential savior. The knowledge Asir has stolen, assembled into a whole, contains the key to rekindling the [[blaze-of-the-winds|Blaze of the Winds]] and restoring Mars's atmosphere.

This paradox reflects a broader theme in Miller's work: the tension between institutional knowledge (controlled, fragmented, owned) and practical knowledge (stolen, synthesized, applied). The Council of Senior Kinsmen possesses the rituals through legitimate ownership but cannot use them effectively; Asir possesses them illegitimately but can.

## Degradation and Loss

The ritual economy is inherently unstable. Over generations, chants are forgotten, misremembered, or fragmented beyond use. The original books — the "holy books" of the Ancient Fathers — no longer exist; their content survives only in memory (002-051). This means knowledge is always decaying, always becoming sparser and more garbled.

The colony's crisis — Mars is losing its atmosphere — is directly traceable to this decay. The knowledge of how to maintain the [[blaze-of-the-winds|Blaze]] was stored in rituals that have since been lost. The economy that once preserved knowledge now accelerates its disappearance.

## Parallels to the Federation

The Mars colony's ritual economy mirrors the [[federation|Federation]]'s system of genetic classification in important ways. Both replace direct relationship with mediated transaction: in the Federation, genetic certificates replace the biological fact of parenthood; on Mars, ritual formulas replace the direct understanding of technology. In both cases, the mediating system — created to serve human needs — becomes an end in itself, and the humans within it forget what the system was for.
