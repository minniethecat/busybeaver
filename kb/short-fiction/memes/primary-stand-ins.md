# Primary Stand-ins

## The Nine

The President of the [[federation|Federation]] is served by nine Primary Stand-ins — men trained to replace him, to speak with his voice, to wear the [[presidential-mask|golden mask]] if necessary. They sit with him in the study, nod dutifully at his pronouncements, and represent the ultimate fungibility of power in a bureaucratic state.

## The Logic of Replaceability

The Stand-in system embodies Miller's vision of bureaucratic depersonalization. If the President is merely a function — an office, not a person — then any qualified body can perform the function. The nine Stand-ins are interchangeable parts in a machine. The horror, for [[stand-in|Smith]], is recognizing that replaceability goes both ways: if the Stand-in can replace the President, the President was always already replaceable.
