# Testament

## The Dying Word

Miller's dying characters do not go quietly. [[old-donegal|Old Donegal]] insists on the terms of his burial: "I want to be buried face-down... I been up — *way* up. Now I'm going straight down" (004). The testament is an assertion of meaning against the void — not just death but the institutional erasure that precedes it.

## Testimony Against the System

The testament in Miller's fiction serves as counter-testimony to the official record. Donegal's burial instructions contradict the institutional narrative that space travel is safe, routine, glamorous. The [[stand-in|Stand-in]]'s final act — killing rather than continuing the performance — is a testament in action, a refusal to let the system have the last word.
