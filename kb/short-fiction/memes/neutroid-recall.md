# Neutroid Recall

## Origin

The neutroid recall originates from the [[delmont|Delmont negligency case]] at the Bermuda Factory, where a technician passed a defective ovum through inspection. The resulting female [[neutroid|neutroid]] reproduced before being detected, spreading genetic defects through the K-99 series. By the time the flaw was discovered, an estimated forty K-99s had entered [[norris|Norris]]'s district alone (001-087).

## Execution

Norris receives a memo instructing him to locate, test, and either clear or destroy every K-99 in his two-hundred-square-mile district. The task is made urgent by the neutroids' reproductive capability — each day's delay risks further contamination.

The recall requires Norris to seize neutroids from families who have bonded with them. Three citizens resist; Norris files charges. [[sheriff-yates|Sheriff Yates]] assists with seizures. The work is emotionally grueling — Norris fills cages with living beings he knows he will have to kill.

## Moral Cost

The recall's true cost is measured in grief. [[sarah-glubbes|Sarah Glubbes]]'s neutroid — possibly one of the K-99s — dies of eighteenth-order virus. Her subsequent murder of [[doctor-georges|Doctor Georges]] is a direct consequence of the system the recall serves. Norris's morning meditation — "Why should he have to kill the things?" (001-335) — implicates not just the recall but the entire structure of manufacture and disposal that makes such recalls necessary.
