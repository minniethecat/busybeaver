# The Peoplesfriend

Ivan Ivanovitch the Ninth, known as the Peoplesfriend and Vicar of the Asian Proletarian League, was the leader of the Eastern hemisphere in "Check and Checkmate." He engaged in a series of diplomatic negotiations with President John Smith XVI. [PN=003-002]

## Diplomatic Negotiations

The Peoplesfriend entered into televiewphone conversations with President Smith shortly after Smith's inauguration, in what appeared to be the first diplomatic contact between the two superpowers since the Big Silence. Communication between the hemispheres was considered nearly impossible due to the Hell Wall and interference stations. The Peoplesfriend's willingness to negotiate came as a surprise to Smith's administration. [PN=003-002]

During their talks, the Peoplesfriend proved blunt and calculating. When Smith suggested mutual armament reduction for economic benefit, the Peoplesfriend threw back his masked head and laughed uproariously — he had sized up the American president correctly. [PN=003-037][PN=003-039][PN=003-042]

## The Arms Negotiations

The Peoplesfriend demonstrated superior knowledge of Western armaments and military capabilities. He had nothing to gain by revealing his own hemisphere's military strength, and Smith was shocked by how readily the Peoplesfriend agreed to terms that appeared disadvantageous to the East. The Peoplesfriend's references to industrial usage of crystal-structure physics and specialized labor hinted at Eastern advances in servomechanisms and auto-control devices. [PN=003-134][PN=003-138][PN=003-130]

## The Conference

At the final in-person conference, the Peoplesfriend arrived with a platoon of lightly armed troops. During the meeting, he ordered his deputies to search the American delegation. When Smith's team revealed evidence of Eastern espionage, the Peoplesfriend smiled coldly and produced a prepared statement. The encounter ended with veiled threats from both sides.
