# J. "Doggy" O'Reilley

J. "Doggy" O'Reilley was the owner of a pet shop in Sherman II, one of the largest Sherman communities. His shop sold pets, goldfish, and neutroids — genetically engineered animals marketed to childless couples. A sign outside advertised: "Buy a bundle of joy." [PN=001-175]

## Pet Shop

O'Reilley's shop was warm and gloomy, permeated by the strong musk of animal odors. It was not, as Norris observed, "a shining example of cleanliness." [PN=001-176] The shop was located in Sherman II, a fifty-block commercial district. [PN=001-173]

## Personal History

O'Reilley's wife Mary had died five years before the events of the story. As a class-B couple, they were allowed one child of their own — but were unable to conceive. Class-B citizens were not permitted to own neutroids, so the O'Reilleys circumvented the restriction by running a pet shop. Mary would cry whenever they sold a neutroid. [PN=001-215]

## Peony and the Falsified Receipt

When a shipment of Bermuda-K-99 neutroids arrived, O'Reilley sold most of them quickly, but one — Peony — was "kinda puny" and no one wanted her. He kept her so long that he grew attached and feared someone would buy her. To protect her, he faked the sales receipt and moved her to his living quarters. [PN=001-215] This falsification ultimately led to the Delmont Negligency Case investigation and brought Inspector Norris to his shop. [PN=001-168]
