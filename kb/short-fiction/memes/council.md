# Council

The **Council** appears across several of Walter M. Miller Jr.'s short stories as an institution of local governance.

It ranges from the Martian village council in "It Takes a Thief" to the councils of the clans in "[[the-ties-that-bind-work|The Ties That Bind]]." Though varying in composition and authority across settings, the council serves as a persistent symbol of collective decision-making and social order.

## Martian Village Council

In "It Takes a Thief," the Martian village council exercises judicial and executive power over the inhabitants. It holds the authority to order banishment, a punishment tantamount to death on the dying planet:

> "In view of your extreme youth, thief," the knifeman growled, "the council has ordered you perpetually banished. The sun is setting. Let dawn find you in the hills. If you return to the plains, you will be chained to a wild hüffen and dragged to death." [002-045]

The council's judgment is final and absolute. When [[asir|Asir]] demands to be heard—wishing to share the wisdom he has accumulated as a thief—the council refuses:

> "The council will hear nothing, and you shall be gone before dawn. And the vaults are guarded by the sleeper called Big Joe. To enter is to die. Now go away." [002-059]

[[asir|Asir]]'s defiance later transforms his relationship with the council entirely; after returning with the [[artificial-intelligence|robot]] Big Joe and the vault's knowledge, he envisions himself as the next leader of the very body that banished him:

> He envisioned the pandemonium that would reign when girl, man, and robot marched through the village to the council house, and he chuckled. "I think that I shall be the next Chief Commoner," he said. "And my councilmen will all be thieves." [002-272]

## Presidential Council

In "Check and Checkmate," the council takes a different form as the advisory body to the President—the [[stand-in|Stand-in]]s who serve as bodyguards and advisors:

> John XVI seemed quite pleased with himself as he sat with his staff of Primary Stand-ins in the study of his presidential palace. ...the nine Stand-ins who were also bodyguards, council-members, and advisors to the chief executive. [003-003]

## Clan Councils

In "[[the-ties-that-bind-work|The Ties That Bind]]," councils appear at the tribal level, serving as forums for collective deliberation among the clans of a future [[earth|Earth]]:

> And a voice of wisdom in the councils of the clans: "The sons of men—they've come home from the Star Exodus. Our brothers." [006-008]
