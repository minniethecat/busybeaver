# Kinship

## The Double Bind

Kinship in Miller's Mars fiction is a double bind: it is both the deepest form of human connection and the structure that most tightly constrains individual action. [[clan-structure|Clans]] protect their members and demand their loyalty; family is refuge and prison in the same structure.

## Blood and Obligation

"The Ties That Bind" takes its epigraph from the ballad "Edward, Edward" — a dialogue between a mother and a son who has killed his father. The kinship tie is literally mortal: blood binds, and blood kills. The [[mars-colony|Mars colony]]'s clan system makes kinship political, transforming family obligation into the mechanism of social control.
