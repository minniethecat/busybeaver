# Norris Home

## Domestic Refuge

[[norris|Norris]]'s home with his wife [[anne|Anne]] functions as a space of domestic refuge from the moral demands of his work as a pound inspector. It is here that Anne tends Norris's bruised face after the Wylo City confrontation; here that they share the small intimacies of a [[class-c-citizen|Class-C]] marriage; here that Norris listens to the radio broadcast of [[doctor-georges|Doctor Georges]]'s murder.

## The Limits of Refuge

But the home cannot seal out the system. The radio brings news of murder; the morning brings the inevitable return to the pound. The home is sanctuary, but sanctuary in Miller's fiction is always temporary, always permeable. The [[federation|Federation]] does not stop at the door.
