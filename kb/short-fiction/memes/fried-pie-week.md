# Fried Pie Week

**Fried Pie Week** is a designated calendar week in the future era depicted in "[[check-and-checkmate|Check and Checkmate]]." It serves as the equivalent of "Veto Week" in the Western Federation's naming convention and was used as a critical diplomatic deadline [003-020].

## Diplomatic Significance

Fried Pie Week became the target date for a major diplomatic and military planning effort between the Western Federation of Autonomous States and the Asian Proletarian League. The Secretary of Defense and the President's mask- wearing counterpart set "Seventhday of Fried Pie Week" as the deadline for preparing "a maximum possible effort" with a comprehensive plan [003-020]. The negotiations involved coordinating the size of armaments and military dispositions between the two rival powers.

## Meeting at Tharviana

The diplomatic effort tied to Fried Pie Week culminated in a planned meeting at Tharviana in the Byrd-Ellsworth Sector of Antarctica. Advance cadres of personnel from both sides were scheduled to arrive at the meeting site two weeks prior to the Fried Pie Week deadline [003-020]. This Antarctic summit represented a rare moment of direct negotiation between the two global powers separated by the Hell Wall and decades of the Big Silence.

## Calendar Context

In the world of "Check and Checkmate," weeks bear distinctive names that differ between cultures. What the Western Federation called "Fried Pie Week" was known to the Asian Proletarian League as "Veto Week," demonstrating the linguistic and cultural divergence between the two societies despite their shared calendar structure [003-020].
