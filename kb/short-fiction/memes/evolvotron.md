# Evolvotron

## Definition

The evolvotron is the genetic engineering apparatus at the heart of Anthropos, Inc.'s neutroid manufacturing process in "[[conditionally-human|Conditionally Human]]" (001). It is a machine that reshapes gene structures through subatomic bombardment, converting unfertilized chimpanzee ova into the genetic templates for quasi-human pets. The name itself — combining "evolution" with the "-tron" suffix of particle physics apparatus — captures Miller's vision of a society that has industrialized the creative forces of biology itself.

## Operation

The evolvotron's operation is described in precise technical detail through [[norris|Norris]]'s explanation to [[anne|Anne]]. The operator "takes the unfertilized chimpanzee ova out of the egg-multiplier, mounts them in his machine, and bombards the gene structure with subatomic particles" (001-107). The work is delicate and visual: "He flashes a huge enlargement of the ovum on the electron microscope screen — large enough so he can see the individual protein molecules" and compares the live image against "an artificial gene pattern" (001-107).

Miller describes the process as "shooting subatomic billiards" — firing alpha-particles into the gene structure to "displace certain links by just the right amount" (001-107). The operator must work quickly, balancing precision against the ovum's limited survival time under the radiation of the enlarger. A skilled operator "can get one success out of seven tries" (001-107).

## Human Fallibility

The evolvotron's technical precision does not eliminate human error. The [[delmont-negligency-case|Delmont case]] — the story's inciting incident — involves a "greenhorn evolvotron operator at the Bermuda plant" who, after a week of failure and facing termination, "reported one success the next day. It was faked" (001-107). The ovum passed to the incubators had "something wrong in the central nervous system's determinants, and in the glandular makeup" — defects that would produce a neutroid capable of sexuality, violating the core design constraint of neuter infertility.

The fake exposes the system's vulnerability: production pressure creates incentives to falsify results, and the defects are not detectable until after birth. The technology that creates life is, like all human technologies, subject to human weakness.

## Cultural Status

By the story's time, the evolvotron has entered popular culture. [[norris|Norris]] recognizes the theme song of "A Chimp to Call My Own" — "a popular soap-opera about a lady evolvotron operator" (001-177). The machine that reshapes genes has become a backdrop for mass entertainment, suggesting a society that has normalized genetic engineering to the point of trivialization. The evolvotron operator replaces the dogcatcher ("before the evolvotron, before Anthropos went into the [[mutant-animals|mutant-animal]] business, people used to elect dogcatchers," 001-016) — a comparison that frames genetic manufacture as an extension of animal control rather than a Promethean act.
