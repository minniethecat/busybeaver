# Death of a Spaceman

## Publication

"Death of a Spaceman" was first published in 1954. It appears as the fourth story in this Standard Ebooks collection.

## Plot

[[old-donegal|Old Donegal]] is dying of radiation cancer — the accumulated toll of a lifetime in space. His wife [[martha|Martha]] and son [[donny|Donny]] watch the slow extinction of a man whose body carries the record of every mission, every risk, every year in the void. Donegal faces death with the same pragmatic grit he brought to space: "I want to be buried face-down... I been up — *way* up. Now I'm going straight down" (004).

## Themes

- **The body as record**: Donegal's failing body documents a working life in space
- **Obsolescence**: The old spacer is being replaced by automation and younger, healthier crews
- **Mortality and legacy**: What survives when the body fails and the profession dies?
