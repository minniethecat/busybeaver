# Class-C Citizen

## Definition

In the [[federation|Western Federation]]'s genetic classification system, a "class-C" designation marks citizens as possessing "defective heredity" — heritable conditions that the state deems unfit for propagation. The [[norris|Norris]]es are class-C "on account of heart-trouble in both our families" (001-043), and this classification carries a single, devastating legal consequence: they are forbidden to have children.

## The Classification System

The [[federation|Federation]]'s class hierarchy is referenced in tiers — the [[norris|Norris]]es are explicitly class-C — but Miller provides only fragmentary detail about the full scheme. What is clear is that class-C is the lowest tier of permitted citizenry (below it lie only the unpersons: the [[neutroid|mutant animals]], the [[mutant-animals|neutroids]], the disposable). The system operates as a soft eugenics regime: it does not sterilize or eliminate the genetically "unfit," but it denies them the right to reproduce while simultaneously manufacturing pet-substitutes — [[mutant-animals|neutroids]] and lesser animal units — to satisfy the human need to nurture.

"A class-C couple, allowed no children of their own, could get quite attached to a cat-Q-5. The felines were emotionally safer than the quasi-human [[neutroid|chimp-K series]] called '[[mutant-animals|neutroids]]'" (001-007). The bureaucratic language — "allowed," "units" — reflects a system in which human desire is quantified and managed like any other resource.

## Substitute Children

The class-C couple's relationship to [[mutant-animals|neutroids]] is the story's emotional core. [[anne|Anne Norris]]'s desire for a child — forbidden by law — finds its outlet in her attachment to a succession of [[mutant-animals|neutroids]]. When one dies, the grief is real: "When a pet neutroid died, a family was broken with grief; but most couples could endure the death of a cat-Q or a dog-F" (001-007).

This is the [[federation|Federation]]'s cruel calculus: it anticipates and manages the emotional consequences of its own restrictions. It doesn't merely forbid reproduction; it provides engineered substitutes that are legally killable, emotionally safer to lose, and deliberately designed to remain childlike — perpetual infants that never grow up to challenge the system that created them.

## Moral Resistance

Terry [[norris|Norris]]'s rebellion against the system is personal rather than political. "I know what I'm saying. We're class-C on account of heart-trouble in both our families. Well, I don't care, Terry. I'm not going to waste a heart over one of these pathetic little artificial animals. We're going to have a baby" (001-043).

[[anne|Anne]]'s declaration — "We're going to have a baby" — is the story's most radical act. It asserts human desire against bureaucratic classification, biological risk against social engineering. That this act of resistance takes the form of an illegal pregnancy, hidden from the authorities, makes the point: in the [[federation|Federation]], even reproduction is political.
