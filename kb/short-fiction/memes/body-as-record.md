# Body as Record

## The Physical Archive

In Miller's fiction, the body tells truths that institutions cannot. [[old-donegal|Old Donegal]]'s radiation-scarred body is a record of every mission, every risk, every year in space — a physical archive more honest than any official log. [[asir|Asir]]'s nail-holes through his forearms and the rust-rubbed scar on his forehead are physical testimony to the [[bleeding-blade|bleeding-blade]]'s justice. These bodies do not forget what systems would prefer to erase.

## Against Bureaucratic Abstraction

The body as record stands in opposition to bureaucratic abstraction. The [[federation|Federation]]'s classification system treats bodies as categories (Class-A, Class-B, Class-C); Donegal's body insists on its irreducible particularity. The Mars colony's [[ritual-economy|ritual economy]] treats knowledge as abstract formulas; Asir's scarred body testifies to the physical cost of that abstraction.

## The Body's Limits

But the body as record is mortal. Donegal's body, which has recorded a lifetime of space travel, is dying — and the record dies with it. Asir's scars will fade. The body can testify but cannot preserve. This is the melancholy at the heart of Miller's materialism: the physical is real, but it is not permanent.
