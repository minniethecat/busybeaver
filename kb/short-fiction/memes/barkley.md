# Captain Barkley

Captain Barkley was a naval officer serving in "Way of a Rebel." He was described as usually jovial and slightly cynical — a seasoned professional whose clipped tones conveyed authority and experience. [PN=005-046]

## Command Style

Barkley issued orders with precision, directing his subordinates through tactical maneuvers. When Laskell surfaced his submarine and reported to Barkley, the captain would plot the navigation coordinates and relay instructions with practiced efficiency. His command style balanced naval discipline with a world-weary humor born of long service. [PN=005-046][PN=005-047]

## Political Cynicism

Barkley was notable for his political detachment. He proclaimed that "a sailor's got no politics" — a cynical stance that reflected the moral ambiguity of the conflict in which he served. His voice, always recognizable by its clipped tones, embodied the professional officer who followed orders without investing in the ideological dimensions of the war. [PN=005-050][PN=005-054]
