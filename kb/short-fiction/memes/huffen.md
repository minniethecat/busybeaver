# Hüffen

## Martian Mount

The *hüffen* is a flying creature native to Mars, domesticated by the colonists for transportation. Mara tugs "at a wing-rein" to bank a hüffen in flight (002-315), and the creature is used as a mount for travel between villages and to the cloister that guards the vaults.

## Wild and Domesticated

Wild hüffen are dangerous. The punishment for a banished criminal who returns is to be "chained to a wild hüffen and dragged to death" (002-099). This makes the hüffen simultaneously a symbol of civilization (as a domesticated mount) and of its absence (as a wild executioner).

## In Miller's Universe

The hüffen is one of the few native Martian lifeforms mentioned in Miller's fiction. Its existence suggests that Mars, despite being a dying world, is not biologically barren — there was (and perhaps still is) a native ecology. The colonists' domestication of the hüffen represents the human adaptation to, and exploitation of, that ecology.
