# It Takes a Thief

## Publication

"It Takes a Thief" was first published in 1952. It appears as the second story in this Standard Ebooks collection of Miller's short fiction.

## Setting

The story is set on a [[mars-colony|dying Mars colony]], centuries after the original Earth colonists — remembered as the [[ancient-gods|Ancient Gods]] — terraformed the planet. The colony has devolved into a tribal society where scientific knowledge has been transformed into religious [[ritual|rituals]], and the memorized chants of the ancients function as [[ritual-economy|currency]].

## Plot

[[asir|Asir]], a young ritual-thief, hangs from an execution post awaiting the stroke of the [[bleeding-blade|bleeding-blade]]. His crime: stealing the memorized ritual chants that constitute wealth in his village. His only hope is [[mara|Mara]], the daughter of a Senior Kinsman, whose ambiguous loyalty may save or doom him.

Banished rather than killed, Asir learns that his stolen knowledge — assembled from countless fragments — contains a terrible truth: Mars is dying, its atmosphere leaking into space, and the [[blaze-of-the-winds|Blaze of the Winds]] must be rekindled or the colony will perish. To reach the Blaze, he must pass [[big-joe|Big Joe]], the metal guardian in the vaults below, whose floor-tile puzzle has killed every intruder for generations.

## Themes

- **Knowledge versus wisdom**: The colony possesses knowledge (in ritual form) but not the wisdom to use it
- **The individual against the tribe**: Asir's criminal knowledge may save the society that condemned him
- **Technological decay**: The loss of understanding that accompanies the passage of generations
