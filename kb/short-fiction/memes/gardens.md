# Gardens

The **Gardens** are the inhabited landscape of future [[earth|Earth]] in Walter M. Miller Jr.'s "[[the-ties-that-bind-work|The Ties That Bind]]." In the millennia following the Star Exodus, the remnant human population has transformed the planet into a network of cultivated green [[space-travel|space]]s—whispering gardens, forest glades, and garden pathways where people live in quiet communion with nature.

## A Verdant World

The gardens are the first thing the returning starfarers see as they approach [[earth|Earth]]. The planet appears not as a technological civilization but as a garden world:

> They broadcast a view of a forest garden, slightly charred for a hundred miles... [006-118]

The image is one of deliberate cultivation—not wilderness, but a carefully tended landscape:

> ...a carefully tended Japanese garden. [006-125]

## Daily Life in the Gardens

The [[earth|Earth]]'s inhabitants have built their lives around the gardens. They emerge from them to witness celestial events, and they retreat into them when events grow overwhelming:

> False dawn was in the east when the slivers of light appeared once again out of the eclipse shadow to rake majestically across the heavens, and again the children of Earth crowded in teeming numbers from the quiet gardens to chatter their excitement at the wonder in the sky. [006-060]

When the fleet appears, the gardens provide refuge:

> ...and fled back into the green garden. [006-133]

## The Gardenboro

The gardens have their own informal economy and culture. Wandering musicians pass through:

> ...who used to wander through our gardenboro every year to play his fiddle... [006-081]

The term "gardenboro" suggests a loose association of garden communities—a decentralized society where homes and public [[space-travel|space]]s blend seamlessly with cultivated nature.
