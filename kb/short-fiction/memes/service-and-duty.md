# Service and Duty

## The Servant's Dilemma

Miller's protagonists are often servants of the systems they question — [[norris|Norris]] the pound inspector, [[stand-in|the Stand-in]], [[laskell|Lieutenant Laskell]]. They have sworn oaths, accepted responsibilities, built careers within institutions that have become, or always were, instruments of injustice. The question is not whether the system is wrong but what a servant of the system should do about it.

## The Cost of Conscience

Each protagonist pays differently. Norris continues working, his guilt private and ineffectual. The Stand-in acts — kills and refuses to continue — but his action is terminal, not transformative. Laskell surfaces his submarine and must decide whether to return. Miller offers no clean resolution because, in his world, there is none.
