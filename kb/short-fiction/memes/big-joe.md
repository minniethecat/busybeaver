# Big Joe

## The Guardian of the Vaults

Big Joe is the ancient robotic guardian stationed in the vaults beneath the Mars colony in "It Takes a Thief." Described as "a creature of metal, twice the height of Asir" (002-385), Big Joe was "obviously designed to kill" — a sentinel left by the original colonists to protect the chamber where the [[blaze-of-the-winds|Blaze of the Winds]] could be rekindled.

## Physical Description

Big Joe's appearance is deliberately terrifying. The robot possesses "tri-fingered hands with gleaming talons, and a monstrous head shaped like a Marswolf, with long silver fangs" (002-385). Miller's narrator asks the pointed question: "Why should a metal-creature have fangs, unless he had been built to kill?"

The robot sits in the center of a large room "amid a heap of broken and whitening bones" (002-385) — the remains of previous intruders who failed to pass his challenge. His very posture communicates threat: a "motionless crouch" from which he can erupt into lethal motion.

## The Puzzle Floor

Big Joe is not merely a combatant; he is a puzzle-guardian. The floor tiles before him form a path that must be navigated in a specific sequence. Most tiles trigger his attack; only four — marked with one, two, three, and four dots — allow safe passage (002-421).

[[asir|Asir]] discovers this mechanism through experimentation, using a barbed pike to test tiles from a safe distance. The puzzle requires not just the memorized twenty-four numbers (a stolen ritual) but the practical intelligence to map numbers to physical space.

Big Joe himself provides limited guidance. When an intruder advances partway, the robot speaks: "*If the intruder makes an error, Big Joe will kill*" (002-439). The message is simultaneously a warning and a taunt — the robot is not a mindless guardian but a programmed entity with a defined (if lethal) protocol.

## Sleep and Awakening

The villagers call Big Joe "the Sleeper" because he appears dormant between intrusions. But his sleep is vigilant: a footstep on the wrong tile, a pike-tip touching the floor, and "Big Joe emitted a deafening roar, straightened with a metallic groan, and lumbered toward him, taloned hands extended and eyes blazing furiously" (002-403).

This sleep-wake cycle parallels the larger theme of Mars itself — a world that is "dying again, its air leaking away once more into space" (002-121) — and the [[blaze-of-the-winds|Blaze of the Winds]] that slumbers beneath, waiting to be rekindled. Big Joe guards not just a chamber but the potential for planetary revival.

## Function in the Story

Big Joe represents the barrier between ignorance and knowledge. The [[ritual|rituals]] that [[asir|Asir]] has stolen contain the fragmentary instructions for passing the guardian, but stolen knowledge is incomplete — Asir must figure out the application himself. The robot thus tests not just courage but understanding: can the thief transform memorized formulas into practical action?

The bones around Big Joe testify to those who failed this test. They are the physical evidence of a society that has forgotten how to use its own technology — a theme that runs through much of Miller's work.
