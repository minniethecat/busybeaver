# Pound Inspection (K-99 Recall)

## The Crisis

The pound inspection is the central plot event of "Conditionally Human." [[norris|Norris]], a pound inspector for the [[federation|Federation]], receives orders to recall and inspect all K-99-series [[neutroid|neutroids]] in his district — an area covering "about two hundred square miles" with "a replacement-quota of new neutroids around three hundred animals a month" (001-087).

The urgency is biological: the K-99s carry a defect traceable to the [[delmont|Delmont negligency case]] — a female neutroid that escaped sterilization and reproduced. The defective strain must be located and destroyed before it spreads further.

## The Inspection Process

Norris's inspection involves visiting every owner of a K-99 neutroid in his district, seizing the animals for testing, and destroying those that test positive. The work is grim: "eleven empty neutroid cages in his kennel" must be filled, and the occupants of those cages — living beings that families have come to love — must be killed (001-087).

The process is complicated by human resistance. Three Wylo City citizens resist inspection, and Norris has to file charges with [[sheriff-yates|Sheriff Yates]]: "three letters charging three Wylo citizens with resisting a Federal official — namely *me* — and charging one of them with assault" (001-151). Families do not want their substitute children taken away, even by law.

## The Moral Calculus

The inspection forces Norris to confront the system he serves. He kills neutroids that families love; he fills cages with "unclaimed inventory — awaiting destruction" (001-087); he processes living beings through a bureaucratic procedure designed for products. His reflection the next morning — "Why should he have to kill the things?" (001-335) — is the moral recognition the inspection produces.

The answer he arrives at is structural: "Society manufactured them because killing them was permissible" (001-335). The inspection is not an aberration but the system functioning as designed. Neutroids exist to be disposable; the pound inspector's job is to dispose of them when the system requires it.

## Aftermath

The inspection's urgency is intensified by the murder of [[doctor-georges|Doctor Georges]] by [[sarah-glubbes|Sarah Glubbes]], whose neutroid was among those that died. The murder makes visible what the inspection obscures: that these are not merely administrative procedures but acts with human consequences that the system cannot contain.
