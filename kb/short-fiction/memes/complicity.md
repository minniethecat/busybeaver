# Complicity

## The Unwilling Participant

Miller's protagonists are not villains; they are participants. [[norris|Norris]] kills [[neutroid|neutroids]] for a living — it is his job, his duty, the work that supports his [[class-c-citizen|Class-C]] household. [[stand-in|The Stand-in]] performs the presidency, knowing the mask conceals a system that dispossesses the rabble. [[laskell|Lieutenant Laskell]] serves a military whose orders he has come to recognize as unjust. None of them chose the system; all of them sustain it.

## The Impossibility of Innocence

Complicity in Miller's fiction is not a choice but a condition. One cannot live within the [[federation|Federation]] without participating in its logic — paying taxes that fund classification enforcement, buying products from corporations that manufacture [[neutroid|substitutes]], accepting benefits from a system one knows to be wrong. Innocence is not an option; the only question is what one does with the recognition of complicity.
