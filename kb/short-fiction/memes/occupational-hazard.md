# Occupational Hazard

## The Cost of Work

Space travel in Miller's fiction is not glamorous; it is work. And work, in Miller's universe, extracts a physical price. [[old-donegal|Old Donegal]] dies of radiation cancer — the accumulated dose of a lifetime in unshielded ships. [[big-hogey-parker|Big Hogey Parker]]'s body is breaking down from years of hopping freighters. These are not heroic sacrifices but occupational hazards — the same logic that gives miners black lung gives spacers cancer.

## The Body's Ledger

Miller treats the damaged body as a ledger of working life. Donegal's cancer sites correspond to specific missions, specific exposures, specific risks taken because the job required them. His body is not randomly failing; it is itemizing.
