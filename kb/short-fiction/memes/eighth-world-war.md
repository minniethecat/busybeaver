# Eighth World War

The Eighth World War is a historical conflict referenced in Walter M. Miller Jr.'s
"Check and Checkmate," part of the story's backstory that establishes the
devastated political landscape in which the narrative unfolds. It belongs to
Miller's vision of cyclical global conflict extending far beyond the
contemporary era.

## Historical Context

In the world of "Check and Checkmate," the Eighth World War is a past cataclysm
that shaped the power structures of the present. The war serves as background
that explains the authoritarian government and the Red regime's rise to power.

## Thematic Role

The numbering of world wars — reaching the eighth — conveys Miller's pessimistic
view of humanity's capacity for repeating catastrophic conflict. It reinforces
the story's atmosphere of entrenched institutional decay.
