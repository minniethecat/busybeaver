# Ursa

**Ursa** is a distant star system in Walter M. Miller Jr.'s "[[the-ties-that-bind-work|The Ties That Bind]]," referenced as the destination of ships emigrating from [[earth|Earth]] and the location of an alleged human colony. It represents the far frontier of human expansion in the post-Star Exodus era.

## A Distant Destination

The returning starfarers' fleet passes through or near Ursa on its long journey:

> The slow parade of signals came from the fleet, tracing the long road from Scorpius to Ursa, and it meant nothing, nothing at all, to the Earthlings. [006-125]

This passage captures the vastness of the galactic scale: a route spanning stellar regions that the [[earth|Earth]]bound humans can barely comprehend.

## The Ursan Colony

A human colony is believed to exist in Ursa, founded by emigrants seeking new worlds:

> ...going to seek out an alleged colony in Ursa, to sell ships, tools, and seeds. [006-235]

The word "alleged" is telling—in the post-Exodus era, communication between star systems is uncertain, and the existence of distant colonies is a matter of rumor rather than confirmed fact.

## The Emigrant Fleet

Ursa is mentioned alongside the broader phenomenon of emigration from [[earth|Earth]]:

> ...reimmigrating, but en route to Ursan stars. Request landing suggestions... [006-061]

The Ursan stars thus represent one node in a scattered human diaspora—civilization fragmented across the galaxy, bound by fragile threads of radio contact and fading memory.
