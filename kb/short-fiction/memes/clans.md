# Clans

The **Clans** form the fundamental social structure of future [[earth|Earth]] in Walter M. Miller Jr.'s "[[the-ties-that-bind-work|The Ties That Bind]]."

Long after humanity's departure to the stars, those who remained on [[earth|Earth]] have organized themselves into specialized clans—each with distinct roles, knowledge, and responsibilities within a post-technological society.

## The Tech Clans

The tech clans are the keepers of ancient technology, maintaining mechanisms whose original purpose has been largely forgotten.

When the starship returns from the "Star Exodus," they are the ones who operate the communication devices:

> But this time, a message came. The men of the tech clans who tended the newly activated mechanisms heard it, and the mechanisms memorized it, and played it again and again for the people, while the linguists puzzled over the unidentified language used in the transmission. [006-060]

When the elders need to respond to the message from [[space-travel|space]], they turn again to the tech clans:

> Embarrassed, the elders of the Geoark immediately called the tech clans. "Can you revive the devices that speak across space?" they asked. [006-109]

> "They are revived," answered the tech clans. [006-110]

The tech clans' role is custodial rather than innovative—they tend and revive rather than create. Their knowledge is practical and hereditary, passed down through clan lineage rather than formal education.

## The Pedaga

The Pedaga clan serves as the teaching order, responsible for educating the young. Their approach is deliberately childlike, as they believe connection with children requires maintaining a child's perspective:

> The Pedaga were teachers of the very young, and deliberately kept themselves childlike in outlook in order to make fuller contact with the children in their charge. [006-068]

This clan-based specialization reflects a society where knowledge is distributed across social units rather than centralized in institutions—each clan holds a piece of the whole, and survival depends on their cooperation.

## Councils of the Clans

Decision-making among the clans occurs through councils—gatherings where representatives deliberate collectively. It is in such a council that the first acknowledged voice of wisdom speaks regarding the returning starfarers:

> And a voice of wisdom in the councils of the clans: "The sons of men—they've come home from the Star Exodus. Our brothers." [006-008]

## Clan Organization

The clan system depicted in "[[the-ties-that-bind-work|The Ties That Bind]]" represents a post-civilization social order: after the collapse of technological civilization that accompanied the Star Exodus, those who remained reorganized around kinship and craft specialization.

The clans maintain fragments of ancient knowledge—each a thread in the tapestry of lost human achievement—without fully understanding the whole they preserve.
