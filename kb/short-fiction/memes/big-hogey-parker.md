# Big Hogey Parker

Big Hogey Parker was a space worker — a "tumbler" — returning to Earth after years in space in "The Hoofer." Disoriented by gravity and drunk, he was determined to reach his wife Marie and meet his newborn son for the first time. [PN=007-007]

## The Return Home

Arriving by bus at a rural crossroads in the Great Plains, Hogey was visibly struggling with readjustment to planetary gravity. He stumbled repeatedly, clutching the bus for support and losing his duffle bag. When asked what was wrong, he blamed "space legs" and muttered that gravity had him — he was "not used to gravity" after years of tumbling in space. [PN=007-016][PN=007-017]

## A Father's Determination

Hogey's motivation was singular: to see his family. He told the bus driver "I got me a son now — a little baby pigeon of a son. Haven't seen him yet." He was a week late arriving home and anticipated that Marie would be angry. Despite his inebriation and disorientation, Hogey pressed on, walking the three miles from the crossroads to Marie's family farm. [PN=007-012][PN=007-014]

## The Final Miles

As Hogey stumbled along the side-road toward the Hauptman place, he was nearly run over by a farmer's car. When the farmer recognized him as Marie's long-absent husband, he was shocked — Marie had been known as Marie Parker for six years. Hogey confirmed his identity: "Hogey, that's me. Big Hogey Parker." The farmer helped him and his duffle bag into the back seat, driving him the last stretch toward the reunion he had crossed space to reach. [PN=007-034][PN=007-050]
