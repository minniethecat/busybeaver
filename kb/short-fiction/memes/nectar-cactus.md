# Nectar-Cactus

## Martian Flora

The nectar-cactus is a plant native to the Mars colony, producing a liquid referred to in a children's rhyme:

> *Woman thirsts for husband,*
> *Man thirsts for wife,*
> *Baby thirsts for breast-milk*
> *Thief thirsts for knife.* (002-037)

The rhyme — "an eenie-meenie-miney for determining who should drink first from a nectar-cactus" (002-037) — suggests the plant is a communal resource, its consumption governed by ritual priority. The progression from wife to husband to baby to thief maps the colony's social hierarchy onto the act of drinking.

## Symbolic Value

The nectar-cactus appears only briefly but serves as a marker of the Mars colony's domesticated ecology — plants brought by the [[ancient-gods|Ancient Fathers]] or native species adapted to human use. The ritual surrounding its consumption mirrors the broader [[ritual-economy|ritual economy]]: even the simplest acts are governed by memorized formulas.
