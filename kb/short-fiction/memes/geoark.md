# Geoark

The **Geoark** is the supreme governing body of future [[earth|Earth]] in Walter M. Miller Jr.'s "[[the-ties-that-bind-work|The Ties That Bind]]." Composed of elders who represent the various clans of a post-technological society, the Geoark holds authority over matters affecting the entire planet, including the momentous decision of whether to welcome the returning starfarers from the Star Exodus.

## Structure and Authority

The Geoark is an oligarchic council of elders whose decisions are final and binding. When the starship returns from humanity's long [[space-travel|space]] journey, all eyes turn to the Geoark for direction:

> "It's up to the elders of the Geoark," he muttered without interest. [006-073]

The very concept of a parallel governing body is unimaginable to the people of [[earth|Earth]]:

> "Maybe they've got their own Geoark. Why should they cooperate with us?" [006-097]

> "Two Geoarks? What a strange idea." [006-098]

## Decision-Making Process

The Geoark operates through deliberation and petition. Citizens can submit formal requests for the elders to consider, though the process is slow and deliberate:

> "We could prepare a petition for the Geoark," someone suggested. [006-102]

When decisive action is required, the elders turn to the [[clans|tech clans]] for practical assistance:

> Embarrassed, the elders of the Geoark immediately called the tech clans. "Can you revive the devices that speak across space?" they asked. [006-109]

## Diplomatic Role

When the starfarers' fleet arrives, it is the Geoark that conducts diplomacy. Their voice is described as gentle but authoritative — befitting an institution that has governed [[earth|Earth]] for millennia:

> "Welcome to Earth, brother," replied the gentle voice of the Geoark. "You are welcome to remain as long as you observe our customs." [006-137]
