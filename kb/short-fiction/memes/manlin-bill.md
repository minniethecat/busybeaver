# Manlin Bill

## The Twenty-Sixth Amendment

In "Way of a Rebel," "Congress today approved the Manlin Bill" (005), also known as the Twenty-Sixth Amendment. The bill expands the [[federation|Federation]]'s military powers, including conscription provisions that directly affect [[laskell|Lieutenant Laskell]], the story's protagonist.

## The Moral Trigger

The Manlin Bill is the legislative event that precipitates Laskell's crisis of conscience. As a military officer, he is sworn to uphold the laws the bill represents; as a man, he recognizes the bill as morally indefensible. The conflict between institutional duty and personal conscience drives the story.
