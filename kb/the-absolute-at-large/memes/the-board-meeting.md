## Overview

The February 20th board meeting of the [[m-e-c|M.E.C.]] (Metallo-Electrical Company) marks the first institutional confrontation with the [[the-absolute|Absolute]]'s power. [[g-h-bondy|G. H. Bondy]], as Chairman, must defend the [[the-karburator|Karburator]]'s commercial viability while concealing its true nature from shareholders — even as his own directors begin succumbing to religious ecstasy in the boardroom.

## Context

By February, the standardized production of [[the-karburator|Karburator]]s at the Vysočany factory is in full swing — eighteen machines per day, projected to reach two hundred by July. Twelve boiler furnaces are under construction, and fifteen kilometres of private railway line have been laid for coal supply. The commercial success is undeniable, but [[g-h-bondy|Bondy]] knows the truth: each [[the-karburator|Karburator]] releases the [[the-absolute|Absolute]], and its effects on human beings are already spreading.

## The Shareholder Challenge

[[dr-hubka|Dr. Hubka]], leader of the opposition, spots the contradiction immediately: if the [[the-karburator|Karburator]] can generate a thousand horsepower from a single bucket of coal, why is [[g-h-bondy|Bondy]] building conventional boiler furnaces? [[g-h-bondy|Bondy]] refuses to answer, citing "reasons known to myself" and framing the issue as a matter of confidence.

> [(006-009)] "I'll give you my objection," cried Dr. Hubka, leaping up. "It's that the whole business looks highly suspicious. Yes, gentlemen, extremely suspicious. Mr. Bondy has forced us to erect a factory for Karburators. The Karburator, he assured us, is the only power-supply of the future. The Karburator, as he expressly stated, can develop a thousand horsepower from a single bucket of coal. And now he is talking about twelve boiler furnaces and whole truckloads of coal for them."

## Machat's Transformation

The meeting's turning point comes when [[g-h-bondy|Bondy]] calls on Mr. Machat, proprietor of a block of buildings heated by a [[the-karburator|Karburator]], to testify. Machat, once a shrewd businessman, has been transformed by the [[the-absolute|Absolute]]'s influence: he has given away his entire property to the poor and can speak only of holiness and divine love.

> [(006-015)] Machat began to smile beatifically. "Oh, we're all of the same mind in that matter. Those poor people are such saints. Some of them are ill. My daughter is looking after them, you know. We've all changed so tremendously."

[[g-h-bondy|Bondy]]'s personal stake in the matter is acute: Machat's daughter Ellen, with her seventy million fortune, had half-consented to become Mrs. [[g-h-bondy|Bondy]] before the [[the-absolute|Absolute]] intervened.

> [(006-019)] G. H. Bondy dropped his eyes. Machat's daughter Ellen, the fair-haired Ellen, with her seventy millions, tending the sick! Ellen, who was ready to be, who ought to be, who had half consented to be, Mrs. Bondy! Bondy bit his lip; things *had* turned out nicely!

## The Collapse of Business Order

The boardroom descends into chaos. Machat proposes converting the [[m-e-c|M.E.C.]] into a religious community. Old [[rosenthal|Rosenthal]], desperate to keep the peace, begs for a [[the-karburator|Karburator]] of his own. [[dr-hubka|Dr. Hubka]] demands the floor. [[g-h-bondy|Bondy]], pale and furious, threatens to resign and take the [[the-karburator|Karburator]] business under his personal control.

> [(006-023)] "We are all brothers in God's sight!" continued the radiant Machat. "Gentlemen, let us give the factory to the poor! I move that we change the M.E.C. into a religious community of 'The Humble of Heart.'"

[[dr-hubka|Dr. Hubka]], who moments earlier had been [[g-h-bondy|Bondy]]'s fiercest critic, now reverses completely — demanding that every director receive a [[the-karburator|Karburator]] for "testing purposes." [[g-h-bondy|Bondy]], realizing the board has already been infected by the [[the-absolute|Absolute]], capitulates with a shrug: "As you please."

## Narrative Significance

The board meeting is the novel's dramatization of capitalism's encounter with divinity. [[the-absolute|The Absolute]] does not attack commerce from outside — it infiltrates through the very product the company manufactures. [[g-h-bondy|Bondy]], the supreme rationalist and industrialist, finds himself helpless against a force that transforms his shareholders into saints and his business plan into an apocalyptic engine. The scene establishes the pattern that will play out globally: institutions that try to use the [[the-absolute|Absolute]] find themselves used by it instead.

## Aftermath

Following the meeting, every board member receives a [[the-karburator|Karburator]]. Within weeks, the entire board has succumbed to religious mania. [[g-h-bondy|Bondy]] later confides to his confidant: "It's a regular madhouse, Chief. You'll have to take the whole idiotic business in hand" [(006-045)]. Despite knowing the danger, [[g-h-bondy|Bondy]] cannot stop production — orders worth twenty-three million have already come in.
