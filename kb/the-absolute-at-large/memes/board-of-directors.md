## Overview

The Board of Directors of the Metallo-Electrical Company is the corporate governing body that presides over the Karburator's transformation from a scientific curiosity into a global industrial phenomenon. Meeting in Chapter X ("The Board Meeting"), the directors hear the Chairman's report on the Karburator's test results and the emerging Absolute crisis, and make the fateful decision to continue production despite full awareness of the risks.

## Role in the Novel

### The Board Meeting (Chapter X)

The Board meeting is the novel's most concentrated scene of corporate deliberation. The Chairman presents the engineering report: thirteen thousand stationary atomic boilers, eight thousand central heating systems, ten thousand atomobiles, six thousand atoplanes — already in production or on order. The scale is staggering, and the engineers confirm: "The Karburator is no swindle".

The Chairman reads the test deployment list — Sázava River, Upper Vltava, Brno Technical Institute, Hradec Králové, Slaný — each a success from an engineering standpoint. But the meeting takes a darker turn when the discussion shifts to the Absolute's side effects. Individual directors report on transformed colleagues: one has become an Orthodox Jew and given ten million to Zionism, another has joined the Bohemian Brethren.

Despite these alarming reports, the Board does not halt production. The corporate machinery — the contracts, the investments, the competitive pressure — has its own momentum that individual moral qualms cannot arrest.

### The Decision

The Board's decision to continue production while the directors themselves are being spiritually transformed by the Absolute is the novel's most damning portrait of institutional inertia. Each director can see what the Karburator is doing — literally, to their own colleagues — but no one has the institutional authority or will to stop the machine they have set in motion.

## Thematic Significance

The Board of Directors embodies bureaucratic responsibility without moral accountability. Each director is individually affected by the Absolute (through Karburators installed in their own homes and offices), yet collectively they vote to continue its spread. The Board's dysfunction is not malice but diffusion: no single director feels personally responsible for a decision reached collectively, even when the consequences are cosmic in scale.
