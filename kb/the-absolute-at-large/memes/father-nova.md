## Overview

Father Novaček is a minor character in Karel Čapek's *The Absolute at Large*, a Catholic priest active during the religious ferment that sweeps through Prague. He performs public miracles of healing, organizes a Catholic armed guard called the Maccabeans, and openly defies the Archbishop's orders to cease his supernatural activities — embodying the chaotic proliferation of religious fervour unleashed by the Absolute (022-001).

## Role & Significance

Father Novaček represents the grassroots religious enthusiasm that the Absolute ignites once it begins to spread freely. Unlike the institutional Church leadership, which attempts to regulate and control the miraculous through formal processes like canonization, Novaček operates in the street — healing the lame, raising the dead, and mobilizing militant Catholic factions. His activities exemplify how the Absolute dissolves institutional authority, replacing hierarchical order with direct, populist religious action (022-001).

His defiance of the Archbishop specifically highlights the theme of authority in crisis. Even within the Church, the chain of command breaks down when individual priests are directly possessed by divine power and no longer answer to their earthly superiors (022-002).

## Appearances

Father Novaček is mentioned in Chapter XXII ("The Old Patriot"), through the observations of Mr. Keval as he reports on the day's events to Mr. Rejzek:

> "The Free Thought Society is holding a service in Havlíček Square today. Father Novaček is performing miracles in Tyl Square. There's sure to be a row between them, you'll see. Yesterday Novaček healed a man who had been lame from birth. Then they had a procession, and just think, the fellow who'd been lame gave a Jew an awful hiding. Broke three of his ribs or more. He was a Zionist, see." (022-001)

Keval further reports on Novaček's escalating activities:

> "Father Novaček is organizing the Maccabeans; you know, a sort of Catholic armed guard. You wait, there will be a scrimmage. The Archbishop has forbidden Novaček to perform miracles, but his Reverence is like one possessed; he even goes and raises the dead." (022-002)

## Relationships

- **The Archbishop** — Novaček's ecclesiastical superior who forbids him from performing miracles. Novaček defies this order, operating as if "possessed" by divine power (022-002).
- **[[mr-rejzek|Mr. Rejzek]]** and **[[cyril-keval|Cyril Keval]]** — The two old patriots who discuss Novaček's activities as part of their ongoing commentary on the chaos engulfing Prague.
