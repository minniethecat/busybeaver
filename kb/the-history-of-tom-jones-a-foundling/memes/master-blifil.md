# Master Blifil

**Master Blifil** is the son of [[captain-blifil|Captain Blifil]] and [[bridget-allworthy|Bridget Allworthy]], nephew to [[Squire Allworthy]], and the principal antagonist of [[The History of Tom Jones, a Foundling]].

Fielding presents him as a study in hypocrisy: a young man whose outward piety conceals a cold, calculating nature bent on destroying his rival [[Tom Jones]].

## Character

Blifil is introduced in direct contrast to Tom. While the infant Tom is discovered in Allworthy's bed, Blifil arrives through the legitimate channel of his mother Bridget's marriage to [[captain-blifil|Captain Blifil]]—a union motivated entirely by greed (Book 3).

The neighborhood praises Blifil as "sober, discreet, and pious beyond his age" (Book 3, Chapter 4), but Fielding's narration reveals the emptiness behind these virtues.

Blifil's piety is performance; his discretion is calculation. When he informs on Tom for killing a partridge, he frames the betrayal as religious duty: "to bring truth to light was the duty of every religious man" (Book 3, Chapter 4).

Blifil's defining trait is his ability to weaponize virtue. He uses the language of morality not to guide his own conduct, but to destroy his rival while appearing righteous. Fielding identifies this as the deadliest form of evil: the hypocrite who believes in his own goodness.

## Role in the Novel

Blifil's machinations drive the novel's central crisis. Jealous of Allworthy's affection for Tom and coveting the entire inheritance, Blifil systematically poisons Allworthy's [[reason|judgment]]. He conceals Tom's virtues, exaggerates his faults, and ultimately engineers Tom's banishment.

His pursuit of [[Sophia Western]] is motivated not by love but by the desire to possess what Tom values. When he learns of Sophia's flight, he pursues her with the same cold calculation.

His eventual unmasking—when Allworthy discovers Blifil's long concealment of Tom's true parentage—is the novel's moral climax. Fielding allows Blifil no redemption: he is stripped of his inheritance and exits the novel unmourned.
