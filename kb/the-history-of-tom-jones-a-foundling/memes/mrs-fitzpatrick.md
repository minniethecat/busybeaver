# Mrs Fitzpatrick

**Mrs Fitzpatrick** (Harriet) is the cousin of [[Sophia Western]] and niece of [[Squire Western]]. Her ill-fated marriage to [[mr-fitzpatrick|Mr Fitzpatrick]] and subsequent flight from him mirror and complicate Sophia's own journey to [[london|London]].

## Character

Mrs Fitzpatrick is a woman of spirit and some vanity, shaped by an imprudent marriage and years of marital cruelty. Her personal history, recounted at length across Books 11–12 (Chapters 4–7), reveals a young woman who married for love against her aunt's wishes and suffered for it. She is worldly, capable of flirtation and intrigue, yet not without genuine affection for her cousin Sophia.

Her [[reason|judgment]] is flawed. She places herself under the protection of [[Lady Bellaston]], a decision that leads her into morally compromised circumstances. Unlike Sophia, whose virtue remains unassailable, Mrs Fitzpatrick navigates the world through accommodation rather than principled resistance.

## Role in the Novel

Mrs Fitzpatrick first enters the story in Book 10, Chapter 6, when her husband mistakenly bursts into the room of [[mrs-waters|Mrs Waters]] at the inn at Upton. She then encounters Sophia at a subsequent inn, and the two cousins travel together towards [[london|London]].

Her marriage serves as a cautionary parallel: she eloped with the handsome but penniless [[mr-fitzpatrick|Mr Fitzpatrick]], endured his jealousy and cruelty, and was forced to flee (Book 11, Chapter 4). In [[london|London]] she takes refuge with [[lady-bellaston|Lady Bellaston]], who uses her to separate Sophia from [[Tom Jones]].

Jones visits her in Book 16, Chapter 9, receiving only ambiguous information about Sophia. By the novel's end, she is partly reconciled with her husband, though the match remains unhappy.

## Themes

Mrs Fitzpatrick embodies the theme of **imprudent love and its consequences**. Her marriage is a warning of what happens when passion overrides [[reason|judgment]]. She also illustrates the vulnerability of women in a world where marriage is their primary security.
