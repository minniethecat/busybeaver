# Lord Fellamar

**Lord Fellamar** is a young nobleman of [[london|London]] society who becomes infatuated with [[Sophia Western]] and is manipulated by [[Lady Bellaston]] into becoming an instrument of her schemes against [[Tom Jones]].

## Character

Lord Fellamar is introduced as a well-mannered, impressionable young peer. Fielding notes that "his reputation was extremely clear, and common fame did him no more than justice, in speaking well of him" (Book 15, Chapter 2).

When he first sees Sophia at [[lady-bellaston|Lady Bellaston]]'s, he is instantly smitten, calling her "the best match in England." However, his character proves vulnerable to corruption. [[lady-bellaston|Lady Bellaston]] manipulates him by playing on his passion and aristocratic vanity.

Under her influence, Fellamar agrees to a scheme of attempted rape, dressed up as a prelude to marriage. Fielding presents him as morally ambivalent: he spends a sleepless night wrestling with his conscience and resolves to abandon the plot — only to be drawn back in.

## Role in the Novel

Lord Fellamar first appears in Book 15, where [[lady-bellaston|Lady Bellaston]] introduces him to Sophia. His infatuation makes him a useful tool for her schemes. She orchestrates a plot in which Fellamar would force himself on Sophia (Book 15, Chapter 5), but the attempt is interrupted by [[Squire Western]]'s dramatic arrival.

Fellamar continues his pursuit through proper channels, enlisting [[mrs-western|Mrs Western]] to advocate for his proposal. Sophia, however, rejects him with contempt — declaring she would as soon have "a pincushion upon my coach" as a coronet.

In the final act, Fellamar's hired gang attacks Jones, leading to the fight in which Jones wounds Fitzpatrick and is imprisoned. This makes Fellamar indirectly responsible for the crisis that nearly destroys Jones.

## Themes

Lord Fellamar embodies the novel's critique of **aristocratic privilege divorced from moral worth**. His title cannot compensate for his weakness of character. He also represents the **corrupting influence of the town** — a man gradually led into actions he would once have condemned.
