## Overview

Radium is the primary power source for Barsoomian technology in Edgar Rice Burroughs' *The Master Mind of Mars*. It serves as the energy foundation for both personal flight devices and larger aerial vessels, making it one of the most essential elements in Barsoomian civilization. The novel depicts radium as a compact, reliable, and widely available power source that has been fully integrated into everyday life on the Red Planet.

## In the Narrative

Radium appears in multiple technological contexts throughout the novel. The most prominent application is in **equilibrimotors** — personal flight belts worn by Barsoomians for individual aerial movement. These devices feature "a small radium motor" permanently attached to the back of the belt, providing propulsive force while the belt's eighth-ray filling neutralizes gravity [008-003]. The controls for the radium motor are mounted on the front of the belt, within easy reach of the wearer's hands [008-003].

Radium also powers larger **fliers**, which serve as the primary means of long-distance transportation on Barsoom. These vessels are described as "radium-powered" and utilize radium motors coupled with the eighth Barsoomian ray for both lift and propulsion [007-015]. The radium motors that power flier systems are characterized as compact yet powerful [009-030].

A third application is **radium bulbs** used for illumination. During the descent into the sub-Barsoomian vaults beneath Ras Thavas's tower, the corridors are described as being "faintly illuminated" by "tiny radium bulbs" [003-154]. These bulbs, though long undisturbed and coated with dust, continue to provide light, indicating their durability and longevity.

## Significance

Radium represents one of the key elements of Barsoomian technological achievement in Burroughs' fictional Mars. The fact that radium powers everything from personal flight belts to massive warships suggests a civilization that has mastered a compact, scalable energy technology far beyond early 20th-century Earth capabilities. The widespread availability of radium-powered devices — equilibrimotors are kept in public depots for rental [008-001] — indicates that radium is not a rare or precious resource on Barsoom but rather an abundant and democratized energy source. This stands in marked contrast to radium's scarcity and expense on Earth during the novel's 1927 publication date.
