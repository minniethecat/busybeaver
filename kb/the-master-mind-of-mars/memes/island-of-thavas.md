## Overview

The Island of Thavas is a rocky island rising approximately a dozen feet above the surrounding [[great-toonolian-marshes|Great Toonolian Marshes]], situated within sight of [[Toonol]]. Spanning roughly a hundred acres, it serves as the secluded fortress-laboratory of [[Ras Thavas]], the Master Mind of Mars. The island is the primary setting for the novel's first act: it is here that [[Vad Varo]] arrives on [[Barsoom]], learns the secrets of brain transplantation, meets [[Valla Dia]], and orchestrates the escape that sets the entire plot in motion [007-014].

## Description

The island is enclosed within a high wall, its hundred-acre expanse containing Ras Thavas's buildings, gardens, and the ancient [[tower-of-thavas|Tower of Thavas]] at its center [007-014]. The tower, built twenty-three thousand years ago by an ancestor of Ras Thavas, predates all other structures and connects to adjacent buildings on every level — "from the roof to the lowest pits beneath the ground" [007-019]. Around the tower cluster the laboratory buildings, vaults, slave quarters, and the hangar housing three slow cargo fliers used for supply runs to Toonol [007-018].

Beyond the outer wall lies a strip of rocky ground supporting a sparse forest interspersed with jungle patches — and beyond that, the oozy marsh with its narrow watercourses and small lakes [007-014]. The island's isolation is reinforced by its natural defenses: great white apes prowl the perimeter, serving the dual purpose of deterring intruders from Toonol and preventing desertion by slaves and assistants [007-017].

Beneath the laboratories extend the pits — a network of subterranean vaults and passages. It is in vault L-42-X, building 4-J-21, that Vad Varo conceals the anesthetized body of Valla Dia and discovers the half-brain transplant subjects — the ape-man Hovan Du and the human-ape [007-005]. The pits provide both concealment and secret access routes, connecting to the tower's runway and ultimately to the roof and hangar beyond [007-021].

## Events

### Arrival of Vad Varo

The novel opens with Vad Varo's mysterious transportation from the battlefields of Earth to the scarlet sward of Ras Thavas's island garden. Here he first encounters the aged surgeon — "as strange a looking individual as my eyes ever had rested upon" [001-003]. It is on this island that Vad Varo learns Barsoomian medicine, becoming an accomplished brain surgeon under Ras Thavas's tutelage.

### The Escape

The island becomes a prison from which Vad Varo and his companions must escape. After hiding Valla Dia in the pits, Vad Varo assembles his unlikely party — the resurrected Gor Hajus, Dar Tarus in his new body, and the half-ape Hovan Du. The group navigates the pits, ascends the tower, and conceals themselves aboard one of Ras Thavas's cargo fliers for the journey to Toonol [007-021].

### The Return

In the novel's climax, the party returns to the island under cover of darkness. Guided by Hovan Du's ape-calls to his brethren on shore, they land silently, descend through the pits, and reach vault L-42-X where Vad Varo had hidden Valla Dia's body thirteen chapters earlier. The brain restoration surgeries — returning Valla Dia to her own body and Xaxa to hers — are performed here, on the very *ersite*-topped tables where Vad Varo first learned the art [013-008].

## Inhabitants

The island is the private domain of Ras Thavas, the Master Mind of Mars. His household includes his body servant Yamdor, numerous slaves, and laboratory assistants. Vad Varo resides here throughout his medical apprenticeship. The great white apes that prowl the perimeter serve as both guards and wardens, their ferocity ensuring that none leave without the master surgeon's knowledge.
