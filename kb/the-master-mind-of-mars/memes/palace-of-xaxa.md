## Overview

The Palace of Xaxa is the seat of the Jeddara of Phundahl, a sprawling complex of halls, chambers, apartments, gardens, and subterranean pits. It is here that Xaxa — in Valla Dia's stolen body — holds court, and where Vad Varo and his companions stage their most audacious deception. The palace is connected by a spiral runway to the [[temple-of-tur]] on one side and the royal hangars on the roof above.

## Description

The palace architecture reflects Phundahlian artistic sensibilities, which the narrator Vad Varo finds "heavy and oppressing and without indication of high imaginative genius" [011-005]. The walls are decorated with scenes from the *Turgan*, the Phundahlian bible, depicting the creation of a flat Mars being hurled into space — a motif that appears repeatedly throughout the corridors.

The palace gates open onto a broad avenue lined with scarlet lawns and *pimalia* shrubs [011-003]. Inside the gates lies a courtyard, from which visitors are led through "an intricate maze of corridors and chambers" deliberately designed to confuse outsiders [011-005]. The complex houses approximately three thousand people — inmates, attaches, and slaves — all of whom were mobilized in the search for the escaped intruders [012-016].

## Audience Chamber

The throne room is a chamber of medium size where Xaxa sits behind a massive table, flanked by heavily armed nobles [011-005]. It was to this chamber that Gor Hajus, Vad Varo, and Hovan Du were brought before the Jeddara under the pretense of performing an ape exhibition. Xaxa watched listlessly, playing with a long, slim dagger, her attention fixed more on Vad Varo than on the performance [011-007].

## Royal Apartments

Xaxa's private apartments occupy an upper level of the palace, accessible via the spiral runway that connects the temple below to the hangars on the roof [012-005]. The apartments include sleeping chambers, dressing rooms, and a small antechamber used to conceal prisoners — Vad Varo and Gor Hajus hid Xaxa and Sag Or there during their final deception [013-002]. Slaves attend to the Jeddara's needs, and word of her return spread through the palace "with the speed of light" [013-001].

## Pits and Secrets

Beneath the palace lie extensive pits — subterranean vaults that mirror those of the House of the Dead. A search of these pits during the hunt for Vad Varo unearthed "queer things — things of which not even Xaxa dreamed," and the priests whispered that "at least one great and powerful house would fall because of what a *dwar* of the Jeddara's Guard had discovered in a remote precinct of the pits" [012-016]. These discoveries had political consequences that rippled through Phundahlian society.

## The Spiral Runway

A narrow spiral runway connects the temple level to Xaxa's apartments and continues upward to the roof and the royal hangars. It was via this runway that Vad Varo, Gor Hajus, and Dar Tarus ascended to steal the Jeddara's flier [012-005]. The priests at the temple directed searching guards to "look quickly to Xaxa's apartments above and to the roof and the hangars" — confirming the runway as the only escape route from the temple level [012-011].

## Events

The palace is the setting for the climax of the novel. Vad Varo and his company gain entry by performing with Hovan Du the white ape, intending to reach Xaxa and restore Valla Dia's consciousness to her rightful body. After their cover is blown, they hide within the head of the Great Tur idol in the adjacent temple, orchestrate a false miracle, and compel the court to accept Valla Dia as their returned Jeddara. The final scenes unfold in Xaxa's apartments, where the real Xaxa (in Valla Dia's body) is taken prisoner and transported back to the [[island-of-thavas]] for the brain-restoration surgery.
