## Overview

Sag Or is a young Phundahlian noble who serves as the favorite courtier of Jeddara [[Xaxa]]. His handsome physical form is not his own — it is the body of [[Dar Tarus]], transferred to him through [[Ras Thavas]]'s brain transplantation in a transaction that exemplifies the corruption of the Phundahlian court [011-002]. As the romantic rival who possesses both Dar Tarus's body and the affections of the woman Dar Tarus loves, Sag Or represents everything the hero seeks to reclaim.

## Biography

Sag Or rose to prominence in the court of [[Phundahl]] as Xaxa's favored companion. When the aging Jeddara sought a beautiful new body, she purchased Valla Dia's form from Ras Thavas — and simultaneously arranged for Sag Or to receive the handsome body of Dar Tarus, who had been condemned to death for his loyalty to Valla Dia [011-002].

### In Xaxa's Court

When [[Vad Varo]]'s company arrived at the Phundahlian court disguised as traveling showmen, Sag Or was present in the throne room. It was Sag Or whom Xaxa commanded to disarm the strangers — and it was at that moment that Vad Varo, [[Dar Tarus]], [[Gor Hajus]], and [[Hovan Du]] all recognized the name of the man who had wronged their companion [011-003]. Sag Or approached to confiscate their weapons, unaware that he stood face to face with the very men whose bodies and lives he had stolen.

### Capture by Hovan Du

When the ruse collapsed and Hovan Du revealed his human intelligence, the great white ape singled out Sag Or. Hovan Du "reached forth and seized him, wrenching his weapons from him and holding him in his giant hands" [011-008]. As Hovan Du leaped through the palace windows into the trees, he carried the struggling Sag Or with him — a hostage whose capture would prove crucial to the resolution of the conflict.

Sag Or remained Hovan Du's prisoner throughout the temple intrigue and the company's return to the House of the Dead. In the final resolution, Vad Varo restored Sag Or to his original body and returned him — along with Xaxa — to Phundahl [013-008].

## Relationships

Sag Or's primary relationship is with Xaxa, the Jeddara whose favor he enjoys. He is hated by Dar Tarus, whose body he inhabits and whose beloved Valla Dia he sought to claim. The court respects him for his proximity to power rather than for any personal merit. His dynamic with Hovan Du — captor and captive — is one of the more darkly comic threads in the novel's final act.

## Significance

Sag Or functions primarily as a symbol of the injustice that Vad Varo's quest seeks to rectify. He is the living evidence of Ras Thavas's amoral commerce in human bodies: a shallow courtier who purchased physical perfection at the expense of an honorable man. Unlike Xaxa, who at least possesses a ruthless intelligence, Sag Or appears as a creature of pure privilege — handsome, favored, and utterly undeserving of the body he wears. His capture by the physically monstrous but intellectually human Hovan Du provides an ironic commentary on the relationship between appearance and worth.
