## Overview

The Tower of Thavas is the isolated island facility where [[Ras Thavas]], the Master Mind of Mars, conducts his surgical experiments. Situated in the remote reaches of the [[Great Toonolian Marshes]], the tower serves as both a fortress of medical science and a prison for those whose bodies have been traded by its master [001-005]. It is at this location that [[Vad Varo]] first arrives on [[Barsoom]], learns the art of brain transplantation, and assembles the company of allies whose quest drives the novel's plot.

> "This tower," said Ras Thavas, "was built some twenty-three thousand years ago by an ancestor of mine who was driven from Toonol by the reigning Jeddak of the time. Here, and upon other islands, he gathered a considerable following, dominated the surrounding marshes and defended himself successfully for hundreds of years. While my family has been permitted to return to Toonol since, this has been their home; to which, one by one, have been added the various buildings which you see about the tower, each floor of which connects with the adjacent building from the roof to the lowest pits beneath the ground." [007-001]

## Description

The tower is described as a structure "in some respects resembling more closely a feudal castle of Europe" than any familiar Barsoomian architecture -- ornately carved, of irregular design, with a broken roofline that almost suggests a ruin yet remains harmonious and beautiful [001-005]. It rises from a small island in the Great Toonolian Marshes, its outer walls forming a high-walled enclosure within which grow "a number of trees and shrubs, all weirdly strange and all, or almost all, profusely flowering" [001-005].

The grounds feature walks of colored pebbles among which scintillate what appear to be rare gems. The building itself is a sprawling complex of interconnected chambers: operating theaters with rows of tables bearing preserved bodies, storage vaults for the living dead, private laboratories, and the apartments of Ras Thavas and his staff [001-006].

> The facade presented to my view was ornately carved and of most irregular design, the roof line being so broken as to almost suggest a ruin, and yet the whole seemed harmonious and not without beauty. Within the enclosure grew a number of trees and shrubs, all weirdly strange and all, or almost all, profusely flowering. About them wound walks of colored pebbles among which scintillated what appeared to be rare and beautiful gems, so lovely were the strange, unearthly rays that leaped and played in the sunshine. [001-001]

> About me spread the first Barsoomian landscape of any extent upon which my eyes had yet rested during the long months that I had spent upon the Red Planet. For almost an Earthly year I had been immured within the grim walls of Ras Thavas' bloody laboratory, until, such creatures of habit are we, the weird life there had grown to seem quite natural and ordinary; but with this first glimpse of open country there surged up within me an urge for freedom, for space, for room to move about, such as I knew would not be long denied. [007-001]

The tower's most notable architectural feature is its landing stage on the roof -- a hangar from which fliers depart and arrive. Ras Thavas keeps three fliers at the tower, including the *Pinsar* and the vessel that Vad Varo's company eventually steals for their escape [007-001]. The tower itself, of immense age, connects at every level to the various buildings that have been added around it over millennia, and its internal runway descends far underground to the pits and vaults where bodies are preserved [007-001].

## Events

### Arrival of Vad Varo

Ulysses Paxton materialized on the crimson sward within the tower's enclosure, naked and disoriented after his trans-space journey from Earth [001-001]. His first act on Barsoom was to defend the nearly blind Ras Thavas from a homicidal attacker wielding a club -- an act of courage that earned him the position of surgical assistant and the Barsoomian name Vad Varo [001-006].

### The Compact

It was in the vaults of the tower that Vad Varo discovered [[Valla Dia]], her beautiful brain trapped in the withered body of an ancient crone. Their conversations in these subterranean chambers led to the Compact -- Vad Varo's sworn oath to restore her brain to her own body [003-004].

> "It may give this horrid Ras Thavas some new ideas for his torture chamber -- some new scheme for coining money from the suffering of his victims," she said, her harsh voice saddened. [003-004]

> "Death, only, renders hope futile." [004-001]

### Ras Thavas's Brain Transplant

It was in the tower's laboratory that Vad Varo performed his most critical test -- transferring the brain of the thousand-year-old Ras Thavas into a new, youthful body, fulfilling the old surgeon's plan for a form of immortality [005-001].

> "My laboratory is filled with wonderful bodies, young and complete with potential strength and health. I have but to select one of these and have my skilled assistant transfer my brain from this old carcass to the new one." [004-001]

### Resurrection and Escape

Vad Varo secretly revived [[Dar Tarus]] and [[Gor Hajus]] in the tower's operating theaters, repairing their damaged hearts and restoring them to life. Along with [[Hovan Du]], the white ape with a human brain, they formed a company and stole one of Ras Thavas's fliers, escaping the island under cover of night [007-001].

> Silently we passed upward along the runway and out upon the roof. Both moons were winging low through the heavens and the scene was almost as light as day. If anyone was about discovery was certain. We hastened towards the hangar and were soon within it, where, for a moment at least, I breathed far more easily than I had beneath those two brilliant moons upon the exposed roof. [007-001]

### Return

The novel's final act brings the company back to the tower, where Vad Varo performs the critical surgeries that restore Valla Dia's brain to her body and return Xaxa and Sag Or to their original forms [013-007]. The tower, which had been the site of the original crime, becomes the site of its rectification.

> Cluros had long since set and Thuria was streaming towards the horizon as we stopped our motor and drifted without lights a mile or two south of the tower while we waited impatiently for Thuria to leave the heavens to darkness and the world to us. To the northwest the lights of Toonol shone plainly against the dark background of the windows of the great laboratory of Ras Thavas, but the tower itself was dark from plinth to pinnacle. [013-007]

## Inhabitants

The tower is the domain of Ras Thavas, who maintains a staff of slaves and surgical assistants. Its vaults house scores of preserved bodies -- patients awaiting surgery, victims of failed experiments, and bodies held in suspended animation against future use [001-008].

> Ras Thavas himself was as remarkable as the things he accomplished. He was never intentionally cruel; he was not, I am sure, intentionally wicked. He was guilty of the most diabolical cruelties and the basest of crimes; yet in the next moment he might perform a deed that if duplicated upon Earth would have raised him to the highest pinnacle of man's esteem. [002-001]

Notable residents during the novel's events include Vad Varo (assistant), Valla Dia (patient/prisoner), and briefly Dar Tarus, Gor Hajus, and Hovan Du (revived subjects).

## Island and Defenses

The tower occupies a rocky island of roughly a hundred acres in the vast, treacherous Great Toonolian Marshes. Beyond its walls lies a sparse forest interspersed with jungle growth, and beyond that, an oozy marsh with narrow watercourses and scattered open-water lakes. The island is guarded by great white apes of Barsoom, which Ras Thavas deliberately maintains as a deterrent against intruders and would-be deserters [007-001].

> "They serve two purposes," explained Ras Thavas. "They discourage those who might otherwise creep upon me by night from the city of Toonol, where I am not without many good enemies, and they prevent desertion upon the part of my slaves and assistants." [007-001]
