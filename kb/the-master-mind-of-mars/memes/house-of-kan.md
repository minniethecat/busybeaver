## Overview

The House of Kan is one of the principal noble dynasties of [[Toonol]], closely connected to the ruling family through Prince Mu Tel, nephew of Jeddak [[Vobis Kan]]. The House distinguishes itself in the narrative by defying the Jeddak's authority: Mu Tel shelters the fugitive assassin [[Gor Hajus]] and his companions, providing them with sanctuary, resources, and safe passage at considerable political risk [008-022]. The House's gardens and palace serve as the setting for the critical transition between the escape from Ras Thavas's [[island-of-thavas|island]] and the expedition to [[Phundahl]].

## Description

The House of Kan occupies a substantial estate within Toonol, featuring extensive gardens of scarlet sward bordered by heavy shrubbery — vegetation dense enough to conceal fugitives from aerial patrols [008-023]. The estate is sufficiently secluded that its gardens provide a safe landing zone for the party's improvised descent from the night sky. The grounds connect to the [[palace-of-mu-tel]] via hidden corridors, secret doors, and narrow passageways that prove essential during the escape from the *calot* and the air patrol [008-028].

Within Toonolian society, the House of Kan commands significant respect tinged with political tension. Mu Tel's growing popularity makes him an object of suspicion to his uncle Vobis Kan, who "had long been jealous of his nephew's growing popularity and fearful that he harbored aspirations for the throne" [009-008].

The House's wealth is evident in its lavish hospitality: Mu Tel hosts elaborate meals "for which the princes of Barsoom are justly famous," featuring rare vintages and luxurious surroundings [009-008].

## Events

### Sanctuary for Fugitives

When Gor Hajus, newly escaped from Ras Thavas and wanted for the murder of a patrol warrior, arrives at the estate, Mu Tel does not turn him away. Instead, the prince receives Vad Varo, Dar Tarus, and Hovan Du as honored guests. Gor Hajus announces the party as "Friends of Mu Tel, Prince of the House of Kan" — a password that carries weight even with the city patrol [008-022].

### Political Defiance

By sheltering Gor Hajus, Mu Tel knowingly defies his uncle's authority. After the night's events, Mu Tel orders that "all traces of the dead patrol be removed before dawn lest their discovery bring upon him the further suspicion of his uncle" [009-007]. This act of covert defiance establishes Mu Tel as a figure willing to protect fugitives from the Jeddak's justice — a position that would prove fateful when the Jeddak later seizes Ras Thavas's island in retaliation [014-012].

## Inhabitants

The House is led by Mu Tel, Prince of the House of Kan. As a prince of the royal blood and nephew to Vobis Kan, Mu Tel maintains a princely household complete with officers, slaves, and retainers. His intellectual curiosity — manifested in his fascination with Earth and his sophisticated astronomical instruments — marks him as a man of learning unusual among the martially-oriented nobility of Toonol.
