## Overview

The Great Toonolian Marshes form one of [[Barsoom]]'s most formidable geographical barriers — a vast wetland extending nearly eighteen hundred Earth miles east to west, reaching widths of up to three hundred miles in places [007-015]. Bounded by [[Phundahl]] at the western end and [[Toonol]] at the east, the marshes serve as a natural fortress isolating these two inhospitable kingdoms from the rest of Barsoom. The region plays a recurring role in the novel: it is the environment surrounding Ras Thavas's [[island-of-thavas|island]], the terrain traversed during the heroes' escape, and the homeland of the provincials who gather at Phundahl's gates.

## Description

The marshes are a labyrinth of oozy ground, narrow watercourses, and small open lakes — the largest scarcely two acres in extent — dotted with rocky islands rising a dozen feet or more above the general level [007-014]. These islands support sparse forests and patches of dense jungle growth, providing precarious footholds in an otherwise impassable landscape.

The region is famously inhospitable. It is "frequented by fierce beasts" and offers no landing places for fliers, rendering conventional travel nearly impossible [007-015]. The marshes are infested with the great white apes of Barsoom — creatures so savage that even the *banth*, the Barsoomian lion, hesitates to cross their path [007-017]. This combination of terrain and fauna has made the marshes a de facto defensive barrier: "Little is known about them in other portions of Barsoom" precisely because of their inaccessibility [007-015].

Despite the harshness, the marshes sustain life. The jungle grasses are "lush and gorgeous," and the islands yield fruits and nuts that Hovan Du, drawing on the simian portion of his brain, identifies as safe for human consumption [013-006]. Freshwater springs bubble from some islands, providing vital resources for travelers.

## Events

### The Marshes as Prison Wall

The marshes surround Ras Thavas's island laboratory, serving as a natural deterrent against escape. [[Valla Dia]] warns Vad Varo that even if he could leave the island, he would "but die uselessly within the first dozen *haads*" amid the marshes' wild hordes, savage beasts, and warlike cities [003-004].

### Refuge During Flight

During the return flight to the Tower of Thavas, the party hides among the marsh islands just before dawn: "we dropped to one of the numerous small islands that dot the Great Toonolian Marshes and nosing the ship between the boles of great trees we came to rest upon the surface of the ground" [013-006]. Here they rest, eat, and drink before resuming their mission.

### Provincial Homeland

The islands of the western marshes owe allegiance to Phundahl's queen, [[Xaxa]], and it is from these provinces that many of the curiosity-seekers at the palace gates originate — "visitors from the islands of that part of the Great Toonolian Marshes which owes allegiance to Phundahl's queen" [010-004].

## Inhabitants

The marshes are home to great white apes, *banths*, *calots*, and other dangerous Barsoomian fauna. Human habitation is limited to the scattered island communities owing allegiance to either Phundahl or Toonol. These provincials are regarded as unsophisticated by city-dwellers, yet their hardy existence in the marsh environment speaks to human adaptability on Barsoom.
