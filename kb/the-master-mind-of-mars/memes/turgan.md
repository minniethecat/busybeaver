## Overview

Turgan is the holy book of the Phundahlian religion, described by its adherents as "the great book written by Tur himself" [010-001]. It functions not merely as a religious scripture but as the exclusive source of all permitted knowledge in Phundahl — the text against which every claim to truth is measured.

## Description

According to the religion of Tur, the god resides upon the sun and created Barsoom one hundred thousand years ago, fashioning the planet and all life upon it [010-001]. Turgan is said to contain the complete and inerrant account of this creation, authored directly by the deity. Dar Tarus, a native of Phundahl, describes it with pride: "every word and work of it is susceptible of proof through the pages of Turgan" [010-001].

The scope of Turgan's authority extends far beyond spiritual matters. It serves as the sole permitted textbook on geography, history, biology, and cosmology [010-002]. Phundahlians reject any knowledge not contained within its pages. As the narrator Vad Varo observes, "Turgan is their only text book — if it is not in Turgan it is a wicked lie" [010-002].

Turgan teaches several doctrines that directly conflict with observable reality and scientific knowledge. It asserts that Barsoom is flat, and Phundahlians "shut their minds to every proof to the contrary," fearing to travel far lest they fall off the edge of the world [010-002]. It denies the existence of other worlds, forbidding the use of telescopes as heretical. It also teaches that Tur personally creates every living thing, and Phundahlians deny the existence of biological reproduction [010-002].

The book's authority is enforced through a comprehensive system of suppression. The teaching of any history predating Tur's creation of Barsoom — despite the existence of "well authenticated written history that reaches back more than one hundred thousand years" — is forbidden [010-002]. Scientific research in biology is prohibited, and no geography outside Turgan may be taught.

## Significance

Turgan represents a central thematic element in *The Master Mind of Mars*: the conflict between dogmatic ignorance and empirical truth. The Phundahlian reliance on Turgan as the sole authority produces what Vad Varo judges as "the least advanced in civilization of any of the red nations upon Barsoom" [010-002]. The book's absolute control over knowledge mirrors the political absolutism of the Jeddara Xaxa, making Phundahl a society where both intellectual and political tyranny reinforce each other. Vad Varo's manipulation of the Great Tur idol — using scientific understanding of mechanics to simulate divine intervention — represents the triumph of genuine knowledge over the false certainties enshrined in Turgan [012-004].
