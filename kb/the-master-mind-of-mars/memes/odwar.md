## Overview

An odwar is a senior Barsoomian military officer, analogous to a general in Earthly military hierarchies. The rank carries command authority over the Jeddara's Guard — the elite protective force of the ruling Jeddara — and odwars occupy a position of considerable trust and proximity to the throne [013-001].

## Description

The odwar of the Guard serves directly under the Jeddara's command, receiving orders that often involve sensitive palace operations. When Valla Dia, posing as Xaxa, summoned the odwar of the Guard, she entrusted him with the order to clear the entire palace from the temple level to the roof — a sweeping command that demonstrated the odwar's executive authority over palace security and personnel [013-001].

The odwar's responsibilities extend beyond routine guard duties to encompass emergency actions on behalf of the throne. The rank holder must be capable of executing complex orders with speed and discretion, as when the odwar was commanded to evacuate every living being from multiple palace levels "an hour before the evening meal" — a logistical challenge requiring coordination across hundreds of personnel [013-001].

The odwar understood the gravity of the Jeddara's commands and, as the narrative notes, "trembled a trifle" at receiving them, for all in the palace were in great fear of the Jeddara Xaxa [013-001]. This fear-based obedience underscores the odwar's role as an instrument of absolute Jeddara authority rather than an independent military commander.

## Significance

The odwar rank represents the highest operational tier of Barsoomian military hierarchy directly attached to the ruling household. Unlike the [[dwar]] (captain), who commands individual ships or guard units, the odwar exercises broader authority over entire palace security operations and acts as the Jeddara's direct military executive. The rank illustrates how Barsoomian political power is enforced through a stratified military chain of command that places high-ranking officers in positions of both privilege and perilous proximity to the throne.
