## Overview

Jal Had, Prince of [[Amhor]], is a secondary antagonist whose actions before the events of the novel create the circumstances that drive the main plot. His invasion of [[Duhor]] and attempt to capture Princess [[Valla Dia]] forced her into hiding, which ultimately led to her capture and the theft of her body by [[Xaxa]].

## The Attack on Duhor

Hearing tales of the great beauty of Valla Dia, the only daughter of [[Kor San]], Jeddak of Duhor, Jal Had traveled in disguise to the city to see her for himself. Upon confirming her beauty, he determined to possess her [003-003]. Returning to Amhor, he sent ambassadors to sue for her hand in marriage.

Kor San, who had no son, had planned to wed his daughter to one of his own Jeds so that a grandson bearing the blood of Kor San might rule Duhor. Jal Had's offer was therefore declined — a rejection that "so incensed the Amhorian that he equipped a great fleet and set forth to conquer Duhor and take by force that which he could not win by honorable methods" [003-003].

Jal Had's timing was impeccable. Duhor was at war with [[Helium]] and nearly all its forces were far afield in the south. Only a small defensive army remained. Duhor fell swiftly, and while his troops looted the city, Jal Had led a picked force to sack the palace of the Jeddak and search for the princess [003-003].

## Aftermath

When Jal Had could not find Valla Dia — she had disguised herself to escape — he ordered that every woman in the palace be seized and taken to Amhor as hostages until the princess should be delivered to him in marriage [003-004]. Among those taken was the real Valla Dia, now traveling under a false identity. The Amhorian war ship carrying the hostages was intercepted en route, and Valla Dia fell into the hands of agents of Xaxa, who brought her to [[Ras Thavas]] for the brain transplant that would give the Jeddara of [[Phundahl]] a beautiful new body.
