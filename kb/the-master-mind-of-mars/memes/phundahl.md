## Overview

Phundahl is a major city-state on [[Barsoom]] (Mars), notable as the domain of [[Xaxa]], the novel's primary antagonist. It is portrayed as a society governed by hereditary monarchy and crippling sentimentality, where the populace willingly endures oppression out of loyalty to a dynasty. [[Ras Thavas]] holds the Phundahlians in particular contempt for their irrational devotion to their rulers. The city stands at the western end of the [[Great Toonolian Marshes]], commanding the approaches through that vast and inhospitable region, and maintaining its independence through inaccessibility and savage aloofness [006-012].

## Description

Phundahl is located within flying distance of [[Toonol]], where Ras Thavas maintains his surgical facility at the [[Tower of Thavas]]. The city is ruled from a [[Palace of Xaxa|palace]] that houses the Jeddara and her retinue of female attendants and armed warriors. Xaxa arrived at the [[House of the Dead]] accompanied by a score of women and armed guards, attesting to both her wealth and the militarized nature of her rule [001-004].

Ras Thavas's assessment of Phundahlian society is scathing. He characterizes its people as "egregious sentimentalists, filled with crass stupidities and superstitions, slaves to every variety of brain-withering conceit" [002-003]. He mocks their willingness to fight and die for Xaxa, whom he describes as an ignorant and cruel tyrant, simply "because her father was Jeddak of Phundahl and his father before him and so on back into antiquity" [002-003].

The political structure of Phundahl appears to be a hereditary monarchy where succession passes through the paternal line. Xaxa inherited the throne from her father, continuing an unbroken dynastic chain. The Phundahlians are taxed heavily under her rule — "until they can scarce stagger beneath their burden" — yet they remain loyal, a phenomenon Ras Thavas attributes to the manipulation of sentiment by wicked rulers [002-003].

### The Walls and Defenses

Phundahl was a walled city, its defenses known in intimate detail to [[Dar Tarus]], a native son who had served both in the [[Jeddara]]'s Guard and in Phundahl's tiny navy. The city wall incorporated low buildings constructed against its inner face, their roofs providing the landing point for [[Vad Varo]]'s party when they arrived secretly aboard a captured [[Toonol|Toonolian]] flier [010-001].

> The following night the Toonolian crew set us down just inside the wall of the city of Phundahl, following the directions of Dar Tarus who was a native of the city, had been a warrior of the Jeddara's Guard and, prior to that, seen service in Phundahl's tiny navy. That he was familiar with every detail of Phundahl's defenses and her systems of patrols was evidenced by the fact that we landed without detection and that the Toonolian ship rose and departed apparently unnoticed. [010-001]

## The Religion of Tur

Phundahl's dominant faith centers on the worship of [[Tur]], a deity whose tenets are recorded in the sacred book [[Turgan]]. The city's great [[Temple of Tur]] was a place of constant activity, drawing streams of worshipers day and night, and serving as the stage for the most dramatic events of the protagonists' mission [010-012].

### Temple Worship and Rituals

The religious practices of Phundahl were elaborate and highly ritualized. Priests cloaked entirely in white mantles received offerings at the temple entrance, changing gold into smaller denominations while performing blessings that involved anointing worshipers' noses with water from a communal bowl [010-013].

> Never have I seen such a gorgeous display of wealth and lavish ornamentation as confronted my eyes in this, the first of the temples of Tur that it was my fortune to behold. [010-013]

Within the temple, the faithful moved from idol to idol performing prescribed rituals. At one figure, worshipers lay prone and bumped their heads seven times against the floor. At another, they hung by their knees from horizontal bars, chanting "bibble-babble-blup." At yet another they crawled seven times around the pedestal, while elsewhere they rolled about the floor repeating "Tur is Tur; Tur is Tur; Tur is Tur" [010-014].

### The Doctrine of Tur

The theology of Phundahl, as explained by Dar Tarus, held that Tur created [[Barsoom]] one hundred thousand years ago from his home upon the sun and hurled it into space. The religion was, in Dar Tarus's words, "simple, natural, scientific and every word and work of it is susceptible of proof through the pages of Turgan" [010-016]. Yet it contained profound contradictions.

> Turgan taught them that Barsoom is flat and they shut their minds to every proof to the contrary. They would not leave Phundahl far for fear of falling off the edge of the world; they would not permit the development of aeronautics because should one of their ships circumnavigate Barsoom it would be a wicked sacrilege in the eyes of Tur who made Barsoom flat. [010-018]

The Phundahlians denied the natural facts of reproduction, insisting to the death that even what they witnessed with their own eyes and experienced with their own bodies in the bringing forth of their young never transpired. They forbade telescopes, teaching that Barsoom was the only world, and permitted no history older than Tur's creation nor any geography beyond [[Turgan]]. As Vad Varo concluded:

> Much of all this and a great deal more I gathered from one source or another during my brief stay in Phundahl, whose people are, I believe, the least advanced in civilization of any of the red nations upon Barsoom. Giving, as they do, all their best thought to religious matters, they have become ignorant, bigoted and narrow, going as far to one extreme as the Toonolians do to the other. [010-019]

### The Great Tur

At the heart of the [[Temple of Tur]] stood the colossus known as the Great Tur, a hollow statue some forty feet in height, equipped with movable eyes operated by periscopic controls, a speaking-tube connected to an amplifier beneath the tongue, and a secret entrance concealed within its jeweled harness [011-001]. This ancient mechanism, unknown to the current generation of priests, had been used by long-dead high priests to simulate the voice and gaze of their god. Within the base of the idol, Vad Varo and [[Gor Hajus]] discovered the skeleton of a high priest who had fallen from the inner platform, carrying to eternity the secret of the statue's interior [011-007].

## Events

### Xaxa's Body Theft

The central crisis involving Phundahl began when [[Xaxa]], the aging Jeddara, traveled to the [[House of the Dead]] to purchase a new body from [[Ras Thavas]]. The surgeon transplanted Xaxa's brain into the stolen body of [[Valla Dia]], the beautiful Princess of [[Duhor]], while Valla Dia's brain was placed in Xaxa's withered old corpse. Xaxa then returned to Phundahl wearing her stolen youth, her secret known only to Ras Thavas and a select few [001-004].

### Infiltration of Phundahl

[[Vad Varo]], having fallen in love with the mind of Valla Dia, assembled a peculiar company to infiltrate Phundahl and recover her body. His allies included [[Dar Tarus]], a former warrior of the Jeddara's Guard who had been assassinated at Xaxa's order; [[Gor Hajus]], the famed Assassin of [[Toonol]]; and [[Hovan Du]], a great [[white-ape|white ape]] animated by a divided human brain [007-005].

The party entered Phundahl at night, landing just inside the city wall under Dar Tarus's expert guidance. They found lodging in a district of second-rate shops and eating places, where Hovan Du attracted attention as a performing ape. To gain entrance to the [[Palace of Xaxa]], Gor Hajus presented Hovan Du as a trained beast of remarkable intelligence, capable of reading and writing — a performance that intrigued the officers of the Jeddara's Guard sufficiently to secure an audience [010-023].

### The Temple Confrontation

Once within Xaxa's palace, however, their cover was broken. Ras Thavas had warned the Jeddara of Vad Varo's purpose. Xaxa recognized them and ordered them thrown into the pits. In the ensuing chaos, Hovan Du fought his way to an open window, seizing [[Sag Or]], Xaxa's favorite noble, and escaping into the trees. Vad Varo and Gor Hajus fled through a hidden corridor behind Xaxa's desk, eventually taking refuge inside the hollow Great Tur idol [011-010].

> "You shall fare ill," she cried. "You have seen all of Barsoom that you are destined to see — you have reached the end of your adventure. You think to deceive me, eh? You do not know, perhaps, that I have heard of your infatuation for Valla Dia or that I am fully conversant with the purpose of your visit to Phundahl." Her eyes left me and swept her nobles and her warriors. "To the pits with them!" she cried. [011-018]

From their hiding place within the Great Tur, Vad Varo and Gor Hajus observed the priesthood and the royal court. They learned that Xaxa was feared and hated even by her own priests, who prayed that assassins might succeed where others had failed [011-023].

> "Worse things might befall Phundahl." [011-026]

The crisis reached its peak when Dar Tarus was captured and brought to the temple as a sacrifice before the Great Tur. Xaxa, as High Priestess, prepared to plunge a dagger into his heart, calling upon Tur to speak after a hundred years of silence. At that moment, Vad Varo operated the ancient controls, causing the idol's eyes to move and its voice to thunder through the chamber [012-009].

> "Judgment is mine!" I cried. "Strike not lest ye be struck! To Tur is the sacrifice!" [012-009]

The worshipers, stunned by what they believed was a genuine miracle, fled the temple at Tur's command, leaving Xaxa alone at the altar. Vad Varo and his companions seized the Jeddara and escaped to the roof hangars, where they commandeered a royal flier and departed for the [[Tower of Thavas]] to restore Valla Dia's brain to her own body [012-014].

## Phundahl Under Dar Tarus

### The Restoration

After successfully restoring Valla Dia, Dar Tarus, and Hovan Du to their rightful bodies at the laboratory of Ras Thavas, Vad Varo returned to Phundahl with Xaxa and Sag Or as prisoners. The city needed provisions and fuel for the journey to [[Duhor]], and Vad Varo believed he held the key to Phundahl's resources through his impersonation of Tur [013-005].

### The New Jeddak

In a second dramatic ceremony at the [[Temple of Tur]], Vad Varo again spoke through the Great Tur, this time decreeing that Dar Tarus should become Jeddak of Phundahl and High Priest of Tur. The assembled nobles, still cowed by the earlier miracle, offered no resistance [014-005].

> "Then it is my will," I boomed, "that Xaxa and Sag Or shall give back to those from whom they stole them, the beautiful bodies they now wear; that he from whom Sag Or took this body shall become Jeddak of Phundahl and High Priest of Tur; and that she from whom Xaxa stole her body shall be returned in pomp to her native country. I have spoken. Let any who would revolt against my word speak now or forever hold his peace." [014-010]

When Xaxa attempted to protest, denouncing Dar Tarus as an impostor, she collapsed and died of apoplexy. Her death removed the last obstacle to the succession. Dar Tarus ascended the throne, and the Phundahlians, freed from a century of tyranny under Xaxa's dynasty, accepted their new ruler with evident relief [014-012].

> They were frankly delighted to be rid of Xaxa; and while they could not understand Tur's purpose in elevating a former warrior of the Guard to the throne, yet they were content if it served to relieve them from the wrath of their god, now a very real and terrible god, since the miracles that had been performed in the temple. [014-016]

### The Reign of Dar Tarus

Dar Tarus's reign began with promise. He had [[Hovan Du]] and [[Gor Hajus]] take service with him, ensuring that the Great Tur would never again fall silent — and that he, as high priest, would control the voice of god [014-016]. The former warrior of the Jeddara's Guard was of noble blood, which eased the transition for the hereditary-minded Phundahlians. Vad Varo foresaw great possibilities for his rule [014-016]. [[Ras Thavas]] himself sought audience at the new Jeddak's court, and Dar Tarus commissioned Gor Hajus to lead an army against [[Toonol]] to restore Ras Thavas to his island [014-018].

The succession was soon recognized by [[Helium]] when [[John Carter]], the Warlord of [[Barsoom]], arrived with a fleet that had originally been dispatched to right the wrongs committed by Xaxa. Upon learning of the change in regime, the Warlord greeted Dar Tarus in peace and attended the nuptials of Dar Tarus and [[Kara Vasa]] before escorting Vad Varo and Valla Dia to Duhor [014-019].

## Inhabitants

The population consists of Red Martians governed by hereditary [[Jeddak|Jeddaks]] and Jeddaras. [[Xaxa]] rules as Jeddara, surrounded by a court of female attendants and armed warriors. The common people are characterized as superstitious and tradition-bound, their loyalty to the throne exploited by the ruling class.

The Phundahlians, as a people, represent the extreme of religious devotion on [[Barsoom]], standing in stark contrast to the atheistic [[Toonol|Toonolians]]. Their entire culture is organized around the worship of [[Tur]] and the dictates of the book [[Turgan]]. This deep religiosity has rendered them, in Vad Varo's assessment, the least advanced of all the red nations, having suppressed scientific inquiry, aeronautics, and natural philosophy in favor of rigid dogma [010-019].

### Dar Tarus

[[Dar Tarus]] was a native of Phundahl, a young warrior attached to the court of Xaxa and a member of the Jeddara's Guard. He was assassinated at Xaxa's order so that [[Sag Or]], a noble, could purchase his body from [[Ras Thavas]] and use it to woo a young beauty named [[Kara Vasa]]. Revived by [[Vad Varo]] at the [[Tower of Thavas]], Dar Tarus joined the mission to Phundahl for the chance to avenge himself upon Xaxa and recover both his body and his love. His intimate knowledge of the city's defenses proved essential to the party's success, and he ultimately became Jeddak of Phundahl, reuniting with Kara Vasa through the intervention of [[John Carter]] [007-004].

### Sag Or

[[Sag Or]] was a Phundahlian noble and the favorite of Jeddara Xaxa. He purchased the body of Dar Tarus from Ras Thavas so that he might court Kara Vasa, the woman Dar Tarus loved. During the temple confrontation, he was seized by Hovan Du and carried away from the palace. Following the restoration of bodies, he was returned to his own aged and dissipated form and permitted to live in peace under the new regime [014-013].

### Kara Vasa

[[Kara Vasa]] was the beloved of Dar Tarus. She disappeared from Phundahl shortly after Dar Tarus's assassination, escaping with some of her father's warriors. A slave reported that she had set out for [[Helium]] to seek the protection of [[John Carter]]. It was the Warlord of Barsoom himself who reunited her with Dar Tarus, bringing her aboard the Helium fleet that arrived at Phundahl [014-020].

## Legacy

Phundahl's transformation under Dar Tarus marked a turning point in the religious and political history of the city. For the first time in generations, the Great Tur spoke through a high priest who was also the Jeddak, unifying religious and secular authority. The old regime of Xaxa, characterized by greed, assassination, and the exploitation of her people's sentimentality, gave way to a rule that promised greater justice.

The city's strategic position at the western end of the [[Great Toonolian Marshes]] ensured its continued importance in the affairs of northeastern [[Barsoom]]. The alliance forged during these events between Phundahl, [[Duhor]], and [[Helium]] laid the foundation for a more peaceful region, while the reform of [[Ras Thavas]]'s medical practices under Dar Tarus's patronage held the potential to transform the morbid brain-transplantation trade into a force for genuine healing.
