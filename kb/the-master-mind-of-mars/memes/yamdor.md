## Overview

Yamdor is the personal slave and body servant of [[Ras Thavas]], the master surgeon of [[Toonol]]. As the keeper of the laboratory's internal discipline, his power is legendary among the slaves and attendants — a word from Yamdor to the master could send anyone to the *ersite* slab for eternity. His unusual nature, rumored to be the result of one of Ras Thavas's experiments combining a woman's brain with a man's body, makes him one of the more unsettling figures in the House of the Dead.

## Character

Yamdor is described as "a huge fellow" whose loyalty Ras Thavas secures "through the bestowal of lavish gifts and countless favors." So great is his power that all fear him [004-004]. He is rumored to be "the result of an unnatural experiment which had combined the brain of a woman with the body of a man, and there was much in his actions and mannerisms to justify this general belief" [004-004].

His physical manner is paradoxically gentle: "his touch, when he worked about his master, was soft and light, his movements graceful, his ways gentle." But this gentleness masks a darker nature — "his mind was jealous, vindictive and unforgiving" [004-004].

## Role in the Plot

Yamdor serves as Ras Thavas's enforcer. When the surgeon discovers that Valla Dia has hidden herself, he commands Yamdor to "take the subject from the apartments of Vad Varo and destroy it — let no vestige of body or brain remain" [004-005]. Yamdor obediently sets out with two slaves to carry out the order, but Vad Varo has already moved Valla Dia to safety.

When Vad Varo encounters Yamdor in the corridor, he notices "an expression of fear and suspicion in his eyes" [006-001]. Seizing the initiative, Vad Varo rushes to Ras Thavas and accuses Yamdor of making Valla Dia disappear — a brilliant deflection that throws suspicion onto the body servant and away from himself [006-001].

Yamdor appears again in the final confrontation, discovering Vad Varo and his companions as they attempt their escape. His cry of recognition nearly betrays them, but they manage to flee before he can raise a full alarm [013-002].
