## Overview

The Temple of Tur is the central religious edifice of [[Phundahl]], housing the enormous idol known as the Great Tur and serving as the focal point for public worship of Tur, the supreme deity of Barsoomian religion. Beyond its religious function, the temple becomes the stage for [[Vad Varo]]'s most daring deception — using the hollow interior of the Great Tur idol to impersonate the god and topple the regime of Jeddara [[Xaxa]].

## Description

The temple is described as "a great building of wondrous beauty in and out of which constant streams of people were pouring" [010-027]. [[Dar Tarus]], a native of Phundahl, explains to Vad Varo that "this was a temple of Tur, the god worshiped by the people of Phundahl" [010-027]. The interior displays an astonishing wealth of ornamentation, with gold and jewels adorning every surface, reflecting both the piety and the commercialized nature of Tur worship.

The temple's interior features an enormous unbroken floor without pillars, arranged with carven images resting upon gorgeous pedestals at regular intervals. Some images represent beautiful men and women, others beasts and grotesque creatures [010-027]. The towering idol of the Great Tur dominates the chamber, facing the main entrance across a broad nave lined with lesser idols [012-029]. The chamber is illuminated by a soft white light, with tall windows along two sides and enormous doors providing the main entrance at the far end [012-004].

The Great Tur idol itself is approximately forty feet tall and hollow, accessible through a hidden passage and a door in the wall behind it. Within, a ladder with flat rungs ascends to a circular platform running around the inside of the neck, just below the mouth, ears, and nostrils. The eyes are functional periscopes that can be turned to observe any portion of the temple below [012-029]. A speaking-tube connects to the mouth, allowing a voice spoken within to project outward with resonant authority audible throughout the chamber [012-029].

The temple also features a spiral runway leading from the hidden door behind the idol upward to the roof, where the royal hangars are located [012-067].

## Religious Practice

Dar Tarus enters the temple to "do honor to my god" after a long absence, demonstrating the personal devotion expected of Phundahlians [010-029]. The Toonolian [[Gor Hajus]] views this piety with "an expression of contempt, since the Toonolians are atheists" [010-031].

Worship in the Temple of Tur is highly ritualized and commercial. Upon entering, worshipers pay a gold coin to a white-robed priest who makes passes above their heads and anoints their noses with water while murmuring unintelligible phrases [010-031]. Inside, different stations feature distinct rituals: swinging from overhead bars while chanting meaningless phrases, crawling on hands and knees seven times around pedestals, and standing on their heads repeating "Tur is Tur; Tur is Tur; Tur is Tur" [010-031]. Each station requires a monetary offering, and large chests throughout the temple collect coins from worshippers.

The priests, their entire bodies including heads and faces covered by white cloth mantles, officiate at these stations in shifts of pairs, rotating at intervals. Young priestesses also serve, chanting "meaningless gibberish" before the idols [012-005].

## The Great Deception

In the novel's climactic sequence, Vad Varo and Gor Hajus conceal themselves inside the head of the Great Tur idol. From within, they use the periscope eyes to spy upon the priests and overhear court intrigues [012-005]. When [[Xaxa]] arrives to conduct a sacrifice — with the captured Dar Tarus as the intended victim — Vad Varo seizes his moment.

"The lights flashed on and the people rose, trembling, to their feet. Every eye was bent upon the altar," where instead of a living sacrifice lay only a dust-covered skull [012-057]. Vad Varo then commands through the speaking-tube: "Light the temple!" and, moments later, "The Great Tur is angry with his people" [012-057, 012-059]. The terrified congregation flees, leaving Xaxa alone at the altar.

Vad Varo, Gor Hajus, and the rescued Dar Tarus descend from the idol, retrieve Xaxa, and ascend the spiral runway to the roof and the royal hangars [012-067].

## Role of Xaxa

Xaxa serves as both Jeddara and High Priestess of Tur, wielding absolute political and religious authority over Phundahl [012-057]. She enters the temple in a splendid chariot drawn by two *banths*, held in leash by slaves, and presides over sacrificial ceremonies. It is her authority that Vad Varo subverts when he speaks through the idol, declaring Tur's judgment upon her and commanding the worshipers to abandon the palace.
