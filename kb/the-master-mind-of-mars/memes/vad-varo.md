## Overview

Vad Varo is the Barsoomian identity of Ulysses Paxton, a young American army captain who, after being gravely wounded in the trenches of World War I, is transported to [[Barsoom]] by the same mysterious force that carried John Carter across space [001-001]. Taken in by [[Ras Thavas]] and trained as a surgical assistant, he becomes the driving force behind the quest to restore [[Valla Dia]] to her stolen body. As both an outsider observing Martian society and an active agent reshaping its injustices, Vad Varo serves as the novel's moral compass and action hero.

## Biography

### Arrival on Barsoom

Ulysses Paxton was a captain in the U.S. Army infantry during the First World War. After receiving a promotion to captaincy, he was caught in a shell burst that severed both his legs [001-001]. As he lay dying in a shell hole in France, he fixed his eyes on Mars and willed himself across the void — awakening naked on the crimson sward of Ras Thavas's island, whole and uninjured [001-005].

His first act on Barsoom was defending the nearly blind Ras Thavas from a homicidal attacker wielding a club. Impressed by the Earthman's agility, Ras Thavas took him as his assistant and gave him the Barsoomian name Vad Varo, declaring his Earth name "meaningless and impractical" [002-001].

### Apprenticeship

Ras Thavas trained Vad Varo in the arts of blood replacement, embalming, and brain transplantation, making him the only being on Barsoom trusted with the master surgeon's closely guarded secrets. Explaining his reasons, Ras Thavas articulated the cold philosophy that governed his entire life:

> "Sentimentalists have words: love, loyalty, friendship, enmity, jealousy, hate, a thousand others; a waste of words — one word defines them all: self-interest. All men of intelligence realize this. They analyze an individual and by his predilections and his needs they classify him as friend or foe, leaving to the weak-minded idiots who like to be deceived the drooling drivel of sentiment." [002-003]

Vad Varo's response was telling: "I smiled as I buckled my weapons to my harness, but I held my peace. Nothing could be gained by arguing with the man" [002-004]. Even as an apprentice, the Earthman quietly rejected his master's nihilism.

### The Compact

While exploring Ras Thavas's vault of preserved bodies, Vad Varo encountered Valla Dia — a beautiful young woman whose brain had been transferred into the withered body of an ancient crone so that Xaxa, Jeddara of Phundahl, could steal her youthful form [003-004]. Moved by her plight and falling in love with her, Vad Varo formed a compact with Valla Dia: he would find a way to restore her brain to her own body and win her freedom [004-003].

Yet his motives were complicated by the physical reality of Valla Dia's situation. In a passage of painful honesty, Vad Varo wrestled with his own feelings:

> Gradually, as I had come to know her through the slow revealment of the wondrous beauty of her mind and soul, there had crept into my heart a knowledge of my love; and yet, explain it I cannot, I could not speak the words to that frightful old hag. I had seen the gorgeous mundane tabernacle that had housed the equally gorgeous spirit of the real Valla Dia — that I could love; her heart and soul and mind I could love; but I could not love the body of Xaxa. [004-006]

When Valla Dia pressed him on why he would risk everything, he gave his answer simply and directly:

> "To right the wrong that has been done you, Valla Dia," I said. [004-007]

### The Confrontation with Ras Thavas

When the moment came to perform the life-saving brain transfer that would give Ras Thavas a new young body, Vad Varo seized his leverage. With his finger on the button controlling the anesthetic pump, he demanded a price:

> "Regardless of your teaching I am afraid that I am still somewhat of a sentimentalist. I crave the redressing of wrongs. I crave friendship and love. The price you offer is not enough. Are you willing to pay more that this operation may be successfully concluded?" [005-003]

He demanded that Ras Thavas promise to restore Valla Dia's body — a promise the surgeon gave, though immediately scheming to betray it [005-004].

### The Quest

To accomplish this monumental task, Vad Varo secretly revived two of Ras Thavas's preserved subjects: [[Dar Tarus]], a Phundahlian warrior, and [[Gor Hajus]], the legendary Assassin of [[Toonol]] [007-001]. Together with [[Hovan Du]], the white ape with a human brain, they stole a flier and embarked on a dangerous journey across the Toonolian Marshes to Phundahl.

As they made their escape, Vad Varo called out a farewell that perfectly captured his blend of defiance and formal courtesy:

> "Goodbye, Ras Thavas! It is I, Vad Varo, going out into a strange world to see what it is like. I shall return. The spirits of your ancestors be with you until then." [007-007]

When a patrol boat from Toonol intercepted their stolen flier and threatened to board and search, Vad Varo's response was instant and unhesitating. He slipped his short-sword into Gor Hajus's hand and said one word:

> "Fight!" [008-005]

Vad Varo's Earthly muscles — granting him superior agility in Barsoom's lower gravity — proved decisive in combat, but his greatest assets were strategic thinking and moral conviction. He orchestrated a complex infiltration of Xaxa's palace, concealed himself inside the head of the Great Tur idol to stage a "divine intervention" [012-005], and ultimately succeeded in restoring Valla Dia's brain to her own body through the surgical skills he had learned from Ras Thavas [013-007].

### The Moment of Triumph

After completing the delicate surgery that restored Valla Dia's brain to her own beautiful body, Vad Varo watched her awaken. When she looked into a mirror and realized what had been accomplished, she put her arms around his neck, called him "my chieftain," and in that moment the entire mad quest crystallized into its true meaning:

> For those two words I had risked my life and faced unknown dangers, and gladly would I risk my life again for that same reward and always, forever. [013-011]

## Relationships

Vad Varo forms a master-apprentice bond with Ras Thavas, who respects his intelligence while remaining suspicious of his motives. His love for Valla Dia drives the entire narrative. With Dar Tarus and Gor Hajus, he builds a warrior's brotherhood founded on mutual loyalty. He earns the trust of [[Mu Tel]], Prince of the House of Kan, and commands the fierce devotion of Hovan Du.

## Significance

Vad Varo stands in the tradition of Burroughs's Earthman-on-Mars protagonists, following the template established by John Carter. Like Carter, he arrives on Barsoom through a metaphysical transport, possesses superior physical abilities due to Earthly musculature, and becomes embroiled in the rescue of a beautiful Martian princess. However, Vad Varo distinguishes himself through his intellectual and surgical capabilities — he does not merely fight his way to victory but uses the very technology of the antagonist (brain transplantation) to undo the injustices it created [005-005]. He represents the synthesis of action and intellect, warrior and healer.
