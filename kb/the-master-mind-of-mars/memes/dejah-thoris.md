## Overview

Dejah Thoris is the Princess of [[Helium]] and the wife of [[John Carter]], the Warlord of [[Barsoom]]. Though she does not appear directly in the events of the novel, she is referenced as one of the most famous figures on Mars — a symbol of beauty, nobility, and the world of Helium that stands in contrast to the darker cities of [[Toonol]] and [[Phundahl]].

## Cultural Presence

Ulysses Paxton first encountered Dejah Thoris through the pages of *A Princess of Mars*, the same novel that introduced him to John Carter. He writes of dreaming about "John Carter, of Dejah Thoris, of Tars Tarkas and of Woola as if they had been entities of my own experience" [000-003]. This literary transmission — from Edgar Rice Burroughs's manuscript to the mind of a young officer — established Dejah Thoris as an archetype of Martian beauty and courage before Paxton ever set foot on the Red Planet.

After the events of the novel, when Paxton has married [[Valla Dia]] and settled in Helium, he writes that he sees daily "that peerless creature, Dejah Thoris, Princess of Mars." Yet even in her presence, he adds that "though to me, beautiful as she is, there is another even more beautiful — Valla Dia, Princess of Duhor — Mrs. Ulysses Paxton" [014-011]. This comparison, placing his wife above the legendary beauty of Barsoom, is the highest compliment a man of two worlds could pay.

## Significance

Dejah Thoris represents the idealized Barsoom that Paxton had dreamed of — a world of honor, beauty, and heroism. Her presence in the epilogue signals that the narrator has not merely survived his adventures but has been welcomed into the highest circles of Martian society. That she, along with her son Cathoris and other legendary figures, is part of Vad Varo's daily life is the ultimate validation of his journey.
