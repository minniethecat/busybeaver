## Toonolian

**Toonolian** refers to the people, culture, and philosophical outlook of the Barsoomian city-state of Toonol. Toonolians possess a distinctive worldview that sets them apart from other Martian peoples, characterized by a pragmatic, science-worshipping ethos and a professed contempt for sentiment.

### Philosophy and Beliefs

Mu Tel, a Toonolian noble, explained to the narrator that Toonolians "believe that no good deed was ever performed except for a selfish motive; they have no god and no religion" (009-066). Unlike most educated Barsoomians who believe the omnipotent being created the Tree of Life, Toonolians deny any creator deity. They hold "that the only sin is failure—success, however achieved, is meritorious" (009-066).

Paradoxically, despite their cynicism, Toonolians "never break their given word" (009-066). Mu Tel explained this as a calculated practice — they avoid binding themselves to loyalty for more than "a definitely prescribed period" to circumvent this "degrading weakness" (009-066).

### The Paradox of Toonolian Character

The narrator observed that much of the Toonolians' "flaunted contempt of the finer sensibilities was specious" (009-070). Generations of cultural conditioning had "atrophied those characteristics of heart and soul which the noblest among us so highly esteem" — friendship ties were lax and even parent-child bonds carried "no high sense of responsibility or love" (009-070). Yet beneath this façade, individuals like Gor Hajus proved "essentially a man of sentiment" who would have killed anyone accusing him of it.

The narrator concluded that Toonolians "denied deity, and in the same breath worshiped the fetish of science that they had permitted to obsess them quite as harmfully as do religious fanatics accept the unreasoning rule of their imaginary gods" (009-070). With "all their vaunted knowledge, they were unintelligent because unbalanced" (009-070).

### Technology and Craft

Toonolian fliers are noted as serviceable but "not overly fast," being "far outstripped in speed by the fliers of such nations as Helium and Ptarth who have for ages devoted themselves to the perfection of their navies" (009-102). A Toonolian craft proves adequate for the narrator's rescue mission across the marshes to Phundahl.

### The Toonolian Marshes

Toonol lies near extensive marshlands. The narrator describes flying "across desolate leagues of marsh" westward towards Phundahl (009-070). This geography contributes to Toonol's relative isolation and distinctive character among Barsoomian city-states.
