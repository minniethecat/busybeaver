## Overview

Gor Hajus is a legendary figure on [[Barsoom]] — the assassin of [[Toonol]] whose name commands both fear and respect. Though his calling is death, he adheres to an unbreakable code of honor: he never kills a woman or a good man, and he never strikes from behind [004-004]. His killings are always the result of fair fights. Betrayed by Vobis Kan, the Jeddak of Toonol, Gor Hajus is executed and his body sold to [[Ras Thavas]] — setting in motion the events that make him one of [[Vad Varo]]'s most trusted allies.

## Biography

Gor Hajus elevated the profession of assassin to unprecedented esteem among his countrymen through his strict personal code. He was famous not only for his lethal skill but for his unwavering loyalty to his friends — a loyalty that ultimately caused his downfall. When Vobis Kan, Jeddak of Toonol, ordered him to assassinate a man who had once befriended him in some slight degree, Gor Hajus refused [004-004]. Vobis Kan, now suspecting that Gor Hajus had *him* marked for death, ordered the assassin's arrest and execution.

> Ras Thavas, himself a Toonolian, had given me something of the history of this man whose grim calling is not without honor upon Barsoom, and which Gor Hajus had raised still higher in the esteem of his countrymen through the fact that he never struck down a woman or a good man and that he never struck from behind. His killings were always the results of fair fights in which the victim had every opportunity to defend himself and slay his attacker; and he was famous for his loyalty to his friends. In fact this very loyalty had been a contributing factor in his downfall which had brought him to one of Ras Thavas' *ersite* slabs some years since, for he had earned the enmity of Vobis Kan, Jeddak of Toonol, through his refusal to assassinate a man who once had befriended Gor Hajus in some slight degree; following which Vobis Kan conceived the suspicion that Gor Hajus had him marked for slaying. The result was inevitable: Gor Hajus was arrested and condemned to death; immediately following the execution of the sentence an agent of Ras Thavas had purchased the body. [004-004]

The sentence was carried out — Gor Hajus was run through the heart with a short-sword — and his body was promptly purchased by an agent of Ras Thavas [004-004]. For six years his corpse lay preserved in the House of the Dead, until [[Vad Varo]] selected him for resurrection.

### Resurrection and Alliance

Vad Varo, needing allies to escape the House of the Dead and rescue [[Valla Dia]], secretly repaired Gor Hajus's damaged heart and revived him alongside [[Dar Tarus]] [007-001]. Upon waking, Gor Hajus immediately recognized his surroundings:

> "Ah!" exclaimed Gor Hajus, whose eyes had been roaming about the chamber. "I can guess where I am. What Toonolian has not heard of Ras Thavas? So they sold my corpse to the old butcher did they? And what now? Did I just arrive?" [007-004]

When Vad Varo proposed an alliance to free Valla Dia and restore her to her rightful body, Gor Hajus's response revealed both his fierce independence and his single-minded thirst for vengeance:

> "Six years!" mused Gor Hajus. "Well, out with it, man. What do you want? If it is to slay Ras Thavas, no! He has saved me from utter destruction; but name me some other, preferably Vobis Kan, Jeddak of Toonol. Find me a blade and I will slay a hundred to regain life." [007-005]

> Gor Hajus grinned. "You have a large contract on your hands," he said, "but I can see that you are a man after my own heart and I am with you. It will give freedom and fighting, and all that I ask is a chance for one thrust at Vobis Kan." [007-006]

His commitment, once given, was absolute:

> "That means that I shall have to serve you for life," he replied, "for the thing you have undertaken you can never accomplish; but that is better than lying here on a cold *ersite* slab waiting for old Ras Thavas to come along and carve out my gizzard. I am yours! Let me up, that I may feel a good pair of legs under me again." [007-007]

### The Escape from Toonol

Gor Hajus's knowledge of Toonol proved invaluable. He piloted the stolen flier away from the island, navigated the equilibrimotor through the city, and led the party to the palace of his friend Mu Tel, Prince of the House of Kan [009-004]. His reputation opened doors that would otherwise have remained shut:

> "There are few Toonolians," he said, "who would not be glad to serve Gor Hajus, whom we had thought long dead." [008-012]

### The Liberation of Phundahl

In Phundahl, Gor Hajus played the role of a traveling showman, presenting [[Hovan Du]] the white ape as a trained beast to gain entrance to the palace of [[Xaxa]] [011-005]. When their ruse was discovered, he fought alongside Vad Varo through the palace corridors. In the temple of Tur, Gor Hajus and Vad Varo concealed themselves inside the head of the great idol, using its periscope eyes to spy upon the priests and orchestrate the dramatic "miracle" that would topple Sag Or and restore Valla Dia to her throne [012-010].

## Relationships

Gor Hajus forms a warrior's bond with [[Dar Tarus]], the fighting man of Phundahl. Though the two come from rival cities, their shared resurrection and common purpose forge them into a formidable pair. He treats Vad Varo with a mix of professional respect and genuine liking, recognizing in the Earthman a kindred spirit. His friendship with Mu Tel, Prince of the House of Kan, speaks to a network of loyalty that survived his apparent death.

## Significance

Gor Hajus embodies the Burroughsian ideal of the honorable warrior — a killer who lives by a code stricter than that of the rulers who condemn him. His refusal to compromise his principles, even at the cost of his life, contrasts sharply with the moral bankruptcy of characters like Vobis Kan and Sag Or. As a structural element in the narrative, Gor Hajus provides the local knowledge, combat prowess, and network of alliances that transform Vad Varo's quixotic rescue mission into a viable insurgency.
