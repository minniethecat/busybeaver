## Overview

Xaxa is the hereditary ruler of Phundahl, a Barsoomian city-state, and the primary antagonist of the novel. An aging, disfigured tyrant, she uses her immense wealth to purchase the body of the beautiful young Valla Dia from Ras Thavas, undergoing a brain transplant that leaves her in possession of Valla Dia's perfect form while Valla Dia's consciousness is trapped in Xaxa's discarded husk. Ras Thavas describes her as "an ignorant, arrogant, selfish, stupid, cruel virago" [002-003].

## Biography

Xaxa inherited the throne of Phundahl from her father, who was Jeddak before her, and his father before him — a dynasty stretching back into antiquity [002-003]. Despite her tyrannical misrule, Ras Thavas notes with contempt that the Phundahlians "would fight and die for her" out of sentimental attachment to the hereditary principle [002-003].

By the time of the novel's events, Xaxa had grown old and her face had become terribly disfigured, apparently by some injury. Unwilling to accept the natural consequences of aging, she traveled to the House of the Dead and paid Ras Thavas an enormous sum — the highest price he had ever received — to transplant her brain into the preserved body of Valla Dia [005-004].

The procedure was successful: Xaxa gained Valla Dia's youth and breathtaking beauty, while Valla Dia's brain was left dormant in Xaxa's aged, disfigured corpse. After the operation, Xaxa returned to Phundahl to continue her reign, now wearing a face of incomparable loveliness that masked her cruel nature. Her warriors, who had accompanied her to the House of the Dead, were fiercely protective of her [001-005].

Xaxa's rule over Phundahl is characterized by oppressive taxation and exploitation. Ras Thavas, himself no moral exemplar, condemns her as someone who "taxes them until they can scarce stagger beneath their burden" and "misrules them, exploits them, betrays them" [002-003].

## Relationships

Xaxa's relationship with Ras Thavas is purely transactional; she pays and he delivers, with mutual disdain on both sides [002-002]. She commands a retinue of women and armed warriors who attend her constantly. Her primary victim is Valla Dia, whose body she stole, but her oppressive rule victimizes the entire population of Phundahl.

## Significance

Xaxa represents the corruption of hereditary power and the vanity that values physical appearance over moral substance. Her theft of Valla Dia's body literalizes the novel's concern with the disjunction between external beauty and internal character. Possessing the most beautiful form on Barsoom while harboring one of its ugliest souls, Xaxa serves as the dark mirror to Valla Dia, whose nobility of spirit persists despite her grotesque external appearance.
