## Overview

Bal Zak is a captain in the service of [[Vobis Kan]], Jeddak of [[Toonol]], who commands the patrol flier *Vosar*. He plays a brief but pivotal role in the escape of [[Vad Varo]]'s company when his ship intercepts the stolen flier carrying the fugitives from Ras Thavas's island [008-006]. His personal connection to [[Gor Hajus]] — whom he genuinely believes to be dead — inadvertently aids the company's passage through the Toonolian patrol cordon.

## Biography

Bal Zak was a flier captain in the Toonolian military, stationed in the vicinity of the Tower of Thavas. When Vobis Kan learned that Gor Hajus had been resurrected and was at large, the Jeddak dispatched patrol boats to search all vessels leaving the island [008-006]. Bal Zak's *Vosar* was one of these patrol craft.

### The Interception

When Bal Zak's ship hailed the fugitives' stolen flier, the company feared discovery. However, Vad Varo had concealed Gor Hajus and [[Dar Tarus]] beneath the deck, and [[Hovan Du]] was disguised. When the patrol's search party boarded, they recognized the ship as the *Vosar* — Bal Zak's own vessel — and relaxed their guard. One of the boarding party called out cheerfully: "What ho! It is Bal Zak himself, my old friend Bal Zak!" [008-005].

Bal Zak, convinced that the fugitives had already fled eastward aboard a different flier (the *Pinsar*), dismissed the search as futile. "Right you are, Bal Zak," agreed his comrade, "and it were a waste of time to search your ship" [008-006]. The search party departed without discovering the hidden fugitives.

### Aftermath

When the company later reached the landing stage at Toonol, they waited aboard the ship, uncertain whether Bal Zak might return and discover them. The captain's connection to the vessel meant that any slip would be fatal. However, the company managed to depart before Bal Zak returned, escaping into the city under cover of darkness [008-008].

## Relationships

Bal Zak is a loyal officer of Vobis Kan's military apparatus, though he appears more dutiful than zealous. His friendship with the search party commander — a comrade who recognizes him by voice alone — suggests he is well-regarded among his fellow officers. He has no direct relationship with Vad Varo's company, interacting with them only through the proxy of his absent authority over the *Vosar*.

## Significance

Bal Zak represents the mundane machinery of Vobis Kan's regime — competent soldiers carrying out orders without particular malice or particular insight. His role in the narrative is that of a gate guardian who is bypassed not through combat but through misdirection: the company escapes not because Bal Zak is incompetent, but because he is operating on false intelligence. His presence underscores the precariousness of the company's situation — a single attentive search would have ended their quest before it truly began.
