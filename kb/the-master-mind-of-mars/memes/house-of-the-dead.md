## Overview

The House of the Dead is the colloquial name for Ras Thavas's enormous medical and research facility located near Toonol on Barsoom. It is here that the thousand-year-old surgeon conducts his brain-transplantation operations, preserves hundreds of bodies in suspended animation, and pursues his lifelong scientific investigations. The facility serves as the novel's primary setting, where Ulysses Paxton arrives, is trained, and where the central crime — the theft of Valla Dia's body — takes place.

## Description

The facility resembles "a feudal castle of Europe" more than any familiar Martian architecture, with ornately carved facades and irregular roof lines that "almost suggest a ruin, and yet the whole seemed harmonious and not without beauty" [001-001]. It contains high-walled enclosures with crimson sward, flowering trees and shrubs, and walks of colored pebbles among which "scintillated what appeared to be rare and beautiful gems" [001-001].

The interior is organized across multiple floors and buildings. The first floor contains chambers filled with rows of tables bearing partially dismembered or mutilated human corpses — the surgical and research areas [001-002]. Above each table hangs a shelf of surgical instruments and containers of various solutions. Case records hang from hooks at the head of each table [001-003].

The second floor houses the body storage chambers, where whole, preserved corpses are kept, "all of which were patched in various places with adhesive tape" [001-003]. One chamber is dedicated entirely to the bodies of young women, "many of them of great beauty" — the inventory from which wealthy clients like Xaxa select their new forms [001-004].

The facility also contains luxurious living apartments, Barsoomian baths, dining chambers, and armories. Ras Thavas maintains a staff of slaves and attendants, though the surgical work is performed by him alone, with Paxton later serving as his sole apprentice [002-001].

## Events

The House of the Dead is the setting for virtually all of the novel's early and middle action: Paxton's arrival on Barsoom [001-001], his duel with the club-wielding attacker [001-001], his training in surgical techniques [002-001], Xaxa's visit and the purchase of Valla Dia's body [001-004], Valla Dia's resurrection [005-005], and the eventual confrontation between Paxton and Ras Thavas.

## Inhabitants

The facility is ruled absolutely by Ras Thavas. It is staffed by "hewers of wood and carriers of water" — slaves and attendants who perform menial tasks but are excluded from the surgical work [002-001]. Ulysses Paxton becomes the sole apprentice and later bodyguard. The preserved bodies themselves constitute a macabre population, some having lain in suspended animation for up to fifty years.
