## Overview

Toonol is one of the principal city-states of [[Barsoom]], situated across the Toonolian Marshes and the snow-clad Artolian Hills from [[Duhor]] [003-003]. It serves as the primary setting for the novel's early chapters and is the seat of power of Vobis Kan, the Jeddak whose paranoia drives several key plot developments. Toonol is distinguished from other Barsoomian nations by its culture of rational self-interest — its people pride themselves on being "less subject to the vagaries of sentiment" than other races [002-002].

## Description

Ras Thavas describes Toonolian society as fundamentally pragmatic. "We of Toonol are probably less subject to its vagaries than most other nations upon Barsoom," he tells Vad Varo, explaining that sentiment is "a bar to all progress" [002-002]. Yet he acknowledges that sentiment has its uses: "Without it we could preserve no stable form of government and the Phundahlians, or some other people, would overrun and conquer us; but enough of our lower classes have sentiment to a sufficient degree to give them loyalty to the Jeddak of Toonol" [002-002].

The city itself, seen from the landing tower during the escape, reveals "broad, well-lighted avenues" crowded with people several hundred feet below [008-012]. Its architecture accommodates both fliers and equilibrimotors, with public depots for the latter on the stages beneath the landing platforms. The palace of Mu Tel, Prince of the House of Kan, provides a glimpse of Toonolian luxury — sleeping apartments "luxuriously furnished" with "magnificent sleeping silks and furs" [009-005].

## Events

The Toonol sequences encompass the novel's entire first act. Vad Varo arrives here after being transported from Earth, becomes Ras Thavas's assistant, and learns the arts of Barsoomian surgery. The escape from Toonol forms the first major action set-piece: the theft of a flier from the Tower of Thavas, the encounter with Vobis Kan's patrol boat, the descent into the city via equilibrimotor, and the refuge at the palace of Mu Tel [008-009].

Toonol's political dynamics drive much of the plot. Vobis Kan's feud with Gor Hajus — triggered by the assassin's refusal to kill a friend — leads to Gor Hajus's execution and eventual resurrection [004-004]. The Jeddak's subsequent belief that Ras Thavas freed Gor Hajus to assassinate him results in the seizure of the surgeon's island, forcing Ras Thavas to flee to Phundahl [014-012].

## Inhabitants

Toonol is a Red Martian city-state. Its ruling class includes Vobis Kan (Jeddak), Mu Tel (Prince of the House of Kan), and the surgeon Ras Thavas — though the latter operates from an island facility separate from the city proper. The assassin Gor Hajus was among Toonol's most famous citizens before his execution. The Toonolian character is marked by a cultivated disdain for sentiment and religion; Toonolians are noted atheists who look upon Phundahl's worship of Tur with contempt [010-005].
