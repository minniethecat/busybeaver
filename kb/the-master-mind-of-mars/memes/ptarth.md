## Ptarth

Ptarth is a sovereign city-state on [[Barsoom]], distinguished among the red Martian nations for the exceptional quality of its aerial fleet. Although the narrative action never takes place within Ptarth itself, the city's naval supremacy is referenced across multiple chapters as a benchmark of Barsoomian technological achievement.

### Naval Prowess

Ptarth stands alongside [[Helium]] at the pinnacle of Barsoomian naval power. When the narrator and his companions escape from [[Toonol]] aboard a stolen flier, he observes that Toonolian crafts are "not overly fast, being far outstripped in speed by the fliers of such nations as Helium and Ptarth who have for ages devoted themselves to the perfection of their navies" (009-102). This passage establishes Ptarth as one of the planet's two great flying nations — a society whose national character and resources have been shaped by centuries of dedication to naval engineering.

The Toonolian landing tower from which the narrator first views a Martian city offers an implicit contrast: "Toonol is in no sense a flying nation, supporting no such enormous fleets of merchant ships and vessels of war as, for example, the twin cities of Helium or the great capital of Ptarth" (008-076). The phrase "great capital of Ptarth" conveys the city's stature among Barsoomian powers.

### Geography and Language

Ptarth is described as a "distant country" from Phundahl (010-105). During the performance staged by [[Gor Hajus]] to gain entry to [[Xaxa]]'s palace, he claims that [[Hovan Du]] "was captured in a deserted city near Ptarth and can read and write the language of that country" (010-105). A slave from Ptarth in the crowd confirms his origin, and his written messages — which Hovan Du correctly delivers — attest to the existence of a distinct Ptarthian written language (010-107).

### Noble Connections

In the novel's epilogue, the narrator — now living in Helium — lists among the notable figures he sees daily: "Cathoris, Thuvia of Ptarth, Tara of Helium, Gahan of Gathol and that peerless creature, Dejah Thoris, Princess of Mars" (014-137). The mention of **Thuvia of Ptarth** — a princess of the city-state — confirms Ptarth's status as a major Barsoomian power whose nobility moves in the highest circles of Martian society, including the court of John Carter at Helium.

### Significance

Ptarth appears in the novel primarily as a standard of comparison — a technologically sophisticated nation whose achievements in naval engineering set the bar for other Barsoomian city-states. Its presence in the narrative, though indirect, helps establish the scale and diversity of Barsoomian civilization: a world of competing sovereign powers, each with its own technological traditions, military capabilities, and cultural identity.
