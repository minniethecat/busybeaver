# Barsoomian Duel

A **Barsoomian duel** is a formal or informal contest of arms between individuals, governed by the broader warrior culture of Barsoom (Mars). Duels occur both as spontaneous clashes and as formalized contests that test a combatant's swordsmanship.

## Ulysses Paxton's First Duel

Upon his arrival on Barsoom, Ulysses Paxton is immediately thrust into combat. Transported into an unfamiliar body within a walled garden enclosure, he faces a club-wielding red warrior: "As I stumbled about endeavoring to accustom myself to the new conditions, I found that instead of offering any serious opposition to my antagonist I was hard put to it to escape death at his hands, so often did I stumble and fall sprawling upon the scarlet sward; so that the duel from its inception became but a series of efforts, upon his part to reach and crush me with his great club, and upon mine to dodge and elude him."[001-032]

Paxton's unfamiliarity with his new body renders him nearly helpless in the opening moments. However, adaptation comes quickly: "soon I learned, and quickly too under the exigencies of the situation, to command my muscles, and then I stood my ground and when he aimed a blow at me, and I had dodged it, I touched him with my point and brought blood along with a savage roar of pain."[001-032]

The duel ends decisively: "In my attempts to elude him in the beginning of the duel we had crossed the enclosure and were now fighting at a considerable distance from the point of our first meeting... I set upon him with redoubled intensity. He missed me by the fraction of an inch, the wind in the wake of his bludgeon fanning my scalp, but he left an opening into which I stepped, running my sword fairly through his heart."[001-034] This victory demonstrates that skill with the blade, not brute strength, prevails in Barsoomian combat.

## Weapons of the Duel

Barsoomian duels employ the full arsenal carried by warriors, notably the long-sword and short-sword. Paxton, upon being armed by Ras Thavas, receives "a long-sword, a short-sword, a pistol and a dagger"[002-028], the standard complement of a Barsoomian fighting man. The long-sword serves as the primary dueling weapon, while the dagger is reserved for the killing stroke in ritual sacrifice rather than dueling proper.

## Duels Within the Enclosure

The walled garden in the Tower of Thavas, where Paxton fights his first duel, is a formal enclosure. The "crimson sward within a high walled enclosure"[001-020] provides a contained arena — private enough for matters of honor to be settled without the involvement of authorities. Ras Thavas, upon witnessing the contest, does not intervene to stop it but rather shouts commands — suggesting that the duel is a recognized form of conflict resolution, not a criminal act.

## Swordsmanship as a Measure of Worth

Skill with the blade is a primary measure of a warrior's worth. The Toonolian assassin **Gor Hajus** achieved his fearsome reputation through "audacity, fearlessness and swordsmanship"[006-060]. When he strikes down an enemy, his blade-work is described with precision: "he struck with the short-sword I had loaned him... The blow was broken by the wing of the warrior's equilibrimotor, which it demolished, yet it inflicted a severe wound in the fellow's shoulder."[008-102]

## Duel and Honor

Duels on Barsoom are inseparable from the honor culture. As Paxton's duel demonstrates, a contest of arms is both a physical and social event — the victor gains standing, the loser is carried to the medical tables of Ras Thavas for repair or oblivion. The absence of formal seconds or elaborate protocols in the duel Paxton fights suggests a culture where combat is sufficiently routine that specialized dueling formalities are unnecessary.
