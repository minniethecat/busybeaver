## Overview

The Pinsar is one of three fliers belonging to Ras Thavas at his island laboratory. A smaller, faster vessel compared to the other cargo carriers in the fleet, the Pinsar plays a pivotal role in the escape of Paxton and his companions: the fugitives start its motors and send it flying unmanned toward the east, creating the illusion that they have fled in that direction while they actually stow away on the Vosar bound for Toonol [008-006].

## Description

Ras Thavas maintains three fliers at his island hangar, and the Pinsar is the smallest and fastest among them. Its superior speed makes it the natural choice for a diversion — a fleeing vessel heading east at speed would plausibly be carrying the wanted fugitives. The protagonists set the Pinsar's motors running and launch it unmanned into the dawn sky, a calculated deception.

The ruse is sufficiently convincing that Ras Thavas himself believes he witnessed the escape: "Ras Thavas himself with his own eyes saw them fly off in the Pinsar before dawn this day and disappear in the east" [008-007]. However, Bal Zak — captain of the Vosar — is not deceived. From his quarters overlooking the hangar, he is awakened by the starting of the Pinsar's motors and observes three figures on the roof with a fourth dropping from the deck as the ship takes off, correctly deducing that the vessel was sent unmanned [008-009].

The naming convention of Ras Thavas's ships follows a pattern: the Pinsar and the Vosar are both referred to with the definite article — "the Pinsar" — suggesting Barsoomian ships are named entities rather than merely described by type.

## Significance

The Pinsar serves a dual narrative function: its launch provides the immediate stimulus that awakens Bal Zak and sets the subsequent stowaway encounter in motion, while the successful deception it creates buys the protagonists crucial time. The fact that Bal Zak sees through the ruse — yet chooses to help rather than betray them — reinforces the theme of honor and gratitude among Toonolians that permeates the escape sequence [008-013].
