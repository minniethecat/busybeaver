## Overview

Hora San was a Jeddak and high priest of [[Phundahl]] who lived roughly a hundred years before the events of the novel. His mysterious disappearance — interpreted by the faithful as being "taken away" by [[Tur]] himself — marked the last time the god had spoken aloud to his people. The legend of Hora San's vanishment provided the theological framework that [[Vad Varo]] and [[Gor Hajus]] exploited to stage their false miracle in the [[temple-of-tur]].

## The Legend

According to the cult of Tur, Hora San was "taken away by you on that long-gone night of mystery" — a direct ascension into the presence of the god. For a hundred years following his disappearance, Tur remained silent, never again deigning to speak directly to the Phundahlian people [012-008]. This long silence created a culture of rote worship among the priesthood, many of whom privately held little genuine faith in their deity.

When [[Xaxa]] prepared to sacrifice [[Dar Tarus]] before the idol, she invoked Hora San's name, crying out: "O, Tur, Father of Barsoom, behold the offering we place before you... For a hundred years you have not deigned to speak aloud to your faithful slaves; never since Hora San, the high priest, was taken away by you on that long-gone night of mystery have you unsealed your lips to your people" [012-008].

## Exploitation of the Legend

Vad Varo and his companions used the legend of Hora San to their advantage. When they staged the "miracle" of Tur speaking to the terrified court, Vad Varo — speaking from inside the idol's head — commanded the priests to leave the temple. He then had [[Dar Tarus]] don the trappings of Hora San, taken from the long-dead Jeddak's bones hidden in the base of the idol [013-002]. When Dar Tarus entered the temple wearing these trappings, the assembled Phundahlians saw what they believed to be the resurrected Hora San — a figure of incontestable religious authority.

The effect was immediate and overwhelming. Dar Tarus, resplendent in Hora San's ancient regalia, "looked every inch a Jeddak as he moved with slow dignity up the wide aisle" [013-002]. [[Xaxa]], recognizing the threat to her authority, shrieked "Impostor! Seize him! Kill him!" — but the religious display, combined with the earlier "miracle," had already turned the court against her.
