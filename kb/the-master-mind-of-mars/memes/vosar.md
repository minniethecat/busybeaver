## Overview

The Vosar is a commercial cargo flier operating on Barsoom, captained by Bal Zak and running the route between the Tower of Thavas and Toonol. Compared to military vessels, the Vosar is notably slow, designed for carrying goods rather than speed. The ship serves as a key element in the escape of Ulysses Paxton and his companions from the island of Ras Thavas [008-002].

## Description

The Vosar is a commercial vessel — a carrier class flier built for cargo transport rather than speed or combat. When the protagonists steal one of Ras Thavas's fliers and send it unmanned toward the east as a diversion, Bal Zak's Vosar is the ship that subsequently intercepts their escape attempt. Upon arriving at the Tower of Thavas, Bal Zak calls out his identity and destination: "The Vosar, Tower of Thavas for Toonol" [008-003].

The ship's most significant feature in the narrative is its role as a hiding place. Paxton, Gor Hajus, Dar Tarus, and Hovan Du board the Vosar and conceal themselves in a closet, believing they are stowing away undetected for passage to Toonol [008-009]. However, Bal Zak — who has quarters overlooking the roof and hangar — is awakened by the starting of the Pinsar's motors and observes their clandestine boarding of his vessel.

Bal Zak dismisses his crew so that he alone knows of the stowaways' presence, motivated by gratitude: six years earlier, Gor Hajus had refused to assassinate Bal Zak's father, considering him a good man [008-013]. The captain offers to help the fugitives reach their destination, suggesting they use equilibrimotors from a nearby public depot while he distracts the watchman with a game of jetan [008-015].

## Significance

The Vosar serves as both a literal vehicle of escape and a narrative device that introduces Bal Zak's honorable character. Unlike the military fliers that pursue the protagonists, the Vosar is slow and non-threatening — its very slowness underscoring the tension between the fugitives' need for speed and their reliance on an unsuspecting ally. The ship becomes a microcosm of the broader theme of gratitude and honor among Toonolians, as Bal Zak's decision to help — motivated by a debt of honor — enables the entire subsequent phase of the escape plan [008-017].
