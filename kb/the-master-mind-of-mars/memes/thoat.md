## Overview

The **thoat** is a domesticated animal native to Barsoom, widely raised as livestock and valued for its meat. Thoat steak is a staple food item consumed across Martian society, available for purchase in shops catering to common soldiers, artisans, and slaves. The creature's role in *The Master Mind of Mars* is primarily culinary — it appears as a food source rather than a living specimen.

## The Thoat in the Broader Barsoom Canon

In the wider Barsoom series, the thoat is a much more prominent creature, depicted as a large, eight-legged riding and draft animal used by the green Martians (Tharks) and other Barsoomian peoples. In *The Master Mind of Mars*, however, the thoat appears solely in its capacity as a food animal. This narrowing of focus — from the majestic war-mount of earlier novels to a simple meat source — reflects the more intimate, urban scale of Vad Varo's narrative, which takes place primarily in the cities of Toonol and Phundahl rather than on the open plains of Barsoom.

## Purchase and Preparation

Thoat meat is sold by the pound in Barsoomian food shops. When the protagonists arrive in Toonol after escaping from Mu Tel's palace, Gor Hajus — who has been furnished with money by Mu Tel — stops at a small shop to purchase "four or five pounds of thoat steak" for Hovan Du, the great white ape traveling with them [P12]. The quantity suggests the thoat is a large animal yielding substantial cuts of meat, and the casual commercial transaction indicates that thoat is a readily available commodity in Barsoomian markets.

The fact that the meat is purchased by the pound, handled as "steak," and sold in ordinary shops places the thoat within a recognizable economic framework. Barsoom, for all its alien features — hurtling moons, brain transplantation, silent flying motors — has butcher shops and steaks, grounding its fantastic elements in mundane commercial reality.

## As Food for the Great White Ape

The thoat meat is purchased specifically for Hovan Du, the great white ape who has joined the party. The white ape's carnivorous diet requires substantial quantities of raw meat, and the thoat steak serves this purpose. The party proceeds to an eating place where the proprietor reluctantly allows the great ape to remain in an inner room with his thoat meat while the others dine in the outer room [P12].

This arrangement highlights the social dynamics of the group: Hovan Du, though intelligent and capable of strategic thought, is still a great ape with bestial appetites and must be accommodated separately. The thoat meat serves as both sustenance and a marker of the ape's otherness within the party.

## The Shop District

The shop where Gor Hajus purchases the thoat meat is located in a district of Toonol described as containing "second-rate shops, eating places and hotels such as are frequented by the common soldiers, artisans and slaves" [P12]. This is deliberate — the party seeks to avoid attention, and such a district is "where the only attention we attracted was due to the curiosity aroused by Hovan Du" [P12].

The thoat meat shop thus serves a narrative function beyond simple world-building: it establishes the socioeconomic stratum in which the protagonists are operating. They are hiding among the common people of Toonol, purchasing common food from common shops, and their only distinguishing feature is not their appearance but the presence of a great white ape in their company.

## Comparison with Other Barsoomian Food Sources

The thoat is the only explicitly named food animal in *The Master Mind of Mars*, but the narrative implies a broader Barsoomian food economy. The eating place where the party dines serves unspecified food to the human members while Hovan Du consumes his thoat steak separately. The implication is that Barsoomian cuisine is diverse, with meat from domesticated animals forming a standard part of the diet, much as beef or pork does on Earth.

## Significance

The thoat represents the mundane domestic economy of Barsoom that exists alongside the epic politics and body-swapping science central to the narrative. The casual purchase of thoat meat in a common shop grounds the alien world in relatable daily practices — even on Mars, one must eat. The detail also serves to characterize Hovan Du as a carnivorous creature requiring substantial quantities of raw meat, distinguishing him from his humanoid companions. For readers familiar with the wider Barsoom canon, the thoat's cameo as a food source is a reminder of how the series shifts perspective between novels, presenting the same world through different narrative lenses.
