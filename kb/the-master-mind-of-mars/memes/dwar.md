## Overview

**Dwar** is a mid-level Barsoomian military rank, roughly equivalent to a captain. A dwar commands a unit of warriors, a ship, or serves as an officer in the Jeddak's or Jeddara's Guard — the elite military force directly under the ruler's command. The rank is subordinate to higher ranks such as odwar (general) but carries significant authority, particularly when attached to the royal household.

## Naval Command

The naval dimension of the dwar's authority is demonstrated when Bal Zak's flier is intercepted by a vessel under the command of the "dwar of Vobis Kan's ship" [P10]. This shows the dwar functioning as a ship captain — boarding other vessels, exercising authority on behalf of the Jeddak (Vobis Kan), and conducting military operations in the skies of Barsoom. Bal Zak mentions this dwar in the context of a reward for capturing Vad Varo, implying that ship's dwar had offered to turn Vad Varo over to the authorities, though Bal Zak declined — suggesting a complex relationship between civilian ship operators and military officers [P10].

## The Dwar of the Jeddara's Guard

The most prominently featured dwar in the narrative is the "dwar of the Jeddara's Guard" in Phundahl. This officer commands the elite guard unit responsible for palace security and the protection of Xaxa, the Jeddara. When an assassination attempt is reported, the dwar personally leads the search party, announcing himself at the temple doors: "'Tis I, dwar of the Jeddara's Guard. We are seeking two who came to assassinate Xaxa" [P13].

The dwar's investigative authority is demonstrated in his conversation with the temple priests. He reports that the assassins were present "scarce twenty tals since" [P13] — a tals being a Barsoomian unit of measurement — establishing a precise and recent timeline for the pursuit. The dwar's ability to question priests and conduct searches within the temple indicates that the Guard's jurisdiction extends even into religious spaces.

## Investigations and Political Sensitivity

The dwar of the Jeddara's Guard is also responsible for sensitive investigative work. The narrative reveals that previous discoveries made by the dwar during the search of the palace's "remote precinct of the pits" — subterranean areas beneath the palace — uncovered material so compromising that "one great and powerful house" was nearly brought down by the findings [P14]. This suggests the dwar's role extends beyond simple security to include intelligence gathering and potentially politically charged investigations. The fact that these discoveries threatened a "great and powerful house" — a noble family — indicates that even a mid-ranking officer can wield considerable influence when their findings touch on the interests of the powerful.

## Plural Usage and Mustering

The plural form **dwars** appears when multiple officers of this rank are referenced collectively. When Xaxa orchestrates the second temple ceremony as part of Vad Varo's plot to overthrow her, she summons "all the higher nobles to the temple after the evening meal tonight, and all the priests, and the commanders and dwars of the Guard, and as many of the lesser nobles as be in the palace" [P15]. This enumeration reveals the dwars' place in the Phundahlian hierarchy: below the commanders but above the common soldiers, forming the officer corps of the Guard.

The inclusion of dwars in this ceremonial muster also serves a practical narrative function: Vad Varo's plan requires the presence of witnesses — the more official, the better — to authenticate the "word of Tur" that will legitimize the transfer of power from Xaxa to Valla Dia.

## Position in the Barsoomian Military Hierarchy

The dwar occupies a middle tier in the Barsoomian command structure:

| Rank | Barsoomian Term | Equivalent | Role |
|------|----------------|------------|------|
| Supreme ruler | Jeddak / Jeddara | Emperor/Empress | Absolute sovereign |
| General | Odwar | General | Commands armies |
| Captain | Dwar | Captain | Commands ships, guard units |
| Common soldier | Panthan | Mercenary/Soldier | Rank-and-file warrior |

The progression from panthan (mercenary soldier) through dwar (captain) to odwar (general) represents the standard military career path. The dwar's position at the midpoint of this hierarchy makes them the critical link between high command and the fighting forces — officers close enough to the troops to lead them in the field, yet senior enough to exercise independent command authority.

## Significance

The dwar rank illustrates the hierarchical military structure that underpins Barsoomian political power. While the Jeddak or Jeddara holds the throne, their authority is exercised through a chain of command that includes dwars as key operational officers. The dwar of the Guard, in particular, occupies a position of trust and access that makes them privy to the secrets of the ruling house — a fact that proves significant when a dwar's discoveries in the pits threaten powerful nobles.
