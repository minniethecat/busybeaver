## Overview

John Carter is the legendary War Lord of Barsoom, an Earthman from Virginia who first came to Mars through astral projection and rose to become the planet's greatest military leader. Though he appears directly only in the novel's final chapter, his presence pervades the entire narrative — it is his example that inspires Ulysses Paxton to wish himself to Mars, and his intervention that resolves the novel's political conflicts.

## In the Narrative

Ulysses Paxton first encountered John Carter through the pages of Edgar Rice Burroughs's novel *A Princess of Mars* while at an officers' training camp in 1917 [000-003]. The story made so profound an impression on him that he dreamed nightly of Mars, and at night on the deck of his transport ship he would lie gazing at the Red Planet, "wishing that, like John Carter, I might be drawn across the great void to the haven of my desire" [000-005].

After Paxton's own miraculous transportation to Barsoom, he recognized the red skin of the Martians and the lesser gravity from "the manuscripts of John Carter, those marvelous and as yet unappreciated contributions to the scientific literature of a world" [001-005]. Carter's writings also taught him that Martian internal organs are not arranged identically to those of Earthmen — knowledge that saved him in his first duel [001-005].

Carter is referenced as a famous figure known even in distant Toonol. When Valla Dia learns that Vad Varo is also from Jasoom, she asks him about the other Jasoomian "whose fame has reached to the remotest corners of Barsoom" — though she notes that as a citizen of Duhor, a city unfriendly to Helium, she had never seen him [002-005].

## Appearance at Phundahl

John Carter makes his dramatic entrance in the novel's final chapter. Following the overthrow of Xaxa, his great ship of Helium intercepts the flier carrying Vad Varo, Valla Dia, and their companions. Breaking the flag of truce, Carter boards their vessel at the head of a party of Heliumite officers. Vad Varo describes him as "the most impressive figure I have ever seen" [014-001].

Carter greets Dar Tarus in peace, noting that his reception "had been different, I think, had Xaxa still reigned" [014-001]. When questions of identity arise surrounding the body-swapped Dar Tarus, Carter's shrewd eyes detect the truth, and Vad Varo reveals himself as a fellow Earthman by speaking to him in English — a moment of recognition that bridges two worlds [014-005].

## Later Role

Carter personally escorted Valla Dia and Vad Varo to Duhor after attending the wedding of Dar Tarus and Kara Vasa [014-011]. He stood as best man at Vad Varo and Valla Dia's wedding — introducing to Barsoom "a good old American custom" — and then insisted the couple follow their nuptials with a honeymoon, bearing them off to Helium [014-013].
