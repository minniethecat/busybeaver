## Overview

Mu Tel is a prince of the noble House of Kan in the city of [[Toonol]]. When [[Gor Hajus]], long thought dead, appeared at his palace with a band of fugitives, Mu Tel honored the bonds of friendship without hesitation, providing shelter, arms, and a flier to [[Vad Varo]]'s company [009-003]. In a world of shifting alliances and court intrigue, Mu Tel represents the enduring value of personal loyalty — a quality that transcends politics and even death itself.

## Biography

Mu Tel was an old friend and ally of Gor Hajus before the assassin's arrest and execution. When Gor Hajus was condemned by Vobis Kan, Mu Tel was unable to prevent it, but he never forgot his friend [009-003]. Years later, when Gor Hajus returned from the dead as part of Vad Varo's company, the party descended into the gardens of Mu Tel's palace during a desperate escape from Toonolian air patrols [009-002].

Despite the danger of harboring fugitives wanted by Vobis Kan, the Jeddak of Toonol, Mu Tel welcomed Gor Hajus and his companions without reservation. He provided them with disguises, arranged for the construction of a flier suited to their needs, and supplied Gor Hajus with money for their journey [010-004]. His palace — a towering structure with a secret hangar in one of its spires — became the company's base of operations during their time in Toonol [010-003].

## Relationships

Mu Tel's bond with Gor Hajus is the emotional core of his character. Their friendship survived execution, resurrection, and the passage of years, and Mu Tel's unwavering loyalty never falters. His relationship with Vad Varo is that of a gracious host to an honored guest. He treats the Earthman with the deference due to a companion of his resurrected friend, asking no payment and placing no conditions on his assistance.

## Significance

Mu Tel serves as a counterpoint to the novel's many examples of treachery and self-interest. In a narrative dominated by stolen bodies and broken loyalties — [[Xaxa]] stealing [[Valla Dia]]'s form, Sag Or usurping [[Dar Tarus]]'s body, [[Vobis Kan]] betraying Gor Hajus — Mu Tel offers an island of steadfast honor. His willingness to risk his position and his life for a friend long thought dead demonstrates that the bonds of loyalty on [[Barsoom]] can be as powerful as the forces of corruption. Structurally, his palace provides the narrative breathing space between the frantic escape from Ras Thavas's island and the dangerous infiltration of Phundahl.
