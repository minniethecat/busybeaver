# Barsoomian Nobility

**Barsoomian nobility** refers to the hereditary aristocratic class that governs the city-states and nations of Barsoom (Mars). The system is organized around the office of the Jeddak (absolute monarch) and the noble houses whose loyalty sustains the throne.

## The Jeddak

The **Jeddak** is the supreme hereditary ruler of a Barsoomian city-state. Jeddaks hold absolute authority over their subjects, ruling through a combination of military power and the traditional loyalty of the masses. As Ras Thavas explains of Toonol: "enough of our lower classes have sentiment to a sufficient degree to give them loyalty to the Jeddak of Toonol and the upper classes are brainy enough to know that it is to their own best interests to keep him upon his throne."[002-044]

The Jeddak's power extends to life and death. Vobis Kan, Jeddak of Toonol, is described by his nephew Mu Tel as "a conscienceless tyrant."[009-036] Similarly, Xaxa, Jeddara of Phundahl, is characterized by Ras Thavas as "an ignorant, arrogant, selfish, stupid, cruel virago, yet the Phundahlians would fight and die for her because her father was Jeddak of Phundahl."[002-046]

## Jeddara — The Female Monarch

A **Jeddara** is the female equivalent of the Jeddak — a reigning queen. Xaxa of Phundahl exemplifies the office: she inherited the throne through her father and his father before him. Ras Thavas's disdain for hereditary rule is evident in his assessment that Phundahlians support Xaxa purely "because they are ruled by sentiment rather than reason; because their wicked rulers play upon this sentiment."[002-046]

Xaxa's wealth as Jeddara enabled her to purchase the body of the beautiful Princess Valla Dia: "It took a Jeddara to afford her. She brought the highest price that I have ever been paid."[002-080]

## The Princely Houses

Beneath the Jeddak, princes hold significant power and often harbor aspirations for the throne. **Mu Tel, Prince of the House of Kan**, represents the ambitious nobility who seek to supplant the reigning monarch. He explains: "The nobles have long been tired of Vobis Kan, and the people are tiring of him... There is also the natural suspicion that I, the next in line for succession, would make them no less tyrannical a Jeddak."[009-036]

Prince Mu Tel's willingness to "destroy my uncle and seize his throne"[009-038] reveals the precarious nature of hereditary succession — advancement through assassination is a calculated political option.

## Noble Warfare and Conquest

Barsoomian nobility pursue power through military aggression. **Jal Had, Prince of Amhor**, upon having his marriage suit rejected by Kor San, Jeddak of Duhor, "equipped a great fleet and set forth to conquer Duhor and take by force that which he could not win by honorable methods."[004-068] The conquest resulted in the sacking of the palace and the seizure of its noble inhabitants.

## Nobility and Honor

The Barsoomian noble class adheres to a code of honor, as when Dar Tarus declares: "I give you the word of honor of a Barsoomian noble that I know absolutely nothing concerning the whereabouts of the princess."[ch04p??] This honor code operates alongside the ruthless political practices that characterize noble competition for power.
