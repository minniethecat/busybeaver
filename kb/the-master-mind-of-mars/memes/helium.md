## Overview

Helium is the preeminent city-state of [[Barsoom]], mentioned repeatedly in *The Master Mind of Mars* as the seat of Barsoomian civilization and power. Though no action in this novel takes place in Helium itself, the city's influence is felt throughout the narrative — from the opening letter dated "Helium, June 8th, 1925" [001-001] to the final chapter in which John Carter's fleet from Helium arrives to receive the surrender of [[Phundahl]] [014-003].

## Description

Helium is described in the broader Barsoomian canon as the greatest of the red Martian cities, a metropolis of towering spires and magnificent architecture situated in the southern hemisphere. In this novel, Helium serves primarily as a political presence: its ruler, the Warlord John Carter, commands such respect that the mere sight of Helium's fleet is sufficient to cow opposing forces. Dar Tarus speaks of the city with the reverence typical of all Barsoomians: "He was of Helium and my people were not friendly with those of Helium" [002-003].

## Events

### The Opening Letter

The novel is framed as a manuscript transmitted to Edgar Rice Burroughs through the agency of John Carter, with the letter of transmittal dated from Helium [001-001]. This establishes Helium as the hub of interplanetary communication and Barsoomian civilization.

### Helium's Fleet

In the novel's climax, a fleet from Helium arrives to intercept the flier carrying [[Vad Varo]], [[Valla Dia]], and [[Dar Tarus]]. When the fleet's colors are identified, Dar Tarus — now Jeddak of Phundahl — hails the commander and offers either peace or war. The Helium fleet, operating under John Carter's mandate to support the restoration of legitimate rule in Phundahl, accepts Dar Tarus's authority and escorts the company to safety [014-005].

### Duhor and Helium

The novel notes that [[Duhor]] was once at war with Helium, which left the smaller city vulnerable to attack by Amhor — a historical detail that explains why Duhor's forces were not present to defend against Xaxa's coup [005-003].

## Inhabitants

Helium is ruled by Tardos Mors, Jeddak of Helium, and his grandson Carthoris. Its most famous resident is John Carter, the Earthman who became Warlord of Mars. The city is also home to Dejah Thoris, Carter's wife and the princess of Helium. Though none of these figures appear directly in *The Master Mind of Mars*, their influence on Barsoomian politics shapes the novel's resolution.
