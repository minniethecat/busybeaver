## Overview

The Palace of Mu Tel is the princely residence of [[Mu Tel|vobis-kan]], Prince of the House of Kan, located within the city of [[Toonol]]. It serves as a crucial refuge for [[Vad Varo]], [[Gor Hajus]], [[Dar Tarus]], and [[Hovan Du]] after their perilous descent from the [[tower-of-thavas|Tower of Thavas]]. The palace is a testament to Toonolian wealth and artistry, its lavish appointments reflecting the status of one of the city's most powerful noble houses [009-003].

## Description

The palace is approached through extensive gardens of scarlet sward planted with *pimalia*, *sorapus*, and other rare Barsoomian flora [008-027]. The main building rises ornately from these grounds, its walls richly carved — Vad Varo first encounters the structure by moonlight, pressing against its sculpted exterior while evading a *calot*, the ferocious Martian watch-beast [008-028].

Within, the palace reveals chambers of extraordinary luxury. The central reception hall is described as "a magnificent apartment, the gorgeous furnishings and decorations of which beggar the meager descriptive powers of my native tongue" [009-003]. Gold, ivory, precious stones, marvelous woods, resplendent fabrics, gorgeous furs, and "startling architecture" combine to create an interior of almost overwhelming opulence.

The palace features advanced Barsoomian technology, including sleeping apartments that can be lowered to daylight levels and a private auditorium — a camera obscura-like projection room capable of magnifying remote views, which Mu Tel uses to show Vad Varo images of distant [[Earth]] [009-014].

## Events

### Refuge After Escape

After their harrowing descent from the Tower of Thavas and evasion of the air patrol, Vad Varo's party makes its way to the Palace of Mu Tel. Gor Hajus, who has pre-existing ties to the prince, uses his influence to secure sanctuary for the fugitives. Vad Varo is snatched from a *calot*'s attack through a hidden door in the palace wall and led through dark corridors to the brilliantly lit reception chamber [008-028].

### Planning the Phundahl Expedition

It is within the palace that the companions rest, re-arm, and plan their journey to [[Phundahl]]. Mu Tel provides them with plain *panthan* harness to disguise their identities, and furnishes Gor Hajus with funds for the expedition. The ape-man Hovan Du is outfitted with a collar and chain, completing the party's cover as traveling mercenaries with a trained beast [009-013].

## Inhabitants

The palace is the seat of Mu Tel, Prince of the House of Kan and nephew of Vobis Kan, Jeddak of Toonol. Mu Tel maintains a household of officers, slaves, and retainers befitting his rank. His relationship with his uncle is strained — Vobis Kan views the prince's popularity with suspicion and jealousy [009-008].
