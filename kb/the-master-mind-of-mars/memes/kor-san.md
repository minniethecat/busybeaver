## Overview

Kor San, Jeddak of [[Duhor]], is the father of Princess [[Valla Dia]]. His political decisions — specifically his refusal to allow his daughter to marry [[Jal Had]], Prince of [[Amhor]] — triggered the invasion of Duhor and the subsequent chain of events that led to Valla Dia's body being stolen by [[Xaxa]]. When finally reunited with his daughter, he heaps honors and riches upon the man who restored her.

## The Marriage Refusal

Kor San had no son, and therefore "had determined to wed his daughter to one of his own Jeds, that the son of this union, with the blood of Kor San in his veins, might rule over the people of Duhor" [003-003]. When Jal Had, Prince of Amhor, sent ambassadors to sue for Valla Dia's hand, Kor San declined the offer. This decision, made to preserve the Duhorian royal line, proved catastrophic: the enraged Jal Had equipped a great fleet and conquered the city while Duhor's main army was away fighting [[Helium]] in the south [003-003].

## Loss and Reunion

Kor San lost both his city and his daughter in the Amhorian attack. Valla Dia was taken as a hostage, fell into the hands of Xaxa's agents, and was brought to [[Ras Thavas]] for the brain transplant. It is unclear whether Kor San knew his daughter's fate during the years she inhabited the withered body of the old Jeddara.

When Vad Varo finally restores Valla Dia to her rightful body and returns her to Duhor, the reunion is a revelation for both parties. Vad Varo writes of "the great surprise that awaited me in Duhor, where I learned for the first time that Kor San, Jeddak of Duhor, was the father of Valla Dia; and of the honors and the great riches that he heaped upon me when Valla Dia and I were wed" [014-011].
