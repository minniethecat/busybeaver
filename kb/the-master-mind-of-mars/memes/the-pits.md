## Overview

The Pits are a network of underground vaults, chambers, and connecting passages beneath the laboratories of [[Ras Thavas]] on the [[island-of-thavas]]. More than mere storage spaces, the pits serve as the narrative's hidden dimension — a place of concealment, secret movement, and ultimately transformation. It is in these subterranean chambers that [[Vad Varo]] hides the body of [[Valla Dia]], assembles his escape party, and eventually performs the brain-restoration surgeries that resolve the novel's central conflicts [007-005].

## Description

The pits extend beneath the entire laboratory complex, connecting to every building on the island. Ras Thavas explains that each floor of the tower "connects with the adjacent building from the roof to the lowest pits beneath the ground" — a architectural feature of the twenty-three-thousand-year-old structure built by his ancestor [007-019].

The individual vaults are numbered systematically — vault L-42-X in building 4-J-21, for example, is where Vad Varo discovers the bodies of the half-brain transplant subjects: an ape with half a human brain, and a man with half an ape's brain [007-005]. The vaults contain *ersite*-topped operating tables, storage for preserved bodies, and dark corners where fugitives can be concealed. The chambers are described as "half forgotten," suggesting that even Ras Thavas does not maintain thorough knowledge of their full extent.

The pits connect to the [[tower-of-thavas]] via a runway — an inclined passage through which one can ascend from the underground darkness to the tower's upper levels and eventually to the roof and its hangar of cargo fliers [007-021]. This vertical connection, spanning from "the lowest pits beneath the ground" to the open sky, makes the pits the island's secret circulatory system.

## Events

### Hiding Valla Dia

When Ras Thavas discovers Valla Dia's apartment empty, Vad Varo hides her anesthetized body in "the half forgotten vaults in the pits beneath the laboratories" — specifically vault L-42-X [007-005]. For weeks thereafter, he smuggles food to her under cover of darkness, navigating the pits while evading Ras Thavas's spies.

### The Escape Route

On the night of the escape, Vad Varo leads Gor Hajus and Dar Tarus "towards the runway that descended to the pits beneath the building" to retrieve Hovan Du and Valla Dia's body. From the pits, they ascend through the tower and emerge on the roof to reach the hangar [007-021].

### The Return and Restoration

Thirteen chapters later, the party returns. "We moved directly to the entrance to the tower, found the runway and descended to the pits" [013-008]. In vault L-42-X, Vad Varo performs the climactic brain restoration surgeries — returning Valla Dia to her own beautiful body and the wicked Xaxa to her aged corpse. The pits, which once concealed the fugitive, now host the novel's surgical resolution.

### Discovery and Flight

The surgeries are interrupted when Yamdor discovers the party. "Without thought of caution or silence we hastened through the pits towards the runway in the tower" — an urgent flight that brings them face to face with Vobis Kan's warriors, who have occupied the island in the party's absence [013-010].

### Secrets Beneath Phundahl

The pits beneath the palace of [[Phundahl]] mirror those of Ras Thavas. When Vad Varo and Gor Hajus vanish from the palace, the Jeddara's Guard searches "the pits" more thoroughly than ever before, "and it seemed that queer things had been unearthed there — things of which not even Xaxa dreamed" [012-016]. These discoveries have political consequences: "at least one great and powerful house would fall because of what a *dwar* of the Jeddara's Guard had discovered in a remote precinct of the pits" [012-016].

## Inhabitants

The pits are not inhabited in the conventional sense, but they house the preserved bodies of Ras Thavas's experimental subjects — warriors, apes, and the hybrid results of his brain-transplantation experiments. For much of the novel, the anesthetized body of Valla Dia (in Xaxa's aged form) lies hidden in the darkness of vault L-42-X, awaiting the day of her restoration.
