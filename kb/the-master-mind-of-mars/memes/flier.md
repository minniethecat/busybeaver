## Overview

The flier is the principal form of aerial transportation on Barsoom, serving as the equivalent of Earth's ships and aircraft combined. Powered by radium motors and utilizing the eighth Barsoomian ray for lift and propulsion, fliers range from small personal craft to massive warships and cargo carriers. They are essential to Barsoomian society, enabling travel, trade, and military operations across the planet's vast expanses [007-015].

## Description

Barsoomian fliers vary considerably in design and capability depending on their intended purpose. The fliers belonging to Ras Thavas at his island laboratory exemplify the cargo-carrier class: low, squat vessels with rounded bows and sterns and fully covered decks, their every line proclaiming them as carriers built for anything but speed [007-020]. One of his three ships was noticeably smaller than the others and correspondingly faster [007-021].

The technological foundation of flier operation rests on the eighth Barsoomian ray, or ray of propulsion. This ray, when properly harnessed, counteracts gravitational pull, allowing a vessel to float and maneuver through the thin Martian atmosphere. The radium motors that power flier systems are compact yet powerful — the equilibrimotor units used for personal flight consist of a belt filled with the eighth ray to neutralize gravity, with a small radium motor and controls attached [009-030].

Military fliers represent the pinnacle of Barsoomian aerial engineering. The warships of major powers like Helium and Toonol are fast, heavily armed vessels capable of long-range pursuit and combat. When Vobis Kan, Jeddak of Toonol, learned that the assassin Gor Hajus was at large, he dispatched his best ships in pursuit — a testament to the military significance of fliers in maintaining political control [007-025].

Personal flight is also achievable through equilibrimotors: belt-mounted devices that combine eighth-ray buoyancy with small radium-powered wings. These personal fliers allow individuals to navigate city streets and short distances independently of larger vessels [009-030].

## Significance

Fliers are not merely transportation in the Barsoomian world — they are instruments of political power, tools of escape, and symbols of technological civilization. The protagonists' entire plan to reach Phundahl hinges on stealing or commandeering a flier from Ras Thavas [007-018]. The limitations of the available ships — their slowness relative to military pursuit vessels — create the central tension of the escape sequence. Fliers also define the geography of power: inaccessible regions like the Great Toonolian Marshes maintain their independence precisely because they afford no landing places for fliers [007-012].
