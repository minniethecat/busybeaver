The Festival of Fools was a popular medieval festival held annually in Paris, most notably celebrated on January 6 (the Feast of Epiphany). In Victor Hugo's *Notre-Dame de Paris* (1831), the festival serves as the novel's opening scene. During the 1482 celebration depicted in the novel, Quasimodo, the deformed bell-ringer of Notre-Dame Cathedral, is elected Pope of Fools due to his physical grotesqueness — a role that grants him temporary license and mock authority.
