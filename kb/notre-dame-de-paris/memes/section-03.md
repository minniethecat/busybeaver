---[003-001] “Hold, I know him” said one of them; “’tis Master Andry Musnier.”

[003-002] “Because he is one of the four sworn booksellers of the

[003-003] university!” said the other.

[003-004] “Everything goes by fours in that shop,” cried a third; “the four

[003-005] nations, the four faculties, the four feasts, the four

[003-006] procurators, the four electors, the four booksellers.”

[003-007] “Well,” began Jean Frollo once more, “we must play the devil with

[003-008] them.”[2]

[003-009] “Musnier, we’ll burn your books.”

[003-010] “Musnier, we’ll beat your lackeys.”

[003-011] “Musnier, we’ll kiss your wife.”

[003-012] “That fine, big Mademoiselle Oudarde.”

[003-013] “Who is as fresh and as gay as though she were a widow.”

[003-014] “Devil take you!” growled Master Andry Musnier.

[003-015] “Master Andry,” pursued Jean Jehan, still clinging to his

[003-016] capital, “hold your tongue, or I’ll drop on your head!”

[003-017] Master Andry raised his eyes, seemed to measure in an instant the

[003-018] height of the pillar, the weight of the scamp, mentally

[003-019] multiplied that weight by the square of the velocity and remained

[003-020] silent.

[003-021] Jehan, master of the field of battle, pursued triumphantly:

[003-022] “That’s what I’ll do, even if I am the brother of an archdeacon!”

[003-023] “Fine gentry are our people of the university, not to have caused

[003-024] our privileges to be respected on such a day as this! However,

[003-025] there is a maypole and a bonfire in the town; a mystery, Pope of

[003-026] the Fools, and Flemish ambassadors in the city; and, at the

[003-027] university, nothing!”

[003-028] “Nevertheless, the Place Maubert is sufficiently large!”

[003-029] interposed one of the clerks established on the window-sill.

[003-030] “Down with the rector, the electors, and the procurators!” cried

[003-031] Joannes.

[003-032] “We must have a bonfire this evening in the Champ-Gaillard,” went

[003-033] on the other, “made of Master Andry’s books.”

[003-034] “And the desks of the scribes!” added his neighbor.

[003-035] “And the beadles’ wands!”

[003-036] “And the spittoons of the deans!”

[003-037] “And the cupboards of the procurators!”

[003-038] “And the hutches of the electors!”

[003-039] “And the stools of the rector!”

[003-040] “Down with them!” put in little Jehan, as counterpoint; “down

[003-041] with Master Andry, the beadles and the scribes; the theologians,

[003-042] the doctors and the decretists; the procurators, the electors and

[003-043] the rector!”

[003-044] “The end of the world has come!” muttered Master Andry, stopping

[003-045] up his ears.

[003-046] “By the way, there’s the rector! see, he is passing through the

[003-047] Place,” cried one of those in the window.

[003-048] Each rivalled his neighbor in his haste to turn towards the

[003-049] Place.

[003-050] “Is it really our venerable rector, Master Thibaut?” demanded

[003-051] Jehan Frollo du Moulin, who, as he was clinging to one of the

[003-052] inner pillars, could not see what was going on outside.

[003-053] “Yes, yes,” replied all the others, “it is really he, Master

[003-054] Thibaut, the rector.”

[003-055] It was, in fact, the rector and all the dignitaries of the

[003-056] university, who were marching in procession in front of the

[003-057] embassy, and at that moment traversing the Place. The students

[003-058] crowded into the window, saluted them as they passed with

[003-059] sarcasms and ironical applause. The rector, who was walking at

[003-060] the head of his company, had to support the first broadside; it

[003-061] was severe.

[003-062] “Good day, monsieur le recteur! Holà hé! good day there!”

[003-063] “How does he manage to be here, the old gambler? Has he abandoned

[003-064] his dice?”

[003-065] “How he trots along on his mule! her ears are not so long as

[003-066] his!”

[003-067] “Holà hé! good day, monsieur le recteur Thibaut! _Tybalde

[003-068] aleator_! Old fool! old gambler!”

[003-069] “God preserve you! Did you throw double six often last night?”

[003-070] “Oh! what a decrepit face, livid and haggard and drawn with the

[003-071] love of gambling and of dice!”

[003-072] “Where are you bound for in that fashion, Thibaut, _Tybalde ad

[003-073] dados_, with your back turned to the university, and trotting

[003-074] towards the town?”

[003-075] “He is on his way, no doubt, to seek a lodging in the Rue

[003-076] Thibautodé?”[3] cried Jehan du M. Moulin.

[003-077] The entire band repeated this quip in a voice of thunder,

[003-078] clapping their hands furiously.

[003-079] “You are going to seek a lodging in the Rue Thibautodé, are you

[003-080] not, monsieur le recteur, gamester on the side of the devil?”

[003-081] Then came the turns of the other dignitaries.

[003-082] “Down with the beadles! down with the mace-bearers!”

[003-083] “Tell me, Robin Pouissepain, who is that yonder?”

[003-084] “He is Gilbert de Suilly, _Gilbertus de Soliaco_, the chancellor

[003-085] of the College of Autun.”

[003-086] “Hold on, here’s my shoe; you are better placed than I, fling it

[003-087] in his face.”

[003-088] “_Saturnalitias mittimus ecce nuces_.”

[003-089] “Down with the six theologians, with their white surplices!”

[003-090] “Are those the theologians? I thought they were the white geese

[003-091] given by Sainte-Geneviève to the city, for the fief of Roogny.”

[003-092] “Down with the doctors!”

[003-093] “Down with the cardinal disputations, and quibblers!”

[003-094] “My cap to you, Chancellor of Sainte-Geneviève! You have done me

[003-095] a wrong. ’Tis true; he gave my place in the nation of Normandy to

[003-096] little Ascanio Falzapada, who comes from the province of Bourges,

[003-097] since he is an Italian.”

[003-098] “That is an injustice,” said all the scholars. “Down with the

[003-099] Chancellor of Sainte-Geneviève!”

[003-100] “Ho hé! Master Joachim de Ladehors! Ho hé! Louis Dahuille! Ho hé

[003-101] Lambert Hoctement!”

[003-102] “May the devil stifle the procurator of the German nation!”

[003-103] “And the chaplains of the Sainte-Chapelle, with their gray

[003-104] _amices; cum tunices grisis_!”

[003-105] “_Seu de pellibus grisis fourratis_!”

[003-106] “Holà hé! Masters of Arts! All the beautiful black copes! all the

[003-107] fine red copes!”

[003-108] “They make a fine tail for the rector.”

[003-109] “One would say that he was a Doge of Venice on his way to his

[003-110] bridal with the sea.”

[003-111] “Say, Jehan! here are the canons of Sainte-Geneviève!”

[003-112] “To the deuce with the whole set of canons!”

[003-113] “Abbé Claude Choart! Doctor Claude Choart! Are you in search of

[003-114] Marie la Giffarde?”

[003-115] “She is in the Rue de Glatigny.”

[003-116] “She is making the bed of the king of the debauchees.”

[003-117] “She is paying her four deniers[4] _quatuor denarios_.”

[003-118] “_Aut unum bombum_.”

[003-119] “Would you like to have her pay you in the face?”

[003-120] “Comrades! Master Simon Sanguin, the Elector of Picardy, with his

[003-121] wife on the crupper!”

[003-122] “_Post equitem sedet atra cura_—behind the horseman sits black

[003-123] care.”

[003-124] “Courage, Master Simon!”

[003-125] “Good day, Mister Elector!”

[003-126] “Good night, Madame Electress!”

[003-127] “How happy they are to see all that!” sighed Joannes de

[003-128] Molendino, still perched in the foliage of his capital.

[003-129] Meanwhile, the sworn bookseller of the university, Master Andry

[003-130] Musnier, was inclining his ear to the furrier of the king’s

[003-131] robes, Master Gilles Lecornu.

[003-132] “I tell you, sir, that the end of the world has come. No one has

[003-133] ever beheld such outbreaks among the students! It is the accursed

[003-134] inventions of this century that are ruining

[003-135] everything,—artilleries, bombards, and, above all, printing, that

[003-136] other German pest. No more manuscripts, no more books! printing

[003-137] will kill bookselling. It is the end of the world that is drawing

[003-138] nigh.”

[003-139] “I see that plainly, from the progress of velvet stuffs,” said

[003-140] the fur-merchant.

[003-141] At this moment, midday sounded.

[003-142] “Ha!” exclaimed the entire crowd, in one voice.

[003-143] The scholars held their peace. Then a great hurly-burly ensued; a

[003-144] vast movement of feet, hands, and heads; a general outbreak of

[003-145] coughs and handkerchiefs; each one arranged himself, assumed his

[003-146] post, raised himself up, and grouped himself. Then came a great

[003-147] silence; all necks remained outstretched, all mouths remained

[003-148] open, all glances were directed towards the marble table. Nothing

[003-149] made its appearance there. The bailiff’s four sergeants were

[003-150] still there, stiff, motionless, as painted statues. All eyes

[003-151] turned to the estrade reserved for the Flemish envoys. The door

[003-152] remained closed, the platform empty. This crowd had been waiting

[003-153] since daybreak for three things: noonday, the embassy from

[003-154] Flanders, the mystery play. Noonday alone had arrived on time.

[003-155] On this occasion, it was too much.

[003-156] They waited one, two, three, five minutes, a quarter of an hour;

[003-157] nothing came. The dais remained empty, the theatre dumb. In the

[003-158] meantime, wrath had succeeded to impatience. Irritated words

[003-159] circulated in a low tone, still, it is true. “The mystery! the

[003-160] mystery!” they murmured, in hollow voices. Heads began to

[003-161] ferment. A tempest, which was only rumbling in the distance as

[003-162] yet, was floating on the surface of this crowd. It was Jehan du

[003-163] Moulin who struck the first spark from it.

[003-164] “The mystery, and to the devil with the Flemings!” he exclaimed

[003-165] at the full force of his lungs, twining like a serpent around his

[003-166] pillar.

[003-167] The crowd clapped their hands.

[003-168] “The mystery!” it repeated, “and may all the devils take

[003-169] Flanders!”

[003-170] “We must have the mystery instantly,” resumed the student; “or

[003-171] else, my advice is that we should hang the bailiff of the courts,

[003-172] by way of a morality and a comedy.”

[003-173] “Well said,” cried the people, “and let us begin the hanging with

[003-174] his sergeants.”

[003-175] A grand acclamation followed. The four poor fellows began to turn

[003-176] pale, and to exchange glances. The crowd hurled itself towards

[003-177] them, and they already beheld the frail wooden railing, which

[003-178] separated them from it, giving way and bending before the

[003-179] pressure of the throng.

[003-180] It was a critical moment.

[003-181] “To the sack, to the sack!” rose the cry on all sides.

[003-182] At that moment, the tapestry of the dressing-room, which we have

[003-183] described above, was raised, and afforded passage to a personage,

[003-184] the mere sight of whom suddenly stopped the crowd, and changed

[003-185] its wrath into curiosity as by enchantment.

[003-186] “Silence! silence!”

[003-187] The personage, but little reassured, and trembling in every limb,

[003-188] advanced to the edge of the marble table with a vast amount of

[003-189] bows, which, in proportion as he drew nearer, more and more

[003-190] resembled genuflections.

[003-191] In the meanwhile, tranquillity had gradually been restored. All

[003-192] that remained was that slight murmur which always rises above the

[003-193] silence of a crowd.

[003-194] “Messieurs the _bourgeois_,” said he, “and mesdemoiselles the

[003-195] _bourgeoises_, we shall have the honor of declaiming and

[003-196] representing, before his eminence, monsieur the cardinal, a very

[003-197] beautiful morality which has for its title, ‘The Good Judgment of

[003-198] Madame the Virgin Mary.’ I am to play Jupiter. His eminence is,

[003-199] at this moment, escorting the very honorable embassy of the Duke

[003-200] of Austria; which is detained, at present, listening to the

[003-201] harangue of monsieur the rector of the university, at the gate

[003-202] Baudets. As soon as his illustrious eminence, the cardinal,

[003-203] arrives, we will begin.”

[003-204] It is certain, that nothing less than the intervention of Jupiter

[003-205] was required to save the four unfortunate sergeants of the

[003-206] bailiff of the courts. If we had the happiness of having invented

[003-207] this very veracious tale, and of being, in consequence,

[003-208] responsible for it before our Lady Criticism, it is not against

[003-209] us that the classic precept, _Nec deus intersit_, could be

[003-210] invoked. Moreover, the costume of Seigneur Jupiter, was very

[003-211] handsome, and contributed not a little towards calming the crowd,

[003-212] by attracting all its attention. Jupiter was clad in a coat of

[003-213] mail, covered with black velvet, with gilt nails; and had it not

[003-214] been for the rouge, and the huge red beard, each of which covered

[003-215] one-half of his face,—had it not been for the roll of gilded

[003-216] cardboard, spangled, and all bristling with strips of tinsel,

[003-217] which he held in his hand, and in which the eyes of the initiated

[003-218] easily recognized thunderbolts,—had not his feet been

[003-219] flesh-colored, and banded with ribbons in Greek fashion, he might

[003-220] have borne comparison, so far as the severity of his mien was

[003-221] concerned, with a Breton archer from the guard of Monsieur de

[003-222] Berry.

[003-224] Nevertheless, as he harangued them, the satisfaction and

[003-225] admiration unanimously excited by his costume were dissipated by

[003-226] his words; and when he reached that untoward conclusion: “As soon

[003-227] as his illustrious eminence, the cardinal, arrives, we will

[003-228] begin,” his voice was drowned in a thunder of hooting.

[003-229] “Begin instantly! The mystery! the mystery immediately!” shrieked

[003-230] the people. And above all the voices, that of Johannes de

[003-231] Molendino was audible, piercing the uproar like the fife’s

[003-232] derisive serenade: “Commence instantly!” yelped the scholar.

[003-233] “Down with Jupiter and the Cardinal de Bourbon!” vociferated

[003-234] Robin Poussepain and the other clerks perched in the window.
