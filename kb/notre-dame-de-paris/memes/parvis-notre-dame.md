The Parvis Notre-Dame is the open square in front of Notre-Dame Cathedral in Paris. In Victor Hugo's novel, the Parvis serves as a crucial gathering place — it is where the Festival of Fools opens, where Esmeralda performs her dances, and where key public scenes unfold. The Parvis functions as a threshold space between the sacred interior of the cathedral and the profane world of medieval Paris.
