---[034-001] Now, the auditor was deaf. A slight defect in an auditor. Master

[034-002] Florian delivered judgment, none the less, without appeal and

[034-003] very suitably. It is certainly quite sufficient for a judge to

[034-004] have the air of listening; and the venerable auditor fulfilled

[034-005] this condition, the sole one in justice, all the better because

[034-006] his attention could not be distracted by any noise.

[034-007] Moreover, he had in the audience, a pitiless censor of his deeds

[034-008] and gestures, in the person of our friend Jehan Frollo du Moulin,

[034-009] that little student of yesterday, that “stroller,” whom one was

[034-010] sure of encountering all over Paris, anywhere except before the

[034-011] rostrums of the professors.

[034-012] “Stay,” he said in a low tone to his companion, Robin Poussepain,

[034-013] who was grinning at his side, while he was making his comments on

[034-014] the scenes which were being unfolded before his eyes, “yonder is

[034-015] Jehanneton du Buisson. The beautiful daughter of the lazy dog at

[034-016] the Marché-Neuf!—Upon my soul, he is condemning her, the old

[034-017] rascal! he has no more eyes than ears. Fifteen sous, four

[034-018] farthings, parisian, for having worn two rosaries! ’Tis somewhat

[034-019] dear. _Lex duri carminis_. Who’s that? Robin Chief-de-Ville,

[034-020] hauberkmaker. For having been passed and received master of the

[034-021] said trade! That’s his entrance money. He! two gentlemen among

[034-022] these knaves! Aiglet de Soins, Hutin de Mailly Two equerries,

[034-023] _Corpus Christi!_ Ah! they have been playing at dice. When shall

[034-024] I see our rector here? A hundred livres parisian, fine to the

[034-025] king! That Barbedienne strikes like a deaf man,—as he is! I’ll be

[034-026] my brother the archdeacon, if that keeps me from gaming; gaming

[034-027] by day, gaming by night, living at play, dying at play, and

[034-028] gaming away my soul after my shirt. Holy Virgin, what damsels!

[034-029] One after the other my lambs. Ambroise Lécuyère, Isabeau la

[034-030] Paynette, Bérarde Gironin! I know them all, by Heavens! A fine! a

[034-031] fine! That’s what will teach you to wear gilded girdles! ten sous

[034-032] parisis! you coquettes! Oh! the old snout of a judge! deaf and

[034-033] imbecile! Oh! Florian the dolt! Oh! Barbedienne the blockhead!

[034-034] There he is at the table! He’s eating the plaintiff, he’s eating

[034-035] the suits, he eats, he chews, he crams, he fills himself. Fines,

[034-036] lost goods, taxes, expenses, loyal charges, salaries, damages,

[034-037] and interests, gehenna, prison, and jail, and fetters with

[034-038] expenses are Christmas spice cake and marchpanes of Saint-John to

[034-039] him! Look at him, the pig!—Come! Good! Another amorous woman!

[034-040] Thibaud-la-Thibaude, neither more nor less! For having come from

[034-041] the Rue Glatigny! What fellow is this? Gieffroy Mabonne, gendarme

[034-042] bearing the crossbow. He has cursed the name of the Father. A

[034-043] fine for la Thibaude! A fine for Gieffroy! A fine for them both!

[034-044] The deaf old fool! he must have mixed up the two cases! Ten to

[034-045] one that he makes the wench pay for the oath and the gendarme for

[034-046] the amour! Attention, Robin Poussepain! What are they going to

[034-047] bring in? Here are many sergeants! By Jupiter! all the

[034-048] bloodhounds of the pack are there. It must be the great beast of

[034-049] the hunt—a wild boar. And ’tis one, Robin, ’tis one. And a fine

[034-050] one too! _Hercle!_ ’tis our prince of yesterday, our Pope of the

[034-051] Fools, our bellringer, our one-eyed man, our hunchback, our

[034-052] grimace! ’Tis Quasimodo!”

[034-053] It was he indeed.

[034-054] It was Quasimodo, bound, encircled, roped, pinioned, and under

[034-055] good guard. The squad of policemen who surrounded him was

[034-056] assisted by the chevalier of the watch in person, wearing the

[034-057] arms of France embroidered on his breast, and the arms of the

[034-058] city on his back. There was nothing, however, about Quasimodo,

[034-059] except his deformity, which could justify the display of halberds

[034-060] and arquebuses; he was gloomy, silent, and tranquil. Only now and

[034-061] then did his single eye cast a sly and wrathful glance upon the

[034-062] bonds with which he was loaded.

[034-063] He cast the same glance about him, but it was so dull and sleepy

[034-064] that the women only pointed him out to each other in derision.

[034-065] Meanwhile Master Florian, the auditor, turned over attentively

[034-066] the document in the complaint entered against Quasimodo, which

[034-067] the clerk handed him, and, having thus glanced at it, appeared to

[034-068] reflect for a moment. Thanks to this precaution, which he always

[034-069] was careful to take at the moment when on the point of beginning

[034-070] an examination, he knew beforehand the names, titles, and

[034-071] misdeeds of the accused, made cut and dried responses to

[034-072] questions foreseen, and succeeded in extricating himself from all

[034-073] the windings of the interrogation without allowing his deafness

[034-074] to be too apparent. The written charges were to him what the dog

[034-075] is to the blind man. If his deafness did happen to betray him

[034-076] here and there, by some incoherent apostrophe or some

[034-077] unintelligible question, it passed for profundity with some, and

[034-078] for imbecility with others. In neither case did the honor of the

[034-079] magistracy sustain any injury; for it is far better that a judge

[034-080] should be reputed imbecile or profound than deaf. Hence he took

[034-081] great care to conceal his deafness from the eyes of all, and he

[034-082] generally succeeded so well that he had reached the point of

[034-083] deluding himself, which is, by the way, easier than is supposed.

[034-084] All hunchbacks walk with their heads held high, all stutterers

[034-085] harangue, all deaf people speak low. As for him, he believed, at

[034-086] the most, that his ear was a little refractory. It was the sole

[034-087] concession which he made on this point to public opinion, in his

[034-088] moments of frankness and examination of his conscience.

[034-089] Having, then, thoroughly ruminated Quasimodo’s affair, he threw

[034-090] back his head and half closed his eyes, for the sake of more

[034-091] majesty and impartiality, so that, at that moment, he was both

[034-092] deaf and blind. A double condition, without which no judge is

[034-093] perfect. It was in this magisterial attitude that he began the

[034-094] examination.

[034-095] “Your name?”

[034-096] Now this was a case which had not been “provided for by law,”

[034-097] where a deaf man should be obliged to question a deaf man.

[034-098] Quasimodo, whom nothing warned that a question had been addressed

[034-099] to him, continued to stare intently at the judge, and made no

[034-100] reply. The judge, being deaf, and being in no way warned of the

[034-101] deafness of the accused, thought that the latter had answered, as

[034-102] all accused do in general, and therefore he pursued, with his

[034-103] mechanical and stupid self-possession,—

[034-104] “Very well. And your age?”

[034-105] Again Quasimodo made no reply to this question. The judge

[034-106] supposed that it had been replied to, and continued,—

[034-107] “Now, your profession?”

[034-108] Still the same silence. The spectators had begun, meanwhile, to

[034-109] whisper together, and to exchange glances.

[034-110] “That will do,” went on the imperturbable auditor, when he

[034-111] supposed that the accused had finished his third reply. “You are

[034-112] accused before us, _primo_, of nocturnal disturbance; _secundo_,

[034-113] of a dishonorable act of violence upon the person of a foolish

[034-114] woman, _in præjudicium meretricis; tertio_, of rebellion and

[034-115] disloyalty towards the archers of the police of our lord, the

[034-116] king. Explain yourself upon all these points.—Clerk, have you

[034-117] written down what the prisoner has said thus far?”

[034-118] At this unlucky question, a burst of laughter rose from the

[034-119] clerk’s table caught by the audience, so violent, so wild, so

[034-120] contagious, so universal, that the two deaf men were forced to

[034-121] perceive it. Quasimodo turned round, shrugging his hump with

[034-122] disdain, while Master Florian, equally astonished, and supposing

[034-123] that the laughter of the spectators had been provoked by some

[034-124] irreverent reply from the accused, rendered visible to him by

[034-125] that shrug of the shoulders, apostrophized him indignantly,—

[034-126] “You have uttered a reply, knave, which deserves the halter. Do

[034-127] you know to whom you are speaking?”

[034-128] This sally was not fitted to arrest the explosion of general

[034-129] merriment. It struck all as so whimsical, and so ridiculous, that

[034-130] the wild laughter even attacked the sergeants of the

[034-131] Parloi-aux-Bourgeois, a sort of pikemen, whose stupidity was part

[034-132] of their uniform. Quasimodo alone preserved his seriousness, for

[034-133] the good reason that he understood nothing of what was going on

[034-134] around him. The judge, more and more irritated, thought it his

[034-135] duty to continue in the same tone, hoping thereby to strike the

[034-136] accused with a terror which should react upon the audience, and

[034-137] bring it back to respect.

[034-138] “So this is as much as to say, perverse and thieving knave that

[034-139] you are, that you permit yourself to be lacking in respect

[034-140] towards the Auditor of the Châtelet, to the magistrate committed

[034-141] to the popular police of Paris, charged with searching out

[034-142] crimes, delinquencies, and evil conduct; with controlling all

[034-143] trades, and interdicting monopoly; with maintaining the

[034-144] pavements; with debarring the hucksters of chickens, poultry, and

[034-145] water-fowl; of superintending the measuring of fagots and other

[034-146] sorts of wood; of purging the city of mud, and the air of

[034-147] contagious maladies; in a word, with attending continually to

[034-148] public affairs, without wages or hope of salary! Do you know that

[034-149] I am called Florian Barbedienne, actual lieutenant to monsieur

[034-150] the provost, and, moreover, commissioner, inquisitor,

[034-151] _controller_, and examiner, with equal power in provostship,

[034-152] bailiwick, preservation, and inferior court of judicature?—”

[034-153] There is no reason why a deaf man talking to a deaf man should

[034-154] stop. God knows where and when Master Florian would have landed,

[034-155] when thus launched at full speed in lofty eloquence, if the low

[034-156] door at the extreme end of the room had not suddenly opened, and

[034-157] given entrance to the provost in person. At his entrance Master

[034-158] Florian did not stop short, but, making a half-turn on his heels,

[034-159] and aiming at the provost the harangue with which he had been

[034-160] withering Quasimodo a moment before,—

[034-161] “Monseigneur,” said he, “I demand such penalty as you shall deem

[034-162] fitting against the prisoner here present, for grave and

[034-163] aggravated offence against the court.”

[034-164] And he seated himself, utterly breathless, wiping away the great

[034-165] drops of sweat which fell from his brow and drenched, like tears,

[034-166] the parchments spread out before him. Messire Robert

[034-167] d’Estouteville frowned and made a gesture so imperious and

[034-168] significant to Quasimodo, that the deaf man in some measure

[034-169] understood it.

[034-170] The provost addressed him with severity, “What have you done that

[034-171] you have been brought hither, knave?”

[034-172] The poor fellow, supposing that the provost was asking his name,

[034-173] broke the silence which he habitually preserved, and replied, in

[034-174] a harsh and guttural voice, “Quasimodo.”

[034-175] The reply matched the question so little that the wild laugh

[034-176] began to circulate once more, and Messire Robert exclaimed, red

[034-177] with wrath,—

[034-178] “Are you mocking me also, you arrant knave?”

[034-179] “Bellringer of Notre-Dame,” replied Quasimodo, supposing that

[034-180] what was required of him was to explain to the judge who he was.

[034-181] “Bellringer!” interpolated the provost, who had waked up early

[034-182] enough to be in a sufficiently bad temper, as we have said, not

[034-183] to require to have his fury inflamed by such strange responses.

[034-184] “Bellringer! I’ll play you a chime of rods on your back through

[034-185] the squares of Paris! Do you hear, knave?”

[034-186] “If it is my age that you wish to know,” said Quasimodo, “I think

[034-187] that I shall be twenty at Saint Martin’s day.”

[034-188] This was too much; the provost could no longer restrain himself.

[034-189] “Ah! you are scoffing at the provostship, wretch! Messieurs the

[034-190] sergeants of the mace, you will take me this knave to the pillory

[034-191] of the Grève, you will flog him, and turn him for an hour. He

[034-192] shall pay me for it, _tête Dieu!_ And I order that the present

[034-193] judgment shall be cried, with the assistance of four sworn

[034-194] trumpeters, in the seven castellanies of the viscomty of Paris.”

[034-195] The clerk set to work incontinently to draw up the account of the

[034-196] sentence.

[034-197] “_Ventre Dieu!_ ’tis well adjudged!” cried the little scholar,

[034-198] Jehan Frollo du Moulin, from his corner.

[034-199] The provost turned and fixed his flashing eyes once more on

[034-200] Quasimodo. “I believe the knave said ‘_Ventre Dieu!_’ Clerk, add

[034-201] twelve deniers Parisian for the oath, and let the vestry of Saint

[034-202] Eustache have the half of it; I have a particular devotion for

[034-203] Saint Eustache.”
