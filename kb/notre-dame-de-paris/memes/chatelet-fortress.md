The Grand Châtelet was a fortress and prison in medieval Paris, located on the Right Bank of the Seine at the entrance to the Pont au Change. It served as the seat of the Provost of Paris and housed the city's criminal courts. In Victor Hugo's *Notre-Dame de Paris*, the Châtelet appears as a symbol of royal judicial authority and a site of punishment — notably connected to the trial and torture scenes in the novel.
