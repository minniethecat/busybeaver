# Justice and Law in Notre-Dame de Paris

Hugo's novel is a sustained critique of medieval justice — arbitrary,
theocratic, and cruel. From Esmeralda's show-trial to the hidden sanctuary
of Notre-Dame, the theme of law versus justice runs through every book.
This page collects all wiki entries touching on these themes.

## Notes

Hugo structures much of the novel's second half around the machinery of
medieval justice: arrest, trial, torture, sanctuary, and execution. The
Parlement of Paris and the ecclesiastical courts represent a system that
Hugo saw as both barbaric and irredeemably corrupt.
