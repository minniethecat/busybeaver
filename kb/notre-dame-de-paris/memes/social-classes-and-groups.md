# Social Classes and Groups

Hugo's 1482 Paris is a society of rigid hierarchies — clergy, nobility,
bourgeoisie, and the vast underworld of truands and outcasts. This page
indexes all wiki entries that illuminate the novel's social landscape.

## Notes

Hugo's social vision in Notre-Dame de Paris anticipates the concerns of *Les
Misérables* — the outcast, the marginalized, and the unjustly condemned occupy
the moral center of both works. The Court of Miracles chapters (Book II) are
among the novel's most celebrated set-pieces.
