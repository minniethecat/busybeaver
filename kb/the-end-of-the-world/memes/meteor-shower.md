# Meteor Shower

**Meteor showers** — in Dennis's dual vision of [[creation|cosmic origin]]s and cosmic endings — occupy a peculiar space: they are at once the **building-blocks of worlds** (in the planetesimal theory) and **potential destroyers** of worlds (as ongoing bombardment). Dennis devotes an entire chapter to the mode of world-ending by collision, and meteor showers are the most continuous, pervasive form of that threat.

## Overview

A meteor shower occurs when Earth passes through a stream of debris left by a comet or asteroid — particles burning up in the atmosphere as "[[meteor-threat|shooting star]]s." Major showers (Perseids, Leonids, Geminids) recur annually and are harmless. But in Dennis's treatment, the distinction between a harmless meteor shower and a world-ending bombardment is a matter of **scale, not kind** — millions of meteors rush hourly upon the Earth, and if the swarm were denser or the particles larger, the result would be [[death|extinction]].

## In the Book

### Context

Meteors and meteor showers appear primarily in the opening chapters: Chapter 1 ("How?") where the **meteoric theory of origin** (planets formed by progressive agglomeration of meteor-swarms) sets up the symmetry that what builds worlds can also unbuild them, and Chapter 7 ("Crash") where Dennis considers collision as a mode of world-ending.

### Dennis's Treatment

Dennis begins with origins. Among the "seven chief guesses" for how the world began, the meteoric theory holds that "millions of meteor-swarms moved nearer together, by progressive agglomeration and concretion formed themselves into solid earths" [001-006]. This is crucial: if the Earth was built by meteors, it can be **un-built** by them.

> Millions of meteors rush hourly upon him, their united impact a force to reckon with. [006-016]

In Chapter 6 ("[[cold|Cold]]"), Dennis calculates the continuous meteor bombardment and finds it wanting as a heat source — "the meteors are paltry helpers" [006-017] — but the same passage establishes the **ubiquity of the threat**: Earth is under constant cosmic bombardment, and it is only the thinness of the present meteor stream that keeps us safe.

> Nor with a meteor. No bolide or shooting-star ever brushed the earth, but some body far huger. [007-004]

In Chapter 7, Dennis shifts from harmless meteor showers to the **collision scenario**: a meteor shower is the warning, a collision with a larger body the execution.

## Significance

Meteor showers serve Dennis as a **bridge concept** between the ordinary and the apocalyptic. Every [[meteor-threat|shooting star]] is a reminder that the Earth moves through a cosmic shooting gallery. The meteoric theory of origin makes the threat particularly elegant: the same process that accreted the Earth from dust and debris could, if the debris were denser or moving faster, reduce it to dust again. This is the **cosmic symmetry** that runs through Dennis's book — every force of creation is also a potential force of [[perish|destruction]].
