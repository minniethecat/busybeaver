# Volcano

**Volcanoes** — in Dennis's geology of [[fate|doom]] — are the **surface vents of the planet's molten interior**, the visible proof that "we are still a gaseous globe, with a solid crust much thinner in proportion than eggshell to egg" [003-025]. A single eruption can bury a city; the simultaneous eruption of all volcanoes would end the world.

## Overview

A volcano is a rupture in the Earth's crust that allows molten rock, ash, and gases to escape from below the surface. Volcanic eruptions range from gentle lava flows to explosive events that can alter global climate for years. In Dennis's treatment, volcanoes are not isolated geological curiosities but **symptoms of a systemic condition**: the Earth's interior is hot, pressurized, and unstable — and the crust that separates us from that inferno is desperately thin.

## In the Book

### Context

Volcanoes appear primarily in Chapter 3 ("[[fire|Fire]]"), where Dennis explores the possibility that the Earth's internal heat — rather than the Sun's — will be the agent of world-ending. The key passage [003-025] describes the terrifying physics: every fifty feet downward adds one degree of temperature, and just two miles down the rock is at the boiling point of water.

### Dennis's Treatment

Dennis's volcanic scenario is a **cascade failure**: not one volcano but all of them. The planet's internal heat, "which, any hour, may renew its ancient zeal" [003-025], needs only the old channels to the old outlets:

> Forth of a thousand reawakened volcanoes, from the lowliest to the highest, from Cosima to Cotopaxi or the Sahama, burning matter, tumults of lava, destroying all before them. [003-025]

The scale is staggering: "In three millenaries seventeen million people have died plutonically; all the seventeen hundred million people on the earth today may so perish in three minutes" [003-025]. A single eruption is a tragedy; the synchronized reawakening of every volcano on Earth is [[death|extinction]].

Dennis also invokes volcanoes historically. Vesuvius, which buried Herculaneum and Pompeii in AD 79, serves as a reminder that volcanic [[disaster|catastrophe]] is not hypothetical — it has happened, repeatedly, and it will happen again:

> Earth trembled and reduced Philadelphia to ashes; under his ashes Vesuvius buried Herculaneum and Pompeii. [011-010]

## Significance

Volcanoes serve Dennis as the **most direct evidence** that the Earth contains within itself the means of its own [[perish|destruction]]. Unlike comets or colliding galaxies, which come from outside, the volcanic threat is **endogenous** — it is the Earth's own heat, the Earth's own instability, the Earth's own thin crust that make death-by-fire not merely possible but, on a long enough timescale, inevitable.

The volcanic scenario also illustrates Dennis's method: take a familiar, localized [[catastrophe|disaster]] (a volcanic eruption), calculate the physics behind it (internal heat, thin crust, pressure seeking release), and **extrapolate to the global scale**. What kills seventeen million over three millennia could, with a shift in conditions, kill seventeen hundred million in three minutes.
