Mount Zion is the hill in Jerusalem sacred to Judaism and Christianity, appearing in Dennis's book in two charged contexts: the Roman destruction of Jerusalem as a type of the Final Judgment, and the environmental degradation of the Holy Land as evidence of irreversible terrestrial change.

## Location

Mount Zion is the southeastern hill of Jerusalem, synonymous in biblical usage with the city itself and the Temple Mount. In the book, it is referenced alongside the Temple of Solomon and the broader "chosen land"（011-010）.

## In the Book

### Context

Zion appears in "Drought"（005）and "This Year"（011）. The first reference is ecological — the desertification of the Holy Land as evidence that once-fertile regions can become permanently barren. The second is historical-apocalyptic — the Roman siege of Jerusalem as a foreshadowing of cosmic destruction.

### Dennis's Treatment

Dennis deploys Zion as a layered symbol. Ecologically, he observes that "in the Holiest Land not all the gold of Zionism will make the milk and honey flow again" — the Sahara has encroached on what was once Rome's granary, and no human effort can reverse such geological-scale change（005-032）. Historically, he recounts the Roman destruction of Jerusalem in harrowing detail: the siege where "without the walls was death and within them was death," culminating in the Temple's burning and Mount Zion delivered "to the sword and the vulture"（011-010）. This historical catastrophe serves as a prefiguration of the ultimate Fire-End.

> In the Holiest Land not all the gold of Zionism will make the milk and honey flow again（005-032）

> the Temple of Solomon delivered at last to the flames and Mount Zion to the sword and the vulture（011-010）

## Significance

Zion operates at two symbolic levels in the book. As a place, its desertification demonstrates that even sacred geography is subject to irreversible environmental change — a microcosm of the Earth's larger trajectory toward collapse. As a historical event, the destruction of Jerusalem provides Dennis with a template for the Final Catastrophe: a city besieged, a temple burned, a people scattered.
