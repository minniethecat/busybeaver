# Albert Einstein

The German-born theoretical physicist whose general theory of relativity (1915) provided the framework for the modern cosmological model — a finite, curved universe — that Dennis uses to foreclose the possibility of an infinite cosmos immune to thermodynamic death.

## Identity

Albert Einstein (1879–1955) revolutionized physics with his theories of special relativity (1905) and general relativity (1915). For Dennis, Einstein's importance lies not in relativity per se but in the cosmological model that followed from it: a universe that is finite but unbounded — curved back upon itself so that a beam of light travelling long enough would return to its starting point. "Einstein's a cylinder, space bending back on itself" (017-008). This model, developed in 1917, provided the mathematical foundation for the argument that the universe is a closed thermodynamic system and therefore subject to entropy's irreversible arrow.

## In the Book

### Appearances

Einstein appears briefly but decisively in Dennis's argument. In Chapter 10 ("This Year"), Dennis references "post-Einsteinian space-time" (010-113) as the modern framework within which any discussion of cosmic chronology must operate. In Chapter 12 ("Some Time"), Einstein appears alongside other philosophers of time — "Bergson, Bolzano; Einstein, Minkowski; Poincaré, Palágyi" (012-004) — in a catalogue of thinkers who have reshaped the understanding of temporality. But the crucial appearance is in Chapter 17 ("What After?"), where Dennis contrasts Einstein's cylindrical universe with de Sitter's spherical one as two variants of the same essential insight: space is finite.

### Role in Dennis's Argument

Einstein serves as a scientific authority whose work closes off an escape route. The argument that an infinite universe could evade entropy is refuted not by abstract reasoning but by appeal to the best physics of Dennis's day. Einstein — along with de Sitter, Eddington, and the other architects of modern cosmology — has shown that the universe is not infinite. And if the universe is not infinite, entropy applies to the whole.

> Einstein's a cylinder, space bending back on itself. (017-008)

Dennis's deployment of Einstein is characteristically compressed. He does not explain general relativity or derive the cosmological constant; he invokes the name as a shorthand for a settled scientific consensus. This is, in a sense, the role that Einstein plays throughout the book's genre: the scientist whose authority is so secure that his conclusions can be stated rather than argued.

## Significance

Einstein represents scientific modernity in Dennis's argument — the figure whose work makes it impossible to sustain pre-modern assumptions about an eternal or infinite cosmos. He is not a character in the book's drama but the author of its stage: the finite universe in which all the book's catastrophes unfold is, in important respects, Einstein's universe.
