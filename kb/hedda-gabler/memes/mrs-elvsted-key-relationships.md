# Mrs. Elvsted — Key Relationships

### Hedda Gabler (former bully, present rival)

Hedda tormented Thea at school — pulling her hair and threatening to "burn it off." Tesman's warm greeting (004-287) contrasts with Hedda's cold formality, and Thea's fear triggers Hedda's predatory curiosity.

Hedda pretends friendship to extract information about Lovborg (005-013), then uses it to destroy him. Thea never fully understands how deeply Hedda has manipulated her — which makes her final triumph, reconstructing the manuscript unaware of Hedda's suicide, all the more poignant.

### Eilert Lovborg (collaborator, love)

Thea followed Lovborg from the provinces, organized his notes, and supported his sobriety. When he claims to have destroyed the manuscript, her cry — "That is our child!" — is the play's most devastating line. Her grief is maternal and creative, and her ability to reconstruct the work from notes makes her the play's agent of continuity.

### George Tesman (future collaborator)

In the closing moments, Thea and Tesman sit together rebuilding Lovborg's manuscript from her notes. Tesman lost his academic rival and Thea lost her creative partner; together they find a shared purpose in preservation while the drama plays out its lethal logic elsewhere.
