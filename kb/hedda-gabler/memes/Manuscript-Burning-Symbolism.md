# Manuscript Burning Symbolism

The burning of [[Eilert-Lovborg|Lovborg]]'s manuscript operates on multiple symbolic levels that make it the play's central act of destruction.

## The Child Metaphor

The manuscript is repeatedly called a "child." Lovborg calls it "our child" (013-126) when speaking of [[Mrs-Elvsted|Thea]]; [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]] echoes the phrase mockingly: "Now I am burning your child, Thea!" (013-125). The metaphor operates on four levels: creative (the product of intellectual union between Lovborg and Thea), biological (Hedda is pregnant with a child she does not want, and destroys another woman's), moral (destroying a "child" is the ultimate transgression), and ironic (Hedda, who refused to bear children for Tesman, becomes the destroyer of children).

## Fire and the Stove

[[The-Stove|The stove]] is the domestic hearth transformed into an instrument of destruction — the same fire that should warm the home becomes the means of annihilation. Fire recurs throughout the play as Hedda's element: she is associated with burning (her threat to "burn off" Thea's hair), heat (her complaint about the open glass door), and finally this literal act of incineration. The stove, like the [[Heddas-Pistols|pistols]], is a domestic object weaponized — the home itself turned against creation.

> HEDDA: [Throws one of the quires into the fire and whispers to herself.] Now I
> am burning your child, Thea!--Burning it, curly-locks! [Throwing one
> or two more quires into the stove.] Your child and Eilert Lovborg's.
>
> (013-127)

## The Inner Room

Ibsen sets the burning in [[The-Inner-Room|the inner room]] — the liminal space between the public drawing-room and the private back room where Hedda will later shoot herself. It is the space of the play's darkest acts, visible but not fully accessible to the audience. The burning happens in this half-seen space, making it simultaneously intimate and inaccessible. Like Tesman, the audience knows what is happening but cannot see it — they can only witness the aftermath, the stove glowing with consumed pages.
