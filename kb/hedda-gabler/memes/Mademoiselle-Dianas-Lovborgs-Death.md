# Mademoiselle Diana's: Lovborg's Death

[[Eilert-Lovborg|Lovborg]] returns to Mademoiselle Diana's after [[The-Bachelor-Party|the disastrous party]] at [[Judge-Brack|Brack]]'s. He is already drunk and desperate, having lost [[The-Manuscript|his manuscript]]. According to Brack's report, Lovborg accused the women of stealing a "lost purse" — which never existed — provoked a violent confrontation, and was shot.

The death is ugly, squalid, and public: the precise antithesis of the "vine leaves in his hair" suicide [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]] had envisioned. The fatal injury occurs "in Mademoiselle Diana's boudoir" (014-362) — not a battlefield, not a study, not a drawing-room, but the most intimate and degraded space available.

When Hedda learns the details, her first question is aesthetic: "Was it — beautiful?" (014-090). Brack's clinical answer — "The shot has hit him in a vital part ... the stomach" (014-106) — destroys the fantasy. Hedda immediately revises: "In the breast. I say in the breast" (014-120). This is not confusion but aesthetic correction. The gut-shot is ugly, accidental, degrading.

> Eilert Lovborg was found shot in--in Mademoiselle Diana's boudoir.
>
> (014-361)

The breast-shot is clean, intentional, beautiful. Hedda cannot accept the death that happened, so she rewrites it.

The death at Diana's is the play's most devastating dramatic irony. Hedda gave Lovborg her [[Heddas-Pistols|pistol]] and instructed him to "do it beautifully." He did not. He lost the pistol in a brawl at a brothel and was shot with it in the stomach. The instrument of the beautiful death became a piece of police evidence.

Brack uses this knowledge to control Hedda, knowing that the pistol can be traced — and her role in the scandal exposed.
