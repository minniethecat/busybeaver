# Burning as Infanticide

When Hedda burns the manuscript in [[The-Stove|the stove]], she does not merely destroy a document. She destroys a child — and she knows it. Her whispered incantation — "Now I am burning your child, Thea! — Burning it, curly-locks! Your child and Eilert Lovborg's. I am burning — I am burning your child" [013-125/128] — is the play's most compressed act of violence.

> *[Throws one of the quires into the fire and whispers to herself.]* Now I am burning your child, Thea!--Burning it, curly-locks! *[Throwing one or two more quires into the stove.]* Your child and Eilert Lovborg's. *[Throws the rest in.]* I am burning--I am burning your child.
>
> (013-125)

The repetition of "burning" (four times in a single speech) and the direct address to the absent Thea reveal the act's true target: not the paper but the creative relationship it embodies. The act is infanticide in multiple registers.

At the creative level, the manuscript is the product of Lovborg and Thea's intellectual union — burning it destroys what they made together. At the competitive level, Hedda destroys Thea's "child" because her own pregnancy is a source of shame, not pride. At the symbolic level, the stove — the domestic hearth — becomes an instrument of destruction, the home weaponized against creation.

At the psychological level, the burning is a displaced attack on Hedda's own pregnancy. The rage she cannot direct at the child in her body she directs at the child on the page. The page-by-page method is crucial: Hedda does not throw the whole manuscript at once. She savors each page's destruction, drawing out the act into a ritual.

The act is also perversely creative. In a world where Hedda has no productive outlet — no profession, no creative work, no acknowledged power — destruction becomes her only medium. She cannot write a manuscript, so she burns one. She cannot love the child she carries, so she destroys the child another woman created. The burning is her signature: the mark of a woman whose will exceeds her possibilities.
