# The Inner Room

The inner room of the [[Tesman-Villa]] is a secondary chamber located upstage, separated from the drawing-room by a curtained doorway. It is where characters go to escape [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]]'s gaze, and where the play's most consequential acts — the burning of [[The-Manuscript|the manuscript]], its reconstruction, and Hedda's suicide — unfold.

For a detailed physical survey, see [[Inner-Room-Physical-Description]]. Its multiple dramatic roles — passage, retreat, conspiracy, and crime scene — are examined in [[Inner-Room-Dramatic-Functions]].

The spatial binary between drawing-room performance and inner-room secrecy is explored in [[Inner-Room-Symbolic-Space]], while [[Inner-Room-Ibsen-Liminal-Space]] examines Ibsen's use of liminal architecture. A scene-by-scene account appears in [[Inner-Room-Key-Scenes]].

The inner room connects to [[Mademoiselle-Dianas]] as its symbolic twin — both are off-stage spaces where the "real" action happens, both are narrated rather than shown, and both are sites of destruction. Together they bracket the play's world: destruction at home and destruction in the world, separate in space, identical in consequence.
