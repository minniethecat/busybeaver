# Mrs. Elvsted — Character Traits

**Courage through love**: Unlike Hedda, whose courage is performative, Thea's is real. She left a loveless marriage to an elderly sheriff, defied social convention, and followed Lovborg to the city — risking everything.

**Creative partnership**: She describes herself as Lovborg's "inspirer" — but her role is substantive, not ornamental. She preserved his notes, understands his method, and can reconstruct the work from fragments. She is not a passive muse but the collaborator who makes genius possible.

**Naivety without weakness**: Thea is innocent of Hedda's machinations but not stupid. She perceives Lovborg's danger when others don't and acts decisively when he disappears.

**Emotional transparency**: Where Hedda dissembles, Thea weeps openly. Her vulnerability is her strength — it disarms Lovborg's self-destruction in ways Hedda's coldness never could (005-013). She is the only character genuinely honest about what she wants and fears.
