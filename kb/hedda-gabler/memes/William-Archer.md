William Archer (1856–1924) was a British drama critic and translator who played a pivotal role in introducing Henrik Ibsen's work to English-speaking audiences. With Edmund Gosse, he produced the first English translation of *Hedda Gabler* directly from the original Norwegian. (001-004)

## Translation and Introduction

Archer wrote the introduction to the English edition of *Hedda Gabler*, providing British readers with the critical and biographical context necessary to understand Ibsen's most challenging play. (001-005) His translation, made in collaboration with Edmund Gosse, was the first to render an Ibsen play directly from Norwegian rather than through a German intermediary. (001-080)

> Introduction by William Archer
>
> (001-005)

> This was the first of Ibsen's plays to be translated from proof-sheets and published in England and America almost simultaneously with its first appearance in Scandinavia.
>
> (001-080–001-081)

## Role in Ibsen's English Reception

Archer was a central figure in the popularisation of Ibsen in England, translating and championing the Norwegian playwright's work at a time when it was considered controversial and avant-garde. (001-096)
