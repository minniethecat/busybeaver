[005-001]Yes, yes--of course it is. Well then, I must tell you--if you don't

[005-002]already know--that [[Eilert-Lovborg|Eilert Lovborg]] is in town, too.

[005-003]HEDDA.

[005-004]Lovborg--!

[005-005]TESMAN.

[005-006]What! Has Eilert Lovborg come back? Fancy that, Hedda!

[005-007]HEDDA.

[005-008]Well well--I hear it.

[005-009]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-010]He has been here a week already. Just fancy--a whole week! In this

[005-011]terrible town, alone! With so many temptations on all sides.

[005-012]HEDDA.

[005-013]But, my dear [[Mrs-Elvsted|Mrs. Elvsted]]--how does he concern you so much?

[005-014]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-015][Looks at her with a startled air, and says rapidly.] He was the

[005-016]children's tutor.

[005-017]HEDDA.

[005-018]Your children's?

[005-019]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-020]My husband's. I have none.

[005-021]HEDDA.

[005-022]Your step-children's, then?

[005-023]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-024]Yes.

[005-025]TESMAN.

[005-026][Somewhat hesitatingly.] Then was he--I don't know how to express

[005-027]it--was he--regular enough in his habits to be fit for the post? Eh?

[005-028]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-029]For the last two years his conduct has been irreproachable.

[005-030]TESMAN.

[005-031]Has it indeed? Fancy that, Hedda!

[005-032]HEDDA.

[005-033]I hear it.

[005-034]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-035]Perfectly irreproachable, I assure you! In every respect. But all the

[005-036]same--now that I know he is here--in this great town--and with a large

[005-037]sum of money in his hands--I can't help being in mortal fear for him.

[005-038]TESMAN.

[005-039]Why did he not remain where he was? With you and your husband? Eh?

[005-040]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-041]After his book was published he was too restless and unsettled to remain

[005-042]with us.

[005-043]TESMAN.

[005-044]Yes, by-the-bye, Aunt Julia told me he had published a new book.

[005-045]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-046]Yes, a big book, dealing with the march of civilisation--in broad

[005-047]outline, as it were. It came out about a fortnight ago. And since it has

[005-048]sold so well, and been so much read--and made such a sensation--

[005-049]TESMAN.

[005-050]Has it indeed? It must be something he has had lying by since his better

[005-051]days.

[005-052]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-053]Long ago, you mean?

[005-054]TESMAN.

[005-055]Yes.

[005-056]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-057]No, he has written it all since he has been with us--within the last

[005-058]year.

[005-059]TESMAN.

[005-060]Isn't that good news, Hedda? Think of that.

[005-061]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-062]Ah yes, if only it would last!

[005-063]HEDDA.

[005-064]Have you seen him here in town?

[005-065]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-066]No, not yet. I have had the greatest difficulty in finding out his

[005-067]address. But this morning I discovered it at last.

[005-068]HEDDA.

[005-069][Looks searchingly at her.] Do you know, it seems to me a little odd of

[005-070]your husband--h'm--

[005-071]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-072][Starting nervously.] Of my husband! What?

[005-073]HEDDA.

[005-074]That he should send you to town on such an errand--that he does not come

[005-075]himself and look after his friend.

[005-076]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-077]Oh no, no--my husband has no time. And besides, I--I had some shopping

[005-078]to do.

[005-079]HEDDA.

[005-080][With a slight smile.] Ah, that is a different matter.

[005-081]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-082][Rising quickly and uneasily.] And now I beg and implore you, Mr.

[005-083]Tesman--receive Eilert Lovborg kindly if he comes to you! And that he

[005-084]is sure to do. You see you were such great friends in the old days.

[005-085]And then you are interested in the same studies--the same branch of

[005-086]science--so far as I can understand.

[005-087]TESMAN.

[005-088]We used to be at any rate.

[005-089]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-090]That is why I beg so earnestly that you--you too--will keep a sharp eye

[005-091]upon him. Oh, you will promise me that, Mr. Tesman--won't you?

[005-092]TESMAN.

[005-093]With the greatest of pleasure, Mrs. Rysing--

[005-094]HEDDA.

[005-095]Elvsted.

[005-096]TESMAN.

[005-097]I assure you I shall do all I possibly can for Eilert. You may rely upon

[005-098]me.

[005-099]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-100]Oh, how very, very kind of you! [Presses his hands.] Thanks, thanks,

[005-101]thanks! [Frightened.] You see, my husband is so very fond of him!

[005-102]HEDDA.

[005-103][Rising.] You ought to write to him, Tesman. Perhaps he may not care to

[005-104]come to you of his own accord.

[005-105]TESMAN.

[005-106]Well, perhaps it would be the right thing to do, Hedda? Eh?

[005-107]HEDDA.

[005-108]And the sooner the better. Why not at once?

[005-109]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-110][Imploringly.] Oh, if you only would!

[005-111]TESMAN.

[005-112]I'll write this moment. Have you his address, Mrs.--Mrs. Elvsted.

[005-113]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-114]Yes. [Takes a slip of paper from her pocket, and hands it to him.] Here

[005-115]it is.

[005-116]TESMAN.

[005-117]Good, good. Then I'll go in-- [Looks about him.] By-the-bye,--my

[005-118]slippers? Oh, here. [Takes the packet and is about to go.

[005-119]HEDDA.

[005-120]Be sure you write him a cordial, friendly letter. And a good long one

[005-121]too.

[005-122]TESMAN.

[005-123]Yes, I will.

[005-124]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-125]But please, please don't say a word to show that I have suggested it.

[005-126]TESMAN.

[005-127]No, how could you think I would? Eh?

[005-128][He goes out to the right, through the inner room.

[005-129]HEDDA.

[005-130][Goes up to MRS. ELVSTED, smiles, and says in a low voice.] There! We

[005-131]have killed two birds with one stone.

[005-132]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-133]What do you mean?

[005-134]HEDDA.

[005-135]Could you not see that I wanted him to go?

[005-136]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-137]Yes, to write the letter--

[005-138]HEDDA.

[005-139]And that I might speak to you alone.

[005-140]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-141][Confused.] [[About]] the same thing?

[005-142]HEDDA.

[005-143]Precisely.

[005-144]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-145][Apprehensively.] But there is nothing more, Mrs. Tesman! Absolutely

[005-146]nothing!

[005-147]HEDDA.

[005-148]Oh yes, but there is. There is a great deal more--I can see that. Sit

[005-149]here--and we'll have a cosy, confidential chat.

[005-150][She forces MRS. ELVSTED to sit in the easy-chair beside the

[005-151]stove, and seats herself on one of the footstools.

[005-152]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-153][Anxiously, looking at her watch.] But, my dear Mrs. Tesman--I was

[005-154]really on the point of going.

[005-155]HEDDA.

[005-156]Oh, you can't be in such a hurry.--Well? Now tell me something about

[005-157]your life at home.

[005-158]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-159]Oh, that is just what I care least to speak about.

[005-160]HEDDA.

[005-161]But to me, dear--? Why, weren't we schoolfellows?

[005-162]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-163]Yes, but you were in the class above me. Oh, how dreadfully afraid of

[005-164]you I was then!

[005-165]HEDDA.

[005-166]Afraid of me?

[005-167]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-168]Yes, dreadfully. For when we met on the stairs you used always to pull

[005-169]my hair.

[005-170]HEDDA.

[005-171]Did I, really?

[005-172]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-173]Yes, and once you said you would burn it off my head.

[005-174]HEDDA.

[005-175]Oh that was all nonsense, of course.

[005-176]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-177]Yes, but I was so silly in those days.--And since then, too--we have

[005-178]drifted so far--far apart from each other. Our circles have been so

[005-179]entirely different.

[005-180]HEDDA.

[005-181]Well then, we must try to drift together again. Now listen. At school

[005-182]we said _du_(4) to each other; and we called each other by our Christian

[005-183]names--

[005-184]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-185]No, I am sure you must be mistaken.

[005-186]HEDDA.

[005-187]No, not at all! I can remember quite distinctly. So now we are going to

[005-188]renew our old friendship. [Draws the footstool closer to MRS. ELVSTED.]

[005-189]There now! [Kisses her cheek.] You must say _du_ to me and call me

[005-190]Hedda.

[005-191]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-192][Presses and pats her hands.] Oh, how good and kind you are! I am not

[005-193]used to such kindness.

[005-194]HEDDA.

[005-195]There, there, there! And I shall say _du_ to you, as in the old days,

[005-196]and call you my dear Thora.

[005-197]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-198]My name is Thea.(5)

[005-199]HEDDA.

[005-200]Why, of course! I meant Thea. [Looks at her compassionately.] So you are

[005-201]not accustomed to goodness and kindness, Thea? Not in your own home?

[005-202]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-203]Oh, if I only had a home! But I haven't any; I have never had a home.

[005-204]HEDDA.

[005-205][Looks at her for a moment.] I almost suspected as much.

[005-206]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-207][Gazing helplessly before her.] Yes--yes--yes.

[005-208]HEDDA.

[005-209]I don't quite remember--was it not as housekeeper that you first went to

[005-210]Mr. Elvsted's?

[005-211]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-212]I really went as governess. But his wife--his late wife--was an

[005-213]invalid,--and rarely left her room. So I had to look after the

[005-214]housekeeping as well.

[005-215]HEDDA.

[005-216]And then--at last--you became mistress of the house.

[005-217]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-218][Sadly.] Yes, I did.

[005-219]HEDDA.

[005-220]Let me see--about how long ago was that?

[005-221]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-222]My marriage?

[005-223]HEDDA.

[005-224]Yes.

[005-225]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-226]Five years ago.

[005-227]HEDDA.

[005-228]To be sure; it must be that.

[005-229]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-230]Oh those five years--! Or at all events the last two or three of them!

[005-231]Oh, if you(6) could only imagine--

[005-232]HEDDA.

[005-233][Giving her a little slap on the hand.] De? Fie, Thea!

[005-234]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-235]Yes, yes, I will try--. Well, if--you could only imagine and

[005-236]understand--

[005-237]HEDDA.

[005-238][Lightly.] Eilert Lovborg has been in your neighbourhood about three

[005-239]years, hasn't he?

[005-240]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-241][Looks at her doubtfully.] Eilert Lovborg? Yes--he has.

[005-242]HEDDA.

[005-243]Had you known him before, in town here?

[005-244]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-245]Scarcely at all. I mean--I knew him by name of course.

[005-246]HEDDA.

[005-247]But you saw a good deal of him in the country?

[005-248]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-249]Yes, he came to us every day. You see, he gave the children lessons; for

[005-250]in the long run I couldn't manage it all myself.

[005-251]HEDDA.

[005-252]No, that's clear.--And your husband--? I suppose he is often away from

[005-253]home?

[005-254]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-255]Yes. Being sheriff, you know, he has to travel about a good deal in his

[005-256]district.

[005-257]HEDDA.

[005-258][Leaning against the arm of the chair.] Thea--my poor, sweet Thea--now

[005-259]you must tell me everything--exactly as it stands.

[005-260]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-261]Well, then you must question me.

[005-262]HEDDA.

[005-263]What sort of a man is your husband, Thea? I mean--you know--in everyday

[005-264]life. Is he kind to you?

[005-265]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-266][Evasively.] I am sure he means well in everything.

[005-267]HEDDA.

[005-268]I should think he must be altogether too old for you. There is at least

[005-269]twenty years' difference between you, is there not?

[005-270]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-271][Irritably.] Yes, that is true, too. Everything about him is repellent

[005-272]to me! We have not a thought in common. We have no single point of

[005-273]sympathy--he and I.

[005-274]HEDDA.

[005-275]But is he not fond of you all the same? In his own way?

[005-276]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-277]Oh I really don't know. I think he regards me simply as a useful

[005-278]property. And then it doesn't cost much to keep me. I am not expensive.

[005-279]HEDDA.

[005-280]That is stupid of you.

[005-281]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-282][Shakes her head.] It cannot be otherwise--not with him. I don't think

[005-283]he really cares for any one but himself--and perhaps a little for the

[005-284]children.

[005-285]HEDDA.

[005-286]And for Eilert Lovborg, Thea?

[005-287]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-288][Looking at her.] For Eilert Lovborg? What puts that into your head?

[005-289]HEDDA.

[005-290]Well, my dear--I should say, when he sends you after him all the way

[005-291]to town-- [Smiling almost imperceptibly.] And besides, you said so

[005-292]yourself, to Tesman.

[005-293]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-294][With a little nervous twitch.] Did I? Yes, I suppose I did.

[005-295][Vehemently, but not loudly.] No--I may just as well make a clean breast

[005-296]of it at once! For it must all come out in any case.

[005-297]HEDDA.

[005-298]Why, my dear Thea--?

[005-299]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-300]Well, to make a long story short: My husband did not know that I was

[005-301]coming.

[005-302]HEDDA.

[005-303]What! Your husband didn't know it!

[005-304]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-305]No, of course not. For that matter, he was away from home himself--

[005-306]he was travelling. Oh, I could bear it no longer, Hedda! I couldn't

[005-307]indeed--so utterly alone as I should have been in future.

[005-308]HEDDA.

[005-309]Well? And then?

[005-310]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-311]So I put together some of my things--what I needed most--as quietly as

[005-312]possible. And then I left the house.

[005-313]HEDDA.

[005-314]Without a word?

[005-315]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-316]Yes--and took the train to town.

[005-317]HEDDA.

[005-318]Why, my dear, good Thea--to think of you daring to do it!

[005-319]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-320][Rises and moves about the room.] What else could I possibly do?

[005-321]HEDDA.

[005-322]But what do you think your husband will say when you go home again?

[005-323]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-324][At the table, looks at her.] Back to him?

[005-325]HEDDA.

[005-326]Of course.

[005-327]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-328]I shall never go back to him again.

[005-329]HEDDA.

[005-330][Rising and going towards her.] Then you have left your home--for good

[005-331]and all?

[005-332]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-333]Yes. There was nothing else to be done.

[005-334]HEDDA.

[005-335]But then--to take flight so openly.

[005-336]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-337]Oh, it's impossible to keep things of that sort secret.

[005-338]HEDDA.

[005-339]But what do you think people will say of you, Thea?

[005-340]MRS. ELVSTED.

[005-341]They may say what they like, for aught _I_ care. [Seats herself wearily

[005-342]and sadly on the sofa.] I have done nothing but what I had to do.
