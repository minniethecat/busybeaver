# George Tesman — Character Traits

Tesman's traits form a coherent portrait of the well-meaning bourgeois intellectual:

| Trait | Expression | Dramatic Consequence |
|-------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Academic diligence** | "Collecting and arranging — no one can beat you at that" (004-010) | He is a compiler, not a creator — and knows it |
| **Genuine affection** | Proud of [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]], grateful to his aunts, enthusiastic about [[Eilert-Lovborg|Lovborg]]'s work | His love is real but misdirected — he loves a Hedda who doesn't exist |
| **Social naivety** | Never perceives Hedda's contempt, [[Judge-Brack|Brack]]'s cynicism, or the sexual dynamics around him | Exploited by Brack, manipulated by Hedda, pitied by the audience |
| **Provincial optimism** | Dreams of professorship, villa, domestic contentment | His ideal is Hedda's nightmare — the petit-bourgeois dream she finds suffocating |
| **Filial devotion** | Raised by his aunts, he is childlike in his gratitude | His emotional life is pre-adult; he married a mother-substitute who despises the role |

These traits are not flaws in a moral sense — Tesman is genuinely good — but they render him catastrophically inadequate as Hedda's partner. His decency cannot bridge the gulf between their emotional universes.

Ibsen's own notes describe Tesman as a "ninny" — a man of genuine academic diligence whose social blindness makes him an unwitting accomplice in the destruction around him. (002-055) His tragedy is not villainy but inadequacy: he loves a woman whose inner life he never perceives and whose contempt he never suspects.

## From the Text

> TESMAN.
> Security? You? My dear Aunt Julia--what sort of security could you give?
> MISS TESMAN.
> I have given a mortgage on our annuity.
> TESMAN.
> [Jumps up.] What! On your--and Aunt Rina's annuity!
> MISS TESMAN.
> Well well--don't get so excited about it. It's only a matter of form you know.
> TESMAN.
> Oh Auntie--will you never be tired of making sacrifices for me!
