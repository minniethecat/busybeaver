# Hedda Gabler: Thematic Significance

Hedda represents the tragedy of unrealized potential — the woman who "hankered and refrained." She has no creative outlet, no intellectual passion — only the will to power through manipulation.

Her _coldness_ — "the chill materialism of Hedda" (001-076) — is not absence of feeling but its redirection toward destructive ends. Ibsen "sought refuge in the chill materialism of Hedda from the ardent" impulses of his own temperament (001-076), suggesting authorial identification with his anti-heroine.

> Ibsen sought refuge in the chill materialism of Hedda from the ardour of his own temperament.
>
> (001-076)

Her suicide is both victory and defeat. She escapes [[Judge-Brack|Brack]]'s power and preserves her reputation — but only by affirming she has no place in the world. The temple shot is the play's final aesthetic gesture: a woman arranging her own exit.
