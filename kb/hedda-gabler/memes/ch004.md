[004-001]TESMAN.

[004-002]It will deal with the domestic industries of Brabant during the Middle

[004-003]Ages.

[004-004]MISS TESMAN.

[004-005]Fancy--to be able to write on such a subject as that!

[004-006]TESMAN.

[004-007]However, it may be some time before the book is ready. I have all these

[004-008]collections to arrange first, you see.

[004-009]MISS TESMAN.

[004-010]Yes, collecting and arranging--no one can beat you at that. There you

[004-011]are my poor brother's own son.

[004-012]TESMAN.

[004-013]I am looking forward eagerly to setting to work at it; especially now

[004-014]that I have my own delightful home to work in.

[004-015]MISS TESMAN.

[004-016]And, most of all, now that you have got the wife of your heart, my dear

[004-017]George.

[004-018]TESMAN.

[004-019][Embracing her.] Oh yes, yes, Aunt Julia! Hedda--she is the best part of

[004-020]it all! I believe I hear her coming--eh?

[004-021]HEDDA enters from the left through the inner room. Her face

[004-022]and figure show refinement and distinction. Her complexion

[004-023]is pale and opaque. Her steel-grey eyes express a cold,

[004-024]unruffled repose. Her hair is of an agreeable brown, but

[004-025]not particularly abundant. She is dressed in a tasteful,

[004-026]somewhat loose-fitting morning gown.

[004-027]MISS TESMAN.

[004-028][Going to meet HEDDA.] Good morning, my dear Hedda! Good morning, and a

[004-029]hearty welcome!

[004-030]HEDDA.

[004-031][Holds out her hand.] Good morning, dear [[Aunt-Julia|Miss Tesman]]! So early a call!

[004-032]That is kind of you.

[004-033]MISS TESMAN.

[004-034][With some embarrassment.] Well--has the bride slept well in her new

[004-035]home?

[004-036]HEDDA.

[004-037]Oh yes, thanks. Passably.

[004-038]TESMAN.

[004-039][Laughing.] Passably! Come, that's good, Hedda! You were sleeping like a

[004-040]stone when I got up.

[004-041]HEDDA.

[004-042]Fortunately. Of course one has always to accustom one's self to new

[004-043]surroundings, Miss Tesman--little by little. [Looking towards the left.]

[004-044]Oh, there the servant has gone and opened the veranda door, and let in a

[004-045]whole flood of sunshine.

[004-046]MISS TESMAN.

[004-047][Going towards the door.] Well, then we will shut it.

[004-048]HEDDA.

[004-049]No no, not that! Tesman, please draw the curtains. That will give a

[004-050]softer light.

[004-051]TESMAN.

[004-052][At the door.] All right--all right.--There now, Hedda, now you have

[004-053]both shade and fresh air.

[004-054]HEDDA.

[004-055]Yes, fresh air we certainly must have, with all these stacks of

[004-056]flowers--. But--won't you sit down, Miss Tesman?

[004-057]MISS TESMAN.

[004-058]No, thank you. Now that I have seen that everything is all right

[004-059]here--thank heaven!--I must be getting home again. My sister is lying

[004-060]longing for me, poor thing.

[004-061]TESMAN.

[004-062]Give her my very best love, Auntie; and say I shall look in and see her

[004-063]later in the day.

[004-064]MISS TESMAN.

[004-065]Yes, yes, I'll be sure to tell her. But by-the-bye, George--[Feeling

[004-066]in her dress pocket]--I had almost forgotten--I have something for you

[004-067]here.

[004-068]TESMAN.

[004-069]What is it, Auntie? Eh?

[004-070]MISS TESMAN.

[004-071][Produces a flat parcel wrapped in newspaper and hands it to him.] Look

[004-072]here, my dear boy.

[004-073]TESMAN.

[004-074][Opening the parcel.] Well, I declare!--Have you really saved

[004-075]them for me, Aunt Julia! Hedda! isn't this touching--eh?

[004-076]HEDDA.

[004-077][Beside the whatnot on the right.] Well, what is it?

[004-078]TESMAN.

[004-079]My old morning-shoes! My slippers.

[004-080]HEDDA.

[004-081]Indeed. I remember you often spoke of them while we were abroad.

[004-082]TESMAN.

[004-083]Yes, I missed them terribly. [Goes up to her.] Now you shall see them,

[004-084]Hedda!

[004-085]HEDDA.

[004-086][Going towards the stove.] Thanks, I really don't care about it.

[004-087]TESMAN.

[004-088][Following her.] Only think--ill as she was, [[Aunt-Rina|Aunt Rina]] embroidered these

[004-089]for me. Oh you can't think how many associations cling to them.

[004-090]HEDDA.

[004-091][At the table.] Scarcely for me.

[004-092]MISS TESMAN.

[004-093]Of course not for Hedda, George.

[004-094]TESMAN.

[004-095]Well, but now that she belongs to the family, I thought--

[004-096]HEDDA.

[004-097][Interrupting.] We shall never get on with this servant, Tesman.

[004-098]MISS TESMAN.

[004-099]Not get on with [[Berta]]?

[004-100]TESMAN.

[004-101]Why, dear, what puts that in your head? Eh?

[004-102]HEDDA.

[004-103][Pointing.] Look there! She has left her old bonnet lying about on a

[004-104]chair.

[004-105]TESMAN.

[004-106][In consternation, drops the slippers on the floor.] Why, Hedda--

[004-107]HEDDA.

[004-108]Just fancy, if any one should come in and see it!

[004-109]TESMAN.

[004-110]But Hedda--that's Aunt Julia's bonnet.

[004-111]HEDDA.

[004-112]Is it!

[004-113]MISS TESMAN.

[004-114][Taking up the bonnet.] Yes, indeed it's mine. And, what's more, it's

[004-115]not old, Madam Hedda.

[004-116]HEDDA.

[004-117]I really did not look closely at it, Miss Tesman.

[004-118]MISS TESMAN.

[004-119][Trying on the bonnet.] Let me tell you it's the first time I have worn

[004-120]it--the very first time.

[004-121]TESMAN.

[004-122]And a very nice bonnet it is too--quite a beauty!

[004-123]MISS TESMAN.

[004-124]Oh, it's no such great things, George. [Looks around her.] My

[004-125]parasol--? Ah, here. [Takes it.] For this is mine too-- [mutters] --not

[004-126]Berta's.

[004-127]TESMAN.

[004-128]A new bonnet and a new parasol! Only think, Hedda.

[004-129]HEDDA.

[004-130]Very handsome indeed.

[004-131]TESMAN.

[004-132]Yes, isn't it? Eh? But Auntie, take a good look at Hedda before you go!

[004-133]See how handsome she is!

[004-134]MISS TESMAN.

[004-135]Oh, my dear boy, there's nothing new in that. Hedda was always lovely.

[004-136][She nods and goes toward the right.

[004-137]TESMAN.

[004-138][Following.] Yes, but have you noticed what splendid condition she is

[004-139]in? How she has filled out on the journey?

[004-140]HEDDA.

[004-141][Crossing the room.] Oh, do be quiet--!

[004-142]MISS TESMAN.

[004-143][Who has stopped and turned.] Filled out?

[004-144]TESMAN.

[004-145]Of course you don't notice it so much now that she has that dress on.

[004-146]But I, who can see--

[004-147]HEDDA.

[004-148][At the glass door, impatiently.] Oh, you can't see anything.

[004-149]TESMAN.

[004-150]It must be the mountain air in the Tyrol--

[004-151]HEDDA.

[004-152][Curtly, interrupting.] I am exactly as I was when I started.

[004-153]TESMAN.

[004-154]So you insist; but I'm quite certain you are not. Don't you agree with

[004-155]me, Auntie?

[004-156]MISS TESMAN.

[004-157][Who has been gazing at her with folded hands.] Hedda is lovely--

[004-158]lovely--lovely. [Goes up to her, takes her head between both hands,

[004-159]draws it downwards, and kisses her hair.] God bless and preserve Hedda

[004-160]Tesman--for George's sake.

[004-161]HEDDA.

[004-162][Gently freeing herself.] Oh--! Let me go.

[004-163]MISS TESMAN.

[004-164][In quiet emotion.] I shall not let a day pass without coming to see

[004-165]you.

[004-166]TESMAN.

[004-167]No you won't, will you, Auntie? Eh?

[004-168]MISS TESMAN.

[004-169]Good-bye--good-bye!

[004-170][She goes out by the hall door. TESMAN accompanies her. The

[004-171]door remains half open. TESMAN can be heard repeating his

[004-172]message to Aunt Rina and his thanks for the slippers.

[004-173][In the meantime, HEDDA walks about the room, raising her arms

[004-174]and clenching her hands as if in desperation. Then she flings

[004-175]back the curtains from the glass door, and stands there looking

[004-176]out.

[004-177][Presently, TESMAN returns and closes the door behind him.

[004-178]TESMAN.

[004-179][Picks up the slippers from the floor.] What are you looking at, Hedda?

[004-180]HEDDA.

[004-181][Once more calm and mistress of herself.] I am only looking at the

[004-182]leaves. They are so yellow--so withered.

[004-183]TESMAN.

[004-184][Wraps up the slippers and lays them on the table.] Well, you see, we

[004-185]are well into September now.

[004-186]HEDDA.

[004-187][Again restless.] Yes, to think of it!--already in--in September.

[004-188]TESMAN.

[004-189]Don't you think Aunt Julia's manner was strange, dear? Almost solemn?

[004-190]Can you imagine what was the matter with her? Eh?

[004-191]HEDDA.

[004-192]I scarcely know her, you see. Is she not often like that?

[004-193]TESMAN.

[004-194]No, not as she was to-day.

[004-195]HEDDA.

[004-196][Leaving the glass door.] Do you think she was annoyed about the bonnet?

[004-197]TESMAN.

[004-198]Oh, scarcely at all. Perhaps a little, just at the moment--

[004-199]HEDDA.

[004-200]But what an idea, to pitch her bonnet about in the drawing-room! No one

[004-201]does that sort of thing.

[004-202]TESMAN.

[004-203]Well you may be sure Aunt Julia won't do it again.

[004-204]HEDDA.

[004-205]In any case, I shall manage to make my peace with her.

[004-206]TESMAN.

[004-207]Yes, my dear, good Hedda, if you only would.

[004-208]HEDDA.

[004-209]When you call this afternoon, you might invite her to spend the evening

[004-210]here.

[004-211]TESMAN.

[004-212]Yes, that I will. And there's one thing more you could do that would

[004-213]delight her heart.

[004-214]HEDDA.

[004-215]What is it?

[004-216]TESMAN.

[004-217]If you could only prevail on yourself to say _du_(3) to her. For my

[004-218]sake, Hedda? Eh?

[004-219]HEDDA.

[004-220]No, no, Tesman--you really mustn't ask that of me. I have told you so

[004-221]already. I shall try to call her "Aunt"; and you must be satisfied with

[004-222]that.

[004-223]TESMAN.

[004-224]Well well. Only I think now that you belong to the family, you--

[004-225]HEDDA.

[004-226]H'm--I can't in the least see why--

[004-227][She goes up towards the middle doorway.

[004-228]TESMAN.

[004-229][After a pause.] Is there anything the matter with you, Hedda? Eh?

[004-230]HEDDA.

[004-231]I'm only looking at my old piano. It doesn't go at all well with all the

[004-232]other things.

[004-233]TESMAN.

[004-234]The first time I draw my salary, we'll see about exchanging it.

[004-235]HEDDA.

[004-236]No, no--no exchanging. I don't want to part with it. Suppose we put it

[004-237]there in the inner room, and then get another here in its place. When

[004-238]it's convenient, I mean.

[004-239]TESMAN.

[004-240][A little taken aback.] Yes--of course we could do that.

[004-241]HEDDA.

[004-242][Takes up the bouquet from the piano.] These flowers were not here last

[004-243]night when we arrived.

[004-244]TESMAN.

[004-245]Aunt Julia must have brought them for you.

[004-246]HEDDA.

[004-247][Examining the bouquet.] A visiting-card. [Takes it out and reads:]

[004-248]"Shall return later in the day." Can you guess whose card it is?

[004-249]TESMAN.

[004-250]No. Whose? Eh?

[004-251]HEDDA.

[004-252]The name is "[[Mrs-Elvsted|Mrs. Elvsted]]."

[004-253]TESMAN.

[004-254]Is it really? [[Sheriff-Elvsted|Sheriff Elvsted]]'s wife? Miss Rysing that was.

[004-255]HEDDA.

[004-256]Exactly. The girl with the irritating hair, that she was always showing

[004-257]off. An old flame of yours I've been told.

[004-258]TESMAN.

[004-259][Laughing.] Oh, that didn't last long; and it was before I met you,

[004-260]Hedda. But fancy her being in town!

[004-261]HEDDA.

[004-262]It's odd that she should call upon us. I have scarcely seen her since we

[004-263]left school.

[004-264]TESMAN.

[004-265]I haven't see her either for--heaven knows how long. I wonder how she

[004-266]can endure to live in such an out-of-the way hole--eh?

[004-267]HEDDA.

[004-268][After a moment's thought, says suddenly.] Tell me, Tesman--isn't it

[004-269]somewhere near there that he--that--[[Eilert-Lovborg|Eilert Lovborg]] is living?

[004-270]TESMAN.

[004-271]Yes, he is somewhere in that part of the country.

[004-272]BERTA enters by the hall door.

[004-273]BERTA.

[004-274]That lady, ma'am, that brought some flowers a little while ago, is here

[004-275]again. [Pointing.] The flowers you have in your hand, ma'am.

[004-276]HEDDA.

[004-277]Ah, is she? Well, please show her in.

[004-278]BERTA opens the door for MRS. ELVSTED, and goes out herself.

[004-279]--MRS. ELVSTED is a woman of fragile figure, with pretty,

[004-280]soft features. Her eyes are light blue, large, round, and

[004-281]somewhat prominent, with a startled, inquiring expression.

[004-282]Her hair is remarkably light, almost flaxen, and unusually

[004-283]abundant and wavy. She is a couple of years younger than

[004-284]HEDDA. She wears a dark visiting dress, tasteful, but not

[004-285]quite in the latest fashion.

[004-286]HEDDA.

[004-287][Receives her warmly.] How do you do, my dear Mrs. Elvsted? It's

[004-288]delightful to see you again.

[004-289]MRS. ELVSTED.

[004-290][Nervously, struggling for self-control.] Yes, it's a very long time

[004-291]since we met.

[004-292]TESMAN.

[004-293][Gives her his hand.] And we too--eh?

[004-294]HEDDA.

[004-295]Thanks for your lovely flowers--

[004-296]MRS. ELVSTED.

[004-297]Oh, not at all--. I would have come straight here yesterday afternoon;

[004-298]but I heard that you were away--

[004-299]TESMAN.

[004-300]Have you just come to town? Eh?

[004-301]MRS. ELVSTED.

[004-302]I arrived yesterday, about midday. Oh, I was quite in despair when I

[004-303]heard that you were not at home.

[004-304]HEDDA.

[004-305]In despair! How so?

[004-306]TESMAN.

[004-307]Why, my dear Mrs. Rysing--I mean Mrs. Elvsted--

[004-308]HEDDA.

[004-309]I hope that you are not in any trouble?

[004-310]MRS. ELVSTED.

[004-311]Yes, I am. And I don't know another living creature here that I can turn

[004-312]to.

[004-313]HEDDA.

[004-314][Laying the bouquet on the table.] Come--let us sit here on the sofa--

[004-315]MRS. ELVSTED.

[004-316]Oh, I am too restless to sit down.

[004-317]HEDDA.

[004-318]Oh no, you're not. Come here.

[004-319][She draws MRS. ELVSTED down upon the sofa and sits at her side.

[004-320]TESMAN.

[004-321]Well? What is it, Mrs. Elvsted--?

[004-322]HEDDA.

[004-323]Has anything particular happened to you at home?

[004-324]MRS. ELVSTED.

[004-325]Yes--and no. Oh--I am so anxious you should not misunderstand me--

[004-326]HEDDA.

[004-327]Then your best plan is to tell us the whole story, Mrs. Elvsted.

[004-328]TESMAN.

[004-329]I suppose that's what you have come for--eh?

[004-330]MRS. ELVSTED.
