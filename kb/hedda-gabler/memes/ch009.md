[009-001]LOVBORG.

[009-002][Looking for his hat.] Oh--then I won't detain you--

[009-003]BRACK.

[009-004]No, but listen--will you not do me the favour of joining us?

[009-005]LOVBORG.

[009-006][Curtly and decidedly.] No, I can't--thank you very much.

[009-007]BRACK.

[009-008]Oh, nonsense--do! We shall be quite a select little circle. And I assure

[009-009]you we shall have a "lively time," as Mrs. Hed--as Mrs. Tesman says.

[009-010]LOVBORG.

[009-011]I have no doubt of it. But nevertheless--

[009-012]BRACK.

[009-013]And then you might bring your manuscript with you, and read it to Tesman

[009-014]at my house. I could give you a room to yourselves.

[009-015]TESMAN.

[009-016]Yes, think of that, Eilert,--why shouldn't you? Eh?

[009-017]HEDDA.

[009-018][Interposing.] But, Tesman, if Mr. Lovborg would really rather not! I

[009-019]am sure Mr. Lovborg is much more inclined to remain here and have supper

[009-020]with me.

[009-021]LOVBORG.

[009-022][Looking at her.] With you, Mrs. Tesman?

[009-023]HEDDA.

[009-024]And with [[Mrs-Elvsted|Mrs. Elvsted]].

[009-025]LOVBORG.

[009-026]Ah-- [Lightly.] I saw her for a moment this morning.

[009-027]HEDDA.

[009-028]Did you? Well, she is coming this evening. So you see you are almost

[009-029]bound to remain, Mr. Lovborg, or she will have no one to see her home.

[009-030]LOVBORG.

[009-031]That's true. Many thanks, Mrs. Tesman--in that case I will remain.

[009-032]HEDDA.

[009-033]Then I have one or two orders to give the servant--

[009-034][She goes to the hall door and rings. BERTA enters. HEDDA talks

[009-035]to her in a whisper, and points towards the inner room. BERTA

[009-036]nods and goes out again.

[009-037]TESMAN.

[009-038][At the same time, to LOVBORG.] Tell me, Eilert--is it this new

[009-039]subject--the future--that you are going to lecture about?

[009-040]LOVBORG.

[009-041]Yes.

[009-042]TESMAN.

[009-043]They told me at the bookseller's that you are going to deliver a course

[009-044]of lectures this autumn.

[009-045]LOVBORG.

[009-046]That is my intention. I hope you won't take it ill, Tesman.

[009-047]TESMAN.

[009-048]Oh no, not in the least! But--?

[009-049]LOVBORG.

[009-050]I can quite understand that it must be very disagreeable to you.

[009-051]TESMAN.

[009-052][Cast down.] Oh, I can't expect you, out of consideration for me, to--

[009-053]LOVBORG.

[009-054]But I shall wait till you have received your appointment.

[009-055]TESMAN.

[009-056]Will you wait? Yes but--yes but--are you not going to compete with me?

[009-057]Eh?

[009-058]LOVBORG.

[009-059]No; it is only the moral victory I care for.

[009-060]TESMAN.

[009-061]Why, bless me--then Aunt Julia was right after all! Oh yes--I knew it!

[009-062]Hedda! Just fancy--[[Eilert-Lovborg|Eilert Lovborg]] is not going to stand in our way!

[009-063]HEDDA.

[009-064][Curtly.] Our way? Pray leave me out of the question.

[009-065][She goes up towards the inner room, where BERTA is placing a

[009-066]tray with decanters and glasses on the table. HEDDA nods

[009-067]approval, and comes forward again. BERTA goes out.

[009-068]TESMAN.

[009-069][At the same time.] And you, [[Judge-Brack|Judge Brack]]--what do you say to this? Eh?

[009-070]BRACK.

[009-071]Well, I say that a moral victory--h'm--may be all very fine--

[009-072]TESMAN.

[009-073]Yes, certainly. But all the same--

[009-074]HEDDA.

[009-075][Looking at TESMAN with a cold smile.] You stand there looking as if you

[009-076]were thunderstruck--

[009-077]TESMAN.

[009-078]Yes--so I am--I almost think--

[009-079]BRACK.

[009-080]Don't you see, Mrs. Tesman, a thunderstorm has just passed over?

[009-081]HEDDA.

[009-082][Pointing towards the room.] Will you not take a glass of cold punch,

[009-083]gentlemen?

[009-084]BRACK.

[009-085][Looking at his watch.] A stirrup-cup? Yes, it wouldn't come amiss.

[009-086]TESMAN.

[009-087]A capital idea, Hedda! Just the thing! Now that the weight has been

[009-088]taken off my mind--

[009-089]HEDDA.

[009-090]Will you not join them, Mr. Lovborg?

[009-091]LOVBORG.

[009-092][With a gesture of refusal.] No, thank you. Nothing for me.

[009-093]BRACK.

[009-094]Why bless me--cold punch is surely not poison.

[009-095]LOVBORG.

[009-096]Perhaps not for everyone.

[009-097]HEDDA.

[009-098]I will keep Mr. Lovborg company in the meantime.

[009-099]TESMAN.

[009-100]Yes, yes, Hedda dear, do.

[009-101][He and BRACK go into the inner room, seat themselves, drink

[009-102]punch, smoke cigarettes, and carry on a lively conversation

[009-103]during what follows. EILERT LOVBORG remains standing beside

[009-104]the stove. HEDDA goes to the writing-table.

[009-105]HEDDA.

[009-106][Raising her voice a little.] Do you care to look at some photographs,

[009-107]Mr. Lovborg? You know Tesman and I made a tour in the Tyrol on our way

[009-108]home?

[009-109][She takes up an album, and places it on the table beside the

[009-110]sofa, in the further corner of which she seats herself. EILERT

[009-111]LOVBORG approaches, stops, and looks at her. Then he takes a

[009-112]chair and seats himself to her left.

[009-113]HEDDA.

[009-114][Opening the album.] Do you see this range of mountains, Mr. Lovborg?

[009-115]It's the Ortler group. Tesman has written the name underneath. Here it

[009-116]is: "The Ortler group near Meran."

[009-117]LOVBORG.

[009-118][Who has never taken his eyes off her, says softly and slowly:]

[009-119]Hedda--Gabler!

[009-120]HEDDA.

[009-121][Glancing hastily at him.] Ah! Hush!

[009-122]LOVBORG.

[009-123][Repeats softly.] [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda Gabler]]!

[009-124]HEDDA.

[009-125][Looking at the album.] That was my name in the old days--when we two

[009-126]knew each other.

[009-127]LOVBORG.

[009-128]And I must teach myself never to say Hedda Gabler again--never, as long

[009-129]as I live.

[009-130]HEDDA.

[009-131][Still turning over the pages.] Yes, you must. And I think you ought to

[009-132]practise in time. The sooner the better, I should say.

[009-133]LOVBORG.

[009-134][In a tone of indignation.] Hedda Gabler married? And married to--

[009-135][[George-Tesman|George Tesman]]!

[009-136]HEDDA.

[009-137]Yes--so the world goes.

[009-138]LOVBORG.

[009-139]Oh, Hedda, Hedda--how could you(9) throw yourself away!

[009-140]HEDDA.

[009-141][Looks sharply at him.] What? I can't allow this!

[009-142]LOVBORG.

[009-143]What do you mean?

[009-144][TESMAN comes into the room and goes towards the sofa.

[009-145]HEDDA.

[009-146][Hears him coming and says in an indifferent tone.] And this is a view

[009-147]from the Val d'Ampezzo, Mr. Lovborg. Just look at these peaks! [Looks

[009-148]affectionately up at TESMAN.] What's the name of these curious peaks,

[009-149]dear?

[009-150]TESMAN.

[009-151]Let me see. Oh, those are the Dolomites.

[009-152]HEDDA.

[009-153]Yes, that's it!--Those are the Dolomites, Mr. Lovborg.

[009-154]TESMAN.

[009-155]Hedda, dear,--I only wanted to ask whether I shouldn't bring you a

[009-156]little punch after all? For yourself at any rate--eh?

[009-157]HEDDA.

[009-158]Yes, do, please; and perhaps a few biscuits.

[009-159]TESMAN.

[009-160]No cigarettes?

[009-161]HEDDA.

[009-162]No.

[009-163]TESMAN.

[009-164]Very well.

[009-165][He goes into the inner room and out to the right. BRACK sits

[009-166]in the inner room, and keeps an eye from time to time on HEDDA

[009-167]and LOVBORG.

[009-168]LOVBORG.

[009-169][Softly, as before.] Answer me, Hedda--how could you go and do this?

[009-170]HEDDA.

[009-171][Apparently absorbed in the album.] If you continue to say _du_ to me I

[009-172]won't talk to you.

[009-173]LOVBORG.

[009-174]May I not say _du_ even when we are alone?

[009-175]HEDDA.

[009-176]No. You may think it; but you mustn't say it.

[009-177]LOVBORG.

[009-178]Ah, I understand. It is an offence against George Tesman, whom

[009-179]you(10)--love.

[009-180]HEDDA.

[009-181][Glances at him and smiles.] Love? What an idea!

[009-182]LOVBORG.

[009-183]You don't love him then!

[009-184]HEDDA.

[009-185]But I won't hear of any sort of unfaithfulness! Remember that.

[009-186]LOVBORG.

[009-187]Hedda--answer me one thing--

[009-188]HEDDA.

[009-189]Hush! [TESMAN enters with a small tray from the inner room.

[009-190]TESMAN.

[009-191]Here you are! Isn't this tempting? [He puts the tray on the table.

[009-192]HEDDA.

[009-193]Why do you bring it yourself?

[009-194]TESMAN.

[009-195][Filling the glasses.] Because I think it's such fun to wait upon you,

[009-196]Hedda.

[009-197]HEDDA.

[009-198]But you have poured out two glasses. Mr. Lovborg said he wouldn't have

[009-199]any--

[009-200]TESMAN.

[009-201]No, but Mrs. Elvsted will soon be here, won't she?

[009-202]HEDDA.

[009-203]Yes, by-the-bye--Mrs. Elvsted--

[009-204]TESMAN.

[009-205]Had you forgotten her? Eh?

[009-206]HEDDA.

[009-207]We were so absorbed in these photographs. [Shows him a picture.] Do you

[009-208]remember this little village?

[009-209]TESMAN.

[009-210]Oh, it's that one just below the Brenner Pass. It was there we passed

[009-211]the night--

[009-212]HEDDA.

[009-213]--and met that lively party of tourists.

[009-214]TESMAN.

[009-215]Yes, that was the place. Fancy--if we could only have had you with us,

[009-216]Eilert! Eh?

[009-217][He returns to the inner room and sits beside BRACK.

[009-218]LOVBORG.

[009-219]Answer me one thing, Hedda--

[009-220]HEDDA.

[009-221]Well?

[009-222]LOVBORG.

[009-223]Was there no love in your friendship for me either? Not a spark--not a

[009-224]tinge of love in it?

[009-225]HEDDA.

[009-226]I wonder if there was? To me it seems as though we were two good

[009-227]comrades--two thoroughly intimate friends. [Smilingly.] You especially

[009-228]were frankness itself.

[009-229]LOVBORG.

[009-230]It was you that made me so.

[009-231]HEDDA.

[009-232]As I look back upon it all, I think there was really something

[009-233]beautiful, something fascinating--something daring--in--in that secret

[009-234]intimacy--that comradeship which no living creature so much as dreamed

[009-235]of.

[009-236]LOVBORG.

[009-237]Yes, yes, Hedda! Was there not?--When I used to come to your father's

[009-238]in the afternoon--and the General sat over at the window reading his

[009-239]papers--with his back towards us--

[009-240]HEDDA.

[009-241]And we two on the corner sofa--

[009-242]LOVBORG.

[009-243]Always with the same illustrated paper before us--

[009-244]HEDDA.

[009-245]For want of an album, yes.

[009-246]LOVBORG.

[009-247]Yes, Hedda, and when I made my confessions to you--told you about

[009-248]myself, things that at that time no one else knew! There I would sit

[009-249]and tell you of my escapades--my days and nights of devilment. Oh,

[009-250]Hedda--what was the power in you that forced me to confess these things?

[009-251]HEDDA.

[009-252]Do you think it was any power in me?

[009-253]LOVBORG.

[009-254]How else can I explain it? And all those--those roundabout questions you

[009-255]used to put to me--

[009-256]HEDDA.

[009-257]Which you understood so particularly well--

[009-258]LOVBORG.

[009-259]How could you sit and question me like that? Question me quite

[009-260]frankly--

[009-261]HEDDA.

[009-262]In roundabout terms, please observe.

[009-263]LOVBORG.

[009-264]Yes, but frankly nevertheless. Cross-question me about--all that sort of

[009-265]thing?

[009-266]HEDDA.

[009-267]And how could you answer, Mr. Lovborg?

[009-268]LOVBORG.

[009-269]Yes, that is just what I can't understand--in looking back upon it. But

[009-270]tell me now, Hedda--was there not love at the bottom of our friendship?

[009-271]On your side, did you not feel as though you might purge my stains

[009-272]away--if I made you my confessor? Was it not so?

[009-273]HEDDA.

[009-274]No, not quite.

[009-275]LOVBORG.

[009-276]What was you motive, then?

[009-277]HEDDA.

[009-278]Do think it quite incomprehensible that a young girl--when it can be

[009-279]done--without any one knowing--

[009-280]LOVBORG.

[009-281]Well?

[009-282]HEDDA.

[009-283]--should be glad to have a peep, now and then, into a world which--?

[009-284]LOVBORG.

[009-285]Which--?

[009-286]HEDDA.

[009-287]--which she is forbidden to know anything about?

[009-288]LOVBORG.

[009-289]So that was it?

[009-290]HEDDA.

[009-291]Partly. Partly--I almost think.

[009-292]LOVBORG.

[009-293]Comradeship in the thirst for life. But why should not that, at any

[009-294]rate, have continued?

[009-295]HEDDA.

[009-296]The fault was yours.

[009-297]LOVBORG.

[009-298]It was you that broke with me.

[009-299]HEDDA.

[009-300]Yes, when our friendship threatened to develop into something more

[009-301]serious. Shame upon you, Eilert Lovborg! How could you think of wronging

[009-302]your--your frank comrade.

[009-303]LOVBORG.

[009-304][Clenches his hands.] Oh, why did you not carry out your threat? Why did

[009-305]you not shoot me down?

[009-306]HEDDA.

[009-307]Because I have such a dread of scandal.

[009-308]LOVBORG.

[009-309]Yes, Hedda, you are a coward at heart.

[009-310]HEDDA.

[009-311]A terrible coward. [Changing her tone.] But it was a lucky thing for

[009-312]you. And now you have found ample consolation at the Elvsteds'.

[009-313]LOVBORG.

[009-314]I know what Thea has confided to you.

[009-315]HEDDA.

[009-316]And perhaps you have confided to her something about us?

[009-317]LOVBORG.

[009-318]Not a word. She is too stupid to understand anything of that sort.

[009-319]HEDDA.

[009-320]Stupid?

[009-321]LOVBORG.

[009-322]She is stupid about matters of that sort.

[009-323]HEDDA.

[009-324]And I am cowardly. [Bends over towards him, without looking him in the

[009-325]face, and says more softly:] But now I will confide something to you.

[009-326]LOVBORG.

[009-327][Eagerly.] Well?

[009-328]HEDDA.

[009-329]The fact that I dared not shoot you down--

[009-330]LOVBORG.

[009-331]Yes!

[009-332]HEDDA.

[009-333]--that was not my arrant cowardice--that evening.

[009-334]LOVBORG.

[009-335][Looks at her a moment, understands, and whispers passionately.] Oh,

[009-336]Hedda! Hedda Gabler! Now I begin to see a hidden reason beneath our

[009-337]comradeship! You(11) and I--! After all, then, it was your craving for

[009-338]life--

[009-339]HEDDA.

[009-340][Softly, with a sharp glance.] Take care! Believe nothing of the sort!

[009-341][Twilight has begun to fall. The hall door is opened from

[009-342]without by BERTA.

[009-343]HEDDA.

[009-344][Closes the album with a bang and calls smilingly:] Ah, at last! My

[009-345]darling Thea,--come along!

[009-346]MRS. ELVSTED enters from the hall. She is in evening dress.

[009-347]The door is closed behind her.

[009-348]HEDDA.

[009-349][On the sofa, stretches out her arms towards her.] My sweet Thea--you

[009-350]can't think how I have been longing for you!

[009-351][MRS. ELVSTED, in passing, exchanges slight salutations with

[009-352]the gentlemen in the inner room, then goes up to the table

[009-353]and gives HEDDA her hand. EILERT LOVBORG has risen. He and

[009-354]MRS. ELVSTED greet each other with a silent nod.
