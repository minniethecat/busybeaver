[014-001]TESMAN.

[014-002]Yes yes--what then?

[014-003]HEDDA.

[014-004]You acknowledged that you envied him his work.

[014-005]TESMAN.

[014-006]Oh, of course I didn't mean that literally.

[014-007]HEDDA.

[014-008]No matter--I could not bear the idea that any one should throw you into

[014-009]the shade.

[014-010]TESMAN.

[014-011][In an outburst of mingled doubt and joy.] Hedda! Oh, is this true?

[014-012]But--but--I never knew you show your love like that before. Fancy that!

[014-013]HEDDA.

[014-014]Well, I may as well tell you that--just at this time-- [Impatiently

[014-015]breaking off.] No, no; you can ask Aunt Julia. She will tell you, fast

[014-016]enough.

[014-017]TESMAN.

[014-018]Oh, I almost think I understand you, Hedda! [Clasps his hands together.]

[014-019]Great heavens! do you really mean it! Eh?

[014-020]HEDDA.

[014-021]Don't shout so. The servant might hear.

[014-022]TESMAN.

[014-023][Laughing in irrepressible glee.] The servant! Why, how absurd you are,

[014-024]Hedda. It's only my old [[Berta]]! Why, I'll tell Berta myself.

[014-025]HEDDA.

[014-026][Clenching her hands together in desperation.] Oh, it is killing me,

[014-027]--it is killing me, all this!

[014-028]TESMAN.

[014-029]What is, Hedda? Eh?

[014-030]HEDDA.

[014-031][Coldly, controlling herself.] All this--absurdity--George.

[014-032]TESMAN.

[014-033]Absurdity! Do you see anything absurd in my being overjoyed at the news!

[014-034]But after all--perhaps I had better not say anything to Berta.

[014-035]HEDDA.

[014-036]Oh--why not that too?

[014-037]TESMAN.

[014-038]No, no, not yet! But I must certainly tell Aunt Julia. And then that you

[014-039]have begun to call me George too! Fancy that! Oh, Aunt Julia will be so

[014-040]happy--so happy!

[014-041]HEDDA.

[014-042]When she hears that I have burnt [[Eilert-Lovborg|Eilert Lovborg]]'s manuscript--for your

[014-043]sake?

[014-044]TESMAN.

[014-045]No, by-the-bye--that affair of the manuscript--of course nobody must

[014-046]know about that. But that you love me so much,(13) Hedda--Aunt Julia

[014-047]must really share my joy in that! I wonder, now, whether this sort of

[014-048]thing is usual in young wives? Eh?

[014-049]HEDDA.

[014-050]I think you had better ask Aunt Julia that question too.

[014-051]TESMAN.

[014-052]I will indeed, some time or other. [Looks uneasy and downcast again.]

[014-053]And yet the manuscript--the manuscript! Good God! it is terrible to

[014-054]think what will become of poor Eilert now.

[014-055]MRS. ELVSTED, dressed as in the first Act, with hat and cloak,

[014-056]enters by the hall door.

[014-057]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-058][Greets them hurriedly, and says in evident agitation.] Oh, dear Hedda,

[014-059]forgive my coming again.

[014-060]HEDDA.

[014-061]What is the matter with you, Thea?

[014-062]TESMAN.

[014-063]Something about Eilert Lovborg again--eh?

[014-064]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-065]Yes! I am dreadfully afraid some misfortune has happened to him.

[014-066]HEDDA.

[014-067][Seized her arm.] Ah,--do you think so?

[014-068]TESMAN.

[014-069]Why, good Lord--what makes you think that, [[Mrs-Elvsted|Mrs. Elvsted]]?

[014-070]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-071]I heard them talking of him at my boarding-house--just as I came in. Oh,

[014-072]the most incredible rumours are afloat about him to-day.

[014-073]TESMAN.

[014-074]Yes, fancy, so I heard too! And I can bear witness that he went straight

[014-075]home to bed last night. Fancy that!

[014-076]HEDDA.

[014-077]Well, what did they say at the boarding-house?

[014-078]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-079]Oh, I couldn't make out anything clearly. Either they knew nothing

[014-080]definite, or else--. They stopped talking when the saw me; and I did

[014-081]not dare to ask.

[014-082]TESMAN.

[014-083][Moving about uneasily.] We must hope--we must hope that you

[014-084]misunderstood them, Mrs. Elvsted.

[014-085]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-086]No, no; I am sure it was of him they were talking. And I heard something

[014-087]about the hospital or--

[014-088]TESMAN.

[014-089]The hospital?

[014-090]HEDDA.

[014-091]No--surely that cannot be!

[014-092]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-093]Oh, I was in such mortal terror! I went to his lodgings and asked for

[014-094]him there.

[014-095]HEDDA.

[014-096]You could make up your mind to that, Thea!

[014-097]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-098]What else could I do? I really could bear the suspense no longer.

[014-099]TESMAN.

[014-100]But you didn't find him either--eh?

[014-101]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-102]No. And the people knew nothing about him. He hadn't been home since

[014-103]yesterday afternoon, they said.

[014-104]TESMAN.

[014-105]Yesterday! Fancy, how could they say that?

[014-106]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-107]Oh, I am sure something terrible must have happened to him.

[014-108]TESMAN.

[014-109]Hedda dear--how would it be if I were to go and make inquiries--?

[014-110]HEDDA.

[014-111]No, no--don't you mix yourself up in this affair.

[014-112]JUDGE BRACK, with his hat in his hand, enters by the hall

[014-113]door, which BERTA opens, and closes behind him. He looks

[014-114]grave and bows in silence.

[014-115]TESMAN.

[014-116]Oh, is that you, my dear Judge? Eh?

[014-117]BRACK.

[014-118]Yes. It was imperative I should see you this evening.

[014-119]TESMAN.

[014-120]I can see you have heard the news about [[Aunt-Rina|Aunt Rina]]?

[014-121]BRACK.

[014-122]Yes, that among other things.

[014-123]TESMAN.

[014-124]Isn't it sad--eh?

[014-125]BRACK.

[014-126]Well, my dear Tesman, that depends on how you look at it.

[014-127]TESMAN.

[014-128][Looks doubtfully at him.] Has anything else happened?

[014-129]BRACK.

[014-130]Yes.

[014-131]HEDDA.

[014-132][In suspense.] Anything sad, [[Judge-Brack|Judge Brack]]?

[014-133]BRACK.

[014-134]That, too, depends on how you look at it, Mrs. Tesman.

[014-135]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-136][Unable to restrain her anxiety.] Oh! it is something about Eilert

[014-137]Lovborg!

[014-138]BRACK.

[014-139][With a glance at her.] What makes you think that, Madam? Perhaps you

[014-140]have already heard something--?

[014-141]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-142][In confusion.] No, nothing at all, but--

[014-143]TESMAN.

[014-144]Oh, for heaven's sake, tell us!

[014-145]BRACK.

[014-146][Shrugging his shoulders.] Well, I regret to say Eilert Lovborg has been

[014-147]taken to the hospital. He is lying at the point of death.

[014-148]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-149][Shrieks.] Oh God! oh God--!

[014-150]TESMAN.

[014-151]To the hospital! And at the point of death!

[014-152]HEDDA.

[014-153][Involuntarily.] So soon then--

[014-154]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-155][Wailing.] And we parted in anger, Hedda!

[014-156]HEDDA.

[014-157][Whispers.] Thea--Thea--be careful!

[014-158]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-159][Not heeding her.] I must go to him! I must see him alive!

[014-160]BRACK.

[014-161]It is useless, Madam. No one will be admitted.

[014-162]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-163]Oh, at least tell me what has happened to him? What is it?

[014-164]TESMAN.

[014-165]You don't mean to say that he has himself-- Eh?

[014-166]HEDDA.

[014-167]Yes, I am sure he has.

[014-168]BRACK.

[014-169][Keeping his eyes fixed upon her.] Unfortunately you have guessed quite

[014-170]correctly, Mrs. Tesman.

[014-171]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-172]Oh, how horrible!

[014-173]TESMAN.

[014-174]Himself, then! Fancy that!

[014-175]HEDDA.

[014-176]Shot himself!

[014-177]BRACK.

[014-178]Rightly guessed again, Mrs. Tesman.

[014-179]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-180][With an effort at self-control.] When did it happen, Mr. Brack?

[014-181]BRACK.

[014-182]This afternoon--between three and four.

[014-183]TESMAN.

[014-184]But, good Lord, where did he do it? Eh?

[014-185]BRACK.

[014-186][With some hesitation.] Where? Well--I suppose at his lodgings.

[014-187]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-188]No, that cannot be; for I was there between six and seven.

[014-189]BRACK.

[014-190]Well then, somewhere else. I don't know exactly. I only know that he was

[014-191]found--. He had shot himself--in the breast.

[014-192]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-193]Oh, how terrible! That he should die like that!

[014-194]HEDDA.

[014-195][To BRACK.] Was it in the breast?

[014-196]BRACK.

[014-197]Yes--as I told you.

[014-198]HEDDA.

[014-199]Not in the temple?

[014-200]BRACK.

[014-201]In the breast, Mrs. Tesman.

[014-202]HEDDA.

[014-203]Well, well--the breast is a good place, too.

[014-204]BRACK.

[014-205]How do you mean, Mrs. Tesman?

[014-206]HEDDA.

[014-207][Evasively.] Oh, nothing--nothing.

[014-208]TESMAN.

[014-209]And the wound is dangerous, you say--eh?

[014-210]BRACK.

[014-211]Absolutely mortal. The end has probably come by this time.

[014-212]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-213]Yes, yes, I feel it. The end! The end! Oh, Hedda--!

[014-214]TESMAN.

[014-215]But tell me, how have you learnt all this?

[014-216]BRACK.

[014-217][Curtly.] Through one of the police. A man I had some business with.

[014-218]HEDDA.

[014-219][In a clear voice.] At last a deed worth doing!

[014-220]TESMAN.

[014-221][Terrified.] Good heavens, Hedda! what are you saying?

[014-222]HEDDA.

[014-223]I say there is beauty in this.

[014-224]BRACK.

[014-225]H'm, Mrs. Tesman--

[014-226]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-227]Oh, Hedda, how can you talk of beauty in such an act!

[014-228]HEDDA.

[014-229]Eilert Lovborg has himself made up his account with life. He has had the

[014-230]courage to do--the one right thing.

[014-231]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-232]No, you must never think that was how it happened! It must have been in

[014-233]delirium that he did it.

[014-234]TESMAN.

[014-235]In despair!

[014-236]HEDDA.

[014-237]That he did not. I am certain of that.

[014-238]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-239]Yes, yes! In delirium! Just as when he tore up our manuscript.

[014-240]BRACK.

[014-241][Starting.] The manuscript? Has he torn that up?

[014-242]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-243]Yes, last night.

[014-244]TESMAN.

[014-245][Whispers softly.] Oh, Hedda, we shall never get over this.

[014-246]BRACK.

[014-247]H'm, very extraordinary.

[014-248]TESMAN.

[014-249][Moving about the room.] To think of Eilert going out of the world

[014-250]in this way! And not leaving behind him the book that would have

[014-251]immortalised his name--

[014-252]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-253]Oh, if only it could be put together again!

[014-254]TESMAN.

[014-255]Yes, if it only could! I don't know what I would not give--

[014-256]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-257]Perhaps it can, Mr. Tesman.

[014-258]TESMAN.

[014-259]What do you mean?

[014-260]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-261][Searches in the pocket of her dress.] Look here. I have kept all the

[014-262]loose notes he used to dictate from.

[014-263]HEDDA.

[014-264][A step forward.] Ah--!

[014-265]TESMAN.

[014-266]You have kept them, Mrs. Elvsted! Eh?

[014-267]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-268]Yes, I have them here. I put them in my pocket when I left home. Here

[014-269]they still are--

[014-270]TESMAN.

[014-271]Oh, do let me see them!

[014-272]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-273][Hands him a bundle of papers.] But they are in such disorder--all mixed

[014-274]up.

[014-275]TESMAN.

[014-276]Fancy, if we could make something out of them, after all! Perhaps if we

[014-277]two put our heads together--

[014-278]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-279]Oh yes, at least let us try--

[014-280]TESMAN.

[014-281]We will manage it! We must! I will dedicate my life to this task.

[014-282]HEDDA.

[014-283]You, George? Your life?

[014-284]TESMAN.

[014-285]Yes, or rather all the time I can spare. My own collections must wait in

[014-286]the meantime. Hedda--you understand, eh? I owe this to Eilert's memory.

[014-287]HEDDA.

[014-288]Perhaps.

[014-289]TESMAN.

[014-290]And so, my dear Mrs. Elvsted, we will give our whole minds to it. There

[014-291]is no use in brooding over what can't be undone--eh? We must try to

[014-292]control our grief as much as possible, and--

[014-293]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-294]Yes, yes, Mr. Tesman, I will do the best I can.

[014-295]TESMAN.

[014-296]Well then, come here. I can't rest until we have looked through the

[014-297]notes. Where shall we sit? Here? No, in there, in the back room. Excuse

[014-298]me, my dear Judge. Come with me, Mrs. Elvsted.

[014-299]MRS. ELVSTED.

[014-300]Oh, if only it were possible!

[014-301][TESMAN and MRS. ELVSTED go into the back room. She takes

[014-302]off her hat and cloak. They both sit at the table under the

[014-303]hanging lamp, and are soon deep in an eager examination of

[014-304]the papers. HEDDA crosses to the stove and sits in the arm-

[014-305]chair. Presently BRACK goes up to her.

[014-306]HEDDA.

[014-307][In a low voice.] Oh, what a sense of freedom it gives one, this act of

[014-308]Eilert Lovborg's.

[014-309]BRACK.

[014-310]Freedom, Mrs. Hedda? Well, of course, it is a release for him--

[014-311]HEDDA.

[014-312]I mean for me. It gives me a sense of freedom to know that a deed

[014-313]of deliberate courage is still possible in this world,--a deed of

[014-314]spontaneous beauty.

[014-315]BRACK.

[014-316][Smiling.] H'm--my dear Mrs. Hedda--

[014-317]HEDDA.

[014-318]Oh, I know what you are going to say. For you are a kind of specialist

[014-319]too, like--you know!

[014-320]BRACK.

[014-321][Looking hard at her.] Eilert Lovborg was more to you than perhaps you

[014-322]are willing to admit to yourself. Am I wrong?

[014-323]HEDDA.

[014-324]I don't answer such questions. I only know that Eilert Lovborg has had

[014-325]the courage to live his life after his own fashion. And then--the last

[014-327]strength to turn away from the banquet of life--so early.

[014-328]BRACK.

[014-329]I am sorry, Mrs. Hedda,--but I fear I must dispel an amiable illusion.

[014-330]HEDDA.

[014-331]Illusion?

[014-332]BRACK.

[014-333]Which could not have lasted long in any case.

[014-334]HEDDA.

[014-335]What do you mean?

[014-336]BRACK.

[014-337]Eilert Lovborg did not shoot himself--voluntarily.

[014-338]HEDDA.

[014-339]Not voluntarily?

[014-340]BRACK.

[014-341]No. The thing did not happen exactly as I told it.

[014-342]HEDDA.

[014-343][In suspense.] Have you concealed something? What is it?

[014-344]BRACK.

[014-345]For poor Mrs. Elvsted's sake I idealised the facts a little.

[014-346]HEDDA.

[014-347]What are the facts?

[014-348]BRACK.

[014-349]First, that he is already dead.

[014-350]HEDDA.

[014-351]At the hospital?

[014-352]BRACK.

[014-353]Yes--without regaining consciousness.

[014-354]HEDDA.

[014-355]What more have you concealed?

[014-356]BRACK.

[014-357]This--the event did not happen at his lodgings.

[014-358]HEDDA.

[014-359]Oh, that can make no difference.

[014-360]BRACK.

[014-361]Perhaps it may. For I must tell you--Eilert Lovborg was found shot

[014-362]in--in [[Mademoiselle-Dianas|Mademoiselle Diana's]] boudoir.

[014-363]HEDDA.

[014-364][Makes a motion as if to rise, but sinks back again.] That is
