# George Tesman — Key Relationships

## Hedda Gabler (wife)

Tesman adores [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]] without understanding her. He purchased the [[Tesman-Villa|Falk Villa]] on credit because she once admired it during a walk — a casual remark converted into a binding financial obligation. He persuaded his aunt [[Aunt-Julia|Juliana]] to mortgage her annuity as security. The villa is built on love, misunderstanding, and sacrifice.

His first exchange with Hedda reveals their dynamic: he is solicitous, she is dismissive. He mentions her "filling out" — an oblique reference to pregnancy — and she cuts him off. He cannot read her signals because he cannot imagine that someone he loves might not love him back.

## Aunt Juliana (surrogate mother)

[[Aunt-Julia]] raised Tesman after his parents' death. She mortgaged her annuity to secure the villa; he is "so glad" to see her. Hedda's contempt for this relationship (insulting Aunt Julia's new bonnet) is one of the play's cruelest moments.

> TESMAN: [Searching among his books.] And look here--I have got hold of Eilert
> Lovborg's new book too. [Offering it to her.] Perhaps you would like to
> glance through it, Hedda? Eh?
>
> (007-321)

## Eilert Lovborg (rival and admirer)

Tesman's relationship with [[Eilert-Lovborg|Lovborg]] is defined by professional rivalry and genuine admiration. Lovborg's new book threatens Tesman's professorship, yet Tesman is "tremendously" impressed by it.

## Mrs. Elvsted (future collaborator)

After Hedda's suicide, Tesman sits with [[Mrs-Elvsted|Thea]] reconstructing [[The-Manuscript|Lovborg's manuscript]] from her notes. This closing image — the plodding scholar and the devoted amanuensis, united in grief and labor — suggests a future partnership built on shared scholarly devotion.
