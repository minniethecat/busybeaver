# Curtains: Location and Function

Ibsen's stage directions specify two sets of curtains in the [[Tesman-Villa]], each controlling visibility across a critical threshold.

**Glass Door Curtains**: "Curtains drawn back from the glass door" (002-147), on the left wall. These control the morning sun and the view of the garden and veranda — the boundary between domestic interior and external world. [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]] manipulates them constantly: drawing them against unwanted sunlight, opening them to watch for [[Eilert-Lovborg|Lovborg]], closing them at evening as the world contracts.

**Inner Room Doorway Curtains**: Covering the upstage center doorway. These control visibility into [[The-Inner-Room|the private chambers]] — the boundary between the social and the intimate, between performance and concealment. Unlike the glass door curtains, which have a solid door behind them, the inner room curtains cover only an opening. There is no door to close — only fabric to draw.

Neither set is a solid barrier. Both are permeable to sound, manipulable by hand, and fundamentally temporary. They can conceal but not lock; they can dim but not darken. Their porosity is their meaning: the curtained boundary is always on the verge of being breached.

The two sets of curtains together divide the villa into three zones of visibility: the outside world (beyond the glass door), the social arena (drawing room, between the curtains), and the private interior (inner room, behind the second curtain). Hedda moves between these zones, adjusting the curtains as she goes, in a choreography of exposure and concealment that maps her psychological state.

## From the Text

> *[A spacious, handsome, and tastefully furnished drawing-room, decorated in dark colours. In the back wall, a wide doorway with curtains drawn back, leading into a smaller room decorated in the same style as the drawing-room. In the right-hand wall of the front room, a folding door leading out to the hall. In the opposite wall, on the left, a glass door, also with curtains drawn back. Through the panes can be seen part of a verandah outside, and trees covered with autumn foliage.]*

> (002-144)
