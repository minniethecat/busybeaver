# Realism and Symbolism in Hedda Gabler

*Hedda Gabler* is both a realist play — with naturalistic dialogue, a meticulously described bourgeois drawing room, and near-real-time action — and a dense symbolic system in which every object and space carries metaphorical weight.

For how objects operate simultaneously on realist and symbolic registers, see [[Realism-Symbolism-Double-Register]]. The villa's spatial arrangement as Ibsen's most ambitious symbolic construction is examined in [[Symbolic-Architecture-Hedda-Gabler]].

[[Realism-Symbolism-Influence]] situates the play as a pivotal moment in dramatic history: the culmination of bourgeois realism and the anticipation of its dissolution into Chekhov's and Strindberg's symbolic methods.

Ibsen's method grounds every symbol in realist probability. The pistols are symbolic and also General Gabler's actual pistols. The manuscript is a symbol of parenthood and also a real scholarly work. The drawing room is just a drawing room — and it is also a cage, a stage, and a tomb. The play's realism makes us believe; its symbolism makes us feel the cost.
