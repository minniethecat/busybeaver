[003-001]MISS TESMAN.

[003-002]Oh, I shall manage to train her. And of course, you know, I shall take

[003-003]most of it upon myself. You needn't be uneasy about my poor sister, my

[003-004]dear [[Berta]].

[003-005]BERTA.

[003-006]Well, but there's another thing, Miss. I'm so mortally afraid I shan't

[003-007]be able to suit the young mistress.

[003-008]MISS TESMAN.

[003-009]Oh well--just at first there may be one or two things--

[003-010]BERTA.

[003-011]Most like she'll be terrible grand in her ways.

[003-012]MISS TESMAN.

[003-013]Well, you can't wonder at that--General Gabler's daughter! Think of

[003-014]the sort of life she was accustomed to in her father's time. Don't you

[003-015]remember how we used to see her riding down the road along with the

[003-016]General? In that long black habit--and with feathers in her hat?

[003-017]BERTA.

[003-018]Yes, indeed--I remember well enough!--But, good Lord, I should never

[003-019]have dreamt in those days that she and Master George would make a match

[003-020]of it.

[003-021]MISS TESMAN.

[003-022]Nor I.--But by-the-bye, Berta--while I think of it: in future you

[003-023]mustn't say Master George. You must say Dr. Tesman.

[003-024]BERTA.

[003-025]Yes, the young mistress spoke of that too--last night--the moment they

[003-026]set foot in the house. Is it true then, Miss?

[003-027]MISS TESMAN.

[003-028]Yes, indeed it is. Only think, Berta--some foreign university has made

[003-029]him a doctor--while he has been abroad, you understand. I hadn't heard a

[003-030]word about it, until he told me himself upon the pier.

[003-031]BERTA.

[003-032]Well well, he's clever enough for anything, he is. But I didn't think

[003-033]he'd have gone in for doctoring people.

[003-034]MISS TESMAN.

[003-035]No no, it's not that sort of doctor he is. [Nods significantly.] But

[003-036]let me tell you, we may have to call him something still grander before

[003-037]long.

[003-038]BERTA.

[003-039]You don't say so! What can that be, Miss?

[003-040]MISS TESMAN.

[003-041][Smiling.] H'm--wouldn't you like to know! [With emotion.] Ah, dear

[003-042]dear--if my poor brother could only look up from his grave now, and

[003-043]see what his little boy has grown into! [Looks around.] But bless me,

[003-044]Berta--why have you done this? Taken the chintz covers off all the

[003-045]furniture.

[003-046]BERTA.

[003-047]The mistress told me to. She can't abide covers on the chairs, she says.

[003-048]MISS TESMAN.

[003-049]Are they going to make this their everyday sitting-room then?

[003-050]BERTA.

[003-051]Yes, that's what I understood--from the mistress. Master George--the

[003-052]doctor--he said nothing.

[003-053]GEORGE TESMAN comes from the right into the inner room,

[003-054]humming to himself, and carrying an unstrapped empty

[003-055]portmanteau. He is a middle-sized, young-looking man of

[003-056]thirty-three, rather stout, with a round, open, cheerful

[003-057]face, fair hair and beard. He wears spectacles, and is

[003-058]somewhat carelessly dressed in comfortable indoor clothes.

[003-059]MISS TESMAN.

[003-060]Good morning, good morning, George.

[003-061]TESMAN.

[003-062][In the doorway between the rooms.] Aunt Julia! Dear Aunt Julia! [Goes

[003-063]up to her and shakes hands warmly.] Come all this way--so early! Eh?

[003-064]MISS TESMAN.

[003-065]Why, of course I had to come and see how you were getting on.

[003-066]TESMAN.

[003-067]In spite of your having had no proper night's rest?

[003-068]MISS TESMAN.

[003-069]Oh, that makes no difference to me.

[003-070]TESMAN.

[003-071]Well, I suppose you got home all right from the pier? Eh?

[003-072]MISS TESMAN.

[003-073]Yes, quite safely, thank goodness. [[Judge-Brack|Judge Brack]] was good enough to see me

[003-074]right to my door.

[003-075]TESMAN.

[003-076]We were so sorry we couldn't give you a seat in the carriage. But you

[003-077]saw what a pile of boxes Hedda had to bring with her.

[003-078]MISS TESMAN.

[003-079]Yes, she had certainly plenty of boxes.

[003-080]BERTA.

[003-081][To TESMAN.] Shall I go in and see if there's anything I can do for the

[003-082]mistress?

[003-083]TESMAN.

[003-084]No thank you, Berta--you needn't. She said she would ring if she wanted

[003-085]anything.

[003-086]BERTA.

[003-087][Going towards the right.] Very well.

[003-088]TESMAN.

[003-089]But look here--take this portmanteau with you.

[003-090]BERTA.

[003-091][Taking it.] I'll put it in the attic.

[003-092][She goes out by the hall door.

[003-093]TESMAN.

[003-094]Fancy, [[Aunt-Julia|Auntie]]--I had the whole of that portmanteau chock full of copies

[003-095]of the documents. You wouldn't believe how much I have picked up from

[003-096]all the archives I have been examining--curious old details that no one

[003-097]has had any idea of--

[003-098]MISS TESMAN.

[003-099]Yes, you don't seem to have wasted your time on your wedding trip,

[003-100]George.

[003-101]TESMAN.

[003-102]No, that I haven't. But do take off your bonnet, Auntie. Look here! Let

[003-103]me untie the strings--eh?

[003-104]MISS TESMAN.

[003-105][While he does so.] Well well--this is just as if you were still at home

[003-106]with us.

[003-107]TESMAN.

[003-108][With the bonnet in his hand, looks at it from all sides.] Why, what a

[003-109]gorgeous bonnet you've been investing in!

[003-110]MISS TESMAN.

[003-111]I bought it on Hedda's account.

[003-112]TESMAN.

[003-113]On Hedda's account? Eh?

[003-114]MISS TESMAN.

[003-115]Yes, so that Hedda needn't be ashamed of me if we happened to go out

[003-116]together.

[003-117]TESMAN.

[003-118][Patting her cheek.] You always think of everything, Aunt Julia. [Lays

[003-119]the bonnet on a chair beside the table.] And now, look here--suppose we

[003-120]sit comfortably on the sofa and have a little chat, till Hedda comes.

[003-121][They seat themselves. She places her parasol in the corner

[003-122]of the sofa.

[003-123]MISS TESMAN.

[003-124][Takes both his hands and looks at him.] What a delight it is to have

[003-125]you again, as large as life, before my very eyes, George! My George--my

[003-126]poor brother's own boy!

[003-127]TESMAN.

[003-128]And it's a delight for me, too, to see you again, Aunt Julia! You, who

[003-129]have been father and mother in one to me.

[003-130]MISS TESMAN.

[003-131]Oh yes, I know you will always keep a place in your heart for your old

[003-132]aunts.

[003-133]TESMAN.

[003-134]And what about [[Aunt-Rina|Aunt Rina]]? No improvement--eh?

[003-135]MISS TESMAN.

[003-136]Oh, no--we can scarcely look for any improvement in her case, poor

[003-137]thing. There she lies, helpless, as she has lain for all these years.

[003-138]But heaven grant I may not lose her yet awhile! For if I did, I don't

[003-139]know what I should make of my life, George--especially now that I

[003-140]haven't you to look after any more.

[003-141]TESMAN.

[003-142][Patting her back.] There there there--!

[003-143]MISS TESMAN.

[003-144][Suddenly changing her tone.] And to think that here are you a married

[003-145]man, George!--And that you should be the one to carry off [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda Gabler]]

[003-146]--the beautiful Hedda Gabler! Only think of it--she, that was so beset

[003-147]with admirers!

[003-148]TESMAN.

[003-149][Hums a little and smiles complacently.] Yes, I fancy I have several

[003-150]good friends about town who would like to stand in my shoes--eh?

[003-151]MISS TESMAN.

[003-152]And then this fine long wedding-tour you have had! More than five--

[003-153]nearly six months--

[003-154]TESMAN.

[003-155]Well, for me it has been a sort of tour of research as well. I have had

[003-156]to do so much grubbing among old records--and to read no end of books

[003-157]too, Auntie.

[003-158]MISS TESMAN.

[003-159]Oh yes, I suppose so. [More confidentially, and lowering her voice a

[003-160]little.] But listen now, George,--have you nothing--nothing special to

[003-161]tell me?

[003-162]TESMAN.

[003-163]As to our journey?

[003-164]MISS TESMAN.

[003-165]Yes.

[003-166]TESMAN.

[003-167]No, I don't know of anything except what I have told you in my letters.

[003-168]I had a doctor's degree conferred on me--but that I told you yesterday.

[003-169]MISS TESMAN.

[003-170]Yes, yes, you did. But what I mean is--haven't you any--any--

[003-171]expectations--?

[003-172]TESMAN.

[003-173]Expectations?

[003-174]MISS TESMAN.

[003-175]Why you know, George--I'm your old auntie!

[003-176]TESMAN.

[003-177]Why, of course I have expectations.

[003-178]MISS TESMAN.

[003-179]Ah!

[003-180]TESMAN.

[003-181]I have every expectation of being a professor one of these days.

[003-182]MISS TESMAN.

[003-183]Oh yes, a professor--

[003-184]TESMAN.

[003-185]Indeed, I may say I am certain of it. But my dear Auntie--you know all

[003-186]about that already!

[003-187]MISS TESMAN.

[003-188][Laughing to herself.] Yes, of course I do. You are quite right there.

[003-189][Changing the subject.] But we were talking about your journey. It must

[003-190]have cost a great deal of money, George?

[003-191]TESMAN.

[003-192]Well, you see--my handsome travelling-scholarship went a good way.

[003-193]MISS TESMAN.

[003-194]But I can't understand how you can have made it go far enough for two.

[003-195]TESMAN.

[003-196]No, that's not easy to understand--eh?

[003-197]MISS TESMAN.

[003-198]And especially travelling with a lady--they tell me that makes it ever

[003-199]so much more expensive.

[003-200]TESMAN.

[003-201]Yes, of course--it makes it a little more expensive. But Hedda had to

[003-202]have this trip, Auntie! She really had to. Nothing else would have done.

[003-203]MISS TESMAN.

[003-204]No no, I suppose not. A wedding-tour seems to be quite indispensable

[003-205]nowadays.--But tell me now--have you gone thoroughly over the house yet?

[003-206]TESMAN.

[003-207]Yes, you may be sure I have. I have been afoot ever since daylight.

[003-208]MISS TESMAN.

[003-209]And what do you think of it all?

[003-210]TESMAN.

[003-211]I'm delighted! Quite delighted! Only I can't think what we are to do

[003-212]with the two empty rooms between this inner parlour and Hedda's bedroom.

[003-213]MISS TESMAN.

[003-214][Laughing.] Oh my dear George, I daresay you may find some use for

[003-215]them--in the course of time.

[003-216]TESMAN.

[003-217]Why of course you are quite right, Aunt Julia! You mean as my library

[003-218]increases--eh?

[003-219]MISS TESMAN.

[003-220]Yes, quite so, my dear boy. It was your library I was thinking of.

[003-221]TESMAN.

[003-222]I am specially pleased on Hedda's account. Often and often, before we

[003-223]were engaged, she said that she would never care to live anywhere but in

[003-224]Secretary Falk's villa.(2)

[003-225]MISS TESMAN.

[003-226]Yes, it was lucky that this very house should come into the market, just

[003-227]after you had started.

[003-228]TESMAN.

[003-229]Yes, Aunt Julia, the luck was on our side, wasn't it--eh?

[003-230]MISS TESMAN.

[003-231]But the expense, my dear George! You will find it very expensive, all

[003-232]this.

[003-233]TESMAN.

[003-234][Looks at her, a little cast down.] Yes, I suppose I shall, Aunt!

[003-235]MISS TESMAN.

[003-236]Oh, frightfully!

[003-237]TESMAN.

[003-238]How much do you think? In round numbers?--Eh?

[003-239]MISS TESMAN.

[003-240]Oh, I can't even guess until all the accounts come in.

[003-241]TESMAN.

[003-242]Well, fortunately, Judge Brack has secured the most favourable terms for

[003-243]me, so he said in a letter to Hedda.

[003-244]MISS TESMAN.

[003-245]Yes, don't be uneasy, my dear boy.--Besides, I have given security for

[003-246]the furniture and all the carpets.

[003-247]TESMAN.

[003-248]Security? You? My dear Aunt Julia--what sort of security could you give?

[003-249]MISS TESMAN.

[003-250]I have given a mortgage on our annuity.

[003-251]TESMAN.

[003-252][Jumps up.] What! On your--and Aunt Rina's annuity!

[003-253]MISS TESMAN.

[003-254]Yes, I knew of no other plan, you see.

[003-255]TESMAN.

[003-256][Placing himself before her.] Have you gone out of your senses, Auntie?

[003-257]Your annuity--it's all that you and Aunt Rina have to live upon.

[003-258]MISS TESMAN.

[003-259]Well well--don't get so excited about it. It's only a matter of form you

[003-260]know--Judge Brack assured me of that. It was he that was kind enough to

[003-261]arrange the whole affair for me. A mere matter of form, he said.

[003-262]TESMAN.

[003-263]Yes, that may be all very well. But nevertheless--

[003-264]MISS TESMAN.

[003-265]You will have your own salary to depend upon now. And, good heavens,

[003-266]even if we did have to pay up a little--! To eke things out a bit at

[003-267]the start--! Why, it would be nothing but a pleasure to us.

[003-268]TESMAN.

[003-269]Oh Auntie--will you never be tired of making sacrifices for me!

[003-270]MISS TESMAN.

[003-271][Rises and lays her hand on his shoulders.] Have I any other happiness

[003-272]in this world except to smooth your way for you, my dear boy. You, who

[003-273]have had neither father nor mother to depend on. And now we have reached

[003-274]the goal, George! Things have looked black enough for us, sometimes;

[003-275]but, thank heaven, now you have nothing to fear.

[003-276]TESMAN.

[003-277]Yes, it is really marvellous how every thing has turned out for the

[003-278]best.

[003-279]MISS TESMAN.

[003-280]And the people who opposed you--who wanted to bar the way for you--

[003-281]now you have them at your feet. They have fallen, George. Your most

[003-282]dangerous rival--his fall was the worst.--And now he has to lie on the

[003-283]bed he has made for himself--poor misguided creature.

[003-284]TESMAN.

[003-285]Have you heard anything of Eilert? Since I went away, I mean.

[003-286]MISS TESMAN.

[003-287]Only that he is said to have published a new book.

[003-288]TESMAN.

[003-289]What! [[Eilert-Lovborg|Eilert Lovborg]]! Recently--eh?

[003-290]MISS TESMAN.

[003-291]Yes, so they say. Heaven knows whether it can be worth anything! Ah,

[003-292]when your new book appears--that will be another story, George! What is

[003-293]it to be about?
