## Notes

Ibsen populated *Hedda Gabler* with a deliberately small cast, believing that concentrated characterization produced greater dramatic intensity. (001-138) The ensemble — centered on Hedda Tesman (née Gabler), her husband George Tesman, and the rival scholars Eilert Lovborg and Judge Brack — forms an intricate web of desire, manipulation, and social constraint. ## From the Text

> It is impossible--or so it seems to me--to extract any sort of general idea from it. One cannot even call it a satire, unless one is prepared to apply that term to the record of a "case" in a work of criminology. Reverting to Dumas's dictum that a play should contain "a painting, a judgment, an ideal," we may say the *Hedda Gabler* fulfils only the first of these requirements. The poet does not even pass judgment on his heroine: he simply paints her full-length portrait with scientific impassivity. But what a portrait! How searching in insight, how brilliant in colouring, how rich in detail!

> (002-066)
