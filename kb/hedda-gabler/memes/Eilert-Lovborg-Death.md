# Eilert Lovborg — Death

Lovborg's death occurs offstage at [[Mademoiselle-Dianas]] but is reported in gruesome detail by [[Judge-Brack|Brack]]:

> "Shot himself — in the breast. Killed himself on the spot."
> "Not through the temple, as you ordered — through the breast." (012-141)

The death is the systematic annihilation of [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]]'s "vine-leaves" fantasy. Every aesthetic detail she specified for his suicide — the temple shot, the beautiful gesture, the heroic solitude — is replaced by its squalid opposite. The pistol meant for a tragic exit becomes the instrument of a farcical brawl.

Lovborg dies not as a Greek hero but as a drunk in a brothel. The location matters: the [[Tesman-Villa|drawing-room]] where Hedda imagines beautiful deaths is replaced by [[Mademoiselle-Dianas|Mademoiselle Diana's boudoir]], the anti-drawing-room where aesthetic fantasies collide with material reality.

Brack's clinical delivery — precise, knowing, faintly amused — compounds the horror. He knows what Hedda wanted; he reports what actually happened. The gap between the two is the death of her last illusion. After this, nothing remains between Hedda and the truth of her own cruelty except the pistol she kept for herself.
