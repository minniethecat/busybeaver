# Mademoiselle Diana

The mysterious "red-haired woman" who presides over the off-stage demimonde in *Hedda Gabler*. She is never seen on stage, but her establishment is the site of Eilert Lovborg's fatal encounter. Diana's shadowy presence represents the world outside the Tesman villa — a world of sexual freedom, danger, and the consequences that Hedda simultaneously fears and is drawn toward.

Her name is loaded with irony: Diana was the Roman goddess of chastity and the hunt, while Mademoiselle Diana runs what amounts to a brothel. She claims Lovborg's accidental self-inflicted wound occurred at her establishment, though the circumstances remain deliberately ambiguous. Her testimony, filtered through Judge Brack, determines the official narrative of Lovborg's death — and ultimately provides Brack the leverage to trap Hedda.

## From the Text

> BRACK. For I must tell you — Eilert Lovborg was found shot in — in
> Mademoiselle Diana's boudoir.
>
> HEDDA. [Makes a motion as if to rise, but sinks back again.] That is
> impossible, Judge Brack! He cannot have been there again to-day.
>
> (014-361 to 014-365)
