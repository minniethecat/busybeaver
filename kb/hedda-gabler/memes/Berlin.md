Berlin was one of the first cities outside Scandinavia to stage *Hedda Gabler*. The Lessing Theater in Berlin presented the play on February 10, 1891, less than two weeks after the Munich world premiere. (001-085) The Berlin production formed part of the play's remarkably rapid spread across European theatres in early 1891.

## Ibsen and Berlin

During the composition of *Hedda Gabler* in Munich, Ibsen received a parcel from Berlin, the contents of which became part of the play's biographical lore. (001-168) The city had a significant Scandinavian expatriate and intellectual community with which Ibsen was connected.

## Critical Context

A Berlin performance of *Hedda Gabler* prompted Ibsen to reflect on the reception of his work in Germany, one of his most important theatrical markets. (002-113) The play was discussed in German literary publications including *Die Literatur* (Berlin). (002-104)
