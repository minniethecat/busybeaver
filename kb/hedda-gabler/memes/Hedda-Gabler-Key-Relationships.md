# Hedda Gabler: Key Relationships

## George Tesman (husband)

A scholarly but naive academic whom Hedda married out of convenience. She treats him with contempt and condescension. His genuine kindness — buying her the house she admired — only deepens her rejection. Hedda cannot love anyone who does not challenge her.

## Eilert Lovborg (former intimate)

Hedda's intellectual equal and romantic past. She "made him tell her things" while engaged to Tesman. When Lovborg returns with [[Mrs-Elvsted|Thea Elvsted]], reformed and productive, Hedda's envy becomes lethal.

## Judge Brack (antagonist)

A cynical friend who maneuvers Hedda into a "triangle" of complicity. Brack understands her and uses that to control her. "People don't do such things," he tells her (015-242) — meaning _you_ don't. But she did. And he knows.

> Where Thea Elvsted dared to venture all, Hedda, in her cowardice, only hankered and refrained.
>
> (002-086)

## Mrs. Elvsted (rival)

A schoolmate Hedda bullied as a girl. Thea has done what Hedda cannot: left a sterile marriage and built a creative partnership.
