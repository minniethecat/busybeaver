# Brack as Information Master

[[Judge-Brack]] is the play's only truly powerful character because he is the only one who understands power as information asymmetry. His strategy is systematic, and the play shows each of its phases in operation.

First, he cultivates access. He befriends [[George-Tesman|Tesman]] to gain entry to the household — "the back way" through the garden (006-096) becomes his established route. The friendship is genuine in Tesman's eyes but instrumental in Brack's: it provides the social cover he needs to position himself at every node in the villa's information network.

Second, he extracts confidences. He draws [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]] into intimate conversation, positioning himself as the trustworthy "man of the world" (007-147) who can be told things that Tesman cannot. Their private talks are the mechanism by which Hedda's secrets become Brack's assets.

Third, he gathers intelligence. He attends [[The-Bachelor-Party|the bachelor party]] not as a participant but as an observer — positioning himself to know everything about Lovborg's relapse, his death, and the pistol's provenance.

Fourth, he conceals his own motives. His "triangle" ambition (012-150) is stated openly to Hedda — but only to Hedda, and only to bind her more tightly. No one else knows his game.

Fifth, he deploys knowledge strategically. He never bluffs. When he threatens, he holds the cards. Brack's power is gendered — he can threaten exposure because society punishes female transgression while protecting male respectability. His method — gather everything, reveal nothing, use knowledge as leverage — is the play's anatomy of how power actually operates in bourgeois society.
