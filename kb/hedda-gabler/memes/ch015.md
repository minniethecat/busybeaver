[015-001]BRACK.

[015-002]He was there this afternoon. He went there, he said, to demand the

[015-003]return of something which they had taken from him. Talked wildly about a

[015-004]lost child--

[015-005]HEDDA.

[015-006]Ah--so that is why--

[015-007]BRACK.

[015-008]I thought probably he meant his manuscript; but now I hear he destroyed

[015-009]that himself. So I suppose it must have been his pocket-book.

[015-010]HEDDA.

[015-011]Yes, no doubt. And there--there he was found?

[015-012]BRACK.

[015-013]Yes, there. With a pistol in his breast-pocket, discharged. The ball had

[015-014]lodged in a vital part.

[015-015]HEDDA.

[015-016]In the breast--yes?

[015-017]BRACK.

[015-018]No--in the bowels.

[015-019]HEDDA.

[015-020][Looks up at him with an expression of loathing.] That too! Oh, what

[015-021]curse is it that makes everything I touch turn ludicrous and mean?

[015-022]BRACK.

[015-023]There is one point more, Mrs. Hedda--another disagreeable feature in the

[015-024]affair.

[015-025]HEDDA.

[015-026]And what is that?

[015-027]BRACK.

[015-028]The pistol he carried--

[015-029]HEDDA.

[015-030][Breathless.] Well? What of it?

[015-031]BRACK.

[015-032]He must have stolen it.

[015-033]HEDDA.

[015-034][Leaps up.] Stolen it! That is not true! He did not steal it!

[015-035]BRACK.

[015-036]No other explanation is possible. He must have stolen it--. Hush!

[015-037]TESMAN and MRS. ELVSTED have risen from the table in the back-

[015-038]room, and come into the drawing-room.

[015-039]TESMAN.

[015-040][With the papers in both his hands.] Hedda, dear, it is almost

[015-041]impossible to see under that lamp. Think of that!

[015-042]HEDDA.

[015-043]Yes, I am thinking.

[015-044]TESMAN.

[015-045]Would you mind our sitting at your writing-table--eh?

[015-046]HEDDA.

[015-047]If you like. [Quickly.] No, wait! Let me clear it first!

[015-048]TESMAN.

[015-049]Oh, you needn't trouble, Hedda. There is plenty of room.

[015-050]HEDDA.

[015-051]No no, let me clear it, I say! I will take these things in and put them

[015-052]on the piano. There!

[015-053][She has drawn out an object, covered with sheet music, from

[015-054]under the bookcase, places several other pieces of music upon

[015-055]it, and carries the whole into the inner room, to the left.

[015-056]TESMAN lays the scraps of paper on the writing-table, and moves

[015-057]the lamp there from the corner table. He and [[Mrs-Elvsted|Mrs. Elvsted]] sit

[015-058]down and proceed with their work. HEDDA returns.

[015-059]HEDDA.

[015-060][Behind Mrs. Elvsted's chair, gently ruffling her hair.] Well, my sweet

[015-061]Thea,--how goes it with [[Eilert-Lovborg|Eilert Lovborg]]'s monument?

[015-062]MRS. ELVSTED.

[015-063][Looks dispiritedly up at her.] Oh, it will be terribly hard to put in

[015-064]order.

[015-065]TESMAN.

[015-066]We must manage it. I am determined. And arranging other people's papers

[015-067]is just the work for me.

[015-068][HEDDA goes over to the stove, and seats herself on one of the

[015-069]footstools. BRACK stands over her, leaning on the arm-chair.

[015-070]HEDDA.

[015-071][Whispers.] What did you say about the pistol?

[015-072]BRACK.

[015-073][Softly.] That he must have stolen it.

[015-074]HEDDA.

[015-075]Why stolen it?

[015-076]BRACK.

[015-077]Because every other explanation ought to be impossible, Mrs. Hedda.

[015-078]HEDDA.

[015-079]Indeed?

[015-080]BRACK.

[015-081][Glances at her.] Of course Eilert Lovborg was here this morning. Was he

[015-082]not?

[015-083]HEDDA.

[015-084]Yes.

[015-085]BRACK.

[015-086]Were you alone with him?

[015-087]HEDDA.

[015-088]Part of the time.

[015-089]BRACK.

[015-090]Did you not leave the room whilst he was here?

[015-091]HEDDA.

[015-092]No.

[015-093]BRACK.

[015-094]Try to recollect. Were you not out of the room a moment?

[015-095]HEDDA.

[015-096]Yes, perhaps just a moment--out in the hall.

[015-097]BRACK.

[015-098]And where was your pistol-case during that time?

[015-099]HEDDA.

[015-100]I had it locked up in--

[015-101]BRACK.

[015-102]Well, Mrs. Hedda?

[015-103]HEDDA.

[015-104]The case stood there on the writing-table.

[015-105]BRACK.

[015-106]Have you looked since, to see whether both the pistols are there?

[015-107]HEDDA.

[015-108]No.

[015-109]BRACK.

[015-110]Well, you need not. I saw the pistol found in Lovborg's pocket, and I

[015-111]knew it at once as the one I had seen yesterday--and before, too.

[015-112]HEDDA.

[015-113]Have you it with you?

[015-114]BRACK.

[015-115]No; the police have it.

[015-116]HEDDA.

[015-117]What will the police do with it?

[015-118]BRACK.

[015-119]Search till they find the owner.

[015-120]HEDDA.

[015-121]Do you think they will succeed?

[015-122]BRACK.

[015-123][Bends over her and whispers.] No, [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda Gabler]]--not so long as I say

[015-124]nothing.

[015-125]HEDDA.

[015-126][Looks frightened at him.] And if you do not say nothing,--what then?

[015-127]BRACK.

[015-128][Shrugs his shoulders.] There is always the possibility that the pistol

[015-129]was stolen.

[015-130]HEDDA.

[015-131][Firmly.] Death rather than that.

[015-132]BRACK.

[015-133][Smiling.] People say such things--but they don't do them.

[015-134]HEDDA.

[015-135][Without replying.] And supposing the pistol was not stolen, and the

[015-136]owner is discovered? What then?

[015-137]BRACK.

[015-138]Well, Hedda--then comes the scandal!

[015-139]HEDDA.

[015-140]The scandal!

[015-141]BRACK.

[015-142]Yes, the scandal--of which you are so mortally afraid. You will, of

[015-143]course, be brought before the court--both you and [[Mademoiselle-Diana|Mademoiselle Diana]].

[015-144]She will have to explain how the thing happened--whether it was an

[015-145]accidental shot or murder. Did the pistol go off as he was trying to

[015-146]take it out of his pocket, to threaten her with? Or did she tear the

[015-147]pistol out of his hand, shoot him, and push it back into his pocket?

[015-148]That would be quite like her; for she is an able-bodied young person,

[015-149]this same Mademoiselle Diana.

[015-150]HEDDA.

[015-151]But _I_ have nothing to do with all this repulsive business.

[015-152]BRACK.

[015-153]No. But you will have to answer the question: Why did you give Eilert

[015-154]the pistol? And what conclusions will people draw from the fact that you

[015-155]did give it to him?

[015-156]HEDDA.

[015-157][Lets her head sink.] That is true. I did not think of that.

[015-158]BRACK.

[015-159]Well, fortunately, there is no danger, so long as I say nothing.

[015-160]HEDDA.

[015-161][Looks up at him.] So I am in your power, [[Judge-Brack|Judge Brack]]. You have me at

[015-162]your beck and call, from this time forward.

[015-163]BRACK.

[015-164][Whispers softly.] Dearest Hedda--believe me--I shall not abuse my

[015-165]advantage.

[015-166]HEDDA.

[015-167]I am in your power none the less. Subject to your will and your demands.

[015-168]A slave, a slave then! [Rises impetuously.] No, I cannot endure the

[015-169]thought of that! Never!

[015-170]BRACK.

[015-171][Looks half-mockingly at her.] People generally get used to the

[015-172]inevitable.

[015-173]HEDDA.

[015-174][Returns his look.] Yes, perhaps. [She crosses to the writing-table.

[015-175]Suppressing an involuntary smile, she imitates TESMAN'S intonations.]

[015-176]Well? Are you getting on, George? Eh?

[015-177]TESMAN.

[015-178]Heaven knows, dear. In any case it will be the work of months.

[015-179]HEDDA.

[015-180][As before.] Fancy that! [Passes her hands softly through Mrs. Elvsted's

[015-181]hair.] Doesn't it seem strange to you, Thea? Here are you sitting with

[015-182]Tesman--just as you used to sit with Eilert Lovborg?

[015-183]MRS. ELVSTED.

[015-184]Ah, if I could only inspire your husband in the same way!

[015-185]HEDDA.

[015-186]Oh, that will come too--in time.

[015-187]TESMAN.

[015-188]Yes, do you know, Hedda--I really think I begin to feel something of the

[015-189]sort. But won't you go and sit with Brack again?

[015-190]HEDDA.

[015-191]Is there nothing I can do to help you two?

[015-192]TESMAN.

[015-193]No, nothing in the world. [Turning his head.] I trust to you to keep

[015-194]Hedda company, my dear Brack.

[015-195]BRACK.

[015-196][With a glance at HEDDA.] With the very greatest of pleasure.

[015-197]HEDDA.

[015-198]Thanks. But I am tired this evening. I will go in and lie down a little

[015-199]on the sofa.

[015-200]TESMAN.

[015-201]Yes, do dear--eh?

[015-202][HEDDA goes into the back room and draws the curtains. A short

[015-203]pause. Suddenly she is heard playing a wild dance on the piano.

[015-204]MRS. ELVSTED.

[015-205][Starts from her chair.] Oh--what is that?

[015-206]TESMAN.

[015-207][Runs to the doorway.] Why, my dearest Hedda--don't play dance-music

[015-208]to-night! Just think of [[Aunt-Rina|Aunt Rina]]! And of Eilert too!

[015-209]HEDDA.

[015-210][Puts her head out between the curtains.] And of Aunt Julia. And of all

[015-211]the rest of them.--After this, I will be quiet. [Closes the curtains

[015-212]again.]

[015-213]TESMAN.

[015-214][At the writing-table.] It's not good for her to see us at this

[015-215]distressing work. I'll tell you what, Mrs. Elvsted,--you shall take the

[015-216]empty room at Aunt Julia's, and then I will come over in the evenings,

[015-217]and we can sit and work there--eh?

[015-218]HEDDA.

[015-219][In the inner room.] I hear what you are saying, Tesman. But how am _I_

[015-220]to get through the evenings out here?

[015-221]TESMAN.

[015-222][Turning over the papers.] Oh, I daresay Judge Brack will be so kind as

[015-223]to look in now and then, even though I am out.

[015-224]BRACK.

[015-225][In the arm-chair, calls out gaily.] Every blessed evening, with all the

[015-226]pleasure in life, Mrs. Tesman! We shall get on capitally together, we

[015-227]two!

[015-228]HEDDA.

[015-229][Speaking loud and clear.] Yes, don't you flatter yourself we will,

[015-230]Judge Brack? Now that you are the one cock in the basket--

[015-231][A shot is heard within. TESMAN, MRS. ELVSTED, and BRACK leap

[015-232]to their feet.

[015-233]TESMAN.

[015-234]Oh, now she is playing with those pistols again.

[015-235][He throws back the curtains and runs in, followed by MRS.

[015-236]ELVSTED. HEDDA lies stretched on the sofa, lifeless.

[015-237]Confusion and cries. BERTA enters in alarm from the right.

[015-238]TESMAN.

[015-239][Shrieks to BRACK.] Shot herself! Shot herself in the temple! Fancy

[015-240]that!

[015-241]BRACK.

[015-242][Half-fainting in the arm-chair.] Good God!--people don't do such

[015-243]things.

[015-244]THE END
