Munich is the city where Henrik Ibsen lived and worked during the composition of *Hedda Gabler* in 1890. From Munich, on June 29, 1890, Ibsen wrote to the Swedish poet Count Carl Snoilsky about the progress of the new play. (001-007) He reported that he would not leave Munich until the manuscript was complete, which he achieved by July of that year. (001-013)

## Ibsen's Residence

During his time in Munich, Ibsen completed the manuscript of *Hedda Gabler* and dispatched it to his publisher in Copenhagen. (001-017) He reportedly received an unexpected parcel from Berlin while still in Munich — an anecdote that forms part of the play's compositional lore. (001-168)

> From Munich, on June 29, 1890, Ibsen wrote to the Swedish poet, Count Carl Soilsky: "Our intention has all along been to spend the summer in the Tyrol again. But circumstances are against our doing so. I am at present engaged upon a new dramatic work, which for several reasons has made very slow progress, and I do not leave Munich until I can take with me the completed first draft."
>
> (001-007–001-011)

## World Premiere

The world premiere of *Hedda Gabler* took place at the Residenz Theater in Munich on the last day of January 1891. (001-083) This production marked the play's first staging before it travelled to other European capitals. The Munich premiere preceded the Christiania production by approximately one month. (001-087)

> The earliest theatrical performance took place at the Residenz Theater, Munich, on the last day of January 1891, in the presence of the poet, Frau Conrad-Ramlo playing the title-part.
>
> (001-083–001-084)
