# Mademoiselle Diana — Role in Lovborg's Death


> "Is she a red-haired woman?"
>
> (012-078)

Diana's establishment is the site of the altercation that ends [[Eilert-Lovborg|Lovborg]]'s life. After [[The-Bachelor-Party|the bachelor party]], Lovborg goes to Diana's with "a jolly fellow" (011-060). There, he accuses one of the women of stealing his manuscript; a fight ensues; the pistol [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]] gave him discharges, shooting him "in the bowels" (012-073).

The location matters: Lovborg dies not in the respectable Tesman drawing-room but in a brothel. The beautiful death Hedda imagined — "with vine-leaves in his hair" (002-009), a temple shot, a heroic exit — becomes a brawl in a brothel. Mademoiselle Diana's establishment is the anti-drawing-room, the space where the play's aesthetic fantasies collide with material reality.

[[Judge-Brack|Brack]] reports that both Diana and Lovborg would have been "before the court" (015-143) had the scandal become public — "both you and Mademoiselle Diana" (015-143). The parallel is deliberate: Hedda and Diana occupy opposite ends of the same shadow-world. Hedda's transgression is invisible (she pulls strings from the drawing-room); Diana's is professional and visible (she runs a brothel). But in the eyes of the law and scandal, they are the same: women whose actions exist outside respectability.

|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Sphere** | Drawing-room, domestic | Boudoir, commercial |
| **Transgression** | Covert (manipulation, suicide) | Overt (brothel-keeping) |
| **Pistol connection** | Gave the pistol to Lovborg | Site of the pistol's discharge |
| **Ending** | Suicide in the inner room | Survives, presumably to face court |
