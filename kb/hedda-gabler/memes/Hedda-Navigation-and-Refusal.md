# Hedda: Navigation and Refusal of Masculine Power

> The scene of the action is Tesman's villa, in the west end of Christiania. A spacious, handsome, and tastefully furnished drawing room, decorated in dark colours. In the back, a wide doorway with curtains drawn back, leading into a smaller room decorated in the same style as the drawing-room.
>
> (002-139–002-143)

[[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]]'s relationship to masculine power is not passive victimhood but active navigation, with a distinct strategy for each of the four forms that structure her world. Understanding these strategies reveals her not as a mere sufferer but as a tactical agent — though one whose tactics ultimately fail.

Against [[George-Tesman|Tesman]], she deploys contempt and manipulation. She marries for security — "I had danced myself out" (008-278) — but refuses to become "Mrs. Tesman" in any meaningful sense. She mocks his scholarship, his aunts, his pleasures. Her strategy is to accept the material benefits of his domestic authority while withholding every form of emotional or personal investment.

Against [[Judge-Brack|Brack]], she attempts alliance. She cultivates him as a confidant, imagining herself his equal — two "people of the world" sharing intimate conversation. But his power is asymmetrical: he extracts without offering, and she becomes his captive, not his partner. The "triangle" (012-150) is the destination her attempted alliance was always heading toward.

Against [[Eilert-Lovborg|Lovborg]], she pursues vicarious possession, then destruction. She wants to "mould a human destiny" (010-559) through him. When [[Mrs-Elvsted|Thea]] displaces her, she burns [[The-Manuscript|the manuscript]]: if she cannot possess his creative power, no woman will.

Against [[General-Gablers-Shadow|General Gabler]]'s absent authority, she internalizes his code — the only strategy available, since he is dead. She becomes her father's daughter through the [[Heddas-Pistols|pistols]], the aestheticized death, the temple shot. Her [[Heddas-Suicide|suicide]] is her refusal of all four forms: she will not be Tesman's wife, Brack's captive, Lovborg's abandoned muse, or the General's displaced daughter.
