## Notes

The entire action of *Hedda Gabler* takes place within the Tesman villa in the west end of Christiania, a setting Ibsen chose deliberately to create an atmosphere of domestic entrapment. (002-139) The drawing room, with its inner room beyond, becomes a stage for psychological warfare where off-stage spaces — the back room, the garden, the town — exert powerful unseen pressure on the characters.
