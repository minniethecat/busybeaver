# Lovborg's Death

[[Eilert-Lovborg|Eilert Lovborg]]'s death — a pistol shot to the bowels in a brothel following [[The-Bachelor-Party|a night of drinking]] — is the event around which the second half of *Hedda Gabler* turns. It is reported, not shown: the audience learns of it through [[Judge-Brack]]'s clinical narration in Act IV. The death is doubly significant: it destroys [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]]'s fantasy of "beautiful" romantic tragedy, and it places in Brack's hands the knowledge that will ultimately trap her.

The death is the culmination of a chain of events set in motion by Hedda herself: goading Lovborg into drinking, concealing [[The-Manuscript|his manuscript]], and giving him one of [[Heddas-Pistols|her pistols]] with the injunction to "die beautifully." What results is not the temple-shot of heroic tragedy but a brawl in [[Mademoiselle-Dianas]] ending in an undignified wound "in the bowels" (012-190). Brack's clinical delivery of the news serves as the play's turning point — Lovborg's squalid end shatters Hedda's [[Vine-Leaves|vine-leaves]] fantasy, while the pistol traceable to her gives Brack the leverage he needs to assert power over her.
