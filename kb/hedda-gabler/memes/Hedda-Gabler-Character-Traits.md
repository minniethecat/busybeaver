# Hedda Gabler: Character Traits

Hedda's personality is structured around immense will paired with absence of purpose.

- **Aristocratic pride**: She values appearances and control above all. Her horror at scandal is aesthetic — scandal is vulgar, and vulgarity is intolerable.
- **Aesthetic cruelty**: She finds beauty in destruction — Lovborg "with vine-leaves in his hair" (005-289), the manuscript burning like a child.
- **Hyperaesthesia**: "Hyperaesthesia lies at the root of her tragedy. With a keenly critical, relentlessly solvent intelligence, she combines a morbid shrinking from all the gross and prosaic detail of the sensual life" (002-079).

- **Cowardice**: Despite pistol-firing bravado, she is terrified of scandal. "I am in your power, Judge Brack" (015-161) — courage that was always theatrical.
- **Ennui**: She married Tesman because he was "the only one offering." Her boredom is not laziness but paralysis of a mind with no outlet.
