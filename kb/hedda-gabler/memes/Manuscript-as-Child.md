# Manuscript as Child

The most explicit child in *Hedda Gabler* is not a child at all. [[Eilert-Lovborg|Lovborg]]'s manuscript — a visionary work on "the social development of the future" (002-043) — is repeatedly described as the offspring of his intellectual partnership with [[Mrs-Elvsted|Thea Elvsted]].

When Lovborg tells Thea he has destroyed it, her cry — "But that is the child, Eilert! That is our child!" — is the play's most emotionally devastating line. She understands the manuscript not as a document but as a living creation, the product of shared labor and shared devotion.

Lovborg himself adopts the metaphor with full conviction. In his confession to Hedda, he describes the manuscript's loss as the worst act a parent can commit: "But to kill his child — that is not the worst thing a father can do to it" (013-063). He has "lost the child — utterly lost it" (013-072). The language of parenthood transforms intellectual failure into moral catastrophe.

This creative parenthood is explicitly contrasted with biological reproduction. Lovborg and Thea's "child" is freely chosen, collaboratively made, and directed toward the future of civilization. [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]]'s pregnancy, by contrast, is an accident she never chose, the product of a marriage to a man she holds in contempt. The manuscript-child represents creation as an act of will; the biological child represents creation as an imposition.

> HEDDA: [Throws one of the quires into the fire and whispers to herself.] Now I
> am burning your child, Thea!--Burning it, curly-locks! [Throwing one
> or two more quires into the stove.] Your child and Eilert Lovborg's.
>
> (013-127)

The collaboration model is striking for its time: Thea is not merely Lovborg's amanuensis but his intellectual partner. She "inspired" him, "discussed everything" with him, and when he dictates she takes down every word because "there mustn't be the slightest mistake." This is not the traditional model of male genius and female support — it is collaborative creation between equals.
