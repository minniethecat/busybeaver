# Manuscript Reaches Hedda

The chain of events that delivers [[Eilert-Lovborg|Lovborg]]'s manuscript into [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]]'s possession begins at [[The-Bachelor-Party|the bachelor party]] at [[Judge-Brack]]'s lodgings. Lovborg, drunk after Hedda's provocation, drops or loses the manuscript. [[George-Tesman|Tesman]] finds it and brings it home, intending to return it.

He shows it to Hedda, explaining its contents: "It is the history of civilization, you see — from the beginning — as he sees it" (009-013). Tesman's genuine admiration for the work is genuine but naive — he does not grasp that possessing the manuscript places a weapon in Hedda's hands.

Tesman is then called away to his dying Aunt Rina, leaving the manuscript in Hedda's possession. This is the critical juncture: Hedda has every opportunity to return the manuscript to Lovborg when he arrives distraught at the villa. Instead, she conceals it, listens to his despair — "I have torn my own life to pieces" (013-262) — and, after he leaves with one of her [[Heddas-Pistols|pistols]], burns it page by page in [[The-Stove|the stove]].

The transfer of the manuscript from public space to domestic space is not accidental. The manuscript belongs to a world of intellectual creation; once it enters the [[Tesman-Villa|Tesman drawing-room]], it becomes subject to Hedda's destructive logic. Tesman, the well-meaning academic, is the unwitting courier who delivers the "child" to its destroyer.
