# Mechanics of Respectability

Respectability in *Hedda Gabler* operates as a social currency. What matters is not what one does but what others know.

[[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]]'s marriage to [[George-Tesman|Tesman]] is respectable because it follows social form. [[Eilert-Lovborg|Lovborg]]'s drinking is scandalous because it is visible. [[Heddas-Pistols|The pistols]] are hidden; [[Judge-Brack|Brack]]'s knowledge is kept "between ourselves."

The play's settings reinforce this economy. The drawing-room is appearances, [[The-Inner-Room|the inner room]] is secrets, [[The-Glass-Door|the glass door]] admits the world's gaze. To be respectable is to perform in the drawing-room; scandal is to be seen through the glass door.

Scandal is the negative pole -- social death through exposure. When [[Judge-Brack|Brack]] reveals he knows she gave [[Eilert-Lovborg|Lovborg]] the pistol, he does not threaten prosecution. He threatens to tell:

> "Henceforth, as before, every respectable house will be open to you -- to both of you -- so long as things are kept -- between ourselves" (012-150)

This is extortion by respectability. His threat is not "I will have you arrested" but "people will know."
