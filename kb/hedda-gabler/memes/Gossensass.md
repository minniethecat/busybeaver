Gossensass (now Colle Isarco, Italy) is a resort town in the Tyrol where Henrik Ibsen spent part of the summer of 1889. It was here that Ibsen met a young Viennese woman — identified through his letters as Fraulein Emilie Bardach — whose personality, manner, and conversation contributed to the conception of Hedda Gabler. (001-036)

## The Gossensass Experience

Ibsen's experiences at Gossensass left a deep impression. The "dreamery" of that summer, as one scholar described it, was begotten of his time at the resort and the intensity of his encounter with the young woman there. (001-075) The atmosphere of that meeting — its mixture of intellectual excitement, romantic tension, and social constraint — fed directly into the emotional world of the play.

> This young lady Ibsen met at Gossensass in the Tyrol in the autumn of 1889. The record of their brief friendship belongs to the history of The Master Builder rather than to that of Hedda Gabler, but the allusions to his work in his letters to her during the winter of 1889 demand some examination.
>
> (001-036–001-038)

> He sought refuge in the chill materialism of Hedda from the ardent transcendentalism of Hilda, whom he already heard knocking at the door.
>
> (001-076–001-077)

## The "Gossensasser Hildetypus"

Literary scholars have traced elements of Hedda's character to what has been called the "Gossensasser Hildetypus" — a type of modern woman Ibsen observed and partly borrowed from his experiences at the resort. (002-107)
