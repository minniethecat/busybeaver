# Hedda's Strategies of Escape

[[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]] does not accept her entrapment passively. She attempts multiple strategies of escape, each more desperate than the last, and each failing in turn until only one remains.

Voyeurism is the first strategy: she lives through [[Eilert-Lovborg|Lovborg]]'s intensity, experiencing freedom vicariously. His "vine-leaves" (009-185) are her fantasy of escape from respectability. She cannot be free, so she watches freedom in others. But voyeurism depends on the object remaining free — and Lovborg is reformed, partnered with [[Mrs-Elvsted|Thea]], no longer available for vicarious consumption.

Manipulation is the second strategy: she exercises "power over a human being's fate" (008-231) as a substitute for power over her own. Controlling others — Thea, Lovborg, even Tesman — is the only agency available to her. But manipulation requires leverage, and Hedda's leverage evaporates as Brack consolidates information.

Destruction is the third strategy: burning [[The-Manuscript]] is a displaced act of self-destruction — "I am burning your child, Thea" (013-111) — expressing the rage she cannot direct at her own pregnancy. But destruction only tightens the cage; it does not open it.

The [[Heddas-Pistols|pistols]] are the fourth strategy — not yet for suicide, but for the temporary experience of power. "I am bored, that is all" (008-193). Handling the pistols is the only activity that makes her feel like her father's daughter rather than her husband's wife.

> Well, I shall have one thing at least to kill time with in the meanwhile. My pistols, George. ... General Gabler's pistols.
>
> (007-015)

But the pistols lead inevitably to the fifth strategy: suicide, the final and only successful escape.
