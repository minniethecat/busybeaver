# Lovborg's Arrival Scene

[[Eilert-Lovborg|Lovborg]]'s entrance in Act II is the play's most anticipated arrival. His return to town — reformed, successful, and accompanied by Thea Elvsted — sets in motion every subsequent event.

## The Entrance

Lovborg arrives as a visitor, not an intimate (007-001 to 007-010). His formality with Hedda — "Mrs. Tesman" rather than "Hedda" — signals both his reformed status and the distance between their past intimacy and present circumstances. The woman who once knew him best is now a stranger to whom he must be introduced.

## The Reunion

Hedda's first move is to re-establish the old intimacy. She insists he call her by her first name; she references their shared past; she positions herself as the one who truly understands him. Lovborg resists but is drawn in. The reunion is a seduction framed as reminiscence.

> HEDDA: [Raises the pistol and points.] Now I'll shoot you, Judge Brack!
> BRACK: [Calling unseen.] No, no, no! Don't stand aiming at me!
> HEDDA: This is what comes of sneaking in by the back way. [She fires.]
>
> (007-047)

## The Seed of Distrust

The crucial moment comes when Hedda reveals that Thea was terrified Lovborg would relapse (007-047). This revelation, presented as casual gossip, is actually a precisely calculated intervention. It plants in Lovborg the suspicion that Thea does not trust his reform — and without trust, the reform's foundation crumbles. Hedda has found her leverage.

## The Invitation

The scene ends with Hedda encouraging Lovborg to attend Brack's bachelor party (007-121). Ostensibly she wants him to enjoy himself; actually she is sending him into the environment most likely to trigger relapse. The invitation is the first move in a campaign of destruction that will claim the manuscript, Lovborg's life, and ultimately Hedda's own.
