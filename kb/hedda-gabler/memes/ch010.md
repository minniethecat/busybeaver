[010-001]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-003]HEDDA.

[010-004]Oh, not at all. Leave those two alone. They will soon be going.

[010-005]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-006]Are they going out?

[010-007]HEDDA.

[010-008]Yes, to a supper-party.

[010-009]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-010][Quickly, to LOVBORG.] Not you?

[010-011]LOVBORG.

[010-012]No.

[010-013]HEDDA.

[010-015]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-016][Takes a chair and is about to seat herself at his side.] Oh, how nice

[010-017]it is here!

[010-018]HEDDA.

[010-020]over here to me. I will sit between you.

[010-021]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-022]Yes, just as you please.

[010-023][She goes round the table and seats herself on the sofa on

[010-024]HEDDA'S right. LOVBORG re-seats himself on his chair.

[010-025]LOVBORG.

[010-026][After a short pause, to HEDDA.] Is not she lovely to look at?

[010-027]HEDDA.

[010-028][Lightly stroking her hair.] Only to look at!

[010-029]LOVBORG.

[010-030]Yes. For we two--she and I--we are two real comrades. We have absolute

[010-031]faith in each other; so we can sit and talk with perfect frankness--

[010-032]HEDDA.

[010-033]Not round about, Mr. Lovborg?

[010-034]LOVBORG.

[010-035]Well--

[010-037][Softly clinging close to HEDDA.] Oh, how happy I am, Hedda! For only

[010-039]HEDDA.

[010-040][Looks at her with a smile.] Ah! Does he say that, dear?

[010-041]LOVBORG.

[010-042]And then she is so brave, Mrs. Tesman!

[010-043]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-044]Good heavens--am I brave?

[010-045]LOVBORG.

[010-046]Exceedingly--where your comrade is concerned.

[010-047]HEDDA.

[010-048]Ah, yes--courage! If one only had that!

[010-049]LOVBORG.

[010-050]What then? What do you mean?

[010-051]HEDDA.

[010-052]Then life would perhaps be liveable, after all. [With a sudden change

[010-053]of tone.] But now, my dearest Thea, you really must have a glass of cold

[010-054]punch.

[010-055]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-056]No, thanks--I never take anything of that kind.

[010-057]HEDDA.

[010-058]Well then, you, Mr. Lovborg.

[010-059]LOVBORG.

[010-060]Nor I, thank you.

[010-061]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-062]No, he doesn't either.

[010-063]HEDDA.

[010-064][Looks fixedly at him.] But if I say you shall?

[010-065]LOVBORG.

[010-066]It would be of no use.

[010-067]HEDDA.

[010-068][Laughing.] Then I, poor creature, have no sort of power over you?

[010-069]LOVBORG.

[010-070]Not in that respect.

[010-071]HEDDA.

[010-072]But seriously, I think you ought to--for your own sake.

[010-073]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-074]Why, Hedda--!

[010-075]LOVBORG.

[010-076]How so?

[010-077]HEDDA.

[010-078]Or rather on account of other people.

[010-079]LOVBORG.

[010-080]Indeed?

[010-081]HEDDA.

[010-082]Otherwise people might be apt to suspect that--in your heart of

[010-083]hearts--you did not feel quite secure--quite confident in yourself.

[010-084]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-085][Softly.] Oh please, Hedda--!

[010-086]LOVBORG.

[010-087]People may suspect what they like--for the present.

[010-088]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-089][Joyfully.] Yes, let them!

[010-090]HEDDA.

[010-091]I saw it plainly in [[Judge-Brack|Judge Brack]]'s face a moment ago.

[010-092]LOVBORG.

[010-093]What did you see?

[010-094]HEDDA.

[010-095]His contemptuous smile, when you dared not go with them into the inner

[010-096]room.

[010-097]LOVBORG.

[010-098]Dared not? Of course I preferred to stop here and talk to you.

[010-099]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-100]What could be more natural, Hedda?

[010-101]HEDDA.

[010-102]But the Judge could not guess that. And I say, too, the way he smiled

[010-103]and glanced at Tesman when you dared not accept his invitation to this

[010-104]wretched little supper-party of his.

[010-105]LOVBORG.

[010-106]Dared not! Do you say I dared not?

[010-107]HEDDA.

[010-108]_I_ don't say so. But that was how Judge Brack understood it.

[010-109]LOVBORG.

[010-110]Well, let him.

[010-111]HEDDA.

[010-112]Then you are not going with them?

[010-113]LOVBORG.

[010-114]I will stay here with you and Thea.

[010-115]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-116]Yes, Hedda--how can you doubt that?

[010-117]HEDDA.

[010-118][Smiles and nods approvingly to LOVBORG.] Firm as a rock! Faithful to

[010-119]your principles, now and for ever! Ah, that is how a man should be!

[010-120][Turns to MRS. ELVSTED and caresses her.] Well now, what did I tell you,

[010-121]when you came to us this morning in such a state of distraction--

[010-122]LOVBORG.

[010-123][Surprised.] Distraction!

[010-124]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-125][Terrified.] Hedda--oh Hedda--!

[010-126]HEDDA.

[010-127]You can see for yourself! You haven't the slightest reason to be in such

[010-128]mortal terror-- [Interrupting herself.] There! Now we can all three

[010-129]enjoy ourselves!

[010-130]LOVBORG.

[010-131][Who has given a start.] Ah--what is all this, Mrs. Tesman?

[010-132]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-133]Oh my God, Hedda! What are you saying? What are you doing?

[010-134]HEDDA.

[010-135]Don't get excited! That horrid Judge Brack is sitting watching you.

[010-136]LOVBORG.

[010-137]So she was in mortal terror! On my account!

[010-138]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-139][Softly and piteously.] Oh, Hedda--now you have ruined everything!

[010-140]LOVBORG.

[010-141][Looks fixedly at her for a moment. His face is distorted.] So that was

[010-142]my comrade's frank confidence in me?

[010-143]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-144][Imploringly.] Oh, my dearest friend--only let me tell you--

[010-145]LOVBORG.

[010-146][Takes one of the glasses of punch, raises it to his lips, and says in a

[010-147]low, husky voice.] Your health, Thea!

[010-148][He empties the glass, puts it down, and takes the second.

[010-149]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-150][Softly.] Oh, Hedda, Hedda--how could you do this?

[010-151]HEDDA.

[010-152]_I_ do it? _I_? Are you crazy?

[010-153]LOVBORG.

[010-154]Here's to your health too, Mrs. Tesman. Thanks for the truth. Hurrah for

[010-155]the truth!

[010-156][He empties the glass and is about to re-fill it.

[010-157]HEDDA.

[010-158][Lays her hand on his arm.] Come, come--no more for the present.

[010-159]Remember you are going out to supper.

[010-160]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-161]No, no, no!

[010-162]HEDDA.

[010-163]Hush! They are sitting watching you.

[010-164]LOVBORG.

[010-165][Putting down the glass.] Now, Thea--tell me the truth--

[010-166]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-167]Yes.

[010-168]LOVBORG.

[010-169]Did your husband know that you had come after me?

[010-170]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-171][Wringing her hands.] Oh, Hedda--do you hear what he is asking?

[010-172]LOVBORG.

[010-173]Was it arranged between you and him that you were to come to town and

[010-174]look after me? Perhaps it was the Sheriff himself that urged you to

[010-175]come? Aha, my dear--no doubt he wanted my help in his office! Or was it

[010-176]at the card-table that he missed me?

[010-177]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-178][Softly, in agony.] Oh, Lovborg, Lovborg--!

[010-179]LOVBORG.

[010-180][Seizes a glass and is on the point of filling it.] Here's a glass for

[010-181]the old Sheriff too!

[010-182]HEDDA.

[010-183][Preventing him.] No more just now. Remember, you have to read your

[010-184]manuscript to Tesman.

[010-185]LOVBORG.

[010-186][Calmly, putting down the glass.] It was stupid of me all this. Thea--to

[010-187]take it in this way, I mean. Don't be angry with me, my dear, dear

[010-188]comrade. You shall see--both you and the others--that if I was fallen

[010-189]once--now I have risen again! Thanks to you, Thea.

[010-190]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-191][Radiant with joy.] Oh, heaven be praised--!

[010-192][BRACK has in the meantime looked at his watch. He and TESMAN

[010-193]rise and come into the drawing-room.

[010-194]BRACK.

[010-195][Takes his hat and overcoat.] Well, Mrs. Tesman, our time has come.

[010-196]HEDDA.

[010-197]I suppose it has.

[010-198]LOVBORG.

[010-199][Rising.] Mine too, Judge Brack.

[010-200]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-201][Softly and imploringly.] Oh, Lovborg, don't do it!

[010-202]HEDDA.

[010-203][Pinching her arm.] They can hear you!

[010-204]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-205][With a suppressed shriek.] Ow!

[010-206]LOVBORG.

[010-207][To BRACK.] You were good enough to invite me.

[010-208]JUDGE BRACK.

[010-209]Well, are you coming after all?

[010-210]LOVBORG.

[010-211]Yes, many thanks.

[010-212]BRACK.

[010-213]I'm delighted--

[010-214]LOVBORG.

[010-215][To TESMAN, putting the parcel of MS. in his pocket.] I should like to

[010-216]show you one or two things before I send it to the printers.

[010-217]TESMAN.

[010-219]get home? Eh?

[010-220]HEDDA.

[010-221]Oh, that can be managed somehow.

[010-222]LOVBORG.

[010-223][Looking towards the ladies.] Mrs. Elvsted? Of course, I'll come again

[010-226]HEDDA.

[010-227]Certainly. That will do capitally.

[010-228]TESMAN.

[010-229]Well, then, that's all right. But you must not expect me so early,

[010-230]Hedda.

[010-231]HEDDA.

[010-232]Oh, you may stop as long--as long as ever you please.

[010-233]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-236]LOVBORG.

[010-237][With his hat in his hand.] Pray do, Mrs. Elvsted.

[010-238]BRACK.

[010-239]And now off goes the excursion train, gentlemen! I hope we shall have a

[010-241]HEDDA.

[010-242]Ah, if only the fair lady could be present unseen--!

[010-243]BRACK.

[010-244]Why unseen?

[010-245]HEDDA.

[010-246]In order to hear a little of your liveliness at first hand, Judge Brack.

[010-247]BRACK.

[010-248][Laughing.] I should not advise the fair lady to try it.

[010-249]TESMAN.

[010-250][Also laughing.] Come, you're a nice one Hedda! Fancy that!

[010-251]BRACK.

[010-252]Well, good-bye, good-bye, ladies.

[010-253]LOVBORG.

[010-254][Bowing.] [[About]] ten o'clock, then,

[010-255][BRACK, LOVBORG, and TESMAN go out by the hall door. At the

[010-256]same time, BERTA enters from the inner room with a lighted

[010-265]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-296]Very well. We are coming

[010-297]MRS. ELVSTED.

[010-298]No, no, no! I would rather go home alone! At once!
