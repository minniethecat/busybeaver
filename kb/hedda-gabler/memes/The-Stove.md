# The Stove

The stove in the [[Tesman-Villa]]'s [[The-Inner-Room|inner room]] is a cast-iron fixture whose ordinary function is transformed by [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]] into an instrument of destruction. It is in this stove that she burns [[The-Manuscript|Lovborg's manuscript]], whispering "Now I am burning your child, Thea!" (013-125).

For a detailed physical survey, see [[The-Stove-Physical-Description]]. The burning itself — the page-by-page ritual, the whispered words, [[Berta]]'s unwitting complicity — is examined in [[The-Stove-The-Burning]].

[[The-Stove-Dramatic-Significance]] explores how the stove transforms the domestic into the destructive along axes of the elemental, gender, and class. The broader fire motif — from Hedda's red hair through the manuscript burning to the final pistol shot — is traced in [[Fire-Imagery-Hedda-Gabler]].

The stove does not discriminate between its functions. The same fire that warms the Tesmans' home consumes the manuscript. The same hearth that symbolizes domestic nurture becomes a pyre. In Act IV, Tesman and [[Mrs-Elvsted|Thea]] reconstruct the manuscript at a table near the same stove — creation and destruction sharing the same room, the same furniture, the same fire.

## From the Text

The burning of the manuscript is the play's most concentrated act of destruction, rendered in stage directions that are themselves a kind of whispered poetry:

> HEDDA. [Throws one of the quires into the fire and whispers to herself.] Now I am burning your child, Thea! — Burning it, curly-locks! [Throwing one or two more quires into the stove.] Your child and Eilert Lovborg's. [Throws the rest in.] I am burning — I am burning your child.
>
> (013-123 to 013-126)

The stage direction is a ritual: the fire, the whisper, the incrementally revealed identification of the manuscript as "your child and Eilert Lovborg's." The stove, an object of domestic warmth, becomes the altar of a sacrifice Hedda alone understands. The same fire that warms the Tesmans' home consumes the "child" of Lovborg and Thea's intellectual union.
