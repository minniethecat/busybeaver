# Bachelor Party Consequences

Every major plot development in the second half of *Hedda Gabler* flows from [[The-Bachelor-Party|the bachelor party]] at [[Judge-Brack|Brack]]'s lodgings. The party is the pivot on which the play turns.

**Loss of the manuscript.** Lovborg drops or leaves [[The-Manuscript|the manuscript]], and [[George-Tesman|Tesman]] finds it on the road. The "child" of Lovborg and [[Mrs-Elvsted|Thea]]'s intellectual union passes into Tesman's hands — and, once he brings it home, into [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]]'s.

**Hedda's knowledge.** She learns through Tesman that he possesses the manuscript and learns through Brack that Lovborg has been involved in a scandal. These two pieces of information converge in her decision to burn it.

**[[The-Burning-of-the-Manuscript|The burning]].** Possessing the manuscript and knowing Lovborg's reputation is destroyed, Hedda burns it page by page in [[The-Stove|the stove]] — "Now I am burning your child, Thea!" (013-125).

> BRACK.
> Well, Hedda--then comes the scandal!
> HEDDA.
> The scandal!
> BRACK.
> Yes, the scandal--of which you are so mortally afraid. You will, of course, be brought before the court--both you and Mademoiselle Diana.
>
> (015-138)

**The pistol.** Hedda gives Lovborg one of [[Heddas-Pistols|General Gabler's pistols]], urging him to "die beautifully." The pistol, and the party's aftermath, lead directly to [[Lovborgs-Death|his death]] in Mademoiselle Diana's boudoir.

**Brack's leverage.** Brack's presence at the party and knowledge of its details give him the information he later uses to threaten Hedda. He was there; he saw everything; he knows the pistol was hers. The party that began as male camaraderie ends as the source of Brack's power over Hedda — a power that proves lethal.
