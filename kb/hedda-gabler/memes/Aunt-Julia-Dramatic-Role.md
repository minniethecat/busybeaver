# Aunt Julia — Dramatic Role

Aunt Julia appears at key structural moments throughout *Hedda Gabler*, her presence framing the domestic world that [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]] despises.

In **Act I**, she welcomes the newlyweds home with gifts and warmth. Her first entrance — beaming, carrying a parcel, greeted by [[George-Tesman|George]] with "Aunt Julia! Dear Aunt Julia!" (003-062) — immediately establishes the warmth that Hedda will spend the entire play undermining.

In **Act II**, Hedda's cruelty toward her intensifies. The bouquet Aunt Julia sends is dismissed; her bonnet is mocked; her very presence in the [[Tesman-Villa|drawing-room]] is treated as an intrusion. Aunt Julia absorbs these slights without retaliation, maintaining her cheerful solicitude.

In **Act IV**, she tends to the dying Rina — the play's moral counterpoint to Hedda's destruction. While Hedda burns [[The-Manuscript|Lovborg's manuscript]] in the drawing-room, Aunt Julia sits at a deathbed in another house, performing the quiet work of love.

She is the play's embodiment of bourgeois domestic virtue — thrifty, loving, self-sacrificing. Her bonnet, her morning visits, and her concern for appearances are the very textures of the world Hedda finds suffocating. Yet Ibsen treats her with genuine respect, never reducing her to mere comic relief. Her virtue is real, her sacrifices are real, and her grief at Rina's death is the play's most sincere emotional moment.

## From the Text

> MISS TESMAN.
> Good morning, good morning, George.
> TESMAN.
> [In the doorway between the rooms.] Aunt Julia! Dear Aunt Julia! [Goes up to her and shakes hands warmly.] Come all this way--so early! Eh?
> MISS TESMAN.
> Why, of course I had to come and see how you were getting on.
> TESMAN.
> In spite of your having had no proper night's rest?
> MISS TESMAN.
> Oh, that makes no difference to me.
> TESMAN.
> Well, I suppose you got home all right from the pier? Eh?
> MISS TESMAN.
> Yes, quite safely, thank goodness. Judge Brack was good enough to see me right to my door.

> (003-062)
