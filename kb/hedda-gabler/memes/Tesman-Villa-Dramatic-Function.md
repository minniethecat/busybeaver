# Tesman Villa: Dramatic Function

The [[Tesman-Villa|Tesman villa]] is not merely scenery but an active dramatic agent in *Hedda Gabler*. Its functions operate on multiple registers simultaneously.

**Financial Burden**: [[George-Tesman|Tesman]] purchased the villa on the assumption of a forthcoming professorship. When [[Eilert-Lovborg|Lovborg]] threatens that position, the mortgage becomes an existential trap. The villa was meant to display status; it now threatens ruin. [[Aunt-Julia|Miss Tesman]]'s annuity secures the loan — the older generation's sacrifice underwriting the younger generation's pretension.

**The Honeymoon Paradox**: The villa represents the couple's supposed "new beginning," yet [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]] returned from the six-month honeymoon already "bored to death." The most enviable address in the neighborhood houses the most miserable marriage. The villa is a monument to the gap between social appearance and psychological reality.

**Unity of Place**: The entire play unfolds within the villa's drawing room. This classical unity intensifies claustrophobia: characters enter, exit, and return to the same suffocating space. There is no escape — only variations of entrapment. When Hedda moves between rooms, she is merely pacing a larger cell.

**Trajectory of Decline**: Hedda's movement through the villa maps her psychological trajectory. Act I shows restless furniture rearrangement. Act II shows manipulation of curtains and lighting. Act III confines her increasingly to [[The-Inner-Room|the inner room]]. Act IV sees her suicide in that same inner room, beneath her father's portrait, transforming the villa from home to mausoleum.
