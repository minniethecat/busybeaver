# Brack's Weapon

[[Judge-Brack]] understands scandal as the ultimate currency of power in a society governed by appearances. His immunity — "correctness and respectability are beyond all question" (007-212) — is the platform from which he controls everyone else. He is scandal-proof, and this immunity lets him wield scandal against others with surgical precision.

Brack's genius is that he never makes explicit threats. He offers "protection" — his silence in exchange for access. The "triangle" he constructs is a ménage à trois of knowledge: Hedda, Brack, and the secret they share. As long as Hedda accepts the triangle, the secret stays between them. The moment she resists, the triangle collapses into public exposure.

His threat is made concrete through the [[Heddas-Pistols|pistol]]. The pistol that killed Lovborg can be traced to Hedda; Brack knows this; and the fact that he knows it — and she knows he knows — is the transaction. "This same Mademoiselle Diana" (015-149) becomes code for everything Brack can destroy if Hedda refuses his terms.

Brack's power is structurally gendered. He, a man "of the world," can maintain perfect respectability while moving freely through every social world — including [[Mademoiselle-Dianas]], where Lovborg dies. Hedda, a woman, cannot even acknowledge such places exist. His immunity is not personal virtue but structural position: the world is built for men like Brack.

His offer of the "triangle" appears benign — evening visits, conversation, relief from boredom — but its terms are absolute. Hedda's refusal (through suicide) is the only answer available to her. Brack's final line — "People don't do such things!" — is the voice of a man whose weapon has been rendered useless by the one target he could not reach: a woman who chose death over his terms.

> Oh, reasons-- [Watching him closely.] And George Tesman--after all, you must admit that he is correctness itself.
> BRACK.
> His correctness and respectability are beyond all question.

> And I don't see anything absolutely ridiculous about him.--Do you?
> BRACK.
> Ridiculous? N--no--I shouldn't exactly say so--
> HEDDA.
> Well--and his powers of research, at all events, are untiring.--I see no reason why he should not one day come to the front, after all.
