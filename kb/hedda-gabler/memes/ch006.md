[006-001]HEDDA.

[006-002][After a short silence.] And what are your plans now? What do you think

[006-003]of doing.

[006-004]MRS. ELVSTED.

[006-005]I don't know yet. I only know this, that I must live here, where Eilert

[006-006]Lovborg is--if I am to live at all.

[006-007]HEDDA.

[006-008][Takes a chair from the table, seats herself beside her, and strokes

[006-009]her hands.] My dear Thea--how did this--this friendship--between you and

[006-010][[Eilert-Lovborg|Eilert Lovborg]] come about?

[006-011]MRS. ELVSTED.

[006-012]Oh it grew up gradually. I gained a sort of influence over him.

[006-013]HEDDA.

[006-014]Indeed?

[006-015]MRS. ELVSTED.

[006-016]He gave up his old habits. Not because I asked him to, for I never dared

[006-017]do that. But of course he saw how repulsive they were to me; and so he

[006-018]dropped them.

[006-019]HEDDA.

[006-020][Concealing an involuntary smile of scorn.] Then you have reclaimed

[006-021]him--as the saying goes--my little Thea.

[006-022]MRS. ELVSTED.

[006-023]So he says himself, at any rate. And he, on his side, has made a real

[006-024]human being of me--taught me to think, and to understand so many things.

[006-025]HEDDA.

[006-026]Did he give you lessons too, then?

[006-027]MRS. ELVSTED.

[006-028]No, not exactly lessons. But he talked to me--talked about such an

[006-029]infinity of things. And then came the lovely, happy time when I began to

[006-030]share in his work--when he allowed me to help him!

[006-031]HEDDA.

[006-032]Oh he did, did he?

[006-033]MRS. ELVSTED.

[006-034]Yes! He never wrote anything without my assistance.

[006-035]HEDDA.

[006-036]You were two good comrades, in fact?

[006-037]MRS. ELVSTED.

[006-038][Eagerly.] Comrades! Yes, fancy, Hedda--that is the very word he

[006-039]used!--Oh, I ought to feel perfectly happy; and yet I cannot; for I

[006-040]don't know how long it will last.

[006-041]HEDDA.

[006-042]Are you no surer of him than that?

[006-043]MRS. ELVSTED.

[006-044][Gloomily.] A woman's shadow stands between Eilert Lovborg and me.

[006-045]HEDDA.

[006-046][Looks at her anxiously.] Who can that be?

[006-047]MRS. ELVSTED.

[006-048]I don't know. Some one he knew in his--in his past. Some one he has

[006-049]never been able wholly to forget.

[006-050]HEDDA.

[006-051]What has he told you--about this?

[006-052]MRS. ELVSTED.

[006-053]He has only once--quite vaguely--alluded to it.

[006-054]HEDDA.

[006-055]Well! And what did he say?

[006-056]MRS. ELVSTED.

[006-057]He said that when they parted, she threatened to shoot him with a

[006-058]pistol.

[006-059]HEDDA.

[006-060][With cold composure.] Oh nonsense! No one does that sort of thing here.

[006-061]MRS. ELVSTED.

[006-062]No. And that is why I think it must have been that red-haired

[006-063]singing-woman whom he once--

[006-064]HEDDA.

[006-065]Yes, very likely.

[006-066]MRS. ELVSTED.

[006-067]For I remember they used to say of her that she carried loaded firearms.

[006-068]HEDDA.

[006-069]Oh--then of course it must have been she.

[006-070]MRS. ELVSTED.

[006-071][Wringing her hands.] And now just fancy, Hedda--I hear that this

[006-072]singing-woman--that she is in town again! Oh, I don't know what to do--

[006-073]HEDDA.

[006-074][Glancing towards the inner room.] Hush! Here comes Tesman. [Rises and

[006-075]whispers.] Thea--all this must remain between you and me.

[006-076]MRS. ELVSTED.

[006-077][Springing up.] Oh yes--yes! For heaven's sake--!

[006-078]GEORGE TESMAN, with a letter in his hand, comes from the right

[006-079]through the inner room.

[006-080]TESMAN.

[006-081]There now--the epistle is finished.

[006-082]HEDDA.

[006-083]That's right. And now [[Mrs-Elvsted|Mrs. Elvsted]] is just going. Wait a moment--I'll

[006-084]go with you to the garden gate.

[006-085]TESMAN.

[006-086]Do you think [[Berta]] could post the letter, Hedda dear?

[006-087]HEDDA.

[006-088][Takes it.] I will tell her to.

[006-089]BERTA enters from the hall.

[006-090]BERTA.

[006-091][[Judge-Brack|Judge Brack]] wishes to know if Mrs. Tesman will receive him.

[006-092]HEDDA.

[006-093]Yes, ask Judge Brack to come in. And look here--put this letter in the

[006-094]post.

[006-095]BERTA. [Taking the letter.] Yes, ma'am.

[006-096][She opens the door for JUDGE BRACK and goes out herself. Brack

[006-097]is a man of forty-five; thick set, but well-built and elastic

[006-098]in his movements. His face is roundish with an aristocratic

[006-099]profile. His hair is short, still almost black, and carefully

[006-100]dressed. His eyebrows thick. His moustaches are also thick,

[006-101]with short-cut ends. He wears a well-cut walking-suit, a

[006-102]little too youthful for his age. He uses an eye-glass, which

[006-103]he now and then lets drop.

[006-104]JUDGE BRACK.

[006-105][With his hat in his hand, bowing.] May one venture to call so early in

[006-106]the day?

[006-107]HEDDA.

[006-108]Of course one may.

[006-109]TESMAN.

[006-110][Presses his hand.] You are welcome at any time. [Introducing him.]

[006-111]Judge Brack--Miss Rysing--

[006-112]HEDDA.

[006-113]Oh--!

[006-114]BRACK.

[006-115][Bowing.] Ah--delighted--

[006-116]HEDDA.

[006-117][Looks at him and laughs.] It's nice to have a look at you by daylight,

[006-118]Judge!

[006-119]BRACK.

[006-120]So you find me--altered?

[006-121]HEDDA.

[006-122]A little younger, I think.

[006-123]BRACK.

[006-124]Thank you so much.

[006-125]TESMAN.

[006-126]But what do you think of Hedda--eh? Doesn't she look flourishing? She

[006-127]has actually--

[006-128]HEDDA.

[006-129]Oh, do leave me alone. You haven't thanked Judge Brack for all the

[006-130]trouble he has taken--

[006-131]BRACK.

[006-132]Oh, nonsense--it was a pleasure to me--

[006-133]HEDDA.

[006-134]Yes, you are a friend indeed. But here stands Thea all impatience to be

[006-135]off--so _au revoir_ Judge. I shall be back again presently.

[006-136][Mutual salutations. MRS. ELVSTED and HEDDA go out by the hall

[006-137]door.

[006-138]BRACK.

[006-139]Well,--is your wife tolerably satisfied--

[006-140]TESMAN.

[006-141]Yes, we can't thank you sufficiently. Of course she talks of a little

[006-142]re-arrangement here and there; and one or two things are still wanting.

[006-143]We shall have to buy some additional trifles.

[006-144]BRACK.

[006-145]Indeed!

[006-146]TESMAN.

[006-147]But we won't trouble you about these things. Hedda says she herself will

[006-148]look after what is wanting.--Shan't we sit down? Eh?

[006-149]BRACK.

[006-150]Thanks, for a moment. [Seats himself beside the table.] There is

[006-151]something I wanted to speak to you about, my dear Tesman.

[006-152]TESMAN.

[006-153]Indeed? Ah, I understand! [Seating himself.] I suppose it's the serious

[006-154]part of the frolic that is coming now. Eh?

[006-155]BRACK.

[006-156]Oh, the money question is not so very pressing; though, for that matter,

[006-157]I wish we had gone a little more economically to work.

[006-158]TESMAN.

[006-159]But that would never have done, you know! Think of Hedda, my dear

[006-160]fellow! You, who know her so well--! I couldn't possibly ask her to put

[006-161]up with a shabby style of living!

[006-162]BRACK.

[006-163]No, no--that is just the difficulty.

[006-164]TESMAN.

[006-165]And then--fortunately--it can't be long before I receive my appointment.

[006-166]BRACK.

[006-167]Well, you see--such things are often apt to hang fire for a long time.

[006-168]TESMAN.

[006-169]Have you heard anything definite? Eh?

[006-170]BRACK.

[006-171]Nothing exactly definite--. [Interrupting himself.] But by-the-bye--I

[006-172]have one piece of news for you.

[006-173]TESMAN.

[006-174]Well?

[006-175]BRACK.

[006-176]Your old friend, Eilert Lovborg, has returned to town.

[006-177]TESMAN.

[006-178]I know that already.

[006-179]BRACK.

[006-180]Indeed! How did you learn it?

[006-181]TESMAN.

[006-182]From that lady who went out with Hedda.

[006-183]BRACK.

[006-184]Really? What was her name? I didn't quite catch it.

[006-185]TESMAN.

[006-186]Mrs. Elvsted.

[006-187]BRACK.

[006-188]Aha--[[Sheriff-Elvsted|Sheriff Elvsted]]'s wife? Of course--he has been living up in their

[006-189]regions.

[006-190]TESMAN.

[006-191]And fancy--I'm delighted to hear that he is quite a reformed character.

[006-192]BRACK.

[006-193]So they say.

[006-194]TESMAN.

[006-195]And then he has published a new book--eh?

[006-196]BRACK.

[006-197]Yes, indeed he has.

[006-198]TESMAN.

[006-199]And I hear it has made some sensation!

[006-200]BRACK.

[006-201]Quite an unusual sensation.

[006-202]TESMAN.

[006-203]Fancy--isn't that good news! A man of such extraordinary talents--. I

[006-204]felt so grieved to think that he had gone irretrievably to ruin.

[006-205]BRACK.

[006-206]That was what everybody thought.

[006-207]TESMAN.

[006-208]But I cannot imagine what he will take to now! How in the world will he

[006-209]be able to make his living? Eh?

[006-210][During the last words, HEDDA has entered by the hall door.

[006-211]HEDDA.

[006-212][To BRACK, laughing with a touch of scorn.] Tesman is for ever worrying

[006-213]about how people are to make their living.

[006-214]TESMAN.

[006-215]Well you see, dear--we were talking about poor Eilert Lovborg.

[006-216]HEDDA.

[006-217][Glancing at him rapidly.] Oh, indeed? [Sets herself in the arm-chair

[006-218]beside the stove and asks indifferently:] What is the matter with him?

[006-219]TESMAN.

[006-220]Well--no doubt he has run through all his property long ago; and he can

[006-221]scarcely write a new book every year--eh? So I really can't see what is

[006-222]to become of him.

[006-223]BRACK.

[006-224]Perhaps I can give you some information on that point.

[006-225]TESMAN.

[006-226]Indeed!

[006-227]BRACK.

[006-228]You must remember that his relations have a good deal of influence.

[006-229]TESMAN.

[006-230]Oh, his relations, unfortunately, have entirely washed their hands of

[006-231]him.

[006-232]BRACK.

[006-233]At one time they called him the hope of the family.

[006-234]TESMAN.

[006-235]At one time, yes! But he has put an end to all that.

[006-236]HEDDA.

[006-237]Who knows? [With a slight smile.] I hear they have reclaimed him up at

[006-238]Sheriff Elvsted's--

[006-239]BRACK.

[006-240]And then this book that he has published--

[006-241]TESMAN.

[006-242]Well well, I hope to goodness they may find something for him to do. I

[006-243]have just written to him. I asked him to come and see us this evening,

[006-244]Hedda dear.

[006-245]BRACK.

[006-246]But my dear fellow, you are booked for my bachelor's party this evening.

[006-247]You promised on the pier last night.

[006-248]HEDDA.

[006-249]Had you forgotten, Tesman?

[006-250]TESMAN.

[006-251]Yes, I had utterly forgotten.

[006-252]BRACK.

[006-253]But it doesn't matter, for you may be sure he won't come.

[006-254]TESMAN.

[006-255]What makes you think that? Eh?

[006-256]BRACK.

[006-257][With a little hesitation, rising and resting his hands on the back of

[006-258]his chair.] My dear Tesman--and you too, Mrs. Tesman--I think I ought

[006-259]not to keep you in the dark about something that--that--

[006-260]TESMAN.

[006-261]That concerns Eilert--?

[006-262]BRACK.

[006-263]Both you and him.

[006-264]TESMAN.

[006-265]Well, my dear Judge, out with it.

[006-266]BRACK.

[006-267]You must be prepared to find your appointment deferred longer than you

[006-268]desired or expected.

[006-269]TESMAN.

[006-270][Jumping up uneasily.] Is there some hitch about it? Eh?

[006-271]BRACK.

[006-272]The nomination may perhaps be made conditional on the result of a

[006-273]competition--

[006-274]TESMAN.

[006-275]Competition! Think of that, Hedda!

[006-276]HEDDA.

[006-277][Leans further back in the chair.] Aha--aha!

[006-278]TESMAN.

[006-279]But who can my competitor be? Surely not--?

[006-280]BRACK.

[006-281]Yes, precisely--Eilert Lovborg.

[006-282]TESMAN.

[006-283][Clasping his hands.] No, no--it's quite impossible! Eh?

[006-284]BRACK.

[006-285]H'm--that is what it may come to, all the same.

[006-286]TESMAN.

[006-287]Well but, Judge Brack--it would show the most incredible lack of

[006-288]consideration for me. [Gesticulates with his arms.] For--just think--I'm

[006-289]a married man! We have married on the strength of these prospects, Hedda

[006-290]and I; and run deep into debt; and borrowed money from Aunt Julia too.

[006-291]Good heavens, they had as good as promised me the appointment. Eh?

[006-292]BRACK.

[006-293]Well, well, well--no doubt you will get it in the end; only after a

[006-294]contest.

[006-295]HEDDA.

[006-296][Immovable in her arm-chair.] Fancy, Tesman, there will be a sort of

[006-297]sporting interest in that.

[006-298]TESMAN.

[006-299]Why, my dearest Hedda, how can you be so indifferent about it?

[006-300]HEDDA.

[006-301][As before.] I am not at all indifferent. I am most eager to see who

[006-302]wins.

[006-303]BRACK.

[006-304]In any case, Mrs. Tesman, it is best that you should know how matters

[006-305]stand. I mean--before you set about the little purchases I hear you are

[006-306]threatening.

[006-307]HEDDA.

[006-308]This can make no difference.

[006-309]BRACK.

[006-310]Indeed! Then I have no more to say. Good-bye! [To TESMAN.] I shall look

[006-311]in on my way back from my afternoon walk, and take you home with me.

[006-312]TESMAN.

[006-313]Oh yes, yes--your news has quite upset me.

[006-314]HEDDA.

[006-315][Reclining, holds out her hand.] Good-bye, Judge; and be sure you call

[006-316]in the afternoon.

[006-317]BRACK.

[006-318]Many thanks. Good-bye, good-bye!

[006-319]TESMAN.

[006-320][Accompanying him to the door.] Good-bye my dear Judge! You must really

[006-321]excuse me-- [JUDGE BRACK goes out by the hall door.

[006-322]TESMAN.

[006-323][Crosses the room.] Oh Hedda--one should never rush into adventures. Eh?

[006-324]HEDDA.

[006-325][Looks at him, smiling.] Do you do that?

[006-326]TESMAN.

[006-327]Yes, dear--there is no denying--it was adventurous to go and marry and

[006-328]set up house upon mere expectations.

[006-329]HEDDA.

[006-330]Perhaps you are right there.

[006-331]TESMAN.

[006-332]Well--at all events, we have our delightful home, Hedda! Fancy, the home

[006-333]we both dreamed of--the home we were in love with, I may almost say. Eh?

[006-334]HEDDA.

[006-335][Rising slowly and wearily.] It was part of our compact that we were to

[006-336]go into society--to keep open house.

[006-337]TESMAN.

[006-338]Yes, if you only knew how I had been looking forward to it! Fancy--to

[006-339]see you as hostess--in a select circle! Eh? Well, well, well--for the

[006-340]present we shall have to get on without society, Hedda--only to invite

[006-341]Aunt Julia now and then.--Oh, I intended you to lead such an utterly

[006-342]different life, dear--!
