# Judge Brack

A cynical and manipulative "family friend" who insinuates himself into the [[Tesman-Villa|Tesman household]] with the explicit goal of forming a "triangle" — he wants to be the third point in the Tesmans' marriage, the trusted confidant who knows everything and controls through knowledge. [[Judge-Brack|Brack]] is the play's most clear-sighted character and its most effective antagonist, the only figure who genuinely frightens [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]] because he operates with the same intelligence she possesses but without her vulnerabilities.

Brack functions as the play's structural counterweight: Hedda's intelligence is destructive; Brack's is instrumental. His power derives from information — he knows about the pistols, Lovborg's death, and Mademoiselle Diana's — and from scandal immunity, since his "correctness and respectability are beyond all question" (007-212). His pursuit of a "triangle" with the Tesmans is thinly veiled blackmail for access to Hedda, transforming private knowledge into social control. When Hedda realizes he holds the pistol secret, her autonomy collapses: "I am in your power, Judge" (015-161). Her suicide is her refusal of Brack's terms — the one move he cannot follow. His closing line, "People don't do such things!" (015-242), registers not grief but social horror at a game interrupted.


> The triangle, you know, ought, if possible, to be spontaneously constructed.
>
> (012-182)
