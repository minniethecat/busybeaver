[012-001]HEDDA.

[012-002]Not you, either?

[012-003]BRACK.

[012-004]No, as you may see. But what has Tesman been telling you of the night's

[012-005]adventures?

[012-006]HEDDA.

[012-007]Oh, some tiresome story. Only that they went and had coffee somewhere or

[012-008]other.

[012-009]BRACK.

[012-010]I have heard about that coffee-party already. [[Eilert-Lovborg|Eilert Lovborg]] was not

[012-011]with them, I fancy?

[012-012]HEDDA.

[012-013]No, they had taken him home before that.

[012-014]BRACK.

[012-015]Tesman too?

[012-016]HEDDA.

[012-017]No, but some of the others, he said.

[012-018]BRACK.

[012-019][Smiling.] [[George-Tesman|George Tesman]] is really an ingenuous creature, Mrs. Hedda.

[012-020]HEDDA.

[012-021]Yes, heaven knows he is. Then is there something behind all this?

[012-022]BRACK.

[012-023]Yes, perhaps there may be.

[012-024]HEDDA.

[012-025]Well then, sit down, my dear Judge, and tell your story in comfort.

[012-026][She seats herself to the left of the table. BRACK sits near

[012-027]her, at the long side of the table.

[012-028]HEDDA.

[012-029]Now then?

[012-030]BRACK.

[012-031]I had special reasons for keeping track of my guests--last night.

[012-032]HEDDA.

[012-033]Of Eilert Lovborg among the rest, perhaps?

[012-034]BRACK.

[012-035]Frankly, yes.

[012-036]HEDDA.

[012-037]Now you make me really curious--

[012-038]BRACK.

[012-039]Do you know where he and one or two of the others finished the night,

[012-040]Mrs. Hedda?

[012-041]HEDDA.

[012-042]If it is not quite unmentionable, tell me.

[012-043]BRACK.

[012-044]Oh no, it's not at all unmentionable. Well, they put in an appearance at

[012-045]a particularly animated soiree.

[012-046]HEDDA.

[012-047]Of the lively kind?

[012-048]BRACK.

[012-049]Of the very liveliest--

[012-050]HEDDA.

[012-051]Tell me more of this, [[Judge-Brack|Judge Brack]]--

[012-052]BRACK.

[012-053]Lovborg, as well as the others, had been invited in advance. I knew all

[012-054]about it. But he had declined the invitation; for now, as you know, he

[012-055]has become a new man.

[012-056]HEDDA.

[012-057]Up at the Elvsteds', yes. But he went after all, then?

[012-058]BRACK.

[012-059]Well, you see, Mrs. Hedda--unhappily the spirit moved him at my rooms

[012-060]last evening--

[012-061]HEDDA.

[012-062]Yes, I hear he found inspiration.

[012-063]BRACK.

[012-064]Pretty violent inspiration. Well, I fancy that altered his purpose; for

[012-065]we menfolk are unfortunately not always so firm in our principles as we

[012-066]ought to be.

[012-067]HEDDA.

[012-068]Oh, I am sure you are an exception, Judge Brack. But as to Lovborg--?

[012-069]BRACK.

[012-070]To make a long story short--he landed at last in [[Mademoiselle-Dianas|Mademoiselle Diana's]]

[012-071]rooms.

[012-072]HEDDA.

[012-073][[Mademoiselle-Diana|Mademoiselle Diana]]'s?

[012-074]BRACK.

[012-075]It was Mademoiselle Diana that was giving the soiree, to a select circle

[012-076]of her admirers and her lady friends.

[012-077]HEDDA.

[012-078]Is she a red-haired woman?

[012-079]BRACK.

[012-080]Precisely.

[012-081]HEDDA.

[012-082]A sort of a--singer?

[012-083]BRACK.

[012-084]Oh yes--in her leisure moments. And moreover a mighty huntress--of

[012-085]men--Mrs. Hedda. You have no doubt heard of her. Eilert Lovborg was one

[012-086]of her most enthusiastic protectors--in the days of his glory.

[012-087]HEDDA.

[012-088]And how did all this end?

[012-089]BRACK.

[012-090]Far from amicably, it appears. After a most tender meeting, they seem to

[012-091]have come to blows--

[012-092]HEDDA.

[012-093]Lovborg and she?

[012-094]BRACK.

[012-095]Yes. He accused her or her friends of having robbed him. He declared

[012-096]that his pocket-book had disappeared--and other things as well. In

[012-097]short, he seems to have made a furious disturbance.

[012-098]HEDDA.

[012-099]And what came of it all?

[012-100]BRACK.

[012-101]It came to a general scrimmage, in which the ladies as well as the

[012-102]gentlemen took part. Fortunately the police at last appeared on the

[012-103]scene.

[012-104]HEDDA.

[012-105]The police too?

[012-106]BRACK.

[012-107]Yes. I fancy it will prove a costly frolic for Eilert Lovborg, crazy

[012-108]being that he is.

[012-109]HEDDA.

[012-110]How so?

[012-111]BRACK.

[012-112]He seems to have made a violent resistance--to have hit one of the

[012-113]constables on the head and torn the coat off his back. So they had to

[012-114]march him off to the police-station with the rest.

[012-115]HEDDA.

[012-116]How have you learnt all this?

[012-117]BRACK.

[012-118]From the police themselves.

[012-119]HEDDA.

[012-120][Gazing straight before her.] So that is what happened. Then he had no

[012-121]vine-leaves in his hair.

[012-122]BRACK.

[012-123]Vine-leaves, Mrs. Hedda?

[012-124]HEDDA.

[012-125][Changing her tone.] But tell me now, Judge--what is your real reason

[012-126]for tracking out Eilert Lovborg's movements so carefully?

[012-127]BRACK.

[012-128]In the first place, it could not be entirely indifferent to me if it

[012-129]should appear in the police-court that he came straight from my house.

[012-130]HEDDA.

[012-131]Will the matter come into court then?

[012-132]BRACK.

[012-133]Of course. However, I should scarcely have troubled so much about that.

[012-134]But I thought that, as a friend of the family, it was my duty to supply

[012-135]you and Tesman with a full account of his nocturnal exploits.

[012-136]HEDDA.

[012-137]Why so, Judge Brack?

[012-138]BRACK.

[012-139]Why, because I have a shrewd suspicion that he intends to use you as a

[012-140]sort of blind.

[012-141]HEDDA.

[012-142]Oh, how can you think such a thing!

[012-143]BRACK.

[012-144]Good heavens, Mrs. Hedda--we have eyes in our head. Mark my words! This

[012-145][[Mrs-Elvsted|Mrs. Elvsted]] will be in no hurry to leave town again.

[012-146]HEDDA.

[012-147]Well, even if there should be anything between them, I suppose there are

[012-148]plenty of other places where they could meet.

[012-149]BRACK.

[012-150]Not a single home. Henceforth, as before, every respectable house will

[012-151]be closed against Eilert Lovborg.

[012-152]HEDDA.

[012-153]And so ought mine to be, you mean?

[012-154]BRACK.

[012-155]Yes. I confess it would be more than painful to me if this personage

[012-156]were to be made free of your house. How superfluous, how intrusive, he

[012-157]would be, if he were to force his way into--

[012-158]HEDDA.

[012-159]--into the triangle?

[012-160]BRACK.

[012-161]Precisely. It would simply mean that I should find myself homeless.

[012-162]HEDDA.

[012-163][Looks at him with a smile.] So you want to be the one cock in the

[012-164]basket(12)--that is your aim.

[012-165]BRACK.

[012-166][Nods slowly and lowers his voice.] Yes, that is my aim. And for that I

[012-167]will fight--with every weapon I can command.

[012-168]HEDDA.

[012-169][Her smile vanishing.] I see you are a dangerous person--when it comes

[012-170]to the point.

[012-171]BRACK.

[012-172]Do you think so?

[012-173]HEDDA.

[012-174]I am beginning to think so. And I am exceedingly glad to think--that you

[012-175]have no sort of hold over me.

[012-176]BRACK.

[012-177][Laughing equivocally.] Well well, Mrs. Hedda--perhaps you are right

[012-178]there. If I had, who knows what I might be capable of?

[012-179]HEDDA.

[012-180]Come come now, Judge Brack! That sounds almost like a threat.

[012-181]BRACK.

[012-182][Rising.] Oh, not at all! The triangle, you know, ought, if possible, to

[012-183]be spontaneously constructed.

[012-184]HEDDA.

[012-185]There I agree with you.

[012-186]BRACK.

[012-187]Well, now I have said all I had to say; and I had better be getting back

[012-188]to town. Good-bye, Mrs. Hedda. [He goes towards the glass door.

[012-189]HEDDA.

[012-190][Rising.] Are you going through the garden?

[012-191]BRACK.

[012-192]Yes, it's a short cut for me.

[012-193]HEDDA.

[012-194]And then it is a back way, too.

[012-195]BRACK.

[012-196]Quite so. I have no objection to back ways. They may be piquant enough

[012-197]at times.

[012-198]HEDDA.

[012-199]When there is ball practice going on, you mean?

[012-200]BRACK.

[012-201][In the doorway, laughing to her.] Oh, people don't shoot their tame

[012-202]poultry, I fancy.

[012-203]HEDDA.

[012-204][Also laughing.] Oh no, when there is only one cock in the basket--

[012-205][They exchange laughing nods of farewell. He goes. She closes

[012-206]the door behind him.

[012-207][HEDDA, who has become quite serious, stands for a moment

[012-208]looking out. Presently she goes and peeps through the

[012-209]curtain over the middle doorway. Then she goes to the

[012-210]writing-table, takes LOVBORG'S packet out of the bookcase,

[012-211]and is on the point of looking through its contents. BERTA

[012-212]is heard speaking loudly in the hall. HEDDA turns and

[012-213]listens. Then she hastily locks up the packet in the drawer,

[012-214]and lays the key on the inkstand.

[012-215]EILERT LOVBORG, with his greatcoat on and his hat in his

[012-216]hand, tears open the hall door. He looks somewhat confused

[012-217]and irritated.

[012-218]LOVBORG.

[012-219][Looking towards the hall.] and I tell you I must and will come in!

[012-220]There!

[012-221][He closes the door, turns, sees HEDDA, at once regains his self-

[012-222]control, and bows.

[012-223]HEDDA.

[012-224][At the writing-table.] Well, Mr Lovborg, this is rather a late hour to

[012-225]call for Thea.

[012-226]LOVBORG.

[012-227]You mean rather an early hour to call on you. Pray pardon me.

[012-228]HEDDA.

[012-229]How do you know that she is still here?

[012-230]LOVBORG.

[012-231]They told me at her lodgings that she had been out all night.

[012-232]HEDDA.

[012-233][Going to the oval table.] Did you notice anything about the people of

[012-234]the house when they said that?

[012-235]LOVBORG.

[012-236][Looks inquiringly at her.] Notice anything about them?

[012-237]HEDDA.

[012-238]I mean, did they seem to think it odd?

[012-239]LOVBORG.

[012-240][Suddenly understanding.] Oh yes, of course! I am dragging her down with

[012-241]me! However, I didn't notice anything.--I suppose Tesman is not up yet.

[012-242]HEDDA.

[012-243]No--I think not--

[012-244]LOVBORG.

[012-245]When did he come home?

[012-246]HEDDA.

[012-247]Very late.

[012-248]LOVBORG.

[012-249]Did he tell you anything?

[012-250]HEDDA.

[012-251]Yes, I gathered that you had had an exceedingly jolly evening at Judge

[012-252]Brack's.

[012-253]LOVBORG.

[012-254]Nothing more?

[012-255]HEDDA.

[012-256]I don't think so. However, I was so dreadfully sleepy--

[012-257]MRS. ELVSTED enters through the curtains of the middle doorway.

[012-258]MRS. ELVSTED.

[012-259][Going towards him.] Ah, Lovborg! At last--!

[012-260]LOVBORG.

[012-261]Yes, at last. And too late!

[012-262]MRS. ELVSTED.

[012-263][Looks anxiously at him.] What is too late?

[012-264]LOVBORG.

[012-265]Everything is too late now. It is all over with me.

[012-266]MRS. ELVSTED.

[012-267]Oh no, no--don't say that!

[012-268]LOVBORG.

[012-269]You will say the same when you hear--

[012-270]MRS. ELVSTED.

[012-271]I won't hear anything!

[012-272]HEDDA.

[012-273]Perhaps you would prefer to talk to her alone? If so, I will leave you.

[012-274]LOVBORG.

[012-275]No, stay--you too. I beg you to stay.

[012-276]MRS. ELVSTED.

[012-277]Yes, but I won't hear anything, I tell you.

[012-278]LOVBORG.

[012-279]It is not last night's adventures that I want to talk about.

[012-280]MRS. ELVSTED.

[012-281]What is it then--?

[012-282]LOVBORG.

[012-283]I want to say that now our ways must part.

[012-284]MRS. ELVSTED.

[012-285]Part!

[012-286]HEDDA.

[012-287][Involuntarily.] I knew it!

[012-288]LOVBORG.

[012-289]You can be of no more service to me, Thea.

[012-290]MRS. ELVSTED.

[012-291]How can you stand there and say that! No more service to you! Am I not

[012-292]to help you now, as before? Are we not to go on working together?

[012-293]LOVBORG.

[012-294]Henceforward I shall do no work.

[012-295]MRS. ELVSTED.

[012-296][Despairingly.] Then what am I to do with my life?

[012-297]LOVBORG.

[012-298]You must try to live your life as if you had never known me.

[012-299]MRS. ELVSTED.

[012-300]But you know I cannot do that!

[012-301]LOVBORG.

[012-302]Try if you cannot, Thea. You must go home again--

[012-303]MRS. ELVSTED.

[012-304][In vehement protest.] Never in this world! Where you are, there will I

[012-305]be also! I will not let myself be driven away like this! I will remain

[012-306]here! I will be with you when the book appears.

[012-307]HEDDA.

[012-308][Half aloud, in suspense.] Ah yes--the book!

[012-309]LOVBORG.

[012-310][Looks at her.] My book and Thea's; for that is what it is.

[012-311]MRS. ELVSTED.

[012-312]Yes, I feel that it is. And that is why I have a right to be with you

[012-313]when it appears! I will see with my own eyes how respect and honour pour

[012-314]in upon you afresh. And the happiness--the happiness--oh, I must share

[012-315]it with you!

[012-316]LOVBORG.

[012-317]Thea--our book will never appear.

[012-318]HEDDA.

[012-319]Ah!

[012-320]MRS. ELVSTED.

[012-321]Never appear!

[012-322]LOVBORG.

[012-323]Can never appear.

[012-324]MRS. ELVSTED.

[012-325][In agonised foreboding.] Lovborg--what have you done with the

[012-326]manuscript?

[012-327]HEDDA.

[012-328][Looks anxiously at him.] Yes, the manuscript--?

[012-329]MRS. ELVSTED.

[012-330]Where is it?

[012-331]LOVBORG.

[012-332]The manuscript--. Well then--I have torn the manuscript into a thousand

[012-333]pieces.

[012-334]MRS. ELVSTED.

[012-335][Shrieks.] Oh no, no--!

[012-336]HEDDA.

[012-337][Involuntarily.] But that's not--

[012-338]LOVBORG.

[012-339][Looks at her.] Not true, you think?

[012-340]HEDDA.

[012-341][Collecting herself.] Oh well, of course--since you say so. But it

[012-342]sounded so improbable--

[012-343]LOVBORG.

[012-344]It is true, all the same.

[012-345]MRS. ELVSTED.

[012-346][Wringing her hands.] Oh God--oh God, Hedda--torn his own work to

[012-347]pieces!

[012-348]LOVBORG.

[012-349]I have torn my own life to pieces. So why should I not tear my

[012-350]life-work too--?

[012-351]MRS. ELVSTED.

[012-352]And you did this last night?

[012-353]LOVBORG.

[012-354]Yes, I tell you! Tore it into a thousand pieces--and scattered them on

[012-355]the fiord--far out. There there is cool sea-water at any rate--let them

[012-356]drift upon it--drift with the current and the wind. And then presently

[012-357]they will sink--deeper and deeper--as I shall, Thea.

[012-358]MRS. ELVSTED.

[012-359]Do you know, Lovborg, that what you have done with the book--I shall

[012-360]think of it to my dying day as though you had killed a little child.
