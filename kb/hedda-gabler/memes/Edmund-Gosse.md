Edmund Gosse (1849–1928) was a British poet, literary critic, and translator. Together with William Archer, he produced the first English translation of *Hedda Gabler* directly from Henrik Ibsen's original Norwegian text. (001-004)

## Translation Work

Gosse's collaborative translation with Archer was significant as the first Ibsen play to be rendered into English from the Norwegian original, rather than through a German translation — marking a new standard of fidelity in Ibsen translation. (001-080) This translation was central to the popularisation of Ibsen in England. (001-096)

> Translated by Edmund Gosse and William Archer
>
> (001-004)

> Hedda Gabler was published in Copenhagen on December 16, 1890. This was the first of Ibsen's plays to be translated from proof-sheets and published in England and America almost simultaneously with its first appearance in Scandinavia.
>
> (001-079–001-081)

## Relationship to Ibsen Studies

Gosse was part of the broader European network of critics and scholars — including Georg Brandes — who interpreted Ibsen's work for an international audience. His translation work bridged Scandinavian modernism and English literary culture at a pivotal moment in theatrical history.
