# Manuscript Burning Step by Step

The destruction of [[Eilert-Lovborg|Lovborg]]'s manuscript unfolds in six deliberate steps, each carrying dramatic weight.

**1. Possession.** Tesman finds the packet on the road after [[The-Bachelor-Party|the bachelor party]] and brings it home (009-013). The manuscript enters the domestic sphere, where it will be subject to [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]]'s control rather than Lovborg's authorship.

**2. Concealment.** When Lovborg arrives distraught at the villa, Hedda hides the manuscript. She listens to his confession of loss and despair — "I have torn my own life to pieces" (013-262) — and says nothing. Her silence is as deliberate as any spoken lie.

**3. The pistol.** Hedda gives Lovborg one of [[Heddas-Pistols|her pistols]], urging him toward a "beautiful" death. The twin instruments — pistol and fire — are now both in play. She retains one for herself.

**4. The burning.** Hedda takes the manuscript into [[The-Inner-Room|the inner room]] and feeds it page by page into [[The-Stove|the stove]]:

> *[Throws one of the quires into the fire and whispers to herself.]* Now I am burning your child, Thea!--Burning it, curly-locks! *[Throwing one or two more quires into the stove.]* Your child and Eilert Lovborg's. *[Throws the rest in.]* I am burning--I am burning your child.
>
> (013-125)

The phrase "your child, Thea" is directed at the absent [[Mrs-Elvsted|Thea]] as much as at Lovborg.

**5. The completion.** The entire manuscript is consumed. [[Berta]] later "makes up the fire" (011-101), unknowingly completing the work of destruction through domestic routine. The home itself — its servant, its stove — is complicit.

**6. The revelation.** Hedda tells Tesman she burned the manuscript "for your sake" — a self-justification that even Tesman, who excuses her in everything, finds horrifying. The act that was performed in secret is now claimed openly, with false motives.
