[011-001]MRS. ELVSTED.

[011-002]Thanks, not for me.

[011-003]BERTA.

[011-004]Oh, very well. [She goes softly out by the hall door.

[011-005]HEDDA.

[011-006][Is wakened by the shutting of the door, and looks up.] What's that--?

[011-007]MRS. ELVSTED.

[011-008]It was only the servant.

[011-009]HEDDA.

[011-010][Looking about her.] Oh, we're here--! Yes, now I remember. [Sits erect

[011-011]upon the sofa, stretches herself, and rubs her eyes.] What o'clock is

[011-012]it, Thea?

[011-013]MRS. ELVSTED.

[011-014][Looks at her watch.] It's past seven.

[011-015]HEDDA.

[011-016]When did Tesman come home?

[011-017]MRS. ELVSTED.

[011-018]He has not come.

[011-019]HEDDA.

[011-020]Not come home yet?

[011-021]MRS. ELVSTED.

[011-022][Rising.] No one has come.

[011-023]HEDDA.

[011-024]Think of our watching and waiting here till four in the morning--

[011-025]MRS. ELVSTED.

[011-026][Wringing her hands.] And how I watched and waited for him!

[011-027]HEDDA.

[011-028][Yawns, and says with her hand before her mouth.] Well well--we might

[011-029]have spared ourselves the trouble.

[011-030]MRS. ELVSTED.

[011-031]Did you get a little sleep?

[011-032]HEDDA.

[011-033]Oh yes; I believe I have slept pretty well. Have you not?

[011-034]MRS. ELVSTED.

[011-035]Not for a moment. I couldn't, Hedda!--not to save my life.

[011-036]HEDDA.

[011-037][Rises and goes towards her.] There there there! There's nothing to be

[011-038]so alarmed about. I understand quite well what has happened.

[011-039]MRS. ELVSTED.

[011-040]Well, what do you think? Won't you tell me?

[011-041]HEDDA.

[011-042]Why, of course it has been a very late affair at [[Judge-Brack|Judge Brack]]'s--

[011-043]MRS. ELVSTED.

[011-044]Yes, yes--that is clear enough. But all the same--

[011-045]HEDDA.

[011-046]And then, you see, Tesman hasn't cared to come home and ring us up in

[011-047]the middle of the night. [Laughing.] Perhaps he wasn't inclined to show

[011-048]himself either--immediately after a jollification.

[011-049]MRS. ELVSTED.

[011-050]But in that case--where can he have gone?

[011-051]HEDDA.

[011-052]Of course he has gone to his Aunts' and slept there. They have his old

[011-053]room ready for him.

[011-054]MRS. ELVSTED.

[011-055]No, he can't be with them for a letter has just come for him from Miss

[011-056]Tesman. There it lies.

[011-057]HEDDA.

[011-058]Indeed? [Looks at the address.] Why yes, it's addressed in Aunt Julia's

[011-059]hand. Well then, he has remained at Judge Brack's. And as for Eilert

[011-060]Lovborg--he is sitting, with vine-leaves in his hair, reading his

[011-061]manuscript.

[011-062]MRS. ELVSTED.

[011-063]Oh, Hedda, you are just saying things you don't believe a bit.

[011-064]HEDDA.

[011-065]You really are a little blockhead, Thea.

[011-066]MRS. ELVSTED.

[011-067]Oh yes, I suppose I am.

[011-068]HEDDA.

[011-069]And how mortally tired you look.

[011-070]MRS. ELVSTED.

[011-071]Yes, I am mortally tired.

[011-072]HEDDA.

[011-073]Well then, you must do as I tell you. You must go into my room and lie

[011-074]down for a little while.

[011-075]MRS. ELVSTED.

[011-076]Oh no, no--I shouldn't be able to sleep.

[011-077]HEDDA.

[011-078]I am sure you would.

[011-079]MRS. ELVSTED.

[011-080]Well, but you husband is certain to come soon now; and then I want to

[011-081]know at once--

[011-082]HEDDA.

[011-083]I shall take care to let you know when he comes.

[011-084]MRS. ELVSTED.

[011-085]Do you promise me, Hedda?

[011-086]HEDDA.

[011-087]Yes, rely upon me. Just you go in and have a sleep in the meantime.

[011-088]MRS. ELVSTED.

[011-089]Thanks; then I'll try. [She goes off to the inner room.

[011-090][HEDDA goes up to the glass door and draws back the curtains.

[011-091]The broad daylight streams into the room. Then she takes a

[011-092]little hand-glass from the writing-table, looks at herself

[011-093]in it, and arranges her hair. Next she goes to the hall

[011-094]door and presses the bell-button.

[011-095]BERTA presently appears at the hall door.

[011-096]BERTA.

[011-097]Did you want anything, ma'am?

[011-098]HEDDA.

[011-099]Yes; you must put some more wood in the stove. I am shivering.

[011-100]BERTA.

[011-101]Bless me--I'll make up the fire at once. [She rakes the embers together

[011-102]and lays a piece of wood upon them; then stops and listens.] That was a

[011-103]ring at the front door, ma'am.

[011-104]HEDDA.

[011-105]Then go to the door. I will look after the fire.

[011-106]BERTA.

[011-107]It'll soon burn up. [She goes out by the hall door.

[011-108][HEDDA kneels on the foot-rest and lays some more pieces of

[011-109]wood in the stove.

[011-110]After a short pause, GEORGE TESMAN enters from the hall. He

[011-111]steals on tiptoe towards the middle doorway and is about to

[011-112]slip through the curtains.

[011-113]HEDDA.

[011-114][At the stove, without looking up.] Good morning.

[011-115]TESMAN.

[011-116][Turns.] Hedda! [Approaching her.] Good heavens--are you up so early?

[011-117]Eh?

[011-118]HEDDA.

[011-119]Yes, I am up very early this morning.

[011-120]TESMAN.

[011-121]And I never doubted you were still sound asleep! Fancy that, Hedda!

[011-122]HEDDA.

[011-123]Don't speak so loud. [[Mrs-Elvsted|Mrs. Elvsted]] is resting in my room.

[011-124]TESMAN.

[011-125]Has Mrs. Elvsted been here all night?

[011-126]HEDDA.

[011-127]Yes, since no one came to fetch her.

[011-128]TESMAN.

[011-129]Ah, to be sure.

[011-130]HEDDA.

[011-131][Closes the door of the stove and rises.] Well, did you enjoy yourselves

[011-132]at Judge Brack's?

[011-133]TESMAN.

[011-134]Have you been anxious about me? Eh?

[011-135]HEDDA.

[011-136]No, I should never think of being anxious. But I asked if you had

[011-137]enjoyed yourself.

[011-138]TESMAN.

[011-139]Oh yes,--for once in a way. Especially the beginning of the evening; for

[011-140]then Eilert read me part of his book. We arrived more than an hour too

[011-141]early--fancy that! And Brack had all sorts of arrangements to make--so

[011-142]Eilert read to me.

[011-143]HEDDA.

[011-144][Seating herself by the table on the right.] Well? Tell me then--

[011-145]TESMAN.

[011-146][Sitting on a footstool near the stove.] Oh, Hedda, you can't conceive

[011-147]what a book that is going to be! I believe it is one of the most

[011-148]remarkable things that have ever been written. Fancy that!

[011-149]HEDDA.

[011-150]Yes yes; I don't care about that--

[011-151]TESMAN.

[011-152]I must make a confession to you, Hedda. When he had finished reading--a

[011-153]horrid feeling came over me.

[011-154]HEDDA.

[011-155]A horrid feeling?

[011-156]TESMAN.

[011-157]I felt jealous of Eilert for having had it in him to write such a book.

[011-158]Only think, Hedda!

[011-159]HEDDA.

[011-160]Yes, yes, I am thinking!

[011-161]TESMAN.

[011-162]And then how pitiful to think that he--with all his gifts--should be

[011-163]irreclaimable, after all.

[011-164]HEDDA.

[011-165]I suppose you mean that he has more courage than the rest?

[011-166]TESMAN.

[011-167]No, not at all--I mean that he is incapable of taking his pleasure in

[011-168]moderation.

[011-169]HEDDA.

[011-170]And what came of it all--in the end?

[011-171]TESMAN.

[011-172]Well, to tell the truth, I think it might best be described as an orgie,

[011-173]Hedda.

[011-174]HEDDA.

[011-175]Had he vine-leaves in his hair?

[011-176]TESMAN.

[011-177]Vine-leaves? No, I saw nothing of the sort. But he made a long, rambling

[011-178]speech in honour of the woman who had inspired him in his work--that was

[011-179]the phrase he used.

[011-180]HEDDA.

[011-181]Did he name her?

[011-182]TESMAN.

[011-183]No, he didn't; but I can't help thinking he meant Mrs. Elvsted. You may

[011-184]be sure he did.

[011-185]HEDDA.

[011-186]Well--where did you part from him?

[011-187]TESMAN.

[011-188]On the way to town. We broke up--the last of us at any rate--all

[011-189]together; and Brack came with us to get a breath of fresh air. And then,

[011-190]you see, we agreed to take Eilert home; for he had had far more than was

[011-191]good for him.

[011-192]HEDDA.

[011-193]I daresay.

[011-194]TESMAN.

[011-195]But now comes the strange part of it, Hedda; or, I should rather say,

[011-196]the melancholy part of it. I declare I am almost ashamed--on Eilert's

[011-197]account--to tell you--

[011-198]HEDDA.

[011-199]Oh, go on--!

[011-200]TESMAN.

[011-201]Well, as we were getting near town, you see, I happened to drop a little

[011-202]behind the others. Only for a minute or two--fancy that!

[011-203]HEDDA.

[011-204]Yes yes yes, but--?

[011-205]TESMAN.

[011-206]And then, as I hurried after them--what do you think I found by the

[011-207]wayside? Eh?

[011-208]HEDDA.

[011-209]Oh, how should I know!

[011-210]TESMAN.

[011-211]You mustn't speak of it to a soul, Hedda! Do you hear! Promise me, for

[011-212]Eilert's sake. [Draws a parcel, wrapped in paper, from his coat pocket.]

[011-213]Fancy, dear--I found this.

[011-214]HEDDA.

[011-215]Is not that the parcel he had with him yesterday?

[011-216]TESMAN.

[011-217]Yes, it is the whole of his precious, irreplaceable manuscript! And he

[011-218]had gone and lost it, and knew nothing about it. Only fancy, Hedda! So

[011-219]deplorably--

[011-220]HEDDA.

[011-221]But why did you not give him back the parcel at once?

[011-222]TESMAN.

[011-223]I didn't dare to--in the state he was then in--

[011-224]HEDDA.

[011-225]Did you not tell any of the others that you had found it?

[011-226]TESMAN.

[011-227]Oh, far from it! You can surely understand that, for Eilert's sake, I

[011-228]wouldn't do that.

[011-229]HEDDA.

[011-230]So no one knows that [[Eilert-Lovborg|Eilert Lovborg]]'s manuscript is in your possession?

[011-231]TESMAN.

[011-232]No. And no one must know it.

[011-233]HEDDA.

[011-234]Then what did you say to him afterwards?

[011-235]TESMAN.

[011-236]I didn't talk to him again at all; for when we got in among the streets,

[011-237]he and two or three of the others gave us the slip and disappeared.

[011-238]Fancy that!

[011-239]HEDDA.

[011-240]Indeed! They must have taken him home then.

[011-241]TESMAN.

[011-242]Yes, so it would appear. And Brack, too, left us.

[011-243]HEDDA.

[011-244]And what have you been doing with yourself since?

[011-245]TESMAN.

[011-246]Well, I and some of the others went home with one of the party, a jolly

[011-247]fellow, and took our morning coffee with him; or perhaps I should rather

[011-248]call it our night coffee--eh? But now, when I have rested a little, and

[011-249]given Eilert, poor fellow, time to have his sleep out, I must take this

[011-250]back to him.

[011-251]HEDDA.

[011-252][Holds out her hand for the packet.] No--don't give it to him! Not in

[011-253]such a hurry, I mean. Let me read it first.

[011-254]TESMAN.

[011-255]No, my dearest Hedda, I mustn't, I really mustn't.

[011-256]HEDDA.

[011-257]You must not?

[011-258]TESMAN.

[011-259]No--for you can imagine what a state of despair he will be in when he

[011-260]wakens and misses the manuscript. He has no copy of it, you must know!

[011-261]He told me so.

[011-262]HEDDA.

[011-263][Looking searchingly at him.] Can such a thing not be reproduced?

[011-264]Written over again?

[011-265]TESMAN.

[011-266]No, I don't think that would be possible. For the inspiration, you

[011-267]see--

[011-268]HEDDA.

[011-269]Yes, yes--I suppose it depends on that--[Lightly.] But, by-the-bye

[011-270]--here is a letter for you.

[011-271]TESMAN.

[011-272]Fancy--!

[011-273]HEDDA.

[011-274][Handing it to him.] It came early this morning.

[011-275]TESMAN.

[011-276]It's from Aunt Julia! What can it be? [He lays the packet on the other

[011-277]footstool, opens the letter, runs his eye through it, and jumps up.] Oh,

[011-278]Hedda--she says that poor [[Aunt-Rina|Aunt Rina]] is dying!

[011-279]HEDDA.

[011-280]Well, we were prepared for that.

[011-281]TESMAN.

[011-282]And that if I want to see her again, I must make haste. I'll run in to

[011-283]them at once.

[011-284]HEDDA.

[011-285][Suppressing a smile.] Will you run?

[011-286]TESMAN.

[011-287]Oh, my dearest Hedda--if you could only make up your mind to come with

[011-288]me! Just think!

[011-289]HEDDA.

[011-290][Rises and says wearily, repelling the idea.] No, no don't ask me. I

[011-291]will not look upon sickness and death. I loathe all sorts of ugliness.

[011-292]TESMAN.

[011-293]Well, well, then--! [Bustling around.] My hat--? My overcoat--? Oh,

[011-294]in the hall--. I do hope I mayn't come too late, Hedda! Eh?

[011-295]HEDDA.

[011-296]Oh, if you run-- [BERTA appears at the hall door.

[011-297]BERTA.

[011-298]Judge Brack is at the door, and wishes to know if he may come in.

[011-299]TESMAN.

[011-300]At this time! No, I can't possibly see him.

[011-301]HEDDA.

[011-302]But I can. [To BERTA.] Ask Judge Brack to come in. [BERTA goes out.

[011-303]HEDDA.

[011-304][Quickly, whispering.] The parcel, Tesman!

[011-305][She snatches it up from the stool.

[011-306]TESMAN.

[011-307]Yes, give it to me!

[011-308]HEDDA.

[011-309]No, no, I will keep it till you come back.

[011-310][She goes to the writing-table and places it in the bookcase.

[011-311]TESMAN stands in a flurry of haste, and cannot get his

[011-312]gloves on.

[011-313]JUDGE BRACK enters from the hall.

[011-314]HEDDA.

[011-315][Nodding to him.] You are an early bird, I must say.

[011-316]BRACK.

[011-317]Yes, don't you think so! [To TESMAN.] Are you on the move, too?

[011-318]TESMAN.

[011-319]Yes, I must rush of to my aunts'. Fancy--the invalid one is lying at

[011-320]death's door, poor creature.

[011-321]BRACK.

[011-322]Dear me, is she indeed? Then on no account let me detain you. At such a

[011-323]critical moment--

[011-324]TESMAN.

[011-325]Yes, I must really rush-- Good-bye! Good-bye!

[011-326][He hastens out by the hall door.

[011-327]HEDDA.

[011-328][Approaching.] You seem to have made a particularly lively night of it

[011-329]at your rooms, Judge Brack.

[011-330]BRACK.

[011-331]I assure you I have not had my clothes off, Mrs. Hedda.
