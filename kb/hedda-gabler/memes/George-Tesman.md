# George Tesman

Hedda Gabler's husband — a "stipendiat i kulturhistorie" (holder of a scholarship for research into the History of Civilisation) (002-134) whose earnest, methodical scholarship stands in deliberate contrast to [[Eilert-Lovborg]]'s visionary brilliance. Ibsen uses Tesman to embody the virtues and limitations of bourgeois respectability: he is kind, hardworking, and sincere — and these very qualities make him catastrophically inadequate as [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]]'s husband.

Raised by his devoted aunts, Tesman is a compiler rather than a creator — his meticulous research into "the domestic industries of Brabant during the Middle Ages" (004-002) marks him as a conscientious drudge who knows his own limits. He adores Hedda genuinely but blindly, purchasing the [[Tesman-Villa|Falk Villa]] on credit because she once admired it, never perceiving her contempt. His key relationships — with [[Aunt-Julia]], [[Eilert-Lovborg]], and [[Mrs-Elvsted]] — map a world of mutual affection and scholarly rivalry. When he recovers Lovborg's lost manuscript, his honesty unwittingly hands Hedda the instrument of destruction. After her suicide, he sits with Thea reconstructing the manuscript — the plodding compiler finding purpose in preservation.
