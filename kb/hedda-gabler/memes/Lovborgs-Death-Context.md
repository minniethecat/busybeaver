# Lovborg's Death Context

> Ibsen had no doubt heard how the wife of a well-known Norwegian composer, in a fit of raging jealousy excited by her husband's prolonged absence from home, burnt the manuscript of a symphony which he had just finished. The circumstances under which Hedda burns Lovborg's manuscript are, of course, entirely different and infinitely more dramatic; but here we have merely another instance of the dramatisation or "poetisation" of the raw material of life.
>
> (001-146–001-149)

[[Eilert-Lovborg|Lovborg]]'s death is the culmination of a chain of events set in motion by [[Hedda-Gabler|Hedda]] across two acts of the play. Understanding this sequence is essential to understanding the death itself.

**1. The provocation.** At the end of Act II, Hedda goads Lovborg into drinking by questioning his [[Courage-and-Cowardice|courage]] before [[The-Bachelor-Party|the bachelor party]]. Her manipulation targets precisely the insecurity that [[Mrs-Elvsted|Thea]]'s influence had healed. Lovborg, stung by the implication of cowardice, drinks the punch he had previously refused.

**2. The party.** Lovborg goes to the bachelor party at [[Judge-Brack]]'s lodgings, drinks heavily, and loses [[The-Manuscript|his manuscript]] — the "child" of his intellectual union with Thea. The party, meant as a celebration of Tesman's professorship, becomes the scene of Lovborg's catastrophic relapse.

**3. The brothel.** After the party, Lovborg accompanies "a jolly fellow" to what Brack euphemistically calls a "coffee-party" (011-246) — in reality, [[Mademoiselle-Dianas]]. The respectable evening ends in the shadow-world of the play.

**4. The manuscript and the pistol.** Tesman finds the dropped manuscript and brings it home. Hedda conceals it from Lovborg, gives him one of [[Heddas-Pistols|her pistols]], and urges him to "die beautifully." She withholds the manuscript that could restore his hope and supplies the weapon that will end his life.

**5. The death.** At the brothel, Lovborg accuses the women of stealing his manuscript. An altercation ensues. The pistol — Hedda's pistol — goes off. He is shot "in the bowels" (012-190), dying not beautifully but squalidly, in a brawl in a brothel.
