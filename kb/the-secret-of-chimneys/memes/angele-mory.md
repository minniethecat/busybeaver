## Role in the Novel

Angèle Mory is a central antagonist whose past actions drive much of the novel's intrigue.
She is revealed to have been Queen Varaga of Herzoslovakia, a former Folies Bergères actress
who married King Nicholas IV and was crowned queen [019-002]. Her identity as both actress
and queen ties together the novel's political and criminal threads.

Prior to her death (before the events of the novel), Angèle Mory was the intermediary between
King Victor and the Comrades of the Red Hand [019-003]. She accepted a bribe from the
revolutionary organization to infatuate the young King Nicholas and decoy him to a location
where they could assassinate him [019-004]. However, she exceeded her instructions by
marrying the king and becoming Queen Varaga of Herzoslovakia [019-005].

Angèle Mory was killed during the Herzoslovakian revolution, her body burned beyond recognition
by the fury of the populace against the lowborn foreign queen [019-007]. Her death before the
novel's opening makes her a shadowy, offstage presence whose schemes continue to reverberate.

## Description

Described as a "clever young actress" from the Folies Bergères [019-002], Angèle Mory was
ambitious and resourceful. She rose from the Parisian stage to become queen of a Balkan
kingdom, described as "not bad for a little Parisian actress" [019-006].

Even as queen, she maintained her criminal connections. The principal stones among the crown
jewels of Herzoslovakia had been replaced by paste under her direction [019-008]. She continued
practising her criminal activities alongside her royal role [019-008].

## Key Relationships

- **King Victor**: Longtime confederate and fellow jewel thief [019-007]
- **Nicholas IV of Herzoslovakia**: Her husband, whom she was originally sent to assassinate [019-004]
- **Comrades of the Red Hand**: Revolutionary organization that recruited her [019-003]
- **Prince Michael Obolovitch**: Her stepson (King Nicholas's son from a previous marriage)

## Key Events

- Recruited by the Comrades of the Red Hand to infiltrate the Herzoslovakian court [019-003]
- Married King Nicholas IV and became Queen Varaga of Herzoslovakia [019-005]
- Visited England as a guest of the Marquis of Caterham at Chimneys [019-009]
- Killed during the Herzoslovakian revolution [019-007]
- Posthumously referred to as "the lady who is dead" and "the late Queen Varaga" by
  Superintendent Battle [030-011]

## Significance

Angèle Mory represents the intersection of crime and politics in the novel. Her dual identity
as actress and queen, criminal and royal, embodies the theme of disguise and impersonation
that runs throughout *The Secret of Chimneys*. Her legacy continues to threaten the stability
of Herzoslovakia and drives the political machinations of the novel's present-day action.
