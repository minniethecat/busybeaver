## Role in the Novel

The British Diplomatic Service forms the professional background of Virginia Revel's late husband, Captain Timothy Revel, whose posting to Herzoslovakia connects the diplomatic world to the novel's central conspiracy [1171-1171]. The Service also employs George Lomax, a key figure in the Foreign Office who orchestrates the invitation to Chimneys.

## Connection to Herzoslovakia

Virginia Revel accompanied her husband to Herzoslovakia during his diplomatic posting there, just before the sensational assassination of the king and queen [4521-4521]. This personal experience gives Virginia firsthand knowledge of Herzoslovakian politics, making her a valuable asset in George Lomax's diplomatic maneuvering at Chimneys.

## Diplomatic Intrigue

The Diplomatic Service's involvement in the Stylptitch memoirs affair illustrates the intersection of personal memoirs and international diplomacy. Count Stylptitch himself was a diplomatic figure whose career spanned multiple countries [327-327]. The British government, through George Lomax and the Foreign Office, attempts to control the publication of Stylptitch's memoirs to protect sensitive diplomatic interests.

> "Your husband was in the Diplomatic Service, was he not? And you were with him in Herzoslovakia just before the assassination of the late king and queen." [4521-4521]

> Virginia's interest heightened a little. Her husband had been in the Diplomatic Service, and they had been together in Herzoslovakia just before the sensational murder of the king and queen. [1171-1171]
