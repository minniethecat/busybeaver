## Overview

In the evening following [[king-victor|King Victor]]'s dramatic unmasking in [[council-chamber|the Council Chamber]], the principal characters gathered to hear [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]]'s explanation of how he had pieced together the mystery. Hermann [[herman-isaacstein|Isaacstein]] opened the questioning, and Anthony modestly laid out the sequence of deductions and actions that had led to the resolution.

## Anthony's Explanation

Hermann [[herman-isaacstein|Isaacstein]], the financier whose oil interests had been central to the conspiracy, was the first to demand an explanation.

> "You owe us an explanation, I think, Mr. Cade," said Herman Isaacstein, somewhat later in the evening. [029-002]

Anthony recounted his journey to [[dover|Dover]], where he and [[hiram-fish|Hiram Fish]] had discovered a mysterious prisoner — the real [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]]'s envoy — whose testimony confirmed the identities of the conspirators. He described the crucial role of fingerprints and Bertillon measurements in exposing the false [[monsieur-lemoine|Lemoine]], and how the coded memoirs had provided the key to understanding Prince Michael's secret dealings.

> "It was a clever idea," said Anthony. "I admired it so much that I felt forced to play up." [029-005]

## George Lomax's Reproach

[[george-lomax|George Lomax]], ever the bureaucrat, could not resist reproaching Anthony for his unorthodox methods, particularly regarding the recovery of [[koh-i-noor-diamond|the K]]oh-i-Noor diamond.

> "I must say, Mr. Cade, that I consider your action in that matter highly reprehensible. If the slightest hitch had occurred in your plans, one of our national possessions might have disappeared beyond the hope of recovery" [029-007]

## Resolution

The denouement tied together all the novel's threads: the [[restoration-of-the-herzoslovakian-monarchy|Herzoslovakian succession]], the oil concessions, the coded memoirs, [[koh-i-noor-diamond|the K]]oh-i-Noor, the murder of Prince Michael, and the true identity of [[king-victor|King Victor]]. Anthony's role evolved from adventurer-for-hire to the hero who, through a combination of luck, intelligence, and nerve, had saved the British government from a diplomatic catastrophe.
