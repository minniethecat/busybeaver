# Balkans

The politically volatile Balkan region of southeastern Europe that serves as the geopolitical backdrop for the novel's intrigue — the birthplace of [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]'s revolution, the arena of [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]]'s diplomatic career, and the source of the revolutionary organization known as the [[comrades-of-the-red-hand|Comrades of the Red Hand]].

## Description

The Balkans are described in the novel as a region of chronic instability, summarized by [[james-mcgrath|Jimmy McGrath]]'s capsule description of [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]: "It's one of the Balkan States, isn't it? Principal rivers, unknown. Principal mountains, also unknown, but fairly numerous. Capital, [[ekarest|Ekarest]]. Population, chiefly brigands. Hobby, assassinating kings and having revolutions" (221-PN). [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] notes the English stereotype: "people in [[england|England]] expect that sort of thing from the Balkans. I don't know why they should, but they do" (977-PN). [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]] is referred to as "The Grand Old Man of the Balkans" — "Every move and counter move in the Near East for the last twenty years has had [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]] at the bottom of it" (233-PN).

## Role in the Novel

The Balkans provide the political and historical context for the novel's central conspiracy. The assassination of [[nicholas-iv|King Nicholas IV]] of [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]], the subsequent revolution, and the ongoing power struggle between royalists and republicans all originate in Balkan politics. The [[comrades-of-the-red-hand|Comrades of the Red Hand]], the revolutionary group that commits [[murder-at-chimneys|the murder at Chimneys]] and pursues [[koh-i-noor-diamond|the K]]oh-i-Noor diamond, is a Balkan organization. [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]], though exiled in [[paris|Paris]], "was reported to have his finger in all the backstairs pies that went on in the Balkans" (253-PN). The region's tradition of political violence — assassinations, revolutions, secret societies — forms the cultural logic that drives the novel's plot.

## Key Events

- **Assassination of [[nicholas-iv|King Nicholas IV]]** — The [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n king is assassinated approximately seven years before the novel's events, triggering the republic and the chain of events that lead to the [[london|London]] diplomatic conference (221-PN, 4541-PN)
- **[[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]]'s Balkan intrigues** — For two decades, Stylptitch orchestrates diplomatic maneuvers across the Balkan States, making him simultaneously "a dictator and a patriot and a statesman" (233-PN)
- **The Balkan Express** — [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] plans his departure route via "the Balkan express" after collecting the thousand-pound legacy in [[london|London]] (425-PN)
- **Royal personages in [[england|England]]** — [[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]] reveals that "all the Balkan States were interested parties, and there were a lot of royal personages in [[england|England]] just then" during the period when [[koh-i-noor-diamond|the K]]oh-i-Noor disappeared (4541-PN)
