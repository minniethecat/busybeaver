# King Victor

King Victor is the alias of one of Europe's most notorious jewel thieves — "a fantastic, daring fellow, not to be daunted by anything" (002-PN). Operating from a base in [[paris|Paris]], his criminal network intersects with the [[herzoslovakia]] political situation, the disappearance of the [[koh-i-noor-diamond]], the blackmail of [[virginia-revel]], and ultimately the murder at [[chimneys]]. Though he rarely appears directly in the narrative, his influence pervades the novel's criminal underworld.

## Identity and Reputation

### The Nickname

"King Victor" was the name by which he was known in [[paris|Paris]], where his gang maintained its headquarters. The name suggests a certain criminal grandeur — a man who rules his underworld domain with royal authority. As [[anthony-cade]] explains to [[james-mcgrath]]: "He's a crook, Jimmy. The most notorious jewel thief in the world" (002-PN).

### Criminal Career

King Victor's gang specialized in high-value jewel thefts. [[paris|Paris]] was their base of operations, and they operated with a daring that earned Victor his fearsome reputation. Eventually, the French authorities "caught him there and put him away for seven years on a minor charge. They couldn't prove the more important things against him" (002-PN). At the time of the novel, he "will be out soon — or he may be out already" (002-PN), making his reappearance in the [[chimneys|Chimneys]] affair entirely plausible.

## Connection to the Plot

### Count Stylptitch

[[count-stylptitch]] knew of King Victor's people and feared them. In his final days in Africa, the count told [[james-mcgrath]] that "he knew the gang that was after him. They were King Victor's people, he said" (002-PN). This connection suggests that Victor's criminal network had an interest in [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the memoirs]], or in something [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the memoirs]] contained — possibly the location of the missing [[koh-i-noor-diamond]].

### The Blackmail Operation

The blackmailer who approaches [[virginia-revel]] at [[pont-street]] is an operative of King Victor's gang. The scheme involves a mix-up: the operative wrongly believes [[virginia-revel|Virginia]] possesses compromising letters, setting off a chain of events that leads to the blackmailer's death and [[anthony-cade]]'s involvement.

### The Koh-i-Noor Connection

As the world's most notorious jewel thief, King Victor has a natural interest in the [[koh-i-noor-diamond]] — one of the largest and most famous gems in existence. The diamond's disappearance from [[chimneys]] years earlier, and the possibility that [[count-stylptitch]]'s memoirs reveal its whereabouts, make Victor's involvement in the affair almost inevitable.

### The Murder at Chimneys

While [[superintendent-battle]] initially suspects the [[red-hand]] revolutionary organization of [[prince-michael-obolovitch]]'s murder, the trail increasingly leads toward more complex motivations — jewel theft, personal vendetta, and the hidden hand of King Victor's network.

## "Not That Kind of a King"

When [[james-mcgrath|Jimmy McGrath]] asks whether King Victor is "another Balkan monarch," [[anthony-cade]] replies: "No. He isn't that kind of a king" (002-PN). This exchange highlights the novel's thematic interest in the gap between title and substance — between the appearance of legitimacy and the reality underneath. King Victor's nickname parodies the very concept of royal authority that the [[herzoslovakia]] restoration seeks to reinstate.

## Criminal Network

King Victor's organization is described as a "gang" with operatives in multiple countries. Its methods include:

- **Jewel Theft**: The organization's primary trade
- **Blackmail**: Using compromising information to extort money
- **International Reach**: Operatives active from [[paris|Paris]] to [[london|London]] to Africa
- **Violence**: Willing to kill to protect their secrets
