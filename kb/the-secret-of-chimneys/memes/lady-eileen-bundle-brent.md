## Overview

Lady Eileen Brent, known to everyone as "Bundle," is the eldest daughter of [[lord-caterham|Clement Edward Alistair Brent]], ninth Marquis of [[lord-caterham|Caterham]]. Tall, slim, and dark, with "an attractive boyish face and a very determined manner," Bundle is one of the most vivacious and perceptive characters at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] [010-005]. Unlike her father, who shuns political involvement, Bundle is keenly engaged with the events unfolding around her, providing both comic relief and genuine investigative assistance.

## Character Profile

### Appearance and Manner

Bundle is described as "tall, slim and dark, with an attractive boyish face, and a very determined manner" [010-005]. She is energetic, outspoken, and irreverent — a young woman of the post-war generation who refuses to be constrained by traditional expectations. Her directness and sharp wit make her a foil to the pompous [[george-lomax|George Lomax]] and a natural ally to [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]].

### Background

As the eldest daughter of the Marquis of [[lord-caterham|Caterham]], Bundle has grown up at [[chimneys|Chimneys]], the historic country house that has hosted generations of political gatherings. Her late uncle, the eighth Marquis, was a prominent Secretary of State for Foreign Affairs, and her late mother was a daughter of the Duke of Perth [004-006]. Unlike her father, who finds politics tedious, Bundle takes an active interest in the affairs of the house — though she describes herself, with characteristic irony, as "a red-hot socialist" [004-0[[TOC|31]]].

## Role in the Plot

Bundle plays a central role in the weekend party at [[chimneys|Chimneys]]. She is the first family member to receive news of the murder, having been summoned by the butler [[tredwell|Tredwell]]. She arrives at the scene with characteristic efficiency, declaring "I've got him" — though she is merely referring to having located her father, not the murderer [010-006].

Throughout the investigation, Bundle serves as [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]]'s most helpful source of household information. She identifies [[mademoiselle-brun|Mademoiselle Brun]]'s room for him, explains the governess's background, and provides witty commentary on the various guests [011-066]. Her sharp grey eyes and shrewd penetration make her an able observer, even if Anthony finds her perceptiveness slightly intimidating [016-026].

> "What an extraordinary question!" said Bundle. "You intrigue me greatly. Let me see — yes — that's Mademoiselle Brun's room. The French governess." [011-066]

Bundle also manages the social dynamics of the house party with considerable skill. She diverts [[hiram-fish|Hiram Fish]] to discuss first editions with her father, handles the foreign guests, and maintains good humor through the ordeal of a murder investigation conducted alongside diplomatic negotiations [015-012].

Her relationship with her father is affectionate and protective — she is seen "standing by poor old Father" when [[baron-lolopretjzyl|the Baron]] arrives [011-071]. She is on good terms with [[bill-eversleigh|Bill Eversleigh]], who is described as a decent shot whom she likes [004-013], though her banter suggests she views him more as a likable younger brother than a romantic prospect.

> "It's no good looking at Virginia," said Bundle sharply. [016-020]

## Personality and Traits

Bundle is candid, irreverent, and thoroughly modern. She has no patience for [[george-lomax|George Lomax]]'s pomposity ("Let George do his own dirty work, say I, and to hell with politics") [011-070] and greets the murder investigation with a mixture of excitement and pragmatism that disconcerts the more conventional members of the household.

> "I'm damned hungry," said Bundle with her mouth full. "It must be the excitement." [010-015]

Her wit is sharp and self-aware. When asked to send George's telegrams, she reads them with "a great deal of interest" [010-028]. She speculates wryly about whether one ought to invite a [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] detective to lunch, acknowledging her own ignorance of "the etiquette of such things" [011-067].

## Relationship to Other Characters

- **[[lord-caterham|Lord Caterham]]**: Her father, whom she both affectionately manages and protects from the more tiresome demands of guests and politicians

- **[[bill-eversleigh|Bill Eversleigh]]**: A family friend whom she likes, though she treats him with sisterly condescension [016-023]
- **[[virginia-revel|Virginia Revel]]**: A close friend who stays at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] for the weekend party
- **Dulcie and Daisy (Guggle and Winkle)**: Her younger sisters, looked after by [[mademoiselle-brun|Mademoiselle Brun]]
