## Overview

Staines is a real town west of [[london|London]], but in [[the-secret-of-chimneys|The Secret of Chimneys]] it appears primarily as the site of two connected crimes. First, [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] drives [[giuseppe-manelli|Giuseppe]]'s body out of [[london|London]] and leaves it "on the inside of a curve" on the road "midway between Hounslow and Staines" [009-100]. Later, [[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]] refers to "that curious business at Staines" — a shooting incident connected to the same criminal underworld [018-037].

## Role in the Novel

The Staines incidents serve as a subplot that links the [[london|London]] criminal underworld to the events at [[chimneys|Chimneys]]. Anthony's disposal of [[giuseppe-manelli|Giuseppe]]'s body near Staines is a direct consequence of the attempt on his life in [[london|London]] — an attempt orchestrated by the same forces pursuing the [[chimneys|Chimneys]] secret.

[[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]]'s pointed references to Staines during his conversations with Anthony suggest that the detective has connected Anthony to the roadside body, though he chooses not to make an issue of it [018-037], [019-111], [020-006]. This creates an undercurrent of tension: Anthony knows that [[superintendent-battle|Battle]] knows, and [[superintendent-battle|Battle]] knows that Anthony knows he knows — yet both men maintain a gentlemanly fiction of ignorance.

The Staines shooting — a separate incident occurring later — deepens the mystery and provides [[superintendent-battle|Battle]] with further evidence of the criminal network operating in [[england|England]].

## Key Events

- Anthony drives through Brentford and Hounslow to the Staines road, where he leaves [[giuseppe-manelli|Giuseppe]]'s body [009-100]
- [[superintendent-battle|Battle]] casually mentions "that curious business at Staines" to Anthony, testing his reaction [018-037]
- A shooting incident near Staines draws police attention [019-111]
- [[superintendent-battle|Battle]] explicitly asks Anthony whether he was connected with "the Staines affair" [020-032]

## Characters Associated

- [[anthony-cade]] — disposed of [[giuseppe-manelli|Giuseppe]]'s body near Staines
- [[superintendent-battle]] — investigated both Staines incidents and connected them to Anthony

## Significance

Staines represents the intrusion of [[london|London]]'s criminal violence into the seemingly tranquil English countryside. The fact that both a body dump and a shooting occur on the same stretch of road underscores the novel's theme that political intrigue, jewel theft, and gangland murder are not confined to continental Europe — they have reached the very outskirts of [[london|London]].
