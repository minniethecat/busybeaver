# Folies Bergère

The Folies Bergère is a real Parisian music hall where [[mademoiselle-brun|Angèle Mory]], a "clever young actress" (4551-PN), performed before becoming involved with [[king-victor|King Victor]]'s criminal operations. The venue serves as a narrative link between the Parisian demi-monde and the political intrigue that unfolds at [[chimneys|Chimneys]].

## Connection to the Plot

The [[comrades-of-the-red-hand|Comrades of the Red Hand]], in collaboration with [[king-victor|King Victor]], used [[mademoiselle-brun|Angèle Mory]] to "infatuate the young king" [[nicholas-iv|Nicholas IV]] and "decoy him to some spot agreed upon with them" for assassination (4551-PN). The Folies Bergère was where Mory first came to the attention of both the [[surete|Sûreté]] — who "suspected that she was associated with the operations of King Victor" but "could not obtain the necessary proof" (4551-PN) — and of the conspirators who recruited her.

## Significance

The Folies Bergère represents the shadowy Parisian underworld that serves as the novel's backstory. It connects [[mademoiselle-brun|Angèle Mory]], [[king-victor|King Victor]], the [[comrades-of-the-red-hand|Comrades of the Red Hand]], and the [[assassination-of-nicholas-iv|assassination of Nicholas IV]] into a single narrative thread, establishing the international scope of the conspiracy that [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] must unravel.
