# Countess Popoffsky

The Countess Popoffsky was the fabricated identity created for [[queen-varaga|Varaga]], a former Parisian music-hall performer, to legitimize her marriage to [[nicholas-iv|King Nicholas IV]] of [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]. The title — "Countess Popoffsky, or something" — was part of a broader fiction that she "had Romanov blood in her veins" (221-PN).

## The Deception

[[nicholas-iv|Nicholas IV]]'s infatuation with a "guttersnipe of a music hall artiste in Paris — not even suitable for a morganatic alliance" led him to construct an elaborate false identity for her (221-PN). The name "Countess Popoffsky" was chosen to suggest Russian aristocratic lineage, specifically Romanov blood, which would lend credibility to her claim to royal status in the eyes of the Herzoslovakian court.

## Consequences

The deception failed. The Herzoslovakian populace, described as "very aristocratic and reactionary," saw through the pretence and rejected the match (221-PN). The marriage — conducted at the [[ekarest-cathedral|cathedral in Ekarest]] with "a couple of unwilling Archbishops" — deepened public discontent and contributed to the [[herzoslovakian-revolution|revolution]] that toppled the monarchy and led to the murders of both [[nicholas-iv|Nicholas IV]] and [[queen-varaga|Queen Varaga]] (221-PN).
