# Varaga Palace

The royal palace in [[ekarest|Ekarest]], [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]], where [[nicholas-iv|King Nicholas IV]] and [[queen-varaga|Queen Varaga]] resided — and where they were murdered during the revolution that toppled the monarchy. The storming of the palace by revolutionaries marked the end of the royalist era and the proclamation of the Herzoslovakian republic.

## Historical Role

Varaga Palace was the seat of the Herzoslovakian monarchy under the [[obolovitch-dynasty|Obolovitch dynasty]]. It was here that [[nicholas-iv|Nicholas IV]], against the advice of his ministers, installed his controversial wife [[queen-varaga|Queen Varaga]] — a former Parisian music-hall performer elevated through a marriage conducted at the cathedral in [[ekarest|Ekarest]].

## The Storming

The palace was the focal point of the [[herzoslovakian-revolution|Herzoslovakian revolution]]. The Herzoslovakian populace, described as "very aristocratic and reactionary," rejected the legitimacy of Queen Varaga's Romanov pretense (221-PN). After "mutterings and discontent, and the usual ruthless suppressions," a "final uprising stormed the palace, murdered the king and queen, and proclaimed a republic" (221-PN).

## Aftermath

Following the storming of the palace and the deaths of [[nicholas-iv|Nicholas IV]] and [[queen-varaga|Queen Varaga]], Herzoslovakia became a republic — though instability persisted, with the country going on to "assassinate a president or two, just to keep their hand in" (221-PN). The palace itself passed from royal control to the republican government.
