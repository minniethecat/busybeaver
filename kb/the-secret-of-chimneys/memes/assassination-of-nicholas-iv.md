# Assassination of Nicholas IV

The murder of King Nicholas IV of [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]] — the foundational political event that precedes the novel's action by seven years, triggering the revolution that abolishes the monarchy, exiling the [[herzoslovakian-royalists|Royalist faction]], and setting in motion the contest for [[koh-i-noor-diamond|the K]]oh-i-Noor diamond that drives the novel's plot.

## Description

The assassination is described by [[james-mcgrath|Jimmy McGrath]] in his capsule summary of [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]: "Last king, [[nicholas-iv|Nicholas IV]]. Assassinated about seven years ago. Since then it's been a republic" (221-PN). [[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]] later provides more detail: the assassination occurred during [[nicholas-iv|King Nicholas]]'s visit to [[paris|Paris]], where the young actress [[mademoiselle-brun|Angèle Mory]] of the Folies Bergères — working with the [[comrades-of-the-red-hand|Comrades of the Red Hand]] — "infatuated the young king, and decoyed him to some spot agreed upon with them" (4551-PN). The [[comrades-of-the-red-hand|Comrades]] carry out the actual killing, but the operation is directed by "abler intelligence" — most likely [[king-victor|King Victor]], working in collaboration with [[mademoiselle-brun|Angèle Mory]] (6769-PN).

## Role in the Novel

The assassination is the distant cause of every event in the novel. Without it: there would be no [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n revolution, no exiled Royalist and Revolutionary factions in [[london|London]], no diplomatic conference at [[chimneys|Chimneys]], no contest for [[koh-i-noor-diamond|the K]]oh-i-Noor (which disappeared "in a way that seemed incredible unless you admitted two things — that the thief was a royal personage" (4541-PN)), and no reason for the [[comrades-of-the-red-hand|Comrades of the Red Hand]] to murder Prince Michael. The assassination is the tectonic shift whose aftershocks are still reverberating when [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] arrives in [[england|England]].

## Key Events

- **The assassination** — [[nicholas-iv|King Nicholas IV]] is killed approximately seven years before the novel's events (221-PN)
- **[[mademoiselle-brun|Angèle Mory]]'s role** — The Folies Bergères actress decoys the king to the assassination site on behalf of the [[comrades-of-the-red-hand|Comrades of the Red Hand]] (4551-PN)
- **The Koh-i-Noor disappears** — The diamond vanishes during the same period, under circumstances implicating a "royal personage" (4541-PN)
- **The republic established** — Following the king's death, [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]] becomes a republic (221-PN)
- **[[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]]'s exile** — The Grand Old Man of the [[balkans|Balkans]] goes to live in [[paris|Paris]], refusing the presidency (253-PN)
