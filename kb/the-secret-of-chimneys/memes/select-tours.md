# Select Tours

A commercial travel-tour operation — "Castle's Select Tours" — mentioned in passing by [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] as an example of a dubious enterprise, hinting at a backstory of shady dealings before the novel's events.

## Description

Select Tours is mentioned only once, when [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] agrees to [[james-mcgrath|Jimmy McGrath]]'s proposal to travel to [[england|England]] in his place: "It's a deal all right. I'm on. And confusion to Castle's Select Tours" (283-PN). The toast-like "confusion to" suggests Select Tours is associated with a figure named Castle, possibly a rival, former employer, or adversary from Anthony's past. The context of Anthony's adventures in South Africa and Rhodesia suggests Select Tours may be a front for less legitimate activities — consistent with the novel's theme of commercial enterprises masking criminal operations.

## Role in the Novel

Select Tours serves as a minor but telling detail — a quick brushstroke that colors [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]]'s backstory as an adventurer familiar with shady operations. The mention reinforces Anthony's character as someone who has navigated dubious enterprises across the Empire and can recognize a front company when he sees one. It also provides a structural echo: just as "Select Tours" may be a legitimate-seeming front for Castle's real business, so "Select Tours" (the travel company) is a front for political conspiracy in the novel's broader plot.
