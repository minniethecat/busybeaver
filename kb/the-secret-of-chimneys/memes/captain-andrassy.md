## Overview

Captain Andrassy is the equerry to [[prince-michael-obolovitch|Prince Michael Obolovitch]], the exiled heir to the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n throne. A short military officer with a fiercely twirled moustache, Andrassy is formal, correct, and deeply devoted to the royalist cause. His initial wariness of outsiders, particularly [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]], gradually softens as events unfold.

## Character Profile

### Appearance and Manner

Little Captain Andrassy is a formal, stiff military officer who twirls his moustache fiercely when agitated [026-018]. He is "equally correct as to demeanour, but with a slightly hostile attitude" when meeting new people [026-002]. His bearing is that of a man who takes his duties seriously and regards strangers with the suspicion befitting a royal bodyguard.

### Background

Andrassy serves as equerry to [[prince-michael-obolovitch|Prince Michael Obolovitch]], the heir to the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n throne. He remained in [[london|London]] when the Prince journeyed to [[chimneys|Chimneys]] with his valet [[boris-anchoukoff|Boris Anchoukoff]] — a fact that [[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]] finds suspicious [014-063]. His loyalty to the Obolovitch dynasty is absolute, and he works alongside [[baron-lolopretjzyl|Baron Lolopretjzyl]] in the effort to restore the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n monarchy.

## Key Actions

Andrassy's absence from [[chimneys|Chimneys]] on the night of Prince Michael's murder is noted by both [[george-lomax|George Lomax]] and [[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]], who question "why did the equerry, Captain Andrassy, not come down with the prince yesterday?" [014-063]. He arrives at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] later and is introduced to [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] by [[lord-caterham|Lord Caterham]] on the terrace [015-120]. During lunch, Andrassy and [[baron-lolopretjzyl|the Baron]] are described as "correct, formal, full of etiquette," with "the air of attending a meal in a mausoleum" [016-030].

When Anthony visits [[baron-lolopretjzyl|the Baron]]'s suite in [[london|London]], Andrassy is present alongside [[baron-lolopretjzyl|the Baron]] [026-002]. He has "never overcome his initial distrust of Anthony" and looks suspicious during their meeting [026-006]. When Anthony outlines his plan and makes a pointed remark, Andrassy springs up, exclaiming "Sir, you are insulting!" while twirling his moustache fiercely [026-018].

> "Sir," said Captain Andrassy, twirling his moustache fiercely, "you are insulting!" [026-018]

Despite his initial hostility, Andrassy eventually unbends and nods his head in agreement with Anthony's proposal [026-012]. He accompanies [[baron-lolopretjzyl|the Baron]] to [[chimneys|Chimneys]] for the final confrontation in the [[london|Council Chamber]], as Anthony instructs [026-023]. During the dramatic denouement, little Captain Andrassy springs up in excitement [029-028].

## Relationships

- **[[prince-michael-obolovitch|Prince Michael Obolovitch]]** — Andrassy's royal master, whose murder he seeks to avenge [011-037]
- **[[baron-lolopretjzyl|Baron Lolopretjzyl]]** — Andrassy's political ally in the royalist cause, with whom he is constantly paired [015-120], [026-002]
- **[[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]]** — initially viewed with deep suspicion, Andrassy eventually comes to cooperate with him [026-006]–[026-023]
- **[[lord-caterham|Lord Caterham]]** — the host at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] with whom Andrassy maintains stiff formality [015-120]

## Significance

Captain Andrassy embodies the old-world military honour and loyalty of the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n royalist faction. His fierce protectiveness and suspicion of outsiders reflect the precarious position of exiled royalty, where every stranger might be an assassin. His eventual cooperation with [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] represents the bridging of the gap between the aristocratic European old guard and the pragmatic English adventurer.
