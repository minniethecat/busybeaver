# Romanov Blood

The claim of "Romanov blood" was the central fiction in the [[countess-popoffsky|Countess Popoffsky]] deception — [[nicholas-iv|Nicholas IV]]'s attempt to legitimize his bride [[queen-varaga|Varaga]] by fabricating a connection to the Russian imperial Romanov dynasty (221-PN). The claim was part of a broader pretence that she was a genuine aristocrat rather than a "guttersnipe of a music hall artiste in Paris" (221-PN).

## The Deception's Failure

The Romanov claim failed to convince the Herzoslovakian populace. As [[james-mcgrath|Jimmy McGrath]] recounts, the Herzoslovakians were "very aristocratic and reactionary" and "like their kings and queens to be the genuine article" (221-PN). The false claim of imperial blood — like the fabricated [[countess-popoffsky|Countess Popoffsky]] title — could not overcome the fundamental illegitimacy of the marriage in the eyes of the people.

## Consequences

The failure of the Romanov pretence contributed directly to the "mutterings and discontent" that culminated in the [[herzoslovakian-revolution|revolution]], the storming of [[varaga-palace|Varaga Palace]], and the murders of both [[nicholas-iv|Nicholas IV]] and [[queen-varaga|Queen Varaga]] (221-PN).
