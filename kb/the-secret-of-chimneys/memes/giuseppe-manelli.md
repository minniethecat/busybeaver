## Role in the Novel

Giuseppe Manelli is a minor antagonist whose actions catalyze the novel's central
plot. Operating under the guise of a waiter at the Blitz Hotel, he is in truth an
operative working for an outside criminal agency — most likely the Comrades of the
Red Hand or the master criminal King Victor — tasked with intercepting the memoirs
of Count Stylptitch before they reach their publisher [005-121].

Giuseppe serves Anthony Cade dinner in his room and displays a feverish,
unmistakable interest in the parcel of manuscript on the table, shooting "little
glances sideways at Anthony's immovable back" and moving "softly round the table"
with twitching hands and dry lips [005-121]. That night, he enters Anthony's room
by way of the window — bypassing the chair-and-water-bottle trap Anthony had set
against the door — and is discovered kneeling by the suitcase [007-013]. Armed with
a "long thin knife," Giuseppe attacks Anthony, evidently "thoroughly at home with
his weapon" [005-134]. The two men grapple on the floor, Anthony desperately bending
Giuseppe's knife-arm back until the weapon falls [005-135].

Giuseppe escapes through the window, leaping to an adjoining balcony and vanishing
through the neighbouring room [005-137]. In his flight, however, he has made a
critical error: he has stolen the packet of letters addressed to "James McGrath"
rather than the Count Stylptitch manuscript they were wrapped around [007-004].
Anthony declines to pursue him through the hotel corridors, fearing unwanted
publicity, and instead reports the incident privately to the hotel manager [007-003].

Having botched his primary assignment, Giuseppe pivots to blackmail. Searching the
stolen letters, he discovers a newspaper cutting identifying the Hon. Mrs. Timothy
Revel (Virginia Revel) and, on the strength of a compromising letter written years
earlier, attempts to extort money from her at her Pont Street residence [020-052].
Virginia pays him forty pounds, but Giuseppe presses for more — a fatal miscalculation
[009-025].

Giuseppe is murdered that same day at Virginia Revel's cottage in Datchet, shot with
a small pistol engraved with the name "Virginia" [009-058]. The killer stripped the
body clean of all evidence — leaving only a torn scrap of paper reading "Chimneys
11:45 Thursday" caught in the lining of his pocket [009-058]. Anthony Cade,
discovering the body in Virginia's drawing room, recognizes the dead man instantly
as the waiter from the Blitz and disposes of the corpse by dumping it on a road
between Hounslow and Staines, then hiding the incriminating pistol high in a beech
tree at Burnham Beeches [009-100].

The body is eventually identified by Scotland Yard. Superintendent Battle reports:
"Nothing. They've identified him, that's all. It seems he was called Giuseppe
Manelli. He was a waiter at the Blitz in London. Curious, isn't it?" [019-113].

## Description

Giuseppe is an Italian who speaks fluent French and Italian [005-124]. He presents
himself as a skilled, experienced hotel waiter — the Blitz manager's records show he
had been in England about five years and had worked at several London hotels,
providing "complete satisfaction" in each post [007-038].

Anthony Cade assesses him as a tall man, "supple like all waiters, with a
clean-shaven, mobile face" [005-121]. During the knife attack, Anthony notes that
the Italian fights with practised competence — "evidently thoroughly at home with
his weapon" [005-134].

A telling observation from Anthony, upon seeing the body in Virginia's drawing
room, sums up Manelli's dual career: "He is an Italian. I should say that his
regular profession was that of a waiter. He only did blackmailing in his spare
time. His name might very possibly be Giuseppe" [009-025].

## Criminal Affiliations

Giuseppe's precise loyalties remain ambiguous throughout the novel. Anthony Cade
and Superintendent Battle consider several possibilities:

- **Comrades of the Red Hand**: Anthony suspects Giuseppe of being an emissary of
  this revolutionary organization, noting that the Comrades had previously
  attempted to obtain the memoirs through direct confrontation [007-005].
  Giuseppe's theatrical villainy — the foreign accent, the knife, the stealth —
  matches the Comrades' flamboyant style.

- **The Loyalist Party**: Anthony considers it "more probable" that Giuseppe was
  employed by Baron Lolopretjzyl and the Herzoslovakian loyalists, who had already
  attempted to purchase the memoirs from Anthony earlier that same day [007-005].

- **King Victor**: Superintendent Battle later theorizes that Giuseppe was got
  hold of "either by King Victor or by the Comrades of the Red Hand," and
  speculates that the Comrades and King Victor may have been working together.
  In Battle's reconstruction, Giuseppe, having stolen the letters instead of the
  memoirs, "sees the cutting from the paper and has the brilliant idea of turning
  them to account on his own by blackmailing the lady" [020-052]. This independent
  blackmail scheme likely led to his death — King Victor, Battle suggests, then
  "pinched them off Giuseppe that day in Pont Street" [020-060].

Employment records show that Giuseppe had worked as a waiter in England for
approximately five years, and had joined the Blitz Hotel staff about three months
before the events of the novel [007-038]. Significantly, "at two of the hotels in
question there had been serious robberies during the time that Giuseppe was
employed there, though no suspicion of any kind had attached to him in either
case" [007-040]. This pattern suggests a career criminal operating under the
cover of a legitimate service profession.

## Key Relationships

- **Anthony Cade**: Victim of Giuseppe's theft attempt at the Blitz Hotel
  [005-134]. Anthony later disposes of Giuseppe's body to protect Virginia Revel
  [009-100].
- **Virginia Revel**: Blackmail victim. Giuseppe extorts money from her using
  stolen letters, leading to the fateful meeting at her Pont Street home
  [020-052].
- **Jimmy McGrath**: Original addressee of the stolen letters. Giuseppe's
  mistake in taking the letters rather than the memoirs determines the course
  of the plot [007-004].
- **King Victor** (suspected): Probable employer who later murdered Giuseppe
  to recover the stolen letters and silence a loose end [020-052].
- **Baron Lolopretjzyl** (suspected): Possible employer representing the
  Herzoslovakian Loyalist party [007-005].

## Significance

Giuseppe Manelli functions as a pivotal hinge in the novel's plot mechanics.
His initial theft, though bungled, is the event that keeps the letters — not
the memoirs — in Anthony Cade's possession, thereby preserving the politically
explosive memorandum for the Herzoslovakian intrigue that follows at Chimneys
[007-004]. His discovery of the newspaper clipping among the stolen letters
transforms a failed espionage operation into a blackmail scheme, directly
linking Virginia Revel to the conspiracy and drawing Anthony Cade into the
role of her protector [020-052].

His murder — with a pistol bearing Virginia's name, at her own cottage —
represents a calculated attempt by the killer (almost certainly King Victor)
to incriminate Virginia and prevent her from attending the Chimneys house
party that weekend [009-058]. The scrap of paper in his pocket lining, bearing
the words "Chimneys 11:45 Thursday," becomes the thread that leads Anthony
to the scene of Prince Michael's murder — placing Cade at the crossroads of
two crimes and establishing him as a central figure in both investigations
[012-053].

In Superintendent Battle's final analysis, Giuseppe is the archetypal
middleman who outgrows his brief: a "regular thief" enlisted by a larger
conspirator, he attempts to turn an accidental windfall into a private profit
and is eliminated for his initiative [020-052]. His career — waiter, spy,
thief, blackmailer, corpse — epitomizes the novel's world of shifting
loyalties and disposable agents.
