# Captain O'Neill

Captain O'Neill was an alias used by [[king-victor|King Victor]] during his operations in [[paris|Paris]] — the name under which he conducted the forgery of love letters supposedly written by [[virginia-revel|Virginia Revel]], which later became the instrument of the [[the-blackmail-of-virginia-revel|blackmail attempt]] at her [[pont-street|Pont Street]] home.

## The Forgery

The letters — supposedly written by [[virginia-revel|Virginia Revel]] to a Captain O'Neill in Paris — were forged as part of [[king-victor|King Victor]]'s broader scheme. The [[surete|Sûreté]] had "suspicions that this Captain O'Neill was none other than 'King Victor'" but "could not obtain the necessary proof" (4551-PN). The choice of alias was characteristic of [[king-victor|King Victor]]'s methods — an identity just plausible enough to pass casual scrutiny but untraceable under investigation.

## Connection to Virginia Revel

The forged letters were later used to attempt the blackmail of [[virginia-revel|Virginia Revel]] at her [[pont-street|Pont Street]] home by [[giuseppe-manelli|Giuseppe Manelli]], a former waiter turned blackmailer (006-001). Virginia immediately recognized the forgery — "the signature on them was her name, but the handwriting was not hers" (006-041) — but the incident drew her into the investigation and, through her, connected [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] to the [[chimneys|Chimneys]] conspiracy.
