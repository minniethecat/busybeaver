# The Dover Expedition

[[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]]'s journey from [[chimneys|Chimneys]] to [[dover|Dover]], where he infiltrates [[king-victor|King Victor]]'s secret headquarters — a house called Hurstmere on [[dover|Langly Road]] — confronts the [[comrades-of-the-red-hand|Comrades of the Red Hand]], rescues a captive, and discovers that [[hiram-fish|Hiram P. Fish]] is a [[pinkerton|Pinkerton]] agent rather than an adversary.

## Description

The expedition begins when Anthony, having seen the address "[[dover|Hurstmere, Langly Road]], [[dover|Dover]]" penciled on [[monsieur-lemoine|M. Lemoine]]'s shirt cuff (5799-PN), abruptly departs [[chimneys|Chimneys]]. [[lady-eileen-bundle-brent|Bundle]] Brent drives him at breakneck speed to Hyde Park Corner, where he takes a taxi to [[king-victor|Victor]]ia Station and catches a train to [[dover|Dover]] (6007-PN). Arriving in [[dover|Dover]], he locates the house — "the last house in [[dover|Langly Road]]," a dilapidated property with rusted gates and overgrown grounds (5909-PN). Anthony observes armed sentries patrolling, notes the open-window conference of Magyar-looking men and rat-like Frenchmen, and climbs a [[virginia-revel|Virginia]] creeper to enter a room where a bound prisoner lies. At the climactic moment, [[hiram-fish|Hiram Fish]] appears in the doorway with a "big blue automatic," having followed Anthony on the same train — setting up their mutual revelation as allies (5909-PN).

## Role in the Novel

The [[dover|Dover]] expedition is the novel's action set-piece and a turning point in the investigation. It confirms [[king-victor|King Victor]]'s presence in [[england|England]], reveals the structure of his organization, and converts Fish from a suspect into an ally. The chapter devoted to this event — "[[dover|The House at Dover]]" (Chapter XXIV) — is one of the longest and most detailed action sequences in the novel.
