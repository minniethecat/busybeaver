## Overview

Baron Lolopretjzyl is the [[london|London]] representative of the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n Loyalist Party, working to restore the monarchy under [[prince-michael-obolovitch|Prince Michael Obolovitch]]. A large man with an "immense fan-like black beard and a high, bald forehead" [005-032], the Baron is a figure of formal dignity whose unpronounceable name provides comic relief — [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] immediately dubs him "Baron Lollipop" [005-048].

## Character Description

The Baron is described as "a big man with an immense fan-like black beard and a high, bald forehead" [005-032]. He speaks with a formal, slightly stilted English manner, characterized by inverted word order: "My lips sealed are" [005-040] and "The apple cart upset will be" [005-042]. His speech patterns, combined with his elaborate politeness, make him at once dignified and slightly comical.

> A big man with an immense fan-like black beard and a high, bald forehead. [005-032]

[[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] finds the Baron's appearance and manner so theatrical that he compares him to "the complete stage villain" and admires his "getup" [006-017]. Despite this, the Baron is not a villain — he is a sincere, if somewhat pompous, servant of the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n royalist cause.

## Role in the Political Intrigue

The Baron's mission in [[london|London]] is twofold: to recover [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]]'s memoirs before they can be published (as they contain politically damaging indiscretions) and to facilitate the restoration of the monarchy. He approaches [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] (whom he believes to be [[james-mcgrath|James McGrath]]) and offers to buy [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the memoirs]], explaining that "these great diplomats are always indiscreet" [005-042].

He represents the formal, diplomatic side of the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n restoration effort, in contrast to the more aggressive and ruthless elements within the movement. During Prince Michael's secret trip to [[london|London]], the Baron "rather frowned on" the Prince's plans, "thinking them injudicious at the present stage of affairs" [024-072].

> "I represent in London the Loyalist party of Herzoslovakia." [005-036]

## At Chimneys

The Baron arrives at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] as part of the weekend party, where his presence adds to the already complicated web of international interests. [[lady-eileen-bundle-brent|Bundle]] Brent finds his name nearly impossible to pronounce over the telephone — "It nearly wrecked the telephone this morning" [024-080] — and treats him with her characteristic flippancy.

At [[chimneys|Chimneys]], the Baron and his companion [[captain-andrassy|Captain Andrassy]] maintain a stiff, formal demeanor. During the tense lunch following Prince Michael's murder, they are described as "correct, formal, full of etiquette, and had the air of attending a meal in a mausoleum" [014-088]. Despite the gravity of the situation, the Baron's interactions with Anthony retain their comic undertone — when Anthony claims the right to speak as "between gentlemen," the Baron accepts this appeal to aristocratic honour [024-106].

## Name and Comedy

The Baron's name — Lolopretjzyl — is one of Christie's more playful inventions. Anthony immediately rechristens him "Baron Lollipop" [005-048], and the name recurs throughout the novel as a running joke. In one scene, a mysterious intruder attempts to pronounce the name and produces "a string of harsh-sounding consonants" [006-018], which Anthony mockingly repeats back as the man's credential.

This comic treatment of the Baron's name serves to humanize the character and prevent the political plot from becoming too ponderous. The Baron himself remains entirely unaware of the amusement his name causes, maintaining his dignity throughout.

## Significance

Baron Lolopretjzyl serves as the face of the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n Loyalist cause in [[london|London]]. Through his formal manners, elaborate speech, and unpronounceable name, Christie creates a character who is simultaneously a figure of genuine political significance and a source of gentle comedy. His presence at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] represents the legitimate, diplomatic dimension of the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n question, in contrast to the criminal elements also pursuing their interests there.
