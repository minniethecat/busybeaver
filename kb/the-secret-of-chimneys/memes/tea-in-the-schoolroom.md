# Tea in the Schoolroom

## Overview

The tea in the schoolroom at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] was a significant investigative moment during the [[the-house-party-at-chimneys|Chimneys house party]]. [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]], having ingratiated himself with the household, was invited by [[lady-eileen-bundle-brent|Lady Eileen "Bundle" Brent]] to take tea in the schoolroom, where she acted as temporary governess to the children of the household. The gathering, which also included [[mademoiselle-brun|Mademoiselle Brun]], provided an opportunity for [[lady-eileen-bundle-brent|Bundle]] to share her observations and for Anthony to advance his investigation.

## Context

By this point in the investigation, Anthony had become a popular figure at [[chimneys|Chimneys]]. His charm and easy manner won over the household, including the children and their governess:

> "About an hour later, Anthony wiped his forehead, and ventured to inquire after Mademoiselle's migraine. He was pleased to hear that that lady had entirely recovered. So popular had he become that he was urgently invited to come and have tea in the schoolroom." [016-001]

## The Gathering

Anthony was directed to the schoolroom by the dignified butler [[tredwell|Tredwell]]:

> "He strode briskly away towards the house. Inquiries of the dignified Tredwell showed him the way to the schoolroom. He tapped on the door and entered, to be greeted by squeals of joy." [016-002]

The schoolroom tea was a convivial affair. Anthony charmed the children and was gracious to [[mademoiselle-brun|Mademoiselle Brun]], who was clearly pleased by the attention of a handsome young man:

> "He was extremely pleasant to Mademoiselle, and she, on her part, was evidently delighted to have a good-looking young man invade her schoolroom. The meal was a great success." [016-003]

## Investigative Significance

The schoolroom tea served multiple purposes in the unfolding investigation. [[lady-eileen-bundle-brent|Bundle]] used the occasion to share her suspicions and observations about the various guests at [[chimneys|Chimneys]]. The informal setting allowed for frank conversation away from the more guarded atmosphere of the drawing room and the diplomatic conference proceedings.

The schoolroom also later became significant in the resolution of the case. It was revealed that a note passed by the false [[monsieur-lemoine|Lemoine]] to [[tredwell|Tredwell]], ostensibly to be delivered urgently, was in fact directed to [[mademoiselle-brun|Mademoiselle Brun]] in the schoolroom—part of [[king-victor|King Victor]]'s scheme to create a diversion and prevent anyone from leaving [[council-chamber|the Council Chamber]] during the critical moment of the [[koh-i-noor-diamond|Koh-i-Noor diamond]]'s disappearance [028-001].

## Mademoiselle Brun's Role

[[mademoiselle-brun|Mademoiselle Brun]], the French governess, was later revealed to be an accomplice of [[king-victor|King Victor]]. Her presence in the schoolroom provided a communications channel within [[chimneys|Chimneys]] that the conspirators exploited. The schoolroom tea thus took on retrospective significance as an instance where Anthony unknowingly socialized with one of the conspirators under the guise of ordinary household routine.
