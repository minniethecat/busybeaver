# Blackfriars Bridge

Blackfriars Bridge is a bridge over the River Thames in [[london|London]], mentioned in connection with the disposal of evidence during the investigation of the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n conspiracy. Its location along the river makes it a natural point for discarding objects — a function it serves in the novel's plot.

## Narrative Context

The bridge appears in the context of evidence disposal, connected to the efforts of various parties to control the flow of information about [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|Count Stylptitch's memoirs]], [[the-code-letters|the coded letters]], and the identities of the conspirators. Its mention ties into the novel's theme of concealment and revelation — what is thrown away and what is recovered.
