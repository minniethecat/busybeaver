## Summary

The memoir packet is the physical MacGuffin that drives the novel's plot — an oilskin-wrapped bundle containing [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]]'s coded memoirs. It passes through multiple hands, from a dying [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n man known as Dutch Pedro to [[james-mcgrath|Jimmy McGrath]], and finally to [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] [0457-0457].

## History

Dutch Pedro, a [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n who had been helped by [[james-mcgrath|Jimmy McGrath]] in Africa, pressed the oilskin packet into [[james-mcgrath|McGrath]]'s hands on his deathbed, whispering "excited jargon about a secret" [0457-0457]. [[james-mcgrath|McGrath]] did not open it for a week afterward, and when he did, he found the coded memoirs of [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]].

[[james-mcgrath|McGrath]] subsequently entrusted [[the-code-letters|the packet]] to [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]], asking him to deliver it to a publisher in [[london|London]]. Anthony later remarked that [[the-code-letters|the packet]] was "as good as a gold mine" to whoever held it, recognizing its political value [4575-4575].

## Significance

The packet is the novel's central MacGuffin — an object whose possession drives the plot. Multiple factions pursue it: the British government wishes to suppress its contents, the [[comrades-of-the-red-hand|Comrades of the Red Hand]] wish to steal it, and [[king-victor|King Victor]]'s criminal network seeks to exploit the secret it contains.
