## Role in the Novel

Chilvers is the butler at Virginia Revel's Pont Street residence. He appears in the
early chapters as a background figure whose formal manner and "subtle phraseology"
provide moments of understated comedy [006-004].

Chilvers opens the door to Bill Eversleigh and, with "the ease of long acquaintance,"
directs him to Mrs. Revel [004-001]. His most notable scene occurs when he announces
an unexpected visitor — Giuseppe Manelli, whom Chilvers delicately describes as
"a—a person" rather than stating outright that the man appears to be a foreigner
of dubious social standing [006-005].

Chilvers announces George Lomax's arrival and serves tea to the household [006-006].
He is later the recipient of a telegram notifying the household of a change of plans,
which is part of the scheme to lure Virginia away from Pont Street [008-012].

## Description

Chilvers is described as a "grave functionary" [004-001] and "impassive" [006-004].
His defining trait is his "subtle phraseology" — a diplomatic way of speaking that
cloaks unpleasant facts in genteel language, such as referring to the rough-looking
Giuseppe as "a—a person" [006-004].

He appears to have been with the Revel household for some time and shows the easy
familiarity with regular visitors like Bill Eversleigh [004-001]. His formal
deportment represents the well-run upper-class London household.

## Key Relationships

- **Virginia Revel**: His employer [006-004]
- **Bill Eversleigh**: A regular visitor whom Chilvers knows well [004-001]
- **Élise**: Virginia's French maid, with whom Chilvers shares household duties [008-011]

## Key Events

- Opens the door to Bill Eversleigh and directs him to Mrs. Revel [004-001]
- Announcing Giuseppe's visit with characteristic delicacy: "a—a person has called
  to see you" [006-004]
- Ushering George Lomax into the drawing-room [006-006]
- Serving tea [006-006]
- Receiving a telegram at Datchet arranging the household's departure — part of
  the scheme to clear the Pont Street house [008-012]

## Significance

Chilvers is a minor character whose function is primarily atmospheric — he embodies
the orderly world of the London upper class that is disrupted by the novel's criminal
and political events. His "subtle phraseology" and formal manners provide a contrast
with the rough-edged characters like Giuseppe and the revolutionary agents who
intrude upon the Revel household.
