# The Blackmail of Virginia Revel

[[giuseppe-manelli|Giuseppe Manelli]]'s attempt to blackmail [[virginia-revel|Virginia Revel]] at her [[pont-street|Pont Street]] residence, using letters forged to appear as though she had written them to a lover — a crime that triggers the cascade of events culminating in Manelli's murder at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] and the unraveling of [[koh-i-noor-diamond|the K]]oh-i-Noor conspiracy.

## Description

The blackmail attempt unfolds at [[pont-street|487 Pont Street]], where [[giuseppe-manelli|Giuseppe Manelli]] — an Italian waiter who has worked in [[england|England]] for five years (1565-PN) — gains entry to [[virginia-revel|Virginia]]'s house under the pretense of urgent business. The butler [[chilvers|Chilvers]] reluctantly admits him, describing him as "a person" and "some kind of foreigner" (1157-PN). [[giuseppe-manelli|Giuseppe]] presents [[virginia-revel|Virginia]] with compromising letters and demands payment. However, [[virginia-revel|Virginia]] immediately recognizes the letters as forgeries — she did not write them — and refuses to be intimidated. The confrontation reveals [[virginia-revel|Virginia]]'s strength of character: as Anthony later observes, "you'd never lie down to be blackmailed. The [[virginia-revel|Virginia Revel]] of those letters was scared stiff. You'd have fought" (3383-PN).

## Role in the Novel

This event is the novel's second inciting incident (alongside Anthony's mission to [[england|England]]). Giuseppe's blackmail attempt connects directly to the larger conspiracy: the forged letters are part of the same packet of correspondence that [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] brought from Africa. The attempt establishes [[virginia-revel|Virginia]] as a target, draws her into the web of intrigue, and makes her vulnerable to suspicion when [[giuseppe-manelli|Giuseppe]] is later found murdered. [[giuseppe-manelli|Giuseppe]]'s theft of [[virginia-revel|Virginia]]'s latchkey during the visit also provides [[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]] with a crucial piece of physical evidence linking the blackmailer to the subsequent events.

## Key Events

- **[[giuseppe-manelli|Giuseppe]]'s approach** — Posing as a distressed leaflet-seller, [[giuseppe-manelli|Giuseppe]] gains entry to [[virginia-revel|Virginia]]'s [[pont-street|Pont Street]] house (1157-PN)
- **The blackmail demand** — [[giuseppe-manelli|Giuseppe]] presents the forged letters and demands money for their return
- **[[virginia-revel|Virginia]]'s refusal** — She recognizes the letters as forgeries and refuses to pay, showing contempt rather than fear
- **The latchkey theft** — [[giuseppe-manelli|Giuseppe]] steals [[virginia-revel|Virginia]]'s latchkey during the visit (4843-PN)
- **Aftermath** — [[giuseppe-manelli|Giuseppe]] returns the next day to find the house empty (servants dismissed by a bogus telegram), and is shot dead in [[virginia-revel|Virginia]]'s study (1861-PN)
