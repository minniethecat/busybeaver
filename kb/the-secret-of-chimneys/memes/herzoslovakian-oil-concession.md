# Herzoslovakian Oil Concession

The discovery of oil in the Balkan kingdom of [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]] transforms the country from an obscure principality into a prize that British and American financial interests compete to control (002-054).

## Economic Significance

The rumour of oil transforms [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]'s geopolitical importance. Before the discovery, [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]] was of interest only to "king-and-queen and assassination enthusiasts" (001-100); afterward, the country becomes a focus of international diplomatic and financial competition. [[james-mcgrath|Jimmy McGrath]] remarks that he will invest Anthony's reward money "in Herzoslovakian oil shares" (002-066), and the [[foreign-office|British Foreign Office]]'s sudden interest in the [[restoration-of-the-herzoslovakian-monarchy|Herzoslovakian succession]] is transparently motivated by oil access.

## Political Dimensions

[[herman-isaacstein|Isaacstein]], the American financier, offers substantial loans to anyone who can control the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n throne—first to Prince Michael, and later to [[prince-nicholas-obolovitch|Prince Nicholas]]. "In return for financial support, he was prepared to grant concessions to a certain group of American financiers" (014-057). The British government counters through [[george-lomax|George Lomax]], who pressures [[virginia-revel|Virginia Revel]] to influence [[james-mcgrath|McGrath]]'s journalistic coverage because "the restoring of the monarchy in [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]] is imperative to the peace of Europe" (006-139)—a euphemism [[virginia-revel|Virginia]] dismisses as "all bosh" (006-140).

## King Victor's Scheme

[[king-victor|King Victor]]'s masterstroke is resurrecting [[prince-nicholas-obolovitch|Prince Nicholas]] as a claimant to the throne: "He resurrects [[prince-nicholas-obolovitch|Prince Nicholas]], and plays him to such purpose that he gets away with a tremendous haul of American dollars—all on account of the supposed oil concessions" (019-076). [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] later summarizes the situation drily: "The one wants a king and the other wants oil" (030-019).
