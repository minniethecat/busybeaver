# Herzoslovakia

Herzoslovakia is a fictional Balkan kingdom that serves as the political backdrop for the intrigue at [[chimneys]]. Once ruled by the Obolovitch dynasty, it has been a republic since the assassination of [[nicholas-iv|King Nicholas IV]] seven years before the novel's events — but a royalist restoration movement, oil concessions, and international diplomacy make it the axis around which the characters' competing ambitions revolve.

## Geography and Characteristics

As [[anthony-cade]] dismissively summarizes: "It's one of the [[balkans|Balkan States]], isn't it? Principal rivers, unknown. Principal mountains, also unknown, but fairly numerous. Capital, [[ekarest|Ekarest]]. Population, chiefly brigands. Hobby, assassinating kings and having revolutions" (002-PN). The capital city, [[ekarest|Ekarest]], is the seat of the republican government and the site of the cathedral where [[nicholas-iv|Nicholas IV]] married the controversial [[queen-varaga|Countess Popoffsky]].

## Political History

### The Obolovitch Monarchy

The [[obolovitch-dynasty|House of Obolovitch]] ruled Herzoslovakia for generations. The last reigning monarch, [[nicholas-iv|Nicholas IV]], was assassinated approximately seven years before the novel's events "along with his wife, [[queen-varaga|Queen Varaga]]" during a popular uprising. The revolution was triggered by Nicholas's marriage to a [[paris|Paris]]ian music-hall artiste whom he passed off as the [[queen-varaga|Countess Popoffsky]], claiming she had Romanov blood. The aristocratic Herzoslovakian populace rejected the match: "They like their kings and queens to be the genuine article" (002-PN).

### The Republic

Since the revolution, Herzoslovakia has been governed as a republic — though, as [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] notes, "things still manage to be pretty lively there. They've assassinated a president or two, just to keep their hand in" (002-PN).

### The Royalist Restoration

At the time of the novel, a Loyalist party is actively working to restore the monarchy, with [[prince-michael-obolovitch]] — a cousin or relative of the last king — as the proposed candidate. The British government supports this restoration, provided Prince Michael grants oil concessions to British companies. The [[baron-lolopretjzyl|Baron Lolopretjzyl]] represents the Herzoslovakian Loyalist party in [[london|London]].

## Economic Significance

### Oil Concessions

The discovery of oil in Herzoslovakia transforms the small Balkan nation into an object of international interest. As [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] observes: "I've a feeling in my bones, James, that people are getting ready to be interested in that unimportant little country" (002-PN). British, American, and other syndicates compete for the rights to exploit these resources: "It is most important that the Oil concessions should be granted to a British company" (003-PN).

### Financial Interests

[[herman-isaacstein]] represents a syndicate prepared to finance Prince Michael's restoration in exchange for oil rights. The [[foreign-office|British Foreign Office]], through [[george-lomax]], brokers these arrangements.

## Revolutionary Factions

### The Comrades of the Red Hand

The [[red-hand]] ([[comrades-of-the-red-hand|Comrades of the Red Hand]]) is a republican revolutionary organization that opposes the royalist restoration. They are suspected of involvement in the murder of [[prince-michael-obolovitch]] at [[chimneys]], though [[superintendent-battle]] doubts this was their work — a paper with a scarlet hand on it was found at the scene, but [[superintendent-battle|Battle]] considers it "put there to suggest the obvious solution" (014-PN).

### King Victor's Connection

[[king-victor]], the international [[king-victor|jewel thief]], has operatives connected to Herzoslovakian affairs. [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]], the late Herzoslovakian statesman, claimed to "know the gang that was after him. They were [[king-victor|King Victor]]'s people" (002-PN).

## Key Figures

- **[[count-stylptitch]]** — Former prime minister, the "Grand Old Man of the [[balkans|Balkans]]" whose memoirs threaten to upset the royalist restoration
- **[[prince-michael-obolovitch]]** — The proposed king, whose murder at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] is the novel's central crime
- **[[nicholas-iv]]** — The last reigning monarch, assassinated with [[queen-varaga|Queen Varaga]] seven years earlier
- **[[baron-lolopretjzyl]]** — Representative of the Herzoslovakian Loyalist party in [[london|London]]
- **[[boris-anchoukoff]]** — Prince Michael's valet, a Herzoslovakian national

## In the Novel

Herzoslovakia functions as more than mere background. The political situation drives the plot: the house party at [[chimneys]] is arranged to facilitate Prince Michael's deal with [[herman-isaacstein|Isaacstein]]; [[count-stylptitch]]'s memoirs threaten to derail the restoration; the murder of the prince implicates factions from the revolution; and the recovery of the [[koh-i-noor-diamond]] ties into the revolutionary conspiracy.
