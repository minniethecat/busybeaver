## Summary

[[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] receives [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]]'s memoirs from [[james-mcgrath|Jimmy McGrath]] in [[bulawayo|Bulawayo]] and later decodes the secret hidden within them [0130-0130]. The memoirs are a crucial MacGuffin that drives the plot forward, as multiple parties—including the British government and revolutionary factions—seek to control their contents [0[[TOC|31]]9-0[[TOC|31]]9].

## Context

[[james-mcgrath|Jimmy McGrath]] explains that the parcel contained [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]]'s memoirs, which he received from a dying [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n man known as Dutch Pedro [0130-0130]. Anthony quickly recognizes the political sensitivity of the documents, noting that "a lot of trouble has been caused by memoirs" with "indiscreet revelations" [0172-0172].

The [[foreign-office|Foreign Office]], through [[george-lomax|George Lomax]], expresses urgent concern about [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the memoirs]]' potential publication. Lomax states that the government had "agents in [[paris|Paris]]" who confirmed [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the memoirs]] were "conveyed away secretly some weeks before his death" [0[[TOC|31]]9-0[[TOC|31]]9]. The government wishes to "defer publication of [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the memoirs]] for at least a month" and to have them "judiciously edited" [0325-0325].

## The Coded Secret

The memoirs contain a coded message that reveals a significant secret. Anthony, whose real identity and capabilities are gradually revealed throughout the novel, is able to decode the message. The decoded secret becomes central to the resolution of the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n political crisis and the unmasking of [[king-victor|King Victor]].
