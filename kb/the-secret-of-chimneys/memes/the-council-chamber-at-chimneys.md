# The Council Chamber at Chimneys

The [[council-chamber|Council Chamber]] is a large oak-panelled room at [[chimneys|Chimneys]], the country estate of the Marquis of [[lord-caterham|Caterham]]. With three long windows opening onto the terrace, the room's historical name refers to its use for high-level political meetings—a function it serves again during the diplomatic conference (010-074).

## Description

The room is "panelled in oak, with three long windows giving on the terrace" (010-074). The sun pours "in through the three windows, flooding the room with light, and bringing out the mellow tone of the old panelling" (012-078). The walls hold "some remarkable pictures" (013-030), and the hall outside is similarly "panelled like [[council-chamber|the Council Chamber]] in old oak" (013-047).

## Central to the Mystery

The [[council-chamber|Council Chamber]] is where the action repeatedly converges. It is the room where [[prince-michael-obolovitch|Prince Michael Obolovitch]]'s body is discovered, where the suit of armour was found overturned at the crime scene, and where [[king-victor|King Victor]] searches for the hidden Koh-i-Noor diamond, systematically tapping the panelling (019-086). The room becomes so central to the investigation that [[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]] remarks, "[[king-victor|King Victor]], he's well aware by now that [[council-chamber|the Council Chamber]] is watched" (022-034).

## The Midnight Tapping

[[bill-eversleigh|Bill Eversleigh]] and [[lady-eileen-bundle-brent|Bundle]] Brent witness the famous midnight scene: "Silhouetted against it was the figure of a man, standing on a chair and gently tapping on the panelling" (017-060). They attempt to surprise the intruder from the secret passage, but "the thieves were dismantling the suits of armour in [[council-chamber|the Council Chamber]] when they were surprised and forced to flee" (018-060).

## Historical and Political Function

The room's name reflects its original purpose as a meeting place for affairs of state. [[george-lomax|George Lomax]] repurposes it for the 20th-century diplomatic conference on the [[restoration-of-the-herzoslovakian-monarchy|Herzoslovakian succession]], continuing a tradition of political decision-making within its walls.
