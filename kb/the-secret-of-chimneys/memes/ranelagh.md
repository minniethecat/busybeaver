# Ranelagh

[[london|London]] social and sporting venue — specifically its tennis courts — where [[virginia-revel|Virginia Revel]] spends the afternoon of the blackmail attempt, providing a narrative contrast between her leisured social world and the violent intrigue awaiting her at [[pont-street|Pont Street]].

## Description

Ranelagh was a fashionable sporting club and social venue in [[london|London]], known for its tennis courts and polo grounds. [[virginia-revel|Virginia Revel]] is a regular visitor — she tells [[bill-eversleigh|Bill Eversleigh]] "I can go to Ranelagh any day" (817-PN). The venue represents the carefree social world of the English upper class that the novel's criminal intrigue disrupts.

## Role in the Novel

Ranelagh serves as both a character note and a plot device. [[virginia-revel|Virginia]]'s afternoon of tennis establishes her as part of [[london|London]] society and provides the timeline for the day of the blackmail attempt. She arrives home "from Ranelagh to find that a bogus telegram had got all the servants except my maid out of the house. Then I walked into the study and found the man shot" (1861-PN). The journey "all the way back to [[pont-street|Pont Street]], as she lay back in the long, luxurious limousine" (1683-PN) creates a dramatic transition from the bright social world of Ranelagh to the dark discovery of [[giuseppe-manelli|Giuseppe]]'s body.

## Key Events

- **[[virginia-revel|Virginia]] skips Ranelagh for [[george-lomax|George Lomax]]** — [[virginia-revel|Virginia]] initially plans to be at Ranelagh, but cancels when [[george-lomax|George Lomax]] insists on an interview: "I think I must chuck Ranelagh. After all, I can go to Ranelagh any day" (817-PN)
- **Tennis at Ranelagh** — On the Thursday of the blackmail attempt, [[virginia-revel|Virginia]] plays tennis at Ranelagh before returning to [[pont-street|Pont Street]] to her fateful encounter (1683-PN)
- **Return from Ranelagh to discover the body** — The leisurely drive back from Ranelagh ends with the discovery of a dead man in her study (1861-PN)
- **Ranelagh as alibi** — [[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]] questions whether [[virginia-revel|Virginia]] could have sent the bogus telegram herself: "You were at Ranelagh this afternoon. What's to prevent you having sent it off yourself?" (1961-PN)
