# Prince Michael Obolovitch

Prince Michael Obolovitch was a member of the Obolovitch dynasty, the former ruling family of the Balkan kingdom of [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]], and a claimant to the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n throne. He arrived in England shortly before a diplomatic conference at [[chimneys|Chimneys]], where he was murdered under an assumed identity.

## Background and Royal Claim

The Obolovitch dynasty had ruled [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]] before the revolution. After the death of [[nicholas-iv|Nicholas IV]], who left no direct heir, the dynasty appeared to be in abeyance. However, Prince Michael emerged as a claimant to the restored throne [002-042]. The [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n people were reportedly prepared to "restore the monarchy, and are prepared to offer the crown to Prince Michael who has the support and encouragement of [[foreign-office|His Majesty's Government]]" [003-040].

> The point is not whether people will read them or not---they'll read them fast enough---but their publication at this juncture might ruin everything---everything. The people of Herzoslovakia wish to restore the monarchy, and are prepared to offer the crown to Prince Michael who has the support and encouragement of His Majesty's Government--- [003-038]

The memoirs of the late [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]] posed a threat to Michael's claim, as their publication at a politically sensitive moment "might ruin everything" regarding the proposed restoration. [[lord-caterham|Lord Caterham]] noted [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the memoirs]] might expose "tyranny and misbehaviour of the Obolovitch family generally" [003-043].

## Arrival at Chimneys

Prince Michael arrived "the end of the week" under the guise of a shooting party at [[chimneys|Chimneys]], where the political negotiations regarding his restoration were to take place [003-014]. He was accompanied by his valet, [[boris-anchoukoff|Boris Anchoukoff]], while his equerry [[captain-andrassy|Captain Andrassy]] remained in [[london|London]] [011-039].

> Prince Michael Obolovitch arrives the end of the week, and the whole thing can be carried through at Chimneys under the guise of a shooting party. [003-012]

Upon arrival at [[chimneys|Chimneys]], the Prince "declared himself greatly fatigued, and retired to the apartments set aside for him. Dinner was served to him there, and he did not meet the other members of the house party" [011-039]. This seclusion set the stage for the events that followed.

## Political Arrangements

Prince Michael's visit to [[chimneys|Chimneys]] was orchestrated by the [[foreign-office|British Foreign Office]], which supported his claim to the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n throne. A critical element of the plan involved American financier [[herman-isaacstein|Herman Isaacstein]]:

> Prince Michael was here for the express purpose of meeting Mr. Herman Isaacstein. A loan was to be arranged on certain terms. [011-028]

In exchange for financial backing, "Prince Michael pledged himself to grant certain oil concessions to those companies in which [[herman-isaacstein|Mr. Isaacstein]] is interested. The British Government was prepared to support the claim of Prince Michael to the throne in view of his pronounced British sympathies" [011-030].

[[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]] summarized the arrangement: "Prince Michael wanted the money, [[herman-isaacstein|Mr. Isaacstein]] wanted oil, and the British Government was ready to do the heavy father" [011-031].

## Murder

Prince Michael was found dead in his room at [[chimneys|Chimneys]]. He had been shot. The investigation revealed that he had been staying at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] incognito as "[[count-stanislaus|Count Stanislaus]]" --- a fact initially withheld from the public [012-073].

> The gentleman who was staying here incognito as Count Stanislaus was His Highness Prince Michael of Herzoslovakia. [012-071]

[[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]] observed that many parties had motives: the [[red-hand|Comrades of the Red Hand]] revolutionary group, rival claimant factions, and those involved in the oil concession negotiations.

## Rival Claimants

A competing claimant backed by "Wall Street" and American financial interests also sought the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n throne. This rival, "a first cousin of Prince Michael's," represented himself as "more in sympathy with modern enlightened ideas" and "in return for financial support he was prepared to grant concessions to a certain group of American financiers" [014-050]. [[herman-isaacstein|Herman Isaacstein]] acknowledged this rivalry, telling [[superintendent-battle|Battle]]: "Find out who killed Prince Michael, [[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]], if you want to do your country a service" [014-062].

## Personal Character

[[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]] described Prince Michael as "inclined to be a rather --- er --- dissipated young man" [014-064]. His equerry [[captain-andrassy|Captain Andrassy]] remained in [[london|London]] partly to "make arrangements with a certain lady, on behalf of Prince Michael, for next weekend," suggesting a private life [[baron-lolopretjzyl|the Baron]] considered "injudicious at the present stage of affairs" [014-064].
