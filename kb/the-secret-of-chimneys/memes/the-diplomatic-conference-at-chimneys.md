# The Diplomatic Conference at Chimneys

The secret diplomatic gathering convened by [[george-lomax|George Lomax]] at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] to negotiate the future governance of [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]] — a meeting that draws European powers, financiers, and royalist claimants to the English countryside and provides the geopolitical stakes driving the novel's criminal conspiracies.

## Description

[[george-lomax|George Lomax]], of the [[foreign-office|Foreign Office]], has arranged for the publication of [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]]'s memoirs to coincide with a carefully planned diplomatic conference at [[chimneys|Chimneys]]. The memoirs, to be published on "the 13th of October" (3565-PN), are intended to shape European opinion on the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n question. The Balkan state has been unstable since the revolution that killed [[nicholas-iv|King Nicholas IV]] and [[queen-varaga|Queen Varaga]] seven years earlier (4541-PN). Oil has reportedly been discovered there, raising the stakes: "I've a feeling in my bones, James, that people are getting ready to be interested in that unimportant little country" (398-PN).

The conference assembles key players: [[baron-lolopretjzyl|Baron Lolopretjzyl]] representing the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n Royalist cause, [[herman-isaacstein|Herman Isaacstein]] as the financial power behind any restoration, and [[george-lomax|George Lomax]] as the [[foreign-office|British Foreign Office]]'s strategist. "Reconstruction was going on — especially in the [[balkans|Near East]]," [[superintendent-battle|Battle]] later explains, "and there were a lot of royal personages in [[england|England]] just then" (4541-PN). The conference is intended to be conducted in secrecy — but [[king-victor|King Victor]] and the [[comrades-of-the-red-hand|Comrades of the Red Hand]] have their own plans for the outcome.

## Role in the Novel

The conference transforms [[chimneys|Chimneys]] from a country-house murder setting into a stage for international intrigue. It explains why so many foreign interests converge on a single English estate, justifies the presence of the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n characters, and reveals that [[koh-i-noor-diamond|the K]]oh-i-Noor diamond is not merely a valuable jewel but a political token — its possession could legitimize a claim to the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n throne. The memoirs' coded final letter, which [[superintendent-battle|Battle]] believes contains "the instructions for finding [[koh-i-noor-diamond|the diamond]]" (4818-PN), makes the conference and the jewel hunt one and the same event.

## Key Events

- **Lomax plans the conference** — The [[foreign-office|Foreign Office]] strategist uses [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the memoirs]]' publication date to convene the diplomatic gathering
- **October 13th is set** — The memoirs are to be published, and the conference convened, on this date (3565-PN)
- **Foreign interests converge** — [[baron-lolopretjzyl|Baron Lolopretjzyl]], [[herman-isaacstein|Isaacstein]], and others arrive at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] for the talks
- **The [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n question** — Negotiations center on whether to restore the monarchy or recognize the republic
- **The diamond's political significance revealed** — It emerges that possession of [[koh-i-noor-diamond|the K]]oh-i-Noor could influence the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n succession
- **The conference is disrupted** — The murder investigation and [[king-victor|King Victor]]'s schemes overshadow the diplomatic proceedings
