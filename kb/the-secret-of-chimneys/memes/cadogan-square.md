# Cadogan Square

Cadogan Square is [[george-lomax|George Lomax]]'s residence in [[london|London]], where the anxious machinery of British diplomacy regarding the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n succession is set in motion. It is at Cadogan Square that [[george-lomax|George Lomax]] confers with [[bill-eversleigh|Bill Eversleigh]], his private secretary, about the coded [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|memoirs of Count Stylptitch]] and the political complications surrounding the [[restoration-of-the-herzoslovakian-monarchy|Herzoslovakian succession]] (003-PN).

## Narrative Function

Cadogan Square is the nerve centre of the [[foreign-office|Foreign Office]]'s involvement in the novel's plot. It is here that [[george-lomax|George Lomax]] — "Codders" to his friends — receives intelligence, dispatches [[bill-eversleigh|Bill Eversleigh]] on errands, and orchestrates the British government's response to the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n crisis. The residence represents the official, bureaucratic side of the novel's intrigue, in contrast to the informal diplomacy conducted at [[chimneys|Chimneys]].
