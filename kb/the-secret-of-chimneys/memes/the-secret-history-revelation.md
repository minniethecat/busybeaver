## Overview

In Chapter 19, the French detective Balderson — whose true identity was that of a [[surete|Sûreté]] officer — gathered the principal characters in the library at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] to reveal the secret history of [[prince-michael-obolovitch|Prince Michael Obolovitch]]. This revelation connected the murder to the complex dynastic politics of [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]] and exposed the true stakes behind the diplomatic conference.

## The Revelation

Balderson began by confirming his true identity as a French police detective, not a member of the criminal underworld as several characters had suspected. His revelation transformed everyone's understanding of the case.

> "But yes," he said, "it is true." [019-003]

[[virginia-revel|Virginia Revel]] was the first to recognise that the time had come for clarity, pressing [[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]] to allow Balderson to speak freely.

> "Do you know what I think, Superintendent Battle? I think the time has come to enlighten us a little" [019-008]

## The Secret History

Balderson explained the complex backstory of Prince Michael's claim to the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n throne. The prince was not merely a minor royal but a central figure in the struggle for control of the Balkan kingdom's oil wealth. His murder was tied to the revolutionary faction that had overthrown the monarchy, and his presence in [[england|England]] was part of a larger scheme involving [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the memoirs]] of [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]].

Balderson's testimony revealed that Prince Michael had been working with British authorities while simultaneously maintaining contact with rival factions — a dangerous double game that ultimately cost him his life.

## Aftermath

The revelation fundamentally shifted the investigation. What had appeared to be a straightforward murder became a case involving international conspiracy, oil concessions, and dynastic succession. [[superintendent-battle|Battle]], who had already suspected much of this, used Balderson's testimony to build his case against [[king-victor|King Victor]] and the [[comrades-of-the-red-hand|Comrades of the Red Hand]].
