## Role in the Novel

South Africa serves as the novel's geographic origin point. Both Anthony Cade and James McGrath come from South Africa to England, setting the entire plot in motion. The colonial distance between South Africa and England creates a crucial information gap that Anthony exploits throughout the story [1611-1611].

## Inhabitants and Connections

James McGrath owned a farm in South Africa before his sudden inheritance of a thousand pounds prompted his return to England [1611-1611]. Anthony Cade, who had been working for Messrs. Castle, the tourist agents, in Bulawayo for about a month prior to his departure, agrees to travel to England in McGrath's place [5103-5103]. The two men had only known each other for a short period before McGrath entrusted Cade with delivering Count Stylptitch's memoirs.

## Investigation

Superintendent Battle cables to South Africa to verify Anthony Cade's story. The police confirm that Cade was indeed in Bulawayo with McGrath at the time he stated [5245-5245]. However, Cade's life before South Africa remains mysterious—Battle notes that Cade had been in Canada ten years earlier, but his activities in the intervening years are undocumented [5095-5095].

> "Here is a man who comes from the wilds. Two months ago he was in South Africa. Before that—where?" [6163-6163]

> "We wired at once to South Africa, and his story has been confirmed on all points. He was in Bulawayo with Mr. McGrath at the time he stated." [5245-5245]
