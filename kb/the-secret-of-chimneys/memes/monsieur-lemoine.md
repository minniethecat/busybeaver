## Overview

Monsieur Lemoine is a French detective from the Sûreté who arrives at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] to collaborate with [[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]]. Introduced as a colleague of [[superintendent-battle|Battle]]'s, Lemoine is clever, observant, and correct in demeanour. He plays a pivotal role in the search for the hidden jewel and the confrontation with the international criminal [[king-victor|King Victor]].

## Character Profile

### Appearance and Manner

Lemoine is described as "black-bearded and correct of demeanour as ever" [021-124]. He maintains a formal, professional exterior and speaks with the precision characteristic of a senior French police officer. He is quick to assess situations and people, studying [[virginia-revel|Virginia Revel]] attentively upon their first meeting [019-013].

### Background

Lemoine is a highly regarded detective at the Sûreté in Paris, described by [[superintendent-battle|Battle]] as a "clever chap" whom "they think no end of at the Sûreté" [022-065]. He was sent from Paris to work with [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] on the case, arriving the morning after Prince Michael's murder. Rather than presenting himself officially, Lemoine chose to study the situation from an unofficial standpoint, believing "great possibilities lay that way" [019-082].

> "I arrived on the morning after the murder. It occurred to me at once that it would be as well for me to study things from an unofficial standpoint without appearing officially as your colleague." [019-082]

## Key Actions

Lemoine is introduced to the household by [[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]] as "a colleague we have been expecting for some time" [018-150]. He participates in the confidential discussion where [[superintendent-battle|Battle]] reveals the history of the Koh-i-Noor diamond theft, contributing his own knowledge of [[mademoiselle-brun|Angèle Mory]] ([[queen-varaga|Queen Varaga]]) and the [[red-hand|Comrades of the Red Hand]] [019-026]–[019-037].

He admits to having been the mysterious figure who gave Anthony "rather violent exercise" during the night-time chase [019-084], revealing that his unofficial investigation involved observing events from the shadows.

Lemoine takes a keen interest in [[virginia-revel|Virginia Revel]] and engages her in a walk through the park, during which he manages to intercept a suspicious piece of luggage [021-133]–[021-161]. He recovers a revolver from the luggage and displays "gloating satisfaction" at his find [021-161].

He participates in the midnight search of the [[london|Council Chamber]]'s secret passage [023-001]–[023-092], where his frustration mounts as the jewel proves elusive. During this search he reveals his Sûreté credentials to Anthony, drawing back his sleeve to show a small badge [023-092].

## Relationships

- **[[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]]** — his professional counterpart who vouches for his credentials [018-150], [019-081]
- **[[virginia-revel|Virginia Revel]]** — Lemoine studies her attentively and she becomes an object of his professional interest [019-013], [019-029]
- **[[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]]** — initially adversarial (the chase in the dark), later cooperative in the [[london|Council Chamber]] search [023-001]–[023-092]
- **[[king-victor|King Victor]]** — Lemoine's ultimate target; his expertise on the international criminal drives much of the investigation [025-048]

## Significance

Monsieur Lemoine represents the international dimension of the investigation — the Sûreté's involvement underscores that the crimes at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] are not merely an English affair but part of a larger European conspiracy. His knowledge of [[king-victor|King Victor]]'s methods and the Parisian underworld proves essential to understanding the case. The ambiguity surrounding his identity — is he truly Lemoine of the Sûreté, or could he be someone else? — forms one of the novel's central questions.
