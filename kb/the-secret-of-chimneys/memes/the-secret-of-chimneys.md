# The Secret of Chimneys

The central mystery that drives the plot of Agatha Christie's detective novel. The "secret" involves a complex web of political intrigue, a [[theft-of-the-koh-i-noor|stolen diamond]], a [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|coded manuscript]], and the true identity of key characters connected to the fictional Balkan nation of [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]].

## Description

The secret of [[chimneys|Chimneys]] refers to the hidden truth about the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n political situation and the location of [[koh-i-noor-diamond|the K]]oh-i-Noor diamond, which was stolen from the Tower of [[london|London]]. The [[chimneys|Chimneys estate]] serves as the locus where various factions — British diplomats, [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n royalists, and revolutionary agents — converge to resolve these intertwined mysteries.

The secret is revealed through the coded memoirs of [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]], the former prime minister of [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]], whose manuscript holds the key to understanding the political conspiracy and [[koh-i-noor-diamond|the diamond]]'s whereabouts.

## Role in the Novel

The secret functions as the primary plot engine. It is introduced early when [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] is hired to deliver [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]]'s memoirs to a [[london|London]] publisher. The manuscript contains coded information that multiple parties are desperate to obtain, leading to blackmail, murder, and international intrigue centered on the [[chimneys|Chimneys]] country house.

[[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]] of [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] is called to [[chimneys|Chimneys]] to investigate the murder that occurs there, gradually uncovering the layers of the secret alongside [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] and [[lady-eileen-bundle-brent|Lady Eileen "Bundle" Brent]].
