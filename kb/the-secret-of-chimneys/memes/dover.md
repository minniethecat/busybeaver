# Dover

English Channel port town where [[king-victor|King Victor]] establishes his headquarters in a house on Langly Road, and the setting for the confrontation between [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] and the [[comrades-of-the-red-hand|Comrades of the Red Hand]] in Chapter XXIV.

## Description

Dover is a real English Channel port in Kent. In the novel, the plot-relevant location is the house called [[hurstmere|Hurstmere]] on Langly Road — "the last house in Langly Road. It stood well back, enclosed in its own grounds, which were ragged and overgrown. The place, Anthony judged, must have been empty for many years. A large iron gate swung rustily on its hinges, and the name on the gatepost was half obliterated" (5909-PN). Anthony describes it as "a lonely spot, and a good one to choose" for a secret headquarters (5909-PN).

## Role in the Novel

Dover serves as the secret base of operations for [[king-victor|King Victor]] and his co-conspirators. The address "Hurstmere, Langly Road, Dover" is the critical clue that [[monsieur-lemoine|M. Lemoine]] drops to test [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]]'s loyalties (6167-PN). After [[monsieur-lemoine|Lemoine]] writes the address on his shirt cuff and Anthony sees it, [[monsieur-lemoine|Lemoine]] "accidentally" drops a paper bearing the same address to see whether [[boris-anchoukoff|Boris Anchoukoff]] — whom he suspects of being a [[red-hand|Red Hand]] emissary — will retrieve it for his master (6167-PN). The house functions as [[king-victor|King Victor]]'s English headquarters until it is precipitately abandoned on the afternoon of Anthony's departure from [[chimneys|Chimneys]] (6167-PN).

## Key Events

- **[[monsieur-lemoine|M. Lemoine]] traces [[king-victor|King Victor]] to Dover** — [[monsieur-lemoine|Lemoine]]'s investigations lead him to the house at Hurstmere, Langly Road, where a body of foreigners was stationed until the afternoon of [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]]'s departure from [[chimneys|Chimneys]] (6169-PN)
- **Address written on shirt cuff** — [[monsieur-lemoine|Lemoine]] pencils "Hurstmere, Langly Road, Dover" on his shirt cuff, which Anthony observes and later uses to travel there (5799-PN)
- **[[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]]'s journey to Dover** — Anthony takes a train from [[king-victor|Victor]]ia Station to Dover after leaving [[lady-eileen-bundle-brent|Bundle]] at Hyde Park Corner, arriving to find the house at Langly Road guarded by armed sentries (6007-PN, 6011-PN)
- **Infiltration of the house** — Anthony climbs a [[virginia-revel|Virginia]] creeper to reach a window, enters a room where a bound prisoner lies, and is surprised by [[hiram-fish|Hiram P. Fish]], who had followed him on the same train from [[king-victor|Victor]]ia (5909-PN)
