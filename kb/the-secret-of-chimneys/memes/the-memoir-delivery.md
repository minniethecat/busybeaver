# The Memoir Delivery

[[james-mcgrath|Jimmy McGrath]] delivers [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]]'s memoirs to [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] at the author's lodgings in [[bulawayo|Bulawayo]], South Africa, setting in motion the chain of events that drives the entire plot.

## Summary

[[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]], working as a tour guide in [[bulawayo|Bulawayo]], encounters his old friend [[james-mcgrath|Jimmy McGrath]] one evening. [[james-mcgrath|McGrath]] explains that he once rescued [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]] from a perilous situation, and as gratitude, the dying Count entrusted him with a parcel containing his memoirs. [[james-mcgrath|McGrath]] now needs someone to deliver [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the manuscript]] to a [[london|London]] publishing firm (009-056).

The memoirs concern the political affairs of [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]] and contain information of great sensitivity. [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]] warns that certain parties would go to great lengths to prevent their publication (001-108). [[james-mcgrath|McGrath]] asks Anthony to take [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the manuscript]] to [[london|London]] on his behalf, along with a packet of letters belonging to a man named [[giuseppe-manelli|Giuseppe]].

Anthony accepts the commission, which brings him back to [[england|England]] and into the heart of the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n conspiracy.

## The Manuscript

The parcel contained [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]]'s memoirs — or reminiscences — documenting his decades of influence over [[balkans|Near East]]ern politics. Stylptitch was the éminence grise behind the throne, a master of international intrigue (001-106).

Unknown to Anthony at the time of delivery, [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the memoirs]] also contained a coded message revealing the location of a hidden fortune — the missing Koh-i-Noor diamond — which would make them the target of revolutionary and royalist factions alike.

## Key Participants

- [[james-mcgrath]] — delivers [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the memoirs]] to Anthony in [[bulawayo|Bulawayo]]
- [[anthony-cade]] — accepts the commission to deliver [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the memoirs]] to [[london|London]]

## Significance

The delivery of [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the memoirs]] is the inciting event of the novel. Without this transaction, [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] would never have returned to [[england|England]], become entangled in the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n affair, or met [[virginia-revel|Virginia Revel]]. The memoirs themselves — and the coded secret they contain — are the MacGuffin that every faction pursues throughout the story.
