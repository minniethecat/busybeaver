# Anthony Cade's Return to England

The novel's inciting incident — [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]], an adventurer working as a tour guide in [[bulawayo|Bulawayo]], agrees to travel to [[england|England]] in place of his friend [[james-mcgrath|Jimmy McGrath]] to deliver [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]]'s memoirs and collect a thousand-pound legacy, unknowingly stepping into a web of Balkan political intrigue.

## Description

The arrangement is made over drinks in [[bulawayo|Bulawayo]], where [[james-mcgrath|Jimmy McGrath]] proposes that Anthony travel to [[england|England]] under his name, using a passage already booked on the *Granarth Castle*. "The job isn't in South America — it's in [[england|England]]," Jimmy tells Anthony. "Return of hero to his native land after many long years" (193-PN). Anthony agrees: "I won't leave [[england|England]] till I have" completed the mission (511-PN). He travels as [[james-mcgrath|James McGrath]], arrives at Southampton on the *Granarth Castle* two days earlier than expected (due to a miscommunication at the Union Castle office where [[bill-eversleigh|Bill Eversleigh]]'s inaudible pronunciation caused the clerk to look up the wrong ship), and takes the boat train to Waterloo, heading to [[blitz-hotel|the Blitz]] Hotel (875-PN).

## Role in the Novel

This event is the hinge on which the entire plot turns. By substituting for [[james-mcgrath|Jimmy McGrath]], Anthony becomes the unwitting courier of politically explosive documents ([[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]]'s memoirs), the recipient of the coded letters hidden in [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the manuscript]] package, and the prime suspect in both the theft of [[koh-i-noor-diamond|the K]]oh-i-Noor and the murder of Prince Michael. The name-swap — Anthony travelling as [[james-mcgrath|James McGrath]] — creates the identity confusion that allows [[george-lomax|George Lomax]]'s invitation to the wrong man, places Anthony at [[chimneys|Chimneys]], and makes him the focal point for every faction seeking [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the memoirs]] and [[koh-i-noor-diamond|the diamond]].

## Key Events

- **The deal in [[bulawayo|Bulawayo]]** — [[james-mcgrath|Jimmy McGrath]] proposes that Anthony take his place on the *Granarth Castle* to [[england|England]] (193-PN)
- **Anthony accepts** — "It's a deal all right. I'm on. And confusion to [[select-tours|Castle's Select Tours]]" (283-PN)
- **The Union Castle mix-up** — [[bill-eversleigh|Bill Eversleigh]]'s poor pronunciation causes the clerk to look up the *Carnfrae Castle* instead of the *Granarth Castle*, leading [[george-lomax|George Lomax]] to expect [[james-mcgrath|McGrath]] a day later than he actually arrives (871-PN)
- **Arrival at Southampton** — The *Granarth Castle* docks at Southampton on Wednesday afternoon, a day ahead of Lomax's intelligence (873-PN)
- **[[london|London]] arrival** — Anthony steps off the boat train at Waterloo, takes a taxi to [[blitz-hotel|the Blitz]] Hotel (875-PN)
- **The thousand-pound cheque** — Anthony delivers [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the manuscript]] to [[balderson-and-hodgkins|Balderson and Hodgkins]], receiving the legacy (1645-PN)
