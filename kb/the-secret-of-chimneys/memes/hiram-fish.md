# Hiram Fish

Hiram Fish was an American guest at the [[chimneys|Chimneys]] house party who initially presented himself as a harmless bibliophile and collector of first editions, but was later revealed to be an operative of the Pinkerton's Detective Agency tracking the international criminal known as [[king-victor|King Victor]].

## Arrival at Chimneys

Fish appeared at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] as one of the house party guests, described by [[lord-caterham|Lord Caterham]] in his account of who was present at the time of the murder: "Let me see, there was Mr. Hiram Fish" — to which [[lady-eileen-bundle-brent|Bundle]] responded: "Who is Mr. Hiram Fish?" [011-050]. His presence was initially unremarkable; he was simply another guest among the diplomatic and social assembly.

## Manner and Demeanor

Fish spoke in a distinctive American drawl, punctuated with dry remarks in "his own peculiar idiom" [017-046]. He had a habit of moving noiselessly — on one occasion, [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] "jumped when addressed suddenly by Mr. Hiram Fish, who had made his usual noiseless entry" [020-156]. His movements were subtle and observant; Anthony noticed "Mr. Hiram Fish prefers sitting in the shade" on a sunny afternoon [017-016], suggesting a man who maintained a low profile while keeping a careful watch on events.

## True Identity Revealed

Fish was not a mere book collector. In the climactic scenes of the investigation, "Mr. Hiram Fish who was standing in the doorway. He was smiling and in his hand was a big blue automatic" [025-023]. He had been tracking [[king-victor|King Victor]] — the master criminal who had assumed multiple identities throughout the novel — and was present at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] to apprehend him.

After the resolution, Fish delivered his own understated assessment of the week's events: "I guess that this has been a great little old week" [0[[TOC|31]]-016]. He had also independently deduced the Rose Garden clue, arriving at the sundial at the same time as [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] [029-008].
