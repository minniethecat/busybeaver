## Summary

During a late-night walk on the grounds of [[chimneys|Chimneys]], [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] hears a shot that seems to come from within the house. He initially dismisses it as a stray poacher in the woods, but the sound proves significant to the unfolding investigation [1053-1054].

## Context

Anthony, walking alone near the terrace, suddenly hears a shot. He "spun round quickly" and was certain the sound had come "from within the house" [1053-1054]. After waiting a minute without further sound, he tells himself he must have been mistaken—"perhaps mistaken a stray shot coming from a poacher in the woods"—and retraces his steps, "vaguely dissatisfied and uneasy" [1054-1054].

Later, when recounting the incident, Anthony explains his reasoning: the sound came from the right or behind him, whereas if it had been fired elsewhere in the house it would have sounded from the left. This is what made him think of poachers—"It's at the extremity of the wing, you see" [1388-1388].

[[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]] takes note of the incident, recognizing that it may connect to the broader crime under investigation. The "poacher shot" becomes one of several suspicious occurrences that surround the events at [[chimneys|Chimneys]].
