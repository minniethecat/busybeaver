## Description

The Jolly Cricketers, commonly referred to simply as "The Cricketers," is a local inn in the village near Chimneys. It serves as temporary lodging for visitors to the area. Lord Caterham describes it disparagingly as a "horrible place—to stay in, I mean" [2995-2995].

## Role in the Novel

The Cricketers plays a notable role as the lodging place for strangers who arrive in the vicinity of Chimneys. Dr. Cartwright notices a "nice-looking fellow" standing in the doorway—a stranger staying at the inn [2165-2165]. Inspector Johnson is instructed to "institute inquiries at the Cricketers" about any strangers, particularly noting that the gentleman was "very sunburnt" and likely "come from foreign parts" [2181-2181].

A young man arrives at the Jolly Cricketers at midnight in a car and engages a room, his boots "very wet and muddy, as though he had been walking through the long grass in the park" [2549-2549]. Later, the neat, dark-bearded stranger staying at the Cricketers is revealed to be a person of significance in the investigation [4447-4447].
