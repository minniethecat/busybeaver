## Role in the Novel

The Union Castle Line operates the passenger service between South Africa and Southampton, including the *Granarth Castle* on which Anthony Cade (as James McGrath) travels to England [871-871]. The company's London offices are the scene of a small but plot-critical misunderstanding: Bill Eversleigh's inaudible pronunciation of "Granarth" is misheard by the clerk as "Carnfrae," causing George Lomax to receive incorrect arrival information [871-871].

## The Miscommunication

Bill Eversleigh, dispatched by George Lomax to inquire about the *Granarth Castle*'s arrival, speaks in "a pleasant, but quite inaudible voice" that any elocution master would have faulted [871-871]. The Union Castle clerk records "Carnfrae Castle" instead of "Granarth Castle," and reports the wrong arrival date. This clerical error gives Anthony Cade a crucial one-day head start in London before anyone expects his arrival.

## Connection to the Plot

The Union Castle Line's reliable service ironically enables the plot's central confusion. The *Granarth Castle* docks at Southampton on Tuesday afternoon, but George Lomax, relying on the Union Castle clerk's erroneous information, expects James McGrath to arrive on Thursday [873-873]. By the time Lomax realizes his mistake, Anthony has already had a full day to establish himself in London.

> Being accustomed to send Bill on errands, George had dispatched him to the Union Castle offices to find out when the *Granarth Castle* was due in. [871-871]
