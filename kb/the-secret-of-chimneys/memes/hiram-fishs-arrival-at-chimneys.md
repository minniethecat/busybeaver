# Hiram Fish's Arrival at Chimneys

[[hiram-fish|Hiram P. Fish]], an American gentleman from New York City, arrives at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] as a guest of [[lord-caterham|Lord Caterham]], introducing himself as a visitor interested in art collections — later revealed to be an undercover operative with a stake in the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n investigation.

## Summary

[[hiram-fish|Mr. Hiram Fish]] is introduced as one of the house guests at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] (012-013). He presents himself as an affable American visitor with a keen interest in the estate's art collection. His manner is described as slow and drawling, delivering "dry remarks in his own peculiar idiom" (014-014). Throughout the weekend, he shows unusual interest in the crime scene and the movements of other guests. His true identity is not revealed until later, when it emerges that he is connected to the international investigation surrounding the [[herzoslovakian-oil-concession|Herzoslovakian oil]] concessions and the death of Prince Michael.
