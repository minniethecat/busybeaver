# Restoration of the Herzoslovakian Monarchy

The restoration of the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n monarchy is the declared political objective that brings diplomats, financiers, and claimants to [[chimneys|Chimneys]]. Following the revolution that overthrew and murdered [[nicholas-iv|King Nicholas IV]] and [[queen-varaga|Queen Varaga]], various factions compete to place a new Obolovitch on the throne—with the oil concession as the unspoken prize (014-057).

## Political Context

After the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n revolution, the country fell under republican control. However, as [[james-mcgrath|Jimmy McGrath]] observes, "I don't wonder at their getting tired of republican institutions" (002-042). The [[foreign-office|British Foreign Office]], through [[george-lomax|George Lomax]], actively promoted restoration, arguing that it was "imperative to the peace of Europe" (006-139)—though [[virginia-revel|Virginia Revel]] correctly identifies this as a pretext for protecting British oil interests.

## The Claimants

Two Obolovitch princes survive the revolution. [[prince-michael-obolovitch|Prince Michael Obolovitch]], the elder, is secretly murdered at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] (022-023). [[prince-nicholas-obolovitch|Prince Nicholas Obolovitch]], thought lost, is in fact [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]], who had been living under an assumed identity: "I especially disbelieved in kings and princes" (030-009). Upon Michael's death, Nicholas becomes the last viable claimant. [[herman-isaacstein|Isaacstein]] promptly switches his financial backing: "Are you prepared to offer the same loan on the same terms to his cousin Nicholas?" (029-023).

## The Resolution

Anthony accepts his identity and the throne, motivated by both pragmatism and love: "I'd do more than be a king for her sake" (030-017). The novel ends with Anthony and [[virginia-revel|Virginia]] planning to depart for [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]: "We'll go to [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]] and pretend to be kings and queens" (030-066). [[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]] wryly approves: "I'm sure you'll make a very fine king out there" (030-012).
