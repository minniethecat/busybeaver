## Overview

Bill Eversleigh is the private secretary to [[george-lomax|George Lomax]] at the [[foreign-office|Foreign Office]]. Though his position suggests importance, Bill is in truth little more than an errand boy — he obtained his post through family connections rather than intellectual merit. A pleasant, good-looking young man of about twenty-five, Bill provides comic relief throughout the novel while serving as a loyal, if not particularly effective, assistant to his demanding employer.

## Character Description

Bill Eversleigh is about twenty-five years old, described as "a very likeable young man" with "a pleasurably ugly face, a splendid set of white teeth and a pair of honest brown eyes" [004-006]. He is big and rather ungainly in his movements, a good cricketer and a scratch golfer [004-016].

His position at the [[foreign-office|Foreign Office]] was gained "not by brains, but by good connections" [004-016]. He does no responsible or brainy work; his part is to be constantly at [[george-lomax|George Lomax]]'s elbow, to interview unimportant people whom George doesn't want to see, to run errands, and generally to make himself useful. When George is absent, Bill stretches himself out in the biggest chair and reads the sporting news — "merely carrying out a time-honoured tradition" [004-016].

> His position in the Foreign Office had been gained, not by brains, but by good connections. For the work he had to do he was quite suitable. He was more or less George's dog. [004-016]

## Role in the Plot

Bill serves several functions in the narrative. He provides comic relief through his well-meaning incompetence — most notably when he mispronounces "Granarth" as "Carnfrae" at the Union Castle offices, leading [[george-lomax|George Lomax]] to believe [[james-mcgrath|James McGrath]] would arrive on Thursday rather than Tuesday [005-021]. This clerical error complicates the [[foreign-office|Foreign Office]]'s plans and adds to the confusion of mistaken identities.

During the weekend at [[chimneys|Chimneys]], Bill stares "longingly at [[virginia-revel|Virginia]]" during lunch, nursing an obvious romantic crush [014-088]. He later assists in the amateur investigation of Prince Michael's murder, helping [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] and [[lady-eileen-bundle-brent|Bundle]] Brent conceal themselves in the [[london|Council Chamber]] to observe the comings and goings of the suspects [021-055].

> One bright spot in the day for Bill Eversleigh. George Lomax, obsessed with the fear that something damaging might leak out, had been exceedingly trying. [024-047]

## Personality

Bill is good-natured, loyal, and entirely lacking in ambition. He accepts his subordinate role without resentment and performs his limited duties faithfully. His simple, honest nature makes him a trustworthy ally, even if his capabilities are modest. In the [[foreign-office|Foreign Office]], [[miss-oscar|Miss Oscar]] does all the "useful and interesting" work, while Bill's part is to deliver messages and decode telegrams [024-047].

## Relationship to Other Characters

- **[[george-lomax|George Lomax]]**: Bill's employer, whom he serves with dog-like loyalty despite Lomax's demanding nature [004-016]
- **[[virginia-revel|Virginia Revel]]**: The object of Bill's romantic interest; he stares at her longingly and is visibly affected by her presence [014-088]
- **[[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]]**: Bill assists Anthony in the night-time stakeout of the [[london|Council Chamber]] [021-055]
- **[[lady-eileen-bundle-brent|Bundle]] Brent**: [[lady-eileen-bundle-brent|Lady Eileen "Bundle" Brent]], who treats Bill with friendly condescension [014-081]

## Significance

Bill Eversleigh represents the type of young upper-class Englishman who populated the lower ranks of government service in the early twentieth century — well-connected, good-natured, and completely unburdened by intellectual ambition. His presence in the novel provides a contrast to the more capable and driven characters like [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] and [[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]], while his romantic interest in [[virginia-revel|Virginia Revel]] adds a light touch to the otherwise dark political intrigue.
