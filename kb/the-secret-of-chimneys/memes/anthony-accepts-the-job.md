## Overview

The novel opens in [[bulawayo|Bulawayo]], South Africa, where [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] — working as a tour guide for Castle's Select Tour — encounters his old friend [[james-mcgrath|Jimmy McGrath]]. [[james-mcgrath|McGrath]] offers Anthony a peculiar job: deliver the coded memoirs of [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]] to a [[london|London]] publisher for a fee of £1000. This chance meeting launches the entire chain of events that culminates in the unmasking of [[king-victor|King Victor]] at [[chimneys|Chimneys]].

## The Meeting

[[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] was leading a tour of seven "depressed-looking females and three perspiring males" through [[bulawayo|Bulawayo]] when [[james-mcgrath|Jimmy McGrath]] spotted him. The two old friends stepped aside to talk privately.

> "Gentleman Joe!" "Why, if it isn't old Jimmy McGrath." [001-003]

[[james-mcgrath|McGrath]], characteristically blunt, demanded to know what Anthony was doing with a tour group, joking that he might be starting a harem. Anthony's dry response — "Not with this little lot" — established the tone of their easy comradeship.

## The Offer

[[james-mcgrath|McGrath]] explained that he had come into possession of [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]]'s memoirs, dictated to him during a period when [[james-mcgrath|McGrath]] had saved the Count's life in Africa. The memoirs were written in a coded shorthand that only a [[london|London]] publisher understood. [[james-mcgrath|McGrath]] needed someone to deliver [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the manuscript]] to [[england|England]], collect the £1000 publication fee, and forward the money to him. Anthony, perpetually short of funds and adventures, accepted.

The memoirs were more than a literary curiosity — they contained explosive political secrets about the [[restoration-of-the-herzoslovakian-monarchy|Herzoslovakian succession]] that would attract the attention of rival factions, revolutionaries, and intelligence services.

## Narrative Significance

This event is the inciting incident of the entire novel. Without this chance encounter in [[bulawayo|Bulawayo]], the coded memoirs would never have reached [[england|England]], Prince Michael's murder might never have been solved, and [[king-victor|King Victor]] might never have been unmasked. The meeting also establishes Anthony's character — a gentleman adventurer, competent and resourceful, with a network of dubious but loyal friends.
