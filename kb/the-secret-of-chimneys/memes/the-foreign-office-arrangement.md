# The Foreign Office Arrangement

[[george-lomax|George Lomax]] of the [[foreign-office|Foreign Office]] corners [[lord-caterham|Lord Caterham]] at their [[london|London]] club and persuades him — through a combination of earnest pleas and physical immobility — to host a secret diplomatic conference at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] under the guise of a weekend shooting party (003-003).

## Summary

[[george-lomax|George Lomax]], a senior [[foreign-office|Foreign Office]] official, approaches [[lord-caterham|Lord Caterham]] at their club with an urgent request. Using a firm grip on [[lord-caterham|Lord Caterham]]'s lapel — "doubtless the result of long experience" in preventing interlocutors from escaping serious conversations — Lomax explains that [[prince-michael-obolovitch|Prince Michael Obolovitch]] is arriving and that the entire [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n question can be settled "at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] under the guise of a shooting party" (003-003). The arrangement is designed to appear as an ordinary country-house weekend while actually serving as a high-stakes diplomatic negotiation over the throne of [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]] and the oil concessions that hang in the balance. [[lord-caterham|Lord Caterham]], who prefers peace and quiet above all, is reluctantly drawn into the affair. [[bill-eversleigh|Bill Eversleigh]], Lomax's assistant at the [[foreign-office|Foreign Office]], helps manage the logistics, though his position was "gained, not by brains, but by good connections" (005-005).
