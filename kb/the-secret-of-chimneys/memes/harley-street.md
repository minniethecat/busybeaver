## Role in the Novel

Harley Street appears in the novel primarily through its medical associations. When Anthony Cade searches for Virginia Revel's identity in the telephone directory, he finds Edward Henry Revel, a surgeon of Harley Street, among the possible connections [1029-1029]. Later, Lord Caterham references Sir Abner Willis, whose consulting room is in Harley Street, as the physician who advised him to "avoid all worry" [2299-2299].

## Associations

The street represents London's medical establishment. Edward Henry Revel, surgeon, is listed among six Revels in the telephone directory that Anthony consults while investigating Virginia's background. The presence of a Revel at Harley Street suggests the family's professional standing and adds a layer to the novel's exploration of English social class.

## Lord Caterham's Physician

Sir Abner Willis, whose Harley Street consulting room is referenced, serves as Lord Caterham's personal physician. Caterham frequently cites Willis's advice to avoid excitement and worry—a recommendation Caterham finds impossible to follow given George Lomax's persistent impositions [2299-2299].

> On an impulse, he picked up the telephone book and looked up the name of Revel. There were six Revels in the book: Edward Henry Revel, surgeon, of Harley Street... [1029-1029]

> "Avoid all worry, that's what Sir Abner Willis said—avoid all worry. So easy for a man sitting in his consulting-room in Harley Street to say that." [2299-2299]
