# Balderson and Hodgkins

[[london|London]] firm of publishers entrusted with [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]]'s memoirs — the commercial endpoint of [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]]'s mission to [[england|England]], where he delivers [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the manuscript]] and collects the thousand-pound legacy that launches the novel's action.

## Description

Balderson and Hodgkins is a [[london|London]] publishing house. [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] contacts them upon arriving in [[england|England]], speaking first to the switchboard operator ("This is Messrs. Balderson and Hodgkins. Just a minute, please. I will put you through to Mr. Balderson") and then to Mr. Balderson himself, who asks urgently "What about that manuscript, Mr. [[james-mcgrath|McGrath]]?" (1597-PN, 1607-PN). The firm's eagerness — "Our worthy publishers, thought Anthony. So they are getting worried too, are they?" (1599-PN) — reflects the political sensitivity of [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the memoirs]]. The following morning, a representative named Mr. Holmes collects [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the manuscript]] and delivers a cheque for one thousand pounds, a transaction described as "very tame" by Anthony (1645-PN).

## Role in the Novel

Balderson and Hodgkins serve as the commercial mechanism that brings [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] to [[england|England]] and connects him to the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n conspiracy. Their role is seemingly mundane — publishers holding a manuscript — but the firm becomes an unwitting node in the intelligence network: the man who collects [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the manuscript]], "Mr. Holmes," is later revealed to be [[prince-michael-obolovitch|Prince Michael Obolovitch]] in disguise. Anthony recognizes him at [[chimneys|Chimneys]]: "I've seen him before... But not as [[prince-michael-obolovitch|Prince Michael Obolovitch]]. He purported to come from Messrs. Balderson and Hodgkins, and he called himself Mr. Holmes" (2867-PN). This revelation transforms the publishing house from a minor detail into a point of convergence for the novel's intersecting plot lines.

- **The manuscript handover** — Mr. Holmes collects [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the manuscript]] and delivers a one-thousand-pound cheque; Anthony finds the transaction anticlimactic (1645-PN)
- **Prince Michael's imposture revealed** — Anthony realizes "Mr. Holmes from Balderson and Hodgkins" was [[prince-michael-obolovitch|Prince Michael Obolovitch]] in disguise (2867-PN)
