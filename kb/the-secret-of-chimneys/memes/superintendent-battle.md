# Superintendent Battle

Superintendent Battle of [[scotland-yard]] is the investigating detective called to [[chimneys]] following the murder of [[prince-michael-obolovitch]]. His most striking characteristic is his imperturbable calm: a squarely built, middle-aged man "with a face so singularly devoid of expression as to be quite remarkable" (011-PN).

## Role in the Novel

As the official detective, Battle represents the methodical, professional arm of the law. Unlike the brilliant amateur detectives of other Christie novels, Battle is a working policeman — unflappable, shrewd, and content to let events unfold while he quietly assembles the facts. He serves as a foil to both the pompous [[george-lomax]] and the intuitive, adventurous [[anthony-cade]].

## Character Profile

### Appearance and Demeanor

Battle is described as "a squarely built middle-aged man with a face so singularly devoid of expression as to be quite remarkable" (011-PN). His eyes "opened just a trifle" when he learns the victim's true identity, but "otherwise he gave no sign" — a demonstration of the impassive professionalism that defines his working method (011-PN).

### Professional Method

Battle's investigative style is characterized by patience, routine, and an unwillingness to jump to conclusions. When confronted with seemingly damning evidence against [[anthony-cade]], he refuses to leap: "I just say it's curious, that's all. Here's a young man who ought to cut and run, but he doesn't cut and run. He stays here, and gives us every facility for comparing footmarks" (011-PN). He openly admits uncertainty: "I don't know what to think. And that's a very disturbing state of mind" (011-PN).

### Attitude to Authority

Battle's dealing with [[george-lomax]] reveals a subtle independence. When Lomax insists on "the utmost discretion," Battle nods "in an offhand manner that rather took [[lord-caterham]]'s fancy" (011-PN). He insists on full disclosure from the authorities: "No concealments from us" (011-PN). He refuses to be dazzled by rank or political significance.

## Key Actions

### Arrival at Chimneys

Battle arrives half an hour before his first appearance in the narrative, having already been round the scene with [[inspector-badgworthy|Inspector Badgworthy]] and spoken with Dr. Cartwright. His method is thorough and systematic — he gathers facts before drawing conclusions.

### The Interrogation

When [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] presents himself voluntarily, Battle questions him methodically, refusing to be hurried by Lomax's eagerness to make an arrest. He recognizes the unlikelihood of a guilty man calmly walking into police hands.

### Managing the Investigation

Throughout the investigation, Battle manages the delicate political dynamics of [[chimneys]]: he handles Lomax's bluster, [[colonel-melrose|Colonel Melrose]]'s professionalism, and [[lord-caterham|Lord Caterham]]'s detachment with equal equanimity. He recognizes that the case involves more than a simple murder — the [[herzoslovakia]] political situation, the [[theft-of-the-koh-i-noor]], and [[count-stylptitch]]'s memoirs all intertwine.

### The Final Revelation

Battle's patience pays off as the various threads — the [[red-hand]], the [[king-victor]] connection, the [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|coded manuscript]], and the diamond — converge. He plays a key role in the novel's denouement, revealing the true identity of the murderer.

## Relationships

- **[[anthony-cade]]** — Battle and Anthony develop a mutual professional respect; Battle recognizes Anthony's intelligence and Anthony respects Battle's methodical competence
- **[[george-lomax]]** — Battle's quiet competence contrasts with Lomax's bluster and political maneuvering
- **[[lord-caterham]]** — The two share a moment of mutual appreciation; [[lord-caterham|Lord Caterham]] finds Battle's offhand manner refreshing
- **[[monsieur-lemoine]]** — The French detective with whom Battle collaborates on the international dimensions of the case
