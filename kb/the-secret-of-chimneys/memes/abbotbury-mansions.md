# Abbotbury Mansions

Abbotbury Mansions is a [[london|London]] mansion block referenced in the novel in connection with [[george-lomax|George Lomax]]'s Foreign Office business. Like [[cadogan-square|Cadogan Square]] — Lomax's own residence — Abbotbury Mansions forms part of the bureaucratic landscape of [[london|London]] against which the novel's political intrigue is set.

## Narrative Function

The mention of Abbotbury Mansions contributes to the novel's detailed mapping of [[london|London]]'s social geography — from the grandeur of [[cadogan-square|Cadogan Square]] to the fashionable [[blitz-hotel|Blitz Hotel]] to the mansion blocks and squares where the machinery of British diplomacy operates. This specificity of place anchors the novel's international intrigue in the recognizable physical world of 1920s London.
