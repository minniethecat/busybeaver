# The Oil Concession Negotiations

The competition for [[herzoslovakian-oil-concession|Herzoslovakian oil]] concessions drives the political intrigue at [[chimneys|Chimneys]], with British financial interests backing Prince Michael's claim to the throne and American interests supporting rival claimants.

## Summary

The discovery of oil in [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]] transforms the small Balkan country into a site of international competition (003-003). [[prince-michael-obolovitch|Prince Michael Obolovitch]], seeking to reclaim the throne, pledges to grant oil concessions to companies in which [[herman-isaacstein|Herman Isaacstein]] is interested in return for financial support (013-013). The British Government, through [[george-lomax|George Lomax]], supports Prince Michael's claim partly because of his "pronounced British sympathies" and the strategic importance of securing the concessions for British companies (013-013). American financiers back rival claimant [[prince-nicholas-obolovitch|Prince Nicholas]], creating a two-sided contest that plays out during the house party at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] (015-015). [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] observes that "Michael was backed by [[england|England]], Nicholas by America" and that in both countries "a group of financiers are anxious to obtain the oil concessions" (015-015).
