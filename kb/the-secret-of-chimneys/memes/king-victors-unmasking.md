# King Victor's Unmasking

The climactic confrontation in [[council-chamber|the Council Chamber]] at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] in which the celebrated international [[king-victor|jewel thief]] [[king-victor|King Victor]] — a master of disguise who has operated for years under aliases across Europe and America — is finally exposed before the assembled house party, his carefully constructed persona collapsing under the weight of [[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]]'s patient investigation.

## Description

Throughout the novel, [[king-victor|King Victor]] is a spectral presence — "Irish father, French mother. Can speak five languages at least" (4241-PN) — the criminal genius behind the theft of [[koh-i-noor-diamond|the K]]oh-i-Noor diamond, the coded letters, and the infiltration of [[chimneys|Chimneys]]. [[monsieur-lemoine|M. Lemoine]] of the [[surete|Sûreté]] describes him as "one of the most celebrated jewel thieves in the world" (4554-PN), known for audacious schemes and flawless disguises. The unmasking occurs when [[superintendent-battle|Battle]] orchestrates a final gathering in [[council-chamber|the Council Chamber]], where the accumulated evidence — the coded letters, the [[dover|Dover]] headquarters, the secret passage, the [[virginia-revel|Virginia]] creeper — is laid out. The person revealed as [[king-victor|King Victor]] is not the obvious suspect ([[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]], whom [[monsieur-lemoine|Lemoine]] has accused) but someone hiding in plain sight within the house party itself.

The revelation is dramatic and complete: "[[king-victor|King Victor]] has never been known to take a life" (3221-PN), [[superintendent-battle|Battle]] notes — a key clue that separates the thief from the murderer and ultimately identifies both. The unmasking ties together every thread of the plot: the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n revolution, [[koh-i-noor-diamond|the stolen jewel]], the blackmail, and the murder of Prince Michael.

## Role in the Novel

The unmasking is the novel's detective-story resolution — the moment when the puzzle-box of identities, aliases, and secret agendas is unlocked. It completes the arc that began with Anthony's conversation in [[bulawayo|Bulawayo]] about "the most notorious [[king-victor|jewel thief]] in the world" (396-PN). The scene also serves as the counterpoint to the murder revelation: [[king-victor|King Victor]] is exposed as the thief, while a different party is revealed as the killer.

## Key Events

- **[[superintendent-battle|Battle]] assembles the house party** — All guests are summoned to [[council-chamber|the Council Chamber]] for the final revelation
- **[[monsieur-lemoine|Lemoine]] narrates [[king-victor|King Victor]]'s history** — The French detective recounts the criminal's career from [[paris|Paris]] to [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]] (4554-PN)
- **The evidence is presented** — The coded letters, the secret passage discoveries, and the [[dover|Dover]] expedition findings are catalogued
- **The identity is revealed** — [[king-victor|King Victor]] is exposed before the assembled company
- **The thief and murderer are distinguished** — [[superintendent-battle|Battle]] clarifies that [[king-victor|King Victor]] came for the jewel, not for blood
