# London

London is the novel's opening urban setting, where the novel's political intrigue is set in motion at an exclusive club, where [[anthony-cade]] first delivers [[count-stylptitch-memoirs|the memoirs]], and where [[virginia-revel]] confronts the blackmailer from [[king-victor]]'s gang. It provides the contrast to the enclosed world of [[chimneys]].

## Key Locations

### The Club

The novel opens on the steps of an exclusive London club where [[george-lomax]] buttonholes [[lord-caterham]], pressing him into hosting the diplomatic house party at [[chimneys]]. The club represents the world of political power that [[lord-caterham|Lord Caterham]] so desperately wishes to escape: "He disliked very much being forced to stand on the steps of the exclusive London club to which he belonged and listen to the interminable eloquence of the [[george-lomax|Hon. George Lomax]]" (003-PN).

### 487 Pont Street

[[virginia-revel]]'s London residence at [[pont-street|487 Pont Street]] is a crucial setting for two major plot threads. It is here that the blackmailer from [[king-victor]]'s gang first approaches her, mistakenly believing her to be in possession of compromising letters. The house is also where [[anthony-cade]] first calls on her, and where the dead body — the blackmailer [[giuseppe-manelli|Giuseppe]] — is temporarily hidden in the trunk of Anthony's car. The address is described as a smart London townhouse with a "grave functionary" — the butler [[chilvers|Chilvers]] — and serves as a refuge from which [[virginia-revel|Virginia]] orchestrates her involvement in the [[chimneys]] affair.

### The Wallace Collection

Referenced in conversation between [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] and [[james-mcgrath]] (002-PN), the Wallace Collection is a real London museum. Anthony jokingly conflates it with the [[koh-i-noor-diamond]] diamond, asking whether [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]] knew which city the Wallace Collection was in — a reference to the geopolitical confusion surrounding the [[theft-of-the-koh-i-noor|stolen diamond]].

### The Jolly Cricketers Inn

Located near [[chimneys]] but outside London proper, the Jolly Cricketers is the inn where [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] stays after disposing of the blackmailer's body. It is here that [[superintendent-battle]]'s investigation first connects Anthony to the crime scene: "At twelve o'clock a young man arrived at the Jolly Cricketers in a car, and engaged a room. He put his boots outside to be cleaned — they were very wet and muddy, as though he had been walking through the long grass in the park" (011-PN).

### The Council Chamber at Chimneys

Though located at [[chimneys]], [[council-chamber|the Council Chamber]] is where much of the official investigation and diplomatic maneuvering occurs, effectively extending London's political sphere into the country house. It serves as the setting for interrogations by [[superintendent-battle]] and conferences between [[george-lomax]], [[lord-caterham]], and [[herman-isaacstein]].

## Plot Function

London in the novel serves as the staging ground from which the central intrigue unfolds:

1. **Political Origin**: The [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n restoration plan is initiated by Lomax at the London club
2. **The Memoirs Delivery**: [[anthony-cade]] comes to London to deliver [[count-stylptitch]]'s memoirs, collected from [[james-mcgrath]] in [[bulawayo|Bulawayo]]
3. **The Blackmail Plot**: [[virginia-revel]]'s [[pont-street|Pont Street]] house is the scene of the blackmail attempt that draws [[king-victor]]'s gang into the story
4. **The Investigation Hub**: Between events, characters shuttle between London and [[chimneys|Chimneys]], with London serving as the connection to the wider world — police, [[foreign-office|Foreign Office]], embassies
