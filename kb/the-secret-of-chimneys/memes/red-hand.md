# Red Hand

The Red Hand ([[comrades-of-the-red-hand|Comrades of the Red Hand]]) is a revolutionary republican organization operating in [[herzoslovakia]], committed to preventing the restoration of the Obolovitch monarchy. They are the prime suspects in the murder of [[prince-michael-obolovitch]] at [[chimneys]], though their role proves more complex than it first appears.

## Organization Type

The Red Hand is a secret revolutionary society, one of the Balkan-style conspiratorial groups that flourished in the turbulent politics of the early twentieth-century [[balkans|Near East]]. Their name — and the scarlet hand emblem they leave as a calling card — evokes the violent traditions of Balkan revolutionary nationalism.

## Political Allegiance

The organization is staunchly republican, formed to oppose the royalist restoration of the [[obolovitch-dynasty]]. Their political program envisions the permanent abolition of the monarchy in [[herzoslovakia]] and the continuation — or intensification — of republican government. This puts them in direct opposition to the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n [[herzoslovakian-royalists|Loyalist Party]] represented in [[london|London]] by [[baron-lolopretjzyl]], and to the British government's plan to install [[prince-michael-obolovitch]] on the throne.

## In the Novel

### The Murder at Chimneys

When [[prince-michael-obolovitch]] is found murdered in his room at [[chimneys]], a paper bearing the scarlet hand emblem is discovered at the scene. This immediately suggests the Red Hand as the perpetrators — the political assassination of a would-be monarch is precisely their modus operandi.

### Battle's Skepticism

[[superintendent-battle]], however, is not convinced. When [[george-lomax]] immediately blames the [[comrades-of-the-red-hand|Comrades of the Red Hand]], [[superintendent-battle|Battle]] waves him aside "with something less than his usual respect" and states bluntly: "It wasn't the [[comrades-of-the-red-hand|Comrades of the Red Hand]], sir, if you're thinking of them" (014-PN). He considers the paper with the scarlet hand a deliberate misdirection — "put there to suggest the obvious solution" (014-PN).

### The True Perpetrators

[[superintendent-battle|Battle]]'s instincts prove correct. The murder was not the work of the Red Hand but of other parties with their own motives. The red hand emblem was planted to divert suspicion toward the obvious political enemies of the prince, concealing a more personal and financially motivated crime.

## Historical Context

The Red Hand fits into the tradition of Balkan secret societies — groups like the Black Hand that were involved in the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand in 1914. Christie draws on this historical reality to give the fictional [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]] a sense of authentic Balkan political turbulence. The name itself may be a nod to the Ulster Red Hand, a symbol used by various organizations in Irish and broader European contexts.
