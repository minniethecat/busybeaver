# Count Stylptitch's Political Legacy

[[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]] was the prime minister of [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]] and, by [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]]'s estimation, the single most influential figure in [[balkans|Near East]]ern politics for two decades. "Every move and counter move in the [[balkans|Near East]] for the last twenty years has had [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]] at the bottom of it" (001-106). His death in [[paris|Paris]] sets the entire plot in motion.

## A Statesman of Balkan Politics

Stylptitch's reputation transcends national boundaries. As Anthony observes, "The Grand Old Man of the [[balkans|Balkans]]. The Greatest Statesman of Them All" (001-106). After the assassination of [[nicholas-iv|King Nicholas IV]] of [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]], Stylptitch went to live in [[paris|Paris]], where he continued to exert influence behind the scenes. "Very deep, the late [[count-stylptitch|Count Stylptitch]]" (001-118). He was remembered with admiration by the aristocracy: "They're very aristocratic and reactionary in [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]" (001-118).

## The Memoirs

Stylptitch's final act was writing his memoirs, which contain politically explosive material. [[lord-caterham|Lord Caterham]] explains: "Some of Stylptitch's reminiscences may upset the apple cart. Appalling to think of—at this difficult time in European politics" (003-041). The memoirs are described as including material that was "all vague but interesting" (002-054), enough to make the British Government and a revolutionary organization both desperate to obtain them.

## Connection to the Plot

Stylptitch's legacy connects every major character. He entrusted his memoirs to [[james-mcgrath|Jimmy McGrath]] before his death (001-114), tasking him to deliver them to a [[london|London]] publisher. This simple act draws [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]], [[virginia-revel|Virginia Revel]], [[king-victor|King Victor]], [[george-lomax|George Lomax]], and the [[comrades-of-the-red-hand|Comrades of the Red Hand]] into a deadly pursuit. The memoirs contain not only political secrets but also, encoded within, the whereabouts of [[koh-i-noor-diamond|the K]]oh-i-Noor diamond.

## Historical Context

Stylptitch was a contemporary of key European diplomatic events. [[lord-caterham|Lord Caterham]] notes: "Stylptitch was there at the time" (003-052) of certain sensitive political negotiations. His presence at the center of Balkan affairs made his reminiscences invaluable—and dangerous.
