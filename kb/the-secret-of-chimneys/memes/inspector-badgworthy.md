## Overview

Inspector Badgworthy is the police inspector stationed at Market Basing, the nearest town to [[chimneys|Chimneys]]. A tall, portly man with "a heavy regulation tread" who is "inclined to breathe hard in moments of professional strain," Badgworthy is the first police official notified of [[prince-michael-obolovitch|Prince Michael Obolovitch]]'s murder [010-001]. He initially relishes the prospect of a high-profile case, but his hopes of glory are soon dashed when [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] inevitably intervenes.

## Character Profile

### Appearance and Manner

Inspector Badgworthy is "a tall portly man" with "a heavy regulation tread" who tends to breathe hard under pressure [010-001]. He carries himself with what the narrator describes as his "usual portentous gravity of action" [010-003]. His subordinate, Constable Johnson, is "very new to the Force, with a downy unfledged look about him, like a human chicken" [010-001], emphasizing Badgworthy's relative seniority within the local constabulary.

Badgworthy's manner shifts notably when he realizes he is speaking to a peer of the realm — [[lord-caterham|Lord Caterham]], reporting the murder. "As he is greater than Johnson, so others are greater than Inspector Badgworthy" [010-005], and the inspector's tone becomes deferential.

## Role in the Plot

Badgworthy receives the telephone call from [[lord-caterham|Lord Caterham]] at 8:30 a.m. on the morning the body is discovered [010-001]. He immediately grasps the significance: "A murder! At [[chimneys|Chimneys]]! Inspector Badgworthy in charge of the case. The police have a clue. Sensational arrest. Promotion and kudos for the aforementioned inspector" [010-010].

His excitement is tempered by a realistic fear: "That is, if [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] doesn't come butting in" [010-011]. This fear proves well-founded — [[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]] arrives within half an hour, and Badgworthy is relegated to a supporting role, showing [[superintendent-battle|Battle]] around the scene and providing local knowledge [011-036].

> Badgworthy was in a seventh heaven. A murder! At Chimneys! Inspector Badgworthy in charge of the case. The police have a clue. Sensational arrest. Promotion and kudos for the aforementioned inspector. [010-010]

Badgworthy's subsequent appearances are peripheral. He accompanies [[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]] on the initial inspection of the scene [011-036], and by the time of the second midnight incident, [[lord-caterham|Lord Caterham]] notes that Badgworthy cannot be reached at that hour: "I can't get hold of Badgworthy at this time of night" [018-008].

## Relationship to Other Characters

- **[[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]]**: The [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] detective who supersedes Badgworthy's investigation — Badgworthy's resignation to this fact highlights the jurisdictional hierarchy [010-011]
- **[[lord-caterham|Lord Caterham]]**: Summons Badgworthy as the local police authority [010-002]
- **Constable Johnson**: His inexperienced subordinate [010-001]
- **Dr. Cartwright**: The local doctor who examines the body alongside Badgworthy [010-009]
- **[[colonel-melrose|Colonel Melrose]]**: The Chief Constable who outranks Badgworthy in the county police hierarchy

## Significance

Inspector Badgworthy functions as a minor comic figure whose professional ambition is comically thwarted by the arrival of higher authorities. His brief moment of glory — imagining himself leading a sensational murder investigation — is undercut by the inevitable involvement of [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]]. His character exemplifies the novel's gentle satirical treatment of minor officialdom, similar to its portrayal of other authority figures like [[george-lomax|George Lomax]].

His name and physical description — "a tall portly man" with "a heavy regulation tread" — evoke the stock figure of the country policeman in Golden Age detective fiction, a type that Agatha Christie would revisit throughout her career.
