## Role in the Novel

Miss Taylor appears only in the novel's opening chapter, as part of a tourist group in Bulawayo, South Africa, guided by Anthony Cade [63-63]. She is described as "the youngest and most skittish of the party" and serves to establish Anthony's charming, roguish character before the main plot begins.

## Interaction with Anthony

Miss Taylor immediately engages Anthony in playful banter, questioning him about an approaching stranger (James McGrath) whom she describes as "a friend of yours" — a remark delivered with "the disarming scorn of a young girl" [67-67]. Anthony deflects her questions with characteristic humor, claiming the stranger is "one of the friends of my blameless youth" [67-67]. The exchange establishes Anthony's easygoing manner with women and his instinct for evasion.

## Narrative Function

Miss Taylor's brief appearance serves as the reader's introduction to Anthony Cade in his natural habitat — a colonial tourist guide charming his charges. Her flirtatious curiosity contrasts with the more sophisticated romantic dynamic that later develops between Anthony and Virginia Revel.

> Anthony rejoined his flock of sheep. Miss Taylor, the youngest and most skittish of the party, instantly attacked him. [63-63]

> "It was, Miss Taylor. One of the friends of my blameless youth." [67-67]
