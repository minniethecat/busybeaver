# Herzoslovakian Revolution

The Herzoslovakian Revolution was the popular uprising approximately seven years before the events of *[[the-secret-of-chimneys|The Secret of Chimneys]]* that overthrew the [[obolovitch-dynasty]], assassinated [[nicholas-iv|King Nicholas IV]] and [[queen-varaga|Queen Varaga]], and established the republic. Its political aftershocks — the royalist restoration movement, the republican [[red-hand]], the oil concessions — are the engine of the novel's plot.

## Background

### The Controversial Marriage

The revolution was precipitated by King [[nicholas-iv]]'s marriage to a [[paris|Paris]]ian music-hall artiste — "some little guttersnipe," as [[anthony-cade]] puts it — whom he passed off as the [[queen-varaga|Countess Popoffsky]], claiming she had Romanov blood (002-PN). Nicholas arranged for the marriage to be solemnized in the cathedral at Ekarest, the capital of [[herzoslovakia]], "with a couple of unwilling Archbishops to do the job" (002-PN). She was crowned as [[queen-varaga|Queen Varaga]].

### Popular Reaction

The [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n people proved unreceptive to the manufactured royalty. As [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] explains: "They're very aristocratic and reactionary in [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]. They like their kings and queens to be the genuine article" (002-PN). The mismatch between the dynasty's pretensions and the queen's actual origins triggered discontent, which Nicholas met with "the usual ruthless suppressions."

## The Uprising

The "final uprising" was decisive: the populace "stormed the palace, murdered the king and queen, and proclaimed a republic" (002-PN). The revolution was swift and violent — a complete break with the Obolovitch dynasty and a repudiation of [[nicholas-iv|Nicholas IV]]'s rule.

## Aftermath

### The Republic

Since the revolution, [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]] has been governed as a republic. However, political stability has proved elusive: "things still manage to be pretty lively there, so I've heard. They've assassinated a president or two, just to keep their hand in" (002-PN).

### The Royalist Faction

Despite — or perhaps because of — the republic's instability, a Loyalist party emerged to advocate for the restoration of the [[obolovitch-dynasty]]. [[prince-michael-obolovitch]], a relative of the last king (the Obolovitch dynasty is far from extinct, with "probably shoals of young 'uns knocking about, cousins and second cousins and third cousins once removed"), was identified as the preferred candidate (002-PN).

### Revolutionary Factions

The republic gave rise to organizations like the [[red-hand]] ([[comrades-of-the-red-hand|Comrades of the Red Hand]]), committed to preventing any restoration of the monarchy and maintaining republican government through any means necessary, including political assassination.

### Foreign Interest

The discovery of oil in [[herzoslovakia]] transformed the domestic political conflict into an international concern. The British [[foreign-office]], through [[george-lomax]], supported the royalist restoration in exchange for oil concessions to companies represented by [[herman-isaacstein]]. American syndicates made competing offers. The revolution's legacy thus became entangled with the geopolitics of petroleum.

## Connection to the Novel's Events

The revolution is the root cause of nearly every major plot element:

- **The House Party at [[chimneys]]**: Arranged for [[prince-michael-obolovitch]] to negotiate his restoration with [[herman-isaacstein|Isaacstein]]
- **The Murder**: The prince is killed, possibly by republican agents of the [[red-hand]] or by parties with their own motives
- **[[count-stylptitch]]'s Memoirs**: The late [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n statesman's writings contain sensitive information about the revolution and its aftermath
- **[[king-victor]]'s Involvement**: The international [[king-victor|jewel thief]]'s gang may have connections to the revolutionary factions or the stolen crown treasures
