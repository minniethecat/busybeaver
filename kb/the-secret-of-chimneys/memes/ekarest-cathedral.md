# Ekarest Cathedral

The cathedral in [[ekarest|Ekarest]], [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]], was the site of the controversial wedding between [[nicholas-iv|King Nicholas IV]] and [[queen-varaga|Varaga]] — a former Parisian music-hall artiste elevated to royalty through Nicholas's determination. The marriage, conducted "with a couple of unwilling Archbishops," was a key factor in destabilizing the monarchy (221-PN).

## The Royal Wedding

[[nicholas-iv|Nicholas IV]] married [[queen-varaga|Varaga]] in the cathedral at [[ekarest|Ekarest]] after presenting her as "the Countess Popoffsky" with fabricated "Romanov blood in her veins" (221-PN). The Archbishops who performed the ceremony were "unwilling" — a detail that underscores the illegitimacy of the match in the eyes of the Herzoslovakian establishment. [[nicholas-iv|Nicholas IV]] "squared his ministers" but "forgot to reckon with the populace" (221-PN).

## Role in the Revolution

The cathedral wedding became a symbol of royal overreach and foreign corruption. The Herzoslovakian people's rejection of the marriage — and of the queen they saw as a "guttersnipe" unworthy of the throne — contributed directly to the "final uprising which stormed the palace" and proclaimed the republic (221-PN).
