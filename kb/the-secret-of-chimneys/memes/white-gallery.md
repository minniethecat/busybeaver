## Description

The White Gallery is a room on the upper floor of [[chimneys|Chimneys]], notable for containing the Priest's Hole—a secret passage hidden within the estate. The room is described as having a secret entrance "ever so much more amusing" than the one at the other end of the house [2871-2871].

## Role in the Novel

During the investigation at [[chimneys|Chimneys]], [[hiram-fish|Mr. Hiram Fish]] expresses particular interest in the White Gallery and its secret passage. He had "heard of the one in here" and asked to be shown the Priest's Hole [2871-2871]. His keen interest in the secret passage is later revealed to be connected to his true identity as a [[pinkerton|Pinkerton]] detective.
