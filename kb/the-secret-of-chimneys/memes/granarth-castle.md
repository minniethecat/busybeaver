## Role in the Novel

The *Granarth Castle* is the Union Castle liner that brings the novel's protagonist, Anthony Cade (traveling under the name James McGrath), from South Africa to England [297-297]. Its arrival timing creates a cascade of confusion that drives the early plot.

## Description

The *Granarth Castle* is a passenger ship operated by the Union Castle Line, serving the South Africa—Southampton route [871-871]. The vessel's name becomes a plot point when Bill Eversleigh, sent by George Lomax to check the ship's arrival, mumbles the name inaudibly. The clerk at the Union Castle offices mishears "Granarth" as "Carnfrae," leading to incorrect information about the ship's arrival date [871-871].

## Plot Significance

The ship's actual arrival on Tuesday afternoon—rather than the Thursday that George Lomax expected—allows Anthony Cade to disembark at Southampton and reach London a full day before anyone anticipates his presence [873-873]. This head start enables him to investigate the situation at his own pace, setting the stage for the events at Chimneys.

> "James McGrath is due to arrive by the *Granarth Castle* tomorrow—Thursday." [645-645]

> Therefore, at the moment he was buttonholing Lord Caterham on the steps of the club on Wednesday morning, he would have been greatly surprised to learn that the *Granarth Castle* had docked at Southampton the preceding afternoon. [873-873]
