## Summary

[[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]] reveals that the notorious French criminal [[king-victor|King Victor]] is believed to be in [[england|England]], potentially connected to the unfolding events at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] [1581-1581]. The revelation intensifies the investigation, as [[king-victor|King Victor]] is one of the most daring international thieves in the world, with his headquarters based in [[paris|Paris]] [0199-0199].

## Context

> "King Victor's supposed to be in England." [1581-1581]

[[superintendent-battle|Battle]]'s information disturbs [[george-lomax|George Lomax]], who believed [[king-victor|King Victor]] was still in prison [1596-1596]. The French police had meant to keep track of him, but he had slipped away some months prior. [[superintendent-battle|Battle]] suggests a possible connection between [[king-victor|King Victor]] and the crime that has occurred [1604-1604].

The search for [[king-victor|King Victor]] spans multiple chapters, intertwining with the [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n political intrigue and the mystery of the stolen memoirs. [[king-victor|King Victor]] was described by [[james-mcgrath|Jimmy McGrath]] as a man the gang was "after"—a figure whom even international criminals feared [0189-0189].
