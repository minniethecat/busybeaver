# Comrades of the Red Hand

A violent [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]n revolutionary organization whose signature is a scarlet hand emblem left at the scene of their crimes. They are responsible for the murder of [[prince-michael-obolovitch|Prince Michael Obolovitch]] at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] and serve as the novel's most visible antagonists, though the narrative progressively reveals them to be tools of a more cunning intelligence — [[king-victor|King Victor]].

## Description

The Comrades of the Red Hand are a secret revolutionary society originating in [[herzoslovakia|Herzoslovakia]]. Their members are uniformly described as "big thickset men, with high cheekbones, and eyes set in Magyar slanting fashion" (5909-PN), physically imposing but intellectually limited. Their signature calling card is a paper bearing the image of a scarlet hand, left on or under the bodies of their victims. When one of their members confronts [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] at [[blitz-hotel|the Blitz]] Hotel, he brandishes this emblem and declares his allegiance:

> "It is the sign of the Comrades of the Red Hand. I am a Comrade of the Red Hand." [005-104]

Anthony's irreverent response — "Are the others all like you? I don't know what the Eugenic Society would have to say about it" (1075-PN) — encapsulates the novel's attitude toward the Comrades: physically formidable, fanatical in their cause, yet easily dismissed by a man of wit and action. After dispatching the emissary with a well-aimed kick, Anthony reflects on the organization's limitations:

> "So much for the Comrades of the Red Hand," he remarked. "Picturesque appearance, but easily routed by direct action." [005-114]

The Comrades operate through intimidation and brute force rather than subtlety. [[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]], assessing their capabilities, observes that "those who talk most about blood have never actually seen it run" (012-118). Despite their fearsome reputation, the Comrades lack the sophistication for complex operations — a weakness that makes them vulnerable to manipulation by more calculating criminal minds.

## Role in the Herzoslovakian Revolution

The Comrades of the Red Hand were instrumental in the upheaval that overthrew the Herzoslovakian monarchy. According to M. Lemoine of the French Sûreté, the Comrades originated as a revolutionary organization dedicated to toppling King Nicholas IV. Their initial strategy involved recruiting [[angele-mory|Angèle Mory]], a Parisian actress, to infiltrate the royal court:

> "About that time, Paris was preparing for the visit of the young King Nicholas IV of Herzoslovakia. At the Sûreté we were given special instructions as to the course to be adopted to ensure the safety of His Majesty. In particular we were warned to superintend the activities of a certain revolutionary organization which called itself the Comrades of the Red Hand. It is fairly certain now that the Comrades approached Angèle Mory and offered her a huge sum if she would aid them in their plans. Her part was to infatuate the young king, and decoy him to some spot agreed upon with them. Angèle Mory accepted the bribe and promised to perform her part." [019-031]

However, Angèle Mory double-crossed her employers. Rather than luring the king to his death, she seduced him, married him, and became Queen Varaga of Herzoslovakia. The Comrades' response was ferocious and ultimately successful:

> "The Comrades of the Red Hand, furious at her betrayal, twice attempted her life. Finally they worked up the country to such a pitch that a revolution broke out in which both the king and queen perished. Their bodies, horribly mutilated and hardly recognizable, were recovered, attesting to the fury of the populace against the lowborn foreign queen." [019-032]

Even years after the revolution, the Comrades continued to pursue their vendetta against Queen Varaga — who had, in fact, survived and was living under a false identity as Mademoiselle Brun. [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] later explains that she "spent a good many years lying low in deadly terror of the Comrades of the Red Hand. They promoted the revolution, remember, and, to use an expressive phrase, they always had it in for her" (6789-PN).

## Connection to King Victor

Though the Comrades provide the visible muscle throughout the novel, the investigation progressively uncovers that they are operating under the direction of the master criminal [[king-victor|King Victor]]. [[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]] articulates this alliance plainly:

> "I should say he was a regular thief, and that he was got hold of, either by King Victor or by the Comrades of the Red Hand, and employed by them. I shouldn't wonder at all if the Comrades and King Victor aren't working together. The organization has plenty of money and power, but it isn't very strong in brains." [020-050]

This partnership is a marriage of complementary resources: the Comrades supply numbers, fanaticism, and revolutionary infrastructure; King Victor supplies the strategic cunning the organization sorely lacks. The murder of [[prince-michael-obolovitch|Prince Michael]] at [[chimneys|Chimneys]] exemplifies this dynamic. While the killing was "the work of the Comrades of the Red Hand, punishing supposed treachery on the part of the victim, the staging of it, and the absence of the Comrades' sign manual, pointed to some abler intelligence directing operations" (6769-PN). King Victor, posing as the Sûreté detective M. Lemoine, orchestrated the murder to serve his own ends — the recovery of the missing [[koh-i-noor-diamond|Koh-i-Noor diamond]].

The Comrades maintained a safe house at [[dover|Dover]] where they were stationed during the novel's events. When [[anthony-cade|Anthony]] and Mr. Fish raided this location, they found the Comrades in a state of frustrated inaction — their violent impulses barely contained:

> "Aha!" roared another big thickset fellow. "You insult the Comrades? I will soon set the sign of the Red Hand round your throat." [024-072]

This outburst, quickly quelled by King Victor's authority, illustrates both the Comrades' brutish nature and their subordination to a more disciplined commander. After securing the house, Anthony and Fish "established martial law at the Dover house, and prevented the Comrades from communicating with the false Lemoine" (6891-PN), effectively neutralizing the organization's role in the final act.

## Role in the Novel

The Comrades provide the visible threat throughout the novel. They attempt to intimidate Anthony at his hotel, they murder Prince Michael at [[chimneys|Chimneys]], and their presence in [[england|England]] triggers [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]]'s surveillance operation. However, the novel progressively reveals that they are not the masterminds but rather tools: [[superintendent-battle|Superintendent Battle]] notes that "we know all about the Comrades of the Red Hand. We've had our eye on them ever since Prince Michael landed in England. That sort of thing is the elementary work of the department. They'd never be allowed to get within a mile of him" (3095-PN). [[anthony-cade|Anthony Cade]] shares this low opinion, remarking that "pointless killing would be rather characteristic of them" (3423-PN).

The Comrades thus serve a dual narrative function. As revolutionary assassins with a grisly trademark, they provide the conventional thriller menace. But as unwitting puppets of King Victor, they also exemplify one of Christie's recurring themes: the dangerous intersection of political fanaticism and criminal opportunism.

## Key Events

- **The Herzoslovakian Revolution** — The Comrades instigated the uprising that killed King Nicholas IV and (supposedly) Queen Varaga, having "worked up the country to such a pitch" after Angèle Mory's betrayal [019-032]
- **Confrontation at [[blitz-hotel|the Blitz]] Hotel** — A Comrade emissary confronts [[anthony-cade|Anthony]] in his hotel room, brandishing the scarlet hand emblem, but Anthony overpowers him (1073-PN)
- **Murder of Prince Michael** — The Comrades kill Prince Michael at [[chimneys|Chimneys]], supposedly as punishment for treachery, though the staging suggests direction by [[king-victor|King Victor]] (6769-PN)
- **Surveillance by [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]]** — [[superintendent-battle|Battle]] reveals that Scotland Yard has been monitoring the Comrades since Prince Michael arrived in [[england|England]] (3095-PN)
- **Involvement in the [[koh-i-noor-diamond|Koh-i-Noor]] Theft** — The Comrades initially approached [[angele-mory|Angèle Mory]] and "offered her a huge sum if she would aid them in their plans" to steal the [[koh-i-noor-diamond|diamond]] from [[nicholas-iv|King Nicholas IV]] (4551-PN)
- **Raid on the Dover Safe House** — [[anthony-cade|Anthony]] and Mr. Fish secure the Comrades' base of operations, imposing martial law and cutting their communication with [[king-victor|King Victor]] (6891-PN)
