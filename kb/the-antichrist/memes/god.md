God, in Nietzsche's _The Antichrist_, is not refuted but **genealogized** — his historical transformation traced, step by step, from the tribal god of Israel to the pallid abstraction of Christian theology. Nietzsche's argument is not "God does not exist" but rather: _look at what this concept has become_. The Christian God is, in Nietzsche's analysis, the metaphysical projection of a declining civilization's self-hatred — "so pale, so weak, so _décadent_."

## The Genealogy of the Christian God

Nietzsche traces a historical devolution of the concept of God through progressive stages of weakening. Each stage eliminates a quality of power, pride, or vitality, until the deity becomes a mere shadow — the "ideal," the "pure spirit," the "thing-in-itself":

> When everything necessary to _ascending_ life; when all that is strong, courageous, masterful and proud has been eliminated from the concept of a god; when he has sunk step by step to the level of a staff for the weary, a sheet-anchor for the drowning; when he becomes the poor man's god, the sinner's god, the invalid's god _par excellence_... what does such a _reduction_ of the godhead imply? (017-001)

The answer Nietzsche gives is devastating: this reduction implies nothing less than the triumph of [[decadence]]. The strong god of a strong people — Yahweh as the god of Israel's power — has been systematically stripped of every quality that affirms [[life]] and replaced with qualities that serve the weak.

## God as the God of the Weak

The Christian God is, for Nietzsche, a **democrat among gods** — a deity who has abandoned all aristocratic qualities in favor of universality and accessibility:

> To be sure, the "kingdom of God" has thus grown larger. Formerly he had only his own people, his "chosen" people. But since then he has gone wandering... finally he has come to feel at home everywhere, and is the great cosmopolitan — until now he has the "great majority" on his side, and half the earth. But this god of the "great majority," this democrat among gods, has not become a proud heathen god: on the contrary, he remains a Jew, he remains a god in a corner, a god of all the dark nooks and crevices, of all the noisesome quarters of the world! (017-001)

The paradox Nietzsche exposes is this: the more "universal" the Christian God becomes, the weaker and more contemptible he grows. Universality is achieved at the price of [[instinct|vitality]]. The God who belongs to everyone belongs to no one; the God who loves all is incapable of discriminating strength from weakness.

## The Two Gods: Good God and Devil

Nietzsche identifies a crucial moment in the genealogy: the splitting of the divine into two opposed principles — the good god and the devil:

> The same instinct which prompts the inferior to reduce their own god to "goodness-in-itself" also prompts them to eliminate all good qualities from the god of their superiors; they make revenge on their masters by making a _devil_ of the latter's god. — The _good_ god, and the devil like him — both are abortions of _décadence_. (017-001)

This is a masterful piece of psychological genealogy. The "good god" is the god of the weak, stripped of everything dangerous; the devil is the god of the strong, demonized. Both are products of the same [[ressentiment|resentful]] instinct — the will of the inferior to invert the values of their superiors.

## God and the Priest

The concept of God functions, in Nietzsche's analysis, as the priest's ultimate instrument of authority:

| Aspect of God | Priestly Function |
|--------------|-------------------|
| God as judge | Threat of eternal punishment secures obedience |
| God as savior | Creates dependency on priestly mediation |
| God as "goodness" | Defines morality in terms favorable to the weak |
| God as "pure spirit" | Devalues the body and earthly life |
| God as omnipresent | Eliminates privacy; every act is monitored |

> The "salvation of the soul" — in plain English: "the world revolves around _me_." (043-001)

The theological apparatus of God, judgment, and salvation is, at bottom, a mechanism for flattering human vanity while simultaneously enslaving it to priestly authority.

## God Becomes Metaphysician

The final stage in Nietzsche's genealogy is God's absorption into philosophy — the transformation of a living deity into a metaphysical abstraction:

> Even the palest of the pale are able to master him — messieurs the metaphysicians, those albinos of the intellect. They spun their webs around him for so long that finally he was hypnotized, and began to spin himself, and became another metaphysician... thereafter he became ever thinner and paler — became the "ideal," became "pure spirit," became "the absolute," became "the thing-in-itself."... _The collapse of a god_: he became a "thing-in-itself." (017-001)

This is Nietzsche at his most ironic: the philosopher-priests (Kant, Hegel, Schopenhauer) who believed they were defending God were in fact completing his dissolution. The God of the philosophers — an abstract "first cause" or "moral lawgiver" — is the terminal stage of the disease. A god who has become a concept is already dead.
