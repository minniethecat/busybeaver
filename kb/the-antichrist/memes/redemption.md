**Redemption** (salvation, _Erlösung_) is, in Nietzsche's analysis, a concept that undergoes a catastrophic transformation between the life of Jesus and the religion of Paul. What Jesus lived as an immanent psychological state — "the Kingdom of God is within you" — becomes, through Paul's theological genius, a transactional doctrine of salvation-through-faith that serves as the church's primary instrument of power. The entire economic apparatus of sin, guilt, punishment, and redemption is, for Nietzsche, a Jewish inheritance that Jesus himself abolished.

## Jesus Abolished the Economy of Salvation

Nietzsche's most radical claim about the historical Jesus is that the concepts of guilt, punishment, reward, and redemption were precisely what he _eliminated_:

> In the whole psychology of the "Gospels" the concepts of guilt and punishment are lacking, and so is that of reward. "Sin," which means anything that puts a distance between God and man, is abolished — _this is precisely the "glad tidings."_ Eternal bliss is not merely promised, nor is it bound up with conditions: it is conceived as the _only_ reality — what remains consists merely of signs useful in speaking of it. (033-001)

Salvation, in Jesus's practice, is not a future state earned through faith or works — it is a present mode of existence. Nietzsche is emphatic on this point:

> The deep instinct which prompts the Christian how to _live_ so that he will feel that he is "in heaven" and is "immortal," despite many reasons for feeling that he is _not_ "in heaven": this is the only psychological reality in "salvation." — A new way of life, _not_ a new faith.... (033-001)

Jesus "no longer needed any formula or ritual in his relations with God — not even prayer. He had rejected the whole of the Jewish doctrine of repentance and atonement" (033-001). The Gospel as Jesus lived it was the _abolition_ of the redemption-economy, not its fulfillment.

## Paul's Counter-Revolution

What happened after Jesus's death was, in Nietzsche's reading, the systematic inversion of everything Jesus represented:

> The very word "Christianity" is a misunderstanding — at bottom there was only one Christian, and he died on the cross. The "Gospels" _died_ on the cross. What, from that moment onward, was called the "Gospels" was the very reverse of what _he_ had lived: "bad tidings," a _Dysangelium_. (039-001)

Paul recognized that the belief in personal immortality was the essential instrument for devaluing "the world" and concentrating power:

> He grasped the fact that he _needed_ the belief in immortality in order to rob "the world" of its value, that the concept of "hell" would master Rome — that the notion of a "beyond" is the _death of life_. (058-001)

| Jesus's Redemption (Immanent) | Paul's Redemption (Transactional) |
|------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| A way of life | A doctrine of faith |
| Eternal bliss is present reality | Salvation is a future reward |
| No conditions or rituals | Faith in the redeemer required |
| God within you | God as external saviour |
| Abolishes sin | Reintroduces sin as leverage |

Paul's genius, Nietzsche argues, lay in putting "the ideas which lent fascination to every sort of Chandala religion into the mouth of the 'Saviour' as his own inventions" (058-001). The cross became a symbol not of a life lived but of a _transaction_ — a sacrifice that purchases salvation for believers.

## The Degradation of God into "Saviour"

In §17, Nietzsche traces how the concept of God is steadily reduced until the attribute of "saviour" or "redeemer" becomes its sole essential feature:

> When everything necessary to _ascending_ life... has been eliminated from the concept of a god; when he has sunk step by step to the level of a staff for the weary, a sheet-anchor for the drowning; when he becomes the poor man's god, the sinner's god, the invalid's god _par excellence_, and the attribute of "saviour" or "redeemer" remains as the one essential attribute of divinity — just _what_ is the significance of such a metamorphosis? what does such a _reduction_ of the godhead imply? (017-001)

A god whose defining attribute is "redeemer" is a god designed by and for the weak — a god who exists to compensate for life rather than to affirm it. The "kingdom of God" expands not in power but in inclusiveness, ultimately becoming the god of "the great majority."

## Salvation as the Ultimate Egoism

Nietzsche's sharpest polemic against the doctrine of personal salvation appears in §43:

> That every man, because he has an "immortal soul," is as good as every other man; that in an infinite universe of things the "salvation" of _every_ individual may lay claim to eternal importance; that insignificant bigots and the three-fourths insane may assume that the laws of nature are constantly _suspended_ in their behalf — it is impossible to lavish too much contempt upon such a magnification of every sort of selfishness to infinity, to _insolence_. (043-001)

The doctrine that "salvation is of the Jews" (024-001, quoting John 4:22) is, for Nietzsche, the marker of Christianity's true origin: it is not a break from Judaism but its "inevitable product" — "simply one more step in the awe-inspiring logic of the Jews" (024-001).

## The Physiological Root

Nietzsche's ultimate reduction of the redemption-concept is physiological. The "doctrine of salvation" springs from two facts about the nervous system: extreme susceptibility to pain, and the consequent need to eliminate all resistance, all hostility, all boundaries:

> These are the two _physiological realities_ upon and out of which the doctrine of salvation has sprung. I call them a sublime super-development of hedonism upon a thoroughly unsalubrious soil. (030-001)

The entire edifice of Christian soteriology — sin, atonement, redemption, eternal life — is, in this reading, the intellectual superstructure built upon a disordered nervous system's inability to tolerate reality.
