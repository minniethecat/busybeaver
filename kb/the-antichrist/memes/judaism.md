**Judaism**, in Nietzsche's analysis of Christianity's origins, occupies a paradoxical position. The Jews are not, as Christian anti-Semites would have it, a degenerate people; on the contrary, they possess "the very strongest vitality" (024-001). Their adoption of _décadence_ values was a strategic choice — a brilliant act of self-preservation by a people facing impossible conditions of existence.

## The Strategic Adoption of Décadence

In §24, Nietzsche lays out his most complete statement on the Jewish question:

> Psychologically, the Jews are a people gifted with the very strongest vitality, so much so that when they found themselves facing impossible conditions of life they chose voluntarily, and with a profound talent for self-preservation, the side of all those instincts which make for _décadence_ — _not_ as if mastered by them, but as if detecting in them a power by which "the world" could be _defied_. The Jews are the very opposite of _décadents_: they have simply been forced into _appearing_ in that guise, and with a degree of skill approaching the _non plus ultra_ of histrionic genius they have managed to put themselves at the head of all _décadent_ movements (—for example, the Christianity of Paul—), and so make of them something stronger than any party frankly saying _Yes_ to life. (024-001)

This is a profound departure from conventional anti-Semitic narratives. The Jews did not succumb to decadence — they _weaponized_ it. Their "histrionic genius" consisted in recognizing that the values of the weak could be turned into an instrument of power.

## The Priest and the Revaluation of Values

The Jewish priestly class is the key agent in this strategic revaluation. In §24, Nietzsche identifies the priests as those who "have a vital interest in making mankind sick, and in confusing the values of 'good' and 'bad,' 'true' and 'false' in a manner that is not only dangerous to life, but also slanders it" (024-001).

The specifically Jewish achievement — later inherited and radicalized by [[paul|Paul]] — was the **transvaluation** of aristocratic values. In §17, Nietzsche traces how the concept of God was transformed:

> Wherever the will to power begins to decline... there is always an accompanying decline physiologically, a _décadence_. The divinity of this _décadence_, shorn of its masculine virtues and passions, is converted perforce into a god of the physiologically degraded, of the weak. Of course, they do not _call_ themselves the weak; they call themselves "the good."... (017-001)

When a people's power declines, they remake their god in their own diminished image:

> The same instinct which prompts the inferior to reduce their own god to "goodness-in-itself" also prompts them to eliminate all good qualities from the god of their superiors; they make revenge on their masters by making a _devil_ of the latter's god. — The _good_ god, and the devil like him — both are abortions of _décadence_. (017-001)

## The Concept of God in Judaism

Nietzsche traces the evolution of the Jewish god from a national deity to the universal Christian god. In §25, he describes this as a process of progressive abstraction and denaturing:

> The concept of God becomes ever more remote: it becomes ever more "spiritual"; the ceremonial sense becomes ever more difficult to maintain. (025-001)

In §26, he frames the Jewish priesthood as the architects of a counter-nature:

> The history of Israel is invaluable as the typical history of every _denaturing_ of natural values. (026-001)

Originally, Yahweh expressed the self-confidence of the Israelite people — their power, their joy, their hope in themselves. But when misfortune struck, the priests reinterpreted every disaster as _punishment_ for insufficient piety, transforming the relationship between people and god from one of pride to one of [[sin|guilt]]:

> The priests...reinterpreted all good fortune as a reward, all misfortune as a punishment for disobedience to God, for "sin": that most fraudulent of all modes of interpretation which contrives to make every priest the master of every people. (026-001)

## Paul: The Jewish Genius Who Created Christianity

In §47, Nietzsche identifies [[paul|Paul]] as the pivotal figure who took the Jewish revaluation to its universal conclusion:

> The God that Paul invented for himself, a God who "reduced to absurdity" "the wisdom of this world"... is in truth only an indication of Paul's resolute _determination_ to accomplish that very thing himself: to give one's own will the name of God, _thora_ — that is essentially Jewish. Paul _wants_ to dispose of the "wisdom of this world"... (047-001)

Paul "invented" a God who reduces to absurdity everything that the noble intellect values — philology, medicine, science — precisely because those disciplines threatened priestly authority. The method was Jewish, but the target was now universal: not just Israel's enemies, but "the world" itself.

In §58, Nietzsche calls Paul "the Chandala hatred of Rome, of 'the world,' in the flesh and inspired by genius — the Jew, the _eternal_ Jew _par excellence_... 'Salvation is of the Jews'" (058-001).

## Judaism and the Code of Manu

In §56–57, Nietzsche contrasts the Jewish-Christian Bible with the Hindu Code of Manu. The Bible is "an evil-smelling mess of Jewish rabbinism and superstition" (056-001), whereas the Code of Manu represents legislation that serves the _noble_ type. The Jewish priests used [[holy-lie|holy lies]] not to preserve a natural order of rank, but to invert it — to make the weak rule over the strong through the concepts of sin, guilt, and redemption.

## Judaism as Foundation, Not Target

It is essential to note that Nietzsche's critique of Judaism is fundamentally different from racial anti-Semitism. He respects the Jews' vitality and intelligence while condemning the _priestly_ revaluation they pioneered. His target is not the Jewish people but the **moral system** — the "slave revolt in morality" — that originated in Jewish priestly culture and was universalized by Christianity. As he writes in §55, the problem is not racial but _axiological_: a certain set of values triumphed that were hostile to life.
