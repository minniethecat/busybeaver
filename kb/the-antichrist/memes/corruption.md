# Corruption (Verderbniss)

**Corruption** (*Verderbniss*) in Nietzsche's critical vocabulary names the *active process* by which Christianity spoils, degrades, and depraves the natural human type. While [[decadence]] (*décadence*, *Entartung*) describes the *condition* of decline — the physiological-psychological state of a life in descent — corruption describes the *mechanism* of that decline: the deliberate corruption of instincts, of intellectual faculties, and of values by priestly morality. The distinction is between the disease and the agent of infection.

## Definition and Mechanism

Nietzsche's concept of corruption operates through *inversion*: natural instincts are relabeled as sinful, and anti-natural behaviors are sanctified as virtuous. The mechanism is not passive decay but *active spoiling* — a conscious or instinct-driven campaign to make the healthy sick:

> Christianity has taken the part of all the weak, the low, the botched; it has made an ideal out of *antagonism* to all the self-preservative instincts of sound life; it has corrupted even the faculties of those natures that are intellectually most vigorous, by representing the highest intellectual values as sinful, as misleading, as full of temptation. (005-001)

The key phrase is "corrupted even the faculties of those natures that are intellectually most vigorous." Corruption is not merely the welcoming of the weak but the *deliberate disabling of the strong* — the corruption of intellect itself. Nietzsche's example is Pascal: "The most lamentable example: the corruption of Pascal, who believed that his intellect had been destroyed by original sin, whereas it was actually destroyed by Christianity!" (005-001)

## Corruption of Philosophy and Reason

Nietzsche extends the analysis of corruption to the history of philosophy, where the priestly inheritance is most visible:

> In the end, with "German" innocence, Kant tried to give a scientific flavour to this form of corruption, this dearth of intellectual conscience, by calling it "practical reason." He deliberately invented a variety of reasons for use on occasions when it was desirable not to trouble with reason — that is, when morality, when the sublime command "thou shalt," was heard. (012-001)

Kant's "practical reason" is diagnosed as a form of *intellectual corruption* — the deliberate construction of a separate epistemic domain where moral convictions need not submit to rational scrutiny. When the philosopher "feels that he has a divine mission," he "stands beyond all merely reasonable standards of judgment" (012-001). The philosopher, descended from the priest, inherits the priest's immunity from intellectual accountability.

## The Corrupt Soil of Christianity

Nietzsche traces Christianity's origin to a specific historical-cultural corruption:

> Christianity sprang from a soil so corrupt that on it everything natural, every natural value, every *reality* was opposed by the deepest instincts of the ruling class — it grew up as a sort of war to the death upon reality, and as such it has never been surpassed. (027-001)

The "corrupt soil" is late Judaism, where the priestly instinct had so thoroughly denaturized values that even *Jewish reality itself* was eventually denied. Christianity is the "logical conclusion" of this corruption — a "war to the death upon reality" that radicalizes the Jewish inversion of values into a universal religion.

## Psychological Corruption in the Gospels

Nietzsche reads the Gospels themselves as *evidence* of corruption — not as innocent documents but as artifacts of an already-advanced psychological decay:

> The gospels are invaluable as evidence of the corruption that was already persistent *within* the primitive community. That which Paul, with the cynical logic of a rabbi, later developed to a conclusion was at bottom merely a process of decay that had begun with the death of the Saviour. (044-001)

The corruption is not an external imposition but an *internal* process — the decay of the original evangelic impulse from within the community itself. Nietzsche calls this "an artistic triumph in psychological corruption" (044-001): the skill with which the early Christians transformed the death of their leader into a theological system of guilt, judgment, and redemption.

| Domain | What Christianity Corrupts | Mechanism |
|--------|--------------------------|-----------|
| Intellect | Reason, scientific spirit | Moralization of truth ("practical reason") |
| Body | Health, instinct, sensuality | Doctrine of sin, soul/body dualism |
| Values | Natural hierarchy, pride | Inversion: strong → evil, weak → good |
| History | Genealogical truth | Holy lie, falsification of texts |
| Psychology | Clear self-perception | Bad conscience, guilt, "inner world" |

## Corruption, Décadence, and the Holy Lie

Corruption operates through the mechanism of the [[holy-lie]] — the priestly fabrication that passes for sacred truth. While décadence is the *physiological condition* of decline, corruption is the *priestly technique* for producing and maintaining that condition. The [[priest]] is the agent of corruption; the holy lie is its instrument; décadence is its product.

> The whole disaster was only made possible by the fact that there already existed in the world a similar megalomania, allied to this one in race, to wit, the *Jewish*... The Christian is simply a Jew of the "reformed" confession. (044-001)

This genealogical connection — Christianity as Judaism's corruption universalized — is central to Nietzsche's analysis. Corruption is not a Christian invention but a Christian *perfection* of Jewish priestly technique.
