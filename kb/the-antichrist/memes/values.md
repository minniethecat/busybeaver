# Values (Werthe)

**Values** (*Werthe*) are, for Nietzsche, not eternal truths discovered by reason but *creations* — projections of the will to power of a people, a type, or an individual. The concept of "values" is central to Nietzsche's project of *Umwertung aller Werte* (transvaluation of all values): the systematic re-examination and inversion of the values that have dominated Western civilization under Christianity. Unlike [[will-to-power]], which names the underlying metaphysical drive, "values" names the *content* produced by that drive — the specific valuations that structure a culture's understanding of good and evil, true and false.

## Definition and Mechanism

Nietzsche understands values as *symptoms* of the physiological-psychological condition of the valuer. Values are not chosen freely by rational agents; they *express* the health or sickness, strength or weakness, ascending or descending life of the individual or group. This is the fundamental naturalism of Nietzsche's axiology: to evaluate values, one asks not "Are they true?" but "What kind of life do they express and promote?"

> My argument is that all the values on which mankind now fixes its highest aspirations are *décadence*-values. (006-001)

This thesis — that "the highest values devalue themselves" — is the cornerstone of Nietzsche's critique of morality. The values that present themselves as universal, eternal, and divine are, on Nietzsche's analysis, *symptoms of decline*.

> Life itself appears to me as an instinct for growth, for survival, for the accumulation of forces, for *power*: whenever the will to power fails there is disaster. My contention is that all the highest values of humanity have been emptied of this will — that the values of *décadence*, of *nihilism*, now prevail under the holiest names. (006-002)

## The Transvaluation of All Values

The *Umwertung aller Werte* is Nietzsche's announced life-project and the subtitle of *The Antichrist* itself. It involves a twofold movement:

1. **Critical-diagnostic**: Exposing how existing "highest values" (pity, humility, equality, peace) are products of ressentiment and décadence, dressed in theological language
2. **Creative-affirmative**: Establishing new values grounded in the affirmation of life, the will to power, and the conditions of human greatness

> *We ourselves*, we free spirits, are already a "transvaluation of all values," a *visualized* declaration of war and victory against all the old concepts of "true" and "not true." (013-001)

The transvaluation is not a future project but an *already-accomplished fact* in the very existence of the free spirit. To think genealogically, to practice intellectual honesty, to affirm the body — these acts are themselves the transvaluation in practice.

## Theological Perversion of Values

Nietzsche identifies the theological instinct as the primary mechanism by which values are inverted:

> Wherever the influence of theologians is felt there is a transvaluation of values, and the concepts "true" and "false" are forced to change places: whatever is most damaging to life is there called "true," and whatever exalts it, intensifies it, approves it, justifies it and makes it triumphant is there called "false." (009-001)

This is the "denaturization" of values that Nietzsche traces through Jewish and Christian history (025-001). Natural values — power, health, self-assertion, pride — are rebranded as "evil," while anti-natural values — submission, self-denial, humility — are sanctified as "good."

| Value Type | Source | Examples | Effect on Life |
|-----------|--------|---------|----------------|
| Master values | Noble self-affirmation | Power, pride, courage, creativity | Enhances, intensifies |
| Slave values | Ressentiment against masters | Pity, humility, equality, peace | Diminishes, denies |
| Décadence-values | Physiological decline | Self-denial, "soul," sin-consciousness | Corrupts, sickens |
| Ascending-life values | Overflowing health | Joy, gratitude, noble egoism | Affirms, creates |

## Historical Naturalism of Values

Nietzsche's method for analyzing values is historical and physiological. Values emerge from the *conditions of existence* of a people:

> Originally, and above all in the time of the monarchy, Israel maintained the *right* attitude of things, which is to say, the natural attitude. Its Jahveh was an expression of its consciousness of power, its joy in itself, its hopes for itself. (025-001)

When a people is strong, its god expresses that strength; when it declines, the concept of god is *denaturized* — moralized, abstracted, turned against life. This historical-genealogical method reveals values as *tools* of particular forms of life, not as eternal truths.

The concept of [[nihilism]] names the endpoint of this process: when décadence-values have so thoroughly triumphed that even the will to create new values is extinguished. The transvaluation of all values is thus Nietzsche's *counter-nihilistic* project.
