For Nietzsche, [[paul|Paul]] is not merely a follower of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] — he is the decisive counter-force who transformed a peaceful life-practice into an instrument of global priestly domination. Nietzsche's term for Paul is _Dysangelist_ — the bearer of "bad tidings" — the man who took the "glad tidings" (evangel) of Jesus and inverted them into a theology of hatred, judgment, and [[christianity|ecclesiastical]] power.

## The Dysangelist

Nietzsche's portrait of Paul is savage and precise. Where Jesus represented the incapacity for enmity, Paul represents its opposite: a genius for hatred so pure and systematic that it reshaped Western civilization.

> In Paul is incarnated the very opposite of the "bearer of glad tidings"; he represents the genius for hatred, the vision of hatred, the relentless logic of hatred. _What_, indeed, has not this dysangelist sacrificed to hatred! Above all, the Saviour: he nailed him to _his own_ cross. (042-001)

Paul's great crime, in Nietzsche's reading, was not that he misunderstood Jesus — it was that he understood perfectly well what he was doing. He "nailed the Saviour to his own cross" by transforming the cross from the natural end of a way of life into a cosmic sacrifice requiring a theology of redemption.

## The Invention of Christianity

For Nietzsche, Christianity as a religion — with its doctrines, its priesthood, its institutional structure — is essentially Paul's creation. Jesus provided the raw material (a life, a death); Paul provided the theology that made that material usable for priestly power.

> The life, the example, the teaching, the death of Christ, the meaning and the law of the whole gospels — nothing was left of all this after that counterfeiter in hatred had reduced it to his uses. Surely _not_ reality; surely _not_ historical truth! (042-001)

Paul "invented his own history of Christian beginnings" — he rewrote the past to make it serve his purposes, turning the Hebrew prophets into mere heralds of Christ and the history of Israel into a prologue to his own theological achievement.

## Paul's Theology as Will to Power

| Pauline Doctrine | Psychological Function | Historical Effect |
|-----------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Resurrection of Jesus | Converts hallucination into "proof" | Shifts Christianity's center of gravity behind existence |
| Immortality of the Soul | Establishes judgment as control mechanism | Creates the lever for priestly tyranny over the masses |
| Faith as Salvation | Vetoes science, reason, and intellectual conscience | Makes "lying at any price" into a virtue |
| Christ as Sacrifice | Replaces Jesus's life-practice with death-cult | Erases the historical Jesus in favor of a theological construct |

> Paul _wants_ to dispose of the "wisdom of this world": his enemies are the _good_ philologians and physicians of the Alexandrine school — on them he makes his war. As a matter of fact no man can be a _philologian_ or a physician without being also _Antichrist_. (047-001)

## Paul and the Jews

Nietzsche identifies Paul's operation as essentially Jewish in its methodology: "to give one's own will the name of God" (047-001). The priestly instinct that Nietzsche sees operating throughout Jewish history — the instinct to rewrite history, to falsify reality in service of power — reaches its apotheosis in Paul. He is, for Nietzsche, the priest _par excellence_, and Christianity is the perfected form of priestly domination.
