Instinct is one of Nietzsche's primary psychological categories — the body's pre-reflective intelligence that has been systematically degraded by two thousand years of Christian [[morality]]. In _The Antichrist_, Nietzsche wages war not on instinct itself but on the **theological instinct** — a counter-instinct, bred into human beings by priestly civilization, that systematically falsifies reality in the service of [[life|anti-life]] values.

## Definition and Mechanism

Nietzsche uses _Instinkt_ in a double sense. On one hand, instinct is the body's innate wisdom — what the organism "knows" without reasoning. On the other, there is a specifically _theological_ instinct: a learned, inherited drive toward falsehood that Nietzsche identifies as the deepest corruption of Western civilization:

> Upon this theological instinct I make war: I find the tracks of it everywhere. Whoever has theological blood in his veins is shifty and dishonourable in all things. The pathetic thing that grows out of this condition is called _faith_: in other words, closing one's eyes upon one's self once for all, to avoid suffering the sight of incurable falsehood. (009-001)

The "theological instinct" is not a belief — it operates below the level of belief, as a **pre-reflective orientation toward falsehood**. The theologian does not choose to lie; his instincts have been shaped over centuries to prefer falsity to truth whenever truth threatens the [[priest|priestly]] order.

## Natural Instinct vs. Theological Instinct

Nietzsche contrasts the healthy, life-affirming instincts with the sick, life-denying theological instinct that Christianity has cultivated:

| Natural Instinct | Theological Instinct |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| Trust in the body | Suspicion of the body |
| Will to power and growth | Will to submission and self-denial |
| Honesty about suffering | Reinterpretation of suffering as [[sin]] |
| Pride and self-reverence | Humility and self-abasement |
| Joy in earthly existence | Longing for the "beyond" |
| Discriminating (creates hierarchy) | Leveling (creates "equality") |

> The poisonous doctrine, " _equal_ rights for all," has been propagated as a Christian principle: out of the secret nooks and crannies of bad instinct Christianity has waged a deadly war upon all feelings of reverence and distance between man and man... (043-001)

## Instinct and Religion

Nietzsche analyzes the religious instinct — the drive to worship, to submit, to believe — not as a divine gift but as a **psychological phenomenon** to be diagnosed:

> To insist upon _chastity_ greatly strengthens the vehemence and subjectivity of the religious instinct — it makes the cult warmer, more enthusiastic, more soulful. — Love is the state in which man sees things most decidedly as they are _not_. The force of illusion reaches its highest here, and so does the capacity for sweetening, for _transfiguring_. (023-001)

The religious instinct exploits the most powerful human emotions — love, hope, longing — and channels them toward illusions. Nietzsche's psychological insight is that the intensity of religious feeling is not evidence of its truth but of its capacity to exploit the instincts.

## Instinct and Truth

The most important instinct for Nietzsche is the **instinct for truth** — the intellectual conscience that refuses to accept comfortable falsehoods. This instinct, he argues, has been systematically undermined by Christianity:

> But when _faith_ is thus exalted above everything else, it necessarily follows that reason, knowledge and patient inquiry have to be discredited: the road to the truth becomes a forbidden road. (023-001)

The elevation of [[faith]] over knowledge is, at the instinctual level, a training in dishonesty. After two millennia, this training has become hereditary — Western humanity no longer _chooses_ to believe falsehoods; it instinctively _prefers_ them.

The task Nietzsche sets for the future is the **re-naturalization of the instincts**: the recovery of the body's wisdom, the re-valuation of natural drives, and the cultivation of an intellectual conscience strong enough to endure truth without the narcotic of faith.

## Instinct and Décadence

The loss of healthy instinct is the very definition of [[decadence]] in Nietzsche's diagnostic framework:

> I call an animal, a species, an individual corrupt, when it loses its instincts, when it chooses, when it _prefers_, what is injurious to it. (006-002)

Décadence is fundamentally a disorder of instinct — the organism turns against its own drives for growth, power, and self-assertion. The long history of Christian morality has produced, in Nietzsche's view, a human animal whose instincts have been so thoroughly perverted that it _prefers_ sickness to health, weakness to strength, and death to life.
