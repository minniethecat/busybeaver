# Mrs. Pratt

## Role

Mrs. Pratt is the cook at Old Hall, the estate of Colonel Protheroe [003-060]. She is mentioned by Mary, the vicarage maid, in conversation about domestic matters at Old Hall and at the vicarage. Her role is purely functional in the domestic hierarchy of the village.

## Key Scenes

### Mary's Account (Chapter 19)

Mrs. Pratt is referenced by Mary the vicarage maid in Chapter 19. Mary expresses concern about "a lot of unpleasantness with Mrs. Pratt, that's the cook, sir" [019-081], indicating tension in the domestic staff at Old Hall. Mary appears anxious that information might reach Mrs. Pratt through gossip channels: "she'd catch it badly if Mrs. Pratt ever came to hear of it" [019-086] and "If it were to get to Mrs. Pratt's ears—" [019-094].

### Old Hall Departure

A visitor to Old Hall (Miss Cram, Dr. Stone's secretary) reportedly "left Old Hall with a good" — the sentence is cut off — after "misdoings being made known to Mrs. Pratt" [019-110], suggesting that Mrs. Pratt's awareness of household activities carried weight in the domestic power structure of the estate.

## Relationships

- **Colonel Protheroe**: Employer at Old Hall [003-060]
- **Mary**: The vicarage maid who references Mrs. Pratt in conversation [019-081]
- **Anne Protheroe**: Mistress of Old Hall, indirectly connected as the household's lady
- **Lettice Protheroe**: Daughter of the household, resident at Old Hall
