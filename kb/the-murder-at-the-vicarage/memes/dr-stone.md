# Dr. Stone

Dr. Stone is a well-known archaeologist who has come to St. Mary Mead to excavate a barrow on Colonel Protheroe's land. His presence introduces an element of academic eccentricity to the village and provides cover for suspicious behavior that, in the end, proves entirely innocent.

## Role in the Story

Stone functions as a classic Christie red herring. He is an outsider with a mysterious purpose, conducting excavations that no one in the village understands, assisted by a noisy young secretary, and involved in a quarrel with the murder victim over access to the barrow. All the elements of suspicion are present — and all are misleading.

## Background

Stone is "a well-known archaeologist who had recently come to live in the neighbourhood, having taken a house for the purpose of carrying out excavations on a barrow" (002-037). He is single-minded in his academic pursuits to the point of social obliviousness, and his assistant Miss Cram — a "healthy young woman of twenty-five, noisy in manner" — provides comic contrast to his scholarly abstraction.

## Key Actions

### The Barrow Excavation

Stone's excavation is his stated reason for being in St. Mary Mead, but it also provides him with an excuse to be in certain places at certain times. His frequent presence at the barrow makes him difficult to locate when the police wish to question him.

### The Quarrel with Protheroe

Griselda mentions that Stone and Protheroe "quarrelled about the barrow" (004-041), a detail that briefly casts suspicion on the archaeologist. The quarrel is never fully explained, adding to the atmosphere of hidden conflict.

### The London Trip

Stone's trip to London shortly after the murder — "my old mother to see tomorrow, some business with my lawyers on Monday" (018-048) — seems suspiciously timed but proves to have no connection to the crime.

## Relationships

- **Miss Cram**: His secretary and assistant. Their relationship is professional but has provided village gossips with material for speculation.

- **Colonel Protheroe**: His host and, briefly, his antagonist. Their quarrel over the barrow is one of the novel's many unresolved tensions.

## Character Analysis

Stone is a study in the innocent suspect — someone whose perfectly legitimate activities look suspicious when viewed through the lens of a murder investigation. His academic abstraction makes him incapable of providing clear alibis or understanding why he is under suspicion, a trait that both incriminates and exonerates him.
