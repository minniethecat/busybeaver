# Mrs. Archer

Mrs. Archer is an elderly woman who works as a daily cleaner at the Vicarage. She is the wife of Archer, a known poacher in St. Mary Mead, and her testimony regarding the pistol's location becomes a significant point of evidence in the investigation of Colonel Protheroe's murder.

## Role in the Story

Mrs. Archer occupies a peripheral but crucial role in the investigation: as the last known person to have seen Lawrence Redding's pistol before the murder, her testimony about its location and timing directly bears on the question of who could have accessed the weapon. She also appears as a witness at the inquest, where her statements fuel village speculation about alternative suspects.

## Background

Mrs. Archer is described as "an old Mrs. Archer" who "comes in every morning to 'do for me' as it's called" (012-054), referring to her domestic work at the Vicarage. She is married to Archer, a man the vicar describes as "an inveterate poacher — one of those cheerful ne'er-do-wells that are to be found in any parish" (015-032). Her connection to the Vicarage household gives her routine access to the domestic spaces where key evidence would later be found.

## Key Actions

### The Pistol Testimony

Mrs. Archer was shown the murder weapon and "recognized it as the one she had seen in Mr. Redding's sitting-room 'over against the bookcase, he kept it, lying about'" (018-018). She confirmed that she had last seen the pistol on the day of the murder and was "quite sure it was there at lunch time on Thursday — quarter to one when she left" (018-018). This testimony placed the pistol in Redding's cottage at 12:45, making her the last known witness to its location before the shooting at approximately 6:20–6:50.

### The Inquest Testimony

At the inquest, Mrs. Archer "swore that the pistol was still in the cottage when she left at midday" (030-035). However, her certainty was later questioned: the vicar's notes record that the 12:45 sighting was "doubtful, as Mrs. Archer had previously said she could not remember" (026-074). This inconsistency in her account — between categorical certainty at the inquest and earlier uncertainty — becomes a subtle but important ambiguity in the timeline of the weapon's movements.

### The Access Question

Miss Marple, in her speculative reconstruction of alternative theories, observes that "Old Mrs. Archer could easily have got the pistol from Mr. Redding's house" for either Mary or Lettice Protheroe (030-019). Mrs. Archer's routine access to Redding's cottage as a cleaner, combined with her connection to Mary (who was "walking out" with Archer the poacher for two years), made her a potential intermediary in any scheme involving the weapon — even if she herself had no motive for the murder.

## Relationships

- **Archer**: Mrs. Archer's husband, a poacher with a history of conflict with Colonel Protheroe. Archer was imprisoned and released shortly before the murder, publicly vowing "vengeance" against the Colonel (004-071). He was an early suspect but had an alibi for the afternoon of the murder.

- **Mary**: The Vicarage housemaid who had been "walking out with Archer for two years" (010-095). Mrs. Archer's connection to Mary, combined with her own access to Redding's cottage, made the two women a potential team in Miss Marple's alternative suspect theory.

- **Lawrence Redding**: Mrs. Archer cleaned his cottage and was familiar with its contents, including the pistol kept "lying about" on the bookcase. Her testimony confirmed the weapon's presence there shortly before the murder.

- **Colonel Protheroe**: Through her husband's poaching, Mrs. Archer was indirectly connected to the victim. Protheroe had "threatened to show Archer what vengeance was worth" (015-039), a threat that made Archer an early suspect and drew Mrs. Archer into the investigation.

## Appearances

Mrs. Archer is mentioned or discussed in chapters 12, 15, 18, 19, 24, 25, 26, 27, and 30. She appears as a witness at the inquest (chapter 30) and is referenced throughout the investigation as the last person to confirm the pistol's location before the murder.
