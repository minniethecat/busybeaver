# Dr. Haydock

Dr. Haydock is the physician of St. Mary Mead, called to examine Colonel Protheroe's body and pronounce the cause of death. More than a medical functionary, he is a confidant to several key characters and a keeper of secrets — including the truth about Mrs. Lestrange's identity, which he has known all along.

## Role in the Story

Haydock serves as the novel's medical authority and, quietly, as one of its moral centers. He examines Protheroe's wound and confirms "in all probability the bullet was fired from a pistol of small calibre — say a Mauser .25" (005-059). But his most important role is extra-medical: he is the person who helped Mrs. Lestrange come to St. Mary Mead under an assumed name, and he knows — as no one else does — the real reason for her presence.

## Background

Haydock is the village doctor, a man of "about forty, with a clean-shaven, sensitive face" and an air of professional competence. He is a friend of the vicar's and a regular at the vicarage. His personal history — including a romantic attachment to Mrs. Lestrange years ago — adds an unexpected depth to his character.

## Key Actions

### The Medical Examination

Haydock's examination of Protheroe's body provides the first forensic evidence: the bullet was small-calibre, consistent with Redding's pistol. His professional testimony is crucial to the investigation's initial direction.

### Protecting Mrs. Lestrange

Haydock has been keeping Mrs. Lestrange's secret from the beginning. He helped her arrange her stay in St. Mary Mead under an assumed name, knowing her real identity and her real purpose. His loyalty to her — "he was keen about her once — you can see that! In a way, he still is" (032-013) — makes him complicit in her deception but also one of the novel's most sympathetic figures.

### Justice vs. Mercy

Dennis reports that Haydock "would never give a murderer up to justice" (022-054), a statement that aligns him with the novel's theme that legal justice is sometimes trumped by a higher moral obligation. Haydock's own actions bear this out: he protects secrets, withholds information, and prioritizes human care over legal procedure.

## Relationships

- **Mr. Clement**: His friend and fellow amateur investigator. Their conversations over coffee provide some of the novel's most thoughtful moments.

- **Mrs. Lestrange**: His former romantic interest and current patient. Haydock's devotion to her is unwavering and, in the novel's terms, noble.

- **Colonel Protheroe**: His patient. Haydock's professional relationship with Protheroe gives him access to information that the police lack.
