# Mrs. Lestrange

Mrs. Lestrange is the most enigmatic figure in *The Murder at the Vicarage*. A "smart, well-dressed lady" who arrives in St. Mary Mead just before the murder, she becomes the focus of Inspector Slack's blackmail theory. Her true identity — revealed only in the novel's final chapter — transforms the entire narrative.

## Role in the Story

Mrs. Lestrange is the novel's central mystery figure. Everything about her seems suspicious: her assumed name, her secretive visit to Protheroe, her avoidance of his family, and her lack of alibi for the critical time window. Inspector Slack constructs an elaborate theory casting her as Protheroe's blackmailer, driven to murder when her victim threatened legal action. The theory is plausible, coherent, and completely wrong.

## Background

Mrs. Lestrange is not who she appears to be. Her true identity is revealed in the novel's climactic chapter: she is the mother of the real murderer, come to St. Mary Mead to see her dying child. Dr. Haydock, an old friend and former admirer, has helped her establish herself under an assumed name. Her secrecy is not criminal but protective — she is shielding her child from exposure.

## Key Actions

### The Visit to Protheroe

Mrs. Lestrange visits Colonel Protheroe at an unusual time and avoids his wife and daughter. This visit, observed by the village's network of watchers, becomes the foundation of Slack's blackmail theory. The real reason for the visit — connected to her true identity — is never fully explained but is clearly not criminal.

### The Lack of Alibi

Mrs. Lestrange "left Dr. Haydock's house some time after six to keep an appointment" (026-085) — an appointment whose nature and recipient remain mysterious. This gap in her timeline, combined with her proximity to the vicarage at the time of the murder, makes her a suspect.

### The Revelation

In the final chapter, Mrs. Lestrange's secret is revealed. She is dying, her child is the real murderer, and her entire presence in St. Mary Mead has been an attempt to be near her child at the end. The revelation transforms her from sinister stranger to tragic figure.

## Relationships

- **Dr. Haydock**: Her old friend, former admirer, and protector. Haydock's loyalty to her is one of the novel's most touching relationships.

- **Colonel Protheroe**: The object of her visit and, in Slack's theory, her victim. Their actual relationship is entirely different from what the police imagine.

- **The Murderer**: Her child, whose identity she protects at the cost of her own reputation and freedom.

## Character Analysis

Mrs. Lestrange embodies the novel's theme that appearances deceive. She looks like a blackmailer, acts like a woman with a guilty secret, and is suspected of murder — but her actual "crime" is maternal love. Christie uses her to demonstrate that the most suspicious behavior often has the most innocent explanation, a lesson that neither Inspector Slack nor the legal system is equipped to learn.

## Key Quotes

> "Mrs. Lestrange doesn't seem to me to be a potential blackmailer. She's — well, it's an old-fashioned word, but she's a — lady." — Mr. Clement (017-049)
