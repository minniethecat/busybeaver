# Inspector Slack

Inspector Slack is the police detective assigned to investigate Colonel Protheroe's murder. Energetic, self-confident, and almost comically wrong in his conclusions, he represents the limitations of professional policing when confronted with a crime that requires understanding of human nature rather than collection of physical evidence.

## Role in the Story

Slack is the novel's embodiment of official authority — and its limitations. He arrives at the vicarage with a "triumphant" air, declaring the solution "plain as a pikestaff" (005-094) — and is wrong. Throughout the investigation, he latches onto one theory after another (Lawrence Redding, Mrs. Lestrange as blackmailer, Archer) without ever questioning his premises. His repeated failures highlight the contrast between professional procedure and Miss Marple's intuitive understanding.

## Background

Slack is "a dark man, restless and energetic in manner, with black eyes that snapped ceaselessly" (005-070). The narrator observes that "never did a man more determinedly strive to contradict his name" — Slack is anything but slack. He is competent within his limits: he collects evidence thoroughly, interviews witnesses systematically, and follows procedure correctly. His failure is not incompetence but imagination — he cannot see beyond the obvious.

## Key Actions

### The Immediate Theory

Slack arrives at the crime scene and immediately constructs a theory: Redding wrote the note, was interrupted by Protheroe, and shot him. "Plain as a pikestaff," he declares (005-094). The theory is logical, fits the physical evidence, and is completely wrong.

### The Blackmail Theory

When the Redding theory weakens, Slack pivots to Mrs. Lestrange as a blackmailer: "If I could only get definite proof of blackmail — but you can't get definite proof of anything in this crime! It's theory, theory, theory" (025-036). His frustration is palpable — and justified, since the truth lies outside his conceptual framework.

### The Trial

Slack is publicly credited with solving the case when Redding and Anne Protheroe are tried and acquitted. The irony — that the trial was a performance and Slack never identified the real killer — is left for the reader to appreciate.

## Relationships

- **Mr. Clement**: Slack's reluctant collaborator. Their relationship is marked by mutual exasperation: Slack finds the vicar evasive; Clement finds Slack obtuse.

- **Miss Marple**: Slack dismisses her entirely, representing the professional's blindness to amateur insight. He never consults her and would not credit her if he did.

- **Colonel Melchett**: Slack's superior, who occasionally restrains his enthusiasm but generally supports his conclusions.

## Character Analysis

Slack is not a fool — he is a competent policeman applying standard methods to a non-standard crime. His limitation is epistemological: he believes that crimes are solved by collecting evidence and constructing theories, whereas this particular crime requires understanding the complex web of human relationships in St. Mary Mead. His failure is the novel's argument for Miss Marple's method.
