# Miss Cram

Miss Cram is Dr. Stone's secretary, a "healthy young woman of twenty-five, noisy in manner, with a high colour, fine animal spirits, and a mouth that always seems to have more than its full share of teeth" (002-037). Despite her comic portrayal, she becomes one of only two people without an alibi for the critical time window of the murder.

## Role in the Story

Miss Cram is primarily a comic figure — the archetypal Christie "bright young thing" whose vulgar vitality contrasts with village respectability. But she acquires unexpected significance late in the investigation when the narrator's notes reveal that she and Mrs. Lestrange are "the only two people who have no kind of alibi for 6:30–6:35" (026-085). Her claim of being at the barrow is unconfirmed, making her a possible suspect — though the narrator dismisses her as having "nothing to connect her with it."

## Background

Miss Cram is employed as Dr. Stone's secretary, assisting with the barrow excavation. She is more interested in the social possibilities of village life than in archaeology, and her presence — a young unmarried woman working for an unmarried man — provides the village gossips with ample material.

## Key Actions

### The Alibi

Miss Cram claims to have been at the barrow at the time of the murder. The alibi is perfectly plausible — she is an archaeologist's assistant and the barrow is her workplace — but it cannot be verified. No one else was at the barrow, and there are no witnesses to confirm her presence.

### The Dismissal

Despite the lack of alibi, Miss Cram is dismissed as a suspect. The investigator's notes conclude that "it seems reasonable to dismiss her from the case as there seems nothing to connect her with it" (026-085). Her innocence, like Dr. Stone's, is a function of her irrelevance to the central drama.

## Relationships

- **Dr. Stone**: Her employer. Their relationship is professional, though village gossip might suggest otherwise.

- **Dennis**: The vicar's nephew, with whom she shares a certain youthful irreverence.

## Character Analysis

Miss Cram is a minor character who serves a structural function: she is one of the two people without an alibi, creating a binary choice that the investigation must resolve. Her dismissal — and the focus on Mrs. Lestrange — shapes the investigation's final direction, even though both women prove innocent.
