# Much Benham

Much Benham is the market town adjoining St. Mary Mead, situated two miles from the village. Larger and better equipped than the village, it provides the institutional infrastructure — police station, hospital, shops — that St. Mary Mead lacks, making it a regular destination for villagers and a key node in the investigation's geography [PN=005-069].

## Location

Much Benham lies two miles from St. Mary Mead, a short distance by car or bicycle. It is the nearest settlement of any size, functioning as the local market town for the surrounding villages [PN=013-002]. The proximity means that villagers regularly travel there for errands, shopping, and official business — a journey short enough to be made casually but far enough to create alibi questions during the investigation [PN=016-077].

## Description

Much Benham is described as "our adjacent town" and "our adjoining town," indicating it is the natural urban complement to rural St. Mary Mead [PN=018-004]. It possesses amenities the village lacks: a police station from which constables are dispatched [PN=022-058], a hospital to which the wounded are taken [PN=029-040], a bookshop frequented by the vicar [PN=032-041], and an authority on old silver whom the curate recommends [PN=025-018]. The town is substantial enough to sustain these specialised services yet small enough to be reached and returned from within an afternoon.

## Story Significance

Much Benham plays a crucial off-stage role in the investigation. It is the source of external authority — the police constable stationed outside the vicarage window comes from Much Benham [PN=022-058], and the hospital that receives the injured is located there [PN=029-040]. More subtly, it serves as an alibi location: Miss Protheroe's presence there at 5:30 on the day of the murder is a key temporal marker [PN=017-010]. The town thus functions as both a resource and a reference point — where characters go, where help comes from, and where timelines are anchored.

## Key Scenes

- **Police Deployment**: A constable from Much Benham is stationed on duty outside the vicarage window during the investigation [PN=022-058]
- **Medical Emergency**: After a character is injured, the immediate decision is to "run him into Much Benham — to the hospital there" [PN=029-040]
- **Alibi Timeline**: Miss Protheroe is reported to have been "over at Much Benham at 5:30" on the afternoon of the murder, providing a temporal checkpoint [PN=017-010]
- **Silver Appraisal**: The curate recommends "a very good man in Much Benham, an authority on old silver," suggesting the town's role as a centre of expertise [PN=025-018]
- **Bookshop Visit**: The vicar notes being "in the bookshop in Much Benham yesterday," indicating the town as a regular errand destination [PN=032-041]

## Residents / Frequent Visitors

Much Benham has no named permanent residents in the novel, though its police constables, hospital staff, and shopkeepers populate its institutions. Villagers from St. Mary Mead — the vicar, Miss Protheroe, the curate, and others — are frequent visitors for shopping, medical care, and official business.
