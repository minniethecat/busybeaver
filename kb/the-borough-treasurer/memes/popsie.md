# Popsie

Popsie is the barmaid at the Grey Mare inn in Highmarket, where she serves the local patrons and is known for her sharp tongue [PN=014-016].

## Role in the Novel

At the Grey Mare, Popsie leans over the bar reading a handbill about the five hundred pound reward offered for information about Kitely's murder. When the younger patrons ask her opinion on whose money it could be, she retorts with characteristic bluntness:

> "What's Popsie say? Why, what she says is that you're a lot of donkeys for wasting your time in wondering whose brass it is. What does it matter whose brass it is, so long as it's safe? What you want to do is to try and earn it. You don't pick up five hundred pounds every day!" [PN=014-018]

## Connection to Herbert Stoner

Herbert Stoner is a regular at the Grey Mare and usually takes a brief stroll there before bed. However, on the night the reward handbill circulates, Stoner forgets his customary visit entirely:

> For once in a way, Stoner took no walk abroad that night. Usually, even when he stopped in of an evening, he had a brief stroll to the Grey Mare and back last thing before going to bed. But on this occasion he forgot all about the Grey Mare, and Popsie the barmaid did not come into his mind for even a second. [PN=014-032]

The narrative notes this detail to underscore how distracted Stoner is by his thoughts that evening, as he sits at home lost in contemplation.

## Significance

Popsie serves as a minor but vivid character who represents the voice of the common townspeople. Her dismissive attitude toward the reward money reflects a practical, working-class perspective that contrasts with the ambition and greed driving the novel's central characters [PN=014-018], [PN=014-032].
