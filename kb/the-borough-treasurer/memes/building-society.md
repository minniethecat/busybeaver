# Wilchester Building Society

The financial institution at the center of the [[past|Wilchester affair]] — the building society whose funds [[Mallalieu]] and [[Cotherstone]] embezzled while serving as its treasurer and secretary.

## Significance

The building society represents the institutional context of the novel's corruption. As treasurer and secretary, [[Mallalieu]] and [[Cotherstone]] "had full [[power|control]]" of its funds (015-019), a concentration of [[power|authority]] that made fraud possible and detection difficult. The society's failure — or rather, the conspirators' exploitation of it — is the original crime from which all subsequent events flow.
