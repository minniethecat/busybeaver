# The Wilchester Affair

The original crime that casts its shadow over the entire novel — the [[fraud|embezzlement]] of building society funds by [[Mallalieu]] and [[Cotherstone]], known to [[James-Kitely|Kitely]] and used as the basis for blackmail years later.

## Narrative Significance

The [[past|Wilchester affair]] is the novel's foundational event — never directly depicted but constantly referenced. [[Mallalieu]] and [[Cotherstone]] served as treasurer and secretary of the building society (001-050), positions that gave them "full [[power|control]]" of its funds (015-019). Their fraud went undetected, and they relocated to [[High-Cross|High Cross]], where they rebuilt their lives on the foundation of their crime.

[[James-Kitely|Kitely]]'s knowledge of the affair transforms him from retired businessman to [[blackmail|blackmailer]]. His threat to expose "the [[past|Wilchester affair]]" (030-063) triggers the murder that drives the plot.
