# Mr. Leykin

Mr. Leykin is an ex-Scotland Yard detective who, with his partner Stobb, runs a private inquiry agency [PN=290-2906]. He is employed by Carfax to investigate Miss Pett's background, tasked with tracing her career in London.

## Role in the Novel

### Appearance and Demeanour

Leykin is described as "a very quiet, observant man who had listened with a sly expression on his face" [PN=293-2938]. Unlike his boisterous partner Stobb, Leykin is reserved and watchful, his sharpness concealed beneath an unassuming exterior.

### The London Investigation

While Stobb travels to Woking to investigate Miss Pett's connection to Major Stilman, Leykin "devoted himself to the London part of the business" [PN=291-2918]. His task is to trace Miss Pett's employment at two London hotels, investigating any suspicious patterns that emerge in her career history.

### The Dual Investigation

Together, Stobb and Leykin represent the two arms of Carfax's private inquiry: Stobb covering the Woking connection (Major Stilman's death) and Leykin covering the London connections [PN=292-2920]. This parallel investigation mirrors the structure of the official police inquiry while operating outside it.

### Gathering Evidence

Though Leykin speaks less in the novel than Stobb, his findings from London are presented alongside Stobb's Woking discoveries. The combined evidence from both agents establishes that Miss Pett's career follows a consistent pattern: she obtains positions with elderly, wealthy men who die under suspicious circumstances after leaving her money in recently-executed wills [PN=292–294].

## Significance

Leykin's quiet competence complements Stobb's cheerfully direct approach, and together their investigation provides the evidence that redirects suspicion from Harborough to Miss Pett. Leykin represents the methodical, detail-oriented detective whose silence at the table belies the thoroughness of his work.
