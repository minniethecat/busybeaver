# The Yard

**The Yard** is the business premises of Mallalieu & Cotherstone, Builders and Contractors, located on the north side of Highmarket's main street. Its ancient stone gateway serves as both a landmark and a boundary between the public street and the private business world of the two partners.

## Location & Appearance

The yard is situated halfway along the north side of Highmarket's main street. Its most distinctive feature is an "ancient stone gateway, imposing enough to suggest that it was originally the entrance to some castellated mansion or manor house."[001-002] A shining brass plate set into the wall just within the gateway bears the engraving: "Mallalieu and Cotherstone, Builders and Contractors."[001-002]

The yard itself is a working space where carts, building materials, and finished products are stored and displayed. The partners' office is located within the yard, and a clerk's station connects the office to the yard.[001-010]

## Business Function

As the operational centre of Mallalieu & Cotherstone's building and contracting business, the yard serves multiple functions:

- **Workshop & Display**: New carts and building materials are assembled and displayed in the yard. On one October afternoon, "half a dozen new carts, just finished" were "drawn up in all the glory of fresh paint and varnish."[001-003]
- **Supervision Point**: Both Mallalieu and Cotherstone, "keen business men who believed in constant supervision of their workmen, were accustomed to meet at the yard at half-past" every morning.[007-048]
- **Office**: The partners conduct business from their office within the yard, receiving letters, messengers, and visitors.[017-004][017-008]

## Role in the Narrative

The yard features prominently in several key scenes:

### Morning Meetings

Cotherstone was regularly first to arrive at the yard. On the morning following Stoner's death, "Cotherstone was in the yard when Mallalieu reached it,"[007-049] and the two partners discussed the unsettling events.

### Police Encounters

A policeman entered the yard from High Street and approached the partners, "touching his helmet"[007-060] — a gesture marking the official intrusion of the investigation into their business domain.

### Stoner's Confrontations

Herbert Stoner's confrontations with the partners took place in and around the yard. A witness noted being "at thirty yards off" and observing that "Stoner was maddening Mallalieu," though they "couldn't distinguish precise words."[020-053]

### Public Scrutiny

The yard's position on the main street makes it a point of public observation. Mallalieu, exiting the yard into High Street, "ran against Bent and Brereton, who were hurrying in the direction" of an investigation scene.[008-005] The yard's gateway thus marks a threshold between the partners' private business and the public scrutiny that increasingly surrounds them.

## Significance

The yard embodies the dual identity of Mallalieu & Cotherstone: the imposing ancient stone gateway conveys respectability and permanence, while the working yard behind it conceals the secrets and crimes that the partners have built their success upon. The gateway — ancient, solid, and suggestive of a "castellated mansion" — stands in ironic contrast to the fragile respectability of the modern business conducted behind it.
