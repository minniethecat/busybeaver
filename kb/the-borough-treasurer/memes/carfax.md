# H. C. Carfax

H. C. Carfax is a short, middle-aged London solicitor, "quick and bustling in manner," who plays a crucial behind-the-scenes role in clearing John Harborough of the charge of murdering Kitely [PN=027-023]. He is the principal of the firm Carfax and Spillington, with Willerby & Hargreaves of Norcaster acting as his local agents [PN=027-016].

## Role in the Novel

### Summoning Brereton

Carfax sends a note to Brereton, summoning him to Norcaster with Miss Harborough — stating that his business is "closely connected" with Brereton's own [PN=027-023]. He arranges a car for immediate transport and awaits them at the Duke's Head hotel in Norcaster marketplace. The messenger who delivers the note explains that "Carfax and Spillington are our London agents" and that "Mr. Carfax would rather tell you everything himself" [PN=027-018].

### The Private Investigation

When John Wraythwaite learns of the murder charge against Harborough, he goes not to Highmarket but to Carfax in London. Carfax advises Wraythwaite to let matters rest temporarily while they finish "his own affair," and subsequently employs two inquiry agents — Stobb and Leykin, both ex-Scotland Yard men now in private practice [PN=027-044].

### Coordinating the Defence

Carfax oversees the parallel investigation into Miss Pett, directing Stobb to Woking (where Miss Pett served as housekeeper to Major Stilman) and Leykin to London. He brings Wraythwaite to Norcaster to meet Brereton and Avice, orchestrating the gathering where evidence of Harborough's complete alibi is revealed [PN=028-003]. Carfax pulls out a memorandum book and lays out the facts in methodical order — "let us proceed in order" [PN=028-009].

### The Revelation

At the Duke's Head gathering, Carfax with theatrical enjoyment reveals piece by piece the evidence that will exonerate Harborough. His dramatic presentation has "the enjoyment of the situation," drawing out each disclosure for maximum effect [PN=027-039]. He announces that Harborough "knows" — referring to the truth about Miss Pett's criminal past [PN=028-007].

## Character & Traits

Carfax is described as "a short, middle-aged man, quick and bustling in manner" who "hurried forward" to shake hands [PN=027-023]. He displays a marked theatrical flair, repeatedly "rubbing his hands as if in enjoyment of the situation" [PN=027-032]. He is good-humoured and patient under pressure — when Brereton presses him to get to the point, Carfax retorts "good-humouredly" that he is "coming to it" [PN=027-039]. He shows a keen appreciation for strong evidence, exclaiming "Capital—capital!" when Brereton provides a credible alibi witness [PN=027-041].

His manner is precise and methodical — he "pulled out a memorandum book" and insists on proceeding "in order" [PN=028-010]. He takes evident satisfaction in building a compelling case through accumulated detail rather than sweeping statements [PN=028-013].

## Key Relationships

- **[[brereton|Brereton]]** — the Norcaster solicitor whom Carfax summons with Miss Harborough; Brereton serves as the local legal liaison [PN=027-022]
- **[[wraythwaite|John Wraythwaite]]** — the wealthy London businessman who goes directly to Carfax upon learning of Harborough's arrest; Carfax acts on his instructions [PN=027-044]
- **[[avice-harborough|Avice Harborough]]** — Harborough's daughter, brought to Norcaster by Brereton at Carfax's direction; Carfax addresses her as "young lady" with avuncular warmth [PN=027-030]
- **[[stobb|Mr. Stobb]]** and **[[leykin|Mr. Leykin]]** — the two ex-Scotland Yard inquiry agents whom Carfax employs to investigate Miss Pett's background [PN=028-020]
- **[[harborough|John Harborough]]** — Carfax's ultimate client, though they never meet on the page; Carfax's coordinated investigation secures his exoneration

## Key Actions

1. **Summoned Brereton and Avice to Norcaster** — initiated the defence coordination by drawing the key figures together [PN=027-013]
2. **Employed Stobb and Leykin** — engaged two ex-Scotland Yard inquiry agents to conduct the parallel investigation into Miss Pett [PN=027-044]
3. **Orchestrated the evidence reveal** — at the Duke's Head hotel, methodically laid out the alibi evidence and the findings about Miss Pett's criminal past [PN=028-009]
4. **Coordinated between London and Norcaster** — bridged the gap between Wraythwaite's London-based legal resources and the local case unfolding in Highmarket and Norcaster [PN=027-016]

## Involvement in the Crime

Carfax has no involvement in Kitely's murder. His role is entirely on the defence side — he constructs the alibi that will clear Harborough and, in the process, uncovers evidence that points toward Miss Pett as a serial poisoner. His parallel investigation, conducted through Stobb and Leykin, diverts the inquiry from Harborough to the true criminal.

## Significance

Carfax represents the intervention of London legal expertise in what began as a local murder case. His coordination of Wraythwaite's alibi evidence and the parallel investigation into Miss Pett — which ultimately unmasks her as a serial poisoner — is instrumental in both saving Harborough and exposing the true criminal. As a character, he embodies the novel's thematic interest in the contrast between provincial Highmarket and metropolitan London, demonstrating that justice in the provinces ultimately depends on resources and expertise from the capital.
