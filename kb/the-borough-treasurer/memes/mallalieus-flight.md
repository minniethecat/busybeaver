## Overview

[[Mallalieu]]'s planned flight, depicted in Chapter XXI ("The Interrupted Flight"), represents the culmination of his survival instinct. Having killed [[herbert-stoner|Stoner]] to protect his secret — and now facing the unraveling of his entire masquerade — [[Mallalieu]] prepares to abandon [[Highmarket]], his mayoralty, and his partnership with [[Cotherstone]]. His plan is [[reputation|character]]istically cunning: he will leave openly on Corporation business, providing a legitimate-seeming reason for departure.

## Planning the Flight

### The Decision

Twenty-four hours after watching [[herbert-stoner|Stoner]] fall into [[hobwick-quarry|Hobwick Quarry]], [[Mallalieu]] reaches a definitive conclusion:

> "Twenty-four hours after he had seen Stoner fall headlong into Hobwick Quarry, Mallalieu made up his mind for flight. And as soon as he had come to that moment of definite decision, he proceeded to arrange for his disappearance with all the craft and subtlety of which he was a past master." [021-002]

His determination is absolute: "He would go, once and for all, and since he was to go he would go in such a fashion that nobody should be able to trace him" [021-002].

### The Pretext

[[Mallalieu]]'s genius for deception is fully displayed in his exit strategy. The [[Highmarket]] Town Council is debating a new water-supply scheme involving amalgamation with a neighbouring town. [[Mallalieu]], presiding over the committee, will announce his intention to visit the other town in person:

> "He would profess that he was not exactly pleased with some of the provisions of the proposed amalgamation, and would state his intention, in open meeting, of going over in person to the other town that very evening to see its authorities on the points whereon he was not satisfied." [021-013]

The route to this town passes through [[Norcaster]] — a large city where [[Mallalieu]] "could enter on the first important stage of his flight" [021-013].

### The Preparation

[[Mallalieu]] has been ready for this moment since [[James-Kitely|Kitely]] first revealed his knowledge. He carries a specially-designed waistcoat with hidden pockets containing:

- Government stock, railway stock, scrip, shares — "all easily convertible, anywhere in the world" [021-015]
- A wad of banknotes secured from a [[london|London]] bank
- A wash-leather pocket lined with gold coins

> "If one man could find all that out, he argued, half a dozen other men might find it out." [021-015]

This premeditated preparation reveals [[Mallalieu]]'s calculating nature — he has been ready to flee since the day of [[murder|Kitely's murder]].

## The Interruption

Despite his careful planning, [[Mallalieu]]'s flight is interrupted. The chapter title — "The Interrupted Flight" — signals that even his best-laid schemes cannot outrun fate. The interruption comes from the accelerating police investigation: [[Brereton]] and [[tallington|Tallington]]'s discoveries close the net before [[Mallalieu]] can execute his escape.

## Character Insight

[[Mallalieu]]'s flight planning epitomises his [[reputation|character]]: meticulous, cunning, utterly self-interested. He abandons [[Cotherstone]] without a backward glance. The thirty-year partnership — built on shared crime, shared secrecy, shared prosperity — dissolves in an instant when self-preservation demands it.
