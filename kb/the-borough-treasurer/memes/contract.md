# Contract

**Contracts** — particularly municipal building contracts — form the economic foundation of Mallalieu & Cotherstone's business and are central to the conspiracy that drives the plot.

## Business Foundation

Mallalieu & Cotherstone are identified on their brass plate as "Builders and Contractors,"[001-002] and their daily operations involve active contract management. A typical morning sees Cotherstone attending to "these three contracts to go through"[002-011] alongside correspondence and specifications.

## Source of Wealth

The partners' prosperity is directly attributed to their contract work. They had been "making money for years, and of late certain highly successful and profitable contracts had increased their wealth in a surprising fashion."[016-004] The narrative emphasizes their charmed record: "Every contract they had taken up had turned out a gold mine."[016-004]

## The Hidden Crime

The partners' extraordinary success with contracts conceals a darker truth: their original conspiracy involved the fraudulent acquisition of contract funds. The borough treasurer position gave them access to municipal contracting, and the original crime that bound Mallalieu and Cotherstone together was financial — connected to public contracts and the misuse of borough funds. Their legitimate business success is built on a foundation of contract fraud.

## Significance

Contracts in the novel function as both the legitimate face of Mallalieu & Cotherstone's enterprise and the buried origin of their criminal partnership. The "gold mine" of successful contracts[016-004] is the visible result of a hidden crime — a pattern of appearance versus reality that runs throughout the novel.
