# David Myler

A commercial traveller based in Darlington and an old friend of [[herbert-stoner|Herbert Stoner]], who provides counsel and a sounding-board during the early investigation into the murder of [[James-Kitely|James Kitely]].

## Role in the Story

David Myler is introduced as an old friend of Herbert Stoner, living in Darlington where he has "set up housekeeping" [015-002]. When Stoner arrives in Norcaster to investigate the Kitely murder, he immediately seeks out Myler, who welcomes him joyously [015-003]. Myler serves as a confidant and adviser to Stoner throughout the early chapters, offering practical, commercially-minded counsel [015-035] and a place for Stoner to discuss his theories [015-006].

## Character & Traits

Myler is portrayed as a genial, business-minded commercial traveller with a practical outlook on life. He is hospitable, immediately providing his guests with refreshments [015-008], and brings "all his business acumen" to bear on problems [015-030]. He is also impressionable — "immediately impressed" by Stoner's theories [015-027] — and has a cautious side, evidenced by his disapproval of certain courses of action [015-038].

> "Then if it was me," said Myler, "I should make a summary of everything that's known and everything that's come out, and read it carefully through. That's what I should do in a business matter." [015-035]

## Key Relationships

- **[[herbert-stoner|Herbert Stoner]]**: Bosom friends since boyhood [015-036]. Myler acts as Stoner's anchor in Norcaster, providing lodging and advice [015-006].
- **Myler's father-in-law**: Mentioned in passing during Stoner's visit [015-007], suggesting Myler is married and settled in Darlington.

## Key Actions

- **Welcomes Stoner to Norcaster**: Receives Stoner joyously upon his arrival, offering hospitality and a sympathetic ear [015-003] [015-005]
- **Provides business-minded counsel**: Advises Stoner to compile a systematic summary of the case facts, treating the murder investigation with commercial discipline [015-035]
- **Serves as sounding-board**: Throughout the early investigation, Myler listens to Stoner's theories and provides feedback, though he ultimately "disapproved" of some of Stoner's conclusions [015-038]
- **Accompanies Stoner**: Travels with Stoner on at least one occasion, along with "two Norcaster men" [021-018]

## Involvement in the Crime

Myler has no direct involvement in the Kitely murder or the borough treasurer conspiracy. His role is entirely as a supporting figure — providing Stoner with a home base, companionship, and practical counsel during the investigation. His commercial traveller background and outsider perspective offer a contrast to the insular world of Highmarket politics [015-010].

## Significance

Myler's narrative function is to ground the amateur detective Stoner, providing a "normal" perspective against which Stoner's deductions can be tested. As a commercial traveller — a man accustomed to evaluating business propositions and assessing risk — Myler represents a practical, no-nonsense foil to Stoner's more speculative investigative approach [015-035]. His friendship with Stoner also humanizes the detective figure, showing Stoner's need for companionship and validation [015-036].
