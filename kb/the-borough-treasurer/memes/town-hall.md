# Highmarket Town Hall

An "old-fashioned building" [007-022] at the centre of Highmarket, housing the council chamber, the borough court, the mayor's private room, and the police station. The Town Hall is the stage upon which both municipal governance and criminal justice unfold — a building where [[Mallalieu]] presides as mayor in one room and faces trial as defendant in another.

## Geography

The Town Hall is centrally located in Highmarket, with the town's single bank situated in an "old-fashioned building near the Town Hall" [007-022]. Its "ancient door" [021-018] speaks to its age and institutional weight. The building's layout — with interconnected rooms for council business, police operations, and the court — makes it a microcosm of Highmarket's intertwined civic and judicial life [022-028] [022-029].

## Description in the Novel

Fletcher portrays the Town Hall through its function rather than its architecture. It is a building of "ins-and-outs" [022-028] whose "geographical situation" proves tactically significant [022-029]. The private rooms, the court, and the police quarters exist in close proximity, enabling the rapid shifts between administrative routine and criminal crisis that drive the plot.

> All the ins-and-outs of that old Town Hall were familiar to Brereton. And as soon as the superintendent had gone, the geographical situation of the Town Hall was a great help. [022-028] [022-029]

## Key Events Here

- **Mallalieu's mayoral duties**: Mallalieu presides at a meeting of the Town Council on the evening of the murder investigation [017-018]
- **Brereton's strategic positioning**: Brereton uses his knowledge of the Town Hall's layout to navigate between the police station, the private rooms, and the court [022-028] [022-029]
- **The trial**: The court within the Town Hall is "packed to the doors" for the proceedings [025-031], with the public peering through the windows at the "Mayor of Highmarket" in the dock [022-004]
- **Mallalieu's confinement**: After his arrest, Mallalieu is held "under lock and key, in a room in the Town Hall — most of the time alone" [022-002]
- **Cotherstone's council business**: As Borough Treasurer, Cotherstone conducts financial matters at the Town Hall, part of the municipal routine that masks his criminal past [017-018]

## Narrative Atmosphere

The Town Hall embodies the central irony of the novel: the building that represents civic order and respectability is also the site where that order unravels. Mallalieu moves from presiding in the council chamber [017-018] to being confined in a private room [022-002] to standing trial in the court [025-031] — all within the same ancient walls. The Town Hall's "packed" courtroom [025-031] and the public straining at its windows [022-004] turn the building into a theatre of exposure, where the carefully maintained facades of Highmarket's ruling class are publicly dismantled.
