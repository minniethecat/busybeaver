# Councillor Northrop

Councillor Northrop is a magistrate and town councillor in Highmarket, described as a "shrewd-faced little man" who plays an important supporting role in the novel's events [PN=008-055]. His home — the Northrop villa — serves as a protective refuge for Avice Harborough during her father's imprisonment and as a meeting place for those working to clear Harborough's name.

## Role in the Novel

### The Night of the Murder

On the night James Kitely is murdered, Mallalieu is at Northrop's house playing cards. Mallalieu reports: "I've just been having a hand at whist with Councillor Northrop and his wife and daughter" [PN=006-040]. This establishes Mallalieu's alibi during part of the evening, though the timing — Mallalieu arrived at Northrop's at ten o'clock — leaves the earlier part of the evening unaccounted for [PN=010-022]. Northrop's own walk through the Shawl woods at around a quarter to ten, during which he heard "not so much as a dead twig snap," provides timeline evidence for the investigation [PN=008-065].

### Supporting Avice Harborough

When John Harborough is arrested, Northrop accompanies Avice Harborough (who works as governess to his children) to the police station, offering his influence as a magistrate [PN=008-047]. Mrs. Northrop insists on Avice staying with them: "I'm going to stay with Mrs. Northrop. They've insisted on it" [PN=009-082]. The Northrop household becomes a sanctuary for Avice, and Brereton's visits to the Northrop villa — meeting Avice in "Mrs. Northrop's drawing-room" — become a recurring locus for the defence effort [PN=013-031].

### Questioning the Official Case

Northrop is openly skeptical of the police's case against Harborough, directing his criticism at Superintendent Lucas's single-minded focus:

> "You know what police are, Mr. Bent — if they get hold of one notion they're deaf to all others. While they're concentrating on Harborough, you know, the real man'll be going free — laughing in his sleeve, very like." [PN=008-055]

He endorses Bent's call for open-minded investigation, exclaiming "Well spoken, sir! That's the style!" [PN=008-059].

### View of Cotherstone

When Brereton questions him about Cotherstone, Northrop provides a rich character sketch:

> "He's a queer chap — a mystery man, sir. A sort of jack-of-all-trades. He's a better sort — you'd say, to hear him talk, he'd been a gentleman. You can see what his daughter is — he educated her well. He's means of some sort — apart from what he earns. Yes, there's some mystery about that man, sir — but I'll never believe he did this job. No, sir!" [PN=008-063]

This assessment, coming from a fellow town councillor, provides the novel's most detailed local perspective on Cotherstone. Northrop reinforces the atmosphere of secrecy surrounding Cotherstone while firmly ruling him out as the murderer: "All mystery, sir!" [PN=008-065].

## Character & Traits

Northrop is a "shrewd-faced little man" — the adjective suggests intelligence and sharp judgment rather than mere cunning [PN=008-055]. He is fair-minded and independent, unafraid to question official police conclusions despite being a magistrate himself. He speaks with conviction, as when he endorses Bent: "Well spoken, sir! That's the style!" [PN=008-059]. His willingness to employ Avice Harborough as governess and to shelter her during the crisis demonstrates both kindness and moral courage.

## Key Actions

1. **Hosted Mallalieu at cards** — provided part of Mallalieu's alibi for the night of the murder [PN=006-040]
2. **Escorted Avice to the police station** — used his standing as magistrate to support her after her father's arrest [PN=008-047]
3. **Provided the Shawl woods timeline** — walked through the woods around 9:45 PM and heard nothing, establishing a crucial timeline point [PN=008-065]
4. **Challenged the police's single-minded focus** — publicly questioned the case against Harborough while the real murderer went free [PN=008-055]
5. **Gave the key character sketch of Cotherstone** — his description of Cotherstone as a "mystery man" shapes Brereton's understanding of the town's secrets [PN=008-063]

## Involvement in the Crime

Northrop has no involvement in Kitely's murder. His role is as a civic authority figure who provides timeline evidence (his walk through Shawl woods) and, more importantly, serves as an independent voice questioning the official narrative. His perspective as a fellow councillor gives weight to his description of Cotherstone — a description that both deepens the novel's mystery and subtly directs suspicion away from the obvious suspect.

## Significance

Northrop represents the respectable civic authority that nevertheless questions official police conclusions. His employment of Avice Harborough as governess places him at the intersection of several key plot threads — the murder investigation, Harborough's defence, and the private lives of Highmarket's leading citizens. His richly detailed character sketch of Cotherstone provides the novel's most textured local perspective on a central figure, and his own home becomes a sanctuary where the defence effort takes shape.
