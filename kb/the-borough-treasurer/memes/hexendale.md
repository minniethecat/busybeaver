# Hexendale

A valley settlement on the far side of the moorlands beyond [[Highmarket]], comprising both a village and a railway station — significant as the direction in which [[Mallalieu]] and [[Stoner]] were last seen walking together, and as a potential escape route considered by [[Cotherstone]] when his guilt is exposed.

## Geography

Hexendale lies "over yonder" (019-049) from [[Highmarket]], on the far side of the moor. [[Avice Harborough]] knows the way there "well enough" (019-049), suggesting it is a familiar landmark to those who live on the moorland edges. Fletcher describes it succinctly:

> Hexendale is a valley — with a village of the same name in it. (019-050)

The presence of a railway station at Hexendale (027-039) makes it a node in the region's transport network — "Hexendale railway station" is where [[Mallalieu]] was last seen before his disappearance, and where [[Brereton]]'s investigation traces his movements. The station connects the remote moorland settlement to the wider railway system that runs through [[Norcaster]] and [[Darlington]].

## Description in the Novel

Hexendale receives little direct description — it is defined less by what it is than by what it represents: a point of departure. When [[Mallalieu]] and [[Stoner]] walk "off across the moor towards Hexendale" (019-037), they are heading away from [[Highmarket]] and toward the railway that can take them out of the district entirely.

> They went away in the direction of Hexendale. (019-047)

This directionality — toward Hexendale, away from Highmarket — is loaded with narrative meaning. [[Highmarket]] is the town of judgement and consequence; Hexendale is escape, a place one passes through rather than arrives at. [[Brereton]]'s question — "Where is Hexendale?" (019-047) — underscores his unfamiliarity with the local geography, but also signals that the investigation must now follow its quarry beyond the known boundaries of the town.

For [[Cotherstone]], Hexendale figures in his desperate calculus of flight: "Norcaster and Hexendale? Is there any way to either of them?" (023-010). The valley represents a possible route out of the trap closing around him.

## Key Events Here

1. **The last sighting of Mallalieu and Stoner** (019-037, 019-047): Witnesses report seeing [[Mallalieu]] and [[Stoner]] walking "off across the moor towards Hexendale" (019-037). This is the last confirmed sighting of both men together before [[Stoner]]'s death.

2. **Avice Harborough's testimony** (019-049–050): [[Avice Harborough]] identifies Hexendale to [[Brereton]] — "Miss there knows Hexendale well enough" — and provides the geographical description that anchors the village in the reader's mental map of the district.

3. **The railway station connection** (027-039): [[Mallalieu]] parted from his companion "near Hexendale railway station" (027-039), establishing the station as a key point in tracing his movements.

4. **Cotherstone's escape calculus** (023-010): As [[Cotherstone]] contemplates flight, he considers "Norcaster and Hexendale" as possible destinations, weighing which route offers better chances of escape.

## Associated Characters

- **[[Mallalieu]]**: Last seen walking toward Hexendale before his disappearance; parted from his companion at Hexendale railway station
- **[[Stoner]]**: Accompanied [[Mallalieu]] on the walk toward Hexendale — the last person to see him before his death
- **[[Avice Harborough]]**: Knows the Hexendale route well, providing geographical intelligence to [[Brereton]]
- **[[Brereton]]**: Traces the movements of [[Mallalieu]] through Hexendale as part of the widening investigation
- **[[Cotherstone]]**: Considers Hexendale as an escape route when his crimes are exposed

## Narrative Atmosphere

Hexendale is a place of transit, not destination — a valley one passes through on the way to somewhere else. Its railway station gives it a tenuous connection to the wider world, but it remains a liminal space in the novel's geography: neither the corrupt respectability of [[Highmarket]] nor the wild isolation of [[the-moor|the moor]]. It is the last place [[Mallalieu]] was seen before vanishing, the point at which the novel's geography opens outward and the quarry slips beyond the investigator's immediate reach.
