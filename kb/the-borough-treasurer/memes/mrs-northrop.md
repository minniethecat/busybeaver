# Mrs. Northrop

Mrs. Northrop is the wife of Councillor Northrop of Highmarket. Though never directly quoted in the novel, her household — the Northrop villa — functions as a critical safe space and operational centre for those working to clear John Harborough's name. Her acts of quiet generosity and moral courage provide essential support to the novel's protagonists.

## Role in the Novel

### Shelter for Avice

When John Harborough is arrested and Avice is left alone at the cottage on the Shawl, Mrs. Northrop immediately and insistently offers refuge. Avice tells Brereton: "I'm going to stay with Mrs. Northrop. They've insisted on it" [PN=009-082]. The word "insisted" signals the Northrops' active moral commitment — they do not merely offer shelter but press it upon a young woman in crisis.

### The Northrop Drawing-Room

Mrs. Northrop's drawing-room becomes the novel's recurring space for strategic meetings and emotional support. It is here that Brereton meets Avice after her father's arrest, observing that "she was looking worn and pale" [PN=013-031]. In this same drawing-room, Brereton urges Avice to join the effort to clear her father and discusses the reward offered for information [PN=013-042].

### A Base of Operations

The Northrop villa serves as a logistical hub. Brereton, "walking across to the Northrop villa," uses it as the first point of contact whenever he needs to reach Avice [PN=013-030]. When Brereton returns to Highmarket later in the novel, he makes the Northrop villa his first destination, underscoring its function as a base for the defence network [PN=027-015]. Avice herself acknowledges their kindness: "The Northrops are very kind — they'll understand" [PN=013-047].

### Later Appearances

Harborough is seen "coming out of the gate of Northrop's garden" — confirming that the Northrop villa remains a centre of activity and reunion throughout the crisis [PN=018-019]. When Brereton runs "into Northrop's house" to fetch Avice for the journey to Norcaster, the villa is depicted as a natural and trusted waypoint in the characters' movements [PN=027-019].

## Character & Traits

Mrs. Northrop is revealed entirely through action rather than description or dialogue. Her insistence on sheltering Avice suggests warmth and moral conviction [PN=009-082]. The drawing-room setting — a formal space made available for private, urgent conversations — implies generosity that extends beyond manners into genuine care [PN=013-031]. Her husband's own forthright character ("a shrewd-faced little man" who openly challenges the police) suggests a household of independent-minded civic conscience, and Mrs. Northrop's quiet hospitality is the domestic expression of that same spirit.

## Key Relationships

- **[[northrop|Councillor Northrop]]** — her husband, a magistrate who openly questions the police case against Harborough; together they form a household of civic conscience [PN=006-040]
- **[[avice-harborough|Avice Harborough]]** — governess to the Northrop children, sheltered by Mrs. Northrop during the crisis; the relationship is both professional and personal, with Avice describing the Northrops as "very kind" [PN=013-047]
- **[[brereton|Brereton]]** — the solicitor who uses the Northrop drawing-room as a base for coordinating Harborough's defence; Mrs. Northrop's hospitality enables the legal strategy [PN=013-031]
- **[[mallalieu|Mallalieu]]** — social acquaintance who played cards at the Northrop house on the night of the murder [PN=006-040]

## Key Actions

1. **Insisted on sheltering Avice** — immediately after Harborough's arrest, Mrs. Northrop pressed Avice to stay with them, providing refuge and moral support [PN=009-082]
2. **Made her drawing-room available for defence meetings** — Brereton's strategic conversations with Avice take place in Mrs. Northrop's drawing-room, a private space opened to an urgent cause [PN=013-031]
3. **Hosted Mallalieu at cards on the night of the murder** — her household was the scene of Mallalieu's whist game, placing it within the timeline of the murder night [PN=006-040]
4. **Provided a stable base throughout the crisis** — the Northrop villa serves as a recurring point of contact, reunion, and departure throughout the novel's events [PN=027-015]

## Involvement in the Crime

Mrs. Northrop has no involvement in Kitely's murder. Her household inadvertently provides part of the timeline of the murder night — Mallalieu's card game — but her own role is purely that of a moral supporter and provider of refuge to Avice Harborough during her father's imprisonment.

## Significance

Though a minor and mostly silent character, Mrs. Northrop represents the quiet infrastructure of decency in Highmarket — the domestic generosity that enables the novel's protagonists to act. Without her insistence on sheltering Avice and making her home available for meetings, the defence effort would lack a secure base. She embodies the novel's implicit argument that justice depends not only on legal expertise (Carfax, Brereton) and independent voices (Northrop, Bent) but also on the everyday kindness of those who open their doors at moments of crisis.
