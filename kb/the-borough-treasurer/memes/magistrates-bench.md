# Highmarket Magistrates' Bench

The judicial body that presides over the preliminary hearings in the murder case — the "bench of his fellow-[[trial|magistrates]]" (022-002) before whom the accused are brought.

## Significance

The [[trial|magistrates]]' bench represents institutional justice in the novel. It is before this body that [[harborough|Harborough]] is first examined (013-002) and the conspirators are eventually committed for trial (022-002). The irony of [[Mallalieu|the Mayor]], as "chief magistrate" (006-064), being both the highest judicial [[power|authority]] and an implicated party underscores the novel's critique of concentrated municipal power.
