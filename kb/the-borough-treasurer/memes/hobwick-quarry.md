# Hobwick Quarry

A remote quarry "up on the moor" [017-021] above Highmarket, where [[herbert-stoner|Herbert Stoner]]'s body is discovered after a fatal fall. The quarry becomes the site of the novel's second violent death, transforming the investigation from a single murder into a pattern of killings connected to the borough treasurer conspiracy.

## Geography

Hobwick Quarry sits on the moorland high above Highmarket — the same wild landscape that the Shawl borders below. It is sufficiently remote that it takes some time for the body to be discovered [017-021]. The quarry is surrounded by a spinney — a small wood — that provides cover for anyone observing the site [025-024]. The location's isolation makes it both a plausible accident site and a convenient place for a murder made to look like one.

## Description in the Novel

Fletcher describes Hobwick Quarry as a "lonely place on the moors" [018-023]. Its remoteness is emphasized by the timeline — Stoner would have reached it "by 4:30 at the latest" [017-052] on the afternoon of his death, hours before anyone came looking. The quarry's physical features — its drop, its surrounding spinney, its distance from habitation — make it a fitting backdrop for the novel's turn toward darker violence.

> The body was found an hour or so ago in Hobwick Quarry, up on the moor, and it's been identified as that of the man Stoner. [017-021]

## Key Events Here

- **Stoner's fatal fall**: Stoner falls — or is pushed — headlong into Hobwick Quarry [021-002], suffering fatal injuries
- **Discovery of the body**: A search party locates Stoner's body "an hour or so ago" in the quarry [017-021]
- **Mallalieu's presence**: Mallalieu is observed at the spinney overhanging Hobwick Quarry, watching Stoner before the fall [025-024]
- **Mallalieu's return**: After the death, Mallalieu "had gone away to Hobwick Quarry and taken a careful look round" [021-003], ostensibly to cover his tracks
- **The "Hobwick Quarry murder"**: The second death is quickly linked to the first, with investigators asking "if the Hobwick Quarry murder had anything to do with" the Kitely case [022-003]

## Associated Characters

- **[[herbert-stoner|Herbert Stoner]]**: The victim, whose investigation into the Kitely murder leads to his own death at the quarry
- **[[Mallalieu]]**: Observed at the quarry before and after Stoner's fall; the primary suspect in what becomes known as the Hobwick Quarry murder
- **[[Brereton]]**: Investigator probing the connections between the quarry death and the Kitely murder
- **[[Cotherstone]]**: Implicated through his partnership with Mallalieu and his knowledge of the quarry incident

## Narrative Atmosphere

Hobwick Quarry represents the novel's escalation from municipal corruption to violent elimination. Where the Shawl is a place of discovery — Kitely's body found, evidence uncovered — Hobwick Quarry is a place of active violence, where Stoner's pursuit of truth is met with lethal force. The quarry's position "up on the moor" [017-021] places it beyond the reach of Highmarket's civic institutions, in a lawless space where the respectable criminals of the town can act without witnesses — or so they believe.
