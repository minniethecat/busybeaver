# Dr. Rockcliffe

Dr. Rockcliffe is the police surgeon in Highmarket who serves as a medical examiner in the investigation of James Kitely's murder [PN=004-048].

## Role in the Investigation

### At Kitely's Cottage

Dr. Rockcliffe is present at the scene of Kitely's murder when Brereton and the others arrive. He examines Kitely's body in the bedroom of the cottage, with the police-sergeant bending over the body beside him:

> looking round still further he became aware of an open door—the door into which Miss Pett had withdrawn—and of a bed within on which Kitely now lay, with Dr. Rockcliffe and the police-sergeant bending over him. [PN=005-009]

Brereton's companion Bent mentions that Dr. Rockcliffe made a significant observation at the scene:

> "Did you hear what Dr. Rockcliffe said?—that whoever had done it was familiar with that sort of thing!" [PN=004-048]

This remark suggests the murderer had a methodical, practiced approach — a medical opinion that carries weight in the investigation.

### The Quarry Fall

Later in the novel, Dr. Rockcliffe is called to attend Herbert Stoner after Stoner's body is discovered at the bottom of a quarry. The superintendent reports:

> "It looks as if it had been an accident... It's fifty feet deep, Mr. Mayor! That's all one can think of. But Dr. Rockcliffe's with him now." [PN=017-024]

When questioned, Rockcliffe's expression becomes telling:

> Dr. Rockcliffe pursed up his lips and his face became inscrutable. He kept silence for a moment—when he spoke his voice was unusually stern. [PN=017-027]

His reaction suggests he has observed something suspicious about Stoner's death that goes beyond a simple accident.

## Significance

Dr. Rockcliffe serves as a minor but important figure whose medical expertise provides the investigation with clinical observations. His inscrutable expression at the quarry scene hints at foul play, contributing to the novel's atmosphere of suspicion [PN=017-027].
