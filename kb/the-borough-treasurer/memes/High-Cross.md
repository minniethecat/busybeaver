# High Cross

The "ancient borough" (001-054) that serves as the novel's primary setting — a town of municipal pride and hidden corruption in northern England.

## Significance

High Cross is not merely backdrop but character — its institutions, its social hierarchies, and its deference to established authority create the conditions in which corruption flourishes. The Mayor governs as "chief magistrate" (006-064); the borough records contain the documentary evidence of past misdeeds (003-018).
