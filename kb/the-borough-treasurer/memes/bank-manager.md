# the bank manager

The bank manager is the head of "the one bank of the little town" of Highmarket, an "old-fashioned building near the Town Hall" where he both lives and works [PN=007-022]. Though unnamed, he is a distinct recurring witness whose evidence is central to the case against Harborough.

## Role in the Novel

### Roused at Midnight

On the night of Kitely's murder, Mallalieu hurries to the bank-house and rings the bell loudly — the sound "seemed to wake innumerable echoes" in the silent street [PN=007-019]. The bank manager comes down in his "dressing-gown and slippers," standing aghast when Mallalieu explains his errand. He is described as a "startled bank-manager, hurrying down to his midnight visitor, blinking in the sudden light" [PN=007-023].

### Kitely's Financial Activity

The bank manager reveals that Kitely had an account, and that his quarterly pension payment had been deposited that very morning. More significantly, he discloses that Kitely had been "an ex-detective" with nearly forty years' service in the police force — a revelation that immediately reorients the investigation [PN=007-026]. The manager attended to Kitely personally when he came to draw money around noon that day [PN=007-024].

### Harborough at the Counter

The bank manager provides a crucial piece of evidence: "Harborough was at the counter when Kitely took his money" [PN=007-030]. He states that Harborough had come in to change a five-pound note and stood beside Kitely during the transaction [PN=007-030]. The manager confirms he "couldn't fail" to notice Harborough standing by Kitely [PN=007-034]. This places Harborough in direct proximity to Kitely on the day of the murder and provides a potential motive.

### The Money Trail

The manager specifies that he gave Kitely "the one half of the money in gold" — notes and gold coins — and that Kitely placed them "in a pocket that he had inside his waistcoat." He records the numbers of the notes given to Kitely, noting they "may be useful if there's any attempt to change any note" [PN=007-036]. The banknotes Kitely received were later found during the search of his lodgings [PN=010-028].

### At the Magistrates' Court

When Harborough is brought before the magistrates, the bank manager is called as a prosecution witness — "amongst them the bank-manager" [PN=009-003]. He testifies about cashing Kitely's draft in Harborough's presence and giving half the money in gold. A constable then follows him in the witness box, testifying that over twenty pounds in gold was found on Harborough at his arrest — evidence directly linked to the bank manager's testimony.

## Character & Traits

The bank manager is portrayed as a diligent and cooperative official. Though roused from sleep at midnight, he promptly assists Mallalieu without complaint [PN=007-023]. He is precise in his record-keeping — recording the serial numbers of the banknotes given to Kitely "in case of any attempt to change any note" [PN=007-036]. He speaks cautiously and refrains from speculation, shaking his head when Mallalieu's questions stray beyond what he knows [PN=007-028].

## Key Relationships

- **[[mallalieu|Mallalieu]]** — the alderman who rouses him at midnight and questions him about Kitely's finances [PN=007-019]
- **[[kitely|James Kitely]]** — his customer, an ex-detective receiving a quarterly pension, who withdrew money on the day of his murder [PN=007-024]
- **[[harborough|John Harborough]]** — was at the counter beside Kitely during the withdrawal, an observation the bank manager couldn't fail to make [PN=007-030]

## Key Actions

1. **Identified Kitely's body** — Mallalieu first roused him to confirm Kitely's identity [PN=007-019]
2. **Disclosed Kitely's police background** — revealed that the murdered man was a pensioned ex-detective, fundamentally altering the investigation's direction [PN=007-026]
3. **Placed Harborough at the scene** — testified that Harborough stood at the counter while Kitely received his money, providing the prosecution's key circumstantial link [PN=007-030]
4. **Provided the money trail** — recorded the serial numbers of the banknotes passed to Kitely, later found at the murder scene [PN=007-036]

## Involvement in the Crime

While not involved in the murder itself, the bank manager's evidence is instrumental in constructing the prosecution's case against Harborough. His testimony — that Harborough was present when Kitely received a sum of money that included gold, and that gold was later found on Harborough — forms the financial motive and circumstantial core of the case. His evidence also reveals Kitely's identity as an ex-detective, which reorients the investigation toward Kitely's past and the Wilchester affair.

## Significance

The bank manager's testimony — placing Harborough at the counter when Kitely received his money, and establishing that the money included gold — provides the prosecution with both means and opportunity. His evidence is the foundation of the circumstantial case against Harborough, though it proves insufficient when Wraythwaite's alibi is eventually established. His revelation of Kitely's police background also serves as the novel's first major disclosure about the dead man's hidden past, opening the investigation into the Wilchester affair.
