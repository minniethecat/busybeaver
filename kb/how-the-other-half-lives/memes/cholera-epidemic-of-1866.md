## The Epidemic

The cholera epidemic of 1866 was a watershed in New York's public health history. Thousands died, overwhelmingly in the tenement districts, and the scale of the disaster forced the state legislature to create the Metropolitan [[health-board|Board of Health]] — the first effective municipal public health agency in the United States.

## Riis's Account

Riis cited the 1866 epidemic as the founding trauma of tenement reform — proof that housing conditions were literally lethal.
