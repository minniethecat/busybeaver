# Second Avenue

Second Avenue ran parallel to [[first-avenue|First Avenue]] through the East Side tenement districts. In the 1890s, the avenue marked a transitional zone between the dense tenement blocks to the east and the somewhat less crowded neighborhoods to the west. The elevated railroad that ran above Second Avenue was both a convenience and a blight — it provided transportation for working-class residents but also darkened the street below and filled the adjacent tenements with noise and soot. Riis did not single out Second Avenue for special treatment, but it formed part of the continuous fabric of tenement housing that he mapped across the Lower East Side.
