# Crosby Street

Crosby Street ran through the tenement belt of the Lower East Side, a district of old-law tenements and immigrant overcrowding. The street was part of the neighborhood that Riis documented in his chapters on the mixed population of the Lower East Side — Irish, Italian, and Jewish families packed into buildings designed for far fewer occupants. Like its neighboring streets, Crosby Street's tenements were characterized by dark interior rooms, inadequate ventilation, and the constant press of population that Riis identified as the root cause of the district's social ills.
