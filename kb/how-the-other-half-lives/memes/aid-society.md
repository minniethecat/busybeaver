# Aid Societies

Charitable aid societies were among the oldest institutions serving New York's poor. By the time Riis wrote *How the Other Half Lives*, these organizations — often religiously affiliated and run by middle-class volunteers — provided emergency food, fuel, and rent assistance to tenement families in crisis. Riis's view of the aid societies was ambivalent: he acknowledged that they prevented starvation and eviction, but he argued that their approach treated symptoms rather than causes. "Charity," he wrote, "had done its best. The tenement had done its worst. The tenement won" [paraphrasing the book's closing argument].
