# Policy Game

The policy game was an illegal lottery — a predecessor of the modern "numbers game" — that Riis identified as endemic in African-American tenement communities. In Chapter XIII, "The Color Line in New York," he described how the game "reaps for its backers rich fortunes wherever colored people congregate" (013-011). Riis condemned policy as a predatory exploitation of the poor, but he also documented the structural conditions — economic exclusion, blocked mobility, the absence of any legitimate path to financial security — that made such desperate gambles appealing.
