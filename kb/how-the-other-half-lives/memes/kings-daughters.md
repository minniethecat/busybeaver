# King's Daughters

The International Order of the King's Daughters was a Christian women's charitable organization founded in 1886. Riis mentioned them in his survey of reform efforts in Chapter XXIV, "What Has Been Done": "the Neighborhood Guilds, through the efforts of the King's Daughters" (024-018). The organization was part of the broader movement of middle-class women's involvement in tenement reform — a phenomenon Riis treated with a mixture of admiration and condescension, praising their dedication while occasionally questioning their effectiveness.
