# City Mission Society

The New York City Mission Society was one of the oldest charitable organizations operating in the tenement districts. Founded in 1812, it predated the great waves of immigration that transformed the city and had, by Riis's time, evolved from a purely religious mission into an organization providing a range of social services. Riis mentioned the city missions in the context of the broader charitable landscape, though he was characteristically skeptical of purely religious approaches to social problems.
