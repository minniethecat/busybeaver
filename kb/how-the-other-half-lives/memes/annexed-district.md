# The Annexed District

The "Annexed District" was the territory north of the Harlem River — what would become parts of the Bronx — that was annexed to New York City in 1874. Riis mentioned the district as a destination for tenement populations displaced from the Lower East Side, and as a frontier where the tenement house system was extending its reach. The annexation was part of the city's explosive growth in the late nineteenth century, and Riis warned that without adequate housing regulation, the new territory would simply reproduce the tenement conditions of the old.
