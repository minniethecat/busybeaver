# Five Points House of Industry

The Five Points House of Industry was a charitable institution located in the heart of the Five Points slum district, operating a nursery and industrial school that served over four hundred day scholars. Jacob Riis cited it in *How the Other Half Lives* as one of the organizations engaged in the difficult work of child rescue within the tenement districts.

## Mission

The House of Industry provided daytime care and industrial training for children whose parents worked in the tenement factories or were otherwise unable to supervise them. Its nursery cared for infants and toddlers; its school taught basic literacy and trade skills to older children. By keeping children off the streets during the day, the institution aimed to interrupt the pipeline from street child to gang member that Riis documented in his chapters on the Street Arab and the growler gangs.

## In Riis's Account

Riis mentioned the Five Points House of Industry alongside the Children's Aid Society as part of the charitable infrastructure that attempted to address the tenement's effects on children. Unlike the Children's Aid Society's emigration program, which sent children to the West, the House of Industry worked with children who remained in the city — providing a stabilizing influence within the slum rather than removing children from it.
