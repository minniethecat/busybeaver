## Design and Failure

The dumbbell tenement was the architectural product of the 1879 Tenement House Act. The law required every room to have access to outside air, which architects achieved by cutting narrow air shafts into building sides. Riis called it "a failure as a reform" — shafts became garbage chutes and fire spreaders.
