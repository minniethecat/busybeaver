# Oak Street

Oak Street was one of the narrow Lower East Side streets lined with old-law tenements — the pre-1879 buildings that Riis described as "the first step in the downward path of the tenement" (001-008). The street's tenements, like those throughout the district, were built to maximize rentable square footage at the expense of light, air, and sanitation. Oak Street's name — evoking solidity and permanence — was typical of the ironic street nomenclature that Riis occasionally remarked upon, where the names promised what the conditions denied.
