# Eldridge Street

Eldridge Street was a major artery of the Jewish Lower East Side — the "Jewtown" that Riis described in Chapters X and XI of *How the Other Half Lives*. The street ran north-south through the heart of the Jewish quarter, connecting the pushcart markets of [[hester-street|Hester Street]] to the garment workshops further downtown. The Eldridge Street Synagogue, completed in 1887, was the first great synagogue built by Eastern European Jews in America and stood as a physical assertion of the community's permanence in a neighborhood that Riis portrayed as a way station on the path to assimilation.
