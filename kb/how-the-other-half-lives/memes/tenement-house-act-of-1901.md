## The New Law

The Tenement House Act of 1901 was the legislative culmination of the reform movement that Riis's book helped inspire. It replaced the [[dumbbell-tenement|dumbbell design]] mandated by the 1879 law with new standards requiring larger courts, better ventilation, running water on every floor, and indoor toilets.

## Significance

The 1901 Act was the first tenement law to be designed by housing experts rather than politicians alone. [[lawrence-veiller|Lawrence Veiller]] and [[robert-w-de-forest|Robert W. De Forest]] were its principal architects.
