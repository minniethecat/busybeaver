# Scrubwomen

Riis mentioned scrubwomen — women who earned a living cleaning tenement apartments, hallways, and public buildings — as part of the lowest tier of the tenement labor force. He described how some of the homeless women in the stale-beer dives survived: "cooking, or steal the pail and scrubbing-brush with which they are expected to earn their living as scrubwomen" (007-019). The scrubwoman's labor was so poorly compensated that even the basic tools of her trade — a pail and brush — had to be stolen, a detail Riis used to illustrate the impossibility of honest escape from tenement poverty.
