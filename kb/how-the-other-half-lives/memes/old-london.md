# Old London

"Old London" was Riis's shorthand for the tenement districts of Victorian London, which served throughout *How the Other Half Lives* as the primary point of international comparison. Riis drew on London examples both to show that New York's problems were not unique and to argue that solutions tried in London — model tenements, municipal reform, public health regulation — could and should be adapted for New York. Sir Sydney Waterlow's London model tenement plans were explicitly referenced as the inspiration for [[alfred-t-white|Alfred T. White]]'s Riverside Buildings (025-034).
