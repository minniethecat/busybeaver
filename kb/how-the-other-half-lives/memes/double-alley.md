# Double Alley

Double Alley was one of the rear tenement alleys that Riis described as the nadir of tenement construction. The "double" configuration — two rows of tenements facing each other across a narrow alley, with additional buildings in the rear — maximized the number of rentable units on a given lot while eliminating any possibility of light, air circulation, or privacy. In Riis's catalog of tenement typologies, the double alley represented the logical endpoint of the profit-maximizing logic that governed tenement construction before the [[tenement-house-act-1867|Tenement-House Act of 1867]] imposed the first minimal standards.
