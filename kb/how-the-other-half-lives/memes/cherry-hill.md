**Cherry Hill** was a neighborhood in the [[fourth-ward|Fourth Ward]] of Lower Manhattan,
once a fashionable residential district that later declined into one of New York's
most infamous slums. By the time [[jacob-riis]] documented it in
*How the Other Half Lives*, Cherry Hill had become synonymous with the
squalor of the old Fourth Ward tenements.

## Decline

Cherry Hill's transformation from respectability to slum was emblematic of the
broader pattern of urban decay in nineteenth-century New York. A resident who had
moved from his house on Cherry Hill "as too far out of town to be reached with
safety" reflected the area's earlier, more genteel character [001-003].

## In Riis's Account

Riis placed Cherry Hill at the center of the Fourth Ward's degradation. He
described the area around the "winding slope of Cherry Street—proud and
fashionable Cherry Hill that was—their broad steps" now overtaken by tenement
conditions [004-003]. The juxtaposition between the neighborhood's elegant past
and squalid present served Riis's argument about the speed of urban decline.

An old-fashioned dwelling on Cherry Hill exemplified the remnants of its former
character, standing in contrast to the overcrowded tenements that had since
engulfed the ward [004-005].
