# Board of Health

The Metropolitan Board of Health was established in 1866 in response to a cholera epidemic that had killed thousands of New Yorkers, most of them tenement dwellers. Riis described the Board as a crucial but underpowered agency: it had the authority to inspect tenements and order violations corrected, but its enforcement powers were limited and its inspectors were too few to cover the hundreds of thousands of tenement apartments in the city. The Board of Health's [[sanitary-police|Sanitary Police]] were the foot soldiers of tenement reform, and Riis often accompanied them on their nighttime rounds.
