# Morrisania

Morrisania was a neighborhood in the annexed territory north of the Harlem River — part of what would become the Bronx. Riis mentioned it in Chapter XIII, "The Color Line in New York," as one of the destinations for African-American families who had managed to escape the downtown tenement districts: "a movement toward the better sort of dwellings, toward quarters in Morrisania" [paraphrasing 013-009]. The move to Morrisania was, in Riis's telling, evidence that the "color line" was beginning to yield — though slowly and incompletely.
