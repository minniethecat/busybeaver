# Alderman Mullins

Alderman Mullins appears in *How the Other Half Lives* as a representative figure of Tammany Hall's ward-level political machinery. Riis mentioned him in connection with the system of political patronage that sustained tenement poverty — aldermen who delivered votes in exchange for jobs, who protected illegal enterprises in exchange for kickbacks, and whose power depended on the very conditions that reformers sought to eliminate. Mullins was not a major figure in the book, but his presence illustrated Riis's argument that political corruption was intertwined with housing degradation.
