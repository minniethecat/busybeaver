# Fan Tan

Fan Tan was a Chinese gambling game that Riis encountered in his exploration of Chinatown's "vices." A simple game of chance played with coins or buttons, it was one of the attractions that drew a mixed clientele — Chinese immigrants and curiosity-seeking white visitors — to the basement gambling rooms of Mott Street. Riis used Fan Tan, like the [[policy-game|policy game]] in the African-American community, as evidence of what he saw as the moral hazards of tenement life — though his treatment of Chinese gambling was colored by the era's racial stereotypes.
