# Sullivan Street

Sullivan Street ran through the Eighth Ward, adjacent to Thompson Street — the heart of what Riis called "Africa," the African-American neighborhood of 1890s New York. In Chapter XIII, "The Color Line in New York," Sullivan Street marked the eastern boundary of the Black settlement area that centered on Thompson Street. The street was part of the zone where Riis observed the "color line" being enforced by landlord collusion — Black tenants confined to specific blocks, paying premium rents for the poorest housing.
