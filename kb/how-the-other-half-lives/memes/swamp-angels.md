# Swamp Angels

The Swamp Angels were one of the Irish street gangs that populated the mythology and reality of the Five Points and Lower East Side tenement districts. Their name derived from the swampy, poorly-drained terrain of their territory — land that had once been actual marsh before being filled in and built over with tenement housing. Riis mentioned these gangs in the context of the street culture that tenement conditions produced, where territorial loyalty and violence were among the few forms of social organization available to the poorest immigrant youth.
