[P03-001]> “With gates of silver and bars of gold

[P03-002]> Ye have fenced my sheep from their father’s fold;

[P03-003]> I have heard the dropping of their tears

[P03-004]> In heaven these eighteen hundred years.”

[P03-005]> “O Lord and Master, not ours the guilt,

[P03-006]> We build but as our fathers built;

[P03-007]> Behold thine images, how they stand,

[P03-008]> Sovereign and sole, through all our land.”

[P03-009]> Then Christ sought out an artisan,

[P03-010]> A low-browed, stunted, haggard man,

[P03-011]> And a motherless girl, whose fingers thin

[P03-012]> Pushed from her faintly want and sin.

[P03-013]> These set he in the midst of them,

[P03-014]> And as they drew back their garment-hem,

[P03-015]> For fear of defilement, “Lo, here,” said he,

[P03-016]> “The *images* ye have made of me!”

[P03-017]> James Russell Lowell
