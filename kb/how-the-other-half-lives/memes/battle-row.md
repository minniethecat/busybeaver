# Battle Row

Battle Row earned its name from the frequency of violent confrontations among its residents. Riis mentioned the block as part of the geography of tenement violence — a place where Irish gang rivalries, fueled by alcohol from the neighborhood's [[stale-beer-dive|stale-beer dives]], regularly spilled into the street. The name itself was a folk designation, conferred by residents and police officers rather than any official map, and it testified to the normalization of violence in the city's poorest districts.
