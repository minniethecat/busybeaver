# Roosevelt Street

## Description

Roosevelt Street was a short Lower East Side street near the East River waterfront, notable primarily for a single photograph Riis included in *How the Other Half Lives*: "An old rear-tenement in Roosevelt Street." (004-025) The image shows a weathered wooden building wedged between brick structures, with no visible windows on the street side — the archetypal rear tenement as it appeared in the 1880s, shortly before demolition.

## In Riis's Account

Roosevelt Street appears in Riis's narrative as evidence rather than as setting. The rear tenement he photographed there was one of the last of its kind — the original tenement form that had spread from Cherry Street across the city. Its survival into the 1880s was itself notable; most rear tenements of that vintage had been replaced by larger double-decker buildings. The photograph, and the brief caption, serve as visual footnote to Riis's Chapter I history of the tenement's origins.

The street abutted the new-model tenement at the corner of Water and Roosevelt Streets, one of the reform projects Riis discusses in Chapter XXV. The contrast between the old rear tenement (the problem) and the new model tenement (the solution) was made physically visible on this one corner. (025-019)
