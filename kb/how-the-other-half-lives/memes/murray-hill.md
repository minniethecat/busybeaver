# Murray Hill

Murray Hill was a middle-class residential neighborhood in Midtown Manhattan, one of the areas that marked the northern boundary of the tenement belt in the 1890s. For Riis, neighborhoods like Murray Hill were significant not in themselves but as benchmarks — places where the tenement had not yet reached, where families lived in single-family homes or small apartment buildings rather than the overcrowded warrens of the Lower East Side. The contrast between Murray Hill and the tenement districts a mile to the south was part of the geography of inequality that structured *How the Other Half Lives*.
