# Immigration & Settlement

The tenement districts of 1890s New York were shaped above all by immigration. Wave after wave — Irish, German, Bohemian, Italian, Jewish, Chinese — poured into the Lower East Side, each group carrying its own traditions and occupying its own niche in the slum ecology. The pages gathered here document the ethnic communities, migration patterns, and settlement geography that defined the world Riis chronicled.
