**Baxter Street** was a street in Manhattan's Lower East Side immigrant quarter,
characterized by "interminable rows" of tenement buildings and documented by
[[jacob-riis]] in his survey of the city's slum districts.

## Tenement Character

Riis described Baxter Street as having "interminable rows of tenements," a
landscape of unbroken tenement blocks that housed the city's poorest immigrant
populations. The street was at the boundary of the [[hebrew-quarter|Hebrew quarter]],
which Riis observed as having begun to "invade" from adjacent blocks, reflecting
the eastward expansion of the Jewish immigrant settlement [010-002].

## Bottle Alley

Around the corner from Baxter Street lay [[bottle-alley|Bottle Alley]], a
particularly notorious tenement block. Riis described Bottle Alley as "a fair
specimen" of the tenement conditions found in the Baxter Street vicinity, using
it as a representative case study of the broader conditions along the street
[006-016].
