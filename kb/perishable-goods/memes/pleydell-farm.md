# The Pleydell Farm

The Pleydell farm, located near the village of [[st-martin|St. Martin]] in [[carinthia|Carinthia]], is the rented holiday residence of the [[pleydell-family|Pleydell]] family. It serves as the initial destination for [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s rescue party and the emotional center of the operation's early phase.

> From the port we should drive to Salzburg as hard and as straight as we could and thence direct to St. Martin, for that was the name of the village which served the Pleydells' farm.

> (001-135)

## Role in the Story

The farm is first mentioned when [[daphne-pleydell|Daphne Pleydell]] invites [[william-chandos|William Chandos]] and [[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]] to stay: "If they will let us the farm we rented before, we shall have plenty of room" (001-038). The farm thus represents hospitality and family — a contrast to the cold fortress of the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]].

The farm's location near [[st-martin|St. Martin]] (001-135) makes it strategically valuable. From here, [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] can coordinate the search for [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]], using the farm as a base while [[major-pleydell|Major Pleydell]] provides local knowledge and [[captain-pleydell|Captain Pleydell]] lies injured in a chamber (002-099).

The emotional atmosphere at the farm is tense. [[major-pleydell|Major Pleydell]] meets the rescue party and relays the grim news of [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]]'s disappearance. [[daphne-pleydell|Daphne Pleydell]] protests when [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] prepares to leave (002-103), her concern for both her sister and her guests reflecting the farm's role as a place of anxious waiting.

## Geography

The farm lies near [[st-martin|St. Martin]], in the direction of [[sava|Sava]]. [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] rode from the farm toward [[sava|Sava]] on the morning of her abduction (002-054). The farm's position in the Austrian countryside places it within reach of both [[salzburg|Salzburg]] and the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]], making it a natural staging point.
