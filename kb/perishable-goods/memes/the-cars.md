# The Cars

The two [[rolls-royce|Rolls-Royce]] motor cars are more than transportation in *Perishable Goods* — they are the logistical backbone of [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s entire operation. Driven and maintained by [[carson|Carson]], [[bell|Bell]], and [[rowley|Rowley]], the cars carry the rescue party across Europe, serve as mobile command posts, and become instruments of pursuit and combat.

> Mansel's Rolls-Royce was furnished with secret lockers and trays, but ours had no such fittings, and, since we must now carry arms, he gave me a note to his coachbuilder and bade me seek him the next morning at eight o'clock. While he and his men were at work, fitting a hidden coffer, Carson and I together were to test and prepare the two cars, so that, with luck, if need be, they could run for a month on end without attention.

> (001-063)

## Operational Role

The cars are prepared in haste by the coachbuilder at [[cleveland-row|Cleveland Row]], working through the night at [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s command (001-138). The party "took two cars" on the expedition (001-063), a redundancy that proves essential — two cars allow the team to split, to pursue different leads, and to provide backup in case of mechanical failure.

Throughout the operation, the cars serve multiple functions: transport, reconnaissance platform, ambulance, and pursuit vehicle. [[carson|Carson]] and [[bell|Bell]] "were accustomed to handling a car" (001-063), and their driving skill is demonstrated in the mountain pursuit when [[bell|Bell]] shoots at an enemy vehicle's fuel tank from a moving car (007-055).

## Symbolic Significance

The [[rolls-royce|Rolls-Royces]] also carry symbolic weight. They represent the resources and competence that [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] brings to bear — not brute force but precision engineering, maintained by skilled professionals, deployed with tactical intelligence.
