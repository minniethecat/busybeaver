# Rose Noble's Pursuit

Rose Noble's Pursuit is the high-speed chase that erupts in the immediate aftermath of [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]]'s escape from the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]]. With [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] freed and borne away in one of the [[rolls-royce|Rolls-Royces]], [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] and the remnants of his gang pursue [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s party through the mountain roads of [[carinthia|Carinthia]], transforming the conflict from a siege into a running battle across the countryside.

## Context / Lead-up

The escape had been a partial success. [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] had been hauled up from the Closet window to the roof, but the remaining ropes had been pulled away — and [[rose-noble|Noble]]'s thick laugh from above (006-317) had confirmed that he now controlled the roof and had intercepted [[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]]'s rescue operation. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] and [[william-chandos|Chandos]] remained trapped inside the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]], while outside, [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] and the servants now had [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] in their care and the [[rolls-royce|Rolls-Royces]] at their disposal.

The stage was set for a pursuit that would carry the conflict out of the castle and onto the open road.

## Sequence of Events

1. **The Car Dispatched** — Earlier, during the terrace rescue, [[rose-noble|Noble]] had already sent his car out under [[bunch|Bunch]] and [[punter|Punter]] with furious orders: "if we don't take her at the foot of this blasted rock I'll twist your block off your body with my bare hands" (006-081). That car had not returned during the night, confirming [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s belief that [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] and the servants had intercepted it (006-283).
2. **Rose Noble Takes Control of the Roof** — On Friday night, as [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]]'s party executes the roof assault, [[rose-noble|Noble]] emerges on the ramparts and seizes control of the ropes. His mocking laugh — "Isn't that nice?" (006-317) — reveals that he has anticipated [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s plan.
3. **The Chase Begins** — With [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] gone from the castle and [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] still inside, the conflict splinters. [[rose-noble|Noble]] must now pursue the fleeing [[rolls-royce|Rolls-Royces]] while simultaneously containing the two men still inside his fortress.
4. **Hanbury's Escape Route** — [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]], having lost the ropes but secured [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]], must race through the beechwoods to the waiting [[rolls-royce|Rolls-Royces]] and drive for safety. The roads from the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] to [[lass|Lass]], [[villach|Villach]], and [[sava|Sava]] become the arena of pursuit.
5. **The Mountain Roads** — The pursuit unfolds on the twisting, rising roads of the [[carinthia|Carinthian]] Alps — the same roads that [[william-chandos|Chandos]] had traversed on the roof of [[rose-noble|Noble]]'s car days earlier. The advantage lies with whoever knows the terrain better and can drive faster on the narrow, precipitous passes.

## Participants

- **[[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]** — the pursuer; his network may be shattered, but his personal ferocity and strategic intelligence make him a lethal adversary even in retreat
- **[[bunch|Bunch]]** — [[rose-noble|Noble]]'s driver; the man ordered to "run down" [[bell|Bell]] if he saw him on the road (004-080)
- **[[punter|Punter]]** — a member of [[rose-noble|Noble]]'s gang; involved in the earlier [[punter-chase|Punter Chase]]
- **[[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]]** — the escapee; now in the care of [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] and the servants, being driven at speed away from the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]]
- **[[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]]** — the rescuer-turned-fugitive; his mission now is to get [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] to safety while [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] and [[william-chandos|Chandos]] remain behind
- **[[carson|Carson]]** — driving one of the [[rolls-royce|Rolls-Royces]]; his skill behind the wheel is now a matter of life and death
- **[[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]** — still inside the castle; his fate now hinges on whether his party outside can outrun [[rose-noble|Noble]]'s pursuit

## Outcome / Consequences

The pursuit from the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] marked the transition from siege warfare to mobile warfare in the [[castle-of-gath-operation|Castle of Gath Operation]]. The consequences unfolded across the novel's final chapters:

- **The Network Collapses**: [[rose-noble|Noble]]'s organization, already weakened by the loss of [[jute|Jute]] and the interception of his car, could no longer maintain the disciplined cordon that had made the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] impregnable. The pursuit scattered his remaining forces.
- **The Final Reckoning Approaches**: With [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] safe and his network in ruins, [[rose-noble|Noble]] had nothing left to lose — making him more dangerous than ever. The pursuit would eventually circle back to the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]], where the [[final-confrontation|Final Confrontation]] awaited.
- **Mansel's Sacrifice**: [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s decision to remain behind in the castle, sending [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] out first, set the terms for the novel's tragic ending. By the time the pursuit was over and [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] could be extracted, he would have sustained the wound that killed him.

The pursuit also demonstrated the tactical value of the [[rolls-royce|Rolls-Royces]] — fast, reliable, and driven by men who knew every inch of the mountain roads — against [[rose-noble|Noble]]'s less disciplined drivers.
