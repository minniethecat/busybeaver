# The English Channel

The English Channel is the body of water separating England from continental Europe, crossed by [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s rescue party in their urgent journey to [[carinthia|Carinthia]]. The Channel represents the physical and symbolic boundary between the familiar world and the unknown dangers ahead.

> We were to meet at Folkestone and cross by the morning boat; and, since it seemed idle to take two cars, yet send three servants by train, we arranged to keep two with us and to send the third to Salzburg in charge of our heavier stuff.

> (001-063)

## Role in the Story

The party plans to "meet at Folkestone and cross by the morning boat" (001-063), making the Channel crossing the first leg of the [[the-sleepless-journey|sleepless journey]]. The crossing is notable for its uneventfulness — a contrast to the tension that will define the Austrian chapters. The calm Channel journey is the last moment of relative peace before the pursuit begins.

A significant moment occurs during the crossing when [[bell|Bell]] spots a suspect on the Channel boat "with a watch in hand" (001-134), demonstrating that even here, in what should be neutral territory, the shadow of [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s network reaches.
