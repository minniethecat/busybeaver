# Mansel's Rolls-Royce

Mansel's Rolls-Royce is far more than a luxury motor-car in Dornford Yates's
*Perishable Goods*. Custom-fitted with concealed compartments, driven across
international borders, and crewed by [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s trusted servants,
it functions as the party's mobile headquarters—transporting personnel, concealing
equipment, and enabling the rapid movement that is essential to every phase of the
campaign against [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]].

## Role in the Story

The Rolls-Royce appears at every critical juncture of the narrative, from the
departure from [[cleveland-row|Cleveland Row]] to the final confrontation at the
[[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]]. Its capabilities directly enable the party's
operational tempo:

> "Mansel's Rolls-Royce was furnished with secret lockers." (001-133)

These concealed compartments—never described in detail, their contents left to the
reader's imagination—transform a gentleman's touring car into a vehicle for
clandestine operations. The car's twin roles, ostentatious wealth and hidden
purpose, mirror [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s own duality.

The party operates two Rolls-Royces during the [[carinthia|Carinthia]] expedition (003-099),
doubling their mobility and permitting the kind of coordinated movements that [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s
tactics demand. The cars are crewed not by [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] himself but by his servants
[[carson|Carson]] and [[bell|Bell]], with [[rowley|Rowley]] also capable of handling them (003-161).

## Technical & Tactical Details

Yates provides few mechanical specifications, but the Rolls-Royce's tactical
characteristics emerge through action:

- **Silent approach**: The car's distinctive quietness—described repeatedly as a
  "sigh" [003-068, 008-272]—allows it to approach positions undetected.
- **Mountain capability**: The Rolls navigates narrow alpine roads where "of the
  little road the Rolls took more than its share" (003-151), demonstrating both the
  car's bulk and its driver's skill.
- **Rapid deployment**: "A shortish wheelbase, but a Rolls takes some getting round"
  (007-054)—even in tight quarters, the car can be maneuvered for quick extraction.
- **Concealment**: The "secret lockers" (001-133) and the car's respectable
  appearance allow the party to cross borders and enter towns without arousing
  suspicion.

The party's mobility depends entirely on these vehicles. When one Rolls is
compromised—"their car had been ruined and the Rolls was at their gate, the
temptation—" (007-066)—the tactical balance shifts immediately.

## Symbolic Significance

In the context of 1920s British adventure fiction, the Rolls-Royce carries layered
meaning. It is the pinnacle of British engineering—quiet, powerful, reliable—and the
preferred conveyance of the landed gentry. That [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] repurposes this icon of
respectability for covert operations across Europe is characteristic of Yates's
heroes: gentlemen who deploy the tools of their class in the service of a private
code of justice.

The car's dual nature—luxury vehicle without, operational asset within—mirrors the
novel's broader preoccupation with surfaces and depths. The party passes through
European villages "as at an apparition" (002-138), their wealth on display yet their
purpose hidden. The Rolls-Royce is simultaneously a statement of identity and a mask.
