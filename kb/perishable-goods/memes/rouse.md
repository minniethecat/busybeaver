# Hannibal Rouse

Hannibal Rouse is a clerk in holy orders — or so he appears. In truth, he is an agent of [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]], planted at [[villach|Villach]] to monitor [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s party as they search for [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]]. His bungling surveillance in Chapter III provides both comic relief and genuine menace before [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] decisively neutralises him.

## Role in the Story

Rouse serves as [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s eyes on the ground in Austria. Posing as a clergyman, he lodges at the same hotel as [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s party and attempts to track their movements (002-159). His incompetence as a secret agent is matched only by his persistence — he badgers the party "nearly out of their lives" with his antics (003-067). When [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] finally confronts him, the encounter turns violent, leaving Rouse limping from the room (003-045). His loss is significant: the party realises that to have shown their hand to Rouse was to show it to [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] himself (003-155).

## Key Appearances

- **Chapter II** — Introduced at [[villach|Villach]] as "Hannibal Rouse, clerk in holy orders" (002-144); [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] judges him by his actions and delays travel to give Rouse time to reveal his hand (002-148); the party discovers Rouse is lodging in the same hotel (002-159).
- **Chapter III** — Rouse is still abroad in his car at the end of the second patrol (003-001); he greets a visitor with "[[wilberforce|Mr. Wilberforce]]" in triumph (003-004); he describes a man to the party with suspicious detail (003-011); he hands over a box of flowers that [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] instantly recognises as a coded message from [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] (003-027); the confrontation escalates — [[george-hanbury|George]] throws himself forward as Rouse recoils against the wall (003-033); Rouse painfully gets to his feet, protesting "It's beyond a joke" (003-035); he winces as he gives [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] particulars of his employer (003-039); without a word, he turns and limps from the room (003-045); [[william-chandos|Chandos]] encounters him on the doorstep later (003-056); the narrator reflects that Rouse had badgered them since sundown (003-067); [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] explains that Rouse was meant to keep in touch — an arrangement that could be turned against [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] (003-084); he predicts Rouse will clear out at once upon suspecting discovery (003-111); the party confirms Rouse is indeed [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s man (003-113); on the train platform, Rouse demands "I must be alone" in a voice that betrays his fear (003-126); he asks impatiently about the schedule (003-131); though the voice is Rouse's, the tone has shifted to that of a man running (003-134); he leaves the train casually but vanishes from the platform, eluding pursuit (003-142); [[william-chandos|Chandos]] reflects that losing Rouse was tiresome but showing their hand was the real cost — for now [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] knew they were coming (003-155).
- **Chapter V** — [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] later reflects that all of "Hannibal Rouse's antics" had been gravely accepted at face value (005-138), a measure of how thoroughly the fake clergyman had been embedded in the local scene.

## Relationships

- **[[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]** — Rouse's employer; he serves as [[rose-noble|Noble]]'s agent in Austria, relaying information about [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s movements (003-113).
- **[[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]** — The target of Rouse's surveillance; [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] sees through the imposture rapidly, recognising the flower-box as a coded message (003-027), and ultimately exposes and routs him (003-045).
- **[[william-chandos|William Chandos]]** — The narrator who records Rouse's antics with a mixture of exasperation and grim amusement (003-067).
- **[[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]]** — Throws himself at Rouse during the confrontation, slamming him against the wall (003-033).
- **[[wilberforce|Wilberforce]]** — A visitor Rouse greets by name, suggesting a connection or shared acquaintance (003-004).
