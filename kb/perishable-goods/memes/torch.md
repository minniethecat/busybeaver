# Electric Torches

The electric torch — a handheld flashlight — is essential equipment throughout
the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] operation in Dornford Yates's *Perishable Goods*. Without
them, the exploration of the fortress's underground passages, cellars, and
secret rooms would be impossible. Like the [[pistol]], the torch is carried by
every member of [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s party and used in nearly every chapter of the operation.

## Equipment of the Expedition

When [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] prepares for the night operation at [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]], torches are
standard issue alongside pistols. The narrator describes the moment when they
venture underground:

> "Mansel," I said. "Give me your pistol and torch." (006-016)

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] parts with both reluctantly: "He gave me his pistol unwillingly enough.
Then he surrendered his torch" (006-019). The torch is valued equipment — when
the tunnel arch collapses, [[william-chandos|Chandos]] records with relief: "I managed to save both
pistols and [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]]'s torch" (006-021).

## Underground Exploration

In the absolute darkness of the castle's underground passages, the torch is
the difference between safety and disaster. [[william-chandos|Chandos]] describes the eerie
atmosphere:

> We ate our meal by the shaded light of a torch, for to do such things in the
> pitch-black darkness of the living rock was out of the question. (006-050)

The torch reveals the castle's hidden spaces: "The torch now showed us a cellar"
(007-034). But it is also a liability — its beam can betray the party's position
to enemies. [[william-chandos|Chandos]] notes: "We dared not use our torches in case their light
should give us away" (007-119). When the enemy fires, "the beam of a torch
swept the wall beneath which we were lying" (007-071).

## Key Moments

The torch features in several pivotal scenes:

- **[[castle-of-gath|The Castle of Gath]] approach**: [[carson|Carson]] brings tools and a torch for the
  nighttime approach (002-089–002-092)
- **The collapse**: [[william-chandos|Chandos]] saves [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]]'s torch when the tunnel arch gives
  way (006-016–006-021)
- **The cellar discovery**: The torch reveals the hidden cellar beneath the
  fortress (007-034)
- **[[adele-pleydell|Adèle]]'s signal**: "[[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] gave one flash with her torch and, in a twinkling,
  [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] was at her side" (006-301)
- **The enemy gunfire**: A hostile marksman fires at the torch beam (007-107)

## Tactical Significance

Torch discipline is a recurring concern. The party must balance the need for
light against the risk of exposure. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] uses torches strategically —
sometimes keeping them dark to maintain stealth, sometimes using the beam to
signal or blind an opponent. The torch is both tool and weapon: "The left struck
aside the pistol, as [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] fired: the right crashed into his face. As I
snatched at the second pistol, he swept it off the board" (006-220) — in this
pitch darkness, every action is illuminated only by torchlight.

## The Torch as Narrative Device

Beyond its practical function, the torch serves as a powerful narrative device
for building atmosphere and controlling the reader's field of vision. When
Chandos enters the great hall of the castle, the torchlight "could not deny the
majesty of that room" (005-215), revealing grandeur that heightens the stakes of
the clandestine operation. When "the beam of a torch illumined his head and
shoulders, and a bullet sang past his ear" (007-002), the torch becomes a weapon
in enemy hands — a targeting mechanism that turns darkness from ally to enemy.
The party's dependence on artificial light in a fortress designed for darkness
creates a recurring rhythm of illumination and concealment that drives the
chapters' suspense.

## Torchlight and the Architecture of Gath

The torch is essential to the exploration of the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]]'s hidden
architecture. When Chandos and Mansel probe the castle's secrets, "after
listening carefully, we ventured to light the torch" (007-043) — the careful
verb "ventured" capturing the risk each illumination carries. The torch reveals
the cellars (007-034), exposes the secret passages, and in one crucial moment
serves as a weapon: Mansel instructs his men, "If you don't strike them at
once, try using the torch" (007-080), transforming a tool of sight into an
instrument of attack. The torch's beam also betrays the party's position to
the gang: "the beam of a torch swept the wall beneath which we stood" (007-071),
and Chandos "turned and fired back at the torch" (007-107) — the light source
itself becoming a target in the darkness.

## From Tool to Symbol

By the novel's climax, the torch has accumulated symbolic weight. Adèle's "one
flash with her torch" (006-301) is not merely a signal but a crystallization of
all the stealth, risk, and precision that Mansel's rescue operation has demanded.
When Mansel "lighted the torch" after the collapse (005-257), the act is one of
recovery and continuation — the party will press on, illuminating the darkness
one beam at a time. The torch's trajectory through the novel mirrors the party's
own: from careful husbanding of resources, to tactical deployment, to victorious
signaling. It is, in the end, the embodiment of Mansel's method — precise,
controlled, and capable of turning darkness to advantage.
