# The Terrace

The terrace is a small outdoor platform at the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]], enclosed by a stone balustrade and overlooking the cascade. It is here that [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]] is first seen by her rescuers, and from here that she is dramatically pulled to safety.

## Role in the Story

The terrace is first described when [[william-chandos|Chandos]] trains his binoculars on the castle from a distant mountain peak: "She was standing on the little terrace, with her hands on the balustrade" (004-167). The setting is vivid and poignant — below her the cascade leaps, behind her an archway frames a shut door.

> She was standing on the little terrace, with her hands on the balustrade. Whether she was watched I cannot say, but she was alone. Below her was the leaping cascade, and, behind, an archway, framing a door that was shut. She was standing very still; her head was up, and her eyes lifted to the peak upon which we stood.
>
> (004-167)

This sighting is the first confirmation that Adèle is alive and within the castle. It also provides the crucial tactical intelligence that leads to the [[adele-signals|signal exchange]] with the watch-case heliograph.

The terrace becomes the stage for the novel's most thrilling moment. When [[casemate|Casemate]] leaves Adèle unattended to fetch his hat, [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] sees his chance. He slips under the archway door, crosses to the terrace, pulls Adèle down into the channel, and the rescue is accomplished (006-068).
