# The Monstrance

The monstrance is a religious artifact that hung on the wall of [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s flat in [[cleveland-row|Cleveland Row]]. Its presence — and the fact that it was left untouched — is one of the first clues that the [[cleveland-row-burglary|burglary of Mansel's flat]] was no ordinary theft.

## Role in the Story

When [[william-chandos|William Chandos]] surveys the aftermath of the burglary, his eye falls on the wall where the monstrance had hung. He notes with surprise: "At once I looked at the wall, where I knew there had hung a monstrance" (001-012). But the monstrance was still there — untouched.

The fact that the burglars left behind a valuable religious artifact while taking other items confirms that their target was specific: [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s men had come for the [[stolen-letters|stolen letters]] and papers, not for common loot. The monstrance's survival is an early signal that this is a targeted intelligence operation, not a casual burglary.

The monstrance also speaks to the character of [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s flat — a gentleman's residence furnished with objects of beauty and meaning, not merely wealth. Its presence on the wall suggests Mansel's connection to older, deeper traditions than the modern world of crime and pursuit that overtakes him.

> At once I looked at the wall, where I knew there had hung a monstrance. This was golden and jewelled, and, though there was plate-glass about it, I could have forced the case in two minutes of time. But the monstrance was there.
>
> (001-012)
