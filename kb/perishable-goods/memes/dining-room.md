# The Royal Dining-Room

The Royal Dining-Room is a chamber within the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] that forms part of the suite of royal apartments the rescue party must navigate. Accessed from the King's Closet, it serves as a waypoint in the operation to free [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]].

## Role in the Story

The dining-room is first entered when [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] leads the party out of the King's Closet: "This admitted us to the royal dining-room" (005-219). The chamber is grand in scale, reflecting the castle's history as a royal residence.

> Then we enter the royal dining-room. The tapestries are very fine. The visitor will observe that the room is not large because the King has not entertained a guest here. We now pass into the King's Bedchamber. This is very stately. The crimson hangings of the magnificent bed and the superb furniture and tapestries, the richery of the polished woodcarvings all reflects the departed majesty of "The Last of the Knights."
>
> (004-129)

During the tense hours of the operation, sounds from the dining-room signal danger. [[william-chandos|Chandos]] reports: "I would have said that it came from the dining-room" when hearing ominous noises (005-250).

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] sets [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] to watch the dining-room door as the party works to force entry into the tower (005-261). The dining-room door becomes one of the critical points of defense.

After the successful rescue of [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]], the party steals back across the dining-room floor "like thieves in the night" (006-109), moving quietly to avoid detection.

The dining-room also conceals a secret: beneath its floor lies a cellar, which becomes a shelter during the final stage of the operation (007-114).
