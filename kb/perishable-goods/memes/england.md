# England

**England** is the homeland of the principal characters in *Perishable Goods* and the setting for the novel's opening chapters. The contrast between the ordered English world — represented by [[cleveland-row|Cleveland Row]], [[white-ladies|White Ladies]], and the [[wiltshire|Wiltshire]] countryside — and the lawless borderlands of [[carinthia|Carinthia]] drives much of the novel's tension.

## The English World

The novel opens in England, where [[william-chandos|William Chandos]] and [[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]] have set up a household in [[wiltshire|Wiltshire]] (001-001). This pastoral English setting — with its established social routines, its servants, its dinner parties — establishes the baseline of civilization against which the later Continental dangers are measured.

[[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s flat in [[cleveland-row|Cleveland Row]] serves as the London headquarters, where plans are laid and intelligence gathered. The English chapters hum with the quiet efficiency of a society at peace: [[bell|Bell]] the servant, dinner at eight, the familiar streets of St. James's.

## Departure

The party's departure from England marks the transition from preparation to action. The Rolls-Royce, loaded with luggage and equipment, carries the expedition from the secure world of English country houses toward the uncertainties of the Continent (001-133).

> And so it fell out—though not as we had expected: for, though Mansel came leisurely to England, he took the road back to Carinthia, like a man possessed. And Hanbury and I with him.
>
> (001-060)

## Englishness Abroad

Throughout the novel, the characters' Englishness remains a defining trait. Their manners, their speech, their assumptions about fair play and decency — all are products of their English upbringing. This English character is tested in the crucible of [[carinthia|Carinthia]], where [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s criminal network operates by a different code entirely.

The novel ultimately affirms the resilience of English values: Mansel's party prevails not through superior force but through the very qualities — patience, preparation, loyalty — that their English world has cultivated.
