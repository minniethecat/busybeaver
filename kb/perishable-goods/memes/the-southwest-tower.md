# The Southwest Tower

The southwest tower of the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] is the specific location of [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]]'s imprisonment. Identified through the [[adele-signals|mirror signal exchange]], it becomes the focal point of the entire rescue operation.

## Role in the Story

The tower is first pinpointed during the signal exchange from the mountain peak. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]], watching through binoculars as [[william-chandos|Chandos]] flashes the heliograph, reports with precision: "She went up the staircase into the left-hand tower. And, after a moment, she showed some white at that window, halfway up" (004-183).

> "She went up the staircase into the left-hand tower. And, after a moment, she showed some white at that window, halfway up. When you acknowledged that, she took it away. She's very wise—Adèle."
>
> (004-183)

The intelligence is exact: Adèle is in the southwest tower, at a window halfway up. This single piece of tactical information reshapes the entire operation. The tower becomes the target; every subsequent action — the reconnaissance of the castle interior, the forcing of doors, the choice of approach route — is oriented toward reaching that window.

The tower's medieval construction — massive stone walls, narrow windows, a spiral stair — makes it both a prison and a fortress within a fortress. The challenge is not merely to reach the castle but to penetrate this innermost strong point without alerting the guards.
