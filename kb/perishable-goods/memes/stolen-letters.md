# Stolen Letters

The stolen letters are the MacGuffin at the heart of Dornford Yates's
*Perishable Goods* — private correspondence taken from [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s safe
that [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] uses as leverage for blackmail. Their theft is the inciting
incident of the novel, and their recovery is the objective that propels [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]
and his companions across Europe from London to [[carinthia|Carinthia]].

## Nature of the Letters

The letters are described as the personal correspondence of a girl —
"occasional letters written at [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s request" (001-079). Their content is
never fully disclosed to the reader, but their value as blackmail material is
made clear. "Half a million for some papers" (001-075) is the figure mentioned —
though [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] never reveals whether this was the actual ransom demand or merely
an illustration of their worth.

The narrator [[william-chandos|William Chandos]] describes the gravity of the situation:
"The papers are the letters of a girl" (001-079), and [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] tells him that
"if he should study those papers, he might see a line of attack" (001-024).

## The Theft

The burglary at [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s [[cleveland-square|Cleveland Square]] flat is reported by his body-servant
[[carson|Carson]]. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] explains to [[william-chandos|Chandos]] and [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]]:

> "They came for my papers," said Mansel. "And got them." (001-016)

The safe had been cut open — a skilled operation that [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] immediately
attributes to [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s organization. The thief entered through the flat's
premises and made directly for the concealed safe, suggesting inside knowledge
of the flat's layout.

## Rose Noble's Letter

After [[cleveland-row-burglary|the theft]], [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] sends a letter to [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] offering to return the
papers — for a price. The body of the letter "was written in a clerkly hand"
(001-071), but it contains a veiled threat:

> "He offers you those letters back. When you ignore his offer — and he knows
> you will — he will use them." (001-082)

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s response is characteristic: after reading the letter, he "folded the
letter and held it up" (001-112), then "put the letter towards a candle's flame"
(001-120) and burned it. The narrator reflects: "Had [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s letter followed
instead of preceding the telegram, the secret was ours" (001-111).

## Significance

The stolen letters are never physically recovered within the pages of
*Perishable Goods* — they are the distant objective that justifies every
operation. [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s knowledge of their contents hangs over the entire
narrative, and [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s determination to suppress them drives the campaign
from [[cleveland-row|Cleveland Row]] to the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] and its final confrontation.
