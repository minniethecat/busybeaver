# The Drive

The drive appears throughout *Perishable Goods* as more than a physical roadway — it is a threshold between safety and danger, between the domestic world of the [[pleydell-family|Pleydell]] farm and the hostile territory of [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s criminal network.

## Role in the Story

The drive features in moments of high urgency. When [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]] receives news of the stolen letters, he "cried to Hanbury and me to bring some tools and a torch and, in spite of his limp... ran well ahead of his cousin down the drive" (002-089):

> He cried to Hanbury and me to bring some tools and a torch and, in spite of his limp,… ran well ahead of his cousin down the drive.
>
> (002-089)

The drive becomes the path to action, the route from deliberation to pursuit.

The drive is also the site of emotional arrivals. [[major-pleydell|Major Pleydell]], learning of his sister's disappearance, "was well up the drive, shouting for Daphne and crying aloud his news" (002-095). The drive channels both the physical movement of characters and the emotional momentum of the plot.

After the rescue operation, the drive signals the return to normalcy. "Two minutes later the cars were clear of the drive and were making towards St. Martin at a leisurely speed" (002-126). The shift from urgency to leisure marks the transition from crisis to resolution.
