# Rope

**Rope** plays a critical tactical role in *Perishable Goods*, serving as the primary means of silent ascent during the infiltration of the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]]. [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s procurement and deployment of rope demonstrates his characteristic attention to operational detail.

## Procurement

During the preparation phase for the [[castle-of-gath-operation|Castle of Gath operation]], Mansel locates a rope factory and purchases a quantity of cordage for the expedition:

> "Then Mansel found a rope factory and purchased a quantity..." (005-068)

The rope is sourced locally in [[carinthia|Carinthia]], avoiding the risk of transporting suspicious equipment across borders.

## Operational Use

### Ascent Equipment

The rope provides what Mansel recognizes as the most silent path of approach to the castle. Each member of the assault team carries a coil:

> "Bell... wore a coil of rope, like a sash." (005-163)

The party uses the rope to scale the castle walls under cover of darkness, avoiding the guarded approaches. Chandos notes the tactical advantage: "since a rope provides the most silent path..." (005-167).

### The Signal Cord

A specialized cord — distinct from the climbing ropes — serves as a signaling system between team members positioned at different levels of the castle:

> "Bell was at the foot of the ladders, with a signal-cord in his hand." (005-163)

This cord allows silent communication between the ground team and those on the walls, essential for coordinating the infiltration without alerting the castle's occupants.

### Descent and Retreat

The rope is equally critical for the descent phase of the operation. After completing their objectives, the party members seek "the rope down which we had come" (005-169) to make their escape.

## The Cord as Plot Device

Beyond its practical climbing function, a length of cord becomes a pivotal plot element. The discovery of the cord by [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s men triggers a chain of retaliation, as detailed in the entry for [[the-cord|The Cord]]. What began as simple equipment transforms into evidence that escalates the conflict.

## Tactical Doctrine

Mansel's rope-based approach reflects a broader tactical philosophy evident throughout the novel: silence over speed, preparation over improvisation. The rope — low-tech, reliable, silent — embodies this approach. Where a modern reader might expect explosives or firearms, Mansel relies on timber hitches and silent ascent, methods that would have been familiar to mountaineers of the 1920s.

> "Carson," said Mansel, "get a rope on that bough. Timber hitch..." (005-111)
