# Rose Noble Confronted

Rose Noble Confronted is the climactic confrontation between [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]] and [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] that spans the boundary between Chapter V and Chapter VI of *[[perishable-goods-phrase|Perishable Goods]]*. Beginning with [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]]'s voice through the bolted door of the "gallery of stone" and ending with a desperate fistfight in the royal Dining-room, this sequence pits [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s cool, chivalric resolve against [[rose-noble|Noble]]'s brute calculation in a battle of words, threats, and finally fists over the fate of [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]].

## Context / Lead-up

In the small hours of the morning, the rescue party had successfully infiltrated the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] through the roof. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] had broken into the State Apartments, reached [[the-antechamber|the antechamber]], and forced the lock of the door to the "gallery of stone" (005-261). After two minutes of hammering that made "the devil's own noise" (005-265), the lock yielded. But before [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] could draw the bolts, a fist pounded on the other side and [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]]'s clear voice called out a warning that changed everything.

> "Is that you Jonah?"
>
> "Yes, dear. One moment, and—"
>
> "*No, no. Stop. Stay where you are. Rose Noble's here by my side. And he says if you open that door you'll lose your match.*" [005-270–005-273]

## Sequence of Events

1. **The Door Held** — [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]]'s warning freezes [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] with his hand on the bolt. [[rose-noble|Noble]] has reached the gallery first and stands beside her, a pistol to her head (005-273).
2. **[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s Decision** — In the darkness, [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] whispers his orders: "Time to be gone... Leave everything and clear out. *I'm going to stay.* Food and dry clothes to me by [[the-cascade|the waterfall]] cord" (006-006). [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] and [[william-chandos|Chandos]] are to evacuate; [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] will remain inside the castle.
3. **[[william-chandos|Chandos]] Defies Orders** — [[william-chandos|Chandos]] refuses to leave: "I stay with [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]. Give me your pistol and torch" (006-016). He takes [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]]'s [[pistol|weapons]] and drops to [[castle-of-gath-terrace|the terrace]], hiding in [[the-archway|the archway]] beneath the cascade (006-021).
4. **[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s Humiliation** — [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]], separated from [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] by the door and forced to listen helplessly, hears [[rose-noble|Noble]] strike her. "He struck her, [[william-chandos|Chandos]]. And, because she wouldn't cry out, he struck her again" (006-031). [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s composure cracks: "Oh, my God, there are times when I'd take your offer and buy her out" (006-031).
5. **The Next Day: The Dining-room Confrontation** — The following morning, having re-entered the State Apartments after [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]]'s near-rescue on [[castle-of-gath-terrace|the terrace]], [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]], [[william-chandos|Chandos]], and [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] cross the royal Dining-room—only to find [[rose-noble|Noble]] already seated at the head of the table, "with his elbows upon the board and a pistol in either hand" (006-159).
6. **The Negotiation** — [[rose-noble|Noble]] opens with cold menace. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] counters with icy precision, beginning: "The first thing is to clear the air. This lady may be your prisoner, but I am not. One doesn't imprison one's broker if one happens to want some funds" (006-174).
7. **[[rose-noble|Noble]]'s Price** — After a long, tense verbal duel in which [[rose-noble|Noble]] threatens [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]]'s reputation as well as her safety, he names his price: "Be here in this room yourself a week from today, with two hundred and fifty thousand in Bank of [[england|England]] notes, and I'll let the three of you go" (006-209).
8. **[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s Stand** — [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] refuses instantly. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] refuses to negotiate further in her presence: "I repeat my request... I decline to continue to talk this over while [[adele-pleydell|Mrs. Pleydell]] sits there" (006-211)(006-214).
9. **The Blow** — Enraged, [[rose-noble|Noble]] leans forward and fires. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s bare left hand strikes the pistol aside; his right fist smashes into [[rose-noble|Noble]]'s mouth, knocking him back into the King's great chair (006-220). But the massive chair, built for an emperor, holds him upright instead of letting him fall.
10. **Retreat to the Bedchamber** — [[william-chandos|Chandos]] snatches the second pistol, but [[rose-noble|Noble]] gets off a second shot that goes wide. A thrown oaken footstool and a third report follow before [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] and [[william-chandos|Chandos]] hustle [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] into the King's Bedchamber and wedge the doors (006-224).

## Participants

- **[[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]** — the antagonist; his intelligence is as formidable as his brutality, and his negotiation reveals the full scope of his extortion scheme
- **[[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]** — the protagonist; his refusal to allow bargaining in [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]]'s presence is a defining moment of chivalric principle
- **[[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]]** — the captive and the bargaining chip; her defiance—"Nothing doing" (006-210)—and her quiet courage under [[rose-noble|Noble]]'s threats are essential to the scene's moral gravity
- **[[william-chandos|William Chandos]]** — the witness and the backup; his action in snatching the second pistol saves [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s life

## Outcome / Consequences

The confrontation in the Dining-room had profound consequences:

- **The Party Is Trapped**: [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]], [[william-chandos|Chandos]], and [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] are now pinned in the King's Closet and Bedchamber, with [[rose-noble|Noble]] in control of the passages beyond the panelled doors (006-235).
- **The Love Declared**: In the aftermath of the violence, [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] puts her arms around [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s neck, draws down his head, and kisses his lips (006-229)—a declaration that transforms the relationship between them and sets the emotional stakes for everything that follows.
- **[[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] Isolated**: [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] deduces that [[rolls-royce|the car]] sent out by [[rose-noble|Noble]] has been intercepted by [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] and the servants, meaning that [[rose-noble|Noble]] is, for the moment, alone (006-265).
- **The Standoff Begins**: The siege of the State Apartments is now formally underway, with both sides holding positions that cannot easily be broken.
