# Poganec

Poganec is a village in [[carinthia|Carinthia]], Austria, associated with the [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydell]] family. It serves as a waypoint, rest stop, and base of operations for [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]] and his companions throughout *Perishable Goods*, appearing from the earliest planning stages to the story's resolution (001-109)(009-193).

> This was Poganec: and I well remember remarking how fantastic a picture it made, for the moonlight was all about it and all its windows were open and lights burning in every room.

> (002-054)

## Location & Setting

Poganec is situated near St. Martin in [[carinthia|Carinthia]], with the address given as "Pleydell Poganec St. Martin [[carinthia|Carinthia]]" (001-109). The village is strategically positioned near a bridge and a torrent—a watercourse that "commands Poganec" (002-121). The geography offers both access and natural defense, as "a man sitting there with" a clear view could observe all approaches (002-121). [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] uses a map to point out its tactical layout: "Here's Poganec, and there's the bridge—due S" (002-125). The village lies along the route from England to the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]], making it a natural staging point for operations (001-150).

## Strategic Importance

Poganec functions as a communications hub and safe house throughout the novel. The protagonists use it to send and receive messages via trusted servants (004-187)(006-053)(009-099). Its location makes it a convenient rest point after long journeys—"we might rest at Poganec after our run" (001-150)—and a fallback position when other locations become compromised (003-101). The antagonists are aware of its importance: [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] notes "they're watching Poganec now. I mean, our movements must" be known to the enemy (002-123). By the end of the novel, [[carson|Carson]] reports that "reporters would cluster round Poganec: charabancs would be run to T" (008-238), indicating the location has become a site of public interest following the story's climactic events.
