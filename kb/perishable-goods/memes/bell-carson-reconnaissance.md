# Bell and Carson Reconnaissance

The reconnaissance by [[bell|Bell]] and [[carson|Carson]] is a vital intelligence-gathering mission conducted before the main assault on the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]]. The two men scout the castle's surroundings, mapping the approaches and identifying defensive features.

> "We're all going there at once. But Chandos and I are going to come up from the West, while Hanbury, Carson and Bell will drive from St. Martin to Sava and come from the East. ... If you find anyone suspicious, detain him, but not by force: if he won't be detained, follow him: drop Bell at the first crossroads, to put us wise: but, whatever you do, don't lose him, for he'll show us the way to Adèle."

> (004-046)

## Lead-up

After establishing a base of operations in Carinthia, [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] dispatches [[bell|Bell]] and [[carson|Carson]] — two of his most reliable men — to conduct preliminary reconnaissance of the castle. Their mission is to observe without being seen, gathering information that will shape the rescue plan.

## The Mission

The reconnaissance provides crucial tactical intelligence. The men map the castle's approaches, identify possible routes of entry, and note the positions of guards and the patterns of their patrols. They also confirm the presence of the cascade and the archway — the hidden entrance that will later prove decisive.

This mission is characteristic of [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s method: before committing to action, he gathers intelligence. The reconnaissance is unglamorous but essential — a quiet, professional piece of soldiering that lays the groundwork for everything that follows.
