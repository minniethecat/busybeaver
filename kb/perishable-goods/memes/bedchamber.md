# The King's Bedchamber

The King's Bedchamber is one of the royal apartments within the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]], entered through the King's Closet. It serves as a refuge and resting place for the rescue party during and after the operation.

## Role in the Story

The party first enters the bedchamber after ascending via the spiral staircase and the trapdoor from the archway. [[william-chandos|Chandos]] records: "So we entered the King's Bedchamber" (005-214).

The room contains the King's Bed — a massive piece of furniture that becomes significant to the operation. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] uses a carpet as a sledge to draw a stone slab across the floor and up to the King's Bed (005-236), securing it as part of the defenses.

After the rescue, the party discovers the bedchamber's other secret: doors concealed in the panelling (006-226). These hidden doors open inwards and connect to other parts of the castle.

Throughout the tense waiting period, one member of the party is always stationed in the bedchamber, listening for sounds of approach (006-286). [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] sets [[william-chandos|Chandos]] at a window with [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] while he himself watches the bedchamber (006-294).

At the story's conclusion, the bedchamber takes on a gentler role: "We bore him into the lovely bedchamber and laid him upon the King's Bed" (009-003), as the wounded [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] is tended by [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]].

## Key References

Chandos's first impression of the King's Bedchamber surpassed even the fairy-tales of his childhood:

> I have never beheld a chamber so fit to lodge a king, and all the standards of greatness which the fairytales I read as a child had set in my heart were in a twinkling supplanted by what I saw.
