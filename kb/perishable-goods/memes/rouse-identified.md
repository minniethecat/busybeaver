# Rouse Identified

The identification of [[rouse|Hannibal Rouse]] as [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s spy is the turning point of Chapter III and the operational breakthrough of the entire search. What had appeared to be a comic nuisance — the bumbling clergyman who attached himself to the party at the waterfall bridge — is revealed as a master of deception, an "old master" of infiltration whom [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]] has suspected from the first moment of their encounter (003-081)(003-084).

## The Box of "Flowers"

On the evening of the second day of the search, the party returned to the inn at [[villach|Villach]] to find [[rouse|Rouse]] still abroad in his car — a temporary relief, since the previous evening he had "sat with us during our supper and, hungry though we were, had gone far to spoil the meal" (003-001).

The respite was short. [[rouse|Rouse]] arrived and thrust his head into the room with an arch announcement: "Guess who I've seen" (003-002). After a series of inconsequential revelations, he produced a shallow white cardboard box and handed it to [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]: "He asked me to give them to you" (003-027).

[[rouse|Rouse]] explained that he had met a man by the roadside — a "Mr. Wilberforce" — who claimed to know [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]], asked after his health, and gave him these "flowers" to deliver. The man, [[rouse|Rouse]] said, was a great gardener who knew of [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s horticultural interests (003-020)(003-029).

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] took a knife to open the box. "I certainly don't know any gardener named Wilberforce," he said. "I'm certainly fond of flowers, but —"

Then he opened the lid.

"The box was full of beautiful, soft, brown hair" (003-031).

The room erupted. [[william-chandos|William Chandos]] leapt to his feet, striking the electric light and plunging the room into darkness. [[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]] threw himself forward. [[rouse|Rouse]] recoiled against the wall. [[tester|Tester]] barked. Through the chaos, [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s voice cut steady and metallic, calling for order (003-033).

When the light was restored, [[rouse|Rouse]] was "painfully getting to his feet": "It's beyond a joke. You might have hurt me very much" (003-035).

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] questioned him sharply — the description of "Wilberforce," his car, his direction — and then dismissed him: "I'm sorry for what's happened. The fault was not yours. And now please go. We've plans to make and rather a lot to discuss" (003-044).

## The Revelation

After [[rouse|Rouse]] had limped from the room, [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] asked the direct question: "Do you believe him?" (003-048).

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s reply was arresting: "Why not? If the enemy knows we're here, you can bet he hasn't missed [[rouse|Rouse]]. And there you are. I said [[rouse|Rouse]] might be of use, and I was right. He's just been of use to [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]. Perhaps tomorrow he'll be of use to us" (003-049).

Then, that night, [[william-chandos|Chandos]] witnessed something private through the shared balcony of their rooms. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] was "on his knees by the side of the bed, with one arm across his eyes, like a weeping child, and the other hand full of the curls which lay in a little heap in the midst of the counterpane" (003-051). After a long time he lifted his head and picked up [[tester|Tester]]. [[william-chandos|Chandos]] stole back to bed (003-052)(003-053).

## Mansel's Full Account

Two evenings later — after [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] had spent the day at [[poganec|Poganec]] rather than [[salzburg|Salzburg]], a deception for [[rouse|Rouse]]'s benefit — he gave [[william-chandos|Chandos]] and [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] the full truth (003-075).

"Last night you asked me if I believed him. The true answer was 'No'; and the whole truth, 'I never did'" (003-079).

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] had suspected [[rouse|Rouse]] from the first moment at the bridge. Two things had struck him. First, [[rouse|Rouse]] was exactly where a spy should be — at the enemy observation post, unable to escape because of a convenient flat tire. Second, and decisive: "He did a thing that no clergyman that wears a round collar would ever do. He put up two hands, as though to straighten a tie" (003-081). A clergyman's round collar leaves no tie to adjust; the gesture was the reflex of a man whose usual dress included one.

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] had decided to "play his game — satisfy him that we didn't suspect him at all" (003-083). He had concealed this even from [[william-chandos|Chandos]] and [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]], knowing that their "undisguised contempt" for [[rouse|Rouse]] was "simply invaluable" — exactly the behavior [[rouse|Rouse]] expected and which confirmed his cover (003-083).

## Rouse's Function

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] analyzed [[rouse|Rouse]]'s role with precision. His job was "to keep in touch" — the most difficult task for a spy facing six alert opponents. [[rouse|Rouse]] had solved it brilliantly: "he steps in *under our guard*" (003-084). The technique, [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] noted, was used in the War, but "only a born artist can bring it off. And [[rouse|Rouse]] is an old master" (003-084).

The flower-box delivery exemplified [[rouse|Rouse]]'s nerve. His story — that "Mr. Wilberforce" had given him flowers for a gardening enthusiast — was absurd on its face, yet delivered with such conviction and such readiness to deflect ("It's beyond a joke") that [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] and [[william-chandos|Chandos]] were thrown back into doubt. The next morning [[rouse|Rouse]] had completed the recovery, apologizing on the inn steps and suggesting the whole affair was a mistake (003-057). "Of course it made you suspect him, but he had you back in your place by eight the next day" (003-116).

## The Trap

Now that [[rouse|Rouse]] was confirmed as [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s man, [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s strategic calculus shifted. [[rouse|Rouse]] still believed his cover intact. Therefore, when news arose that his chief needed to hear, [[rouse|Rouse]] would move — "he'll go when he thinks he'd better and never take the trouble to look behind" (003-085).

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] predicted: "I think he'll think he'd better before very long, for he'll have some news for [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] which will not wait" (003-085). The trap was set. "And now, if you please, we shall all go to bed at once, for I've no idea when or where we shall sleep again" (003-086).

The prediction would be fulfilled within hours, when [[major-pleydell|Major Pleydell]]'s staged arrival at the inn — shouting that [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] "had got a message through" (003-094) — sent [[rouse|Rouse]] scrambling for a train to [[lass|Lass]], with [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s party aboard the same sleeping car (003-117).

## Significance

The identification of [[rouse|Rouse]] is the narrative's most elegant piece of tradecraft. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] detects a spy by a single gesture no larger than two fingers at a collar; he then runs the spy as a double agent for days, extracting value from [[rouse|Rouse]]'s continued presence while feeding him disinformation; and, at the critical moment, he exploits [[rouse|Rouse]]'s confidence to follow him directly to [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]]'s location.

The episode also contains one of the novel's most intimate moments: [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] kneeling by his bed with a handful of [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]]'s hair. The image — the implacable commander broken, if only in the privacy of night — humanizes the calculating mind that has just laid a flawless trap.

Mansel explains how Rouse outmaneuvered their surveillance:

> "Now, first, what was his job? His job was to keep in touch. Rose Noble holds a fine hand, but he knows us too well to sleep sound when we're out of his ken. So Rouse was to keep in touch — an extremely difficult job. We were six to one, you see, and ready to find a spy under every hat. So what does he do but step in *under our guard*? It was done in the War, you know: but only a born artist can bring it off. And Rouse is an old master."
>
> (003-084)
