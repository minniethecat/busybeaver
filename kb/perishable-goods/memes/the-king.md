# The King

The King is a spectral presence in *Perishable Goods* — the unnamed historical monarch whose royal apartments give the chambers of the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] their grandiose names. Though never personified or directly referenced in the narrative, the King's legacy infuses the fortress with a sense of faded majesty.

## Role in the Story

The royal nomenclature of the Castle of Gath's chambers — the King's Closet (005-201), the King's Bedchamber (004-129), the Royal Dining-Room (007-141) — evokes a past when the castle was a seat of power rather than a criminal stronghold. This historical layering creates a sharp contrast with [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s current occupation of the fortress. Where kings once held court, a gangster now runs a prison.

> Gath was builted in the end of the fifteenth century by the great King Maximilian, Holy Roman Emperor, "The Last of the Knights," as he is named by his proud loving subjects, so as to be a tranquil castles where he can retire to rest sometimes from the cares of reign.
>
> (004-129)

The King is invoked most directly through a bookseller's guide to the Castle of Gath, which [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] consults before the operation. The guide, written for tourists, describes the royal apartments in reverent detail — the King's Bedchamber, the King's Closet, the Royal Dining-Room — all preserved as they were when "the great King died" (004-129). This guide becomes a tactical document; Mansel's study of it enables him to navigate the castle's secret passages and anticipate the geography of the coming fight.

## The King as Symbol

Beyond its practical function, the guide's description of the King serves a symbolic purpose. The image of King Maximilian — "The Last of the Knights" — seeking peace and refreshment in his mountain retreat stands in pointed contrast to [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]], who has turned the same chambers into a prison for [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]]. The "departed majesty" of the King's apartments becomes a measure of how far the castle has fallen.
