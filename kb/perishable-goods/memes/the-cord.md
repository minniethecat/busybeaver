# The Cord

The cord — a strong rescue rope — is a critical piece of equipment in the [[castle-of-gath-operation|Castle of Gath Operation]]. It serves a dual role: first as the means of [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]]'s escape, and later as the evidence of the party's entrapment.

## Role in the Story

When [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] seizes the moment to rescue Adèle, his first command is "Pull up the rope" (006-067). The rope, already run from the archway to the base of the cliff, is the pre-planned escape route. Adèle is lowered down the cliff face, and the rope is then withdrawn into the channel, lying "as snug and as taut as though it had never been touched" (006-083).

> "Pull up the rope," said Mansel; and, before I could think, he was gone.
>
> (006-067)

But the discovery of the cord becomes the trigger for [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s retaliation. His voice rings out in fury: "By ——, she's gone. Look at that —— cord" (006-079). The sight of the abandoned rope on the terrace tells Noble everything: his hostage has been taken from under his nose.

Later, Noble's men cut the waterfall rope — the line that had been used to haul supplies up the cliff face. This act traps the party inside the castle, transforming their position from triumphant rescuers to besieged fugitives.
