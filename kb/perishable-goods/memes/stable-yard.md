# Stable Yard

**Stable Yard** is a location in St. James's, London, adjacent to [[cleveland-row|Cleveland Row]], where [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]] kept a garage that housed his [[rolls-royce|Rolls-Royce]] motor-car. It appears in Chapter I as the site of the intense mechanical preparations undertaken on the eve of the party's departure for [[carinthia|Carinthia]], and it serves as the final staging point from which the cars were brought round to Cleveland Row for the expedition's start.

> Except that we drove to St. James's and back again, we laboured incessantly till four, but, when I reported to Mansel at five o'clock, the cars were as ready for the road as the wit of man could make them and both were at hand in his garage by Stable Yard. There they were packed and loaded by Carson alone, and at half past six they were standing in Cleveland Row.

> (001-133)

## The Day of Preparation

The period between the receipt of the telegram announcing [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]]'s kidnapping and the party's departure for France was barely thirty-six hours, and every hour was filled with urgent preparation. On the morning of the day before departure, [[william-chandos|William Chandos]] was dispatched to Mansel's coachbuilder with instructions to have a hidden coffer fitted to his car — necessary because the party would be carrying arms and needed secure, concealed storage (001-133). Mansel's own Rolls-Royce already possessed such fittings, having been "furnished with secret lockers and trays" (001-133).

The work continued through the day. The coachbuilder urged his men; Chandos and [[carson|Carson]] laboured beside them. Except for a drive to St. James's and back, they worked "incessantly till four" (001-138). When Chandos reported to Mansel at five o'clock, the work was complete: "the cars were as ready for the road as the wit of man could make them and both were at hand in his garage by Stable Yard" (001-138).

## The Final Loading

With the cars mechanically prepared and the hidden compartments fitted, the final task fell to Carson alone. The two cars — Mansel's Rolls-Royce and Chandos's own vehicle — were "packed and loaded by Carson alone" (001-138) in the garage at Stable Yard. This was a task requiring considerable skill and discretion: the cars needed to carry not only the party's personal luggage but also the arms and ammunition now concealed in their secret compartments, the food and supplies for a cross-continental journey, and — critically — [[dressing-case|Adèle's dressing-case]], packed at Mansel's request with the things she would need upon her recovery.

At half past six, the loaded cars were driven the short distance from Stable Yard to Cleveland Row, where they stood ready for the departure that would commence at a quarter to seven [001-138, 001-141].

## Location and Significance

The precise location of Stable Yard within St. James's is not elaborated in the text, but its name — "Stable Yard" — is itself suggestive. In the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries, the mews and stable yards behind the great houses of St. James's served exactly the function their name implies: they were the service quarters where horses, carriages, and later motor-cars were kept, maintained, and made ready. Mansel's garage by Stable Yard thus belongs to a long tradition of service architecture tucked discreetly behind the grand facades of gentleman's London.

The fact that the garage was at Stable Yard rather than within the Cleveland Row flat itself is also significant. It speaks to Mansel's habit of maintaining separation between the different spheres of his life: the flat was for living and for conference, the garage was for the machines and the work of preparation. This separation of functions — the clean, quiet world of the flat, the greasy, urgent world of the garage — mirrors the larger separation in Mansel's life between the gentleman of leisure and the operational commander.
