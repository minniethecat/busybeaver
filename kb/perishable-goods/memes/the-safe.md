# The Safe

The wall-safe in [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s flat at [[cleveland-row|Cleveland Row]] is the target of [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s burglary — the crime that sets the novel's plot in motion. The burglars cut open the safe and extracted its contents, but tellingly left behind money and notes, revealing that the theft was targeted at specific documents.

## Role in the Story

[[william-chandos|William Chandos]] arrives at [[cleveland-row|Cleveland Row]] to find the safe "cut open" and its contents stolen (001-016). The selective nature of the theft — valuables left behind, papers taken — immediately signals to [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] that [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] is responsible and that the stolen items are of personal rather than monetary value.

> "They came for my papers," said Mansel. "And got them." He pointed to his writing-table. "In the right-hand pedestal of that is a little safe. They cut it open and took my papers away. There were fifty sovereigns there and five hundred pounds in notes: but they didn't take them: so it looks as though they meant me to understand that they came for my papers alone."
>
> (001-016)

The safe's contents include letters belonging to [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]] — private correspondence that [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] can use as leverage or intelligence. The theft of the safe thus prefigures the kidnapping: in both cases, [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] takes what is most personal and uses it as a weapon.
