# Dr. Buchinger

Dr. Buchinger is an Innsbruck surgeon summoned to the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]]
after [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]] is shot in the final confrontation.
More experienced and composed than his colleague [[rachel|Dr. Rachel]],
Buchinger leads the operation that saves [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s life and
provides the medical judgment that guides his recovery.

## Role in the Story

Introduced by [[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]] as "an Innsbruck man" (009-062),
Buchinger arrives at a critical moment — [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] is just showing
him out when [[william-chandos|Chandos]] makes a timely intervention (009-071).

Buchinger's surgical skill is decisive: without him, [[william-chandos|Chandos]]
reflects, they would have been "hopeless" (009-077).
After the operation, Buchinger tells the party plainly that
[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] will need six days of immobility before he can be
moved (009-082), (009-084).

Unlike the bemused [[rachel|Rachel]], Buchinger is "far too wary to ask any
questions" about the irregular circumstances (009-088) — a discretion that
serves the party well. His quiet professionalism and medical authority
provide the final assurance that [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] will survive.

## The Summoning

[[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]] introduces Buchinger as "an Innsbruck surgeon, who happened to be at Lass" (009-062). The phrasing is characteristically understated — in fact, [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] has been dispatched on a desperate medical errand after [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] is shot in the final confrontation. That an Innsbruck surgeon of Buchinger's caliber should be present in the small town of [[lass|Lass]] at precisely the right moment is one of the strokes of fortune that allows the party to survive.

When [[william-chandos|Chandos]] first encounters Buchinger, he mistakes him for the local doctor and the local for Buchinger's man (009-071). The confusion is quickly resolved, but it underscores the irregular and improvised nature of the medical arrangements. Buchinger was actually leaving to catch his train when intercepted (009-079); he is persuaded to stay and operate only after [[william-chandos|Chandos]] explains that "this gentleman is saving a lady's name," which seems to reassure the surgeon (009-071).

## The Operation

The operation is described through [[william-chandos|Chandos]]'s retrospective narration. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]], despite his grave wound, gives the surgeons directions and tells [[rachel|Rachel]] to "get those things into the car" (009-077). [[william-chandos|Chandos]]'s assessment is blunt: "Without Buchinger, he'd have been hopeless — forgotten from A to Z. But Buchinger knows his job" (009-077). The contrast between the two doctors is stark: while [[rachel|Rachel]] is "too much bemused" by the pressure, Buchinger is composed, professional, and entirely in command (009-088).

Buchinger's post-operative honesty is bracing. He tells the party plainly that [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] "might die any minute during the next three days" (009-082). This is not pessimism but surgical candor — Buchinger sets realistic expectations and then works to exceed them. He prescribes six days of immobility before [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] can be moved (009-082), and then departs with the reassuring prognosis that his patient "will recover fast" (009-102).

## Discretion and Departure

Buchinger's most valuable quality, beyond his surgical skill, is his discretion. The narrator observes that Buchinger "must have suspected that Mansel's was not the only blood that had been shed, but he saw the danger of knowledge and held his peace" (009-088). In a narrative filled with secrets — the identity of [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]], the death of [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]], the death of [[casemate|Casemate]] — Buchinger's refusal to inquire is a form of professional wisdom. He treats the patient, asks no questions, and departs.

A room is cleaned and prepared for Buchinger and [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] in the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] (009-098). [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] lies for three days "as Buchinger had said" (009-100), confirming the surgeon's prognosis. With his departure, the crisis of [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s wounding is formally closed, and the long recovery — six days of immobility, then twice-daily attendance by [[rachel|Rachel]] — can begin.
