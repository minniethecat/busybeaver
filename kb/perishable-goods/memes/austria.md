# Austria

Austria is the central European country where much of *Perishable Goods* takes place. [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]] is kidnapped and held captive there, and the [[castle-of-gath-operation|Castle of Gath Operation]] unfolds across its Carinthian landscape.

## Role in the Story

The shadow of the missing Adèle haunts the journey from its outset:

> We had but four to travel as far as Dieppe: yet in a way we were five, for the shadow of Adèle Pleydell went with us wherever we went.
>
> (001-140)

The certainty that Adèle is in Austria shapes the entire rescue mission. [[william-chandos|Chandos]] reasons: "That Adèle was in Austria seemed certain, for without her passport and against her will she could hardly be taken out: moreover the countryside lent itself admirably to such a crime" (001-136).

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] is more circumspect: "It's slight enough in all conscience, but — well, I don't know where Adèle is, and Austria's quite a big place" (002-020). The country's scale makes the search daunting, and the party must rely on intelligence and deduction to narrow their search.

The party enters Austria after a long night drive from France: "Night came upon us soon after eight o'clock, but a fine moon was westering, and two and a half hours later we entered Austria" (002-029).

The Austrian setting — with its remote villages, mountain passes, and medieval fortresses — provides the perfect backdrop for a thriller of abduction and rescue. The isolation of the countryside means that the law is distant and the party must rely entirely on their own resources.
