# Major Pleydell

Major Pleydell is the head of the [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydell]] family and the owner of [[white-ladies|White Ladies]], the family estate in the [[new-forest|New Forest]]. When his youngest sister [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] is kidnapped in [[carinthia|Carinthia]], Major Pleydell travels to Austria to assist [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s rescue party, providing local knowledge, a base of operations, and the steady presence of a concerned brother.

## Role in the Story

Major Pleydell serves as the [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydell]] family's representative in the rescue effort. It is he who first proposes the trip to Austria (001-037) and who meets [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s party upon their arrival at the station (002-044). Though he is not a man of action in the mould of [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] or [[william-chandos|Chandos]], his steadiness and his willingness to place his family's resources at the party's disposal make him an essential ally. He drives [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] to the spot where [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]]'s glove was found (002-108) and provides his car for the party's operations (003-100).

## Key Appearances

- **Chapter I** — Proposes that the party go to Austria to stay with [[pleydell-family|the Pleydells]] (001-037); [[pleydell-family|the Pleydells]] and [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] leave for the continent shortly after (001-058).
- **Chapter II** — Meets [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s party at the top of the stairs upon their arrival (002-044); his sister [[daphne-pleydell|Daphne]] rises to greet them (002-045); comes forward with a glass in his hand (002-052); speaks to the party about the search (002-082); thrusts his hand into a small door that has been opened (002-093); is well up the drive, shouting, as the party regains their wits after a startling discovery (002-095); the decision is made that [[pleydell-family|the Pleydells]] should take no action, whatever happened (002-106); drives with [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] to the spot where the glove was found (002-108).
- **Chapter III** — Steps back on the pavement after a tense exchange (003-093); the party leaves in his car, with [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] driving and Major Pleydell beside him (003-100); to the party's surprise, [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] bids him goodbye shortly after (003-103).
- **Chapter IV** — [[carson|Carson]] and [[rowley|Rowley]] are sent to [[poganec|Poganec]] to take [[captain-pleydell|Captain Pleydell]] the party's news (004-157).

## Quotations

Major Pleydell has relatively little direct speech in the narrative; his presence is felt through action and the respect he commands as head of the [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydell]] family. When he did speak, however, his words carried the weight of his position — as when he responded to Adèle's playful self-deprecation at White Ladies:

> Yet all were equal; and, when once Adèle Pleydell affirmed that she was an interloper and the only one of them whose ancestors had not known White Ladies, there was an amicable uproar, and Major Pleydell said gravely, "That I regard as one of the misfortunes of our House."
>
> (001-032)
