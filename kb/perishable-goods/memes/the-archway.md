# The Archway

The archway of the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] is one of the castle's defensive passages — a stone portal whose heavy door poses a tactical obstacle for the rescue party. [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s reconnaissance identifies it as a point that must be secured before any assault on the [[castle-of-gath-tower|tower]].

> "The door at the mouth of the archway is shut and locked. I managed to pass beneath it, by lying down in the channel and working my way along. In the side of the archway I found a flight of steps — a very steep spiral staircase, that comes to a sudden end."

> (005-201)

## Role in the Story

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] reports that "the door at the mouth of the archway is shut and locked" (005-201), identifying it as one of three locked doors that guard the approach to [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]]'s prison. The archway is the outermost of these barriers — the first line of the castle's internal defenses.

The archway's massive construction reflects the castle's medieval origins. Its presence forces the rescue party to plan carefully, treating the castle not as a building but as a fortress — each door and passage requiring either stealth, force, or guile to overcome.
