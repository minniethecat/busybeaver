# Carson and Bell Reconnaissance

The Carson and Bell Reconnaissance was the sustained surveillance and support operation carried out by [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s two servants, [[carson|Carson]] and [[bell|Bell]], during the early phases of the [[castle-of-gath-operation|Castle of Gath Operation]]. While [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]], [[william-chandos|William Chandos]], and [[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]] conducted close reconnaissance on foot, [[carson|Carson]] and [[bell|Bell]] guarded the approaches, watched the crossroads, maintained the [[rolls-royce|Rolls-Royces]], and provided the logistical backbone that made the entire campaign possible.

## Context / Lead-up

Following [[william-chandos|Chandos]]'s return from his rooftop ride to the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]], the party gathered at the crossroads and laid their plans. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] divided his forces with characteristic precision: two cars would scour the countryside openly—courting observation from the castle—while secretly studying the terrain. The servants were assigned specific duties that would prove critical to the operation's success.

## Sequence of Events

1. **Division of Forces** — After surveying the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] from the wood's edge, [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] lays out the reconnaissance plan: two patrols will comb the countryside for five miles around, making no attempt at concealment, while secretly visiting vantage points from which the castle can be studied (004-155).
2. **Servant Assignments** — [[carson|Carson]] and [[bell|Bell]] are assigned to specific roles. [[bell|Bell]] drives with [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] in the second car, conducting patrols; [[carson|Carson]] and [[rowley|Rowley]] are ordered to lie close in a wood and take their rest, "for tonight you two must drive to Poganec and take Captain Pleydell our news" (004-157).
3. **Crossroads Watch** — While the patrols are out, one servant always watches the crossroads. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s orders are clear: "If one of the enemy passed he was not to be stopped or followed, but only marked"—unless it was [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] himself, in which case "you will shoot him at sight" (004-114).
4. **Evening Courier Run** — After sundown, having eaten at [[lass|Lass]], [[carson|Carson]] and [[rowley|Rowley]] set out for [[poganec|Poganec]], carrying a note from [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] to Captain [[daphne-pleydell|Daphne Pleydell]] (004-187). The note, brief and measured, reads: "I know where Adèle is confined and saw her today from a distance... I managed to attract her attention, and she now knows that help is at hand" (004-188).
5. **Reconnaissance Surveys** — Throughout the following days, [[carson|Carson]] and [[bell|Bell]] participate in the mapping of the countryside and the study of the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] from every angle, including two specific vantage points from which the cliffs might be scaled (004-155).
6. **The Jute Capture** — On the second morning, as both cars head out together from [[lass|Lass]], [[bell|Bell]] helps [[william-chandos|Chandos]] capture [[jute|Jute]], who has risen from the bushes to watch [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s car pass (005-078). [[bell|Bell]] leans out and drags [[jute|Jute]]'s legs into the car, and later straps his ankles and wrists (005-079).

## Participants

- **[[carson|Carson]]** — [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s body-servant; a meticulous observer who makes critical tactical assessments, including the exact height of the castle walls ("forty feet high... the first windows are fourteen feet up") (005-055)
- **[[bell|Bell]]** — the resourceful servant who earlier executed the bicycle diversion; drives the second car, assists in reconnaissance, and captures [[jute|Jute]]
- **[[rowley|Rowley]]** — the third servant; rises from a ditch at the crossroads to guide [[william-chandos|Chandos]] to [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] (004-104); later accompanies [[carson|Carson]] on the courier run to [[poganec|Poganec]]
- **[[tester|Tester]]** — [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s dog; left with the cars during foot patrols and fed by [[carson|Carson]] (004-104)

## Outcome / Consequences

The reconnaissance conducted by [[carson|Carson]] and [[bell|Bell]] established the framework of intelligence and logistics upon which the entire [[castle-of-gath-operation|Castle of Gath Operation]] rested. Key outcomes included:

- **Tactical Intelligence**: [[carson|Carson]]'s precise observations of the castle walls—forty feet high, first windows at fourteen feet, heavily barred—directly informed [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s decision to commission the three custom-made wrought-iron ladders (005-057)(005-059).
- **Lines of Communication**: The courier run to [[poganec|Poganec]] kept Captain [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydell]] informed and maintained the vital link to the outside world.
- **Capture of [[jute|Jute]]**: The serendipitous capture on the road to the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] led directly to the [[jute-confrontation|Jute Confrontation]] and, ultimately, to the high-stakes interrogation that would culminate in [[jute|Jute]]'s execution.
- **Deception Maintained**: By continuing to patrol openly and scout the countryside, [[carson|Carson]] and [[bell|Bell]] helped sustain [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s fiction that the party had not yet located the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]].
