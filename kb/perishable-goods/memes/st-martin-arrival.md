# Arrival at St. Martin

The arrival at St. Martin — more precisely, at the Pleydell farm of [[poganec|Poganec]] — marks the end of the thousand-mile cross-continental drive and the beginning of the search for [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]] on the ground in [[carinthia|Carinthia]]. The scene, set in the small hours of the morning by moonlight, introduces the full emotional gravity of the situation through the shattered state of the [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydell]] family.

## The Approach

After dropping [[rowley|Rowley]] at [[villach|Villach]] to secure lodgings there, the party of [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]], [[william-chandos|William Chandos]], [[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]], [[bell|Bell]], and [[carson|Carson]] pressed on through "most handsome country, very mountainous and closely wooded, with streams and pastures lying in every dale" (002-038). The mountain air invigorated the engines, and "the cars sailed over the hills like giants refreshed" (002-038).

They swept through St. Martin village to hear the church clock strike one (002-039). Ten minutes later they came in sight of [[poganec|Poganec]]: "a long low house, lying in the lap of a meadow, with rising woods behind" (002-040). The scene was rendered "fantastic" by the moonlight that lay all about it, and by the fact that every window was open and lights burning in every room (002-040) — a house awake with anguish.

> We swept through St. Martin, to hear the church clock strike one, and ten minutes later sighted a long low house, lying in the lap of a meadow, with rising woods behind. This was Poganec: and I well remember remarking how fantastic a picture it made, for the moonlight was all about it and all its windows were open and lights burning in every room.
>
> (002-039)

## The Meeting

[[william-chandos|Chandos]] and [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] waited downstairs while [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] went up to brief his cousins. After some ten minutes, [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] appeared at a first-floor window and summoned them: "Will you two come up?" (002-042).

The front door stood open. [[major-pleydell|Major Pleydell]] met them at the top of the stairs and brought them into a room at the back of the house (002-044). There, [[daphne-pleydell|Daphne Pleydell]] rose to greet them, and her brother, Captain "Boy" [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydell]], propped with pillows, called to them from the bed. Beside him sat [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] (002-045).

## Captain Pleydell's Condition

The sight of Captain [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydell]] shocked [[william-chandos|Chandos]] profoundly. The man greeted him with a smile and a jest about [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s driving: "[[jonathan-mansel|Jonah]] can drive, can't he?" (002-047). He spoke lightly of cars, recalling timed runs, "making smooth an encounter which I had been dreading more than I care to admit" (002-048).

But the physical evidence of his suffering was unmistakable. "I could not cope with such bravery," [[william-chandos|Chandos]] wrote. "The man was jesting: his eyes were steady and his speech was firm, but the thick, dark hair I remembered was white as snow" (002-051).

The transformation had occurred in three days: "His hair was grey on Tuesday, and yesterday it was white" (002-083). [[major-pleydell|Major Pleydell]] would later explain the terrible logic: "If they don't make a demand, he'll lose his mind. He hasn't slept since she went... If they'd only hold her to ransom, he wouldn't care. If they asked a million, he wouldn't care a damn. We might not be able to pay it, but he'd know she was safe and sound. But when the hours go by and there's no demand —" (002-083).

## The Briefing

[[major-pleydell|Major Pleydell]] — by unspoken consent treated as head of the house alongside [[daphne-pleydell|Daphne]] — turned to business (002-053).

He recited the known facts with a soldier's precision (002-054): [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] had gone for a ride at seven on Monday morning, riding in the direction of [[sava|Sava]], a village ten miles away. Her mare returned alone at a quarter past eight, clean and unhurt. All farms in the direction of [[sava|Sava]] were searched without success. No one had seen [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] that morning.

Then the critical detail: "Early on Tuesday morning Fitch found one of her gloves. It was lying by the side of the road almost exactly eleven miles from here, *but not in the direction of Sava*" (002-055).

"Well, there you are," [[major-pleydell|Major Pleydell]] concluded. "I'm afraid I've no more to say" (002-056).

## The Dark Turn

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] asked the obvious question: "You've had no demand for money?" (002-058).

Captain [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydell]] looked away. "None," he said quietly, and pointed to a chest of drawers containing five thousand pounds in notes. "They've only to come and ask. But they — they don't do that" (002-060).

His next words struck the room with the force of a physical blow: "I know. I didn't get it at first. You know, I made quite certain that they were out for money. *It never occurred to me that Adèle had been taken for herself*" (002-062).

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] started to his feet. "I don't believe it," he cried (002-064). A terrible exchange followed in which Captain [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydell]] painted the future — days becoming weeks, no demand, people forgetting what she looked like — while [[daphne-pleydell|Daphne]] sobbed and [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] insisted, "I know they're out for money" (002-069)(002-075).

The scene reached its nadir when Captain [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydell]] lay back and closed his eyes: "Face the facts: and, when you've had a good look at them, come back and try to tell me not to turn my face to the wall" (002-076).

## Significance

The arrival at [[poganec|Poganec]] is the emotional low point from which the novel's recovery narrative ascends. It establishes the human cost of [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s crime — not merely the abduction of a woman, but the psychological destruction of her husband, the terror of a household, and the wounding of a family. Captain [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydell]]'s prematurely white hair becomes a silent emblem of what is at stake.

The scene also demonstrates the peculiar courage of the [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydells]]: the husband who can jest while breaking, the sister who ministers while weeping, the brother who recites facts while his world collapses. They are, [[william-chandos|Chandos]] had earlier noted, the kind of people "all five members of one family could prove so charming" (001-031). Here that charm is tested under fire.
