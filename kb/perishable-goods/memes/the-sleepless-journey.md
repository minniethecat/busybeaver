# The Sleepless Journey

The sleepless journey is the punishing cross-continental drive that carries [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s rescue party from the English Channel to the Austrian village of [[st-martin|St. Martin]]. Covering hundreds of miles with minimal rest, the drive establishes the novel's breathless pace and the physical toll of the rescue mission.

## The Route

From Folkestone, the party crosses the Channel and drives "to Salzburg as hard and as straight as we could and thence direct to St. Martin" (001-135). The route takes them through France and across the Austrian border, bypassing cities and pushing through the night. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] hopes to "be at St. Martin in twenty-four hours" (001-149) — an almost impossibly ambitious target.

> From the port we should drive to Salzburg as hard and as straight as we could and thence direct to St. Martin, for that was the name of the village which served the Pleydells' farm.
>
> (001-135)

The party reaches St. Martin as the church clock strikes one in the morning (002-039), having driven through the night and most of the previous day. The physical cost is implicit but palpable — [[william-chandos|William Chandos]]'s narration, while characteristically understated, conveys the grinding exhaustion of the drive.

## Logistics

The journey is enabled by the [[rolls-royce|Rolls-Royces]] and the skilled driving of [[carson|Carson]] and [[bell|Bell]], who "were accustomed to handling a car" (001-063). The party travels in two cars, with the servants sharing the driving duties. Stops are limited to fueling and essential maintenance — the mission allows no time for comfort.
