# The Cascade

The cascade is a waterfall that tumbles down the cliff face of the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]], directly in front of the archway door. This natural feature provides both concealment and hardship for the rescue party.

## Role in the Story

The cascade is the reason the archway entrance is so well hidden. Water pours continuously over the rock, masking the door and the channel beneath it. When [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] and [[william-chandos|Chandos]] shelter in the archway after the failed nighttime assault, they are drenched by the cascade's spray.

The archway door opens beneath the cascade, and a channel of flowing water passes under the door. This channel is what allows [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] to observe the terrace — peering under the door through the running water — and what permits a rope to be extended to the cliff base for the rescue.

The cascade's physical form is precisely rendered:

> The cliff falls down directly and the cascade curves over it like a bow.
>
> (004-129)

The cascade embodies the dual nature of the castle: a place of beauty that is also a place of hardship. Its constant roar masks the sounds of the party's movements, but its cold, wet embrace makes the night in the archway a miserable ordeal.
