# Channel Crossing

The Channel Crossing is the operational departure of [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s rescue party from [[england|England]] to [[france|France]], following the crisis triggered by the arrival of the telegram reporting [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]]'s disappearance (001-097). This crossing, undertaken the night after the emergency council at [[cleveland-square|Cleveland Row]], marks the transition from planning to execution and reveals that [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s network is already tracking their movements (001-142).

## Preparations and Departure

The previous day had been consumed by frantic preparation. [[william-chandos|William Chandos]] spent it at a coachbuilder's fitting a hidden coffer for arms into [[rolls-royce|the car]] (001-133), while [[bell|Bell]] had been dispatched ahead to [[dieppe|Dieppe]] by an earlier boat to arrange food, petrol, and bribes for [[customs-frontier|customs]] (001-134). [[rowley|Rowley]] departed with the heavier baggage by train (001-132). By half past six in the evening, both [[rolls-royce|Rolls-Royce]] cars were standing loaded in [[cleveland-square|Cleveland Row]], having been prepared "as ready for the road as the wit of man could make them" (001-138).

The party — [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]], [[william-chandos|Chandos]], [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]], [[carson|Carson]], and [[tester|Tester]] the Sealyham — departed at a quarter to seven (001-141). They dined at Newhaven, saw [[the-cars|the cars]] taken aboard the steamer, and then retired to their cabins. All four men knew that "until we were come to [[carinthia|Carinthia]], we should none of us sleep again" (001-143).

## The Channel Passage

The steward roused [[william-chandos|Chandos]] half an hour before [[the-pinnace|the boat]] was due at [[dieppe|Dieppe]]. He came on deck to find "a clean morning and two or three lights marking the coast of [[france|France]]" (001-144). Below decks, [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] and [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] were making "a wretched meal" (001-145) — the tension of the mission already weighing on them.

During breakfast, [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] gave [[william-chandos|Chandos]] a [[map|map]] with the route to [[carinthia|Carinthia]] marked in blue pencil, instructing him to use it only if the two [[rolls-royce|cars]] became separated (001-147). [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] would lead; [[william-chandos|Chandos]] was to keep him in sight. "If you want to attract my attention, use your horn," [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] said (001-147). The goal was stark: "First stop, [[carinthia|Carinthia]]" (001-148). [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] hoped they would reach [[st-martin|St. Martin]] in twenty-four hours (001-149).

The party had already decided to leave their baggage at [[villach|Villach]], at an inn they knew, rather than stay at [[st-martin-arrival|the Pleydell farm]] in [[poganec|Poganec]] — a harsh but necessary decision, since they expected to be "constantly moving in search or pursuit of [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]]" and could not afford any obligation that would hamper their movements (001-150).

## Bell's Efficiency at Dieppe

As the steamer entered [[dieppe|Dieppe]] harbour, the party came on deck and made out [[bell|Bell]] standing with three officials at the edge of the quay (001-151). [[bell|Bell]] had promised the officials ten thousand francs if the party cleared [[customs-frontier|customs]] in half an hour (001-134). He had improved on this: one [[customs-frontier|customs]] officer boarded the steamer before she was at rest and took [[stolen-letters|the papers]] without a word (001-151).

The cars were normally unloaded after the registered baggage, but [[bell|Bell]]'s arrangements reversed this. "[[bell|Bell|Full marks to Bell]]," [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] said. "They're going to take [[the-cars|the cars]] first" (001-154). [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s [[rolls-royce|Rolls]] was ashore before any of the passengers, and, as the second car was landed, the [[customs-frontier|customs]] official returned with the documents stamped and signed (001-156). The baggage was hastily chalked.

When [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] asked how long the clearance had taken, [[bell|Bell]] replied: "Just under a quarter of an hour, sir. I promised them five thousand if they did it in half an hour, and I said I'd double the money if they did it in half the time" (001-159). Such was the power of ready money.

## The Planted Warning

As the second car moved slowly towards the street in the darkness, with the eastern sky pale but the quay still dark, [[william-chandos|Chandos]] became aware of a paper he seemed to have sat upon. He plucked it from under his legs: it proved to be a dirty envelope, sealed but bearing no superscription (001-167).

[[william-chandos|Chandos]] assumed it was a bill of lading that had fallen from the baggage passing overhead on the crane's nets (001-169). But when [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] ripped open the envelope and held the contents to the light of the instrument-board lamp, a chill spread through [[rolls-royce|the car]].

It was a half-sheet of notepaper bearing four printed words: **"The goods are perishable"** (001-176).

The message was unmistakable. [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s agents had been on the quay, watching. One of them had dropped this envelope onto [[rolls-royce|the car]] seat while it stood unattended during unloading. The warning from the ransom letter — "The stolen goods will be returned... No time should be wasted for the goods are perishable" (001-070) — had been delivered again, directly to the rescue party at the very moment of their arrival on the Continent. [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] was demonstrating his reach and reminding them that he held [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] in his power.

## Significance

The Channel crossing transforms the crisis from a domestic emergency in [[cleveland-square|Cleveland Row]] into an active pursuit on European soil. The discovery of the planted note proves that [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s surveillance is continuous and that the rescue party's every movement is being watched. This knowledge shapes [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s subsequent strategy — he will not attempt stealth but rather accept visibility, knowing that so long as [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] knows where the party is searching, he may be lulled into a false sense of security (001-142).

The crossing also demonstrates the efficiency of [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s household staff. [[bell|Bell]]'s deft handling of [[customs-frontier|the French customs]] — shaving the clearance from a possible two hours to under fifteen minutes — gains the party precious time. As [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] later reflects, such speed is essential when "[[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] in [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s hands" (001-107) is [[rolls-royce|the car]]go they must recover before it "perishes."

The party crosses [[the-channel|the English Channel]] under the shadow of urgency:

> We dined at Newhaven and saw the cars taken on board: then we turned into our cabins to take what rest we could, for, though Mansel had not said so, we all four knew very well that, until we were come to Carinthia, we should none of us sleep again.
>
> (001-143)
