# Poganec Reconnaissance

The Poganec Reconnaissance is the systematic search operation mounted by [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s party from their base at [[poganec|Poganec]] and [[villach|Villach]] in the days immediately following their arrival in [[carinthia|Carinthia]]. Spanning Chapter II, the operation combines physical searches, strategic observation, and the identification of the enemy's own observation post — while the party contends with the baffling presence of the clergyman [[rouse|Hannibal Rouse]].

> This was Poganec: and I well remember remarking how fantastic a picture it made, for the moonlight was all about it and all its windows were open and lights burning in every room.
>
> (002-040)

## The Morning Council

A brief council was held the next morning at a quarter to twelve (002-101). Captain [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydell]], buoyed by the discovery of the ransom demand in the gate-box, was "a new man" — the grey look gone from his face, speaking with eagerness (002-101). His sister sat beside him "with shining eyes" (002-101).

All agreed that the half-million-pound demand was "fantastic" and should be ignored (002-103). When [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] announced that the party would leave after lunch and not return — to operate independently and without the constraint of "reporting progress" — [[daphne-pleydell|Daphne]] began to protest, but the sick man inclined his head: "You were ever a dark horse, [[jonathan-mansel|Jonah]], and dark horses train alone" (002-104).

A security protocol was established: the Pleydells would take no action without communicating with the party, accept no message purporting to come from them unless one of the principals delivered it, and recognize no intermediary but [[major-pleydell|Major Pleydell]] or servants they knew (002-106).

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] had already begun his own reconnaissance. He had ridden most of the way to [[sava|Sava]] and driven with [[major-pleydell|Major Pleydell]] to the spot where [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]]'s glove had been found. He had also interviewed all the farm servants, finding them "honest, though painfully unobservant" (002-108).

## The Observation Post

Before departing, [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] took [[william-chandos|William Chandos]] and [[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]] into a parlour and shut the door. He asked them to look out the window.

The view was of a valley, fresh and green, and beyond it "a press of high hills, rising up very sudden and wooded cap-à-pie," four miles distant (002-118). High in the hills a waterfall plunged down — a considerable cascade, visible as an unbroken white line from where they stood. A fifth of the way down the fall was a bridge (002-119).

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s deduction was precise: "Their observation post was somewhere about that bridge. A post in the woods would be useless, for the trees would get in the way; but that torrent commands [[poganec|Poganec]]; and a man sitting there with a glass could see anyone come and go" (002-121).

The bridge also served a road to [[sava|Sava]] — the only road in the vicinity. And, critically, from the bridge a watcher could see [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] ride out each morning and note the direction she took, then radio or telephone ahead so that the snatch party could move to intercept her six miles away (002-116)(002-117).

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] warned them not to stare directly: "I hope and believe that they're watching [[poganec|Poganec]] now. I mean, our movements must interest them no end. So don't give them food for thought by looking straight into their eyes" (002-123).

## The First Sortie

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] immediately organized a two-pronged approach to the bridge. [[william-chandos|Chandos]] would go with him, approaching from the west; [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]], [[carson|Carson]], and [[bell|Bell]] would drive from St. Martin to [[sava|Sava]] and come from the east (002-125).

The instructions to [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] were precise: "If you find anyone suspicious, detain him, but not by force: if he won't be detained, follow him: drop [[bell|Bell]] at the first crossroads, to put us wise: but, whatever you do, don't lose him, for he'll show us the way to [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]]" (002-125).

## The Encounter with Rouse

Approaching the bridge through dense woods where the roar of the waterfall drowned all sound, [[william-chandos|Chandos]] and [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] came upon a car in the middle of the road, with a man bent double beside it trying to pull off a wheel (002-137).

The man proved to be [[rouse|Hannibal Rouse]], a touring English clergyman — or so he presented himself. He was "a genial simpleton, in whom there was no guile" (002-139). He had been attempting to change a wheel without first jacking up his car; he had confused a punctured tire with a sound one; he attributed his errors to being "no engineer" (002-146).

Then came a dangerous development. After staring at [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]], [[rouse|Rouse]] addressed him by name, recalling he had attended a fête at [[white-ladies|White Ladies]] before the War (002-147). [[william-chandos|Chandos]] "groaned in spirit and wished the man at the devil," but [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] was perfectly civil if cold.

[[rouse|Rouse]] then insisted on photographing the party — laughing "like a maniac" — before they could escape (002-148).

## The Three-Day Search

The party established their base at the inn in [[villach|Villach]], where — to [[william-chandos|Chandos]]'s disgust — [[rouse|Rouse]] had also taken rooms (002-159). For the next three days, [[rouse|Rouse]] "continually invited violence, taking the most curious interest in all we did, pressing his company upon us whenever we were at hand and openly trying to follow us when we went forth" (002-160).

The search itself proceeded methodically. Each morning at break of day, the party searched the country to the west, the quarter to which they were certain [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] had been carried. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]], the only German speaker, visited villages, stopping at wayside inns and questioning those whose business kept them in sight of the roads. One of the others always accompanied him.

The remaining men repaired to "a great hog's back" — a high ridge from which four men with glasses could command an immense view. Any car sighted was carefully watched; if its line could be presumed, two would leave in pursuit (002-161).

But the search yielded nothing. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] learned nothing of value; the few cars seen gave no cause for suspicion. "At the end of three days, we seemed to be no nearer [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] than when we left [[cleveland-square|Cleveland Row]]" (002-161).

## The Savage Warning

The one development was ominous. [[william-chandos|Chandos]] recorded that "in that time, however, we received a savage monition that, though we had no idea where [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] was, he had his hand upon us and could, so to speak, twist our tail whenever he pleased" (002-161). This "monition" would materialize on the third evening, when [[rouse|Rouse]] delivered a box from "Mr. Wilberforce" containing a lock of [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]]'s beautiful brown hair (003-031).

## Significance

The Poganec Reconnaissance establishes the operational pattern of the rescue — systematic, multi-directional, combining local inquiry with long-range observation. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s deduction about the enemy observation post demonstrates his military mind: he reads the landscape tactically, recognizing that a waterfall bridge commands the target and that the kidnappers must have established surveillance before the snatch.

Equally significant is the introduction of [[rouse|Hannibal Rouse]], whose presence at the bridge, at the inn, and in the party's affairs will prove far from coincidental. [[rouse|Rouse]]'s performance as a bumbling fool is so complete that [[william-chandos|Chandos]] and [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] never suspect him (002-149). Only [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]], detecting a single tell — the reflexive gesture of a man adjusting a tie he is not wearing (003-081) — keeps his suspicions alive.
