# The Customs Frontier

The customs frontier between France and its neighbouring countries represents a significant logistical obstacle for [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s rescue party. Crossing borders in a powerful motorcar, carrying provisions and equipment for a covert operation, requires careful planning and strategic bribery.

## Role in the Story

The preparations begin before the party leaves England. [[bell|Bell]] is dispatched ahead with a specific mission: "to cross to France by a morning boat, there to buy food and petrol against our coming and to promise ten thousand francs to the officials concerned if the Rolls was passed through the customs without any delay" (001-134).

This bribe — ten thousand francs — is a significant sum in 1920s Europe, reflecting the seriousness with which [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] treats border logistics. A delay at customs could jeopardise the entire rescue timetable.

On the return journey, the customs present an even greater challenge. With a stolen car in their possession, [[rowley|Rowley]] quips darkly: "Wot price the customs?" (008-220). But the resourceful [[rowley|Rowley]] already has a plan: "An' he'll put us through the customs — if we remember to ask him while Mansel's up on the wall" (008-222), referring to a contact who can facilitate passage.
