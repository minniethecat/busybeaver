# The Frontier

The Austrian frontier is the border crossed by [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s rescue party as they enter [[carinthia|Carinthia]] (002-029). The frontier marks the transition from the relative safety of the European road network into the territory where [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] holds sway.

> Night came upon us soon after eight o'clock, but a fine moon was westering, and two and a half hours later we entered Austria.

> (002-029)

## Narrative Significance

The frontier crossing is not described in detail — [[william-chandos|William Chandos]]'s narrative skips over it, focusing instead on the destination. This narrative choice is telling: the border, once crossed, is no longer relevant. What matters is what lies beyond — the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]], [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]], and the confrontation with [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]].

The frontier also represents a jurisdictional boundary. On one side, [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] and the rule of law; on the other, the private justice of [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] and the lawlessness of [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]. The novel's moral geography is shaped by this crossing — once in Austria, the protagonists are beyond the reach of any authority but their own.
