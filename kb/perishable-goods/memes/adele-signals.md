# Adèle's Signals

Adèle's Signals mark the first direct contact between [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]] and the captive [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]] since her abduction in the [[carinthia-kidnapping|Carinthia Kidnapping]].

From a mountain peak commanding the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]], [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] and [[william-chandos|William Chandos]] used binoculars to locate [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] on the terrace.

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] devised an improvised heliograph using his wristwatch case, flashing coded signals in the sun that [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] understood and answered with remarkable presence of mind.

## Context / Lead-up

On the afternoon of the first full day of the [[castle-of-gath-operation|Castle of Gath Operation]], [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] and [[william-chandos|Chandos]] began the ascent of a mountain that they believed must command the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] and be commanded in turn (004-161).

They left the [[rolls-royce|Rolls-Royce]] with [[tester|Tester]] by the roadside and climbed with powerful binoculars in hand. From the summit, the castle lay "not directly opposed, but lying a little to the left," allowing them to observe "the whole of its south and east sides" (004-162).

What [[william-chandos|Chandos]] saw through the naked eye was a miracle of emplacement—"the face of the cliff was 'produced' in the castle walls, and Gath was the crown upon a crag that no man could ever scale" (004-164). But the true revelation came through the binoculars.

## Sequence of Events

1. [[william-chandos|Chandos]] trains his binoculars on the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] and sees [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]]: "She was standing on the little terrace, with her hands on the balustrade" (004-167).
2. The setting is vivid and poignant: below her the cascade leaps, behind her an archway frames a shut door; she stands very still, her head up, her eyes lifted to the very peak on which [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] and [[william-chandos|Chandos]] are concealed (004-167).
3. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] acts instantly, tearing off his wristwatch, ripping its case open, and thrusting the watch into [[william-chandos|Chandos]]'s hand: "Flash it in the sun... If she sees it, she'll understand" (004-168)(004-169).
4. [[william-chandos|Chandos]] uses the silver watch-case as a heliograph, twitching it to and fro while [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] watches [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] through the binoculars (004-170).
5. [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] sees the signal. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] directs: "Now flash it six times, and cover it up with your hand between each flash" (004-172). Then: "Now flash it once more... And then cover up" (004-174).
6. After a tense wait, [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] responds. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]], his voice thick with emotion, reports: "She saw the signals... She raised her hand. When you had acknowledged her gesture, she turned away" (004-180).
7. The intelligence is precise: "She went up the staircase into the left-hand tower. And, after a moment, she showed some white at that window, halfway up. When you acknowledged that, she took it away" (004-183).

## Participants

- **[[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]]** — the captive; her poise and quick wit during the signal exchange confirms what [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] has always known: "She's very wise" (004-183)
- **[[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]** — the observer and director; his improvised heliograph and coded signals demonstrate the "common language which others did not know" (004-185)
- **[[william-chandos|William Chandos]]** — the signalman; operating the watch-case mirror from the mountain peak, fully aware of the enormity of the moment

## Outcome / Consequences

The signal exchange achieved three vital objectives:

First, it confirmed to [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] that help was at hand, ending her isolation and renewing her morale. The psychological impact on a captive who must have feared she was utterly alone cannot be overstated.

Second, it confirmed to [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] that [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] was alive, apparently well, and housed in the southwest tower—a critical piece of tactical intelligence that would shape the entire rescue plan.

Third, and most significantly, it demonstrated the extraordinary attunement between [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] and [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]]. As [[william-chandos|Chandos]] reflected: "for the first time I saw how well and truly she and Mansel were matched. They were made of the same fine substance, a little higher than their fellows, and could speak a common language which others did not know" (004-185).

The same evening, [[carson|Carson]] and [[rowley|Rowley]] were dispatched to [[poganec|Poganec]] with a note to Captain [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydell]] reporting the contact.

The note was brief, as was [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s way: "I know where Adèle is confined and saw her today from a distance. She seemed to be well. I am glad to say that I managed to attract her attention, and she now knows that help is at hand" (004-188).
