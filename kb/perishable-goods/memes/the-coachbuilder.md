# The Coachbuilder

The unnamed London coachbuilder is one of the many minor figures whose skilled labor enables [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s rescue mission. Summoned in the middle of the night to prepare the [[rolls-royce|Rolls-Royces]] for the continental journey, he works with an urgency that mirrors the rescue party's own.

## Role in the Story

After the midnight council at [[cleveland-row|Cleveland Row]], [[william-chandos|William Chandos]] reports that "six hours later the coachbuilder was urging his men" (001-138). The detail captures the mobilization of resources that accompanies [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s decisions — when he commits to action, an entire network of suppliers, mechanics, and servants springs into motion.

The coachbuilder's role, though brief, illustrates the novel's attention to the practical foundations of adventure. The [[rolls-royce|Rolls-Royces]] are not merely symbols of wealth — they are machines that must be maintained and prepared by skilled professionals. The success of the rescue depends as much on the coachbuilder's competence as on [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s strategy.

> "Six hours later the coachbuilder was urging his men, and Carson and I were at work."
>
> (001-138)
