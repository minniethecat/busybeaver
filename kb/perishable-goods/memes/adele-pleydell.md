# Adèle Pleydell

Adèle Pleydell is the youngest of the [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydell]] sisters and the wife of Captain "[[captain-pleydell|Boy]]" [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydell]].
A woman of remarkable beauty and spirit, she is kidnapped by [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s criminal network while riding alone in [[carinthia|Carinthia]].

Her abduction sets in motion the pursuit and rescue operation that occupies the entire novel.
The stakes could not be higher: [[rose-noble|Noble]] calls her "[[perishable-goods-phrase|perishable goods]]" — if [[scotland-yard|the police]] are called in, she will be killed.

Her courage during captivity — signalling to her rescuers from a tower window, keeping her wits about her — and her deepening bond with [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]] make her far more than a passive damsel in distress.

## Role in the Story

Adèle is the catalyst of the entire narrative. Her disappearance — communicated by telegram to [[william-chandos|William Chandos]] (001-097) — transforms a pleasant country-house visit to [[pleydell-family|the Pleydells]]' [[austria|Austria]]n farm into a desperate rescue mission.

[[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s chilling description of her as "[[perishable-goods-phrase|perishable goods]]" (001-107) underscores the stakes: if [[scotland-yard|the police]] are called in, she will pay with her life (001-137).

Throughout her captivity in the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]], Adèle demonstrates resourcefulness and nerve.
She signals from the [[castle-of-gath-tower|southwest tower]] window, enabling [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s party to locate her (005-170).

When the rescue attempt reaches her door, her clear voice rings out: "No, no. Stop. Stay where you are" (005-273) — a warning that saves the rescuers from walking into a trap.

After her liberation, her emotional intelligence and frankness — particularly her declaration of love for [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] (006-251) — shift the novel's emotional register from thriller to romance.

## Key Appearances

- **Chapter I** — Introduced as the youngest [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydell]] sister, capable of diminishing anyone by comparison (001-034).
  She rides with [[william-chandos|Chandos]] at six o'clock in the morning (001-039).
  [[william-chandos|Chandos]], not [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]], tells her of [[cleveland-row-burglary|the theft]] at [[cleveland-row|Cleveland Row]] (001-056).
  Her husband [[captain-pleydell|Boy]] has broken his leg (001-065).
  In a long silence, all she had said comes back to [[william-chandos|Chandos]]'s mind with force (001-080).
  The telegram arrives: "[[the-telegram-arrives|Adèle disappeared]]" (001-097).
  A book written by her is in [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s possession (001-102).
  [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] describes her as "[[perishable-goods-phrase|perishable goods]]" in [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s hands (001-107).
  The horror of her plight deals [[william-chandos|Chandos]] three swingeing blows (001-125).
  The party knows that calling in [[scotland-yard|the police]] would mean her death (001-137).

- **Chapter II** — [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] reconstructs her abduction: at seven on Monday morning she went out for a ride and never returned (002-054).
  It never occurred to [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] that Adèle had been taken for herself — he initially assumed it was a case of mistaken identity (002-062).
  Her dressing-case is demanded by the kidnappers (002-109).
  [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] declares she is "very lucky" to have such friends (002-112).
  He concludes it is inconceivable that she was taken by chance — her movements must have been watched (002-116).

- **Chapter V** — Adèle signals from the [[castle-of-gath-tower|southwest tower]] of the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] (005-170).
  The party sees her on [[castle-of-gath-terrace|the terrace]] through [[field-glasses|binoculars]] (005-172).
  Her clear voice comes through the door during the rescue attempt, crying "No, no. Stop. Stay where you are" (005-270), (005-273).

- **Chapter VI** — [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] greets her with "My dear Adèle, I'm delighted to see you" (006-008).
  She replies "Same here" with cheerful sarcasm (006-009).
  She asks "How's [[captain-pleydell|Boy]]?" — thinking of her husband even in captivity (006-012).
  Her scornful laugh is heard as [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] grows angry (006-020).
  She tells [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] privately: "I'm in love with [[jonah|Jonah]]. I think I've loved him for years" (006-251).
  She reassures [[william-chandos|Chandos]] not to feel left out (006-260).

- **Chapter IX** — She tends [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s wounds, asking quietly if [[tester|Tester]] will let her (009-046).
  She addresses him as "[[jonah|Jonah]]" with deep affection (009-033), (009-035), (009-040).
  She speaks of changing the tide together (009-052).
  She reflects that "all the world would say that [[jonah|Jonah]] and I were doing a rotten thing" (009-142).
  Her final words to him are "Oh, [[jonah|Jonah]] — my love, my darling" (009-180).

## Relationships

- **[[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]** ("[[jonah|Jonah]]") — The man who leads her rescue and with whom she falls in love.
  She calls him "[[jonah|Jonah]]" with an intimacy that speaks of years of unspoken feeling (006-251).
  By the novel's end, they are bound together, having "changed the tide" together (009-052).

- **[[william-chandos|William Chandos]]** — A close friend who rides with her at [[white-ladies|White Ladies]] (001-039) and is among her rescuers.
  She reassures him not to feel excluded by her bond with [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] (006-260).

- **[[daphne-pleydell|Daphne Pleydell]]** — Adèle's sister, who proposes that the party stay with them at [[white-ladies|White Ladies]] (001-038).

- **[[major-pleydell|Major Pleydell]]** — Her brother, head of the [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydell]] family, who meets the rescue party in [[austria|Austria]] (002-044).

- **[[captain-pleydell|Captain Pleydell]]** ("[[captain-pleydell|Boy]]") — Her husband, who has broken his leg at the start of the story (001-065) and lies in a chamber at [[pleydell-family|the Pleydells]]' farm (002-099).

- **[[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]** — Her kidnapper, who calls her "[[perishable-goods-phrase|perishable goods]]" (001-107) and whose cold politeness during her captivity she meets with scornful laughter (006-020).

- **[[tester|Tester]]** — [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s Sealyham, whose permission she seeks before tending [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s wounds (009-046) — a small, telling moment of her character.
