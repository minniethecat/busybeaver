# Carinthia Kidnapping

The Carinthia Kidnapping is the abduction of [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]] in [[carinthia|Carinthia]], [[austria|Austria]], orchestrated by [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s criminal network. It is the inciting incident of *[[perishable-goods-phrase|Perishable Goods]]*: the event that transforms the story from domestic English life into a continental rescue operation (001-097)(001-107).

## Context / Lead-up

The [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydell]] family and [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]] travel to [[carinthia|Carinthia]], staying at the family property near [[poganec|Poganec]] and [[st-martin|St. Martin]] (001-058)(001-109). Shortly after their arrival, a telegram reaches [[william-chandos|William Chandos]] in [[england|England]]: "[[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] disappeared shall I call in police" (001-097). [[william-chandos|Chandos]] had previously received a personal letter from [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]], making the news of her disappearance particularly alarming (001-102). The situation is further complicated by the fact that [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]]'s husband, Captain "[[captain-pleydell|Boy]]" [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydell]], has recently broken his leg and cannot travel (001-065).

## Sequence of Events

1. The [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydell]] party departs [[england|England]] for [[carinthia|Carinthia]] (001-058).
2. [[william-chandos|Chandos]] receives a telegram reporting [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]]'s disappearance (001-097).
3. [[william-chandos|Chandos]] shares [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]]'s earlier letter with [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]—a letter that had been written to him by [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] personally (001-102).
4. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] reveals the stakes: "I suppose you might call [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] in [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s hands—'[[perishable-goods-phrase|perishable goods]]'" (001-107), referencing the novel's title.
5. The reply address is given as "[[poganec|Pleydell Poganec]] [[st-martin|St. Martin]] [[carinthia|Carinthia]]" (001-109).
6. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] concludes: "[[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] has disappeared... we suspect abduction: I think anyone would" (001-121).
7. The party departs [[england|England]]: "The rest of us go to [[poganec|Poganec]], and, whilst we are there, Ca..." (002-034).
8. Further evidence confirms: "Everything points to abduction—by no ordinary gang" (002-032).

## Participants

- **[[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]]** — the victim; youngest of the [[daphne-pleydell|Pleydell]] siblings, abducted while abroad
- **[[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]** — leads the response and rescue planning
- **[[william-chandos|William Chandos]]** — receives the telegram in [[england|England]] and relays critical information
- **[[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]** — the antagonist behind the abduction; [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] is described as being "in [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s hands" (001-107)
- **[[major-pleydell|Major Pleydell]]** — [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]]'s father, present in [[carinthia|Carinthia]] when she disappears

## Outcome / Consequences

The kidnapping transforms the novel's trajectory from a domestic English opening to an international pursuit. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s response is immediate: within hours, he, [[william-chandos|Chandos]], and [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] are planning their journey to [[carinthia|Carinthia]] and the operation to recover [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] from [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s captivity. The event establishes [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] as a formidable antagonist and gives the title *[[perishable-goods-phrase|Perishable Goods]]* its meaning—[[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] is "[[perishable-goods-phrase|perishable goods]]" in [[rose-noble|Noble]]'s hands (001-107). The kidnapping directly leads to the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] operation that occupies the remainder of the novel.
