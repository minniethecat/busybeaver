# The Observation Post

[[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]] establishes a system of observation posts around the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]], drawing on his military experience to create a surveillance network that tracks every movement in and out of the fortress.

## Tactical Doctrine

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s approach to observation is methodical. He evaluates terrain for sightlines: "A post in the woods would be useless, for the trees would get in the way; but that torrent commands Poganec; and a man sitting there with a glass could see anyone come and go" (002-121):

> A post in the woods would be useless, for the trees would get in the way; but that torrent commands Poganec; and a man sitting there with a glass could see anyone come and go.
>
> (002-121)

Each post is chosen for its specific field of view and its concealment from enemy observation.

The primary observation post is [[the-spur|the spur]], from which the team watches the [[castle-of-gath-terrace|terrace]], the [[castle-of-gath-tower|tower]], and the castle's entrances. Supplementary posts cover the [[the-beechwoods|beechwoods]], the road approaches, and the [[the-bridge|bridge]]. Together, these positions create an information network that gives [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] tactical dominance over [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]].
