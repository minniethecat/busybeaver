# Punter and Bunch Captured

The capture of [[punter|Punter]] and [[bunch|Bunch]] marks the decisive turning point of the final confrontation in the wood near [[castle-of-gath|the Castle of Gath]]. Following the death of [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]], his two remaining henchmen were taken completely by surprise and offered no resistance.

> When we left the thicket, we took with us Punter and Bunch and cast them into the kitchen which they had turned into a gaol. When they saw their quarters, the two looked ready to burst: but, if they disliked their fortune, they had only themselves to thank.

> (008-255)

## The Ambush

The capture occurred moments after [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] arrived in the wooded dell where [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] had been holding [[william-chandos|Chandos]] prisoner. Rose Noble, Punter, and Bunch had been lying in wait, anticipating that Mansel would emerge from the castle to rescue Chandos. Rose Noble had been confidently asserting his tactical superiority: "He gave me this wood for the taking—just the kind of damfool error a squirt like Mansel would make. The poor trash couldn't see that, if ever it got to a dogfight, *the --- that had this thicket was bound to win*" (008-244).

Punter and Bunch, emboldened by Rose Noble's confidence, had been celebrating the prospect of action. Punter had shaken his fist at the castle, shouting: "Come out, you one-legged ---, and take your gruel" (008-255). The scene was one of supreme confidence. Bunch had been worrying about whether they should "spread" their positions, but Rose Noble dismissed the concern: "If and when I say so, but not before. What the hell's the use of spreading before they show up? Or even then?" (008-259).

At that precise moment, Mansel's voice answered from behind them: "None whatever" (008-260). Immediately, a shot was fired just behind Chandos, and Rose Noble fell back, staring, with blood running into his eyes (008-261).

## The Surrender

The effect was immediate and total. [[william-chandos|Chandos]] describes the scene: "Himself, Mansel unbound me and took the gag from my mouth, while Carson and Bell, who were with him, stood covering Punter and Bunch—in a way, a needless precaution, for the two seemed stupefied and gazed about them slowly, as though they had just been translated into another world" (008-262).

Chandos adds a perceptive observation: "And so, I suppose, in a sense, they had been, for Rose Noble was stone dead, shot through the brain" (008-262). The removal of their leader, who had dominated and directed their every action, left Punter and Bunch utterly disoriented. Their world—the world of Rose Noble's command—had been shattered in an instant.

The two men who moments before had been crowing over the prospect of capturing Mansel, who had been abusing Chandos and gloating over their impending wealth, were now "stupefied." They made no move to resist. Bell and Carson, covering them with pistols, found this a "needless precaution" (008-262)—Punter and Bunch were psychologically incapable of mounting any defence.

## Immediate Aftermath

With the prisoners secured, Mansel turned his attention to the wounded Chandos. "When he saw the state of my mouth, Mansel drew in his breath. Then his hands went under my arms, and he lifted me up" (008-263). Chandos was in a terrible state—his mouth torn by the gag, his wrists raw from the bonds, dehydrated, and barely able to walk.

Mansel led Chandos to a rill in the wood, where the water revived him (008-264). He then returned to deal with the prisoners, telling Chandos: "Those swine must be disposed of, and the cord's way back with the cars. You will stay here and rest, and I'll come back and find you as soon as ever I can" (008-266).

## The Prisoners Disposed

The capture was so total, the reversal of fortune so complete, that Chandos found it hard to process: "One moment the enemy was rampant, and the next Rose Noble was dead: before he had left the castle, Mansel had appeared in the wood: the inevitable had not happened, the impossible come to pass" (008-271). An absurd fear seized Chandos that the whole thing was a dream—dispelled only by the sigh of one of the [[rolls-royce|Rolls]] approaching (008-272).

The capture of Punter and Bunch marked the effective end of [[rose-noble-criminal-network|Rose Noble's criminal network]]. With their leader dead and both remaining active henchmen in custody, the operation that had begun with the kidnapping of [[adele-pleydell|Adele Pleydell]] was finished. The two prisoners would later be searched for money, given tickets to London, and sent away under escort (009-012)(009-160)—an ignominious end for the men who had nearly held half a million pounds for ransom.
