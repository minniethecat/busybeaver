# Rowley

Rowley is [[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]]'s old servant and a loyal member of [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s party throughout the pursuit of [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]] and the operation at the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]]. Though a servant, Rowley serves with quiet competence, handling baggage, reconnaissance, and even armed confrontation alongside the principals.

## Role in the Story

Rowley functions as one of [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s trusted support staff, alongside [[bell|Bell]] and [[carson|Carson]]. He is introduced early as having made the journey before, suggesting long service to [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] (001-063). Throughout the narrative he transitions from logistical support — managing luggage and vehicles — to active participation in the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] operation, where he stands guard, carries signals, and assists in the final rescue of [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]].

## Key Appearances

- **Chapter I** — Introduced as [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]]'s old servant preparing for the journey to Austria (001-063); dispatched to leave with the party's baggage (001-132).
- **Chapter II** — Waiting at the station in Calais, stretched on the luggage (002-030); [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] arranges to drop him at [[villach|Villach]] to make inquiries (002-033).
- **Chapter III** — At [[villach|Villach]], Rowley watches for [[rouse|Rouse]] on the platform (003-112); leaps from his seat in [[rolls-royce|the car]]riage chase against [[rouse|Rouse]] (003-154); taken up by the party as they pursue [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] and [[carson|Carson]] (003-157).
- **Chapter IV** — Drives with [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] in [[rolls-royce|the Rolls]] (004-014); rises from a ditch to report on the terrain (004-104); lies close with [[carson|Carson]] during the reconnaissance (004-157); leaves for [[poganec|Poganec]] with [[carson|Carson]] carrying a note (004-187).
- **Chapter V** — Assists [[bell|Bell]] in climbing a tree for reconnaissance (005-112); brings the party back to the dell two hours later (005-158); stands fast with a signal cord during the approach to the tower (005-173).
- **Chapter VII** — Guards the foot of the cliff with [[carson|Carson]] and [[bell|Bell]] (007-052); discovered standing at the foot of a tower staircase during the infiltration (007-084); steals up the stairs with [[bell|Bell]], ready to shoot at sight (007-127); dispatched with [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] and [[bell|Bell]] to rescue [[adele-pleydell|Mrs. Pleydell]] (007-144); hands over his coil of rope (007-181).
- **Chapter VIII** — Posted by [[william-chandos|Chandos]] to guard a position with [[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]] during the night watch (008-279); remains with [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] as safeguard after her rescue (008-283).
- **Chapter IX** — Drives up to the wood with [[bell|Bell]] and [[william-chandos|Chandos]] (009-089); relates to [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] who fetched the milk, prompting her quiet remark (009-150); departs with [[george-hanbury|George]], [[bell|Bell]], [[punter|Punter]], and [[bunch|Bunch]] (009-160); watches to ensure the prisoners' souls are chastened (009-161).

## Quotations

Rowley has little direct speech in the narrative, reflecting his role as a servant. His actions speak through the narrator's accounts:

> "Only Rowley stood fast, with a signal cord in his hand" (005-173)

> "Rowley, who was watching to see that they had chastened their souls" (009-161)

## Relationships

- **[[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]]** — Rowley's master; Rowley is described as "[[george-hanbury|Hanbury]]'s old servant" and serves him faithfully throughout (001-063).
- **[[bell|Bell]]** — Fellow servant in [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s party; the two often work in tandem, from guarding positions to climbing trees and storming staircases (005-112), (007-127).
- **[[carson|Carson]]** — [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s body-servant; Rowley cooperates with [[carson|Carson]] on reconnaissance and messenger duties (004-157), (004-187).
- **[[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]** — The leader of the party; Rowley follows [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s orders without question, driving with him (004-014) and executing his tactical instructions (005-173).
- **[[william-chandos|William Chandos]]** — The narrator; [[william-chandos|Chandos]] posts Rowley during the night watch (008-279) and runs into him at the foot of the cliff (007-084).
