# Castle of Gath Tower

The southwest tower of the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] is the most tactically significant structure in *Perishable Goods*. It is the prison of [[adele-pleydell|Adele Pleydell]], the point from which she signals to her rescuers, and the objective toward which every phase of [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s operation is directed. More than a physical location, the tower is the narrative's gravitational centre: every reconnaissance mission, every tactical plan, and every armed confrontation in the novel's second half is oriented toward reaching it.

## The Prison of Adele Pleydell

[[adele-pleydell|Adele Pleydell]] is held in the southwest tower throughout her captivity at the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]]. The tower is identified by its position relative to the main gateway: "the southwest tower, from which Adele had signalled, lay the length of the castle away" from "the tower to the right of the gateway as you came from the wood" (005-171). This spatial relationship---the southwest tower at one end of the castle, the gate tower at the other---defines the geography of the entire operation.

The tower contains three apartments, "consisting of two rooms each" (007-125). [[adele-pleydell|Adele]] occupies the middle apartment, and the precise location of her window becomes critical intelligence: "The window from which Adele had signalled was that of the middle apartment, the door of which, as she had told us, gave into the 'gallery of stone'" (007-125). The gallery of stone is the interior corridor providing access to the tower, and controlling the gallery proves to be the operational key: "the gallery was the key to the tower, and whoever held the gallery held Adele" (005-197).

## The Signal

The tower's most dramatic moment comes when [[adele-pleydell|Adele]] signals from its window. The rescue party, observing from a distance, sees her on the terrace and waving---a moment of visual contact that transforms the operation from a search into a siege. The signal confirms both her location and her survival, providing the intelligence that shapes every subsequent tactical decision. At the head of each flight of steps leading to the tower, there are massive doors, both of which are kept locked (005-201), presenting formidable obstacles to any rescue attempt.

During the actual rescue, as the party reaches the tower door, [[adele-pleydell|Adele]] cries out: "No, no. Stop. Stay where you are" (005-273). Her warning saves the rescuers from walking into a trap---evidence that even in captivity, she has maintained awareness of her surroundings and the gang's defensive preparations.

## Architecture and Access

The physical difficulty of reaching the tower is a central challenge. [[william-chandos|Chandos]] records: "So far as we knew, there were two ways into that tower from the open air---one by the roof and another by the steps from the terrace upon which we had seen Adele" (005-172). Both approaches present formidable obstacles. "The windows which lighted the tower were not to be reached, and the conical roof of the tower gave us no hope" (005-172).

The stairway of the southwest tower is described as a winding stair accessed through a wicket in a guardroom: "if a man ascended he came to the roof, but, if he went down, he came to the 'gallery of stone'" (007-121). The vertical geography---roof above, gallery below, apartments between---determines the tactical options.

One access point merits particular description: a door "like a church door, that is to say, iron-studded, and Gothic in shape" (005-224), admitting to the gallery leading to the tower. The ecclesiastical comparison imbues the tower with a quasi-sacred quality.

## The Southeast Tower

The [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] possesses two main towers: the southwest tower where [[adele-pleydell|Adele]] is held, and the southeast tower, which becomes [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s refuge after the rescue party seizes the southwest. When [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] and her prisoners withdraw "to the opposite tower" (007-137), the geography of the castle becomes a map of the conflict. The southeast tower also serves as a hiding place for [[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]] and the servants: "finding their purpose frustrated, George and the servants were probably lying concealed in the southeast tower" (008-007).

## The Tower as Convalescent Ward

In a quiet coda to the violence, the tower becomes a place of healing. After the [[final-confrontation|final confrontation]], [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] lies "for three days, as Buchinger had said, in the room that Adele had once used in the southwest tower" (009-100). The room that was a prison becomes a sickroom; the tower that was the objective of a military operation becomes the setting for a slow recovery. The transformation is complete: the southwest tower, once the symbol of [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s power over [[adele-pleydell|Adele]], becomes the place where [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s life is saved and where the relationship between rescuer and rescued deepens.
