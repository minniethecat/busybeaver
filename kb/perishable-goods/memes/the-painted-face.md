# The Painted Face

The painted face is a covert signal mark used by [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s criminal network. Painted on milestones and rocks along the route to the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]], these marks guide members of the gang through the unfamiliar Carinthian countryside.

> How Rose Noble had come to be so installed I could not think, but, had he spent his life searching, he could not have found a prison one half so suitable. The place was solitary and most secure: no cries could be heard, nor any signals seen: the one approach was hidden, and such as found it could not conceal their coming, for there were not six trees on the spur and the wood lay two hundred yards from the castle wall.
>
> (004-100)

## Role in the Story

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] discovers the painted face during reconnaissance and immediately understands its significance. The marks form a breadcrumb trail leading to the castle — a silent communication system that the gang uses to navigate without leaving written directions that could be intercepted.

The painted face exemplifies the novel's interest in codes and signals. Throughout the story, information is conveyed through indirect means — telegrams (001-091), mirror flashes, and painted marks — reflecting a world where open communication is dangerous and the clever interpretation of clues separates the hunters from the hunted.
