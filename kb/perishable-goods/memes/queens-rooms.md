# The Queen's Rooms

The Queen's Rooms are a suite within the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] through which [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s rescue party navigates during the climactic operation to free [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]].

> So we came back to the Closet and, passing through the Queen's rooms, entered the other antechamber which the bookseller's guide had shown us that we might expect.

> (006-119)

## Role in the Story

The Queen's Rooms appear at a critical juncture in the rescue. After reaching [[the-closet|the King's Closet]], the party proceeds "through the Queen's rooms" to "enter the other antechamber which the bookseller's guide had shown us" (006-119). The rooms thus form part of the maze-like interior of the castle — a network of chambers, antechambers, and passages that the rescue party must navigate while avoiding [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s men.

The naming of the rooms — King's and Queen's — reflects the castle's history as a seat of power, now repurposed as a criminal fortress. The royal nomenclature adds a layer of irony to the proceedings: a kidnapping and ransom operation conducted in chambers once occupied by monarchs.

## Strategic Importance

Within the castle's internal geography, the Queen's Rooms serve as a connector between the [[castle-of-gath-tower|tower]] and the outer areas of the castle. The rescue party's ability to navigate these rooms without detection depends on the intelligence gathered from the bookseller's guide — a reminder that information, as much as weapons, enables the rescue.
