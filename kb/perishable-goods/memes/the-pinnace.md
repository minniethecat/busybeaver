# The Pinnace

The pinnace is a motor-boat commanded by [[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]] during the [[castle-of-gath-operation|Castle of Gath Operation]]. It is deployed on the lake below the castle to intercept any waterborne escape by [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s gang.

> "Very well," said Mansel, hoisting himself to the floor. "But keep in the shadow and report the first sign of life. If Hanbury's sunk the pinnace, I don't think what's left of her crew will come aboard before dark. At least, they'll be fools if they try. But we can't hang our lives on guesswork and so we must watch. And, by the time I've eaten, I may have some plan."
>
> (006-272)

## Role in the Story

After the successful rescue of [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]], [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] assesses the situation with quiet confidence: "If Hanbury's sunk the pinnace, I don't think what's left of her crew will come aboard before dark" (006-272).

The pinnace operation is a classic Mansel tactic: cut off every avenue of escape before striking. By controlling the lake, Hanbury ensures that Noble's men cannot flee by water, forcing them to remain within the castle where they can be dealt with. The laconic reference — "If Hanbury's sunk the pinnace" — implies a naval engagement of which the reader sees nothing, a reflection of Yates's technique of suggesting larger action beyond the narrator's immediate view.
