# Bicycle Diversion

The Bicycle Diversion was a crucial piece of street-level improvisation during the surveillance of [[casemate|Casemate]] in [[lass|Lass]], early in the [[castle-of-gath-operation|Castle of Gath Operation]]. [[bell|Bell]], one of [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s servants, deliberately wheeled a bicycle into [[rolls-royce|the Rolls-Royce]] driven by [[carson|Carson]] in order to create an opportunity for the reconnaissance party to communicate with [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] without alerting their quarry.

## Context / Lead-up

The party had arrived in [[lass|Lass]] on the trail of [[rose-noble-criminal-network|Rose Noble's criminal network]] and quickly found themselves tailing [[casemate|Casemate]], a tall, pasty-faced lieutenant of [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]], through the narrow streets of the town (004-012). [[william-chandos|William Chandos]], [[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]], and [[bell|Bell]] spread out to follow [[casemate|Casemate]] without being detected, but they were acutely aware that their dress was "fatally distinctive" and that even a casual glance might betray them (004-004).

Hanbury acquired a black overcoat and a bookseller's hat as a rough disguise (004-008); Chandos shed his coat, hat, and collar and shouldered a sack of refuse (004-005); Bell stripped himself of coat, hat, and collar, clipped his trousers at the ankle, and dirtied his face and hands until he was "filthy beyond compare" (004-010). In this state, Bell procured a bicycle and began working his way along the route.

## Sequence of Events

1. [[bell|Bell]], disguised as a dirty workman, wheels a bicycle through the streets of [[lass|Lass]] (004-010).
2. [[william-chandos|Chandos]], still tracking [[casemate|Casemate]], sees that the road ahead is blocked by a crowd gathered around a church, through which [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] and [[rowley|Rowley]] are driving the first of the [[rolls-royce|Rolls-Royces]], with [[carson|Carson]] following in the second (004-014).
3. [[casemate|Casemate]] notices the cars and immediately becomes suspicious, stopping under an archway to watch them (004-020).
4. [[bell|Bell]], seeing the danger, deliberately steers his bicycle into [[carson|Carson]]'s car, creating a crash that draws everyone's attention (004-016).
5. As Bell picks himself up, [[carson|Carson]] delivers the pre-arranged code phrase: "a cyclist walked into the car. And, excuse me, sir, but you're rather down on the right. *I think you must have broken a spring*" (004-018).
6. The phrase "broken a spring" was the party's established code for a covert communication: "if, when upon the road, one of us wished to say something which was not for everyone's ears, he had but to state that the car before or behind him had broken a spring" (004-019).

## Participants

- **[[bell|Bell]]** — the cyclist; stripped to near-rags and dirtied beyond recognition, he executed the diversion with perfect timing
- **[[carson|Carson]]** — driver of the second [[rolls-royce|Rolls-Royce]]; used the crash to pass the coded message to [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]
- **[[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]** — the party's leader; received the intelligence through the "broken spring" code
- **[[william-chandos|William Chandos]]** — field observer; watched the entire diversion unfold from his position behind [[casemate|Casemate]]
- **[[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]]** — rear-guard; shuffling along the far side of the street during the diversion
- **[[casemate|Casemate]]** — the surveillance target; witnessed the crash but remained suspicious of the cars (004-020)

## Outcome / Consequences

The bicycle diversion succeeded in getting the message through to [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]], who now knew that his party was actively engaged in surveillance and that the trail was hot. The "broken spring" code had been rehearsed and worked flawlessly.

However, [[casemate|Casemate]]'s suspicions were far from assuaged. He stopped under an archway to watch the two cars, with "a hand to his mouth" (004-020), and the party soon found themselves in a far more dangerous cat-and-mouse game that extended miles beyond [[lass|Lass]] into the mountain roads. [[bell|Bell]]'s resourcefulness in acquiring the bicycle and executing the diversion on the fly demonstrated the initiative and adaptability of [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s servants, and his willingness to debase himself into the guise of a filthy cyclist showed a commitment that would prove essential throughout the [[castle-of-gath-operation|Castle of Gath Operation]].

The diversion also marked the first time that the party tested their pre-arranged signals under field conditions. That [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] understood at once the meaning of the "broken spring" confirmed that the system was sound, and it would be used again in subsequent operations.
