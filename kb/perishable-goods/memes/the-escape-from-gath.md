# The Escape from Gath

The Escape from Gath was the night operation in which [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s party, having secured [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]] but remained trapped in the King's Closet and Bedchamber, attempted to escape the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] through a coordinated assault orchestrated by [[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]] from outside. The escape — a sequence of ropework, diversion, and desperate improvisation — brought the [[castle-of-gath-operation|Castle of Gath Operation]] to its breathtaking climax.

## Context / Lead-up

The party had been pinned in the State Apartments for over a day. [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] had been rescued from the terrace, but the waterfall rope had been cut (006-147), and [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] had demonstrated his ability to move unseen through the hidden passage behind the panelling (006-170). The only hope was a coordinated attack from outside.

On Thursday night, [[william-chandos|Chandos]] had hurled a detailed plan over the cliff, wrapped in oiled silk and weighted with his wet coat (006-280). The note directed [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] to attack on Friday, half an hour after dark: gain the roof, guard the southwest tower door, let down three ropes to the Closet windows, and stage a one-minute diversion — breaking the glass of a passage window — while the party escaped (006-279).

The car sent out by [[rose-noble|Noble]] had not returned during the night, confirming that [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] and the servants had intercepted it. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] believed that [[rose-noble|Noble]] was now alone (006-283).

## Sequence of Events

1. **The Day Passes** — Friday is a day of tense silence. [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] changes into a powder-blue frock; she and [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] are "happy as the day was long" in each other's company, but the "absolute silence which reigned without our doors" unsettles all three of them (006-287)(006-288).
2. **The Windows Open** — As darkness falls, the party opens the Closet windows and shuts the trapdoor. [[william-chandos|Chandos]] takes position at a window with [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] beside him, while [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] watches the Bedchamber door (006-294).
3. **The Ropes Arrive** — After an agonizing wait — [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] is late — [[william-chandos|Chandos]] hears movement above. An instant later, he has a rope in his hands. A second rope brushes his arms, then a third (006-298)(006-300).
4. **Adèle's Signal** — At [[william-chandos|Chandos]]'s silent direction, [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] flashes her torch once, and [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] is at their side in a twinkling (006-301).
5. **Mansel Tests the Ropes** — [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] tests each rope himself, then binds one about [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] and lifts her onto the windowsill (006-302).
6. **The Embrace** — [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] puts her arms around [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s neck and presses her face against his. They wait, poised between escape and disaster (006-303).
7. **The Diversion** — From the passage comes a clatter and "the shiver of broken glass" — [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]]'s demonstration has begun (006-305).
8. **Adèle Ascends** — "My beautiful darling," says [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]], and swings her out of the window and into the night. For a second he watches her rising, then draws back: "You next, William, as quick as you can" (006-306)(006-308).
9. **The Second Diversion** — A second clatter comes from beyond the doors. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] breathes: "That demonstration's too thin" — sensing that something is wrong (006-311).
10. **The Ropes Vanish** — [[william-chandos|Chandos]] flings out both arms, sweeping the air — and finds nothing. "They're gone," he cries (006-312)(006-313).
11. **Rose Noble's Laugh** — From above comes a thick laugh. "Isn't that nice?" says [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] (006-316)(006-317).

## Participants

- **[[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]]** — the first to ascend; her silent embrace of [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] on the windowsill is the scene's emotional heart
- **[[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]** — the commander; his instinct that the second demonstration is "too thin" proves fatally accurate
- **[[william-chandos|William Chandos]]** — the signalman and second in line; his desperate sweep of the empty air marks the moment of catastrophe
- **[[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]]** — the external commander; his precise execution of the roof assault and diversion plan is cut short by [[rose-noble|Noble]]'s counter-move
- **[[carson|Carson]]** — part of [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]]'s assault team on the roof
- **[[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]** — the ambusher; his appearance on the roof with his thick, mocking laugh shows that he has anticipated [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s plan and countered it with devastating precision

## Outcome / Consequences

The escape was both a success and a failure. [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] was out — hauled up the rope to the roof and, presumably, rushed to the [[rolls-royce|Rolls-Royces]] waiting in the beechwoods. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] had gotten her out, which had been his primary objective from the beginning of the [[castle-of-gath-operation|Castle of Gath Operation]].

But [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] and [[william-chandos|Chandos]] remained. The remaining ropes had been pulled away from above, and [[rose-noble|Noble]]'s laughter indicated that he now controlled the roof. The two men, trapped once more in the Closet, would have to find another way out — or fight their way through [[rose-noble|Noble]] himself. This standoff would lead directly to the [[final-confrontation|Final Confrontation]] and, ultimately, to [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s fatal wounding.

The escape also marked the final severance of any pretense of negotiation. [[rose-noble|Noble]] had lost his hostage; his only remaining asset was his control of the castle itself. From this moment forward, the conflict between [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] and [[rose-noble|Noble]] would be fought to the death.
