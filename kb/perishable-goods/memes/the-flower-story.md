# The Flower Story

The flower story is a piece of theatrical deception deployed by [[rouse|Rouse]] — a fabricated tale about a chrysanthemum enthusiast named [[wilberforce|Wilberforce]] — that [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]] sees through with characteristic perceptiveness. The incident exemplifies [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s ability to detect falsehood through attention to detail.

## The Deception

[[rouse|Rouse]], attempting to establish a cover, tells a story about a man named Wilberforce who is passionate about chrysanthemums. The story is elaborate and detailed — too detailed, perhaps. [[rouse|Rouse]] declares triumphantly: "Mr. Wilberforce" (003-004), as if the name itself proves his story.

> "Mr. Wilberforce," said Rouse triumphantly.
>
> "Who's Wilberforce?" said Mansel wearily.
>
> (003-004, 003-007)

But the name is the flaw. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] asks the obvious question: "Who's Wilberforce?" (003-007). The name, borrowed from a real person, serves as the thread that, when pulled, unravels [[rouse|Rouse]]'s entire fabric of lies.

## Mansel's Perception

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s weary perceptiveness is on display here. He recognizes the technique — borrowing a real name to lend credibility to a false story — because he has seen it before. His response is not anger but a kind of tired recognition: another criminal using another worn-out trick.
