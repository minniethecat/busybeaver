# Ladder

The **ladders** used in the [[castle-of-gath-operation|Castle of Gath operation]] are custom-built wrought-iron climbing devices, forged to [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s precise specifications by a village smith in [[carinthia|Carinthia]]. They represent Mansel's characteristic blend of advance planning and tactical improvisation.

## Design and Manufacture

When planning the approach to the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]], Mansel recognizes that the party will need specialized climbing equipment. His solution is characteristically bespoke:

> "Then we must have a ladder?" (005-058)
>
> "Wrought-iron, one-pole ladders, made by a village smith: enough to reach from the ground to the top of the wall." (005-062)

The choice of wrought iron — rather than wood or rope — reflects the operational requirements. Iron ladders would be strong enough to support multiple climbers, resistant to detection (no creaking), and could be made quickly by any competent smith.

Mansel's specification of "one-pole" design — a single central spar with rungs on either side — creates a lightweight but rigid climbing frame. The design allows the ladders to be carried in sections and assembled on site:

> "The smith is of no consequence: the ladders were simple to make, and Mansel's design was clear." (005-067)

## Operational Deployment

The ladders are deployed during the night infiltration of the castle. [[bell|Bell]] stations himself at the foot of the ladders, maintaining the signal cord system:

> "Bell was at the foot of the ladders, with a signal-cord in his hand." (005-163)

Later, during a subsequent phase of the operation, Bell again takes up this post:

> "...standing at the foot of the ladders, with a cord in his hand." (007-084)

## Tactical Risk

The ladder ascent is not without danger. The climbers are exposed during their ascent, and the ladders themselves could be detected by a vigilant sentry. Chandos captures the tension of the moment:

> "I was on the second ladder, when someone above me looked..." (007-090)

The psychological weight of the ladder work is acknowledged in the narrative. Those ascending must trust both the equipment and the men stationed below:

> "...to stand at the foot of the ladder in the knowledge that those you have sent up are depending absolutely upon you." (007-099)

## The Ladder as Demonstration

During a tense negotiation, Mansel uses the lowering of a ladder as a demonstration of capability, proving to observers that the party can access the castle's upper levels at will:

> "...demonstrate by letting down a ladder to one of the windows..." (006-279)

## Significance

The ladders embody a recurring theme in *Perishable Goods*: the victory of patient preparation over brute force. Rather than assaulting the castle gates, Mansel's team ascends silently in the dark, using equipment forged in a village smithy. The ladders — unglamorous, practical, locally sourced — are the antithesis of the theatrical villainy practiced by [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]].
