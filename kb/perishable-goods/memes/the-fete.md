# The Fete

The fete at [[white-ladies|White Ladies]] is a seemingly innocent social gathering that conceals a dangerous encounter. [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]], attending the event among the summer guests, recognizes [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]] — a moment of recognition that transforms the criminal's interest in the [[pleydell-family|Pleydell]] household into a focused threat.

## Context

The Pleydells left for [[carinthia|Carinthia]] in July (001-058), but before their departure, they hosted a fete at [[white-ladies|White Ladies]]. [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] attended — or at least observed — the gathering, and it was there that he recognized [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] from a previous encounter before the War.

## The Recognition

The recognition is reported later in the narrative: "the antagonist recognizing Mansel from pre-war days" (002-147). This moment is crucial because it elevates [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s interest from random opportunity to personal vendetta. He now knows who he is dealing with — not merely the husband of a wealthy woman but a formidable adversary with a history.

> He then told us that he was touring and cared not where he went, but proposed to stay at Villach and prove the country round. This was ill news enough; but when, after staring upon Mansel, he presently addressed him by name and then, in an ecstasy of triumph, went on to remember White Ladies and how he had attended some fête there before the War, I know that I groaned in spirit and wished the man at the devil. But Mansel was perfectly civil, though something cold.
>
> (002-147)

## Consequences

The recognition at the fete leads directly to the targeting of [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]]. [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] now understands that the [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] he recognizes is connected to the [[pleydell-family|Pleydells]], and that striking at the family would wound his old adversary. The fete, in retrospect, is the moment when [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] became "perishable goods."
