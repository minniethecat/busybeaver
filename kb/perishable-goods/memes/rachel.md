# Dr. Rachel

Dr. Rachel is a physician from [[lass|Lass]] who, alongside [[buchinger|Dr. Buchinger]], attends to [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]] after he is gravely wounded in the final confrontation at the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]]. Though his role is confined to Chapter IX, his medical intervention is critical to [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s survival.

## Role in the Story

When [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] is shot during the climax of the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] operation, [[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]] fetches Dr. Rachel from [[lass|Lass]]. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] himself insists: "Dr. Rachel must help me" (009-072). Rachel assists [[buchinger|Buchinger]] in the operation — described as "too much bemused" by the pressure (009-088) — but together the two doctors save [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s life. In the aftermath, Rachel is fetched and carried by devious routes to maintain secrecy, attending [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] twice daily (009-102), (009-108).

## Key Appearances

- **Chapter IX** — Summoned by [[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]] from [[lass|Lass]] at [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s insistence (009-072). Makes "a noise like a siphon" as he steadies himself for the procedure (009-076). [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] gives him directions and tells him to "get those things" (009-077). [[william-chandos|Chandos]] reflects that Rachel "could no more have done it than fly" — yet he did as he was bidden (009-079). After the operation, [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] declares he is "no longer dying: Dr. Rachel and I have stopped that" (009-083). Rachel is driven back to [[lass|Lass]] by [[george-hanbury|George]] (009-089). In the days that follow, [[george-hanbury|George]] fetches Rachel from [[lass|Lass]] each afternoon (009-095). Rachel must continue to attend [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] twice daily (009-102). When a more dangerous doctor refuses to look at Rachel "for all his zeal," Rachel maintains his story that [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] is a patient of his (009-106), (009-111). The party fetches and carries Rachel by devious ways, and his comings and goings cannot be too secretly made (009-108), (009-113).

## Character and Limitations

Dr. Rachel is presented as a competent but limited local physician — "clever enough, no doubt," as [[william-chandos|Chandos]] concedes, "but take him out of his groove, and he loses his mind" (009-079). The pressures of emergency surgery in an irregular setting overwhelm him. Before the operation begins, he makes "a noise like a siphon" as he steadies himself (009-076), a detail that captures both his nervousness and the narrator's wry observation. Once [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] gives him clear instructions, however, Rachel performs: "As Rachel was bidden, he did — with an excellent grace: but the poor man's composure was gone, and his efforts to bring it to heel were as obvious as they were vain" (009-080).

The contrast with [[buchinger|Dr. Buchinger]] is deliberate and instructive. Where Buchinger is "far too wary to ask any questions," Rachel is "too much bemused" (009-088). Rachel is the follower, not the leader — he carries out [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s instructions, assists Buchinger in the surgery, and handles the routine of post-operative care. [[william-chandos|Chandos]] is blunt: Rachel "could no more have done it than you or I" — meaning the full surgical procedure was beyond him — but he "did as he was bidden" (009-079), and that obedience proves valuable.

## The Cover Story

Rachel's most sustained contribution to the party is maintaining the cover story about [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s injury. [[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]] lays the groundwork when he fetches Rachel's belongings from the inn at [[lass|Lass]], "giving out that Mansel had crushed his foot and lay in a peasant's cabin in danger of losing his leg," thereby sowing "the seed of a story for Rachel to tell" (009-095). Rachel faithfully maintains this fiction, telling the townspeople that [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] had crushed his leg (009-111).

This cover story is essential to the party's security. The party draws supplies not from [[lass|Lass]] but from towns twenty to fifty miles distant; they fetch and carry Rachel "by devious ways"; the castle gates are kept shut and no lights are shown (009-108). Rachel's comings and goings "cannot be too secretly made" (009-113). The town of [[lass|Lass]], whose sleep is "seldom broken by anything more stirring than a chimney afire or an instance of petty theft" (009-111), is kept in ignorance of the violence that has transpired at the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]].

## The Dangerous Dog

A curious detail emerges in the recovery period: a fierce dog at the castle — more dangerous than any the party has encountered — refuses to look at Rachel "for all his zeal" (009-106). This small moment underscores Rachel's outsider status even as he performs his essential medical duty. Rachel is tolerated, relied upon, but never truly admitted into the inner circle. He remains the local doctor from [[lass|Lass]] who happens to be useful — and his discretion, like [[buchinger|Buchinger]]'s, is sustained by the understanding that certain questions are better left unasked.
