# Jute's Intelligence Report

Jute's Intelligence Report was a critical information exchange between [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] and his lieutenant [[casemate|Casemate]] on the mountain road leading to the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]]. Overheard by [[william-chandos|William Chandos]] from his perch in an oak tree, the conversation revealed not only the depth of [[rose-noble|Noble]]'s intelligence network but also the volatile dynamics within his criminal organization.

## Context / Lead-up

After trailing [[casemate|Casemate]] for miles through the countryside beyond [[lass|Lass]], [[william-chandos|Chandos]] found himself in desperate straits. The road offered no cover, and when [[casemate|Casemate]] doubled back, [[william-chandos|Chandos]] was forced to leap into the bough of an oak tree that overhung the road (004-041). From this precarious vantage point, lying flat along the massive branch, he became an unwitting eavesdropper on one of the most revealing conversations in the novel.

As he hung there, a car horn sounded in the distance. [[casemate|Casemate]] stiffened at the sound—"like a pointer"—and stood with his ears pricked (004-044). The approaching car was not one of [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s, but [[casemate|Casemate]]'s reaction showed that its occupant was someone of far greater significance.

## Sequence of Events

1. [[casemate|Casemate]] hears the horn of an approaching car and stands frozen, "a match in one of his hands and its box in the other" (004-044).
2. When the car rounds the corner, [[casemate|Casemate]] waves his arms wildly to stop it (004-046).
3. The car stops, and a head emerges. [[william-chandos|Chandos]] is "dumbfounded" to recognize [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] himself (004-049).
4. [[casemate|Casemate]] delivers his intelligence: "Somebody's talking. She's got word through to mother, and Big Willie's at Lass" (004-051).
5. Pressed by [[rose-noble|Noble]] for details, [[casemate|Casemate]] explains: "Last night they came for Big Willie and told him that she had made touch. Jute heard them say so. Big Willie was off like a cracker, and, when he was good and gone, Jute slipped up to the station and took the train" (004-055).
6. [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] instantly deduces that [[jute|Jute]] has been duped: "Of course he brought him... What do you take me for? 'Got word through to mother.' This isn't Marlborough Street" (004-064).
7. [[rose-noble|Noble]] flies into a rage, damning [[jute|Jute]] for disobeying orders never to move when [[jonathan-mansel|Big Willie]] was out of sight (004-064).
8. When [[rose-noble|Noble]] asks who was with [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]], [[casemate|Casemate]] reports "Two of the servants" (004-070). [[rose-noble|Noble]] instantly replies: "Quite right... You only saw two of the servants. *I passed the third on the road five minutes ago.*" (004-073).

## Participants

- **[[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]** — the criminal mastermind; his swift, penetrating intelligence is on full display as he dismantles [[casemate|Casemate]]'s account
- **[[casemate|Casemate]]** — the messenger; a "snake-faced" lieutenant who bears the brunt of [[rose-noble|Noble]]'s fury
- **[[jute|Jute]]** (absent but central) — the spy whose panicked dash to [[lass|Lass]] by train has compromised the entire operation
- **[[william-chandos|William Chandos]]** — hidden in the oak, taking in every word and realizing that "the luck I had found so unkind a moment ago was playing clean into our hands" (004-050)

## Outcome / Consequences

This conversation revealed several vital pieces of intelligence to [[william-chandos|Chandos]] and, through him, to [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]:

First, it confirmed that [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] was the mastermind and that he was far more intelligent than his physical appearance suggested. [[william-chandos|Chandos]] reflected that "no matter how clogged, he had the truth free in an instant" (004-075).

Second, it exposed the fault lines within [[rose-noble|Noble]]'s organization. [[jute|Jute]] had panicked and led [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] to [[lass|Lass]]; [[casemate|Casemate]] had compounded the error by vouching for [[jute|Jute]]'s story; and [[rose-noble|Noble]]'s savage dressing-down of his subordinates showed that loyalty in his network was maintained through fear, not trust.

Third, and most consequentially, [[rose-noble|Noble]]'s fury led him to order the car turned around, giving [[william-chandos|Chandos]] the opportunity to ride on its roof directly to the [[castle-of-gath|Castle of Gath]] (004-077). This daring feat of improvisation would give the party their first sight of the prison that held [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]].
