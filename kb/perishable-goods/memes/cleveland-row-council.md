# The Cleveland Row Council

The Cleveland Row Council is the strategic planning session held in [[jonathan-mansel|Jonathan Mansel]]'s flat at [[cleveland-square|Cleveland Row]] immediately following the arrival of the telegram announcing [[adele-pleydell|Adèle Pleydell]]'s disappearance (001-097) and [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s revelation that she is the "[[perishable-goods-phrase|perishable goods]]" held by [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] (001-107). In the hours between the telegram's arrival and midnight, [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] transforms shock into a coordinated, multi-front expedition.

## The Decision Against Ransom

After dispatching his reply to the telegram — "On no account" call in [[scotland-yard|the police]] (001-109) — [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] picked up [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]'s earlier letter and lighted a cigarette. His agitation had passed; "from this time on until the end, he was, as always, the coolest and most patient of us all" (001-111).

He made his position clear immediately: "I am not going to act upon the suggestion here made, because, for one thing, such a sum is ruinous, and, for another, I do not trust [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]]" (001-113).

[[william-chandos|William Chandos]] and [[george-hanbury|George Hanbury]] immediately offered their shares of the fortune they had made together — two hundred thousand pounds each (001-117)(001-118) — but [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] refused. "It would break her heart. Sooner or later she would most surely find out, and then — well, you can't lay anyone under a debt like that" (001-119).

He put the letter toward a candle's flame, but then withdrew it and put it away in his case — a decision that would prove critical days later at [[poganec|Poganec]] (001-120).

## The Cover Story

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] established the official narrative: "So all that we know is that [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] has disappeared: and, since my cousin, her husband, is out of action and we three know [[carinthia|Carinthia]] as the palm of our hand, we are naturally going to seek her with all our might. Of course we suspect abduction: I think anyone would. *But that is all*" (001-121).

This careful formulation concealed the three explosive truths: that [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] was behind the abduction, that [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] loved [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]], and that a half-million-pound ransom demand had already been made.

## Logistics and Preparation

The council then turned to practical arrangements, demonstrating [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s genius for operational planning.

**Departure timing:** [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] determined they would sail the next night, not before, because he needed a full day to prepare for "the battle to which we were now to go" (001-131).

**Staff deployment:** [[rowley|Rowley]] was to leave with the heavier baggage by an earlier train. [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]] was to change the arrangements for shipping [[the-cars|the cars]] (001-132).

**Armament and vehicles:** [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s own [[rolls-royce|Rolls-Royce]] had secret lockers and trays, but [[william-chandos|Chandos]]'s car had no such fittings. [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] gave him a note to his coachbuilder and instructed him to seek him the next morning at eight o'clock. While [[the-coachbuilder|the coachbuilder]]'s men fitted a hidden coffer, [[carson|Carson]] and [[william-chandos|Chandos]] together were to test and prepare both cars so that they could "run for a month on end without attention" (001-133).

**Advance party:** [[bell|Bell]] was to cross to [[france|France]] by a morning boat, buy food and petrol, and promise ten thousand francs to the [[customs-frontier|customs]] officials if the party cleared in half an hour (001-134).

**Route:** From the [[dieppe|French port]] they would drive "as hard and as straight as we could" to [[salzburg|Salzburg]], then direct to [[st-martin|St. Martin]], the village serving [[pleydell-farm|the Pleydells' farm]] at [[poganec|Poganec]] (001-135).

## Strategic Analysis

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] reasoned that [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] must still be in [[austria|Austria]], since without her passport and against her will she could hardly be taken out. The [[carinthia|Carinthian]] countryside, solitary and with inhabitants too poor to intrude on suspicious matters, lent itself to violence (001-136).

Crucially, [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] decided not to involve [[scotland-yard|the police]]. His reasoning was grim: "had [[scotland-yard|the police]] been called in, [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]] would have paid with her life for their assistance" (001-137). The fight was between them and [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]], and [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] was ruthless — a man who would "have slain ten pawns, had they stood in the way of his safety or revenge" (001-137).

## Execution

The council broke up at midnight. [[william-chandos|Chandos]] slept only three hours (001-138). By six hours later, [[the-coachbuilder|the coachbuilder]] was at work, and by four in the afternoon, "[[the-cars|the cars]] were as ready for the road as the wit of man could make them" (001-138). At half past six, both cars stood loaded in [[cleveland-square|Cleveland Row]] (001-138).

[[rowley|Rowley]] and [[bell|Bell]] were already gone. The traveling party numbered four — [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]], [[william-chandos|Chandos]], [[george-hanbury|Hanbury]], and [[carson|Carson]] — plus [[tester|Tester]], [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s Sealyham, "ever better company than many a man" (001-139)(001-140).

At a quarter to seven, they passed out of [[cleveland-square|Cleveland Row]] (001-141). [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] acknowledged they could not hide their departure: "it doesn't very much matter if they do send [[rose-noble|Rose Noble]] a wire. He never expected that I should pay out of hand: but he means me to find it hopeless and then to put on the screw" (001-142).

## Significance

The Cleveland Row Council demonstrates [[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]]'s capacity to convert a personal catastrophe into a military-grade operation within hours. His refusal of a ruinous ransom, his insistence on a cover story that protects [[adele-pleydell|Adèle]], his deployment of three servants on independent missions, his preparation of vehicles for sustained combat conditions, and his strategic analysis of the [[austria|Austria]]n terrain all reflect a mind trained for action and a character unwilling to let personal feeling compromise tactical judgment.

[[jonathan-mansel|Mansel]] lays down the operational doctrine at the [[cleveland-row|Cleveland Row]] council:

> "So all that we know," he continued, "is that Adèle has disappeared: and, since my cousin, her husband, is out of action and we three know Carinthia as the palm of our hand, we are naturally going to seek her with all our might. Of course we *suspect* abduction: I think anyone would. *But that is all.* Have I made myself clear?"
>
> (001-121)
