# Genius

In _[[A Vindication of the Rights of Woman]]_, **genius** is the summit of human intellectual achievement. [[Mary Wollstonecraft]] uses the concept to make one of her most radical arguments: if women lack genius, it is not nature but education that is to blame. The term appears in 20 passages, concentrated in the Introduction, Chapters 1, 2, 4, and 6.

Denying women the opportunity to develop genius is both an injustice and a waste of human potential. Wollstonecraft's defense of female genius is also a defense of reason against sensibility, and of Enlightenment optimism against aristocratic prejudice.

## Definition

Wollstonecraft defines genius as the highest expression of human intellectual power — the capacity for original thought, creative insight, and sustained rational inquiry. It is distinguished from mere learning by its creative dimension and from mere cleverness by its depth:

> "The lightning glance of genius." (004-004)

This metaphor emphasizes speed and illumination: genius sees what others miss, piercing through convention to truth. But genius is not magic — it requires cultivation, which women are systematically denied.

> "Denying her genius and judgment, it is scarcely possible to enumerate the benefits that flow from giving proper scope to the female mind." (004-002)

## Wollstonecraft's Argument

### Genius Requires Education

Wollstonecraft insists that genius is not inborn but developed through rigorous education. The claim that women lack genius is circular: deny them the education that produces genius, then cite their lack of genius as proof of inferiority:

> "If women are to be excluded from the light of reason, and the exercise of genius, they must be degraded." (001-020)

The passive construction "are to be excluded" exposes the mechanism: women's exclusion from genius is a choice made by men, not a fact of nature. Genius is not denied to women — it is prevented.

### Genius as the Opposite of Accomplishment

Wollstonecraft contrasts genius with the "accomplishments" taught to women — drawing, dancing, polite conversation. Accomplishments are ornamental; genius is substantive. Women are trained in the former and denied the cultivation for the latter:

> "Men of genius and talents have started out of a class... while women of the same class remain in obscurity." (004-101)

The middle class produces male geniuses because it combines the necessity of industry with the opportunity for cultivation. Women of the same class receive only the ornamental part of education — sufficient for display but not for genius.

### Genius and Sensibility

Wollstonecraft acknowledges that "the page of genius has always been blurred by the prejudices of the age" (003-015). The association of genius with sensibility — with "vivid feelings" and "the concomitant of genius" (002-061) — is both true and dangerous. Genius requires sensibility to animate it, but sensibility ungoverned by reason produces "impractical enthusiasts" rather than real geniuses.

### Genius and Moral Character

True genius requires virtue: "any vigorous exertions of genius or benevolence" (005-118) are impossible without moral seriousness. The conjunction of "genius or benevolence" is revealing — genius without benevolence is mere cleverness; benevolence without genius is good intention without effect. Both require the moral discipline that corrupt society undermines.

## Relationship to Other Concepts

Genius is the highest expression of [[Reason]] and the opposite of mere [[Accomplishment]]. It requires [[Education]] — specifically, the rigorous cultivation of understanding that women are denied. [[Sensibility]] is "the concomitant of genius" (002-061) — genius needs emotional vitality but must be governed by reason.

The concept connects to [[Equality]]: the denial of genius to women is both evidence and consequence of inequality. It also relates to [[Virtue]] — true genius requires moral seriousness. [[Jean-Jacques Rousseau]] is Wollstonecraft's implied interlocutor on this topic, as his educational theory treated male genius as natural and female genius as impossible.
