## Overview

Ignorance is a central target of Wollstonecraft's critique in _A Vindication of the Rights of Woman_. She repeatedly identifies the deliberate cultivation of ignorance in women — disguised under the name of "innocence" — as the mechanism by which they are kept subordinate and the primary obstacle to their moral development. The phrase "unsightly ignorance" [001-011] captures her view that ignorance is not a natural female condition but an imposed and damaging one.

## Ignorance Disguised as Innocence

One of Wollstonecraft's most pointed arguments is that what society calls female "innocence" is in fact "profound ignorance" [005-043]. She exposes the equation of ignorance with virtue as a dangerous deception: women are "kept in ignorance under the specious name of innocence" [002-003], and "ignorance is courteously termed" innocence [003-021]. This linguistic trick, she argues, serves to make women complicit in their own subordination: believing their ignorance to be a virtue, they have no motive to seek knowledge.

## Consequences

The consequences of cultivated ignorance are severe. Wollstonecraft argues that ignorance produces the very defects it is supposed to prevent: women kept ignorant become frivolous, cunning, and morally unreliable — not because these are natural female traits, but because ignorance leaves no other path. "The emulation produced by ignorance" [005-142] drives women to compete for male attention rather than pursue genuine virtue.

Without knowledge, women cannot exercise reason. Without reason, they cannot be virtuous. The chain — ignorance to unreason to vice — is one Wollstonecraft traces through the entire work.

## The Antidote

Wollstonecraft's remedy is clear: education that develops reason rather than merely imparting accomplishments. She urges women to proceed "regardless of the sneers of ignorance" [003-011] on a plan of genuine self-improvement through knowledge. Ignorance is overcome not by more rules or better conduct books, but by the cultivation of the rational faculties that distinguish human beings from animals.
