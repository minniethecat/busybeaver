## Definition

"False refinement" is one of Wollstonecraft's central critical concepts, denoting the artificial cultivation of [[sensibility]], manners, and taste at the expense of genuine moral and intellectual development. She opens the *Vindication* by asserting that "the minds of women are enfeebled by false refinement" [P03-002], positioning it as the primary mechanism by which women are rendered weak, dependent, and incapable of [[virtue]]. For Wollstonecraft, refinement is not inherently objectionable — it is *false* refinement that she targets: the substitution of polished surfaces for substance, of elegant [[sensibility]] for rational [[knowledge|understanding]].

The concept appears throughout the text as a diagnosis of what ails both individuals and society. Wollstonecraft traces "the seeds of false refinement, im[[morality]], and vanity" to the upper classes [P03-006], arguing that luxury and idle wealth produce artificial beings who "undermine the very foundation of [[virtue]], and spread corruption through the whole mass of society."

## In Context

The critique of refinement runs through the entire *Vindication*. In the Introduction, Wollstonecraft identifies false refinement as the cause of women's enfeeblement [P03-002] and associates "[[taste|refinement of taste]]" with "epithets of weakness" [P03-008]. In Chapter V she extends the critique to writers who "dread a refinement of [[knowledge|wisdom]] as a deviation into absurdity" [005-119], mocking those who fear intellectual development in women.

In Chapter VII, Wollstonecraft draws a pointed analogy between false [[modesty]] and false refinement: the former "is near akin to that refinement of humanity, which never resides in the same breast with real [[sensibility]]" [007-008]. She also critiques the sexual double standard, noting that men's "refinement on sensual [[passion|desire]]" [007-019] exploits women's reputation while demanding their [[modesty|chastity]].

The concept takes on a more material dimension in Chapter VIII, where Wollstonecraft observes that "luxury has introduced a refinement in eating that destroys the constitution" [008-024], connecting bodily refinement to the broader corruption of [[character]]. In Chapter IX she links "the refinements of luxury" to "the vicious restlessness of an effeminate mind" [009-025], arguing that the pursuit of refined pleasure ultimately degrades both individual and society.

## Key Passages

> "...the minds of women are enfeebled by false refinement; that the books of instruction, written by men of genius, have had the same tendency as more frivolous productions." [P03-002]

> "Perhaps the seeds of false refinement, immorality, and vanity have ever been shed by the great. Weak, artificial beings raised above the common wants and affections of their race, in a premature unnatural manner, undermine the very foundation of virtue, and spread corruption through the whole mass of society!" [P03-006]

> "...the soft phrases, susceptibility of heart, delicacy of sentiment, and refinement of taste, are almost synonymous with epithets of weakness." [P03-008]

> "Luxury has introduced a refinement in eating that destroys the constitution; and a refinement in dress, that, far from concealing, only renders the person a more disgusting object." [008-024]

> "...the refinements of luxury, or the vicious restlessness of an effeminate mind, that has not strength to enjoy the pleasures of life without the aid of artificial stimulants." [009-025]

## Related Concepts

- [[virtue]] — false refinement is the antithesis of genuine [[virtue]], substituting surface for substance
- [[sensibility]] — refinement cultivates a false, performative [[sensibility]] rather than genuine fellow-feeling
- [[education]] — Wollstonecraft argues that women's [[education]] promotes false refinement rather than [[knowledge|understanding]]
- [[taste]] — [[taste|refinement of taste]] is cited as a symptom of weakness rather than an accomplishment
- [[modesty]] — false [[modesty]] is compared to false refinement as a species of the same moral corruption
- [[nature]] — refinement is artificial; [[nature]], by contrast, is simple and true

## Related Persons

- [[Rousseau]] — though Wollstonecraft admires aspects of his thought, she sees his celebration of [[sensibility]] as contributing to the culture of false refinement
- [[charles-maurice-de-talleyrand-perigord|Charles-Maurice de Talleyrand-Périgord]] — his report on [[education]] exemplifies the class-based refinement Wollstonecraft critiques
