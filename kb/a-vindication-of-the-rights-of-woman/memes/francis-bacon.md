# Francis Bacon

## Overview

Francis Bacon, Viscount St Alban (1561–1626), was an English philosopher and statesman whose advocacy of the empirical method made him a foundational figure of the Scientific Revolution. Wollstonecraft references Bacon — "Lord Bacon" — as an exemplar of the intellectual achievement that education makes possible, implicitly contrasting women's exclusion from such achievement with Bacon's example of what the cultivated mind can accomplish.

## In the Text

Bacon is referenced briefly as an authority and exemplar. His name serves a rhetorical function in Wollstonecraft's argument: if education and opportunity can produce a Bacon in one sex, what comparable achievements might the other sex produce if given the same advantages?

## Biographical Summary

Francis Bacon (1561–1626) was an English philosopher, statesman, and essayist. He served as Lord Chancellor under James I. His philosophical works, particularly *Novum Organum* (1620), advocated for empirical scientific method and helped lay the foundations of modern science.
