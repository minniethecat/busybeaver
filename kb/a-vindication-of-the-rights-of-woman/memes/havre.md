# Havre

Le Havre, a port city in Normandy, appears in the biographical sketch as the destination to which Wollstonecraft followed Gilbert Imlay during a pivotal period of their relationship.

## Location

Le Havre is a major port city on the English Channel, located in the Normandy region of northern France. In the 1790s, it was a significant commercial and maritime center, connecting France with Britain and the wider Atlantic world.

## Relevance to the Text

Havre is mentioned only in the biographical sketch prefixed to the text, but the reference marks a crucial moment in Wollstonecraft's personal history — a time when her emotional attachment to Imlay drew her across France.

### In Wollstonecraft's Life

The biographical sketch records that "Imlay left Paris for Havre, whither after a time Mary followed him" (P01-014). This journey occurred during the turbulent years of the French Revolution, when Wollstonecraft was living in Paris. Her pursuit of Imlay to Havre reflects the intensity of an attachment that would ultimately end in profound disappointment — Imlay's affections had "waned, if they were not estranged" (P01-015), and upon her return to England, the relationship dissolved.

The Havre episode encapsulates the personal cost of Wollstonecraft's philosophy of emotional independence. Her own life exemplified the tension she diagnosed in _A Vindication_: between the rational ideal of autonomy and the human reality of emotional vulnerability.

## Historical Context

Le Havre in the 1790s was a busy port city caught up in the upheavals of Revolutionary France. Its position on the Channel made it a transit point between Britain and France, and it played a role in both commerce and military operations during the Revolutionary Wars.
