# Sermons to Young Women

## Overview

Conduct book by James Fordyce (1766) — dismissed by Wollstonecraft as florid and sentimental, an example of the literature that infantilises women under the guise of moral instruction.

## In the Text

Wollstonecraft engages this work in her broader argument about education, virtue, and the status of women. Her treatment reflects her characteristic method: accepting an author's principles and then demonstrating that their conclusions about women violate those very principles.
