# Chastity

A traditional virtue Wollstonecraft reexamines, arguing that true chastity is a moral quality of the mind, not merely a physical condition enforced on women.

## Definition

Wollstonecraft treats chastity as a genuine virtue but fundamentally redefines its basis. She rejects the reduction of chastity to physical purity — a reduction that, she argues, makes women's virtue entirely dependent on reputation rather than on moral character. True chastity, in her framework, requires the exercise of reason and moral judgment, not merely the avoidance of sexual transgression. She connects chastity to modesty, arguing that "modesty is the effect of chastity" (007-011), but only when both are understood as qualities of mind rather than as bodily states.

## Wollstonecraft's Argument

The author argues that the conventional emphasis on female chastity is a mechanism of control rather than a genuine moral concern. By making women's entire moral worth contingent on sexual purity, society reduces virtue to a physical condition that can be lost through force or deception — an absurdity if virtue is truly a quality of character. Wollstonecraft insists that chastity, like all virtues, must be grounded in rational understanding: a woman who is chaste only because she is guarded cannot be said to possess the virtue of chastity.

She is particularly critical of the double standard by which male chastity is considered irrelevant or even risible while female chastity is treated as the sum of moral character. If chastity is a genuine virtue, she argues, it must bind both sexes equally — the "one rule of right" must apply universally.

### Contrast with Contemporary Views

Conduct books treated female chastity as a woman's most precious possession — something to be guarded by parents, enforced by social opinion, and valued above life itself. Wollstonecraft radicalizes this: chastity is worthless if it is merely physical. The woman who is chaste from fear or ignorance has no more virtue than the woman who is unchaste from passion. Only rational commitment to moral principle constitutes genuine chastity.

## Key Passages

> "modesty is the effect of chastity" — on the relationship between sexual virtues (007-011)

> "the sexual distinction respecting modesty has proved fatal to virtue and happiness" (007-015)

> "the want of modesty, which I principally deplore" — distinguishing genuine from false sexual virtue (007-017)
