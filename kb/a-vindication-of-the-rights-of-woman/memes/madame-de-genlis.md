# Madame de Genlis

1746 – 1830. French writer, educator, and governess to the children of the Duke of Orléans, whose educational works Wollstonecraft references in her survey of contemporary thought on female education.

## Life

Stéphanie Félicité du Crest de Saint-Aubin, Comtesse de Genlis, was one of the most prolific female writers of her time. She served as governess to the children of the Duke of Orléans (including the future King Louis-Philippe) and wrote extensively on education, producing both theoretical works and instructional materials. Her *Théâtre d'éducation* and *Adèle et Théodore* were widely read across Europe.

## Role in the Text

Wollstonecraft references Genlis as part of her survey of writers who have addressed the subject of female education. While she acknowledges Genlis's influence, she does not accord her the same sustained engagement she gives to Rousseau or Gregory. Genlis represents, in the Vindication, the broader landscape of educational writing against which Wollstonecraft positions her own more radical arguments.

### Works Referenced

- *Adèle et Théodore* (1782) — educational novel
- *Théâtre d'éducation* (1779–1780) — plays for children

## Key Ideas

Genlis advocated for a more serious education for girls than was conventional, but her vision remained within the framework of preparing women for their domestic roles. Wollstonecraft's argument goes further, demanding not merely improved domestic education but the full intellectual cultivation that would enable women to participate as equal rational agents in society.
