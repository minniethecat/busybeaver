# All Events

A chronological index of the key events that drive the plot of Hogg's novel. The dual-narrative structure — first an objective "Editor's Narrative," then Wringhim's own "Confessions" — means many events appear twice, each version casting the other into doubt.

## About This List

The novel's events form a pattern of doubling and reversal. George Colwan's murder is narrated first from the outside (as a mystery) and then from within (as Wringhim's divine mission). This structural irony is the novel's central artistic achievement.
