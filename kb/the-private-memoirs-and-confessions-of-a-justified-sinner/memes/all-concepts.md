# All Concepts

A complete index of the theological, philosophical, and literary concepts that appear in Hogg's novel. The work is saturated with Calvinist theology — predestination, election, reprobation, antinomianism — and these doctrines drive the plot as much as any character.

## About This List

*The Private Memoirs and Confessions of a Justified Sinner* is one of the most theologically dense novels in English literature. Its concepts are not mere backdrop — they are the engine of the narrative. Understanding the doctrine of election, for instance, is essential to understanding why Robert Wringhim believes himself incapable of sin.
