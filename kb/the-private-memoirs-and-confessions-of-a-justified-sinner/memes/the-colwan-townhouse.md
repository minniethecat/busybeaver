# The Colwan Townhouse

## Description

The Colwan townhouse in Edinburgh is the family's urban residence — a typical early 18th-century Edinburgh tenement or townhouse where the family stays during the social season. It provides a domestic space within the city, contrasting with the rural setting of Dalcastle.

> "The upper, or third, story of the old mansion-house was awarded to the lady for her residence. She had a separate door, a separate stair, a separate garden, and walks that in no instance intersected the laird's." (004-032)

> "Very shortly after their arrival in Edinburgh, Robert, for the first time, met with the young laird his brother, in a match at tennis." (004-065)

> "But the High Street of Edinburgh, which they soon reached, is a dangerous place in which to make an open attack upon a mob." (004-086)

## In the Novel

The townhouse appears in scenes of Edinburgh domestic life. It is one of the spaces where the family's internal dynamics — already fractured by religious division — play out in the urban context.
