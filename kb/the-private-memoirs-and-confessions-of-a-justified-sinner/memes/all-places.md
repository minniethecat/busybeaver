# All Places

An index of every significant location in the novel, from the family estates of Dalcastle and Fin-Castle to the streets and closes of early 18th-century Edinburgh. Place in this novel is never neutral — it marks the boundary between the rational Enlightenment world of George Colwan and the fevered Calvinist interior of Robert Wringhim.

## About This List

The novel moves between rural Scotland (the Border regions around Dalcastle), urban Edinburgh (Arthur's Seat, the Parliament Close), and the wild interior landscapes where Wringhim's final flight unfolds. Each setting carries theological and psychological weight.
