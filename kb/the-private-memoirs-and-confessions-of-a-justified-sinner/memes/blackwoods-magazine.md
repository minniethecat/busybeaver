# Blackwood's Magazine

## Overview

*Blackwood's Edinburgh Magazine* (commonly called *Blackwood's Magazine* or simply *Maga*) was the influential Tory monthly periodical founded by William Blackwood in 1817. It became one of the most important literary magazines of the 19th century, known for its sharp reviews, original fiction, and vigorous political commentary.

> "take the following extract from an authentic letter, published in Blackwood's Magazine for August, 1823." (008-002)

## Publication of *Confessions*

James Hogg's *The Private Memoirs and Confessions of a Justified Sinner* was first published anonymously in the July 1824 issue of *Blackwood's Magazine* before appearing in book form later that same year. The periodical context is significant: *Blackwood's* was known for its playful use of multiple personae, unreliable narrators, and hoaxes -- conventions that Hogg drew upon and subverted in his novel's layered narrative structure.

> "so often had I been hoaxed by the ingenious fancies displayed in that Magazine, that when this relation met my eye I did not believe it" (007-014)

Hogg had been a regular contributor to *Blackwood's* since its founding, and his relationship with the magazine's editor John Wilson (who wrote as "Christopher North") was complex, marked by both collaboration and condescension. The portrayal of Hogg in the magazine's *Noctes Ambrosianae* series as "the Ettrick Shepherd" -- a rustic, semi-comical figure -- was a source of lifelong resentment for the author.

> "Hogg has imposed as ingenious lies on the public ere now." (007-014)

## In the Novel

The novel's "Editor's Narrative" frame directly invokes *Blackwood's*: the editor's interest in the case is sparked by a letter Hogg himself published in the magazine, and the editor's party literally carries a copy of *Blackwood's Magazine for August* with them on their journey to investigate the suicide's grave.

> "at an early hour next morning we started for the ewe-fair of Thirlestane, taking Blackwood's Magazine for August along with us." (008-033)
