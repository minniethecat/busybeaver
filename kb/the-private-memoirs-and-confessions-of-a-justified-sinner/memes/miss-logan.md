# Miss Logan

## Biography

Miss ([[arabella-colwan|Arabella]]) Logan is a friend of the [[george-colwan|Colwan]] family who becomes the novel's chief investigator after [[george-colwan|George]]'s murder. Unlike most characters, who accept the official verdict, Miss Logan suspects [[robert-wringhim|Robert Wringhim]]'s involvement and gathers evidence against him. She is described as a woman of "good sense" and determination — one of the few characters in the novel who sees clearly through the fog of theological obfuscation. Her efforts to bring [[robert-wringhim|Robert]] to justice are ultimately unsuccessful, but her testimony preserves the truth that the [[the-editor|Editor]]'s Narrative later uncovers.

## Role in the Narrative

Miss Logan functions as the novel's moral compass — the character who insists, against all pressure, that murder is murder and that the guilty must be held accountable. Her presence in the narrative is a structural counterweight to [[robert-wringhim|Robert]]'s self-justifying "Confessions": she represents the perspective of simple moral clarity that [[robert-wringhim|Robert]]'s theology has obliterated.

## Relationships

- **[[george-colwan|George Colwan]]**: A family friend whose murder she investigates.
- **[[robert-wringhim|Robert Wringhim]]**: The man she suspects — correctly — of the murder.
- **[[the-editor|The Editor]]**: The anonymous editor who later compiles her testimony.
