# Rabina's Circle

## Biography

The circle of [[antinomianism|Antinomian]] believers surrounding the Reverend [[robert-wringhim|Robert Wringhim]] forms the religious subculture in which [[robert-wringhim|Robert]] is raised. This group — never fully enumerated but evoked through descriptions of secret meetings, extreme sermons, and shared theological convictions — represents the institutional dimension of [[antinomianism|Antinomianism]]: it is not merely one man's error but a communal system of belief and practice.

> "One Scripture text followed another, not in the least connected, and one sentence of the profound Mr. Wringhim's sermons after another, proving the duty of family worship." (004-012)

> "'Rabina, where are you? What's become of you, my dear?' cried the laird. But there was no voice nor anyone that answered or regarded." (004-015)

> "She declared her helpmate to be a limb of Antichrist, and one with whom no regenerated person could associate. She therefore bespoke a separate establishment." (004-032)

## Role in the Narrative

The circle functions as [[robert-wringhim|Robert]]'s only community — and it is a community defined by shared theological extremism. The novel suggests that [[antinomianism|Antinomianism]] flourishes in isolated, self-reinforcing groups that are closed off from moderating influences. [[robert-wringhim|Robert]]'s tragedy is not merely individual but the product of a toxic religious culture.
