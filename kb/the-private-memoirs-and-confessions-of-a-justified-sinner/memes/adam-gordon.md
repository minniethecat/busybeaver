# Adam Gordon

## Biography

Adam Gordon was a young gentleman of Edinburgh and an intimate friend of [[george-colwan|George Colwan]]. He appears during the pivotal events at [[arthurs-seat|Arthur's Seat]] and the days leading up to [[the-murder-of-george-colwan|George's murder]].

Gordon first appears at Arthur's Seat, where he attempts to persuade the mysterious stranger ([[robert-wringhim|Robert Wringhim]] in disguise) to leave so that George and his companions may enjoy their sport undisturbed. When the stranger refuses, Gordon challenges his manners: "You are no gentleman, Sir" [004-299].

## Role in the Narrative

Gordon serves as George's confidant and the voice of rational skepticism. After George witnesses the eerie phenomenon on Arthur's Seat — a halo of glory surrounding a point of cloud — Gordon dismisses supernatural explanations, arguing that the vision was merely the shadow of Robert approaching behind George. He offers to take George to his father's mountain in Aberdeenshire to demonstrate a similar natural phenomenon [004-331].

It was Gordon who warned George of his growing unpopularity in Edinburgh, advising him to keep retired during the daytime [004-309]. The two planned a Highland excursion together — a trip that was repeatedly delayed and ultimately never realized due to George's murder.

## Key Appearances

- **Arthur's Seat (004-291–299)**: Gordon confronts the stranger and is rebuffed
- **Warning George (004-309)**: Gordon alerts George to his unpopularity and the withdrawal of their acquaintances
- **The Morning After (004-331)**: Gordon hears George's account of the supernatural vision and offers a rational explanation
- **Plans for the Highlands (004-409)**: Gordon and George arrange their long-delayed excursion, the night before George's murder
