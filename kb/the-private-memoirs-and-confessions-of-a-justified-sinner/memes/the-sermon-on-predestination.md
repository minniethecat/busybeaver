# The Sermon on Predestination

## Description

The Reverend Wringhim's sermon on "the standard doctrine of absolute predestination, which put the crown on the system of the Reformation" (004-032) is the novel's theological set-piece. Preached with emotional intensity that reduces his congregation to "pangs of the most unendurable anguish," the sermon establishes the doctrinal framework that will enable Robert's crimes. The Laird of Dalcastle, who hears the sermon, is both repelled and fascinated — recognizing in it something that, taken to its logical conclusion, "overthrows all religion and morality."

## Narrative Significance

The sermon is the novel's most concentrated presentation of Antinomian theology as preached (rather than as internalized). It shows how bad theology operates at the level of rhetoric and emotional manipulation, not merely at the level of abstract doctrine.
