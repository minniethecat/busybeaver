# The Wringhim Chapel

## Description

The Reverend Wringhim's meeting-house — referred to as a chapel or meeting-house rather than a parish church — is the architectural center of the Antinomian sect that shapes Robert Wringhim's theology. It is described as a place of intense, emotionally charged worship where the doctrines of absolute predestination and the infallibility of the elect are preached with fervent conviction.

## In the Novel

The chapel is the site of the novel's key theological set-pieces: the Reverend Wringhim's sermons on predestination (004-032), the emotional responses of the congregation, and the formation of Robert's theological worldview. It functions as the institutional counterpoint to the established Church — a space where theological extremism can flourish unchecked by moderating ecclesiastical authority.

## Significance

The chapel represents the institutional dimension of Antinomianism: it is not merely a set of ideas but a community gathered around those ideas, a physical space where bad theology is preached, received, and reinforced. Hogg's novel suggests that architecture and institution matter — that heresy requires a home.
