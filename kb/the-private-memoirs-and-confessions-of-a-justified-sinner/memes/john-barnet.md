# John Barnet

## Biography

John Barnet is a servant in the [[george-colwan|Colwan]] household who has the courage to question [[robert-wringhim|Robert Wringhim]]'s extreme theology. When he objects to [[robert-wringhim|Robert]]'s claims about election and reprobation, [[robert-wringhim|Robert]] dismisses him with the devastating theological put-down: "the language of reprobation cannot affect me" (006-046). Barnet's fate — he is described as "still living in a state of reprobation" (006-011) — illustrates how [[antinomianism|Antinomian]] theology functions as a social weapon: those who disagree are not merely wrong but damned.

## Role in the Narrative

Barnet represents the ordinary believer who is crushed by the theological system [[robert-wringhim|Robert]] embodies. His function is to show that [[antinomianism|Antinomianism]] is not merely an intellectual error but a form of spiritual violence against those who dare to question it.

## Relationships

- **[[robert-wringhim|Robert Wringhim]]**: His theological antagonist, who dismisses him as reprobate.
- **[[gil-martin|Gil-Martin]]**: He instinctively distrusts [[robert-wringhim|Robert]]'s companion.

## Key Passages
