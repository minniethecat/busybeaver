# Credit

In the world of *The School for [[scandal|Scandal]]*, "[[debt|credit]]" carries its full eighteenth-century double weight: it is simultaneously a financial instrument and a measure of social trust. When a [[reputation|character]]'s [[debt|credit]] is attacked, their ability to borrow [[fortune|money]] and their [[reputation|standing]] in society collapse together — Sheridan's London offers no distinction between the two forms of currency.

## Credit as Social Trust

[[joseph-surface|Joseph Surface]] is introduced as "a [[debt|credit]] to his [[family|Family]]" (002-405), the phrase conferring a public, almost notarial validation of his [[reputation|character]]. The irony, of course, is that [[joseph-surface|Joseph]]'s entire [[debt|credit]] is counterfeit — a [[appearance-vs-reality|performance]] sustained by the very society that certifies it. [[sir-peter-teazle|Sir Peter]]'s anguished cry — "O [[sir-peter-teazle|Sir Peter]] you mustn't [[debt|credit]] the" appearance (004-384) — captures the play's central crisis: [[debt|credit]], once given, is almost impossible to revoke without the kind of dramatic revelation that only [[screen|the screen scene]] provides.

## The Fragility of Credibility

[[sir-peter-teazle|Sir Peter]] articulates the play's epistemology of trust when he observes that "[[honour|integrity]] is ever slowest to [[debt|credit]] another's Treachery" (004-391). The honest man is structurally disadvantaged in a society of appearances — his own [[honour|integrity]] blinds him to the possibility of duplicity in others. Sheridan suggests that [[debt|credit]], like reputation, is a system that rewards the performer and punishes the sincere.

## Credit and Gender

[[lady-sneerwell|Lady Sneerwell]]'s entire apparatus of scandal functions as a [[debt|credit]] market in reverse: she issues negative [[debt|credit]], destroying the "[[debt|credit]]" of her targets with the same mechanisms that [[joseph-surface|Joseph]] uses to build his. The parallel is exact — both trade in appearances, and both succeed because the society has no mechanism for auditing the difference between genuine and counterfeit [[debt|credit]].
