# Mrs. Candour

"Candour" means frankness, openness, honesty — and Mrs. Candour is none of these things. Sheridan's naming is at its most savage here: this is a [[reputation|character]] whose entire existence is a lie, whose "candour" is the vehicle for the opposite of candour. She is the scandal-monger who pretends to deplore scandal.

## Role in the Play

Mrs. Candour represents the most insidious form of the scandal economy — the participant who maintains the fiction of reluctance. "I'll not bear your abusing any Friend of mine" (002-285) — her [[reputation|character]]istic move is to defend the person she is destroying, spreading the scandal while claiming to suppress it.

Her function is to show that the scandal economy depends on performers who deny their participation. More dangerous than [[lady-sneerwell|Lady Sneerwell]] (who admits her malice) or [[sir-benjamin-backbite|Sir Benjamin]] (who is too foolish to conceal his), Mrs. Candour represents the respectable face of scandal — the face that makes the system sustainable.

## Key Scenes

Mrs. Candour appears in the early acts as a member of [[lady-sneerwell|Lady Sneerwell]]'s circle. Her "defence" of acquaintances — which always manages to repeat the most damaging details — is a comic set piece that Sheridan repeats with variations. Her exit from the play after Act II reflects her limited dramatic importance, but her satirical function is precise.
