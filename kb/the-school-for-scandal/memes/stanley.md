# Stanley (Mr. Stanley)

'Mr. Stanley' is the alias adopted by Sir Oliver Surface in Act V to test his nephew Joseph's charity. Where Sir Oliver's first disguise — 'Mr. Premium' the moneylender — tested Charles's integrity, the Stanley disguise tests Joseph's generosity. The test is devastatingly effective.

## The Charity Test

Sir Oliver, disguised as a poor relation in distress, presents himself to Joseph and asks for financial assistance. Joseph, with elaborate professions of regret and sentiment, refuses — claiming he has already given what little he could spare. The refusal is carefully calibrated to expose Joseph's true character: he speaks the language of benevolence while performing the act of a miser (005-001). Sheridan thus completes the moral contrast between the brothers. Charles, who gave what he did not have, passes the test; Joseph, who has abundance, fails.

## The Name

The choice of 'Stanley' as alias is significant. Stanley is a relation — someone with a claim on family feeling, not a stranger. Joseph's refusal to help a family member is therefore doubly damning: it is not merely uncharitable but unnatural, a violation of the bonds that the play consistently upholds as the foundation of moral life.
