# Act III, Scene I — Sir Oliver Tests Joseph

**Setting**: At Sir Peter's — Sir Peter, Sir Oliver, and Rowley  
**Characters present**: [[sir-peter-teazle]], [[sir-oliver-surface]], [[rowley]]  
**Dramatic function**: exposition

## Summary

Sir Peter briefs Sir Oliver on the Surface brothers' characters and confirms Rowley's earlier assessment. Sir Oliver announces he will test Joseph first, disguised as 'Mr. Stanley,' a poor relation seeking financial assistance. The scene sets up the charity test that will be the play's moral fulcrum. Sir Peter's marital complaints also surface — Lady Teazle has become 'the very Pattern of all that's extravagant and ridiculous in Dress,' and her intimacy with Lady Sneerwell's circle alarms him. The audience now understands both the test to come and the domestic tensions that will intersect with it.
