# Snake

Snake is named for his method — he slides through the play's moral landscape, serving whoever pays, loyal to no one. His function is to be the scandal economy's mechanic: he forges letters, inserts paragraphs, and circulates [[scandal-theme|rumour]]s for [[lady-sneerwell|Lady Sneerwell]], then betrays her when [[joseph-surface|Joseph]] offers better terms.

## Role in the Play

Snake's dramatic function is to demonstrate that the scandal economy is fundamentally transactional. "The Paragraphs you say were all inserted" (001-003) — [[lady-sneerwell|Lady Sneerwell]]'s opening line to Snake establishes their [[family|relations]]hip: he does the work, she directs the strategy. His eventual betrayal — revealing [[lady-sneerwell|Lady Sneerwell]]'s schemes in exchange for [[joseph-surface|Joseph]]'s payment — is not a moral conversion but a market transaction.

Snake's betrayal provides the plot mechanism for the play's resolution. The documents he produces expose [[lady-sneerwell|Lady Sneerwell]]'s machinations, clearing [[charles-surface|Charles]]'s path to [[maria|Maria]] and completing the comic restoration of order.

## Key Scenes

Snake appears briefly but decisively: the opening scene establishes his role in [[lady-sneerwell|Lady Sneerwell]]'s operation; his Act V confession unravels her schemes. His function is instrumental — he is the tool that builds the scandal machinery and the tool that dismantles it.
