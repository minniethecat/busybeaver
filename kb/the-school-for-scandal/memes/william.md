# William

William is a minor servant character in The School for Scandal, referenced in stage directions in Joseph Surface's household. He is one of several domestic functionaries whose presence maintains the play's sense of a fully populated social world — a world in which masters are always attended, doors are always opened, and news is always being delivered.

## Function

Like the other servants in the play (Lappet, Trip, the anonymous Servant), William exists to facilitate entrances, exits, and the flow of information. His anonymity is itself significant: Sheridan's servants are interchangeable precisely because the play's concern is with their masters' moral choices, not with the servant class as such.
