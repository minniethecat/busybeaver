# Intrigue

Intrigue — the web of scheming, plotting, and deception — is the structural mechanism that drives the plot of The School for Scandal.

## The Intrigue Plot

The play opens with intrigue: Lady Sneerwell and Snake are planting paragraphs, forging letters, and manufacturing scandals (001-001). The intrigue plot is the dark twin of the comic plot — it generates the misunderstandings that the play must unravel. Lady Sneerwell's scheme — to destroy Charles's reputation so that Joseph can marry Maria and share the fortune — is the central intrigue from which all other complications flow.

## Joseph's Double Intrigue

Joseph Surface's intrigue is layered: he conspires with Lady Sneerwell to win Maria's fortune while simultaneously attempting to seduce Lady Teazle. This double intrigue is the mechanism of his exposure — the screen scene (004-190) collapses both schemes simultaneously, as the seduction and the conspiracy are laid bare in a single motion.

## The Unraveling

Sheridan resolves the intrigue plot through counter-intrigue. Rowley bribes Snake to confess (005-240), Sir Oliver adopts disguises to test his nephews, and the conspiracy that seemed impregnable in Act I collapses under the weight of accumulated testimony. The play's argument is that intrigue, however ingeniously constructed, cannot withstand the pressure of truth.
