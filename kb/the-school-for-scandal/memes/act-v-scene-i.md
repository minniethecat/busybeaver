# Act V, Scene I — Aftermath in the Library

**Setting**: The Library in Joseph Surface's House  
**Characters present**: [[joseph-surface]], [[servant]]  
**Dramatic function**: falling action

## Summary

Joseph Surface, exposed but not yet fully disgraced, attempts damage control. He receives a visit from 'Mr. Stanley' — Sir Oliver in his second disguise — and, believing the man to be a poor relation, dismisses him coldly, claiming he has already given whatever he could spare. This is the charity test's completion: where Charles gave what he did not have, Joseph refuses to give what he has in abundance. His refusal is accompanied by the language of sentiment — he professes the warmest feelings while performing the coldest actions. Sir Oliver now has the full measure of both nephews.
