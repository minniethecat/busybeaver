# Act IV, Scene III — The Screen Scene

**Setting**: A Library in Joseph Surface's House — the famous screen scene  
**Characters present**: [[joseph-surface]], [[lady-teazle]], [[sir-peter-teazle]], [[charles-surface]]  
**Dramatic function**: confrontation

## Summary

The screen scene is the comic and dramatic climax of the play. Joseph Surface is attempting to seduce Lady Teazle in his library when Sir Peter is announced. Lady Teazle hides behind a screen. Sir Peter confides in Joseph — whom he still believes is a man of honour — about his marital troubles and his suspicions about Lady Teazle's affair with Charles. The dramatic irony is excruciating: the wife is behind the screen hearing her husband's anguished confession. When Charles himself unexpectedly arrives, Sir Peter, wanting to eavesdrop on the brothers' conversation, hides in a closet. Charles is then accused by Joseph of having designs on Lady Teazle — a charge Charles vehemently and honestly denies. The screen falls (or is thrown down), revealing Lady Teazle. Sir Peter emerges from the closet. The exposure is total: Joseph's hypocrisy is laid bare, Lady Teazle's folly is exposed, and the moral order begins its restoration. It is one of the most perfectly constructed comic climaxes in English drama.
