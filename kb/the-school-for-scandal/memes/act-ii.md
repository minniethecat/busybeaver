# ACT II

SCENE I.--SIR PETER and LADY TEAZLE

[002-001]SIR PETER. [[lady-teazle|Lady Teazle]]--Lady Teazle I'll not bear it.

[002-002]LADY TEAZLE. [[sir-peter-teazle|Sir Peter]]--Sir Peter you--may scold or smile, according to

[002-003]your Humour[,] but I ought to have my own way in everything, and what's

[002-004]more I will too--what! tho' I was educated in the country I know very

[002-005]well that women of [[fashion|Fashion]] in [[london|London]] are accountable to nobody after

[002-006]they are married.

[002-007]SIR PETER. Very well! ma'am very well! so a husband is to have no

[002-008]influence, no authority?

[002-009]LADY TEAZLE. [[power|Authority]]! no, to be sure--if you wanted authority over me,

[002-010]you should have adopted me and not married me[:] I am sure you were old

[002-011]enough.

[002-012]SIR PETER. Old enough--aye there it is--well--well--Lady Teazle, tho'

[002-013]my life may be made unhappy by your [[temper|Temper]]--I'll not be ruined by your

[002-014]extravagance--

[002-015]LADY TEAZLE. My extravagance! I'm sure I'm not more extravagant than a

[002-016]woman of Fashion ought to be.

[002-017]SIR PETER. No no Madam, you shall throw away no more sums on such

[002-018]unmeaning Luxury--'Slife to spend as much to furnish your Dressing Room

[002-019]with Flowers in winter as would suffice to turn the Pantheon into a

[002-020]Greenhouse, and give a Fete Champetre at Christmas.

[002-021]LADY TEAZLE. Lord! Sir Peter am I to blame because Flowers are dear in

[002-022]cold weather? You should find fault with the Climate, and not with me.

[002-023]For my Part I'm sure I wish it was spring all the year round--and that

[002-024]Roses grew under one's Feet!

[002-025]SIR PETER. Oons! Madam--if you had been born to those Fopperies I

[002-026]shouldn't wonder at your talking thus;--but you forget what your

[002-027]situation was when I married you--

[002-028]LADY TEAZLE. No, no, I don't--'twas a very disagreeable one or I should

[002-029]never nave married you.

[002-030]SIR PETER. Yes, yes, madam, you were then in somewhat a humbler

[002-031]Style--the daughter of a plain country Squire. Recollect Lady Teazle

[002-032]when I saw you first--sitting at your tambour in a pretty figured linen

[002-033]gown--with a Bunch of Keys at your side, and your apartment hung round

[002-034]with Fruits in worsted, of your own working--

[002-035]LADY TEAZLE. O horrible!--horrible!--don't put me in mind of it!

[002-036]SIR PETER. Yes, yes Madam and your daily occupation to inspect

[002-037]the Dairy, superintend the Poultry, make extracts from the [[family|Family]]

[002-038]Receipt-book, and comb your aunt Deborah's Lap Dog.

[002-039]LADY TEAZLE. Abominable!

[002-040]SIR PETER. Yes Madam--and what were your evening amusements? to draw

[002-041]Patterns for Ruffles, which you hadn't the materials to make--play Pope

[002-042]Joan with the Curate--to read a sermon to your Aunt--or be stuck down to

[002-043]an old Spinet to strum your father to sleep after a Fox Chase.

[002-044]LADY TEAZLE. [[scandalous|Scandalous]]--Sir Peter not a word of it true--

[002-045]SIR PETER. Yes, Madam--These were the recreations I took you from--and

[002-046]now--no one more extravagantly in the Fashion--Every Fopery adopted--a

[002-047]head-dress to o'er top Lady Pagoda with feathers pendant horizontal and

[002-048]perpendicular--you forget[,] Lady Teazle--when a little wired gauze with

[002-049]a few Beads made you a fly Cap not much bigger than a blew-bottle, and

[002-050]your Hair was comb'd smooth over a Roll--

[002-051]LADY TEAZLE. Shocking! horrible Roll!!

[002-052]SIR PETER. But now--you must have your coach--Vis-a-vis, and three

[002-053]powder'd Footmen before your Chair--and in the summer a pair of white

[002-054]cobs to draw you to Kensington Gardens--no recollection when y ou were

[002-055]content to ride double, behind the Butler, on a docked Coach-Horse?

[002-056]LADY TEAZLE. Horrid!--I swear I never did.

[002-057]SIR PETER. This, madam, was your situation--and what have I not done

[002-058]for you? I have made you woman of Fashion of [[fortune|Fortune]] of Rank--in short I

[002-059]have made you my wife.

[002-060]LADY TEAZLE. Well then and there is but one thing more you can make me

[002-061]to add to the [[debt|obligation]].

[002-062]SIR PETER. What's that pray?

[002-063]LADY TEAZLE. Your widow.--

[002-064]SIR PETER. Thank you Madam--but don't flatter yourself for though your

[002-065]ill-conduct may disturb my Peace it shall never break my Heart I promise

[002-066]you--however I am equally obliged to you for the Hint.

[002-067]LADY TEAZLE. Then why will you endeavour to make yourself so

[002-068]disagreeable to me--and thwart me in every little elegant expense.

[002-069]SIR PETER. 'Slife--Madam I pray, had you any of these elegant expenses

[002-070]when you married me?

[002-071]LADY TEAZLE. Lud Sir Peter would you have me be out of the Fashion?

[002-072]SIR PETER. The Fashion indeed!--what had you to do with the Fashion

[002-073]before you married me?

[002-074]LADY TEAZLE. For my Part--I should think you would like to have your

[002-075]wife thought a woman of [[taste|Taste]]--

[002-076]SIR PETER. Aye there again--Taste! Zounds Madam you had no Taste when

[002-077]you married me--

[002-078]LADY TEAZLE. That's very true indeed Sir Peter! after having married you

[002-079]I should never pretend to Taste again I allow.

[002-080]SIR PETER. So--so then--Madam--if these are your Sentiments pray how

[002-081]came I to be honour'd with your Hand?

[002-082]LADY TEAZLE. Shall I tell you the [[truth|Truth]]?

[002-083]SIR PETER. If it's not too great a Favour.

[002-084]LADY TEAZLE. Why the Fact is I was tired of all those agreeable

[002-085]Recreations which you have so good naturally [naturedly] Described--and

[002-086]having a Spirit to spend and enjoy a [[fortune|Fortune]]--I determined to marry the

[002-087]first rich man that would have me.

[002-088]SIR PETER. A very honest confession--truly--but pray madam was there no

[002-089]one else you might have tried to ensnare but me.

[002-090]LADY TEAZLE. O lud--I drew my net at several but you were the only one I

[002-091]could catch.

[002-092]SIR PETER. This is plain dealing indeed--

[002-093]LADY TEAZLE. But now Sir Peter if we have finish'd our daily Jangle I

[002-094]presume I may go to my engagement at [[lady-sneerwell|Lady Sneerwell]]'s?

[002-095]SIR PETER. Aye--there's another Precious circumstance--a charming set of

[002-096]acquaintance--you have made there!

[002-097]LADY TEAZLE. Nay Sir Peter they are People of Rank and Fortune--and

[002-098]remarkably tenacious of reputation.

[002-099]SIR PETER. Yes egad they are tenacious of [[reputation|Reputation]] with a vengeance,

[002-100]for they don't chuse anybody should have a Character but themselves!

[002-101]Such a crew! Ah! many a wretch has rid on hurdles who has done less

[002-102]mischief than these utterers of forged Tales, coiners of [[scandal|Scandal]], and

[002-103]clippers of Reputation.

[002-104]LADY TEAZLE. What would you restrain the freedom of speech?

[002-105]SIR PETER. Aye they have made you just as bad [as] any one of the

[002-106][[society|Society]].

[002-107]LADY TEAZLE. Why--I believe I do bear a Part with a tolerable Grace--But

[002-108]I vow I bear no malice against the People I abuse, when I say an

[002-109]ill-natured thing, 'tis out of pure Good Humour--and I take it for

[002-110]granted they deal exactly in the same manner with me, but Sir Peter you

[002-111]know you promised to come to Lady Sneerwell's too.

[002-112]SIR PETER. Well well I'll call in, just to look after my own character.

[002-113]LADY TEAZLE. Then, indeed, you must make Haste after me, or you'll be

[002-114]too late--so good bye to ye.

[002-115]SIR PETER. So--I have gain'd much by my intended expostulation--yet

[002-116]with what a charming air she contradicts every thing I say--and how

[002-117]pleasingly she shows her contempt of my authority--Well tho' I can't

[002-118]make her love me, there is certainly a great satisfaction in quarrelling

[002-119]with her; and I think she never appears to such advantage as when she is

[002-120]doing everything in her Power to plague me.

[002-121][Exit.]
