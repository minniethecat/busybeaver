# Act III, Scene II — At Charles's House

**Setting**: At Charles Surface's House — Trip, Moses, and Sir Oliver  
**Characters present**: [[trip]], [[moses]], [[sir-oliver-surface]]  
**Dramatic function**: encounter

## Summary

Sir Oliver, still disguised as 'Mr. Premium' (a moneylender), arrives at Charles's house accompanied by Moses, the honest Jewish broker who serves as intermediary. Trip, Charles's insolent servant, provides a comic prelude — he has borrowed his master's manners along with his clothes and treats the visitors with fashionable contempt. Moses explains Charles's financial situation: he is perpetually borrowing but always at the mercy of usurers. Sir Oliver, posing as a lender, waits to meet Charles. The scene introduces the motif of disguise as moral test and establishes the chaotic, good-natured atmosphere of Charles's household as a contrast to Joseph's calculated respectability.
