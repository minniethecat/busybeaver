# Town and Country

The moral geography of *The School for [[scandal|Scandal]]* is organised around the opposition between Town (London) and Country. The Town is the site of the School — the place where reputations are manufactured and destroyed, where fashion governs behaviour, and where [[appearance-vs-reality|surfaces]] are mistaken for substance. The Country is the place of origin and the memory of uncorrupted virtue — the world [[lady-teazle|Lady Teazle]] has left and to which, morally, she must return.

## Overview

[[lady-teazle|Lady Teazle]]'s complaint that [[sir-peter-teazle|Sir Peter]] makes "no allowance for the ills one suffers from a Country Education" (002-038) encapsulates the Town's attitude toward rural origins: they are a liability to be overcome. The play traces [[lady-teazle|Lady Teazle]]'s progress through the Town's curriculum — fashion, extravagance, the [[appearance-vs-reality|performance]] of sentiment — and her eventual graduation into genuine moral under[[reputation|standing]].

[[sir-oliver-surface|Sir Oliver]]'s return from the East Indies introduces a third term: the colonial. His sixteen years abroad place him outside the Town-Country binary, enabling his role as an external investigator of metropolitan corruption. The colonial [[wealth-and-class|fortune]] he brings home — "nothing of the family property left" (003-577), he discovers — becomes the means by which the moral order is restored.

## Key Scenes

[[lady-teazle|Lady Teazle]]'s trajectory from country girl to town wife is traced through her domestic scenes with [[sir-peter-teazle|Sir Peter]] and her near-seduction by [[joseph-surface|Joseph]]. Her reconciliation with [[sir-peter-teazle|Sir Peter]] — and his admission that he has been a "Tyrant" — restores the moral balance that the Town has disrupted.

## Characters Associated

- **[[lady-teazle|Lady Teazle]]**: the country girl educated (and nearly corrupted) by the Town
- **[[sir-oliver-surface|Sir Oliver Surface]]**: the colonial outsider whose perspective enables moral judgment
- **[[lady-sneerwell|Lady Sneerwell]]**: the Town personified — her dressing table is the scandal economy's metropolitan headquarters
