# Maria

Maria is the still point around which the play's romantic intrigues revolve. As [[lady-sneerwell|Lady Sneerwell]]'s ward, she is caught between her guardian's schemes and her own [[love|affection]]s — and her unwavering preference for [[charles-surface|Charles Surface]], despite his reputation, makes her the play's first and most consistent moral judge.

## Role in the Play

Maria's dramatic function is to be the [[reputation|character]] who sees clearly from the beginning. "Such reports are highly scandalous" (001-209) — her response to [[scandal-theme|gossip]] about [[charles-surface|Charles]] establishes her as someone who refuses to participate in the scandal economy. While other [[reputation|character]]s must be deceived and undeceived ([[sir-peter-teazle|Sir Peter]]) or tested and vindicated ([[charles-surface|Charles]]), Maria requires no correction — she knows the [[appearance-vs-reality|truth]] from the start.

Her position as ward places her at the intersection of the play's power dynamics. [[lady-sneerwell|Lady Sneerwell]] schemes to marry her to [[joseph-surface|Joseph]]; [[sir-benjamin-backbite|Sir Benjamin Backbite]] pursues her with comic ineptitude; [[sir-peter-teazle|Sir Peter]] serves as her guardian. Through it all, Maria maintains her preference for [[charles-surface|Charles]] — a preference that the play's resolution validates.

## Key Scenes

Maria's scenes with [[lady-sneerwell|Lady Sneerwell]] (Act I) establish her resistance to the scandal-monger's influence. Her exchanges with [[sir-benjamin-backbite|Sir Benjamin Backbite]] provide comic relief while demonstrating her clear-sightedness about [[reputation|character]]. Her reunion with [[charles-surface|Charles]] at the play's conclusion rewards the constancy she has maintained throughout.

## Relationships

- **[[charles-surface|Charles Surface]]**: beloved; Maria's steadfast preference for [[charles-surface|Charles]], despite his reputation, is the play's first moral judgment
- **[[lady-sneerwell|Lady Sneerwell]]**: guardian and antagonist; [[lady-sneerwell|Lady Sneerwell]] seeks to control Maria's [[marriage-and-gender|marriage]] for her own purposes
- **[[joseph-surface|Joseph Surface]]**: unwelcome suitor; [[joseph-surface|Joseph]]'s mercenary [[love|courtship]] of Maria contrasts with [[charles-surface|Charles]]'s genuine [[sentiment|feeling]]
- **[[sir-benjamin-backbite|Sir Benjamin Backbite]]**: comic suitor; [[sir-benjamin-backbite|Backbite]]'s inept pursuit provides comic contrast to the main romantic plot
