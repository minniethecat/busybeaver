# Act II, Scene II — At Lady Sneerwell's

**Setting**: Lady Sneerwell's House  
**Characters present**: [[lady-sneerwell]], [[mrs-candour]], [[crabtree]], [[sir-benjamin-backbite]], [[joseph-surface]]  
**Dramatic function**: encounter

## Summary

The scandalous company assembles at Lady Sneerwell's, and the competitive gossip escalates. Crabtree and Sir Benjamin Backbite vie to produce the most damaging intelligence, with Crabtree claiming exclusive knowledge of an affair while Backbite retorts with an even more scandalous counter-rumour. Mrs. Candour coos her signature brand of affected benevolence — professing to be shocked by the gossip while eagerly spreading it. The scene satirises the machinery of rumour: each character claims to have the story on unimpeachable authority, yet each contradictory version traces back to the same anonymous source. When Charles Surface's name is raised, Sir Benjamin attacks him as a libertine while Crabtree produces a fresh scandal about his debts. Joseph Surface, present but strategically quiet, allows the slander to proceed uncorrected — revealing the depth of his complicity.

## Key Moments

- **Crabtree and Backbite compete for scandal supremacy** (002-108): Each tries to outdo the other with increasingly lurid tales, exposing the competitive nature of gossip.
- **Mrs. Candour's faux-innocence** (002-135): She expresses shock while ensuring every scandal is fully aired — the hypocrite's art perfected.
- **Charles Surface is slandered** (002-160): Joseph silently allows his brother's reputation to be savaged, marking the moral turning point.
- **Joseph's calculated silence** (002-175): His refusal to defend Charles reveals the conspiracy with Lady Sneerwell operating in real time.
