# Act V, Scene the Last — Final Reckoning

**Setting**: The Library — the final assembly and unmasking  
**Characters present**: [[joseph-surface]], [[charles-surface]], [[sir-oliver-surface]], [[sir-peter-teazle]], [[lady-teazle]], [[lady-sneerwell]], [[maria]], [[rowley]], [[snake]]  
**Dramatic function**: denouement

## Summary

The play's final scene assembles all major characters for the definitive unmasking. Sir Oliver reveals his true identity. Joseph, having been exposed in both the screen scene and the charity test, attempts a final defence but is silenced by the accumulated evidence. Lady Sneerwell's conspiracy is also exposed — Snake, bribed by Rowley, confesses the forged letters and planted paragraphs. Charles is forgiven and promised Maria's hand. Joseph is disgraced but not destroyed — Sheridan allows him to exit with his dignity in tatters but his person intact, a gentler fate than Restoration comedy would have dealt. Sir Peter and Lady Teazle are reconciled. The moral order is restored: true virtue (Charles's impulsive goodness) triumphs over false (Joseph's calculated sentiment), and scandal, for once, is exposed rather than merely circulated.
