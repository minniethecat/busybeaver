# Tale

"A Tale of [[scandal|Scandal]] is as fatal to the [[reputation|Reputation]] of a Woman as a Pistol is to the Life of a Man" (001-293). Sheridan's metaphor is startling in its precision: the tale is a weapon, its medium is narrative, and its target is reputation. The play's anatomy of the scandalous tale — who tells it, how it circulates, and whom it destroys — is among the most sophisticated treatments of [[scandal-theme|gossip]] in English literature.

## The Tale-Makers

Sheridan [[wealth-and-class|class]]ifies the participants in the scandal economy with sociological precision. There are the "talemakers" (001-234) — the originators who fabricate or embellish. There are the disseminators — [[mrs-candour|Mrs. Candour]], whose name is its own satire, and [[sir-benjamin-backbite|Sir Benjamin Backbite]], whose very name describes his method. And there are the consumers — the "tale-bearers" and "tale-hearers" who, as [[lady-sneerwell|Lady Sneerwell]] observes, "are just as bad as the talemakers" (001-234). The moral is clear: a society gets the tales it deserves.

## The Tale as Currency

Tales in *The School for [[scandal|Scandal]]* behave like [[fortune|money]]. They circulate, they appreciate and depreciate, and they can be coined by anyone with the requisite malice and wit. [[lady-sneerwell|Lady Sneerwell]] runs what amounts to a tale-mint, producing and distributing scandalous narratives in exchange for social power. The play's deep insight is that tales, like coin, require [[credit|trust]] to function — a society must *believe* in the tales it circulates, and this belief is what Sheridan's satire seeks to undermine.

## Tales Without Foundation

[[sir-peter-teazle|Sir Peter]]'s lament that [[lady-sneerwell|Lady Sneerwell]] has spread "scandalous tales of me, and all without any" foundation (004-274) exposes the tale's essential nature: it requires no basis in [[appearance-vs-reality|truth]] to do its work. The tale, in Sheridan's world, is self-validating — its circulation is its proof. The play's resolution, in which [[appearance-vs-reality|truth]] finally overtakes tale, is a comic fantasy that the preceding five acts have done everything to render improbable.
