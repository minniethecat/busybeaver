# Act II, Scene I — The Teazles' Quarrel

**Setting**: Sir Peter and Lady Teazle — a domestic quarrel  
**Characters present**: sir-peter-teazle, lady-teazle  
**Dramatic function**: confrontation

## Summary

The scene opens with Sir Peter and Lady Teazle in the midst of a domestic dispute — he reproaches her extravagance and her determination to participate in London's fashionable life; she retorts with mockery of his jealousy and controlling temper. Their quarrel escalates through a series of witty exchanges, establishing the comedy of their mismatched marriage. Sir Peter laments the days when Lady Teazle was a modest country girl; she reminds him that he married her precisely because she was 'so very simple.' The argument turns to her association with Lady Sneerwell's scandalous circle, which Sir Peter warns will destroy her reputation. Lady Teazle dismisses his concerns. The scene ends with Lady Teazle refusing to give up her fashionable life and declaring she will go out that evening.
