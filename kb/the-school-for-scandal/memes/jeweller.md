# Jeweller

The jeweller, though a peripheral figure, represents the commercial infrastructure that underpins the fashionable world of The School for Scandal.

## The Credit Economy

The jeweller appears in the context of Charles Surface's financial difficulties — a creditor among many, a reminder of the web of debt in which Charles is entangled. The jeweller, the moneylender, the broker — these commercial figures constitute the play's shadow economy, the material reality beneath the glittering surface of fashionable life.

## Commerce and Character

Sheridan uses the commercial figures — Moses the honest broker, the anonymous jeweller, the usurers — to establish a moral economy that runs parallel to the social one. Moses is honest despite his profession; the jeweller is patient despite his unpaid bills; the usurers are predatory despite their respectable façades. These figures remind the audience that the world of fashion rests on a foundation of commerce, and that character may be found in unexpected places.
