# Moses

Moses is a [[fortune|money]]lender — one of the play's several financial functionaries who service the gentry's need for [[debt|credit]]. His comic function is to be the honest tradesman in a dishonest world, the professional who knows exactly what his clients are worth and charges accordingly.

## Role in the Play

Moses's dramatic function is to provide comic commentary on the play's financial themes. His introduction of "[[sir-oliver-surface|Mr. Premium]]" ([[sir-oliver-surface|Sir Oliver]] in [[appearance-vs-reality|disguise]]) to [[charles-surface|Charles]] sets the auction scene in motion. His professional frankness — "I know [[fortune|money]] isn't to be bought without paying for it" (003-503) — is a comic antidote to the play's more elaborate [[appearance-vs-reality|deception]]s.

## Key Scenes

Act III: Moses introduces [[sir-oliver-surface|Sir Oliver]] (as [[sir-oliver-surface|Mr. Premium]]) to [[charles-surface|Charles]]. His scenes with [[charles-surface|Charles]] and [[sir-oliver-surface|Sir Oliver]] provide the transactional framework for the play's moral testing.

## Relationships

- **[[charles-surface|Charles Surface]]**: client; Moses facilitates [[charles-surface|Charles]]'s [[debt|borrowing]] with professional detachment
- **[[sir-oliver-surface|Sir Oliver Surface]]**: unwitting instrument; Moses introduces [[sir-oliver-surface|Sir Oliver]]'s [[appearance-vs-reality|disguise]]d persona into [[charles-surface|Charles]]'s circle
