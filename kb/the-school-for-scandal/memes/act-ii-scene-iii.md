# Act II, Scene III — Sir Oliver's Arrival

**Setting**: At Sir Peter's — Rowley and Sir Oliver Surface  
**Characters present**: [[rowley]], [[sir-oliver-surface]]  
**Dramatic function**: exposition

## Summary

Sir Oliver Surface, newly returned from the East Indies after sixteen years abroad, meets Rowley to inquire about his two nephews. Rowley paints a nuanced picture: Joseph is outwardly respectable but inwardly calculating; Charles is extravagant and dissolute but fundamentally good-hearted. Sir Oliver resolves to test both nephews himself — Joseph first, then Charles — by adopting disguises. The scene establishes the play's central moral experiment: can outward appearances be trusted? Sir Oliver embodies the audience's own desire to distinguish substance from show, and his decision to test rather than judge marks him as the play's moral centre.
