# Epilogue

The epilogue to The School for Scandal, like the prologue, frames the audience's experience of the play and reflects on its moral design.

## Concluding the Play

The epilogue serves the traditional function of releasing the audience from the play's moral world back into their own. After two hours of watching hypocrisy exposed and virtue rewarded, the epilogue reminds the audience that they too live in a world of appearances — and that the play's lessons apply beyond the theatre walls.

## The Epilogue's Tone

The epilogue maintains the play's characteristic balance of wit and moral seriousness. It does not preach — Sheridan was too skilled a dramatist for that — but it gently insists that the distinctions the play has made (between sincerity and performance, generosity and selfishness, true virtue and false) are not merely theatrical but real.
