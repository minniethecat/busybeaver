# ACT IV

SCENE I.--A Picture Room in CHARLES SURFACE'S House

[004-001]Enter CHARLES, SIR OLIVER, MOSES, and CARELESS

[004-002]CHARLES. Walk in, gentlemen, pray walk in;--here they are, the family of

[004-003]the Surfaces, up to the Conquest.

[004-004]SIR OLIVER. And, in my opinion, a goodly collection.

[004-005]CHARLES. Ay, ay, these are done in the true spirit of portrait-painting;

[004-006]no volontiere grace or expression. Not like the works of your modern

[004-007]Raphaels, who give you the strongest resemblance, yet contrive to make

[004-008]your portrait independent of you; so that you may sink the original

[004-009]and not hurt the [[portrait|picture]]. No, no; the merit of these is the inveterate

[004-010]likeness--all stiff and awkward as the originals, and like nothing in

[004-011]human nature besides.

[004-012]SIR OLIVER. Ah! we shall never see such figures of men again.

[004-013]CHARLES. I hope not. Well, you see, Master Premium, what a domestic

[004-014][[reputation|character]] I am; here I sit of an evening surrounded by my family. But

[004-015]come, get to your pulpit, Mr. Auctioneer; here's an old gouty chair of

[004-016]my grandfather's will answer the purpose.

[004-017]CARELESS. Ay, ay, this will do. But, [[charles-surface|Charles]], I haven't a hammer; and

[004-018]what's an auctioneer without his hammer?

[004-019]CHARLES. Egad, that's true. What parchment have we here? Oh, our

[004-020]genealogy in full. [Taking pedigree down.] Here, [[careles|Careless]], you shall

[004-021]have no common bit of mahogany, here's the family tree for you,

[004-022]you rogue! This shall be your hammer, and now you may knock down my

[004-023]ancestors with their own pedigree.

[004-024]SIR OLIVER. What an unnatural rogue!--an ex post facto parricide!

[004-025][Aside.]

[004-026]CARELESS. Yes, yes, here's a list of your generation indeed;--faith,

[004-027]Charles, this is the most convenient thing you could have found for the

[004-028]business, for 'twill not only serve as a hammer, but a catalogue into

[004-029]the bargain. Come, begin--A-going, a-going, a-going!

[004-030]CHARLES. Bravo, Careless! Well, here's my great uncle, Sir Richard

[004-031]Ravelin, a marvellous good general in his day, I assure you. He served

[004-032]in all the Duke of Marlborough's wars, and got that cut over his eye

[004-033]at the battle of Malplaquet. What say you, [[sir-oliver-surface|Mr. Premium]]? look at

[004-034]him--there's a hero! not cut out of his feathers, as your modern clipped

[004-035]captains are, but enveloped in wig and regimentals, as a general should

[004-036]be. What do you bid?

[004-037]SIR OLIVER. [Aside to [[moses|Moses]].] Bid him speak.

[004-038]MOSES. Mr. Premium would have you speak.

[004-039]CHARLES. Why, then, he shall have him for ten pounds, and I'm sure

[004-040]that's not dear for a staff-officer.

[004-041]SIR OLIVER. [Aside.] Heaven deliver me! his famous uncle Richard for ten

[004-042]pounds!--[Aloud.] Very well, sir, I take him at that.

[004-043]CHARLES. Careless, knock down my uncle Richard.--Here, now, is a maiden

[004-044]sister of his, my great-aunt Deborah, done by Kneller, in his best

[004-045]manner, and esteemed a very formidable likeness. There she is, you see,

[004-046]a shepherdess feeding her flock. You shall have her for five pounds

[004-047]ten--the sheep are worth the [[wealth-and-class|money]].

[004-048]SIR OLIVER. [Aside.] Ah! poor Deborah! a woman who set such a value on

[004-049]herself!--[Aloud.] Five pounds ten--she's mine.

[004-050]CHARLES. Knock down my aunt Deborah! Here, now, are two that were a sort

[004-051]of cousins of theirs.--You see, Moses, these pictures were done some

[004-052]time ago, when beaux wore wigs, and the ladies their own hair.

[004-053]SIR OLIVER. Yes, truly, head-dresses appear to have been a little lower

[004-054]in those days.

[004-055]CHARLES. Well, take that couple for the same.

[004-056]MOSES. 'Tis a good bargain.

[004-057]CHARLES. Careless!--This, now, is a grandfather of my mother's, a

[004-058]learned judge, well known on the western circuit,--What do you rate him

[004-059]at, Moses?

[004-060]MOSES. Four guineas.

[004-061]CHARLES. Four guineas! Gad's life, you don't bid me the price of his

[004-062]wig.--Mr. Premium, you have more respect for the woolsack; do let us

[004-063]knock his lordship down at fifteen.

[004-064]SIR OLIVER. By all means.

[004-065]CARELESS. Gone!

[004-066]CHARLES. And there are two brothers of his, William and Walter Blunt,

[004-067]Esquires, both members of Parliament, and noted speakers; and, what's

[004-068]very extraordinary, I believe, this is the first time they were ever

[004-069]bought or sold.

[004-070]SIR OLIVER. That is very extraordinary, indeed! I'll take them at your

[004-071]own price, for the honour of Parliament.

[004-072]CARELESS. Well said, little Premium! I'll knock them down at forty.

[004-073]CHARLES. Here's a jolly fellow--I don't know what relation, but he was

[004-074]mayor of Norwich: take him at eight pounds.

[004-075]SIR OLIVER. No, no; six will do for the mayor.

[004-076]CHARLES. Come, make it guineas, and I'll throw you the two aldermen here

[004-077]into the bargain.

[004-078]SIR OLIVER. They're mine.

[004-079]CHARLES. Careless, knock down the mayor and aldermen. But, plague on't!

[004-080]we shall be all day retailing in this manner; do let us deal wholesale:

[004-081]what say you, little Premium? Give me three hundred pounds for the rest

[004-082]of the family in the lump.

[004-083]CARELESS. Ay, ay, that will be the best way.

[004-084]SIR OLIVER. Well, well, anything to accommodate you; they are mine. But

[004-085]there is one portrait which you have always passed over.

[004-086]CARELESS. What, that ill-looking little fellow over the settee?

[004-087]SIR OLIVER. Yes, sir, I mean that; though I don't think him so

[004-088]ill-looking a little fellow, by any means.

[004-089]CHARLES. What, that? Oh; that's my uncle Oliver! 'Twas done before he

[004-090]went to India.

[004-091]CARELESS. Your uncle Oliver! Gad, then you'll never be friends,

[004-092]Charles. That, now, to me, is as stern a looking rogue as ever I saw; an

[004-093]unforgiving eye, and a damned disinheriting countenance! an inveterate

[004-094]knave, depend on't. Don't you think so, little Premium?

[004-095]SIR OLIVER. Upon my soul, Sir, I do not; I think it is as honest a

[004-096]looking face as any in the room, dead or alive. But I suppose uncle

[004-097]Oliver goes with the rest of the lumber?

[004-098]CHARLES. No, hang it! I'll not part with poor Noll. The old fellow has

[004-099]been very good to me, and, egad, I'll keep his picture while I've a room

[004-100]to put it in.

[004-101]SIR OLIVER. [Aside.] The rogue's my nephew after all!--[Aloud.] But,

[004-102]sir, I have somehow taken a fancy to that picture.

[004-103]CHARLES. I'm sorry for't, for you certainly will not have it. Oons,

[004-104]haven't you got enough of them?

[004-105]SIR OLIVER. [Aside.] I forgive him everything!--[Aloud.] But, Sir, when

[004-106]I take a whim in my head, I don't value [[fortune|money]]. I'll give you as much for

[004-107]that as for all the rest.

[004-108]CHARLES. Don't tease me, master broker; I tell you I'll not part with

[004-109]it, and there's an end of it.

[004-110]SIR OLIVER. [Aside.] How like his father the dog is.--[Aloud.] Well,

[004-111]well, I have done.--[Aside.] I did not perceive it before, but I think

[004-112]I never saw such a striking resemblance.--[Aloud.] Here is a draught for

[004-113]your sum.

[004-114]CHARLES. Why, 'tis for eight hundred pounds!

[004-115]SIR OLIVER. You will not let Sir Oliver go?

[004-116]CHARLES. Zounds! no! I tell you, once more.

[004-117]SIR OLIVER. Then never mind the difference, we'll balance that another

[004-118]time. But give me your hand on the bargain; you are an honest fellow,

[004-119]Charles--I beg pardon, sir, for being so free.--Come, Moses.

[004-120]CHARLES. Egad, this is a whimsical old fellow!--But hark'ee, Premium,

[004-121]you'll prepare lodgings for these gentlemen.

[004-122]SIR OLIVER. Yes, yes, I'll send for them in a day or two.

[004-123]CHARLES. But, hold; do now send a genteel conveyance for them, for, I

[004-124]assure you, they were most of them used to ride in their own carriages.

[004-125]SIR OLIVER. I will, I will--for all but Oliver.

[004-126]CHARLES. Ay, all but the little nabob.

[004-127]SIR OLIVER. You're fixed on that?

[004-128]CHARLES. Peremptorily.

[004-129]SIR OLIVER. [Aside.] A dear extravagant rogue!--[Aloud.] Good day! Come,

[004-130]Moses.--[Aside.] Let me hear now who dares call him profligate!

[004-131][Exit with MOSES.]

[004-132]CARELESS. Why, this is the oddest genius of the sort I ever met with!

[004-133]CHARLES. Egad, he's the prince of brokers, I think. I wonder how

[004-134]the devil Moses got acquainted with so honest a fellow.--Ha! here's

[004-135][[rowley|Rowley]].--Do, Careless, say I'll join the company in a few moments.

[004-136]CARELESS. I will--but don't let that old blockhead persuade you to

[004-137]squander any of that money on old musty debts, or any such nonsense; for

[004-138]tradesmen, Charles, are the most exorbitant fellows.

[004-139]CHARLES. Very true, and paying them is only encouraging them.

[004-140]CARELESS. Nothing else.

[004-141]CHARLES. Ay, ay, never fear.--

[004-142][Exit CARELESS.]

[004-143]So! this was an odd old fellow, indeed. Let me see, two-thirds of these

[004-144]five hundred and thirty odd pounds are mine by right. Fore Heaven!

[004-145]I find one's ancestors are more valuable [[family|relations]] than I took them

[004-146]for!--Ladies and gentlemen, your most obedient and very grateful

[004-147]servant. [Bows ceremoniously to the pictures.]

[004-148]Enter ROWLEY

[004-149]Ha! old Rowley! egad, you are just come in time to take leave of your

[004-150]old acquaintance.

[004-151]ROWLEY. Yes, I heard they were a-going. But I wonder you can have such

[004-152]spirits under so many distresses.

[004-153]CHARLES. Why, there's the point! my distresses are so many, that I can't

[004-154]affort to part with my spirits; but I shall be rich and splenetic, all

[004-155]in good time. However, I suppose you are surprised that I am not more

[004-156]sorrowful at parting with so many near relations; to be sure, 'tis very

[004-157]affecting; but you see they never move a muscle, so why should I?

[004-158]ROWLEY. There's no making you serious a moment.

[004-159]CHARLES. Yes, faith, I am so now. Here, my honest Rowley, here, get me

[004-160]this changed directly, and take a hundred pounds of it immediately to

[004-161]old Stanley.

[004-162]ROWLEY. A hundred pounds! Consider only----

[004-163]CHARLES. Gad's life, don't talk about it! poor Stanley's wants are

[004-164]pressing, and, if you don't make haste, we shall have some one call that

[004-165]has a better right to the money.

[004-166]ROWLEY. Ah! there's the point! I never will cease dunning you with the

[004-167]old proverb----

[004-168]CHARLES. BE JUST BEFORE YOU'RE GENEROUS.--Why, so I would if I could;

[004-169]but Justice is an old hobbling beldame, and I can't get her to keep pace

[004-170]with [[charity|Generosity]], for the soul of me.

[004-171]ROWLEY. Yet, Charles, believe me, one hour's reflection----

[004-172]CHARLES. Ay, ay, it's very true; but, hark'ee, Rowley, while I have, by

[004-173]Heaven I'll give; so, damn your economy! and now for hazard.

[004-174][Exeunt.]

[004-182]SIR OLIVER. But He wouldn't sell my Picture. O--here's Rowley!

[004-184]ROWLEY. So--Sir Oliver--I find you have made a Purchase----

[004-185]SIR OLIVER. Yes--yes--our young Rake has parted with his Ancestors like

[004-188]ROWLEY. And here has he commissioned me to re-deliver you Part of

[004-189]the purchase-money--I mean tho' in your necessitous character of old

[004-190]Stanley----

[004-192]ROWLEY. And I left a Hosier and two Tailors in the Hall--who I'm sure

[004-193]won't be paid, and this hundred would satisfy 'em.

[004-194]SIR OLIVER. Well--well--I'll pay his debts and his Benevolences

[004-195]too--I'll take care of old Stanley--myself--But now I am no more

[004-196]a Broker, and you shall introduce me to the elder Brother as Stanley----

[004-199]Enter TRIP

[004-200]TRIP. O Gentlemen--I beg [[mercy|Pardon]] for not showing you out--this

[004-201]way--Moses, a word.

[004-202][Exit TRIP with MOSES.]

[004-203]SIR OLIVER. There's a Fellow for you--Would you believe it that Puppy

[004-204]intercepted the Jew, on our coming, and wanted to raise money before he

[004-206]ROWLEY. Indeed!

[004-207]SIR OLIVER. Yes--they are now planning an annuity Business--Ah Master

[004-208]Rowley[,] in my Day Servants were content with the Follies of their

[004-209]Masters when they were worn a little Thread Bare but now they have their

[004-210]Vices like their Birth Day cloaths with the gloss on.

[004-211][Exeunt.]
