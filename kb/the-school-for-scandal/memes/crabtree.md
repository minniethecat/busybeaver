# Crabtree

Crabtree is the senior faculty of the School for [[scandal|Scandal]]. As [[sir-benjamin-backbite|Sir Benjamin Backbite]]'s uncle, he represents the generational transmission of [[scandal-theme|scandal-mongering]] — the art of [[slander|character assassination]] passed down from one generation to the next. His name, suggesting a crab-like sideways movement, captures his method: he advances by indirection.

## Role in the Play

Crabtree's dramatic function is to show that the scandal economy is intergenerational. Where [[sir-benjamin-backbite|Sir Benjamin]] is a student of scandal, Crabtree is a master — his [[scandal-theme|gossip]] is more practised, his malice more refined. Together, uncle and nephew form a miniature dynasty of [[scandal-theme|defamation]], suggesting that the "School" is an institution with traditions, not a passing fashion.

Crabtree's appearance in Act V, spreading false reports of duels and deaths, demonstrates the scandal economy's capacity for escalation — from wounding reputations to fabricating catastrophes.

## Key Scenes

Act II, Scene 2: The scandal-mongers' gathering, where Crabtree contributes his share of malicious invention. Act V: The false reports that precipitate the play's comic climax.
