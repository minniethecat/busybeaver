# Rowley

Rowley occupies a unique position in *The School for [[scandal|Scandal]]*: he is the only [[reputation|character]] whom no one deceives. As [[sir-peter-teazle|Sir Peter]]'s steward, he moves between the play's social worlds — [[credit|trust]]ed by [[sir-peter-teazle|Sir Peter]], respected by [[sir-oliver-surface|Sir Oliver]], and undeceived by [[joseph-surface|Joseph]]'s [[appearance-vs-reality|performance]] — providing the steady moral compass that the other [[reputation|character]]s lack.

## Role in the Play

Rowley's dramatic function is to be the play's [[appearance-vs-reality|truth]]-teller. "Very bad — Master Rowley — very bad!" (001-405) — [[sir-peter-teazle|Sir Peter]]'s opening exchange with Rowley establishes their [[family|relations]]hip: Rowley tells [[sir-peter-teazle|Sir Peter]] what he needs to hear, not what he wants to hear. When [[sir-peter-teazle|Sir Peter]] stakes his life on [[joseph-surface|Joseph]]'s honour, Rowley demurs. When [[sir-oliver-surface|Sir Oliver]] returns, Rowley assists in arranging the [[appearance-vs-reality|disguise]]s that will test both nephews.

Rowley is the mechanism by which the [[appearance-vs-reality|truth]] enters the play. It is Rowley who communicates [[sir-oliver-surface|Sir Oliver]]'s return, Rowley who helps arrange the testing, and Rowley whose steady presence anchors the play's moral perspective.

## Key Scenes

Rowley's scenes with [[sir-peter-teazle|Sir Peter]] frame the play's action. His early warnings about [[joseph-surface|Joseph]] — delivered with the deference of a servant but the clarity of a friend — establish the dramatic irony that the audience will carry through the subsequent acts. His assistance in [[sir-oliver-surface|Sir Oliver]]'s schemes makes him the backstage operator who enables the play's moral machinery.

## Relationships

- **[[sir-peter-teazle|Sir Peter Teazle]]**: master and friend; Rowley's honest counsel distinguishes him from the flatterers who surround [[sir-peter-teazle|Sir Peter]]
- **[[sir-oliver-surface|Sir Oliver Surface]]**: co-conspirator; Rowley assists [[sir-oliver-surface|Sir Oliver]]'s testing of the nephews
- **[[joseph-surface|Joseph Surface]]**: undiscovered fraud; Rowley sees through [[joseph-surface|Joseph]] before any other [[reputation|character]]
