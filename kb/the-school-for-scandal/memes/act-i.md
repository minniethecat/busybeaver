# ACT I

SCENE I.--LADY SNEERWELL'S House

[001-001]LADY SNEERWELL at her dressing table with LAPPET; MISS VERJUICE drinking

[001-002]chocolate

[001-003]LADY SNEERWELL. The Paragraphs you say were all inserted:

[001-004]VERJUICE. They were Madam--and as I copied them myself in a feigned Hand

[001-005]there can be no suspicion whence they came.

[001-006]LADY SNEERWELL. Did you circulate the Report of Lady Brittle's Intrigue

[001-007]with Captain Boastall?

[001-008]VERJUICE. Madam by this Time Lady Brittle is the Talk of half the

[001-009][[london|Town]]--and I doubt not in a week the Men will toast her as a Demirep.

[001-010]LADY SNEERWELL. What have you done as to the insinuation as to a certain

[001-011]Baronet's Lady and a certain Cook.

[001-012]VERJUICE. That is in as fine a Train as your Ladyship could wish. I told

[001-013]the [[tale|story]] yesterday to my own maid with directions to communicate it

[001-014]directly to my Hairdresser. He I am informed has a Brother who courts a

[001-015]Milliners' Prentice in Pallmall whose mistress has a first cousin whose

[001-016]sister is Feme [Femme] de Chambre to [[mrs-clackit|Mrs. Clackit]]--so that in the

[001-017]common course of Things it must reach Mrs. Clackit's Ears within

[001-018]four-and-twenty hours and then you know the Business is as good as done.

[001-019]LADY SNEERWELL. Why truly Mrs. Clackit has a very pretty Talent--a great

[001-020]deal of industry--yet--yes--been tolerably successful in her way--To my

[001-021]knowledge she has been the cause of breaking off six matches[,] of three

[001-022]sons being disinherited and four Daughters being turned out of Doors.

[001-023]Of three several Elopements, as many close confinements--nine separate

[001-024]maintenances and two Divorces.--nay I have more than once traced her

[001-025]causing a Tete-a-Tete in the [[town-and-country|Town and Country]] Magazine--when the Parties

[001-026]perhaps had never seen each other's Faces before in the course of their

[001-027]Lives.

[001-028]VERJUICE. She certainly has Talents.

[001-029]LADY SNEERWELL. But her manner is gross.

[001-030]VERJUICE. 'Tis very true. She generally designs well[,] has a free

[001-031]tongue and a bold invention--but her colouring is too dark and her

[001-032]outline often extravagant--She wants that delicacy of Tint--and

[001-033]mellowness of sneer--which distinguish your Ladyship's [[scandal|Scandal]].

[001-034]LADY SNEERWELL. Ah you are Partial [[miss-verjuice|Verjuice]].

[001-035]VERJUICE. Not in the least--everybody allows that [[lady-sneerwell|Lady Sneerwell]] can do

[001-036]more with a word or a Look than many can with the most laboured Detail

[001-037]even when they happen to have a little [[appearance-vs-reality|truth]] on their side to support

[001-038]it.

[001-039]LADY SNEERWELL. Yes my dear Verjuice. I am no Hypocrite to deny the

[001-040]satisfaction I reap from the Success of my Efforts. Wounded myself, in

[001-041]the early part of my Life by the envenomed Tongue of [[slander|Slander]] I confess

[001-042]I have since known no [[pleasure|Pleasure]] equal to the reducing others to the Level

[001-043]of my own injured [[reputation|Reputation]].

[001-044]VERJUICE. Nothing can be more natural--But my dear Lady Sneerwell There

[001-045]is one affair in which you have lately employed me, wherein, I confess I

[001-046]am at a Loss to guess your motives.

[001-047]LADY SNEERWELL. I conceive you mean with respect to my neighbour, Sir

[001-048]Peter Teazle, and his [[family|Family]]--Lappet.--And has my conduct in this matter

[001-049]really appeared to you so mysterious?

[001-050][Exit MAID.]

[001-051]VERJUICE. Entirely so.

[001-052]LADY SNEERWELL. [VERJUICE.?] An old Batchelor as [[sir-peter-teazle|Sir Peter]] was[,] having

[001-053]taken a young wife from out of the Country--as [[lady-teazle|Lady Teazle]] is--are

[001-054]certainly fair subjects for a little mischievous raillery--but here are

[001-055]two young men--to whom Sir Peter has acted as a kind of Guardian since

[001-056]their Father's death, the eldest possessing the most amiable Character

[001-057]and universally well spoken of[,] the youngest the most dissipated

[001-058]and extravagant young Fellow in the Kingdom, without Friends or

[001-059]caracter--the former one an avowed admirer of yours and apparently

[001-060]your Favourite[,] the latter attached to [[maria|Maria]] Sir Peter's ward--and

[001-061]confessedly beloved by her. Now on the face of these circumstances it

[001-062]is utterly unaccountable to me why you a young Widow with no great

[001-063]jointure--should not close with the [[love|passion]] of a man of such character

[001-064]and expectations as Mr. Surface--and more so why you should be so

[001-065]uncommonly earnest to destroy the mutual Attachment subsisting between

[001-066]his Brother [[charles-surface|Charles]] and Maria.

[001-067]LADY SNEERWELL. Then at once to unravel this mistery--I must inform you

[001-068]that Love has no share whatever in the intercourse between Mr. Surface

[001-069]and me.

[001-070]VERJUICE. No!

[001-071]LADY SNEERWELL. His real attachment is to Maria or her [[fortune|Fortune]]--but

[001-072]finding in his Brother a favoured [[enemy|Rival]], He has been obliged to mask his

[001-073]Pretensions--and profit by my Assistance.

[001-074]VERJUICE. Yet still I am more puzzled why you should interest yourself

[001-075]in his success.

[001-076]LADY SNEERWELL. Heavens! how dull you are! cannot you surmise the

[001-077]weakness which I hitherto, thro' shame have concealed even from

[001-078]you--must I confess that Charles--that Libertine, that extravagant, that

[001-079]Bankrupt in [[fortune|Fortune]] and Reputation--that He it is for whom I am thus

[001-080]anxious and malicious and to gain whom I would sacrifice--everything----

[001-081]VERJUICE. Now indeed--your conduct appears consistent and I no longer

[001-082]wonder at your enmity to Maria, but how came you and Surface so

[001-083]confidential?

[001-084]LADY SNEERWELL. For our mutual interest--but I have found out him a long

[001-085]time since[,] altho' He has contrived to deceive everybody beside--I

[001-086]know him to be artful selfish and malicious--while with Sir Peter, and

[001-087]indeed with all his acquaintance, He passes for a youthful Miracle of

[001-088][[prudence|Prudence]]--good sense and [[benevolence|Benevolence]].

[001-089]VERJUICE. Yes yes--I know Sir Peter vows He has not his equal in

[001-090]England; and, above all, He praises him as a MAN OF SENTIMENT.

[001-091]LADY SNEERWELL. True and with the assistance of his sentiments and

[001-092][[hypocrisy-and-deception|hypocrisy]] he has brought Sir Peter entirely in his interests with

[001-093]respect to Maria and is now I believe attempting to flatter Lady Teazle

[001-094]into the same good opinion towards him--while poor Charles has no Friend

[001-095]in the House--though I fear he has a powerful one in Maria's Heart,

[001-096]against whom we must direct our schemes.

[001-097]SERVANT. Mr. Surface.

[001-098]LADY SNEERWELL. Shew him up. He generally calls about this Time. I don't

[001-099]wonder at People's giving him to me for a [[lover|Lover]].

[001-102]obedient.

[001-103]LADY SNEERWELL. Miss Verjuice has just been arraigning me on our mutual

[001-104]attachment now; but I have informed her of our real views and the

[001-105]Purposes for which our Geniuses at present co-operate. You know

[001-107]ill-placed.

[001-109]Verjuice's [[sentiment|sensibility]] and discernment----

[001-111]your mistress or what is more material to me your Brother.

[001-112]SURFACE. I have not seen either since I saw you--but I can inform you

[001-113]that they are at present at Variance--some of your stories have taken

[001-114]good effect on Maria.

[001-115]LADY SNEERWELL. Ah! my dear Verjuice the merit of this belongs to you.

[001-116]But do your Brother's Distresses encrease?

[001-117]SURFACE. Every hour. I am told He had another execution in his house

[001-118]yesterday--in short his Dissipation and extravagance exceed anything I

[001-119]have ever heard of.

[001-120]LADY SNEERWELL. Poor Charles!

[001-121]SURFACE. True Madam--notwithstanding his Vices one can't help [[sentiment|feeling]]

[001-124]the Distresses of a Brother--even though merited by his own

[001-125]misconduct--deserves----

[001-126]LADY SNEERWELL. O Lud you are going to be moral, and forget that you are

[001-127]among Friends.

[001-128]SURFACE. Egad, that's true--I'll keep that sentiment till I see Sir

[001-129]Peter. However it is certainly a [[benevolence|charity]] to rescue Maria from such a

[001-130]Libertine who--if He is to be reclaim'd, can be so only by a Person of

[001-131]your Ladyship's superior accomplishments and understanding.

[001-132]VERJUICE. 'Twould be a Hazardous experiment.

[001-133]SURFACE. But--Madam--let me caution you to place no more confidence in

[001-134]our Friend [[snake|Snake]] the Libeller--I have lately detected him in frequent

[001-135]conference with old Rowland [[rowley|Rowley]] who was formerly my Father's

[001-136]Steward and has never been a friend of mine.

[001-137]LADY SNEERWELL. I'm not disappointed in Snake, I never suspected the

[001-138]fellow to have virtue enough to be faithful even to his own Villany.

[001-166]SURFACE. Certainly madam--that conversation where the Spirit of Raillery

[001-172]LADY SNEERWELL. I wish my Cousin Verjuice hadn't left us--she should

[001-174]SURFACE. Ah! she's an old maid and is privileged of course.
