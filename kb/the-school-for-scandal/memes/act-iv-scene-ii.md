# Act IV, Scene II — Sir Oliver Reports

**Setting**: The Parlour — Sir Oliver Surface and Moses  
**Characters present**: [[sir-oliver-surface]], [[moses]]  
**Dramatic function**: revelation

## Summary

Sir Oliver and Moses discuss what they have witnessed. Sir Oliver is now entirely convinced of Charles's essential goodness — the auction proved what Rowley had claimed: Charles's extravagance is a fault of youth, not of heart. Moses counsels patience and advises Sir Oliver to now test Joseph. The scene is a brief but crucial pivot: having exonerated the 'bad' brother, Sir Oliver now turns his attention to the 'good' one, whose mask has yet to be stripped away.

## Key Moments

- **Moses advises Sir Oliver to test Joseph as well** (004-180)
