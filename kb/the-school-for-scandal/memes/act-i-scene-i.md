# Act I, Scene I — Lady Sneerwell's Dressing Room

**Setting**: Lady Sneerwell's House — her dressing room  
**Characters present**: lady-sneerwell, snake, miss-verjuice, maria, mrs-candour, crabtree, sir-benjamin-backbite, joseph-surface  
**Dramatic function**: exposition

## Summary

The play opens in Lady Sneerwell's dressing room, immediately establishing the machinery of scandal. Lady Sneerwell and Snake discuss the paragraphs they have planted in newspapers to destroy reputations. Maria enters, and Lady Sneerwell attempts to persuade her to accept Joseph Surface's courtship, but Maria reveals her love for Charles Surface. Mrs. Candour, Crabtree, and Sir Benjamin Backbite arrive, engaging in competitive gossip about Lady Brittle, Miss Piper, and Colonel Gary. Joseph Surface arrives and privately confirms to Lady Sneerwell their conspiracy: he courts Maria for her fortune while maintaining an affair with Lady Sneerwell. Snake departs to place more scandal paragraphs, and the scene ends with Lady Sneerwell securing Joseph's return that evening.
