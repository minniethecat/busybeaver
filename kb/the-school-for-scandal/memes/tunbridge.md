# Tunbridge Wells

Tunbridge Wells, like Bath, was a fashionable eighteenth-century spa town that features in The School for Scandal's geography of gossip.

## The Spa Circuit

Tunbridge Wells, in Kent, was one of the principal gathering places for fashionable society — a smaller, more select version of Bath. Its season ran through the summer months, and its promenades, wells, and assembly rooms were the setting for the same rituals of courtship, display, and gossip that characterised London life.

## The Miss Shepherd Story

The play mentions Tunbridge Wells in connection with the story of Miss Shepherd, 'who lost her Lover and her Character one Summer at Tunbridge' — a scandalous anecdote that illustrates the speed with which reputation could be destroyed in the hothouse atmosphere of a spa town. The story, told by Sir Peter, demonstrates that the play's scandal machinery operates wherever fashionable people congregate, not merely in London.

## The Provincial Stage

Tunbridge Wells serves, in the play's moral geography, as a provincial stage for the same dramas that play out in London. It is a reminder that the culture of scandal is not metropolitan in origin but portable — a set of practices that fashionable society carries with it wherever it goes.
