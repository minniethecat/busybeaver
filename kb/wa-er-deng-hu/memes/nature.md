# Nature

Nature is the foundational concept in Henry David Thoreau's *Walden*, serving as both the physical setting of his experiment and the ultimate source of wisdom and spiritual truth.

## Nature as Teacher

Thoreau treats nature not as something to be conquered but as a guide to living well. The natural world reveals truths that civilization obscures.

> The finest qualities of our nature, like the bloom on fruits, can be preserved only by the most delicate handling. (001-012)

## The Natural vs. The Artificial

Throughout Walden, Thoreau contrasts the authenticity of nature with the artificiality of society. His retreat to Walden Pond represents a deliberate choice to immerse himself in the natural order.

## Human Nature

Thoreau extends the concept of nature to human character:

> But alert and healthy natures remember that the sun rose clear. (001-016)

## Labor and Nature

The relationship between human labor and nature is examined repeatedly:

> ...the coarse labors of life that its finer fruits cannot be plucked by them. (001-012)
