# Fair Haven

An expansion of the Concord River and a wooded hill south of Walden Pond, frequented by Thoreau for fishing, berry-picking, and evening walks.

## Location and Geography

Fair Haven is described by Thoreau as "an expansion of Concord River, said to contain some seventy acres," located about a mile southwest of the village. Fair Haven Hill rises near the water, offering views of the surrounding landscape. Together with Walden, Flint's, White Pond, and Goose Pond, Fair Haven forms Thoreau's self-described "lake country."

> [009-041] Goose Pond, of small extent, is on my way to Flint's; Fair-Haven, an expansion of Concord River, said to contain some seventy acres, is a mile south-west; and White Pond, of about forty acres, is a mile and a half beyond Fair-Haven. This is my lake country.

## In Walden

Fair Haven appears throughout Walden as Thoreau's destination for solitary excursions. He picks huckleberries on Fair Haven Hill, fishes in its waters, listens for owls and geese crossing toward it on winter nights, and hears the distant cry of hounds pursuing fox toward its ledges. Fair Haven represents the wider landscape of Thoreau's rambles beyond Walden Pond itself.

> [009-001] Sometimes, having had a surfeit of human society and gossip, and worn out all my village friends, I rambled still farther westward than I habitually dwell, into yet more unfrequented parts of the town, "to fresh woods and pastures new," or, while the sun was setting, made my supper of huckleberries and blueberries on Fair Haven Hill.

## Wildlife and Hunting

Fair Haven is associated with Thoreau's accounts of hunting in the Walden woods. In the Winter Animals chapter, Thoreau recounts the story of Sam Nutting, who "used to hunt bears on Fair Haven Ledges," and the hunter's tale of the fox chased by hounds from Wayland road to Fair Haven and finally shot. Geese pass over Thoreau's house toward Fair Haven on winter nights.

> [015-002] One night in the beginning of winter, before the pond froze over... I was startled by the loud honking of a goose, and, stepping to the door, heard the sound of their wings like a tempest in the woods as they flew low over my house. They passed over the pond toward Fair Haven.
