# Wisdom

Wisdom in *Walden* is practical understanding achieved through deliberate living. Thoreau distinguishes between the recorded wisdom of books and the lived wisdom of experience.

## Definition

Thoreau defines wisdom as a characteristic of deliberate, unhurried thought:

> It is a characteristic of wisdom not to do desperate things. (001-015)

True wisdom demands action, not mere contemplation:

> ...so to love wisdom as to live according to its dictates... (001-025)

## In Walden

Thoreau celebrates the wisdom found in great books — particularly classical texts — while insisting that wisdom must be personally verified through experience:

> ...have been convicted by the wisdom of one good book, the Bible, and then dismissed it. (003-008)

He distinguishes between the recorded wisdom of antiquity and the living wisdom of the present:

> ...as for the recorded wisdom of mankind, the ancient classics... (003-009)

## Thoreau's Perspective

Wisdom requires both study and solitude. Thoreau's experiment at Walden Pond was itself an attempt to live wisely — to strip away distractions and focus on what is essential.
