# Philosophy

In *Walden*, Thoreau redefines philosophy from an academic discipline to a way of living. True philosophy is not abstract theory but the daily practice of wisdom.

## Definition

Philosophy for Thoreau means living according to wisdom, not merely studying it:

> There are nowadays professors of philosophy, but not philosophers. Yet it is admirable to profess because it was once admirable to live. (001-025)

## In Walden

Thoreau repeatedly contrasts genuine philosophy with conventional learning. He argues that the true philosopher lives simply and deliberately:

> To be a philosopher is not merely to have subtle thoughts... but so to love wisdom as to live according to its dictates, a life of simplicity, independence, magnanimity, and trust. (001-025)

Philosophy is intertwined with poetry and religion as one of the great human pursuits:

> ...through poetry and philosophy and religion, till we come to a hard bottom... (002-029)

## Thoreau's Perspective

Thoreau elevates lived philosophy above book learning. Even the most learned scholar cannot replace direct experience:

> ...what is a course of history, or philosophy, or poetry, no matter how well selected... (004-001)
