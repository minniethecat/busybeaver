# Solitude (Concept)

Solitude is a defining philosophical theme of *Walden*, treated by Thoreau not as deprivation but as a rich, companionable state that enables deeper connection with nature and the self.

## The Companionable Nature of Solitude

Thoreau finds solitude to be a positive, even social condition:

> I have never found the companion that was so companionable as solitude. (005-015)

## Solitude in Nature

The natural world provides the setting for Thoreau's experience:

> ...in undisturbed solitude and stillness, while the birds sang around... (004-002)

## A Chair for Solitude

Thoreau's cabin furnishings reflect the importance he places on solitude:

> I had three chairs in my house; one for solitude, two for friendship, three for society. (006-002)

## Beyond Loneliness

Thoreau distinguishes solitude from the oppressive sense of being alone:

> I have never felt lonesome, or in the least oppressed by a sense of solitude, but once... (005-004)
