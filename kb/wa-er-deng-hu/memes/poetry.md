# Poetry

Poetry holds a special place in *Walden* as the highest form of human expression. Thoreau treats poetry not merely as literary art but as a mode of perception and a model for authentic living.

## Definition

For Thoreau, poetry is closely allied with philosophy and religion as one of humanity's deepest pursuits:

> ...through poetry and philosophy and religion, till we come to a hard bottom and rocks in place, which we can call reality... (002-029)

## In Walden

Poetry represents an ideal state of perception where truth is made beautiful:

> If men would steadily observe realities only... music and poetry would resound along the streets. (002-028)

Thoreau contrasts the vitality of ancient poetry with the shallow reading habits of his contemporaries:

> ...the difficulties of the wit and poetry of a Greek poet... (003-009)

He acknowledges the enduring power of mythology and poetry to illuminate human experience:

> Ancient poetry and mythology suggest, at least, that husbandry was once a sacred art... (007-039)

## Thoreau's Perspective

Thoreau reads the classics in their original languages — Greek and Latin — treating poetry as worthy of sustained, serious attention. Poetry provides a standard against which he measures the depth of living.
