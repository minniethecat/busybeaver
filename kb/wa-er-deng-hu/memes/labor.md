# Labor

Labor is a central economic and philosophical theme in *Walden*. Thoreau distinguishes between necessary, meaningful work and the mindless toil that consumes most lives.

## The Tyranny of Work

Thoreau opens Walden by observing how labor consumes and degrades human life:

> But men labor under a mistake. The better part of the man is soon plowed into the soil for compost. (001-005)

## Living by One's Own Hands

Thoreau's experiment at Walden Pond was itself a demonstration of meaningful labor:

> I earned my living by the labor of my hands only. (001-001)

## The Labors of Hercules

Thoreau uses classical allusion to contrast heroic labor with modern drudgery:

> The twelve labors of Hercules were trifling in comparison with those which my neighbors have undertaken. (001-003)

## Coarse Labor vs. Fine Fruits

Excessive labor prevents the cultivation of life's finer dimensions:

> ...the coarse labors of life that its finer fruits cannot be plucked by them. (001-012)
