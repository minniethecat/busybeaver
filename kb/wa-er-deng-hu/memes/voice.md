# Voice

## Definition

In Walden, voice names the authentic sound of a being — whether human, animal, or elemental. Thoreau contrasts the true voice (what one genuinely is and says) with the false voices of society: gossip, convention, the trivial sounds that fill the air.

"I hear an irresistible voice which invites me away from all that" (001-020). The voice that calls Thoreau to Walden is the voice of his own deepest self, which is also the voice of nature calling him home.

## In Walden

The soundscape of Walden is a chorus of voices, and Thoreau listens to each with a naturalist's attention. The wind "gives a voice to the air" as it passes through the woods behind his house (004-005). The fishhawk dimples the pond and gives a voice to the sky.

The hounds barking in the distance produce "the most harsh and tremendous voice I ever heard from any inhabitant of the woods" (015-002) — this voice is felt as much as heard. Each creature contributes its note to Walden's polyphony.

Thoreau pays attention to the voices that urban life drowns out. The church bell, "partly the voice of the wood" (004-029), reaches him not as a summons to worship but as a note in the forest's own music. Distance clarifies what proximity obscures.

He is also fascinated by the voice that overpowers. The bullfrogs' "voices waxed hoarse and solemn" from age (004-035), like ancient revelers at a feast. The loon's "demoniac laughter" (012-017) is a voice from another order of being — wild and unintelligible.

## Thoreau's Perspective

For Thoreau, the voice is the most reliable index of authenticity. "If we speak reservedly and thoughtfully, we cannot possibly hear each other's voice in any case" (006-003). True speech is so rare that even when it occurs, we may not recognize it.

Thoreau extends this ethic to his own writing. Walden itself is his voice — measured, deliberate, truthful. The "I" of Walden is not a literary persona but the sound of a man speaking honestly about his life (001-001).

The voice that calls each person to "live deliberately" is the "different drummer" to whose beat each must march. To hear this voice requires silence, solitude, and the courage to trust what one hears above what the crowd shouts.
