# Truth

Truth is a foundational virtue in Thoreau's philosophical framework. In *Walden*, truth is inseparable from simplicity and sincerity, forming part of the moral foundation of a well-lived life.

## Truth and Immortality

Thoreau elevates truth to a transcendent principle:

> ...but in dealing with truth we are immortal, and need fear no change nor accident. (003-001)

## Truth vs. Convention

Thoreau critiques the gap between conventional wisdom and genuine truth:

> ...opinions are esteemed for soundest truths, while reality is fabulous. (002-028)

## Truth and Simplicity

Truth is closely linked with simplicity as a cardinal virtue:

> He told me, with the utmost simplicity and truth, quite superior... (006-024)

## Confessing the Truth

Thoreau values honest confession over comfortable illusion:

> ...if we will confess the truth, much more to see it burn... (002-025)
