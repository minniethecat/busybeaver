## Threshold and Transition

Staircases in *Interim* mark the boundary between downstairs sociability and upstairs privacy. Miriam's movements on stairs trace her oscillation between these two registers of experience. The "basement stairs" from which chanting comes [002-004] connect the world of domestic labour to the parlour above.

> …chanting came up the basement stairs. When their work was done… [002-004]

## Ascent and Retreat

Going upstairs signals withdrawal from the social world. "They all went busily upstairs" [001-014] marks a collective retreat, while Miriam's solitary run upstairs — "gathered up her blouse and ran upstairs" [001-015] — enacts escape. The staircase is where characters shed their public selves.

> They all went busily upstairs. Even Grace did not linger… [001-014]

The stair is also a space of sensory intensity: light "quickening on walls and stairways" [001-017] transforms the transitional space into a site of almost mystical perception.

## Sound and Social Signaling

The staircase is also an acoustic register of the household's social life. The "shambling" footsteps of an unknown man coming up the stairs [001-010] signal intrusion and threat, a sound that halts conversation as Miriam and her listeners strain to identify the source. Conversely, Miriam's own movement on the stairs is marked by careful silence — she "crept downstairs through the silent empty house" [002-008], pausing to listen to "faint faraway subterranean sounds" drifting up from the kitchen. The way characters move on the stairs — heavily or softly, quickly or slowly — broadcasts their relationship to the household and their intention towards others.

> …the steps came shambling up their stairs—and they heard the man collapse with a groan against their door. [001-010]

The staircase also exerts a psychological pull. Miriam, returning home in the evening, finds the "dim staircase" that "beckoned her up into darkness" [003-002] — an ambiguous invitation that is part threat, part promise of solitude. The remark "its parky upstairs" [003-009] reduces the upper floor to a cold, unwelcoming space, spoken aloud as a social signal that registers discomfort while performing stoicism. In these moments, the stair is not merely a passage but a resonant chamber where the house's emotional temperature is taken.

> Beyond the gaslight pouring down on to the smeary marble of the hall table … the dim staircase beckoned her up into darkness. [003-002]
