## Nature and Landscape

Green in the novel is first and foremost the colour of living growth. The "flaring green" that hems the road [008-008] and the "feathery green floored woods of Endsleigh Gardens" [008-009] register the encroachment of the natural world into the urban setting.

> …the green flush in the squares, the rippling green of the garden trees… [008-012]

The green of London squares repeatedly draws Miriam's attention: "a London square turning to green below in the lamplight" [008-019] — a moment where the colour becomes almost luminous, transforming the city into a pastoral vision.

The "green squares at either end" of a calm balconied street [002-006] establish green as a structuring element of the urban landscape, punctuating the built environment with pockets of organic life. The pastoral impulse extends even to remembered landscape: "deciding away down in green Wiltshire" [004-004] uses green as metonymy for rural escape, contrasting with the London setting.

## Domestic and Material Green

Green registers in domestic interiors as well as landscapes. The "strip of green carpet between the polished boards" [001-015] and the "squat green teapot on the table" [009-003] embed the colour in the material texture of everyday life. These small domestic details accumulate across the novel, part of Richardson's method of rendering consciousness through the sensory particularity of objects.

The "brown and green landscape" of a picture that catches Miriam's eye [001-013] shows green functioning in visual art and decoration as well as in direct perception. A dress that is "large straight green" [001-001] introduces the colour through clothing — green as a quality of fabric and self-presentation.

## Green and Light

Richardson frequently pairs green with effects of light, particularly lamplight and the diffuse glow of London evenings. The "brilliant lamplit green" of trees [008-022] and the "green shadows about the small pale face" [008-024] demonstrate how green functions not as a static property of objects but as a dynamic quality modulated by illumination.

The "green feathering up into the intens…" vista [008-014] and the "lamplit green trees" with "plane-tree shadows" [008-021] show green and light in continuous interplay. "Green squares and high houses moving" through the mind [008-247] internalises the colour — green becoming not just a property of the seen world but a residue in consciousness.

## Green and Identity

The colour carries a distinctive emotional charge for Miriam, associated with home and national identity. "Green. It was green all through … Ireland; home-rule" [009-100] — this passage links green to political identity and the idea of belonging, suggesting the colour operates at the level of collective as well as individual consciousness.

At a more intimate level, "green eyes" [007-005] register the colour as a quality of the human face — green moving from landscape to the body, from the impersonal to the personal.
