## Overview

The hall in *Interim* functions as more than an architectural passageway. It is a liminal space where characters transition between outside and inside, between social performance and private self. Letters arrive on the hall table, gaslights are lit or extinguished, and the hall becomes a stage for encounters that would not happen elsewhere in the boarding house.

## Manifestations

The hall appears throughout the novel as a site of arrival and observation. Miriam passes through it "with a preoccupied manner, ignoring the letters on the hall table" [002-006], using the space to signal her detachment from household rhythms. The hall table itself is a recurring object — "the smeary marble of the hall table" [003-002] catches light, while letters from Eve rest on it [004-003], making the hall a node in the novel's communication network.

Evening transforms the hall's atmosphere. "The hall gas was out" [004-013] marks a moment of domestic quiet, while characters appear in the hall as spectral presences: Eve "stepped back into the hall and stood serenely glowing" [006-017], and Mr. Mendizabal materialises "in a funny little coat" [006-019]. The hall is where the household's suppressed life surfaces — laughter heard "in the hall, of course" suggests a space where social masks slip [006-018].

Miriam's own relationship to the hall is one of deliberate performance: she "let herself into the hall with an air of returning from an important engagement" [008-023], using the threshold as a stage for self-presentation. The hall also connects to practical domestic life — "getting one of the hall chairs" [001-014] — grounding its symbolic resonance in material routine.

## Key Passages

- **[002-006]**: "coming briskly through the hall with a preoccupied manner, ignoring the letters on the hall table" — the hall as a space of performed indifference.
- **[004-013]**: "The hall gas was out. The dining-room..." — a threshold moment, the extinguished gas signalling a shift in domestic time.
- **[006-017]**: "Eve stepped back into the hall and stood serenely glowing in the light of the dining-room gas" — the hall as a frame for illuminated presence.
- **[008-023]**: "She let herself into the hall with an air of returning from an important engagement" — the threshold as stage for social performance.
