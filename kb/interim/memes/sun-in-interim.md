## Sunlight as Revelation

Sunlight in *Interim* punctuates the narrative with moments of heightened clarity. The "bright sunshine" [003-009] transforms ordinary scenes, while "sunlight through the endless process" [004-014] registers as a radiance that aestheticises experience. These moments of illumination offer Miriam brief escape from the weight of self-consciousness.

> It sent a radiance like sunlight through the endless process… [004-014]

## Sunlit Spaces

The play of sunlight across domestic interiors creates spaces of temporary beauty. The "sunlit white façade of a large London house" [001-016] stands in contrast to darker interiors, and "caverns of the fire life moved sunlit" [001-017] suggests warmth and animation in the domestic sphere.

> …caverns of the fire life moved sunlit. She dropped her eyes… [001-017]

The recurring image of being "bathed in sunshine" [005-003] captures moments of physical contentment that anchor Miriam's wandering consciousness in bodily sensation.

## Sunlight and Temporality

Sunlight also functions as a temporal marker in the novel, tracing the passage of hours and seasons. The quality of light at different times of day registers shifts in mood: morning "sunlight pouring in on the thin" curtains [007-001] signals new beginnings, while the image of a "happy sunsetting" [006-019] closes a day with a sense of peaceful resolution. The phrase "unsunned by the sunlight" [001-015] captures a paradoxical state — being in the presence of light yet untouched by its warmth, a condition that mirrors Miriam's frequent sense of detachment from the scenes she observes. Through these variations, sunlight becomes an index of both external time and internal emotional weather.

> …glinted back at her unsunned by the sunlight, rootless… [001-015]

> …bathed in sunshine. The room was full of light… [005-003]
