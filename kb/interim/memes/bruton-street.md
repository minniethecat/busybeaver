# Bruton Street

Bruton Street, in London's Mayfair district, appears in Dorothy Richardson's *Interim* as the address of a flower shop — the place where Eve Henderson, Miriam's sister, secures employment for a six-month course in floral decorations. It is mentioned only once, but the single reference carries the weight of London's fashionable commercial geography.

## In the Novel

Eve's decision to leave her teaching position in Wiltshire and come to London arrives in a letter that transforms Miriam's sense of the city:

> Too excited to read word by word she scanned the pages and learned that Madame Leroy a friend of Mrs. Green who had a flower shop in Bruton Street had engaged her. … I decorated the table for dinner each night when she was here at Christmas … the Greens have been charming, quite excited about the plans … coming up next week. … Miriam leapt to her feet and began hastily putting on her things. "Eve is coming to London for a six months' course in floral decorations." [004-004]

The name "Bruton Street" anchors the news in a specific London geography. Mayfair — the district of Bond Street, Berkeley Square, and Bruton Street — is the city's fashionable centre, associated with wealth, elegance, and commercial luxury. That Eve, penniless and in uncertain health, should find a place there through Madame Leroy's flower shop makes the opportunity both improbable and thrilling.

## Significance

Bruton Street in *Interim* functions less as a place Miriam inhabits than as a coordinate in London's social map — a marker of the world Eve is entering, distinct from the boarding-house geography of Banbury Park and Tansley Street. The flower shop on Bruton Street represents the commercial, fashionable London that exists alongside — but largely separate from — Miriam's own experience of the city.
