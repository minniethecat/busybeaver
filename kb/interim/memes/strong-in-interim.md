## Overview

Strength in *Interim* is a quality Miriam observes more than possesses. It resides in others — in the "strong closed lips" of a commanding face [003-002], the "strong small toil-disfigured hand" of Mrs. Bailey [006-001], the "strong resourceful world" from which Miriam feels hidden [006-026]. The novel maps strength across bodies, voices, and social positions, tracing what Miriam lacks and what she admires, fears, or resists.

## Manifestations

Physical strength is rendered through precise bodily description. A figure is "square short strong well-knit" [003-010]; a voice is "suave strong courteous" [004-013]; teeth form a "flawless perfect arch of his strong even" [008-027]. These details accumulate into a taxonomy of masculine and working-class strength — the bodies that move confidently through a world that makes Miriam feel insubstantial.

The novel also registers strength as an inner state. Miriam feels "strong and sleepy" after coffee [001-010], "rested and strong, undisturbed by changes" [002-002] — these are rare moments of self-possession. The feeling of being "careless and strong" [008-247] is a fleeting liberation from the chronic self-consciousness that otherwise defines her interior life.

Strength and weakness are structurally opposed. The "strong resourceful world" [006-026] is the world Miriam cannot access; her "helplessness and resourcelessness" are defined against it. Mrs. Bailey's "strong small toil-disfigured hand" [006-001] embodies a strength earned through labour, foreign to Miriam's genteel paralysis. The "strong spirit; impossible of approach" [008-025] suggests that strength can also mean inaccessibility — a carapace that keeps others at distance.

## Key Passages

- **001-010** — "beginning to feel strong and sleepy": strength as temporary bodily well-being.
- **003-002** — "moulded the strong closed lips": facial strength as character index.
- **005-003** — "resolute and strong": moral strength in a face's details.
- **006-001** — "Mrs. Bailey's strong small toil-disfigured hand": strength earned through labour.
- **006-026** — "helplessness and resourcelessness in a strong resourceful world": the structural opposition that defines Miriam's position.
