## Overview

Freedom in *Interim* operates at multiple registers: the literal freedom of movement through London streets, the social freedom aspired to by characters in their gatherings, and a deeper, more elusive inner freedom that Miriam experiences in solitude or through music. It is not a fixed state but something achieved in moments — a release from constraint, an opening into possibility.

## Manifestations

Freedom appears first as physical movement. Miriam walks London streets with the "longing for solitude and to be free to wander slowly along the near north-western elm-shaded roads" [005-005], and a house standing "outside London, in blank space, free, and exactly alike in their trim brick solidity" [005-002] embodies a kind of spatial liberty. The city becomes a territory of release.

Social freedom is a recurring preoccupation. At gatherings, Miriam wonders about those "both people who must be free to be nothing; saying to everyone that they were nothing" [006-025] — freedom as the absence of social obligation, the right to drift without definition. Mr. Parrow's "swift sunlit decision and freedom of his innocent reception of everything" [006-018] presents freedom as a quality of personality, a lightness of being that others admire.

Inner freedom is tied to music and solitude. Miriam reflects on "the freedom of following certainties" [006-025], and when playing piano experiences the "freedom and forgetfulness of the everlasting present" [006-028]. The garret scenes offer freedom as escape: "She shut the door and stepped into freedom. The cistern gurgled from the dark corner" [009-101] — freedom as threshold, a passage from the downstairs world of obligation into the private space above.

Wealth, too, is coded as freedom: "bringing a sense of the freedom of wealth to have things out without thinking" [004-007]. Yet the novel consistently values the freedoms that cost nothing — walking, music, solitude — over those that money buys.

## Key Passages

- **[005-005]**: "longing for solitude and to be free to wander slowly" — freedom as urban movement, the walker's autonomy.
- **[006-025]**: "both people who must be free to be nothing" — freedom as release from social definition, the right to anonymity.
- **[006-028]**: "sharing the freedom and forgetfulness of the everlasting present" — music as vehicle of inner freedom.
- **[009-101]**: "She shut the door and stepped into freedom" — the garret as sanctuary, freedom as spatial boundary.
- **[004-007]**: "a sense of the freedom of wealth" — the economic dimension, and the novel's quiet critique of it.
