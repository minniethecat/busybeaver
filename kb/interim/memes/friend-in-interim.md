## Overview

Friendship in *Interim* represents a sphere of social life that Miriam Henderson approaches with a mixture of curiosity and detachment. As a young woman working in London, her friendships are shaped by the tensions between intimacy and distance, English conventions and European sensibilities.

## Manifestations

The word "friend" recurs throughout the novel as Miriam negotiates her social world. She reflects on the "few friends gathered together" in a room [004-008] and experiences the peculiar sensation of looking "into the eyes of a friend" as if gazing across centuries [004-010]. Her circle includes Madame Leroy, described as "a friend of Mrs. Green who had a flower shop in Bruton Street" [004-004].

In social gatherings, friends become objects of anxious observation. Miriam notes "her competing friends, the friends of all nice young women" [006-018], capturing the competitive undercurrent of social life. The novel also dramatises the claims friends make on one another: "She has some very nice friends and some extraordinary ones" [008-236].

## Bohemian Friends

The novel registers a distinct category of friendship through Miriam's association with artistic and unconventional circles. The remark about "having no one but Bohemians for friends" [005-003] captures a social identity defined by aesthetic allegiance rather than class convention. Bohemian friendship, in the world of *Interim*, promises freedom from the rigid expectations of middle-class sociability — but also carries the implication of outsider status, a social world at a slight angle to respectability. Miriam's position between these two modes of friendship — the conventional and the bohemian — reflects her broader navigation between inherited social forms and the life she is attempting to make for herself.

> …having no one but Bohemians for friends. There must be a special way…
> (005-003)

## Key Passages

- [008-233] "You have hosts of friends." — A direct statement that opens the question of what friendship means in Miriam's London life.
- [003-009] Miriam "forced her friends to appear" in conversation, showing how friendship is at times a performance.
- [008-004] "She set out from the house of friends to meet the darkened daylight" — friendship as a point of departure.
