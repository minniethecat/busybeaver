## Overview

Tansley Street is the London address at the heart of *Interim*, where Miriam Henderson lives and works in a boarding house. It is not merely a setting but the gravitational centre of the novel's social world — the place characters return to, depart from, and measure other locations against. The street anchors the narrative's dense texture of urban experience.

## Daily Rhythms

Life on Tansley Street is structured by the comings and goings of its residents. Miriam arrives "at Tansley Street as from a great distance" [006-017], the phrase capturing how even a short journey across London can feel like an epic traversal when consciousness registers every impression. Eve's scheduled appearances — "Eve would not appear at Tansley Street until half-past" [006-016] — mark the street as a place governed by temporal expectation, where the household's rhythm is shaped by the predictable movements of its members.

> She arrived at Tansley Street as from a great distance. [006-017]

## The Street as Social Hub

Tansley Street is where the social world converges. The "people at Tansley Street" [004-011] form a distinct community, and the phrase "Tansley Street life" [008-202] becomes shorthand for the entire texture of boarding-house existence — its tensions, its small dramas, its familiar routines. Miriam "flung down Tansley Street telling her news" [008-245] with the energy of someone whose home street is the natural stage for self-expression. Yet this centrality is impermanent: "The Tansley Street life was fading into the" past [009-009], and the street's hold on the narrative loosens as the novel progresses, making it not just a location but a phase of experience.

> …and then on to the people at Tansley Street… [004-011]
