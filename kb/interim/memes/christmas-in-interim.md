## Overview

Christmas serves as the central temporal setting and a major thematic motif in *Interim*. The novel takes place during Miriam Henderson's Christmas visit to the Broom family, and the holiday functions as both a narrative frame and a lens through which questions of tradition, memory, social class, and emotional connection are explored.

## Manifestations

Christmas in the novel is not merely a backdrop but an active presence that shapes characters' behavior and consciousness. The exchange of gifts — most notably the cream blouse Mrs. Philps gives Miriam [001-013] — highlights social ritual and the complexities of gratitude and obligation. The Christmas breakfast, with its "rashers and eggs" and rich coffee [001-013], establishes the holiday's material pleasures.

The holiday also provokes reflection on change and loss. Mrs. Philps recalls Yule logs and snowy northern Christmases of the past [001-013], while Miriam contemplates the "cold and desolate and harsh" experience of those who spend Christmas in trams [001-015]. The Christmas waits playing outside in the night [001-012] create a moment of transcendent connection to the past.

## Key Passages

- [001-012] Miriam wakes in the night to hear Christmas waits playing "a curious sorrowful little air" that connects her to the past and to the sleeping households around her
- [001-013] Christmas Day begins with gift-giving and breakfast; Mrs. Philps reflects that "Christmas is a very different thing to what it was"
- [001-013] Mrs. Philps declares "it's the little simple things that make you happy; the things that happen every day," a Christmas morning philosophy
- [001-015] Miriam observes people "keeping Christmas in their homes, carrying it out into the streets," and reflects on the "cold and desolate" Christmas of those in trams
- [001-015] "It was Christmas Day for them all" — a moment of universalizing reflection
