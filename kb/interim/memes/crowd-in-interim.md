# Crowd in _Interim_

**Crowding** in _Interim_ is experienced at multiple scales — the press of thoughts in the mind, the congestion of London life, the physical squeeze of bodies in domestic spaces. Richardson renders crowding not simply as too-muchness but as a pressure that shapes consciousness, forcing the self to seek the relief of "uncrowded" spaces.

## Thematic Dimensions

### Crowding as Mental Overwhelm

The novel's first register of crowding is internal. "All this coming and crowding of mean little things" [001-013] images the mind as a space invaded by petty concerns — thoughts crowd in, jostling for attention. "Crowding thoughts" [003-014] returns to this sense of mental congestion, the narrator seeking to "forsake" her own crowded interior. The mind is a room that fills too easily.

### Crowding as Social Density

Crowding extends outward into social life. The sense of "a complex London life crowded with engagements" [004-008] makes crowding a feature of urban existence — the calendar itself is crowded, filled with obligations. The phrase captures the specifically modern experience of social saturation, where the density of possible interactions becomes itself a form of pressure.

### Crowding as Physical Congestion

The novel also registers literal, physical crowding. A stool "crowded in between the mantelpiece" [003-002] makes crowding a spatial condition — furniture pressed into inadequate space. The domestic interior replicates the crowding of the city, with objects and bodies competing for room.

### The Relief of the Uncrowded

Against crowding, Richardson sets the relief of space. "There was leisure in cool uncrowded streets" [009-009] offers the counter-image: streets emptied of density, cool where crowding is warm, leisurely where crowding is urgent. The "uncrowded" is not simply the absence of people but a quality of space — cool, open, breathable.

## Key Passages

- **[001-013]** "All this coming and crowding of mean little things" — Mental crowding as the invasion of petty concerns.
- **[004-008]** "A complex London life crowded with engagements" — Social crowding as the texture of urban modernity.
- **[003-002]** "Crowded in between the mantelpiece" — Physical crowding in domestic space.
- **[009-009]** "Leisure in cool uncrowded streets" — The relief of spatial openness.
