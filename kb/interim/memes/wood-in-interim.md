## Overview

Wood is a recurring material presence in *Interim*, appearing primarily in domestic interiors. Unlike the public grey stone of London's streets, wood belongs to the intimate spaces Miriam Henderson inhabits: polished woodwork in bathrooms, carved wooden stools, and the "wooden roof" of window spaces.

## Manifestations

The novel opens with wood in a domestic interior: Miriam observes "the highly polished woodwork above the tiles" [001-001] of the bathroom. A "little carved wooden stool" [001-011] appears in a remembered scene, and later a "wooden stool crowded in between the table and the fireplace" [003-002] anchors a domestic setting.

Wooden surfaces are closely associated with feminine domestic space. Miriam's back is "supported by little wooden pillars" [002-008] in a reading room, and the window area is described with its "wooden roof" [008-021], framing a private nook. The material also appears in sensory description: "a bare wet wooden floor" [006-015] in a flower shop blends texture with scent.

In imagined scenes, wood takes on almost musical qualities: "nothing but music sounding in a wooden room" [005-004] merges material and sound.

## Key Passages

- [001-001] "The highly polished woodwork above the tiles" — wood as the novel's opening domestic detail.
- [002-008] The reading room seat "supported by little wooden pillars" — furniture and feminine leisure.
- [006-015] "A bare wet wooden floor" in the flower shop — wood and sensory memory.
