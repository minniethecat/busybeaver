## Overview

Florrie Broom is one of the Broom sisters in *Interim*. She is characterized by her "grinding" voice, her shy yet direct manner, and her practical competence in household matters. Unlike her sister Grace's tender emotionalism, Florrie expresses affection through teasing, brisk care, and watchful attention.

## Role in the Narrative

Florrie is the first to open the door to Miriam, "smiling brilliantly from under the wide brim of a heavily trimmed hat" [001-001]. She manages household logistics — ringing for the maid Christine, overseeing meals, and maintaining the rhythms of the Broom household.

Florrie's interactions with Miriam are marked by shy warmth and occasional awkwardness. She asks careful questions to keep conversation flowing and is sensitive to social tensions at the table. Her personality balances Grace's emotional intensity and Mrs. Philps's dignified reserve, providing a pragmatic counterpoint within the family dynamic.
