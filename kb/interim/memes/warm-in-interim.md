## Overview

Warmth in *Interim* bridges the physical and the emotional — a room's heat, a cup of coffee's steam, the glow of lamplight — and extends into the affective realm of belonging, comfort, and human kindness. The novel maps temperature as a gauge of inner state: to be warm is to be safe, seen, and momentarily freed from the chill of isolation. Cold, by contrast, is the default condition Miriam seeks to escape.

## Manifestations

Physical warmth is most often anchored in domestic interiors. The Bailey house radiates "life and warmth and golden light" [005-003], while Mrs. Bailey's house is "a secret warmth and brightness" [006-001]. The "warm brownness" of twilight [002-006] softens the harsh edges of the day. These warm spaces are havens — the firelit den, the café ending in "warm brilliance" [006-028], the "warm yellow gaslight" of an evening interior [008-022].

Miriam actively seeks warmth: "I came in here to get warm first" she explains on a cold day [001-014], and she is advised to "go down and get a warm up" [002-007]. Warmth is something one must pursue, a scarce resource in a world that defaults to cold. After walking through "drizzling rain," she returns "warmed and fed and with a glow" [004-012] — warmth as restoration.

The word also carries an emotional register. Eve speaks "warmly" [006-015], and firelight "shedding a warmth in the cold room" [002-003] suggests that warmth can be generated by presence as much as by fuel. The "warm hours in the den" [002-004] are not just physically warm but socially and emotionally sustaining — time spent in company, in the glow of shared space.

## Key Passages

- **001-014** — "I came in here to get warm first": warmth as deliberate refuge from cold.
- **002-003** — "shedding a warmth in the cold room": warmth generated by presence and fire.
- **004-012** — "warmed and fed and with a glow at": the triad of physical restoration.
- **005-003** — "The room was full of life and warmth and golden light": warmth equated with vitality.
- **006-028** — "every day ending in warm brilliance, forgetfulness": the café as warm escape.
