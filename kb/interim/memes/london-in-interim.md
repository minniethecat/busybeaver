## Overview

London in *Interim* is more than backdrop; it is a living presence that shapes Miriam Henderson's consciousness at every turn. The city provides the physical and social architecture of her life — from the boarding-house on Tansley Street to the dental practice where she works, from the streets she walks alone to the drawing-rooms where evening gatherings unfold.

## The City as Atmosphere

London's atmospheric presence permeates the novel through its weather, its light, and its sounds. The city's physical grandeur registers through Miriam's observant gaze.

> From the sunlit white façade of a large London house the laurels looked down through a white stone-pillared balustrade. (001-016)

The "unbroken stillness" that filters out from quiet rooms "into the quiet street out and away into London" registers the city as both immediate and expansive, a container for solitude.

> When the room was still there was an unbroken stillness in the house and the street. (002-009)

The north London shops, the large London houses, and the omnibuses moving through darkened streets create a textured urban landscape that Miriam observes with a painterly attention to detail and light.

## Navigating Urban Space

Miriam's movement through London is central to the novel's structure. She walks through streets, rides omnibuses, and navigates the city's geography as a form of independent consciousness. The dental practice at Wimpole Street anchors her working life, while the boarding-house on Tansley Street provides a precarious domestic center.

> There seemed to be nothing more; she had gathered the gist of it all in glancing through it at Wimpole Street. (002-006)

Between these points, the streets of London become a space for thinking, for being alone among crowds.

> That was why it was a relief to be in London; surrounded by people who did not know what one was really like. (002-006)

This urban anonymity — the "relief" of being unknown — is something the modern city uniquely affords, and Richardson captures its paradox: the comfort of invisibility amid the press of strangers.

## London and Modernity

The London of *Interim* embodies the tensions of modernity: the collision of old and new, the mixing of social classes, the presence of foreigners and cosmopolitans who challenge provincial assumptions. The names of unfamiliar parts of London on omnibus destination boards — "people standing about on the terminal islands" — suggest the city's vastness and the multiplicity of lives unfolding beyond Miriam's immediate horizon. The city is both liberating and alienating, offering freedom from provincial constraints while imposing its own forms of isolation.

The novel registers London not as a static backdrop but as a dynamic field of perception, where every street corner, every façade, every interior, offers a new arrangement of light and shadow for consciousness to explore.

## Key Passages

- The "sunlit white façade of a large London house" with laurels looking down "through a white stone-pillared" entrance captures the city's physical grandeur as seen through Miriam's observant gaze (001-016).
- Miriam's reflection that "it was a relief to be in London; surrounded by people who did not know what one was really like" articulates the urban anonymity that the novel repeatedly explores (002-006).
- The "quiet street out and away into London" — when the room was still, "there was an unbroken stillness" — renders the city as both intimate silence and infinite extension (002-009).
