## Overview

Mrs. Philps is the widowed mother of Grace and Florrie Broom, and the head of the household where Miriam Henderson stays for Christmas in *Interim*. She is characterized by her small, "shrivelled" hands, her dignified manner, her occasional acidity, and her underlying warmth. She represents an older generation with different sensibilities and memories.

## Role in the Narrative

Mrs. Philps presides over the Broom household with a mixture of maternal authority and wistful nostalgia. She gave Miriam a cream nun's veiling blouse as a Christmas gift, chosen at a "big north London shop" [001-013]. Throughout the visit, she offers food and drink, makes sardonic observations, and occasionally retreats into memories of the past — Yule logs, snowy Christmases, and a different era.

Her relationship with Miriam is affectionate but marked by generational and temperamental distance. She finds Miriam's restless energy amusing ("Giddy girl" [001-011]) but cannot follow her flights of thought. Her presence anchors the household in routine and tradition.
