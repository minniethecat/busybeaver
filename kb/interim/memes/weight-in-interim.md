# Weight in _Interim_

**Weight** in _Interim_ operates across physical, emotional, and atmospheric registers. It can press down from above, blossom from within, or lift entirely — shifting with the narrator's state of being. Richardson uses weight to render the felt quality of experience: the heaviness of the world and the rare lightness of release.

## Thematic Dimensions

### Weight as Atmosphere

Weight is first encountered as something in the air. "The weight in the air was dreadful" [001-013] registers atmospheric pressure as emotional — the weight is not a physical object but a quality of the space itself, felt bodily. Later, "even from above a weight seemed to press" [006-026] makes the direction of pressure explicit: weight descends, bears down, oppresses from overhead.

### Weight as Bodily Experience

The body is the primary site where weight is felt. "Heavy blossoming weight, weighed down with her heavy" [008-147] doubles the register — weight blossoms (organic, growing) and weighs down (gravitational, oppressive). The repetition of "heavy" amplifies the sensation. By contrast, the narrator can also experience weightlessness: "as lightly as if there were no weight in her body" [011-002] captures a moment of physical liberation, the body freed from its own mass.

### Weight and Emotion

Richardson makes weight indistinguishable from emotional state. The "weight in the air" is dreadful because it saturates experience with heaviness. Weight is never neutral — it is always weighted toward the oppressive or the liberating. The body that moves "as lightly as if there were no weight" is also a self that has temporarily shed its burdens.

## Key Passages

- **[001-013]** "The weight in the air was dreadful" — Atmospheric weight as emotional register.
- **[006-026]** "Even from above a weight seemed to press" — The directionality of weight as pressure from overhead.
- **[008-147]** "Heavy blossoming weight, weighed down with her heavy" — Bodily weight as both organic growth and gravitational burden.
- **[011-002]** "As lightly as if there were no weight in her body" — The rare experience of weightlessness.
