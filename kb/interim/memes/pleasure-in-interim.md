## Overview

Pleasure in *Interim* operates not as grand happiness but as a "hair's breadth" phenomenon — fleeting sensory intensities that flicker through Miriam Henderson's consciousness as she navigates boarding-house evenings and dental-assistant days. Richardson tracks pleasure where it actually occurs: in the taste of coffee, the play of light across a room, the texture of a voice. These moments do not resolve Miriam's circumstances but suspend them, creating intervals of vivid aliveness within an otherwise constrained existence.

## Thematic Dimensions

Miriam's capacity for joy exists in tension with her surroundings. She holds herself still "to listen" when happiness surfaces — "she had awakened because of her happiness... clear gentle and soft" [001-012] — suggesting pleasure arrives as a visitation rather than a pursuit. The Grant family's preoccupation with social form illustrates pleasure's opposite: "It's one of the greatest pleasures of their lives keeping up" [001-013], where enjoyment is hollowed into performance. Yet Miriam resists this flattening; as Mrs. Bailey's manner shifts "to delight" at the return of Sissie and Emile [003-013], Miriam registers the genuine warmth beneath social surfaces. Richardson insists that pleasure is perceptual — something that happens *to* sensation before it becomes thought. The coffee ritual, the sound of music, the London light effects are not mere backdrop but the primary site of value, a conviction crystallized in the claim that Miriam "enjoys life tremendously" [001-013] even as the narrative offers no conventional reason why she should.

## Key Passages

- **[001-012]** — "She held in her joy to listen. There was no sadness..." Miriam's joy is held, contained, almost protected from narration itself. The sentence enacts the very stillness it describes.

- **[001-012]** — "she had awakened because of her happiness... clear gentle and soft..." Happiness here is not a conclusion but a condition of waking — perceptual clarity as the ground of experience.

- **[001-013]** — "You know you enjoy life tremendously..." Spoken to Miriam by another character, this external recognition confirms what the narrative's inward style already demonstrates: her capacity for pleasure is a defining trait.

- **[001-013]** — "...at this moment to pin down the happiness of everything. She looked up..." The impulse to "pin down" happiness confesses its elusiveness; pleasure must be seized in the instant or lost.

- **[003-013]** — "...with surprise and indignation to delight. Sissie and Emile were back..." The rapid emotional arc from indignation to delight traces pleasure's sudden irruption, its refusal to be earned or scheduled.
