## Overview

Music appears as a significant motif in *Interim*, functioning as a vehicle for transcendent experience and connection across time. The most prominent musical moment is the Christmas waits (carolers) who play outside in the night, creating an experience that pulls Miriam out of ordinary consciousness into a state of heightened awareness and historical connection.

## Manifestations

The central musical episode occurs when Miriam wakes in the night to hear the waits playing [001-012]. The music comes to her as "a little thread of melody sounded from far away in the night straight into her heart." The experience is characterized by its gentle, melancholy quality — "a curious sorrowful little air" — and its power to dissolve the boundaries between self and world, past and present.

The tune is later identified as "The Mistletoe Bough" [001-013], a traditional Christmas carol. Richardson uses the music to explore how aesthetic experience can bridge generational distance: "It came from far back amongst the generations where everything was different; telling you that they were the same."

The way the musicians play — "neither instrument louder than the others" — suggests a democratic, communal mode of expression that Miriam interprets as embodying deep human knowledge: "Something that everybody knows."
