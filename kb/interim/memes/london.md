## Overview

London is the primary setting of *Interim* and the larger *Pilgrimage* series. It is where Miriam Henderson lives in a boarding house, works, and navigates her independent life as a young woman in the early twentieth century. The city appears in the novel both as a physical backdrop and as a symbolic space of modernity, freedom, and social complexity.

## Significance

London represents the world of work, independence, and adult responsibility that Miriam inhabits outside the sheltered Christmas visit to the Brooms. The contrast between London's boarding-house life and the domestic warmth of the suburban Broom household structures much of the novel's emotional geography.

Specific London locations mentioned include Wimpole Street [001-001 reference to the doctor], Tansley Street, the west end, and various shops. The city's sounds — the "shallow suburban slam" of the front door [001-001] — and its rhythms of work and leisure permeate Miriam's consciousness even during her holiday.
