## Overview

Aloneness in *Interim* is neither purely positive nor purely negative. It is a condition that sharpens perception, enables music and reverie, and sometimes becomes an unbearable weight. Miriam's experience of being alone oscillates between liberation and loneliness, and the novel traces this oscillation through domestic scenes, musical moments, and the peculiar solitude of being among others.

## Manifestations

The novel opens with Miriam's solitude as deprivation: she "spent New Year's Eve alone in a cold bedroom" [002-002], a fact that marks her as unmoored from family and conventional festivity. Yet being alone also produces insight — "left alone with silence all along the street" [001-015], Miriam experiences a heightened awareness of her surroundings, and when playing music "alone, without thoughts," something ineffable emerges [002-009].

The boundary between chosen and imposed solitude is blurred. Mrs. Bailey "was sitting alone poised socially in a low armchair" [004-013] — a solitude that is staged, performative. Miriam herself reflects that "there was nothing but to be alone. And yet it needed people in the background" [006-011], articulating the paradox of wanting solitude without isolation.

The novel also presents aloneness as a quality of perception itself: a house "standing alone in its garden" [001-017], an object that "stood out alone and complete against the reflection" [002-008]. In these moments, "alone" describes not a person but a form of visual distinctness — things seen in their separateness, their self-contained wholeness. The darkest iteration appears when Miriam imagines finding herself "alone" in the light and fears the silence of a deserted London "alone and deserted like a country place" [008-022].

## Key Passages

- **[001-015]**: "Left alone with silence all along the street" — solitude as a perceptual state, the street becoming an auditorium for silence.
- **[002-002]**: "She had spent New Year's Eve alone in a cold bedroom" — the emblematic moment of social exclusion, solitude as failure.
- **[002-009]**: "Something in music when one played alone, without thoughts" — solitude enabling a kind of pure, unselfconscious engagement.
- **[006-011]**: "there was nothing but to be alone. And yet it needed people in the background" — the paradox of chosen solitude, wanting distance without disappearance.
