## Overview

The past in *Interim* is not a closed chapter but an active presence. It surfaces through music, through memory, through the sudden recognition of something formerly known. Miriam moves through London with the past as her constant companion — it shapes how she sees, what she hears, and who she understands herself to be.

## Manifestations

Music is the novel's primary technology for accessing the past. At the piano, Miriam experiences music as coming "from far back amongst scenes that were gone" [001-012]; it conjures "the inmost mind of the unchanging past. Nothing in her life had any meaning apart from it" [002-009]. Music collapses temporal distance, making the past viscerally present.

The past also operates as a narrative filter: "In their midst lived the past and the thing that had ended" [001-016]. Miriam inhabits a present saturated with aftermath. The novel's prose style — associative, recursive — formally enacts this layering of past onto present.

Miriam develops a theory of the past's function: "if you can speak of a thing, it is past... Speaking makes it glow with the things that have been" [001-017]. Articulation transforms experience into memory, and memory into narrative. This is the novel's own method: speech as the mechanism by which the past becomes bearable, even luminous.

Yet the past can also be a weight. "Their shared past flowed in the room" [009-089] — the past as atmosphere, shared history that fills a space between two people. At Tansley Street, waiting "until half-past" [006-016], time becomes a medium of anticipation and constraint. The novel distinguishes between the past that enriches ("summer was happiness" [008-002]) and the past that merely accumulates.

## Key Passages

- **[001-016]**: "In their midst lived the past and the thing that had ended" — past as ongoing presence, aftermath as atmosphere.
- **[001-017]**: "if you can speak of a thing, it is past... Speaking makes it glow" — articulation as the mechanism of memory.
- **[002-009]**: "the inmost mind of the unchanging past" — music as access to a stable, interior temporality.
- **[008-005]**: "to be judged by the part of the past it brings up" — the present moment evaluated by its relationship to what came before.
- **[008-027]**: "his strong even teeth brought past and future into the moment" — a character who synthesises time, making past and future converge in presence.
