# Smile in _Interim_

The **smile** in _Interim_ is a primary instrument of social navigation. Richardson's narrative registers smiles with taxonomic precision — distinguishing the "derisive smile," the "solicitous smile," the "proud smile," the "gentle keen smile" — each variety carrying a distinct social meaning. Smiles in the novel are rarely simple expressions of happiness; they are performances, negotiations, and defences.

## The Taxonomy of Smiles

Richardson's descriptive attention to smiles produces a kind of informal taxonomy. Mrs. Philps is instructed to "smile into the fire" [PN=001-001] — the smile here directed at an object rather than a person, suggesting a private satisfaction. Grace's "anxious pleading solicitous smile" [PN=001-014] layers three adjectives to capture a complex emotional performance. The "derisive smile" that "seemed to snort from the firm mouth" [PN=003-002] is barely a smile at all — it is mockery disguised as facial expression.

Mrs. Bailey's smile, by contrast, is professional and maternal: "Don't catch cold young lady, smiled Mrs. Bailey" [PN=002-006]. The smile here is part of the routine of hospitality, a social gesture as practiced as the serving of meals. Miriam "broadened her smile" in response [PN=002-007], the deliberate amplification of expression marking a strategic social move.

> …her anxious pleading solicitous smile.—I tell you never berrer… [PN=001-014]

## Smile and Power

Smiles in the novel often mark asymmetries of power and knowledge. The "ironically chuckling hilarious smile" with which a remark is met [PN=006-018] suggests a shared understanding from which the smiler is excluded. The "sympathetic glancing smile" directed at Miriam [PN=003-011] offers momentary alliance across a social divide. A "bridling smile" that masks vexation [PN=006-013] reveals the effort involved in maintaining social composure.

Heber's "proud smile" as he speaks of music [PN=004-003] registers self-possession and cultural confidence — the smile as an assertion of superiority disguised as warmth. The "faint shape of a tired smile" worn by a character "worn out with the fatigue" [PN=006-015] is a smile at its limit, reduced to a trace.

## Smile as Mask and Signal

The smile in *Interim* is frequently a mask — a facial arrangement that conceals rather than reveals. Miriam "smiled back at him without saying anything, rapidly piecing together the world" [006-018], the smile buying time while her mind works urgently behind it. Dr. von Heber's smile envelops his interlocutor: "he smiled rising, and surrounding her with his smile" [008-261] — the smile becoming an atmosphere, a form of social occupation that fills the space between bodies. When Miriam "smiled into the room" feeling "decorated" [009-015], the smile is a performance staged for no particular recipient, a costume of well-being worn for the room's general gaze.

The "deepening smile" that moves "forwards" [008-257] between two people who have just discovered a shared literary interest — "I must" and "we both smiled a wide agreement" [008-257] — indexes the moment when a smile shifts from social form to genuine connection. This graduation from the performed to the spontaneous is one of Richardson's finest observations of interpersonal life.

## Smile and the Servant Class

Smiles in *Interim* also encode class. Sissie "sat back smiling, and sat watching Miriam's face" [003-011], the servant's smile at once deferential and appraising. The "bright sweet smile" [003-009] Mrs. Bailey performs for her lodgers is part of her professional repertoire — a managed warmth that sustains the fiction of the boarding house as a family. When "Eve glanced down wisely smiling and moved slenderly towards the door" [006-017], the smile is knowing, condescending — an assertion of superior understanding.

## Smile and Character

For Miriam, observing smiles is a form of social intelligence. Her own smiles are strategic: she smiles to put others at ease, to acknowledge, to deflect. The "bright sweet smile" of a servant [PN=003-009] and the "gentle keen smile on her delicately shrivelled face" [PN=005-004] of an older woman capture character through facial expression in a single stroke — Richardson's method of rendering personality through surface detail.
