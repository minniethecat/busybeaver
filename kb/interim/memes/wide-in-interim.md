## Introduction

Wideness in *Interim* is the spatial counterforce to confinement — the broad thoroughfare, the wide pavement, the meadow stream spreading in the foreground of a painting. It appears in streets and skies, in the sense of a life that opens outward from the narrow boarding-house world. Miriam's consciousness repeatedly reaches toward wide spaces: the space of a room transformed by golden light, the vastness of a twilit sky, the breadth of a London street. Wideness promises freedom from the "crowding of mean little things" [001-013].

## Streets and Pavements

The wide thoroughfare is a recurring presence in Miriam's London walks. Crossing it, the wind is "reinforced from the north" [002-005], the wide street becoming a channel for elemental forces. The wide pavement accommodates a laughing group approaching [001-015], a stage for the social life Miriam observes from her outsider's vantage. Even the "wide open door" [001-015] carries the suggestion of welcome, a threshold into another life.

## The Painting's Distances

In the Brooms' parlour, a large oil-painting offers a vision of pastoral wideness: "its distances where a meadow stream that was wide in the foreground with a stone bridge" [001-011]. The painting is a portal — its wide stream and distant horizon are the antithesis of the confined domestic space in which Miriam stands, "shut away" from the "wide untroubled leisure" [001-013] she glimpses in the Brooms' world.

## Rooms and Openness

"Triumphant echoes filled its wide spaces, pressed against the windows, filtered out into the quiet street" [002-009] — the wide spaces of a room become resonant, alive with sound. The fire's "wide clear core striped by black bars" sends "a broad rose-gold shaft" glowing across the room [004-007], the width of the fire-core becoming a miniature sun. A door opens "quietly wide as if someone had been waiting behind it" [002-006], the movement of wideness creating suspense and invitation.

## The Hat's Wide Brim

The novel's opening scene establishes width through dress: Florrie appears "smiling brilliantly from under the wide brim of a heather mixture hat" [001-001]. The wide-brimmed hat frames the face, creates a perimeter of personality. It is the first of many thresholds between the self and the world, the wide brim a small shelter.
