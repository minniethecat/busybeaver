## Introduction

Miss Rogers appears briefly in chapter 5 of *Interim*, at the Bowdoins' musical evening. She is a boarder, newly introduced to the musical circle — "Miss Rogers has only lately joined musical circles" [005-004] — and her presence illuminates the intricate social choreography of the gathering. Mr. Bowdoin speaks of her in "an affectionate indulgent tone" [005-004], positioning her as a newcomer under his protective regard.

## At the Bowdoins' Evening

Miss Rogers enters with the other guests, murmured into the room by Mr. Bowdoin: "they stood about in the gloom near the door while Mr. Bowdoin carried away their things and came back and murmured Miss Rogers and Miss Henderson" [005-003]. She is part of the small crowd at the threshold, before the party finds its shape. When seated, she is the object of attention from another woman guest who "had crossed over to a chair near Miss Rogers and was in eager conversation" [005-004] — the newcomer already a magnet for those seeking social alliance.

## Composure and Ambition

Miss Rogers's manner is one of guarded composure. "Even Miss Rogers was sitting less stiffly" [005-004] in the warmth of the room, suggesting an initial rigidity that gradually softens. She is "calm and easily supercilious and secret" [005-004], the three adjectives building a portrait of someone who holds herself apart. Her ambition — to be "a candidate" [005-004] for the musical circle — is visible to Miriam but not discussed openly. The woman eager to speak with her "did not seem to think it strange that Miss Rogers should be a candidate" [005-004], naturalising the social climbing that Miss Rogers herself tries to conceal.

## A Mirror for Miriam

Miss Rogers serves as a mirror for Miriam's own social unease. Where Miss Rogers is calm and secret, Miriam is watchful and registering. Miss Rogers's quiet candidacy for admission to the circle mirrors Miriam's own ambiguous position — both are outsiders, but Miss Rogers pursues belonging with a strategic composure that Miriam cannot or will not adopt.
