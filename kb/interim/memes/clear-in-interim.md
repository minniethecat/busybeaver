## Overview

Clarity in *Interim* is an elusive ideal. The novel's central consciousness, Miriam, moves through a world thick with ambiguity — social signals she cannot decode, her own desires she cannot name. "Clear" moments are rare and prized: a "clear field" for action [001-017], a room "full of clear strength" [002-002], notes that sound "soft and clear and true" [002-009]. Clarity is what Miriam reaches toward and what the novel's prose itself aspires to — precision of perception, honesty of feeling.

## Manifestations

Physical clarity appears in descriptions of light, sound, and space. Firelight has a "wide clear core striped by black bars" [004-007]; piano notes sound "clear and true into her mind" [002-009]; a room is "plain and clear for things to happen" [005-003]. These moments of perceptual sharpness offer respite from the blur of social confusion that otherwise dominates Miriam's experience.

Epistemological clarity — knowing, understanding — is more fraught. Miriam senses that "it was quite clear that she belonged to the lympic" [002-006], but this clarity is often an illusion produced by her own yearning. The desire for someone to "make it clear from the beginning, or bring" order to experience [006-016] is a desire the novel never fulfills. Florrie "saw the whole thing clearly" [001-010] — but Miriam rarely shares this certainty.

The concept extends to moral clarity. "Perhaps if she had a clear field she could disarm them" [001-017]: clarity as the precondition for effective action, a space free of obstruction and second-guessing. The "clear neat patterns" into which experience might resolve [003-016] represent an ordering impulse — the wish to transform messy life into legible form, a wish the novel both entertains and ironizes.

## Key Passages

- **002-002** — "The room was full of clear strength": clarity as atmosphere, a quality of space.
- **001-017** — "if she had a clear field she could disarm them": clarity as precondition for agency.
- **002-009** — "notes sounded soft and clear and true into her mind": auditory clarity as mental relief.
- **005-003** — "plain and clear for things to happen and shine": clarity as transparency and receptivity.
- **003-016** — "become clear neat patterns, lose some of their": the ordering impulse and its cost.
