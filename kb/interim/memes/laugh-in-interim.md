## Overview

Laughter in *Interim* is never simple. It functions as a dense social signal — marking power imbalances, performing ease, deflecting intimacy, or betraying genuine feeling. Characters laugh at, with, and against one another; the novel tracks each laugh's texture — "derisive," "fresh," "ironic," "secretly" — as carefully as it tracks dialogue. Laughter is a second language, and Miriam, the central consciousness, is an anxious interpreter of its meanings.

## Manifestations

Laughter most often appears in social gatherings, where it operates as a lubricant and a weapon. At the dinner table, "everyone laughed" at a witticism [001-011], and Florrie laughs while drawing mustard [001-013] — these are the ritual laughs of domestic performance. But Miriam experiences laughter differently: she "flushed through her laughter, steadily meeting the expression" of others [001-014], her laughter a shield rather than release.

Male characters deploy laughter as assertion. Mr. Mendizabal's face "seemed to be laughing ironically behind a proud" facade [004-003]; a hay merchant "laughed derisively in rich singing" tones [003-004]. Women's laughter is more often social or defensive — Eve speaks "warmly" of loving something [006-015] while Miriam sits "stiffly through the laughter of the Baileys" [003-009], excluded from its warmth.

Laughter also marks thresholds. Grace "laughed" at the suggestion of lighting candles [001-017]; the "sound of the laugh rang in her head; a single" note [003-002] — laughter can isolate as sharply as it can join. The Baileys are heard "laughing and talking on their doorsteps" [002-003], a sound of communal ease from which Miriam is spatially and emotionally removed.

## Key Passages

- **001-001** — "on a choke of laughter, come in": the novel opens with laughter that is choked, ambiguous.
- **003-002** — "the sound of the laugh rang in her head; a single": laughter as haunting, isolating signal.
- **003-009** — "Miriam sat stiffly through the laughter of the Baileys": exclusion through communal mirth.
- **004-003** — "his face seemed to be laughing ironically behind a proud": laughter as social mask.
- **002-004** — "when their work was done they could laugh and sing in a warm room": laughter as reward, earned release.
