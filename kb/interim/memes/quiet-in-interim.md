## Overview

Quiet pervades the world of *Interim* as both an atmospheric condition and a social mode. It is not the absence of sound but a quality of restraint — rooms hold a "quiet air" [008-020], streets stretch "quiet" and "empty" [005-005], voices carry a "low quiet glad confident" tone [004-014]. Quiet shapes how characters inhabit space and how they present themselves to others.

## Manifestations

Quiet appears most often as a descriptor of domestic interiors. Miriam's room enfolds her in "freshness and quietude" [001-001]; the "fresh quiet room" gleams with newness [001-015]. These quiet spaces offer refuge from social demands, a buffer against the noisy world of the Baileys' household and the clatter of London streets.

Outdoors, quiet defines the neighbourhood itself — the "quiet road of large high grey mysterious houses" [002-006] and the "quiet street" into which piano music filters [002-009]. The novel maps London through its quiet zones, contrasting them with the café's "warm brilliance" and the "excited talk and loud laughter" of social gatherings.

Quiet also governs interpersonal conduct. Mr. Bowdoin speaks "quietly" [004-003]; von Heber announces his name "quietly, his restrained uncontrollable" [004-013]; Dr. Heber exits with "quiet formal phrases of politeness" [006-019]. Miriam herself is advised: "If you keep quite quiet and gentle, asking for nothing" [004-011]. Quiet becomes a strategy — a way of navigating a world where assertiveness invites scrutiny.

## Key Passages

- **001-001** — "the freshness and quietude of the room enfolded Miriam": the novel's first invocation of quiet as embrace.
- **002-006** — "the quiet road of large high grey mysterious houses": quiet mapped onto the London streetscape.
- **004-011** — "If you keep quite quiet and gentle, asking for nothing": quiet as survival strategy.
- **005-005** — "the high quiet empty blue-lit street": quiet and emptiness converge in the nocturnal city.
- **008-020** — "something waiting within the quiet air of the room that would be": quiet as charged anticipation, not vacancy.
