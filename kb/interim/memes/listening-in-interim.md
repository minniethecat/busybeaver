## Overview

Listening in *Interim* is never passive. It is an active perceptual stance that shapes the narrative's distinctive consciousness. Miriam listens to music, to clocks striking, to the rain, to the voices of others — and in each instance listening becomes a way of knowing, a form of attention that yields insight unavailable to the merely observing eye.

## Manifestations

Music is the novel's primary listening scene. Miriam listens to the piano "heart foremost, listening, following the claim of the music" [001-012], her body oriented toward sound. At dinner she eats "listening for the tune with an intelligent attention waiting for the end" [001-013] — listening as a mode of social navigation, using music as a thread through the dinner's conversational noise.

Listening also reveals intimacy and its limits. Mrs. Bailey's guests present "the pose of listening. The eyes saw nothing" [005-003] — a critique of performative attention, listening as social display rather than genuine engagement. Later, "No one was listening. They were quite alone among the jostling groups" [008-144] — the failure of listening as the failure of community.

Eavesdropping shades into listening proper. Miriam stands "listening in amazement. Mrs. Bailey had gone" [002-006]; she waits "listening for silence" at Tansley Street [005-005]. In these moments listening is a boundary practice — hearing what is not meant for her, or straining to hear what is absent. The rain becomes a listening scene: "She was listening to it and hearing it in exactly the way it was meant" [004-004], where listening merges with understanding.

Listening to clocks — St. Pancras striking [004-013], the guilty listening for nine o'clock [008-017] — introduces temporality, the sensation of time passing as a sonic event.

## Key Passages

- **[001-012]**: "heart foremost, listening, following the claim of the music" — music listening as whole-body attention.
- **[005-003]**: "The pose of listening. The eyes saw nothing" — the critique of performative attention, the social ritual hollowed out.
- **[004-004]**: "She was listening to it and hearing it in exactly the way it was meant" — listening as understanding, the sound of rain as communication.
- **[008-144]**: "No one was listening. They were quite alone among the jostling groups" — the failure of listening as isolation within the crowd.
