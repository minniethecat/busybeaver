# Knowledge in _Interim_

**Knowledge** in _Interim_ is never neutral. It is wielded, withheld, performed, and felt — a social currency as much as a cognitive state. Richardson's narrator is acutely aware of who knows what, who assumes knowledge, and what it means to hold knowledge back.

## Thematic Dimensions

### Knowledge as Social Power

Knowledge operates as a form of social leverage. A speaker deploys "the unctuous suggestion of superior knowledge" [004-009], using knowingness as a weapon — "he would use them to crush someone." Knowledge here is not enlightenment but domination, the performance of expertise designed to intimidate. The contrast emerges when "all his knowledge was put aside and he listened" [004-010] — the suspension of performed knowledge becomes a marker of genuine engagement.

### Knowledge as Withholding

The narrator herself holds knowledge in reserve. She sits "holding all her knowledge of the Brooms back and back" [001-013], the repetition of "back" emphasising the effort of containment. Knowledge unspoken becomes a kind of pressure, a fullness that must be managed. This is knowledge as private resource — what one knows about others and chooses not to reveal.

### Knowledge as World-Access

Knowledge also opens worlds. The "knowledge and the fineness of a world not her own" [004-013] makes knowledge a portal — to know is to glimpse a realm otherwise inaccessible. The speaker's "strong courteous voice" carries knowledge as a quality of being, not merely information possessed but a world inhabited. Conversely, "the knowledge of being starved through fear" [004-012] makes knowledge a burden — to know one's own fear is to be trapped by it.

### Knowledge as Assumption

Richardson is attentive to the social dynamics of assumed knowledge. Someone speaks "reassuringly assuming her equal knowledge" [006-026], the assumption itself a social gesture — flattering, inclusive, but also potentially mistaken. The gap between assumed and actual knowledge is one of the novel's recurring tensions.

## Key Passages

- **[001-013]** "Holding all her knowledge of the Brooms back and back" — Knowledge withheld as private resource.
- **[004-009]** "The unctuous suggestion of superior knowledge" — Knowledge as social weapon.
- **[004-013]** "The knowledge and the fineness of a world not her own" — Knowledge as access to another's world.
- **[006-026]** "Reassuringly assuming her equal knowledge" — The social gesture of assumed shared knowledge.
