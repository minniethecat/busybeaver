## Introduction

The body in *Interim* is at once the vehicle of perception and its burden. Miriam experiences her body as something she inhabits uneasily — "her body was heavy on her chair, ill-clothed" [001-017] — a physical presence that anchors her consciousness while simultaneously betraying it. Bodies in the novel are observed, weighed, compared: the "thick expressionless figure and hands" of the servant Christine, the "small hands" of Grace, the "sturdy strength and polished refinement" of the stranger Mendizabal. Each body tells a social story.

## The Body as Burden

Miriam's body is rarely at ease. Sitting in Mrs. Philps's velvet armchair, the cushion sends "her body forward, her small chest crouched" [001-011] — the furniture that should offer comfort instead makes her body an awkward object to be managed. Her body becomes "a blank" [001-017], the sense of physical heaviness erasing the richness of interior life. This heaviness is partly physical, partly the weight of being seen, of being a body among other bodies who notice and judge.

## Bodies as Social Texts

The novel reads bodies as texts of class and character. Christine's body — "the thick expressionless figure and hands and the heavy sallow sullen face" [001-011] — announces her working-class position and her emotional opacity. The Philps girls have "six small hands labouring amongst the large things on the table" [001-013], their smallness suggesting refinement and gentility. Grace's "small hands and mouth gravely labouring" [001-017] over her meal conveys a kind of earnestness. Reading bodies becomes a way of reading the social world.

## The Perceiving Body

Yet the body is also the instrument of perception. Miriam's consciousness streams through her body — she feels cold in her bones, hears music through her fingers at the piano, experiences "the cool sense of life pouring from some inner source" [001-012]. The body is both the obstacle to pure consciousness and its only channel. This paradox — the body as burden and gift — runs through the novel like a bass note.
