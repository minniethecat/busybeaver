# Picture in _Interim_

**Pictures** in _Interim_ appear throughout the novel's interiors — on walls, above pianos, in frames — as objects that draw the eye and provoke reflection. They are part of the visual texture of domestic space, but they also serve as triggers for Miriam's characteristic inward turning, moments when an external image prompts an internal response.

## Pictures in Domestic Space

The walls of the Bailey household are populated with pictures. Miriam notices "the picture of Queen Victoria leaning on" a landscape [PN=001-004], a detail that places the Baileys' décor within a recognisable late-Victorian English idiom. These pictures are not fine art; they are the accumulated visual background of a boarding-house, the kind of images that long-term residents cease to see.

Yet Miriam sees them. She observes "the pictures on the wall" and finds them "calmed and contemplable" [PN=001-015], transforming ordinary décor into objects worthy of sustained attention. Her gaze moves across the walls as if reading them: "faded pictures on the faded walls" [PN=002-008] convey the passage of time through the deterioration of images, the fading of pictures mirroring the fading of the lives that hung them.

> …she looked at all the pictures. The people in the pictures… [PN=004-012]

## Pictures and Mental Imagery

Richardson extends "picture" beyond the literal object into the realm of mental imagery. Miriam's eyes are described as "drawing pictures of Mr. Bowdoin's back view" [PN=004-003], the act of perception itself becoming a kind of picture-making. The mind forms "pictures that grew clearer, each time" [PN=004-007] — internal images that sharpen with repeated attention, a description of memory itself as a pictorial process.

This movement between external picture and internal image is characteristic of the novel's method: the seen world is always being remade as inner vision.

## The Limits of Pictures

Miriam is not uncritical of pictures. There is a recognition that they can be a "flattery of the pictures which were nothing" [PN=005-003] — mere decoration, surfaces without depth. The space "between the pictures" where "the walls sent back sheeny reflections" [PN=006-001] registers the blank wall as perhaps more honest than the images that cover it. Pictures promise meaning but do not always deliver it.
