## Overview

Banbury Park is the London suburb where the Broom family home is situated in *Interim*. It is the setting for Miriam Henderson's Christmas visit and represents a space of domestic warmth, comfort, and emotional refuge. The name evokes the earlier visits Miriam made to the Brooms "in the Banbury Park days" [001-011].

## Significance

The Broom home in Banbury Park serves as a sanctuary for Miriam, contrasting sharply with her London boarding-house life. The house itself — with its velvet armchairs, oil paintings, Satsuma vases, blazing fires, and carefully maintained routines — embodies a settled domesticity that Miriam both cherishes and feels ambivalent about.

The garden at Banbury Park is another significant space: "the little square of summer garden showed brilliantly in this darkest winter blackness" [001-012]. It represents continuity across visits and seasons, a fixed point in Miriam's otherwise transient life.
