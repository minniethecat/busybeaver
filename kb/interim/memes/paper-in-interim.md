# Paper in _Interim_

**Paper** in _Interim_ appears in its many everyday forms — newspapers, notepaper, wallpaper, pamphlets — forming a quiet material substrate to the novel's world. Richardson's registration of paper goods anchors the narrative in the textures of late-Victorian domestic and public life.

## Paper and Writing

Paper is the medium of writing and correspondence. The novel opens with Miriam's luggage containing "a bundle of notepaper and envelopes" [PN=001-001], the materials of letter-writing packed alongside her nightgown — communication as essential as sleep. Mr. Mendizabal is observed "with a pencil and paper at the side of his plate" [PN=006-019], the diner who writes between courses. A stranger's "piece of paper, held against the wall" [PN=003-013] becomes the surface for a hasty sketch.

> …a bundle of notepaper and envelopes. A nightgown was visible… [PN=001-001]

## Newspapers and Public Life

Newspapers appear as markers of the wider world intruding into domestic space. Miriam handles "the pile of newspapers and magazines" [PN=001-014] as part of household routine. The "little man with an armful of newspapers and a top hat pushed back" [PN=009-099] is a street type, and the "paper, a pamphlet … coloured; blue" [PN=009-100] glimpsed under streetlamps suggests political or commercial print culture. "I edit their newspaper. I conduct a café" [PN=003-006] is a boast that yokes print and hospitality.

## Wallpaper as Domestic Surface

Wallpaper is the paper most intimately lived with — the "brown-yellow varnish of the wallpaper above the mantelpiece" [PN=002-003] and the patterned wall "between the two french windows" [PN=002-008] create the visual environment of the Baileys' house. Miriam "consult[s] the pictures and the wallpaper" [PN=004-013] as if reading the room.
