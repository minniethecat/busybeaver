## Overview

The "moment" in *Interim* is the fundamental unit of Miriam Henderson's consciousness. Richardson's narrative method builds itself from instants — a glance, a sensation, a half-formed thought — that accumulate into the texture of lived experience. These moments are not mere fragments but concentrated units of meaning, each carrying the weight of past and the anticipation of future within its brief duration.

## The Structure of the Instant

Richardson's prose renders the moment as layered and expansive rather than fleeting.

> She paused a moment, looking boldly across at the figures moving on the islands. (001-015)

In that pause an entire world of perception opens. The novel demonstrates that a moment is never empty: it is filled with sensation, memory, judgment, and the continuous pressure of consciousness.

> Suddenly her mind became a blank. (001-017)

Even the apparent absence of thought within a moment is itself a kind of content, registered with the same fidelity as the most vivid impression.

> I can smell the smell of the inside at this moment. (001-011)

Sensory memory compressed into an instantaneous impression, collapsing past and present into a single charged instant.

## Moments of Recognition

Throughout *Interim*, certain moments stand out as nodes of heightened awareness. These are not dramatic revelations but subtle shifts in perception: a sudden understanding of a social situation, a fleeting sense of connection with another person, an unexpected memory surfacing unbidden.

> In a moment she would be coming in. (001-014)

The anticipation of an arrival crystallizes into a moment of suspended time, charged with emotional possibility. Richardson finds the epiphanic not in extraordinary events but in the ordinary texture of daily consciousness.

> There was something that she had forgotten, some point that was being missed, something that must be said at this moment to pin down the happiness of everything. (001-013)

## The Accumulation of Moments

The novel's structure — its apparent formlessness — is actually an architecture of accumulated moments. Each paragraph registers a new instant of consciousness, and the movement from one moment to the next constitutes the narrative itself. There is no plot in the conventional sense; instead, the succession of moments builds a portrait of a mind in time, demonstrating that lived experience is always "moment-to-moment," a continuous present that carries the residue of every previous instant.

## Key Passages

- "She paused a moment, looking boldly across at the figures moving on the" street — a moment of suspended observation that captures the novel's method of arrested attention (001-015).
- "Suddenly her mind became a blank. Her body was heavy" — the moment of mental emptiness rendered with the same precision as the moment of vivid perception (001-017).
- "In a moment she would be coming in" — anticipation compressed into a single charged instant, demonstrating how the future inhabits the present moment (001-014).
- "I can smell the smell of the inside at this moment" — sensory memory condensed into an instantaneous impression, collapsing past and present into a single charged instant (001-011).
