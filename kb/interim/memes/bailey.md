## Overview

Mrs. Bailey is Miriam Henderson's landlady in London, running a boarding house that features prominently in Miriam's thoughts throughout *Interim*. Though she never appears directly in the novel's Christmas setting, she is a constant presence in Miriam's reflections, representing Miriam's ordinary London life outside the holiday visit.

## Role in the Narrative

The Baileys' boarding house serves as the backdrop to Miriam's working life in London. Miriam thinks about the Baileys frequently during her Christmas visit, contrasting the boarding-house atmosphere with the warm domesticity of the Brooms' home [002-003]. Mrs. Bailey had spoken of taking in "boarders" [002-006], a development that caused Miriam apprehension.

The Bailey household represents a different social world — more transient, less emotionally secure — against which the Broom family's settled domesticity is measured. Miriam's thoughts return to the Baileys as a way of processing her own ambiguous social position and uncertain future.
