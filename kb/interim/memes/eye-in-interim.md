## Eyes as Windows of Perception

Miriam's consciousness in *Interim* is intensely visual, with eyes serving as the primary instrument of perception. Her "eye running round the broad gilt-edged cream-coloured basins" [001-001] demonstrates how seeing anchors her in the physical world. The narrative frequently tracks what Miriam sees before recording what she thinks.

> …her eye running round the broad gilt-edged cream-coloured basins… [001-001]

## The Gaze and Social Encounter

Eyes in *Interim* mediate social encounters fraught with tension. Characters communicate through "downcast eyes" [001-015] and "blind eyes unwillingly turned towards her" [001-016], revealing dynamics of power and vulnerability that dialogue cannot express.

> …towards them through downcast eyes. They glinted back at her… [001-015]

Miriam's self-consciousness manifests in her awareness of being seen. She "dropped her eyes" [002-003] under scrutiny, the gesture marking the boundary between inner experience and external judgment.

## Eyes and Emotional Expression

Eyes in *Interim* carry emotion with a directness that speech cannot match. At the dinner table, Mrs. Philps is seen "mopping her eyes and dimpling and sighing" [001-010], the gesture of wiping eyes carrying the residue of feeling — tears, laughter, or the ambiguous border between them. Miriam herself takes her "eyes from the protruding eyes of the" [001-004], the shift of gaze marking a withdrawal from what is too insistently present. Later, Miriam meets "frank eyes still waiting for a verdict" [001-017], the frankness of the look demanding a response that Miriam may not be ready to give. Across these scenes, eyes function as emotional barometers — registering, withholding, and demanding feeling in a social world where verbal expression is constrained.

> …said Mrs. Philps mopping her eyes and dimpling and sighing upon…
> (001-010)

## The Eye and Artistic Observation

The eye in *Interim* transcends passive perception to become an instrument of artistic vision. Miriam "glanced about" [001-013] and "gazed" at scenes with an attentiveness that transforms ordinary domestic moments into aesthetic objects. The "brown and green landscape caught her eye, old and still" [001-013], suggesting how visual attention can crystallise fleeting impressions into lasting images.

Miriam's glancing functions as a mode of narrative consciousness — the rapid movement of her gaze across faces and objects mirrors the stream of her thought. She eats "rapidly glancing from face to face" [001-010], her restless eye recording the social world in fragments that the narrative assembles into meaning.

## Eyes and Inner Experience

The eye in *Interim* marks the frontier between public performance and private feeling. When Miriam sits at dinner, her "bright dark eye gazed derision into the flames" [003-002], the gaze directed away from company and toward the fire signalling a withdrawal into interior space. The "firm black-lashed white lid" [003-002] that shelters the eye suggests a guarded self, while the derisive gaze reveals an unspoken critique of the social scene.

In moments of solitude, Miriam's visual experience intensifies. She "stared into the velvety blackness trying to see the furniture" [001-012], her straining eye embodying consciousness reaching outward into darkness — a figure for the mind's effort to apprehend the world even when sensory data thins. The "extinguished eye" [006-028] of an old man in a café becomes an emblem of mortality and the waning of perception itself.
