## Overview

Roundness in *Interim* is a recurring spatial and perceptual motif. The novel repeatedly frames experience through circular configurations — people gathered around tables and pianos, the mind drawing circles around family groups, darkness surrounding Miriam in her bedroom. This circular imagery serves as both a structural device for domestic scenes and a lens through which Miriam perceives her world.

## Manifestations

The motif appears in several distinct forms throughout the novel. Domestic gatherings are repeatedly described with circular language: at the supper table, Miriam "got herself round" to join the family group [001-001]; later, guests sit "close round the piano surprised and attentive" [004-001]. These physical arrangements encode the social dynamics of the boarding house.

Miriam's interior perception also takes circular form. Her mind "drew a little circle round the family group" as it "spun round faster and faster" [001-017], suggesting both enclosure and the dizzying speed of consciousness. Darkness and silence are experienced as surrounding presences: "the fresh spaces of the darkness all round her" [001-012] and "silence, triumphantly silent and heavy all round her" [002-009].

The novel extends the motif to spatial description — the "globe rounding vaguely off on either side" [003-011] and the "square roundly modelled head" [003-014] — blending geometry with perception. Movement through domestic space is circular too: a character "rounded the stairs" [003-010] or "turned on his heel and swung round" [003-016].

## Key Passages

- **[001-001]**: "suburban slam and got herself round the supper table to stand with her back to the fireplace" — the inaugural domestic round, establishing the circular configuration of family life.
- **[001-017]**: "Her mind drew a little circle round the family group. It spun round faster and faster" — circularity as a model of consciousness itself.
- **[002-009]**: "silence, triumphantly silent and heavy all round her" — the oppressive weight of surrounding quiet, a sensory extension of the round motif.
- **[004-001]**: "sitting close round the piano surprised and attentive" — the aesthetic circle, music drawing people into attentive proximity.
