# Lord Advocate

The Lord Advocate (Mintlaw) serves as Counsel for the Crown in [[eustace-macallan|Eustace Macallan]]'s trial. He represents the legal system's attempt to convict [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]] of murder — an attempt that fails to secure a guilty verdict but succeeds in leaving the defendant neither convicted nor acquitted, suspended in the ambiguous space of Not Proven.

## Role in the Novel

The Lord Advocate appears only in the trial transcript, but his presence is felt throughout the novel as the institutional voice of the accusation. The indictment he presents — "[[eustace-macallan|Eustace Macallan]] was indicted and accused, at the instance of His Majesty's Advocate, of the murder of his wife by poison" (015-018) — is the legal framework that [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]] must dismantle.

## Key Actions & Plot Involvement

### The Prosecution

The Lord Advocate presents the Crown's case: [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]] purchased arsenic, had opportunity to administer it, and had motive (the presence of [[mrs-beauly|Mrs. Beauly]] at [[gleninch|Gleninch]]). His case is circumstantial but compelling — enough to persuade the jury that the poisoning was proven even if [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]]'s guilt was not.

### The Limitations

Despite his institutional power, the Lord Advocate cannot overcome the evidentiary gaps in his case. The novel treats this not as prosecutorial failure but as a structural limitation of adversarial justice: the prosecutor can only work with the evidence available, and the evidence available is insufficient to establish truth.
