# Mrs. Beauly

Mrs. Beauly is a minor but narratively crucial figure in the [[gleninch|Gleninch]] drama. A "young widow lady" and cousin of [[eustace-macallan|Eustace Macallan]], she was staying at [[gleninch|Gleninch]] during the period leading up to [[sara-macallan|Sara Macallan]]'s death. Her presence became a focal point of the trial: the prosecution suggested she was a motive for murder, while the defense noted that [[sara-macallan|Sara]]'s jealousy of her was a possible source of marital tension.

## Role in the Novel

Mrs. Beauly appears in the trial transcript as a figure whose relationship to [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]] was scrutinized. She "came to stay at [[gleninch|Gleninch]]" (016-018), and [[sara-macallan|Sara]] "was jealous of" her (016-018) — jealousy that the defense suggested was unfounded but that colored the atmosphere of the household. She represents the social world of [[gleninch|Gleninch]]: the guests, the relationships, the tensions that the trial reduced to evidence.

## Character Description

She is described through the trial testimony as "a most" attractive woman (016-019), creating a contrast with [[sara-macallan|Sara Macallan]]. Her physical beauty becomes a narrative detail with legal implications — it supports the prosecution's theory that [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]] had a motive (desire for another woman) to kill his wife.

## Key Actions & Plot Involvement

### Presence at Gleninch

Mrs. Beauly was a guest at [[gleninch|Gleninch]] alongside "[[miserrimus-dexter|Mr. Dexter]], an old friend" of [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]]'s (016-040). The household configuration — husband, wife, attractive female guest, eccentric male friend — creates the social chemistry that the trial attempts to parse as criminal evidence.

### The Jealousy

[[sara-macallan|Sara Macallan]]'s jealousy of Mrs. Beauly is attested by witnesses. Whether this jealousy was justified (pointing to [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]]'s infidelity) or paranoid (pointing to [[sara-macallan|Sara]]'s mental state) is one of the ambiguities the trial cannot resolve.
