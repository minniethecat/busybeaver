# Gleninch Cook

The cook at [[gleninch|Gleninch]] appears briefly but crucially in the trial of [[eustace-macallan|Eustace Macallan]]. Alongside the gardener, the cook "wound the chain of hostile evidence around the prisoner more mercilessly still" (017-031). The cook's need for arsenic — to rid the lower part of the house of rats — was the second ostensible reason for [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]]'s arsenic purchase, and like the gardener's insects, this domestic pest control became evidence in a murder case.

## Role in the Novel

The cook is one of the invisible infrastructure of [[gleninch|Gleninch]] — a servant whose testimony matters only because of the fatal context in which ordinary household tasks are reinterpreted. The cook's arsenic use for rat poison is the mirror of the gardener's use for insect control: two mundane purposes that the prosecution seeks to transform into a pattern of deception.

## Character Description

The cook is barely characterized — a functional presence in the trial transcript who "spoke as positively as the gardener" (017-034). The lack of individuation is itself significant: the cook is Everyman (or Everywoman), the ordinary domestic worker whose daily duties have been weaponized.

## Key Actions & Plot Involvement

### The Arsenic Request

The cook needed arsenic "for ridding the lower part of the house of rats" (017-057). This request — routed through [[sara-macallan|Sara Macallan]] to [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]] to the chemist — forms the second link in the chain of arsenic acquisition that the prosecution presents as evidence of premeditation.

### The Testimony

Like the gardener, the cook testifies under oath, and the combined testimony of the two servants creates the impression of a household with an unusual appetite for poison. The defense counters that [[sara-macallan|Sara]] herself managed the household arsenic — "My wife was the person who gave orders to the gardener and cook — not I" (017-058).

### The Counter-Narrative

[[miserrimus-dexter|Dexter]]'s later testimony reframes the cook's evidence: what the prosecution presents as suspicious arsenic accumulation was, in [[miserrimus-dexter|Dexter]]'s account, [[sara-macallan|Sara Macallan]]'s secret use of arsenic as a cosmetic — a practice unknown to the servants who handled the poison for its stated domestic purposes.
