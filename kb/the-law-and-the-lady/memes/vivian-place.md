# Vivian Place

[[major-fitz-david|Major Fitz-David]]'s house at number sixteen Vivian Place is [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]]'s first destination in her investigation. "[[major-fitz-david|Major Fitz-David]]'s address was number sixteen" (006-069), and it is here that she encounters the gallant Major, receives his evasive courtesies, and begins to understand the social network that has protected [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]]'s secret.

## Description

The house is a bachelor establishment — comfortable, tasteful, and designed for entertaining. A servant "out of livery, an old man" (008-004) opens the door. The atmosphere is one of cultivated leisure, with nosegays from [[lady-clarinda|Lady Clarinda]] arriving during [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]]'s visit.

## Role in the Story

Vivian Place is the novel's social gateway — the space where [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]] transitions from private wife to public investigator. Fitz-David's drawing room is where she learns to navigate the social codes that govern the disclosure (and concealment) of information.
