# Benjamin's Villa

[[benjamin|Benjamin]]'s "little villa, in the byways of the suburb of [[london|London]]" (006-039) is [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]]'s sanctuary and operational base. It is here that she comes for advice, for comfort, and for the practical assistance (directories, legal opinions, letters of introduction) that her investigation requires.

## Description

The villa is modest but comfortable, centered on [[benjamin|Benjamin]]'s "little library" (006-040) with its "bright fireside." It is the home of a lifelong bachelor clerk — organized, quiet, and infused with the values of discretion and loyalty that [[benjamin|Benjamin]] embodies.

## Role in the Story

The villa represents the domestic stability that [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]] has lost. [[benjamin|Benjamin]]'s fireside is where she can think clearly, away from the theatrical chaos of [[miserrimus-dexter|Dexter]]'s house and the social maneuvering of Fitz-David's drawing room.
