# Major Fitz-David

Major Fitz-David is the keeper of [[eustace-macallan|Eustace Macallan]]'s secret. A mutual friend of [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]] and [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]]'s uncle, he is the recipient of the letter that first reveals [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]]'s true identity — "My uncle has written to Major Fitz-David" (002-033) — and he becomes one of [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]]'s first sources of information about her husband's past.

## Role in the Novel

Fitz-David functions as a gatekeeper figure: he possesses crucial information but releases it only under pressure. He is loyal to [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]], genuinely concerned for [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]], and caught between his duty to his friend and his sympathy for the deceived wife.

This conflict — between honor and disclosure, between protecting a friend and helping a wife — makes Fitz-David one of the novel's most morally complex minor characters. He is neither villain nor hero; he is a man of principle navigating an impossible situation.

## Character Description

He is presented as a man of the world — a retired military officer with social connections and a sense of honor. His response to [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]]'s situation reveals a character torn between discretion and compassion. The "Major" title is significant: it signals a man accustomed to command and loyalty, whose ethical framework is shaped by military codes of honor rather than legal or domestic ones.

## Key Actions & Plot Involvement

### The Letter

[[valeria-brinton|Valeria]]'s uncle writes to Fitz-David in search of information about [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]] Woodville: "My uncle has written to Major Fitz-David" (002-033). Fitz-David's response — which [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]] intercepts — contains the first confirmation that "Woodville" is not [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]]'s real name.

### The Revelation

Fitz-David becomes one of [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]]'s allies in her investigation. He provides access to documents and connections that advance her inquiry, though always within the bounds of what he considers honorable disclosure. His cooperation is conditional — he will help [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]] but will not betray [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]].
