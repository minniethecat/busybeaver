# Ramsgate

Ramsgate is the Kent seaside resort where [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]] and [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]] spend their honeymoon — a place intended for domestic happiness that becomes instead the site of the novel's first major crisis. It is here, on the beach at Broadstairs, that [[mrs-macallan|Mrs. Macallan]] refuses to acknowledge [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]], and here, at the lodging-house, that the landlady reveals [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]]'s deception.

## Description

Collins presents Ramsgate through the sensory pleasures of a seaside holiday: "the brisk breeze, the brilliant sky, the flashing blue sea, the sun-bright cliffs and the tawny sands at their feet" (003-024). The beauty of the setting creates a sharp contrast with the emotional turmoil it hosts.

## Role in the Story

Ramsgate is the novel's threshold space — the point where [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]] passes from the security of her old life into the uncertainty of her investigation. The seaside, with its liminal quality (between land and sea), is the appropriate setting for this transition.
