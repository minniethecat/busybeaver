# Mr. Playmore

Mr. Playmore, W.S. (Writer to the Signet), is [[eustace-macallan|Eustace Macallan]]'s solicitor — the legal professional charged with defending an almost indefensible case. He appears in the trial record as a representative of the legal establishment, a figure whose competence cannot overcome the structural limitations of a case built on circumstantial evidence.

## Role in the Novel

Playmore is listed among the legal participants in the trial — "Mr. Playmore, W.S." (015-015) — alongside the [[lord-advocate|Lord Advocate]] and other officers of the court. His role is institutional rather than dramatic: he represents the defense function within the adversarial system that the novel critiques.

## Key Actions & Plot Involvement

### The Defense

Playmore's defense strategy is to challenge the prosecution's chain of inference — to argue that arsenic purchase does not prove arsenic administration, that opportunity does not prove intent, and that circumstantial evidence does not constitute proof. His success is partial: he secures Not Proven rather than Guilty, but cannot achieve Not Guilty.
