# Christina Ormsay

Christina Ormsay is the nurse who attended [[sara-macallan|Sara Macallan]] during her last illness at [[gleninch|Gleninch]]. Her appearance in court "created a marked sensation" (016-009), and her testimony — examined by both the [[lord-advocate|Lord Advocate]] and the Dean of Faculty — provides the trial's most intimate portrait of the household tensions that preceded [[sara-macallan|Sara]]'s death.

## Role in the Novel

Ormsay is the novel's primary witness to the domestic reality of [[gleninch|Gleninch]]. Unlike the legal professionals who argue from inference, she testifies from direct observation: the jealousy, the arguments, the mysterious comings and goings. Her evidence is mined by both prosecution and defense, each extracting the version of events that supports their case.

## Character Description

Ormsay is presented as a professional — competent and observant, though her interpretations are contested. [[miserrimus-dexter|Dexter]] dismisses her as "the ignorant and prejudiced nurse" (020-011), but the trial record suggests a careful witness whose observations resist easy instrumentalization by either side.

## Key Actions & Plot Involvement

### The Illness

Ormsay attended [[sara-macallan|Sara]] from the onset of her illness to her death. She testifies about the symptoms, the treatments, and — crucially — the household atmosphere: [[sara-macallan|Sara]]'s jealousy of [[mrs-beauly|Mrs. Beauly]] (016-018), the tensions between husband and wife, and the presence of [[miserrimus-dexter|Mr. Dexter]] at [[gleninch|Gleninch]].

### The Dean of Faculty's Cross-Examination

Under pressure from the defense counsel, Ormsay was "obliged to exhibit my husband's first wife under an entirely new aspect" (016-063) — revealing dimensions of [[sara-macallan|Sara]]'s character that complicated the prosecution's simple narrative.

### The Arsenic Timeline

Ormsay's testimony about the timing of [[sara-macallan|Sara]]'s illness and the administration of medications becomes central to the arsenic narrative. She reports [[sara-macallan|Sara]]'s claim that "Mr. Macallan came in about an hour since; he found me still sleepless, and gave me my composing draught" (017-040) — a detail with profound implications for the murder case.
