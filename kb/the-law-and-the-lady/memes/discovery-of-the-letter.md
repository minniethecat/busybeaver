# Discovery of the Letter

The discovery of [[major-fitz-david|Major Fitz-David]]'s letter is the novel's first major plot reversal — the moment when [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]]'s comfortable assumptions about her marriage collapse. The letter, addressed to her uncle Starkweather from [[major-fitz-david|Major Fitz-David]], reveals that "[[eustace-macallan|Eustace]] [[eustace-macallan|Woodville]]" is actually [[eustace-macallan|Eustace Macallan]], and that he has been tried at [[edinburgh|Edinburgh]] for the murder of his first wife.

## Summary

Shortly after her wedding, [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]] intercepts a letter from [[major-fitz-david|Major Fitz-David]] to her uncle. Her husband's reaction to the letter — "He almost snatched the letter out of my hand; he turned his back on me to read it" (002-045) — alerts her to its importance. The letter's content, which she pieces together from fragments and later investigation, reveals that [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]] has been concealing both his real name and his criminal history.

## Context

[[valeria-brinton|Valeria]]'s uncle had written to Fitz-David seeking information about [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]]: "My uncle has written to [[major-fitz-david|Major Fitz-David]]" (002-033). The letter is Fitz-David's reply, and its guarded language — while protecting [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]]'s confidence — contains enough information to shatter [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]]'s peace of mind.

## Key Details

### The Interception

[[valeria-brinton|Valeria]] sees the letter in [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]]'s hands and, noting his agitation, demands to know its contents. [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]]'s evasions only increase her suspicion.

### The Name Revelation

The letter reveals that [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]]'s real surname is Macallan — a name associated with a notorious Scottish murder trial. This is the first time [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]] hears her husband's true name.

### The Consequences

The discovery transforms [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]] from a passive newlywed into an active investigator. Within chapters, she will seek out [[major-fitz-david|Major Fitz-David]], confront [[mrs-macallan|Eustace's mother]] at [[ramsgate|Ramsgate]], and begin the detective work that will ultimately clear her husband's name.

## Consequences

This event is the narrative's hinge: everything before it is setup (the wedding, the honeymoon), and everything after it is investigation. The letter transforms [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]]'s relationship to her husband from that of wife to that of detective — she must now discover what [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]] will not tell her. It also introduces the novel's central motif: the document that reveals a hidden truth (letters, trial transcripts, diary entries, and [[miserrimus-dexter|Dexter]]'s narrative all function similarly throughout the novel).
