# The Key Discovery

The discovery of the key — the physical object that [[miserrimus-dexter|Dexter]]'s narrative allegorically referenced — is the material confirmation that transforms [[miserrimus-dexter|Dexter]]'s story from performance into evidence. The key unlocks the dressing-room at [[gleninch|Gleninch]], and what it reveals — the dressing-gown that [[sara-macallan|Sara]] wore on the night of her death — provides the physical proof that [[miserrimus-dexter|Dexter]]'s narrative was truth, not fiction.

## Summary

During [[miserrimus-dexter|Dexter]]'s improvised story, [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]] recognized the mention of "the key" as significant: "The key! I instantly thought of the missing key at [[gleninch|Gleninch]]" (040-124). The subsequent search for and discovery of this key — and what it unlocked — provides the hard evidence that converts [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]]'s theory into demonstrable fact.

## Context

The key had been noted as missing during the original investigation of [[sara-macallan|Sara]]'s death but had never been found. Its absence was one of the minor mysteries of the case — unexplained but not considered significant enough to affect the verdict. [[miserrimus-dexter|Dexter]]'s narrative identified its location and significance.

## Key Details

The key unlocks a dressing-room at [[gleninch|Gleninch]] containing [[sara-macallan|Sara]]'s dressing-gown — which she was wearing on the night she died. The dressing-gown's condition (or the objects found in its pockets) provides evidence consistent with the cosmetic-arsenic theory and inconsistent with the murder theory.

## Consequences

The discovery of the key and its associated evidence provides the "new evidence" that allows [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]]'s case to be reconsidered. It is the physical proof that validates the interpretive work [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]] has done — the material fact that confirms the narrative truth.
