# Ramsgate Landlady

The [[ramsgate|Ramsgate]] landlady is a classic Collins comic peripheral character — a "gentlewoman reduced by family misfortunes to let lodgings" (005-014) whose irrepressible volubility accidentally exposes the truth. Her gossip about [[mrs-macallan|Eustace's mother]] is the first external confirmation that something is wrong with [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]]'s marriage, making her an unwitting agent of revelation.

## Role in the Novel

The landlady's role is that of the accidental truth-teller. She accompanies [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]] on a beach walk alongside [[mrs-macallan|Mrs. Macallan]] ([[mrs-macallan|Eustace's mother]]), chattering incessantly and repeatedly calling [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]] "[[valeria-brinton|Mrs. Woodville]]" — a name that [[mrs-macallan|Eustace's mother]] conspicuously fails to recognize (003-029, 003-037). Later, at the lodging house, the landlady declares: "A villain has deceived you. You are no more married than I am!" (005-020) — the first direct statement of the novel's central crisis.

## Character Description

Collins draws the landlady as a comic type: "voluble," "irrepressible," and socially assertive — a woman who "took a seat, without waiting to be invited, close by my side" (005-011). She alternates between self-dramatizing martyrdom ("I have degraded myself as a gentlewoman") and righteous fury, her moods shifting with the speed of farce.

## Key Actions & Plot Involvement

### The Beach Walk

Accompanying [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]] and [[mrs-macallan|Mrs. Macallan]] on the sands at Broadstairs, the landlady's repeated use of "[[valeria-brinton|Mrs. Woodville]]" creates a dramatic irony: the reader sees what [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]] does not yet fully grasp — that her mother-in-law does not recognize her own son's wife by name (003-048).

### The Revelation

Back at the lodging house, the landlady reveals that she followed [[mrs-macallan|Mrs. Macallan]] to the train station and discovered [[eustace-macallan|Eustace]]'s real identity. Her announcement — "You are no more married than I am!" — is the novel's first explicit articulation of [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]]'s predicament.

### The Investigation

The landlady's gossip provides [[valeria-brinton|Valeria]] with the address of [[mrs-macallan|Mrs. Macallan]]'s [[london|London]] residence, a crucial piece of information that advances the investigation. She is, for all her comic excess, a plot-efficient character: every one of her indiscretions moves the story forward.
