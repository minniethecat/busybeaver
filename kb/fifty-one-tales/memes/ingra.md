## Overview

Ingra is the joyous city of dancers in the Demagogue's vision in "[[the-demagogue-and-the-demi-monde|The Demagogue and the Demi-monde]]." It is the third stop on the heralds' journey, after [[sendara]] and [[sooma]], and is distinguished from all other lands by its defining activity: dance.

## In "The Demagogue and the Demi-monde"

After the heralds deliver their tidings to the kings of [[sooma|Sooma]], they continue their journey:

> And the heralds passing thence shall come even to Ingra, to Ingra where they dance. And there they shall tell of thee, so that thy name long hence shall be sung in that joyous city. [004-017]–[004-018]

From Ingra, the heralds borrow camels and pass over the sands to [[nirid]].

## Description

Ingra is defined by a single, recurring activity — dance. This distinguishes it from every other place in the Demagogue's vision:

| Place | Defining Activity |
|-------|------------------|
| [[sendara|Sendara]] | Sheep-herding |
| [[zoth|Zoth]] | Sacred flow (transmission) |
| [[sooma|Sooma]] | Kingship and trade |
| **Ingra** | **Dancing and singing** |
| [[nirid|Nirid]] | Monastic solitude |

The repetition of the name — "to Ingra, to Ingra where they dance" — gives it an incantatory quality. [[the-demagogue|The Demagogue]]'s rhetoric lingers on the name as though its very syllables contain joy. Ingra is the city where beauty's fame becomes [[poetry|song]], and where that [[poetry|song]] will outlast the moment of its telling ("thy name long hence shall be sung").

The detail that the heralds "borrow camels" at Ingra to cross the desert to [[nirid|Nirid]] also gives it a transitional geographical character: it is the last green place before the sands.

## Thematic Significance

Ingra represents the aesthetic endpoint of the Demagogue's argument. [[sendara|Sendara]] receives the rumour; the plains-people sing; [[sooma|Sooma]] trades in stories; but Ingra *is* the thing itself — a city organized around joy. It is the Demagogue's promise made geographical: that beauty, once known, transforms the world that receives it.
