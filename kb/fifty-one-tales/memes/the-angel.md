## Overview

The Angel is a supernatural figure who appears in two of Lord Dunsany's tales in *Fifty-One Tales*. In one tale, an angel walks by the sea and converses with [[the-worm|The Worm]] about the nature of destruction. In another, a huge angel is found building an expansion to Hell with a hammer, in response to the suffering caused by a commercial yeast product.

## Role in the Tales

In "The Worm and the Angel," the angel encounters a worm who has crawled from the tombs of the fallen. Together they survey the world — its kings, kingdoms, warriors, and cities — and the worm declares all of it to be its food. The angel responds with a line of Greek poetry, asking whether the worm can destroy even the sea.

> "Be dakeon para Thina poluphloisboio Thalassaes," murmured the angel, for they walked by the sea, "and can you destroy that too?"
>
> — 003-039

In another tale, the narrator comes upon a huge angel at the edge of Hell, hammering steel uprights into place. When questioned, the angel explains that Hell is being expanded to keep pace with the times, citing a new cheap yeast that has caused widespread suffering.
