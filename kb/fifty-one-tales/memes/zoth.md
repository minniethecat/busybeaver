## Overview

Zoth is the holy river in the Demagogue's seductive vision in "[[the-demagogue-and-the-demi-monde|The Demagogue and the Demi-monde]]." It flows from the pastoral land of [[sendara]] and serves as the conduit by which the rumour of the Demi-mondaine's beauty spreads through the imagined geography.

## In "The Demagogue and the Demi-monde"

In the Demagogue's elaborate geographical fantasy, the heralds' route follows the course of the river:

> and from Sendara the rumour shall spread on, down either bank of the holy river of Zoth, till the people that make wattles in the plains shall hear of it and sing. [004-010]

## Significance

Zoth is the only river named in the Demagogue's vision, and its epithet — "holy" — sets it apart from the other geographical features. While [[sendara|Sendara]] is defined by its pastoral economy (brown sheep) and [[sooma|Sooma]] by its political and commercial character, Zoth is defined by sacrality. It is a river whose banks are a medium of transmission: rumour travels along Zoth as naturally as water flows.

The detail that the "people that make wattles in the plains" hear the rumour and sing aligns Zoth with orality and folk culture. The river's holiness is not institutional (no temples or priests are mentioned) but elemental — it sanctifies the act of transmission itself.

## In the Chain of Lands

Zoth occupies a liminal position in the Demagogue's geography:

| Direction | From | To |
|-----------|------|-----|
| Upstream | [[sendara]] (pastoral land) | — |
| Downstream | — | The plains (home of the wattle-makers) |
| Northward | — | [[sooma]] (the golden city) |

It connects the humble sheep-herding of [[sendara|Sendara]] to the wider world, and its "either bank" suggests a river broad enough to be a geographical feature of consequence.
