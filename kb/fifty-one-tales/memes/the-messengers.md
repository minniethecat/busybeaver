## Overview

"The Messengers" is a compact parable about the relationship between the divine and the mundane. The Muses, high on Parnassus, choose not a poet or a priest to carry their message to the Golden Town, but a hare-hunter — a man who protests that the divine does not speak to "such as me." The tale's argument is that messengers are chosen not for their worthiness but for their availability, and that the divine message arrives through whatever channel happens to be passing.

## Summary

A man wandering near Parnassus, chasing hares, hears the high Muses sing. They call to him: "Take us a message to the Golden Town" [008-003].

The man protests: "They do not call to me. Not to such as me speak the Muses." But the Muses insist, and the man — reluctantly, disbelievingly — becomes the bearer of a message he never sought and does not fully understand [008-003].

## Analysis

The tale is a study in reluctant election. The hare-hunter is not seeking the Muses; he is chasing hares. The Muses do not seek a poet; they seek anyone who will listen. The gap between the messenger's self-conception and the task he is given is the tale's central tension — and Dunsany leaves it unresolved. We never learn what the message was, whether it was delivered, or what the Golden Town made of it. The tale is interested only in the moment of call and protest, the instant when the ordinary is interrupted by the numinous.

## Characters

- **The hare-hunter** — an ordinary man, wandering near Parnassus, who becomes an unwilling messenger of the Muses. His protest — "Not to such as me" — is both humble and an evasion.
- **The Muses** — heard but not seen, singing from the heights of Parnassus. They choose their messenger without explanation and with apparent indifference to his qualifications.

## Themes

- **[[inspiration]] and the ordinary** — the divine does not distinguish between poets and hare-hunters; it speaks to whoever is nearby.
- **Reluctance and calling** — the tale explores the gap between being chosen and feeling worthy of the choice.
