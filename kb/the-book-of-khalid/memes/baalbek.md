# Baalbek

## Role in the Book

Baalbek is Khalid's birthplace and the symbolic point of origin for his journey. Located in the Bekaa Valley of present-day Lebanon, Baalbek is famous for its monumental Roman ruins — the Temple of Jupiter, the Temple of Bacchus — which form an imposing backdrop to [[khalid|Khalid]]'s humble beginnings. The village represents traditional Eastern life: family, religion, social constraint, and the beauty of the [[nature-as-temple|Lebanese landscape]].

## Significance

Baalbek embodies both what [[khalid|Khalid]] [[love-sacred-and-profane|love]]s and what he must escape. It is the site of his childhood, his first [[love-sacred-and-profane|love]] ([[najma|Najma]]), and his early religious education. But it is also the source of the constraints — familial, social, religious — that drive him to emigrate. The "mountain-road to the [[mount-lebanon|Cedars of Lebanon]]" (004-004) begins in Baalbek and leads outward, into the unknown.

Baalbek's Roman ruins serve as an implicit symbol of the layered history of Lebanon — Phoenician, Roman, Arab, Ottoman — and of the possibility of cultural synthesis that [[khalid|Khalid]] will later embody.
