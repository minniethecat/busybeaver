# Boss O'Donohue

## Role in the Book

Boss O'Donohue (referred to as "the Boss") is the [[tammany-hall|Tammany Hall]] political boss who represents the corrupt reality of [[democracy-and-its-dowry|American democracy]]. He recruits [[khalid|Khalid]] as a Syrian community canvasser through his subordinate [[mr-hoolihan|Mr. Hoolihan]], then destroys him when [[khalid|Khalid]] takes ideas seriously. His scornful question — "What do you mean by immanent morality?" (014-019) — is the book's most concise statement of the collision between philosophy and power.

## Biographical Sketch

O'Donohue is a [[tammany-hall|Tammany Hall]] boss of presumably Irish extraction, wielding power through the Democratic Party machine. He commands a network of henchmen ([[mr-hoolihan|Mr. Hoolihan]] is his Secretary and "first henchman"). His power is absolute within his domain, and his response to [[khalid|Khalid]]'s philosophical idealism — contempt followed by legal persecution — reveals the ruthlessness of machine politics.
