# Mr. Hoolihan

## Role in the Book

Mr. Hoolihan is the Secretary to [[boss-odonohue|Boss O'Donohue]] and the bureaucratic functionary who sends [[khalid|Khalid]] the official notification of a "Canvassership." His letter — "a high post, indeed, you fond and pitiful dreamer" (014-007) — represents the seductive surface of machine politics: official, impressive, and ultimately hollow.

Hoolihan is the middleman of corruption — not the source of power but its conduit. He embodies the banality of political evil: a functionary who processes papers and sends letters, indifferent to the human consequences of the machine he serves.

## Biographical Sketch

Hoolihan is "Secretary to [[boss-odonohue|Mr. O'Donohue]], who is first henchman to [[boss-odonohue|the Boss]]" (014-007). He occupies a subordinate position in the [[tammany-hall|Tammany]] hierarchy but wields real power as gatekeeper. His letter to [[khalid|Khalid]] triggers the chain of events leading to [[khalid|Khalid]]'s political awakening and imprisonment.
