# The Ulema

## Role in the Book

The Ulema (Arabic: "the learned ones") are the Islamic religious scholars and jurists who hold spiritual and social authority in [[damascus|Damascus]]. [[khalid|Khalid]] confronts them directly in Book III, challenging their monopoly on religious interpretation and their alliance with political power. They represent institutional religion — the force that has buried the "lofty [[oriental-spirituality|spirituality of the Orient]]" beneath "thick, obscene growths."

## Significance

The Ulema are the Eastern counterpart to [[tammany-hall|Tammany Hall]] — an institutional structure that has lost its spiritual purpose and become a mechanism of social control. [[khalid|Khalid]]'s message bypasses their authority entirely, addressing the people directly: "Come out of the superstitions of the sheikhs and ulema" (031-007).
