# The Jesuits

## Role in the Book

The Jesuits (Society of Jesus) appear as the educators of [[khalid|Khalid]]'s youth in Lebanon. Their college in [[beirut|Beirut]] represents Western intellectual and religious influence in the Ottoman East. The Jesuit education gives [[khalid|Khalid]] his discipline of thought and his exposure to Western philosophy — tools he later uses to critique both Eastern and Western societies.

## Significance

The Jesuit presence in Lebanon is part of the broader pattern of Western missionary and educational activity in the Ottoman Empire. For [[khalid|Khalid]], Jesuit education is a double-edged gift: it opens his mind to free thought while binding him to a Western intellectual tradition that he must eventually transcend. His later synthesis of [[oriental-spirituality|Eastern spirituality]] and Western rationalism is made possible by — and is also a reaction against — his Jesuit training.
