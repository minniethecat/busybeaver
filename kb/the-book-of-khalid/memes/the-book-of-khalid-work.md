# The Book of Khalid

## Role in the Book

*The Book of Khalid* is itself a character in its own narrative. The Al-Fatihah (Opening Word) presents it as a found manuscript, a "book of travels in an impalpable country" (001-004). Throughout, the narrator comments on the act of writing and the nature of the book, creating a meta-literary frame that distinguishes it from conventional novels.

## Significance

Published in 1911 by Dodd, Mead and Company, *The Book of Khalid* is widely considered the first Arab-[[new-york-city|America]]n novel. It defies genre classification: it is part philosophical treatise, part immigrant narrative, part spiritual autobiography, and part political critique. Its self-consciousness about its own form — the book as "[[book-as-propylon|propylon]]" or gateway — makes it a precursor to modernist experiments in narrative.

## Key Passages

> "This then is a book of travels in an impalpable country, an enchanted country, from which we have all risen, and towards which we are still rising. It is, as it were, the chart and history of one little kingdom of the Soul — the Soul of a philosopher, poet and criminal."
>
> — (001-004)
