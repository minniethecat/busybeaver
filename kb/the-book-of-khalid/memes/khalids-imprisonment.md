# Khalid's Imprisonment

## Role in the Book

[[khalid|Khalid]]'s imprisonment is the climactic event of his [[new-york-city|America]]n experience. After his idealistic letter offends the [[tammany-hall|Tammany Hall]] Boss, he is arrested on charges of "misappropriation of public funds" and escorted "across the Bridge of Sighs to gaol" (014-020). For ten days and nights, he is confined in the Tombs — the notorious [[new-york-city|New York City]] prison — where he writes the chapter "In Prison" in the [[the-kl-manuscript|K.L. MS.]]

## Significance

The imprisonment transforms [[khalid|Khalid]] from political naïf to critical observer of [[democracy-and-its-dowry|American democracy]]. The "ten days and nights — and it might have been ten months were it not for his devoted and steadfast friend" [[shakib|Shakib]] — become a philosophical retreat, an enforced contemplation. [[khalid|Khalid]] emerges not broken but clarified: the gap between democratic ideals and political reality can no longer be ignored.
