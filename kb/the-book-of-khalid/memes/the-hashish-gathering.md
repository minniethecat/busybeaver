# The Hashish Gathering

## Role in the Book

The Hashish Gathering is a pivotal early episode in Book I, Chapter I. Under the influence of hashish, [[khalid|Khalid]] delivers an impromptu philosophical discourse that provokes both laughter and admiration from his companions. It is here that he is first called "[[dervish-philosophy|the dervish]] of science" and "the ropedancer of metaphysics" (004-008) — epithets that will define his public identity throughout the book.

## Significance

The gathering is [[khalid|Khalid]]'s "call" moment — the first public expression of his philosophical nature. The "chorus of laughter" is both mockery and acknowledgment. The altered state of consciousness (hashish) suggests that philosophical vision requires the suspension of normal perception — a theme that recurs throughout the book in different forms ([[bohemianism|Bohemia]]n intoxication, mountain-top exaltation, prison contemplation).

## Key Passages

> "And the chorus of laughter, which is part and parcel of a hashish jag, was tremendous. Everyone thereupon had something to say on the subject. The contagion could not be checked. And Khalid was called 'the dervish of science' by one; 'the ropedancer of metaphysics' by another."
>
> — (004-008)
