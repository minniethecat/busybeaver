# Najma

## Role in the Book

Najma is Khalid's cousin and first [[love-sacred-and-profane|love]], the woman he "[[love-sacred-and-profane|love]]d... and whom he was not permitted to marry" (005-012). Though she appears only briefly and indirectly in the narrative, her absence is as significant as her presence. She represents the lost possibility of rooted [[love-sacred-and-profane|love]] — a life [[khalid|Khalid]] might have lived had he remained in Lebanon and accepted its social constraints.

Najma functions as a psychological touchstone. When [[khalid|Khalid]] encounters other women — in [[bohemianism|Bohemia]], in [[new-york-city|New York]], in [[damascus|Damascus]] — they are measured against her. The emigrant girl on the ship who "much resembles his cousin Najma" (006-013) triggers memory and longing. Najma is the [[love-sacred-and-profane|love]] that was denied, the wound that fuels the journey.

## Biographical Sketch

Little is revealed about Najma's own life. She is Khalid's cousin in [[baalbek|Baalbek]]. Family prohibition prevents their marriage — the reasons are not specified, but the implication is that social or familial considerations overrode personal [[love-sacred-and-profane|desire]]. Her fate after [[khalid|Khalid]]'s departure is not recorded.

Her name — Najma, Arabic for "star" — carries symbolic weight. She is the fixed point by which [[khalid|Khalid]] navigates, even when he can no longer see her.
