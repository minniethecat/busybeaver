# Nature as Temple

## Definition

Nature as Temple is the concept, woven throughout *[[the-book-of-khalid-work|The Book of Khalid]]*, that the Lebanese landscape — its mountains, cedars, valleys, and skies — constitutes a sacred space more authentic than any built temple, mosque, or church. [[khalid|Khalid]]'s deepest spiritual experiences occur not in houses of worship but on mountain peaks, in cedar groves, and beneath open skies. Nature is the original revelation, older and truer than scripture.

This concept draws on multiple traditions: the [[mount-lebanon|Cedars of Lebanon]] as Biblical symbol, the Romantic sublime (Wordsworth's nature mysticism), and the pre-Islamic Arabian reverence for natural sacred sites. Rihani synthesizes these into a distinctively Lebanese environmental spirituality — the landscape itself as the text of the divine.

## Development in the Book

The Cedars appear early as a symbol of what [[khalid|Khalid]] leaves behind: "from [[mount-lebanon|the mountain-road to the Cedars]] of Lebanon" (004-004) marks the threshold between his old life and the unknown. The "palingenesis" (spiritual rebirth) that [[khalid|Khalid]] seeks is associated from the beginning with the Lebanese highlands.

During [[the-atlantic-voyage|the emigration voyage]], the loss of nature is felt acutely. The "City on the Hills of the Cedar Groves" (006-011) is glimpsed only in memory as the ship pulls away. In [[new-york-city|New York]], [[khalid|Khalid]] retreats to [[battery-park|Battery Park]] — the only nature available — carving his name on benches, seeking in urban greenery a substitute for the forests of home.

Book III marks the return to nature. [[khalid|Khalid]]'s ascent of the mountain is the book's most sustained nature mysticism: "From the cliffs 'neath which the lily blooms to the cliffs round which the eagles flit — what a glorious promontory! What a contrast at this height, in this immensity, to the depths and the density of [[new-york-city|the City]]!" (029-003). The mountain becomes both physical place and spiritual state — elevation as enlightenment.

In [[damascus|Damascus]], [[khalid|Khalid]]'s message is grounded in natural imagery. The "cedars of Lebanon" — "as famous as the [[mount-lebanon|Cedars of Lebanon]]" (007-003) — become a rhetorical resource, a symbol of authentic Lebanese identity uncorrupted by urban and institutional religion.
