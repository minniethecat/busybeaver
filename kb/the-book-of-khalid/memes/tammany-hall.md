# Tammany Hall

## Role in the Book

Tammany Hall is the Democratic Party political machine that dominated [[new-york-city|New York City]] politics in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. In the book, it represents the corrupt reality beneath [[new-york-city|America]]n democratic ideals. [[khalid|Khalid]] is recruited as a canvasser for the Syrian immigrant community, only to be imprisoned when his philosophical idealism offends [[boss-odonohue|the Boss]].

## Significance

Tammany Hall is the institutional antagonist of the book's political philosophy. It is the "Wigwam" where power is brokered without principle, where ethnic communities are manipulated for votes, and where anyone who takes ideas seriously is destroyed. The Boss's dismissal of "immanent morality" as "the morality of hayseeds, and priests, and philosophical fools" (014-019) is Tammany's creed.
