# Hashish Vision

## Definition

The Hashish Vision is a pivotal early episode in *[[the-book-of-khalid-work|The Book of Khalid]]* where a hashish-induced state of expanded consciousness reveals [[khalid|Khalid]]'s philosophical nature to himself and his companions. During a gathering, [[khalid|Khalid]] delivers an impromptu metaphysical discourse that provokes both laughter and recognition — it is here that he is first called "[[dervish-philosophy|the dervish]] of science" (004-008).

The episode establishes altered consciousness as a legitimate mode of philosophical insight in Rihani's world. Unlike Western rationalism, which privileges sober analysis, the hashish vision suggests that truth may require the suspension of ordinary perception. The "chorus of laughter, which is part and parcel of a hashish jag" (004-008) is both mockery and acknowledgment — the group recognizes something genuine even as they ridicule it.

## Development in the Book

The hashish vision occurs early in Book I, before [[khalid|Khalid]] leaves Lebanon. It functions as a kind of prophetic call — the moment when his philosophical vocation is revealed, albeit in a form that nobody (including [[khalid|Khalid]] himself) fully understands. The epithets bestowed during [[the-hashish-gathering|the vision]] — "[[dervish-philosophy|the dervish]] of science," "the ropedancer of metaphysics" — become the templates for his later identity.

The episode also introduces the theme of intoxication as revelation, which recurs in [[bohemianism|Bohemia]] (where other intoxicants — [[love-sacred-and-profane|love]], art, ambition — serve similar functions) and in the mountain-top visions of Book III. The hashish vision is the prototype of [[khalid|Khalid]]'s characteristic mode of knowing: altered, ecstatic, outside social norms.
