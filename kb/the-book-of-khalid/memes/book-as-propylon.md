# Book as Propylon

## Definition

"Book as Propylon" captures the meta-literary self-conception at the heart of *[[the-book-of-khalid-work|The Book of Khalid]]*. In the Al-Fatihah (Opening Word), the narrator declares: "I am all together and nothing: a propylon through which my race and those above and below my race are brought together" (001-005). The book presents itself not as a finished work of art but as a gateway — a "propylon" (Greek: entrance gate) — through which civilizations, epochs, and peoples may encounter one another.

This is a radical literary gesture. Rihani refuses the conventional authorial posture of mastery. Instead, he positions the book — and himself — as a conduit, a passage, a threshold. The book is not the destination but the door. Its value lies not in what it contains but in what it enables: the meeting of [[east-west-duality|East and West]], past and future, self and other.

## Development in the Book

The propylon conceit is established in the opening pages and never revisited explicitly, yet it governs the entire narrative. Every episode in [[khalid|Khalid]]'s life — his emigration, his [[bohemianism|Bohemia]]n period, his imprisonment, his return — is an act of passage, a crossing between worlds. The book's structure (three Books, each a phase of the soul's journey) reflects the propylon architecture: three successive gateways.

The propylon also explains the book's genre-defying nature. It is neither novel nor philosophy nor travelogue but a hybrid form that exists to facilitate passage between genres, just as [[khalid|Khalid]] exists to facilitate passage between cultures. The book is "a book of travels in an impalpable country" (001-004) — not a record of having arrived but an invitation to journey.
