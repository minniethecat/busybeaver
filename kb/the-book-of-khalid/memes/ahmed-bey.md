# Ahmed Bey

## Role in the Book

Ahmed Bey is a Syrian friend who appears in Book III as [[khalid|Khalid]]'s social connector in [[beirut|Beirut]]. He introduces [[khalid|Khalid]] to [[mrs-gotfry|Mrs. Gotfry]], the [[new-york-city|America]]n Baha'i votary, facilitating one of the book's most significant cross-cultural encounters. Ahmed Bey represents the cosmopolitan Easterner — comfortable in both Ottoman and European social circles, fluent in the languages of diplomacy and hospitality.

## Biographical Sketch

Ahmed Bey is a member of the Syrian elite, bearing the Ottoman title "Bey." He moves easily between Eastern and Western social worlds and serves as a bridge for [[khalid|Khalid]]'s re-entry into Levantine society after his years in [[new-york-city|America]].
