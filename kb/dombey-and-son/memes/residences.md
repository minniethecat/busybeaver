# Residences

Houses in *[[Dombey and Son|[[dombey|[[paul|paul-dombey-jr]]-dombey]]-and-son-firm]]* are never merely settings. They are expressions of character, indices of social standing, and — in the case of [[the Dombey mansion|dombey-house]] — prisons of the heart. Dickens uses domestic spaces to reveal what his characters cannot or will not say. This page aggregates all entries tagged "residence."

## About This List

From the imposing Dombey house on its "dismal" street to the snug parlour of [[the Wooden Midshipman|the-wooden-midshipman]], the novel's residences map its moral geography. Each dwelling collected here tells a story about the people who inhabit it — their aspirations, their anxieties, and their capacity for [[love|love]].
