# Childhood and Upbringing

Few Victorian novels are as fixated on [[childhood|childhood]] as *[[Dombey and Son|[[dombey|paul-dombey]]-and-son-firm]]* — and few are as bleak about what adults do to children. [[Little Paul|paul-dombey-jr]] Dombey, starved of warmth and sent too early to [[Dr. Blimber|education]]'s hothouse academy, is the novel's most famous casualty. But the question of how children are raised echoes through every corner of the book: in [[Florence|florence-dombey]]'s dogged pursuit of a father who cannot see her, in the young [[Walter Gay|walter-gay]]'s hunger for adventure, in the fates of servant children and abandoned waifs. This page aggregates all entries tagged "childhood."

## About This List

The entries collected here span the institutions that shape children ([[Brighton|brighton]], [[Dr. Blimber's school|dr-blimbers-school]]), the failures of parental care ([[neglect|parental-neglect]], abduction), and the moments of nurture that break through the novel's prevailing coldness. Together they form a sustained meditation on what it means to grow up in a world that prizes [[commerce|money-and-commerce]] over care.
