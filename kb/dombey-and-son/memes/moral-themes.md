# Moral Themes

The moral architecture of *[[Dombey and Son|[[dombey|[[paul|paul-dombey-jr]]-dombey]]-and-son-firm]]* is built around the question of what it means to be a good person — and what it costs to become one. [[Pride|pride]], the novel's cardinal sin, must be broken before [[love|love]] can enter; characters who cannot [[change|time-and-change]] are destroyed, while those who can are transformed. ## About This List

The moral themes of *Dombey and Son* are embodied rather than merely stated. Paul Dombey's pride is not an abstract concept but a lived condition that shapes every relationship he has. [[Florence|florence-dombey]]'s [[forgiveness|forgiveness]] is not a sermon but a practice. The entries collected here trace these ethical threads through character, event, and explicit thematic discussion alike.
