# Love and Marriage

[[Marriage|marriage]] in *[[Dombey and Son|dombey-and-son-firm]]* is rarely what it should be. The novel presents a spectrum of unions: the cold transactional bargain of [[Paul Dombey|[[paul|paul-dombey-jr]]-dombey]] and [[Edith|edith-dombey]], the mercenary scheming of those who marry for advancement, and — in [[Walter|walter-gay]] and [[Florence|florence-dombey]] — the quiet promise of something founded on genuine [[affection|love]]. Dickens is unsparing in his critique of the marriage market, yet he never abandons the hope that love might find a way. This page aggregates all entries tagged "marriage."

## About This List

The entries collected here follow the novel's major marital plotlines — from Dombey's disastrous second courtship to the modest wedding that closes the book. Together they reveal a novelist for whom the question of who marries whom, and why, is never merely romantic: it is the question of what a society values, and what it destroys in the name of propriety.
