# Business and Commerce

The world of *[[Dombey and Son|dombey-and-son-firm]]* is built on trade. The firm of Dombey and Son is not merely a [[family|family-and-kinship]] [[business|money-and-commerce]] — it is the organising principle of [[Paul Dombey|[[paul|paul-dombey-jr]]-dombey]]'s life, the measure by which he judges all value, and the engine whose failure reduces him from titan to ruin. Dickens knew the City of London intimately, and his portrait of commercial life is animated by a deep ambivalence: admiration for honest enterprise, and horror at a system that converts human beings into instruments of profit. ## About This List

The entries collected here span the physical sites of commerce ([[the Dombey warehouse|dombeys-warehouse]], [[the Wooden Midshipman|the-wooden-midshipman]], [[Solomon Gills's shop|solomon-gills-shop]]) and the human institutions that animate them. Together they map a world in which everything — [[love|love]], loyalty, even life itself — can be priced, and in which the most precious things turn out to have no price at all.
