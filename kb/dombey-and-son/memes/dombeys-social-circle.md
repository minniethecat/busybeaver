# Dombey's Social Circle

## Overview

The network of social climbers and hangers-on that surrounds [[Dombey|[[paul|paul-dombey-jr]]-dombey]] in his prosperity and abandons him in his ruin.

## Role in the Novel

Operating in London, this social appears from Section 1 to Section 148, involving key characters in its orbit.

- [[Paul Dombey Sr.]]
- [[Major Bagstock]]
- [[Mrs. Skewton]]

## Significance

This social contributes to Dickens's panoramic portrait of Victorian society and its institutions.
