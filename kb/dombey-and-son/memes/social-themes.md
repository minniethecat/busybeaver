# Social Themes

*[[Dombey and Son|[[dombey|[[paul|paul-dombey-jr]]-dombey]]-and-son-firm]]* is as much a novel of social criticism as it is a [[family|family-and-kinship]] drama. Dickens anatomises a society in which [[money|money-and-commerce]] has become the measure of all worth — where a shipping merchant can believe himself a kind of god, and where the poor are invisible until they become useful. ## About This List

The entries collected here range from explicit social critiques ("[[class and society|class-and-society]]," "[[the railway|the-railway]]" as a force of social [[change|time-and-change]]) to the quieter social commentary embedded in character studies and domestic scenes. Together they map Dickens's comprehensive indictment of a society organized around commerce and status rather than human connection.
