# Servants in Dombey and Son

Servants occupy a distinctive position in *[[Dombey and Son|[[dombey|[[paul|paul-dombey-jr]]-dombey]]-and-son-firm]]* — at once intimate members of the household and perpetual outsiders to its emotional life. Dickens uses servant characters to expose the private realities of the families they serve, often granting them a moral clarity denied to their employers.
