# Domestic Life

The houses of *[[Dombey and Son|[[dombey|[[paul|paul-dombey-jr]]-dombey]]-and-son-firm]]* are maps of the souls who inhabit them. [[The Dombey mansion|dombey-house]] on its "dark, cold" corner speaks of its master's frozen heart; [[Miss Tox|miss-tox]]'s modest establishment radiates anxious gentility; [[Mrs. Pipchin|mrs-pipchin]]'s boarding-house is a fortress of grim discipline. Dickens understood that domestic spaces are never merely settings — they are arguments about how life should be lived. This page aggregates all entries tagged "domestic."

## About This List

The entries collected here range from the grandeur of [[the Dombey household|the-dombey-household]] to the cramped decency of the [[Chick|mr-chick]] establishment, from the cold comfort of Mrs. Pipchin's to the modest warmth of [[the Wooden Midshipman|the-wooden-midshipman]]. Together they trace a novel's worth of thinking about what makes a house a home — and what happens when it fails to become one.
