# Discovery of the Betrayal

The shattering revelation that the party has been deceived by [[harris|Harris]], the American guide who has led them not toward the coast of South America but deep into equatorial [[africa|Africa]], directly into the hands of [[slavery|slave-trade]]rs.

## Overview

- **Type**: Betrayal
- **Chapters**: 26–28
- **Key participants**: [[dick-sands|Dick Sands]], [[harris|Harris]], [[tom|Tom]], [[mrs-weldon|Mrs. Weldon]]
- **Location**: [[africa|Equatorial Africa]]

## Narrative

The party has been traveling under [[harris|Harris]]'s guidance, believing they are approaching the coast of Bolivia. Suspicious details accumulate: the unfamiliar vegetation, the absence of expected landmarks, and Old [[tom|Tom]]'s growing unease. [[tom|Tom]], who was born in [[africa|Africa]], begins to recognize things from his childhood.

The discovery crystallizes when the party finds human remains with iron fetters. [[tom|Tom]], seeing the "forks" and "chains," is transported back to his enslaved past, repeating "Long ago ... forks ... chains!" in horror (026-014–019). [[dick-sands|Dick]] realizes the truth: "Yes, it is too true; we are in [[africa|Africa]]! in equatorial [[africa|Africa]]! in the land of slavery! in the very haunt of slave-drivers!" (026-071–072).

[[harris|Harris]], confronted, drops all pretense. He reveals that [[negoro|Negoro]] sabotaged the compass: "one night the compass was tampered with, so that the *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]*... was carried altogether out of her course" (028-120–121). The entire journey has been a carefully orchestrated trap.

## Significance

This revelation transforms the novel from a maritime survival story into a captivity narrative. Every assumption the party held—about their location, about [[harris|Harris]]'s identity, about their chances of rescue—collapses. The discovery also validates Verne's extensive historical exposition on the [[slavery|slave trade]] that opens Part II, connecting the abstract horror of the [[slavery|slave trade]] to the immediate peril of the protagonists.

> "Yes, it is too true; we are in Africa! in equatorial Africa! in the land of slavery!" (026-071–072)
