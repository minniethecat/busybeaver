# Weldon & Co.

The [[san-francisco|San Francisco]] whaling and shipping enterprise owned by James W. [[mrs-weldon|Weldon]], whose fleet includes the brig *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]* under [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]]'s command.

## Overview

- **Type**: Shipping firm / whaling enterprise
- **Active period**: Mid-19th century (ante-bellum through post-bellum)
- **Key members**: James W. [[mrs-weldon|Weldon]] (owner), [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]] (master of the *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]*)
- **Chapters**: Referenced throughout (especially ch001)

## Operations

Weldon & Co. operated a fleet of whaling vessels that plied both polar regions. James W. [[mrs-weldon|Weldon]] "was accustomed every season to send his [[right-whale|whale]]rs both to the Arctic and Antarctic seas" (001-197–198). The firm was substantial enough to maintain multiple vessels and employ experienced captains like [[captain-hull|Hull]].

The *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]*, a whaling brig, was one of the firm's vessels, returning from a season in the southern [[right-whale|whale]]-fisheries when the novel opens. Her cargo included "an ample cargo of cut and uncut [[right-whale|whale]]bone" (001-227), though the [[right-whale|whale]] oil yield had been disappointing—"the supply of [[right-whale|whale]]s that [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]] had procured had fallen far short of his expectations" (001-236).

## The Weldon Family

James W. [[mrs-weldon|Weldon]]'s wife, [[mrs-weldon|Mrs. Weldon]], is aboard the *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]* with their young son Jack, returning from a visit to [[new-zealand|New Zealand]]. The [[mrs-weldon|Weldon]] family's commercial interests thus intersect directly with the novel's action. [[james-weldon|James Weldon]]'s plan for [[dick-sands|Dick Sands]]—to "let him have systematic training in navigation, so that he may be able ultimately to become a captain" (006-097–099)—shows the firm's investment in developing maritime talent.

## Significance

Weldon & Co. represents the legitimate commercial world that the protagonists are torn from. The contrast between the orderly business of whaling and the lawless world of the [[slavery|slave trade]] is one of the novel's implicit moral structures.

> "He was accustomed every season to send his whalers both to the Arctic and Antarctic seas." (001-197–198)
