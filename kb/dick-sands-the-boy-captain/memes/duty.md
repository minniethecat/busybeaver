# Duty

The moral and professional obligation to fulfill one's responsibilities, the ethical principle that anchors the characters' actions throughout *[[dick-sands|Dick Sands]]: The Boy [[captain-hull|Captain]]*.

## Definition

Duty, in the novel's 19th-century context, encompasses both professional obligation (the sailor's duty to his ship and captain) and moral obligation (the duty to protect the vulnerable, to keep one's word, to resist evil). It is the internal compass that guides conduct when external authority is absent.

## Role in the Novel

Duty defines [[dick-sands|Dick Sands]]' character. The crew follows orders not only because it is "duty, but because they were" personally loyal (003-044). [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]] praises [[dick-sands|Dick]]'s professional ethic: "Nothing distracts him from his duty; he is as much to be" trusted as any seasoned officer (006-109). When [[dick-sands|Dick]] assumes command, the crew assures him: "There is no fear but that you will do your duty" (012-026).

[[dick-sands|Dick]]'s "moral energy and sense of duty" are what sustain him through the crisis (025-149). The [[ships-log|log]]—a tool of professional duty—becomes symbolic: "The [[ships-log|log]] was fastened to" its line, a last remnant of proper maritime procedure (015-082). Even in extremis, [[dick-sands|Dick]] reminds himself: "duty, and it will be time enough for" fear later (017-060).
