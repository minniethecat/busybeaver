# Right Whale

The species of baleen whale that was the primary target of 19th-century whaling and whose declining population frames the economic context of the *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]*'s voyage.

## Overview

- **Type**: Species
- **Species**: Right whale (*Eubalaena* spp.)
- **Associated with**: The whaling industry, the *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]*'s voyage
- **Chapters**: 1, 8–9

## Role in the Novel

The Right whale is central to the novel's economic premise. Verne notes that "the whale known as 'the Right whale,' the object of repeated attacks, had become very scarce" (001-230). This scarcity explains [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]]'s disappointing catch and the *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]*'s early return.

The species appears directly in the whale hunt sequence. A whale is sighted, and [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]] decides to pursue it despite being short-handed—a decision that leads to the [[loss-of-captain-hull|whaleboat disaster]]. The whale "seemed utterly unconscious of" the approaching boats (001-093), but the hunt ends catastrophically when the whale capsizes the boat, killing [[captain-hull|Hull]] and the crew.

## Whaling Context

The Right whale was so named by whalers because it was the "right" whale to hunt: it floated when killed, yielded large quantities of oil and whalebone, and was relatively slow-moving. However, these same characteristics made it vulnerable to overexploitation. By 1878, the species had been hunted to commercial scarcity in many traditional grounds.
