# Dick Sands

The fifteen-year-old orphan apprentice aboard the whaling brig *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]* who must assume command when [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]] and the crew perish at sea, becoming the "boy captain" of Verne's novel.

## Role & Identity

- **Role**: Apprentice seaman aboard the *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]*; acting captain after the [[loss-of-captain-hull|whaleboat disaster]]
- **Affiliation**: Crew of the *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]*; protégé of the [[mrs-weldon|Weldon]] family
- **First appearance**: Chapter 1
- **Last appearance**: Chapter 51

## Background

Dick Sands is an orphan who has been trained in a "hard school" (006-103). Found as an abandoned infant, he was raised by charitable strangers and went to sea at a young age. His natural intelligence and diligence impressed [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]], who mentored him, and James W. [[mrs-weldon|Weldon]], who planned to send him for formal training after the voyage. [[mrs-weldon|Mrs. Weldon]] observes that "it is quite wonderful how much he knows about a ship" for his age (006-094).

## Character

"Dick Sands was truly courageous; he was one of the daring" (003-106). [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]] describes him as "as modest as he is courageous" (009-133). His defining traits are his sense of duty—"nothing distracts him from his duty" (006-109)—and his "moral energy and sense of duty that kept him" through crisis (025-149).

Dick combined theoretical study with practical experience. He applied himself to "the branches of mathematics as were necessary for the science of navigation" while spending his leisure reading "every book of adventure in travel that came in his way" (003-142–144). He "did not omit duly to combine the practical with the theoretical" (003-145).
