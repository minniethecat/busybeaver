[051-001]The anxious watchers on the bank instantly discerned [[dick-sands|Dick]]'s purpose,

[051-002]and understood that in order to save them he had formed the resolution

[051-003]of precipitating himself with the savages into the seething waters.

[051-004]Nothing could avail to arrest the swift descent. [[mrs-weldon|Mrs Weldon]] in an agony

[051-005]of despair waved her hands in a last sad farewell, Jack and Benedict

[051-006]seemed paralyzed, whilst Hercules involuntarily extended his great

[051-007]strong arm that was powerless to aid.

[051-008]Suddenly the natives, impelled by a last frantic effort to reach the

[051-009]shore, plunged into the water, but then movement capsized the boat.

[051-010]Face to face with death, Dick lost nothing of his indomitable presence

[051-011]of mind. Might not that light canoe, floating bottom upwards, be made

[051-012]the means for yet another grasp at life? The danger that threatened him

[051-013]was twofold, there was the risk of suffocation as well as the peril of

[051-014]being drowned; could not the inverted canoe be used for a kind of float

[051-015]at once to keep his head above water and to serve as a screen from the

[051-016]rushing air? He had some faint recollection of how it had been proved

[051-017]possible under some such conditions to descend in safety the falls of

[051-018]Niagara.

[051-019]Quick as lightning he seized hold of the cross-bench of the canoe, and

[051-020]with his head out of water beneath the upturned keel, he was dashed

[051-021]down the furious and well-nigh perpendicular fall.

[051-022]The craft sank deep into the abyss, but rose quickly again to the

[051-023]surface. Here was Dick's chance, he was a good swimmer, and his life

[051-024]depended now upon his strength of arm.

[051-025]It was a hard struggle, but he succeeded. In a quarter of an hour he

[051-026]had landed on the left hand bank, where he was greeted with the joyful

[051-027]congratulations of his friends, who had hurried to the foot of the fall

[051-028]to assure themselves of his fate.

[051-029][Illustration: The bullet shattered the rudder scull into fragments]

[051-030]The cannibals had all disappeared in the surging waters. Unprotected in

[051-031]their fall, they had doubtless ceased to breathe before reaching the

[051-032]lowest depths of the cataract where their lifeless bodies would soon be

[051-033]dashed to pieces against the sharp rocks that were scattered along the

[051-034]lower course of the stream.

[051-035]CHAPTER XX.

[051-036]A HAPPY REUNION.

[051-037]Two days after Dick's marvellous [[rescue|deliverance]] the party had the good

[051-038]fortune to fall in with a [[slave-caravan|caravan]] of honest Portuguese ivory-traders on

[051-039]their way to Emboma, at the mouth of the Congo. They rendered the

[051-040]fugitives every assistance, and thus enabled them to reach the coast

[051-041]without further discomfort.

[051-042]This meeting with the caravan was a most fortunate occurrence, as any

[051-043]project of launching a raft upon the Zaire would have been quite

[051-044]impracticable, the river between the Ntemo and Yellala Falls being a

[051-045]continuous series of cataracts. Stanley counted as many as sixty-two,

[051-046]and it was hereabouts that that brave traveller sustained the last of

[051-047]thirty-one conflicts with the natives, escaping almost by a miracle

[051-048]from the Mbelo cataract.

[051-049]Before the middle of August the party arrived at Emboma, where they

[051-050]were hospitably received by M. Motta Viega and Mr. Harrison. A steamer

[051-051]was just on the point of starting for the Isthmus of Panama; in this

[051-052]they took their passage, and in due time set foot once more upon

[051-053]American soil.

[051-054]Forthwith a message was despatched to Mr. Weldon, apprising him of the

[051-055]return of the wife and child over whose loss he had mourned so long On

[051-056]the 25th the railroad deposited the travellers at [[san-francisco|San Francisco]], the

[051-057]only thing to mar their happiness being the recollection that Tom and

[051-058]his partners were not with them to share their joy.

[051-059]Mr. Weldon had every reason to congratulate himself that [[negoro|Negoro]] had

[051-060]failed to reach him. No doubt he would have been ready to sacrifice the

[051-061]bulk of his fortune, and without a moment's hesitation would have set

[051-062]out for the coast of [[africa|Africa]], but who could question that he would there

[051-063]have been exposed to the vilest treachery? He felt that to Dick Sands

[051-064]and to Hercules he owed a debt of gratitude that it would be impossible

[051-065]to repay; Dick assumed more than ever the place of an adopted son,

[051-066]whilst the brave negro was regarded as a true and faithful friend.

[051-067]Cousin Benedict, it must be owned, failed to share for long the general

[051-068]joy. After giving Mr. Weldon a hasty shake of the hand, he hurried off

[051-069]to his private room, and resumed his studies almost as if they had

[051-070]never been interrupted. He set himself vigorously to work with the

[051-071]design of producing an elaborate treatise upon the "Hexapodes

[051-072]Benedictus" hitherto unknown to entomological research. Here in his

[051-073]private chamber spectacles and magnifying-glass were ready for his use,

[051-074]and he was now able for the first time with the aid of proper

[051-075]appliances to examine the unique production of Central Africa.

[051-076]A shriek of horror and disappointment escaped his lips. The Hexapodes

[051-077]Benedictus was not a hexapod at all. It was a common spider. Hercules,

[051-078]in catching it, had unfortunately broken off its two front legs, and

[051-079]Benedict, almost blind as he was, had failed to detect the accident.

[051-080]His chagrin was most pitiable, the wonderful discovery that was to have

[051-081]exalted his name high in the annals of science belonged simply to the

[051-082]common order of the arachnidæ The blow to his aspirations was very

[051-083]heavy; it brought on a fit of illness from which it took him some time

[051-084]to recover.

[051-085]For the next three years Dick was entrusted with the education of

[051-086]little Jack during the intervals he could spare from the prosecution of

[051-087]his own studies, into which he threw himself with an energy quickened

[051-088]by a kind of remorse.

[051-089]"If only I had known what a seaman ought to know when I was left to

[051-090]myself on board the '[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]],'" he would continually say, "what misery

[051-091]and suffering we might have been spared!"

[051-092]So diligently did he apply himself to the technical branches of his

[051-093]profession that at the age of eighteen he received a special

[051-094]certificate of honour, and was at once raised to the rank of a captain

[051-095]in Mr. Weldon's firm.

[051-096]Thus by his industry and good conduct did the poor foundling of Sandy

[051-097]Hook rise to a post of distinction. In spite of his youth, he commanded

[051-098]universal respect; his native modesty and straightforwardness never

[051-099]failed him, and for his own part, he seemed to be unconscious of those

[051-100]fine traits in his character which had impelled him to deeds that made

[051-101]him little short of a hero.

[051-102]His leisure moments, however, were often troubled by one source of

[051-103]sadness; he could never forget the four negroes for whose misfortunes

[051-104]he held himself by his own inexperience to be in a way responsible.

[051-105]Mrs. Weldon thoroughly shared his regret, and would have made many

[051-106]sacrifices to discover what had become of them. This anxiety was at

[051-107]length relieved.

[051-108]Owing to the large correspondence of Mr. Weldon in almost every quarter

[051-109]of the world, it was discovered that the whole of them had been sold in

[051-110]one lot, and that they were now in Madagascar. Without listening for a

[051-111]moment to Dick's proposal to apply all his savings to effect their

[051-112][[freedom|liberation]], Mr. Weldon set his own agents to negotiate for their

[051-113]freedom, and on the 15th of November, 1877, Tom, Bat, Actæon, and

[051-114]Austin awaited their welcome at the merchant's door. It is needless to

[051-115]say how warm were the greetings they received.

[051-116]Out of all the survivors of the "Pilgrim" that had been cast upon the

[051-117]fatal coast of Africa, old Nan alone was wanting to complete the

[051-118]number. Considering what they had all undergone, and the perils to

[051-119]which they had been exposed, it seemed little short of a miracle that

[051-120]she and poor [[dingo|Dingo]] should be the only victims.

[051-121]High was the festivity that night in the house of the Californian

[051-122]merchant, and the toast, proposed at Mrs. Weldon's request, that was

[051-123]received with the loudest acclamation was

[051-124]"DICK SANDS, THE BOY CAPTAIN!"

[051-125]THE END.
