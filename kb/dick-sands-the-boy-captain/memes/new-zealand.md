# New Zealand

The country from whose port the *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]* departs at the start of the novel. The schooner had been on a whaling expedition when it put in at [[auckland|Auckland]], where [[mrs-weldon|Mrs. Weldon]], her son Jack, and [[benedict|Cousin Benedict]] boarded for the return voyage to [[san-francisco|San Francisco]].

## Geography

- **Type**: Island country / port of call
- **Real or fictional**: Real
- **Relevant to**: [[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]'s voyage origin / boarding point
- **First mentioned**: Chapter 1 (PN=001-207)

## Role in the Narrative

New Zealand is the geographic and narrative starting point. The *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]* had been fitted out at [[san-francisco|San Francisco]] for an "annual voyage to the Antarctic fishing grounds" and put in at [[auckland|Auckland]] where she took on passengers (PN=001-251). It is here that [[mrs-weldon|Mrs. Weldon]], Jack, and [[benedict|Cousin Benedict]] join the ship, and it is from the port of [[auckland|Auckland]] that the *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]* sails on the voyage that will end in disaster.

The ship "sailed from [[auckland|Auckland]]" (PN=002-005) on what should have been a routine return passage. [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]] reassures [[mrs-weldon|Mrs. Weldon]] that it is "a voyage of between 5000 and 6000 miles" (PN=002-034), and her husband James W. [[mrs-weldon|Weldon]] had received word of the ship's arrival at [[auckland|Auckland]] and arranged the passenger manifest (PN=002-029). [[auckland|Auckland]] is also where [[negoro|Negoro]] — the cook who will later betray the ship — was "temporarily engaged" (PN=002-159) after the previous cook deserted.

New Zealand reappears in the resolution: [[dick-sands|Dick Sands]], after his adventures, is "found his way to New Zealand" (PN=050-056), and [[mrs-weldon|Mrs. Weldon]] later learns "that the vessel had sailed from [[auckland|Auckland]] with his wife" aboard (PN=043-025).

## Description in the Text

Verne's description of New Zealand is functional rather than scenic. [[auckland|Auckland]] is described as the port of [[auckland|Waitemata]], "the port of [[auckland|Auckland]]" (PN=001-251), where the *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]* anchored. The ship "quitted New Zealand" at the outset (PN=018-014), and from that point New Zealand exists in the narrative only as a point of reference — the place from which the voyage began and to which, in happier times, the *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]* could have returned.

The New Zealand [[right-whale|whale]]men who were part of the crew's complement (PN=009-058) reflect the real-world whaling presence in New Zealand waters in the 1870s.

> "The 'Pilgrim' had been very unfortunate since she quitted New Zealand; it was true that during the first part of the voyage she had been impeded by protracted calms." (PN=018-014–PN=018-015)

## Historical Context

In 1878, New Zealand was a British colony, with [[auckland|Auckland]] serving as a major port in the South [[pacific-ocean|Pacific]]. New Zealand waters were part of the southern whaling grounds, and the country was a common point of call for whaling ships operating in the Antarctic region. The [[pacific-ocean|Pacific]] whaling industry brought regular traffic between New Zealand, the west coast of South America, and the American [[pacific-ocean|Pacific]] ports like [[san-francisco|San Francisco]].
