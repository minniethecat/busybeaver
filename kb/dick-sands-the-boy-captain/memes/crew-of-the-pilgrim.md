# Crew of the Pilgrim

The professional complement of the whaling brig *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]* under [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]], whose catastrophic loss during a [[right-whale|whale]] hunt leaves the ship in the hands of a fifteen-year-old apprentice.

## Overview

- **Type**: Ship's crew
- **Active period**: 1873–1874 (the novel's present)
- **Key members**: [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]] (master), Howick (boatswain), [[dick-sands|Dick Sands]] (apprentice), [[negoro|Negoro]] (cook)
- **Chapters**: 1–10

## Composition

The *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]*'s crew was a typical whaling complement, though [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]] had "difficulty in engaging as many [[right-whale|whale]]men as he wanted" due to the declining whaling industry (001-221). The crew included specialist [[right-whale|whale]]men who manned the [[right-whale|whale]]boats and processed the catch.

Key members included:
- **[[captain-hull|Captain Hull]]**: Experienced whaling captain, mentor to [[dick-sands|Dick Sands]]
- **Howick**: The boatswain who participates in the fatal [[right-whale|whale]] hunt
- **[[dick-sands|Dick Sands]]**: The fifteen-year-old apprentice learning navigation and [[navigation|seamanship]]
- **[[negoro|Negoro]]**: The ship's cook, hired at [[auckland|Auckland]], whose true identity as a [[slavery|slave-trade]]r is concealed

## Demise

The crew was devastated during the [[right-whale|whale]] hunt. [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]] descended "the rope-ladder and took his place in the" [[right-whale|whale]]boat (ch010), and none returned. The entire crew aboard the [[right-whale|whale]]boat perished when it was capsized by the [[right-whale|whale]]. This left only [[dick-sands|Dick Sands]], [[negoro|Negoro]], and the passengers aboard the *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]*.

## Significance

The loss of the crew creates the novel's central crisis. The *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]* is now crewed by a fifteen-year-old apprentice, a secretly hostile cook, and a complement of passengers with no maritime experience. The crew's professional competence and loyalty—they served from "duty, but because they were" personally devoted (003-044)—stands in stark contrast to the treachery of [[negoro|Negoro]], the sole surviving crew member besides [[dick-sands|Dick]].

> "Duty, but because they were" personally loyal to their captain. (003-044)
