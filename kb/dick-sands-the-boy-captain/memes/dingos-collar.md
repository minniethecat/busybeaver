# Dingo's Collar

The dog collar bearing engraved letters that connects [[dingo|Dingo]] to a mysterious past and helps expose [[negoro|Negoro]]'s crimes.

## Overview

- **Type**: Personal artifact / clue
- **Owner/Operator**: Worn by [[dingo|Dingo]]
- **Chapters**: Referenced in the narrative as a key clue
- **Tags**: artifact, clue, plot-device

## Role in the Novel

Dingo's collar bears engraved letters—"S. V." — that become a crucial clue in the mystery surrounding [[dingo|the dog]] and [[negoro|Negoro]]. The letters connect [[dingo|Dingo]] to a specific person and place, providing evidence of [[negoro|Negoro]]'s past crimes. The collar transforms [[dingo|Dingo]] from a mere companion animal into a bearer of evidence, a living witness to events that occurred before the novel's opening.

The collar's inscription is one of several clues—along with [[dingo|Dingo]]'s hostility toward [[negoro|Negoro]] and the discovery of human remains with chains and forks—that gradually reveal the truth about the novel's antagonists. Like the sabotaged compass, the collar is a physical object that embodies hidden truth.
