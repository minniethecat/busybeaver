[020-001][Illustration: Not without emotion could [[mrs-weldon|Mrs. Weldon]], or indeed any of

[020-002]them, behold the unfortunate ship.]

[020-003]The day wore onwards to its close. The sun was approaching the vernal

[020-004]equinox, and sank almost perpendicularly on to the horizon. Twilight

[020-005]was very short, and the rapidity with which darkness came on confirmed

[020-006][[dick-sands|Dick]] in his belief that they had got ashore at some spot lying between

[020-007]the tropic of Capricorn and the equator.

[020-008]They all assembled in the grotto again for the purpose of getting some

[020-009]sleep.

[020-010]"Another rough night coming on!" said Tom, pointing to the heavy clouds

[020-011]that hung over the horizon.

[020-012]"No doubt, Tom!" answered Dick, "and I think we may congratulate

[020-013]ourselves on being safe out of our poor ship."

[020-014]As the night could not be otherwise than very dark, it was arranged

[020-015]that the negroes should take their turns in keeping guard at the

[020-016]entrance of the grotto. [[dingo|Dingo]] also would be upon the alert.

[020-017]Benedict had not yet returned. Hercules shouted his name with the full

[020-018]strength of his capacious lungs, and shortly afterwards the

[020-019]entomologist was seen making his way down the face of the cliff at the

[020-020]imminent risk of breaking his neck. He was in a great rage. He had not

[020-021]found a single insect worth having, scorpions, scolopendra, and other

[020-022]myriapoda were in the forest in abundance; but not one of these of

[020-023]course could be allowed a place in his collection.

[020-024]"Have I come six thousand miles for this?" he cried: "have I endured

[020-025]storm and shipwreck only to be cast where not a hexapod is to be seen?

[020-026]The country is detestable! I shall not stay in it another hour!"

[020-027]Ever gentle to his eccentricities, Mrs. Weldon soothed him as she would

[020-028]a child, she told him that he had better take some rest now, and most

[020-029]likely he would have better luck to-morrow.

[020-030]Cousin Benedict had hardly been pacified when Tom remarked that [[negoro|Negoro]]

[020-031]too had not returned.

[020-032]"Never mind!" said Bat, "his room is as good as his company."

[020-033]"I cannot say that I altogether think so. The man is no favourite of

[020-034]mine, but I like him better under my own eye," said Mrs. Weldon.

[020-035]"Perhaps he has his own reasons for keeping away," said Dick, and

[020-036]taking Mrs. Weldon aside, he communicated to her his suspicions of the

[020-037]fellow's dishonesty.

[020-038]He found that she coincided with him in her view of Negoro's conduct;

[020-039]but she did not agree with him in his proposal to have him searched at

[020-040]once. If he returned, she should be convinced that he had deposited the

[020-041]money in some secret spot; and as there would be no proof of his guilt,

[020-042]it would be better to leave him, at least for a time, uninterrogated.

[020-043][Illustration: The entomologist was seen making his way down the face

[020-044]of the cliff at the imminent risk of breaking his neck.]

[020-045]Dick was convinced by her representations, and promised to act upon her

[020-046]advice.

[020-047]Before they resigned themselves to sleep, they had repeatedly summoned

[020-048]Negoro back, but he either could not or would not hear. Mrs. Weldon and

[020-049]Dick scarcely knew what to think; unless he had lost his way; it was

[020-050]unaccountable why he should be wandering about alone on a dark night in

[020-051]a strange country.

[020-052]Presently Dingo was heard barking furiously. He had left the opening of

[020-053]the grotto, and was evidently down at the water's edge. Imagining that

[020-054]Negoro must be coming, Dick sent three of the negroes in the direction

[020-055]of the river to meet him; but when they reached the bank not a soul

[020-056]could be seen, and as Dingo was quiet again, they made their way back

[020-057]to the grotto.

[020-058]Excepting the man left on watch, they now all lay down, hoping to get

[020-059]some repose. Mrs. Weldon, however, could not sleep. The land for which

[020-060]she had sighed so ardently had been reached, but it had failed to give

[020-061]either the security or the comfort which she had anticipated!

[020-062]CHAPTER XV.

[020-063]A STRANGER.

[020-064]At daybreak, next morning, Austin, who happened to be on guard, heard

[020-065]Dingo bark, and noticed that he started up and ran towards the river.

[020-066]Arousing the inmates of the grotto, he announced to them that some one

[020-067]was coming.

[020-068]"It isn't Negoro," said Tom; "Dingo would bark louder than that if

[020-069]Negoro were to be seen."

[020-070]"Who, then, can it be?" asked Mrs. Weldon, with an inquiring glance

[020-071]towards Dick.

[020-072]"We must wait and see, madam," replied Dick quietly.

[020-073]Bidding Bat, Austin, and Hercules follow his example, Dick Sands took

[020-074]up a cutlass and a rifle, into the breach of which he slipped a

[020-075]cartridge. Thus armed, the four young men made their way towards the

[020-076]river bank. Tom and Actæon were left with Mrs. Weldon at the entrance

[020-077]of the grotto.

[020-078]The sun was just rising. Its rays, intercepted by the lofty range of

[020-079]mountains in the east, did not fall directly on the cliff; but the sea

[020-080]to its western horizon was sparkling in the sunbeams as the party

[020-081]marched along the shore. Dingo was motionless as a setter, but did not

[020-082]cease barking. It soon proved not to be his old enemy who was

[020-083]disturbing him. A man, who was not Negoro, appeared round the angle of

[020-084]the cliff, and advancing cautiously along the bank of the stream,

[020-085]seemed by his gestures to be endeavouring to pacify the dog, with which

[020-086]an encounter would certainly have been by no means desirable.

[020-087]"That's not Negoro!" said Hercules.

[020-088][Illustration: "Good morning, my young friend."]

[020-089]"No loss for any of us," muttered Bat.

[020-090]"You are right," replied Dick; "perhaps he is a native; let us hope he

[020-091]may be able to tell us our whereabouts, and save us the trouble of

[020-092][[exploration|exploring]]."

[020-093]With their rifles on their shoulders, they advanced steadily towards

[020-094]the new arrival. The stranger, on becoming aware of their approach,

[020-095]manifested great surprise; he was apparently puzzled as to how they had

[020-096]reached the shore, for the "[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]" had been entirely broken up during

[020-097]the night, and the spars that were floating about had probably been too

[020-098]few and too scattered to attract his attention. His first attitude

[020-099]seemed to betray something of fear; and raising to his shoulder a gun

[020-100]that had been slung to his belt, he began to retrace his steps; but

[020-101]conciliatory gestures on the part of Dick quickly reassured him, and

[020-102]after a moment's hesitation, he continued to advance.

[020-103]He was a man of about forty years of age, strongly built, with a keen,

[020-104]bright eye, grizzly hair and beard, and a complexion tanned as with

[020-105]constant exposure to the forest air. He wore a broad-brimmed hat, a

[020-106]kind of leather jerkin, or tunic, and long boots reaching nearly to his

[020-107]knees. To his high heels was fastened a pair of wide-rowelled spurs,

[020-108]which clanked as he moved.

[020-109]Dick Sands in an instant saw that he was not looking upon one of the

[020-110]roving Indians of the pampas, but upon one of those adventurers, often

[020-111]of very doubtful character, who are not unfrequently to be met with in

[020-112]the remotest quarters of the earth. Clearly this was neither an Indian

[020-113]nor a Spaniard. His erect, not to say rigid deportment, and the reddish

[020-114]hue with which his hair and beard were streaked, betokened him to be of

[020-115]Anglo-Saxon origin, a conjecture which was at once confirmed when upon

[020-116]Dick's wishing him "good morning," he replied in unmistakable English,

[020-117]with hardly a trace of foreign accent,--

[020-118]"Good morning, my young friend."

[020-119]He stepped forward, and having shaken hands with Dick, nodded to all

[020-120]his companions.

[020-121]"Are you English?" he asked.

[020-122]"No; we are Americans," replied Dick.

[020-123]"North or South?" inquired the man.

[020-124]"North," Dick answered.

[020-125]The information seemed to afford the stranger no little satisfaction,

[020-126]and he again wrung Dick's hand with all the enthusiasm of a

[020-127]fellow-countryman.

[020-128]"And may I ask what brings you here?" he continued.

[020-129]Before, however, Dick had time to reply, the stranger had courteously

[020-130]raised his hat, and, looking round, Dick saw that his bow was intended

[020-131]for Mrs. Weldon, who had just reached the river-bank. She proceeded to

[020-132]tell him the particulars of how they had been [[shipwreck|shipwrecked]], and how the

[020-133]vessel had gone to pieces on the reefs.

[020-134]A look of pity crossed the man's face as he listened, and he cast his

[020-135]eye, as it might be involuntarily, upon the sea, in order to discern

[020-136]some vestige of the stranded ship.

[020-137]"Ah! there is nothing to be seen of our poor schooner!" said Dick

[020-138]mournfully; "the last of her was broken up in the storm last night."

[020-139]"And now," interposed Mrs. Weldon, "can you tell us where we are?"

[020-140]"Where?" exclaimed the man, with every indication of surprise at her

[020-141]question; "why, on the coast of South America, of course!"

[020-142]"But on what part? are we near Peru?" Dick inquired eagerly.

[020-143]"No, my lad, no; you are more to the south; you are on the coast of

[020-144]Bolivia; close to the borders of Chili."

[020-145]"A good distance, I suppose, from Lima?" asked Dick.

[020-146]"From Lima? yes, a long way; Lima is far to the north."

[020-147]"And what is the name of that promontory?" Dick said, pointing to the

[020-148]adjacent headland.

[020-149]"That, I confess, is more than I am able to tell you," replied the

[020-150]stranger; "for although I have travelled a great deal in the interior

[020-151]of the country, I have never before visited this part of the coast."

[020-152]Dick pondered in thoughtful silence over the information he had thus

[020-153]received. He had no reason to doubt its accuracy; according to his own

[020-154]reckoning he would have expected to come ashore somewhere between the

[020-155]latitudes of 27° and 30°; and by this stranger's showing he had made

[020-156]the latitude 25°; the discrepancy was not very great; it was not more

[020-157]than might be accounted for by the action of the currents, which he

[020-158]knew he had been unable to estimate; moreover, the deserted character

[020-159]of the whole shore inclined him to believe more easily that he was in

[020-160]Lower Bolivia.

[020-161]Whilst this conversation was going on, Mrs. Weldon, whose suspicions

[020-162]had been excited by Negoro's disappearance, had been scrutinizing the

[020-163]stranger with the utmost attention; but she could detect nothing either

[020-164]in his manner or in his words to give her any cause to doubt his good

[020-165]faith.

[020-166]"Pardon me," she said presently; "but you do not seem to me to be a

[020-167]native of Peru?"

[020-168]"No; like yourself, I am an American, Mrs. ----;" he paused, as if

[020-169]waiting to be told her name.

[020-170]The lady smiled, and gave her name; he thanked her, and continued,--

[020-171]"My name is Harris. I was born in South Carolina; but it is now twenty

[020-172]years since I left my home for the pampas of Bolivia; imagine,

[020-173]therefore, how much pleasure it gives me to come across some countrymen

[020-174]of my own."

[020-175]"Do you live in this part of the province, Mr. Harris?" Mrs. Weldon

[020-176]asked.

[020-177]"No, indeed; far away; I live down to the south, close to the borders

[020-178]of Chili. At present I am taking a journey north-eastwards to Atacama."

[020-179]"Atacama!" exclaimed Dick; "are we anywhere near the desert of Atacama?"

[020-180]"Yes, my young friend," rejoined Harris, "you are just on the edge of

[020-181]it. It extends far beyond those mountains which you see on the horizon,

[020-182]and is one of the most curious and least explored parts of the

[020-183]continent."

[020-184]"And are you travelling through it alone?" Mrs. Weldon inquired.

[020-185]"Yes, quite alone; and it is not the first time I have performed the

[020-186]journey. One of my brothers owns a large farm, the hacienda of San

[020-187]Felice, about 200 miles from here, and I have occasion now and then to

[020-188]pay him business visits."

[020-189][Illustration: "He is my little son."]

[020-190]After a moment's hesitation, as if he were weighing a sudden thought,

[020-191]he continued,--

[020-192]"I am on my way there now, and if you will accompany me I can promise

[020-193]you a hearty welcome, and my brother will be most happy to do his best

[020-194]to provide you with means of conveyance to [[san-francisco|San Francisco]]."
