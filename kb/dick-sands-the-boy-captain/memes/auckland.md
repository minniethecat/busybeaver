# Auckland

**Auckland** is a port city on [[new-zealand|New Zealand]]'s North Island, situated on the Waitematā Harbour. In *[[dick-sands|Dick Sands]]: The Boy [[captain-hull|Captain]]*, it serves as the *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]*'s port of departure — the point of origin for a voyage that would spiral into disaster, and the place where several key figures, including the treacherous [[negoro|Negoro]], joined the ship.

## Geography

- **Type**: Port city
- **Real or fictional**: Real
- **First mentioned**: Chapter 1 (001-251)

Auckland was, in the 1870s, the largest city in [[new-zealand|New Zealand]] and the primary port for whaling vessels operating in the South [[pacific-ocean|Pacific]]. Verne references the port by its harbor name, Waitemata: the *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]* "on the 15th of January, and on reaching Waitemata, the port of Auckland" (001-251), completed its previous whaling voyage and prepared to take on passengers for the return journey to [[san-francisco|San Francisco]].

## The Pilgrim's Departure

The *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]*'s departure from Auckland sets the entire narrative in motion. [[mrs-weldon|Mrs. Weldon]], her young son [[jack-weldon|Jack]], and her eccentric cousin [[benedict|Cousin Benedict]] were all at Auckland, awaiting passage to California. [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]] was "on the point of leaving Auckland" (002-005) when they decided to board. [[mrs-weldon|Mrs. Weldon]] had been "obliged to leave him behind at Auckland with his wife and Cousin" (002-015) — her husband [[james-weldon|James Weldon]] stayed behind on business, a decision that would later prove fateful when [[negoro|Negoro]] attempted to use him as leverage.

The *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]* "had sailed from Auckland with his wife" (043-025), carrying the passengers eastward across the [[pacific-ocean|Pacific]] on what was supposed to be a routine commercial voyage. Instead, the journey would become a nightmare of shipwreck, treachery, and survival in the [[africa|Africa]]n interior.

## Crew Changes at Auckland

Auckland was also the site of critical crew changes that shaped the novel's trajectory. Several crew members were "temporarily engaged at Auckland" (002-159) to replace sailors who had left the ship. Most significantly, [[negoro|Negoro]] — the ship's cook — had "deserted the ship at Auckland" (003-055) from his previous vessel and opportunistically joined the *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]*'s crew. This seemingly minor staffing decision introduced the novel's primary antagonist onto the ship, setting in motion the chain of [[treachery|betrayal]]s that would define the plot.

The voyage had only been underway for a short time "since the '[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]' had left Auckland" (008-057) when the first signs of trouble appeared — a wounded [[right-whale|whale]], a fateful hunting expedition, and the catastrophic loss of [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]] and most of the crew.

## Narrative Significance

Auckland functions as the novel's "before" — the last point of normalcy before the cascading disasters that follow. Every subsequent event in the novel can be traced back to decisions made in this port: the [[mrs-weldon|Weldon]]s choosing to sail without James, crew members being hired at short notice, and [[negoro|Negoro]] insinuating himself into the ship's company. Verne uses Auckland to establish the *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]*'s world before it unravels, making the port a silent witness to the quiet origins of catastrophe.
