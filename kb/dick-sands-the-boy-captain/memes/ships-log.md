# Ship's Log

The maritime instrument for measuring a vessel's speed through water, one of the two navigational tools [[dick-sands|Dick Sands]] uses to command the *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]*.

## Overview

- **Type**: [[navigation|Navigation]]al instrument
- **Owner/Operator**: The *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]* / [[dick-sands|Dick Sands]]
- **Chapters**: Referenced in ch015, ch028
- **Tags**: instrument, navigation, maritime

## Role in the Novel

The log, along with the compass, constitutes the sum of [[dick-sands|Dick Sands]]' navigational toolkit. [[harris|Harris]] mockingly notes that "Young Sands understood nothing more of navigation than the use of the log and compass" (028-117–118).

The log "was fastened to" its line and used regularly (015-082). However, during the storm, "the log remained at the bottom of the sea" when it was lost overboard (028-119). Without the log, [[dick-sands|Dick]] could no longer measure the *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]*'s speed, and combined with the sabotaged compass, he was [[navigation|navigating]] blindly.

The log's loss symbolizes the progressive stripping away of [[dick-sands|Dick]]'s command. First the captain and crew are lost, then the compass is sabotaged, and finally the log is lost—leaving [[dick-sands|Dick]] with no reliable means of determining the ship's position or course.

> "The log remained at the bottom of the sea, and one night the compass was tampered with." (028-119–120)
