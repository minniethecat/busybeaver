# Rescue of the Africans

The opening rescue in which the *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]* saves five [[africa|Africa]]n men from a sinking vessel, bringing them aboard as passengers and setting in motion the relationships that will prove crucial to survival.

## Overview

- **Type**: [[rescue|Rescue]]
- **Chapters**: 4–5
- **Key participants**: [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]], [[dick-sands|Dick Sands]], [[tom|Tom]], Bat, Austin, Actaeon, [[hercules|Hercules]]
- **Location**: [[pacific-ocean|Pacific Ocean]]

## Narrative

While sailing off the coast of South America, the *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]*'s lookout spots a derelict vessel. [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]] orders an investigation, discovering "some unfortunate [[shipwreck|shipwrecked]] men who must be rescued if possible" (004-047). The party prepares for "those who were coming to its rescue" (004-139).

The five men are found in desperate condition. [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]] suspects from the outset that they might be connected to the [[slavery|slave trade]]: the wrecked vessel was likely carrying a "slave-cargo which was on its way to some [[pacific-ocean|Pacific]] island" (005-075). However, the men prove to be free—they confirm "that they had regained their freedom" through the "anti-slavery war" in the United States (005-077, 005-118).

Old [[tom|Tom]], the eldest, recounts his personal history: sold as a slave at age six, brought from [[africa|Africa]] to America, he eventually "recovered his freedom" along with his companions (005-103–105).

## Significance

This rescue introduces the five [[africa|Africa]]n characters who become essential to the party's survival. [[hercules|Hercules]]' strength, [[tom|Tom]]'s wisdom, and the collective loyalty of the group prove decisive throughout the narrative. The rescue also establishes the moral framework of the novel—the duty to save those in distress, and the contrast between freedom and slavery.
