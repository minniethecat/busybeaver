[009-001]With a critical eye [[dick-sands|Dick Sands]] looked long and steadily at the spout.

[009-002]"It blows out water, sir," said the apprentice, "water, as well as

[009-003]vapour. I should think it is a finback. But it must be a rare large

[009-004]one."

[009-005]"Seventy feet, at least!" rejoined the captain, flushing with his

[009-006]enthusiasm.

[009-007]"What a big fellow!" said Jack, catching the excitement of his elders.

[009-008][Illustration: "What a big fellow!"]

[009-009]"Ah, Jack, my boy," chuckled the captain, "the [[right-whale|whale]] little thinks who

[009-010]are watching him enjoy his breakfast!"

[009-011]"Yes," said the boatswain; "a dozen such gentlemen as that would

[009-012]freight a craft twice the size of ours; but this one, if only we can

[009-013]get him, will go a good way towards filling our empty barrels."

[009-014]"Rather rough work, you know," said Dick, "to attack a finback!"

[009-015]"You are right, Dick," answered the captain; "the boat has yet to be

[009-016]built which is strong enough to resist the flap of a jubarte's tail."

[009-017]"But the profit is worth the risk, captain, isn't it?"

[009-018]"You are right again, Dick," replied [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]], and as he spoke, he

[009-019]clambered on to the bowsprit in order that he might get a better view

[009-020]of the whale.

[009-021]The crew were as eager as their captain. Mounted on the fore-shrouds,

[009-022]they scanned the movements of their coveted prey in the distance,

[009-023]freely descanting upon the profit to be made out of a good finback and

[009-024]declaring that it would be a thousand pities if this chance of filling

[009-025]the casks below should be permitted to be lost.

[009-026]Captain Hull was perplexed. He bit his nails and knitted his brow.

[009-027]"Mamma!" cried little Jack, "I should so much like to see a whale

[009-028]close,--quite close, you know."

[009-029]"And so you shall, my boy," replied the captain, who was standing by,

[009-030]and had come to the resolve that if his men would back him, he would

[009-031]make an attempt to capture the prize.

[009-032]He turned to his crew,--

[009-033]"My men! what do you think? shall we make the venture? Remember, we are

[009-034]all alone; we have no whalemen to help us; we must rely upon ourselves;

[009-035]I have thrown a harpoon before now; I can throw a harpoon again; what

[009-036]do you say?"

[009-037]The crew responded with a ringing cheer,--

[009-038]"Ay, ay, sir! Ay, ay!"

[009-039]CHAPTER VII.

[009-040]PREPARATIONS FOR AN ATTACK.

[009-041]Great was the excitement that now prevailed, and the question of an

[009-042]attempt to capture the sea-monster became the ruling theme of

[009-043]conversation. [[mrs-weldon|Mrs. Weldon]] expressed considerable doubt as to the

[009-044]prudence of venturing upon so great a risk with such a limited number

[009-045]of hands, but when Captain Hull assured her that he had more than once

[009-046]successfully attacked a whale with a single boat, and that for his part

[009-047]he had no fear of failure, she made no further remonstrance, and

[009-048]appeared quite satisfied.

[009-049]Having formed his resolve, the captain lost no time in setting about

[009-050]his preliminary arrangements. He could not really conceal from his own

[009-051]mind that the pursuit of a finback was always a matter of some peril,

[009-052]and he was anxious, accordingly, to make every possible provision which

[009-053]forethought could devise against all emergencies.

[009-054]Besides her long-boat, which was kept between the two masts, the

[009-055]"[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]" had three whale-boats, two of them slung to the starboard and

[009-056]larboard davits, and the third at the stern, outside the taffrail.

[009-057]During the fishing season, when the crew was reinforced by a hired

[009-058]complement of [[new-zealand|New Zealand]] whalemen, all three of these boats would be

[009-059]brought at once into requisition, but at present the whole crew of the

[009-060]"Pilgrim" was barely sufficient to man one of the three boats. Tom and

[009-061]his friends were ready to volunteer their assistance, but any offers of

[009-062]service from them were necessarily declined; the manipulation of a

[009-063]whale-boat can only be entrusted to those who are experienced in the

[009-064]work, as a false turn of the tiller or a premature stroke of the oar

[009-065]may in a moment compromise the safety of the whole party. Thus

[009-066]compelled to take all his trained sailors with him on his venturous

[009-067]expedition, the captain had no alternative than to leave his apprentice

[009-068]in charge of the schooner during his absence. Dick's choice would have

[009-069]been very much in favour of taking a share in the whale-hunt, but he

[009-070]had the good sense to know that the developed strength of a man would

[009-071]be of far greater service in the boat, and accordingly without a murmur

[009-072]he resigned himself to remain behind.

[009-073]Of the five sailors who were to man the boat, there were four to take

[009-074]the oars, whilst Howick the boatswain was to manage the oar at the

[009-075]stern, which on these occasions generally replaces an ordinary rudder

[009-076]as being quicker in action in the event of any of the side oars being

[009-077]disabled. The post of harpooner was of course assigned to Captain Hull,

[009-078]to whose lot it would consequently fall first to hurl his weapon at the

[009-079]whale, then to manage the unwinding of the line to which the harpoon

[009-080]was attached, and finally to kill the creature by lance-wounds when it

[009-081]should emerge again from below the sea.

[009-082]A method sometimes employed for commencing an attack is to place a sort

[009-083]of small cannon on the bows or deck of the boat and to discharge from

[009-084]it either a harpoon or some explosive bullets, which make frightful

[009-085]lacerations on the body of the victim; but the "Pilgrim" was not

[009-086]provided with apparatus of this description; not only are all the

[009-087]contrivances of this kind very costly and difficult to manage, but the

[009-088]fishermen generally are averse to innovations, and prefer the

[009-089]old-fashioned harpoons. It was with these alone that Captain Hull was

[009-090]now about to encounter the finback that was lying some four miles

[009-091]distant from his ship.

[009-092]The weather promised as favourably as could be for the enterprise. The

[009-093]sea was calm, and the wind moreover was still moderating, so that there

[009-094]was no likelihood of the schooner drifting away during the captain's

[009-095]absence.

[009-096]When the starboard whale-boat had been lowered, and the four sailors

[009-097]had entered it, Howick passed a couple of harpoons down to them, and

[009-098]some lances which had been carefully sharpened; to these were added

[009-099]five coils of stout and supple rope, each 600 feet long, for a whale

[009-100]when struck often dives so deeply that even these lengths of line

[009-101]knotted together are found to be insufficient. After these implements

[009-102]of attack had been properly stowed in the bows, the crew had only to

[009-103]await the pleasure of their captain.

[009-104]The "Pilgrim," before the sailors left her, had been made to heave to,

[009-105]and the yards were braced so as to secure her remaining as stationary

[009-106]as possible. As the time drew near for the captain to quit her, he gave

[009-107]a searching look all round to satisfy himself that everything was in

[009-108]order; he saw that the halyards were properly tightened, and the sails

[009-109]trimmed as they should be, and then calling the young apprentice to his

[009-110]side, he said,--

[009-111]"Now, Dick, I am going to leave you for a few hours: while I am away, I

[009-112]hope that it will not be necessary for you to make any movement

[009-113]whatever. However, you must be on the watch. It is not very likely, but

[009-114]it is possible that this finback may carry us out to some distance. If

[009-115]so, you will have to follow; and in that case, I am sure you may rely

[009-116]upon Tom and his friends for assistance."

[009-117]One and all, the negroes assured the captain of their willingness to

[009-118]obey Dick's instructions, the sturdy Hercules rolling up his capacious

[009-119]shirt-sleeves as if to show that he was ready for immediate action.

[009-120]The captain went on,--

[009-121]"The weather is beautifully fine, Dick, and I see no prospect of the

[009-122]wind freshening; but come what may, I have one direction to give you

[009-123]which I strictly enforce. You must not leave the ship. If I want you to

[009-124]follow us, I will hoist a flag on the boat-hook."

[009-125]"You may trust me, sir," answered Dick; "and I will keep a good

[009-126]look-out."

[009-127]"All right, my lad; keep a cool head and a good heart. You are second

[009-128]captain now, you know. I never heard of any one of your age being

[009-129]placed in such a post; be a credit to your position!"

[009-130]Dick blushed, and the bright flush that rose to his cheeks spoke more

[009-131]than words.

[009-132]"The lad may be trusted," murmured the captain to himself; "he is as

[009-133]modest as he is courageous. Yes; he may be trusted."

[009-134]It cannot be denied that the captain was not wholly without compunction

[009-135]at the step he was taking; he was aware of the danger to which he was

[009-136]exposing himself, but he beguiled himself with the persuasion that it

[009-137]was only for a few hours; and his fisherman's instinct was very keen.

[009-138]It was not only for himself; the desire upon the part of the crew was

[009-139]almost irresistibly strong that every opportunity ought to be employed

[009-140]for making the cargo of the schooner equal to her owner's expectations.

[009-141]And so he finally prepared to start.

[009-142]"I wish you all success!" said Mrs. Weldon.

[009-143]"Many thanks!" he replied.

[009-144]Little Jack put in his word,--

[009-145]"And you will try and catch the whale without hurting him much?"

[009-146]"All right, young gentleman," answered the captain; "he shall hardly

[009-147]feel the tip of our fingers!"

[009-148]"Sometimes," said Cousin Benedict, as if he had been pondering the

[009-149]expedition in relation to his pet science, "sometimes there are strange

[009-150][[entomology|insects]] clinging to the backs of these great mammifers; do you think

[009-151]you are likely to procure me any specimens?"

[009-152]"You shall soon have the opportunity of investigating for yourself,"

[009-153]was the captain's reply.

[009-154]"And you, Tom; we shall be looking to you for help in cutting up our

[009-155]prize, when we get it alongside," continued he.

[009-156]"We shall be quite ready, sir," said the negro.

[009-157]"One thing more, Dick," added the captain; "you may as well be getting

[009-158]up the empty barrels out of the hold; they will be all ready."

[009-159]"It shall be done, sir," answered Dick promptly.

[009-160]If everything went well it was the intention that the whale after it

[009-161]had been killed should be towed to the side of the schooner, where it

[009-162]would be firmly lashed. Then the sailors with their feet in spiked

[009-163]shoes would get upon its back and proceed to cut the blubber, from head

[009-164]to tail, in long strips, which would first be divided into lumps about

[009-165]a foot and a half square, the lumps being subsequently chopped into

[009-166]smaller portions capable of being stored away in casks. The ordinary

[009-167]rule would be for a ship, as soon as the flaying was complete, to make

[009-168]its way to land where the blubber could be at once boiled down, an

[009-169]operation by which it is reduced by about a third of its weight, and by

[009-170]which it yields all its oil, the only portion of it which is of any

[009-171]value. Under present circumstances, however, Captain Hull would not

[009-172]think of melting down the blubber until his arrival at Valparaiso, and

[009-173]as he was sanguine that the wind would soon set in a favourable

[009-174]direction, he calculated that he should reach that port in less than

[009-175]three weeks, a period during which his cargo would not be deteriorated.

[009-176]The latest movement with regard to the "Pilgrim" had been to bring her

[009-177]somewhat nearer the spot where the spouts of vapour indicated the

[009-178]presence of the coveted prize. The creature continued to swim about in

[009-179]the reddened waters, opening and shutting its huge jaws like an

[009-180]automaton, and absorbing at every mouthful whole myriads of animalcula.

[009-181]No one entertained a fear that it would try to make an escape; it was

[009-182]the unanimous verdict that it was "a fighting whale," and one that

[009-183]would resist all attacks to the very end.
