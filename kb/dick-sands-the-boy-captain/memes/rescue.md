# Rescue

The act of saving others from peril, a recurring theme in *[[dick-sands|Dick Sands]]* that establishes the moral character of the protagonists and sets the plot in motion.

## Definition

Rescue is the act of delivering someone from danger, imprisonment, or harm. In the novel, rescue operates as both a practical action and a moral imperative—the duty to save those in distress is never questioned by the virtuous characters.

## Role in the Novel

The novel opens with a rescue. The *[[the-pilgrim|Pilgrim]]* spots "some unfortunate [[shipwreck|shipwrecked]] men who must be rescued if possible" (004-047). This rescue of the five [[africa|Africa]]n passengers—[[tom|Tom]], Bat, Austin, Actaeon, and [[hercules|Hercules]]—is the inciting event that brings the novel's key characters together.

The rescue establishes a moral economy: those who rescue are themselves in need of rescue later. [[dick-sands|Dick Sands]], who had previously "succeeded in restoring the [[shipwreck|shipwrecked]] men to their homes" (006-019), finds himself needing rescue when stranded in [[africa|Africa]]. The five [[africa|Africa]]ns, rescued at sea, become the rescuers in turn when they protect and assist the party in captivity.

The novel also explores failed or delayed rescue. [[mrs-weldon|Mrs. Weldon]]'s husband has not "effected their rescue" (005-163), and the party's hope of being found diminishes as they are marched deeper into the interior. The tension between hope of rescue and the reality of captivity drives much of the second half's dramatic tension.
