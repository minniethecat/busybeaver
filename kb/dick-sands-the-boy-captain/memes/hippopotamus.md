# Hippopotamus

A large semi-aquatic [[africa|Africa]]n mammal that appears repeatedly along the rivers and waterways of the novel, its snorting and wallowing serving as an ominous backdrop to the travelers' difficult journey.

## Overview

- **Type**: Species
- **Species**: *Hippopotamus amphibius*
- **Owner/Associated with**: None (wild animal)
- **Chapters**: 25–28, 37, 48

## Role in the Narrative

The hippopotamus functions as an ambient indicator of the [[africa|Africa]]n setting. Its presence — the sound of its snorting, the traces of its passage, the shapes moving through the undergrowth — continually reinforces that the party is deep in the [[africa|Africa]]n wilderness, far from any coastline or civilization.

The animal first appears as part of the rich fauna encountered during the overland trek. Traces of "elephants and hippopotamuses" are noted as markers of the unfamiliar landscape the party must cross, confirming that the region's wildlife is unmistakably [[africa|Africa]]n [028-020]. The travelers hear "the snorting of hippopotamuses" near what is probably a lake or watercourse, their presence suggesting hidden water [037-005].

In the novel's final chapters, as the survivors make their way toward safety, hippopotamuses remain part of the ambient threat — their snorting heard alongside the scraping of crocodile scales, the two sounds together creating a sense of constant, unseen danger in the rivers [048-104].

## Description in the Text

Verne describes hippopotamuses primarily through sound and trace rather than detailed visual description. The animals "trample down the underwood" as they move through dense vegetation, their bulk bending the landscape to their passage [026-062]. The text emphasizes their size and power through implication: the underbrush they flatten, the water they disturb, the fear their snorting inspires.

> Traces of elephants and hippopotamuses, which of course are as often found in Africa as they are rare in America. [028-020]

## Historical/Natural History Context

The common hippopotamus (*Hippopotamus amphibius*) was well-documented by European explorers of [[africa|Africa]] by Verne's time, though encounters were still dangerous and dramatic. Verne's inclusion of hippopotamuses is accurate to the central [[africa|Africa]]n river systems through which the characters travel — the animal's range in 1878 extended broadly across sub-Saharan [[africa|Africa]]n waterways.
