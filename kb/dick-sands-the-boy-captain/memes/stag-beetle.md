# Stag Beetle

A beetle of the family Lucanidae, prized by collectors for its large mandibles, and one of the species in [[benedict|Cousin Benedict]]'s entomo[[ships-log|log]]ical studies.

## Overview

- **Type**: Species (taxonomic group)
- **Species**: *Lucanidae* (stag beetles)
- **Owner/Associated with**: [[benedict|Cousin Benedict]] (collector and student)
- **Chapters**: Referenced in ch007

## Role in the Novel

Stag beetles are among the [[entomology|insects]] in [[benedict|Cousin Benedict]]'s "extensive collection of stag-beetles, tiger-beetles and lady-birds" (007-019–020). [[benedict|Benedict]], the novel's comic scientist figure, devotes himself entirely to entomo[[ships-log|log]]y, studying these beetles with single-minded dedication while remaining oblivious to the dangers surrounding the party.

[[benedict|Benedict]] even trained [[hercules|Hercules]] as an assistant in handling the specimens: "he soon learned that the man's gentle docility was a sufficient guarantee against his clumsiness" despite [[hercules|Hercules]]' "huge grasp" (007-020–022). The image of the giant [[hercules|Hercules]] delicately handling tiny beetle specimens epitomizes the novel's blend of adventure and gentle humor.
