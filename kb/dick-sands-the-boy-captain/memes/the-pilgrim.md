The whaling brig that serves as the novel's primary setting for its first half, carrying its passengers from [[new-zealand|New Zealand]] toward [[san-francisco|California]] before sabotage by [[negoro|Negoro]] and a [[storm-at-sea|hurricane]] drive it to the coast of [[africa|Africa]]. The *Pilgrim* is both a physical vessel and a symbolic microcosm of civilization under threat.

## Description

The *Pilgrim* was a brig-rigged whaling vessel, equipped for long voyages in remote waters. Her hold contained "an ample cargo of cut and uncut [[right-whale|whale]]bone" at the time of the novel's opening (001-227). The ship was fitted with [[right-whale|whale]]boats for pursuit, facilities for "cutting up the [[right-whale|whale]]s on board" (001-216), and all the equipment of a working [[right-whale|whale]]r.

The vessel's deck had no poop, which meant "[[mrs-weldon|Mrs. Weldon]] had no" private quarters of the kind found on larger vessels (003-029). Despite her workmanlike nature, the *Pilgrim* was a sound vessel, capable of withstanding the [[storm-at-sea|hurricane]] that drove her across [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]].

## Voyage

The *Pilgrim* departed from [[new-zealand|Auckland, New Zealand]], where [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]] had put in for repairs and supplies. Her intended course was eastward across the [[pacific-ocean|Pacific Ocean]] to [[valparaiso|Valparaiso]], and thence to [[san-francisco|San Francisco]]. Aboard were [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]], the crew, [[dick-sands|Dick Sands]] (apprentice), [[negoro|Negoro]] (cook, hired at [[auckland|Auckland]]), and passengers: [[mrs-weldon|Mrs. Weldon]], young [[jack-weldon|Jack]], Cousin [[benedict|Benedict]], and the five rescued [[africa|Africa]]ns including [[hercules|Hercules]] and [[tom|Tom]].

After the [[loss-of-captain-hull|whaleboat disaster]] that claimed [[captain-hull|Captain Hull]]'s life, the *Pilgrim* continued under [[dick-sands|Dick Sands]]' command. [[negoro|Negoro]] sabotaged the [[compass|compass]], and "one night the compass was tampered with, so that the *Pilgrim*... was carried altogether out of her course" (028-120–121). The vessel was driven by a [[storm-at-sea|hurricane]] northeastward and eventually wrecked on the [[africa|Africa]]n coast.

> "The log remained at the bottom of the sea, and one night the compass was tampered with." — Harris, describing the *Pilgrim*'s fate (028-119–120)

## Fate

The *Pilgrim*'s wreck on the [[africa|Africa]]n coast marked the end of the maritime phase of the narrative. Survivors salvaged what they could from the wreck, but the vessel was lost. [[dick-sands|Dick Sands]]' brief career as acting captain ended with the ship's destruction, and the story shifted to the overland journey through [[angola|Angola]].

## Significance

The *Pilgrim* serves as more than a setting. It is the contained world of civilization — with its hierarchy, discipline, and moral order — that [[negoro|Negoro]]'s treachery shatters. Its destruction on the [[africa|Africa]]n coast marks the narrative's transition from the known to the unknown, from the maritime world of free men to the [[africa|Africa]]n interior dominated by the [[slavery|slave trade]].
