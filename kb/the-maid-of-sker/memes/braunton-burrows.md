# Braunton Burrows

The vast sand dune system of North Devon, where Davy seeks refuge and where key events of the novel's second half unfold — a landscape of isolation, danger, and unexpected encounter.

## Location

Braunton Burrows is a real location on the North Devon coast, one of the largest sand dune systems in Britain. In the novel, it appears as a place of concealment and exposure: "Braunton Burrows" (026) is where characters hide, meet, and confront each other against a backdrop of shifting sands and maritime light.

## Significance in the Novel

Braunton Burrows serves as the novel's second great landscape after Sker — a Devonshire counterpart to the Welsh coast. It is a place of "gentleman of high rank who wandered" (027-029) and of desperate concealment, where the novel's social and Gothic registers intersect. The Burrows represent the wider world beyond Glamorganshire — Devon, the gentry, the naval establishment — that the plot must engage for its resolution.

## Historical Note

Braunton Burrows is a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, recognized for its unique dune ecology. In the 18th century, it was a wilder, less-managed landscape — the kind of liminal coastal space that Blackmore uses to such atmospheric effect throughout the novel.
