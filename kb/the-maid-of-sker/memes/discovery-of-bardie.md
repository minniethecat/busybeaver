# Discovery of Bardie

The moment Davy Llewellyn finds a small child, alone and shipwrecked, in a derelict boat off Sker Point — the event from which all subsequent plot developments flow.

## Event Overview

Returning from a phenomenally successful day of fishing at Sker, Davy discovers a beached boat containing a small child — a girl of perhaps three or four, wearing clothing of unexpected quality, speaking a strange mixture of baby-English and Welsh. "I'se Bardie. Didn't 'a know that?" (005-018) is her self-introduction. Davy, despite his poverty and misgivings, takes her home — a decision that transforms his life and drives the novel's plot.

## Key Developments

- **The derelict boat**: Davy spots the abandoned vessel and claims it as salvage (004-012)
- **The child discovered**: "A little orphan mermaid" (Ch. V title) is found inside, alive but alone
- **Davy's decision**: Despite his poverty, Davy resolves to care for the child — "what did I do but take her home?" (005-045)

## Key Participants

- [[davy-llewellyn]] — The fisherman who discovers and rescues Bardie
- [[bardie]] — The discovered child, whose identity becomes the novel's central question

## Significance

This event functions as the novel's inciting incident. Bardie's discovery introduces the mystery of identity, the theme of providential care, and the conflict between Davy's poverty and his conscience that will animate the entire narrative.
