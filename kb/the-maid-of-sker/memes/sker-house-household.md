# Sker House Household

The small, isolated domestic establishment at Sker — Evan Thomas the warren-keeper, his wife Moxy, the boy Watty — whose members are drawn, reluctantly, into the mystery of the shipwrecked child.

## Organization Overview

Sker House is a real building but Blackmore's rendering of its household is fictional. Evan Thomas presides over the rabbit-warren; his wife Moxy manages the domestic sphere; the boy Watty runs errands and provides information. The household is isolated, self-contained, and suspicious of outsiders — a domestic microcosm of the novel's larger themes of belonging and exclusion.

## Role in the Novel

The Sker household provides an alternative domestic space to Davy's cottage at Newton Nottage. It is darker, stranger, more isolated — a Gothic household in miniature. The children Bardie and Bunny spend time there, and the household's members become witnesses to and participants in the central mystery.

## Significance

As an organization, the Sker household represents the pre-modern domestic unit — hierarchical, isolated, rooted in place — against which the novel measures other forms of social organization, from the Navy to the parish.
