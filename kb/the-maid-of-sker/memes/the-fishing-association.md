# The Fishing Association

The commercial partnership that Davy forms with his patron — a modest business organization that represents his social ascent and the novel's theme of earned prosperity.

## Organization Overview

Late in the novel, Davy enters into a fishing partnership with his wealthy patron: "we established a fishing association, under the name" (024-040). This organization formalizes what had been Davy's solitary livelihood, transforming him from independent fisherman to partner in a commercial enterprise. The association is a marker of Davy's rise — from subsistence to security, from isolation to institutional belonging.

## Role in the Novel

The fishing association represents the novel's vision of legitimate economic organization — neither the corrupt network of the smuggling ring nor the inherited hierarchy of the parish, but a voluntary partnership based on skill and mutual benefit. It is the institutional form of Davy's competence, and its success validates the novel's faith in honest labor rewarded.

## Significance

The fishing association, though briefly described, is thematically important: it completes Davy's arc from isolated widower to established member of a commercial community. It is the institutional expression of the novel's conviction that virtue and competence can, with patronage and luck, achieve modest prosperity.
