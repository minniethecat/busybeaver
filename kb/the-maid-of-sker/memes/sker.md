# Sker

The desolate stretch of Glamorganshire coast — comprising Sker Point, Sker House, and the surrounding sandhills — that is the novel's central location and the source of its title.

## Location

Sker is a real place on the Glamorganshire coast of South Wales, a rocky headland with dangerous offshore reefs. Blackmore renders it as a landscape of "desolate waste of sand, and the continual roar of the sea" (006-004) — a fringe where land and sea meet in mutual hostility. Sker House, "the ancient house" (003-002), is the seat of the rabbit-warren kept by Evan Thomas.

## Significance in the Novel

Sker is where Davy fishes, where Bardie is discovered, and where the great shipwreck draws the community to the shore. The house "always seemed even more desolate and forlorn with the summer sunlight upon it" (041-021) — a place where Gothic atmosphere intensifies rather than lifts with fair weather. Sker is also the site of the wrecker's depredations and Chowne's final madness.

> To fish at Sker had always been a matter of some risk and conflict.

## Historical Note

Sker House and Sker Point are real locations in present-day Bridgend County Borough, Wales. The house dates from the 16th century and the point is known for its dangerous reefs — the same reefs that feature in Blackmore's novel.
