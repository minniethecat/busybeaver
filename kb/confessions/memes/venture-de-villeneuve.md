## Biography

Venture de Villeneuve was a French musician and adventurer who arrived in Annecy one cold February evening, presenting himself to Le Maitre's household with an easy confidence. Rousseau described him vividly: "he was short and thick, having some fault in his shape, though without any particular deformity" (003-077). Despite his threadbare appearance, Venture possessed an undeniable charm and a wide range of talents.

> He told us his name was Venture de Villeneuve, that he came from Paris, had lost his way, and seeming to forget that he had announced himself for a musician, added that he was going to Grenoble to see a relation that was in the parliament. (003-077)

His musical abilities were genuine. The next day he sang at the cathedral, astonishing everyone with his skill. "He sung his two recitatives with all imaginable taste and judgment; and what was yet more, with a very agreeable voice. I never enjoyed a more pleasing surprise" (003-079).

Rousseau's admiration for Venture was profound: "My attachment to M. Venture, more reasonable in its cause, was also less extravagant in its effects, though more lively and durable than that I had conceived for M. Bacle. I loved to see him, to hear him, all his actions appeared charming, everything he said was an oracle to me" (003-081).

> Playful, giddy, inexhaustible, seducing in conversation, ever smiling, but never laughing, and repeating the rudest things in the most elegant manner—even the most modest women were astonished at what they heard. (003-080)

## Relation to Rousseau

Venture made a lasting impression on the sixteen-year-old Rousseau, who became infatuated with the worldly adventurer. However, Madame de Warens disapproved of the friendship, perceiving Venture as a libertine and forbidding Rousseau from associating with him.

> I spoke of him with transport to Madam de Warens, Le Maitre likewise spoke in his praise, and she consented we should bring him to her house. This interview did not succeed; he thought her affected, she found him a libertine, and, alarmed that I had formed such an ill acquaintance, not only forbade me bringing him there again, but likewise painted the danger I ran in so strong a light, that I became a little more circumspect in my intercourse with him. (003-081)

Rousseau's identification with Venture was so strong that he later modeled his own persona on him. When traveling to Lausanne, Rousseau anagrammatically transformed his name from Rousseau into "Vaussore de Villeneuve," deliberately imitating Venture's style.

> He called himself Venture de Villeneuve. I changed, by anagram, the name Rousseau into that of Vaussore, calling myself Monsieur Vaussore de Villeneuve. Venture was a good composer, though he had not said so; without knowing anything of the art, I boasted of my skill to every one. (004-043)

Years later, Rousseau encountered Venture again, this time in Paris, where Venture had since found success.

> My friend Venture, accompanied by another man, came to see me unexpectedly one morning when I was going to dine at the palace. (009-035)

## Significance

Venture de Villeneuve represents the archetype of the charming adventurer in Rousseau's *Confessions* — a figure whose worldly talents and self-confidence fascinated the young, impressionable Rousseau. The episode illustrates Rousseau's susceptibility to charismatic personalities and his tendency toward hero-worship. Venture's influence extended beyond mere admiration: Rousseau literally adopted Venture's name and manner, revealing how deeply the young man craved a model of sophisticated, cosmopolitan identity distinct from his provincial Genevan origins.

Rousseau coined the verb "Venturise" (to Venturise) from Venture's name, meaning to engage in extravagant, self-dramatizing behavior: "to comprehend how completely my brain was turned, and to what degree I had 'Venturised' (if I may be allowed the expression), the many extravagances I ran into at the same time should be considered" (004-043). This neologism testifies to Venture's enduring place in Rousseau's imaginative landscape.
