M. de Montmollin was the Protestant minister of [[Motiers|Môtiers]] during Rousseau's refuge there from 1762 to 1765. Initially sympathetic to Rousseau, Montmollin later became one of his most determined persecutors, orchestrating the campaign that forced Rousseau to flee the village.

## Biography

Montmollin served as the minister of the Protestant parish in Môtiers, a village in the [[Neuchâtel|Principality of Neuchâtel]] then under Prussian rule. As minister, he held considerable influence over the local Consistory (church court) and the Classe (the regional assembly of pastors).

When Rousseau arrived in Môtiers, Montmollin initially received him favorably:

> I sent a copy of them to M. de Montmollin, who received it favorably... (012-078)

However, this goodwill did not last. After Rousseau's controversial writings drew criticism, Montmollin turned against him and actively worked to have him expelled.

## Relation to Rousseau

Rousseau's relationship with Montmollin deteriorated rapidly. Rousseau wrote to Montmollin to demonstrate his Christian faith ahead of a planned communion:

> ...communion approaching, I wrote to M. de Montmollin, the minister, to prove to him... (012-029)

This gesture backfired. Rousseau was severely rebuked for having written to Montmollin and for having "compromised" himself:

> ...severely for having written to M. de Montmollin, and especially for having compromised... (012-030)

From that point, Montmollin became Rousseau's enemy. He maneuvered within church institutions to build opposition:

> ...the manoeuvres and bottles of Montmollin, most of the elders were well... (012-081)

> Montmollin procured from the Classe (the regional church assembly) support for his campaign against Rousseau... (012-079)

When Montmollin sought to proceed to excommunication, he faced some resistance:

> ...the rest to their duty, and when Montmollin wished to proceed to excommunication... (012-083)

Montmollin acted as mediator in dealings with the Classe, while secretly working against Rousseau:

> ...with the Classe, in which Montmollin was the mediator. He feigned... (012-084)

A colonel sympathetic to Rousseau eventually managed to make Montmollin submit in his Consistory:

> ...colonel who found means to make Montmollin submit in his Consistory, by... (012-086)

Even [[Madame d'Epinay|Madam d'Epinay]]'s associate was seen in conversation with Montmollin, suggesting a network of opposition:

> ...was seen in conversation with Montmollin. She did not, however, seem to... (012-087)

After [[Thérèse Levasseur]] departed Môtiers, Montmollin continued his machinations:

> After her departure, Montmollin carried on his manoeuvres with... (012-089)

## Significance

Montmollin is a key figure in the narrative of Rousseau's persecution during his Swiss exile. He exemplifies the conflict between Rousseau's personal religious convictions and institutional orthodoxy — Rousseau saw himself as a sincere Christian, while Montmollin and the Protestant establishment viewed him as a dangerous heretic. The episode in Môtiers, culminating in the stoning of Rousseau's house and his flight to [[Saint-Pierre Island]], is one of the most dramatic in [[Confessions|the Confessions]].
