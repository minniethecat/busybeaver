# The Demonic

**The Demonic** (*daemonicum*) is the reality of evil spiritual beings opposed to God. In Hrotsvit's plays, the demonic is the invisible enemy — operating through pagan persecution, sexual temptation, and resistance to [[conversion]].

## Demons and Pagan Gods

Hrotsvit identifies the Roman gods with demons. When [[agape|Agape]] and [[irena|Irena]] refuse to sacrifice, they are refusing not merely a civic ritual but participation in demonic worship. The equation of pagan gods with demons — drawn from Psalm 96 and the patristic tradition — transforms the martyr plays from political drama into [[spiritual-warfare|spiritual combat]]. Gallicanus admits that he "sought the help of gods and demons" (001-043), and the virgin martyrs refuse to "sacrifice to demons" (002-023). The emperor is not merely a tyrant but a demon's instrument.

> Agape: We offer a perpetual sacrifice of praise to the true God, the eternal Father, to His Son, co-eternal, and to the Holy Ghost.
>
> Sisinnius: I do not speak of that sacrifice. That is prohibited on pain of the most severe penalties.
>
> Agape: You have no power over us, and can never compel us to sacrifice to demons.
>
> (002-023)

## Demons and Temptation

The hermit dramas treat temptation as demonic in origin. [[mary|Mary]]'s fall and [[thais|Thais]]'s life of prostitution are not merely human moral failures but demonic captivities — the women have been ensnared by spiritual enemies. This is why their rescue requires a saint: only someone who has defeated demons in the [[the-desert|desert]] can liberate those the demons have captured. When Mary falls, Ephrem laments that "we are at the mercy of the demons" (004-009), and Antony must "wrestle with the demon" (005-018) to secure Thais's salvation.

## Demonic Resistance to Conversion

Every [[conversion]] in the plays encounters demonic resistance. [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]]'s conversion requires a heavenly host to counter the demonic forces of paganism. [[callimachus|Callimachus]]'s conversion requires a serpent's lethal intervention — extreme measures for an extreme possession. The demonic does not yield territory willingly.
