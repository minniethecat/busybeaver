**Chionia** is the middle sister of the three [[chastity|virgin]] [[martyrdom|martyr]]s in [[Dulcitius|Dulcitius (Play II)]]. (002-001) When [[agape|Agape]] — the eldest — is dismissed by [[diocletian|Diocletian]] as "raving," Chionia steps forward immediately to defend her: "My sister does not rave. She is right." (002-013) Her role is that of the loyal [[martyrdom|witness]] — shorter in speech than [[agape]] or [[irena]], but unwavering in solidarity, and sharing [[agape]]'s [[martyrdom|martyr]]dom by fire at the play's climax. (002-023)

> Chionia | My sister does not rave. She is right.
>
> (002-013)

## Biography

Chionia is a Christian virgin of noble Roman family in [[thessalonica|Thessalonica]], living under the [[diocletian|Diocletian]]ic persecution. (002-001) With her elder sister [[agape]] and younger sister [[irena]], she has consecrated her life to Christ. When the Emperor demands the three sisters marry his courtiers and sacrifice to the Roman gods, Chionia stands with [[agape]] from the first moment — and when [[diocletian|Diocletian]] insults [[agape]], Chionia's swift defense reveals the unbreakable bond of sisterly solidarity that defines the play. (002-013)

## Role in the Plays

Chionia's role in [[play-Dulcitius|Dulcitius]] is defined by three dramatic beats:

1. **Defense of [[agape|Agape]]** — Her single most important line ("My sister does not rave. She is right") establishes her role as the loyal [[martyrdom|witness]] and second voice (002-013)
2. **Witness to [[dulcitius-character|Dulcitius]]'s Madness** — With her sisters, she observes the divine humiliation of [[dulcitius-character|Governor Dulcitius]] from their cell, joining in the wry humor of the scene: "Now may God protect us!" (002-016)
3. **[[martyrdom|Martyrdom]] alongside [[agape|Agape]]** — When [[sisinnius|Sisinnius]] orders the executions, Chionia is burned with [[agape]]; [[irena]], as the youngest, is killed separately (002-023)

## Key Scenes

1. **The Defense** — When [[diocletian|Diocletian]] dismisses [[agape]] as raving and orders her removed, Chionia speaks her crucial line of solidarity: "My sister does not rave. She is right." (002-013)
2. **The Humiliation Scene** — With her sisters, Chionia watches through the cell door as [[dulcitius-character|Dulcitius]], driven mad by divine intervention, embraces sooty pots and pans, believing he is assaulting the virgins. (002-016)
3. **Before [[sisinnius|Sisinnius]]** — The new governor singles out [[agape]] and Chionia together for his demand to sacrifice; they refuse together. (002-023)
4. **[[martyrdom|Martyrdom]]** — Chionia dies with [[agape]] in the flames, the two elder sisters united in death as in [[martyrdom|witness]]. (002-023)

> Sisinnius | Agape, and you, Chionia, take my advice. | Agape | And if we do, what then?
>
> (002-023)

## Themes

Chionia exemplifies loyal [[martyrdom|witness]] — the middle voice that amplifies the elder's defiance and models courage for the younger. (002-013) Her name may evoke "snow" (Greek *chiōn*), suggesting [[chastity|purity]], fitting for a virgin [[martyrdom|martyr]]. In Hrotsvit's dramatic economy, Chionia is neither the rhetorical champion ([[agape]]) nor the final survivor ([[irena]]), but the steady second — the one who confirms, who stands beside, who refuses alone when alone is required but prefers to stand with. Her role is small but structurally essential: without her defense of [[agape]], the sisters' solidarity would not be dramatized. (002-013)
