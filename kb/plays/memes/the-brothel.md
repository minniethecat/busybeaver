# The Brothel

**The Brothel** (*lupanar*) is a crucial dramatic setting in Hrotsvit's two hermit dramas — the place of maximum distance from God, where the rescue mission of [[abraham|Abraham]] and [[paphnutius|Paphnutius]] must penetrate.

## The Descent into the Brothel

Both hermit dramas are structured around a descent: the holy man leaves the [[the-desert|desert]] and enters the brothel. [[abraham|Abraham]] disguises himself as a lover to gain access to [[mary|Mary]] (004-011), eating and drinking with her before revealing his identity (004-013). [[paphnutius|Paphnutius]] similarly adopts disguise to reach [[thais|Thais]] (005-011). The brothel is the anti-desert — the place where the body rules, where souls are sold, where God seems absent. The saint's entry into this space is an incarnational act, mirroring Christ's descent into the world of sin to rescue the lost.

## The Brothel as Dramatic Device

The brothel functions dramaturgically as the site of maximum tension: will the saint be recognized? Will the fallen woman listen? The disguise scenes are among Hrotsvit's most theatrically effective — comedy and pathos intertwined as the holy man plays a role that contradicts his nature. The revelatory moment, when the disguise drops and the sinner recognizes her rescuer, is the emotional climax of both plays.

## Sin and Place

Hrotsvit's use of the brothel reflects a broader pattern in her spatial theology: sin has a geography. The [[the-desert|desert]] is the place of purity; the city is the place of temptation; the brothel is the place of consummated sin. But Hrotsvit's innovation is that no place is beyond reach — the saint penetrates even the brothel, and the brothel's inmate can become a saint.


Sisinnius orders Irena to be dragged to the brothel as punishment for her defiance:

> Have no pity. Be rough with her, and drag her to the lowest brothel you can find.
>
> (002-024)
