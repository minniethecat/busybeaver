# The Roman Empire

**The Roman Empire** (*Imperium Romanum*) is the political framework of all six of Hrotsvit's plays — the late antique world in which Christian [[martyrdom]] and [[conversion]] unfold. It is simultaneously the arena of persecution and the field of Christian mission.

## Empire as Antagonist

In the martyr plays, the Empire is the antagonist — the political system that demands [[sacrifice]] to Roman gods and kills those who refuse. The emperors [[diocletian|Diocletian]], [[hadrian|Hadrian]], and [[julian-the-apostate|Julian]] are not private individuals acting from personal malice but officers of a system that cannot tolerate Christian allegiance to a higher king. The Empire's power is absolute over bodies — imprisonment, torture, death — and nothing over souls.

> According to the old Roman custom. — To my thinking, a bad custom. — I am of the same mind.
>
> (001-043)

## Empire as Mission Field

But the Empire is also the field of Christian mission. [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]] is converted within the imperial army; [[constantine|Constantine]] Christianizes the imperial office itself. The trajectory of the plays — from Diocletian's persecution to Constantine's conversion — traces the Empire's transformation from persecutor to protector of the Church (006-010). Hrotsvit's Ottonian patrons understood themselves as the heirs of this Constantinian Christian empire.

## The Two Romes

Hrotsvit distinguishes implicitly between pagan [[rome|Rome]] (the city of the Capitol, the amphitheater, the persecuting emperor) and Christian Rome (the city of Constantine, the [[the-catacombs|catacombs]], the martyrs' tombs). The same city contains both realities — which is why the plays are set in Rome but oriented toward its Christian future.
