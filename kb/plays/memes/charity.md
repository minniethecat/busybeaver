**Charity** (Latin: *Caritas*) is the eight-year-old youngest daughter of [[sapientia|Sapientia]] ([[sapientia|Wisdom]]) in [[sapientia|Sapientia (Play VI)]]. (006-001) Though the smallest and youngest — "that obstinate little neck" as her executioner calls her — Charity is the fiercest of the three sisters in her defiance. Her age makes her [[martyrdom|martyr]]dom the most shocking; her courage makes it the most compelling. She dies last, after [[faith|Faith]] and [[hope|Hope]], having [[martyrdom|witness]]ed both their deaths and having only grown more resolute.

> The youngest Charity. | Hadrian | And how old are you, child? | Charity | Eight years.
>
> (006-012)

## Biography

Charity is eight years old — "the youngest Charity" as [[hadrian|Hadrian]] describes her. (006-012) She is the third daughter of [[sapientia|Sapientia]] and personifies *caritas* (love/charity), the greatest of the three theological virtues in the [[john-and-paul|Paul]]ine formulation. In Hrotsvit's dramatic scheme, Charity's extreme youth creates a crescendo of horror and glory: each successive death is visited upon a younger victim, and each successive [[martyrdom|martyr]] is more resolute than the last.

## Role in the Plays

Charity's role in [[sapientia]] is the dramatic climax of the [[martyrdom|martyr]]dom sequence:

1. **The Littlest Voice** — In the opening interrogation, Charity confirms her faith alongside her sisters (006-013)
2. **Witness to Two Deaths** — She watches [[faith|Faith]] and [[hope|Hope]] die before her, a trial that would break most adults (006-014)
3. **Obstinate Little Neck** — The executioner singles her out as particularly stubborn: "that obstinate little neck" (006-015)
4. **Last [[martyrdom|Martyrdom]]** — Charity dies third, completing the triptych of virtue and the sacrifice of [[sapientia|Wisdom]]'s children (006-014)

> Antiochus | You mean to Charity? | Hadrian | Who else? She is the youngest — but the most stubborn.
>
> (006-015)

## Key Scenes

1. **First Confession** — Before [[hadrian|Hadrian]], the eight-year-old declares her faith with her sisters. (006-013)
2. **Maternal Teaching** — [[sapientia|Sapientia]] reminds her of the faith she was taught at the breast; Charity responds that she cannot forget. (006-013)
3. **Witness to Sisters' Deaths** — Charity watches both [[faith|Faith]] and [[hope|Hope]] die, each death strengthening rather than weakening her resolve. (006-014)
4. **The Sword** — The executioner calls for "that obstinate little neck" to be prepared for the sword — [[play-Sapientia|and Charity]] meets it. (006-015)

> Charity | Can we forget what we learned at our mother's knee — and at her breast?
>
> (006-013)

## Themes

Charity is Hrotsvit's most extreme statement on the power of grace in weakness. An eight-year-old facing a Roman executioner is the play's master image — the disproportion between threat and victim is total, yet the child's courage reverses the power dynamic. (006-012) Her youth is not weakness but [[martyrdom|witness]]: the smaller the [[martyrdom|martyr]], the more evident the divine power that sustains her. As the personification of *caritas*, Charity holds the climactic position because love is the greatest of the three — and the last to die is the one who dies most fiercely.
