# Baptism

Baptism is the Christian sacrament of initiation, presented in Hrotsvit's plays as the decisive ritual moment of conversion and purification. In the world of the plays, baptism marks the boundary between the old life of paganism and the new life of Christian faith.

## In Gallicanus

Baptism is most elaborately treated in [[play-Gallicanus|Gallicanus]], where it serves as the culmination of the general's conversion narrative. After his battlefield vision and return to Rome, Gallicanus declares: "purified in the waters of baptism, I have surrendered myself completely to the keeping of Christ and will make a humble confession of faith in the Church of the Apostles" (001-041). The ritual is not merely symbolic but transformative — it purifies the convert and enrolls him in the visible community of the Church.

## The Martyrs and Baptism

Baptism recurs in connection with martyrdom. When Terentianus's son is possessed before the tomb of John and Paul, the martyrs' intercession restores him — but only "if you were first cleansed by baptism" (001-052). After the boy's healing, the assembled Christians pray "that we, purified in the waters of baptism, may persevere in the faith" (001-053). The juxtaposition of baptism and martyrdom reflects the early Christian theology of "baptism of blood" — that martyrdom itself serves as baptism for the unbaptized.

## Baptismal Imagery in Paphnutius

In [[play-Paphnutius|Paphnutius]], the conversion of Thais is described in baptismal terms. Though the play does not depict the ritual directly, Antony's disciple Paul reports that "she wore the baptismal robes of a child of God" (005-015), suggesting that her repentance culminated in sacramental initiation.


Gallicanus declares his baptismal transformation before the Emperor:

> For my own part, purified in the waters of baptism, I have surrendered myself completely to the will of God. I am ready to renounce even your daughter, whom I love more than anything in the world. I wish to abstain from marriage that I may devote myself wholly to the service of the Virgin's Son.
>
> (001-041)
