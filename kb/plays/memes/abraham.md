**Abraham** is the [[conversion|hermit]] protagonist of [[play-Abraham|Play IV]] — a desert ascetic whose spiritual perfection is tested not by his own temptations but by the fall of his niece [[mary|Mary]]. When [[mary|Mary]] abandons her religious vows and becomes a harlot, Abraham disguises himself as a lover, travels to the city, and reclaims her — enacting one of Hrotsvit's most psychologically complex dramas of [[penance|mercy]] and redemption. (004-001)

> The fall and repentance of Mary, the niece of the hermit Abraham, who, after she has spent twenty years in the desert as a penitent, is restored to her uncle.
>
> (004-001)

## Biography

Abraham is a hermit living in the Egyptian desert near [[ephrem|Ephrem]], his fellow ascetic and friend. (004-007) He has raised his orphaned niece [[mary|Mary]] from childhood, dedicating her to the religious life in a nearby cell. (004-008) When [[mary|Mary]], seduced by a false monk, flees to the city and becomes a prostitute, Abraham undertakes a mission of [[conversion|rescue]] that spans two years of searching before he finds her. (004-001)

> Abraham and Paphnutius show Roswitha at her best as a dramatist. In Abraham, the episode of the hermit disguising himself to reclaim his fallen niece has a psychological truth rare in medieval literature.
>
> (P06-009)

## Role in the Plays

Abraham is the protagonist of [[play-Abraham|Play IV]] and, alongside [[paphnutius|Paphnutius]], represents Hrotsvit's ideal of the active contemplative. His dramatic function parallels [[paphnutius|Paphnutius]] — both leave the desert to save a fallen woman — but with a crucial difference: [[mary|Mary]] is his adopted child. Abraham's mission is motivated by personal love as well as [[conversion|spiritual]] duty, giving the play a familial tenderness absent from the more abstract [[play-Paphnutius|Paphnutius]]. (P06-009)

## Key Scenes

1. **[[mary|Mary]]'s Fall** — Abraham learns from [[ephrem|Ephrem]] that [[mary|Mary]] has abandoned her cell and is working in a brothel. (004-009)
2. **The Search** — Abraham spends two years searching for [[mary|Mary]] before finding her. (004-001)
3. **The Disguised Visit** — Abraham adopts the guise of a lover to gain access to [[mary|Mary]]'s chamber — the same device [[paphnutius|Paphnutius]] uses with [[thais|Thais]]. (004-001)
4. **The Revelation** — Abraham reveals his true identity, and [[mary|Mary]]'s [[conversion|repentance]] is immediate. (004-013)
5. **Twenty Years of [[penance|Penance]]** — [[mary|Mary]] undertakes two decades of ascetic [[penance]] in the desert, far longer than her two years of sin. (004-001)

> Abraham | Mary, my child by adoption, whom I have cherished from your cradle — what has become of your virginal modesty?
>
> (004-013)

## Themes

Abraham embodies spiritual fatherhood — the perfected soul whose love for [[mary|Mary]] compels him to descend from the desert into the world of sin, not to condemn but to [[conversion|reclaim]]. (P06-009) His disguise as a lover is Hrotsvit's most psychologically acute use of the device: the father performs the role of seducer to save his child from seduction. (004-001) The twenty years of [[mary|Mary]]'s [[penance]] are supervised by Abraham — he does not merely rescue her but guides her through the long discipline of restoration. (004-001)
