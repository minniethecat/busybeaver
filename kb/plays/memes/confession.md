# Confession

**Confession** (*confessio*) carries a double meaning in Hrotsvit's plays: it is both the public profession of Christian faith before persecutors and the private acknowledgment of sin that initiates [[penance]].

## Public Confession

In the martyr plays, confession is the speech-act that triggers persecution. When [[agape|Agape]], [[chionia|Chionia]], and [[irena|Irena]] confess Christ before [[diocletian|Diocletian]], they are not merely stating a belief — they are performing the act for which they will be killed. Confession is the verbal form of [[martyrdom]], the testimony (*martyria*) that precedes the death.

> You have no power over us, and can never compel us to sacrifice to demons.
> (002-026)

## Private Confession

In the hermit dramas, confession functions as the gateway to [[penance]]. [[mary|Mary]] must acknowledge her fall before [[abraham|Abraham]] can guide her restoration. [[thais|Thais]] confesses her sinful life to [[paphnutius|Paphnutius]] before entering the sealed cell. In [[play-Callimachus|Callimachus]], the protagonist's confession of faith after his resurrection is his first speech as a converted soul (006-014).

## Confession and Conversion

Confession is the verbal component of [[conversion]] — the moment when inner transformation becomes outer declaration. Gallicanus's battlefield confession of faith (001-041), followed immediately by [[baptism]], demonstrates the sequence: interior change, public declaration, sacramental seal. The pattern recurs across the corpus.
