# Wisdom

**Wisdom** (*sapientia*) is the intellectual virtue of right judgment illuminated by faith. In Hrotsvit's plays, wisdom is personified in [[sapientia|Sapientia]], the mother who instructs her daughters in [[martyrdom]] and confounds Emperor [[hadrian|Hadrian]] with arithmetic.

## Sapientia's Wisdom

[[sapientia|Sapientia]] in the play that bears her name embodies wisdom as both knowledge and virtue. She defeats Hadrian not with suffering but with discourse — her mathematical exposition on the properties of numbers leaves the emperor baffled and the audience instructed (006-014). Wisdom here is not merely passive endurance but active intellectual engagement — the mind that grasps truth cannot be coerced by power.

## Wisdom and Fear of God

Hrotsvit inherits the biblical teaching that "the [[fear-of-god|fear of the Lord]] is the beginning of wisdom." Wisdom without fear is cleverness; fear without wisdom is terror. Sapientia's daughters — [[faith|Faith]], [[hope|Hope]], and [[charity|Charity]] — are educated in both: they fear God rightly and understand what that fear requires, which is why they can face [[martyrdom]] with clarity rather than panic (006-012).

## Hrotsvit's Wisdom

In her Prefaces, Hrotsvit claims wisdom as her authorial gift — the "perspicacious mind" given by God and cultivated through study (P04-003). Her wisdom is not that of the university but of the cloister: formed by [[liturgical-prayer|liturgy]], [[monastic-rule|monastic discipline]], and the study of classical and patristic texts. She is Sapientia's daughter as much as Faith, Hope, and Charity are.
