# The Catacombs

**The Catacombs** (*catacumbae*) are the underground burial networks beneath [[rome|Rome]] where early Christians interred their dead — especially [[martyrdom|martyrs]] — and worshipped in secret during periods of persecution.

## Burial of the Martyrs

The catacombs are the resting place of the martyrs whose stories Hrotsvit dramatizes. Though the plays focus on the moment of [[martyrdom]] rather than its aftermath, the catacombs are the implied destination of the martyred body — the place where the witness's remains become relics. [[john-and-paul|John and Paul]], executed secretly under [[julian-the-apostate|Julian]], were buried in their own house (001-003), which became a site of posthumous [[miracle|miracles]] (001-052).

## Hidden Church

The catacombs represent the hidden dimension of the early Church — the community that worships underground while the Empire rules above. This spatial opposition (surface/catacomb, visible/hidden, imperial/Christian) mirrors the plays' central theological opposition between [[the-world|the world]] and the Kingdom of God. The true Church is invisible to imperial eyes.

## Relics and Pilgrimage

By Hrotsvit's time, the catacombs had become sites of pilgrimage. The bones of martyrs were translated to churches throughout Europe; [[gandersheim-abbey|Gandersheim]] itself possessed relics (P05-003). Hrotsvit's plays participate in this cult of the martyrs — they are literary pilgrimages to the sites where sanctity was perfected.
