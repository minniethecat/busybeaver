# Prayer

**Prayer** in Hrotsvit's plays is both a constant background practice and a decisive dramatic mechanism. Hermits chant divine praises; [[martyrdom|martyr]]s pray before death; saints intercede for sinners; and communal prayer produces miracles. Prayer is the primary channel through which divine power enters the dramatic world.

## As Dramatic Turning Point

Prayer in Hrotsvit is rarely passive — it drives the plot. [[constance|Constance]]'s prayer for [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]]'s conversion directly precipitates the heavenly vision that saves his army and transforms his soul (001-004)(002-011). [[paphnutius|Paphnutius]]'s inability to discern whether [[thais|Thais]]'s penance is accepted leads him to seek [[antony|Antony]]'s intercessory prayers, which produce the vision of the heavenly bed (005-009)(006-010). The Apostle [[john-and-paul|John]]'s prayer at [[drusiana|Drusiana]]'s tomb restores both her and [[callimachus|Callimachus]] to life (003-008).

## The Antony-Paphnutius Model

The play *[[paphnutius|Paphnutius]]* contains Hrotsvit's most developed theology of intercessory prayer. [[paphnutius|Paphnutius]], himself a holy hermit, nevertheless feels his prayers are insufficient and seeks the greater sanctity of [[antony|Antony]]. The two gather [[antony|Antony]]'s disciples for communal supplication, and the result is [[john-and-paul|Paul]]'s vision — divine confirmation that [[thais|Thais]] is forgiven (005-010)(006-011).

| [[antony|Antony]] | Those who unite in prayer can obtain whatever they desire. (005-011)(006-012) |

This line articulates the play's — and arguably Hrotsvit's — theology of prayer: it is most powerful when communal, and its efficacy is guaranteed by Christ's promise.

## Prayer as Identity

For the hermits, prayer defines their very mode of existence. [[abraham|Abraham]] approaches [[ephrem|Ephrem]] by asking whether he should wait until [[ephrem|Ephrem]] finishes "your divine praises" (004-001)(003-005). [[mary|Mary]]'s failure to chant the divine office is the first sign of her fall; her resumption of "continual vigils and fasts" marks her restoration (004-007)(003-011). For the hermits, ceasing to pray is the first step toward sin; resuming prayer is the first step toward redemption.

## Martyrs' Prayer

The virgin [[martyrdom|martyr]]s pray in extremis — not for deliverance but for death. [[agape|Agape]], facing the flames, prays: "O Lord, we know Thy power! It would not be anything strange or new if the fire forgot its nature and obeyed Thee. But we are weary of this world, and we implore Thee to break the bonds that chain our souls" (002-011)(001-023). [[sapientia|Sapientia]]'s final prayer at her daughters' graves is her own passage into eternity — forty days of prayer culminating in the soul's flight to heaven (006-014).


John and Paul reflect on the power of Gallicanus's battlefield prayer:

> How much more powerful is one fervent prayer than all the pride of man!
>
> (001-040)
