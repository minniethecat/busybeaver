# Terentianus

Terentianus is a supporting character in the second half of Hrotsvit's play *Gallicanus*, serving as the agent of Emperor Julian the Apostate in the persecution of the Christian martyrs John and Paul.

## Role in the Play

Terentianus is introduced in the dramatis personae (001-026) and appears after Julian's accession to the throne. The Emperor commands him: "Go, Terentianus. Take with you a few trusted soldiers and compel John and Paul to sacrifice to Jupiter. If they persist in their refusal, let them be put to death, not publicly, but with the greatest possible secrecy, since they once held office in this palace" (001-050).

## The Execution

Terentianus dutifully carries out Julian's orders. He presents John and Paul with a gold statue of Jupiter and demands they burn incense before it. When they refuse, declaring "we have no master except our Lord Jesus Christ" (001-051), Terentianus orders the soldiers to execute them: "Draw your swords and strike these traitors to the gods and to their Emperor. When they have breathed their last bury them secretly in this house and remove every trace of blood" (001-051).

## Repentance and Conversion

Terentianus's son is later struck with demonic possession before the tomb of the martyrs. When confronted with the boy's suffering, Terentianus confesses: "Mine the fault! Mine the crime! It was at my command that the wretched boy laid his impious hands on those holy martyrs" (001-052). Acknowledging divine justice, he throws himself before the tombs of John and Paul, praying: "Glorious witnesses of Christ, John and Paul, follow the example and commandment of your Master, and pray for your persecutors" (001-053). His son is healed through the martyrs' intercession, and Terentianus is baptized.

## Significance

Terentianus represents Hrotsvit's theme of repentance and divine mercy extended even to persecutors. His story arc — from obedient agent of persecution to penitent convert — parallels the conversion of Gallicanus himself and reinforces the play's message that no sin is beyond divine forgiveness. The healing of his son at the martyrs' tomb also serves as miraculous testimony to the power of the saints.

## The Imperial Command

> Terentianus | Paul and John, the Emperor Julian, my master, of his clemency sends you this gold statue of Jupiter, and commands you to burn incense before it. Refuse, and you will be put to death. |
> | John | Since Julian is your master, live at peace with him, and enjoy his favour. But we have no master except our Lord Jesus Christ, for Whose love we ardently desire to die that we may the more quickly taste the joys of eternity.
>
> (001-051)
