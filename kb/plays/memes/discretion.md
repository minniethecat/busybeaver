# Discretion

**Discretion** (*discretio*, *discretio spirituum*) is the spiritual gift of distinguishing between good and evil impulses — what the desert tradition called the "mother of virtues." In Hrotsvit's plays, discretion is the quality that enables [[abraham|Abraham]] and [[paphnutius|Paphnutius]] to guide souls.

## The Mother of Virtues

The desert fathers taught that discretion was the virtue that governed all other virtues. [[fasting|Fasting]] without discretion becomes bodily destruction; [[prayer]] without discretion becomes fantasy; [[asceticism]] without discretion becomes pride. Discretion is the capacity to judge what is needed, when, and in what measure — the spiritual prudence that distinguishes the saint from the fanatic.

## Discretion in the Hermit Dramas

[[abraham|Abraham]] exercises discretion in his approach to [[mary|Mary]]. He does not storm the brothel denouncing sin — he enters disguised, eats and drinks with her, and only gradually reveals himself. This patient approach, calibrated to Mary's condition, is discretion in action. (004-009) [[paphnutius|Paphnutius]] shows the same quality in his Socratic dialogue with [[thais|Thais]] — reasoning rather than exhorting, adapting his method to the soul he seeks to save.

> | Ephrem | No one will blame you, brother. It will be but praiseworthy discretion on your part to loosen the bridle of strict observance for the sake of bringing back a soul.
>
> (004-009)

## Discretion and Authority

The hermit's spiritual authority depends on discretion. Abraham and Paphnutius can guide others because they can read souls — knowing what remedy each condition requires. This is not psychological insight but spiritual charism, earned through decades of [[asceticism]] in the [[the-desert|desert]]. The term also appears in political contexts within the plays: Hadrian praises Antiochus for the discretion with which he guards imperial interests (006-010).
