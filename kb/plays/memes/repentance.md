# Repentance

Repentance is the interior act of sorrow for sin and turning (*metanoia*) toward God. In Hrotsvit's plays, it is dramatized as a transformative crisis — the moment when a character acknowledges guilt and turns to divine mercy. It is distinct from [[penance|Penance]], which refers to the outward works of satisfaction that follow repentance.

## In the Plays

### Gallicanus

The play [[play-Gallicanus|Gallicanus]] traces the general's movement from pagan military commander to repentant Christian. After his battlefield conversion, Gallicanus confesses his past persecution: "purified in the waters of baptism, I have surrendered myself completely to the keeping of Christ" (001-043).

### Callimachus

The repentance of Callimachus is the play's dramatic center. After Drusiana's miraculous resurrection, the apostle John declares: "great must be the sins which must be atoned for by a great repentance" (003-017). Callimachus responds: "Oh, if I could but repent!" The play dramatizes repentance as possible even for the most grievous sins.

### Abraham and Paphnutius

Both [[play-Abraham|Abraham]] and [[play-Paphnutius|Paphnutius]] center on the repentance of fallen women. The fall and repentance of Mary, "the niece of the hermit Abraham" (004-001), is presented as a pattern for all sinners. In Paphnutius, the reformed courtesan Thais demonstrates that "tears of repentance are precious in the sight of God" (005-011). Antony observes of Thais: "the repentance of the sinner has caused that there should be joy among the citizens of heaven" (P06-010).

## Theological Significance

In Hrotsvit's theology, repentance is never too late. As Abraham tells Mary: "It matters not how wickedness may have abounded, if the grace of penitence superabounds" (004-013). The hermit Ephrem echoes: "The tale of her repentance has turned many from their sins" (004-015). Repentance is not merely personal but exemplary — the repentant sinner becomes a witness drawing others to God.


Abraham pleads with the despairing Mary to trust in divine mercy:

> The mercy of heaven is greater than you or your sins. Let your sadness be dispersed by its glorious beams. Oh, Mary, do not let apathy prevent your seizing the moment for repentance. It matters not how wickedness has flourished. Divine grace can flourish still more abundantly!
>
> (004-013)
