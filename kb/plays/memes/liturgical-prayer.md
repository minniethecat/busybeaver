# Liturgical Prayer

**Liturgical Prayer** (*liturgia*, *opus Dei*) is the communal prayer of the Divine Office — the eight daily services that structured the day at [[gandersheim-abbey|Gandersheim]]. This liturgical rhythm was the framework within which [[hrotsvit|Hrotsvit]] lived, prayed, and wrote. (P05-005)

## The Divine Office

The Benedictine Office divides the day into eight prayer periods: Vigils (night), Lauds (dawn), Prime (first hour), Terce (third hour), Sext (sixth hour), None (ninth hour), Vespers (evening), and Compline (nightfall). Each Office includes psalms, readings, hymns, and prayers. The entire psalter is recited over the course of a week. This constant return to prayer creates a rhythm that sanctifies time itself.

## Liturgy and Literature

Hrotsvit's immersion in the psalms and scriptural readings of the Office shaped her Latin style. The psalter's vocabulary of praise, lament, and trust echoes through the plays' dialogue. The liturgical calendar — with its cycles of feasts and fasts, martyr commemorations and penitential seasons — provided the imaginative framework within which she understood the saints' stories.

## Liturgy in the Plays

Though the plays are set before the development of the Divine Office, liturgical patterns appear throughout. The martyrs' deaths are framed as sacrifices of praise — the language of the Eucharist. The hermits' [[vigil|night vigils]] anticipate the Office of Vigils. Hrotsvit reads the 4th-century Church through the lens of 10th-century liturgy.
