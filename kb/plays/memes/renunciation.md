# Renunciation

**Renunciation** (*renuntiatio*, *abrenuntiatio*) is the formal act of giving up worldly goods, status, and attachments for the sake of spiritual freedom. In Hrotsvit's plays, renunciation is the visible sign of [[conversion]] — the moment when inner transformation becomes outer action.

## Forms of Renunciation

Hrotsvit dramatizes several modes of renunciation. [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]] renounces military command, marriage to [[constance|Constance]], and ultimately his life under [[julian-the-apostate|Julian]] — a triple renunciation that traces the arc from world to God (001-043). [[thais|Thais]] renounces her wealth in a public bonfire — the most theatrical renunciation in the corpus (005-013). [[constance|Constance]] renounces marriage before the play opens, her secret vow the invisible engine of the entire plot (001-044).

## Renunciation as Liberation

For Hrotsvit, renunciation is never merely loss. Gallicanus loses a military career but gains spiritual freedom. Thais loses wealth but gains sanctity. The pattern is consistent: what is renounced is always lesser than what is gained. This is the economic logic of the plays — the exchange of earthly goods for heavenly ones, always at a profit.

## Renunciation and Conversion

Renunciation is the material expression of conversion. [[conversion]] is the interior turning; renunciation is the exterior enactment that makes the turning visible and irreversible. Gallicanus cannot be half-converted — his renunciation of [[constance|Constance]] and military command seals the conversion in embodied action.
