**John and Paul** are the Grand Almoners to [[constance|Constance]], daughter of [[constantine|Emperor Constantine]], and the twin instruments of the central conversion in [[play-Gallicanus|Gallicanus]]. Sent to accompany [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]] on his [[scythia|Scythian]] campaign in a strategic exchange — [[constance|Constance]] receives [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]]'s daughters [[attica|Attica]] and [[artemia|Artemia]] as pledges — John and Paul become the agents of [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]]'s battlefield [[conversion]] and later suffer [[martyrdom]] under [[julian-the-apostate|Julian the Apostate]] for refusing to sacrifice to Jupiter (001-003).

> Gallicanus is converted by John and Paul, Grand Almoners to Constance. He is immediately baptized and takes a vow of celibacy.
>
>
> (001-002)

## Biography

John and Paul are court officials in the imperial household of Emperor [[constantine|Constantine]] in the fourth century (001-001). As Grand Almoners to [[constance|Constance]] — the Emperor's consecrated virgin daughter — they hold high rank in the Christianized court (001-015). Their lives span two imperial reigns: they serve faithfully under [[constantine|Constantine]], then face persecution under his successor [[julian-the-apostate|Julian the Apostate]]. In their final act, they distribute the fortune [[constance|Constance]] left them to the poor, an act of Christian witness that provokes their arrest and execution (001-003).

> John and Paul, from the cradle you have been attached to the Emperor's household. You served my predecessor.
>
>
> (001-049)

## Role in the Plays

John and Paul are the spiritual engines of [[play-Gallicanus|Gallicanus]] — they convert the pagan general, witness to him during campaign, and later crown their faith with [[martyrdom]]. Their role spans the entire play across five key movements:

1. At [[constance|Constance]]'s direction, they accompany [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]] to war in the [[scythia|Scythian]] campaign (001-015)
2. When [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]] faces defeat, they counsel him to make a vow to the Creator, leading to his battlefield [[conversion]] and baptism (001-002)
3. On return, they testify to [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]]'s new faith before the Emperor
4. Under [[julian-the-apostate|Julian]], they refuse to sacrifice to Jupiter and are executed in secret in their own house (001-003)
5. Posthumously, their relics heal the possessed son of their executioner, who confesses and is baptized

## Key Scenes

1. **Appointment** — [[constance|Constance]] tells her father to send John and Paul with [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]], receiving his daughters as pledges in return — a gambit that ensures the general is surrounded by Christian witnesses.
2. **Battlefield Conversion** — Surrounded by the [[scythia|Scythian]] enemy, John and Paul advise [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]] to vow to the Creator; he does, and divine aid brings victory (001-002).
3. **Return and Testimony** — John and Paul present [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]]'s conversion to the imperial court as a fait accompli.
4. **Defiance Before [[julian-the-apostate|Julian]]** — Summoned by [[julian-the-apostate|Julian]], they refuse to sacrifice to Jupiter (001-049) and are killed secretly in their own house.
5. **Posthumous Miracle** — The son of [[terentianus|Terentianus]] (their executioner) is possessed by a devil at their tomb; their relics heal him, and both father and son are baptized (001-003).

## Themes

John and Paul represent active Christian witness within imperial power structures. Unlike the [[abraham|hermits]] who retreat from the world, they serve in the palace yet refuse to compromise their faith when tested. Their dual [[martyrdom]] — executed secretly, buried in their own house — creates a domestic shrine where miracles later occur. Their posthumous healing of their executioner's son completes Hrotsvit's chiastic structure: the convertors of [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]] become, in death, the convertors of their own persecutor.

The pairing of John and Paul as a dual character — always appearing together, always acting in unison — is unique in Hrotsvit's corpus. They function as a single dramatic entity, a "two-in-one" that embodies the communal nature of the Church's witness. Their paired martyrdom mirrors the paired conversion of Gallicanus and Bradan in Part I.

## Historical Background

The historical John and Paul were 4th-century Roman martyrs whose *passio* records their execution under Julian the Apostate and burial in their own house on the Caelian Hill. The Basilica of Santi Giovanni e Paolo in Rome stands on the traditional site of their martyrdom. Hrotsvit adapts their historical cult for dramatic purposes, making them court officials of Constance rather than independent martyrs, and linking their fate to the larger narrative of Gallicanus's conversion. This integration of historical martyr cult into dramatic plot is typical of her method: she uses the authority of established saints to lend weight to her fictionalized narratives.
