# The Baptismal Font

**The Baptismal Font** (*fons baptismalis*) is the place where [[baptism]] is administered. In [[play-Gallicanus|Gallicanus]], the font is improvised on the [[scythian-campaign|Scythian battlefield]] — a military camp transformed into a church by the administration of the sacrament.

## Battlefield as Font

Gallicanus's baptism on the battlefield is one of Hrotsvit's most striking spatial transpositions. The place of war becomes the place of sacramental birth. Where Roman arms failed, Christian grace succeeds. The font is not a marble basin in a Roman church but whatever vessel can hold water on a campaign — yet the sacrament is no less efficacious for its improvised setting (005-015).

> When threatened with defeat in battle, Gallicanus is converted by John and Paul, Grand Almoners to Constance, and immediately baptized.
> (001-002)

## Baptism and Conversion

The sequence is precise: [[conversion]], then [[baptism]]. [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]] is converted by [[john-and-paul|John and Paul]]'s ministry, then immediately baptized — the interior transformation sealed by the exterior sacrament. The Scythian king [[bradan|Bradan]] follows the same sequence (001-040). The battlefield font witnesses a double baptism, Roman and barbarian receiving the same sacrament.

## The Font as Threshold

In Christian theology, the baptismal font is the threshold between the old life and the new — the tomb of the old self and the womb of the new. Gallicanus enters the font a pagan general and emerges a Christian celibate. The transformation is instantaneous and irreversible: the font effects what it signifies.
