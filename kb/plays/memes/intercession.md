# Intercession

Intercession is the theological belief that saints in heaven can pray for those on earth, acting as mediators of divine grace. In Hrotsvit's martyr plays, the martyrs' intercession repeatedly brings miraculous healing and conversion — a narrative pattern that underscores the continuing power of the saints beyond death.

## In the Plays

### Gallicanus

Intercession is most fully dramatized in [[play-Gallicanus|Gallicanus]], where the martyred [[john-and-paul|John and Paul]] intercede after their execution. Constance prays to Christ "through the intercession of Thy holy martyr Agnes" (001-036) for the preservation of her vowed chastity — establishing the pattern that saints in heaven actively assist those on earth.

> | Constance | O Christ, lover of virginity and fount of chastity! Thou Who through the intercession of Thy holy martyr Agnes hast preserved my body from stain and my mind from pagan errors! Thou Who hast shown me as an example Thy Mother's virgin bed where Thou didst manifest Thyself true God!
>
> (001-036)

The pattern reaches its dramatic climax when Terentianus's son is possessed by a demon before the martyrs' tomb. After Terentianus confesses his guilt, the boy "recovered his health and his reason through the intercession of the martyrs" (001-053). This miracle leads directly to Terentianus's conversion and baptism.

### Dulcitius and Sapientia

Though the word "intercession" does not appear directly in the [[play-Dulcitius|Dulcitius]] and [[play-Sapientia|Sapientia]] texts, the martyrdom narratives implicitly invoke the intercessory role of the saints. The virgin martyrs' final prayers embrace both their persecutors and the wider Christian community, enacting the intercessory function that their cults would later serve.

## Theological Context

In the medieval Christian understanding, intercession was closely tied to the cult of the saints. Martyrs, having died in witness to Christ, were believed to enjoy immediate access to God's presence and to exercise a continuing ministry of prayer for the Church on earth. Hrotsvit's plays dramatize this theology by showing the newly martyred saints immediately exercising intercessory power.
