**Hope** (Latin: *Spes*) is the ten-year-old middle daughter of [[sapientia|Sapientia]] ([[sapientia|Wisdom]]) in [[sapientia|Sapientia (Play VI)]]. The second of the three allegorical virgin [[martyrdom|martyr]]s, she follows her elder sister [[faith|Faith]] in refusing to sacrifice to the Roman gods — and follows her into death. Her role exemplifies Hrotsvit's pattern: the middle voice amplifies the elder's defiance while modeling the courage the youngest will need.

> Hope | You must help us with your prayers that we may be strong enough to bear it.
> (006-013)

## Biography

Hope is identified by her Latin name Spes — the theological virtue of hope from 1 Corinthians 13. She is ten years old, the middle child, and present throughout the interrogation, torture, and execution sequence alongside her mother [[sapientia|Sapientia]] and her sisters [[faith|Faith]] (twelve) and [[charity|Charity]] (eight). Like her sisters, she is a personification rather than a historically attested figure — but Hrotsvit treats the allegory with psychological realism, giving each child a distinct age and a distinct voice.

## Role in the Plays

Hope's role in [[sapientia]] is structurally necessary:

1. **The Confirming Voice** — When [[faith|Faith]] declares they scorn the idols, Hope confirms the collective position
2. **Fear and Courage** — Hope acknowledges that "our weak bodies may dread the pain" but trusts in maternal prayer and divine strength — a more emotionally honest response than [[faith|Faith]]'s categorical defiance
3. **Second [[martyrdom|Martyrdom]]** — After [[martyrdom|witness]]ing [[faith|Faith]]'s death, Hope endures her own torture and execution

## Key Scenes

1. **The Confession** — Before [[hadrian|Hadrian]], Hope stands with her sisters and confirms their shared faith.
2. **Maternal Exhortation** — [[sapientia|Sapientia]] urges her daughters to endure; Hope responds not with bravado but with a request for prayer.
3. **Witness to [[faith|Faith]]'s Death** — Hope sees her elder sister [[martyrdom|martyr]]ed and must then face her own trial.
4. **[[martyrdom|Martyrdom]]** — Hope is tortured and killed second, her death bridging [[faith|Faith]]'s opening [[martyrdom|witness]] and [[charity|Charity]]'s final suffering.

## Themes

Hope embodies the most psychologically complex of the three theological virtues in Hrotsvit's treatment. Unlike [[faith|Faith]] (whose certainty is absolute) or [[charity|Charity]] (whose love is fierce), Hope operates in the space between promise and fulfillment — she knows what is coming (she has seen [[faith|Faith]] die) and must trust that the outcome is worth the cost. Her request for maternal prayer acknowledges the limits of her own strength — a moment of vulnerability that deepens the allegory.
