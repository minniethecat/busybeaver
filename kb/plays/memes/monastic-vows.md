# Monastic Vows

The **Monastic Vows** — or evangelical counsels — of poverty, [[chastity]], and [[obedience]] are the three pillars of the religious life as understood in Hrotsvit's 10th-century Benedictine context. While the plays dramatize early Christian martyrs and hermits rather than medieval monastics, the values of the three vows structure every dramatic action.

## Chastity as the Central Vow

[[Chastity]] (*castitas*) is the vow most explicitly dramatized, because it generates the most dramatic conflict. [[constance|Constance]]'s secret vow of [[virginity]] drives the plot of [[play-Gallicanus|Gallicanus]] (P06-005); the three sisters of [[play-Dulcitius|Dulcitius]] die defending theirs (002-001). In Hrotsvit's dramatic economy, chastity is the vow that produces narrative — it is the refusal that sets events in motion.

## Obedience as Dramatic Pivot

[[Obedience]] (*oboedientia*) is the vow that structures the conflict between divine and earthly authority. The martyrs' refusal to obey imperial commands is, paradoxically, an act of obedience to a higher authority (001-033). This double obedience — the rejection of one command as fulfilment of another — is the formal mechanism of the martyr play.

## Poverty as Implicit Framework

Poverty (*paupertas*) is the least dramatized of the three vows but is everywhere assumed. The hermits [[abraham|Abraham]] and [[paphnutius|Paphnutius]] own nothing (004-001) (005-001). [[thais|Thais]] renounces her wealth publicly (005-018). The vow of poverty underlies the entire ascetic economy of the plays: earthly possessions are obstacles to spiritual freedom.

## The Dramatic Motive

> Although considerable variety is shown in the treatment of each story, the motive is always the same — to glorify uncompromising fidelity to the vow of chastity. This nun dramatist deals courageously, but, it must be added, delicately, when it is remembered that she lived in an age when even the best educated were neither fastidious nor restrained in manners or conversation, with the temptations which her characters overcome.
>
> (P05-011)
