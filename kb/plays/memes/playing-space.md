# Playing Space

**Playing Space** (*ludus*, dramatic space) in Hrotsvit's plays is remarkably sophisticated for 10th-century drama — alternating between locations, using off-stage action, and displaying meta-theatrical awareness of the performance situation.

## Alternating Location

[[play-Gallicanus|Gallicanus]] alternates between [[rome|Rome]] and the [[scythian-campaign|Scythian battlefield]], requiring the audience to follow action across vast geographical distances. Hrotsvit manages this through reported action — characters describe what has happened elsewhere — a technique learned from [[terence|Terence]] and the classical tradition. The alternation creates dramatic rhythm: the imperial court's intrigue set against the battlefield's crisis.

## Off-Stage Violence

Following classical decorum, Hrotsvit keeps violence off-stage. Executions, the serpent's lethal attack in [[play-Callimachus|Callimachus]], and the torments of martyrs are reported rather than shown. This restraint paradoxically intensifies their effect — the audience's imagination supplies what the stage withholds.

## Meta-Theatricality

Hrotsvit displays occasional meta-theatrical awareness, most notably in [[play-Dulcitius|Dulcitius]] where the kitchen-delusion scene is explicitly theatrical — characters on stage see what Dulcitius in his delusion cannot, creating dramatic irony that depends on the audience's awareness of the performance situation.

## Theatrical Evidence

> All the fun of *Dulcitius* lies in the action. No dramatist who had not in mind the effect on *spectators* could have conceived the scene in which the foolish governor, black as a sweep from his amorous encounter with the kitchen pots and pans which he mistakes for young women, is chased away from the palace gates, asking the while if there is anything amiss with his fine and handsome appearance.
>
> (P06-006)
