**Faith** (Latin: *Fides*) is the eldest daughter of [[sapientia|Sapientia]] ([[sapientia|Wisdom]]) in [[sapientia|Sapientia (Play VI)]]. Along with her sisters [[hope|Hope]] (*[[hope|Spes]]*) and [[charity|Charity]] (*[[charity|Caritas]]*), she is a personified theological virtue — and the first to undergo [[martyrdom]] when [[hadrian|Emperor Hadrian]] demands they sacrifice to the Roman gods. True to her name, Faith's steadfastness sets the pattern her younger sisters follow: she scorns the idols, refuses to falter, and goes to death first (006-012).

> The martyrdom of the holy virgins Faith, Hope, and Charity, who are put to the torture by the Emperor Hadrian and receive the crown of martyrdom.
>
>
> (006-001)

## Biography

Faith is presented as the twelve-year-old daughter of [[sapientia|Sapientia]], a Christian widow of noble Roman birth. She and her sisters are explicitly allegorical: [[sapientia|Wisdom]] gives birth to Faith, [[hope|Hope]], and [[charity|Charity]] — the three theological virtues of 1 Corinthians 13 (006-001). Faith, as the eldest, speaks first in their interrogations and models the highest degree of contempt for the pagan idols. Her age (twelve) is given in dialogue, along with those of her sisters (ten and eight), emphasizing the shocking youth of the [[martyrdom|martyr]]s.

## Role in the Plays

Faith's role in [[sapientia]] is to lead her sisters in [[martyrdom|witness]]:

1. **First to Speak** — When [[hadrian|Hadrian]] interrogates the sisters, Faith is named first and responds with the most comprehensive defiance
2. **Scorn of Idols** — Her contempt for pagan worship is categorical: "They scorn it as I do. Yes, and laugh at it in our hearts"
3. **First to Die** — Faith is tortured and executed first, her death [[martyrdom|witness]]ed by her mother and sisters
4. **Model for the Young** — Her composure under torture encourages [[hope|Hope]] and [[charity|Charity]] to face their own trials

## Key Scenes

1. **The Interrogation** — [[hadrian|Hadrian]] questions the three sisters; Faith speaks first, confirming their shared refusal to sacrifice.
2. **[[sapientia|Sapientia]]'s Exhortation** — Their mother exhorts them to endure suffering for Christ, and Faith responds with readiness.
3. **Torture and Death** — Faith undergoes the first round of tortures and is the first to receive the crown of [[martyrdom]].

> Faith | Our weak bodies may dread the pain, but our souls are strong with the love of Christ.
>
>
> (006-013)

## Themes

Faith as a character personifies the theological virtue that bears her name. In Hrotsvit's moral scheme, Faith is the eldest child of [[sapientia|Wisdom]] because faith is the foundation — the first virtue from which hope and charity proceed. Her age (twelve) and that of her sisters (ten and eight) make the play Hrotsvit's most extreme statement on the power of grace in weakness: children face an emperor's torturers with courage that shames [[rome|Rome]]. The three sisters function as a single moral entity — Faith leads, but their [[martyrdom|witness]] is collective, as the virtue of faith cannot be separated from hope and love.
