**Ludolph of Saxony** (also Liudolf, died 864/866) was Duke of Saxony and the progenitor of the [[ottonian-dynasty|Ottonian dynasty]] (P05-007). At the request of his wife [[oda-of-saxony|Oda]], he founded [[gandersheim-abbey|Gandersheim Abbey]] around 850 CE (P05-006) — an act of patronage that, two centuries later, would make [[hrotsvit|Hrotsvit]]'s literary career possible.

## Founder of a Dynasty

Ludolph was the first clearly attested member of the Liudolfing line that would produce the Ottonian emperors. His son Otto the Illustrious succeeded him as Duke of Saxony; his grandson Henry the Fowler became the first Ottonian king; his great-grandson Otto the Great became Holy Roman Emperor. The dynasty Ludolph founded would dominate German and European politics for over a century.

## Foundation of Gandersheim

The foundation of Gandersheim was a joint act: Ludolph provided the endowment, [[oda-of-saxony|Oda]] provided the inspiration. The abbey was established for secular canonesses — noblewomen living under a rule without permanent vows — and enjoyed imperial immediacy from its founding (P05-003). Ludolph's granddaughter [[gerberga|Gerberga]] would later serve as abbess, continuing the family's connection to the house.

## Legacy

Ludolph's foundation of Gandersheim is his most enduring legacy. The abbey survived over a millennium, and within its walls Hrotsvit wrote the first dramas of the Christian West — an achievement Ludolph could never have anticipated when he endowed a house for noblewomen in the Saxon countryside.

## Historical Record
