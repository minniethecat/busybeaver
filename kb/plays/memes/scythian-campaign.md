# Scythian Campaign

**The Scythian Campaign** is the military expedition in [[play-Gallicanus|Gallicanus]] that becomes the instrument of [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]]'s [[conversion]]. What begins as an imperial war against [[scythia|Scythia]] ends as a spiritual conquest in which the conqueror himself is conquered by grace.

## From Battlefield to Baptismal Font

The campaign's trajectory is a reversal of expectations. Gallicanus, Commander-in-Chief of the Roman army, marches out confident in Roman arms and pagan sacrifice (001-001). He returns baptized, celibate, and Christian. The battlefield becomes a baptismal font; the military camp becomes a church. [[john-and-paul|John and Paul]], sent by [[constance|Constance]] as Grand Almoners, transform a campaign of territorial conquest into a mission of spiritual conquest.

> When threatened with defeat in battle, Gallicanus is converted by John and Paul, Grand Almoners to Constance, and immediately baptized — taking a vow of celibacy.
> (001-002)

## Providence on the Battlefield

The turning point is a military crisis. Facing defeat, Gallicanus is at his most vulnerable — the moment that prepares him for conversion. A heavenly host intervenes (P06-005), saving the Roman army and demonstrating that the Christian God is stronger than the pagan gods Gallicanus had propitiated at the [[the-capitol|Capitol]]. The Scythian king [[bradan|Bradan]] also submits and is baptized, extending the campaign's spiritual harvest beyond its Roman protagonist (001-043).

## Dramatic Function

The Scythian campaign serves Hrotsvit's dramatic structure by removing Gallicanus from [[constance|Constance]]'s presence, creating the conditions for his conversion through other agents. It also demonstrates a key aspect of her theology: that divine [[providence]] operates through military and political events as readily as through explicitly religious ones.
