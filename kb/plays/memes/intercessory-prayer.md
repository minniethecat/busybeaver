# Intercessory Prayer

**Intercessory Prayer** (*intercessio*) is prayer offered on behalf of others — a core function of the saint in Hrotsvit's plays, who stands between the sinner and God pleading for [[divine-grace|grace]].

## The Saint as Intercessor

Every Hrotsvit saint functions as an intercessor. [[constance|Constance]] prays for [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]]'s [[conversion]] before sending [[john-and-paul|John and Paul]] to the battlefield (001-033). [[abraham|Abraham]] prays for [[mary|Mary]] throughout her twenty-year fall. [[paphnutius|Paphnutius]] prays for [[thais|Thais]] during her three-year enclosure. In each case, the saint's prayer is not merely supportive but effective — it obtains what it requests.

## Intercession and Agency

Intercessory prayer is the saint's primary mode of action at a distance. Constance never goes to [[scythia|Scythia]], but her prayer reaches the battlefield before [[john-and-paul|John and Paul]] do (001-036). Abraham cannot physically accompany Mary into the [[the-brothel|brothel]] for twenty years, but his prayer sustains her through her degradation. Intercession extends the saint's agency beyond physical presence.

## The Economy of Intercession

Hrotsvit's intercessory economy is communal: no one is saved alone. Gallicanus is saved through Constance's prayer, John and Paul's ministry, and his own response. Mary is saved through Abraham's prayer and her own [[penance]]. Salvation is a collaborative work — the saint intercedes, the sinner responds, and [[divine-grace|grace]] accomplishes what neither could alone.
