**The Executioner** (*carnifex*) is a recurring minor figure in Hrotsvit's martyr plays — the agent who carries out the death sentences that imperial authority decrees. Though never developed as a character, the executioner's presence illuminates the mechanics of persecution and the theater of [[martyrdom]].

## Function in the Martyr Plays

The executioner appears wherever a martyr is to be killed. In [[play-Dulcitius|Dulcitius]], soldiers execute [[agape|Agape]] and [[chionia|Chionia]] while [[irena|Irena]] is reserved (002-023) for a more elaborate death. In [[play-Sapientia|Sapientia]], [[faith|Faith]], [[hope|Hope]], and [[charity|Charity]] are killed in sequence before their mother (006-014)'s eyes. The executioner is the visible instrument of imperial violence — the hand that turns the emperor's word into the martyr's death.

## The Executioner as Dramatic Device

Hrotsvit uses the executioner to stage the theater of persecution. The execution is always public, always witnessed, always narratively significant. The executioner's violence is meant to deter; instead, it inspires — onlookers are converted, the martyr's [[confession]] is vindicated, and the persecutor's power is revealed as limited to the body. The executioner, intending to serve empire, unwittingly serves the [[heavenly-crown|heavenly crown]].

> | Antiochus | Uncover that obstinate little neck, Charity, and prepare for the sword of the executioner. | | Charity | This time I do not wish to resist. I am glad to obey. | | Sapientia | Now, little one, now we must give thanks; now we must exult in Christ.
>
> (006-016)

## Contrast with the Saints

The executioner functions as a foil to the martyr: where the martyr acts from faith, the executioner acts from obedience to [[the-imperial-court|imperial command]]; where the martyr's violence is received, the executioner's is inflicted; where the martyr gains eternal life, the executioner gains only a wage. The contrast encodes the plays' central value judgment: the power that kills is weaker than the power that dies.
