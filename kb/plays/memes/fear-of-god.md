# Fear of God

**Fear of God** (*timor Dei*) is the reverential awe that the creature owes the Creator — the beginning of wisdom and the foundation of [[humility]]. In Hrotsvit's plays, the fear of God is what distinguishes the saint from the sinner and the Christian from the pagan.

## Fear and Conversion

The fear of God is often the first movement of [[conversion]]. [[thais|Thais]] is brought to [[repentance]] not primarily by argument but by the dawning realization of what God's judgment means for her life. The fear of God — not servile terror but the recognition of divine majesty — cracks open the soul that worldly pleasure has sealed. [[callimachus|Callimachus]] learns the fear of God through a serpent's lethal bite and subsequent [[resurrection]] — the most violent pedagogy in the corpus.

## Fear and Wisdom

In [[play-Sapientia|Sapientia]], the connection between fear and wisdom is personified. [[sapientia|Sapientia]] (Wisdom) instructs her daughters [[faith|Faith]], [[hope|Hope]], and [[charity|Charity]] in the fear of God as the precondition of [[martyrdom]]. Wisdom without fear is mere cleverness; fear without wisdom is mere terror. Together they constitute the mature spiritual disposition.

> Be at peace, most holy virgins. Persevere in the fear of God, and preserve untouched the treasure of your virginity. Then you will be worthy of the embraces of the eternal King.
>
> (001-044)


## Fear and Love

Hrotsvit's fear of God is never the cringing fear of a slave before a tyrant. It is filial fear — the awe of a child before a loving father, the reverence that preserves intimacy from presumption. This fear is entirely compatible with [[divine-love|love]]; indeed, it is love's safeguard. The soul that fears God rightly loves God purely.
