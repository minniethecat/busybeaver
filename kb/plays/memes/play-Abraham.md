# Abraham (Play)

**[[abraham|Abraham]]** is the fourth of [[hrotsvit|Hrotsvit]]'s six dramatic works and one of her most emotionally complex — a hermit drama that traces the fall of the anchoress [[mary|Mary]] into prostitution and her recovery through the disguised intervention of her spiritual father [[abraham|Abraham]].

> The fall and repentance of Mary, the niece of the hermit Abraham. Mary, after twenty years of life as an anchoress, is seduced and goes back to the world, where in despair she becomes a harlot. But Abraham, in the disguise of a lover, seeks her out and restores her to the desert, where she accomplishes a rigorous penance.
> (004-001)

## Plot Summary

The play opens with the hermit [[abraham|Abraham]] confiding in his friend [[ephrem|Ephrem]] about his young niece [[mary|Mary]], whom he has raised in a cell beside his hermitage. Together they persuade the eight-year-old to consecrate her [[virginity]] to Christ. [[mary|Mary]] lives as an anchoress for twenty years — until a man disguised as a monk seduces her. In despair, she flees to a distant city and becomes a prostitute (004-003)(003-007).

[[abraham|Abraham]], learning of her fall, is devastated. He resolves to find her — but in the guise of a worldling, even accepting wine and meat to maintain his disguise. [[ephrem|Ephrem]] provides theological justification: "That scrupulous and fair Judge will not condemn a man for relaxing our strict rule for a time and descending to the level of weaker mortals if by so doing he can make more sure of rescuing an errant soul" (004-003)(003-007).

In the brothel, [[abraham|Abraham]] — unrecognized — shares a meal with [[mary|Mary]]. Only when they are alone does he reveal himself, and his words break through her despair: "Oh, [[mary|Mary]], has anyone ever lived on earth without sin except the Virgin's Son?" (004-005)(003-009). She returns to the desert, where she undertakes a rigorous [[penance]] — hair shirt, continual vigils, fasting. [[ephrem|Ephrem]] rejoices at the news, and the play closes with the two hermits singing a hymn of thanksgiving (004-007)(003-011).

## Themes

*[[abraham|Abraham]]* is Hrotsvit's most intimate drama — a play of two or three voices rather than the crowded stages of the [[martyrdom|martyr]] plays. Its central theme is the possibility of return: that even the most catastrophic fall — from consecrated virgin to brothel — can be redeemed through [[penance]] and [[divine-grace]]. [[abraham|Abraham]]'s disguise is the play's masterstroke: to rescue [[mary|Mary]], the holy man must become, in appearance, the very thing he exists to oppose. The play's theology is one of radical mercy, grounded not in human effort but in the example of [[mary|Mary]] the sinless Virgin, whose [[chastity|purity]] makes every other human being a penitent.
