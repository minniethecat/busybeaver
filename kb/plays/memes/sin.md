# Sin

**Sin** (*peccatum*) is the violation of divine law — the condition from which every Hrotsvit protagonist must be rescued. The plays are structured around sin's varieties, its consequences, and the paths of restoration available to the sinner.

## Varieties of Sin

Hrotsvit's plays catalogue a range of sins. [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]] sins through idolatry and ambition — the sins of power. [[callimachus|Callimachus]] sins through disordered passion — the sin of desire that pursues its object beyond death. [[mary|Mary]] and [[thais|Thais]] sin through sexual commerce — the sins of the body. Mary does "penance for her sins with many tears, fastings, vigils, and prayers" (004-001); she cries out that "the thought of my sins crushes me" (004-013). Callimachus confesses "all the sins of my past life" (003-017). The persecuting emperors sin through pride and cruelty — the sins of absolute authority.

## Sin and Restoration

No sin in Hrotsvit is beyond restoration — but different sins require different remedies. Idolatry is cured by [[conversion]]. Disordered passion is cured by death and [[resurrection]]. Sexual sin is cured by sustained [[penance]]. The diversity of remedies reflects Hrotsvit's pastoral realism: sin is not one thing, and its cure must be calibrated to its nature.

## Sin as Captivity

Hrotsvit consistently represents sin as captivity rather than merely choice. The sinner is enslaved — by passion, by [[demonic|demons]], by habit. This is why rescue requires an external agent: the hermit-saint who descends from the [[the-desert|desert]], the divine intervention that breaks what human will cannot. Ephrem observes that "the conversion of a sinner" (004-015) is cause for rejoicing, while the disciples note that Jesus "went among sinners" (005-009) — a precedent for the hermit's descent into the world of sin. Sin is not merely wrong choice but bondage from which one must be freed.


The hermits rejoice at Mary’s repentance:

> And with us rejoice phalanxes of angels, praising the Lord for the conversion of a sinner.
>
> (004-015)
