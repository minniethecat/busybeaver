**Oda of Saxony** (also called Oda of Franconia) was a Frankish princess and the wife of Duke Ludolph of Saxony. It was at her request that [[gandersheim-abbey|Gandersheim Abbey]] was founded around the year 850 — making her, in a real sense, the distant enabler of [[hrotsvit|Hrotsvit]]'s literary career two centuries later.

## Foundation of Gandersheim

According to the historical record, Oda requested the foundation of Gandersheim as a religious house for women. Her husband Ludolph, Duke of Saxony and progenitor of the [[ottonian-dynasty|Ottonian dynasty]], endowed the foundation. The abbey was established as a community of secular canonesses — noblewomen who lived under a rule but without permanent vows, free to own property and maintain family connections.

> The Abbey of Gandersheim, where these plays were written, was founded about the year 850 by Ludolph, Duke of Saxony, at the request of his wife Oda, a Frankish princess.
> (P05-006)

## Oda's Legacy

Oda's foundation proved remarkably durable. Gandersheim survived for over a thousand years, through the Ottonian, Salian, and Hohenstaufen dynasties, the Reformation, and the secularizations of the modern era. Within its walls, in the late 10th century, Hrotsvit wrote the first dramas of the Christian West — a literary achievement made possible by the institution Oda had founded.
