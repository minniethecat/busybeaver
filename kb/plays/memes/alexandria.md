# Alexandria

**Alexandria** is an Egyptian city that appears in two of Hrotsvit's plays, serving as both the site of [[martyrdom|martyr]]dom and the arena of conversion. It is the destination of the exiled [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]] in the play that bears his name, and the city where [[thais|Thais]] the harlot lives and is converted by [[paphnutius|Paphnutius]].

## Gallicanus's Exile and Martyrdom

In the second part of *[[play-Gallicanus|Gallicanus]]*, the converted general is exiled by [[julian-the-apostate|Julian the Apostate]]. [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]] chooses Alexandria as his place of exile — not to hide but to seek [[martyrdom|martyr]]dom:

| [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]] | Banished for Christ's sake, I shall retire to Alexandria, where I hope to win the [[martyrdom|martyr]]'s crown. (001-014)(002-021) |

His hope is fulfilled. Soldiers later report to [[julian-the-apostate|Julian]]: "[[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]], exiled by your orders, fled to Alexandria. He was arrested in that city by the Governor, Ratianus, and has perished by the sword" (001-015)(002-022). Alexandria thus becomes the site of [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]]'s final transformation — from converted general to crowned [[martyrdom|martyr]].

> Gallicanus: Do not waste your breath, fellows. Your advice is useless. I hold all that exists beneath the sun as nothing compared with eternal life. Banished for Christ's sake, I shall retire to Alexandria, where I hope to win the martyr's crown.
>
> (001-047)

## Thais's City

In *[[play-Paphnutius|Paphnutius]]*, Alexandria is the "great town on the edge of the desert" where [[thais|Thais]] lives as a celebrated harlot, "through whom many were destroyed body and soul" (005-001)(006-003). [[paphnutius|Paphnutius]] journeys from his desert hermitage to Alexandria disguised as a lover, seeking her conversion.

The city represents the world in its most seductive and destructive form — wealth, beauty, and sensual pleasure concentrated in a single urban space. [[paphnutius|Paphnutius]]'s entry into Alexandria is a descent into enemy territory, and his success in converting [[thais|Thais]] demonstrates that even the most worldly of cities cannot resist divine [[divine-grace|grace]].

## Symbolic Function

Alexandria in Hrotsvit's dramatic geography is the counterpoint to the desert. The desert is the place of [[prayer]], asceticism, and [[hermit|hermit life]]; Alexandria is the place of commerce, pleasure, and spiritual danger. [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]] goes to Alexandria to die for Christ; [[paphnutius|Paphnutius]] goes to Alexandria to rescue a soul from the devil. In both cases, the city is the arena where faith is either consummated (through [[martyrdom]]) or demonstrated (through [[conversion]]). The city that represents the world's power is ultimately powerless — it can kill the body but cannot touch the soul.
