# The World

**The World** (*saeculum*, *mundus*) in Hrotsvit's theological vocabulary is the secular order in opposition to God (P05-008) — the system of values, powers, and desires from which the saint must separate. It is represented spatially by the city ([[rome|Rome]], [[alexandria|Alexandria]]), institutionally by the imperial court, and personally by the persecuting emperor.

## World vs. Desert

The primary spatial opposition in the plays is between the world (the city) and the [[the-desert|desert]]. The city is the site of power, wealth, pleasure, and persecution — everything the saint renounces. The desert is the site of poverty, discipline, prayer, and freedom — everything the saint embraces. Movement between them traces the arc of [[conversion]]: the saint descends from desert to world on a rescue mission, then returns with the rescued soul for the work of [[penance]].

## Renunciation of the World

Every protagonist in Hrotsvit renounces the world in some form. [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]] renounces military command and marriage. [[thais|Thais]] renounces wealth and sexual commerce. [[constance|Constance]] renounces imperial marriage. The martyrs renounce life itself. In each case, renunciation is not loss but exchange — the world is surrendered for something greater.

> | Abraham | Mary, my child by adoption, whom I love as my own soul! Listen to my advice as to a father's, and to Brother Ephrem's as that of a very wise man. Strive to imitate the chastity of the holy Virgin whose name you bear.
>
> (004-008)

## The World as Persecutor

The world is not merely a temptation but an active enemy. The imperial persecutions are the world's violent response to those who refuse its terms (P06-005). [[diocletian|Diocletian]], [[hadrian|Hadrian]], and [[julian-the-apostate|Julian]] are not merely individual antagonists but embodiments of the world-system that cannot tolerate the Christian's allegiance to a higher king.
