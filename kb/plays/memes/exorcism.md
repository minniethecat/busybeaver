# Exorcism

**Exorcism** (*exorcismus*) is the expulsion of demons from persons or places through divine authority. In Hrotsvit's plays, exorcism is part of the invisible repertoire of the hermit-saints — a power assumed rather than dramatized, but essential to their spiritual authority.

## Exorcism and the Hermit

The desert tradition that produced [[abraham|Abraham]] and [[paphnutius|Paphnutius]] understood the [[hermit-life|hermit's life]] as a sustained engagement with demonic forces. The hermit who had mastered his own passions through [[asceticism]] gained authority over demons — the power to command them to depart. This authority was the sign that the hermit had won his own [[spiritual-warfare|spiritual battle]] and could now fight for others.

## Implicit Exorcism in the Plays

Hrotsvit does not stage exorcisms directly, but the power is implicit in her hermits' actions. When Abraham enters [[the-brothel|the brothel]] to rescue [[mary|Mary]], he is confronting more than human sin — he is confronting the demonic forces that have claimed her. His authority to extract her depends on his prior victory over demons in the [[the-desert|desert]].

## Exorcism and Conversion

Exorcism and [[conversion]] are closely related in early Christian thought. Conversion is liberation from demonic bondage; exorcism is the dramatic, visible form of that liberation. The serpent that kills [[callimachus|Callimachus]] at [[the-tomb|the tomb]] might be understood as an exorcistic agent — a divine violence that expels the demonic passion possessing him.
