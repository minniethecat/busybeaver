# Monastic Rule

The **Monastic Rule** (*regula*) is the written code governing the life of a religious community. At [[gandersheim-abbey|Gandersheim]], the *Rule of Saint Benedict* (c. 530) provided the framework — adapted for a community of secular canonesses — within which [[hrotsvit|Hrotsvit]] lived, prayed, and wrote. (P05-007)

## The Rule of St. Benedict

The Benedictine Rule prescribes a balanced life of *ora et labora* — prayer and work. The day is structured around the Divine Office: Vigils, Lauds, Prime, Terce, Sext, None, Vespers, and Compline. Between these hours of common [[prayer]], time is allocated for *lectio divina* (sacred reading), manual labour, and — for the gifted — study and composition. Hrotsvit's literary output was produced within this disciplined schedule.

## Rule and Freedom

The paradox of the Rule is that its constraints create freedom. The regulated day liberates the mind from decisions about what to do next; the vow of [[obedience]] liberates the will from the tyranny of impulse. Hrotsvit's creative freedom — her ability to compose Latin dramas of considerable complexity — was enabled by, not impeded by, the Rule's structure.

## Rule and the Plays

The plays reflect a Rule-shaped imagination. The hermits [[abraham|Abraham]] and [[paphnutius|Paphnutius]], though pre-Benedictine, live according to patterns that map onto Benedictine observance — [[prayer]], [[fasting]], manual labour, [[vigil|night vigils]]. Hrotsvit reads the desert fathers through the lens of the Rule she knows.
