# Heavenly Crown

The **Heavenly Crown** (*corona*, *aureola*) is the eschatological reward promised to [[martyrdom|martyrs]] and [[virginity|virgins]] — the visible sign of victory that transforms death from defeat into triumph. In Hrotsvit's plays, the crown is the horizon toward which every martyr moves.

## Crown of Martyrdom

The martyr's crown is earned through death. [[gallicanus-character|Gallicanus]] receives "the crown of martyrdom" (001-003) under [[julian-the-apostate|Julian]], his military honours transmuted into heavenly ones. The three sisters of [[play-Dulcitius|Dulcitius]] and the three daughters of [[sapientia|Sapientia]] each receive a crown whose radiance far exceeds any earthly diadem. Sapientia rejoices that her "mother will be crowned" by her daughters' virginity (006-013). The crown is the dramatic proof that the martyr has not lost but won.

> But I shall receive the martyr's palm, and, adorned with the crown of virginity, I shall enter the azure palace of the Eternal King.
> (002-026)

## Crown of Virginity

The virgin's crown is a specialized form — the reward for those who defended [[chastity]] unto death. Hrotsvit distinguishes two crowns not because they differ in value but because they testify to different aspects of sanctity: the martyr's crown witnesses to faith under persecution; the virgin's crown witnesses to the defence of bodily consecration. Often they are received together, as the martyr-virgin earns both.

## Dramatic Function

The heavenly crown serves a crucial dramatic function: it provides the future-tense resolution that makes present-tense suffering bearable. The audience knows — and the martyrs know — that the death is not the end. The crown is the promised sequel that retroactively transforms the entire plot from tragedy to triumph.
