## Overview

Prospero's Usurpation is the foundational backstory event of *The Tempest*, recounted by [[prospero|Prospero]] to his daughter [[miranda|Miranda]] in Act 1, Scene 2. Twelve years before the play's action (003-075), [[prospero|Prospero]] was the rightful Duke of [[milan|Milan]] (003-082). Neglecting his state duties to pursue the [[knowledge|study]] of magic, he delegated governance to his brother [[antonio|Antonio]]. [[antonio|Antonio]], "dry for sway" (003-148), conspired with [[alonso|Alonso]], King of [[naples|Naples]], to depose [[prospero|Prospero]]. One midnight, [[antonio|Antonio]]'s forces seized [[prospero|Prospero]] and the infant [[miranda|Miranda]], set them adrift in a leaky boat, and left them to die at sea. They survived thanks to [[gonzalo|Gonzalo]] (003-217), who secretly pro[[dream|vision]]ed the boat with food, water, and [[prospero|Prospero]]'s precious books.

## Dramatic Function

The usurpation establishes the play's central theme: the violation of legitimate [[authority|rule]] and the quest for restoration. It mirrors every other power struggle in the play — [[antonio|Antonio]] and [[sebastian|Sebastian]]'s conspiracy against [[alonso|Alonso]], [[caliban|Caliban]]'s mutiny with [[stephano|Stephano]], even [[caliban|Caliban]]'s claim that [[prospero|Prospero]] usurped the [[island|island]] from him. The event drives [[prospero|Prospero]]'s entire project: [[the-shipwreck|the tempest]], the [[storm|shipwreck]], and the elaborate orchestration of Act 5's [[forgiveness|reconciliation]] are all consequences of this single act of betrayal.

## Key Passages

> "Thy father was the Duke of Milan, and / A prince of power." (003-076–077)

> "I, thus neglecting worldly ends, all dedicated / To closeness and the bettering of my mind... in my false brother / Awaked an evil nature." (003-125–127)

> "So dry he was for sway, wi' the King of Naples / To give him annual tribute, do him homage." (003-148–149)

> "A treacherous army levied, one midnight / Fated to the purpose, did Antonio open / The gates of Milan." (003-170–171)

## Aftermath

The usurpation set in motion every event of the play. [[prospero|Prospero]] and [[miranda|Miranda]] survived on the [[island|island]] for twelve years. The [[storm|tempest]] of Act 1, Scene 1 was [[prospero|Prospero]]'s calculated revenge — bringing [[antonio|Antonio]], [[alonso|Alonso]], and their p[[magic|art]]y within his power. Through the play's action, [[prospero|Prospero]] chooses forgiveness over vengeance, and by Act 5 he reclaims his dukedom while [[forgiveness|pardon]]ing all who wronged him.
