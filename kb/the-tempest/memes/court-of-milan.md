## Overview

The Court of Milan is the political entity at the centre of the play's backstory. Under [[prospero|Prospero]], it was "through all [[milan|the signories]] … the first" (003-101) — the most prestigious of the Italian city-states, [[authority|rule]]d by a duke renowned for his [[knowledge|learning]] (003-076). But [[prospero|Prospero]]'s absorption in "[[art|secret studies]]" (003-107) created a vacuum that his brother [[antonio|Antonio]] filled, and the court was transformed from a centre of scholarship into an instrument of usurpation. Twelve years before the play opens, [[antonio|Antonio]] — with the military support of [[alonso|Alonso]], King of [[naples|Naples]] — deposed [[prospero|Prospero]], set him adrift with the infant [[miranda|Miranda]], and made [[milan|Milan]] a tributary of [[naples|Naples]] (003-145). The court [[prospero|Prospero]] reclaims at the play's end is the same entity, but he returns to it not as a ruling duke but as a man preparing to die.

## Composition

The [[milan|Milan]]ese court, as narrated by [[prospero|Prospero]], consists of the duke ([[prospero|Prospero]], then [[antonio|Antonio]]), the ducal family ([[miranda|Miranda]]), and the administrative apparatus that [[antonio|Antonio]] mastered. [[prospero|Prospero]]'s description of his brother's takeover is a case [[knowledge|study]] in court politics:

> I, thus neglecting worldly ends, all dedicated
> To closeness and the bettering of my mind
> With that which, but by being so retired,
> O'er-prized all popular rate, in my false brother
> Awaked an evil nature; and my trust,
> Like a good parent, did beget of him
> A falsehood in its contrary as great
> As my trust was …
> — (003-121–128)

[[antonio|Antonio]] redistributed offices, "new-created / The creatures that were mine … or [[transformation|change]]d 'em, / Or else new-form'd 'em" (003-114–116), and "having both the key / Of officer and office, set all he[[magic|art]]s i' the state / To what tune pleased his ear" (003-117–119). The court, under [[antonio|Antonio]], became an extension of his will — its officials his creatures, its loyalties his [[music|music]] (003-111).

## Political Role

The Court of Milan, under [[antonio|Antonio]], is the play's example of illegitimate [[authority|rule]]. [[antonio|Antonio]]'s power rests on the original usurpation — the betrayal of his brother — and on the tributary relationship with [[naples|Naples]] that made [[milan|Milan]] a client state. The court is thus doubly corrupted: its [[authority|rule]]r is a usurper, and its [[authority|sovereignty]] is compromised by subordination to a foreign power.

The contrast with [[alonso|Alonso]]'s [[court-of-naples|Court of Naples]] is instructive. [[alonso|Alonso]] is a legitimate king who committed a political sin (supporting the usurpation) but is capable of [[guilt|remorse]] and restoration. [[antonio|Antonio]] is silent throughout the final act, makes no apology, and is not clearly restored to any role. The [[milan|Milan]]ese court at the play's end is ambiguous: [[prospero|Prospero]] reclaims his dukedom, forgives his brother ("I do forgive thee, / Unnatural though thou [[magic|art]]," 014-119–120), but will not remain to [[authority|rule]]. The court is returned to its rightful duke at the moment the duke ceases to want it (014-119).

## Related Characters

- **[[prospero|Prospero]]**: The rightful duke, deposed by his brother. His relationship to the court is transformed over twelve years: from scholarly neglect to magical exile to reluctant repossession.
- **[[antonio|Antonio]]**: The usurper. His [[servitude|mastery]] of the court's machinery of governance — "how to grant suits, / How to deny them, whom to advance" (003-111–112) — made the coup possible.
- **[[miranda|Miranda]]**: The heir to the [[milan|Milan]]ese dukedom, who will never [[authority|rule]] it. She goes to [[naples|Naples]] as [[ferdinand|Ferdinand]]'s wife, transferring [[milan|Milan]]'s dynastic future to its former rival.
- **[[alonso|Alonso]]**: The King of [[naples|Naples]] whose support enabled the usurpation and whose restitution of the dukedom heals the political breach.
