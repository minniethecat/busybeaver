## Overview

Nymphs in *The Tempest* are nature spirits of classical origin — female divinities associated with specific features of the natural world: sea-nymphs (Nereids) of the ocean, and Naiads of rivers and springs. They appear in two registers: as categories of spirit invoked in the poetry of the play, and as staged presences in [[prospero|Prospero]]'s betrothal masque. Like all spirits on the [[island|island]], they are ultimately under [[prospero|Prospero]]'s [[authority|command]] through [[ariel|Ariel]].

## In the Play

The nymph motif recurs across the play's register of [[magic|enchantment]]. [[ariel|Ariel]]'s first significant transformation is into a "nymph o' the sea" at [[prospero|Prospero]]'s [[authority|command]] in Act 1, Scene 2 — "Go make thyself like a nymph o' the sea: be subject / To no sight but thine and mine, invisible / To every eyeball else" (003-410). [[ariel|Ariel]] re-enters shortly after "like a water-nymph" (003-435), and [[ferdinand|Ferdinand]]'s [[music|song]] "Full fathom five" invokes "Sea-nymphs hourly ring his knell" (003-540) — associating nymphs with the ritualised, musical deaths the [[island|island]] seems to produce.

The most elaborate deployment of nymphs comes in the Act 4 masque. [[iris|Iris]] summons the Naiads — "You nymphs, call'd Naiads, of the windring brooks, / With your sedg'd crowns and ever-harmless looks" (012-160) — to join the celebration. [[juno|Juno]] then [[authority|command]]s: "Come, temperate nymphs, and help to celebrate / A contract of true love" (012-164). The nymphs perform a dance with the reapers, linking the pastoral and aquatic dimensions of the [[island|island]]'s supernatural ecology.

The nymphs in the masque are notably chaste. [[ceres|Ceres]]'s earlier insistence that [[venus|Venus]] be banished ensures that the nymphs who dance are "temperate" — their sexuality contained within the decorous frame of matrimonial celebration. As with all spirits in [[prospero|Prospero]]'s pageant, the nymphs are aestheticised and controlled.
