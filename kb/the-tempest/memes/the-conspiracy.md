## Overview

The Conspiracy is the comic subplot of Shakespeare's [[the-tempest|The Tempest]], running beneath the main romantic and revenge plots. [[caliban|Caliban]], the enslaved inhabitant of the [[island]], recruits two [[storm|shipwreck]]ed drunkards -- the butler [[stephano|Stephano]] and the jester [[trinculo|Trinculo]] -- to murder [[prospero|Prospero]] and seize control of the island (009-054). The plot unfolds through Acts 2-4, weaving together themes of [[servitude]], [[authority]], and rebellion, before collapsing into farce when the conspirators are distracted by glittering garments and ultimately hunted by spirit-dogs.

## Events

The conspiracy takes shape in Act 2, Scene 2, when [[trinculo|Trinculo]] and [[stephano|Stephano]] separately encounter [[caliban|Caliban]] after the [[the-shipwreck|shipwreck]]. [[stephano|Stephano]] shares his bottle of sack with [[caliban|Caliban]], who in his drunken gratitude declares [[stephano|Stephano]] his new god and [[master|master]], vowing: "I'll swear upon that bottle to be thy true subject, for the liquor is not e[[magic|art]]hly." [[caliban|Caliban]] renounces [[prospero|Prospero]]'s [[authority|rule]] and offers to serve [[stephano|Stephano]], promising to show him "every fertile inch o' the island."

The plot solidifies in Act 3, Scene 2, where [[caliban|Caliban]] lays out the murder plan with chilling clarity:

> "Why, as I told thee, 'tis a custom with him / I' th' afternoon to sleep: there thou mayst brain him, / Having first seized his books; or with a log / Batter his skull, or paunch him with a stake, / Or cut his wezand with thy knife. Remember / First to possess his books; for without them / He's but a sot, as I am, nor hath not / One spirit to command" (009-054-009-061)

[[caliban|Caliban]]'s plan reveals his intimate [[knowledge|knowledge]] of [[prospero|Prospero]]'s habits and his understanding that [[prospero|Prospero]]'s [[power|power]] resides in his books of [[magic]] rather than in any innate superiority (009-054). He also appeals to [[stephano|Stephano]]'s ambition by extolling [[miranda|Miranda]]'s beauty: "Ay, lord; she will become thy bed, I warrant, / And bring thee forth brave brood" (009-073-009-074).

All the while, [[ariel|Ariel]] -- invisible -- listens and stirs trouble, calling "Thou liest" from unseen corners, turning the trio against each other with petty provocations (009-019).

In Act 4, Scene 1, the conspirators approach [[prosperos-cell|Prospero's cell]], wet and bedraggled after falling into a "filthy-mantled pool." [[prospero|Prospero]], who has been monitoring everything through [[ariel|Ariel]]'s intelligence, remarks aside: "I had forgot that foul conspiracy / Of the beast [[caliban|Caliban]] and his confederates" (012-173–012-174).

[[ariel|Ariel]] sets a trap by hanging "glistering apparel" on a line near [[prosperos-cell|the cell]]. [[caliban|Caliban]] urges stealth: "Do that good mischief which may make this island / Thine own for ever, and I, thy [[caliban|Caliban]], / For aye thy foot-licker" (012-258-012-260). But [[stephano|Stephano]] and [[trinculo|Trinculo]] are immediately mesmerized by the fine garments, squabbling over the wardrobe like children. [[caliban|Caliban]]'s furious pleas -- "Let it alone, thou fool; it is but trash" (012-263) -- are ignored.

Finally, [[prospero|Prospero]] and [[ariel|Ariel]] loose spirit-hounds upon the trio. Spirits "in shape of dogs and hounds" hunt them about, and [[prospero|Prospero]] orders his goblins to "grind their joints with dry convulsions, / Shorten up their sinews with aged cramps" (012-282 stage direction, 012-283+). The conspirators flee, driven "through / Toothed briers, sharp furzes, pricking gorse, and thorns."

## Aftermath

The conspiracy ends in utter humiliation. The trio is chased "over the stage" by spirit-hounds, their rebellion reduced to slapstick. In Act 5, [[prospero|Prospero]] reveals the three to the assembled court, and [[alonso|Alonso]] recognizes the "strange thing as e'er I looked on." [[prospero|Prospero]]'s famous line -- "this thing of darkness I / Ac[[knowledge|knowledge]] mine" (014-350-014-351) -- claims responsibility for [[caliban|Caliban]] as p[[magic|art]] of his own story and domain (014-350). The conspirators are sent to "trim" [[prosperos-cell|Prospero's cell]] handsomely, a final penance before their [[forgiveness|pardon]].

The conspiracy serves as a dark comic foil to [[antonio|Antonio]] and [[sebastian|Sebastian]]'s more serious plot against [[alonso|Alonso]] in Act 2. Both conspiracies target a [[authority|rule]]r; both are detected and defeated. But where [[antonio|Antonio]]'s treason is met with silence and implicit threat, [[caliban|Caliban]]'s rebellion is resolved with public shaming and grudging acknowledgment.
