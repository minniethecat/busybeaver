[006-001] *Enter [[caliban|Caliban]] with a burden of wood. A noise of thunder heard.*

[006-002] *Cal.* All the infections that the sun sucks up

[006-003] From bogs, fens, flats, on Prosper fall, and make him

[006-004] By inch-meal a disease! His spirits hear me,

[006-005] And yet I needs must curse. But they’ll nor pinch,

[006-006] 5 Fright me with urchin-shows, pitch me i’ the mire,

[006-007] Nor lead me, like a firebrand, in the dark

[006-008] Out of my way, unless he bid ’em: but

[006-009] For every trifle are they set upon me;

[006-010] Sometime like apes, that mow and chatter at me,

[006-011] 10 And after bite me; then like hedgehogs, which

[006-012] Lie tumbling in my barefoot way, and mount

[006-013] Their pricks at my footfall; sometime am I

[006-014] All wound with adders, who with cloven tongues

[006-015] Do hiss me into madness.

[006-016] *Enter Trinculo.*

[006-017] Lo, now, lo!

[006-018] 15 Here comes a spirit of his, and to torment me

[006-019] For bringing wood in slowly. I’ll fall flat;

[006-020] Perchance he will not mind me.

[006-021] *Trin.* Here’s neither bush nor shrub, to bear off any weather at all, and another [[storm|storm]] brewing; I hear it sing i’ 20 the wind: yond same black cloud, yond huge one, looks like a foul bombard that would shed his liquor. If it should thunder as it did before, I know not where to hide my head: yond same cloud cannot choose but fall by pailfuls. What have we here? a man or a fish? dead or alive? A fish: he II. 2. 25 smells like a fish; a very ancient and fish-like smell; a kind of not of the newest Poor-John. A strange fish! Were I in England now, as once I was, and had but this fish painted, not a holiday fool there but would give a piece of silver: there would this monster make a man; any strange 30 beast there makes a man: when they will not give a doit to relieve a lame beggar, they will lay out ten to see a dead Indian. Legged like a man! and his fins like arms! Warm o’ my troth! I do now let loose my opinion; hold it no longer: this is no fish, but an islander, that hath lately suffered 35 by a thunderbolt. [*Thunder.*] Alas, [[the-shipwreck|the storm]] is come again! my best way is to creep under his gaberdine; there is no other shelter hereabout: misery acquaints a man with strange bed-fellows. I will here shroud till the dregs of the [[storm|storm]] be past.

[006-022] *Enter Stephano, singing: a bottle in his hand.*

[006-023] 40 *Ste.* I shall no more to sea, to sea,

[006-024] Here shall I die a-shore,—

[006-025] This is a very scurvy tune to sing at a man’s funeral: well, here’s my comfort. Drinks.

[006-026] [*Sings.* The [[master|master]], the swabber, the [[boatswain|boatswain]], and I,

[006-027] 45 The gunner, and his mate,

[006-028] Loved Mall, Meg, and Marian, and Margery,

[006-029] But none of us cared for Kate;

[006-030] For she had a tongue with a tang,

[006-031] Would cry to a sailor, Go hang!

[006-032] II. 2. 50 She loved not the savour of tar nor of pitch;

[006-033] Yet a tailor might scratch her where’er she did itch.

[006-034] Then, to sea, boys, and let her go hang!

[006-035] This is a scurvy tune too: but here’s my comfort. Drinks.

[006-036] *Cal.* Do not torment me:—O!

[006-037] 55 *Ste.* What’s the matter? Have we devils here? Do you put tricks upon ’s with savages and men of Ind, ha? I have not scaped drowning, to be afeard now of your four legs; for it hath been said, As proper a man as ever went on four legs cannot make him give ground; and it shall be 60 said so again, while Stephano breathes at’s nostrils.

[006-038] *Cal.* The spirit torments me:—O!

[006-039] *Ste.* This is some monster of the [[island|isle]] with four legs, who hath got, as I take it, an ague. Where the devil should he learn our language? I will give him some relief, if it be 65 but for that. If I can recover him, and keep him tame, and get to [[naples|Naples]] with him, he’s a present for any emperor that ever trod on neat’s-leather.

[006-040] *Cal.* Do not torment me, prithee; I’ll bring my wood home faster.

[006-041] 70 *Ste.* He’s in his fit now, and does not talk after the wisest. He shall taste of my bottle: if he have never drunk wine afore, it will go near to remove his fit. If I can recover him, and keep him tame, I will not take too much for him; he shall pay for him that hath him, and that soundly.

[006-042] II. 2. 75 *Cal.* Thou dost me yet but little hurt; thou wilt anon, I know it by thy trembling: now Prosper works upon thee.

[006-043] *Ste.* Come on your ways; open your mouth; here is that which will give language to you, cat: open your mouth; this will shake your shaking, I can tell you, and that soundly: 80 you cannot tell who’s your friend: open your chaps again.

[006-044] *Trin.* I should know that voice: it should be—but he is drowned; and these are devils:—O defend me!

[006-045] *Ste.* Four legs and two voices,—a most delicate monster! His forward voice, now, is to speak well of his friend; 85 his backward voice is to utter foul speeches and to detract. If all the wine in my bottle will recover him, I will help his ague. Come:—Amen! I will pour some in thy other mouth.

[006-046] *Trin.* Stephano!

[006-047] 90 *Ste.* Doth thy other mouth call me? Mercy, mercy! This is a devil, and no monster: I will leave him; I have no long spoon.

[006-048] *Trin.* Stephano! If thou beest Stephano, touch me, and speak to me; for I am Trinculo,—be not afeard,—thy 95 good friend Trinculo.

[006-049] *Ste.* If thou beest Trinculo, come forth: I’ll pull thee by the lesser legs: if any be Trinculo’s legs, these are they. Thou [[magic|art]] very Trinculo indeed! How earnest thou to be the siege of this moon-calf? can he vent Trinculos?

[006-050] II. 2. 100 *Trin.* I took him to be killed with a thunder-stroke. But art thou not drowned, Stephano? I hope, now, thou art not drowned. Is the [[storm|storm]] overblown? I hid me under the dead moon-calf’s gaberdine for fear of the [[storm|storm]]. And art thou living, Stephano? O Stephano, two Neapolitans 105 scaped!

[006-051] *Ste.* Prithee, do not turn me about; my stomach is not constant.

[006-052] *Cal.* [*aside*] These be fine things, an if they be not sprites.

[006-053] That’s a brave god, and bears celestial liquor:

[006-054] 110 I will kneel to him.

[006-055] *Ste.* How didst thou ’scape? How camest thou hither? swear, by this bottle, how thou camest hither. I escaped upon a butt of sack, which the sailors heaved o’erboard, by this bottle! which I made of the bark of a tree with mine 115 own hands, since I was cast ashore.

[006-056] *Cal.* I’ll swear, upon that bottle, to be thy true subject; for the liquor is not earthly.

[006-057] *Ste.* Here; swear, then, how thou escapedst.

[006-058] *Trin.* Swum ashore, man, like a duck: I can swim 120 like a duck, I’ll be sworn.

[006-059] *Ste.* Here, kiss the book. Though thou canst swim like a duck, thou art made like a goose.

[006-060] *Trin.* O Stephano, hast any more of this?

[006-061] *Ste.* The whole butt, man: my cellar is in a rock by II. 2. 125 the sea-side, where my wine is hid. How now, moon-calf! how does thine ague?

[006-062] *Cal.* Hast thou not dropp’d from heaven?

[006-063] *Ste.* Out o’ the moon, I do assure thee: I was the man i’ the moon when time was.

[006-064] 130 *Cal.* I have seen thee in her, and I do adore thee: My mistress show’d me thee, and thy dog, and thy bush.

[006-065] *Ste.* Come, swear to that; kiss the book: I will furnish it anon with new contents: swear.

[006-066] *Trin.* By this good light, this is a very shallow monster! 135 I afeard of him! A very weak monster! The man i’ the moon! A most poor credulous monster! Well drawn, monster, in good sooth!

[006-067] *Cal.* I’ll show thee every fertile inch o’ th’ island;

[006-068] And I will kiss thy foot: I prithee, be my god.

[006-069] 140 *Trin.* By this light, a most perfidious and drunken monster! when’s god’s asleep, he’ll rob his bottle.

[006-070] *Cal.* I’ll kiss thy foot; I’ll swear myself thy subject.

[006-071] *Ste.* Come on, then; down, and swear.

[006-072] *Trin.* I shall laugh myself to death at this puppy-headed monster. A most scurvy monster! I could find in 145 my heart to beat him,—

[006-073] *Ste.* Come, kiss.

[006-074] *Trin.* But that the poor monster’s in drink: an abominable monster!

[006-075] II. 2. 150 *Cal.* I’ll show thee the best springs; I’ll pluck thee berries;

[006-076] I’ll fish for thee, and get thee wood enough.

[006-077] A plague upon the tyrant that I serve!

[006-078] I’ll bear him no more sticks, but follow thee,

[006-079] Thou wondrous man.

[006-080] *Trin.* A most ridiculous monster, to make a wonder 155 of a poor drunkard!

[006-081] *Cal.* I prithee, let me bring thee where crabs grow;

[006-082] And I with my long nails will dig thee pig-nuts;

[006-083] Show thee a jay’s nest, and instruct thee how

[006-084] 160 To snare the nimble marmoset; I’ll bring thee

[006-085] To clustering filberts, and sometimes I’ll get thee

[006-086] Young scamels from the rock. Wilt thou go with me?

[006-087] *Ste.* I prithee now, lead the way, without any more talking. Trinculo, the king and all our company else being drowned, 165 we will inherit here: here; bear my bottle: fellow Trinculo, we’ll fill him by and by again.

[006-088] *Cal. sings drunkenly.*] Farewell, [[master|master]]; farewell, farewell!

[006-089] *Trin.* A howling monster; a drunken monster!

[006-090] *Cal.* No more dams I’ll make for fish;

[006-091] 170 Nor fetch in firing

[006-092] At requiring;

[006-093] Nor scrape trencher, nor wash dish:

[006-094] ’Ban, ’Ban, Cacaliban

[006-095] Has a new [[master|master]]:—get a new man.

[006-096] II. 2. 175 [[freedom|Freedom]], hey-day! hey-day, freedom! freedom, hey-day, freedom!

[006-097] *Ste.* O brave monster! Lead the way. Exeunt.
