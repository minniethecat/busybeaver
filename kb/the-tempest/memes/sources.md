[045-001] The editors’ Preface (e-text 23041) discusses the 17th- and 18th-century editions in detail; the newer (19th-century) editions are simply listed by name. The following editions may appear in the Notes. All inset text is quoted from the Preface.

[045-002] **Folios**:

[045-003] F1 1623; F2 (no date given); F3 1663; F4 1685.

[045-004] “The five plays contained in this volume occur in the first Folio in the same order, and ... were there printed for the first time.”

[045-005] **Early editions**:

[045-006] Rowe 1709

[045-007] Pope 1715

[045-008] “Pope was the first to indicate the *place* of each new scene; as, for instance, *Tempest*, I. 1. ‘On a ship at sea.’ He also subdivided the scenes as given by the Folios and Rowe, making a fresh scene whenever a new character entered—an arrangement followed by Hanmer, Warburton, and Johnson. For convenience of reference to these editions, we have always recorded the commencement of Pope’s scenes.”

[045-009] Theobald 1733

[045-010] Hanmer (“Oxford edition”) 1744

[045-011] Warburton 1747

[045-012] Johnson 1765

[045-013] Capell 1768; *also Capell’s annotated copy of F2*

[045-014] Steevens 1773

[045-015] Malone 1790

[045-016] Reed 1803

[045-017] **Later editions**:

[045-018] Singer, Knight, Cornwall, Collier, Phelps, Halliwell, Dyce, Staunton

[045-019] **Dryden**:

[045-020] “*The Tempest* was altered by Dryden and D’Avenant, and published as *The Tempest; or the Enchanted [[island|Island]]*, in 1669. We mark the emendations derived from it: ‘Dryden’s version.’”
