## Overview

Adrian is a Neapolitan lord who serves in the court of [[alonso|King Alonso]]. He is among the p[[magic|art]]y [[storm|shipwreck]]ed on [[prospero|Prospero]]'s island and appears throughout the play as a companion to the king. Like his fellow lord [[francisco]], Adrian is a loyal and honest courtier, though his dialogue is limited. He features most notably in Act 2, where he engages in verbal sparring with [[antonio]] and [[sebastian]], and appears again with the court p[[magic|art]]y in Act 3 (010-001).

## Character and Role

Adrian is a minor but consistent presence in the court p[[magic|art]]y. He is identified in the dramatis personae alongside [[francisco|Francisco]] as one of the "Lords" attending [[alonso|Alonso]] (P02-001). His most distinctive moment comes in a brief ex[[transformation|change]] where [[antonio|Antonio]] and [[sebastian|Sebastian]] mock him, wagering on which of the two lords — Adrian or [[francisco|Francisco]] — will speak first.

> "Which, of he or Adrian, for a good wager, first begins to crow?"
>
> (005-028)

Adrian's role is primarily structural: he helps populate the courtly world of [[naples|Naples]] and reinforces the political dimension of the play. The editorial apparatus in Note VII remarks on the unusual construction of [[antonio|Antonio]]'s wager: "'Of' is found in the same construction in other writers of the period" (023-001), confirming that the idiom "Which, of he or Adrian" was a recognised colloquialism rather than a printer's error. Along with [[gonzalo]], he represents the loyal, well-meaning faction of [[alonso|Alonso]]'s entourage.

## Key Relationships

- **[[alonso]]** — Adrian's king and liege lord, with whom he is [[storm|shipwreck]]ed and whom he accompanies throughout the island sojourn
- **[[gonzalo]]** — Fellow loyal courtier; Adrian and [[francisco|Francisco]] are the younger counterp[[magic|art]]s to the honest old counsellor
- **[[antonio]]** and **[[sebastian]]** — The cynical usurpers who mock Adrian and [[francisco|Francisco]], using them as targets for their wit

## Key Scenes

Adrian appears in the opening [[storm|shipwreck]] scene (Act 1, Scene 1), entering with the court p[[magic|art]]y (005-001). He is present in the island scenes of Act 2, and again in Act 3 (010-001). In the final [[forgiveness|reconciliation]] scene in Act 5, he re-enters alongside [[francisco|Francisco]] "attended by" him as they discover the circle drawn by [[prospero|Prospero]]'s magic.

> "...in like manner, attended by Adrian and Francisco: they all enter the circle which Prospero had made..."
>
> (014-069)
