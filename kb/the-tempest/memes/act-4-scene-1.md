[012-001] *Enter [[prospero|Prospero]], Ferdinand, and [[miranda|Miranda]].*

[012-002] *Pros.* If I have too austerely punish’d you,

[012-003] Your compensation makes amends; for I

[012-004] Have given you here a third of mine own life,

[012-005] Or that for which I live; who once again

[012-006] 5 I tender to thy hand: all thy vexations

[012-007] Were but my trials of thy love, and thou

[012-008] Hast strangely stood the test: here, afore Heaven,

[012-009] I ratify this my rich gift. O Ferdinand,

[012-010] Do not smile at me that I boast her off,

[012-011] 10 For thou shalt find she will outstrip all praise,

[012-012] And make it halt behind her.

[012-013] *Fer.*

[012-014] I do believe it

[012-015] Against an oracle.

[012-016] *Pros.* Then, as my gift, and thine own acquisition

[012-017] Worthily purchased, take my daughter: but

[012-018] 15 If thou dost break her virgin-knot before

[012-019] All sanctimonious ceremonies may

[012-020] With full and holy rite be minister’d,

[012-021] No sweet aspersion shall the heavens let fall

[012-022] To make this contract grow; but barren hate,

[012-023] 20 Sour-eyed disdain and discord shall bestrew

[012-024] The union of your bed with weeds so loathly

[012-025] That you shall hate it both: therefore take heed,

[012-026] As Hymen’s lamps shall light you.

[012-027] *Fer.*

[012-028] As I hope

[012-029] For quiet days, fair issue and long life,

[012-030] IV. 1. 25 With such love as ’tis now, the murkiest den,

[012-031] The most opportune place, the strong’st suggestion

[012-032] Our worser Genius can, shall never melt

[012-033] Mine honour into lust, to take away

[012-034] The edge of that day’s celebration

[012-035] 30 When I shall think, or Phœbus’ steeds are founder’d,

[012-036] Or Night kept chain’d below.

[012-037] *Pros.*

[012-038] Fairly spoke.

[012-039] Sit, then, and talk with her; she is thine own.

[012-040] What, [[ariel|Ariel]]! my industrious servant, Ariel!

[012-041] *Enter Ariel.*

[012-042] *Ari.* What would my potent [[master|master]]? here I am.

[012-043] 35 *Pros.* Thou and thy meaner fellows your last [[servitude|service]]

[012-044] Did worthily perform; and I must use you

[012-045] In such another trick. Go bring the rabble,

[012-046] O’er whom I give thee power, here to this place:

[012-047] Incite them to quick motion; for I must

[012-048] 40 Bestow upon the eyes of this young couple

[012-049] Some vanity of mine [[magic|art]]: it is my promise,

[012-050] And they expect it from me.

[012-051] *Ari.*

[012-052] Presently?

[012-053] *Pros.* Ay, with a twink.

[012-054] *Ari.* Before you can say, ‘come,’ and ‘go,’

[012-055] 45 And breathe twice, and cry, ‘so, so,’

[012-056] Each one, tripping on his toe,

[012-057] Will be here with mop and mow.

[012-058] Do you love me, [[master|master]]? no?

[012-059] *Pros.* Dearly, my delicate Ariel. Do not approach

[012-060] Till thou dost hear me call.

[012-061] *Ari.*

[012-062] IV. 1. 50 Well, I conceive. Exit.

[012-063] *Pros.* Look thou be true; do not give dalliance

[012-064] Too much the rein: the strongest oaths are straw

[012-065] To the fire i’ the blood: be more abstemious,

[012-066] Or else, good night your vow!

[012-067] *Fer.*

[012-068] I warrant you, sir;

[012-069] 55 The white cold virgin snow upon my heart

[012-070] Abates the ardour of my liver.

[012-071] *Pros.*

[012-072] Well.

[012-073] Now come, my Ariel! bring a corollary,

[012-074] Rather than want a spirit: appear, and pertly!

[012-075] No tongue! all eyes! be silent. Soft [[music|music]].

[012-076] *Enter Iris.*

[012-077] 60 *Iris.* Ceres, most bounteous lady, thy rich leas

[012-078] Of wheat, rye, barley, vetches, oats, and pease;

[012-079] Thy turfy mountains, where live nibbling sheep,

[012-080] And flat meads thatch’d with stover, them to keep;

[012-081] Thy banks with pioned and twilled brims,

[012-082] 65 Which spongy April at thy best betrims,

[012-083] To make cold nymphs chaste crowns; and thy broom-groves,

[012-084] Whose shadow the dismissed bachelor loves,

[012-085] Being lass-lorn; thy pole-clipt vineyard;

[012-086] And thy sea-marge, sterile and rocky-hard,

[012-087] 70 Where thou thyself dost air;—the queen o’ the sky,

[012-088] Whose watery arch and messenger am I,

[012-089] Bids thee leave these; and with her sovereign grace,

[012-090] Here on this grass-plot, in this very place,

[012-091] To come and sport:—her peacocks fly amain:

[012-092] IV. 1. 75 Approach, rich Ceres, her to entertain.

[012-093] *Enter Ceres.*

[012-094] *Cer.* Hail, many-colour’d messenger, that ne’er

[012-095] Dost disobey the wife of Jupiter;

[012-096] Who, with thy saffron wings, upon my flowers

[012-097] Diffusest honey-drops, refreshing showers;

[012-098] 80 And with each end of thy blue bow dost crown

[012-099] My bosky acres and my unshrubb’d down,

[012-100] Rich scarf to my proud earth;—why hath thy queen

[012-101] Summon’d me hither, to this short-grass’d green?

[012-102] *Iris.* A contract of true love to celebrate;

[012-103] 85 And some donation freely to estate

[012-104] On the blest lovers.

[012-105] *Cer.*

[012-106] Tell me, heavenly bow,

[012-107] If Venus or her son, as thou dost know,

[012-108] Do now attend the queen? Since they did plot

[012-109] The means that dusky Dis my daughter got,

[012-110] 90 Her and her blind boy’s scandal’d company

[012-111] I have forsworn.

[012-112] *Iris.*

[012-113] Of her society

[012-114] Be not afraid: I met her Deity

[012-115] Cutting the clouds towards Paphos, and her son

[012-116] Dove-drawn with her. Here thought they to have done

[012-117] 95 Some wanton charm upon this man and maid,

[012-118] Whose vows are, that no bed-right shall be paid

[012-119] Till Hymen’s torch be lighted: but in vain;

[012-120] Mars’s hot minion is returned again;

[012-121] Her waspish-headed son has broke his arrows,

[012-122] IV. 1. 100 Swears he will shoot no more, but play with sparrows,

[012-123] And be a boy right out.

[012-124] *Cer.*

[012-125] High’st queen of state,

[012-126] Great Juno, comes; I know her by her gait.

[012-127] *Enter Juno.*

[012-128] *Juno.* How does my bounteous sister? Go with me

[012-129] To bless this twain, that they may prosperous be,

[012-130] 105 And honour’d in their issue. They sing:

[012-131] *Juno.* Honour, riches, marriage-blessing,

[012-132] Long continuance, and increasing,

[012-133] Hourly joys be still upon you!

[012-134] Juno sings her blessings on you.

[012-135] 110 *Cer.* Earth’s increase, foison plenty,

[012-136] Barns and garners never empty;

[012-137] Vines with clustering bunches growing;

[012-138] Plants with goodly burthen bowing;

[012-139] Spring come to you at the farthest

[012-140] 115 In the very end of harvest!

[012-141] Scarcity and want shall shun you;

[012-142] Ceres’ blessing so is on you.

[012-143] *Fer.* This is a most majestic [[dream|vision]], and

[012-144] Harmonious charmingly. May I be bold

[012-145] To think these spirits?

[012-146] *Pros.*

[012-147] 120 Spirits, which by mine art

[012-148] I have from their confines call’d to enact

[012-149] My present fancies.

[012-150] *Fer.*

[012-151] Let me live here ever;

[012-152] So rare a wonder’d father and a wife

[012-153] Makes this place Paradise.

[012-154] Juno and Ceres whisper, and send Iris on employment.

[012-155] *Pros.*

[012-156] Sweet, now, silence!

[012-157] IV. 1. 125 Juno and Ceres whisper seriously;

[012-158] There’s something else to do: hush, and be mute,

[012-159] Or else our spell is marr’d.

[012-160] *Iris.* You nymphs, call’d Naiads, of the windring brooks,

[012-161] With your sedged crowns and ever-harmless looks,

[012-162] 130 Leave your crisp channels, and on this green land

[012-163] Answer your summons; Juno does [[authority|command]]:

[012-164] Come, temperate nymphs, and help to celebrate

[012-165] A contract of true love; be not too late.

[012-166] *Enter certain Nymphs.*

[012-167] You sunburnt sicklemen, of August weary,

[012-168] 135 Come hither from the furrow, and be merry:

[012-169] Make holiday; your rye-straw hats put on,

[012-170] And these fresh nymphs encounter every one

[012-171] In country footing.

[012-172] *Enter certain Reapers, properly habited: they join with the Nymphs in a graceful dance; towards the end whereof Prospero starts suddenly, and speaks; after which, to a strange, hollow, and confused noise, they heavily vanish.*

[012-173] *Pros.* [*Aside*] I had forgot that foul conspiracy

[012-174] 140 Of the beast [[caliban|Caliban]] and his confederates

[012-175] Against my life: the minute of their plot

[012-176] Is almost come. [*To the Spirits.*] Well done! avoid; no more!

[012-177] *Fer.* This is strange: your father’s in some passion

[012-178] That works him strongly.

[012-179] *Mir.*

[012-180] Never till this day

[012-181] 145 Saw I him touch’d with anger so distemper’d.

[012-182] *Pros.* You do look, my son, in a moved sort,

[012-183] As if you were dismay’d: be cheerful, sir.

[012-184] Our revels now are ended. These our actors,

[012-185] As I foretold you, were all spirits, and

[012-186] IV. 1. 150 Are melted into air, into thin air:

[012-187] And, like the baseless fabric of this vision,

[012-188] The cloud-capp’d towers, the gorgeous palaces,

[012-189] The solemn temples, the great globe itself,

[012-190] Yea, all which it inherit, shall dissolve,

[012-191] 155 And, like this insubstantial pageant faded,

[012-192] Leave not a rack behind. We are such stuff

[012-193] As dreams are made on; and our little life

[012-194] Is rounded with a sleep. Sir, I am vex’d;

[012-195] Bear with my weakness; my old brain is troubled:

[012-196] 160 Be not disturb’d with my infirmity:

[012-197] If you be pleased, retire into my cell,

[012-198] And there repose: a turn or two I’ll walk,

[012-199] To still my beating mind.

[012-200] *Fer.* *Mir.*

[012-201] We wish your peace. Exeunt.

[012-202] *Pros.* Come with a thought. I thank thee, Ariel: come.

[012-203] *Enter Ariel.*

[012-204] 165 *Ari.* Thy thoughts I cleave to. What’s thy pleasure?

[012-205] *Pros.*

[012-206] Spirit,

[012-207] We must prepare to meet with Caliban.

[012-208] *Ari.* Ay, my commander: when I presented Ceres,

[012-209] I thought to have told thee of it; but I fear’d

[012-210] Lest I might anger thee.

[012-211] 170 *Pros.* Say again, where didst thou leave these varlets?

[012-212] *Ari.* I told you, sir, they were red-hot with drinking;

[012-213] So full of valour that they smote the air

[012-214] For breathing in their faces; beat the ground

[012-215] For kissing of their feet; yet always bending

[012-216] IV. 1. 175 Towards their project. Then I beat my tabor;

[012-217] At which, like unback’d colts, they prick’d their ears,

[012-218] Advanced their eyelids, lifted up their noses

[012-219] As they smelt [[music|music]]: so I charm’d their ears,

[012-220] That, calf-like, they my lowing follow’d through

[012-221] 180 Tooth’d briers, sharp furzes, pricking goss, and thorns,

[012-222] Which enter’d their frail shins: at last I left them

[012-223] I’ the filthy-mantled pool beyond your cell,

[012-224] There dancing up to the chins, that the foul lake

[012-225] O’erstunk their feet.

[012-226] *Pros.*

[012-227] This was well done, my bird.

[012-228] 185 Thy shape invisible retain thou still:

[012-229] The trumpery in my house, go bring it hither,

[012-230] For stale to catch these thieves.

[012-231] *Ari.*

[012-232] I go, I go. Exit.

[012-233] *Pros.* A devil, a born devil, on whose [[nature|nature]]

[012-234] Nurture can never stick; on whom my pains,

[012-235] 190 Humanely taken, all, all lost, quite lost;

[012-236] And as with age his body uglier grows,

[012-237] So his mind cankers. I will plague them all,

[012-238] Even to roaring.

[012-239] *Re-enter Ariel, loaden with glistering apparel, &c.*

[012-240] Come, hang them on this line.

[012-241] *Prospero and Ariel remain, invisible. Enter Caliban, Stephano, and Trinculo, all wet.*

[012-242] *Cal.* Pray you, tread softly, that the blind mole may not

[012-243] 195 Hear a foot fall: we now are near his cell.

[012-244] *Ste.* Monster, your fairy, which you say is a harmless fairy, has done little better than played the Jack with us.

[012-245] *Trin.* Monster, I do smell all horse-piss; at which my nose is in great indignation.

[012-246] IV. 1. 200 *Ste.* So is mine. Do you hear, monster? If I should take a displeasure against you, look you,—

[012-247] *Trin.* Thou wert but a lost monster.

[012-248] *Cal.* Good my lord, give me thy favour still.

[012-249] Be patient, for the prize I’ll bring thee to

[012-250] 205 Shall hoodwink this mischance: therefore speak softly.

[012-251] All’s hush’d as midnight yet.

[012-252] *Trin.* Ay, but to lose our bottles in the pool,—

[012-253] *Ste.* There is not only disgrace and dishonour in that, monster, but an infinite loss.

[012-254] 210 *Trin.* That’s more to me than my wetting: yet this is your harmless fairy, monster.

[012-255] *Ste.* I will fetch off my bottle, though I be o’er ears for my labour.

[012-256] *Cal.* Prithee, my king, be quiet. See’st thou here,

[012-257] 215 This is the mouth o’ the cell: no noise, and enter.

[012-259] Thine own for ever, and I, thy Caliban,

[012-260] For aye thy foot-licker.

[012-261] *Ste.* Give me thy hand. I do begin to have bloody 220 thoughts.

[012-263] *Cal.* Let it alone, thou fool; it is but trash.

[012-264] *Trin.* O, ho, monster! we know what belongs to a frippery. IV. 1. 225 O King Stephano!

[012-265] *Ste.* Put off that gown, Trinculo; by this hand, I’ll have that gown.

[012-266] *Trin.* Thy Grace shall have it.

[012-267] *Cal.* The dropsy drown this fool! what do you mean

[012-268] 230 To dote thus on such luggage? Let’s alone,

[012-269] And do the murder first: if he awake,

[012-270] From toe to crown he’ll fill our skins with pinches,

[012-271] Make us strange stuff.

[012-278] With foreheads villanous low.
