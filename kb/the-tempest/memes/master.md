## Overview

The Master of the ship appears in the opening scene of the play, [[authority|command]]ing [[the-ship|the vessel]] as it is battered by [[the-shipwreck|the storm]] that [[prospero]] has raised through [[ariel]]. His role is brief but intense: he barks orders at the [[boatswain|Boatswain]] (002-003), struggles against the elements, and ultimately yields to the overwhelming [[storm|tempest]]. He reappears in the final scene, bewildered but alive, led onto the [[island|island]] by [[ariel|Ariel]] (014-280).

## Character and Role

The Master's first en[[dream|trance]] — "Enter a Ship-Master and a [[boatswain|Boatswain]]" (002-001) — immediately establishes the crisis that frames the entire play. He is a practical, no-nonsense maritime [[power|authority]] confronted with a supernatural force he cannot comprehend or combat. His shouted [[authority|command]]s — "Tend to the master's whistle" (002-006) — and ex[[transformation|change]]s with the [[boatswain|Boatswain]] and the panicking courtiers, p[[magic|art]]icularly [[gonzalo]], drive the tension of the opening scene. [[gonzalo|Gonzalo]]'s demand "Where's the master?" (002-008) underscores the chaos aboard [[the-ship|the vessel]].

> "Enter a Ship-Master and a Boatswain."
>
> (002-001)

In the final act, [[ariel|Ariel]] brings the Master and [[boatswain|Boatswain]] onto the [[island|island]] in a state of amazement. They have been preserved by [[ariel]]'s magic — the ship is safe in harbour, its crew sleeping below decks — and they arrive to witness the great [[forgiveness|reconciliation]].

> "Re-enter Ariel, with the Master and Boatswain amazedly following."
>
> (014-280)

## Key Scenes

The Master dominates the opening of Act 1, Scene 1, as the ship founders in [[the-shipwreck|the storm]]. His reappearance in Act 5, Scene 1, among the reunited court p[[magic|art]]y confirms that [[prospero|Prospero]]'s revenge has stopped short of taking innocent lives — the ship and its crew have been preserved intact.
