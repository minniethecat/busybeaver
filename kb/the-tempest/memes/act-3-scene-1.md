[008-001] *Enter Ferdinand, bearing a log.*

[008-002] *Fer.* There be some sports are painful, and their labour

[008-003] Delight in them sets off: some kinds of baseness

[008-004] Are nobly undergone, and most poor matters

[008-005] Point to rich ends. This my mean task

[008-006] 5 Would be as heavy to me as odious, but

[008-007] The mistress which I serve quickens what’s dead,

[008-008] And makes my labours pleasures: O, she is

[008-009] Ten times more gentle than her father’s crabbed.

[008-010] And he’s composed of harshness. I must remove

[008-011] 10 Some thousands of these logs, and pile them up,

[008-012] Upon a sore injunction: my sweet mistress

[008-013] Weeps when she sees me work, and says, such baseness

[008-014] Had never like executor. I forget:

[008-015] But these sweet thoughts do even refresh my labours,

[008-016] Most busy lest, when I do it.

[008-017] *Enter [[miranda|Miranda]]; and [[prospero|Prospero]] at a distance, unseen.*

[008-018] *Mir.*

[008-019] 15 Alas, now, pray you,

[008-020] Work not so hard: I would the lightning had

[008-021] Burnt up those logs that you are enjoin’d to pile!

[008-022] Pray, set it down, and rest you: when this burns,

[008-023] ’Twill weep for having wearied you. My father

[008-024] 20 Is hard at [[knowledge|study]]; pray, now, rest yourself;

[008-025] He’s safe for these three hours.

[008-026] *Fer.*

[008-027] O most dear mistress,

[008-028] The sun will set before I shall discharge

[008-029] What I must strive to do.

[008-030] *Mir.*

[008-031] If you’ll sit down,

[008-032] I’ll bear your logs the while: pray, give me that;

[008-033] I’ll carry it to the pile.

[008-034] *Fer.*

[008-035] III. 1. 25 No, precious creature;

[008-036] I had rather crack my sinews, break my back,

[008-037] Than you should such dishonour undergo,

[008-038] While I sit lazy by.

[008-039] *Mir.*

[008-040] It would become me

[008-041] As well as it does you: and I should do it

[008-042] 30 With much more ease; for my good will is to it,

[008-043] And yours it is against.

[008-044] *Pros.*

[008-045] Poor worm, thou [[magic|art]] infected!

[008-046] This visitation shows it.

[008-047] *Mir.*

[008-048] You look wearily.

[008-049] *Fer.* No, noble mistress; ’tis fresh morning with me

[008-050] When you are by at night. I do beseech you,—

[008-051] 35 Chiefly that I might set it in my prayers,—

[008-052] What is your name?

[008-053] *Mir.*

[008-054] Miranda.—O my father,

[008-055] I have broke your hest to say so!

[008-056] *Fer.*

[008-057] Admired Miranda!

[008-058] Indeed the top of admiration! worth

[008-059] What’s dearest to the world! Full many a lady

[008-060] 40 I have eyed with best regard, and many a time

[008-061] The [[music|harmony]] of their tongues hath into [[servitude|bondage]]

[008-062] Brought my too diligent ear: for several virtues

[008-063] Have I liked several women; never any

[008-064] With so full soul, but some defect in her

[008-065] 45 Did quarrel with the noblest grace she owed,

[008-066] And put it to the foil: but you, O you,

[008-067] So perfect and so peerless, are created

[008-068] Of every creature’s best!

[008-069] *Mir.*

[008-070] I do not know

[008-071] One of my sex; no woman’s face remember,

[008-072] III. 1. 50 Save, from my glass, mine own; nor have I seen

[008-073] More that I may call men than you, good friend,

[008-074] And my dear father: how features are abroad,

[008-075] I am skilless of; but, by my modesty,

[008-076] The jewel in my dower, I would not wish

[008-077] 55 Any companion in the world but you;

[008-078] Nor can imagination form a shape,

[008-079] Besides yourself, to like of. But I prattle

[008-080] Something too wildly, and my father’s precepts

[008-081] I therein do forget.

[008-082] *Fer.*

[008-083] I am, in my condition,

[008-084] 60 A prince, Miranda; I do think, a king;

[008-085] I would, not so!—and would no more endure

[008-086] This wooden slavery than to suffer

[008-087] The flesh-fly blow my mouth. Hear my soul speak:

[008-088] The very instant that I saw you, did

[008-089] 65 My heart fly to your service; there resides,

[008-090] To make me slave to it; and for your sake

[008-091] Am I this patient log-man.

[008-092] *Mir.*

[008-093] Do you love me?

[008-094] *Fer.* O heaven, O earth, bear witness to this sound,

[008-095] And crown what I profess with kind event,

[008-096] 70 If I speak true! if hollowly, invert

[008-097] What best is boded me to mischief! I,

[008-098] Beyond all limit of what else i’ the world,

[008-099] Do love, prize, honour you.

[008-100] *Mir.*

[008-101] I am a fool

[008-102] To weep at what I am glad of.

[008-103] *Pros.*

[008-104] Fair encounter

[008-105] III. 1. 75 Of two most rare affections! Heavens rain grace

[008-106] On that which breeds between ’em!

[008-107] *Fer.*

[008-108] Wherefore weep you?

[008-109] *Mir.* At mine unworthiness, that dare not offer

[008-110] What I desire to give; and much less take

[008-111] What I shall die to want. But this is trifling;

[008-112] 80 And all the more it seeks to hide itself,

[008-113] The bigger bulk it shows. Hence, bashful cunning!

[008-114] And prompt me, plain and holy innocence!

[008-115] I am your wife, if you will marry me;

[008-116] If not, I’ll die your maid: to be your fellow

[008-117] 85 You may deny me; but I’ll be your servant,

[008-118] Whether you will or no.

[008-119] *Fer.*

[008-120] My mistress, dearest;

[008-121] And I thus humble ever.

[008-122] *Mir.*

[008-123] My husband, then?

[008-124] *Fer.* Ay, with a heart as willing

[008-125] As bondage e’er of [[freedom|freedom]]: here’s my hand.

[008-126] 90 *Mir.* And mine, with my heart in’t: and now farewell

[008-127] Till half an hour hence.

[008-128] *Fer.*

[008-129] A thousand thousand!

[008-130] Exeunt Fer. and Mir. severally.

[008-131] *Pros.* So glad of this as they I cannot be,

[008-132] Who are surprised withal; but my rejoicing

[008-133] At nothing can be more. I’ll to my book;

[008-134] 95 For yet, ere supper-time, must I perform

[008-135] Much business appertaining. Exit.
