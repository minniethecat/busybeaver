[009-001] *Enter [[caliban|Caliban]], Stephano, and Trinculo.*

[009-002] *Ste.* Tell not me;—when the butt is out, we will drink water; not a drop before: therefore bear up, and board ’em. Servant-monster, drink to me.

[009-003] *Trin.* Servant-monster! the folly of this island! They 5 say there’s but five upon this [[island|isle]]: we are three of them; if th’ other two be brained like us, the state totters.

[009-004] *Ste.* Drink, servant-monster, when I bid thee: thy eyes are almost set in thy head.

[009-005] *Trin.* Where should they be set else? he were a brave 10 monster indeed, if they were set in his tail.

[009-006] *Ste.* My man-monster hath drowned his tongue in sack: for my part, the sea cannot drown me; I swam, ere I could recover the shore, five-and-thirty leagues off and on. By this light, thou shalt be my lieutenant, monster, or my 15 standard.

[009-007] *Trin.* Your lieutenant, if you list; he’s no standard.

[009-008] *Ste.* We’ll not run, Monsieur Monster.

[009-009] *Trin.* Nor go neither; but you’ll lie, like dogs, and yet say nothing neither.

[009-010] 20 *Ste.* Moon-calf, speak once in thy life, if thou beest a good moon-calf.

[009-011] *Cal.* How does thy honour? Let me lick thy shoe. I’ll not serve him, he is not valiant.

[009-012] *Trin.* Thou liest, most ignorant monster: I am in case III. 2. 25 to justle a constable. Why, thou debauched fish, thou, was there ever man a coward that hath drunk so much sack as I to-day? Wilt thou tell a monstrous lie, being but half a fish and half a monster?

[009-013] *Cal.* Lo, how he mocks me! wilt thou let him, my lord?

[009-014] 30 *Trin.* ‘Lord,’ quoth he! That a monster should be such a natural!

[009-015] *Cal.* Lo, lo, again! bite him to death, I prithee.

[009-016] *Ste.* Trinculo, keep a good tongue in your head: if you prove a mutineer,—the next tree! The poor monster’s my 35 subject, and he shall not suffer indignity.

[009-017] *Cal.* I thank my noble lord. Wilt thou be pleased to hearken once again to the suit I made to thee?

[009-018] *Ste.* Marry, will I: kneel and repeat it; I will stand, and so shall Trinculo.

[009-019] *Enter [[ariel|Ariel]], invisible.*

[009-020] 40 *Cal.* As I told thee before, I am subject to a tyrant, a sorcerer, that by his cunning hath cheated me of the island.

[009-021] *Ari.* Thou liest.

[009-022] *Cal.*

[009-023] Thou liest, thou jesting monkey, thou:

[009-024] I would my valiant [[master|master]] would destroy thee!

[009-025] I do not lie.

[009-026] 45 *Ste.* Trinculo, if you trouble him any more in’s tale, by this hand, I will supplant some of your teeth.

[009-027] *Trin.* Why, I said nothing.

[009-028] *Ste.* Mum, then, and no more. Proceed.

[009-029] *Cal.* I say, by [[magic|sorcery]] he got this isle;

[009-030] III. 2. 50 From me he got it. If thy greatness will

[009-031] Revenge it on him,—for I know thou darest,

[009-032] But this thing dare not,—

[009-033] *Ste.* That’s most certain.

[009-034] *Cal.* Thou shalt be lord of it, and I’ll serve thee.

[009-035] 55 *Ste.* How now shall this be compassed? Canst thou bring me to the party?

[009-036] *Cal.* Yea, yea, my lord: I’ll yield him thee a[[sleep|sleep]],

[009-037] Where thou mayst knock a nail into his head.

[009-038] *Ari.* Thou liest; thou canst not.

[009-039] 60 *Cal.* What a pied ninny’s this! Thou scurvy patch!

[009-040] I do beseech thy Greatness, give him blows,

[009-041] And take his bottle from him: when that’s gone,

[009-042] He shall drink nought but brine; for I’ll not show him

[009-043] Where the quick freshes are.

[009-044] 65 *Ste.* Trinculo, run into no further danger: interrupt the monster one word further, and, by this hand, I’ll turn my mercy out o’ doors, and make a stock-fish of thee.

[009-045] *Trin.* Why, what did I? I did nothing. I’ll go farther off.

[009-046] 70 *Ste.* Didst thou not say he lied?

[009-047] *Ari.* Thou liest.

[009-048] *Ste.* Do I so? take thou that. [*Beats him.*] As you like this, give me the lie another time.

[009-049] *Trin.* I did not give the lie. Out o’ your wits, and III. 2. 75 hearing too? A pox o’ your bottle! this can sack and drinking do. A murrain on your monster, and the devil take your fingers!

[009-050] *Cal.* Ha, ha, ha!

[009-051] *Ste.* Now, forward with your tale.—Prithee, stand farther 80 off.

[009-052] *Cal.* Beat him enough: after a little time, I’ll beat him too.

[009-053] *Ste.* Stand farther. Come, proceed.

[009-054] *Cal.* Why, as I told thee, ’tis a custom with him

[009-055] I’ th’ afternoon to [[sleep|sleep]]: there thou mayst brain him,

[009-056] 85 Having first seized his books; or with a log

[009-057] Batter his skull, or paunch him with a stake,

[009-058] Or cut his wezand with thy knife. Remember

[009-059] First to possess his books; for without them

[009-060] He’s but a sot, as I am, nor hath not

[009-061] 90 One spirit to [[authority|command]]: they all do hate him

[009-062] As rootedly as I. Burn but his books.

[009-063] He has brave utensils,—for so he calls them,—

[009-064] Which, when he has a house, he’ll deck withal.

[009-065] And that most deeply to consider is

[009-066] 95 The beauty of his daughter; he himself

[009-067] Calls her a nonpareil: I never saw a woman,

[009-068] But only Sycorax my dam and she;

[009-069] But she as far surpasseth Sycorax

[009-070] As great’st does least.

[009-071] *Ste.*

[009-072] Is it so brave a lass?

[009-073] III. 2. 100 *Cal.* Ay, lord; she will become thy bed, I warrant,

[009-074] And bring thee forth brave brood.

[009-075] *Ste.* Monster, I will kill this man: his daughter and I will be king and queen,—save our Graces!—and Trinculo and thyself shall be viceroys. Dost thou like the plot, 105 Trinculo?

[009-076] *Trin.* Excellent.

[009-077] *Ste.* Give me thy hand: I am sorry I beat thee; but, while thou livest, keep a good tongue in thy head.

[009-078] *Cal.* Within this half hour will he be a[[sleep|sleep]]:

[009-079] Wilt thou destroy him then?

[009-080] *Ste.*

[009-081] 110 Ay, on mine honour.

[009-082] *Ari.* This will I tell my [[master|master]].

[009-083] *Cal.* Thou makest me merry; I am full of pleasure:

[009-084] Let us be jocund: will you troll the catch

[009-085] You taught me but while-ere?

[009-086] 115 *Ste.* At thy request, monster, I will do reason, any reason. —Come on. Trinculo, let us sing. Sings.

[009-087] Flout ’em and scout ’em, and scout ’em and flout ’em;

[009-088] Thought is free.

[009-089] *Cal.* That’s not the tune.

[009-090] Ariel plays the tune on a tabor and pipe.

[009-091] 120 *Ste.* What is this same?

[009-092] *Trin.* This is the tune of our catch, played by the picture of Nobody.

[009-093] *Ste.* If thou beest a man, show thyself in thy likeness: if thou beest a devil, take’t as thou list.

[009-094] III. 2. 125 *Trin.* O, forgive me my sins!

[009-095] *Ste.* He that dies pays all debts: I defy thee. Mercy upon us!

[009-096] *Cal.* [[art|Art]] thou afeard?

[009-097] *Ste.* No, monster, not I.

[009-098] 130 *Cal.* Be not afeard; the isle is full of noises,

[009-099] Sounds and sweet airs, that give delight, and hurt not.

[009-100] Sometimes a thousand twangling instruments

[009-101] Will hum about mine ears; and sometime voices,

[009-102] That, if I then had waked after long [[sleep|sleep]],

[009-103] 135 Will make me [[sleep|sleep]] again: and then, in [[dream|dream]]ing,

[009-104] The clouds methought would open, and show riches

[009-105] Ready to drop upon me; that, when I waked,

[009-106] I cried to [[dream|dream]] again.

[009-107] *Ste.* This will prove a brave kingdom to me, where I 140 shall have my [[music|music]] for nothing.

[009-108] *Cal.* When [[prospero|Prospero]] is destroyed.

[009-109] *Ste.* That shall be by and by: I remember the story.

[009-110] *Trin.* The sound is going away; let’s follow it, and after do our work.

[009-111] 145 *Ste.* Lead, monster; we’ll follow. I would I could see this taborer; he lays it on.

[009-112] *Trin.* Wilt come? I’ll follow, Stephano. Exeunt.
