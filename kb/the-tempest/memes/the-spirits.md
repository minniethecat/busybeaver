## Overview

The spirits of the island are the invisible infrastructure of [[prospero|Prospero]]'s magic. They appear in the play under [[ariel|Ariel]]'s [[authority|command]] — executing [[the-shipwreck|the storm]], singing [[ferdinand|Ferdinand]] to [[miranda|Miranda]], conjuring the masque of goddesses, hunting [[caliban|Caliban]]'s conspirators as hounds — but they predate [[prospero|Prospero]]'s arrival. [[caliban|Caliban]] describes them in the play's most lyrical passage: "[[island|the isle]] is full of noises, / Sounds and sweet airs, that give delight and hurt not" (009-098–099). The spirits are native to the island in a way that [[prospero|Prospero]] can never be, and their enchanted music — which [[caliban|Caliban]] heard before [[prospero|Prospero]] taught him language — represents a magic older and more fundamental than [[prospero|Prospero]]'s [[magic|art]].

> My spirits, as in a dream, are all bound up.
> My father's loss, the weakness which I feel,
> The wreck of all my friends, nor this man's threats,
> To whom I am subdued, are but light to me,
> Might I but through my prison once a day
> Behold this maid: all corners else o' th' earth
> Let liberty make use of; space enough
> Have I in such a prison.
>
> (003-668)

[[prospero|Prospero]]'s relationship to [[ariel|the spirit]]s is mediated entirely through [[ariel|Ariel]], who summons them, directs them, and dismisses them. The spirits have no individual voices (except when assuming the roles of goddesses in the masque), no names except those [[ariel|Ariel]] gives them, and no agency independent of [[prospero|Prospero]]'s [[authority|command]]s. They are the play's most eloquent metaphor for the cost of [[prospero|Prospero]]'s [[power|power]]: a whole order of being conscripted into [[servitude|service]] by a magician who will, at the play's end, set them free.

The spirits appear in several forms throughout the play:

| [[ariel|Ariel]]'s music | Acts 1–5 | Invisible spirits playing "solemn music" (005-174) that puts courtiers to sleep; the "thousand twangling instruments" (009-098) [[caliban|Caliban]] hears |

## Political Role

The spirits occupy an ambiguous position in the play's political ecology. They are not slaves ([[ariel|Ariel]] is promised freedom, and the lesser spirits presumably share in that liberation), but neither are they free agents. They are conscripted labour — beings whose native [[power|power]]s are repurposed for [[prospero|Prospero]]'s project. The spirits never speak in their own voices; when they speak at all, it is through [[ariel|Ariel]]'s ventriloquism or in the ceremonial language of the masque.

The spirits' relationship to [[caliban|Caliban]] is p[[magic|art]]icularly complex. [[caliban|Caliban]] hears [[ariel|the spirit]]s' music and is moved by it — "in [[dream|dream]]ing, / The clouds methought would open and show riches / Ready to drop upon me; that, when I waked, / I cried to [[dream|dream]] again" (009-104–106). The spirits are the one aspect of the island that [[caliban|Caliban]] does not resent, the one experience of [[magic|enchantment]] that is not mediated through [[prospero|Prospero]]'s domination. This suggests an affinity between the island's original inhabitant and its native spirits that [[prospero|Prospero]]'s regime cannot erase, even as it [[authority|command]]s both.

## Related Characters

- **[[ariel|Ariel]]**: The "airy spirit" who [[authority|command]]s the island's spirits on [[prospero|Prospero]]'s behalf. Their relationship is hierarchical — [[ariel|Ariel]] is the overseer, [[ariel|the spirit]]s are the workers — but their shared origin (native to the island, conscripted by [[prospero|Prospero]]) suggests solidarity.
- **[[prospero|Prospero]]**: The magician who [[authority|command]]s [[ariel|the spirit]]s through [[ariel|Ariel]]. His abjuration in Act 5 [[freedom|release]]s them from [[servitude|service]], though it is [[ariel|Ariel]], not [[ariel|the spirit]]s, who speaks of freedom.
- **[[caliban|Caliban]]**: The island's original inhabitant, who hears [[ariel|the spirit]]s' music as a native presence. His relationship to [[ariel|the spirit]]s is one of affinity, not [[authority|command]] — they speak to him in [[dream|dream]]s, not orders.
