[010-001] *Enter Alonso, Sebastian, [[antonio|Antonio]], Gonzalo, Adrian, Francisco, and others.*

[010-002] *Gon.* By’r lakin, I can go no further, sir;

[010-003] My old bones ache: here’s a maze trod, indeed,

[010-004] Through forth-rights and meanders! By your patience,

[010-005] I needs must rest me.

[010-006] *Alon.*

[010-007] Old lord, I cannot blame thee,

[010-008] 5 Who am myself attach’d with weariness,

[010-009] To the dulling of my spirits: sit down, and rest.

[010-010] Even here I will put off my hope, and keep it

[010-011] No longer for my flatterer: he is drown’d

[010-012] Whom thus we stray to find; and the sea mocks

[010-013] 10 Our frustrate search on land. Well, let him go.

[010-014] *Ant.* [*Aside to Seb.*] I am right glad that he’s so out of hope.

[010-015] Do not, for one repulse, forego the purpose

[010-016] That you resolved to effect.

[010-017] *Seb.* [*Aside to Ant.*]

[010-018] The next advantage

[010-019] Will we take throughly.

[010-020] *Ant.* [*Aside to Seb.*]

[010-021] Let it be to-night;

[010-022] 15 For, now they are oppress’d with travel, they

[010-023] Will not, nor cannot, use such vigilance

[010-024] As when they are fresh.

[010-025] *Seb.* [*Aside to Ant.*]

[010-026] I say, to-night: no more.

[010-027] Solemn and strange music.

[010-028] *Alon.* What [[music|harmony]] is this?—My good friends, hark!

[010-029] *Gon.* Marvellous sweet music!

[010-030] *Enter [[prospero|Prospero]] above, invisible. Enter several strange Shapes, bringing in a banquet: they dance about it with gentle actions of salutation; and, inviting the King, &c. to eat, they depart.*

[010-031] 20 *Alon.* Give us kind keepers, heavens!—What were these?

[010-032] *Seb.* A living drollery. Now I will believe

[010-033] That there are unicorns; that in Arabia

[010-034] There is one tree, the phœnix’ throne; one phœnix

[010-035] At this hour reigning there.

[010-036] *Ant.*

[010-037] I’ll believe both;

[010-038] III. 3. 25 And what does else want credit, come to me,

[010-039] And I’ll be sworn ’tis true: travellers ne’er did lie,

[010-040] Though fools at home condemn ’em.

[010-041] *Gon.*

[010-042] If in [[naples|Naples]]

[010-043] I should report this now, would they believe me?

[010-044] If I should say, I saw such islanders,—

[010-045] 30 For, certes, these are people of the island,—

[010-046] Who, though they are of monstrous shape, yet, note,

[010-047] Their manners are more gentle-kind than of

[010-048] Our human generation you shall find

[010-049] Many, nay, almost any.

[010-050] *Pros.* [*Aside*]

[010-051] Honest lord,

[010-052] 35 Thou hast said well; for some of you there present

[010-053] Are worse than devils.

[010-054] *Alon.*

[010-055] I cannot too much muse

[010-056] Such shapes, such gesture, and such sound, expressing—

[010-057] Although they want the use of tongue—a kind

[010-058] Of excellent dumb discourse.

[010-059] *Pros.* [*Aside*]

[010-060] Praise in departing.

[010-061] *Fran.* They vanish’d strangely.

[010-062] *Seb.*

[010-063] 40 No matter, since

[010-064] They have left their viands behind; for we have stomachs.—

[010-065] Will’t please you taste of what is here?

[010-066] *Alon.*

[010-067] Not I.

[010-068] *Gon.* Faith, sir, you need not fear. When we were boys,

[010-069] Who would believe that there were mountaineers

[010-070] 45 Dew-lapp’d like bulls, whose throats had hanging at ’em

[010-071] Wallets of flesh? or that there were such men

[010-072] Whose heads stood in their breasts? which now we find

[010-073] Each putter-out of five for one will bring us

[010-074] Good warrant of.

[010-075] *Alon.*

[010-076] I will stand to, and feed,

[010-077] III. 3. 50 Although my last: no matter, since I feel

[010-078] The best is past. Brother, my lord the duke,

[010-079] Stand to, and do as we.

[010-080] *Thunder and lightning. Enter [[ariel|Ariel]], like a [[harpy|harpy]]; claps his wings upon the table; and, with a quaint device, the banquet vanishes.*

[010-081] *Ari.* You are three men of sin, whom Destiny,—

[010-082] That hath to instrument this lower world

[010-083] 55 And what is in’t,—the never-surfeited sea

[010-084] Hath caused to belch up you; and on this island,

[010-085] Where man doth not inhabit,—you ’mongst men

[010-086] Being most unfit to live. I have made you mad;

[010-087] And even with such-like valour men hang and drown

[010-088] Their proper selves. Alon., Seb. &c. draw their swords.

[010-089] 60 You fools! I and my fellows

[010-090] Are ministers of Fate: the elements,

[010-091] Of whom your swords are temper’d, may as well

[010-092] Wound the loud winds, or with bemock’d-at stabs

[010-093] Kill the still-closing waters, as diminish

[010-094] 65 One dowle that’s in my plume: my fellow-ministers

[010-095] Are like invulnerable. If you could hurt,

[010-096] Your swords are now too massy for your strengths,

[010-097] And will not be uplifted. But remember,—

[010-098] For that’s my business to you,—that you three

[010-099] 70 From [[milan|Milan]] did supplant good Prospero;

[010-100] Exposed unto the sea, which hath requit it,

[010-101] Him and his innocent child: for which foul deed

[010-102] The [[power|power]]s, delaying, not forgetting, have

[010-103] Incensed the seas and shores, yea, all the creatures,

[010-104] III. 3. 75 Against your peace. Thee of thy son, Alonso,

[010-105] They have bereft; and do pronounce by me:

[010-106] Lingering perdition—worse than any death

[010-107] Can be at once—shall step by step attend

[010-108] You and your ways; whose wraths to guard you from,—

[010-109] 80 Which here, in this most desolate [[island|isle]], else falls

[010-110] Upon your heads,—is nothing but heart-sorrow

[010-111] And a clear life ensuing.

[010-112] *He vanishes in thunder; then, to soft music, enter the Shapes again, and dance, with mocks and mows, and carrying out the table.*

[010-113] *Pros.* Bravely the figure of this [[harpy|harpy]] hast thou

[010-114] Perform’d, my Ariel; a grace it had, devouring:

[010-115] 85 Of my instruction hast thou nothing bated

[010-116] In what thou hadst to say: so, with good life

[010-117] And observation strange, my meaner ministers

[010-118] Their several kinds have done. My high charms work,

[010-119] And these mine enemies are all knit up

[010-120] 90 In their distractions: they now are in my [[power|power]];

[010-121] And in these fits I leave them, while I visit

[010-122] Young Ferdinand,—whom they suppose is drown’d,—

[010-123] And his and mine loved darling. Exit above.

[010-124] *Gon.* I’ the name of something holy, sir, why stand you

[010-125] In this strange stare?

[010-126] *Alon.*

[010-127] 95 O, it is monstrous, monstrous!

[010-128] Methought the billows spoke, and told me of it;

[010-129] The winds did sing it to me; and the thunder,

[010-130] That deep and dreadful organ-pipe, pronounced

[010-131] The name of Prosper: it did bass my trespass.

[010-132] III. 3. 100 Therefore my son i’ th’ ooze is bedded; and

[010-133] I’ll seek him deeper than e’er plummet sounded,

[010-134] And with him there lie mudded. Exit.

[010-135] *Seb.*

[010-136] But one fiend at a time,

[010-137] I’ll fight their legions o’er.

[010-138] *Ant.*

[010-139] I’ll be thy second.

[010-140] Exeunt Seb. and Ant.

[010-141] *Gon.* All three of them are desperate: their great [[guilt|guilt]],

[010-142] 105 Like poison given to work a great time after,

[010-143] Now ’gins to bite the spirits. I do beseech you,

[010-144] That are of suppler joints, follow them swiftly,

[010-145] And hinder them from what this ecstasy

[010-146] May now provoke them to.

[010-147] *Adr.*

[010-148] Follow, I pray you. Exeunt.
