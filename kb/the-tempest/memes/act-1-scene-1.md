[002-001] *Enter *a Ship-[[master|master]]* and *a [[boatswain|boatswain]]*.*

[002-002] *Mast.* [[boatswain|boatswain]]!

[002-003] *Boats.* Here, [[master|master]]: what cheer?

[002-004] *Mast.* Good, speak to the [[mariners|mariners]]: fall to’t, yarely, or we run ourselves aground: bestir, bestir. Exit.

[002-005] *Enter *[[mariners|mariners]]*.*

[002-006] 5 *Boats.* Heigh, my hearts! cheerly, cheerly, my hearts! yare, yare! Take in the topsail. Tend to the [[master|master]]’s whistle. Blow, till thou burst thy wind, if room enough!

[002-007] *Enter Alonso, Sebastian, [[antonio|Antonio]], Ferdinand, Gonzalo, and others.*

[002-008] *Alon.* Good [[boatswain|boatswain]], have care. Where’s the [[master|master]]? Play the men.

[002-009] 10 *Boats.* I pray now, keep below.

[002-010] *Ant.* Where is the [[master|master]], [[boatswain|boatswain]]?

[002-011] *Boats.* Do you not hear him? You mar our labour: keep your cabins: you do assist [[the-shipwreck|the storm]].

[002-012] *Gon.* Nay, good, be patient.

[002-013] 15 *Boats.* When the sea is. Hence! What cares these roarers for the name of king? To cabin: silence! trouble us not.

[002-014] *Gon.* Good, yet remember whom thou hast aboard.

[002-015] *Boats.* None that I more love than myself. You are a 20 Counsellor; if you can [[authority|command]] these elements to silence, and work the peace of the present, we will not hand a rope more; use your [[power|authority]]: if you cannot, give thanks you have lived so long, and make yourself ready in your cabin for the mischance of the hour, if it so hap. Cheerly, good I. 1. 25 hearts! Out of our way, I say. Exit.

[002-016] *Gon.* I have great comfort from this fellow: methinks he hath no drowning mark upon him; his complexion is perfect gallows. Stand fast, good Fate, to his hanging: make the rope of his [[fortune|destiny]] our cable, for our own doth 30 little advantage. If he be not born to be hanged, our case is miserable. Exeunt.

[002-017] *Re-enter *[[boatswain|boatswain]]*.*

[002-018] *Boats.* Down with the topmast! yare! lower, lower! Bring her to try with main-course. [*A cry within.*] A plague upon this howling! they are louder than the weather 35 or our office.

[002-019] *Re-enter Sebastian, Antonio, and Gonzalo.*

[002-020] Yet again! what do you here? Shall we give o’er, and drown? Have you a mind to sink?

[002-021] *Seb.* A pox o’ your throat, you bawling, blasphemous, incharitable dog!

[002-022] 40 *Boats.* Work you, then.

[002-023] *Ant.* Hang, cur! hang, you whoreson, insolent noise-maker. We are less afraid to be drowned than thou [[magic|art]].

[002-024] *Gon.* I’ll warrant him for drowning; though the ship were no stronger than a nutshell, and as leaky as an unstanched 45 wench.

[002-025] *Boats.* Lay her a-hold, a-hold! set her two courses off to sea again; lay her off.

[002-026] *Enter *[[mariners|mariners]]* wet.*

[002-027] *[[mariners|mariners]].* All lost! to prayers, to prayers! all lost!

[002-028] *Boats.* What, must our mouths be cold?

[002-029] I. 1. 50 *Gon.* The king and prince at prayers! let’s assist them,

[002-030] For our case is as theirs.

[002-031] *Seb.*

[002-032] I’m out of patience.

[002-033] *Ant.* We are merely cheated of our lives by drunkards:

[002-034] This wide-chapp’d rascal,—would thou mightst lie drowning

[002-035] The washing of ten tides!

[002-036] *Gon.*

[002-037] He’ll be hang’d yet,

[002-038] 55 Though every drop of water swear against it,

[002-039] And gape at widest to glut him.

[002-040] *A confused noise within: *“Mercy on us!”—“We split, we split!”—“Farewell my wife and children!”—“Farewell, brother!”—“We split, we split, we split!”**

[002-041] 60 *Ant.* Let’s all sink with the king.

[002-042] *Seb.* Let’s take leave of him. Exeunt Ant. and Seb.

[002-043] *Gon.* Now would I give a thousand furlongs of sea for an acre of barren ground, long heath, brown furze, any thing. The wills above be done! but I would fain die a 65 dry death. Exeunt.
