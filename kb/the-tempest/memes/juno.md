## Overview

Juno is the queen of the Roman gods, presented as a spirit in [[prospero]]'s betrothal masque in Act 4, Scene 1. She is listed among [[ariel|the spirit]]s in the Dramatis Personae (P02-001). She descends at the climax of the masque — the most spectacular en[[dream|trance]] in [[the-masque|the pageant]], with the Folio stage direction instructing "Juno descends" (042-020) — to deliver a formal blessing of honour, riches, and marital prosperity upon [[ferdinand]] and [[miranda]]. As the highest-ranking deity in the masque, Juno represents the celestial sanction without which [[prospero|Prospero]]'s dynastic project would lack divine [[authority|legitimacy]]. Her descent from the "heavens" — almost certainly via a mechanical throne or winch in the Jacobean playhouse — is the visual and symbolic apex of [[the-masque|the pageant]].

## Character and Role

Juno's appearance is the ceremonial high point of the masque. Her arrival is heralded by [[iris]], who recognizes her by her majestic gait: "Great Juno, comes; I know her by her gait" (012-126). The stage direction "Enter Juno" (012-127) marks her en[[dream|trance]] at this climactic moment. Juno addresses [[ceres]] as her "bounteous sister" (012-128) — linking the divine household of Olympus — and proceeds to sing a blessing that encompasses the three principal goods of a prosperous marriage: social standing, material wealth, and fruitful union.

> "Honour, riches, marriage-blessing, / Long continuance, and increasing, / Hourly joys be still upon you! / Juno sings her blessings on you."
> — (012-131)

The blessing is concise but comprehensive. "Honour" confers social [[authority|legitimacy]]; "riches" ensures material prosperity; "marriage-blessing" sanctifies the union; "long continuance, and increasing" prays for both longevity and progeny — the dual guarantees of dynastic succession. The triplet structure and rhyming couplets invoke the formal rhetoric of liturgical blessing, lending the masque an almost sacramental gravity.

After delivering her blessing, Juno and [[ceres|Ceres]] engage in a whispered conference, sending [[iris|Iris]] to summon the [[nymph|Naiads]] — the [[nymph|nymphs]] of rivers and streams — to perform a dance (012-154). Her [[authority|command]]ing presence anchors the supernatural hierarchy of the masque as queen of the gods.

> "Juno does command: / Come, temperate nymphs, and help to celebrate..."
> — (012-163)

## Key Scenes

Juno appears exclusively in Act 4, Scene 1, within [[the-masque|the betrothal masque]]. Her descent, blessing, and whispered ex[[transformation|change]] with [[ceres|Ceres]] and [[iris|Iris]] constitute the formal and ceremonial climax of [[the-masque|the pageant]] before its abrupt dissolution when [[prospero|Prospero]] remembers [[caliban]]'s conspiracy.

## Critical Reception

Juno's descent has attracted considerable stage-historical attention. The Folio stage direction "Juno descends" implies the use of a mechanical lowering device — a throne, chariot, or cloud-machine — that would have been one of the more elaborate special effects available to the King's Men at the Blackfriars or Globe playhouse. The moment is, in effect, a Jacobean equivalent of the *deus ex machina* of classical theatre, repurposed here not to resolve the plot but to sanctify a betrothal within a play-within-the-play.

Critics have also explored the political theology of Juno's blessing. As queen of the gods, she represents the highest [[power|authority]] [[prospero|Prospero]] can invoke — yet she is, within the fiction, entirely his creation, a spirit conjured by his "[[magic|art]]." This paradox — [[prospero|the magician]] summoning a goddess who outranks him — has been read as a figure for [[prospero|Prospero]]'s complex relationship to power throughout the play: he [[authority|command]]s forces greater than himself, but only temporarily, and only within the enchanted circle of his [[island|island]]. When Juno descends, it is [[prospero|Prospero]] who has engineered the descent; when she disappears, it is [[prospero|Prospero]] who remembers the "foul conspiracy / Of the beast [[caliban|Caliban]]" and dissolves [[the-masque|the pageant]] with the famous speech "Our revels now are ended" (012-197).
