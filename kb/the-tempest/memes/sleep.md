## Overview

Sleep in *The Tempest* is never merely rest. It is the medium of [[prospero|Prospero]]'s [[power|power]]—characters are put to sleep, woken from sleep, and caught in states between sleep and waking where perception blurs and moral transformation becomes possible. The play opens with a [[storm|shipwreck]] that [[miranda|Miranda]] calls a "direful spectacle" from which she cannot look away; it ends with [[prospero|Prospero]] promising to tell his story in a space of shared wakefulness. Between these poles, sleep marks every significant transition: the court falls into enchanted slumber (005-179)(005-194), [[miranda|Miranda]] sleeps while [[prospero|Prospero]] works his [[magic|art]], and [[alonso|Alonso]] awakens from a [[guilt|guilt]]-haunted [[dream|trance]] to find [[forgiveness|forgiveness]].

## In the Play

[[prospero|Prospero]]'s control over sleep is his most intimate [[power|power]]. He puts [[miranda|Miranda]] to sleep mid-conversation—"Thou [[magic|art]] inclined to sleep; 'tis a good dulness" (003-247)—and she obeys instantly: "thou canst not choose. [[miranda|Miranda]] sleeps" (003-248). The gesture demonstrates his [[authority|command]] over even her consciousness. The courtiers are put to sleep by [[ariel|Ariel]]'s music, and [[alonso|Alonso]] and [[gonzalo|Gonzalo]] doze while [[antonio|Antonio]] and [[sebastian|Sebastian]] plot murder—sleep as the vulnerability that enables treachery (005-179)(005-194).

The [[storm|shipwreck]]ed mariners sleep through the entire action: "The mariners all under hatches stow'd; / Who, with a charm join'd to their suffer'd labour, / I have left asleep" (003-309–311). Their enchanted slumber is the condition of [[prospero|Prospero]]'s plan—they must not witness the [[island|island]]'s wonders because they have no moral transformation to undergo. In the final act, [[prospero|Prospero]] sends [[ariel|Ariel]] to wake them: "There shalt thou find [[mariners|the mariners]] asleep / Under the hatches" (014-111–112).

[[alonso|Alonso]]'s sleep is the most heavily freighted. Waking from what he believes is the loss of his son—"What, all so soon asleep! I wish mine eyes / Would, with themselves, shut up my thoughts" (005-180–181)—he experiences [[prospero|Prospero]]'s revelation as a second waking: "Thy dukedom I resign" (014-135). His sleep—whether enchanted or [[guilt|guilt]]-induced—is the passage through which [[forgiveness|forgiveness]] enters.

At the play's end, [[prospero|Prospero]] renounces his [[power|power]] over sleep alongside his other magic (014-065)(014-068). The Epilogue asks the audience's "good hands" to "[[freedom|release]] me from my bands" (015-019–020)—a final waking from the [[magic|enchantment]] of theatre itself.

## Key Passages

> Here cease more questions:
> Thou art inclined to sleep.
>
> (003-247)

[[miranda|Miranda]]'s instantaneous sleep at [[prospero|Prospero]]'s [[authority|command]] demonstrates his [[power|power]]. He controls not just the [[island|island]]'s spirits but his daughter's consciousness—the most intimate form of [[servitude|mastery]].

> The mariners all under hatches stow'd;
> Who, with a charm join'd to their suffer'd labour,
> I have left asleep
>
> (003-309–311)

The mariners' enchanted sleep marks the boundary between those who undergo transformation (the court) and those who merely function ([[mariners|the crew]]). [[ariel|Ariel]]'s report to [[prospero|Prospero]] confirms they are preserved rather than tested.
