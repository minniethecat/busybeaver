## Overview

The Mariners are the working crew of the ship that founders in the opening [[storm|tempest]] of *The Tempest*. Listed in the Dramatis Personae alongside the [[master|Master]] and [[boatswain|Boatswain]] (P02-001), they are the silent labourers whose physical effort — hauling ropes, trimming sails, crying out in despair — creates the visceral panic of the play's first scene. Unlike the aristocratic passengers, they have no names and almost no individual lines; their one collective utterance is a cry of despair: "All lost! to prayers, to prayers! all lost!" (002-027).

## Role in the Play

The Mariners appear in two bursts during Act 1, Scene 1. They first enter at the [[boatswain|Boatswain]]'s [[authority|command]] — "speak to the mariners: fall to't, yarely, or we run ourselves aground" (002-004) — and exit to work the ship (002-005). They re-enter moments later, "wet" from [[the-shipwreck|the storm]] (002-026), and deliver their single line before the scene collapses into chaos.

After the opening scene, the Mariners vanish from the stage but not from the play's awareness. [[prospero|Prospero]] asks [[ariel|Ariel]] specifically about their [[fortune|fate]]: "The mariners, say how thou hast disposed" (003-302), and [[ariel|Ariel]] reports them "all under hatches stow'd" and "charmed asleep" (003-309). They remain in this enchanted [[sleep|slumber]] through the entire [[island|island]] action — preserved, unconscious, exempt from the moral trials undergone by their social superiors. In the final act, [[ariel|Ariel]] confirms "There shalt thou find the mariners asleep" (014-111), and they are presumably among the crew restored to the ship for the voyage home.

## Dramatic Function

The Mariners serve as a collective foil to the named characters. Their physical labour anchors the opening scene in material reality — these are working men doing a dangerous job — before the play shifts to the magical and political concerns of the [[island|island]]. Their enchanted sleep for the remainder of the play marks a boundary: the Mariners are untouched by [[prospero|Prospero]]'s moral drama, preserved rather than tested. They represent ordinary humanity, the unremarkable many whose lives are caught up in the quarrels of the powerful but bear no [[guilt|guilt]] and require no transformation.

## Key Passages

The Mariners' only spoken line in the play is their cry of despair as the ship founders in the opening storm:

> *Mariners.* All lost! to prayers, to prayers! all lost!
>
> (002-027)

[[prospero|Prospero]]'s first inquiry after [[the-shipwreck|the tempest]] is for the Mariners' welfare, and [[ariel|Ariel]]'s report confirms their enchanted preservation through the play's entire action:

> The mariners all under hatches stow'd;
> Who, with a charm join'd to their suffer'd labour,
> I have left asleep
>
> (003-309–311)

In the final act, [[prospero|Prospero]] dispatches [[ariel|Ariel]] to wake the sleeping crew for the voyage home to [[naples|Naples]]:

> There shalt thou find the mariners asleep
> Under the hatches; the master and the boatswain
> Being awake, enforce them to this place
>
> (014-111–113)
