# Carthage

## Overview

Carthage is the ancient Phoenician city (in present-day [[tunis|Tunis]]ia) invoked in a comic geographical debate among the courtiers in Act II, Scene I of *The Tempest*. The reference serves as a vehicle for wordplay that reveals the characters' wit and the play's engagement with classical history.

## The Carthage-Tunis Debate

The debate begins when [[adrian|Adrian]] remarks that "[[tunis|Tunis]] was never graced before with such a paragon [as [[claribel|Claribel]]] to their queen" (005-061). [[gonzalo|Gonzalo]] counters with a historical correction: "she was of Carthage, not of [[tunis|Tunis]]" (005-065) — insisting that [[widow-dido|Dido]], the legendary queen, [[authority|rule]]d from Carthage, not from the later city of [[tunis|Tunis]]. [[gonzalo|Gonzalo]] doubles down: "This [[tunis|Tunis]], sir, was Carthage" (005-066). When [[adrian|Adrian]] questions this ("Carthage?" (005-067)), [[gonzalo|Gonzalo]] affirms with finality: "I assure you, Carthage" (005-068).

## The Widow Dido Connection

The Carthage discussion is interwoven with running jokes about "[[widow-dido|Widow Dido]]," the C[[magic|art]]haginian queen from Virgil's *Aeneid*. [[antonio|Antonio]] and [[sebastian|Sebastian]] mock [[gonzalo|Gonzalo]]'s erudition, repeatedly exclaiming "widow [[widow-dido|Dido]]!" (005-063) (005-077) (005-078). The conflation of [[widow-dido|Dido]], Carthage, and [[tunis|Tunis]] creates a layered classical allusion that the courtiers use as a weapon of wit against [[gonzalo|Gonzalo]]'s discursive rambling.

## Dramatic Function

The Carthage reference serves multiple dramatic purposes: it establishes the Mediterranean geography of the play's world; it provides an opportunity for [[antonio|Antonio]] and [[sebastian|Sebastian]] to display their cynical humour (contrasting with [[gonzalo|Gonzalo]]'s idealism); and it foreshadows the themes of political marriage and Mediterranean power dynamics, as [[claribel|Claribel]]'s marriage to [[tunis|the King of Tunis]] connects the play to the broader classical world referenced through Carthage.
