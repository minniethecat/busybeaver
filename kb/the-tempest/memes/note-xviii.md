[034-001] V. 1. 309. *Of these our dear-beloved solemnized.* The Folios have ‘belov’d’; a mode of spelling, which in this case is convenient as indicating the probable rhythm of the verse. We have written ‘beloved,’ in accordance with the general [[authority|rule]] mentioned in the Preface.

[034-002] ‘Solemnized’ occurs in four other verse passages of Shakespeare. It is three times to be accented ‘sólemnized’ and once (*Love’s Labour’s Lost*, II. 1. 41) ‘solémnized.’
