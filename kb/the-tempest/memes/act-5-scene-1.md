[014-001] *Enter [[prospero|Prospero]] in his magic robes, and [[ariel|Ariel]].*

[014-002] *Pros.* Now does my project gather to a head:

[014-003] My charms crack not; my spirits obey; and time

[014-004] Goes upright with his carriage. How’s the day?

[014-005] *Ari.* On the sixth hour; at which time, my lord,

[014-006] You said our work should cease.

[014-007] *Pros.*

[014-008] 5 I did say so,

[014-009] When first I raised [[the-shipwreck|the tempest]]. Say, my spirit,

[014-010] How fares the king and’s followers?

[014-011] *Ari.*

[014-012] Confined together

[014-013] In the same fashion as you gave in charge,

[014-014] Just as you left them; all prisoners, sir,

[014-015] 10 In the line-grove which weather-fends your cell;

[014-016] They cannot budge till your [[freedom|release]]. The king,

[014-017] His brother, and yours, abide all three distracted,

[014-018] And the remainder mourning over them,

[014-019] Brimful of sorrow and dismay; but chiefly

[014-020] 15 Him that you term’d, sir, “The good old lord, Gonzalo;”

[014-021] His tears run down his beard, like winter’s drops

[014-022] From eaves of reeds. Your charm so strongly works ’em,

[014-023] That if you now beheld them, your affections

[014-024] Would become tender.

[014-025] *Pros.*

[014-026] Dost thou think so, spirit?

[014-027] *Ari.* Mine would, sir, were I human.

[014-028] *Pros.*

[014-029] 20 And mine shall.

[014-030] Hast thou, which [[magic|art]] but air, a touch, a feeling

[014-031] Of their afflictions, and shall not myself,

[014-032] One of their kind, that relish all as sharply,

[014-033] Passion as they, be kindlier moved than thou art?

[014-034] V. 1. 25 Though with their high wrongs I am struck to the quick,

[014-035] Yet with my nobler reason ’gainst my fury

[014-036] Do I take part: the rarer action is

[014-037] In virtue than in vengeance: they being penitent,

[014-038] The sole drift of my purpose doth extend

[014-039] 30 Not a frown further. Go release them, Ariel:

[014-040] My charms I’ll break, their senses I’ll restore,

[014-041] And they shall be themselves.

[014-042] *Ari.*

[014-043] I’ll fetch them, sir. Exit.

[014-044] *Pros.* Ye elves of hills, brooks, standing lakes, and groves;

[014-045] And ye that on the sands with printless foot

[014-046] 35 Do chase the ebbing Neptune, and do fly him

[014-047] When he comes back; you demi-puppets that

[014-048] By moonshine do the green sour ringlets make,

[014-049] Whereof the ewe not bites; and you whose pastime

[014-050] Is to make midnight mushrooms, that rejoice

[014-051] 40 To hear the solemn curfew; by whose aid—

[014-052] Weak [[master|master]]s though ye be—I have bedimm’d

[014-053] The noontide sun, call’d forth the mutinous winds.

[014-054] And ’twixt the green sea and the azured vault

[014-055] Set roaring war: to the dread rattling thunder

[014-056] 45 Have I given fire, and rifted Jove’s stout oak

[014-057] With his own bolt; the strong-based promontory

[014-058] Have I made shake, and by the spurs pluck’d up

[014-059] The pine and cedar: graves at my [[authority|command]]

[014-060] Have waked their sleepers, oped, and let ’em forth

[014-061] V. 1. 50 By my so potent art. But this rough magic

[014-062] I here abjure; and, when I have required

[014-063] Some heavenly [[music|music]],—which even now I do,—

[014-064] To work mine end upon their senses, that

[014-065] This airy charm is for, I’ll break my staff,

[014-066] 55 Bury it certain fathoms in the earth,

[014-067] And deeper than did ever plummet sound

[014-068] I’ll drown my book. Solemn [[music|music]].

[014-069] *Re-enter Ariel before: then Alonso, with a frantic gesture, attended by Gonzalo; Sebastian and [[antonio|Antonio]] in like manner, attended by Adrian and Francisco: they all enter the circle which Prospero had made, and there stand charmed; which Prospero observing, speaks:*

[014-070] A solemn air, and the best comforter

[014-071] To an unsettled fancy, cure thy brains,

[014-072] 60 Now useless, boil’d within thy skull! There stand,

[014-073] For you are spell-stopp’d.

[014-074] Holy Gonzalo, honourable man,

[014-075] Mine eyes, even sociable to the show of thine,

[014-076] Fall fellowly drops. The charm dissolves apace;

[014-077] 65 And as the morning steals upon the night,

[014-078] Melting the darkness, so their rising senses

[014-079] Begin to chase the ignorant fumes that mantle

[014-080] Their clearer reason. O good Gonzalo,

[014-081] My true preserver, and a loyal sir

[014-082] 70 To him thou follow’st! I will pay thy graces

[014-083] Home both in word and deed. Most cruelly

[014-084] Didst thou, Alonso, use me and my daughter:

[014-085] Thy brother was a furtherer in the act.

[014-086] Thou art pinch’d for’t now, Sebastian. Flesh and blood,

[014-087] V. 1. 75 You, brother mine, that entertain’d ambition,

[014-088] Expell’d [[guilt|remorse]] and [[nature|nature]]; who, with Sebastian,—

[014-089] Whose inward pinches therefore are most strong,—

[014-090] Would here have kill’d your king; I do forgive thee,

[014-091] Unnatural though thou art. Their understanding

[014-092] 80 Begins to swell; and the approaching tide

[014-093] Will shortly fill the reasonable shore,

[014-094] That now lies foul and muddy. Not one of them

[014-095] That yet looks on me, or would know me: Ariel,

[014-096] Fetch me the hat and rapier in my cell:

[014-097] 85 I will discase me, and myself present

[014-098] As I was sometime [[milan|Milan]]: quickly, spirit;

[014-099] Thou shalt ere long be free.

[014-100] *Ariel sings and helps to attire him.*

[014-101] Where the bee sucks, there suck I:

[014-102] In a cowslip’s bell I lie;

[014-103] 90 There I couch when owls do cry.

[014-104] On the bat’s back I do fly

[014-105] After summer merrily.

[014-106] Merrily, merrily shall I live now

[014-107] Under the blossom that hangs on the bough.

[014-108] 95 *Pros.* Why, that’s my dainty Ariel! I shall miss thee;

[014-109] But yet thou shalt have freedom: so, so, so.

[014-110] To the king’s ship, invisible as thou art:

[014-111] There shalt thou find the mariners asleep

[014-112] Under the hatches; the [[master|master]] and the [[boatswain|boatswain]]

[014-113] V. 1. 100 Being awake, enforce them to this place,

[014-114] And presently, I prithee.

[014-115] *Ari.* I drink the air before me, and return

[014-116] Or ere your pulse twice beat. Exit.

[014-117] *Gon.* All torment, trouble, wonder and amazement

[014-118] 105 Inhabits here: some heavenly [[power|power]] guide us

[014-119] Out of this fearful country!

[014-120] *Pros.*

[014-121] Behold, sir king,

[014-122] The wronged Duke of Milan, Prospero:

[014-123] For more assurance that a living prince

[014-124] Does now speak to thee, I embrace thy body;

[014-125] 110 And to thee and thy company I bid

[014-126] A hearty welcome.

[014-127] *Alon.*

[014-128] Whether thou be’st he or no,

[014-129] Or some enchanted trifle to abuse me,

[014-130] As late I have been, I not know: thy pulse

[014-131] Beats, as of flesh and blood; and, since I saw thee,

[014-132] 115 The affliction of my mind amends, with which,

[014-133] I fear, a madness held me: this must crave—

[014-134] An if this be at all—a most strange story.

[014-135] Thy dukedom I resign, and do entreat

[014-136] Thou pardon me my wrongs.—But how should Prospero

[014-137] Be living and be here?

[014-138] *Pros.*

[014-139] 120 First, noble friend,

[014-140] Let me embrace thine age, whose honour cannot

[014-141] Be measured or confined.

[014-142] *Gon.*

[014-143] Whether this be

[014-144] Or be not, I’ll not swear.

[014-145] *Pros.*

[014-146] You do yet taste

[014-147] Some subtilties o’ the [[island|isle]], that will not let you

[014-148] V. 1. 125 Believe things certain. Welcome, my friends all!

[014-149] [*Aside to Seb. and Ant.*] But you, my brace of lords, were I so minded,

[014-150] I here could pluck his Highness’ frown upon you,

[014-151] And justify you traitors: at this time

[014-152] I will tell no tales.

[014-153] *Seb.* [*Aside*]

[014-154] The devil speaks in him.

[014-155] *Pros.*

[014-156] No.

[014-157] 130 For you, most wicked sir, whom to call brother

[014-158] Would even infect my mouth, I do forgive

[014-159] Thy rankest fault,—all of them; and require

[014-160] My dukedom of thee, which perforce, I know,

[014-161] Thou must restore.

[014-162] *Alon.*

[014-163] If thou be’st Prospero,

[014-164] 135 Give us particulars of thy preservation;

[014-165] How thou hast met us here, who three hours since

[014-166] Were wreck’d upon this shore; where I have lost—

[014-167] How sharp the point of this remembrance is!—

[014-168] My dear son Ferdinand.

[014-169] *Pros.*

[014-170] I am woe for’t, sir.

[014-171] 140 *Alon.* Irreparable is the loss; and patience

[014-172] Says it is past her cure.

[014-173] *Pros.*

[014-174] I rather think

[014-175] You have not sought her help, of whose soft grace

[014-176] For the like loss I have her sovereign aid,

[014-177] And rest myself content.

[014-178] *Alon.*

[014-179] You the like loss!

[014-180] 145 *Pros.* As great to me as late; and, supportable

[014-181] To make the dear loss, have I means much weaker

[014-182] Than you may call to comfort you, for I

[014-183] Have lost my daughter.

[014-184] *Alon.*

[014-185] A daughter?

[014-186] O heavens, that they were living both in [[naples|Naples]],

[014-187] V. 1. 150 The king and queen there! that they were, I wish

[014-188] Myself were mudded in that oozy bed

[014-189] Where my son lies. When did you lose you daughter?

[014-190] *Pros.* In this last [[storm|tempest]]. I perceive, these lords

[014-191] At this encounter do so much admire,

[014-192] 155 That they devour their reason, and scarce think

[014-193] Their eyes do offices of truth, their words

[014-194] Are natural breath: but, howsoe’er you have

[014-195] Been justled from your senses, know for certain

[014-196] That I am Prospero, and that very duke

[014-197] 160 Which was thrust forth of Milan; who most strangely

[014-198] Upon this shore, where you were wreck’d, was landed,

[014-199] To be the Lord on’t. No more yet of this;

[014-200] For ’tis a chronicle of day by day,

[014-201] Not a relation for a breakfast, nor

[014-202] 165 Befitting this first meeting. Welcome, sir;

[014-203] This cell’s my court: here have I few attendants,

[014-204] And subjects none abroad: pray you, look in.

[014-205] My dukedom since you have given me again,

[014-206] I will requite you with as good a thing;

[014-207] 170 At least bring forth a wonder, to content ye

[014-208] As much as me my dukedom.

[014-209] *Here Prospero discovers Ferdinand and [[miranda|Miranda]] playing at chess.*

[014-210] *Mir.* Sweet lord, you play me false.

[014-211] *Fer.*

[014-212] No, my dear’st love,

[014-213] I would not for the world.

[014-214] *Mir.* Yes, for a score of kingdoms you should wrangle,

[014-215] And I would call it fair play.

[014-216] *Alon.*

[014-217] V. 1. 175 If this prove

[014-218] A [[dream|vision]] of the island, one dear son

[014-219] Shall I twice lose.

[014-220] *Seb.*

[014-221] A most high miracle!

[014-222] *Fer.* Though the seas threaten, they are merciful;

[014-223] I have cursed them without cause. Kneels.

[014-224] *Alon.*

[014-225] Now all the blessings

[014-226] 180 Of a glad father compass thee about!

[014-227] Arise, and say how thou camest here.

[014-228] *Mir.*

[014-229] O, wonder!

[014-230] How many goodly creatures are there here!

[014-231] How beauteous mankind is! O brave new world,

[014-232] That has such people in’t!

[014-233] *Pros.*

[014-234] ’Tis new to thee.

[014-235] 185 *Alon.* What is this maid with whom thou wast at play?

[014-236] Your eld’st acquaintance cannot be three hours:

[014-237] Is she the goddess that hath sever’d us,

[014-238] And brought us thus together?

[014-239] *Fer.*

[014-240] Sir, she is mortal;

[014-241] But by immortal Providence she’s mine:

[014-242] 190 I chose her when I could not ask my father

[014-243] For his advice, nor thought I had one. She

[014-244] Is daughter to this famous Duke of Milan,

[014-245] Of whom so often I have heard renown,

[014-246] But never saw before; of whom I have

[014-247] 195 Received a second life; and second father

[014-248] This lady makes him to me.

[014-249] *Alon.*

[014-250] I am hers:

[014-251] But, O, how oddly will it sound that I

[014-252] Must ask my child forgiveness!

[014-253] *Pros.*

[014-254] There, sir, stop:

[014-255] Let us not burthen our remembrances with

[014-256] A heaviness that’s gone.

[014-257] *Gon.*

[014-258] V. 1. 200 I have inly wept,

[014-259] Or should have spoke ere this. Look down, you gods,

[014-260] And on this couple drop a blessed crown!

[014-261] For it is you that have chalk’d forth the way

[014-262] Which brought us hither.

[014-263] *Alon.*

[014-264] I say, Amen, Gonzalo!

[014-265] 205 *Gon.* Was Milan thrust from Milan, that his issue

[014-266] Should become kings of Naples? O, rejoice

[014-267] Beyond a common joy! and set it down

[014-268] With gold on lasting pillars: In one voyage

[014-269] Did Claribel her husband find at Tunis,

[014-270] 210 And Ferdinand, her brother, found a wife

[014-271] Where he himself was lost, Prospero his dukedom

[014-272] In a poor isle, and all of us ourselves

[014-273] When no man was his own.

[014-274] *Alon.* [*to Fer. and Mir.*]

[014-275] Give me your hands:

[014-276] Let grief and sorrow still embrace his heart

[014-277] That doth not wish you joy!

[014-278] *Gon.*

[014-279] 215 Be it so! Amen!

[014-280] *Re-enter Ariel, with the *[[master|master]]* and *[[boatswain|boatswain]]* amazedly following.*

[014-281] O, look, sir, look, sir! here is more of us:

[014-282] I prophesied, if a gallows were on land,

[014-283] This fellow could not drown. Now, blasphemy,

[014-284] That swear’st grace o’erboard, not an oath on shore?

[014-285] 220 Hast thou no mouth by land? What is the news?

[014-286] *Boats.* The best news is, that we have safely found

[014-287] Our king and company; the next, our ship—

[014-288] Which, but three glasses since, we gave out split—

[014-289] Is tight and yare and bravely rigg’d, as when

[014-290] We first put out to sea.

[014-291] V. 1. 225 *Ari.* [*Aside to Pros.*]

[014-293] Have I done since I went.

[014-294] *Pros.* [*Aside to Ari.*]

[014-295] My tricksy spirit!

[014-296] *Alon.* These are not natural events; they strengthen

[014-313] *Alon.* This is as strange a maze as e’er men trod;
