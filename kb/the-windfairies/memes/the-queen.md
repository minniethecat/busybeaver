# The Queen

The Queen is the primary antagonist in the second half of "The Windfairies" (Chapter 1). Having recently married the King, she perceives Lucilla's extraordinary dancing — a gift from the Windfairies — as a threat to her status and schemes to destroy her rival. She represents the world of human courtly intrigue that stands in stark contrast to the innocent magic of the Windfairies.

## Role in the Story

The Queen first appears seated beside the King during the wedding celebrations, when Lucilla is brought to dance before the court. She is "a handsome woman, but she had a cruel look about her mouth" (001-036). When Lucilla's Windfairy-gifted dancing captivates the court, the Queen becomes consumed with jealousy. She repeatedly tries to frame Lucilla as a deceiver, pressuring the King to have her punished (001-045, 001-050). She goes so far as to disguise herself to spy on Lucilla and ultimately attempts to have her executed — making her one of the most morally dark characters in the collection.

## Character Traits

- **Jealous** — Her envy of Lucilla's dancing ability is immediate and consuming: "It made her angry that the King should admire the new dancer's dancing so much" (001-036)
- **Deceitful** — She hides her true feelings behind courtly politeness, "but she hid her rage" while plotting against Lucilla (001-055)
- **Manipulative** — She pressures the King, who is initially fair-minded, to act against Lucilla: "the Queen insisted that she was deceiving them" (001-050)
- **Vindictive** — Her jealousy escalates from suspicion to a determination to destroy Lucilla. She tells herself "That lying wench has been tricking me" and resolves to have Lucilla executed (001-041, 001-055)

## Key Actions

1. **The wedding observation** — At the wedding feast, the Queen watches Lucilla dance with growing resentment. She masks her anger behind a composed exterior but inwardly seethes (001-034, 001-036).

2. **Questioning Lucilla's honesty** — She dismisses the King's admiration, questioning how a "poor miller's daughter" could possibly possess such skill: "It was not possible that she could have learnt dancing from looking at windmill sails" (001-043, 001-044).

3. **Demanding punishment** — Despite Lucilla having done no wrong, the Queen pressures the King to have her imprisoned or worse (001-045, 001-050).

4. **The disguised visit** — The Queen dons a mask and visits Lucilla, hoping to catch her in a lie. Lucilla sees through the disguise: "when Lucilla looked through the mask she saw it was the Queen" (001-053).

5. **The final fury** — Her schemes exposed, the Queen becomes "furious" and resolves to destroy Lucilla by any means necessary, regardless of the truth (001-055).

## Relationships

- [[the-king]] — Her new husband, whom she manipulates with her charm and through whom she channels her jealous schemes
- [[lucilla]] — Her rival, the Windfairies' protegée whose dancing threatens the Queen's status at court
- [[windfairies]] — The supernatural beings whose gift to Lucilla is the indirect cause of the Queen's jealousy
