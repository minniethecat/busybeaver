## Description

The Miller's Sails were the four great canvas-covered arms of the [[the-windmill|windmill]] that stood on the downs by the sea. They turned continuously in the coastal wind, and their rhythmic motion became the foundation of [[lucillas-first-dance|Lucilla's]] extraordinary dancing ability.

The sails were not merely mechanical; they held a special attraction for the [[windfairies]], who would rest upon them and play among their folds. It was during one such visit that a windfairy first noticed the young Lucilla watching the sails.

> "See, here is a little human creature watching the sails go round!" cried one windfairy to another. [001-002]

## Role in the Story

The sails served as both Lucilla's teacher and the windfairies' perch:

> She rested on one of the great windmill sails, and, with the little girl watching, began to dance upon it as it turned. [001-008]

Lucilla learned to dance by observing the rhythmic turning of the sails. Throughout her life, she credited the windmill sails as her only instructor:

> "I learnt dancing from watching the windmill sails going round." [001-043]

> "I would dance now, and I watched the sails of my father's windmill, and I danced as they seemed to dance." [001-048]

## Symbolism

The sails represent the connection between the natural world (the wind) and human industry (the mill). They are the physical medium through which the [[windfairies]] first made contact with Lucilla, bridging the mortal and fairy realms. The circular, rhythmic motion of the sails mirrors the cyclical nature of Lucilla's own journey — from the mill, to the [[the-dukes-palace|Duke's court]], to imprisonment, and ultimately to freedom.
