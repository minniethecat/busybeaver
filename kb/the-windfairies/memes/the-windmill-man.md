## Overview

The Windmill Man is the miller who lived with his daughter Lucilla in a windmill on the downs by the sea. A poor working man, he raised his daughter alone after his wife died. His life changes dramatically when Lucilla's extraordinary dancing catches the attention of a royal court and ultimately the Queen's jealousy.

## Appearances

### The Windmill

The miller is introduced at the very beginning of the tale as Lucilla's sole parent:

> There was once a windmill which stood on the downs by the sea, far from any town or village, and in which the miller lived alone with his little daughter. His wife had died when the little girl was a baby. [001-001]

When Lucilla grows up, the miller sees her as a beautiful young woman:

> She grew up to be a beautiful young woman, and her father was very proud of her. [001-020]

As the miller grows old and can no longer work, Lucilla suggests they seek better fortune. She goes to dance before the court, hoping to earn a sack of gold to support her aging father:

> Her father was growing old and no longer cared to work [001-023]

When Lucilla is imprisoned by the jealous Queen, the miller is never seen abandoning his daughter — she remains loyal to her humble origins, eventually returning to her old home by the windmill.

## Role in the Tales

The Windmill Man represents:
- **Honest rural poverty** — a poor miller who works his whole life to raise his daughter
- **The aging parent** — his declining strength motivates Lucilla's journey to the court
- **Humble origins** — his windmill by the sea is the anchor Lucilla returns to after her trials

His windmill is the setting where Lucilla first learns to dance by watching the great sails turning [001-043].
