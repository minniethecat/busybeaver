# The Wood Snakes

The Wood Snakes are among the strange creatures that inhabit the dwarf musician's underground kingdom in "Dumb Othmar" (Chapter 7). Alongside toads and other reptiles, they emerge from the undergrowth to listen when the supernatural music begins.

## Nature

When Othmar and the dwarf enter the underground realm and the music starts, "strange wood-snakes and toads had come out boldly, and with them many other creeping things" (007-014). The snakes are not threatening — they are listeners, drawn by the same supernatural music that entrances Othmar. Their presence emphasizes that the underground kingdom is a complete ecosystem of enchanted creatures.

## Appearance

They are "strange" — their forms recognizable as snakes but their behaviour (gathering openly to listen to music) marks them as different from ordinary reptiles (007-014).

## Role in the Story

The wood snakes are part of the "weird crowd" that assembles in the underground kingdom. Their function is atmospheric: they establish the otherness of the realm, where even the reptiles gather to hear the dwarf's music.
