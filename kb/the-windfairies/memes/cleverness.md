# Cleverness

Mental quickness and strategic intelligence — a trait that cuts across moral categories, serving the faithful and the faithless alike, and judged not by its presence but by its purpose.

## Definition

Cleverness in these tales is morally neutral — it is a capacity, not a virtue or vice. Kesta's cleverness enables her scheme of escalating betrothals (002-004). The cat's cleverness defeats its greedy master (006-001). The gypsy's cleverness creates the magical cup. What distinguishes good cleverness from bad is its end: cleverness in service of justice or survival is admirable; cleverness in service of vanity or greed is corrupt. The tales thus offer a sophisticated view of intelligence — it is a tool whose value depends entirely on the hand that wields it (002-004).

## Manifestations

### In "Vain Kesta"

Kesta is the collection's most purely clever character — and her cleverness is entirely self-serving. She calculates each escalation of suitor with precision: Adam the farmhand, the miller, the bailiff, the banker (002-004) (002-005) (002-010) (002-015). Her cleverness is the engine of her ambition and the cause of her downfall. The tale does not condemn cleverness itself but its misdirection — Kesta is clever about the wrong things.

### In "The Story of a Cat"

The cat embodies cleverness in service of justice. It outwits its greedy, hard-hearted master through patience and strategy (006-001). The cat's cleverness is contrasted with the master's brutish greed — intelligence aligned with moral purpose triumphs over mere power (006-015).

### In "Dumb Othmar"

Cleverness appears through its apparent absence. Othmar cannot speak, and his muteness is mistaken for stupidity (007-076). The tale suggests that cleverness is not always visible — true intelligence may be hidden behind silence or disability.

## Associated Characters

- **Kesta** — Cleverness misdirected: her intelligence serves vanity and ambition (002-004)
- **The Cat** — Cleverness rightly directed: intelligence in service of justice against a cruel master (006-001)
- **Othmar** — Apparent absence of cleverness: his silence masks depth (007-076)

## Related Concepts

## Moral Dimension

Cleverness is the tales' most morally ambivalent trait. It is never condemned in itself, but its value is determined entirely by its ends. Kesta's cleverness is impressive but futile — she schemes her way into humiliation. The cat's cleverness is admirable and effective — it liberates the oppressed. The tales' implicit moral: be clever if you must, but be clever for good. Intelligence without moral purpose is merely sophisticated selfishness (002-004) (006-001).
