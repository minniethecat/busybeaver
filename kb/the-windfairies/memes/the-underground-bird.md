# The Underground Bird

The Underground Bird is one of the most haunting creatures in "Dumb Othmar" (Chapter 7), a bird that dwells in the dwarf's subterranean kingdom and remembers things that happened "thousands of years ago" (007-026). Its song carries the memory of all the human voices that have been lost to the dwarf's enchantment through the ages.

## Nature

The bird is ancient beyond reckoning. It tells the raven: "The dwarf came to my nest, and told me if I would go with him I should sing far better than the birds you hear now. That was thousands of years ago" (007-026). This makes the bird the oldest being in the collection — a creature who has been singing in the underground darkness since before recorded history. Its presence transforms the dwarf's curse from a single tragic event into an eternal cycle: Othmar is not the first musician to lose his voice underground, and he will not be the last.

## Appearance

The bird is not described in physical detail — what matters is its voice, which is preternaturally beautiful, and its ancientness. It sings alongside many other birds in the underground forest (007-025).

## Powers & Abilities

- **Eternal singing** — It has been singing for thousands of years without end
- **Ancient memory** — It remembers its origins before the dwarf took it underground, providing the only external testimony about the dwarf's methods
- **Witness** — It serves as silent witness to the underground kingdom's history, having seen countless human musicians come and go

## Role in the Story

The Underground Bird is the key witness to the dwarf's true nature. Its testimony — that it was once an ordinary bird lured underground by the same promise of "better singing" — reveals the pattern behind the dwarf's actions. Every beautiful voice the dwarf finds, he takes underground. Some he returns to the surface, mute. Some — like the bird — he keeps forever.
