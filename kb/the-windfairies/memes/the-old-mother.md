## Overview

The Old Mother is a minor character in "The Story of the Singing Mouse." She is the widowed mother of Othmar, the young man with the beautiful singing voice. She lives with her son in a village on top of a mountain and works for both of them while Othmar provides his singing to the village.

## Appearances

### The Story of the Singing Mouse

The Old Mother is introduced at the beginning of the tale. Othmar lives with his widowed mother, who supports the household through her work:

> He lived with his old mother, who was a widow, and he worked for them both, and always smiled. [007-001]

When Othmar loses his voice to the magical singing girl from the dwarf's spell, the Old Mother continues to care for him. After Othmar returns from his journey to the mountain land, he finds his way back to the village:

> The dwarf led him into the village where his old mother sat and waited for him [007-034]

Her steadfast presence represents the enduring familial bond that grounds Othmar even after his supernatural experiences.

## Role in the Tales

The Old Mother represents:
- **Maternal devotion** — a widowed mother who works tirelessly for her son
- **Domestic stability** — the home and hearth that Othmar returns to after magical trials
- **The quiet strength of village life** — in contrast to the magical interventions of the dwarf and singing girl
