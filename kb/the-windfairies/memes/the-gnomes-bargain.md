The Gnome's Bargain is the inciting event of "The Ploughman and the Gnome" (Story 9). A young ploughman, struggling to work his field, discovers a gnome living in the earth beneath his furrow. The gnome offers him a supernatural bargain: share the harvest, and receive prosperity beyond his imagining.

## Context

A young ploughman was following his plough in a field one morning when his horses suddenly stopped and would not move [009-001]. When he bent down to investigate, a deep voice from the earth commanded him to wait [009-002]. The ploughman discovered a gnome — a little black creature — who had been living beneath his field.

## The Event

The gnome emerged from the earth and proposed a bargain to the ploughman. "On the contrary," the gnome replied when the ploughman protested his poverty, "I shall give a very great deal" [009-014]. The gnome offered the ploughman prosperity in exchange for a share of what the field produced.

The ploughman, though wary, was intrigued by the offer. "Well," he said, "I don't mind trying. I think I have nothing to lose and something to gain" [009-015]. The exact terms of the bargain were deceptively simple: the gnome would take what grew below the ground, while the ploughman would keep what grew above. The ploughman, believing he had secured the better portion of the deal, agreed. He planted wheat, expecting to keep the grain while the gnome received only the worthless roots.

However, the gnome had tricked him: the bargain's terms meant that the gnome was entitled not merely to roots, but to the ploughman's growing prosperity — and eventually to increasingly precious things from the ploughman's life.

## Outcome

The bargain brought the ploughman immediate prosperity: his crops flourished, and his fortunes rose [009-032][009-033]. But the gnome's demands escalated over time. What began as a share of the harvest grew into claims on the ploughman's possessions, his money, and eventually his family [009-029][009-069]. The bargain, struck in a moment of hope, became an inescapable obligation that shadowed the ploughman's life.

## Significance

The Gnome's Bargain introduces the central theme of "The Ploughman and the Gnome": the danger of supernatural contracts made without full understanding. Like many fairy-tale bargains, the agreement appears generous on the surface but conceals escalating demands. The event establishes the pattern of initial gain followed by mounting cost that drives the entire story. The gnome represents the folkloric figure of the trickster who traps mortals through their own desires.
