# The Farm at Plötzin

The farm at Plötzin is the rural setting where much of the story "Vain Kesta" unfolds. It is a large working farm where Kesta is employed as a dairymaid and Adam as a farmhand. The farm serves as the starting point and final destination of Kesta's vain journey — she leaves it seeking a grander life, only to return to it in humiliation.

## Description

The farm is described as "a large farm" (002-001) where Kesta works milking cows and making cheese and butter. It is located in the countryside, some distance from the town where Kesta goes to sell her cheeses (002-005). A mill stands on the road between the farm and the town (002-007).

## Daily Life

At the farm, Kesta's work includes:

- Milking the cows (002-001)
- Making cheese and butter (002-001)
- Taking goods to the town market to sell for the farmer (002-005)

Adam's work includes:

- Driving in the cows for Kesta to milk (002-002)
- General farm labour

## Role in the Story

The farm at Plötzin is the place Kesta is trying to escape. When Adam proposes marriage with the dream of saving money to "buy a farm for ourselves" (002-003), Kesta is unsatisfied. She sets out for the town with cheeses to sell, and at every stop along the road — the mill, the bailiff's post, the bank, the Duke's garden — she claims she is "too good" for the men she left behind, including "the man at the farm" (002-033).

When her pretensions are exposed and she is chased from the town by pages, soldiers, clerks, and farm workers (002-070), Kesta flees back to the farm. Adam, having learned of her vanity, rejects her: "Indeed, Kesta, I cannot marry you now, since you've been to the town and tried to get a finer husband than me" (002-075). Kesta is left "sitting all alone" at the farm, having lost everything through her vanity.
