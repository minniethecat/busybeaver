# The Ploughman's Field

The ploughman's field is the rural setting where the central event of "The Gnome's Revenge" begins. It is here that a young ploughman, while following his plough one morning, discovers a small black woman — a gnome — in his field, setting in motion a chain of events that will transform his life.

## Description

The field is an ordinary working field, ploughed by a young man who follows his plough across it (009-001). It is located in a rural area where the ploughman lives alone before his marriage. The field's unremarkable appearance belies its significance as a threshold place — the boundary between the mortal world and the gnome's subterranean realm.

## The Encounter

One morning, as the ploughman was "following his plough in a field" (009-001), he discovered a small black woman. This was a gnome, who proposed a bargain: in exchange for a share of the harvest, she would bring him great fortune. When the ploughman expressed scepticism, the gnome replied, "I shall give a very great deal, and ask but a very little" (009-014).

## Role in the Story

The field is the scene of the original compact between the ploughman and the gnome. The bargain — a share of the harvest in exchange for prosperity — is struck in this field, and it is from this field that the ploughman's fortune begins to grow. However, the field also becomes a place of hidden obligation; the ploughman cannot forget what he owes, and when he later marries and breaks the gnome's house rules, the consequences of that field-side bargain come back to haunt him.

After the gnome's ultimatum and the loss of his wife's hair, the ploughman "hoped he should not hear of the gnome again" (009-070), but the field remains a silent witness to the unbreakable compact made upon its soil.
