## Overview

The Piper is a fairy creature who appears in "Nanina's Sheep" (Chapter 4). Each night at sunset, he emerged from the [[the-old-palace|old palace]] on the hill, followed by a flock of black goats. In one hand he held a shepherd's crook and in the other a [[the-magic-pipe|magic pipe]], whose music compelled all who heard it to dance uncontrollably.

## Appearance and Nature

The Piper appeared as a beautiful shepherd boy, crowned with jewels and dressed in scarlet and gold. His beauty was otherworldly, and his pipe spoke words through its music. He was a fairy being — the narrative reveals the old palace as an enchanted fairy palace, and the shepherd and his goats as supernatural entities.

## Powers

The Piper's primary power lay in his [[the-magic-pipe|magic pipe]]. Its music had an irresistible compulsive effect: anyone who heard it felt their feet begin to move of their own accord. [[nanina|Nanina]] found herself dancing against her will night after night, losing one of her sheep each time until none remained but what the [[the-beech-tree|Beech Tree]] helped her save.

The music could change in character — from merry dance tunes to long sad notes like a funeral march. On the seventh and final night, the Piper played a long low note that caused the entire fairy palace to collapse into a heap of stones, ending the enchantment.

## Role in the Story

The Piper served as both tempter and antagonist. His dancing led Nanina's sheep away one by one into the fairy palace, threatening her livelihood and her position with [[the-old-farmer|the Old Farmer]]. Yet his enchantment also revealed the magical world hidden within the ordinary landscape — the old ruin that was really a fairy palace, the ordinary hill that was a gateway to the supernatural.

## The Final Night

On the seventh night, the Piper appeared more beautiful than ever, wearing a crown set with jewels. The music was mournful rather than merry. When the final note sounded, the palace walls collapsed and the Piper disappeared forever, leaving Nanina with the two sheep she had saved through her painful resistance.
