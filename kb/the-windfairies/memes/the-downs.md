## Overview

The Downs are the coastal highlands by the sea that form the primary setting of "The Windfairies" (Chapter 1). A windswept, sparsely populated landscape far from any town or village, the Downs provided the isolated world in which [[lucilla|Lucilla]] grew up and where the [[windfairies|Windfairies]] came to dance at sunset.

## Geography and Character

The Downs stood by the sea, a stretch of elevated coastal grassland. The landscape was defined by three elements: the [[the-windmill|windmill]] where Lucilla lived with her father [[the-miller|the Miller]], the sea with its storms and shipwrecks, and the cottages of the fisher folk who lived on the beach below.

The narrative emphasizes the Downs' isolation — "scarce anybody came to the downs" — making it a place where Lucilla grew up largely unseen, her beauty known only to the wind and the sea. This solitude made the Downs a natural gateway for supernatural encounters: the windfairies could dance there unseen by the wider world.

## Role in the Story

The Downs serve as both home and stage. Lucilla spent her childhood running and dancing freely across them — a practice that, unbeknownst to her, attracted the [[windfairies|Windfairies]] who taught her their supernatural dancing. It was on the Downs that [[the-sailor|the Sailor]] first saw Lucilla and proposed marriage. It was also across the Downs that Lucilla's father heard the approach of his horse, signalling the end of each night's magical dancing.

In the story's closing, when Lucilla performed her dance of the windmill sails before the King's court, her memories reached back to "a windmill, on the downs far away" — the landscape of her girlhood, forever tied to the magic that shaped her.

## Inhabitants

- **[[lucilla|Lucilla]]** — The miller's daughter, who grew up on the Downs
- **[[the-miller|The Miller]]** — Lucilla's father, who lived in the windmill
- **Fisher folk** — Poor families living in cottages on the beach below the Downs
