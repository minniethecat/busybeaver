The Midnight Concert is the central supernatural event in "Dumb Othmar" (Story 7). At midnight, the dwarf's self-playing musical instruments transform into singing beings — men and women whose voices are impossibly beautiful. Their enchanted performance steals Othmar's singing voice, leaving him mute.

## Context

Earlier that day, the dwarf had arrived in the village with his donkey laden with musical instruments [007-002]. He demonstrated their self-playing power to the amazed villagers: when he whistled, the fiddles, viols, and pipes rose from the ground and made music on their own [007-013]. Othmar, known throughout the village for his beautiful singing voice, was drawn to the instruments and approached them [007-006].

## The Event

At midnight, the dwarf led Othmar out to where the instruments lay upon the ground [007-013]. As the dwarf whistled, the instruments rose into the air and began to transform. Each instrument became a person: the fiddles became singing girls, the viols became women with deep voices, and the pipes became piping men [007-013][007-023].

The transformed beings began to sing together, each voice perfectly matching the sound of its original instrument. The violin girls' voices were high and sweet, while the viol women sang with a deeper resonance [007-014]. The music was of a beauty that surpassed anything the village had ever heard.

"Now," said the dwarf to Othmar with a twisted smile, "you shall hear what real music sounds like" [007-013]. As the enchanted concert continued, Othmar's own voice was drawn into the music. When the singing stopped and the beings transformed back into instruments, Othmar found he could no longer sing [007-023].

## Outcome

When the music ceased and the instruments returned to their inanimate forms, Othmar discovered that his singing voice was gone [007-023]. The dwarf departed the next morning, calling back "Goodbye, Othmar" with an eerie finality [007-024]. The voice-theft was absolute and devastating: Othmar, once the pride of the village for his singing, was now mute. The event drove the remainder of the story — Othmar's silent suffering and Hulda's determination to find the dwarf and restore her beloved's voice.

## Significance

The Midnight Concert is the story's emotional and narrative center. It represents the dark side of supernatural music — beauty that comes at a terrible price. The dwarf's "twisted smile" as he promises to show Othmar "real music" reveals the predatory nature of the supernatural in this tale [007-013]. The event establishes the story's central conflict of loss and the need for restoration through love and courage.
