# The Shepherd's Wife

The shepherd's wife is a secondary but pivotal character in "The Rain Maiden." Her anger at the rain maiden's prophecy and her inability to accept the supernatural visitor's warning contribute directly to the story's tragic outcome.

## Family Life

The shepherd's wife lives with her husband in a cottage by the sea. After a year of marriage, she gives birth to "a tiny daughter, a beautiful little girl" (008-007) who grows up "in great happiness" (008-010). The mother loves her daughter tenderly, and as the girl grows, the shepherd's wife "would say to herself, 'She is much too good for any of the young men about here'" (008-010).

## The Rain Maiden's Visit

One stormy night, a tall woman in a grey cloak — the rain maiden — seeks shelter at the shepherd's cottage. The shepherd's wife takes her in, and the rain maiden, in gratitude, offers a prophecy about the daughter's future: she will marry a great king and live in a palace.

## Anger and Rejection

When the rain maiden reveals that the daughter, despite her royal marriage, will ultimately "disappear into the rain," the shepherd's wife reacts with fury. "On hearing this the shepherd's wife became very angry" (008-021). She refuses to accept that her daughter's happiness will be cut short — a rejection of the supernatural warning that, in the logic of fairy tales, ensures the prophecy will come to pass.

## The Wedding Tragedy

The shepherd's wife witnesses the king's grand wedding to her daughter (008-022) — the promised elevation of her family. But the prophecy fulfills itself: on the wedding night, catastrophic floods sweep through, and the bride disappears into the rain (008-025), leaving the shepherd's wife to mourn the daughter whose fate she could not prevent.
