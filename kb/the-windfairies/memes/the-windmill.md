## Overview

The windmill stands on the downs by the sea, far from any other houses. It is the childhood home of Lucilla, the protagonist of "The Windfairies," where she lives alone with her father after her mother's death.

## Setting

Situated on the downs by the sea, the windmill is a modest, isolated building. It is described as "a little windmill by the sea" [001-039], with great sails that turn in the wind blowing in from the ocean.

## In the Story

The windmill is the starting point and frame for Lucilla's story. She lives there with her father, the miller [001-035], and it is here that she discovers her gift for dancing:

> she had learnt dancing from watching the windmill sails going round [001-043]

Lucilla explains to the King and Queen that as a child she "watched the sails of my father's windmill, and I danced in time to the whirring and the buzzing" [001-048]. This connection between the natural rhythm of the windmill and her art becomes central to the tale.

When the windfairies visit, they rest "on one of the great windmill sails" [001-008], bringing their magical gift of dance.

## Significance

- Represents Lucilla's humble origins, contrasting with the royal court
- Serves as the physical origin of her dancing ability — the turning sails provided her only "teachers"
- Links the isolated, wind-swept downs to the magical windfairies who ride the same winds
