The Windfairies' Rescue is the climactic event in "The Windfairies" (Story 1), in which the supernatural windfairies intervene to save their protégée Lucilla from execution. After Lucilla survives drowning and fire, the King orders her beheading — but the windfairies summon a devastating storm that destroys the scaffold while leaving Lucilla unharmed.

## Context

Lucilla had been brought to the King's court after a courtier promised the King she could dance more beautifully than any performer at court [001-028]. When she refused to break the windfairies' gift — never to dance for any man but her husband — the King grew enraged and condemned her to death [001-046]. The execution was delayed when neither drowning nor fire could harm her, as the windfairies protected her [001-058][001-061]. The King then commanded that Lucilla should be beheaded on a scaffold erected in the marketplace [001-062].

## The Event

On the day appointed for the execution, a great crowd gathered in the marketplace around the scaffold [001-064]. The guards led Lucilla up onto the scaffold, and she began to fear that the windfairies had abandoned her at last [001-065]. But as the executioner raised his axe, there came a sudden roar of wind, and the axe was swept from his hands [001-066].

The wind grew into a tempest that drove through the marketplace with such violence that every structure around it collapsed. The palace itself was reduced to a mass of ruins [001-066]. Yet through it all, only Lucilla knelt upon the scaffold unhurt, protected by the windfairies who had summoned the storm in her defense [001-066].

## Outcome

The storm's destruction was total: the palace lay in ruins, and those who had condemned Lucilla were swept away [001-066]. The execution could not proceed. This event demonstrated that the windfairies' protection over Lucilla was absolute and that mortal authority could not overcome it. Following the rescue, Lucilla's status at court was transformed — she was no longer a condemned prisoner but a woman visibly favored by supernatural powers.

## Significance

The Windfairies' Rescue is the story's turning point. It marks the culmination of the windfairies' long promise to protect Lucilla, first made when they granted her the gift of dance [001-018]. The destruction of the palace and the King's power symbolizes the supremacy of the supernatural realm over mortal authority. The event also completes the pattern of three execution attempts — drowning, fire, and beheading — in which the windfairies' protection prevails each time.
