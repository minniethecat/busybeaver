# The King's Wedding Feast

The King's wedding feast is the event that brings Lucilla from a fisherman's poverty into the glittering world of the royal court — summoned to dance at the celebration, she achieves a triumph that also sets the stage for the Queen's jealous persecution.

## Context

Lucilla, living in poverty after her husband's departure on a long sea voyage, was discovered by a courtier who witnessed her dancing on the shore. The courtier, recognizing her extraordinary gift, summoned her to appear before the royal court. The timing was fortuitous: the King was about to be married, and festivities and rejoicing were planned for days (001-032).

## The Event

The Princess arrived, and the wedding was celebrated with much magnificence. After the ceremony came a feast, and in the evening there was to be singing and dancing and all sorts of play for the royal couple and the court to see (001-034). This was the event at which Lucilla was to perform.

The courtier who brought her wished her to be dressed in the most gorgeous dress with gold and jewels, but Lucilla pleaded to wear a light grey gown like the windfairies, because she remembered how they looked when they danced on the downs (001-035). This choice — humble grey rather than court gold — was her silent tribute to the beings she could never name.

When Lucilla danced, the court was spellbound. The King and the new Queen watched with wonder, and all the courtiers declared they had never seen such dancing. The King called her to him and asked where she came from and who had taught her such wonderful steps — the question Lucilla could never answer truthfully without breaking her covenant with the windfairies (001-035).

## Outcome

Lucilla's performance was a triumph. She was asked to dance again on subsequent evenings, and her fame grew. But the King's persistent questions about her teacher placed her in an impossible position — she dared not answer truthfully, and her evasions planted the seed of suspicion that the Queen would later exploit to accuse her of witchcraft.

## Significance

The King's wedding feast is the moment Lucilla's private gift becomes public destiny. Her choice to wear grey rather than gold — a small act of fidelity to the windfairies — encapsulates the entire tale's central tension: she carries a secret that both elevates and imperils her. The feast begins her rise to fame and simultaneously sets the trap that will lead to her trial.
