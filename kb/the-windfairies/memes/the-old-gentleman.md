# The Old Gentleman

The old gentleman is the central human character in "The Story of a Cat." He begins the story as a wealthy but cold-hearted man who refuses charity to a beggar on Christmas Day, and ends it transformed by the magical cat that comes into his life.

## Wealth and Miserliness

The story opens by establishing his character: "Once there lived an old gentleman who was a very rich old gentleman" (006-001). He lives alone in comfort, "well wrapped up in furs" against the Christmas snow (006-002), but his wealth has not made him generous.

When a poor beggar comes to his door on Christmas Day asking for "charity for God's sake," the old gentleman's response reveals his hardness of heart. He offers a single silver shilling but instructs the beggar to "go away" and declares, "I never give to beggars, and you must have done something very wicked to be so poor" (006-007). The beggar replies prophetically: "I will go. And I will also send you something that will make your heart softer. The heart that has no pity for others becomes very hard" (006-009).

## The Cat's Arrival

That same Christmas night, a large grey cat mysteriously appears at his house — the "something" the beggar promised. The cat is unusual: it sits at the table, rubs against him, and "looked at him as if she would speak" (006-016). The old gentleman, though initially dismissive, lets the cat stay.

## Transformation

Under the cat's influence, the old gentleman's character softens. The cat brings him luck and wealth, but more importantly, it teaches him compassion. By the story's end, he has become a charitable man who opens his home to the poor, fulfilling the beggar's prophecy that his hard heart would be softened.

In the sequel story, the old gentleman's transformation is complete: he has become a guardian to the widow and her children, his wealth now used for the benefit of others rather than hoarded for himself.
