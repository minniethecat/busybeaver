# The Old Palace

The Old Palace is the key setting of "Nanina's Sheep" (Chapter 3), a ruined palace on the other side of the hill where a mysterious light appears at night. It is the dwelling place of the Fairy Shepherd and the site of Nanina's seven-night ordeal.

## Description

The palace is "half in ruins" (004-003), a once-grand building now fallen into decay. Despite — or because of — its ruined state, it serves as a gathering place for fairy beings. At night, a light appears in one of its windows, visible from Nanina's shepherding grounds across the hill.

## Location

The palace sits "on the other side of the hill" from where Nanina keeps her sheep. The old farmer warns her explicitly to avoid it: "Mind you don't go near the old palace on the other side of the hill" (004-001).

## Events

1. **Nanina's curiosity** — Despite the old farmer's warning, Nanina drives her flock toward the palace, drawn by stories of a fairy she wants to see (004-002, 004-003).

2. **The fairy's court** — The Fairy Shepherd emerges from the palace with his black goats and his enchanted pipe, and the seven-night ordeal begins (004-004, 004-012).

3. **The final night** — After seven nights of torment, the palace witnesses the breaking of the enchantment. The shepherd and goats vanish "as if the earth had opened" (004-028).
