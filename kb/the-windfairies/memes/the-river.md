# The River

The River is a natural feature that appears in several tales of the Windfairies collection, most prominently in "The Rain Maiden" (Chapter 8), where it serves as the meeting place between the King's son and the mysterious maiden.

## Location

The river runs near the shepherd's cottage where [[the-rain-maiden-creature|the Rain Maiden]] lives:

> "A river ran near the cottage, and by the river the maiden sat every day." [008-009]

It is situated in a pastoral landscape, with the cottage on one side and fields and hills beyond.

## Role in "The Rain Maiden"

The river is the central meeting point in the romance between the King's son and the Rain Maiden. The shepherd's daughter goes down to the river daily:

> "… the shepherd's daughter went down to the river, as was her wont, and sat on the bank." [008-011]

The King's son, who is fishing on the river, sees her and is captivated:

> "… day after day he came down to the river to fish, and day after day he saw the maiden sitting on the bank." [008-015]

The river thus becomes the setting for the courtship that leads to their marriage. It is from his boat on the river that the King's son proposes:

> "… sitting in the boat on the river, and the maiden on the bank, the King's son told her of his love." [008-021]

## The River in Flood

The river also reflects the supernatural elements of the story. During the wedding of the King's son and the Rain Maiden, it swells dramatically:

> "… the river swelled and overflowed its banks." [008-022]

This flooding parallels the torrential rain that falls during the wedding — a manifestation of the Rain Maiden's elemental nature and her connection to water.
