# The Black Goats

The Black Goats are the supernatural flock of the Fairy Shepherd in "Nanina's Sheep" (Chapter 3). Unlike ordinary livestock, these goats are creatures of enchantment — they follow the piper's music wherever it leads, and their wild dancing is a spectacle both mesmerizing and terrifying.

## Nature

The black goats are under the fairy's control, compelled to follow and dance by the magic of his pipe. They appear suddenly with the shepherd and his music, and vanish just as suddenly "as if the earth had opened and swallowed them" (004-028). Their dancing is relentless — they caper alongside Nanina through seven nights of torment, neither tiring nor ceasing.

## Appearance

They are described as "black goats" (004-004) and "a flock of black goats" who follow the piper wherever he goes. The uniformity of their colour — all black — marks them as belonging to a different order than ordinary mixed livestock.

## Powers & Abilities

- **Compulsive dancing** — They cannot resist the piper's music, dancing "in time" to every note (004-012)
- **Sudden appearance and disappearance** — They emerge and vanish with the shepherd, as if they exist only while the music plays (004-028)

## Role in the Story

The goats are the visible evidence of the fairy shepherd's supernatural power. Their wild dancing creates the spectacle that traps Nanina — she cannot look away, and she cannot refuse to join. They function as a chorus of compulsion, the fairy's entourage of the enchanted.
