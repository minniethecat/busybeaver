# The Magic Pipe

The Magic Pipe is the Fairy Shepherd's instrument of power in "Nanina's Sheep" (Chapter 3). When he plays it, no living creature can resist its call — goats dance, sheep follow, and even a determined shepherdess finds her feet moving against her will.

## Description

The pipe produces music that is both beautiful and impossible to disobey. Its notes sound as words: "When the young birds sing" (004-005, 004-009, 004-013). It is described as "the wonderful pipe" (004-023), and its power is presented as an elemental force — like wind or water, something that cannot be reasoned with or refused.

## Properties

- **Compulsive dancing** — Any creature that hears the pipe must dance. Nanina, despite knowing she should run, cannot resist: "as I hear the pipe beginning, my feet begin to move" (004-021)
- **Animal control** — The pipe commands the black goats and was used to steal Nanina's entire flock (004-017)
- **Long-range effect** — The pipe's music can be heard from far away, drawing listeners toward its source

## Owner / Creator

- [[the-fairy-shepherd]] — The fairy musician who wields the pipe in his trial of Nanina

## Role in the Story

The pipe is the instrument of Nanina's trial. Its music is the mechanism of her suffering — seven nights of forced dancing — and also the measure of her perseverance. When she endures without breaking, the pipe's final note shatters the enchantment.
