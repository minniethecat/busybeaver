## Overview

The Old Farmer is a minor character in "Nanina's Sheep" (Chapter 4). He is the farmer who employed the young girl [[nanina|Nanina]] to keep his sheep. Though barely described in the narrative, his role as Nanina's employer sets the stage for her encounter with the [[the-piper|fairy shepherd]] and the [[the-old-palace|old palace]].

## Role in the Story

Nanina kept sheep for the old farmer, grazing them on the hillside near the old palace. When she began losing sheep to the enchanted dancing, the old farmer's reaction is not directly shown — the story focuses on Nanina's internal struggle with the magical compulsion. However, his position as her employer establishes the economic stakes: losing the sheep meant losing her livelihood.
