After losing her first lamb to the fairy's dance, Nanina wanders the hills in despair until a voice from an ancient beech tree teaches her how to resist the enchantment.

## Context

Nanina had lost her first lamb after dancing all night with the fairy shepherd. She feared she would lose all seven lambs, one each night, and spent the day wandering and crying in the hills [004-017]–[004-018].

## The Event

As Nanina wandered near a great beech tree on the hillside, she heard a voice speaking to her from within the tree. The tree, ancient and wise, told her the secret of resisting the fairy's enchantment: she must bury her feet in the earth like the tree's own roots, anchoring herself so she could not be compelled to dance [004-018]–[004-022].

The tree explained that the fairy magic could not pull her away if she made herself as firm and rooted as the earth itself. Nanina, desperate to recover her lambs, listened carefully and resolved to follow the tree's advice that very night [004-022].

## Participants

- [[nanina]] — the shepherdess, desperate for a way to resist the fairy's music and recover her lost lambs [004-018]
- [[the-beech-tree]] — an ancient, sentient beech tree whose voice offers Nanina the key to breaking the fairy's spell [004-018]

## Outcome

Nanina followed the beech tree's counsel. That night, she buried her feet deep in the earth among the tree's roots. Though the fairy shepherd's music still pulled at her, she could not be moved from her spot. She endured the painful pull of the enchantment but resisted dancing, and by morning she had recovered one of her lost lambs [004-023]–[004-025].

## Significance

The beech tree's advice is the turning point in Nanina's story. Where before she was a passive victim of the fairy's enchantment, the tree gives her agency — a painful but effective method of resistance. The tree represents the wisdom of the natural world, contrasting with the seductive danger of the fairy realm. Each night of resistance costs Nanina physical pain but restores something she has lost.

## Quotes

> "Bury your feet in the earth like my roots," said the voice from the beech tree. [004-022]
