# Obedience

The honoring of rightful authority through compliance with commands — a baseline virtue whose violation, even through innocent motives, brings consequences that must be worked through.

## Definition

Obedience in these tales is not blind submission. It is the recognition of legitimate authority and the honoring of explicit commands. Nanina's master tells her "whatever you do, don't go near the old palace" (004-001) — a clear command from a legitimate authority. Her disobedience, though driven by curiosity rather than rebellion, leads to real consequences: her entanglement with the fairy shepherd and her lameness (004-029). The tales distinguish between obedience to rightful authority (the master, the windfairies' conditions) and submission to illegitimate power (the King's demands). Obedience is virtuous when the authority is legitimate; resistance is virtuous when it is not (001-043).

## Manifestations

### In "Nanina's Sheep"

Obedience is the tale's explicit theme. Nanina "promised, and her master started" (004-001). Her subsequent disobedience — crossing to the fairy side of the hill — is motivated by innocent curiosity: "it won't hurt me just to look at it; I should like to see a fairy" (004-003). The tale treats her disobedience gently — she is not wicked, merely young and curious — but the consequences are real: she is lamed by dancing with the fairy shepherd (004-029). Obedience here functions as protective wisdom: the master's command was meant to keep her safe.

### In "The Windfairies"

Obedience to the windfairies' command of silence is the story's central test. Lucilla's obedience to their prohibition — "I will never tell anyone" (001-015) — is what the King's persecution tests. Her obedience to supernatural authority takes precedence over obedience to royal authority, establishing a hierarchy: sacred promises outrank secular power (001-043).

## Associated Characters

- **Nanina** — The obedient child who disobeys through curiosity, not malice; her story is obedience's gentlest treatment (004-001) (004-003)
- **Lucilla** — Obedience to sacred promise outweighing obedience to royal command (001-043)
- **The Master** — The voice of legitimate authority: his command is protective, not oppressive (004-001)

## Moral Dimension

Obedience is the tales' quietest virtue — less dramatic than faith-keeping, less visible than truth-telling, but foundational. The tales present a moral hierarchy: obedience to sacred promises > obedience to legitimate human authority > resistance to illegitimate power. Nanina's disobedience is treated with compassion — she is curious and young — but it is still disobedience, and it still has consequences. The moral is not "obey or be punished" but "commands from legitimate authority exist to protect you, and violating them carries natural consequences you may not foresee" (004-003) (004-029).
