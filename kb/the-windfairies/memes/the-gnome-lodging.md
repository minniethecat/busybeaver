# The Gnome's Lodging

The Gnome's Lodging is the central symbolic object of "The Ploughman and the Gnome" (Chapter 9) — not a physical thing but a binding supernatural agreement. The bargain the ploughman strikes with the gnome transforms his life, but its terms are absolute and its enforcement is ruthless.

## Description

The lodging agreement is simple in its terms but profound in its consequences: the gnome will lodge in the ploughman's house for a year and a day, and in exchange, the ploughman will become "the richest farmer in all that countryside" (009-014). The quid pro quo is explicit: "Remember, if you fail in your part, I shall fail in mine" (009-016).

## Properties

- **Wealth generation** — The bargain directly transforms the ploughman's economic fortunes
- **Conditional enforcement** — The gnome holds the ploughman to every letter of the agreement. When the ploughman's wife interferes, the gnome reminds him: "You gave her the money" (009-029)
- **Symbolic exchange** — The lodging is not a gift but a trade — supernatural favour for domestic space — and this reciprocity defines its power

## Owner / Creator

- [[the-gnome]] — The originator of the bargain and its enforcer
- [[the-ploughman]] — The human party who accepts the terms

## Role in the Story

The lodging agreement is the engine of "The Ploughman and the Gnome." Every event in the story is driven by the terms of this supernatural contract, and the story's moral turns on the ploughman's ability to honour it precisely.
