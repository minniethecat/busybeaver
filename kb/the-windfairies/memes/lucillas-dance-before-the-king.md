# Lucilla's Dance Before the King

Lucilla's dance before the King and his court is the pivotal event of "The Windfairies," the moment when the supernatural gift she received as a child is revealed to the wider world — with transformative and ultimately destructive consequences.

## Context

Lucilla has been brought by the Windfairies to a distant kingdom. The King is about to marry, and the wedding feast demands entertainment. The Windfairies have arranged for Lucilla to dance before the assembled court (001-029, 001-030, 001-031). Her dancing, taught by the Windfairies themselves, is unlike anything ever seen in that kingdom — "a new thing that had not been heard before" (001-035).

## The Event

On the night of the wedding, Lucilla wears a "light grey gown like the windfairies" (001-032). She throws open her window and dances. Her movements are so beautiful that the King sends for her and asks who she is (001-035). The new Queen, seated beside the King, watches with growing jealousy — "it made her angry that the King should admire the new dancer's dancing so much" (001-036). The Queen's envy sets in motion all the conflict that follows.

## Outcome

The dance brings Lucilla to the King's attention and earns his admiration. But it also earns her the Queen's undying hatred. The Queen immediately begins scheming to destroy Lucilla (001-041, 001-045), setting in motion a chain of events that culminates in Lucilla's arrest and the palace's destruction.
