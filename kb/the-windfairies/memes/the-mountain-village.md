# The Mountain Village

The Mountain Village is the setting of "Dumb Othmar" (Chapter 7), a remote community "on the top of a mountain" (007-001). It is a place where music is woven into daily life — the farmers sing in the fields, the birds sing in the woods, and Othmar's voice rises above them all.

## Description

The village sits high on a mountain, isolated from the wider world. Its people are farmers who "sowed their fields" and then "sent for Othmar" to sing for them (007-001). It is a community of labour and music, where supernatural visitors can arrive unannounced on the high road.

## Location

"On the top of a mountain" — the remoteness of the village is crucial. The dwarf musician comes "down the high road into the village" (007-002), suggesting the village is at a crossroads between the human world and the supernatural highlands.

## Events

1. **The musical gathering** — Village musicians gather to play; Othmar's performance moves the crowd to tears and laughter (007-003, 007-006).

2. **The dwarf's arrival** — A dwarf in a yellow cap leads a mule covered with fiddles into the village, changing everything (007-002).

3. **Othmar's return** — Othmar returns from the underground kingdom mute. Hulda sees him standing in the road (007-027, 007-030).
