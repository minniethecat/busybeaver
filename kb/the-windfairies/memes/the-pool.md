# The Pool

The Pool is a sentient body of water in "The Pool and the Tree" (Chapter 3), the companion and conversational partner of the Talking Tree. Together they form a philosophical dialogue about permanence and change — the tree rooted in one place, the pool forever reflecting the sky.

## Nature

The Pool is a small body of water nestled beneath the branches of the Talking Tree (003-001). Like the tree, it is fully conscious: it speaks, feels, fears, and loves. The pool's consciousness is tied to its physical form — when winter comes and the pool freezes over, it "remained hard and silent" (003-004), and when it is threatened with being drained, it cries out that it will die if separated from its setting (003-012).

## Appearance

The Pool is "little" — small enough to be sheltered by the tree's overhanging branches (003-001). Its surface reflects the sky, the moon, and the tree above it, and its beauty is such that "when the moon rose, the pool looked up and saw its beautiful reflection" (003-015).

## Powers & Abilities

- **Speech** — The pool converses freely with the tree, discussing the passage of time and the humans who come near. It asks anxious questions and offers observations (003-002, 003-008, 003-011)
- **Reflection** — Its surface serves as a mirror, and its ability to reflect things — the sky, the tree — is framed as a form of seeing and understanding
- **Sentience** — The pool's consciousness is not merely an anthropomorphic device; it genuinely experiences fear, love, and the dread of death (003-012)

## Role in the Story

The Pool serves as the more anxious, emotionally responsive foil to the Tree's ancient calm. While the Tree speaks of the permanence of cycles — "never mind, the spring will come again" (003-009) — the Pool worries about the lovers, the soldiers who might cut the tree down, and its own mortality. Their dialogue is the heart of "The Pool and the Tree," a meditation on love and impermanence unlike any other story in the collection.
