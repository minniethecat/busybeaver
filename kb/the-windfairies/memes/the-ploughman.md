# The Ploughman

The Ploughman is the protagonist of "The Ploughman and the Gnome" (Chapter 9), a poor young farmer who is following his plough in a field when he discovers a tiny gnome. His decision to welcome this strange being into his home leads to a bargain that changes his life.

## Role in the Story

The Ploughman is a hard-working but impoverished farmer with no wife and no luck. His encounter with the gnome — which begins with the tiny creature asking to be a lodger — tests his generosity and his willingness to trust the supernatural (009-005, 009-007). Through a sequence of tests and rewards, the ploughman's fortunes are reversed: from being a poor ploughman he becomes "the richest farmer in all that country" (009-014).

## Character Traits

- **Hard-working** — He is following his plough in the field when the story opens, performing honest labour (009-001)
- **Sceptical but open-minded** — When the gnome first speaks, he is astonished and laughs outright at the idea of a lodger, but he is willing to listen and consider the proposal (009-005, 009-009)
- **Honest** — He admits his circumstances plainly: "I have no wife, and I am only a poor ploughman" (009-007)
- **Bold enough to bargain** — He asks the gnome directly about terms, showing a practical mind even in the face of the supernatural (009-011, 009-013)

## Key Actions

1. **The discovery** — While ploughing his field, the ploughman finds a tiny figure — a gnome — beginning to stir on the palm of his hand (009-001, 009-003).

2. **The lodger's request** — The gnome asks to become a lodger in the ploughman's house. The ploughman is astonished and laughs at the idea at first (009-005, 009-009).

3. **The bargain** — After negotiating terms and hearing the gnome's promises, the ploughman agrees: "Well, I don't mind trying. I think I could put up with you for a year and a day" (009-011, 009-015).

4. **Transformation** — Through the gnome's supernatural assistance, the ploughman's fortunes change dramatically, and he rises from poverty to wealth (009-014, 009-017).
