## Description

The Duke's Horn was a hunting horn carried at the side of [[the-duke|the Duke]] who lived in a grand palace. It served as an instrument of authority and summons — whenever the Duke blew it, his four pages would come running in alarm.

The horn appears at a pivotal moment in the story of [[kestas-audience-with-the-duke|Kesta's encounter with the Duke]], when her deception is discovered.

> Then the Duke took from his side a horn and blew it loudly. [002-038]

## Role in the Story

The horn is sounded when the Duke learns that Kesta has been making cheeses — revealing her as a common farm worker rather than the noblewoman she pretended to be:

> "Good gracious, you made them!" cried the Duke with a scream. "Then you must be a common dairymaid, and your hands are quite rough. How terrible! And I was just going to marry you. How dare you think yourself good enough to marry me!" and he sprang to his feet in a towering passion, and seizing his horn blew it so loudly that the four pages ran up in great alarm. "Hunt her away," cried the Duke, "she is an impostor—a common farm wench and makes cheeses. She thought herself good enough to be the Duchess!" [002-047]

## Symbolism

The horn symbolises the Duke's absolute authority within his domain, but also his impulsive and tyrannical nature. It transforms instantly from an ornament at his side to an instrument of humiliation and expulsion. The horn's blast marks the moment of Kesta's social unmasking and the collapse of her ambitions.
