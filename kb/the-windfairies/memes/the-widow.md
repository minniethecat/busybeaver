# The Widow

The Widow is a supporting character in "The Story of a Cat" (Chapter 5), a poor widow who lives with her two children in desperate poverty. Her story arc in the tale illustrates the virtue of kindness to the helpless — both animal and human — and the supernatural rewards that follow.

## Role in the Story

The Widow appears in the aftermath of the potter's encounter with the mysterious girl. She is destitute, "all in rags" (005-087), with two children — a boy and a girl — and barely enough to survive. Her past kindness in taking a dying woman into her house becomes the key that unlocks the cat's magical assistance. The cat, bound by a promise to aid those who showed mercy to its former human form, takes the widow and her children under its protection and transforms their lives.

## Character Traits

- **Compassionate** — She once took a dying woman into her house, an act of charity performed without expectation of reward (005-094). This kindness reverberates through the entire tale
- **Patient** — Despite her poverty and hunger, she endures without complaint. Her children's welfare is her sole concern
- **Beloved** — Her daughter speaks of her with deep affection, and the cat's protection is extended because of the love and loyalty she inspired in the dying woman she sheltered

## Key Actions

1. **The act of mercy** — Before the story's events, the Widow took in and cared for a dying woman who had nowhere else to go. This act of compassion sets in motion the entire chain of supernatural rewards (005-094).

2. **Teaching her children** — She taught her daughter to be kind to animals: "That is what my mother told me to do, if I wanted to be lucky" (005-096). This lesson proves crucial to the family's survival.

3. **Enduring poverty** — Despite being in rags and barely surviving, she holds her family together. Her daughter describes their condition with dignity: "If my mother had a handful of straw she can make a bed" (005-087).
