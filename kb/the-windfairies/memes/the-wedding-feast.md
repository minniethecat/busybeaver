# The Wedding Feast

The Wedding Feast is the grand celebration that opens the second half of "The Windfairies," the occasion for which the King's bride arrives and Lucilla is brought to dance.

## Context

The King, ruler of a distant land, is about to marry. The Windfairies have brought Lucilla to this kingdom specifically to dance at this event (001-029, 001-030, 001-031). The feast is the culmination of the King's search for the most beautiful dancers in the world.

## The Event

The feast is celebrated "with much magnificence" (001-032). The King and his new Queen sit on thrones in the great hall. Musicians play. Dancers perform one by one. And then Lucilla — wearing her grey gown like the Windfairies — enters and dances, and everything changes. Her performance is so transcendent that the King forgets all other dancers, and the Queen begins to hate her.

## Participants

- [[the-king]] — The host, newly married, captivated by Lucilla's dancing
- [[the-queen]] — The bride, whose jealousy is born at this feast
- [[lucilla]] — The dancer whose Windfairy-gifted performance is the feast's defining moment
- [[windfairies]] — Makers of the gift that transforms a wedding feast into the opening act of a cosmic conflict

## Outcome

The feast sets in motion the Queen's campaign against Lucilla, which leads to the great storm and the palace's destruction. A celebration becomes the seed of catastrophe.
