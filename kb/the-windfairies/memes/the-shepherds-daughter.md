# The Shepherd's Daughter

The shepherd's daughter is the central human figure in "The Rain Maiden." Born to a humble shepherd and his wife, she is prophesied to marry a king — but the prophecy comes with a tragic condition: after her wedding, she will disappear into the rain.

## Birth and Childhood

The shepherd's daughter is born a year after the rain maiden's first visit to the shepherd's cottage. She is described as "a tiny daughter, a beautiful little girl" (008-007) who grows up in "great happiness" (008-010). Her mother believes she is "much too good for any of the young men about here" (008-010), a pride that foreshadows the royal match to come.

## The Suitors

As she comes of age, "great preparations" are made for her wedding (008-011). The king himself, having heard of her beauty, arrives to court her. He promises to "rejoice and love her as his daughter even though she be a beggar-maid" (008-016) — a promise that elevates her from shepherd's daughter to royal bride.

## The Wedding and Disappearance

The wedding is a grand affair, the king's wedding feast a celebration of the union between the highest and lowliest in the land. But the rain maiden's prophecy proves inescapable: on the wedding night, torrential rains fall, floods sweep through the celebrations, and the bride vanishes — "disappeared into the rain" — leaving behind only her wedding garments and a grieving king.

## Significance

The shepherd's daughter embodies the fairy tale theme of the mortal caught between two worlds — the humble world of her birth and the royal destiny the rain maiden foretells. Her disappearance into the rain suggests she has been claimed by the elemental forces that blessed and cursed her from the beginning, joining the rain maiden's realm rather than remaining in the human world.
