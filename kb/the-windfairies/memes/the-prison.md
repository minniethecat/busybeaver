## Description

The Prison was a dark confinement cell near the King's court where [[lucillas-trial|Lucilla]] was held after she refused to reveal the secret of her dancing. The jealous [[the-queen|Queen]] had convinced the King that Lucilla's dancing was witchcraft, leading to her imprisonment.

The cell became a place of testing — where Lucilla's faith in the [[windfairies]] was challenged, and where their loyalty to her was demonstrated.

> One evening she sat in her dark cell alone, grieving as usual, when the prison door opened, and there entered a woman wrapped in a cloak and with her face hidden by a mask. [001-053]

## Role in the Story

The prison serves as the setting for Lucilla's darkest moments and her ultimate vindication:

1. **The Queen's Visit**: The Queen visits Lucilla in her cell, masked, demanding to learn the secret of the dancing in exchange for freedom. Lucilla refuses to betray the windfairies.

2. **The Execution Attempts**: After the failed drowning, Lucilla is returned to the prison while the court decides on beheading:

> The King sent down to the prison and told her that if she would not speak, she must die. [001-052]

3. **The Final Night**: On the eve of her scheduled execution, Lucilla sleeps peacefully in her cell, hearing the wind begin to roar outside. The [[windfairies]] are gathering their strength for her final rescue:

> That evening Lucilla felt no fear, and she would have slept calmly in her cell, but the wind was beginning to blow in all directions, and all round she heard it roaring, and the trees were bending and breaking in the gale. [001-063]

## Symbolism

The prison represents the mortal world's attempt to contain what it cannot understand. Lucilla's imprisonment parallels the broader theme of innocence persecuted by jealousy and ignorance. Yet the dark cell also becomes a sanctuary — the windfairies' power penetrates even stone walls, proving that true freedom cannot be confined.
