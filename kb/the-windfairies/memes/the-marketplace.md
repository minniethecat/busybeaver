# The Marketplace

The Marketplace is a central town square that serves as the setting for the climactic scenes of "The Windfairies," the opening tale of the collection. It is where Lucilla, the miller's daughter, is brought to be publicly executed as a witch — and where the windfairies stage their dramatic, destructive rescue.

## Description

The Marketplace is described as an open public square in the King's town, large enough to accommodate a crowd of townspeople gathered for public spectacles. It features a scaffold for executions — an elevated wooden structure where the condemned are displayed:

> a scaffold should be erected in the marketplace, on which the block should be set [001-062]

The square is large enough to hold "a great pile of wood" for a burning at the stake [001-060], and to contain the King, Queen, soldiers, and assembled townspeople. In its final appearance, the windfairies' hurricane reduces it to ruins:

> the houses in the marketplace tottered and fell, and high up on the hill the palace was a mass of ruins [001-066]

## Location

The Marketplace is situated in the King's town, below the royal palace on the hill. It is the public heart of the urban center — accessible to both commoners and royalty, who "came down to the marketplace together" [001-061] for the execution. Its position between the palace above and the surrounding countryside makes it a threshold space where royal authority and public spectacle intersect.

## Events

Three key events unfold in the Marketplace during "The Windfairies":

1. **Lucilla's trial and condemnation**: After being accused of witchcraft by the jealous Queen, Lucilla is sentenced "to be burnt in the marketplace" [001-059]. She is "taken by the arms and led to the marketplace" [001-060] where a great pyre awaits.

2. **The failed execution**: The King and Queen "came down to the marketplace together, and saw Lucilla sitting on the scaffold with her hands tied" [001-061]. But when Lucilla dances, she cannot be condemned — her dancing is so beautiful that the crowd cannot bring themselves to execute her.

3. **The windfairies' rescue**: As a final test, Lucilla is brought back to the Marketplace to face the block. But the windfairies arrive: "when the gale they flocked to the marketplace, and crowded round the scaffold" [001-064]. The hurricane they unleash destroys the Marketplace and the palace, carrying Lucilla away to safety. After the storm passes, "the houses in the marketplace tottered and fell" [001-066], and the windfairies "all trooped up into the marketplace" [001-067] having completed their rescue.

## Symbolism

The Marketplace embodies the themes of public judgment and mob justice in the fairy tale. It is a space of exposure and vulnerability — where private accusations become public spectacle, and where the innocent (Lucilla) face the machinery of institutional power (the scaffold, the pyre, the King and Queen's authority).

The Marketplace's destruction by the windfairies is thematically significant: it represents nature's rejection of human cruelty. The wind, which normally turns the mill's sails peacefully, becomes a force that literally levels the structures of unjust authority. The Marketplace's fall mirrors the collapse of the false accusations against Lucilla — when the storm passes, the institutions that condemned her lie in ruins.
