# The Mule

The Mule is the dwarf musician's beast of burden in "Dumb Othmar" (Chapter 7), a pack animal "covered with fiddles" and other musical instruments (007-002). While not explicitly supernatural in its own right, the mule is the bearer of the dwarf's enchanted instruments and an integral part of the supernatural apparatus that ensnares Othmar.

## Nature

The Mule is a donkey or mule laden with musical instruments — fiddles, pipes, and other noise-makers — that the dwarf "unpacked" to test the villagers' musical talents (007-002). Its role is servile but symbolically important: it carries the instruments of enchantment from the spirit world into the human village.

## Appearance

The mule is "covered with fiddles" (007-002, 007-057), so thoroughly laden with instruments that it is defined by its burden. It is led by the dwarf "by the bridle" and follows quietly through the underground landscape.

## Role in the Story

The mule appears at the dwarf's first entrance into the village, and later when Othmar and the dwarf journey underground, the mule carries the instruments (007-011). At the story's climax, the instruments are "packed again upon the mule" and the dwarf departs — the mule's departure signals the end of the enchantment and Othmar's permanent loss (007-069).
