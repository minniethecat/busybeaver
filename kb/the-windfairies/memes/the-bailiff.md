# The Bailiff

The Bailiff is a supporting character in "Vain Kesta" (Chapter 2), the officer of the great Duke at the Palace. He encounters Kesta on the road and, like the Miller before him, attempts to win her favour. His defining trait is his personal hatred of the Miller — a vendetta that makes him a useful but untrustworthy figure in Kesta's journey.

## Role in the Story

The Bailiff rides up to Kesta on a white horse, clearly a man of some authority (002-010). He asks her why she is running, and when she tells him of the Miller's forwardness, he lights up with malicious delight — "the bailiff, who hated the miller" — and presses her for details to use against his rival (002-013). Like each suitor in the story, he sees Kesta as a prize to be won rather than a person to be respected. His pursuit escalates the pattern of temptation that defines Kesta's journey.

## Character Traits

- **Vengeful** — His personal hatred of the Miller colours all his actions. He actively seeks information to use against his enemy (002-013)
- **Petty** — His authority as the Duke's officer is wielded not for justice but for personal satisfaction. He "chuckles" at the thought of the Miller's humiliation (002-014)
- **Proud** — He rides a white horse and carries himself with the authority of the Duke's court, looking down on common farmers and millers
- **Predatory** — Like the other suitors, he views Kesta as an object to be acquired. His interest in her is inseparable from his rivalry with the Miller

## Key Actions

1. **The roadside encounter** — The Bailiff stops his horse upon seeing the fleeing Kesta and questions her about her distress (002-011).

2. **The interrogation** — Learning that the Miller has been pursuing Kesta, the Bailiff eagerly demands details: "Is this really so? Did the miller really want you for his wife?" (002-013). His glee is transparent.

3. **The rivalry** — The Bailiff positions himself against the Miller, effectively competing for Kesta as part of their ongoing feud rather than out of genuine interest in her (002-014).

4. **The pursuit** — Kesta eventually flees the Bailiff too, adding him to the list of predatory men she learns to distrust (002-033).
