# The Gnome's Discovery in the Field

The ploughman's discovery of a small black woman in his field is the inciting event of "The Ploughman and the Gnome" — a chance encounter that begins a supernatural bargain bringing prosperity, but at a cost that will grow increasingly unbearable.

## Context

The ploughman was a hardworking but poor farmer who tilled his fields alone. He had no family and little wealth, but he was cheerful and willing to try his luck. One day, while working his land, he came upon something extraordinary hidden in the soil.

## The Event

As the ploughman worked his field, he uncovered a small black woman — a gnome — living in the earth. She was tiny, dark-skinned, and spoke to him with authority. She proposed a bargain: if he would share a portion of everything that came into his household with her — the best bits of food, the finest cloth, a share of all good things — she would bring him wealth and prosperity beyond anything he had known.

"Well," said the ploughman, "I don't mind trying. I think it would rather amuse me to take you to my cottage; but if you don't keep your part of the bargain, and I don't find things are going very well with me, I warn you I'll pretty soon turn you out" (009-005).

"Agreed," said the gnome, "but remember, if you fail in your compact with me, I shall go by myself. Now carry me home and let me choose where I will live" (009-006).

The ploughman carried the gnome home, and she chose to live in a small hole in the cottage wall, from which she would emerge to claim her share.

## Outcome

The ploughman began setting aside the very best portions of his meals, the choicest bits of new cloth, and a share of whatever came into the house. Placed near the gnome's hole, the offerings would vanish when he looked away. In return, his farm prospered — his harvests grew abundant, his livestock multiplied, and his fortune rose steadily. But the gnome's demands would grow, and the compact he had struck so casually would prove to have hidden terms.

## Significance

This discovery establishes the central dynamic of the tale: a supernatural bargain that seems generous at first but contains an unspoken escalation clause. The gnome's condition — "if you fail in your compact with me, I shall go by myself" and the implicit threat that departure means taking all the wealth — creates a trap. Each gift the ploughman gives makes him more prosperous, which gives the gnome more to claim, tightening the spiral.
