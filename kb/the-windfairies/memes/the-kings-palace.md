# The King's Palace

The King's Palace is the primary setting of the second half of "The Windfairies," the royal seat to which Lucilla and the Sailor are brought. It is a place of spectacle and danger — a court where Lucilla's Windfairy-gifted dancing captivates a king and enrages a queen.

## Description

The palace is a "royal palace" situated "high up on the hill" (001-029, 001-066). It contains a great hall where musicians play and dancers perform, with the King and Queen seated on thrones at the centre. The palace is a space of political theatre: every performance is judged, every display of talent is scrutinized, and beauty can be a weapon as well as a gift.

## Location

The palace sits in a distant land to which Lucilla is brought by the Windfairies — "a country of which Lucilla had never even heard" (001-029). It is the seat of royal power, visible from afar and unreachable except by those who have been granted access.

## Events

1. **The wedding feast** — The King's wedding is celebrated at the palace, and Lucilla is brought to dance before the assembled court (001-030, 001-031, 001-032).

2. **Lucilla's performance** — Lucilla dances in a light grey gown like the Windfairies wear, and her performance is unlike anything the court has seen (001-032, 001-033).

3. **The Queen's scheming** — Within the palace walls, the Queen's jealousy festers. She pressures the King and plots to destroy Lucilla (001-036, 001-045, 001-050).

4. **Destruction** — At the story's climax, the palace is reduced to "a mass of ruins" (001-066) — the Windfairies' elemental power destroys the seat of human authority that tried to harm their protegée.
