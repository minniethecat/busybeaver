# The Miller

The Miller is a supporting character in "Vain Kesta" (Chapter 2), a man "all white with flour" who intercepts Kesta on her journey to town. He is one of several suitors who tempt her away from her faithful Adam, embodying the seductive but ultimately hollow promises of a life beyond the farm.

## Role in the Story

The Miller appears standing in the doorway of his mill as Kesta passes by. He immediately calls out to her and begins a campaign of flattery, telling her she seems "out of breath" and inviting her inside (002-005, 002-006). He represents the first of several escalating temptations Kesta encounters on her journey — each suitor offering a grander life that proves to be illusory. The Miller, like the Bailiff and the Banker who follow him, offers Kesta the promise of easy status at the cost of her integrity.

## Character Traits

- **Flattering** — He uses compliments to disarm Kesta: "Perhaps you are really a pretty girl" (002-008). His praise is calculated rather than sincere
- **False** — His promises are empty. He is more interested in conquest than in genuine relationship
- **Opportunistic** — He seizes the moment when Kesta appears vulnerable, pressing his advantage immediately (002-005, 002-006)
- **Symbolic** — Like other figures in Kesta's journey, he represents a specific temptation: the allure of settled comfort without the dignity of honest work

## Key Actions

1. **The doorstep encounter** — As Kesta passes the mill on her way to town, the Miller calls out and flatters her into stopping (002-005).

2. **The invitation** — He invites her inside his mill, playing on her exhaustion and vanity: "You seem to be out of breath, my girl" (002-006).

3. **The proposal** — Like the other suitors, he proposes marriage as a transaction: "It would be much better to marry the miller than to marry Adam" (002-010). He offers status, property, and escape from farm life — exactly what Kesta's vanity craves.

4. **Learning to reject temptation** — Kesta eventually passes the Miller by, learning through the arc of the story that his promises are as empty as those of the other town suitors (002-019).

## Relationships

- [[kesta]] — The object of his flattery and pursuit; he represents her temptation to abandon her humble life
- [[adam]] — The loyal farm labourer whom the Miller views as his lesser rival: "who is this Adam?" (002-010)
- [[the-bailiff]] — The next in the escalating hierarchy of Kesta's suitors; the Miller's social inferior
- [[the-farm-at-ploetzin]] — The place Kesta is trying to leave, contrasted with the Miller's mill
