# Dance

Dance is one of the most significant recurring motifs in the Windfairies collection. It functions simultaneously as a supernatural gift, a test of character, and a vehicle for personal transformation. From the wind-taught grace of Lucilla to the enchanted movements of Nanina, dance marks the boundary between the ordinary and the magical.

## Dance as Supernatural Gift

### Lucilla's Wind-Taught Dance

The [[windfairies]] themselves teach [[lucilla]] to dance, bestowing a grace that no mortal could achieve alone:

> "… they think we might teach her to dance, for she has not cried at all." [001-009]

> "Would you like us to teach you how to dance as we dance?" [001-010]

Lucilla's dancing becomes so extraordinary that it attracts the attention of a King:

> "… when she ran or danced it seemed as if she were as light as the wind itself." [001-020]

### The Enchanted Dance of Nanina

In "Nanina's Sheep" (Chapter 4), [[nanina]] is drawn into an enchanted dance by [[the-fairy-shepherd]] and his [[the-black-goats|black goats]]. Unlike Lucilla's gift, Nanina's dance is a form of compulsion — she dances for seven nights against her will, caught in the fairy's spell.

## Dance as Performance and Test

Lucilla's dancing is put to the ultimate test when she must perform before the King and his court:

> "… here the courtiers bid her dance on a platform at one end of the hall." [001-031]

Her performance becomes the means by which her fate is decided. The King is captivated:

> "She felt as light as the sea foam on the shore…" [001-034]

However, Lucilla's dance also carries risk: the windfairies have warned her never to reveal who taught her, on pain of losing the gift forever (001-016).

## Dance as Transformation

Across the tales, dance functions as a transformative act. For Lucilla, dance elevates her from a poor miller's daughter to a queen. For Nanina, the enchanted dance is both a trial and a gateway to the fairy realm. In both cases, to dance is to cross a threshold — from mortal existence into contact with the supernatural.
