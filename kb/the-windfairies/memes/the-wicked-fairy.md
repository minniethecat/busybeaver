# The Wicked Fairy

The Wicked Fairy is the shadow antagonist of "Dumb Othmar" (Chapter 7), a malevolent supernatural musician identified by Hulda as the true source of the curse that stole Othmar's voice. While the Dwarf Musician is the visible agent, the fairy is the power behind the enchantment.

## Nature

The Wicked Fairy is identified late in the story when Hulda declares: "The old man with the fiddles was a wicked fairy" (007-037). This identification reframes the entire tale: the dwarf was not acting alone but as an agent of a more powerful and explicitly malevolent being. The fairy's motivation is never clarified — it simply seems to be in the nature of this being to ensnare talented mortals and claim their gifts.

## Appearance

The fairy's appearance is conflated with that of the dwarf — it is described as "old" and carrying fiddles (007-037). Whether the fairy and the dwarf are two distinct beings or the dwarf is the fairy in a different form is left deliberately ambiguous by the text. The ambiguity is characteristic of the collection's treatment of the supernatural.

## Powers & Abilities

- **Voice theft** — The fairy's primary power: the ability to steal a human's voice, leaving them mute (007-037, 007-048)
- **Music as weapon** — The fiddles are instruments of enchantment, drawing in talented musicians like Othmar and binding them
- **Curse-casting** — The fairy's malice extends beyond simple theft: the muteness is described as a curse, suggesting lasting supernatural harm

## Role in the Story

The Wicked Fairy serves as the story's true antagonist, the hidden power behind the dwarf. Hulda's identification of the fairy's wickedness (007-037) is a turning point — it transforms the story from a tale of supernatural accident ("Othmar followed the wrong guide") to one of deliberate malice, and sets the stage for Hulda's quest to break the curse.

## Dwelling / Realm

Like the dwarf, the Wicked Fairy is associated with the underground realm. But as a fairy being, it may range more widely — the forest, the crossroads where musicians gather, and the underground kingdom all fall within its sphere of influence.
