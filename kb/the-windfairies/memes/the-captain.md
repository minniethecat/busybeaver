# The Captain

The Captain is a minor figure mentioned in passing in "The Windfairies," the opening tale of the collection. Though he never appears directly in the narrative, he represents the world of adventure, travel, and the sea that forms the backdrop to Lucilla's life at the windmill on the downs.

## Role in the Story

The Captain is referenced twice in connection with the ships that sail past the coast below Lucilla's windmill. In the first mention, he is part of the bustling maritime activity visible from the shore:

> the captain wanted men for a long journey [001-024]

This fragment, embedded in a description of the coastal village activity, positions the Captain as a figure of authority — one who commands ships and undertakes long voyages. It establishes the sea as a realm of masculine endeavor and adventure, contrasting with Lucilla's solitary life at the mill.

The second reference comes when Lucilla watches the ships from her high vantage point, imagining the distant places they travel to:

> how brave the captain must be, who knew so well how to guide his ship over the sea [001-064]

This passage frames the Captain through Lucilla's admiring, imaginative gaze — he is not just a sailor but a figure of bravery and skill, someone whose knowledge of the sea inspires wonder in a child who has never left the downs.

## Character Traits

- **Brave**: Described as knowing "how to guide his ship over the sea" [001-064], suggesting courage and competence
- **Authoritative**: He "wanted men" for his crew, implying the power to hire and command
- **Symbolic**: As a peripheral figure seen from a distance, the Captain represents the wider world that Lucilla can observe but not yet join

## Key Actions

The Captain's actions are entirely offstage — he exists only in description:

1. **Recruits crew**: He is said to have "wanted men for a long journey" [001-024], indicating preparation for a voyage
2. **Commands his ship**: Lucilla imagines him guiding his vessel over the sea with expert knowledge [001-064]

## Significance

The Captain is a liminal figure — he exists at the boundary between Lucilla's known world (the windmill on the downs) and the unknown world (the sea and distant lands). His brief, admiring descriptions serve the narrative function of establishing the sea as a place of adventure and possibility, heightening the sense that Lucilla's environment is rich with unseen dramas.

In the broader collection, the Captain also foreshadows the maritime settings that recur throughout *The Windfairies* — from the sea that the windfairies themselves shape ("we make all the little waves on the sea" [001-006]) to the coastal landscapes of multiple tales. He is a small but meaningful thread in the collection's tapestry of sea imagery.
