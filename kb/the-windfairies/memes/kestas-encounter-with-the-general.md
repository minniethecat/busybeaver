# Kesta's Encounter with the General

At the barracks square, Kesta encounters the General of the army and secures her fifth marriage proposal — the most socially elevated yet — by inflating her list of suitors to include every man she has met that day in "Vain Kesta" (Story 2).

## Context

After leaving the banker with yet another conditional acceptance, Kesta continued through the town. Each successful proposal had emboldened her ambition, and she now believed herself entitled to the highest match available in the Duke's realm (002-021).

## The Encounter

Kesta arrived at the great square before the barracks, where soldiers were drilling with "helmets and swords glittering in the sun" (002-021). At their head rode the General himself — his voice hoarse from shouting commands, yet "covered with gold" and looking "very grand" (002-021).

Assessing him as the finest catch yet, Kesta calculated coldly: "Now supposing he has no wife, it would be a really fine thing to marry him: I can but try" (002-021). She walked so close under his horse's feet as he rode away that he shouted at her in alarm. Seizing the opening, she cried out:

> "Alas! what a life is mine, hunted here and there till I don't know where I go!" (002-021)

When the General dismissed her complaint as "nonsense," Kesta grew indignant and reeled off her entire catalogue of supposed suitors:

> "First it was a man at the farm, then the miller, then the duke's bailiff, then the banker — they all wanted to marry me, and I am much too good for any of them!" (002-027)

When the General remarked that he did not think her "so pretty," Kesta retorted that "it's only stupid people who don't see it" and directed him to verify her claims with the men of the town (002-027).

## The Proposal

Remarkably, the General's scepticism crumbled before Kesta's sheer audacity:

> "If this is all true, of course you must be exceedingly pretty, and as you say you are much too good for them, perhaps you might suit me." (002-028)

Kesta accepted — but even with a General of the army, she would not commit immediately, promising to meet him "on my way back" as she still had "important business" further in the town (002-029). Her ambition was not yet satisfied: she had already resolved to try the Duke himself.

## Significance

This encounter represents the apex of Kesta's audacity in the town. She has escalated from farmhand to miller to bailiff to banker to General — a progression driven entirely by her vanity and the cumulative lie she has constructed. The General's proposal, won through effrontery rather than genuine attraction, sets the stage for her final and most disastrous gambit: approaching the Duke himself.
