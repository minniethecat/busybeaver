# Nanina's Seven Nights

Nanina's Seven Nights is the central ordeal of "Nanina's Sheep" (Chapter 3), a brutal trial in which the shepherdess must dance each night to the Fairy Shepherd's enchanted pipe in order to win back her stolen flock.

## Context

The Fairy Shepherd has stolen Nanina's sheep. When she finds him, he offers her terms: each night, if she refuses to dance, one sheep is returned. Each night, if she dances, she earns nothing — but endures (004-019, 004-020).

## The Event

Each night for seven consecutive nights, the pipe begins to play and Nanina cannot resist: "how can I refuse to dance?" (004-021). The black goats caper around her while her feet, already worn and bleeding, are forced to move. She cries out that she doesn't mind the pain — "only tell me, and I will do anything in the world" (004-019). On the seventh night, a comforting voice tells her: "tonight is the last" (004-025).

## Participants

- [[nanina]] — The shepherdess who endures the ordeal

## Outcome

Nanina's perseverance breaks the fairy's spell. Her sheep are returned. Her feet heal. The fairy shepherd is moved by her suffering and the enchantment dissolves.
