## Description

The Barracks stood on a great square in the town where [[vain-kesta|Kesta]] sought her fortune. It was the site where soldiers drilled daily with their helmets and swords, commanded by the General of the army.

> She went on till she came to a great square in front of the barracks where the soldiers were drilling, with their helmets and swords glittering in the sun, and at their head rode the General of the army. [002-021]

## Role in the Story

The Barracks is the third stop in Kesta's social-climbing journey through the town. After failing to secure [[the-bailiff|the Bailiff]] and being dismissed by [[the-banker|the Banker]], Kesta approaches the General outside the barracks. She employs the same sighing strategy:

> "Alas! what a life is mine," she cried very loud that he might hear, "hunted here and there till I don't know where I go!" [002-021]

The General initially responds with military gallantry, but later joins the crowd that pursues Kesta through the streets after her deceptions unravel. The soldiers from the barracks are among those who chase her:

> "Our General!" cried the soldiers angrily; "why, she's only a dairymaid!" [002-065]

## Significance

The Barracks represents military authority in the town's social hierarchy — one of the four institutions Kesta attempts to marry into (the law, banking, the military, and the nobility). Like the other institutions, it ultimately rejects her when her true social status is revealed.
