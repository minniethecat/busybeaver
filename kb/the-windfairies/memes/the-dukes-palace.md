## Description

The Duke's Palace was a grand residence in the town, home to [[the-duke|the Duke]] and his household. It stood as the pinnacle of social achievement in [[vain-kesta|Kesta's]] ambitious journey — the highest rung on the ladder she attempted to climb.

The palace was served by four pages who attended the Duke, and it was the site of Kesta's most dramatic confrontation with social reality.

## Role in the Story

After successfully manipulating the Bailiff, the Banker, and the General into believing she was a persecuted noblewoman, Kesta sets her sights on the Duke himself:

> "I should try higher, and see what the Duke himself would say? There is no harm in trying." [002-029]

At the palace, Kesta presents herself to the Duke, who — like the others — is taken in by her dramatic sighs:

> "Who are you?—don't you know I am the Duke?" said he. [002-030]

The Duke, charmed by what he believes is a high-born lady in distress, contemplates marriage. But the truth emerges when he asks about her accomplishments and she mentions making cheeses:

> "Good gracious, you made them!" cried the Duke with a scream. "Then you must be a common dairymaid." [002-047]

The Duke then blows [[the-dukes-horn|his horn]] to summon his pages and orders Kesta expelled from the palace — the place that represented her highest ambition becomes the scene of her most complete humiliation.

## Significance

The Palace functions as the ultimate symbol of the rigid class hierarchy that Kesta vainly attempts to transcend. Each room she passes through represents a social barrier she cannot truly cross.
