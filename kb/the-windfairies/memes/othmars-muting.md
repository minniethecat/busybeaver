# Othmar's Muting

Othmar's Muting is the central tragedy of "Dumb Othmar," the moment when a gifted young musician's voice is stolen by supernatural forces.

## Context

Othmar has accepted the dwarf's invitation to visit the underground kingdom (007-008, 007-011). Despite Hulda's warning, he has dismissed her and followed the supernatural musician into the darkness. In the underground realm, he hears music of unearthly beauty — and at the climactic moment, when he opens his mouth to sing, something goes terribly wrong.

## The Event

At a critical moment, Othmar cries out — "Othmar who sang like the birds" — and the dwarf "starts up" (007-022). The spell is broken. The instruments are packed onto the mule. Othmar is returned to the surface. When he appears again in the village, Hulda sees him standing in the road, shading his eyes (007-027). She runs to him, sees his tears, and "knew that Othmar was dumb" (007-031). He who had the sweetest voice in the village can no longer speak a single word.

## Outcome

Othmar lives the rest of his life mute. Only Hulda suspects the truth: "it was the dwarf" (007-034). His silence becomes the burden that drives her quest to the wise old man and, ultimately, to the raven that leads her toward a potential cure.
