# The Potter

The Potter is the initial protagonist of "The Story of a Cat" (Chapter 5), a young craftsman who makes his living by making pots and dishes. His encounter with a mysterious girl who appears in his workshop one night changes his understanding of what truly matters.

## Role in the Story

The Potter lives in a village and supports himself by his craft. One night, a strange and beautiful child appears in his workshop and asks him to make her a set of miniature pots and dishes (005-001, 005-006). Though initially impatient and sceptical — "What do you mean by making such silly little things?" (005-005) — he is gradually won over by the girl's skill and otherworldly wisdom. His encounter with her marks the beginning of the story's central mystery and leads to the introduction of the cat.

## Character Traits

- **Diligent** — He loves his craft and takes pride in his work, explaining he works carefully so "I may do it well, for I live by my work" (005-003)
- **Impatient** — He initially reacts with irritation to the girl's unusual requests, calling them "nonsense" (005-007)
- **Capable of wonder** — Despite his pragmatism, he is moved by the girl's supernatural skill and sits "staring in silence" at what she creates (005-011, 005-013)
- **Receptive to change** — His encounter with the mysterious girl opens his mind to possibilities beyond his everyday craft

## Key Actions

1. **The visitor** — While working at his trade, the potter is visited by a strange, beautiful girl who sits down before him and asks him to make miniature pots and dishes (005-001, 005-006).

2. **Reluctant compliance** — Though he finds the request absurd, he agrees to let her use his wheel. To his amazement, her hands work "more quickly and skilfully than the potter could have done" (005-007, 005-010).

3. **The awakening** — Watching the girl work with supernatural dexterity, the potter is struck with awe. He "stares after her" as she departs in the moonlight (005-011, 005-013, 005-014).
