Kesta the dairymaid encounters a wealthy miller on the road to town, sparking her ambitions beyond the farm. This is the first rung on Kesta's ladder of vanity — the moment her ambition shifts from simple farm life toward progressively higher social stations.

## Context

Kesta was a young dairymaid working at a large farm. Adam, a farm worker, had just proposed marriage to her, but Kesta was not ready to say yes and wanted to consider her options. She set off on a journey into town to sell cheeses, with Adam's proposal unresolved [002-001]–[002-004].

## The Event

### The Meeting

On her way to town, Kesta passed a mill where the miller, covered in white flour, stood at his door. Seeing Kesta out of breath from running, the miller asked if someone was chasing her. Kesta seized the opportunity to present herself as a desirable woman pursued by admirers: she replied that men were always running after her because of her beauty [002-005]–[002-007]. The miller examined her carefully and remarked that she was indeed pretty.

### The Proposal

The miller, who was a widower, saw an opportunity. He suggested that instead of remaining a dairymaid — laboring on the farm and making cheese — she might marry him and become a miller's wife, a position of considerably more comfort and respectability [002-008]–[002-009].

### Kesta's Calculation

Kesta did not give an immediate answer. She thought to herself: "It would be much better to marry the miller than to marry Adam." But her ambition now ignited, she reasoned further: "who knows if I may not do better than either, so I must not be in any hurry" [002-010]. She told the miller she had business in town and suggested they arrange things when she returned [002-014].

## Participants

- [[kesta]] — the dairymaid, flattered by the miller's attention and beginning to weigh her marriage options as commodities [002-005]
- [[the-miller]] — a widowed miller, prosperous but covered in flour, who proposes marriage to Kesta on first sight [002-006]

## Outcome

Kesta continued on her way to town without accepting the miller's proposal. She later referenced the miller as one of her many suitors when approaching the bailiff, the banker, the General, and finally the Duke — each time listing him as proof of her desirability [002-012][002-033]. This non-committal pattern became her signature tactic: using each man's interest to attract the next, higher-status one.

## Significance

This encounter marks the first rung of Kesta's ladder of vanity. Having already dismissed Adam's proposal as beneath her, the miller's interest reinforces her belief that her beauty entitles her to a husband of higher station. Critically, Kesta learns her central maneuver here: she does not refuse the miller outright but defers her answer, using the possibility of his proposal as leverage with subsequent suitors. The miller thus becomes the template for her pattern of serial non-commitment — a strategy that works brilliantly until it catastrophically fails with the Duke.
