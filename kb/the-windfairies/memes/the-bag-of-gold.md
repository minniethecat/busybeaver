# The Bag of Gold

The Bag of Gold is the Sailor's bargaining tool in "The Windfairies," the wealth he offers to the supernatural beings who have claimed his wife. It represents the limits of mortal wealth against fairy power.

## Description

The Sailor "came with a sack of gold" (001-028) when he attempts to ransom Lucilla back from the Windfairies. Gold — the currency of the human world — is what he knows, and it is what he offers the beings of wind and sky.

## Properties

- **Mortal currency** — Gold represents everything the Sailor has earned and owns. It is his highest earthly value
- **Insufficient** — The Windfairies do not want gold. They want Lucilla. The bag's failure to secure her release demonstrates the incompatibility of fairy values with human wealth

## Owner / Creator

- [[the-sailor]] — Brings the gold as ransom for his wife

## Role in the Story

The bag of gold embodies the story's theme about the insufficiency of material wealth in the face of supernatural claims. The Sailor's gold cannot buy Lucilla's freedom — only the Windfairies' own inscrutable purposes can determine her fate.
