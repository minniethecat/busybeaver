# The Green Snake

The Green Snake is a creature of the underground realm in "Dumb Othmar" (Chapter 7), a "bright green snake" whose sudden appearance and swift movement through the forest marks a turning point in Hulda's quest to find the witch who can help Othmar.

## Nature

The snake appears "twisted up and apparently asleep" (007-065) before suddenly darting into action. It is found alongside a "long worm" that the raven has discovered. When the raven croaks, the snake wakes and darts "through the short grass of the field" (007-066), guiding Hulda to the next stage of her journey.

## Appearance

The snake is "bright green," "lithe," and "long" — a striking creature that stands out against the forest floor (007-065, 007-066). Its colour associates it with life, growth, and the supernatural — unlike the more mundane brown or grey snakes of the ordinary world.

## Role in the Story

The Green Snake functions as a guide, its sudden movement directing Hulda's attention and her path. In the underground realm where every creature seems to be part of the dwarf's design, the snake's appearance may be deliberate — another servant of the supernatural powers that are testing Hulda and Othmar.
