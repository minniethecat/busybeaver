## Overview

Yakov Buk was a Jewish trader, about twenty-four years old, who became [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka Bochkareva]]'s common-law husband after she fled her first marriage to [[afanasy-bochkarev|Afanasy Bochkarev]]. Buk represented, briefly, an escape from [[afanasy-bochkarev|Afanasy]]'s brutality. But his criminal conviction for butchering stolen cattle and subsequent exile to [[siberia|Siberia]] drew [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] into a new form of hardship. His own abuse of her completed the pattern that would ultimately drive her to enlist in the army — the only escape that proved permanent.

## Role in Narrative

### A New Beginning

Yakov Buk entered [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]]'s life when she was desperate to escape [[afanasy-bochkarev|Afanasy]]:

> "The man who came to my rescue, when I was about twenty-four, was Yakov Buk. He was a man of education, handsome, and of a passionate nature." — PN 004-029

Buk was a butcher by trade, and [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] helped him in his business. The relationship offered temporary respite:

> "For a time I was happy. Buk treated me with kindness." — PN 004-035

### The Buk-Bochkareva Name

The couple became known in the community by a compound name that marked [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]]'s ambiguous status — neither fully Buk's wife nor fully separate:

> "There goes Buk-Bochkareva!" people would point at me, with a mixture of scorn and pity and respect. — PN 004-043

When questioned about her name, [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] explained:

> "'My husband, your Excellency, Yasha Buk,' I replied." — PN 004-058

### Exile

Buk's criminal activities caught up with him. He was convicted of butchering stolen cattle and sentenced to exile in [[siberia|Siberia]]. [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] faced a choice — return to [[afanasy-bochkarev|Afanasy]], or follow Buk into exile:

> "Buk was sentenced to exile in Siberia. I determined to follow him." — PN 005-010

In [[siberia|Siberia]], Buk's treatment of [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] deteriorated. He drank, neglected her, and the relationship that had been her escape became another cage. It was during this [[siberia|Siberia]]n exile that [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] resolved on her most radical escape yet — not to another man, but to the army.

### Significance

Buk appears only briefly in [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]]'s memoir, but his role in her life story is structurally important. He represents the middle term in her progression: from [[afanasy-bochkarev|Afanasy]]'s brute violence, through Buk's more sophisticated but ultimately similar selfishness, to the army — the first institution that, however harsh, gave her dignity rather than degradation.
