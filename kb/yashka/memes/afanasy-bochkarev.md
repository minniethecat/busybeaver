## Overview

Afanasy Bochkarev was the first husband of Maria "[[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]]" Bochkareva. Their marriage, arranged when [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] was fifteen, became the crucible of her early suffering. Afanasy's brutality, drunkenness, and intellectual dullness are recorded in the memoir's early chapters with a visceral clarity that explains — and, in [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]]'s telling, justifies — her eventual flight from domestic life into the masculine world of soldiering. He is not a political figure or a soldier. He is simply the first of the men who tried to break her.

## Role in Narrative

### Courtship and Marriage

Afanasy entered [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]]'s life when she was a girl in the Novgorod countryside:

> "His name was Afanasy Bochkarev. He began to court me." — PN 003-023

The marriage took place when [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] was fifteen. Almost immediately, Afanasy revealed himself:

> "Afanasy's low intelligence was a suffering to me, and his brutality soon manifested itself. He drank heavily and beat me." — PN 003-029

### Escape and Return

[[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] fled to her mother, but Afanasy pursued her:

> "Afanasy came to my mother after my disappearance. He said he would not give me up." — PN 003-044

At her mother's house, [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]]'s blood froze when she recognized his voice:

> "In a moment I recognized it as that of Afanasy. My blood froze and my flesh crept." — PN 003-045

She hid while he searched for her, and eventually faced him down:

> "Afanasy was waiting in the anteroom, and I walked past him without a word. He followed me home." — PN 003-050

### Brief Reform, Then Worse

For a short time after she returned, Afanasy reformed:

> "For a while Afanasy was truly a different man. In the presence of others he was gentle and attentive." — PN 003-052

But the reform was temporary:

> "Gradually life with Afanasy grew as insufferable as it had been before. The beatings resumed." — PN 003-053

### Attempted Murder

In a moment of desperation, [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] resolved to kill him:

> "'Give me an axe. I am going to kill Afanasy.'" — PN 003-062

When he returned drunk, she attacked him:

> "Afanasy returned, drunk, and began to abuse me. I seized the axe and struck him." — PN 003-064

He survived, stunned:

> "Afanasy was stupefied. He had not expected this." — PN 003-066

She told the authorities everything:

> "I told them everything. Afanasy was taken to the police-station." — PN 003-068

### Death in Battle

Years later, during the war, [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] received news of his fate:

> "Somebody informed me of the death in battle of Afanasy Bochkarev. Of course, it was a lie, but I was not sorry to hear it." — PN 020-025

Even in her final reflections, Afanasy remained a dark presence:

> "I had escaped the cold, deep waters from the ugly Afanasy." — PN 022-154
