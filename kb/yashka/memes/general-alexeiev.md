## Overview

General Mikhail Vasilyevich Alexeiev was one of the most respected staff officers in the [[imperial-russian-army|Imperial Russian Army]]. He served as Chief of Staff to Tsar Nicholas II and later as the principal military advisor to the Provisional Government. A man of immense organizational talent and quiet patriotism, Alexeiev became, alongside [[lavr-kornilov|General Kornilov]], the co-founder of the [[white-volunteer-army|White Volunteer Army]] in the Don region — the first organized military resistance to Bolshevik rule. In [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka Bochkareva]]'s memoir, Alexeiev appears only briefly, but always in contexts that underscore his integrity and his opposition to the destruction of the army.

## Biography

Born in 1857 to a modest military family in the Tver region, Alexeiev rose through merit rather than connections. He graduated from the General Staff Academy and served with distinction in the Russo-Turkish War of 1877–78 and the [[russo-japanese-war|Russo-Japanese War]]. At the outbreak of [[world-war-i|World War I]], he served as Chief of Staff of the Southwestern Front before being appointed Chief of Staff to the Supreme Commander, Grand Duke Nicholas Nikolaevich.

When Tsar Nicholas II assumed personal command in 1915, Alexeiev became the de facto operational commander of the Russian Army. He remained Chief of Staff under the Provisional Government and was briefly considered for Commander-in-Chief before [[general-brusilov|Brusilov]]'s appointment. After the Bolshevik takeover, Alexeiev escaped to the Don region and, with Kornilov, organized the Volunteer Army. He died of heart failure in October 1918.

## Role in Narrative

Alexeiev appears in three brief but significant moments in [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]]'s memoir, each reflecting his reputation as a man caught between duty and despair.

During the [[alexander-kerensky|Kerensky]]-Kornilov confrontation, [[alexander-kerensky|Kerensky]] threatened to replace Kornilov:

> "'I will appoint Alexeiev in your place, and use force if necessary,' Kerensky exclaimed." — PN 017-104

The threat of replacing Kornilov with Alexeiev was an acknowledgement of Alexeiev's stature — he was the one general whose authority might command respect even in a disintegrating army. [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] later learned:

> "I also learned that General Alexeiev had more than once been offered the supreme command, but had declined." — PN 017-107

During the final weeks of the Provisional Government, Alexeiev was still attempting to hold the military together:

> "Kerensky replied with a message through Alexeiev, requesting a written certification." — PN 018-099

Though Alexeiev never appears in person in [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]]'s narrative, his name is invoked as a symbol of the old army's competence and honor — a counterpoint to the political chaos engulfing Russia.

## Key Relationships

- **[[lavr-kornilov]]** — Co-founder of the [[white-volunteer-army|White Volunteer Army]]; Alexeiev and Kornilov worked together to organize resistance to the [[bolshevik-party|Bolsheviks]] in the Don region.
- **[[alexander-kerensky]]** — [[alexander-kerensky|Kerensky]] respected Alexeiev's military judgment and considered appointing him Commander-in-Chief in place of Kornilov.
- **[[maria-bochkareva]]** — Though they never met directly in the memoir, [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]]'s journey to Kornilov's lines in the Don region brought her into the orbit of the White Army that Alexeiev helped create.
