## Overview

Captain Dementiev was a Russian army officer who served as adjutant to War Minister [[alexander-kerensky|Alexander Kerensky]] and as Commandant of the [[home-for-invalids|Home for Invalids]] in [[petrograd|Petrograd]]. Though a minor figure in the broader sweep of the Russian Revolution, Dementiev played an important supporting role in [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka Bochkareva]]'s memoir as her guide, facilitator, and intermediary with the political and military elite. His practical assistance and personal kindness helped the rough-hewn peasant soldier navigate the intimidating world of [[petrograd|Petrograd]] politics.

## Role in Narrative

Captain Dementiev enters [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]]'s story through [[michael-rodzianko|Michael Rodzianko]], who introduced him as a practical aide:

> "Rodzianko introduced me to Captain Dementiev, Commandant of the Home for Invalids." — PN 014-034

### Guide to Power

Dementiev's first and most important task was escorting [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] to Army Headquarters at Mogilev to meet [[general-brusilov|General Brusilov]]:

> "Captain Dementiev and I went to obtain an audience with the Commander-in-Chief." — PN 014-036

He discussed the project with [[general-brusilov|Brusilov]] and approved of her idea:

> "Captain Dementiev discussed the project from all points of view and approved of my idea." — PN 014-038

Dementiev drove [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] to the [[winter-palace|Winter Palace]] for her first crucial meeting with [[alexander-kerensky|Kerensky]]:

> "Captain Dementiev drove me to the Winter Palace." — PN 014-040

### Personal Support

Beyond his official duties, Dementiev provided personal support. When [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] was overwhelmed before her speech at the Marinsky Theatre:

> "I was nervous all day. Captain Dementiev suggested that I should commit my thoughts to writing, and helped me." — PN 014-051

He and his wife escorted her into the theatre:

> "I was taken to the Marinsky Theatre and escorted by Captain Dementiev and his wife into the former Imperial box." — PN 014-053

### At the Conference

Dementiev was present at the planning meeting with [[general-polovtzev|General Polovtzev]]:

> "A conference was held immediately between Captain Dementiev and General Polovtzev, who made the following suggestion." — PN 014-044

And he attended the larger organizational meeting at the [[home-for-invalids|Home for Invalids]]:

> "Present at the meeting were General Kuzmin, assistant to General Polovtzev, Captain Dementiev and General Anosov." — PN 014-058

He was also among the small group that accompanied [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] when she reorganized the battalion:

> "I was informed that General Polovtzev, the adjutant of Kerensky, Captain Dementiev, and several of the women who had been most active in the battalion, were waiting for me." — PN 015-064

## Key Relationships

- **[[maria-bochkareva]]** — Dementiev was [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]]'s first guide through the corridors of power in [[petrograd|Petrograd]], providing practical assistance and moral support.
- **[[alexander-kerensky]]** — As [[alexander-kerensky|Kerensky]]'s adjutant, Dementiev served as the bridge between the War Minister and [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]].
- **[[michael-rodzianko]]** — [[michael-rodzianko|Rodzianko]] introduced Dementiev to [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]], recognizing that she needed a capable military aide to assist her.
- **[[general-polovtzev]]** — Dementiev worked closely with [[general-polovtzev|Polovtzev]] on the logistics and administration of the Women's Battalion.
- **[[general-brusilov]]** — Dementiev accompanied [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] to her critical meeting with the Commander-in-Chief at Mogilev.
