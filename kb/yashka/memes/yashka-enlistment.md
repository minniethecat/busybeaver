## Overview

Yashka's Enlistment by the Tsar is an event documented in [[maria-bochkareva|Maria Bochkareva]]'s memoir *[[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]]* (1919), providing a firsthand account of the Russian Revolution and civil war from the perspective of a woman soldier.

## Course of Events

[[maria-bochkareva|Yashka Bochkareva]] recorded this event in her memoir, providing unique eyewitness testimony from the perspective of a frontline soldier and commander of the [[womens-battalion-of-death|Women's Battalion of Death]]. Her account captures the chaos, violence, and human drama of revolutionary Russia.

## Yashka's Perspective

As a participant and witness, [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] recorded her direct experience. Her memoir preserves details that official histories often omit — the reactions of ordinary soldiers, the texture of daily life amid catastrophe, and the moral judgments of a peasant woman thrust into world-historical events.
