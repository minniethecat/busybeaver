## Overview

General Alexander Ivanovich Verkhovsky served as the last Minister of War of the [[provisional-government|Russian Provisional Government]], appointed in September 1917 as the government's authority crumbled. A relatively young officer who had risen rapidly, Verkhovsky inherited an impossible situation — an army in terminal disintegration, a government with no real power, and a Bolshevik insurrection looming. In [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka Bochkareva]]'s memoir, Verkhovsky appears in the final days before the [[october-revolution|October Revolution]], when she made one last desperate attempt to persuade the government to act.

## Role in Narrative

Verkhovsky appears in [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]]'s memoir at a moment of maximum crisis. Earlier, as Commander of the [[moscow|Moscow]] Military District, he had intervened to save her from a lynch mob:

> "Verkhovsky arrived not an instant too soon. The mob was howling for my blood." — PN 017-084

But his most significant appearance comes later, after he succeeded [[alexander-kerensky|Kerensky]] as War Minister. [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]], desperate to save the situation, went to the War Ministry:

> "Desperate, I went to the Ministry. 'I am going now to Verkhovsky, the new War Minister. Would you like to come with me?'" — PN 018-129

On the way, she and her companion discussed the appointment:

> "On the way we discussed Verkhovsky's appointment. He was the same Verkhovsky who had saved me from the mob in Moscow." — PN 018-130

At the Ministry, they found the old and new War Ministers together — a symbol of the government's paralysis:

> "At the War Ministry, we found that Kerensky was in Verkhovsky's study." — PN 018-132

It was in Verkhovsky's office that [[alexander-kerensky|Kerensky]] seized his head and moaned "Everything is lost." The last War Minister of democratic Russia could do nothing.

[[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]]'s final reflection on the period records her last impression of both men:

> "Such was the impression made upon me by Kerensky and Verkhovsky two days before the Bolshevik uprising." — PN 020-010

## Key Relationships

- **[[alexander-kerensky]]** — Verkhovsky succeeded [[alexander-kerensky|Kerensky]] as War Minister in the government's final weeks.
- **[[maria-bochkareva]]** — [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]]'s last appeal to the collapsing government was made in Verkhovsky's office.
