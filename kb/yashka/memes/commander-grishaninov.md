## Overview

Commander Grishaninov was a colonel in the [[imperial-russian-army|Imperial Russian Army]] who served as [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka Bochkareva]]'s company commander at the front. Unlike many officers who struggled to accept a woman in the ranks, Grishaninov treated [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] with professional respect, defending her status against skeptical superiors and valuing her as a soldier. His death in battle was a personal loss for [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]], recorded with genuine grief in her memoir.

## Role in Narrative

### First Meeting

Grishaninov entered [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]]'s story when he took command of her company:

> "Our new Company Commander, Grishaninov, was a short, jolly fellow, with an infectious laugh." — PN 009-002

He immediately treated her as a soldier, not a curiosity:

> "'Bochkareva,' said Commander Grishaninov, when I entered his car, 'would you like to go on a reconnoitering expedition?'" — PN 009-004

### Defender of Her Rights

When a higher-ranking officer questioned [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]]'s presence in the trenches, Grishaninov defended her:

> "The Commander immediately sent for Colonel Grishaninov, demanding an explanation. But Grishaninov stood by me." — PN 009-008

When the officer ordered [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] removed from the front, Grishaninov invoked the Tsar's authority:

> "'Your Excellency!' I exclaimed to Colonel Grishaninov, 'I was enlisted by the grace of the Tsar as a regular soldier. You cannot send me to the rear!'" — PN 009-011

The confrontation ended with Grishaninov's quiet triumph:

> "There followed a heated discussion between him and Commander Grishaninov, which ended in an order for me to remain." — PN 009-016

### Death in Battle

Grishaninov was killed at the front, a loss [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] records with uncharacteristic tenderness:

> "At that moment our beloved Commander, Grishaninov, was struck to the ground. He fell without a cry, his jolly face still wearing something like a smile." — PN 010-075
