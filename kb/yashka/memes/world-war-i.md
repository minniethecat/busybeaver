## Overview

World War I (1914-1918) was the defining catastrophe of early twentieth-century Europe. On the Eastern Front, the Russian Empire mobilized millions of soldiers against the German and Austro-Hungarian empires. The war's immense casualties, the collapse of logistics, and the incompetence of the Tsarist high command eroded faith in the monarchy and fueled the revolutions of 1917. For [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka Bochkareva]], the war was personal — it was where she fled from domestic abuse, found purpose as a soldier, and witnessed the destruction of everything she had fought for.

## Background

The war began in August 1914 after the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand triggered a chain of alliances that drew Europe's great powers into conflict. Russia, allied with France and Britain, mobilized against [[germany-wwi|Germany]] and Austria-Hungary. Initial Russian victories in Galicia gave way to catastrophic defeats in 1915, including the loss of Poland. By 1916, the front had stabilized, but at immense cost.

## Yashka's Perspective

For [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]], the war was escape and transformation. Fleeing an abusive husband and [[siberia|Siberia]]n exile, she petitioned the Tsar for permission to enlist:

> "I was searched and taken to the Tsar. He listened to me, and he was very gentle. Then he smiled and issued the order that I be enlisted as a regular soldier." — PN 008-024

She served in the trenches with the [[fifth-regiment|5th Regiment]], was wounded multiple times, and rose to command respect from officers and men alike. Her war experience gave her the moral authority that later made the Women's Battalion possible.

Yet the war also destroyed what it created. The losses — over two million Russian dead — broke the army's spirit:

> "At that moment our beloved Commander, Grishaninov, was struck to the ground. He fell without a cry." — PN 010-075

The war that liberated [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] from domestic bondage ultimately consumed the army and the country she loved.

## Aftermath

Russia's withdrawal from the war via the Brest-Litovsk Treaty in March 1918 freed [[germany-wwi|Germany]] to concentrate on the Western Front. The war ended in November 1918 with the Allied victory. But for Russia, the war's end was only the beginning of civil war.
