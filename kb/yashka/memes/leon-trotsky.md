## Overview

Leon Trotsky (born Lev Davidovich Bronstein) was a Marxist revolutionary, theorist, and [[soviet|Soviet]] statesman who, alongside [[vladimir-lenin|Vladimir Lenin]], led the [[october-revolution|Bolshevik seizure of power]] in October 1917. A formidable orator and organizer, Trotsky served as Chairman of the [[petrograd-soviet|Petrograd Soviet]], Commissar for Foreign Affairs, and the founder of the Red Army. In [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka Bochkareva]]'s memoir, Trotsky appears as [[vladimir-lenin|Lenin]]'s intellectual partner and co-interrogator — a figure whose theoretical commitment to revolution blinded him to its human cost. [[afanasy-bochkarev|Bochkarev]]a's personal account of facing both [[vladimir-lenin|Lenin]] and Trotsky provides one of the memoir's most memorable passages.

## Biography

Trotsky was born in 1879 to a prosperous Jewish farming family in what is now Ukraine. He became involved in revolutionary politics as a student and was first arrested in 1898. After escaping [[siberia|Siberia]]n exile, he joined [[vladimir-lenin|Lenin]] in London and became a leading figure in the Russian Social Democratic Labour Party. During the 1905 Revolution, he served briefly as Chairman of the [[petrograd|St. Petersburg]] [[soviet|Soviet]].

Trotsky spent the [[world-war-i|First World War]] years in exile, returning to Russia in May 1917 — shortly after [[vladimir-lenin|Lenin]]. He joined the [[bolshevik-party|Bolshevik Party]] and became one of its most effective spokesmen:

> "They are a group led by one Lenin, who has just returned from abroad by way of Germany, and Trotsky, Kollontai and other political exiles who have been permitted to come back." — PN 014-015

As Commissar for Military and Naval Affairs, Trotsky built the Red Army from scratch. After [[vladimir-lenin|Lenin]]'s death in 1924, he lost a power struggle with Stalin, was expelled from the Communist Party, exiled, and eventually assassinated in Mexico in 1940.

## Role in Narrative

Trotsky appears in [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]]'s memoir in two contexts: as a distant propagandist whose name becomes a rallying cry for revolutionary soldiers, and as a direct personal antagonist during her arrest and interrogation.

### The Face-to-Face Encounter

[[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] was brought before [[vladimir-lenin|Lenin]] and Trotsky after being arrested in revolutionary [[petrograd|Petrograd]]. The encounter captures her unique position — a peasant soldier confronting the intellectual architects of revolution:

> "I was soon led into a room where Lenin and Trotsky sat at a table. They both rose as I entered." — PN 021-022

When [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]] spoke from the heart about Russia, patriotism, and the army, she was met not with argument but with condescending laughter:

> "Lenin and Trotsky laughed. I could not understand what was so amusing. I had spoken from my heart, simply and with passion for my country." — PN 021-032

The laughter is the memoir's most damning detail — the intellectual revolutionaries mocking the emotional patriotism of a soldier.

### Trotsky as Symbol

Throughout the memoir, Trotsky's name is paired with [[vladimir-lenin|Lenin]]'s as a shorthand for the Bolshevik regime. Soldiers chant his name in triumph, and later, ordinary Russians curse both men in defeat:

> "'We are going home! Hurrah for Lenin! Hurrah for Trotsky! Hurrah for Kollontai!'" — PN 020-034

> "'They wanted an end to the war, but Lenin has got us into a worse position than when we were fighting the Germans.' And Trotsky is no better." — PN 024-080

[[afanasy-bochkarev|Bochkarev]]a's verdict is unambiguous — the revolutionaries who promised peace delivered only a new and crueler war:

> "I would, myself, shoot Lenin and Trotsky for this outrageous betrayal of Russia!" — PN 024-015

### Trotsky and the Women's Battalion

Trotsky's ideological hostility to the Women's Battalion is implicit. His rhetoric about class war and international revolution had no room for [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]]'s simple patriotism or her project of shaming men back to the front. The battalion was, in Bolshevik eyes, a bourgeois and [[counterrevolution|counter-revolution]]ary project — an extension of [[alexander-kerensky|Kerensky]]'s discredited government.

## Key Relationships

- **[[vladimir-lenin]]** — Trotsky's closest political collaborator; together they led the [[october-revolution|Bolshevik Revolution]] and jointly interrogated [[maria-bochkareva|Yashka]].
- **[[maria-bochkareva]]** — The memoir's author; Trotsky interrogated her personally and responded to her patriotic appeals with dismissive laughter.
- **[[alexander-kerensky]]** — The leader whose government Trotsky helped overthrow in October 1917.
- **[[alexandra-kollontai]]** — Fellow Bolshevik who returned from exile alongside Trotsky and [[vladimir-lenin|Lenin]] to agitate against the war.
- **[[lavr-kornilov]]** — The general whom Trotsky's Bolshevik forces later fought in the Russian Civil War.
