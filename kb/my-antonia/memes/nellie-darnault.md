## Overview

Nellie d'Arnault — called "Miss Nellie" in the text — is the young daughter of the white d'Arnault family of the Far South, on whose plantation the blind enslaved boy who became the celebrated pianist [[Samson d'Arnault|Blind d'Arnault]] was born. (026-012) Every morning she practised the piano in the south wing of the family's Big House, and it was her daily lessons that gave the gifted blind child his first, secret schooling in music. (026-012)

## In the Text

Nellie appears within the embedded story of how Blind d'Arnault discovered his gift — a story told when the pianist visits [[Black Hawk]], a man [[Mrs. Harling]] "had known for years." (026-002)

As a small blind boy on the d'Arnault plantation, Samson would feel his way through the lilacs and along the boxwood hedge up to the south wing of the Big House, "where Miss Nellie d'Arnault practised the piano every morning." (026-012) One day he stood there listening "while Miss Nellie was playing her lesson to her music-teacher." (026-013) When the room fell quiet, he crept to the instrument and "began to finger out passages from things Miss Nellie had been practising, passages that were already his." (026-015)

The boy's secret was discovered when "the door opened; Miss Nellie and her music-master stood behind it," watching the blind child reproduce her lesson by ear. (026-016) Sensing nothing, Samson played on until a wrong note made him pause — and in that moment "Miss Nellie spoke softly," revealing her presence. (026-016)

## Significance

Though she appears only within the story of d'Arnault's origins, Nellie is the unwitting medium through which his extraordinary talent first found expression: her morning practice at the Big House became the blind boy's hidden conservatory. (026-012) (026-015) Her family's plantation lay in the Far South, "where the spirit if not the fact of slavery persisted" — a setting of casual privilege against which Samson's smothered genius quietly asserts itself. (026-012)
