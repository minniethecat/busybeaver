## Overview

Mrs. Gardener is the proprietor of the Boys' Home hotel in Black Hawk, Nebraska, and one of the town's most prominent businesswomen. Known as the best-dressed woman in Black Hawk, she manages the hotel and its staff of hired girls, including [[Tiny Soderball]]. Her periodic trips to Omaha for shopping and cultural events mark her as a figure of sophistication and ambition on the prairie.

## Key Details

Mrs. Gardener's first name is Molly. (026-028) She runs the Boys' Home hotel, where she keeps a watchful eye on her waitresses, maintaining order among the young hired girls who work for her. (023-028) She is acknowledged as the best-dressed woman in town, a distinction that sets her apart from the farmers' wives and other Black Hawk women. (026-005)

She travels regularly to Omaha, both for business and pleasure. On one occasion she went to Omaha to hear the renowned actors Booth and Barrett perform — a mark of her interest in the cultural life beyond the prairie. (026-006) She sometimes leaves the hotel for a week at a time, during which her absence is keenly felt by the staff and boarders alike. (026-004)

## In the Text

Mrs. Gardener first appears in the narrative through the gossip of the town ladies, who note that she "is having a purple velvet made" for an upcoming event. (023-025) This detail captures both her fashion consciousness and the town's fascination with her.

At the Boys' Home, Mrs. Gardener maintains a piano and keeps it tuned, a rare refinement in a frontier hotel. (026-010) The hired girls are aware of her expectations: [[Tiny Soderball]] once nervously refused a social invitation by protesting, "Mrs. Gardener wouldn't like it." (026-021) Another girl reassures her companions that "Mrs. Gardener's in Omaha," freeing them — temporarily — from her supervision. (026-022)

Her influence extends beyond the hotel walls. [[Lena Lingard]] was observed copying one of Mrs. Gardener's new party dresses, indicating that her style set the standard for fashion among the younger women of Black Hawk. (031-001) She also made a purple velvet gown that drew the attention and admiration of the town. (023-025)

## Significance

Mrs. Gardener represents a particular form of female authority in Black Hawk — the businesswoman who has built her own domain. Unlike the immigrant hired girls she employs, she is established, propertied, and integrated into the town's social fabric. She occupies a middle ground between the farm women and the elite town ladies, running a commercial establishment while maintaining the standards of dress and conduct that command respect.

Her periodic journeys to Omaha also embody the town's connection to the wider world. When she goes to hear Booth and Barrett, she brings back a touch of metropolitan culture to the prairie — a reminder that Black Hawk, for all its isolation, is not entirely cut off from the artistic currents of the age.
