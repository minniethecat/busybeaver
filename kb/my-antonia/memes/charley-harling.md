## Overview

Charley Harling is the only son of Mr. and Mrs. Harling, the Burden family's nearest neighbors in Black Hawk. He is the leader among the neighborhood children, admired for his prowess and good nature. Jim Burden counts him as one of his closest boyhood friends:

> "Three of the Harling children were near me in age. Charley, the only son — they had lost an older boy — was sixteen, a tall, slender, brown-eyed fellow, very good to look at, and I think the most popular boy in Black Hawk." (021-003)

Charley is notably kind to Ántonia Shimerda (known as Tony), who adores him:

> "Charley was always first in his class at school, and he was always first in all the sports and games. Ántonia was jealous of Tony's admiration for Charley Harling." (022-005)

## Life in Black Hawk

Charley was not interested in business. His father indulged him:

> "With Charley, who was not interested in business, but was already preparing for Annapolis, Mr. Harling was very indulgent; bought him guns and tools and electric batteries, and never asked what he did with them." (021-004)

In the evenings, the Harling house was lively with music. Charley participated in the family's musical life, though his true passion was the outdoors. He and Jim hunted together through the countryside around Black Hawk:

> "Charley Harling and I had hunted through these woods, lying on our stomachs on the icy ground, watching the cranes." (033-006)

## Ántonia's Admiration

Ántonia had a special fondness for Charley that bordered on romantic admiration. She found excuses to talk about him:

> "Next to Charley, I think she loved Nina best." (022-006)

Tony would make Charley's favorite cakes, and small gestures from him meant the world to her:

> "If Charley whispered in her ear, or Charley gave her three winks, Tony would burst into a giggle." (025-005)

## Departure and Later Life

Charley left Black Hawk to attend the United States Naval Academy at Annapolis:

> "Charley Harling was already at Annapolis." (031-013)

He served on a battleship during the Spanish-American War:

> "Charley was off in his battleship, cruising somewhere in the Caribbean." (040-005)

Years later, when Jim Burden visits the Harlings after Mrs. Harling's death, Charley appears briefly at the end to greet the old friend.

## Significance

Charley Harling represents the confident, well-adjusted American boy of Black Hawk — the standard against which immigrant children like Ántonia measure themselves. He is comfortable in his world because the rules were made for him. Ántonia's admiration for him reflects both her desire to assimilate and the gap that remains between the immigrant's daughter and the established families of the town.

He also serves as a narrative anchor for Jim Burden's memory of Black Hawk boyhood — the golden age before Charley goes to Annapolis, before Jim goes to the university, before everything scatters.
