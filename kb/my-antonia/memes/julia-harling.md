## Overview

Julia Harling is one of the children of the [[The Harling Family|Harling family]] of [[Black Hawk]], known within the household as "the musical one." (021-003) She was the same age as the narrator [[Jim Burden]] — fourteen during the years he spent among the Harlings — falling between her elder brother [[Charley Harling]], who was sixteen, and her younger sister [[Sally Harling]], the short-haired tomboy a year her junior. (021-003)

## In the Text

Julia belongs to the lively, music-filled Harling household where [[Ántonia Shimerda]] works as the hired girl. Among the Harling children she is singled out for her devotion to music: she "was the only one who was held down to regular hours of practising, but they all played." (022-010)

On a summer evening at the Harling place, Julia "was in the hammock — she was fond of repose —" while her eldest sister [[Frances Harling]] played the piano in the dark, talking to their mother through the open window. (021-008) The small scene captures both Julia's easygoing temperament and the casual musical atmosphere of the home.

## Significance

Julia is a minor figure among the Harling children, but her identity as "the musical one" reinforces the novel's portrait of the [[The Harling Family|Harlings]] as a cultured, music-loving household — the setting that gives Ántonia some of her happiest years in town. (021-003) (022-010) Her fondness for repose lightly distinguishes her from her more energetic siblings, the tomboyish Sally and the capable, managing Frances.
