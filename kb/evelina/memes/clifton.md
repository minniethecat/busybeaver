# Clifton

**Clifton** is an elevated suburb near [[bristol|Bristol]], celebrated for its hot springs and scenic views, that serves as a key setting in the latter portion of *[[evelina|Evelina]]* (1778). [[fanny-burney|Burney]] uses Clifton as both a picturesque landscape and a stage for the novel's climactic social confrontations.

## Clifton in the Novel

Mrs. Beaumont, a relation of [[lord-orville|Lord Orville]], has "a very fine house upon [[clifton-hill|Clifton Hill]]" (062-064). [[evelina|Evelina]] visits "[[clifton-hill|Clifton Hill]], where, beautifully situated, is the house of Mrs. Beaumont" (063-005), noting the scenic qualities of the location. The party returns "but just arrived from [[clifton-hill|Clifton Hill]]" (064-003) after a visit.

## Scenic and Social Setting

Clifton's natural beauty provides a counterpoint to [[london|London]]'s urban frenzy. [[lord-orville|Lord Orville]] speaks of "the beauties of Clifton" (064-061), and the elevated situation of [[clifton-hill|Clifton Hill]] places it literally and figuratively above the spa-town bustle of the [[bristol|Hotwells]] below.

## Resort Culture

As part of the [[hot-wells|Bristol Hotwells]] resort area, Clifton participates in the 18th-century culture of spa visiting — a setting that brings together [[reputation|character]]s of different [[social-class|class]]es and facilitates the chance encounters that drive the novel's plot.
