# Mrs. Selwyn

Mrs. Selwyn is one of the most distinctive characters in the latter half of *Evelina* — a woman of keen intellect and unsparing wit who refuses to perform the simpering femininity expected of her sex. She chaperones Evelina to Bristol and Clifton, where her acerbic observations expose the follies of fashionable society.

## Identity

- **Title/Rank**: Mrs.
- **Role**: widowed or independent gentlewoman; Evelina's chaperone at Clifton

## Role in the Novel

Mrs. Selwyn enters the novel when Evelina, having returned to Berry Hill in distress, is sent to Bristol Hot Wells to recover her health. Mrs. Selwyn acts as her guardian during this period, attending the assemblies and social functions at Clifton where the novel's climactic confrontations unfold. Her presence provides Evelina with the protection of respectability while allowing her a degree of latitude that a more conventional chaperone might deny.

## Character Traits

Mrs. Selwyn is defined by her sharp tongue and refusal to flatter. Unlike Mrs. Mirvan — whose kindness expresses itself through tact — Mrs. Selwyn prefers direct, often sardonic commentary. She takes evident pleasure in puncturing masculine pretension, and her wit makes her a formidable ally for Evelina in the socially treacherous terrain of Clifton.

## Key Relationships

### With Evelina
Mrs. Selwyn's relationship with Evelina is protective but unsentimental. She does not offer the maternal warmth of Mrs. Mirvan; instead she provides the armour of her wit, deflecting the impertinence of men like Lord Merton and Sir Clement Willoughby with pointed rebukes.

### With Lord Orville
Mrs. Selwyn regards Lord Orville with guarded approval — a rare concession from a woman who generally holds the male sex in low esteem.
