# Fantoccini

The fantoccini (an Italian word for puppets or mechanical figures) was a popular form of eighteenth-century [[london|London]] entertainment. [[evelina|Evelina]] attends the fantoccini during her first [[london|London]] season, experiencing a diversion quite different from the balls and operas that dominate her social calendar.

## Overview

- **Type**: theatrical puppet entertainment
- **Venue**: a [[london|London]] pleasure resort (likely [[pantheon|the Pantheon]] or a similar venue)
- **Date**: early in [[evelina|Evelina]]'s [[london|London]] stay

## The Event

The fantoccini is announced by [[captain-mirvan|Captain Mirvan]]: "The [[captain-mirvan|Captain]] intends to take us [[fashion|ton]]ight to the fantoccini" (009-014). [[evelina|Evelina]], still recovering from the mortifications of the previous evening's [[assembly-rooms|assembly]], regards the prospect with mixed [[sentiment|feeling]]s — her dislike of the [[captain-mirvan|Captain]]'s coarse [[breeding|manners]] vies with her curiosity about a novel entertainment.

What follows is a charming and unexpected success: "We went last night to see the fantoccini, where we had infinite entertainment from the performance of a little comedy in French and Italian, by puppets, so admirably managed, that they both as[[fashion|ton]]ished and diverted us all, except the [[captain-mirvan|Captain]], who has a fixed and most prejudiced hatred of whatever is not English" (009-018).

The [[captain-mirvan|Captain]]'s xenophobic rejection of the entertainment — because it is not English — provides a comic counterpoint to the genuine delight of the rest of the party. His "fixed and most prejudiced hatred" of foreign things marks him as an unreconstructed John Bull, whose blunt chauvinism contrasts with the cosmopolitan tastes of fashionable [[london|London]].

## Significance

The fantoccini episode, though brief, serves several narrative purposes. It introduces [[evelina|Evelina]] to yet another variety of [[london|London]] amusement — adding to the rapid succession of balls, operas, [[ridotto]]s, and theatre visits that overwhelm and enchant her. It also demonstrates the [[captain-mirvan|Captain]]'s intractable provincialism: even a universally praised entertainment cannot please him if it carries the taint of foreign origin.
