# Mr. Evelyn

Mr. Evelyn is the grandfather of Evelina and the father of Caroline Evelyn, who later became Lady Belmont. Though deceased long before the novel's present action, his choices — particularly his appointment of Rev. Arthur Villars as his daughter's guardian and the legacy he left — establish the legal and moral framework within which Evelina's upbringing unfolds. His estate becomes a point of contention between Villars and Madame Duval.

## Identity

- **Full Name**: Mr. Evelyn (first name not given)
- **Title/Rank**: Mr. — a gentleman of means
- **Family**: Father of Caroline Evelyn (later Lady Belmont); grandfather of Evelina; father-in-law (posthumously) of Sir John Belmont
- **Residence**: England

## Role in the Novel

Mr. Evelyn's significance to the novel lies entirely in the past — in the arrangements he made before his death that shaped the custody and financial circumstances of his descendants. Rev. Villars relates that he "had the honour to accompany Mr. Evelyn, the grandfather of my young charge, when upon his travels, in the capacity of a tutor" (002-007), establishing a relationship of trust that would prove decisive.

Upon his death, Mr. Evelyn left Villars "a legacy of a thousand pounds, and the sole guardianship of his daughter's person till her eighteenth year; conjuring me, in the most affecting terms, to take the charge of her education" (002-010). This bequest and guardianship forms the legal basis for Villars's authority over Caroline and, by extension, over Evelina. It is the foundation on which Villars's claim to direct Evelina's upbringing rests, and it becomes a key point of contention when Madame Duval attempts to assert her own authority over her granddaughter.

The disposal of Mr. Evelyn's estate is also a matter of dispute. Lady Howard warns that "as to Mr. Evelyn's estate, I have no doubt but that Madame Duval and her relations will dispose of it among themselves" (004-006), suggesting that Duval — Caroline's former guardian — may have designs on the inheritance that should rightfully pass to Evelina.

## Character Traits

What little is known of Mr. Evelyn comes filtered through Villars's respectful recollection. He is portrayed as a gentleman of property who valued education and trusted Villars's judgment implicitly. His choice to appoint a tutor, rather than a family member, as his daughter's guardian suggests both a concern for proper upbringing and perhaps a lack of suitable family alternatives. The "most affecting terms" (002-010) in which he entrusted Caroline to Villars indicate genuine paternal devotion.

## Key Relationships

### With Rev. Arthur Villars

Mr. Evelyn's relationship with Villars began when Villars served as his tutor during continental travels (002-007). This connection deepened into a bond of complete trust: the legacy of a thousand pounds and the sole guardianship of his daughter represent an extraordinary act of confidence. Villars reciprocates this trust throughout his life, referring to his "regard for Mr. Evelyn, and his amiable daughter" as a permanent principle of conduct (031-005).

### With Caroline Evelyn (Lady Belmont)

Mr. Evelyn was the father of Caroline Evelyn and, by his deathbed arrangements, attempted to secure her future and education through Villars's guardianship (002-010). The tragedy that subsequently befell Caroline — her marriage to Sir John Belmont, his denial of the marriage, and her early death — occurred after Mr. Evelyn's own death, beyond his power to prevent.

### With Madame Duval

The nature of Mr. Evelyn's relationship with Madame Duval is not directly described, but Duval's later involvement in Caroline's upbringing suggests some form of prior connection. The dispute over his estate (004-006) implies ongoing tension between Duval's interests and those of Evelyn's descendants.

## Significant Appearances

Mr. Evelyn does not appear in the novel's present action. References to him are confined to recollections:

- **Letter I**: Lady Howard mentions him in connection with Madame Duval's vulgarity — her first husband was Mr. Evelyn (001-006)
- **Letter II**: Rev. Villars recounts his service as Mr. Evelyn's travelling tutor and the legacy and guardianship entrusted to him (002-007, 002-010)
- **Letter IV**: Lady Howard warns of Madame Duval's designs on Mr. Evelyn's estate (004-006)
- **Letter XXXI**: Lady Howard invokes Villars's "regard for Mr. Evelyn, and his amiable daughter" (031-005)

## Narrative Significance

Mr. Evelyn, though a remote background figure, is the legal and moral anchor of Evelina's world. The thousand-pound legacy he left to Villars, and the guardianship over his daughter, constitute the framework within which the novel's conflicts over Evelina's custody and inheritance play out. His posthumous influence persists through Villars's faithful execution of his wishes and through the contested estate that represents Evelina's material inheritance.
