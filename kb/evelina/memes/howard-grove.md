# Howard Grove

Howard Grove is the country seat of Lady Howard and the residence of the [[captain-mirvan|Mirvan]] family in [[Fanny Burney]]'s *[[evelina|Evelina]]*. It is here that [[Evelina]] first experiences company beyond the seclusion of [[Berry Hill]], and it is from here that the [[london|London]] expedition — which forms the novel's main action — is launched.

## Location and Setting

Howard Grove is situated within traveling distance of [[berry-hill|Berry Hill]], where [[Rev. Villars]] resides with [[evelina|Evelina]]. The proximity of the two estates enables the close relationship between the families. Lady Howard, the matriarch of Howard Grove, was acquainted with [[evelina|Evelina]]'s mother, Lady Belmont, and feels a duty of care toward the orphaned girl.

## Social Center

The household at Howard Grove comprises:
- **Lady Howard**: The widowed matriarch
- **Mrs. [[captain-mirvan|Mirvan]]**: Lady Howard's daughter
- **Maria [[captain-mirvan|Mirvan]]**: Mrs. [[captain-mirvan|Mirvan]]'s daughter and [[evelina|Evelina]]'s closest friend
- **[[captain-mirvan|Captain Mirvan]]**: Mrs. [[captain-mirvan|Mirvan]]'s husband, returned from seven years at sea

The arrival of [[Captain Mirvan]] transforms Howard Grove from a quiet country household into a scene of "hurry and perturbation" (008-001). His return is the proximate cause of the [[london|London]] journey — Mrs. [[captain-mirvan|Mirvan]] must go to town to meet him, and Lady Howard petitions Rev. [[rev-villars|Villars]] to allow [[Evelina]] to accompany the party.

## Evelina at Howard Grove

[[evelina|Evelina]]'s visits to Howard Grove mark the beginning of her social education. Here she first encounters the warmth of female friendship with Maria [[captain-mirvan|Mirvan]], and here she first tastes the excitement of impending travel. Her letter describing the household's preparations captures the flutter of anticipation:

> This house seems to be the house of joy; every face wears a smile, and a laugh is at everybody's service. It is quite amusing to walk about and see the general confusion; a room leading to the garden is fitting up for Captain Mirvan's study.

— (008-001)

## Significance

Howard Grove represents a transitional space in the novel's geography — neither the complete seclusion of [[berry-hill|Berry Hill]] nor the dangerous freedom of [[london|London]]. It is a halfway house between innocence and experience, where [[evelina|Evelina]] first begins to imagine a world beyond her guardian's protection.
