# Lord Orville

Lord Orville is the romantic hero of *Evelina* and the novel's embodiment of ideal gentlemanly conduct. Unlike the predatory Sir Clement Willoughby or the boorish Captain Mirvan, Orville treats Evelina with genuine respect, protecting her reputation even at cost to his own social standing.

## Role in the Novel

Orville first encounters Evelina at an assembly, where he distinguishes himself by his polite conduct. Throughout the novel, he serves as both romantic interest and moral compass, demonstrating that true nobility lies in character rather than rank.

> Lord Orville, whom we saw in the stage-box, came to us; and he honoured us with his company all the evening.
>
> (020-003)

## Character Traits

Orville's defining quality is his adherence to genuine honour — not the superficial code of gallantry, but a deep-seated respect for others regardless of their social position. He is the only male character who consistently values Evelina's comfort and reputation above his own desires.

## Key Relationships

### Evelina
The central relationship of the novel. Orville's courtship of Evelina is marked by restraint and genuine affection, contrasting sharply with Sir Clement's aggressive pursuit.

### Sir Clement Willoughby
Orville's foil — where Sir Clement uses the forms of politeness to mask selfish intent, Orville embodies their substance.
