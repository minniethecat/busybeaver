# Bristol Hotwells

The Bristol Hotwells (or Hot Wells) is the spa resort near [[bristol|Bristol]] where the final act of *[[evelina|Evelina]]* takes place. Like [[bath|Bath]], its more famous counterpart, the [[bristol|Hotwells]] drew visitors seeking both the medicinal benefits of its waters and the pleasures of polite society.

## Overview

- **Type**: spa / resort
- **Location**: near [[bristol|Bristol]], southwest [[england|England]]
- **Period**: 18th century (August–September in the novel)

## Description in the Novel

[[evelina|Evelina]] arrives at the [[bristol|Hotwells]] in late summer, and her letters from this period are dated from "Bristol Hotwells, August 28th" (061-002), marking the beginning of the novel's final movement. Subsequent letters carry datelines from "Bristol Hotwells, Sept. 12th" (062-002), "Sept. 16th" (063-002), and "Sept. 19th" (064-002), establishing a compressed timeline for the revelations and reversals that conclude the story.

The [[bristol|Hotwells]] is described as a place of resort and society, where visitors take the waters and participate in social activities. Mrs. Selwyn, [[evelina|Evelina]]'s companion at [[bristol|Bristol]], is noted to be "engaged at the Hot Wells in the afternoon" (066-034), and [[evelina|Evelina]] "begged Mrs. Selwyn's leave to send her man to the Hot Wells" (066-036) to deliver a note — suggesting that the spa is a distinct destination from their lodgings.

## Significance to the Plot

The [[bristol|Bristol]] section of the novel is the crucible in which all of [[evelina|Evelina]]'s trials are resolved. It is at the [[bristol|Hotwells]] that she encounters Mr. Macartney, that the truth of her birth is finally revealed, and that [[lord-orville|Lord Orville]] makes his declaration. The spa setting is significant: a place of healing and restoration provides the appropriate backdrop for the restoration of [[evelina|Evelina]]'s name and fortune.

A walking party to the Hot Wells is recorded: "Yesterday morning, as soon as I had finished my hasty letter, I was summoned to attend a walking party to the Hot Wells" (070-004). These social rituals — the promenade, the taking of waters, the visiting of company — structure the days at [[bristol|Bristol]] much as the [[london|London]] season structured [[evelina|Evelina]]'s earlier experiences.

## Social Context

The [[bristol|Hotwells]] operates as a more intimate and less dangerous version of the [[london|London]] social scene. Unlike the perilous public venues of the capital — [[vauxhall|Vauxhall]] with its dark walks, the [[assembly|assemblies]] with their forward suitors — the [[bristol|Bristol]] spa offers a contained society where [[reputation|character]]s of genuine merit can be distinguished from those of mere fashion. The medicinal associations of the waters (the "healing" of various ailments) provide a symbolic frame for the moral and social healing that occurs there.
