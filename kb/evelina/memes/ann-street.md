# Queen Ann Street

[[queen-ann-street|Queen Ann Street]] is the [[london|London]] address of the [[captain-mirvan|Mirvan]] family, serving as [[evelina|Evelina]]'s home base during her first visit to the capital. From this respectable but unassuming street, [[evelina|Evelina]] sets out to encounter the fashionable world of balls, [[assembly|assemblies]], and pleasure gardens.

## Overview

- **Type**: street / residence
- **Location**: [[london|London]], West End area
- **Period**: 18th century (April–June, the [[london|London]] Season)

## Description in the Novel

[[evelina|Evelina]]'s letters from [[london|London]] are invariably dated from [[queen-ann-street|Queen Ann Street]], establishing it as her domestic anchor in the city. The first such letter announces her arrival: "[[queen-ann-street|Queen Ann Street]], [[london|London]], Saturday, April 2" (010-002). Subsequent letters continue this pattern, with datelines from "[[queen-ann-street|Queen Ann Street]], April 5, Tuesday Morning" (011-002) and "[[queen-ann-street|Queen Ann Street]], April 13" (014-002).

The street represents the sphere of Mrs. [[captain-mirvan|Mirvan]]'s sensible guardianship, in contrast to the fashionable public venues that expose [[evelina|Evelina]] to social peril.

## Significance to the Plot

[[queen-ann-street|Queen Ann Street]] is the location of many domestic incidents that advance the plot. It is here that [[evelina|Evelina]] prepares, "most reluctantly, to wait upon [[madame-duval|Madame Duval]]" (014-045), and where "Mrs. [[captain-mirvan|Mirvan]] proposed to the [[captain-mirvan|Captain]], that she should be invited to dinner in [[queen-ann-street|Queen Ann Street]]" (018-021).

The street also serves as a point of return after [[evelina|Evelina]]'s adventures and ordeals. After the dangerous episode with [[sir-clement-willoughby|Sir Clement Willoughby]] at [[vauxhall|Vauxhall]], the destination is telling: "bid him make haste to [[queen-ann-street|Queen Ann Street]]" (021-172). The street represents safety and respectability — a haven from the perils of public [[london|London]]. [[evelina|Evelina]]'s guardian Rev. [[rev-villars|Villars]] had warned against "the gaieties of a [[london|London]] life" (004-004), and [[queen-ann-street|Queen Ann Street]] embodies the domestic alternative.

## Social Context

[[queen-ann-street|Queen Ann Street]] occupies an intermediate social position in [[fanny-burney|Burney]]'s [[london|London]] geography. It is neither the aristocratic West End of [[lord-orville|Lord Orville]] nor the commercial quarters of the Brangh[[fashion|ton]] family. It represents the solid [[breeding|gentility]] of a naval officer's household — respectable but not grand, comfortable but not fashionable. This middling position mirrors [[evelina|Evelina]]'s own uncertain social status as a young woman of questionable birth navigating the stratified world of eighteenth-century [[london|London]].
