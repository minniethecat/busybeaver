# Drury Lane Theatre

The Theatre Royal Drury Lane was [[london|London]]'s most celebrated playhouse in the eighteenth century. In [[Fanny Burney]]'s *[[evelina|Evelina]]*, it is the site of the heroine's first [[london|London]] entertainment — a performance featuring the legendary actor David Garrick — and the occasion of her earliest recorded transport of delight.

## The Theatre

Drury Lane had been a theatre since 1663, but the building [[evelina|Evelina]] visits was the third incarnation, rebuilt by Christopher Wren in 1674 (and later replaced by the current structure in 1812). Under Garrick's management from 1747 to 1776, it was the cultural heart of Georgian [[london|London]] — the place where Shakespeare was revived, where new comedies of [[breeding|manners]] premiered, and where [[fashion|fashionable society]] displayed itself as much as the players on stage.

## Garrick's Performance

[[evelina|Evelina]]'s enthusiasm is unrestrained when she learns that Garrick is to perform: "The celebrated Mr. Garrick performs Ranger. I am quite in ecstasy. So is Miss [[captain-mirvan|Mirvan]]. How fortunate that he should happen to play!" (010-001). David Garrick (1717–1779) was the greatest actor of the age — his naturalistic [[fashion|style]] revolutionized English acting, and his appearance at Drury Lane was a major social event.

The play [[evelina|Evelina]] sees is *The Suspicious Husband* by Benjamin Hoadly, with Garrick in the role of Ranger — a rakish but charming gentleman. The casting is apt: Ranger's blend of charm and unreliability anticipates the very type of gentleman — [[Sir Clement Willoughby]] — whom [[evelina|Evelina]] will soon encounter in earnest.

## The Power of Performance

[[evelina|Evelina]]'s reaction to the performance reveals her [[sentiment|emotion]]al susceptibility to art: "I am afraid you will think me mad, so I won't say any more; yet, I really believe Mr. Garrick would make you mad too if you could see him" (010-001). This capacity for transport — the same [[compassion|sensibility]] that will make her vulnerable to [[Lord Orville]]'s charms — is both her gift and her danger.

## Significance

The Drury Lane visit is [[evelina|Evelina]]'s first taste of [[london|London]]'s cultural life and establishes a pattern that will define her education: an encounter with something beautiful, a rush of [[sentiment|feeling]], and a subsequent need to reflect — through her letters to [[Rev. Villars]] — on what that [[sentiment|feeling]] means and whether it can be trusted.
