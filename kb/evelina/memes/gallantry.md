# Gallantry

**Gallantry** — the 18th-century code of male attentions toward women — appears in *Evelina* (1778) in both its benign and predatory forms. Burney uses gallantry as a diagnostic tool: how a man performs gallantry reveals his character.

## Gallantry as Social Code

In the ballrooms and assemblies of fashionable London, gallantry is the expected currency of male-female interaction. Lord Orville's gallantry is marked by genuine respect: his attentions are "gentle, attentive, and infinitely engaging" (011-015). His compliments never transgress the boundaries of propriety.

## Predatory Gallantry

Sir Clement Willoughby embodies gallantry's dark side. His elaborate compliments and persistent attentions — including his theatrical declaration "Be nobler than your sex" (013-046) — transform gallantry into a weapon of seduction. Burney exposes how the language of gallantry can mask predatory intent.

## Gallantry and Female Agency

Evelina's difficulty in distinguishing genuine gallantry from its counterfeit reflects the predicament of women in a society where male attention is both required and dangerous. Her education consists partly in learning to read the difference — to recognise that true gallantry serves the woman, not the man.
