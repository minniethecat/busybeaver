# Queen Ann Street

A [[london|London]] street where Mrs. [[captain-mirvan|Mirvan]]'s town residence is located. It serves as [[evelina|Evelina]]'s home base during her first extended stay in [[london|London]], and is the address from which several of her letters to [[rev-villars|Mr. Villars]] are dated.

## Overview

- **Type**: street
- **Location**: [[london|London]]
- **Period**: 18th century

## Description in the Novel

Queen Ann Street is never described physically in the novel; it is known only as the [[captain-mirvan|Mirvan]]s' [[london|London]] address. Several of [[evelina|Evelina]]'s letters are headed from this street: "Queen Ann Street, [[london|London]], Saturday, April 2" (010-002), "Queen Ann Street, April 5, Tuesday Morning" (011-002), "Queen Ann Street, April 13" (014-002), and "Queen Ann Street, Thursday morning, April 14" (016-002). The street functions as a fixed point in the narrative geography — a respectable address from which [[evelina|Evelina]] ventures into [[london|London]] society and to which she returns after her various adventures.

## Significance to the Plot

Queen Ann Street anchors [[evelina|Evelina]]'s [[london|London]] experience. It is here that she prepares "most reluctantly, to wait upon [[madame-duval|Madame Duval]]" (018-021), and where [[madame-duval|Madame Duval]] "made no scruple of returning to dine" despite their tensions (019-038). A dramatic episode occurs as the coach "had hardly turned out of Queen Ann Street" when [[captain-mirvan|Captain Mirvan]]'s servant, with "a broad grin on his face," intercepts [[madame-duval|Madame Duval]]'s carriage as part of the [[captain-mirvan|Captain]]'s scheme to humiliate her (021-049). Later, [[rev-villars|Mr. Villars]] reflects that "had not your vehemence frightened him, Queen Ann Street would have been the first place to which he would have hastened" in reference to [[sir-clement-willoughby|Sir Clement Willoughby]]'s [[conduct|behaviour]] after the opera (024-005).

## Social Context

As a [[london|London]] street address for a naval family of respectable but not aristocratic standing, Queen Ann Street represents the comfortable middle [[social-class|rank]] of Georgian society. It is neither the fashionable West End of [[lord-orville|Lord Orville]]'s world nor the commercial [[snow-hill|Snow Hill]] of the Brangh[[fashion|ton]]s — it occupies a secure middle ground that suits the [[captain-mirvan|Mirvan]]s' status and offers [[evelina|Evelina]] a safe vantage from which to observe the city's social extremes.
