# Bernard's Return to Profitendieu

After his long experiment in independence — the flight from home, the secretaryship with Edouard, the sojourn in Switzerland, the brief liaison with Sarah — Bernard Profitendieu returns to his father's house, completing the novel's central arc from rebellion to reconciliation.

## Summary

Bernard's return to the Profitendieu home is the novel's quiet climax. After the violence of Olivier's suicide attempt and the unraveling of the counterfeit coin ring, Bernard comes back to the man he once rejected as not his real father. The return is not a defeat — Bernard does not come back chastened or broken — but an acknowledgment that authentic living does not require permanent estrangement. Profitendieu receives him without recrimination, and the novel ends on a note of qualified domestic peace (048-020).

## Context

### Narrative Position

The return occurs in Part III, the novel's final movement, after the major crises have resolved: Olivier has survived his suicide attempt, the schoolboy ring has been exposed, and the counterfeit coin conspiracy has collapsed. Bernard's return is the last significant event before the novel's conclusion.

### Preceding Events

Bernard has witnessed Olivier's suicide attempt at the Argonauts banquet, an event that clarified the fragility of the relationships he had been treating as provisional. He has also stepped aside from the secretaryship, recognizing that Olivier should occupy the position Edouard originally intended for him. His brief encounter with Sarah has run its course, leaving no residue of attachment (043-033, 048-001).

## Detailed Account

The return is narrated with characteristic understatement. Gide does not stage an emotional reunion scene; instead, the fact of Bernard's return is reported almost in passing — "I hear from Olivier that Bernard has gone back to his father's; and, indeed, it was the best thing he could do" (048-020). The very casualness of the narration is significant: Bernard's return is not a dramatic climax but a quiet resolution, the natural terminus of an arc that began with the violent rupture of his departure.

Profitendieu's reception is equally understated. The man who was devastated by Bernard's flight — whose grief manifested as physical pain in his liver — receives his returned son without drama. There is no confession, no forgiveness scene, no sentimental reconciliation. The family simply resumes, altered but intact.

## Consequences

### Immediate Aftermath

The novel ends. Bernard's return is, in effect, the last word on his arc. Gide does not show us Bernard's life after the return — whether he stays, whether he is happy, whether his reconciliation with Profitendieu endures. The silence is deliberate: closure of this kind would falsify the novel's commitment to openness and becoming.

### Narrative Ripple Effects

Bernard's return completes the novel's most sustained character arc — from the boy who declared "I have never loved Profitendieu" to the young man who understands that love and biology need not coincide. The return also functions as a structural bookend to the novel's opening, in which Bernard's flight from the same household set everything in motion.

## Thematic Significance

The return represents Gide's most nuanced statement on the relationship between freedom and belonging. Bernard does not return because he has failed at freedom but because he has succeeded at it — he has proven to himself that he can live independently, and having proven it, he no longer needs to maintain the posture of rebellion. Authenticity, the novel suggests, does not require permanent estrangement from one's origins; it requires only the freedom to leave and, having left, the freedom to return.

## Literary Technique

Gide's refusal to dramatize the return scene is itself a literary decision of great sophistication. By reporting rather than rendering Bernard's homecoming, he denies the reader the emotional catharsis that conventional narrative would provide. The return is allowed to be what it is — a fact, not a climax — and the reader is trusted to understand its significance without having it performed.
