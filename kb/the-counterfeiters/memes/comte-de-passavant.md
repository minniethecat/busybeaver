# Comte de Passavant

A fashionable novelist whose commercial success masks artistic and moral emptiness — the novel's embodiment of counterfeit values in the literary world and Edouard's rival for Olivier's affection.

## Role in the Novel

Passavant is Edouard's antithesis: the artist as careerist, whose work "is not so much an end as a means" (009-026). Where Edouard agonizes over artistic sincerity, Passavant produces with facile speed — he admits he wrote his last book "much too quickly. To tell the truth, the whole time I was writing it I was thinking of my next one" (016-051).

He represents the worldly temptation that threatens young talents like Olivier. "Nothing spreads more ruin or receives more applause than men of his stamp" (027-009). His threat is not villainy but glamour: he makes counterfeit look more attractive than the genuine.

## Biography

### Background

A count with literary ambitions and social connections. His novel _The Horizontal Bar_ has brought him fashionable success. He moves in international circles and cultivates the young.

### Key Actions

Passavant competes with Edouard for Olivier's attention and allegiance. He hosts literary salons and cultivates a reputation for modernity. His aesthetic positions "are evoked by the passing hour; their mot d'ordre is opportunism" (009-026).

### Relationships

**Edouard**: Passavant's rival — aesthetically, personally, and for Olivier. **Olivier Molinier**: The young man Passavant attempts to draw into his orbit. The competition for Olivier is the competition between sincere and counterfeit art.

## Character Analysis

### Motivations

Passavant is motivated by the desire for influence and recognition. Art is his instrument, not his end. He "snaps his fingers at the future" — writing only for the present generation, unconcerned with survival.

### Symbolic Role

Passavant is the counterfeit artist — successful, applauded, hollow. His work "is in danger of disappearing" with the generation it flatters (009-026). He represents everything Gide's novel is written against.

## Key Passages
