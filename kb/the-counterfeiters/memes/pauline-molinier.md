# Pauline Molinier

The half-sister of [[Edouard]], wife of [[Monsieur Molinier]], and mother of [[Olivier Molinier|Olivier]], [[George Molinier|George]], and [[Vincent Molinier]] — the maternal center of the Molinier household who manages her sons' crises while maintaining the family's social composure.

## Role in the Novel

Pauline Molinier is the novel's primary maternal figure — a practical, emotionally present counterweight to the absent or corrupting adults who surround the novel's young characters. Edouard's visits to her household provide many of the novel's domestic scenes, and her concern for her sons — particularly Vincent, who is recovering in a sanatorium — anchors the family's emotional life.

## Biography

Pauline is Edouard's half-sister and the wife of Monsieur Molinier. She presides over a household of three sons, each in some form of crisis: Vincent has suffered a breakdown and is convalescing after his disastrous affair with Laura; Olivier is caught between Passavant and Edouard; and George is involved in the counterfeit coin ring. Edouard frequently visits: "It was the first time I had taken a meal at Pauline's" (PN=012-032). She keeps Edouard informed about Vincent: "Pauline says she has good news from the sanatorium where Vincent is staying; he has almost completely recovered" (PN=012-045). Her concern extends to George's education: "Pauline wants him to try for the École Normale afterwards" (PN=014-073).

## Character Analysis

Pauline Molinier represents maternal competence in a novel where parenthood is more often a source of harm than of nurture. Unlike Sophroniska, whose therapeutic interventions damage Boris, or Profitendieu, whose paternal authority is undone by a single revelation, Pauline manages her family's difficulties with pragmatic attention. Her role in the novel is understated but essential — she is the stable ground against which her sons' dramas play out.
