## About This Page

Maud Gonne dominates the love poetry — from the early rose poems through the middle period's bitter meditations to the late celebrations of "the folly that is man's." But Yeats's poetry of love extends beyond Gonne to include Iseult Gonne, Olivia Shakespear, and the mythic lovers of Irish legend (Aengus, Niamh and Oisin, Deirdre and Naoise) who serve as archetypes for human passion.

For the mythological love stories, see also [[irish-mythology]]. For the symbol of the rose as a love emblem, see [[Yeatsian-Symbolism]].
