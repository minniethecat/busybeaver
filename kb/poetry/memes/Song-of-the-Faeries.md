# Song of the Faeries

[001-001]A man has a hope for heaven,   

[001-002]But soulless a faery dies,   

[001-003]As a leaf that is old, and withered and cold   

[001-004]When the wint’ry vapours rise.

[001-005]Soon shall our wings be stilled,   

[001-006]And our laughter over and done,   

[001-007]So let us dance where the yellow lance   

[001-008]Of the barley shoots in the sun.

[001-009]So let us dance on the fringed waves,   

[001-010]And shout at the wisest owls   

[001-011]In their downy caps, and startle the naps   

[001-012]Of the [[dream-and-vision|dreaming]] water-fowls.

[001-013]And fight for the black sloe-berries,   

[001-014]For soulless a faery dies,   

[001-015]As a leaf that is old, and withered and cold   

[001-016]When the wintry vapours rise.
