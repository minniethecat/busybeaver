# On Mr. Nettleship’s Picture at the Royal Hibernian Academy

[007-001]Yonder the sickle of the moon sails on,   

[007-002]But here the Lioness licks her soft cub   

[007-003]Tender and fearless on her funeral pyre;   

[007-004]Above, saliva dripping from his jaws,   

[007-005]The Lion, the world’s great solitary, bends   

[007-006]Lowly the head of his magnificence   

[007-007]And roars, mad with the touch of the unknown,   

[007-008]Not as he shakes the forest; but a cry   

[007-009]Low, long and musical. A dew-drop hung   

[007-010]Bright on a grass blade’s under side, might hear   

[007-011]Nor tremble to its fall. The fire sweeps round   

[007-012]Re-shining in his eyes. So ever moves   

[007-013]The flaming circle of the outer Law,   

[007-014]Nor heeds the old, dim protest and the cry   

[007-015]The orb of the most inner living heart   

[007-016]Gives forth. He, the Eternal, works His will.
