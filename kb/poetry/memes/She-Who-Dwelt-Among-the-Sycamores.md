# She Who Dwelt Among the Sycamores

[026-001]A Fancy

[026-002]A little boy outside the sycamore wood   

[026-003]Saw on the wood’s edge gleam an ash-grey feather;   

[026-004]A kid, held by one soft white ear for tether,   

[026-005]Trotted beside him in a playful mood.   

[026-006]A little boy inside the sycamore wood   

[026-007]Followed a ringdove’s ash-grey gleam of feather.   

[026-008]Noon wrapt the trees in veils of violet weather,   

[026-009]And on tiptoe the winds a-whispering stood.   

[026-010]Deep in the woodland paused they, the six feet   

[026-011]Lapped in the lemon daffodils; a bee   

[026-012]In the long grass﻿—four eyes droop low﻿—a seat   

[026-013]Of moss, a maiden weaving. Singeth she:   

[026-014]“I am lone Lady Quietness, my sweet,   

[026-015]And on this loom I weave thy destiny.”
