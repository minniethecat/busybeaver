# A Summer Evening

[011-001]The living woods forego their care,   

[011-002]Their dread of autumn’s mortal wing,   

[011-003]And shake their birds upon the air,   

[011-004]And like a silver trumpet ring.

[011-005]The giddy bee’s complacent croon,   

[011-006]Where long grey grasses bow and bend,   

[011-007]In all its honey-thickened tune   

[011-008]Has no word of the sulphurous end.

[011-009]The sunflowers weave a golden clime,   

[011-010]As though their season had no date,   

[011-011]Nod to the iron shoes of Time,   

[011-012]And play with his immortal hate.

[011-013]And, maiden, be thou mirthful too,   

[011-014]Lay down the burden of thy race,   

[011-015]For God is walking in the dew,   

[011-016]An evening presence fills the place.

[011-017]The hollow woodlands feel Him there,   

[011-018]And dread no more foul autumn’s wing,   

[011-019]And shake their birds upon the air,   

[011-020]And like a silver trumpet ring.
