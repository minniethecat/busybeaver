# Introductory Poems from “The Shadowy Waters”

## The Shadowy Waters

[042-001]I walked among the seven woods of [[coole-park|Coole]],   

[042-002]Shan-walla, where a willow-bordered pond   

[042-003]Gathers the wild duck from the winter dawn;   

[042-004]Shady Kyle-dortha; sunnier Kyle-na-gno,   

[042-005]Where many hundred squirrels are as happy   

[042-006]As though they had been hidden by green boughs,   

[042-007]Where old age cannot find them; Pairc-na-lea,   

[042-008]Where hazel and ash and privet blind the paths;   

[042-009]Dim Pairc-na-carraig, where the wild bees fling   

[042-010]Their sudden fragrances on the green air;   

[042-011]Dim Pairc-na-tarav, where enchanted eyes   

[042-012]Have seen immortal, mild, proud shadows walk;   

[042-013]Dim Inchy wood, that hides badger and fox   

[042-014]And marten-cat, and borders that old wood   

[042-015]Wise Biddy Early called the wicked wood:   

[042-016]Seven odours, seven murmurs, seven woods.   

[042-017]I had not eyes like those enchanted eyes,   

[042-018]Yet dreamed that beings happier than men   

[042-019]Moved round me in the shadows, and at night   

[042-020]My dreams were cloven by voices and by fires;   

[042-021]And the images I have woven in this story   

[042-022]Of Forgael and Dectora and the empty waters   

[042-023]Moved round me in the voices and the fires,   

[042-024]And more I may not write of, for they that cleave   

[042-025]The waters of sleep can make a chattering tongue   

[042-026]Heavy like stone, their wisdom being half silence.   

[042-027]How shall I name you, immortal, mild, proud shadows?   

[042-028]I only know that all we know comes from you,   

[042-029]And that you come from Eden on flying feet.   

[042-030]Is Eden far away, or do you hide   

[042-031]From human thought, as hares and mice and coneys   

[042-032]That run before the reaping-hook and lie   

[042-033]In the last ridge of the barley? Do our wood   

[042-034]And winds and ponds cover more quiet woods,   

[042-035]More shining winds, more star-glimmering ponds?   

[042-036]Is Eden out of time and out of space?   

[042-037]And do you gather about us when pale [[shadow-and-light|light]]   

[042-038]Shining on water and fallen among leaves,   

[042-039]And winds blowing from flowers, and whirr of feathers   

[042-040]And the green quiet, have uplifted the heart?

[042-041]I have made this poem for you, that men may read it   

[042-042]Before they read of Forgael and Dectora,   

[042-043]As men in the old times, before the harps began,   

[042-044]Poured out wine for the high invisible ones.

[042-045]September 1900.

## The Harp of Aengus

[042-046]Edain came out of Midher’s hill, and lay   

[042-047]Beside young [[aengus-god|Aengus]] in his tower of glass,   

[042-048]Where time is drowned in odour-laden winds   

[042-049]And druid moons, and murmuring of boughs,   

[042-050]And sleepy boughs, and boughs where apples made   

[042-051]Of opal and ruby and pale chrysolite   

[042-052]Awake unsleeping fires; and wove seven strings,   

[042-053]Sweet with all [[song-and-poetry|music]], out of his long hair,   

[042-054]Because her hands had been made wild by love.   

[042-055]When Midher’s wife had changed her to a fly,   

[042-056]He made a harp with druid apple wood   

[042-057]That she among her winds might know he wept;   

[042-058]And from that hour he has watched over none   

[042-059]But faithful lovers.
