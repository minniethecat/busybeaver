# Drumcliff Churchyard

Drumcliff (or Drumcliffe) is a small village in Co. Sligo, at the foot of Ben Bulben mountain. Its churchyard is the burial place of W. B. Yeats, who died in France in 1939 and whose remains were brought back to Ireland in 1948, as he had instructed in his poem "Under Ben Bulben."

## Yeats's Choice

Yeats chose Drumcliff for his burial in the closing lines of "Under Ben Bulben":

> Under bare Ben Bulben's head
> In Drumcliff churchyard Yeats is laid.
> An ancestor was rector there
> Long years ago...

The choice was deliberate and symbolic. Drumcliff was in his beloved Co. Sligo, the landscape of his childhood and of so much of his poetry. His great-grandfather, John Yeats, had been rector of Drumcliff Church. The grave looks towards Ben Bulben, the flat-topped mountain that dominates the Sligo skyline and appears throughout Yeats's work.

## The Epitaph

Yeats's epitaph — the closing lines of "Under Ben Bulben" — is carved on his headstone:

> Cast a cold eye
> On life, on death.
> Horseman, pass by!

The words are among the most famous in twentieth-century poetry. They embody Yeats's late ideal: the aristocratic, impersonal gaze of the artist who has made his peace with mortality and demands nothing of posterity but a clear-eyed acknowledgment.
