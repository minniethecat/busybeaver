# Street Dancers

[028-001]Singing in this London street   

[028-002]To the rhythm of their feet,   

[028-003]By a window’s feeble [[shadow-and-light|light]]   

[028-004]Are two ragged children bright﻿—   

[028-005]Larger sparrows of the town,   

[028-006]Nested ’mong the vapours brown.   

[028-007]Far away the starry mirth   

[028-008]Hangs o’er all the wooded earth.

[028-009]If these merry ones should know,   

[028-010]Footing in the feeble glow,   

[028-011]Of a wide wood’s leafy leisure,   

[028-012]Would they foot so fleet a measure?   

[028-013]Ah no!

[028-014]Maybe now in some far lane,   

[028-015]Dancing on the moon’s broad stain,   

[028-016]Watched of placid poplar trees,   

[028-017]Children sing in twos and threes.   

[028-018]Hush! hush! hush! on every lip   

[028-019]Lies a chubby finger-tip,   

[028-020]As there floats from fields afar   

[028-021]Clamour of the lone nightjar.

[028-022]If these merry ones should know,   

[028-023]Footing in the feeble glow,   

[028-024]Other people’s mirth and pleasure,   

[028-025]Would they foot so fleet a measure?   

[028-026]Ah no!

[028-027]Maybe in some isle of isles,   

[028-028]In the south seas’ azure miles,   

[028-029]Dance the savage children small,   

[028-030]Singing to their light footfall.   

[028-031]Hush! hush! hush! they pause and point   

[028-032]Where a shell, the seas anoint,   

[028-033]Dropping liquid rainbow light,   

[028-034]Rolls along the [[water-imagery|sea]]-sands white.

[028-035]If these merry ones should know,   

[028-036]Footing in the feeble glow,   

[028-037]Other people’s mirth and pleasure,   

[028-038]Would they foot so fleet a measure?   

[028-039]Ah no!

[028-040]Maybe now a Bedouin’s brood   

[028-041]Laughing goes in wildest mood,   

[028-042]Past the spears and palm-stems dry,   

[028-043]Past the camel’s [[dream-and-vision|dreaming]] eye.   

[028-044]Hush! hush! hush! they pause them where   

[028-045]Bows the Bedouin’s whitening hair﻿—   

[028-046]Peace of youth and peace of age,   

[028-047]Thoughtless joys and sorrows sage.

[028-048]If these merry ones should know,   

[028-049]Footing in the feeble glow,   

[028-050]Other people’s mirth and pleasure,   

[028-051]Would they foot so fleet a measure?   

[028-052]Ah no!

[028-053]Others know the healing earth,   

[028-054]Others know the starry mirth;   

[028-055]They will wrap them in the shroud,   

[028-056]Sorrow-worn, yet placid browed.   

[028-057]London streets have heritage   

[028-058]Blinder sorrows, harder wage﻿—   

[028-059]Sordid sorrows of the mart,   

[028-060]Sorrows sapping brain and heart.

[028-061]If these merry ones should know,   

[028-062]Footing in the feeble glow,   

[028-063]All the healing earth may treasure,   

[028-064]Would they foot so fleet a measure?   

[028-065]Ah no!
