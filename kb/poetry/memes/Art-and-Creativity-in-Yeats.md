# Art and Creativity in Yeats

Yeats thought deeply about the act of making — the poet as craftsman, the poem as "a mouthful of air," the image as a deliberate artifact rather than spontaneous overflow. This page aggregates all wiki entries that illuminate his lifelong meditation on art, poetry, and the creative imagination.

## About This Page

From the jeweled mosaics of Byzantium to the "artifice of eternity," from the Rose as a symbol of transcendent beauty to the careful architecture of his late verse, Yeats understood artistic creation as a form of knowledge — a way of seeing that rivals philosophy and religion. The entries below cover his poetic collections, his theories of the image and symbol, and the individual works that embody his evolving aesthetic.

For the symbolic system that underlies his art, see [[Symbolism-in-Yeats]] and [[Yeatsian-Symbolism]]. For the philosophical traditions that shaped his aesthetics, see [[Philosophy-in-Yeats]].
