# Life

[005-001]The child pursuing lizards in the grass,   

[005-002]The sage, who deep in central nature delves,   

[005-003]The preacher watching for the evil hour to pass,   

[005-004]All these are souls that fly from their dread selves.

[005-005]The squirrel yonder, hushed and wise   

[005-006]Forswears his wandering ’mong the pine,   

[005-007]And wherefore, then, should thy grey eyes   

[005-008]Wander away from mine?

[005-009]The talking winds have found their home,   

[005-010]Eve-soothed in some far leafy rest,   

[005-011]And wherefore should thy bright brow roam   

[005-012]Madonna from my breast?

[005-013]A little while and﻿—red eve dies﻿—   

[005-014]Our love shall be of yesterday,   

[005-015]Ah, let us kiss each other’s eyes,   

[005-016]And laugh our love away.

[005-017]“I laughed upon the lips of Sophocles,   

[005-018]I go as soft as folly; I am Fate.”   

[005-019]This heard I where among the apple trees,   

[005-020]Wild indolence and [[song-and-poetry|music]] have no date.
