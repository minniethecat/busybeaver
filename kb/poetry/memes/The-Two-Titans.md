# The Two Titans

[006-001]A Political Poem

[006-002]The vision of a [[stone-imagery|rock]] where lightnings whirl’d   

[006-003]Bruising the darkness with their crackling [[shadow-and-light|light]];   

[006-004]The waves, enormous wanderers of the world,   

[006-005]Beat on it with their hammers day and night.   

[006-006]Two figures crouching on the black rock, bound   

[006-007]To one another with a coiling chain;   

[006-008]A grey-haired youth, whose cheeks had never found,   

[006-009]Or long ere this had lost their ruddy stain;   

[006-010]A sibyl, with fierce face as of a hound   

[006-011]That dreams. She moveth, feeling in her brain   

[006-012]The lightnings pulse﻿—behold her, aye behold﻿—   

[006-013]Ignoble joy, and more ignoble pain   

[006-014]Cramm’d all her youth; and hates have bought and sold   

[006-015]Her [[soul-in-yeats|spirit]]. As she moves, the foam-globes burst   

[006-016]Over her spotted flesh and flying hair   

[006-017]And her gigantic limbs. The weary thirst   

[006-018]Unquenchable still glows in her dull stare,   

[006-019]As round her, slow on feet that have no blood,   

[006-020]The phantoms of her faded pleasures walk;   

[006-021]And trailing crimson vans, a mumbling brood,   

[006-022]Ghosts of her vanished glories, muse and stalk   

[006-023][[About]] the [[water-imagery|sea]]. Before her lies that youth,   

[006-024]Worn with long struggles; and the waves have sung   

[006-025]Their [[fire-passion|passion]] and their restlessness and ruth   

[006-026]Through his sad soul for ever old and young,   

[006-027]Till their fierce miseries within his eyes   

[006-028]Have lit lone tapers. Now the night was cast,   

[006-029]Making all one o’er rock and sea and skies;   

[006-030]And when once more the lightning Genii passed,   

[006-031]Strewing upon the rocks their steel-blue hair,   

[006-032]I saw him stagger with the clanking chain,   

[006-033]Trailing and shining ’neath the flickering glare.   

[006-034]With little cries of joy he kissed the rain   

[006-035]In creviced rocks, and laughed to the old sea,   

[006-036]And, nodding to and fro, sang songs of love,   

[006-037]And flowers and little children. Suddenly   

[006-038]Dropt down the velvet darkness from above,   

[006-039]Hiding away the ocean’s yelping flocks.   

[006-040]When flash on flash once more the lightning came,   

[006-041]The youth had flung his arms around the rocks,   

[006-042]And in the sibyl’s eyes a languid flame   

[006-043]Was moving. Bleeding now, his grasp unlocks,   

[006-044]And he is dragged again before her feet.   

[006-045]Why not? He is her own; and crouching nigh   

[006-046]Bending her face o’er his, she watches meet   

[006-047]And part his foaming mouth with eager eye﻿—

[006-048]To place a kiss of fire on the dim brow   

[006-049]Of Failure, and to crown her crownless head,   

[006-050]That all men evermore may humbly bow   

[006-051]Down to the mother of the foiled and dead.   

[006-052]For this did the Eternal Darkness bring   

[006-053]Thither thy dust, and knead it with a cry,   

[006-054]Gathered on her own lips, Oh youth, and fling   

[006-055]Failure for glory down on thee, and mould   

[006-056]Thy withered foe, and with the purple wing   

[006-057]Of ocean fan thee into life, and fold   

[006-058]For ever round thy waking and thy sleep   

[006-059]The darkness of the whirlwind shattered deep.
