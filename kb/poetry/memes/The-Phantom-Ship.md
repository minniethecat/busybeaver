# The Phantom Ship

[020-001]Flames the shuttle of the lightning across the driving sleet,   

[020-002]Ay, and shakes in [[water-imagery|sea]]-green waverings along the fishers’ street;   

[020-003]Gone the stars and gone the white moon, gone and puffed away and dead.   

[020-004]Never storm arose so swiftly; scarce the children were in bed,   

[020-005]Scarce the old and wizen houses had their doors and windows shut.   

[020-006]Ah! it dwelt within the twilight as the worm within the nut.   

[020-007]“Waken, waken, sleepy fishers; no hour is this for sleep,”   

[020-008]Cries a voice at roaring midnight beside the moonless deep.   

[020-009]Half dizzy with the lightning there runs a gathering band﻿—   

[020-010]“Watcher, wherefore have ye called us?” Eyes go after his lean hand,   

[020-011]And the fisher men and women from the dripping harbour wall   

[020-012]See the darkness slow disgorging a vessel blind with squall.   

[020-013]“Bring the ropes now! Stand ye by now! See, she rounds the harbour clear.   

[020-014]God! they’re mad to fly such canvas!” Ah! what bell-notes do they hear?   

[020-015]Say what ringer rings at midnight; for, in the belfry high,   

[020-016]Slow the chapel bell is tolling as though the dead passed by.   

[020-017]Round she comes in stays before them; cease the winds, and on their poles   

[020-018]Cease the sails their flapping uproar, and the hull no longer rolls.   

[020-019]Now a scream from all those fishers, for there on deck there be   

[020-020]All the drowned that ever were drowned from that village by the sea;   

[020-021]And the ghastly ghost-flames glimmer all along the taffrail rails   

[020-022]On the drowned men’s hands and faces, on the spars and on the sails.   

[020-023]Hush’d the fishers, till a mother calls by name her drownèd son;   

[020-024]Then each wife and maid and mother calls by name some drownèd one.   

[020-025]Stands each grey and silent phantom on the same regardless spot﻿—   

[020-026]Joys and fears in their grey faces that the live earth knoweth not;   

[020-027]Down the vapours fall and hide them from the children of a day,   

[020-028]And the winds come down and blow them with the vapours far away.   

[020-029]Hang the mist-threads for a little while like cobwebs in the air;   

[020-030]Then the stars grow out of heaven with their countenances fair.

[020-031]“Pray for the souls in purgatory,” the pale priest trembling cries.

[020-032]* * *

[020-033]Prayed those forgotten fishers, till in the eastern skies   

[020-034]Came olive fires of morning and on the darkness fed,   

[020-035]By the slow heaving ocean﻿—mumbling mother of the dead.
