# The Well and the Tree

[037-001]“The Man that I praise,”   

[037-002]Cries out the empty [[water-imagery|well]],   

[037-003]“Lives all his days   

[037-004]Where a hand on the bell   

[037-005]Can call the milch-cows   

[037-006]To the comfortable door of his house.   

[037-007]Who but an idiot would praise   

[037-008]Dry stones in a well?”

[037-009]“The Man that I praise,”   

[037-010]Cries out the leafless tree,   

[037-011]“Has married and stays   

[037-012]By an old hearth, and he   

[037-013]On naught has set store   

[037-014]But children and dogs on the floor.   

[037-015]Who but an idiot would praise   

[037-016]A withered tree?”
