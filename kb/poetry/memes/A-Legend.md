# A Legend

[027-001]A drowned city is supposed to lie under the waters of [[lough-gill|Lough Gill]].

[027-002]The Maker of the stars and worlds   

[027-003]Sat underneath the market cross,   

[027-004]And the old men were walking, walking,   

[027-005]And little boys played pitch and toss.

[027-006]“The props,” said He, “of stars and worlds   

[027-007]Are prayers of patient men and good.”   

[027-008]The boys, the women, and old men,   

[027-009]Listening, upon their shadows stood.

[027-010]A grey professor passing cried,   

[027-011]“How few the mind’s intemperance rule!   

[027-012]What shallow thoughts about deep things!   

[027-013]The world grows old and plays the fool.”

[027-014]The mayor came, leaning his deaf ear﻿—   

[027-015]There was some talking of the poor﻿—   

[027-016]And to himself cried, “Communist!”   

[027-017]And hurried to the guard-house door.

[027-018]The bishop came with open book,   

[027-019]Whispering along the sunny path;   

[027-020]There was some talking of man’s god,   

[027-021]His god of stupor and of wrath.

[027-022]The bishop murmured, “Atheist!   

[027-023]How sinfully the wicked scoff!”   

[027-024]And sent the old men on their way,   

[027-025]And drove the boys and women off.

[027-026]The place was empty now of people.   

[027-027]A cock came by upon his toes;   

[027-028]An old horse looked across a fence,   

[027-029]And rubbed along the rail his nose.

[027-030]The Maker of the stars and worlds   

[027-031]To His own house did him betake,   

[027-032]And on that city dropped a tear,   

[027-033]And now that city is a [[water-imagery|lake]].
