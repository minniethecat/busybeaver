## About This Page

Yeats's concern with death intensified with age — from the Celtic twilight's "hosting of the Sidhe" to the stark epitaph of "Under Ben Bulben." His system in *A Vision* frames death as a phase in the soul's cyclical journey. The elegies for friends and heroes trace a line from personal grief to national mourning, while poems like "Sailing to Byzantium" imagine art as a triumph over bodily decay.
