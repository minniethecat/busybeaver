# The Priest and the Fairy

[024-001]Unto the heart of the woodland straying,   

[024-002]Where the shaking leaf with the beam was playing,

[024-003]Musingly wandered the village priest.   

[024-004]As the summer voice of the daytime ceased,

[024-005]He came to the home of the forest people   

[024-006]From where the old ivy crawls round the old steeple,

[024-007]And under a shady oak-tree sat,   

[024-008]Where the moss was spread like his own doormat.

[024-009]The tangled thoughts of the finished day   

[024-010]Fled from his brow where the hair was grey;

[024-011]And as the time to darkness plodded,   

[024-012]He thought wise things as his grey head nodded.

[024-013]How “the only good is musing mild,   

[024-014]And evil still is action’s child.

[024-015]“With action all the world is vexed,”   

[024-016]He’d find for this some holy text.

[024-017]* * *

[024-018]He’d slept among the [[song-and-poetry|singing]] trees,   

[024-019]Among the murmurs of the bees,

[024-020]A full hour long, when rose a feather   

[024-021]Out of a neighbouring bunch of heather;

[024-022]And then a pointed face was seen   

[024-023]Beneath a pointed cap of green;

[024-024]And straight before the sleeping priest   

[024-025]There stood a man, of men the least﻿—

[024-026]Three spans high as he rose to his feet,   

[024-027]And his hair was as yellow as waving wheat.

[024-028]Now, what has a fairy to do with a priest   

[024-029]Who is six feet high in his socks at least?

[024-030]He drew from his cap a feather grey,   

[024-031]On the nose of the sleeper he made it play;

[024-032]The sleeper awoke with a sudden start,   

[024-033]With open mouth and beating heart.

[024-034]He had dreamed the cow had got within   

[024-035]His garden ringed with jessamine,

[024-036]And many a purple gillyflower eaten,   

[024-037]And under her hoofs the marigolds beaten.

[024-038]Then ’gan to speak that goblin rare,   

[024-039]Brushing back his yellow hair:

[024-040]“Man of wisdom, from thy sleeping   

[024-041]I have roused thee; for the weeping

[024-042]“Of our great queen is ever heard   

[024-043]Among the haunts of bee and bird.

[024-044]“We buried late in a hazel dell   

[024-045]A fairy whom we loved full [[water-imagery|well]];

[024-046]“The swiftest he to dance or fly,   

[024-047]And his hair was as dark as a plover’s eye.

[024-048]“Man of wisdom, dost thou know   

[024-049]Where the souls of fairies go?”

[024-050]This priest looked neither to right nor left,   

[024-051]Nigh of his wits by fear bereft.

[024-052]“Ave Marie,” muttered he   

[024-053]Over the beads of his rosary.

[024-054]The fairies’ herald spake once more:   

[024-055]“Say and thrice anigh thy door

[024-056]“Every summer wilt thou see   

[024-057]Wild bees’ honey laid for thee.”

[024-058]The father dropt his rosary﻿—   

[024-059]“They are lost, they are lost, each one,” cried he.

[024-060]And then his heart grew well-nigh dead   

[024-061]Because of the thing his tongue had said.

[024-062]As a wreath of smoke in wind-blown flight   

[024-063]The fairy vanished from his sight,

[024-064]And came to where his brethren stood,   

[024-065]Away in the heart of the antique wood;

[024-066]And when they heard that tale of his   

[024-067]They grew so very still, I wis

[024-068]Were you a fairy you’d have heard   

[024-069]The breathing of the smallest bird,

[024-070]The beating of a lev’ret’s heart;   

[024-071]And then the fay queen sobbed apart,

[024-072]And all the sad fay chivalry   

[024-073]Upraised their voices bitterly.

[024-074]* * *

[024-075]A woodman on his homeward way   

[024-076]Heard the voice of their dismay,

[024-077]And said, “Yon bittern cries, in truth,   

[024-078]As though his days were full of ruth.

[024-079]“If I were free to do as little   

[024-080]As dance upon the spear-grass brittle,

[024-081]“Or seek where sweetest water bubbles,   

[024-082]Remote from all the hard earth troubles,

[024-083]“And cut no wood the whole day long,   

[024-084]I’d glad folks’ hearts with blither song.”
