# A Lover’s Quarrel Among the Fairies

[021-001]A moonlit moor. Fairies leading a child.

[021-002]Male Fairies. |  Do not fear us, earthly maid!   

[021-003]We will lead you hand in hand   

[021-004]By the willows in the glade,   

[021-005]By the gorse on the high land, By the pasture where the lambs   

[021-006]Shall awake with lonely bleat,   

[021-007]Shivering closer to their dams   

[021-008]From the rustling of our feet. You will with the banshee chat,   

[021-009]And will find her good at heart,   

[021-010]Sitting on a warm smooth mat   

[021-011]In the green hill’s inmost part. We will bring a crown of gold,   

[021-012]Bending humbly every knee,   

[021-013]Now thy great white doll to hold﻿—   

[021-014]Oh, so happy would we be! Ah! it is so very big,   

[021-015]And we are so very small!   

[021-016]So we dance a fairy jig   

[021-017]To the fiddle’s rise and fall. Yonder see the fairy girls   

[021-018]All their jealousy display,   

[021-019]Lift their chins and toss their curls,   

[021-020]Lift their chins and turn away. See you, brother, Cranberry Fruit﻿—   

[021-021]Ho! ho! ho! the merry blade!﻿—   

[021-022]Hugs and pets and pats yon newt,   

[021-023]Teasing every wilful maid.  

[021-024]---|---  

[021-025]Girl Fairies. |  Lead they one with foolish care,   

[021-026]Deafening us with idle sound﻿—   

[021-027]One whose breathing shakes the air,   

[021-028]One whose footfall shakes the ground. Come you, Coltsfoot, Mousetail, come!   

[021-029]Come I know where, far away,   

[021-030]Owls there be whom age makes numb;   

[021-031]Come and tease them till the day Puffed like puff-balls on a tree,   

[021-032]Scoff they at the modern earth﻿—   

[021-033]Ah! how large mice used to be   

[021-034]In their days of youthful mirth! Come, beside a sandy [[water-imagery|lake]],   

[021-035]Feed a fire with stems of grass;   

[021-036]Roasting berries steam and shake﻿—   

[021-037]Talking hours swiftly pass! Long before the morning fire   

[021-038]Wake the larks upon the green.   

[021-039]Yonder foolish ones will tire   

[021-040]Of their tall, new-fangled queen. They will lead her home again   

[021-041]To the orchard-circled farm;   

[021-042]At the house of weary men   

[021-043]Raise the door-pin with alarm, And come kneeling on one knee,   

[021-044]While we shake our heads and scold   

[021-045]This their wanton treachery,   

[021-046]And our slaves be as of old.
