# The Seeker

[003-001]A Dramatic Poem in Two Scenes

##  Scene I

[003-002]A woodland valley at evening. Around a wood-fire sit **three shepherds** ; without a curve rises the smoke.

[003-003]First Shepherd |  Heavy with wool the sheep are gathered in,   

[003-004]And through the mansion of the [[soul-in-yeats|spirit]] rove   

[003-005]My dreams o’er thoughts of plenty as the red-   

[003-006]Eyed panthers in their desert caverns rove   

[003-007]And rove unceasing round their dreadful brood.  

[003-008]---|---  

[003-009]Second Shepherd |  O brother, lay thy flute upon thy lips,   

[003-010]It is the voice of all our hearts that laugh.  

[003-011]|  _The**first Shepherd** puts the flute to his lips; there comes from it a piercing cry. He drops it._  

[003-012]First Shepherd |  It is possessed.  

[003-013]Third Shepherd |  Nay, give it me, and I will sound a measure;   

[003-014]And unto it we’ll dance upon the sward.  

[003-015]|  _Puts it to his lips. A voice out of the flute still more mournful._  

[003-016]First Shepherd |  An omen!  

[003-017]Second Shepherd |  An omen!  

[003-018]Third Shepherd |  A creeping horror is all over me.  

[003-019]|  _Enter an**Old Knight**. They cast themselves down before him._  

[003-020]Knight |  Are all things [[water-imagery|well]] with you and with your sheep?  

[003-021]Second Shepherd |  Yes, all is very well.  

[003-022]First Shepherd |  Whence comest thou?  

[003-023]Knight |  Shepherds, I came this morning to your land   

[003-024]From threescore years of dream-led wandering   

[003-025]Where spice isles nestle on the star-trod seas,   

[003-026]And where the polar winds and waters wrestle   

[003-027]In endless dark, and by the weedy marge   

[003-028]Of India’s rivers, rolling on in [[shadow-and-light|light]].   

[003-029]But soon my wandering shall be done I know.   

[003-030]A voice has told me how within this land   

[003-031]There lies the long-lost forest of the sprite,   

[003-032]The sullen wood. But many woods I see   

[003-033]Where to themselves innumerable birds   

[003-034]Make moan and cry.  

[003-035]First Shepherd |  Within yon sunless valley   

[003-036]Between the horned hills﻿—  

[003-037]Knight |  Shepherds, farewell!   

[003-038]And peace be with you, peace and wealth of days.  

[003-039]Second Shepherd |  Seek not that wood, for there the goblin snakes   

[003-040]Go up and down, and raise their heads and sing   

[003-041]With little voices songs of fearful things.  

[003-042]Third Shepherd |  No shepherd foot has ever dared its depths.  

[003-043]First Shepherd |  The very squirrel dies that enters it.  

[003-044]Knight |  Shepherds, farewell!  

[003-045]|  _Goes._  

[003-046]Second Shepherd |  He soon will be  

[003-047]First Shepherd |  Ashes   

[003-048]Before the wind.  

[003-049]Third Shepherd |  Saw you his eyes a-glitter,   

[003-050]His body shake?  

[003-051]Second Shepherd |  Aye, quivering as yon smoke   

[003-052]That from the fire is ever pouring up,   

[003-053]Within the woodways, blue as the halcyon’s wing,   

[003-054]Star-envious.  

[003-055]Third Shepherd |  He was a spirit, brother.  

[003-056]Second Shepherd |  The blessed God was good to send us such,   

[003-057]To make us glad with wonder as we sat   

[003-058]Weary of watching round the fire at night.  
  
##  Scene II

[003-059]A ruined palace in the forest. Away in the depth of the shadow of the pillars a motionless **Figure**.

[003-060]|  _Enter the**Old Knight**._  

[003-061]---|---  

[003-062]Knight |  Behold I bend before thee to the ground   

[003-063]Until my beard is in the twisted leaves   

[003-064]That with their fiery ruin fill the hall,   

[003-065]As words of thine through fourscore years have filled   

[003-066]My echoing heart. Now raise thy voice and speak!   

[003-067]Even from boyhood, in my father’s house,   

[003-068]That was beside the waterfall, thy words   

[003-069]Abode as banded adders in my breast.   

[003-070]Thou knowest this, and how from mid the dance   

[003-071]Thou called’st me forth, And how thou madest me   

[003-072]A coward in the field; and all men cried:   

[003-073]Behold the Knight of the Waterfall, whose heart   

[003-074]The spirits stole, and gave him in its stead   

[003-075]A peering hare’s; and yet I murmured not,   

[003-076]Knowing that thou hadst singled me with word   

[003-077]Of love from out a dreamless race for strife,   

[003-078]Through miseries unhuman ever on   

[003-079]To joys unhuman, and to thee﻿—Speak! Speak  

[003-080]|  _He draws nearer to the**Figure**. A pause._  

[003-081]|  Behold I bend before thee to the ground;   

[003-082]Thou wilt not speak, and I with age am near   

[003-083]To [[death-in-yeats|Death]]. His lips are glued, with quivering touch,   

[003-084]To mine, and he is slowly sucking forth   

[003-085]My soul. His darkness and his chill I feel.   

[003-086]Were all my wandering days of no avail   

[003-087]Untouched of human joy or human love?   

[003-088]Then let me see thy face before I die.   

[003-089]Behold I bow before thee to the ground!   

[003-090]Behold I bow! Around my beard in drifts   

[003-091]Lie strewn the clotted leaves﻿—the dead old leaves.  

[003-092]|  _He gathers up the leaves and presses them to his breast._  

[003-093]|  Thou wilt not speak, Oh cruel art thou yet!   

[003-094]Mine heart-strings are all broken saving one,   

[003-095]That trembles and resounds with hymns to thee,   

[003-096]That fill the blazing hollows of my heart.   

[003-097]I’m dying! Oh forgive me if I touch   

[003-098]Thy garment’s hem, thou visionary one!  

[003-099]|  _He approaches close to the**Figure**. A sudden light bursts over it._  

[003-100]Knight |  A bearded witch, her sluggish head low bent   

[003-101]On her broad breast! beneath her withered brows   

[003-102]Shine dull unmoving eyes. What thing art thou?   

[003-103]I sought thee not.  

[003-104]Figure |  Men call me Infamy.   

[003-105]I know not what I am.  

[003-106]Knight |  I sought thee not.  

[003-107]Figure |  Lover, the voice that summoned thee was mine.  

[003-108]Knight |  For all I gave the voice, for all my youth,   

[003-109]For all my joy.﻿—Ah woe!  

[003-110]|  _The**Figure** raises a mirror in which the face and the form of the **Knight** are shadowed. He falls forward._  

[003-111]The Figure |  _Bending over him and speaking in his ear._ What! Lover, die before our lips have met?  

[003-112]Knight |  Again, the voice! the voice!  

[003-113]|  _Dies._
