# Love and Death

[002-001]Behold the flashing waters,   

[002-002]A cloven, [[dance-in-yeats|dancing]] jet,   

[002-003]That from the milk-white [[stone-imagery|marble]]   

[002-004]For ever foam and fret;   

[002-005]Far off in drowsy valleys   

[002-006]Where the meadow-saffrons blow,   

[002-007]The feet of summer dabble   

[002-008]In their coiling calm and slow.   

[002-009]The banks are worn for ever   

[002-010]By a people sadly gay:   

[002-011]A Titan, with loud laughter,   

[002-012]Made them of fire and clay.   

[002-013]Go ask the springing flowers,   

[002-014]And the flowing air above,   

[002-015]What are the twin-born waters,   

[002-016]And they’ll answer [[death-in-yeats|Death]] and [[love-in-yeats|Love]].

[002-017]With wreaths of withered flowers   

[002-018]Two lonely spirits wait,   

[002-019]With wreaths of withered flowers,   

[002-020]’Fore paradise’s gate.   

[002-021]They may not pass the portal,   

[002-022]Poor earth-enkindled pair,   

[002-023]Though sad is many a [[soul-in-yeats|spirit]]   

[002-024]Το pass and leave them there   

[002-025]Still staring at their flowers,   

[002-026]That dull and faded are.   

[002-027]If one should rise beside thee,   

[002-028]The other is not far.   

[002-029]Go ask the youngest angel,   

[002-030]She will say with bated breath,   

[002-031]By the door of Mary’s garden   

[002-032]Are the spirits Love and Death.
