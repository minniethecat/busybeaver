# The Fairy Pedant

[025-001]**Scene:** ﻿—A circle of Druidic stones.

[025-002]First [[fairy-and-faery-lore|Fairy]] |  Afar from our lawn and our levee,   

[025-003]O sister of sorrowful gaze!   

[025-004]Where the roses in scarlet are heavy   

[025-005]And dream of the end of their days,   

[025-006]You move in another dominion   

[025-007]And hang o’er the historied stone:   

[025-008]Unpruned is your beautiful pinion   

[025-009]Who wander and whisper alone.  

[025-010]---|---  

[025-011]All |  Come away while the moon’s in the woodland,   

[025-012]We’ll dance and then feast in a dairy.   

[025-013]Though youngest of all in our good band,   

[025-014]You are wasting away, little fairy.  

[025-015]Second Fairy |  Ah! cruel ones, leave me alone now   

[025-016]While I murmur a little and ponder   

[025-017]The history here in the stone now;   

[025-018]Then away and away will I wander,   

[025-019]And measure the minds of the flowers,   

[025-020]And gaze on the meadow-mice wary,   

[025-021]And number their days and their hours﻿—  

[025-022]All |  You are wasting away, little fairy.  

[025-023]Second Fairy |  O shining ones, lightly with song pass,   

[025-024]Ah! leave me, I pray you and beg.   

[025-025]My mother drew forth from the long grass   

[025-026]A piece of a nightingale’s egg,   

[025-027]And cradled me here where are sung,   

[025-028]Of birds even, longings for aery   

[025-029]Wild wisdoms of [[soul-in-yeats|spirit]] and tongue.  

[025-030]All |  You are wasting away, little fairy.  

[025-031]First Fairy |  _Turning away._ Though tenderest roses were round you,   

[025-032]The soul of the pitiless place   

[025-033]With pitiless magic has bound you﻿—   

[025-034]Ah! woe for the loss of your face,   

[025-035]And loss of your laugh and its lightness﻿—   

[025-036]Ah! woe for your wings and your head﻿—   

[025-037]Ah! woe for your eyes and their brightness﻿—   

[025-038]Ah! woe for your slippers of red.  

[025-039]All |  Come away while the moon’s in the woodland,   

[025-040]We’ll dance and then feast in a dairy.   

[025-041]Though youngest of all in our good band,   

[025-042]She is wasting away, little fairy.
