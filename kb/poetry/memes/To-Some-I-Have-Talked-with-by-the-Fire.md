# To Some I Have Talked with by the Fire

[033-001]While I wrought out these fitful Danaan rhymes,   

[033-002]My heart would brim with dreams about the times   

[033-003]When we bent down above the fading coals;   

[033-004]And talked of the dark folk, who live in souls   

[033-005]Of passionate men, like bats in the dead trees;   

[033-006]And of the wayward twilight companies,   

[033-007]Who sigh with mingled sorrow and content,   

[033-008]Because their blossoming dreams have never bent   

[033-009]Under the fruit of evil and of good:   

[033-010]And of the embattled flaming multitude   

[033-011]Who rise, wing above wing, [[fire-passion|flame]] above flame,   

[033-012]And, like a storm, cry the Ineffable Name,   

[033-013]And with the clashing of their sword blades make   

[033-014]A rapturous [[song-and-poetry|music]], till the morning break,   

[033-015]And the white hush end all, but the loud beat   

[033-016]Of their long wings, the flash of their white feet.
