# Philosophy in Yeats

Yeats engaged deeply with Western and Eastern philosophical traditions — Platonic idealism, Neoplatonism, the thought of Plotinus and Blake, and his own systematic framework in *A Vision*. This page aggregates all wiki entries exploring these philosophical dimensions.

## About This Page

Yeats's philosophical interests were not academic exercises — they directly shaped his poetic imagery, his understanding of the soul, and his theory of history. Platonic forms appear as eternal archetypes in his verse; Neoplatonic emanation becomes a model for artistic creation; and Blake's visionary philosophy provides a template for the poet as prophet.

For the occult and mystical dimensions of Yeats's thought, see [[Mysticism-and-Occult]]. For his systematic cosmology, see [[A-Vision-Hub]].
