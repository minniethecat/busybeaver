## About This Page

Yeats's poetry is saturated with the supernatural — not as decorative whimsy but as lived conviction. From the "host of the Sidhe" riding the wind in his early Celtic verse to the spirit instructors of *A Vision*, from peasant fairy doctors to séance tables, the border between this world and the Otherworld was, for Yeats, always permeable. This page gathers pages that touch that border — distinct from the occult systems and mystical philosophies catalogued elsewhere, focusing instead on the presence of the uncanny in the poems themselves.
