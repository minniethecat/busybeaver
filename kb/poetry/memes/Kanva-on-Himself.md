# Kanva on Himself

[019-001]Now wherefore hast thou tears innumerous?   

[019-002]Hast thou not known all sorrow and delight   

[019-003]Wandering of yore in forests rumorous,   

[019-004]Beneath the flaming eyeballs of the night,

[019-005]And as a slave been wakeful in the halls   

[019-006]Of Rajas and Mahrajas beyond number?   

[019-007]Hast thou not ruled among the gilded walls?   

[019-008]Hast thou not known a Raja’s dreamless slumber?

[019-009]Hast thou not sat of yore upon the knees   

[019-010]Of myriads of beloveds, and on thine   

[019-011]Have not a myriad swayed below strange trees   

[019-012]In other lives? Hast thou not quaffed old wine

[019-013]By tables that were fallen into dust   

[019-014]Ere yonder palm commenced his thousand years?   

[019-015]Is not thy body but the garnered rust   

[019-016]Of ancient passions and of ancient fears?

[019-017]Then wherefore fear the usury of Time,   

[019-018]Or [[death-in-yeats|Death]] that cometh with the next life-key?   

[019-019]Nay, rise and flatter her with golden rhyme,   

[019-020]For as things were so shall things ever be.
