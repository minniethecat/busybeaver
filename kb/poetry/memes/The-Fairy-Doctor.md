# The Fairy Doctor

[023-001]The fairy doctor comes our way   

[023-002]Over the sorrel-covered wold﻿—   

[023-003]Now sadly, now unearthly gay,   

[023-004]A little withered man, and old.

[023-005]He knows by signs of secret wit   

[023-006]The man whose hour of death draws nigh,   

[023-007]And who will moan in the under pit,   

[023-008]And who foregather in the sky.

[023-009]He sees the fairy hosting move   

[023-010]By heath or hollow or rushy mere,   

[023-011]And then his heart fills full of love,   

[023-012]And full his eyes of fairy cheer.

[023-013]Cures he hath for cow or goat   

[023-014]With fairy-smitten udders dry﻿—   

[023-015]Cures for calves with ’plaining throat,   

[023-016]That sickening near their mothers lie;

[023-017]And many a herb and many a spell   

[023-018]For hurts and ails and lover’s moan﻿—   

[023-019]For all save him who pining fell,   

[023-020]Glamoured by fairies for their own.

[023-021]Now be courteous, now be kind,   

[023-022]Lest he may some glamour fold   

[023-023]Closely round us, body and mind﻿—   

[023-024]The little withered man, and old.
