# The Lamentation of the Old Pensioner

[014-001]I had a chair at every hearth,   

[014-002]When no one turned to see,   

[014-003]With “Look at that old fellow there,   

[014-004]And who may he be?”   

[014-005]And therefore do I wander now,   

[014-006]And the fret lies on me.

[014-007]The road-side trees keep murmuring   

[014-008]Ah, wherefore murmur ye,   

[014-009]As in the old days long gone by,   

[014-010]Green oak and poplar tree?   

[014-011]The [[water-imagery|well]]-known faces are all gone   

[014-012]And the fret lies on me.
