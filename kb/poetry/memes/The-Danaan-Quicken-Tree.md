# The Danaan Quicken Tree[2](endnotes.xhtml#note-2)

[016-001]Beloved, hear my bitter tale!﻿—   

[016-002]Now making busy with the oar,   

[016-003]Now flinging loose the slanting sail,   

[016-004]I hurried from the woody shore,   

[016-005]And plucked small fruits on [[innisfree-isle|Innisfree]].   

[016-006](Ah, mournful Danaan quicken tree!)

[016-007]A murmuring faery multitude,   

[016-008]When flying to the heart of [[shadow-and-light|light]]   

[016-009]From playing hurley in the wood   

[016-010]With creatures of our heavy night,   

[016-011]A berry threw for me﻿—or thee.   

[016-012](Ah, mournful Danaan quicken tree!)

[016-013]And thereon grew a tender root,   

[016-014]And thereon grew a tender stem,   

[016-015]And thereon grew the ruddy fruit   

[016-016]That are a poison to all men   

[016-017]And meat to the Aslauga Shee.   

[016-018](Ah, mournful Danaan quicken tree!)

[016-019]If when the battle is half won,   

[016-020]I fling away my sword, blood dim,   

[016-021]Or leave some service all undone,   

[016-022]Beloved, blame the Danaan whim,   

[016-023]And blame the snare they set for me.   

[016-024](Ah, mournful Danaan quicken tree!)

[016-025]Cast out all hope, cast out all fear,   

[016-026]And taste with me the faeries’ meat,   

[016-027]For while I blamed them I could hear   

[016-028]Dark Joan call the berries sweet,   

[016-029]Where Niam heads the revelry.   

[016-030](Ah, mournful Danaan quicken tree!)
