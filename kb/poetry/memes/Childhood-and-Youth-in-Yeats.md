# Childhood and Youth in Yeats

"Sligo was my school," Yeats wrote — and indeed, the landscapes, people, and stories of his early years supplied the imaginative material for a lifetime of poetry. This page brings together all wiki entries that illuminate the poet's formative years, from his family's artistic milieu to the western landscapes of his summers.

## About This Page

Yeats was born in Dublin but made in Sligo. His mother's family, the Pollexfens, gave him the west of Ireland; his father John Butler Yeats gave him art, talk, and a model of the contemplative life; the stories of the local people gave him the fairies, the sidhe, and the sense that the world was "full of invisible presences." The entries below trace these early influences — the places, people, and experiences that precede the famous poet.

For the mature poet's engagement with place, see [[Connacht-and-West-of-Ireland]]. For the occult and folkloric traditions he absorbed in youth, see [[Supernatural-and-Otherworldly-in-Yeats]] and [[Mysticism-and-Occult]].
