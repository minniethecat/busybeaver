# Yeatsian Symbolism

Yeats built a personal symbolic vocabulary across his career, drawing on Irish mythology, occult tradition, Neoplatonic philosophy, and his own visionary experiences. Symbols like the rose, the tower, the swan, and the gyre recur across decades of poems, accruing layered meanings. This page indexes the wiki's treatment of these symbols.
