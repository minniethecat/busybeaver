# Quatrains and Aphorisms

## I

[029-001]The child who chases lizards in the grass,   

[029-002]The sage who deep in central nature delves,   

[029-003]The preacher watching for the ill hour to pass﻿—   

[029-004]All these are souls who fly from their dread selves.

## II

[029-005]Two [[soul-in-yeats|spirit]]-things a man hath for his friends﻿—   

[029-006]Sorrow, that gives for guerdon liberty,   

[029-007]And joy, the touching of whose finger lends   

[029-008]To lightest of all [[shadow-and-light|light]] things sanctity.

## III

[029-009]Long thou for nothing, neither sad nor gay;   

[029-010]Long thou for nothing, neither night nor day;   

[029-011]Not even “I long to see thy longing over,”   

[029-012]To the ever-longing and mournful spirit say.

## IV

[029-013]The ghosts went by me with their lips apart   

[029-014]From death’s late languor as these lines I read   

[029-015]On Brahma’s gateway, “They within have fed   

[029-016]The soul upon the ashes of the heart.”

## V

[029-017]This heard I where, amid the apple trees,   

[029-018]Wild indolence and [[song-and-poetry|music]] have no date,   

[029-019]“I laughed upon the lips of Sophocles,   

[029-020]I go as soft as folly; I am Fate.”

## VI

[029-021]“Around, the twitter of the lips of dust   

[029-022]A tossing laugh between their red abides;   

[029-023]With patient beauty yonder Attic bust   

[029-024]In the deep alcove’s dimness smiles and hides.”

## VII

[029-025]The heart of noon folds silence and folds sleep,   

[029-026]For noon and midnight from each other borrow,   

[029-027]And Joy, in growing deeper and more deep,   

[029-028]Walks in the vesture of her sister Sorrow.
