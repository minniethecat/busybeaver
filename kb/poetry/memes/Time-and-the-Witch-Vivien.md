# Time and the Witch Vivien

[018-001]A [[stone-imagery|marble]]-flagged, pillared room. Magical instruments in one corner. A [[water-imagery|fountain]] in the centre.

[018-002]Vivien |  _Looking down into the fountain._ Where moves there any beautiful as I,   

[018-003]Save, with the little golden greedy carp,   

[018-004]Gold unto gold, a gleam in its long hair,   

[018-005]My image yonder? _Spreading her hand over the water._ Ah, my beautiful,   

[018-006]What roseate fingers! _Turning away._ No; nor is there one   

[018-007]Of equal power in spells and secret rites.   

[018-008]The proudest or most coy of [[soul-in-yeats|spirit]] things,   

[018-009]Hide where he will, in wave or wrinkled moon,   

[018-010]Obeys. Some fierce magician flies or walks   

[018-011]Beyond the gateway﻿—by the sentries now﻿—   

[018-012]Close and more close﻿—I feel him in my heart﻿—   

[018-013]Some great one. No; I hear the wavering steps   

[018-014]Without there of a little, [[shadow-and-light|light]] old man;   

[018-015]I dreamt some great one. _Catching sight of her image, and spreading her hand over the water._   

[018-016]Ah, my beautiful,   

[018-017]What roseate fingers!  

[018-018]---|---  

[018-019]|  _Enter**Time** as an old pedlar, with a scythe, an hour-glass, and a black bag._  

[018-020]|  Ha, ha! ha, ha, ha!   

[018-021]The wrinkled squanderer of human wealth.   

[018-022]Come here. Be seated now; I’d buy of you.   

[018-023]Come, father.  

[018-024]Time |  Lady, I nor rest nor sit.  

[018-025]Vivien |  Well then, to business; what is in your bag?  

[018-026]Time |  _Putting the bag and hour-glass on the table and resting on his scythe._ Grey hairs and crutches, crutches and grey hairs,   

[018-027]Mansions of memories and mellow thoughts   

[018-028]Where dwell the minds of old men having peace,   

[018-029]And﻿—  

[018-030]Vivien |  No; I’ll none of these, old Father Wrinkles.  

[018-031]Time |  Some day you’ll buy them, maybe.  

[018-032]Vivien |  Never!  

[018-033]Time | _Laughing._ Never?  

[018-034]Vivien |  Why do you laugh?  

[018-035]Time |  I laugh the last always.  

[018-036]|  _She lays the hour-glass on one side.**Time** rights it again._  

[018-037]Vivien |  I do not need your scythe. May that bring peace   

[018-038]To those your “mellow” wares have wearied out.   

[018-039]I’d buy your glass.  

[018-040]Time |  My glass I will not sell.   

[018-041]Without my glass I’d be a sorry clown.  

[018-042]Vivien |  Yet whiter beard have you than Merlin had.  

[018-043]Time |  No taste have I for slumber ’neath an oak.  

[018-044]Vivien |  When were you born?  

[018-045]Time |  Before your grandam Eve.  

[018-046]Vivien |  Oh, I am weary of that foolish tale.   

[018-047]They say you are a gambler and a player   

[018-048]At chances and at moments with mankind.   

[018-049]I’ll play you for your old hour-glass. _Pointing to the instruments of magic._ You see   

[018-050]I keep such things about me; they are food   

[018-051]For antiquarian meditation.  

[018-052]|  _Brings dice._  

[018-053]Time |  Ay,   

[018-054]We throw three times.  

[018-055]Vivien |  Three-six.  

[018-056]Time |  Four-six.  

[018-057]Vivien |  Five-six. Ha, Time!  

[018-058]Time |  Double sixes!  

[018-059]Vivien |  I lose! They’re loaded dice. Time always plays   

[018-060]With loaded dice. Another chance! Come, father;   

[018-061]Come to the chess, for young girls’ wits are better   

[018-062]Than old men’s any day, as Merlin found.  

[018-063]|  _Places the chess-board on her knees._  

[018-064]|  The passing of those little grains is snow   

[018-065]Upon my soul, old Time.  

[018-066]|  _She lays the hour-glass on its side._  

[018-067]Vivien |  No; thus it stands. _Rights it again._   

[018-068]For other stakes we play. You lost the glass.  

[018-069]Vivien |  Then give me triumph in my many plots.  

[018-070]Time |  Defeat is death.  

[018-071]Vivien |  Should my plots fail I’d die.  

[018-072]|  _They play._  

[018-073]|  Thus play we first with pawns, poor things and weak;   

[018-074]And then the great ones come, and last the king.   

[018-075]So men in life and I in magic play;   

[018-076]First dreams, and goblins, and the lesser sprites,   

[018-077]And now with Father Time I’m face to face.  

[018-078]|  _They play._  

[018-079]|  I trap you.  

[018-080]Time |  Check.  

[018-081]Vivien |  I do miscalculate.   

[018-082]I am dull to-day, or you were now all lost.   

[018-083]Chance, and not skill, has favoured you, old father!  

[018-084]|  _She plays._  

[018-085]Vivien |  Check.  

[018-086]Vivien |  Ah! how bright your eyes. How swift your moves.   

[018-087]How still it is! I hear the carp go splash,   

[018-088]And now and then a bubble rise. I hear   

[018-089]A bird walk on the doorstep.  

[018-090]|  _She plays._  

[018-091]Time |  Check once more.  

[018-092]Vivien |  I must be careful now. I have such plots﻿—   

[018-093]Such war plots, peace plots, love plots﻿—every side;   

[018-094]I cannot go into the bloodless land   

[018-095]Among the whimpering ghosts.  

[018-096]Time |  Mate thus.  

[018-097]Vivien |  Already?   

[018-098]Chance hath a skill!  

[018-099]|  _She dies._
