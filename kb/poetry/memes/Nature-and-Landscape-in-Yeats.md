## About This Page

From the "Lake Isle of Innisfree" to the wild swans at Coole, from Ben Bulben's stark profile to the hazel woods of Irish myth, nature in Yeats is never mere backdrop — it is an active participant in the symbolic drama. The entries below cover real Irish places, symbolic landscapes, animal imagery, and the elemental forces (water, fire, air) that recur throughout his work.
