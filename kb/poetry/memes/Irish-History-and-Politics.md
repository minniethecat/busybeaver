## About This Page

From the failed Fenian uprising his father witnessed, through the Easter Rising he memorialized in "Easter 1916," to the Civil War that darkened his later verse, Yeats's poetry is inseparable from Ireland's struggle for independence. The Abbey Theatre, which he co-founded with Lady Gregory and John Synge, was itself a political and cultural institution — a platform for a distinctively Irish dramatic voice.

The entries below cover key events (the Easter Rising), movements (Irish nationalism, the Literary Revival), organizations (the Abbey Theatre), and the political figures who crossed paths with the poet — Maud Gonne the revolutionary, Constance Markievicz the rebel countess, John MacBride the executed martyr, and others.
