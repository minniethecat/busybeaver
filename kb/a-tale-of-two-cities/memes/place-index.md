## About the Places

Dickens uses setting as more than backdrop—the twin cities of London and Paris function as narrative foils, each embodying distinct political and moral atmospheres. The places indexed here range from public squares and courtrooms to private homes and prisons, each mapped to its chapter appearances and thematic significance on its dedicated page.
