Woodford Bridge is a suburban village northeast of Ilford, in what was historically part of Essex and is now within Greater London. In the novel, it appears as a destination for Bertha, one of the figures in Mary's social circle during her maturity.

## Bertha's Destination

Woodford Bridge is mentioned in connection with Bertha, who goes there and returns on the third day. The distance from Ilford is sufficient to require hiring a wagonette, suggesting it lies several miles away, beyond convenient walking distance.[^1] The expense of hiring transport is cited as a constraint on visiting: "Because I can't afford to be always hiring wagonettes to go to Woodford Bridge."[^2]

> On the third day Bertha went back to Woodford Bridge. [015-040]

## Geography and Transport

The need for a wagonette places Woodford Bridge in the network of villages and suburbs connected by road to Ilford. In the late Victorian period, Woodford Bridge was a semi-rural area on the edge of Epping Forest, accessible from Ilford via the roads through Barkingside and Chadwell Heath. The journey would have passed through the suburban-rural fringe landscape that Mary frequently traverses and observes.
