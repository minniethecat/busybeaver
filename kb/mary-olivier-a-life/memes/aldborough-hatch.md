Aldborough Hatch is a village near Ilford in Essex, significant in Mary Olivier's childhood for its church and vicarage, which serve as social and religious centres for the Olivier family and their acquaintances.

## The Church

Aldborough Hatch Church is a whitewashed building with clear windows, distinct from the older, darker parish churches of the period. Mary finds comfort in its brightness, preferring it to other local churches. She is not initially allowed to attend services there because of "Mr. Propart's pupils" — local boys who pose a risk of spreading whooping-cough to the children at Dr. Barnardo's Homes.[^1] This restriction places Aldborough Hatch in a network of local institutions that includes the Barkingside church and the Barnardo's orphanage.

> They were not allowed to go to Aldborough Hatch Church because of Mr. Propart's pupils. [008-004]

Mary develops a genuine affection for the church. She reflects that "if the darkness does come it won't be so bad to bear at Aldborough Hatch," liking "the new whitewashed church with the clear windows, where you could stand on the hassock and look out at the green hill framed in the white arch."[^2]

> She thought: "If the darkness does come it won't be so bad to bear at Aldborough Hatch." She liked the new whitewashed church with the clear windows. [008-093]

## Social Connections

The Vicarage at Aldborough Hatch is a social destination. Uncle Victor and Aunt Lavvy and Aunt Charlotte attend gatherings there, along with the "big boys from Vinings."[^3] The church and vicarage form part of the rural-suburban social circuit that connects the Olivier family's Ilford home with the surrounding gentry and clergy households.

> Uncle Victor and Aunt Lavvy and Aunt Charlotte were there, and the big boys from Vinings and the Vicarage at Aldborough Hatch. [008-024]

## Geography and Views

Aldborough Hatch sits in a landscape of fields, roads, and open views. From Chadwell Grange, one can look beyond Aldborough Hatch towards Wanstead Flats and the City of London Cemetery.[^4] The Aldborough Road runs through the area, mentioned when Mark recalls seeing Mr. Propart attempting to jump a ditch along it.[^5] Mary later imagines, with a child's apocalyptic logic, the possibility that "Barkingside church and Aldborough Hatch church fell to bits and there were no more clergymen."[^6]
