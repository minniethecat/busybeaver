# Mr. Peacock

**Mr. Peacock** of Sarrack is the oldest inhabitant of the Dale, a figure whose great age makes him a local landmark. His [[funerals|death]] marks a generational turning point in the community.

## The Oldest Inhabitant

Mr. Peacock's longevity is a defining feature of his identity. At the Kendal gatherings, [[miss-kendal|Miss Kendal]] remarks that Mr. Peacock of Sarrack is the one person older than everyone else, adding with affectionate humour: "Don't you forget Mr. Peacock of Sarrack, or he'll be so upset."[024-258][024-259]

## Death and Legacy

Mr. Peacock's [[funerals|death]] is noted as a significant event: "Old Mr. Peacock of Sarrack was dead, and [[old-dr-kendal|Dr. Kendal]] was the oldest man in the Dale."[030-001] The passing of the title of "oldest inhabitant" from Mr. Peacock to Dr. Kendal marks the gradual passing of a generation in the rural community.

Later, when [[old-dr-kendal|Dr. Kendal]] himself dies, it is noted that "He had beaten Mr. Peacock of Sarrack"[030-181] — a wry observation on the quiet competition of longevity between the two old men of the Dale.
