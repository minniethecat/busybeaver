# Mr. James

**Mr. James** is a figure in the Kendal social circle, frequently present at the family gatherings hosted by the Kendals. He is notable for his short stature and his childlike mind.

## Appearance and Manner

Mr. James is described as the only short person among the Kendal gathering.[024-249] He walks with a "queer, jerky movement," with his arms bowed out from his sides.[024-388]

[[mary-olivier|Mary Olivier]] observes him with a mixture of curiosity and unease. She learns that Mr. James's mind "hadn't grown since he was a child,"[024-390] a fact that prompts her to reflect on her own fears: "If I stay here I shall look like Mr. James. I shall walk with my arms bowed out."[024-391]

## At the Gatherings

At the Kendal dinner table, Mr. James sits at [[mary-olivier|Mary]]'s corner, beside her.[024-269] The family treats him with a protective, slightly patronizing affection. When someone recounts his achievements — "He walked all the way to Garthdale and back, eleven miles" — Mr. James "would look gratified."[024-271][024-272]

He is later encountered on the Garthdale Road, still walking.[033-007]

## Role

Mr. James represents one of the village "characters" that populate the social landscape of the novel. His presence contributes to [[mary-olivier|Mary]]'s growing awareness of the limitations and peculiarities of those around her, and her fear of being trapped in a narrow provincial life.
