## Description

England appears in the novel not as a described landscape but as an idea — a national identity invoked in patriotic rhetoric, contrasted with foreign destinations, and framing the domestic world of the Olivier family. It is the England of Empire: "the man who saved England" is [[emilius-olivier|Papa]]'s phrase, linking national identity to military service and imperial duty. [[PN=006-089]]

## National Identity

The England of the novel is defined by its contrasts. [[mark-olivier|Mark]] is going to [[india|India]]; the French Army is a hypothetical alternative to English military service; the imperial map draws Englishmen outward while English women remain at home. [[PN=009-017]] England is the centre from which imperial journeys depart and to which soldiers return — [[mark-olivier|Mark]]'s promotion brings him back to Woolwich, back to England, back within reach of home. [[PN=029-222]]

The patriotic language of "saving England" belongs to [[emilius-olivier|Papa]]'s generation, the rhetoric of imperial masculinity that shapes the expectations placed on [[mark-olivier|Mark]] and [[roddy-olivier|Roddy]]. England is what one serves, what one saves, what one leaves and returns to. [[PN=006-089]]
