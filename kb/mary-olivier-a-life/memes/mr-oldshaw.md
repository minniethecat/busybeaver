## Role and Significance

Mr. Oldshaw is a minor character in the novel, appearing as the banker of [[morfe|Morfe]]. He is consistently listed among the village's prominent residents — the "worthies" whose social standing defines the community's hierarchy. His name recurs in passages that sketch the topography of [[morfe|Morfe]] society, marking him as part of the institutional fabric within which the Olivier family's story unfolds.

## Appearances in the Novel

### The Morfe Worthies

Mr. Oldshaw first appears in a roll call of [[morfe|Morfe]] residents whose properties and establishments delineate the village's geography. He is grouped alongside Mr. Horn, the grocer, and the Kendals, his position as banker giving him a distinct professional identity within the community [024-349]. This listing is repeated in later passages, confirming his established place: "Mr. Horn, the grocer, and Mr. Oldshaw at the bank" are paired as fixtures of [[morfe|Morfe]]'s commercial life [028-001]. His bank is one of the key locations that map the economic relations of the village.

### Agent for Greffington Hall

Mr. Oldshaw's most concrete role in the plot involves his work as an agent. The agent at [[greffington|Greffington Hall]], he passes information to Mr. Horn regarding Major Frank, who "would be back" from somewhere [031-333]. This brief action — relaying news — situates Mr. Oldshaw within the novel's larger pattern of information circulation, where professional intermediaries (bankers, agents, solicitors) play a recurring role in moving events forward behind the scenes.

## Character and Social Position

As a banker and agent, Mr. Oldshaw occupies a middle tier in [[morfe|Morfe]]'s social structure — below the landed gentry but above the tradesmen and labourers. His professional functions (banking, agency work) place him at the intersection of economic and social life, giving him access to information about property, money, and the movements of notable persons. His role resembles that of other intermediary figures in the novel — [[mr-belk|Mr. Belk]] (Justice of the Peace), [[mr-propart|Mr. Propart]] ([[church-and-chapel|church]] organist) — who collectively form the institutional backbone of village society.
