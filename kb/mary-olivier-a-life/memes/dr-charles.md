## Overview

Dr. Charles Kendal is the son of [[old-dr-kendal|Old Dr. Kendal]] and the principal medical man of [[morfe|Morfe]]. He is physically striking — "a hard, blond giant" — and socially dominant, taking charge of rooms that are not his own, yet beneath his "savage, shaggy eyebrows" there is a kindness that the community, and particularly [[mary-olivier]], comes to recognise.

## Physical Presence

[[mary-olivier|Mary]]'s first description of Dr. Charles is vivid and unsparing: "a hard, blond giant; high cheeks, raw ruddied; high bleak nose jutting out with a steep fall to the long upper lip; savage mouth under a straight blond fringe, a shark's keen tooth pointing at the dropped jaw. Arched forehead drooping to the spread ears, blond eyebrows drooping over slack lids" [024-247]. The accumulation of hard-edged, almost grotesque detail — the shark's tooth, the drooping jaw — conveys a man whose physical power is in visible decline from youth into middle age.

Indeed, [[mary-olivier|Mary]] notes the trajectory: "Once he had been young, like Roddy. Then he had been middle-aged, with hanging jaw and weak eyelids, like Dr. Charles" [024-284]. He is what [[roddy-olivier|Roddy]] might become, a mirror held up to masculine ageing.

## Character and Manner

Despite his rough exterior, Dr. Charles is universally regarded with kindness. "And there was Dr. Charles's kind look under his savage, shaggy eyebrows" [028-004] — this juxtaposition of the savage and the kind is his defining trait. At dinner he "gave orders" [024-262] and "talked" [024-263] at length, arranging chairs and screens for guests with a commanding authority.

At dances he watches. [[mary-olivier|Mary]] notes that "Dr. Charles watched them. You knew what he was thinking" [029-119] — a reminder that his medical gaze is never entirely off-duty, even at social functions. He dances with [[mary-olivier|Mary]] herself and is described as kind: "He was kind. You trod on his feet and he bore it" [029-115].

## Medical Role

Dr. Charles attends the community's health. He drives his dogcart through the grey [[morfe|Morfe]] roads [026-114], tends to [[mrs-heron|old Mrs. Heron]], prescribes "pills and arsenic" to [[dorsy|Dorsy Heron]] [031-270], and is summoned when Roddy is ill [026-065]. His medical authority merges with his social standing; he is doctor, neighbour, and friend in one.
