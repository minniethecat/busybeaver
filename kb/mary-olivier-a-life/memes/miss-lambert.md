## Overview

Miss Lambert enters [[mary-olivier]]'s life as a governess during her adolescence, at a time when [[mary-olivier|Mary]]'s intellectual ambitions are beginning to strain against the constraints of her family. Unlike the earlier [[miss-thompson|Miss Thompson]], Miss Lambert is active and consequential — she runs a school, negotiates with [[emilius-olivier|Papa]], and accompanies [[mary-olivier|Mary]] to [[london|London]] for a decisive meeting.

## The School Question

Miss Lambert first appears at a railway platform, meeting [[emilius-olivier|Papa]] — "on the platform, to [[emilius-olivier|Papa]] — Miss Lambert" [021-013]. She is associated from the start with the question of [[mary-olivier|Mary]]'s [[school|education]]. She runs a school of her own, and [[mary-olivier|Mary]]'s future at it becomes a point of contention between her parents.

When [[mary-olivier|Mary]]'s mother decides against the school, Miss Lambert is visibly affected: "She had frightened Miss Lambert and made her tremble: all for nothing" [021-062]. A tear runs "like a glazed snail's track down Miss Lambert's cheek" [021-065] — an image of distress that [[mary-olivier|Mary]] registers with sympathy but cannot remedy. Miss Lambert's "eyes turned aside slantways" [021-071] betray her discomfort at the family dynamics she has been drawn into.

## The London Journey

Miss Lambert's most significant action is taking [[mary-olivier|Mary]] to [[london|London]]. "In the morning Miss Lambert took her up to [[london|London]]. She had not been to [[london|London]] since she was six years old" [021-098]. At Victoria Station, "Miss Lambert talked to [[emilius-olivier|Papa]] outside, in the waiting-room" [021-099] — a conversation whose contents [[mary-olivier|Mary]] cannot hear but whose importance she intuits.

Years later, a letter from Miss Lambert resurfaces: "the wish letter at the bottom was Miss Lambert's" [034-048]. [[mary-olivier|Mary]]'s memory retrieves the image of "Miss Lambert talking to her father on the platform" [034-057] — the governess's role in facilitating that father-daughter meeting preserved as a fixed point in [[mary-olivier|Mary]]'s mental chronology.

## Significance

Miss Lambert belongs to the series of governesses who shape [[mary-olivier|Mary]]'s [[school|education]], but she is distinguished by her willingness to act — to run a school, to take [[mary-olivier|Mary]] to [[london|London]], to negotiate with Emilius. She is one of the few adults in [[mary-olivier|Mary]]'s youth who treats her future as something worth planning for.
