# Old Dr. Kendal

**Old Dr. Kendal** is the retired doctor of the district, father of [[dr-charles|Dr. Charles Kendal]] and [[miss-kendal|Miss Kendal]]. He resides at the Chapel house and, in his later years, becomes the oldest man in the Dale.

## Appearance

In old age, Dr. Kendal is described as "six feet of leanness doubled" — a tall, thin man now stooped with age.[024-246] At the Kendal family gatherings, he sits behind a table by the corner of the fireplace.[024-252]

## Medical Role

As the district's doctor before his retirement, Dr. Kendal's presence carries authority. [[mary-olivier|Mary Olivier]] hopes he will intervene when her brother [[roddy-olivier|Roddy]]'s illness worsens — "if he said Roddy was to stop at home," suggesting the weight his medical opinion still holds.[026-065]

He drives about the district in his dogcart, a familiar sight on the grey roads.[026-114] [[mary-olivier|Mary]] is sometimes afraid he would say something she does not wish to hear.[026-116]

## Later Years and Death

Dr. Kendal lives to a great age. After [[mr-peacock|Mr. Peacock]] of Sarrack dies, Dr. Kendal becomes the oldest man in the Dale.[030-001] Eventually, [[mary-olivier|Mary]] reflects on "old Dr. Kendal, sloughing and rotting in his grave,"[028-154] and later notes his [[funerals|death]]: "Old Dr. Kendal was dead. He had had nothing."[030-181]
