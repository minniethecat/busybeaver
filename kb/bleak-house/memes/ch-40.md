[040-001] England has been in a dreadful state for some weeks. Lord Coodle

[040-002] would go out, Sir Thomas Doodle wouldn’t come in, and there being

[040-003] nobody in Great Britain (to speak of) except Coodle and Doodle, there

[040-004] has been no government. It is a mercy that the hostile meeting

[040-005] between those two great men, which at one time seemed inevitable, did

[040-006] not come off, because if both pistols had taken effect, and Coodle

[040-007] and Doodle had killed each other, it is to be presumed that England

[040-008] must have waited to be governed until young Coodle and young Doodle,

[040-009] now in frocks and long stockings, were grown up. This stupendous

[040-010] national calamity, however, was averted by Lord Coodle’s making the

[040-011] timely discovery that if in the heat of debate he had said that he

[040-012] scorned and despised the whole ignoble career of Sir Thomas Doodle,

[040-013] he had merely meant to say that party differences should never induce

[040-014] him to withhold from it the tribute of his warmest admiration; while

[040-015] it as opportunely turned out, on the other hand, that Sir Thomas

[040-016] Doodle had in his own bosom expressly booked Lord Coodle to go down

[040-017] to posterity as the mirror of virtue and honour. Still England has

[040-018] been some weeks in the dismal strait of having no pilot (as was well

[040-019] observed by [[sir-leicester-dedlock|Sir Leicester Dedlock]]) to weather the storm; and the

[040-020] marvellous part of the matter is that England has not appeared to

[040-021] care very much about it, but has gone on eating and drinking and

[040-022] marrying and giving in marriage as the old world did in the days

[040-023] before the flood. But Coodle knew the danger, and Doodle knew the

[040-024] danger, and all their followers and hangers-on had the clearest

[040-025] possible perception of the danger. At last Sir Thomas Doodle has not

[040-026] only condescended to come in, but has done it handsomely, bringing in

[040-027] with him all his nephews, all his male cousins, and all his

[040-028] brothers-in-law. So there is hope for the old ship yet.

[040-029] Doodle has found that he must throw himself upon the country, chiefly

[040-030] in the form of sovereigns and beer. In this metamorphosed state he is

[040-031] available in a good many places simultaneously and can throw himself

[040-032] upon a considerable portion of the country at one time. Britannia

[040-033] being much occupied in pocketing Doodle in the form of sovereigns,

[040-034] and swallowing Doodle in the form of beer, and in swearing herself

[040-035] black in the face that she does neither—plainly to the advancement

[040-036] of her glory and morality—the [[london|London]] season comes to a sudden end,

[040-037] through all the Doodleites and Coodleites dispersing to assist

[040-038] Britannia in those religious exercises.

[040-039] Hence Mrs. Rouncewell, housekeeper at [[chesney-wold|Chesney Wold]], foresees, though

[040-040] no instructions have yet come down, that the family may shortly be

[040-041] expected, together with a pretty large accession of cousins and

[040-042] others who can in any way assist the great Constitutional work. And

[040-043] hence the stately old dame, taking Time by the forelock, leads him up

[040-044] and down the staircases, and along the galleries and passages, and

[040-045] through the rooms, to witness before he grows any older that

[040-046] everything is ready, that floors are rubbed bright, carpets spread,

[040-047] curtains shaken out, beds puffed and patted, still-room and kitchen

[040-048] cleared for action—all things prepared as beseems the Dedlock

[040-049] dignity.

[040-050] This present summer evening, as the sun goes down, the preparations

[040-051] are complete. Dreary and solemn the old house looks, with so many

[040-052] appliances of habitation and with no inhabitants except the pictured

[040-053] forms upon the walls. So did these come and go, a Dedlock in

[040-054] possession might have ruminated passing along; so did they see this

[040-055] gallery hushed and quiet, as I see it now; so think, as I think, of

[040-056] the gap that they would make in this domain when they were gone; so

[040-057] find it, as I find it, difficult to believe that it could be without

[040-058] them; so pass from my world, as I pass from theirs, now closing the

[040-059] reverberating door; so leave no blank to miss them, and so die.

[040-060] Through some of the fiery windows beautiful from without, and set, at

[040-061] this sunset hour, not in dull-grey stone but in a glorious house of

[040-062] gold, the light excluded at other windows pours in rich, lavish,

[040-063] overflowing like the summer plenty in the land. Then do the frozen

[040-064] Dedlocks thaw. Strange movements come upon their features as the

[040-065] shadows of leaves play there. A dense justice in a corner is beguiled

[040-066] into a wink. A staring baronet, with a truncheon, gets a dimple in

[040-067] his chin. Down into the bosom of a stony shepherdess there steals a

[040-068] fleck of light and warmth that would have done it good a hundred

[040-069] years ago. One ancestress of Volumnia, in high-heeled shoes, very
