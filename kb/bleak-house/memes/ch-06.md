[006-001] The day had brightened very much, and still brightened as we went

[006-002] westward. We went our way through the sunshine and the fresh air,

[006-003] wondering more and more at the extent of the streets, the brilliancy

[006-004] of the shops, the great traffic, and the crowds of people whom the

[006-005] pleasanter weather seemed to have brought out like many-coloured

[006-006] flowers. By and by we began to leave the wonderful city and to

[006-007] proceed through suburbs which, of themselves, would have made a

[006-008] pretty large town in my eyes; and at last we got into a real country

[006-010] scents of old hay, swinging signs, and horse troughs: trees, fields,

[006-011] and hedge-rows. It was delightful to see the green landscape before

[006-013] of beautiful horses, furnished with red trappings and clear-sounding

[006-014] bells, came by us with its music, I believe we could all three have

[006-015] sung to the bells, so cheerful were the influences around.

[006-017] said [[richard-carstone|Richard]], “and that waggon is the finishing touch. Halloa! What’s

[006-018] the matter?”

[006-022] little shower of bell-ringing.

[006-033] hat again (which was like a soft bowl), cracked his whip, re-awakened

[006-036] post-boy.

[006-037] “Yes, sir,” he replied. “Going to [[london|London]].”

[006-039] contained these words in a solid, plain hand.

[006-041] without constraint on either side. I therefore have to

[006-042] propose that we meet as old friends and take the past for

[006-043] granted. It will be a relief to you possibly, and to me

[006-044] certainly, and so my love to you.
