# Harold Skimpole
## Overview
Skimpole is the novel's most unsettling parasite — a man of genuine charm and cultivated helplessness who disclaims all responsibility for his actions because "I am a child, you know." His introduction of Richard to Vholes is the act that seals Richard's doom, and his self-exculpation ("I had nothing to do with it") is the novel's coldest depiction of moral evasion.
## Role
Dickens based Skimpole on Leigh Hunt, a fact that scandalized contemporary readers. The character's danger lies in his plausibility: Skimpole's charm disarms criticism, and his philosophy (that responsibility is an imposition on freedom) is seductive. Esther, who initially finds him delightful, gradually recognizes the damage he causes.
