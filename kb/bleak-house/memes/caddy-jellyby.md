# Caddy Jellyby
## Overview
Caddy Jellyby is the neglected eldest daughter of Mrs. Jellyby, drafted as unpaid secretary for her mother's African charity while her own education and happiness are ignored. Her escape through marriage to Prince Turveydrop — a modest, kindly dancing-master — is one of the novel's few unambiguous successes, representing the possibility of generational correction.
## Role
Caddy's story is a domestic parallel to the Chancery plot: just as the legal system consumes its wards, telescopic philanthropy consumes its children. Her marriage is portrayed as genuine achievement — not romantic transcendence but practical escape from a mother who "can see nothing nearer than Africa."
