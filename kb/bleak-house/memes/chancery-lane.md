# Chancery Lane
## Overview
Chancery Lane is the novel's legal geography made literal — a street lined with lawyers' offices and the rag-and-bone shop of Krook. It is the spatial link between the Court of Chancery (at Lincoln's Inn) and the commercial city, embodying the novel's argument that law and commerce are continuous in their extraction of value.
## In the Novel
Krook's shop stands on Chancery Lane, making it a physical neighbour to the Court — a proximity that reinforces the parodic relationship between Krook (the "Lord Chancellor" of his shop) and the real Lord Chancellor. Mr. Vholes keeps his office in Symond's Inn, off Chancery Lane, where Richard's resources are slowly drained.
