[062-002] courage to see myself, for I was afraid that my tears might a little

[062-006] from the place where I kept it, and repeated its contents by its own

[062-007] clear light of integrity and love, and went to sleep with it on my

[062-008] pillow.

[062-010] walk. We bought flowers for the breakfast-table, and came back and

[062-011] arranged them, and were as busy as possible. We were so early that I

[062-013] (who was not in the least improved in the old defective article of

[062-014] grammar) came through it with great applause; and we were altogether

[062-015] very notable. When my guardian appeared he said, “Why, little woman,

[062-016] you look fresher than your flowers!” And Mrs. [[allan-woodcourt|Woodcourt]] repeated and

[062-017] translated a passage from the Mewlinnwillinwodd expressive of my

[062-033] morning.”

[062-041] disturb it at all.

[062-046] to your letter, guardian?”
