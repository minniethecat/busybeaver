# Allan Woodcourt
## Overview
Allan Woodcourt is the novel's model professional — a physician whose skill and compassion take him from the slums of Tom-all-Alone's to the Arctic as a naval surgeon. His ability to see past Esther's scarred face to her essential self is presented as the novel's deepest act of perception: Woodcourt loves what is real, not what is visible.
## Role
Woodcourt functions as a moral contrast to the novel's legal professionals. Where Tulkinghorn and Vholes extract, Woodcourt serves. His quiet courtship of Esther — conducted largely through glances and professional visits — culminates in the novel's happiest resolution, enabled by Jarndyce's renunciation.
