# St. Albans
## Overview
The Hertfordshire town near Bleak House whose brickmaking community represents the rural poverty that metropolitan charity (Mrs. Pardiggle) fails to address — a site of the novel's intersection between urban institutional failure and rural destitution.
## In the Novel
St. Albans is significant in the novel's topography of Victorian England.
