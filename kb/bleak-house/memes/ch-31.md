[031-001] I had not been at home again many days when one evening I went

[031-002] upstairs into my own room to take a peep over Charley’s shoulder and

[031-003] see how she was getting on with her copy-book. Writing was a trying

[031-004] business to Charley, who seemed to have no natural power over a pen,

[031-005] but in whose hand every pen appeared to become perversely animated,

[031-006] and to go wrong and crooked, and to stop, and splash, and sidle into

[031-007] corners like a saddle-donkey. It was very odd to see what old [[document-and-letter|letters]]

[031-008] Charley’s young hand had made, they so wrinkled, and shrivelled, and

[031-009] tottering, it so plump and round. Yet Charley was uncommonly expert

[031-010] at other things and had as nimble little fingers as I ever watched.

[031-011] “Well, Charley,” said I, looking over a copy of the letter O in which

[031-012] it was represented as square, triangular, pear-shaped, and collapsed

[031-013] in all kinds of ways, “we are improving. If we only get to make it

[031-014] round, we shall be perfect, Charley.”

[031-015] Then I made one, and Charley made one, and the pen wouldn’t join

[031-016] Charley’s neatly, but twisted it up into a knot.

[031-017] “Never mind, Charley. We shall do it in time.”

[031-018] Charley laid down her pen, the copy being finished, opened and shut

[031-019] her cramped little hand, looked gravely at the page, half in pride

[031-020] and half in doubt, and got up, and dropped me a curtsy.

[031-021] “Thank you, miss. If you please, miss, did you know a poor person of

[031-022] the name of Jenny?”

[031-023] “A brickmaker’s wife, Charley? Yes.”

[031-024] “She came and spoke to me when I was out a little while ago, and said

[031-025] you knew her, miss. She asked me if I wasn’t the young lady’s little

[031-026] maid—meaning you for the young lady, miss—and I said yes, miss.”

[031-027] “I thought she had left this neighbourhood altogether, Charley.”

[031-028] “So she had, miss, but she’s come back again to where she used to

[031-029] live—she and Liz. Did you know another poor person of the name of

[031-030] Liz, miss?”

[031-031] “I think I do, Charley, though not by name.”

[031-032] “That’s what she said!” returned Charley. “They have both come back,

[031-033] miss, and have been tramping high and low.”

[031-034] “Tramping high and low, have they, Charley?”

[031-035] “Yes, miss.” If Charley could only have made the letters in her copy

[031-036] as round as the eyes with which she looked into my face, they would

[031-037] have been excellent. “And this poor person came about the house three

[031-038] or four days, hoping to get a glimpse of you, miss—all she wanted,

[031-039] she said—but you were away. That was when she saw me. She saw me

[031-040] a-going about, miss,” said Charley with a short laugh of the greatest

[031-041] delight and pride, “and she thought I looked like your maid!”

[031-042] “Did she though, really, Charley?”

[031-043] “Yes, miss!” said Charley. “Really and truly.” And Charley, with

[031-044] another short laugh of the purest glee, made her eyes very round

[031-045] again and looked as serious as became my maid. I was never tired of

[031-046] seeing Charley in the full enjoyment of that great dignity, standing

[031-047] before me with her youthful face and figure, and her steady manner,

[031-048] and her childish exultation breaking through it now and then in the

[031-049] pleasantest way.

[031-050] “And where did you see her, Charley?” said I.

[031-051] My little maid’s countenance fell as she replied, “By the doctor’s

[031-052] shop, miss.” For Charley wore her black frock yet.

[031-053] I asked if the brickmaker’s wife were ill, but Charley said no. It

[031-054] was some one else. Some one in her cottage who had tramped down to

[031-055] Saint Albans and was tramping he didn’t know where. A poor boy,

[031-056] Charley said. No father, no mother, no any one. “Like as Tom might

[031-057] have been, miss, if Emma and me had died after father,” said Charley,

[031-060] “She said, miss,” returned Charley, “how that he had once done as

[031-061] much for her.”

[031-066] Jenny’s and see what’s the matter.”

[031-084] as solemn as might be.
