# George Rouncewell
## Overview
George Rouncewell bridges the novel's class divisions. Son of a housekeeper, brother of a successful industrialist, George chose the army over industry and now runs a modest shooting-gallery. His refusal to implicate Lady Dedlock when Tulkinghorn pressures him — and his later loyalty to Sir Leicester after the baronet's stroke — demonstrate a moral integrity that transcends class.
## Role
George is the novel's figure of masculine honour uncorrupted by institutional logic. His physical strength is matched by a moral simplicity that the novel's more sophisticated characters lack. When Tulkinghorn tries to use him as a tool against Lady Dedlock, George's refusal — at personal cost — is a quiet act of heroism.
