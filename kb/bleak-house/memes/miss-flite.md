# Miss Flite
## Overview
Miss Flite is Chancery's most visible human ruin — a gentle, educated woman whose decades of waiting for a Chancery judgement have consumed her mind. Her caged birds — named Hope, Joy, Youth, Peace, and other abstractions — are the novel's most compressed image of what Chancery does to those who depend on it: encloses, names, and indefinitely suspends.
## Role in the Novel
Miss Flite haunts the margins of the Chancery scenes, a living warning that Richard refuses to heed. Her kindness to Esther and her periodic appearances at the court — always waiting, always expecting judgement "shortly" — make her simultaneously comic and heartbreaking. The birds' names invert the novel's moral vocabulary: Hope, in Miss Flite's cage, is not a virtue but a captivity.
