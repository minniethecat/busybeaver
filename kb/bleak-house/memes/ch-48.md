[048-001] The place in Lincolnshire has shut its many eyes again, and the house

[048-002] in town is awake. In Lincolnshire the Dedlocks of the past doze in

[048-003] their picture-frames, and the low wind murmurs through the long

[048-004] drawing-room as if they were breathing pretty regularly. In town the

[048-005] Dedlocks of the present rattle in their fire-eyed carriages through

[048-006] the darkness of the night, and the Dedlock Mercuries, with ashes (or

[048-007] hair-powder) on their heads, symptomatic of their great humility,

[048-008] loll away the drowsy mornings in the little windows of the hall. The

[048-009] fashionable world—tremendous orb, nearly five miles round—is in

[048-010] full swing, and the solar system works respectfully at its appointed

[048-011] distances.

[048-012] Where the throng is thickest, where the lights are brightest, where

[048-013] all the senses are ministered to with the greatest delicacy and

[048-014] refinement, [[lady-dedlock|Lady Dedlock]] is. From the shining heights she has scaled

[048-015] and taken, she is never absent. Though the belief she of old reposed

[048-016] in herself as one able to reserve whatsoever she would under

[048-017] her mantle of pride is beaten down, though she has no assurance

[048-018] that what she is to those around her she will remain another day,

[048-019] it is not in her nature when envious eyes are looking on to

[048-020] yield or to droop. They say of her that she has lately grown

[048-021] more handsome and more haughty. The debilitated cousin says of

[048-022] her that she’s beauty nough—tsetup shopofwomen—but rather

[048-023] larming kind—remindingmanfact—inconvenient woman—who WILL

[048-026] is to be found in doorways of rooms, with his limp white cravat

[048-027] loosely twisted into its old-fashioned tie, receiving patronage from

[048-028] the peerage and making no sign. Of all men he is still the last who

[048-029] might be supposed to have any influence upon my Lady. Of all women

[048-030] she is still the last who might be supposed to have any dread of him.

[048-031] One thing has been much on her mind since their late interview in his

[048-032] turret-room at [[chesney-wold|Chesney Wold]]. She is now decided, and prepared to

[048-033] throw it off.

[048-035] sun. The Mercuries, exhausted by looking out of window, are reposing

[048-036] in the hall and hang their heavy heads, the gorgeous creatures, like

[048-037] overblown sunflowers. Like them, too, they seem to run to a deal of

[048-038] seed in their tags and trimmings. [[sir-leicester-dedlock|Sir Leicester]], in the library, has

[048-039] fallen asleep for the good of the country over the report of a

[048-040] Parliamentary committee. My Lady sits in the room in which she gave

[048-043] embroidery or some such pretty thing, and as she bends her head over

[048-044] it, my Lady watches her in silence. Not for the first time to-day.
