# Brickmaker's Hovel
## Overview
The squalid dwelling on the outskirts of St. Albans where the brickmaker's family lives in degrading poverty — visited by Mrs. Pardiggle on her tract-distributing charity rounds, and the site of the baby's death that Esther attends.
## In the Novel
Brickmaker's Hovel is significant in the novel's topography of Victorian England.
