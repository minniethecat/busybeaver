# Lincoln's Inn
## Overview
Lincoln's Inn is the institutional seat of the Court of Chancery and the novel's legal origin point. The opening chapter places the Lord Chancellor in Lincoln's Inn Hall "at the very heart of the fog" — a geographical and symbolic centre from which the novel's critique of legal England radiates outward. Tulkinghorn keeps his chambers in Lincoln's Inn Fields, making his proximity to both the court and the Dedlock townhouse a spatial diagram of his function: the law that connects aristocrat to secret to ruin.
## In the Novel
The court scenes occur at Lincoln's Inn, and the surrounding streets and chambers house the novel's legal professionals. The Inn is presented as simultaneously grand and decaying — an institution whose physical presence is imposing but whose function is parasitic.
