# Inns of Court
## Overview
The four legal societies of London — Lincoln's Inn, Middle Temple, Inner Temple, and Gray's Inn — that constitute the institutional landscape of the legal profession. Lincoln's Inn houses Chancery; the Inns collectively represent the closed, self-regulating world of the law.
## In the Novel
Inns of Court is significant in the novel's topography of Victorian England.
