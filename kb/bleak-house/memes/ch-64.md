[064-001] Soon after I had that conversation with my [[john-jarndyce|guardian]], he put a sealed

[064-002] paper in my hand one morning and said, “This is for next month, my

[064-003] dear.” I found in it two hundred pounds.

[064-004] I now began very quietly to make such preparations as I thought were

[064-005] necessary. Regulating my purchases by my guardian’s taste, which I

[064-006] knew very well of course, I arranged my wardrobe to please him and

[064-007] hoped I should be highly successful. I did it all so quietly because

[064-008] I was not quite free from my old apprehension that [[ada-clare|Ada]] would be

[064-009] rather sorry and because my guardian was so quiet himself. I had no

[064-010] doubt that under all the circumstances we should be married in the

[064-011] most private and simple manner. Perhaps I should only have to say to

[064-012] Ada, “Would you like to come and see me married to-morrow, my pet?”

[064-013] Perhaps our wedding might even be as unpretending as her own, and I

[064-014] might not find it necessary to say anything about it until it was

[064-015] over. I thought that if I were to choose, I would like this best.

[064-016] The only exception I made was Mrs. [[allan-woodcourt|Woodcourt]]. I told her that I was

[064-017] going to be married to my guardian and that we had been engaged some

[064-018] time. She highly approved. She could never do enough for me and was

[064-019] remarkably softened now in comparison with what she had been when we

[064-020] first knew her. There was no trouble she would not have taken to have

[064-021] been of use to me, but I need hardly say that I only allowed her to

[064-022] take as little as gratified her kindness without tasking it.

[064-023] Of course this was not a time to neglect my guardian, and of course

[064-024] it was not a time for neglecting my darling. So I had plenty of

[064-025] occupation, which I was glad of; and as to Charley, she was

[064-026] absolutely not to be seen for needlework. To surround herself with

[064-028] spend a great deal of time in staring with her round eyes at what

[064-029] there was to do, and persuade herself that she was going to do it,

[064-030] were Charley’s great dignities and delights.
