[052-001] But one other day had intervened when, early in the morning as we

[052-002] were going to breakfast, [[allan-woodcourt|Mr. Woodcourt]] came in haste with the

[052-003] astounding news that a terrible murder had been committed for which

[052-004] [[george-rouncewell|Mr. George]] had been apprehended and was in custody. When he told us

[052-007] understand why; but a few more words explained to me that the

[052-008] murdered person was Sir Leicester’s lawyer, and immediately my

[052-009] mother’s dread of him rushed into my remembrance.

[052-010] This unforeseen and violent removal of one whom she had long watched

[052-011] and distrusted and who had long watched and distrusted her, one for

[052-012] whom she could have had few intervals of kindness, always dreading in

[052-013] him a dangerous and secret enemy, appeared so awful that my first

[052-014] thoughts were of her. How appalling to hear of such a death and be

[052-015] able to feel no pity! How dreadful to remember, perhaps, that she had

[052-016] sometimes even wished the old man away who was so swiftly hurried out

[052-017] of life!

[052-019] felt when the name was mentioned, made me so agitated that I could

[052-022] came to myself and saw how shocked my [[john-jarndyce|guardian]] was and found that

[052-024] favourable impression we had formed of him out of the good we had

[052-025] known of him, my interest and my fears were so strongly aroused in
