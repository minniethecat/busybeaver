# Tom-all-Alone's
## Overview
Tom-all-Alone's is the novel's most damning image of institutional neglect — a slum owned by the Court of Chancery, left to rot while the Jarndyce suit consumes its litigants. The name is taken from a Chancery litigant, and the slum's condition is the direct consequence of Chancery's paralysis: property without care, people without shelter, lives without value.
## In the Novel
The slum is the origin point of the novel's contagion chain: Jo catches smallpox here, infects Charley, who infects Esther. The physical connection between Tom-all-Alone's and the Dedlock sphere — via disease — is the novel's most direct argument that the classes are not separate.
