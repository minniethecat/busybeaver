# The Governor's Preparations

## Overview

After learning of the supposed inspector's presence, the Governor launches a comprehensive but entirely cosmetic campaign to prepare the town for inspection. This event, spanning Scenes IV and V of Act I, is a comic tour of the town's institutional failures — each preparation revealing the very dysfunction it is meant to conceal.

## The Event

The Governor's preparations proceed through a series of instructions, each exposing a different scandal:

**To the Police Captain (005-002)**: "Get the policemen together. Let them each take a street in his hand — I don't mean a street — a broom — and sweep the street leading to the inn." He simultaneously warns Ukhovertov about the sergeants who steal silver spoons from the inn and Prokhorov, who is dead drunk. The enforcers cannot enforce anything, least of all their own discipline.

**To the Courts**: The Governor orders the assessor removed — he reeks of vodka — and the geese cleared from the courthouse anteroom. The suggestion that "the court-room ought not to smell of spirits" (005-002) barely needs Gogol's elaboration.

**To the Charitable Hospital (006-002)**: Patients should have clean caps and Latin inscriptions. The church funded five years ago but never built should be explained as "destroyed by fire."

**To the School (006-002)**: The teachers' eccentricities should be temporarily suppressed.

## Dramatic Significance

The preparations function as a catalogue of corruption. Each instruction is a confession: the Governor knows exactly what is wrong and exactly how to disguise it. The event's comedy derives from the Governor's simultaneous competence (he knows every detail of institutional failure) and incompetence (he cannot fix any of it, only conceal it).
