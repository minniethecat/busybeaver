# Khlestakov Courts the Governor's Family

## Summary

The courtship sequence in Act IV is Gogol's most concentrated farce. Khlestakov, left alone with Marya Antonovna, makes increasingly forward advances until he falls to his knees. Anna Andreyevna enters and, misinterpreting the tableau, drives Marya out — only to find Khlestakov immediately transferring his marital proposal to her. When Marya returns, Khlestakov pivots again, asking Anna to bless his union with her daughter. The scene distills the play's central insight: in this world, nothing is what it seems, and everything is for sale.

## The Seduction of Marya

Khlestakov, emboldened by the bribery parade, encounters Marya alone. His compliments are clichéd but relentless: "Your eyes are better than important matters" (043-002), "How I long to be your scarf, so that I might embrace your lily neck" (043-002). Marya, simultaneously flattered and alarmed, tries to maintain propriety — "I wasn't frightened" (043-002), "Peculiar weather today, isn't it?" (043-002) — but Khlestakov pursues her chair around the room in a comic chase: "Why do you move your chair away? It is better for us to sit near each other" (043-002). He kisses her shoulder, then falls to his knees to beg forgiveness.

## The Mother Intervenes

Anna Andreyevna enters to find Khlestakov kneeling before her daughter. "Oh, what a situation!" (044-002) she exclaims, and immediately banishes Marya: "Go away from here. Do you hear? And don't you dare to show your face to me" (044-002). But Khlestakov, seeing Anna, notes aside: "She's very appetizing, too. She's not bad-looking, either" (044-002) — and immediately flings himself on his knees again, this time before the mother.

## The Proposal

Khlestakov declares his love for Anna: "I am in love with you. My life hangs by a thread" (044-002). When Anna protests she is married, Khlestakov invokes Karamzin: "Love knows no distinction. It was Karamzin who said: 'The laws condemn'" (044-002). The absurdity peaks when Marya returns and Khlestakov, without missing a beat, seizes Marya's hand and asks Anna: "Give your blessing to our constant love" (045-002). Anna, now forced to accept what she had interrupted, scolds Marya for her good fortune: "Our guest is pleased to go down on his knees for such trash as you" (045-002).

## Dramatic Significance

The scene is a parody of romantic convention. Khlestakov follows no genuine desire — he merely continues the momentum of his imposture. That Anna, moments after being herself the object of a proposal, can seamlessly pivot to blessing her daughter's engagement, reveals her vanity as equal to Khlestakov's emptiness.
