[059-002]*The Inspector General*  

[059-003]was published in 1836 by  

[059-004][Nikolai Gogol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nikolai_Gogol).  

[059-005]It was translated from Russian in 1916 by  

[059-006][Thomas Seltzer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Seltzer).

[059-007]This ebook was produced for  

[059-008][Standard Ebooks](https://standardebooks.org/)  

[059-009]by  

[059-010]**Asher Smith**,  

[059-011]and is based on a transcription produced in 2003 by  

[059-012]**Judy Boss** and **David Widger**  

[059-013]for  

[059-014][Project Gutenberg](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/3735)  

[059-015]and on digital scans from the  

[059-016][Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/inspectorgeneral00gogouoft).

[059-017]The cover page is adapted from  

[059-018]*The Princess of Orange Receiving Alexander II (1818-1881), Grand Duke and Heir to the Throne of Russia, in the Czar Peter’s House in Zaandam, 17 April 1839*,  

[059-019]a painting completed circa 1840 by  

[059-020]**Christiaan Julius Lodewijk Portman**.  

[059-021]The cover and title pages feature the  

[059-022]**League Spartan** and **Sorts Mill Goudy**  

[059-023]typefaces created in 2014 and 2009 by  

[059-024][The League of Moveable Type](https://www.theleagueofmoveabletype.com/).
