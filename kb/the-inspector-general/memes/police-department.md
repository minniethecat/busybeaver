# Police Department

## Overview

The Police Department was the municipal institution responsible for public order, street maintenance, and law enforcement in the [[provincial-town|provincial town]] of [[the-inspector-general|Gogol's comedy]]. Under the [[table-of-ranks|Table of Ranks]] established by Peter the Great, the urban police operated as part of the [[civil-service|imperial civil service]], with the police captain (*kapitan-ispravnik*) answerable to the [[anton-antonovich|Governor]] and, nominally, to the Ministry of Internal Affairs in St. Petersburg. In practice, the police serve a more immediate master: the Governor's personal interests.

## In the Play

The police appear at every stage of the comedy, listed in the [[dramatis-personae|Dramatis Personae]] under Captain "Stepan Ilyich Ukhovertov, the police captain" (P02-020) and his three named sergeants: Pugovichyn, Derzhimorda, and Svistunov -- "police sergeants" (P02-024). Two unnamed police sergeants are present in the opening scene when the Governor announces the [[announcement-of-inspector|inspector general's arrival]] at the [[governors-residence|Governor's residence]], a staging choice that foreshadows the police's role as instruments of the Governor's panic rather than guardians of the law.

The Governor's first instinct upon learning of the [[announcement-of-inspector|impending inspector]] is to mobilize the police not to ensure justice but to conceal evidence (005-002). Streets must be swept; the drunken sergeant Prokhorov hidden; an old fence pulled down and dressed with straw to simulate construction activity. The [[stepan-ukhovertov|Police Captain]] arrives belatedly and reports: "I sent Sergeant Pugovichyn with policemen to clean the street" (006-002) -- a task that reveals the police's true priority: appearances over order.

In Act III, the sergeants reappear as ceremonial ushers, opening folding doors for [[ivan-khlestakov|Khlestakov]] and scrambling to retrieve a dropped paper on the Governor's signal (024-002) -- a perfect image of their function as theatrical props. By Act IV, the sergeant Derzhimorda actively blocks [[merchant-class|merchants]] and petitioners from reaching the supposed inspector, bellowing that "He doesn't receive anybody. He's asleep."

When the police finally exercise their nominal duty -- responding to a disturbance at the [[provincial-town|market]] -- the result is farcical. The [[deception-and-self-deception|widow of a noncommissioned officer]] reports that the police arrived after the squabble had ended, flogged her "by mistake," and reported her anyway: "I couldn't sit down for two days" (042-002).

In the final act, the [[stepan-ukhovertov|Police Captain]] and sergeants join the congratulatory crowd at the [[governors-residence|Governor's house]] (055-002), only to be frozen with everyone else in the [[letter-reading-finale|famous silent scene]] when a real inspector is announced.

> Be off and get the policemen together. Let them each take a street... a broom -- and sweep the street leading to the inn, and sweep it clean.
>
> -- Governor to Svistunov, Act I, Scene 4 (005-002)

## Personnel

The police force is staffed by a captain and three named sergeants, each a study in institutional dysfunction. A fourth sergeant, Prokhorov, is comically absent:

| Officer | Rank | Role | Notable Detail |
|---------|------|------|----------------|
| [[stepan-ukhovertov|Stepan Ilyich Ukhovertov]] | Police Captain | Commander | Arrives late, offers excuses, reports his men's incompetence without embarrassment (P02-020) |
| Pugovichyn | Sergeant | Street duty | Tall -- stationed on the bridge "for appearance' sake"; dispatched with policemen to clean the street (006-002) |
| Derzhimorda | Sergeant | Fire brigade / crowd control | Name means "muzzle-snouter"; physically blocks petitioners; operates the fire engine (006-002) |
| Svistunov | Sergeant | General duty / messenger | Name means "whistler"; dispatches the Governor's orders; reports Prokhorov's drunkenness (005-002) |
| Prokhorov | Sergeant | Absent | Too drunk to stand throughout the play -- "they poured two buckets of water over him, but he hasn't sobered up yet" (005-002) |

The sergeants' names are Gogolian satire: Derzhimorda ("Hold-Muzzle") suggests brute force, Svistunov ("Whistler") suggests emptiness, and Pugovichyn ("Button") suggests triviality. Together they compose a portrait of a police force that, when not actively subverting justice, is simply useless.

> What have you been up to with the merchant, Chorniayev, eh? He gave you two yards of cloth for your uniform and you stole the whole piece. Take care. You're only a Sergeant. Don't graft higher than your rank.
>
> -- Governor to Svistunov, Act I, Scene 4 (005-002)

## Satirical Function

The Police Department embodies three of Gogol's central critiques of provincial Russian governance.

First, **the police serve power, not law**. The [[anton-antonovich|Governor]] directs his officers to sweep streets, guard doors, and block petitioners -- never to investigate crimes or protect citizens. The police are an extension of the Governor's personal apparatus, much like the [[district-court|District Court]] under [[ammos-liapkin-tiapkin|Judge Liapkin-Tiapkin]] or the [[office-of-charities|Office of Charities]] under [[artemy-zemlianika|Zemlianika]] -- institutions that serve their administrators' interests rather than their nominal missions. This reflects a structural truth of Nikolaevan Russia, where local police were appointed by the Governor and accountable upward, not to the communities they served.

Second, **the police are themselves corrupt**. The Governor's remark about stealing cloth -- delivered as knowing acknowledgment of a shared practice rather than discipline -- normalizes [[bribery|graft]] as the currency of police work. The police steal, the Governor steals, and the system accommodates theft rather than preventing it.

Third, **the police are incompetent performers of even their minimal duties**. Prokhorov is drunk. Derzhimorda suppresses complaints. The one time police are called to a real incident, they flog the wrong person (042-002). Gogol's police are barely functional cogs in a machine whose failure is so systemic that no one finds it remarkable -- not even the Governor, who asks "How could you allow him to get drunk?" with a kind of weary bureaucratic fatalism (006-002).

The ultimate satirical pay-off arrives when a real inspector is announced: the [[stepan-ukhovertov|Police Captain]] and his sergeants are frozen in the silent scene, paralyzed before the authority they pretended to serve.

> Where is Derzhimorda? -- He has gone off on the fire engine. -- And Prokhorov is drunk? -- Yes.
>
> -- Governor and Police Captain, Act I, Scene 5 (006-002)
