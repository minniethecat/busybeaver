## Description

The Educational Department (Учебное ведомство) was the branch of the tsarist bureaucracy responsible for overseeing schools and educational institutions in provincial Russia. In the early 19th century, education was administered through a hierarchy stretching from the Ministry of Education in St. Petersburg down to local school inspectors. The department wielded significant ideological power — teachers and inspectors were expected to uphold loyalty to the autocracy, and any hint of freethinking could jeopardize a career.

## In the Play

The Educational Department is invoked by Luka Lukich Khlopov, the Inspector of Schools, during the officials' panicked conference in Act I. When the Governor warns of the incognito inspector's arrival, Khlopov laments: "Ah, Heaven save us from being in the educational department! One's afraid of everything. Everybody meddles and wants to show that he is as clever as you" ([006-002]).

This brief outburst encapsulates Gogol's satire of the tsarist bureaucracy. The educational department, rather than being a noble enterprise of enlightenment, is portrayed as a climate of fear where officials feel perpetually scrutinized. Khlopov's terror extends beyond the inspector to his own colleagues within the department — a world of petty rivalry and mutual suspicion.

The department's reach is felt through the character of the schoolteacher whom Khlopov describes as a "hot one" who will "sacrifice his life" for learning ([006-002]). This teacher, with his passionate mannerisms and grimaces, represents the kind of eccentric that the conformist educational bureaucracy finds threatening.

## Key Mentions

- Khlopov's lament: "Heaven save us from being in the educational department! One's afraid of everything" ([006-002])
- The teacher's fervor: "in the cause of learning I will even sacrifice my life" ([006-002])
- The Governor's cynical observation: "Your clever man is either a drunkard, or he makes such grimaces that you feel like running away" ([006-002])
