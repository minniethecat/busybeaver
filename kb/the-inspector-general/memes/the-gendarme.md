# The Gendarme

## Identity

The Gendarme is an unnamed official messenger of the imperial government who appears in the final moments of Gogol's *The Inspector General*. He delivers the single most consequential line in the play: the announcement that the real Inspector General has arrived.

## Role in the Play

The Gendarme's entrance in the Last Scene of Act V serves as the play's culminating reversal — the moment when the elaborate edifice of [[mistaken-identity|misidentification]] and self-deception collapses. After five acts of [[anton-antonovich|the Governor]] and his officials mistaking the penniless [[ivan-khlestakov|Khlestakov]] for a high-ranking inspector, and after the Governor has already begun celebrating his daughter's engagement and fantasizing about a generalship, the Gendarme arrives with devastating news [057-001]:

> "An official from St. Petersburg sent by imperial order has arrived, and wants to see you all at once. He is stopping at the inn." [057-002]

The effect is instantaneous and total. The stage direction describes all present as "struck as by a thunderbolt," the ladies cry out in amazement, and "the whole group suddenly shifts positions and remains standing as if petrified" [057-002]. This frozen tableau segues directly into the famous Silent Scene [058-001], where the characters remain motionless for nearly a minute and a half as the curtain falls.

The Gendarme himself is not a developed character — he is a dramatic device, the embodiment of external, impersonal authority. He speaks exactly one sentence, has no name, no backstory, and no personality. Yet his function is essential: he restores the proper order of things, replacing the farce of [[mistaken-identity|misidentification]] with the cold reality of imperial bureaucracy. The real inspector has been watching all along, and the Governor's world is about to unravel.

## Key Scenes

- **(057-001–057-002)**: Arrives at the Governor's house and delivers the announcement that the real Inspector General has arrived from St. Petersburg. The assembled company is struck motionless.
- **(058-001)**: His announcement triggers the Silent Scene — the famous pantomime finale in which the entire cast freezes in a tableau of shock and terror.

## Relationships

- **[[anton-antonovich|The Governor]]**: The primary target of the news he delivers; the Governor's world collapses upon hearing the Gendarme's announcement.
- **[[anna-andreyevna|Anna Andreyevna]]** and **[[marya-antonovna|Marya Antonovna]]**: Present when he arrives; their matrimonial ambitions are among the first casualties of his news.
- **[[ivan-khlestakov|Khlestakov]]**: The false inspector whose unmasking the Gendarme's news precipitates, though Khlestakov has already departed.
