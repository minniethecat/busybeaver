# Mishka

## Identity

Mishka is the Governor's household servant, listed in the Dramatis Personae simply as "Mishka, the governor's servant" (P02-028). His name — the diminutive of Mikhail, also meaning "little bear" — suits his role as the lumbering domestic factotum of the [[governors-residence|Governor's residence]]. He appears in brief, wordless moments that accumulate into a portrait of the household as a place of constant, chaotic motion.

## Role in the Play

Mishka never speaks a line, but his silent presence provides comic punctuation to the play's domestic scenes. When Dobchinsky arrives breathless at the Governor's house, he calls for Mishka to announce him (021-002). In Act II, Mishka performs one of the play's most memorable visual gags: "a door opens through which Mishka sweeps dirt on to the stage" (022-002) — a gesture that literalizes the town's inability to clean up its own corruption.

In Act III, Mishka encounters [[osip]] and directs him where to place Khlestakov's luggage — "In here, in here" (023-002) — a rare moment of cross-household servant interaction. The Governor later calls on him to summon the police sergeants (028-002) and, in Act V, to bring chairs for the assembled notables: "Ho, Mishka, bring some more chairs in" (054-002).

## Key Scenes

- **(022-002)**: Sweeps dirt onto the stage through an opening door — a silent commentary on the Governor's "clean-up" operation.
- **(023-001–023-002)**: Meets Osip and directs the placement of Khlestakov's luggage.
- **(028-002)**: Sent by the Governor to summon Svistunov and Derzhimorda.
- **(054-002)**: Brings chairs for the Act V assembly, the last domestic act before the catastrophe.

## Relationships

- **[[anton-antonovich|The Governor]]**: His master, whom he serves with wordless efficiency amid growing chaos.
- **[[osip]]**: The only other servant character with whom he interacts directly.
- **[[anna-andreyevna|Anna Andreyevna]]** and **[[marya-antonovna|Marya Antonovna]]**: The ladies of the house he serves.
