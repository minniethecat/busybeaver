# Stepan Ukhovertov

## Identity

Stepan Ilyich Ukhovertov is the police captain (*kapitan-ispravnik*) of the provincial town. He is listed in the [[dramatis-personae|Dramatis Personae]] as "Stepan Ilyich Ukhovertov, the police captain" (P02-020). His surname derives from the Russian words *ukho* ("ear") and *vertet* ("to twist"), yielding the broadly comic meaning "ear-twister" -- a name that telegraphs the casual violence and petty tyranny that characterize Gogol's provincial police force.

## Role in the Play

Ukhovertov commands the town's [[police-department|police]] force under the direction of the [[anton-antonovich|Governor]]. His role in the play is to embody the incompetence and venality of provincial law enforcement: he arrives late to the Governor's urgent summons (006-001), reports his men's failures without a trace of shame, and participates in the congratulatory charade of Act V with the same blank compliance he brings to every other assignment.

The police captain is neither the play's most corrupt character nor its most foolish, but he is arguably its most hollow. He offers no resistance to any order, expresses no opinion of his own, and in the final [[letter-reading-finale|silent scene]] he freezes alongside everyone else -- a policeman struck immobile by the news that a real authority has finally arrived (058-001).

## Key Scenes

- **(P02-020)**: Listed in the Dramatis Personae as police captain.
- **(004-002)**: The Governor sends Svistunov to summon the Police Captain to his office.
- **(006-001)**: Ukhovertov arrives. The Governor demands: "Hello, Stepan Ilyich, where the dickens have you been keeping yourself?" and interrogates him about the police force's state. Ukhovertov reports that Sergeant Prokhorov is drunk, Derzhimorda has gone off on the fire engine, and Pugovichyn has been dispatched to clean the street (006-002).
- **(055-002)**: In Act V, Ukhovertov and his sergeants join the throng congratulating the Governor on his daughter's engagement to the supposed inspector: "I have the honor to congratulate you, your Honor, and to wish you long years of prosperity" (055-002).

## Relationships

- **[[anton-antonovich|Anton Antonovich]]** (Governor): Ukhovertov's direct superior. The Governor treats the police captain as a tool for managing appearances -- sweeping streets, posting guards on bridges, destroying evidence -- and Ukhovertov complies without question (006-002).
- **The Police Sergeants** (Svistunov, Pugovichyn, Derzhimorda, Prokhorov): Ukhovertov's subordinates, each more useless than the last. Their collective dysfunction -- drunkenness, brutality, absenteeism -- occurs under his command and without his apparent concern.
- **The [[district-court|District Court]]**: The judicial arm of the town's administration, with which the police are institutionally linked through the [[civil-service|imperial civil service]].
