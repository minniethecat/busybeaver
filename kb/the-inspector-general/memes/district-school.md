# District School

## Overview

The district school (*uchilishche*) is the educational institution of the provincial town, overseen by [[luka-khlopov|Luka Lukich Khlopov]], the [[luka-khlopov|Inspector of Schools]]. Like every other municipal institution in the play, the school embodies the gap between official purpose and actual function.

## The Teachers

The [[anton-antonovich|Governor]]'s catalogue focuses on two teachers. One, "the man with the fat face," has a compulsive tic: the moment he takes his chair, he screws up his face and then "sticking his hand under his necktie and smoothing down his beard" (002-002). Before a visitor "it may turn out very badly. The Inspector, or anyone else, might take it as meant for himself" (002-002).

Khlopov confirms the problem: "Only the other day when the marshal of the nobility came into the classroom, he made such a face at him as I had never in my life seen before" (002-002). The teacher's mannerism is politically dangerous — a grimace can be interpreted as insubordination.

The second teacher is the history instructor. "He has a lot of learning in his head," the [[anton-antonovich|Governor]] says, but his passion is destructive. Assyrians and Babylonians were fine. But Alexander of Macedon: "I can't describe what came over him. Upon my word, I thought a fire had broken out. He jumped down from the platform, picked up a chair and dashed it to the floor" (002-002).

The [[anton-antonovich|Governor]]'s objection is characteristically materialist — not that the teacher's pedagogy is unsound, but that "the state must bear the cost" of broken chairs (002-002). Khlopov's defense is equally revealing: the teacher says, "As you please, but in the cause of learning I will even sacrifice my life" (002-002). Genuine pedagogical passion runs headlong into bureaucratic cost accounting.

## Khlopov's Fear

Khlopov is the play's most timid character, and his fear is institutionally grounded. He knows the school is in disarray. He laments: "Ah, Heaven save us from being in the educational department! One's afraid of everything. Everybody meddles and wants to [[appearance|show]] that he is as clever as you" (002-002).

When the [[anton-antonovich|Governor]] suggests inspecting the school ("we can go from there and inspect the district school and see our method of education" [018-002]), Khlopov's blood runs cold. His relief when escape seems possible is palpable: "Well, thank the Lord! Maybe he won't inspect the [[education|schools]]" (036-002). The school's purpose — education — is secondary to avoiding scrutiny.

## Significance

The school's failure is different. Other institutions fail through [[bribery|corruption]]; the school fails through fear. Khlopov is not venal — he is terrified. His teachers are passionate and learned, but their eccentricities make them liabilities. In a system where appearance trumps substance, the history teacher who smashes chairs for Alexander is the most principled figure — and the most dangerous.
