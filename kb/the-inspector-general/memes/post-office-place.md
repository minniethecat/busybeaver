# Post Office

## Overview

The [[post-office|post]] office (*pochtamt*) is [[provincial-town-place|the town]]'s communications hub, administered by [[ivan-shpekin|Postmaster]] Ivan Kuzmich Shpekin. It serves as both a setting for [[bribery|corruption]] and the physical source of [[ivan-khlestakov|Khlestakov]]'s incriminating [[letter-as-plot-device|letter]] — making it the mechanism of the play's dramatic reversal.

## The Governor's Request

When the [[anton-antonovich|Governor]] hears of [[ivan-khlestakov|the inspector]], his mind turns to communication. He takes Shpekin aside: "don't you think you could — ahem! — just open a little every [[letter-as-plot-device|letter]] that passes through your office and read it — for the common benefit of us all" (003-002). If innocent, "you can seal it up again, or simply deliver the [[letter-as-plot-device|letter]] opened" (003-002).

The [[anton-antonovich|Governor]] treats [[post-office|post]]al privacy as dispensable. "For the common benefit" cloaks surveillance in civic virtue. "Simply deliver it opened" — the adverb is the key: he cannot conceive why anyone would object.

## Shpekin's Vocation

Shpekin does not need the [[anton-antonovich|Governor]]'s prompting. "Oh, I know. You needn't teach me that," he replies. "I do it not so much as a precaution as out of curiosity. I just itch to know what's doing in the world" (003-002).

He describes [[letter-as-plot-device|letter]]s with connoisseur's language: "more edifying than the Moscow Gazette" (003-002). He kept a lieutenant's [[letter-as-plot-device|letter]] about a ball: "'I live in the regions of the Empyrean, lots of girls, bands playing, flags flying.' He's put a lot of feeling" into it (003-002). The [[post-office|post]]master is an amateur critic of stolen [[letter-as-plot-device|correspondence]].

[[ivan-khlestakov|Khlestakov]], predictably, borrows 300 rubles from him (035-002). Shpekin complies eagerly: "I am ready to serve you with all my heart" (035-002).

## The Letter

The [[post-office|post]] office becomes the engine of the play's climax. A [[letter-as-plot-device|letter]] arrives addressed to Pochtamtskaya Street in [[st-petersburg|St. Petersburg]]. Shpekin recognizes the handwriting: "I see Pochtamtskaya Street. I was struck dumb. 'Well,' I think to myself, 'I suppose he found something wrong in the [[post-office|post]] office department and is informing the government.' So I unsealed it" (056-002).

His description of opening the [[letter-as-plot-device|letter]] is a set-piece of Gogolian comedy. "I was overcome by a greater curiosity than I have ever felt in my life. 'I can't, I can't,' I hear a voice telling me. 'I can't.' But it pulled me and pulled me" (056-002). The devil whispers "Open it, open it." When he breaks the seal: "I felt as if I were on fire; and when I opened the [[letter-as-plot-device|letter]], I froze" (056-002).

The [[letter-as-plot-device|letter]]'s description of Shpekin is devastating: "The [[ivan-shpekin|Postmaster]] is just like the porter Mikheyev in our office, and the scoundrel must drink just as hard" (056-002). The man who read everyone's [[letter-as-plot-device|letter]]s is now read by everyone — and found ridiculous.

## Significance

The [[post-office|post]] office is where Gogol's surveillance theme finds literal expression. Officials spy on citizens; the [[anton-antonovich|Governor]] spies for denunciations; the [[post-office|post]]master reads everyone's mail. The reversal comes through the same mechanism. Shpekin opens [[ivan-khlestakov|Khlestakov]]'s [[letter-as-plot-device|letter]] expecting a government report; he finds mockery of everyone — including himself, "just like the porter Mikheyev" (056-002).
