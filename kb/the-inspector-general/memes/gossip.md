# Gossip

## Definition

Gossip in Gogol's provincial Russia functions as both a social pastime and an information network. In the absence of reliable news sources, the townspeople rely on word-of-mouth, which transforms fragmentary observations into elaborate narratives. Gossip is not merely idle talk — it is the medium through which the town collectively constructs and dismantles its reality.

## In the Play

Gossip is the engine of the plot. The entire crisis begins when Bobchinsky and Dobchinsky, having observed a well-dressed stranger at the inn who has been there two weeks without paying, conclude he must be the inspector general traveling incognito. Their breathless report to the Governor — "An extraordinary occurrence! An unexpected piece of news!" (004-002) — sets in motion the machinery of panic, bribery, and self-deception that constitutes the play's action.

The Governor's wife, Anna Andreyevna, embodies the town's insatiable appetite for gossip. She sends her servant repeatedly to gather intelligence about the visitor, demanding descriptions of his appearance, rank, and manner. "An old officer's wife will gossip, or a pretty chambermaid will gossip" (009-001) — the line captures the universality of the practice across social strata.

By Act V, gossip has become self-devouring. The Postmaster, who habitually opens private correspondence, reads Khlestakov's letter aloud, exposing the imposture. The town's collective construction collapses in an instant. The final revelation is itself delivered through the same gossip channels that created the illusion — underscoring gossip's essential unreliability even when it tells the truth.

## Satirical Function

Gossip in *The Inspector General* exposes the town's intellectual poverty. Lacking any external standard of verification, the characters treat the most flimsy evidence — a stranger's fashionable coat, his refusal to pay the bill — as conclusive proof of high rank. Gogol's satire cuts in two directions: against provincial credulity, but also against a system in which an inspector traveling incognito is so plausible that any stranger might be one.
