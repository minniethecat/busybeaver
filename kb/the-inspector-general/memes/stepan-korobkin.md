# Stepan Korobkin

**Stepan Ivanovich Korobkin** is a country squire and local notable in the provincial town. He is listed in the *Dramatis Personae* alongside his wife and appears in the final acts of the play.

## Role in the Play

Korobkin is introduced in the *Dramatis Personae* as a "country squire" [P02-017], one of the local personages who populate the provincial society. His wife is also listed separately — she appears as a distinct character who engages with Anna Andreyevna in social settings [P02-019].

In Act IV, Korobkin serves as a social connector among the town officials — Bobchinsky mentions running to find him to share news [004-002], and later Anna Andreyevna sends her daughter to seek out both Korobkin and Ammos Fiodorovich [026-002].

## In the Final Act

Korobkin plays a minor but visible role in Act V's climactic gathering at the Governor's house. He enters with his wife and Liuliukov [052-001], and offers formal congratulations to the Governor on the impending marriage between his daughter and Khlestakov [052-002].

During the letter-reading finale, Korobkin is present and notably interrupts Artemy Zemlianika's stammering attempt to read the letter aloud [056-002]. The final stage directions place him among the assembled company, "turned toward the audience" in the tableau of shock that closes the play [058-001].
