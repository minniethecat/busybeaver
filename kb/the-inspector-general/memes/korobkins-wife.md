# Korobkin's Wife

## Identity

Korobkin's wife, identified in the Dramatis Personae as "Liuliukov, Korobkin's wife" [P02-019], is a minor character who appears in Act V of Gogol's *The Inspector General*. She belongs to the circle of town notables — the wives and families of the "esteemed personages" — who populate the Governor's social gatherings. Her husband is [[korobkin|Stepan Korobkin]], a retired official and one of the town's respected citizens.

## Role in the Play

Korobkin's wife appears in Act V, Scene IV as part of the procession of well-wishers who arrive to congratulate [[anton-antonovich|the Governor]] and his family on the news of [[marya-antonovna|Marya Antonovna]]'s engagement to the supposed high-ranking official [052-001]. Her function, like that of the other guests in this scene, is to illustrate the sycophancy and [[social-status|social climbing]] that define provincial society.

Her congratulatory address follows the formula established by the other visitors:

> "I have the honor to congratulate you, Anna Andreyevna." — She kisses her hand and turns to the audience, smacks his lips, putting on a bold front. "Marya Antonovna, I have the honor to congratulate you." — Kisses her hand and turns to the audience in the same way. [052-002]

The stage direction is revealing: her performance of deference is directed as much to the audience as to the Governor's family. The "bold front" and the conspicuous hand-kissing — turned outward for maximum visibility — transform a private congratulation into a public display of social allegiance. Gogol's satire here targets not merely the Governor's vanity but the entire social ecosystem that enables it: a world in which every gesture is calculated for its effect on spectators, and sincerity is indistinguishable from performance.

Korobkin's wife, like the other town notables who flood the Governor's house in Act V, participates in what the play presents as a ritual of [[gossip|collective self-congratulation]]. The news of the engagement — which the audience knows to be based on a complete misapprehension — spreads through the town's social network as a contagion of ambition, each visitor hoping to secure a position in the reflected glory of the Governor's imagined elevation.

## Key Scenes

- **(052-001–052-002, Act V Scene IV)**: Arrives at the Governor's house to congratulate Anna Andreyevna and Marya Antonovna on the engagement, performing elaborate deference for the benefit of all present.

## Relationships

- **[[korobkin|Stepan Korobkin]]**: Her husband, a retired official and town notable.
- **[[anna-andreyevna|Anna Andreyevna]]**: The Governor's wife, whom she flatters with ceremonial hand-kissing.
- **[[marya-antonovna|Marya Antonovna]]**: The Governor's daughter, the supposed fiancée whom she also congratulates.
- **[[anton-antonovich|The Governor]]**: The beneficiary of her social performance, though she addresses his wife and daughter rather than him directly.
