# Ivanova

## Identity

Ivanova is identified in the Dramatis Personae as "the widow of a noncommissioned officer" [P02-027]. She is one of the petitioners who approach [[ivan-khlestakov|Khlestakov]] in Act IV to seek justice against [[anton-antonovich|the Governor]]'s abuses, alongside the locksmith's wife [[poshliopkina|Fevronya Poshliopkina]] and other aggrieved townspeople.

## Role in the Play

Ivanova's appearance in Act IV serves as a crucial counterpoint to the Governor's self-serving narrative. Earlier in the play, the Governor dismissively mentions having "flogged the wife of a noncommissioned officer" as a minor infraction [004-002], treating it as routine administration. When Ivanova finally appears before Khlestakov, she presents the other side of the story [042-001]:

> "Ivanova, widow of a noncommissioned officer of this town. My father, a noncommissioned officer in the local garrison, was killed in action during the last war." [042-002]

Her petition reveals the Governor's casual brutality. She was flogged, she claims, without cause — an accusation the Governor later attempts to deflect by insisting "she flogged herself" [046-002]. Ivanova never receives justice within the play; Khlestakov accepts her petition but does nothing with it. Her presence, like that of the other petitioners, accumulates evidence of the Governor's corruption that the audience — if not the characters — can plainly see.

Ivanova's complaint touches on a sensitive legal question in Imperial Russia: whether the widow of a noncommissioned officer could lawfully be subjected to corporal punishment. The Governor's defensive insistence that she flogged herself — a transparently absurd claim — reveals his awareness that the flogging was illegal. Gogol uses this detail to expose not just the Governor's brutality but his contempt for the [[justice|law]] itself.

- **(042-001–042-002)**: Appears before Khlestakov as a petitioner, presenting her case against the Governor.

## Relationships

- **[[anton-antonovich|The Governor]]**: Her antagonist; the official who ordered her flogging and later lies about it.
- **[[ivan-khlestakov|Khlestakov]]**: The supposed inspector to whom she appeals for justice, without result.
- **[[poshliopkina|Fevronya Poshliopkina]]**: Fellow petitioner; the locksmith's wife who also seeks redress from the Governor.
