# Derzhimorda

## Identity

Derzhimorda is one of three police sergeants in the provincial town, listed in the Dramatis Personae with Svistunov and Pugovitzyn (P02-023). His name, derived from Russian roots suggesting forceful restraint, reflects his role as the Governor's heavy-handed enforcer.

## Role in the Play

Derzhimorda appears in Acts I, IV, and V as an instrument of municipal order. The Governor specifically warns Captain Ukhovertov to "tell Derzhimorda not to be so free with his fists. Guilty or innocent, he makes them all see stars in the cause of public order" (006-002), establishing the character's reputation for brutality as a matter of official policy.

## Key Scenes

- **(006-002)**: The Governor inquires after Derzhimorda's whereabouts when Ukhovertov reports for duty; the Captain explains he has gone off on police business. The Governor then delivers the instruction to restrain Derzhimorda's violence.
- **(028-002)**: In Act IV, the Governor summons Derzhimorda and Svistunov to guard Khlestakov's quarters against petitioners, particularly merchants seeking to present complaints.
- **(030-001)**: Derzhimorda and Svistunov appear together, receiving explicit orders. When Derzhimorda begins to speak — "I had your order—" — the Governor claps a hand over his mouth: "Hush! Like a bull bellowing. Makes a noise like an empty barrel" (030-002).
- **(040-002)**: In Act V, Derzhimorda's voice is heard shouting "Where are you going, whiskers? You've been told that nobody is allowed to come in," as he blocks petitioners while Khlestakov seals his letter (040-002).

## Relationships

- **Stepan Ukhovertov** (police captain): Derzhimorda's immediate superior, responsible for his conduct.
- **Svistunov**: Fellow police sergeant; the two are frequently paired for crowd-control duties.
- **Anton Antonovich** (Governor): The ultimate authority, who both deploys Derzhimorda's violence and occasionally attempts to moderate it for appearances' sake.

## Character Analysis

Derzhimorda embodies the physical violence underpinning the provincial administration. The Governor's instruction to restrain him is telling: the concern is not with the sergeant's brutality per se, but with its visibility, which might attract unwanted attention from the supposed inspector. The Governor's mockery of Derzhimorda's voice — "Like a bull bellowing" (030-002) — underscores the contempt in which even his own superiors hold the enforcer.
