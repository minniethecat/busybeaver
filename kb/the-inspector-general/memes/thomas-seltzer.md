Thomas Seltzer (1875–1943) was a Russian-American translator, editor, and publisher. He translated numerous works of Russian literature into English, including Maxim Gorky, Leonid Andreyev, and Nikolai Gogol.

His translation of Gogol's *The Inspector General* was published as part of the Project Gutenberg collection. Seltzer also founded his own publishing house, Thomas Seltzer, Inc., which published works by D. H. Lawrence and other modernist authors.
