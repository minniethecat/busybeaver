# Stepan Korobkin

## Identity

Stepan Ivanovich Korobkin is a retired ex-official and esteemed personage of the provincial town (P02-017). Listed alongside Fiodr Luliukov and Ivan Rastakovsky in the Dramatis Personae, he belongs to the stratum of town notables who exist outside the active bureaucracy but remain deeply enmeshed in its social rituals. His wife -- identified simply as "Korobkin's wife" -- appears alongside him throughout Act V (P02-019).

## Role in the Play

Korobkin embodies the provincial town's social establishment -- the class of former officials and respectable citizens who confer legitimacy on the Governor's regime through their participation in its ceremonies. He enters the play only in Act V, arriving with his wife to offer congratulations on what the town believes is the Governor's triumph: his daughter's betrothal to the supposed inspector general (052-001). His presence in these scenes reveals the web of mutual performance that sustains provincial society: the notables flatter the Governor, the Governor basks in their flattery, and both parties understand the transaction perfectly.

Korobkin's most consequential moment comes during the climactic letter-reading scene (056-002). When [[artemy-zemlianika|Zemlianika]] stammers over the description of himself in [[ivan-khlestakov|Khlestakov]]'s letter, Korobkin presses him to continue and ultimately wrests the letter from the reluctant Superintendent of Charities. His insistence on reading aloud -- "Give it to me. I believe my eyesight is better" (056-002) -- ensures that every official's humiliation is made public. It is Korobkin who reads the most damning lines: that Zemlianika is "a regular pig in a cap," that [[luka-khlopov|the School Inspector]] "reeks of onions," and that [[ammos-liapkin-tiapkin|the Judge]] is "extremely *mauvais ton*" (056-002). His eagerness to expose the officials reverses his earlier posture of deference, suggesting that the social hierarchy in the town is a thin veneer over mutual contempt.

## Key Scenes

- **P02-017**: Listed in the Dramatis Personae as "Stepan Ivanovich Korobkin," an ex-official and esteemed personage of the town.
- **004-002**: Bobchinsky mentions visiting Korobkin's house during the frantic moment when the officials first believe an inspector has arrived -- finding him not at home.
- **052-001**: Enters Act V with his wife, joining the assembled guests at the Governor's residence. Steps forward to congratulate the Governor and kiss the hands of Anna Andreyevna and Marya Antonovna (052-002).
- **055-002**: During the extended congratulatory scene, his wife discusses greyhounds with Anna Andreyevna. Korobkin himself inquires after the "distinguished guest" and asks the Governor to act as patron to his son, whom he plans to place in government service: "Next year I am going to take my son to the capital to put him in government service. So do me the kindness to give me your protection. Be a father to the orphan" (055-002).
- **056-002**: The pivotal moment in the letter-reading scene. When Zemlianika falters, Korobkin seizes the letter and reads aloud the insults directed at each official, one by one -- Zemlianika ("a regular pig in a cap"), the School Inspector ("reeks of onions"), and the Judge ("extremely *mauvais ton*").
- **058-001**: In the silent-scene tableau, Korobkin stands frozen "turned toward the audience, with eyes screwed up and making a venomous gesture at the Governor" -- a posture of indignant contempt that caps his transformation from flattering notable to accusatory witness.

## Relationships

- **[[anton-antonovich]]** (Governor): Korobkin's relationship with the Governor is defined by the rituals of provincial sycophancy. In Act V he is all congratulations and hand-kissing (052-002); by the silent scene he is making "a venomous gesture" at him (058-001). The arc from deference to disgust traces the collapse of the Governor's authority in real time.
- **[[artemy-zemlianika]]** (Superintendent of Charities): It is Korobkin who overrides Zemlianika's attempt to suppress the letter's contents and reads aloud his humiliating description (056-002).
- **[[ivan-shpekin|The Postmaster]]**: While the Postmaster brings the letter, it is Korobkin who ensures its full public reading, making him a secondary agent of the exposure.
- **[[ivan-khlestakov|Ivan Khlestakov]]**: Though they never meet directly, Khlestakov's letter -- and Korobkin's insistence on reading it aloud -- is the instrument of the town's undoing.
