# Ivan Rastakovsky

## Identity

Ivan Lazarevich Rastakovsky is listed in the Dramatis Personae among the "ex-officials, esteemed personages of the town," alongside Fiodr Luliukov and Stepan Korobkin (P02-016). He represents the retired gentry class whose social standing, though diminished from active service, still commands a measure of deference.

## Role in the Play

Rastakovsky appears exclusively in Act V, where his function is to populate the Governor's triumph. As news of Marya Antonovna's engagement to the supposed high-ranking official spreads, the town's notables rush to offer congratulations. Rastakovsky's presence among them illustrates the Governor's social ascent and the network of obligation and flattery that constitutes provincial society.

## Key Scenes

- **(004-002)**: Rastakovsky is mentioned in passing by Bobchinsky, who, in his breathless recounting of the day's events, notes that after failing to find Korobkin at home, "I went off to Rastakovsky, and not seeing him, I went to Ivan Kuzmich" — indicating Rastakovsky's place in the town's social circuit, even before the crisis fully unfolds.
- **(051-001)**: In Act V, Rastakovsky is among the guests who arrive to congratulate the Governor, entering after Artemy Filippovich.
- **(051-002)**: Rastakovsky formally offers his congratulations: "I congratulate you, Anton Antonovich. May God give you and the new couple long life and may He grant you numerous progeny — grandchildren and greatgrandchildren" (051-002), kissing the hands of both Anna Andreyevna and Marya Antonovna.
- **(055-002)**: When the Governor boasts of attaining a generalship, Rastakovsky offers the cautionary observation: "From man it is impossible, but from God everything is possible" (055-002) — a remark that simultaneously flatters and gently deflates the Governor's hubris.

## Relationships

- **Anton Antonovich** (Governor): The object of Rastakovsky's congratulatory visit; a social peer whose elevation Rastakovsky acknowledges.
- **Korobkin**: Fellow "esteemed personage" and ex-official; part of the same social circle.
- **Luliukov**: The third member of the trio of retired notables.

## Character Analysis

Rastakovsky's single-scene appearance in Act V serves a structural purpose: it populates the Governor's moment of apparent victory with witnesses. His blessing — invoking God's will as a counterweight to the Governor's ambition (055-002) — introduces a subtle note of dramatic irony. The audience knows the Governor's triumph is built on a misidentification that is about to collapse, making Rastakovsky's pious platitudes a prelude to the coming reversal.
