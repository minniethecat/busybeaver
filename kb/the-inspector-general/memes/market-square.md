# Market Square

## Overview

The market square is the commercial heart of the provincial town, where [[merchant-class|merchants]] like [[abdulin|Abdulin]] conduct their trade. It functions as a site of economic exchange, public gathering, and official predation — the [[anton-antonovich|Governor]]'s extortion is most visible here.

## In the Play

The market first appears in the [[anton-antonovich|Governor]]'s preparations. He orders the streets leading to the inn swept clean (005-002), but the market square itself remains unimproved — there is no cosmetic fix for systematic exploitation.

The [[merchant-class|merchants]] who petition [[ivan-khlestakov|Khlestakov]] describe their treatment in detail. "He catches hold of your beard and says, 'Oh, you Tartar!'" (041-002). He takes goods without payment and demands tribute on multiple name days: "He says St. Onufry's is also his name day. What's to be done? You have to take things to him on St. Onufry's day, too" (041-002).

The [[merchant-class|merchants]] describe a reign of extortion: "When you see him coming you hide everything in the shop. It isn't only that he wants a few delicacies and fineries. He takes every bit of trash, too — prunes that have been in the barrel seven years" (041-002). The [[anton-antonovich|Governor]]'s greed is indiscriminate; quality is irrelevant when everything is free.

The locksmith's wife, [[poshliopkina|Fevronya Poshliopkina]], describes another dimension of market justice. "Our women got into a squabble in the market, and when the [[police-department|police]] came, it was all over" (042-002) — the market is where grievances surface and are summarily suppressed.

## Key Figures

**[[abdulin|Abdulin]]** is the principal merchant mentioned. The [[anton-antonovich|Governor]] denounces him for failing to provide a new sword: "It's that dog of a merchant, [[abdulin|Abdulin]]. He sees the [[anton-antonovich|Governor]]'s sword is old and doesn't provide a new one" (005-002). The [[anton-antonovich|Governor]]'s expectation of a free sword from a merchant is treated as normal — an entitlement that goes unexamined.

**[[poshliopkina|Fevronya Poshliopkina]]** is [[poshliopkina|the locksmith's wife]] whose husband was un[[justice|law]]fully conscripted. Her story originates in the market place (042-002), where a squabble escalated into [[police-department|police]] intervention and ultimately a forced conscription.

## Significance

The market is where Gogol's system of [[bribery|corruption]] is most visible. Unlike the [[charitable-institutions|hospital]] or [[district-courthouse|courthouse]] — seen only by officials — the market is a public stage. The [[anton-antonovich|Governor]] extracts goods for his house (041-002) and demands gifts on multiple name days. The [[merchant-class|merchants]] comply not out of respect but because refusal means "quartering soldiers" (041-002).
