# Christian Hubner

## Identity

Christian Ivanovich Hubner is the district doctor of the unnamed provincial town, listed in the [[dramatis-personae|Dramatis Personae]] as "Christian Ivanovich Hubner, the district doctor" (P02-014). His German surname and patronymic mark him as one of the many Baltic German professionals who served in the Russian imperial bureaucracy, but his defining comic trait is that he "doesn't know a word of Russian" (002-002) -- a detail that renders his medical expertise, whatever it may be, entirely inaccessible to his patients.

## Role in the Play

Hubner is a character of pure physical presence rather than speech. He appears in the Governor's opening meeting (002-001) but never speaks a single line -- only emitting, twice, "a sound intermediate between *M* and *A*" (002-002). This nearly wordless performance is itself a devastating satirical comment: the man charged with the health of the town's indigent cannot communicate with anyone under his care. He is the medical counterpart to the [[office-of-charities|Office of Charities]]' institutional neglect, his silence the professional equivalent of the hospital's missing church and filthy wards.

The Governor treats Hubner as a utility. He calls on him to write Latin placards over the hospital beds (002-002) and to provide nostrums for the alcohol-soaked assessor (002-002) -- tasks that reduce medicine to window-dressing and quackery. Artemy Zemlianika, the Superintendent of Charities, later reveals the philosophy they share: "If he dies, he'd die anyway. If he gets well, he'd get well anyway" (002-002). Hubner's silence suggests complicity in this fatalism.

## Key Scenes

- **(P02-014)**: Listed in the Dramatis Personae as the district doctor.
- **(002-001)**: Present at the Governor's initial meeting to announce the inspector general's arrival. He is listed alongside the other officials but stands apart from their panicked dialogue.
- **(002-002)**: Twice emits an inarticulate sound when addressed on medical matters. The Governor calls him "Christian Ivanovich" while discussing hospital placards, and later suggests his "nostrums" for the assessor's vodka-stench. Zemlianika explains: "Besides, the doctor would have a hard time making the patients understand him. He doesn't know a word of Russian" (002-002).
- **(016-003)**: The Governor invokes "Christian philanthropy" in conversation with Khlestakov -- an ironic echo of the doctor's given name, though Hubner himself is not present. The phrase connects the language of Christian charity to the institutional hypocrisy Hubner embodies.

## Relationships

- **[[artemy-zemlianika|Artemy Zemlianika]]** (Superintendent of Charities): Hubner's superior. Together they have "worked out our own system" of medical neglect (002-002).
- **[[anton-antonovich|Anton Antonovich]]** (Governor): Treats Hubner as a tool for cosmetic improvements at the hospital, never consulting him on medicine.
- **The Patients of the [[office-of-charities|Office of Charities]]**: The silent, suffering recipients of Hubner's silent "care."
