Nikolai Vasilievich Gogol (1809–1852) was a Russian novelist, short story writer, and playwright of Ukrainian origin. He is considered one of the greatest figures of Russian literature and the father of modern Russian realism.

Gogol wrote *The Inspector General* (Russian: *Ревизор*) in 1835, and it was first performed in 1836 at the Alexandrinsky Theatre in St. Petersburg. The play is a comedy of errors satirizing the corruption and bureaucratic stupidity of provincial Russian officialdom. It is said that Tsar Nicholas I himself laughed heartily at the premiere and remarked, "Everyone gets it, and I most of all."

His other major works include *Dead Souls* (1842), *The Overcoat* (1842), and the collection of Ukrainian stories *Evenings on a Farm Near Dikanka* (1831–1832).
