# Fevronya Poshliopkina

## Identity

Fevronya Petrova Poshliopkina is listed in the Dramatis Personae as "Fevronya Petrova Poshliopkina, the locksmith's wife" (P02-026). She is a burgess of the provincial town and one of the citizens who seek to present petitions to the supposed inspector general.

## Role in the Play

Poshliopkina appears in Act IV as one of the petitioners who attempt to reach Khlestakov with complaints against the Governor. Her grievance — that her husband was unlawfully conscripted into military service — represents the most concrete and personal of the abuses detailed by the townspeople. Unlike the merchants whose complaints center on economic exploitation, Poshliopkina's petition concerns the destruction of a household through corrupt administration.

## Key Scenes

- **(042-002)**: Poshliopkina presents herself before Khlestakov, identifying herself as "Fevronya Petrova Poshliopkina, the wife of a locksmith, a burgess of this town" (042-002). She begins to state her case, starting with her father's identity, before the scene is interrupted by the arrival of other petitioners and the escalating chaos of the petition scene.

## Relationships

- **Her husband** (the locksmith): The victim of the Governor's unlawful conscription order, whose absence defines Poshliopkina's grievance.
- **Anton Antonovich** (Governor): The object of her complaint; the official who conscripted her husband.
- **The Other Petitioners**: Fellow citizens seeking redress, including the merchants and the widow of the noncommissioned officer.

## Character Analysis

Poshliopkina represents the most vulnerable stratum of provincial society: women whose families are destroyed by arbitrary official action. Her petition gives voice to a specific, personal injury that cuts through the play's broader satire with a note of genuine grievance. The conscription of her husband exemplifies the Governor's abuse of power — military service is deployed not as a civic duty but as a tool of personal revenge against those who cannot pay sufficient tribute.
