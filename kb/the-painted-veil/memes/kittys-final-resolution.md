# Kitty's Final Resolution

## Overview

The novel's final scene: [[kitty-fane|Kitty]], having returned to [[london|England]], goes to her father — a man she barely knows — and asks to accompany him to his new judicial post in the Bahamas (080-018). She tells him she wants her child (a daughter, she hopes) to be raised differently: not to depend on male admiration, not to trade on beauty, but to be "free and fearless" (080-058). It is the most hopeful moment in a novel that has earned its hope through suffering.
