# Kitty's Return to Hong Kong

## Overview

After [[death|Walter's death]], [[kitty-fane|Kitty]] returns to [[hong-kong|Hong Kong]] (070-001), where [[dorothy-townsend|Dorothy Townsend]] insists she stay at the [[charles-townsend|Townsend]]s' house. In a moment of weakness and self-loathing, [[kitty-fane|Kitty]] succumbs again to [[charles-townsend|Townsend]]'s advances (076-015). This relapse is more devastating than the original [[adultery|affair]] because [[kitty-fane|Kitty]] now knows better — she is no longer the innocent adulteress but someone who has seen truth and chosen illusion (070-002).

## Key Passages

Kitty's return to Hong Kong begins not with reunion but with the unspoken weight of her own emotional failure:

> At first because she had not wept when Walter died she was ashamed. It seemed dreadfully callous. Why, the eyes of the Chinese officer, Colonel Yü, had been wet with tears. She was dazed by her husband's death.
>
> (070-001)
