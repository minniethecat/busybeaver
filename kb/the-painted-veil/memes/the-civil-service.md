# The British Civil Service

## Overview

The Civil Service is the career framework within which the novel's male characters operate. It is an institution that, the novel suggests, is poorly designed to distinguish between genuine merit and social performance. [[charles-townsend|Townsend]] rises; [[walter-fane|Walter]] is overlooked (001-034). Only the cholera [[cholera|epidemic]] — a crisis that demands actual competence — reveals the institution's failure to value what matters (035-012). Townsend's own position is protected not by talent but by social connections: even scandal may not force him from the service (025-024).
