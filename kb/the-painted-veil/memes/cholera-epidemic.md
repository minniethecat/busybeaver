# The Cholera Epidemic

## Overview

The cholera [[cholera|epidemic]] is the novel's central event in its second half. It provides the context for [[walter-fane|Walter]]'s heroic medical work (022-019), [[kitty-fane|Kitty]]'s moral awakening through service at the convent, and the novel's meditation on death as the ultimate reality that renders social values meaningless (034-002).

## Significance

The [[cholera|epidemic]] is both historical reality and moral metaphor — the [[cholera|disease]] that kills indiscriminately, revealing the equality that social convention denies (039-014). It is in the [[cholera|epidemic]]'s shadow that the novel's characters discover what they truly value.

> "No," she said. "You've inspired me with courage. To go into the midst of a cholera epidemic will be a unique experience and if I die of it—well, I die."
>
> (026-060)
