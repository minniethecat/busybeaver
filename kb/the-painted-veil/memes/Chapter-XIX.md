# Chapter XIX

[019-001]As soon as she could next morning she rang [[charles-townsend|Townsend]] up at his office.

[019-002]“Yes, what is it?”

[019-003]“I want to see you.”

[019-004]“My dear, I’m awfully busy. I’m a working man.”

[019-005]“It’s very important. Can I come down to the office?”

[019-006]“Oh, no, I wouldn’t do that if I were you.”

[019-007]“Well, come here then.”

[019-008]“I can’t possibly get away. What about this afternoon? And don’t you think it would be better if I didn’t come to your house?”

[019-009]“I must see you at once.”

[019-010]There was a pause and she was afraid that she had been cut off.

[019-011]“Are you there?” she asked anxiously.

[019-012]“Yes, I was thinking. Has anything happened?”

[019-013]“I can’t tell you over the telephone.”

[019-014]There was another silence before he spoke again.

[019-015]“Well, look here, I can manage to see you for ten minutes at one if that’ll do. You’d better go to Ku-Chou’s and I’ll come along as soon as I can.”

[019-016]“The curio shop?” she asked in dismay.

[019-017]“Well, we can’t meet in the lounge at the [[hong-kong|Hong Kong]] Hotel very well,” he answered.

[019-018]She noticed a trace of irritation in his voice.

[019-019]“Very well. I’ll go to Ku-Chou’s.”
