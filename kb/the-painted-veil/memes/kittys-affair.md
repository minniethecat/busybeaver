# Kitty's Affair with Townsend

## Overview

The central event of the novel's first half. [[kitty-fane|Kitty]], starved for admiration in her marriage to [[walter-fane|Walter]], falls completely for [[charles-townsend|Townsend]]'s practiced charm (015-002). Their [[adultery|affair]], conducted in the Fanes' bungalow and later at a curio shop, represents [[love|romantic love]] as [[illusion|self-deception]] — [[kitty-fane|Kitty]] projects heroic qualities onto a man who possesses none of them.

## Significance

[[kitty-fane|Kitty]]'s discovery that [[charles-townsend|Townsend]] will not sacrifice his career for her — "He loves me with all his heart and soul" (023-025) giving way to devastating [[illusion|disillusion]] — is her first genuine moral education.

> "He loves me with all his heart and soul. He loves me as passionately as I love him. You've found out. I'm not going to deny anything. Why should I? We've been lovers for a year and I'm proud of it. He means everything in the world to me and I'm glad that you know at last."
>
> (023-025)
