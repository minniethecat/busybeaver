# The Morning After

## Overview

Waking in the [[charles-townsend|Townsend]]s' house after her relapse, [[kitty-fane|Kitty]] is seized with [[humiliation|shame]]. Townsend stands over her with self-satisfaction; she can only repeat "I'm so ashamed" (076-008). She sees herself clearly for perhaps the first time: not as the romantic heroine of an adulterous [[desire|passion]] but as a woman who has chosen, with full knowledge, to repeat her worst mistake. Looking at her reflection, weeping bitterly, she confronts the full horror of her relapse: she had thought herself free, but she was a slave — "Weak, weak! It was hopeless, it was no good to try, she was a slut" (076-015). This [[humiliation|shame]] is different from her earlier fear of exposure — it is the [[humiliation|shame]] of self-knowledge, and it drives her final, irreversible decision to leave [[hong-kong|Hong Kong]].
