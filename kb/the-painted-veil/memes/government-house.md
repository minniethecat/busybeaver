# Government House

## Overview

Government House is the official residence of the Governor of Hong Kong and the symbolic apex of British colonial society. It appears early in the novel when Kitty, as a bride, is taken in to dinner by the Governor — a moment of social triumph that is immediately undercut by her realization that "as the wife of the Government bacteriologist she was of no particular consequence" (004-001).

## Description

The novel provides little physical description of Government House, treating it as a social symbol rather than an architectural space. Its significance lies entirely in what it represents: the summit of the colonial hierarchy that Kitty, through her marriage, had hoped to ascend.

Kitty's first encounter with the realities of colonial social hierarchy is crystallized at Government House:

> when they dined at Government House the Governor took her in as a bride; but she had understood quickly that as the wife of the Government bacteriologist she was of no particular consequence. It made her angry.
>
> (004-001)

## Events at This Location

- Kitty's attendance at a Government House dinner as a new bride (004-001)
- Her realization that her husband's profession determines her social standing, not her beauty or charm

## Associated Characters

- [[Kitty Fane]] — who briefly tastes the social prominence she craves
- [[Walter Fane]] — whose professional position limits his wife's social ascent
- [[Charles Townsend]] — a regular presence at Government House functions

## Symbolic Significance

Government House represents the social hierarchy that the novel critiques — a world where status is determined by official position rather than human worth. Kitty's exclusion from its inner circle, despite her beauty, is the novel's first hint that the values she has been taught are inadequate.
