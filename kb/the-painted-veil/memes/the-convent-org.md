# The Convent

## Overview

The convent is the novel's most important institution. Founded by French missionaries (022-022), it operates an orphanage, an infirmary, and a school in one of China's poorest regions (039-006). Its members — women who have given up family, country, and comfort — embody a form of institutional life organized entirely around service. The convent represents the novel's vision of what an organization can be when it is animated by love rather than power. Even the skeptical Waddington acknowledges that the nuns at the convent swear by Walter (038-035).
