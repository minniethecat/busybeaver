# Colonel Yü

## Overview

Colonel Yü is the Chinese military commander stationed in Mei-tan-fu (031-017). He appears only briefly in the novel but represents an important counterpoint to the British colonial characters: a figure of indigenous authority whose competence requires no British validation. His cooperation with Walter's cholera-control efforts (038-035) demonstrates the practical alliances that can exist across colonial divides.

## Background

Colonel Yü is a career military officer serving the Chinese government. Unlike the British characters, who are posted to the East temporarily, Colonel Yü belongs to China — his authority derives from the Chinese state and his presence requires no colonial justification.

## Role in the Novel

Colonel Yü appears in scenes involving the cholera epidemic response, where he cooperates with Walter's public health measures. His presence reminds the reader that the British are not the only — or even the primary — actors in Mei-tan-fu. The Chinese have their own structures of authority, their own responses to the crisis, their own perspective on the British interlopers.

## Key Passages

> [Colonel Yü] was a tall, stout man with a round, smooth face. (036-001)

## Relationships

- [[Walter Fane]] — collaborator in cholera response; their professional relationship crosses colonial boundaries
- [[Waddington]] — acquaintance; Waddington's familiarity with Chinese officials distinguishes him from other British characters

## Significance

Colonel Yü represents the Chinese world that British colonial fiction typically marginalizes. His presence — brief but dignified — is the novel's acknowledgment that the British story is not the only story being told in Mei-tan-fu.
