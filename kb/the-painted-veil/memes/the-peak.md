# The Peak

## Overview

The Peak is the highest residential district on Hong Kong Island, where the Governor's residence and the homes of the most senior colonial officials are located. Its physical elevation mirrors the social elevation of its inhabitants — a geography of exclusion that Kitty can see but never fully enter (020-001).

## Description

The Peak commands a view of the harbor and the city below. It is where the truly powerful live, visible from the Pleasant Vale where the Fanes reside but socially inaccessible to those of Walter's middling rank (003-002).

## Events at This Location

- Townsend's social life revolves around Peak society
- Kitty's exclusion from the Peak represents her social limitation as Walter's wife

## Associated Characters

- [[Charles Townsend]] — a regular presence in Peak society
- [[Dorothy Townsend]] — the competent Peak hostess

## Symbolic Significance

The Peak represents the social summit Kitty aspires to. Its elevation makes exclusion literal: the powerful look down on everyone else.
