# Chapter LXV

[065-001]They buried him three hours later. It seemed horrible to [[kitty-fane|Kitty]] that he must be put into a Chinese coffin, as though in so strange a bed he must rest uneasily, but there was no help for it. The nuns, learning of [[walter-fane|Walter]]’s death as they learned everything that happened in the city, sent by a messenger a cross of dahlias, stiff and formal, but made as though by the accustomed hands of a florist; and the cross, alone on the Chinese coffin, looked grotesque and out of place. When all was ready they had to wait for [[colonel-yu|Colonel Yü]] who had sent to [[waddington|Waddington]] to say that he desired to attend the funeral. He came accompanied by an A.D.C. They walked up the hill, the coffin borne by half a dozen coolies, to a little plot of land where lay buried the missionary whose place Walter had taken. Waddington had found among the missionary’s effects an English prayerbook and in a low voice, with an embarrassment that was unusual to him, read the burial service. Perhaps, reciting those solemn but terrible words, the thought hovered in his mind that if he in his turn fell a victim to the [[cholera|pestilence]] there would be no one now to say them over him. The coffin was lowered into the grave and the gravediggers began to throw in the earth.

[065-002]Colonel Yü, who had stood with bared head by the grave-side, put on his hat, saluted Kitty gravely, said a word or two to Waddington and followed by his A.D.C. walked away. The coolies, curious to watch a Christian burial, had lingered and now in a straggling group, their yokes trailing in their hands, sauntered off. Kitty and Waddington waited till the grave was filled and then placed on the mound, smelling of fresh earth, the nuns’ prim dahlias. She had not wept, but when the first shovelful of earth rattled on the coffin she felt a dreadful pang at her heart.

[065-003]She saw that Waddington was waiting for her to come away.

[065-004]“Are you in a hurry?” she asked. “I don’t want to go back to [[fanes-bungalow|the bungalow]] just yet.”

[065-005]“I have nothing to do. I am entirely in your hands.”
