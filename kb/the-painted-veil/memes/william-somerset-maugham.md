## Overview

William Somerset Maugham (1874–1965) was one of the most popular English writers of the early twentieth century. *The Painted Veil*, published in 1925, draws on his knowledge of British colonial society and his travels in East Asia.

## The Painted Veil

The novel was inspired in part by Maugham's travels through China and Hong Kong in the early 1920s. The title is taken from Percy Bysshe Shelley's sonnet "Lift not the painted veil which those who live / Call Life."

The story reflects Maugham's characteristic concerns: the constraints of social convention, the complexities of marriage, and the possibility of moral growth through suffering.

## Literary Significance

Maugham was known for his clear, unadorned prose style and his unsentimental view of human nature. *The Painted Veil* exemplifies both qualities, offering a portrait of colonial society that is both satirical and compassionate.
