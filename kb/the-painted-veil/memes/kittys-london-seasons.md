# Kitty's London Seasons

## Overview

For several [[london|London]] seasons, [[kitty-fane|Kitty]] was the most beautiful debutante, admired and courted (008-004). But no proposal met her mother's standard of "brilliance" (008-005). As the seasons passed and [[kitty-fane|Kitty]]'s beauty began to fade, the pool of eligible bachelors sh[[social-class|rank]]. The refusal of a widowed Knight of the Bath (010-017) — her last serious prospect before [[walter-fane|Walter]] — represents the moment when [[kitty-fane|Kitty]]'s romantic ambitions collided with social reality.

## Key Passages

Kitty's entrance into the London marriage market is rendered with the precision of a social historian:

> She was launched upon the world and Mrs. Garstin performed prodigies in getting herself invited to dances where her daughter might meet eligible men. Kitty was a success. She was amusing as well as beautiful, and very soon she had a dozen men in love with her.
>
> (008-004)
