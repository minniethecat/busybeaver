# Journey to Mei-tan-fu

## Overview

The journey upriver and overland to [[mei-tan-fu|Mei-tan-fu]] is [[kitty-fane|Kitty]]'s passage from one world to another. The landscape changes, the comforts of colonial life recede (026-008), and the physical hardship of the journey — heat, discomfort, fear — begins to strip away the protective layers of [[kitty-fane|Kitty]]'s social identity (029-002).

## Significance

The journey functions as a symbolic death and rebirth: [[kitty-fane|Kitty]] leaves behind the woman she was in [[hong-kong|Hong Kong]] and enters the crucible that will form the woman she becomes (030-001).

> She looked in the direction he pointed and there, on the top of a hill, saw an archway; but this one, silhouetted against the westering sun, was more fantastic and beautiful than any she had seen. Yet, she knew not why, it made her uneasy; it had a significance which she felt but could not put into words: Was it a menace that she vaguely discerned or was it derision?
>
> (030-001)
