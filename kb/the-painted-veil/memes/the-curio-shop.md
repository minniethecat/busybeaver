# The Curio Shop

## Overview

When the risk of meeting at the Fanes' bungalow becomes too great, Townsend suggests the curio shop — a rented room above a Chinese shop (003-003) — as an alternative meeting place (005-002). Its anonymity and overtones of transaction capture the nature of Kitty's relationship with Townsend: a secret exchanged for a secret, passion purchased at the cost of dignity. Kitty ascends the dark, rickety stairway to the back of the shop, each step a descent into the sordid reality of her situation (020-003).

## Description

The shop is a Chinese curio dealer's establishment with a private room above. It is anonymous, discreet, and faintly sordid — a space designed for transactions rather than intimacy.

## Events at This Location

- Kitty and Townsend's clandestine meetings after the bungalow becomes too risky
- The shift from domestic adultery to furtive assignation marks the relationship's degradation

## Associated Characters

- [[Kitty Fane]] — the adulterous wife seeking concealment
- [[Charles Townsend]] — the practiced adulterer who knows how to arrange such things

## Symbolic Significance

The curio shop represents the reduction of passion to transaction. The shift from the bungalow to the shop marks Kitty's descent from romantic self-deception into something closer to the sordid reality of her situation.
