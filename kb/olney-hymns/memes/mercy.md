## Definition

Mercy in the Olney Hymns is God's compassionate restraint — His willingness to forgive rather than condemn. When paired with grace (which gives undeserved favor), mercy withholds deserved judgment. [[adam|Adam]], after his fall, "cast on God himself the blame, nor once for mercy cried" (001-005), illustrating humanity's natural refusal to seek mercy.

## Biblical Basis

Scripture declares God "rich in mercy" (Ephesians 2:4), and the Psalms repeatedly appeal to divine mercy. The mercy seat — the covering of the Ark of the Covenant — becomes a powerful image in the hymns: "Before the mercy-seat" Christ stands with "clouds of incense from His hands" (019-003).

## In the Olney Hymns

- **The Mercy Seat**: "The sinner near despair sought thy mercy-seat by prayer; mercy heard and set him free" (010-004). The mercy seat represents access to divine forgiveness.

- **Mercy's Door**: "His mercy's door stands open wide; He hath for all prepared a place" (011-007). Mercy is not exclusive but offered freely.

- **Past Mercy as Ground for [[hope|Hope]]**: "After so much mercy past, canst thou let me sink at last?" (010-006). The believer appeals to God's track record of mercy.

- **Mercy and [[repentance|Repentance]]**: [[david|David]] "no compunction felt, till God in mercy Nathan sent" (029-005). Mercy initiates repentance.
