## Definition

Love in the Olney Hymns is primarily the divine love of God shown in Christ — a sovereign, sustaining, and personal affection that redeems and keeps the believer. [[john-newton|Newton]]'s hymns emphasize love as both God's motivation for salvation and the believer's response. "He reveals His love to me" (004-005) captures the personal, experiential nature of divine love in these hymns.

## Biblical Basis

Scripture declares "God is love" (1 John 4:8) and that love motivated the incarnation and atonement. The Olney Hymns reflect this, especially in hymns that dwell on Christ's sacrificial love. The third book of the collection, focusing on the spiritual life, repeatedly returns to love as the believer's foundation: "Nor rob me of the [[saviour|Lord]] my portion of love" (022-006).

## In the Olney Hymns

- **God's Sustaining Love**: Amid affliction, [[john-newton|Newton]] declares "But cannot quench thy love to me" (022-006), affirming love's permanence.

- **Love and the Word**: "[[affliction|Affliction]]s make them love the word, stir up their hearts to prayer" (024-005), showing how trials deepen love for Scripture.

- **Love and [[salvation|Salvation]]**: [[john-newton|Newton]] dedicates his work to "all who love the [[saviour|Lord]] [[saviour|Jesus Christ]] in sincerity" (P02-005) and entreats "all who love His name" (P02-006).

- **Love's Triumph**: "Thus saved by grace, I live to sing the love and triumphs of my King" (021-008). Love is the song of the redeemed.
