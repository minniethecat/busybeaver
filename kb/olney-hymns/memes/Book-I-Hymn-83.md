[083-001] Physician of my sin-sick soul,
    To thee I bring my case;
My raging malady control,
    And heal me by thy grace.

[083-002] Pity the anguish I endure,
    See how I mourn and pine;
For never can I hope a cure
    From any hand but thine.

[083-003] I would disclose my whole complaint,
    But where shall I begin?
No words of mine can fully paint
    That worst distemper, sin.

[083-004] It lies not in a single part,
    But through my frame is spread;
A burning fever in my heart,
    A palsy in my head.

[083-005] It makes me deaf and dumb and blind,
    And impotent and lame;
It overclouds and fills my mind
    With folly, fear, and shame.

[083-006] A thousand evil thoughts intrude
    Tumultuous in my breast;
Which indispose me for my food,
    And rob me of my rest.

[083-007] Lord, I am sick, regard my cry,
    And set my spirit free:
Say, canst thou let a sinner die,
    Who longs to live to thee?
