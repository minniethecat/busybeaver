[254-001] To those who know the Lord I speak,
    Is my Beloved near?
The Bridegroom of my soul I seek,
    Oh! when will he appear?

[254-002] Though once a man of grief and shame,
    Yet now he fills a throne,
And bears the greatest, sweetest name,
    That earth or heaven have known.

[254-003] Grace flies before, and love attends
    His steps where’er he goes;
Though none can see him but his friends,
    And they were once his foes.

[254-004] He speaks﻿—obedient to his call
    Our warm affections move;
Did he but shine alike on all,
    Then all alike would love.

[254-005] Then love in every heart would reign,
    And war would cease to roar;
And cruel and blood-thirsty men
    Would thirst for blood no more.

[254-006] Such Jesus is, and such his grace,
    Oh may it shine on you!
And tell him, when you see his face,
    I long to see him too.
