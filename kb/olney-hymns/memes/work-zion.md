## Overview

"[[zion|Zion]]; or the [[zion|City of God]]" (Book I, Hymn 60) is [[John Newton]]'s magnificent hymn on the [[Church]] as the [[Zion]] of God. Opening with the line "Glorious things of thee are spoken, / [[zion|Zion]], city of our God," the hymn paints a vision of the Church as a secure, well-watered, divinely founded city — a contrast to the shifting fortunes of earthly kingdoms.

The hymn is one of the most theologically rich in the Olney collection. [[john-newton|Newton]] draws on Old Testament [[Zion]] theology (the city of [[david|David]], the dwelling place of God) and reinterprets it through New Testament ecclesiology: [[zion|Zion]] is not a geographical location but the communion of saints, the body of [[saviour|Christ]].

## Authorship

[[John Newton]] wrote this hymn for Book I, placing it among the "Select Texts of Scripture" hymns. The primary text is Psalm 87:3 — "Glorious things are spoken of thee, O city of God" (KJV). [[john-newton|Newton]] augments this with imagery from Isaiah's [[zion|Zion]] prophecies and Revelation's New [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]].

The meter is 8.7.8.7 D (trochaic tetrameter), a more expansive form than his usual Common Meter. This longer line allows for the grandeur and sweep appropriate to the city-of-God theme.

## Scripture Reference

- **Primary**: Psalm 87 ([[zion|Zion]], the city of God)
- **Supporting**: Isaiah 33:20–21 ([[zion|Zion]] as a quiet habitation with broad rivers); Hebrews 12:22–24 (Mount [[zion|Zion]], the heavenly [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]]); Revelation 21 (the New [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]])

## Theological Themes

The hymn develops a sustained architectural metaphor of the Church as God's city:

| Feature | Theological Meaning |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Divine Foundation** | [[church|The Church]] is built by God, not human effort |
| **Unshakable Walls** | God's protection surrounds the Church |
| **Living Waters** | [[grace|Grace]] flows through the Church like a river |
| **Divine Inhabitation** | God dwells in [[zion|Zion]], his chosen habitation |
| **Eternal Security** | [[zion|Zion]] cannot be moved; the Church will endure |

[[john-newton|Newton]]'s ecclesiology here is both high (the Church as God's glorious city) and practical ([[zion|Zion]] is where believers find refreshment and security). The hymn functions as both doxology and encouragement — reminding the persecuted or discouraged believer that the Church's true foundation is unshakable.
