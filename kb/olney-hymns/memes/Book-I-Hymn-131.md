[131-001] Fierce passions discompose the mind,
    As tempests vex the sea;
But calm content and peace we find
    When, Lord, we turn to thee.

[131-002] In vain by reason and by rule
    We try to bend the will;
For none but in the Saviour’s school
    Can learn the heavenly skill.

[131-003] Since at his feet my soul has sat,
    His gracious words to hear;
Contented with my present state,
    I cast on him my care.

[131-004] “Art thou a sinner, soul?” he said,
    “Then how canst thou complain?
How light thy troubles here, if weigh’d
    With everlasting pain?

[131-005] If thou of murm’ring wouldst be cured,
    Compare thy griefs with mine;
Think what my love for thee endured,
    And thou wilt not repine.

[131-006] ’Tis I appoint thy daily lot,
    And I do all things well:
Thou soon shalt leave this wretched spot,
    And rise with me to dwell.

[131-007] In life, my grace shall strength supply
    Proportion’d to thy day;
At death, thou still shalt find me nigh,
    To wipe thy tears away.”

[131-008] Thus I, who once my wretched days
    In vain repinings spent,
Taught in my Saviour’s school of grace,
    Have learn’d to be content.
