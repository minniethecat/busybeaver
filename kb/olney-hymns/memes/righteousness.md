## Definition

Righteousness denotes the state of being right with God — conforming to His holy standard. Evangelical theology distinguishes between two kinds: self-righteousness, which is the futile human attempt to establish one's own moral standing before God, and [[jesus]]'s righteousness, which is the perfect obedience of Jesus credited to the believer through [[faith]] alone.

## Biblical Basis

[[paul]] writes that "the righteousness of God through faith in [[jesus]]" is given "unto all and upon all them that believe" (Romans 3:22). In Philippians 3:9, Paul contrasts his own righteousness "which is of the [[law]]" with "that which is through the faith of Christ, the righteousness which is of God by faith."

## In the Olney Hymns

The [[Olney-Hymns-Title]] consistently contrast self-righteousness with the righteousness of Christ. [[john-newton]] warns of the danger: "But unbelief, self-will, Self-righteousness, and pride, How often do they spoil" [PN=017-005]. Self-righteousness is likened to a "Babylonish vest" that "provokes the rod" [PN=269-004]. The remedy is to look away from self to Christ: "His righteousness to faith reveal'd, Wrought out by pain and blood" [PN=059-004]. The believer can declare, "Jesus is my righteousness" [PN=287-006] — not a personal achievement but a gift received. The promise for the faithful is that "[[peace]] and righteousness shall reign" [PN=065-002] eternally. The hymns conclude that all true righteousness comes from Christ alone, for "Jesus is our sun and shield, Our righteousness and strength" [PN=328-009].
