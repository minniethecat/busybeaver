[066-001] As parched in the barren sands,
    Beneath a burning sky,
The worthless bramble with’ring stands,
    And only grows to die;

[066-002] Such is the sinner’s awful case,
    Who makes the world his trust,
And dares his confidence to place
    In vanity and dust.

[066-003] A secret curse destroys his root,
    And dries his moisture up;
He lives awhile, but bears no fruit,
    Then dies without a hope.

[066-004] But happy he whose hopes depend
    Upon the Lord alone;
The soul that trusts in such a Friend
    Can ne’er be overthrown.

[066-005] Though gourds should wither, cisterns break.
    And creature-comforts die,
No change his solid hope can shake,
    Or stop his sure supply.

[066-006] So thrives and blooms the tree whose roots
    By constant streams are fed;
Array’d in green, and rich in fruits,
    It rears its branching head.

[066-007] It thrives though rain should be denied,
    And drought around prevail;
’Tis planted by a river side
    Whose waters cannot fail.
