[270-001] O Lord, my best desire fulfill,
    And help me to resign
Life, health, and comfort to thy will,
    And make thy pleasure mine.

[270-002] Why should I shrink at thy command,
    Whose love forbids my fear’s
Or tremble at the gracious hand
    That wipes away my tears?

[270-003] No, rather let me freely yield
    What most I prize to thee,
Who never hast a good withheld,
    Nor wilt withhold from me.

[270-004] Thy favour all my journey through,
    Thou art engaged to grant;
What else I want, or think I do,
    ’Tis better still to want.

[270-005] Wisdom and mercy guide my way,
    Shall I resist them both?
A poor blind creature of a day,
    And crush’d before the moth!

[270-006] But ah! my inward spirit cries,
    Still bind me to thy sway;
Else the next cloud that veils the skies,
    Drives all these thoughts away.
