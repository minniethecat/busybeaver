[281-001] Be still, my heart! these anxious cares
To thee are burdens, thorns, and snares;
They cast dishonour on thy Lord,
And contradict his gracious word.

[281-002] Brought safely by his hand thus far,
Why wilt thou now give place to fear?
How canst thou want if he provide,
Or lose thy way with such a guide?

[281-003] When first before his mercy-seat
Thou didst to him thy all commit,
He gave the warrant from that hour,
To trust his wisdom, love, and power.

[281-004] Did ever trouble yet befall,
And he refuse to hear thy call?
And has he not his promise pass’d,
That thou shalt overcome at last?

[281-005] Like David thou may’st comfort draw,
Saved from the bear’s and lion’s paw;
Goliath’s rage I may defy,
For God, my Saviour, still is nigh.

[281-006] He who has help’d me hitherto,
Will help me all my journey through,
And give me daily cause to raise
New Ebenezers to his praise.

[281-007] Though rough and thorny be the road,
It leads thee home apace to God;
Then count thy present trials small,
For heav’n will make amends for all.
