[068-001] My God, till I received thy stroke,
    How like a beast was I!
So unaccustom’d to the yoke,
    So backward to comply.

[068-002] With grief my just reproach I bear,
    Shame fills me at the thought;
How frequent my rebellions were!
    What wickedness I wrought!

[068-003] 3 Thy merciful restraint I scorn’d,
    And left the pleasant road;
Yet turn me, and I shall be turn’d:
    Thou art the Lord my God.

[068-004] Is Ephraim banish’d from my thoughts,
    Or vile in my esteem?
No, saith the Lord, with all his faults
    I still remember him.

[068-005] Is he a dear and pleasant child?
    Yes, dear and pleasant still;
Though sin his foolish heart beguiled.
    And he withstood my will.

[068-006] My sharp rebuke has laid him low,
    He seeks my face again;
My pity kindles at his woe,
    He shall not seek in vain.
