[180-001] Now let us join with hearts and tongues,
And emulate the angels’ songs;
Yea, sinners may address their King
In songs that angels cannot sing.

[180-002] They praise the Lamb who once was slain;
But we can add a higher strain﻿—
Not only say “He suffer’d thus,
But that “He suffer’d all for us.”

[180-003] When angels by transgression fell,
Justice consign’d them all to hell;
But mercy form’d a wondrous plan,
To save and honour fallen man.

[180-004] Jesus, who pass’d the angels by,
Assumed our flesh to bleed and die;
And still he makes it his abode﻿—
As man he fills the throne of God.

[180-005] Our next of kin, our Brother now,
Is he to whom the angels bow;
They join with us to praise his name,
But we the nearest int’rest claim.

[180-006] But ah! how faint our praises rise!
Sure, ’tis the wonder of the skies,
That we, who share his richest love,
So cold and unconcern’d should prove.

[180-007] Oh glorious hour! it comes with speed,
When we from sin and darkness freed,
Shall see the God who died for man,
And praise him more than angels can.
