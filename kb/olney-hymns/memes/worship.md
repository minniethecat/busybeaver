## Definition

Worship is the reverent devotion and [[praise]] directed toward God, expressed through public gatherings, private [[prayer]], and the singing of [[index-of-hymns]]. In the Christian tradition, it centers on acknowledging [[sovereignty]] and responding to His [[grace]] with gratitude and praise.

## Biblical Basis

The Scriptures establish worship as a primary duty of believers. In Psalm 95:6, the [[david]] calls the faithful to "worship and bow down," while John 4:24 teaches that true worshipers "shall worship the Father in [[spirit]] and in [[truth]]." Daniel exemplified steadfast worship by bowing his knee and worshiping three times a day despite persecution [PN=073-003].

## In the Olney Hymns

[[john-newton]]'s [[Preface]] describes the hymns as designed "for public worship, and for the use of plain [[index-of-persons]]" [PN=P02-002]. The hymns repeatedly exhort the reader to worship God alone: "tear it from thy throne, And worship only thee" [PN=003-005]. The language of worship also points toward the eschatological [[hope]], anticipating that growing numbers would "worship here, And praise thee in our eternal home" [PN=148-005]. Angels themselves worshiped [[jesus]] when He took the form of a servant [PN=078-004], and the believer's heart "glows with heav'nly zeal To worship Him who died for me" [PN=179-005].
