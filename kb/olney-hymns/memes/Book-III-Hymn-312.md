[312-001] The Lord receives his highest praise
From humble minds and hearts sincere;
While all the loud professor says
Offends the righteous Judge’s ear.

[312-002] To walk as children of the day,
To mark the precept’s holy light,
To wage the warfare, watch, and pray,
Show who are pleasing in his sight.

[312-003] Not words alone it cost the Lord
To purchase pardon for his own;
Nor will a soul by grace restored
Return the Saviour words alone.

[312-004] With golden bells the priestly vest,
And rich pomegranates border’d round,
The need of holiness express’d,
And call’d for fruit as well as sound.

[312-005] Easy, indeed, it were to reach
A mansion in the courts above,
If swelling words and fluent speech
Might serve instead of faith and love.

[312-006] But none shall gain the blissful place
Or God’s unclouded glory see,
Who talks of free and sov’reign grace,
Unless that grace has made him free!
