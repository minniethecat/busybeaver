[079-001] There is a fountain fill’d with blood
    Drawn from Emmanuel’s veins;
And sinners plunged beneath that flood
    Lose all their guilty stains.

[079-002] The dying thief rejoiced to see
    That fountain in his day;
And there have I, as vile as he,
    Wash’d all my sins away.

[079-003] Dear dying Lamb, thy precious blood
    Shall never lose its power
Till all the ransom’d church of God
    Be saved, to sin no more.

[079-004] E’er since, by faith, I saw the stream
    Thy flowing wounds supply,
Redeeming love has been my theme,
    And shall be till I die.

[079-005] Then in a nobler, sweeter song,
    I’ll sing thy power to save﻿—
When this poor lisping stamm’ring tongue
    Lies silent in the grave.

[079-006] Lord, I believe thou hast prepared
    (Unworthy though I be,)
For me a blood-bought free reward,
    A golden harp for me!

[079-007] ’Tis strung, and tuned for endless years,
    And form’d by power divine;
To sound in God the Father’s ears
    No other name but thine.
