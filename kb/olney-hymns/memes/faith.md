## Definition

Faith in the Olney Hymns is the trusting reliance on God and Christ that receives salvation and sustains the believer through life's trials. It is not mere intellectual assent but an active, living trust. [[john-newton|Newton]] portrays faith as the instrument that connects the sinner to Christ's saving work: "By faith the promised Seed he view'd, and felt His power to save" (001-006).

## Biblical Basis

The Bible defines faith as "the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen" (Hebrews 11:1). The Olney Hymns echo this understanding, presenting faith as the means of spiritual vision: "By faith in Christ I walk with God, with heaven in view I press the road" (004-001). [[john-newton|Newton]] structured his third book around "a life of faith in the Son of God" (P02-004), making faith the organizing principle for the believer's spiritual journey.

## In the Olney Hymns

Faith appears throughout the collection as both the entry point to Christian life and its sustaining power:

- **Faith and Justification**: Faith is the means by which the sinner receives pardon. [[abel|Abel]]'s example shows that "the [[saviour|Lord]] approved his faith" (002-004).

- **Faith as Boldness**: Faith conquers fear. "But faith makes us bold: for though we stumble, we shall not fall" (007-004).

- **Faith as Triumph**: "I triumph over all by faith, guarded by His almighty hand" (004-003).

- **Faith and [[perseverance|Perseverance]]**: [[john-newton|Newton]] writes of being "preserved faithful to the end" (P02-006), linking faith to endurance.

The hymns encourage both "the faith and comfort of sincere Christians" (P02-001).
