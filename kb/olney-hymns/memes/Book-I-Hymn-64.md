[064-001] The Lord will happiness divine
    On contrite hearts bestow:
Then tell me, gracious God, is mine
    A contrite heart or no?

[064-002] I hear, but seem to hear in vain,
    Insensible as steel;
If aught is felt, ’tis only pain
    To find I cannot feel.

[064-003] I sometimes think myself inclined
    To love thee, if I could;
But often feel another mind,
    Averse to all that’s good.

[064-004] My best desires are faint and few,
    I fain would strive for more;
But when I cry, “My strength renew,”
    Seem weaker than before.

[064-005] Thy saints are comforted, I know,
    And love thy house of prayer;
I therefore go where others go,
    But find no comfort there.

[064-006] O make this heart rejoice or ache,
    Decide this doubt for me;
And if it be not broken break,
    And heal it if it be.
