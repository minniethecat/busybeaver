[251-001] Breathe from the gentle south Lord,
    And cheer me from the north;
Blow on the treasures of thy word.
    And call the spices forth!

[251-002] I wish, thou know’st, to be resign’d,
    And wait with patient hope;
But hope delay’d fatigues the mind
    And drinks the spirits up.

[251-003] Help me to reach the distant goal,
    Confirm my feeble knee
Pity the sickness of a soul
    That faints for love of thee.

[251-004] Cold as I feel this heart of mine,
    Yet since I feel it so,
It yields some hope of life divine
    Within, however low.

[251-005] I seem forsaken and alone,
    I hear the lion roar;
And every door is shut but one,
    And that is mercy’s door.

[251-006] There, till the dear Deliv’rer come,
    I’ll wait with humble pray’r;
And when he calls his exile home,
    The Lord shall find him there.
