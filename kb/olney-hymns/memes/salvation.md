## Definition

Salvation in the Olney Hymns is the comprehensive work of God in rescuing sinners from the guilt, power, and consequences of sin through the life, death, and resurrection of [[saviour|Jesus Christ]]. [[john-newton|Newton]] presents it as both a completed act and an ongoing experience. "Jesus is become at length my salvation and my strength" (058-003).

## Biblical Basis

The New Testament presents salvation as God's deliverance through Christ (Acts 4:12). The hymns echo this with frequent appeals to Christ as the sole agent of salvation. "I have wrought out full salvation; [[sin|Sin]]ner, look to me and live" (061-001) — the hymn presents Christ Himself speaking the gospel invitation.

## In the Olney Hymns

- **Salvation's Source**: "Wells of free salvation yield streams of life, a plenteous store" (058-002). Salvation is freely offered, not earned.

- **Salvation's Security**: "With salvation's walls surrounded, thou may'st smile at all thy foes" (060-001). The believer is protected by salvation.

- **Salvation's [[hope|Hope]]**: "Still we for salvation wait, every hour it nearer comes" (127-003). Salvation has a future dimension — the consummation.

- **Salvation for the Next Generation**: "Let them thy salvation see" (155-006) — [[john-newton|Newton]] prays for children to know salvation.

- **Christ the [[saviour|Savior]]**: "The [[saviour|Lord]], our salvation and light, the guide and the strength of our days" (182-001).
