[267-001] Dear Lord! accept a sinful heart,
    Which of itself complains,
And mourns, with much and frequent smart,
    The evil it contains.

[267-002] There fiery seeds of anger lurk,
    Which often hurt my frame;
And wait but for the tempter’s work,
    To fan them to a flame.

[267-003] Legality holds out a bribe
    To purchase life from thee;
And discontent would fain prescribe
    How thou shalt deal with me.

[267-004] While unbelief withstands thy grace,
    And puts the mercy by,
Presumption, with a brow of brass,
    Says, “Give me, or I die.”

[267-005] How eager are my thoughts to roam
    In quest of what they love!
But ah! when duty calls them home,
    How heavily they move!

[267-006] Oh, cleanse me in a Saviour’s blood,
    Transform me by thy power,
And make me thy beloved abode,
    And let me rove no more!
