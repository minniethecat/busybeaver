[106-001] Our Lord, who knows full well
    The heart of every saint,
Invites us, by a parable.
    To pray and never faint.

[106-002] He bows his gracious ear,
    We never plead in vain;
Yet we must wait till he appear,
    And pray, and pray again.

[106-003] Though unbelief suggest,
    Why should we longer wait?
He bids us never give him rest,
    But be importunate.

[106-004] ’Twas thus a widow poor,
    Without support or friend,
Beset the unjust judge’s door,
    And gain’d, at last, her end.

[106-005] For her he little cared,
    As little for the laws;
Nor god nor man did he regard,
    Yet he espoused her cause.

[106-006] She urged him day and night,
    Would no denial take;
At length he said, “I’ll do her right,
    For my own quiet’s sake.”

[106-007] And shall not Jesus hear
    His chosen when they cry?
Yes, though he may a while forbear,
    He’ll help them from on high.

[106-008] His nature, truth, and love,
    Engage him on their side;
When they are grieved, his bowels move,
    And can they be denied?

[106-009] Then let us earnest be,
    And never faint in prayer;
He loves our importunity,
    And makes our cause his care.
