[201-001] What various hindrances we meet
In coming to a mercy-seat!
Yet who that knows the worth of pray’r
But wishes to be often there?

[201-002] Pray’r makes the darken’d cloud withdraw
Pray’r climbs the ladder Jacob saw,
Gives exercise to faith and love,
Brings every blessing from above.

[201-003] Restraining pray’r, we cease to fight:
Pray’r makes the Christian’s armour bright;
And Satan trembles when he sees
The weakest saint upon his knees.

[201-004] While Moses stood with arms spread wide,
Success was found on Isr’el’s side;
But when through weariness they fail’d,
That moment Amalek prevail’d.

[201-005] Have you no words? Ah! think again;
Words flow apace when you complain,
And fill your fellow-creature’s ear
With the sad tale of all your care.

[201-006] Were half the breath thus vainly spent
To Heaven in supplication sent,
Your cheerful song would oftener be,
“Hear what the Lord has done for me.”
