[050-001] For mercies, countless as the sands,
    Which daily I receive
From Jesus my Redeemer’s hands,
    My soul, what canst thou give?

[050-002] Alas! from such a heart as mine
    What can I bring him forth?
My best is stain’d and dyed with sin,
    My all is nothing worth.

[050-003] Yet this acknowledgment I’ll make
    For all he has bestow’d﻿—
Salvation’s sacred cup I’ll take,
    And call upon my God.

[050-004] The best returns for one like me,
    So wretched and so poor,
Is from his gifts to draw a plea,
    And ask him still for more.

[050-005] I cannot serve him as I ought,
    No works have I to boast;
Yet would I glory in the thought
    That I should owe him most.
