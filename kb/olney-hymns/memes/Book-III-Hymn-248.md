[248-001] O Lord, how vile am I,
    Unholy and unclean!
How can I dare to venture nigh
    With such a load of sin?

[248-002] Is this polluted heart
    A dwelling fit for thee;
Swarming, alas! in every part,
    What evils do I see!

[248-003] If I attempt to pray,
    And lisp thy holy name,
My thoughts are hurried soon away,
    I know not where I am.

[248-004] If in thy word I look,
    Such darkness fills my mind,
I only read a sealed book,
    But no relief can find.

[248-005] Thy gospel oft I hear,
    But hear it still in vain:
Without desire or love or fear,
    I like a stone remain.

[248-006] Myself can hardly bear
    This wretched heart of mine;
How hateful then must it appear
    To those pure eyes of thine!

[248-007] And must I then indeed
    Sink in despair and die?
Fain would I hope that thou didst bleed
    For such a wretch as I.

[248-008] That blood which thou hast spilt,
    That grace which is thine own,
Can cleanse the vilest sinner’s guilt,
    And soften hearts of stone.

[248-009] Low at thy feet I bow,
    Oh pity and forgive;
Here will I lie, and wait till thou
    Shalt bid me raise and live.
