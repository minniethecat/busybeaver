[269-001] Lord, who hast suffer’d all for me,
My peace and pardon to procure,
The lighter cross I bear for thee
Help me with patience to endure.

[269-002] The storm of loud repining hush,
I would in humble silence mourn;
Why should th unburnt though burning bush
Be angry as the crackling thorn?

[269-003] Man should not faint at thy rebuke,
Like Joshua falling on his face,
When the cursed thing that Achan took
Brought Isr’el into just disgrace.

[269-004] Perhaps some golden wedge suppress’d,
Some secret sin, offends my God;
Perhaps that Babylonish vest,
Self-righteousness, provokes the rod.

[269-005] Ah! were I buffeted all day,
Mock’d, crown’d with thorns, and spit upon,
I yet should have no right to say,
My great distress is mine alone.

[269-006] Let me not angrily declare
No pain was ever sharp like mine;
Nor murmur at the cross I bear,
But rather weep, rememb’ring thine.
