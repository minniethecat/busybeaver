[067-001] My God, how perfect are thy ways!
    But mine polluted are;
Sin twines itself about my praise,
    And slides into my prayer.

[067-002] When I would speak what thou hast done
    To save me from my sin,
I cannot make thy mercies known
    But self-applause creeps in.

[067-003] Divine desire, that holy flame,
    Thy grace creates in me;
Alas! impatience is its name
    When it returns to thee.

[067-004] This heart, a fountain of vile thoughts,
    How does it overflow!
While self upon the surface floats,
    Still bubbling from below.

[067-005] Let others in the gaudy dress
    Of fancied merits shine,
The Lord shall be my righteousness,
    The Lord for ever mine.
