[020-001] How bless’d the righteous are
    When they resign their breath!
No wonder Balaam wish’d to share
    In such a happy death.

[020-002] “Oh! let me die,” said he,
    “The death the righteous do﻿—
When life is ended, let me be
    Found with the faithful few.”

[020-003] The force of truth, how great!
    When enemies confess,
None but the righteous, whom they hate,
    A solid hope possess.

[020-004] But Balaam’s wish was vain,
    His heart was insincere;
He thirsted for unrighteous gain.
    And sought a portion here.

[020-005] He seem’d the Lord to know,
    And to offend him loath;
But Mammon proved his overthrow,
    For none can serve them both.

[020-006] May you, my friends, and I,
    Warning from hence receive,
If like the righteous we would die,
    To choose the life they live.
