[126-001] I would, but cannot sing﻿—
    Guilt has untuned my voice,
The serpent sin’s envenom’d sting
    Has poison’d all my joys.

[126-002] I know the Lord is nigh,
    And would, but cannot pray;
For Satan meets me when I try,
    And frights my soul away.

[126-003] I would, but can’t repent,
    Though I endeavour oft;
This stony heart can ne’er relent
    Till Jesus make it soft.

[126-004] I would, but cannot love,
    Though woo’d by love divine:
No arguments have power to move
    A soul so base as mine.

[126-005] I would, but cannot rest
    In God’s most holy will;
I know what he appoints is best,
    Yet murmur at it still.

[126-006] O could I but believe!
    Then all would easy be;
I would, but cannot﻿—Lord, relieve;
    My help must come from thee!

[126-007] But if indeed I would,
    Though I can nothing do;
Yet the desire is something good,
    For which my praise is due.

[126-008] By nature prone to ill,
    Till thine appointed hour,
I was as destitute of will,
    As now I am of power.

[126-009] Wilt thou not crown at length
    The work thou hast begun,
And with a will afford me strength
    In all thy ways to run?
