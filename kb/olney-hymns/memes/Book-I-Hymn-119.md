[119-001] ’Tis a point I long to know,
Oft it causes anxious thought﻿—
Do I love the Lord, or no?
Am I his, or am I not?

[119-002] If I love, why am I thus?
Why this dull, this lifeless frame?
Hardly sure can they be worse
Who have never heard his name!

[119-003] Could my heart so hard remain,
Pray’r a task and burden prove,
Every trifle give me pain,
If I knew a Saviour’s love?

[119-004] When I turn my eyes within,
All is dark and vain and wild:
Fill’d with unbelief and sin,
Can I deem myself a child?

[119-005] If I pray or hear or read,
Sin is mix’d with all I do;
You that love the Lord indeed,
Tell me, is it thus with you?

[119-006] Yet I mourn my stubborn will,
Find my sin a grief and thrall:
Should I grieve for what I feel,
If I did not love at all?

[119-007] Could I joy his saints to meet,
Choose the ways I once abhorr’d,
Find, at times, the promise sweet,
If I did not love the Lord?

[119-008] Lord, decide the doubtful case!
Thou who art thy people’s sun,
Shine upon thy work of grace,
If it be indeed begun.

[119-009] Let me love thee more and more,
If I love at all, I pray;
If I have not loved before,
Help me to begin to-day.
