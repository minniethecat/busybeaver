[198-001] In evil long I took delight,
    Unawed by shame or fear,
Till a new object struck my sight,
    And stopp’d my wild career.

[198-002] I saw one hanging on a tree,
    In agonies and blood,
Who fix’d his languid eyes on me
    As near his cross I stood.

[198-003] Sure never till my latest breath
    Can I forget that look;
It seem’d to charge me with his death
    Though not a word He spoke.

[198-004] My conscience felt, and own’d the guilt
    And plunged me in despair;
I saw my sins his blood had spilt,
    And help’d to nail Him there.

[198-005] Alas! I knew not what I did:
    But now my tears are vain;
Where shall my trembling soul be hid?
    For I the Lord have slain.

[198-006] A second look He gave, which said,
    “I freely all forgive;
This blood is for thy ransom paid,
    I die, that thou may’st live.”

[198-007] Thus while his death my sin displays
    In all its blackest hue,
(Such is the mystery of grace,)
    It seals my pardon too.

[198-008] With pleasing grief and mournful joy
    My spirit now is fill’d,
That I should such a life destroy
    Yet live by Him I kill’d.
