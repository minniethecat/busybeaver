## Definition

Judgment refers to God's righteous assessment of human conduct and His execution of [[justice]]. It encompasses both temporal judgments within [[index-of-events]] — where God intervenes to correct or punish — and the final eschatological judgment at the end of the age, when all humanity will stand before the divine tribunal.

## Biblical Basis

Scripture presents judgment as an inescapable reality. Revelation 20:12 describes the dead standing before God's throne and being "judged out of those things which were written in the books, according to their works." Matthew 25:31–46 portrays [[jesus]] separating the sheep from the goats in the final judgment. [[john-newton]] wrote his [[index-of-hymns]] with "great judgment" [PN=P02-002], conscious that the [[index-of-concepts]] of [[justice]] must be treated with care.

## In the Olney Hymns

The [[Olney-Hymns-Title]] treat judgment with solemn gravity. "Day of judgment, day of wonders! Hark! the trumpet's awful sound" [PN=218-001] captures the dramatic expectation of the final assize. The hymns ask believers to examine themselves: "Shall I stand in judgment then, When I see the open'd books?" [PN=220-002]. Temporal judgments also appear: "[[saviour]], while thy judgments shake the land, Thy [[index-of-persons]]'s eyes are fixed on Thee" [PN=206-002]. The faithful find comfort in Christ's advocacy — they will "walk with me in white: When in judgment I appear" [PN=138-003]. Yet unrepentant sinners are warned to prepare, for "his judgment stand prepared, Thou must either stand approved Or be cast away for good" [PN=245-001].
