[014-001] Heal us, Emmanuel; here we are,
    Waiting to feel thy touch:
Deep-wounded souls to thee repair,
    And, Saviour, we are such.

[014-002] Our faith is feeble, we confess﻿—
    We faintly trust thy word;
But wilt thou pity us the less?
    Be that far from thee, Lord!

[014-003] Remember him who once applied
    With trembling for relief;
“Lord, I believe,” with tears he cried,
    “0 help my unbelief!”

[014-004] She too who touch’d thee in the press,
    And healing virtue stole,
Was answer’d, “Daughter, go in peace:
    Thy faith hath made thee whole.”

[014-005] Conceal’d amid the gathering throng,
    She would have shunn’d thy view,
And if her faith was firm and strong﻿—
    Had strong misgivings too.

[014-006] Like her, with hopes and fears we come,
    To touch thee if we may;
Oh! send us not despairing home,
    Send none unheal’d away.
