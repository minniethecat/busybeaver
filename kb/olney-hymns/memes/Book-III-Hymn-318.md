[318-001] Destruction’s dangerous road
    What multitudes pursue,
While that which leads the soul to God
    Is known or sought by few.

[318-002] Believers enter in
    By Christ, the living gate;
But they who will not leave their sin,
    Complain it is too strait.

[318-003] If self must be denied,
    And sin forsaken quite,
They rather choose the way that’s wide,
    And strive to think it right.

[318-004] Encompass’d by a throng,
    On numbers they depend;
So many surely can’t be wrong,
    And miss a happy end.

[318-005] But numbers are no mark
    That men will right be found;
A few were saved in Noah’s ark,
    For many millions drown’d.

[318-006] Obey the gospel call,
    And enter while you may,
The flock of Christ is always small,
    And none are safe but they.

[318-007] Lord, open sinners eyes
    Their awful state to see;
And make them, ere the storm arise,
    To thee for safety flee.
