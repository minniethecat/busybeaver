[184-001] O Lord, our languid souls inspire,
    For here we trust thou art;
Send down a coal of heav’nly fire
    To warm each waiting heart.

[184-002] Dear Shepherd of thy people, hear;
    Thy presence now display:
As thou hast given a place for pray’r,
    So give us hearts to pray.

[184-003] Show us some token of thy love,
    Our fainting hope to raise;
And pour thy blessings from above,
    That we may render praise.

[184-004] Within these walls let holy peace
    And love and concord dwell;
Here give the troubled conscience ease,
    The wounded spirit heal.

[184-005] The feeling heart, the melting eye,
    The humbled mind bestow;
And shine upon us from on high,
    To make our graces grow!

[184-006] May we in faith receive thy word,
    In faith present our pray’rs;
And in the presence of our Lord
    Unbosom all our cares!

[184-007] And may the gospel’s joyful sound,
    Enforced by mighty grace,
Awaken many sinners round
    To come and fill the place!
