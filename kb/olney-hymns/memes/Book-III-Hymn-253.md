[253-001] Approach, my soul, the mercy-seat
    Where Jesus answers pray’r;
There humbly fall before his feet,
    For none can perish there.

[253-002] Thy promise is my only plea,
    With this I venture nigh:
Thou callest burden’d souls to thee,
    And such, O Lord, am I.

[253-003] Bow’d down beneath a load of sin,
    By Satan sorely press’d;
By war without and fears within,
    I come to thee for rest.

[253-004] Be thou my shield and hiding-place!
    That, shelter’d near thy side,
I may my fierce accuser face,
    And tell him, Thou hast died.

[253-005] Oh wond’rous love, to bleed and die,
    To bear the cross and shame,
That guilty sinners, such as I,
    Might plead thy gracious name.

[253-006] “Poor tempest-tossed soul, be still,
    My promised grace receive;”
’Tis Jesus speaks﻿—I must, I will,
    I can, I do believe.
