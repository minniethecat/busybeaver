[187-001] O Happy they who know the Lord,
    With whom he deigns to dwell!
He feels and cheers them by his word,
    His arm supports them well.

[187-002] To them, in each distressing hour,
    His throne of grace is near;
And when they plead his love and power,
    He stands engaged to hear.

[187-003] He help’d his saints in ancient days,
    Who trusted in his name;
And we can witness, to his praise,
    His love is still the same.

[187-004] Wand’ring in sin, our souls he found,
    And bade us seek his face;
Gave us to hear the gospel sound,
    And taste the gospel grace.

[187-005] Oft in his house his glory shines
    Before our wond’ring eyes;
We wish not then for golden mines,
    Or aught beneath the skies.

[187-006] His presence sweetens all our cares,
    And makes our burdens light;
A word from him dispels our fears,
    And gilds the gloom of night.

[187-007] Lord, we expect to suffer here,
    Nor would we dare repine;
But give us still to find thee near,
    And own us still for thine.

[187-008] Let us enjoy and highly prize
    These tokens of thy love
Till thou shalt bid our spirits rise
    To worship thee above.
