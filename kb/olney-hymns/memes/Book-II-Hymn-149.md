[149-001] Bestow, dear Lord, upon our youth
    The gift of saving grace;
And let the seed of sacred truth
    Fall in a fruitful place.

[149-002] Grace is a plant, where’er it grows,
    Of pure and heav’nly root;
But fairest in the youngest shows,
    And yields the sweetest fruit.

[149-003] Ye careless ones, hear betimes
    The voice of sovereign love!
Your youth is stain’d with many crimes,
    But mercy reigns above.

[149-004] True, you are young, but there’s a stone
    Within the youngest breast;
Or half the crimes which you have done
    Would rob you of your rest.

[149-005] For you the public prayer is made,
    Oh! join the public prayer;
For you the sacred tear is shed,
    Oh! shed yourselves a tear.

[149-006] We pray that you may early prove
    The Spirit’s power to teach;
You cannot be too young to love
    That Jesus whom we preach.
