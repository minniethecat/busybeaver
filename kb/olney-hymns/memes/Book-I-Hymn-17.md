[017-001] By whom was David taught
    To aim the dreadful blow,
    When he Goliath fought,
    And laid the Gittite low?
No sword nor spear the stripling took,
But chose a peeble from the brook.

[017-002] ’Twas Israel’s God and King
    Who sent him to the fight﻿—
    Who gave him strength to sling,
    And skill to aim aright.
Ye feeble saints, your strength endures,
Because young David’s God is yours.

[017-003] Who order’d Gideon forth
    To storm th’ invader’s camp,
    With arms of little worth﻿—
    A pitcher and a lamp?
The trumpets made his coming known,
And all the host was overthrown.

[017-004] Oh! I have seen the day
    When, with a single word,
    God helping me to say,
    My trust is in the Lord,
My soul has quell’d a thousand foes,
Fearless of all that could oppose.

[017-005] But unbelief, self-will,
    Self-righteousness, and pride,
    How often do they steal
    My weapon from my side!
Yet David’s Lord and Gideon’s friend
Will help his servant to the end.
