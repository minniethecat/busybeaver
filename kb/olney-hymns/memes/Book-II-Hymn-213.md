[213-001] In vain our fancy strives to paint
    The moment after death,
The glories that surround the saints
    When yielding up their breath.

[213-002] One gentle sigh their fetters breaks;
    We scarce can say, “They’re gone!”
Before the willing spirit takes
    Her mansion near the throne.

[213-003] Faith strives, but all its efforts fail
    To trace her in her flight;
No eye can pierce within the veil
    Which hides that world of light

[213-004] Thus much (and this is all) we know,
    They are completely bless’d﻿—
Have done with sin and care and woe,
    And with their Saviour rest.

[213-005] On harps of gold they praise his name,
    His face they always view;
Then let us foll’wers be of them,
    That we may praise him too.

[213-006] Their faith and patience, love and zeal,
    Should make their mem’ry dear;
And, Lord, do thou the pray’rs fulfill
    They offer’d for us here.

[213-007] While they have gain’d we losers are,
    We miss them day by day:
But thou canst every breach repair,
    And wipe our tears away.

[213-008] We pray, as in Elisha’s case,
    When great Elijah went﻿—
May double portions of thy grace
    To us who stay be sent.
