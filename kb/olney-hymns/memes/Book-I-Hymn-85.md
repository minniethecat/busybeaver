[085-001] Ye sons of earth, prepare the plough,
    Break up your fallow ground!
The sower is gone forth to sow,
    And scatter blessings round.

[085-002] The seed that finds a stony soil
    Shoots forth a hasty blade;
But ill repays the sower’s toil﻿—
    Soon wither’d, scorch’d, and dead.

[085-003] The thorny ground is sure to baulk
    All hopes of harvest there;
We find a tall and sickly stalk,
    But not the fruitful ear.

[085-004] The beaten path and highway-side
    Receive the trust in vain;
The watchful birds the spoil divide,
    And pick up all the grain.

[085-005] But where the Lord of grace and power
    Has bless’d the happy field,
How plenteous is the golden store
    The deep-wrought furrows yield!

[085-006] Father of mercies, we have need
    Of thy preparing grace;
Let the same hand that gives the seed
    Provide a fruitful place.
