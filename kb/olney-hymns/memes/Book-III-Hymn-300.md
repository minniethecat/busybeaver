[300-001] Let worldly minds the world pursue
    It has no charms for me;
Once I admired its trifles too,
    But grace has set me free.

[300-002] Its pleasures now no longer please,
    No more content afford;
Far from my heart be joys like these,
    Now I have seen the Lord.

[300-003] As by the light of op’ning day
    The stars are all conceal’d;
So earthly pleasures fade away
    When Jesus is reveal’d.

[300-004] Creatures no more divide my choice,
    I bid them all depart;
His name and love and gracious voice
    Have fix’d my roving heart.

[300-005] Now, Lord, I would be thine alone,
    And wholly live to thee;
But may I hope that thou wilt own
    A worthless worm like me?

[300-006] Yes! though of sinners I’m the worst,
    I cannot doubt thy will;
For if thou hadst not loved me first,
    I had refused thee still.
