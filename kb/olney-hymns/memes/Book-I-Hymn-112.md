[112-001] Beside the gospel pool,
    Appointed for the poor,
From year to year my helpless soul
    Has waited for a cure.

[112-002] How often have I seen
    The healing waters move,
And others, round me, stepping in,
    Their efficacy prove!

[112-003] But my complaints remain:
    I feel the very same;
As full of guilt and fear and pain
    As when at first I came.

[112-004] O would the Lord appear
    My malady to heal;
He knows how long I’ve languish’d here,
    And what distress I feel.

[112-005] How often have I thought,
    Why should I longer lie?
Surely the mercy I have sought
    Is not for such as I?

[112-006] But whether can I go?
    There is no other pool
Where streams of sovereign virtue flow
    To make a sinner whole.

[112-007] Here, then, from day to day,
    I’ll wait and hope and try;
Can Jesus hear a sinner pray,
    Yet suffer him to die!

[112-008] No: he is full of grace;
    He never will permit
A soul that fain would see his face
    To perish at his feet.
