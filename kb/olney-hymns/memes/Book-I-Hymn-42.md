[042-001] Joy is a fruit that will not grow
    In Nature’s barren soil;
All we can boast till Christ we know
    Is vanity and toil.

[042-002] But where the Lord has planted grace,
    And made his glories known,
There fruits of heavenly joy and peace
    Are found, and there alone.

[042-003] A bleeding Saviour seen by faith,
    A sense of pard’ning love,
A hope that triumphs over death,
    Give joys like those above.

[042-004] To take a glimpse within the veil,
    To know that God is mine,
Are springs of joy that never fail,
    Unspeakable! divine!

[042-005] These are the joys which satisfy
    And sanctify the mind;
Which make the spirit mount on high,
    And leave the world behind.

[042-006] No more, believers, mourn your lot,
    But if you are the Lord’s,
Resign to them that know him not
    Such joys as earth affords.
