[282-001] One glance of thine, eternal Lord,
    Pierces all nature through;
Nor heaven nor earth nor hell afford
    A shelter from thy view!

[282-002] The mighty whole, each smaller part,
    At once before thee lies;
And every thought of every heart
    Is open to thine eyes.

[282-003] Though greatly from myself conceal’d,
    Thou seest my inward frame;
To thee I always stand reveal’d
    Exactly as I am.

[282-004] Since, therefore, I can hardly bear
    What in myself I see,
How vile and black must I appear
    Most holy God, to thee!

[282-005] But since my Saviour stands between,
    In garments dyed in blood,
’Tis he, instead of me, is seen,
    When I approach to God.

[282-006] Thus, though a sinner, I am safe;
    He pleads before the throne
’Tis life and death in my behalf,
    And calls my sins his own.

[282-007] What wondrous love, what mysteries,
    In this appointment shine!
My breaches of the law are his,
    And his obedience mine.
