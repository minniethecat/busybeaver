## Definition

The Kingdom of God is the realm of God's sovereign rule — both a present spiritual reality in the hearts of believers and a future visible reign when Christ returns. It is characterized by righteousness, peace, and joy in the Holy Spirit (Romans 14:17). Entrance into the kingdom is granted by grace through faith, not by human achievement.

## Biblical Basis

Jesus inaugurated his ministry proclaiming "the kingdom of God is at hand" (Mark 1:15). He taught his disciples to pray "your kingdom come" (Matthew 6:10). The kingdom is both already present — "the kingdom of God is in the midst of you" (Luke 17:21) — and yet to be consummated, when Christ will say to the redeemed, "Come, you who are blessed by my Father, inherit the kingdom" (Matthew 25:34).

## In the Olney Hymns

The Olney Hymns portray the kingdom as Christ's gift to his redeemed people. Newton wrote: "Come near, ye blessed, See the kingdom I bestow: You for ever shall possess My kingdom of eternal love!" [PN=218-006]. The kingdom is not earned but bestowed — a reflection of Newton's Evangelical theology of grace. The hymns also connect Christ's kingdom with his love: "He gives him all he can bestow His kingdom of eternal love!" [PN=291-005], emphasizing that the kingdom's essence is not territory or power but the eternal love of Christ for his people.

## Related Concepts

The kingdom motif in the Olney Hymns connects to several related doctrines. [[eternity]] provides the duration of Christ's kingdom — "My kingdom of eternal love." [[grace]] is the basis of kingdom entrance — the kingdom is "bestowed," not earned. [[judgment]] is the moment when Christ separates the blessed (who inherit the kingdom) from the rejected. [[sovereignty]] — God's kingly rule — is the broader theological category within which the kingdom concept operates.
