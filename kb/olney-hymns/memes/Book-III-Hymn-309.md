[309-001] The new-born child of gospel grace,
Like some fair tree when summer’s nigh,
Beneath Emmanuel’s shining face,
Lifts up his blooming branch on high.

[309-002] No fears he feels, he sees no foes.
No conflict yet his faith employs,
Nor has he learn’d to whom he owes
The strength and peace his soul enjoys.

[309-003] But sin soon darts its cruel sting,
And comforts sinking day by day;
What seem’d his own, a self-fed spring,
Proves but a brook that glides away.

[309-004] When Gideon arm’d his num’rous host,
The Lord soon made his numbers less;
And said, “Lest Isr’el vainly boast,
My arm procured me this success.”

[309-005] Thus will he bring our spirits down,
And draw our ebbing comforts low,
That, saved by grace, but not our own,
We may not claim the praise we owe.
