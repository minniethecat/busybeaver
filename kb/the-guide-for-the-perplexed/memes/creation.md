## Definition

Creation (création ex nihilo) is the doctrine that [[god|God]] brought the universe into existence from absolute nothingness, by a free act of divine will. [[maimonides|Maimonides]] contrasts this with the [[aristotle|Aristote]]lian view of eternal emanation, arguing that creation ex nihilo is both philosophically defensible and scripturally grounded. (P01-021)

## In the Guide

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] treats creation as foundational to the entire structure of the Guide. He argues that the incorporeality of [[god|God]] and creation ex nihilo are the two pillars upon which correct [[wisdom|understanding]] of scripture rests. (P03-013) (P03-024)

The "account of creation" (Maasé beréschîth) is identified by [[maimonides|Maimonides]] with natural [[knowledge|science]] (physics), suggesting that the Genesis narrative contains, in allegorical form, the principles of [[aristotle|Aristote]]lian physics adapted to monotheistic theology. (002-003)
