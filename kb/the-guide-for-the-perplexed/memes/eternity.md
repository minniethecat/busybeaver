## Definition

Eternity (éternité) in the Guide refers to the philosophical question of whether the universe has always existed (the [[aristotle|Aristote]]lian doctrine of the eternity of the world) or was created at a specific moment by divine will (the doctrine of creation ex nihilo). [[maimonides|Maimonides]] argues for creation, but treats the question with philosophical nuance. (P02-005) (P03-023)

## In the Guide

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] engages seriously with the [[aristotle|Aristote]]lian argument for eternity, noting that neither creation nor eternity can be demonstrated conclusively from physics alone. The author who "combats the eternity of prime matter" ac[[wisdom|knowledge]]s the philosophical difficulty while maintaining the religious doctrine. (010-002)

The attributes of [[god|God]] — existence, unity, and eternity — are discussed together as the foundational divine predicates that must be reinterpreted through [[divine-attributes|negative theology]]. (P02-005)
