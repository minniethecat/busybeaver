## Overview

Moses (Moïse) is, for [[maimonides|Maimonides]], the greatest of all prophets. His uniqueness lies in the nature of his [[prophets|prophecy]]: unlike other prophets who received [[revelation|divine communication]] through the [[imagination|imaginative faculty]] (in dreams and visions), Moses received [[prophets|prophecy]] directly through the [[wisdom|intellect]] while awake. (P01-024) (P03-016)

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] grounds this distinction in the biblical text itself: "And the Eternal spoke to Moses face to face" — a phrase that indicates direct [[wisdom|intellect]]ual apprehension without the mediation of the [[imagination|imaginative faculty]] that characterizes all other prophetic experience. (P02-003) (P03-021)

## In the Guide

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] cites Moses throughout the Guide as the p[[accident|arad]]igmatic prophet and lawgiver. The [[law|Torah]] — given through Moses — is the perfect [[law|divine law]], combining [[truth|philosophical truth]] with practical legislation for the governance of a community. (009-002)

The Guide interprets Moses' unique status through the lens of [[aristotle|Aristote]]lian psychology: Moses' [[wisdom|intellect]] was so perfected that his [[imagination|imaginative faculty]] was entirely subordinated to reason, enabling him to receive divine emanation purely [[wisdom|intellect]]ually. This is why Mosaic [[prophets|prophecy]] is described as "face to face" — a metaphor for direct [[wisdom|intellect]]ual vision. (019-001) (019-003)

> Moses alone among the prophets received divine communication while fully awake, without the symbolic imagery of dreams and visions that characterized all other prophetic experiences.

## Significance

For [[maimonides|Maimonides]], Moses is not merely a historical figure but the cornerstone of Jewish theology. The uniqueness of Mosaic [[prophets|prophecy]] guarantees the uniqueness and permanence of the [[law|Torah]]: be[[causality|cause]] Moses' [[prophets|prophecy]] was purely [[wisdom|intellect]]ual, the [[law|Law]] he transmitted is perfect and immutable. No subsequent prophet can abrogate or am[[final-cause|end]] it. (021-002)

This doctrine simultaneously exalts Moses above all other prophets — including Jesus and Muhammad — while grounding the authority of rabbinic Judaism in the unique epistemic status of its founding revelation.
