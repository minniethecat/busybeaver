## Overview

Israel (Israël) is the covenantal nation to whom the [[law|Torah]] was given at Sinai. [[maimonides|Maimonides]] addresses the Guide primarily to Jews who are versed in both [[law|Torah]] and philosophy, seeking to resolve apparent contradictions between the two. (P02-002) (P03-020)

The people of Israel occupy a unique position in [[maimonides|Maimonides]]' theology: they are the community chosen to receive the divine [[law|Law]], and their history — from the [[exodus|Exodus]] through the prophetic era — constitutes the empirical evidence for [[god|God]]'s providential governance of the world.

## In the Guide

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] writes for the "religious person in whom the truth of our [[law|Law]] is established" — a member of the community of Israel who has also studied philosophy and finds apparent conflicts. The Guide aims to show that the [[law|Law]] of Israel, properly interpreted, is in harmony with rational truth. ([[exodus|Exodus]], XXIV, 11)

> Israel's election is not arbitrary but grounded in the intellectual perfection attained by the patriarchs and prophets, culminating in Moses.

The Guide interprets Israel's biblical history allegorically: the elders of Israel who "beheld [[god|God]]" at Sinai represent different levels of [[wisdom|intellect]]ual apprehension. (019-004) (019-005) The nation's historical trajectory — slavery in [[egypt|Egypt]], liberation, wandering, entry into the land — encodes the journey of the soul from material bondage to [[wisdom|intellect]]ual freedom. ([[exodus|Exodus]], XIX, 22)

[[maimonides|Maimonides]]' conception of Israel ext[[final-cause|end]]s beyond ethnicity to [[wisdom|intellect]]ual community: any person who affirms the fundamental doctrines (creation ex nihilo and [[incorporeality|divine incorporeality]]) and observes the [[law|Law]] is a member of Israel, regardless of lineage. (025-001)
