## Definition

Evil (mal), in [[maimonides|Maimonides]]' theodicy, is not a positive reality but a privation — the absence of good, just as darkness is the absence of light. [[god|God]] creates only existence and good; evil arises from the limitations of matter and the absence of divine [[knowledge|wisdom]]. (031-001) (044-007)

## In the Guide

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] classifies evils into three categories: natural evils (arising from matter and generation/corruption), evils inflicted by humans upon one another, and self-inflicted evils (from excessive desires). The greatest evils, he argues, are self-inflicted through ignorance and vice.

Divine providence mitigates evil in proportion to [[wisdom|intellect]]ual attainment — the [[wisdom|sage]] suffers less than the fool be[[causality|cause]] [[knowledge|wisdom]] provides perspective and resilience.
