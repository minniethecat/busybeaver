## Definition

The doctrine of divine attributes is central to [[maimonides|Maimonides]]' negative theology. He argues that [[god|God]], being absolutely simple and incorporeal, cannot possess positive attributes. Any positive attribution would imply multiplicity in the divine essence. Instead, one can only say what [[god|God]] is *not* (via negativa), or describe [[god|God]]'s actions in the world. (P01-021) (P02-005)

## In the Guide

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] devotes extensive chapters to the systematic elimination of positive divine attributes. He argues that even seemingly essential attributes — existence, unity, eternity — when applied to [[god|God]], must be understood as negations of their opposites rather than positive descriptions. (P03-022)

[[god|God]] is "stripped of every kind of positive attributes" — this is the philosophical conclusion toward which the entire first part of the Guide is directed. (P02-004) (P03-013)
