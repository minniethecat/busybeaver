# Homonymy

**Homonymy** (Arabic: *ishtirāk al-lafẓ*, Hebrew: *shittuf*) is a foundational methodological concept in [[Moses|Maimonides]]' *Guide for the Perplexed*. The identification and analysis of **homonymous terms** — Hebrew words that share the same spelling but carry multiple distinct meanings — forms the backbone of the Guide's Part I, where [[maimonides|Maimonides]] systematically demonstrates that biblical [[anthropomorphism]]s are not literal descriptions of [[God]] but rather equivocal expressions whose true referents are incorporeal.

## Maimonides' Program of Homonymic Analysis

In the Introduction to Part I, [[maimonides|Maimonides]] explicitly states that the first [[final-cause|purpose]] of the Guide is to "explain the meanings of certain equivocal terms occurring in the books of the prophets" (001-004). This lexicographical project serves a theological [[final-cause|purpose]]: by showing that terms such as "seeing," "hearing," "speaking," "standing," and "asc[[final-cause|end]]ing" — when applied to [[god|God]] — carry figurative rather than corporeal meanings, [[maimonides|Maimonides]] dismantles the literalist reading that leads to [[Incorporeality|anthropomorphism]].

> Ceux qui ne comprennent pas le sens de ces noms homonymes, ou métaphoriques, ou amphibologiques, s'imaginent que Dieu possède toutes les qualités corporelles. (002-001)

The Guide devotes approximately thirty chapters (I:1–I:30) to this lexicographical exercise, examining terms including *ra'ah* (to see), *shama'* (to hear), *dibber* (to speak), *yashav* (to sit), *kam* (to stand), *alah* (to asc[[final-cause|end]]), and *y[[accident|arad]]* (to desc[[final-cause|end]]).

## Three Categories of Figurative Language

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] distinguishes three categories of non-literal language in Scripture:

| Category | Arabic Term | Definition | Example |
|----------|------------|------------|---------|
| Homonym | *ishtirāk* | One word with multiple distinct meanings | *regel* (foot / [[causality|cause]] / occurrence) |
| Metaphor | *isti'āra* | A word ext[[final-cause|end]]ed from its primary sense | [[god|God]]'s "hand" as power |
| Amphibology | *tashkīk* | A word applied analogically across degrees | "Being" applied to [[god|God]] vs. creatures |

> On s'est servi de mots homonymes, afin que les hommes vulgaires les comprennent selon la mesure de leur intelligence, tandis que le savant les comprend dans un autre sens. (003-002)

## The Theological Function

The homonymic method is not merely philological. It serves [[maimonides|Maimonides]]' core theological project: establishing [[Divine Attributes|divine simplicity]] and [[Incorporeality]]. By reducing all apparently corporeal biblical descriptions to homonyms whose "secondary" meanings are incorporeal, [[maimonides|Maimonides]] creates an interpretive framework in which Scripture never contradicts philosophical monotheism.

This method also establishes a **hermeneutic of [[wisdom|intellect]]ual stratification**: the same biblical text conveys different truths to different audiences — the vulgar understand the corporeal sense, while the philosophically trained perceive the abstract, incorporeal meaning.
