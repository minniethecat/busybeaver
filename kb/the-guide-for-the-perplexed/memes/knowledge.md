## Definition

Knowledge (vérité, science) in the Guide is the apprehension of truth — both through rational demonstration and through the correct interpretation of scripture. [[maimonides|Maimonides]] sees no ultimate conflict between reason and revelation, though apparent contradictions require [[esoteric-interpretation|allegorical interpretation]]. (001-003) (002-001)

## In the Guide

The [[final-cause|purpose]] of the Guide is "that the truth be established for you methodically" — [[maimonides|Maimonides]] aims to lead the reader from surface-level [[wisdom|understanding]] to the deeper, allegorical truths that scripture and philosophy share. (001-003)

For the religious person in whom "the truth of our [[law|Law]] is established in his soul," philosophical study deepens rather than threatens faith. The truths should be "glimpsed" and then fully appreh[[final-cause|end]]ed through systematic study. (002-001) (002-004)
