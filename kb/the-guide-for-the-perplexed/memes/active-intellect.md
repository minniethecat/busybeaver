## Definition

The Active Intellect (*al-'aql al-fa''āl* in Arabic, *[[intellect|Intellect]]us agens* in Latin) is a central concept in medieval [[aristotle|Aristote]]lian philosophy. Within the cosmological hierarchy inherited by [[maimonides|Maimonides]] from [[alfarabi|Alfarabi]] and [[avicenna|Avicenna]], the Active Intellect is the tenth and lowest of the **separate [[intellect|intelligence]]s** — the incorporeal [[wisdom|intellect]]s that govern the celestial spheres.

Its specific function is to actualize the human material [[wisdom|intellect]]: just as light makes potentially visible colors actually visible, the Active Intellect transforms the human mind's latent capacity for thought into actual [[wisdom|knowledge]].

In Maimonidean thought, this same [[wisdom|intellect]] also serves as the intermediary for prophetic emanation (075-002).

## In the Guide

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] integrates the Active Intellect into his theory of [[prophets|prophecy]], developed most fully in Part II of the Guide. Divine emanation flows through the hierarchy of separate [[intellect|intelligence]]s, and the Active Intellect, being nearest to the sublunar world, transmits this overflow to suitably prepared human [[wisdom|intellect]]s (060-002).

The Active Intellect also figures in Maimonidean thought on human perfection. He holds that the ultimate human goal is the actualization of the [[wisdom|intellect]] through the acquisition of intelligible forms, a process mediated by the Active Intellect.

When the human [[wisdom|intellect]] has fully realized its potential, it becomes conjoined with the Active Intellect, achieving the highest form of [[wisdom|knowledge]] accessible to a mortal being. This conjunction represents the [[wisdom|intellect]]ual [[prayer|worship]] that [[maimonides|Maimonides]] identifies as the true [[final-cause|purpose]] of human existence.

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] carefully distinguishes the Active Intellect from [[god|God]]. While the Active Intellect is an immaterial, eternal [[intellect|intelligence]], it is a created entity — the first of the separate [[intellect|intelligence]]s emanated from [[god|God]] (051-001). This hierarchical structure preserves divine transc[[final-cause|end]]ence.

## Interpretation

Maimonidean treatment of the Active Intellect exemplifies the strategy of reading [[aristotle|Aristote]]lian philosophy into Scripture.

Biblical pas[[wisdom|sage]]s describing prophetic encounters with angels are interpreted as encounters with the Active Intellect. The "angel" that speaks to the prophet is, in truth, this cosmic [[intellect|intelligence]].
