## Definition

Matter and form (matière et forme) are the two constitutive principles of all physical beings in [[aristotle|Aristote]]lian philosophy. Matter is the substrate of potentiality; form is the principle of actuality and intelligibility. [[maimonides|Maimonides]] integrates this framework into his theological system. (010-002)

## In the Guide

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] uses the matter-form distinction to explain both the nature of physical existence and the allegorical meaning of Genesis. The "account of creation" (Maasé beréschîth) is [[aristotle|Aristotle]]'s physics, expressed in the language of scripture. The eternity of prime matter is philosophically contested, with [[maimonides|Maimonides]] arguing for created matter.
