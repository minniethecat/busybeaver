## Definition

Divine incorporeality (incorporealité) is the doctrine that [[god|God]] has absolutely no body, form, or material aspect. Together with creation ex nihilo, [[maimonides|Maimonides]] identifies this as one of the two fundamental principles upon which correct [[wisdom|understanding]] of [[god|God]] dep[[final-cause|end]]s. (P01-021) (P03-013)

## In the Guide

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] argues at length against anthropomorphic conceptions of [[god|God]], demonstrating that all scriptural references to [[god|God]]'s "hand", "face", or "sitting" must be interpreted allegorically. The belief in divine incorporeality is so fundamental that [[maimonides|Maimonides]] considers its denial tantamount to idolatry.
