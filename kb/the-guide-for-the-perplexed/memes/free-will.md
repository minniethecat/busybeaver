## Definition

Free will (volonté, liberté) is the human capacity to choose between alternatives without external compulsion. [[maimonides|Maimonides]] affirms genuine human freedom as a prerequisite for the meaningfulness of divine commandments. (P02-004) (P03-022)

## In the Guide

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] holds that human beings are guided by divine will in their general dispositions, just as their physical forms are determined by nature — yet individual choices remain free. This is the basis of moral responsibility. (003-001)

The concept of divine will itself serves as a model: just as [[god|God]]'s creative act was a free choice (not necessitated by divine nature), so human actions are genuinely free.
