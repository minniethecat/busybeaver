## Definition

Divine providence (providence divine) is [[maimonides|Maimonides]]' doctrine of [[god|God]]'s ongoing governance and care for creation. Unlike the general providence that maintains natural laws, [[maimonides|Maimonides]] argues for a special providence that ext[[final-cause|end]]s to human beings in proportion to their [[wisdom|intellect]]ual and moral development. (031-001)

## In the Guide

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] addresses the problem of providence in the context of [[evil|theodicy]] — how can a just [[god|God]] allow suffering? He argues that divine providence is not arbitrary but is linked to the perfection of the human [[wisdom|intellect]]. The more one knows [[god|God]], the more one is under divine providence. (044-007)

[[god|God]]'s government and providence attach to the whole of creation, but with varying degrees of intimacy. The prophet, by virtue of [[wisdom|intellect]]ual perfection, receives the highest degree of providential care. (071-029)
