# Divine Will

**Divine Will** (*al-irāda al-ilāhiyya*, Hebrew: *ha-ratzon ha-elohi*) is a central theological concept in [[Maimonides]]' *Guide for the Perplexed*. The notion of [[god|God]]'s will serves as the explanatory principle for creation, [[Providence|providence]], [[Prophecy|prophecy]], and the giving of the [[Law|Torah]]. [[maimonides|Maimonides]] carefully distinguishes the divine will from both arbitrary volition and necessitated emanation, positioning it as the free but eternal ground of [[necessary-existence|contingent existence]].

## Will and Creation

[[maimonides|Maimonides]]' theory of divine will is inseparable from his doctrine of [[Creation|creation]]. Unlike the [[Emanation|emanationist]] model — in which the world flows necessarily and eternally from [[god|God]]'s essence — [[maimonides|Maimonides]] maintains that the world exists through an act of divine will that is temporally originary yet eternally decreed. The will is not a separate faculty added to [[god|God]]'s essence, but rather identical with [[god|God]]'s [[wisdom|knowledge]] and [[knowledge|wisdom]]:

> La volonté divine, par laquelle les êtres ont été créés, n'est pas une chose surajoutée à l'essence divine. (018-001)

## Will and Providence

In the domain of [[Providence]], [[maimonides|Maimonides]] argues that divine will operates through the natural order rather than through constant miraculous intervention. The eternal will decreed at creation encompasses all particular events — a position that reconciles divine governance with the stability of natural law.

> Les actes qui émanent de sa volonté se manifestent dans l'ordre de la nature. (032-007)

## Will and Human Freedom

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] addresses the apparent tension between divine will and [[Free Will|human freedom]] by distinguishing between [[god|God]]'s eternal [[wisdom|knowledge]]-will and human temporal choice. The divine will does not compel human action but establishes the framework within which [[free-will|free choice]] operates:

> L'homme contraria la volonté de Dieu dans lui; car la volonté est aussi appelée cœur. (033-001)

## The Eternal and Unchanging Will

A distinctive feature of [[maimonides|Maimonides]]' doctrine is the insistence that divine will, though free, does not change. [[maimonides|Maimonides]] rejects the [[Kalam|Mutakallimun]] view that [[god|God]] exercises new volitions in time, arguing instead that all divine decrees are encompassed within a single, eternal act of will. This preserves both divine immutability and the genuine contingency of created things.
