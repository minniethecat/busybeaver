## Definition

The soul (néphesch, âme) in [[maimonides|Maimonides]]' u[[wisdom|sage]] is a multi-layered concept. The Hebrew term néphesch can denote the vital soul (shared with ani[[evil|mal]]s), the rational soul (unique to humans), and the principle of will and appetite. [[maimonides|Maimonides]] follows the [[aristotle|Aristote]]lian division of the soul into nutritive, sensitive, appetitive, and rational faculties. (P02-004) (P03-022)

## In the Guide

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] discusses the affections of the soul — emotions, desires, and moral dispositions — and their relationship to [[wisdom|intellect]]ual perfection. The soul's affections can either impede or support the pursuit of [[wisdom|knowledge]], dep[[final-cause|end]]ing on whether they are governed by reason. (P02-005) (P03-023)

The rational soul's yearning for [[wisdom|knowledge]] is what drives the seeker to overcome obstacles. [[maimonides|Maimonides]] speaks of the "noble soul" that stimulates one to persevere in the search for truth despite difficulties and distractions. (001-003)
