## Definition

Truth (Arabic: *al-ḥaqīqa*, Hebrew: *emet*, French: *[[knowledge|vérité]]*) for [[maimonides|Maimonides]] is not merely correct belief but demonstrated [[wisdom|knowledge]] — propositions established by rational proof that compel the assent of the [[wisdom|intellect]]. The Guide opens by declaring that its [[final-cause|purpose]] is "que la [[knowledge|vérité]] s'établît pour toi méthodiquement" (that truth be established for you methodically), signaling that truth is not given but constructed through a disciplined [[wisdom|intellect]]ual process (001-003).

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] distinguishes sharply between those who possess truth through demonstration and those who merely hold true opinions through authority or convention. The latter may accidentally coincide with the truth, but they lack the [[wisdom|intellect]]ual grounding that makes one a genuine knower. This distinction underpins his entire pedagogical method in the Guide: he does not simply tell the reader what is true but leads them through the reasoning that establishes the truth (002-006).

## In the Guide

Throughout the Guide, [[maimonides|Maimonides]] returns to the theme of truth and the conditions of its attainment. He identifies three obstacles to the apprehension of truth: the difficulty of the subject matter, the limitations of the human [[wisdom|intellect]], and — most insidiously — the attachment to opinions acquired through habit and social conformity (003-003). The [[wisdom|intellect]]ual virtue of the philosopher consists precisely in the willingness to follow the argument wherever it leads, even when it contradicts received opinion.

In his discussion of divine attributes, [[maimonides|Maimonides]] illustrates how truth can be arrived at through negation. While the positive essence of [[god|God]] cannot be known, the systematic negation of privations — determining what [[god|God]] is not — yields a progressively more adequate [[wisdom|intellect]]ual grasp of the divine. This negative method is itself a form of truth-seeking, and the Guide characterizes it as the most that human reason can achieve with respect to [[god|God]] (008-002).

> Je sais que toutes les vérités que nous concevons, surtout en ce qui concerne Dieu, ne sont connues que par la démonstration.
> (009-001)

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] addresses the relationship between truth and Scripture in several key pas[[wisdom|sage]]s. He insists that apparent conflicts between philosophical demonstration and the plain sense of Scripture must be resolved through [[esoteric-interpretation|allegorical interpretation]] — truth cannot contradict truth. If a biblical pas[[wisdom|sage]] appears to assert something contrary to demonstrated truth, the pas[[wisdom|sage]] must be interpreted figuratively, be[[causality|cause]] "les principes d'une [[knowledge|vérité]] absolue" (the principles of absolute truth) once demonstrated cannot be set aside (016-001). This hermeneutical principle is one of the most radical and consequential claims of the Guide.

The ethical dimension of truth is equally important for [[maimonides|Maimonides]]. In a pas[[wisdom|sage]] that echoes [[plato|Plato]]'s parable of the cave, he describes how most people "se figure la [[knowledge|vérité]] dans le sens contraire à celui qu'elle a véritablement" (imagine the truth in the sense contrary to what it truly is), mistaking images for realities (027-001). The Guide aims to turn the reader's [[wisdom|intellect]]ual gaze toward the source of truth.

## Interpretation

[[maimonides|Maimonides]]' concept of truth is fundamentally [[aristotle|Aristote]]lian in its identification of truth with demonstrated [[wisdom|knowledge]], but it is inflected by his Jewish context in important ways. Unlike [[aristotle|Aristotle]], for whom philosophy and theology are separate inquiries, [[maimonides|Maimonides]] holds that the [[law|Law]] itself commands the pursuit of philosophical truth. The Guide is thus not an apology for philosophy within Judaism but an argument that Judaism requires philosophy for its own fulfillment (004-001).
