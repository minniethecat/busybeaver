# Human Perfection

**Human Perfection** (*al-kamāl al-insānī*, Hebrew: *ha-shelemut ha-enoshit*) is the ethical-teleological doctrine at the heart of [[Maimonides]]' philosophical anthropology. In the concluding chapters of the *Guide for the Perplexed*, [[maimonides|Maimonides]] distinguishes four types of human perfection and identifies the fourth — [[wisdom|intellect]]ual perfection — as man's ultimate [[Knowledge|end]] (*[[final-cause|telos]]*), while acknowledging the essential role of moral and practical perfections as prerequisites.

## The Four Types of Perfection

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] enumerates four asc[[final-cause|end]]ing grades of human perfection:

| Grade | Type | Description | Value |
|-------|------|-------------|-------|
| 1 | Material | Wealth, possessions, bodily health | Lowest — external and transient |
| 2 | Bodily | Physical strength, beauty, constitution | Low — shared with ani[[evil|mal]]s |
| 3 | Moral | Ethical virtue, justice, character | Higher — but only social, not intrinsic |
| 4 | [[intellect|Intellect]]ual | [[knowledge|Knowledge]] of [[god|God]], metaphysics, true opinions | Highest — the true human *[[final-cause|telos]]* |

> Celui qui veut obtenir la perfection humaine s'instruise d'abord dans la logique, ensuite dans les mathématiques, puis dans la physique, et enfin dans la métaphysique. (041-003)

## Intellectual Perfection as Final End

The fourth type of perfection — the acquisition of true metaphysical [[wisdom|knowledge]] concerning [[God]], the [[Angels|separate intelligences]], and the governance of the universe — constitutes, for [[maimonides|Maimonides]], the ultimate [[final-cause|purpose]] of human existence. This perfection alone is "truly yours" (*al-kamāl al-ḥaqīqī*), as it inheres in the [[Intellect|intellect]] rather than in external goods or bodily states:

> Lorsque tu es parvenu à la perfection humaine, tu es au rang de Rabbi Akiba, qui entra en paix et sortit en paix. (035-003)

## The Relationship Between Moral and Intellectual Perfection

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] emphasizes that moral perfection (Grade 3) is a necessary **prerequisite** for [[wisdom|intellect]]ual perfection (Grade 4), but not its equal. The [[ethics|moral virtues]] — justice, courage, temperance — regulate the [[Soul|soul's]] passions and create the conditions for contemplative activity. However, moral perfection is instrumental; [[wisdom|intellect]]ual perfection is intrinsic.

> Si l'homme est parvenu à cette perfection, il a atteint la perfection humaine véritable, et non pas celle qui n'est que dans l'imagination des hommes. (069-021)

## Imitatio Dei

The perfected human [[wisdom|intellect]] participates, in a limited way, in the divine [[wisdom|intellect]]. Through the [[Active Intellect]]'s illumination, the perfected person achieves a form of *imitatio Dei* — knowing as [[god|God]] knows, governing as [[god|God]] governs — which [[maimonides|Maimonides]] identifies with the prophetic state and the world-to-come.
