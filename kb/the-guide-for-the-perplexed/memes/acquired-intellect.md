# Acquired Intellect

The **Acquired Intellect** (*al-'aql al-mustafād*, Hebrew: *ha-sekhel ha-nikneh*) is the third and highest stage of human [[wisdom|intellect]]ual development in [[Maimonides]]' [[knowledge|epistemology]], following the material (hylic) [[wisdom|intellect]] and the [[wisdom|intellect]] *in habitu*. Drawing on [[Aristotle|Aristotelian]] and Arabic philosophical traditions — particularly [[Alfarabi]] and [[Avicenna]] — [[maimonides|Maimonides]] describes the acquired [[wisdom|intellect]] as the state in which the human mind has actualized all intelligible forms and achieved conjunction (*ittiṣāl*) with the [[Active Intellect]].

## The Three Stages of Intellect

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] inherited from the Arabic [[aristotle|Aristote]]lian tradition a tripartite [[divine-names|schem]]e of [[wisdom|intellect]]ual development:

| Stage | Arabic Term | Description |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| Material [[intellect|Intellect]] | *al-'aql al-hayūlānī* | Pure potentiality for thought; the innate rational capacity |
| [[intellect|Intellect]] *in habitu* | *al-'aql bi-l-[[evil|mal]]aka* | The [[wisdom|intellect]] after acquiring primary intelligibles and logical tools |
| Acquired Intellect | *al-'aql al-mustafād* | Full actualization; all intelligible forms possessed and contemplated |

> L'intellect acquis n'est autre chose que la perfection de l'intellect en acte, lorsqu'il a saisi toutes les formes intelligibles. (069-006)

## Conjunction with the Active Intellect

The defining characteristic of the acquired [[wisdom|intellect]] is its **conjunction** (*ittiṣāl*) with the [[Active Intellect]] — the separate cosmic [[intellect|intelligence]] that illuminates the sublunar world and actualizes human thought. Through this conjunction, the perfected human [[wisdom|intellect]] achieves a kind of immortality, becoming capable of surviving the death of the body:

> L'intellect actif universel devient intellect acquis et intellect en acte, et, comme tel, il est immortel. (047-002)

This doctrine has profound implications for [[Maimonides]]' eschatology: the [[Soul|soul's]] survival after death is not automatic but dep[[final-cause|end]]s on the degree of [[wisdom|intellect]]ual actualization achieved during life. Only the acquired [[wisdom|intellect]] — the portion of the soul that has become pure form — attains immortality.

## Relation to Prophecy

The acquired [[wisdom|intellect]] is intimately connected to [[Prophecy|prophecy]] in [[maimonides|Maimonides]]' system. The prophet represents the highest degree of [[wisdom|intellect]]ual perfection, where conjunction with the [[active-intellect|Active Intellect]] overflows from the rational faculty into the [[imagination|imaginative faculty]], producing prophetic visions and legislative [[knowledge|wisdom]].

> De même qu'il existe un rapport entre les Intelligences séparées et l'idée de l'intellect acquis, qui est également séparée. (071-030)

## Distinction from the Active Intellect

It is crucial to distinguish the acquired [[wisdom|intellect]] from the [[Active Intellect]] itself. The [[active-intellect|Active Intellect]] is a transc[[final-cause|end]]ent, separate [[intellect|intelligence]] — one of the [[Angels|celestial intelligences]]. The acquired [[wisdom|intellect]], by contrast, is a **human achievement**: it comes into being through individual [[wisdom|intellect]]ual effort and exists as the perfected state of a particular person's rational faculty.
