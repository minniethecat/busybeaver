## Definition

Prophecy (prophétie), in [[maimonides|Maimonides]]' account, is an emanation from [[god|God]] that flows through the [[active-intellect|Active Intellect]] to the rational and imaginative faculties of a human being who has attained [[wisdom|intellect]]ual and moral perfection. It is not a miraculous suspension of nature but the highest natural human attainment. (003-003)

## In the Guide

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] devotes extensive discussion to the nature and grades of [[prophets|prophecy]]. He distinguishes between the [[prophets|prophecy]] of [[moses|Moses]] — which was purely [[wisdom|intellect]]ual, without the involvement of the [[imagination|imaginative faculty]] — and the [[prophets|prophecy]] of other prophets, which involved both [[wisdom|intellect]] and [[imagination]].

The external words of prophecies are accommodated to the [[wisdom|understanding]] of the prophet's audience. The true content of [[prophets|prophecy]] is [[wisdom|intellect]]ual, but it is clothed in imagery and language that ordinary people can grasp.
