## Definition

The [[wisdom|intellect]] (intelligence) is the human faculty by which one appreh[[final-cause|end]]s intelligible truths. [[maimonides|Maimonides]] distinguishes between the material [[wisdom|intellect]] (potential), the acquired [[wisdom|intellect]] (in act), and the [[active-intellect|Active Intellect]] — the transc[[final-cause|end]]ent cosmic [[wisdom|intellect]] that actualizes human [[wisdom|knowledge]]. (P02-002)

## In the Guide

[[maimonides|Maimonides]] compares the faculty of [[wisdom|intellect]] with the faculty of the senses, arguing that just as sensory perception requires both a sensing subject and a sensed object, [[wisdom|intellect]]ual cognition requires a knowing subject and an intelligible object — ultimately grounded in the [[active-intellect|Active Intellect]]. (P02-003)

The biblical term "heart" (leb) is interpreted by [[maimonides|Maimonides]] as referring to thought, will, and intelligence — reflecting the Hebrew conception of the heart as the seat of [[wisdom|intellect]]ual activity. (P02-004)

The perfection of the [[wisdom|intellect]] is, for [[maimonides|Maimonides]], the ultimate human [[final-cause|telos]]. Through [[wisdom|intellect]]ual perfection, one achieves conjunction with the [[active-intellect|Active Intellect]] and thereby attains the highest degree of providence and, ultimately, immortality of the acquired [[wisdom|intellect]].
