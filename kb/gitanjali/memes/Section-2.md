[001-001]W.B. YEATS

[001-002]_September_ 1912.

[001-003]GITANJALI

[001-004]1.

[001-005]Thou hast made me endless, such is thy pleasure. This frail vessel thou

[001-006]emptiest again and again, and fillest it ever with fresh life.

[001-007]This little flute of a reed thou hast carried over hills and dales, and

[001-008]hast breathed through it melodies eternally new.

[001-009]At the immortal touch of thy hands my little heart loses its limits in

[001-010]joy and gives birth to utterance ineffable.

[001-011]Thy infinite gifts come to me only on these very small hands of mine.

[001-012]Ages pass, and still thou pourest, and still there is room to fill.

[001-013]2.

[001-014]When thou commandest me to sing it seems that my heart would break with

[001-015][[vanity|pride]]; and I look to thy face, and tears come to my eyes.

[001-016]All that is harsh and dissonant in my life melts into one sweet

[001-017]harmony—and my [[worship|adoration]] spreads wings like a glad bird on its flight

[001-018]across the sea.

[001-019]I know thou takest pleasure in my [[song|singing]]. I know that only as a [[poet|singer]]

[001-020]I come before thy presence.

[001-021]I touch by the [[shore|edge]] of the far-spreading wing of my song thy feet which

[001-022]I could never aspire to reach.

[001-023]Drunk with the joy of singing I forget myself and call thee friend who

[001-024]art my [[king|lord]].

[001-025]3.

[001-026]I know not how thou singest, my master! I ever listen in silent

[001-027][[wonder|amazement]].

[001-028]The light of thy music illumines the world. The life breath of thy

[001-029]music runs from sky to sky. The holy [[river|stream]] of thy music breaks through

[001-030]all stony obstacles and rushes on.

[001-031]My heart longs to join in thy song, but vainly struggles for a voice. I

[001-032]would speak, but speech breaks not into song, and I cry out baffled.

[001-033]Ah, thou hast made my heart captive in the endless meshes of thy music,

[001-034]my master!

[001-035]4.

[001-036][[life|Life]] of my life, I shall ever try to keep my body [[holy|pure]], knowing that

[001-037]thy living touch is upon all my limbs.

[001-038]I shall ever try to keep all untruths out from my thoughts, knowing

[001-039]that thou art that truth which has kindled the light of reason in my

[001-040]mind.

[001-041]I shall ever try to drive all evils away from my heart and keep my love

[001-042]in flower, knowing that thou hast thy seat in the inmost [[sacred|shrine]] of my

[001-043]heart.

[001-044]And it shall be my endeavour to reveal thee in my actions, knowing it

[001-045]is thy power gives me strength to act.

[001-046]5.

[001-047]I ask for a [[time|moment]]’s indulgence to sit by thy side. The works that I

[001-048]have in hand I will finish afterwards.

[001-049]Away from the [[vision|sight]] of thy face my heart knows no [[peace|rest]] nor respite, and

[001-050]my work becomes an endless toil in a shoreless sea of toil.

[001-051]Today the summer has come at my window with its sighs and murmurs; and

[001-052]the bees are plying their minstrelsy at the court of the flowering

[001-053][[garden|grove]].

[001-054]Now it is time to sit quite, face to face with thee, and to sing

[001-055][[offering|dedication]] of life in this silent and overflowing leisure.

[001-056]6.

[001-057]Pluck this little flower and take it, delay not! I fear lest it droop

[001-058]and drop into the [[earth|dust]].

[001-059]I may not find a place in thy garland, but honour it with a touch of

[001-060]pain from thy hand and pluck it. I fear lest the day end before I am

[001-061]aware, and the time of offering go by.

[001-062]Though its colour be not [[ocean|deep]] and its smell be faint, use this flower

[001-063]in thy service and pluck it while there is time.

[001-064]7.

[001-065]My song has put off her adornments. She has no pride of dress and

[001-066]decoration. Ornaments would mar our union; they would come between thee

[001-067]and me; their jingling would drown thy whispers.

[001-068]My poet’s vanity dies in shame before thy sight. O master poet, I have

[001-069]sat down at thy feet. Only let me make my life simple and straight,

[001-070]like a flute of reed for thee to fill with music.

[001-071]8.

[001-072]The child who is decked with prince’s robes and who has jewelled chains

[001-073]round his neck loses all pleasure in his play; his dress hampers him at

[001-074]every step.

[001-075]In fear that it may be frayed, or stained with dust he keeps himself

[001-076]from the world, and is afraid even to move.

[001-077][[mother|Mother]], it is no gain, thy bondage of finery, if it keep one shut off

[001-078]from the healthful dust of the [[world|earth]], if it rob one of the right of

[001-079][[door|entrance]] to the great fair of common human life.

[001-080]9.

[001-081]O Fool, try to carry thyself upon thy own shoulders! O beggar, to come

[001-082]beg at thy own door!

[001-083]Leave all thy burdens on his hands who can bear all, and never look

[001-084]behind in regret.

[001-085]Thy [[longing|desire]] at once puts out the light from the lamp it touches with its

[001-086]breath. It is unholy—take not thy gifts through its unclean hands.

[001-087]Accept only what is offered by [[love|sacred love]].

[001-088]10.

[001-089]Here is thy footstool and there rest thy feet where live the poorest,

[001-090]and lowliest, and lost.

[001-091]When I try to bow to thee, my obeisance cannot reach down to the depth

[001-092]where thy feet rest among the poorest, and lowliest, and lost.

[001-093][[pride|Pride]] can never approach to where thou walkest in the clothes of the

[001-094]humble among the poorest, and lowliest, and lost.

[001-095]My heart can never find its [[journey|way]] to where thou keepest company with the

[001-096]companionless among the poorest, the lowliest, and the lost.

[001-097]11.

[001-098]Leave this chanting and singing and telling of beads! Whom dost thou

[001-099]worship in this lonely dark corner of a temple with doors all shut?

[001-100]Open thine eyes and see thy [[divine|God]] is not before thee!

[001-101]He is there where the tiller is tilling the hard ground and where the

[001-102]pathmaker is breaking stones. He is with them in sun and in shower, and

[001-103]his garment is covered with dust. Put of thy holy mantle and even like

[001-104]him come down on the dusty soil!

[001-105]Deliverance? Where is this deliverance to be found? Our master himself

[001-106]has joyfully taken upon him the bonds of creation; he is bound with us

[001-107]all for ever.

[001-108]Come out of thy meditations and leave aside thy flowers and incense!

[001-109]What harm is there if thy clothes become tattered and stained? Meet him

[001-110]and stand by him in toil and in sweat of thy brow.

[001-111]12.

[001-112]The time that my journey takes is long and the [[path|way]] of it long.

[001-113]I came out on the chariot of the first gleam of light, and pursued my

[001-114]voyage through the wildernesses of worlds leaving my track on many a

[001-115]star and planet.

[001-116]It is the most distant course that comes nearest to thyself, and that

[001-117]training is the most intricate which leads to the utter simplicity of a

[001-118]tune.

[001-119]The traveller has to knock at every alien door to come to his own, and

[001-120]one has to wander through all the outer worlds to reach the innermost

[001-121][[temple|shrine]] at the end.

[001-122]My eyes strayed far and wide before I shut them and said “Here art

[001-123]thou!”

[001-124]The question and the cry “Oh, where?” melt into tears of a thousand

[001-125]streams and deluge the world with the flood of the assurance “I am!”

[001-126]13.

[001-127]The song that I came to sing remains unsung to this day.

[001-128]I have spent my days in stringing and in unstringing my instrument.

[001-129]The time has not come true, the words have not been rightly set; only

[001-130]there is the agony of wishing in my heart.

[001-131]The blossom has not opened; only the wind is sighing by.

[001-132]I have not seen his face, nor have I listened to his voice; only I have

[001-133]heard his gentle footsteps from the road before my [[home|house]].

[001-134]The livelong day has passed in spreading his seat on the floor; but the

[001-135]lamp has not been lit and I cannot ask him into my house.

[001-136]I live in the hope of meeting with him; but this meeting is not yet.

[001-137]14.

[001-138]My desires are many and my cry is pitiful, but ever didst thou save me

[001-139]by hard refusals; and this strong mercy has been wrought into my life

[001-140]through and through.

[001-141]Day by day thou art making me worthy of the simple, great gifts that

[001-142]thou gavest to me unasked—this sky and the light, this body and the

[001-143]life and the mind—saving me from perils of overmuch desire.

[001-144]There are times when I languidly linger and times when I awaken and

[001-145]hurry in [[seeking|search]] of my goal; but cruelly thou hidest thyself from before

[001-146]me.

[001-147]Day by day thou art making me worthy of thy full acceptance by refusing

[001-148]me ever and anon, saving me from perils of weak, uncertain desire.

[001-149]15.

[001-150]I am here to sing thee songs. In this hall of thine I have a corner

[001-151]seat.

[001-152]In thy world I have no work to do; my useless life can only break out

[001-153]in tunes without a purpose.

[001-154]When the hour strikes for thy silent worship at the dark temple of

[001-155][[night|midnight]], command me, my master, to stand before thee to sing.

[001-156]When in the morning air the golden harp is tuned, honour me, commanding

[001-157]my presence.

[001-158]16.

[001-159]I have had my invitation to this world’s festival, and thus my life has

[001-160]been blessed. My eyes have seen and my ears have heard.

[001-161]It was my part at this feast to play upon my instrument, and I have

[001-162]done all I could.

[001-163]Now, I ask, has the time come at last when I may go in and see thy face

[001-164]and offer thee my silent salutation?

[001-165]17.

[001-166]I am only waiting for love to give myself up at last into his hands.

[001-167]That is why it is so late and why I have been guilty of such omissions.

[001-168]They come with their laws and their codes to bind me fast; but I evade

[001-169]them ever, for I am only waiting for love to give myself up at last

[001-170]into his hands.

[001-171]People blame me and call me heedless; I doubt not they are right in

[001-172]their blame.

[001-173]The market day is over and work is all done for the busy. Those who

[001-174]came to call me in vain have gone back in anger. I am only waiting for

[001-175]love to give myself up at last into his hands.

[001-176]18.

[001-177]Clouds heap upon clouds and it darkens. Ah, love, why dost thou let me

[001-178]wait outside at the door all alone?

[001-179]In the busy moments of the noontide work I am with the crowd, but on

[001-180]this dark lonely day it is only for thee that I hope.

[001-181]If thou showest me not thy face, if thou leavest me wholly aside, I

[001-182]know not how I am to pass these long, rainy hours.

[001-183]I keep gazing on the far-away gloom of the sky, and my heart wanders

[001-184]wailing with the restless wind.

[001-185]19.

[001-186]If thou speakest not I will fill my heart with thy silence and endure

[001-187]it. I will keep still and wait like the night with starry vigil and its

[001-188]head bent low with patience.

[001-189]The morning will surely come, the darkness will vanish, and thy voice

[001-190]pour down in golden streams breaking through the sky.

[001-191]Then thy words will take wing in songs from every one of my birds’

[001-192]nests, and thy melodies will break forth in flowers in all my forest

[001-193]groves.

[001-194]20.

[001-195]On the day when the lotus bloomed, alas, my mind was straying, and I

[001-196]knew it not. My basket was empty and the flower remained unheeded.

[001-197]Only now and again a sadness fell upon me, and I started up from my

[001-198]dream and felt a sweet trace of a strange fragrance in the south wind.

[001-199]That vague sweetness made my heart ache with [[desire|longing]] and it seemed to

[001-200]me that is was the eager breath of the summer seeking for its

[001-201]completion.

[001-202]I knew not then that it was so near, that it was mine, and that this

[001-203]perfect sweetness had blossomed in the depth of my own heart.
