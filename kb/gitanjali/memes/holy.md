# Holy

The **Holy** in [[rabindranath-tagore]]'s *[[gitanjali-song-offerings]]* is not confined to temples, scriptures, or ritual specialists. It is a quality that inheres in the world itself — a "holy stream" that flows through all things, a "holy mantle" that covers even the dusty and the ordinary. Tagore's vision of holiness is radically inclusive: what is holy is not the separated and the pure but the common world touched by divine presence.

## In Gitanjali

### The Holy Stream

Holiness flows like water, crossing all boundaries:

> …sic runs from sky to sky. The holy stream of thy music breaks th… (001-029)

"The holy stream of thy music" — holiness is a stream, not a reservoir. It flows, it moves, it crosses boundaries. "Breaks through" — the holy is not contained by human categories or institutions. It breaks through all barriers, all attempts to channel or control it. The image of a stream suggests both power and accessibility: anyone can come to the water.

### The Holy Mantle

The divine covers the soul with holiness:

> …covered with dust. Put of thy holy mantle and even like (001-103)

"Put off thy holy mantle" — holiness is like a garment the divine wears and can remove. The "holy mantle" covers and protects; to ask the divine to "put off" this mantle is to ask for intimacy, for the divine to approach without the distance that holiness creates. Yet the mantle remains holy — holiness is not diminished by being set aside; it is simply relocated, brought closer.

### The Unholy as Warning

Holiness is defined partly through its violation:

> … turbulent, and snatch with unholy greed the offerings from (002-125)

"Unholy greed" — the negative term illuminates the positive. What is unholy is what takes rather than receives, what treats sacred offerings as objects to be seized. The unholy is not the opposite of the holy but its desecration — the holy violated, the sacred space invaded. To call greed "unholy" is to say that there is a way of desiring that profanes what it touches.

### The Holy One in Delusion

Even holiness can be obscured:

> …ith delusion and dust, O thou holy one, (002-183)

"O thou holy one" — the direct address to the divine as holy. But the context is striking: the holy one is covered "with delusion and dust." Holiness does not mean separation from the world's messiness; the holy one is found precisely in the delusion and dust of ordinary existence. This is Tagore's great reversal: holiness is not in escaping the world but in encountering the divine within it.

### The Holy Stream of Oblivion

Holiness is associated with forgetting, with release from the burden of self:

> holy stream of oblivion when their… (006-042)

"Holy stream of oblivion" — the stream of holiness is also a stream of forgetting. To be holy is not to accumulate merit or memory but to let go. The holy stream carries away what the soul no longer needs — the regrets, the achievements, the weight of a separate self. Holiness is lightness, release, the freedom of being carried by something larger than oneself.

## Philosophical Background

- **Otto's phenomenology of the holy**: Rudolf Otto defined the holy (*das Heilige*) as the *mysterium tremendum et fascinans* — a mystery that both awes and attracts. Tagore's "holy stream" shares this double character: it is overwhelming (breaking all barriers) and inviting (accessible to all).
- **Durkheim's sacred/profane**: Emile Durkheim saw the sacred as what a society sets apart from the ordinary. Tagore reverses this: the holy is what breaks into the ordinary, what refuses to be set apart. The holy stream flows through the profane world and sanctifies it.
- **Bhakti's immanent sacred**: In the bhakti traditions, the divine is not transcendent but immanent — present in the world, accessible through love. Tagore's holiness reflects this: the holy is not elsewhere but here, not then but now.

## Poetic Expression

| Strategy | Example | Effect |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Flowing imagery | "the holy stream of thy music" | Holiness as movement, crossing all boundaries |
| Garment metaphor | "put off thy holy mantle" | Holiness as divine covering, removable for intimacy |
| Negative definition | "unholy greed" | Defining holiness through its violation |
| Paradoxical address | "O thou holy one" amid "delusion and dust" | Holiness found in the impure world |
| Oblivion association | "holy stream of oblivion" | Holiness as release and forgetting |
