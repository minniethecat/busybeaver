# Truth

In [[rabindranath-tagore]]'s *[[gitanjali-song-offerings]]*, **truth** is not a doctrine to be affirmed but a presence to be inhabited. It is what "kindles the light of reason," what gives words their substance, and what demands that the knower align their entire life with what they know. Tagore presents truth as simultaneously intellectual, moral, and devotional — a unity that the modern fragmentation of knowledge cannot capture.

## In Gitanjali

### Truth as the Source of Reason

Truth is not opposed to reason but is its very ground:

> I shall ever try to keep all untruths out from my thoughts, knowing (001-038)
> that thou art that truth which has kindled the light of reason in my mind. (001-039)

"Thou art that truth which has kindled the light of reason" — truth is prior to reason, its source and sustainer. Reason does not discover truth; truth kindles reason. This reverses the modern assumption that reason is the tool for finding truth. Instead, truth is the flame that lights the lamp of reason. The speaker's ethical commitment — "I shall ever try to keep all untruths out from my thoughts" — is grounded in this recognition: because truth is the source of thought itself, falsehood in thought is a kind of self-betrayal.

### Words from the Depth of Truth

Truth gives language its substance:

> Where words come out from the depth of truth; (002-141)

This line appears in the famous prayer for India's awakening (poem 35), alongside "Where the mind is without fear" and "Where knowledge is free." "Words come out from the depth of truth" — truth has depth, it is not a surface property of propositions. Words that come from this depth have weight, authority, the power to move. Words that come from anywhere else are hollow, however clever. The "depth" image suggests that truth is not easily accessed; one must descend, go below the surface of convention and habit.

### Truth as the Kindler of Reason

Truth is not discovered by reason; reason is ignited by truth:

> I shall ever try to keep all untruths out from my thoughts, knowing that thou art that truth which has kindled the light of reason in my mind. (001-038)

"Thou art that truth which has kindled the light of reason" — truth is prior to reason, its source and sustainer. This reverses the modern assumption that reason is the tool for finding truth. Instead, truth is the flame that lights the lamp of reason. The speaker's ethical commitment — "I shall ever try to keep all untruths out from my thoughts" — is grounded in this recognition: because truth is the source of thought itself, falsehood in thought is a kind of self-betrayal.

### The Time That Has Not Come True

Truth is not only a property of statements but of timing — the right word at the right moment:

> The time has not come true, the words have not been rightly set; only there is the agony of wishing in my heart. (001-129)

"The time has not come true" — truth has a temporal dimension. Something can be true in itself but not yet "come true" in time. The words exist but are not "rightly set" — they await their proper arrangement, their kairos. "Only there is the agony of wishing in my heart" — the gap between truth known and truth realized is experienced as agony, as longing. Truth deferred is truth that hurts.

### The True Being Lost Behind Walls

Truth is also authenticity — the self that is hidden behind constructed identities:

> …day by day I lose sight of my true being in its dark shadow. (002-084)

"Lose sight of my true being" — the self that builds walls of name and reputation loses contact with what is most real within. The "dark shadow" is cast by the very wall meant to protect and define. This is truth as existential authenticity: not a proposition to be asserted but a self to be lived. The tragedy is that the wall-builder "takes pride in this great wall" — the false self is not an accident but an achievement, carefully constructed and defended. Truth here is what gets buried beneath identity.

### Truth Embodied in Action

The naming of truth as the source of reason (001-038) is part of a fourfold commitment: to keep the body pure, to keep untruth out of thoughts, to drive evils from the heart, and to reveal the divine in action. This sequence — body, mind, heart, deed — insists that truth must flow through every dimension of existence. Truth is made visible not in assertions but in conduct. The ultimate verification of truth is a life.

## Philosophical Background

- **The Upanishadic satya**: The Upanishads identify truth (*satya*) with being itself (*sat*). What is most real is most true; illusion (*maya*) is what appears real but is not. Tagore inherits this equation: truth is not primarily a property of statements but of reality. To know truth is to be in contact with what is real.
- **Gandhi's satyagraha**: Tagore's contemporary Gandhi developed *satyagraha* — "truth-force" or "holding fast to truth" — as a method of political action. While Gitanjali predates Gandhi's movement, both share the conviction that truth is not passive: it demands embodiment, action, the alignment of life with understanding. Tagore's "reveal thee in my actions" is a personal satyagraha.
- **The Romantic critique of abstraction**: Tagore, like Wordsworth and Coleridge, mistrusted the Enlightenment's reduction of truth to abstract propositions. Truth must be felt, lived, experienced in nature and relationship. "Words come out from the depth of truth" — truth is an existential depth, not a logical surface.

## Poetic Expression

| Strategy | Example | Effect |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Causal reversal | "thou art that truth which has kindled the light of reason" | Truth precedes and enables reason, not the reverse |
| Spatial depth | "words come out from the depth of truth" | Truth has vertical dimension; superficial speech is untruthful |
| Temporal becoming | "the time has not come true" | Truth unfolds in time, has a season of arrival |
| Hidden being | "lose sight of my true being" | Truth as authentic self buried beneath constructed identity |
| Volitional grammar | "I shall ever try to keep all untruths out" | Truth requires active, sustained commitment |
