# Nest

The **nest** in [[rabindranath-tagore]]'s *[[gitanjali-song-offerings]]* is the architecture of shelter — the intimate dwelling place where "love encloses the soul," the counterpart to the infinite [[sky]] that is also the divine. "Thou art the sky and thou art the nest as well" — this single line from the *Gitanjali* condenses the book's deepest spiritual vision: the divine is both the boundless above and the intimate enclosure, the vast and the close, the infinite space and the small shelter that holds the soul.

## In Gitanjali

### Nests and Melodies

> ...nests, and thy melodies will break... (001-192)

The nests are linked to melodies — the nest as the place from which song emerges. The bird sings from its nest; the poet sings from the place of shelter. The nest is the origin point of beauty, the small, protected space from which music "breaks forth" into the world.

### Birds Flying to Their Nests

> ...flying to their nests... (003-020)

"Flying to their nests" — the return at evening. The nest is the destination of the day's flight, the place the bird comes back to when the journey is done. The image is one of homecoming: after the freedom of the sky, the shelter of the nest. In the *Gitanjali*'s spiritual geography, the soul too has a nest — a place of return, of rest, of belonging.

### Thou Art the Nest

> ...Thou art the sky and thou art the nest as well... (004-190)

This is the *Gitanjali*'s climactic statement about the nature of the divine. "Thou art the sky" — the infinite, the boundless, the transcendent. "And thou art the nest as well" — the intimate, the sheltering, the close. The divine is not one or the other but both at once: the overwhelming vastness that surrounds and the tender enclosure that holds. No line in the *Gitanjali* more perfectly expresses Tagore's vision of a God who is simultaneously infinite and intimate.

### Love Encloses the Soul

> ...thou beautiful, there in the nest is thy love that encloses the... (004-191)

"In the nest is thy love that encloses the soul" — the nest is defined by love. It is not the structure itself but the love within it that makes the nest a shelter. The nest is love made spatial, love given architectural form. What encloses the soul is not wood and straw but the love that builds the nest and waits within it.

### Mountain Nests

> ...mountain nests let all my life take its voyage... (007-004)

"Mountain nests" — the nest placed high, on the mountain. The nest is not only the lowly shelter of the valley but can be set on the heights. From the mountain nest, "all my life" takes its voyage — the nest is the launching point, the place from which the journey begins. Even the highest flight starts from a nest.

## Nest and Infinity

The nest is the *Gitanjali*'s most powerful image of the divine immanence — the presence that is not only "out there" in the infinite sky but "right here" in the small, close, sheltering place. Tagore's nest belongs to the Indian tradition of the *hrdaya-guhā* — the cave of the heart where the divine dwells. But it is also the literal nest of the birds of Bengal, the woven shelter in the trees. The nest is where the cosmic and the domestic meet, where infinity folds itself small enough to hold a single soul.
