[003-001]I sit on the grass and gaze upon the sky and dream of the sudden

[003-002][[beauty|splendour]] of thy coming—all the lights ablaze, golden pennons flying

[003-003]over thy car, and they at the roadside standing agape, when they see

[003-004]thee come down from thy seat to raise me from the [[earth|dust]], and set at thy

[003-005]side this ragged beggar girl a-tremble with shame and [[vanity|pride]], like a

[003-006]creeper in a summer breeze.

[003-007]But time glides on and still no sound of the wheels of thy chariot.

[003-008]Many a procession passes by with noise and shouts and glamour of glory.

[003-009]Is it only thou who wouldst stand in the shadow silent and behind them

[003-010]all? And only I who would wait and weep and wear out my heart in vain

[003-011][[desire|longing]]?

[003-012]42.

[003-013]Early in the day it was whispered that we should sail in a boat, only

[003-014]thou and I, and never a soul in the world would know of this our

[003-015][[journey|pilgrimage]] to no country and to no end.

[003-016]In that shoreless [[sea|ocean]], at thy silently listening smile my songs would

[003-017]swell in melodies, free as waves, free from all bondage of words.

[003-018]Is the time not come yet? Are there works still to do? Lo, the evening

[003-019]has come down upon the shore and in the fading light the seabirds come

[003-020]flying to their nests.

[003-021]Who knows when the chains will be off, and the boat, like the last

[003-022]glimmer of sunset, vanish into the night?

[003-023]43.

[003-024]The day was when I did not keep myself in readiness for thee; and

[003-025]entering my heart unbidden even as one of the common crowd, [[stranger|unknown]] to

[003-026]me, my [[master|king]], thou didst press the signet of eternity upon many a

[003-027]fleeting [[time|moment]] of my life.

[003-028]And today when by chance I light upon them and see thy signature, I

[003-029]find they have lain scattered in the dust mixed with the memory of joys

[003-030]and sorrows of my trivial days forgotten.

[003-031]Thou didst not turn in contempt from my childish play among dust, and

[003-032]the steps that I heard in my playroom are the same that are echoing

[003-033]from star to star.

[003-034]44.

[003-035]This is my [[joy|delight]], thus to wait and watch at the wayside where shadow

[003-036]chases light and the rain comes in the wake of the summer.

[003-037]Messengers, with tidings from unknown skies, greet me and speed along

[003-038]the [[path|road]]. My heart is glad within, and the breath of the passing breeze

[003-039]is sweet.

[003-040]From [[morning|dawn]] till dusk I sit here before my door, and I know that of a

[003-041]sudden the happy moment will arrive when I shall see.

[003-042]In the meanwhile I smile and I sing all alone. In the meanwhile the air

[003-043]is filling with the perfume of promise.

[003-044]45.

[003-045]Have you not heard his silent steps? He comes, comes, ever comes.

[003-046]Every moment and every age, every day and every night he comes, comes,

[003-047]ever comes.

[003-048]Many a song have I sung in many a mood of mind, but all their notes

[003-049]have always proclaimed, “He comes, comes, ever comes.”

[003-050]In the fragrant days of sunny April through the forest path he comes,

[003-051]comes, ever comes.

[003-052]In the rainy gloom of July nights on the thundering chariot of clouds

[003-053]he comes, comes, ever comes.

[003-054]In sorrow after sorrow it is his steps that press upon my heart, and it

[003-055]is the golden touch of his feet that makes my joy to shine.

[003-056]46.

[003-057]I know not from what distant time thou art ever coming nearer to meet

[003-058]me. Thy sun and stars can never keep thee hidden from me for aye.

[003-059]In many a morning and eve thy footsteps have been heard and thy

[003-060]messenger has come within my heart and called me in secret.

[003-061]I know not only why today my life is all astir, and a feeling of

[003-062]tremulous joy is passing through my heart.

[003-063]It is as if the time were come to wind up my work, and I feel in the

[003-064]air a faint smell of thy sweet presence.

[003-065]47.

[003-066]The night is nearly spent waiting for him in vain. I fear lest in the

[003-067]morning he suddenly come to my door when I have fallen asleep wearied

[003-068]out. Oh friends, leave the [[road|way]] open to him— forbid him not.

[003-069]If the sounds of his steps does not wake me, do not try to rouse me, I

[003-070]pray. I wish not to be called from my sleep by the clamorous choir of

[003-071]birds, by the riot of wind at the festival of [[light|morning light]]. Let me

[003-072]sleep undisturbed even if my [[king|lord]] comes of a sudden to my door.

[003-073]Ah, my sleep, precious sleep, which only waits for his touch to vanish.

[003-074]Ah, my closed eyes that would open their lids only to the light of his

[003-075]smile when he stands before me like a dream emerging from [[night|darkness]] of

[003-076]sleep.

[003-077]Let him appear before my [[vision|sight]] as the first of all lights and all

[003-078]forms. The first thrill of joy to my awakened soul let it come from his

[003-079]glance. And let my return to myself be immediate return to him.

[003-080]48.

[003-081]The morning sea of silence broke into ripples of bird songs; and the

[003-082]flowers were all merry by the roadside; and the wealth of gold was

[003-083]scattered through the rift of the clouds while we busily went on our

[003-084]way and paid no heed.

[003-085]We sang no glad songs nor played; we went not to the village for

[003-086]barter; we spoke not a word nor smiled; we lingered not on the way. We

[003-087]quickened our pace more and more as the time sped by.

[003-088]The sun rose to the mid sky and doves cooed in the shade. Withered

[003-089]leaves danced and whirled in the hot air of noon. The shepherd boy

[003-090]drowsed and dreamed in the shadow of the banyan tree, and I laid myself

[003-091]down by the water and stretched my tired limbs on the grass.

[003-092]My companions laughed at me in scorn; they held their heads high and

[003-093]hurried on; they never looked back nor rested; they vanished in the

[003-094]distant [[sky|blue]] haze. They crossed many meadows and hills, and passed

[003-095]through strange, far-away countries. All honour to you, heroic host of

[003-096]the interminable path! Mockery and reproach pricked me to rise, but

[003-097]found no response in me. I gave myself up for lost in the depth of a

[003-098]glad [[shame|humiliation]]—in the shadow of a dim delight.

[003-099]The repose of the sun-embroidered green gloom slowly spread over my

[003-100]heart. I forgot for what I had travelled, and I surrendered my mind

[003-101]without struggle to the maze of shadows and songs.

[003-102]At last, when I woke from my slumber and opened my eyes, I saw thee

[003-103]standing by me, flooding my sleep with thy smile. How I had feared that

[003-104]the path was long and wearisome, and the struggle to reach thee was

[003-105]hard!

[003-106]49.

[003-107]You came down from your throne and stood at my cottage door.

[003-108]I was [[song|singing]] all alone in a corner, and the melody caught your ear.

[003-109]You came down and stood at my cottage door.

[003-110]Masters are many in your hall, and songs are sung there at all hours.

[003-111]But the simple carol of this novice struck at your love. One plaintive

[003-112]little strain mingled with the great music of the world, and with a

[003-113]flower for a prize you came down and stopped at my cottage door.

[003-114]50.

[003-115]I had gone a-begging from door to door in the village path, when thy

[003-116]golden chariot appeared in the distance like a gorgeous dream and I

[003-117]wondered who was this King of all kings!

[003-118]My hopes rose high and methought my evil days were at an end, and I

[003-119]stood waiting for alms to be given unasked and for wealth scattered on

[003-120]all sides in the dust.

[003-121]The chariot stopped where I stood. Thy glance fell on me and thou

[003-122]camest down with a smile. I felt that the luck of my life had come at

[003-123]last. Then of a sudden thou didst hold out thy right hand and say “What

[003-124]hast thou to give to me?”

[003-125]Ah, what a kingly jest was it to open thy palm to a beggar to beg! I

[003-126]was confused and stood undecided, and then from my wallet I slowly took

[003-127]out the least little grain of corn and gave it to thee.

[003-128]But how great my surprise when at the day’s end I emptied my bag on the

[003-129]floor to find a least little gram of gold among the poor heap. I

[003-130]bitterly wept and wished that I had had the heart to give thee my all.

[003-131]51.

[003-132]The night darkened. Our day’s works had been done. We thought that the

[003-133]last guest had arrived for the night and the doors in the village were

[003-134]all shut. Only some said the king was to come. We laughed and said “No,

[003-135]it cannot be!”

[003-136]It seemed there were knocks at the door and we said it was nothing but

[003-137]the wind. We put out the lamps and lay down to sleep. Only some said,

[003-138]“It is the messenger!” We laughed and said “No, it must be the wind!”

[003-139]There came a sound in the dead of the night. We sleepily thought it was

[003-140]the distant thunder. The [[world|earth]] shook, the walls rocked, and it troubled

[003-141]us in our sleep. Only some said it was the sound of wheels. We said in

[003-142]a drowsy murmur, “No, it must be the rumbling of clouds!”

[003-143]The night was still dark when the drum sounded. The voice came “Wake

[003-144]up! delay not!” We pressed our hands on our hearts and shuddered with

[003-145]fear. Some said, “Lo, there is the king’s flag!” We stood up on our

[003-146]feet and cried “There is no time for delay!”

[003-147]The king has come—but where are lights, where are wreaths? Where is the

[003-148]throne to seat him? Oh, shame! Oh utter shame! Where is the hall, the

[003-149]decorations? Someone has said, “Vain is this cry! Greet him with empty

[003-150]hands, lead him into thy rooms all bare!”

[003-151]Open the doors, let the conch-shells be sounded! in the depth of the

[003-152]night has come the king of our dark, dreary [[home|house]]. The thunder roars in

[003-153]the sky. The darkness shudders with lightning. Bring out thy tattered

[003-154]piece of mat and spread it in the courtyard. With the storm has come of

[003-155]a sudden our king of the fearful night.

[003-156]52.

[003-157]I thought I should ask of thee—but I dared not—the rose wreath thou

[003-158]hadst on thy neck. Thus I waited for the morning, when thou didst

[003-159]depart, to find a few fragments on the bed. And like a beggar I

[003-160]searched in the dawn only for a stray petal or two.

[003-161]Ah me, what is it I find? What token left of thy love? It is no flower,

[003-162]no spices, no vase of perfumed water. It is thy mighty sword, flashing

[003-163]as a flame, heavy as a bolt of thunder. The young light of morning

[003-164]comes through the window and spreads itself upon thy bed. The morning

[003-165]bird twitters and asks, “Woman, what hast thou got?” No, it is no

[003-166]flower, nor spices, nor vase of perfumed water—it is thy dreadful

[003-167]sword.

[003-168]I sit and muse in wonder, what [[offering|gift]] is this of thine. I can find no

[003-169]place to hide it. I am ashamed to wear it, frail as I am, and it hurts

[003-170]me when I press it to my bosom. Yet shall I bear in my heart this

[003-171]honour of the burden of pain, this gift of thine.

[003-172]From now there shall be no fear left for me in this world, and thou

[003-173]shalt be victorious in all my strife. Thou hast left death for my

[003-174][[friend|companion]] and I shall crown him with my life. Thy sword is with me to

[003-175]cut asunder my bonds, and there shall be no fear left for me in the

[003-176]world.
