[006-001]When the warriors marched back again to their [[king|master]]’s hall where did

[006-002]they hide their power?

[006-003]They had dropped the sword and dropped the bow and the arrow; peace was

[006-004]on their foreheads, and they had left the fruits of their life behind

[006-005]them on the day they marched back again to their master’s hall.

[006-006]86.

[006-007][[death|Death]], thy servant, is at my door. He has crossed [[mystery|the unknown]] sea and

[006-008]brought thy call to my home.

[006-009]The night is dark and my heart is fearful—yet I will take up the lamp,

[006-010]open my gates and bow to him my welcome. It is thy messenger who stands

[006-011]at my door.

[006-012]I will worship him placing at his feet the treasure of my heart.

[006-013]He will go back with his errand done, leaving a dark shadow on my

[006-014]morning; and in my desolate home only my forlorn [[soul|self]] will remain as my

[006-015]last offering to thee.

[006-016]87.

[006-017]In desperate hope I go and [[seeking|search]] for her in all the corners of my

[006-018]room; I find her not.

[006-019]My [[home|house]] is small and what once has gone from it can never be regained.

[006-020]But infinite is thy mansion, my [[master|lord]], and seeking her I have to come to

[006-021]thy door.

[006-022]I stand under the golden canopy of thine evening sky and I lift my

[006-023]eager eyes to thy face.

[006-024]I have come to the brink of eternity from which nothing can vanish—no

[006-025]hope, no happiness, no vision of a face seen through tears.

[006-026]Oh, dip my emptied life into that [[sea|ocean]], plunge it into the deepest

[006-027]fullness. Let me for once feel that lost sweet touch in the allness of

[006-028]the universe.

[006-029]88.

[006-030]Deity of the ruined temple! The broken strings of _Vina_ sing no more

[006-031]your praise. The bells in the evening proclaim not your time of

[006-032]worship. The air is still and silent about you.

[006-033]In your desolate [[nest|dwelling]] comes the vagrant spring breeze. It brings

[006-034]the tidings of flowers—the flowers that for your worship are offered no

[006-035]more.

[006-036]Your worshipper of old wanders ever [[desire|longing]] for favour still refused.

[006-037]In the eventide, when fires and shadows mingle with the gloom of [[earth|dust]],

[006-038]he wearily comes back to the ruined temple with hunger in his heart.

[006-039]Many a festival day comes to you in silence, deity of the ruined

[006-040]temple. Many a night of worship goes away with lamp unlit.

[006-041]Many new images are built by masters of cunning art and carried to the

[006-042]holy [[river|stream]] of oblivion when their time is come.

[006-043]Only the deity of the ruined temple remains unworshipped in deathless

[006-044]neglect.

[006-045]89.

[006-046]No more noisy, loud words from me—such is my master’s will. Henceforth

[006-047]I deal in whispers. The speech of my heart will be carried on in

[006-048]murmurings of a song.

[006-049]Men hasten to the King’s market. All the buyers and sellers are there.

[006-050]But I have my untimely leave in the middle of the day, in the thick of

[006-051]work.

[006-052]Let then the flowers come out in my garden, though it is not their

[006-053]time; and let the midday bees strike up their lazy hum.

[006-054]Full many an [[time|hour]] have I spent in the strife of the good and the evil,

[006-055]but now it is the pleasure of my playmate of the empty days to draw my

[006-056]heart on to him; and I know not why is this sudden call to what useless

[006-057]inconsequence!

[006-058]90.

[006-059]On the day when death will knock at thy door what wilt thou offer to

[006-060]him?

[006-061]Oh, I will set before my guest the full vessel of my life—I will never

[006-062]let him go with empty hands.

[006-063]All the sweet vintage of all my autumn days and summer nights, all the

[006-064]earnings and gleanings of my busy life will I place before him at the

[006-065]close of my days when death will knock at my door.

[006-066]91.

[006-067]O thou the last fulfilment of life, Death, my death, come and whisper

[006-068]to me!

[006-069]Day after day I have kept watch for thee; for thee have I borne the

[006-070]joys and pangs of life.

[006-071]All that I am, that I have, that I hope and all my love have ever

[006-072]flowed towards thee in depth of secrecy. One final glance from thine

[006-073]eyes and my life will be ever thine own.

[006-074]The flowers have been woven and the garland is ready for the

[006-075]bridegroom. After the wedding the bride shall leave her home and meet

[006-076]her lord alone in the solitude of night.

[006-077]92.

[006-078]I know that the day will come when my [[vision|sight]] of this [[world|earth]] shall be

[006-079]lost, and life will take its leave in silence, drawing the last curtain

[006-080]over my eyes.

[006-081]Yet stars will watch at night, and morning rise as before, and hours

[006-082]heave like sea waves casting up pleasures and pains.

[006-083]When I think of this end of my moments, the barrier of the moments

[006-084]breaks and I see by the light of death thy world with its careless

[006-085]treasures. Rare is its lowliest seat, rare is its meanest of lives.

[006-086]Things that I longed for in vain and things that I got—let them pass.

[006-087]Let me but truly possess the things that I ever spurned and overlooked.

[006-088]93.

[006-089]I have got my leave. Bid me farewell, my brothers! I bow to you all and

[006-090]take my departure.

[006-091]Here I give back the keys of my door—and I give up all claims to my

[006-092]house. I only ask for last kind words from you.

[006-093]We were neighbours for long, but I received more than I could give. Now

[006-094]the day has dawned and the lamp that lit my dark corner is out. A

[006-095]summons has come and I am ready for my journey.

[006-096]94.

[006-097]At this time of my parting, wish me good luck, my friends! The sky is

[006-098]flushed with the [[morning|dawn]] and my [[journey|path]] lies beautiful.

[006-099]Ask not what I have with me to take there. I start on my journey with

[006-100]empty hands and expectant heart.

[006-101]I shall put on my wedding garland. Mine is not the red-brown dress of

[006-102]the traveller, and though there are dangers on the [[path|way]] I have no fear

[006-103]in mind.

[006-104]The evening star will come out when my voyage is done and the plaintive

[006-105]notes of the twilight melodies be struck up from the King’s [[gate|gateway]].

[006-106]95.

[006-107]I was not aware of the moment when I first crossed the [[door|threshold]] of

[006-108]this life.

[006-109]What was the power that made me open out into this vast [[wonder|mystery]] like a

[006-110]bud in the forest at [[night|midnight]]!

[006-111]When in the morning I looked upon the light I felt in a moment that I

[006-112]was no stranger in this world, that the inscrutable without name and

[006-113]form had taken me in its arms in the form of my own mother.

[006-114]Even so, in death the same [[stranger|unknown]] will appear as ever known to me. And

[006-115]because I love this life, I know I shall love death as well.

[006-116]The child cries out when from the right breast the mother takes it

[006-117]away, in the very next moment to find in the left one its consolation.

[006-118]96.

[006-119]When I go from hence let this be my parting word, that what I have seen

[006-120]is unsurpassable.

[006-121]I have tasted of the hidden honey of this lotus that expands on the

[006-122]ocean of light, and thus am I blessed—let this be my parting word.

[006-123]In this playhouse of infinite forms I have had my play and here have I

[006-124]caught sight of him that is formless.

[006-125]My whole body and my limbs have thrilled with his touch who is beyond

[006-126]touch; and if the end comes here, let it come—let this be my parting

[006-127]word.

[006-128]97.

[006-129]When my play was with thee I never questioned who thou wert. I knew nor

[006-130]shyness nor fear, my life was boisterous.

[006-131]In the early morning thou wouldst call me from my sleep like my own

[006-132]comrade and lead me running from glade to glade.

[006-133]On those days I never cared to know the meaning of songs thou sangest

[006-134]to me. Only my voice took up the tunes, and my heart danced in their

[006-135]cadence.

[006-136]Now, when the playtime is over, what is this sudden sight that is come

[006-137]upon me? The world with eyes bent upon thy feet stands in awe with all

[006-138]its silent stars.

[006-139]98.

[006-140]I will deck thee with trophies, garlands of my defeat. It is never in

[006-141]my power to escape unconquered.

[006-142]I surely know my [[vanity|pride]] will go to the wall, my life will burst its

[006-143]bonds in exceeding pain, and my empty heart will sob out in [[song|music]] like

[006-144]a hollow reed, and the stone will melt in tears.

[006-145]I surely know the hundred petals of a lotus will not remain closed for

[006-146]ever and the secret [[corner|recess]] of its honey will be bared.

[006-147]From the [[sky|blue]] sky an eye shall gaze upon me and summon me in silence.

[006-148]Nothing will be left for me, nothing whatever, and utter death shall I

[006-149]receive at thy feet.

[006-150]99.

[006-151]When I give up the helm I know that the time has come for thee to take

[006-152]it. What there is to do will be instantly done. Vain is this struggle.

[006-153]Then take away your hands and silently put up with your defeat, my

[006-154]heart, and think it your good fortune to sit perfectly still where you

[006-155]are placed.

[006-156]These my lamps are blown out at every little puff of wind, and trying

[006-157]to light them I forget all else again and again.

[006-158]But I shall be wise this time and wait in the dark, spreading my mat on

[006-159]the floor; and whenever it is thy pleasure, my lord, come silently and

[006-160]take thy seat here.

[006-161]100.

[006-162]I dive down into the depth of the ocean of forms, hoping to gain the

[006-163]perfect pearl of the formless.

[006-164]No more sailing from harbour to harbour with this my weather-beaten

[006-165]boat. The days are long passed when my sport was to be tossed on waves.

[006-166]And now I am eager to die into the deathless.

[006-167]Into the audience hall by the fathomless [[ocean|abyss]] where swells up the

[006-168]music of toneless strings I shall take this harp of my life.

[006-169]I shall tune it to the notes of forever, and when it has sobbed out its

[006-170]last utterance, lay down my silent harp at the feet of the silent.

[006-171]101.

[006-172]Ever in my life have I sought thee with my songs. It was they who led

[006-173]me from door to door, and with them have I felt about me, searching and

[006-174]touching my world.

[006-175]It was my songs that taught me all the lessons I ever learnt; they

[006-176]showed me secret paths, they brought before my sight many a star on the

[006-177]horizon of my heart.

[006-178]They guided me all the day long to the mysteries of the country of

[006-179]pleasure and pain, and, at last, to what palace gate have they brought

[006-180]me in the evening at the end of my journey?

[006-181]102.

[006-182]I boasted among men that I had known you. They see your pictures in all

[006-183]works of mine. They come and ask me, “Who is he?” I know not how to

[006-184]answer them. I say, “Indeed, I cannot tell.” They blame me and they go

[006-185]away in scorn. And you sit there smiling.

[006-186]I put my tales of you into lasting songs. The secret gushes out from my

[006-187]heart. They come and ask me, “Tell me all your meanings.” I know not

[006-188]how to answer them. I say, “Ah, who knows what they mean!” They smile

[006-189]and go away in utter scorn. And you sit there smiling.

[006-190]103.

[006-191]In one salutation to thee, my [[divine|God]], let all my senses spread out and

[006-192]touch this world at thy feet.

[006-193]Like a rain-cloud of July hung low with its burden of unshed showers

[006-194]let all my mind bend down at thy door in one salutation to thee.
