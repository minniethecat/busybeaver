[002-001]21.

[002-002]I must launch out my boat. The languid hours pass by on the shore—Alas

[002-003]for me!

[002-004]The spring has done its flowering and taken leave. And now with the

[002-005]burden of faded futile flowers I wait and linger.

[002-006]The waves have become clamorous, and upon the [[shore|bank]] in the shady lane

[002-007]the yellow leaves flutter and fall.

[002-008]What emptiness do you gaze upon! Do you not feel a thrill passing

[002-009]through the air with the notes of the far-away song floating from the

[002-010]other shore?

[002-011]22.

[002-012]In the [[ocean|deep]] shadows of the rainy July, with secret steps, thou walkest,

[002-013]silent as night, eluding all watchers.

[002-014]Today the morning has closed its eyes, heedless of the insistent calls

[002-015]of the loud east wind, and a thick veil has been drawn over the

[002-016]ever-wakeful [[sky|blue]] sky.

[002-017]The woodlands have hushed their songs, and doors are all shut at every

[002-018][[home|house]]. Thou art the solitary wayfarer in this deserted [[road|street]]. Oh my

[002-019]only friend, my best [[bride|beloved]], the gates are open in my house—do not

[002-020]pass by like a dream.

[002-021]23.

[002-022]Art thou abroad on this stormy night on thy journey of love, my friend?

[002-023]The sky groans like one in despair.

[002-024]I have no sleep tonight. Ever and again I open my door and look out on

[002-025]the [[night|darkness]], my friend!

[002-026]I can see nothing before me. I wonder where lies thy [[journey|path]]!

[002-027]By what dim shore of the ink-black river, by what far edge of the

[002-028]frowning forest, through what mazy depth of gloom art thou threading

[002-029]thy course to come to me, my friend?

[002-030]24.

[002-031]If the day is done, if birds sing no more, if the wind has flagged

[002-032]tired, then draw the veil of darkness thick upon me, even as thou hast

[002-033]wrapt the [[world|earth]] with the coverlet of sleep and tenderly closed the

[002-034]petals of the drooping lotus at dusk.

[002-035]From the traveller, whose sack of provisions is empty before the voyage

[002-036]is ended, whose garment is torn and dustladen, whose strength is

[002-037]exhausted, remove shame and poverty, and renew his life like a flower

[002-038]under the cover of thy kindly night.

[002-039]25.

[002-040]In the night of weariness let me give myself up to sleep without

[002-041]struggle, resting my [[hope|trust]] upon thee.

[002-042]Let me not force my flagging [[soul|spirit]] into a poor preparation for thy

[002-043]worship.

[002-044]It is thou who drawest the veil of night upon the tired eyes of the day

[002-045]to renew its [[vision|sight]] in a fresher gladness of awakening.

[002-046]26.

[002-047]He came and sat by my side but I woke not. What a cursed sleep it was,

[002-048]O miserable me!

[002-049]He came when the night was still; he had his harp in his hands, and my

[002-050]dreams became resonant with its melodies.

[002-051]Alas, why are my nights all thus lost? Ah, why do I ever miss his sight

[002-052]whose breath touches my sleep?

[002-053]27.

[002-054][[light|Light]], oh where is the light? Kindle it with the burning fire of

[002-055][[longing|desire]]!

[002-056]There is the lamp but never a flicker of a flame—is such thy fate, my

[002-057]heart? Ah, death were better by far for thee!

[002-058]Misery knocks at thy door, and her message is that thy [[king|lord]] is wakeful,

[002-059]and he calls thee to the love-tryst through the darkness of night.

[002-060]The sky is overcast with clouds and the rain is ceaseless. I know not

[002-061]what this is that stirs in me—I know not its meaning.

[002-062]A [[time|moment]]’s flash of lightning drags down a deeper gloom on my sight,

[002-063]and my heart gropes for the path to where the [[song|music]] of the night calls

[002-064]me.

[002-065]Light, oh where is the light! Kindle it with the burning fire of

[002-066]desire! It thunders and the wind rushes screaming through the void. The

[002-067]night is black as a black stone. Let not the hours pass by in the dark.

[002-068]Kindle the lamp of love with thy life.

[002-069]28.

[002-070]Obstinate are the trammels, but my heart aches when I try to break

[002-071]them.

[002-072][[freedom|Freedom]] is all I want, but to hope for it I feel ashamed.

[002-073]I am certain that priceless wealth is in thee, and that thou art my

[002-074]best friend, but I have not the heart to sweep away the tinsel that

[002-075]fills my room.

[002-076]The shroud that covers me is a shroud of [[earth|dust]] and death; I hate it, yet

[002-077]hug it in love.

[002-078]My debts are large, my failures great, my shame secret and heavy; yet

[002-079]when I come to ask for my good, I quake in fear lest my prayer be

[002-080]granted.

[002-081]29.

[002-082]He whom I enclose with my name is weeping in this dungeon. I am ever

[002-083]busy building this wall all around; and as this wall goes up into the

[002-084]sky day by day I lose sight of my true being in its dark shadow.

[002-085]I take [[vanity|pride]] in this great wall, and I plaster it with dust and sand

[002-086]lest a least hole should be left in this name; and for all the care I

[002-087]take I lose sight of my true being.

[002-088]30.

[002-089]I came out alone on my [[path|way]] to my tryst. But who is this that follows me

[002-090]in the silent dark?

[002-091]I move aside to avoid his presence but I escape him not.

[002-092]He makes the dust rise from the earth with his swagger; he adds his

[002-093]loud voice to every word that I utter.

[002-094]He is my own little self, my [[master|lord]], he knows no shame; but I am ashamed

[002-095]to come to thy door in his company.

[002-096]31.

[002-097]“Prisoner, tell me, who was it that bound you?”

[002-098]“It was my master,” said the prisoner. “I thought I could outdo

[002-099]everybody in the world in wealth and power, and I amassed in my own

[002-100]treasure-house the money due to my king. When sleep overcame me I lay

[002-101]upon the bed that was for my lord, and on waking up I found I was a

[002-102]prisoner in my own treasure-house.”

[002-103]“Prisoner, tell me, who was it that wrought this unbreakable chain?”

[002-104]“It was I,” said the prisoner, “who forged this chain very carefully. I

[002-105]thought my invincible power would hold the world captive leaving me in

[002-106]a freedom undisturbed. Thus night and day I worked at the chain with

[002-107]huge fires and cruel hard strokes. When at last the work was done and

[002-108]the links were complete and unbreakable, I found that it held me in its

[002-109]grip.”

[002-110]32.

[002-111]By all means they try to hold me secure who love me in this world. But

[002-112]it is otherwise with thy love which is greater than theirs, and thou

[002-113]keepest me free.

[002-114]Lest I forget them they never venture to leave me alone. But day passes

[002-115]by after day and thou art not seen.

[002-116]If I call not thee in my prayers, if I keep not thee in my heart, thy

[002-117]love for me still waits for my love.

[002-118]33.

[002-119]When it was day they came into my house and said, “We shall only take

[002-120]the smallest room here.”

[002-121]They said, “We shall help you in the worship of your [[divine|God]] and humbly

[002-122]accept only our own share in his grace”; and then they took their seat

[002-123]in a corner and they sat [[silence|quiet]] and meek.

[002-124]But in the darkness of night I find they break into my [[holy|sacred]] [[sacred|shrine]],

[002-125]strong and turbulent, and snatch with unholy greed the offerings from

[002-126]God’s [[temple|altar]].

[002-127]34.

[002-128]Let only that little be left of me whereby I may name thee my all.

[002-129]Let only that little be left of my will whereby I may feel thee on

[002-130]every side, and come to thee in everything, and offer to thee my love

[002-131]every moment.

[002-132]Let only that little be left of me whereby I may never hide thee.

[002-133]Let only that little of my fetters be left whereby I am bound with thy

[002-134]will, and thy purpose is carried out in my life—and that is the fetter

[002-135]of thy love.

[002-136]35.

[002-137]Where the mind is without fear and the head is held high;

[002-138]Where knowledge is free;

[002-139]Where the world has not been broken up into fragments by narrow

[002-140]domestic walls;

[002-141]Where words come out from the depth of truth;

[002-142]Where tireless striving stretches its arms towards perfection;

[002-143]Where the clear [[river|stream]] of reason has not lost its way into the dreary

[002-144]desert sand of dead habit;

[002-145]Where the mind is led forward by thee into ever-widening thought and

[002-146]action—

[002-147]Into that heaven of freedom, my Father, let my country awake.

[002-148]36.

[002-149]This is my prayer to thee, my lord—strike, strike at the root of penury

[002-150]in my heart.

[002-151]Give me the strength lightly to bear my joys and sorrows.

[002-152]Give me the strength to make my love fruitful in service.

[002-153]Give me the strength never to disown the poor or bend my knees before

[002-154]insolent might.

[002-155]Give me the strength to raise my mind high above daily trifles.

[002-156]And give me the strength to surrender my strength to thy will with

[002-157]love.

[002-158]37.

[002-159]I thought that my voyage had come to its end at the last limit of my

[002-160]power,—that the path before me was closed, that provisions were

[002-161]exhausted and the time come to take [[nest|shelter]] in a silent obscurity.

[002-162]But I find that thy will knows no end in me. And when old words die out

[002-163]on the tongue, new melodies break forth from the heart; and where the

[002-164]old tracks are lost, new country is revealed with its wonders.

[002-165]38.

[002-166]That I want thee, only thee—let my heart repeat without end. All

[002-167]desires that distract me, day and night, are false and empty to the

[002-168]core.

[002-169]As the night keeps hidden in its gloom the [[prayer|petition]] for light, even

[002-170]thus in the depth of my unconsciousness rings the cry—I want thee, only

[002-171]thee.

[002-172]As the storm still seeks its end in peace when it strikes against peace

[002-173]with all its might, even thus my rebellion strikes against thy love and

[002-174]still its cry is—I want thee, only thee.

[002-175]39.

[002-176]When the heart is hard and parched up, come upon me with a shower of

[002-177]mercy.

[002-178]When grace is lost from life, come with a burst of song.

[002-179]When tumultuous work raises its din on all sides shutting me out from

[002-180]beyond, come to me, my lord of silence, with thy peace and [[peace|rest]].

[002-181]When my beggarly heart sits crouched, shut up in a corner, break open

[002-182]the door, my king, and come with the ceremony of a king.

[002-183]When desire blinds the mind with delusion and dust, O thou holy one,

[002-184]thou wakeful, come with thy light and thy thunder.

[002-185]40.

[002-186]The rain has held back for days and days, my God, in my arid heart. The

[002-187]horizon is fiercely naked—not the thinnest cover of a soft cloud, not

[002-188]the vaguest hint of a distant cool shower.

[002-189]Send thy angry storm, dark with death, if it is thy wish, and with

[002-190]lashes of lightning startle the sky from end to end.

[002-191]But call back, my lord, call back this pervading silent heat, still and

[002-192]keen and cruel, burning the heart with dire despair.

[002-193]Let the cloud of grace bend low from above like the tearful look of the

[002-194]mother on the day of the father’s wrath.

[002-195]41.

[002-196]Where dost thou stand behind them all, my lover, hiding thyself in the

[002-197]shadows? They push thee and pass thee by on the dusty road, taking thee

[002-198]for naught. I wait here weary hours spreading my offerings for thee,

[002-199]while passers-by come and take my flowers, one by one, and my basket is

[002-200]nearly empty.

[002-201]The morning time is past, and the noon. In the shade of evening my eyes

[002-202]are drowsy with sleep. Men going home glance at me and smile and fill

[002-203]me with shame. I sit like a beggar maid, drawing my skirt over my face,

[002-204]and when they ask me, what it is I want, I drop my eyes and answer them

[002-205]not.

[002-206]Oh, how, indeed, could I tell them that for thee I wait, and that thou

[002-207]hast promised to come. How could I utter for shame that I keep for my

[002-208]dowry this poverty. Ah, I hug this pride in the secret of my heart.
