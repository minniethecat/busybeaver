[005-001]There comes the morning with the golden basket in her right hand

[005-002]bearing the wreath of beauty, silently to crown the [[world|earth]].

[005-003]And there comes the evening over the lonely meadows deserted by herds,

[005-004]through trackless paths, carrying cool draughts of peace in her golden

[005-005]pitcher from the western [[sea|ocean]] of [[peace|rest]].

[005-006]But there, where spreads the infinite sky for the soul to take her

[005-007]flight in, reigns the stainless white radiance. There is no day nor

[005-008]night, nor form nor colour, and never, never a word.

[005-009]68.

[005-010]Thy sunbeam comes upon this earth of mine with arms outstretched and

[005-011]stands at my door the livelong day to carry back to thy feet clouds

[005-012]made of my tears and sighs and songs.

[005-013]With fond [[joy|delight]] thou wrappest about thy starry breast that mantle of

[005-014]misty cloud, turning it into numberless shapes and folds and colouring

[005-015]it with hues everchanging.

[005-016]It is so light and so fleeting, tender and tearful and dark, that is

[005-017]why thou lovest it, O thou spotless and serene. And that is why it may

[005-018]cover thy awful white light with its pathetic shadows.

[005-019]69.

[005-020]The same [[river|stream]] of life that runs through my veins night and day runs

[005-021]through the world and dances in rhythmic measures.

[005-022]It is the same life that shoots in joy through the [[earth|dust]] of the earth in

[005-023]numberless blades of grass and breaks into tumultuous waves of leaves

[005-024]and flowers.

[005-025]It is the same life that is rocked in the ocean-cradle of birth and of

[005-026]death, in ebb and in flow.

[005-027]I feel my limbs are made glorious by the touch of this world of life.

[005-028]And my [[vanity|pride]] is from the life-throb of ages dancing in my blood this

[005-029][[time|moment]].

[005-030]70.

[005-031]Is it beyond thee to be glad with the gladness of this rhythm? to be

[005-032]tossed and lost and broken in the whirl of this fearful joy?

[005-033]All things rush on, they stop not, they look not behind, no power can

[005-034]hold them back, they rush on.

[005-035]Keeping steps with that restless, rapid [[song|music]], seasons come dancing and

[005-036]pass away—colours, tunes, and perfumes pour in endless cascades in the

[005-037]abounding joy that scatters and gives up and dies every moment.

[005-038]71.

[005-039]That I should make much of myself and turn it on all sides, thus

[005-040]casting coloured shadows on thy radiance—such is thy _maya_.

[005-041]Thou settest a barrier in thine own being and then callest thy severed

[005-042][[soul|self]] in myriad notes. This thy self-separation has taken body in me.

[005-043]The poignant song is echoed through all the sky in many-coloured tears

[005-044]and smiles, alarms and hopes; waves rise up and sink again, dreams

[005-045]break and form. In me is thy own defeat of self.

[005-046]This screen that thou hast raised is painted with innumerable figures

[005-047]with the brush of the night and the day. Behind it thy seat is woven in

[005-048]wondrous mysteries of curves, casting away all barren lines of

[005-049]straightness.

[005-050]The great pageant of thee and me has overspread the sky. With the tune

[005-051]of thee and me all the air is vibrant, and all ages pass with the

[005-052]hiding and seeking of thee and me.

[005-053]72.

[005-054]He it is, the innermost one, who awakens my being with his [[ocean|deep]] hidden

[005-055]touches.

[005-056]He it is who puts his enchantment upon these eyes and joyfully plays on

[005-057]the chords of my heart in varied cadence of pleasure and pain.

[005-058]He it is who weaves the web of this _maya_ in evanescent hues of gold

[005-059]and silver, [[sky|blue]] and green, and lets peep out through the folds his

[005-060]feet, at whose touch I forget myself.

[005-061]Days come and ages pass, and it is ever he who moves my heart in many a

[005-062]name, in many a guise, in many a rapture of joy and of sorrow.

[005-063]73.

[005-064]Deliverance is not for me in renunciation. I feel the embrace of

[005-065]freedom in a thousand bonds of delight.

[005-066]Thou ever pourest for me the fresh draught of thy wine of various

[005-067]colours and fragrance, filling this earthen vessel to the brim.

[005-068]My world will light its hundred different lamps with thy flame and

[005-069]place them before the [[temple|altar]] of thy temple.

[005-070]No, I will never shut the doors of my senses. The delights of [[vision|sight]] and

[005-071]hearing and touch will bear thy delight.

[005-072]Yes, all my illusions will burn into illumination of joy, and all my

[005-073]desires ripen into fruits of love.

[005-074]74.

[005-075]The day is no more, the shadow is upon the earth. It is time that I go

[005-076]to the stream to fill my pitcher.

[005-077]The evening air is eager with the sad music of the water. Ah, it calls

[005-078]me out into the dusk. In the lonely lane there is no passer-by, the

[005-079]wind is up, the ripples are rampant in the river.

[005-080]I know not if I shall come back home. I know not whom I shall chance to

[005-081]meet. There at the fording in the little boat [[mystery|the unknown]] man plays

[005-082]upon his lute.

[005-083]75.

[005-084]Thy gifts to us mortals fulfil all our needs and yet run back to thee

[005-085]undiminished.

[005-086]The river has its everyday work to do and hastens through fields and

[005-087]hamlets; yet its incessant stream winds towards the washing of thy

[005-088]feet.

[005-089]The flower sweetens the air with its perfume; yet its last service is

[005-090]to offer itself to thee.

[005-091]Thy worship does not impoverish the world.

[005-092]From the words of the poet men take what meanings please them; yet

[005-093]their last meaning points to thee.

[005-094]76.

[005-095]Day after day, O [[king|lord]] of my life, shall I stand before thee face to

[005-096]face. With folded hands, O [[master|lord]] of all worlds, shall I stand before

[005-097]thee face to face.

[005-098]Under thy great sky in solitude and silence, with humble heart shall I

[005-099]stand before thee face to face.

[005-100]In this laborious world of thine, tumultuous with toil and with

[005-101]struggle, among hurrying crowds shall I stand before thee face to face.

[005-102]And when my work shall be done in this world, O King of kings, alone

[005-103]and speechless shall I stand before thee face to face.

[005-104]77.

[005-105]I know thee as my [[divine|God]] and stand apart—I do not know thee as my own and

[005-106]come closer. I know thee as my father and bow before thy feet—I do not

[005-107]grasp thy hand as my friend’s.

[005-108]I stand not where thou comest down and ownest thyself as mine, there to

[005-109]clasp thee to my heart and take thee as my comrade.

[005-110]Thou art the Brother amongst my brothers, but I heed them not, I divide

[005-111]not my earnings with them, thus sharing my all with thee.

[005-112]In pleasure and in pain I stand not by the side of men, and thus stand

[005-113]by thee. I shrink to give up my life, and thus do not plunge into the

[005-114]great waters of life.

[005-115]78.

[005-116]When the creation was new and all the stars shone in their first

[005-117][[beauty|splendour]], the gods held their assembly in the sky and sang “Oh, the

[005-118]picture of perfection! the joy unalloyed!”

[005-119]But one cried of a sudden—“It seems that somewhere there is a break in

[005-120]the chain of light and one of the stars has been lost.”

[005-121]The golden string of their harp snapped, their song stopped, and they

[005-122]cried in dismay—“Yes, that lost star was the best, she was the glory of

[005-123]all [[heaven|heavens]]!”

[005-124]From that day the [[seeking|search]] is unceasing for her, and the cry goes on from

[005-125]one to the other that in her the world has lost its one joy!

[005-126]Only in the deepest silence of night the stars smile and whisper among

[005-127]themselves—“Vain is this seeking! unbroken perfection is over all!”

[005-128]79.

[005-129]If it is not my portion to meet thee in this life then let me ever feel

[005-130]that I have missed thy sight—let me not forget for a moment, let me

[005-131]carry the pangs of this sorrow in my dreams and in my wakeful hours.

[005-132]As my days pass in the crowded market of this world and my hands grow

[005-133]full with the daily profits, let me ever feel that I have gained

[005-134]nothing—let me not forget for a moment, let me carry the pangs of this

[005-135]sorrow in my dreams and in my wakeful hours.

[005-136]When I sit by the roadside, tired and panting, when I spread my bed low

[005-137]in the dust, let me ever feel that the long journey is still before

[005-138]me—let me not forget a moment, let me carry the pangs of this sorrow in

[005-139]my dreams and in my wakeful hours.

[005-140]When my rooms have been decked out and the flutes sound and the

[005-141]laughter there is loud, let me ever feel that I have not invited thee

[005-142]to my [[home|house]]—let me not forget for a moment, let me carry the pangs of

[005-143]this sorrow in my dreams and in my wakeful hours.

[005-144]80.

[005-145]I am like a remnant of a cloud of autumn uselessly roaming in the sky,

[005-146]O my sun ever-glorious! Thy touch has not yet melted my vapour, making

[005-147]me one with thy light, and thus I count months and years separated from

[005-148]thee.

[005-149]If this be thy wish and if this be thy play, then take this fleeting

[005-150]emptiness of mine, paint it with colours, gild it with gold, float it

[005-151]on the wanton wind and spread it in varied wonders.

[005-152]And again when it shall be thy wish to end this play at night, I shall

[005-153]melt and vanish away in the dark, or it may be in a smile of the white

[005-154]morning, in a coolness of purity transparent.

[005-155]81.

[005-156]On many an idle day have I grieved over lost time. But it is never

[005-157]lost, my lord. Thou hast taken every moment of my life in thine own

[005-158]hands.

[005-159]Hidden in the heart of things thou art nourishing seeds into sprouts,

[005-160]buds into blossoms, and ripening flowers into fruitfulness.

[005-161]I was tired and sleeping on my idle bed and imagined all work had

[005-162]ceased. In the morning I woke up and found my garden full with wonders

[005-163]of flowers.

[005-164]82.

[005-165]Time is endless in thy hands, my lord. There is none to count thy

[005-166]minutes.

[005-167]Days and nights pass and ages bloom and fade like flowers. Thou knowest

[005-168]how to wait.

[005-169]Thy centuries follow each other perfecting a small wild flower.

[005-170]We have no time to lose, and having no time we must scramble for a

[005-171]chances. We are too poor to be late.

[005-172]And thus it is that time goes by while I give it to every querulous man

[005-173]who claims it, and thine altar is empty of all offerings to the last.

[005-174]At the end of the day I hasten in fear lest thy gate to be shut; but I

[005-175]find that yet there is time.

[005-176]83.

[005-177][[mother|Mother]], I shall weave a chain of pearls for thy neck with my tears of

[005-178]sorrow.

[005-179]The stars have wrought their anklets of light to deck thy feet, but

[005-180]mine will hang upon thy breast.

[005-181]Wealth and fame come from thee and it is for thee to give or to

[005-182]withhold them. But this my sorrow is absolutely mine own, and when I

[005-183]bring it to thee as my offering thou rewardest me with thy grace.

[005-184]84.

[005-185]It is the pang of separation that spreads throughout the world and

[005-186]gives birth to shapes innumerable in the infinite sky.

[005-187]It is this sorrow of separation that gazes in silence all nights from

[005-188]star to star and becomes lyric among rustling leaves in rainy [[night|darkness]]

[005-189]of July.

[005-190]It is this overspreading pain that deepens into loves and desires, into

[005-191]sufferings and joy in human homes; and this it is that ever melts and

[005-192]flows in songs through my poet’s heart.

[005-193]85.

[005-194]When the warriors came out first from their master’s hall, where had

[005-195]they hid their power? Where were their armour and their arms?

[005-196]They looked poor and helpless, and the arrows were showered upon them

[005-197]on the day they came out from their master’s hall.
