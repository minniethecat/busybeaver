# Hope

**Hope** in [[rabindranath-tagore]]'s *[[gitanjali-song-offerings]]* is the soul's forward-leaning posture — the expectation of meeting, the anticipation of divine presence. It is not easy optimism but a fragile and persistent force, capable of surviving even when circumstances argue against it. Hope is what keeps the soul moving when the goal is invisible and the path is dark.

## In Gitanjali

### Living in Hope

The poet defines his existence as suspended in hope:

> I live in the hope of meeting with him; but this... (001-136)

"I live in the hope of meeting with him" — hope is not an occasional feeling but the very element in which the poet lives. Life *is* hope; to stop hoping would be to stop living. "But this..." — the sentence breaks off, suggesting that hope is always incomplete, always waiting, never yet fulfilled. Hope is defined by what it lacks: the meeting it anticipates has not yet happened.

### Hope as Exclusive Orientation

Hope narrows the soul's field of vision to a single point:

> ...ay it is only for thee that I hope. (001-180)

"Only for thee that I hope" — hope is exclusive, focused, undivided. The poet does not hope for many things; he hopes for one thing. This is hope purified of distraction, hope as the total orientation of a life. Everything else is secondary; all other hopes are subsets of this one hope.

### The Paradox of Hoping for Freedom

Hope and [[freedom]] enter into tension:

> Freedom is all I want, but to hope for it I feel ashamed. (002-072)

"To hope for it I feel ashamed" — hoping itself becomes shameful. Why? Because hope implies lack, implies waiting, implies dependence. To hope for freedom is already to acknowledge that one is not free. The paradox is sharp: the posture of hoping contradicts the thing hoped for. Yet hope persists — ashamed but undeterred.

### Hopes That Rise and Fall

Hope is not steady; it rises and collapses like a wave:

> My hopes rose high and methought my e... (003-118)

"Hopes rose high" — the upward surge of optimism, the sudden conviction that fulfillment is near. "Methought" — but it was only thought, not fact. The hopes that rise also fall; the pattern is tidal. Hope is not a constant state but a rhythm, a wave that must learn to rise again after every crash.

### Desperate Hope

The most striking image is of hope pushed to its limit:

> In desperate hope I go and search for her in al... (006-017)

"Desperate hope" — the phrase is almost a contradiction. Desperation suggests the absence of hope; yet here hope persists, though desperate. This is hope at the edge of hopelessness, hope that has exhausted every reason for hope and yet continues. It "goes and searches" — it is active, not passive. Desperate hope is still hope, perhaps the truest kind.

## Philosophical Background

- **Asha in Indian thought**: *Asha* (hope) is not one of the cardinal virtues in classical Indian philosophy, which tends to emphasize detachment over expectation. Tagore's valuation of hope represents a distinctive departure — a more this-worldly, engaged spirituality.
- **Hope as theological virtue**: In Christian theology, hope is one of the three theological virtues (with faith and love). Tagore's hope shares this structure — it is directed toward God, sustains the soul through darkness, and is never fully satisfied in this life.
- **Ernst Bloch's philosophy of hope**: Tagore's "desperate hope" resonates with Bloch's concept of hope as a principle that persists even in the face of apparent impossibility — hope is not about probability but about possibility.

## Poetic Expression

| Strategy | Example | Effect |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Existential framing | "I live in the hope of meeting with him" | Hope as the soul's native element |
| Exclusive focus | "only for thee that I hope" | Hope purified to a single object |
| Shame paradox | "to hope for it I feel ashamed" | The contradiction between hoping and having |
| Wave rhythm | "hopes rose high" | Hope as rising and falling, tidal |
| Desperation intensifier | "desperate hope" | Hope at the edge, hope purified by extremity |
