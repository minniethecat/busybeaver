[004-001]From now I leave off all petty decorations. Lord of my heart, no more

[004-002]shall there be for me waiting and weeping in corners, no more coyness

[004-003]and sweetness of demeanour. Thou hast given me thy sword for adornment.

[004-004]No more doll’s decorations for me!

[004-005]53.

[004-006]Beautiful is thy wristlet, decked with stars and cunningly wrought in

[004-007]myriad-coloured jewels. But more beautiful to me thy sword with its

[004-008]curve of lightning like the outspread wings of the [[holy|divine]] bird of

[004-009]Vishnu, perfectly poised in the angry red light of the sunset.

[004-010]It quivers like the one last response of life in ecstasy of pain at the

[004-011]final stroke of death; it shines like the pure flame of being burning

[004-012]up earthly sense with one fierce flash.

[004-013]Beautiful is thy wristlet, decked with starry gems; but thy sword, O

[004-014][[king|lord]] of thunder, is wrought with uttermost beauty, terrible to behold

[004-015]or think of.

[004-016]54.

[004-017]I asked nothing from thee; I uttered not my name to thine ear. When

[004-018]thou took’st thy leave I stood silent. I was alone by the well where

[004-019]the shadow of the tree fell aslant, and the women had gone home with

[004-020]their brown earthen pitchers full to the brim. They called me and

[004-021]shouted, “Come with us, the morning is wearing on to noon.” But I

[004-022]languidly lingered awhile lost in the midst of vague musings.

[004-023]I heard not thy steps as thou camest. Thine eyes were sad when they

[004-024]fell on me; thy voice was tired as thou spokest low—“Ah, I am a thirsty

[004-025]traveller.” I started up from my day-dreams and poured water from my

[004-026]jar on thy joined palms. The leaves rustled overhead; the cuckoo sang

[004-027]from the unseen dark, and perfume of _babla_ flowers came from the bend

[004-028]of the [[path|road]].

[004-029]I stood speechless with shame when my name thou didst ask. Indeed, what

[004-030]had I done for thee to keep me in [[memory|remembrance]]? But the memory that I

[004-031]could give water to thee to allay thy [[longing|thirst]] will cling to my heart and

[004-032]enfold it in sweetness. The morning [[time|hour]] is late, the bird sings in

[004-033]weary notes, _neem_ leaves rustle overhead and I sit and think and

[004-034]think.

[004-035]55.

[004-036]Languor is upon your heart and the slumber is still on your eyes.

[004-037]Has not the word come to you that the flower is reigning in [[beauty|splendour]]

[004-038]among thorns? Wake, oh awaken! let not the time pass in vain!

[004-039]At the end of the stony [[journey|path]], in the country of virgin solitude, my

[004-040]friend is sitting all alone. Deceive him not. Wake, oh awaken!

[004-041]What if the sky pants and trembles with the heat of the midday sun—what

[004-042]if the burning sand spreads its mantle of thirst—

[004-043]Is there no joy in the [[ocean|deep]] of your heart? At every footfall of yours,

[004-044]will not the harp of the road break out in sweet [[song|music]] of pain?

[004-045]56.

[004-046]Thus it is that thy joy in me is so full. Thus it is that thou hast

[004-047]come down to me. O thou [[master|lord]] of all [[heaven|heavens]], where would be thy love if

[004-048]I were not?

[004-049]Thou hast taken me as thy partner of all this wealth. In my heart is

[004-050]the endless play of thy [[joy|delight]]. In my life thy will is ever taking

[004-051]shape.

[004-052]And for this, thou who art the King of kings hast decked thyself in

[004-053]beauty to captivate my heart. And for this thy love loses itself in the

[004-054]love of thy lover, and there art thou seen in the perfect union of two.

[004-055]57.

[004-056][[light|Light]], my light, the world-filling light, the eye-kissing light,

[004-057]heart-sweetening light!

[004-058]Ah, the light dances, my darling, at the centre of my life; the light

[004-059]strikes, my darling, the chords of my love; the sky opens, the wind

[004-060]runs wild, laughter passes over the [[world|earth]].

[004-061]The butterflies spread their sails on the sea of light. Lilies and

[004-062]jasmines surge up on the crest of the waves of light.

[004-063]The light is shattered into gold on every cloud, my darling, and it

[004-064]scatters gems in profusion.

[004-065]Mirth spreads from leaf to leaf, my darling, and gladness without

[004-066]measure. The heaven’s river has drowned its banks and the flood of joy

[004-067]is abroad.

[004-068]58.

[004-069]Let all the strains of joy mingle in my last song—the joy that makes

[004-070]the earth flow over in the riotous excess of the grass, the joy that

[004-071]sets the twin brothers, life and death, dancing over the wide world,

[004-072]the joy that sweeps in with the tempest, shaking and waking all life

[004-073]with laughter, the joy that sits still with its tears on the open red

[004-074]lotus of pain, and the joy that throws everything it has upon the [[earth|dust]],

[004-075]and knows not a word.

[004-076]59.

[004-077]Yes, I know, this is nothing but thy love, O [[bride|beloved]] of my heart— this

[004-078]golden light that dances upon the leaves, these idle clouds sailing

[004-079]across the sky, this passing breeze leaving its coolness upon my

[004-080]forehead.

[004-081]The morning light has flooded my eyes—this is thy message to my heart.

[004-082]Thy face is bent from above, thy eyes look down on my eyes, and my

[004-083]heart has touched thy feet.

[004-084]60.

[004-085]On the [[shore|seashore]] of endless worlds [[child|children]] meet. The infinite sky is

[004-086]motionless overhead and the restless water is boisterous. On the

[004-087]seashore of endless worlds the children meet with shouts and dances.

[004-088]They build their houses with sand and they play with empty shells. With

[004-089]withered leaves they weave their boats and smilingly float them on the

[004-090]vast [[sea|deep]]. Children have their play on the seashore of worlds.

[004-091]They know not how to swim, they know not how to cast nets. Pearl

[004-092]fishers dive for pearls, merchants sail in their ships, while children

[004-093]gather pebbles and scatter them again. They seek not for hidden

[004-094]treasures, they know not how to cast nets.

[004-095]The sea surges up with laughter and pale gleams the smile of the sea

[004-096]beach. [[death|Death]]-dealing waves sing meaningless ballads to the children,

[004-097]even like a mother while rocking her baby’s cradle. The sea plays with

[004-098]children, and pale gleams the smile of the sea beach.

[004-099]On the seashore of endless worlds children meet. Tempest roams in the

[004-100]pathless sky, ships get wrecked in the trackless water, death is abroad

[004-101]and children play. On the seashore of endless worlds is the great

[004-102]meeting of children.

[004-103]61.

[004-104]The sleep that flits on baby’s eyes—does anybody know from where it

[004-105]comes? Yes, there is a rumour that it has its [[home|dwelling]] there, in the

[004-106]fairy village among shadows of the forest dimly lit with glow-worms,

[004-107]there hang two timid buds of enchantment. From there it comes to kiss

[004-108]baby’s eyes.

[004-109]The smile that flickers on baby’s lips when he sleeps—does anybody know

[004-110]where it was born? Yes, there is a rumour that a young pale beam of a

[004-111]crescent moon touched the edge of a vanishing autumn cloud, and there

[004-112]the smile was first born in the dream of a dew-washed morning—the smile

[004-113]that flickers on baby’s lips when he sleeps.

[004-114]The sweet, soft freshness that blooms on baby’s limbs—does anybody know

[004-115]where it was hidden so long? Yes, when the mother was a young girl it

[004-116]lay pervading her heart in tender and silent [[wonder|mystery]] of love—the sweet,

[004-117]soft freshness that has bloomed on baby’s limbs.

[004-118]62.

[004-119]When I bring to you coloured toys, my child, I understand why there is

[004-120]such a play of colours on clouds, on water, and why flowers are painted

[004-121]in tints—when I give coloured toys to you, my child.

[004-122]When I sing to make you dance I truly now why there is music in leaves,

[004-123]and why waves send their chorus of voices to the heart of the listening

[004-124]earth—when I sing to make you dance.

[004-125]When I bring sweet things to your greedy hands I know why there is

[004-126]honey in the cup of the flowers and why fruits are secretly filled with

[004-127]sweet juice—when I bring sweet things to your greedy hands.

[004-128]When I kiss your face to make you smile, my darling, I surely

[004-129]understand what pleasure streams from the sky in morning light, and

[004-130]what delight that is that is which the summer breeze brings to my

[004-131]body—when I kiss you to make you smile.

[004-132]63.

[004-133]Thou hast made me known to friends whom I knew not. Thou hast given me

[004-134]seats in homes not my own. Thou hast brought the distant near and made

[004-135]a brother of the stranger.

[004-136]I am uneasy at heart when I have to leave my accustomed [[nest|shelter]]; I

[004-137]forget that there abides the old in the new, and that there also thou

[004-138]abidest.

[004-139]Through birth and death, in this world or in others, wherever thou

[004-140]leadest me it is thou, the same, the one [[friend|companion]] of my endless life

[004-141]who ever linkest my heart with bonds of joy to the unfamiliar.

[004-142]When one knows thee, then alien there is none, then no door is shut.

[004-143]Oh, grant me my prayer that I may never lose the bliss of the touch of

[004-144]the one in the play of many.

[004-145]64.

[004-146]On the slope of the desolate river among tall grasses I asked her,

[004-147]“Maiden, where do you go shading your lamp with your mantle? My house

[004-148]is all dark and lonesome—lend me your light!” she raised her dark eyes

[004-149]for a moment and looked at my face through the dusk. “I have come to

[004-150]the river,” she said, “to float my lamp on the [[river|stream]] when the daylight

[004-151]wanes in the west.” I stood alone among tall grasses and watched the

[004-152]timid flame of her lamp uselessly drifting in the tide.

[004-153]In the silence of gathering night I asked her, “Maiden, your lights are

[004-154]all lit—then where do you go with your lamp? My house is all dark and

[004-155]lonesome—lend me your light.” She raised her dark eyes on my face and

[004-156]stood for a moment doubtful. “I have come,” she said at last, “to

[004-157]dedicate my lamp to the sky.” I stood and watched her light uselessly

[004-158]burning in the void.

[004-159]In the moonless gloom of [[night|midnight]] I ask her, “Maiden, what is your

[004-160][[seeking|quest]], holding the lamp near your heart? My house is all dark and

[004-161]lonesome—lend me your light.” She stopped for a minute and thought and

[004-162]gazed at my face in the dark. “I have brought my light,” she said, “to

[004-163]join the carnival of lamps.” I stood and watched her little lamp

[004-164]uselessly lost among lights.

[004-165]65.

[004-166]What divine drink wouldst thou have, my [[divine|God]], from this overflowing cup

[004-167]of my life?

[004-168]My poet, is it thy delight to see thy creation through my eyes and to

[004-169]stand at the portals of my ears silently to listen to thine own eternal

[004-170]harmony?

[004-171]Thy world is weaving words in my mind and thy joy is adding music to

[004-172]them. Thou givest thyself to me in love and then feelest thine own

[004-173]entire sweetness in me.

[004-174]66.

[004-175]She who ever had remained in the depth of my being, in the twilight of

[004-176]gleams and of glimpses; she who never opened her veils in the morning

[004-177]light, will be my last [[offering|gift]] to thee, my God, folded in my final song.

[004-178]Words have wooed yet failed to win her; persuasion has stretched to her

[004-179]its eager arms in vain.

[004-180]I have roamed from country to country keeping her in the core of my

[004-181]heart, and around her have risen and fallen the growth and decay of my

[004-182]life.

[004-183]Over my thoughts and actions, my slumbers and dreams, she reigned yet

[004-184]dwelled alone and apart.

[004-185]Many a man knocked at my door and asked for her and turned away in

[004-186]despair.

[004-187]There was none in the world who ever saw her face to face, and she

[004-188]remained in her loneliness waiting for thy recognition.

[004-189]67.

[004-190]Thou art the sky and thou art the nest as well.

[004-191]O thou beautiful, there in the nest is thy love that encloses the soul

[004-192]with colours and sounds and odours.
