# St Paul's Crescent

St Paul's Crescent is the [[london|London]] residence of [[maud-milvain|Maud]] and [[dora-milvain|Dora Milvain]], [[jasper-milvain|Jasper Milvain's]] sisters. The address represents the respectable but constrained circumstances of the [[jasper-milvain|Milvain]] sisters, and serves as a recurring setting for social calls and domestic scenes throughout the novel.

## 位置与特征

The Crescent consists of "small, decent" houses (014-079), indicating a neighborhood of modest respectability rather than wealth or [[literary-poverty|poverty]]. It is distant enough from central [[london|London]] that visits involve planning and travel time (048-118), yet close enough to participate in literary society.

## 小说中的出场

St Paul's Crescent is where the [[jasper-milvain|Milvain]] sisters receive visitors. [[marian-yule|Marian Yule]] visits (029-032); [[jasper-milvain|Jasper]] uses it as a base between his [[london|London]] engagements. [[alfred-yule|Mr Yule]] pays a Sunday visit (029-032). The Crescent is also where crucial social arrangements are made — "It was arranged that he should..." (058-179) — linking domestic space to narrative decision-making.

## 叙事功能

As a domestic space occupied by unmarried women of limited means, St Paul's Crescent embodies the restricted sphere of the respectable but unconnected woman in Victorian literary society. The sisters' home is a waystation between the rural [[finden|Finden]] and [[jasper-milvain|Jasper]]'s ambitious [[london|London]] life. Social calls at the Crescent drive minor plot mechanics and provide occasions for character revelation.

## 象征意义

The Crescent represents the gendered geography of literary [[london|London]]: men move freely through public spaces (clubs, the [[british-museum|British Museum]], newspapers offices), while the [[jasper-milvain|Milvain]] sisters wait at home to be visited. Their address is both a connection to literary society and a marker of their exclusion from its full participation.
