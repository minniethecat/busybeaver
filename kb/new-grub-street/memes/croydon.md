# Croydon

Croydon is a town south of [[london|London]] that figures in the novel as the location of a home for destitute boys, offering a secretaryship — a position of secure, non-literary employment that becomes a recurring plot device. For characters caught in the uncertainties of the [[grub-street|literary marketplace]], Croydon represents both a practical escape and an admission of failure.

## 位置与特征

Croydon is situated outside [[london|London]], accessible by rail. The novel describes it as not "the most inviting" of places (060-035) — a provincial town lacking the cultural life of the capital. The home for destitute boys is the sole institution associated with Croydon in the narrative.

## 小说中的出场

The Croydon secretaryship is introduced as a potential position for a character struggling in [[london|London]] (056-175). The post is "far from a sinecure" (060-015) but offers the security that literary work cannot. The question "'What do you propose to do in case I go to Croydon?'" (061-001) reveals how the position implicates not just the office-holder but his dependents. The commitment to "come to Croydon if you wish" (061-152) represents a decision point for multiple characters. The recurring phrase "at Croydon" (064-064, 065-035, 066-059, 067-023, 067-060, 077-021, 077-091) threads through several chapters as characters deliberate, commit, and adjust to the prospect.

## 叙事功能

The Croydon secretaryship functions as a narrative alternative to literary [[london|London]]. It offers the possibility of honorable escape from the grinding uncertainties of authorship. Characters who accept Croydon choose security over artistic [[literary-ambition|ambition]]; those who refuse it recommit to the literary gamble. The position thus serves as a moral and practical test.

## 象征意义

Croydon represents the world of regular employment that stands outside and beneath the literary vocation. To go to Croydon is to admit that literature cannot support a life — a confession that the novel's characters resist, negotiate, and ultimately must face. The town's uninviting character underscores the sacrifice involved in choosing security over art.
