# Hampstead

Hampstead is a district in north [[london|London]], notable in the novel for its elevated position offering "views" across [[london|London]] and for Hampstead Road, a thoroughfare that connects several characters' residences and movements. It is associated particularly with [[harold-biffen|Harold Biffen]] and with the novel's mapping of north [[london|London]] literary geography.

## 位置与特征

Hampstead occupies the high ground of north [[london|London]]. "The green ridge from Hampstead to Highgate, with..." (007-076) offers panoramic views — a rare moment of natural beauty in a novel otherwise dominated by interior rooms and urban streets. Hampstead Road serves as a commercial and residential artery (019-004, 031-025, 031-047), with furniture shops and modest lodgings lining its length.

## 小说中的出场

Hampstead appears in connection with several characters' movements through north [[london|London]]. The junction of roads "which lead to Hampstead, to Highgate, and..." (031-142) marks a key geographical node. [[amy-reardon|Amy Reardon]] spends "a couple of hours in the Hampstead region" (038-118), using the elevated district as a recreational escape. [[harold-biffen|Biffen]] lives near Hampstead Road, at "a little street off Hampstead Road, some distance from..." (075-070).

## 叙事功能

Hampstead's elevated position makes it a space of temporary removal from the grind of literary [[london|London]] below. Characters go to Hampstead to walk, to think, to escape — but they always return. The district marks the northern boundary of the novel's [[london|London]] geography, beyond which lies the suburban and the rural.

## 象征意义

The green ridge of Hampstead offers one of the novel's few glimpses of natural beauty within [[london|London]], functioning as a visual and symbolic counterpoint to the enclosed spaces where literary labor occurs. Yet Hampstead is never a true escape — it is still [[london|London]], still within the gravitational field of the [[grub-street|literary marketplace]].
