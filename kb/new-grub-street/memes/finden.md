# Finden

Finden is a rural village near [[wattleborough|Wattleborough]] where the [[jasper-milvain|Milvain]] family home is located. It serves as the pastoral counterpoint to literary [[london|London]] — a place of family ties, childhood memory, and the social world that characters have left behind or must return to.

## 位置与特征

Finden stands in rural contrast to the [[london|London]] settings that dominate the novel. It is "remote from [[wattleborough|Wattleborough]]" (002-041), a village with its own church (001-009) and an early postal schedule (003-185). The lanes around Finden provide the setting for moments of pastoral reflection absent from the urban chapters.

## 小说中的出场

Finden appears throughout the novel as the [[jasper-milvain|Milvain]] family home. [[jasper-milvain|Jasper Milvain]] visits between his [[london|London]] engagements; his sisters [[maud-milvain|Maud]] and [[dora-milvain|Dora]] reside there. [[marian-yule|Marian Yule]] first encounters the [[jasper-milvain|Milvain]]s at Finden (070-097), a meeting [[jasper-milvain|Jasper]] recalls as "a very unusual and agreeable thing" (070-103). After [[jasper-milvain|Jasper]]'s departure, "his sisters had seen [[marian-yule|Marian]] several times" (014-186), establishing the social connections that drive later plot developments.

## 叙事功能

Finden functions as the novel's rural retreat — a place characters leave to pursue [[literary-ambition|literary ambition]]s in [[london|London]] and return to when those [[literary-ambition|ambition]]s fail or pause. The question "Would it be a little better down at Finden?" (018-129) recurs as a motif of retreat from the pressures of literary life. [[jasper-milvain|Jasper]]'s periodic returns to Finden mark the rhythm of his [[london|London]] career.

## 象征意义

Finden represents the world of inherited [[social-class|social position]] and family obligation that stands in tension with the individualistic pursuit of [[literary-success|literary success]]. The village's rural simplicity contrasts with [[london|London]]'s commercial complexity, but Finden is not idealized — it is a place of limited "opportunities every way" (019-185) compared to [[london|London]].
