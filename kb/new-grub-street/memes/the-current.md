# The Current

*The Current* is one of the principal periodicals in the competitive landscape of [[grub-street]], edited by [[fadge|Clement Fadge]] and representing the commercial end of literary [[journalism-trade|journalism]]. It is the venue where [[jasper-milvain]] places his early work, where [[literary-success|reputation]]s are made and broken, and where the novel's characters measure their professional standing by whether they appear in its pages.

> "Why, they propose The Current. Not bad, in a way" (012-088), a character remarks, and the guarded approval captures the periodical's position — respectable but not prestigious, commercial but not vulgar.

## Role in the Literary Marketplace

*The Current* occupies a specific niche in the novel's ecology of periodicals. It sits between the fashionable society papers like [[chit-chat]] and the more serious literary reviews. For [[jasper-milvain]], placing work in *The Current* is a stepping-stone — a credential that opens doors to better-paying venues. The periodical is both a destination and a way station, and the characters' relationships to it trace their career trajectories.
