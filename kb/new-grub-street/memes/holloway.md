# Holloway

Holloway is a district in north [[london|London]] where [[edwin-reardon|Edwin Reardon]] and his wife [[amy-reardon|Amy]] reside during a period of financial strain. The phrase "going to Holloway" (014-135) takes on idiomatic weight in the novel, signifying the [[edwin-reardon|Reardon]]s' descent from [[social-class|middle-class]] comfort into impoverished domesticity.

## 位置与特征

Holloway lies in north [[london|London]], on Holloway Road (049-138). The district is characterized in the novel primarily through the [[edwin-reardon|Reardon]]s' experience of it — as a place of reduced circumstances, rented rooms, and the daily friction of [[literary-poverty|poverty]]. The name "Holloway" is spoken with a particular tonal weight by other characters.

## 小说中的出场

Holloway is introduced through the [[edwin-reardon|Reardon]]s' move there. "'Yes; I went to Holloway'" (014-134) is spoken by a character visiting [[amy-reardon|Amy]], and the narrator immediately glosses: "By 'going to Holloway' was..." (014-135) — establishing the phrase as code for the [[edwin-reardon|Reardon]]s' downward mobility. Other characters refer to "Those Holloway people" (016-152) with dismissive condescension. The [[edwin-reardon|Reardon]]s' address on Holloway Road (049-138) marks a specific location for their domestic life.

## 叙事功能

Holloway serves as a geographical marker of the [[edwin-reardon|Reardon]]s' declining fortunes. Each mention of the district carries economic and social weight. The move to Holloway is not merely a change of address but a visible sign of [[edwin-reardon|Reardon]]'s failure to support his family through literary work — a failure that drives the novel's central marital crisis.

## 象征意义

In the novel's geography of [[social-class|class]], Holloway occupies the zone between respectability and [[literary-poverty|poverty]]. It is not the depths of [[grub-street]] or the slums, but it represents a step below the comfort the [[edwin-reardon|Reardon]]s once enjoyed. The euphemistic use of "going to Holloway" demonstrates how place-names become coded with social meaning.
