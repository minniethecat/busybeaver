## Definition

In [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s *On the [[genealogy|Genealogy]] of Morality*, **religion** is analyzed not as a system of belief but as a system of [[cruelty]]. "All religions are really at bottom systems of cruelties" (019-001). Religion, for [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]], is the historical mechanism through which humanity's innate [[cruelty]] was first externalized in [[sacrifice|ritual sacrifice]] and then internalized as [[Bad Conscience|bad conscience]].

Religion is not opposed to [[cruelty]]—it is [[cruelty]]'s greatest organizer and sublimator.

## Nietzsche's Analysis

### Religion as Cruelty System

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s most radical claim about religion is that it is fundamentally a system for organizing and giving meaning to [[cruelty]]. The religious cults, with their sacrifices, penances, and mortifications, are "systems of cruelties" (019-001). What appears as piety is, at bottom, the satisfaction of the human instinct for inflicting and witnessing suffering.

The genius of religion is that it transforms [[cruelty]] from a raw instinct into a sacred [[obligation|duty]]—and in doing so, makes suffering bearable by giving it meaning.

### The Internalization of Religious Cruelty

With the rise of the [[Ascetic Priest|ascetic priest]], religion's [[cruelty]] is turned inward. "The impossibility of paying the debt" produces "the idea of an irredeemable debt" (037-001)—the concept of eternal [[guilt]] before God.

This is the birth of the Christian [[bad-conscience|bad conscience]]: [[cruelty]] that once flowed outward in ritual and war is now directed against the self. The believer becomes both torturer and tortured, and calls this condition "sin."

### Religion and the Meaning of Suffering

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] identifies the crucial function of religion: it gives meaning to suffering. "All religions are mainly concerned with fighting a certain weariness and heaviness that has invaded the soul" (058-001 area). The religious interpretation of suffering—as [[punishment]], as test, as purification—makes suffering bearable by making it intelligible.

This is the deepest service of the [[Ascetic Priest]]: he provides an answer to the question "Why do I suffer?" And any answer, [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] suggests, is better than no answer at all.

### Religion as Symptom

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s [[genealogy|genealogical method]] treats religion not as revelation but as symptom. The religious valuation of the world expresses the [[health|physiological]] condition of the religious type. "In sacerdotal societies every element is on a more dangerous scale... pride, revenge, cunning, exaltation, love, ambition, virtue, morbidity" (006-001).

Religion reveals the health or [[disease|sickness]] of those who create and sustain it. The triumph of [[Christianity]] is, for [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]], not a proof of its truth but a symptom of the decadence of the ancient world.

## Key Passages

> How much blood and cruelty is the foundation of all "good things"! (019-001)

> All religions are really at bottom systems of cruelties... without blood, tortures, and sacrifice; the most dreadful sacrifices and pledges (to which belongs the sacrifice of the first-born), the most loathsome mutilations (for instance, castration), the cruellest ritual forms of all religious cults. (019-001)

> Primarily against the "ower," in whom the bad conscience now establishes itself... with the impossibility of paying the debt, there becomes conceived the idea of an irredeemable debt, the idea that it cannot be paid off—the idea of "eternal punishment." (037-001)

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s analysis of religion culminates in the recognition that the ascetic [[ideals|ideal]], embodied in the religious valuation of existence, is "the most widespread and longest of all human errors." But it is not merely an error—it is a strategy for survival, a way of making existence bearable when it would otherwise be intolerable.
