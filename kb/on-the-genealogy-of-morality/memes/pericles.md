## Overview

Pericles (c. 495–429 BCE) was an Athenian statesman, orator, and general during the Golden Age of Athens.

In *On the [[genealogy|Genealogy]] of Morality*, [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] invokes Pericles' funeral oration as a primary exhibit in his analysis of [[master-morality|aristocratic morality]].

## Role in the Genealogy

Pericles appears in Essay 1, §11, where [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] elaborates his theory of the "blonde brute" — the beast of prey at the core of all [[noble|aristocratic]] races.

The Athenian leader's words serve as an authentic voice of [[noble|aristocratic]] self-consciousness, untainted by the [[ressentiment|resentment]] that [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] argues would later invert all values (010-004).

## Nietzsche's Engagement

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] deploys Pericles' own words: "Our audacity has forced a way over every land and sea, rearing everywhere imperishable memorials of itself for *good* and for *evil*" (010-004).

For [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]], this reveals [[noble|aristocratic]] pride in monuments of power, with no distinction between memorials "for good" and "for evil." The [[noble|aristocratic]] conscience operates beyond the binary [[slave-morality|slave morality]] would later impose.

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] further highlights Pericles' emphasis on ῥᾳθυμία (*rhathymia*) — the Athenians' "nonchalance and contempt for safety, body, life, and comfort" (010-004).

From the perspective of [[slave-morality|slave morality]], this catalogue of [[noble|aristocratic]] [[virtue|virtues]] becomes a catalogue of vices — and that reversal is [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s central point.

## Key Passages

> ...when Pericles says to his Athenians in that celebrated funeral oration, "Our audacity has forced a way over every land and sea, rearing everywhere imperishable memorials of itself for *good* and for *evil*." (010-004)

> Pericles sets in special relief and glory the ῥᾳθυμία of the Athenians, their nonchalance and contempt for safety, body, life, and comfort, their awful joy and intense delight in all destruction... (010-004)
