# Europe

## Overview

In [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s *[[genealogy|Genealogy]] of Morality*, **Europe** is not merely a geographical designation but the symbolic stage of Western moral history. The entire drama [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] narrates — the rise of [[noble|aristocratic]] values, the [[Jews|Jewish]] revaluation, the triumph of [[christianity|Christianity]], and the emerging crisis of nihilism — takes place within and through European civilization. Europe is the patient whose "[[disease|sickness]]" [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] diagnoses.

> I realised that a sinister symptom of our modern European civilisation was in the course of being discussed. (P07-001)

## Europe as Moral Palimpsest

### The Layered History of Values

Europe, for [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]], is a palimpsest — a surface on which successive value systems have been written over one another. The [[noble|aristocratic]] values of [[Greece]] and [[Rome]] lie beneath the Christian values of medieval Europe, which in turn lie beneath the secular-democratic values of modernity. Each layer does not simply replace the previous one but transforms and incorporates it.

> The subject-race has ended by coming to the top, and in point of fact, with its own particular ideas and values, has become the master of substantially the whole of Europe. (005-001, paraphrased)

### The French Aristocracy as Last Expression

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] identifies the French [[aristocracy]] of the seventeenth century as the last genuine expression of [[noble|aristocratic]] values in European history:

> The last political aristocracy that existed in Europe, that of the *French* seventeenth and eighteenth centuries, collapsed under the popular instincts of ressentiment. (015-002)

## Europe's Modern Condition

### The Dwarfing of Man

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] sees modern Europe as characterized by a "dwarfing and levelling" of the human type. Democratic values, which [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] regards as the secularized expression of Christian [[slave-morality|slave morality]], produce a uniform, mediocre humanity — comfortable but without greatness:

> The dwarfing and levelling of the European man lurks *our* greatest peril, for the sight of him makes us *weary*. (011-001)

### Nihilism as European Destiny

The "death of God" — the collapse of the Christian framework that gave European life its meaning for two millennia — is for [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] the defining event of modern Europe. The [[Ascetic Ideal]], which provided the interpretive structure for European civilization, has exhausted itself, leaving behind the specter of meaninglessness:

> For two thousand years Europe has been past masters — these masters of the ascetic ideal. (039-001, paraphrased)
