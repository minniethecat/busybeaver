# Ascetic Ideal

## Definition

The **ascetic [[ideals|ideal]]** (*asketische Ideal*) is the most encompassing concept in [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s *On the [[genealogy|Genealogy]] of Morality* — the interpretative framework through which human beings have historically assigned meaning to suffering by denying life, the body, and the senses. The entire Third Essay is structured around the question: "What is the meaning of ascetic ideals?" [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s answer is that the ascetic [[ideals|ideal]] has been the primary means by which the human will — which fundamentally *wills nothingness* rather than not will at all — has preserved itself against the threat of meaninglessness.

> But in the very fact that the ascetic ideal has meant so much to man, lies the fundamental fact of the human will, its *horror vacui*: *it needs an aim* — and it would rather will *nothingness* than not will at all. (042-002)

## Nietzsche's Analysis

### The Ascetic Ideal Across Domains

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] systematically examines how the ascetic [[ideals|ideal]] manifests in different spheres of human activity:

| Domain | Meaning of Ascetic Ideal | Key Figure |
|--------|--------------------------|------------|
| Art | A temporary escape from the self; [[artist|art]] as narcotic | [[Richard Wagner]] |
| Philosophy | Detachment from the senses; the will to objectivity | [[Arthur Schopenhauer]] |
| [[religion|Religion]] | The most complete expression; life-denial as salvation | The [[ascetic-priest|ascetic priest]] |
| [[science|Science]] | The latest form; "truth at any price" as secularized asceticism | Modern scholars |

### The Philosopher and the Ascetic Ideal

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] argues that philosophy has always been dependent on the ascetic [[ideals|ideal]]. The philosopher's pose of detachment, objectivity, and contempt for the body is itself an expression of ascetic values. Philosophy did not develop *against* the ascetic [[ideals|ideal]] but *within* it — using the ascetic mask as a condition of survival and legitimacy.

> What, then, is the meaning of ascetic ideals? In the case of an artist we are getting to understand their *nothingness*, or rather the *multiplicity* of their nothingness. In the case of philosophers and scholars, the ascetic ideal has, as it were, a *preliminary* purpose. (046-001)

### The Ascetic Priest: Exploiting Suffering

The [[ascetic-priest|ascetic priest]] is [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s central figure in the Third Essay — the one who does not cure suffering but *redirects* it. The [[priests|priest]] transforms the sick person's [[ressentiment]] outward (against "sinners," "unbelievers") or inward (as "sin," "[[bad-conscience|bad conscience]]"), thereby preserving the herd while deepening its [[disease|sickness]].

> The ascetic priest must be accepted by us as the predestined saviour, herdsman, and champion of the sick herd: only so can we understand his immense historic mission. (052-001)

### The Will to Nothingness

The ascetic [[ideals|ideal]] reveals the fundamental structure of the human will: it would rather will *nothingness* — self-denial, self-annihilation, the void — than face the horror of having nothing to will. This "will to nothingness" is still a *will*, and thus still an expression of the [[Will to Power]].

> The ascetic ideal springs from the prophylactic and self-preservative instincts of a decadent life, which seeks by every means to maintain itself and struggles for existence. (054-001)

## Key Passages

> What is the meaning of ascetic ideals? In artists, nothing, or too many things. In philosophers and scholars, something like an instinct and a perception of the most favourable conditions for a high spirituality. In women, at best an additional charm of *morbidezza*. In the physiologically distorted and deranged (the majority of mortals), an attempt to pose as "too good" for this world. (042-001)

> The advent of the Christian God as the maximum God hitherto attained, was accompanied by the maximum of the feeling of guilt on earth. (034-001)

## Relationship to Other Concepts

The ascetic [[ideals|ideal]] is the interpretive framework that exploits and amplifies [[Bad Conscience]], channels [[Ressentiment]] into self-directed [[cruelty]], and provides the metaphysical justification for the inversion of values carried out by the [[Priests|priestly caste]]. It is the ultimate expression of the [[Slave Morality|slave revolt in morals]].
