## Definition

In [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s *On the [[genealogy|Genealogy]] of Morality*, **interpretation** is not merely an intellectual activity but the fundamental mode through which all life operates. Every drive, every instinct, every moral system is an interpretation—an act of domination that imposes a meaning upon reality.

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s [[genealogy|genealogical method]] is itself an exercise in interpretation: it does not claim to discover objective truths but rather offers a competing interpretation, one that exposes the hidden [[will-to-power|will to power]] behind other interpretations.

## Nietzsche's Analysis

### Interpretation as Domination

For [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]], interpretation is inseparable from power. "All subduing and domination is a new interpretation and adjustment" (028-001). When a new ruling class imposes its values, it is not discovering truth but interpreting the world according to its own instincts.

The slave revolt in morality is, at its core, an act of interpretation: the weak reinterpret their weakness as "virtue" and the strength of the [[aristocracy|masters]] as "evil."

### The Interpretation of Weakness

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s most penetrating analysis of interpretation concerns how the weak interpret their own condition. The weak have produced "that most sublime specimen of self-deception, the interpretation of weakness as freedom, of their actual *being* *thus* *and* *thus* as *merit*" (012-001).

This is the interpretive mechanism at the he[[artist|art]] of [[Slave Morality|slave morality]]: powerlessness is reinterpreted as chosen meekness, inability to retaliate as chosen forgiveness, and subjugation as chosen obedience.

### Perspectivism and Science

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] extends his analysis of interpretation to the sciences themselves. "All the sciences have now to pave the way for the future task of the philosopher; this task being understood to mean that he has to fix the *hierarchy of values*" (016-004). [[science|Science]] does not escape interpretation—it is itself an interpretive framework, one that covertly inherits the structure of the [[Ascetic Ideal|ascetic ideal]].

There is no interpretation-free standpoint. The claim to "objectivity" is itself an interpretation that serves p[[artist|art]]icular interests.

### The Body as Interpreter

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] locates interpretation not in a disembodied intellect but in the [[health|physiological]] drives themselves. Every instinct "gives a new interpretation to these animal instincts" (038-001). The body, with its competing drives and instincts, is the primary site of interpretation.

This [[health|physiological]] grounding means that interpretations are never neutral—they express the health or [[disease|sickness]], strength or weakness, of the interpreting body.

| Mode of Interpretation | Source | Function |
|----------------------|--------|---------|
| [[noble|Noble]] interpretation | Strength, plenitude | Spontaneous self-affirmation |
| Slave interpretation | Weakness, [[ressentiment]] | Revaluation of master values |
| Scientific interpretation | Ascetic [[ideals|ideal]] (inherited) | Claim to objectivity (hidden will to truth) |
| Genealogical interpretation | [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s own method | Exposes [[will-to-power|will to power]] behind other interpretations |

## Key Passages

> The subject (or, to use popular language, the *soul*) has perhaps proved itself the best dogma in the world simply because it rendered possible to the horde of mortal, weak, and oppressed individuals of every kind, that most sublime specimen of self-deception, the interpretation of weakness as freedom, of their actual *being* *thus* *and* *thus* as *merit*. (012-001)

> All subduing and domination is a new interpretation and adjustment, which must necessarily cause the previous "sense" and "purpose" to be obscured or completely obliterated. (028-001)

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s [[genealogy|genealogical method]] is self-consciously an interpretation—but one that claims superiority by exposing the interpretive character of all other claims to truth. The question is not which interpretation is "true," but which interpretation serves life and which serves its negation.
