# England

## Overview

In [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s *[[genealogy|Genealogy]] of Morality*, **England** represents the antithesis of everything [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] values: it is the home of utilitarianism, of "English psychologists" who reduce morality to utility, of small-mindedness and democratic leveling. When [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] speaks of "England," he is speaking less of a geographical nation than of a spiritual condition — the condition of modern man who has lost the capacity for grandeur and tragic affirmation.

## Role in the Genealogy

### The "English Psychologists"

The *[[genealogy|Genealogy]]* opens with a critique of the "English psychologists" — the utilitarian and evolutionary moralists ([[herbert-spencer|Herbert Spencer]], David Hume, and their successors) who attempt to derive morality from utility, habit, or natural selection. For [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]], these English thinkers are not genealogists at all — they are merely the latest expression of the very morality they claim to explain, incapable of the historical depth and psychological courage that genuine genealogy requires.

> Philosophers have existed from India to England, to take the opposite poles of philosophical aptitude. (048-001)

The opposition between [[india|India]] and England is deliberate: [[india|India]] represents the deepest, most radical philosophical tradition; England represents the shallowest — a philosophy that has forgotten what philosophy once was.

### English Small-Mindedness

In the later sections of the *[[genealogy|Genealogy]]*, [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s critique of England sharpens into contempt. Where [[Germany]] is "dangerous" — capable of philosophical depth and cultural transformation — England is merely "small-minded," a nation of shopkeepers whose moral philosophy reflects its commercial spirit.

> England's small-mindedness is the great danger now on earth. (086-001)

This is not chauvinism but diagnostic: English culture, in [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s view, represents the complete triumph of the [[slave-morality|slave]] value system, naturalized as common sense and economic rationality.

### English Colonies and Global Leveling

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] extends his critique to the English colonial project, which he sees as the globalization of English mediocrity. The English do not conquer — they *level*; their empire spreads not [[noble|aristocratic]] values but the democratic-commercial flattening of human types.

> The English colonies are needed by England as a safety valve for the surplus of her criminal and semi-criminal population. (087-002)
