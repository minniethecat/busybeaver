#### PEOPLES AND COUNTRIES.

##### Translated by J. M. KENNEDY.

[073-001]How much we have learnt and learnt anew in fifty years! The whole Romantic School with its belief in "the people" is refuted! No Homeric poetry as "popular" poetry! No deification of the great powers of Nature! No deduction from language-relationship to race-relationship! No "intellectual contemplations" of the supernatural! No truth enshrouded in religion!

[073-002]The problem of truthfulness is quite a new one. I am astonished. From this standpoint we regard such natures as Bismarck as culpable out of carelessness, such as Richard Wagner out of want of modesty; we would condemn Plato for his _pia fraus_ , Kant for the derivation of his Categorical Imperative, his own belief certainly not having come to him from this source.

[073-003]Finally, even doubt turns against itself: doubt in doubt. And the question as to the _value_ of truthfulness and its extent lies _there_.
