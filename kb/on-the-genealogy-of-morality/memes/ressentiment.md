# Ressentiment

## Definition

**Ressentiment** (from the French, deliberately used by [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] rather than the German *Groll*) is the psychological mechanism through which the weak exact imaginary revenge upon the strong. Unable to act upon their hostility directly, those possessed by ressentiment create a moral system in which their own powerlessness is reinterpreted as virtue and the strength of their enemies as evil. The concept is the foundation of [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s entire critique of morality in the First Essay.

> The revolt of the slaves in morals begins in the very principle of *ressentiment* becoming creative and giving birth to values — the ressentiment of those beings who are prevented from a genuine reaction, from a deed, and who compensate themselves through an imaginary revenge. (010-001)

## Nietzsche's Analysis

### Imaginary Revenge

The defining feature of ressentiment is that it operates in the imagination rather than in action. The man of ressentiment cannot strike back — so he redefines striking back as evil. His weakness becomes "goodness," his impotence becomes "patience," his inability to take revenge becomes "forgiveness."

> While every aristocratic morality springs from a triumphant affirmation of itself, slave morality from the outset says No to what is "outside," "different," "not itself": and this No is its creative deed. (010-001)

### The Priest as Master of Ressentiment

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] identifies the [[Priests|priestly caste]] as the historical [[aristocracy|masters]] of ressentiment. The [[priests|priest]], physically weak but intellectually cunning, develops ressentiment into an [[artist|art]] form — a system of [[revenge|spiritual revenge]] that ultimately conquers the physically stronger warrior caste through the invention of "sin," "[[guilt]]," and "evil."

> The really great haters in the history of the world have always been priests, who are also the cleverest haters — in comparison with the cleverness of priestly revenge, every other piece of cleverness is practically negligible. (007-001)

### The Jews as the Priestly Nation

The [[Jews]] are, for [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]], the nation that perfected ressentiment as a world-historical force. Their act of revaluation — calling the powerful "evil" and the weak "good" — was an act of "the most [[revenge|spiritual revenge]]," one that has shaped the entire course of Western morality.

> It was the Jews who, in opposition to the aristocratic equation, dared with a terrifying logic to suggest the contrary equation. (007-001)

### Ressentiment and the Ascetic Ideal

In the Third Essay, ressentiment is shown to be one of the primary tools of the [[Ascetic Ideal]]. The [[Ascetic Ideal|ascetic priest]] redirects the ressentiment of the suffering masses away from their actual oppressors and toward themselves — transforming social resentment into [[Bad Conscience]].

> Here rules ressentiment without parallel, the ressentiment of an insatiable instinct and will to power that would become master not over something in life but over life itself. (052-001)

## Key Distinction: Noble vs. Slave Morality

| Aspect | [[noble|Noble]] Morality | [[slave-morality|Slave Morality]] (Ressentiment) |
|--------|---------------|------------------------------|
| Origin | Spontaneous self-affirmation | Reactive negation of the "other" |
| Direction | "Yes" to self first, then "No" to the low | "No" to the other first, then "Yes" to self |
| Psychology | Action-oriented, forgetful | Brooding, rememberful, calculating |
| Value creation | Creates values from strength | Inverts existing values from weakness |
