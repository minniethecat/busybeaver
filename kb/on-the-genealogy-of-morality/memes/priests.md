# Priests

## Overview

In [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s *[[genealogy|Genealogy]] of Morality*, **priests** (*Priester*) form a distinct caste whose psychological characteristics — weakness, cunning, and [[ressentiment]] — make them the principal agents of the "slave revolt in morals." The priest is [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s archetype of the weak who triumphs through spiritual manipulation rather than physical strength.

## Nietzsche's Characterization

### The Weakest Become the Most Dangerous

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] describes priests as the physically weakest caste, whose impotence drives them to develop an unparalleled capacity for hatred and revenge. Unlike the warrior who discharges aggression outward, the priest channels it into spiritual and intellectual forms.

> The priests are, as is notorious, *the worst enemies* — why? Because they are the weakest. Their weakness causes their hate to expand into a monstrous and sinister shape, a shape which is most crafty and most poisonous. (007-001)

### Masters of Ressentiment

The priest's defining capacity is *[[ressentiment]]* — the ability to transmute weakness into moral superiority. Unable to compete on the [[noble|aristocratic]] plane of physical excellence, the priest redefines the terms of evaluation itself: what the strong call "good," the priest calls "evil"; what the priest is (weak, suffering), he calls "good."

> It was the Jews who, in opposition to the aristocratic equation (good = aristocratic = beautiful = happy = loved by the gods), dared with a terrifying logic to suggest the contrary equation... (007-001)

## Role in Moral History

### The Slave Revolt in Morals

The priestly caste, according to [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]], orchestrated what he calls the "slave revolt in morals" — a millennia-long campaign that inverted the [[noble|aristocratic]] value system. This is [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s most consequential historical claim: Western morality itself is the product of priestly [[ressentiment]].

> The slave revolt in morals begins with the Jews: a revolt which has behind it a history of two millennia, and which at the present day has only moved out of our sight, because it — has achieved victory. (007-001)

### The Ascetic Priest

In the Third Essay, [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] analyzes the "[[ascetic-priest|ascetic priest]]" — the figure who exploits human suffering and [[bad-conscience|bad conscience]] to consolidate power. The [[ascetic-priest|ascetic priest]] does not cure suffering but *redirects* it, turning the sufferer against himself.

> The ascetic priest must be accepted by us as the predestined saviour, herdsman, and champion of the sick herd. (Essay 3)

## Key Passages

> The really great haters in the history of the world have always been priests, who are also the cleverest haters — in comparison with the cleverness of priestly revenge, every other piece of cleverness is practically negligible. (007-001)
