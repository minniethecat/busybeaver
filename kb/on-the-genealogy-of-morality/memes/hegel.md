# Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel

## Overview

Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel (1770–1831) was the dominant figure of German Idealism, whose dialectical philosophy and philosophy of history exerted enormous influence across nineteenth-century [[europe|Europe]]an thought. In the *[[genealogy|Genealogy]]*, Hegel appears not as a subject of sustained analysis but as a symptomatic figure — a representative of German intellectual tendencies that [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] diagnoses as dangerous.

## Role in the Genealogy

Hegel is mentioned in two distinct contexts in the *[[genealogy|Genealogy]]*. In the Third Essay, [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] invokes Hegel as an example of the "enemies" that a philosopher needs to remain vital — in this case, [[arthur-schopenhauer|Schopenhauer]]'s need for Hegel as an antagonist. In the appended "Peoples and Countries," Hegel appears in a list of German "intoxicants," alongside [[richard-wagner|Richard Wagner]] and the historical sense.

> Schopenhauer... needed enemies to keep him in a good humour... without his enemies, without Hegel, woman, sensuality, and the whole will to existence... (048-001)

> The Germans are a dangerous people: they are experts at inventing intoxicants. Gothic, rococo... the historical sense and exoticism, Hegel, Richard Wagner... (076-001)

## Nietzsche's Critique

### Hegel as Intellectual Narcotic

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s characterization of Hegel as an "intoxicant" reflects his deeper critique of German philosophy as a form of cultural [[disease|pathology]]. For [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]], Hegelian dialectics — with its [[religion|faith]] in historical progress and the rationality of the real — represents a sedative for the intellect: it makes suffering, contradiction, and [[cruelty]] appear meaningful within a larger rational scheme, thereby performing the same consolatory function as religion.

### The Philosopher's Need for an Enemy

In the passage on [[arthur-schopenhauer|Schopenhauer]], [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] makes a broader point about [[philosopher|philosophical psychology]]: the greatest thinkers require antagonists to sharpen their thought. Hegel served this function for [[arthur-schopenhauer|Schopenhauer]], whose vitriol against Hegelianism was legendary. [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] sees this not as a flaw but as a necessary condition of authentic philosophical creativity.

## Key Passages

> ...without his enemies, without Hegel, woman, sensuality, and the whole will to existence, the whole will to remain on earth... Schopenhauer would have grown ill, would have become a pessimist... (048-001)

> The Germans are a dangerous people: they are experts at inventing intoxicants. Gothic, rococo (according to Semper), the historical sense and exoticism, Hegel, Richard Wagner — Leibniz, too (dangerous at the present day)... (076-001)
