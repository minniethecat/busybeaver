## Definition

In [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s *On the [[genealogy|Genealogy]] of Morality*, **sacrifice** is not a peripheral religious phenomenon but the foundation of all religion and morality. "All religions are really at bottom systems of cruelties... without blood, tortures, and sacrifice" (019-001). Sacrifice is the primal act through which humanity's innate [[Cruelty|cruelty]] is organized, ritualized, and given meaning.

The history of sacrifice traces an arc from external human sacrifice to internalized self-sacrifice—from the literal blood of the victim to the psychological blood of [[Bad Conscience|bad conscience]].

## Nietzsche's Analysis

### Sacrifice as Organized Cruelty

The earliest religions, [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] argues, were built upon sacrifice—and sacrifice was built upon [[cruelty]]. "The most dreadful sacrifices and pledges (to which belongs the sacrifice of the first-born), the most loathsome mutilations (for instance, castration), the cruellest ritual forms of all [[religion|religious cults]]" (019-001) constitute the foundation of human religious history.

Sacrifice gave form to the human instinct for [[cruelty]], directing it toward ritually prescribed targets and thereby making it socially productive. The gods were the original spectators of human suffering—the audience for whom [[cruelty]] was performed.

### Self-Sacrifice and the Ascetic Ideal

The transition from external sacrifice to self-sacrifice marks the triumph of the ascetic [[ideals|ideal]]. "The instincts of pity, self-denial, and self-sacrifice which [[arthur-schopenhauer|Schopenhauer]] had so persistently painted in gilt" (P07-001) become the new moral [[ideals|ideal]]s.

Self-sacrifice appears as the highest virtue, but [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] sees it as the same [[cruelty]] turned inward—the self as victim. "Such opposite ideas as '*selflessness*,' *self-denial*, *self-sacrifice*" (034-001) are not the negation of [[cruelty]] but its internalization. The ascetic [[ideals|ideal]] makes the self the sacrificial altar.

### Sacrifice and the Creation of Values

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] sees sacrifice at work even in the most "secular" domains. The pursuit of truth, for instance, requires the sacrifice of comfort, belief, and occasionally life itself. "They have specifically trained themselves to sacrifice what is desirable to what is true" (001-003).

The greatness of a value, in [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s view, is measured by the sacrifice it demands: "The greatness of a sacrifice is gauged by the greatness of the sacrifice that it requires" (028-001). Humanity values most what costs it most—a psychological economy that the ascetic [[ideals|ideal]] exploits to its fullest.

### Beyond Sacrifice

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s critique of sacrifice does not call for its abolition but for its recognition. The question is not whether humanity will make sacrifices—it always has and always will—but *for what* and *in what spirit*.

The [[Noble|noble]] type also sacrifices, but the noble sacrifice is an overflow of strength, not a negation of self. The warrior who risks his life for glory is not practicing self-denial but self-affirmation. The [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]an alternative to ascetic sacrifice is not the absence of sacrifice but sacrifice in the service of life rather than against it.

## Key Passages

> All religions are really at bottom systems of cruelties... without blood, tortures, and sacrifice; the most dreadful sacrifices and pledges (to which belongs the sacrifice of the first-born), the most loathsome mutilations. (019-001)

> They have specifically trained themselves to sacrifice what is desirable to what is true. (001-003)

> The greatness of a sacrifice is gauged by the greatness of the sacrifice that it requires: humanity as mass sacrificed to the prosperity of the single *stronger* species man—*that* would be a *progress*. (028-001)

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s most provocative claim about sacrifice appears in his vision of the future: the mass of humanity sacrificed for the production of a higher type. This is sacrifice not as self-negation but as the condition for the enhancement of life—a "sacrifice" that the ascetic [[ideals|ideal]] would condemn but that [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s revaluation affirms.
