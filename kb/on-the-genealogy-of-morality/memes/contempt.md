# Contempt

## Definition

In [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s *[[genealogy|Genealogy]] of Morality*, **Contempt** (*Verachtung*) operates in two fundamentally opposed registers. On one side stands the noble, outward-directed contempt of the [[noble|aristocratic]] spirit — a spontaneous disdain for what is base, weak, or unworthy, born of self-affirmation rather than [[ressentiment|resentment]]. On the other side stands the ascetic self-contempt — the inwardly-turned negation of one's own nature, cultivated by the [[Ascetic Priest]] as a method of spiritual domination. The concept spans all three Essays, appearing as an [[noble|aristocratic]] affect (Essay 1), as a component of the violent self-critique that constitutes [[Bad Conscience]] (Essay 2), and as the endpoint of the ascetic [[ideals|ideal]]'s logic (Essay 3).

> At any case the mood of contempt, of disdain, of superciliousness… is a much higher authority for the value of morality than that of praise. (010-002)

## Nietzsche's Analysis

### The Noble Contempt — Outward and Affirmative

In the First Essay, [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] presents contempt as one of the defining affects of the noble mode of valuation. The noble spirit does not calculate his contempt — it arises as naturally as praise, and is rooted not in hatred of the other but in the sheer, unshakeable certainty of his own worth. The noble man's contempt for the base, the cowardly, the utilitarian, and the pitiful is the negative side of his self-affirmation: he says "I am good" not by comparing himself to others but by feeling the distance between himself and what is beneath him.

> The nonchalance of the aristocrat, his contempt for safety, body, life, and comfort, his appalling cheerfulness and depth of delight in all destruction, in all the debauches of victory and cruelty. (010-004)

This contempt is inseparable from the [[Pathos of Distance]] — the felt superiority of the noble type that makes his "good" a spontaneous creation rather than a reaction. It is a productive, world-making contempt, not a defensive one.

### Contempt as Artistic Form-Giving

In Essay 2, contempt appears as one element in the violent self-critique that constitutes [[bad-conscience|bad conscience]]. When the [[will-to-power|will to power]] is denied external objects, it turns inward and takes the form of an "[[artist|art]]ist's [[cruelty]]" directed at the self. Here contempt is a tool of formation — the soul that molds itself as resistant material must first hold itself in contempt, negate what it finds, in order to impose a new form.

> A critique, a contradiction, a contempt, a negation; this sinister and ghastly labour of a soul voluntarily at variance with itself makes it suffer. (034-001)

The same contempt that the noble directs outward at the base becomes, when internalized, the violence through which the self remakes itself. This is the productive dimension of contempt in the service of [[Will to Power]].

### Self-Contempt and the Ascetic Ideal

The Third Essay traces the dark culmination of contempt: the ascetic self-contempt, in which the [[will-to-power|will to power]] of the weak turns upon the self under the guidance of the [[Ascetic Priest]]. This is no longer a productive, form-giving contempt but a pathological one — the sick animal wounding itself because it cannot wound others.

> The ascetic ideal culminates in the ascetic self-contempt, the ascetic scorn of one's own self. (053-001)

The [[priests|priest]] cultivates self-contempt as his instrument of power: by making the sufferer despise himself, the [[priests|priest]] becomes indispensable as the interpreter of that suffering. The soil of self-contempt is the swamp in which every kind of poisonous growth — sin, [[Guilt]], the need for redemption — takes root.

> On such a soil of self-contempt, a veritable swamp soil, grows every kind of weed, every poisonous plant. (055-001)

### Contempt of Opinion and Philosophical Courage

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] also invokes contempt in a positive sense in the Third Essay: the philosopher's contempt of public opinion, of popular crazes, of the modern "work" ethic. This is contempt as intellectual independence — the courage to stand ap[[artist|art]] from the herd. It is one of the "manly" [[virtue|virtues]] [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] associates with the capacity for truth.

> Courage, contempt of opinion, intellectual stoicism. (059-001)

Here contempt returns to its noble, affirmative register — not a reactive negation, but an active assertion of the philosopher's own standard of value against the prevailing noise.
