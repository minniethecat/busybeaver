# Aristocracy

## Overview

In [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s *[[genealogy|Genealogy]] of Morality*, the **aristocracy** (*Aristokratie*, *Adel*) is the original value-creating caste — the class of "masters" whose spontaneous self-affirmation generates the foundational distinction between "good" and "bad." The [[noble|aristocratic]] mode of valuation is prior to, and fundamentally different from, the reactive [[slave-morality|morality of the slaves]]. Understanding the aristocracy is essential to grasping [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s entire genealogical project.

> Everywhere "aristocrat," "noble" (in the social sense), is the root idea out of which there necessarily developed "good" in the sense of "with aristocratic soul," "noble," in the sense of "with a soul of high calibre," "with a privileged soul." (004-001)

## Nietzsche's Analysis

### The Original Value-Creators

The aristocracy does not *discover* values — it *creates* them. The fundamental [[noble|aristocratic]] gesture is to look upon oneself and one's own way of being and to say: *this* is good. This spontaneous self-affirmation is the origin of all value. The aristocrat does not need an enemy to know what he is; he knows himself through his own activity, his own excellence.

> The judgment "good" did not originate among those to whom "goodness" was shown. On the contrary, it was the "good" themselves, that is, the aristocratic, the powerful, the high-stationed, the high-minded, who felt and ranked themselves and their doings as good — in contradistinction to all that was base, low-minded, and plebeian. (002-002)

### The Pathos of Distance

Central to [[noble|aristocratic]] valuation is what [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] calls the *[[pathos-of-distance|Pathos der Distanz]]* — the "pathos of distance." The aristocrat experiences his superiority not as a claim to be argued but as a fact to be lived. This distance is constitutive of [[noble|aristocratic]] identity; without it, the aristocrat would not know himself as noble.

> It is out of this *pathos of distance* that they first arrogated the right to create values for their own profit, and to coin the names of such values. (002-002)

### The Aristocratic Value Equation

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] reconstructs the original [[noble|aristocratic]] value equation through etymological analysis:

| Term | Aristocratic Meaning | Later (Slave) Meaning |
|------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Good (*gut*) | [[noble|Noble]], powerful, beautiful, happy | Humble, meek, self-sacrificing |
| Bad (*schlecht*) | Base, plebeian, low | — |
| Evil (*böse*) | — (aristocrats don't use this) | The powerful, the rich, the strong |

> The aristocratic morality springs from a triumphant saying of "yes" to one's own self. (010-001)

## Historical Declines

### The Priestly Schism

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] identifies a crucial split *within* the aristocracy itself: the knightly-[[noble|aristocratic]] and the [[priests|priest]]ly-[[noble|aristocratic]] modes. The [[priests|priestly caste]], though also [[noble|aristocratic]], develops a different relationship to power — one mediated by purity, ritual, and ultimately [[ressentiment]].

> There is from the very beginning a split in the aristocratic caste: the knightly-aristocratic mode of valuation branches off from the priestly. (007-001)

### Decay of Aristocratic Values

The decay of [[noble|aristocratic]] values is not caused by external conquest but by internal corruption — when the [[noble|aristocratic]] value equation no longer spontaneously affirms itself but begins to *doubt* itself. This self-doubt opens the door for the slave revolt in morals.

> It was on the occasion of the *decay* of aristocratic values, that the antitheses "good" and "evil" came to be felt more and more strongly. (002-003)
