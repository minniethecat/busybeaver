## Overview

Ernest Renan (1823–1892) was a French historian, philologist, and philosopher of religion, best known for his *Life of [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]]* (1863) and his studies of Semitic languages. A pivotal figure in nineteenth-century French secular thought, Renan attempted to apply critical historical methods to religious texts while maintaining a posture of aesthetic reverence toward their cultural residues.

In the third essay of the *[[genealogy|Genealogy]]*, [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] unleashes one of his most sustained rhetorical assaults on Renan and the historiographical school he represents. The attack is embedded in a broader polemic against modern "objectivity" as a disguised form of nihilism.

## Role in the Genealogy

Renan appears in Essay 3 §26 (067-001), within [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s extended critique of modern historiography. The immediate context is [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s disgust with "contemplative" historians — scholars who affect the stance of disinterested observation while covertly indulging an ascetic hostility to life.

The reference is compressed into a single, brutal image: "a thing half-[[priests|priest]], half-satyr (Renan *parfum*)." The French tag "*parfum*" — meaning "perfume" or "aroma" — marks the phrase as a sardonic evocation of Renan's own style. [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] is mimicking Renan's aestheticized prose even as he condemns it.

## Nietzsche's Critique

**The half-[[priests|priest]], half-satyr.** [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s image fuses two figures that [[christianity|Christianity]] historically opposed: the [[priests|priest]], who renounces sensuality for spiritual authority, and the satyr, the mythological embodiment of unbridled appetite. Renan, in [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s diagnosis, wants both — the sanctity of the [[priests|priest]] and the pleasure of the sensualist, without the honesty of either. The hyphenated compound "half-[[priests|priest]], half-satyr" designates not integration but hypocrisy: a figure who exploits the prestige of religious seriousness while covertly gratifying the desires that seriousness is meant to discipline.

**The "scented mannikins-about-town."** [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s assault extends beyond Renan to the entire class of "objective" historians. They are "scented" — effete, cosmetic, urban — and "mannikins," hollow figures who go through the motions of scholarship without the substance of genuine intellectual engagement. The metaphor of the "chair" (*Katheder*) of history reduces the academic position to a piece of furniture, a platform for performance rather than thought.

**The falsetto of applause.** [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] hears in Renan's appreciative tone — his "high, shrill falsetto" — the betrayal of what he lacks. The falsetto is a voice that cannot reach the lower registers: in [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s audiology, it is the sound of castration, of the "surgeon-like" operation the Fates have performed on these "lascivious eunuchs of history." The critique is brutal but pointed: the posture of contemplative objectivity is, for [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]], a symptom of [[health|physiological]] and psychological impoverishment.

**Renan and the ascetic [[ideals|ideal]].** Renan's significance in the *[[genealogy|Genealogy]]* extends beyond the single named reference. He epitomizes the "modern" form of the ascetic [[ideals|ideal]] that [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] diagnoses in Essay 3: the [[ideals|ideal]] that no longer believes in God but preserves the ascetic posture — the will to nothingness — in secularized form. The "objective" historian who claims to merely "describe" is, in [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s analysis, practicing a covert nihilism: the refusal to evaluate, to affirm, to create values.

## Key Passages

> The "contemplative" are a hundred times worse—I never knew anything which produced such intense nausea as one of those "objective" _chairs_, one of those scented mannikins-about-town of history, a thing half-priest, half-satyr (Renan _parfum_), which betrays by the high, shrill falsetto of his applause what he lacks and where he lacks it, who betrays where in this case the Fates have plied their ghastly shears, alas! in too surgeon-like a fashion! (067-001)

> All reverence on my part to the ascetic ideal, _in so far as it is honourable_! So long as it believes in itself and plays no pranks on us! But I like not all these coquettish bugs who have an insatiate ambition to smell of the infinite, until eventually the infinite smells of bugs. (067-001)

> Its loftiest pretension is now to be a _mirror_; it repudiates all teleology; it will have no more "proving"; it disdains to play the judge, and thereby shows its good taste—it asserts as little as it denies, it fixes, it "describes." All this is to a high degree ascetic, but at the same time it is to a much greater degree _nihilistic_. (067-001)
