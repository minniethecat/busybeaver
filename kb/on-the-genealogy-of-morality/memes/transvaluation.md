## Definition

**Transvaluation** (*Umwertung*) is [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s term for the radical inversion of values. The concept operates on two levels: historically, it describes what [[Slave Morality|slave morality]] accomplished—the revaluation of noble "good" into "evil" and of noble "bad" into slave "good." Programmatically, it names what [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] himself seeks to accomplish—a counter-transvaluation that would overcome the [[slave-morality|slave morality]]'s 2,000-year dominance.

"The [[jews|Jews]] achieved that miracle of inversion of values" (007-001). Transvaluation is the central event in [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s history of morality and the central task of his philosophy.

## Nietzsche's Analysis

### The Slave Transvaluation

The first and most consequential transvaluation in [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s history is the slave revolt in morality. The weak, the suffering, the [[Ressentiment|ressentiment]]-filled—unable to compete with the strong on their own terms—invent a new mode of valuation.

"The slave revolt in morality begins when [[ressentiment]] itself becomes creative and gives birth to values" (010-002). The slaves do not merely reject the [[aristocracy|masters]]' values—they invert them. What the [[aristocracy|masters]] called "good" (strength, pride, power) becomes "evil." What the [[aristocracy|masters]] called "bad" (weakness, humility, poverty) becomes "good."

This is "a radical transvaluation of values, which was at the same time the act of the most *[[revenge|spiritual revenge]]*" (007-001). The weak, unable to take physical revenge on the strong, take [[revenge|spiritual revenge]] by redefining the very terms of valuation.

### Israel vs. Rome

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] figures the slave transvaluation as the victory of Judea over [[rome|Rome]]—of the [[priests|priest]]ly over the noble, of weakness over strength. "It is at least certain that *sub hoc signo* [[jews|Israel]], with its revenge and transvaluation of all values, has up to the present always triumphed again over all other [[ideals|ideal]]s, over all more [[noble|aristocratic]] [[ideals|ideal]]s" (008-001).

This is not merely a historical observation but a diagnosis of the present. The slave transvaluation is the value-system within which modern [[europe|Europe]] still operates. Its triumph is so complete that we no longer recognize it *as* a transvaluation—we mistake it for morality itself.

### The Workshop of Ideals

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s genealogy exposes the "workshop" where this transvaluation was manufactured. "These workshops *where [[ideals|ideal]]s are manufactured*—verily they reek with the fumes of lying and the stale air of hidden things!" (013-009).

The transvaluation of values is not an honest philosophical argument but a psychological operation. It is driven by [[ressentiment]], refined by [[priests|priest]]ly cunning, and disguised as revelation. The genealogist's task is to expose this manufacturing process.

### Nietzsche's Counter-Transvaluation

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s own philosophical project is a counter-transvaluation: an "Umwertung aller Werte" (transvaluation of all values) that would overcome the [[slave-morality|slave morality]] and restore the conditions for noble valuation.

This counter-transvaluation does not simply return to pre-moral noble values—that is impossible. Rather, it seeks to create new values beyond the opposition of good and evil, values that affirm life rather than negate it. The philosopher of the future is the agent of this counter-transvaluation.

| Transvaluation | Agent | Direction | Result |
|--------------|-------|----------|--------|
| Slave transvaluation | [[priests|Priests]], the weak | [[noble|Noble]] good → evil; slave bad → good | 2,000 years of ascetic dominance |
| [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s counter-transvaluation | [[philosopher|Philosopher]]s of the future | Overcome good/evil; affirm life | Creation of new values |

## Key Passages

> The Jews achieved that miracle of inversion of values, thanks to which life on earth has for two millennia acquired a new and dangerous charm. (007-001)

> It is at least certain that *sub hoc signo* Israel, with its revenge and transvaluation of all values, has up to the present always triumphed again over all other ideals, over all more aristocratic ideals. (008-001)

> These workshops *where ideals are manufactured*—verily they reek with the fumes of lying and the stale air of hidden things! (013-009)

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s concept of transvaluation is at once a historical analysis and a philosophical program. It exposes the slave revolt as an act of [[revenge|spiritual revenge]] disguised as moral progress, while calling for a new transvaluation that would liberate humanity from the ascetic [[ideals|ideal]].
