# Arthur Schopenhauer

## Overview

Arthur Schopenhauer (1788–1860) was a German philosopher whose metaphysical pessimism and concept of the will as the fundamental reality profoundly influenced [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s intellectual development. In the *[[genealogy|Genealogy]]*, Schopenhauer appears both as [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s "great teacher" and as a target of critique — p[[artist|art]]icularly regarding his stance on pity and the value of suffering.

## Role in the Genealogy

Schopenhauer is mentioned across all three Essays, serving as a key reference point for [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s arguments about morality, [[artist|art]], and [[ascetic-ideal|asceticism]]. In the Preface, [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] identifies Schopenhauer as the primary interlocutor of his early work. In the Third Essay, Schopenhauer becomes a central case study in [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s analysis of the ascetic [[ideals|ideal]] and the meaning of [[artist|art]].

> It will be obvious from the whole discourse of this treatise that I am concerned with a problem much more profound and radical than the whole Utilitarian and Schopenhauerian treatment of morality. (Preface)

## Nietzsche's Engagement

### Admiration: The Honest Atheist

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] respected Schopenhauer as one of the few [[europe|Europe]]an [[philosopher|philosophers]] who confronted suffering honestly, without religious consolation. Schopenhauer's atheism and his recognition of the pervasiveness of suffering aligned with [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s own anti-metaphysical commitments.

> Schopenhauer, as its most eloquent and most fascinating representative... there was need of an interpreter of the ascetic ideal, an artist among philosophers. (081-001)

### Critique: The Problem of Pity

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s sharpest criticism targets Schopenhauer's elevation of pity (*Mitleid*) as the foundation of morality. For [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]], pity is not a virtue but a symptom of decadence — a weakening of the [[instinct|life-instinct]]s disguised as moral goodness. Schopenhauer's morality of [[pity|compassion]] represents precisely the "[[slave-morality|slave morality]]" [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] seeks to unmask.

> Schopenhauer was hostile to life: therefore pity became for him a virtue. (Preface)

### The Ascetic Ideal in Art

In the Third Essay, [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] analyzes how Schopenhauer used [[artist|art]] — p[[artist|art]]icularly music — as a "quietener" of the will, a temporary escape from suffering. This, [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] argues, is a form of the ascetic [[ideals|ideal]]: [[artist|art]] serving as a narcotic against life rather than an affirmation of it.

> The case of Schopenhauer... what does he actually find in art that is personally valuable? That it gives him *respite* from himself. (048-001)
