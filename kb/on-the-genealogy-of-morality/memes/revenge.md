## Definition

In [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s genealogy, **revenge** (*Rache*) is the most ancient and persistent human passion — and the hidden engine of moral systems. The "slave revolt in morality" is fundamentally an act of spiritual revenge: unable to exact physical revenge upon their oppressors, the weak create an "imaginary revenge" by revaluing all values, transforming the master's strength into "evil" and their own powerlessness into "good."

> The slave revolt in morality begins when *ressentiment* itself becomes creative and gives birth to values: the ressentiment of beings denied the true reaction, that of the deed, who compensate themselves with an imaginary revenge. (010-002)

## Nietzsche's Analysis

### Revenge as Creative Force

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s most radical thesis is that revenge is *creative*. It does not merely destroy — it invents new values, new interpretations, new gods. The entire edifice of Judeo-[[christianity|Christian morality]] is, on this account, a monument to the creativity of revenge:

> From the trunk of that tree of revenge and hate, Jewish hate — that deepest and most sublime hate, which creates ideals and changes values, a hate the like of which has never been on earth — from this grew something equally incomparable, a *new love*, the deepest and most sublime of all kinds of love. (008-001)

The "new love" — Christian *agape* — is revealed as transformed hatred, the revenge of the powerless spiritualized into an ethic of universal love.

### Imaginary Revenge and the Priest

The [[priests|priest]] is the architect of imaginary revenge. Unable to act directly, he perfects the [[artist|art]] of spiritual vengeance:

> With the cleverness of priestly revenge, every other piece of cleverness is poor compared. (007-001)

The [[priests|priest]]ly revaluation transforms suffering into merit, powerlessness into piety, and makes the powerful feel [[guilt]]y for their own strength. This is revenge perfected — the victim makes the victor feel like the sinner.

### Revenge Disguised as Justice

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] is p[[artist|art]]icularly attentive to how revenge disguises itself as justice:

> What they demand they call not revenge but "the triumph of *righteousness*"; what they hate in their enemy is not *himself* — no, they hate "injustice," "godlessness." (013-011)

This self-deception is characteristic of the man of [[ressentiment]]: he cannot admit to himself that he seeks revenge, so he invents moral categories that justify his hatred as righteous indignation.

### The Reformation as Revenge Movement

In a striking historical diagnosis, [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] characterizes [[martin-luther|the Reformation]] as a delayed explosion of [[ressentiment]]-driven revenge:

> That written outburst which has revenge on its conscience... the German and English movement of revenge, which is called the Reformation. (015-001, paraphrase)
