## Overview

Otto von Bismarck (1815–1898), the "Iron Chancellor" who unified [[germany|Germany]] under Prussian leadership, appears in [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s _On the [[genealogy|Genealogy]] of Morality_ as a rare modern instance of the [[noble|aristocratic]] type.

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]—who generally held modern politics and [[germany|German nation]]alism in contempt—makes a striking exception for Bismarck, grouping him with [[Johann Wolfgang von Goethe]] and [[Ludwig van Beethoven]] as manifestations of genuine human greatness amid the "racial aberrations" of modernity.

## Role in the Genealogy

Bismarck appears exclusively in Essay 2 (on "[[guilt|Guilt]], [[bad-conscience|Bad Conscience]], and Related Matters"), specifically in the sections where [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] discusses the relationship between the state, the people, and the exceptional individual.

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s references to Bismarck serve a specific argumentative function: to demonstrate that even in the modern democratic era, the "blond beast" of [[noble|aristocratic]] predation can still manifest—not in literal violence, but in the form of commanding political will.

The key passage (073-002) presents Bismarck as a figure whom modern moral sentiment instinctively regards as "culpable"—not because he committed any specific crime, but because his very nature offends the democratic-egalitarian conscience. This is [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s genealogical point: the modern sense of "[[guilt]]" functions as a weapon of the weak against the strong, a way of pathologizing excellence.

## Nietzsche's Engagement

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s treatment of Bismarck is characterized by three interconnected claims:

1. **Bismarck as the [[noble|aristocratic]] type**: In a passage that groups Bismarck with [[Johann Wolfgang von Goethe|Goethe]] and [[Ludwig van Beethoven|Beethoven]], [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] identifies Bismarck as one of the rare modern individuals who embody the "successful individual type" (081-001).

These are the exceptions that prove the rule of modern degeneration—men whose instinctual health and creative power resist the leveling forces of democratic culture.

2. **Bismarck and the criminal type**: [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] provocatively links Bismarck to the "criminal" type discussed throughout Essay 2. The great statesman, like the great criminal, is driven by the same fundamental force—the [[will-to-power]]—which society labels "evil" when it operates outside sanctioned channels but "greatness" when it succeeds.

Bismarck represents the "successful" criminal, the one whose transgressions become the foundation of a new order.

3. **Bismarck's Slavic ancestry**: In a characteristically idiosyncratic genealogical move, [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] speculates about Bismarck's possible Slavic ancestry (082-002). This is not merely ethnic commentary; it serves [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s broader argument that greatness in modern [[europe|Europe]] often draws on pre-modern, non-Christian, and non-bourgeois sources.

The "Slavic" element represents a reservoir of untamed vitality beneath the surface of German respectability.

## Key Passages

> At this point we regard such natures as Bismarck as culpable out of carelessness—for the instinct of the community, the _morality of custom_, feels threatened by every exceptional individual. (073-002)

> There is more of the "successful type" before us in [[Johann Wolfgang von Goethe|Goethe]], [[Ludwig van Beethoven|Beethoven]], and Bismarck than in these racial aberrations that pass for modern men. (081-001)

> Consider the great figures of German history—for example, [[Martin Luther|Luther]], Niebuhr, Bismarck. Each of them drew on reserves of strength that the "good European" can no longer comprehend. (082-001)

> Bismarck a Slav. Let any one look upon the German Reich without prejudice: it is not the Germans who have built it, but the Slavic and Celtic elements that the German soil has absorbed and transformed. (082-002)
