# Zarathustra

## Overview

Zarathustra (Zoroaster) was the ancient Persian prophet who founded Zoroastrianism, one of the world's first monotheistic religions. In [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s thought, he is transfigured into the central persona of *Thus Spoke Zarathustra*.

He is the prophet of the death of God, the Übermensch, and the eternal return. In the *[[genealogy|Genealogy]]*, his name is invoked with reverence at two crucial moments.

## Role in the Genealogy

Zarathustra appears at the boundaries of the *[[genealogy|Genealogy]]* — in the Preface and at the close of the Third Essay. These invocations frame the entire work and signal that the *[[genealogy|Genealogy]]* is, in an important sense, preparatory to the world of *Zarathustra*: it clears the ground of moral prejudices so that Zarathustra's teaching can be heard.

> Take, for instance, my *Zarathustra*; I allow no one to pass muster as knowing that book, unless every single word therein has at some time wrought in him a profound wound... (P10-001)

> ...open alone to *Zarathustra, Zarathustra the godless*. (041-001)

## Nietzsche's Zarathustra

### The Godless Prophet

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] chose Zarathustra as his philosophical persona because the historical Zarathustra was the first to conceive of the world in terms of a cosmic moral struggle between good and evil. By placing his own teaching in the mouth of Zarathustra, [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] enacts a symbolic reversal.

The prophet who invented moral dualism now returns to *overcome* it. "Zarathustra the godless" — the epithet with which the *[[genealogy|Genealogy]]* closes — marks this transformation.

### The Difficulty of True Reading

In the Preface, [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] warns that his *Zarathustra* cannot be understood casually. To know the book, one must have been wounded by it — a formulation that echoes the *[[genealogy|Genealogy]]*'s own demand that its readers confront uncomfortable truths about the origins and functions of their moral beliefs.

### The Threshold Figure

The final words of the Third Essay place Zarathustra beyond the text. [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] breaks off his discourse precisely at the point where Zarathustra would speak. He acknowledges that the territory beyond the critique of morality belongs to a younger, more "future" voice. The *[[genealogy|Genealogy]]* thus ends in self-imposed silence, pointing toward the Zarathustra who alone can speak what comes next.

## Key Passages
