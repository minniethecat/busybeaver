# Greece

## Overview

In [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s *[[genealogy|Genealogy]] of Morality*, **Greece** is the cradle of the [[Master Morality|aristocratic]] value system — the cultural-geographical matrix within which the original equation of good = noble = powerful emerged. The Greece of [[homer|Homer]], of the tragic festivals, of the pre-Socratic [[philosopher|philosophers]], represents a world *before* the [[slave-morality|slave revolt in morals]] — a world whose values modern [[europe|Europe]] has not merely forgotten but actively inverted.

## Role in the Genealogy

### The Greek Moral Philosophers and the "Eyes of God"

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] invokes Greek moral philosophy as evidence for his genealogical thesis. The Greek [[philosopher|philosophers]] conceived of divine judgment — "the eyes of God" — not as a source of [[obligation|moral obligation]] but as a source of *spectacle*. The gods watched human suffering and found it *interesting*, even beautiful.

> The moral philosophers of Greece conceived the eyes of God as looking down upon the moral struggle, upon the heroism and self-torture of the virtuous — these gods were the *spectators* of the moral drama. (023-002)

This is a crucial contrast with the [[Christianity|Christian]] God, who watches in order to *judge*. The Greek gods are amoral spectators; their gaze does not create [[guilt]] but affirms the spectacle of human greatness.

### Goethe and Greece

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] invokes [[johann-wolfgang-von-goethe|Goethe]] — the German poet who most deeply assimilated the Greek spirit — as evidence that the Greek possibility is not entirely lost to modern [[europe|Europe]]. [[johann-wolfgang-von-goethe|Goethe]]'s relationship to Greece represents the highest aspiration of German culture: to recover, in modern form, the health and affirmation that Greece embodied.

> Goethe and Greece. All things are impelled toward the Olympian. (092-001)

This fragmentary late reference suggests that Greece, for [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]], is not merely historical but *exemplary* — a standard against which modern life can be measured and found wanting, but also a possibility that might, through figures like [[johann-wolfgang-von-goethe|Goethe]], be reactivated.
