# Germany

## Overview

In [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s *[[genealogy|Genealogy]] of Morality*, **Germany** (*Deutschland*) occupies an intensely ambivalent position. It is the nation that produced figures [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] regards as the highest possibilities of human culture — [[Johann Wolfgang von Goethe|Goethe]], [[ludwig-van-beethoven|Beethoven]], and, in some respects, [[martin-luther|Luther]]. Yet modern Germany is also, for [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]], the epicenter of a cultural [[disease|sickness]]: moral vulgarity, petty nationalism, and the intoxicated self-importance of the *Reich*.

> What future is prepared for us in Goethe, Beethoven, and Bismarck rather than in that "greatest" of all phenomena, the ascetic ideal? (081-001, paraphrased)

## Germany's Best Blood

### The Noble German Type

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] identifies a lineage of "manly" German figures who represent, in his view, the nation's highest potential:

> Good blood is still the best blood in Germany — for example, Luther, Niebuhr, Bismarck. (082-001, paraphrased)

These figures embody qualities [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] associates with [[noble|aristocratic]] virtue: independence of spirit, hardness, and the capacity to stand against the current of the age. They represent what Germany *could be* — a counter-force to the leveling tendencies of [[europe|Europe]]an modernity.

### Goethe as the Culmination

For [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]], [[Johann Wolfgang von Goethe]] represents the highest synthesis of German culture — a figure who absorbed the classical heritage, the [[renaissance|Renaissance]] spirit, and German depth without succumbing to any of the pathologies that characterize modern Germany:

> Goethe, in the most natural way in the world, possesses distinction. (078-002)

## The Pathology of Modern Germany

### Moral Vulgarity

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s critique of modern Germany centers on what he sees as a pervasive moral vulgarity — a coarseness of spirit that manifests in self-righteousness, [[anti-semites|anti-Semitism]], and the conflation of national power with cultural excellence:

> The revolting use in all present-day Germany of moral refuse; Dühring, the first apostle of revenge in Germany. (055-002, paraphrased)

### The Want of Wit

Germany lacks, according to [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]], the quality of *esprit* — the lightness, irony, and self-distance that characterize genuine cultural maturity:

> There has always been a want of wit in Germany, and mediocre heads attain there to the highest honours. (084-001)

### The Reformation as German Fate

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] sees the [[The Reformation|Reformation]] as a characteristically German event — one that, for all its apparent rebellion against [[rome|Rome]], actually represented a *deepening* of the ascetic [[ideals|ideal]] rather than an escape from it. [[martin-luther|Luther]]'s revolt preserved [[christianity|Christianity]] when the [[renaissance|Renaissance]] seemed poised to overcome it.

> When Luther saw the Rome of the Renaissance, he turned away in disgust. (060-001, paraphrased)
