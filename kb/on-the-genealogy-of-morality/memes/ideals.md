## Definition

In [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s *On the [[genealogy|Genealogy]] of Morality*, **ideals** are neither eternal truths nor mere illusions. They are *fabricated*—produced by specific psychological types under specific historical conditions to serve specific functions. The central task of [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s genealogy is to uncover "the mystery of how *ideals* are *manufactured* in this world" (013-001). Ideals, for [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]], are not what they claim to be; they are weapons, tools, and symptoms—expressions of the [[will-to-power|will to power]] of those who create and propagate them.

## Nietzsche's Analysis

### The Workshops of Ideal-Fabrication

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s most vivid image for the production of ideals is the workshop. "These workshops *where ideals are manufactured*—verily they reek with the fumes of lying and the stale air of hidden things!" (013-009). The fabrication of ideals is not a clean, rational process but a messy, psychological one—driven by [[Ressentiment|ressentiment]], revenge, and the [[will-to-power|will to power]]. Ideals are manufactured by specific types: the [[priests|priest]], the philosopher, the [[artist|art]]ist—each for their own purposes.

### Noble Ideals vs. Ascetic Ideals

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] distinguishes between two fundamentally different kinds of ideals. [[noble|Noble]] ideals—"the sublime hate, which creates ideals and changes old values to new" (008-001)—emerge from strength, self-affirmation, and the pathos of distance. Ascetic ideals, by contrast, emerge from weakness, self-denial, and the need to give meaning to suffering. When [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s interlocutor asks "Why do you talk of nobler ideals? Let us submit to the facts" (009-001), the challenge is precisely to recognize that not all ideals are created equal: some express health and plenitude, others express [[disease|sickness]] and lack.

### The Function of Ascetic Ideals

The [[Ascetic Ideal]], [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s central exhibit in Essay 3, serves a crucial function: it gives meaning to suffering. "Man would rather will *nothingness* than not will at all" (Essay 3 conclusion). The ascetic ideal provides an answer to the question "Why suffer?"—and in doing so, it preserves life even as it devalues it. "The ascetic ideal springs from the protective and healing instincts of a *degenerating* life" (Essay 3). This paradox—that the ascetic ideal is a form of life-preservation that works by negating life—is at the he[[artist|art]] of [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s analysis.

### The Artist's Relationship to Ideals

As [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] explores in his analysis of the [[Artist]], the relationship between [[artist|art]] and ideals is p[[artist|art]]icularly revealing. In [[Richard Wagner]]'s case, [[artist|art]] becomes a vehicle for [[ascetic-ideal|ascetic ideals]] when the [[artist|art]]ist borrows values from philosophy or religion rather than creating from his own fullness. "What is the meaning of [[ascetic-ideal|ascetic ideals]]? In [[artist|art]]ists, nothing, or too much" (042-001). The [[artist|art]]ist who serves ideals not his own illustrates the general principle: ideals are always fabricated by someone, for some purpose, under some condition of health or [[disease|sickness]].

## Key Passages

> The sublime hate, which creates ideals and changes old values to new, is a much greater and more powerful reality than the pretended "objectivity" of those who claim to merely describe what is. (008-001)

> One should not fail to gain an insight into—the mystery of how *ideals* are *manufactured* in this world. (013-001)

> These workshops *where ideals are manufactured*—verily they reek with the fumes of lying and the stale air of hidden things! (013-009)

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s [[genealogy|genealogical method]] is itself a tool for exposing the manufacturing process of ideals. By tracing ideals back to their psychological and [[health|physiological]] origins, genealogy strips them of their apparent necessity and reveals them as contingent products of specific wills under specific conditions. The question "What is the meaning of [[ascetic-ideal|ascetic ideals]]?" (042-001) is thus the genealogical question par excellence: it asks not whether ideals are true, but what they *do*—what function they serve, whose power they express, and what condition of life they indicate.
