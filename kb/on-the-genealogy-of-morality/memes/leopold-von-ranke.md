# Leopold von Ranke

## Overview

Leopold von Ranke (1795–1886) was a German historian and the founding figure of modern source-based historiography. His methodological principle — that history should be written *wie es eigentlich gewesen* ("as it actually happened") — established the professional standards of the modern historical discipline. [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] mentions Ranke in §19 of the Third Essay of the *[[genealogy|Genealogy]] of Morality*, in one of the most acid one-sentence character assassinations in his entire corpus. Ranke is introduced parenthetically, almost as an afterthought, but the afterthought is devastating: he is "that born classical advocate of every *causa fortior*, that cleverest of all the clever opportunists" (060-001). The founder of objective historiography is unmasked as the supreme practitioner of intellectual submission to power.

> The Germans, by the bye, have already produced the classic specimen of this toleration—they may well be allowed to reckon him as one of their own, in Leopold Ranke, that born classical advocate of every *causa fortior*, that cleverest of all the clever opportunists. (060-001)

## Role in the Genealogy

Ranke appears at the end of a long paragraph (060-001) that has been cataloguing the varieties of modern intellectual dishonesty. The paragraph moves from the general claim — that modern "good men" are incapable of truth-telling — through a series of concrete examples: [[thomas-moore|Thomas Moore]] burning [[lord-byron|Byron]]'s autobiography, Dr. Gwinner suppressing [[arthur-schopenhauer|Schopenhauer]]'s papers, Thayer abandoning his [[ludwig-van-beethoven|Beethoven]] biography. These are instances of scholars who could not "stand" the truth about their subjects.

The paragraph then pivots to a hypothetical: what if a "real psychologist" ([[hippolyte-taine|Taine]]) were to write about [[martin-luther|Luther]] with genuine fearlessness? And then, in the parenthetical close, Ranke: the German historical profession has already produced its "classic specimen" of the opposite — not fearlessness but toleration, not truth-telling but advocacy, not character but opportunism. The placement is deliberate. Ranke is the punchline.

## Nietzsche's Critique

### *Causa Fortior*: The Advocate of the Stronger Cause

The Latin phrase [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] uses — *causa fortior* — is precise and lethal. In [[law|Roman law]], the *causa fortior* is the stronger case, the side that is likely to win. An advocate of the *causa fortior* is not a seeker of truth but a lawyer for the winning side. [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s charge is that Ranke's vaunted objectivity is, in practice, the habit of aligning one's historical judgments with whatever force has already prevailed. Ranke does not tell the truth about history; he tells the history that power wants to hear.

This charge attacks the very foundation of Ranke's reputation. Ranke's methodological revolution was built on the claim that rigorous source criticism could free history from p[[artist|art]]isanship, from the "myth and legend" that had plagued earlier historiography. [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] turns this claim inside out: the methodological apparatus of objectivity — the archives, the footnotes, the insistence on primary sources — is not a liberation from p[[artist|art]]isanship but its most sophisticated disguise. Ranke's method does not transcend power; it serves it, more effectively than any openly p[[artist|art]]isan historian ever could.

### "Toleration" as Cowardice

The word "toleration" (*Toleranz*) in the passage is key. [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] has been discussing the "prudent toleration of force" that he contrasts with [[hippolyte-taine|Taine]]'s fearlessness. Ranke is the "classic specimen" of this toleration — not the active courage of the truth-teller but the passive accommodation of the scholar who adjusts his conclusions to what the powerful will accept. Toleration, in this sense, is not a virtue of liberal pluralism but a vice of intellectual cowardice. Ranke tolerates the *causa fortior* because he lacks the strength to challenge it.

This critique extends beyond Ranke personally. The entire German university system, in [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s view, trains scholars in this form of toleration — the habilitation, the *Fachzeitschrift*, the scholarly consensus, all the mechanisms of professional legitimation that reward the "clever opportunist" and punish the fearless. Ranke is not an aberration; he is the system's [[ideals|ideal]] product.

### Ranke as Anti-Genealogist

There is a deeper methodological opposition at work. Rankean historiography and [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]an genealogy are rival conceptions of what history is for. Ranke's "as it actually happened" assumes that the past is a stable object that can be neutrally described. [[genealogy|Genealogy]] assumes that the past is a field of struggle whose meaning is never settled — that every historical account is an intervention in present conflicts, and that the pretense of neutrality is itself a move in the game. Ranke is, in this sense, the anti-genealogist: the historian who denies that he is taking sides, and by that denial takes the side of whoever has already won.

The *causa fortior* formulation captures this perfectly. To be "objective" about a past in which the strong have always triumphed over the weak is, in practice, to write history as a justification of the victors. Ranke's famous phrase — "every epoch is immediate to God" — is the theological version of the same move: if every epoch stands in direct relation to God, then no epoch can be judged, and the historian's task is merely to record what God has permitted. [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s response, implicit but unmistakable, is that God is dead and the historian who still writes as if every epoch were immediate to Him is not pious but dishonest.
