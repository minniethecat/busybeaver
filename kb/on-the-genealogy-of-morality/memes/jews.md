# Jews

## Overview

In [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s *[[genealogy|Genealogy]] of Morality*, the **Jews** occupy a position of world-historical significance: they are the "[[priests|priest]]ly nation" (*das [[priests|priest]]erliche Volk*) who, through an act of [[revenge|spiritual revenge]], accomplished the most consequential [[transvaluation|revaluation of values]] in Western history. [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s treatment of the Jews is deliberately provocative — he ascribes to them both the "slave revolt in morals" and, paradoxically, the greatest intellectual achievement in the history of value-creation.

> It was the Jews who, in opposition to the aristocratic equation (good = aristocratic = beautiful = happy = loved by the gods), dared with a terrifying logic to suggest the contrary equation. (007-001)

## Nietzsche's Analysis

### The Priestly Nation

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] frames the Jews as the archetypal [[priests|priest]]ly people — a nation whose historical experience of powerlessness cultivated the psychological conditions for [[ressentiment]] on a civilizational scale. Unlike the [[Aristocracy|aristocratic]] conquerors who impose values through strength, the Jews redefined values through the inversion of the [[noble|aristocratic]] equation.

> The Jews — that priestly nation which in the last resort was able to satisfy itself on its enemies and conquerors only by a radical revaluation of their values, that is, by an act of the *most spiritual revenge*. (007-001)

### The Slave Revolt in Morals

The "slave revolt in morals" (*Sklavenaufstand in der Moral*) begins, in [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s genealogy, with the Jews. This is not a political revolt but a spiritual one — a transformation of the criteria by which "good" and "evil" are judged. What the aristocrats called "good" (power, beauty, strength), the Jews called "evil"; what the aristocrats called "bad" (weakness, suffering, humility), the Jews called "good."

> The slave revolt in morals begins with the Jews: a revolt which has behind it a history of two millennia, and which at the present day has only moved out of our sight, because it — has achieved victory. (007-001)

### Rome vs. Judea

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] dramatizes the conflict as a cosmic struggle between two value systems: [[rome|Rome]] ([[noble|aristocratic]], life-affirming, [[master-morality|master morality]]) and Judea ([[priests|priest]]ly, life-denying, [[slave-morality|slave morality]]). The triumph of Judea over [[rome|Rome]] — of Christian values over pagan ones — is the defining event of Western moral history.

> Rome found in the Jew the incarnation of the unnatural, as though it were its antithetical *monstrum*. (015-001)

> To *three Jews*, as we know, and *one Jewess*, it was granted to conquer Rome through the Christian faith. (015-002)

## Nietzsche's Ambivalence

It is essential to recognize [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s complex attitude. The Jews are not simply villains in his story. They are the *creators* of the most sophisticated system of values ever devised. Their act of revaluation required a "terrifying logic" and "most [[revenge|spiritual revenge]]" — a feat of intellectual creativity that [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]], in other moments, seems to regard with something approaching admiration:

> In that case no nation on earth has had a more world-historic mission. (009-001)
