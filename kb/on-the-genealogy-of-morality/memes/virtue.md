## Definition

In [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s *On the [[genealogy|Genealogy]] of Morality*, **virtue** is never what it claims to be. What passes for virtue—pity, humility, patience, chastity—is, upon genealogical examination, revealed as something else: sublimated [[cruelty]], self-deception, the internalized violence of the "morality of custom," or the [[Ressentiment|ressentiment]] of the weak.

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s project is to expose the "virtues" of the [[slave-morality|slave morality]] as inverted vices and to recover the original meaning of virtue as excellence, strength, and self-affirmation.

## Nietzsche's Analysis

### Virtue in Sacerdotal Societies

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] is p[[artist|art]]icularly suspicious of the virtues cultivated in [[priests|priest]]ly societies. "In sacerdotal societies every element is on a more dangerous scale, not merely cures and remedies, but also pride, revenge, cunning, exaltation, love, ambition, virtue, morbidity" (006-001).

The [[priests|priest]]ly cultivation of virtue is, for [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]], a dangerous enterprise—not because virtue itself is dangerous, but because what the [[priests|priest]] calls "virtue" is often a weapon. The virtue of the [[ascetic-priest|priestly type]] is the virtue of the [[Ascetic Priest|ascetic priest]]: self-denial as power, humility as domination.

### The Slave Revaluation of Virtue

The [[Slave Morality|slave revolt]] in morality fundamentally transformed the meaning of virtue. The weak "also talk of the 'love of their enemies' and sweat thereby" (013-005). What the noble called "good"—strength, pride, self-assertion—the slave revalues as "evil." And what the slave cannot possess—power, wealth, beauty—is reinterpreted as morally suspect.

The "virtues" of the slave—meekness, poverty of spirit, turning the other cheek—are not freely chosen excellences but the necessary consolations of the powerless. They are "the interpretation of weakness as freedom, of their actual *being thus and thus* as *merit*" (012-001).

### The Morality of Custom

Behind all later moral virtues lies what [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] calls the "morality of custom" (*Sittlichkeit der Sitte*)—the prehistoric system through which humanity was made "calculable" through the brutal enforcement of norms. Virtue, in its earliest form, was not moral goodness but conformity to custom, enforced through public [[cruelty]].

The "virtuous" individual of the morality of custom is the one who has been successfully tamed—whose instincts have been "internalized" and turned against himself. "Virtue without witnesses was something quite inconceivable" (023-002 area): virtue was originally a public performance, not an inner state.

### Authentic Virtue

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] does not reject all virtue—he seeks to recover its original, pre-moral meaning. The [[Noble|noble]] type possesses virtues in the classical sense: courage, generosity, truthfulness. But these are not "moral" virtues; they are expressions of strength, plenitude, and self-affirmation.

The noble's virtue is not a [[obligation|duty]] but an overflow. The noble is truthful not because he "ought" to be but because it is beneath him to lie. This is virtue as excellence (*arete*), not virtue as obedience.

## Key Passages

> In sacerdotal societies every element is on a more dangerous scale, not merely cures and remedies, but also pride, revenge, cunning, exaltation, love, ambition, virtue, morbidity. (006-001)

> That most sublime specimen of self-deception, the interpretation of weakness as freedom, of their actual *being thus and thus* as *merit*. (012-001)

> They also talk of the 'love of their enemies' and sweat thereby. (013-005)

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s critique of virtue is not a rejection of excellence but an exposure of counterfeit virtue. The "virtue" that springs from weakness, that demands the negation of the strong, that disguises [[ressentiment]] as righteousness—this is what genealogy unmasks. What remains, after the unmasking, is the possibility of a virtue that says "yes" to life rather than "no."
