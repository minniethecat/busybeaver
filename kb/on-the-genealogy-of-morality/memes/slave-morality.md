# Slave Morality

## Definition

**Slave morality** (*Sklavenmoral*) is [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s term for the reactive value system that originates with the oppressed, the weak, and the powerless — those who, unable to assert themselves through action, redefine their condition as virtuous through an act of [[Ressentiment]]. Unlike the spontaneous self-affirmation of [[Master Morality|master morality]], slave morality begins with a "No" to what is outside, different, and powerful — labeling it "evil" — before it can say "Yes" to itself as "good."

> The revolt of the slaves in morals begins in the very principle of *ressentiment* becoming creative and giving birth to values. (010-001)

## Nietzsche's Genealogy

### The Jewish Origin

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] locates the historical origin of slave morality in the [[Jews|Jewish people]] — the "[[priests|priest]]ly nation" whose experience of political subjugation drove them to effect a "radical revaluation" of the [[noble|aristocratic]] values of their conquerors.

> It was the Jews who, in opposition to the aristocratic equation (good = aristocratic = beautiful = happy = loved by the gods), dared with a terrifying logic to suggest the contrary equation: "the wretched alone are the good; the poor, the impotent, the lowly alone are the good." (007-001)

### The Victory of Slave Morality

The triumph of slave morality is, for [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]], the defining event of Western civilization. What began as a defensive spiritual maneuver by a subjugated people became, through [[christianity|Christianity]], the dominant moral framework of [[europe|Europe]]. The values of the weak — humility, pity, [[sacrifice|self-sacrifice]] — became the universal standard.

> The slave revolt in morals begins with the Jews: a revolt which has behind it a history of two millennia, and which at the present day has only moved out of our sight, because it — has achieved victory. (007-001)

### Slave Morality in Modernity

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] sees modern democratic and egalitarian values as the secularized continuation of slave morality. The demand for equality, the valorization of pity, the suspicion of excellence — all are, in his analysis, descendants of the original slave revolt:

> The dwarfing and levelling of the European man lurks *our* greatest peril. (011-001)

## Contrast with Master Morality

| Aspect | [[Master Morality]] | Slave Morality |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|
| Origin | Spontaneous self-affirmation | Reactive negation |
| Primary category | "Good" vs. "Bad" | "Good" vs. "Evil" |
| "Good" means | [[noble|Noble]], powerful, beautiful | Humble, meek, suffering |
| "Evil" means | (not a primary category) | The powerful, the rich, the strong |
| Psychology | Active, forgetful, proud | Reactive, brooding, calculating |
| Historical carriers | [[Aristocracy]] | [[Jews]], [[Priests]], Christians |
