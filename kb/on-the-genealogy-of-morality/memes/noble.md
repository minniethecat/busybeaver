## Definition

In [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s *On the [[genealogy|Genealogy]] of Morality*, the **noble** mode of valuation is the original, spontaneous form of value-creation. It is characterized not by reaction against an external enemy but by an affirmative "yes" to oneself.

The noble individual says: "I am good, therefore you are bad." This is fundamentally different from the [[Slave Morality|slave]] mode, which says: "You are evil, therefore I am good."

The noble valuation emerges from what [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] calls "the pathos of [[aristocracy|nobility]] and distance" (002-002)—the fundamental instinct of a higher dominant race that distinguishes itself from and looks down upon everything base and low.

## Nietzsche's Analysis

### The Pathos of Distance

The origin of the antithesis between "good" and "bad" lies, for [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]], in the "pathos of [[aristocracy|nobility]] and distance, the chronic and despotic *esprit de corps* and fundamental instinct of a higher dominant race coming into association with a meaner race, an 'under race'" (002-002).

This pathos of distance is not a moral judgment but a pre-moral, almost [[health|physiological]] sense of superiority. The noble type experiences its own power, beauty, and fullness of life as "good," and everything contrary—the weak, the common, the vulgar—as merely "bad" by contrast.

### The Naming Right

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] discovers through etymological analysis that the original concept of "good" was not altruistic or moral but social and aristocratic. The "good" were the nobles, the [[aristocracy|masters]], those with the right to name.

"Everywhere 'aristocrat,' 'noble' (in the social sense), is the root idea out of which 'good' in the sense of 'aristocratic in soul,' 'noble in soul,' necessarily developed" (004-001/005 area).

The [[aristocracy|masters]]' right of giving names is so fundamental that [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] suggests we should "grasp the right of the [[aristocracy|masters]] of giving names as going so far that we could permit ourselves to grasp the origin of language itself as the expression of the power of the [[aristocracy|masters]]" (002 area).

### Spontaneous Self-Affirmation

The noble mode of valuation is fundamentally spontaneous, not reactive. "The aristocrat's system of values... acts and grows spontaneously, it merely seeks its antithesis in order to pronounce a more grateful and exultant 'yes' to its own self" (010-001).

Its negative conception, "low," "vulgar," "bad," is merely a pale late-born foil in comparison with its positive and fundamental conception, "saturated as it is with life and passion" (010-001).

The noble does not need an enemy to define himself; his "bad" is an afterthought, a pale contrast that only highlights his own radiance.

### Contrast with Slave Valuation

The noble valuation stands in stark opposition to the [[Slave Morality|slave]] or [[Ressentiment|ressentiment]]-based valuation. Where the noble says "I am good, therefore you are bad," the slave says "You are evil, therefore I am good."

The noble's "bad" is a secondary, comparative judgment; the slave's "evil" is primary and constitutive. This structural difference—affirmation first vs. negation first—is the core of [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s analysis of the two moral systems. The noble creates values; the slave inverts them.

| Dimension | Noble Valuation | Slave Valuation |
|-----------|----------------|-----------------|
| Direction | Affirmative ("yes" to self first) | Reactive ("no" to other first) |
| Primary judgment | "We are good" | "They are evil" |
| Secondary judgment | "They are bad" (afterthought) | "We are good" (derivative) |
| Psychological basis | Plenitude, power, health | Lack, weakness, [[ressentiment]] |
| Historical type | [[Master Morality]] | Slave morality |

## Key Passages

> The pathos of nobility and distance, as I have said, the chronic and despotic *esprit de corps* and fundamental instinct of a higher dominant race coming into association with a meaner race, an "under race," this is the origin of the antithesis of good and bad. (002-002)

> The contrary is the case when we come to the aristocrat's system of values: it acts and grows spontaneously, it merely seeks its antithesis in order to pronounce a more grateful and exultant "yes" to its own self;—its negative conception, "low," "vulgar," "bad," is merely a pale late-born foil in comparison with its positive and fundamental conception (saturated as it is with life and passion). (010-001)

The noble, for [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]], is characterized by a fullness that overflows: "All [[master-morality|noble morality]] grows out of a triumphant yes said to oneself" (010 area). The noble type does not calculate, does not hold grudges, and possesses "no memory for insults and meannesses" (010-002 area)—qualities that [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] associates with [[Mirabeau]] and the old noble [[master-morality|aristocratic morality]].
