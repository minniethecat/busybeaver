## Definition

In [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]''s genealogy, **responsibility** (*Verantwortlichkeit*) is not a natural human capacity but a historical achievement — the end-product of the longest and most violent training humanity has undergone. To be responsible is to be *calculable*, *regular*, *necessary* — able to stand surety for oneself as a future, to answer for one''s past, and to make promises that bind across time.

> The task of breeding an animal that can *promise* — is not this precisely the paradoxical task which nature has set itself with regard to man? (018-001)

## Nietzsche''s Analysis

### The Morality of Custom as Breeding Ground

Responsibility is bred into humanity through the "[[virtue|morality of custom]]" (*Sittlichkeit der Sitte*) — the long prehistory of social discipline that punishes deviation and rewards conformity. This process creates the *calculable* human being:

> The long history of the origin of *responsibility*. That task of breeding an animal that can promise, includes, as we have already grasped, as its condition and preparation, the more immediate task of first *making* man to a certain extent necessary, uniform, like among like, regular, and consequently calculable. (018-001, paraphrase)

### Responsibility and the Freedom of the Will

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] explicitly rejects the metaphysical interpretation of responsibility — the idea that the "evil-doer" could have done otherwise and is therefore morally culpable:

> Punishment was *never* based on the responsibility of the evil-doer for his action... The thought that the evil-doer *deserved* punishment because he could have acted otherwise is a very late and refined form of human judgment. (020-002, paraphrase)

The "freedom of the will" is a theological invention, not a genealogical fact. Responsibility does not presuppose free will; it *produces* the feeling of freedom.

### Responsibility and the Sovereign Individual

The sovereign individual represents the *ripest fruit* of the breeding of responsibility — the human being whose self-mastery is so complete that responsibility has become instinct:

> The proud knowledge of the extraordinary privilege of *responsibility*, this consciousness of this rare freedom, this power over oneself and over fate, has sunk into his deepest depth and become instinct, the dominating instinct. (018-001)

This is responsibility not as burden but as *privilege* — the right to make promises, to stand as guarantor of one''s own future.

### Responsibility and Bad Conscience

The dark side of responsibility is [[bad-conscience|bad conscience]]. The same process that creates the calculable individual also creates self-torture:

> The meaning of guilt, responsibility, consideration — are these born, let us ask, in the sphere of the reactive feelings? (033-001, paraphrase)

Responsibility becomes [[guilt]] when the [[freedom|instinct of freedom]], blocked from external discharge, turns inward. The responsible self is also the self that can be *[[guilt]]y* — the self that owes.

## Key Passages

> The task of breeding an animal that can *promise* — is not this precisely the paradoxical task which nature has set itself with regard to man? Is not this the real problem of man? (018-001)

> Enough will for responsibility, enough freedom of the will, to be able to answer for oneself and to stand surety for one''s own future. (051-002, paraphrase)

> Punishment was *never* based on the responsibility of the evil-doer for his action, and was never based on the presupposition that only the guilty should be punished. (020-002, paraphrase)
