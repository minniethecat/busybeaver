# Will to Truth

## Definition

The **will to truth** is, for [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]], not a pure, disinterested faculty of reason but a historical and psychological phenomenon that requires genealogical investigation. Rather than accepting truth-seeking as self-evidently valuable, [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] asks what the value of this will to truth itself might be. His diagnosis is that the unconditional will to truth — the insistence that truth must be pursued at any cost, including the cost of life itself — is a disguised form of the [[Ascetic Ideal]].

> ...illusions, as "knowledge," as "truth," as "being" as an escape from the reality of the senses. (058-001)

## Nietzsche's Analysis

### The Value of Truth Questioned

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s [[genealogy|genealogical method]] reaches its most radical moment when it turns upon the very value of truth itself. If every other [[ideals|ideal]] has been unmasked as a form of [[will-to-power|will to power]], why should the [[ideals|ideal]] of truth be exempt? The unconditional will to truth is, he argues, the latest and most refined expression of the [[Ascetic Ideal]] — the belief that there is something higher than life that life must serve.

> This truth came insidiously enough to the consciousness of the artist of violence. (031-001)

### Truth as the Core of the Ascetic Ideal

In the Third Essay, [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] traces how the will to truth developed from the [[ascetic-priest|ascetic priest]]'s discipline of self-denial. The scholar, the scientist, the "free spirit" — all believe they have overcome the ascetic [[ideals|ideal]] when in fact they embody its most sublimated form: the belief in truth as something that transcends human needs, desires, and life itself.

> ...where the life instinct fixes truth with the greatest positiveness... (053-001)

### The Self-Sublation of Truth

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s most radical insight is that the will to truth, pushed to its limit, eventually turns against itself. [[christianity|Christianity]]'s cultivation of truthfulness leads to its own destruction when that truthfulness is applied to the moral presuppositions of [[christianity|Christianity]] itself. The will to truth becomes "conscious of itself as a problem."

> ...reactive feelings, our love of truth compels us drastically to investigate... (027-001)

### Beyond Unconditional Truth

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] does not reject truth altogether but rejects the unconditional valuation of truth over life. His [[interpretation|perspectivism]] is precisely the attempt to preserve truth-seeking while abandoning the ascetic [[religion|faith]] in a truth beyond life — a truth that would stand "over the gateway of existence" as a moral imperative.

> The martyr, "to suffer for truth" — he leaves all that to the ambitious and the actors of the spirit. (049-001)
