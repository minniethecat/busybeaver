## Definition

In [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s *On the [[genealogy|Genealogy]] of Morality*, **health** is not merely a biological state but a fundamental condition that determines the character of all valuation. The healthy and the sick, [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] argues, produce fundamentally different kinds of values. The healthy affirm life, strength, and self-overcoming; the sick produce [[ideals|ideal]]s that devalue life, body, and worldly existence. This physiological-psychological framework is central to [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s entire genealogical project: moral systems are symptoms of underlying conditions of health or [[disease|sickness]].

## Nietzsche's Analysis

### Health as the Ground of Values

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] repeatedly insists that philosophical and moral positions are expressions of physiological states. "Every philosophy," he suggests, "is the unconscious memoir of its author." The opposition between health and [[disease|sickness]] is not merely one theme among many—it is the explanatory framework that underpins the entire genealogy. The [[Master Morality|noble]] valuation springs from "robust health" (017-001), while the [[Slave Morality|slave]] [[transvaluation|revaluation of values]] is the product of physiological and psychological [[disease|sickness]].

### Great Health

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] contrasts ordinary health with what he calls "great health"—a condition of overflowing vitality that goes beyond mere absence of [[disease|illness]]. "We need," he writes, "habituation to sharp, rare air, to winter wanderings, to ice and mountain heights in every sense... we need a kind of sublime wickedness, a supremely self-confident mischievousness which is the appanage of great health" (040-001). Great health is creative, dangerous, and affirmative—the opposite of the "healthy sensuality" of [[ludwig-feuerbach|Feuerbach]] that [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] dismisses as a "motto" (044-001).

### The Danger of the Sick

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] argues that the greatest danger to the healthy comes not from external threats but from the sick themselves. "It is not from the strongest that the greatest harm comes to the strong, but from the weakest" (055-001). The sick, driven by [[Ressentiment|ressentiment]], develop a "tyranny over the healthy" (055-002) through the propagation of [[ascetic-ideal|ascetic ideals]]. The [[Ascetic Priest]] channels this [[disease|sickness]] into a system of values that inverts health into [[guilt]] and [[disease|sickness]] into virtue.

### The Corruption of Health

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] traces how [[ascetic-ideal|ascetic ideals]] have systematically corrupted health across [[europe|Europe]]an history. "The ascetic [[ideals|ideal]] has corrupted not only health and taste, there are also things that it has corrupted which are far higher" (064-001). The [[priests|priest]]ly reevaluation of values represents a physiological poisoning: "He has corrupted the health of the soul" (063-001). [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s genealogy is, at its deepest level, a diagnosis of how [[disease|sickness]] came to dominate health in the human value-system.

| Condition | Produces | Values | Historical Expression |
|-----------|---------|--------|----------------------|
| Health / Great health | Spontaneous self-affirmation | Strength, beauty, power | [[Noble]] / [[Master Morality]] |
| Sickness | Reactive negation | Asceticism, pity, self-denial | [[Slave Morality]] / [[Christianity]] |

## Key Passages

> It is not from the strongest that the greatest harm comes to the strong, but from the weakest... The sick are the greatest danger for the healthy; it is not from the strongest that harm comes to the strong. (055-001)

> We need habituation to sharp, rare air, to winter wanderings, to ice and mountain heights in every sense... we need a kind of sublime wickedness, a supremely self-confident mischievousness which is the appanage of great health. (040-001)

[[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s physiological approach extends to the evaluation of entire civilizations: "The ascetic [[ideals|ideal]] has corrupted not only health and taste" (064-001) but the very capacity for honest self-assessment. The "[[virtue|morality of custom]]" (050-003 area), which [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] identifies as the earliest form of social control, operated by making humanity "calculable" through the brutal re-shaping of physiological instincts—what he calls the "[[pain|mnemotechnics of pain]]" (019 area).
