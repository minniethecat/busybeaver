# Guilt

## Definition

In [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s *[[genealogy|Genealogy]] of Morality*, **guilt** (*Schuld*) is not a primitive moral intuition but a historical construction built upon the material concept of debt (*Schulden*). The German language preserves this link — *Schuld* means both "guilt" and "debt" — and [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]] exploits this etymological connection to argue that the feeling of [[obligation|moral obligation]], of "owing" something to God or society, derives from the most primitive [[creditor-debtor-relationship|contractual relationship]]: that between creditor and debtor.

> The concept of "guilt" had its origin in the very material concept of "debt." (020-002)

## Nietzsche's Genealogy

### From Debt to Guilt

The primitive form of [[debt|obligation]] was not moral but economic: the debtor who failed to repay gave the creditor the right to extract compensation through [[Punishment]], including the infliction of physical suffering. The feeling of "owing" — originally a material relation — was gradually internalized and moralized, becoming the sense of guilt that haunts the modern conscience.

> Have these current genealogists of morals ever allowed themselves to have even the remotest suspicion that the main moral concept of "guilt" (*Schuld*) took its origin from the very material concept of "debt" (*Schulden*)? (020-002)

### The Internalization of Guilt

The transition from external debt to internal guilt parallels the development of [[Bad Conscience]]. As the human animal was forcibly pacified within society, the instinct for [[cruelty]] — previously discharged outward upon debtors and enemies — turned inward upon the self. Guilt is the psychological form this self-directed [[cruelty]] takes: the self punishing itself for an unpayable debt.

> The advent of the Christian God as the maximum God hitherto attained, was accompanied by the maximum of the feeling of guilt on earth. (034-001)

### The Christian Intensification

[[christianity|Christianity]], in [[friedrich-nietzsche|Nietzsche]]'s account, represents the maximum exploitation of guilt. The doctrine of original sin transforms every human being into an infinite debtor, owing a debt that can never be repaid. The [[Crucifixion of Jesus|crucifixion]] — God sacrificing himself to himself to pay humanity's debt — is the theological expression of this infinite guilt economy.

> Now the worst and most painful secret of the conscience is once more removed, thrown outside — man has again a *scapegoat*. (039-005, paraphrased)
