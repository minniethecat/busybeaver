# London

London, the capital of England and the British Empire, appears in Ernest Poole's novel
*His Family* (1917) as a significant international reference point. The novel, set
primarily in New York City, references London in the context of transatlantic travel
and the unfolding events of the Great War.

## Role in the Novel

London serves primarily as a destination and as a symbol of the Old World. A character
is described as having "lived in London and Paris, Munich and Rome, always in queer old
lodgings" (005-105), establishing the cosmopolitan experience that contrasts with the
Gale family's rooted New York existence.

## Significance

- **Transatlantic Connection**: London represents the cultural and political ties
  between America and Europe that were central to the era
- **Wartime Context**: As the novel progresses into the World War I period, London
  becomes associated with the distant but growing conflict
- **Old World Symbol**: For the American Gale family, London embodies the traditions
  and institutions of the Old World that the younger generation engages with
