# Germany

Germany appears in Ernest Poole's novel *His Family* (1917) primarily in the context
of World War I. As the novel was written and set during the war years, Germany serves
as a symbol of the distant European conflict that increasingly intrudes upon the
consciousness and daily lives of the Gale family in New York.

## Role in the Novel

Germany is referenced in connection with the Great War that forms the backdrop of
the novel's later chapters. The war's impact on American society — from political
debates about neutrality to the personal anxieties of families with members abroad —
is filtered through the Gale family's experiences.

## Significance

- **Wartime Antagonist**: Germany represents the distant enemy in the conflict that
  reshapes the world of the novel
- **Generational Impact**: The war, and Germany's role in it, directly affects the
  younger generation of the Gale family through enlistment and political engagement
- **Historical Context**: The novel captures the moment when America's isolation
  from European affairs was ending, with Germany serving as the focal point of
  that transformation
