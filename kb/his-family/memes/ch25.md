(025-001) [[roger-gale|Roger]] saw little of [[deborah-gale|Deborah]] in the weeks that followed. She was gathering

(025-002) her forces for the long struggle she saw ahead. And his own worries filled

(025-003) his mind. On his [[home|house]] he succeeded in borrowing five thousand dollars at

(025-004) ten per cent, and in [[rogers-office|his office]] he worked out a scheme along the lines of

(025-005) Deborah's plan. At first it was only a struggle to save the remnants of

(025-006) what was left. Later the tide began to turn, new business came into the

(025-007) office again. But only a little, and then it stopped. Hard times were here

(025-008) for the winter.

(025-009) Soon [[edith-sidney|Edith]] would come with the [[childhood|children]]. He wondered how sensible she would

(025-010) be. It was going to mean a daily fight to make ends meet, he told himself,

(025-011) and guiltily he decided not to let his daughter know how matters stood in

(025-012) his office. Take care of your own flesh and blood, and then be generous as

(025-013) you please--that had always been his way. And now Deborah had upset it by

(025-014) her emotional appeal. "How dramatic she is at times!" he reflected in

(025-015) annoyance. "Just lets herself out and enjoys herself!" He grew angry at her

(025-016) interference, and more than once he resolved to shut down. But back in the

(025-017) office, before those watchful faces, still again he would put it off.

(025-018) "Wait a little. We'll see," he thought.

(025-019) * * * * *

(025-020) In the meantime, in this interplay, these shifting lights and shadows which

(025-021) played upon the history of the life of Roger's [[family|home]], there came to him a

(025-022) diversion from an unexpected source. [[laura-sloane|Laura]] and [[harold-sloane|Harold]] returned from [[europe|abroad]].

(025-023) Soon after landing they came to [[the-gale-home|the house]], and talking fast and eagerly

(025-024) they told how they had eluded [[the-great-war|the war]].

(025-025) For them it had been a glorious game. In Venice in early August, Harold

(025-026) had seen a chance for a big stroke of business. He had a friend who lived

(025-027) in [[rome|Rome]], an [[italy|Italian]] close to his government. At once they had joined

(025-028) forces, worked day and night, pulled wires, used money judiciously here and

(025-029) there, and so had secured large orders for munitions from the U.S.A. Then

(025-030) to get back to God's country! There came the hitch, they were too late.

(025-031) Naples, Genoa, and Milan, all were filled with tourist mobs. They took a

(025-032) train for [[paris|Paris]], and reaching [[new-york-city|the city]] just a week before the end of the

(025-033) German drive they found it worse than Italy. But there Hal had a special

(025-034) pull--and by the use of those wits of his, not to be downed by refusals, he

(025-035) got passage at last for Laura, himself and his new Italian partner. At

(025-036) midnight, making their way across the panic-stricken city, and at the

(025-037) station struggling through a wild and half crazed multitude of men and

(025-038) [[womens-roles|women]] and children, they boarded a train and went rushing westward right

(025-039) along the edge of the storm. To the north the Germans were so close that

(025-040) Laura was sure she could hear the big guns. The train kept stopping to take

(025-041) on troops. At dawn some twenty wounded men came crowding into their very

(025-042) car, bloody and dirty, pale and worn, but gaily smiling at the pain, and

(025-043) saying, "Ça n'fait rien, madame." Later Harold opened his flask for some

(025-044) splendid Breton soldier boys just going into action. And they stood up with

(025-045) flashing eyes and shouted out the Marseillaise, while Laura shivered and

(025-046) thrilled with delight.

(025-047) "I nearly kissed them all!" she cried.

(025-048) Roger greatly enjoyed the evening. He had heard so much of the horrors of

(025-049) war. Here was something different, something bright and vibrant with youth

(025-050) and adventure! Here at last was the thrill of war, the part he had always

(025-051) read about!

(025-052) He glanced now and then at Deborah and was annoyed by what he saw. For

(025-053) although she said nothing and forced a smile, he could easily tell by the

(025-054) set of her lips that Deborah thoroughly disapproved. All right, that was

(025-055) her way, he thought. But this was Laura's way, shedding the gloom and the

(025-056) tragic side as a duck will shed water off its back, a duck with bright new

(025-057) plumage fresh from the shops of the Rue de la Paix and taking some pleasure

(025-058) out of life! What an ardent [[beauty|gleaming beauty]] she was, he thought as he

(025-059) watched this daughter of his. And underneath his enjoyment, too, though

(025-060) Roger would not have admitted it, was a sense of relief in the news that at

(025-061) least one man in the family was growing rich instead of poor. Already Hal

(025-062) and his partner--a fascinating creature according to Laura's

(025-063) description--were fast equipping shrapnel mills. Plainly they expected a

(025-064) tremendous rush of business. And no matter how you felt about war, the word

(025-065) "profits" at least had a pleasant sound.

(025-066) "How has the war hit you, sir?" Harold asked his father-in-law.

(025-067) "Oh, so-so, I'll get on, my boy," was Roger's quiet answer. For Harold was

(025-068) not quite the kind he would ever like to ask for aid. Still, if the worst

(025-069) came to the worst, he would have someone to turn to.

(025-070) * * * * *

(025-071) Long after they had left the house, he kept thinking over all they had

(025-072) said. What an amazing time they had had, the two young scalawags.

(025-073) Deborah was still in the room. As she sat working at her desk, her back was

(025-074) turned and she did not speak. But little by little her father's mood

(025-075) changed. Of course she was right, he admitted. For now they were gone, the

(025-076) spell they had cast was [[loss|losing]] a part of its glamor. Yes, their talk had

(025-077) been pretty raw. Sheer unthinking selfishness, a bold rush for plunder and

(025-078) a dash to get away, trampling over people half crazed, women and children

(025-079) in panicky crowds, and leaving behind them, so to speak, Laura's joyous

(025-080) rippling laugh over their own success in the game. Yes, there was no

(025-081) denying the fact that Hal was rushing headlong into a savage dangerous

(025-082) game, a scramble and a gamble, with adventurers from all over Europe

(025-083) gathering here and making a little world of their own. He would [[work-and-vocation|work]] and

(025-084) live at a feverish pitch, and Laura would go it as hard as he. Roger

(025-085) thought he could see their winter ahead. How they would pile up money and

(025-086) spend!

(025-087) All at once, as though some figure silent and invisible were standing close

(025-088) beside him, from far back in his childhood a memory flashed into his mind

(025-089) of a keen and clear October night, when Roger, a little shaver of nine, had

(025-090) stood with his mother in front of the farmhouse and listened to the faint

(025-091) sharp roll of a single drum far down in the valley. And his mother's grip

(025-092) had hurt his hand, and a lump had risen in his throat--as Dan, his oldest

(025-093) brother, had marched away with his company of [[new-hampshire-farm|New Hampshire]] mountain boys.

(025-094) "We are coming, Father Abraham, three hundred thousand more." Dan had been

(025-095) killed at Shiloh.

(025-096) And it must be like that now in [[france|France]]. No, he did not like the look which

(025-097) he had seen on Laura's face as she had talked about the war and the fat

(025-098) profits to be made. Was this all we Yankees had to say to the people over

(025-099) in Europe?

(025-100) Frowning and glancing at Deborah's back, he saw that she was tired. It was

(025-101) nearly midnight, but still she kept working doggedly on, moving her

(025-102) shoulder muscles at times as though to shake off aches and pains, then

(025-105) of the appeal she had made to him only the month before:

(025-106) "Can't you see that we're all of us stunned, and trying to see what war

(025-107) will mean to all the children in the world? And while we're groping,

(025-108) groping, can't we give each other a hand?"

(025-109) And as he looked at his daughter, she made him think of her grandmother,

(025-110) as she had so often done before. For Deborah, too, was a pioneer. She, too,

(025-111) had lived in the wilderness. Clearing roads through jungles? Yes. And

(025-112) freeing slaves of ignorance and building a nation of new men. And now she

(025-114) the Indians but from the ravages of this war which was sweeping

(025-115) civilization aside. With her school behind her, so to speak, she stood

(025-116) facing this great enemy with stern and angry, steady eyes. Her pioneer

(025-117) grandmother come to life.

(025-118) So, with the deep craving which was a part of his inmost self, Roger tried

(025-119) to bind together what was old and what was new. But his thoughts grew vague

(025-121) bed. There, just before he fell asleep, again he had a feeling of relief at

(025-122) the knowledge that one at least in the family was to be rich this year.

(025-123) With a guilty sensation he shook off the thought, and within a few moments

(025-124) after that his harsh regular breathing was heard in the room.
