# Modernity

## Overview

*His [[family|Family]]* is set at a moment of dizzying transformation. [[new-york-city|New York City]] in the early 1900s is a place of skyscrapers, automobiles, telephones, and shifting social norms — a "tumultuous modern town" whose "sparkling pinnacles" rise around [[roger-gale|Roger Gale]]'s old [[home|house]] (001-189). For [[roger-gale|Roger]], modernity is not an abstraction but a daily intrusion: apartment buildings "overhanging" his home (002-122), daughters who speak of "modern ideas" and "modern [[womens-roles|women]]" (017-053), and a city that seems to be dismantling every familiar landmark.

## Manifestations

The modern makes itself felt through the novel in four registers. **Physical**: the changing cityscape — new buildings, electric lights, crowded streets — creates what [[roger-gale|Roger]] feels as "a savage modern town unrelentingly pressing" (006-197). **Technological**: telephones, automobiles, and new forms of communication accelerate the pace of life and shrink distance. **Social**: the "modern woman" (017-112) and "modern ideas" (007-081) challenge inherited [[womens-roles|gender roles]] and [[social-class|class]] hierarchies. **Moral**: new freedoms in [[love-and-courtship|courtship]], marriage, and [[womens-roles|women]]'s [[work-and-vocation|work]] unsettle the Victorian frame[[work-and-vocation|work]] [[roger-gale|Roger]] was raised in.

The novel is nuanced about modernity. It is not simply condemned or celebrated. [[deborah-gale|Deborah]]'s school — "her latest and most modern fad" as [[edith-sidney|Edith]] derisively calls it (007-081) — is genuinely innovative and humane. Yet the speed of change also causes genuine [[death|loss]]. [[rogers-evening-walks|Roger's walks]] through [[new-york-city|the city]] become elegiac meditations on a world disappearing before his eyes.

## Characters and Perspectives

- **[[roger-gale|Roger Gale]]** is modernity's reluctant witness. He observes, judges, and sometimes resists — but cannot stop the "tumultuous modern town" from reshaping the world his daughters will inherit.
- **[[deborah-gale|Deborah Gale]]** is modernity's agent. Her progressive school, her [[freedom|independence]], and her rejection of [[tradition|convention]]al marriage make her the [[nature|character]] most at home in the new era.
- **[[laura-sloane|Laura Sloane]]** embraces modernity as style and status — fashion, travel, the social whirl of a "modern woman" (017-112) who lives for the present.
- **[[bruce|Bruce]]**, the architect and war veteran, embodies modernity's ambiguities: a builder of the new city who is also broken by modern warfare.
