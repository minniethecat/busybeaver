# Broadway

Broadway is the iconic New York City thoroughfare that appears in Ernest Poole's novel
*His Family* (1917), symbolizing the urban spectacle, modernity, and cultural energy of
early 20th-century New York. At the time of the novel, Broadway was already established
as the entertainment and commercial heart of the city.

## Role in the Novel

Broadway appears as a backdrop to key moments in the Gale family's engagement with
the changing world around them. The street represents the vibrant, sometimes overwhelming,
pace of modern urban life that stands in contrast to the more traditional domestic sphere
of the older generation.

## Significance

In *His Family*, Broadway embodies several thematic dimensions:

- **Modernity and Change**: The bustling thoroughfare represents the rapid social and
  cultural transformation that Roger Gale struggles to comprehend
- **Generational Divide**: The younger Gale daughters are drawn to the energy and freedom
  of the city's public spaces, while Roger views them with ambivalence
- **Urban Spectacle**: Broadway's theaters, lights, and crowds symbolize the new
  century's promise and its threat to traditional values
