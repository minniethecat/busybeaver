(020-001) [[roger-gale|Roger]] had been spending a long quiet evening at [[family|home]]. He had asked John to

(020-002) dine with him and they had chatted for a time. Then John had started up to

(020-003) his room. And listening to the slow shuffling step of the cripple going

(020-004) upstairs, Roger had thought of the quick eager feet and the sudden scampers

(020-005) that would be heard as the silent old [[home|house]] renewed its life. Later he had

(020-006) gone to bed.

(020-007) He awakened with a start. The telephone bell was ringing.

(020-008) "Nice time to be calling folks out of bed," he grumbled, as he went into

(020-009) the hall. The next moment he heard [[deborah-gale|Deborah]]'s voice. It was clear and sharp

(020-010) with a note of alarm.

(020-011) "Father--it's I! You must come to [[edith-sidney|Edith]]'s apartment at once! [[bruce|Bruce]] is hurt

(020-012) badly! Come at once!"

(020-013) When Roger reached the apartment, it was Deborah who opened the door. Her

(020-014) face had changed, it was drawn and gray. She took him into the living room.

(020-015) "Tell me," he said harshly.

(020-016) "It was just outside the theater. Bruce and Edith were out in the street

(020-017) and got caught by some idiot of a chauffeur. Bruce threw Edith out of the

(020-018) way, but just as he did it he himself got struck in the back and went under

(020-019) a wheel. [[allan-baird|Allan]] brought him here at once, while I telephoned for a friend of

(020-020) his--a surgeon. They're with Bruce now."

(020-021) "Where's Edith?"

(020-022) "She's trying to quiet the [[childhood|children]]. They all woke up--" Deborah

(020-023) frowned--"when he was brought in," she added.

(020-024) "Well!" breathed Roger. "I declare!" Dazed and stunned, he sank into a

(020-025) chair. Soon the door opened and Allan came in.

(020-026) "He's gone," he said. And Deborah jumped. "No, no, I meant the [[medicine|doctor]]."

(020-027) "What does he say?"

(020-028) "Bruce can't live," said Allan gently. In the tense silence there came a

(020-029) chill. "And he knows it," Allan added. "He made me tell him--he said he

(020-030) must know--for business reasons. He wants to see you both at once, before

(020-031) Edith gets that child asleep."

(020-032) As they entered the room they saw Bruce on his bed. He was breathing

(020-033) quickly through his narrow tight-set jaws and staring up at the ceiling

(020-034) with a straining fixed intensity. As they entered he turned his head. His

(020-035) eyes met theirs and lighted up in a hard and terrible manner.

(020-036) "I'm not leaving them a dollar!" he cried.

(020-037) "We'll see to them, boy," said Roger, hoarsely, but Bruce had already

(020-038) turned to Baird.

(020-039) "I make you my executor, Allan--don't need it in writing--there isn't

(020-040) time." He drew a sudden quivering breath. "I have no will," he muttered on.

(020-041) "Never made one--never thought of this. [[business|Business]] life just

(020-042) starting--booming!--and I put in every cent!" There broke from him a low,

(020-043) bitter groan. "Made my money settling other men's muddles! Never thought of

(020-044) making this mess of my own! But even in mine--I could save something

(020-045) still--if I could be there--if I could be there--"

(020-046) The sweat broke out on his temples, and Deborah laid her hand on his head.

(020-047) "Sh-h-h," she breathed. He shut his eyes.

(020-048) "Hard to think of anything any more. I can't keep clear." He shuddered with

(020-049) pain. "Fix me for _them_," he muttered to Baird. "[[george-sidney|George]] and his mother.

(020-050) Fix me up--give me a couple of minutes clear. And Deborah--when you bring

(020-051) 'em in--don't let 'em know. You understand? No infernal last good-byes!"

(020-052) Deborah sharply set her teeth.

(020-053) "No, dear, no," she whispered. She followed her father out of the room,

(020-054) leaving Allan bending over the bed with a hypodermic in his hand. And when,

(020-055) a few moments later, George came in with his mother, they found Bruce

(020-056) soothed and quieted. He even smiled as he reached up his hand.

(020-057) "They say I've got to sleep, old girl--just sleep and sleep--it'll do me

(020-058) good. So you mustn't stay in the room to-night. Stay with the kiddies and

(020-059) get 'em to sleep." He was still smiling up at her. "They say it'll be a

(020-060) long time, little wife--and I'm so sorry--I was to blame. If I'd done as

(020-061) you wanted and gone in their taxi. Remember? You said it might rain." He

(020-062) turned to George: "Look here, my boy, I'm counting on you. I'll be sick,

(020-063) you know--no good at all. You must stand by your mother."

(020-064) George gulped awkwardly:

(020-065) "Sure I will, dad." His father sharply pressed his hand:

(020-066) "That's right, old fellow, I know what you are. Now good-night, son.

(020-067) Good-night, Edith dear." He looked at her steadily just for a moment, then

(020-068) closed his eyes. "Oh, but I'm sleepy," he murmured. "Good-night."

(020-069) And they left him. Alone with Allan, Bruce looked up with a savage glare.

(020-070) "Look here!" he snarled, between his teeth. "If you think I'm going to lie

(020-071) here and die you're mistaken! I won't! I won't let go! I'll show you chaps

(020-072) you can be wrong! Been wrong before, haven't you, thousands of times! Why

(020-073) be so damnably sure about _me_?" He fell back suddenly, limp and weak. "So

(020-074) damnably sure," he panted.

(020-075) "We're never sure, my dear old boy," said Allan very tenderly. Again he

(020-076) was bending close over the bed. "We're not sure yet--by any means. You're

(020-077) so strong, old chap, so amazingly strong. You've given me [[dream|hope]]--"

(020-078) "What are you sticking into my arm?" But Allan kept talking steadily on:

(020-079) "You've given me hope you'll pull through still. But not like this. You've

(020-080) got to rest. Let go, and try to go to sleep."

(020-081) "I'm afraid to," came the whisper. But soon, as again the drug took hold,

(020-082) he mumbled in a drowsy tone, "Afraid to go to sleep in the dark.... Say,

(020-083) Allan--get Deborah in here, will you--just for a minute. One thing more."

(020-084) When she came, he did not open his eyes.

(020-085) "That you, Deborah? Where's your hand?... Oh--there it is. Just one more

(020-086) point. You--you--" Again his mind wandered, but with an effort he brought

(020-087) it back. "You and Edith," he said in a whisper. "So--so--so different.

(020-088) Not--not like each other at all. But you'll stick together--eh?

(020-089) Always--always. Don't let go--I mean of my hand."

(020-090) "No, dear, no."

(020-091) And with her hand holding his, she sat for a long time perfectly still.

(020-092) Then the baby was heard crying, and Deborah went to the nursery.

(020-093) "Now, Edith, I'll see to the children," she said. "Allan says you can go to

(020-094) Bruce if you like."

(020-095) Edith looked up at Deborah quickly, and as quickly turned away. She went in

(020-096) to her husband. And there, hour by hour through the night, while he lay

(020-097) inert with his hand in hers, little by little she understood. But she asked

(020-098) no question of anyone.

(020-099) At last Bruce stirred a little and began breathing deep and fast.

(020-100) And so death came into the family.
