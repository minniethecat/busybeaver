(018-001) In [[deborah-gale|Deborah]]'s school, in the meantime, affairs had drawn to a climax. The

(018-002) moment had come for [[new-york-city|the city]] to say whether her new experiment should be

(018-003) dropped the following year or allowed to go on and develop. There came a

(018-004) day of sharp suspense when Deborah's friends and enemies on the Board of

(018-005) [[education|Education]] sat down to discuss and settle her fate. They were at it for

(018-006) several hours, but late in the afternoon they decided not only to let her

(018-007) go on the next year but to try her idea in four other schools and place her

(018-008) in charge with ample funds. The long strain came to an end at last in a

(018-009) triumph beyond her wildest hopes; when the news arrived she relaxed, grew

(018-010) limp, and laughed and cried a little. And her father felt her tremble as he

(018-011) held her a moment in his arms.

(018-012) "Now, Baird," he thought, "your chance has come. For God's sake, take it

(018-013) while it's here!"

(018-014) But in place of Baird that afternoon came men and [[womens-roles|women]] from the press, and

(018-015) friends and fellow workers. The door-bell and the telephone kept ringing

(018-016) almost incessantly. Why couldn't they leave her a moment's peace? [[roger-gale|Roger]]

(018-017) buried himself in his study. Later, when he was called to dinner, he found

(018-018) that [[allan-baird|Allan]] was there, too, but at first the conversation was all upon

(018-019) Deborah's victory. Flushed with success, for the moment engrossed in the

(018-020) wider field she saw ahead, she had not a thought for anything else. But

(018-022) "To hear me talk," she told them, "you'd think the whole world depended on

(018-023) me, and on my school and my ideas. Me, me, me! And it has been me all

(018-024) winter long! What a time I've given both of you!"

(018-025) She grew repentant and grateful, first to her father and then to Allan, and

(018-026) then more and more to Allan, with her happy eyes on his. And with a keen

(018-027) worried look at them both, Roger rose and left the room.

(018-030) "Well?" he asked. "Will you marry me now?"

(018-034) little queer and breathless:

(018-036) "Yes."

(018-037) "So do I. And with children, what of my [[work-and-vocation|work]]?"

(018-038) "I don't want to stop your work. If you marry me we'll go right on. You see

(018-039) I know you, Deborah, I know you've always grown like that--by risking what

(018-042) "I tell you this time it's no risk! Because you're a grown woman--formed!

(018-054) "Let's wait awhile, please! It won't be long--I'm in love with you, Allan,

(018-062) "By [[george-sidney|George]]," he cried, "he asked you!"

(018-063) "Yes!"

(018-064) "And you've taken him!"

(018-065) "No!"

(018-068) threw back her head and laughed at him.

(018-071) "We're going to wait a bit, that's all, till we're sure of everything!" she

(018-076) "Oh, dearie," she begged him smilingly. "Please don't be so unhappy just

(018-081) But again he was mistaken. It was in fact astonishing how the old house

(018-085) making her work fit into her swiftly deepening passion for a full happy

(018-088) smoothly things were working out. The monstrous new buildings around his

(018-089) house seemed to him to draw back as though balked of their prey.
