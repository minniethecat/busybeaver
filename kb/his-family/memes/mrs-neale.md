## Overview

Mrs. Neale is a minor background character in Ernest Poole's "His Family" (1917). She is mentioned once in the novel as a domestic worker who assists with washing in one of the Gale family households, representing the invisible labor that sustained middle-class family life in early twentieth-century New York.

## Life and Role in the Novel

Mrs. Neale appears only briefly in the narrative. She is described as "the woman who comes in to wash" (028-132), indicating her role as a periodic domestic servant rather than a live-in member of the household. Her presence is typical of the novel's understated treatment of domestic staff — characters like Mrs. Neale facilitate the daily functioning of the Gale household but remain at the periphery of the family's emotional and narrative focus.
