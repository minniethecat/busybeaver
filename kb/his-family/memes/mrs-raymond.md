# Mrs. Raymond

Mrs. Raymond is a minor background character in Ernest Poole's novel *His Family*
(1917), mentioned in Chapter 3 as a neighbor of the Gale family in their New York
apartment building. Though she appears only in passing, her presence helps establish
the social texture of the Gales' domestic world.

## In the Novel

Mrs. Raymond is referenced in two consecutive paragraphs of Chapter 3. When Roger
Gale asks one of his daughters about dinner plans, he is told they will be dining
"With the Raymonds" (003-038). The reference is so casual that Roger silently
wonders "who they were" (003-038) — an indication of how little he knows about the
neighbors with whom his family socializes. Shortly afterward, a family member
directs the maid to "Call up Mrs. Raymond's apartment and ask what time is dinner"
(003-039), confirming that Mrs. Raymond lives in the same building and that the
families share meals together.

## Role and Significance

Mrs. Raymond illustrates several aspects of the Gale family's urban life:

- **Urban Anonymity**: Even within the same apartment building, neighbors like the
  Raymonds remain barely known to Roger — "He wondered who they were" (003-038).
  This casual anonymity is characteristic of early 20th-century New York apartment
  living, where physical proximity did not guarantee intimacy.

- **Social Circle**: The Gales' social world extends beyond blood relatives to
  include building neighbors with whom they share dinners. The Raymonds are part
  of the invisible network of acquaintances that sustain the family's daily life.

- **Background Realism**: Characters like Mrs. Raymond, who never appear directly
  in a scene but are mentioned in passing, contribute to Poole's realistic
  portrayal of a busy urban household. The Gales do not live in isolation; their
  lives are embedded in a web of casual social obligations.
