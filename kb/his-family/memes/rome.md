## Overview

Rome appears in *His Family* as part of the European cultural landscape that shapes the experiences and aspirations of the Gale family's more worldly members. Like [[paris|Paris]], it is never a setting for direct action in the novel but exists in memory, conversation, and as a destination that signals cultivation and sophistication.

## Description in the Novel

Rome is first mentioned alongside other European capitals in the account of Deborah's youthful travels: she "lived in London and Paris, Munich and Rome, always in queer old lodgings" (005-105). The city reappears later in connection with Laura's European tastes — she speaks "blithely of a Rome reborn" (031-050), reflecting her engagement with fashionable cultural discourse. An Italian character is described as being "in Rome, an Italian close to his government" (025-027), connecting the city to political intrigue.

Laura's art collecting draws on Roman connections: items "brought from Rome dressed her several times each" (031-014) season, using the city's cultural cachet to enhance her social standing. The narrative also alludes to the extended European sojourn of certain characters who lived "abroad for years and years — in Rome and Berlin, in" (031-143) various European centers, suggesting a cosmopolitan existence far removed from the New York world of Roger Gale.

## Scenes and Events

Rome is referenced rather than depicted. It appears in Deborah's backstory, in Laura's conversations about art and fashion, and as part of the broader European milieu that characters invoke when discussing culture, politics, and refinement.

## Thematic Significance

Rome represents the weight of European history and culture that stands in contrast to the raw energy of America. For characters like Laura, it is a source of cultural legitimacy — art, fashion, and refinement that money can access but not create. The novel's treatment of Rome and other European cities reflects the early twentieth-century American fascination with European culture as a marker of sophistication.
