# Betsy Sidney

## Overview

Betsy Sidney is Edith Sidney's daughter and Roger Gale's granddaughter — one of five children in the Sidney household who represent the continuity of the Gale family into a new generation. "Young Betsy" (002-053) appears in the novel primarily through the eyes of others: her mother frets over "the dentist's bills" for her teeth (002-053), her grandfather passes her "on the stairs" and notes her presence (034-304), and her aunt Deborah includes her in the family's schedule. Betsy is less a fully realized character than a marker of what Roger values most — "new lives in his family" (004-202) — and her presence anchors the novel's concern with generational continuity.

## Life and Role in the Novel

Betsy's life is the ordinary life of a child in a prosperous early twentieth-century household. She is "intrusted" along with her brother George to the care of others when her parents are occupied (026-133). She shares a room that her mother visits — Edith "would go into Betsy's room" (026-175). She is old enough to be excluded from adult activities ("Afraid you can't, Betsy," her aunt replied [027-116]) but young enough to be sent to bed early ("When Betsy and George had gone to bed" [030-054]).

Her most vivid moment comes when she "thought, 'How beautiful she is'" while watching her aunt admiringly (027-149) — a child's perspective on the adult world she is growing into. At the novel's end, Roger passes "young Betsy" on the stairs (034-304), a silent acknowledgment that the family continues beyond him.

## Relationships

- **Edith Sidney** (mother): Betsy is one of Edith's five children, and her mother's practical concern for her (dentist bills, household arrangements) defines her presence in the novel.
- **George Sidney** (father): Mentioned together with Betsy in the household's daily rhythms — bedtime, childcare arrangements.
- **Roger Gale** (grandfather): Betsy is one of the "new lives" Roger treasures. Their interaction is minimal but symbolically weighty — the grandfather passing the granddaughter on the stairs.
