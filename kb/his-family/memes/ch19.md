(019-001) [[edith-sidney|Edith]] was radiant at the news.

(019-002) "I do [[dream|hope]] they're not going to grudge themselves a good long [[marriage|wedding]]

(019-003) trip!" she exclaimed.

(019-004) "They're going [[europe|abroad]]," said [[roger-gale|Roger]].

(019-005) "Oh, splendid! And the wedding! Church or [[family|home]]?"

(019-006) "[[home|Home]]," said Roger blissfully, "and short and simple, not a frill. Just the

(019-007) family."

(019-008) "Oh, that's so nice," sighed Edith. "I was afraid she'd [[poverty|want]] to drag in her

(019-009) school."

(019-010) "School will be out by then," he said.

(019-011) "Well, I hope it stays out--for the remainder of her days. She can't do

(019-012) both, and she'll soon see. Wait till she has a child of her own."

(019-013) "Well, she wants one bad enough."

(019-014) "Yes, but can she?" Edith asked, with the engrossed expression which came

(019-015) on her pretty florid face whenever she neared such a topic. She spoke with

(019-016) evident awkwardness. "That's the trouble. Is it too late? [[deborah-gale|Deborah]]'s

(019-017) thirty-one, you know, and she has lived her life so hard. The sooner she

(019-018) gives up her school the better for her chances."

(019-019) The face of her father clouded.

(019-020) "Look here," he said uneasily, "I wouldn't go talking to her--quite along

(019-021) those lines, my dear."

(019-022) "I'm not such an idiot," she replied. "She thinks me homely enough as it

(019-023) is. And she's not altogether wrong. [[bruce|Bruce]] and I were talking it over last

(019-024) night. We want to be closer, after this, to Deborah and [[allan-baird|Allan]]. Bruce says

(019-025) it will do us _all_ good, and for once I think he's right. I _have_ given

(019-026) too much time to my [[childhood|children]], and Bruce to [[rogers-office|his office]]--I see it now. Not

(019-027) that I regret it, but--well, we're going to blossom out."

(019-028) * * * * *

(019-029) She struck the same note with Deborah. And so did Bruce.

(019-030) "Oh, Deborah dear," he said smiling, when he found a chance to see her

(019-031) alone, "if you knew how long I've waited for this big fine thing to happen.

(019-032) A. Baird is my best chum in the world. Don't yank him gently away from us

(019-033) now. We'll keep close--eh?--all four of us."

(019-034) "Very," said Deborah softly.

(019-035) "And you mustn't get too solemn, you know. You won't pull too much of the

(019-036) highbrow stuff."

(019-037) "Heaven forbid!"

(019-038) "That's the right idea. We'll have some fine little parties together. You

(019-039) and A. Baird will give us a hand and get us out in the evenings. We need

(019-040) it, God knows, we've been getting old." Deborah threw him a glance of

(019-041) affection.

(019-042) "Why, Brucie," she said, in admiring tones, "I knew you had it in you."

(019-043) "So has Edith," he sturdily declared. "She only needs a little shove. We'll

(019-044) show you two that we're regular fellows. Don't you be all school and we

(019-045) won't be all home. We'll jump out of our skins and be young again."

(019-046) * * * * *

(019-047) In pursuance of this gay resolve, Bruce planned frequent parties to

(019-048) theaters and musical shows, and to Edith's consternation he even began to

(019-049) look about for a teacher from whom he could learn to dance. "A. Baird," he

(019-050) told her firmly, "isn't going to be the only soubrette in this family."

(019-051) One of the most hilarious of these small celebrations came early in June,

(019-052) when they dined all four together and went to the summer's opening of "The

(019-053) Follies of 1914." The show rather dragged a bit at first, but when Bert

(019-054) Williams took the stage Bruce's laugh became so contagious that people in

(019-055) seats on every hand turned to look at him and join in his glee. Only one

(019-056) thing happened to mar the evening's pleasure. When they came outside the

(019-057) theater Bruce found in his car something wrong with the engine. He tinkered

(019-058) but it would not go. Allan hailed a taxi.

(019-059) "Why not come with us?" asked Deborah.

(019-060) "No, thanks," said Bruce. "I've got this car to look after."

(019-061) "Oh, let it wait," urged Allan.

(019-062) "It does look a little like rain," put in Edith. Bruce glanced up at the

(019-063) cloudy sky and hesitated a moment.

(019-064) "Rain, piffle," he said good-humoredly. "Come on, wifey, stick by me. I

(019-065) won't be long." And he and Edith went back to his car.

(019-066) "What a dear he is," said Deborah. Allan put his arm around her, and they

(019-067) looked at each other and smiled. It was only nine days to the wedding.

(019-068) Out of the street's commotion came a sharp cry of warning. It was followed

(019-069) by a shriek and a crash. Allan looked out of the window, and then with a

(019-070) low exclamation he jumped from the taxi and slammed the door.
