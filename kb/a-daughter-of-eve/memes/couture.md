Couture is a minor figure mentioned in Balzac's *A Daughter of Eve* (1839) among the regular visitors to [[florine|Florine]]'s apartment. Listed alongside [[emile-blondet|Blondet]], [[finot|Finot]], and [[etienne-lousteau|Lousteau]] in the catalog of guests, Couture is one of the many secondary characters who populate the novel's journalistic and theatrical milieu [005-014].

Like many of Balzac's minor characters, Couture appears only briefly but contributes to the texture of a fully realized social world. The enumeration of Florine's circle — with its precise inventory of names, professions, and personal histories — is part of Balzac's technique of social density, creating the impression of a society where every individual has a history and every gathering contains hidden connections.
