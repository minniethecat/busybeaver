Conti is a composer mentioned in Balzac's *A Daughter of Eve* (1839) among the guests at [[florine|Florine]]'s apartment. He appears in the catalog of Florine's regular visitors, listed alongside journalists, bankers, and other figures from the intersecting worlds of art, finance, and journalism [005-014].

The presence of a composer in Florine's circle reflects the actress's position at the intersection of theatrical, musical, and journalistic Paris. Florine's apartment draws talent from every corner of the city's cultural life, creating the mixture of artistic and commercial interests that Balzac portrays with characteristic ambivalence.
