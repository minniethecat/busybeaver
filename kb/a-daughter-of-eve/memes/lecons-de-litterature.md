The *Leçons de littérature* (Lessons in Literature) is a work referenced in Balzac's *A Daughter of Eve* (1839). Part of the educational and cultural apparatus of the Granville sisters' upbringing, it represents the formal, approved literary culture that shaped Marie-Angélique's worldview — a culture of classical models, moral instruction, and carefully curated reading.

The *Leçons de littérature* belongs to the tradition of French literary pedagogy that treated literature as a branch of rhetoric and morality rather than as an expression of individual genius or passion. Marie-Angélique's education in such works left her intellectually prepared for the salon but emotionally unprepared for the passions that literature itself describes — a gap that the novel's plot exploits with devastating precision.
