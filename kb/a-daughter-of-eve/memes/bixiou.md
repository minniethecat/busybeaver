Bixiou is a figure in Balzac's *A Daughter of Eve* (1839) and a recurring character across *La Comédie Humaine*. A journalist and caricaturist known for his savage wit, he appears among the regulars at [[florine|Florine]]'s apartment [005-014].

In Balzac's world, Bixiou represents the caustic, observant spirit of Parisian journalism — a man whose talent for mockery makes him both entertaining and dangerous. His caricatures and witticisms circulate through the same social channels as the rumors and epigrams that shape reputations in the novel's salon society.

Bixiou appears more prominently in other Balzac novels including *Les Employés* and *La Maison Nucingen*, where his role as a satirical observer is more fully developed.
