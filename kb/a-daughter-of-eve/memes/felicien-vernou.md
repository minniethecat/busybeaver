Félicien Vernou is a minor journalist in Balzac's *A Daughter of Eve* (1839), one of the regulars at [[florine|Florine]]'s apartment. He belongs to the circle of journalists and writers who orbit [[raoul-nathan|Raoul Nathan]], forming part of the "diabolic legion of ferocious egotists" [005-014] that constitutes the Parisian press corps.

Vernou appears briefly in the roll-call of Florine's guests, listed alongside [[emile-blondet|Blondet]], [[finot|Finot]], and [[etienne-lousteau|Lousteau]]. His presence contributes to the novel's portrait of journalism as a world of compromised talent and corrosive camaraderie, where "this *camaraderie* corrodes the noblest minds" [004-018].

In Balzac's larger *La Comédie Humaine*, Vernou reappears in other novels as part of the recurring cast of journalists, contributing to the density of Balzac's fictional world.
