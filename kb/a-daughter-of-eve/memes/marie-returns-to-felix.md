## Overview

Marie-Angélique's return to her husband [[comte-felix-de-vandenesse|Félix de Vandenesse]] forms the emotional resolution of *A Daughter of Eve* (1839). After the crisis of her infatuation with [[raoul-nathan|Raoul Nathan]], the discovery of her financial entanglement, and Félix's counter-maneuver, Marie returns to her marriage — not as the innocent she was, but as a woman who has faced temptation and been forgiven.

## The Confession

The return begins with Marie's confession to Félix. Cornered by the evidence of her financial dealings and the revelation of Nathan's relationship with [[florine|Florine]], Marie breaks down. "How much do you know?" she asks, and Félix's answer — "Everything" — leaves no room for evasion [009-041].

Félix's response is magnanimous. Rather than punishing his wife, he demonstrates his superiority over Nathan not through vengeance but through protection and forgiveness. He burns the promissory notes, destroys the compromising letters, and assures Marie that the crisis is over.

## The Nature of the Reconciliation

Balzac's treatment of the reconciliation is characteristically unsentimental. Marie returns to her husband not because she has ceased to feel for Nathan but because she has seen through him. The reconciliation is a victory of understanding over illusion, of marriage as institution over passion as impulse.

Félix's final words to Marie — "I have no reproaches to make you" — establish the terms of the reconciliation: not a return to innocence, which is impossible, but a new beginning founded on honesty and mutual recognition of human weakness.

## A New Beginning

The novel concludes with the Vandenesse marriage transformed. Marie has lost her innocence but gained knowledge — of the world, of herself, of her husband's character. Félix has demonstrated a magnanimity that transcends the conventional role of the wronged husband. Their marriage, tested by crisis, emerges stronger — not because passion has been rekindled, but because illusion has been replaced by reality.
