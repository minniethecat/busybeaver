"Vergiss mein nicht" (Forget-me-not) is a German song mentioned in Balzac's *A Daughter of Eve* (1839). Its presence in the novel is associated with the musical education the Granville sisters received from their German music-master, [[schmucke|Wilhelm Schmucke]].

The song — whose title means "forget me not" — carries emotional resonance in the novel's context. It belongs to the world of the sisters' shared childhood, the innocent musical culture they learned from Schmucke, and the bond of memory and affection that persists between them even as their lives diverge. The German title, left untranslated in the French text, evokes the Germanic musical tradition that Schmucke represents and the emotional sincerity associated with German Romantic culture in the French imagination.
