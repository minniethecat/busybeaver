The Hôtel du Mail is a Paris residence mentioned in *A Daughter of Eve* (1839). A private mansion (*hôtel particulier*) of the kind that characterized aristocratic Paris, it appears in the novel's mapping of the city's social geography.

In Balzac's Paris, the *hôtel particulier* — the grand townhouse of the aristocracy — was the architectural expression of social standing. The Hôtel du Mail belongs to this world of private mansions, enclosed courtyards, and guarded entrances, standing in contrast to the apartment buildings and commercial streets of the newer Paris.
