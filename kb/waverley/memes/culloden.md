# Culloden

## Description / Overview

Culloden Moor, near Inverness, was the site of the last pitched battle fought on British soil. On 16 April 1746, the exhausted and outnumbered Jacobite army was destroyed in less than an hour by the Duke of Cumberland's forces. The battle and its brutal aftermath — the suppression of the Highlands, the proscription of Highland dress, the abolition of heritable jurisdictions — ended the clan system as a military and political force.

## Significance in Waverley

Although Culloden itself is not depicted directly in the novel (Scott ends his military narrative before the final catastrophe), its shadow falls over the book's conclusion. Fergus MacIvor's trial and execution, the dispersal of the clans, and the restoration of Tully-Veolan are all consequences of Culloden. The battle is the unspoken end-point toward which the entire novel tends.
