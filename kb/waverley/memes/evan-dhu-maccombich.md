# Evan Dhu Maccombich

# Evan Dhu Maccombich

## Biography / Character Overview

Evan Dhu Maccombich is a clansman of Clan Ivor who serves as Fergus MacIvor's personal attendant, messenger, and bodyguard. His name — "Dhu" meaning "black" or "dark" in Gaelic — and his role as the chief's right hand make him an exemplar of the ordinary Highlander whose military service and personal loyalty were the foundation of the clan system's power.

Evan Dhu is not a complex character — he is defined entirely by his loyalty to his chief — but this simplicity is precisely Scott's point. The clan system worked because men like Evan Dhu gave their unquestioning allegiance to men like Fergus MacIvor. Evan Dhu's devotion is not blind (he is shrewd in his own way) but it is absolute: he would die for his chief without hesitation, and at the novel's end, he offers his own life in exchange for Fergus's — a scene of genuine pathos that Scott handles with restraint.

## Role in the Narrative

Evan Dhu serves several functions: he is Waverley's guide into Highland society, a source of ethnographic information about clan customs, and — in the novel's tragic conclusion — the human face of the loyalty that the clan system commanded and that the defeat of 1745 destroyed. His offer to exchange his life for his chief's is one of the novel's most moving moments.

## Appearances

Evan Dhu appears throughout the Highland and military chapters, from ch19 through the novel's conclusion.
