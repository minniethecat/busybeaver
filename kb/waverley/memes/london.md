# London

## Description / Overview

London in the mid-18th century was the largest city in Europe, the seat of the Hanoverian court, and the centre of British political and commercial life. For Waverley, London is the place of reckoning: it is here that he must answer for his Jacobite involvement and secure his pardon through the intercession of Colonel Talbot.

## Significance in Waverley

London represents the Hanoverian order to which Waverley ultimately returns. His journey from Waverley-Honour to Scotland to London traces a political arc: departure from England, immersion in Jacobite Scotland, return to the Hanoverian fold. London is where the novel's political themes are resolved — not through triumph or tragedy but through the quiet mechanisms of law, influence, and personal connection.
