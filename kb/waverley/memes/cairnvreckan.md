# Cairnvreckan

## Description / Overview

Cairnvreckan is the estate of Major Melville, a Hanoverian magistrate and militia officer. Unlike the warmly hospitable Tully-Veolan, Cairnvreckan is a place of law and order: it is here that Waverley is interrogated, his political loyalties scrutinised, and his fate — for a time — hangs in the balance.

## Significance in Waverley

Cairnvreckan represents the Hanoverian legal order that stands in opposition to the Jacobite world of Tully-Veolan and Glennaquoich. Major Melville's interrogation of Waverley, while fair-minded, embodies the apparatus of the state: suspicion, evidence, legal procedure. The scene at Cairnvreckan is the novel's most explicit dramatisation of the conflict between personal loyalty and political allegiance.
