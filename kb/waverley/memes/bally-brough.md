# Bally-Brough

## Description / Overview

Bally-Brough is a Highland mountain pass, celebrated in clan tradition as the site of a legendary defensive action in which ten men of Clan Donnochie held the pass against a hundred Lowland soldiers. Fergus MacIvor mentions it to Waverley as an example of the martial qualities of the Highlander fighting on his native ground.

## Significance in Waverley

The pass of Bally-Brough appears only in conversation, but its function is significant: it is one of the many ways Scott shows the Highland world as a landscape saturated with memory and legend. Every glen, every pass, has its story — and these stories are weapons in the arsenal of Highland self-definition.
