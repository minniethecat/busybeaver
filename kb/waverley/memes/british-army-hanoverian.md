# British Army (Hanoverian)

## Overview

The British Army of the mid-18th century was a professional force, increasingly well-organised and disciplined, serving the Hanoverian monarchy established by the Glorious Revolution and the Act of Settlement. It was the military instrument of the state that the Jacobite Rising sought to overthrow. Waverley's commission in a regiment of dragoons marks him as a member of this establishment — a fact that makes his Jacobite entanglement all the more politically dangerous.

## Role in Waverley

The Hanoverian army appears in the novel both as Waverley's original allegiance and as the force that ultimately defeats the Jacobite cause. Scott's treatment of it is notably even-handed: the government soldiers are neither heroes nor villains but professionals doing their duty. Colonel Talbot, the novel's moral exemplar, is a Hanoverian officer.

## Key Figures

- **Colonel Talbot**: English officer, embodiment of military honour
- **Sir John Cope**: Commander defeated at Prestonpans
