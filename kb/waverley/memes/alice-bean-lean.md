# Alice Bean Lean

## Biography / Character Overview

Alice Bean Lean is the daughter of Donald Bean Lean, the Highland cateran. She appears only briefly in the novel, but her presence is significant for what it reveals: that even within the lawless margins of the clan system — the world of cattle-raiders and protection rackets — there exist the ordinary human relationships of family, courtship, and domestic affection. Alice is the object of Evan Dhu Maccombich's romantic interest, and Fergus MacIvor's reference to their courtship provides one of the novel's few glimpses of everyday Highland life.

## Role in the Narrative

Alice's primary narrative function is to humanise the world of Donald Bean Lean. She is a reminder that the cateran, for all his lawlessness, has a household, a daughter, and a stake in the social fabric. Her relationship with Evan Dhu also provides a minor romantic counterpoint to the central Waverley-Flora-Rose triangle.

## Quotations

> "Evan Dhu Maccombich is in love with Donald's daughter, Alice, and you cannot expect me to disturb him in his amours" — Fergus on the courtship (023-027)
