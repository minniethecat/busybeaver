# The Faculty of Advocates

## Overview

The Faculty of Advocates is the professional body of Scottish advocates (barristers), based in Parliament House, Edinburgh. In the 18th century, it was the premier professional institution for educated Scottish gentlemen — a training ground for public life that combined legal expertise with the intellectual culture of the Scottish Enlightenment. Baron Bradwardine, with his obsessive legal erudition, embodies a comic version of the advocate's cast of mind.

## Role in Waverley

The Faculty of Advocates does not appear directly in the novel, but its ethos pervades it. The Baron's legal pedantry, the Bailie's practical legal knowledge, and the novel's general concern with the forms and procedures of the law all reflect the legal culture that the Faculty sustained. Scott himself was an advocate, and his intimate knowledge of Scottish legal culture informs every legal reference in the novel.

## Key Figures

- **Baron Bradwardine**: A fictional product of Scottish legal education
- **Bailie Macwheeble**: The practical, commercial face of Scottish law
