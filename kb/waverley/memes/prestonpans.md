# Prestonpans

## Description / Overview

Prestonpans is a village in East Lothian, a few miles east of Edinburgh, and the site of the Battle of Prestonpans on 21 September 1745. The battle was the first major engagement of the Jacobite Rising and resulted in a crushing Jacobite victory: the government army under Sir John Cope was routed in a matter of minutes by the Highland charge, with over a thousand government soldiers killed, wounded, or captured against minimal Jacobite losses.

## Significance in Waverley

The battle is a turning point in the novel as it was in history. Scott's description of the eve of battle — the Highland army sleeping in the open, the Chieftain's tactical planning, the ominous quiet before dawn — is one of his most accomplished set-pieces. The aftermath of the battle, in which Waverley tends to the wounded (including Colonel Talbot) and confronts the human cost of the cause he has joined, introduces the novel's moral complexity: victory is real but the carnage is real too.
