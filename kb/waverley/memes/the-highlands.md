# The Scottish Highlands

## Description / Overview

The Scottish Highlands — the *Gaidhealtachd* in Gaelic — are the mountainous northern and western region of Scotland, distinct in language, culture, and social organisation from the English-speaking Lowlands. In the 18th century, the Highlands were still predominantly Gaelic-speaking, organised around the clan system, and only tenuously integrated into the British state. The landscape itself — wild, sublime, and, to the English eye, alien — is a constant presence in the novel.

## Significance in Waverley

The Highlands are more than a setting in *Waverley*: they are almost a character. Waverley's journey from the tame English countryside to the wild Highland landscape is the novel's central geographical and psychological movement. The Highlands represent romance, danger, otherness — everything that Waverley's English education has prepared him to be fascinated by and everything that the Hanoverian state must subdue.
