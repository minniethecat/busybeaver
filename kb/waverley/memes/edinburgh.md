# Edinburgh

## Description / Overview

Edinburgh in 1745 was a city of stark contrasts: the medieval Old Town crowding along the Royal Mile from the Castle to Holyroodhouse, and the elegant New Town not yet built. During the Jacobite occupation, the city became the stage for the Stuart court's brief, brilliant restoration — a few months of balls, levees, and political intrigue before the army marched south to its fate.

## Significance in Waverley

Edinburgh is the setting for the novel's central political scenes: Waverley's reception at the Chevalier's court, the Jacobite ball, and the factional manoeuvring among the Jacobite leadership. Scott captures the city's divided loyalties — some citizens enthusiastic for the Stuart cause, others anxiously waiting for the Hanoverian authorities to return.
