# Carlisle

## Description / Overview

Carlisle is an ancient border city and fortress in northwest England, close to the Scottish border. Its castle, dating from the 12th century, served as a military prison and place of execution for captured Jacobites after the 1745 Rising.

## Significance in Waverley

Carlisle appears in the novel's tragic conclusion: it is where Fergus MacIvor is held, tried, and executed. Scott's handling of Fergus's final scenes — his dignified farewell to his clan, his refusal to plead for mercy, his composed walk to the scaffold — is among the most powerful writing in the novel. Carlisle is the place where the romance of the Jacobite cause meets its historical reckoning.

## Key Scenes

- Fergus MacIvor's imprisonment
- The farewell between Fergus and his clan
- Fergus's execution
