# Doune Castle

## Description / Overview

Doune Castle is a formidable 14th-century fortress in Perthshire, near Stirling. During the 1745 Rising, it served as a Jacobite prison, holding captured government soldiers and political prisoners. Its grim, medieval character makes it a fitting setting for the novel's darker passages.

## Significance in Waverley

Doune Castle appears in the novel as the destination to which Waverley is dispatched as a prisoner after his arrest. The castle's associations with captivity, political violence, and the harsh realities of civil war provide a counterpoint to the romantic glamour of the Jacobite court at Holyrood.
