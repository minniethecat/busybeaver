# Derby

## Description / Overview

Derby is a town in the English Midlands, some 130 miles from London. On 4 December 1745, the Jacobite army reached Derby — the furthest point of its advance into England. Here, faced with the absence of the promised English Jacobite support and reports of government armies converging, the Jacobite war council made the fatal decision to retreat.

## Significance in Waverley

Derby represents the turning-point of the Rising. Prince Charles Edward argued passionately for continuing the march on London, but his council overruled him. The retreat from Derby began the unraveling of the Jacobite cause — a strategic failure that Scott treats with elegiac restraint, acknowledging the courage of the decision-makers while making clear its consequences.
