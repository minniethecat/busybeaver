# Scottish Episcopal Church

## Overview

The Scottish Episcopal Church was the non-established Episcopal church in Scotland, distinct from the Presbyterian Church of Scotland (the Kirk). After the Glorious Revolution, many Episcopal clergy refused the oath of allegiance to William III, becoming nonjurors. The church became closely associated with Jacobite politics: its clergy often ministered to Jacobite gentry families, and its refusal to abjure the Stuarts made it, in effect, the spiritual wing of the Jacobite movement.

## Role in Waverley

The Episcopal Church enters the novel indirectly, through the figure of Mr. Pembroke, the nonjuring clergyman who tutors Edward Waverley. Pembroke's nonjuring status is cited by Major Melville as evidence of Waverley's Jacobite contamination: "his tutor a nonjuror" (032-018). The church represents the religious dimension of the Jacobite cause — the persistence of loyalties that are simultaneously political and spiritual.
