# Callum Beg

# Callum Beg

## Biography / Character Overview

Callum Beg ("little Callum" or "young Callum" in Gaelic) is a young clansman of Clan Ivor assigned to attend Edward Waverley during his stay at Glennaquoich. He is fiercely loyal, quick to take offence, and entirely unconcerned with the legal distinctions that govern Lowland society. Callum carries a dirk and is prepared to use it — his casual attitude toward violence is both alarming and, in Scott's ethnographic portrait, characteristic of the Highland world in which personal honour was enforced at blade-point.

## Role in the Narrative

Callum serves as Waverley's guide and protector in the Highlands, but his presence also underscores the cultural gulf between Waverley's English sensibility and Highland norms. When Callum casually suggests murdering a rival, Waverley is shocked; Callum is puzzled by Waverley's squeamishness. This clash of moral frameworks is one of the ways Scott dramatises the cultural difference at the heart of the novel.

## Appearances

Callum appears throughout the Glennaquoich chapters (ch19–ch30).
