# The Scottish Lowlands

## Description / Overview

The Scottish Lowlands are the southern and eastern region of Scotland, traditionally English-speaking (and Scots-speaking), more densely populated and economically developed than the Highlands, and more fully integrated into British political and legal institutions. The Lowlands of *Waverley* are the world of Baron Bradwardine's Tully-Veolan — feudal in ethos but legally sophisticated, Jacobite in sympathy but cautious in action.

## Significance in Waverley

The Lowlands serve as the novel's middle ground — geographically between England and the Highlands, culturally between Hanoverian loyalty and Jacobite sympathy, legally between the modern state and feudal survival. The Baron of Bradwardine, the novel's quintessential Lowlander, embodies this intermediary position.
