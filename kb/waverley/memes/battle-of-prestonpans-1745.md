# Battle of Prestonpans

## Overview

The Battle of Prestonpans was fought on 21 September 1745 near the village of Prestonpans in East Lothian, a few miles east of Edinburgh. The Jacobite army, approximately 2,500 strong, faced a government force of about 2,300 under Sir John Cope. The battle lasted less than fifteen minutes: the Highland charge — a rapid advance, a single volley at close range, and a ferocious broadsword assault — shattered Cope's inexperienced troops. Government losses exceeded 1,000 killed, wounded, and captured; Jacobite losses were minimal.

## Role in Waverley

Scott's depiction of Prestonpans is the novel's military centrepiece. The eve of battle (ch46), with the Highland army sleeping in the open fields and the chiefs making their final dispositions, is rendered with extraordinary atmospheric precision. The battle itself (ch47) is shown through Waverley's eyes — chaotic, terrifying, and morally complex. The aftermath, in which Waverley cares for the wounded Colonel Talbot, introduces the novel's central theme of honour across political lines.
