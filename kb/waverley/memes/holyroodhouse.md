# Holyroodhouse

## Description / Overview

The Palace of Holyroodhouse, at the foot of Edinburgh's Royal Mile, was the historic residence of the Stuart monarchs in Scotland. In September 1745, following the Jacobite victory at Prestonpans, Prince Charles Edward Stuart entered Edinburgh and established his court at Holyroodhouse — a moment of extraordinary symbolic power, as the Stuart claimant occupied his ancestors' palace.

## Significance in Waverley

Holyroodhouse is the setting for the Chevalier's court in the novel, including Waverley's reception, the ball, and the political manoeuvring among the Jacobite leadership. Scott uses the palace to dramatise the glamour and the factionalism of the Jacobite court: it is at Holyroodhouse that Fergus MacIvor's ambition and the Chevalier's charisma are most on display, and it is there that the structural weaknesses of the Jacobite cause — personal rivalries, strategic indecision — become apparent.
