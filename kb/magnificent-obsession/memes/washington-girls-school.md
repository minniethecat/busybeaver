# The Washington Girls' School

## Overview

The girls' school in Washington where [[joyce-hudson|Joyce Hudson]] was educated — mentioned in the novel's opening chapter.

## Description

Part of the institutional landscape of Lloyd C. Douglas's *Magnificent Obsession* (1929).

## Narrative Role

This organization plays a role in the novel's exploration of the "Magnificent Obsession" philosophy, serving as a setting for key events and relationships.

## Associated Characters

Connected to the central characters of the novel including **[[bobby-merrick|Bobby Merrick]]**, **[[wayne-hudson|Wayne Hudson]]**, and others in the [[brightwood-hospital|Brightwood Hospital]] community.

## Key Passages

Mentioned across the novel's 22 chapters, this institution helps define the social and professional world of the narrative.
