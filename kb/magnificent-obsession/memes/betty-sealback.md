# Betty Sealback

## Overview

Betty Sealback is a member of [[doctor-mclaren|Dr. McLaren]]'s progressive congregation in *Magnificent Obsession*. Her presence in the theological discussions between McLaren and [[bobby-merrick|Bobby Merrick]] provides an attentive lay audience for the novel's most explicit explorations of [[faith]], prayer, and the redefinition of [[christianity|Christianity]].

## Background

Betty is associated with Dr. McLaren's church, described as "really the most forward-looking — indeed the only forward-looking church in town" (018-006). She is mentioned as having recommended McLaren to offer the invocation at a Social Congress session.

## Role in the Narrative

Betty's most significant moment comes during the extended dinner conversation in Chapter 18, when she asks Bobby: "That sounds a little as if you believed in prayer, Doctor Merrick" (018-064). Her question crystallizes the gap between Bobby's experiential practice and conventional religious language.

## Relationships

Betty is connected to **Doctor McLaren** as a congregant and to **Bobby Merrick** through the theological discussions that McLaren facilitates.

## Thematic Significance

Betty represents the thoughtful layperson seeking a form of faith compatible with modern understanding — precisely the audience Douglas was writing for. Her question about prayer is the novel's invitation to readers to reconsider what prayer means.

## Key Passages

> "That sounds a little as if you believed in prayer, Doctor Merrick," said Betty wistfully. (018-064)
