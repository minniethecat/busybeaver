# Doctor Donelli

## Overview

Doctor Donelli is a surgeon at [[brightwood-hospital|Brightwood Hospital]] in *Magnificent Obsession*. His most significant appearance occurs during the novel's climactic operation, when [[bobby-merrick|Bobby Merrick]] performs [[brain-surgery|brain surgery]] on [[helen-hudson|Helen Hudson]]. Donelli assists in the operating room and asks to close the incision — a request Bobby refuses, choosing to complete every aspect of the procedure himself.

## Background

Donelli is part of the surgical staff at Brightwood, working alongside Bobby Merrick after the latter's rise to prominence. His Italian name — he is described as "the impulsive Italian" (020-018) — adds texture to the hospital's cosmopolitan character.

## Role in the Narrative

Donelli's primary dramatic function is to witness the climactic surgery. When "Donelli inquired, with an entreating look and a hand outstretched for the needle, if he might not do the scalp stitches," Bobby "shook his head" (020-017) — choosing to complete the operation entirely by himself. This refusal underscores the personal stakes of the surgery: Bobby will not delegate any part of Helen's care to another surgeon.

## Relationships

Donelli is a colleague of **Bobby Merrick** at Brightwood Hospital. He was also connected to the late **Wayne Hudson**, whose reputation drew surgeons to the institution.

## Thematic Significance

Donelli represents the professional community that validates Bobby's transformation. His presence in the operating room — as witness to both Bobby's surgical skill and his emotional struggle — reinforces the novel's theme of professional discipline as moral achievement.

## Key Passages

> "Donelli inquired, with an entreating look and a hand outstretched for the needle, if he might not do the scalp stitches; but Merrick shook his head." (020-017)
