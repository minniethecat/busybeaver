# Interest

## The Antagonism of Interests as Structural Foundation

The single most important structural diagnosis in [[jean-grave|Grave]]'s critique of capitalist society is the concept of the "antagonism of interests." He returns to this formulation repeatedly across the work, identifying it as the organizing principle of the existing social order. "This society, as we have observed in the preceding chapter, is based upon the antagonism of interests, and makes every individual the enemy of his neighbor" [004-003]. This is not a contingent feature of capitalism that could be corrected through reform — it is the system's essential logic.

What does this antagonism look like in practice? Grave offers concrete examples: "The seller's interest is opposed to the buyer's; the stock-raiser and the agriculturist ask for nothing better than a 'good epidemic and a good hailstorm' among their neighbors, in order to raise the price of their commodities" [004-003]. "The development of mechanical appliances tends to greater and greater division of the workers, throwing them out of employment and leading them to disputes among themselves for the chance to take each other's jobs" [004-004]. "Everything in our traditional 'society' tends to split up mankind" [004-005].

> Founded upon this struggle between individuals, society makes of every creature the enemy of all others; it provokes war, crime, theft, and all the misdemeanors which are attributed to the evil nature of man, though they are but the consequence of the social order. [010-016]

## Antagonism Within and Between Classes

The antagonism of interests operates at multiple levels simultaneously. It divides society into two hostile classes — exploiters and exploited — but it also "opposes individual against individual within each class" [010-015]. The capitalists compete with one another, and the workers are set against each other in the struggle for employment. "If the capitalistic classes were truly united among themselves, if their members no longer had private interests and were moved solely by the interests of caste," they might be more effective exploiters — but even this unity is impossible under capitalism [010-017].

> Based upon antagonism of interests it opposes individual against individual within each class; it sows warfare among them by its institution of private property which forces people to hoard in order to secure themselves against the morrow those necessaries which society cannot guarantee to them. [010-015]

This intra-class antagonism has the paradoxical effect of stabilizing the system: workers divided against each other cannot unite against their exploiters, and capitalists absorbed in private competition against each other cannot see their shared class interest clearly enough to coordinate their defense of privilege.

## The Capitalist Weaponization of Interest

[[jean-grave|Grave]] is particularly attentive to how the ruling class manipulates the concept of interest for its own ends. "The capitalistic classes have interested him in the existing society," he writes of the worker, "persuaded him to defend the privileges of his exploiters while he believes himself defending his own interests in an organization which, in fact, has nothing for him but promises never to be realized" [003-015].

This is achieved partly through ideological mystification — the worker is made to feel that his interests are aligned with those of his employer — and partly through material concessions: "the privileged, to succeed in maintaining their privileges, are obliged to make people believe themselves exploited and gagged in their own interest" [004-009]. The worker's genuine interest in survival and security is co-opted to sustain the very system that exploits him.

## The Hypocrisy of Bourgeois Interest-Talk

The capitalists themselves are not confused about where their interests lie when they address each other rather than the workers. Grave quotes the economist Dunoyer, who plainly states: "the true motive of every war is always the interest of the class or party in possession of the government — an interest which must not be confounded with that of the nation" [012-011]. Among themselves, the capitalists freely acknowledge the antagonism of interests that they publicly denounce Anarchists for naming.

> Our good capitalists, who declaim so loudly against the frightful Anarchists that have the audacity to demonstrate to the workers how their interest is antagonistic to the interests of the bourgeois class, make no mistake among themselves in properly defining this antagonism. [012-009]

The language of "collective interest" and "national interest" is deployed to compel workers to sacrifice themselves for causes that serve only the bourgeoisie — whether in war, colonization, or the maintenance of "public order." "The defense of the soil was but too good a pretext for maintaining the army necessary to the support of their privileges, and the 'collective interest' an invincible argument for compelling the workers to contribute to the defense of said privileges" [011-009].

> War, consequently, can bring no good to the workers; we have no interests engaged in it, nothing to defend but our skins. [011-017]

## Interest Rightly Understood: The Anarchist Alternative

Against the antagonism of interests, Grave proposes a society organized around solidarity — an alignment of private and public welfare. "Certainly we do combat this society because it does not afford us satisfaction for all our aspirations; but we also comprehend that our own interests, rightly understood, would have this satisfaction of our needs extended to all the members of society" [002-012].

The anarchist ideal is a society "no longer based, as now, upon the antagonism of interests, but, on the contrary, upon the strictest solidarity." In such a society, "personal well-being shall flow from the general well-being" [021-002]. Competition may still exist — "some emulation in attaining what is best" — but "neither mercantile, proprietary, nor governmental interest will stand in the way, and those competitors who are behindhand will have every facility for assimilating the progress made by their happier competitors" [021-003].

> Conceive an organization based, on the contrary, upon the strictest solidarity. Make it so that private interests shall no longer be opposed to each other, nor contrary to public interests. Make it so that personal well-being shall flow from the general well-being, or produce it. [010-020]
