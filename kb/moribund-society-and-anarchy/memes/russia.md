# Russia

## Overview

Russia appears in Jean Grave's *Moribund Society and Anarchy* in two distinct contexts: as part of the European civilized world in his discussion of racial theories, and indirectly through references to the geopolitical dynamics of late nineteenth-century Europe, particularly in the context of war and class solidarity.

## In Grave's Text

**War and Patriotism**: In Chapter XI ("The Country"), Grave argues that workers have no stake in war, and that the bourgeoisie would readily appeal to foreign armies—"be they German, Russian, or of no matter what country"—to defend their privileges against the working class [011-012]. He draws a parallel with Voltaire, who did not believe in God but thought religion necessary for the common people, just as the bourgeoisie maintain frontiers between their slaves while disregarding them when their own interests are at stake.

**Civilization and Race**: In Chapter XV ("There Are No Inferior Races"), Grave references Russia as part of the argument that modern civilization forms a continuum "from France to Russia and from America to Australia," suggesting that racial distinctions are dissolving into class distinctions as international relations expand [015-021].

**Franco-Prussian War Context**: While not directly naming Russia, the discussion of the 1871 Franco-Prussian War indemnity of five billion francs [ch11] occurs within the broader European balance-of-power system in which Russia was a major participant, allied with Prussia through the League of the Three Emperors (1873).

## Historical Context

In Grave's era (the 1880s–1890s), the Russian Empire under Tsar Alexander III was marked by severe political repression. The Russian anarchist movement, associated with Mikhail Bakunin and Peter Kropotkin, had deep connections to the French anarchist circles in which Grave moved. Russia's alliance with France (formalized in 1894) was a defining geopolitical reality of the period. Grave's casual reference to "Russian" armies reflects the French anarchist understanding that national armies served capital, not workers, regardless of which state commanded them.
