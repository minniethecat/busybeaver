# Production

Production — the creation of goods and services necessary for human existence — occupies a central place in [[jean-grave|Grave]]'s critique of capitalist society. In a well-ordered society, production would be a cooperative activity from which all benefit. Under capitalism, however, production serves not human needs but the accumulation of private wealth.

## Cooperative Origins, Capitalist Distortion

Grave envisions a society in which "all having cooperated in production, we shall all have rights in consumption" [001-023], with "the sum of needs... calculated (counting in those which must be foreseen) that the satisfaction of all may be attained" [001-023]. In such a society, "solidarity will have no trouble in securing to each his share" [001-023]. Production would respond to genuine human desire: "the more intense his desire is the greater an amount of activity will he devote to its realization" [001-024].

In contrast, capitalist society has reversed this relationship. "In our present society, wherein we are accustomed to depend upon the toil of others to obtain the things necessary for existence, there is but one object: to procure money enough to enable one to buy what he wants" [001-024]. People "engage in all sorts of occupations, more or less dishonorable, except the one thing necessary that all might have their share — *useful production*" [001-024]. The result is "this unreasoned egoism which seems to have become the sole motive of human actions" [001-024].

## Machinery and Exploitation

The relationship between machinery and the worker is a central example of capitalist distortion. Machinery is "a development begotten by all our acquired knowledge transmitted from generation to generation, and which consequently ought to benefit every human being, rendering all lives broader and easier by the fact that it increases their power of production" [005-014]. Instead, "machinery has brought nothing but an increase of misery and privation to the workers" [005-014].

The mechanism of this paradox is clear: "the capitalists are the only ones to benefit by the advantages of mechanical inventions, which enable them to reduce the number of their employees, and with the help of the antagonism thus established, competition between the unemployed and the employed, they profit by lowering the wages of the latter" [005-014]. The very advances that should liberate humanity instead deepen its enslavement.

## The Economic Ideology

The economists have constructed an elaborate ideology to justify this arrangement. "Bourgeois society being founded upon capital, and this being represented by money, the economists, in order to mask the peculiar role it plays in the work of production and exchange, have reduced everything to capital" [005-013]. Even human labor is reduced: "the muscular power which the workman spends in production — capital!" [005-013]. Crucially, the economists omit "the amount of intelligence often superior to that of the contractor" that workers bring to production, "because it would then be necessary to count two portions of capital for the workman, and as that would embarrass the economists in their calculations, they pass this over in silence" [005-013].

The division of the product that follows is grotesquely unequal: "the capitalist pockets three-fourths of the product for his share and the trick is played. The workman has received his pay, why should he complain?" [005-013]. This ideological framework transforms [[exploitation]] into a seemingly natural economic arrangement.

## Society Without Production for Profit

In Grave's alternative vision, production would be organized by voluntary [[association]] rather than by the wage system. "Many of us will combine with the intention of realizing one of our aspirations. This association having nothing forced in it, nothing arbitrary, prompted only by some need of our being" [001-023]. "Man's needs being infinite, infinite will be his means of satisfying them, and it is this variety of needs which will concur in the establishment of general harmony" [001-024].

The anarchist alternative to capitalist production is not state ownership — which merely transfers the power of exploitation from private capitalists to state bureaucrats — but the direct organization of production by the workers themselves, coordinated through [[cooperation|voluntary cooperation]] rather than hierarchical command.
