# Propaganda by Deed

## Definition

[[propaganda|Propaganda]] by deed (*propagande par le fait*) is the anarchist strategy of spreading r[[progress|evolution]]ary ideas through exemplary actions rather than through speech and writing alone. In [[jean-grave|Grave]]'s treatment, the concept encompasses both the affirmative dimension — workers creating cooperative institutions, organizing autonomously — and the more controversial dimension of insurrectionary acts intended to awaken r[[progress|evolution]]ary consciousness [001-009]. [[jean-grave|Grave]] engages with the concept critically, acknowledging the "arduous" nature of [[propaganda|anarchist propaganda]] while interrogating whether certain forms of deed-based propaganda serve or undermine the r[[progress|evolution]]ary cause [003-005].

## The Necessity of Action

[[jean-grave|Grave]] recognizes that anarchist ideas, originating from "unknown persons without means of propaganda," face an inherent difficulty in reaching a broad audience through conventional channels [001-005]. The anarchist press is perpetually under threat: "readily suspended, the Socialist propaganda muzzled, authority rein[[the-state|state]]d" [011-011]. Given these constraints, direct action — the visible, tangible demonstration of anarchist principles — becomes not merely an option but a necessity.

> We understand and proclaim action, propaganda by deed; that is to say, that the act is the best means of propagating ideas, of making them penetrate into the masses. [001-009]

## The Two Faces of Propaganda by Deed

[[jean-grave|Grave]] distinguishes between two modes of deed-based propaganda. The constructive mode involves workers organizing their own institutions — cooperatives, unions, mutual aid societies — that prefigure the anarchist society within the shell of the existing order [019-024]. The insurrectionary mode, more controversial, involves acts of rebellion — strikes, occupations, and in some interpretations, individual acts of [[force-and-violence|violence]] against representatives of authority.

[[jean-grave|Grave]]'s position on the latter is shaped by his own experience: the chapter on [[war|militarism]] led to his arrest and [[prison|imprisonment]], demonstrating that even written propaganda can be met with [[the-state|state]] [[force-and-violence|violence]] when it threatens established power [[TOC|P03]]-001]. This experience informs his nuanced treatment — he does not condemn insurrectionary propaganda outright but insists that acts must be judged by their effects on the broader r[[progress|evolution]]ary project [017-013].

> It is likewise with "the propaganda by deed." How it has been wrapped in calumny! The bourgeoisie has made it synonymous with bomb-throwing and assassination. [017-014]

## Critique and Refinement

[[jean-grave|Grave]] is not uncritical of certain interpretations of [[direct-action|propaganda by deed]]. He emphasizes that "we fulfill our task of propaganda, we sow our ideas of renovation" — the propagandist's role is to plant seeds, not to impose blueprints [016-006]. He questions whether some forms of dramatic action "injurious to the propaganda and contrary to the end in view" might alienate the very [[class|working class]] they aim to awaken [017-013].

The effectiveness of propaganda is ultimately measured not by its dramatic visibility but by its capacity to build lasting r[[progress|evolution]]ary consciousness. "The Anarchist propaganda is far from having returned all that it can give" — a [[the-state|state]]ment of patience and persistence rather than disappointment [018-004].
