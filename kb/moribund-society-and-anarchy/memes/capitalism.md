# Capitalism

## Definition and Function

In [[jean-grave|Grave]]'s anarchist analysis, capitalism is the social and economic organization that rests on private ownership of the means of production, the extraction of surplus value through wage labor, and the maintenance of class rule by [[government|the state]]. It is "so paltry, so narrow" that "large aspirations find themselves so cramped in it" — a system that "annihilates so many good intents, so many hopes, crushing and killing so many individualities that cannot stoop to its narrow views" [002-017]. The capitalist order is not merely an economic arrangement but a total social system whose logic of competition and exploitation permeates every sphere of human activity, from art and science to [[the-law|law]] and morality.

> Capitalistic society is so paltry, so narrow; large aspirations find themselves so cramped in it; it annihilates so many good intents, so many hopes, crushing and killing so many individualities that cannot stoop to its narrow views, that, could it succeed in stifling the voice of every living Anarchist, its oppressions would raise up new ones, equally implacable. [002-017]

## Private Property as Foundation

The whole edifice of capitalism rests upon private property — an institution that [[jean-grave|Grave]] traces not to any natural right but to "robbery, fraud, and the right of [[force-and-violence|force]]" [010-002]. The economists who treat private property as a natural [[the-law|law]] have inverted cause and effect: the inequalities generated by a "bad organization" are presented as "natural [[the-law|law]]s" that legitimize "the most monstrous social crimes, the most heinous piracies of capitalism" [010-014]. Under this arrangement, the vast majority of social wealth is concentrated in the hands of a parasitic minority who produce nothing themselves but live "as parasites at the expense of the mass whom they have enslaved" [013-003].

> Economists have taken social facts resulting from a bad organization, and setting them up as "natural laws," making them the cause of what exists while they are but the effects, decorating these absurdities with the name of science, they have pretended to legitimize the most monstrous social crimes, the most heinous piracies of capitalism. [010-014]

## Wage Labor and Exploitation

The mechanism of capitalist exploitation operates through the [[wage-labor|wage system]]. The economists' division of the product of labor into three shares — the worker's wage, the return on fixed capital, and the capitalist's profit — appears fair on the surface but conceals a fundamental theft. The worker's wage, [[jean-grave|Grave]] observes, is insufficient even to meet the most basic needs: "Can this workman live the larger life which ought to be assured to those who produce, in order to satisfy their physical and intellectual needs while working? Nonsense!" [011-014]. The capitalist, by contrast, pockets "three-fourths of the product" without producing anything himself [011-012].

> The development of machinery has permitted the exploiters to reduce the number of their hands; the unemployed, become more and more numerous, have diminished wages and multiplied periods of enforced idleness. [011-016]

The lie that capital represents savings from the capitalist's own labor — "the pleasures of which he has deprived himself" — is exposed by the fact that large fortunes "are reserved to the big proprietors, big shareholders, big manufacturers, big speculators, who do not work themselves but employ workmen by hundreds" [011-018]. [[private-property|Capital]] is indeed accumulated labor — but it is the labor of others "accumulated in the hands of one person — a robber!" [011-018].

## Competition and the Antagonism of Interests

[[private-property|Capital]]ist society, [[jean-grave|Grave]] argues, "is based upon the antagonism of interests, and makes every individual the enemy of his neighbor" [012-001]. The seller's interest opposes the buyer's; each branch of production hopes for calamity to befall its competitors; workers driven into unemployment by machinery are pitted against one another for diminishing opportunities. "Everything in our traditional 'society' tends to split up mankind" [012-002]. This structural antagonism is not an unfortunate byproduct but the very engine of the system — competition is the mechanism that drives [[wage-labor|wages]] down, concentrates wealth, and reproduces class power.

> The reign of solidarity must replace that of competition. But can anyone seriously hope to establish solidarity while maintaining the cause which engenders competition? [016-009]

## Machinery and the Paradox of Progress

[[jean-grave|Grave]] identifies one of capitalism's deepest contradictions in its relationship to technology. Machinery, "a development begotten by all our acquired knowledge transmitted from generation to generation," ought to benefit every human being by increasing productive power and reducing necessary labor [011-020]. Under capitalism, however, machinery has brought "nothing but an increase of misery and privation to the workers" [011-020]. The capitalists alone benefit from mechanical inventions, which they use to reduce their work[[force-and-violence|force]] and intensify competition among the unemployed and employed, driving [[wage-labor|wages]] below subsistence levels. The more the stores are "crammed with products the more the producers die of hunger" [011-020] — an absurdity that exposes the fundamental irrationality of the system.

## Collapse and Revolution

Despite its apparent stability, capitalism carries within itself the seeds of its own destruction. [[jean-grave|Grave]] notes that "the complete collapse at the end of so short a period of power is hurrying it on to a speedy fall" [011-024]. The [[class|working class]]es, recognizing the injustice of their condition, "will demand free possession of what they produce, and will revolt if the denial of the request continues" [013-005]. No argument about the alleged wickedness of human nature can hold back this transformation: "However it be, *the r[[progress|evolution]] will come*!" [013-007].

> Vainly does capitalism seek to entrench itself behind the argument that 'man is evil;' the revolution will come… The sphinx interrogates us and we answer without fear, for we Anarchists, destroyers of laws and property, we know the key to the enigma. [013-007]
