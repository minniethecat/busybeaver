# Class

## The Origin of Class Division

For [[jean-grave|Grave]], class is not a natural category but a historical product of private property and the concentration of social wealth in the hands of a minority. The institution of property creates a structural division between those who own the means of production and those who must sell their labor to survive. As this division consolidates over generations, it produces not only material inequality but distinct interests, cultures, and ideologies. The ruling class develops an elaborate apparatus — [[government|the state]], the [[the-law|law]], the magistracy — to defend its privileges, while the working class is systematically deprived of the leisure, education, and means required for its own emancipation [001-010].

The capitalist class, [[jean-grave|Grave]] observes, absorbs "the greater share of the product" while contributing nothing to production itself [011-012]. This arrangement is maintained not by superior ability or natural [[the-law|law]] but by [[force-and-violence|force]]: "from the moment it is admitted that the capitalists cannot put their capital into use without the [[association|cooperation]] of the worker, the latter becomes the most important factor in production" [011-020]. The irrationality of the class system is exposed by the simple fact that the capitalists "could produce nothing without the [[association|cooperation]] of the workers, whilst the latter, by coming to an understanding among themselves and uniting their [[force-and-violence|force]]s, could produce very well without the assistance of capital" [011-020].

## The Ruling Class and Its Instruments

The ruling class, [[jean-grave|Grave]] argues, does not govern by economic power alone. It relies on an interlocking system of institutions — [[government|the state]], the [[the-law|law]], patriotism, and the [[militarism|military]] — that collectively ensure the continued submission of the exploited. The [[the-state|state]] is "the instrument by which the privileged class maintains its domination" over those it exploits. The [[the-law|law]] is not an impartial arbiter but a tool that codifies class privilege. The magistracy dispenses "justice" while serving class interests: "It is hard to understand that, amongst the countless number of persons who have undergone the examinations of 'justice,' not one has yet been found sufficiently free from prejudice to go and lift up the robes of those who had struck him and show the public that all these togs serve but to mask men subject to the same weaknesses and errors as the rest of humanity, not counting the crimes inspired by their class interests" [010-005].

[[patriotism|Patriotism]], in turn, is the ideological instrument that persuades the working class to fight and die in wars from which only the ruling class benefits. As [[jean-grave|Grave]] notes, whether a war is won or lost, the workers remain "the ever-to-be-exploited, submissive cattle, subject to impress, which the capitalist class is anxious to keep under its thumb" [013-007]. [[colonization|Colonization]] extends this logic abroad, opening new territories "for the benefit of the ruling classes" — a process [[jean-grave|Grave]] describes as "brigandage and highway robbery" conducted not by private criminals but by [[the-state|government]]s acting in the name of civilization [014-002].

## The Working Class and Intellectual Emancipation

[[jean-grave|Grave]] places particular emphasis on the intellectual dimension of class oppression. The working class is kept in subjection not only by material deprivation but by ignorance — a condition deliberately maintained by the ruling class. "The possibility and the continuance of their exploitation by the capitalistic class proceed from their ignorance. They must know how to free themselves intellectually if they wish to be able to free themselves materially" [011-024].

This intellectual emancipation is not a matter of acquiring credentials within the existing system but of developing the capacity for independent thought and collective reasoning. The bourgeoisie succeeded in 1789 only "because it had prepared itself intellectually for such substitution" [011-024]. The workers must do the same — not to replace one ruling class with another but to establish a society in which no class can rule. "The idea of free individual initiative once being established, people should be enabled to learn how to reason and to combine their initiative" [011-024].

## Class and the Coming Revolution

[[jean-grave|Grave]] is clear that the class struggle cannot be resolved through reform or gradual improvement. The structural antagonism between classes is built into the very foundations of [[capitalism|capitalist society]] and can only be abolished by abolishing those foundations. "Vainly does capitalism seek to entrench itself behind the argument that 'man is evil;' the r[[progress|evolution]] will come" [013-007].

Yet the r[[progress|evolution]] [[jean-grave|Grave]] envisions is not a simple inversion of class power — the workers seizing [[government|the state]] in order to rule in their turn. That would merely reproduce the class relation under new management. The anarchist r[[progress|evolution]] aims at the abolition of class as such, through the destruction of the institutions — property, [[government|the state]], wage labor — that generate class division. The workers "will demand free possession of what they produce, and will revolt if the denial of the request continues" [013-005]. In doing so, they act not as a class seeking power but as human beings reclaiming the conditions of a fully human existence.

> However it be, *the revolution will come*! The sphinx interrogates us and we answer without fear, for we Anarchists, destroyers of laws and property, we know the key to the enigma. [013-007]
