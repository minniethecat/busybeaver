# Labor

## Definition

In *Moribund [[society|Society]] and [[anarchy|Anarchy]]*, [[jean-grave|Jean Grave]] analyzes labor as the creative human activity that capitalism transforms into a commodity. Under the existing system, "manual labor does not even" earn the worker full subsistence value — the worker's product is appropriated by the [[capitalism|capitalist]] through the mechanism of [[wage-labor|wages]]. Grave's critique of labor under capitalism is central to his broader argument that the existing social order is fundamentally exploitative and must be replaced by [[cooperation|voluntary cooperation]].

## The Capitalist Appropriation of Labor

### The Wage System

Grave argues that capitalism systematically undervalues labor. The worker "is paid right along for his labor and consequently runs no risks" — but this apparent security is illusory, as the worker receives only enough "to feed, clothe, and lodge himself and finally to repair the waste of his organism" (003-034), while the surplus value of labor is extracted by the employer. This arrangement, Grave contends, is presented by economists as equitable exchange when in fact it is systematic theft.

### Capital vs. Labor

Grave directly challenges the claim that capitalists contribute superior intelligence or risk-bearing that justifies their disproportionate rewards. He asks rhetorically whether "those who, by a stroke on the bourse, by stock-jobbing and monopolies, sweep away millions, have expended an intelligence a million times superior" to the worker (003-037). The real productive power, Grave insists, lies with workers: "the capitalists, with all their capital, all their machinery, could produce nothing without the cooperation of the workers" (003-040).

## Labor and Social Transformation

Grave connects the labor question to the necessity of social revolution. In [[bourgeois]] society, labor is organized around the imperative of [[money]] accumulation rather than human need. Grave envisions a transformed society where labor is freely associated: "the workers, by coming to an understanding among themselves and uniting their forces" can reorganize production without capitalist direction (003-040). This is not merely an economic reform but a fundamental restructuring of social relations around the principle of free [[association]].

## The Dignity of Labor

Grave challenges the devaluation of manual labor that pervades bourgeois society. The worker, in his analysis, is the true producer of social wealth, yet is systematically degraded by a system that exalts [[money|financial speculation]] over productive work. Grave's anarchism restores dignity to labor by envisioning it as a freely chosen activity performed in [[solidarity]] with others, rather than as a coerced necessity imposed by economic compulsion.
