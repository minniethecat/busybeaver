# Civilization

## Civilization as Ideology

In [[jean-grave|Grave]]'s anarchist analysis, "civilization" functions as one of the master terms of bourgeois ideology — a word that transforms conquest into benevolence, exploitation into uplift, and mass death into moral progress. [[colonization|Colonization]] is presented not as "brigandage and highway robbery for the benefit of the [[class|ruling class]]es" but as the "civilizing" of undeveloped peoples [014-002]. The [[militarism|military]] expeditions that carve up Africa and Asia are framed as missions of enlightenment rather than campaigns of plunder. The term provides moral cover for atrocities that, committed by private individuals, would be recognized as crimes.

> If a government… declare war against remote peoples which it knows to be too feeble to resist it, let it take possession of their country, subject them to an entire system of exploitation, force its products upon them, massacre them if they attempt to escape this exploitation — oh, then, this is moral! It is no longer called robbery or assassination; there is an honorable word for covering up the dishonorable deeds that government commits: this is called "civilizing" undeveloped peoples. [014-002]

## The Civilizers and Their Work

[[jean-grave|Grave]] relentlessly catalogs the human cost of the civilizing mission. "Let anyone take up the history of conquests, and then tell us which were the most savage — those who were called so, or the 'civilized'" [016-018]. The pattern is universal: wherever Europeans have imposed themselves by [[force-and-violence|force]], "the native populations decrease and disappear; everywhere drunkenness, syphilis, and other European importations mow them down in great swaths, atrophy and anaemiate those who survive" [016-018].

The genuine motive, [[jean-grave|Grave]] insists, is not cultural benevolence but commercial greed. "What the [[class|ruling class]]es must have is new markets for their products and new peoples to exploit" [014-004]. The process follows a predictable logic: "They commence by exploiting commercially and finish by exploiting in every way, when once these tribes have been brought under their protectorate" [014-004]. The question is never whether these peoples want European civilization — it is imposed at gunpoint, and resistance is met with massacre.

> We have taken a people, strong, robust, and healthy; in forty or fifty years from now we shall have them turned into a horde of anaemics, brutalized, miserable, decimated, corrupted, who will shortly disappear from the surface of the globe. Then the civilizing job will be finished! [016-018]

## Civilization Against the Worker

The ideology of civilization operates not only abroad but at home. The same logic that declares non-European peoples "inferior" and in need of tutelage is applied to the [[class|working class]], who are "taxed with belonging to the inferior classes" [016-002]. The capitalist, [[jean-grave|Grave]] notes, treats the worker as "a beast of burden whose sole role consists in creating prosperity for the 'elect.'" Civilization, in this sense, is the ideology of class rule — it naturalizes hierarchy by presenting the dominant group as inherently more developed, more rational, more entitled to command.

> As you have undertaken to destroy these races — not inferior, as we shall show later on, but merely retarded — you tend in like manner to destroy the working class, which you also qualify as inferior. [014-010]

## The Decline of Civilizations and the Hope of Renewal

[[jean-grave|Grave]]'s historical perspective places European civilization itself within the cycle of rise and fall that has claimed every previous civilization. "Every civilization, in its decline, has seen a new race surging in, which, being capable of assimilating the knowledge of the race it replaced, yielded in exchange a new brain, new aptitudes, young and vigorous blood" [016-016]. The Latin races, he observes, are already entering their decadence, which "will shortly become a death-agony unless a social transformation occurs in time to rejuvenate the physical and moral decay entailed by the [[capitalism|capitalistic system]]" [016-016].

This is not a counsel of despair but a call to action. Civilization in its existing form — predatory, hierarchical, exploitative — is unsustainable and will collapse under the weight of its own contradictions. But a different kind of civilization is possible: one built not on conquest and exploitation but on solidarity, [[association|free association]], and the full development of every human being. The anarchist task is to hasten the decay of the old and midwife the birth of the new.

> But at the rate your civilization is going on… you, in your turn, will not be long in succumbing likewise under your indolence and laziness, even as fell the Greek and Roman civilizations. [014-010]
