# London

## Overview

London is mentioned in *Moribund Society and Anarchy* as the location where translator Voltairine de Cleyre met author Jean Grave at the residence of an exiled French comrade, leading to her undertaking the English translation of the work. London in the late nineteenth century served as a significant center for exiled European anarchists and radicals.

## In Grave's Text

The translator's preface provides the key reference to London. De Cleyre recounts: "In 1897 the writer met Jean Grave at the residence of an exiled French comrade in London, and there engaged to undertake the work, the author concurring" [013-013]. This meeting in London was the catalyst for the English translation of *Moribund Society and Anarchy*.

> In 1897 the writer met Jean Grave at the residence of an exiled French comrade in London, and there engaged to undertake the work, the author concurring. [013-013]

The London meeting connects to the broader theme of international anarchist networks. The Pouget edition of Grave's work, from which de Cleyre translated, was "ostensibly published in London" after the original Stock edition was suppressed in France [013-013], highlighting London's role as a refuge for radical publishing beyond the reach of French authorities.

> The P. V. Stock edition having been suppressed, E. Pouget, the daring publisher of *Père Peinard*, brought out another, ostensibly published in London. [013-013]
