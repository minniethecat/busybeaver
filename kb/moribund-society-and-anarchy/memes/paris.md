# Paris

## Overview

Paris, the capital of [[france|France]], was in the late nineteenth century the undisputed center of [[france|French]] political and intellectual life — and, for [[jean-grave|Jean Grave]], the city where the anarchist movement maintained its most active presence. As editor of *Les Temps Nouveaux* (formerly *La Révolte*), [[jean-grave|Grave]] worked from Paris, engaging in the intense debates and organizational work that sustained [[france|French]] anarchism through decades of repression. Paris was also the site of the [[paris-commune|1871 Commune]], the r[[progress|evolution]]ary experiment that became both inspiration and cautionary tale for the international anarchist movement.

## Connection to Moribund Society and Anarchy

Paris appears in [[jean-grave|Grave]]'s text primarily through its historical significance as the site of [[paris-commune|the Commune]] and through his analysis of urban working-class conditions. The concentration of workers in Paris — the "dangerous classes" whom the bourgeoisie feared and the anarchists sought to organize — exemplifies the social dynamics that [[jean-grave|Grave]] analyzes throughout *Moribund [[society|Society]] and [[anarchy|Anarchy]]*. The city's role as both the seat of [[the-state|state]] power and the locus of r[[progress|evolution]]ary resistance embodies the contradiction that [[jean-grave|Grave]] identifies at the heart of capitalist civilization.
