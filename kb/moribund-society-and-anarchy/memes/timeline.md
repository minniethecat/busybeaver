*Moribund Society and Anarchy* draws on two centuries of radical thought and revolutionary upheaval. This page presents the historical figures, events, and organizations that populate Grave's argument in chronological order, from the French Revolution through the Dreyfus Affair. The persons referenced are overwhelmingly 19th-century European radicals, while the events stretch from 1789 to the 1890s.

The chronological arc runs from the French Revolution (1789) through the Paris Commune (1871) to the Dreyfus Affair (1894–1906). Grave wrote *Moribund Society and Anarchy* in the aftermath of these events, and his references form a coherent historical narrative of state oppression and popular resistance.
