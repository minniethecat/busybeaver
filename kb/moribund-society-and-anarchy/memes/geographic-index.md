Grave's anarchism is rooted in the European experience, and the places he discusses — France above all, but also Russia, Switzerland, and England — are not mere backdrop but active participants in his argument. Each location carries a specific political meaning: Paris is the crucible of revolution; Russia is the home of Bakunin and Kropotkin's anti-authoritarian traditions; the Jura Federation in Switzerland represents anarchist organizational practice.
