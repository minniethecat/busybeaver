# The State

## Definition

For [[jean-grave|Grave]], [[government|the state]] is not a neutral arbiter of social conflicts nor an expression of the general will — it is the organized instrument through which a [[class|ruling class]] maintains its privileges. The state apparatus encompasses "government," "the [[the-law|law]]," "the magistracy," "the police," and "[[militarism|the army]]" — a composite machinery of [[force-and-violence|coercion]] that presents itself as the guarantor of order while functioning as the protector of exploitation [001-007]. The anarchist critique of [[government|the state]] is therefore total: it is not a matter of reforming or democratizing [[government|the state]] but of abolishing it entirely.

## The State as Class Instrument

[[jean-grave|Grave]] traces [[government|the state]]'s origins to the earliest formations of social hierarchy. When the warrior caste "imposed its authority upon other members of the clan," the primordial form of [[government|the state]] was born [013-002]. Successive historical forms — the feudal state, the absolute monarchy, the bourgeois republic — differ in their constitutional arrangements but share a common essence: the concentration of coercive power in the hands of a minority [[class|ruling class]].

> The government, which is the very essence of the state, is nothing but the organization of the privileges of a class. [001-007]

The bourgeois state represents the most sophisticated form of this arrangement, precisely because it disguises class rule behind the forms of democratic participation. Universal suffrage, parliamentary representation, and the rule of [[the-law|law]] do not eliminate class domination; they render it less visible and therefore more durable.

## The State and the Individual

The state's claim to authority over the individual is absolute and without legitimate foundation. Every state demands that individuals surrender their autonomy "for the common good" — a phrase [[jean-grave|Grave]] treats as the classic sophism of authoritarianism [011-003]. The state's pretension to represent the collective interest is exposed as a lie by the simple observation that it consistently acts to protect the interests of the possessing class against the producing class.

> Since they are the strongest and have the law — which they have made — on their side, the "collective interest" becomes an invincible argument with which to crush individual claims. [011-003]

## Abolition, Not Reform

[[jean-grave|Grave]] rejects all reformist approaches to [[government|the state]]. The history of concessions — the [[emancipation|freedom]] of the communes wrested from the baronage, the suffrage extensions won through struggle — demonstrates that improvements come not from [[government|the state]]'s benevolence but from resistance to its power [003-009]. To seek to capture [[government|the state]] for socialist purposes is to misunderstand its nature: [[government|the state]] is not a neutral instrument that can be wielded for different ends but a specific form of social organization that inevitably reproduces hierarchy.

> The State would become the proprietor. The workers would be no more advanced; they would simply have changed masters. [019-025]

This critique of state socialism — the idea that nationalization of industry represents progress toward emancipation — is a recurring theme. [[jean-grave|Grave]] insists that substituting [[government|the state]] for the private capitalist does not abolish exploitation but merely transfers it to a new master.

## The State in International Relations

The state is also the organizing principle of international [[force-and-violence|violence]]. The "arbitrary boundaries" between peoples — frontiers, nationalities, "the country" — are state constructs that serve to divide the international [[class|working class]] and justify [[militarism|military]] expenditure [013-015]. Colonialism, [[war|militarism]], and commercial competition are not aberrations of particular states but logical expressions of [[government|the state]] form itself [014-003].
