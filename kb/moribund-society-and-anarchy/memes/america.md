# America

## Overview

America, specifically the United States, is referenced by Jean Grave in *Moribund Society and Anarchy* as part of the global landscape of anarchist propaganda and as an example of capitalist militarism and colonial exploitation. The translator Voltairine de Cleyre specifically adapted the text for an American audience, noting the country's recent turn toward militarism and colonization.

## In Grave's Text

Grave mentions America in multiple contexts. In the translator's preface, de Cleyre observes that America had embarked upon the "manifest destiny" of "civilized nations," with increased standing army and military ambition [013-013]. She notes that the anarchist idea had spread widely, with propagandist organs created in many countries including America [044-044].

Grave draws parallels between European and American colonialism, referencing "the history of the conquests in America by Spain" as an example of the brutality of so-called civilization [488-488]. He also observes that "from France to Russia and from America to Australia" the same civilization extends everywhere, with opposing classes rather than opposing races [527-527].

> ... the anarchist idea has made headway. Groups have been founded, propagandist organs have been created in France, Belgium, Italy, Spain, Portugal, Holland, England, Norway, America, Australia... [044-044]

Grave further notes the economic reality that even where workers achieved higher wages in North and South America, the cost of living rose proportionately, leaving them in the same position [866-866].
