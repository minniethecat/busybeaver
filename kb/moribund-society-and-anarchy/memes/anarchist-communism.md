# Anarchist Communism

## Definition

Anarchist communism is the strand of anarchist thought that combines the rejection of all forms of government and authority with the communist principle of collective ownership of the means of production. Unlike authoritarian forms of communism, anarchist communism holds that the abolition of private property must be accompanied by the simultaneous abolition of the state, and that social organization should rest on free association and voluntary cooperation rather than centralized direction. In *Moribund Society and Anarchy*, Jean Grave contrasts the Communist-Anarchists with the extreme individualists, noting that neither camp intends to produce the caricatures their opponents ascribe to them [075-001].

## Grave's Analysis

Jean Grave addresses anarchist communism primarily by situating it in relation to its philosophical opposite — extreme individualism. He observes that "the Communist-Anarchists" have never "meant to preach abnegation and renunciation for the individual in the society which they anticipate" [075-001]. Grave's position is that communism and individualism, properly understood, are not contradictory but complementary. He argues that "individuality cannot develop except in the community" and that "the latter cannot exist unless the former evolves freely" [036-001]. This mutual interdependence is central to Grave's anarchist vision: the individual flourishes only within a cooperative social framework, and genuine community is impossible without the free development of each person.

Grave traces the intuitive origins of anarchist communism to Rabelais and the Abbey of Thelemes, though he notes its limitations there — the community was still "reserved to a minority of privileged persons attended by a train of domestics" [040-001]. The movement only began to take coherent shape through the work of Bakunin, who in the Jurassian Federation "propagated the idea of Proudhon — Anarchy the enemy of authority — but developing, enlarging, incarnating it in social demands" [042-001].
