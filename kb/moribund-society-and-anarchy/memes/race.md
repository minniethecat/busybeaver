# Race

## The Doctrine of Racial Hierarchy

[[jean-grave|Grave]] devotes sustained attention to the theory of racial inferiority, recognizing it as the ideological justification for both [[colonization|colonial conquest]] abroad and [[exploitation|class exploitation]] at home. The argument that certain races are inherently inferior is not, he contends, a disinterested scientific conclusion but a political weapon: "Is not this, moreover, the same argument employed against the worker to justify the exploitation to which he is subjected by taxing him with belonging to the inferior classes?" [016-002]. The doctrine serves a single function — to legitimize domination by claiming that the dominated deserve their fate.

[[jean-grave|Grave]] traces the intellectual lineage of this doctrine to the same spirit of domination that once placed man at the center of the universe. Having been [[force-and-violence|force]]d by science to abandon the anthropocentric theory, "man's spirit of domination was incapable of resolving to accept the facts as they are and consider itself an intruder; summing everything up it re-entrenched itself in this idea of superior races, and as a matter of course every race declared itself to be the most intelligent, the most beautiful, the most perfect" [016-004].

## The Scientific Critique

[[jean-grave|Grave]] systematically dismantles the scientific pretensions of racial hierarchy. The claim that certain races have remained stationary while whites have progressed ignores the vast scale of [[progress|evolution]]ary time. The ten thousand years of visible stagnation among certain African tribes "represent but an insignificant amount in the history of the unfoldment of humanity" [016-010]. The primitive Egyptian, ten thousand years after he first shaped a stone, "would have presented hardly any sensible improvement to the observer, and would also have appeared to be of a fundamentally inferior race" [016-010].

More devastating still is the historical evidence: the Egyptians who built the great monuments of antiquity "are not whites; and this same people, which is classed among the 'superior' races of antiquity, is now classed among the 'inferior' races" [016-010]. The categories of superior and inferior shift according to the needs of power, exposing their true nature as political constructs rather than scientific facts.

> What a mass of contradictions! For the necessities of the argument the Egyptians are alternately both "superior" and "inferior." [016-010]

## Civilization as Destruction

The real history of contact between Europeans and non-European peoples, [[jean-grave|Grave]] argues, is not one of civilizing uplift but of extermination. "Highly flourishing civilizations thus disappeared, no one knowing what they might have brought forth; we cannot judge of them by the brutalized and degenerate natives which the United States is in the course of wiping out" [016-014]. The Iroquois, the Hurons, the empires of Mexico and Peru — all were "hunted, despoiled, massacred" by conquerors who then cited the resulting degradation as evidence of innate inferiority.

> By what right do we speak of the inferiority of other races when their present condition proceeds from our barbarous persecutions? [016-014]

The same pattern appears wherever European colonization has taken root: "everywhere the native populations decrease and disappear; everywhere drunkenness, syphilis, and other European importations mow them down in great swaths" [016-018]. This is not the unfortunate byproduct of an otherwise beneficent process but its essential character.

## Race, Class, and the Loss of Diversity

[[jean-grave|Grave]] insists that the destruction of non-European peoples is not only a crime against them but a loss to humanity as a whole. Each people possesses "certain aptitudes, certain moral, intellectual, and physical qualities, which, had they been allowed to evolve freely, would have enabled them to take their part in the labor of human civilization" [016-016]. The Australians who invented the boomerang — a device "which the Europeans, in spite of their ability, have not succeeded in imitating" — demonstrate that even the most "backward" peoples possess unique creative faculties [016-016].

> When we reflect upon the massacre of inoffensive tribes, on whole races vanished or about to disappear, our thoughts overflow with sadness and melancholy; for we ask ourselves if these "inferior" brothers did not possess some of those qualities, so many of which are lacking in us. [016-018]

The anarchist vision, by contrast, recognizes that race — like class — is a division to be overcome, not a hierarchy to be en[[force-and-violence|force]]d. The increasing interconnection of the world's peoples means that "races are bound to disappear by fusing, mixing with each other through intercrossings" [016-018]. The tragedy is not that this fusion is occurring but that entire peoples are being destroyed before they can contribute their unique share to the common human inheritance.
