# Europe

## Overview

Europe serves as both the geographic center of anarchist activity and a critical foil in Jean Grave's *Moribund Society and Anarchy*. Grave invokes European civilization primarily to expose its hypocrisy: a continent that claims to embody progress and enlightenment while perpetrating colonial violence, racial hierarchy, and class exploitation.

## In Grave's Text

**Colonial Hypocrisy**: In Chapter XIV ("Colonization"), Grave contrasts European property protections at home with colonial lawlessness abroad: "the institution which protects property in Europe does not recognize it in another latitude" [014-009]. He documents how European colonists treat natives as "beasts of burden" and introduces "drunkenness, syphilis, and other European importations" that decimate native populations [014-011].

**Civilization's Cycle**: In Chapter XV, Grave places Europe within a broader historical cycle. He observes the "decadence of the Latin races" and speculates that the Slavic races, "having more lately come into the current of European civilization," may succeed them [015-020]. He argues that European conquest destroyed flourishing indigenous civilizations [015-011].

**Anarchist Propagation**: Europe is the hub of the anarchist movement Grave describes. Groups and propagandist organs were created "a little everywhere" across the continent—France, Belgium, Italy, Spain, Holland, England, Norway—and in multiple European languages [ch01].

## Historical Context

In the late nineteenth century, Europe was at its imperial zenith, controlling most of Africa and large parts of Asia. The Berlin Conference of 1884–1885 had formalized the "Scramble for Africa." Grave's critique of European colonialism was part of a broader anarchist anti-imperialist tradition that linked state authority abroad with class domination at home. The anarchist movement itself was centered in European cities—Paris, London, Geneva, Barcelona—where exile communities and publishing networks sustained international revolutionary culture.
