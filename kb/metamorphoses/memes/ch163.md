[146-001] The superiority of Augustus to his great predecessor is insisted on by the courteous poet.

[146-002] Thus raised, his glorious offspring Julius view’d.

[146-003] Beneficently great, and scattering good,

[146-004] Deeds, that his own surpass’d, with joy beheld,

[146-005] And his large heart dilates to be excell’d.

[146-006] What though this prince refuses to receive

[146-007] The preference, which his juster subjects give;

[146-008] Fame uncontroll’d, that no restraint obeys,

[146-009] The homage, shunn’d by modest virtue, pays,

[146-010] And proves disloyal only in his praise.

[146-011] Though great his sire, him greater we proclaim:

[146-012] So Atreus yields to Agamemnon’s fame;

[146-013] Achilles so superior honours won,

[146-014] And Peleus must submit to Peleus’ son:

[146-015] Examples yet more noble to disclose,

[146-016] So Saturn was eclipsed, when [[jupiter|Jove]] to empire rose:

[146-017] Jove rules the heavens, the earth Augustus sways;

[146-018] Each claims a monarch’s, and a father’s praise.

[146-019] Celestials, who for Rome your cares employ;

[146-020] Ye gods, who guarded the remains of [[troy|Troy]];

[146-021] Ye native gods, here born, and fix’d by fate;

[146-022] Quirinus, founder of the Roman state;

[146-023] Oh parent Mars, from whom Quirinus sprung;

[146-024] Chaste Vesta, Caesar’s household gods among

[146-025] Most sacred held; domestic [[apollo|Phoebus]], thou,

[146-026] To whom with Vesta chaste alike we bow;

[146-027] Great guardian of the high Tarpeian rock;

[146-028] And all ye powers, whom poets may invoke;

[146-029] Oh grant, that day may claim our sorrows late,

[146-030] When loved Augustus shall submit to fate,

[146-031] Visit those seats, where gods and heroes dwell,

[146-032] And leave, in tears, the world he ruled so well!
