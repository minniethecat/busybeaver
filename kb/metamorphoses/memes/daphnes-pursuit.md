# Apollo's Pursuit of Daphne

Apollo's pursuit of Daphne is the *Metamorphoses*' founding love story: the god of light, poetry, and prophecy is struck by Cupid's golden arrow and falls hopelessly in love with a nymph vowed to virginity. Her transformation into the laurel tree is the first human-to-plant metamorphosis in the poem and establishes the laurel as Apollo's sacred emblem.

## Characters

| Character | Role |
|-----------|------|
| Apollo | God of light, struck by Cupid's arrow, becomes the amorous pursuer |
| Daphne | River-nymph, daughter of Peneus, vowed to virginity, the pursued |
| Cupid | Strikes Apollo with desire and Daphne with aversion |

## Setting

The woods and river valleys of Thessaly, where Daphne hunts with Diana's retinue, and the banks of the Peneus River, where her father answers her prayer.

## Narrative

Apollo, proud from slaying Python, mocks Cupid's archery. Cupid retaliates: a golden arrow makes Apollo love Daphne; a leaden arrow makes Daphne reject all love. Apollo pursues; Daphne flees. As Apollo gains on her, Daphne prays to her father. Her feet root in the earth; bark encases her body; her arms become branches; her hair, leaves. Apollo embraces the laurel, declares it his sacred tree, and wreathes his brow with its leaves.

| Aspect | Detail |

| **Transformed Form** | Laurel tree (*Laurus nobilis*) |

| **Cause** | Escape from Apollo's pursuit |
