# Cadmus

Cadmus, son of Agenor of Tyre and brother of the abducted Europa, is the founder and first king of Thebes — one of the great city-founders of classical myth and the patriarchal origin of the most tragic dynasty in Ovid's *Metamorphoses*. His quest to find Europa leads him to consult the Delphic oracle, to follow a magical heifer, to slay a dragon sacred to Mars, and to sow the dragon's teeth — from which spring the armed Spartoi, the first citizens of Thebes. But the curse attached to the dragon's death shadows his entire lineage: his daughters Semele, Ino, Agave, and Autonoe, his grandson Actaeon, and his great-grandson Pentheus all meet violent or transformative ends, until Cadmus himself, weary of grief, acknowledges the dragon's sacredness and is transformed into a serpent — a metamorphosis that bookends the Theban cycle, returning the founder to the form of the creature whose death made Thebes possible.

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Cadmus's story spans Books III and IV. After Europa's abduction by Jupiter in the form of a bull, King Agenor orders his sons to search for their sister and never to return without her. Cadmus, unable to find Europa, consults the oracle of Apollo at Delphi, which instructs him to follow a heifer marked with a white crescent and to found a city where it lies down. The heifer leads him to the site of Thebes. When his companions go to fetch water from a nearby spring, they are killed by a dragon sacred to Mars. Cadmus slays the dragon and, at Minerva's instruction, sows its teeth in the ground — armed men spring up and immediately begin fighting each other until only five remain: the Spartoi, who help Cadmus found Thebes (019-001). But the dragon's death places a curse on Cadmus's house. His daughters and their children suffer a cascade of transformations and violent deaths across the next three books: Semele is incinerated by Jupiter's glory, Actaeon is torn apart by his own hounds, Pentheus is dismembered by his mother Agave, and Ino leaps into the sea with her son Melicerta. At last, the aged Cadmus, witnessing the ruin of his line, recognizes the sacredness of the dragon he killed and prays that if the gods' anger is so great, they should transform him too. As he speaks, he becomes a serpent, and Harmonia joins him in the same form, the two serpents gliding together into the forest (035-001).

### Key Episodes

- **The Search for Europa**: Agenor commands Cadmus to find his sister or face exile; the quest leads him to Greece
- **The Delphic Oracle**: Apollo instructs Cadmus to follow a heifer and found a city where it rests
- **The Dragon of Mars**: Cadmus slays the dragon at the future site of Thebes, incurring a curse on his lineage (019-001)
- **The Spartoi**: Cadmus sows the dragon's teeth; armed men spring up and battle until five survivors remain to become the first Thebans
- **The Final Transformation**: After witnessing the deaths and transformations of his descendants, Cadmus and Harmonia are transformed into serpents (035-001)

## Transformation

- **Form**: Man (hero, king) → Serpent
- **Agent**: The gods, in answer to Cadmus's own prayer — the curse of the dragon of Mars finally claiming its killer
- **Cause**: A lifetime of grief over the ruin of his house, and the delayed justice for the dragon he slew
