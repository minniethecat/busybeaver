[103-001] A wolf, which desolates the plains of Trachinia, is changed into marble by the intercession of Thetis.

[103-002] While they astonish’d heard the king relate

[103-003] These wonders of his hapless brother’s fate,

[103-004] The prince’s herdsman at the court arrives,

[103-005] And fresh surprise to all the audience gives.

[103-006] “Oh Peleus! Peleus! dreadful news I bear,”

[103-007] He said, and trembled as he spoke for fear.

[103-008] The worst affrighted Peleus bid him tell,

[103-009] While Ceyx too grew pale with friendly zeal.

[103-010] Thus he began: “When [[apollo|Sol]] mid-heaven had gain’d,

[103-011] And half his way was pass’d, and half remain’d,

[103-012] I to the level shore my cattle drove,

[103-013] And let them freely in the meadows rove.

[103-014] Some stretch’d at length, admire the watery plain,

[103-015] Some cropp’d the herb, some wanton swam the main

[103-016] A temple stands of antique make hard by,

[103-017] Where no gilt domes, or marble, lure the eye.

[103-018] Unpolish’d rafters bear its lowly height,

[103-019] Hid by a grove, as ancient, from the sight.

[103-020] Here Nereus, and the Nereids they adore:

[103-021] I learn’d it from the man who thither bore

[103-022] His net to dry it on the sunny shore.

[103-023] Adjoins a lake, enclosed with willows round,

[103-024] Where swelling waves have overflow’d the mound,

[103-025] And muddy, stagnate, on the lower ground,

[103-026] From thence a rustling noise, increasing, flies,

[103-027] Strikes the still shore, and frights us with surprise;

[103-028] Straight a huge wolf rush’d from the marshy wood,

[103-029] His jaws besmear’d with mingled foam and blood,

[103-030] Though equally by hunger urged, and rage,

[103-031] His appetite he minds not to assuage;

[103-032] Naught that he meets his rapid fury spares,

[103-033] But the whole herd with mad disorder tears.

[103-034] Some of our men, who strove to drive him thence,

[103-035] Torn by his teeth, have died in their defence;

[103-036] The echoing lakes, the sea, and fields, and shore,

[103-037] Impurpled blush with streams of reeking gore:

[103-038] Delay is loss, nor have we time for thought,

[103-039] While yet some few remain alive, we ought

[103-040] To seize our arms, and, with confederate force,

[103-041] Try if we so can stop his bloody course.”

[103-042] But Peleus cared not for his ruin’d herd,

[103-043] His crime he call’d to mind, and thence inferr’d

[103-044] That Psamathe’s revenge this havoc made,

[103-045] In sacrifice to murder’d Phocus’ shade.

[103-046] The king commands his servants to their arms,

[103-047] Resolved to go, but the loud noise alarms

[103-048] His lovely queen, who from her chamber flew,

[103-049] And her half-platted hair behind her threw,

[103-050] [[About]] his neck she hung with loving fears,

[103-051] And now with words, and now with pleading tears,

[103-052] Entreated that he’d send his men alone,

[103-053] And stay himself, to save two lives in one.

[103-054] Then Peleus: “Your just fears, oh queen, forget,

[103-055] Too much the offer leaves me in your debt:

[103-056] No arms against the monster I shall bear,

[103-057] But the sea nymphs appease with humble prayer.”

[103-058] The citadel’s high turrets pierce the sky,

[103-059] Which home-bound vessels glad, from far descry;

[103-060] This they ascend, and thence with sorrow ken

[103-061] The mangled heifers lie, and bleeding men;

[103-062] The inexorable ravager they view,

[103-063] With blood discolour’d, still the rest pursue:

[103-064] There, Peleus pray’d submissive towards the sea,

[103-065] And deprecates the ire of injured Psamathe.

[103-066] But deaf to all his prayers the nymph remain’d,

[103-067] Till Thetis for her spouse the boon obtain’d.

[103-068] Pleased with the luxury, the furious beast,

[103-069] Unstopp’d, continues still his bloody feast:

[103-070] While yet upon a sturdy bull he flew,

[103-071] Changed by the nymph, a marble block he grew.

[103-072] No longer dreadful now the wolf appears,

[103-073] Buried in stone, and vanish’d like their fears.

[103-074] Yet still the fates unhappy Peleus vex’d,

[103-075] To the Magnesian shore he wanders next.

[103-076] Acastus there, who ruled the peaceful clime,

[103-077] Grants his request, and expiates his crime.
