# Arethusa

A nymph of Elis and a devoted follower of [[Diana]], Arethusa is pursued by the river-god [[Alpheus]], who burns with desire for her. When flight exhausts her, she prays to Diana for protection, and the goddess transforms her into a fountain. Even in this form, Alpheus pursues her — his river-waters mingle with her spring, flowing beneath the Ionian Sea to rise together on the island of Ortygia at Syracuse (051-001).

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Arethusa appears in Book V in a narrative told to [[Ceres]] (Demeter) as the goddess searches for her lost daughter Proserpina. Rising from her fountain, Arethusa reveals that she has seen Proserpina in the underworld, and she recounts her own story of transformation and subterranean passage to establish her credibility as a witness who has traversed both worlds (048-069).

### Key Episodes

- **The Pursuit**: After bathing in a clear stream in Elis, Arethusa is startled by the voice of the river-god Alpheus calling to her from his waters. She flees, and he pursues her across mountains and plains. Alpheus, relentless, strips away his human form and resumes his shape as a river to move faster (051-036).
- **Diana's Intervention**: Exhausted and with Alpheus gaining, Arethusa cries out to Diana for aid. The goddess wraps her in a thick cloud, obscuring her from the pursuer. While Alpheus circles the cloud in confusion, Diana transforms the nymph into a fountain (051-081).
- **The Subterranean Passage**: Even as a spring, Arethusa is not safe — Alpheus returns to his river form and mingles his waters with hers. Diana opens a channel through the earth, and Arethusa's waters travel beneath the Ionian Sea, emerging pure and separate on the island of Ortygia, at Syracuse in Sicily (051-106).

## Transformation

- **Form**: Nymph → Freshwater spring / fountain
- **Agent**: [[Diana]] (at Arethusa's own pleading)
- **Cause**: Protection — Diana transforms Arethusa to shield her from the relentless pursuit of the river-god [[Alpheus]], then opens an underground channel to carry her waters to Sicily
