[130-001] Latinus, king of [[latium|Latium]], bestows the hand of his daughter on Aeneas, who is opposed by Turnus, the affianced husband of the maiden—Aeneas obtains a supply of auxiliary troops from the Etruscans; while the Rutuli despatch an embassy to Diomed in behalf of Tumus.

[130-002] Thus Macareus. Now with a pious aim

[130-003] Had good Aeneas raised a funeral flame,

[130-004] In honour of his hoary nurse’s name.

[130-005] Her epitaph he fix’d; and setting sail,

[130-006] Cajeta left, and catch’d at every gale.

[130-007] He steer’d at distance from the faithless shore,

[130-008] Where the false goddess reigns with fatal power,

[130-009] And sought those grateful groves, that shade the plain,

[130-010] Where Tiber roll’s majestic to the main,

[130-011] And fattens, as he runs, the fair champaign.

[130-012] His kindred gods the hero’s wishes crown

[130-013] With fair Lavinia, and Latinus’ throne;

[130-014] But not without a war the prize he won.

[130-015] Drawn up in bright array the battle stands:

[130-016] Turnus with arms his promised wife demands.

[130-017] Etrurians, Latians equal fortune share,

[130-018] And doubtful long appears the face of war.

[130-019] Both powers from neighbouring princes seek supplies,

[130-020] And embassies appoint for new allies.

[130-021] Aeneas, for relief, Evander moves;

[130-022] His quarrel he asserts, his case approves.

[130-023] The bold Rutulians, with an equal speed,

[130-024] Sage Venulus despatch to Diomed.

[130-025] The king, late griefs revolving in his mind,

[130-026] These reasons for neutrality assign’d:

[130-027] “Shall I, of one poor dotal town possess’d,

[130-028] My people thin, my wretched country waste;

[130-029] An exiled prince, and on a shaking throne;

[130-030] Or risk my patron’s subjects, or my own?

[130-031] You’ll grieve the harshness of our hap to hear;

[130-032] Nor can I tell the tale without a tear.
