[044-001] The challengers select the rebellion of the giants, and the various transformations of the gods to avoid their rage, as the subject of their song.

[044-002] “Then rises one of the presumptuous throng,

[044-003] Steps rudely forth, and first begins the song;

[044-004] =With vain address describes the giants’ wars,

[044-005] And to the gods their fabled acts prefers.

[044-006] She sings from earth’s dark womb how Typhon rose,

[044-007] And struck with mortal fear his heavenly foes;

[044-008] How the gods fled to Egypt’s slimy soil,

[044-009] And hid their heads beneath the banks of Nile;

[044-010] How Typhon from the conquer’d skies pursued

[044-011] Their routed godheads to the seven-mouth’d flood:

[044-012] Forced every god, his fury to escape,

[044-013] Some beastly form to take, or earthly shape.

[044-014] [[jupiter|Jove]] (so she sung) was changed into a ram,

[044-015] From whence the horns of Lybian Ammon came:

[044-016] [[bacchus|Bacchus]] a goat; [[apollo|Apollo]] was a crow;

[044-017] Phoebe a cat; the wife of Jove a cow,

[044-018] Whose hue was whiter than the falling snow;

[044-019] Mercury to a nasty ibis turn’d,

[044-020] The change obscene, afraid of Typhon mourn’d;

[044-021] While [[venus|Venus]] from a fish protection craves,

[044-022] And once more plunges in her native waves.

[044-023] “She sung, and to her harp her voice applied:

[044-024] Then us again to match her they defied:

[044-025] But our poor song, perhaps, for you to hear,

[044-026] Nor leisure serves, nor is it worth your ear.”

[044-027] “That causeless doubt remove, O muse; rehearse,”

[044-028] The goddess cried, “your ever-grateful verse:”

[044-029] Beneath a checker’d shade she takes her seat,

[044-030] And bids the sister her whole song repeat.

[044-031] The sister thus: “Calliope we chose

[044-032] For the performance.” The sweet virgin rose,

[044-033] With ivy crown’d; she tunes her golden strings,

[044-034] And to her harp this composition sings:
