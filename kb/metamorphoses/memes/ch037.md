[031-001] A beautiful youth, named Hermaphroditus, is beloved by a river nymph, who surprises him while bathing, and entreats the gods to unite them in one body; a request which is granted by the indulgent deities.

[031-002] “How Salmacis, with weak, enfeebling streams,

[031-003] Softens the body, and unnerves the limbs,

[031-004] And what the secret cause, shall here be shown;

[031-005] The cause is secret, but the effect is known.

[031-006] “The Naiads nursed an infant heretofore,

[031-007] That Citherea once to Hermes bore:

[031-008] From both the illustrious authors of his race

[031-009] The child was named, nor was it hard to trace

[031-010] Both the bright parents through the infant’s face.

[031-011] When fifteen years in Ida’s cool retreat

[031-012] The boy had told, he left his native seat,

[031-013] And sought fresh fountains in a foreign soil:

[031-014] The pleasure lessen’d the attending toil.

[031-015] With eager steps the Lycian fields he cross’d,

[031-016] And fields that border on the Lycian coast:

[031-017] A river here he view’d, so lovely bright,

[031-018] It show’d the bottom in a fairer light,

[031-019] Nor kept a sand conceal’d from human sight:

[031-020] The stream produced nor slimy ooze, nor weeds,

[031-021] Nor miry rushes, nor the spiky reeds,

[031-022] But dealt enriching moisture all around,

[031-023] The fruitful banks with cheerful verdure crown’d,

[031-024] And kept the spring eternal on the ground.

[031-025] A nymph presides, not practised in the chase,

[031-026] Nor skilful at the bow, nor at the race;

[031-027] Of all the blue-eyed daughters of the main,

[031-028] The only stranger to Diana’s train.

[031-029] Her sisters often, as ’tis said, would cry,

[031-030] ‘Fy, Salmacis: what! always idle; fy!

[031-031] Or take thy quiver, or thy arrows seize,

[031-032] And mix the toils of hunting with they ease.’

[031-033] Nor quivers she, nor arrows, e’er would seize,

[031-034] Nor mix the toils of hunting with her ease;

[031-035] But oft would bathe her in the crystal tide,

[031-036] Oft with a comb her dewy locks divide;

[031-037] Now in the limpid stream she views her face,

[031-038] And dress’d her image in the floating glass:

[031-039] On beds of leaves she pow reposed her limbs,

[031-040] Now gather’d flowers that grew about her streams,

[031-041] And then by chance was gathering, as she stood

[031-042] To view the boy, and long’d for what she view’d.

[031-043] “Fain would she meet the youth with hasty feet,

[031-044] She fain would meet him, but refused to meet

[031-045] Before her looks were set with nicest care,

[031-046] And well deserved to be reputed fair.

[031-047] “ ‘Bright youth,’ she cries, ‘whom all thy features prove

[031-048] A god, and, if a god, the god of love;

[031-049] But if a mortal, bless’d thy nurse’s breast,

[031-050] Bless’d are thy parents, and thy sisters bless’d:

[031-051] But O! how bless’d, how more than bless’d thy bride!

[031-052] Allied in bliss, if any yet allied.

[031-053] If so, let mine the stolen enjoyments be;

[031-054] If not, behold a willing bride in me.’

[031-055] “The boy knew naught of love, and, touch’d with shame,

[031-056] He strove, and blush’d, but still the blush became;

[031-057] In rising blushes still fresh beauties rose;

[031-058] The sunny side of fruit such blushes shows,

[031-059] And such the moon, when all her silver white

[031-060] Turns in eclipses to a ruddy light.

[031-061] The nymph still begs, if not a nobler bliss,

[031-062] A cold salute at least, a sister’s kiss;

[031-063] And now prepares to take the lovely boy

[031-064] Between her arms. He, innocently coy,

[031-065] Replies, ‘Or leave me to myself alone,

[031-066] You rude uncivil nymph, or I’ll be gone.’

[031-067] ‘Fair stranger, then,’ says she, ‘it shall be so;’

[031-068] And, for she fear’d his threats, she feign’d to go;

[031-069] But, hid within a covert’s neighbouring green,

[031-070] She kept him still in sight, herself unseen.

[031-071] The boy now fancies all the danger o’er,

[031-072] And innocently sports about the shore;

[031-073] Playful and wanton to the stream he trips,

[031-074] And dips his foot, and shivers as he dips.

[031-075] The coolness pleased him, and, with eager haste,

[031-076] His airy garments on the banks he cast;

[031-077] His godlike features, and his heavenly hue,

[031-078] And all his beauties, were exposed to view.

[031-079] “Now all undress’d upon the banks he stood,

[031-080] And clapp’d his sides, and leap’d into the flood:

[031-081] His lovely limbs the silver waves divide;

[031-082] His limbs appear more lovely through the tide;

[031-083] As lilies, shut within a crystal case,

[031-084] Receive a glossy lustre from the glass.

[031-085] ‘He’s mine, he’s all my own,’ the Naiad cries,

[031-086] And flings of all, and after him she flies.

[031-087] And now she fastens on him as he swims,

[031-088] And holds him close, and wraps about his limbs.

[031-089] ‘And why, coy youth,’ she cries, ‘why thus unkind?

[031-090] O may the gods thus keep us over join’d!

[031-091] O may we never, never part again!’

[031-092] So pray’d the nymph, nor did she pray in vain:

[031-093] For now she finds him, as his limbs she press’d.

[031-094] Grow nearer still, and nearer to her breast,

[031-095] Till, piercing each the other’s flesh, they run

[031-096] Together, and incorporate in one:

[031-097] Last, in one face are both their faces join’d,

[031-098] As when the stock and grafted twig combined

[031-099] Shoot up the same, and wear a common ring.”
