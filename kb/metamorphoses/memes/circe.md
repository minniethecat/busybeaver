# Circe

Circe, daughter of the Sun (Sol) and the ocean-nymph Perse, is the supreme enchantress of classical mythology, dwelling on the island of Aeaea. Her mastery of magical herbs, potions, and incantations gives her the power to transform humans into animals — a power she wields with capricious cruelty in the *Metamorphoses*, most notably in the stories of Scylla, Picus, and the crew of Ulysses. Unlike the divine transformations elsewhere in the poem, Circe's metamorphoses are wrought through art and knowledge rather than inherent divine power, making her a uniquely human (or semi-divine) agent of change.

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Circe appears prominently in Book XIV of the *Metamorphoses* as a figure of transformative power exercised through craft rather than divine fiat. When the sea-god Glaucus seeks her help to win the love of the nymph Scylla, Circe herself falls in love with Glaucus. Rejected, she directs her jealousy not at Glaucus but at her rival Scylla: she poisons the pool where Scylla bathes, transforming the nymph's lower body into a ring of snarling dogs — the origin of the monstrous Scylla of the strait (122-001). Later, when the Trojan hero Picus, son of Saturn and king of Latium, rejects her advances in favor of his wife Canens, Circe transforms him into a woodpecker and his companions into various wild beasts. Even Ulysses' crew falls victim to her transformative magic — though Ovid treats this episode with notable brevity, alluding to it rather than narrating it fully. Circe thus represents a specifically feminine and artisanal mode of metamorphosis: change wrought through knowledge, jealousy, and the power of the rejected.

### Key Episodes

- **The Transformation of Scylla**: Jealous of Glaucus's love for Scylla, Circe poisons the nymph's bathing pool, transforming her into the monstrous sea-creature with dogs about her waist (122-001)
- **The Transformation of Picus**: When Picus, the handsome king of Latium, rejects Circe for his wife Canens, she transforms him into a woodpecker, forever pecking at trees
- **The Companions of Ulysses**: Circe transforms Ulysses' crew into beasts; Ulysses, protected by the herb moly given by Mercury, compels her to restore them
