[001-001] The formation of the world from the confusion of [[chaos|Chaos]] by the wisdom and power of the Deity is here described, together with a delineation of the harmonious system of the universe, and the mutual dependences and operations of powers of nature—Birds, beasts, and fishes, brought into existence—The creation of man: his superiority to other animals evinced in the structure of his body and the faculties of his mind.

[001-002] Of bodies changed to various forms I sing:

[001-003] Ye gods, from whom these miracles did spring,

[001-004] Inspire my numbers with celestial heat,

[001-005] Till I my long laborious work complete;

[001-006] And add perpetual tenor to my rhymes,

[001-007] Deduced from Nature’s birth to Caesar’s times.

[001-008] Before the seas, and this terrestrial ball,

[001-009] And heaven’s high canopy that covers all,

[001-010] One was the face of Nature; if a face:

[001-011] Rather a rude and indigested mass:

[001-012] A lifeless lump, unfashion’d and unframed,

[001-013] Of jarring seeds, and justly Chaos named.

[001-014] No sun was lighted up the world to view,

[001-015] No moon did yet her blunted horns renew,

[001-016] Nor yet was earth suspended in the sky,

[001-017] Nor poised, did on her own foundations lie,

[001-018] Nor seas about the shores their arms had thrown;

[001-019] But earth, and air, and water were in one.

[001-020] Thus air was void of light, and earth unstable,

[001-021] And water’s dark abyss unnavigable.

[001-022] No certain form on any was impress’d;

[001-023] All were confused, and each disturb’d the rest.

[001-024] For hot and cold were in one body fix’d,

[001-025] And soft with hard, and light with heavy, mix’d.

[001-026] But God, or Nature, while they thus contend,

[001-027] To these intestine discords put an end.

[001-028] Then earth from air, and seas from earth, were driven,

[001-029] And grosser air sunk from ethereal heaven.

[001-030] Thus disembroil’d, they take their proper place;

[001-031] The next of kin contiguously embrace;

[001-032] And foes are sunder’d by a larger space.

[001-033] The force of fire ascended first on high,

[001-034] And took its dwelling in the vaulted sky:

[001-035] Then air succeeds, in lightness next to fire,

[001-036] Whose atoms from unactive earth retire;

[001-037] Earth sinks beneath, and draws a numerous throng

[001-038] Of ponderous, thick, unwieldy, seeds along.

[001-039] [[About]] her coasts unruly waters roar,

[001-040] And, rising on a ridge, insult the shore.

[001-041] Thus when the god, whatever god was he,

[001-042] Had form’d the whole, and made the parts agree,

[001-043] That no unequal portions might be found,

[001-044] He moulded earth into a spacious round:

[001-045] Then, with a breath, he gave the winds to blow,

[001-046] And bade the congregated waters flow.

[001-047] He adds the running springs, and standing lakes;

[001-048] And bounding banks for winding rivers makes.

[001-049] Some part, in earth are swallow’d up, the most

[001-050] In ample oceans disembogued, are lost.

[001-051] He shades the woods, the valleys he restrains

[001-052] With rocky mountains, and extends the plains.

[001-053] And as five zones the ethereal regions bind,

[001-054] Five, correspondent, are to earth assign’d:

[001-055] The sun, with rays directly darting down,

[001-056] Fires all beneath, and fries the middle zone;

[001-057] The two beneath the distant poles complain

[001-058] Of endless winter, and perpetual rain.

[001-059] Betwixt the extremes, two happier climates hold

[001-060] The temper that partakes of hot and cold.

[001-061] The fields of liquid air, enclosing all,

[001-062] Surround the compass of this earthly ball:

[001-063] The lighter parts lie next the fires above,

[001-064] The grosser near the watery surface move:

[001-065] Thick clouds are spread, and storms engender there,

[001-066] And thunder’s voice, which wretched mortals fear,

[001-067] And winds, that on their wings cold winter bear.

[001-068] Nor were those blust’ring brethren left at large,

[001-069] On seas and shores their fury to discharge:

[001-070] Bound as they are, and circumscribed in place,

[001-071] They rend the world, resistless, where they pass,

[001-072] And mighty marks of mischief leave behind;

[001-073] Such is the rage of their tempestuous kind.

[001-074] First Eurus to the rising morn is sent

[001-075] (The regions of the balmy continent),

[001-076] And eastern realms, where, early, Persians run

[001-077] To greet the bless’d appearance of the sun.

[001-078] Westward, the wanton zephyr wings his flight,

[001-079] Pleased with the remnants of departing light.

[001-080] Fierce Boreas, with his offspring issues forth

[001-081] To invade the frozen wagon of the north;

[001-082] While frowning Auster seeks the southern sphere,

[001-083] And rots, with endless rain, the unwholesome year.

[001-084] High o’er the clouds, and empty realms of wind,

[001-085] The god a clearer space for heaven design’d;

[001-086] Where fields of light, and liquid ether flow,

[001-087] Purged from the ponderous dregs of earth below.

[001-088] Scarce had the power distinguish’d these, when straight

[001-089] The stars, no longer overlaid with weight,

[001-090] Exert their heads from underneath the mass,

[001-091] And upward shoot, and kindle as they pass,

[001-092] And with diffusive light adorn their heavenly place.

[001-093] Then, every void of nature to supply,

[001-094] With forms of gods he fills the vacant sky;

[001-095] New herds of beasts he sends the plains to share;

[001-096] New colonies of birds to people air;

[001-097] And to their oozy beds the finny fish repair.

[001-098] A creature of a more exalted kind

[001-099] Was wanting yet, and then was man design’d;

[001-100] Conscious of thought, of more capacious breast,

[001-101] For empire form’d, and fit to rule the rest:

[001-102] Whether with particles of heavenly fire

[001-103] The God of nature did his soul inspire,

[001-104] Or earth, but new divided from the sky,

[001-105] And pliant, still retained the ethereal energy,

[001-106] Which wise Prometheus temper’d into paste,

[001-107] And, mix’d with living streams, the godlike image cast.

[001-108] Thus, while the mute creation downward bend

[001-109] Their sight, and to their earthly mother tend,

[001-110] Man looks aloft, and with erected eyes

[001-111] Beholds his own hereditary skies.

[001-112] From such rude principles our form began,

[001-113] And earth was metamorphosed into man.
