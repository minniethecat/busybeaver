[119-001] Aeneas, with his father Anchises, is hospitably entertained at Delos, by Anius the priest of [[apollo|Apollo]]—After visiting the island of Phaeacia, the hero at length arrives at the dangerous rocks of Scylla.

[119-002] [[troy|Troy]] thus destroy’d, ’twas still denied by fate,

[119-003] The hopes of Troy should perish with the state.

[119-004] His sire, the son of [[venus|Cytherea]] bore,

[119-005] And household gods from burning Ilium’s shore.

[119-006] The pious prince (a double duty paid)

[119-007] Each sacred burden through the flames convey’d.

[119-008] With young Ascanius, and this only prize,

[119-009] Of heaps of wealth, he from Antandros flies;

[119-010] But struck with horror, left the Thracian shore,

[119-011] Stain’d with the blood of murder’d Polydore.

[119-012] The Delian isle receives the banish’d train,

[119-013] Driven by kind gales, and favour’d by the main.

[119-014] Here pious Anius, priest and monarch, reign’d,

[119-015] And either charge with equal care sustain’d;

[119-016] His subjects ruled, to Phoebus homage paid,

[119-017] His god obeying, and by those obey’d.

[119-018] The priest displays his hospitable gate,

[119-019] And shows the riches of his church and state;

[119-020] The sacred shrubs, which eased Latona’s pain,

[119-021] The palm, and olive, and the votive fane.

[119-022] Here grateful flames with fuming incense fed,

[119-023] And mingled wine ambrosial odours shed;

[119-024] Of slaughter’d steers the crackling entrails burn’d;

[119-025] And then the strangers to the court return’d.

[119-026] On beds of tap’stry placed aloft, they dine

[119-027] With Ceres’ gift, and flowing bowls of wine;

[119-028] When thus Anchises spoke, amid the feast:

[119-029] “Say, mitred monarch, Phoebus’ chosen priest,

[119-030] Or (ere from Troy by cruel fate expell’d)

[119-031] When first mine eyes these sacred walls beheld,

[119-032] A son, and twice two daughters crown’d thy bliss?

[119-033] Or errs my memory, and I judge amiss?”

[119-034] The royal prophet shook his hoary head,

[119-035] With snowy fillets bound, and sighing, said:

[119-036] “Thy memory errs not, prince; thou saw’st me then

[119-037] The happy father of so large a train:

[119-038] Behold me now, (such turns of chance befall

[119-039] The race of man!) almost bereft of all.

[119-040] For ah! what comfort can my son bestow,

[119-041] What help afford, to mitigate my wo!

[119-042] While far from hence, in Andros’ isle he reigns,

[119-043] (From him so named,) and there my place sustains.

[119-044] Him Delius prescience gave; the twice-born god

[119-045] A boon more wondrous on the maids bestow’d.

[119-046] Whate’er they touch’d, he gave them to transmute,

[119-047] (A gift past credit, and above their suit,)

[119-048] To Ceres, [[bacchus|Bacchus]], and [[minerva|Minerva]]’s fruit.

[119-049] How great their value, and how rich their use,

[119-050] Whose only touch such treasures could produce!

[119-051] “The dire destroyer of the Trojan reign,

[119-052] Fierce Agamemnon, such a prize to gain,

[119-053] (A proof we also were design’d by fate

[119-054] To feel the tempest that o’erturn’d your state,)

[119-055] With force superior, and a ruffian crew,

[119-056] From these weak arms the helpless virgins drew;

[119-057] And sternly bade them use the grant divine,

[119-058] To keep the fleet in corn, in oil, and wine.

[119-059] Each, as they could, escaped: two strove to gain

[119-060] Euboea’s isle, and two their brother’s reign.

[119-061] The soldier follows, and demands the dames;

[119-062] If held by force, immediate war proclaims.

[119-063] Fear conquer’d nature in their brother’s mind,

[119-064] And gave them up to punishment assign’d.

[119-065] Forgive the deed; nor Hector’s arm was there,

[119-066] Nor thine, Aeneas, to maintain the war;

[119-067] Whose only force upheld your Ilium’s towers,

[119-068] For ten long years against the Grecian powers.

[119-069] Prepared to bind their captive arms in bands,

[119-070] To heaven they rear’d their yet unfetter’d hands,

[119-071] ‘Help, Bacchus, author of the gift,’ they pray’d;

[119-072] The gift’s great author gave immediate aid;

[119-073] If such destruction of the human frame,

[119-074] By ways so wondrous, may deserve the name;

[119-075] Nor could I hear, nor can I now relate

[119-076] Exact the manner of their alter’d state;

[119-077] But this in general of my loss I knew,

[119-078] Transform’d to doves, on milky plumes they flew,

[119-079] Such as on Ida’s mount thy consort’s chariot drew.”

[119-080] With such discourse they entertain’d the feast;

[119-081] Then rose from table, and withdrew to rest.

[119-082] The following morn, ere Sol was seen to shine,

[119-083] The inquiring Trojans sought the sacred shrine;

[119-084] The mystic power commands them to explore

[119-085] Their ancient mother, and a kindred shore.

[119-086] Attending to the sea, the generous prince

[119-087] Dismiss’d his guests with rich munificence,

[119-088] In old Anchises’ hand a sceptre placed,

[119-089] A vest and quiver young Ascanius graced,

[119-090] His sire a cup; which from the Aonian coast,

[119-091] Ismenian Therses sent his royal host.

[119-092] Alcon of Myle made what Therses sent,

[119-093] And carved thereon this ample argument.

[119-094] A town with seven distinguish’d gates was shown,

[119-095] Which spoke its name, and made the city known;

[119-096] Before it, piles and tombs, and rising flames,

[119-097] The rites of death, and choirs of mourning dames

[119-098] Who bared their breasts, and gave their hair to flow,

[119-099] The signs of grief, and marks of public wo.

[119-100] Their fountains dried, the weeping Naiads mourn’d,

[119-101] The trees stood bare, with searing cankers burn’d,

[119-102] No herbage clothed the ground, a ragged flock

[119-103] Of goats half famish’d lick’d the naked rock

[119-104] Of manly courage, and with mind serene,

[119-105] Orion’s daughters in the town were seen;

[119-106] One heav’d her chest to meet the lifted knife,

[119-107] One plunged the poniard through the seat of life,

[119-108] Their country’s victims; mourns the rescued state,

[119-109] The bodies burns, and celebrates their fate.

[119-110] To save the failure of the illustrious line,

[119-111] From the pale ashes rose, of form divine,

[119-112] Two generous youths; these, fame Coronae calls,

[119-113] Who join the pomp, and mourn their mother’s falls.

[119-114] These burnish’d figures form’d of antique mould,

[119-115] Shone on the brass, with rising sculpture bold;

[119-116] A wreath of gilt acanthus round the brim was roll’d.

[119-117] Nor less expense the Trojan gifts express’d;

[119-118] A fuming censor for the royal priest,

[119-119] A chalice, and a crown of princely cost,

[119-120] With ruddy gold, and sparkling gems emboss’d.

[119-121] Now hoisting sail, to [[crete|Crete]] the Trojans stood,

[119-122] Themselves remembering sprung from Teucer’s blood;

[119-123] But heaven forbids, and pestilential [[jupiter|Jove]],

[119-124] From noxious skies the wandering navy drove.

[119-125] Her hundred cities left, from Crete they bore,

[119-126] And sought the destined land, Ausonia’s shore;

[119-127] But toss’d by storms at either Strophas lay,

[119-128] Till scared by harpies from the faithless bay.

[119-129] Then passing onward with a prosperous wind,

[119-130] Left sly Uylsses’ spacious realms behind;

[119-131] Ambracia’s state, in former ages known

[119-132] The strife of gods, the judge transform’d to stone

[119-133] They saw; for Actian Phoebus since renown’d,

[119-134] Who Caesar’s arms with naval conquest crown’d;

[119-135] Next pass’d Dodona, wont of old to boast

[119-136] Her vocal forest; and Chaonia’s coast,

[119-137] Where King Molossus’ sons on wings aspired,

[119-138] And saw secure the harmless fuel fired.

[119-139] Now to Phaeacia’s happy isle they came,

[119-140] For fertile orchards known to early fame;

[119-141] Epirus pass’d, they next beheld with joy

[119-142] A second Ilium, and fictitious Troy;

[119-143] Here Trojan Helenus the sceptre sway’d,

[119-144] Who show’d their fate, and mystic truths display’d;

[119-145] By him confirm’d, Sicilia’s isle they reach’d,

[119-146] Whose sides to sea, three promontories stretch’d;

[119-147] Pachynos to the stormy south is placed,

[119-148] On Lilybaeum blows the gentle west,

[119-149] Peloro’s cliffs the northern Bear survey,

[119-150] Who rolls above, and dreads to touch the sea;

[119-151] By this they steer, and favour’d by the tide,

[119-152] Secure by night in Zancle’s harbour ride.

[119-153] Here cruel Sylla gains the rocky shore,

[119-154] And there the waves of loud Charybdis roar;

[119-155] This sucks, and vomits ships, and bodies drown’d,

[119-156] And ravenous dogs the womb of that surround;

[119-157] In face a virgin, and (if aught be true

[119-158] By bards recorded) once a virgin too.

[119-159] A train of youths in vain desired her bed,

[119-160] By sea nymphs loved, to nymphs of seas she fled;

[119-161] The maid to these, with female pride, display’d

[119-162] Their baffled courtship, and their love betray’d.

[119-163] When Galatea thus bespoke the fair,

[119-164] (But first she sigh’d,) while Scylla comb’d her hair,

[119-165] “You, lovely maid, a generous race pursues,

[119-166] Whom safe you may (as now you do) refuse;

[119-167] To me, though powerful in a numerous train

[119-168] Of sisters, sprung from gods, who rule the main,

[119-169] My native seas could scarce a refuge prove,

[119-170] To shun the fury of the cyclop’s love.”

[119-171] Tears choked her utterance here; the pitying maid

[119-172] With marble fingers wiped them off, and said;

[119-173] “My dearest goddess, let thy Scylla know

[119-174] (For I am faithful) whence these sorrows flow.”

[119-175] The maid’s entreaties o’er the nymph prevail,

[119-176] Who thus to Scylla tells the mournful tale.
