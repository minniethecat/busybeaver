# Transformation of Io

Io, daughter of the river-god Inachus, is loved by Jupiter, who transforms her into a beautiful heifer to conceal her from Juno. Juno, suspecting, demands the heifer as a gift and sets the hundred-eyed Argus to guard her. Jupiter sends Mercury, who lulls Argus to sleep with his pipes and beheads him. Juno places Argus's eyes on the tail of the peacock.

## Setting

The river valleys of Arcadia, where Io's father Inachus mourns, and the fields where the transformed Io grazes, recognized by her father through the hoof-prints she traces in the dust.

## Narrative

Jupiter, pursuing Io, wraps the earth in a sudden darkness to conceal his approach. Juno, suspecting, descends from Olympus; Jupiter hastily transforms Io into a white heifer. Juno, pretending not to recognize the transformation, asks for the heifer as a gift. Unable to refuse without confirming guilt, Jupiter yields. Juno sets Argus to guard the heifer. Jupiter sends Mercury, who tells Argus the story of Pan and Syrinx — lulling the hundred-eyed guard to sleep before beheading him. Juno places the eyes on her sacred peacock's tail.

## Themes

- **Body as disguise**: Jupiter transforms Io to hide her; the metamorphosed body is a visible sign of invisible desire
- **The witness killed**: Argus's hundred eyes — the ultimate surveillance — are silenced by story and sleep
- **Recognition through writing**: The transformed Io traces her name in the dust with her hoof, the only way a heifer can identify herself
