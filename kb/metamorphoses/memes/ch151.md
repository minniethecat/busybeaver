[135-001] Vertumnus prosecutes his suit to the nymph Pomona in the disguise of an old woman.

[135-002] A hamadryad flourish’d in these days,

[135-003] Her name Pomona, from her woodland race.

[135-004] In garden culture none could so excel,

[135-005] Or form the pliant souls of plants so well;

[135-006] Or to the fruit more generous flavours lend,

[135-007] Or teach the trees with nobler loads to bend.

[135-008] The nymph frequented not the flattering stream,

[135-009] Nor meads, the subject of a virgin’s dream;

[135-010] But to such joys her nursery did prefer,

[135-011] Alone to attend her vegetable care.

[135-012] A pruning hook she carried in her hand,

[135-013] And taught the stragglers to obey command;

[135-014] Lest the licentious, and unthrifty bough,

[135-015] The too-indulgent parent should undo.

[135-016] She shows, how stocks invite to their embrace

[135-017] A graft, and naturalize a foreign race

[135-018] To mend the savage teint; and in its stead

[135-019] Adopt new nature, and a nobler breed.

[135-020] Now hourly she observes her growing care,

[135-021] And guards the nonage from the bleaker air:

[135-022] Then opes her streaming sluices, to supply

[135-023] With flowing draughts her thirsty family.

[135-024] Long had she labour’d to continue free

[135-025] From chains of love, and nuptial tyranny;

[135-026] And in her orchard’s small extent immured,

[135-027] Her vow’d virginity she still secured,

[135-028] Oft would loose Pan, and all the brutal train

[135-029] Of satyrs, tempt her innocence in vain.

[135-030] Vertumnus too pursued the maid no less;

[135-031] But with his rivals shared a like success.

[135-032] To gain access a thousand ways he tries:

[135-033] Oft, in the hind, the lover would disguise.

[135-034] The heedless lout comes shambling on, and seems

[135-035] Just sweating from the labour of his teams.

[135-036] Then, from the harvest of the mimic swain,

[135-037] Seems bending with a load of bearded grain.

[135-038] Sometimes a dresser of the vine he feigns,

[135-039] And lawless tendrils to their bounds restrains.

[135-040] Sometimes his sword a soldier shows; his rod

[135-041] An angler; still so various is the god.

[135-042] Now, in a forehead cloth, some crone he seems,

[135-043] A staff supplying the defect of limbs;

[135-044] Admittance thus he gains; admires the store

[135-045] Of fairest fruit; the fair possessor more;

[135-046] Then greets her with a kiss: the unpractised dame

[135-047] Admired a grandame kiss’d with such a flame.

[135-048] Now, seated by her, he beholds a vine

[135-049] Around an elm in amorous foldings twine.

[135-050] “If that fair elm,” he cried, “alone should stand,

[135-051] No grapes would glow with gold and tempt the hand;

[135-052] Or if that vine without her elm should grow,

[135-053] ’Twould creep a poor neglected shrub below.

[135-054] “Be then, fair nymph, by these examples led;

[135-055] Nor shun, for fancied fears, the nuptial bed.

[135-056] Not she for whom the Lapithites took arms,

[135-057] Nor Sparta’s queen, could boast such heavenly charms.

[135-058] And if you would on woman’s faith rely,

[135-059] None can your choice direct so well as I.

[135-060] Though old, so much Pomona I adore,

[135-061] Scarce does the bright Vertumnus love her more.

[135-062] ’Tis your fair self alone his breast inspires

[135-063] With softest wishes, and unsoil’d desires.

[135-064] Then fly all vulgar followers, and prove

[135-065] The god of seasons only worth your love:

[135-066] On my assurance well you may repose;

[135-067] Vertumnus scarce Vertumnus better knows.

[135-068] True to his choice, all looser flames he flies;

[135-069] Nor for new faces fashionably dies.

[135-070] The charms of youth, and every smiling grace

[135-071] Bloom in his features, and the god confess.

[135-072] Besides, he puts on every shape at ease;

[135-073] But those the most that best Pomona please.

[135-074] Still to oblige her is her lover’s aim;

[135-075] Their likings and aversions are the same.

[135-076] Nor the fair fruit your burden’d branches bear,

[135-077] Nor all the youthful product of the year,

[135-078] Could bribe his choice; yourself alone can prove

[135-079] A fit reward for so refined a love.

[135-080] Relent, fair nymph, and with a kind, regret,

[135-081] Think ’tis Vertumnus weeping at your feet.

[135-082] A tale attend, through [[cyprus|Cyprus]] known, to prove

[135-083] How [[venus|Venus]] once revenged neglected love.
