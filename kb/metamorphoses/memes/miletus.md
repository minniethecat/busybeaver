# Miletus

Miletus, the eponymous founder of the city that bears his name, is a figure whose story bridges Crete and Asia Minor. In the *Metamorphoses*, he appears as the son of Apollo, fleeing from King Minos of Crete to found a new city on the coast of Caria — one of the many foundation myths that connect the poem's mythological world to the historical geography of the Mediterranean.

## Key Episodes

- **Flight from Crete**: Miletus, pursued by the enmity of King Minos, flees his Cretan homeland across the Aegean Sea — a journey that transforms a refugee into a city-founder
- **Foundation of Miletus**: On the coast of Asia Minor, in the region of Caria, Miletus establishes the city that will become one of the most important centers of Greek philosophy and science — a foundation myth that connects the heroic age to the historical world
