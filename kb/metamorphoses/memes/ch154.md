[138-001] The god Mars translates Romulus to the skies, where hew inrolled in the number of the gods under the name of Qurinus.

[138-002] Now warrior Mars his burnish’d helm puts on,

[138-003] And thus addresses heaven’s imperial throne:

[138-004] “Since the inferior world is now become

[138-005] One vassal globe, and colony to Rome,

[138-006] This grace, oh [[jupiter|Jove]], for Romulus I claim,

[138-007] Admit him to the skies, from whence he came.

[138-008] Long hast thou promised an ethereal state

[138-009] To Mars’s lineage; and thy word is fate.”

[138-010] The sire, that rules the thunder with a nod,

[138-011] Declared the fiat, and dismiss’d the god.

[138-012] Soon as the power armipotent survey’d

[138-013] The flashing skies, the signal he obey’d;

[138-014] And leaning on his lance, he mounts his car,

[138-015] His fiery coursers lashing through the air.

[138-016] Mount Palatine he gains, and finds his son

[138-017] Good laws enacting on a peaceful throne;

[138-018] The scales of heavenly justice holding high,

[138-019] With steady hand, and a discerning eye.

[138-020] Then vaults upon his car, and to the spheres,

[138-021] Swift, as a flying shaft, Rome’s founder bears.

[138-022] The parts more pure, in rising are refined,

[138-023] The gross and perishable lag behind.

[138-024] His shrine in purple vestments stands in view;

[138-025] He looks a god, and is Quirinus now.
