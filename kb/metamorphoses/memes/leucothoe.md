# Leucothoe

Daughter of Orchamus, king of Persia, whose secret love affair with [[Apollo]] ends in tragedy when the jealous [[Clytie]] betrays her to her father. Buried alive as punishment, she is transformed by Apollo into a frankincense tree.

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Leucothoe appears in Book IV in the cycle of solar love stories. After [[Apollo]]'s affair with [[Clytie]] cools, the sun-god becomes infatuated with Leucothoe. He disguises himself as her mother Eurynome to gain entry to her chamber, reveals his true identity, and wins her affection. But Clytie, consumed by jealousy, spreads word of the affair to Leucothoe's father Orchamus, who orders his daughter buried alive. Apollo attempts to revive her but cannot; instead he transforms her body into a frankincense tree, whose fragrant tears memorialise her forever. (029-001)

### Key Episodes

- **Apollo's Approach**: Disguised as Leucothoe's mother Eurynome, Apollo enters her chamber alone. Once inside, he reveals his divine radiance and declares his love. Leucothoe, though startled, does not resist the god. (029-030)
- **Clytie's Jealousy**: Clytie, who had been Apollo's lover before Leucothoe, is driven mad with envy. She spreads the secret to Orchamus, ensuring that "Leucothoe is grown thy only care" reaches the father's ears. (029-043, 029-049)
- **Burial and Transformation**: Orchamus, enraged, has Leucothoe buried alive in a deep grave. Apollo, arriving too late, pours nectar over her body, and she rises from the earth not as a woman but as a frankincense shrub — her fragrant resin an eternal tribute to her brief beauty. (029-001)

## Transformation

- **Form**: Mortal woman → Frankincense tree (frankincense shrub)
- **Agent**: [[Apollo]] (pours nectar over her grave to effect the transformation)
- **Cause**: Buried alive by her father Orchamus for the forbidden affair; Apollo's intervention transforms her rather than reviving her
- **Source**: (029-001)

> **Note**: A different [[Ino]], daughter of [[Cadmus]], is also called Leucothoe after her transformation into a sea-goddess. (033-201) These are distinct figures sharing the same post-transformation name.
