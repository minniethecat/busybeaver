# Latona

Latona (Greek: Leto), a Titaness beloved by Jupiter, is the mother of the twin deities Apollo and Diana. Though she appears only indirectly in Ovid's *Metamorphoses*, her maternal power — exercised through her divine children — drives two of the poem's most memorable episodes of divine vengeance: the destruction of Niobe's children and the transformation of the Lycian peasants into frogs.

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Latona's presence in the *Metamorphoses* is felt through the power of her children rather than her own direct action. When Niobe, queen of Thebes and mother of fourteen children, boasts of her superior fertility and forbids the Theban women from worshipping Latona, the offended goddess entreats Apollo and Diana to avenge the insult. Apollo slays all seven of Niobe's sons with his arrows, and Diana destroys all seven daughters, leaving Niobe frozen into a weeping stone (055-001). Later, when Latona flees from Juno's wrath after giving birth to the twins and arrives in Lycia, a group of peasants refuse her water and mock her thirst — she prays to Jupiter, who transforms them into frogs, condemned to live forever in the muddy water they denied her (057-001).

### Key Episodes

- **The Punishment of Niobe**: Insulted by Niobe's boast that she is more worthy of worship than Latona, the goddess summons Apollo and Diana to destroy all fourteen of Niobe's children, after which Niobe is transformed into a weeping rock (055-001)
- **The Lycian Peasants Transformed**: While concealing herself from Juno's persecution and wandering with her infant twins, Latona begs water from Lycian peasants — their refusal and mockery provoke her to pray to Jupiter, who transforms them into frogs (057-001)

## Transformation

Latona herself undergoes no metamorphosis, but she serves as a powerful agent of transformation through her children:
- **Niobe**: Woman → Weeping stone (055-001)
- **Lycian Peasants**: Men → Frogs (057-001)

- **Agent**: N/A — she invokes Apollo, Diana, and Jupiter to execute the transformations
- **Cause**: Divine vengeance for impiety and inhospitality
