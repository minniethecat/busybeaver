# Creation of the World

The Creation of the World is the founding event of the *Metamorphoses*. From the "rude and undigested mass" of Chaos (001-008), a divine creator — "God, or kindly Nature" — separates the elements, assigns each its proper sphere, and fashions the ordered cosmos. This first metamorphosis establishes the poem's governing principle: all change is rearrangement, not annihilation.

## Characters

| Character | Role |
|-----------|------|
| The Creator | Divine agent who separates the elements and orders the cosmos |
| Chaos | The primordial material — not a character but the condition to be transformed |

## Setting

The primordial void — "Before the seas, and this terrestrial ball, / And heaven's high canopy that covers all" (001-008). Before creation, all elements exist mixed in confusion: earth, air, fire, and water undifferentiated.

## Narrative

The creator, whether a god or nature itself, resolves the discord of Chaos by separating the elements: fire rises to the highest sphere, air settles below it, earth sinks to the center by its own weight, and water surrounds the landmass. The heavens are studded with stars, the earth populated with living creatures in ascending order — sea creatures, land animals, birds, and finally man, "a being of a more exalted kind" (001-001), made in the image of the gods.

## The Transformation

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Original Form** | Chaos — formless, undifferentiated mixture of all elements |
| **Transformed Form** | Cosmos — ordered universe with earth, sea, sky, and living beings |
| **Agent** | "God, or kindly Nature" — deliberately ambiguous |
| **Cause** | The resolution of elemental discord |
| **Source** | (001-001, 001-008) |

## Themes

- **Order from disorder**: The poem's fundamental pattern — transformation as the imposition of form
- **Divine ambiguity**: "God, or kindly Nature" — Ovid refuses to specify whether creation is divine or natural
- **Hierarchy of being**: The ascending chain from inert matter to man establishes a cosmos that later transformations will disrupt
