[053-001] The Muses are unanimously pronounced victorious, and the daughters of Pierus are punished for their presumption by their transformation into magpies.

[053-002] The chosen muse here ends her sacred lays:

[053-003] The nymphs, unanimous, decree the bays,

[053-004] And give the Heliconian goddesses the praise.

[053-005] Then, far from vain that we should thus prevail.

[053-006] But much provoked to hear the vanquish’d rail.

[053-007] Calliope resumes: “Too long we’ve borne

[053-008] Your daring taunts, and your affronting scorn:

[053-009] Your challenge justly merited a curse,

[053-010] And this unmanner’d railing makes it worse:

[053-011] Since you refuse us calmly to enjoy

[053-012] Our patience, next our passions we’ll employ,

[053-013] The dictates of a mind enraged pursue,

[053-014] And what our just resentment bids us, do.”

[053-015] The railers laugh, our threats and wrath despise,

[053-016] And clap their hands, and make a scolding noise:

[053-017] But in the fact they’re seized: beneath their nails

[053-018] Feathers they feel, and on their faces scales:

[053-019] Their horny beaks at once each other scare;

[053-020] Their arms are plumed, and on their backs they bear

[053-021] Pied wings, and flutter in the fleeting air:

[053-022] Chatt’ring, the scandal of the woods they fly,

[053-023] And there continue still their clam’rous cry;

[053-024] The same their eloquence as maids or birds,

[053-025] Now only noise, and nothing then but words.
