# Lycaon

Lycaon, tyrant of Arcadia, is the first mortal to undergo punitive transformation in Ovid's *Metamorphoses* — and his punishment sets the moral and cosmic stakes for the entire poem. When Jupiter, investigating reports of human wickedness, visits Lycaon's palace in mortal disguise, the tyrant tests the god's divinity by serving him human flesh. Jupiter's response — transforming Lycaon into a wolf and destroying the human race in the great flood — establishes the pattern of divine punishment through metamorphosis that structures the poem (006-001).

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Lycaon appears in Book I as the paradigmatic example of human impiety. When Jupiter comes down from Olympus to investigate the wickedness of humanity — whose reputation has reached the heavens — he visits Lycaon's court in human form. The tyrant doubts the god's divinity and devises a test: he plans to murder Jupiter in his sleep, and first slaughters a Molossian hostage, serving the cooked flesh at dinner (006-044). Jupiter, recognizing the sacrilege, destroys Lycaon's palace with a thunderbolt. Lycaon flees, but his human form dissolves: he runs on all fours, his mouth fills with savage fury, and he attacks the flocks he once ruled. His clothing becomes fur, his arms become legs — he is a wolf, but retains something of his former self: the same grey hair, the same violent eyes, the same ferocious expression (006-023, 006-066).

### Key Episodes

- **Jupiter's Visit**: Jupiter, in mortal form, visits Lycaon's palace in Arcadia to investigate human wickedness (006-001)
- **The Test of Divinity**: Lycaon attempts to test whether Jupiter is truly a god by serving him cooked human flesh and planning to murder him (006-044)
- **The Transformation**: Jupiter destroys the palace and transforms Lycaon into a wolf — the first punitive metamorphosis in the poem (006-023)
- **The Council of the Gods**: Jupiter recounts Lycaon's crime at the council of the gods to justify the destruction of humanity by flood (006-066)

## Transformation

- **Form**: Mortal tyrant → Wolf
- **Agent**: Jupiter
- **Cause**: Impiety — testing the gods and serving human flesh at the divine table
