[042-001] The Muses find shelter from the fury of the elements in the house of Pyreneus, King of [[thrace|Thrace]], who presumes to offer violence to his guests—The goddesses take to their wings, and are pursued by their perfidious host, who is killed by a fall from a lofty precipice.

[042-002] Then one replies: “O goddess, fit to guide

[042-003] Our humble works, and in our choir preside,

[042-004] Who sure would wisely to these fields repair,

[042-005] To taste our pleasures, and our labours share,

[042-006] Were not your virtue and superior mind,

[042-007] To higher arts and nobler deeds inclined;

[042-008] Justly you praise our works, and pleasing seat,

[042-009] Which all might envy in this soft retreat,

[042-010] Were we secured from dangers and from harms;

[042-011] But maids are frighten’d with the least alarms,

[042-012] And none are safe in this licentious time:

[042-013] Still fierce Pyreneus, and his daring crime,

[042-014] With lasting horror strikes my feeble sight,

[042-015] Nor is my mind recover’d from the fright.

[042-016] With Thracian arms this bold usurper gain’d

[042-017] Daulis and Phocis, where he proudly reign’d.

[042-018] It happen’d once, as through his lands we went,

[042-019] For the bright temple of [[delphi|Parnassus]] bent,

[042-020] He met us there, and, in his artful mind,

[042-021] Hiding the faithless action he design’d,

[042-022] Conferr’d on us (whom, O too well he knew!)

[042-023] All honours that to goddesses are due.

[042-024] ‘Stop, stop, ye muses, ’tis your friend who calls,’

[042-025] The tyrant said; ‘behold the rain that falls

[042-026] On every side, and that ill-boding sky,

[042-027] Whose lowering face portends more storms are nigh:

[042-028] Pray make my house your own, and, void of fear,

[042-029] While this bad weather lasts, take shelter here:

[042-030] Gods have made meaner places their resort,

[042-031] And for a cottage left their shining court.’

[042-032] “Obliged to stop, by the united force

[042-033] Of pouring rains, and complaisant discourse,

[042-034] His courteous invitation we obey,

[042-035] And in his hall resolve a while to stay.

[042-036] Soon it clear’d up, the clouds began to fly,

[042-037] The driving north refined the showery sky;

[042-038] Then to pursue our journey we began;

[042-039] But the false traitor to his portal ran;

[042-040] Stopp’d our escape; the door securely barr’d,

[042-041] And to our honour violence prepared;

[042-042] But we, transform’d to birds, avoid his snare,

[042-043] On pinions rising in the yielding air.

[042-044] “But he, by lust and indignation fired,

[042-045] Up to his highest tower with speed retired,

[042-046] And cries ‘In vain you from my arms withdrew,

[042-047] The way you go your lover will pursue.’

[042-048] Then in a flying posture wildly placed,

[042-049] And daring from that height himself to cast,

[042-050] The wretch fell headlong, and the ground bestrew’d

[042-051] With broken bones, and stains of guilty blood.”
