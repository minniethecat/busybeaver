[121-001] Glaucus, a fisherman of Boeotia, is transformed into a sea god, and becomes enamoured of a nereid, named Scylla, who rejects his suit.

[121-002] Here ceased the nymph; the fair assembly broke,

[121-003] The sea-green nereids to the waves betook;

[121-004] While Scylla, fearful of the wide-spread main,

[121-005] Swift to the safer shore returns again;

[121-006] There o’er the sandy margin, unarray’d,

[121-007] With printless footsteps, flies the bounding maid;

[121-008] Or in some winding creek’s secure retreat

[121-009] She bathes her weary limbs, and shuns the noonday heat.

[121-010] Her, Glaucus saw, as o’er the deep he rode,

[121-011] New to the seas, and late received a god.

[121-012] He saw, and languish’d for the virgin’s love.

[121-013] With many an artful blandishment he strove

[121-014] Her flight to hinder, and her fears remove.

[121-015] The more he sues, the more she wings her flight.

[121-016] And nimbly gains a neighbouring mountain’s height

[121-017] Steep shelving to the margin of the flood,

[121-018] A neighbouring mountain bare and woodless stood.

[121-019] Here, by the place secured, her steps she stay’d,

[121-020] And, trembling still, her lover’s form survey’d.

[121-021] His shape, his hue, her troubled sense appal,

[121-022] And drooping locks, that o’er his shoulders fall;

[121-023] She sees his face divine, and manly brow,

[121-024] End in a fish’s writhy tail below;

[121-025] She sees, and doubts within her anxious mind,

[121-026] Whether he comes of god or monster kind.

[121-027] This Glaucus soon perceived, and, “Oh, forbear!”

[121-028] His hand supporting on a rock lay near,

[121-029] “Forbear,” he cried, “fond maid, this needless fear;

[121-030] Nor fish am I, nor monster of the main,

[121-031] But equal with the watery gods I reign;

[121-032] Nor Proteus, nor Palaemon me excel,

[121-033] Nor he whose breath inspires the sounding shell.

[121-034] My birth, ’tis true, I owe to mortal race,

[121-035] And I myself but late a mortal was:

[121-036] Ev’n then, in seas, and seas alone, I joy’d,

[121-037] The seas my hours and all my cares employ’d.

[121-038] In meshes now the twinkling prey I drew;

[121-039] Now skilfully the slender line I threw,

[121-040] And silent sat the moving float to view.

[121-041] Not far from shore there lies a verdant mead,

[121-042] With herbage half, and half with water spread:

[121-043] There nor the horned heifers browsing stray,

[121-044] Nor shaggy kids, nor wanton lambkins play:

[121-045] There nor the sounding bees their nectar cull,

[121-046] Nor rural swains their genial chaplets pull,

[121-047] Nor flocks, nor herds, nor mowers, haunt the place,

[121-048] To crop the flowers, or cut the bushy grass:

[121-049] Thither sure first of living race came I,

[121-050] And sat, by chance, my drooping nets to dry.

[121-051] My scaly prize, in order all display’d,

[121-052] By number on the greensward there I laid

[121-053] My captives, which or in my nets I took,

[121-054] Or hung unwary on my wily hook.

[121-055] Strange to behold! yet what avails a lie?

[121-056] I saw them bite the grass as I sat by,

[121-057] Then sudden darting o’er the verdant plain,

[121-058] They spread their fins, as in their native main;

[121-059] I paused, with wonder’struck, while all my prey

[121-060] Left their new master, and regain’d the sea.

[121-061] Amazed, within my secret self I sought,

[121-062] What god, what herb, the miracle had wrought.

[121-063] “But sure no herbs have power like this,” I cried,

[121-064] And straight I pluck’d some neighbouring herbs and tried.

[121-065] Scarce had I bit, and proved the wondrous taste,

[121-066] When strong convulsions shook my troubled breast,

[121-067] I felt my heart grow fond of something strange,

[121-068] And my whole nature labouring with a change.

[121-069] Restless I grew, and ev’ry place forsook,

[121-070] And still upon the seas I bent my look.

[121-071] “Farewell for ever! farewell, land!” I said,

[121-072] And plunged among the waves my sinking head.

[121-073] The gentle powers, who that low empire keep,

[121-074] Received me as a brother of the deep:

[121-075] To Tethys, and to Ocean old they pray

[121-076] To purge my mortal earthy parts away.

[121-077] The watery parents to their suit agreed,

[121-078] And thrice nine times a secret charm they read,

[121-079] Then with lustrations purify my limbs,

[121-080] And bid me bathe beneath a hundred streams:

[121-081] A hundred streams from various fountains run,

[121-082] And on my head at once come rushing down.

[121-083] Thus far each passage I remember well,

[121-084] And faithfully thus far the tale I tell;

[121-085] But then oblivion dark on all my senses fell.

[121-086] Again, at length, my thoughts reviving came,

[121-087] When I no longer found myself the same;

[121-088] Then first this sea-green beard I felt to grow,

[121-089] And these large honours on my spreading brow,

[121-090] My long descending locks the billows sweep,

[121-091] And my broad shoulders cleave the yielding deep;

[121-092] My fishy tail, my arms of azure hue,

[121-093] And every part divinely changed, I view.

[121-094] But what avails these useless honours now?

[121-095] What joys can immortality bestow?

[121-096] What, though our Nereids all my form approve?

[121-097] What boots it, while fair Scylla scorns my love?”

[121-098] Thus far the god; and more he would have said;

[121-099] When from his presence flew the ruthless maid.

[121-100] Stung with repulse, in such disdainful sort,

[121-101] He seeks Titanian Circe’s horrid court.
