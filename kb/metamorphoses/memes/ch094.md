[083-001] Hercules, finding his end approaching, bestows his bow and arrows on his friend Philoctetes, and expires on Mount Oeta; after which the hero is enrolled in the number of the gods.

[083-002] But now the hero of immortal birth

[083-003] Fells Oete’s forests on the groaning earth;

[083-004] A pile he builds; to Philoctetes’ care

[083-005] He leaves his deathful instruments of war;

[083-006] To him commits those arrows, which again

[083-007] Shall see the bulwarks of the Trojan reign.

[083-008] The son of Paeon lights the lofty pyre,

[083-009] High round the structure climbs the greedy fire;

[083-010] Placed on the top, thy nervous shoulders spread

[083-011] With the Nemaean spoils, thy careless head

[083-012] Raised on the knotty club, with look divine,

[083-013] Here thou, dread hero of celestial line,

[083-014] Wert stretch’d at ease; as when a cheerful guest,

[083-015] Wine crown’d thy bowls, and flowers thy temples dress’d.

[083-016] Now on all sides the potent flames aspire,

[083-017] And crackle round those limbs that mock the fire.

[083-018] A sudden terror seized the immortal host,

[083-019] Who thought the world’s profess’d defender lost.

[083-020] This when the [[jupiter|Thunderer]] saw, with smiles he cries,

[083-021] “ ’Tis from your fears, ye gods, my pleasures rise;

[083-022] Joy swells my breast, that my all-ruling hand

[083-023] O’er such a grateful people boasts command,

[083-024] That you my suffering progeny would aid;

[083-025] Though to his deeds this just respect be paid,

[083-026] Me you’ve obliged. Be all your fears forborne,

[083-027] The Oetean fires do thou, great hero, scorn.

[083-028] Who vanquish’d all things shall subdue the flame

[083-029] That part alone of gross material frame

[083-030] Fire shall devour; while what from me he drew

[083-031] Shall live immortal, and its force subdue

[083-032] That, when he’s dead, I’ll raise to realms above;

[083-033] May all the powers the righteous act approve!

[083-034] If any god dissent, and judge too great

[083-035] The sacred honours of the heavenly seat,

[083-036] Ev’n he shall own his deeds deserve the sky,

[083-037] Ev’n he reluctant shall at length comply.”

[083-038] The assembled powers assent. No frown till now

[083-039] Had mark’d with passion vengeful Juno’s brow.

[083-040] Meanwhile whate’er was in the power of flame

[083-041] Was all consumed; his body’s nervous frame

[083-042] No more was known; of human form bereft,

[083-043] The eternal part of Jove alone was left.

[083-044] As an old serpent casts his scaly vest,

[083-045] Writhes in the sun, in youthful glory dress’d,

[083-046] So when Alcides mortal mould resign’d,

[083-047] His better part enlarged, and grew refined;

[083-048] August his visage shone; almighty Jove

[083-049] In his swift car his honour’d offspring drove;

[083-050] High o’er the hollow clouds the coursers fly,

[083-051] And lodge the hero in the starry sky.
