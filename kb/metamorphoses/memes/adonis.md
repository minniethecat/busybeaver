# Adonis

Adonis is the mortal youth whose beauty captivated Venus herself, drawing the goddess of love down from Olympus to the terrestrial world. Their story, told in Book X of the *Metamorphoses*, is one of the most celebrated tragic romances in classical literature. Venus, accidentally grazed by Cupid's arrow, falls helplessly in love with Adonis. She abandons her celestial haunts to accompany him on the hunt — but, prophetically, she warns him against lions, boars, and all beasts that "turn their backs and fight." Adonis, heedless of her advice, confronts a wild boar and is gored to death. In her grief, Venus transforms his blood into the anemone, a short-lived flower whose petals fall with the wind — a memorial as fleeting as the youth himself.

## Role in the Metamorphoses

The story of Venus and Adonis is sung by Orpheus in Book X as part of his extended song of tragic loves. "Venus becomes enamoured of young Adonis" (096-001), and the goddess, abandoning heaven, follows him through the woods dressed as a huntress. She tells him the cautionary tale of Atalanta and Hippomenes — lovers turned into lions for offending the gods — as a warning, then urges him to avoid the boar. But "Adonis far a sweeter heaven is thought" (096-013) than Venus's arms: the youth prefers the thrill of the chase. His death by the boar's tusk is swift and brutal; Venus, hearing his groans, rushes to find him dead. She sprinkles nectar on his blood, and the anemone — the "wind-flower" — rises, crimson and delicate, destined to bloom briefly and shed its petals with the first breeze.

### Key Episodes

- **Venus's Warning**: Venus tells Adonis the story of Atalanta and urges him to avoid dangerous beasts (096-001)
- **The Boar Hunt**: Adonis ignores Venus's counsel and confronts a wild boar, which gores him to death
- **The Anemone**: Venus transforms Adonis's spilled blood into the anemone flower, a living memorial (096-013)

## Transformation

Adonis's blood, after his death, is transformed by Venus into the anemone flower — a metamorphosis that preserves his beauty in a different form. The anemone's brief bloom and fragile petals mirror the youth's own fleeting life.

- **Form**: Adonis's blood → Anemone flower
- **Agent**: Venus, sprinkling nectar on the blood
- **Cause**: Grief and memorialization — love refusing death
- **Source**: (096-001, 096-013)
