[082-001] Lychas is thrown into the Euboean Sea by his angry master, and is changed into a rock by the compassion of the gods.

[082-002] The hero said; and, with the torture stung,

[082-003] Furious o’er Oete’s lofty hills he sprung.

[082-004] Stuck with the shaft, thus scours the tiger round,

[082-005] And seeks the flying author of his wound.

[082-006] Now might you see him trembling, now he vents

[082-007] His anguish’d soul in groans, and loud laments;

[082-008] He strives to tear the clinging vest in vain,

[082-009] And with uprooted forests strows the plain;

[082-010] Now kindling into rage, his hands he rears,

[082-011] And to his kindred gods directs his prayers.

[082-012] When Lychas, lo, he spies; who trembling flew,

[082-013] And in a hollow rock conceal’d from view,

[082-014] Had shunn’d his wrath. Now grief renew’d his pain,

[082-015] His madness chafed, and thus he raves again:

[082-016] “Lychas, to thee alone my fate I owe,

[082-017] Who bore the gift, the cause of all my wo.”

[082-018] The youth all pale with shiv’ring fear was stung,

[082-019] And vain excuses falter’d on his tongue.

[082-020] Alcides snatch’d him, as with suppliant face

[082-021] He strove to clasp his knees, and beg for grace:

[082-022] He toss’d him o’er his head with airy course,

[082-023] And hurl’d with more than with an engine’s force:

[082-024] Far o’er the Euboean main aloof he flies,

[082-025] And hardens by degrees amid the skies.

[082-026] So show’ry drops, when chilly tempests blow,

[082-027] Thicken at first, then whiten into snow,

[082-028] In balls congeal’d the rolling fleeces bound,

[082-029] In solid hail result upon the ground.

[082-030] Thus, whirl’d with nervous force through distant air,

[082-031] The purple tide forsook his veins with fear;

[082-032] All moisture left his limbs. Transform’d to stone,

[082-033] In ancient days the craggy flint was known:

[082-034] Still in the Euboean waves his front he rears,

[082-035] Still the small rock in human form appears,

[082-036] And still the name of hapless Lychas bears.
