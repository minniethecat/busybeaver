[095-001] [[pygmalion|Pygmalion]], a celebrated artist, becomes enamoured of a beautiful statue of ivory which he has made; and at his request [[venus|Venus]] endues it with animation, and crowns their union by the birth of a son.

[095-002] Pygmalion, loathing their lascivious life,

[095-003] Abhorr’d all womankind, but most a wife;

[095-004] So single chose to live, and shunn’d to wed,

[095-005] Well pleased to want a consort of his bed;

[095-006] Yet fearing idleness, the nurse of ill,

[095-007] In sculpture exercised his happy skill,

[095-008] And carved in ivory such a maid, so fair,

[095-009] As nature could not with his art compare,

[095-010] Were she to work; but, in her own defence,

[095-011] Must take her pattern here, and copy hence.

[095-012] Pleased with his idol, he commends, admires,

[095-013] Adores, and last, the thing adored desires:

[095-014] A very virgin in her face was seen,

[095-015] And had she moved, a living maid had been:

[095-016] One would have thought she could have stirr’d, but strove

[095-017] With modesty, and was ashamed to move:

[095-018] Art hid with art, so well perform’d the cheat,

[095-019] It caught the carver with his own deceit:

[095-020] He knows ’tis madness, yet he must adore,

[095-021] And still the more he knows it, loves the more.

[095-022] The flesh, or what so seems, he touches oft,

[095-023] Which feels so smooth that he believes it soft;

[095-024] Fired with this thought, at once he strain’d the breast,

[095-025] And on the lips a loving kiss impress’d.

[095-026] ’Tis true, the harden’d breast resists the gripe,

[095-027] And the cold lips return a kiss unripe:

[095-028] But when, retiring back, he look’d again,

[095-029] To think it ivory was a thought too mean;

[095-030] With flattery now he seeks her mind to move,

[095-031] And now with gifts, the powerful bribes of love:

[095-032] He furnishes her closet first, and fills

[095-033] The crowded shelves with rarities of shells;

[095-034] Adds orient pearls, which from the conchs he drew,

[095-035] And all the sparkling stones of various hue;

[095-036] And parrots, imitating human tongue,

[095-037] And singing birds, in silver cages hung;

[095-038] And every fragrant flower and odorous green

[095-039] Were sorted well, with lumps of amber laid between:

[095-040] Rich fashionable robes her person deck,

[095-041] Pendants her ears, and pearls adorn her neck:

[095-042] Her taper’d fingers too with rings are graced,

[095-043] And an embroider’d zone surrounds her slender waist.

[095-044] Thus like a queen array’d, so richly dress’d,

[095-045] Beauteous she show’d, but unadorn’d the best.

[095-046] Then from the floor he raised a royal bed,

[095-047] With coverings of Sidonian purple spread.

[095-048] The feast of Venus came, a solemn day,

[095-049] To which the Cypriots due devotion pay;

[095-050] With gilded horns the milk-white heifers led,

[095-051] Slaughter’d before the sacred altars bled.

[095-052] Pygmalion offering, first approach’d the shrine,

[095-053] And then with prayers implored the powers divine:

[095-054] Almighty gods, if all we mortals want,

[095-055] If all we can require, be yours to grant,

[095-056] Make this fair statue mine, he would have said,

[095-057] But changed his words for shame, and only pray’d,

[095-058] “Give me the likeness of my ivory maid.”

[095-059] The golden goddess, present at the prayer,

[095-060] Well knew he meant th’ inanimated fair,

[095-061] And gave the sign of granting his desire;

[095-062] For thrice in cheerful flames ascends the fire.

[095-063] The youth, returning to his mistress hies,

[095-064] And, impudent in hope, with ardent eyes,

[095-065] And beating breast, by the dear statue lies.

[095-066] He kisses her white lips, renews the bliss,

[095-067] And looks, and thinks they redden at the kiss;

[095-068] He thought them warm before, nor longer stays,

[095-069] But next his hand on the hard substance lays;

[095-070] Hard as it was, beginning to relent,

[095-071] It seem’d the block beneath his fingers bent:

[095-072] He felt again—his fingers made a print—

[095-073] ’Twas flesh, but flesh so firm, it rose against the dint:

[095-074] The pleasing task he fails not to renew;

[095-075] Soft, and more soft, at every touch it grew;

[095-076] Like pliant wax, when chafing hands reduce

[095-077] The former mass to form, and frame for use.

[095-078] He would believe, but yet is still in pain,

[095-079] And tries his argument of sense again,

[095-080] Presses the pulse, and feels the leaping vein:

[095-081] Convinced, o’erjoy’d, his studied thanks and praise,

[095-082] To her who made the miracle, he pays:

[095-083] Then lips to lips he join’d; now freed from fear,

[095-084] He found the savour of the kiss sincere.

[095-085] At this the waken’d image oped her eyes,

[095-086] And view’d at once the light and lover with surprise.

[095-087] The goddess, present at the match she made,

[095-088] So bless’d the bed, such fruitfulness convey’d,

[095-089] That ere ten months had sharpen’d either horn,

[095-090] To crown their bliss, a lovely boy was born:

[095-091] [[cyprus|Paphos]] his name, who, grown to manhood, wall’d

[095-092] The city Paphos, from the founder call’d.
