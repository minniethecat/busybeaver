# Olympus

Olympus is the divine center of Ovid's cosmos: the mountain-palace where Jupiter holds court, the gods assemble in council, and the decisions that drive transformation are made. It is simultaneously a physical mountain (in Thessaly), a celestial palace, and a metonym for divine power itself.

## Mythological Significance

Olympus is the **seat of authority** in the *Metamorphoses*. Every major divine decision — the Flood, the apotheosis of Caesar, the punishment of hubris — originates here. The gods' departure from Olympus (to walk the earth in disguise) and their return (for council or rest) structure the poem's alternation between divine and mortal perspectives.
