# Iris

Iris, the rainbow goddess and messenger of [[Juno]], moves freely between the realms of gods, mortals, and the dead. Where [[Mercury]] serves [[Jupiter]]'s will, Iris executes Juno's — descending to summon the Fury [[Tisiphone]], visiting the sea goddess [[Thetis]], and traveling to the underworld to gather powers for the queen of heaven's retribution. She is the visible sign of divine communication, her many-colored arc a bridge across the poem's cosmic geography. (006-177)

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Iris appears at critical junctures throughout the poem, always in service to Juno's will. She is summoned when the queen of the gods needs a message delivered to realms beyond Olympus: during the Flood, she draws water from the earth to replenish the rivers (006-177); in the Io episode, Juno employs her to guard the transformed heifer (008-001); in the Theban cycle, Iris descends to the underworld to fetch [[Tisiphone]] for Juno's assault on [[Athamas]] and [[Ino]] (033-113). Her role, though always subordinate to Juno, is essential — she is the mechanism through which divine wrath becomes terrestrial reality.

### Key Episodes

- **Replenishing the Rivers**: After the Flood recedes, Juno sends Iris to draw fresh moisture from the earth, restoring the world's rivers and springs (006-177)
- **The Io Episode**: Juno, suspecting Jupiter's infidelity, employs Iris to help secure the transformed Io — the rainbow goddess becomes an instrument of jealousy (008-001)
- **Summoning Tisiphone**: Juno, enraged at [[Athamas]] and [[Ino]], sends Iris to the underworld to rouse the sleeping Furies — Iris's descent links the poem's upper and lower worlds (033-113)
- **The Lock of Nisus**: After Scylla's betrayal, the transformed Nisus attacks his daughter; the episode's conclusion echoes Iris's earlier association with color and transformation (068-233)

## Transformation

Iris herself does not undergo metamorphosis — she is a fixed divine presence whose very nature is transformation made visible: the rainbow that appears and disappears, bridging realms. Her body is the arc of light between heaven and earth, a permanent emblem of passage and communication.

- **Form**: None — Iris is a constant divine form

- **Cause**: She is the agent of others' transformations, never the subject
