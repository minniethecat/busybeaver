[045-001] The Muses commence their song with describing the arts of [[venus|Venus]] and Cupid to inflame the god Pluto with a passion for Proserpine.

[045-002] “First Ceres taught the labouring hind to plough

[045-003] The pregnant earth, and quick’ning seed to sow;

[045-004] She first for man did wholesome food provide,

[045-005] And with just laws the wicked world supplied:

[045-006] All good from her derived, to her belong

[045-007] The grateful tributes of the muse’s song;

[045-008] Her more than worthy of our verse we deem;

[045-009] Oh! were our verse more worthy of the theme!

[045-010] “[[jupiter|Jove]] on the giant fair Trinacria hurl’d,

[045-011] And with one bolt revenged his starry world.

[045-012] Beneath her burning hills Typhoeus lies,

[045-013] And, struggling always, strives in vain to rise.

[045-014] Down does Pelorus his right hand suppress

[045-015] Towards [[latium|Latium]]; on the left Pachyne weighs:

[045-016] His legs are under Lilybaeum spread,

[045-017] And Aetna presses hard his horrid head:

[045-018] On his broad back he there extended lies,

[045-019] And vomits clouds of ashes to the skies:

[045-020] Oft labouring with his load, at last he tires,

[045-021] And pours out in revenge a flood of fires:

[045-022] Mountains he struggles to o’erwhelm, and towns;

[045-023] Earth’s inmost bowels quake, and Nature groans:

[045-024] His terrors reach the direful king of hell;

[045-025] He fears his throes will to the day reveal

[045-026] The realms of night, and fright his trembling ghosts.

[045-027] “This to prevent, he quits the Stygian coasts,

[045-028] In his black car, by sooty horses drawn,

[045-029] Fair Sicily he seeks, and dreads the dawn:

[045-030] Around her plains he casts his eager eyes,

[045-031] And every mountain to the bottom tries.

[045-032] But when, in all the careful search, he saw

[045-033] No cause of fear, no ill-suspected flaw;

[045-034] Secure from harm, and wand’ring on at will,

[045-035] Venus beheld him from her flowery bill;

[045-036] When straight the dame her little Cupid press’d,

[045-037] With secret rapture, to her snowy breast,

[045-038] And in these words the fluttering boy address’d:

[045-039] “ ‘O thou, my arms, my glory, and my power,

[045-040] My son, whom men and deathless gods adore,

[045-041] Bend thy sure bow, whose arrows never miss’d,

[045-042] No longer let hell’s king thy sway resist;

[045-043] Take him, while straggling from his dark abodes,

[045-044] He coasts the kingdoms of superior gods.

[045-045] If sovereign Jove, if gods who rule the waves,

[045-046] And Neptune, who rules them, have been thy slaves,

[045-047] Shall hell be free? The tyrant strike, my son;

[045-048] Enlarge thy mother’s empire, and thy own:

[045-049] Let not our heaven be made the mock of hell,

[045-050] But Pluto to confess thy power compel.

[045-051] Our rule is slighted in our native skies;

[045-052] See [[minerva|Pallas]], see Diana too, defies

[045-053] Thy darts, which Ceres’ daughter would despise:

[045-054] She too our empire treats with awkward scorn:

[045-055] Such insolence no longer’s to be borne:

[045-056] [[revenge|Revenge]] our slighted reign, and with thy dart

[045-057] Transfix the virgin’s to the uncle’s heart.’

[045-058] “She said; and from his quiver straight he drew

[045-059] A dart that surely would the business do;

[045-060] She guides his hand; she makes her touch the test,

[045-061] And of a thousand arrows chose the best:

[045-062] No feather better poised, a sharper head

[045-063] None had, and sooner none, and surer sped.

[045-064] He bends his how, he draws it to his ear,

[045-065] Through Pluto’s heart it drives, and fixes there.”
