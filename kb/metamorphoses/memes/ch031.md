[026-001] Pentheus is punished for his impiety by being torn in pieces by his mother and aunts, while under the influence of [[bacchus|Bacchus]].

[026-002] But Pentheus, grown more furious than before,

[026-003] Resolved to send his messengers no more,

[026-004] But went himself to the distracted throng,

[026-005] Where high Cithaeron echo’d with their song.

[026-006] And as the fiery warhorse paws the ground,

[026-007] And snorts and trembles at the trumpet’s sound,

[026-008] Transported thus he heard the frantic rout,

[026-009] And raved and madden’d at the distant shout.

[026-010] A spacious circuit on the hill there stood,

[026-011] Level and wide, and skirted round with wood;

[026-012] Here the rash Pentheus, with unhallow’d eyes,

[026-013] The howling dames and mystic orgies spies.

[026-014] His mother sternly view’d him where he stood,

[026-015] And kindled into madness as she view’d:

[026-016] Her leafy javelin at her son she cast,

[026-017] And cries, “The boar that lays our country waste!

[026-018] The boar, my sisters! Aim the fatal dart,

[026-019] And strike the brindled monster to the heart.”

[026-020] Pentheus astonish’d heard the dismal sound,

[026-021] And sees the yelling matrons gathering round;

[026-022] He sees, and weeps at his approaching fate,

[026-023] And begs for mercy, and repents too late.

[026-024] “Help! help! my aunt Autonoe,” he cried,

[026-025] “Remember how your own Actaeon died.”

[026-026] Deaf to his cries, the frantic matron crops

[026-027] One stretch’d-out arm, the other Ino lops.

[026-028] In vain does Pentheus to his mother sue,

[026-029] And the raw bleeding stumps present to view.

[026-030] His mother howl’d, and, heedless of his prayer,

[026-031] Her trembling hand she twisted in his hair,

[026-032] “And this,” she cried, “shall be Agave’s share;”

[026-033] When from his neck his struggling head she tore,

[026-034] And in her hands the ghastly visage bore.

[026-035] With pleasure all the hideous trunk survey,

[026-036] Then pull’d and tore the mangled limbs away,

[026-037] As starting in the pangs of death it lay.

[026-038] Soon as the wood its leafy honours casts,

[026-039] Blown off and scatter’d by autumnal blasts,

[026-040] With such a sudden death lay Pentheus slain,

[026-041] And in a thousand pieces strow’d the plain.

[026-042] By so distinguishing a judgment awed,

[026-043] The Thebans tremble and confess the god.
