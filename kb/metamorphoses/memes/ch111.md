[098-001] [[bacchus|Bacchus]] punishes the cruelty of the Thracian women by transforming them into trees.

[098-002] Bacchus, resolving to revenge the wrong,

[098-003] Of [[orpheus|Orpheus]] murder’d, on the madding throng,

[098-004] Decreed that each accomplice dame should stand,

[098-005] Fix’d by the roots, along the conscious land.

[098-006] Their wicked feet, that late so nimbly ran

[098-007] To wreak their malice on the guiltless man,

[098-008] Sudden with twisted ligatures were bound,

[098-009] Like trees, deep planted in the turfy ground:

[098-010] And as the fowler, with his subtle gins,

[098-011] His feather’d captives by the feet entwines,

[098-012] That fluttering pant, and struggle to get loose,

[098-013] Yet only closer draw the fatal noose;

[098-014] So these were caught; and, as they strove in vain

[098-015] To quit the place, they but increased their pain.

[098-016] They flounce and toil, yet find themselves controll’d;

[098-017] The root, though pliant, toughly keeps its hold.

[098-018] In vain their toes and feet they look to find,

[098-019] For even their shapely legs are clothed with rind.

[098-020] One smites her thighs with a lamenting stroke,

[098-021] And finds the flesh transform’d to solid oak;

[098-022] Another, with surprise and grief distress’d,

[098-023] Lays on above, but beats a wooden breast.

[098-024] A rugged bark their softer neck invades;

[098-025] Their branching arms shoot up delightful shades:

[098-026] At once they seem and are a real grove,

[098-027] With mossy trunks below, and verdant leaves above.
