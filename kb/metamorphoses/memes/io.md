# Io

Io, daughter of the river-god Inachus, is one of the most mobile figures in Ovid's *Metamorphoses* — her transformation into a heifer by Juno's jealous wrath sends her wandering across the known world. Her story occupies much of Book I and introduces several of the poem's central themes: the violence of divine desire, the cruelty of divine jealousy, and the possibility of metamorphic restoration. Her eventual return to human form and worship as the goddess Isis makes her one of the few figures in the poem to undergo a positive final transformation (008-001).

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Io's story occupies chapters 8–11 of Book I. Jupiter, seeing her, desires her and covers the earth with a dark cloud to conceal his assault. Juno, suspicious, sweeps the cloud away — but Jupiter has already transformed Io into a beautiful white heifer to hide her. Juno, admiring the animal, asks for it as a gift; Jupiter, unable to refuse without incriminating himself, consents (019-001). Juno sets the hundred-eyed Argus to guard the heifer. Unable to bear her bovine existence, Io attempts to speak to her father Inachus by tracing her name in the sand with her hoof. Inachus, recognizing his daughter, laments her fate.

Jupiter sends Mercury to kill Argus. Mercury, disguised as a shepherd, lulls all hundred eyes to sleep with his pan-pipe and beheads him. Juno places Argus's eyes in the tail of the peacock, her sacred bird — but her wrath is not sated. She sends a Fury to torment Io, driving her in a mad wandering across the world: through Greece, across the strait that bears her name (the Bosporus, "cow-crossing"), and finally to Egypt. There, Jupiter restores her to human form, and she gives birth to Epaphus. She is worshipped as the goddess Isis — one of the poem's rare upward metamorphoses (008-001).

### Key Episodes

- **The Seduction and Transformation**: Jupiter transforms Io into a heifer to conceal her from Juno (008-001)
- **The Guard of Argus**: The hundred-eyed Argus guards Io; Mercury kills him with a story and a sword
- **The Wandering**: Pursued by a Fury, Io wanders across the world — a literal journey of transformation
- **The Restoration**: In Egypt, Io is restored to human form and gives birth to Epaphus (019-001)
- **Apotheosis as Isis**: Io is worshipped as the Egyptian goddess Isis
