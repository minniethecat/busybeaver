[063-001] [[medea|Medea]], at the request of her husband, restores his aged father, Aeson, to the vigour and sprightliness of youth.

[063-002] Aemonian matrons, who their absence mourn’d,

[063-003] Rejoice to see their prosp’rous sons reiurn’d:

[063-004] Rich curling fumes of incense feast the skies,

[063-005] A hecatomb of voted victims dies,

[063-006] With gilded horns, and garlands on their head,

[063-007] And all the pomp of death, to the altar led.

[063-008] Congratulating bowls go briskly round,

[063-009] Triumphant shouts in louder music drown’d.

[063-010] Amid these revels, why that cloud of care

[063-011] On Jason’s brow? (to whom the largest share

[063-012] Of mirth was due)—his father was not there.

[063-013] Aeson was absent, once the young and brave,

[063-014] Now crush’d with years, and bending to the grave.

[063-015] At last withdrawn, and by the crowd unseen,

[063-016] Pressing her hand (with starting sighs between),

[063-017] He supplicates his kind and skilful queen.

[063-018] “O patroness, preserver of my life!

[063-019] (Dear when my mistress, and much dearer wife)

[063-020] Your favours to so vast a sum amount,

[063-021] ’Tis past the power of numbers to recount;

[063-022] Or, could they be to computation brought,

[063-023] The history would a romance be thought:

[063-024] And yet, unless you add one favour more,

[063-025] Greater than all that you conferr’d before,

[063-026] But not too hard for love and magic skill,

[063-027] Your past are thrown away, and Jason’s wretched still.

[063-028] The morning of my life is just begun,

[063-029] But my declining father’s race is run;

[063-030] From my large stock retrench the long arrears,

[063-031] And add them to expiring Aeson’s years.”

[063-032] Thus spake the generous youth, and wept the rest

[063-033] Moved with the piety of his request,

[063-034] To his aged sire such filial duty shown,

[063-035] So different from her treatment of her own,

[063-036] But still endeav’ring her remorse to hide,

[063-037] She check’d her rising sighs, and thus replied:

[063-038] “How could the thought of such inhuman wrong

[063-039] Escape,” said she, “from pious Jason’s tongue?

[063-040] Does the whole world another Jason bear,

[063-041] Whose life Medea can to yours prefer?

[063-042] Or could I with so dire a change dispense,

[063-043] Hecate will never join in that offence:

[063-044] Unjust is the request you make, and I,

[063-045] In kindness, your petition shall deny;

[063-046] Yet she, that grants not what you do implore,

[063-047] Shall yet essay to give her Jason more,

[063-048] Find means to increase the stock of Aeson’s years,

[063-049] Without retrenchment of your life’s arrears,

[063-050] Provided that the triple goddess join

[063-051] A strong confederate in my bold design.”

[063-052] Thus was her enterprise resolved; but still

[063-053] Three tedious nights are wanting to fulfil

[063-054] The circling crescents of the increasing moon;

[063-055] Then, in the height of her nocturnal noon,

[063-056] Medea steals from court; her ankles bare,

[063-057] Her garments closely girt, but loose her hair;

[063-058] Thus sallied, like a solitary sprite,

[063-059] She traverses the terrors of the night.

[063-060] Men, beasts, and birds, in soft repose lay charm’d,

[063-061] No boist’rous wind the mountain-woods alarm’d;

[063-062] Nor did those walks of love, the myrtle-trees,

[063-063] Of am’rous Zephyr hear the whisp’ring breeze;

[063-064] All elements chain’d in unactive rest,

[063-065] No sense but what the twinkling stars express’d;

[063-066] To them (that only waked) she rears her arms,

[063-067] And thus commences her mysterious charms.

[063-068] She turn’d her thrice about, as oft she threw

[063-069] On her pale tresses the nocturnal dew,

[063-070] Then yelling thrice a most enormous sound,

[063-071] Her bare knee bended on the flinty ground,

[063-072] “O Night,” said she, “thou confidant and guide

[063-073] Of secrets, such as darkness ought to hide;

[063-074] Ye stars and moon, that, when the sun retires,

[063-075] Support his empire with succeeding fires;

[063-076] And thou, great Hecate, friend to ny design;

[063-077] Songs, mutt’ring spells, your magic forces join;

[063-078] And thou, O Earth, the magazine that yields

[063-079] The midnight sorc’rer drugs; skies, mountains, fields;

[063-080] Ye watery powers of fountain, stream, and lake;

[063-081] Ye sylvan gods, and gods of night, awake,

[063-082] And generously your parts in my adventure take.

[063-083] “Oft, by your aid, swift currents I have led,

[063-084] Through wand’ring banks, back to their fountain-head;

[063-085] Transform’d the prospect of the briny deep;

[063-086] Made sleeping billows rave, and raving billows sleep

[063-087] Made clouds or sunshine, tempests rise or fall,

[063-088] And stubborn, lawless winds obey my call;

[063-089] With mutter’d words disarm’d the viper’s jaw,

[063-090] Up by the roots vast oaks and rocks could draw;

[063-091] Make forests dance, and trembling mountains come,

[063-092] Like malefactors, to receive their doom:

[063-093] Earth groan, and frighted ghosts forsake their tomb;

[063-094] Thee, Cynthia, my resistless rhymes drew down,

[063-095] When tinkling cymbals strove my voice to drown,

[063-096] Nor stronger Titan could their force sustain,

[063-097] In full career, compell’d to stop his wain;

[063-098] Nor could Aurora’s virgin blush avail,

[063-099] With pois’nous herbs I turn’d their roses pale;

[063-100] The fury of the fiery bulls I broke,

[063-101] Their stubborn necks submitting to my yoke;

[063-102] And when the sons of Earth with fury burn’d,

[063-103] Their hostile rage upon themselves I turn’d;

[063-104] The brothers made with mutual wounds to bleed,

[063-105] And by their fatal strife my lover freed;

[063-106] And, while the dragon slept, to distant Greece,

[063-107] Through cheated guards, convey’d the golden fleece.

[063-108] But now to bolder action I proceed,

[063-109] Of such prevailing juices now have need,

[063-110] That wither’d years back to their bloom can bring,

[063-111] And in dead winter raise a second spring.

[063-112] And you’ll perform ’t—

[063-113] You will; for lo! the stars, with sparkling fires,

[063-114] Presage as bright success to my desires:

[063-115] And, now, another happy omen see!

[063-116] A chariot drawn by dragons waits for me.”

[063-117] With these last words she leaps into the wain,

[063-118] Strokes the snakes’ necks, and shakes the golden rein;

[063-119] That signal given, they mount up is the skies,

[063-120] And now beneath her fruitful Tempe lies,

[063-121] Whose stores she ransacks; then to [[crete|Crete]] she flies;

[063-122] There Ossa, Pelion, Othrys, Pindus, all

[063-123] To the fair ravisher a booty fall;

[063-124] The tribute of their verdure she collects,

[063-125] Nor proud [[olympus|Olympus]]’ height his plants protects.

[063-126] Some by the roots she plucks; the tender tops

[063-127] Of others with her culling sickle crops.

[063-128] Nor could the plunder of the hills suffice,

[063-129] Down to the humble vales and meads she flies.

[063-130] A pidanus, Amphrysus, the next rape

[063-131] Sustain, nor could Enipeus’ bank escape;

[063-132] Through Beebes marsh, and through the border ranged,

[063-133] Whose pasture Glaucus to a triton changed.

[063-134] Now the ninth day, and ninth successive night,

[063-135] Had wonder’d at the restless rover’s flight;

[063-136] Meanwhile her dragons, fed with no repast,

[063-137] But her exhaling simples’ od’rous blast,

[063-138] Their tarnish’d scales and wrinkled skins had cast.

[063-139] At last return’d before her palace gate,

[063-140] Quitting her chariot, on the ground she sate,

[063-141] The sky her only canopy of state.

[063-142] All conversation with her sex she fled,

[063-143] Shunn’d the caresses of the nuptial bed;

[063-144] Two altars next of grassy turf she rears,

[063-145] This Hecate’s name, that youth’s inscription bears;

[063-146] With forest boughs and vervain these she crown’d,

[063-147] Then delves a double trench in lower ground,

[063-148] And sticks a black-fleeced ram, that ready stood,

[063-149] And drench’d the ditches with devoted blood:

[063-150] New wine she pours, and milk from the udder warm,

[063-151] With mystic murmurs to complete the charm,

[063-152] And subterranean deities alarm.

[063-153] To the stern king of ghosts she next applied,

[063-154] And gentle Proserpine, his injured bride,

[063-155] That for old Aeson with the laws of fate

[063-156] They would dispense, and lengthen his short date.

[063-157] Thus with repeated prayers she long assails

[063-158] The infernal tyrant, and at last prevails;

[063-159] Then calls to have decrepit Aeson brought,

[063-160] And stupifies him with a sleeping draught.

[063-161] On earth his body, like a corpse, extends,

[063-162] Then charges Jason and his waiting friends

[063-163] To quit the place, that no unhallow’d eye

[063-164] Into her art’s forbidden secrets pry.

[063-165] This done, the enchantress, with her locks unbound,

[063-166] [[About]] her altars trips a frantic round;

[063-167] Piecemeal the consecrated wood she splits,

[063-168] And dips the splinters in the bloody pits,

[063-169] Then hurls them on the piles; the sleeping sire

[063-170] She lustrates thrice, with sulphur, water, fire.

[063-171] In a large cauldron now the med’cine boils,

[063-172] Compounded of her late collected spoils;

[063-173] Blending into the mesh the various powers

[063-174] Of wonder-working juices, roots, and flowers;

[063-175] With gems i’ the eastern ocean’s cell refined,

[063-176] And such as ebbing tides had left behind;

[063-177] To them the midnight’s pearly dew she flings,

[063-178] A screech-owl’s carcass, and ill-boding wings;

[063-179] Nor could the wizard wolf’s warm entrails ’scape

[063-180] (That wolf who counterfeits a human shape).

[063-181] Then, from the bottom of her conj’ring bag,

[063-182] Snakes’ skins, and liver of a long-lived stag;

[063-183] Last a crow’s head, to such an age arrived,

[063-184] That he had now nine centuries survived.

[063-185] These, and with these a thousand more that grew

[063-186] In sundry soils, into her pot she threw;

[063-187] Then with a wither’d olive-bough she rakes

[063-188] The bubbling broth; the bough fresh verdure takes;

[063-189] Green leaves at first the perish’d plant surround,

[063-190] Which the next minute with ripe fruit were crown’d.

[063-191] The foaming juices now the brink o’erswell;

[063-192] The barren heath, where’er the liquor fell,

[063-193] Sprang out with vernal grass, and all the pride

[063-194] Of blooming May. When this Medea spied,

[063-195] She cut her patient’s throat; the exhausted blood

[063-196] Recruiting with her new-enchanted flood;

[063-197] While at his mouth, and through his opening wound,

[063-198] A double inlet her infusion found;

[063-199] His feeble frame resumes a youthful air,

[063-200] A glossy brown his hoary beard and hair.

[063-201] The meager paleness from his aspect fled,

[063-202] And in its room sprang up a florid red;

[063-203] Through all his limbs a youthful vigour flies,

[063-204] His emptied arteries swell with fresh supplies;

[063-205] Gazing spectators scarce believe their eyes.

[063-206] But Aeson is the most surprised to find

[063-207] A happy change in body and in mind;

[063-208] In sense and constitution the same man,

[063-209] As when his fortieth active year began.

[063-210] [[bacchus|Bacchus]], who from the clouds this wonder view’d,

[063-211] Medea’s method instantly pursued,

[063-212] And his indulgent nurse’s youth renew’d.
