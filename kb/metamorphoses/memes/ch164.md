[147-001] Ovid concludes with a rapturous anticipation of the renown which will follow the publication of this work.

[147-002] The work is finish’d, which nor dreads the rage

[147-003] Of tempests, fire, or war, or wasting age;

[147-004] Come, soon or late, death’s undetermined day,

[147-005] This mortal being only can decay;

[147-006] My nobler part, my fame, shall reach the skies,

[147-007] And to late times with blooming honours rise:

[147-008] Whate’er the unbounded Roman power obeys,

[147-009] All climes and nations shall record thy praise:

[147-010] If ’tis allow’d to poets to divine.

[147-011] One half of round eternity is mine.
