[046-001] Pluto surprises Proserpine while gathering towers in the plains of Enna, and transports her to the internal regions.

[046-002] Near Enna’s walls a spacious lake is spread,

[046-003] Famed for the sweetly-singing swans it bred;

[046-004] Pergusa is its name: and never more

[046-005] Were heard, or sweeter on Cayster’s shore.

[046-006] Woods crown the lake; and [[apollo|Phoebus]] ne’er invades

[046-007] The tufted fences, or offends the shades:

[046-008] Fresh fragrant breezes fan the verdant bowers,

[046-009] And the moist ground smiles with enamell’d flowers:

[046-010] The cheerful birds their airy carols sing,

[046-011] And the whole year is one eternal spring.

[046-012] Here while young Proserpine, among the maids,

[046-013] Diverts herself in these delicious shades;

[046-014] While, like a child, with busy speed and care,

[046-015] She gathers lilies here, and violets there;

[046-016] While first to fill her little lap she strives,

[046-017] Hell’s grisly monarch at the shade arrives;

[046-018] Sees her thus sporting on the flowery green,

[046-019] And loves the blooming maid as soon as seen.

[046-020] His urgent flame impatient of delay,

[046-021] Swift as his thought he seized the beauteous prey,

[046-022] And bore her in his sooty car away.

[046-023] The frighted goddess to her mother cries;

[046-024] But all in vain, for now far off she flies;

[046-025] Far she behind her leaves her virgin train;

[046-026] To them too cries, and cries to them in vain;

[046-027] And while with passion she repeats her call,

[046-028] The violets from her lap and lilies fall:

[046-029] She misses them, poor heart! and makes new moan;

[046-030] Her lilies, ah! are lost, her violets gone.

[046-031] O’er hills the ravisher and valleys speeds,

[046-032] By name encouraging his foamy steeds;

[046-033] He rattles o’er their necks the rusty reins,

[046-034] And ruffles with the stroke their shaggy manes.

[046-035] O’er lakes he whirls his flying wheels, and comes

[046-036] To the Palici, breathing sulph’rous fumes;

[046-037] And thence to where the Bacchiads of renown,

[046-038] Between unequal havens, built their town;

[046-039] Where Arethusa, round the imprison’d sea,

[046-040] Extends her crooked coast to Cyane;

[046-041] The nymph who gave the neighb’ring lake a name,

[046-042] Of all Sicilian nymphs the first in fame:

[046-043] She from the waves advanced her beauteous head;

[046-044] The goddess knew, and thus to Pluto said:

[046-045] “Farther thou shalt not with the virgin run;

[046-046] Ceres unwilling, canst thou be her son?

[046-047] The maid should be by sweet persuasion won:

[046-048] Force suits not with the softness of the fair;

[046-049] For, if great things with small I may compare,

[046-050] Me Anapis once loved; a milder course

[046-051] He took, and won me by his words, not force.”

[046-052] Then, stretching out her arms, she stopp’d his way:

[046-053] But he, impatient of the shortest stay,

[046-054] Throws to his dreadful steeds the slacken’d rein,

[046-055] And strikes his iron sceptre through the main;

[046-056] The depths profound through yielding waves he cleaves,

[046-057] And to hell’s centre a free passage leaves;

[046-058] Down sinks his chariot, and his realms of night

[046-059] The god soon reaches with a rapid flight.
