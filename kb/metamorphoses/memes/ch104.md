[092-001] Cyparissus by accident kills a favourite stag, which affects him with so much grief, that he pines away, and is changed into a cypress-tree by [[apollo|Apollo]].

[092-002] Amid the throng of this promiscuous wood,

[092-003] With pointed top, the taper cypress stood,

[092-004] A tree, which once a youth, and heavenly fair,

[092-005] Was of that deity the darling care,

[092-006] Whose hand adapts, with equal skill, the strings

[092-007] To bows with which he kills, and harps to which he sings.

[092-008] For heretofore, a mighty stag was bred,

[092-009] Which on the fertile fields of Caea fed;

[092-010] In shape and size he all his kind excell’d,

[092-011] And to Carthaean nymphs was sacred held;

[092-012] His beamy head, with branches high display’d,

[092-013] Afforded to itself an ample shade;

[092-014] His horns were gilt, and his smooth neck was graced

[092-015] With silver collars thick with gems enchased;

[092-016] A silver boss upon his forehead hung,

[092-017] And brazen pendants in his ear-rings rung;

[092-018] Frequenting houses he familiar grew,

[092-019] And learn’d, by custom, nature to subdue,

[092-020] Till by degrees, of fear and wildness broke,

[092-021] Ev’n stranger hands his proffer’d neck might stroke.

[092-022] Much was the beast by Caea’s youth caress’d,

[092-023] But thou, sweet Cyparissus, lovedst him best;

[092-024] By thee, to pastures fresh, he oft was led,

[092-025] By thee oft water’d at the fountain’s head;

[092-026] His horns with garlands, now, by thee were tied,

[092-027] And, now, thou on his back wouldst wanton ride;

[092-028] Now here, now there, wouldst bound along the plains,

[092-029] Ruling his tender mouth with purple reins.

[092-030] ’Twas when the summer sun, at noon of day,

[092-031] Through glowing Cancer shot his burning ray,

[092-032] ’Twas then, the fav’rite stag, in cool retreat,

[092-033] Had sought a shelter from the scorching heat:

[092-034] Along the grass his weary limbs he laid,

[092-035] Inhaling freshness, from the breezy shade,

[092-036] When Cyparissus, with his pointed dart,

[092-037] Unknowing, pierced him to the panting heart;

[092-038] But when the youth, surprised, his error found,

[092-039] And saw him dying of the cruel wound,

[092-040] Himself he would have slain through desperate grief;

[092-041] What said not Phoebus, that might yield relief:

[092-042] To cease his mourning he the boy desired,

[092-043] Or mourn no more than such a loss required;

[092-044] But ho incessant grieved. At length address’d

[092-045] To the superior powers a last request;

[092-046] Praying, in expiation of his crime,

[092-047] Thenceforth to mourn to all succeeding time.

[092-048] And now of blood exhausted he appears,

[092-049] Drain’d by a torrent of continual tears;

[092-050] The fleshy colour in his body fades,

[092-051] And a green tincture all his limbs invades:

[092-052] From his fair head, where curling locks late hung,

[092-053] A horrid bush with bristled branches sprung,

[092-054] Which, stiffening by degrees, its stem extends,

[092-055] Till to the starry skies the spire ascends.

[092-056] Apollo sad look’d on, and sighing, cried:

[092-057] “Then, be for ever what thy prayer implied,

[092-058] Bemoan’d by me, in others grief excite,

[092-059] And still preside at every funeral rite.”

[092-060] Thus the sweet artist in a wondrous shade

[092-061] Of verdant trees, which harmony had made,

[092-062] Encircled sat, with his own triumphs crown’d,

[092-063] Of listening birds and savages around.

[092-064] Again the trembling strings he dext’rous tries,

[092-065] Again from discord makes soft music rise;

[092-066] Then tunes his voice: “Oh muse, from whom I sprung,

[092-067] [[jupiter|Jove]] be my theme, and thou inspire my song:

[092-068] To Jove ray grateful voice I oft have raised,

[092-069] Oft his almighty power with pleasure praised.

[092-070] I sung the giants in a solemn strain,

[092-071] Blasted and thunderstruck on Phlegra’s plain.

[092-072] Now be my lyre in softer accents moved,

[092-073] To sing of blooming boys by gods beloved,

[092-074] And to relate what virgins, void of shame,

[092-075] Have suffer’d vengeance for a lawless flame.”
