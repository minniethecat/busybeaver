# Medea's Revenge on Jason

When Jason abandons Medea to marry Creusa, the sorceress's revenge is comprehensive and devastating. The poisoned crown and robe — the products of her magical art — consume the bride in unquenchable fire, destroy the palace, and leave Jason bereft. Medea's escape in her dragon-drawn chariot makes her one of the few mortals who enacts terrible transformation and evades its consequences.

## Characters

| Character | Role |
|-----------|------|
| Medea | Sorceress, abandoned wife, agent of revenge |
| Jason | Argonaut hero, betrayer of Medea |
| Creusa | Jason's new bride, killed by Medea's poisoned gifts |

## Setting

Corinth, where Jason has abandoned Medea for Creusa, daughter of King Creon. The palace burns as Medea's poisonous gifts work their destruction.

## Narrative

Abandoned by Jason, Medea prepares her revenge. She sends a poisoned crown and robe to Creusa as wedding gifts. When the bride puts them on, they ignite — unquenchable fire consuming her flesh. Her father Creon, embracing her, catches the fire and perishes. Medea, having killed her own children by Jason to complete the revenge, ascends in her dragon-drawn chariot and flies away — untouchable, unpunished.
