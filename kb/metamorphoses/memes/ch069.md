[061-001] Boreas is enamoured of the beautiful Orithyia, whom he carries off against her inclination—She afterward becomes the mother of Zethes and Calais, who accompany the Argonautic expedition.

[061-002] Erechtheus next the Athenian sceptre sway’d

[061-003] Whose rule the state with joint consent obey’d;

[061-004] So mix’d his justice with his valour flow’d,

[061-005] His reign one scene of princely goodness show’d.

[061-006] Four hopeful youths, as many females bright,

[061-007] Sprung from his loins, and sooth’d him with delight.

[061-008] Two of these sisters, of a lovelier air,

[061-009] Excell’d the rest, though all the rest were fair.

[061-010] Procris to Cephalus in wedlock tied,

[061-011] Bless’d the young sylvan with a blooming bride:

[061-012] For Orithyia Boreas suffer’d pain;

[061-013] For the coy maid sued long, but sued in vain;

[061-014] Tereus, his neighbour, and his Thracian blood,

[061-015] Against the match a main objection stood,

[061-016] Which made his vows, and all his suppliant love,

[061-017] Empty as air, and ineffectual, prove.

[061-018] But when he found his soothing flatteries fail,

[061-019] Nor saw his soft addresses could avail,

[061-020] Blustering with ire, he quickly has recourse

[061-021] To rougher arts, and his own native force.

[061-022] “ ’Tis well,” he said; “such usage is my due,

[061-023] When thus disguised by foreign ways I sue;

[061-024] When my stern airs and fierceness I disclaim,

[061-025] And sigh for love, ridiculously tame;

[061-026] When soft addresses foolishly I try,

[061-027] Nor my own stronger remedies apply.

[061-028] By force and violence I chiefly live,

[061-029] By them the low’ring stormy tempests drive,

[061-030] In foaming billows raise the hoary deep,

[061-031] Writhe knotted oaks, and sandy deserts sweep,

[061-032] Congeal the falling flakes of fleecy snow,

[061-033] And bruise with rattling hail the plain below.

[061-034] I and my brother winds, when join’d above,

[061-035] Through the waste champaign of the skies we rove,

[061-036] With such a boisterous full career engage,

[061-037] That heaven’s whole concave thunders at our rage.

[061-038] While, struck from nitrous clouds, fierce lightnings play,

[061-039] Dart through the storm, and gild the gloomy day:

[061-040] Or when, in subterraneous caverns pent,

[061-041] My breath against the hollow earth is bent,

[061-042] The quaking world above, and ghosts below,

[061-043] My mighty power, by dear experience, know,

[061-044] Tremble with fear, and dread the fatal blow.

[061-045] This is the only cure to be applied,

[061-046] Thus to Erechtheus I should be allied;

[061-047] And thus the scornful virgin should be woo’d,

[061-048] Not by entreaty, but by force subdued.”

[061-049] Boreas, in passion, spoke these huffing things,

[061-050] And, as he spoke, he shook his dreadful wings,

[061-051] At which, afar the shivering sea was fann’d,

[061-052] And the wide surface of the distant land:

[061-053] His dusty mantle o’er the hills he drew,

[061-054] And swept the lowly valleys as he flew;

[061-055] Then, with his yellow wings, embraced the maid,

[061-056] And, wrapp’d in dusky clouds, far off convey’d.

[061-057] The sparkling blaze of love’s prevailing fire

[061-058] Shone brighter as he flew, and flamed the higher

[061-059] And now the god, possess’d of his delight,

[061-060] To northern [[thrace|Thrace]] pursued his airy flight.

[061-061] Two lovely twins, the effect of this embrace,

[061-062] Crown their soft labours, and their nuptials grace,

[061-063] Who, like their mother, beautiful and fair,

[061-064] Their father’s strength, and feather’d pinions, share:

[061-065] Yet these at first were wanting, as ’tis said,

[061-066] And after, as they grew, their shoulders spread.

[061-067] Zethes and Calais, the pretty twins,

[061-068] Remain’d unfledg’d, while smooth their beardless chins:

[061-069] But when, in time, the budding silver down

[061-070] Shaded their face, and on their cheeks was grown,

[061-071] Two sprouting wings upon their shoulders sprung,

[061-072] Like those in birds, that veil the callow young.

[061-073] Then, as their age advanced, and they began

[061-074] From greener youth to ripen into man,

[061-075] With Jason’s Argonauts they cross’d the seas,

[061-076] Embark’d in quest of the fam’d golden fleece;

[061-077] There, with the rest, the first frail vessel tried,

[061-078] And boldly ventured on the swelling tide.
