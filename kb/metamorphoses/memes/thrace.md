# Thrace

Thrace, the wild region north of Greece, is the homeland of Orpheus and the site of his death. After losing Eurydice, Orpheus retreats to the Thracian wilderness, where his songs move trees and stones. It is here that the Maenads, enraged by his turn to love of boys, tear him apart — making Thrace the landscape where art and violence converge.
