[051-001] The god Alpheus, becoming enamoured of Arethusa, a follower of Diana, pursues her for a considerable distance, when the nymph, ready to sink under fatigue, implores the aid of her protectress, who changes her into a fountain, with whose streams the river Alpheus mingles.

[051-002] Still were the purling waters, and the maid

[051-003] From the smooth surface raised her beauteous head,

[051-004] Wipes off the drops that from her tresses ran,

[051-005] And thus to tell Alpheus’ loves began.

[051-006] “In Elis first I breathed the living air;

[051-007] The chase was all my pleasure, all my care:

[051-008] None loved like me the forest to explore,

[051-009] To pitch the toils, and drive the bristled boar.

[051-010] Of fair, though masculine, I had the name,

[051-011] But gladly would to that have quitted claim:

[051-012] It less my pride than indignation raised,

[051-013] To hear the beauty I neglected praised;

[051-014] Such compliments I loathed, such charms as these

[051-015] I scorn’d, and thought it infamy to please.

[051-016] “Once, I remember, in the summer’s heat,

[051-017] Tired with the chase, I sought a cool retreat,

[051-018] And walking on, a silent current found,

[051-019] Which gently glided o’er the gravelly ground;

[051-020] The crystal water was so smooth, so clear,

[051-021] My eye distinguish’d every pebble there;

[051-022] So soft its motion, that I scarce perceived

[051-023] The running stream, or what I saw believed:

[051-024] The hoary willow and the poplar made,

[051-025] Along the shelving bank, a grateful shade.

[051-026] In the cool rivulet my feet I dipp’d,

[051-027] Then waded to the knee, and then I stripp’d:

[051-028] My robe I careless on an osier threw,

[051-029] That near the place commodiously grew;

[051-030] Nor long upon the border naked stood,

[051-031] But plunged with speed into the silver flood:

[051-032] My arms a thousand ways I moved, and tried

[051-033] To quicken, if I could, the lazy tide,

[051-034] Where, while I play’d my swimming gambols o’er,

[051-035] I heard a murm’ring voice, and frighted sprung to shore.

[051-036] ‘Oh! whither, Arethusa, dost thou fly?’

[051-037] From the brook’s bottom did Alpheus cry.

[051-038] Again I heard him, in a hollow tone:

[051-039] ‘Oh! whither, Arethusa, dost thou run?’

[051-040] Naked I flew, nor could I stay to hide

[051-041] My limbs; my robe was on the other side:

[051-042] Alpheus follow’d fast; the inflaming sight

[051-043] Quicken’d his speed, and made his labour light:

[051-044] He sees me ready for his eager arms,

[051-045] And with a greedy glance devours my charms.

[051-046] As trembling doves from pressing danger fly,

[051-047] When the fierce hawk comes sousing from the sky,

[051-048] And as fierce hawks the trembling doves pursue,

[051-049] From him I fled, and after me he few.

[051-050] First by Orchomenus I took my flight,

[051-051] And soon had Psophis and Cyllene in sight;

[051-052] Behind me then high Maenalus I lost,

[051-053] And craggy Erimanthus, scaled with frost;

[051-054] Elis was next: thus far the ground I trod,

[051-055] With nimble feet, before the distanced god:

[051-056] But here I lagg’d, unable to sustain

[051-057] The labour longer, and my flight maintain;

[051-058] While he, more strong, more patient of the toil,

[051-059] And fired with hopes of beauty’s speedy spoil,

[051-060] Gain’d my lost ground, and, by redoubled pace,

[051-061] Now left between us but a narrow space.

[051-062] Unwearied I till now o’er hills and plains,

[051-063] O’er rocks and rivers, ran, and felt no pains;

[051-064] The sun behind me and the god I kept;

[051-065] But when I fastest should have run, I stepp’d.

[051-066] Before my feet his shadow now appear’d;

[051-067] As what I saw, or rather what I fear’d:

[051-068] Yet there I could not be deceived by fear,

[051-069] Who felt his breath pant on my braided hair,

[051-070] And heard his sounding tread, and knew him to be near.

[051-071] Tired and despairing, ‘O celestial maid,

[051-072] I’m caught,’ I cried, ‘without thy heavenly aid;

[051-073] Help me, Diana, help a nymph forlorn,

[051-074] Devoted to the woods, who long has worn

[051-075] Thy livery, and long thy quiver borne.’

[051-076] The goddess heard; my pious prayer prevail’d;

[051-077] In muffling clouds my virgin head was veil’d.

[051-078] The am’rous god, deluded of his hopes,

[051-079] Searches the gloom, and through the darkness gropes:

[051-080] Twice where Diana did her servant hide

[051-081] He came, and twice, ‘O Arethusa!’ cried.

[051-082] How shaken was my soul, how sunk my heart!

[051-083] The terror seized on every trembling part.

[051-084] Thus when the wolf about the mountain prowls

[051-085] For prey, the lambkin hears his horrid howls:

[051-086] The tim’rous hare, the pack approaching nigh

[051-087] Thus hearkens to the hounds, and trembles at the cry;

[051-088] Nor dares she stir, for fear her scented breath

[051-089] Direct the dogs, and guide the threaten’d death.

[051-090] Alpheus in the cloud no traces found

[051-091] To mark my way, yet stays to guard the ground.

[051-092] The god so near, a chilly sweat possess’d

[051-093] My fainting limbs, at every pore express’d;

[051-094] My strength distill’d in drops, my hair in dew;

[051-095] My form was changed, and all my substance new:

[051-096] Each motion was a stream, and my whole frame

[051-097] Turn’d to a fount, which still preserves my name.

[051-098] Resolved I should not his embrace escape,

[051-099] Again the god resumes his fluid shape;

[051-100] To mix his streams with mine he fondly tries,

[051-101] But still Diana his attempt denies:

[051-102] She cleaves the ground; through caverns dark I run

[051-103] A different current, while he keeps his own;

[051-104] To dear Ortygia she conducts my way,

[051-105] And here I first review the welcome day.”

[051-106] Here Arethusa stopp’d; then Ceres takes

[051-107] Her golden car, and yokes her fiery snakes;

[051-108] With a rein, along mid-heaven she flies,

[051-109] O’er earth and seas, and cuts the yielding skies:

[051-110] She halls at Athens, dropping like a star,

[051-111] And to Triptolemus resigns her car.

[051-112] Parent of seed, she gave him fruitful grain,

[051-113] And bade him teach to till and plough the plain;

[051-114] The seed to sow, as well in fallow fields,

[051-115] As where the soil manured a richer harvest yields.
