[123-001] After being detained at Carthage, Aeneas at length arrives on the coast of Naples.

[123-002] Here bulged the pride of famed Ulysses’ fleet,

[123-003] But good Aeneas ’scaped the fate he met.

[123-004] As to the Latian shore the Trojan stood,

[123-005] And cut with well-timed oars the foaming flood,

[123-006] He weather’d fell Charybdis; but ere long

[123-007] The skies were darken’d, and the tempest strong;

[123-008] Then to the Libyan coast he stretches o’er,

[123-009] And makes at length the Carthaginian shore.

[123-010] Here Dido, with a hospitable care,

[123-011] Into her heart receives the wanderer.

[123-012] From her kind arms the ungrateful hero flies,

[123-013] The injured queen looks on with dying eyes,

[123-014] Then to her folly falls a sacrifice.

[123-015] Aeneas now sets sail, and plying gains

[123-016] Fair Eryx, where his friend Acestes reigns:

[123-017] First to his sire does funeral rites decree,

[123-018] Then gives the signal next, and stands to sea,

[123-019] Outruns the islands where volcanoes roar,

[123-020] Gets clear of sirens and their faithless shore;

[123-021] But loses Palinurus in the way;

[123-022] Then makes Inarime and Prochyta.
