[054-001] [[arachne|Arachne]] presumes to challenge [[minerva|Minerva]] to a trial of skill in needlework—Being defeated, she hangs herself in despair, and is changed into a spider by the goddess.

[054-002] Pallas, attending to the muse’s song,

[054-003] Approved the just resentment of their wrong,

[054-004] And thus reflects: “While tamely I commend

[054-005] Those who their injured deities defend,

[054-006] My own divinity affronted stands,

[054-007] And calls aloud for justice at my hands;”

[054-008] Then takes the hint, ashamed to lag behind,

[054-009] And on Arachne bends her vengeful mind;

[054-010] One at the loom so excellently skill’d,

[054-011] That to the goddess she refused to yield.

[054-012] Low was her birth, and small her native town:

[054-013] She from her art alone obtain’d renown.

[054-014] Idmon, her father, made it his employ

[054-015] To give the spongy fleece a purple die:

[054-016] Of vulgar strain her, mother, lately dead,

[054-017] With her own rank had been content to wed;

[054-018] Yet she their daughter, though her time was spent

[054-019] In a small hamlet, and of mean descent,

[054-020] Through the great towns of Lydia gain’d a name,

[054-021] And fill’d the neighb’ring countries with her fame.

[054-022] Oft, to admire the niceness of her skill,

[054-023] The nymphs would quit their fountain, shade, or hill;

[054-024] Thither, from green Tymolus, they repair,

[054-025] And leave the vineyards, their peculiar care:

[054-026] Thither, from famed Partolus’ golden stream,

[054-027] Drawn by her art, the curious Naiads came:

[054-028] Nor would the work, when finish’d, please so much,

[054-029] As, while she wrought, to view each graceful touch:

[054-030] Whether the shapeless wool in balls she wound,

[054-031] Or with quick motion turn’d the spindle round,

[054-032] Or with her pencil drew the neat design,

[054-033] Pallas, her mistress, shone in every line.

[054-034] This the proud maid, with scornful air, denies,

[054-035] And ev’n the goddess at her work defies;

[054-036] Disowns her heavenly mistress every hour,

[054-037] Nor asks her aid, nor deprecates her power.

[054-038] “Let us,” she cries, “but to a trial come,

[054-039] And, if she conquers, let her fix my doom.”

[054-040] The goddess then a beldam’s form put on;

[054-041] With silver hairs her hoary temples shone;

[054-042] Propp’d hy a staff, she hobbles in her walk,

[054-043] And, tottering, thus begins her old wives’ talk:

[054-044] “Young maid attend, nor stubbornly despise

[054-045] The admonitions of the old and wise;

[054-046] For age, though scorn’d, a ripe experience bears,

[054-047] That golden fruit, unknown to blooming years:

[054-048] Still may remotest fame your labours crown,

[054-049] And mortals your superior genius own;

[054-050] But to the goddess yield, and, humbly meek,

[054-051] A pardon for your bold presumption seek:

[054-052] The goddess will forgive.” At this the maid,

[054-053] With passion fired, her gliding shuttle stay’d,

[054-054] And, darting vengeance, with an angry look,

[054-055] To Pallas in disguise thus fiercely spoke:

[054-056] “Thou doting thing, whose idle, babbling tongue

[054-057] But too well shows the plague of living long,

[054-058] Hence, and reprove, with this your sage advice,

[054-059] Your giddy daughter, or your awkward niece:

[054-060] Know I despise your counsel, and an still

[054-061] A woman, ever wedded to my will;

[054-062] And, if your skilful goddess better knows,

[054-063] Let her accept the trial I propose.”

[054-064] “She does,” impatient Pallas straight replies,

[054-065] And, clothed with heavenly light, sprung from her odd disguise.

[054-066] The nymphs and virgins of the plain adore

[054-067] The awful goddess, and confess her power:

[054-068] The maid alone stood unappall’d, yet show’d

[054-069] A transient blush, that for a moment glow’d,

[054-070] Then disappear’d, as purple streaks adorn

[054-071] The opening beauties of the rosy morn;

[054-072] Till [[apollo|Phoebus]], rising prevalently bright,

[054-073] Allays the tincture with his silver light.

[054-074] Yet she persists, and, obstinately great,

[054-075] In hopes of conquest, hurries on her fate.

[054-076] The goddess now the challenge waves no more,

[054-077] Nor, kindly good, advises as before.

[054-078] Straight to their posts appointed both repair,

[054-079] And fix their threaded looms with equal care:

[054-080] Around the solid beam the web is tied,

[054-081] While hollow canes the parting warp divide,

[054-082] Through which, with nimble flight, the shuttles play,

[054-083] And for the woof prepare a ready way:

[054-084] The woof and warp unite, press’d by the toothy sley.

[054-085] Thus both, their mantles button’d to their breast,

[054-086] Their skilful fingers play with willing haste,

[054-087] And work with pleasure, while they cheer the eye

[054-088] With glowing purple of the Tyrian die:

[054-089] Or, justly intermixing shades with light,

[054-090] Their colourings insensibly unite.

[054-091] As when a shower, transpierced with sunny rays,

[054-092] Its mighty arch along the heaven displays,

[054-093] From whence a thousand different colours rise,

[054-094] Whose fine transition cheats the clearest eyes:

[054-095] So like the intermingled shading seems,

[054-096] And only differs in the last extremes,

[054-097] Then threads of gold both artfully dispose,

[054-098] And, as each part in just proportion rose,

[054-099] Some antique fable in their work disclose.

[054-100] Pallas in figures wrought the heavenly powers,

[054-101] And Mars’s hill among the Athenian towers:

[054-102] On lofty thrones twice six celestials sate,

[054-103] [[jupiter|Jove]] in the midst, and held their warm debate;

[054-104] The subject weighty, and well known to fame.

[054-105] “From whom the city should receive its name.”

[054-106] Each god by proper features was express’d;

[054-107] Jove, with majestic mien, excell’d the rest:

[054-108] His three-fork’d mace the dewy sea-god shook,

[054-109] And, looking sternly, smote the ragged rock,

[054-110] When from the stone leap’d forth a sprightly steed,

[054-111] And Neptune claims the city for the deed.

[054-112] Herself she blazons, with a glittering spear,

[054-113] And crested helm, that veil’d her braided hair,

[054-114] With shields, and scaly breastplate, implements of war.

[054-115] Struck with her pointed lance, the teeming earth

[054-116] Seem’d to produce a new surprising birth,

[054-117] When, from the glebe, the pledge of conquest sprung—

[054-118] A tree pale green, with fairest olives hung.

[054-119] And then, to let her giddy rival learn

[054-120] What just rewards such boldness was to earn,

[054-121] Four trials at each corner had their part,

[054-122] Design’d in miniature, and touch’d with art.

[054-123] Haemus in one, and Rhodope of [[thrace|Thrace]],

[054-124] Transform’d to mountains, fill’d the foremost place,

[054-125] Who claim’d the titles of the gods above,

[054-126] And vainly used the epithets of Jove.

[054-127] Another show’d where the Pigmaean dame,

[054-128] Profaning Juno’s venerable name,

[054-129] Turn’d to an airy crane, descends from far,

[054-130] And with her pygmy subjects wages war.

[054-131] In a third part, the rage of heaven’s great queen,

[054-132] Display’d on proud Antigone, was seen,

[054-133] Who, with presumptuous boldness, dared to vie,

[054-134] For beauty, with the emperess of the sky.

[054-135] Ah! what avails her ancient princely race;

[054-136] Her sire a king, and [[troy|Troy]] her native place?

[054-137] Now, to a noisy stork transform’d, she flies,

[054-138] And with her whiten’d pinions cleaves the skies:

[054-139] And in the last remaining part was drawn

[054-140] Poor Cinyras, that seem’d to weep in stone;

[054-141] Clasping the temple steps, ne sadly mourn’d

[054-142] His lovely daughters, now to marble turn’d.

[054-143] With her own tree the finish’d piece is crown’d

[054-144] And wreaths of peaceful olive all the work surround.

[054-145] Arachne drew the famed intrigues of Jove,

[054-146] Changed to a bull, to gratify his love;

[054-147] How through the briny tide, all foaming hoar,

[054-148] Lovely Europa on his back he bore.

[054-149] The sea seem’d waving, and the trembling maid

[054-150] Shrunk up her tender feet, as if afraid,

[054-151] And, looking back on the forsaken strand,

[054-152] To her companions wafts her distant hand.

[054-153] Next she design’d Asteria’s fabled rape,

[054-154] When Jove assumed a soaring eagle’s shape:

[054-155] And show’d how Leda lay supinely press’d,

[054-156] While the soft snowy swan sat hovering o’er her breast:

[054-157] How in a satyr’s form the god beguiled,

[054-158] When fair Antiope with twins he fill’d:

[054-159] Then, like Amphitryon, but a real Jove,

[054-160] In fair Alcmena’s arms he cool’d his love:

[054-161] In fluid gold to Danae’s heart he came:

[054-162] Aegina felt him in a lambent flame:

[054-163] He took Mnemosyne in shepherd’s make

[054-164] And for Deois was a speckled snake.

[054-165] She made thee, Neptune, like a wanton steer,

[054-166] Pacing the meads for love of Arne dear:

[054-167] Next, like a stream, thy burning flame to slake;

[054-168] And like a ram, for fair Bisaltis’ sake.

[054-169] Then Ceres in a steed your vigour tried,

[054-170] Nor could the mare the yellow goddess hide:

[054-171] Next, to a fowl transform’d, you won by force

[054-172] The snake-hair’d mother of the winged horse;

[054-173] And, in a dolphin’s fishy form, subdued

[054-174] Melantho sweet, beneath the oozy flood.

[054-175] All these the maid with lively features drew,

[054-176] And open’d proper landscapes to the view.

[054-177] There Phobus, roving like a country swain,

[054-178] Attunes his jolly pipe along the plain;

[054-179] For lovely Isso’s sake, in stepherd’s weeds,

[054-180] O’er pastures green his bleating flock he feeds.

[054-181] There [[bacchus|Bacchus]], imaged like the clustering grape,

[054-182] Melting, bedrops Erigone’s fair lap:

[054-183] And there old Saturn, stung with youthful heat,

[054-184] Form’d like a stallion, rushes to the feat.

[054-185] Fresh flowers, which twists of ivy intertwine,

[054-186] Mingling a running foliage, close the neat design.

[054-187] This the bright goddess, passionately moved,

[054-188] With envy saw, yet inwardly approved.

[054-189] The scene of heavenly guilt with haste she tore,

[054-190] Nor longer the affront with patience bore:

[054-191] A boxen shuttle in her hand she took,

[054-192] And more than once Arachne’s forehead struck.

[054-193] The unhappy maid, impatient of the wrong,

[054-194] Down from a beam her injured person hung;

[054-195] When Pallas, pitying her wretched state,

[054-196] At once prevented and pronounced her fate:

[054-197] “Live; but depend, vile wretch,” the goddess cried,

[054-198] “[[fate|Doom]]’d in suspense for ever to be tied;

[054-199] That all your race, to utmost date of time,

[054-200] May feel the vengeance, and detest the crime.”

[054-201] Then, going off, she sprinkled her with juice,

[054-202] Which leaves of baneful aconite produced.

[054-203] Touch’d with the pois’nous drug, her flowing hair

[054-204] Fell to the ground, and left her temples bare;

[054-205] Her usual features vanish’d from their place

[054-206] Her body lessen’d all, but most her face:

[054-207] Her slender fingers, hanging on each side,

[054-208] With many joints, the use of legs supplied;

[054-209] A spider’s bag the rest, from which she gives

[054-210] A thread, and still by constant weaving lives.
