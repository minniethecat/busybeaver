# Mount Sipylus

Mount Sipylus, in Lydia (Asia Minor), is the final resting place of Niobe — or rather, it *is* Niobe, transformed into a weeping rock that still sheds tears. The mountain is the *Metamorphoses*' most powerful image of landscape absorbing tragedy, of place becoming identical with the grief it witnesses.

## Mythological Significance

Mount Sipylus is a **petrified witness**: the mountain that weeps is itself the evidence of the transformation. Ovid's detail that "tears still trickle from the marble" (053-001) collapses the distinction between geology and grief — the mountain is Niobe, and Niobe is the mountain.
