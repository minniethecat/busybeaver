[104-001] Ceyx, the husband of Alcyone, is drowned while on a voyage to consult the oracle of [[apollo|Apollo]]—The wife is apprized, in a dream, of his fate, and throws herself into the sea, when she and Ceyx are transformed into halcyons or kingfishers.

[104-002] These prodigies affect the pious prince:

[104-003] But more perplex’d with those that happen’d since,

[104-004] He purposes to seek the Clarian god,

[104-005] Avoiding [[delphi|Delphi]], his more famed abode.

[104-006] Since Phrygian robbers made unsafe the road:

[104-007] Yet could he not, from her he loved so well,

[104-008] The fatal voyage he resolved, conceal.

[104-009] But when she saw her lord prepared to part,

[104-010] A deadly cold ran shivering to her heart,

[104-011] Her faded cheeks are changed to boxen hue,

[104-012] And in her eyes the tears are ever new.

[104-013] She thrice essay’d to speak, her accents hung,

[104-014] And, faltering, died unfinish’d on her tongue,

[104-015] Or vanish’d into sighs: with long delay

[104-016] Her voice return’d, and found the wonted way.

[104-017] “Tell me, my lord,” she said, “what fault unknown

[104-018] Thy once beloved Alcyone has done?

[104-019] Whither, ah! whither is thy kindness gone?

[104-020] Can Ceyx, then, sustain to leave his wife,

[104-021] And, unconcern’d, forsake the sweets of life?

[104-022] What can thy mind to this long journey move?

[104-023] Or need’st thou absence to renew thy love?

[104-024] Yet, if thou goest by land, though grief possess

[104-025] My soul, ev’n then my fears will be the less.

[104-026] But, ah! be warn’d to shun the watery way,

[104-027] The face is frightful of the stormy sea;

[104-028] For late I saw adrift disjointed planks,

[104-029] And empty tombs erected on the banks.

[104-030] Nor let false hopes to trust betray thy mind,

[104-031] Because my sire in caves constrains the wind,

[104-032] Can with a breath their clam’rous rage appease,

[104-033] They fear his whistle, and forsake the seas:

[104-034] Not so: for, once indulged, they sweep the main,

[104-035] Deaf to the call, or, hearing, hear in vain;

[104-036] But bent on mischief bear the waves before,

[104-037] And not content with seas, insult the shore,

[104-038] When ocean, air, and earth at once engage,

[104-039] And rooted forests fly before their rage:

[104-040] At once the clashing clouds to battle move,

[104-041] And lightnings rim across the fields above:

[104-042] I know them well, and mark’d their rude comport,

[104-043] While yet a child within my father’s court:

[104-044] In times of tempest they command alone:

[104-045] And he but sits precarious on the throne:

[104-046] The more I know, the more my fears augment;

[104-047] And fears are oft prophetic of the event;

[104-048] But if not fears, or reasons will prevail,

[104-049] If fate has fix’d thee obstinate to sail,

[104-050] Go not without thy wife, but let me bear

[104-051] My part of danger with an equal share,

[104-052] And present, what I suffer only fear;

[104-053] Then o’er the bounding billows shall we fly,

[104-054] Secure to live together, or to die.”

[104-055] These reasons moved her starlike husband’s heart,

[104-056] But still he held his purpose to depart;

[104-057] For as he loved her equal to his life,

[104-058] He would not to the seas expose his wife;

[104-059] Nor could be wrought his voyage to refrain,

[104-060] But sought by arguments to soothe her pain;

[104-061] Nor these avail’d; at length he lights on one,

[104-062] With which so difficult a case he won:

[104-063] “My love, so short an absence cease to fear,

[104-064] For by my father’s holy flame I swear,

[104-065] Before two moons their orb with light adorn,

[104-066] If Heaven allow me life, I will return.”

[104-067] This promise of so short a stay prevails;

[104-068] He soon equips the ships, supplies the sails,

[104-069] And gives the word to launch; she trembling views

[104-070] This pomp of death, and parting tears renews;

[104-071] Last with a kiss, she took a long farewell,

[104-072] Sigh’d with a sad presage, and swooning fell:

[104-073] While Ceyx seeks delays, the lusty crew,

[104-074] Raised on their banks, their oars in order drew

[104-075] To their broad breasts, the ship with fury flew.

[104-076] The queen recover’d, rears her humid eyes,

[104-077] And first her husband on the poop espies,

[104-078] Shaking his hand at distance on the main;

[104-079] She took the sign, and shook her hand again:

[104-080] Still as the ground recedes, contracts her view

[104-081] With sharpen’d sight, till she no longer knew

[104-082] The much-loved face; that comfort lost supplies

[104-083] With less, and with the galley feeds her eyes;

[104-084] The galley borne from view by rising gales,

[104-085] She follow’d with her sight the flying sails;

[104-086] When ev’n the flying sails were seen no more,

[104-087] Forsaken of all sight she left the shore.

[104-088] Then on her bridal bed her body throws

[104-089] And sought in sleep her wearied eyes to close;

[104-090] Her husband’s pillow, and the widow’d part

[104-091] Which once he press’d, renew’d the former smart.

[104-092] And now a breeze from shore began to blow,

[104-093] The sailors ship their oars, and cease to row,

[104-094] Then hoist their yards a-trip, and all their sails

[104-095] Let fall, to court the wind, and catch the gales.

[104-096] By this the vessel half her course had run,

[104-097] And as much rested till the rising sun;

[104-098] Both shores were lost to sight, when at the close

[104-099] Of day a stiffer gale at east arose:

[104-100] The sea grew white, the rolling waves from far,

[104-101] Like heralds, first denounce the watery war.

[104-102] This seen, the master soon began to cry:

[104-103] “Strike, strike the topsail, let the mainsheet fly,

[104-104] And furl your sails:” the winds repel the sound,

[104-105] And in the speaker’s mouth the speech is drown’d.

[104-106] Yet of their own accord, as danger taught

[104-107] Each in his way, officiously they wrought;

[104-108] Some stow their oars, or stop the leaky sides;

[104-109] Another bolder, yet the yard bestrides,

[104-110] And folds the sails; a fourth with labour laves

[104-111] The intruding seas, and waves ejects on waves.

[104-112] In this confusion, while their work they ply,

[104-113] The winds augment the winter of the sky,

[104-114] And wage intestine wars, the suffering seas

[104-115] Are toss’d, and mingled, as their tyrants please.

[104-116] The master would command, but, in despair

[104-117] Of safety, stands amazed with stupid care;

[104-118] Nor what to bid, or what forbid he knows,

[104-119] The ungovern’d tempest to such fury grows:

[104-120] Vain is his force, and vainer is his skill,

[104-121] With such a concourse comes the flood of ill;

[104-122] The cries of men are mix’d with rattling shrouds,

[104-123] Seas dash on seas, and clouds encounter clouds;

[104-124] At once from east to west, from pole to pole,

[104-125] The forky lightnings flash, the roaring thunders roll.

[104-126] Now waves on waves ascending scale the skies,

[104-127] And in the fires above the water fries;

[104-128] When yellow sands are sifted from below,

[104-129] The glittering billows give a golden show;

[104-130] And when the fouler bottom spews the black,

[104-131] The Stygian die the tainted waters take;

[104-132] Then frothy white appear the flatted seas,

[104-133] And change their colour, changing their disease.

[104-134] Like various fits the Trachin vessel finds;

[104-135] And now sublime, she rides upon the winds;

[104-136] As from a lofty summit looks from high,

[104-137] And from the clouds beholds the nether sky;

[104-138] Now from the depth of hell they lift their sight,

[104-139] And at a distance see superior light;

[104-140] The lashing billows make a loud report,

[104-141] And beat her sides, as battering rams a fort;

[104-142] Or as a lion bounding in his way,

[104-143] With force augmented, bears against his prey,

[104-144] Sidelong to seize, or unappall’d with fear,

[104-145] Springs on the toils, and rushes on the spear;

[104-146] So seas impell’d by winds, with added power

[104-147] Assault the sides, and o’er the hatches tower.

[104-148] The planks (their pitchy covering wash’d away)

[104-149] Now yield, and now a yawning breach display;

[104-150] The roaring waters with a hostile tide

[104-151] Rush through the ruins of her gaping side

[104-152] Meantime in sheets of rain the sky descends,

[104-153] And ocean swell’d with waters upward tends;

[104-154] One rising, falling one, the heavens and sea

[104-155] Meet at their confines, in the middle way:

[104-156] The sails are drunk with showers, and drop with rain;

[104-157] Sweet waters mingle with the briny main;

[104-158] No star appears to lend his friendly light;

[104-159] Darkness and tempest make a double night;

[104-160] But flashing fires disclose the deep by turns,

[104-161] And while the lightnings blaze, the water burns.

[104-162] Now all the waves their scatter’d force unite,

[104-163] And, as a soldier foremost in the fight,

[104-164] Make way for others, and a host alone

[104-165] Still presses on, and urging gains the town;

[104-166] So, while the invading billows come abreast,

[104-167] The hero tenth advanced before the rest,

[104-168] Sweeps all before him with impetuous sway,

[104-169] And from the walls descends upon the prey;

[104-170] Part following enter, part remain without,

[104-171] With envy hear their fellows’ conquering shout,

[104-172] And mount on others’ backs, in hopes to share

[104-173] The city, thus become the seat of war.

[104-174] A universal cry resounds aloud,

[104-175] The sailors run in heaps, a helpless crowd;

[104-176] Art fails, and courage fails, no succour near;

[104-177] As many waves, as many deaths appear:

[104-178] One weeps, and yet despairs of late relief;

[104-179] One cannot weep, his fears congeal his grief,

[104-180] But, stupid, with dry eyes expects his fate;

[104-181] One with loud shrieks laments his lost estate,

[104-182] And calls those happy, whom their funerals wait:

[104-183] This wretch with prayers and vows the gods implores,

[104-184] And ev’n the skies he cannot see, adores:

[104-185] That other, on his friends his thoughts bestows,

[104-186] His careful father, and his faithful spouse;

[104-187] The covetous worldling, in his anxious mind,

[104-188] Thinks only on the wealth he left behind.

[104-189] All Ceyx his Alcyone employs,

[104-190] For her he grieves, yet in her absence joys;

[104-191] His wife he wishes, and would still be near,

[104-192] Not her with him, but wishes him with her:

[104-193] Now with last looks he seeks his native shore,

[104-194] Which fate has destined him to see no more;

[104-195] He sought, but, in the dark tempestuous night,

[104-196] He knew not whither to direct his sight;

[104-197] So whirl the seas, such darkness blinds the sky,

[104-198] That the black night receives a deeper dye.

[104-199] The giddy ship ran round, the tempest tore

[104-200] Her mast, and overboard the rudder bore;

[104-201] One billow mounts, and with a scornful brow,

[104-202] Proud of her conquest gain’d, insults the waves below;

[104-203] Nor lighter falls, than if some giant tore

[104-204] Pindus and Athos with the freight they bore,

[104-205] And toss’d on seas, press’d with the ponderous blow,

[104-206] Down sinks the ship within the abyss below;

[104-207] Down with the vessel sink into the main

[104-208] The many, never more to rise again.

[104-209] Some few on scatter’d planks, with fruitless care,

[104-210] Lay hold, and swim, but while they swim despair.

[104-211] Ev’n he who late a sceptre did command,

[104-212] Now grasps a floating fragment in his hand;

[104-213] And while he struggles on the stormy main,

[104-214] Invokes his father, and his wife’s, in vain.

[104-215] But yet his consort is his greatest care,

[104-216] Alcyone he names amid his prayer;

[104-217] Names as a charm against the waves and wind;

[104-218] Most in his mouth, and ever in his mind.

[104-219] Tired with his toil, all hopes of safety pass’d,

[104-220] From pray’rs to wishes he descends at last,

[104-221] That his dead body, wafted to the sands,

[104-222] Might have its burial from her friendly hands.

[104-223] As oft as he can catch a gulp of air,

[104-224] And peep above the seas, he names the fair;

[104-225] And ev’n when plunged beneath, on her he raves,

[104-226] Murmuring Alcyone below the waves:

[104-227] At last a falling billow stops, his breath,

[104-228] Breaks o’er his head, and whelms him underneath.

[104-229] Bright Lucifer unlike himself appears

[104-230] That night, his heavenly form obscured with tears,

[104-231] And since he was forbid to leave the skies,

[104-232] He muffled with a cloud his mournful eyes.

[104-233] Meantime Alcyone (his fate unknown)

[104-234] Computes how many nights he had been gone:

[104-235] Observes the waning moon with hourly view,

[104-236] Numbers her age, and wishes for a new;

[104-237] Against the promised time provides with care,

[104-238] And hastens in the woof the robes he was to wear;

[104-239] And for herself employs another loom,

[104-240] New dress’d to meet her lord returning home,

[104-241] Flattering her heart with joys that never were to come:

[104-242] She fumed the temples with an odorous flame,

[104-243] And oft before the sacred altars came,

[104-244] To pray for him, who was an empty name.

[104-245] All powers implored, but far above the rest

[104-246] To Juno she her pious vows address’d,

[104-247] Her much-loved lord from perils to protect,

[104-248] And safe o’er seas his voyage to direct:

[104-249] Then pray’d, that she might still possess his heart,

[104-250] And no pretending rival share a part.

[104-251] This last petition heard of all her prayer,

[104-252] The rest, dispersed by winds, were lost in air.

[104-253] But she, the goddess of the nuptial bed,

[104-254] Tired with her vain devotions for the dead,

[104-255] Resolved the tainted hand should be repell’d,

[104-256] Which incense offer’d, and her altar held.

[104-257] Then Iris thus bespoke: “Thou faithful maid,

[104-258] By whom thy queen’s commands are well convey’d,

[104-259] Haste to the house of sleep, and bid the god,

[104-260] Who rules the night by visions with a nod,

[104-261] Prepare a dream, in figure and in form

[104-262] Resembling him who perish’d in the storm:

[104-263] This form before Alcyone present,

[104-264] To make her certain of the sad event.”

[104-265] Indued with robes of various hue, she flies,

[104-266] And flying draws an arch, (a segment of the skies,)

[104-267] Then leaves her bending bow, and from the steep

[104-268] Descends, to search the silent house of sleep.

[104-269] Near the Cimmerians, in his dark abode,

[104-270] Deep in a cavern dwells the drowsy god,

[104-271] Whose gloomy mansion nor the rising sun,

[104-272] Nor setting, visits, nor the lightsome noon:

[104-273] But lazy vapours round the region fly,

[104-274] Perpetual twilight, and a doubtful sky;

[104-275] No crowing cock does there his wings display,

[104-276] Nor with his horny bill provoke the day,

[104-277] Nor watchful dogs, nor the more wakeful geese,

[104-278] Disturb with nightly noise the sacred peace,

[104-279] Nor beast of nature, nor the tame are nigh,

[104-280] Nor trees with tempests rock’d, nor human cry,

[104-281] But safe repose, without an air of breath,

[104-282] Dwells here, and a dumb quiet next to death.

[104-283] An arm of Lethe, with a gentle flow

[104-284] Arising upward from the rock below,

[104-285] The palace moats, and o’er the pebbles creeps,

[104-286] And with soft murmurs calls the coming sleeps.

[104-287] Around its entry nodding poppies grow,

[104-288] And all cool simples that sweet rest bestow;

[104-289] Night from the plants their sleepy virtue drains,

[104-290] And, passing, sheds it on the silent plains.

[104-291] No door there was, the unguarded house to keep,

[104-292] On creaking hinges turn’d, to break his sleep.

[104-293] But in the gloomy court was raised a bed,

[104-294] Stuff’d with black plumes, and on an ebon ’sted;

[104-295] Black was the covering too, where lay the god,

[104-296] And slept supine, his limbs display’d abroad;

[104-297] [[About]] his head fantastic visions fly,

[104-298] Which various images of things supply,

[104-299] And mock their forms, the leaves on trees not more,

[104-300] Nor bearded ears in fields, nor sands upon the shore.

[104-301] The virgin entering bright, indulged the day

[104-302] To the brown cave, and brush’d the dreams away,

[104-303] The god, disturb’d with this new glare of light,

[104-304] Cast sudden on his face, unseal’d his sight,

[104-305] And raised his tardy head, which sunk again,

[104-306] And, sinking, on his bosom knock’d his chin;

[104-307] At length shook off himself, and ask’d the dame

[104-308] (And asking yawn’d) for what intent she came.

[104-309] To whom the goddess thus: “Oh sacred rest,

[104-310] Sweet pleasing sleep, of all the powers the best!

[104-311] Oh peace of mind! repairer of decay!

[104-312] Whose balms renew the limbs to labours of the day,

[104-313] Care shuns thy soft approach, and sullen flies away!

[104-314] Adorn a dream, expressing human form,

[104-315] The shape of him who suffer’d in the storm,

[104-316] And send it flitting to the Trachin court,

[104-317] The wreck of wretched Ceyx to report;

[104-318] Before his queen bid the pale spectre stand,

[104-319] Who begs a vain relief at Juno’s hand.”

[104-320] She said, and scarce awake her eyes could keep,

[104-321] Unable to support the fumes of sleep,

[104-322] But fled, returning by the way she went,

[104-323] And swerved along her bow with swift ascent.

[104-324] The god, uneasy till he slept again,

[104-325] Resolved at once to rid himself of pain;

[104-326] And, though against his custom, call’d aloud,

[104-327] Exciting Morpheus from the sleepy crowd;

[104-328] Morpheus, of all his numerous train, express’d

[104-329] The shape of man, and imitated best;

[104-330] The walk, the words, the gesture, could supply,

[104-331] The habit mimic, and the mien bely;

[104-332] Plays well, but all his action is confined,

[104-333] Extending not beyond our humankind.

[104-334] Another, birds, and beasts, and dragons apes,

[104-335] And dreadful images, and monster shapes;

[104-336] This demon, Icelos, in heaven’s high hall,

[104-337] The gods have named, but men Phobetor call.

[104-338] A third is Phantasus, whose actions roll

[104-339] On meaner thoughts, and things devoid of soul;

[104-340] Earth, fruits, and flowers, he represents in dreams,

[104-341] And solid rocks unmoved, and running streams.

[104-342] These three to kings and chiefs their scenes display,

[104-343] The rest before the ignoble commons play.

[104-344] Of these the chosen Morpheus is despatch’d,

[104-345] Which done, the lazy monarch, overwatch’d,

[104-346] Down from his propping elbow drops his head,

[104-347] Dissolved in sleep, and shrinks within his bed.

[104-348] Darkling the demon glides, for flight prepared,

[104-349] So soft, that scarce his fanning wings are heard.

[104-350] To Trachin, swift as thought, the flitting shade

[104-351] Through air his momentary journey made;

[104-352] Then lays aside the steerage of his wings,

[104-353] Forsakes his proper form, assumes the king’s;

[104-354] And, pale as death, despoil’d of his array,

[104-355] Into the queen’s apartment takes his way,

[104-356] And stands before the bed at dawn of day:

[104-357] Unmoved his eyes, and wet his beard appears,

[104-358] And shedding vain, but seeming real, tears,

[104-359] The briny waters dropping from his hairs;

[104-360] Then, staring on her with a ghastly look,

[104-361] And hollow voice, he thus the queen bespoke:

[104-362] “Know’st thou not me? Not yet, unhappy wife?

[104-363] Or are my features perish’d with my life?

[104-364] Look once again, and for thy husband lost,

[104-365] Lo! all that’s left of him, thy husband’s ghost!

[104-366] Thy vows for my return were all in vain,

[104-367] The stormy south o’ertook us in the main,

[104-368] And never shalt thou see thy living lord again.

[104-369] Bear witness Heaven, I call’d on thee in death,

[104-370] And, while I call’d, a billow stopp’d my breath.

[104-371] Think not that flying fame reports my fate,

[104-372] I present, I appear, and my own wreck relate.

[104-373] Rise, wretched widow, rise, nor undeplored

[104-374] Permit my soul to pass the Stygian ford;

[104-375] But rise, prepared in black, to mourn thy perish’d lord.”

[104-376] Thus said the player god, and adding art

[104-377] Of voice and gesture, so perform’d his part,

[104-378] She thought (so like her love the shade appears)

[104-379] That Ceyx spoke the words, and Ceyx shed the tears.

[104-380] She groan’d, her inward soul with grief oppress’d,

[104-381] She sigh’d, she wept, and, sleeping, beat her breast;

[104-382] Then stretch’d her arms to embrace his body bare;

[104-383] Her clasping arms enclose but empty air;

[104-384] At this, not yet awake, she cried, “Oh stay!

[104-385] One is our fate, and common is our way!”

[104-386] So dreadful was the dream, so loud she spoke,

[104-387] That, starting sudden up, the slumber broke,

[104-388] Then cast her eyes around, in hope to view

[104-389] Her vanish’d lord, and find the vision true;

[104-390] For now the maids, who waited her commands,

[104-391] Ran in with lighted tapers in their hands.

[104-392] Tired with the search, not finding what she seeks,

[104-393] With cruel blows she pounds her blubber’d cheeks;

[104-394] Then from her beaten breast the linen tear,

[104-395] And cut the golden caul that bound her hair.

[104-396] Her nurse demands the cause: with louder cries

[104-397] She prosecutes her griefs, and thus replies:

[104-398] “No more Alcyone; she suffered death

[104-399] With her lov’d lord, when Ceyx lost his breath:

[104-400] No flattery, no false comfort, give me none,

[104-401] My shipwreck’d Ceyx is for ever gone.

[104-402] I saw, I saw him manifest in view,

[104-403] His voice, his figure, and his gestures knew;

[104-404] His lustre lost, and every living grace,

[104-405] Yet I retain’d the features of his face;

[104-406] Though with pale cheeks, wet beard, and dropping hair,

[104-407] None but my Ceyx could appear so fair;

[104-408] I would have strain’d him with a strict embrace,

[104-409] But through my arms he slipp’d, and vanish’d from the place.

[104-410] There, ev’n just there, he stood:” and, as she spoke,

[104-411] Where last the spectre was she cast her look;

[104-412] Fain would she hope, and gazed upon the ground,

[104-413] If any printed footsteps might be found.

[104-414] Then sigh’d, and said, “This I too well foreknew,

[104-415] And my prophetic fears presaged too true:

[104-416] ’Twas what I begg’d, when with a bleeding heart

[104-417] I took my leave, and suffer’d thee to part;

[104-418] Or I to go along, or thou to stay,

[104-419] Never, ah! never, to divide our way!

[104-420] Happier for me, that all our hours assign’d

[104-421] Together we had lived, ev’n not in death disjoin’d!

[104-422] So had my Ceyx still been living here,

[104-423] Or with my Ceyx I had perish’d there;

[104-424] Now I die absent, in the vast profound,

[104-425] And me, without myself, the seas have drown’d.

[104-426] The storms were not so cruel: should I strive

[104-427] To lengthen life, and such a grief survive;

[104-428] But neither will I strive, nor wretched thee

[104-429] In death forsake, but keep thee company:

[104-430] If not one common sepulchre contains

[104-431] Our bodies, or one urn our last remains,

[104-432] Yet Ceyx and Alcyone shall join,

[104-433] Their names remember’d in one common line.”

[104-434] No further voice her mighty grief affords,

[104-435] For sighs come rushing in between her words

[104-436] And stopp’d her tongue; but what her tongue denied,

[104-437] Soft tears, and groans, and dumb complaints supplied.

[104-438] ’Twas morning: to the port she takes her way,

[104-439] And stands upon the margin of the sea;

[104-440] That place, that very spot of ground, she sought,

[104-448] And somewhat floating from afar descries:

[104-449] It seem’d a corpse adrift to distant sight,

[104-451] It wafted nearer yet, and then she knew

[104-452] That what before she but surmised was true:

[104-453] A corpse it was, but whose it was unknown;

[104-454] Yet moved, howe’er, she made the case her own,

[104-455] Took the bad omen of a shipwreck’d man,

[104-456] As for a stranger wept, and thus began:

[104-457] “Poor wretch, on stormy seas to lose thy life:

[104-458] Unhappy thou, but more thy widow’d wife!”

[104-459] At this she paused, for now the flowing tide

[104-460] Had brought the body nearer to the side.

[104-461] The more she looks, the more her fears increase

[104-462] At nearer sight, and she’s herself the less.

[104-463] Now driven ashore, and at her feet it lies,

[104-464] She knows too much in knowing whom she sees,

[104-465] Her husband’s corpse; at this she loudly shrieks,

[104-466] “ ’Tis he! ’tis he!” she cries, and tears her cheeks,

[104-467] Her hair, and vest; and stooping to the sands,

[104-468] About his neck she cast her trembling hands.

[104-469] “And is it thus, oh dearer than my life!

[104-470] Thus, thus return’st thou to thy longing wife?”

[104-471] She said, and to the neighbouring mole she strode:

[104-472] (Raised there to break the incursions of the flood:)

[104-473] Headlong from hence to plunge herself she springs,

[104-474] But shoots along, supported on her wings;

[104-475] A bird new made, about the banks she plies,

[104-476] Not far from shore, and short excursions tries;

[104-477] Nor seeks in air her humble flight to raise,

[104-478] Content to skim the surface of the seas.

[104-480] And imitates a lamentable voice.

[104-483] At all her stretch, her little wings she spread,
