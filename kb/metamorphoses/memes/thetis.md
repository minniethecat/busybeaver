# Thetis

Thetis is a Nereid (sea-nymph) in Ovid's *Metamorphoses*, the daughter of [[nereus|Nereus]] and Doris. She is best known for her resistance to marrying the mortal Peleus — a courtship in which she deployed her shape-shifting abilities to assume various terrifying forms. The sea-god Proteus advised Peleus to bind her while she slept, and after her transformations proved futile, she yielded to the marriage. Thetis later used her divine influence to obtain boons for her mortal husband, including turning a ravaging wolf into marble. She is also the mother of [[achilles|Achilles]] and appears in the post-Homeric tradition as a goddess who knew the fates and attempted to protect her son from his destiny (101-001).

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Thetis appears in three episodes across Book XI and Book XIII. Her primary story concerns her courtship by Peleus, which Ovid presents as a comedy of pursuit and evasion — "A bridled dolphin oft fair Thetis bore" (101-023) — ending in a reluctant but fated marriage. She then acts as a divine intercessor, successfully petitioning the gods to turn a wolf to marble. In the later books, she appears as the grieving mother of Achilles, using her knowledge of the fates to try to shield her son.

### Key Episodes

- **The Pursuit of Peleus**: Thetis assumes various shapes (bird, tree, tigress, etc.) to evade Peleus's advances; Proteus advises Peleus to bind her while she sleeps, and after exhausting all her transformations, she consents to marriage (101-001)
- **The Wolf of Trachis**: A great wolf devastates the plains of Trachinia, and "Thetis for her spouse the boon obtain'd" — the wolf is transformed into marble by her intercession (103-067)
- **Knowledge of Fate**: "Thetis, who knew the fates, applied her care" to protect Achilles from the Trojan War, disguising him as a girl on Scyros (115-262)

## Transformation

Thetis is both a shape-shifter herself and an agent of transformation for others.

- **Form (self)**: Thetis freely shifts between forms — bird, tree, tigress, dolphin, water, fire — to avoid capture
- **Form (other)**: Wolf → Marble (at Thetis's intercession)
- **Agent**: Self (shape-shifting ability); gods (granting her petition)
- **Cause**: Escape from unwanted marriage (self); protection of her husband's kingdom (wolf)
- **Source**: (101-001), (103-001, 103-067)
