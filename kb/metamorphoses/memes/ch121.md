[107-001] The goddess Fame reports through the whole world the invasion of [[troy|Troy]]—Protesilaus, who first lands on the hostile shore, is slain by Hector.

[107-002] Full in the midst of this created space,

[107-003] Between heaven, earth, and skies, there stands a place,

[107-004] Confining on all three, with triple bound,

[107-005] Whence all things, though remote, are view’d around,

[107-006] And thither bring their undulating sound.

[107-007] The palace of loud Fame, her seat of power,

[107-008] Placed on the summit of a lofty tower;

[107-009] A thousand winding entries long and wide

[107-010] Receive of fresh reports a flowing tide.

[107-011] A thousand crannies in the walls are made,

[107-012] Nor gate, nor bars, exclude the busy trade:

[107-013] ’Tis built of brass, the better to diffuse

[107-014] The spreading sounds, and multiply the news;

[107-015] Where echoes in repeated echoes play;

[107-016] A mart for ever full, and open night and day.

[107-017] Nor silence is within, nor voice express,

[107-018] But a deaf noise of sounds, that never cease.

[107-019] Confused, and chiding, like the hollow roar

[107-020] Of tides receding from the insulted shore;

[107-021] Or like the broken thunder heard from far,

[107-022] When [[jupiter|Jove]] to distance drives the rolling war.

[107-023] The courts are fill’d with a tumultuous din

[107-024] Of crowds, or issuing forth, or entering in;

[107-025] A thoroughfare of news, where some devise

[107-026] Things never heard, some mingle truth with lies;

[107-027] The troubled air with empty sounds they beat,

[107-028] Intent to hear, and eager to repeat;

[107-029] Error sits brooding there, with added train

[107-030] Of vain credulity, and joys as vain:

[107-031] Suspicion, with sedition join’d, are near,

[107-032] And rumours raised, and murmurs mix’d, and panic fear.

[107-033] Fame sits aloft, and sees the subject ground,

[107-034] And seas about, and skies above; inquiring all around.

[107-035] The goddess gives the alarm, and soon is known

[107-036] The Grecian fleet descending on the town.

[107-037] Fix’d on defence, the Trojans are not slow

[107-038] To guard their shore from an expected foe.

[107-039] They meet in fight. By Hector’s fatal hand

[107-040] Protesilaus falls, and bites the strand;

[107-041] Which with expense of blood the Grecians won,

[107-042] And proved the strength unknown of Priam’s son:

[107-043] And to their cost the Trojan leaders felt

[107-044] The Grecian heroes, and what deaths they dealt.
