# The Slaughter of the Niobids

Niobe's boast — that she, with fourteen children, surpasses Latona, who has only two — provokes the most systematically brutal punishment in the *Metamorphoses*. Apollo kills the seven sons, one by one, with his arrows. Diana, undeterred by the carnage, kills the seven daughters. Niobe, sitting among fourteen corpses, hardens into a weeping stone.

| Latona | The goddess whose honor is defended |

## Setting

The plain before the walls of Thebes, where the sons are exercising on horseback, and the palace, where the daughters mourn over their brothers' bodies.

## Narrative

Niobe demands that the Thebans worship her rather than Latona. The goddess, enraged, dispatches Apollo and Diana. Apollo's arrows strike each son: on horseback, wrestling, running — one by one they fall. Niobe's husband Amphion kills himself in grief. The daughters, gathered around their brothers' bodies, are killed by Diana — even the youngest, who hides in her mother's robes. Niobe, petrified by grief, becomes a stone on Mount Sipylus — but even as stone, she weeps.
