[057-001] The goddess Latona, while concealing herself from the rage of Juno, arrives in Lycia, where she is insulted and ridiculed hy peasants, of whom she begs a draught of water, while they are weeding a marsh—Their refusal and insolence provoke her, and she implores [[jupiter|Jupiter]] to punish their barbarity—The god consents, and transforms them into frogs.

[057-002] Then all, reclaim’d by this example, show’d

[057-003] A due regard for each peculiar god:

[057-004] Both men and women their devoirs express’d,

[057-005] And great Latona’s awful power confess’d.

[057-006] Then, tracing instances of older time,

[057-007] To suit the nature of the present crime,

[057-008] Thus one begins his tale:—“Where Lycia yields

[057-009] A golden harvest from its fertile fields,

[057-010] Some churlish peasants, in the days of yore,

[057-011] Provoked the goddess to exert her power.

[057-012] The thing, indeed, the meanness of the place

[057-013] Has made obscure, surprising as it was;

[057-014] But I myself once happen’d to behold

[057-015] This famous lake, of which the story’s told.

[057-016] My father, then, worn out by length of days,

[057-017] Nor able to sustain the tedious ways,

[057-018] Me with a guide had sent the plains to roam,

[057-019] And drive his well-fed straggling heifers home.

[057-020] Here, as we saunter’d through the verdant meads,

[057-021] We spied a lake o’ergrown with trembling reeds,

[057-022] Whose wavy tops an op’ning scene disclose,

[057-023] From which an antique smoky altar rose.

[057-024] I, as my superstitious guide had done,

[057-025] Stopp’d short, and bless’d myself, and then went on

[057-026] Yet I inquired to whom the altar stood,

[057-027] Faunus, the Naiads, or some native god?

[057-028] ‘No sylvan deity,’ my friend replies,

[057-029] ‘Enshrined within this hallow’d altar lies:

[057-030] For this, O youth, to that famed goddess stands,

[057-031] Whom, at the imperial Juno’s rough commands,

[057-032] Of ev’ry quarter of the earth bereaved,

[057-033] Delos, the floating isle, at length received;

[057-034] Who there, in spite of enemies, brought forth,

[057-035] Beneath an olive shade, her great twin birth.

[057-036] “ ‘Hence too she fled the furious stepdame’s power

[057-037] And in her arms a double godhead bore;

[057-038] And now the borders of fair Lycia gain’d,

[057-039] Just when the summer solstice parch’d the land.

[057-040] With thirst the goddess languishing, no more

[057-041] Her emptied breast would yield its milky store,

[057-042] When, from below, the smiling valley show’d

[057-043] A silver lake that in its bottom flow’d:

[057-044] A sort of clowns were reaping, near the bank,

[057-045] The bending osier, and the bulrush dank,

[057-046] The cress, and water-lily, fragrant weed,

[057-047] Whose juicy stalk the liquid fountains feed:

[057-048] The goddess came, and kneeling on the brink,

[057-049] Stoop’d at the fresh repast, prepared to drink.

[057-050] Then thus, being hinder’d by the rabble race,

[057-051] In accents mild, expostulates the case:

[057-052] “Water I only ask, and sure ’tis hard

[057-053] From Nature’s common rights to be debarr’d:

[057-054] This, as the genial sun, and vital air,

[057-055] Should flow alike to ev’ry creature’s share.

[057-056] Yet still I ask, and as a favour crave,

[057-057] That which a public bounty nature gave:

[057-058] Nor do I seek my weary limbs to drench,

[057-059] Only, with one cool draught, my thirst I’d quench.

[057-060] Now from my throat the usual moisture dries,

[057-061] And ev’n my voice in broken accents dies:

[057-062] One draught as dear as life I should esteem,

[057-063] And water, now I thirst, would nectar seem:

[057-064] O! let my little babes your pity move,

[057-065] And melt your hearts to charitable love;

[057-066] They (as by chance they did) extend to you

[057-067] Their little hands, and my request pursue.” ’

[057-068] “Whom would these soft persuasions not subdue,

[057-069] Though the most rustic and unmanner’d crew?

[057-070] Yet they the goddess’s request refuse,

[057-071] And with rude words reproachfully abuse.

[057-072] Nay, more, with spiteful feet the villains trode

[057-073] O’er the soft bottom of the marshy flood,

[057-074] And blacken’d all the lake with clouds of rising mud.

[057-075] “Her thirst, by indignation, was suppress’d;

[057-076] Bent on revenge, the goddess stood confess’d.

[057-077] Her suppliant hands uplifting to the skies,

[057-078] For a redress to heaven she now applies:

[057-079] ‘And may you live,’ she passionately cried,

[057-080] ‘[[fate|Doom]]’d in that pool for ever to abide.’

[057-081] “The goddess has her wish: for now they choose

[057-082] To plunge and dive among the watery ooze;

[057-083] Sometimes they show their head above the brim,

[057-084] And on the glassy surface spread to swim;

[057-085] Often upon the bank their station take,

[057-086] Then spring and leap into the cooly lake.

[057-087] Still, void of shame, they lead a clam’rous life.

[057-088] And, croaking, still scold on in endless strife;

[057-089] Compell’d to live beneath the liquid stream,

[057-090] Where still they quarrel, and attempt to scream.

[057-091] Now, from their bloated throat, their voice puts on

[057-092] Imperfect murmurs in a hoarser tone;

[057-093] Their noisy jaws, with bawling now grown wide,

[057-094] An ugly sight! extend on either side;

[057-095] Their motley back, streak’d with a list of green,

[057-096] Join’d to their head, without a neck, is seen;

[057-097] And, with a belly broad and white, they look

[057-098] Mere frogs, and still frequent the muddy brook.”
