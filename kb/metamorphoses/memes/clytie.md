# Clytie

Clytie is a figure in Ovid's *Metamorphoses* whose story illustrates the destructive power of jealousy and unrequited love. A nymph who tenderly loved [[apollo|Apollo]], the sun-god, she betrayed his secret affair with Leucothoe to Leucothoe's father out of jealousy — an act that led to Leucothoe being buried alive. Scorned by Apollo for her betrayal, Clytie wasted away, sitting on the bare ground for nine days without food or water, gazing at the sun as it crossed the sky. The gods transformed her into a heliotrope (or sunflower), a flower that perpetually turns its head to follow the sun's course across the heavens (030-001).

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Clytie appears in Book IV of the *Metamorphoses*, where her story is intertwined with that of Leucothoe and Apollo. She serves as the jealous antagonist whose betrayal sets the tragedy in motion, and then becomes the subject of her own transformation — one of the poem's most poignant images of love's enduring gaze (029-001).

### Key Episodes

- **Love for Apollo and Jealousy**: Clytie tenderly loves Apollo, but when the god shifts his attention to Leucothoe, Clytie's jealousy drives her to reveal the affair to Leucothoe's father, Orchamus, who orders his daughter buried alive (029-001)
- **Scorned by Apollo**: Apollo, unable to save Leucothoe (whom he transforms into a frankincense tree), despises Clytie for her betrayal: "This Clytie knew, and knew she was undone" (029-087)
- **The Nine-Day Vigil**: Deserted by Apollo, Clytie sits on the bare ground for nine days without food or water, her face constantly turned toward the sun as it moves across the sky: "Fond Clytie, scorn'd, yet loved and sought thy bed" (029-047)
- **Transformation into a Flower**: The gods finally transform her into a heliotrope (sunflower/turnsole), which still turns its head toward the sun in token of her love (030-001)

## Transformation

Clytie's metamorphosis is a consequence of unrequited love and grief, making her one of the poem's most memorable symbols of devotion.

- **Form**: Nymph → Heliotrope (sunflower / turnsole)
- **Agent**: The gods (pitying her suffering)
- **Cause**: Unrequited love for Apollo and grief after being scorned
- **Source**: (030-001)
