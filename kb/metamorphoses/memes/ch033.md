[027-001] Undeterred by the punishment of Pentheus, Alcithoe and her sisters dare to ridicule the orgies of [[bacchus|Bacchus]], and to employ themselves in the labours of the loom during the festival of that god.

[027-002] Yet still Alcithoe perverse remains,

[027-003] And Bacchus still and all his rites disdains.

[027-004] Too rash and madly bold, she bids him prove

[027-005] Himself a god, nor owns the son of [[jupiter|Jove]]:

[027-006] Her sisters too unanimous agree,

[027-007] Faithful associates in impiety.

[027-008] Be this a solemn feast, the priest had said;

[027-009] Be, with each mistress, unemploy’d each maid.

[027-010] With skins of beasts your tender limbs enclose,

[027-011] And with an ivy crown adorn your brows;

[027-012] The leafy thyrsus high in triumph bear,

[027-013] And give your locks to wanton in the air.

[027-014] These rites profaned, the holy seer foreshow’d

[027-015] A mourning people, and a vengeful god.

[027-016] Matrons and pious wives obedience show,

[027-017] Distaffs, and wool half spun, away they throw:

[027-018] Then incense burn, and, Bacchus, thee adore:

[027-019] Or lovest thou Neseus, or Lyaeus, more?

[027-020] O, doubly got! O, doubly born! they sung,

[027-021] Thou mighty Bromius, hail! from lightning sprung.

[027-022] Hail! Thyon, Eleleus, each name is thine:

[027-023] Or, listen parent of the genial vine!

[027-024] Iacchus! Evan! loudly they repeat,

[027-025] And not one Grecian attribute forget,

[027-026] Which to thy praise, great deity, belong,

[027-027] Styled, justly, Liber in the Roman song.

[027-028] Eternity of youth is thine! enjoy

[027-029] Years roll’d on years, yet still a blooming boy.

[027-030] In heaven thou shinest with a superior grace;

[027-031] Conceal thy horns, and ’tis a virgin’s face.

[027-032] Thou taught’st the tawny Indian to obey,

[027-033] And Ganges, smoothly flowing, own’d thy sway.

[027-034] Lycurgus, Pentheus, equally profane,

[027-035] By thy just vengeance equally were slain.

[027-036] By thee the Tuscans, who conspired to keep

[027-037] Thee captive, plunged and cut with fins the deep.

[027-038] With painted reins, all glittering from afar,

[027-039] The spotted Lynxes proudly draw thy car;

[027-040] Around the Bacchae and the Satyrs throng,

[027-041] Behind, Silenus, drunk, lags slow along;

[027-042] On his dull ass he nods from side to side,

[027-043] Forbears to fall, yet half forgets to ride.

[027-044] Still at thy near approach applauses loud

[027-045] Are heard, with yellings of the female crowd;

[027-046] Timbrels, and boxen pipes, with mingled cries,

[027-047] Swell up in sounds confused and rend the skies.

[027-048] Come, Bacchus, come propitious, all implore,

[027-049] And act thy secret orgies o’er and o’er.

[027-050] But Mineus’ daughters, while these rites were paid,

[027-051] At home impertinently busy stay’d;

[027-052] Their wicked tasks they ply with various art,

[027-053] And through the loom the sliding shuttle dart,

[027-054] Or at the fire to comb the wool they stand,

[027-055] Or twirl the spindle with a dext’rous hand.

[027-056] Guilty themselves, they force the guiltless in,

[027-057] Their maids, who share the labour, share the sin.

[027-058] At last one sister cries, who nimbly knew

[027-059] To draw nice threads, and wind the finest clue,

[027-060] “While others idly rove, and gods revere,

[027-061] Their fancied gods! they know not who or where;

[027-062] Let us, whom [[minerva|Pallas]] taught her better arts,

[027-063] Still working, cheer with mirthful chat our hearts;

[027-064] And, to deceive the time, let me prevail

[027-065] With each by turns to tell some antique tale.”

[027-066] She said: her sisters liked the humour well,

[027-067] And, smiling, bade her the first story tell.

[027-068] But she a while profoundly seem’d to muse,

[027-069] Perplex’d amid variety to choose;

[027-070] And knew not whether she should first relate

[027-071] The poor Dircetis, and her wondrous fate

[027-072] (The Palestines believe it to a man,

[027-073] And show the lake in which her scales began):

[027-074] Or if she rather should the daughter sing,

[027-075] Who in the hoary verge of life took wing;

[027-076] Who soar’d from earth, and dwelt in towers on high,

[027-077] And now a dove she flits along the sky:

[027-078] Or how the tree, which once white berries bore,

[027-079] Still crimson bears, since stain’d with crimson gore.

[027-080] The tree was new; she likes it, and begins

[027-081] To tell the tale, and as she tells she spins.
