# Ceyx and Alcyone

The story of Ceyx and Alcyone is among the most tender and celebrated tales in Ovid's *Metamorphoses*. Ceyx, the king of Trachis and son of Lucifer (the Morning Star), is married to Alcyone, daughter of [[aeolus|Aeolus]], the god of the winds. After a series of ominous portents, Ceyx resolves to consult the oracle of [[apollo|Apollo]] at Claros. Alcyone, terrified of the sea, begs him not to go — but Ceyx sails anyway and is drowned in a storm. Juno, taking pity, sends a dream-vision to Alcyone revealing the shipwreck. Grief-stricken, Alcyone finds Ceyx's body washed ashore and throws herself into the sea. The gods transform both into halcyons (kingfishers), and Aeolus calms the seas for seven days each winter — the "halcyon days" — so the birds may nest in peace (104-001).

## Role in the Metamorphoses

The story occupies the entirety of Chapter 104 (Book XI) and is one of the longer and more emotionally developed episodes in the poem. It follows the pattern of a devoted couple separated by death, with supernatural dreams and a double transformation that unites them eternally as birds. Ovid uses the tale to explore themes of marital devotion, the terror of the sea, and the mercy of the gods.

### Key Episodes

- **Ominous Portents**: After his brother Daedalion is transformed into a hawk, Ceyx witnesses further terrifying prodigies and resolves to consult Apollo's oracle at Claros (104-001)
- **Alcyone's Plea**: Alcyone, terrified of the sea's dangers, begs Ceyx not to go: "Can Ceyx, then, sustain to leave his wife, / Unmov'd, to trust the hazards of his life?" (104-020)
- **The Shipwreck**: Despite seeking delays, Ceyx sets sail; a violent storm arises, and "the wreck of wretched Ceyx" is reported (104-317)
- **The Dream-Vision**: Juno sends Iris to the cave of Somnus (Sleep), commanding him to dispatch a dream to Alcyone showing Ceyx's drowned body (104-189)
- **Discovery and Transformation**: Alcyone discovers Ceyx's body on the shore and throws herself into the sea; both are transformed into halcyons: "Yet Ceyx and Alcyone shall join, / In mutual love" (104-432)

## Transformation

The double transformation of Ceyx and Alcyone into halcyons (kingfishers) is an act of divine mercy, granting the devoted couple eternal union.

- **Form**: Mortal couple → Halcyons (kingfishers)
- **Agent**: The gods (pitying their devotion)
- **Cause**: Grief and faithful love; Alcyone's suicide after Ceyx's drowning at sea
- **Source**: (104-001)
