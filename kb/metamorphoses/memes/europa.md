# Europa

Europa, a princess of Phoenicia and daughter of King Agenor, is the mortal woman whose abduction by Jupiter — in the form of a beautiful white bull — sets in motion one of the great narrative chains of the *Metamorphoses*. Carried across the sea to Crete, where she gives birth to Minos, Rhadamanthus, and Sarpedon, her disappearance triggers her father's desperate command to her brothers: find her or never return. This command sends Cadmus to Greece, where he founds Thebes, and thus Europa's story stands at the head of the entire Theban cycle that dominates Books III and IV of the poem.

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Europa's story is told briefly but pivotally in Book II, at the conclusion of the book's sequence of divine seductions. Jupiter, having observed Europa playing with her companions on the shore of Tyre, assumes the form of a magnificent white bull and mingles with the herds grazing nearby. His beauty and gentleness entice Europa to approach, to garland his horns with flowers, and eventually to climb onto his back. The moment she does, the god-bull bolts for the sea and carries her across the waves to Crete, where he reveals his true identity (018-001). Her brothers — Cadmus, Phoenix, and Cilix — are sent by Agenor to search for her with the command never to return without her. Cadmus's search leads him to consult the Delphic oracle, to follow a magical heifer, to slay a dragon, and to found the city of Thebes, making Europa's abduction the indirect cause of the entire Theban dynasty and its cascade of tragic metamorphoses. Juno's enduring resentment of Europa — kept always in her mind as another of Jupiter's conquests — fuels her persecution of Europa's descendants, particularly Semele, Ino, and Pentheus (018-001).

### Key Episodes

- **The Bull Among the Herd**: Jupiter transforms into a snow-white bull and joins the royal herds near the Tyrian shore, captivating Europa with his gentleness
- **The Abduction**: Europa climbs onto the bull's back; he carries her across the sea to Crete, where he resumes his divine form (018-001)
- **The Command of Agenor**: Europa's father orders Cadmus and his brothers to find her or face exile — a command that leads Cadmus to found Thebes
- **Juno's Lingering Wrath**: Juno's jealousy over Europa drives her persecution of the Theban royal house across subsequent books

## Transformation

Europa herself undergoes no physical metamorphosis, but Jupiter transforms himself to approach her:
- **Form**: Jupiter → White Bull → Jupiter
- **Agent**: Self-transformation by Jupiter
- **Cause**: Desire — Jupiter uses metamorphosis to seduce, not to punish
- **Source**: (018-001)
