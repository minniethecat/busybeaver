# Nessus

Nessus is a centaur whose brief but consequential appearance in Book IX of the *Metamorphoses* makes him one of the most significant minor characters in the poem. A ferryman at the river Euenus, he offers to carry Dejanira — the wife of Hercules — across the flood while Hercules swims. But when he attempts to violate her mid-stream, Hercules shoots him with an arrow tipped in the Hydra's venom. As Nessus dies, he gives Dejanira his blood-soaked tunic, telling her — falsely — that it possesses the power to restore a straying husband's love. Years later, when Dejanira fears she is losing Hercules to Iole, she sends him the tunic. The Hydra's venom, preserved in Nessus's blood, burns into Hercules's flesh, and the hero's agonizing death — described in one of the poem's most harrowing passages — is the centaur's dying revenge. Nessus thus achieves in death what no monster or giant could accomplish in life: the destruction of the greatest of the Greek heroes.

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Nessus appears in Book IX as Hercules returns to his native land with his new bride Dejanira. Reaching the flooded river Euenus, Hercules — confident in his own strength — prepares to swim across, but entrusts Dejanira to the centaur Nessus, who offers to ferry her. Midway across, Nessus attempts to rape Dejanira, and Hercules, hearing her cries, shoots the centaur with an arrow poisoned with the blood of the Lernaean Hydra. As Nessus lies dying, the black venom mingling with his blood, he conceives his revenge: he tears off his tunic, soaked in the lethal mixture, and presents it to Dejanira as a love charm — a garment that, he claims, will restore a husband's wandering affections (080-001). Years pass. When Dejanira later hears that Hercules intends to marry Iole, she sends the tunic to him via the servant Lichas. The moment Hercules puts it on, the venom ignites: the tunic clings to his skin, burning through flesh and bone. Hercules, in unspeakable agony, hurls Lichas into the sea (where he becomes a rock) and builds his own funeral pyre on Mount Oeta. Nessus, long dead, has taken the greatest of heroes with him.

### Key Episodes

- **The Ferry Across the Euenus**: Nessus offers to carry Dejanira across the swollen river while Hercules swims
- **The Attempted Violation**: Mid-stream, Nessus tries to rape Dejanira; Hercules's arrow strikes him down
- **The Poisoned Tunic**: Dying, Nessus gives Dejanira his blood-soaked tunic, calling it a love charm (080-001)
- **The Revenge from the Grave**: The tunic, steeped in Hydra venom through Nessus's blood, kills Hercules years later

## Transformation

Nessus himself is not transformed, but his poisoned blood, mingled with the Hydra's venom, becomes the agent of Hercules's transformation from hero to ash:
- **Form**: Centaur → Corpse → Instrument of Hercules's death
- **Agent**: Hercules's arrow (the centaur's death); the poisoned tunic (Hercules's death)
- **Cause**: Nessus's attempted rape of Dejanira and his dying gift of false hope
- **Source**: (080-001)
