# Alcmena

Alcmena is a mortal woman in the *Metamorphoses*, renowned for her beauty and for being the mother of [[hercules|Hercules]] (Alcides) by [[jupiter|Jupiter]]. The god assumed the form of her husband [[amphitryon|Amphitryon]] to sleep with her — "In fair Alcmena's arms he cool'd his love" (054-160). Her most prominent episode in Ovid concerns the birth of Hercules: [[juno|Juno]], jealous of Jupiter's infidelity, sat cross-legged with clasped hands before Alcmena's door, magically preventing the delivery. After seven days of agonizing labor, Alcmena's clever servant Galanthis tricked Juno by announcing the birth had already occurred. Juno, startled into rising, released the spell — but then punished Galanthis by transforming her into a weasel. Alcmena herself appears in later episodes as a maternal figure, comforting Iole after Hercules's death (084-001, 086-003).

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Alcmena appears across several books of the *Metamorphoses*. Her primary episode is the story of Hercules's birth — an elaborate narrative of Juno's obstruction and the trickery that defeats it. Ovid uses Alcmena's suffering in childbirth and her grief as narrative bookends: from the story of Hercules's difficult entry into the world to her mourning after his death. She also represents the collateral damage of divine affairs — a mortal woman caught between Jupiter's desire and Juno's vengeance.

### Key Episodes

- **Conception of Hercules**: Jupiter takes the form of Amphitryon and sleeps with Alcmena, fathering Hercules: "In fair Alcmena's arms he cool'd his love" (054-160)
- **The Obstructed Labor**: Juno sits before Alcmena's door with clasped hands and crossed legs, magically preventing the birth for seven days and nights (084-004)
- **Galanthis's Trick**: The servant-girl Galanthis tricks Juno by announcing the birth; Juno rises in shock, breaking the spell, and Alcmena delivers Hercules (084-051)
- **Comforting Iole**: After Hercules's death, Alcmena consoles Iole, wiping away her tears: "Alcmena's pitying hand oft wiped her tears" (086-003)
- **Defense of Hercules's Lineage**: Alcmena recounts the story to Iole, defending Hercules's divine parentage and recounting the trials of his birth (084-011)

## Transformation

Though Alcmena herself is not transformed, her story is intimately connected with transformation: her servant Galanthis is changed into a weasel by Juno as punishment for the birth-trick, and the entire episode is framed as a tale Alcmena tells to comfort Iole — a narrative of endurance through metamorphic hardship.
