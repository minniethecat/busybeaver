# Lycia

Lycia, a mountainous coastal region in southern Anatolia (modern Turkey), features in the *Metamorphoses* as the setting for several significant transformations. Its landscape — of rivers, mountains, and coastal plains — serves as a stage where human impiety meets divine retribution, most memorably in the tale of the Lycian peasants who, refusing water to Latona, are transformed into frogs.

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Lycia is most prominently featured in the story of Latona and the Lycian peasants. Latona (Leto), fleeing Juno's persecution with her infant twins Apollo and Diana, arrives in Lycia exhausted and thirsty. When she kneels at a lake to drink, the local peasants refuse her, stirring up the mud with their hands and feet to foul the water. The goddess, her divine wrath kindled by this cruelty to a thirsty mother, transforms them into frogs — condemned to live forever in the very water they denied her, croaking their denial eternally.

### Key Episodes

- **Latona and the Lycian Peasants**: Denied water by the local peasants, Latona transforms them into frogs — "May you live in your pool forever!" — a metamorphosis of perfect poetic justice (057-001)
- **The Chimera's Domain**: Lycia is the homeland of the Chimera, the fire-breathing monster (part lion, part goat, part serpent) slain by Bellerophon riding Pegasus
- **Sacred to Leto**: Lycia was a center of the cult of Leto (Latona), with important sanctuaries at Xanthus and Patara

## Transformation

The transformation of the Lycian peasants into frogs is one of the poem's most thematically neat metamorphoses. The peasants denied water to a mother; they are condemned to live in water forever. Their croaking voices — "brekekekex koax koax" — become the eternal expression of their original rudeness, a vocal transformation that preserves their sin in sound.

- **Transformation**: Peasants → Frogs
- **Agent**: Latona (divine punishment)
- **Cause**: Denial of water to a thirsty mother; violation of the sacred duty of hospitality
- **Source**: (057-001)
