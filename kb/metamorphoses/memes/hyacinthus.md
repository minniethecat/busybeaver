# Hyacinthus

Hyacinthus, a beautiful youth of Sparta, is the beloved of Apollo. During a game of discus, the god's throw strikes Hyacinthus in the head, killing him instantly. From his blood, Apollo creates the hyacinth flower — its petals marked with "AI AI" (alas, alas), the eternal cry of the god's mourning. The story is one of the poem's most tender meditations on love, accident, and the power of metamorphosis to preserve grief in beauty.

## Key Episodes

- **The Fatal Game**: Apollo and Hyacinthus, stripped for sport, compete in throwing the discus. Apollo's throw, powerful and precise, rebounds from the earth and strikes Hyacinthus in the face — the god's own strength killing what he most loves
- **The Flower Created**: Apollo, unable to save the youth, transforms his blood into the hyacinth flower — "a flower that shall keep your name" — the petals marked with the letters of grief (093-001)
