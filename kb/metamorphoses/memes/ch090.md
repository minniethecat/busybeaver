[079-001] Achelous relates to Theseus the contest between himself and Hercules for the hand of Dejanira, who becomes the wife of the latter.

[079-002] Theseus requests the god to tell his woes,

[079-003] Whence his maim’d brow, and whence his groans arose:

[079-004] When thus the Calydonian stream replied,

[079-005] With twining reeds his careless tresses tied:

[079-006] “Ungrateful is the tale, for who can bear,

[079-007] When conquer’d, to rehearse the shameful war?

[079-008] Yet I’ll the melancholy story trace;

[079-009] So great a conqueror softens the disgrace:

[079-010] Nor was it still so mean the prize to yield,

[079-011] As great and glorious to dispute the field.

[079-012] “Perhaps you’ve heard of Dejanira’s name,

[079-013] For all the country spoke her beauty’s fame.

[079-014] Long was the nymph by numerous suitors woo’d,

[079-015] Each with address his envied hopes pursued:

[079-016] I join’d the loving band to gain the fair,

[079-017] Reveal’d my passion to her father’s ear:

[079-018] Their vain pretensions all the rest resign,

[079-019] Alcides only strove to equal mine;

[079-020] He boasts his birth from [[jupiter|Jove]], recounts his spoils,

[079-021] His stepdame’s hate subdued, and finish’d toils.

[079-022] “ ‘Can mortals then,’ said I, ‘with gods compare?

[079-023] Behold a god! mine is the watery care:

[079-024] Through your wide realms I take my mazy way,

[079-025] Branch into streams, and o’er the region stray:

[079-026] No foreign guest your daughter’s charms adores,

[079-027] But one who rises in your native shores.

[079-028] Let not his punishment your pity move:

[079-029] Is Juno’s hate an argument for love?

[079-030] Though you your life from fair Alcmena drew,

[079-031] Jove’s a feign’d father, or by fraud a true.

[079-032] Choose then, confess thy mother’s honour lost,

[079-033] Or thy descent from Jove no longer boast.’

[079-034] “While thus I spoke, he look’d with stern disdain,

[079-035] Nor could the sallies of his wrath restrain,

[079-036] Which thus break forth: ‘This arm decides our right,

[079-037] Vanquish’d in words, be mine the prize in fight.’

[079-038] “Bold he rush’d on. My honour to maintain,

[079-039] I fling my verdant garments on the plain,

[079-040] My arms stretch forth, my pliant limbs prepare,

[079-041] And with bent hands expect the furious war.

[079-042] O’er my sleek skin now gather’d dust he throws,

[079-043] And yellow sand his mighty muscles strows:

[079-044] Oft he my neck and nimble legs assails;

[079-045] He seems to grasp me, but as often fails;

[079-046] Each part he now invades with eager hand,

[079-047] Safe in my bulk immoveable I stand;

[079-048] So when loud storms break high, and foam and roar,

[079-049] Against some mole that stretches from the shore,

[079-050] The firm foundation lasting tempests braves,

[079-051] Defies the warring winds and driving waves.

[079-052] “Awhile we breathe, then forward rush amain,

[079-053] Renew the combat, and our ground maintain;

[079-054] Foot strove with foot, I, prone, extend my breast,

[079-055] Hands war with hands, and forehead forehead press’d.

[079-056] Thus have I seen two furious bulls engage,

[079-057] Inflamed with equal love and equal rage,

[079-058] Each claims the fairest heifer of the grove,

[079-059] And conquest only can decide their love:

[079-060] The trembling herds survey the fight from far,

[079-061] Till victory decides the important war:

[079-062] Three times, in vain, he strove my joints to wrest,

[079-063] To force my hold, and throw me from his breast;

[079-064] The fourth he broke my gripe, that clasp’d him round,

[079-065] Then with new force he stretch’d me on the ground;

[079-066] Close to my back the mighty burden clung,

[079-067] As if a mountain o’er my limbs were flung;

[079-068] Believe my tale; nor do I, boastful, aim

[079-069] By feign’d narration to extol my fame;

[079-070] No sooner from his arm I freedom get,

[079-071] Unlock my arms, that flow’d with trickling sweat,

[079-072] But quick he seized me, and renew’d the strife,

[079-073] As my exhausted bosom pants for life;

[079-074] My neck he gripes, my knee to earth he strains,

[079-075] I fall, and bite the sand with shame and pains.

[079-076] “O’ermatch’d in strength, to wiles and arts I take,

[079-077] And slip his hold in form of speckled snake,

[079-078] Who, when I writhed in spires my body round,

[079-079] Or show’d my forky tongue with hissing sound,

[079-080] Smiles at my threats: ‘Such foes my cradle knew,’

[079-081] He cries; ‘dire snakes my infant hand o’erthrew:

[079-082] A dragon’s form might other conquests gain;

[079-083] To war with me you take that shape in vain:

[079-084] Art thou proportion’d to the hydra’s length,

[079-085] Who by his wounds received augmented strength?

[079-086] He raised a hundred hissing heads in air;

[079-087] When one I lopp’d, up sprung a dreadful pair:

[079-088] By his wounds fertile, and with slaughter strong,

[079-089] Singly I quell’d him, and stretch’d dead along.

[079-090] What canst thou do, a form precarious, prone,

[079-091] To rouse my rage with terrors not thy own?’

[079-092] He said, and round my neck his hands he cast,

[079-093] And with his straining fingers wrung me fast;

[079-094] My throat he tortured close as pincers clasp;

[079-095] In vain I strove to loose the forceful grasp.

[079-096] “Thus vanquish’d too, a third form still remains,

[079-097] Changed to a bull, my lowing fills the plains:

[079-098] Straight on the left his nervous arms were thrown

[079-099] Upon my brindled neck, and tugg’d it down;

[079-100] Then deep he struck my horn into the sand,

[079-101] And fell’d my bulk along the dusty land:

[079-102] Nor yet his fury cool’d; ’twixt rage and scorn,

[079-103] From my maim’d front he tore the stubborn horn;

[079-104] This, heap’d with flowers and fruits, the Naiads bear,

[079-105] Sacred to plenty, and the bounteous year.”

[079-106] He spoke, when lo! a beauteous nymph appears,

[079-107] Girt, like Diana’s train, with flowing hairs:

[079-108] The horn she brings, in which all autumn’s stored,

[079-109] And ruddy apples for the second board.

[079-110] Now morn begins to dawn, the sun’s bright fire

[079-111] Gilds the high mountains, and the youths retire;

[079-112] Nor stay’d they till the troubled stream subsides,

[079-113] And in its bounds with peaceful current glides;

[079-114] But Achelous in his oosy bed

[079-115] Deep hides his brow deform’d, and rustic head;

[079-116] No real wound the victor’s triumph show’d,

[079-117] But his lost honours grieved the watery god;

[079-118] Yet ev’n that loss the willow’s leaves o’erspread,

[079-119] And verdant reeds, in garlands, bind his head.
