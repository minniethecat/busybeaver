[018-001] Europa, the daughter of Agenor, is beloved by [[jupiter|Jupiter]], who assumes the shape of a bull, and mingles with the herd—The maiden caresses the beautiful animal, and at length ventures to sit on his back; when the god immediately hastens to the shore, and crosses the sea—He arrives safe at [[crete|Crete]] with his mistress, where he resumes his original shape.

[018-002] When now the god his fury had allay’d,

[018-003] And taken vengeance of the stubborn maid,

[018-004] From where the bright Athenian turrets rise

[018-005] He mounts aloft, and reascends the skies.

[018-006] Jove saw him enter the sublime abodes,

[018-007] And, as he mix’d among the crowd of gods,

[018-008] Beckon’d him out, and drew him from the rest,

[018-009] And in soft whispers thus his will express’d:

[018-010] “My trusty Hermes, by whose ready aid

[018-011] Thy sire’s commands are through the world convey’d,

[018-012] Resume thy wings, exert their utmost force,

[018-013] And to the walls of Sidon speed thy course;

[018-014] There find a herd of heifers wandering o’er

[018-015] The neighb’ring hill, and drive them to the shore.”

[018-016] Thus spoke the god, concealing his intent.

[018-017] The trusty Hermes on his message went,

[018-018] And found the herd of heifers wand’ring o’er

[018-019] A neighb’ring hill, and drove them to the shore

[018-020] Where the king’s daughter, with a lovely train

[018-021] Of fellow-nymphs, was sporting on the plain.

[018-022] The dignity of empire laid aside

[018-023] (For love but ill agrees with kingly pride),

[018-024] The ruler of the skies, the thund’ring god,

[018-025] Who shakes the world’s foundations with a nod,

[018-026] Among a herd of lowing heifers ran,

[018-027] Frisk’d in a bull, and bellow’d o’er the plain.

[018-028] Large rolls of fat about his shoulders clung,

[018-029] And from his neck the double dewlap hung;

[018-030] His skin was whiter than the snow that lies

[018-031] Unsullied by the breath of southern skies:

[018-032] Small shining horns on his curl’d forehead stand,

[018-033] As turn’d and polish’d by the workman’s hand;

[018-034] His eyeballs roll’d, not formidably bright,

[018-035] But gazed and languish’d with a gentle light;

[018-036] His every look was peaceful, and express’d

[018-037] The softness of the lover in the beast.

[018-038] Agenor’s royal daughter, as she play’d

[018-039] Among the fields, the milk-white bull survey’d,

[018-040] And view’d his spotless body with delight,

[018-041] And at a distance kept him in her sight.

[018-042] At length she pluck’d the rising flowers, and fed

[018-043] The gentle beast, and fondly stroked his head.

[018-044] He stood, well pleased to touch the charming fair,

[018-045] But hardly could confine his pleasures there.

[018-046] And now he wantons o’er the neighb’ring strand,

[018-047] Now rolls his body on the yellow sand;

[018-048] And, now perceiving all her fears decay’d,

[018-049] Comes tossing forward to the royal maid.

[018-050] Gives her his breast to stroke, and downward turns

[018-051] His grisly brow, and gently stoops his horns.

[018-052] In flowery wreaths the royal virgin dress’d

[018-053] His bending horns, and kindly clapp’d his breast;

[018-054] Till now grown wanton and devoid of fear,

[018-055] Not knowing that she press’d the Thunderer,

[018-056] She placed herself upon his back, and rode

[018-057] O’er fields and meadows, seated on the god.

[018-058] He gently march’d along, and by degrees

[018-059] Left the dry meadow, and approach’d the seas,

[018-060] Where now he dips his hoofs and wets his thighs,

[018-061] Now plunges in, and carries off the prize.

[018-062] The frighted nymph looks backward on the shore,

[018-063] And hears the tumbling billows round her roar;

[018-064] But still she holds him fast; one hand is borne

[018-065] Upon his back, the other grasps a horn;

[018-066] Her train of ruffling garments flies behind,

[018-067] Swells in the air, and hovers in the wind.

[018-068] Through storms and tempests he the virgin bore,

[018-069] And lands her safe on the Dictaean shore;

[018-070] Where now, in his divinest form array’d,

[018-071] In his true shape he captivates the maid,

[018-072] Who gazes on him, and with wond’ring eyes

[018-073] Beholds the new majestic figure rise,

[018-074] His glowing features, and celestial light,

[018-075] And all the god discover’d to her sight.
