# Deucalion

Deucalion, son of Prometheus, is the Noah-like survivor of the great flood that Jupiter sends to destroy the wicked race of men. Along with his wife Pyrrha, Deucalion survives by virtue of his piety, and together they perform one of the most famous metamorphoses in the poem: casting stones behind them that soften into human form, repopulating a cleansed earth. His story in Book I marks the first cosmic reset in the *Metamorphoses* — from destruction to renewal, from chaos to a second creation (006-001).

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Deucalion's story occupies chapters 6–7 of Book I. After Jupiter destroys the impious human race through the great flood — a punishment provoked by Lycaon's crime — only Deucalion and Pyrrha survive. Their ark comes to rest on the summit of Mount Parnassus. When the waters recede, they find themselves alone in a silent, empty world. They weep, they pray, and they consult the oracle of Themis at Delphi. The oracle's cryptic command — "Leave the temple, veil your heads, loosen your robes, and throw behind you the bones of your great mother" — terrifies them until Deucalion interprets it: the "great mother" is Earth, and her "bones" are stones (019-015). Casting the stones behind them, the couple witness a miracle: the stones soften, take on human shape, and become a new race of people — hardier than the one destroyed, as befits their stony origin.

### Key Episodes

- **The Deluge**: The great flood, sent by Jupiter, destroys all humanity except the pious Deucalion and Pyrrha (006-001)
- **The Ark on Parnassus**: Deucalion and Pyrrha, on their small vessel, come to rest on Mount Parnassus as the waters subside
- **The Oracle at Delphi**: The couple consults Themis's oracle and receives the cryptic command to throw their mother's bones (019-015)
- **The Stone Transformation**: The stones cast by Deucalion become men; those cast by Pyrrha become women — a new, hardier human race (006-345, 006-351)
