# Icarus

Son of the master craftsman [[Daedalus]], whose famous flight on wax-bound wings ended in disaster when, ignoring his father's warnings, he soared too near the sun; the wax melted, the wings disintegrated, and he plunged into the Icarian Sea. (070-001)

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Icarus appears in Book VIII of the *Metamorphoses*, in the story of [[Daedalus]]'s escape from [[Crete]]. Imprisoned by King [[Minos]], Daedalus fashions two pairs of wings from feathers and wax — one for himself and one for his son. He instructs Icarus to follow a middle course: neither too low (where sea-spray would weigh down the wings) nor too high (where the sun would melt the wax). But the exhilaration of flight overcomes the boy. He climbs toward the sun; the wax softens; the feathers scatter; and Icarus plummets into the sea, calling his father's name as he falls. Daedalus, now a father without a son, names the waters the Icarian Sea and buries the boy on the nearby island of Icaria. (070-001, 070-018, 070-071)

### Key Episodes

- **The Warning**: Daedalus fits Icarus with his wings and delivers the famous warning: fly a middle course. He kisses his son — a kiss destined to be their last — with tears streaming down his face, his hands trembling as he fastens the unnatural wings. (070-018)
- **The Flight and Fall**: The pair launch into the air. Fishermen, shepherds, and ploughmen look up in wonder, thinking them gods. But Icarus, drunk on the freedom of flight, abandons his father's path and rises toward the sun. The wax melts; the wings disintegrate; the boy tumbles through the sky. (070-001)
- **The Lament**: Daedalus, already flying onward, looks back and cannot see his son. He cries out "Ho, Icarus! where are you?" — then spots the scattered feathers floating on the waves below. (070-071)

## Transformation

Icarus undergoes no supernatural metamorphosis, but his fall transforms the landscape:

- **The Icarian Sea**: Named for the boy who fell into it
- **The Island of Icaria**: His burial place, named in his memory

- **Agent**: The sun — an impersonal force, not a divine punishment
- **Cause**: Youthful recklessness and the intoxication of flight; Daedalus's technology, which granted escape but could not guarantee safety
- **Source**: (070-001)
