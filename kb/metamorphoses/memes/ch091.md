[080-001] The centaur Nessus, who offers violence to Dejanira, is killed by the shafts of Hercules—Before he expires, he presents a poisoned tunic to the woman he has injured, assuring her of its efficacy to recall the affections of a faithless husband.

[080-002] This virgin too, thy love, O Nessus, found;

[080-003] To her alone you owe the fatal wound.

[080-004] As the strong son of [[jupiter|Jove]] his bride conveys,

[080-005] Where his paternal lands their bulwarks raise;

[080-006] Where from her slopy urn Evenus pours

[080-007] Her rapid current, swell’d by wintry showers,

[080-008] He came. The frequent eddies whirl’d the tide,

[080-009] And the deep rolling waves all pass denied.

[080-010] As for himself, he stood unmoved by fears,

[080-011] For now his bridal charge employ’d his cares.

[080-012] The strong-limb’d Nessus thus officious cried

[080-013] (For he the shallows of the stream had tried),

[080-014] “Swim thou, Alcides, all thy strength prepare,

[080-015] On yonder bank I’ll lodge thy nuptial care.”

[080-016] The Aonian chief to Nessus trusts his wife,

[080-017] All pale and trembling for her hero’s life.

[080-018] Clothed as he stood in the fierce lion’s hide,

[080-019] The laden quiver o’er his shoulder tied

[080-020] (For cross the stream his bow and club were cast),

[080-021] Swift he plunged in: “These billows shall be pass’d,”

[080-022] He said, nor sought where smoother waters glide,

[080-023] But stemm’d the rapid dangers of the tide.

[080-024] The bank he reach’d, again the bow he bears,

[080-025] When, hark! his bride’s known voice alarms his ears.

[080-026] “Nessus, to thee I call,” aloud he cries,

[080-027] “Vain is thy trust in flight, be timely wise:

[080-028] Thou monster double-shaped, my right set free:

[080-029] If thou no rev’rence owe my fame and me,

[080-030] Yet kindred should thy lawless lust deny.

[080-031] Think not, perfidious wretch, from me to fly;

[080-032] Though wing’d with horses’ speed, wounds shall pursue.”

[080-033] Swift as his words the fatal arrow flew:

[080-034] The centaur’s back admits the feather’d wood,

[080-035] And through his breast the barbed weapon stood,

[080-036] Which when, in anguish, through the flesh he tore,

[080-037] From both the wounds gush’d forth the spumy gore,

[080-038] Mix’d with Lernaean venom; this he took,

[080-039] Nor dire revenge his dying breast forsook;

[080-040] His garment, in the reeking purple died,

[080-041] To rouse love’s passion, he presents the bride.
