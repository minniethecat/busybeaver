[124-001] The inhabitants of the island Pithecusa are changed into monkeys as a punishment of their dishonesty.

[124-002] The galleys now by Pithecusa pass;

[124-003] The name is from the natives of the place.

[124-004] The father of the gods detesting lies,

[124-005] Oft with abhorrence heard their perjuries.

[124-006] The abandon’d race, transform’d to beasts, began

[124-007] To mimic the impertinence of man:

[124-008] Flatnosed and furrow’d, with grimace they grin,

[124-009] And look to what they were too near akin;

[124-010] Merry in make, and busy to no end,

[124-011] This moment they divert, the next offend:

[124-012] So much this species of their past retains,

[124-013] Though lost the language, yet the noise remains.
