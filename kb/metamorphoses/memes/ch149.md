[133-001] Turnus sets fire to the Trojan ships, which are transformed into sea deities by Cybele—Aeneas, at length, surmounts all opposition, and is united to Lavinia.

[133-002] Meanwhile the Latians all their power prepare,

[133-003] ’Gainst fortune, and the foe to push the war.

[133-004] With Phrygian blood the floating-fields they stain;

[133-005] But short of succours, still contend in vain.

[133-006] Turnus remarks the Trojan fleet ill mann’d,

[133-007] Unguarded, and at anchor near the strand;

[133-008] He thought; and straight a lighted brand he bore,

[133-009] And fire invades what ’scaped the waves before.

[133-010] The billows from the kindling prow retire;

[133-011] Pitch, rosin, searwood on red wings aspire,

[133-012] And Vulcan on the seas exerts his attribute of fire.

[133-013] This when the mother of the gods beheld,

[133-014] Her towery crown she shook, and stood reveal’d;

[133-015] Her brindled lions rein’d, unveil’d her head,

[133-016] And hovering o’er her favour’d fleet, she said:

[133-017] “Cease, Turnus, and the heavenly powers respect,

[133-018] Nor dare to violate what I protect.

[133-019] These galleys once fair trees on Ida stood,

[133-020] And gave their shade to each descending god.

[133-021] Nor shall consume; irrevocable fate

[133-022] Allots their being no determined date.”

[133-023] Straight peals of thunder heaven’s high arches rend

[133-024] The hailstones leap, the showers in spouts descend.

[133-025] The winds with widen’d throats the signal give;

[133-026] The cables break, the smoking vessels drive.

[133-027] Now, wondrous, as they beat the foaming flood,

[133-028] The timbers soften into flesh and blood;

[133-029] The yards and oars new arms and legs design;

[133-030] A trunk the hull; the slender keel a spine:

[133-031] The prow a female face; and by degrees

[133-032] The gallies rise green daughters of the seas.

[133-033] Sometimes on coral beds they sit in state,

[133-034] Or wanton on the waves they fear’d of late.

[133-035] The barks that beat the seas are still their care,

[133-036] Themselves remembering what of late they were;

[133-037] To save a Trojan sail in throngs they press,

[133-038] But smile to see Alcinous in distress.

[133-039] Unable were those wonders to deter

[133-040] The Latians from their unsuccessful war.

[133-041] Both sides for doubtful victory contend;

[133-042] And on their courage and their gods depend.

[133-043] Nor bright Lavinia, nor Latinus’ crown,

[133-044] Warm their great soul to war, like fair renown.

[133-045] [[venus|Venus]] at last beholds her godlike son

[133-046] Triumphant, and the field of battle won;

[133-047] Brave Turnus slain, strong Ardea but a name,

[133-048] And buried in fierce deluges of flame.

[133-049] Her towers, that boasted once a sovereign sway,

[133-050] The fate of fancied grandeur now betray.

[133-051] A famish’d heron from the ashes springs,

[133-052] And beats the ruin with disastrous wings.

[133-053] Calamities of towns distress’d she feigns,

[133-054] And oft, with woeful shrieks, of war complains.
