# Medea

Medea, daughter of Aeëtes of Colchis, is the most powerful mortal sorceress in the *Metamorphoses*. Her magic — which can rejuvenate the aged Aeson, deceive the daughters of Pelias into killing their father, and destroy her rival through a poisoned gift — blurs the boundary between mortal craft and divine power. Ovid presents her not as a simple villain but as a figure of terrifying agency in a world where women are typically the passive objects of transformation.

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Medea dominates the latter part of Book VII in an extended episode that showcases her magical power through a series of transformations. Unlike the gods, who transform at will, Medea must gather ingredients, brew potions, and invoke Hecate — her magic is labor, not fiat. This makes her both more formidable (she achieves what requires no divine birthright) and more vulnerable (her power cannot preserve her marriage).

### Key Episodes

- **Rejuvenation of Aeson**: Medea brews a potion from herbs gathered across the world and restores Jason's aged father to youth — the only true rejuvenation in the poem (074-001)
- **Death of Pelias**: Medea deceives the daughters of Pelias into killing their father, promising to rejuvenate him as she did Aeson — but she withholds the spell, and Pelias dies (074-001)
- **Flight and Infanticide**: Escaping in her dragon-drawn chariot, Medea kills her own children to punish Jason — transformation through destruction (074-001)
- **Revenge on Jason's Bride**: Medea sends a poisoned crown and robe to Jason's new bride, who burns to death; her father perishes embracing her (074-001)

## Transformation

Medea enacts transformation through **art and labor** rather than divine power: her potions, invocations, and gathered herbs represent a mortal alternative to the metamorphic power of the gods. Her tragedy is that this power cannot secure what she most desires — Jason's fidelity — making her the poem's most complex meditation on the limits of transformative agency.

- **Form**: Mortal sorceress → Agent of transformation
- **Agent**: Medea herself, through magical practice
- **Cause**: Love (rejuvenating Aeson), deception (killing Pelias), revenge (destroying Jason's bride)
- **Source**: (074-001)
