[084-001] The delivery of Alcmena is effected by the sagacity of a servant-maid, named Galanthis, whose fidelity excites the displeasure of Juno, who converts her into a weasel.

[084-002] Atlas perceived the load of heaven’s new guest.

[084-003] [[revenge|Revenge]] still rancour’d in Eurystheus’ breast

[084-004] Against Alcides’ race. Alcmena goes

[084-005] To Iole, to vent maternal woes;

[084-006] Here she pours forth her grief, recounts the spoils

[084-007] Her son had bravely reap’d in glorious toils.

[084-008] This Iole, by Hercules’ commands,

[084-009] Hyllus had loved, and join’d in nuptial bands.

[084-010] Her swelling sides the teeming birth confess’d,

[084-011] To whom Alcmena thus her speech address’d:

[084-012] “O may the gods protect thee, in that hour,

[084-013] When, midst thy throes, thou call’st the Ilithyian power!

[084-014] May no delays prolong thy racking pain,

[084-015] As when I sued for Juno’s aid in vain.

[084-016] “When now Alcides’ mighty birth drew nigh,

[084-017] And the tenth sign roll’d forward on the sky,

[084-018] My sides extend with such a mighty load,

[084-019] As [[jupiter|Jove]] the parent of the burden show’d.

[084-020] I could no more the increasing smart sustain,

[084-021] My horror kindles to recount the pain;

[084-022] Cold chills my limbs while I the tale pursue,

[084-023] And now methinks I feel my pangs anew.

[084-024] Seven days and nights amid incessant throes,

[084-025] Fatigued with ills I lay, nor knew repose;

[084-026] When lifting high my hands, in shrieks I pray’d,

[084-027] Implored the gods, and call’d Lucina’s aid.

[084-028] She came, but prejudiced, to give my fate

[084-029] A sacrifice to vengeful Juno’s hate.

[084-030] She hears the groaning anguish of my fits,

[084-031] And on the altar at my door she sits.

[084-032] O’er her left knee her crossing leg she cast,

[084-033] Then knits her fingers close, and wrings them fast:

[084-034] This stay’d the birth; in mutt’ring verse she pray’d;

[084-035] The mutt’ring verse the unfinish’d birth delay’d.

[084-036] Now with fierce struggles, raging with my pain,

[084-037] At Jove’s ingratitude I rage in vain.

[084-038] How did I wish for death! such groans I sent,

[084-039] As might have made the flinty heart relent.

[084-040] “Now the Cadmeian matrons round me press,

[084-041] Offer their vows, and seek to bring redress;

[084-042] Among the Theban dames Galanthis stands,

[084-043] Strong-limb’d, red-hair’d, and just to my commands:

[084-044] She first perceived that all these racking woes

[084-045] From the persisting hate of Juno rose.

[084-046] As here and there she pass’d, by chance she sees

[084-047] The seated goddess; on her close-press’d knees

[084-048] Her fast-knit hands she leans; with cheerful voice

[084-049] Galanthis cries, ‘Whoe’er thou art, rejoice,

[084-050] Congratulate the dame, she lies at rest,

[084-051] At length the gods Alcmena’s prayers have bless’d.’

[084-052] Swift from her seat the startled goddess springs;

[084-053] No more conceal’d, her hands abroad she flings:

[084-054] The charm unloosed, the birth my pangs relieved;

[084-055] Galanthis’ laughter vex’d the power deceived.

[084-056] Fame says, the goddess dragg’d the laughing maid

[084-057] Fast by the hair; in vain her force essay’d

[084-058] Her grovelling body from the ground to rear;

[084-059] Changed to forefeet her shrinking arms appear:

[084-060] Her hairy back her former hue retains,

[084-061] The form alone is lost; her strength remains;

[084-062] Who, since the lie did from her mouth proceed,

[084-063] Shall from her pregnant mouth bring forth her breed;

[084-064] Nor shall she quit her long-frequented home,

[084-065] But haunt those houses where she loved to roam.”
