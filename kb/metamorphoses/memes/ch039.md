[033-001] Juno, jealous of the prosperity of Ino, the nurse of [[bacchus|Bacchus]], sends the fury Tisiphone to the house of Athamas, her husband, who is seized with such a sudden frenzy, that he mistakes his wife and children for a lioness with her whelps, and dashes his son Learchus against a wall—Ino effects her escape, and from a high rock precipitates herself into the sea with Melicerta in her arms—She is promoted by Neptune to the dignity of a sea-deity, afterward called Leucothoe, while Melicerta becomes a sea-god, known by the name of Palaemon.

[033-002] The power of Bacchus now o’er [[thebes|Thebes]] had flown:

[033-003] With awful reverence soon the god they own.

[033-004] Proud Ino all around the wonder tells,

[033-005] And on her nephew deity still dwells.

[033-006] Of numerous sisters, she alone yet knew

[033-007] No grief, but grief which she from sisters drew.

[033-008] Imperial Juno saw her with disdain

[033-009] Vain in her offspring, in her consort vain,

[033-010] Who ruled the trembling Thebans with a nod,

[033-011] But saw her vainest in her foster-god.

[033-012] “Could then,” she cried, “a bastard boy have power

[033-013] To make a mother her own son devour?

[033-014] Could he the Tuscan crew to fishes change,

[033-015] And now three sisters damn to forms so strange?

[033-016] Yet shall the wife of [[jupiter|Jove]] find no relief?

[033-017] Shall she still unrevenged disclose her grief?

[033-018] Have I the mighty freedom to complain?

[033-019] Is that my power? Is that to ease my pain?

[033-020] A foe has taught me vengeance; and who ought

[033-021] To scorn that vengeance which a foe has taught?

[033-022] What sure destruction frantic rage can throw,

[033-023] The gaping wounds of slaughter’d Pentheus show.

[033-024] Why should not Ino, fired with madness, stray,

[033-025] Like her mad sisters her own kindred slay?

[033-026] Why she not follow where they lead the way?”

[033-027] Down a steep yawning cave where yews display’d

[033-028] In arches meet, and lend a baleful shade,

[033-029] Through silent labyrinths a passage lies

[033-030] To mournful regions and infernal skies.

[033-031] Here Styx exhales its noisome clouds, and here,

[033-032] The funeral rites once paid, all souls appear,

[033-033] Stiff, cold; and horror, with a ghastly face,

[033-034] And staring eyes, infests the dreary place.

[033-035] Ghosts, new-arrived, and strangers to these plains,

[033-036] Know not the palace where grim Pluto reigns;

[033-037] They journey doubtful, nor the road can tell,

[033-038] Which leads to the metropolis of hell.

[033-039] A thousand avenues those towers command,

[033-040] A thousand gates for ever open stand.

[033-041] As all the rivers, disembogued, find room

[033-042] For all their waters in old Ocean’s womb,

[033-043] So this vast city worlds of shades receives,

[033-044] And space for millions still of worlds she leaves.

[033-045] The unbodied spectres freely rove, and show

[033-046] Whate’er they loved on earth they love below:

[033-047] The lawyers still, or right or wrong support,

[033-048] The courtiers smoothly glide to Pluto’s court,

[033-049] Still airy heroes thoughts of glory fire,

[033-050] Still the dead poet strings his deathless lyre,

[033-051] And lovers still with fancied darts expire.

[033-052] The queen of heaven, to gratify her hate,

[033-053] And sooth immortal wrath, forgets her state;

[033-054] Down from the realms of day to realms of night,

[033-055] The goddess swift precipitates her flight.

[033-056] At hell arrived, the noise hell’s porter heard,

[033-057] The enormous dog his triple head uprear’d:

[033-058] Thrice from three grisly throats he howl’d profound,

[033-059] Then suppliant couch’d, and stretch’d along the ground.

[033-060] The trembling threshold, which Saturnia press’d,

[033-061] The weight of such divinity confess’d.

[033-062] Before a lofty adamantine gate,

[033-063] Which closed a tower of brass, the Furies sate;

[033-064] Misshapen forms, tremendous to the sight,

[033-065] The implacable foul daughters of the night.

[033-066] A sounding whip each bloody sister shakes,

[033-067] Or from her tresses combs the curling snakes.

[033-068] But now great Juno’s majesty was known;

[033-069] Through the thick gloom all heavenly bright she shone;

[033-070] The hideous monsters their obedience show’d,

[033-071] And, rising from their seats, submissive bow’d.

[033-072] This is the place of wo, here groan the dead:

[033-073] Huge Tityus o’er nine acres here is spread:

[033-074] Fruitful for pain the immortal liver breeds,

[033-075] Still grows, and still the insatiate vulture feeds:

[033-076] Poor Tantalus to taste the water tries,

[033-077] But from his lips the faithless water flies:

[033-078] Then thinks the bending tree he can command;

[033-079] The tree starts backwards, and eludes his hand:

[033-080] The labour too of Sisyphus is vain;

[033-081] Up the steep mount he heaves the stone with pain,

[033-082] Down from the summit rolls the stone again:

[033-083] The Belides their leaky vessels still

[033-084] Are ever filling, and yet never fill;

[033-085] [[fate|Doom]]’d to this punishment for blood they shed,

[033-086] For bridegrooms slaughter’d in the bridal bed;

[033-087] Stretch’d on the rolling wheel Ixion lies;

[033-088] Himself he follows, and himself he flies.

[033-089] Ixion, tortured, Juno sternly eyed,

[033-090] Then turn’d, and toiling Sisyphus espied:

[033-091] “And why,” she said, “so wretched is the fate

[033-092] Of him, whose brother proudly reigns in state?

[033-093] Yet still my altars unadored have been

[033-094] By Athamas and his presumptuous queen.”

[033-095] What caused her hate, the goddess thus confess’d,

[033-096] What caused her journey now was more than guess’d,

[033-097] That hate, relentless, its revenge did want,

[033-098] And that revenge the Furies soon could grant:

[033-099] They could the glory of proud Thebes efface,

[033-100] And hide in ruin the Cadmean race.

[033-101] For this she largely promises, entreats,

[033-102] And to entreaties adds imperial threats.

[033-103] Then fell Tisiphone with rage was stung,

[033-104] And from her mouth the untwisted serpents flung.

[033-105] “To gain this trifling boon, there is no need,”

[033-106] She cried, “in formal speeches to proceed.

[033-107] Whatever thou command’st to do is done;

[033-108] Believe it finish’d, though not yet begun.

[033-109] But from these melancholy seats repair

[033-110] To happier mansions, and to purer air.”

[033-111] She spoke. The goddess, darting upwards, flies,

[033-112] And joyous reascends her native skies:

[033-113] Nor enter’d there, till round her Iris threw

[033-114] Ambrosial sweets, and pour’d celestial dew.

[033-115] The faithful fury, guiltless of delays,

[033-116] With cruel haste the dire command obeys.

[033-117] Girt in a bloody gown, a torch she shakes,

[033-118] And round her neck twines speckled wreaths of snakes.

[033-119] Fear, and dismay, and agonizing pain,

[033-120] With frantic rage, complete her loveless train.

[033-121] To Thebes her flight she sped, and hell forsook;

[033-122] At her approach the Theban turrets shook;

[033-123] The sun shrunk back, thick clouds the day o’ercast,

[033-124] And springing greens were wither’d as she pass’d.

[033-125] Now, dismal yellings heard, strange spectre seen,

[033-126] Confound as much the monarch as the queen.

[033-127] In vain to quit the palace they prepared,

[033-128] Tisiphone was there, and kept the ward.

[033-129] She wide extended her unfriendly arms,

[033-130] And all the fury lavish’d all her harms,

[033-131] Part of her tresses loudly hiss, and part

[033-132] Spread poison, as their forky tongues they dart:

[033-133] Then from her middle locks two snakes she drew,

[033-134] Whose merit from superior mischief grew:

[033-135] The envenom’d ruin, thrown with spiteful care,

[033-136] Clung to the bosoms of the hapless pair.

[033-137] The hapless pair soon with wild thoughts were fired,

[033-138] And madness by a thousand ways inspired.

[033-139] ’Tis true, the unwounded body still was sound,

[033-140] But ’twas the soul which felt the deadly wound.

[033-141] Nor did the unsated monster here give o’er,

[033-142] But dealt of plagues a fresh unnumber’d store.

[033-143] Each baneful juice too well she understood,

[033-144] Foam churn’d by Cerberus, and Hydra’s blood.

[033-145] Hot hemlock and cold aconite she chose,

[033-146] Delighted in variety of woes.

[033-147] Whatever can untune the harmonious soul,

[033-148] And its mild reas’ning faculties control,

[033-149] Give false ideas, raise desires profane,

[033-150] And whirl in eddies the tumultuous brain,

[033-151] Mix’d with cursed art, she direfully around

[033-152] Through all their nerves diffused the sad compound;

[033-153] Then toss’d her torch in circles still the same,

[033-154] Improved their rage, and added flame to flame.

[033-155] The grinning fury her own conquest spied,

[033-156] And to her rueful shades return’d with pride,

[033-157] And threw the exhausted useless snakes aside.

[033-158] Now Athamas cries out, his reason fled,

[033-159] “Here, fellow-hunters, let the toils be spread.

[033-160] I saw a lioness, in quest of food,

[033-161] With her two young, run roaring in this wood.”

[033-162] Again the fancied savages were seen,

[033-163] As through his palace still he chased his queen;

[033-164] Then tore Learchus from her breast: the child

[033-165] Stretch’d little arms, and on its father smiled:

[033-166] A father now no more, who now begun

[033-167] Around his head to whirl his giddy son,

[033-168] And, quite insensible to nature’s call,

[033-169] The helpless infant flung against the wall.

[033-170] The same mad poison in the mother wrought:

[033-171] Young Melicerta in her arms she caught,

[033-172] And with disorder’d tresses, howling, flies,

[033-173] “O! Bacchus, Evoe, Bacchus!” loud she cries.

[033-174] The name of Bacchus Juno laugh’d to hear,

[033-175] And said, “Thy foster-god has cost thee dear.”

[033-176] A rock there stood, whose side the beating waves

[033-177] Had long consumed, and hollow’d into caves;

[033-178] The head shot forwards in a bending steep,

[033-179] And cast a dreadful covert o’er the deep.

[033-180] The wretched Ino, on destruction bent,

[033-181] Climb’d up the cliff, such strength her fury lent,

[033-182] Thence with her guiltless boy, who wept in vain,

[033-183] At one bold spring she plunged into the main.

[033-184] Her niece’s fate touch’d [[venus|Cytherea]]’s breast,

[033-185] And in soft sounds she Neptune thus address’d:

[033-186] “Great god of waters, whose extended sway

[033-187] Is next to his whom heaven and earth obey,

[033-188] Let not the suit of Venus thee displease,

[033-189] Pity the floaters on the Ionian seas.

[033-190] Increase thy subject-gods, nor yet disdain

[033-191] To add my kindred to that glorious train.

[033-192] If from the sea I may such honours claim,

[033-193] If ’tis desert that from the sea I came,

[033-194] As Grecian poets artfully have sung,

[033-195] And in the name confess’d from whence I sprung.”

[033-196] Pleased Neptune nodded his assent, and free

[033-197] Both soon became from frail mortality.

[033-198] He gave them form, and majesty divine,

[033-199] And bade them glide along the foamy brine.

[033-200] For Melicerta is Palaemon known,

[033-201] And Ino once, Leucothoe is grown.
