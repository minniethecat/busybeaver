[094-001] The Cerastae are punished for their cruelty to strangers, by being changed into oxen by [[venus|Venus]]—The angry goddess punishes the wantonness of the Propoetides by their transformation into stones.

[094-002] Inquire of Amathus, whose wealthy ground

[094-003] With veins of every metal does abound,

[094-004] If she to her Propoetides would show

[094-005] The honour Sparta does to him allow.

[094-006] “No more,” she’d say, “such wretches would we grace,

[094-007] Than those whose crooked horns deform’d their face,

[094-008] From thence Cerastae call’d, an impious race,

[094-009] Before whose gates a reverend altar stood,

[094-010] To [[jupiter|Jove]] inscribed, the hospitable god:

[094-011] This had some stranger seen, with gore besmear’d,

[094-012] The blood of lambs and bulls it had appear’d:

[094-013] Their slaughter’d guests’ it was; not flock nor herd.”

[094-014] Venus these barb’rous sacrifices view’d

[094-015] With just abhorrence, and with wrath pursued.

[094-016] At first, to punish such nefarious crimes,

[094-017] Their towns she meant to leave, her once-loved climes.

[094-018] “But why,” said she, “for their offence should I

[094-019] My dear delightful plains and cities fly?

[094-020] No, let the impious people, who have sinn’d,

[094-021] A punishment in death or exile find:

[094-022] If death or exile too severe be thought,

[094-023] Let them in some vile shape bemoan their fault;

[094-024] While next her mind a proper form employs,

[094-025] Admonish’d by their horns, she fix’d her choice,

[094-026] Their former crest remains upon their heads,

[094-027] And their strong limbs an ox’s shape invades.

[094-028] The blasphemous Propoetides denied

[094-029] Worship of Venus, and her power defied;

[094-030] Unknowing how to blush, and shameless grown,

[094-031] A small transition changes them to stone.
