# Alcyone

Alcyone (or Halcyone), daughter of Aeolus, god of the winds, is the faithful wife of Ceyx, king of Trachis. Their story, told in Book XI of the *Metamorphoses*, is one of the poem's most tender portrayals of marital love. When Ceyx resolves to sail across the sea to consult the oracle at Delphi — despite Alcyone's terrified premonitions — a storm strikes his ship, and he drowns, his last word her name. Alcyone, unaware, prays daily to Juno for his safe return. Juno, moved, sends Iris to the god Sleep, who dispatches his son Morpheus to appear to Alcyone in a dream as the drowned Ceyx and reveal his death. Rushing to the shore at dawn, Alcyone finds his body washing in on the tide. As she leaps toward him, both are transformed into halcyon birds (kingfishers). Aeolus, in tribute, calms the winds for seven days each winter so the birds may nest safely on the sea — the origin of the "halcyon days."

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Alcyone's story is told across Book XI, intertwined with her husband Ceyx. Her role is defined by devotion: she begs Ceyx not to sail, prays for him ceaselessly while he is gone, and when she learns of his death through the dream-vision sent by Morpheus, her grief transforms her. The narrative emphasizes her piety (constant prayers at Juno's altar), her intuition (she senses disaster before any news arrives), and the power of her love — so great that even the gods intervene, first with the dream and then with the transformation. The halcyon days, when Aeolus stills the winds for the nesting birds, remain as a permanent trace of her story in the natural world.

### Key Episodes

- **The Premonition**: Alcyone begs Ceyx not to sail; he insists, and she watches his ship until it vanishes over the horizon
- **The Storm and Ceyx's Death**: A tempest destroys Ceyx's ship; his dying thought is of Alcyone
- **The Dream of Morpheus**: Juno sends Iris to Sleep, who dispatches Morpheus in Ceyx's form to tell Alcyone of the death
- **Transformation**: Alcyone finds Ceyx's body on the shore; as she rushes to him, both become kingfishers

## Transformation

Alcyone and Ceyx are transformed together into halcyon birds, their love preserved in avian form. The transformation is a reward for devotion rather than a punishment — the gods answer Alcyone's grief by giving both a new, enduring existence together.

- **Form**: Alcyone (mortal queen) → Halcyon / kingfisher
- **Form**: Ceyx (drowned king) → Halcyon / kingfisher  
- **Agent**: The gods, moved by Alcyone's devotion
- **Cause**: Reward for faithful love and profound grief
- **Source**: Book XI
