[035-001] Wearied with toil and infirm with age, Cadmus and his wife retire to Illyricum, and at their own request are changed into Serpents.

[035-002] Meantime the wretched Cadmus mourns, nor knows

[035-003] That they who mortal fell, immortal rose.

[035-004] With a long series of new ills oppress’d,

[035-005] He droops, and all the man forsakes his breast:

[035-006] Strange prodigies confound his frighted eyes;

[035-007] From the fair city, which he raised, he flies;

[035-008] As it misfortune not pursued his race,

[035-009] But only hung o’er that devoted place.

[035-010] Resolved by sea to seek some distant land,

[035-011] At last he safely gain’d the Illyrian strand.

[035-012] Cheerless himself, his consort still he cheers,

[035-013] Hoary, and laden both with woes and years.

[035-014] Then to recount past sorrows they begin,

[035-015] And trace them to the gloomy origin.

[035-016] “That serpent sure was hallow’d,” Cadmus cried,

[035-017] “Which once my spear transfix’d with foolish pride;

[035-018] When the big teeth, a seed before unknown,

[035-019] By me along the wond’ring glebe were sown,

[035-020] And sprouting armies by themselves o’erthrown.

[035-021] If thence the wrath of heaven on me is bent,

[035-022] May heaven conclude it with one sad event;

[035-023] To an extended serpent change the man;”

[035-024] And, while he spoke, the wish’d-for change began.

[035-025] His skin with sea-green spots was varied round,

[035-026] And on his belly prone he press’d the ground;

[035-027] He glitter’d soon with many a golden scale,

[035-028] And his shrunk legs closed in a spiry tail;

[035-029] Arms yet remain’d, remaining arms he spread

[035-030] To his loved wife, and human tears yet shed.

[035-031] “Come, my Harmonia, come, thy face recline

[035-032] Down to my face; still touch what still is mine.

[035-033] O! let these hands, while hands, be gently press’d,

[035-034] While yet the serpent has not all possess’d.”

[035-035] More he had spoke, but strove to speak in vain,

[035-036] The forky tongue refused to tell his pain,

[035-037] And learn’d in hissings only to complain.

[035-038] Then shriek’d Harmonia: “Stay, my Cadmus, stay,

[035-039] Glide not in such a monstrous shape away!

[035-040] Destruction, like impetuous waves, rolls on.

[035-041] Where are thy feet, thy legs, thy shoulders, gone?

[035-042] Changed is thy visage, changed is all thy frame,

[035-043] Cadmus is only Cadmus now in name.

[035-044] Ye gods, my Cadmus to himself restore,

[035-045] Or me like him transform; I ask no more.”

[035-046] The husband serpent show’d he still had thought,

[035-047] With wonted fondness an embrace he sought,

[035-048] Play’d round her neck in many a harmless twist,

[035-049] And lick’d that bosom which, a man, he kiss’d.

[035-050] The lookers-on (for lookers-on there were),

[035-051] Shock’d at the sight, half died away with fear.

[035-052] The transformation was again renew’d,

[035-053] And, like the husband, changed the wife they view’d.

[035-054] Both serpents now, with fold involved in fold,

[035-055] To the next covert amicably roll’d.

[035-056] There curl’d they lie, or wave along the green,

[035-057] Fearless see men, by men are fearless seen,

[035-058] Still mild, and conscious what they once have been.
