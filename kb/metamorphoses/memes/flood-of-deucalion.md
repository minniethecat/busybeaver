# The Great Flood

The Great Flood of Deucalion is the first mass catastrophe in the *Metamorphoses*. Enraged by Lycaon's cannibal banquet, Jupiter resolves to destroy the entire human race — not with fire (which might ignite the heavens) but with water. Only Deucalion and Pyrrha survive, and from the stones they cast behind them, a new race of humanity emerges.

## Characters

| Character | Role |
|-----------|------|
| Jupiter | Divine agent of destruction; enraged by Lycaon's impiety |
| Lycaon | Impious Arcadian king whose cannibal feast triggers the Flood |
| Deucalion | Pious survivor, son of Prometheus |
| Pyrrha | Pious survivor, daughter of Epimetheus |

## Setting

The entire earth, submerged beneath the waters released by Jupiter. Neptune aids the destruction by commanding the rivers to overflow and the seas to rise. Only the summit of Parnassus remains above the waves, where Deucalion and Pyrrha's ark comes to rest.

## Narrative

Jupiter, having witnessed Lycaon's attempt to serve him human flesh, calls a council of the gods and declares his intention to destroy the human race. Neptune floods the earth; the waters rise over fields, cities, and mountains. Deucalion and Pyrrha, the sole survivors, drift to Mount Parnassus. Consulting the oracle of Themis, they receive the cryptic command: "Throw behind you your great mother's bones." Deucalion interprets "mother" as Earth and "bones" as stones; they cast stones behind them, which soften and take human form — Deucalion's stones becoming men, Pyrrha's becoming women.

## Themes

- **Piety as survival**: Deucalion and Pyrrha are saved because they are "innocent, and worshippers of the gods"
- **Destruction and renewal**: The Flood is a second creation, a return to near-Chaos followed by reordering
- **Oracular ambiguity**: Themis's riddle ("your great mother's bones") requires interpretation — transformation through understanding
