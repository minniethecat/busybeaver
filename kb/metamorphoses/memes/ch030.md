[025-001] [[bacchus|Bacchus]] here personates Acoetes, the pilot of a ship which carried away the infant Bacchus from the Isle of Naxos—The crew were changed into sea monsters, but Acaetes was preserved.

[025-002] Him Pentheus view’d with fury in his look,

[025-003] And scarce withheld his hands while thus he spoke:

[025-004] “Vile slave! whom speedy vengeance shall pursue,

[025-005] And terrify thy base seditious crew,

[025-006] Thy country and thy parentage reveal,

[025-007] And why thou join’st in these mad orgies tell.”

[025-008] The captive views him with undaunted eyes,

[025-009] And, arm’d with inward innocence, replies:

[025-010] “From high Maeonia’s rocky shores I came,

[025-011] Of poor descent, Acoetes is my name.

[025-012] My sire was meanly born; no oxen plough’d

[025-013] His fruitful fields, nor in his pastures low’d;

[025-014] His whole estate within the waters lay,

[025-015] With lines and hooks he caught the finny prey;

[025-016] His art was all his livelihood, which he

[025-017] Thus with his dying lips bequeathed to me:

[025-018] ‘In streams, my boy, and rivers, take thy chance,

[025-019] There swims,’ said he, ‘thy whole inheritance.’

[025-020] Long did I live on this poor legacy,

[025-021] Till, tired with rocks and my old native sky,

[025-022] To arts of navigation I inclined,

[025-023] Observed the turns and changes of the wind,

[025-024] Learn’d the fit havens, and began to note

[025-025] The stormy Hyades, the rainy Goat,

[025-026] The bright Taygete, and the shining Bears,

[025-027] With all the sailors’ catalogue of stars.

[025-028] Once, as by chance for Delos I design’d,

[025-029] My vessel, driven by a strong gust of wind,

[025-030] Moor’d in a Chian creek; ashore I went,

[025-031] And all the following night in Chios spent.

[025-032] When morning rose, I sent my mates to bring

[025-033] Supplies of water from a neighb’ring spring,

[025-034] While I the motion of the winds explored;

[025-035] Then summon’d in my crew and went aboard.

[025-036] Opheltes heard my summons, and with joy

[025-037] Brought to the shore a soft and lovely boy,

[025-038] With more than female sweetness in his look,

[025-039] Whom straggling in the neighb’ring fields he took.

[025-040] With fumes of wine the little captive glows,

[025-041] And nods with sleep, and staggers as he goes.

[025-042] “I view’d him nicely, and began to trace

[025-043] Each heavenly frature, each immortal grace,

[025-044] And saw divinity in all his face:

[025-045] ‘I know not who,’ said I, ‘this god should be,

[025-046] But that he is a god I plainly see.

[025-047] And thou, whoe’er thou art, excuse the force

[025-048] These men have used; and O befriend our course!’

[025-049] ‘Pray not for us,’ the nimble Dictys cried,

[025-050] Dictys, that could the main-topmast bestride,

[025-051] And down the ropes with active vigour slide.

[025-052] To the same purpose old Epopeus spoke,

[025-053] Who overlook’d the oars, and timed the stroke:

[025-054] The same the pilot, and the same the rest,

[025-055] Such impious avarice their souls possess’d.

[025-056] ‘Nay, Heaven forbid that I should bear away

[025-057] Within my vessel so divine a prey,’

[025-058] Said I; and stood to hinder their intent,

[025-059] When Lycabas, a wretch for murder sent

[025-060] From Tuscany, to suffer banishment,

[025-061] With his clinch’d fist had struck me overboard,

[025-062] Had not my hands in falling grasp’d a cord.

[025-063] “His base confederates the fact approve,

[025-064] When Bacchus (for ’twas he) began to move,

[025-065] Waked by the noise and clamours which they raised,

[025-066] And shook his drowsy limbs, and round him gazed:

[025-067] ‘What means this noise?’ he cries, ‘am I betray’d?

[025-068] Ah! whither, whither must I be convey’d?’

[025-069] ‘Fear not,’ said Proteus, ‘child, but tell us where

[025-070] You wish to land, and trust our friendly care.’

[025-071] ‘To Naxos then direct your course,’ said he,

[025-072] ‘Naxos a hospitable port shall be

[025-073] To each of you, a joyful home to me.’

[025-074] By every god that rules the sea or sky,

[025-075] The perjured villains promise to comply,

[025-076] And bid me hasten to unmoor the ship.

[025-077] With eager joy I launch into the deep;

[025-078] And, heedless of the fraud, for Naxos stand.

[025-079] They whisper oft, and beckon with the hand,

[025-080] And give me signs, all anxious for their prey,

[025-081] To tack about and steer another way.

[025-082] ‘Then let some other to my post succeed,’

[025-083] Said I, ‘I’m guiltless of so foul a deed.’

[025-084] ‘What!’ says Ethalion, ‘must the ship’s whole crew

[025-085] Follow your humour, and depend on you?’

[025-086] And straight himself he seated at the prore,

[025-087] And tack’d about and sought another shore.

[025-088] “The beauteous youth now found himself betray’d,

[025-089] And from the deck the rising waves survey’d,

[025-090] And seem’d to weep, and as he wept he said:

[025-091] ‘And do you thus my easy faith beguile?

[025-092] Thus do ye bear me to my native isle?

[025-093] Will such a multitude of men employ

[025-094] Their strength against a weak, defenceless boy?’

[025-095] “In vain did I the godlike youth deplore,

[025-096] The more I begg’d, they thwarted me the more.

[025-097] And now by all the gods in heaven, that hear

[025-098] This solemn oath, by Bacchus’ self I swear,

[025-099] The mighty miracle that did ensue,

[025-100] Although it seems beyond belief, is true.

[025-101] The vessel, fix’d and rooted in the flood,

[025-102] Unmoved by all the beating billows, stood.

[025-103] In vain the mariners would plough the main

[025-104] With sails unfurl’d, and strike their oars in vain;

[025-105] Around their oars a twining ivy cleaves,

[025-106] And climbs the mast, and hides the cords in leaves:

[025-107] The sails are cover’d with a cheerful green,

[025-108] And berries in the fruitful canvass seen.

[025-109] Amid the waves a sudden forest rears

[025-110] Its verdant head, and a new spring appears.

[025-111] “The god we now behold with open’d eyes;

[025-112] A herd of spotted panthers round him lies

[025-113] In glaring forms, the grapy clusters spread

[025-114] On his fair brows, and dangle on his head.

[025-115] And while he frowns and brandishes his spear,

[025-116] My mates, surprised with madness or with fear,

[025-117] Leap’d overboard; first perjur’d Madon found

[025-118] Rough scales and fins his stiff’ning sides surround.

[025-119] “ ‘Ah! what,’ cries one, ‘has thus transform’d thy look?’

[025-120] Straight his own mouth grew wider as he spoke:

[025-121] And now himself he views with like surprise.

[025-122] Still at his oar the industrious Libys plies;

[025-123] But, as he plies, each busy arm shrinks in,

[025-124] And by degrees is fashion’d to a fin.

[025-125] Another, as he catches at a cord,

[025-126] Misses his arms, and, tumbling overboard,

[025-127] With his broad fins and forky tail he laves

[025-128] The rising surge, and flounces in the waves.

[025-129] Thus all my crew transform’d around the ship,

[025-130] Or dive below, or on the surface leap,

[025-131] And spout the waves, and wanton in the deep.

[025-132] Full nineteen sailors did the ship convey,

[025-133] A shoal of nineteen dolphins round her play.

[025-134] I only in my proper shape appear,

[025-135] Speechless with wonder, and half dead with fear,

[025-136] Till Bacchus kindly bid me fear no more.

[025-137] With him I landed on the Chian shore,

[025-138] And him shall ever gratefully adore.”

[025-139] “This forging slave,” says Pentheus, “would prevail

[025-140] O’er our just fury by a far-fetch’d tale:

[025-141] Go; let him feel the whips, the swords, the fire,

[025-142] And in the tortures of the rack expire.”

[025-143] The officious servants hurry him away,

[025-144] And the poor captive in a dungeon lay.

[025-145] But, while the whips and tortures are prepared,

[025-146] The gates fly open, of themselves unbarr’d;

[025-147] At liberty the unfetter’d captive stands,

[025-148] And flings the loosen’d shackles from his hands.
