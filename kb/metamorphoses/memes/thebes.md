# Thebes

Thebes is the most frequently appearing Greek city in the *Metamorphoses*, dominating Books III–VI. Founded by Cadmus after his search for Europa, Thebes is the birthplace of Bacchus and the setting for the punishments of Pentheus, the daughters of Minyas, and Niobe. The city functions as a theater of transformation — a place where the relationship between mortals and gods is tested with particular violence.

## Mythological Significance

Thebes is the **testing ground** for the recognition of divine power. Three times in Books III–VI, Theban rulers or citizens deny a god's divinity — Pentheus denies Bacchus, the Minyades refuse his worship, Niobe boasts superiority over Latona — and each time the punishment is catastrophic. Thebes thus embodies the danger of mortal proximity to divine power without proper reverence.
