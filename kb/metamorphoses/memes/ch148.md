[132-001] The disrespectful treatment of the wood nymphs by Appulus is punished by his transformation into a wild olive-tree.

[132-002] Thus Diomedes. Venulus withdraws;

[132-003] Unsped the service of the common cause.

[132-004] Puteoli he passes, and survey’d

[132-005] A cave long honour’d for its awful shade.

[132-006] Here trembling reeds exclude the piercing ray,

[132-007] Here streams in gentle falls through windings stray,

[132-008] And with a passing breath cool zephyrs play.

[132-009] The goatherd god frequents the silent place,

[132-010] As once the wood nymphs of the sylvan race,

[132-011] Till Appulus with a dishonest air,

[132-012] And gross behaviour, banish’d thence the fair:

[132-013] The bold buffoon, whene’er they tread the green,

[132-014] Their motion mimics, but with jest obscene.

[132-015] Loose language oft he utters; but ere long

[132-016] A bark in filmy network binds his tongue.

[132-017] Thus changed, a base wild olive he remains;

[132-018] The shrub the coarseness of the clown retains.
