[037-001] [[perseus|Perseus]], after the conquest of the Gorgons, passes by the palace of Atlas, and solicits the rites of hospitality, which are refused—Perseus exhibits the head of [[medusa|Medusa]], and the monarch is instantly changed into a large mountain, on which the world is supposed to rest.

[037-002] Thence Perseus, like a cloud, by storms was driven,

[037-003] Through all the expanse beneath the cope of heaven.

[037-004] The jarring winds unable to control,

[037-005] He saw the southern and the northern pole;

[037-006] And eastward thrice, and westward thrice, was whirl’d,

[037-007] And from the skies survey’d the nether world.

[037-008] But when gray evening show’d the verge of night,

[037-009] He fear’d in darkness to pursue his flight.

[037-010] He poised his pinions, and forgot to soar,

[037-011] And, sinking, closed them on the Hesperian shore

[037-012] Then begg’d to rest, till Lucifer begun

[037-013] To wake the morn, the morn to wake the sun.

[037-014] Here Atlas reign’d, of more than human size,

[037-015] And in his kingdom the world’s limit lies.

[037-016] Here Titan bids his wearied coursers sleep,

[037-017] And cools the burning axle in the deep:

[037-018] The mighty monarch, uncontroll’d, alone

[037-019] His sceptre sways: no neighb’ring states are known:

[037-020] A thousand flocks on shady mountains fed,

[037-021] A thousand herds o’er grassy plains were spread:

[037-022] Here wondrous trees their shining stores unfold,

[037-023] Their shining stores too wondrous to be told,

[037-024] Their leaves, their branches, and their apples, gold.

[037-025] Then Perseus the gigantic prince address’d,

[037-026] Humbly implored a hospitable rest:

[037-027] “If bold exploits thy admiration fire,”

[037-028] He said, “I fancy mine thou wilt admire:

[037-029] Or, if the glory of a race can move,

[037-030] Not mean my glory, for I spring from [[jupiter|Jove]].”

[037-031] At this confession Atlas ghastly stared,

[037-032] Mindful of what an oracle declared,

[037-033] That the dark womb of time conceal’d a day,

[037-034] Which should, disclosed, the bloomy gold betray;

[037-035] All should at once be ravish’d from his eyes,

[037-036] And Jove’s own progeny enjoy the prize.

[037-037] For this, the fruit he loftily immured,

[037-038] And a fierce dragon the strait pass secured:

[037-039] For this, all strangers he forbade to land,

[037-040] And drove them from the inhospitable strand.

[037-041] To Perseus then: “Fly, quickly fly, this coast,

[037-042] Nor falsely dare thy acts and race to boast.”

[037-043] In vain the hero for one night entreats,

[037-044] Threat’ning he storms, and next adds force to threats.

[037-045] By strength not Perseus could himself defend;

[037-046] For who in strength with Atlas could contend?

[037-047] “But since short rest to me thou wilt not give,

[037-048] A gift of endless rest from me receive.”

[037-049] He said, and backward turn’d, no more conceal’d

[037-050] The present, and Medusa’s head reveal’d.

[037-051] Soon the high Atlas a high mountain stood;

[037-052] His locks and beard became a leafy wood;

[037-053] His hands and shoulders into ridges went;

[037-054] The summit-head still crown’d the steep ascent;

[037-055] His bones a solid, rocky hardness gain’d,

[037-056] He, thus immensely grown (as [[fate|Fate]] ordain’d),

[037-057] The stars, the heavens, and all the gods, sustain’d.
