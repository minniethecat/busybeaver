# Phrygia

Phrygia, in central Asia Minor, is the setting for the *Metamorphoses*' most celebrated story of piety and transformation through hospitality. The humble cottage of Baucis and Philemon, where Jupiter and Mercury are welcomed, becomes a temple — and the aged couple themselves become the oak and linden that still mark the sacred site.

## Mythological Significance

Phrygia is the **landscape of piety**. The Baucis and Philemon episode transforms not just the couple (into trees) and their cottage (into a temple) but the entire region: the surrounding countryside is destroyed by the Flood, sparing only the sacred site. Phrygia thus represents the principle that piety transforms place — the humble becomes sacred, the poor become eternal.
