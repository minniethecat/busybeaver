[127-001] Achaemenides, a companion of Ulysses, is left behind on the coast of Sicily, where Aeneas finds him on his voyage to Italy.

[127-002] Thus Achaemenides: “With thanks I name

[127-003] Aeneas, and his piety proclaim.

[127-004] I ’scaped the cyclop through the hero’s aid,

[127-005] Else in his maw my mangled limbs had laid.

[127-006] When first your navy under sail he found,

[127-007] He raved till Aetna labour’d with the sound;

[127-008] Raging, he stalk’d along the mountain’s side,

[127-009] And vented clouds of breath at every stride;

[127-010] His staff a mountain ash, and in the clouds,

[127-011] Oft as he walks, his grisly front he shrowds;

[127-012] Eyeless he groped about with vengeful haste,

[127-013] And justled promontories as he pass’d:

[127-014] Then heaved a rock’s high summit to the main,

[127-015] And bellow’d like some bursting hurricane:

[127-016] “ ‘Oh! could I seize Ulysses in his flight,

[127-017] How unlamented were my loss of sight!

[127-018] These jaws should piecemeal tear each panting vein,

[127-019] Grind every crackling bone, and pound his brain.’

[127-020] “As thus he raved my joints with horror shook;

[127-021] The tide of blood my chilling heart forsook;

[127-022] I saw him once disgorge huge morsels, raw,

[127-023] Of wretches undigested in his maw.

[127-024] From the pale breathless trunks whole limbs he tore,

[127-025] His beard all clotted with o’erflowing gore.

[127-026] My anxious hours I pass’d in caves, my food

[127-027] Was forest fruits and wildings of the wood;

[127-028] At length a sail I wafted, and aboard

[127-029] My fortune found a hospitable lord.

[127-030] “Now, in return, your own adventures tell,

[127-031] And what, since first you put to sea, befell.”
