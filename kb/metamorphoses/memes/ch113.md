[100-001] [[apollo|Apollo]] and Neptune engage with Laomedon to build the walls of [[troy|Troy]] for a stipulated sum, which he refuses to pay: for which breach of faith his territories are laid waste by the encroachments of the sea—He is delivered from the rage of a sea monster by the valour of Hercules, whom he in like manner defrauds: the hero is therefore obliged to besiege Troy, and take it by force of arms.

[100-002] Phoebus, with full revenge, from Tmolus flies,

[100-003] Darts through the air, and cleaves the liquid skies;

[100-004] Near Hellespont he lights, and treads the plains

[100-005] Where great Laomedon sole monarch reigns;

[100-006] Where, built between the two projecting strands,

[100-007] To Panomphaean [[jupiter|Jove]] an altar stands;

[100-008] Here first aspiring thoughts the king employ

[100-009] To found the lofty towers of future Troy.

[100-010] The work, from schemes magnificent begun,

[100-011] At vast expense, was slowly carried on;

[100-012] Which Phoebus seeing, with the trident god,

[100-013] Who rules the swelling surges with his nod,

[100-014] Assuming each a mortal shape, combine,

[100-015] At a set price, to finish his design.

[100-016] The work was built, the king their price denies,

[100-017] And his injustice backs with perjuries:

[100-018] This Neptune could not brook, but drove the main,

[100-019] A mighty deluge, o’er the Phrygian plain;

[100-020] ’Twas all a sea, the waters of the deep

[100-021] From every vale the copious harvest sweep;

[100-022] The briny billows overflow the soil,

[100-023] Ravage the fields, and mock the ploughman’s toil.

[100-024] Nor this appeased the god’s revengeful mind,

[100-025] For still a greater plague remains behind;

[100-026] A huge sea monster lodges on the sands,

[100-027] And the king’s daughter for his prey demands.

[100-028] To him, that saved the, damsel, was decreed

[100-029] A set of horses of the sun’s fine breed;

[100-030] But, when Alcides from the rock untied

[100-031] The trembling fair, the ransom was denied.

[100-032] He, in revenge, the new-built walls attack’d,

[100-033] And the twice-perjured city bravely sack’d.

[100-034] Telamon aided; and, in justice, shared

[100-035] Part of the plunder as his due reward:

[100-036] The princess, rescued late, with all her charms,

[100-037] Hesione, was yielded to his arms:

[100-038] For Peleus, with a goddess bride, was more

[100-039] Proud of his spouse than of his birth before;

[100-040] Grandsons to Jove there might be more than one,

[100-041] But he the goddess had beloved alone.
