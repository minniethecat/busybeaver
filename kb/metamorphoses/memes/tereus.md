# Tereus

Tereus, king of Thrace and husband of Procne, is the perpetrator of one of the most brutal crimes in Ovid's *Metamorphoses*: the rape and mutilation of his sister-in-law Philomela. His violence sets in motion a revenge of equal ferocity — Procne's murder of their son Itys, served to Tereus as a feast — and his eventual transformation into a lapwing, a bird that Ovid describes as armed with a beak-like sword, forever marked by the violence he unleashed.

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Tereus enters Book VI of the *Metamorphoses* as an ally of Athens, summoned by Pandion to aid in war. In reward, Pandion gives him his daughter Procne in marriage. Years later, Procne asks Tereus to fetch her sister Philomela from Athens. The moment Tereus sees Philomela, he is consumed with lust. He abducts her to a remote hut in the woods, rapes her, and — when she threatens to tell the world of his crime — cuts out her tongue to silence her. He tells Procne that Philomela has died (060-001). But Philomela weaves a tapestry depicting the crime and sends it to her sister. Procne, driven to madness, rescues Philomela during the Bacchic revels, and the two sisters exact a terrible revenge: Procne kills their son Itys and serves his flesh to Tereus at a banquet. When Philomela reveals the severed head of Itys, Tereus draws his sword to kill them both — but before he can strike, all three are transformed: Tereus into a lapwing (a crested bird with a beak like a blade), Procne into a swallow, and Philomela into a nightingale (060-001).

### Key Episodes

- **Marriage to Procne**: Tereus aids Athens in war and receives Procne, daughter of Pandion, as his bride
- **The Abduction of Philomela**: Sent to fetch Philomela from Athens, Tereus is overcome with lust at first sight and abducts her
- **The Rape and Mutilation**: Tereus rapes Philomela and cuts out her tongue to prevent her from revealing his crime
- **The Cannibal Feast**: Procne serves their son Itys to Tereus as a banquet dish in revenge
- **The Transformation**: Pursuing the sisters with his sword, Tereus is transformed into a lapwing (060-001)

## Transformation

- **Form**: Man (Thracian king) → Lapwing (a crested bird)
- **Agent**: The gods, in response to the collective tragedy
- **Cause**: Divine intervention halting the cycle of violence — the transformation preserves the fury of his drawn sword in the bird's crest
- **Source**: (060-001)
