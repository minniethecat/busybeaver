[030-001] Clytie, being deserted by [[apollo|Apollo]], pines away, and is changed into a sunflower, which still turns its head towards the sun, in token of her love.

[030-002] “Though guilty Clytie thus the Sun betray’d,

[030-003] By too much passion she was guilty made.

[030-004] Excess of love begot excess of grief,

[030-005] Grief fondly bade her hence to hope relief.

[030-006] But angry Phoebus hears unmoved her sighs,

[030-007] And scornful from her loath’d embraces flies.

[030-008] All day, all night, in trackless wilds alone

[030-009] She pined, and taught the listening rocks her moan.

[030-010] On the bare earth she lies, her bosom bare,

[030-011] Loose her attire, dishevell’d is her hair.

[030-012] Nine times the morn unbarr’d the gates of light,

[030-013] As oft were spread the alternate shades of night,

[030-014] So long no sustenance the mourner knew,

[030-015] Unless she drank her tears, or suck’d the dew.

[030-016] She turn’d about, but rose not from the ground,

[030-017] Turn’d to the sun still as he roll’d his round;

[030-018] On his bright face hung her desiring eyes,

[030-019] Till, fix’d to earth, she strove in vain to rise;

[030-020] Her looks their paleness in a flower retain’d,

[030-021] But here and there some purple streaks they gain’d.

[030-022] Still the loved object the fond leaves pursue,

[030-023] Still move their root the moving sun to view,

[030-024] And in the heliotrope the nymph is true.”

[030-025] The sisters heard these wonders with surprise,

[030-026] But part received them as romantic lies,

[030-027] And pertly rallied, that they could not see

[030-028] In powers divine so vast an energy.

[030-029] Part own’d true gods such miracles might do,

[030-030] But own’d not [[bacchus|Bacchus]] one among the true.

[030-031] At last a common, just request they make,

[030-032] And beg Alcithoe her turn to take.

[030-033] “I will,” said she, “and please you if I can;”

[030-034] Then shot her shuttle swift, and thus began:

[030-035] “The fate of Daphnis is a fate too known,

[030-036] Whom an enamour’d nymph transform’d to stone;

[030-037] Because she fear’d another nymph might see

[030-038] The lovely youth, and love as much as she:

[030-039] So strange the madness is of jealousy!

[030-040] Nor shall I tell what changes Scython made,

[030-041] And how he walk’d a man, or tripp’d a maid.

[030-042] You too would peevish frown, and patience want

[030-043] To hear, how Celmis grew an adamant:

[030-044] He once was dear to [[jupiter|Jove]], and saw of old

[030-045] Jove when a child; but what he saw he told.

[030-046] Crocus and Smilax may be turn’d to flowers,

[030-047] And the Curetes spring from bounteous showers.

[030-048] I pass a hundred legends stale as these,

[030-049] And with sweet novelty your taste will please.”
