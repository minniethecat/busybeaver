[041-001] [[minerva|Minerva]] visits Mount Helicon, the seat of the Muses, by whom she is hospitably entertained.

[041-002] Thus far Minerva was content to rove

[041-003] With [[perseus|Perseus]], offspring of her father [[jupiter|Jove]]:

[041-004] Now hid in clouds Seriphus she forsook,

[041-005] And to the Theban towers her journey took;

[041-006] Cythnos and Gyaros, lying to the right,

[041-007] She pass’d unheeded in her eager flight;

[041-008] And choosing first on Helicon to rest,

[041-009] The virgin muses in these words address’d:

[041-010] “Me the strange tidings of a new-found spring,

[041-011] Ye learned sisters, to this mountain bring.

[041-012] If all the true that Fame’s wide rumours tell,

[041-013] ’Twas Pegasus discover’d first your well;

[041-014] Whose piercing hoof gave the soft earth a blow,

[041-015] Which broke the surface where these waters flow.

[041-016] I saw that horse by miracle obtain

[041-017] Life, from the blood of dire [[medusa|Medusa]] slain;

[041-018] And now this equal prodigy to view,

[041-019] From distant isles to famed Boeotia few.”

[041-020] The muse Urania said: “Whatever cause

[041-021] So great a goddess to this mansion draws,

[041-022] Our shades are happy with so bright a guest;

[041-023] You, queen, are welcome, and we muses bless’d.

[041-024] What Fame has publish’d of our spring is true;

[041-025] Thanks for our spring to Pegasus are due.”

[041-026] Then with becoming courtesy, she led

[041-027] The curious stranger to their fountain’s head,

[041-028] Who long survey’d, with wonder and delight,

[041-029] Their sacred water, charming to the sight;

[041-030] Their ancient groves, dark grottoes, shady bowers,

[041-031] And smiling plains, adorn’d with various flowers.

[041-032] “O happy muses!” she with rapture cried,

[041-033] “Who, safe from cares, on this fair hill reside;

[041-034] Bless’d in your seat, and free, yourselves to please

[041-035] With joys of study, and with glorious ease.”
