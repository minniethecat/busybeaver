# Argus

Argus Panoptes (the "All-Seeing") is a hundred-eyed giant whom Juno appointed as the guardian of Io, a nymph transformed by Jupiter into a heifer to conceal his affair. The hundred eyes of Argus — some always open, even in sleep — made him the perfect watchman. Jupiter, desperate to free Io, sent Mercury (Hermes) to dispatch the giant. Disguised as a shepherd, Mercury charmed Argus with the story of the reed-pipe (Syrinx) and, once all hundred eyes had closed in sleep, cut off his head. Juno, mourning her faithful servant, gathered his eyes and set them into the tail-feathers of her sacred bird, the peacock — an aetiology for the peacock's eye-spotted plumage.

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Argus appears in Book I as part of the Io narrative. After Jupiter transforms Io into a heifer to hide his infidelity, Juno — suspicious of the beautiful white cow — demands it as a gift and appoints Argus as its guardian. The giant tethers Io and watches her ceaselessly. Jupiter, unable to bear Io's suffering, sends Mercury, who approaches Argus disguised as a goatherd. Mercury lulls Argus to sleep with the story of Pan's pursuit of the nymph Syrinx — a tale within a tale, itself a metamorphosis narrative — and then beheads the sleeping giant. Juno, in grief and gratitude, places Argus's hundred eyes in the tail of the peacock, where they remain as a celestial memorial.

### Key Episodes

- **Appointment as Guardian**: Juno sets the hundred-eyed Argus to watch over Io, the transformed heifer
- **Mercury's Ruse**: Mercury, disguised as a shepherd, tells Argus the story of Syrinx while playing the reed-pipe, lulling all hundred eyes to sleep
- **Death and Memorial**: Mercury beheads Argus; Juno transfers his eyes to the peacock's tail

## Transformation

Argus himself is not transformed in life — but after death, his hundred eyes become the eye-spots of the peacock's plumage. This is transformation as memorial: Juno preserves her servant's defining feature in her own sacred bird, turning death into an enduring visual emblem.

- **Form**: Argus's hundred eyes → Peacock's tail-feathers
- **Agent**: Juno, mourning her faithful servant
- **Cause**: Memorial — preserving Argus's essence after death
- **Source**: Book I (024-001)
