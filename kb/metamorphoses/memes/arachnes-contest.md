# Arachne's Weaving Contest

The weaving contest between Arachne and Minerva is the *Metamorphoses*' definitive exploration of art, power, and the boundaries between mortal craft and divine authority. Arachne's tapestry, depicting twenty-one scenes of divine seduction, is technically perfect — but its content enrages Minerva, who transforms the mortal into a spider.

## Characters

| Character | Role |
|-----------|------|
| Arachne | Lydian weaver of consummate skill; refuses to yield to Minerva |
| Minerva | Goddess of weaving, appears disguised, challenged by the mortal |

## Setting

Arachne's loom in her humble Lydian home. The two tapestries — Minerva's and Arachne's — are the competing representations of divine power.

## Narrative

Minerva, hearing of Arachne's skill, appears disguised as an old woman and warns the mortal to yield. Arachne refuses. They set up looms and weave. Minerva depicts the gods in majesty — herself winning Athens from Neptune, and four examples of mortals punished for hubris. Arachne depicts twenty-one instances of divine seduction: Europa and the bull, Leda and the swan, Danaë and the shower of gold, and others. Minerva, finding no technical flaw, tears the tapestry and strikes Arachne with her shuttle. When Arachne hangs herself, Minerva transforms her into a spider.
