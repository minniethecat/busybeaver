[036-001] Acrisius, the grandfather of [[perseus|Perseus]], is at length compelled to acknowledge the divinity of [[bacchus|Bacchus]], and to commemorate the splendid achievements of his descendant.

[036-002] Yet though this harsh inglorious fate they found,

[036-003] Each in the deathless grandson lived renown’d.

[036-004] Through conquer’d India Bacchus nobly rode,

[036-005] And Greece with temples hail’d the conquering god.

[036-006] In Argos only proud Acrisius reign’d,

[036-007] Who all the consecrated rites profaned.

[036-008] Audacious wretch! thus Bacchus to deny,

[036-009] And the great [[jupiter|Thunderer]]’s great son defy!

[036-010] Nor him alone: thy daughter vainly strove

[036-011] Brave Perseus of celestial stem to prove,

[036-012] And herself pregnant by a golden Jove.

[036-013] Yet this was true, and truth in time prevails;

[036-014] Acrisius now his unbelief bewails.

[036-015] His former thought an impious thought he found,

[036-016] And both the hero and the god were own’d.

[036-017] He saw, already, one in heaven was placed,

[036-018] And one with more than mortal triumphs graced.

[036-019] The victor Perseus, with the Gorgon head,

[036-020] O’er Libyan sands his airy journey sped.

[036-021] The gory drops distill’d, as swift he flew,

[036-022] And from each drop envenom’d serpents grew.

[036-023] The mischiefs brooded on the barren plains,

[036-024] And still the unhappy fruitfulness remains.
