[086-001] Hebe, at the request of Hercules, renews the youth of her son Iolaus.

[086-002] While Iole the fatal change declares,

[086-003] Alcmena’s pitying hand oft wiped her tears.

[086-004] Grief too stream’d down her cheeks; soon sorrow flies,

[086-005] And rising joy the trickling moisture dries,

[086-006] Lo Iolaus stands before their eyes.

[086-007] A youth he stood, and the soft down began

[086-008] O’er his smooth chin to spread, and promise man.

[086-009] Hebe submitted to her husband’s prayers,

[086-010] Instill’d new vigour, and restored his years.
