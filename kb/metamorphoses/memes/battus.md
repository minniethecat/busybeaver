# Battus

Battus, a shepherd of Pylos, is the protagonist of one of the briefest and most morally pointed transformation stories in the *Metamorphoses*. When Mercury steals Apollo's cattle, Battus witnesses the theft and promises the god — who rewards him with a heifer — that he will keep silent, swearing that a stone would sooner betray the secret than he would. Mercury, testing his fidelity, returns in disguise and offers a bribe for information about the stolen cattle. Battus immediately reveals the location and is transformed into a touchstone (Latin: *index*), the very stone to which he had compared his discretion — a metamorphosis of exquisite Ovidian irony in which the punishment perfectly mirrors the crime.

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Battus's story appears in Book II as a brief comic interlude within the narrative of Mercury's adventures. After Mercury (at Jupiter's command) has dealt with the hundred-eyed Argus, he turns to the simpler business of stealing Apollo's herds grazing on the plains of Pylos. Battus, an old shepherd, sees Mercury driving the cattle away and, upon being promised a heifer, swears that "that stone there will tell of your theft before I do." Mercury pretends to leave, then returns disguised as a different man offering a reward for information. Battus immediately points out the cattle's hiding place, and Mercury, revealing his identity, laughs at the perjured shepherd and transforms him into a flint-like touchstone — a hard stone that, to this day, is used to test gold for its purity, forever identifying the false from the true (016-001). The story, told in barely fifty lines, is a miniature masterpiece of Ovid's metamorphic wit: the punishment is not arbitrary violence but a literalization of Battus's own metaphor.

### Key Episodes

- **The Promise of Silence**: Battus witnesses Mercury stealing Apollo's cattle and swears secrecy, comparing his discretion to a silent stone
- **The Bribe and the Betrayal**: Mercury returns in disguise and offers a bribe — Battus immediately reveals the hiding place (016-001)
- **The Transformation**: Mercury reveals himself and transforms Battus into a touchstone — the stone to which he had compared his fidelity

## Transformation

- **Form**: Man (shepherd) → Touchstone (flint used to test gold)
- **Agent**: Mercury, the god of thieves and tricksters
- **Cause**: Perjury and venality — Battus's willingness to sell his word for profit
