[020-001] Actaeon, in pursuing the amusements of the chase, sees Diana and her nymphs bathing near Gargaphia; for which he is changed into a stag by the angry goddess, and is devoured by his own dogs.

[020-002] In a fair chase a shady mountain stood,

[020-003] Well stored with game, and mark’d with trails of blood;

[020-004] Here did the huntsmen, till the heat of day,

[020-005] Pursue the stag, and load themselves with prey;

[020-006] When thus Actaeon calling to the rest:

[020-007] “My friends,” said he, “our sport is at the best,

[020-008] The sun is high advanced, and downward sheds

[020-009] His burning beams directly on our heads;

[020-010] Then by consent abstain from further spoils,

[020-011] Call off the dogs, and gather up the toils,

[020-012] And ere to-morrow’s sun begins his race

[020-013] Take the cool morning to renew the chase.”

[020-014] They all consent, and in a cheerful train

[020-015] The jolly huntsmen, laden with the slain,

[020-016] Return in triumph from the sultry plain.

[020-017] Down in a vale with pine and cypress clad,

[020-018] Refresh’d with gentle winds, and brown with shade,

[020-019] The chaste Diana’s private haunt there stood,

[020-020] Full in the centre of the darksome wood,

[020-021] A spacious grotto, all around o’ergrown

[020-022] With hoary moss, and arch’d with pumice-stone.

[020-023] From out its rocky clefts the waters flow,

[020-024] And trickling swell into a lake below.

[020-025] Nature had everywhere so play’d her part,

[020-026] That everywhere she seem’d to vie with art.

[020-027] Here the bright goddess, toil’d and chafed with heat,

[020-028] Was wont to bathe her in the cool retreat.

[020-029] Here did she now with all her train resort,

[020-030] Panting with heat, and breathless from the sport;

[020-031] Her armour-bearer laid her bow aside,

[020-032] Some loosed her sandals, some her veil untied;

[020-033] Each busy nymph her proper part undress’d,

[020-034] While Crocale, more handy than the rest,

[020-035] Gather’d her flowing hair, and in a noose

[020-036] Bound it together, while her own hung loose;

[020-037] Five of the more ignoble sort, by turns,

[020-038] Fetch up the water, and unlade the urns.

[020-039] Now all undress’d the shining goddess stood,

[020-040] When young Actaeon wilder’d in the wood,

[020-041] To the cool grot by his hard fate betray’d,

[020-042] The fountains fill’d with naked nymphs survey’d.

[020-043] The frighted virgins shriek’d at the surprise

[020-044] (The forest echo’d with their piercing cries),

[020-045] Then in a huddle round their goddess press’d;

[020-046] She, proudly eminent above the rest,

[020-047] With blushes glow’d; such blushes as adorn

[020-048] The ruddy welkin or the purple morn;

[020-049] And though the crowding nymphs her body hide,

[020-050] Half backward shrunk, and view’d him from aside.

[020-051] Surprised, at first she would have snatch’d her bow,

[020-052] But sees the circling waters round her flow;

[020-053] These in the hollow of her hand she took,

[020-054] And dash’d them in his face, while thus she spoke:

[020-055] “Tell, if thou canst, the wondrous sight disclosed,

[020-056] A goddess naked to thy view exposed.”

[020-057] This said, the man began to disappear

[020-058] By slow degrees, and ended in a deer.

[020-059] A rising horn on either brow he wears,

[020-060] And stretches out his neck, and pricks his ears;

[020-061] Rough is his skin, with sudden hairs o’ergrown,

[020-062] His bosom pants with fears before unknown;

[020-063] Transform’d at length, he flies away in haste,

[020-064] And wonders why he flies away so fast.

[020-065] But, as by chance within a neighb’ring brook,

[020-066] He saw his branching horns and alter’d look,

[020-067] Wretched Actaeon! in a doleful tone

[020-068] He tried to speak, but only gave a groan;

[020-069] And as he wept, within the watery glass

[020-070] He saw the big round drops, with silent pace,

[020-071] Run trickling down a savage hairy face.

[020-072] What should he do? Or seek his old abodes,

[020-073] Or herd among the deer and skulk in woods?

[020-074] Here shame dissuades him, there his fear prevails,

[020-075] And each by turns his aching heart assails.

[020-076] As he thus ponders, he behind him spies

[020-077] His op’ning hounds, and now he hears their cries:

[020-078] A gen’rous pack, or to maintain the chase,

[020-079] Or snuff the vapour from the scented grass.

[020-080] He bounded off with fear, and swiftly ran

[020-081] O’er craggy mountains and the flow’ry plain,

[020-082] Through brakes and thickets forced his way, and flew

[020-083] Through many a ring where once he did pursue.

[020-084] In vain he oft endeavour’d to proclaim

[020-085] His new misfortune, and to tell his name;

[020-086] Nor voice, nor words, the brutal tongue supplies,

[020-087] From shouting men, and horns, and dogs, he flies,

[020-088] Deafen’d and stunn’d with their promiscuous cries.

[020-089] When now the fleetest of the pack, that press’d

[020-090] Close at his heels and sprung before the rest,

[020-091] Had fasten’d on him, straight another pair

[020-092] Hung on his wounded haunch, and held him there,

[020-093] Till all the pack came up, and every hound

[020-094] Tore the sad huntsman grovelling on the ground,

[020-095] Who now appear’d but one continued wound.

[020-096] With dropping tears his bitter fate he moans,

[020-097] And fills the mountain with his dying groans.

[020-098] His servants with a piteous look he spies,

[020-099] And turns about his supplicating eyes.

[020-100] His servants, ignorant of what had chanced,

[020-101] With eager haste and joyful shouts advanced,

[020-102] And call’d their lord, Actaeon, to the game;

[020-103] He shook his head in answer to the name;

[020-104] He heard, but wish’d he had indeed been gone;

[020-105] Or only to have stood a looker-on:

[020-106] But to his grief he finds himself too near,

[020-107] And feels his ravenous dogs with fury tear

[020-108] Their wretched master panting in a deer.
