[085-001] Dryope, who incautiously plucks a branch of the lotus-tree for the amusement of her infant son, is herself transformed by the angry sylvan deities into a tree of the same species.

[085-002] She said, and for her lost Galanthis sighs;

[085-003] When the fair consort of her son replies;

[085-004] “Since you a servant’s ravish’d form bemoan,

[085-005] And kindly sigh for sorrows not your own,

[085-006] Let me (tears and grief permit) relate

[085-007] A nearer wo, a sister’s stranger fate.

[085-008] No nymph of all Oechalia could compare,

[085-009] For beauteous form, with Dryope the fair;

[085-010] Her tender mother’s only hope and pride

[085-011] (Myself the offspring of a second bride),

[085-012] This nymph, compress’d by him who rules the day,

[085-013] Whom [[delphi|Delphi]] and the Delian isle obey,

[085-014] Andraemon loved; and bless’d in all those charms

[085-015] That pleased a god, succeeded to her arms.

[085-016] “A lake there was, with shelving banks around,

[085-017] Whose verdant summit fragrant myrtles crown’d.

[085-018] Those shades, unknowing of [[fate|the Fates]], she sought,

[085-019] And to the Naiads flowery garlands brought;

[085-020] Her smiling babe (a pleasing charge) she press’d

[085-021] Between her arms, and nourish’d at her breast.

[085-022] Not distant far a watery lotus grows;

[085-023] The spring was new, and all the verdant boughs,

[085-024] Adorn’d with blossoms, promised fruits that vie

[085-025] In glowing colours with the Tyrian die.

[085-026] Of these she cropp’d, to please her infant son,

[085-027] And I myself the same rash act had done,

[085-028] But, lo! I saw (as near her side I stood)

[085-029] The violated blossoms drop with blood;

[085-030] Upon the tree I cast a frightful look,

[085-031] The trembling tree with sudden horror shook:

[085-032] Lotis the nymph (if rural tales be true),

[085-033] As from Priapus’ lawless love she flew,

[085-034] Forsook her form; and fixing here became

[085-035] A flowery plant, which still preserves her name.

[085-036] “This change unknown, astonish’d at the sight,

[085-037] My trembling sister strove to urge the flight;

[085-038] Yet first the pardon of the nymphs implored,

[085-039] And those offended sylvan powers adored:

[085-040] But when she backward would have fled, she found

[085-041] Her stiff’ning feet were rooted to the ground:

[085-042] In vain to free her fasten’d feet she strove,

[085-043] And as she struggles only moves above;

[085-044] She feels the encroaching bark around her grow

[085-045] By slow degrees, and cover all below.

[085-046] Surprised at this, her trembling hand she heaves

[085-047] To rend her hair; her hand is fill’d with leaves;

[085-048] Where late was hair, the shooting leaves are seen

[085-049] To rise, and shade her with a sudden green.

[085-050] The child Amphisus, to her bosom press’d,

[085-051] Perceived a colder and a harder breast,

[085-052] And found the springs, that ne’er till then denied

[085-053] Their milky moisture, on a sudden dried.

[085-054] I saw, unhappy, what I now relate,

[085-055] And stood the helpless witness of thy fate:

[085-056] Embraced thy boughs, the rising bark delay’d,

[085-057] There wish’d to grow, and mingle shade with shade.

[085-058] “Behold Andraemon and the unhappy sire

[085-059] Appear, and for their Dryope inquire;

[085-060] A springing tree for Dryope they find,

[085-061] And print warm kisses on the panting rind;

[085-062] Prostrate, with tears their kindred plant bedew,

[085-063] And close embraced, as to the roots they grew;

[085-064] The face was all that now remain’d of thee;

[085-065] No more a woman, nor yet quite a tree:

[085-066] Thy branches hung with humid pearls appear,

[085-067] From every leaf distils a trickling tear;

[085-068] And straight a voice, while yet a voice remains,

[085-069] Thus through the trembling boughs in sighs com plains:

[085-070] “ ‘If to the wretched any faith be given,

[085-071] I swear by all the unpitying powers of heaven,

[085-072] No wilful crime this heavy vengeance bred,

[085-073] In mutual innocence our lives we led.

[085-074] If this be false, let these new greens decay,

[085-075] Let sounding axes lop my limbs away,

[085-076] And crackling flames on all my honours prey.

[085-077] Now from my branching arms this infant bear,

[085-078] Let some kind nurse supply a mother’s care;

[085-079] Yet to his mother let him oft be led,

[085-080] Sport in her shades, and in her shades be fed;

[085-081] Teach him, when first his infant voice shall frame

[085-082] Imperfect words, and lisp his mother’s name,

[085-083] To hail this tree, and say with weeping eyes,

[085-084] “Within this plant my hapless parent lies:”

[085-085] And when in youth he seeks the shady woods,

[085-086] O, let him fly the crystal lakes and floods,

[085-087] Nor touch the fatal flowers; but, warn’d by me,

[085-088] Believe a goddess shrined in every tree.

[085-089] My sire, my sister, and my spouse, farewell!

[085-090] If in your breasts or love or pity dwell,

[085-091] Protect your plant, nor let my branches feel

[085-092] The browsing cattle, or the piercing steel.

[085-093] Farewell! and since I cannot bend to join

[085-094] My lips to yours, advance at least to mine.

[085-095] My son, thy mother’s parting kiss receive,

[085-096] While yet thy mother has a kiss to give.

[085-097] I can no more, the creeping rind invades

[085-098] My closing lips, and hides my head in shades:

[085-099] Remove your hands; the bark shall soon suffice,

[085-100] Without their aid, to seal these dying eyes.’

[085-101] She ceased at once to speak, and ceased to be,

[085-102] And all the nymph was lost within the tree:

[085-103] Yet latent life through her new branches reign’d,

[085-104] And long the plant a human heat retain’d.”
