# Midas

Midas, king of Phrygia, is the subject of two of the *Metamorphoses*' most enduring moral fables. In the first, his kindness to the captured satyr Silenus earns him a reward from Bacchus: he may choose any recompense. Midas's foolish wish — that everything he touches turn to gold — transforms food, drink, and even his own daughter into gleaming metal, a curse only lifted when he bathes in the river Pactolus (099-001). In the second tale, Midas serves as judge in a musical contest between Apollo and Pan (or Marsyas). When Midas alone prefers Pan's rustic pipe to Apollo's lyre, the god punishes his poor judgment by giving him the ears of an ass — "Midas alone dissatisfied appear'd" (099-140). Both stories are linked by a single theme: the catastrophic consequences of choosing unwisely.

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Midas's story occupies a substantial portion of Book XI. It begins with the capture of Silenus, Bacchus's tutor, whom Midas's subjects drag before the king (099-014). Recognizing Silenus, Midas entertains him hospitably. In gratitude, Bacchus offers Midas any reward — and Midas, "from Orpheus had been taught his lore" (099-016), asks for the golden touch. The gift quickly becomes a curse: his food hardens into metal, his wine solidifies, and his embrace turns his daughter into a golden statue. Only by bathing in the river Pactolus (whose sands have borne gold ever since) is the curse washed away. In the second episode, Midas judges a musical contest — and when all others acclaim Apollo, "Midas alone dissatisfied appear'd" (099-140), earning him ass's ears as a visible sign of his poor taste.

### Key Episodes

- **Hospitality to Silenus**: Midas's men capture Silenus; Midas recognizes him and entertains him generously (099-014)
- **The Golden Touch**: Bacchus grants Midas a wish; Midas chooses the golden touch, which immediately becomes a curse (099-001)
- **The Cure at Pactolus**: Midas bathes in the river Pactolus, washing away the golden curse; the river's sands have borne gold ever since
- **The Musical Contest**: Midas judges between Apollo and Pan; he prefers Pan and is punished with ass's ears (099-140)
- **The Barber's Secret**: Midas hides his ears beneath a turban, but his barber whispers the secret into a hole in the ground; reeds grow there and whisper "Midas has ass's ears" to the wind

## Transformation

Midas himself is not fully transformed but is marked by transformation: his touch transforms objects into gold, and his body is altered (ass's ears) as a punishment. The golden touch is both a gift and a curse — transformation as excess and loss of control.

- **Form**: Midas's touch → gold (all objects, including his daughter)
- **Form**: Midas's ears → ass's ears (punishment by Apollo)
- **Agent**: Bacchus (gift) / Apollo (punishment)
- **Cause**: Foolish wish (golden touch); poor aesthetic judgment (ass's ears)
- **Source**: (099-001, 099-140)
