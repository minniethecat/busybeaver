[034-001] The companions of Ino, lamenting the fate of their unhappy mistress, excite the displeasure of Juno, who transforms them into stones and birds.

[034-002] The Theban matrons their loved queen pursued,

[034-003] And tracing to the rock, her footsteps view’d.

[034-004] Too certain of her fate, they rend the skies

[034-005] With piteous shrieks, and lamentable cries;

[034-006] All beat their breasts, and Juno all upbraid,

[034-007] Who still remember’d a deluded maid,

[034-008] Who, still revengeful for one stolen embrace,

[034-009] Thus wreak’d her hate on the Cadmean race.

[034-010] This Juno heard: “And shall such elfs,” she cried

[034-011] “Dispute my justice, or my power deride?

[034-012] You too shall feel my wrath not idly spent;

[034-013] A goddess never for insults was meant.”

[034-014] She who loved most, and who most loved had been,

[034-015] Said: “Not the waves shall part me from my queen.”

[034-016] She strove to plunge into the roaring flood,

[034-017] Fix’d to the stone, a stone herself she stood;

[034-018] This, on her breast would fain her blows repeat;

[034-019] Her stiffen’d hands refused her breast to beat;

[034-020] That stretch’d her arms unto the seas, in vain

[034-021] Her arms she labour’d to unstretch again.

[034-022] To tear her comely locks another tried;

[034-023] Both comely locks and fingers petrified.

[034-024] Part thus; but Juno, with a softer mind,

[034-025] Part doom’d to mix among the feather’d kind.

[034-026] Transform’d, the name of Theban birds they keep,

[034-027] And skim the surface of that fatal deep.
