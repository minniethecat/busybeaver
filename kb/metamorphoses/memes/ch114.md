[101-001] Thetis, after assuming various shapes to avoid the importunities of Peleus, is at length compelled to yield her consent to the nuptials.

[101-002] For Proteus thus to virgin Thetis said:

[101-003] “Fair goddess of the waves, consent to wed,

[101-004] And take some sprightly lover to your bed:

[101-005] A son you’ll have, the terror of the field,

[101-006] To whom, in fame and power, his sire shall yield.”

[101-007] [[jupiter|Jove]], who adored the nymph with boundless love,

[101-008] Did from his breast the dangerous flame remove;

[101-009] He knew the fates, nor cared to raise up one

[101-010] Whose fame and greatness should eclipse his own.

[101-011] On happy Peleus he bestow’d her charms,

[101-012] And bless’d his grandson in the goddess’ arms.

[101-013] A silent creek Thessalia’s coast can show,

[101-014] Two arms project, and shape it like a bow;

[101-015] ’Twould make a bay, but the transparent tide

[101-016] Does scarce the yellow-gravell’d bottom hide;

[101-017] For the quick eye may through the liquid wave

[101-018] A firm, unweedy, level beach perceive:

[101-019] A grove of fragrant myrtle near it grows,

[101-020] Whose boughs, though thick, a beauteous grot disclose;

[101-021] The well-wrought fabric, to discerning eyes,

[101-022] Rather by art than nature seems to rise.

[101-023] A bridled dolphin oft fair Thetis bore

[101-024] To this her loved retreat, her favourite shore;

[101-025] Here Peleus seized her, slumbering while she lay,

[101-026] And urged his suit with all that love could say.

[101-027] The nymph, o’erpower’d, to art for succour flies,

[101-028] And various shapes the eager youth surprise;

[101-029] A bird she seems, but plies her wings in vain,

[101-030] His hands the fleeting substance still detain;

[101-031] A branchy tree high in the air she grew,

[101-032] [[About]] its bark his nimble arms he threw;

[101-033] A tiger next, she glares with flaming eyes,

[101-034] The frighten’d lover quits his hold, and flies

[101-035] The sea gods he with sacred rites adores,

[101-036] Then a libation on the ocean pours;

[101-037] While the fat entrails crackle in the fire,

[101-038] And sheets of smoke, in sweet perfume, aspire;

[101-039] Till Proteus, rising from his oozy bed,

[101-040] Thus to the poor desponding lover said:

[101-041] “No more in anxious thoughts your mind employ,

[101-042] For yet you shall possess the dear expected joy.

[101-043] You must, once more, the unwary nymph surprise,

[101-044] As coolly in her grot she slumbering lies;

[101-045] Then bind her fast with unrelenting hands,

[101-046] And strain her tender limbs with knotted bands;

[101-047] Still hold her under every different shape,

[101-048] Till, tired she tries no longer to escape.”

[101-049] Thus he, then sunk beneath the glassy flood,

[101-050] And broken accents flutter’d where he stood.

[101-051] Bright [[apollo|Sol]] had almost now his journey done,

[101-052] And down the steepy western convex run,

[101-053] When the fair Nereid left the briny wave,

[101-054] And, as she used, retreated to her cave.

[101-055] He scarce had bound her fast, when she arose,

[101-056] And into various shapes her body throws;

[101-057] She went to move her arms, and found them tied,

[101-058] Then, with a sigh, “Some god assists ye,” cried,

[101-059] And in her proper shape stood blushing by his side.

[101-060] About her waist his longing arms he flung,

[101-061] From which alliance great Achilles sprung.
