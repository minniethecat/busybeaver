# Proserpine

Proserpine (Persephone in Greek, Proserpina in Latin) is the daughter of Ceres (Demeter), the goddess of agriculture. Her abduction by Pluto, the god of the underworld, is one of the pivotal mythological narratives in the *Metamorphoses*, explained by Ovid as a conspiracy of Venus, who sought to extend the power of love even to the realm of the dead. Proserpine's story — divided between her mother's sunlit world and her husband's shadowy kingdom — becomes the mythic explanation for the cycle of the seasons: her annual return to the surface brings spring and summer, while her descent to Pluto marks autumn and winter.

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Proserpine's story is told in Book V, framed as one of the songs of the Muses. Venus, ambitious to extend her dominion, commands Cupid to strike Pluto with an arrow of love (045-001). While Proserpine gathers flowers with her companions in the Sicilian plains of Enna — "Here while young Proserpine, among the maids, / Diverts herself" (046-012) — Pluto bursts from the earth in his chariot and seizes her. The abduction is swift and violent: Pluto carries her down to the underworld, where she becomes his queen. Ceres, discovering her daughter gone, searches the world in grief and rage, withholding the harvest and threatening all life. Jupiter eventually negotiates a compromise: Proserpine will spend part of the year with Pluto and part with Ceres, giving rise to the seasons.

### Key Episodes

- **Gathering Flowers at Enna**: Proserpine, attended by nymphs, gathers flowers in the Sicilian meadow when Pluto strikes (046-012)
- **The Abduction**: Pluto in his chariot seizes Proserpine and plunges into the earth, carrying her to the underworld (046-001)
- **Ceres's Search**: Ceres searches the world for her lost daughter, withholds the harvest, and eventually learns the truth
- **The Seasonal Compromise**: Jupiter rules that Proserpine shall divide her time between the underworld and the upper world

## Transformation

Proserpine herself does not physically transform, but her abduction transforms the natural world: Ceres's grief brings winter, and Proserpine's return brings spring. She undergoes a biographical metamorphosis — from maiden to queen of the dead, from daughter to wife — that is reflected in the annual cycle of death and rebirth in nature.

- **Agent of Change**: Pluto, acting under Venus's scheme
- **Cause**: Venus's desire to conquer the underworld with love
- **Source**: (045-001, 046-001)
