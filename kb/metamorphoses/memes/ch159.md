[142-001] Egeria, the wife of Numa, while lamenting the loss of her husband, is changed by [[apollo|Apollo]] into a fountain.

[142-002] But others’ woes were useless to appease

[142-003] Egeria’s grief, or set her mind at ease:

[142-004] Beneath the hill fill comfortless she laid;

[142-005] The dropping tears her eyes incessant shed,

[142-006] Till pitying Phoebus eased her pious wo,

[142-007] Thaw’d to a spring, whose streams for ever flow.

[142-008] The nymphs and Virbius like amazement fill’d,

[142-009] As seized the swains who Tyrrhene furrows till’d,

[142-010] When heaving up, a clod was seen to roll,

[142-011] Untouch’d, self-moved, and big with human soul.

[142-012] The spreading mass, in former shape deposed,

[142-013] Began to shoot, and arms and legs disclosed,

[142-014] Till, form’d a perfect man, the living mould

[142-015] Oped its new mouth, and future truths foretold;

[142-016] And, Tages named by natives of the place,

[142-017] Taught arts prophetic to the Tuscan race.

[142-018] Or such as once by Romulus was shown,

[142-019] Who saw his lance with sprouting leaves o’ergrown,

[142-020] When fix’d in earth the point began to shoot,

[142-021] And, growing downward, turn’d a fibrous root;

[142-022] While spread aloft, the branching arms display’d,

[142-023] O’er wondering crowds, an unexpected shade.
