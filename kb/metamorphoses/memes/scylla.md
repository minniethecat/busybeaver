# Scylla

Scylla appears in Ovid's *Metamorphoses* as two distinct figures, both of whom undergo profound transformations. The first is Scylla, daughter of King Nisus of Megara, who falls in love with the besieging King Minos and betrays her father — only to be transformed into the seabird *ciris*. The second is the nymph Scylla, beloved by the sea-god Glaucus, whom Circe transforms into the infamous sea monster with dogs' heads at her waist (068-001, 122-001). Together, these two Scyllas represent Ovid's exploration of female desire and its cosmic consequences.

## Role in the Metamorphoses

### Scylla of Megara (Book VIII)

While King Minos of Crete besieges Megara, Scylla watches from the city walls and falls in love with the enemy king. Her father Nisus has a purple lock of hair on which his life and the city's safety depend. Scylla, driven by passion, cuts the fatal lock while Nisus sleeps and brings it to Minos, expecting his love in return. Instead, Minos recoils in horror at her treachery and sails away. Scylla, swimming after his ship, clings to its stern — but her father, now transformed into a sea-eagle, attacks her, and she is transformed into the seabird *ciris* (068-001).

### Scylla the Sea Monster (Books XIII–XIV)

Glaucus, a fisherman transformed into a sea-god, falls in love with the nymph Scylla. When she rejects him, he seeks help from the sorceress Circe — who falls in love with Glaucus herself. Enraged by his preference for Scylla, Circe poisons the pool where Scylla bathes. When Scylla enters the water, her lower body is transformed: dogs' heads sprout from her waist. She becomes the monstrous peril of the strait between Italy and Sicily, a hybrid creature forever associated with maritime danger. In Book XIV, she is eventually transformed into a rock by the gods to prevent further destruction (122-001).

### Key Episodes

- **The Purple Lock**: Scylla cuts the fatal lock of her father Nisus — an act of betrayal born of love (068-001)
- **Rejection by Minos**: Minos spurns Scylla for her treachery, and she is transformed into the seabird *ciris* (068-001)
- **Scylla and Glaucus**: Glaucus confesses his love for the nymph Scylla, who rejects him (121-001)
- **Circe's Revenge**: Circe poisons Scylla's bathing pool, transforming her into the six-headed sea monster (122-001)

## Transformation

### Scylla of Megara
- **Form**: Mortal princess → Seabird (*ciris*)
- **Agent**: The gods, possibly Venus, in response to her flight from her father's avenging form
- **Cause**: Betrayal of father and city for love
- **Source**: (068-001)

### Scylla the Nymph
- **Form**: Beautiful nymph → Sea monster with dogs' heads at her waist
- **Agent**: Circe, through poisoned waters
- **Cause**: Circe's jealousy over Glaucus's love for Scylla
- **Source**: (122-001)
