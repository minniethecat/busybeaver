[002-001] During the reign of Saturn the inhabitants of the earth enjoy a state of primeval happiness, secure from the intrusion of evil passions.

[002-002] The golden age was first, when man, yet new,

[002-003] No rule but uncorrupted reason knew,

[002-004] And, with a native bent did good pursue.

[002-005] Unforced by punishment, unawed by fear,

[002-006] His words were simple, and his soul sincere;

[002-007] Needless was written law where none oppress’d;

[002-008] The law of man was written in his breast:

[002-009] No suppliant crowds before the judge appear’d,

[002-010] No court erected yet, nor cause was heard,

[002-011] But all was safe; for conscience was their guard.

[002-012] The mountain trees in distant prospect please,

[002-013] Ere yet the pine descended to the seas;

[002-014] Ere sails were spread new oceans to explore,

[002-015] And happy mortals, unconcern’d for more,

[002-016] Confined their wishes to their native shore.

[002-017] No walls were yet, nor fence, nor moat, nor mound,

[002-018] Nor drum was heard, nor trumpet’s angry sound,

[002-019] Nor swords were forged; but, void of care and crime,

[002-020] The soft creation slept away their time.

[002-021] The teeming earth, yet guiltless of the plough,

[002-022] And unprovoked, did fruitful stores allow:

[002-023] Content with food which Nature freely bred,

[002-024] On wildings and on strawberries they fed;

[002-025] Cornels and brambleberries gave the rest,

[002-026] And falling acorns furnish’d out a feast.

[002-027] The flowers unsown, in fields and meadows reign’d;

[002-028] And western winds immortal spring maintain’d.

[002-029] In following years the bearded corn ensued

[002-030] From earth unask’d, nor was that earth renew’d.

[002-031] From veins of valleys milk and nectar broke,

[002-032] And honey sweating through the pores of oak.
