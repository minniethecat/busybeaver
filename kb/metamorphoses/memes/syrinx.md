# Syrinx

Syrinx, a nymph of Arcadia devoted to Diana and sworn to chastity, is pursued by the wild god Pan through the marshes of Ladon. Her desperate prayer for deliverance is answered when she is transformed into a stand of reeds at the water's edge. When Pan, still seeking to embrace her, finds only the reeds and sighs in frustration, the wind moving through the hollow stems produces a hauntingly beautiful sound. Recognizing the music as the nymph's transformed voice, Pan cuts the reeds to unequal lengths, binds them with wax, and creates the first pan-pipe — the instrument that will forever bear her name: the syrinx. Her story, told as an embedded narrative within Mercury's tale in Book I, is one of the *Metamorphoses*'s most delicate and bittersweet transformation myths, in which loss is transmuted into art.

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Syrinx's story appears as an inset narrative within the story of Io in Book I. After Mercury slays the hundred-eyed Argus and frees Io (still in the form of a heifer), he pauses on a mountainside to tell the tale of how the pan-pipe came to be. Syrinx, the most beautiful of the Arcadian nymphs, had rejected the advances of satyrs and woodland gods alike, remaining faithful to her vows to Diana. When Pan, the goat-footed god of the wild, saw her returning from Mount Lycaeus, he was overcome with desire and pursued her. She fled across the fields and marshlands until the river Ladon blocked her path. With Pan closing in, she prayed to her sister nymphs of the water to transform her. As Pan reached out to grasp her, he found himself clutching not a nymph but a stand of marsh reeds. The wind, blowing through the hollow stalks, produced a mournful music, and Pan — recognizing Syrinx transformed — declared: "This much of you shall remain to me." He fashioned the reeds into the first set of pipes, forever carrying her voice with him (009-001).

### Key Episodes

- **The Flight from Pan**: Syrinx flees the pursuing Pan across the fields of Arcadia, preserving her chastity
- **The Transformation**: Blocked by the river Ladon, Syrinx prays to the water-nymphs and is transformed into marsh reeds at the moment of Pan's grasp (009-001)
- **The Creation of the Pan-Pipe**: Pan, hearing the wind's music through the hollow reeds, fashions the first syrinx — the instrument that bears the nymph's name

## Transformation

- **Form**: Nymph (Naiad) → Marsh Reeds → Musical Instrument (pan-pipe)
- **Agent**: The sister nymphs of the river Ladon, answering Syrinx's prayer
- **Cause**: Escape from Pan's unwanted pursuit — a metamorphosis that preserves virginity by dissolving the body
