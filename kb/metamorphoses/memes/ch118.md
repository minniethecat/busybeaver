[105-001] Aesacus, a prince of [[troy|Troy]], becomes enamoured of Hesperia, whom he pursues into the woods, where the maiden is killed by the venom of a snake—Her lover in despair throws himself into the sea, and is changed into a cormorant.

[105-002] These some old man sees wanton in the air,

[105-003] And praises the unhappy constant pair;

[105-004] Then to his friend the long-neck’d cormorant shows,

[105-005] The former tale reviving others’ woes.

[105-006] “That sable bird,” he cries, “which cuts the flood,

[105-007] With slender legs, was once of royal blood,

[105-008] His ancestors from mighty Tros proceed,

[105-009] The brave Laomedon, and Ganymede,

[105-010] (Whose beauty tempted [[jupiter|Jove]] to steal the boy,)

[105-011] And Priam, hapless prince! who fell with Troy:

[105-012] Himself was Hector’s brother, and (had fate

[105-013] But given this hopeful youth a longer date)

[105-014] Perhaps had rivall’d warlike Hector’s worth,

[105-015] Though on the mother’s side of meaner birth.

[105-016] Fair Alyxothoe, a country maid,

[105-017] Bare Aesacus, by stealth, in Ida’s shade.

[105-018] He fled the noisy town, and pompous court,

[105-019] Loved the lone hills and simple rural sport,

[105-020] And seldom to the city would resort;

[105-021] Yet he no rustic clownishness profess’d,

[105-022] Nor was soft love a stranger to his breast;

[105-023] The youth had long the nymph Hesperia woo’d,

[105-024] Oft through the thicket, or the mead, pursued:

[105-025] Her haply on her father’s bank he spied,

[105-026] While fearless she her silver tresses dried;

[105-027] Away she fled; not stags with half such speed,

[105-028] Before the prowling wolf, scud o’er the mead;

[105-029] Not ducks, when they the safer flood forsake,

[105-030] Pursued by hawks, so swift regain the lake;

[105-031] As fast he follow’d in the hot career,

[105-032] [[love|Desire]] the lover wing’d, the virgin fear,

[105-033] A snake unseen now pierced her heedless foot,

[105-034] Quick through the veins the venom’d juices shoot;

[105-035] She fell, and ’scaped by death his fierce pursuit.

[105-036] Her lifeless body, frighted, he embraced,

[105-037] And cried, ‘Not this I dreaded, but thy haste;

[105-038] Oh! had my love been less, or less thy fear:

[105-039] The victory, thus bought, is far too dear.

[105-040] Accursed snake! yet I more cursed than he:

[105-041] He gave the wound; the cause was given by me.

[105-042] Yet none shall say, that unrevenged you died.’

[105-043] He spoke; then climb’d a cliff’s o’erhanging side,

[105-044] And, resolute, leap’d on the foaming tide.

[105-045] Tethys received him gently on the wave,

[105-046] The death he sought denied, and feathers gave.

[105-047] Debarr’d the surest remedy of grief,

[105-048] And forced to live, he cursed th’ unask’d relief,

[105-049] Then on his airy pinions upward flies,

[105-050] And at a second fall successless tries:

[105-051] The downy plume a quick descent denies.

[105-052] Enraged, he often dives beneath the wave,

[105-053] And there in vain expects to find a grave.

[105-054] His ceaseless sorrow for the unhappy maid

[105-055] Meager’d his look, and on his spirits prey’d.

[105-056] Still near the sounding deep he lives: his name

[105-057] From frequent diving and emerging came.”
