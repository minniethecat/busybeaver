# Pyrrha

A mortal woman of exceptional piety, Pyrrha is the daughter of [[Epimetheus]] and wife of [[Deucalion]]. When Jupiter destroys the corrupt human race with a universal deluge, Pyrrha and Deucalion alone are spared. Following the goddess [[Themis]]'s cryptic oracle, they repopulate the earth by throwing stones behind them — stones that soften into human form, creating a new and hardier race (006-001).

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Pyrrha appears in Book I, in the poem's second major transformation episode. After Jupiter's flood has scoured the earth clean, Deucalion and Pyrrha's small boat grounds on Mount Parnassus. Finding themselves the sole survivors of humanity, they seek guidance at the shrine of Themis, where the goddess gives them the enigmatic command to "throw behind you the bones of your great mother" (006-001).

### Key Episodes

- **Survival of the Flood**: Pyrrha and Deucalion, noted for their righteousness, are the only mortals spared when Jupiter floods the world to punish Lycaon's impiety and the general corruption of the Iron Age. Their boat comes to rest on the summit of Mount Parnassus (006-001).
- **The Oracle of Themis**: The couple visits the oracle of Themis, still veiled in seaweed from the receding waters. Themis commands them to veil their heads, loosen their garments, and "throw behind you the bones of your great mother." While Deucalion interprets this as stones — the bones of Mother Earth — Pyrrha initially refuses, hesitating to desecrate her mother's remains (006-328).
- **The Re-creation of Humanity**: Convinced by Deucalion's interpretation, Pyrrha joins him in casting stones. The stones they throw take on human shape: those cast by Deucalion become men, those cast by Pyrrha become women — a race hardened by their stony origin, suited to toil and endurance (006-001).

## Transformation

- **Form**: Stones → Human beings (the post-flood race)
- **Agent**: Pyrrha and [[Deucalion]], acting on the oracle of [[Themis]]
- **Cause**: Divine mandate — the stones are interpreted as "the bones of the great mother" (Earth), and the transformation repopulates a world emptied by the [[Flood]]
