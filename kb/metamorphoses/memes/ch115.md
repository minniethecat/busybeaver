[102-001] Daedalion is so much afflicted at the death of his daughter Chione, that he throws himself from [[mount-parnassus|Mount Parnassus]], and is changed into a hawk by [[apollo|Apollo]].

[102-002] Peleus unmix’d felicity enjoy’d,

[102-003] (Bless’d in a valiant son and virtuous bride,)

[102-004] Till fortune did in blood his hands imbrue,

[102-005] And his own brother, by cursed chance, he slew:

[102-006] Then driven from [[thessaly|Thessaly]], his native clime,

[102-007] Trachinia first gave shelter to his crime,

[102-008] Where peaceful Ceyx mildly fill’d the throne,

[102-009] And like his sire, the morning’ planet, shone;

[102-010] But now, unlike himself, bedew’d with tears,

[102-011] Mourning a brother lost, his brow appears:

[102-012] First to the town, with travel spent and care,

[102-013] Peleus, and his small company, repair;

[102-014] His herds and flocks the while at leisure feed

[102-015] On the rich pasture of a neighbouring mead.

[102-016] The prince before the royal presence brought,

[102-017] Show’d, by the suppliant olive, what he sought;

[102-018] Then tells his name, and race, and country, right,

[102-019] But hides the unhappy reason of his flight.

[102-020] He begs the king some little town to give,

[102-021] Where they may safe his faithful vassals live.

[102-022] Ceyx replied, “To all my bounty flows,

[102-023] A hospitable realm your suit has chose.

[102-024] Your glorious race, and far-resounding fame,

[102-025] And grandsire [[jupiter|Jove]], peculiar favours claim;

[102-026] All you can wish I grant; entreaties spare;

[102-027] My kingdom (would ’twere worth the sharing) share.”

[102-028] Tears stopp’d his speech: astonish’d Peleus pleads

[102-029] To know the cause from whence his grief proceeds.

[102-030] The prince replied, “There’s none of ye but deems

[102-031] This hawk was ever such as now it seems;

[102-032] Know ’twas a hero once, Daedalion named,

[102-033] For warlike deeds, and haughty valour, famed;

[102-034] Like me, to that bright luminary born,

[102-035] Who wakes Aurora, and brings on the morn.

[102-036] His fierceness still remains, and love of blood,

[102-037] Now dread of birds and tyrant of the wood:

[102-038] My make was softer, peace my greatest care;

[102-039] But this, my brother, wholly bent on war;

[102-040] Late, nations fear’d, and routed armies fled,

[102-041] That force, which now the timorous pigeons dread.

[102-042] A daughter he possess’d, divinely fair,

[102-043] And scarcely yet had seen her fifteenth year,

[102-044] Young Chione. A thousand rivals strove

[102-045] To win the maid, and teach her how to love.

[102-046] Phoebus and Mercury, by chance, one day,

[102-047] From [[delphi|Delphi]] and Cyllene pass’d this way;

[102-048] Together they the virgin saw: desire

[102-049] At once warm’d both their breasts with am’rous fire.

[102-050] Her time complete nine circling moons had run,

[102-051] To either god she bore a lovely son;

[102-052] To Mercury Autolycus she brought,

[102-053] Who turn’d to thefts and tricks his subtle thought:

[102-054] Possess’d he was of all his father’s slight,

[102-055] At will made white look black, and black look white.

[102-056] Philammon born to Phoebus, like his sire,

[102-057] The muses loved, and finely struck the lyre,

[102-058] And made his voice and touch in harmony conspire.

[102-059] In vain, fond maid, you boast this double birth,

[102-060] The love of gods, and royal father’s worth,

[102-061] And Jove among your ancestors rehearse!

[102-062] Could blessings such as these e’er prove a curse?

[102-063] To her they did, who with audacious pride,

[102-064] Vain of her own, Diana’s charms decried.

[102-065] Her taunts the goddess with resentment fill,

[102-066] ‘My face you like not, you shall try my skill.’

[102-067] She said, and straight her vengeful bow she strung,

[102-068] And sent a shaft, that pierced her guilty tongue.

[102-069] The bleeding tongue in vain its accents tries,

[102-070] In the red stream her soul reluctant flies.

[102-071] With sorrow wild I ran to her relief,

[102-072] And tried to moderate my brother’s grief;

[102-073] He, deaf as rocks by stormy surges beat,

[102-074] Loudly laments, and hears me not entreat.

[102-075] When on the funeral pile he saw her laid,

[102-076] Thrice he to rush into the flames essay’d,

[102-077] Thrice with officious care by us was stay’d.

[102-078] Now, mad with grief, away he fled amain,

[102-079] Like a stung heifer, that resents the pain,

[102-080] And, bellowing loudly, bounds along the plain.

[102-081] O’er the most rugged ways so fast he ran,

[102-082] He seem’d a bird already, not a man;

[102-083] He left us breathless all behind, and now,

[102-084] In quest of death, had gain’d Parnassus’ brow;

[102-085] But when from thence headlong himself he threw,

[102-086] He fell not, but with airy pinions flew.

[102-087] Phoebus in pity changed him to a fowl,

[102-088] Whose crooked beak and claws the birds control,

[102-089] Little of bulk, but of a warlike soul.

[102-090] A hawk become, the feather’d race’s foe,

[102-091] He tries to ease his own, by others’ wo.”
