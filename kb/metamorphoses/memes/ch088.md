[078-001] Metra, the daughter of Erisichthon, uses her powers of transformation for the support of her father, who at last devours his own flesh for want of food.

[078-002] Now riches hoarded by paternal care

[078-003] Were sunk, the glutton swallowing up the heir.

[078-004] Yet the devouring flame no stores abate,

[078-005] Nor less the hunger grew with his estate.

[078-006] One daughter left, as left his keen desire,

[078-007] A daughter worthy of a better sire:

[078-008] Her too he sold, spent nature to sustain;

[078-009] She scorn’d a lord with generous disdain,

[078-010] And flying, spread her hands upon the main.

[078-011] The god was moved at what the fair had sued,

[078-012] When she so lately by her master view’d

[078-013] In her known figure, on a sudden took

[078-014] A fisher’s habit, and a manly look.

[078-015] To whom her owner hasted to inquire:

[078-016] “O thou,” said he, “whose baits hide treacherous wire;

[078-017] Whose art can manage, and experienced skill

[078-018] The taper angle, and the bobbing quill,

[078-019] So may the sea be ruffled with no storm,

[078-020] But smooth with calms, as you the truth inform;

[078-021] So your deceit may no shy fishes feel,

[078-022] Till struck, and fasten’d on the bearded steel.

[078-023] Did not you standing view upon the strand

[078-024] A wandering maid? I’m sure I saw her stand,

[078-025] Her hair disorder’d, and her homely dress

[078-026] Betray’d her want, and witness’d her distress.”

[078-027] “Me heedless,” she replied, “whoe’er you are,

[078-028] Excuse, attentive to another care.

[078-029] I settled on the deep my steady eye,

[078-030] Fix’d on my float, and bent on my employ:

[078-031] And that you may not doubt what I impart,

[078-032] So may the ocean’s god assist my art,

[078-033] If on the beach since I my sport pursued,

[078-034] Or man or woman, but myself, I view’d.”

[078-035] Back o’er the sands, deluded, he withdrew,

[078-036] While she for her old form put off her new.

[078-037] Her sire her shifting power to change perceived,

[078-038] And various chapmen by her sale deceived.

[078-039] A fowl with spangled plumes, a brinded steer,

[078-040] Sometimes a crested mare, or antler’d deer:

[078-041] Sold for a price, she parted, to maintain

[078-042] Her starving parent with dishonest gain.

[078-043] At last all means, as all provisions, fail’d;

[078-044] For the disease by remedies prevail’d;

[078-045] His muscles with a furious bite he tore,

[078-046] Gorged his own tatter’d flesh, and gulf’d his gore.

[078-047] Wounds were his feast, his life to life a prey,

[078-048] Supporting nature by its own decay.

[078-049] “But foreign stories why should I relate?

[078-050] I too myself can to new forms translate;

[078-051] Though the variety’s not unconfined,

[078-052] But fix’d in number, and restrain’d in kind:

[078-053] For often I this present shape retain,

[078-054] Oft curl a snake the volumes of my train.

[078-055] Sometimes my strength into my horns transferr’d,

[078-056] A bull I march, the captain of the herd.

[078-057] But while I once those goring weapons wore,

[078-058] Vast wresting force one from my forehead tore,

[078-059] Lo, my maim’d brows the injury still own.”

[078-060] He ceased; his words concluding with a groan.
