[134-001] The prayers of [[venus|Venus]] prevail, and Aeneas is admitted into the number of the gods, while his descendants sway the sceptre of [[latium|Latium]].

[134-002] Now had Aeneas, as ordain’d by fate,

[134-003] Survived the period of Saturnia’s hate,

[134-004] And, by a sure irrevocable doom,

[134-005] Fix’d the immortal majesty of Rome,

[134-006] Fit for the station of his kindred stars,

[134-007] His mother goddess thus her suit prefers:

[134-008] “Almighty arbiter, whose powerful nod

[134-009] Shakes distant earth, and bows our own abode;

[134-010] To thy great progeny indulgent be,

[134-011] And rank the goddess-born a deity.

[134-012] Already has he view’d, with mortal eyes,

[134-013] Thy brother’s kingdoms of the nether skies.”

[134-014] Forthwith a conclave of the godhead meets,

[134-015] Where Juno in the shining senate sits.

[134-016] Remorse for past revenge the goddess feels;

[134-017] Then thundering [[jupiter|Jove]] the almighty mandate seals;

[134-018] Allots the prince of his celestial line

[134-019] An apotheosis, and rites divine.

[134-020] The crystal mansions echo with applause,

[134-021] And, with her Graces, love’s bright queen withdraws,

[134-022] Shoots in a blaze of light along the skies,

[134-023] And, born by turtles, to Laurentum flies:

[134-024] Alights, where through the reeds Numicius strays,

[134-025] And to the seas his watery tribute pays.

[134-026] The god she supplicates to wash away

[134-027] The parts more gross and subject to decay,

[134-028] And cleanse the goddess-born from radical allay.

[134-029] The horned flood with glad attention stands,

[134-030] Then bids his streams obey their sire’s commands.

[134-031] His better parts by lustral waves refined,

[134-032] More pure, and nearer to ethereal mind,

[134-033] With gums of fragrant scent the goddess strews,

[134-034] And on his features breathes ambrosial dews.

[134-035] Thus deified, new honours Rome decrees,

[134-036] Shrines, festivals; and styles him Indiges.

[134-037] Ascanius now the Latian sceptre sways;

[134-038] The Alban nation, Sylvius, next obeys.

[134-039] Then young Latinus; next an Alba came,

[134-040] The grace and guardian of the Alban name.

[134-041] Then Epitus; then gentle Capys reign’d;

[134-042] Then Capetis the regal power sustain’d.

[134-043] Next he who perish’d on the Tuscan flood,

[134-044] And honour’d with his name the river god.

[134-045] Now haughty Remulus began his reign,

[134-046] Who fell by thunder he aspired to feign.

[134-047] Meek Acrota succeeded to the crown;

[134-048] From peace endeavouring, more than arms, renown,

[134-049] To Aventinus well resign’d his throne.

[134-050] The mount, on which he ruled, preserves his name,

[134-051] And Procas wore the regal diadem.
