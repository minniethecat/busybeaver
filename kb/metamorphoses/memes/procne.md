# Procne

Procne, daughter of Pandion, king of Athens, is the wife of Tereus, king of Thrace, and the sister of Philomela. When she learns — through Philomela's woven tapestry — of the violence her husband has committed against her sister, Procne enacts one of the most horrifying revenges in classical literature: she kills her own son Itys and serves him to his father at a banquet. Her transformation, along with Philomela and Tereus, into birds — Procne becomes a swallow — completes one of the *Metamorphoses*' most powerful explorations of justice, violence, and the costs of revenge (060-001).

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Procne's story occupies Book VI of the *Metamorphoses* and forms a single continuous narrative with the story of her sister Philomela. After five years of marriage to Tereus in Thrace, Procne asks her husband to bring her sister Philomela from Athens for a visit. Tereus agrees — but, inflamed with lust at the sight of Philomela, he abducts her, imprisons her, rapes her, and cuts out her tongue to prevent her from speaking of the crime (060-025). He then returns to Procne with the lie that Philomela is dead.

A year passes. Philomela, imprisoned in a forest tower, weaves the story of her violation into a tapestry and sends it to Procne. When Procne receives the tapestry and comprehends its message, she is transformed not physically but psychologically: her mind fills with a single purpose — revenge. During the Bacchic festival, she rescues Philomela, and together they plan their vengeance. Procne kills her own son Itys, cooks his flesh, and serves him to Tereus at a feast. When Tereus, realizing what he has eaten, pursues the sisters with a sword, all three are transformed into birds: Procne becomes a swallow (060-030).

### Key Episodes

- **The Request**: Procne asks Tereus to bring Philomela to Thrace — setting the tragedy in motion (060-001)
- **The Tapestry**: Procne receives and deciphers Philomela's woven message — a moment of silent communication between sisters
- **The Revenge**: Procne kills her own son Itys and serves him as a feast to his father Tereus (060-030)
- **The Triple Transformation**: Procne becomes a swallow, Philomela a nightingale, Tereus a hoopoe

## Transformation

- **Form**: Athenian princess / Queen of Thrace → Swallow
- **Agent**: The gods, in response to the sisters' flight from Tereus's sword
- **Cause**: Escape from Tereus's violent pursuit; part of the triple metamorphosis that also transforms Philomela and Tereus
