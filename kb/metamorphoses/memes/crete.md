# Crete

Crete appears throughout the *Metamorphoses* as an island of ambiguous character: Jupiter's birthplace and the destination of his abduction of Europa, the kingdom of Minos with its labyrinth and Minotaur, and the site of Daedalus's escape on artificial wings. Crete is thus a place of both divine origin and human ingenuity, of both the bull that carried Europa and the bull that fathered the Minotaur.

## Mythological Significance

Crete is a **threshold island**: the meeting point of Europe and Asia, of divine power (Jupiter's birth and seduction) and human craft (Daedalus's wings). The island's association with bulls — Europa's divine abductor, Pasiphaë's monstrous passion, the Minotaur — links it to transformation at its most transgressive.
