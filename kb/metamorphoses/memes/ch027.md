[022-001] Juno punishes the loquacity of [[echo|Echo]], which frequently prevented her from discovering the intrigues of her husband, by restricting her tongue to the mere repetitions of sound—The nymph, after this, falls in love with [[narcissus|Narcissus]]; which, not being returned, she pines away, and is changed into a stone, which still retains the power of utterance.

[022-002] Famed far and near for knowing things to come,

[022-003] From him the inquiring nations sought their doom.

[022-004] The fair Liriope his answers tried,

[022-005] And first the unerring prophet justified.

[022-006] This nymph the god Cephisus had abused,

[022-007] With all his winding waters circumfused,

[022-008] And by the Nereid had a lovely boy,

[022-009] Whom the soft maids ev’n then beheld with joy.

[022-010] The tender dame, solicitous to know

[022-011] Whether her child should reach old age or no,

[022-012] Consults the sage Tiresias; who replies,

[022-013] “If e’er he knows himself he surely dies.”

[022-014] Long lived the dubious mother in suspense,

[022-015] Till time unriddled all the prophet’s sense.

[022-016] Narcissus now his sixteenth year began,

[022-017] Just turn’d of boy, and on the verge of man;

[022-018] Many a friend the blooming youth caress’d,

[022-019] Many a lovesick maid in vain her flame confess’d,

[022-020] Such was his pride, in vain the friend caress’d,

[022-021] The lovesick maid in vain her flame confess’d.

[022-022] Once, in the woods, as he pursued the chase,

[022-023] The babbling Echo had descried his face,

[022-024] She, who in other words her silence breaks,

[022-025] Nor speaks herself but when another speaks.

[022-026] Echo was then a maid of speech bereft,

[022-027] Of wonted speech; for though her voice was left,

[022-028] Juno a curse did on her tongue impose,

[022-029] To sport with every sentence in the close.

[022-030] Full often when the goddess might have caught

[022-031] [[jupiter|Jove]] and her rivals in the very fault,

[022-032] This nymph with subtle stories would delay

[022-033] Her coming, till the lovers slipp’d away.

[022-034] The goddess found out the deceit in time,

[022-035] And then she cried, “That tongue, for this thy crime,

[022-036] Which could so many subtle tales produce,

[022-037] Shall be hereafter but of little use.”

[022-038] Hence ’tis she prattles in a fairer tone,

[022-039] With mimic sounds and accents not her own.

[022-040] This lovesick virgin, overjoy’d to find

[022-041] The boy alone, still follow’d him behind;

[022-042] When glowing warmly at her near approach,

[022-043] As sulphur blazes at the taper’s touch,

[022-044] She long’d her hidden passion to reveal

[022-045] And tell her pains, but had not words to tell;

[022-046] She can’t begin, but waits for the rebound

[022-047] To catch his voice, and to return the sound.

[022-048] The nymph, when nothing could Narcissus move,

[022-049] Still dash’d with blushes for her slighted love,

[022-050] Lived in the shady covert of the woods,

[022-051] In solitary caves and dark abodes,

[022-052] Where pining wander’d the rejected fair,

[022-053] Till harass’d out and worn away with care,

[022-054] The sounding skeleton, of blood bereft,

[022-055] Besides her bones and voice, had nothing left.

[022-056] Her bones are petrified, her voice is found

[022-057] In vaults, where still it doubles every sound.
