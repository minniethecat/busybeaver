[125-001] Aeneas entreats the sibyl to permit him to seek the shade of his father in the Elysian fields.

[125-002] Now, on his right, he leaves Parthenope,

[125-003] His left, Misenus jutting in the sea;

[125-004] Arrives at Cuma, and with awe survey’d

[125-005] The grotto of the venerable maid:

[125-006] Begs leave through black Avernus to retire,

[125-007] And view the much-loved manes of his sire.

[125-008] Straight the divining virgin raised her eyes;

[125-009] And, foaming with a holy rage, replies:

[125-010] “Oh thou, whose worth thy wondrous works proclaim,

[125-011] The flames thy piety, the world thy fame,

[125-012] Though great be thy request, yet shalt thou see

[125-013] The Elysian fields, the infernal monarchy,

[125-014] Thy parent’s shade. This arm thy steps shall guide:

[125-015] To suppliant virtue nothing is denied.”

[125-016] She spoke, and pointing to the golden bough,

[125-017] Which in the Avernian grove refulgent grew,

[125-018] “Seize that,” she bids: he listens to the maid,

[125-019] Then views the mournful mansions of the dead;

[125-020] The shade of great Anchises, and the place

[125-021] By fates determined to the Trojan race.

[125-022] As back to upper light the hero came,

[125-023] He thus salutes the visionary dame:

[125-024] “Oh! whether some propitious deity,

[125-025] Or loved by those bright rulers of the sky,

[125-026] With grateful incense I shall style you one,

[125-027] And doom no godhead greater than your own.

[125-028] ’Twas you restored me from the realms of night,

[125-029] And gave me to behold the fields of light,

[125-030] To feel the breezes of congenial air,

[125-031] And nature’s best benevolence to share.”
