[089-001] Byblis falls passionately in love with her brother Caunus, who rejects her advances with horror—The nymph becomes frantic with despair, and is converted into a fountain by the indulgent deities.

[089-002] Let the sad fate of wretched Byblis prove

[089-003] A dismal warning to unlawful love:

[089-004] One birth gave being to the hapless pair,

[089-005] But more was Caunus than a sister’s care;

[089-006] Unknown she loved, for yet the gentle fire

[089-007] Rose not in flames, nor kindled to desire.

[089-008] ’Twas thought no sin to wonder at his charms,

[089-009] Hang on his neck, and languish in his arms.

[089-010] Thus wing’d with joy fled the soft hours away,

[089-011] And all the fatal guilt on harmless Nature lay.

[089-012] But love (too soon from piety declined)

[089-013] Insensibly depraved her yielding mind.

[089-014] Dress’d she appears, with nicest art adorn’d,

[089-015] And every youth, but her loved brother, scorn’d;

[089-016] For him alone she labour’d to be fair,

[089-017] And cursed all charms that might with hers compare.

[089-018] ’Twas she, and only she, must Caunus please,

[089-019] Sick at her heart, yet knew not her disease:

[089-020] She call’d him lord, for brother was a name

[089-021] Too cold and dull for her aspiring flame;

[089-022] And, when he spoke, if sister he replied,

[089-023] “For Byblis change that frozen word,” she cried.

[089-024] Yet waking still she watch’d her struggling breast,

[089-025] And love’s approaches were in vain address’d,

[089-026] Till gentle sleep an easy conquest made,

[089-027] And by her side the conqueror was laid.

[089-028] “Ah me!” she cried, “how monstrous do I seem!

[089-029] Why these vile thoughts, and this ill-omen’d dream?

[089-030] Envy herself (’tis true) must own his charms,

[089-031] But what is beauty in a sister’s arms?

[089-032] Oh! were I not that despicable she,

[089-033] How bless’d, how pleased, how happy, should I be!

[089-034] But unregarded now must bear my pain,

[089-035] And but in dreams my wishes can obtain.

[089-036] Oh! gentle Caunus, quit thy hated line,

[089-037] Or let thy parents be no longer mine:

[089-038] Oh! that in common all things were enjoy’d,

[089-039] But those alone who have our hopes destroy’d.

[089-040] Were I a princess, thou an humble swain,

[089-041] The proudest kings should rival thee in vain.

[089-042] It cannot be: alas! the dreadful ill

[089-043] Is fix’d by fate, and he’s my brother still.

[089-044] Hear me, ye gods! I must have friends in heaven,

[089-045] For [[jupiter|Jove]] himself was to a sister given:

[089-046] But what are their prerogatives above,

[089-047] To the short liberties of human love?

[089-048] Fantastic thoughts! down, down, forbidden fires,

[089-049] Or instant death extinguish my desires.

[089-050] Strict virtue, then, with thy malicious leave

[089-051] Without a crime, I may a kiss receive.

[089-052] But say, should I in spite of laws comply,

[089-053] Yet cruel Caunus might himself deny.

[089-054] Yet why should youth, and charms like mine, despair?

[089-055] Such fears ne’er startled the Aetolian pair;

[089-056] No ties of blood could their full hopes destroy,

[089-057] They broke through all for the prevailing joy;

[089-058] And who can tell but Caunus too may be

[089-059] Rack’d and tormented in his breast for me?

[089-060] Like me, to the extremest anguish drove;

[089-061] Like me, just waking from a dream of love?

[089-062] But stay, O whither would my fury run?

[089-063] What arguments I urge to be undone!

[089-064] Away! fond Byblis, quench these guilty flames,

[089-065] Caunus thy love but as a brother claims;

[089-066] Yet had he first been touch’d with love of me,

[089-067] The charming youth could I despairing see?

[089-068] Oppress’d with grief, and dying by disdain?

[089-069] Ah! no; too sure I should have eased his pain:

[089-070] Since, then, if Caunus ask’d me, it were done,

[089-071] Asking myself, what dangers can I run?

[089-072] But canst thou ask, and see that right betray’d,

[089-073] From Pyrrha down to thy whole sex convey’d?

[089-074] That self-denying gift we all enjoy,

[089-075] Of wishing to be won, yet seeming to be coy.

[089-076] Well, then, for once, let a fond mistress woo,

[089-077] The force of love no custom can subdue;

[089-078] This frantic passion he by words shall know,

[089-079] Soft as the melting heart from whence they flow.”

[089-080] The pencil then in her fair hand she held,

[089-081] By fear discouraged, but by love compell’d;

[089-082] She writes, then blots, writes on, and blots again,

[089-083] Likes it as fit, then razes it as vain;

[089-084] Shame and assurance in her face appear,

[089-085] And a faint hope just yielding to despair.

[089-086] Sister was wrote and blotted, as a word

[089-087] Which she, and Caunus too (she hoped) abhorr’d;

[089-088] But now resolved to be no more controll’d,

[089-089] By scrup’lous virtue, thus her grief she told:

[089-090] “Thy lover, gentle Caunus, wishes thee

[089-091] That health, which thou alone canst give to me.

[089-092] O charming youth! the gift I ask bestow,

[089-093] Ere thou the name of the fond writer know;

[089-094] To thee without a name I would be known,

[089-095] Since, knowing that, my frailty I must own.

[089-096] Yet why should I my wretched name conceal,

[089-097] When thousand instances my flames reveal?

[089-098] Wan looks and weeping eyes have spoke my pain,

[089-099] And sighs discharged from my heaved heart in vain:

[089-100] Had I not wish’d my passion might be seen,

[089-101] What could such fondness and embraces mean?

[089-102] Yet (though extremest rage has rack’d my soul,

[089-103] And raging fires in my parch’d bosom roll)

[089-104] Be witness gods! how piously I strove

[089-105] To rid my thoughts of this enchanting love.

[089-106] But who could ’scape so fierce and sure a dart,

[089-107] Aim’d at a tender, a defenceless heart?

[089-108] Alas! what maid could suffer I have borne,

[089-109] Ere the dire secret from my breast was torn;

[089-110] To thee, a helpless, vanquish’d wretch I come;

[089-111] ’Tis you alone can save, or give my doom:

[089-112] My life or death this moment you may choose,

[089-113] Yet think, O think, no hated stranger sues,

[089-114] No foe; but one, alas! too near allied,

[089-115] And wishing still much nearer to be tied.

[089-116] The forms of decency let age debate,

[089-117] And virtue’s rules by their cold morals state;

[089-118] Their ebbing joys give leisure to inquire,

[089-119] And blame those noble flights our youth inspire:

[089-120] O pardon and oblige a blushing maid,

[089-121] Whose rage the pride of her vain sex betray’d,

[089-122] Nor let my tomb thus mournfully complain—

[089-123] Here Byblis lies, by her loved Caunus slain.”

[089-124] Forced here to end, she with a falling tear

[089-125] Temper’d the pliant wax which did the signet bear

[089-126] The curious cipher was impress’d by art,

[089-127] But love had stamp’d one deeper in her heart.

[089-128] Her page, a youth of confidence and skill

[089-129] (Secret as night), stood waiting on her will;

[089-130] Sighing, she cried, “Bear this, thou faithful boy,

[089-131] To my sweet partner in eternal joy.”

[089-132] Here a long pause her secret guilt confess’d;

[089-133] And when, at length, she would have spoken the rest,

[089-134] Half the dear name lay buried in her breast.

[089-135] Thus, as he list’ned to her vain command,

[089-136] Down fell the letter from her trembling hand.

[089-137] The omen shock’d her soul. “Yet go,” she cried.

[089-138] “Can a request from Byblis be denied?”

[089-139] To the Maeandrian youth this message’s borne;

[089-140] The half-read lines by his fierce rage were torn.

[089-141] “Hence,” he exclaim’d, “thou vile accomplice, hence;

[089-142] Enjoy the triumph of thy great offence.

[089-143] Thy instant death will but divulge her shame,

[089-144] Or thy life’s blood should quench the guilty flame.”

[089-145] Frighted, from threat’ning Caunus he withdrew,

[089-146] And with the dreadful news to his lost mistress flew.

[089-147] The sad repulse so struck the wounded fair,

[089-148] Her sense was buried in her wild despair:

[089-149] Pale was her visage, as the ghastly dead,

[089-150] And her scared soul from the sweet mansion fled;

[089-151] Yet with her life renew’d, her love returns,

[089-152] And faintly thus her cruel fate she mourns:

[089-153] “ ’Tis just, ye gods! was my false reason blind

[089-154] To write a secret of this tender kind?

[089-155] With female craft, I should at first have strove,

[089-156] By dubious hints to sound his distant love,

[089-157] And tried those useful, though dissembled, arts,

[089-158] Which women practise on disdainful hearts.

[089-159] I should have watch’d whence the black storm might rise,

[089-160] Ere I had trusted the unfaithful skies.

[089-161] Now on the rolling billows I am toss’d,

[089-162] And with extended sails on the blind shelves am lost.

[089-163] Did not indulgent heaven my deem foretell,

[089-164] When from my hand the fatal letter fell?

[089-165] What madness seized my soul, and urged me on,

[089-166] To take the only course to be undone?

[089-167] I could myself have told the moving tale,

[089-168] With such alluring grace as must prevail;

[089-169] Then had his eyes beheld my blushing fears,

[089-170] My rising sighs, and my descending tears.

[089-171] Round his dear neck these arms I then had spread,

[089-172] And, if rejected, at his feet been dead:

[089-173] If singly these had not his thoughts inclined,

[089-174] Yet all united would have shock’d his mind.

[089-175] Perhaps my careless page might be in fault,

[089-176] And, in a luckless hour, the fatal message brought;

[089-177] Business and worldly thoughts might fill his breast,

[089-178] Sometimes ev’n love itself may be an irksome guest;

[089-179] He could not else have treated me with scorn,

[089-180] For Caunus was not of a tigress born,

[089-181] Nor steel, nor adamant, has fenced his heart;

[089-182] Like mine, ’tis naked to the burning dart.

[089-183] “Away, false fears! he must, he shall be mine,

[089-184] In death alone I will my claim resign:

[089-185] ’Tis vain to wish my written crime unknown,

[089-186] And for my guilt much vainer to atone.”

[089-187] Repulsed and baffled, fiercer still she burns,

[089-188] And Caunus, with disdain, her impious love returns.

[089-189] He saw no end of her injurious flame,

[089-190] And fled his country to avoid the shame.

[089-191] Forsaken Byblis, who had hopes no more,

[089-192] Burst out in rage, and her loose robes she tore;

[089-193] With her fair hands she smote her tender breast,

[089-194] And to the wond’ring world her love confess’d.

[089-195] O’er hills and dales, o’er rocks and streams she flew,

[089-196] But still in vain did her wild love pursue.

[089-197] Wearied, at length, on the cold earth she fell,

[089-198] And now in tears alone could her sad story tell.

[089-199] Relenting gods in pity fix’d her there,

[089-200] And to a fountain turn’d the weeping fair.
