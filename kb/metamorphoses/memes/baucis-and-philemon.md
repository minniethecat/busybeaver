# The Hospitality of Baucis and Philemon

The story of Baucis and Philemon is the *Metamorphoses*' definitive statement on piety and hospitality. When Jupiter and Mercury, disguised as mortals, are turned away by a thousand households, only this impoverished aged couple welcomes them — and is rewarded with salvation, a temple, and eternal union.

## Setting

A humble cottage in the Phrygian hills, with a roof of straw and reeds. The surrounding neighborhood is destroyed by the Flood that spares only the cottage — and only because the gods are within it.

## Narrative

Jupiter and Mercury, in human disguise, are rejected by a thousand homes before reaching the cottage of Baucis and Philemon. The aged couple welcomes them with what little they have — a fire, a cushion stuffed with sedge, a meal of olives, eggs, and wine. When the wine bowl keeps refilling itself, the couple recognizes their guests as gods. They attempt to kill their goose in the gods' honor; Jupiter stops them. The gods lead them up a mountain, and from the summit they watch their neighborhood submerged in a flood. Their cottage, meanwhile, has become a temple. Jupiter grants their wish: to serve as priests of the temple and to die together. In old age, they are simultaneously transformed into an oak and a linden tree, growing from a single trunk.
