# Troy

Troy (Ilium) appears in the later books of the *Metamorphoses* at the moment of its fall. The burning city is the point of departure for Aeneas's journey westward, and the destruction of Troy is framed as the necessary precondition for Rome's rise. Through Aeneas, Troy is transformed from a physical city into an ancestor of the Roman people.
