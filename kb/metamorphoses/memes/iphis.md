# Iphis

Iphis is the central figure of one of the *Metamorphoses*' most remarkable stories — a tale of gender, love, and miraculous transformation. Born to Ligdus and Telethusa in Crete, Iphis was a girl whose father, a poor man, had decreed that a female child would be killed. Telethusa, warned in a dream by the goddess Isis (along with Anubis, Osiris, and other Egyptian deities), defied her husband and raised Iphis as a boy. The ruse held for thirteen years. When Ligdus arranged a marriage between Iphis and the beautiful Ianthe, the two fell deeply in love — but Iphis, knowing her true sex, despaired, believing their union impossible. On the wedding day, Telethusa prayed at the temple of Isis, and the goddess answered: Iphis was transformed into a male, and the marriage of Iphis and Ianthe was joyfully consummated.

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Iphis's story occupies the latter part of Book IX, following the tale of Byblis and her forbidden love. While Byblis's incestuous desire for her brother Caunus ends in destruction, Iphis's seemingly impossible love for Ianthe ends in miraculous resolution — the two stories form a deliberate contrast in Ovid's design. Iphis's narrative explores the fluidity of identity: raised as a boy, loved as a boy, and finally transformed to match the identity she had always inhabited. The deep, requited love between Iphis and Ianthe is presented as entirely legitimate, needing only divine intervention to align body with social role.

### Key Episodes

- **Ligdus's Decree**: Ligdus warns the pregnant Telethusa that a female child must die; Isis appears to Telethusa in a dream, urging her to save the child
- **Thirteen Years of Disguise**: Iphis is raised as a boy; her true sex remains a secret between mother and child
- **Love for Ianthe**: Iphis and Ianthe are betrothed and fall deeply in love; Iphis, knowing her secret, agonizes in a soliloquy
- **The Transformation**: Telethusa prays at Isis's temple; Iphis is transformed into a male on the wedding day

## Transformation

Iphis's transformation — female to male — is performed by Isis and her attendant Egyptian deities. Unlike Caeneus's change (which included invulnerability), Iphis's is focused purely on enabling marriage and social recognition. It is transformation as divine kindness, resolving a seemingly intractable human dilemma.

- **Form**: Iphis (female) → Iphis (male)
- **Agent**: Isis, with Anubis, Osiris, and other Egyptian gods attending
- **Cause**: Answering Telethusa's prayer; enabling the love of Iphis and Ianthe
- **Source**: Book IX
