# Cyprus

Cyprus is Venus's island in the *Metamorphoses*, associated with both the goddess's birth from the sea foam and her role as the power of love and transformation. The stories set on Cyprus — the Propoetides' punishment, Pygmalion's creation, the birth of Paphos — explore love's relationship to art, piety, and stone.

## Mythological Significance

Cyprus is the **island of transformation through love and its negation**. The Propoetides, who deny Venus's divinity, become the first prostitutes and then stone — the anti-transformation, love turned to immobility. Pygmalion, in the same landscape, achieves the opposite: stone (ivory) transformed into living flesh through Venus's grace.
