[111-001] The centaur Cyllarus is mortally wounded in the conflict with the Lapithae; and his mistress Hylonome expires in his arms.

[111-002] “Nor could thy form, oh Cyllarus, foreslow

[111-003] Thy fate: (if form to monsters men allow:)

[111-004] Just bloom’d thy beard; thy beard of golden hue:

[111-005] Thy locks in golden waves about thy shoulders flew:

[111-006] Sprightly thy look! Thy shapes in every part

[111-007] So clean, as might instruct the sculptor’s art,

[111-008] As far as man extended: where began

[111-009] The beast, the beast was equal to the man:

[111-010] Add but a horse’s head and neck, and he,

[111-011] Oh Castor, was a courser worthy thee:

[111-012] So was his back proportion’d for the seat;

[111-013] So rose his brawny chest; so swiftly moved his feet:

[111-014] Coal black his colour, but like jet it shone;

[111-015] His legs and flowing tail were white alone:

[111-016] Beloved by many maidens of his kind;

[111-017] But fair Hylonome possess’d his mind;

[111-018] Hylonome, for features, and for face,

[111-019] Excelling all the nymphs of double race:

[111-020] Nor less her blandishments than beauty move;

[111-021] At once both living, and confessing love.

[111-022] For him she dress’d; for him, with female care,

[111-023] She comb’d, and set in curl her auburn hair:

[111-024] Of roses, violets, and lilies mix’d,

[111-025] And sprigs of flowing rosemary betwixt,

[111-026] She form’d the chaplet that adorn’d her front:

[111-027] In waters of the Pegasaean fount,

[111-028] And in the streams that from the fountain play,

[111-029] She wash’d her face, and bathed her twice a day.

[111-030] The scarf of furs, that hung below her side,

[111-031] Was ermine, or the panther’s spotted pride:

[111-032] Spoils of no common beast. With equal flame

[111-033] They loved: their sylvan pleasures were the same.

[111-034] “Uncertain from what hand, a flying dart

[111-035] At Cyllarus was sent, which pierced his heart.

[111-036] The javelin drawn from out the mortal wound,

[111-037] He faints with stagg’ring steps, and seeks the ground:

[111-038] The fair within her arms received his fall,

[111-039] And strove his wandering spirits to recall;

[111-040] And while her hand the streaming blood opposed,

[111-041] Join’d face to face, his lips with hers she closed.

[111-042] Stifled with kisses, a sweet death he dies:

[111-043] She fills the fields with undistinguish’d cries;

[111-044] At last her words were in her clamour drown’d;

[111-045] For my stunn’d ears received no vocal sound.

[111-046] In madness of her grief, she seized the dart

[111-047] New drawn, and reeking from her lover’s heart;

[111-048] To her bare bosom the sharp point applied,

[111-049] And wounded fell; and falling by his side,

[111-050] Embraced him in her arms; and thus embracing died.

[111-051] “Ev’n still methinks I see Phaeocomes;

[111-052] Strange was his habit, and as odd his dress:

[111-053] Six lions’ hides, with thongs together fast,

[111-054] His upper part defended to his waist:

[111-055] And where man ended, the continued vest,

[111-056] Spread on his back, the houss and trappings of a beast.

[111-057] A stump too heavy for a team to draw,

[111-058] (It seems a fable, though the fact I saw,)

[111-059] He threw at Pholon; the descending blow

[111-060] Divides the scull, and cleaves his head in two.

[111-061] The brains, from nose, and mouth, and either ear,

[111-062] Came issuing out, as through a colander

[111-063] The curdled milk, or from the press the whey,

[111-064] Driven down by weights above, is drain’d away.

[111-065] “But him, while stooping down to spoil the slain,

[111-066] Pierced through the paunch, I tumbled on the plain.

[111-067] Then Chthonius and Teleboas I slew:

[111-068] A fork the former arm’d; a dart his fellow threw.

[111-069] The javelin wounded me; (behold the scar:

[111-070] Then was my time to seek the Trojan war;

[111-071] Then I was Hector’s match in open field;

[111-072] But he was then unborn, at least a child:

[111-073] Now I am nothing.) I forbear to tell

[111-074] By Periphantas how Pyretus fell;

[111-075] The centaur by the knight: nor will I stay

[111-076] On Amphyx, or what deaths he dealt that day:

[111-077] What honour, with a pointless lance, he won,

[111-078] Stuck in the front of a four-footed man:

[111-079] What fame young Macareus obtain’d in fight;

[111-080] Or dwell on Nessus, now return’d from flight:

[111-081] How Prophet Mopsus not alone divined,

[111-082] Whose valour equall’d his foreseeing mind.”
