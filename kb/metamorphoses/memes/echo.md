# Echo

Echo, an Oread (mountain) nymph, is one of the *Metamorphoses*' most poignant figures: a being whose identity is progressively erased — first her independent speech, then her body — until only sound remains. Her story is intertwined with Narcissus's in Book III, the two forming complementary studies in the failure of communication and the dissolution of the self.

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Echo appears in Book III as part of the Narcissus episode. Originally a talkative nymph who distracted Juno with conversation while Jupiter dallied with other nymphs, Echo is punished by Juno: her tongue is restricted to repeating only the last words she hears (022-001). When she encounters Narcissus, she can only echo his words — "Come here!" "Here!" — and when she attempts to embrace him, he flees. Rejected, she wastes away until only her voice remains — and even that voice is only a repetition of others' sounds.

### Key Episodes

- **The Curse**: Juno, discovering Echo's role in distracting her from Jupiter's infidelities, curses her to "repeat only the last words" she hears — the transformation of independent voice into pure repetition (022-001)
- **Encounter with Narcissus**: Echo sees Narcissus and falls in love, but can only echo his words — "Let us meet!" "Meet!" — and when she rushes to embrace him, he flees with "I'd rather die than let you have me" (022-001)
- **Bodily Dissolution**: Rejected, Echo wastes away — her body shrinks until only bones remain, then only voice — the nymph transformed into pure acoustic phenomenon (022-001)

## Transformation

Echo undergoes a **staged dissolution**: first her power of speech is reduced to repetition (Juno's curse), then her body wastes away (unrequited love), leaving only the disembodied voice that still "replies to every sound." This progressive erasure — speaker → repeater → voice without body — is transformation as subtraction rather than change of form.

- **Form**: Nymph → Disembodied voice
- **Agent**: Juno (curse of repetition); self-inflicted (bodily dissolution through grief)
- **Cause**: Punishment for aiding Jupiter's infidelities; grief over Narcissus's rejection
- **Source**: (022-001)
