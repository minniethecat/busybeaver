[109-001] The nymph Caenis, having suffered violence from Neptune, prevails on her ravisher to change her sex, and make her in vulnerable.

[109-002] Then Nestor thus: “What once this age has known

[109-003] In fated Cycnus, and in him alone,

[109-004] These eyes have seen in Caeneus long before,

[109-005] Whose body not a thousand swords could bore,

[109-006] Caeneus in courage and in strength excell’d;

[109-007] And still his Othrys with his fame is fill’d:

[109-008] But what did most his martial deeds adorn—

[109-009] Though since he changed his sex, a woman born.”

[109-010] A novelty so strange, and full of fate,

[109-011] His listening audience ask’d him to relate.

[109-012] Achilles thus commends their common suit:

[109-013] “Oh father, first for prudence in repute,

[109-014] Tell, with that eloquence so much thy own,

[109-015] What thou hast heard, or what of Caeneus known:

[109-016] What was he; whence his change of sex begun:

[109-017] What trophies, join’d in wars with thee, he won:

[109-018] Who conquer’d him; and in what fatal strife

[109-019] The youth, without a wound, could lose his life?”

[109-020] Neleides then: “Though tardy age and time

[109-021] Have shrunk my sinews and decay’d my prime;

[109-022] Though much I have forgotten of my store,

[109-023] Yet, not exhausted, I remember more.

[109-024] Of all that arms achieved, or peace design’d,

[109-025] That action still is fresher in my mind

[109-026] Than aught beside. If reverend age can give

[109-027] To faith a sanction, in my third I live.

[109-028] “ ’Twas in my second century I survey’d

[109-029] Young Caenis, then a fair Thessalian maid:

[109-030] Caenis the bright, was born to high command;

[109-031] A princess, and a native of thy land,

[109-032] Divine Achilles: every tongue proclaim’d

[109-033] Her beauty, and her eyes all hearts inflamed.

[109-034] Peleus, thy sire, perhaps had sought her bed,

[109-035] Among the rest; but he had either led

[109-036] Thy mother then, or was by promise tied:

[109-037] But she to him, and all, alike her love denied.

[109-038] “It was her fortune once to take her way

[109-039] Along the sandy margin of the sea:

[109-040] The power of ocean view’d her as she pass’d,

[109-041] And, loved as soon as seen, by force embraced:

[109-042] Then thus, transported, to the nymph he cried:

[109-043] ‘Ask what thou wilt, no prayer shall be denied:’

[109-044] This also fame relates. The haughty fair,

[109-045] Who not the rape ev’n of a god could bear,

[109-046] This answer, proud, return’d: ‘To mighty wrongs

[109-047] A mighty recompense, of right, belongs:

[109-048] Give me no more to suffer such a shame,

[109-049] But change the woman for a better name;

[109-050] One gift for all:’ she said; and while she spoke,

[109-051] A stern, majestic, manly tone she took:

[109-052] A man she was: and, as the godhead swore,

[109-053] To Caeneus turn’d, who Caenis was before.

[109-054] “To this the lover adds, without request,

[109-055] No force of steel should violate his breast.

[109-056] Glad of the gift, the new-made warrior goes,

[109-057] And arms among the Greeks, and longs for equal foes.
