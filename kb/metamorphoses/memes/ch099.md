[088-001] The gods are forbidden by [[jupiter|Jupiter]] to renew the youth of those mortals whom they favour.

[088-002] When Themis this with prescient voice had spoke,

[088-003] Among the gods a various murmur broke;

[088-004] Dissension rose in each immortal breast,

[088-005] That one should grant what was denied the rest.

[088-006] Aurora for her aged spouse complains,

[088-007] And Ceres grieves for Jason’s freezing veins;

[088-008] Vulcan would Erichthonius’ years renew;

[088-009] Her future race the care of [[venus|Venus]] drew,

[088-010] She would Anchises’ blooming age restore;

[088-011] A diff’rent care employ’d each heavenly power:

[088-012] Thus various interests did their jars increase,

[088-013] Till Jove arose: he spoke; their tumults cease.

[088-014] “Is any rev’rence to our presence given,

[088-015] Then why this discord ’mong the powers of heaven?

[088-016] Who can the settled will of fate subdue?

[088-017] ’Twas by [[fate|the Fates]] that Iolaus knew

[088-018] A second youth. The Fates’ determined doom

[088-019] Shall give Callirhoe’s race a youthful bloom.

[088-020] Arms nor ambition can this power obtain;

[088-021] Quell your desires; ev’n me the Fates restrain.

[088-022] Could I their will control, no rolling years

[088-023] Had Aeacus bent down with silver hairs;

[088-024] Then Rhadamanthus still had youth possess’d,

[088-025] And Minos with eternal bloom been bless’d.”

[088-026] Jove’s words the synod moved; the powers give o’er,

[088-027] And urge in vain unjust complaint no more.

[088-028] Since Rhadamanthus’ veins now slowly flow’d,

[088-029] And Aeacus and Minos bore the load;

[088-030] Minos, who in the flower of youth and fame

[088-031] Made mighty nations tremble at his name,

[088-032] Infirm with age, the proud Miletus fears,

[088-033] Vain of his birth, and in the strength of years;

[088-034] And now regarding all his realms as lost,

[088-035] He durst not force him from his native coast.

[088-036] But you by choice, Miletus, fled his reign,

[088-037] And thy swift vessel plough’d the Aegean main;

[088-038] On Asiatic shores a town you frame,

[088-039] Which still is honour’d with the founder’s name.

[088-040] Here you Cyanee knew, the beauteous maid,

[088-041] As on her father’s7 winding banks she stray’d:

[088-042] Caunus and Byblis hence their lineage trace,

[088-043] The double offspring of your warm embrace.
