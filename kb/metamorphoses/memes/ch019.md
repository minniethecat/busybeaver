[015-001] Ocyrrhoe, the daughter of Chiron, is transformed into a mare, for abusing her gift of prophecy.

[015-002] Old Chiron took the babe with secret joy,

[015-003] Proud of the charge of the celestial boy.

[015-004] His daughter too, whom on the sandy shore

[015-005] The nymph Chariclo to the centaur bore,

[015-006] With hair dishevell’d on her shoulders, came

[015-007] To see the child, Ocyrrhoe was her name;

[015-008] She knew her father’s arts, and could rehearse

[015-009] The depths of prophecy in sounding verse.

[015-010] Once as the sacred infant she survey’d,

[015-011] The god was kindled in the raving maid,

[015-012] And thus she utter’d her prophetic tale:

[015-013] “Hail! great physician of the world, all hail!

[015-014] Hail! mighty infant! who in years to come

[015-015] Shalt heal the nations and defraud the tomb.

[015-016] Swift be thy growth! thy triumphs unconfined!

[015-017] Make kingdoms thicker, and increase mankind.

[015-018] Thy daring art shall animate the dead,

[015-019] And draw the thunder on thy guilty head:

[015-020] Then shalt thou die; but from the dark abode

[015-021] Rise up victorious, and be twice a god.

[015-022] And thou, my sire, not destined by thy birth

[015-023] To turn to dust, and mix with common earth,

[015-024] How wilt thou toss, and rave, and long to die,

[015-025] And quit thy claim to immortality,

[015-026] When thou shalt feel, enraged with inward pains,

[015-027] The Hydra’s venom rankling in thy veins!

[015-028] The gods, in pity, shall contract thy date,

[015-029] And give thee over to the power of [[fate|Fate]].”

[015-030] Thus, entering into destiny, the maid

[015-031] The secrets of offended [[jupiter|Jove]] betray’d:

[015-032] More had she still to say; but now appears

[015-033] Qppress’d with sobs and sighs, and drown’d in tears:

[015-034] “My voice,” says she, “is gone, my language fails,

[015-035] Through every limb my kindred shape prevails:

[015-036] Why did the god this fatal gift impart,

[015-037] And with prophetic raptures swell my heart?

[015-038] What new desires are these? I long to pace

[015-039] O’er flowery meadows, and to feed on grass;

[015-040] I hasten to a brute, a maid no more:

[015-041] But why, alas! am I transform’d all o’er?

[015-042] My sire does half a human shape retain,

[015-043] And in his upper parts preserves the man.”

[015-044] Her tongue no more distinct complaints affords,

[015-045] But in shrill accents and misshapen words

[015-046] Pours forth such hideous wailings, as declare

[015-047] The human form confounded in the mare,

[015-048] Till by degrees accomplish’d in the beast,

[015-049] She neigh’d outright, and all the steed expressed;

[015-050] Her stooping body on her hands is borne,

[015-051] Her hands are turn’d to hoofs and shod in horn;

[015-052] Her yellow tresses ruffle in a mane,

[015-053] And in a flowing tail she frisks her train.

[015-054] The mare was finish’d in her voice and look,

[015-055] And a new name from the new figure took.
