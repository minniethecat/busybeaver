[043-001] The daughters of Pierus challenge the Muses to a trial in music.

[043-002] The muse yet spoke, when they began to hear

[043-003] A noise of wings that flutter’d in the air;

[043-004] And straight a voice, from some high-spreading bough,

[043-005] Seem’d to salute the company below.

[043-006] The goddess wonder’d, and inquired from whence

[043-007] That tongue was heard, that spoke so plainly sense.

[043-008] (It seem’d to her a human voice to be,

[043-009] But proved a bird’s; for in a shady tree

[043-010] Nine magpies perch’d, lament their alter’d state,

[043-011] And what they hear are skilful to repeat.)

[043-012] The sister to the wondering goddess said,

[043-013] “These, foil’d by us, by us were thus repaid:

[043-014] These did Evippe of Paeonia bring,

[043-015] With nine hard labour-pangs, to Pella’s king.

[043-016] The foolish virgins, of their number proud,

[043-017] And puff’d with praises of the senseless crowd,

[043-018] Through all Achaia and the Aemonian plains,

[043-019] Defied us thus, to match their artless strains:

[043-020] ‘No more, ye Thespian girls, your notes repeat,

[043-021] Nor with false harmony the vulgar cheat;

[043-022] In voice or skill if you with us will vie,

[043-023] As many we in voice or skill will try:

[043-024] Surrender you to us, if we excel,

[043-025] Famed Aganippe, and [[medusa|Medusa]]’s well:

[043-026] The conquest yours, your prize from us shall be

[043-027] The Aemathian plains to snowy Paeone:

[043-028] The nymphs our judges.’ To dispute the field

[043-029] We thought a shame; but greater shame to yield.

[043-030] On seats of living stone the sisters sit,

[043-031] And by the rivers swear to judge aright.”
