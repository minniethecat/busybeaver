[047-001] The nymph Cyane, bewailing the loss of Proserpine, is changed into a fountain.

[047-002] But still does Cyane the rape bemoan,

[047-003] And with the goddess’ wrongs laments her own:

[047-004] For the stolen maid, and for her injured spring,

[047-005] Time to her trouble no relief can bring;

[047-006] In her sad heart a heavy load she bears,

[047-007] Till the dumb sorrow turns her all to tears:

[047-008] Her mingling waters with that fountain pass,

[047-009] Of which she late immortal goddess was;

[047-010] Her varied members to a fluid melt;

[047-011] A pliant softness in her bones is felt;

[047-012] Her wavy locks first drop away in dew,

[047-013] And liquid next her slender fingers grew;

[047-014] The body’s change soon seizes its extreme;

[047-015] Her legs dissolve, and feet flow off in stream;

[047-016] Her arms, her back, her shoulders, and her side,

[047-017] Her swelling breasts, in little currents glide;

[047-018] A silver liquor only now remains

[047-019] Within the channel of her purple veins;

[047-020] Nothing to fill love’s grasp: her husband chaste

[047-021] Bathes in that bosom he before embraced.
