[016-001] Battus, a shepherd of Pylos, promises Mercury that he will not reveal his theft of the flocks of Admetus, which [[apollo|Apollo]] tended—The promise is violated, and Battus turned into a pumice-stone.

[016-002] Sore wept the centaur, and to Phoebus pray’d.

[016-003] But how could Phoebus give the centaur aid?

[016-004] Degraded of his power by angry [[jupiter|Jove]],

[016-005] In Elis then a herd of bees he drove,

[016-006] And wielded in his hand a staff of oak,

[016-007] And o’er his shoulders threw the shepherd’s cloak.

[016-008] On seven compacted reeds he used to play,

[016-009] And on his rural pipe to waste the day.

[016-010] As once attentive to his pipe he play’d,

[016-011] The crafty Hermes from the god convey’d

[016-012] A drove, that separate from their fellows stray’d.

[016-013] The theft an old insidious peasant view’d

[016-014] (They call’d him Battus in the neighbourhood),

[016-015] Hired by a wealthy Pylian prince to feed

[016-016] His fav’rite mares, and watch the generous breed.

[016-017] The thievish god suspected him, and took

[016-018] The hind aside, and thus in whispers spoke:

[016-019] “Discover not the theft, whoe’er thou be,

[016-020] And take that milk-white heifer for thy fee.”

[016-021] “Go, stranger,” cries the clown, “securely on,

[016-022] That stone shall sooner tell,” and showed a stone.

[016-023] The god withdrew, but straight return’d again,

[016-024] In speech and habit like a country swain,

[016-025] And cries out: “Neighbour, hast thou seen a stray

[016-026] Of bullocks and of heifers pass this way?

[016-027] In the recovery of my cattle join,

[016-028] A bullock and a heifer shall be thine.”

[016-029] The peasant quick replies: “You’ll find them there

[016-030] In yon dark vale;” and in the vale they were.

[016-031] The double bribe had his false heart beguiled.

[016-032] The god, successful in the trial, smiled:

[016-033] “And dost thou thus betray myself to me?

[016-034] Me to myself dost thou betray?” says he.

[016-035] Then to a touchstone turns the faithless spy,

[016-036] And in his name records his infamy.
