[131-001] Diomed briefly recounts to the Rutulian embassy the misfortunes he has encountered since the destruction of [[troy|Troy]].

[131-002] “After famed Ilium was by Argives won,

[131-003] And flames had finish’d what the sword begun;

[131-004] [[minerva|Pallas]], incensed, pursued us to the main,

[131-005] In vengeance of her violated fane.

[131-006] Alone Oileus forced the Trojan maid,

[131-007] Yet all were punish’d for the brutal deed.

[131-008] A storm begins, the raging waves run high,

[131-009] The clouds look heavy, and benight the sky;

[131-010] Red sheets of lightning o’er the seas are spread,

[131-011] Our tackling yields, and wrecks at last succeed.

[131-012] ’Tis tedious our disastrous state to tell;

[131-013] Ev’n Priam would have pitied what befell.

[131-014] Yet Pallas saved me from the swallowing main;

[131-015] At home new wrongs to meet, as fates ordain.

[131-016] Chased from my country, I once more repeat

[131-017] All sufferings seas could give, or war complete.

[131-018] For [[venus|Venus]], mindful of her wound, decreed

[131-019] Still new calamities should past succeed.

[131-020] Agmon, impatient through successive ills,

[131-021] With fury, love’s bright goddess thus reviles:

[131-022] ‘These plagues in spite of Diomed are sent;

[131-023] The crime is his, but ours the punishment.

[131-024] Let each my friends her puny spleen despise,

[131-025] And dare that proud dictator of the skies.’

[131-026] “The rest of Agmons insolence complain,

[131-027] And of irreverence the wretch arraign.

[131-028] [[About]] to answer, his blaspheming throat

[131-029] Contracts, and shrieks in some disdainful note.

[131-030] To his new skin a fleece of feathers clings,

[131-031] Hides his late arms and lengthens into wings.

[131-032] The lower features of his face extend,

[131-033] Warp into horn, and in a beak descend.

[131-034] Some more experience Agmon’s destiny,

[131-035] And wheeling in the air, like swans they fly:

[131-036] These thin remains to Daunus’ realms I bring,

[131-037] And here I reign, a poor precarious king.”
