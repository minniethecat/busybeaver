# Jason

Jason, son of Aeson and leader of the Argonauts, enters the *Metamorphoses* in Book VII as the hero whose quest for the Golden Fleece brings him to Colchis and into the orbit of the enchantress Medea. In Ovid's telling, Jason is less a protagonist than a catalyst: his heroic credentials — the taming of the fire-breathing bulls, the sowing of the dragon's teeth, the theft of the Fleece — are all achieved through Medea's magical assistance, making him the poem's most conspicuous example of a hero dependent on a more powerful woman. His subsequent abandonment of Medea for Creusa, barely mentioned in the *Metamorphoses*, is the silent betrayal that transforms Medea from lover into the poem's most terrifying agent of revenge.

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Jason's story is told in Book VII, primarily as the frame for Medea's more psychologically intense drama. Summoned by his uncle Pelias — who hopes the quest will destroy him — Jason assembles the Argonauts and sails to Colchis to claim the Golden Fleece. King Aeetes sets him impossible tasks: to yoke fire-breathing bulls, plow a field, and sow dragon's teeth that spring up as armed men. Medea, struck by Cupid's arrow and burning with love for the stranger, uses her magic to protect him and enable his success (062-001). Jason swears an oath of eternal fidelity, and Medea flees Colchis with him. After their return to Greece, Medea restores Jason's aged father Aeson to youth — but pointedly, when Jason asks her to likewise rejuvenate his uncle Pelias, Medea engineers Pelias's murder instead, tricking his daughters into killing him. Jason's subsequent marriage to Creusa, daughter of Creon of Corinth, triggers Medea's most horrific revenge, though Ovid treats this briefly in the *Metamorphoses*, focusing instead on Medea's inner torment. Throughout, Jason remains curiously passive — a hero whose great deeds are performed by the woman who loves him, and whose betrayal unleashes consequences he cannot control.

### Key Episodes

- **The Quest for the Golden Fleece**: Jason arrives in Colchis and faces the tasks set by King Aeetes — all accomplished through Medea's magic (062-001)
- **The Oath of Fidelity**: Jason swears to remain faithful to Medea forever; his later breach of this oath transforms her love into vengeance
- **The Rejuvenation of Aeson**: Medea, at Jason's request, restores his father Aeson to youth
- **The Betrayal**: Jason abandons Medea to marry Creusa, triggering the poem's most elaborate revenge sequence

## Transformation
