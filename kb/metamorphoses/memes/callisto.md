# Callisto

Callisto, a nymph of Arcadia devoted to Diana, is seduced by Jupiter — who approaches her in the form of the goddess herself — and suffers a double transformation: into a bear by Juno's jealous wrath, and finally into the constellation Ursa Major, the Great Bear. Her story, told in Book II of the *Metamorphoses*, is one of the poem's most poignant narratives of victimization, exploring the intersection of divine desire, female solidarity betrayed, and the cosmic permanence that only metamorphosis can confer (013-001).

## Role in the Metamorphoses

Callisto's story occupies chapters 13–14 of Book II. A devoted follower of Diana, she has sworn an oath of perpetual virginity. Jupiter, seeing her alone in the forest, assumes the form of Diana to approach her without suspicion. When Callisto realizes the deception, it is too late — she is pregnant. Months later, when Diana and her nymphs undress to bathe, Callisto's pregnancy is exposed. Diana, though herself a victim of Callisto's violation under her own stolen form, banishes Callisto from her company. When Callisto bears a son, Arcas, Juno — furious at yet another of Jupiter's infidelities — punishes not Jupiter but his victim: she transforms Callisto into a bear (013-001).

For years, Callisto wanders the forest in ursine form, still retaining her human consciousness within the animal body. When her son Arcas, now a hunter, comes upon her in the woods and is about to kill her, Jupiter intervenes: he elevates both mother and son to the heavens as the constellations Ursa Major and Ursa Minor. But Juno, still unsatisfied, forbids them from ever setting below the ocean — the circumpolar stars that never touch the sea.

### Key Episodes

- **The Seduction**: Jupiter approaches Callisto in Diana's form, violating her trust and her vow of chastity (013-001)
- **The Discovery**: Diana discovers Callisto's pregnancy when the nymphs bathe; Callisto is cast out
- **The First Transformation**: Juno transforms Callisto into a bear as punishment for bearing Jupiter's child
- **The Near Catastrophe**: Arcas, now grown, nearly kills his mother in bear-form; Jupiter stays his hand (013-027)
- **Catasterism**: Callisto and Arcas are placed among the stars as Ursa Major and Ursa Minor
