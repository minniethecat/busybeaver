[091-001] [[orpheus|Orpheus]], by his skill in music, obtains from Pluto the restoration of his wife Eurydice on condition of not looking behind him till his arrival in the upper regions: his promises are for gotten; and he turns to gaze on his long-lost wife, who instantly vanishes from his eyes—Her husband, in despair, totally separates himself from the society of mankind.

[091-002] Thence, in his saffron robe, for distant [[thrace|Thrace]],

[091-003] Hymen departs, through air’s unmeasured space,

[091-004] By Orpheus call’d, the nuptial power attends,

[091-005] But with ill-omen’d augury descends;

[091-006] Nor cheerful look’d the god, nor prosperous spoke,

[091-007] Nor blazed his torch, but wept in hissing smoke.

[091-008] In vain they whirl it round, in vain they shake,

[091-009] No rapid motion can its flames awake.

[091-010] With dread these inauspicious signs were view’d,

[091-011] And soon a more disastrous end ensued;

[091-012] For as the bride, amid the Naiad train,

[091-013] Ran joyful, sporting o’er the flow’ry plain,

[091-014] A venom’d viper bit her as she pass’d;

[091-015] Instant she fell, and sudden breathed her last.

[091-016] When long his loss the Thracian had deplored,

[091-017] Not by superior powers to be restored,

[091-018] Inflamed by love, and urged by deep despair,

[091-019] He leaves the realms of light and upper air,

[091-020] Daring to tread the dark Tenarian road,

[091-021] And tempt the shades in their obscure abode,

[091-022] Through gliding spectres of the interr’d to go.

[091-023] And phantom people of the world below:

[091-024] Persephone he seeks, and him who reigns

[091-025] O’er ghosts, and hell’s uncomfortable plains.

[091-026] Arrived, he, tuning to his voice his strings,

[091-027] Thus to the king and queen of shadows sings:

[091-028] “Ye powers, who under earth your realms extend,

[091-029] To whom all mortals must one day descend,

[091-030] If here ’tis granted sacred truth to tell,

[091-031] I come not curious to explore your hell,

[091-032] Nor come to boast (by vain ambition fired)

[091-033] How Cerberus at my approach retired;

[091-034] My wife alone I seek, for her loved sake

[091-035] These terrors I support, this journey take:

[091-036] She, luckless wandering, or by fate misled,

[091-037] Chanced on a lurking viper’s crest to tread;

[091-038] The vengeful beast, inflamed with fury, starts,

[091-039] And through her heel his deathful venom darts.

[091-040] Thus was she snatch’d untimely to her tomb.

[091-041] Her growing years cut short, and springing bloom.

[091-042] Long I my loss endeavour’d to sustain,

[091-043] And strongly strove; but strove, alas! in vain:

[091-044] At length I yielded, won by mighty love;

[091-045] Well known is that omnipotence above:

[091-046] But here, I doubt, his unfelt influence fails;

[091-047] And yet a hope within my heart prevails,

[091-048] That here, ev’n here, he has been known of old,

[091-049] At least if truth be by tradition told.

[091-050] If fame of former loves belief may find,

[091-051] You both by love, and love alone, were join’d.

[091-052] Now, by the horrors which these realms surround,

[091-053] By the vast chaos of these depths profound,

[091-054] By the sad silence, which eternal reigns

[091-055] O’er all the waste of these wide-stretching plains,

[091-056] Let me again Eurydice receive,

[091-057] Let fate her quick-spun thread of life reweave.

[091-058] All our possessions are but loans from you,

[091-059] And soon or late you must be paid your due;

[091-060] Hither we haste to humankind’s last seat,

[091-061] Your endless empire, and our sure retreat.

[091-062] She too, when ripen’d years she shall attain,

[091-063] Must, of avoidless right, be yours again.

[091-064] I but the transient use of that require,

[091-065] Which soon, too soon, I must resign entire.

[091-066] But if the destinies refuse my vow,

[091-067] And no remission of her doom allow,

[091-068] Know, I’m determined to return no more;

[091-069] So both retain, or both to life restore.”

[091-070] Thus, while the bard melodiously complains,

[091-071] And to his lyre accords his vocal strains,

[091-072] The very bloodless shades attention keep,

[091-073] And silent seem compassionate to weep;

[091-074] Ev’n Tantalus his flood unthirsty views,

[091-075] Nor flies the stream, nor he the stream pursues,

[091-076] Ixion’s wondering wheel its whirl suspends,

[091-077] And the voracious vulture, charm’d, attends;

[091-078] No more the Belides their toil bemoan,

[091-079] And Sisyphus, reclined, sits listening on his stone.

[091-080] Then first, (’tis said,) by sacred verse subdued,

[091-081] The furies felt their cheeks with tears bedew’d.

[091-082] Nor could the rigid king or queen of hell

[091-083] The impulse of pity in their hearts repel.

[091-084] Now, from a troop of shades that last arrived,

[091-085] Eurydice was call’d, and stood revived:

[091-086] Slow she advanced, and halting, seem’d to feel

[091-087] The fatal wound yet painful in her heel.

[091-088] Thus he obtains the suit so much desired,

[091-089] On strict observance of the terms required;

[091-090] For if, before he reach the realms of air,

[091-091] He backward cast his eyes to view the fair,

[091-092] The forfeit grant, that instant, void is made,

[091-093] And she for ever left a lifeless shade.

[091-094] Now through the noiseless throng their way they bend,

[091-095] And both with pain the rugged road ascend:

[091-096] Dark was the path, and difficult, and steep,

[091-097] And thick with vapours from the smoky deep.

[091-098] They wellnigh now had pass’d the bounds of night,

[091-099] And just approach’d the margin of the light,

[091-100] When he, mistrusting lest her steps might stray,

[091-101] And gladsome of the glimpse of dawning day,

[091-102] His longing eyes impatient backward cast

[091-103] To catch a lover’s look, but look’d his last;

[091-104] For, instant dying, she again descends,

[091-105] While he to empty air his arm extends:

[091-106] Again she died, nor yet her lord reproved:

[091-107] What could she say but that too well he loved?

[091-108] One last farewell she spoke, which scarce he heard;

[091-109] So soon she dropp’d, so sudden disappear’d.

[091-110] All stunn’d he stood when thus his wife he view’d,

[091-111] By second fate and double death subdued;

[091-112] Not more amazement by that wretch was shown

[091-113] Whom Cerberus beholding turn’d to stone;

[091-114] Nor Olenus could more astonish’d look,

[091-115] When on himself Lethea’s fault he took;

[091-116] His beauteous wife, who, too secure, had dared

[091-117] Her face to vie with goddesses, compared;

[091-118] Once join’d by love, they stand united still,

[091-119] Turn’d to contiguous rocks on Ida’s hill.

[091-120] Now to repass the Styx in vain he tries;

[091-121] Charon, averse, his pressing suit denies.

[091-122] Seven days entire, along the infernal shores

[091-123] Disconsolate, the bard Eurydice deplores;

[091-124] Defiled with filth his robe, with tears his cheeks;

[091-125] No sustenance, but grief and cares he seeks;

[091-126] Of rigid fate incessant he complains,

[091-127] And hell’s inexorable gods arraigns.

[091-128] This ended, to high Rhodope he hastes,

[091-129] And Haemus mountain, bleak with northern blasts

[091-130] And now his yearly race the circling sun

[091-131] Had thrice complete through watery Pisces run,

[091-132] Since Orpheus fled the face of womankind,

[091-133] And all soft union with the sex declined.

[091-134] Whether his ill success this change had bred,

[091-135] Or binding vows made to his former bed,

[091-136] Whate’er the cause, in vain the nymphs contest,

[091-137] With rival eyes, to warm his frozen breast;

[091-138] For every nymph with love his lays inspired,

[091-139] But every nymph, repulsed, with grief retired.

[091-140] A hill there was, and on that hill a mead,

[091-141] With verdure thick, but destitute of shade;

[091-142] Where, now, the muse’s son no sooner sings,

[091-143] No sooner strikes his sweet-resounding strings,

[091-144] But distant groves the flying sounds receive,

[091-145] And listening trees their rooted stations leave;

[091-146] Themselves transplanting, all around they grow,

[091-147] And various shades their various kinds bestow:

[091-148] Here, tall Chaonian oaks their branches spread,

[091-149] While weeping poplars, there, erect their head;

[091-150] The foodful esculus here shoots his leaves;

[091-151] That turf, soft lime-tree, this, fat beech, receives:

[091-152] Here, brittle hazels; laurels, here, advance;

[091-153] And there, tough ash, to form the hero’s lance:

[091-154] Here, silver firs, with knotless trunks, ascend;

[091-155] There, scarlet oaks beneath their acorns bend:

[091-156] That spot admits the hospitable plane;

[091-157] On this, the maple grows with clouded grain:

[091-158] Here, watery willows are with lotus seen;

[091-159] There, tamarisk, and box, for ever green:

[091-160] With double hue, here, myrtles grace the ground,

[091-161] And laurustines with purple berries crown’d;

[091-162] With pliant feet, now, ivies this way wind,

[091-163] Vines yonder rise, and elms with vines entwined;

[091-164] Wild ornus now, the pitch-tree next, takes root,

[091-165] And arbutus adorn’d with blushing fruit;

[091-166] Then easy-bending palms, the victor’s prize,

[091-167] And pines erect with bristly tops arise;

[091-168] For Rhea grateful still, the pine remains,

[091-169] For Atys still some favour she retains;

[091-170] He once in human shape her breast had warm’d,

[091-171] And now is cherish’d, to a tree transform’d.
