[087-001] The events and consequences of the Theban war are foretold by Themis.

[087-002] Now from her lips a solemn oath had pass’d,

[087-003] That Iolaus this gift alone should taste,

[087-004] Had not just Themis thus maturely said

[087-005] (Which check’d her vow, and awed the blooming maid):

[087-006] “[[thebes|Thebes]] is embroil’d in war. Capaneus stands

[087-007] Invincible, but by the [[jupiter|Thunderer]]’s hands.

[087-008] Ambition shall the guilty brothers4 fire,

[087-009] Both rush to mutual wounds, and both expire.

[087-010] The reeling earth shall ope her gloomy womb,

[087-011] Where the yet breathing bard shall find his5 tomb.

[087-012] The son6 shall bathe his hands in parents’ blood,

[087-013] And in one act be both unjust and good.

[087-014] Of home and sense deprived, where’er he flies,

[087-015] The Furies, and his mother’s ghost, he spies.

[087-016] His wife the fatal bracelet shall implore,

[087-017] And Phegeius stain his sword in kindred gore.

[087-018] Callirhoe shall then with suppliant prayer

[087-019] Prevail on Jupiter’s relenting ear.

[087-020] Jove shall with youth her infant sons inspire,

[087-021] And bid their bosoms glow with manly fire.”
