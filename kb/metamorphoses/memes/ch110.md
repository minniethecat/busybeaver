[097-001] The Thracian women, offended at the coldness of [[orpheus|Orpheus]], tear him to pieces, and throw his head into the Hebras, whose streams convey it to the coast of the Aegean sea, where a serpent, while sucking his blood, is changed into a stone.

[097-002] Here, while the Thracian bard’s enchanting strain

[097-003] Soothes beasts, and woods, and all the listening plain,

[097-004] The female Bacchanals, devoutly mad,

[097-005] In shaggy skins, like savage creatures, clad,

[097-006] Warbling in air, perceived his lovely lay,

[097-007] And from a rising ground beheld him play;

[097-008] When one, the wildest, with dishevell’d hair,

[097-009] That loosely stream’d and ruffled in the air,

[097-010] Soon as her frantic eye the lyrist spied,

[097-011] “See, see, the hater of our sex,” she cried;

[097-012] Then at his face her missive javelin sent,

[097-013] Which wizz’d along, and brush’d him as it went;

[097-014] But the soft wreaths of ivy twisted round

[097-015] Prevent a deep impression of the wound.

[097-016] Another, for a weapon, hurls a stone,

[097-017] Which, by the sound subdued as soon as thrown,

[097-018] Falls at his feet, and, with a seeming sense,

[097-019] Implores his pardon for its late offence.

[097-020] But now their frantic rage unbounded grows,

[097-021] Turns all to madness, and no measure knows:

[097-022] Yet this the charms of music might subdue;

[097-023] But that, with all its charms, is conquer’d too:

[097-024] In louder strains their hideous yellings rise,

[097-025] And squeaking hornpipes echo through the skies,

[097-026] Which, in hoarse concert with the drum, confound

[097-027] The moving lyre, and every gentle sound:

[097-028] Then ’twas the deafen’d stones flew on with speed,

[097-029] And saw, unsoothed, their tuneful poet bleed.

[097-030] The birds, the beasts, and all the savage crew

[097-031] Which the sweet lyrist to attention drew,

[097-032] Now by the female mob’s more furious rage

[097-033] Are driven, and forced to quit the shady stage.

[097-034] Next their fierce hands the bard himself assail,

[097-035] Nor can his song against their wrath prevail:

[097-036] They flock like birds, when, in a clustering flight,

[097-037] By day they chase the boding fowl of night:

[097-038] So crowded amphitheatres survey

[097-039] The stag, to greedy dogs a future prey.

[097-040] Their steely javelins, which soft curls entwine

[097-041] Of budding tendrils from the leafy vine,

[097-042] For sacred rites of mild religion made,

[097-043] Are flung promiscuous at the poet’s head.

[097-044] Those, clods of earth or flints discharge; and these

[097-045] Hurl prickly branches, sliver’d from the trees

[097-046] And lest their passion should be unsupplied,

[097-047] The rabble crew, by chance, at distance spied

[097-048] Where oxen, straining at the heavy yoke,

[097-049] The fallow’d field with slow advances broke;

[097-050] Nigh which the brawny peasants dug the soil,

[097-051] Procuring food with long laborious toil:

[097-052] These, when they saw the ranting throng draw near

[097-053] Quitted their tools, and fled, possess’d with fear.

[097-054] Long spades, and rakes of mighty size, were found,

[097-055] Carelessly left upon the broken ground:

[097-056] With these the furious lunatics engage—

[097-057] And first the labouring oxen feel their rage;

[097-058] Then to the poet they return with speed,

[097-059] Whose fate was, past prevention, now decreed:

[097-060] In vain he lifts his suppliant hands, in vain

[097-061] He tries, before, his never-failing strain:

[097-062] And from those sacred lips, whose thrilling sound

[097-063] Fierce tigers and insensate rocks could wound.

[097-064] Ah, gods! how moving was the mournful sight!

[097-065] To see the fleeting soul now take its flight.

[097-066] Thee the soft warblers of the feather’d kind

[097-067] Bewail’d; for thee thy savage audience pined;

[097-068] Those rocks and woods that oft thy strain had led,

[097-069] Mourn for their charmer, and lament him dead;

[097-070] And drooping trees their leafy glories shed:

[097-071] Naiads and Dryads, with dishevell’d hair,

[097-072] Promiscuous weep, and scarfs of sable wear;

[097-073] Nor could the river gods conceal their moan,

[097-074] But with new floods of tears augment their own.

[097-075] His mangled limbs lay scatter’d all around;

[097-076] His head and harp a better fortune found—

[097-077] In Hebrus’ streams they gently roll’d along,

[097-078] And soothed the waters with a mournful song:

[097-079] Soft deadly notes the lifeless tongue inspire;

[097-080] A doleful tune sounds from the floating lyre:

[097-081] The hollow banks in solemn concert mourn,

[097-082] And the sad strain in echoing groans return:

[097-083] Now with the current to the sea they glide,

[097-084] Borne by the billows of the briny tide,

[097-085] And driven where waves round rocky Lesbos roar,

[097-086] They strand, and lodge upon Methymna’s shore.

[097-087] But here, when landed on the foreign soil,

[097-088] A venom’d snake, the product of the isle,

[097-089] Attempts the head, and sacred locks, imbrued

[097-090] With clotted gore and still fresh-dropping blood.

[097-091] [[apollo|Phoebus]] at last his kind protection gives,

[097-092] And from the fact the greedy monster drives;

[097-093] Whose marbled jaws his impious crime atone—

[097-094] Still grinning ghastly, though transform’d to stone.

[097-095] His ghost flies downward to the Stygian shore,

[097-096] And knows the places it had seen before:

[097-097] Among the shadows of the pious train

[097-098] He finds Eurydice, and loves again;

[097-099] With pleasure views the beauteous phantom’s charms,

[097-100] And clasps her in his unsubstantial arms:

[097-101] There side by side they unmolested walk,

[097-102] Or pass their blissful hours in pleasing talk;

[097-103] Aft or before the bard securely goes,

[097-104] And without danger can review his spouse.
