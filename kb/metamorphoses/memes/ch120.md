[106-001] Preparations are making by the Greeks for the hostile invasion of [[troy|Troy]]—Iphigenia, the daughter of Agamemnon, is about to be sacrificed to Diana, when her life is saved by the indulgent goddess, and a hind substituted in her stead.

[106-002] Priam, to whom the story was unknown,

[106-003] As dead, deplored his metamorphosed son.

[106-004] A cenotaph his name and title kept,

[106-005] And Hector round the tomb, with all his brothers, wept.

[106-006] This pious office Paris did not share,

[106-007] Absent alone, and author of the war,

[106-008] Which, for the Spartan queen, the Grecians drew

[106-009] To avenge the rape, and Asia to subdue.

[106-010] A thousand ships were mann’d to sail the sea;

[106-011] Nor had their just resentments found delay,

[106-012] Had not the winds and waves opposed their way.

[106-013] At Aulis, with united powers, they meet;

[106-014] But there, cross winds or calms detain’d the fleet.

[106-015] Now, while they raise an altar on the shore,

[106-016] And [[jupiter|Jove]] with solemn sacrifice adore,

[106-017] A boding sign the priests and people see:

[106-018] A snake of size immense ascends a tree,

[106-019] And in the leafy summit spied a nest,

[106-020] Which o’er her callow young a sparrow press’d;

[106-021] Eight were the birds, unfledged; their mother flew

[106-022] And hover’d round her care, but still in view,

[106-023] Till the fierce reptile first devour’d the brood;

[106-024] Then seized the fluttering dam, and drank her blood.

[106-025] This dire ostent the fearful people view;

[106-026] Calchas alone, by [[apollo|Phoebus]] taught, foreknew

[106-027] What heaven decreed; and with a smiling glance,

[106-028] Thus gratulates to Greece her happy chance:

[106-029] “Oh Argives, we shall conquer; Troy is ours,

[106-030] But long delays shall first afflict our powers;

[106-031] Nine years of labour the nine birds portend,

[106-032] The tenth shall in the town’s destruction end.”

[106-033] The serpent, which his maw obscene had fill’d,

[106-034] The branches in his curl’d embraces held;

[106-035] But, as in spires he stood, he turn’d to stone;

[106-036] The stony snake retain’d the figure still his own.

[106-037] Yet, not for this, the wind-bound navy weigh’d,

[106-038] Slack were their sails, and Neptune disobey’d.

[106-039] Some thought him loth the town should be destroy’d,

[106-040] Whose building had his hands divine employ’d:

[106-041] Not so the seer, who knew, and known foreshow’d,

[106-042] The virgin Phoebe, with a virgin’s blood

[106-043] Must first be reconciled. The common cause

[106-044] Prevail’d, and pity yielding to the laws,

[106-045] Fair Iphigenia, the devoted maid,

[106-046] Was, by the weeping priests, in linen robes array’d.

[106-047] All mourn her fate, but no relief appear’d;

[106-048] The royal victim bound, the knife already rear’d

[106-049] When that offended power, who caused their wo,

[106-050] Relenting ceased her wrath, and stopp’d the coming blow.

[106-051] A mist before the ministers she cast,

[106-052] And, in the virgin’s room, a hind she placed.

[106-053] The oblation slain, and Phoebe reconciled,

[106-054] The storm was hush’d, and dimpled ocean smiled:

[106-055] A favourable gale arose from shore,

[106-056] Which to the port desired the Grecian galleys bore.
