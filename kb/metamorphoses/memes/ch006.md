[003-001] The earth, no longer under the dominion of Saturn, begins to exhibit marks of degeneracy.

[003-002] But when good Saturn, banish’d from above,

[003-003] Was driven to hell, the world was under [[jupiter|Jove]].

[003-004] Succeeding times a silver age behold,

[003-005] Excelling brass, but more excell’d by gold.

[003-006] Then summer, autumn, winter, did appear,

[003-007] And spring was but a season of the year;

[003-008] The sun his annual course obliquely made,

[003-009] Good days contracted, and enlarged the bad.

[003-010] Then air with sultry heats began to glow,

[003-011] The wings of winds were clogg’d with ice and snow;

[003-012] And shivering mortals, into houses driven,

[003-013] Sought shelter from the inclemency of heaven.

[003-014] Those houses, then, were caves or homely sheds,

[003-015] With twining osiers fenced, and moss their beds.

[003-016] Then ploughs, for seed, the fruitful furrows broke,

[003-017] And oxen labour’d first beneath the yoke.
