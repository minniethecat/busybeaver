# Love of God

## Definition

The love of God is the central attribute of the divine character. In the words of the apostle John, "God is love" (001-004) — a declaration that [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]] takes as the single text from which to preach the entire Gospel. God's love is not merely something God does; it is who God is. This love surpasses all human comprehension: "To know the love of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] which passeth knowledge" (001-002). Yet it is the foundation of all [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian hope, for it is this love that sent [[jesus-christ|Christ]] to [[the-cross|the cross]] for the salvation of sinners.

## Key Aspects of God's Love

### Unchangeable Love

One of the most distinctive features of God's love, as Moody emphasizes, is its unchangeable nature. "If you will turn to the Word of God, you will find that the love of God is unchangeable" (001-015). Many who claim to love God grow cold and wander away, "but God's love never changes" (001-015). The challenge is to "make a sinner believe in this unchangeable love of God. When a man has wandered away from God, he thinks that God hates him" (001-019). But God's love is not only unchangeable — it is everlasting. Moody contrasts it with human love, which can fail and grow cold over time, while God's love remains steadfast even toward the wandering sinner.

### Demonstrated at Calvary

The supreme demonstration of God's love is found at the cross. "When we wish to know the love of God we should go to [[calvary|Calvary]]. Can we look upon that scene, and say God does not love us?" (001-014). Moody presses this point with urgency: "Nothing speaks to us of the love of God, like the cross of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]. Come with me to [[calvary|Calvary]], and look upon the Son of God as He hangs there" (001-027). The cross is the answering argument to every doubt about God's love. [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s sacrificial death — "the love of God displayed in the death of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]" (006-024) — is the ultimate proof that God loves sinners.

### Surpassing Human Knowledge

God's love exceeds all finite measurement. "Many of us think we know something of God's love; but centuries hence we shall admit we have never found out much about it. Columbus discovered America; but what did he know about its great lakes, rivers, forests, and the Mississippi? He died, without knowing much about what he had discovered" (001-010). In the same way, the believer who has tasted God's love has only begun to explore its "breadth, and length, and depth, and height" (001-010).

> If I could only make men understand the real meaning of the words of the apostle John — "God is love," I would take that single text, and would go up and down the world proclaiming this glorious truth. If you can convince a man that you love him you have won his heart. If we really make people believe that God loves them, how we should find them crowding into the kingdom of heaven! The trouble is that men think God hates them; and so they are all the time running away from Him.
>
> (001-004)

## False Views of God's Love

Moody identifies several errors that prevent people from experiencing God's love. The first is the devil's work: "He succeeded in making the world believe that God does not love them" (001-007). A second error comes from "false teaching" (001-008) that distorts the character of God. A third arises when "many people think God does not love them because they are measuring God by their own human standards" (001-009), judging divine love by its imperfect human counterpart.

## Human Love as a Shadow

Moody uses the strongest human love — that of a mother for her child — as a pointer to the greater divine reality. "Now the strongest human love that we know of is a mother's love" (001-021). He recounts the story of a mother whose wayward son faced execution: "This is my boy; I love him still" (001-023). The mother "never faltered. She begged for his life" (001-024). Yet even this fierce maternal love is but a shadow: "No human love is to be compared with the love of God; it does not measure the height or the depth of God's love" (001-026).

## Love and the New Life

God's love is not only a doctrine to be believed but a power to be experienced. "Let the love of God be shed abroad in your heart by the [[holy-spirit|Holy Ghost]]" (001-046) — this is the means by which the believer enters into new life. [[jesus-christ|Christ]] promised that "he that loveth Me shall be loved of My Father, and I will love him" (001-016). The love between the Father and the Son becomes the pattern and source of God's love for the believer: "Thou hast loved them as Thou hast loved Me" (001-018).
