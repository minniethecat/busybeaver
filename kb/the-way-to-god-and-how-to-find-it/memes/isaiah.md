# Isaiah

## Overview

Isaiah was one of the greatest prophets of Judah, whose book contains some of the most tender expressions of God's love in the Old Testament. [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]] draws on Isaiah to demonstrate God's unfailing love — exceeding even a mother's love — and to show that God has done everything necessary for salvation.

## Role in Moody's Teaching

### God's Unfailing Love

Moody cites Isaiah 49:15–16 as one of his most powerful texts on divine love: "Can a woman forget her sucking child that she should not have compassion on the son of her womb? yea, they may forget; yet will I not forget thee. Behold I have graven thee upon the palms of My hands" (001-020). This promise demonstrates that God's love is more constant than even a mother's — which Moody considers the strongest human love.

### God Has Done All He Can

Quoting Isaiah 5:4, Moody asks "what more could He have done?" (002-021). God sent His prophets — they were killed. He sent His Son — He was murdered. He sent the [[holy-spirit|Holy Spirit]] to convince of sin. There is nothing left for God to do; salvation is fully provided, waiting only for the sinner's response.


> Isaiah tells us that, “He was wounded for our transgressions; He was bruised for our iniquities; the chastisement of our peace was upon Him: and with His stripes we are healed.”
>
> (007-047)
