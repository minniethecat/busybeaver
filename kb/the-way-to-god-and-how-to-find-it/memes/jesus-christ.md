# Jesus Christ

Jesus Christ is the centre and substance of [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s entire gospel message. [[eve|Eve]]ry doctrine in *The Way to God and How to Find It* — [[salvation]], [[sin]], [[repentance]], [[faith]], the [[new-birth]] — converges upon His person and work. Moody presents Christ not as a distant historical figure but as a living, present Saviour who **can** and **will** save any sinner who comes to Him in faith. He is at once the Revealer of God's [[love-of-god|love]], the Sacrifice for [[sin]], the Giver of [[new-birth|new life]], and the coming [[judgment|Judge]].

## Overview

Jesus Christ (c. 4 BC – c. AD 30–33) is the incarnate Son of God, the promised Messiah of Israel, and the Saviour of the world. Born in Bethlehem, raised in Nazareth, He conducted a public ministry of approximately three years in Judea and Galilee, teaching, healing, and calling disciples. He was crucified under Pontius Pilate, rose from the dead on the third day, and ascended to the right hand of God. For Moody, the historical reality of Christ's life, death, and resurrection is the non-negotiable foundation of the Christian faith.

## Role in Moody's Teaching

### The Centre of the Gospel

Moody places Christ at the very heart of the gospel. When he preaches on the love of God, he points to the cross: "Come with me to [[calvary|Calvary]], and look at the cross of Christ" (001-027). The cross is where God's love is most clearly displayed: "Greater love hath no man than this, that a man lay down his life for his friends. But Jesus Christ laid down His life for His enemies" (001-027).

The person of Christ gives the gospel its power. Moody argues that Christ's teaching — particularly on the new birth — carries divine authority. He recounts asking an audience if they believed Jesus Christ taught the doctrine of the new birth, then pressing them to explain why, if they believed His teaching, they had not been born again (002-001).

### The Divine Saviour

Moody devotes an entire chapter to establishing Christ's deity. He argues that Jesus Christ is "either an impostor or the Son of God" — there is no middle ground (005-006). Moody rejects the notion that Christ was merely a good teacher who was deceived about His own identity: "As if Jesus Christ were carried away by a kind of mental aberration" (005-008). Either Christ is who He claimed to be, or He is a fraud; a merely "good teacher" who falsely claimed to be God is a contradiction.

> "This is the true God, and eternal life. Little children, keep yourselves from idols." — 1 John 5:20–21, cited by Moody as proof of Christ's deity (005-003)

### The Finished Work of Christ

The core of Moody's gospel is that Christ completed the work of salvation on the cross. "When Christ cried out on [[calvary|Calvary]], 'It is finished!' He meant what He said. All that men have to do now is just to accept of the work of Jesus Christ" (002-015). There is nothing to add to His work — no penance, no reformation, no religious observance can supplement what Christ has already done.

### Christ as Prophet, Priest, and King

Quoting the Westminster Shorter Catechism, Moody presents Christ in His threefold office: as Prophet, He instructs us in the way of salvation; as Priest, "He died for us, and now lives to intercede for us" (008-022); as King, He rules over His people and will one day judge the world. This comprehensive presentation means that Christ is sufficient for every need of the sinner and the saint alike.

### The Present Saviour

Moody insists that Christ is not merely a historical figure but a living, present Saviour: "Bear in mind, sinner, that Jesus died for all. You need not look at your wound — look at the Remedy, hold up Christ" (002-029). [[salvation|Salvation]] is not about looking at one's own sinfulness but looking to the living Christ who saves.

## Divine Nature

Moody is unequivocal about the deity of Christ. He cites John's declaration that Jesus is "the true God, and eternal life" (005-003) and Jesus' own words in His high-priestly prayer: "This is life eternal, that they might know Thee, the only true God, and Jesus Christ whom Thou hast sent" (005-004). The logic is inescapable: if eternal life consists in knowing God, and knowing Christ is essential to that knowledge, then Christ must be God.

The cross demonstrates that Christ is no ordinary man. "No man can save himself — much less can he save others. But Christ is able to save to the uttermost all that come unto God by Him" (008-004). His ability to save is directly tied to His divine identity.
