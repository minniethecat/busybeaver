Baptism is the [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian ordinance of water immersion or sprinkling signifying identification with [[jesus-christ|Christ]]. In [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, baptism is important in its proper place, but it must never be confused with the new birth — baptism alone cannot save a soul.

## Baptism Is Not Regeneration

Moody is emphatic that baptism does not produce the new birth. He warns against those who say, "'Why, I have been baptized; and I was born again when I was baptized.' They believe that because they were baptized into the church, they were baptized into the [[kingdom-of-god|Kingdom of God]]" (002-008). This is a dangerous confusion.

"Baptism is all right in its place," Moody acknowledges, but the distinction is absolute: "You may be baptized into the church, and yet not be baptized into the Son of God" (002-008). The new birth is a spiritual work that water cannot accomplish. "Nor will being baptized do you any good" if one rests on it for salvation (002-008).

## The Biblical Foundation

Moody grounds his view in [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s words to [[nicodemus|Nicodemus]]: "You cannot be baptized into the [[kingdom-of-god|Kingdom of God]]. 'Except a man be born again, he cannot see the [[kingdom-of-god|Kingdom of God]]'" (002-008). The new birth and baptism are distinct works — one spiritual, one physical.

Yet Moody does not diminish the importance of baptism when properly understood. He notes the examples of immediate baptism following conversion in the early church: "They were not only converted, but they were baptized the same day" (004-010, referring to Acts 2). The Ethiopian eunuch, upon believing, asked Philip, "'See, here is water: what doth hinder me to be baptized?'" and was baptized immediately upon his [[confession]] of faith (004-011, referencing Acts 8:26–38).

## Baptism and the Holy Spirit

Moody distinguishes water baptism from the baptism of the [[holy-spirit|Holy Spirit]]. At the house of Cornelius, "Peter preached [[jesus-christ|Christ]] to him and his company the [[holy-spirit|Holy Ghost]] fell on them, and they were baptized" (004-009, referencing Acts 10). The Spirit's coming preceded water baptism, demonstrating that the inward work of God is primary.

## A Warning Against False Trust

Moody's central concern is pastoral: he fears that many rest their hope of salvation on the fact of their baptism rather than on the work of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] received by faith. "If anyone reading this rests his hopes on anything else — on any other foundation — I pray that God may sweep it away" (002-008). The sole foundation must be [[jesus-christ|Christ]], not an ordinance.
