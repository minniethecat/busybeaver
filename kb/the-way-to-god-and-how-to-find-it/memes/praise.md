# Praise

## Definition

Praise, in [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s "The Way to God," is the joyful response of the human heart to God's saving work. It is the natural language of the redeemed — the outward expression of inward transformation. Moody presents praise not as a religious duty but as the spontaneous overflow of a heart that has tasted God's grace.

## Praise as Evidence of Conversion

Moody uses praise as a definitive mark of genuine [[conversion|conversion]]. He describes the transformation of the regenerate: "Their tongues were cursing and blaspheming; and now are occupied in praising God. Old things have passed away, and all things have become new" (002-017). The tongue that once cursed now praises — this is not mere reformation but regeneration. The converted soul cannot help but praise.

In the illustration of the healed Israelite, Moody pictures the young convert immediately turning to "call on all those who were with him to praise God" (002-027). The first instinct of the newly saved is to praise the One who saved them.

> That Hebrew boy is a young convert. I can fancy that I see him now calling on all those who were with him to praise God. He sees another young man bitten as he was; and he runs up to him and tells him, "You need not die."
>
> (002-027)

## Praise as the Fruit of Assurance

The believer who knows [[assurance|assurance]] of salvation will be marked by praise. In Moody's account of a sudden conversion, the new [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian's first words were "'Glory to God!' What I said after that I do not know" (006-019). The soul too full of joy to form measured words — this is the essence of true praise. "To [[jesus-christ|Christ]] be all the praise" (006-019).

## The Shout of Praise

Moody envisions a church united in praise as a powerful witness to the world. He laments cold formalism and declares that if believers "were all brightly shining for the Master, those about us would soon be reached, and there would be a shout of praise going to heaven" (008-014). Praise is not merely personal — it is corporate, contagious, and evangelistic.

## Praise and the Cross

The ultimate ground of praise is the work of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]. The cross transforms the blasphemer into a worshiper. The one who has looked upon [[the-cross|Calvary]] and received forgiveness cannot but praise. Moody's own testimony reflects this: the love of God, once believed, produces praise that is irrepressible.
