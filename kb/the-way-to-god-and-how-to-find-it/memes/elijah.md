# Elijah

## Overview

Elijah was one of the greatest prophets of Israel, known for his miracles and his confrontation with the prophets of Baal on Mount Carmel. [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]] references Elijah to make a christological point: in the rare instances where men like Elijah raised the dead, "they did it by the power of God. They called on God to do it." But [[jesus-christ|Christ]] simply said, "Damsel, I say unto thee, Arise" (005-023). Elijah's dependence on God contrasts with [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s inherent divine authority.

## Role in Moody's Teaching

### Contrast with Christ's Divinity

Moody's argument for [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s divinity is strengthened by contrasting [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s miracles with those of the prophets. [[elisha|Elisha]] and Elijah raised the dead, but they prayed for God to do it. [[jesus-christ|Christ]] commanded life by His own word: "He had power to impart life" (005-024). This demonstrates that [[jesus-christ|Christ]] is more than a prophet — He is divine.

### A Blasphemous Comparison

If [[jesus-christ|Christ]] were merely a prophet like Elijah, His claims to be honored as the Father is honored would be "downright blasphemy" (005-011). [[sin|Sin]]ce [[jesus-christ|Christ]] is not a blasphemer, He must be what He claimed to be.


> God can do what He has done before. He kept Joseph in Egypt; Moses before Pharaoh; Daniel in Babylon; and enabled Elijah to stand before Ahab in that dark day. And I am so thankful that these I have mentioned were men of like passions with ourselves. It was God who made them so great.
>
> (004-018)

## Biblical Foundation
- **1 Kings 17** — Elijah raises the widow's son (by prayer to God).
- **Mark 5:41** — [[jesus-christ|Christ]] raises Jairus's daughter by His own word (005-023).
- **Luke 7:14** — [[jesus-christ|Christ]] raises the widow's son at Nain (005-024).
