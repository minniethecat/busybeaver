# Elisha

## Biblical Role

Elisha was the prophetic successor of [[elijah|Elijah]], receiving a double portion of [[elijah|Elijah]]'s spirit. He performed many miracles during his ministry in the northern kingdom of Israel, including the raising of a dead child (2 Kings 4).

## Moody's Teaching

[[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]] uses Elisha to make a critical theological point about the [[blasphemy|divinity of Christ]]. Some skeptics object: "Did not Elisha also raise the dead?" (005-023). Moody acknowledges this but draws a sharp distinction: "In the rare instances in which men have raised the dead, they did it by the power of God. They called on God to do it." Elisha prayed; God answered.

[[jesus-christ|Christ]], by contrast, "did not call upon the Father to bring the dead to life." He spoke directly: "Damsel, I say unto thee, Arise" (005-023). The difference is not one of degree but of kind: Elisha was an instrument; [[jesus-christ|Christ]] is the source.

Moody also groups Elisha among the mortals whose claims, if they had asserted equality with God, would constitute blasphemy: "If [[moses|Moses]], or [[elijah|Elijah]], or Elisha, or any other mortal had said, 'You must honour me as you honor God,' it would have been downright blasphemy" (005-011). [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s divinity is proved by the fact that He made precisely such claims — and they were true.
