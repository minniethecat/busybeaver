# Judgment

## Definition

Judgment is God's righteous assessment of every human soul — the divine verdict upon sin and sinners. In [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, judgment is certain, yet the believer in [[jesus-christ|Christ]] need not fear it. Those who have truly trusted [[jesus-christ|Christ]] "shall not come into condemnation" but have already "passed from death unto life" (003-026). For the believer, judgment is behind them.

## Moody's Teaching

### Judgment Is Certain

Moody affirms the reality of divine judgment. God has "committed all judgment unto the Son: that all men should honor the Son, even as they honor the Father" (005-010). The judgment is universal — every person will give account. Yet the gospel provides the way of escape.

### No Judgment for the Believer

The believer who has trusted [[jesus-christ|Christ]] possesses eternal life now and faces no condemnation. John 5:24 is emphatic: "He that heareth My word, and believeth on Him that sent Me, hath everlasting life, and shall not come into condemnation; but is passed from death unto life" (003-026). Moody underscores that this is present tense — not a future hope but a current reality.

### Boldness in the Day of Judgment

First John 4:17 promises that believers "may have boldness in the day of judgment: because as He is, so are we in this world" (003-027). Moody uses the illustration of a condemned man who went through his trial with a pardon already in his pocket — when sentence was pronounced, he presented the pardon and walked out free (003-029). The believer has already been pardoned.

### Judgment and Repentance

Moody holds that there is no opportunity for repentance after death. "[[the-word-of-god|The Bible]] teaches clearly that there is no repentance after this life" (006-032). Now is "the only day of mercy you will ever have" — the record can be blotted out now, but not later (006-032).

### Christ as Judge

It is significant that the same [[jesus-christ|Christ]] who died for sinners will be their judge. "The Father judgeth no man, but hath committed all judgment unto the Son" (005-010). Those who have trusted the Son have nothing to fear from His judgment.

> If a person really hears the word of Jesus and believes with the heart on God who sent the Son to be the Saviour of the world, and lays hold of and appropriates this great salvation, there is no fear of judgment. He will not be looking forward with dread to the Great White Throne; for we read in 1 John 4:17: "Herein is our love made perfect, that we may have boldness in the day of judgment: because as He is, so are we in this world."
>
> (003-027)

## Biblical Foundation
- **John 5:24** — Believers shall not come into condemnation (003-026).
- **1 John 4:17** — Boldness in the day of judgment (003-027).
- **Acts 17:30–31** — God has appointed a day to judge the world.
- **Hebrews 9:27** — "It is appointed unto men once to die, but after this the judgment."
