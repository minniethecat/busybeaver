# Joy

## Definition

Joy, in [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, is a fruit of the [[holy-spirit|Holy Spirit]] and one of the distinguishing marks of genuine [[faith|Christian faith]]. Unlike worldly happiness, which depends on favorable circumstances, [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian joy persists through trial and springs from the believer's relationship with [[jesus-christ|Christ]]. "There is no rest, joy, or peace — no liberty, nor power — where doubts and uncertainty exist" (007-004). Joy is the companion of [[peace|peace]] and the evidence of [[assurance|assurance]].

## Moody's Teaching

### Joy as a Test of Faith

Moody presents the fruit of the Spirit as a diagnostic tool: "Another good test is to read Galatians 5, and notice the fruits of the Spirit; and see if you have them. 'The fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, long-suffering, gentleness, goodness, faith, meekness, temperance'" (007-030). The presence of joy in the believer's life is not optional — it is evidence that the Spirit dwells within. "If I am meek, gentle, and forgiving; if I have a spirit filled with peace and joy; if I am long-suffering and gentle, like the Son of God — that is a test: and in that way we are to tell whether we have eternal life" (007-021).

### Joy Through the Holy Spirit

Joy is a direct result of the Spirit's work. "Let the love of God be shed abroad in your heart by the [[holy-spirit|Holy Ghost]] today: it will drive away darkness; it will drive away gloom; it will drive away sin; and peace and joy shall be yours" (001-046). The Spirit does not merely suppress negative emotions — He positively produces joy, along with peace, as the atmosphere of the believing soul.

### Joy as Evidence of the New Birth

The reality of regeneration is confirmed by its experiential fruit: "if, by believing, I get a new life, and a hope, a peace, a joy, and a rest to my soul, that I never had before; if I get self-control, and find that I have a power to resist evil and to do good — I may reason that I have been born of the Spirit" (003-022). Joy is not self-generated optimism but a new capacity given in the [[new-birth|new birth]].

### Restoring Lost Joy

Moody addresses the [[backsliding|backslider]] who has lost the joy of salvation. "If God will restore His joy to your soul let Him do it in His way" (009-027). The path to restored joy is [[confession|confession]]: "If you confess your sins and tell Him that you have wandered from the path of His commandments He will restore unto you the joy of His salvation" (009-027). Joy lost through sin is joy restored through repentance — not through trying to feel better, but through honest dealing with God.

### Looking to Christ, Not Feelings

Like peace, joy is a byproduct of beholding [[jesus-christ|Christ]] rather than scrutinizing one's own emotions. "Many people are hunting after light, and peace, and joy" (008-010), but the hunt itself is misdirected. "When our faces are directed to the Sun of [[righteousness|Righteousness]], the peace and joy are sure to come" (008-010). "Some [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ians are all the time thinking about their feelings; and because they do not feel just right they think their joy is all gone" (008-010). The cure is to look away from self and toward [[jesus-christ|Christ]].

### Exceeding Joy at Christ's Return

The joy of the present is a foretaste of the joy to come. Quoting Jude, Moody reminds believers that [[jesus-christ|Christ]] "will 'present you faultless before the presence of His glory with exceeding joy'" (007-024). The final presentation of the redeemed before God's throne will be an occasion not of shame but of "exceeding joy" — [[jesus-christ|Christ]] Himself rejoices in the completed salvation of His people.

> And if, by believing, I get a new life, and a hope, a peace, a joy, and a rest to my soul, that I never had before; if I get self-control, and find that I have a power to resist evil and to do good, I have pretty good proof that I am in the right road to the "city which hath foundations, whose builder and maker is God."

> (003-022)
