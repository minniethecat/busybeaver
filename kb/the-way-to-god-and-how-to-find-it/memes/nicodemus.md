# Nicodemus

## Overview

Nicodemus was a Pharisee, a ruler of the Jews, and a member of the Sanhedrin — one of the most respected religious figures in [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]]. [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]] presents him as the supreme example that even the best, most moral, and most religious person must be born again. [[jesus-christ|Christ]] chose Nicodemus, not a publican or harlot, to receive the foundational teaching on the new birth, precisely so that no one could claim exemption (002-005).

## Role in Moody's Teaching

### The Night Visit

Nicodemus came to Jesus by night with a troubled mind, and [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s response was direct: "Except a man be born again, he cannot see the kingdom of God" (002-003). He was "treated altogether differently from what he expected; but I venture to say that was the most blessed night in all his life" (002-003).

### Why Nicodemus?

Moody is thankful that [[jesus-christ|Christ]] spoke of the new birth to Nicodemus rather than to the woman at the well, Matthew the publican, or [[zaccheus|Zaccheus]]. If [[jesus-christ|Christ]] had reserved this teaching for notorious sinners, people would say: "Oh yes, these publicans and harlots need to be converted: but I am an upright man; I do not need to be converted." Instead, [[jesus-christ|Christ]] delivered it to "one of the very best men in [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]]… an honorable Councillor… an orthodox man… one of the very soundest men." Yet to him [[jesus-christ|Christ]] said: "Except a man be born again, he cannot see the kingdom of God" (002-005, 002-011).

### Nicodemus's Question

Nicodemus's question — "How can these things be?" — represents the natural skepticism of the educated mind. Moody imagines "the scowl on that Pharisee's brow" as he hears [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s mysterious words (002-015). [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s answer uses the wind as an analogy: one cannot explain the wind's movements but can feel its effects — so is everyone born of the Spirit.


> Christ said to Nicodemus: "Except a man be born again, he cannot see the kingdom of God." You may see many countries; but there is one country — the land of Beulah, which John Bunyan saw in vision — you shall never behold, unless you are born again — regenerated by Christ.
>
> (002-034)

## Biblical Foundation
- **John 3:1–21** — The night visit and [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s discourse on the new birth (002-003, 002-005, 002-011, 002-015).
- **John 7:50–52** — Nicodemus defends Jesus before the Sanhedrin.
- **John 19:39–42** — Nicodemus brings spices for [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s burial.
