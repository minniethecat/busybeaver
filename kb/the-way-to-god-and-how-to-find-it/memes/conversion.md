# Conversion

## Definition

Conversion is the decisive turning of a sinner from sin to God, effected through repentance toward God and faith in the Lord [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]]. In [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, conversion is not a gradual process but an instantaneous event: "[[eve|Eve]]ry case of conversion recorded in the Bible was instantaneous. [[repentance|Repentance]] and faith came very suddenly. The moment a man made up his mind, God gave him the power" (006-015).

## Moody's Teaching

### Christ's Requirement

[[jesus-christ|Christ]] declared: "Except ye be converted, and become as little children, ye shall not enter the kingdom of heaven" (003-008). Conversion is not optional but essential. It involves a fundamental reorientation — a turning "right about face" from walking away from God to walking toward Him (006-011).

### Instantaneous Decision

Moody emphasizes the suddenness of true conversion with the testimony of a minister from Ohio who decided to begin a [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian life "within the last thirty seconds" while sitting in a class meeting. Between the time he started to kneel and when his knees struck the floor, "the Lord converted my soul" (006-017, 006-018). From that hour, "the devil has never dared to challenge my conversion" (006-019).

### Conversion Cannot Be Delayed

Moody warns against waiting. A man told him for five years that it "had not struck him yet" — and went to his grave undecided after sixty years of opportunity (006-020). Like a ship heading for the shore, "there is a moment… when he can halt and say, 'By the grace of God I will go no further towards death and ruin'" — but there is also "a moment when it was too late" (006-013).

### The Evidences of Conversion

True conversion produces visible change. The drunkard's home is transformed into a place of singing and peace (002-018). The converted man who had defrauded the government sent a check for $1,500 to the Treasury — "bringing forth fruits meet for repentance" (006-029). A changed life is the evidence of genuine conversion.

## Biblical Foundation

- **Matthew 18:3** — "Except ye be converted, and become as little children" (003-008).
- **Acts 3:19** — "[[repentance|Repent]] ye therefore, and be converted, that your sins may be blotted out."
- **Luke 22:32** — "When thou art converted, strengthen thy brethren."
