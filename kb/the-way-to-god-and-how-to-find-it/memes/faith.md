# Faith

## Definition

Faith, as [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]] presents it, is the act of taking God at His word without condition — "not a leap in the dark" but a belief in testimony (003-019). It involves three indispensable elements: knowledge of God, assent to the truth, and appropriation of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] as Saviour (003-023). Faith is "the hand that takes the blessing" (003-035), and it is the sole means by which a sinner receives eternal life.

> Faith is not a leap in the dark, as some tell us; but it is a belief in testimony. God does not ask any one to believe without giving him something to believe. You might as well ask a man to see without eyes, to hear without ears, or to walk without feet, as to bid him believe without giving him something to believe. (003-019)

## Moody's Teaching

### What Faith Is

Moody defines faith as "a belief in testimony" (003-019). God does not ask anyone to believe without giving them something to believe — that would be like asking a man to see without eyes or hear without ears (003-019). The testimony is God's own record concerning His Son:

> God hath given to us eternal life, and this life is in His Son. (003-017)

Using an analogy of a guidebook for a journey to California, Moody argues that if one finds a guidebook correct for nine-tenths of the journey, it is reasonable to trust it for the remainder (003-020). Similarly, if believing produces a new life, peace, joy, rest, self-control, and power to resist evil, one has good proof of being on the right road (003-022).

### The Three Elements: Knowledge, Assent, Appropriation

Moody identifies three things indispensable to faith (003-023):

1. **Knowledge** — "This is life eternal, that they might know Thee, the only true God, and [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]] whom Thou hast sent" (003-024).
2. **Assent** — Agreeing that what God has said is true. But assent alone will not save: "If a man simply give his assent to the plan of salvation, it will not save him" (003-024).
3. **Appropriation** — Actually laying hold of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] as Saviour, receiving and appropriating Him personally.

The beggar who received a thousand dollars did not worry about which hand received it — what mattered was that he had the money (003-035). The essential thing is not the quality of one's faith but having "the right kind of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]" (003-035).

### Faith Distinguished from Feeling

A major theme in Moody's treatment is that faith and feeling are separate:

> Feeling has nothing whatever to do with Believing. The Bible does not say "He that feeleth, or he that feeleth and believeth, hath everlasting life." I cannot control my feelings. If I could, I would never feel ill, or have a headache or toothache. But I can believe God. (003-034)

### Faith and the Brazen Serpent

Faith is illustrated by the Israelites bitten by serpents who were healed simply by looking at the brazen serpent on the pole (002-026). They did not need to understand the philosophy of the cure or have a certain feeling — they simply looked and lived. In the same way, "there is eternal life for every poor sinner. Look, and you can be saved, my reader, this very hour" (002-029).

Moody tells the story of a dying soldier at Murfreesboro who, upon hearing John 3:14–15 read three times, pillowed his head on those two verses and died in peace (002-033).

### Faith and the Believer's Security

The believer who has truly trusted [[jesus-christ|Christ]] possesses eternal life now. Moody emphasizes John 5:24:

> He that heareth My word, and believeth on Him that sent Me, hath — hath — everlasting life, and shall not come into condemnation; but is passed from death unto life. (003-026)

This is a present possession. The believer need not fear judgment: "Herein is our love made perfect, that we may have boldness in the day of judgment" (003-027).

### The Reasonableness of Faith

Moody argues that human society depends on testimony. "Human affairs would come to a standstill if we did not take the testimony of men" (003-018). Commerce would reach a deadlock within forty-eight hours if people refused to believe one another. If we receive the witness of fallible men, "why should we not take God at His word and believe His testimony?" (003-018).

### Faith and Works

[[salvation|Salvation]] is not obtained by works. It is "to him that worketh not, but believeth" (Romans 4:5) (002-014). The believer works because he is saved, not to be saved:

> We work from the cross; but not towards it. (002-014)

[[salvation|Salvation]] must be possessed before it can be worked out — just as one must have money before being careful how to spend it (002-014).

### The Simplicity of Faith

Faith requires no special education. "Thank God, we do not require an education to teach us how to look. That little girl, that little boy, only four years old, who cannot read, can look" (002-030). The way to be saved is simply to "look at the Lamb of God who taketh away the sin of the world" (002-030). And this believing must be immediate: "this very day — this very hour — this very moment" (002-031).

## Biblical Foundation

- **Numbers 21:4–9** — The bronze serpent, a type of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] lifted on the cross (002-025, 002-026).
- **John 3:14–15** — "As [[moses|Moses]] lifted up the serpent … even so must the Son of Man be lifted up; that whosoever believeth in Him should not perish" (002-021, 002-033).
- **John 5:24** — Present possession of eternal life through hearing and believing (003-026).
- **Acts 16:31** — "Believe on the Lord [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]], and thou shalt be saved" (003-012).
- **Romans 4:5** — [[salvation|Salvation]] "to him that worketh not, but believeth" (002-014).
- **1 John 5:9–12** — God's testimony concerning His Son (003-017).
