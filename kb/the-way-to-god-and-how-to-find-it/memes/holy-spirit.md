# Holy Spirit

## Definition

The Holy Spirit is the third Person of the Triune God, sent by the Father after [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s ascension to carry on the work of salvation in the world. In [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, the Holy Spirit is the active agent in [[conviction-of-sin|conviction of sin]], the [[new-birth|new birth]], and the [[assurance|assurance]] of the believer. Along with the Father and the Son, the Holy Spirit is pledged to keep every true child of God secure (007-048).

## Moody's Teaching

### The Spirit Convicts of Sin

After sending His prophets and His beloved Son — both rejected by humanity — God sent the Holy Spirit "to convince us of sin, and to show how we are to be saved" (002-021). This conviction is not a mere intellectual acknowledgment but an experiential work: Moody testifies, "I have felt the spirit of God working in my heart, just as really and as truly as I have felt the wind blowing in my face" (002-016). While he cannot reason it out — any more than he can reason out [[creation|creation]] — the reality of the Spirit's inward work is as undeniable as the wind.

### The Spirit at Pentecost

The Holy Spirit's descent at [[pentecost|Pentecost]] inaugurated the apostolic preaching of [[repentance|repentance]]. "After He had been glorified, and when the Holy Ghost came down, we find Peter on the day of [[pentecost|Pentecost]] raising the same cry, '[[repentance|Repent]]!'" (006-003). This preaching — "[[repentance|Repent]], and believe the Gospel" — produced the conversion of three thousand. The same Spirit fell upon the household of Cornelius when Peter preached [[jesus-christ|Christ]] to them, and they were baptized (004-009). The Spirit empowers the [[gospel|Gospel]] message.

### The Spirit as Guide and Keeper

"For as many as are led by the Spirit of God they are the sons of God" (007-032). Just as the soldier follows his captain or the traveler his guide, "the Holy Spirit will be the guide of every true child of God." The Spirit's keeping power is part of the Triune pledge: "The Father, the Son, and the Holy Ghost, are pledged to keep us" (007-048). No believer is kept by their own strength — it is the operation of the Spirit of God that produces security (007-064).

### The Spirit's Transforming Power

"Let the love of God be shed abroad in your heart by the Holy Ghost today: it will drive away darkness; it will drive away gloom; it will drive away sin; and peace and joy shall be yours" (001-046). Moody presents the Spirit not merely as a theological abstraction but as the source of daily [[grace|grace]], joy, and victory over sin. The believer who is "ignorant," "simple and foolish" needs the guidance of the Holy Spirit in every situation and at every time (008-024).
