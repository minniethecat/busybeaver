# Heaven

Heaven is the eternal dwelling place of God and the final destination of the redeemed. In [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, heaven is not an automatic destination for all humanity but a "prepared place for a prepared people" (002-020). Entry is conditioned on the [[new-birth|new birth]] — "except ye be converted, and become as little children, ye shall not enter into the kingdom of heaven" (Matthew 18:3) (002-004). A person must be made fit for heaven through regeneration.

## Definition

Heaven is the realm of God's immediate presence, the abode of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] since His ascension, and the eternal inheritance awaiting those who have been born again. Moody emphasizes that heaven is a real place — not a state of mind or a metaphor — prepared by [[jesus-christ|Christ]] Himself. "Bear in mind the mansions which [[jesus-christ|Christ]] has gone to prepare" (002-035). It is characterized by perfect holiness, joy, and the unveiled presence of God.

## Moody's Teaching

### A Prepared Place for a Prepared People

Moody's most distinctive teaching on heaven is that it requires preparation. "Heaven is a prepared place for a prepared people" (002-020). An unregenerated person would not want to be in heaven. "Take a man whose heart is full of corruption and wantonness, and place him in heaven among the pure and holy, and he would not want to stay there" (002-020). If unregenerate people were taken to heaven, "there would be another rebellion in heaven" (002-020).

> Heaven is a prepared place for a prepared people. If a gambler or a blasphemer were taken out of the streets of New York and placed on the crystal pavement of heaven and under the shadow of the tree of life, he would say, "I do not want to stay here." If men were taken to heaven just as they are by nature, without having their hearts regenerated, there would be another rebellion in heaven.
>
> (002-020)

### The Attraction of Heaven

Moody asks what makes heaven attractive, and answers: it is not golden streets or pearly gates, but the presence of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]. "The thought that we shall see [[jesus-christ|Christ]] who has loved us and given Himself for us — that is the charm of heaven" (001-030). The love of [[jesus-christ|Christ]], not the splendor of the surroundings, draws the believer heavenward.

### Citizens of Two Worlds

Moody draws on Paul's language: "The first man is of the earth, earthy: the second man is the Lord from heaven" (005-002). The natural man belongs to earth; the regenerate man belongs to heaven. The new birth transfers citizenship from earth to heaven, though the believer still lives as a pilgrim here.

### The Narrow Entrance

The law of entrance into heaven is non-negotiable: "Except ye be converted, and become as little children, ye shall not enter into the kingdom of heaven" (002-004). [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s words in John 3 — "except a man be born again, he cannot see the kingdom of God" (002-002) — establish that the new birth is the sole entrance requirement.

## Biblical Foundation

- **Matthew 18:3** — "Except ye be converted... ye shall not enter the kingdom of heaven" (002-004)
- **John 14:2–3** — [[jesus-christ|Christ]] prepares mansions for His people (002-035)
- **John 3:3** — "Except a man be born again, he cannot see the kingdom of God" (002-002)
- **1 Corinthians 15:47** — "The second man is the Lord from heaven" (005-002)
- **Matthew 28:18** — "All power is given unto Me in heaven and in earth" (005-020)
