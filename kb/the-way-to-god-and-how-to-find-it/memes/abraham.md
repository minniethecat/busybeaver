# Abraham

## Overview

Abraham was the founding patriarch of Israel, called by God to leave his homeland and journey to a promised land. [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]] references Abraham as an example of faith: he "caught a glimpse of" the heavenly city "and from that time became a pilgrim and sojourner" (002-035). Abraham's faith-motivated pilgrimage prefigures the journey of every believer toward the heavenly kingdom.

## Role in Moody's Teaching

### The Pilgrim of Faith

Moody cites Hebrews 11:8, 10–16 to show that Abraham's faith made him a stranger on earth. He saw the city "which hath foundations, whose builder and maker is God" and lived accordingly. Those who are not born again "shall never see" that city (002-035).

### The Pattern of Belief

Abraham is the biblical archetype of faith — the one who "believed God, and it was counted unto him for righteousness" (Romans 4:3). While Moody does not develop this extensively, he references Abraham as the model of taking God at His word.
