# Justification

Justification is the judicial act of God by which He declares a believing [[sin|sinner]] righteous on the basis of [[jesus-christ|Christ's]] finished work. For [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]], this doctrine is the heart of the gospel: the sinner who believes is counted righteous before God — not because of any personal merit, but solely because [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s righteousness is imputed to him through [[faith]].

## Definition

Justification is a legal declaration, not a moral transformation. It is God the Judge pronouncing the believing sinner "not guilty" and positively righteous on the basis of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s substitutionary death and resurrection. As Paul declares, [[jesus-christ|Christ]] "was delivered for our offences, and was raised again for our justification" (Romans 4:25) (007-047). It is received by faith alone — "to him that worketh not, but believeth" (Romans 4:5) (002-014).

## Moody's Teaching

### Received by Faith, Not Works

Moody insists that justification cannot be earned. It is the gift of God to those who believe. The believer is justified the moment he trusts [[jesus-christ|Christ]] — there is no probationary period, no gradual improvement, no accumulation of merit. [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s righteousness is credited to the believer's account instantly and irrevocably.

### The Publican's Justification

Moody uses the parable of the [[the-pharisee-and-the-publican|Pharisee and the Publican]] to illustrate justification. [[the-pharisee-and-the-publican|The Pharisee]] — respectable, religious, morally upright — was not justified. The publican — a despised tax collector who could only cry "God be merciful to me a sinner" — went down to his house justified (006-035). "Whosoever exalteth himself shall be abased; and he that humbleth himself shall be exalted" (006-035). Justification is for the undeserving who know they are undeserving.

### The Ground: Christ's Work

The sole ground of justification is [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s death and resurrection. Moody repeatedly returns to Romans 4:25: [[jesus-christ|Christ]] was delivered to death because of our offences; He was raised to life because our justification was secured. The resurrection is God's receipt — the public declaration that the debt has been paid in full.

> It is not your badness, therefore, but His goodness that leads to repentance; hence the true way to repent is to believe on the Lord Jesus Christ, "who was delivered for our offences, and was raised again for our justification."
>
> (006-025)
