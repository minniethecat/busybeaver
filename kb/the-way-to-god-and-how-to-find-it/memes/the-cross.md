# The Cross

## Definition

The cross of [[calvary|Calvary]] is the central event of the [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian faith — where [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]], the Son of God, was lifted up to die for the sins of the world. In [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, the cross is the supreme demonstration of God's love, the completed work of salvation, and the object toward which every sinner must look in faith to receive eternal life. "Nothing speaks to us of the love of God, like the cross of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]" (001-027).

## Moody's Teaching

### The Cross Reveals God's Love

The cross is the ultimate proof that God loves humanity. "When we wish to know the love of God we should go to [[calvary|Calvary]]. Can we look upon that scene, and say God did not love us? That cross speaks of the love of God. Greater love never has been taught than that which the cross teaches" (001-014).

What prompted God to give up [[jesus-christ|Christ]]? What prompted [[jesus-christ|Christ]] to die? The answer is love. "Greater love hath no man than this, that a man lay down his life for his friends" (001-014). But [[jesus-christ|Christ]] went further — He laid down His life for His enemies, for His murderers, for those who hated Him. [[eve|Eve]]n when mocked and derided on the cross, [[jesus-christ|Christ]] prayed "Father, forgive them, for they know not what they do" — there was nothing but love in His heart (001-014).

Moody makes the striking observation that it required more love for the Father to give His Son than for the Son to die. Reflecting on his own experience as a father with an only son, he says: "Oh, the love that God must have had for the world when He gave His Son to die for it!" (001-026).

> When we wish to know the love of God we should go to Calvary. Can we look upon that scene, and say God did not love us? That cross speaks of the love of God. Greater love never has been taught than that which the cross teaches. What prompted God to give up Christ? — what prompted Christ to die? — if it were not love? "Greater love hath no man than this, that a man lay down his life for his friends."
>
> (001-014)

### The Cross as the Completed Work

When [[jesus-christ|Christ]] cried on [[calvary|Calvary]], "It is finished!" (John 19:30), He meant exactly what He said. "All that men have to do now is just to accept of the work of [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]]" (002-015). Nothing can be added to what [[jesus-christ|Christ]] accomplished. There is no hope for anyone trying to work out salvation for themselves — the work is done, and the only response is to receive it by faith.

### The Cross as the Remedy

Moody draws his most extended illustration from Numbers 21, where [[moses|Moses]] lifted up a brazen serpent on a pole in the wilderness. Those Israelites bitten by fiery serpents who looked at the uplifted serpent were healed. [[jesus-christ|Christ]] applied this to Himself: "As [[moses|Moses]] lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, even so must the Son of Man be lifted up; that whosoever believeth in Him should not perish, but have eternal life" (002-021).

The key lesson is that healing did not come from understanding the remedy or having certain feelings — it came simply from looking. "It was just a look; and they were well" (002-026). So sinners need not look at the church (the pole) or at ministers, but must look "beyond the pole" to "the Crucified One" — to [[calvary|Calvary]] (002-029).

### The Look of Faith

Moody repeatedly emphasizes the simplicity of the saving look. No education is required: "That little girl, that little boy, only four years old, who cannot read, can look" (002-030). The dying soldier at Murfreesboro, upon hearing John 3:14–15 read three times, pillowed his head on those two verses and died in peace. His last words were a whisper of the [[the-word-of-god|Scripture]]: "As [[moses|Moses]] lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, even so must the Son of Man be lifted up; that whosoever believeth in Him should not perish, but have eternal life" — then, "That's enough; don't read any more" (002-033).

### The Cross and Unchanging Love

The cross demonstrates that God's love is unchanging. [[jesus-christ|Christ]] knew that [[judas-iscariot|Judas]] would betray Him and Peter would deny Him, yet He loved them to the end (001-015). On the night of His betrayal, He washed the disciples' feet — wanting to convince them of His unchanging love. The cross is the ultimate expression of this love that never wavers.

### Love Before We Loved

A crucial point Moody emphasizes is that God loved us before we ever thought of Him. "Herein is love, not that we loved God, but that He loved us, and sent His Son to be the propitiation for our sins" (001-028). The cross was not God's response to human love; it was God's initiative while humanity was still in rebellion.

### The Spirit of Calvary

The spirit of the cross is love. While being mocked, [[jesus-christ|Christ]] did not call down fire from heaven — His response was forgiveness (001-014). This spirit of [[calvary|Calvary]] — love in the face of hatred, forgiveness in response to mockery — is the model for [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian living.

## Biblical Foundation

- **Numbers 21:4–9** — The brazen serpent, the Old Testament type of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s crucifixion (002-025, 002-026).
- **John 3:14–15** — [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s own application of the serpent to His coming crucifixion (002-021).
- **John 15:13** — "Greater love hath no man than this, that a man lay down his life for his friends" (001-014).
- **John 19:30** — "It is finished!" — the completed work (002-015).
- **Romans 5:6–8** — [[jesus-christ|Christ]] died for the ungodly; God's love demonstrated at the cross (001-032, 001-033).
- **1 John 4:10** — [[jesus-christ|Christ]] as the propitiation for sins, sent because God loved us first (001-028).
