# Holiness

## Definition

Holiness in [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching is closely tied to sanctification — the lifelong process by which a believer is conformed to the character of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]. While [[justification]] is instantaneous, "sanctification is a lifework" (007-032). Holiness is not something the believer produces by effort; [[jesus-christ|Christ]] Himself is our Sanctification, and "we draw all our power for holy life from Him" (008-022).

## Moody's Teaching

### Sanctification as a Lifework

Moody draws a clear distinction between justification and sanctification: "[[justification|Justification]] is instantaneous; but sanctification is a lifework" (007-032). A new believer may be a true child of God yet not a matured [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian — just as "a tree may be perfect in its first year of growth; but it does not attain its maturity." The [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian life is one of progressive growth: Peter instructs believers to "grow in grace, and in the knowledge of our Lord and Saviour [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]]," adding to faith virtue, knowledge, temperance, patience, godliness, brotherly kindness, and charity (007-032). These graces are to be added one upon another.

### Christ Our Sanctification

[[jesus-christ|Christ]] is not only Saviour but "our Mediator, our Sanctifier, our Justifier" (008-021). Moody emphasizes that "it would take volumes to tell what He desires to be to every individual soul." In a notable catalog of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s offices, he declares: "He is our Sanctification; we draw all our power for holy life from Him" (008-022). The believer contributes nothing to their own holiness — it is entirely derived from union with [[jesus-christ|Christ]].

### The Need for Holiness

Moody, quoting an extended meditation on the soul's need, writes: "I am naked and bare; and I need His holiness and innocence for a covering" (008-024). The believer's own righteousness is insufficient; [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s holiness must clothe them. This need is not occasional but constant — "in no situation, and at no time, can I do without Him." The pursuit of holiness is thus not a matter of self-improvement but of deeper dependence on [[jesus-christ|Christ]].

### False Starts and the Danger of Forgetting

Moody observes that dramatic events — even death in a family — often produce temporary religious impressions: "Often, when death comes into a family, it looks as if the event would be sanctified to the [[conversion|conversion]] of all who are in the house. Yet in six months' time all may be forgotten" (006-005). True holiness is not the product of passing emotion but of sustained [[faith|faith]] and the Spirit's continuing work.
