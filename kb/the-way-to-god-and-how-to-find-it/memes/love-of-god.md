# Love of God

## Definition

The love of God is the unchanging, unfailing, and unsearchable affection that God has toward every human being — not only the righteous but also the ungodly and the sinner (001-033). It is supremely demonstrated in the cross of [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]] and is the foundational truth upon which the Gospel rests. As [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]] presents it, if people could truly be convinced that God loves them, "how we should find them crowding into the kingdom of heaven" (001-004).

## Moody's Teaching

### A Truth Under Attack

Moody opens his book with this theme because he considers it the most important truth in the Bible — and the one [[satan|Satan]] most wants to destroy. "For more than six thousand years he has been trying to persuade men that God does not love them" (001-007). The devil succeeded with [[adam|Adam]] and [[eve|Eve]] and continues to succeed with their descendants. The result is that people run from God, believing He hates them, when in reality He loves them (001-004).

Moody illustrates this with the story of a prodigal in [[chicago|Chicago]] who saw the text "God is Love" displayed in gas jets over a church pulpit. Though he resisted the thought at first, the words lodged in his heart, he went into the meeting, and left rejoicing in the discovery that God had loved him all the time he had wandered (001-005).

### The Unchanging Nature of God's Love

Human love is changeable — it may grow cold or turn to hatred — but God's love is constant. Moody cites John 13:1: of Jesus it is recorded that "having loved His own which were in the world, He loved them unto the end" (001-015). [[jesus-christ|Christ]] knew [[judas-iscariot|Judas]] would betray Him and Peter would deny Him, yet He loved them both. It was this unchanging love that broke Peter's heart and brought him back in penitence.

On the night of His betrayal, Jesus took a basin of water and washed the disciples' feet — He wanted to convince them of His unchanging love (001-015). Moody considers John 14 one of the sweetest portions of [[the-word-of-god|Scripture]], where [[jesus-christ|Christ]] promises: "If a man love Me, he will keep My words; and My Father will love him; and We will come unto him, and make Our abode with him" (001-016).

### Love Beyond Comprehension

God's love is vast beyond human understanding. Quoting Ephesians 3:18, Moody speaks of "the breadth, and length, and depth, and height, of God's love" — an ocean into which one must plunge before knowing anything of it (001-010). Like Columbus discovering America without knowing its great lakes and rivers, believers have only begun to discover God's love.

The most remarkable expression of this love is John 17:23, where [[jesus-christ|Christ]] declares that the Father loves believers "as Thou hast loved Me" (001-018). Though believers are rebellious and foolish while [[jesus-christ|Christ]] was perfectly obedient, God loves those who trust in [[jesus-christ|Christ]] with the same love He has for His own Son.

### The Cross as Supreme Proof

"Nothing speaks to us of the love of God, like the cross of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]" (001-027). When one wishes to know the love of God, one should go to [[calvary|Calvary]]. [[jesus-christ|Christ]] laid down His life not for friends but for enemies, for His murderers, for those who hated Him (001-014). [[eve|Eve]]n while being mocked and derided, [[jesus-christ|Christ]] prayed "Father, forgive them, for they know not what they do" — there was nothing but love in His heart (001-014).

Moody makes the striking point that it required more love for the Father to give His Son than for the Son to die. Reflecting on his own experience as a father with an only son, he says: "Oh, the love that God must have had for the world when He gave His Son to die for it!" (001-026). He confesses he has never been able to preach from John 3:16 — "it is so high that I can never climb to its height" (001-026).

### God Loves the Sinner, Not Just the Righteous

A crucial distinction Moody makes is between sin and the sinner: "God loves the sinner; but He hates the sin" (001-019). God hates sin because it mars human life — it is precisely because He loves the sinner that He hates sin. Romans 5:8 affirms that "God commendeth His love toward us, in that, while we were yet sinners, [[jesus-christ|Christ]] died for us" (001-033).

God did not begin to love us after we loved Him. "Herein is love, not that we loved God, but that He loved us, and sent His Son to be the propitiation for our sins" (001-028). Long before we thought of God, we were in His thoughts.

### A Mother's Love as Comparison

Moody uses maternal love — the strongest human love known — as a comparison, but insists it falls infinitely short. "No mother in this world ever loved her child as God loves you and me" (001-026). He tells the story of a mother who sat through her son's murder trial and, after his execution, asked to be buried beside him, unashamed of being known as the mother of a murderer (001-024).

He also recounts the story of a prodigal daughter in Glasgow who, seeing her mother's picture hung on the wall of a mission, was broken by the realization that her mother had not forgotten or cast off her sinning child. The sight of her mother's face brought her to repentance and home (001-025).

### What Makes Heaven Attractive

[[heaven|Heaven]] is attractive not because of pearly gates or golden streets, but because there believers will behold the One who loved them enough to give His Son to die (001-030). Moody illustrates this with a story of a little girl whose mother died; after the burial she ran through the house crying "Mamma! mamma!" — when she could not find her loved one, the house held no attraction. So it will be in heaven: the presence of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] is its central joy.

### The Call to Respond

Moody closes with an earnest appeal: "If anyone is lost it will not be because God does not love him: it will be because he has resisted the love of God" (001-029). The love of the Father should draw sinners home. He urges the reader not to let unbelief keep them from God, insisting that God loves even the ungodly and wants to save (001-031).

> Nothing speaks to us of the love of God, like the cross of Christ. Come with me to Calvary, and look upon the Son of God as He hangs there. Can you hear that piercing cry from His dying lips: "Father, forgive them; for they know not what they do!" and say that He does not love you? "Greater love hath no man than this, that a man lay down his life for his friends." But Jesus Christ laid down His life for his enemies.
>
> (001-027)

## Biblical Foundation

### Old Testament
- **[[isaiah|Isaiah]] 49:15–16** — God's love exceeds even a mother's: "Can a woman forget her sucking child? … yea, they may forget; yet will I not forget thee" (001-020).

### New Testament
- **John 3:16** — "God so loved the world, that He gave His only begotten Son" — the supreme text that Moody finds too high to preach from (001-026).
- **John 13:1** — [[jesus-christ|Christ]] "loved them unto the end" even knowing betrayal awaited (001-015).
- **John 14:20–23** — The Father and Son will come and make their abode with those who love [[jesus-christ|Christ]] (001-016).
- **John 15:13** — "Greater love hath no man than this, that a man lay down his life for his friends" (001-014).
- **John 17:23** — The Father loves believers as He loves the Son (001-018).
- **Romans 5:6–8** — [[jesus-christ|Christ]] died for the ungodly, demonstrating God's love while we were still sinners (001-032, 001-033).
- **Ephesians 3:18–19** — The breadth, length, depth, and height of God's love which "passeth knowledge" (001-002, 001-010).
- **1 John 4:10** — "Not that we loved God, but that He loved us" (001-028).
