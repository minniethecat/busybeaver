# Blessedness

## Definition

In [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, blessedness (or beatitude) is the state of divine favour and spiritual happiness that belongs to those who receive God's grace. It is not mere earthly happiness or material prosperity, but the deep, settled joy of knowing that one's sins are forgiven and that one belongs to God. "Blessed Gospel; blessed, precious, news. Believe it today; receive it into your heart; and enter into a new life" (001-008). For Moody, blessedness is inseparable from the [[new-birth|New Birth]], the forgiveness of sins, and the restoration of the [[backsliding|backslider]].

## Moody's Teaching

### The Blessedness of Being Born Again

Moody declares that the night of [[nicodemus|Nicodemus]]'s interview with [[jesus-christ|Christ]] "was the most blessed night in all his life" and that "to be 'born again' is the greatest blessing that will ever come to us in this world" (002-003). The new birth is not merely a theological doctrine but the gateway into blessedness — the single greatest blessing a human being can receive in this life. [[eve|Eve]]ry other blessing flows from this one: to be made a child of God is to enter the sphere of all spiritual blessing.

> "He brought me to the banqueting house, and His banner over me was love... His left hand is under my head, and His right hand doth embrace me" (Song of Solomon 2:4, 6). Thank God we can come under the banner today if we will. Any poor sinner can come under that banner today. His banner of love is over us. Blessed Gospel; blessed, precious, news. Believe it today; receive it into your heart; and enter into a new life.
>
> (001-046)

### Forgiveness as the Ground of Blessedness

Quoting the restored backslider [[david|David]], Moody writes: "Blessed is he whose transgression is forgiven, whose sin is covered" (009-037). Forgiven sin is the foundation of blessedness. Moody uses this truth to encourage the backslider: "We should never have had the thirty-second Psalm if [[david|David]] had not been restored" (009-037). The blessedness of forgiveness is available not only at initial conversion but also upon restoration after wandering. [[david|David]]'s "beautiful fifty-first Psalm" and Peter's "wonderful sermon on the day of [[pentecost|Pentecost]] when three thousand were converted" are both the fruits of restored backsliders (009-037). Blessedness is not a one-time condition that can be permanently lost; it is recoverable through repentance.

### The Blessed Hope of Christ's Return

In his presentation of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s deity, Moody cites Revelation 22:7: "Behold, I come quickly: blessed is he that keepeth the sayings of the prophecy of this book" (005-031). The return of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] is a source of blessedness for those who hold to His word. This is the forward-looking dimension of beatitude: the believer does not only enjoy present blessedness but anticipates the consummation of all things at [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s appearing.

### The Blessing of Faith

Moody uses the illustration of a beggar who receives a gift of a thousand dollars to teach that "faith is the hand that takes the blessing" (003-030). The blessing is already provided — it is the gift of God in [[jesus-christ|Christ]]. What matters is not the quality of one's faith (which hand receives the gift) but the object of one's faith (the gift itself). "Many people are always thinking whether the faith by which they lay hold of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] is the right kind — but what is far more essential is to see that we have the right kind of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]" (003-030). The blessedness is in the Saviour, not in the act of believing.

### Blessedness and Obedience

Moody relates blessedness to keeping God's word: "Behold, I come quickly: blessed is he that keepeth the sayings of the prophecy of this book" (005-031). The blessed life is not merely a life of passive enjoyment but one of active obedience. Those who keep [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s commandments dwell in Him and He in them, and the result is the consciousness of His presence — which is itself blessedness.

### Blessedness Through Restitution

Moody recounts the story of a friend who had defrauded the government and, after conversion, could find no peace. "He had a terrible struggle; his conscience kept rising up and smiting him. At last he drew a check for $1,500 and sent it to the United States Treasury. He told me he received such a blessing when he had done it" (006-015). This is "bringing forth fruits meet for repentance" — the blessedness that follows restitution is not earning forgiveness but removing the obstacle to fellowship. The man who withholds what belongs to another cannot know the blessedness of a clear conscience.

## Biblical Foundation

Moody's references to blessedness draw from Psalm 32:1 (the blessedness of forgiven sin), Revelation 22:7 (blessedness of keeping [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s words), and the broader biblical theme of beatitude. His use is practical and experiential — blessedness is not an abstract state but the tangible result of receiving God's grace in the new birth, walking in obedience, making restitution, and awaiting [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s return.
