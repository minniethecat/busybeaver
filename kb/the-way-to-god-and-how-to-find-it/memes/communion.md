# Communion

## Definition

Communion, in [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s "The Way to God," refers to the intimate, ongoing fellowship between the believer and the Lord. It is more than occasional prayer — it is a continuous state of spiritual connection and mutual indwelling. Moody presents communion as both the privilege and the protection of the [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian life.

## Communion as Discernment

Moody teaches that a believer in true communion with God develops spiritual discernment — the ability to recognize whether a messenger speaks truth. "If he is really in communion with God," the believer can "soon tell" whether a voice is the Shepherd's or a stranger's (008-019). When God sends a true messenger, "his words will find a ready response in the [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian heart." This communion-grounded discernment protects against being "carried away by errors and heresies."

> An Eastern shepherd was once telling a gentleman that his sheep knew his voice, and that no stranger could deceive them. The gentleman thought he would like to put the statement to the test. So he put on the shepherd's frock and turban, and took his staff and went to the flock. He disguised his voice, and tried to speak as much like the shepherd as he could; but he could not get a single sheep in the flock to follow him.
>
> (008-019)

## Daily Communion as a Safeguard

Moody presents daily communion with the Lord as the surest defense against [[backsliding|backsliding]]. He observes that those "once in fellowship and in daily communion with the Lord" who later fall away, "now think more of their dresses and ornaments than of their precious souls" (009-010). The loss of daily communion is the first step toward spiritual decline.

## The Closet and Communion

The secret of maintaining communion lies in private devotion. Moody argues that if one would "get into the closet and shut out the world and hold communion with the Master you cannot leave Him" (009-025). The language of the heart in such communion is, "To whom shall we go? Thou hast the words of eternal life" (John 6:68). The backslider never formally bids [[jesus-christ|Christ]] farewell — they simply cease the daily communion that keeps the soul bound to the Saviour.

## Communion and the New Birth

True communion with God is a mark of the [[new-birth|regenerate]] heart. Moody distinguishes between mere religious observance and genuine spiritual fellowship. The believer who is "really in communion with God" (008-019) has passed from death unto life and possesses the inward witness of the Spirit.
