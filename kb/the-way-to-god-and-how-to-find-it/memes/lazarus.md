# Lazarus

## Biblical Account

Lazarus of Bethany was the brother of Mary and Martha and a close friend of [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]]. When Lazarus fell ill and died, Jesus deliberately delayed His journey, arriving four days after Lazarus had been laid in the tomb. At the tomb, Jesus "cried with a loud voice, 'Lazarus, come forth!' And Lazarus heard, and came forth" (005-026, citing John 11:43).

## Moody's Teaching

### Christ's Inherent Power

[[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]] uses the raising of Lazarus as a key argument for the [[blasphemy|divinity of Christ]]. He draws a sharp distinction between [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s miracles and those of the Old Testament prophets: "In the rare instances in which men have raised the dead, they did it by the power of God. They called on God to do it. But when [[jesus-christ|Christ]] was on earth He did not call upon the Father to bring the dead to life" (005-023). Jesus spoke directly: "Lazarus, come forth!" (005-026). He had power to impart life by His own word.

### The Specificity of the Call

Moody notes a striking detail: "Someone has said, It was a good thing that Lazarus was mentioned by name, or all the dead within the sound of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s voice would immediately have risen" (005-027). This observation underscores the absolute authority of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s voice over death — a power that belongs to God alone.

### The Divinity Argument

The raising of Lazarus forms part of Moody's cumulative case for [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s deity. If Jesus were merely a man, His claim in John 5:25 — "The hour is coming, and now is, when the dead shall hear the voice of the Son of God; and they that hear shall live" — would be "downright blasphemy" (005-028). The miracle at Bethany demonstrates that [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s claims were true: He is the [[resurrection|Resurrection]] and the Life.
