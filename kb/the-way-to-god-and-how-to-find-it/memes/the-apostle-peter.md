# The Apostle Peter

## Overview

Simon Peter was one of the twelve apostles of [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]] and became the foremost preacher of the early church. [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]] uses Peter as a powerful illustration of the unchanging love of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]. Despite knowing that Peter would deny Him and swear he never knew Him, "yet He loved Peter. It was the love which [[jesus-christ|Christ]] had for Peter that broke his heart, and brought him back in penitence to the feet of his Lord" (001-015).

## Role in Moody's Teaching

### The Object of Unchanging Love

Peter is Moody's prime example that [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s love does not waver even when His followers fail. On the night of His betrayal, Jesus washed the disciples' feet — including Peter's — "He wanted to convince them of His unchanging love" (001-015). Though Peter would deny Him, [[jesus-christ|Christ]] loved him to the end (John 13:1).

### The Preacher of Repentance

After [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s glorification and the descent of the [[holy-spirit|Holy Spirit]], Peter stood on the day of [[pentecost|Pentecost]] and raised the same cry that [[john-the-baptist|John the Baptist]] and [[jesus-christ|Christ]] had raised: "[[repentance|Repent]]!" It was this preaching — "[[repentance|Repent]], and believe the Gospel — that wrought such marvellous results then" (Acts 2:38–47) (006-003).

### A Witness to Instantaneous Conversion

Peter's own restoration demonstrates that conversion can be instantaneous. After his denial, the look of Jesus broke his heart, and he was restored. His preaching produced instantaneous results at [[pentecost|Pentecost]], where thousands were converted in a single day.


> He knew that one of His disciples would betray Him; yet He loved Judas. He knew that another disciple would deny Him, and swear that he never knew Him; and yet He loved Peter. It was the love which Christ had for Peter that broke his heart, and brought him back in penitence to the feet of his Lord.
>
> (001-015)

## Biblical Foundation
- **John 13:1–17** — [[jesus-christ|Christ]] washes Peter's feet, demonstrating unchanging love (001-015).
- **Matthew 26:69–75** — Peter's denial and bitter weeping.
- **Acts 2:38–47** — Peter's [[pentecost|Pentecost]] sermon and the "marvellous results" (006-003).
