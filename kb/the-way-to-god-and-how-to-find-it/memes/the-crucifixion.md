# The Crucifixion

The crucifixion of [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]] at [[calvary]] is the central event of the [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian faith and the fulcrum upon which [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s entire message turns. It is at the cross that the [[love-of-god|love of God]] is supremely displayed, the work of [[salvation]] is completed, and the [[sin|sinner]] finds the object for saving [[faith]]. [[eve|Eve]]ry doctrine Moody preaches — sin, grace, redemption, new birth — converges at [[calvary|Calvary]]. Without the crucifixion, there is no gospel.

## Overview

The crucifixion of [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]] occurred around AD 30 at [[calvary|Golgotha]] ([[calvary|Calvary]]), outside the walls of [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]], under the Roman governor Pontius Pilate. [[jesus-christ|Christ]] was scourged, mocked, and nailed to a cross between two criminals. He died after approximately six hours and was buried in a nearby tomb. For Moody, the historical fact of the crucifixion is inseparable from its theological meaning — it is the event in which God's love and justice meet.

## Moody's Treatment

### The Demonstration of Love

Moody points to [[calvary|Calvary]] as the irrefutable proof of God's love. "When we wish to know the love of God we should go to [[calvary|Calvary]]. Can we look upon that scene, and say God did not love us? That cross speaks of the love of God" (001-014). [[jesus-christ|Christ]] did not die for friends but "laid down His life for His enemies; [[jesus-christ|Christ]] laid down His life for His murderers; [[jesus-christ|Christ]] laid down His life for them that hated Him" (001-014).

> "Nothing speaks to us of the love of God, like the cross of Christ. Come with me to Calvary, and look at the cross of Christ." (001-027)

### The Completed Work

[[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s cry on [[calvary|Calvary]] — "It is finished!" (John 19:30) — means exactly what it says. "All that men have to do now is just to accept of the work of [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]]. There is no hope for man or woman so long as they are trying to work out salvation for themselves" (002-015). The crucifixion is not the beginning of a process the sinner must complete; it is the finished ground of salvation, to which nothing can be added.

### The Spirit of the Cross

While being mocked and derided on the cross, [[jesus-christ|Christ]] did not call down fire from heaven. He prayed, "Father, forgive them; for they know not what they do." Moody's summary: "That is love. He did not call down fire from heaven to consume them; there was nothing but love in His heart" (001-014). The spirit of [[calvary|Calvary]] — love in the face of hatred, forgiveness in the face of cruelty — is the measure of true [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ianity.

### The Brazen Serpent Fulfilled

[[jesus-christ|Christ]] Himself connected the crucifixion to the Old Testament type: "As [[moses|Moses]] lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, even so must the Son of Man be lifted up; that whosoever believeth in Him should not perish, but have eternal life" (002-021). The brazen serpent in Numbers 21 was lifted on a pole so that bitten Israelites could look and live; [[jesus-christ|Christ]] lifted on the cross is the antitype — all who look to Him in faith receive eternal life.

## Historical Context

Crucifixion was a Roman method of execution reserved for slaves, rebels, and the worst criminals. It was designed to be public, prolonged, and maximally humiliating. The victim was typically scourged before being nailed or tied to a crossbeam. Death came by suffocation as the victim lost the strength to push up and breathe. Moody's 19th-century audience understood crucifixion as the lowest form of death — which makes the theological claim that God chose this death to display His love all the more striking.
