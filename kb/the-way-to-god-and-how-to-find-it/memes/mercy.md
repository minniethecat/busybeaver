# Mercy

## Definition

Mercy is God's compassionate willingness to forgive, bless, and restore sinners who turn to Him. In [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, mercy is not something God must be persuaded to show — He is "already merciful, as He has fully shown at the Cross of [[calvary|Calvary]]" (006-025). The sinner does not need to incline God to mercy; God already extends it.

## Moody's Teaching

### Mercy Already Extended

Moody strongly warns against the idea that repentance earns mercy: "If you mean by this that you must repent in order to incline God to be merciful to you, the sooner you give over such repentance the better. God is already merciful" (006-025). The cross is the full and final demonstration of divine mercy — nothing more needs to be done to persuade God.

### Mercy and the Prodigal

The prodigal son's story illustrates God's mercy. When the son returned, the father did not demand penance — he ran to meet him. The thought that brought the prodigal home was not fear of punishment but the belief that his father loved him (001-029). God's mercy is like a father's — ready, eager, waiting.

### Mercy Received Through Repentance

While mercy is always available, it is received through repentance. "Wherever God sees true repentance in the heart He meets that soul" (006-036). The publican's prayer — "God be merciful to me a sinner!" — is the model of receiving mercy: humble, direct, trusting (006-034).

### Mercy and Restitution

Moody tells of a defaulter who stole from his employers and, when confronted, asked if he could use the stolen money to start a business and pay it back. Moody's response: "that was a delusion of [[satan|Satan]]." True repentance requires restitution before expecting mercy (006-027). When the man returned the money, his employers showed mercy — "Forgive him! Yes, we will be glad to forgive him" (006-028).

## Biblical Foundation
- **Luke 18:13** — "God be merciful to me a sinner!" — the publican's prayer (006-034).
- **Ephesians 2:4** — "God, who is rich in mercy, for His great love wherewith He loved us."
- **Psalm 103:8** — "The Lord is merciful and gracious, slow to anger, and plenteous in mercy."
