## Overview

Dwight Lyman Moody (1837–1899) was a prominent American evangelist and publisher, closely associated with the revivalist movement of the late nineteenth century. He founded the Moody Church in [[chicago|Chicago]], the Moody Bible Institute, and Moody Publishers. "The Way to God and How to Find It," first published in 1884, is among his most widely read works, presenting the core of his evangelical gospel message in accessible, illustration-rich prose.

## Role in Moody's Teaching

Although Moody is the author rather than a subject of his own theological exposition, he appears in the text through personal anecdotes that illustrate his preaching points. These self-references reveal something of the man behind the message.

In one extended illustration, Moody describes himself as a man dying of consumption — an inherited disease — who is offered a remedy by a trusted friend (002-023). The friend testifies that he was once in the same condition and was cured by this medicine. The illustration serves as an analogy for the gospel: humanity is dying of the inherited disease of sin, and [[jesus-christ|Christ]] — the trusted Friend — offers the only remedy, freely, on the basis of His own experience of its power.

Elsewhere, Moody recounts an encounter with "an infidel" who challenged him to preach [[john-the-baptist|John the Baptist]] rather than [[jesus-christ|Christ]], claiming the Baptist was the greater figure (005-017). Moody's response — offering to follow the man preaching John while himself preaching [[jesus-christ|Christ]], to see who would accomplish more — reveals his unwavering conviction that the risen [[jesus-christ|Christ]], not any mere prophet, is the power of the gospel.

Moody also offers a personal reflection on the [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian life, recalling that a man once asked him, "Moody, how do you feel?" (008-010). He observes that he had to stop and consider, because it had been so long since he thought about his feelings. His counsel is that believers who fix their gaze on [[jesus-christ|Christ]], rather than constantly examining their emotions, will find peace and joy following them naturally — just as a shadow follows the one who walks facing the sun.
