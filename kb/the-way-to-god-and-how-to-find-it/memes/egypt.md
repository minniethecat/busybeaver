# Egypt

## In Moody's Teaching

[[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]] references Egypt throughout "The Way to God" as a symbol with dual significance. On one hand, Egypt represents God's providential preservation of His people — "He kept [[joseph-ot|Joseph]] in Egypt" (004-018). On the other, it stands for the bondage from which God delivers: "the children of Israel were placed behind the blood...but they would still have heard the crack of the slave-driver's whip if they had not been delivered from the Egyptian yoke of bondage" (008-005).

## The Exodus and Deliverance

Moody uses Egypt's most famous biblical episode — the Exodus — to illustrate the completeness of God's deliverance. "When God had redeemed the children of Israel from the bondage of Egypt, and brought them through the Red Sea, they struck out for the wilderness; and then God became to them their Way" (008-007). Just as Israel was brought out of Egypt, the believer is brought out of the bondage of sin — not partially but completely.

The Exodus also serves as a warning about the hardness of the human heart. Moody observes that "you might think that the king of Egypt repented when God sent the terrible plagues upon him and his land. But it was not repentance at all. The moment God's hand was removed [[pharaoh|Pharaoh]]'s heart was harder than ever" (006-004). This illustrates the difference between mere fear and true [[repentance|repentance]].

## Egypt as the World

In his teaching on [[backsliding|backsliding]], Moody uses Egypt as a metaphor for the world that tempts believers away from God. The backslider says in effect, "I am going back to the world, to the fleshpots of Egypt" (009-025). This echoes Israel's complaint in the wilderness when they longed to return to Egypt. Moody warns that true fellowship with [[jesus-christ|Christ]] is incompatible with a heart turned toward the world.


> When God had redeemed the children of Israel from the bondage of Egypt, and brought them through the Red Sea, they struck out for the wilderness; and then God became to them their Way. I am so thankful the Lord has not left us in darkness as to the right way. There is no living man who has been groping in the darkness but may know the way. "I am the Way," says Christ.
>
> (008-007)

## God's Sovereignty Over Nations

Moody notes that God's power extended over Egypt even when Israel was in bondage. "He kept [[joseph-ot|Joseph]] in Egypt; [[moses|Moses]] before [[pharaoh|Pharaoh]]; [[daniel|Daniel]] in [[babylon|Babylon]]; and enabled [[elijah|Elijah]] to stand before Ahab in that dark day" (004-018). Egypt, for all its might, was subject to the God of Israel. The nation that forgot God's deliverance — "the God who brought them out of Egypt, and who led them through the desert into the promised land" — would face judgment (009-017).
