# Hope

## Definition

Hope, in [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, is not the uncertain wishing of common speech but the confident expectation of future blessing grounded in the character and promises of God. The doctrine of the [[new-birth|New Birth]] is "the foundation of all our hopes for the world to come" (002-002). [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian hope is inseparable from [[faith|faith]] and [[assurance|assurance]] — it is the forward-looking dimension of a salvation already possessed. "We do not say that we 'hope' we are [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ians. I do not say that I 'hope' I am an American, or that I 'hope' I am a married man" (007-041). For the believer, hope is not conjecture but certainty.

## Moody's Teaching

### Hope as Certainty, Not Conjecture

Moody draws a sharp distinction between worldly hope and [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian hope. "I may say that I 'hope' to go back to my home, or I hope to attend such a meeting. I do not say that I 'hope' to come to this country, for I am here" (007-041). In the same way, the believer does not merely hope to be saved — he knows he is saved because he is already in [[jesus-christ|Christ]]. The confidence of Paul in his final letter to Timothy — "I know whom I have believed, and am persuaded that He is able to keep that which I have committed unto Him" (007-041) — is the model of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian hope. It is not optimism but knowledge.

### The Foundation of Hope

"The doctrine of the [[new-birth|New Birth]] is therefore the foundation of all our hopes for the world to come" (002-002). Without regeneration, there is no valid hope of heaven. "You cannot be baptized into the [[kingdom-of-god|Kingdom of God]]. 'Except a man be born again, he cannot see the [[kingdom-of-god|Kingdom of God]].' If anyone reading this rests his hopes on anything else — on any other foundation — I pray that God will open his eyes" (002-008). Hope built on religious observance, moral effort, or church membership is a hope without foundation. Only the new birth provides a ground for confidence.

### Hope and the New Birth

The evidence of the new birth includes a new kind of hope: "if, by believing, I get a new life, and a hope, a peace, a joy, and a rest to my soul, that I never had before; if I get self-control, and find that I have a power to resist evil and to do good — I may reason that I have been born of the Spirit" (003-022). Hope, [[peace|peace]], and [[joy|joy]] are the experiential triad that confirms regeneration. A person without hope lacks the testimony of the Spirit.

### False Hopes

Moody warns against resting hope on the wrong foundation. "There is no hope for man or woman so long as they are trying to work out salvation for themselves" (002-015). Self-righteousness is a false hope. So is delay: "There is no hope for you if you neglect the remedy" (002-024). The only ground of hope is the finished work of [[jesus-christ|Christ]], received by faith now — not a vague intention to repent someday. "Parents, if you hope to see your children who have gone before, you must be born of the Spirit" (002-036). [[eve|Eve]]n the deepest natural affection cannot substitute for the new birth.

### Hope as a Mark of the Redeemed Life

Moody uses the illustration of a mother's love to picture the tenacity of true hope: "In good repute, in bad repute, in the face of the world's condemnation, a mother loves on, and hopes that her child may turn from his evil ways and repent" (001-021). A hope that persists despite all contrary evidence — like the mother who "still lives in the hope that she will see her long lost Charlie" (001-034) — reflects something of the divine [[love-of-god|love]] that "hopeth all things." [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian hope, however, rests on a far surer foundation than parental affection: it rests on the promise of God.

> Now the strongest human love that we know of is a mother's love. Many things will separate a man from his wife. A father may turn his back on his child; brothers and sisters may become inveterate enemies; husbands may desert their wives; wives, their husbands. But a mother's love endures through all. In good repute, in bad repute, in the face of the world's condemnation, a mother loves on, and hopes that her child may turn from his evil ways and repent.
>
> (001-021)
