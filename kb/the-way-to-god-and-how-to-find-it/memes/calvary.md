# Calvary

Calvary (Golgotha, Aramaic for "the place of the skull") is, in [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s preaching, far more than a geographical location. It is the theological centre of the [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian faith — the place where the [[love-of-god|love of God]] was definitively displayed, where [[the-crucifixion|Christ died]] for sinners, and where [[salvation]] was purchased. [[eve|Eve]]ry sinner, Moody insists, must come to Calvary. It is the destination toward which his entire book points.

## Overview

Calvary is the hill outside the walls of first-century [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]] where [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]] was crucified around AD 30. The name "Calvary" derives from the Latin *calvaria* (skull), translating the Aramaic *Gulgolta*. The exact location is uncertain — the traditional site is within the Church of the Holy Sepulchre, though some identify it with Gordon's Calvary (the "skull hill" near the Garden Tomb). Moody is unconcerned with the archaeological question; for him, Calvary's significance is entirely redemptive.

## References in the Text

### The Demonstration of Love

Moody continually directs his readers to Calvary as the place where God's love is seen most clearly. "When we wish to know the love of God we should go to Calvary. Can we look upon that scene, and say God did not love us? That cross speaks of the love of God" (001-014). The cross is the answer to every doubt about God's disposition toward sinners.

> "Come with me to Calvary, and look at the cross of Christ." (001-027)

### The Completed Work

It was at Calvary that [[jesus-christ|Christ]] cried, "It is finished!" (John 19:30). "When [[jesus-christ|Christ]] cried out on Calvary, 'It is finished!' He meant what He said. All that men have to do now is just to accept of the work of [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]]" (002-015). The location is inseparable from the event — Calvary is where the debt was paid in full.

### The Spirit of Calvary

The spirit of Calvary is love in the face of hatred. While being mocked on the cross, [[jesus-christ|Christ]] prayed for His murderers' forgiveness. Moody's summary: "the spirit of the cross, the spirit of Calvary, is love" (001-014). This spirit defines authentic [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ianity — it is not doctrinal precision or religious observance but the willingness to love enemies as [[jesus-christ|Christ]] loved His.

### The Object of Faith

Moody urges the sinner: "Look to Calvary. Bear in mind, sinner, that Jesus died for all" (002-029). The saving look is not to the church — "it is not the pole; it is to the Crucified One" — but to the Person who died at Calvary. The bronze serpent was lifted in the wilderness; [[jesus-christ|Christ]] was lifted at Calvary.

### Divine Mercy Demonstrated

"God is already merciful, as He has fully shown at the Cross of Calvary; and it is a grievous dishonor to His heart of love if you think that your tears and anguish will move Him" (006-025). Calvary proves that God's mercy is not something to be earned by human sorrow — it is an accomplished fact.

## Significance in Moody's Theology

Calvary is not merely the scene of a historical event; it is a present-tense reality for the seeking sinner. Moody's repeated refrain — "go to Calvary," "look to Calvary," "come to Calvary" — treats the site as a living reality accessible by faith. The sinner does not need to travel to [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]]; he needs only to look in faith to the One who died there.
