# John the Baptist

## Overview

John the Baptist was the divinely appointed forerunner of [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]], whose ministry prepared the way for the Messiah. [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]] presents John as the first herald of the gospel call to repentance — a voice crying in the wilderness whose single message was then taken up by [[jesus-christ|Christ]], the disciples, Peter at [[pentecost|Pentecost]], and Paul at Athens.

## Role in Moody's Teaching

### The First Herald of Repentance

Moody traces the call to repentance as a chain, beginning with John: "Until John the Baptist met [[jesus-christ|Christ]], he had but one text, '[[repentance|Repent]] ye; for the kingdom of heaven is at hand' (Matthew 3:2). But if he had continued to say this, and had stopped there without pointing the people to [[jesus-christ|Christ]] the Lamb of God, he would not have accomplished much" (006-002).

### The Pattern for All Preaching

John's ministry established the pattern: repentance and faith together. He called for repentance, but he also pointed to "[[jesus-christ|Christ]] the Lamb of God." The message is incomplete without both elements. Moody uses John to show that the call to repentance is not a later invention but the very foundation of gospel preaching.

### Greater Than Christ?

Moody relates an encounter with an infidel who claimed John was "a greater character than [[jesus-christ|Christ]]" and urged Moody to preach him instead. Moody's response: "My friend, you preach John the Baptist; and I will follow you and preach [[jesus-christ|Christ]]: and we will see who will do the most good." The infidel admitted Moody would do more good. Moody's conclusion: "John was beheaded; and his disciples begged his body and buried it: but [[jesus-christ|Christ]] has risen from the dead" (005-017).
