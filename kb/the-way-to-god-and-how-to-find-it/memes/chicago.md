# Chicago

## Overview

Chicago, Illinois, was the primary base of [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s ministry. He built a church there and conducted evangelistic work in the city. Several of the personal anecdotes in "The Way to God" are set in Chicago, giving the book its intimate, first-hand character.

## References in the Text

### The Gas-Jet Text

"We built a church in Chicago some years ago; and were very anxious to teach the people the love of God. We thought if we could not preach it into their hearts we would try and burn it in; so we put right over the pulpit in gas-jets these words — God is Love" (001-005). A prodigal saw the text from the street, came in, and was converted.

### The Defaulter's Restitution

Moody tells of preaching in "one of our large cities" and the story of a defaulter who restored stolen money — a Chicago narrative illustrating the fruit of repentance (006-027, 006-028).
