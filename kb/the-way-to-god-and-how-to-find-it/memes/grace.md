# Grace

## Definition

Grace is the unmerited, undeserved favor of God extended to sinful humanity. In [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, grace is inseparable from the love of God displayed at [[calvary|Calvary]]. It is not human badness that moves God to mercy but "the goodness of God leadeth thee to repentance" — grace is the initiative of a merciful God, not a response to human worthiness (006-025).

## Moody's Teaching

### Grace vs. Works

Grace stands in direct opposition to salvation by works. "It is not your badness, therefore, but His goodness that leads to repentance; hence the true way to repent is to believe on the Lord [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]]" (006-025). The sinner does not earn repentance through tears or anguish — God is already merciful, as fully shown at the Cross.

### Grace and Repentance

True repentance is itself a response to grace. Moody quotes [[dr-brooks|Dr. Brooks]]: repentance is "the judgment which the sinner pronounces upon himself, in view of the love of God displayed in the death of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]" (006-024). Grace leads; repentance follows. This is why the gospel is good news — God has taken the initiative.

### Grace in Conversion

The instantaneous nature of conversion demonstrates grace. "The moment a man made up his mind, God gave him the power" (006-015). God does not ask anyone to do what they lack the power to do — when a sinner turns, grace supplies the power.


> "Use me then, my Saviour, for whatever purpose, and in whatever way, Thou mayest require. Here is my poor heart, an empty vessel; fill it with Thy grace. Here is my sinful and troubled soul; quicken and refresh it with Thy love. Take my heart for Thine abode; my mouth to spread the glory of Thy name; my love and all my powers, for the advancement of Thy believing people."
>
> (008-025)

## Biblical Foundation
- **Ephesians 2:8–9** — "By grace are ye saved through faith… not of works."
- **Romans 5:20** — "Where sin abounded, grace did much more abound."
- **Romans 3:24** — "Justified freely by His grace through the redemption that is in [[jesus-christ|Christ Jesus]]."
