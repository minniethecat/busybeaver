# Pentecost

## Overview

The day of Pentecost, recorded in Acts 2, is the event when the [[holy-spirit|Holy Spirit]] descended upon the disciples gathered in [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]]. [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]] references Pentecost as the occasion when Peter raised the same cry of repentance that [[john-the-baptist|John the Baptist]], [[jesus-christ|Christ]], and the disciples had preached — and it "wrought such marvellous results" (006-003).

## Moody's Treatment

### The Continuity of the Gospel Call

Moody traces the call to repentance through a chain: [[john-the-baptist|John the Baptist]] → [[jesus-christ|Christ]] → the disciples → Peter at Pentecost → Paul at Athens. Each preached the same message: "[[repentance|Repent]], and believe the Gospel." At Pentecost, this message was accompanied by the power of the [[holy-spirit|Holy Spirit]] and produced the conversion of thousands (006-003).

### Marvellous Results

The result of Peter's Pentecost sermon was three thousand souls added to the church in a single day (Acts 2:41). Moody emphasizes that "it was this preaching — [[repentance|Repent]], and believe the Gospel — that wrought such marvellous results then" (006-003). The same message, preached in the power of the same Spirit, produces the same results.


> On the day of Pentecost three thousand gladly received the Word. They were not only converted, but they were baptized the same day.
>
> (004-010)
