# Zaccheus

## Biblical Account

Zaccheus was a chief tax collector (publican) in Jericho, a man of wealth and, in the eyes of his fellow Jews, a sinner by profession. When Jesus passed through Jericho, Zaccheus "sought to see Jesus; and because he was little of stature he climbed up a tree" (004-008). Seeing him, Jesus called, "Zaccheus, make haste, and come down" — and salvation entered that house that very day.

## Moody's Teaching

### Sudden Conversion

[[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]] points to Zaccheus as a prime example of instantaneous [[conversion|conversion]]. "His conversion must have taken place somewhere between the branch and the ground," Moody observes (004-008). There was no prolonged process, no gradual awakening — the moment Zaccheus encountered [[jesus-christ|Christ]], everything changed.

### Evidence of Genuine Change

Moody emphasizes that Zaccheus' conversion was immediately validated by action. "We are told that he received Jesus joyfully, and said, 'Behold, Lord, the half of my goods I give to the poor; and if I have taken anything from any man by false accusation, I restore him fourfold'" (004-008, citing Luke 19:8). This is what Moody calls "fruits meet for [[repentance|repentance]]" — true conversion produces concrete [[restitution|restitution]].

### A Pattern for All

Moody notes the significance that [[jesus-christ|Christ]] taught the doctrine of the [[new-birth|New Birth]] to [[nicodemus|Nicodemus]], a respected ruler, rather than to Zaccheus the publican or Matthew the publican (002-005). If [[jesus-christ|Christ]] had reserved this teaching for notorious sinners, the self-righteous would have dismissed it as irrelevant to them. Zaccheus represents the class of obvious sinners whose need of salvation is clear to all — yet his conversion demonstrates that no one is beyond the reach of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s saving call.
