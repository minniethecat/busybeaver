# Adam

## Overview

Adam is the first man, created directly by God and placed in [[heaven|Paradise]]. [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]] uses Adam theologically to explain the origin of sin and the pattern of redemption. Adam fell through unbelief — by not believing God's word — and humanity "got life back again by believing — by taking God at His word. In other words we get up where Adam fell down" (003-014). Adam is also used to contrast with [[jesus-christ|Christ]]: "The first man is of the earth earthy: the second man is the Lord from heaven" (005-002).

## Role in Moody's Teaching

### The Fall Through Unbelief

Moody's key insight is that Adam fell by unbelief. "Man lost life by unbelief — by not believing God's word; and we got life back again by believing" (003-014). The parallel is exact: what was lost through unbelief is recovered through faith. Adam "stumbled and fell over the stone of unbelief; and we are lifted up and stand upright by believing" (003-014).

### Satan's Deception of Adam

"For more than six thousand years [[satan|Satan]] has been trying to persuade men that God does not love them. He succeeded in making our first parents believe this lie; and he too often succeeds with their children" (001-007). The devil's original lie — that God does not love — continues to deceive.

### The Contrast with Christ

Moody cites 1 Corinthians 15:47 to distinguish Adam from [[jesus-christ|Christ]]: "The first man is of the earth earthy: the second man is the Lord from heaven" (005-002). Adam's disobedience brought death; [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s obedience brings life.

### A Richer Inheritance

"The redeemed child of God is heir to a richer and more glorious inheritance than Adam in Paradise could ever have conceived; yea, and that inheritance endures forever — it is inalienable" (003-031). Moody notes he "would much rather have my life hid with [[jesus-christ|Christ]] in God than have lived in [[heaven|Paradise]]; for Adam might have sinned and fallen after being there ten thousand years. But the believer is safer" (003-032).

### Adam's Sin and Personal Responsibility

Moody addresses those who complain about being held responsible for Adam's sin: "If you are lost, it will not be on account of Adam's sin" (002-022). Each person is lost because they, like Adam, refuse to believe — they spurn the remedy offered in [[jesus-christ|Christ]].

> Men are trying to patch up this "old Adam" nature. There must be a new creation. Regeneration is a new creation; and if it is a new creation, it must be the work of God.
>
> (002-012)

## Biblical Foundation
- **Genesis 1–3** — [[creation|Creation]], [[heaven|Paradise]], and the Fall (003-014, 001-007).
- **1 Corinthians 15:45–47** — "The first man is of the earth earthy: the second man is the Lord from heaven" (005-002).
- **Romans 5:12–19** — Adam as the federal head through whom sin entered.
