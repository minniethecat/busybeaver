# Commandments

## Definition

In [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, the commandments of God are not merely external rules to be obeyed but the expression of a living relationship with [[jesus-christ|Christ]]. They serve two primary functions in his thought: as the test of genuine [[love-of-god|love]] for God ("He that hath My commandments, and keepeth them, he it is that loveth Me," John 14:21), and, in the case of the First Commandment, as the decisive argument for the deity of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]. [[obedience|Obedience]] to the commandments is not the ground of [[salvation|salvation]] — that is by [[faith|faith]] alone — but it is the evidence that salvation has truly taken place, and the path along which [[assurance|assurance]] is strengthened.

## Moody's Teaching

### Keeping Commandments as the Evidence of Love

Moody repeatedly returns to Jesus's words in John 14 as the heart of the matter: "He that hath My commandments, and keepeth them, he it is that loveth Me: and he that loveth Me shall be loved by My Father, and I will love him, and will manifest Myself to him" (001-006). The relationship is reciprocal: love for [[jesus-christ|Christ]] issues in obedience, and obedience is met with further revelation of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]. "If a man love Me, he will keep My words; and My Father will love him; and We will come unto him, and make Our abode with him" (001-006). Moody marvels that "the Father and the Son so love us that They desire to come and abide with us. Not to tarry for a night, but to come and abide in our hearts" (001-006). [[obedience|Obedience]] is not servile compliance but the response of love to love.

> There is no portion of Scripture I read so often as John 14; and there is none that is more sweet to me. I never tire of reading it. Hear what our Lord says, as He pours out His heart to His Disciples: "At that day ye shall know that I am in My Father, and ye in Me, and I in you. He that hath My commandments, and keepeth them, he it is that loveth Me: and he that loveth Me shall be loved by My Father" (John 14:20-21). Think of the great God who created heaven and earth loving you and me!

### Commandments and Assurance

In his exposition of the tests of [[assurance|assurance]], Moody cites 1 John 3:24: "And he that keepeth His commandments dwelleth in Him, and He in him. And hereby we know that He abideth in us, by the Spirit which He hath given us" (007-041). Keeping the commandments is one of the objective grounds upon which a believer may know that he has [[everlasting|eternal life]]. The inward witness of the Spirit is confirmed by the outward evidence of a life that obeys God. Moody teaches that the Spirit's presence can be discerned: "We can tell what kind of Spirit we have if we possess the Spirit of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] — a [[jesus-christ|Christ]]-like spirit — not the same in degree, but the same in kind" (007-042). The person who is "meek, gentle, and forgiving," who has "a spirit filled with peace and joy," who is "long-suffering and gentle, like the Son of God" (007-042) — such a person has passed the test.

### The First Commandment and the Deity of Christ

Moody's most distinctive use of the commandments is his appeal to the First Commandment — "Thou shalt have no other gods before Me" (Exodus 20:3) — as proof of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s deity. "Look at the millions throughout [[jesus-christ|Christ]]endom who worship [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]] as God. If [[jesus-christ|Christ]] be not God this is idolatry. We are all guilty of breaking the first commandment if [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]] were mere man — if He were a created being, and not what He claims to be" (005-028). This is a profound argument: either [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]] is God, or the entire [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian church is guilty of the most fundamental violation of God's law. The fact that [[jesus-christ|Christ]] Himself accepted worship — and that [[angel|angels]], in contrast, refused it — confirms that He is not a creature but the Creator. The First Commandment thus becomes not merely a moral directive but a [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ological touchstone.

### Commandments and Backsliding

In his treatment of [[backsliding|backsliding]], Moody uses "commandments" to describe the path from which the backslider has wandered. Quoting Revelation 2:4–5, he notes that the backslider has "left the first love" and must "repent, and do the first works" (009-035). The backslider is one who has "wandered from the path of His commandments" (009-036). The remedy is not to seek a repetition of the same experience but to return to the path of obedience: "If you confess your sins and tell Him that you have wandered from the path of His commandments He will restore unto you the joy of His salvation" (009-036). Restoration comes not through emotional experience but through renewed obedience.

### Obedience Not Legalism

Moody is careful to distinguish keeping [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s commandments from legalism. The man trying to "work out salvation for himself" has "no hope" (002-003). [[salvation|Salvation]] is by [[faith|faith]] alone, through the new birth. But the new birth produces a new nature that delights in God's commandments. The commandments are not the ladder by which we climb to God but the path on which we walk with Him. The distinction is critical: commandments-keeping is the fruit of salvation, never its root.

## Biblical Foundation

Moody's teaching on the commandments draws primarily from John 14:20–23 (love and obedience), 1 John 3:24 (obedience and assurance), Exodus 20:3 (the First Commandment as [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ological argument), and Revelation 2:4–5 (backsliding as departure from the commandments). His use of the commandments is consistently relational rather than legal — they define the shape of the believer's love-relationship with [[jesus-christ|Christ]].
