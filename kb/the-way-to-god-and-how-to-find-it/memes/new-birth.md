# New Birth

## Definition

The new birth — also called regeneration, being "born again," or being "born from above" — is the supernatural work of God by which a sinner receives a new spiritual nature and becomes a child of God. As [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]] presents it, this is not human reformation or turning over a new leaf, but a new creation accomplished by God alone. [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s words are absolute: "Except a man be born again, he cannot see the kingdom of God" (002-003).

## Moody's Teaching

### The Absolute Necessity

The doctrine of the new birth is "the foundation of all our hopes for the world to come" and "the A B C of the [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian religion" (002-002). Moody asserts that a person who is unsound on this doctrine will be unsound on almost every other fundamental doctrine, while a true understanding of it helps solve a thousand difficulties in the Word of God (002-002).

The necessity is absolute and universal. [[jesus-christ|Christ]] spoke these words not to a publican, a harlot, or an outcast, but to [[nicodemus|Nicodemus]] — a ruler of the Jews, a doctor of the law, one of the best men in [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]], a member of the Sanhedrin (002-005). If [[nicodemus|Nicodemus]] needed to be born again, so does everyone.

[[the-word-of-god|Scripture]] reinforces this with multiple "except" passages: "Except ye repent, ye shall all likewise perish" (Luke 13:3); "Except ye be converted, and become as little children, ye shall not enter the kingdom of heaven" (Matthew 18:3); "Except your righteousness shall exceed the righteousness of the Scribes and Pharisees, ye shall in no case enter the kingdom of heaven" (Matthew 5:20). "They all really mean the same thing" (002-004).

### What the New Birth Is Not

Moody is careful to distinguish regeneration from common misunderstandings (002-007):

- **It is not going to church.** Many think church attendance makes them [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ians.
- **It is not turning over a new leaf.** Making new resolutions is not the new birth.
- **It is not [[baptism]].** One may be baptized into the church without being born into the kingdom of God. Baptism is right in its place, but if put in the place of regeneration, "it is a terrible mistake" (002-007).
- **It is not taking the Lord's Supper.** The sacrament commemorates [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s death but does not impart new life.
- **It is not saying prayers.** A person may pray regularly and yet never be born of the Spirit.

> Another class say, "I go to the Lord's Supper; I partake uniformly of the Sacrament." Blessed ordinance! Jesus hath said that as often as ye do it ye commemorate His death. Yet, that is not being "born again;" that is not passing from death unto life. Jesus says plainly — and so plainly that there need not be any mistake about it — "Except a man be born of the Spirit, he cannot enter into the Kingdom of God."
>
> (002-012)

### Regeneration Is God's Work Alone

Regeneration is a new creation, and if it is a new creation, "it must be the work of God" (002-012). In Genesis 1, when God created the earth, man was not present to take part. When [[jesus-christ|Christ]] redeemed the world, He was alone (002-012). Human effort cannot produce the new birth any more than the Ethiopian can change his skin or the leopard his spots.

"That which is born of the flesh is flesh; and that which is born of the Spirit is spirit" (002-013). No amount of self-improvement can transform flesh into spirit. "It is utterly impossible to make a man better without [[jesus-christ|Christ]]" (002-012). People try to "patch up this 'old [[adam|Adam]]' nature" but what is needed is not patching but a completely new creation.

### The Mystery and Reality of the New Birth

Moody acknowledges that regeneration cannot be fully explained by human reason. Like [[nicodemus|Nicodemus]], many ask, "How can these things be?" [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s answer uses the wind as an analogy: "The wind bloweth where it listeth, and thou hearest the sound thereof, but canst not tell whence it cometh and whither it goeth: so is every one that is born of the Spirit" (002-015).

Just as one cannot see the wind but can feel its effects, one cannot fully explain regeneration but can observe its transforming power. Moody tells the story of a poor drunkard whose home was like hell on earth — his wife bearing marks of his blows, his children hiding in fear. After conversion, the same home is filled with singing, the children cluster around his knee, and his wife's face glows with happiness. "Is not that a picture of Regeneration?" (002-018).

### Evidence of the New Birth

The evidence of regeneration is a transformed life. Old things pass away and all things become new (2 Corinthians 5:17). The person is "not reformed only, but regenerated — new men in [[jesus-christ|Christ Jesus]]" (002-017). The tongues that once cursed and blasphemed now praise God. The new birth imparts power to overcome temptation and live a righteous life.

### Heaven for the Twice-Born

"For a prepared place" there must be "a prepared people" (002-020). An unregenerated person would not be happy in heaven. Placed among the pure and holy, one whose heart is full of corruption would say, "I do not want to stay here." If people were taken to heaven without regenerated hearts, "there would be another rebellion in heaven" (002-020).

### The Kingdom Seen Only by the Regenerated

Moody closes his chapter on the new birth with a solemn listing of what the unregenerated will never experience: they will never see the land of Beulah, the tree of life, the river from God's throne, the King of kings, or the crown of life; their ears will never hear the song of [[moses|Moses]] and the Lamb; they will never see the mansions [[jesus-christ|Christ]] has prepared or attend the marriage supper of the Lamb (002-034, 002-035).

## Biblical Foundation

- **John 3:3–8** — [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s discourse with [[nicodemus|Nicodemus]]: "Except a man be born again, he cannot see the kingdom of God" (002-003, 002-004, 002-015).
- **Matthew 18:3** — "Except ye be converted, and become as little children" (002-004, 003-008).
- **Matthew 5:20** — [[righteousness|Righteousness]] exceeding that of the scribes and Pharisees (002-004).
- **Luke 13:3, 5** — "Except ye repent, ye shall all likewise perish" (002-004).
- **2 Corinthians 5:17** — "If any man be in [[jesus-christ|Christ]], he is a new creature" (002-017).
- **John 1:12–13** — Born "not of blood, nor of the will of the flesh, nor of the will of man, but of God."
