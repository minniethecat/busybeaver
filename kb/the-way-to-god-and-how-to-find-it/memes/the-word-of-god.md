# The Word of God

## Definition

The Word of God is the inspired, authoritative revelation of God to humanity — the Scriptures that testify of [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]] and provide everything necessary for salvation and godly living. In [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, the Word is the instrument God uses to bring sinners to conviction and conversion, and the foundation upon which faith rests.

## Moody's Teaching

### The Power of the Word

Moody emphasizes the Word's inherent power to convict. A prodigal in [[chicago|Chicago]] saw the text "God is Love" in gas jets over a pulpit, and though he resisted, the words "seemed to stand out right before him in letters of fire" (001-005). He went into the meeting, and "the light of the Gospel broke into his mind." Moody comments: "It is of little account what men say if the Word of God only gets an entrance into the sinner's heart" (001-005).

### The Word as Testimony

[[faith|Faith]] is built upon the testimony of God's Word. "God has borne witness to [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]]. And if man can believe his fellow men who are frequently telling untruths… why should we not take God at His word and believe His testimony?" (003-018). God has never broken a single promise in six thousand years, and "there would be a jubilee in hell today if one word that He has spoken could be broken" (003-014).

### The Word as Seed

The Word is the means of the new birth. "Being born again, not of corruptible seed, but of incorruptible, by the word of God, which liveth and abideth forever" (1 Peter 1:23). It is "the truth" by which men are sanctified (John 17:17).

### The Sufficiency of the Word

Moody demonstrates the sufficiency of Scripture by the story of the dying soldier who, upon hearing John 3:14–15 read three times, pillowed his head on those two verses and found peace. "That's enough; don't read any more" (002-033). One verse — or two — believed, is enough for salvation.

### The Word and Prayer

While Moody distinguishes prayer from regeneration, he affirms the Word and prayer together as vital: "Many people, when they become anxious about their soul's salvation, say, 'I will pray, and read the Bible'" — but he warns these alone, without repentance, do not effect the new birth (006-009).

## Biblical Foundation
- **John 5:24** — "He that heareth My word, and believeth… hath everlasting life" (003-026).
- **1 Peter 1:23** — "Being born again… by the word of God, which liveth and abideth forever."
- **Hebrews 4:12** — "The word of God is quick, and powerful, and sharper than any two-edged sword."
