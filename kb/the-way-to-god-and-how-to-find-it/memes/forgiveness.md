# Forgiveness

## Definition

Forgiveness is God's gracious pardon of sin, extended freely to all who truly repent and trust in [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]]. As [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]] presents it, forgiveness is not earned by penance or feeling but is the immediate response of God to a repentant heart. It is the expression of the love displayed at [[calvary|Calvary]], where [[jesus-christ|Christ]] prayed, "Father, forgive them; for they know not what they do" (001-014).

## Moody's Teaching

### God's Readiness to Forgive

God is already merciful and ready to forgive. The cross of [[calvary|Calvary]] fully demonstrates this: "God is already merciful, as He has fully shown at the Cross of [[calvary|Calvary]]; and it is a grievous dishonor to His heart of love if you think that your tears and anguish will move Him" (006-025). It is not human badness but "the goodness of God leadeth thee to repentance" (006-025).

### The Condition of Forgiveness

Forgiveness requires true repentance. Moody illustrates this with a parent whose son steals money. If the son refuses to confess and says he will do it again, the parent cannot rightfully forgive. "Do you think God can forgive a man when he does not want to be forgiven? Would he be happy if God forgave him in this state of mind?" (006-022).

### Forgiveness and Restitution

Genuine forgiveness is tied to restitution. "If we have done wrong to anyone we should never ask God to forgive us, until we are willing to make restitution" (006-026). Moody tells the story of a defaulter who stole $1,500 from his employers. Only after returning the money and confessing did he find forgiveness and peace (006-027, 006-028).

### The Spirit of Forgiveness

[[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s prayer on the cross — "Father, forgive them; for they know not what they do" — exemplifies the spirit of forgiveness (001-014). [[eve|Eve]]n while being mocked and derided, [[jesus-christ|Christ]] did not call down fire from heaven; "there was nothing but love in His heart" (001-014).

### Forgiveness and the Prodigal

The story of the prodigal son illustrates God's forgiving heart. What brought the prodigal home was the thought that his father loved him still (001-029). The father did not cast off his sinning child — he ran to meet him. In the same way, God waits to forgive and bless those who return to Him.

## Biblical Foundation

- **Luke 23:34** — "Father, forgive them; for they know not what they do" — [[jesus-christ|Christ]] on the cross (001-014).
- **Acts 2:38** — "[[repentance|Repent]] … for the remission of sins."
- **Ephesians 1:7** — "In whom we have redemption through His blood, the forgiveness of sins."
- **1 John 1:9** — "If we confess our sins, He is faithful and just to forgive us."
