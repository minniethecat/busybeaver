# Everlasting Life

## Definition

In [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, everlasting life (also called eternal life) is the unending, indestructible life that God gives to every person who believes on [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]]. It is not merely endless existence — for the lost also exist forever — but the quality of life that belongs to God Himself, imparted to the believer at the moment of [[faith|faith]]. "He that believeth on the Son hath everlasting life" (John 3:36, cited 003-024). This is a present possession, not a future hope: "The gift of God is eternal life through [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]] our Lord" (Romans 6:23, cited 004-017). The believer does not wait to receive it at death; he has it now.

## Moody's Teaching

### Life Received by Faith

The dominant theme of Moody's preaching on everlasting life is its accessibility through simple faith. In his extended exposition of the brazen serpent (John 3:14–15), he repeatedly emphasises the offer: "As [[moses|Moses]] lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, even so must the Son of Man be lifted up; that whosoever believeth in Him should not perish, but have eternal life" (002-009, 011–012). The word "whosoever" is the key — the offer is universal. Moody tells of a dying soldier in a hospital at Murfreesboro who found peace in those very verses: "He lingered a few hours, pillowing his head on those two verses; and then went up in one of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s chariots, to take his seat in the kingdom of God" (002-012). A man who felt he had "sinned all my life" found that "whosoever" included him.

### A Present Possession

Moody insists that everlasting life is not something the believer hopes to receive someday but something he possesses now. Citing John 5:24, he writes: "Verily, verily — which means truly, truly — I say unto you, He that heareth My word, and believeth on Him that sent Me, hath — hath — everlasting life, and shall not come into condemnation; but is passed from death unto life" (004-006). The double "hath" is emphatic: the believer already has it. "There is enough truth in that one verse for every soul to rest upon for salvation. It does not admit the shadow of a doubt" (004-006).

### The Life That Cannot Die

Moody uses vivid imagery to convey the indestructibility of everlasting life: "All the gravediggers in the world cannot dig a grave large enough and deep enough to hold eternal life; all the coffin makers in the world cannot make a coffin large enough and tight enough to hold eternal life" (004-008). Death had its hand on [[jesus-christ|Christ]] once at [[calvary|Calvary]], "but never again" (004-008). Because [[jesus-christ|Christ]] lives forevermore, the life He imparts shares His own immortality. The believer is not merely promised a future resurrection; he is united to the One who is Himself the [[resurrection|Resurrection]].

### More Than Adam Lost

"The redeemed child of God is heir to a richer and more glorious inheritance than [[adam|Adam in Paradise]] could ever have conceived; yea, and that inheritance endures forever — it is inalienable" (004-008). In [[jesus-christ|Christ]], the believer receives not merely restoration to [[adam|Adam]]'s original state but something infinitely greater — a life that is not merely created but divine, sharing in the very life of the Son of God.

### The Test of Everlasting Life

Moody teaches that the possession of everlasting life can be known with certainty. Citing 1 John 5:13, he notes: "These things have I written unto you that believe on the name of the Son of God; that ye may know that ye have eternal life" (007-038). The word "know" occurs over forty times in First John's five short chapters. "It is 'Know! Know!! Know!!!' The Key to it is Know! and all through the Epistle there rings out the refrain — 'that we might know that we have eternal life'" (007-038). [[eve|Eve]]rlasting life is not a matter of conjecture but of conscious possession.

### Not Coming Into Judgment

One of the most precious aspects of everlasting life for Moody is the believer's deliverance from judgment. Citing John 5:24 in the newer translation, he writes: "He that heareth my word, and believeth on Him that sent Me, hath everlasting life, and shall not come into 'judgment'" (007-046). The believer whose "life is hid with [[jesus-christ|Christ]] in God" will not stand before the great white throne for condemnation. He may be judged for stewardship and reward, "but salvation — eternal life — is another" matter entirely (007-046).

### The Father, Son, and Spirit as Keepers

Moody emphasises the Trinitarian security of everlasting life from John 10:27–29: "I give unto them eternal life; and they shall never perish, neither shall any man pluck them out of my hand. My Father which gave them is greater than all; and no man is able to pluck them out of my Father's hand" (007-046). "The Father, the Son, and the [[holy-spirit|Holy Ghost]], are pledged to keep us" (007-046). The security of everlasting life is grounded not in the believer's grip on God but in God's grip on the believer.

### How Long Does It Take?

To those who say a man cannot be saved all at once, Moody replies with Romans 6:23: "The wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life through [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]] our Lord. How long does it take to accept a gift? … It does not take six months to get eternal life" (004-017). The gift is received in a moment, though its outworking may be gradual: "It may however in some cases be like the mustard seed, very small at the commencement" (004-017).

### The Everlasting Word

Moody extends the concept of everlasting to the [[the-word-of-god|Word of God]] itself, quoting Deuteronomy 29:29: "The secret things belong unto the Lord our God: but those things which are revealed belong unto us, and to our children, forever" (004-019). What God has spoken endures forever. The believer feeds on the everlasting word and gains spiritual strength from it throughout eternity: "I expect to be finding out something fresh about God throughout eternity" (004-019).

## Biblical Foundation

Moody's teaching on everlasting life is rooted primarily in John's writings: John 3:14–16 (the brazen serpent), John 5:24 (present possession), John 6:47 (believing and having), John 10:27–29 (security in Father and Son), John 11:25–26 ([[jesus-christ|Christ]] the [[resurrection|Resurrection]]), 1 John 5:13 (knowing we have eternal life), and Romans 6:23 (the gift of God). His use of the doctrine is consistently pastoral and evangelistic — everlasting life is the great offer of the Gospel, received now by faith.
