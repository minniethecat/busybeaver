# The Law

## Definition

The Law, in [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, is God's revealed moral standard given through [[moses|Moses]] — holy, just, and good, yet powerless to save. Its function is not to justify but to condemn: "Now we know what things soever the law saith, it saith to them who are under the law; that every mouth may be stopped, and all the world may become guilty before God" (003-002). The law drives the sinner to [[jesus-christ|Christ]] by exposing the impossibility of self-righteousness. "To those who utter this penitential cry there is no necessity to administer the law" (003-012) — the [[gospel|Gospel]], not the law, is God's remedy for sin.

## Moody's Teaching

### The Law's Purpose — Condemnation, Not Salvation

Moody presents the law as a diagnostic instrument, not a cure. It shows the disease but cannot heal it. "Now we know what things soever the law saith, it saith to them who are under the law; that every mouth may be stopped, and all the world may become guilty before God" (003-002). The law leaves no one with a defense — every mouth is silenced. This is the law's proper work: to strip away self-justification and bring the sinner to the point of crying out for mercy.

### Law and Gospel

"There is no necessity to administer the law" to those who already feel their guilt (003-012). The law is for the self-righteous, not the broken-hearted. "Many will meet you with a scowl and say, 'I don't know what it is to believe;' and though it is the law of heaven that they must believe, in order to be saved — yet they ask for something besides that" (003-012). The demand of the law is satisfied in [[jesus-christ|Christ]]; the sinner's part is simply to believe.

### The Law as the Requirement for Heaven

"Has not God a right to make a law that all those who become heirs of eternal life must be 'born' into His kingdom?" (002-019). Just as "the law of this country requires that the President should be born in the country" — a requirement foreigners cannot complain against — so God has the right to establish the conditions of entrance into His kingdom. The new birth is not an arbitrary rule but the necessary transformation that fits a sinner for heaven.

### The Mosaic Law and Christ

Moody uses the Mosaic law's penalty for blasphemy — death by stoning (Leviticus 24:16) — to argue for [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s divinity. "This law obliged them to put to death everyone who blasphemed. It was making the statement that He was divine that cost Him His life; and by the Mosaic law He ought to have suffered the death penalty" (005-014). If [[jesus-christ|Christ]] were merely human, the Jews acted rightly under their law in condemning Him. The logic of the law confirms who [[jesus-christ|Christ]] claimed to be.

### Christ's Obedience to the Law

[[jesus-christ|Christ]] "was obedient unto death; He never transgressed the Father's law, or turned aside from the path of perfect obedience by one hair's breadth" (001-018). Where every other human being has broken God's law, [[jesus-christ|Christ]] alone kept it perfectly — and His righteousness is credited to those who believe. The law that condemns the sinner is the same law that [[jesus-christ|Christ]] fulfilled in the believer's place.

### Warnings About Striving Over the Law

Moody counsels against fruitless theological controversy: "But avoid foolish questions, and genealogies, and contentions, and strivings about the law; for they are unprofitable and vain" (004-034). The law's purpose is practical — to lead to [[jesus-christ|Christ]] — not to provide material for endless debate. The sinner who is still arguing about the law has not yet felt its weight.
