# Joseph (OT)

## Biblical Role

Joseph was the eleventh son of Jacob and the first son of Rachel. Sold into slavery by his jealous brothers, he was taken to [[egypt|Egypt]] where, through God's providence, he rose from prisoner to prime minister, saving [[egypt|Egypt]] and his own family from famine.

## Moody's Teaching

[[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]] references Joseph in two contexts. First, as an example of God's preserving power: "God can do what He has done before. He kept Joseph in [[egypt|Egypt]]" (004-018). The God who sustained Joseph through slavery, false accusation, and imprisonment is the same God who keeps every believer. Joseph's story is a testament to divine providence — what men meant for evil, God meant for good.

Second, Moody mentions Joseph in the context of [[jesus-christ|Christ's]] uniqueness. Before his conversion, Moody "thought that He was a good man like [[moses|Moses]], Joseph, or [[abraham|Abraham]]" (005-006). But he came to realize that [[jesus-christ|Christ]] was far more — not merely the best of men, but God manifest in the flesh. Joseph, for all his virtue, was a sinner saved by grace; [[jesus-christ|Christ]] is the sinless Saviour.
