# Daniel

## Biblical Role

Daniel was a young nobleman of Judah taken into [[babylon|Babylon]]ian captivity who rose to prominence in the courts of Nebuchadnezzar and Darius through his wisdom and unwavering faithfulness to God. He is known for his prophetic visions, his deliverance from the lions' den, and his role as an administrator in a foreign empire.

## Moody's Teaching

[[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]] invokes Daniel in a single but significant reference. In urging believers who fear they will not hold out in the [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian life, Moody reminds them: "God can do what He has done before. He kept [[joseph-ot|Joseph]] in [[egypt|Egypt]]; [[moses|Moses]] before [[pharaoh|Pharaoh]]; Daniel in [[babylon|Babylon]]; and enabled [[elijah|Elijah]] to stand before Ahab in that dark day" (004-018).

The point is that God's preserving power is demonstrated in history. Daniel was kept faithful in the most hostile of environments — a pagan court that demanded conformity to its customs and worship. The same God who kept Daniel keeps every believer who trusts in Him. "I am so thankful that these I have mentioned were men of like passions with ourselves. It was God who made them so great. What man wants is to look to God."
