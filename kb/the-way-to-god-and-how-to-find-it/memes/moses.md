# Moses

## Overview

Moses was the great lawgiver and prophet of Israel, through whom God delivered the Law and led the Israelites out of [[egypt|Egypt]]. In [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, Moses's most significant act — beyond the giving of the Law — was lifting up the brazen serpent in the wilderness, which [[jesus-christ|Christ]] Himself applied as a type of His own crucifixion. Moses thus stands as a bridge figure: the giver of the Law that condemns, and the prefigurer of the Remedy that saves.

## Role in Moody's Teaching

### The Brazen Serpent

Moody's most extensive use of Moses is the story from Numbers 21. When the Israelites were bitten by fiery serpents in the wilderness, God instructed Moses to make a brazen serpent and lift it on a pole. Whosoever looked upon it lived (002-025). [[jesus-christ|Christ]] applied this to Himself: "As Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, even so must the Son of Man be lifted up; that whosoever believeth in Him should not perish, but have eternal life" (002-021).

The key lesson of this type is simplicity: healing came not from understanding or feeling but simply from looking. "How long did it take to cure that boy? How long did it take to cure those serpent-bitten Israelites? It was just a look; and they were well" (002-026).

### The Law That Condemns

Moody references the Mosaic law to argue that [[jesus-christ|Christ]] must be divine. Leviticus 24:16 mandated death for blasphemers. The Jews condemned [[jesus-christ|Christ]] to death precisely because He claimed to be the Son of God — and "by the Mosaic law He ought to have suffered the death penalty" if He were merely a man claiming to be God (005-013, 005-014). Therefore, either [[jesus-christ|Christ]] is divine and spoke truth, or He was a blasphemer who justly died under Moses's law.

### The First Commandment

Moody references the first commandment given through Moses — "Thou shalt have no other gods before Me" (Exodus 20:2) — to argue that if [[jesus-christ|Christ]] is not God, the millions throughout [[jesus-christ|Christ]]endom who worship Him are guilty of idolatry (005-006).

> "As Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, even so must the Son of Man be lifted up; that whosoever believeth in Him should not perish, but have eternal life." "Whosoever." Mark that! Let me tell you who are unsaved what God has done for you. He has done everything that He could do toward your salvation. You need not wait for God to do anything more.
>
> (002-021)

## Biblical Foundation
- **Numbers 21:4–9** — The brazen serpent lifted in the wilderness (002-021, 002-025, 002-026).
- **Exodus 20:2** — The first commandment (005-006).
- **Leviticus 24:16** — The death penalty for blasphemy (005-013, 005-014).
- **John 3:14–15** — [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s application of the brazen serpent type (002-021).
