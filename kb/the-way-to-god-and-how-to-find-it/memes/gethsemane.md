# Gethsemane

## The Place

Gethsemane, meaning "oil press," was a garden at the foot of the Mount of Olives in [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]]. It was here that [[jesus-christ|Jesus]] withdrew with His disciples on the night of His betrayal, praying in agony while [[the-apostle-peter|Peter]], [[james]], and [[john-the-baptist|John]] slept.

## Moody's Teaching

### A Step in Peter's Downfall

[[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]] uses Gethsemane as a critical stage in Peter's progressive fall. After Peter's self-confident boasting — "Though all shall be offended because of Thee, yet will I never be offended" — the next step was spiritual slumber. "Instead of Peter watching one short hour in Gethsemane, he fell asleep, and the Lord asked him, 'What, could ye not watch with Me one hour?'" (009-033, citing Matthew 26:40).

Moody draws a practical warning: "If [[satan|Satan]] can rock the Church to sleep he does his work through God's own people." The failure to watch and pray in Gethsemane led directly to Peter's denial in the courtyard.

### Christ's Tender Remembrance

Despite Peter's failure at Gethsemane, [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s love remained unchanged. Moody movingly asks whether [[jesus-christ|Christ]] might have rebuked Peter: "Have you forgotten when, on the Mount of Transfiguration, with [[james|James]] and John, you said to Me, 'Lord, it is good to be here'? Have you forgotten being with Me at the supper-table, and in Gethsemane?" (009-036). But [[jesus-christ|Christ]] did nothing of the kind — He "cast one look on Peter: and there was so much love in it that it broke that bold disciple's heart."
