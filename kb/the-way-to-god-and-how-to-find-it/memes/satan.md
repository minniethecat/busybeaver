# Satan

## Definition

In [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, Satan (also called the Devil, the Adversary, and the Tempter) is a personal, intelligent, and malevolent spiritual being who is the sworn enemy of God and of human souls. "He has had six thousand years' experience" (008-038) — a long career of deceiving, tempting, and destroying. Moody does not treat Satan as a mythological symbol or a mere personification of evil, but as a real adversary whose strategies must be understood and resisted by every believer. His chief objective is to keep sinners from [[faith|faith]] in [[jesus-christ|Christ]] and, failing that, to cast believers into doubt and spiritual ruin.

## Moody's Teaching

### Satan's Great Lie

Satan's primary strategy, Moody teaches, is to persuade men that God does not love them. "I know of no truth in the whole Bible that ought to come home to us with such power and tenderness as that of the [[love|Love of God]]; and there is no truth in the Bible that Satan would so much like to blot out. For more than six thousand years he has been trying to persuade men that God does not love them. He succeeded in making our first parents believe this lie; and he too often succeeds with their children" (001-003). The doctrine of the [[love-of-god|Love of God]] is the truth Satan most hates because it is the truth that most powerfully draws sinners to God. If he can make a man believe God hates him, he has won.

### The Three Wiles of Satan

Moody identifies a three-stage strategy in Satan's warfare against souls: "Now it seems as if there are three wiles of Satan against which we ought to be on our guard. In the first place he moves all his kingdom to keep us away from [[jesus-christ|Christ]]; then he devotes himself to get us into 'Doubting Castle': but if we have, in spite of him, a clear ringing witness for the Son of God, he will do all he can to blacken our characters and belie our testimony" (007-039). The first wile is to prevent conversion; the second is to destroy [[assurance|assurance]]; the third is to discredit the believer's testimony. Each stage requires a different kind of vigilance.

### Satan and Self-Confidence

The story of Peter's fall is Moody's chief illustration of Satan's method. [[jesus-christ|Christ]] warned: "Simon, Simon, behold, Satan hath desired to have you, that he may sift you as wheat: but I have prayed for thee, that thy faith fail not" (009-034). Peter's response — "I am ready to go with Thee, both into prison and to death" — was self-confidence, not faith. "If any man thinks that he is strong enough to resist the devil at any one point he needs special watch there, for the tempter comes that way" (009-034). The place of greatest perceived strength is often the place of greatest vulnerability. "Most men who have fallen have done so on the strongest side of their character" (009-034).

### The Steps of Peter's Downfall

Moody traces Peter's fall as a pattern of how Satan works: "The first step in Peter's downfall was his self-confidence" (009-034). The second step "was that he went to sleep" — "If Satan can rock the Church to sleep he does his work through God's own people" (009-034). The third step was fighting in the energy of the flesh. The fourth was following [[jesus-christ|Christ]] "afar off." Each step is a cautionary lesson: "It is a sad thing when a child of God follows afar off … it will not be long before disgrace will be brought upon the old family name, and [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]] will be wounded in the house of his friends" (009-035). Satan does not typically bring about a great fall in a single moment; he works by successive degrees.

### Satan as the Source of False Teaching

Moody warns that false ideas about salvation originate from Satan. When a defaulter asked whether he could use stolen money to start a business and repay his employers later, Moody replied: "that was a delusion of Satan; that he could not expect to prosper on stolen money" (006-014). Satan is a deceiver who offers plausible reasons to delay [[repentance|repentance]] and [[restitution|restitution]]. The man who listens to such reasoning is being led not by wisdom but by the Adversary.

### Satan's Defeat at Conversion

When a person truly believes, Satan's power over them is broken. Moody quotes a new convert: "From that hour the devil has never dared to challenge my conversion" (006-030). The [[assurance|assurance]] that follows genuine conversion is a direct rebuke to the accuser. This is not arrogance but the testimony of one who has passed from Satan's kingdom into [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s. The believer now has a new Keeper: "We are no match for Satan; he has had six thousand years' experience. But then we remember that the One who neither slumbers nor sleeps is our keeper" (008-038).

### Satan Cannot Break God's Promise

Moody makes a bold apologetic assertion: "there would be a jubilee in hell today if one word that He has spoken could be broken" (003-028). The integrity of God's word is absolute, and Satan's entire kingdom has laboured for millennia to find a single broken promise — and failed. The believer's security does not rest on his own ability to resist Satan but on the unbreakable character of God's word.

## Biblical Foundation

Moody's teaching on Satan draws from Luke 22:31–32 (Satan's desire to sift Peter), Matthew 26:33–52 (Peter's self-confidence, sleep, and fleshly fighting), 1 Corinthians 10:12 (warning against self-confidence), and the broader biblical theme of spiritual warfare. His approach is consistently pastoral — not speculative demonology but practical warning rooted in [[the-word-of-god|Scripture]].
