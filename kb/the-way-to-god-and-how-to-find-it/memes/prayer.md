# Prayer

## Definition

Prayer is the believer's communion with God — the lifting of the heart and voice to the Almighty in adoration, [[confession]], thanksgiving, and supplication. In [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, prayer is essential to the [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian life, yet he is careful to distinguish it from the new birth: a person may pray regularly without ever being born again (002-010).

## Moody's Teaching

### Prayer Is Not Regeneration

Moody directly addresses those who say "I say my prayers regularly" as evidence of being [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian. His response: "Still I say that is not being born of the Spirit" (002-010). Prayer is good and right, but it must not be confused with the new birth. One can "read the Bible and cry to God a great deal, and yet never repent" (006-009).

### Prayer and Repentance

Moody distinguishes between crying to God from fear (like sailors in a storm) and true repentance. Many "cry loudly to God, and yet do not repent" (006-009). The publican's prayer — "God be merciful to me a sinner!" — exemplifies true repentant prayer (006-034).

### The Believer's Prayer

For those who have been born again, prayer is a vital part of the [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian life. Moody recounts the story of a newly converted man who, when called upon to close a meeting with prayer, had never prayed in public before. His first thought was "I cannot pray," but his second was "I have said I will begin a [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian life; and this is a part of it." Between kneeling and his knees striking the floor, the Lord converted his soul — and his first words were "Glory to God!" (006-017, 006-018, 006-019).

### The Simplicity of Prayer

Moody's examples show that effective prayer is simple and direct, not eloquent or lengthy. The publican's prayer was seven words. The converted lawyer's first public prayer was full of praise. Prayer flows from a relationship with God, not from religious technique.

> Let such read Psalm 121; "I will lift up mine eyes unto the hills, from whence cometh my help. My help cometh from the Lord, which made heaven and earth."
>
> (004-016)

## Biblical Foundation

- **Luke 18:13** — The publican's prayer: "God be merciful to me a sinner!" (006-034).
- **1 Thessalonians 5:17** — "Pray without ceasing."
- **Philippians 4:6** — "In everything by prayer and supplication with thanksgiving."
