# Peace

## Definition

Peace, in [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, is the inward rest of soul that accompanies genuine [[faith|faith]] in [[jesus-christ|Christ]]. It is not a natural temperament or the absence of external trouble, but a supernatural fruit of the [[holy-spirit|Holy Spirit]]'s work in the believer. "There is no rest, joy, or peace — no liberty, nor power — where doubts and uncertainty exist" (007-004). True peace is inseparable from [[assurance|assurance]] — the confidence that one's sins are forgiven and one's standing with God is secure.

## Moody's Teaching

### Peace Through the Holy Spirit

"Let the love of God be shed abroad in your heart by the [[holy-spirit|Holy Ghost]] today: it will drive away darkness; it will drive away gloom; it will drive away sin; and peace and joy shall be yours" (001-046). Moody presents peace not as something the believer manufactures through effort but as a direct result of the Spirit's indwelling. The [[holy-spirit|Holy Spirit]], sent by the Father and the Son, produces peace as part of His transforming work in the heart.

### The Fruit of the Spirit

Peace belongs to the catalog of the Spirit's fruit enumerated in Galatians 5: "The fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, long-suffering, gentleness, goodness, faith, meekness, temperance" (007-030). Moody urges believers to read this passage as a self-test: "If I am meek, gentle, and forgiving; if I have a spirit filled with peace and joy; if I am long-suffering and gentle, like the Son of God — that is a test: and in that way we are to tell whether we have eternal life" (007-021). Peace is evidence of the Spirit's presence.

### Looking to Christ for Peace

"It is only when we look fixedly on [[jesus-christ|Christ]] that we find perfect peace" (007-053). Moody uses the image of the sunflower turning toward the sun: just as the flower naturally follows the light, "when our faces are directed to the Sun of [[righteousness|Righteousness]], the peace and joy are sure to come" (008-010). The believer who is constantly introspecting about feelings will lose their joy; the one who looks away from self and toward [[jesus-christ|Christ]] finds peace sustained. "Many people are hunting after light, and peace, and joy" (008-010) — but these are byproducts of beholding [[jesus-christ|Christ]], not goals to be pursued directly.

### Peace as Evidence of the New Birth

The reality of the new birth is verified by its effects: "if, by believing, I get a new life, and a hope, a peace, a joy, and a rest to my soul, that I never had before; if I get self-control, and find that I have a power to resist evil and to do good — I may reason that I have been born of the Spirit" (003-022). Peace is not merely a feeling but a transformed condition of the soul that demonstrates regeneration.

### The Ground of Peace

The ground of the believer's peace is objective, not subjective — it rests on what [[jesus-christ|Christ]] has done, not on fluctuating emotions. "That was the ground of their peace" (007-053). "There cannot be true peace where there is fear. 'Perfect love casteth out fear'" (003-022). Peace and fear are mutually exclusive; where God's love is received by faith, fear is driven out and peace takes its place.
