Confession of sin is the honest acknowledgment of wrongdoing before God. In [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, confession is an essential step toward forgiveness and restoration — the sinner must not only feel sorrow for sin but must openly admit it to God.

## The Nature of Confession

Confession is more than admitting guilt when caught. Moody observes that some people, "even when he finds he cannot deny it any longer, he will not confess the sin, but says he will do it again the first chance he gets" (006-022). True confession requires a willingness to name the sin and turn from it.

Moody teaches that confession is directed toward God: "Go to Him today and tell Him that you have not loved Him, that you have not treated Him right; confess your sins, and see how quickly He will bless you" (001-040). The act of confession restores the broken relationship between the sinner and God.

## Confession and Forgiveness

The promise of [[the-word-of-god|Scripture]] connects confession directly to divine forgiveness. Moody quotes 1 John 1:9: "'If we confess our sins, He is faithful and just to forgive us our sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness'" (007-027). Confession is the condition on which forgiveness rests.

Moody emphasizes the completeness of God's forgiveness once sin is confessed: "The past sins of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ians are all forgiven as soon as they are confessed; and they are never to be mentioned" (007-025). This reflects the finality of divine pardon — confessed sins are not held against the believer.

## Confession and the Unforgiving Heart

Moody warns that an unwillingness to confess sin keeps a person in spiritual bondage: "There are men today who are in darkness and bondage because they are not willing to turn from their sins and confess them; and I do not know how a man can hope to be forgiven if he is not willing to confess his sins" (006-031). Confession is not optional — it is the path out of darkness into light.

## Confession in Practice

Moody illustrates the practical power of confession through a personal account: "After he had confessed his guilt and been forgiven, we all got down on our knees and had a blessed prayer-meeting" (006-028). Confession brings release not only to the individual but restores fellowship within the community of believers.

For the backslider, confession is the way back to God: "If you confess your sins and tell Him that you have wandered from the path of His commandments He will restore unto you the joy of His salvation" (009-027). The same principle applies to the unconverted: "If you will go right to Him, confessing your sin and your need, He will save you" (001-043).

## Confession and Repentance

Confession in Moody's framework is closely tied to repentance. It is not merely verbal acknowledgment but involves forsaking the sin: "Our sins are gone for time and eternity, if God forgives: and what we have to do is to confess and forsake our sins" (007-028). Confession without forsaking is incomplete.
