# Eternal Life

## Definition

Eternal life, in [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, is the gift of God bestowed upon every person who believes on the Lord [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]]. It is not merely endless existence — for the unrepentant face eternal [[perish|ruin]] — but a new quality of life imparted at the [[new-birth|new birth]] that will never end. "This is the record, that God hath given to us eternal life, and this life is in His Son" (003-017). Eternal life is not something the believer hopes to receive at death; it is a present possession: "And this is the true God, and eternal life" (005-003).

## Moody's Teaching

### The Promise of Eternal Life

The central promise of the Gospel is summarized in [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s words to [[nicodemus|Nicodemus]]: "As [[moses|Moses]] lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, even so must the Son of Man be lifted up; that whosoever believeth in Him should not perish, but have eternal life" (002-021). This is the "whosoever" Gospel — offered to every sinner without distinction. "And there is eternal life for every poor sinner" (002-029). No one is excluded; the only condition is faith.

### Eternal Life as a Present Possession

Moody emphasizes that eternal life is not deferred but immediate. "It does not take six months to get eternal life" (004-004). Quoting Romans 6:23 — "The wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life through [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]] our Lord" — he insists that the gift is received the moment a sinner believes (004-004). The believer does not wait until death to discover whether they have eternal life; they may know it now.

### Knowing That We Have Eternal Life

The First Epistle of John is, for Moody, the charter of [[assurance|assurance]]: "These things have I written unto you that believe on the name of the Son of God... that we might know that we have eternal life" (007-015). The apostle writes "to you that believe on the name of the Son of God" — not to an elite class of saints but to ordinary believers. "All through the Epistle there rings out the refrain — 'that we might know that we have eternal life'" (007-015). [[assurance|Assurance]] is not presumption but the birthright of every true child of God.

### The Indestructibility of Eternal Life

"All the gravediggers in the world cannot dig a grave large enough and deep enough to hold eternal life; all the coffin makers in the world cannot make a coffin large enough and tight enough to hold eternal life" (003-029). Moody's language is vivid: eternal life cannot be extinguished by death, sin, or any power in the universe. It is not a fragile possession that the believer must guard in their own strength — it is God's gift, sustained by God's power.

### The Law of Entrance

"Has not God a right to make a law that all those who become heirs of eternal life must be 'born' into His kingdom?" (002-019). The new birth is the non-negotiable condition of entrance. No one inherits eternal life by natural descent, religious ritual, or moral effort — "Except a man be born again, he cannot see the [[kingdom-of-god|Kingdom of God]]." The new birth is not an obstacle to eternal life but its gateway.

### The Tragedy of Unbelief

"Some men complain and say that God has not provided for the salvation of all" (002-022). But the provision is universal — [[jesus-christ|Christ]] was lifted up "that whosoever believeth in Him should not perish, but have eternal life." The tragedy is not that God has excluded anyone but that sinners refuse the offered gift. For those who neglect the remedy, there is only the fearful alternative — to [[perish|perish]].
