# Kingdom of God

## Definition

The kingdom of God is the realm of God's sovereign rule, entered not by natural birth, religious observance, or moral effort, but solely through the new birth. As [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]] presents it, the kingdom is "a prepared place for a prepared people" (002-020) — those who have been born again through faith in [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]]. The gateway is narrow: "Except a man be born again, he cannot see the kingdom of God" (002-003).

## Moody's Teaching

### The Sole Entrance Requirement

The new birth is the absolute prerequisite for entering God's kingdom. This is not Moody's opinion but [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s own declaration to [[nicodemus|Nicodemus]], one of the best men in [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]]. If a ruler of the Jews, a doctor of the law, needed to be born again to enter, so does everyone (002-005).

The [[the-word-of-god|Scripture]] reinforces this with multiple "except" passages: "Except ye repent, ye shall all likewise perish" (Luke 13:3); "Except ye be converted, and become as little children, ye shall not enter" (Matthew 18:3); "Except your righteousness shall exceed the righteousness of the Scribes and Pharisees, ye shall in no case enter" (Matthew 5:20). "They all really mean the same thing" (002-004).

### Born Into the Kingdom

The only way into the kingdom is to be "born" into it — just as the law requires the US President to be born in the country (002-019). God has the right to establish the terms of entrance into His kingdom, and that law is the new birth.

### Heaven for the Twice-Born

An unregenerated person would not want to be in heaven. Placed among the pure and holy, one whose heart is full of corruption would say, "I do not want to stay here." If people were taken to heaven without regenerated hearts, "there would be another rebellion in heaven" (002-020). Those in the kingdom are "a company of those who have been twice born" (002-020).

### The Attractiveness of the Kingdom

What makes heaven attractive is not material splendor — not pearly gates or golden streets — but the presence of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]. Like a child who runs through the house crying "Mamma! mamma!" after her mother's death, the believer's joy in heaven is seeing the One who loved them enough to die for them (001-030).

### The Elder Brother Outside

Moody draws a sobering picture from the parable of the prodigal son: the elder brother, though outwardly moral and dutiful, remained outside the feast because of his self-righteous heart. "The curtain drops and leaves him outside, and the younger brother within" (003-010). The kingdom is not for the self-righteous but for the repentant.

> The only way to get into the kingdom of God is to be "born" into it. The law of this country requires that the President should be born in the country. When foreigners come to our shores they have no right to complain against such a law, which forbids them from ever becoming Presidents. Now, has not God a right to make a law that all those who become heirs of eternal life must be "born" into His kingdom?
>
> (002-019)

## Biblical Foundation
- **John 3:3–5** — "Except a man be born again, he cannot see the kingdom of God" (002-003).
- **Matthew 18:3** — "Except ye be converted, and become as little children" (002-004).
- **Matthew 5:20** — [[righteousness|Righteousness]] exceeding that of scribes and Pharisees required (002-004).
