# Blasphemy

## Definition

Blasphemy, in [[the-word-of-god|Scripture]], is the sin of speaking against the name of the Lord or claiming divine prerogatives that belong to God alone. Under Mosaic law, the penalty for blasphemy was death by stoning (005-013). In [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s "The Way to God," blasphemy serves a distinctive apologetic function: the fact that [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]] was condemned for blasphemy by the Jewish authorities actually confirms His divinity. If Jesus were merely a good man, His claims to be one with the Father would indeed be "downright blasphemy." The charge itself proves who He claimed to be.

## Moody's Teaching

### The Jewish Law on Blasphemy

Moody carefully establishes the legal framework. "In Leviticus 24:16, we read: 'And he that blasphemeth the name of the Lord, he shall surely be put to death, and all the congregation shall certainly stone him: as well the stranger, as he that is born in the land, when he blasphemeth the name of the Lord, shall be put to death'" (005-013). "This law obliged them to put to death everyone who blasphemed. It was making the statement that He was divine that cost Him His life; and by the Mosaic law He ought to have suffered the death penalty" (005-014). If [[jesus-christ|Christ]] were merely human, the Jews acted rightly under their law.

### The Trial of Christ

At [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s trial before the high priest, the critical moment came when Jesus was placed under oath. "The high priest stood up in the midst, and asked Jesus, saying, Answerest Thou nothing? What is it which these witness against thee?" (005-005). When the high priest asked directly, "Art Thou the [[jesus-christ|Christ]], the Son of the [[blessed|Blessed]]?" — "And Jesus said, I am: and ye shall see the Son of Man sitting on the right hand of power, and coming in the clouds of heaven." At this, "the high priest rent his clothes, and saith, What need we any further witnesses? Ye have heard the blasphemy: what think ye? And they all condemned Him to be guilty of death" (005-005).

### The Logic of Divinity

Moody presses the logical conclusion: "By the Jewish law if a man were a blasphemer he was to be put to death; and supposing [[jesus-christ|Christ]] to be merely human if this be not blasphemy I do not know where you will find it. 'He that honoureth not the Son, honoureth not the Father.' That is downright blasphemy if [[jesus-christ|Christ]] be not divine" (005-011). He extends the reasoning: "If [[moses|Moses]], or [[elijah|Elijah]], or [[elisha|Elisha]], or any other mortal had said, 'You must honour me as you honor God;' and had put himself on a level with God, it would have been downright blasphemy" (005-011). No prophet ever made such claims.

### Christ's Claims to Deity

Moody catalogs [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s unmistakable claims. When Jesus declared, "I and My Father are one," the Jews "took up stones again to stone Him. They said they did not want to hear more, for that was blasphemy" (005-012). When He forgave sins, the scribes protested: "Why doth this Man thus speak blasphemies? who can forgive sins but God only?" (005-022). And when He said, "The hour is coming, and now is, when the dead shall hear the voice of the Son of God; and they that hear shall live," Moody exclaims: "What blasphemy would this have been, had He not been divine! The proof is overwhelming, if you will but examine the Word of God" (005-028).

### The Transformed Blasphemer

In a powerful illustration of [[new-birth|regeneration]], Moody describes the transformation of those who once cursed God: "Their tongues were cursing and blaspheming; and now are occupied in praising God. Old things have passed away, and all things have become new. They are not reformed only, but regenerated — new men in [[jesus-christ|Christ Jesus]]" (002-017). This is the opposite of blasphemy: the tongue that once cursed now praises. A blasphemer untransformed would not want heaven — "If a gambler or a blasphemer were taken out of the streets of New York and placed on the crystal pavement of heaven and under the shadow of the tree of life, he would say, 'I do not want to stay here'" (002-020). [[heaven|Heaven]] requires a prepared heart.
