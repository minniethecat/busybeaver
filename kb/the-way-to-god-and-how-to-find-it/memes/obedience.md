## Definition

Obedience, in Moody's presentation, is the perfect conformity to the will and law of God. It serves as a theological backdrop against which both the holiness of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] and the fallenness of humanity are understood. [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s life of flawless obedience stands in stark contrast to the universal human condition of disobedience and rebellion (001-018).

## Moody's Teaching

Moody addresses obedience primarily through the lens of parental love and divine grace. In explaining that God's love does not depend on human merit, he draws an analogy from family life: a parent does not cast out a child who has committed an act of disobedience — the child remains a child, and the parent's love endures despite the wrongdoing (001-008). This illustration serves to show that God distinguishes between the sinner and the sin, hating the disobedience while continuing to love the disobedient one.

The supreme example of obedience in Moody's teaching is [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]] himself. Reflecting on John 17:23, Moody marvels at the remarkable statement that the Father loves believers as He loves the Son. He notes that there is no reason why the Father should not love [[jesus-christ|Christ]], for [[jesus-christ|Christ]] "was obedient unto death; He never transgressed the Father's law, or turned aside from the path of perfect obedience by one hair's breadth" (001-018). The wonder of the gospel, as Moody presents it, is that despite all human rebellion and foolishness, those who trust in [[jesus-christ|Christ]] are loved with the same love the Father has for the perfectly obedient Son.


> Christ was not only a teacher, but He lived His teaching. He never once stepped aside from the path of perfect obedience by one hair's breadth. It is His obedience that is our righteousness.
>
> (001-018)
