# The Apostle Paul

The Apostle Paul is, after [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]] Himself, the most frequently cited authority in [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s *The Way to God and How to Find It*. Moody draws on Paul's epistles — particularly Romans, Ephesians, and Galatians — as the theological foundation for nearly every major doctrine he expounds: [[sin]], [[salvation]], [[faith]], [[grace]], [[repentance]], and [[assurance]]. Paul is not merely a supporting voice in Moody's preaching; he is the primary apostolic interpreter of the [[the-cross|cross]].

## Overview

The Apostle Paul (c. AD 5 – c. AD 64–67) was the foremost missionary and theologian of the early church. Originally a zealous Pharisee and persecutor of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ians known as Saul of Tarsus, he was dramatically converted on the road to [[damascus|Damascus]] when the risen [[jesus-christ|Christ]] appeared to him. His subsequent missionary journeys established churches across the Mediterranean world, and his thirteen epistles form the theological backbone of the New Testament. Moody presents Paul as a definitive authority — his words carry the weight of divine revelation.

## Role in Moody's Teaching

### The Preacher of Repentance

Like [[john-the-baptist|John the Baptist]], [[jesus-christ|Christ]], and Peter before him, Paul preached the universal call to repentance. At Athens, Paul declared that "God commandeth all men, everywhere, to repent" (Acts 17:30) (006-003). This text is one of Moody's key proof texts for the universal obligation to repent — it leaves no one exempt.

### The Theologian of Sin and Grace

Paul's writings provide Moody with the definitive statements on human sinfulness and divine grace. Romans 3:10 — "There is none righteous, no, not one" — is central to Moody's argument that all need a Saviour (003-002). Romans 5:6–8 demonstrates that [[jesus-christ|Christ]] died for the ungodly while they were still sinners (001-032, 001-033):

> "Paul prayed that he might know the love of God which passeth knowledge. The love of God is unchangeable." (001-026)

### The Apostle of Faith

Paul's teaching that salvation is "to him that worketh not, but believeth" (Romans 4:5) is foundational to Moody's soteriology. The believer "works from the cross; but not towards it" — salvation is received, not earned (002-014). Moody returns to this text repeatedly to counter the natural human instinct to try to earn salvation by effort.

### The Testimony of Revelation

Quoting Galatians 1:15–16 — "When it pleased God to reveal His Son in me" — Moody emphasizes that the gospel is "a matter of revelation and not of investigation" (002-017). [[faith|Faith]] is not contrary to reason, but it rests on divine testimony, not human deduction. Paul's own conversion is Exhibit A: he did not arrive at faith through intellectual inquiry but through direct divine intervention.

### The Herald of God's Love

It is Paul who prayed that believers might comprehend "the breadth, and length, and depth, and height, of God's love" which "passeth knowledge" (Ephesians 3:18–19) — the text from which Moody draws the opening theme of his entire book (001-002, 001-010).

### The Voice of Assurance

Moody turns to Paul's personal testimony for the doctrine of assurance. In 2 Timothy 1:12, the aged apostle declares: "I know whom I have believed, and am persuaded that He is able to keep that which I have committed unto Him against that day" (004-021). This is not presumption but confident trust grounded in the character of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]. Moody also notes Paul's teaching in Romans 8:9 — "If any man have not the Spirit of [[jesus-christ|Christ]], he is none of His" (007-031) — as the ground of assurance through the indwelling Spirit.

## Character & Significance

Paul embodies the transformation that the gospel produces. A former persecutor becomes the foremost preacher; a Pharisee zealous for the law becomes the apostle of grace. Moody sees in Paul not only a theologian but a living demonstration of the power of the gospel he preached. Paul's confidence in the face of martyrdom — "I am now ready to be offered, and the time of my departure is at hand" (007-041) — exemplifies the assurance that is available to every believer who rests on [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s finished work.
