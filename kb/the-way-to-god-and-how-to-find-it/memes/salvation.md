# Salvation

Salvation is the central promise of the [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian gospel — the free gift of [[eternal-life|eternal life]] received by [[faith]] in [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]], not by human effort or merit. [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]] declares this truth with unwavering clarity throughout *The Way to God and How to Find It*: [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s finished work on [[the-cross|the cross]] is the sole ground of salvation, and **believing** is the sole means of receiving it. This doctrine is inseparable from the doctrines of [[sin]], [[grace]], and the [[new-birth|new birth]].

## Definition

Salvation, in [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, is the free gift of eternal life that God offers to every sinner through faith in [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]]. It is not something to be worked for or earned — [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s cry on [[calvary|Calvary]], "It is finished!" (John 19:30), means exactly what it says (002-015). Salvation is received by simply believing on the Lord [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]]. It is a present possession, not merely a future hope.

> "How shall we escape, if we neglect so great salvation?" (Hebrews 2:3). There is no hope for you if you neglect the wonderful plan of salvation that God has provided at such a cost. (002-024)

## Moody's Teaching

### Salvation Is a Gift, Not a Wage

Moody is emphatic that salvation cannot be earned. It is "to him that worketh not, but believeth" (002-014). The believer works because he is saved, not to be saved. "We work from the cross; but not towards it" (002-014). A person must possess salvation before working it out (Philippians 2:12) — just as one must have money in the pocket before being careful how to spend it (002-014).

The work of salvation was completed by [[jesus-christ|Christ]] on the cross. "When [[jesus-christ|Christ]] cried out on [[calvary|Calvary]], 'It is finished!' He meant what He said. All that men have to do now is just to accept of the work of [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]]. There is no hope for man or woman so long as they are trying to work out salvation for themselves" (002-015).

### Salvation Received by Faith

The Philippian jailer's question — "What must I do to be saved?" — receives the apostolic answer: "Believe on the Lord [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]], and thou shalt be saved" (003-012). [[faith|Faith]] is taking God at His word and trusting in the finished work of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]. John 5:24 contains "enough truth in that one verse for every soul to rest upon for salvation. It does not admit the shadow of a doubt" (003-026): the one who hears [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s word and believes has — present tense — everlasting life and has passed from death unto life.

> "He that heareth My word, and believeth on Him that sent Me, hath everlasting life, and shall not come into condemnation; but is passed from death unto life." (003-026)

### The Greatness of Salvation

Moody warns against neglecting so great a salvation. Quoting Hebrews 2:3, he insists there is no hope for those who neglect the remedy God has provided (002-024). The analogy is the Israelites bitten by fiery serpents: looking at the wound does no good; what is needed is to look at the Remedy — the brazen serpent lifted on the pole, a type of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] (002-024).

Salvation is immediate and instantaneous. In the story of the brazen serpent, healing came with just a look. "How long did it take to cure that boy? How long did it take to cure those serpent-bitten Israelites? It was just a look; and they were well" (002-026).

### Assurance of Salvation

A believer who has truly trusted [[jesus-christ|Christ]] has passed from death to life and need not fear judgment. John 5:24 is unconditional: the one who believes "shall not come into condemnation" (003-026). Moody uses the illustration of a condemned man who went through his trial with a pardon already in his pocket — when sentence was pronounced, he presented the pardon and walked out free (003-029). Similarly, the believer has already been pardoned.

The redeemed child of God is heir to a richer inheritance than [[adam|Adam]] ever possessed in [[heaven|Paradise]], and it "endures forever — it is inalienable" (003-031).

### Heaven as a Prepared Place for a Prepared People

Salvation is necessary because "heaven is a prepared place for a prepared people" (002-020). An unregenerated person would not want to be in heaven; placed among the pure and holy, one whose heart is full of corruption would say, "I do not want to stay here." If people were taken to heaven without regenerated hearts, "there would be another rebellion in heaven" (002-020).

### The Only Way

The only way into the kingdom of God is to be "born" into it, just as the law requires the President of the United States to be born in the country (002-019). God has the right to establish the law of entrance into His kingdom, and that law is the new birth through faith in [[jesus-christ|Christ]].

## Illustrations & Anecdotes

Moody illustrates the simplicity of salvation with the story of the brazen serpent (Numbers 21). The Israelites bitten by fiery serpents were told simply to look at the serpent of brass lifted on a pole. They did not need to look at their wounds, make an offering, or perform any religious act — a single look brought healing (002-024, 002-026). This is a type of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] lifted on the cross: salvation comes by looking to Him in faith.

The story of the condemned man who carried a pardon in his pocket throughout his trial (003-029) illustrates assurance: the believer already possesses the pardon, and no condemnation can stand against it. When the sentence was pronounced, he simply presented the pardon and walked free.
