# Righteousness

## Definition

Righteousness, in [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, is the perfect standing before God's holy law that no human being possesses by nature. [[jesus-christ|Christ]] declared that "except your righteousness shall exceed the righteousness of the Scribes and Pharisees, ye shall in no case enter the kingdom of heaven" (002-004). This righteousness is not achieved by human effort but is imputed to the believer through faith in [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]], who was "made sin for us… that we might be made the righteousness of God in Him" (2 Corinthians 5:21).

## Moody's Teaching

### No One Is Righteous by Nature

[[the-word-of-god|The Bible]]'s verdict is absolute: "There is none righteous; no, not one" (003-002). This includes the best, most moral, most religious people — including [[nicodemus|Nicodemus]], a ruler of the Jews and a teacher of Israel. All are "gone out of the way" and "together become unprofitable" (003-002).

### Self-Righteousness vs. True Righteousness

Moody strongly warns against self-righteousness — the pharisaical spirit that thanks God for not being like other men. He tells of a man in Vermont who was "so clad in self-righteousness that you could not get the point of the needle of conviction in anywhere" (003-004). This man later came under deep conviction, realizing he was "a conceited, self-righteous Pharisee" (003-005).

The elder brother in the parable of the prodigal son represents the self-righteous person who "needed to be converted as much as the younger" (003-011). The elder brother who could not rejoice at his younger brother's return "would not be 'fit' for the kingdom of God" (003-010).

### Christ Our Righteousness

The righteousness that exceeds that of the scribes and Pharisees is not something the believer produces but something received — the righteousness of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] Himself, imputed through faith. The repentant publican "went down to his house justified rather than the other" — not because of his works but because he cast himself on God's mercy (003-035).

### Faith and Righteousness

[[faith|Faith]] — not works — is the means of receiving righteousness. It is "to him that worketh not, but believeth" (Romans 4:5). The believer does not work for righteousness but receives it as a gift, just as a beggar receives money placed in his hand (003-035).

## Biblical Foundation

- **Matthew 5:20** — Righteousness must exceed that of the scribes and Pharisees (002-004, 003-009).
- **Romans 3:10** — "There is none righteous; no, not one" (003-002).
- **Romans 4:5** — Righteousness imputed to the one who believes, not works (002-014).
- **2 Corinthians 5:21** — [[jesus-christ|Christ]] made sin for us that we might become God's righteousness.
