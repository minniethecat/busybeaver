## A Fundamental Doctrine

Moody identifies the Atonement as one of the core doctrines of the [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian faith. In his chapter on repentance, he lists it among the essential truths: "There are more people today in the mist and darkness about [[repentance|Repentance]], [[new-birth|Regeneration]], the Atonement, and suchlike fundamental truths, than perhaps on any other doctrines" (006-001). Moody expresses concern that many who have heard these terms from their earliest years nevertheless hold "a very strange and false idea" of them (006-001), underscoring the need for clear biblical teaching on the atonement.

## Christ as the Atoning Priest

In his exposition of the all-sufficiency of [[jesus-christ|Christ]], Moody presents the atoning work as one aspect of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s comprehensive role in salvation. Quoting a beloved passage, he declares: "He is our Priest, having atoned for us. He is our Advocate, ever living to make intercession for us. He is our Saviour, saving to the uttermost" (008-022). The atonement is presented not as an isolated doctrine but as part of the complete work of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] — who is simultaneously Priest, Advocate, Saviour, and "the fairest among ten thousand" (008-022). This comprehensive vision places the atonement within the broader reality of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] being "all and in all."

## Theological Context

In Moody's theological framework, the Atonement stands alongside [[repentance|Repentance]], [[new-birth|Regeneration]], and other fundamental truths as indispensable to understanding the gospel. His approach is pastoral rather than systematic — he is less concerned with theories of the atonement than with ensuring his readers understand its reality and personal application.
