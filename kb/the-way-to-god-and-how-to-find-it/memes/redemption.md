# Redemption

## Definition

Redemption is the work of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] by which He purchased sinners back from the bondage of sin and restored them to God. In [[dwight-l-moody|D. L. Moody]]'s teaching, redemption is something more than [[salvation]]: it is the price paid to bring the lost back to their rightful owner. As [[jesus-christ|Christ]] Himself declared, "In whom we have redemption through His blood, even the [[forgiveness|forgiveness of sins]]" (007-036). The redeemed child of God receives "a richer and more glorious inheritance than [[adam|Adam in Paradise]] could ever have conceived" — an inheritance that "endures forever" and is inalienable (003-031).

## Moody's Teaching

### Redemption — Christ Alone

Moody underscores the solitary nature of redemption: "When [[jesus-christ|Christ]] redeemed the world He was alone" (002-012). Just as God alone created the heavens and the earth in Genesis — with no human assistant — so redemption is a divine work in which sinners contribute nothing. This point is central to Moody's [[gospel|Gospel]]: "It is utterly impossible to make a man better without [[jesus-christ|Christ]]; but that is what men are trying to do. They are trying to patch up this 'old [[adam|Adam]]' nature" (002-012). [[new-birth|Regeneration]] is a new creation, and if a new creation, "it must be the work of God."

### Not Redeemed with Corruptible Things

[[jesus-christ|Christ]] is "not only our Deliverer; He is our Redeemer. That is something more than being our Saviour" (008-006). Moody explains: "He has brought us back. 'Ye have sold yourselves for nought; and ye shall be redeemed without money' ([[isaiah|Isaiah]] 52:3). 'We were not redeemed with corruptible things, as silver and gold' (1 Peter 1:18)." If gold could have redeemed humanity, Moody reasons, could God not have created ten thousand worlds full of gold? The cost was infinitely higher — the blood of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] (008-006).

### The Redeemed Inheritance

The inheritance of the redeemed far surpasses what [[adam|Adam]] possessed before the Fall. "In fact we get more than [[adam|Adam]] lost; for the redeemed child of God is heir to a richer and more glorious inheritance than [[adam|Adam in Paradise]] could ever have conceived; yea, and that inheritance endures forever — it is inalienable" (003-031). Redemption is not merely restoration to a pre-Fall state but elevation to a status higher than [[adam|Adam]] ever knew.

### The Redeemer Lives

Moody draws on Job's confidence as a model of [[assurance|assurance]]: "Job lived in a darker day than we do; but we read in Job 19:25: 'I know that my Redeemer liveth, and that He shall stand in the latter day upon the earth'" (007-040). This cry — "I know that my Redeemer liveth" — is for Moody the voice of assurance itself: "[[assurance|Assurance]] is Job sitting in the dust, covered with sores, and saying, 'I know that my Redeemer liveth;' 'Though He slay me, yet will I trust in Him'" (007-058). The Redeemer is not a distant abstraction but a living Person.

### Redemption and the Way

The pattern of redemption is illustrated in Israel's exodus from [[egypt|Egypt]]. "When God had redeemed the children of Israel from the bondage of [[egypt|Egypt]], and brought them through the Red Sea, they struck out for the wilderness; and then God became to them their Way" (008-007). [[jesus-christ|Christ]] has gone before — "He has been down here, and has made the rough places smooth, and the dark places light, and the crooked places straight." If believers will only be led by Him, "all will be peace, and joy, and rest" (008-007). Redemption is not the end but the beginning of a guided journey.

### A Prepared People

Redemption transforms the sinner's nature and destination. "[[heaven|Heaven]] is a prepared place for a prepared people" (002-020). The unregenerated person "would rather be in hell than in heaven" — place a man "whose heart is full of corruption and wickedness" among the pure, holy, and redeemed, "and he would not want to stay there." [[heaven|Heaven]] is filled with "a company of those who have been twice born" (002-020). Redemption changes what a person desires.
