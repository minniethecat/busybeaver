## Definition

Intercession, in Moody's theological framework, refers to the present, ongoing work of the risen [[jesus-christ|Christ]] on behalf of believers. Having completed His atoning work on the cross, [[jesus-christ|Christ]] now occupies the place of supreme authority at the right hand of God, where He continually intercedes — advocates, mediates, and pleads — for those who belong to Him (007-038).

## Moody's Teaching

Moody grounds the doctrine of intercession in Romans 8:33–34, which he deploys as part of his larger argument for the assurance of salvation. The rhetorical questions Paul asks — "Who shall lay anything to the charge of God's elect?" and "Who is he that condemneth?" — are answered by the reality of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s intercessory work. Moody emphasizes that it is "[[jesus-christ|Christ]] that died, yea rather, that is risen again, who is even at the right hand of God, who also maketh intercession for us" (007-038). The logic is unassailable: if [[jesus-christ|Christ]], the judge, is also the advocate, no charge against the believer can stand.

In his exposition of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]'s all-sufficiency, Moody expands on this theme, listing the many roles [[jesus-christ|Christ]] fulfills for the believer. Among them, [[jesus-christ|Christ]] is "our Advocate, ever living to make intercession for us" and "our Saviour, saving to the uttermost" (008-022). The intercession of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] is presented not as an occasional activity but as a continuous, living ministry — "ever living" — that guarantees the believer's security. This ongoing advocacy is the foundation of the [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian's confidence: the same [[jesus-christ|Christ]] who died for sins now lives to intercede, ensuring that nothing can separate the believer from the love of God.


> He is our Advocate, ever living to make intercession for us. He is our Saviour, saving to the uttermost. He is our Root; we grow from Him. He is our Bread; we feed upon Him. He is our Shepherd, leading us into green pastures. He is our true Vine; we abide in Him. He is the Water of Life; we slake our thirst from Him.
>
> (008-022)
