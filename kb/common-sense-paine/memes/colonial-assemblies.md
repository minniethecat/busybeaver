# Colonial Assemblies

## Overview
Each [[america|America]]n colony had its own elected assembly (such as the Virginia House of Burgesses, founded in 1619) that legislated on internal matters. These assemblies were the primary locus of [[america|America]]n political identity and the institutional foundation on which independence would be built.

## In Common Sense
[[thomas-paine|Paine]] saw the colonial assemblies as proof that [[america|America]]ns were already capable of self-government. The assemblies' "spirit of good order and obedience to continental government" demonstrated that [[america|America]]ns possessed the political maturity to govern themselves without British supervision (004-002). The assemblies were the embryo of the republican institutions that independence would bring to full development.
