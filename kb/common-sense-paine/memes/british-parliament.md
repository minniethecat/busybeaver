# British Parliament

## Overview
The British Parliament was the supreme legislative body of [[england|Great Britain]], consisting of the [[british-house-of-commons|House of Commons]], the [[british-house-of-lords|House of Lords]], and [[british-crown|the Crown]]-in-Parliament. It claimed [[power|authority]] to legislate for the [[america|American colonies]] "in all cases whatsoever" — a claim that [[thomas-paine|Paine]] systematically rejected.

## In Common Sense
[[thomas-paine|Paine]]'s critique targets Parliament's claim to represent the colonies. The Commons, "the republican part in the constitution," is the sole legitimate element of English government — yet even it is corrupted by royal influence and unrepresentative of the [[america|America]]n people (003-001). The doctrine of [[house-of-commons-representation|virtual representation]] is an evasion: [[america|America]]ns who elect no members to Parliament are not represented by it.

[[thomas-paine|Paine]] also attacks the hereditary basis of the [[british-house-of-lords|House of Lords]]. The peers are "independent of the people" and serve as "the base remains of two ancient tyrannies" compounded with republican forms (002-002). The entire parliamentary structure, for all its vaunted balance, is designed to preserve aristocratic privilege, not to secure popular liberty.

## Notable Quotations
