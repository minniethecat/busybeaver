# British-Colonial Constitutional Conflict

## Overview
The constitutional conflict between [[england|Britain]] and the [[america|American colonies]] (1763–1776) concerned the fundamental nature of the British Empire. Was [[british-parliament|Parliament]] sovereign over the colonies in all matters, or did [[colonial-assemblies|colonial legislatures]] possess autonomy over internal affairs? The conflict escalated from disputes over taxation to a comprehensive rejection of British [[power|authority]].

## In Common Sense
[[thomas-paine|Paine]]'s contribution to this debate was to reframe it entirely. Rather than arguing about the precise limits of [[british-parliament|Parliament]]ary [[power|authority]] — a debate that assumed the legitimacy of the British constitutional order — [[thomas-paine|Paine]] argued that the order itself was illegitimate. "I have never met with a man... who hath not confessed his opinion, that a [[independence|separation]] between the countries, would take place" (005-002). The only question was when and how.
