# British Ministry

## Overview
The British Ministry (or His Majesty's Government) was the executive branch of British government, led by the Prime Minister and composed of ministers appointed by [[british-crown|the Crown]]. In the 1770s, the ministry of [[lord-north|Lord North]] pursued coercive policies toward the [[america|American colonies]].

## In Common Sense
[[thomas-paine|Paine]] distinguishes between the structural defects of the British system and the personal qualities of individual ministers. Even "an able minister" like [[mr-pelham|Mr. Pelham]] could not remedy the fundamental injustice of colonial governance because the system itself was designed to extract resources from the colonies for the benefit of the metropole (004-001). The problem is not bad ministers but a bad constitution — and changing the ministers without changing the constitution is futile.

> But to expend millions for the sake of getting a few vile acts repealed, and routing the present ministry only, is unworthy the charge, and is using posterity with the utmost cruelty; because it is leaving them the great work to do, and a debt upon their backs, from which they derive no advantage.
>
> (005-002)


## Structure and Role
The ministry comprised the Prime Minister and the principal secretaries of state. In [[thomas-paine|Paine]]'s analysis, the ministry forms the "monarchical" branch of the [[british-constitution|British constitution]] alongside the "aristocratical" [[british-house-of-lords|House of Lords]] and the "republican" [[british-house-of-commons|House of Commons]] (003-001). The ministry executed the will of the [[british-crown|Crown]] rather than the people, making it structurally hostile to [[consent-of-the-governed|consent-based governance]].
