# Sir John Dalrymple

## Biography

Sir John Dalrymple (1726–1810) was a Scottish lawyer, historian, and political writer. He served as a Baron of the Exchequer in Scotland and wrote *Memoirs of [[england|Great Britain]] and Ireland*, a historical work covering Anglo-Scottish relations.

## Role in Common Sense

[[thomas-paine|Paine]] references Dalrymple in the Appendix with characteristic irony: "Sir John Dalrymple, the putative father of a whining jesuitical piece, fallaciously called, The Address of the People of ENGLAND to the Inhabitants of AMERICA" (006-001). The reference is part of [[thomas-paine|Paine]]'s rebuttal to British propaganda aimed at dividing colonial opinion.

The mention of Dalrymple appears in the context of [[thomas-paine|Paine]]'s broader argument that British appeals to colonial loyalty are dishonest — "jesuitical" in their willingness to use any argument, however fallacious, to preserve power. The naming of specific British propagandists personalizes the political conflict and helps readers see the debate as a contest between identifiable actors rather than abstract principles.
