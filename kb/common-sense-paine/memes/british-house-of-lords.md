# House of Lords

## Overview
The House of Lords was the upper house of [[british-parliament|the British Parliament]], composed of hereditary peers and bishops of the [[church-of-england|Church of England]]. Its members sat by birthright, not election — a principle that [[thomas-paine|Paine]] considered fundamentally illegitimate.

## In Common Sense
[[thomas-paine|Paine]] identifies the Lords as one of "the two first" elements of the [[british-constitution|English constitution]] that are "independent of the people" — that is, unaccountable to those they govern (002-002). Along with the monarchy, the hereditary aristocracy represents the survival of "ancient tyrannies" into the present, their power preserved not by merit or consent but by the accident of birth.

## Notable Quotations

> …The remains of aristocratical tyranny in the persons of the peers.
>
> (002-002)
