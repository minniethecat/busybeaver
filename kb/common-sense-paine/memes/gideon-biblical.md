# Gideon

## Biography

Gideon is a figure from the Book of Judges in the Hebrew Bible, one of the judges who led the Israelites before the establishment of the monarchy. After his military victory over the Midianites, the Israelites offered Gideon hereditary [[monarchy|kingship]] — an offer he famously refused, declaring that neither he nor his son would rule over them, for "the Lord shall rule over you."

## Role in Common Sense

[[thomas-paine|Paine]] deploys Gideon as his principal scriptural witness against monarchy. After the Israelites offer Gideon the crown, "Gideon in the piety of his soul replied, I will not rule over you, neither shall my son rule over you. THE LORD SHALL RULE OVER YOU" (003-001). This refusal, [[thomas-paine|Paine]] argues, represents the authentic biblical attitude toward [[monarchy|kingship]]: it is a rejection of divine rule, not an expression of it.

The force of the Gideon example lies in its timing. Gideon's refusal occurs at the height of his popularity and power — precisely when a lesser man would have seized the crown. That even this heroic figure declined [[monarchy|kingship]] demonstrates, for [[thomas-paine|Paine]], that monarchy is contrary to both reason and revelation.

[[thomas-paine|Paine]] contrasts Gideon's piety with the subsequent Israelite demand for a [[monarchy|king]], which he characterizes as a sin. The monarchy of [[saul-biblical|Saul]], and later [[david-biblical|David]], was a punishment inflicted on the people for their ingratitude — "a matter of sorrow, not of glory" (003-001). Gideon thus serves as the biblical standard against which all subsequent [[monarchy|kingship]] is measured and found wanting.
