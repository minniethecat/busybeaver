# Battle of Bunker Hill

## Overview
The Battle of Bunker Hill (June 17, 1775) was one of the first major engagements of the [[american-revolution|American Revolution]]. Though technically a British victory, the heavy casualties inflicted on the British army demonstrated that colonial militias could stand against professional soldiers.

## In Common Sense
[[thomas-paine|Paine]] invokes Bunker Hill to argue that reconciliation has become materially impossible. It is "as great a folly to pay a Bunker-hill price for law, as for land" (004-001) — the blood already shed has raised the cost of submission beyond any reasonable calculation. The battle is not merely a military engagement but a moral threshold: after Bunker Hill, reconciliation would require [[america|America]]ns to accept that their dead died for nothing.

## Notable Quotations

> Dearly, dearly, do we pay for the repeal of the acts, if that is all we fight for; for in a just estimation, it is as great a folly to pay a Bunker-hill price for law, as for land.
>
> (004-001)
