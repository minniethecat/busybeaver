# Equality

## Definition

Equality, for [[thomas-paine|Paine]], is the original condition of humankind — the state in which all people existed before the artificial distinctions of monarchy, aristocracy, and hereditary privilege were imposed. "Mankind being originally equals in the order of creation," he writes, "the equality could only be destroyed by some subsequent circumstance" (003-001). Equality is not a political aspiration to be achieved through reform; it is a natural fact to be restored by abolishing the institutions that have violated it.

This conception of equality is specifically political, not economic. [[thomas-paine|Paine]] is not arguing for the equal distribution of property but for the equal distribution of political standing. No person is born with a right to rule over another; all distinctions of rank are human inventions, and most are pernicious ones.

## In Paine's Argument

[[thomas-paine|Paine]] deploys the principle of equality most forcefully in his attack on monarchy and hereditary [[hereditary-succession|succession]]. The distinction "of men into KINGS and SUBJECTS" is, he insists, as unnatural as distinguishing between "male and female" — it is a category error that confuses convention with nature (003-001). Kings are made, not born; and what human artifice has made, human will can unmake.

Equality also grounds [[thomas-paine|Paine]]'s argument for [[republic|republican government]]. In a republic, "perfect equality affords no temptation" to corruption because no individual stands permanently above others (004-002). The rotation of office ensures that those who exercise power today will be subject to the power of others tomorrow — a structural guarantee of moderation that monarchy, by its very nature, cannot provide.

[[thomas-paine|Paine]]'s egalitarianism has theological roots as well. He argues that the original equality of mankind reflects divine intention; monarchy, far from being divinely ordained, is a rebellion against God's design. "The Heathens paid divine honors to their deceased [[monarchy|king]]s," he notes, "and the christian world hath since improved on the plan by doing the same to their living ones" (003-001). The worship of [[monarchy|king]]s is a form of idolatry — the elevation of a human being to divine status in defiance of the Creator's equal design.
