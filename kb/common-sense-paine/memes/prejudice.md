# Prejudice

## Definition

Prejudice, in [[thomas-paine|Paine]]'s usage, is not merely personal bigotry but a systematic cognitive distortion — the tendency of the mind to be "warped" (002-002) by habit, interest, and inherited opinion rather than guided by reason and evidence.

[[thomas-paine|Paine]] contrasts prejudice with both reason and feeling, suggesting it operates on a middle register: it is not as intellectually rigorous as reason, nor as instinctively trustworthy as genuine moral sentiment. Prejudice occupies the space between thought and emotion that should be filled by judgment, and in filling it, corrupts both.

The concept is paired with "prepossession" throughout *Common Sense* — together they form what [[thomas-paine|Paine]] sees as the primary internal obstacle to [[america|America]]n independence. External obstacles (British military power, economic dependence) can be overcome, but only if [[america|America]]ns first overcome the prejudices that make those obstacles appear insurmountable.

## In Paine's Argument

[[thomas-paine|Paine]] opens the main body of *Common Sense* with a direct appeal: he asks the reader to "divest himself of prejudice and prepossession, and suffer his reason and his feelings to determine for themselves" (004-001). This is not a rhetorical flourish — it is a methodological statement. The entire pamphlet is structured as an exercise in clearing away prejudice so that common sense can operate.

In his discussion of the British navy, [[thomas-paine|Paine]] identifies prejudice as a structural obstacle to clear thin[[monarchy|king]] about [[america|America]]n defense. He notes that "from a mixture of prejudice and inattention, we have continued" to rely on [[england|Britain]] for naval [[security|protection]] (005-002) long after the arrangement had ceased to serve [[america|America]]n interests.

In the Appendix, [[thomas-paine|Paine]] turns his analysis of prejudice against the opponents of independence. Those whose "narrow and prejudiced souls, are habitually opposing" the cause of independence (006-001) are not merely mistaken — they are cognitively incapable of seeing the truth. Their prejudice has become a permanent condition, not a correctable error.
