# Revolution of 1688

## Overview
The Revolution of 1688 was the overthrow of King James II of [[england|England]] by a union of [[british-parliament|Parliament]]arians and William of Orange. It established the principle that the monarch could not rule without [[british-parliament|Parliament]]'s consent and led to the Bill of [[natural-rights|Rights]] of 1689.

## In Common Sense
Though [[thomas-paine|Paine]] does not discuss 1688 extensively, his constitutional argument draws on its legacy. The English "constitution" was shaped by this revolution — yet it preserved monarchy itself, merely constraining it. For [[thomas-paine|Paine]], this was a half-measure: 1688 corrected the excesses of monarchy without abolishing the institution. [[america|America]], he argues, should complete the work that 1688 left unfinished by establishing a republic without a monarch at all.
