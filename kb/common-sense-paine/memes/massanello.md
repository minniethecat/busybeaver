# Massanello

## Biography

Masaniello (Tommaso Aniello, 1620–1647) was a Neapolitan fisherman who led a popular revolt against Spanish Habsburg rule in Naples. The revolt, sparked by a tax on fruit, briefly succeeded before Masaniello was murdered. His story became a symbol of uncontrolled popular uprising throughout [[europe|Europe]].

## Role in Common Sense

[[thomas-paine|Paine]] invokes Massanello as a warning. "If we omit it now, some Massanello may hereafter arise, who laying hold of popular disquietudes, may collect together the desperate and the discontented" (004-002). The lesson is stark: if [[america|America]]ns fail to establish legitimate [[constitution|constitutional government]] now, they invite a future demagogue to seize power through mob violence.

This use of Massanello reveals [[thomas-paine|Paine]]'s keen awareness of the dangers of revolution. He is not advocating chaos but ordered liberty — a constitutional settlement that channels popular energy into stable institutions rather than destructive upheavals. The choice is not between monarchy and anarchy but between monarchy and a well-designed republic.

## Notable Quotations

> …If we omit it now, some Massanello may hereafter arise, who laying hold of popular disquietudes, may collect together the desperate and the discontented, and by assuming to themselves the powers of government, may sweep away the liberties of the continent like a deluge.
>
> (004-002)
