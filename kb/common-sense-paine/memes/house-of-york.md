# House of York

## Overview
The House of York was a cadet branch of the Plantagenet dynasty that contended with the [[house-of-lancaster|House of Lancaster]] for the English throne during the [[wars-of-the-roses|Wars of the Roses]]. Its emblem was the white rose.

## In Common Sense
[[thomas-paine|Paine]] references the York-Lancaster conflict not by naming the houses explicitly but by narrating the bloodshed their rivalry produced: battles "fought between Henry and Edward" (003-001). The very names have become synonyms for dynastic violence — a testament to the absurdity of a political system that determines governance by genealogy rather than consent.

## Notable Quotations

> …For monarchy and succession, between the houses of York and Lancaster, laid England in a scene of blood for many years.
>
> (003-001)
