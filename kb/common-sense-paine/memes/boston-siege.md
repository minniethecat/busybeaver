# Siege of Boston

## Overview
The Siege of Boston (April 1775–March 1776) began after the [[lexington-and-concord|Battles of Lexington and Concord]], as colonial militias surrounded British forces occupying the city. It ended when the British evacuated [[boston|Boston]] by sea. The siege was ongoing when [[thomas-paine|Paine]] wrote *Common Sense*.

## In Common Sense
[[thomas-paine|Paine]] evokes the suffering of [[boston|Boston]] to personalize the costs of British rule. "Transport us for a few moments to [[boston|Boston]], that seat of wretchedness" (004-001) — the city, under military occupation, represents the future that awaits all of [[america|America]] if reconciliation prevails. The image of a major colonial city under siege by its own government's troops makes the absurdity of the colonial relationship viscerally real.

## Notable Quotations

> But let our imaginations transport us for a few moments to Boston, that seat of wretchedness will teach us wisdom, and instruct us for ever to renounce a power in whom we can have no trust. The inhabitants of that unfortunate city, who but a few months ago were in ease and affluence, have now, no other alternative than to stay and starve, or turn out to beg.
>
> (004-001)
