# Church of England

## Overview
The Church of England was the established Christian church in [[england|England]], with the monarch as its supreme governor. Its bishops sat in the [[british-house-of-lords|House of Lords]], and its clergy were supported by tithes extracted from the population.

## In Common Sense
[[thomas-paine|Paine]]'s critique of the Church of England is implicit but unmistakable. He notes that "the Heathens paid divine honors to their deceased [[monarchy|king]]s, and the christian world hath since improved on the plan by doing the same to their living ones" (003-001). The established church, by sacralizing monarchy, has perverted Christianity into an instrument of [[oppression|political oppression]]. The alliance of throne and altar is, for [[thomas-paine|Paine]], a corruption of both religion and government.
