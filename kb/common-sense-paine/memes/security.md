# Security

## Definition

Security, in [[thomas-paine|Paine]]'s political philosophy, is the primary purpose for which government is instituted. It encompasses the protection of individual rights — life, liberty, and property — from both external threats and internal disorder. "Security being the true design and end of government" (002-002).

[[government|Government]] does not exist to glorify rulers; it exists to make people safe. [[thomas-paine|Paine]]'s definition includes physical safety from invasion, economic [[freedom|security]] through commerce, and constitutional [[freedom|security]] — the assurance that laws will be stable and predictable.

## In Paine's Argument

[[thomas-paine|Paine]] derives government from the necessity of mutual protection. In the [[natural-liberty|state of nature]], isolated individuals are vulnerable; they band together for safety. As settlements grow, representative institutions emerge, but their purpose remains unchanged: "the design and end of government, viz. [[freedom|freedom and security]]" (002-002).

Security and freedom are paired: [[freedom|security]] without freedom is mere order, and freedom without [[freedom|security]] is anarchy. [[government|Government]] must deliver both. The [[freedom|security]] argument becomes urgent in Part 3, where [[thomas-paine|Paine]] addresses independence.

[[reconciliation|Reconciliation]] with [[england|Britain]] cannot provide even minimal [[freedom|security]]. "The utmost stretch of human wisdom cannot, at this time, compass a plan short of [[independence|separation]], which can promise the continent even a year's [[freedom|security]]" (004-001). Continued union offers not diminished [[freedom|security]] but *no* [[freedom|security]] at all.

[[thomas-paine|Paine]] proposes commerce as [[america|America]]'s [[freedom|security]] strategy. "Our plan is commerce," he writes, "and that, well attended to, will secure us the peace and friendship of all [[europe|Europe]]; because, it is the interest of all [[europe|Europe]] to have [[america|America]] a free port" (004-001). Economic self-interest replaces military alliance as the guarantor of safety.

[[america|America]]'s "barrenness of gold and silver secure her from invaders" — a resource-poor continent is not worth conquering when [[commerce|trade]] provides the same benefits. In the Appendix, [[thomas-paine|Paine]] extends the logic to property: without proper constitution, "the property of no man is secure" (006-001). Security becomes both the benchmark for government and the justification for revolution.

## Key Passages

> Security being the true design and end of government, it unanswerably follows that whatever form thereof appears most likely to ensure it to us, with the least expence and greatest benefit, is preferable to all others.
> (002-002)

> Here then is the origin and rise of government; namely, a mode rendered necessary by the inability of moral virtue to govern the world; here too is the design and end of government, viz. freedom and security.
> (002-002)

> The utmost stretch of human wisdom cannot, at this time, compass a plan short of separation, which can promise the continent even a year's security. Reconciliation is now a fallacious dream.
> (004-001)
