# British East India Company

## Overview
The British East India Company was a joint-stock company granted a royal charter for [[commerce|trade]] in the East Indies. By the 1770s, it had become a territorial power in India and was deeply entangled with the British government.

## In Common Sense
[[thomas-paine|Paine]] references the East India Company's interests to make a point about British motives. "It is the commerce and not the conquest of [[america|America]], by which [[england|England]] is benefited" (006-001). The Company exemplifies the entanglement of commercial and [[power|political power]] that characterizes the British Empire — a system in which private profit and public policy are indistinguishable. [[america|America]]ns who accept British rule are accepting subordination to a commercial enterprise as much as to a [[authority|political authority]].
