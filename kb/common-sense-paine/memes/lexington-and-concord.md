# Battles of Lexington and Concord

## Overview
The Battles of Lexington and Concord (April 19, 1775) were the first military engagements of the [[american-revolution|American Revolution]]. British troops marched from [[boston|Boston]] to seize colonial military supplies; colonial militia confronted them, and the ensuing fighting produced the "shot heard round the world."

## In Common Sense
By the time [[thomas-paine|Paine]] wrote *Common Sense*, Lexington and Concord were recent and raw memories. The blood shed there forms the emotional foundation of his argument against reconciliation: "Ye that tell us of [[reconciliation|harmony]] and reconciliation, can ye restore to us the time that is past?" (004-003). The dead of Lexington and Concord cannot be brought back by any diplomatic accommodation. Their sacrifice already constitutes a de facto declaration of independence.

## Notable Quotations

> Every thing that is right or natural pleads for separation. The blood of the slain, the weeping voice of nature cries, 'Tis time to part.
