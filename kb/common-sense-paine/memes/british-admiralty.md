# British Admiralty

## Overview
The British Admiralty was the government department responsible for the command of the Royal Navy. In the 1770s, the Admiralty enforced the blockade of [[america|America]]n ports and transported British troops across the Atlantic.

## In Common Sense
[[thomas-paine|Paine]] addresses "the commanders in chief at [[boston|Boston]], to the Admirals and Captains who are proudly ravaging our coasts" as agents of a tyrannical government — men who have lent their professional abilities to an unjust cause (006-002). The Admiralty represents the military dimension of British power: the capacity to project force across oceans that makes colonial domination possible. For [[thomas-paine|Paine]], this naval power is not a source of [[freedom|security]] but an instrument of oppression.
