# Norman Dynasty

## Overview
The Norman Dynasty ruled [[england|England]] from [[norman-conquest|the Conquest]] of 1066 until the accession of the Plantagenets in 1154. It included [[william-the-conqueror|William the Conqueror]], his sons [[william-the-conqueror|William I]]I and Henry I, and the civil war period of Stephen's reign.

## In Common Sense
For [[thomas-paine|Paine]], the Norman Dynasty is not merely a historical period but the original sin of English monarchy. "A French bastard landing with an armed banditti" established a line of [[monarchy|king]]s whose claim to rule rested entirely on violence (003-001). Every subsequent English monarch, including [[george-iii|George III]], ultimately traces [[power|authority]] to this act of conquest — and conquest, [[thomas-paine|Paine]] insists, confers no right that binds posterity.

## Notable Quotations

> A French bastard landing with an armed banditti, and establishing himself king of England against the consent of the natives, is in plain terms a very paltry rascally original.
>
> (003-001)
