# Dragonetti

## Biography

Giacinto Dragonetti (1738–1818) was an Italian political writer and jurist, author of *A Treatise on [[virtue|Virtue]]s and Rewards* (1766). His work explored the relationship between civic virtue and institutional incentives, arguing that good government depends on rewarding virtue as much as punishing vice.

## Role in Common Sense

[[thomas-paine|Paine]] cites Dragonetti as "a wise observer on [[government|governments]]" who articulated a principle central to republican thought: "The science of the politician consists in fixing the true point of happiness and freedom. Those men would deserve the gratitude of ages, who should discover a mode of government that contained the greatest sum of individual happiness, with the least national expense" (004-002). This citation positions [[thomas-paine|Paine]]'s argument within a broader [[europe|Europe]]an [[custom|tradition]] of Enlightenment political thought — [[america|the American cause]] is not provincial but universal.

[[thomas-paine|Paine]]'s use of Dragonetti also serves a rhetorical function. By citing an Italian [[power|authority]] on government, he demonstrates that the critique of monarchy is not merely an [[america|America]]n grievance but a conclusion supported by the best [[europe|Europe]]an thinkers. The appeal to cosmopolitan learning strengthens [[thomas-paine|Paine]]'s case against the charge that independence is merely a colonial tantrum.
