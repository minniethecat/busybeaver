# David

## Biography

David is the most celebrated [[monarchy|king]] in the Hebrew Bible — the slayer of Goliath, the composer of psalms, and the founder of a dynasty that would rule Judah for centuries. In Jewish and Christian [[custom|tradition]], he represents the ideal of righteous [[monarchy|kingship]].

## Role in Common Sense

[[thomas-paine|Paine]]'s treatment of David is characteristically subversive. He acknowledges "the high encomium given of David" in scripture but notes that it "takes no notice of him officially as a [[monarchy|king]]" (003-001). The praise of David, [[thomas-paine|Paine]] argues, is directed at his personal qualities — his faith, his courage, his repentance — not at his [[monarchy|king]]ly office. Scripture praises the man, not the monarch.

This distinction is crucial to [[thomas-paine|Paine]]'s anti-monarchical reading of the Bible. If even David — the greatest of Israel's [[monarchy|king]]s — is praised for his character rather than his crown, then monarchy cannot claim scriptural endorsement. The Bible's highest human exemplar is a man whose royal office was incidental to his virtue. The political implication is clear: virtue, not birth, is the proper basis for honor and [[power|authority]].

## Notable Quotations

> …The high encomium given of David takes no notice of him *officially as a king*, but only as a *man* after God's own heart.
