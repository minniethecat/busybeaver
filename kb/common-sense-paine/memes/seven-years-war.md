# Seven Years' War

## Overview
The Seven Years' War (1756–1763) was a global conflict between [[england|Great Britain]] and [[france|France]]. In North [[america|America]], it was known as the French and Indian War. [[england|Britain]]'s victory expelled [[france|France]] from Canada but left the British treasury deeply in debt, leading directly to the taxation policies that sparked the [[america|America]]n crisis.

## In Common Sense
[[thomas-paine|Paine]] argues that the British claim to have protected the colonies during the war is self-serving: "It is the commerce and not the conquest of [[america|America]], by which [[england|England]] is benefited" (006-001). [[england|Britain]] fought the war for its own imperial interests, not for colonial welfare — and the subsequent taxes are an attempt to make the colonies pay for a war they did not choose. The war thus serves as background for [[thomas-paine|Paine]]'s argument that the British-colonial relationship is fundamentally extractive.
