# Herreshoff Manufacturing Company

The Herreshoff Manufacturing Company was a Rhode Island-based manufacturer of coil boilers and marine steam engines, noted in the journal for its use of [[heat-radiation|magnesia covering]] as a lining for boiler shells, where the material was "subjected to a very intense heat resulting from the forced draught used in this type of boiler" (006-004).

## Significance

The company's adoption of [[heat-radiation|magnesia covering]] as boiler lining provided real-world validation of the material's heat-resistance claims. The forced-draught firing used in Herreshoff boilers generated exceptionally high temperatures, making the company's successful use of magnesia a compelling demonstration of the material's durability for skeptical architects and engineers (006-004).
