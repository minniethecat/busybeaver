# Charles Keely

Charles Keely (c. 1855–1890) was an American architect based in Brooklyn, New York, and the son of [[Peter C. Keely]], the prolific architect of Catholic churches. The January 11, 1890 issue of *The American Architect and Building News* reported his death at about thirty-five years of age.

## Biography

### Career

Charles Keely was associated in business with his father, Peter C. Keely, forming the firm Keely & Son. The practice was among the largest ecclesiastical architectural offices in the United States, with as many as fifty churches in various stages of design and construction at one time. The journal noted that much of the excellent work produced by the office was due to the younger Keely's talent.

> [001-012] "The practice of the office is enormous, fifty churches, it is said, being sometimes in process of execution from the designs of the father and son, and of the excellent work done there, no doubt much was due to the younger man's talent."

### Death

Charles Keely died of pneumonia in Hartford, Connecticut, while visiting the bishop on business, at the age of about thirty-five. He was described as "active and popular."
