## Francesco Toso

Francesco Toso was a Venetian manufacturer of mirrors active in the late 19th century. He participated in the Italian exhibition at the 1889 Paris Exposition Universelle, held at the Champ de Mars.

### The Double-Image Statue

At the Italian exhibition in the Champ de Mars, Toso exhibited a wooden statue that attracted much attention from visitors. The work depicted Goethe's Marguerite standing before a mirror, with the mirror producing by reflection the image of Faust — an artifice conceived by the sculptor by forming Faust's figure from the folds of Marguerite's robe and carving Faust's face into the back of her hair. (013-028)

> We do not know the name of the author of the statue, but we can say that it was exhibited by Mr. Francesco Toso, a Venetian manufacturer of mirrors. The statue was of wood, and of nearly life-size.--*La Nature*. (013-028)
