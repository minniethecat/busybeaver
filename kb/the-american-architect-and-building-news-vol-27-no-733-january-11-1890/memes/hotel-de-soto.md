# Hotel de Soto

The Hotel de Soto, completed in 1889, was a major commercial hotel in Savannah, Georgia, designed by Boston architect William Gibbons Preston. Occupying an entire city square in the heart of Savannah, it represented the scale and ambition of late-19th-century American hotel architecture.

## Architecture

The hotel occupied a full square with a frontage of 300 feet on Liberty Street and 200 feet on Bull Street, forming two sides of the square (010-005). A two-story kitchen and servants' wing completed the service accommodation. The scale of the building — occupying an entire city block — placed it among the larger hotel projects reported in the architectural press of the period.

## Architect

William Gibbons Preston (1844–1910) was a prominent Boston architect whose practice included numerous public and commercial buildings. The Hotel de Soto commission reflected the reach of Boston architectural firms into the growing southern market following Reconstruction.
