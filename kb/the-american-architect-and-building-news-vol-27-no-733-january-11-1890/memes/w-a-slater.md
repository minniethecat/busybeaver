# W.A. Slater

Philanthropist and founder of the [[Slater Memorial Museum]] in [[Norwich, Connecticut]], whose museum of reproductions served as a model for public art education and whose open disclosure of collection costs was praised by the editors of *The American Architect and Building News*.

## Biography

### Early Life

William Albert Slater was born in 1857 in Norwich, Connecticut, the son of John Fox Slater, a prominent textile manufacturer and philanthropist. The Slater family was among the wealthiest industrial families in New England, with deep roots in the textile industry that had made Norwich a manufacturing center.

### Career and Philanthropy

Slater's principal philanthropic achievement was the founding of the [[Slater Memorial Museum]] on the campus of the Norwich Free Academy in 1888. The museum was conceived as an educational institution to bring art and culture to the people of Norwich. As the journal noted, "it has been Mr. Slater's desire that the museum established by him should serve not only to educate his townsmen, but also to stimulate others who had the means to follow his example in other parts of the country" (006-010).

Unusually for the time, Slater permitted the journal to publish the full cost of his museum's collections. "He has given us permission to make public the cost of his collections, which, we doubt not, will be a revelation to many" (006-010). The gross cost to Slater — "excluding, of course, the building itself — was exactly $27,112.97" (006-014). This transparency was intended to demonstrate that a museum of high educational value could be established at a reasonable cost, encouraging other philanthropists to undertake similar projects.

Slater established clear guiding principles for the museum's collection: it would contain "only reproductions, and no original works" (006-010), making it accessible as a teaching collection rather than a repository of rare originals. Under these guidelines, the museum assembled a comprehensive collection of plaster casts, photographs, and electrotype reproductions of coins and medals.

### Later Years and Legacy

Slater continued to support the museum throughout his life. He died in 1919. The Slater Memorial Museum remains in operation today, still housed in its original Romanesque Revival building on the Norwich Free Academy campus, and is recognized as one of the finest collections of plaster casts in the United States.

## In the Journal

The journal's editors devoted a substantial portion of Section 7 to documenting the costs and operations of the Slater Memorial Museum, treating it as a model for other communities:

> "Thanks to the liberality of Mr. W.A. Slater, the experiment has been tried in Norwich, Conn., and the results of the first year of the Slater Memorial Museum in attracting and holding popular interest have far exceeded the anticipations of its founder and his advisers." (006-010)

On the question of whether such an institution was worth the investment in a smaller city:

> "If to the founder or founders of such an institution it be sufficient recompense for their liberality to see their gift used, appreciated and enjoyed by people of all classes, the brief experience of the Slater Memorial Museum answers the question with a strong affirmative." (007-002)
