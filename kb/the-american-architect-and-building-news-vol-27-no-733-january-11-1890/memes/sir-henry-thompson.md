## Sir Henry Thompson

Sir Henry Thompson was a British surgeon and author whose writings on sanitary entombment were cited in *The American Architect and Building News* in January 1890. His 1880 article in the *Nineteenth Century* argued for cremation over earth burial on public-health grounds.

### Sanitary Entombment Advocacy

Thompson's arguments appeared in Section 8 (Sanitary Entombment) of the journal, which explored the public-health risks of traditional earth burial and considered alternatives. The journal quoted Thompson as a prominent voice in the debate:

> I state, as a fact of the highest importance, that, by burial in earth, we effectively provide—whatever sanitary precautions are taken by ventilation and drainage, whatever disinfection is applied after contagion has occurred—that the pestilential germs, which have destroyed the body in question, are thus so treasured and protected as to propagate and multiply, ready to reappear and work like ruin hereafter for others. (007-007)

Thompson was associated with the cremation movement in Britain, which gained traction in the late Victorian era alongside broader sanitary reform.
