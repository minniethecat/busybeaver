# Marble in Architecture

**Marble** as an architectural material was referenced in the January 11, 1890 issue of *The American Architect and Building News* in a cautionary note about the deterioration of Carrara marble in building applications.

## In the Journal

The journal noted a specific case of marble degradation:

> In 1883, the Carrara marble of which it was built had so decayed. (002-004)

This observation, made in the context of ancient and classical architecture (Section 2, "[[civil-architecture|Civil Architecture]] in Antiquity"), highlighted the material durability concerns that architects faced when specifying marble for exterior applications. Carrara marble, prized for its pure white appearance, was known to be vulnerable to atmospheric pollution and weathering in urban environments.
