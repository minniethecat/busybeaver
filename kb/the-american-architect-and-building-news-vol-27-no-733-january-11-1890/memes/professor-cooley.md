## Professor Cooley

Professor Cooley was an engineer and educator associated with Ann Arbor, Michigan, active in the Michigan Engineering Society during the late 19th century. His research on steam-pipe heat radiation was published in the *American Engineer* and cited in *The American Architect and Building News*.

### Steam-Pipe Insulation Research

Cooley presented a paper to the Michigan Engineering Society on the benefits of covering steam-pipes to prevent radiation. His findings were published in the *American Engineer* of June 12, 1890, and subsequently referenced in Section 6 (Heat Radiation Through Magnesia Covering) of *The American Architect*. (006-002)

> Recently, however, I have seen in the *American Engineer* of June 12, a report of the proceedings of the Michigan Engineering Society containing a paper by Professor Cooley, of Ann Arbor, Mich., in which he says: "The benefits of covering steam-pipes to prevent radiation are strikingly illustrated..." (006-002)

Cooley's paper provided practical case-study data that complemented the theoretical analysis of magnesia covering presented in the journal.
