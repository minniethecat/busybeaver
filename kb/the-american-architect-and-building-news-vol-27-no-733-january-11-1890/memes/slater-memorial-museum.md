# Slater Memorial Museum

The Slater Memorial Museum, in [[Norwich, Connecticut|Norwich]], was founded through the liberality of industrialist [[W.A. Slater]] and designed by the [[Boston]] architectural firm Sturgis & Brigham.

Dedicated in November [[1888]], it housed an extensive collection of plaster casts, electrotypes, and photographs representing European art masterpieces (007-001). The museum was intended to educate the American public and inspire similar institutions.

## Description

The [[museum-design-and-construction|museum building]], designed by [[Sturgis & Brigham]], was purpose-built to display reproductions of classical and Renaissance sculpture. The collection represented masterpieces of [[European art]] inaccessible to most Americans who could not travel abroad.

## Collection and Cost

The total collection cost of $27,112.97 (excluding the building) covered 491 casts, 215 photographs, 6 electrotypes, and additional display cases and furnishings (007-002).

## Impact

> If to the founder or founders of such an institution it be sufficient recompense for their liberality to see their gift used, appreciated and enjoyed by people of all classes, the brief experience of the Slater Memorial Museum answers the question with a strong affirmative.
> — (007-002)

The museum served as a model for other American institutions considering similar educational art collections. It demonstrated that a small-city museum could assemble a world-class teaching collection at modest cost.

## In the Journal

The January 11, 1890 issue devoted [[Section-7]] to "Museum Costs & Management," using the Slater Memorial Museum as its primary case study. The analysis detailed the museum's collection costs, acquisition strategy, and public reception.
