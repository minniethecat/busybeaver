# Philip Verplanck

Colonial American engineer, surveyor, and political representative, a grandson of [[Gulian Verplanck]] the original grantee of the Rombout-Verplanck patent. The location known as **Verplanck's Point** in [[Westchester County, New York]] was named for him.

## In the Journal

Philip Verplanck appears in Section 9 of the journal, within the history of the [[Verplanck Homestead]] and the Verplanck family. The account describes the family's return to the homestead after the Revolutionary War and gives a brief sketch of Philip's career:

> "Philip Verplanck, a grandson of Gulian the original grantee, was a native of the patent, but his public life was spent elsewhere. He was an engineer and surveyor, and an able man. Verplanck's Point in Westchester County, where Fort Lafayette stood during the Revolution, was named for him, and he represented that Manor in the Colonial Assembly from 1734 to 1768." (009-006)

## Key Facts

- Grandson of Gulian Verplanck, the original grantee of the Rombout-Verplanck land patent
- Served as an engineer and surveyor in colonial New York
- Represented the Manor of Cortlandt (Verplanck's Point) in the Colonial Assembly (1734–1768)
- Verplanck's Point, the site of Fort Lafayette during the Revolutionary War, was named after him
- His public life was primarily conducted outside the Verplanck patent lands
