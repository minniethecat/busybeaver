## Mr. Botch

Mr. Botch was an architectural illustrator whose original water-color drawings were selected and published by *The American Architect and Building News* as exemplary models for students of architectural sketching.

### Published Drawings

In a discussion of water-color painting resources for architectural students (Section 13, Trade Surveys & Business Review), the journal recommended among its own offerings:

> We have ourselves published one or two, from originals by Mr. Botch, which will answer as well as anything we know, being admirable in color and architectural feeling, and just sketchy enough. (013-016)

The journal commended Botch's work for maintaining the appropriate level of sketchiness — architectural water-colors were recommended to avoid becoming overly elaborate paintings, and Botch's drawings struck this balance effectively. His published works served alongside Winsor & Newton's *Handbooks on Sketching in Water-Colors* and Penley's *System of Water-Color Painting* as recommended study materials.
