# Elevator Systems

Mechanical lifts for vertical transportation of passengers and goods within multi-story buildings, installed as part of the integrated building services package in late 19th-century construction.

## Definition

Elevator systems in the 1890s encompassed the mechanical apparatus — steam or hydraulic engines, cables, guide rails, and cars — required to move people and freight vertically within buildings. By this period, elevators had become standard equipment in commercial and institutional buildings of three or more stories, typically specified alongside heating, [[building-ventilation|ventilation]], and plumbing systems as part of the mechanical services contract. [010-006]

## In Practice

In the construction and outfitting of significant 19th-century buildings, elevator installation was treated as a specialized trade subcontracted to machinery companies. The work was commissioned alongside other mechanical services, reflecting the integration of vertical transportation into the standard building services package of the era. [010-006]
