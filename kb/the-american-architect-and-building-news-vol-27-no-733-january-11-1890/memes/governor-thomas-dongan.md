# Governor Thomas Dongan

Thomas Dongan (1634–1715) was the colonial Governor of the Province of New York from 1683 to 1688. He issued the license under which the Wappinger Indians granted 85,000 acres of land to [[Gulian Verplanck]] and [[Francis Rombout]] in 1683 (008-011).

## Biography

### Early Life

Thomas Dongan was born in 1634 in County Kildare, Ireland, into a prominent Anglo-Irish family. He served in the French army before entering English service, and his military experience prepared him for colonial administration.

### Career

Appointed Governor of the Province of New York in 1683 by the Duke of York (later [[King James II]]), Dongan's administration was notable for establishing the first representative assembly in the colony — the Charter of Liberties and Privileges of 1683. His governorship oversaw significant territorial expansion, including the formal acquisition of lands through negotiated Indian deeds.

As Commander-in-Chief of the Province of New York, Governor Dongan authorized the land transaction between the Wappinger Indians and the patentees Gulian Verplanck and Francis Rombout. The grant was confirmed in 1685 by letters patent from [[King James II]] (008-011).

### Later Years / Legacy

Dongan returned to England after the Glorious Revolution of 1688. He died in 1715 in London. His 1683 Charter of Liberties is considered a foundational document in the development of representative government in New York.

## In the Journal

Dongan appears in the journal in connection with the legal foundation of the [[Verplanck Homestead]]:

> "The Verplanck homestead stands on the lands granted by the Wappinger Indians, in 1683, to Gulian Verplanck and Francis Rombout, under a license given by Governor Thomas Dongan Commander-in-Chief of the Province of New York." (008-011)

The original terms of the grant required the patentees to pay Governor Dongan an annual quitrent:

> "The patentees were also to pay Governor Dongan six bushels of good and merchantable winter wheat." (009-001)
