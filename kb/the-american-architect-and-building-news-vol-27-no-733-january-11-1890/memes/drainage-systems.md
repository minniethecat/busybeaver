# Drainage Systems

**Drainage systems** in late 19th-century architectural practice were considered essential sanitary infrastructure, mentioned alongside [[ventilation-systems]] in the January 11, 1890 issue of *The American Architect and Building News*.

## In the Journal

The journal noted in its discussion of [[Museum Design and Construction]] (Section 7) that:

> Sanitary precautions are taken by ventilation and drainage, whatever disinfection is applied. (007-007)

This brief reference places drainage within the broader context of public health engineering in institutional buildings. The conjunction of drainage with [[building-ventilation|ventilation]] and disinfection reflects the late 19th-century understanding of miasma theory and the importance of environmental controls in building design.
