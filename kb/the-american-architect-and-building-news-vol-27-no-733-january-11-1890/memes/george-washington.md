# George Washington

Commander of the Continental Army during the American Revolutionary War and first President of the United States. Washington appears in the journal through his connection to the [[Verplanck Homestead]] in [[Fishkill, New York]], where the proximity of his headquarters and the forging of his ceremonial sword linked the site to the Revolutionary era (009-003).

## Biography

### Early Life

George Washington was born in 1732 in Westmoreland County, Virginia. He worked as a surveyor in his youth before gaining military experience during the French and Indian War.

### Career

Washington served as Commander-in-Chief of the Continental Army from 1775 to 1783, leading the American colonies to victory in the Revolutionary War. He later presided over the Constitutional Convention of 1787 and served as the first President of the United States from 1789 to 1797.

His Hudson Valley headquarters during the Revolutionary War formed a strategic network of command posts. The proximity of his headquarters to the Verplanck Homestead made Fishkill a significant military location (009-003).

### Later Years / Legacy

Washington died in 1799 at his Mount Vernon estate in Virginia. He is remembered as the "Father of His Country" and his legacy is honored through numerous monuments and institutions. He served as the first President General of the [[Society of the Cincinnati]] from its founding in 1783.

## In the Journal

Washington is referenced three times in Section 9 of the journal, within the article on the Verplanck Homestead at Fishkill, New York. The homestead served as the headquarters of [[Baron Steuben]] during the Revolutionary War, and its historical significance was partly derived from its proximity to Washington's own headquarters across the Hudson River:

> "Baron Steuben chose it for his headquarters, no doubt for its nearness to Washington's headquarters across the river, and for the beauty and charm of the situation." (009-003)

The journal also evokes the gatherings of officers at the homestead during the war:

> "In fancy we can picture the assembly of officers grouped about Washington, in that west room overlooking the river, pledging themselves to preserve the memories of the years during which they had struggled for their country's being." (009-003)

A notable artifact connected to Washington was forged at the Fishkill supply depot, near the Verplanck estate:

> "Here was forged the sword of Washington, now in the keeping of the United States Government, and exhibited in the late Centennial collection." (009-004)

## Key Works / Contributions

- First President of the United States (1789–1797)
