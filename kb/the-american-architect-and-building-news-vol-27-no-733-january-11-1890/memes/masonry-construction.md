# Masonry Construction

**Masonry construction** in late 19th-century architecture was one of the primary methods for achieving fire-resistant buildings, discussed in the January 11, 1890 issue of *The American Architect and Building News* in connection with [[Fire Protection]] and [[slow-burning-mill-construction|mill construction]].

## In the Journal

The journal referenced masonry in its discussion of fire-resistant mill buildings, noting:

> Such buildings, whose iron and masonry construction is called fireproof. (010-035)

This places masonry construction alongside iron framing as the preferred combination for [[Slow-Burning Mill Construction]] and other industrial architecture where fire resistance was paramount.
