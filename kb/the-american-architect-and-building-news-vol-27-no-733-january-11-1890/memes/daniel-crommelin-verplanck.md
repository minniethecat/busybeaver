# Daniel Crommelin Verplanck

Daniel Crommelin Verplanck was a member of the prominent [[Verplanck Family]] of Fishkill, New York, who lived at the historic [[Verplanck Homestead]] on the Hudson River.

## Biography

### Family and Residence

Daniel C. Verplanck was a descendant of [[Gulian Verplanck]], the original patentee of the 85,000-acre Verplanck land grant of 1683. The Verplanck homestead remained in the family for generations, and Daniel C. Verplanck lived and died there, as his father had before him and as his descendants continued to do into the late nineteenth century.

> [009-007] "It was from under its roof that Daniel C. Verplanck was carried to his last resting-place as his father before him, and generations after him lived and still live in the old Homestead."

### The Homestead

The Verplanck homestead at Fishkill Landing is a stone, plastered house one and one-half stories high, with a gambrel shingled roof and dormer windows. About 1810, a north end addition of wood was built, which altered the original Dutch architectural type, though the interior remained "quaint, dignified and interesting" according to the journal's account.
