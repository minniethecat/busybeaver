# Baron Steuben

Baron Friedrich Wilhelm von Steuben was a Prussian-born military officer who served as a major general in the Continental Army during the American Revolutionary War. During the war, he chose the [[Verplanck Homestead]] at Fishkill, New York, as his headquarters.

## Biography

### Revolutionary War Service

During the American Revolutionary War, Baron Steuben selected the Verplanck homestead at Fishkill Landing as his headquarters. The choice was made for its strategic proximity to George Washington's headquarters across the Hudson River, and for the beauty and charm of the location.

> [009-003] "Baron Steuben chose it for his headquarters, no doubt for its nearness to Washington's headquarters across the river, and for the beauty and charm of the situation."

The homestead became a site of historical significance when, in 1783, the [[Society of the Cincinnati]] was organized under its roof — a fraternal order of Continental Army officers.

## In the Journal

The journal describes the Verplanck homestead as being in excellent preservation at the time of publication (1890), with the room where Washington and his officers assembled still carefully preserved.
