# Temperature Control in Buildings

**Temperature control** was a recurring concern in the January 11, 1890 issue of *The American Architect and Building News*, appearing in discussions of ancient Roman thermal engineering, modern [[Heat Radiation]] prevention, and structural fire resistance.

## Historical Context

The journal's article on [[Civil Architecture in Antiquity]] noted that Roman architects designed buildings so "the variations in temperature should not be felt in the interior" (003-026), demonstrating an ancient awareness of thermal comfort in architectural design.

## Structural Considerations

Temperature's effect on building materials was a practical concern in 1890 fire-protection engineering. The journal observed that iron structural elements, "being weakened by the temperature only to a slight extent as the fire increases" (011-008), could maintain structural integrity longer than unprotected wood — a key principle in [[Slow-Burning Mill Construction]].
