# Deputy Parker

Deputy Parker is the law enforcement officer whom Fred Gross recruits for the gambling raid on the Hamilton residence — a key figure in the most dramatic episode of neighborly warfare in the stories. Parker is, in Fred's telling, both an ally and a liability.

## The Recruitment

Fred approaches Deputy Parker with information about illegal gambling at the Hamilton residence, expecting the deputy to act with professional vigor. The deputy's willingness to participate — perhaps fortified, as Fred notes, with "a few glassis of currage" (004-017) — suggests a man whose enforcement decisions are influenced by factors beyond strict legality.

## The Raid

The raid on the Hamilton residence is the climax of "Uncivil War" and Deputy Parker is its instrument. His presence transforms Fred's personal grievance into official action, lending the weight of law enforcement to what is, at root, a neighborly grudge. The deputy's role in the episode raises questions about the use and abuse of legal authority in personal disputes.

Fred's account of the raid does little to flatter the deputy's professionalism:

> ...it came near being a bad mix up on acct. of this bone head deputy Parker that Bob sent out to make the pinch. He pored a few glassis of currage down his throte before he went on the job & when he got ready to work he was all lit up like a church...
>
> (003-054)

## The Character

Fred's portrait of Deputy Parker is characteristically unreliable. Is Parker a competent officer doing his duty, or a suggestible man manipulated by Fred's private vendetta? Fred's account, which presents the deputy's actions as proper and justified, leaves the reader to infer the more complex reality.
