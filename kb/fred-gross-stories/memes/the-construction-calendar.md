# The Construction Calendar

The construction of Fred's bungalow follows a calendar dictated by weather, finance, and the availability of trades. Fred's expectations about this calendar — when the foundation will be laid, when the house will be finished, when the family will move in — are consistently confounded by realities he did not anticipate.

## The Original Schedule

Fred's initial plan is optimistic: foundation laid before cold weather, construction through the winter, occupancy in the spring. This schedule assumes that everything will go according to contract, that the bank will cooperate, that the weather will oblige. None of these assumptions proves reliable.

## The Delays

The bank's refusal to extend a building loan until the lot is paid off imposes a six-month delay. The foundation, dug "the rong way," requires additional work. Weather intervenes — good weather for grading creates an expense, and cold weather threatens the construction sequence. Each delay costs money as well as time, and Fred's careful budgeting cannot accommodate all of them.

## The Final Timeline

The actual construction timeline, pieced together from Fred's letters, is longer and more expensive than the original schedule. Yet the bungalow is eventually built, and the Grosses eventually move in. Fred's persistence — his refusal to abandon the project despite the delays and costs — is one of his more admirable qualities, visible beneath the comic surface of his complaints.
