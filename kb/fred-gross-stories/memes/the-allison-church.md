# The Allison Church

The church in Allison is one of the town's foundational institutions — a place of worship, a center of community, and a stage for the public performance of respectability. The Gross family's attendance is assumed, and the church's rhythms organize the weekly calendar of suburban life.

## The Institution

A town like Allison in 1915 would have had at least one Protestant church — Methodist, Presbyterian, Congregational, or Baptist — serving as both religious and social center. The church building was typically among the more substantial structures in town, reflecting the community's investment in religious infrastructure.

## Sunday as Social Theater

Sunday morning services are as much social as religious occasions. Families arrive in their best clothes, occupy their accustomed pews, and are seen by their neighbors in the act of worship. For families like the Grosses, church attendance is not optional — it is a visible marker of respectability, and absence would be noticed and interpreted.

## The Minister

The minister is a figure of moral authority whose presence in the stories is implicit rather than explicit. Fred does not record sermons or theological reflections; he records the social facts of church life. The minister's role, like the church's, is assumed rather than examined — part of the institutional background of Allison life.
