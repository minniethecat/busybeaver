# The Allison Butcher

The butcher shop is a staple of the local food economy in Allison — a specialized merchant providing meat to the town's households. For families like the Grosses, the butcher represents both the convenience of local commerce and another line item in the household budget.

## The Shop

A butcher shop in 1915 was a specialized retail establishment — cool, tiled, smelling of meat and sawdust. The butcher was a skilled tradesman who cut carcasses to order, advised on cuts and preparation, and maintained personal relationships with his customers. Unlike the anonymous supermarket meat counter of later decades, the butcher shop was a place of personal commerce.

## The Butcher

The butcher was a familiar figure in the community — someone who knew his customers' preferences, their budgets, their family situations. He extended credit, offered recommendations, and participated in the local gossip economy. For Grace Gross, who managed the household's food purchasing, the butcher was a regular point of contact with the commercial world.

## The Budget

Meat was one of the larger items in the household budget, and Fred's price consciousness extended to the butcher's charges. The cost of a roast for Sunday dinner, the price of chops for a weekday meal — these expenses were part of the running calculation that shaped the Gross family's standard of living. The butcher's bill, like all bills, occasioned Fred's characteristic mixture of resignation and resentment.
