The Dance Rejection is the climactic event of Story 02 (Welcome to Our City). After moving to Allison and attempting to integrate into its social scene, Fred Gross secures what he believes are invitations to the village's premier social event — the Allison dance. He and Grace prepare elaborately, only to be turned away at the door.

## What Happened

Fred, misunderstanding the social signals of Allison's informal networks, believes he and Grace have been invited to the dance. He buys new clothes, arranges for a babysitter, and builds up the event in his letters to Charley as proof of his social arrival. On the night of the dance, Fred and Grace present themselves — and are refused entry. They are not on the guest list. The humiliation is total.

> Well I guess you know how I all ways been a bout danceing Charley & never cared nothing a bout it & I was going to tell Grace of corse we wouldent go but when I give her the invatation & seen how good she felt about it I dident have the heart to say nothing a bout not going.
>
> (002-058)

## Correction to Existing Page

The SCN29 semantic scan (Phase 7, 2026-07-14) identified that the existing page [[the-allison-dance]] incorrectly states that Fred attended the dance successfully. The source text is clear: Fred was rejected. This page corrects that factual error by documenting the actual event.

## Thematic Significance

The Dance Rejection is the definitive Fred Gross social humiliation. It crystallizes the gap between Fred's self-perception (a man of standing, entitled to attend) and village reality (an outsider, not on the list). The elaborate buildup — new clothes, babysitter arrangements, excited letters to Charley — makes the rejection more devastating, and more comic.
