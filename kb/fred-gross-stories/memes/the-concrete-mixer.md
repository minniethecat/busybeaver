# The Concrete Mixer

The concrete mixer that pours the foundation of Fred's bungalow is a small but significant presence in the stories — a machine that represents the industrialization of construction and the replacement of manual labor with mechanical power. Fred's house is built by machines as well as men, and the concrete mixer is the most visible of these machines.

## The Machine

Portable concrete mixers were relatively new on American construction sites in the 1910s. Before their introduction, concrete was mixed by hand — a laborious process involving shovels, wheelbarrows, and backbreaking work. The mechanical mixer accelerated construction, reduced labor costs, and made concrete foundations more affordable for middle-class homes like Fred's.

## The Foundation

The concrete foundation is the literal base of Fred's bungalow — the part of the house sunk into the ground, invisible once construction is complete but essential to everything above it. The concrete mixer pours this foundation, and its brief appearance in the construction sequence marks the transition from excavation to building — the moment when the house begins to rise.

## The Symbolism

The concrete mixer, like the automobile, the telephone, and the installment plan, is a technology of modernity — a machine that transforms how things are done and what things cost. It is part of the technological infrastructure that makes suburban development possible, and its presence in the stories, however brief, signals the industrial context of Fred's domestic dream.
