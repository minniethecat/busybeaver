# Weather and Seasons

Weather in the Fred Gross stories is never merely atmospheric. It is a practical force that shapes the calendar of construction, the rhythms of suburban life, and the emotional temperature of Fred's domestic world. Lardner uses weather with journalistic precision — it is what it is, and what it is matters.

## The Construction Calendar

Weather is the invisible co-author of "Own Your Own Home." The entire construction timeline bends to it. Fred plans to "get the foundashun layed before real cold weather & then the carpenters can finnish up dureing the winter" (001-017). The hope is that "we can be in the house next spring" (001-017). This is weather as practical constraint, not metaphor — the foundation must be in before the ground freezes, and that deadline drives the narrative.

Later, when the architect advises grading "because the weather was so good & then we wouldent have to do no gradeing in the spring" (001-050), weather again intervenes in Fred's plans and finances. The $60 grading expense that follows is, in a sense, a weather event — an unplanned cost imposed by the calendar.

## Seasonal Rhythms

The stories follow a rough seasonal logic. Construction occupies the autumn and winter; the country club comes alive in spring and summer. Fred's golf education begins as "the season opens" (P03-037). Winter brings parties and indoor socializing; summer brings outdoor activity and heightened visibility among neighbors.

## Weather and Mood

While Lardner rarely uses weather as explicit emotional metaphor, the practical effects of weather shape Fred's emotional state. Bad weather delays construction; good weather accelerates social activity. When Fred worries about whether the weather will cooperate with his building schedule, the reader understands that weather, like everything else in Fred's world, is another variable he cannot control.

Well I says I want to borry $1,200 to build a bungello out to Allison & he says do you own a lot & I says yes & he says let me take a look at your papers so I show him my papers & he says yes you own a lot all but paying $700 more on it. I says yes but I own it because there is the deed & the garantee polisy & when I get threw paying you back what you lone me to build the house I will pay off the bal. on the lot & he says o no you wont because we dont do no busness that way & 1st you got to pay off what you owe on the lot & then may be we can fix it up a bout a building lone but I wont promus nothing. Well I argude with him but nothing doing & when I come home & told Grace I thot shed cry her eyesout but finely we seen they wasent no use in that so all we can do now is save up & pay off the $700 we still owe on the lot & then we can start building & meen time we can be planing the bungello & we figure that we can save $30 per wk. in sted of $25 if we live pretty clost & can have the lot payed for in less than 6 mos. & that will be in Nov. & we can be all ready to start building & get the foundashun layed before real cold weather & then the carpenters can finnish up dureing the winter & we can be in the house next spring & we will half to renew our lease for 6 mos. but we dont care nothing a bout that because it aint so bad liveing in a flat dureing winter when the kids couldent be out much any way.
