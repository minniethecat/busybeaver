# War Bribes

The fifth Fred Gross story shifts from suburban social conflict to the world of wartime business, as Fred becomes an enthusiastic participant in the defense economy.

**Author:** Ring Lardner (as Fred Gross)
**Published:** 1917
**Story Number:** 5

## Synopsis

Fred Gross discovers the world of war profiteering — "what they call the war bribes" (005-029) — and eagerly invests in defense-related stocks. The story traces his journey from enthusiastic investor to disillusioned participant, using the war economy as a lens through which to examine American business ethics, patriotism, and the gap between Fred's understanding and reality.

## Plot Summary

Fred observes that "some of the automobile plants and boat factorys" (005-030) are profiting from wartime contracts. He finds an inside track on a defense investment: "I got an" (005-042) opportunity and pursues it. The specific investment involves "1 of these here war bribes Charley and its a big factory down in Ind. some wheres" (005-046). Fred's involvement with "a automobile stock" (005-081) shows his investment strategy — enthusiastic, under-researched, and ultimately disappointing.

> This rumley is 1 of these here war bribes Charley and its a big factory down in Ind. some wheres and there going to make riffles for the war and of coarse when that gets out in the papers evry body will want some of the stock and thats what makes it go up.
>
> (005-046)

## Themes

- **War Profiteering** — the ethical ambiguities of the wartime economy
- **Speculation** — Fred's financial reach exceeding his grasp
- **Business Ethics** — patriotism vs. profit

## Style & Form

The story marks a shift in the series from domestic comedy to social satire with broader targets. The war provides a backdrop that elevates Fred's personal foibles to national significance.
