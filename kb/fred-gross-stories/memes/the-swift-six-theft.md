The Swift Six Theft is the climactic event of Story 06 (The Swift Six). After purchasing the Swift Six automobile with $600 from Grace's father, Fred takes the family for their inaugural ride. During a rainstorm, with Grace and the children in the car, the vehicle is stolen — the ultimate humiliation in Fred's automotive saga.

## What Happened

Fred, eager to show off his new Swift Six, takes Grace and the children for a Sunday drive. A rainstorm forces them to stop. In the confusion of the storm, with the family sheltering, the car is stolen. Fred is left stranded with his family, having lost the expensive automobile he purchased with his father-in-law's money — the very symbol of his middle-class aspiration.

## The Chain of Events

The theft is the culmination of a chain of misfortunes:
1. Alec Welsh takes Fred to the auto show (catalyst)
2. Fred becomes obsessed with the Swift Six
3. Grace's father provides $600 for the purchase
4. Fred buys the car despite not knowing how to drive properly
5. Coworkers play pranks on the new car (flattened tire)
6. Jerry Donahue stops Fred for driving without a license
7. The inaugural family ride — rainstorm — theft

## Thematic Significance

The theft is the final humiliation in Fred's cycle of aspiration and failure. The car — the most expensive and visible symbol of middle-class status — is taken from him almost immediately, in the most humiliating circumstances possible (with his family present, in a storm, helpless). It is the definitive Lardner comic set piece: the moment when all of Fred's pretensions collapse at once.
