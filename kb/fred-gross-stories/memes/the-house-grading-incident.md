# The House Grading Incident

Among the many small financial shocks of "Own Your Own Home," the house grading incident stands out for its purity as an illustration of Fred's predicament. The contract said one thing; the architect said another; Fred paid $60 he had not budgeted for. The incident encapsulates the gap between contractual promises and practical realities that defines Fred's experience of homebuilding.

## The Contract vs. Reality

The construction contract specifies that the contractor is responsible for grading — using the dirt excavated from the foundation to level the ground around the house. This is, in Fred's understanding, settled. But when the architect advises that "we should ought to do some gradeing on the lot a round the house because the weather was so good" (001-050), the settled becomes unsettled.

> He says we should ought to do some gradeing on the lot a round the house because the weather was so good and he says it could be done for $60 and that would save us from haveing to wait till spring to do it and he says on acct. of there diging the foundashun the rong way at the start they wasent no dirt left over.
>
> (001-050)

## The Architect's Logic

The architect explains that "on acct of there diging the foundashun the rong way at the start they wasent no dirt left over because they had to fill in where they dug out and shouldent of dug" (001-050). The foundation was dug wrong; therefore the expected dirt is not available; therefore Fred must pay someone else $60 to bring in fill. The logic is impeccable in its circularity: the contractor's error becomes Fred's expense.

## Fred's Response

Fred hires a man to do the grading for $60. He reports the expense to Charley with weary resignation: "thats all over now & out of the way" (001-050). The $60 is another item on the growing list of unplanned costs, another small defeat in Fred's ongoing war with the construction process. He does not fight the charge; he absorbs it and moves on, preserving his faith that the system, in the end, is working for him.
