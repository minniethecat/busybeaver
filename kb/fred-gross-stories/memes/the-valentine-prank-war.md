The Valentine Prank War is the central plot arc of Story 04 (Uncivil War), spanning seven letters from February 3 to March 20, 1916. What begins as a Valentine's Day card exchange between the Gross and Martin households escalates into an all-out neighbor feud involving fire hoses, mourning crepe, fake smallpox quarantine signs, tire deflation, and telephone harassment.

## Sequence of Events

### The Valentine Exchange (Feb 3–14)
Fred sends a Valentine card to Mrs. Martin as a neighborly gesture. Mr. Martin misinterprets this as a romantic overture and retaliates. The exchange of cards grows increasingly hostile.

### The Escalation (Feb 14–28)
- **Fire Hose Ambush**: Someone ambushes Fred with a garden hose
- **Mourning Crepe Incident**: Black crepe appears on Fred's door, implying a death in the family
- **Smallpox Quarantine Sign**: A fake Board of Health quarantine sign is posted on the Gross house
- **Tire Deflation**: Fred's automobile tires are let down

### The Climax (March)
Fred, believing his neighbor Hamilton is behind the attacks, retaliates. The conflict peaks with a telephone harassment campaign and culminates in the police (or constable) being called. The story's ironic revelation: Martin, not Hamilton, orchestrated the entire prank war — Fred has been fighting the wrong neighbor all along.

## Thematic Significance

The Valentine Prank War is Lardner's satire of suburban social warfare — minor slights escalating through petty retaliation into full-scale conflict. The irony that Fred targets the wrong neighbor underscores his fundamental misreading of social situations, the defining comic trait of the entire story cycle.

Fred's scheme to sabotage Hamilton's Valentine party with a quarantine sign:

> Martin says if I could get a hold of 1 of them sines the helth bord hangs up on a house when the residence has got some decease and got to be quarnteed and hang it under the electrick light on Hamiltons porch the night of the party the guests would all of them see it and be scared to go in the house and turn around and go home.
>
> (004-015)
