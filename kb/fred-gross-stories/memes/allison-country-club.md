# Allison Country Club

The **Allison Country Club** is a social and recreational institution in [[allison]] where [[fred-gross]] becomes a member and takes up golf. The club represents the aspirational social world of Allison's suburban middle class.

## Fred's Membership

Fred joins the club at the encouragement of Mr. Ewing and his wife Grace. He reports to his brother Charley:

> "I have joined the Allison country club Charley and the first day I c…" (P03-003)

Grace sees the club as "a grand thing" for social advancement (P03-007), though Fred himself admits he is more interested in the social status than the sport: "it isent on acct. of playing golf that I have joined the club but mostly on acct. of the Mrs." (P03-004).

Initially a beginner who has never played golf (P03-006), Fred takes lessons from "the golf teacher out to the club" (P03-028) and gradually improves, even participating in club tournaments and competing for "the presidents cup" (P03-055).

## Club Facilities

During Fred's membership, the club is constructing a new clubhouse:

> "It seems like there trying to build a new club house so they been makeing a…" (P03-005)

The club provides golf equipment — bags, balls, and "brass clubs" [P03-017–018] — as well as food and drink in the clubhouse [P03-103, P03-105]. Members socialize between rounds and Fred notes the club is a place "where you can get all the…" (P03-086).

## Financial Disputes

Fred's relationship with the club sours over fees. He receives an unexpectedly large bill:

> "…handed me a bill from the golf club and how much do you think it…" (P03-114)

When he questions the club treasurer about the charges (P03-116), he is told "you will be fired from the club" (P03-119). Fred's response is characteristically defiant: "you can take your golf club and go and jump in the Des Pl…" (P03-121).

By the end of his membership, Fred plans to sell his bag and clubs, though he retains "there silver pres…" (P03-126) as a souvenir.
