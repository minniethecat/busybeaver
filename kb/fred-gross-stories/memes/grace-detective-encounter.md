# Grace's Detective Encounter

The afternoon Grace Gross called on the Hamiltons and was mistaken for a female detective — a comic episode of suburban misapprehension in "Welcome to Our City" (story-02) that Fred Gross recounts with characteristic relish.

## Overview

When Grace Gross paid a neighborly call on the newly arrived Hamiltons, she was greeted not with hospitality but with suspicion. Mr. Hamilton, observing Grace's formal demeanor and the calling card she presented, concluded that she must be a female detective investigating his household. The misunderstanding — equal parts paranoia and social awkwardness — became one of Fred's favorite stories about his new neighbors. (002-026)

## Background

The Hamiltons had recently moved into the house next door to the Grosses, and the two families had not yet established a relationship. Grace, following the social conventions of the day, decided to pay a call on the new neighbors. She dressed carefully and brought a calling card — the standard equipment of a formal social visit in 1916. (002-024)

What Grace did not know was that the Hamiltons were sensitive about their household activities. Their regular card games, which involved money changing hands, occupied a gray area between social recreation and gambling. Mr. Hamilton, perhaps already nervous about legal exposure, saw in Grace's formal visit something more sinister than a welcome to the neighborhood. (002-027)

## Key Events

- **The visit**: Grace crossed the yard to the Hamilton residence after an early dinner and presented her card. Mrs. Hamilton received her coolly, and Grace sensed something was wrong. (002-024) (002-025)

The moment of absurd accusation is preserved in Fred's vivid retelling:

> ...Mr. Hamilton says what do you mean yourself comeing a round trying to pry in to some bodys affares. We aint use to receiving calls from female detectives. & then all of a sudden Grace caught on to what was he getting at.
>
> (002-026)

- **Mr. Hamilton's suspicion**: When Mr. Hamilton appeared, he studied Grace with evident suspicion. He later explained to his wife — overheard by Grace — that he was "used to receiving calls from female detectives." (002-026) Grace, bewildered, continued to make polite conversation while trying to understand the strange atmosphere.

- **The card mix-up**: The calling card Grace had presented became the center of the misunderstanding. Mr. Hamilton examined it and concluded it must be a detective's credential. Fred, recounting the story to Charley, found the Hamiltons' paranoia hilarious. (002-027)

- **The resolution**: Grace eventually clarified that she was simply a neighbor paying a call. The Hamiltons, embarrassed by their mistake, attempted to smooth over the incident, but the damage to neighborly relations was done. (002-028)

## Aftermath & Consequences

The detective encounter established the tone for all subsequent Gross-Hamilton interactions: suspicion, misunderstanding, and Fred's running narrative of Hamilton foolishness. The incident also revealed the Hamiltons' anxiety about their card games — an anxiety that Fred would later exploit when he arranged the gambling raid. (004-006)

## Literary Significance

The detective encounter is Lardner at his most economically comic. In a single misunderstanding — a calling card misinterpreted as a badge — he captures the paranoia, class anxiety, and social friction of suburban life. The episode also demonstrates Fred's skill as a storyteller: his retelling transforms a simple social awkwardness into an absurdist comedy at the Hamiltons' expense.
