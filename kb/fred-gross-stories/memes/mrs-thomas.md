Mrs. Thomas is an offstage character in the Fred Gross stories — the wife of Allison's wealthiest man and a social gatekeeper whose approval or disapproval determines Fred and Grace's standing in village society. She is referenced in the Fore! preface and in Story 02.

## Role in the Stories

In the Fore! preface, Ring Lardner mentions Mrs. Thomas as the wife of the richest man in Allison — a figure of local social authority. In Welcome to Our City, the Thomases represent the pinnacle of Allison society that Fred aspires to join. Mrs. Thomas never appears directly; her power is exercised through social judgment at one remove — invitations extended or withheld, opinions formed and circulated through the village's informal networks.

> Well Grace is all excited about me playing Mr. Thomas and she meeting Mrs. Thomas and the cup and every thing and cant hardly wait for tomorrow to come and niether can I Chas. because I am wild to go to it and no more nervous than as if I was pairing my finger nails.
>
> (P03-050)

## Narrative Function

Mrs. Thomas functions as the invisible apex of Allison's social hierarchy. Her absence from direct narrative makes her more powerful: she is a standard against which social acceptance is measured, never a character who can be directly negotiated with. This reflects the story's satire of suburban social climbing — the people whose approval matters most are the ones you never actually meet.
