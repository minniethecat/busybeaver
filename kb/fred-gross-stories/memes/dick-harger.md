Dick Harger is a named coworker of Fred Gross who appears in Story 05 (War Bribes). He is the source of the stock-market tips that drive Fred's speculative investing subplot — a chain of insider information that runs from the Chief through Harger to Fred.

## Role in War Bribes

Harger passes stock tips to Fred, claiming inside knowledge of impending price movements in industrial stocks — Bethlehem Steel, Crucible Steel, General Motors, and others. Fred, eager to make money without effort, invests based on these tips. The subplot satirizes the culture of wartime speculation: every office worker fancies himself a market insider, trading on tips of uncertain provenance.

Fred reports his latest stock tip to Charley:

> This here is the same stock Dick Harger told me a bout it the time I bought the rumley but he dident know nothing a bout it but was jest guessing but this time I got the tip from a man that knows what hes talking a bout.
>
> (005-079)

## Character Function

Dick Harger represents the everyman speculator — the ordinary office worker who believes he has access to inside information. His tips to Fred illustrate the contagion of speculative fever during the WWI economic boom. Whether Harger's tips are genuine or merely office gossip is left ambiguous.
