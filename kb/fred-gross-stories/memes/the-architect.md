The Architect is an unnamed recurring figure in Story 01 (Own Your Own Home). Hired by Fred Gross to draw plans for his new bungalow at 489 Roberson Ave. in Allison, the Architect becomes a source of escalating frustration as his errors drive up construction costs and delay the project.

## Role in the Story

Fred hires the Architect to design the bungalow, but the plans contain significant errors that require costly corrections during construction. The Architect's mistakes — wrong measurements, impractical designs — compound the already strained budget. The situation culminates when the Architect suffers a nervous breakdown and withdraws from the project entirely, leaving Fred to manage the consequences.

> Its all in the contrack & the contracker cant get a way from it because if he does he wont get no money & the $2,000 includs evry thing & hes got to be all done with the house by the 1 of march & the archateck over looks evry thing & sees that all the work is done right.
>
> (001-029)

## Narrative Function

The Architect serves as one of several professional antagonists in Fred's homeownership ordeal (alongside the Contractor, the lumber company, and the bank). Each professional Fred hires proves incompetent or unreliable, reinforcing the story's satire of middle-class aspiration — Fred's dream of respectable homeownership is undermined at every turn by the very experts he pays to realize it.
