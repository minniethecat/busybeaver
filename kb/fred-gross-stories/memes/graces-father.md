Grace's Father is an offstage character in Ring Lardner's Fred Gross stories. A wealthy Milwaukee resident, he provides crucial financial support to Fred and Grace Gross, enabling major plot developments without ever appearing directly in the narrative. He is referenced exclusively by familial role — "Grace's father," "her old man," "the old man" — making him invisible to mechanical entity discovery methods.

## Financial Role

Across the stories, Grace's Father provides at least $1,030 in documented support:

- **Story 01 (Own Your Own Home)**: Contributes $430 toward the bungalow construction, sent through Grace
- **Story 02 (Welcome to Our City)**: Referenced as a financial backstop during the Allison move
- **Story 05 (War Bribes)**: Fred mentions the father-in-law's wealth as context for his own speculative investing
- **Story 06 (The Swift Six)**: Provides $600 that enables Fred to purchase the Swift Six automobile — the central plot driver

>
> (005-018)

## Character Ambiguity

The SCN29 scan (2026-07-14) identified a potential identity question: whether Grace's Father is the same person as the "Mr. Carpenter" who appears as a golf companion in story 05, or a separate individual. The source text does not resolve this definitively. The "Carpenter" surname appears in multiple contexts — as neighbors to the Hamiltons and possibly as Grace's maiden name — introducing ambiguity the mechanical discovery pipeline did not flag.

## Narrative Function

Grace's Father represents the economic link between Fred's middle-class pretensions and actual middle-class solvency. Fred's schemes consistently require more capital than his salary provides; Grace's Father bridges the gap, functioning as a deus ex machina of respectable bourgeois finance.
