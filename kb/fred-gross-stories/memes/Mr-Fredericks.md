# Mr. Fredericks

Mr. Fredericks, the owner of the local garage, is Fred Gross's golfing partner at the Allison Country Club and a figure whose automobile ownership and athletic competence embody the capabilities Fred admires but cannot match.

## Role & Overview

Fredericks appears in the Fore! section as Fred's golfing companion. Fred reports their round in detail: "His name is Fredericks and he owns the garage" (P03-017). Fredericks is a competent golfer who "done it in 6 strikes" (P03-020) and, significantly, owns an automobile — "Fredericks had his automobile along and ast me to ride home with him" (P03-022).

The combination of garage ownership, automobile possession, and golf skill makes Fredericks a composite of everything Fred is not: mechanically adept, athletically capable, and mobile. Fred's report of their round together is characteristically admiring and self-deprecating.

> They was a man I know just going to start too and he ast me would I play with him so I says yes. His name is Fredericks and he owns the garage.
>
> (P03-017)

## Character Profile

### Background

Fredericks owns a garage — likely an automobile repair and service business — in or near Allison. This occupation places him in the emerging automotive economy, a sector Fred can only participate in as a consumer and spectator.

### Personality & Traits

Fredericks is portrayed as competent and friendly. He offers Fred a ride home, demonstrating the casual generosity of someone for whom automobile ownership is unremarkable. His golf game is better than Fred's — "Fredericks shot 1st and his ball went" (P03-018) — but he is not portrayed as competitive or dismissive. He is simply someone who inhabits his capabilities naturally, without the self-consciousness that characterizes Fred's every social interaction.

## Key Appearances

Fred plays golf with Fredericks, walks "along with Fredericks while he played the last" (P03-021) holes, and accepts a ride home in Fredericks's automobile. The encounter is brief but revealing: in Fredericks's company, Fred's limitations — financial, athletic, social — are silently displayed.

## Literary Significance

Fredericks is a minor character who serves a major thematic function. He is the embodiment of competent masculinity — a man who owns a business, plays golf well, drives a car — and his casual presence beside Fred on the golf course creates an implicit comparison that Fred's narration registers without fully understanding.
