# Mrs. Martin

**Mrs. Martin** is a young, fashion-conscious woman in [[allison]] who features prominently in the social rivalry depicted in [[uncivil-war]]. She is one of several Allison residents caught between [[grace-gross]]'s social circle and that of [[mrs-hamilton]].

## Character

Fred Gross describes her youth and appearance:

> "…she aint nothing but a girl…" (004-003)

Mrs. Martin has an eye for fashion — Fred notes that Grace and [[mary-gross]] wanted to see her "dolled up for the evening" (004-008). Her stylishness is a source of insecurity for other women:

> "…with a lady that dresses like Mrs. Martin a womman must feel pretty slo…" (004-031)

## Social Role

Mrs. Martin occupies an important position in Allison's social landscape. She teaches Grace a ballad (004-032) and dances with Fred at a gathering:

> "…I and Mrs. Martin danced to gather and I thot I…" (004-033)

She advises Grace on social obligations, suggesting they "ought to give a house party" (004-044) and offering opinions on what it costs to live in Allison society (004-045).

## The Hamilton Rivalry

Mrs. Martin becomes entangled in the tension between the Gross and Hamilton households. When [[mr-hamilton]] fails to call on Mrs. Martin, it becomes a point of contention (004-070). Grace expresses sympathy for Mrs. Martin's position in the conflict [004-071, 004-078].
