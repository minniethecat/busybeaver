# The Architect Dispute

Fred Gross hired an architect for $100 to handle the bungalow project so that "I dont half to do no worring" (001-016). The architect, in Fred's telling, does precisely the opposite — calling five times a day with questions Fred cannot answer, proposing upgrades that increase costs, and shifting responsibility onto the client he was hired to serve.

## The Promise vs. Reality

The architect was supposed to be Fred's advocate and shield: "the archateck over looks evry thing & sees that all the work is OK so I dont half to do no worring a bout that" (001-019). But Fred soon discovers that the architect is more interested in covering himself than protecting Fred. "They got me all most drove crazy," Fred explodes to Charley, "& if any body ever says to you build a house bust them in the jaw" (001-020).

## The Phone Calls

The architect's daily phone calls become a torment. He asks Fred to choose between tin and ivory bathtubs, to approve changes that cost extra, to make decisions that the contract supposedly settled. "What do I know a bout it," Fred protests, "& he is getting payed for doing the worring but in sted of him doing it he lays it all off on to me" (001-020). The architect has inverted the relationship: Fred, who paid for expertise, is being asked to supply it.

## The Cost

Every call from the architect brings a new expense. The ivory bathtub costs "$25 or $30 more" (001-020). Each upgrade chips away at Fred's budget and his sanity. The $100 architect's fee, which seemed like a bargain, becomes a source of endless aggravation — a small price for a service that is not being delivered.

## Red Tape

> **Dear Charley**. Well Charley I guess I was in to much of a hurry & it dont look now like we would start building this summer but will half to wait till later on. I dident know how they done them things but found out that theys a lot of red tape & you cant do them things in no hurry like I thot. The real estate man dident have no papers fixed up the day before yest. like he promused but had them fixed up yest. & he give me the warranty deed or what ever it is they call it & I give him my note for $700 for the rest of what we owe on the lot & the notes for 2 yrs. with 6% int. per annum & all so cost me $25 to get what they call a garantee polisy from the title & trust Co. which garantees that the title is OK & they cant no body take the lot a way from me un less I dont pay up the bal. which of corse I will pay up as soon is posable.
>
> (001-016)
