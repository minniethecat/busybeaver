Alec Welsh is a named friend of Fred Gross who appears in Story 06 (The Swift Six). His casual invitation to the Chicago automobile show sets in motion the entire plot — Fred sees the Swift Six, becomes obsessed, and the cascading financial and social consequences follow.

## Role in The Swift Six

Welsh takes Fred to the auto show, where Fred is captivated by the Swift Six automobile. Without this outing, Fred would not have seen the car, would not have purchased it, and the story's chain of events — the purchase financed by Grace's father's $600, the tire incidents, the theft, the final humiliation — would not have occurred.

Fred's casual description of the outing underscores Welsh's role as an unwitting catalyst:

> Tomorrow Im going up to the Coliseum to the automobile show and Id rather lay a round headquarters but I promised Alec Welsh Id go a long with him.
>
> (006-020)

## Character Function

Alec Welsh functions as the plot catalyst: a friend whose innocent social invitation produces disproportionate consequences. His role is small but structurally essential — he is the butterfly whose wing-flap sets off the hurricane of Fred's automotive misadventure.
