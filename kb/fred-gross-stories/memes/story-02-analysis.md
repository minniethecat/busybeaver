# Welcome to Our City

The second Fred Gross story introduces the Hamiltons as new neighbors and establishes the practical joke as the primary currency of suburban social interaction.

**Author:** Ring Lardner (as Fred Gross)
**Published:** 1915
**Story Number:** 2

## Synopsis

New neighbors — the Hamiltons — arrive in Allison, and the community greets them with a practical joke. Fred and Grace participate in the welcome while navigating the social obligations of established residents toward newcomers. The story establishes the neighborly dynamics that will escalate into conflict in subsequent stories.

## Plot Summary

The Hamiltons purchase a home in Allison and are immediately subjected to a prank — a test of whether they can take the humor that defines the community's social interactions. Grace introduces herself and Fred to the newcomers (002-027), explaining the joke. Fred reports his letter-writing difficulties (002-045) and worries about responses to his correspondence (002-050). The Carrys host social gatherings and provide childcare (002-038), while Grace navigates the social landscape through calls and visits.

> The Hamiltons has got a swell big house build out of stones or concrete blocks & they probly got a bbl. of money but you know the old saying Charley all men was crated free and wear just as good is them even if we got all my pay for the next 30 yrs. spent all ready.
>
> (002-019)

## Style & Form

The epistolary form captures the fragmentary nature of community gossip — events reported, reinterpreted, and re-reported across multiple letters. The practical joke serves as both plot and metaphor.
