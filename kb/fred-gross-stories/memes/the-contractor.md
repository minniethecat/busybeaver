The Contractor is an unnamed figure in Story 01 (Own Your Own Home) who wins Fred Gross's bungalow construction job with a low bid, then abandons the project partway through, leaving Fred with an unfinished house and mounting costs.

## Role in the Story

Fred solicits bids for the construction of his bungalow at 489 Roberson Ave. The Contractor submits the lowest bid and is awarded the job. Once work begins, however, the Contractor proves unreliable — cutting corners, missing deadlines, and ultimately quitting the job before completion. Fred is forced to find replacement labor at higher cost, exacerbating the budget overruns that define the story's financial arc.

## The Contractor Default Event

The Contractor's abandonment constitutes a key plot event (see [[contractor-default]]). Fred's inability to enforce the contract — he lacks a formal written agreement and is repeatedly told "you can't make a contractor finish a job" — exposes the vulnerability of the amateur homeowner in an unregulated construction market.

## Narrative Function

Alongside the Architect and the lumber company, the Contractor represents the systemic failures Fred encounters in his pursuit of homeownership. Each professional relationship dissolves into incompetence or abandonment, satirizing the gap between middle-class aspiration and the realities of the building trade.
