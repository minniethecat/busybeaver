# Mrs. Carry

**Mrs. Carry** is the wife of [[Mr-Carry|Mr. Carry]], the grocer of [[allison-illinois|Allison]], and a close friend of [[Grace-Gross|Grace Gross]]. She appears in [[story-02-welcome-to-our-city|Welcome to Our City]] and [[story-03-the-last-laugh|The Last Laugh]], serving as a key figure in Grace's social integration into the village.

## Friendship with Grace Gross

Mrs. Carry quickly becomes one of Grace's closest allies in Allison. During an early shopping trip, the two women connect:

> "Grace & Mrs. Carry had a nice long talk" (002-029)

Mrs. Carry offers Grace practical advice about navigating village commerce, telling her "next time Grace wants to…" (002-031). Fred observes that the friendship is genuine: "her and Mrs. Carry is pretty good frends" (002-038).

## Social Role

Mrs. Carry occupies a central position in Allison's social fabric as the grocer's wife, connected to both the merchant class and the broader village society. She and [[Mr-Carry|Mr. Carry]] socialize with [[Mr-Carpenter|Mr.]] and [[mrs-carpenter|Mrs. Carpenter]] (002-029), and she serves as a conduit for social information.

When Grace organizes social calls, Fred notes that Mrs. Carry is instrumental: Grace "had called up Mrs. Carry & told her all about the dance" (002-092).

## The Dance Incident

At a social dance, Mrs. Carry's behavior becomes a point of friction. Fred recounts:

> "1st thing you know him & Mrs. Carry went out on the walk & begin …" (003-037)

This incident at the Curtis party leads to tension between the Carrys and the Grosses, with [[Mr-Carry|Mr. Carry]] later asking Fred "how did I in joy my self" in a pointed manner (003-013).

## Childcare and Domestic Help

Mrs. Carry is frequently called upon to assist with the Gross children. When the Grosses attend a dance, Fred suggests "she should ask Mrs. Carry to take care of them" (002-066), and Grace plans to "have Mrs. Carry come over & stay with the childern" (002-070).

However, the relationship is not without strain. After a disagreement, Fred complains that "Mrs. Carry aint going to take care of" the children (002-072), and remarks bitterly that "If Mrs. Carry had of been decent we might of…" (002-071).
