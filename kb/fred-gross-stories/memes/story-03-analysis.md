# The Last Laugh

The third Fred Gross story traces the escalation of the practical joke competition between Fred Gross and Mr. Hamilton, exploring the thin line between humor and hostility.

**Author:** Ring Lardner (as Fred Gross)
**Published:** 1916
**Story Number:** 3

## Synopsis

The practical joke war between Fred and Hamilton intensifies. What began as neighborly ribbing in Story 2 becomes a competition for social dominance, with each man seeking the definitive "last laugh." Fred's narration reveals both his eagerness to win and his growing discomfort with the conflict's escalation.

## Plot Summary

Fred and Grace host social gatherings, providing "my frends enough to eat and drink when they come to see me" (003-010). A confrontation on the train with Martin (003-029) adds another layer to the social dynamics. Fred's boast that what he "said done the business" (003-029) reveals his satisfaction at scoring points, even as the humor grows increasingly sharp. The story cycles through jokes, retaliations, and the community's shifting allegiances.

> I guess when we put this over them Hamiltons & Carpenters will wisht they act it a little more frendily and hadent been so swelled on them self.
>
> (003-024)

## Themes

- **Humor vs. Hostility** — the practical joke as social weapon
- **Competition** — status rivalry conducted through humor
- **Community Judgment** — neighbors as audience and jury

## Style & Form

Lardner uses the accumulating structure of the letters to show escalation: each letter adds a new joke, a new retaliation, a new grievance, building tension through repetition and variation.
