# The Handshake

The handshake is the physical token of agreement in Fred Gross's world — the gesture that transforms a conversation into a commitment, a negotiation into a deal. Fred shakes hands with the real estate agent, the contractor, the architect, the bank officer, and each handshake represents a moment of trust that will later be tested.

## The Ritual

The handshake in 1915 was the standard form of sealing an agreement — more binding than a verbal promise, less formal than a written contract. It was a gesture of masculine equality, performed between men who acknowledged each other as peers. For Fred, the handshake is a moment of social recognition — proof that he is being taken seriously as a man of business.

## The Betrayal

Fred's handshakes are often betrayed. The real estate agent shakes his hand and then delivers a tax bill that contradicts his promises. The contractor shakes his hand and then produces change orders that increase the cost. The handshake, which Fred experiences as a bond of honor, is experienced by his counterparts as a preliminary to the real negotiation. Fred's faith in the handshake is another instance of his innocence.

## The Symbol

The handshake symbolizes the personal economy that Fred believes he inhabits — a world where a man's word is his bond, where agreements are honored, where trust is reciprocated. The stories document the erosion of this economy, the replacement of personal trust with contractual obligation, of the handshake with the fine print. Fred is a man of the handshake in a world of contracts, and his handshake is continually overridden by the documents he signs without fully reading.
