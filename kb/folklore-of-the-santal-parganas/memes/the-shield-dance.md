The shield and sword dance is a traditional Santal performance event that appears in the folklore as a setting for supernatural intervention. It is at this dance that witches are said to cast spells, transforming a communal celebration into an occasion of vulnerability and danger.

## The Dance

The shield and sword dance involves performers wielding shields and swords in a coordinated display. The dance is mentioned in connection with witchcraft — it provides the public gathering where witches can access their victims. The dance's martial symbolism (shields, swords) contrasts with the invisible nature of the witch's attack.

## Supernatural Vulnerability

At the shield and sword dance, witches once cast a spell on a youth until his clothes fell off him in shreds and he was ashamed to dance (176-006). The public humiliation — clothes disintegrating during a performance before the community — represents the witch's power to destroy social standing as well as physical well-being. The dance, meant to display skill and honour, becomes the occasion for its opposite.
