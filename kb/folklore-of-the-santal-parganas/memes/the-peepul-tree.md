The peepul tree (Ficus religiosa) appears in Santal folklore as a ritual site for bonga exorcism. It is the tree into which nails are driven with uttered vows during a ceremony to fix a troublesome bonga in place.

## Ritual Use

In the exorcism described in chapter 159, the villagers sacrificed rams and, uttering vows over nails, drove them into the peepul tree (159-005). The nails serve to pin the bonga — to fix it in the tree so that it can no longer trouble the village. The peepul tree, sacred across South Asian religious traditions, serves here specifically as a container for contained spiritual danger.
