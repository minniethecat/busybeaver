Champa appears in Santal tradition as a place of origin — the region where the Santals once lived before their migration to the Santal Parganas. It is remembered as a time when the Kiskus were their kings and Santal religious practice was simpler and more devout.

## Historical Memory

When the Santals lived in Champa and the Kiskus were their kings, the Santals were very simple and religious and only worshipped Thakur (160-003). This reference preserves a memory of Santal history before the migration to their current homeland — a time of political autonomy under Kiskus kings and religious purity focused on Thakur worship.

## Significance

Champa functions in Santal tradition as a point of origin and contrast. The simplicity and devotion of the Champa period is implicitly contrasted with the more complex religious landscape of the present, where bongas, witchcraft, and the influence of Hindu caste society have complicated Santal spiritual life. The reference to Champa is brief but carries considerable historical and cultural weight.
