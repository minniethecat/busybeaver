The ojha is the traditional Santal healer and exorcist — a figure who operates at the boundary between medicine and ritual, treating illnesses believed to be caused by witchcraft or bonga malevolence. The ojha is the witch's counterpart and antagonist in the Santal supernatural economy.

## Role in the Community

The ojha's function is to diagnose and cure supernatural illness. When a person falls ill, the ojha is called to determine whether the cause is witchcraft, a displeased bonga, or natural disease. Treatment may involve herbal remedies, ritual actions, or negotiation with the supernatural agent believed to be responsible.

## Relationship with Witches

The boundary between witch and ojha is not always clear. In one striking account, villagers who discovered a woman's witchcraft secret told her that she must henceforth be their ojha and cure their diseases (183-004). The same knowledge that enables a witch to cause illness can, when redirected, enable an ojha to cure it. This suggests that witchcraft and healing draw from a common body of esoteric knowledge — the moral valence determined by how it is used rather than what it is.
