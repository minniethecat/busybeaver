The jungle is the dominant non-human space in Santal folklore — the wild forest that surrounds villages, harbours predators and bongas, and serves as the setting for encounters that cross the boundary between the human and spirit worlds.

## Description

The jungle is a dense forest that begins where the village ends. It is the habitat of tigers, leopards, elephants, and snakes. It is also where bongas dwell, where witches lead tigers after initiation, and where young men tend buffaloes at the boundary between domestic and wild space.

## In Stories

The jungle is the primary setting for predator encounters. A tigress and a cow once grazed in a dense jungle, both with young (111-001). Man-eating leopards make their depredations from the jungle, prompting great hunts (107-001). The jungle road is where the merchant encounters the fleeing leopard (107-002).

The jungle is also the space of bonga encounter. Young men tending buffaloes at the jungle's edge attract bonga girls (148-002). The bonga household, hidden within the jungle, contains snakes and tigers as domestic furniture — the wild tamed within the supernatural domestic space.
