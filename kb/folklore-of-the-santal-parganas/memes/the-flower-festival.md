The Flower festival is one of the two major Santal festivals mentioned alongside Sohrai in Bompas's collection. Both festivals serve the same essential ritual function: sacrifices are offered to ancestral bongas, maintaining the relationship between the living and the dead.

## Practice

When grown-up people die they become ancestral bongas, and sacrifices are offered to them at the Flower and Sohrai festivals (166-001). The Flower festival is paired with Sohrai as one of the two annual occasions when the ancestors receive formal ritual attention. While Sohrai is associated with the harvest and the sacrifice of pigs and goats, the Flower festival appears to be associated with the flowering season — a complementary moment in the agricultural calendar.

## Significance

The pairing of the Flower and Sohrai festivals establishes a twice-yearly rhythm of ancestral observance. The ancestors are not forgotten between festivals, but these two occasions provide the formal, communal structure for honouring them. The festivals thus maintain the temporal architecture of Santal religious life — the year divided by moments of intensified contact between the living and the dead.
