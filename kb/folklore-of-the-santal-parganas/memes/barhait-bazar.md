Barhait Bazar is a market town in the Santal Parganas, mentioned in the folklore as a geographic reference point for the village of Sarjomghutu. It represents the commercial and administrative nodes of the Santal landscape.

## Description

Barhait Bazar is a market town — bazar indicating its function as a trading centre. Sarjomghutu is described as being about four miles from Barhait Bazar on the banks of the Badi river (149-001). This spatial relationship — village at a distance from market town — reflects the settlement pattern of the Santal Parganas, where villages are scattered through the jungle at varying distances from commercial centres.

## In Stories

Barhait Bazar itself is not the setting for any story — it appears only as a point of reference. Its function in the narrative is to locate Sarjomghutu in real geographic space, lending credibility to the account of the bonga headman. The use of real place names — Barhait Bazar, Badi river, Silingi — is a consistent feature of the bonga narratives, anchoring supernatural events in a mappable landscape.
