The ant appears in Santal animal tales as the embodiment of the principle that small size does not mean weakness. The story of the Elephant and the Ants (chapter 115) is the classic expression of this theme.

## Description

Ants are tiny insects that live in colonies. In the Santal landscape, they build nests in the ground and in trees. In the tale, their smallness is their weapon — they can enter spaces too small for larger creatures to defend.

## Behaviour

The ants' defining trait is collective persistence. When an elephant wrongs them, the ants do not fight individually — they swarm, entering the elephant's ears where his great strength cannot reach them. The elephant's enormous power is useless against adversaries too small to strike.

## In Stories

The Elephant and the Ants (chapter 115) belongs to a worldwide folk tradition of the mighty brought low by the humble. In the Santal version, the ants' victory is a triumph of numbers and persistence over individual strength — a lesson with clear social implications for a tribal community often outmatched in physical power by dominant groups.
