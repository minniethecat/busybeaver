The promise in Santal folklore is a speech act with binding force, not merely a statement of intent. It seals betrothals (100-004), secures release from captivity (100-003), and establishes temporary truces between predator and prey (109-002). When broken, the promise does not simply evaporate — it summons trickster justice, supernatural intervention, or social shame. In the Santal narrative world, the spoken word is a deed.

## In Santal Belief

A promise made under duress is still a promise. The jackal, extracting a written pledge from the grasping Raja to restore a poor man's cow, treats the Raja's signature as a document of enforceable law (099-007). The Santal storyteller draws no line between coerced and voluntary promises — the obligation is inherent in the utterance. This extends to courtship: a prince, imprisoned by his father, promises to marry whomever his father chooses, and exchanges rings with the chosen bride to seal the pact (100-003, 100-004).

Promises also structure daily life. A bridegroom promises to hurry with his cooking before a journey (104-003). A mother extracts a promise from her son before sending him into the world (105-006). These small pledges carry the same narrative weight as grand oaths — they are the threads from which the moral fabric is woven.

## Broken Promises and Consequences

Not all promises are kept. The egret promises to catch the jackal a fine fish, then eats it himself — launching a revenge plot that spans multiple episodes (120-002). The promise-breaking does not slip by unnoticed; it is the pivot on which the entire story turns. When a wife promises not to add salt to a dish and does so anyway, the domestic rebellion signals a deeper defiance of household order (105-001).

The money-lender, extracting a promise from a Santal to teach him the secret of wealth, finds that the promise is itself the trap — the Santal's lesson is a parable about the emptiness of grasping (126-001). In this architecture, promises are not neutral vessels of information but instruments that reveal the character of both promiser and promisee.
