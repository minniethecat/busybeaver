Darbar village, located near Silingi in the Santal Parganas, is the setting for the most detailed bonga marriage narrative in Bompas's collection. The village is named as the home of two men who married bongas, grounding the supernatural narrative in a specific, real-world location.

## Description

Darbar is a Santal village near Silingi. It is characterized in the story as a place where the boundary between the human and spirit worlds is unusually thin — two of its residents have married bongas, and their experiences are recounted as local knowledge rather than distant legend.

## In Stories

The account of marriage with bongas in chapter 148 is anchored to Darbar village: In Darbar village near Silingi there are two men who married bonga (148-002). One of these men, a flute-player, attracted a bonga girl while tending buffaloes. The specificity of the location — Darbar village, near Silingi — gives the supernatural narrative the texture of local reportage. This is not a story set in an imaginary kingdom but an account of events said to have happened to named residents of a real village.
