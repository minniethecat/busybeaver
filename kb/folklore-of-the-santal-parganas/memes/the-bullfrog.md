The bullfrog appears in Santal folklore in a contest with a crab. Its loud voice and pride make it a foil for the quieter but more persistent crab.

## Description

The bullfrog is a large amphibian known for its loud call. In the Santal landscape, bullfrogs inhabit ponds and water bodies. In the tale, the bullfrog's voice is its defining characteristic — and its weakness.

## Behaviour

The bullfrog's pride in its voice leads it into a contest with the crab. The story of the Bullfrog and the Crab (chapter 118) follows the classic folk pattern of the proud character brought low by a humbler opponent. The bullfrog's loudness — initially an advantage — becomes the source of its undoing.

## In Stories

The Bullfrog and the Crab (chapter 118) is a brief animal fable about pride, competition, and the unexpected strengths of seemingly weak opponents. It belongs to the same tradition as the Elephant and the Ants — the small overcoming the large through persistence and cleverness.
