[020-001] BOOK XX.

[020-002] ARGUMENT.

[020-003] While [[odysseus|Ulysses]] lies in the vestibule of the palace, he is witness to the

[020-004] disorders of the women. [[athena|Minerva]] comforts him, and casts him asleep. At

[020-005] his waking he desires a favourable sign from [[zeus|Jupiter]], which is granted.

[020-006] The feast of [[apollo|Apollo]] is celebrated by the people, and the suitors

[020-007] banquet in the palace. [[telemachus|Telemachus]] exerts his authority amongst them;

[020-008] notwithstanding which, Ulysses is insulted by Caesippus, and the rest

[020-009] continue in their excesses. Strange prodigies are seen by [[theoclymenus|Theoclymenus]],

[020-010] the augur, who explains them to the destruction of the wooers.

[020-011] An ample hide devine Ulysses spread.

[020-012] And form’d of fleecy skins his humble bed

[020-013] (The remnants of the spoil the suitor-crowd

[020-014] In festival devour’d, and victims vow’d).

[020-015] Then o’er the chief, [[eurynome|Eurynome]] the chaste

[020-016] With duteous care a downy carpet cast:

[020-017] With dire revenge his thoughtful bosom glows,

[020-018] And, ruminating wrath, he scorns repose.

[020-019] As thus pavilion’d in the porch he lay,

[020-020] Scenes of lewd loves his wakeful eyes survey,

[020-021] Whilst to nocturnal joys impure repair,

[020-022] With wanton glee, the prostituted fair.

[020-023] His heart with rage this new dishonour stung,

[020-024] Wavering his thoughts in dubious balance hung:

[020-025] Or instant should he quench the guilty flame

[020-026] With their own blood, and intercept the shame:

[020-027] Or to their lust indulge a last embrace,

[020-028] And let the peers consummate the disgrace

[020-029] Round his swoln heart the murmurous fury rolls,

[020-030] As o’er her young the mother-mastiff growls,

[020-031] And bays the stranger groom: so wrath compress’d,

[020-032] Recoiling, mutter’d thunder in his breast.

[020-033] “Poor suffering heart! (he cried,) support the pain

[020-034] Of wounded honour, and thy rage restrain.

[020-035] Not fiercer woes thy fortitude could foil,

[020-036] When the brave partners of thy ten years’ toil

[020-037] Dire Polypheme devour’d; I then was freed

[020-038] By patient prudence from the death decreed.”

## Section 50

[020-039] Thus anchor’d safe on reason’s peaceful coast,

[020-040] Tempests of wrath his soul no longer toss’d;

[020-041] Restless his body rolls, to rage resign’d

[020-042] As one who long with pale-eyed famine pined,

[020-043] The savoury cates on glowing embers cast

[020-044] Incessant turns, impatient for repast

[020-045] Ulysses so, from side to side-devolved,

[020-046] In self-debate the suitor’s doom resolved

[020-047] When in the form of mortal nymph array’d,

[020-048] From heaven descends the Jove-born martial maid;

[020-049] And hovering o’er his head in view confess’d,

[020-050] The goddess thus her favourite care address’d:

[020-051] “O thou, of mortals most inured to woes!

[020-052] Why roll those eyes unfriended of repose?

[020-053] Beneath thy palace-roof forget thy care;

[020-054] Bless’d in thy queen! bless’d in thy blooming heir!

[020-055] Whom, to the gods when suppliant fathers bow

[020-056] They name the standard of their dearest vow.”

[020-057] “Just is thy kind reproach (the chief rejoin’d),

[020-058] Deeds full of fate distract my various mind,

[020-059] In contemplation wrapp’d. This hostile crew

[020-060] What single arm hath prowess to subdue?

[020-061] Or if, by Jove’s and thy auxiliar aid,

[020-062] They’re doom’d to bleed; O say, celestial maid!

[020-063] Where shall Ulysses shun, or how sustain

[020-064] Nations embattled to revenge the slain?”

[020-065] “Oh impotence of faith! (Minerva cries,)

[020-066] If man on frail unknowing man relies,

[020-067] Doubt you the gods? Lo, Pallas self descends,

[020-068] Inspires thy counsels, and thy toils attends.

[020-069] In me affianced, fortify thy breast,

[020-070] Though myriads leagued thy rightful claim contest

[020-071] My sure divinity shall bear the shield,

[020-072] And edge thy sword to reap the glorious field.

[020-073] Now, pay the debt to craving nature due,

[020-074] Her faded powers with balmy rest renew.”

[020-075] She ceased, ambrosial slumbers seal his eyes;

[020-076] Her care dissolves in visionary joys

[020-077] The goddess, pleased, regains her natal skies.

[020-078] Not so the queen; the downy bands of sleep

[020-079] By grief relax’d she waked again to weep:

[020-080] A gloomy pause ensued of dumb despair;

[020-081] Then thus her fate invoked, with fervent prayer

[020-082] “Diana! speed thy deathful ebon dart,

[020-083] And cure the pangs of this convulsive heart.

[020-084] Snatch me, ye whirlwinds! far from human race,

[020-085] Toss’d through the void illimitable space

[020-086] Or if dismounted from the rapid cloud,

[020-087] Me with his whelming wave let [[oceanus|Ocean]] shroud!

[020-088] So, Pandarus, thy hopes, three orphan fair,

[020-089] Were doom’d to wander through the devious air;

[020-090] Thyself untimely, and thy consort died,

[020-091] But four celestials both your cares supplied.

[020-092] Venus in tender delicacy rears

[020-093] With honey, milk, and wine their infant years;

[020-094] Imperial [[hera|Juno]] to their youth assigned

[020-095] A form majestic, and sagacious mind;

[020-096] With shapely growth Diana graced their bloom;

[020-097] And Pallas taught the texture of the loom.

[020-098] But whilst, to learn their lots in nuptial love,

[020-099] Bright Cytherea sought the bower of Jove

[020-100] (The God supreme, to whose eternal eye

[020-101] The registers of fate expanded lie);

[020-102] Wing’d Harpies snatch the unguarded charge away,

[020-103] And to the Furies bore a grateful prey.

[020-104] Be such my lot! Or thou, Diana, speed

[020-105] Thy shaft, and send me joyful to the dead;

[020-106] To seek my lord among the warrior train,

[020-107] Ere second vows my bridal faith profane.

[020-108] When woes the waking sense alone assail,

[020-109] Whilst Night extends her soft oblivious veil,

[020-110] Of other wretches’ care the torture ends;

[020-111] No truce the warfare of my heart suspends!

[020-112] The night renews the day distracting theme,

[020-113] And airy terrors sable every dream.

[020-114] The last alone a kind illusion wrought,

[020-115] And to my bed my loved Ulysses brought,

[020-116] In manly bloom, and each majestic grace,

[020-117] As when for [[troy|Troy]] he left my fond embrace;

[020-118] Such raptures in my beating bosom rise,

[020-119] I deem it sure a vision of the skies.”

[020-120] Thus, whilst Aurora mounts her purple throne,

[020-121] In audible laments she breathes her moan;

[020-122] The sounds assault Ulysses’ wakeful ear;

[020-123] Misjudging of the cause, a sudden fear

[020-124] Of his arrival known, the chief alarms;

[020-125] He thinks the queen is rushing to his arms.

[020-126] Upspringing from his couch, with active haste

[020-127] The fleece and carpet in the dome he placed

[020-128] (The hide, without, imbibed the morning air);

[020-129] And thus the gods invoked with ardent prayer:

[020-130] “Jove, and eternal thrones! with heaven to friend,

[020-131] If the long series of my woes shall end;

[020-132] Of human race now rising from repose,

[020-133] Let one a blissful omen here disclose;

[020-134] And, to confirm my faith, propitious Jove!

[020-135] Vouchsafe the sanction of a sign above.”

[020-136] Whilst lowly thus the chief adoring bows,

[020-137] The pitying god his guardian aid avows.

[020-138] Loud from a sapphire sky his thunder sounds;

[020-139] With springing hope the hero’s heart rebounds.

[020-140] Soon, with consummate joy to crown his prayer,

[020-141] An omen’d voice invades his ravish’d ear.

[020-142] Beneath a pile that close the dome adjoin’d,

[020-143] Twelve female slaves the gift of Ceres grind;

[020-144] Task’d for the royal board to bolt the bran

[020-145] From the pure flour (the growth and strength of man)

[020-146] Discharging to the day the labour due,

[020-147] Now early to repose the rest withdrew;

[020-148] One maid unequal to the task assign’d,

[020-149] Still turn’d the toilsome mill with anxious mind;

[020-150] And thus in bitterness of soul divined:

[020-151] “Father of gods and men, whose thunders roll

[020-152] O’er the cerulean vault, and shake the pole:

[020-153] Whoe’er from Heaven has gain’d this rare ostent

[020-154] (Of granted vows a certain signal sent),

[020-155] In this blest moment of accepted prayer,

[020-156] Piteous, regard a wretch consumed with care!

[020-157] Instant, O Jove! confound the suitor-train,

[020-158] For whom o’ertoil’d I grind the golden grain:

[020-159] Far from this dome the lewd devourers cast,

[020-160] And be this festival decreed their last!”

[020-161] Big with their doom denounced in earth and sky,

[020-162] Ulysses’ heart dilates with secret joy.

[020-163] Meantime the menial train with unctious wood

[020-164] Heap’d high the genial hearth, Vulcanian food:

[020-165] When, early dress’d, advanced the royal heir;

[020-166] With manly grasp he waved a martial spear;

[020-167] A radiant sabre graced his purple zone,

[020-168] And on his foot the golden sandal shone.

[020-169] His steps impetuous to the portal press’d;

[020-170] And Euryclea thus he there address’d:

[020-171] “Say thou to whom my youth its nurture owes,

[020-172] Was care for due refection and repose

[020-173] Bestow’d the stranger-guest? Or waits he grieved,

[020-174] His age not honour’d, nor his wants relieved?

[020-175] Promiscuous grace on all the queen confers

[020-176] (In woes bewilder’d, oft the wisest errs).

[020-177] The wordy vagrant to the dole aspires,

[020-178] And modest worth with noble scorn retires.”

[020-179] She thus: “O cease that ever-honour’d name

[020-180] To blemish now: it ill deserves your blame,

[020-181] A bowl of generous wine sufficed the guest;

[020-182] In vain the queen the night refection press’d;

[020-183] Nor would he court repose in downy state,

[020-184] Unbless’d, abandon’d to the rage of [[moira|Fate]]!

[020-185] A hide beneath the portico was spread,

[020-186] And fleecy skins composed an humble bed;

[020-187] A downy carpet cast with duteous care,

[020-188] Secured him from the keen nocturnal air.”

[020-189] His cornel javelin poised with regal port,

[020-190] To the sage Greeks convened in Themis’ court,

[020-191] Forth-issuing from the dome the prince repair’d;

[020-192] Two dogs of chase, a lion-hearted guard,

[020-193] Behind him sourly stalked. Without delay

[020-194] The dame divides the labour of the day;

[020-195] Thus urging to the toil the menial train;

[020-196] “What marks of luxury the marble stain

[020-197] Its wonted lustre let the floor regain;

[020-198] The seats with purple clothe in order due;

[020-199] And let the abstersive sponge the board renew;

[020-200] Let some refresh the vase’s sullied mould;

[020-201] Some bid the goblets boast their native gold;

[020-202] Some to the spring, with each a jar, repair,

[020-203] And copious waters pure for bathing bear;

[020-204] Dispatch! for soon the suitors will essay

[020-205] The lunar feast-rites to the god of day.”

[020-206] She said: with duteous haste a bevy fair

[020-207] Of twenty virgins to the spring repair;

[020-208] With varied toils the rest adorn the dome.

[020-209] Magnificent, and blithe, the suitors come.

[020-210] Some wield the sounding axe; the dodder’d oaks

[020-211] Divide, obedient to the forceful strokes.

[020-212] Soon from the fount, with each a brimming urn

[020-213] ([[eumaeus|Eumaeus]] in their train), the maids return.

[020-214] Three porkers for the feast, all brawny-chined,

[020-215] He brought; the choicest of the tusky-kind;

[020-216] In lodgments first secure his care he viewed,

[020-217] Then to the king this friendly speech renew’d:

[020-218] “Now say sincere, my guest! the suitor-train

[020-219] Still treat thy worth with lordly dull disdain;

[020-220] Or speaks their deed a bounteous mind humane?”

[020-221] “Some pitying god (Ulysses sad replied)

[020-222] With vollied vengeance blast their towering pride!

[020-223] No conscious blush, no sense of right, restrains

[020-224] The tides of lust that swell the boiling veins;

[020-225] From vice to vice their appetites are toss’d,

[020-226] All cheaply sated at another’s cost!”

[020-227] While thus the chief his woes indignant told,

[020-228] [[melanthius|Melanthius]], master of the bearded fold,

[020-229] The goodliest goats of all the royal herd

[020-230] Spontaneous to the suitors’ feast preferr’d;

[020-231] Two grooms assistant bore the victims bound;

[020-232] With quavering cries the vaulted roofs resound;

[020-233] And to the chief austere aloud began

[020-234] The wretch unfriendly to the race of man:

[020-235] “Here vagrant, still? offensive to my lords!

[020-236] Blows have more energy than airy words;

[020-237] These arguments I’ll use: nor conscious shame,

[020-238] Nor threats, thy bold intrusion will reclaim.

[020-239] On this high feast the meanest vulgar boast

[020-240] A plenteous board! Hence! seek another host!”

[020-241] Rejoinder to the churl the king disdain’d,

[020-242] But shook his head, and rising wrath restrain’d.

[020-243] From Cephalenia ’cross the surgy main

[020-244] Philaetius late arrived, a faithful swain.

[020-245] A steer ungrateful to the bull’s embrace.

[020-246] And goats he brought, the pride of all their race;

[020-247] Imported in a shallop not his own;

[020-248] The dome re-echoed to the mingl’d moan.

[020-249] Straight to the guardian of the bristly kind

[020-250] He thus began, benevolent of mind:

[020-251] “What guest is he, of such majestic air?

[020-252] His lineage and paternal clime declare:

[020-253] Dim through the eclipse of fate, the rays divine

[020-254] Of sovereign state with faded splendour shine.

[020-255] If monarchs by the gods are plunged in woe,

[020-256] To what abyss are we foredoom’d to go!”

[020-257] Then affable he thus the chief address’d,

[020-258] Whilst with pathetic warmth his hand he press’d:

[020-259] “Stranger, may fate a milder aspect show,

[020-260] And spin thy future with a whiter clue!

[020-261] O Jove! for ever deaf to human cries;

[020-262] The tyrant, not the father of the skies!

[020-263] Unpiteous of the race thy will began!

[020-264] The fool of fate, thy manufacture, man,

[020-265] With penury, contempt, repulse, and care,

[020-266] The galling load of life is doom’d to bear.

[020-267] Ulysses from his state a wanderer still,

[020-268] Upbraids thy power, thy wisdom, or thy will!

[020-269] O monarch ever dear!-O man of woe!

[020-270] Fresh flow my tears, and shall for ever flow!

[020-271] Like thee, poor stranger guest, denied his home,

[020-272] Like thee: in rags obscene decreed to roam!

[020-273] Or, haply perish’d on some distant coast,

[020-274] In stygian gloom he glides, a pensive ghost!

[020-275] Oh, grateful for the good his bounty gave,

[020-276] I’ll grieve, till sorrow sink me to the grave!

[020-277] His kind protecting hand my youth preferr’d,

[020-278] The regent of his Cephalenian herd;

[020-279] With vast increase beneath my care it spreads:

[020-280] A stately breed! and blackens far the meads.

[020-281] Constrain’d, the choicest beeves I thence import,

[020-282] To cram these cormorants that crowd his court:

[020-283] Who in partition seek his realm to share;

[020-284] Nor human right nor wrath divine revere,

[020-285] Since here resolved oppressive these reside,

[020-286] Contending doubts my anxious heart divide:

[020-287] Now to some foreign clime inclined to fly,

[020-288] And with the royal herd protection buy;

[020-289] Then, happier thoughts return the nodding scale,

[020-290] Light mounts despair, alternate hopes prevail:

[020-291] In opening prospects of ideal joy,

[020-292] My king returns; the proud usurpers die.”

[020-293] To whom the chief: “In thy capacious mind

[020-294] Since daring zeal with cool debate is join’d,

[020-295] Attend a deed already ripe in fate:

[020-296] Attest, O Jove! the truth I now relate!

[020-297] This sacred truth attest, each genial power,

[020-298] Who bless the board, and guard this friendly bower!

[020-299] Before thou quit the dome (nor long delay)

[020-300] Thy wish produced in act, with pleased survey,

[020-301] Thy wondering eyes shall view: his rightful reign

[020-302] By arms avow’d Ulysses shall regain,

[020-303] And to the shades devote the suitor-train.”

[020-304] “O Jove supreme! (the raptured swain replies,)

[020-305] With deeds consummate soon the promised joys!

[020-306] These aged nerves, with new-born vigour strung,

[020-307] In that blest cause should emulate the young.”

[020-308] Assents Eumaeus to the prayer address’d;

[020-309] And equal ardours fire his loyal breast.

[020-310] Meantime the suitors urge the prince’s fate,

[020-311] And deathful arts employ the dire debate:

[020-312] When in his airy tour, the bird of Jove

[020-313] Truss’d with his sinewy pounce a trembling dove;

[020-314] Sinister to their hope! This omen eyed

[020-315] [[amphinomus|Amphinomus]], who thus presaging cried:

## Section 51

[020-316] “The gods from force and fraud the prince defend;

[020-317] O peers! the sanguinary scheme suspend:

[020-318] Your future thought let sable fate employ;

[020-319] And give the present hour to genial joy.”

[020-320] From council straight the assenting peerage ceased,

[020-321] And in the dome prepared the genial feast.

[020-322] Disrobed, their vests apart in order lay,

[020-323] Then all with speed succinct the victims slay:

[020-324] With sheep and shaggy goats the porkers bled,

[020-325] And the proud steer was on the marble spread.

[020-326] With fire prepared, they deal the morsels round,

[020-327] Wine, rosy-bright, the brimming goblets crown’d,

[020-328] By sage Eumaeus borne; the purple tide

[020-329] Melanthius from an ample jar supplied:

[020-330] High canisters of bread Philaetius placed;

[020-331] And eager all devour the rich repast.

[020-332] Disposed apart, Ulysses shares the treat;

[020-333] A trivet table, and ignobler seat,

[020-334] The prince appoints; but to his sire assigns

[020-335] The tasteful inwards, and nectareous wines.

[020-336] “Partake, my guest (he cried), without control

[020-337] The social feast, and drain the cheering bowl:

[020-338] Dread not the railer’s laugh, nor ruffian’s rage;

[020-339] No vulgar roof protects thy honour’d age;

[020-340] This dome a refuge to thy wrongs shall be,

[020-341] From my great sire too soon devolved to me!

[020-342] Your violence and scorn, ye suitors, cease,

[020-343] Lest arms avenge the violated peace.”

[020-344] Awed by the prince, so haughty, brave, and young,

[020-345] Rage gnaw’d the lip, amazement chain’d the tongue.

[020-346] “Be patient, peers! (at length [[antinous|Antinous]] cries,)

[020-347] The threats of vain imperious youth despise:

[020-348] Would Jove permit the meditated blow,

[020-349] That stream of eloquence should cease to flow.”

[020-350] Without reply vouchsafed, Antinous ceased:

[020-351] Meanwhile the pomp of festival increased:

[020-352] By heralds rank’d; in marshall’d order move

[020-353] The city tribes, to pleased Apollo’s grove:

[020-354] Beneath the verdure of which awful shade,

[020-355] The lunar hecatomb they grateful laid;

[020-356] Partook the sacred feast, and ritual honours paid.

[020-357] But the rich banquet, in the dome prepared

[020-358] (An humble sideboard set) Ulysses shared.

[020-359] Observant of the prince’s high behest,

[020-360] His menial train attend the stranger-guest;

[020-361] Whom Pallas with unpardoning fury fired,

[020-362] By lordly pride and keen reproach inspired.

[020-363] A Samian peer, more studious than the rest

[020-364] Of vice, who teem’d with many a dead-born jest;

[020-365] And urged, for title to a consort queen,

[020-366] Unnumber’d acres arable and green

[020-367] ([[ctesippus|Ctesippus]] named); this lord Ulysses eyed,

[020-368] And thus burst out the imposthumate with pride:

[020-369] “The sentence I propose, ye peers, attend:

[020-370] Since due regard must wait the prince’s friend,

[020-371] Let each a token of esteem bestow:

[020-372] This gift acquits the dear respect I owe;

[020-373] With which he nobly may discharge his seat,

[020-374] And pay the menials for a master’s treat.”

[020-375] He said: and of the steer before him placed,

[020-376] That sinewy fragment at Ulysses cast,

[020-377] Where to the pastern-bone, by nerves combined,

[020-378] The well-horn’d foot indissolubly join’d;

[020-379] Which whizzing high, the wall unseemly sign’d.

[020-380] The chief indignant grins a ghastly smile;

[020-381] [[poine|Revenge]] and scorn within his bosom boil:

[020-382] When thus the prince with pious rage inflamed:

[020-383] “Had not the inglorious wound thy malice aim’d

[020-384] Fall’n guiltless of the mark, my certain spear

[020-385] Had made thee buy the brutal triumph dear:

[020-386] Nor should thy sire a queen his daughter boast;

[020-387] The suitor, now, had vanish’d in a ghost:

[020-388] No more, ye lewd compeers, with lawless power

[020-389] Invade my dome, my herds and flocks devour:

[020-390] For genuine worth, of age mature to know,

[020-391] My grape shall redden, and my harvest grow

[020-392] Or, if each other’s wrongs ye still support,

[020-393] With rapes and riot to profane my court;

[020-394] What single arm with numbers can contend?

[020-395] On me let all your lifted swords descend,

[020-396] And with my life such vile dishonours end.”

[020-397] A long cessation of discourse ensued,

[020-398] By gentler Agelaus thus renew’d:

[020-399] “A just reproof, ye peers! your rage restrain

[020-400] From the protected guest, and menial train:

[020-401] And, prince! to stop the source of future ill,

[020-402] Assent yourself, and gain the royal will.

[020-403] Whilst hope prevail’d to see your sire restored,

[020-404] Of right the queen refused a second lord:

[020-405] But who so vain of faith, so blind to fate,

[020-406] To think he still survives to claim the state?

[020-407] Now press the sovereign dame with warm desire

[020-408] To wed, as wealth or worth her choice inspire:

[020-409] The lord selected to the nuptial joys

[020-410] Far hence will lead the long-contested prize:

[020-411] Whilst in paternal pomp with plenty bless’d,

[020-412] You reign, of this imperial dome possess’d.”

[020-413] Sage and serene Telemachus replies:

[020-414] “By him at whose behest the thunder flies,

[020-415] And by the name on earth I most revere,

[020-416] By great Ulysses and his woes I swear!

[020-417] (Who never must review his dear domain;

[020-418] Enroll’d, perhaps, in Pluto’s dreary train),

[020-419] Whene’er her choice the royal dame avows,

[020-420] My bridal gifts shall load the future spouse:

[020-421] But from this dome my parent queen to chase!

[020-422] From me, ye gods! avert such dire disgrace.”

[020-423] But Pallas clouds with intellectual gloom

[020-424] The suitors’ souls, insensate of their doom!

[020-425] A mirthful frenzy seized the fated crowd;

[020-426] The roofs resound with causeless laughter loud;

[020-427] Floating in gore, portentous to survey!

[020-428] In each discolour’d vase the viands lay;

[020-429] Then down each cheek the tears spontaneous flow

[020-430] And sudden sighs precede approaching woe.

[020-431] In vision wrapp’d, the Hyperesian seer

[020-432] Uprose, and thus divined the vengeance near:

[020-433] “O race to death devote! with Stygian shade

[020-434] Each destin’d peer impending fates invade;

[020-435] With tears your wan distorted cheeks are drown’d;

[020-436] With sanguine drops the walls are rubied round:

[020-437] Thick swarms the spacious hall with howling ghosts,

[020-438] To people Orcus, and the burning coasts!

[020-439] Nor gives the sun his golden orb to roll,

[020-440] But universal night usurps the pole!”

[020-441] Yet warn’d in vain, with laughter loud elate

[020-442] The peers reproach the sure divine of Fate;

[020-443] And thus [[eurymachus|Eurymachus]]: “The dotard’s mind

[020-444] To every sense is lost, to reason blind;

[020-445] Swift from the dome conduct the slave away;

[020-446] Let him in open air behold the day.”

[020-447] “Tax not (the heaven-illumined seer rejoin’d)

[020-448] Of rage, or folly, my prophetic mind,

[020-449] No clouds of error dim the ethereal rays,

[020-450] Her equal power each faithful sense obeys.

[020-451] Unguided hence my trembling steps I bend,

[020-452] Far hence, before yon hovering deaths descend;

[020-453] Lest the ripe harvest of revenge begun,

[020-454] I share the doom ye suitors cannot shun.”

[020-455] This said, to sage Piraeus sped the seer,

[020-456] His honour’d host, a welcome inmate there.

[020-457] O’er the protracted feast the suitors sit,

[020-458] And aim to wound the prince with pointless wit:

[020-459] Cries one, with scornful leer and mimic voice,

[020-460] “Thy charity we praise, but not thy choice;

[020-461] Why such profusion of indulgence shown

[020-462] To this poor, timorous, toil-detesting drone?

[020-463] That others feeds on planetary schemes,

[020-464] And pays his host with hideous noon-day dreams.

[020-465] But, prince! for once at least believe a friend;

[020-466] To some Sicilian mart these courtiers send,

[020-467] Where, if they yield their freight across the main,

[020-468] Dear sell the slaves! demand no greater gain.”

[020-469] Thus jovial they; but nought the prince replies;

[020-470] Full on his sire he roll’d his ardent eyes:

[020-471] Impatient straight to flesh his virgin-sword;

[020-472] From the wise chief he waits the deathful word.

[020-473] Nigh in her bright alcove, the pensive queen

[020-474] To see the circle sate, of all unseen.

[020-475] Sated at length they rise, and bid prepare

[020-476] An eve-repast, with equal cost and care:

[020-477] But vengeful Pallas, with preventing speed,

[020-478] A feast proportion’d to their crimes decreed;

[020-479] A feast of death, the feasters doom’d to bleed!
