[013-001] BOOK XIII.

[013-002] ARGUMENT.

[013-003] THE ARRIVAL OF ULYSSES IN ITHACA.

[013-004] [[odysseus|Ulysses]] takes his leave of [[alcinous|Alcinous]] and [[arete|Arete]], and embarks in the

[013-005] evening. Next morning the ship arrives at [[ithaca|Ithaca]]; where the sailors, as

[013-006] Ulysses is yet sleeping, lay him on the shore with all his treasures.

[013-007] On their return, [[poseidon|Neptune]] changes their ship into a rock. In the

[013-008] meantime Ulysses, awaking, knows not his native Ithaca, by reason of a

[013-009] mist which Pallas had cast around him. He breaks into loud

[013-010] lamentations; till the goddess appearing to him in the form of a

[013-011] shepherd, discovers the country to him, and points out the particular

[013-012] places. He then tells a feigned story of his adventures, upon which she

[013-013] manifests herself, and they consult together of the measures to be

[013-014] taken to destroy the suitors. To conceal his return, and disguise his

[013-015] person the more effectually, she changes him into the figure of an old

[013-016] beggar.

[013-017] He ceased; but left so pleasing on their ear

[013-018] His voice, that listening still they seem’d to hear.

[013-019] A pause of silence hush’d the shady rooms:

[013-020] The grateful conference then the king resumes:

[013-021] “Whatever toils the great Ulysses pass’d,

[013-022] Beneath this happy roof they end at last;

[013-023] No longer now from shore to shore to roam,

[013-024] Smooth seas and gentle winds invite him home.

[013-025] But hear me, princes! whom these walls inclose,

[013-026] For whom my chanter sings: and goblet flows

[013-027] With wine unmix’d (an honour due to age,

[013-028] To cheer the grave, and warm the poet’s rage);

[013-029] Though labour’d gold and many a dazzling vest

[013-030] Lie heap’d already for our godlike guest;

[013-031] Without new treasures let him not remove,

[013-032] Large, and expressive of the public love:

[013-033] Each peer a tripod, each a vase bestow,

[013-034] A general tribute, which the state shall owe.”

[013-035] This sentence pleased: then all their steps address’d

[013-036] To separate mansions, and retired to rest.

[013-037] Now did the rosy-finger’d morn arise,

[013-038] And shed her sacred light along the skies.

[013-039] Down to the haven and the ships in haste

[013-040] They bore the treasures, and in safety placed.

[013-041] The king himself the vases ranged with care;

[013-042] Then bade his followers to the feast prepare.

[013-043] A victim ox beneath the sacred hand

[013-044] Of great Alcinous falls, and stains the sand.

[013-045] To [[zeus|Jove]] the Eternal (power above all powers!

[013-046] Who wings the winds, and darkens heaven with showers)

[013-047] The flames ascend: till evening they prolong

[013-048] The rites, more sacred made by heavenly song;

[013-049] For in the midst, with public honours graced,

[013-050] Thy lyre divine, [[demodocus|Demodocus]]! was placed.

[013-051] All, but Ulysses, heard with fix’d delight;

[013-052] He sate, and eyed the sun, and wish’d the night;

[013-053] Slow seem’d the sun to move, the hours to roll,

[013-054] His native home deep-imaged in his soul.

[013-055] As the tired ploughman, spent with stubborn toil,

[013-056] Whose oxen long have torn the furrow’d soil,

[013-057] Sees with delight the sun’s declining ray,

[013-058] When home with feeble knees he bends his way

[013-059] To late repast (the day’s hard labour done);

[013-060] So to Ulysses welcome set the sun;

[013-061] Then instant to Alcinous and the rest

[013-062] (The Scherian states) he turn’d, and thus address’d:

[013-063] “O thou, the first in merit and command!

[013-064] And you the peers and princes of the land!

[013-065] May every joy be yours! nor this the least,

[013-066] When due libation shall have crown’d the feast,

[013-067] Safe to my home to send your happy guest.

[013-068] Complete are now the bounties you have given,

[013-069] Be all those bounties but confirm’d by Heaven!

[013-070] So may I find, when all my wanderings cease,

[013-071] My consort blameless, and my friends in peace.

[013-072] On you be every bliss; and every day,

[013-073] In home-felt joys, delighted roll away;

[013-074] Yourselves, your wives, your long-descending race,

[013-075] May every god enrich with every grace!

[013-076] Sure fix’d on virtue may your nation stand,

[013-077] And public evil never touch the land!”

[013-078] His words well weigh’d, the general voice approved

[013-079] Benign, and instant his dismission moved,

[013-080] The monarch to Pontonus gave the sign.

[013-081] To fill the goblet high with rosy wine;

[013-082] “Great Jove the Father first (he cried) implore;

[013-083] Then send the stranger to his native shore.”

[013-084] The luscious wine the obedient herald brought;

[013-085] Around the mansion flow’d the purple draught;

[013-086] Each from his seat to each immortal pours,

[013-087] Whom glory circles in the Olympian bowers

[013-088] Ulysses sole with air majestic stands,

[013-089] The bowl presenting to Arete’s hands;

[013-090] Then thus: “O queen, farewell! be still possess’d

[013-091] Of dear remembrance, blessing still and bless’d!

[013-092] Till age and death shall gently call thee hence,

[013-093] (Sure fate of every mortal excellence!)

[013-094] Farewell! and joys successive ever spring

[013-095] To thee, to thine, the people, and the king!”

## Section 35

[013-096] Thus he: then parting prints the sandy shore

[013-097] To the fair port: a herald march’d before,

[013-098] Sent by Alcinous; of Arete’s train

[013-099] Three chosen maids attend him to the main;

[013-100] This does a tunic and white vest convey,

[013-101] A various casket that, of rich inlay,

[013-102] And bread and wine the third. The cheerful mates

[013-103] Safe in the hollow poop dispose the cates;

[013-104] Upon the deck soft painted robes they spread

[013-105] With linen cover’d, for the hero’s bed.

[013-106] He climbed the lofty stern; then gently press’d

[013-107] The swelling couch, and lay composed to rest.

[013-108] Now placed in order, the Phæacian train

[013-109] Their cables loose, and launch into the main;

[013-110] At once they bend, and strike their equal oars,

[013-111] And leave the sinking hills and lessening shores.

[013-112] While on the deck the chief in silence lies,

[013-113] And pleasing slumbers steal upon his eyes.

[013-114] As fiery coursers in the rapid race

[013-115] Urged by fierce drivers through the dusty space,

[013-116] Toss their high heads, and scour along the plain,

[013-117] So mounts the bounding vessel o’er the main.

[013-118] Back to the stern the parted billows flow,

[013-119] And the black ocean foams and roars below.

[013-120] Thus with spread sails the winged galley flies;

[013-121] Less swift an eagle cuts the liquid skies;

[013-122] Divine Ulysses was her sacred load,

[013-123] A man, in wisdom equal to a god!

[013-124] Much danger, long and mighty toils he bore,

[013-125] In storms by sea, and combats on the shore;

[013-126] All which soft sleep now banish’d from his breast,

[013-127] Wrapp’d in a pleasing, deep, and death-like rest.

[013-128] But when the morning-star with early ray

[013-129] Flamed in the front of heaven, and promised day;

[013-130] Like distant clouds the mariner descries

[013-131] Fair Ithaca’s emerging hills arise.

[013-132] Far from the town a spacious port appears,

[013-133] Sacred to Phorcys’ power, whose name it bears;

[013-134] Two craggy rocks projecting to the main,

[013-135] The roaring wind’s tempestuous rage restrain;

[013-136] Within the waves in softer murmurs glide,

[013-137] And ships secure without their halsers ride.

[013-138] High at the head a branching olive grows,

[013-139] And crowns the pointed cliffs with shady boughs.

[013-140] Beneath, a gloomy grotto’s cool recess

[013-141] Delights the Nereids of the neighbouring seas,

[013-142] Where bowls and urns were form’d of living stone,

[013-143] And massy beams in native marble shone,

[013-144] On which the labours of the nymphs were roll’d,

[013-145] Their webs divine of purple mix’d with gold.

[013-146] Within the cave the clustering bees attend

[013-147] Their waxen works, or from the roof depend.

[013-148] Perpetual waters o’er the pavement glide;

[013-149] Two marble doors unfold on either side;

[013-150] Sacred the south, by which the gods descend;

[013-151] But mortals enter at the northern end.

[013-152] Thither they bent, and haul’d their ship to land

[013-153] (The crooked keel divides the yellow sand).

[013-154] Ulysses sleeping on his couch they bore,

[013-155] And gently placed him on the rocky shore.

[013-156] His treasures next, Alcinous’ gifts, they laid

[013-157] In the wild olive’s unfrequented shade,

[013-158] Secure from theft; then launch’d the bark again,

[013-159] Resumed their oars, and measured back the main,

[013-160] Nor yet forgot old [[oceanus|Ocean]]’s dread supreme,

[013-161] The vengeance vow’d for eyeless Polypheme.

[013-162] Before the throne of mighty Jove he stood,

[013-163] And sought the secret counsels of the god.

[013-164] “Shall then no more, O sire of gods! be mine

[013-165] The rights and honours of a power divine?

[013-166] Scorn’d e’en by man, and (oh severe disgrace!)

[013-167] By soft Phæacians, my degenerate race!

[013-168] Against yon destined head in vain I swore,

[013-169] And menaced vengeance, ere he reach’d his shore;

[013-170] To reach his natal shore was thy decree;

[013-171] Mild I obey’d, for who shall war with thee?

[013-172] Behold him landed, careless and asleep,

[013-173] From all the eluded dangers of the deep;

[013-174] Lo where he lies, amidst a shining store

[013-175] Of brass, rich garments, and refulgent ore;

[013-176] And bears triumphant to his native isle

[013-177] A prize more worth than [[troy|Ilion]]’s noble spoil.”

[013-178] To whom the Father of the immortal powers,

[013-179] Who swells the clouds, and gladdens earth with showers,

[013-180] “Can mighty Neptune thus of man complain?

[013-181] Neptune, tremendous o’er the boundless main!

[013-182] Revered and awful e’en in heaven’s abodes,

[013-183] Ancient and great! a god above the gods!

[013-184] If that low race offend thy power divine

[013-185] (Weak, daring creatures!) is not vengeance thine?

[013-186] Go, then, the guilty at thy will chastise.”

[013-187] He said. The shaker of the earth replies:

[013-188] “This then, I doom: to fix the gallant ship,

[013-189] A mark of vengeance on the sable deep;

[013-190] To warn the thoughtless, self-confiding train,

[013-191] No more unlicensed thus to brave the main.

[013-192] Full in their port a Shady hill shall rise,

[013-193] If such thy will.”—“We will it (Jove replies).

[013-194] E’en when with transport blackening all the strand,

[013-195] The swarming people hail their ship to land,

[013-196] Fix her for ever, a memorial stone:

[013-197] Still let her seem to sail, and seem alone.

[013-198] The trembling crowds shall see the sudden shade

[013-199] Of whelming mountains overhang their head!”

[013-200] With that the god whose earthquakes rock the ground

[013-201] Fierce to Phæacia cross’d the vast profound.

[013-202] Swift as a swallow sweeps the liquid way,

[013-203] The winged pinnace shot along the sea.

[013-204] The god arrests her with a sudden stroke,

[013-205] And roots her down an everlasting rock.

[013-206] Aghast the [[the-phaeacians|Scherians]] stand in deep surprise;

[013-207] All press to speak, all question with their eyes.

[013-208] What hands unseen the rapid bark restrain!

[013-209] And yet it swims, or seems to swim, the main!

[013-210] Thus they, unconscious of the deed divine;

[013-211] Till great Alcinous, rising, own’d the sign.

[013-212] “Behold the long predestined day! (he cries;)

[013-213] O certain faith of ancient prophecies

[013-214] These ears have heard my royal sire disclose

[013-215] A dreadful story, big with future woes;

[013-216] How, moved with wrath, that careless we convey

[013-217] Promiscuous every guest to every bay,

[013-218] Stern Neptune raged; and how by his command

[013-219] Firm rooted in the surge a ship should stand

[013-220] (A monument of wrath); and mound on mound

[013-221] Should hide our walls, or whelm beneath the ground.

[013-222] “The Fates have follow’d as declared the seer.

[013-223] Be humbled, nations! and your monarch hear.

[013-224] No more unlicensed brave the deeps, no more

[013-225] With every stranger pass from shore to shore;

[013-226] On angry Neptune now for mercy call;

[013-227] To his high name let twelve black oxen fall.

[013-228] So may the god reverse his purposed will,

[013-229] Nor o’er our city hang the dreadful hill.”

[013-230] The monarch spoke: they trembled and obey’d,

[013-231] Forth on the sands the victim oxen led;

[013-232] The gathered tribes before the altars stand,

[013-233] And chiefs and rulers, a majestic band.

[013-234] The king of ocean all the tribes implore;

[013-235] The blazing altars redden all the shore.

[013-236] Meanwhile Ulysses in his country lay,

[013-237] Released from sleep, and round him might survey

[013-238] The solitary shore and rolling sea.

[013-239] Yet had his mind through tedious absence lost

[013-240] The dear resemblance of his native coast;

[013-241] Besides, [[athena|Minerva]], to secure her care,

[013-242] Diffused around a veil of thickened air;

[013-243] For so the gods ordain’d to keep unseen

[013-244] His royal person from his friends and queen;

[013-245] Till the proud suitors for their crimes afford

[013-246] An ample vengeance to their injured lord.

[013-247] Now all the land another prospect bore,

[013-248] Another port appear’d, another shore.

[013-249] And long-continued ways, and winding floods,

[013-250] And unknown mountains, crown’d with unknown woods

[013-251] Pensive and slow, with sudden grief oppress’d,

[013-252] The king arose, and beat his careful breast,

[013-253] Cast a long look o’er all the coast and main,

[013-254] And sought, around, his native realm in vain;

[013-255] Then with erected eyes stood fix’d in woe,

[013-256] And as he spoke, the tears began to flow.

[013-257] “Ye gods (he cried), upon what barren coast,

[013-258] In what new region, is Ulysses toss’d?

[013-259] Possess’d by wild barbarians, fierce in arms?

[013-260] Or men whose bosom tender pity warms?

[013-261] Where shall this treasure now in safely lie?

[013-262] And whither, whither its sad owner fly?

[013-263] Ah, why did I Alcinous’ grace implore?

[013-264] Ah, why forsake Phæacia’s happy shore?

[013-265] Some juster prince perhaps had entertain’d,

[013-266] And safe restored me to my native land.

[013-267] Is this the promised, long-expected coast,

[013-268] And this the faith Phæacia’s rulers boast?

[013-269] O righteous gods! of all the great, how few

[013-270] Are just to Heaven, and to their promise true!

[013-271] But he, the power to whose all-seeing eyes

[013-272] The deeds of men appear without disguise,

[013-273] ’Tis his alone to avenge the wrongs I bear;

[013-274] For still the oppress’d are his peculiar care.

[013-275] To count these presents, and from thence to prove,

[013-276] Their faith is mine; the rest belongs to Jove.”

[013-277] Then on the sands he ranged his wealthy store,

[013-278] The gold, the vests, the tripods number’d o’er:

[013-279] All these he found, but still in error lost,

[013-280] Disconsolate he wanders on the coast,

[013-281] Sighs for his country, and laments again

[013-282] To the deaf rocks, and hoarse-resounding main.

[013-283] When lo! the guardian goddess of the wise,

[013-284] Celestial Pallas, stood before his eyes;

[013-285] In show a youthful swain, of form divine,

[013-286] Who seem’d descended from some princely line.

[013-287] A graceful robe her slender body dress’d;

[013-288] Around her shoulders flew the waving vest;

[013-289] Her decent hand a shining javelin bore,

[013-290] And painted sandals on her feet she wore.

[013-291] To whom the king: “Whoe’er of human race

[013-292] Thou art, that wanderest in this desert place,

[013-293] With joy to thee, as to some god I bend,

[013-294] To thee my treasures and myself commend.

[013-295] O tell a wretch in exile doom’d to stray,

[013-296] What air I breathe, what country I survey?

[013-297] The fruitful continent’s extremest bound,

[013-298] Or some fair isle which Neptune’s arms surround?

[013-299] “From what far clime (said she) remote from fame

[013-300] Arrivest thou here, a stranger to our name?

[013-301] Thou seest an island, not to those unknown

[013-302] Whose hills are brighten’d by the rising sun,

[013-303] Nor those that placed beneath his utmost reign

[013-304] Behold him sinking in the western main.

[013-305] The rugged soil allows no level space

[013-306] For flying chariots, or the rapid race;

[013-307] Yet, not ungrateful to the peasant’s pain,

[013-308] Suffices fulness to the swelling grain;

[013-309] The loaded trees their various fruits produce,

[013-310] And clustering grapes afford a generous juice;

[013-311] Woods crown our mountains, and in every grove

[013-312] The bounding goats and frisking heifers rove;

[013-313] Soft rains and kindly dews refresh the field,

[013-314] And rising springs eternal verdure yield.

[013-315] E’en to those shores is Ithaca renown’d,

[013-316] Where Troy’s majestic ruins strew the ground.”

[013-317] At this, the chief with transport was possess’d;

[013-318] His panting heart exulted in his breast;

[013-319] Yet, well dissembling his untimely joys,

[013-320] And veiling truth in plausible disguise,

[013-321] Thus, with an air sincere, in fiction bold,

[013-322] His ready tale the inventive hero told:

[013-323] “Oft have I heard in Crete this island’s name;

[013-324] For ’twas from Crete, my native soil, I came,

[013-325] Self-banished thence. I sail’d before the wind,

[013-326] And left my children and my friends behind.

[013-327] From fierce [[idomeneus|Idomeneus]]’ revenge I flew,

[013-328] Whose son, the swift Orsilochus, I slew

[013-329] (With brutal force he seized my Trojan prey,

[013-330] Due to the toils of many a bloody day).

[013-331] Unseen I ’scaped, and favour’d by the night,

[013-332] In a Phoenician vessel took my flight,

[013-333] For Pyle or Elis bound; but tempests toss’d

[013-334] And raging billows drove us on your coast.

[013-335] In dead of night an unknown port we gain’d;

[013-336] Spent with fatigue, and slept secure on land.

[013-337] But ere the rosy morn renew’d the day,

[013-338] While in the embrace of pleasing sleep I lay,

[013-339] Sudden, invited by auspicious gales,

[013-340] They land my goods, and hoist their flying sails.

[013-341] Abandon’d here, my fortune I deplore

[013-342] A hapless exile on a foreign shore,”

[013-343] Thus while he spoke, the blue-eyed maid began

[013-344] With pleasing smiles to view the godlike man;

[013-345] Then changed her form: and now, divinely bright,

[013-346] Jove’s heavenly daughter stood confess’d to sight;

[013-347] Like a fair virgin in her beauty’s bloom,

[013-348] Skill’d in the illustrious labours of the loom.

[013-349] “O still the same Ulysses! (she rejoin’d,)

[013-350] In useful craft successfully refined!

[013-351] Artful in speech, in action, and in mind!

[013-352] Sufficed it not, that, thy long labours pass’d,

[013-353] Secure thou seest thy native shore at last?

[013-354] But this to me? who, like thyself, excel

[013-355] In arts of counsel and dissembling well;

[013-356] To me? whose wit exceeds the powers divine,

[013-357] No less than mortals are surpass’d by thine.

[013-358] Know’st thou not me; who made thy life my care,

[013-359] Through ten years’ wandering, and through ten years’ war;

[013-360] Who taught thee arts, Alcinous to persuade,

[013-361] To raise his wonder, and engage his aid;

[013-362] And now appear, thy treasures to protect,

[013-363] Conceal thy person, thy designs direct,

[013-364] And tell what more thou must from [[moira|Fate]] expect;

[013-365] Domestic woes far heavier to be borne!

[013-366] The pride of fools, and slaves’ insulting scorn?

[013-367] But thou be silent, nor reveal thy state;

[013-368] Yield to the force of unresisted Fate,

[013-369] And bear unmoved the wrongs of base mankind,

[013-370] The last, and hardest, conquest of the mind.”

## Section 36

[013-371] “Goddess of wisdom! (Ithacus replies,)

[013-372] He who discerns thee must be truly wise,

[013-373] So seldom view’d and ever in disguise!

[013-374] When the bold Argives led their warring powers,

[013-375] Against proud Ilion’s well-defended towers,

[013-376] Ulysses was thy care, celestial maid!

[013-377] Graced with thy sight, and favoured with thy aid.

[013-378] But when the Trojan piles in ashes lay,

[013-379] And bound for Greece we plough’d the watery way;

[013-380] Our fleet dispersed, and driven from coast to coast,

[013-381] Thy sacred presence from that hour I lost;

[013-382] Till I beheld thy radiant form once more,

[013-383] And heard thy counsels on Phæacia’s shore.

[013-384] But, by the almighty author of thy race,

[013-385] Tell me, oh tell, is this my native place?

[013-386] For much I fear, long tracts of land and sea

[013-387] Divide this coast from distant Ithaca;

[013-388] The sweet delusion kindly you impose,

[013-389] To soothe my hopes, and mitigate my woes.”

[013-390] Thus he. The blue-eyed goddess thus replies;

[013-391] “How prone to doubt, how cautious are the wise!

[013-392] Who, versed in fortune, fear the flattering show,

[013-393] And taste not half the bliss the gods bestow.

[013-394] The more shall Pallas aid thy just desires,

[013-395] And guard the wisdom which herself inspires.

[013-396] Others long absent from their native place,

[013-397] Straight seek their home, and fly with eager pace

[013-398] To their wives’ arms, and children’s dear embrace.

[013-399] Not thus Ulysses; he decrees to prove

[013-400] His subjects’ faith, and queen’s suspected love;

[013-401] Who mourn’d her lord twice ten revolving years,

[013-402] And wastes the days in grief, the nights in tears.

[013-403] But Pallas knew (thy friends and navy lost)

[013-404] Once more ’twas given thee to behold thy coast;

[013-405] Yet how could I with adverse Fate engage,

[013-406] And mighty Neptune’s unrelenting rage?

[013-407] Now lift thy longing eyes, while I restore

[013-408] The pleasing prospect of thy native shore.

[013-409] Bebold the port of Phorcys! fenced around

[013-410] With rocky mountains, and with olives crown’d,

[013-411] Behold the gloomy grot! whose cool recess

[013-412] Delights the Nereids of the neighbouring seas;

[013-413] Whose now-neglected altars in thy reign

[013-414] Blush’d with the blood of sheep and oxen slain,

[013-415] Behold! where [[the-mountain-of-neriton|Neritus]] the clouds divides,

[013-416] And shakes the waving forests on his sides.”

[013-417] So spake the goddess; and the prospect clear’d,

[013-418] The mists dispersed, and all the coast appeared.

[013-419] The king with joy confess’d his place of birth,

[013-420] And on his knees salutes his mother earth;

[013-421] Then, with his suppliant hands upheld in air,

[013-422] Thus to the sea-green sisters sends his prayer;

[013-423] “All hail! ye virgin daughters of the main!

[013-424] Ye streams, beyond my hopes, beheld again!

[013-425] To you once more your own Ulysses bows;

[013-426] Attend his transports, and receive his vows!

[013-427] If Jove prolong my days, and Pallas crown

[013-428] The growing virtues of my youthful son,

[013-429] To you shall rites divine be ever paid,

[013-430] And grateful offerings on your altars laid.”

[013-431] Thus then Minerva: “From that anxious breast

[013-432] Dismiss those cares, and leave to heaven the rest.

[013-433] Our task be now thy treasured stores to save,

[013-434] Deep in the close recesses of the cave;

[013-435] Then future means consult.” She spoke, and trod

[013-436] The shady grot, that brighten’d with the god.

[013-437] The closest caverns of the grot she sought;

[013-438] The gold, the brass, the robes, Ulysses brought;

[013-439] These in the secret gloom the chief disposed;

[013-440] The entrance with a rock the goddess closed.

[013-441] Now, seated in the olive’s sacred shade,

[013-442] Confer the hero and the martial maid.

[013-443] The goddess of the azure eyes began:

[013-444] “Son of [[laertes|Laertes]]! much-experienced man!

[013-445] The suitor-train thy earliest care demand,

[013-446] Of that luxurious race to rid the land;

[013-447] Three years thy house their lawless rule has seen,

[013-448] And proud addresses to the matchless queen.

[013-449] But she thy absence mourns from day to day,

[013-450] And inly bleeds, and silent wastes away;

[013-451] Elusive of the bridal hour, she gives

[013-452] Fond hopes to all, and all with hopes deceives.”

[013-453] To this Ulysses: “O celestial maid!

[013-454] Praised be thy counsel, and thy timely aid;

[013-455] Else had I seen my native walls in vain,

[013-456] Like great [[agamemnon|Atrides]], just restored and slain.

[013-457] Vouchsafe the means of vengeance to debate,

[013-458] And plan with all thy arts the scene of fate.

[013-459] Then, then be present, and my soul inspire,

[013-460] As when we wrapp’d Troy’s heaven-built walls in fire.

[013-461] Though leagued against me hundred heroes stand.

[013-462] Hundreds shall fall, if Pallas aid my hand.”

[013-463] She answer’d: “In the dreadful day of fight

[013-464] Know, I am with thee, strong in all my might.

[013-465] If thou but equal to thyself be found,

[013-466] What gasping numbers then shall press the ground!

[013-467] What human victims stain the feastful floor!

[013-468] How wide the pavements float with guilty gore!

[013-469] It fits thee now to wear a dark disguise,

[013-470] And secret walk unknown to mortal eyes.

[013-471] For this, my hand shall wither every grace,

[013-472] And every elegance of form and face;

[013-473] O’er thy smooth skin a bark of wrinkles spread,

[013-474] Turn hoar the auburn honours of thy head;

[013-475] Disfigure every limb with coarse attire,

[013-476] And in thy eyes extinguish all the fire;

[013-477] Add all the wants and the decays of life;

[013-478] Estrange thee from thy own; thy son, thy wife;

[013-479] From the loathed object every sight shall turn,

[013-480] And the blind suitors their destruction scorn.

[013-481] “Go first the master of thy herds to find,

[013-482] True to his charge, a loyal swain and kind;

[013-483] For thee he sighs; and to the loyal heir

[013-484] And chaste [[penelope|Penelope]] extends his care.

[013-485] At the Coracian rock he now resides,

[013-486] Where Arethusa’s sable water glides;

[013-487] The sable water and the copious mast

[013-488] Swell the fat herd; luxuriant, large repast!

[013-489] With him rest peaceful in the rural cell,

[013-490] And all you ask his faithful tongue shall tell.

[013-491] Me into other realms my cares convey,

[013-492] To [[sparta|Sparta]], still with female beauty gay;

[013-493] For know, to Sparta thy loved offspring came,

[013-494] To learn thy fortunes from the voice of [[kleos|Fame]].”

[013-495] At this the father, with a father’s care:

[013-496] “Must he too suffer? he, O goddess! bear

[013-497] Of wanderings and of woes a wretched share?

[013-498] Through the wild ocean plough the dangerous way,

[013-499] And leave his fortunes and his house a prey?

[013-500] Why would’st not thou, O all-enlighten’d mind!

[013-501] Inform him certain, and protect him, kind?”

[013-502] To whom Minerva: “Be thy soul at rest;

[013-503] And know, whatever heaven ordains is best.

[013-504] To fame I sent him, to acquire renown;

[013-505] To other regions is his virtue known;

[013-506] Secure he sits, near great [[menelaus|Atrides]] placed;

[013-507] With friendships strengthen’d, and with honours graced,

[013-508] But lo! an ambush waits his passage o’er;

[013-509] Fierce foes insidious intercept the shore;

[013-510] In vain; far sooner all the murderous brood

[013-511] This injured land shall fatten with their blood.”

[013-512] She spake, then touch’d him with her powerful wand:

[013-513] The skin shrunk up, and wither’d at her hand;

[013-514] A swift old age o’er all his members spread;

[013-515] A sudden frost was sprinkled on his head;

[013-516] Nor longer in the heavy eye-ball shined

[013-517] The glance divine, forth-beaming from the mind.

[013-518] His robe, which spots indelible besmear,

[013-519] In rags dishonest flutters with the air:

[013-520] A stag’s torn hide is lapp’d around his reins;

[013-521] A rugged staff his trembling hand sustains;

[013-522] And at his side a wretched scrip was hung,

[013-523] Wide-patch’d, and knotted to a twisted thong.

[013-524] So looked the chief, so moved: to mortal eyes

[013-525] Object uncouth! a man of miseries!

[013-526] While Pallas, cleaving the wild fields of air,

[013-527] To Sparta flies, [[telemachus|Telemachus]] her care.
