[019-001] BOOK XIX.

[019-002] ARGUMENT.

[019-003] THE DISCOVERY OF ULYSSES TO EURYCLEA.

[019-004] [[odysseus|Ulysses]] and his son remove the weapons out of the armoury. Ulysses, in

[019-005] conversation with [[penelope|Penelope]], gives a fictitious account of his

[019-006] adventures; then assures her he had formerly entertained her husband in

[019-007] Crete; and describes exactly his person and dress; affirms to have

[019-008] heard of him in Phæacia and Thesprotia, and that his return is

[019-009] certain, and within a month. He then goes to bathe, and is attended by

[019-010] Euryclea, who discovers him to be Ulysses by the scar upon his leg,

[019-011] which he formerly received in hunting the wild boar on Parnassus. The

[019-012] poet inserts a digression relating that accident, with all its

[019-013] particulars.

[019-014] Consulting secret with the blue-eyed maid,

[019-015] Still in the dome divine Ulysses stay’d:

[019-016] [[poine|Revenge]] mature for act inflamed his breast;

[019-017] And thus the son the fervent sire address’d:

[019-018] “Instant convey those steely stores of war

[019-019] To distant rooms, disposed with secret care:

[019-020] The cause demanded by the suitor-train,

[019-021] To soothe their fears, a specious reason feign:

[019-022] Say, since Ulysses left his natal coast,

[019-023] Obscene with smoke, their beamy lustre lost,

[019-024] His arms deform the roof they wont adorn:

[019-025] From the glad walls inglorious lumber torn.

[019-026] Suggest, that [[zeus|Jove]] the peaceful thought inspired,

[019-027] Lest they, by sight of swords to fury fired,

[019-028] Dishonest wounds, or violence of soul,

[019-029] Defame the bridal feast and friendly bowl.”

[019-030] The prince, obedient to the sage command,

[019-031] To Euryclea thus: “The female band

[019-032] In their apartments keep; secure the doors;

[019-033] These swarthy arms among the covert stores

[019-034] Are seemlier hid; my thoughtless youth they blame,

[019-035] Imbrown’d with vapour of the smouldering flame.”

[019-036] “In happier hour (pleased Euryclea cries),

[019-037] Tutour’d by early woes, grow early wise;

[019-038] Inspect with sharpen’d sight, and frugal care,

[019-039] Your patrimonial wealth, a prudent heir.

[019-040] But who the lighted taper will provide

[019-041] (The female train retired) your toils to guide?”

[019-042] “Without infringing hospitable right,

[019-043] This guest (he cried) shall bear the guiding light:

[019-044] I cheer no lazy vagrants with repast;

[019-045] They share the meal that earn it ere they taste.”

[019-046] He said: from female ken she straight secures

[019-047] The purposed deed, and guards the bolted doors:

[019-048] Auxiliar to his son, Ulysses bears

[019-049] The plumy-crested helms and pointed spears,

[019-050] With shields indented deep in glorious wars.

[019-051] [[athena|Minerva]] viewless on her charge attends,

[019-052] And with her golden lamp his toil befriends.

[019-053] Not such the sickly beams, which unsincere

[019-054] Gild the gross vapour of this nether sphere!

[019-055] A present deity the prince confess’d,

[019-056] And wrapp’d with ecstasy the sire address’d:

[019-057] “What miracle thus dazzles with surprise!

[019-058] Distinct in rows the radiant columns rise;

[019-059] The walls, where’er my wondering sight I turn,

[019-060] And roofs, amidst a blaze of glory burn!

[019-061] Some visitant of pure ethereal race

[019-062] With his bright presence deigns the dome to grace.”

[019-063] “Be calm (replies the sire); to none impart,

[019-064] But oft revolve the vision in thy heart:

[019-065] Celestials, mantled in excess of light,

[019-066] Can visit unapproach’d by mortal sight.

[019-067] Seek thou repose: whilst here I sole remain,

[019-068] To explore the conduct of the female train:

[019-069] The pensive queen, perchance, desires to know

[019-070] The series of my toils, to soothe her woe.”

[019-071] With tapers flaming day his train attends,

[019-072] His bright alcove the obsequious youth ascends:

[019-073] Soft slumberous shades his drooping eyelids close,

[019-074] Till on her eastern throne Aurora glows.

[019-075] Whilst, forming plans of death, Ulysses stay’d,

[019-076] In counsel secret with the martial maid,

[019-077] Attendant nymphs in beauteous order wait

[019-078] The queen, descending from her bower of state.

[019-079] Her cheeks the warmer blush of Venus wear,

[019-080] Chasten’d with coy Diana’s pensive air.

[019-081] An ivory seat with silver ringlets graced,

[019-082] By famed Icmalius wrought, the menials placed:

[019-083] With ivory silver’d thick the footstool shone,

[019-084] O’er which the panther’s various hide was thrown.

[019-085] The sovereign seat with graceful air she press’d;

[019-086] To different tasks their toil the nymphs address’d:

[019-087] The golden goblets some, and some restored

[019-088] From stains of luxury the polish’d board:

[019-089] These to remove the expiring embers came,

[019-090] While those with unctuous fir foment the flame.

[019-091] ’Twas then [[melantho|Melantho]] with imperious mien

[019-092] Renew’d the attack, incontinent of spleen:

[019-093] “Avaunt (she cried), offensive to my sight!

[019-094] Deem not in ambush here to lurk by night,

[019-095] Into the woman-state asquint to pry;

[019-096] A day-devourer, and an evening spy!

[019-097] Vagrant, begone! before this blazing brand

[019-098] Shall urge”—and waved it hissing in her hand.

[019-099] The insulted hero rolls his wrathful eyes

[019-100] And “Why so turbulent of soul? (he cries;)

[019-101] Can these lean shrivell’d limbs, unnerved with age,

[019-102] These poor but honest rags, enkindle rage?

[019-103] In crowds, we wear the badge of hungry fate:

[019-104] And beg, degraded from superior state!

[019-105] Constrain’d a rent-charge on the rich I live;

[019-106] Reduced to crave the good I once could give:

[019-107] A palace, wealth, and slaves, I late possess’d,

[019-108] And all that makes the great be call’d the bless’d:

[019-109] My gate, an emblem of my open soul,

[019-110] Embraced the poor, and dealt a bounteous dole.

[019-111] Scorn not the sad reverse, injurious maid!

[019-112] ’Tis Jove’s high will, and be his will obey’d!

[019-113] Nor think thyself exempt: that rosy prime

[019-114] Must share the general doom of withering time:

[019-115] To some new channel soon the changeful tide

[019-116] Of royal grace the offended queen may guide;

[019-117] And her loved lord unplume thy towering pride.

[019-118] Or, were he dead, ’tis wisdom to beware:

[019-119] Sweet blooms the prince beneath [[apollo|Apollo]]’s care;

[019-120] Your deeds with quick impartial eye surveys,

[019-121] Potent to punish what he cannot praise.”

[019-122] Her keen reproach had reach’d the sovereign’s ear:

[019-123] “Loquacious insolent! (she cries,) forbear;

[019-124] To thee the purpose of my soul I told;

[019-125] Venial discourse, unblamed, with him to hold;

[019-126] The storied labours of my wandering lord,

[019-127] To soothe my grief he haply may record:

[019-128] Yet him, my guest, thy venom’d rage hath stung;

[019-129] Thy head shall pay the forfeit of thy tongue!

[019-130] But thou on whom my palace cares depend,

[019-131] [[eurynome|Eurynome]], regard the stranger-friend:

[019-132] A seat, soft spread with furry spoils, prepare;

[019-133] Due-distant for us both to speak, and hear.”

[019-134] The menial fair obeys with duteous haste:

[019-135] A seat adorn’d with furry spoils she placed:

[019-136] Due-distant for discourse the hero sate;

[019-137] When thus the sovereign from her chair of state:

[019-138] “Reveal, obsequious to my first demand,

[019-139] Thy name, thy lineage, and thy natal land.”

[019-140] He thus: “O queen! whose far-resounding fame

[019-141] Is bounded only by the starry frame,

[019-142] Consummate pattern of imperial sway,

[019-143] Whose pious rule a warlike race obey!

[019-144] In wavy gold thy summer vales are dress’d;

[019-145] Thy autumns bind with copious fruit oppress’d:

[019-146] With flocks and herds each grassy plain is stored;

[019-147] And fish of every fin thy seas afford:

[019-148] Their affluent joys the grateful realms confess;

[019-149] And bless the power that still delights to bless,

[019-150] Gracious permit this prayer, imperial dame!

[019-151] Forbear to know my lineage, or my name:

[019-152] Urge not this breast to heave, these eyes to weep;

[019-153] In sweet oblivion let my sorrows sleep!

[019-154] My woes awaked, will violate your ear,

[019-155] And to this gay censorious train appear

[019-156] A whiny vapour melting in a tear.”

[019-157] “Their gifts the gods resumed (the queen rejoin’d),

[019-158] Exterior grace, and energy of mind,

[019-159] When the dear partner of my nuptial joy,

[019-160] Auxiliar troops combined, to conquer [[troy|Troy]].

[019-161] My lord’s protecting hand alone would raise

[019-162] My drooping verdure, and extend my praise!

[019-163] Peers from the distant Samian shore resort:

[019-164] Here with Dulichians join’d, besiege the court:

[019-165] [[zacynthus|Zacynthus]], green with ever-shady groves,

[019-166] And [[ithaca|Ithaca]], presumptuous, boast their loves:

[019-167] Obtruding on my choice a second lord,

[019-168] They press the Hymenaean rite abhorr’d.

[019-169] Misrule thus mingling with domestic cares,

[019-170] I live regardless of my state affairs;

[019-171] Receive no stranger-guest, no poor relieve;

[019-172] But ever for my lord in secret grieve!—

[019-173] This art, instinct by some celestial power,

[019-174] I tried, elusive of the bridal hour:

[019-175] “‘Ye peers, (I cry,) who press to gain a heart,

[019-176] Where dead Ulysses claims no future part;

[019-177] Rebate your loves, each rival suit suspend,

[019-178] Till this funeral web my labours end:

[019-179] Cease, till to good [[laertes|Laertes]] I bequeath

[019-180] A pall of state, the ornament of death.

[019-181] For when to fate he bows, each Grecian dame

[019-182] With just reproach were licensed to defame,

[019-183] Should he, long honour’d in supreme command,

[019-184] Want the last duties of a daughter’s hand.’

[019-185] The fiction pleased; their loves I long elude;

[019-186] The night still ravell’d what the day renew’d:

[019-187] Three years successful in my heart conceal’d,

[019-188] My ineffectual fraud the fourth reveal’d:

[019-189] Befriended by my own domestic spies,

[019-190] The woof unwrought the suitor-train surprise.

[019-191] From nuptial rites they now no more recede,

[019-192] And fear forbids to falsify the brede.

[019-193] My anxious parents urge a speedy choice,

[019-194] And to their suffrage gain the filial voice.

[019-195] For rule mature, [[telemachus|Telemachus]] deplores

[019-196] His dome dishonour’d, and exhausted stores—

[019-197] But, stranger! as thy days seem full of fate,

[019-198] Divide discourse, in turn thy birth relate:

[019-199] Thy port asserts thee of distinguish’d race;

[019-200] No poor unfather’d product of disgrace.”

## Section 48

[019-201] “Princess! (he cries,) renew’d by your command,

[019-202] The dear remembrance of my native land

[019-203] Of secret grief unseals the fruitful source;

[019-204] Fond tears repeat their long-forgotten course!

[019-205] So pays the wretch whom fate constrains to roam,

[019-206] The dues of nature to his natal home!—

[019-207] But inward on my soul let sorrow prey,

[019-208] Your sovereign will my duty bids obey.

[019-209] “Crete awes the circling waves, a fruitful soil!

[019-210] And ninety cities crown the sea-born isle:

[019-211] Mix’d with her genuine sons, adopted names

[019-212] In various tongues avow their various claims:

[019-213] Cydonians, dreadful with the bended yew,

[019-214] And bold Pelasgi boast a native’s due:

[019-215] The Dorians, plumed amid the files of war,

[019-216] Her foodful glebe with fierce Achaians share;

[019-217] Cnossus, her capital of high command;

[019-218] Where sceptred Minos with impartial hand

[019-219] Divided right: each ninth revolving year,

[019-220] By Jove received in council to confer.

[019-221] His son Deucalion bore successive sway:

[019-222] His son, who gave me first to view the day!

[019-223] The royal bed an elder issue bless’d,

[019-224] [[idomeneus|Idomeneus]] whom Ilion fields attest

[019-225] Of matchless deeds: untrain’d to martial toil,

[019-226] I lived inglorious in my native isle,

[019-227] Studious of peace, and Æthon is my name.

[019-228] ’Twas then to Crete the great Ulysses came.

[019-229] For elemental war, and wintry Jove,

[019-230] From Malea’s gusty cape his navy drove

[019-231] To bright Lucina’s fane; the shelfy coast

[019-232] Where loud Amnisus in the deep is lost.

[019-233] His vessels moor’d (an incommodious port!)

[019-234] The hero speeded to the Cnossian court:

[019-235] Ardent the partner of his arms to find,

[019-236] In leagues of long commutual friendship join’d.

[019-237] Vain hope! ten suns had warm’d the western strand

[019-238] Since my brave brother, with his Cretan band,

[019-239] Had sail’d for Troy: but to the genial feast

[019-240] My honour’d roof received the royal guest:

[019-241] Beeves for his train the Cnossian peers assign,

[019-242] A public treat, with jars of generous wine.

[019-243] Twelve days while Boreas vex’d the aërial space,

[019-244] My hospitable dome he deign’d to grace:

[019-245] And when the north had ceased the stormy roar,

[019-246] He wing’d his voyage to the Phrygian shore.”

[019-247] Thus the fam’d hero, perfected in wiles,

[019-248] With fair similitude of truth beguiles

[019-249] The queen’s attentive ear: dissolved in woe,

[019-250] From her bright eyes the tears unbounded flow,

[019-251] As snows collected on the mountain freeze;

[019-252] When milder regions breathe a vernal breeze,

[019-253] The fleecy pile obeys the whispering gales,

[019-254] Ends in a stream, and murmurs through the vales:

[019-255] So, melting with the pleasing tale he told,

[019-256] Down her fair cheek the copious torrent roll’d:

[019-257] She to her present lord laments him lost,

[019-258] And views that object which she wants the most,

[019-259] Withering at heart to see the weeping fair,

[019-260] His eyes look stern, and cast a gloomy stare;

[019-261] Of horn the stiff relentless balls appear,

[019-262] Or globes of iron fix’d in either sphere;

[019-263] Firm wisdom interdicts the softening tear.

[019-264] A speechless interval of grief ensues,

[019-265] Till thus the queen the tender theme renews.

[019-266] “Stranger! that e’er thy hospitable roof

[019-267] Ulysses graced, confirm by faithful proof;

[019-268] Delineate to my view my warlike lord,

[019-269] His form, his habit, and his train record.”

[019-270] “‘Tis hard (he cries,) to bring to sudden sight

[019-271] Ideas that have wing’d their distant flight;

[019-272] Rare on the mind those images are traced,

[019-273] Whose footsteps twenty winters have defaced:

[019-274] But what I can, receive.—In ample mode,

[019-275] A robe of military purple flow’d

[019-276] O’er all his frame: illustrious on his breast,

[019-277] The double-clasping gold the king confess’d.

[019-278] In the rich woof a hound, mosaic drawn,

[019-279] Bore on full stretch, and seized a dappled fawn;

[019-280] Deep in the neck his fangs indent their hold;

[019-281] They pant and struggle in the moving gold.

[019-282] Fine as a filmy web beneath it shone

[019-283] A vest, that dazzled like a cloudless sun:

[019-284] The female train who round him throng’d to gaze,

[019-285] In silent wonder sigh’d unwilling praise.

[019-286] A sabre, when the warrior press’d to part,

[019-287] I gave, enamell’d with Vulcanian art:

[019-288] A mantle purple-tinged, and radiant vest,

[019-289] Dimension’d equal to his size, express’d

[019-290] Affection grateful to my honour’d guest.

[019-291] A favourite herald in his train I knew,

[019-292] His visage solemn, sad of sable hue:

[019-293] Short woolly curls o’erfleeced his bending head,

[019-294] O’er which a promontory shoulder spread;

[019-295] Eurybates; in whose large soul alone

[019-296] Ulysses view’d an image of his own.”

[019-297] His speech the tempest of her grief restored;

[019-298] In all he told she recognized her lord:

[019-299] But when the storm was spent in plenteous showers,

[019-300] A pause inspiriting her languish’d powers,

[019-301] “O thou, (she cried,) whom first inclement [[moira|Fate]]

[019-302] Made welcome to my hospitable gate;

[019-303] With all thy wants the name of poor shall end:

[019-304] Henceforth live honour’d, my domestic friend!

[019-305] The vest much envied on your native coast,

[019-306] And regal robe with figured gold emboss’d,

[019-307] In happier hours my artful hand employ’d,

[019-308] When my loved lord this blissful bower enjoy’d:

[019-309] The fall of Troy erroneous and forlorn

[019-310] Doom’d to survive, and never to return!”

[019-311] Then he, with pity touch’d: “O royal dame!

[019-312] Your ever-anxious mind, and beauteous frame,

[019-313] From the devouring rage of grief reclaim.

[019-314] I not the fondness of your soul reprove

[019-315] For such a lord! who crown’d your virgin love

[019-316] With the dear blessing of a fair increase;

[019-317] Himself adorn’d with more than mortal grace:

[019-318] Yet while I speak the mighty woe suspend;

[019-319] Truth forms my tale; to pleasing truth attend.

[019-320] The royal object of your dearest care

[019-321] Breathes in no distant clime the vital air:

[019-322] In rich Thesprotia, and the nearer bound

[019-323] Of Thessaly, his name I heard renown’d:

[019-324] Without retinue, to that friendly shore

[019-325] Welcomed with gifts of price, a sumless store!

[019-326] His sacrilegious train, who dared to prey

[019-327] On herds devoted to the god of day,

[019-328] Were doom’d by Jove, and Phœbus’ just decree,

[019-329] To perish in the rough Trinacrian sea.

[019-330] To better fate the blameless chief ordain’d,

[019-331] A floating fragment of the wreck regain’d,

[019-332] And rode the storm; till, by the billows toss’d,

[019-333] He landed on the fair Phæacian coast.

[019-334] That race who emulate the life of gods,

[019-335] Receive him joyous to their bless’d abodes;

[019-336] Large gifts confer, a ready sail command,

[019-337] To speed his voyage to the Grecian strand.

[019-338] But your wise lord (in whose capacious soul

[019-339] High schemes of power in just succession roll)

[019-340] His Ithaca refused from favouring Fate,

[019-341] Till copious wealth might guard his regal state.

[019-342] Phedon the fact affirm’d, whose sovereign sway

[019-343] Thesprotian tribes, a duteous race, obey;

[019-344] And bade the gods this added truth attest

[019-345] (While pure libations crown’d the genial feast),

[019-346] That anchor’d in his port the vessels stand,

[019-347] To waft the hero to his natal land.

[019-348] I for [[dulichium|Dulichium]] urge the watery way,

[019-349] But first the Ulyssean wealth survey:

[019-350] So rich the value of a store so vast

[019-351] Demands the pomp of centuries to waste!

[019-352] The darling object of your royal love

[019-353] Was journey’d thence to Dodonean Jove;

[019-354] By the sure precept of the sylvan shrine,

[019-355] To form the conduct of his great design;

[019-356] Irresolute of soul, his state to shroud

[019-357] In dark disguise, or come, a king avow’d!

[019-358] Thus lives your lord; nor longer doom’d to roam;

[019-359] Soon will he grace this dear paternal dome.

[019-360] By Jove, the source of good, supreme in power!

[019-361] By the bless’d genius of this friendly bower!

[019-362] I ratify my speech, before the sun

[019-363] His annual longitude of heaven shall run;

[019-364] When the pale empress of yon starry train

[019-365] In the next month renews her faded wane,

[019-366] Ulysses will assert his rightful reign.”

[019-367] “What thanks! what boon! (replied the queen), are due,

[019-368] When time shall prove the storied blessing true!

[019-369] My lord’s return should fate no more retard,

[019-370] Envy shall sicken at thy vast reward.

[019-371] But my prophetic fears, alas! presage

[019-372] The wounds of Destiny’s relentless rage.

[019-373] I long must weep, nor will Ulysses come,

[019-374] With royal gifts to send you honour’d home!—

[019-375] Your other task, ye menial train forbear:

[019-376] Now wash the stranger, and the bed prepare:

[019-377] With splendid palls the downy fleece adorn:

[019-378] Uprising early with the purple morn.

[019-379] His sinews, shrunk with age, and stiff with toil,

[019-380] In the warm bath foment with fragrant oil.

[019-381] Then with Telemachus the social feast

[019-382] Partaking free, my soul invited guest;

[019-383] Whoe’er neglects to pay distinction due,

[019-384] The breach of hospitable right may rue.

[019-385] The vulgar of my sex I most exceed

[019-386] In real fame, when most humane my deed;

[019-387] And vainly to the praise of queen aspire,

[019-388] If, stranger! I permit that mean attire

[019-389] Beneath the feastful bower. A narrow space

[019-390] Confines the circle of our destin’d race;

[019-391] ’Tis ours with good the scanty round to grace.

[019-392] Those who to cruel wrong their state abuse,

[019-393] Dreaded in life the mutter’d curse pursues;

[019-394] By death disrobed of all their savage powers,

[019-395] Then, licensed rage her hateful prey devours.

[019-396] But he whose inborn worth his acts commend,

[019-397] Of gentle soul, to human race a friend;

[019-398] The wretched he relieves diffuse his fame,

[019-399] And distant tongues extol the patron-name.”

[019-400] “Princess? (he cried) in vain your bounties flow

[019-401] On me, confirm’d and obstinate in woe.

[019-402] When my loved Crete received my final view,

[019-403] And from my weeping eyes her cliffs withdrew;

[019-404] These tatter’d weeds (my decent robes resign’d)

[019-405] I chose, the livery of a woful mind!

[019-406] Nor will my heart-corroding care abate

[019-407] With splendid palls, and canopies of state:

[019-408] Low-couch’d on earth, the gift of sleep I scorn,

[019-409] And catch the glances of the waking morn.

[019-410] The delicacy of your courtly train

[019-411] To wash a wretched wanderer would disdain;

[019-412] But if, in tract of long experience tried,

[019-413] And sad similitude of woes allied,

[019-414] Some wretch reluctant views aërial light,

[019-415] To her mean hand assign the friendly rite.”

[019-416] Pleased with his wise reply, the queen rejoin’d:

[019-417] “Such gentle manners, and so sage a mind,

[019-418] In all who graced this hospitable bower

[019-419] I ne’er discerned, before this social hour.

[019-420] Such servant as your humble choice requires,

[019-421] To light received the lord of my desires,

[019-422] New from the birth; and with a mother’s hand

[019-423] His tender bloom to manly growth sustain’d:

[019-424] Of matchless prudence, and a duteous mind;

[019-425] Though now to life’s extremest verge declined,

[019-426] Of strength superior to the toil design’d—

[019-427] Rise, Euryclea! with officious care

[019-428] For the poor friend the cleansing bath prepare:

[019-429] This debt his correspondent fortunes claim,

[019-430] Too like Ulysses, and perhaps the same!

[019-431] Thus old with woes my fancy paints him now!

[019-432] For age untimely marks the careful brow.”

[019-433] Instant, obsequious to the mild command,

[019-434] Sad Euryclea rose: with trembling hand

[019-435] She veils the torrent of her tearful eyes;

[019-436] And thus impassion’d to herself replies:
