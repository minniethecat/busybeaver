[023-001] BOOK XXIII.

[023-002] ARGUMENT.

[023-003] Euryclea awakens [[penelope|Penelope]] with the news of [[odysseus|Ulysses]]’ return, and the

[023-004] death of the suitors. Penelope scarcely credits her; but supposes some

[023-005] god has punished them, and descends from her department in doubt. At

[023-006] the first interview of Ulysses and Penelope, she is quite unsatisfied.

[023-007] [[athena|Minerva]] restores him to the beauty of his youth; but the queen

[023-008] continues incredulous, till by some circumstances she is convinced, and

[023-009] falls into all the transports of passion and tenderness. They recount

[023-010] to each other all that has passed during their long separation. The

[023-011] next morning Ulysses, arming himself and his friends, goes from the

[023-012] city to visit his father.

[023-013] Then to the queen, as in repose she lay,

[023-014] The nurse with eager rapture speeds her way:

[023-015] The transports of her faithful heart supply

[023-016] A sudden youth, and give her wings to fly.

[023-017] “And sleeps my child? (the reverend matron cries)

[023-018] Ulysses lives! arise, my child, arise!

[023-019] At length appears the long-expected hour!

[023-020] Ulysses comes! the suitors are no more!

[023-021] No more they view the golden light of day!

[023-022] Arise, and bless thee with the glad survey?”

[023-023] Touch’d at her words, the mournful queen rejoin’d:

[023-024] “Ah! whither wanders thy distemper’d mind?

[023-025] The righteous powers, who tread the starry skies,

[023-026] The weak enlighten, and confound the wise,

[023-027] And human thought, with unresisted sway,

[023-028] Depress or raise, enlarge or take away:

[023-029] Truth, by their high decree, thy voice forsakes,

[023-030] And folly with the tongue of wisdom speaks.

[023-031] Unkind, the fond illusion to impose!

[023-032] Was it to flatter or deride my woes?

[023-033] Never did I sleep so sweet enjoy,

[023-034] Since my dear lord left [[ithaca|Ithaca]] for [[troy|Troy]].

[023-035] Why must I wake to grieve, and curse thy shore,

[023-036] O Troy?—may never tongue pronounce thee more!

[023-037] Begone! another might have felt our rage,

[023-038] But age is sacred, and we spare thy age.”

[023-039] To whom with warmth: “My soul a lie disdains;

[023-040] Ulysses lives, thy own Ulysses reigns:

[023-041] That stranger, patient of the suitors’ wrongs,

[023-042] And the rude license of ungovern’d tongues!

[023-043] He, he is thine! Thy son his latent guest

[023-044] Long knew, but lock’d the secret in his breast:

[023-045] With well concerted art to end his woes,

[023-046] And burst at once in vengeance on the foes.”

[023-047] While yet she spoke, the queen in transport sprung

[023-048] Swift from the couch, and round the matron hung;

[023-049] Fast from her eye descends the rolling tear:

[023-050] “Say, once more say, is my Ulysses here?

[023-051] How could that numerous and outrageous band

[023-052] By one be slain, though by a hero’s hand?”

[023-053] “I saw it not (she cries), but heard alone,

[023-054] When death was busy, a loud dying groan;

[023-055] The damsel-train turn’d pale at every wound,

[023-056] Immured we sate, and catch’d each passing sound;

[023-057] When death had seized her prey, thy son attends,

[023-058] And at his nod the damsel-train descends;

[023-059] There terrible in arms Ulysses stood,

[023-060] And the dead suitors almost swam in blood:

[023-061] Thy heart had leap’d the hero to survey,

[023-062] Stern as the surly lion o’er his prey,

[023-063] Glorious in gore, now with sulphereous fire

[023-064] The dome he purges, now the flame aspires;

[023-065] Heap’d lie the dead without the palace walls—

[023-066] Haste, daughter, haste, thy own Ulysses calls!

[023-067] Thy every wish the bounteous gods bestow;

[023-068] Enjoy the present good, and former woe.

[023-069] Ulysses lives, his vanquish’d foes to see;

[023-070] He lives to thy [[telemachus|Telemachus]] and thee!”

[023-071] “Ah, no! (with sighs Penelope rejoin’d,)

[023-072] Excess of joy disturbs thy wandering mind;

[023-073] How blest this happy hour, should he appear,

[023-074] Dear to us all, to me supremely dear;

[023-075] Ah, no! some god the suitors death decreed,

[023-076] Some god descends, and by his hand they bleed;

[023-077] Blind! to contemn the stranger’s righteous cause,

[023-078] And violate all hospitable laws!

[023-079] The good they hated, and the powers defied!

[023-080] But heaven is just, and by a god they died.

[023-081] For never must Ulysses view this shore;

[023-082] Never! the loved Ulysses is no more!”

[023-083] “What words (the matron cries) have reach’d my ears?

[023-084] Doubt we his presence, when he now appears!

[023-085] Then hear conviction: Ere the fatal day

[023-086] That forced Ulysses o’er the watery way,

[023-087] A boar, fierce rushing in the sylvan war,

[023-088] Plough’d half his thigh; I saw, I saw the scar,

[023-089] And wild with transport had reveal’d the wound;

[023-090] But ere I spoke, he rose, and check’d the sound.

[023-091] Then, daughter, haste away! and if a lie

[023-092] Flow from this tongue, then let thy servant die!”

[023-093] To whom with dubious joy the queen replies:

[023-094] “Wise is thy soul, but errors seize the wise;

[023-095] The works of gods what mortal can survey?

[023-096] Who knows their motives, who shall trace their way?

[023-097] But learn we instant how the suitors trod

[023-098] The paths of death, by man, or by a god.”

[023-099] Thus speaks the queen, and no reply attends,

[023-100] But with alternate joy and fear descends;

[023-101] At every step debates her lord to prove;

[023-102] Or, rushing to his arms, confess her love!

[023-103] Then gliding through the marble valves, in state

[023-104] Opposed, before the shining sire she sate.

[023-105] The monarch, by a column high enthroned,

[023-106] His eye withdrew, and fix’d it on the ground;

[023-107] Curious to hear his queen the silence break:

[023-108] Amazed she sate, and impotent to speak;

[023-109] O’er all the man her eyes she rolls in vain,

[023-110] Now hopes, now fears, now knows, then doubts again.

[023-111] At length Telemachus: “Oh, who can find

[023-112] A woman like Penelope unkind?

[023-113] Why thus in silence? why with winning charms

[023-114] Thus slow to fly with rapture to his arms?

[023-115] Stubborn the breast that with no transport glows,

[023-116] When twice ten years are pass’d of mighty woes;

[023-117] To softness lost, to spousal love unknown,

[023-118] The gods have formed that rigid heart of stone!”

[023-119] “O my Telemachus! (the queen rejoin’d,)

[023-120] Distracting fears confound my labouring mind;

[023-121] Powerless to speak. I scarce uplift my eyes,

[023-122] Nor dare to question; doubts on doubts arise.

[023-123] Oh deign he, if Ulysses, to remove

[023-124] These boding thoughts, and what he is, to prove!”

[023-125] Pleased with her virtuous fears, the king replies:

[023-126] “Indulge, my son, the cautions of the wise;

[023-127] Time shall the truth to sure remembrance bring:

[023-128] This garb of poverty belies the king:

[023-129] No more. This day our deepest care requires,

[023-130] Cautious to act what thought mature inspires.

[023-131] If one man’s blood, though mean, distain our hands,

[023-132] The homicide retreats to foreign lands;

[023-133] By us, in heaps the illustrious peerage falls,

[023-134] The important deed our whole attention calls.”

## Section 56

[023-135] “Be that thy care (Telemachus replies)

[023-136] The world conspires to speak Ulysses wise;

[023-137] For wisdom all is thine! lo, I obey,

[023-138] And dauntless follow where you led the way;

[023-139] Nor shalt thou in the day of danger find

[023-140] Thy coward son degenerate lag behind.”

[023-141] “Then instant to the bath (the monarch cries),

[023-142] Bid the gay youth and sprightly virgins rise,

[023-143] Thence all descend in pomp and proud array,

[023-144] And bid the dome resound the mirthful lay;

[023-145] While the sweet lyrist airs of rapture sings,

[023-146] And forms the dance responsive to the strings,

[023-147] That hence the eluded passengers may say,

[023-148] ‘Lo! the queen weds! we hear the spousal lay!’

[023-149] The suitor’s death, unknown, till we remove

[023-150] Far from the court, and act inspired by [[zeus|Jove]].”

[023-151] Thus spoke the king: the observant train obey,

[023-152] At once they bathe, and dress in proud array:

[023-153] The lyrist strikes the string; gay youths advance,

[023-154] And fair-zoned damsels form the sprightly dance.

[023-155] The voice, attuned to instrumental sounds,

[023-156] Ascends the roof, the vaulted roof rebounds;

[023-157] Not unobserved: the Greeks eluded say,

[023-158] “Lo! the queen weds, we hear the spousal lay!

[023-159] Inconstant! to admit the bridal hour.”

[023-160] Thus they—but nobly chaste she weds no more.

[023-161] Meanwhile the wearied king the bath ascends;

[023-162] With faithful cares [[eurynome|Eurynome]] attends,

[023-163] O’er every limb a shower of fragrance sheds;

[023-164] Then, dress’d in pomp, magnificent he treads.

[023-165] The warrior-goddess gives his frame to shine

[023-166] With majesty enlarged, and grace divine.

[023-167] Back from his brows in wavy ringlets fly

[023-168] His thick large locks of hyacinthine dye.

[023-169] As by some artist to whom Vulcan gives

[023-170] His heavenly skill, a breathing image lives;

[023-171] By Pallas taught, he frames the wondrous mould,

[023-172] And the pale silver glows with fusile gold:

[023-173] So Pallas his heroic form improves

[023-174] With bloom divine, and like a god he moves!

[023-175] More high he treads, and issuing forth in state,

[023-176] Radiant before his gazing consort sate.

[023-177] “And, O my queen! (he cries) what power above

[023-178] Has steel’d that heart, averse to spousal love?

[023-179] Canst thou, Penelope, when heaven restores

[023-180] Thy lost Ulysses to his native shores,

[023-181] Canst thou, O cruel! unconcern’d survey

[023-182] Thy lost Ulysses, on this signal day?

[023-183] Haste, Euryclea, and despatchful spread

[023-184] For me, and me alone, the imperial bed,

[023-185] My weary nature craves the balm of rest.

[023-186] But Heaven with adamant has arm’d her breast.”

[023-187] “Ah no! (she cries) a tender heart I bear,

[023-188] A foe to pride: no adamant is there;

[023-189] And now, e’en now it melts! for sure I see

[023-190] Once more Ulysses my beloved in thee!

[023-191] Fix’d in my soul, as when he sailed to Troy,

[023-192] His image dwells: then haste the bed of joy,

[023-193] Haste, from the bridal bower the bed translate,

[023-194] Fram’d by his hand, and be it dress’d in state!”

[023-195] Thus speaks the queen, still dubious, with disguise

[023-196] Touch’d at her words, the king with warmth replies

[023-197] “Alas for this! what mortal strength can move

[023-198] The enormous burden, who but Heaven above?

[023-199] It mocks the weak attempts of human hands!

[023-200] But the whole earth must move if Heaven commands

[023-201] Then hear sure evidence, while we display

[023-202] Words seal’d with sacred truth and truth obey:

[023-203] This hand the wonder framed; an olive spread

[023-204] Full in the court its ever verdant head.

[023-205] Vast as some mighty column’s bulk, on high

[023-206] The huge trunk rose, and heaved into the sky;

[023-207] Around the tree I raised a nuptial bower,

[023-208] And roof’d defensive of the storm and shower;

[023-209] The spacious valve, with art inwrought conjoins;

[023-210] And the fair dome with polished marble shines.

[023-211] I lopp’d the branchy head: aloft in twain

[023-212] Sever’d the bole, and smoothed the shining grain;

[023-213] Then posts, capacious of the frame, I raise,

[023-214] And bore it, regular, from space to space:

[023-215] Athwart the frame, at equal distance lie

[023-216] Thongs of tough hides, that boast a purple dye;

[023-217] Then polishing the whole, the finished mould

[023-218] With silver shone, with elephant, and gold.

[023-219] But if o’erturn’d by rude, ungovern’d hands,

[023-220] Or still inviolate the olive stands,

[023-221] ’Tis thine, O queen, to say, and now impart,

[023-222] If fears remain, or doubts distract thy heart.”

[023-223] While yet he speaks, her powers of life decay;

[023-224] She sickens, trembles, falls, and faints away.

[023-225] At length recovering, to his arms she flew,

[023-226] And strain’d him close, as to his breast she grew.

[023-227] The tears pour’d down amain, and “O (she cries)

[023-228] Let not against thy spouse thine anger rise!

[023-229] O versed in every turn of human art,

[023-230] Forgive the weakness of a woman’s heart!

[023-231] The righteous powers, that mortal lot dispose,

[023-232] Decree us to sustain a length of woes.

[023-233] And from the flower of life the bliss deny

[023-234] To bloom together, fade away, and die.

[023-235] O let me, let me not thine anger move,

[023-236] That I forbore, thus, thus to speak my love:

[023-237] Thus in fond kisses, while the transport warms

[023-238] Pour out my soul and die within thine arms!

[023-239] I dreaded fraud! Men, faithless men, betray

[023-240] Our easy faith, and make our sex their prey:

[023-241] Against the fondness of my heart I strove:

[023-242] ’Twas caution, O my lord! not want of love.

[023-243] Like me had [[helen|Helen]] fear’d, with wanton charms

[023-244] Ere the fair mischief set two worlds in arms;

[023-245] Ere Greece rose dreadful in the avenging day;

[023-246] Thus had she fear’d, she had not gone astray.

[023-247] But Heaven, averse to Greece, in wrath decreed

[023-248] That she should wander, and that Greece should bleed:

[023-249] Blind to the ills that from injustice flow,

[023-250] She colour’d all our wretched lives with woe.

[023-251] But why these sorrows when my lord arrives?

[023-252] I yield, I yield! my own Ulysses lives!

[023-253] The secrets of the bridal bed are known

[023-254] To thee, to me, to Actoris alone

[023-255] (My father’s present in the spousal hour,

[023-256] The sole attendant on our genial bower).

[023-257] Since what no eye hath seen thy tongue reveal’d,

[023-258] Hard and distrustful as I am, I yield.”

[023-259] Touch’d to the soul, the king with rapture hears,

[023-260] Hangs round her neck, and speaks his joy in tears.

[023-261] As to the shipwreck’d mariner, the shores

[023-262] Delightful rise, when angry [[poseidon|Neptune]] roars:

[023-263] Then, when the surge in thunder mounts the sky,

[023-264] And gulf’d in crowds at once the sailors die;

[023-265] If one, more happy, while the tempest raves,

[023-266] Outlives the tumult of conflicting waves,

[023-267] All pale, with ooze deform’d, he views the strand,

[023-268] And plunging forth with transport grasps the land:

[023-269] The ravish’d queen with equal rapture glows,

[023-270] Clasps her loved lord, and to his bosom grows.

[023-271] Nor had they ended till the morning ray,

[023-272] But Pallas backward held the rising day,

[023-273] The wheels of night retarding, to detain

[023-274] The gay Aurora in the wavy main;

[023-275] Whose flaming steeds, emerging through the night,

[023-276] Beam o’er the eastern hills with streaming light.

[023-277] At length Ulysses with a sigh replies:

[023-278] “Yet [[moira|Fate]], yet cruel Fate repose denies;

[023-279] A labour long, and hard, remains behind;

[023-280] By heaven above, by hell beneath enjoin’d:

[023-281] For to [[tiresias|Tiresias]] through the eternal gates

[023-282] Of hell I trode, to learn my future fates.

[023-283] But end we here—the night demands repose,

[023-284] Be deck’d the couch! and peace awhile, my woes!”

[023-285] To whom the queen: “Thy word we shall obey,

[023-286] And deck the couch; far hence be woes away:

[023-287] Since the just gods, who tread the starry plains,

[023-288] Restore thee safe, since my Ulysses reigns.

[023-289] But what those perils heaven decrees, impart;

[023-290] Knowledge may grieve, but fear distracts the heart.”

[023-291] To this the king: “Ah, why must I disclose

[023-292] A dreadful story of approaching woes?

[023-293] Why in this hour of transport wound thy ears,

[023-294] When thou must learn what I must speak with tears?

[023-295] Heaven, by the Theban ghost, thy spouse decrees,

[023-296] Torn from thy arms, to sail a length of seas;

[023-297] From realm to realm, a nation to explore

[023-298] Who ne’er knew salt, or heard the billows roar,

[023-299] Nor saw gay vessel storm the surgy plain,

[023-300] A painted wonder, flying on the main:

[023-301] An oar my hand must bear; a shepherd eyes

[023-302] The unknown instrument with strange surprise,

[023-303] And calls a corn-van; this upon the plain

[023-304] I fix, and hail the monarch of the main;

[023-305] Then bathe his altars with the mingled gore

[023-306] Of victims vow’d, a ram, a bull, a boar;

[023-307] Thence swift re-sailing to my native shores,

[023-308] Due victims slay to all the ethereal powers.

[023-309] Then Heaven decrees, in peace to end my days

[023-310] And steal myself from life by slow decays!

[023-311] Unknown to pain, in age resign my breath,

[023-312] When late stern Neptune points the shaft of death;

[023-313] To the dark grave retiring as to rest;

[023-314] My people blessing, by my people bless’d.

[023-315] Such future scenes the all-righteous powers display

[023-316] By their dread seer, and such my future day.”

[023-317] To whom thus firm of soul: “If ripe for death,

[023-318] And full of days, thou gently yield thy breath;

[023-319] While Heaven a kind release from ills foreshows,

[023-320] Triumph, thou happy victor of thy woes?”

[023-321] But Euryclea, with dispatchful care,

[023-322] And sage Eurynome, the couch prepare;

[023-323] Instant they bid the blazing torch display

[023-324] Around the dome and artificial day;

[023-325] Then to repose her steps the matron bends,

[023-326] And to the queen Eurynome descends;

[023-327] A torch she bears, to light with guiding fires

[023-328] The royal pair; she guides them, and retires

[023-329] The instant his fair spouse Ulysses led

[023-330] To the chaste love-rites of the nuptial bed.

[023-331] And now the blooming youths and sprightly fair

[023-332] Cease the gay dance, and to their rest repair;

[023-333] But in discourse the king and consort lay,

[023-334] While the soft hours stole unperceived away;

[023-335] Intent he hears Penelope disclose

[023-336] A mournful story of domestic woes,

[023-337] His servants’ insults, his invaded bed,

[023-338] How his whole flocks and herds exhausted bled,

[023-339] His generous wines dishonour’d shed in vain,

[023-340] And the wild riots of the suitor-train.

[023-341] The king alternate a dire tale relates,

[023-342] Of wars, of triumphs, and disastrous fates;

[023-343] All he unfolds; his listening spouse turns pale

[023-344] With pleasing horror at the dreadful tale;

[023-345] Sleepless devours each word; and hears how slain

[023-346] Cicons on Cicons swell the ensanguined plain;

[023-347] How to the land of Lote unbless’d he sails;

[023-348] And images the rills and flowery vales!

[023-352] From friendly [[aeolus|Aeolus]] with prosperous gales:

[023-354] And whirls him groaning from his native shores:

[023-355] How on the barbarous Laestrigonian coast,

[023-356] By savage hands his fleet and friends lie lost;

[023-357] How scarce himself survived: he paints the bower,

[023-373] Where the gay blooming nymph constrain’d his stay,

[023-374] With sweet, reluctant, amorous delay;

[023-382] A sweet forgetfulness of all his cares.

[023-383] Soon as soft slumber eased the toils of day,

[023-384] Minerva rushes through the aërial way,

[023-385] And bids Aurora with her golden wheels

[023-386] Flame from the ocean o’er the eastern hills;

[023-387] Uprose Ulysses from the genial bed,

[023-388] And thus with thought mature the monarch said:

[023-389] “My queen, my consort! through a length of years

[023-391] Thou, for thy lord; while me the immortal powers

[023-392] Detain’d reluctant from my native shores.

[023-393] Now, bless’d again by Heaven, the queen display,

[023-397] But now I haste to bless [[laertes|Laertes]]’ eyes

[023-399] The good old man, to wasting woes a prey,

[023-400] Weeps a sad life in solitude away.

[023-401] But hear, though wise! This morning shall unfold

[023-402] The deathful scene, on heroes heroes roll’d.

[023-403] Thou with thy maids within the palace stay,

[023-404] From all the scene of tumult far away!”

## Section 57
