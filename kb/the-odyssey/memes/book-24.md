[024-001] BOOK XXIV.

[024-002] ARGUMENT.

[024-003] The souls of the suitors are conducted by [[hermes|Mercury]] to the infernal

[024-004] shades. [[odysseus|Ulysses]] in the country goes to the retirement of his father,

[024-005] [[laertes|Laertes]]; he finds him busied in his garden all alone; the manner of his

[024-006] discovery to him is beautifully described. They return together to his

[024-007] lodge, and the king is acknowledged by [[dolius|Dolius]] and the servants. The

[024-008] Ithacensians, led by Eupithes, the father of [[antinous|Antinous]], rise against

[024-009] Ulysses, who gives them battle in which Eupithes is killed by Laertes:

[024-010] and the goddess Pallas makes a lasting peace between Ulysses and his

[024-011] subjects, which concludes the [[the-odyssey|Odyssey]].

[024-012] Cyllenius now to Pluto’s dreary reign

[024-013] Conveys the dead, a lamentable train!

[024-014] The golden wand, that causes sleep to fly,

[024-015] Or in soft slumber seals the wakeful eye,

[024-016] That drives the ghosts to realms of night or day,

[024-017] Points out the long uncomfortable way.

[024-018] Trembling the spectres glide, and plaintive vent

[024-019] Thin, hollow screams, along the deep descent.

[024-020] As in the cavern of some rifted den,

[024-021] Where flock nocturnal bats, and birds obscene;

[024-022] Cluster’d they hang, till at some sudden shock

[024-023] They move, and murmurs run through all the rock!

[024-024] So cowering fled the sable heaps of ghosts,

[024-025] And such a scream fill’d all the dismal coasts.

[024-026] And now they reach’d the earth’s remotest ends,

[024-027] And now the gates where evening [[helios|Sol]] descends,

[024-028] And Leucas’ rock, and [[oceanus|Ocean]]’s utmost streams,

[024-029] And now pervade the dusky land of dreams,

[024-030] And rest at last, where souls unbodied dwell

[024-031] In ever-flowing meads of asphodel.

[024-032] The empty forms of men inhabit there,

[024-033] Impassive semblance, images of air!

[024-034] Naught else are all that shined on earth before:

[024-035] [[ajax|Ajax]] and [[achilles|great Achilles]] are no more!

[024-036] Yet still a master ghost, the rest he awed,

[024-037] The rest adored him, towering as he trod;

[024-038] Still at his side is [[nestor|Nestor]]’s son survey’d,

[024-039] And loved [[patroclus|Patroclus]] still attends his shade.

[024-040] New as they were to that infernal shore,

[024-041] The suitors stopp’d, and gazed the hero o’er.

[024-042] When, moving slow, the regal form they view’d

[024-043] Of great [[agamemnon|Atrides]]: him in pomp pursued

[024-044] And solemn sadness through the gloom of hell,

[024-045] The train of those who by AEgysthus fell:

[024-046] “O mighty chief! (Pelides thus began)

[024-047] Honour’d by [[zeus|Jove]] above the lot of man!

[024-048] King of a hundred kings! to whom resign’d

[024-049] The strongest, bravest, greatest of mankind

[024-050] Comest thou the first, to view this dreary state?

[024-051] And was the noblest, the first mark of [[moira|Fate]],

[024-052] Condemn’d to pay the great arrear so soon,

[024-053] The lot, which all lament, and none can shun!

[024-054] Oh! better hadst thou sunk in Trojan ground,

[024-055] With all thy full-blown honours cover’d round;

[024-056] Then grateful Greece with streaming eyes might raise

[024-057] Historic marbles to record thy praise:

[024-058] Thy praise eternal on the faithful stone

[024-059] Had with transmissive glories graced thy son.

[024-060] But heavier fates were destined to attend:

[024-061] What man is happy, till he knows his end?”

[024-062] “O son of Peleus! greater than mankind!

[024-063] (Thus Agamemnon’s kingly shade rejoin’d)

[024-064] Thrice happy thou, to press the martial plain

[024-065] ’Midst heaps of heroes in thy quarrel slain:

[024-066] In clouds of smoke raised by the noble fray,

[024-067] Great and terrific e’en in death you lay,

[024-068] And deluges of blood flow’d round you every way.

[024-069] Nor ceased the strife till Jove himself opposed,

[024-070] And all in Tempests the dire evening closed.

[024-071] Then to the fleet we bore thy honour’d load,

[024-072] And decent on the funeral bed bestow’d;

[024-073] Then unguents sweet and tepid streams we shed;

[024-074] Tears flow’d from every eye, and o’er the dead

[024-075] Each clipp’d the curling honours of his head.

[024-076] Struck at the news, thy azure mother came,

[024-077] The sea-green sisters waited on the dame:

[024-078] A voice of loud lament through all the main

[024-079] Was heard; and terror seized the Grecian train:

[024-080] Back to their ships the frighted host had fled;

[024-081] But Nestor spoke, they listen’d and obey’d

[024-082] (From old experience Nestor’s counsel springs,

[024-083] And long vicissitudes of human things):

[024-084] ‘Forbear your flight: fair Thetis from the main

[024-085] To mourn Achilles leads her azure train.’

[024-086] Around thee stand the daughters of the deep,

[024-087] Robe thee in heavenly vests, and round thee weep:

[024-088] Round thee, the Muses, with alternate strain,

[024-089] In ever-consecrating verse, complain.

[024-090] Each warlike Greek the moving music hears,

[024-091] And iron-hearted heroes melt in tears.

[024-092] Till seventeen nights and seventeen days return’d

[024-093] All that was mortal or immortal mourn’d,

[024-094] To flames we gave thee, the succeeding day,

[024-095] And fatted sheep and sable oxen slay;

[024-096] With oils and honey blazed the augmented fires,

[024-097] And, like a god adorn’d, thy earthly part expires.

[024-098] Unnumber’d warriors round the burning pile

[024-099] Urge the fleet coursers or the racer’s toil;

[024-100] Thick clouds of dust o’er all the circle rise,

[024-101] And the mix’d clamour thunders in the skies.

[024-102] Soon as absorb’d in all-embracing flame

[024-103] Sunk what was mortal of thy mighty name,

[024-104] We then collect thy snowy bones, and place

[024-105] With wines and unguents in a golden vase

[024-106] (The vase to Thetis Bacchus gave of old,

[024-107] And Vulcan’s art enrich’d the sculptured gold).

[024-108] There, we thy relics, great Achilles! blend

[024-109] With dear Patroclus, thy departed friend:

[024-110] In the same urn a separate space contains

[024-111] Thy next beloved, [[antilochus|Antilochus]]’ remains.

[024-112] Now all the sons of warlike Greece surround

[024-113] Thy destined tomb and cast a mighty mound;

[024-114] High on the shore the growing hill we raise,

[024-115] That wide the extended Hellespont surveys;

[024-116] Where all, from age to age, who pass the coast,

[024-117] May point Achilles’ tomb, and hail the mighty ghost.

[024-118] Thetis herself to all our peers proclaims

[024-119] Heroic prizes and exequial games;

[024-120] The gods assented; and around thee lay

[024-121] Rich spoils and gifts that blazed against the day.

[024-122] Oft have I seen with solemn funeral games

[024-123] Heroes and kings committed to the flames;

[024-124] But strength of youth, or valour of the brave,

[024-125] With nobler contest ne’er renown’d a grave.

[024-126] Such were the games by azure Thetis given,

[024-127] And such thy honours, O beloved of Heaven!

[024-128] Dear to mankind thy fame survives, nor fades

[024-129] Its bloom eternal in the Stygian shades.

[024-130] But what to me avail my honours gone,

[024-131] Successful toils, and battles bravely won?

[024-132] Doom’d by stern Jove at home to end my life,

[024-133] By cursed Ægysthus, and a faithless wife!”

[024-134] Thus they: while Hermes o’er the dreary plain

[024-135] Led the sad numbers by Ulysses slain.

[024-136] On each majestic form they cast a view,

[024-137] And timorous pass’d, and awfully withdrew.

[024-138] But Agamemnon, through the gloomy shade,

[024-139] His ancient host Amphimedon survey’d:

[024-140] “Son of [[melanthius|Melanthius]]! (he began) O say!

[024-141] What cause compell’d so many, and so gay,

[024-142] To tread the downward, melancholy way?

[024-143] Say, could one city yield a troop so fair?

[024-144] Were all these partners of one native air?

[024-145] Or did the rage of stormy [[poseidon|Neptune]] sweep

[024-146] Your lives at once, and whelm beneath the deep?

[024-147] Did nightly thieves, or pirates’ cruel bands,

[024-148] Drench with your blood your pillaged country’s sands?

[024-149] Or well-defending some beleaguer’d wall,

[024-150] Say,—for the public did ye greatly fall?

[024-151] Inform thy guest: for such I was of yore

[024-152] When our triumphant navies touch’d your shore;

[024-153] Forced a long month the wintry seas to bear,

[024-154] To move the great Ulysses to the war.”

[024-155] “O king of men! I faithful shall relate

[024-156] (Replied Amphimedon) our hapless fate.

[024-157] Ulysses absent, our ambitious aim

[024-158] With rival loves pursued his royal dame;

[024-159] Her coy reserve, and prudence mix’d with pride,

[024-160] Our common suit nor granted, nor denied;

[024-161] But close with inward hate our deaths design’d;

[024-162] Versed in all arts of wily womankind.

[024-163] Her hand, laborious, in delusion spread

[024-164] A spacious loom, and mix’d the various thread.

[024-165] ‘Ye peers (she cried) who press to gain my heart,

[024-166] Where dead Ulysses claims no more a part,

[024-167] Yet a short space your rival suit suspend,

[024-168] Till this funereal web my labours end:

[024-169] Cease, till to good Laertes I bequeath

[024-170] A task of grief, his ornaments of death:

[024-171] Lest when the Fates his royal ashes claim,

[024-172] The Grecian matrons taint my spotless fame;

[024-173] Should he, long honour’d with supreme command,

[024-174] Want the last duties of a daughter’s hand.’

[024-175] “The fiction pleased, our generous train complies,

[024-176] Nor fraud mistrusts in virtue’s fair disguise.

[024-177] The work she plied, but studious of delay,

[024-178] Each following night reversed the toils of day.

[024-179] Unheard, unseen, three years her arts prevail;

[024-180] The fourth, her maid reveal’d the amazing tale,

[024-181] And show’d as unperceived we took our stand,

[024-182] The backward labours of her faithless hand.

[024-183] Forced she completes it; and before us lay

[024-184] The mingled web, whose gold and silver ray

[024-185] Display’d the radiance of the night and day.

[024-186] “Just as she finished her illustrious toil,

[024-187] Ill fortune led Ulysses to our isle.

[024-188] Far in a lonely nook, beside the sea,

[024-189] At an old swineherd’s rural lodge he lay:

[024-190] Thither his son from sandy Pyle repairs,

[024-191] And speedy lands, and secretly confers.

[024-192] They plan our future ruin, and resort

[024-193] Confederate to the city and the court.

[024-194] First came the son; the father next succeeds,

[024-195] Clad like a beggar, whom [[eumaeus|Eumaeus]] leads;

[024-196] Propp’d on a staff, deform’d with age and care,

[024-197] And hung with rags that flutter’d in the air.

[024-198] Who could Ulysses in that form behold?

[024-199] Scorn’d by the young, forgotten by the old,

[024-200] Ill-used by all! to every wrong resigned,

[024-201] Patient he suffered with a constant mind.

[024-202] But when, arising in his wrath to obey

[024-203] The will of Jove, he gave the vengeance way:

[024-204] The scattered arms that hung around the dome

[024-205] Careful he treasured in a private room;

[024-206] Then to her suitors bade his queen propose

[024-207] The archer’s strife, the source of future woes,

[024-208] And omen of our death! In vain we drew

[024-209] The twanging string, and tried the stubborn yew:

[024-210] To none it yields but great Ulysses’ hands;

[024-211] In vain we threat; [[telemachus|Telemachus]] commands:

[024-212] The bow he snatch’d, and in an instant bent;

[024-213] Through every ring the victor arrow went.

[024-214] Fierce on the threshold then in arms he stood;

[024-215] Poured forth the darts that thirsted for our blood,

[024-216] And frown’d before us, dreadful as a god!

[024-217] First bleeds Antinous: thick the shafts resound,

[024-218] And heaps on heaps the wretches strew the ground;

[024-219] This way, and that, we turn, we fly, we fall;

[024-220] Some god assisted, and unmann’d us all;

[024-221] Ignoble cries precede the dying groans;

[024-222] And battered brains and blood besmear the stones.

[024-223] “Thus, great [[menelaus|Atrides]], thus Ulysses drove

[024-224] The shades thou seest from yon fair realms above;

[024-225] Our mangled bodies now deformed with gore,

[024-226] Cold and neglected, spread the marble floor.

[024-227] No friend to bathe our wounds, or tears to shed

[024-228] O’er the pale corse! the honours of the dead.”

[024-229] “Oh bless’d Ulysses! (thus the king express’d

[024-230] His sudden rapture) in thy consort bless’d!

[024-231] Not more thy wisdom than her virtue shined;

[024-232] Not more thy patience than her constant mind.

[024-233] [[icarius|Icarius]]’ daughter, glory of the past,

[024-234] And model to the future age, shall last:

[024-235] The gods, to honour her fair fame, shall rise

[024-236] (Their great reward) a poet in her praise.

[024-237] Not such, O Tyndarus! thy daughter’s deed,

[024-238] By whose dire hand her king and husband bled;

[024-239] Her shall the Muse to infamy prolong,

[024-240] Example dread, and theme of tragic song!

[024-241] The general sex shall suffer in her shame,

[024-242] And e’en the best that bears a woman’s name.”

[024-243] Thus in the regions of eternal shade

[024-244] Conferr’d the mournful phantoms of the dead;

[024-245] While from the town, Ulysses and his band

[024-246] Pass’d to Laertes’ cultivated land.

[024-247] The ground himself had purchased with his pain,

[024-248] And labour made the rugged soil a plain,

[024-249] There stood his mansion of the rural sort,

[024-250] With useful buildings round the lowly court;

[024-251] Where the few servants that divide his care

[024-252] Took their laborious rest, and homely fare;

[024-253] And one Sicilian matron, old and sage,

[024-254] With constant duty tends his drooping age.

## Section 58

[024-255] Here now arriving, to his rustic band

[024-256] And martial son, Ulysses gave command:

[024-257] “Enter the house, and of the bristly swine

[024-258] Select the largest to the powers divine.

[024-259] Alone, and unattended, let me try

[024-260] If yet I share the old man’s memory:

[024-261] If those dim eyes can yet Ulysses know

[024-262] (Their light and dearest object long ago),

[024-263] Now changed with time, with absence and with woe.”

[024-264] Then to his train he gives his spear and shield;

[024-265] The house they enter; and he seeks the field,

[024-266] Through rows of shade, with various fruitage crown’d,

[024-267] And labour’d scenes of richest verdure round.

[024-268] Nor aged Dolius; nor his sons, were there,

[024-269] Nor servants, absent on another care;

[024-270] To search the woods for sets of flowery thorn,

[024-271] Their orchard bounds to strengthen and adorn.

[024-272] But all alone the hoary king he found;

[024-273] His habit course, but warmly wrapp’d around;

[024-274] His head, that bow’d with many a pensive care,

[024-275] Fenced with a double cap of goatskin hair:

[024-276] His buskins old, in former service torn,

[024-277] But swell repair’d; and gloves against the thorn.

[024-278] In this array the kingly gardener stood,

[024-279] And clear’d a plant, encumber’d with its wood.

[024-280] Beneath a neighbouring tree, the chief divine

[024-281] Gazed o’er his sire, retracing every line,

[024-282] The ruins of himself, now worn away

[024-283] With age, yet still majestic in decay!

[024-284] Sudden his eyes released their watery store;

[024-285] The much-enduring man could bear no more.

[024-286] Doubtful he stood, if instant to embrace

[024-287] His aged limbs, to kiss his reverend face,

[024-288] With eager transport to disclose the whole,

[024-289] And pour at once the torrent of his soul.—

[024-290] Not so: his judgment takes the winding way

[024-291] Of question distant, and of soft essay;

[024-292] More gentle methods on weak age employs:

[024-293] And moves the sorrows to enhance the joys.

[024-294] Then, to his sire with beating heart he moves,

[024-295] And with a tender pleasantry reproves;

[024-296] Who digging round the plant still hangs his bead,

[024-297] Nor aught remits the work, while thus he said:

[024-298] “Great is thy skill, O father! great thy toil,

[024-299] Thy careful hand is stamp’d on all the soil,

[024-300] Thy squadron’d vineyards well thy art declare,

[024-301] The olive green, blue fig, and pendent pear;

[024-302] And not one empty spot escapes thy care.

[024-303] On every plant and tree thy cares are shown,

[024-304] Nothing neglected, but thyself alone.

[024-305] Forgive me, father, if this fault I blame;

[024-306] Age so advanced, may some indulgence claim.

[024-307] Not for thy sloth, I deem thy lord unkind:

[024-308] Nor speaks thy form a mean or servile mind;

[024-309] I read a monarch in that princely air,

[024-310] The same thy aspect, if the same thy care;

[024-311] Soft sleep, fair garments, and the joys of wine,

[024-312] These are the rights of age, and should be thine.

[024-313] Who then thy master, say? and whose the land

[024-314] So dress’d and managed by thy skilful hand?

[024-315] But chief, oh tell me! (what I question most)

[024-316] Is this the far-famed Ithacensian coast?

[024-317] For so reported the first man I view’d

[024-318] (Some surly islander, of manners rude),

[024-319] Nor farther conference vouchsafed to stay;

[024-320] Heedless he whistled, and pursued his way.

[024-321] But thou whom years have taught to understand,

[024-322] Humanely hear, and answer my demand:

[024-323] A friend I seek, a wise one and a brave:

[024-324] Say, lives he yet, or moulders in the grave?

[024-325] Time was (my fortunes then were at the best)

[024-326] When at my house I lodged this foreign guest;

[024-327] He said, from [[ithaca|Ithaca]]’s fair isle he came,

[024-328] And old Laertes was his father’s name.

[024-329] To him, whatever to a guest is owed

[024-330] I paid, and hospitable gifts bestow’d:

[024-331] To him seven talents of pure ore I told,

[024-332] Twelve cloaks, twelve vests, twelve tunics stiff with gold:

[024-333] A bowl, that rich with polish’d silver flames,

[024-334] And skill’d in female works, four lovely dames.”

[024-335] At this the father, with a father’s fears

[024-336] (His venerable eyes bedimm’d with tears):

[024-337] “This is the land; but ah! thy gifts are lost,

[024-338] For godless men, and rude possess the coast:

[024-339] Sunk is the glory of this once-famed shore!

[024-340] Thy ancient friend, O stranger, is no more!

[024-341] Full recompense thy bounty else had borne:

[024-342] For every good man yields a just return:

[024-343] So civil rights demand; and who begins

[024-344] The track of friendship, not pursuing, sins.

[024-345] But tell me, stranger, be the truth confess’d,

[024-346] What years have circled since thou saw’st that guest?

[024-347] That hapless guest, alas! for ever gone!

[024-348] Wretch that he was! and that I am! my son!

[024-349] If ever man to misery was born,

[024-350] ’Twas his to suffer, and ’tis mine to mourn!

[024-351] Far from his friends, and from his native reign,

[024-352] He lies a prey to monsters of the main;

[024-353] Or savage beasts his mangled relics tear,

[024-354] Or screaming vultures scatter through the air:

[024-355] Nor could his mother funeral unguents shed;

[024-356] Nor wail’d his father o’er the untimely dead:

[024-357] Nor his sad consort, on the mournful bier,

[024-358] Seal’d his cold eyes, or dropp’d a tender tear!

[024-359] “But, tell me who thou art? and what thy race?

[024-360] Thy town, thy parents, and thy native place?

[024-361] Or, if a merchant in pursuit of gain,

[024-362] What port received thy vessel from the main?

[024-363] Or comest thou single, or attend thy train?”

[024-364] Then thus the son: “From Alybas I came,

[024-365] My palace there; Eperitus my name

[024-366] Not vulgar born: from Aphidas, the king

[024-367] Of Polyphemon’s royal line, I spring.

[024-368] Some adverse demon from Sicania bore

[024-369] Our wandering course, and drove us on your shore;

[024-370] Far from the town, an unfrequented bay

[024-371] Relieved our wearied vessel from the sea.

[024-372] Five years have circled since these eyes pursued

[024-373] Ulysses parting through the sable flood:

[024-374] Prosperous he sail’d, with dexter auguries,

[024-375] And all the wing’d good omens of the skies.

[024-376] Well hoped we then to meet on this fair shore,

[024-377] Whom Heaven, alas! decreed to meet no more.”

[024-378] Quick through the father’s heart these accents ran;

[024-379] Grief seized at once, and wrapp’d up all the man:

[024-380] Deep from his soul he sigh’d, and sorrowing spread

[024-381] A cloud of ashes on his hoary head.

[024-382] Trembling with agonies of strong delight

[024-383] Stood the great son, heart-wounded with the sight:

[024-384] He ran, he seized him with a strict embrace,

[024-385] With thousand kisses wander’d o’er his face:

[024-386] “I, I am he; O father, rise! behold

[024-387] Thy son, with twenty winters now grown old;

[024-388] Thy son, so long desired, so long detain’d,

[024-389] Restored, and breathing in his native land:

[024-390] These floods of sorrow, O my sire, restrain!

[024-391] The vengeance is complete; the suitor train,

[024-392] Stretch’d in our palace, by these hands lie slain.”

[024-393] Amazed, Laertes: “Give some certain sign

[024-394] (If such thou art) to manifest thee mine.”

[024-395] “Lo here the wound (he cries) received of yore,

[024-396] The scar indented by the tusky boar,

[024-397] When, by thyself, and by [[anticlea|Anticlea]] sent,

[024-398] To old Autolycus’ realms I went.

[024-399] Yet by another sign thy offspring know;

[024-400] The several trees you gave me long ago,

[024-401] While yet a child, these fields I loved to trace,

[024-402] And trod thy footsteps with unequal pace;

[024-403] To every plant in order as we came,

[024-404] Well-pleased, you told its nature and its name,

[024-405] Whate’er my childish fancy ask’d, bestow’d:

[024-406] Twelve pear-trees, bowing with their pendent load,

[024-407] And ten, that red with blushing apples glow’d;

[024-408] Full fifty purple figs; and many a row

[024-409] Of various vines that then began to blow,

[024-410] A future vintage! when the Hours produce

[024-411] Their latent buds, and Sol exalts the juice.”

[024-412] Smit with the signs which all his doubts explain,

[024-413] His heart within him melts; his knees sustain

[024-414] Their feeble weight no more: his arms alone

[024-415] Support him, round the loved Ulysses thrown;

[024-416] He faints, he sinks, with mighty joys oppress’d:

[024-417] Ulysses clasps him to his eager breast.

[024-418] Soon as returning life regains its seat,

[024-419] And his breath lengthens, and his pulses beat:

[024-420] “Yes, I believe (he cries) almighty Jove!

[024-421] Heaven rules us yet, and gods there are above.

[024-422] ’Tis so—the suitors for their wrongs have paid—

[024-423] But what shall guard us, if the town invade?

[024-424] If, while the news through every city flies,

[024-425] All Ithaca and Cephalenia rise?”

[024-426] To this Ulysses: “As the gods shall please

[024-427] Be all the rest: and set thy soul at ease.

[024-428] Haste to the cottage by this orchard’s side,

[024-429] And take the banquet which our cares provide;

[024-430] There wait thy faithful band of rural friends,

[024-431] And there the young Telemachus attends.”

[024-432] Thus, having said, they traced the garden o’er

[024-433] And stooping entered at the lowly door.

[024-434] The swains and young Telemachus they found.

[024-435] The victim portion’d and the goblet crown’d.

[024-436] The hoary king, his old Sicilian maid

[024-437] Perfum’d and wash’d, and gorgeously arrayed.

[024-438] Pallas attending gives his frame to shine

[024-439] With awful port, and majesty divine;

[024-440] His gazing son admires the godlike grace,

[024-441] And air celestial dawning o’er his face.

[024-442] “What god (he cried) my father’s form improves!

[024-443] How high he treads and how enlarged he moves!”

[024-444] “Oh! would to all the deathless powers on high,

[024-445] Pallas and Jove, and him who gilds the sky!

[024-446] (Replied the king elated with his praise)

[024-447] My strength were still, as once in better days:

[024-448] When the bold Cephalens the leaguer form’d.

[024-449] And proud Nericus trembled as I storm’d.

[024-450] Such were I now, not absent from your deed

[024-451] When the last sun beheld the suitors bleed,

[024-452] This arm had aided yours, this hand bestrown

[024-453] Our shores with death, and push’d the slaughter on;

[024-454] Nor had the sire been separate from the son.”

[024-455] They communed thus; while homeward bent their way

[024-456] The swains, fatigued with labours of the day:

[024-457] Dolius, the first, the venerable man;

[024-458] And next his sons, a long succeeding train.

[024-459] For due refection to the bower they came,

[024-460] Call’d by the careful old Sicilian dame,

[024-461] Who nursed the children, and now tends the sire,

[024-462] They see their lord, they gaze, and they admire.

[024-463] On chairs and beds in order seated round,

[024-464] They share the gladsome board; the roofs resound,

[024-465] While thus Ulysses to his ancient friend:

[024-466] “Forbear your wonder, and the feast attend:

[024-467] The rites have waited long.” The chief commands

[024-468] Their love in vain; old Dolius spreads his hands,

[024-469] Springs to his master with a warm embrace,

[024-470] And fastens kisses on his hands and face;

[024-471] Then thus broke out: “O long, O daily mourn’d!

[024-472] Beyond our hopes, and to our wish return’d!

[024-473] Conducted sure by Heaven! for Heaven alone

[024-474] Could work this wonder: welcome to thy own!

[024-475] And joys and happiness attend thy throne!

[024-476] Who knows thy bless’d, thy wish’d return? oh say,

[024-477] To the chaste queen shall we the news convey?

[024-478] Or hears she, and with blessings loads the day?”

[024-479] “Dismiss that care, for to the royal bride

[024-480] Already is it known” (the king replied,

[024-481] And straight resumed his seat); while round him bows

[024-482] Each faithful youth, and breathes out ardent vows:

[024-483] Then all beneath their father take their place,

[024-484] Rank’d by their ages, and the banquet grace.

[024-485] Now flying [[kleos|Fame]] the swift report had spread

[024-486] Through all the city, of the suitors dead,

[024-487] In throngs they rise, and to the palace crowd;

[024-488] Their sighs were many and the tumult loud.

[024-489] Weeping they bear the mangled heaps of slain;

[024-490] Inhume the natives in their native plain,

[024-491] The rest in ships are wafted o’er the main.

[024-492] Then sad in council all the seniors sate,

[024-493] Frequent and full, assembled to debate:

[024-494] Amid the circle first Eupithes rose,

[024-495] Big was his eye with tears, his heart with woes:

[024-496] The bold Antinous was his age’s pride,

[024-497] The first who by Ulysses’ arrow died.

[024-498] Down his wan cheek the trickling torrent ran,

[024-499] As mixing words with sighs he thus began:

[024-500] “Great deeds, O friends! this wondrous man has wrought,

[024-501] And mighty blessings to his country brought!

[024-502] With ships he parted, and a numerous train,

[024-503] Those, and their ships, he buried in the main.

[024-504] Now he returns, and first essays his hand

[024-505] In the best blood of all his native land.

[024-506] Haste, then, and ere to neighbouring Pyle he flies,

[024-507] Or sacred Elis, to procure supplies;

[024-508] Arise (or ye for ever fall), arise!

[024-509] Shame to this age, and all that shall succeed!

[024-510] If unrevenged your sons and brothers bleed.

[024-511] Prove that we live, by vengeance on his head,

[024-512] Or sink at once forgotten with the dead.”

[024-513] Here ceased he, but indignant tears let fall

[024-514] Spoke when he ceased: dumb sorrow touch’d them all.

[024-515] When from the palace to the wondering throng

[024-516] Sage [[medon|Medon]] came, and [[phemius|Phemius]] came along

[024-517] (Restless and early sleep’s soft bands they broke);

[024-518] And Medon first the assembled chiefs bespoke;

## Section 59

[024-519] “Hear me, ye peers and elders of the land,

[024-520] Who deem this act the work of mortal hand;

[024-521] As o’er the heaps of death Ulysses strode,

[024-522] These eyes, these eyes beheld a present god,

[024-523] Who now before him, now beside him stood,

[024-524] Fought as he fought, and mark’d his way with blood:

[024-525] In vain old [[mentor|Mentor]]’s form the god belied;

[024-526] ’Twas Heaven that struck, and Heaven was on his side.”

[024-527] A sudden horror all the assembly shook,

[024-528] When slowly rising, [[halitherses|Halitherses]] spoke

[024-529] (Reverend and wise, whose comprehensive view

[024-530] At once the present and the future knew):

[024-531] “Me too, ye fathers, hear! from you proceed

[024-532] The ills ye mourn; your own the guilty deed.

[024-533] Ye gave your sons, your lawless sons, the rein

[024-534] (Oft warn’d by Mentor and myself in vain);

[024-535] An absent hero’s bed they sought to soil,

[024-536] An absent hero’s wealth they made their spoil;

[024-537] Immoderate riot, and intemperate lust!

[024-538] The offence was great, the punishment was just.

[024-539] Weigh then my counsels in an equal scale,

[024-540] Nor rush to ruin. [[dike|Justice]] will prevail.”

[024-541] His moderate words some better minds persuade:

[024-542] They part, and join him: but the number stay’d.

[024-543] They storm, they shout, with hasty frenzy fired,

[024-544] And second all Eupithes’ rage inspired.

[024-545] They case their limbs in brass; to arms they run;

[024-546] The broad effulgence blazes in the sun.

[024-547] Before the city, and in ample plain,

[024-548] They meet: Eupithes heads the frantic train.

[024-549] Fierce for his son, he breathes his threats in air;

[024-550] Fate bears them not, and Death attends him there.

[024-551] This pass’d on earth, while in the realms above

[024-552] [[athena|Minerva]] thus to cloud-compelling Jove!

[024-553] “May I presume to search thy secret soul?

[024-554] O Power Supreme, O Ruler of the whole!

[024-555] Say, hast thou doom’d to this divided state

[024-556] Or peaceful amity or stern debate?

[024-557] Declare thy purpose, for thy will is fate.”

[024-558] “Is not thy thought my own? (the god replies

[024-559] Who rolls the thunder o’er the vaulted skies;)

[024-560] Hath not long since thy knowing soul decreed

[024-561] The chief’s return should make the guilty bleed.

[024-562] ’Tis done, and at thy will the Fates succeed.

[024-563] Yet hear the issue: Since Ulysses’ hand

[024-564] Has slain the suitors, Heaven shall bless the land.

[024-565] None now the kindred of the unjust shall own;

[024-566] Forgot the slaughter’d brother and the son:

[024-567] Each future day increase of wealth shall bring,

[024-568] And o’er the past Oblivion stretch her wing.

[024-569] Long shall Ulysses in his empire rest,

[024-570] His people blessing, by his people bless’d.

[024-571] Let all be peace.”—He said, and gave the nod

[024-572] That binds the Fates; the sanction of the god

[024-573] And prompt to execute the eternal will,

[024-574] Descended Pallas from the Olympian hill.

[024-575] Now sat Ulysses at the rural feast

[024-576] The rage of hunger and of thirst repress’d:

[024-577] To watch the foe a trusty spy he sent:

[024-578] A son of Dolius on the message went,

[024-579] Stood in the way, and at a glance beheld

[024-580] The foe approach, embattled on the field.

[024-581] With backward step he hastens to the bower,

[024-582] And tells the news. They arm with all their power.

[024-583] Four friends alone Ulysses’ cause embrace,

[024-584] And six were all the sons of Dolius’ race:

[024-585] Old Dolius too his rusted arms put on;

[024-586] And, still more old, in arms Laertes shone.

[024-587] Trembling with warmth, the hoary heroes stand,

[024-588] And brazen panoply invests the band.

[024-589] The opening gates at once their war display:

[024-590] Fierce they rush forth: Ulysses leads the way.

[024-591] That moment joins them with celestial aid,

[024-592] In Mentor’s form, the Jove-descended maid:

[024-593] The suffering hero felt his patient breast

[024-594] Swell with new joy, and thus his son address’d:

[024-595] “Behold, Telemachus! (nor fear the sight,)

[024-596] The brave embattled, the grim front of fight!

[024-597] The valiant with the valiant must contend.

[024-598] Shame not the line whence glorious you descend.

[024-599] Wide o’er the world their martial fame was spread;

[024-600] Regard thyself, the living and the dead.”

[024-601] “Thy eyes, great father! on this battle cast,

[024-602] Shall learn from me [[penelope|Penelope]] was chaste.”

[024-603] So spoke Telemachus: the gallant boy

[024-604] Good old Laertes heard with panting joy.

[024-605] “And bless’d! thrice bless’d this happy day! (he cries,)

[024-606] The day that shows me, ere I close my eyes,

[024-607] A son and grandson of the Arcesian name

[024-608] Strive for fair virtue, and contest for fame!”

[024-609] Then thus Minerva in Laertes’ ear:

[024-610] “Son of Arcesius, reverend warrior, hear!

[024-611] Jove and Jove’s daughter first implore in prayer,

[024-612] Then, whirling high, discharge thy lance in air.”

[024-613] She said, infusing courage with the word.

[024-614] Jove and Jove’s daughter then the chief implored,

[024-615] And, whirling high, dismiss’d the lance in air.

[024-616] Full at Eupithes drove the deathful spear:

[024-617] The brass-cheek’d helmet opens to the wound;

[024-618] He falls, earth thunders, and his arms resound.

[024-619] Before the father and the conquering son

[024-620] Heaps rush on heaps, they fight, they drop, they run

[024-621] Now by the sword, and now the javelin, fall

[024-622] The rebel race, and death had swallow’d all;

[024-623] But from on high the blue-eyed virgin cried;

[024-624] Her awful voice detain’d the headlong tide:

[024-625] “Forbear, ye nations, your mad hands forbear

[024-626] From mutual slaughter; Peace descends to spare.”

[024-627] Fear shook the nations: at the voice divine

[024-628] They drop their javelins, and their rage resign.

[024-629] All scatter’d round their glittering weapons lie;

[024-630] Some fall to earth, and some confusedly fly.

[024-631] With dreadful shouts Ulysses pour’d along,

[024-632] Swift as an eagle, as an eagle strong.

[024-633] But Jove’s red arm the burning thunder aims:

[024-634] Before Minerva shot the livid flames;

[024-635] Blazing they fell, and at her feet expired;

[024-636] Then stopped the goddess, trembled and retired.

[024-637] “Descended from the gods! Ulysses, cease;

[024-638] Offend not Jove: obey, and give the peace.”

[024-639] So Pallas spoke: the mandate from above

[024-640] The king obey’d. The virgin-seed of Jove,

[024-641] In Mentor’s form, confirm’d the full accord,

[024-642] And willing nations knew their lawful lord.
