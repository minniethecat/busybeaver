[017-001] BOOK XVII.

[017-002] ARGUMENT.

[017-003] [[telemachus|Telemachus]] returning to the city, relates to [[penelope|Penelope]] the sum of his

[017-004] travels. [[odysseus|Ulysses]] is conducted by [[eumaeus|Eumaeus]] to the palace, where his old

[017-005] dog Argus acknowledges his master, after an absence of twenty years,

[017-006] and dies with joy. Eumaeus returns into the country, and Ulysses

[017-007] remains among the suitors, whose behaviour is described.

## Section 43

[017-008] Soon as Aurora, daughter of the dawn,

[017-009] Sprinkled with roseate light the dewy lawn,

[017-010] In haste the prince arose, prepared to part;

[017-011] His hand impatient grasps the pointed dart;

[017-012] Fair on his feet the polish’d sandals shine,

[017-013] And thus he greets the master of the swine:

[017-014] “My friend, adieu! let this short stay suffice;

[017-015] I haste to meet my mother’s longing eyes,

[017-016] And end her tears, her sorrows and her sighs.

[017-017] But thou, attentive, what we order heed:

[017-018] This hapless stranger to the city lead:

[017-019] By public bounty let him there be fed,

[017-020] And bless the hand that stretches forth the bread.

[017-021] To wipe the tears from all afflicted eyes,

[017-022] My will may covet, but my power denies.

[017-023] If this raise anger in the stranger’s thought,

[017-024] The pain of anger punishes the fault:

[017-025] The very truth I undisguised declare;

[017-026] For what so easy as to be sincere?”

[017-027] To this Ulysses: “What the prince requires

[017-028] Of swift removal, seconds my desires.

[017-029] To want like mine the peopled town can yield

[017-030] More hopes of comfort than the lonely field:

[017-031] Nor fits my age to till the labour’d lands,

[017-032] Or stoop to tasks a rural lord demands.

[017-033] Adieu! but since this ragged garb can bear

[017-034] So ill the inclemencies of morning air,

[017-035] A few hours’ space permit me here to stay:

[017-036] My steps Eumaeus shall to town convey,

[017-037] With riper beams when Phœbus warms the day.”

[017-038] Thus he: nor aught Telemachus replied,

[017-039] But left the mansion with a lofty stride:

[017-040] Schemes of revenge his pondering breast elate,

[017-041] Revolving deep the suitors’ sudden fate,

[017-042] Arriving now before the imperial hall,

[017-043] He props his spear against the pillar’d wall;

[017-044] Then like a lion o’er the threshold bounds;

[017-045] The marble pavement with his steps resounds:

[017-046] His eye first glanced where Euryclea spreads

[017-047] With furry spoils of beasts the splendid beds:

[017-048] She saw, she wept, she ran with eager pace,

[017-049] And reach’d her master with a long embrace.

[017-050] All crowded round, the family appears

[017-051] With wild entrancement, and ecstatic tears.

[017-052] Swift from above descends the royal fair

[017-053] (Her beauteous cheeks the blush of Venus wear,

[017-054] Chasten’d with coy Diana’s pensive air);

[017-055] Hangs o’er her son, in his embraces dies;

[017-056] Rains kisses on his neck, his face, his eyes:

[017-057] Few words she spoke, though much she had to say;

[017-058] And scarce those few, for tears, could force their way.

[017-059] “Light of my eyes: he comes! unhoped-for joy!

[017-060] Has Heaven from [[pylos|Pylos]] brought my lovely boy?

[017-061] So snatch’d from all our cares!—Tell, hast thou known

[017-062] Thy father’s fate, and tell me all thy own.”

[017-063] “Oh dearest! most revered of womankind!

[017-064] Cease with those tears to melt a manly mind

[017-065] (Replied the prince); nor be our fates deplored,

[017-066] From death and treason to thy arms restored.

[017-067] Go bathe, and robed in white ascend the towers;

[017-068] With all thy handmaids thank the immortal powers;

[017-069] To every god vow hecatombs to bleed.

[017-070] And call [[zeus|Jove]]’s vengeance on their guilty deed.

[017-071] While to the assembled council I repair:

[017-072] A stranger sent by Heaven attends me there;

[017-073] My new accepted guest I haste to find,

[017-074] Now to [[peiraeus|Peiraeus]]’ honour’d charge consign’d.”

[017-075] The matron heard, nor was his word in vain.

[017-076] She bathed; and, robed in white, with all her train,

[017-077] To every god vow’d hecatombs to bleed,

[017-078] And call’d Jove’s vengeance on the guilty deed,

[017-079] Arm’d with his lance, the prince then pass’d the gate,

[017-080] Two dogs behind, a faithful guard, await;

[017-081] Pallas his form with grace divine improves:

[017-082] The gazing crowd admires him as he moves.

[017-083] Him, gathering round, the haughty suitors greet

[017-084] With semblance fair, but inward deep deceit,

[017-085] Their false addresses, generous, he denied.

[017-086] Pass’d on, and sate by faithful [[mentor|Mentor]]’s side;

[017-087] With [[antiphus|Antiphus]], and [[halitherses|Halitherses]] sage

[017-088] (His father’s counsellors, revered for age).

[017-089] Of his own fortunes, and Ulysses’ fame,

[017-090] Much ask’d the seniors; till Peiraeus came.

[017-091] The stranger-guest pursued him close behind;

[017-092] Whom when Telemachus beheld, he join’d.

[017-093] He (when Peiraeus ask’d for slaves to bring

[017-094] The gifts and treasures of the Spartan king)

[017-095] Thus thoughtful answer’d: “Those we shall not move,

[017-096] Dark and unconscious of the will of Jove;

[017-097] We know not yet the full event of all:

[017-098] Stabb’d in his palace if your prince must fall,

[017-099] Us, and our house, if treason must o’erthrow,

[017-100] Better a friend possess them than a foe;

[017-101] If death to these, and vengeance Heaven decree,

[017-102] Riches are welcome then, not else, to me.

[017-103] Till then retain the gifts.”—The hero said,

[017-104] And in his hand the willing stranger led.

[017-105] Then disarray’d, the shining bath they sought

[017-106] (With unguents smooth) of polish’d marble wrought:

[017-107] Obedient handmaids with assistant toil

[017-108] Supply the limpid wave, and fragrant oil:

[017-109] Then o’er their limbs refulgent robes they threw,

[017-110] And fresh from bathing to their seats withdrew.

[017-111] The golden ewer a nymph attendant brings,

[017-112] Replenish’d from the pure translucent springs;

[017-113] With copious streams that golden ewer supplies

[017-114] A silver layer of capacious size.

[017-115] They wash: the table, in fair order spread,

[017-116] Is piled with viands and the strength of bread.

[017-117] Full opposite, before the folding gate,

[017-118] The pensive mother sits in humble state;

[017-119] Lowly she sate, and with dejected view

[017-120] The fleecy threads her ivory fingers drew.

[017-121] The prince and stranger shared the genial feast,

[017-122] Till now the rage of thirst and hunger ceased.

[017-123] When thus the queen: “My son! my only friend!

[017-124] Say, to my mournful couch shall I ascend?

[017-125] (The couch deserted now a length of years;

[017-126] The couch for ever water’d with my tears;)

[017-127] Say, wilt thou not (ere yet the suitor crew

[017-128] [[nostos|Return]], and riot shakes our walls anew),

[017-129] Say, wilt thou not the least account afford?

[017-130] The least glad tidings of my absent lord?”

[017-131] To her the youth. “We reach’d the Pylian plains,

[017-132] Where [[nestor|Nestor]], shepherd of his people, reigns.

[017-133] All arts of tenderness to him are known,

[017-134] Kind to Ulysses’ race as to his own;

[017-135] No father with a fonder grasp of joy

[017-136] Strains to his bosom his long-absent boy.

[017-137] But all unknown, if yet Ulysses breathe,

[017-138] Or glide a spectre in the realms beneath;

[017-139] For farther search, his rapid steeds transport

[017-140] My lengthen’d journey to the Spartan court.

[017-141] There Argive [[helen|Helen]] I beheld, whose charms

[017-142] (So Heaven decreed) engaged the great in arms.

[017-143] My cause of coming told, he thus rejoin’d;

[017-144] And still his words live perfect in my mind:

[017-145] “‘Heavens! would a soft, inglorious, dastard train

[017-146] An absent hero’s nuptial joys profane

[017-147] So with her young, amid the woodland shades,

[017-148] A timorous hind the lion’s court invades,

[017-149] Leaves in that fatal lair her tender fawns,

[017-150] And climbs the cliffs, or feeds along the lawns;

[017-151] Meantime returning, with remorseless sway

[017-152] The monarch savage rends the panting prey:

[017-153] With equal fury, and with equal fame,

[017-154] Shall great Ulysses reassert his claim.

[017-155] O Jove! supreme! whom men and gods revere;

[017-156] And thou whose lustre gilds the rolling sphere!

[017-157] With power congenial join’d, propitious aid

[017-158] The chief adopted by the martial maid!

[017-159] Such to our wish the warrior soon restore,

[017-160] As when, contending on the Lesbian shore,

[017-161] His prowess Philomelides confess’d,

[017-162] And loud acclaiming Greeks the victor bless’d:

[017-163] Then soon the invaders of his bed, and throne,

[017-164] Their love presumptuous shall by death atone.

[017-165] Now what you question of my ancient friend,

[017-166] With truth I answer; thou the truth attend.

[017-167] Learn what I heard the sea-born seer relate,

[017-168] Whose eye can pierce the dark recess of fate

[017-169] Sole in an isle, imprison’d by the main,

[017-170] The sad survivor of his numerous train,

[017-171] Ulysses lies; detain’d by magic charms,

[017-172] And press’d unwilling in [[calypso|Calypso]]’s arms.

[017-173] No sailors there, no vessels to convey,

[017-174] No oars to cut the immeasurable way.’

[017-175] This told [[agamemnon|Atrides]], and he told no more.

[017-176] Then safe I voyaged to my native shore.”

[017-177] He ceased; nor made the pensive queen reply,

[017-178] But droop’d her head, and drew a secret sigh.

[017-179] When [[theoclymenus|Theoclymenus]] the seer began:

[017-180] “O suffering consort of the suffering man!

[017-181] What human knowledge could, those kings might tell,

[017-182] But I the secrets of high heaven reveal.

[017-183] Before the first of gods be this declared,

[017-184] Before the board whose blessings we have shared;

[017-185] Witness the genial rites, and witness all

[017-186] This house holds sacred in her ample wall!

[017-187] E’en now, this instant, great Ulysses, laid

[017-188] At rest, or wandering in his country’s shade,

[017-189] Their guilty deeds, in hearing, and in view,

[017-190] Secret revolves; and plans the vengeance due.

[017-191] Of this sure auguries the gods bestow’d,

[017-192] When first our vessel anchor’d in your road.”

[017-193] “Succeed those omens, Heaven! (the queen rejoin’d)

[017-194] So shall our bounties speak a grateful mind;

[017-195] And every envied happiness attend

[017-196] The man who calls Penelope his friend.”

[017-197] Thus communed they: while in the marble court

[017-198] (Scene of their insolence) the lords resort:

[017-199] Athwart the spacious square each tries his art,

[017-200] To whirl the disk, or aim the missile dart.

[017-201] Now did the hour of sweet repast arrive,

[017-202] And from the field the victim flocks they drive:

[017-203] [[medon|Medon]] the herald (one who pleased them best,

[017-204] And honour’d with a portion of their feast),

[017-205] To bid the banquet, interrupts their play:

[017-206] Swift to the hall they haste; aside they lay

[017-207] Their garments, and succinct the victims slay.

[017-208] Then sheep, and goats, and bristly porkers bled,

[017-209] And the proud steer was o’er the marble spread.

[017-210] While thus the copious banquet they provide,

[017-211] Along the road, conversing side by side,

[017-212] Proceed Ulysses and the faithful swain;

[017-213] When thus Eumaeus, generous and humane:

[017-214] “To town, observant of our lord’s behest,

[017-215] Now let us speed; my friend no more my guest!

[017-216] Yet like myself I wish thee here preferr’d,

[017-217] Guard of the flock, or keeper of the herd,

[017-218] But much to raise my master’s wrath I fear;

[017-219] The wrath of princes ever is severe.

[017-220] Then heed his will, and be our journey made

[017-221] While the broad beams of Phœbus are display’d,

[017-222] Or ere brown evening spreads her chilly shade.”

[017-223] “Just thy advice (the prudent chief rejoin’d),

[017-224] And such as suits the dictate of my mind.

[017-225] Lead on: but help me to some staff to stay

[017-226] My feeble step, since rugged is the way.”

[017-227] Across his shoulders then the scrip he flung,

[017-228] Wide-patch’d, and fasten’d by a twisted thong.

[017-229] A staff Eumaeus gave. Along the way

[017-230] Cheerly they fare: behind, the keepers stay:

[017-231] These with their watchful dogs (a constant guard)

[017-232] Supply his absence, and attend the herd.

[017-233] And now his city strikes the monarch’s eyes,

[017-234] Alas! how changed! a man of miseries;

[017-235] Propp’d on a staff, a beggar old and bare

[017-236] In rags dishonest fluttering with the air!

[017-237] Now pass’d the rugged road, they journey down

[017-238] The cavern’d way descending to the town,

[017-239] Where, from the rock, with liquid drops distils

[017-240] A limpid fount; that spread in parting rills

[017-241] Its current thence to serve the city brings;

[017-242] An useful work, adorn’d by ancient kings.

[017-243] [[ithaca|Neritus]], Ithacus, Polyctor, there,

[017-244] In sculptured stone immortalized their care,

[017-245] In marble urns received it from above,

[017-246] And shaded with a green surrounding grove;

[017-247] Where silver alders, in high arches twined,

[017-248] Drink the cool stream, and tremble to the wind.

[017-249] Beneath, sequester’d to the nymphs, is seen

[017-250] A mossy altar, deep embower’d in green;

[017-251] Where constant vows by travellers are paid,

[017-252] And holy horrors solemnize the shade.

[017-253] Here with his goats (not vow’d to sacred fame,

[017-254] But pamper’d luxury) [[melanthius|Melanthius]] came:

[017-255] Two grooms attend him. With an envious look

[017-256] He eyed the stranger, and imperious spoke:

[017-257] “The good old proverb how this pair fulfil!

[017-258] One rogue is usher to another still.

[017-259] Heaven with a secret principle endued

[017-260] Mankind, to seek their own similitude.

[017-261] Where goes the swineherd with that ill-look’d guest?

[017-262] That giant-glutton, dreadful at a feast!

[017-263] Full many a post have those broad shoulders worn,

[017-264] From every great man’s gate repulsed with scorn:

[017-265] To no brave prize aspired the worthless swain,

[017-266] ’Twas but for scraps he ask’d, and ask’d in vain.

[017-267] To beg, than work, he better understands,

[017-268] Or we perhaps might take him off thy hands.

[017-269] For any office could the slave be good,

[017-270] To cleanse the fold, or help the kids to food.

[017-271] If any labour those big joints could learn,

[017-272] Some whey, to wash his bowels, he might earn.

[017-273] To cringe, to whine, his idle hands to spread,

[017-274] Is all, by which that graceless maw is fed.

[017-275] Yet hear me! if thy impudence but dare

[017-276] Approach yon wall, I prophesy thy fare:

[017-277] Dearly, full dearly, shalt thou buy thy bread

[017-278] With many a footstool thundering at thy head.”

## Section 44

[017-279] He thus: nor insolent of word alone,

[017-280] Spurn’d with his rustic heel his king unknown;

[017-281] Spurn’d, but not moved: he like a pillar stood,

[017-282] Nor stirr’d an inch, contemptuous, from the road:

[017-283] Doubtful, or with his staff to strike him dead,

[017-284] Or greet the pavement with his worthless head.

[017-285] Short was that doubt; to quell his rage inured,

[017-286] The hero stood self-conquer’d, and endured.

[017-287] But hateful of the wretch, Eumaeus heaved

[017-288] His hands obtesting, and this prayer conceived:

[017-289] “Daughters of Jove! who from the ethereal bowers

[017-290] Descend to swell the springs, and feed the flowers!

[017-291] Nymphs of this fountain! to whose sacred names

[017-292] Our rural victims mount in blazing flames!

[017-293] To whom Ulysses’ piety preferr’d

[017-294] The yearly firstlings of his flock and herd;

[017-295] Succeed my wish, your votary restore:

[017-296] Oh, be some god his convoy to our shore!

[017-297] Due pains shall punish then this slave’s offence,

[017-298] And humble all his airs of insolence,

[017-299] Who, proudly stalking, leaves the herds at large,

[017-300] Commences courtier, and neglects his charge.”

[017-301] “What mutters he? (Melanthius sharp rejoins;)

[017-302] This crafty miscreant, big with dark designs?

[017-303] The day shall come—nay, ’tis already near—

[017-304] When, slave! to sell thee at a price too dear

[017-305] Must be my care; and hence transport thee o’er,

[017-306] A load and scandal to this happy shore.

[017-307] Oh! that as surely great [[apollo|Apollo]]’s dart,

[017-308] Or some brave suitor’s sword, might pierce the heart

[017-309] Of the proud son; as that we stand this hour

[017-310] In lasting safety from the father’s power!”

[017-311] So spoke the wretch, but, shunning farther fray,

[017-312] Turn’d his proud step, and left them on their way.

[017-313] Straight to the feastful palace he repair’d,

[017-314] Familiar enter’d, and the banquet shared;

[017-315] Beneath [[eurymachus|Eurymachus]], his patron lord,

[017-316] He took his place, and plenty heap’d the board.

[017-317] Meantime they heard, soft circling in the sky

[017-318] Sweet airs ascend, and heavenly minstrelsy

[017-319] (For [[phemius|Phemius]] to the lyre attuned the strain):

[017-320] Ulysses hearken’d, then address’d the swain:

[017-321] “Well may this palace admiration claim,

[017-322] Great and respondent to the master’s fame!

[017-323] Stage above stage the imperial structure stands,

[017-324] Holds the chief honours, and the town commands:

[017-325] High walls and battlements the courts inclose,

[017-326] And the strong gates defy a host of foes.

[017-327] Far other cares its dwellers now employ;

[017-328] The throng’d assembly and the feast of joy:

[017-329] I see the smokes of sacrifice aspire,

[017-330] And hear (what graces every feast) the lyre.”

[017-331] Then thus Eumaeus: “Judge we which were best;

[017-332] Amidst yon revellers a sudden guest

[017-333] Choose you to mingle, while behind I stay?

[017-334] Or I first entering introduce the way?

[017-335] Wait for a space without, but wait not long;

[017-336] This is the house of violence and wrong:

[017-337] Some rude insult thy reverend age may bear;

[017-338] For like their lawless lords the servants are.”

[017-339] “Just is, O friend! thy caution, and address’d

[017-340] (Replied the chief, to no unheedful breast:)

[017-341] The wrongs and injuries of base mankind

[017-342] Fresh to my sense, and always in my mind.

[017-343] The bravely-patient to no fortune yields:

[017-344] On rolling oceans, and in fighting fields,

[017-345] Storms have I pass’d, and many a stern debate;

[017-346] And now in humbler scene submit to fate.

[017-347] What cannot want? The best she will expose,

[017-348] And I am learn’d in all her train of woes;

[017-349] She fills with navies, hosts, and loud alarms,

[017-350] The sea, the land, and shakes the world with arms!”

[017-351] Thus, near the gates conferring as they drew,

[017-352] Argus, the dog, his ancient master knew:

[017-353] He not unconscious of the voice and tread,

[017-354] Lifts to the sound his ear, and rears his head;

[017-355] Bred by Ulysses, nourish’d at his board,

[017-356] But, ah! not fated long to please his lord;

[017-357] To him, his swiftness and his strength were vain;

[017-358] The voice of glory call’d him o’er the main.

[017-359] Till then in every sylvan chase renown’d,

[017-360] With Argus, Argus, rung the woods around;

[017-361] With him the youth pursued the goat or fawn,

[017-362] Or traced the mazy leveret o’er the lawn.

[017-363] Now left to man’s ingratitude he lay,

[017-364] Unhoused, neglected in the public way;

[017-365] And where on heaps the rich manure was spread,

[017-366] Obscene with reptiles, took his sordid bed.

[017-367] He knew his lord; he knew, and strove to meet;

[017-368] In vain he strove to crawl and kiss his feet;

[017-369] Yet (all he could) his tail, his tears, his eyes,

[017-370] Salute his master, and confess his joys.

[017-371] Soft pity touch’d the mighty master’s soul;

[017-372] Adown his cheek a tear unbidden stole,

[017-373] Stole unperceived: he turn’d his head and dried

[017-374] The drop humane: then thus impassion’d cried:

[017-375] “What noble beast in this abandon’d state

[017-376] Lies here all helpless at Ulysses’ gate?

[017-377] His bulk and beauty speak no vulgar praise:

[017-378] If, as he seems, he was in better days,

[017-379] Some care his age deserves; or was he prized

[017-380] For worthless beauty? therefore now despised;

[017-381] Such dogs and men there are, mere things of state;

[017-382] And always cherish’d by their friends, the great.”

[017-383] “Not Argus so, (Eumaeus thus rejoin’d,)

[017-384] But served a master of a nobler kind,

[017-385] Who, never, never shall behold him more!

[017-386] Long, long since perish’d on a distant shore!

[017-387] Oh had you seen him, vigorous, bold, and young,

[017-388] Swift as a stag, and as a lion strong:

[017-389] Him no fell savage on the plain withstood,

[017-390] None ’scaped him bosom’d in the gloomy wood;

[017-391] His eye how piercing, and his scent how true,

[017-392] To wind the vapour on the tainted dew!

[017-393] Such, when Ulysses left his natal coast:

[017-394] Now years unnerve him, and his lord is lost!

[017-395] The women keep the generous creature bare,

[017-396] A sleek and idle race is all their care:

[017-397] The master gone, the servants what restrains?

[017-398] Or dwells humanity where riot reigns?

[017-399] Jove fix’d it certain, that whatever day

[017-400] Makes man a slave, takes half his worth away.”

[017-401] This said, the honest herdsman strode before;

[017-402] The musing monarch pauses at the door:

[017-403] The dog, whom [[moira|Fate]] had granted to behold

[017-404] His lord, when twenty tedious years had roll’d,

[017-405] Takes a last look, and having seen him, dies;

[017-406] So closed for ever faithful Argus’ eyes!

[017-407] And now Telemachus, the first of all,

[017-408] Observed Eumaeus entering in the hall;

[017-409] Distant he saw, across the shady dome;

[017-410] Then gave a sign, and beckon’d him to come:

[017-411] There stood an empty seat, where late was placed,

[017-412] In order due, the steward of the feast,

[017-413] (Who now was busied carving round the board,)

[017-414] Eumaeus took, and placed it near his lord.

[017-415] Before him instant was the banquet spread,

[017-416] And the bright basket piled with loaves of bread.

[017-417] Next came Ulysses lowly at the door,

[017-418] A figure despicable, old, and poor.

[017-419] In squalid vests, with many a gaping rent,

[017-420] Propp’d on a staff, and trembling as he went.

[017-421] Then, resting on the threshold of the gate,

[017-422] Against a cypress pillar lean’d his weight

[017-423] Smooth’d by the workman to a polish’d plane);

[017-424] The thoughtful son beheld, and call’d his swain

[017-425] “These viands, and this bread, Eumaeus! bear,

[017-426] And let yon mendicant our plenty share:

[017-427] And let him circle round the suitors’ board,

[017-428] And try the bounty of each gracious lord.

[017-429] Bold let him ask, encouraged thus by me:

[017-430] How ill, alas! do want and shame agree!”

[017-431] His lord’s command the faithful servant bears:

[017-432] The seeming beggar answers with his prayers:

[017-433] “Bless’d be Telemachus! in every deed

[017-434] Inspire him. Jove! in every wish succeed!”

[017-435] This said, the portion from his son convey’d

[017-436] With smiles receiving on his scrip he laid.

[017-437] Long has the minstrel swept the sounding wire,

[017-438] He fed, and ceased when silence held the lyre.

[017-439] Soon as the suitors from the banquet rose,

[017-440] [[athena|Minerva]] prompts the man of mighty woes

[017-441] To tempt their bounties with a suppliant’s art,

[017-442] And learn the generous from the ignoble heart

[017-443] (Not but his soul, resentful as humane,

[017-444] Dooms to full vengeance all the offending train);

[017-445] With speaking eyes, and voice of plaintive sound,

[017-446] Humble he moves, imploring all around.

[017-447] The proud feel pity, and relief bestow,

[017-448] With such an image touch’d of human woe;

[017-449] Inquiring all, their wonder they confess,

[017-450] And eye the man, majestic in distress.

[017-451] While thus they gaze and question with their eyes,

[017-452] The bold Melanthius to their thought replies:

[017-453] “My lords! this stranger of gigantic port

[017-454] The good Eumaeus usher’d to your court.

[017-455] Full well I mark’d the features of his face,

[017-456] Though all unknown his clime, or noble race.”

[017-457] “And is this present, swineherd! of thy band?

[017-458] Bring’st thou these vagrants to infest the land?

[017-459] (Returns [[antinous|Antinous]] with retorted eye)

[017-460] Objects uncouth, to check the genial joy.

[017-461] Enough of these our court already grace;

[017-462] Of giant stomach, and of famish’d face.

[017-463] Such guests Eumaeus to his country brings,

[017-464] To share our feast, and lead the life of kings.”

[017-465] To whom the hospitable swain rejoins:

[017-466] “Thy passion, prince, belies thy knowing mind.

[017-467] Who calls, from distant nations to his own,

[017-468] The poor, distinguish’d by their wants alone?

[017-469] Round the wide world are sought those men divine

[017-470] Who public structures raise, or who design;

[017-471] Those to whose eyes the gods their ways reveal,

[017-472] Or bless with salutary arts to heal;

[017-473] But chief to poets such respect belongs,

[017-474] By rival nations courted for their songs;

[017-475] These states invite, and mighty kings admire,

[017-476] Wide as the sun displays his vital fire.

[017-477] It is not so with want! how few that feed

[017-478] A wretch unhappy, merely for his need!

[017-479] Unjust to me, and all that serve the state,

[017-480] To love Ulysses is to raise thy hate.

[017-481] For me, suffice the approbation won

[017-482] Of my great mistress, and her godlike son.”

[017-483] To him Telemachus: “No more incense

[017-484] The man by nature prone to insolence:

[017-485] Injurious minds just answers but provoke”—

[017-486] Then turning to Antinous, thus he spoke:

[017-487] “Thanks to thy care! whose absolute command

[017-488] Thus drives the stranger from our court and land.

[017-489] Heaven bless its owner with a better mind!

[017-490] From envy free, to charity inclined.

[017-491] This both Penelope and I afford:

[017-492] Then, prince! be bounteous of Ulysses’ board.

[017-493] To give another’s is thy hand so slow?

[017-494] So much more sweet to spoil than to bestow?”

[017-495] “Whence, great Telemachus! this lofty strain?

[017-496] (Antinous cries with insolent disdain):

[017-497] Portions like mine if every suitor gave,

[017-498] Our walls this twelvemonth should not see the slave.”

[017-499] He spoke, and lifting high above the board

[017-500] His ponderous footstool, shook it at his lord.

[017-501] The rest with equal hand conferr’d the bread:

[017-502] He fill’d his scrip, and to the threshold sped;

[017-503] But first before Antinous stopp’d, and said:

[017-504] “Bestow, my friend! thou dost not seem the worst

[017-505] Of all the Greeks, but prince-like and the first;

[017-506] Then, as in dignity, be first in worth,

[017-507] And I shall praise thee through the boundless earth.

[017-508] Once I enjoy’d in luxury of state

[017-509] Whate’er gives man the envied name of great;

[017-510] Wealth, servants, friends, were mine in better days

[017-511] And hospitality was then my praise;

[017-512] In every sorrowing soul I pour’d delight,

[017-513] And poverty stood smiling in my sight.

[017-514] But Jove, all-governing, whose only will

[017-515] Determines fate, and mingles good with ill,

[017-516] Sent me (to punish my pursuit of gain)

[017-517] With roving pirates o’er the Egyptian main

[017-518] By Egypt’s silver flood our ships we moor;

[017-519] Our spies commission’d straight the coast explore;

[017-520] But impotent of mind, the lawless will

[017-521] The country ravage, and the natives kill.

[017-522] The spreading clamour to their city flies,

[017-523] And horse and foot in mingled tumults rise:

[017-524] The reddening dawn reveals the hostile fields,

[017-525] Horrid with bristly spears, and gleaming shields:

[017-526] Jove thunder’d on their side: our guilty head

[017-527] We turn’d to flight; the gathering vengeance spread

[017-528] On all parts round, and heaps on heaps lay dead.

[017-529] Some few the foe in servitude detain;

[017-530] Death ill exchanged for bondage and for pain!

[017-531] Unhappy me a Cyprian took aboard,

[017-532] And gave to Dmetor, Cyprus’ haughty lord:

[017-533] Hither, to ’scape his chains, my course I steer,

[017-534] Still cursed by Fortune, and insulted here!”

[017-535] To whom Antinous thus his rage express’d:

[017-536] “What god has plagued us with this gourmand guest?

[017-537] Unless at distance, wretch! thou keep behind,

[017-538] Another isle, than Cyprus more unkind,

[017-539] Another Egypt shalt thou quickly find.

[017-540] From all thou begg’st, a bold audacious slave;

[017-541] Nor all can give so much as thou canst crave.

[017-542] Nor wonder I, at such profusion shown;

## Section 45

[017-544] The chief, retiring: “Souls, like that in thee,

[017-545] Ill suits such forms of grace and dignity.

[017-546] Nor will that hand to utmost need afford

[017-547] The smallest portion of a wasteful board,

[017-548] Whose luxury whole patrimonies sweeps,

[017-549] Yet starving want, amidst the riot, weeps.”

[017-550] The haughty suitor with resentment burns,

[017-551] And, sourly smiling, this reply returns:

[017-552] “Take that, ere yet thou quit this princely throng;

[017-553] And dumb for ever be thy slanderous tongue!”

[017-554] He said, and high the whirling tripod flung.

[017-555] His shoulder-blade received the ungentle shock;

[017-556] He stood, and moved not, like a marble rock;

[017-557] But shook his thoughtful head, nor more complain’d,

[017-558] Sedate of soul, his character sustain’d,

[017-559] And inly form’d revenge; then back withdrew:

[017-560] Before his feet the well fill’d scrip he threw,

[017-561] And thus with semblance mild address’d the crew:

[017-562] “May what I speak your princely minds approve,

[017-563] Ye peers and rivals in this noble love!

[017-564] Not for the hurt I grieve, but for the cause.

[017-565] If, when the sword our country’s quarrel draws,

[017-566] Or if, defending what is justly dear,

[017-567] From Mars impartial some broad wound we bear,

[017-568] The generous motive dignifies the scar.

[017-569] But for mere want, how hard to suffer wrong!

[017-570] Want brings enough of other ills along!

[017-571] Yet, if injustice never be secure,

[017-572] If fiends revenge, and gods assert the poor,

[017-573] Death shall lay low the proud aggressor’s head,

[017-574] And make the dust Antinous’ bridal bed.”

[017-575] “Peace, wretch! and eat thy bread without offence

[017-576] (The suitor cried), or force shall drag thee hence,

[017-577] Scourge through the public street, and cast thee there,

[017-578] A mangled carcase for the hounds to tear.”

[017-579] His furious deed the general anger moved,

[017-580] All, even the worst, condemn’d; and some reproved.

[017-581] “Was ever chief for wars like these renown’d?

[017-582] Ill fits the stranger and the poor to wound.

[017-583] Unbless’d thy hand! if in this low disguise

[017-584] Wander, perhaps, some inmate of the skies;

[017-585] They (curious oft of mortal actions) deign

[017-586] In forms like these to round the earth and main,

[017-587] Just and unjust recording in their mind,

[017-588] And with sure eyes inspecting all mankind.”

[017-589] Telemachus, absorb’d in thought severe,

[017-590] Nourish’d deep anguish, though he shed no tear;

[017-591] But the dark brow of silent sorrow shook:

[017-592] While thus his mother to her virgins spoke:

[017-593] “On him and his may the bright god of day

[017-594] That base, inhospitable blow repay!”

[017-595] The nurse replies: “If Jove receives my prayer,

[017-596] Not one survives to breathe to-morrow’s air.”

[017-597] “All, all are foes, and mischief is their end;

[017-598] Antinous most to gloomy death a friend

[017-599] (Replies the queen): the stranger begg’d their grace,

[017-600] And melting pity soften’d every face;

[017-601] From every other hand redress he found,

[017-602] But fell Antinous answer’d with a wound.”

[017-603] Amidst her maids thus spoke the prudent queen,

[017-604] Then bade Eumaeus call the pilgrim in.

[017-605] “Much of the experienced man I long to hear,

[017-606] If or his certain eye, or listening ear,

[017-607] Have learn’d the fortunes of my wandering lord?”

[017-608] Thus she, and good Eumaeus took the word:

[017-609] “A private audience if thy grace impart,

[017-610] The stranger’s words may ease the royal heart.

[017-611] His sacred eloquence in balm distils,

[017-612] And the soothed heart with secret pleasure fills.

[017-613] Three days have spent their beams, three nights have run

[017-614] Their silent journey, since his tale begun,

[017-615] Unfinish’d yet; and yet I thirst to hear!

[017-616] As when some heaven-taught poet charms the ear

[017-617] (Suspending sorrow with celestial strain

[017-618] Breathed from the gods to soften human pain)

[017-619] Time steals away with unregarded wing,

[017-620] And the soul hears him, though he cease to sing

[017-621] “Ulysses late he saw, on Cretan ground

[017-622] (His fathers guest), for Minos’ birth renown’d.

[017-623] He now but waits the wind to waft him o’er,

[017-624] With boundless treasure, from Thesprotia’s shore.”

[017-625] To this the queen: “The wanderer let me hear,

[017-626] While yon luxurious race indulge their cheer,

[017-627] Devour the grazing ox, and browsing goat,

[017-628] And turn my generous vintage down their throat.

[017-629] For where’s an arm, like thine, Ulysses! strong,

[017-630] To curb wild riot, and to punish wrong?”

[017-631] She spoke. Telemachus then sneezed aloud;

[017-632] Constrain’d, his nostril echoed through the crowd.

[017-633] The smiling queen the happy omen bless’d:

[017-634] “So may these impious fall, by Fate oppress’d!”

[017-635] Then to Eumaeus: “Bring the stranger, fly!

[017-636] And if my questions meet a true reply,

[017-637] Graced with a decent robe he shall retire,

[017-638] A gift in season which his wants require.”

[017-639] Thus spoke Penelope. Eumaeus flies

[017-640] In duteous haste, and to Ulysses cries:

[017-641] “The queen invites thee, venerable guest!

[017-642] A secret instinct moves her troubled breast,

[017-643] Of her long absent lord from thee to gain

[017-644] Some light, and soothe her soul’s eternal pain.

[017-645] If true, if faithful thou, her grateful mind

[017-646] Of decent robes a present has design’d:

[017-647] So finding favour in the royal eye,

[017-648] Thy other wants her subjects shall supply.”

[017-649] “Fair truth alone (the patient man replied)

[017-650] My words shall dictate, and my lips shall guide.

[017-651] To him, to me, one common lot was given,

[017-652] In equal woes, alas! involved by Heaven.

[017-653] Much of his fates I know; but check’d by fear

[017-655] Here boundless wrongs the starry skies invade,

[017-656] And injured suppliants seek in vain for aid.

[017-658] Nor claim my story till the sun descend;

[017-659] Then in such robes as suppliants may require,

[017-660] Composed and cheerful by the genial fire,

[017-661] When loud uproar and lawless riot cease,

[017-665] The cautious stranger? With the begging kind

[017-666] Shame suits but ill.” Eumaeus thus rejoin’d:

[017-667] “He only asks a more propitious hour,

[017-668] And shuns (who would not?) wicked men in power;

[017-669] At evening mild (meet season to confer)

[017-670] By turns to question, and by turns to hear.”

[017-673] For men like these on earth he shall not find
