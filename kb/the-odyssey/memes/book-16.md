[016-001] BOOK XVI.

[016-002] ARGUMENT.

[016-003] THE DISCOVERY OF ULYSSES TO TELEMACHUS.

[016-004] [[telemachus|Telemachus]] arriving at the lodge of [[eumaeus|Eumaeus]], sends him to carry

[016-005] [[penelope|Penelope]] the news of his return. [[athena|Minerva]] appearing to [[odysseus|Ulysses]], commands

[016-006] him to discover himself to his son. The princes, who had lain in ambush

[016-007] to intercept Telemachus in his way, their project being defeated,

[016-008] return to [[ithaca|Ithaca]].

[016-009] Soon as the morning blush’d along the plains,

[016-010] Ulysses, and the monarch of the swains,

[016-011] Awake the sleeping fires, their meals prepare,

[016-012] And forth to pasture send the bristly care.

[016-013] The prince’s near approach the dogs descry,

[016-014] And fawning round his feet confess their joy.

[016-015] Their gentle blandishment the king survey’d,

[016-016] Heard his resounding step, and instant said:

[016-017] “Some well-known friend, Eumaeus, bends this way;

[016-018] His steps I hear; the dogs familiar play.”

[016-019] While yet he spoke, the prince advancing drew

[016-020] Nigh to the lodge, and now appear’d in view.

[016-021] Transported from his seat Eumaeus sprung,

[016-022] Dropp’d the full bowl, and round his bosom hung;

[016-023] Kissing his cheek, his hand, while from his eye

[016-024] The tears rain’d copious in a shower of joy,

[016-025] As some fond sire who ten long winters grieves,

[016-026] From foreign climes an only son receives

[016-027] (Child of his age), with strong paternal joy,

[016-028] Forward he springs, and clasps the favourite boy:

[016-029] So round the youth his arms Eumaeus spread,

[016-030] As if the grave had given him from the dead.

[016-031] “And is it thou? my ever-dear delight!

[016-032] Oh, art thou come to bless my longing sight?

[016-033] Never, I never hoped to view this day,

[016-034] When o’er the waves you plough’d the desperate way.

[016-035] Enter, my child! Beyond my hopes restored,

[016-036] Oh give these eyes to feast upon their lord.

[016-037] Enter, oh seldom seen! for lawless powers

[016-038] Too much detain thee from these sylvan bowers,”

[016-039] The prince replied: “Eumaeus, I obey;

[016-040] To seek thee, friend, I hither took my way.

[016-041] But say, if in the court the queen reside

[016-042] Severely chaste, or if commenced a bride?”

[016-043] Thus he; and thus the monarch of the swains:

[016-044] “Severely chaste Penelope remains;

[016-045] But, lost to every joy, she wastes the day

[016-046] In tedious cares, and weeps the night away.”

[016-047] He ended, and (receiving as they pass

[016-048] The javelin pointed with a star of brass),

[016-049] They reach’d the dome; the dome with marble shined.

[016-050] His seat Ulysses to the prince resign’d.

[016-051] “Not so (exclaims the prince with decent grace)

[016-052] For me, this house shall find an humbler place:

[016-053] To usurp the honours due to silver hairs

[016-054] And reverend strangers modest youth forbears.”

[016-055] Instant the swain the spoils of beasts supplies,

[016-056] And bids the rural throne with osiers rise.

[016-057] There sate the prince: the feast Eumaeus spread,

[016-058] And heap’d the shining canisters with bread.

[016-059] Thick o’er the board the plenteous viands lay,

[016-060] The frugal remnants of the former day.

[016-061] Then in a bowl he tempers generous wines,

[016-062] Around whose verge a mimic ivy twines.

[016-063] And now, the rage of thirst and hunger fled,

[016-064] Thus young Ulysses to Eumaeus said:

[016-065] “Whence, father, from what shore this stranger, say?

[016-066] What vessel bore him o’er the watery way?

[016-067] To human step our land impervious lies,

[016-068] And round the coast circumfluent oceans rise.”

[016-069] The swain returns: “A tale of sorrows hear:

[016-070] In spacious Crete he drew his natal air;

[016-071] Long doom’d to wander o’er the land and main,

[016-072] For Heaven has wove his thread of life with pain.

[016-073] Half breathless ’scaping to the land he flew

[016-074] From Thesprot mariners, a murderous crew.

[016-075] To thee, my son, the suppliant I resign;

[016-076] I gave him my protection, grant him thine.”

[016-077] “Hard task (he cries) thy virtue gives thy friend,

[016-078] Willing to aid, unable to defend.

[016-079] Can strangers safely in the court reside,

[016-080] ’Midst the swell’d insolence of lust and pride?

[016-081] E’en I unsafe: the queen in doubt to wed,

[016-082] Or pay due honours to the nuptial bed.

[016-083] Perhaps she weds regardless of her fame,

[016-084] Deaf to the mighty Ulyssean name.

[016-085] However, stranger! from our grace receive

[016-086] Such honours as befit a prince to give;

[016-087] Sandals, a sword and robes, respect to prove,

[016-088] And safe to sail with ornaments of love.

[016-089] Till then, thy guest amid the rural train,

[016-090] Far from the court, from danger far, detain.

[016-091] ’Tis mine with food the hungry to supply,

[016-092] And clothe the naked from the inclement sky.

[016-093] Here dwell in safety from the suitors’ wrongs,

[016-094] And the rude insults of ungovern’d tongues.

[016-095] For should’st thou suffer, powerless to relieve,

[016-096] I must behold it, and can only grieve.

[016-097] The brave, encompass’d by an hostile train,

[016-098] O’erpower’d by numbers, is but brave in vain.”

[016-099] To whom, while anger in his bosom glows,

[016-100] With warmth replies the man of mighty woes:

[016-101] “Since audience mild is deign’d, permit my tongue

[016-102] At once to pity and resent thy wrong.

[016-103] My heart weeps blood to see a soul so brave

[016-104] Live to base insolence or power a slave,

[016-105] But tell me, dost thou, prince, dost thou behold,

[016-106] And hear their midnight revels uncontroll’d?

[016-107] Say, do thy subjects in bold faction rise,

[016-108] Or priests in fabled oracles advise?

[016-109] Or are thy brothers, who should aid thy power,

[016-110] Turn’d mean deserters in the needful hour?

[016-111] Oh that I were from great Ulysses sprung,

[016-112] Or that these wither’d nerves like thine were strung,

[016-113] Or, heavens! might he return! (and soon appear

[016-114] He shall, I trust; a hero scorns despair:)

[016-115] Might he return, I yield my life a prey

[016-116] To my worst foe, if that avenging day

[016-117] Be not their last: but should I lose my life,

[016-118] Oppress’d by numbers in the glorious strife,

[016-119] I chose the nobler part, and yield my breath,

[016-120] Rather than bear dishonor, worse than death;

[016-121] Than see the hand of violence invade

[016-122] The reverend stranger and the spotless maid;

[016-123] Than see the wealth of kings consumed in waste,

[016-124] The drunkard’s revel, and the gluttons’ feast.”

[016-125] Thus he, with anger flashing from his eye;

[016-126] Sincere the youthful hero made reply:

[016-127] “Nor leagued in factious arms my subjects rise,

[016-128] Nor priests in fabled oracles advise;

[016-129] Nor are my brothers, who should aid my power,

[016-130] Turn’d mean deserters in the needful hour.

[016-131] Ah me! I boast no brother; heaven’s dread King

[016-132] Gives from our stock an only branch to spring:

[016-133] Alone [[laertes|Laertes]] reign’d Arcesius’ heir,

[016-134] Alone Ulysses drew the vital air,

[016-135] And I alone the bed connubial graced,

[016-136] An unbless’d offspring of a sire unbless’d!

[016-137] Each neighbouring realm, conducive to our woe,

[016-138] Sends forth her peers, and every peer a foe:

[016-139] The court proud [[same|Samos]] and [[dulichium|Dulichium]] fills,

[016-140] And lofty [[zacynthus|Zacinth]] crown’d with shady hills.

[016-141] E’en Ithaca and all her lords invade

[016-142] The imperial sceptre, and the regal bed:

[016-143] The queen, averse to love, yet awed by power,

[016-144] Seems half to yield, yet flies the bridal hour:

[016-145] Meantime their licence uncontroll’d I bear;

[016-146] E’en now they envy me the vital air:

[016-147] But Heaven will sure revenge, and gods there are.

[016-148] “But go Eumaeus! to the queen impart

[016-149] Our safe return, and ease a mother’s heart.

[016-150] Yet secret go; for numerous are my foes,

[016-151] And here at least I may in peace repose.”

[016-152] To whom the swain: “I hear and I obey:

[016-153] But old Laertes weeps his life away,

[016-154] And deems thee lost: shall I my speed employ

[016-155] To bless his age: a messenger of joy?

[016-156] The mournful hour that tore his son away

[016-157] Sent the sad sire in solitude to stray;

[016-158] Yet busied with his slaves, to ease his woe,

[016-159] He dress’d the vine, and bade the garden blow,

[016-160] Nor food nor wine refused; but since the day

[016-161] That you to [[pylos|Pylos]] plough’d the watery way,

[016-162] Nor wine nor food he tastes; but, sunk in woes,

[016-163] Wild springs the vine, no more the garden blows,

[016-164] Shut from the walks of men, to pleasure lost,

[016-165] Pensive and pale he wanders half a ghost.”

[016-166] “Wretched old man! (with tears the prince returns)

[016-167] Yet cease to go—what man so blest but mourns?

[016-168] Were every wish indulged by favouring skies,

[016-169] This hour should give Ulysses to my eyes.

[016-170] But to the queen with speed dispatchful bear,

[016-171] Our safe return, and back with speed repair;

[016-172] And let some handmaid of her train resort

[016-173] To good Laertes in his rural court.”

[016-174] While yet he spoke, impatient of delay,

[016-175] He braced his sandals on, and strode away:

[016-176] Then from the heavens the martial goddess flies

[016-177] Through the wild fields of air, and cleaves the skies:

[016-178] In form, a virgin in soft beauty’s bloom,

[016-179] Skill’d in the illustrious labours of the loom.

[016-180] Alone to Ithaca she stood display’d,

[016-181] But unapparent as a viewless shade

[016-182] Escaped Telemachus (the powers above,

[016-183] Seen or unseen, o’er earth at pleasure move):

[016-184] The dogs intelligent confess’d the tread

[016-185] Of power divine, and howling, trembling, fled.

[016-186] The goddess, beckoning, waves her deathless hands:

[016-187] Dauntless the king before the goddess stands:

[016-188] “Then why (she said), O favour’d of the skies!

[016-189] Why to thy godlike son this long disguise?

[016-190] Stand forth reveal’d; with him thy cares employ

[016-191] Against thy foes; be valiant and destroy!

[016-192] Lo! I descend in that avenging hour,

[016-193] To combat by thy side, thy guardian power.”

[016-194] She said, and o’er him waves her wand of gold

[016-195] Imperial robes his manly limbs infold;

[016-196] At once with grace divine his frame improves;

[016-197] At once with majesty enlarged he moves:

[016-198] Youth flush’d his reddening cheek, and from his brows

[016-199] A length of hair in sable ringlets flows;

[016-200] His blackening chin receives a deeper shade;

[016-201] Then from his eyes upsprung the warrior-maid.

[016-202] The hero reascends: the prince o’erawed

[016-203] Scarce lifts his eyes, and bows as to a god,

[016-204] Then with surprise (surprise chastised by fears):

[016-205] “How art thou changed! (he cried)—a god appears!

[016-206] Far other vests thy limbs majestic grace,

[016-207] Far other glories lighten from thy face!

[016-208] If heaven be thy abode, with pious care,

[016-209] Lo! I the ready sacrifice prepare:

[016-210] Lo! gifts of labour’d gold adorn thy shrine,

[016-211] To win thy grace: O save us, power divine!”

[016-212] “Few are my days (Ulysses made reply),

[016-213] Nor I, alas! descendant of the sky.

[016-214] I am thy father. O my son! my son!

[016-215] That father, for whose sake thy days have run

[016-216] One scene of woe! to endless cares consign’d,

[016-217] And outraged by the wrongs of base mankind.”

[016-218] Then, rushing to his arms, he kiss’d his boy

[016-219] With the strong raptures of a parent’s joy.

[016-220] Tears bathe his cheek, and tears the ground bedew:

[016-221] He strain’d him close, as to his breast he grew.

[016-222] “Ah me! (exclaims the prince with fond desire)

[016-223] Thou art not—no, thou canst not be my sire.

[016-224] Heaven such illusion only can impose,

[016-225] By the false joy to aggravate my woes.

[016-226] Who but a god can change the general doom,

[016-227] And give to wither’d age a youthful bloom!

[016-228] Late, worn with years, in weeds obscene you trod;

[016-229] Now, clothed in majesty, you move a god!”

[016-230] “Forbear (he cried,) for Heaven reserve that name;

[016-231] Give to thy father but a father’s claim;

[016-232] Other Ulysses shalt thou never see,

[016-233] I am Ulysses, I, my son, am he.

[016-234] Twice ten sad years o’er earth and ocean toss’d,

[016-235] ’Tis given at length to view my native coast.

[016-236] Pallas, unconquer’d maid, my frame surrounds

[016-237] With grace divine: her power admits no bounds;

[016-238] She o’er my limbs old age and wrinkles shed;

[016-239] Now strong as youth, magnificent I tread.

[016-240] The gods with ease frail man depress or raise,

[016-241] Exalt the lowly, or the proud debase.”

[016-242] He spoke and sate. The prince with transport flew,

[016-243] Hung round his neck, while tears his cheek bedew;

[016-244] Nor less the father pour’d a social flood;

[016-245] They wept abundant, and they wept aloud.

[016-246] As the bold eagle with fierce sorrow stung,

[016-247] Or parent vulture, mourns her ravish’d young;

[016-248] They cry, they scream, their unfledged brood a prey

[016-249] To some rude churl, and borne by stealth away:

[016-250] So they aloud: and tears in tides had run,

[016-251] Their grief unfinish’d with the setting sun;

[016-252] But checking the full torrent in its flow,

[016-253] The prince thus interrupts the solemn woe.

[016-254] “What ship transported thee, O father, say;

[016-255] And what bless’d hands have oar’d thee on the way?”

## Section 42

[016-256] “All, all (Ulysses instant made reply),

[016-257] I tell thee all, my child, my only joy!

[016-258] Phæacians bore me to the port assign’d,

[016-259] A nation ever to the stranger kind;

[016-260] Wrapp’d in the embrace of sleep, the faithful train

[016-261] O’er seas convey’d me to my native reign:

[016-262] Embroider’d vestures, gold, and brass, are laid

[016-263] Conceal’d in caverns in the sylvan shade.

[016-264] Hither, intent the rival rout to slay,

[016-265] And plan the scene of death, I bend my way;

[016-266] So Pallas wills—but thou, my son, explain

[016-267] The names and numbers of the audacious train;

[016-268] ’Tis mine to judge if better to employ

[016-269] Assistant force, or singly to destroy.”

[016-270] “O’er earth (returns the prince) resounds thy name,

[016-271] Thy well-tried wisdom, and thy martial fame,

[016-272] Yet at thy words I start, in wonder lost;

[016-273] Can we engage, not decades but an host?

[016-274] Can we alone in furious battle stand,

[016-275] Against that numerous and determined band?

[016-276] Hear then their numbers; from Dulichium came

[016-277] Twice twenty-six, all peers of mighty name.

[016-278] Six are their menial train: twice twelve the boast

[016-279] Of Samos; twenty from Zacynthus’ coast:

[016-280] And twelve our country’s pride; to these belong

[016-281] [[medon|Medon]] and [[phemius|Phemius]], skill’d in heavenly song.

[016-282] Two sewers from day to day the revels wait,

[016-283] Exact of taste, and serve the feast in state.

[016-284] With such a foe the unequal fight to try,

[016-285] Were by false courage unrevenged to die.

[016-286] Then what assistant powers you boast relate,

[016-287] Ere yet we mingle in the stern debate.”

[016-288] “Mark well my voice, (Ulysses straight replies:)

[016-289] What need of aids, if favour’d by the skies?

[016-290] If shielded to the dreadful fight we move,

[016-291] By mighty Pallas, and by thundering [[zeus|Jove]]?”

[016-292] “Sufficient they (Telemachus rejoin’d)

[016-293] Against the banded powers of all mankind:

[016-294] They, high enthroned above the rolling clouds,

[016-295] Wither the strength of man, and awe the gods.”

[016-296] “Such aids expect (he cries,) when strong in might

[016-297] We rise terrific to the task of fight.

[016-298] But thou, when morn salutes the aërial plain,

[016-299] The court revisit and the lawless train:

[016-300] Me thither in disguise Eumaeus leads,

[016-301] An aged mendicant in tatter’d weeds.

[016-302] There, if base scorn insult my reverend age,

[016-303] Bear it, my son! repress thy rising rage.

[016-304] If outraged, cease that outrage to repel;

[016-305] Bear it, my son! howe’er thy heart rebel.

[016-306] Yet strive by prayer and counsel to restrain

[016-307] Their lawless insults, though thou strive in vain:

[016-308] For wicked ears are deaf to wisdom’s call,

[016-309] And vengeance strikes whom Heaven has doom’d to fall.

[016-310] Once more attend: when she whose power inspires

[016-311] The thinking mind, my soul to vengeance fires,

[016-312] I give the sign: that instant, from beneath,

[016-313] Aloft convey the instruments of death,

[016-314] Armour and arms; and, if mistrust arise,

[016-315] Thus veil the truth in plausible disguise:

[016-316] “‘These glittering weapons, ere he sail’d to [[troy|Troy]],

[016-317] Ulysses view’d with stern heroic joy:

[016-318] Then, beaming o’er the illumined wall they shone;

[016-319] Now dust dishonours, all their lustre gone.

[016-320] I bear them hence (so Jove my soul inspires),

[016-321] From the pollution of the fuming fires;

[016-322] Lest when the bowl inflames, in vengeful mood

[016-323] Ye rush to arms, and stain the feast with blood:

[016-324] Oft ready swords in luckless hour incite

[016-325] The hand of wrath, and arm it for the fight.’

[016-326] “Such be the plea, and by the plea deceive:

[016-327] For Jove infatuates all, and all believe.

[016-328] Yet leave for each of us a sword to wield,

[016-329] A pointed javelin, and a fenceful shield.

[016-330] But by my blood that in thy bosom glows,

[016-331] By that regard a son his father owes;

[016-332] The secret, that thy father lives, retain

[016-333] Lock’d in thy bosom from the household train;

[016-334] Hide it from all; e’en from Eumaeus hide,

[016-335] From my dear father, and my dearer bride.

[016-336] One care remains, to note the loyal few

[016-337] Whose faith yet lasts among the menial crew;

[016-338] And noting, ere we rise in vengeance, prove

[016-339] Who love his prince; for sure you merit love.”

[016-340] To whom the youth: “To emulate, I aim,

[016-341] The brave and wise, and my great father’s fame.

[016-342] But reconsider, since the wisest err,

[016-343] [[poine|Vengeance]] resolved, ’tis dangerous to defer.

[016-344] What length of time must we consume in vain,

[016-345] Too curious to explore the menial train!

[016-346] While the proud foes, industrious to destroy

[016-347] Thy wealth, in riot the delay enjoy.

[016-348] Suffice it in this exigence alone

[016-349] To mark the damsels that attend the throne:

[016-350] Dispersed the youth reside; their faith to prove

[016-351] Jove grants henceforth, if thou hast spoke from Jove.”

[016-352] While in debate they waste the hours away,

[016-353] The associates of the prince repass’d the bay:

[016-354] With speed they guide the vessel to the shores;

[016-355] With speed debarking land the naval stores:

[016-356] Then, faithful to their charge, to Clytius bear,

[016-357] And trust the presents to his friendly care.

[016-358] Swift to the queen a herald flies to impart

[016-359] Her son’s return, and ease a parent’s heart:

[016-360] Lest a sad prey to ever-musing cares,

[016-361] Pale grief destroy what time awhile forbears.

[016-362] The incautious herald with impatience burns,

[016-363] And cries aloud, “Thy son, O queen, returns;”

[016-364] Eumaeus sage approach’d the imperial throne,

[016-365] And breathed his mandate to her ear alone,

[016-366] Then measured back the way. The suitor band,

[016-367] Stung to the soul, abash’d, confounded, stand;

[016-368] And issuing from the dome, before the gate,

[016-369] With clouded looks, a pale assembly sate.

[016-370] At length [[eurymachus|Eurymachus]]: “Our hopes are vain;

[016-371] Telemachus in triumph sails the main.

[016-372] Haste, rear the mast, the swelling shroud display;

[016-373] Haste, to our ambush’d friends the news convey!”

[016-374] Scarce had he spake, when, turning to the strand,

[016-375] [[amphinomus|Amphinomus]] survey’d the associate band;

[016-376] Full to the bay within the winding shores

[016-377] With gather’d sails they stood, and lifted oars.

[016-378] “O friends!” he cried, elate with rising joy,

[016-379] “See to the port secure the vessel fly!

[016-380] Some god has told them, or themselves survey

[016-381] The bark escaped; and measure back their way.”

[016-382] Swift at the word descending to the shores,

[016-383] They moor the vessel and unlade the stores:

[016-384] Then, moving from the strand, apart they sate,

[016-385] And full and frequent form’d a dire debate.

[016-386] “Lives then the boy? he lives ([[antinous|Antinous]] cries),

[016-387] The care of gods and favourite of the skies.

[016-388] All night we watch’d, till with her orient wheels

[016-389] Aurora flamed above the eastern hills,

[016-390] And from the lofty brow of rocks by day

[016-391] Took in the ocean with a broad survey

[016-392] Yet safe he sails; the powers celestial give

[016-393] To shun the hidden snares of death, and live.

[016-394] But die he shall, and thus condemn’d to bleed,

[016-395] Be now the scene of instant death decreed.

[016-396] Hope ye success? undaunted crush the foe.

[016-397] Is he not wise? know this, and strike the blow.

[016-398] Wait ye, till he to arms in council draws

[016-399] The Greeks, averse too justly to our cause?

[016-400] Strike, ere, the states convened, the foe betray

[016-401] Our murderous ambush on the watery way.

[016-402] Or choose ye vagrant from their rage to fly,

[016-403] Outcasts of earth, to breathe an unknown sky?

[016-404] The brave prevent misfortune; then be brave,

[016-405] And bury future danger in his grave.

[016-406] Returns he? ambush’d we’ll his walk invade,

[016-407] Or where he hides in solitude and shade;

[016-408] And give the palace to the queen a dower,

[016-409] Or him she blesses in the bridal hour.

[016-410] But if submissive you resign the sway,

[016-411] Slaves to a boy, go, flatter and obey.

[016-412] Retire we instant to our native reign,

[016-413] Nor be the wealth of kings consumed in vain;

[016-414] Then wed whom choice approves: the queen be given

[016-415] To some blest prince, the prince decreed by Heaven.”

[016-416] Abash’d, the suitor train his voice attends;

[016-417] Till from his throne Amphinomus ascends,

[016-418] Who o’er Dulichium stretch’d his spacious reign,

[016-419] A land of plenty, bless’d with every grain:

[016-420] Chief of the numbers who the queen address’d,

[016-421] And though displeasing, yet displeasing least.

[016-422] Soft were his words; his actions wisdom sway’d;

[016-423] Graceful awhile he paused, then mildly said:

[016-424] “O friends, forbear! and be the thought withstood:

[016-425] ’Tis horrible to shed imperial blood!

[016-426] Consult we first the all-seeing powers above,

[016-427] And the sure oracles of righteous Jove.

[016-428] If they assent, e’en by this hand he dies;

[016-429] If they forbid, I war not with the skies.”

[016-430] He said: the rival train his voice approved,

[016-431] And rising instant to the palace moved.

[016-432] Arrived, with wild tumultuous noise they sate,

[016-433] Recumbent on the shining thrones of state.

[016-435] The murderous counsel to the queen relates.

[016-436] Touch’d at the dreadful story, she descends:

[016-437] Her hasty steps a damsel train attends.

[016-438] Full where the dome its shining valves expands,

[016-439] Sudden before the rival powers she stands;

[016-440] And, veiling, decent, with a modest shade

[016-441] Her cheek, indignant to Antinous said:

[016-442] “O void of faith! of all bad men the worst!

[016-443] [[kleos|Renown]]’d for wisdom, by the abuse accursed!

[016-444] Mistaking fame proclaims thy generous mind:

[016-445] Thy deeds denote thee of the basest kind.

[016-446] Wretch! to destroy a prince that friendship gives,

[016-447] While in his guest his murderer he receives;

[016-448] Nor dread superior Jove, to whom belong

[016-449] The cause of suppliants, and revenge of wrong.

[016-450] Hast thou forgot, ungrateful as thou art,

[016-451] Who saved thy father with a friendly part?

[016-452] Lawless he ravaged with his martial powers

[016-453] The Taphian pirates on Thesprotia’s shores;

[016-454] Enraged, his life, his treasures they demand;

[016-455] Ulysses saved him from the avenger’s hand.

[016-456] And would’st thou evil for his good repay?

[016-457] His bed dishonour, and his house betray?

[016-458] Afflict his queen, and with a murderous hand

[016-459] Destroy his heir!—but cease, ’tis I command.”

[016-460] “Far hence those fears (Eurymachus replied,)

[016-461] O prudent princess! bid thy soul confide.

[016-462] Breathes there a man who dares that hero slay,

[016-463] While I behold the golden light of day?

[016-464] No: by the righteous powers of heaven I swear,

[016-465] His blood in vengeance smokes upon my spear.

[016-466] Ulysses, when my infant days I led,

[016-467] With wine sufficed me, and with dainties fed:

[016-468] My generous soul abhors the ungrateful part,

[016-469] And my friend’s son lives nearest to my heart.

[016-470] Then fear no mortal arm; if Heaven destroy,

[016-471] We must resign: for man is born to die.”

[016-472] Thus smooth he ended, yet his death conspired:

[016-473] Then sorrowing, with sad step the queen retired,

[016-474] With streaming eyes, all comfortless deplored,

[016-475] Touch’d with the dear remembrance of her lord:

[016-476] Nor ceased till Pallas bids her sorrows fly,

[016-477] And in soft slumber seal’d her flowing eye.

[016-478] And now Eumaeus, at the evening hour,

[016-479] Came late, returning to his sylvan bower.

[016-480] Ulysses and his son had dress’d with art

[016-481] A yearling boar, and gave the gods their part.

[016-482] Holy repast! That instant from the skies

[016-483] The martial goddess to Ulysses flies:

[016-484] She waves her golden wand, and reassumes

[016-485] From every feature every grace that blooms;

[016-486] At once his vestures change; at once she sheds

[016-487] Age o’er his limbs, that tremble as he treads:

[016-488] Lest to the queen the swain with transport fly,

[016-489] Unable to contain the unruly joy;

[016-490] When near he drew, the prince breaks forth: “Proclaim

[016-491] What tidings, friend? what speaks the voice of fame?

[016-492] Say, if the suitors measure back the main,

[016-493] Or still in ambush thirst for blood in vain?”

[016-494] “Whether (he cries) they measure back the flood,

[016-495] Or still in ambush thirst in vain for blood,

[016-496] Escaped my care: where lawless suitors sway,

[016-497] Thy mandate borne my soul disdain’d to stay.

[016-498] But from the Hermaean height I cast a view,

[016-499] Where to the port a bark high-bounding flew;

[016-500] Her freight a shining band: with martial air

[016-501] Each poised his shield, and each advanced his spear;

[016-502] And, if aright these searching eyes survey,

[016-503] The eluded suitors stem the watery way.”

[016-504] The prince, well pleased to disappoint their wiles,

[016-505] Steals on his sire a glance, and secret smiles.

[016-506] And now, a short repast prepared, they fed

[016-507] Till the keen rage of craving hunger fled:

[016-508] Then to repose withdrawn, apart they lay,

[016-509] And in soft sleep forgot the cares of day.
