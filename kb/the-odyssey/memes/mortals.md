Mortals in Homer exist in a world shaped by divine will, yet their choices — Odysseus's determination to return, Penelope's cunning, Telemachus's coming of age — drive the epic's central narrative. This catalogue spans all social strata, from the royal house of Ithaca to the lowliest swineherd.
