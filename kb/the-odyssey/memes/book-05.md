[005-002] ARGUMENT.

[005-003] THE DEPARTURE OF ULYSSES FROM CALYPSO

[005-004] Pallas in a council of the gods complains of the detention of [[odysseus|Ulysses]]

[005-005] in the [[calypsos-island|Island of Calypso]]: whereupon [[hermes|Mercury]] is sent to command his

[005-006] removal. The seat of [[calypso|Calypso]] described. She consents with much

[005-007] difficulty; and Ulysses builds a vessel with his own hands, in which he

[005-008] embarks. [[poseidon|Neptune]] overtakes him with a terrible tempest, in which he is

[005-009] shipwrecked, and in the last danger of death; till Lencothea, a

[005-010] sea-goddess, assists him, and, after innumerable perils, he gets ashore

[005-011] on Phæacia.

[005-012] The saffron morn, with early blushes spread,

[005-013] Now rose refulgent from Tithonus’ bed;

[005-014] With new-born day to gladden mortal sight,

[005-015] And gild the courts of heaven with sacred light.

[005-016] Then met the eternal synod of the sky,

[005-017] Before the god, who thunders from on high,

[005-018] Supreme in might, sublime in majesty.

[005-019] Pallas, to these, deplores the unequal fates

[005-020] Of wise Ulysses and his toils relates:

[005-021] Her hero’s danger touch’d the pitying power,

[005-022] The nymph’s seducements, and the magic bower.

[005-023] Thus she began her plaint: “Immortal [[zeus|Jove]]!

[005-024] And you who fill the blissful seats above!

[005-025] Let kings no more with gentle mercy sway,

[005-026] Or bless a people willing to obey,

[005-027] But crush the nations with an iron rod,

[005-028] And every monarch be the scourge of God.

[005-029] If from your thoughts Ulysses you remove,

[005-030] Who ruled his subjects with a father’s love,

[005-031] Sole in an isle, encircled by the main,

[005-032] Abandon’d, banish’d from his native reign,

[005-033] Unbless’d he sighs, detained by lawless charms,

[005-034] And press’d unwilling in Calypso’s arms.

[005-035] Nor friends are there, nor vessels to convey,

[005-036] Nor oars to cut the immeasurable way.

[005-037] And now fierce traitors, studious to destroy

[005-038] His only son, their ambush’d fraud employ;

[005-039] Who, pious, following his great father’s fame,

[005-040] To sacred [[pylos|Pylos]] and to [[sparta|Sparta]] came.”

[005-041] “What words are these? (replied the power who forms

[005-042] The clouds of night, and darkens heaven with storms;)

[005-043] Is not already in thy soul decreed,

[005-044] The chief’s return shall make the guilty bleed?

[005-045] What cannot Wisdom do? Thou may’st restore

[005-046] The son in safety to his native shore;

[005-047] While the fell foes, who late in ambush lay,

[005-048] With fraud defeated measure back their way.”

[005-049] Then thus to Hermes the command was given:

[005-050] “Hermes, thou chosen messenger of heaven!

[005-051] Go, to the nymph be these our orders borne

[005-052] ’Tis Jove’s decree, Ulysses shall return:

[005-053] The patient man shall view his old abodes,

[005-054] Nor helped by mortal hand, nor guiding gods

[005-055] In twice ten days shall fertile [[scheria|Scheria]] find,

[005-056] Alone, and floating to the wave and wind.

[005-057] The bold Phæacians there, whose haughty line

[005-058] Is mixed with gods, half human, half divine,

[005-059] The chief shall honour as some heavenly guest,

[005-060] And swift transport him to his place of rest,

[005-061] His vessels loaded with a plenteous store

[005-062] Of brass, of vestures, and resplendent ore

[005-063] (A richer prize than if his joyful isle

[005-064] Received him charged with [[troy|Ilion]]’s noble spoil),

[005-065] His friends, his country, he shall see, though late:

[005-066] Such is our sovereign will, and such is fate.”

[005-067] He spoke. The god who mounts the winged winds

[005-068] Fast to his feet the golden pinions binds,

[005-069] That high through fields of air his flight sustain

[005-070] O’er the wide earth, and o’er the boundless main:

[005-071] He grasps the wand that causes sleep to fly,

[005-072] Or in soft slumber seals the wakeful eye;

[005-073] Then shoots from heaven to high Pieria’s steep,

[005-074] And stoops incumbent on the rolling deep.

[005-075] So watery fowl, that seek their fishy food,

[005-076] With wings expanded o’er the foaming flood,

[005-077] Now sailing smooth the level surface sweep,

[005-078] Now dip their pinions in the briny deep;

[005-079] Thus o’er the world of waters Hermes flew,

[005-080] Till now the distant island rose in view:

[005-081] Then, swift ascending from the azure wave,

[005-082] He took the path that winded to the cave.

[005-083] Large was the grot, in which the nymph he found

[005-084] (The fair-hair’d nymph with every beauty crown’d).

[005-085] The cave was brighten’d with a rising blaze;

[005-086] Cedar and frankincense, an odorous pile,

[005-087] Flamed on the hearth, and wide perfumed the isle;

[005-088] While she with work and song the time divides,

[005-089] And through the loom the golden shuttle guides.

[005-090] Without the grot a various sylvan scene

[005-091] Appear’d around, and groves of living green;

[005-092] Poplars and alders ever quivering play’d,

[005-093] And nodding cypress form’d a fragrant shade:

[005-094] On whose high branches, waving with the storm,

[005-095] The birds of broadest wing their mansions form,—

[005-096] The chough, the sea-mew, the loquacious crow,—

[005-097] and scream aloft, and skim the deeps below.

[005-098] Depending vines the shelving cavern screen.

[005-099] With purple clusters blushing through the green.

[005-100] Four limpid fountains from the clefts distil:

[005-101] And every fountain pours a several rill,

[005-102] In mazy windings wandering down the hill:

[005-103] Where bloomy meads with vivid greens were crown’d,

[005-104] And glowing violets threw odours round.

[005-105] A scene, where, if a god should cast his sight,

[005-106] A god might gaze, and wander with delight!

[005-107] Joy touch’d the messenger of heaven: he stay’d

[005-108] Entranced, and all the blissful haunts surveyed.

[005-109] Him, entering in the cave, Calypso knew;

[005-110] For powers celestial to each other’s view

[005-111] Stand still confess’d, though distant far they lie

[005-112] To habitants of earth, or sea, or sky.

[005-113] But sad Ulysses, by himself apart,

[005-114] Pour’d the big sorrows of his swelling heart;

[005-115] All on the lonely shore he sate to weep,

[005-116] And roll’d his eyes around the restless deep:

[005-117] Toward his loved coast he roll’d his eyes in vain,

[005-118] Till, dimm’d with rising grief, they stream’d again.

[005-119] Now graceful seated on her shining throne,

[005-120] To Hermes thus the nymph divine begun:

[005-121] “God of the golden wand! on what behest

[005-122] Arrivest thou here, an unexpected guest?

[005-123] Loved as thou art, thy free injunctions lay;

[005-124] ’Tis mine with joy and duty to obey.

[005-125] Till now a stranger, in a happy hour

[005-126] Approach, and taste the dainties of my bower.”

[005-127] Thus having spoke, the nymph the table spread

[005-128] (Ambrosial cates, with nectar rosy-red);

[005-129] Hermes the hospitable rite partook,

[005-130] Divine refection! then, recruited, spoke:

[005-131] “What moves this journey from my native sky,

[005-132] A goddess asks, nor can a god deny.

[005-133] Hear then the truth. By mighty Jove’s command

[005-134] Unwilling have I trod this pleasing land:

[005-135] For who, self-moved, with weary wing would sweep

[005-136] Such length of ocean and unmeasured deep;

[005-137] A world of waters! far from all the ways

[005-138] Where men frequent, or sacred altars blaze!

[005-139] But to Jove’s will submission we must pay;

[005-140] What power so great to dare to disobey?

[005-141] A man, he says, a man resides with thee,

[005-142] Of all his kind most worn with misery.

[005-143] The Greeks, (whose arms for nine long year employ’d

[005-144] Their force on Ilion, in the tenth destroy’d,)

[005-145] At length, embarking in a luckless hour,

[005-146] With conquest proud, incensed [[athena|Minerva]]’s power:

[005-147] Hence on the guilty race her vengeance hurl’d,

[005-148] With storms pursued them through the liquid world.

[005-149] There all his vessels sunk beneath the wave!

[005-150] There all his dear companions found their grave!

[005-151] Saved from the jaws of death by Heaven’s decree,

[005-152] The tempest drove him to these shores and thee.

[005-153] Him, Jove now orders to his native lands

[005-154] Straight to dismiss: so destiny commands:

[005-155] Impatient [[moira|Fate]] his near return attends,

[005-156] And calls him to his country, and his friends.”

[005-157] E’en to her inmost soul the goddess shook;

[005-158] Then thus her anguish, and her passion broke:

[005-159] “Ungracious gods! with spite and envy cursed!

[005-160] Still to your own ethereal race the worst!

[005-161] Ye envy mortal and immortal joy,

[005-162] And love, the only sweet of life destroy,

[005-163] Did ever goddess by her charms engage

[005-164] A favour’d mortal, and not feel your rage?

[005-165] So when Aurora sought Orion’s love,

[005-166] Her joys disturbed your blissful hours above,

[005-167] Till, in Ortygia Dian’s winged dart

[005-168] Had pierced the hapless hunter to the heart,

[005-169] So when the covert of the thrice-eared field

[005-170] Saw stately Ceres to her passion yield,

[005-171] Scarce could Iasion taste her heavenly charms,

[005-172] But Jove’s swift lightning scorched him in her arms.

[005-173] And is it now my turn, ye mighty powers!

[005-174] Am I the envy of your blissful bowers?

[005-175] A man, an outcast to the storm and wave,

[005-176] It was my crime to pity, and to save;

[005-177] When he who thunders rent his bark in twain,

[005-178] And sunk his brave companions in the main,

[005-179] Alone, abandon’d, in mid-ocean tossed,

[005-180] The sport of winds, and driven from every coast,

[005-181] Hither this man of miseries I led,

[005-182] Received the friendless, and the hungry fed;

[005-183] Nay promised (vainly promised) to bestow

[005-184] Immortal life, exempt from age and woe.

[005-185] ’Tis past-and Jove decrees he shall remove;

[005-186] Gods as we are, we are but slaves to Jove.

[005-187] Go then he must (he must, if he ordain,

[005-188] Try all those dangers, all those deeps, again);

[005-189] But never, never shall Calypso send

[005-190] To toils like these her husband and her friend.

[005-191] What ships have I, what sailors to convey,

[005-192] What oars to cut the long laborious way?

[005-193] Yet I’ll direct the safest means to go;

[005-194] That last advice is all I can bestow.”

[005-195] To her the power who hears the charming rod;

[005-196] “Dismiss the man, nor irritate the god;

[005-197] Prevent the rage of him who reigns above,

[005-198] For what so dreadful as the wrath of Jove?”

[005-199] Thus having said, he cut the cleaving sky,

[005-200] And in a moment vanished from her eye,

[005-201] The nymph, obedient to divine command,

[005-202] To seek Ulysses, paced along the sand,

[005-203] Him pensive on the lonely beach she found,

[005-204] With streaming eyes in briny torrents drown’d,

[005-205] And inly pining for his native shore;

[005-206] For now the soft enchantress pleased no more;

[005-207] For now, reluctant, and constrained by charms,

[005-208] Absent he lay in her desiring arms,

[005-209] In slumber wore the heavy night away,

[005-210] On rocks and shores consumed the tedious day;

[005-211] There sate all desolate, and sighed alone,

[005-212] With echoing sorrows made the mountains groan.

[005-213] And roll’d his eyes o’er all the restless main,

[005-214] Till, dimmed with rising grief, they streamed again.

[005-215] Here, on his musing mood the goddess press’d,

[005-216] Approaching soft, and thus the chief address’d:

[005-217] “Unhappy man! to wasting woes a prey,

[005-218] No more in sorrows languish life away:

[005-219] Free as the winds I give thee now to rove:

[005-220] Go, fell the timber of yon lofty grove,

[005-221] And form a raft, and build the rising ship,

[005-222] Sublime to bear thee o’er the gloomy deep.

[005-223] To store the vessel let the care be mine,

[005-224] With water from the rock and rosy wine,

[005-225] And life-sustaining bread, and fair array,

[005-226] And prosperous gales to waft thee on the way.

[005-227] These, if the gods with my desire comply

[005-228] (The gods, alas, more mighty far than I,

[005-229] And better skill’d in dark events to come),

[005-230] In peace shall land thee at thy native home.”

[005-231] With sighs Ulysses heard the words she spoke,

[005-232] Then thus his melancholy silence broke:

[005-233] “Some other motive, goddess! sways thy mind

[005-234] (Some close design, or turn of womankind),

[005-235] Nor my return the end, nor this the way,

[005-236] On a slight raft to pass the swelling sea,

[005-237] Huge, horrid, vast! where scarce in safety sails

[005-238] The best-built ship, though Jove inspires the gales.

[005-239] The bold proposal how shall I fulfil,

[005-240] Dark as I am, unconscious of thy will?

[005-241] Swear, then, thou mean’st not what my soul forebodes;

[005-242] Swear by the solemn oath that binds the gods.”

## Section 17

[005-243] Him, while he spoke, with smiles Calypso eyed,

[005-244] And gently grasp’d his hand, and thus replied:

[005-245] “This shows thee, friend, by old experience taught,

[005-246] And learn’d in all the wiles of human thought,

[005-247] How prone to doubt, how cautious, are the wise!

[005-248] But hear, O earth, and hear, ye sacred skies!

[005-249] And thou, O Styx! whose formidable floods

[005-250] Glide through the shades, and bind the attesting gods!

[005-251] No form’d design, no meditated end,

[005-252] Lurks in the counsel of thy faithful friend;

[005-253] Kind the persuasion, and sincere my aim;

[005-254] The same my practice, were my fate the same.

[005-255] Heaven has not cursed me with a heart of steel,

[005-256] But given the sense to pity, and to feel.”

[005-257] Thus having said, the goddess marched before:

[005-258] He trod her footsteps in the sandy shore.

[005-259] At the cool cave arrived, they took their state;

[005-260] He filled the throne where Mercury had sate.

[005-261] For him the nymph a rich repast ordains,

[005-262] Such as the mortal life of man sustains;

[005-263] Before herself were placed the the cates divine,

[005-264] Ambrosial banquet and celestial wine.

[005-265] Their hunger satiate, and their thirst repress’d,

[005-266] Thus spoke Calypso to her godlike guest:

[005-267] “Ulysses! (with a sigh she thus began;)

[005-268] O sprung from gods! in wisdom more than man!

[005-269] Is then thy home the passion of thy heart?

[005-270] Thus wilt thou leave me, are we thus to part?

[005-271] Farewell! and ever joyful mayst thou be,

[005-272] Nor break the transport with one thought of me.

[005-273] But ah, Ulysses! wert thou given to know

[005-274] What Fate yet dooms these still to undergo,

[005-275] Thy heart might settle in this scene of ease.

[005-276] And e’en these slighted charms might learn to please.

[005-277] A willing goddess, and immortal life,

[005-278] Might banish from thy mind an absent wife.

[005-279] Am I inferior to a mortal dame?

[005-280] Less soft my feature less august my frame?

[005-281] Or shall the daughters of mankind compare

[005-282] Their earth born beauties with the heavenly fair?”

[005-283] “Alas! for this (the prudent man replies)

[005-284] Against Ulysses shall thy anger rise?

[005-285] Loved and adored, O goddess as thou art,

[005-286] Forgive the weakness of a human heart.

[005-287] Though well I see thy graces far above

[005-288] The dear, though mortal, object of my love,

[005-289] Of youth eternal well the difference know,

[005-290] And the short date of fading charms below;

[005-291] Yet every day, while absent thus I roam,

[005-292] I languish to return and die at home.

[005-293] Whate’er the gods shall destine me to bear;

[005-294] In the black ocean or the watery war,

[005-295] ’Tis mine to master with a constant mind;

[005-296] Inured to perils, to the worst resign’d,

[005-297] By seas, by wars, so many dangers run;

[005-298] Still I can suffer; their high will he done!”

[005-299] Thus while he spoke, the beamy sun descends,

[005-300] And rising night her friendly shade extends,

[005-301] To the close grot the lonely pair remove,

[005-302] And slept delighted with the gifts of love.

[005-303] When rosy morning call’d them from their rest,

[005-304] Ulysses robed him in the cloak and vest.

[005-305] The nymph’s fair head a veil transparent graced,

[005-306] Her swelling loins a radiant zone embraced

[005-307] With flowers of gold; an under robe, unbound,

[005-308] In snowy waves flow’d glittering on the ground.

[005-309] Forth issuing thus, she gave him first to wield

[005-310] A weighty axe with truest temper steeled,

[005-311] And double-edged; the handle smooth and plain,

[005-312] Wrought of the clouded olive’s easy grain;

[005-313] And next, a wedge to drive with sweepy sway

[005-314] Then to the neighboring forest led the way.

[005-315] On the lone island’s utmost verge there stood

[005-316] Of poplars, pine, and firs, a lofty wood,

[005-317] Whose leafless summits to the skies aspire,

[005-318] Scorch’d by the sun, or seared by heavenly fire

[005-319] (Already dried). These pointing out to view,

[005-320] The nymph just show’d him, and with tears withdrew.

[005-321] Now toils the hero: trees on trees o’erthrown

[005-322] Fall crackling round him, and the forests groan:

[005-323] Sudden, full twenty on the plain are strow’d,

[005-324] And lopp’d and lighten’d of their branchy load.

[005-325] At equal angles these disposed to join,

[005-326] He smooth’d and squared them by the rule and line,

[005-327] (The wimbles for the work Calypso found)

[005-328] With those he pierced them and with clinchers bound.

[005-329] Long and capacious as a shipwright forms

[005-330] Some bark’s broad bottom to out-ride the storms,

[005-331] So large he built the raft; then ribb’d it strong

[005-332] From space to space, and nail’d the planks along;

[005-333] These form’d the sides: the deck he fashion’d last;

[005-334] Then o’er the vessel raised the taper mast,

[005-335] With crossing sail-yards dancing in the wind;

[005-336] And to the helm the guiding rudder join’d

[005-337] (With yielding osiers fenced, to break the force

[005-338] Of surging waves, and steer the steady course).

[005-339] Thy loom, Calypso, for the future sails

[005-340] Supplied the cloth, capacious of the gales.

[005-341] With stays and cordage last he rigged the ship,

[005-342] And, roll’d on levers, launch’d her in the deep.

[005-343] Four days were pass’d, and now the work complete,

[005-344] Shone the fifth morn, when from her sacred seat

[005-345] The nymph dismiss’d him (odorous garments given),

[005-346] And bathed in fragrant oils that breathed of heaven:

[005-347] Then fill’d two goatskins with her hands divine,

[005-348] With water one, and one with sable wine:

[005-349] Of every kind, provisions heaved aboard;

[005-350] And the full decks with copious viands stored.

[005-351] The goddess, last, a gentle breeze supplies,

[005-352] To curl old [[oceanus|Ocean]], and to warm the skies.

[005-353] And now, rejoicing in the prosperous gales,

[005-354] With beating heart Ulysses spreads his sails;

[005-355] Placed at the helm he sate, and mark’d the skies,

[005-356] Nor closed in sleep his ever-watchful eyes.

[005-357] There view’d the Pleiads, and the Northern Team,

[005-358] And great Orion’s more refulgent beam.

[005-359] To which, around the axle of the sky,

[005-360] The Bear, revolving, points his golden eye:

[005-361] Who shines exalted on the ethereal plain,

[005-362] Nor bathes his blazing forehead in the main.

[005-363] Far on the left those radiant fires to keep

[005-364] The nymph directed, as he sail’d the deep.

[005-365] Full seventeen nights he cut the foaming way:

[005-366] The distant land appear’d the following day:

[005-367] Then swell’d to sight Phæacia’s dusky coast,

[005-368] And woody mountains, half in vapours lost;

[005-369] That lay before him indistinct and vast,

[005-370] Like a broad shield amid the watery waste.

[005-371] But him, thus voyaging the deeps below,

[005-372] From far, on Solyme’s aërial brow,

[005-373] The king of ocean saw, and seeing burn’d

[005-374] (From Æthiopia’s happy climes return’d);

[005-375] The raging monarch shook his azure head,

[005-376] And thus in secret to his soul he said:

[005-377] “Heavens! how uncertain are the powers on high!

[005-378] Is then reversed the sentence of the sky,

[005-379] In one man’s favour; while a distant guest

[005-380] I shared secure the Æthiopian feast?

[005-381] Behold how near Phæacia’s land he draws;

[005-382] The land affix’d by Fate’s eternal laws

[005-383] To end his toils. Is then our anger vain?

[005-384] No; if this sceptre yet commands the main.”

[005-385] He spoke, and high the forky trident hurl’d,

[005-386] Rolls clouds on clouds, and stirs the watery world,

[005-387] At once the face of earth and sea deforms,

[005-388] Swells all the winds, and rouses all the storms.

[005-389] Down rushed the night: east, west, together roar;

[005-390] And south and north roll mountains to the shore.

[005-391] Then shook the hero, to despair resign’d,

[005-392] And question’d thus his yet unconquer’d mind;

[005-393] “Wretch that I am! what farther fates attend

[005-394] This life of toils, and what my destined end?

[005-395] Too well, alas! the island goddess knew

[005-396] On the black sea what perils should ensue.

[005-397] New horrors now this destined head inclose;

[005-398] Untill’d is yet the measure of my woes;

[005-399] With what a cloud the brows of heaven are crown’d;

[005-400] What raging winds! what roaring waters round!

[005-401] ’Tis Jove himself the swelling tempest rears;

[005-402] Death, present death, on every side appears.

[005-403] Happy! thrice happy! who, in battle slain,

[005-404] Press’d in [[agamemnon|Atrides]]’ cause the Trojan plain!

[005-405] Oh! had I died before that well-fought wall!

[005-406] Had some distinguish’d day renown’d my fall

[005-407] (Such as was that when showers of javelins fled

[005-408] From conquering Troy around [[achilles|Achilles]] dead),

[005-409] All Greece had paid me solemn funerals then,

[005-410] And spread my glory with the sons of men.

[005-411] A shameful fate now hides my hapless head,

[005-412] Unwept, unnoted, and for ever dead!”

[005-413] A mighty wave rush’d o’er him as he spoke,

[005-414] The raft is cover’d, and the mast is broke;

[005-415] Swept from the deck and from the rudder torn,

[005-416] Far on the swelling surge the chief was borne;

[005-417] While by the howling tempest rent in twain

[005-418] Flew sail and sail-yards rattling o’er the main.

[005-419] Long-press’d, he heaved beneath the weighty wave,

[005-420] Clogg’d by the cumbrous vest Calypso gave;

[005-421] At length, emerging, from his nostrils wide

[005-422] And gushing mouth effused the briny tide;

[005-423] E’en then not mindless of his last retreat,

[005-424] He seized the raft, and leap’d into his seat,

[005-425] Strong with the fear of death. In rolling flood,

[005-426] Now here, now there, impell’d the floating wood

[005-427] As when a heap of gather’d thorns is cast,

[005-428] Now to, now fro, before the autumnal blast;

[005-429] Together clung, it rolls around the field;

[005-430] So roll’d the float, and so its texture held:

[005-431] And now the south, and now the north, bear sway,

[005-432] And now the east the foamy floods obey,

[005-498] Now, scarce withdrawn the fierce earth-shaking power,

[005-512] The winds were hush’d, the billows scarcely curl’d,
