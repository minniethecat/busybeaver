[001-001] BOOK I.

[001-002] ARGUMENT.

[001-003] MINERVA’S DESCENT TO ITHACA.

[001-004] The poem opens within forty eight days of the arrival of [[odysseus|Ulysses]] in his

[001-005] dominions. He had now remained seven years in the [[calypsos-island|Island of Calypso]],

[001-006] when the gods assembled in council, proposed the method of his

[001-007] departure from thence and his return to his native country. For this

[001-008] purpose it is concluded to send [[hermes|Mercury]] to [[calypso|Calypso]], and Pallas

[001-009] immediately descends to [[ithaca|Ithaca]]. She holds a conference with [[telemachus|Telemachus]],

[001-010] in the shape of Mantes, king of Taphians; in which she advises him to

[001-011] take a journey in quest of his father Ulysses, to [[pylos|Pylos]] and [[sparta|Sparta]],

[001-012] where [[nestor|Nestor]] and [[menelaus|Menelaus]] yet reigned; then, after having visibly

[001-013] displayed her divinity, disappears. The suitors of [[penelope|Penelope]] make great

[001-014] entertainments, and riot in her palace till night. [[phemius|Phemius]] sings to

[001-015] them the return of the Grecians, till Penelope puts a stop to the song.

[001-016] Some words arise between the suitors and Telemachus, who summons the

[001-017] council to meet the day following.

## Section 6

[001-018] The man for wisdom’s various arts renown’d,

[001-019] Long exercised in woes, O Muse! resound;

[001-020] Who, when his arms had wrought the destined fall

[001-021] Of sacred [[troy|Troy]], and razed her heaven-built wall,

[001-022] Wandering from clime to clime, observant stray’d,

[001-023] Their manners noted, and their states survey’d,

[001-024] On stormy seas unnumber’d toils he bore,

[001-025] Safe with his friends to gain his natal shore:

[001-026] Vain toils! their impious folly dared to prey

[001-027] On herds devoted to the god of day;

[001-028] The god vindictive doom’d them never more

[001-029] (Ah, men unbless’d!) to touch that natal shore.

[001-030] Oh, snatch some portion of these acts from fate,

[001-031] Celestial Muse! and to our world relate.

[001-032] Now at their native realms the Greeks arrived;

[001-033] All who the wars of ten long years survived;

[001-034] And ’scaped the perils of the gulfy main.

[001-035] Ulysses, sole of all the victor train,

[001-036] An exile from his dear paternal coast,

[001-037] Deplored his absent queen and empire lost.

[001-038] Calypso in her caves constrain’d his stay,

[001-039] With sweet, reluctant, amorous delay;

[001-040] In vain-for now the circling years disclose

[001-041] The day predestined to reward his woes.

[001-042] At length his Ithaca is given by fate,

[001-043] Where yet new labours his arrival wait;

[001-044] At length their rage the hostile powers restrain,

[001-045] All but the ruthless monarch of the main.

[001-046] But now the god, remote, a heavenly guest,

[001-047] In Æthiopia graced the genial feast

[001-048] (A race divided, whom with sloping rays

[001-049] The rising and descending sun surveys);

[001-050] There on the world’s extremest verge revered

[001-051] With hecatombs and prayer in pomp preferr’d,

[001-052] Distant he lay: while in the bright abodes

[001-053] Of high [[olympus|Olympus]], [[zeus|Jove]] convened the gods:

[001-054] The assembly thus the sire supreme address’d,

[001-055] AEgysthus’ fate revolving in his breast,

[001-056] Whom [[orestes|young Orestes]] to the dreary coast

[001-057] Of Pluto sent, a blood-polluted ghost.

[001-058] “Perverse mankind! whose wills, created free,

[001-059] Charge all their woes on absolute degree;

[001-060] All to the dooming gods their guilt translate,

[001-061] And follies are miscall’d the crimes of fate.

[001-062] When to his lust AEgysthus gave the rein,

[001-063] Did fate, or we, the adulterous act constrain?

[001-064] Did fate, or we, when great [[agamemnon|Atrides]] died,

[001-065] Urge the bold traitor to the regicide?

[001-066] Hermes I sent, while yet his soul remain’d

[001-067] Sincere from royal blood, and faith profaned;

[001-068] To warn the wretch, that young Orestes, grown

[001-069] To manly years, should re-assert the throne.

[001-070] Yet, impotent of mind, and uncontroll’d,

[001-071] He plunged into the gulf which Heaven foretold.”

[001-072] Here paused the god; and pensive thus replies

[001-073] [[athena|Minerva]], graceful with her azure eyes:

[001-074] “O thou! from whom the whole creation springs,

[001-075] The source of power on earth derived to kings!

[001-076] His death was equal to the direful deed;

[001-077] So may the man of blood be doomed to bleed!

[001-078] But grief and rage alternate wound my breast

[001-079] For brave Ulysses, still by fate oppress’d.

[001-080] Amidst an isle, around whose rocky shore

[001-081] The forests murmur, and the surges roar,

[001-082] The blameless hero from his wish’d-for home

[001-083] A goddess guards in her enchanted dome;

[001-084] (Atlas her sire, to whose far-piercing eye

[001-085] The wonders of the deep expanded lie;

[001-086] The eternal columns which on earth he rears

[001-087] End in the starry vault, and prop the spheres).

[001-088] By his fair daughter is the chief confined,

[001-089] Who soothes to dear delight his anxious mind;

[001-090] Successless all her soft caresses prove,

[001-091] To banish from his breast his country’s love;

[001-092] To see the smoke from his loved palace rise,

[001-093] While the dear isle in distant prospect lies,

[001-094] With what contentment could he close his eyes!

[001-095] And will Omnipotence neglect to save

[001-096] The suffering virtue of the wise and brave?

[001-097] Must he, whose altars on the Phrygian shore

[001-098] With frequent rites, and pure, avow’d thy power,

[001-099] Be doom’d the worst of human ills to prove,

[001-100] Unbless’d, abandon’d to the wrath of Jove?”

[001-101] “Daughter! what words have pass’d thy lips unweigh’d!

[001-102] (Replied the Thunderer to the martial maid;)

[001-103] Deem not unjustly by my doom oppress’d,

[001-104] Of human race the wisest and the best.

[001-105] [[poseidon|Neptune]], by prayer repentant rarely won,

[001-106] Afflicts the chief, to avenge his giant son,

[001-107] Whose visual orb Ulysses robb’d of light;

[001-108] Great Polypheme, of more than mortal might?

[001-109] Him young Thousa bore (the bright increase

[001-110] Of Phorcys, dreaded in the sounds and seas);

[001-111] Whom Neptune eyed with bloom of beauty bless’d,

[001-112] And in his cave the yielding nymph compress’d

[001-113] For this the god constrains the Greek to roam,

[001-114] A hopeless exile from his native home,

[001-115] From death alone exempt—but cease to mourn;

[001-116] Let all combine to achieve his wish’d return;

[001-117] Neptune atoned, his wrath shall now refrain,

[001-118] Or thwart the synod of the gods in vain.”

[001-119] “Father and king adored!” Minerva cried,

[001-120] “Since all who in the Olympian bower reside

[001-121] Now make the wandering Greek their public care,

[001-122] Let Hermes to the Atlantic isle repair;

[001-123] Bid him, arrived in bright Calypso’s court,

[001-124] The sanction of the assembled powers report:

[001-125] That wise Ulysses to his native land

[001-126] Must speed, obedient to their high command.

[001-127] Meantime Telemachus, the blooming heir

[001-128] Of sea-girt Ithaca, demands my care;

[001-129] ’Tis mine to form his green, unpractised years

[001-130] In sage debates; surrounded with his peers,

[001-131] To save the state, and timely to restrain

[001-132] The bold intrusion of the suitor-train;

[001-133] Who crowd his palace, and with lawless power

[001-134] His herds and flocks in feastful rites devour.

[001-135] To distant Sparta, and the spacious waste

[001-136] Of Sandy Pyle, the royal youth shall haste.

[001-137] There, warm with filial love, the cause inquire

[001-138] That from his realm retards his god-like sire;

[001-139] Delivering early to the voice of fame

[001-140] The promise of a green immortal name.”

[001-141] She said: the sandals of celestial mould,

[001-142] Fledged with ambrosial plumes, and rich with gold,

[001-143] Surround her feet: with these sublime she sails

[001-144] The aërial space, and mounts the winged gales;

[001-145] O’er earth and ocean wide prepared to soar,

[001-146] Her dreaded arm a beamy javelin bore,

[001-147] Ponderous and vast: which, when her fury burns,

[001-148] Proud tyrants humbles, and whole hosts o’erturns.

[001-149] From high Olympus prone her flight she bends,

[001-150] And in the realms of Ithaca descends,

[001-151] Her lineaments divine, the grave disguise

[001-152] Of [[mentor|Mentes]]’ form conceal’d from human eyes

[001-153] (Mentes, the monarch of the Taphian land);

[001-154] A glittering spear waved awful in her hand.

[001-155] There in the portal placed, the heaven-born maid

[001-156] Enormous riot and misrule survey’d.

[001-157] On hides of beeves, before the palace gate

[001-158] (Sad spoils of luxury), the suitors sate.

[001-159] With rival art, and ardour in their mien,

[001-160] At chess they vie, to captivate the queen;

[001-161] Divining of their loves. Attending nigh,

[001-162] A menial train the flowing bowl supply.

[001-163] Others, apart, the spacious hall prepare,

[001-164] And form the costly feast with busy care.

[001-165] There young Telemachus, his bloomy face

[001-166] Glowing celestial sweet, with godlike grace

[001-167] Amid the circle shines: but hope and fear

[001-168] (Painful vicissitude!) his bosom tear.

[001-169] Now, imaged in his mind, he sees restored

[001-170] In peace and joy the people’s rightful lord;

[001-171] The proud oppressors fly the vengeful sword.

[001-172] While his fond soul these fancied triumphs swell’d,

[001-173] The stranger guest the royal youth beheld;

[001-174] Grieved that a visitant so long should wait

[001-175] Unmark’d, unhonour’d, at a monarch’s gate;

[001-176] Instant he flew with hospitable haste,

[001-177] And the new friend with courteous air embraced.

[001-178] “Stranger, whoe’er thou art, securely rest,

[001-179] Affianced in my faith, a ready guest;

[001-180] Approach the dome, the social banquet share,

[001-181] And then the purpose of thy soul declare.”

[001-182] Thus affable and mild, the prince precedes,

[001-183] And to the dome the unknown celestial leads.

[001-184] The spear receiving from the hand, he placed

[001-185] Against a column, fair with sculpture graced;

[001-186] Where seemly ranged in peaceful order stood

[001-187] Ulysses’ arms now long disused to blood.

[001-188] He led the goddess to the sovereign seat,

[001-189] Her feet supported with a stool of state

[001-190] (A purple carpet spread the pavement wide);

[001-191] Then drew his seat, familiar, to her side;

[001-192] Far from the suitor-train, a brutal crowd,

[001-193] With insolence, and wine, elate and loud:

[001-194] Where the free guest, unnoted, might relate,

[001-195] If haply conscious, of his father’s fate.

[001-196] The golden ewer a maid obsequious brings,

[001-197] Replenish’d from the cool, translucent springs;

[001-198] With copious water the bright vase supplies

[001-199] A silver laver of capacious size;

[001-200] They wash. The tables in fair order spread,

[001-201] They heap the glittering canisters with bread:

[001-202] Viands of various kinds allure the taste,

[001-203] Of choicest sort and savour, rich repast!

[001-204] Delicious wines the attending herald brought;

[001-205] The gold gave lustre to the purple draught.

[001-206] Lured with the vapour of the fragrant feast,

[001-207] In rush’d the suitors with voracious haste;

[001-208] Marshall’d in order due, to each a sewer

[001-209] Presents, to bathe his hands, a radiant ewer.

[001-210] Luxurious then they feast. Observant round

[001-211] Gay stripling youths the brimming goblets crown’d.

[001-212] The rage of hunger quell’d, they all advance

[001-213] And form to measured airs the mazy dance;

[001-214] To Phemius was consign’d the chorded lyre,

[001-215] Whose hand reluctant touch’d the warbling wire;

[001-216] Phemius, whose voice divine could sweetest sing

[001-217] High strains responsive to the vocal string.

[001-218] Meanwhile, in whispers to his heavenly guest

[001-219] His indignation thus the prince express’d:

[001-220] “Indulge my rising grief, whilst these (my friend)

[001-221] With song and dance the pompous revel end.

[001-222] Light is the dance, and doubly sweet the lays,

[001-223] When for the dear delight another pays.

[001-224] His treasured stores those cormarants consume,

[001-225] Whose bones, defrauded of a regal tomb

[001-226] And common turf, lie naked on the plain,

[001-227] Or doom’d to welter in the whelming main.

[001-228] Should he return, that troop so blithe and bold,

[001-229] With purple robes inwrought, and stiff with gold,

[001-230] Precipitant in fear would wing their flight,

[001-231] And curse their cumbrous pride’s unwieldy weight.

[001-232] But ah, I dream!-the appointed hour is fled.

[001-233] And hope, too long with vain delusion fed,

[001-234] Deaf to the rumour of fallacious fame,

[001-235] Gives to the roll of death his glorious name!

[001-236] With venial freedom let me now demand

[001-237] Thy name, thy lineage, and paternal land;

[001-238] Sincere from whence began thy course, recite,

[001-239] And to what ship I owe the friendly freight?

[001-240] Now first to me this visit dost thou deign,

[001-241] Or number’d in my father’s social train?

[001-242] All who deserved his choice he made his own,

[001-243] And, curious much to know, he far was known.”

[001-244] “My birth I boast (the blue-eyed virgin cries)

[001-245] From great Anchialus, renown’d and wise;

[001-246] Mentes my name; I rule the Taphian race,

[001-247] Whose bounds the deep circumfluent waves embrace;

[001-248] A duteous people, and industrious isle,

[001-249] To naval arts inured, and stormy toil.

[001-250] Freighted with iron from my native land,

[001-251] I steer my voyage to the Brutian strand

[001-252] To gain by commerce, for the labour’d mass,

[001-253] A just proportion of refulgent brass.

[001-254] Far from your capital my ship resides

[001-255] At Reitorus, and secure at anchor rides;

[001-256] Where waving groves on airy Neign grow,

[001-257] Supremely tall and shade the deeps below.

[001-258] Thence to revisit your imperial dome,

[001-259] An old hereditary guest I come;

[001-260] Your father’s friend. [[laertes|Laertes]] can relate

[001-261] Our faith unspotted, and its early date;

[001-262] Who, press’d with heart-corroding grief and years,

[001-263] To the gay court a rural shed prefers,

[001-264] Where, sole of all his train, a matron sage

[001-265] Supports with homely fond his drooping age,

[001-266] With feeble steps from marshalling his vines

[001-267] Returning sad, when toilsome day declines.

[001-268] “With friendly speed, induced by erring fame,

[001-269] To hail Ulysses’ safe return I came;

[001-270] But still the frown of some celestial power

[001-271] With envious joy retards the blissful hour.

[001-272] Let not your soul be sunk in sad despair;

[001-273] He lives, he breathes this heavenly vital air,

[001-274] Among a savage race, whose shelfy bounds

[001-275] With ceaseless roar the foaming deep surrounds.

[001-276] The thoughts which roll within my ravish’d breast,

[001-277] To me, no seer, the inspiring gods suggest;

[001-278] Nor skill’d nor studious, with prophetic eye

[001-279] To judge the winged omens of the sky.

[001-280] Yet hear this certain speech, nor deem it vain;

[001-281] Though adamantine bonds the chief restrain,

[001-282] The dire restraint his wisdom will defeat,

[001-283] And soon restore him to his regal seat.

[001-284] But generous youth! sincere and free declare,

[001-285] Are you, of manly growth, his royal heir?

[001-286] For sure Ulysses in your look appears,

[001-287] The same his features, if the same his years.

[001-288] Such was that face, on which I dwelt with joy

[001-289] Ere Greece assembled stemm’d the tides to Troy;

[001-290] But, parting then for that detested shore,

[001-291] Our eyes, unhappy never greeted more.”

## Section 7

[001-292] “To prove a genuine birth (the prince replies)

[001-293] On female truth assenting faith relies.

[001-294] Thus manifest of right, I build my claim

[001-295] Sure-founded on a fair maternal fame,

[001-296] Ulysses’ son: but happier he, whom fate

[001-297] Hath placed beneath the storms which toss the great!

[001-298] Happier the son, whose hoary sire is bless’d

[001-299] With humble affluence, and domestic rest!

[001-300] Happier than I, to future empire born,

[001-301] But doom’d a father’s wretch’d fate to mourn!”

[001-302] To whom, with aspect mild, the guest divine:

[001-303] “Oh true descendant of a sceptred line!

[001-304] The gods a glorious fate from anguish free

[001-305] To chaste Penelope’s increase decree.

[001-306] But say, yon jovial troops so gaily dress’d,

[001-307] Is this a bridal or a friendly feast?

[001-308] Or from their deed I rightlier may divine,

[001-309] Unseemly flown with insolence and wine?

[001-310] Unwelcome revellers, whose lawless joy

[001-311] Pains the sage ear, and hurts the sober eye.”

[001-312] “Magnificence of old (the prince replied)

[001-313] Beneath our roof with virtue could reside;

[001-314] Unblamed abundance crowned the royal board,

[001-315] What time this dome revered her prudent lord;

[001-316] Who now (so Heaven decrees) is doom’d to mourn,

[001-317] Bitter constraint, erroneous and forlorn.

[001-318] Better the chief, on Ilion’s hostile plain,

[001-319] Had fall’n surrounded with his warlike train;

[001-320] Or safe return’d, the race of glory pass’d,

[001-321] New to his friends’ embrace, and breathed his last!

[001-322] Then grateful Greece with streaming eyes would raise,

[001-323] Historic marbles to record his praise;

[001-324] His praise, eternal on the faithful stone,

[001-325] Had with transmissive honour graced his son.

[001-326] Now snatch’d by harpies to the dreary coast.

[001-327] Sunk is the hero, and his glory lost;

[001-328] Vanish’d at once! unheard of, and unknown!

[001-329] And I his heir in misery alone.

[001-330] Nor for a dear lost father only flow

[001-331] The filial tears, but woe succeeds to woe

[001-332] To tempt the spouseless queen with amorous wiles

[001-333] Resort the nobles from the neighbouring isles;

[001-334] From [[same|Samos]], circled with the Ionian main,

[001-335] [[dulichium|Dulichium]], and [[zacynthus|Zacynthas]]’ sylvan reign;

[001-336] Ev’n with presumptuous hope her bed to ascend,

[001-337] The lords of Ithaca their right pretend.

[001-338] She seems attentive to their pleaded vows,

[001-339] Her heart detesting what her ear allows.

[001-340] They, vain expectants of the bridal hour,

[001-341] My stores in riotous expense devour.

[001-342] In feast and dance the mirthful months employ,

[001-343] And meditate my doom to crown their joy.”

[001-344] With tender pity touch’d, the goddess cried:

[001-345] “Soon may kind Heaven a sure relief provide,

[001-346] Soon may your sire discharge the vengeance due,

[001-347] And all your wrongs the proud oppressors rue!

[001-348] Oh! in that portal should the chief appear,

[001-349] Each hand tremendous with a brazen spear,

[001-350] In radiant panoply his limbs incased

[001-351] (For so of old my father’s court he graced,

[001-352] When social mirth unbent his serious soul,

[001-353] O’er the full banquet, and the sprightly bowl);

[001-354] He then from Ephyre, the fair domain

[001-355] Of Ilus, sprung from [[jason|Jason]]’s royal strain,

[001-356] Measured a length of seas, a toilsome length, in vain.

[001-357] For, voyaging to learn the direful art

[001-358] To taint with deadly drugs the barbed dart;

[001-359] Observant of the gods, and sternly just,

[001-360] Ilus refused to impart the baneful trust;

[001-361] With friendlier zeal my father’s soul was fired,

[001-362] The drugs he knew, and gave the boon desired.

[001-363] Appear’d he now with such heroic port,

[001-364] As then conspicuous at the Taphian court;

[001-365] Soon should yon boasters cease their haughty strife,

[001-366] Or each atone his guilty love with life.

[001-367] But of his wish’d return the care resign,

[001-368] Be future vengeance to the powers divine.

[001-369] My sentence hear: with stern distaste avow’d,

[001-370] To their own districts drive the suitor-crowd;

[001-371] When next the morning warms the purple east,

[001-372] Convoke the peerage, and the gods attest;

[001-373] The sorrows of your inmost soul relate;

[001-374] And form sure plans to save the sinking state.

[001-375] Should second love a pleasing flame inspire,

[001-376] And the chaste queen connubial rights require;

[001-377] Dismiss’d with honour, let her hence repair

[001-378] To great [[icarius|Icarius]], whose paternal care

[001-379] Will guide her passion, and reward her choice

[001-380] With wealthy dower, and bridal gifts of price.

[001-381] Then let this dictate of my love prevail:

[001-382] Instant, to foreign realms prepare to sail,

[001-383] To learn your father’s fortunes; [[kleos|Fame]] may prove,

[001-384] Or omen’d voice (the messenger of Jove),

[001-385] Propitious to the search. Direct your toil

[001-386] Through the wide ocean first to sandy Pyle;

[001-387] Of Nestor, hoary sage, his doom demand:

[001-388] Thence speed your voyage to the Spartan strand;

[001-389] For young Atrides to the Achaian coast

[001-390] Arrived the last of all the victor host.

[001-391] If yet Ulysses views the light, forbear,

[001-392] Till the fleet hours restore the circling year.

[001-393] But if his soul hath wing’d the destined flight,

[001-394] Inhabitant of deep disastrous night;

[001-395] Homeward with pious speed repass the main,

[001-396] To the pale shade funereal rites ordain,

[001-397] Plant the fair column o’er the vacant grave,

[001-398] A hero’s honours let the hero have.

[001-399] With decent grief the royal dead deplored,

[001-400] For the chaste queen select an equal lord.

[001-401] Then let revenge your daring mind employ,

[001-402] By fraud or force the suitor train destroy,

[001-403] And starting into manhood, scorn the boy.

[001-404] Hast thou not heard how young Orestes, fired

[001-405] With great revenge, immortal praise acquired?

[001-406] His virgin-sword AEgysthus’ veins imbrued;

[001-407] The murderer fell, and blood atoned for blood.

[001-408] O greatly bless’d with every blooming grace!

[001-409] With equal steps the paths of glory trace;

[001-410] Join to that royal youth’s your rival name,

[001-411] And shine eternal in the sphere of fame.

[001-412] But my associates now my stay deplore,

[001-413] Impatient on the hoarse-resounding shore.

[001-414] Thou, heedful of advice, secure proceed;

[001-415] My praise the precept is, be thine the deed.

[001-416] “The counsel of my friend (the youth rejoin’d)

[001-417] Imprints conviction on my grateful mind.

[001-418] So fathers speak (persuasive speech and mild)

[001-419] Their sage experience to the favourite child.

[001-420] But, since to part, for sweet refection due,

[001-421] The genial viands let my train renew;

[001-422] And the rich pledge of plighted faith receive,

[001-423] Worthy the heir of Ithaca to give.”

[001-424] “Defer the promised boon (the goddess cries,

[001-425] Celestial azure brightening in her eyes),

[001-426] And let me now regain the Reithrian port;

[001-427] From Temese return’d, your royal court

[001-428] I shall revisit, and that pledge receive;

[001-429] And gifts, memorial of our friendship, leave.”

[001-430] Abrupt, with eagle-speed she cut the sky;

[001-431] Instant invisible to mortal eye.

[001-432] Then first he recognized the ethereal guest;

[001-433] Wonder and joy alternate fire his breast;

[001-434] Heroic thoughts, infused, his heart dilate;

[001-435] Revolving much his father’s doubtful fate.

[001-436] At length, composed, he join’d the suitor-throng;

[001-437] Hush’d in attention to the warbled song.

[001-438] His tender theme the charming lyrist chose.

[001-439] Minerva’s anger, and the dreadful woes

[001-440] Which voyaging from Troy the victors bore,

[001-441] While storms vindictive intercept the store.

[001-442] The shrilling airs the vaulted roof rebounds,

[001-443] Reflecting to the queen the silver sounds.

[001-444] With grief renew’d the weeping fair descends;

[001-445] Their sovereign’s step a virgin train attends:

[001-446] A veil, of richest texture wrought, she wears,

[001-447] And silent to the joyous hall repairs.

[001-448] There from the portal, with her mild command,

[001-449] Thus gently checks the minstrel’s tuneful hand:

[001-450] “Phemius! let acts of gods, and heroes old,

[001-451] What ancient bards in hall and bower have told,

[001-452] Attemper’d to the lyre, your voice employ;

[001-453] Such the pleased ear will drink with silent joy.

[001-454] But, oh! forbear that dear disastrous name,

[001-455] To sorrow sacred, and secure of fame;

[001-456] My bleeding bosom sickens at the sound,

[001-457] And every piercing note inflicts a wound.”

[001-458] “Why, dearest object of my duteous love,

[001-459] (Replied the prince,) will you the bard reprove?

[001-460] Oft, Jove’s ethereal rays (resistless fire)

[001-461] The chanter’s soul and raptured song inspire

[001-462] Instinct divine? nor blame severe his choice,

[001-463] Warbling the Grecian woes with heart and voice;

[001-464] For novel lays attract our ravish’d ears;

[001-465] But old, the mind with inattention hears:

[001-466] Patient permit the sadly pleasing strain;

[001-467] Familiar now with grief, your tears refrain,

[001-468] And in the public woe forget your own;

[001-469] You weep not for a perish’d lord alone.

[001-470] What Greeks new wandering in the Stygian gloom,

[001-471] Wish your Ulysses shared an equal doom!

[001-472] Your widow’d hours, apart, with female toil

[001-473] And various labours of the loom beguile;

[001-474] There rule, from palace-cares remote and free;

[001-475] That care to man belongs, and most to me.”

[001-476] Mature beyond his years, the queen admires

[001-477] His sage reply, and with her train retires.

[001-478] Then swelling sorrows burst their former bounds,

[001-479] With echoing grief afresh the dome resounds;

[001-480] Till Pallas, piteous of her plaintive cries,

[001-481] In slumber closed her silver-streaming eyes.

[001-482] Meantime, rekindled at the royal charms,

[001-483] Tumultuous love each beating bosom warms;

[001-484] Intemperate rage a wordy war began;

[001-485] But bold Telemachus assumed the man.

[001-486] “Instant (he cried) your female discord end,

[001-487] Ye deedless boasters! and the song attend;

[001-488] Obey that sweet compulsion, nor profane

[001-489] With dissonance the smooth melodious strain.

[001-490] Pacific now prolong the jovial feast;

[001-491] But when the dawn reveals the rosy east,

[001-492] I, to the peers assembled, shall propose

[001-493] The firm resolve, I here in few disclose;

[001-494] No longer live the cankers of my court;

[001-495] All to your several states with speed resort;

[001-496] Waste in wild riot what your land allows,

[001-497] There ply the early feast, and late carouse.

[001-498] But if, to honour lost, ’tis still decreed

[001-499] For you my bowl shall flow, my flock shall bleed;

[001-500] Judge and revenge my right, impartial Jove!

[001-501] By him and all the immortal thrones above

[001-502] (A sacred oath), each proud oppressor slain,

[001-503] Shall with inglorious gore this marble stain.”

[001-504] Awed by the prince, thus haughty, bold, and young,

[001-505] Rage gnaw’d the lip, and wonder chain’d the tongue.

[001-506] Silence at length the gay [[antinous|Antinous]] broke,

[001-507] Constrain’d a smile, and thus ambiguous spoke:

[001-508] “What god to your untutor’d youth affords

[001-509] This headlong torrent of amazing words?

[001-510] May Jove delay thy reign, and cumber late

[001-511] So bright a genius with the toils of state!”

[001-512] “Those toils (Telemachus serene replies)

[001-513] Have charms, with all their weight, t’allure the wise.

[001-514] Fast by the throne obsequious fame resides,

[001-515] And wealth incessant rolls her golden tides.

[001-516] Nor let Antinous rage, if strong desire

[001-517] Of wealth and fame a youthful bosom fire:

[001-518] Elect by Jove, his delegate of sway,

[001-519] With joyous pride the summons I’d obey.

[001-520] Whene’er Ulysses roams the realm of night,

[001-521] Should factious power dispute my lineal right,

[001-522] Some other Greeks a fairer claim may plead;

[001-523] To your pretence their title would precede.

[001-524] At least, the sceptre lost, I still should reign

[001-525] Sole o’er my vassals, and domestic train.”

[001-526] To this [[eurymachus|Eurymachus]]: “To Heaven alone

[001-527] Refer the choice to fill the vacant throne.

[001-528] Your patrimonial stores in peace possess;

[001-529] Undoubted, all your filial claim confess:

[001-530] Your private right should impious power invade,

[001-531] The peers of Ithaca would arm in aid.

[001-532] But say, that stranger guest who late withdrew,

[001-533] What and from whence? his name and lineage shew.

[001-534] His grave demeanour and majestic grace

[001-535] Speak him descended of no vulgar race:

[001-536] Did he some loan of ancient right require,

[001-537] Or came forerunner of your sceptr’d sire?”

[001-538] “Oh son of Polybus!” the prince replies,

[001-539] “No more my sire will glad these longing eyes;

[001-540] The queen’s fond hope inventive rumour cheers,

[001-541] Or vain diviners’ dreams divert her fears.

[001-542] That stranger-guest the Taphian realm obeys,

[001-543] A realm defended with encircling seas.

[001-544] Mentes, an ever-honour’d name, of old

[001-547] Answer’d evasive of the sly request.

[001-556] In his pavilion there, to sleep repairs;
