[003-001] BOOK III.

[003-002] ARGUMENT.

[003-003] THE INTERVIEW OF TELEMACHUS AND NESTOR.

[003-004] [[telemachus|Telemachus]], guided by Pallas in the shape of [[mentor|Mentor]], arrives in the

[003-005] morning at [[pylos|Pylos]], where [[nestor|Nestor]] and his sons are sacrificing on the sea-

[003-006] shore to [[poseidon|Neptune]]. Telemachus declares the occasion of his coming: and

[003-007] Nestor relates what passed in their return from [[troy|Troy]], how their fleets

[003-008] were separated, and he never since heard of [[odysseus|Ulysses]]. They discourse

[003-009] concerning the death of [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]], the revenge of [[orestes|Orestes]], and the

[003-010] injuries of the suitors. Nestor advises him to go to [[sparta|Sparta]], and

[003-011] inquire further of [[menelaus|Menelaus]]. The sacrifice ending with the night,

[003-012] [[athena|Minerva]] vanishes from them in the form of an eagle: Telemachus is

[003-013] lodged in the palace. The next morning they sacrifice a bullock to

[003-014] Minerva; and Telemachus proceeds on his journey to Sparta, attended by

[003-015] [[pisistratus|Pisistratus]].

[003-016] The scene lies on the sea-shore of Pylos.

[003-017] The sacred sun, above the waters raised,

[003-018] Through heaven’s eternal brazen portals blazed;

[003-019] And wide o’er earth diffused his cheering ray,

[003-020] To gods and men to give the golden day.

[003-021] Now on the coast of Pyle the vessel falls,

[003-022] Before old Neleus’ venerable walls.

[003-023] There suppliant to the monarch of the flood,

[003-024] At nine green theatres the Pylians stood,

[003-025] Each held five hundred (a deputed train),

[003-026] At each, nine oxen on the sand lay slain.

[003-027] They taste the entrails, and the altars load

[003-028] With smoking thighs, an offering to the god.

[003-029] Full for the port the Ithacensians stand,

[003-030] And furl their sails, and issue on the land.

[003-031] Telemachus already press’d the shore;

[003-032] Not first, the power of wisdom march’d before,

[003-033] And ere the sacrificing throng he join’d,

[003-034] Admonish’d thus his well-attending mind:

## Section 10

[003-035] “Proceed, my son! this youthful shame expel;

[003-036] An honest business never blush to tell.

[003-037] To learn what fates thy wretched sire detain,

[003-038] We pass’d the wide immeasurable main.

[003-039] Meet then the senior far renown’d for sense

[003-040] With reverend awe, but decent confidence:

[003-041] Urge him with truth to frame his fair replies;

[003-042] And sure he will; for wisdom never lies.”

[003-043] “Oh tell me, Mentor! tell me, faithful guide

[003-044] (The youth with prudent modesty replied),

[003-045] How shall I meet, or how accost the sage,

[003-046] Unskill’d in speech, nor yet mature of age?

[003-047] Awful th’approach, and hard the task appears,

[003-048] To question wisely men of riper years.”

[003-049] To whom the martial goddess thus rejoin’d:

[003-050] “Search, for some thoughts, thy own suggesting mind;

[003-051] And others, dictated by heavenly power,

[003-052] Shall rise spontaneous in the needful hour.

[003-053] For nought unprosperous shall thy ways attend,

[003-054] Born with good omens, and with heaven thy friend.”

[003-055] She spoke, and led the way with swiftest speed;

[003-056] As swift, the youth pursued the way she led;

[003-057] and join’d the band before the sacred fire,

[003-058] Where sate, encompass’d with his sons, the sire.

[003-059] The youth of Pylos, some on pointed wood

[003-060] Transfix’d the fragments, some prepared the food:

[003-061] In friendly throngs they gather to embrace

[003-062] Their unknown guests, and at the banquet place,

[003-063] Pisistratus was first to grasp their hands,

[003-064] And spread soft hides upon the yellow sands;

[003-065] Along the shore the illustrious pair he led,

[003-066] Where Nestor sate with youthful Thrasymed,

[003-067] To each a portion of the feast he bore,

[003-068] And held the golden goblet foaming o’er;

[003-069] Then first approaching to the elder guest,

[003-070] The latent goddess in these words address’d:

[003-071] “Whoe’er thou art, whom fortune brings to keep

[003-072] These rites of Neptune, monarch of the deep,

[003-073] Thee first it fits, O stranger! to prepare

[003-074] The due libation and the solemn prayer;

[003-075] Then give thy friend to shed the sacred wine;

[003-076] Though much thy younger, and his years like mine,

[003-077] He too, I deem, implores the power divine;

[003-078] For all mankind alike require their grace,

[003-079] All born to want; a miserable race!”

[003-080] He spake, and to her hand preferr’d the bowl;

[003-081] A secret pleasure touch’d Athena’s soul,

[003-082] To see the preference due to sacred age

[003-083] Regarded ever by the just and sage.

[003-084] Of [[oceanus|Ocean]]’s king she then implores the grace.

[003-085] “O thou! whose arms this ample globe embrace,

[003-086] Fulfil our wish, and let thy glory shine

[003-087] On Nestor first, and Nestor’s royal line;

[003-088] Next grant the Pylian states their just desires,

[003-089] Pleased with their hecatomb’s ascending fires;

[003-090] Last, deign Telemachus and me to bless,

[003-091] And crown our voyage with desired success.”

[003-092] Thus she: and having paid the rite divine,

[003-093] Gave to Ulysses’ son the rosy wine.

[003-094] Suppliant he pray’d. And now the victims dress’d

[003-095] They draw, divide, and celebrate the feast.

[003-096] The banquet done, the narrative old man,

[003-097] Thus mild, the pleasing conference began:

[003-098] “Now gentle guests! the genial banquet o’er,

[003-099] It fits to ask ye, what your native shore,

[003-100] And whence your race? on what adventure say,

[003-101] Thus far you wander through the watery way?

[003-102] Relate if business, or the thirst of gain,

[003-103] Engage your journey o’er the pathless main

[003-104] Where savage pirates seek through seas unknown

[003-105] The lives of others, venturous of their own.”

[003-106] Urged by the precepts by the goddess given,

[003-107] And fill’d with confidence infused from Heaven,

[003-108] The youth, whom Pallas destined to be wise

[003-109] And famed among the sons of men, replies:

[003-110] “Inquir’st thou, father! from what coast we came?

[003-111] (Oh grace and glory of the Grecian name!)

[003-112] From where high [[ithaca|Ithaca]] o’erlooks the floods,

[003-113] Brown with o’er-arching shades and pendent woods

[003-114] Us to these shores our filial duty draws,

[003-115] A private sorrow, not a public cause.

[003-116] My sire I seek, where’er the voice of fame

[003-117] Has told the glories of his noble name,

[003-118] The great Ulysses; famed from shore to shore

[003-119] For valour much, for hardy suffering more.

[003-120] Long time with thee before proud Ilion’s wall

[003-121] In arms he fought; with thee beheld her fall.

[003-122] Of all the chiefs, this hero’s fate alone

[003-123] Has [[zeus|Jove]] reserved, unheard of, and unknown;

[003-124] Whether in fields by hostile fury slain,

[003-125] Or sunk by tempests in the gulfy main?

[003-126] Of this to learn, oppress’d with tender fears,

[003-127] Lo, at thy knee his suppliant son appears.

[003-128] If or thy certain eye, or curious ear,

[003-129] Have learnt his fate, the whole dark story clear

[003-130] And, oh! whate’er Heaven destined to betide,

[003-131] Let neither flattery soothe, nor pity hide.

[003-132] Prepared I stand: he was but born to try

[003-133] The lot of man; to suffer, and to die.

[003-134] Oh then, if ever through the ten years’ war

[003-135] The wise, the good Ulysses claim’d thy care;

[003-136] If e’er he join’d thy council, or thy sword,

[003-137] True in his deed, and constant to his word;

[003-138] Far as thy mind through backward time can see

[003-139] Search all thy stores of faithful memory:

[003-140] ’Tis sacred truth I ask, and ask of thee.”

[003-141] To him experienced Nestor thus rejoin’d:

[003-142] “O friend! what sorrows dost thou bring to mind!

[003-143] Shall I the long, laborious scene review,

[003-144] And open all the wounds of Greece anew?

[003-145] What toils by sea! where dark in quest of prey

[003-146] Dauntless we roved; [[achilles|Achilles]] led the way;

[003-147] What toils by land! where mix’d in fatal fight

[003-148] Such numbers fell, such heroes sunk to night;

[003-149] There [[ajax|Ajax]] great, Achilles there the brave,

[003-150] There wise [[patroclus|Patroclus]], fill an early grave:

[003-151] There, too, my son—ah, once my best delight

[003-152] Once swift of foot, and terrible in fight;

[003-153] In whom stern courage with soft virtue join’d

[003-154] A faultless body and a blameless mind;

[003-155] [[antilochus|Antilochus]]—What more can I relate?

[003-156] How trace the tedious series of our fate?

[003-157] Not added years on years my task could close,

[003-158] The long historian of my country’s woes;

[003-159] Back to thy native islands might’st thou sail,

[003-160] And leave half-heard the melancholy tale.

[003-161] Nine painful years on that detested shore;

[003-162] What stratagems we form’d, what toils we bore!

[003-163] Still labouring on, till scarce at last we found

[003-164] Great Jove propitious, and our conquest crown’d.

[003-165] Far o’er the rest thy mighty father shined,

[003-166] In wit, in prudence, and in force of mind.

[003-167] Art thou the son of that illustrious sire?

[003-168] With joy I grasp thee, and with love admire.

[003-169] So like your voices, and your words so wise,

[003-170] Who finds thee younger must consult his eyes.

[003-171] Thy sire and I were one; nor varied aught

[003-172] In public sentence, or in private thought;

[003-173] Alike to council or the assembly came,

[003-174] With equal souls, and sentiments the same.

[003-175] But when (by wisdom won) proud Ilion burn’d,

[003-176] And in their ships the conquering Greeks return’d,

[003-177] ’Twas God’s high will the victors to divide,

[003-178] And turn the event, confounding human pride;

[003-179] Some he destroy’d, some scatter’d as the dust

[003-180] (Not all were prudent, and not all were just).

[003-181] Then Discord, sent by Pallas from above,

[003-182] Stern daughter of the great avenger Jove,

[003-183] The brother-kings inspired with fell debate;

[003-184] Who call’d to council all the Achaian state,

[003-185] But call’d untimely (not the sacred rite

[003-186] Observed, nor heedful of the setting light,

[003-187] Nor herald sword the session to proclaim),

[003-188] Sour with debauch, a reeling tribe they came.

[003-189] To these the cause of meeting they explain,

[003-190] And Menelaus moves to cross the main;

[003-191] Not so the king of men: he will’d to stay,

[003-192] The sacred rites and hecatombs to pay,

[003-193] And calm Minerva’s wrath. Oh blind to fate!

[003-194] The gods not lightly change their love, or hate.

[003-195] With ireful taunts each other they oppose,

[003-196] Till in loud tumult all the Greeks arose.

[003-197] Now different counsels every breast divide,

[003-198] Each burns with rancour to the adverse side;

[003-199] The unquiet night strange projects entertain’d

[003-200] (So Jove, that urged us to our fate, ordain’d).

[003-201] We with the rising morn our ships unmoor’d,

[003-202] And brought our captives and our stores aboard;

[003-203] But half the people with respect obey’d

[003-204] The king of men, and at his bidding stay’d.

[003-205] Now on the wings of winds our course we keep

[003-206] (For God had smooth’d the waters of the deep);

[003-207] For Tenedos we spread our eager oars,

[003-208] There land, and pay due victims to the powers;

[003-209] To bless our safe return, we join in prayer;

[003-210] But angry Jove dispersed our vows in air,

[003-211] And raised new discord. Then (so Heaven decreed)

[003-212] Ulysses first and Nestor disagreed!

[003-213] Wise as he was, by various counsels sway’d,

[003-214] He there, though late, to please the monarch, stay’d.

[003-215] But I, determined, stem the foamy floods,

[003-216] Warn’d of the coming fury of the gods.

[003-217] With us, Tydides fear’d, and urged his haste:

[003-218] And Menelaus came, but came the last,

[003-219] He join’d our vessels in the Lesbian bay,

[003-220] While yet we doubted of our watery way;

[003-221] If to the right to urge the pilot’s toil

[003-222] (The safer road), beside the Psyrian isle;

[003-223] Or the straight course to rocky Chios plough,

[003-224] And anchor under Mimas’ shaggy brow?

[003-225] We sought direction of the power divine:

[003-226] The god propitious gave the guiding sign;

[003-227] Through the mid seas he bid our navy steer,

[003-228] And in Euboea shun the woes we fear.

[003-229] The whistling winds already waked the sky;

[003-230] Before the whistling winds the vessels fly,

[003-231] With rapid swiftness cut the liquid way,

[003-232] And reach Gerestus at the point of day.

[003-233] There hecatombs of bulls, to Neptune slain,

[003-234] High-flaming please the monarch of the main.

[003-235] The fourth day shone, when all their labours o’er,

[003-236] Tydides’ vessels touched the wish’d-for shore.

[003-237] But I to Pylos scud before the gales,

[003-238] The god still breathing on my swelling sails;

[003-239] Separate from all, I safely landed here;

[003-240] Their fates or fortunes never reach’d my ear.

[003-241] Yet what I learn’d, attend; as here I sat,

[003-242] And ask’d each voyager each hero’s fate;

[003-243] Curious to know, and willing to relate.

[003-244] “Safe reach’d the Myrmidons their native land,

[003-245] Beneath Achilles’ warlike son’s command.

[003-246] Those, whom the heir of great [[apollo|Apollo]]’s art,

[003-247] Brave Philoctetes, taught to wing the dart;

[003-248] And those whom Idomen from Ilion’s plain

[003-249] Had led, securely cross’d the dreadful main

[003-250] How Agamemnon touch’d his Argive coast,

[003-251] And how his life by fraud and force he lost,

[003-252] And how the murderer, paid his forfeit breath;

[003-253] What lands so distant from that scene of death

[003-254] But trembling heard the fame? and heard, admire.

[003-255] How well the son appeased his slaughter’d sire!

[003-256] Ev’n to the unhappy, that unjustly bleed,

[003-257] Heaven gives posterity, to avenge the deed.

[003-258] So fell Ægysthus; and mayest thou, my friend,

[003-259] (On whom the virtues of thy sire descend,)

[003-260] Make future times thy equal act adore,

[003-261] And be what brave Orestes was before!”

[003-262] The prudent youth replied: “O thou the grace

[003-263] And lasting glory of the Grecian race!

[003-264] Just was the vengeance, and to latest days

[003-265] Shall long posterity resound the praise.

[003-266] Some god this arm with equal prowess bless!

[003-267] And the proud suitors shall its force confess;

[003-268] Injurious men! who while my soul is sore

[003-269] Of fresh affronts, are meditating more.

[003-270] But Heaven denies this honour to my hand,

[003-271] Nor shall my father repossess the land;

[003-272] The father’s fortune never to return,

[003-273] And the sad son’s to softer and to mourn!”

[003-274] Thus he; and Nestor took the word: “My son,

[003-275] Is it then true, as distant rumours run,

[003-276] That crowds of rivals for thy mother’s charms

[003-277] Thy palace fill with insults and alarms?

[003-278] Say, is the fault, through tame submission, thine?

[003-279] Or leagued against thee, do thy people join,

[003-280] Moved by some oracle, or voice divine?

[003-281] And yet who knows, but ripening lies in fate

[003-282] An hour of vengeance for the afflicted state;

[003-283] When great Ulysses shall suppress these harms,

[003-284] Ulysses singly, or all Greece in arms.

[003-285] But if Athena, war’s triumphant maid,

[003-286] The happy son will as the father aid,

[003-287] (Whose fame and safety was her constant care

[003-288] In every danger and in every war:

[003-289] Never on man did heavenly favour shine

[003-290] With rays so strong, distinguish’d and divine,

[003-291] As those with which Minerva mark’d thy sire)

[003-292] So might she love thee, so thy soul inspire!

[003-293] Soon should their hopes in humble dust be laid,

[003-294] And long oblivion of the bridal bed.”

[003-295] “Ah! no such hope (the prince with sighs replies)

[003-296] Can touch my breast; that blessing Heaven denies.

[003-297] Ev’n by celestial favour were it given,

[003-298] Fortune or fate would cross the will of Heaven.”

## Section 11

[003-299] “What words are these, and what imprudence thine?

[003-300] (Thus interposed the martial maid divine)

[003-301] Forgetful youth! but know, the Power above

[003-302] With ease can save each object of his love;

[003-303] Wide as his will, extends his boundless grace;

[003-304] Nor lost in time nor circumscribed by place.

[003-305] Happier his lot, who, many sorrows pass’d,

[003-306] Long labouring gains his natal shore at last;

[003-307] Than who, too speedy, hastes to end his life

[003-308] By some stern ruffian, or adulterous wife.

[003-309] Death only is the lot which none can miss,

[003-310] And all is possible to Heaven but this.

[003-311] The best, the dearest favourite of the sky,

[003-312] Must taste that cup, for man is born to die.”

[003-313] Thus check’d, replied Ulysses’ prudent heir:

[003-314] “Mentor, no more—the mournful thought forbear;

[003-315] For he no more must draw his country’s breath,

[003-316] Already snatch’d by fate, and the black doom of death!

[003-317] Pass we to other subjects; and engage

[003-318] On themes remote the venerable sage

[003-319] (Who thrice has seen the perishable kind

[003-320] Of men decay, and through three ages shined

[003-321] Like gods majestic, and like gods in mind);

[003-322] For much he knows, and just conclusions draws,

[003-323] From various precedents, and various laws.

[003-324] O son of Neleus! awful Nestor, tell

[003-325] How he, the mighty Agamemnon, fell;

[003-326] By what strange fraud Ægysthus wrought, relate

[003-327] (By force he could not) such a hero’s fate?

[003-328] Live Menelaus not in Greece? or where

[003-329] Was then the martial brother’s pious care?

[003-330] Condemn’d perhaps some foreign shore to tread;

[003-331] Or sure Ægysthus had not dared the deed.”

[003-332] To whom the full of days: Illustrious youth,

[003-333] Attend (though partly thou hast guess’d) the truth.

[003-334] For had the martial Menelaus found

[003-335] The ruffian breathing yet on Argive ground;

[003-336] Nor earth had bid his carcase from the skies,

[003-337] Nor Grecian virgins shriek’d his obsequies,

[003-338] But fowls obscene dismember’d his remains,

[003-339] And dogs had torn him on the naked plains.

[003-340] While us the works of bloody Mars employ’d,

[003-341] The wanton youth inglorious peace enjoy’d:

[003-342] He stretch’d at ease in Argos’ calm recess

[003-343] (Whose stately steeds luxuriant pastures bless),

[003-344] With flattery’s insinuating art

[003-345] Soothed the frail queen, and poison’d all her heart.

[003-346] At first, with worthy shame and decent pride,

[003-347] The royal dame his lawless suit denied.

[003-348] For virtue’s image yet possess’d her mind.

[003-349] Taught by a master of the tuneful kind;

[003-350] Atrides, parting for the Trojan war,

[003-351] Consign’d the youthful consort to his care.

[003-352] True to his charge, the bard preserved her long

[003-353] In honour’s limits; such the power of song.

[003-354] But when the gods these objects of their hate

[003-355] Dragg’d to destruction by the links of fate;

[003-356] The bard they banish’d from his native soil,

[003-357] And left all helpless in a desert isle;

[003-358] There he, the sweetest of the sacred train,

[003-359] Sung dying to the rocks, but sung in vain.

[003-360] Then virtue was no more; her guard away,

[003-361] She fell, to lust a voluntary prey.

[003-362] Even to the temple stalk’d the adulterous spouse,

[003-363] With impious thanks, and mockery of the vows,

[003-364] With images, with garments, and with gold;

[003-365] And odorous fumes from loaded altars roll’d.

[003-366] “Meantime from flaming Troy we cut the way

[003-367] With Menelaus, through the curling sea.

[003-368] But when to Sunium’s sacred point we came,

[003-369] Crown’d with the temple of the Athenian dame;

[003-370] Atride’s pilot, Phrontes, there expired

[003-371] (Phrontes, of all the songs of men admired

[003-372] To steer the bounding bark with steady toil,

[003-373] When the storm thickens, and the billows boil);

[003-374] While yet he exercised the steerman’s art,

[003-375] Apollo touch’d him with his gentle dart;

[003-376] Even with the rudder in his hand, he fell.

[003-377] To pay whole honours to the shades of hell,

[003-378] We check’d our haste, by pious office bound,

[003-379] And laid our old companion in the ground.

[003-380] And now the rites discharged, our course we keep

[003-381] Far on the gloomy bosom of the deep:

[003-382] Soon as Malae’s misty tops arise,

[003-383] Sudden the Thunderer blackens all the skies,

[003-384] And the winds whistle, and the surges roll

[003-385] Mountains on mountains, and obscure the pole.

[003-386] The tempest scatters, and divides our fleet;

[003-387] Part, the storm urges on the coast of Crete,

[003-388] Where winding round the rich Cydonian plain,

[003-389] The streams of Jardan issue to the main.

[003-390] There stands a rock, high, eminent and steep,

[003-391] Whose shaggy brow o’erhangs the shady deep,

[003-392] And views Gortyna on the western side;

[003-393] On this rough Auster drove the impetuous tide:

[003-394] With broken force the billows roll’d away,

[003-395] And heaved the fleet into the neighb’ring bay.

[003-396] Thus saved from death, they gain’d the Phæstan shores,

[003-397] With shatter’d vessels and disabled oars;

[003-398] But five tall barks the winds and water toss’d,

[003-399] Far from their fellows, on the Ægyptian coast.

[003-400] There wander’d Menelaus through foreign shores

[003-401] Amassing gold, and gathering naval stores;

[003-402] While cursed Ægysthus the detested deed

[003-403] By fraud fulfilled, and his great brother bled.

[003-404] Seven years, the traitor rich [[mycenae|Mycenae]] sway’d,

[003-405] And his stern rule the groaning land obey’d;

[003-406] The eighth, from Athens to his realm restored,

[003-407] Orestes brandish’d the avenging sword,

[003-408] Slew the dire pair, and gave to funeral flame

[003-409] The vile assassin and adulterous dame.

[003-410] That day, ere yet the bloody triumphs cease,

[003-411] [[nostos|Return]]’d Atrides to the coast of Greece,

[003-412] And safe to Argos port his navy brought,

[003-413] With gifts of price and ponderous treasure fraught.

[003-414] Hence warn’d, my son, beware! nor idly stand

[003-415] Too long a stranger to thy native land;

[003-416] Lest heedless absence wear thy wealth away,

[003-417] While lawless feasters in thy palace away;

[003-418] Perhaps may seize thy realm, and share the spoil;

[003-419] And though return, with disappointed toil,

[003-420] From thy vain journey, to a rifled isle.

[003-421] However, my friend, indulge one labour more,

[003-422] And seek Atrides on the Spartan shore.

[003-423] He, wandering long a wider circle made,

[003-424] And many-languaged nations has survey’d:

[003-425] And measured tracks unknown to other ships,

[003-426] Amid the monstrous wonders of the deeps,

[003-427] (A length of ocean and unbounded sky.

[003-428] Which scarce the sea-fowl in a year o’erfly);

[003-429] Go then; to Sparta take the watery way,

[003-430] Thy ship and sailors but for orders stay;

[003-431] Or, if my land then choose thy course to bend,

[003-432] My steeds, my chariots, and my songs, attend;

[003-433] Thee to Atrides they shall safe convey,
