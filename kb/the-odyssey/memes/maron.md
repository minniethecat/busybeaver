## Overview

Maron, "of Evantheus' line," is the priest of [[apollo|Phoebus Apollo]] at the Ismarian shrine in [[ismarus|Ismarus]], the city of the Cicones in Thrace [^1]. His gift of an extraordinary wine to [[odysseus|Odysseus]] becomes one of the most consequential objects in the epic — the very wine used to intoxicate [[polyphemus|Polyphemus]] and enable the hero's escape from the Cyclops's cave.

## The Wine

The wine Maron gives Odysseus is described in remarkable detail — a testament to its importance in the narrative:

> "Seven golden talents to perfection wrought,
> A silver bowl that held a copious draught,
> And twelve large vessels of unmingled wine,
> Mellifluous, undecaying, and divine!" [^2]

The wine's potency is extraordinary: "scarce twenty measures from the living stream / To cool one cup sufficed" — a 20:1 dilution ratio, far beyond the normal Greek practice of 3:1 or 4:1. When poured, "the goblet crowned / Breathed aromatic fragrances around" [^2].

## The Sack of Ismarus

Odysseus recounts that after sacking Ismarus, "Heaven moved my mind to save" Maron and his household, which stood "in sacred shade" amidst Apollo's consecrated wood [^1]. In gratitude, Maron presented these lavish gifts — gold, a silver bowl, and the twelve jars of divine wine. This act of mercy and reciprocal gift-giving stands in stark contrast to the later encounter with Polyphemus, where no such civilized exchange is possible.

## Role in the Cyclops Episode

The wine proves decisive in [[the-cyclops-episode|Odysseus's escape from Polyphemus]]. Odysseus brings a goatskin of Maron's wine into the Cyclops's cave and offers it to the monster unmixed — a drink so potent that it puts Polyphemus into a deep sleep, allowing Odysseus and his men to blind him with the sharpened stake [^3]. Without Maron's gift, one of the Odyssey's most famous stratagems would have been impossible.

## Significance

Maron embodies the positive potential of xenia (guest-friendship). Odysseus's decision to spare the priest yields a gift that saves his life — a narrative counterweight to the many episodes where hospitality is violated or denied.
