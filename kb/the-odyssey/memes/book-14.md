[014-001] BOOK XIV.

[014-002] ARGUMENT.

[014-003] THE CONVERSATION WITH EUMAEUS.

[014-004] [[odysseus|Ulysses]] arrives in disguise at the house of [[eumaeus|Eumaeus]], where he is

[014-005] received, entertained, and lodged with the utmost hospitality. The

[014-006] several discourses of that faithful old servant, with the feigned story

[014-007] told by Ulysses to conceal himself, and other conversations on various

[014-008] subjects, take up this entire book.

[014-009] But he, deep-musing, o’er the mountains stray’d

[014-010] Through mazy thickets of the woodland shade,

[014-011] And cavern’d ways, the shaggy coast along

[014-012] With cliffs and nodding forests overhung.

[014-013] Eumaeus at his sylvan lodge he sought,

[014-014] A faithful servant, and without a fault.

[014-015] Ulysses found him busied as he sate

[014-016] Before the threshold of his rustic gate;

[014-017] Around the mansion in a circle shone

[014-018] A rural portico of rugged stone

[014-019] (In absence of his lord with honest toil

[014-020] His own industrious hands had raised the pile).

[014-021] The wall was stone from neighbouring quarries borne,

[014-022] Encircled with a fence of native thorn,

[014-023] And strong with pales, by many a weary stroke

[014-024] Of stubborn labour hewn from heart of oak:

[014-025] Frequent and thick. Within the space were rear’d

[014-026] Twelve ample cells, the lodgments of his herd.

[014-027] Full fifty pregnant females each contain’d;

[014-028] The males without (a smaller race) remain’d;

[014-029] Doom’d to supply the suitors’ wasteful feast,

[014-030] A stock by daily luxury decreased;

[014-031] Now scarce four hundred left. These to defend,

[014-032] Four savage dogs, a watchful guard, attend.

[014-033] Here sat Eumaeus, and his cares applied

[014-034] To form strong buskins of well-season’d hide.

[014-035] Of four assistants who his labour share,

[014-036] Three now were absent on the rural care;

[014-037] The fourth drove victims to a suitor train:

[014-038] But he, of ancient faith, a simple swain,

[014-039] Sigh’d, while he furnish’d the luxurious board,

[014-040] And wearied Heaven with wishes for his lord.

[014-041] Soon as Ulysses near the inclosure drew,

[014-042] With open mouths the furious mastiffs flew:

[014-043] Down sat the sage, and cautious to withstand,

[014-044] Let fall the offensive truncheon from his hand.

[014-045] Sudden, the master runs; aloud he calls;

[014-046] And from his hasty hand the leather falls:

[014-047] With showers of stones he drives then far away:

[014-048] The scattering dogs around at distance bay.

[014-049] “Unhappy stranger! (thus the faithful swain

[014-050] Began with accent gracious and humane),

[014-051] What sorrow had been mine, if at my gate

[014-052] Thy reverend age had met a shameful fate!

[014-053] Enough of woes already have I known;

[014-054] Enough my master’s sorrows and my own.

[014-055] While here (ungrateful task!) his herds I feed,

[014-056] Ordain’d for lawless rioters to bleed!

[014-057] Perhaps, supported at another’s board!

[014-058] Far from his country roams my hapless lord;

[014-059] Or sigh’d in exile forth his latest breath,

[014-060] Now cover’d with the eternal shade of death!

[014-061] “But enter this my homely roof, and see

[014-062] Our woods not void of hospitality.

[014-063] Then tell me whence thou art, and what the share

[014-064] Of woes and wanderings thou wert born to bear.”

[014-065] He said, and, seconding the kind request,

[014-066] With friendly step precedes his unknown guest.

[014-067] A shaggy goat’s soft hide beneath him spread,

[014-068] And with fresh rushes heap’d an ample bed;

[014-069] [[zeus|Jove]] touch’d the hero’s tender soul, to find

[014-070] So just reception from a heart so kind:

[014-071] And “Oh, ye gods! with all your blessings grace

[014-072] (He thus broke forth) this friend of human race!”

[014-073] The swain replied: “It never was our guise

[014-074] To slight the poor, or aught humane despise:

[014-075] For Jove unfolds our hospitable door,

[014-076] ’Tis Jove that sends the stranger and the poor,

[014-077] Little, alas! is all the good I can

[014-078] A man oppress’d, dependent, yet a man:

[014-079] Accept such treatment as a swain affords,

[014-080] Slave to the insolence of youthful lords!

[014-081] Far hence is by unequal gods removed

[014-082] That man of bounties, loving and beloved!

[014-083] To whom whate’er his slave enjoys is owed,

[014-084] And more, had [[moira|Fate]] allow’d, had been bestow’d:

[014-085] But Fate condemn’d him to a foreign shore;

[014-086] Much have I sorrow’d, but my Master more.

[014-087] Now cold he lies, to death’s embrace resign’d:

[014-088] Ah, perish [[helen|Helen]]! perish all her kind!

[014-089] For whose cursed cause, in [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]’s name,

[014-090] He trod so fatally the paths of fame.”

[014-091] His vest succinct then girding round his waist,

[014-092] Forth rush’d the swain with hospitable haste.

[014-093] Straight to the lodgments of his herd he run,

[014-094] Where the fat porkers slept beneath the sun;

[014-095] Of two, his cutlass launch’d the spouting blood;

[014-096] These quarter’d, singed, and fix’d on forks of wood,

[014-097] All hasty on the hissing coals he threw;

[014-098] And smoking, back the tasteful viands drew.

[014-099] Broachers and all then an the board display’d

[014-100] The ready meal, before Ulysses laid

[014-101] With flour imbrown’d; next mingled wine yet new,

[014-102] And luscious as the bees’ nectareous dew:

[014-103] Then sate, companion of the friendly feast,

[014-104] With open look; and thus bespoke his guest:

[014-105] “Take with free welcome what our hands prepare,

[014-106] Such food as falls to simple servants’ share;

[014-107] The best our lords consume; those thoughtless peers,

[014-108] Rich without bounty, guilty without fears;

[014-109] Yet sure the gods their impious acts detest,

[014-110] And honour justice and the righteous breast.

[014-111] Pirates and conquerors of harden’d mind,

[014-112] The foes of peace, and scourges of mankind,

[014-113] To whom offending men are made a prey

[014-114] When Jove in vengeance gives a land away;

[014-115] E’en these, when of their ill-got spoils possess’d,

[014-116] Find sure tormentors in the guilty breast:

[014-117] Some voice of God close whispering from within,

[014-118] ‘Wretch! this is villainy, and this is sin.’

[014-119] But these, no doubt, some oracle explore,

[014-120] That tells, the great Ulysses is no more.

[014-121] Hence springs their confidence, and from our sighs

[014-122] Their rapine strengthens, and their riots rise:

[014-123] Constant as Jove the night and day bestows,

[014-124] Bleeds a whole hecatomb, a vintage flows.

[014-125] None match’d this hero’s wealth, of all who reign

[014-126] O’er the fair islands of the neighbouring main.

[014-127] Nor all the monarchs whose far-dreaded sway

[014-128] The wide-extended continents obey:

[014-129] First, on the main land, of Ulysses’ breed

[014-130] Twelve herds, twelve flocks, on ocean’s margin feed;

[014-131] As many stalls for shaggy goats are rear’d;

[014-132] As many lodgments for the tusky herd;

[014-133] Two foreign keepers guard: and here are seen

[014-134] Twelve herds of goats that graze our utmost green;

[014-135] To native pastors is their charge assign’d,

[014-136] And mine the care to feed the bristly kind;

[014-137] Each day the fattest bleeds of either herd,

[014-138] All to the suitors’ wasteful board preferr’d.”

[014-139] Thus he, benevolent: his unknown guest

[014-140] With hunger keen devours the savoury feast;

[014-141] While schemes of vengeance ripen in his breast.

[014-142] Silent and thoughtful while the board he eyed,

[014-143] Eumaeus pours on high the purple tide;

[014-144] The king with smiling looks his joy express’d,

[014-145] And thus the kind inviting host address’d:

## Section 37

[014-146] “Say now, what man is he, the man deplored,

[014-147] So rich, so potent, whom you style your lord?

[014-148] Late with such affluence and possessions bless’d,

[014-149] And now in honour’s glorious bed at rest.

[014-150] Whoever was the warrior, he must be

[014-151] To fame no stranger, nor perhaps to me:

[014-152] Who (so the gods and so the Fates ordain’d)

[014-153] Have wander’d many a sea, and many a land.”

[014-154] “Small is the faith the prince and queen ascribe

[014-155] (Replied Eumaeus) to the wandering tribe.

[014-156] For needy strangers still to flattery fly,

[014-157] And want too oft betrays the tongue to lie.

[014-158] Each vagrant traveller, that touches here,

[014-159] Deludes with fallacies the royal ear,

[014-160] To dear remembrance makes his image rise,

[014-161] And calls the springing sorrows from her eyes.

[014-162] Such thou mayst be. But he whose name you crave

[014-163] Moulders in earth, or welters on the wave,

[014-164] Or food for fish or dogs his relics lie,

[014-165] Or torn by birds are scatter’d through the sky.

[014-166] So perish’d he: and left (for ever lost)

[014-167] Much woe to all, but sure to me the most.

[014-168] So mild a master never shall I find;

[014-169] Less dear the parents whom I left behind,

[014-170] Less soft my mother, less my father kind.

[014-171] Not with such transport would my eyes run o’er,

[014-172] Again to hail them in their native shore,

[014-173] As loved Ulysses once more to embrace,

[014-174] Restored and breathing in his natal place.

[014-175] That name for ever dread, yet ever dear,

[014-176] E’en in his absence I pronounce with fear:

[014-177] In my respect, he bears a prince’s part;

[014-178] But lives a very brother in my heart.”

[014-179] Thus spoke the faithful swain, and thus rejoin’d

[014-180] The master of his grief, the man of patient mind:

[014-181] “Ulysses, friend! shall view his old abodes

[014-182] (Distrustful as thou art), nor doubt the gods.

[014-183] Nor speak I rashly, but with faith averr’d,

[014-184] And what I speak attesting Heaven has heard.

[014-185] If so, a cloak and vesture be my meed:

[014-186] Till his return no title shall I plead,

[014-187] Though certain be my news, and great my need.

[014-188] Whom want itself can force untruths to tell,

[014-189] My soul detests him as the gates of hell.

[014-190] “Thou first be witness, hospitable Jove!

[014-191] And every god inspiring social love!

[014-192] And witness every household power that waits,

[014-193] Guard of these fires, and angel of these gates!

[014-194] Ere the next moon increase or this decay,

[014-195] His ancient realms Ulysses shall survey,

[014-196] In blood and dust each proud oppressor mourn,

[014-197] And the lost glories of his house return.”

[014-198] “Nor shall that meed be thine, nor ever more

[014-199] Shall loved Ulysses hail this happy shore.

[014-200] (Replied Eumaeus): to the present hour

[014-201] Now turn thy thought, and joys within our power.

[014-202] From sad reflection let my soul repose;

[014-203] The name of him awakes a thousand woes.

[014-204] But guard him, gods! and to these arms restore!

[014-205] Not his true consort can desire him more;

[014-206] Not old [[laertes|Laertes]], broken with despair:

[014-207] Not young [[telemachus|Telemachus]], his blooming heir.

[014-208] Alas, Telemachus! my sorrows flow

[014-209] Afresh for thee, my second cause of woe!

[014-210] Like some fair plant set by a heavenly hand,

[014-211] He grew, he flourish’d, and he bless’d the land;

[014-212] In all the youth his father’s image shined,

[014-213] Bright in his person, brighter in his mind.

[014-214] What man, or god, deceived his better sense,

[014-215] Far on the swelling seas to wander hence?

[014-216] To distant [[pylos|Pylos]] hapless is he gone,

[014-217] To seek his father’s fate and find his own!

[014-218] For traitors wait his way, with dire design

[014-219] To end at once the great Arcesian line.

[014-220] But let us leave him to their wills above;

[014-221] The fates of men are in the hand of Jove.

[014-222] And now, my venerable guest! declare

[014-223] Your name, your parents, and your native air:

[014-224] Sincere from whence begun, your course relate,

[014-225] And to what ship I owe the friendly freight?”

[014-226] Thus he: and thus (with prompt invention bold)

[014-227] The cautious chief his ready story told.

[014-228] “On dark reserve what better can prevail,

[014-229] Or from the fluent tongue produce the tale,

[014-230] Than when two friends, alone, in peaceful place

[014-231] Confer, and wines and cates the table grace;

[014-232] But most, the kind inviter’s cheerful face?

[014-233] Thus might we sit, with social goblets crown’d,

[014-234] Till the whole circle of the year goes round:

[014-235] Not the whole circle of the year would close

[014-236] My long narration of a life of woes.

[014-237] But such was Heaven’s high will! Know then, I came

[014-238] From sacred Crete, and from a sire of fame:

[014-239] Castor Hylacides (that name he bore),

[014-240] Beloved and honour’d in his native shore;

[014-241] Bless’d in his riches, in his children more.

[014-242] Sprung of a handmaid, from a bought embrace,

[014-243] I shared his kindness with his lawful race:

[014-244] But when that fate, which all must undergo,

[014-245] From earth removed him to the shades below,

[014-246] The large domain his greedy sons divide,

[014-247] And each was portion’d as the lots decide.

[014-248] Little, alas! was left my wretched share,

[014-249] Except a house, a covert from the air:

[014-250] But what by niggard fortune was denied,

[014-251] A willing widow’s copious wealth supplied.

[014-252] My valour was my plea, a gallant mind,

[014-253] That, true to honour, never lagg’d behind

[014-254] (The sex is ever to a soldier kind).

[014-255] Now wasting years my former strength confound,

[014-256] And added woes have bow’d me to the ground;

[014-257] Yet by the stubble you may guess the grain,

[014-258] And mark the ruins of no vulgar man.

[014-259] Me, Pallas gave to lead the martial storm,

[014-260] And the fair ranks of battle to deform;

[014-261] Me, Mars inspired to turn the foe to flight,

[014-262] And tempt the secret ambush of the night.

[014-263] Let ghastly Death in all his forms appear,

[014-264] I saw him not, it was not mine to fear.

[014-265] Before the rest I raised my ready steel,

[014-266] The first I met, he yielded, or he fell.

[014-267] But works of peace my soul disdain’d to bear,

[014-268] The rural labour, or domestic care.

[014-269] To raise the mast, the missile dart to wing,

[014-270] And send swift arrows from the bounding string,

[014-271] Were arts the gods made grateful to my mind;

[014-272] Those gods, who turn (to various ends design’d)

[014-273] The various thoughts and talents of mankind.

[014-274] Before the Grecians touch’d the Trojan plain,

[014-275] Nine times commander or by land or main,

[014-276] In foreign fields I spread my glory far,

[014-277] Great in the praise, rich in the spoils of war;

[014-278] Thence charged with riches, as increased in fame,

[014-279] To Crete return’d, an honourable name.

[014-280] But when great Jove that direful war decreed,

[014-281] Which roused all Greece, and made the mighty bleed;

[014-282] Our states myself and Idomen employ

[014-283] To lead their fleets, and carry death to [[troy|Troy]].

[014-284] Nine years we warr’d; the tenth saw Ilion fall;

[014-285] Homeward we sail’d, but heaven dispersed us all.

[014-286] One only month my wife enjoy’d my stay;

[014-287] So will’d the god who gives and takes away.

[014-288] Nine ships I mann’d, equipp’d with ready stores,

[014-289] Intent to voyage to the Ægyptian shores;

[014-290] In feast and sacrifice my chosen train

[014-291] Six days consum’d; the seventh we plough’d the main.

[014-292] Crete’s ample fields diminish to our eye;

[014-293] Before the Boreal blast the vessels fly;

[014-294] Safe through the level seas we sweep our way;

[014-295] The steersman governs, and the ships obey.

[014-296] The fifth fair morn we stem the Ægyptian tide,

[014-297] And tilting o’er the bay the vessels ride:

[014-298] To anchor there my fellows I command,

[014-299] And spies commission to explore the land.

[014-300] But, sway’d by lust of gain, and headlong will,

[014-301] The coasts they ravage, and the natives kill.

[014-302] The spreading clamour to their city flies,

[014-303] And horse and foot in mingled tumult rise.

[014-304] The reddening dawn reveals the circling fields,

[014-305] Horrid with bristly spears, and glancing shields.

[014-306] Jove thunder’d on their side. Our guilty head

[014-307] We turn’d to flight; the gathering vengeance spread

[014-308] On all parts round, and heaps on heaps lie dead.

[014-309] I then explored my thought, what course to prove

[014-310] (And sure the thought was dictated by Jove):

[014-311] Oh, had he left me to that happier doom,

[014-312] And saved a life of miseries to come!

[014-313] The radiant helmet from my brows unlaced,

[014-314] And low on earth my shield and javelin cast,

[014-315] I meet the monarch with a suppliant’s face,

[014-316] Approach his chariot, and his knees embrace,

[014-317] He heard, he saved, he placed me at his side;

[014-318] My state he pitied, and my tears he dried,

[014-319] Restrain’d the rage the vengeful foe express’d,

[014-320] And turn’d the deadly weapons from my breast.

[014-321] Pious! to guard the hospitable rite,

[014-322] And fearing Jove, whom mercy’s works delight.

[014-323] “In Ægypt thus with peace and plenty bless’d,

[014-324] I lived (and happy still have lived) a guest.

[014-325] On seven bright years successive blessings wait;

[014-326] The next changed all the colour of my fate.

[014-327] A false Phoenician, of insiduous mind,

[014-328] Versed in vile arts, and foe to humankind,

[014-329] With semblance fair invites me to his home;

[014-330] I seized the proffer (ever fond to roam):

[014-331] Domestic in his faithless roof I stay’d,

[014-332] Till the swift sun his annual circle made.

[014-333] To Libya then he mediates the way;

[014-334] With guileful art a stranger to betray,

[014-335] And sell to bondage in a foreign land:

[014-336] Much doubting, yet compell’d I quit the strand,

[014-337] Through the mid seas the nimble pinnace sails,

[014-338] Aloof from Crete, before the northern gales:

[014-339] But when remote her chalky cliffs we lost,

[014-340] And far from ken of any other coast,

[014-341] When all was wild expanse of sea and air,

[014-342] Then doom’d high Jove due vengeance to prepare.

[014-343] He hung a night of horrors o’er their head

[014-344] (The shaded ocean blacken’d as it spread):

[014-345] He launch’d the fiery bolt: from pole to pole

[014-346] Broad burst the lightnings, deep the thunders roll;

[014-347] In giddy rounds the whirling ship is toss’d,

[014-348] And all in clouds of smothering sulphur lost.

[014-349] As from a hanging rock’s tremendous height,

[014-350] The sable crows with intercepted flight

[014-351] Drop endlong; scarr’d, and black with sulphurous hue,

[014-352] So from the deck are hurl’d the ghastly crew.

[014-353] Such end the wicked found! but Jove’s intent

[014-354] Was yet to save the oppress’d and innocent.

[014-355] Placed on the mast (the last resource of life)

[014-356] With winds and waves I held unequal strife:

[014-357] For nine long days the billows tilting o’er,

[014-358] The tenth soft wafts me to Thesprotia’s shore.

[014-359] The monarch’s son a shipwreck’d wretch relieved,

[014-360] The sire with hospitable rites received,

[014-361] And in his palace like a brother placed,

[014-362] With gifts of price and gorgeous garments graced

[014-363] While here I sojourn’d, oft I heard the fame

[014-364] How late Ulysses to the country came.

[014-365] How loved, how honour’d in this court he stay’d,

[014-366] And here his whole collected treasure laid;

[014-367] I saw myself the vast unnumber’d store

[014-368] Of steel elaborate, and refulgent ore,

[014-369] And brass high heap’d amidst the regal dome;

[014-370] Immense supplies for ages yet to come!

[014-371] Meantime he voyaged to explore the will

[014-372] Of Jove, on high Dodona’s holy hill,

[014-373] What means might best his safe return avail,

[014-374] To come in pomp, or bear a secret sail?

[014-375] Full oft has Phidon, whilst he pour’d the wine,

[014-376] Attesting solemn all the powers divine,

[014-377] That soon Ulysses would return, declared

[014-378] The sailors waiting, and the ships prepared.

[014-379] But first the king dismiss’d me from his shores,

[014-380] For fair [[dulichium|Dulichium]] crown’d with fruitful stores;

[014-381] To good Acastus’ friendly care consign’d:

[014-382] But other counsels pleased the sailors’ mind:

[014-383] New frauds were plotted by the faithless train,

[014-384] And misery demands me once again.

[014-385] Soon as remote from shore they plough the wave,

[014-386] With ready hands they rush to seize their slave;

[014-387] Then with these tatter’d rags they wrapp’d me round

[014-388] (Stripp’d of my own), and to the vessel bound.

[014-389] At eve, at [[ithaca|Ithaca]]’s delightful land

[014-390] The ship arriv’d: forth issuing on the sand,

[014-391] They sought repast; while to the unhappy kind,

[014-392] The pitying gods themselves my chains unbind.

[014-393] Soft I descended, to the sea applied

[014-394] My naked breast, and shot along the tide.

[014-395] Soon pass’d beyond their sight, I left the flood,

[014-396] And took the spreading shelter of the wood.

[014-397] Their prize escaped the faithless pirates mourn’d;

[014-398] But deem’d inquiry vain, and to their ships return’d.

[014-399] Screen’d by protecting gods from hostile eyes,

[014-400] They led me to a good man and a wise,

[014-401] To live beneath thy hospitable care,

[014-402] And wait the woes Heaven dooms me yet to bear.”

## Section 38

[014-403] “Unhappy guest! whose sorrows touch my mind!

[014-404] (Thus good Eumaeus with a sigh rejoin’d,)

[014-405] For real sufferings since I grieve sincere,

[014-406] Check not with fallacies the springing tear:

[014-407] Nor turn the passion into groundless joy

[014-408] For him whom Heaven has destined to destroy.

[014-409] Oh! had he perish’d on some well-fought day,

[014-410] Or in his friend’s embraces died away!

[014-411] That grateful Greece with streaming eyes might raise

[014-412] Historic marbles to record his praise;

[014-413] His praise, eternal on the faithful stone,

[014-414] Had with transmissive honours graced his son.

[014-415] Now, snatch’d by harpies to the dreary coast,

[014-416] Sunk is the hero, and his glory lost!

[014-417] While pensive in this solitary den,

[014-418] Far from gay cities and the ways of men,

[014-419] I linger life; nor to the court repair,

[014-420] But when my constant queen commands my care;

[014-421] Or when, to taste her hospitable board,

[014-422] Some guest arrives, with rumours of her lord;

[014-423] And these indulge their want, and those their woe,

[014-424] And here the tears and there the goblets flow.

[014-425] By many such have I been warn’d; but chief

[014-426] By one Aetolian robb’d of all belief,

[014-427] Whose hap it was to this our roof to roam,

[014-428] For murder banish’d from his native home.

[014-429] He swore, Ulysses on the coast of Crete

[014-430] Stay’d but a season to refit his fleet;

[014-431] A few revolving months should waft him o’er,

[014-432] Fraught with bold warriors, and a boundless store

[014-433] O thou! whom age has taught to understand,

[014-434] And Heaven has guided with a favouring hand!

[014-435] On god or mortal to obtrude a lie

[014-436] Forbear, and dread to flatter as to die.

[014-437] Nor for such ends my house and heart are free,

[014-438] But dear respect to Jove, and charity.”

[014-439] “And why, O swain of unbelieving mind!

[014-440] (Thus quick replied the wisest of mankind)

[014-441] Doubt you my oath? yet more my faith to try,

[014-442] A solemn compact let us ratify,
