# Circe's Island

## Summary

The sojourn on [[circe|Circe]]'s island ([[aeaea|Aeaea]]) is the central episode of Book 10. Having lost eleven ships to the [[telepylus|Laestrygonia]]ns, [[odysseus|Odysseus]] and his sole remaining crew land on [[aeaea|Aeaea]]. [[eurylochus|Eurylochus]] leads a scouting party to [[circe|Circe]]'s palace — "Of [[circe|Circe]]'s palace bosom'd in the grove" (010-189) — where the enchantress transforms all but [[eurylochus|Eurylochus]] into swine. [[odysseus|Odysseus]] undertakes the rescue alone, receiving the herb Moly from [[hermes|Hermes]] as protection. He overcomes [[circe|Circe]]'s magic, compels her to restore his men, and remains on the island for a full year. At year's end, [[circe|Circe]] commands the hero to voyage to the Underworld to consult [[tiresias|Tiresias]].

## Context & Causes

The episode follows the catastrophic encounter with the [[telepylus|Laestrygonia]]ns, which reduced [[odysseus|Odysseus]]'s fleet from twelve ships to one. Desperate for provisions and shelter, the crew lands on [[aeaea|Aeaea]]. The island's apparent tranquillity — smoke rising from [[circe|Circe]]'s palace through the trees — conceals a supernatural danger. This episode is the first in the *[[the-odyssey|Odyssey]]* where a female divine figure exercises independent power over [[odysseus|Odysseus]], establishing a pattern repeated with [[calypso|Calypso]] and [[nausicaa|Nausicaa]]. Unlike the [[telepylus|Laestrygonia]]ns (pure violence) or [[polyphemus|the Cyclops]] (force countered by metis), [[circe|Circe]] represents seduction as danger — the threat of losing human form and memory.

## Key Moments

1. **The Scouting Party**: [[eurylochus|Eurylochus]] leads twenty-two men inland. They discover [[circe|Circe]]'s palace, where she welcomes them with food and wine laced with her drug. One touch of her wand transforms them — "To hogs transforms them, and the sty receives" [010-454: alt] — their minds remaining human within bestial bodies.

2. **[[eurylochus|Eurylochus]]'s Flight**: Only [[eurylochus|Eurylochus]], who suspected treachery and remained outside, escapes. His terrified report nearly demoralizes the crew, and he initially refuses to lead [[odysseus|Odysseus]] back to the palace.

3. **[[hermes|Hermes]]'s Intervention**: As [[odysseus|Odysseus]] approaches [[circe|Circe]]'s palace alone, [[hermes|Hermes]] appears in the form of a young man. He reveals [[circe|Circe]]'s magic and gives [[odysseus|Odysseus]] the herb Moly: "Moly the name, to mortals hard to find" [010-486: alt]. The god explains the counter-spell: [[odysseus|Odysseus]] must drink [[circe|Circe]]'s drug, then draw his sword when she strikes with her wand.

4. **Confrontation and Pact**: [[circe|Circe]]'s magic fails against the Moly-protected [[odysseus|Odysseus]]. When she cries out "'And how, dread [[circe|Circe]]!' (furious I rejoin)" (010-412), he demands an oath that she will not harm him. She swears, and they seal a pact that also guarantees his men's restoration.

5. **The Year's Stay**: [[circe|Circe]] restores the crew — "the late transform'd repairs" [010-539: alt] — and hosts all of them for a full year of feasting. At its end, she freely commands [[odysseus|Odysseus]]'s voyage to the Underworld: "'Rise, rise, my mates! 'tis [[circe|Circe]] gives command'" (010-668).

## Aftermath & Consequences

The [[circe|Circe]] episode transforms [[the-wanderings-of-odysseus|Odysseus's journey]] in three ways. First, it provides the essential directive for the [[the-underworld|Nekyia]] ([[the-underworld|Book 11]]) — without [[circe|Circe]]'s command, [[odysseus|Odysseus]] would not consult [[tiresias|Tiresias]] and learn his fate. Second, it establishes the hero's relationship with a goddess who shifts from antagonist to ally: "E'en [[circe|Circe]] wept, her adamantine heart" (010-486) when he departs. Third, the year's delay becomes part of the poem's meditation on time and nostos — a year spent in comfort on [[aeaea|Aeaea]] is as much an obstacle to return as any monster. The episode also prefigures the [[calypso|Calypso]] episode: a goddess detains the hero on her island, offering pleasures that threaten to erase his desire for home.
