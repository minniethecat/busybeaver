[015-001] BOOK XV.

[015-002] ARGUMENT.

[015-003] THE RETURN OF TELEMACHUS.

[015-004] The goddess [[athena|Minerva]] commands [[telemachus|Telemachus]] in a vision to return to

[015-005] [[ithaca|Ithaca]]. [[pisistratus|Pisistratus]] and he take leave of [[menelaus|Menelaus]], and arrive at [[pylos|Pylos]],

[015-006] where they part: and Telemachus sets sail, after having received on

[015-007] board [[theoclymenus|Theoclymenus]] the soothsayer. The scene then changes to the

[015-008] cottage of [[eumaeus|Eumaeus]], who entertains [[odysseus|Ulysses]] with a recital of his

[015-009] adventures. In the meantime Telemachus arrives on the coast, and

[015-010] sending the vessel to the town, proceeds by himself to the lodge of

[015-011] Eumaeus.

[015-012] Now had Minerva reach’d those ample plains,

[015-013] Famed for the dance, where Menelaus reigns:

[015-014] Anxious she flies to great Ulysses’ heir,

[015-015] His instant voyage challenged all her care.

[015-016] Beneath the royal portico display’d,

[015-017] With [[nestor|Nestor]]’s son Telemachus was laid:

[015-018] In sleep profound the son of Nestor lies;

[015-019] Not thine, Ulysses! Care unseal’d his eyes:

[015-020] Restless he grieved, with various fears oppress’d,

[015-021] And all thy fortunes roll’d within his breast.

[015-022] When, “O Telemachus! (the goddess said)

[015-023] Too long in vain, too widely hast thou stray’d,

[015-024] Thus leaving careless thy paternal right

[015-025] The robbers’ prize, the prey to lawless might.

[015-026] On fond pursuits neglectful while you roam,

[015-027] E’en now the hand of rapine sacks the dome.

[015-028] Hence to [[agamemnon|Atrides]]; and his leave implore

[015-029] To launch thy vessel for thy natal shore;

[015-030] Fly, whilst thy mother virtuous yet withstands

[015-031] Her kindred’s wishes, and her sire’s commands;

[015-032] Through both, [[eurymachus|Eurymachus]] pursues the dame,

[015-033] And with the noblest gifts asserts his claim.

[015-034] Hence, therefore, while thy stores thy own remain;

[015-035] Thou know’st the practice of the female train,

[015-036] Lost in the children of the present spouse,

[015-037] They slight the pledges of their former vows;

[015-038] Their love is always with the lover past;

[015-039] Still the succeeding flame expels the last.

[015-040] Let o’er thy house some chosen maid preside,

[015-041] Till Heaven decrees to bless thee in a bride.

[015-042] But now thy more attentive ears incline,

[015-043] Observe the warnings of a power divine;

[015-044] For thee their snares the suitor lords shall lay

[015-045] In [[same|Samos]]’ sands, or straits of Ithaca;

[015-046] To seize thy life shall lurk the murderous band,

[015-047] Ere yet thy footsteps press thy native land.

[015-048] No!—sooner far their riot and their lust

[015-049] All-covering earth shall bury deep in dust!

[015-050] Then distant from the scatter’d islands steer,

[015-051] Nor let the night retard thy full career;

[015-052] Thy heavenly guardian shall instruct the gales

[015-053] To smooth thy passage and supply thy sails:

[015-054] And when at Ithaca thy labour ends,

[015-055] Send to the town the vessel with thy friends;

[015-056] But seek thou first the master of the swine

[015-057] (For still to thee his loyal thoughts incline);

[015-058] There pass the night: while he his course pursues

[015-059] To bring [[penelope|Penelope]] the wish’d-for news,

[015-060] That thou, safe sailing from the Pylian strand,

[015-061] Art come to bless her in thy native land.”

[015-062] Thus spoke the goddess, and resumed her flight

[015-063] To the pure regions of eternal light,

[015-064] Meanwhile Pisistratus he gently shakes,

[015-065] And with these words the slumbering youth awakes:

[015-066] “Rise, son of Nestor; for the road prepare,

[015-067] And join the harness’d coursers to the car.”

[015-068] “What cause (he cried) can justify our flight

[015-069] To tempt the dangers of forbidding night?

[015-070] Here wait we rather, till approaching day

[015-071] Shall prompt our speed, and point the ready way.

[015-072] Nor think of flight before the Spartan king

[015-073] Shall bid farewell, and bounteous presents bring;

[015-074] Gifts, which to distant ages safely stored,

[015-075] The sacred act of friendship shall record.”

[015-076] Thus he. But when the dawn bestreak’d the east,

[015-077] The king from [[helen|Helen]] rose, and sought his guest.

[015-078] As soon as his approach the hero knew,

[015-079] The splendid mantle round him first he threw,

[015-080] Then o’er his ample shoulders whirl’d the cloak,

[015-081] Respectful met the monarch, and bespoke:

[015-082] “Hail, great Atrides, favour’d of high [[zeus|Jove]]!

[015-083] Let not thy friends in vain for licence move.

[015-084] Swift let us measure back the watery way,

[015-085] Nor check our speed, impatient of delay.”

[015-086] “If with desire so strong thy bosom glows,

[015-087] Ill (said the king) should I thy wish oppose;

[015-088] For oft in others freely I reprove

[015-089] The ill-timed efforts of officious love;

[015-090] Who love too much, hate in the like extreme,

[015-091] And both the golden mean alike condemn.

[015-092] Alike he thwarts the hospitable end,

[015-093] Who drives the free, or stays the hasty friend:

[015-094] True friendship’s laws are by this rule express’d,

[015-095] Welcome the coming, speed the parting guest.

[015-096] Yet, stay, my friends, and in your chariot take

[015-097] The noblest presents that our love can make;

[015-098] Meantime commit we to our women’s care

[015-099] Some choice domestic viands to prepare;

[015-100] The traveller, rising from the banquet gay,

[015-101] Eludes the labours of the tedious way,

[015-102] Then if a wider course shall rather please,

[015-103] Through spacious Argos and the realms of Greece,

[015-104] Atrides in his chariot shall attend;

[015-105] Himself thy convoy to each royal friend.

[015-106] No prince will let Ulysses’ heir remove

[015-107] Without some pledge, some monument of love:

[015-108] These will the caldron, these the tripod give;

[015-109] From those the well-pair’d mules we shall receive,

[015-110] Or bowl emboss’d whose golden figures live.”

[015-111] To whom the youth, for prudence famed, replied:

[015-112] “O monarch, care of heaven! thy people’s pride!

[015-113] No friend in Ithaca my place supplies,

[015-114] No powerful hands are there, no watchful eyes:

[015-115] My stores exposed and fenceless house demand

[015-116] The speediest succour from my guardian hand;

[015-117] Lest, in a search too anxious and too vain,

[015-118] Of one lost joy, I lose what yet remain.”

[015-119] His purpose when the generous warrior heard,

[015-120] He charged the household cates to be prepared.

[015-121] Now with the dawn, from his adjoining home,

[015-122] Was Boethoedes Eteoneus come;

[015-123] Swift at the word he forms the rising blaze,

[015-124] And o’er the coals the smoking fragments lays.

[015-125] Meantime the king, his son, and Helen went

[015-126] Where the rich wardrobe breathed a costly scent;

[015-127] The king selected from the glittering rows

[015-128] A bowl; the prince a silver beaker chose.

[015-129] The beauteous queen revolved with careful eyes

[015-130] Her various textures of unnumber’d dyes,

[015-131] And chose the largest; with no vulgar art

[015-132] Her own fair hands embroider’d every part;

[015-133] Beneath the rest it lay divinely bright,

[015-134] Like radiant Hesper o’er the gems of night,

[015-135] Then with each gift they hasten’d to their guest,

[015-136] And thus the king Ulysses’ heir address’d:

[015-137] “Since fix’d are thy resolves, may thundering Jove

[015-138] With happiest omens thy desires approve!

[015-139] This silver bowl, whose costly margins shine

[015-140] Enchased with gold, this valued gift be thine;

[015-141] To me this present, of Vulcanian frame,

[015-142] From Sidon’s hospitable monarch came;

[015-143] To thee we now consign the precious load,

[015-144] The pride of kings, and labour of a god.”

[015-145] Then gave the cup, while Megapenthe brought

[015-146] The silver vase with living sculpture wrought.

[015-147] The beauteous queen, advancing next, display’d

[015-148] The shining veil, and thus endearing said:

[015-149] “Accept, dear youth, this monument of love,

[015-150] Long since, in better days, by Helen wove:

[015-151] Safe in thy mother’s care the vesture lay,

[015-152] To deck thy bride and grace thy nuptial day.

[015-153] Meantime may’st thou with happiest speed regain

[015-154] Thy stately palace, and thy wide domain.”

[015-155] She said, and gave the veil; with grateful look

[015-156] The prince the variegated present took.

[015-157] And now, when through the royal dome they pass’d,

[015-158] High on a throne the king each stranger placed.

[015-159] A golden ewer the attendant damsel brings,

[015-160] Replete with water from the crystal springs;

[015-161] With copious streams the shining vase supplies

[015-162] A silver layer of capacious size.

[015-163] They wash. The tables in fair order spread,

[015-164] The glittering canisters are crown’d with bread;

[015-165] Viands of various kinds allure the taste,

[015-166] Of choicest sort and savour; rich repast!

[015-167] Whilst Eteoneus portions out the shares

[015-168] Atrides’ son the purple draught prepares,

[015-169] And now (each sated with the genial feast,

[015-170] And the short rage of thirst and hunger ceased)

[015-171] Ulysses’ son, with his illustrious friend,

[015-172] The horses join, the polish’d car ascend,

[015-173] Along the court the fiery steeds rebound,

[015-174] And the wide portal echoes to the sound.

[015-175] The king precedes; a bowl with fragrant wine

[015-176] (Libation destined to the powers divine)

[015-177] His right hand held: before the steed he stands,

[015-178] Then, mix’d with prayers, he utters these commands:

[015-179] “Farewell, and prosper, youths! let Nestor know

[015-180] What grateful thoughts still in this bosom glow,

[015-181] For all the proofs of his paternal care,

[015-182] Through the long dangers of the ten years’ war.”

[015-183] “Ah! doubt not our report (the prince rejoin’d)

[015-184] Of all the virtues of thy generous mind.

[015-185] And oh! return’d might we Ulysses meet!

[015-186] To him thy presents show, thy words repeat:

[015-187] How will each speech his grateful wonder raise!

[015-188] How will each gift indulge us in thy praise!”

[015-189] Scarce ended thus the prince, when on the right

[015-190] Advanced the bird of Jove: auspicious sight!

[015-191] A milk-white fowl his clinching talons bore,

[015-192] With care domestic pampered at the floor.

[015-193] Peasants in vain with threatening cries pursue,

[015-194] In solemn speed the bird majestic flew

[015-195] Full dexter to the car; the prosperous sight

[015-196] Fill’d every breast with wonder and delight.

[015-197] But Nestor’s son the cheerful silence broke,

[015-198] And in these words the Spartan chief bespoke:

[015-199] “Say if to us the gods these omens send,

[015-200] Or fates peculiar to thyself portend?”

[015-201] Whilst yet the monarch paused, with doubts oppress’d

[015-202] The beauteous queen relieved his labouring breast:

[015-203] “Hear me (she cried), to whom the gods have given

[015-204] To read this sign, and mystic sense of heaven,

[015-205] As thus the plumy sovereign of the air

[015-206] Left on the mountain’s brow his callow care,

[015-207] And wander’d through the wide ethereal way

[015-208] To pour his wrath on yon luxurious prey;

[015-209] So shall thy godlike father, toss’d in vain

[015-210] Through all the dangers of the boundless main,

[015-211] Arrive (or if perchance already come)

[015-212] From slaughter’d gluttons to release the dome.”

[015-213] “Oh! if this promised bliss by thundering Jove

[015-214] (The prince replied) stand fix’d in fate above;

[015-215] To thee, as to some god, I’ll temples raise.

[015-216] And crown thy altars with the costly blaze.”

[015-217] He said; and bending o’er his chariot, flung

[015-218] Athwart the fiery steeds the smarting thong;

[015-219] The bounding shafts upon the harness play,

[015-220] Till night descending intercepts the way.

[015-221] To Diocles at Pherae they repair,

[015-222] Whose boasted sire was sacred Alpheus’ heir;

[015-223] With him all night the youthful stranger stay’d,

[015-224] Nor found the hospitable rites unpaid,

[015-225] But soon as morning from her orient bed

[015-226] Had tinged the mountains with her earliest red,

[015-227] They join’d the steeds, and on the chariot sprung,

[015-228] The brazen portals in their passage rung.

[015-229] To Pylos soon they came; when thus begun

[015-230] To Nestor’s heir Ulysses’ godlike son:

[015-231] “Let not Pisistratus in vain be press’d,

[015-232] Nor unconsenting hear his friend’s request;

[015-233] His friend by long hereditary claim,

[015-234] In toils his equal, and in years the same.

[015-235] No farther from our vessel, I implore,

[015-236] The coursers drive; but lash them to the shore.

[015-237] Too long thy father would his friend detain;

[015-238] I dread his proffer’d kindness urged in vain.”

[015-239] The hero paused, and ponder’d this request,

[015-240] While love and duty warr’d within his breast.

[015-241] At length resolved, he turn’d his ready hand,

[015-242] And lash’d his panting coursers to the strand.

[015-243] There, while within the poop with care he stored

[015-244] The regal presents of the Spartan lord,

[015-245] “With speed begone (said he); call every mate,

[015-246] Ere yet to Nestor I the tale relate:

[015-247] ’Tis true, the fervour of his generous heart

[015-248] Brooks no repulse, nor couldst thou soon depart:

[015-249] Himself will seek thee here, nor wilt thou find,

[015-250] In words alone, the Pylian monarch kind.

[015-251] But when, arrived, he thy return shall know

[015-252] How will his breast with honest fury glow!”

[015-253] This said, the sounding strokes his horses fire,

[015-254] And soon he reached the palace of his sire.

[015-255] “Now (cried Telemachus) with speedy care

[015-256] Hoist every sail, and every oar prepare.”

[015-257] Swift as the word his willing mates obey,

[015-258] And seize their seats, impatient for the sea.

[015-259] Meantime the prince with sacrifice adores

[015-260] Minerva, and her guardian aid implores;

[015-261] When lo! a wretch ran breathless to the shore,

[015-262] New from his crime; and reeking yet with gore.

[015-263] A seer he was, from great Melampus sprung,

[015-264] Melampus, who in Pylos flourish’d long,

[015-265] Till, urged by wrongs, a foreign realm he chose,

[015-266] Far from the hateful cause of all his woes.

[015-267] Neleus his treasures one long year detains,

[015-268] As long he groan’d in Philacus’s chains:

[015-269] Meantime, what anguish and what rage combined

[015-270] For lovely Pero rack’d his labouring mind!

[015-271] Yet ’scaped he death; and vengeful of his wrong

[015-272] To Pylos drove the lowing herds along:

[015-273] Then (Neleus vanquish’d, and consign’d the fair

[015-274] To Bias’ arms) he so sought a foreign air;

[015-275] Argos the rich for his retreat he chose,

[015-276] There form’d his empire; there his palace rose.

[015-278] The first begot Oicleus great in fame,

[015-279] And he Amphiaraus, immortal name!

[015-280] The people’s saviour, and divinely wise,

[015-281] Beloved by Jove, and him who gilds the skies;

[015-282] Yet short his date of life! by female pride he dies.

[015-283] From Mantius Clitus, whom Aurora’s love

[015-284] Snatch’d for his beauty to the thrones above;

[015-285] And Polyphides, on whom Phœbus shone

[015-286] With fullest rays, Amphiaraus now gone;

[015-287] In Hyperesia’s groves he made abode,

[015-290] (The sacred wine yet foaming on the ground)

[015-291] Telemachus: whom, as to Heaven he press’d

[015-292] His ardent vows, the stranger thus address’d:

[015-293] “O thou! that dost thy happy course prepare

[015-294] With pure libations and with solemn prayer:

[015-295] By that dread power to whom thy vows are paid;

[015-296] By all the lives of these; thy own dear head,

[015-299] “Prepare, then (said Telemachus), to know

[015-302] And great Ulysses (ever honour’d name!)

[015-337] “Yet one night more, my friends, indulge your guest;

[015-338] The last I purpose in your walls to rest:

[015-339] To-morrow for myself I must provide,

[015-340] And only ask your counsel, and a guide;

[015-341] Patient to roam the street, by hunger led,

[015-342] And bless the friendly hand that gives me bread.

[015-344] Ulysses’ wanderings to his royal mate;

[015-438] A part we consecrate to soft repose,

[015-439] A part in pleasing talk we entertain;

[015-440] For too much rest itself becomes a pain.

[015-441] Let those, whom sleep invites, the call obey,

[015-442] Their cares resuming with the dawning day:

[015-443] Here let us feast, and to the feast be join’d

[015-444] Discourse, the sweeter banquet of the mind;

[015-445] Review the series of our lives, and taste

[015-446] The melancholy joy of evils pass’d:

[015-447] For he who much has suffer’d, much will know,

[015-448] And pleased remembrance builds delight on woe.

[015-449] “Above Ortygia lies an isle of fame,

[015-450] Far hence remote, and Syria is the name

[015-451] (There curious eyes inscribed with wonder trace

[015-452] The sun’s diurnal, and his annual race);

[015-453] Not large, but fruitful; stored with grass to keep

[015-454] The bellowing oxen and the bleating sheep;

[015-455] Her sloping hills the mantling vines adorn,

[015-456] And her rich valleys wave with golden corn.

[015-457] No want, no famine, the glad natives know,

[015-458] Nor sink by sickness to the shades below;

[015-459] But when a length of years unnerves the strong,

[015-460] [[apollo|Apollo]] comes, and Cynthia comes along.

[015-461] They bend the silver bow with tender skill,

[015-462] And, void of pain, the silent arrows kill.

[015-463] Two equal tribes this fertile land divide,

[015-464] Where two fair cities rise with equal pride.

[015-465] But both in constant peace one prince obey,

[015-466] And Ctesius there, my father, holds the sway.

[015-467] Freighted, it seems, with toys of every sort,

[015-468] A ship of Sidon anchor’d in our port;

[015-469] What time it chanced the palace entertain’d,

[015-470] Skill’d in rich works, a woman of their land:

[015-471] This nymph, where anchor’d the Phoenician train,

[015-472] To wash her robes descending to the main,

[015-473] A smooth tongued sailor won her to his mind

[015-474] (For love deceives the best of womankind).

[015-475] A sudden trust from sudden liking grew;

[015-476] She told her name, her race, and all she knew,

[015-477] ‘I too (she cried) from glorious Sidon came,

[015-478] My father Arybas, of wealthy fame:

[015-479] But, snatch’d by pirates from my native place,

[015-480] The Taphians sold me to this man’s embrace.’

[015-481] “‘Haste then (the false designing youth replied),

[015-482] Haste to thy country; love shall be thy guide;

[015-483] Haste to thy father’s house, thy father’s breast,

[015-484] For still he lives, and lives with riches blest.’

[015-485] “‘Swear first (she cried), ye sailors! to restore

[015-486] A wretch in safety to her native shore.’

[015-487] Swift as she ask’d, the ready sailors swore.

[015-488] She then proceeds: ‘Now let our compact made

[015-489] Be nor by signal nor by word betray’d,

[015-490] Nor near me any of your crew descried,

[015-491] By road frequented, or by fountain side.

[015-492] Be silence still our guard. The monarch’s spies

[015-493] (For watchful age is ready to surmise)

[015-494] Are still at hand; and this, revealed, must be

[015-495] Death to yourselves, eternal chains to me.

[015-496] Your vessel loaded, and your traffic pass’d,

[015-497] Despatch a wary messenger with haste;

[015-498] Then gold and costly treasures will I bring,

[015-499] And more, the infant offspring of the king.

[015-500] Him, child-like wandering forth, I’ll lead away

[015-501] (A noble prize!) and to your ship convey.’

[015-502] “Thus spoke the dame, and homeward took the road.

[015-503] A year they traffic, and their vessel load.

[015-504] Their stores complete, and ready now to weigh,

[015-505] A spy was sent their summons to convey:

[015-506] An artist to my father’s palace came,

[015-507] With gold and amber chains, elaborate frame:

[015-508] Each female eye the glittering links employ;

[015-509] They turn, review, and cheapen every toy.

[015-510] He took the occasion, as they stood intent,

[015-511] Gave her the sign, and to his vessel went.

[015-512] She straight pursued, and seized my willing arm;

[015-513] I follow’d, smiling, innocent of harm.

[015-514] Three golden goblets in the porch she found

[015-515] (The guests not enter’d, but the table crown’d);

[015-516] Hid in her fraudful bosom these she bore:

[015-517] Now set the sun, and darken’d all the shore.

[015-518] Arriving then, where tilting on the tides

[015-519] Prepared to launch the freighted vessel rides,

[015-520] Aboard they heave us, mount their decks, and sweep

[015-521] With level oar along the glassy deep.

[015-522] Six calmy days and six smooth nights we sail,

[015-523] And constant Jove supplied the gentle gale.

[015-524] The seventh, the fraudful wretch (no cause descried),

[015-525] Touch’d by Diana’s vengeful arrow, died.

[015-526] Down dropp’d the caitiff-corse, a worthless load,

[015-527] Down to the deep; there roll’d, the future food

[015-528] Of fierce sea-wolves, and monsters of the flood.

[015-529] An helpless infant I remain’d behind;

[015-530] Thence borne to Ithaca by wave and wind;

[015-531] Sold to Laertes by divine command,

[015-532] And now adopted to a foreign land.”

[015-533] To him the king: “Reciting thus thy cares,

[015-534] My secret soul in all thy sorrow shares;

[015-535] But one choice blessing (such is Jove’s high will)

[015-536] Has sweeten’d all thy bitter draught of ill:

[015-537] Torn from thy country to no hapless end,

[015-538] The gods have, in a master, given a friend.

[015-539] Whatever frugal nature needs is thine

[015-540] (For she needs little), daily bread and wine.

[015-541] While I, so many wanderings past, and woes,

[015-542] Live but on what thy poverty bestows.”

[015-543] So passed in pleasing dialogue away

[015-544] The night; then down to short repose they lay;

[015-545] Till radiant rose the messenger of day.

[015-546] While in the port of Ithaca, the band

[015-547] Of young Telemachus approach’d the land;

[015-548] Their sails they loosed, they lash’d the mast aside,

[015-549] And cast their anchors, and the cables tied:

[015-550] Then on the breezy shore, descending, join

[015-551] In grateful banquet o’er the rosy wine.

[015-552] When thus the prince: “Now each his course pursue;

[015-553] I to the fields, and to the city you.

[015-554] Long absent hence, I dedicate this day

[015-555] My swains to visit, and the works survey.

[015-556] Expect me with the morn, to pay the skies

[015-557] Our debt of safe return in feast and sacrifice.”

[015-558] Then Theoclymenus: “But who shall lend,

[015-559] Meantime, protection to thy stranger friend?

[015-560] Straight to the queen and palace shall I fly,

[015-561] Or yet more distant, to some lord apply?”

[015-562] The prince return’d: “[[kleos|Renown]]’d in days of yore

[015-563] Has stood our father’s hospitable door;

[015-564] No other roof a stranger should receive,

[015-565] No other hands than ours the welcome give.

[015-566] But in my absence riot fills the place,

[015-567] Nor bears the modest queen a stranger’s face;

[015-568] From noiseful revel far remote she flies,

[015-569] But rarely seen, or seen with weeping eyes.

[015-570] No—let Eurymachus receive my guest,

[015-571] Of nature courteous, and by far the best;

[015-572] He woos the queen with more respectful flame,

[015-573] And emulates her former husband’s fame,

[015-574] With what success, ’tis Jove’s alone to know,

[015-575] And the hoped nuptials turn to joy or woe.”

[015-576] Thus speaking, on the right up-soar’d in air

[015-577] The hawk, Apollo’s swift-wing’d messenger:

[015-578] His dreadful pounces tore a trembling dove;

[015-579] The clotted feathers, scatter’d from above,

[015-580] Between the hero and the vessel pour

[015-581] Thick plumage mingled with a sanguine shower.

[015-582] The observing augur took the prince aside,

[015-583] Seized by the hand, and thus prophetic cried:

[015-584] “Yon bird, that dexter cuts the aërial road,

[015-585] Rose ominous, nor flies without a god:

[015-586] No race but thine shall Ithaca obey,

[015-587] To thine, for ages, Heaven decrees the sway.”

[015-588] “Succeed the omens, gods! (the youth rejoin’d:)

[015-589] Soon shall my bounties speak a grateful mind,

[015-590] And soon each envied happiness attend

[015-591] The man who calls Telemachus his friend.”
