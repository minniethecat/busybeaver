[018-001] BOOK XVIII.

[018-002] ARGUMENT.

[018-003] THE FIGHT OF ULYSSES AND IRUS.

[018-004] The beggar [[irus|Irus]] insults [[odysseus|Ulysses]]; the suitors promote the quarrel, in

[018-005] which Irus is worsted, and miserably handled. [[penelope|Penelope]] descends, and

[018-006] receives the presents of the suitors. The dialogue of Ulysses with

[018-007] [[eurymachus|Eurymachus]].

[018-008] While fix’d in thought the pensive hero sate,

[018-009] A mendicant approach’d the royal gate;

[018-010] A surly vagrant of the giant kind,

[018-011] The stain of manhood, of a coward mind:

[018-012] From feast to feast, insatiate to devour,

[018-013] He flew, attendant on the genial hour.

[018-014] Him on his mother’s knees, when babe he lay,

[018-015] She named Arnaeus on his natal day:

[018-016] But Irus his associates call’d the boy,

[018-017] Practised the common messenger to fly;

[018-018] Irus, a name expressive of the employ.

[018-019] From his own roof, with meditated blows,

[018-020] He strove to drive the man of mighty woes:

[018-021] “Hence, dotard! hence, and timely speed thy way,

[018-022] Lest dragg’d in vengeance thou repent thy stay;

[018-023] See how with nods assent yon princely train!

[018-024] But honouring age, in mercy I refrain:

[018-025] In peace away! lest, if persuasions fail,

[018-026] This arm with blows more eloquent prevail.”

[018-027] To whom, with stern regard: “O insolence,

[018-028] Indecently to rail without offence!

[018-029] What bounty gives without a rival share;

[018-030] I ask, what harms not thee, to breathe this air:

[018-031] Alike on alms we both precarious live:

[018-032] And canst thou envy when the great relieve?

[018-033] Know, from the bounteous heavens all riches flow,

[018-034] And what man gives, the gods by man bestow;

[018-035] Proud as thou art, henceforth no more be proud,

[018-036] Lest I imprint my vengeance in thy blood;

[018-037] Old as I am, should once my fury burn,

[018-038] How would’st thou fly, nor e’en in thought return!”

[018-039] “Mere woman-glutton! (thus the churl replied;)

[018-040] A tongue so flippant, with a throat so wide!

[018-041] Why cease I, gods! to dash those teeth away,

[018-042] Like some wild boar’s, that, greedy of his prey,

[018-043] Uproots the bearded corn? Rise, try the fight,

[018-044] Gird well thy loins, approach, and feel my might:

[018-045] Sure of defeat, before the peers engage:

[018-046] Unequal fight, when youth contends with age!”

[018-047] Thus in a wordy war their tongues display

[018-048] More fierce intents, preluding to the fray;

[018-049] [[antinous|Antinous]] hears, and in a jovial vein,

[018-050] Thus with loud laughter to the suitor train:

[018-051] “This happy day in mirth, my friends, employ,

[018-052] And lo! the gods conspire to crown our joy;

[018-053] See ready for the fight, and hand to hand,

[018-054] Yon surly mendicants contentious stand:

[018-055] Why urge we not to blows!” Well pleased they spring

[018-056] Swift from their seats, and thickening form a ring.

[018-057] To whom Antinous: “Lo! enrich’d with blood,

[018-058] A kid’s well-fatted entrails (tasteful food)

[018-059] On glowing embers lie; on him bestow

[018-060] The choicest portion who subdues his foe;

[018-061] Grant him unrivall’d in these walls to stay,

[018-062] The sole attendant on the genial day.”

[018-063] The lords applaud: Ulysses then with art,

[018-064] And fears well-feign’d, disguised his dauntless heart.

[018-065] “Worn as I am with age, decay’d with woe;

[018-066] Say, is it baseness to decline the foe?

[018-067] Hard conflict! when calamity and age

[018-068] With vigorous youth, unknown to cares, engage!

[018-069] Yet, fearful of disgrace, to try the day

[018-070] Imperious hunger bids, and I obey;

[018-071] But swear, impartial arbiters of right,

[018-072] Swear to stand neutral, while we cope in fight.”

[018-073] The peers assent: when straight his sacred head

[018-074] [[telemachus|Telemachus]] upraised, and sternly said:

[018-075] “Stranger, if prompted to chastise the wrong

[018-076] Of this bold insolent, confide, be strong!

[018-077] The injurious Greek that dares attempt a blow,

[018-078] That instant makes Telemachus his foe;

[018-079] And these my friends shall guard the sacred ties

[018-080] Of hospitality, for they are wise.”

[018-081] Then, girding his strong loins, the king prepares

[018-082] To close in combat, and his body bares;

[018-083] Broad spread his shoulders, and his nervous thighs

[018-084] By just degrees, like well-turn’d columns, rise

[018-085] Ample his chest, his arms are round and long,

[018-086] And each strong joint [[athena|Minerva]] knits more strong

[018-087] (Attendant on her chief): the suitor-crowd

[018-088] With wonder gaze, and gazing speak aloud:

[018-089] “Irus! alas! shall Irus be no more?

[018-090] Black fate impends, and this the avenging hour!

[018-091] Gods! how his nerves a matchless strength proclaim,

[018-092] Swell o’er his well-strong limbs, and brace his frame!”

[018-093] Then pale with fears, and sickening at the sight;

[018-094] They dragg’d the unwilling Irus to the fight;

[018-095] From his blank visage fled the coward blood,

[018-096] And his flesh trembled as aghast he stood.

[018-097] “O that such baseness should disgrace the light?

[018-098] O hide it, death, in everlasting night!

[018-099] (Exclaims Antinous;) can a vigorous foe

[018-100] Meanly decline to combat age and woe?

[018-101] But hear me wretch! if recreant in the fray

[018-102] That huge bulk yield this ill-contested day,

[018-103] Instant thou sail’st, to Eschetus resign’d;

[018-104] A tyrant, fiercest of the tyrant kind,

[018-105] Who casts thy mangled ears and nose a prey

[018-106] To hungry dogs, and lops the man away.”

[018-107] While with indignant scorn he sternly spoke,

[018-108] In every joint the trembling Irus shook.

[018-109] Now front to front each frowning champion stands,

[018-110] And poises high in air his adverse hands.

[018-111] The chief yet doubts, or to the shades below

[018-112] To fell the giant at one vengeful blow,

[018-113] Or save his life, and soon his life to save

[018-114] The king resolves, for mercy sways the brave

[018-115] That instant Irus his huge arm extends,

[018-116] Full on his shoulder the rude weight descends;

[018-117] The sage Ulysses, fearful to disclose

[018-118] The hero latent in the man of woes,

[018-119] Check’d half his might; yet rising to the stroke,

[018-120] His jawbone dash’d, the crashing jawbone broke:

[018-121] Down dropp’d he stupid from the stunning wound;

[018-122] His feet extended quivering, beat the ground;

[018-123] His mouth and nostrils spout a purple flood;

[018-124] His teeth, all shatter’d, rush inmix’d with blood.

[018-125] The peers transported, as outstretch’d he lies,

[018-126] With bursts of laughter rend the vaulted skies;

[018-127] Then dragg’d along, all bleeding from the wound,

[018-128] His length of carcase trailing prints the ground:

[018-129] Raised on his feet, again he reels, he falls,

[018-130] Till propp’d, reclining on the palace walls:

[018-131] Then to his hand a staff the victor gave,

[018-132] And thus with just reproach address’d the slave:

[018-133] “There terrible, affright with dogs, and reign

[018-134] A dreaded tyrant o’er the bestial train!

[018-135] But mercy to the poor and stranger show,

[018-136] Lest Heaven in vengeance send some mightier woe.”

[018-137] Scornful he spoke, and o’er his shoulder flung

[018-138] The broad-patch’d scrip in tatters hung

[018-139] Ill join’d, and knotted to a twisted thong.

[018-140] Then, turning short, disdain’d a further stay;

[018-141] But to the palace measured back the way.

[018-142] There, as he rested gathering in a ring,

[018-143] The peers with smiles address’d their unknown king:

[018-144] “Stranger, may [[zeus|Jove]] and all the aërial powers

[018-145] With every blessing crown thy happy hours!

[018-146] Our freedom to thy prowess’d arm we owe

[018-147] From bold intrusion of thy coward foe:

[018-148] Instant the flying sail the slave shall wing

[018-149] To Eschetus, the monster of a king.”

[018-150] While pleased he hears, Antinous bears the food,

[018-151] A kid’s well-fatted entrails, rich with blood;

[018-152] The bread from canisters of shining mould

[018-153] [[amphinomus|Amphinomus]]; and wines that laugh in gold:

[018-154] “And oh! (he mildly cries) may Heaven display

[018-155] A beam of glory o’er thy future day!

[018-156] Alas, the brave too oft is doom’d to bear

[018-157] The gripes of poverty and stings of care.”

[018-158] To whom with thought mature the king replies:

[018-159] “The tongue speaks wisely, when the soul is wise:

[018-160] Such was thy father! in imperial state,

[018-161] Great without vice, that oft attends the great;

[018-162] Nor from the sire art thou, the son, declin’d;

[018-163] Then hear my words, and grace them in thy mind!

[018-164] Of all that breathes, or grovelling creeps on earth,

[018-165] Most vain is man! calamitous by birth:

[018-166] To-day, with power elate, in strength he blooms;

[018-167] The haughty creature on that power presumes:

[018-168] Anon from Heaven a sad reverse he feels:

[018-169] Untaught to bear, ’gainst Heaven the wretch rebels.

[018-170] For man is changeful, as his bliss or woe!

[018-171] Too high when prosperous, when distress’d too low.

[018-172] There was a day, when with the scornful great

[018-173] I swell’d in pomp and arrogance of state;

[018-174] Proud of the power that to high birth belongs;

[018-175] And used that power to justify my wrongs.

[018-176] Then let not man be proud; but firm of mind,

[018-177] Bear the best humbly; and the worst resign’d;

[018-178] Be dumb when Heaven afflicts! unlike yon train

[018-179] Of haughty spoilers, insolently vain;

[018-180] Who make their queen and all her wealth a prey:

[018-181] But vengeance and Ulysses wing their way.

[018-182] O may’st thou, favour’d by some guardian power,

[018-183] Far, far be distant in that deathful hour!

[018-184] For sure I am, if stern Ulysses breathe,

[018-185] These lawless riots end in blood and death.”

[018-186] Then to the gods the rosy juice he pours,

[018-187] And the drain’d goblet to the chief restores.

[018-188] Stung to the soul, o’ercast with holy dread,

[018-189] He shook the graceful honours of his head;

[018-190] His boding mind the future woe forestalls,

[018-191] In vain! by great Telemachus he falls,

[018-192] For Pallas seals his doom: all sad he turns

[018-193] To join the peers; resumes his throne, and mourns.

[018-194] Meanwhile Minerva with instinctive fires

[018-195] Thy soul, Penelope, from Heaven inspires;

[018-196] With flattering hopes the suitors to betray,

[018-197] And seem to meet, yet fly, the bridal day:

[018-198] Thy husband’s wonder, and thy son’s to raise;

[018-199] And crown the mother and the wife with praise.

[018-200] Then, while the streaming sorrow dims her eyes,

[018-201] Thus, with a transient smile, the matron cries:

[018-202] “[[eurynome|Eurynome]]! to go where riot reigns

[018-203] I feel an impulse, though my soul disdains;

[018-204] To my loved son the snares of death to show,

[018-205] And in the traitor friend, unmask the foe;

[018-206] Who, smooth of tongue, in purpose insincere,

[018-207] Hides fraud in smiles, while death is ambush’d there.”

[018-208] “Go, warn thy son, nor be the warning vain

[018-209] (Replied the sagest of the royal train);

[018-210] But bathed, anointed, and adorn’d, descend;

[018-211] Powerful of charms, bid every grace attend;

[018-212] The tide of flowing tears awhile suppress;

[018-213] Tears but indulge the sorrow, not repress.

[018-214] Some joy remains: to thee a son is given,

[018-215] Such as, in fondness, parents ask of Heaven.”

[018-216] “Ah me! forbear!” returns the queen, “forbear,

[018-217] Oh! talk not, talk not of vain beauty’s care;

[018-218] No more I bathe, since he no longer sees

[018-219] Those charms, for whom alone I wish to please.

[018-220] The day that bore Ulysses from this coast

[018-221] Blasted the little bloom these cheeks could boast.

[018-222] But instant bid Autonoe descend,

[018-223] Instant Hippodame our steps attend;

[018-224] Ill suits it female virtue, to be seen

[018-225] Alone, indecent, in the walks of men.”

[018-226] Then while Eurynome the mandate bears,

[018-227] From heaven Minerva shoots with guardian cares;

[018-228] O’er all her senses, as the couch she press’d,

[018-229] She pours, a pleasing, deep and death-like rest,

[018-230] With every beauty every feature arms,

[018-231] Bids her cheeks glow, and lights up all her charms;

[018-232] In her love-darting eyes awakes the fires

[018-233] (Immortal gifts! to kindle soft desires);

[018-234] From limb to limb an air majestic sheds,

[018-235] And the pure ivory o’er her bosom spreads.

[018-236] Such Venus shines, when with a measured bound

[018-237] She smoothly gliding swims the harmonious round,

[018-238] When with the Graces in the dance she moves,

[018-239] And fires the gazing gods with ardent loves.

[018-240] Then to the skies her flight Minerva bends,

[018-241] And to the queen the damsel train descends;

[018-242] Waked at their steps, her flowing eyes unclose;

[018-243] The tears she wipes, and thus renews her woes:

[018-244] “Howe’er ’tis well that sleep awhile can free,

[018-245] With soft forgetfulness a wretch like me;

[018-246] Oh! were it given to yield this transient breath,

[018-247] Send, O Diana! send the sleep of death!

[018-248] Why must I waste a tedious life in tears,

[018-249] Nor bury in the silent grave my cares?

[018-250] O my Ulysses! ever honour’d name!

[018-251] For thee I mourn till death dissolves my frame.”

[018-252] Thus wailing, slow and sadly she descends,

[018-253] On either band a damsel train attends:

[018-254] Full where the dome its shining valves expands,

[018-255] Radiant before the gazing peers she stands;

[018-256] A veil translucent o’er her brow display’d,

[018-257] Her beauty seems, and only seems, to shade:

[018-258] Sudden she lightens in their dazzled eyes,

[018-259] And sudden flames in every bosom rise;

[018-260] They send their eager souls with every look.

[018-261] Till silence thus the imperial matron broke:

[018-262] “O why! my son, why now no more appears

[018-263] That warmth of soul that urged thy younger years?

[018-264] Thy riper days no growing worth impart,

[018-265] A man in stature, still a boy in heart!

[018-266] Thy well-knit frame unprofitably strong,

[018-267] Speaks thee a hero, from a hero sprung:

[018-268] But the just gods in vain those gifts bestow,

[018-269] O wise alone in form, and grave in show!

[018-270] Heavens! could a stranger feel oppression’s hand

[018-271] Beneath thy roof, and couldst thou tamely stand!

[018-272] If thou the stranger’s righteous cause decline

[018-273] His is the sufferance, but the shame is thine.”

[018-274] To whom, with filial awe, the prince returns:

[018-275] “That generous soul with just resentment burns;

[018-276] Yet, taught by time, my heart has learn’d to glow

[018-277] For others’ good, and melt at others’ woe;

[018-278] But, impotent those riots to repel,

[018-279] I bear their outrage, though my soul rebel;

[018-280] Helpless amid the snares of death I tread,

[018-281] And numbers leagued in impious union dread;

[018-282] But now no crime is theirs: this wrong proceeds

[018-283] From Irus, and the guilty Irus bleeds.

[018-284] Oh would to Jove! or her whose arms display

[018-285] The shield of Jove, or him who rules the day!

[018-286] That yon proud suitors, who licentious tread

[018-287] These courts, within these courts like Irus bled:

[018-288] Whose loose head tottering, as with wine oppress’d,

[018-289] Obliquely drops, and nodding knocks his breast;

[018-290] Powerless to move, his staggering feet deny

[018-291] The coward wretch the privilege to fly.”

[018-292] Then to the queen Eurymachus replies:

[018-293] “O justly loved, and not more fair than wise!

[018-294] Should Greece through all her hundred states survey

[018-295] Thy finish’d charms, all Greece would own thy sway

[018-296] In rival crowds contest the glorious prize.

[018-297] Dispeopling realms to gaze upon thy eyes:

[018-298] O woman! loveliest of the lovely kind,

[018-299] In body perfect, and complete in mind.”

[018-300] “Ah me! (returns the queen) when from this shore

[018-301] Ulysses sail’d, then beauty was no more!

[018-302] The gods decreed these eyes no more should keep

[018-303] Their wonted grace, but only serve to weep.

[018-304] Should he return, whate’er my beauties prove,

[018-305] My virtues last; my brightest charm is love.

[018-306] Now, grief, thou all art mine! the gods o’ercast

[018-307] My soul with woes, that long, ah long must last!

[018-308] Too faithfully my heart retains the day

[018-309] That sadly tore my royal lord away:

[018-310] He grasp’d my hand, and, ‘O, my spouse! I leave

[018-311] Thy arms (he cried), perhaps to find a grave:

[018-312] [[kleos|Fame]] speaks the Trojans bold; they boast the skill

[018-313] To give the feather’d arrow wings to kill,

[018-314] To dart the spear, and guide the rushing car

[018-315] With dreadful inroad through the walks of war.

[018-316] My sentence is gone forth, and ’tis decreed

[018-317] Perhaps by righteous Heaven that I must bleed!

[018-318] My father, mother, all I trust to three;

[018-319] To them, to them, transfer the love of me:

[018-320] But, when my son grows man, the royal sway

[018-321] Resign, and happy be thy bridal day!’

[018-322] Such were his words; and Hymen now prepares

[018-323] To light his torch, and give me up to cares;

[018-324] The afflictive hand of wrathful Jove to bear:

[018-325] A wretch the most complete that breathes the air!

[018-326] Fall’n e’en below the rights to woman due!

[018-327] Careless to please, with insolence ye woo!

[018-328] The generous lovers, studious to succeed,

[018-329] Bid their whole herds and flocks in banquets bleed;

[018-330] By precious gifts the vow sincere display:

[018-331] You, only you, make her ye love your prey.”

[018-332] Well-pleased Ulysses hears his queen deceive

[018-333] The suitor-train, and raise a thirst to give:

[018-334] False hopes she kindles, but those hopes betray,

[018-335] And promise, yet elude, the bridal day.

[018-336] While yet she speaks, the gay Antinous cries:

[018-337] “Offspring of kings, and more than woman wise!

[018-338] ’Tis right; ’tis man’s prerogative to give,

[018-339] And custom bids thee without shame receive;

[018-340] Yet never, never, from thy dome we move,

[018-341] Till Hymen lights the torch of spousal love.”

[018-342] The peers despatch’d their heralds to convey

[018-343] The gifts of love; with speed they take the way.

[018-344] A robe Antinous gives of shining dyes,

[018-345] The varying hues in gay confusion rise

[018-346] Rich from the artist’s hand! Twelve clasps of gold

[018-347] Close to the lessening waist the vest infold!

[018-348] Down from the swelling loins the vest unbound

[018-349] Floats in bright waves redundant o’er the ground,

[018-350] A bracelet rich with gold, with amber gay,

[018-351] That shot effulgence like the solar ray,

[018-352] Eurymachus presents: and ear-rings bright,

[018-353] With triple stars, that cast a trembling light.

[018-354] Pisander bears a necklace wrought with art:

[018-355] And every peer, expressive of his heart,

[018-356] A gift bestows: this done, the queen ascends,

[018-357] And slow behind her damsel train attends.

[018-358] Then to the dance they form the vocal strain,

[018-359] Till Hesperus leads forth the starry train;

[018-360] And now he raises, as the daylight fades,

[018-361] His golden circlet in the deepening shades:

[018-362] Three vases heap’d with copious fires display

[018-363] O’er all the palace a fictitious day;

[018-364] From space to space the torch wide-beaming burns,

[018-365] And sprightly damsels trim the rays by turns.

[018-366] To whom the king: “Ill suits your sex to stay

[018-367] Alone with men! ye modest maids, away!

[018-368] Go, with the queen; the spindle guide; or cull

[018-369] (The partners of her cares) the silver wool;

[018-370] Be it my task the torches to supply

[018-371] E’en till the morning lamp adorns the sky;

[018-372] E’en till the morning, with unwearied care,

[018-373] Sleepless I watch; for I have learn’d to bear.”

[018-374] Scornful they heard: [[melantho|Melantho]], fair and young,

[018-375] (Melantho, from the loins of [[dolius|Dolius]] sprung,

[018-376] Who with the queen her years an infant led,

[018-377] With the soft fondness of a daughter bred,)

[018-378] Chiefly derides: regardless of the cares

[018-379] Her queen endures, polluted joys she shares

[018-380] Nocturnal with Eurymachus: with eyes

[018-381] That speak disdain, the wanton thus replies:

[018-382] “Oh! whither wanders thy distemper’d brain,

[018-383] Thou bold intruder on a princely train?

[018-384] Hence, to the vagrants’ rendezvous repair;

[018-385] Or shun in some black forge the midnight air.

[018-386] Proceeds this boldness from a turn of soul,

[018-387] Or flows licentious from the copious bowl?

[018-388] Is it that vanquish’d Irus swells thy mind?

[018-389] A foe may meet thee of a braver kind,

[018-390] Who, shortening with a storm of blows thy stay,

[018-391] Shall send thee howling all in blood away!”

[018-434] These gates would seem too narrow for thy flight.”

[018-435] While yet he speaks, Eurymachus replies,

[018-436] With indignation flashing from his eyes:

[018-437] “Slave, I with justice might deserve the wrong,

[018-438] Should I not punish that opprobrious tongue.

[018-439] Irreverent to the great, and uncontroll’d,

[018-440] Art thou from wine, or innate folly, bold?

[018-441] Perhaps these outrages from Irus flow,

[018-442] A worthless triumph o’er a worthless foe!”

[018-443] He said, and with full force a footstool threw;

[018-444] Whirl’d from his arm, with erring rage it flew:

[018-445] Ulysses, cautious of the vengeful foe,

[018-446] Stoops to the ground, and disappoints the blow.

[018-447] Not so a youth, who deals the goblet round,

[018-448] Full on his shoulder it inflicts a wound;

[018-449] Dash’d from his hand the sounding goblet flies,

[018-450] He shrieks, he reels, he falls, and breathless lies.

[018-451] Then wild uproar and clamour mount the sky,

[018-452] Till mutual thus the peers indignant cry:

[018-453] “Oh had this stranger sunk to realms beneath,

[018-454] To the black realms of darkness and of death,

[018-455] Ere yet he trod these shores! to strife he draws

[018-456] Peer against peer; and what the weighty cause?

[018-457] A vagabond! for him the great destroy,

[018-458] In vile ignoble jars, the feast of joy.”

[018-459] To whom the stern Telemachus uprose;

[018-460] “Gods! what wild folly from the goblet flows!

[018-461] Whence this unguarded openness of soul,

[018-462] But from the license of the copious bowl?

[018-463] Or Heaven delusion sends: but hence away!

[018-464] Force I forbear, and without force obey.”

[018-465] Silent, abash’d, they hear the stern rebuke,

[018-466] Till thus Amphinomus the silence broke:

[018-467] “True are his words, and he whom truth offends,

[018-468] Not with Telemachus, but truth contends;

[018-469] Let not the hand of violence invade

[018-470] The reverend stranger, or the spotless maid;

[018-471] Retire we hence, but crown with rosy wine

[018-472] The flowing goblet to the powers divine!

[018-473] Guard he his guest beneath whose roof he stands:

[018-474] This justice, this the social rite demands.”

[018-475] The peers assent: the goblet Mulius crown’d

[018-476] With purple juice, and bore in order round:

[018-477] Each peer successive his libation pours

[018-478] To the blest gods who fill’d the ethereal bowers:

[018-479] Then swill’d with wine, with noise the crowds obey,

[018-480] And rushing forth, tumultuous reel away.
