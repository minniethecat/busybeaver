[007-001] BOOK VII.

[007-002] ARGUMENT.

[007-003] THE COURT OF ALCINOUS.

[007-004] The princess [[nausicaa|Nausicaa]] returns to the city and [[odysseus|Ulysses]] soon after

[007-005] follows thither. He is met by Pallas in the form of a young virgin, who

[007-006] guides him to the palace, and directs him in what manner to address the

[007-007] queen [[arete|Arete]]. She then involves him in a mist which causes him to pass

[007-008] invisible. The palace and gardens of [[alcinous|Alcinous]] described. Ulysses

[007-009] falling at the feet of the queen, the mist disperses, the Phæacians

[007-010] admire, and receive him with respect. The queen inquiring by what means

[007-011] he had the garments he then wore, be relates to her and Alcinous his

[007-012] departure from [[calypso|Calypso]], and his arrival in their dominions.

[007-013] The same day continues, and the book ends with the night.

[007-014] The patient heavenly man thus suppliant pray’d;

[007-015] While the slow mules draws on the imperial maid;

[007-016] Through the proud street she moves, the public gaze;

[007-017] The turning wheel before the palace stays.

[007-018] With ready love her brothers, gathering round,

[007-019] Received the vestures, and the mules unbound.

[007-020] She seeks the bridal bower: a matron there

[007-021] The rising fire supplies with busy care,

[007-022] Whose charms in youth her father’s heart inflamed,

[007-023] Now worn with age, Eurymedusa named;

[007-024] The captive dame Phæacian rovers bore,

[007-025] Snatch’d from Epirus, her sweet native shore

[007-026] (A grateful prize), and in her bloom bestow’d

[007-027] On good Alcinous, honor’d as a god;

[007-028] Nurse of Nausicaa from her infant years,

[007-029] And tender second to a mother’s cares.

[007-030] Now from the sacred thicket where he lay,

[007-031] To town Ulysses took the winding way.

[007-032] Propitious Pallas, to secure her care,

[007-033] Around him spread a veil of thicken’d air;

[007-034] To shun the encounter of the vulgar crowd,

[007-035] Insulting still, inquisitive and loud.

[007-036] When near the famed Phæacian walls he drew,

[007-037] The beauteous city opening to his view,

[007-038] His step a virgin met, and stood before:

[007-039] A polish’d urn the seeming virgin bore,

[007-040] And youthful smiled; but in the low disguise

[007-041] Lay hid the goddess with the azure eyes.

[007-042] “Show me, fair daughter (thus the chief demands),

[007-043] The house of him who rules these happy lands

[007-044] Through many woes and wanderings, do I come

[007-045] To good Alcinous’ hospitable dome.

[007-046] Far from my native coast, I rove alone,

[007-047] A wretched stranger, and of all unknown!”

[007-048] The goddess answer’d: “Father, I obey,

[007-049] And point the wandering traveller his way:

[007-050] Well known to me the palace you inquire,

[007-051] For fast beside it dwells my honour’d sire:

[007-052] But silent march, nor greet the common train

[007-053] With question needless, or inquiry vain;

[007-054] A race of ragged mariners are these,

[007-055] Unpolish’d men, and boisterous as their seas

[007-056] The native islanders alone their care,

[007-057] And hateful he who breathes a foreign air.

[007-058] These did the ruler of the deep ordain

[007-059] To build proud navies, and command the main;

[007-060] On canvas wings to cut the watery way;

[007-061] No bird so light, no thought so swift as they.”

[007-062] Thus having spoke, the unknown celestial leads:

[007-063] The footsteps of the deity he treads,

[007-064] And secret moves along the crowded space,

[007-065] Unseen of all the rude Phæacian race.

[007-066] (So Pallas order’d, Pallas to their eyes

[007-067] The mist objected, and condensed the skies.)

[007-068] The chief with wonder sees the extended streets,

[007-069] The spreading harbours, and the riding fleets;

[007-070] He next their princes’ lofty domes admires,

[007-071] In separate islands, crown’d with rising spires;

[007-072] And deep entrenchments, and high walls of stone.

[007-073] That gird the city like a marble zone.

[007-074] At length the kingly palace-gates he view’d;

[007-075] There stopp’d the goddess, and her speech renew’d;

[007-076] “My task is done: the mansion you inquire

[007-077] Appears before you: enter, and admire.

[007-078] High-throned, and feasting, there thou shalt behold

[007-079] The sceptred rulers. Fear not, but be bold:

[007-080] A decent boldness ever meets with friends,

[007-081] Succeeds, and even a stranger recommends

[007-082] First to the queen prefer a suppliant’s claim,

[007-083] Alcinous’ queen, Arete is her name.

[007-084] The same her parents, and her power the same.

[007-085] For know, from ocean’s god Nausithous sprung,

[007-086] And Peribæa, beautiful and young

[007-087] (Eurymedon’s last hope, who ruled of old

[007-088] The race of giants, impious, proud, and bold:

[007-089] Perish’d the nation in unrighteous war,

[007-090] Perish’d the prince, and left this only heir),

[007-091] Who now, by [[poseidon|Neptune]]’s amorous power compress’d,

[007-092] Produced a monarch that his people bless’d,

[007-093] Father and prince of the Phæacian name;

[007-094] From him Rhexenor and Alcinous came.

[007-095] The first by Phœbus’ hurtling arrows fired,

[007-096] New from his nuptials, hapless youth! expired.

[007-097] No son survived; Arete heir’d his state,

[007-098] And her, Alcinous chose his royal mate.

[007-099] With honours yet to womankind unknown.

[007-100] This queen he graces, and divides the throne;

[007-101] In equal tenderness her sons conspire,

[007-102] And all the children emulate their sire.

[007-103] When through the streets she gracious deigns to move

[007-104] (The public wonder and the public love),

[007-105] The tongues of all with transport sound her praise,

[007-106] The eyes of all, as on a goddess, gaze.

[007-107] She feels the triumph of a generous breast;

[007-108] To heal divisions, to relieve the oppress’d;

[007-109] In virtue rich; in blessing others, bless’d.

[007-110] (to then secure, thy humble suit prefer

[007-111] And owe thy country and thy friends to her.”

[007-112] With that the goddess deign’d no longer stay,

[007-113] But o’er the world of waters wing’d her way;

[007-114] Forsaking [[scheria|Scheria]]’s ever-pleasing shore,

[007-115] The winds to Marathon the virgin bore:

[007-116] Thence, where proud Athens rears her towery head,

[007-117] With opening streets and shining structures spread,

[007-118] She pass’d, delighted with the well-known seats;

[007-119] And to Erectheus’ sacred dome retreats.

[007-120] Meanwhile Ulysses at the palace waits,

[007-121] There stops, and anxious with his soul debates,

[007-122] Fix’d in amaze before the royal gates.

[007-123] The front appear’d with radiant splendours gay,

[007-124] Bright as the lamp of night, or orb of day,

[007-125] The walls were massy brass: the cornice high

[007-126] Blue metals crown’d in colours of the sky,

[007-127] Rich plates of gold the folding doors incase;

[007-128] The pillars silver, on a brazen base;

[007-129] Silver the lintels deep-projecting o’er,

[007-130] And gold the ringlets that command the door.

[007-131] Two rows of stately dogs, on either hand,

[007-132] In sculptured gold and labour’d silver stood

[007-133] These Vulcan form’d with art divine, to wait

[007-134] Immortal guardians at Alcinous’ gate;

[007-135] Alive each animated frame appears,

[007-136] And still to live beyond the power of years,

[007-137] Fair thrones within from space to space were raised,

[007-138] Where various carpets with embroidery blessed,

[007-139] The work of matrons: these the princes press’d,

[007-140] Day following day, a long-continued feast.

[007-141] Refulgent pedestals the walls surround,

[007-142] Which boys of gold with flaming torches crown’d;

[007-143] The polish’d ore, reflecting every ray,

[007-144] Blazed on the banquets with a double day.

[007-145] Full fifty handmaids form the household train;

[007-146] Some turn the mill, or sift the golden grain;

[007-147] Some ply the loom; their busy fingers move

[007-148] Like poplar-leaves when Zephyr fans the grove.

[007-149] Not more renown’d the men of Scheria’s isle

[007-150] For sailing arts and all the naval toil,

[007-151] Than works of female skill their women’s pride,

[007-152] The flying shuttle through the threads to guide:

[007-153] Pallas to these her double gifts imparts,

[007-154] Incentive genius, and industrious arts.

[007-155] Close to the gates a spacious garden lies,

[007-156] From storms defended and inclement skies.

[007-157] Four acres was the allotted space of ground,

[007-158] Fenced with a green enclosure all around.

[007-159] Tall thriving trees confess’d the fruitful mould:

[007-160] The reddening apple ripens here to gold.

[007-161] Here the blue fig with luscious juice o’erflows,

[007-162] With deeper red the full pomegranate glows;

[007-163] The branch here bends beneath the weighty pear,

[007-164] And verdant olives flourish round the year,

[007-165] The balmy spirit of the western gale

[007-166] Eternal breathes on fruits, unthought to fail:

[007-167] Each dropping pear a following pear supplies,

[007-168] On apples apples, figs on figs arise:

[007-169] The same mild season gives the blooms to blow,

[007-170] The buds to harden, and the fruits to grow.

[007-171] Here order’d vines in equal ranks appear,

[007-172] With all the united labours of the year;

[007-173] Some to unload the fertile branches run,

[007-174] Some dry the blackening clusters in the sun,

[007-175] Others to tread the liquid harvest join:

[007-176] The groaning presses foam with floods of wine

[007-177] Here are the vines in early flower descried,

[007-178] Here grapes discolour’d on the sunnyside,

[007-179] And there in autumn’s richest purple dyed,

[007-180] Beds of all various herbs, for ever green,

[007-181] In beauteous order terminate the scene.

[007-182] Two plenteous fountains the whole prospect crown’d

[007-183] This through the gardens leads its streams around

[007-184] Visits each plant, and waters all the ground;

[007-185] While that in pipes beneath the palace flows,

[007-186] And thence its current on the town bestows:

[007-187] To various use their various streams they bring,

[007-188] The people one, and one supplies the king.

[007-189] Such were the glories which the gods ordain’d,

[007-190] To grace Alcinous, and his happy land.

[007-191] E’en from the chief whom men and nations knew,

[007-192] The unwonted scene surprise and rapture drew;

[007-193] In pleasing thought he ran the prospect o’er,

[007-194] Then hasty enter’d at the lofty door.

[007-195] Night now approaching, in the palace stand,

[007-196] With goblets crown’d, the rulers of the land;

[007-197] Prepared for rest, and offering to the god

[007-198] Who bears the virtue of the sleepy rod,

[007-199] Unseen he glided through the joyous crowd,

[007-200] With darkness circled, and an ambient cloud.

[007-201] Direct to great Alcinous’ throne he came,

[007-202] And prostrate fell before the imperial dame.

[007-203] Then from around him dropp’d the veil of night;

[007-204] Sudden he shines, and manifest to sight.

[007-205] The nobles gaze, with awful fear oppress’d;

[007-206] Silent they gaze, and eye the godlike guest.

[007-207] “Daughter of great Rhexenor! (thus began,

[007-208] Low at her knees, the much-enduring man)

[007-209] To thee, thy consort, and this royal train,

[007-210] To all that share the blessings of your reign,

[007-211] A suppliant bends: oh pity human woe!

[007-212] ’Tis what the happy to the unhappy owe.

[007-213] A wretched exile to his country send,

[007-214] Long worn with griefs, and long without a friend

[007-215] So may the gods your better days increase,

[007-216] And all your joys descend on all your race;

[007-217] So reign for ever on your country’s breast,

[007-218] Your people blessing, by your people bless’d!”

[007-219] Then to the genial hearth he bow’d his face,

[007-220] And humbled in the ashes took his place.

[007-221] Silence ensued. The eldest first began,

[007-222] Echeneus sage, a venerable man!

[007-223] Whose well-taught mind the present age surpass’d,

[007-224] And join’d to that the experience of the last.

[007-225] Fit words attended on his weighty sense,

[007-226] And mild persuasion flow’d in eloquence.

[007-227] “Oh sight (he cried) dishonest and unjust!

[007-228] A guest, a stranger, seated in the dust!

[007-229] To raise the lowly suppliant from the ground

[007-230] Befits a monarch. Lo! the peers around

[007-231] But wait thy word, the gentle guest to grace,

[007-232] And seat him fair in some distinguish’d place.

[007-233] Let first the herald due libation pay

[007-234] To [[zeus|Jove]], who guides the wanderer on his way:

[007-235] Then set the genial banquet in his view,

[007-236] And give the stranger-guest a stranger’s due.”

[007-237] His sage advice the listening king obeys,

[007-238] He stretch’d his hand the prudent chief to raise,

[007-239] And from his seat [[laodamas|Laodamas]] removed

[007-240] (The monarch’s offspring, and his best-beloved);

[007-241] There next his side the godlike hero sate;

[007-242] With stars of silver shone the bed of state.

[007-243] The golden ewer a beauteous handmaid brings,

[007-244] Replenish’d from the cool translucent springs,

[007-245] Whose polish’d vase with copious streams supplies

[007-246] A silver layer of capacious size.

[007-247] The table next in regal order spread,

[007-248] The glittering canisters are heap’d with bread:

[007-249] Viands of various kinds invite the taste,

[007-250] Of choicest sort and savour, rich repast!

[007-251] Thus feasting high, Alcinous gave the sign,

[007-252] And bade the herald pour the rosy wine;

[007-253] “Let all around the due libation pay

[007-254] To Jove, who guides the wanderer on his way.”

## Section 21

[007-255] He said. Pontonous heard the king’s command;

[007-256] The circling goblet moves from hand to hand;

[007-257] Each drinks the juice that glads the heart of man.

[007-258] Alcinous then, with aspect mild, began:

[007-259] “Princes and peers, attend; while we impart

[007-260] To you the thoughts of no inhuman heart.

[007-261] Now pleased and satiate from the social rite

[007-262] Repair we to the blessings of the night;

[007-263] But with the rising day, assembled here,

[007-264] Let all the elders of the land appear,

[007-265] Pious observe our hospitable laws,

[007-266] And Heaven propitiate in the stranger’s cause;

[007-267] Then join’d in council, proper means explore

[007-268] Safe to transport him to the wished-for shore

[007-269] (How distant that, imports us not to know,

[007-270] Nor weigh the labour, but relieve the woe).

[007-271] Meantime, nor harm nor anguish let him bear

[007-272] This interval, Heaven trusts him to our care

[007-273] But to his native land our charge resign’d,

[007-274] Heaven’s is his life to come, and all the woes behind.

[007-275] Then must he suffer what the Fates ordain;

[007-276] For [[moira|Fate]] has wove the thread of life with pain!

[007-277] And twins, e’en from the birth, are Misery and Man!

[007-278] But if, descended from the Olympian bower,

[007-279] Gracious approach us some immortal power;

[007-280] If in that form thou comest a guest divine:

[007-281] Some high event the conscious gods design.

[007-282] As yet, unbid they never graced our feast,

[007-283] The solemn sacrifice call’d down the guest;

[007-284] Then manifest of Heaven the vision stood,

[007-285] And to our eyes familiar was the god.

[007-286] Oft with some favour’d traveller they stray,

[007-287] And shine before him all the desert way;

[007-288] With social intercourse, and face to face,

[007-289] The friends and guardians of our pious race.

[007-290] So near approach we their celestial kind,

[007-291] By justice, truth, and probity of mind;

[007-292] As our dire neighbours of Cyclopean birth

[007-293] Match in fierce wrong the giant-sons of earth.”

[007-294] “Let no such thought (with modest grace rejoin’d

[007-295] The prudent Greek) possess the royal mind.

[007-296] Alas! a mortal, like thyself, am I;

[007-297] No glorious native of yon azure sky:

[007-298] In form, ah how unlike their heavenly kind!

[007-299] How more inferior in the gifts of mind!

[007-300] Alas, a mortal! most oppress’d of those

[007-301] Whom Fate has loaded with a weight of woes;

[007-302] By a sad train of Miseries alone

[007-303] Distinguish’d long, and second now to none!

[007-304] By Heaven’s high will compell’d from shore to shore;

[007-305] With Heaven’s high will prepared to suffer more.

[007-306] What histories of toil could I declare!

[007-307] But still long-wearied nature wants repair;

[007-308] Spent with fatigue, and shrunk with pining fast,

[007-309] My craving bowels still require repast.

[007-310] Howe’er the noble, suffering mind may grieve

[007-311] Its load of anguish, and disdain to live,

[007-312] Necessity demands our daily bread;

[007-313] Hunger is insolent, and will be fed.

[007-314] But finish, oh ye peers! what you propose,

[007-315] And let the morrow’s dawn conclude my woes.

[007-316] Pleased will I suffer all the gods ordain,

[007-317] To see my soil, my son, my friends again.

[007-318] That view vouchsafed, let instant death surprise

[007-319] With ever-during shade these happy eyes!”

[007-320] The assembled peers with general praise approved

[007-321] His pleaded reason, and the suit he moved.

[007-322] Each drinks a full oblivion of his cares,

[007-323] And to the gifts of balmy sleep repairs,

[007-324] Ulysses in the regal walls alone

[007-325] Remain’d: beside him, on a splendid throne,

[007-326] Divine Arete and Alcinous shone.

[007-327] The queen, on nearer view, the guest survey’d,

[007-328] Rob’d in the garments her own hands had made,

[007-329] Not without wonder seen. Then thus began,

[007-330] Her words addressing to the godlike man:

[007-331] “Camest thou hither, wondrous stranger! say,

[007-332] From lands remote and o’er a length of sea?

[007-333] Tell, then, whence art thou? whence, that princely air?

[007-334] And robes like these, so recent and so fair?”

[007-335] “Hard is the task, O princess! you impose

[007-336] (Thus sighing spoke the man of many woes),

[007-337] The long, the mournful series to relate

[007-338] Of all my sorrows sent by Heaven and Fate!

[007-339] Yet what you ask, attend. An island lies

[007-340] Beyond these tracts, and under other skies,

[007-341] [[calypsos-island|Ogygia]] named, in [[oceanus|Ocean]]’s watery arms;

[007-342] Where dwells Calypso, dreadful in her charms!

[007-343] Remote from gods or men she holds her reign,

[007-344] Amid the terrors of a rolling main.

[007-345] Me, only me, the hand of fortune bore,

[007-346] Unblest! to tread that interdicted shore:

[007-347] When Jove tremendous in the sable deeps

[007-348] Launch’d his red lightning at our scattered ships;

[007-349] Then, all my fleet and all my followers lost.

[007-350] Sole on a plank on boiling surges toss’d,

[007-351] Heaven drove my wreck the Ogygian Isle to find,

[007-352] Full nine days floating to the wave and wind.

[007-353] Met by the goddess there with open arms,

[007-354] She bribed my stay with more than human charms;

[007-355] Nay, promised, vainly promised, to bestow

[007-356] Immortal life, exempt from age and woe;

[007-357] But all her blandishments successless prove,

[007-358] To banish from my breast my country’s love.

[007-359] I stay reluctant seven continued years,

[007-360] And water her ambrosial couch with tears,

[007-361] The eighth she voluntary moves to part,

[007-362] Or urged by Jove, or her own changeful heart.

[007-363] A raft was formed to cross the surging sea;

[007-364] Herself supplied the stores and rich array,

[007-365] And gave the gales to waft me on my way,

[007-366] In seventeen days appear’d your pleasing coast,

[007-367] And woody mountains half in vapours lost.

[007-368] Joy touched my soul; my soul was joy’d in vain,

[007-369] For angry Neptune roused the raging main;

[007-370] The wild winds whistle, and the billows roar;

[007-371] The splitting raft the furious tempest tore;

[007-372] And storms vindictive intercept the shore.

[007-373] Soon as their rage subsides, the seas I brave

[007-374] With naked force, and shoot along the wave,

[007-375] To reach this isle; but there my hopes were lost,

[007-376] The surge impell’d me on a craggy coast.

[007-377] I chose the safer sea, and chanced to find

[007-378] A river’s mouth impervious to the wind,

[007-379] And clear of rocks. I fainted by the flood;

[007-380] Then took the shelter of the neighbouring wood.

[007-381] ’Twas night, and, covered in the foliage deep,

[007-382] Jove plunged my senses in the death of sleep.

[007-383] All night I slept, oblivious of my pain:

[007-384] Aurora dawned and Phœbus shined in vain,

[007-385] Nor, till oblique he sloped his evening ray,

[007-386] Had Somnus dried the balmy dews away.

[007-387] Then female voices from the shore I heard:

[007-388] A maid amidst them, goddess-like appear’d;

[007-389] To her I sued, she pitied my distress;

[007-390] Like thee in beauty, nor in virtue less.

[007-391] Who from such youth could hope considerate care?

[007-392] In youth and beauty wisdom is but rare!

[007-393] She gave me life, relieved with just supplies

[007-394] My wants, and lent these robes that strike your eyes.

[007-395] This is the truth: and oh, ye powers on high!

[007-396] Forbid that want should sink me to a lie.”

[007-397] To this the king: “Our daughter but express’d

[007-398] Her cares imperfect to our godlike guest.

[007-399] Suppliant to her, since first he chose to pray,

[007-400] Why not herself did she conduct the way,

[007-401] And with her handmaids to our court convey?”

[007-402] “Hero and king (Ulysses thus replied)

[007-403] Nor blame her faultless nor suspect of pride:

[007-404] She bade me follow in the attendant train;

[007-405] But fear and reverence did my steps detain,

[007-406] Lest rash suspicion might alarm thy mind:

[007-407] Man’s of a jealous and mistaken kind.”

[007-408] “Far from my soul (he cried) the gods efface

[007-409] All wrath ill-grounded, and suspicion base!

[007-410] Whate’er is honest, stranger, I approve,

[007-411] And would to Phœbus, Pallas, and to Jove,

[007-412] Such as thou art, thy thought and mine were one,

[007-413] Nor thou unwilling to be called my son.

[007-414] In such alliance couldst thou wish to join,

[007-415] A palace stored with treasures should be thine.

[007-416] But if reluctant, who shall force thy stay?

[007-417] Jove bids to set the stranger on his way,

[007-420] The wakeful mariners shall watch the skies,

[007-423] Where thy soul rests, and labour is no more.

[007-424] Far as Euboea though thy country lay,

[007-425] Our ships with ease transport thee in a day.

[007-426] Thither of old, earth’s giant son to view,

[007-435] But thus to heaven (and heavenward lifts his eyes):

[007-436] “O Jove! O father! what the king accords

[007-437] Do thou make perfect! sacred be his words!

[007-438] Wide o’er the world Alcinous’ glory shine!

[007-439] Let fame be his, and ah! my country mine!”

[007-441] Ordains the fleecy couch, and covering vest;

[007-442] Bids her fair train the purple quilts prepare,

[007-443] And the thick carpets spread with busy care.

[007-446] Then gave the signal to the willing guest:

[007-447] He rose with pleasure, and retired to rest.

[007-448] There, soft extended, to the murmuring sound

[007-449] Of the high porch, Ulysses sleeps profound!

[007-450] Within, released from cares, Alcinous lies;

[007-451] And fast beside were closed Arete’s eyes.
