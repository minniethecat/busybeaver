[011-001] BOOK XI.

[011-002] ARGUMENT.

[011-003] THE DESCENT INTO HELL.

[011-004] [[odysseus|Ulysses]] continues his narration. How he arrived at the land of the

[011-005] Cimmerians, and what ceremonies he performed to invoke the dead. The

[011-006] manner of his descent, and the apparition of the shades: his

[011-007] conversation with [[elpenor|Elpenor]], and with [[tiresias|Tiresias]], who informs him in a

[011-008] prophetic manner of his fortunes to come. He meets his mother [[anticlea|Anticlea]],

[011-009] from whom he learns the state of his family. He sees the shades of the

[011-010] ancient heroines, afterwards of the heroes, and converses in particular

[011-011] with [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]] and [[achilles|Achilles]]. [[ajax|Ajax]] keeps at a sullen distance, and

[011-012] disdains to answer him. He then beholds Tityus, Tantalus, Sisyphus,

[011-013] Hercules; till he is deterred from further curiosity by the apparition

[011-014] of horrid spectres, and the cries of the wicked in torments.

[011-015] “Now to the shores we bend, a mournful train,

[011-016] Climb the tall bark, and launch into the main;

[011-017] At once the mast we rear, at once unbind

[011-018] The spacious sheet, and stretch it to the wind;

[011-019] Then pale and pensive stand, with cares oppress’d,

[011-020] And solemn horror saddens every breast.

[011-021] A freshening breeze the magic power supplied,

[011-022] While the wing’d vessel flew along the tide;

[011-023] Our oars we shipp’d; all day the swelling sails

[011-024] Full from the guiding pilot catch’d the gales.

[011-025] “Now sunk the sun from his aërial height,

[011-026] And o’er the shaded billows rush’d the night;

[011-027] When lo! we reach’d old [[oceanus|Ocean]]’s utmost bounds,

[011-028] Where rocks control his waves with ever-during mounds.

[011-029] “There in a lonely land, and gloomy cells,

[011-030] The dusky nation of Cimmeria dwells;

[011-031] The sun ne’er views the uncomfortable seats,

[011-032] When radiant he advances, or retreats:

[011-033] Unhappy race! whom endless night invades,

[011-034] Clouds the dull air, and wraps them round in shades.

[011-035] “The ship we moor on these obscure abodes;

[011-036] Disbark the sheep, an offering to the gods;

[011-037] And, hellward bending, o’er the beach descry

[011-038] The doleful passage to the infernal sky.

[011-039] The victims, vow’d to each Tartarian power,

[011-040] [[eurylochus|Eurylochus]] and [[perimedes|Perimedes]] bore.

[011-041] “Here open’d hell, all hell I here implored,

[011-042] And from the scabbard drew the shining sword:

[011-043] And trenching the black earth on every side,

[011-044] A cavern form’d, a cubit long and wide.

[011-045] New wine, with honey-temper’d milk, we bring,

[011-046] Then living waters from the crystal spring:

[011-047] O’er these was strew’d the consecrated flour,

[011-048] And on the surface shone the holy store.

[011-049] “Now the wan shades we hail, the infernal gods,

[011-050] To speed our course, and waft us o’er the floods:

[011-051] So shall a barren heifer from the stall

[011-052] Beneath the knife upon your altars fall;

[011-053] So in our palace, at our safe return,

[011-054] Rich with unnumber’d gifts the pile shall burn;

[011-055] So shall a ram, the largest of the breed,

[011-056] Black as these regions, to Tiresias bleed.

[011-057] “Thus solemn rites and holy vows we paid

[011-058] To all the phantom-nations of the dead;

[011-059] Then died the sheep: a purple torrent flow’d,

[011-060] And all the caverns smoked with streaming blood.

[011-061] When lo! appear’d along the dusky coasts,

[011-062] Thin, airy shoals of visionary ghosts:

[011-063] Fair, pensive youths, and soft enamour’d maids;

[011-064] And wither’d elders, pale and wrinkled shades;

[011-065] Ghastly with wounds the forms of warriors slain

[011-066] Stalk’d with majestic port, a martial train:

[011-067] These and a thousand more swarm’d o’er the ground,

[011-068] And all the dire assembly shriek’d around.

[011-069] Astonish’d at the sight, aghast I stood,

[011-070] And a cold fear ran shivering through my blood;

[011-071] Straight I command the sacrifice to haste,

[011-072] Straight the flay’d victims to the flames are cast,

[011-073] And mutter’d vows, and mystic song applied

[011-074] To grisly Pluto, and his gloomy bride.

[011-075] “Now swift I waved my falchion o’er the blood;

[011-076] Back started the pale throngs, and trembling stood,

[011-077] Round the black trench the gore untasted flows,

[011-078] Till awful from the shades Tiresias rose.

[011-079] “There wandering through the gloom I first survey’d,

[011-080] New to the realms of death, Elpenor’s shade:

[011-081] His cold remains all naked to the sky

[011-082] On distant shores unwept, unburied lie.

[011-083] Sad at the sight I stand, deep fix’d in woe,

[011-084] And ere I spoke the tears began to flow.

[011-085] “‘O say what angry power Elpenor led

[011-086] To glide in shades, and wander with the dead?

[011-087] How could thy soul, by realms and seas disjoin’d,

[011-088] Outfly the nimble sail, and leave the lagging wind?

[011-089] “The ghost replied: ‘To hell my doom I owe,

[011-090] Demons accursed, dire ministers of woe!

[011-091] My feet, through wine unfaithful to their weight,

[011-092] Betray’d me tumbling from a towery height:

[011-093] Staggering I reel’d, and as I reel’d I fell,

[011-094] Lux’d the neck-joint—my soul descends to hell.

[011-095] But lend me aid, I now conjure thee lend,

[011-096] By the soft tie and sacred name of friend!

[011-097] By thy fond consort! by thy father’s cares!

[011-098] By loved [[telemachus|Telemachus]]’ blooming years?

[011-099] For well I know that soon the heavenly powers

[011-100] Will give thee back to-day, and [[circe|Circe]]’s shores:

[011-101] There pious on my cold remains attend,

[011-102] There call to mind thy poor departed friend.

[011-103] The tribute of a tear is all I crave,

[011-104] And the possession of a peaceful grave.

[011-105] But if, unheard, in vain compassion plead,

[011-106] Revere the gods. The gods avenge the dead!

[011-107] A tomb along the watery margin raise,

[011-108] The tomb with manly arms and trophies grace,

[011-109] To show posterity Elpenor was.

[011-110] There high in air, memorial of my name,

[011-111] Fix the smooth oar, and bid me live to fame.’

[011-112] “To whom with tears: ‘These rites, O mournful shade,

[011-113] Due to thy ghost, shall to thy ghost be paid.’

[011-114] “Still as I spoke the phantom seem’d to moan,

[011-115] Tear follow’d tear, and groan succeeded groan.

[011-116] But, as my waving sword the blood surrounds,

[011-117] The shade withdrew, and mutter’d empty sounds.

[011-118] “There as the wondrous visions I survey’d,

[011-119] All pale ascends my royal mother’s shade:

[011-120] A queen, to [[troy|Troy]] she saw our legions pass;

[011-121] Now a thin form is all Anticlea was!

[011-122] Struck at the sight I melt with filial woe,

[011-123] And down my cheek the pious sorrows flow,

[011-124] Yet as I shook my falchion o’er the blood,

[011-125] Regardless of her son the parent stood.

[011-126] “When lo! the mighty Theban I behold,

[011-127] To guide his steps he bore a staff of gold;

[011-128] Awful he trod; majestic was his look!

[011-129] And from his holy lips these accents broke:

[011-130] “‘Why, mortal, wanderest thou from cheerful day,

[011-131] To tread the downward, melancholy way?

[011-132] What angry gods to these dark regions led

[011-133] Thee, yet alive, companion of the dead?

[011-134] But sheathe thy poniard, while my tongue relates

[011-135] Heaven’s steadfast purpose, and thy future fates.’

[011-136] “While yet he spoke, the prophet I obey’d,

[011-137] And in the scabbard plunged the glittering blade:

[011-138] Eager he quaff’d the gore, and then express’d

[011-139] Dark things to come, the counsels of his breast.

## Section 30

[011-140] “Weary of light, Ulysses here explores

[011-141] A prosperous voyage to his native shores;

[011-142] But know—by me unerring Fates disclose

[011-143] New trains of dangers, and new scenes of woes.

[011-144] I see, I see, thy bark by [[poseidon|Neptune]] toss’d,

[011-145] For injured Cyclops, and his eyeball lost!

[011-146] Yet to thy woes the gods decree an end,

[011-147] If Heaven thou please: and how to please attend

[011-148] Where on Trinacrian rocks the ocean roars,

[011-149] Graze numerous herds along the verdant shores;

[011-150] Though hunger press, yet fly the dangerous prey,

[011-151] The herds are sacred to the god of day,

[011-152] Who all surveys with his extensive eye,

[011-153] Above, below, on earth, and in the sky!

[011-154] Rob not the god; and so propitious gales

[011-155] Attend thy voyage, and impel thy sails:

[011-156] But, if his herds ye seize, beneath the waves

[011-157] I see thy friends o’erwhelm’d in liquid graves!

[011-158] The direful wreck Ulysses scarce survives!

[011-159] Ulysses at his country scarce arrives!

[011-160] Strangers thy guides! nor there thy labours end;

[011-161] New foes arise; domestic ills attend!

[011-162] There foul adulterers to thy bride resort,

[011-163] And lordly gluttons riot in thy court.

[011-164] But vengeance hastes amain! These eyes behold

[011-165] The deathful scene, princes on princes roll’d!

[011-166] That done, a people far from sea explore,

[011-167] Who ne’er knew salt, or heard the billows roar,

[011-168] Or saw gay vessel stem the watery plain,

[011-169] A painted wonder flying on the main!

[011-170] Bear on thy back an oar: with strange amaze

[011-171] A shepherd meeting thee, the oar surveys,

[011-172] And names a van: there fix it on the plain,

[011-173] To calm the god that holds the watery reign;

[011-174] A threefold offering to his altar bring,

[011-175] A bull, a ram, a boar; and hail the ocean king.

[011-176] But home return’d, to each ethereal power

[011-177] Slay the due victim in the genial hour:

[011-178] So peaceful shalt thou end thy blissful days,

[011-179] And steal thyself from life by slow decays:

[011-180] Unknown to pain, in age resign thy breath,

[011-181] When late stern Neptune points the shaft with death:

[011-182] To the dark grave retiring as to rest,

[011-183] Thy people blessing, by thy people bless’d!

[011-184] “Unerring truths, O man, my lips relate;

[011-185] This is thy life to come, and this is fate.’

[011-186] “To whom unmoved: ‘If this the gods prepare,

[011-187] What Heaven ordains the wise with courage bear.

[011-188] But say, why yonder on the lonely strands,

[011-189] Unmindful of her son, Anticlea stands?

[011-190] Why to the ground she bends her downcast eye?

[011-191] Why is she silent, while her son is nigh?

[011-192] The latent cause, O sacred seer, reveal!’

[011-193] “‘Nor this (replies the seer) will I conceal.

[011-194] Know, to the spectres that thy beverage taste,

[011-195] The scenes of life recur, and actions past:

[011-196] They, seal’d with truth, return the sure reply;

[011-197] The rest, repell’d, a train oblivious fly.’

[011-198] “The phantom-prophet ceased, and sunk from sight,

[011-199] To the black palace of eternal night.

[011-200] “Still in the dark abodes of death I stood,

[011-201] When near Anticlea moved, and drank the blood.

[011-202] Straight all the mother in her soul awakes,

[011-203] And, owning her Ulysses, thus she speaks;

[011-204] ‘Comest thou, my son, alive, to realms beneath,

[011-205] The dolesome realms of darkness and of death!

[011-206] Comest thou alive from pure, ethereal day?

[011-207] Dire is the region, dismal is the way!

[011-208] Here lakes profound, there floods oppose their waves,

[011-209] There the wide sea with all his billows raves!

[011-210] Or (since to dust proud Troy submits her towers)

[011-211] Comest thou a wanderer from the Phrygian shores?

[011-212] Or say, since honour call’d thee to the field,

[011-213] Hast thou thy [[ithaca|Ithaca]], thy bride, beheld?’

[011-214] “‘Source of my life,’ I cried, ‘from earth I fly

[011-215] To seek Tiresias in the nether sky,

[011-216] To learn my doom; for, toss’d from woe to woe,

[011-217] In every land Ulysses finds a foe:

[011-218] Nor have these eyes beheld my native shores,

[011-219] Since in the dust proud Troy submits her towers.

[011-220] “‘But, when thy soul from her sweet mansion fled,

[011-221] Say, what distemper gave thee to the dead?

[011-222] Has life’s fair lamp declined by slow decays,

[011-223] Or swift expired it in a sudden blaze?

[011-224] Say, if my sire, good old [[laertes|Laertes]], lives?

[011-225] If yet Telemachus, my son, survives?

[011-226] Say, by his rule is my dominion awed,

[011-227] Or crush’d by traitors with an iron rod?

[011-228] Say, if my spouse maintains her royal trust;

[011-229] Though tempted, chaste, and obstinately just?

[011-230] Or if no more her absent lord she wails,

[011-231] But the false woman o’er the wife prevails?’

[011-232] “Thus I, and thus the parent-shade returns:

[011-233] ‘Thee, ever thee, thy faithful consort mourns:

[011-234] Whether the night descends or day prevails,

[011-235] Thee she by night, and thee by day bewails.

[011-236] Thee in Telemachus thy realm obeys;

[011-237] In sacred groves celestial rites he pays,

[011-238] And shares the banquet in superior state,

[011-239] Graced with such honours as become the great

[011-240] Thy sire in solitude foments his care:

[011-241] The court is joyless, for thou art not there!

[011-242] No costly carpets raise his hoary head,

[011-243] No rich embroidery shines to grace his bed;

[011-244] Even when keen winter freezes in the skies,

[011-245] Rank’d with his slaves, on earth the monarch lies:

[011-246] Deep are his sighs, his visage pale, his dress

[011-247] The garb of woe and habit of distress.

[011-248] And when the autumn takes his annual round,

[011-249] The leafy honours scattering on the ground,

[011-250] Regardless of his years, abroad he lies,

[011-251] His bed the leaves, his canopy the skies.

[011-252] Thus cares on cares his painful days consume,

[011-253] And bow his age with sorrow to the tomb!

[011-254] “‘For thee, my son, I wept my life away;

[011-255] For thee through hell’s eternal dungeons stray:

[011-256] Nor came my fate by lingering pains and slow,

[011-257] Nor bent the silver-shafted queen her bow;

[011-258] No dire disease bereaved me of my breath;

[011-259] Thou, thou, my son, wert my disease and death;

[011-260] Unkindly with my love my son conspired,

[011-261] For thee I lived, for absent thee expired.’

[011-262] “Thrice in my arms I strove her shade to bind,

[011-263] Thrice through my arms she slipp’d like empty wind,

[011-264] Or dreams, the vain illusions of the mind.

[011-265] Wild with despair, I shed a copious tide

[011-266] Of flowing tears, and thus with sighs replied:

[011-267] “‘Fliest thou, loved shade, while I thus fondly mourn!

[011-268] Turn to my arms, to my embraces turn!

[011-269] Is it, ye powers that smile at human harms!

[011-270] Too great a bliss to weep within her arms?

[011-271] Or has hell’s queen an empty image sent,

[011-272] That wretched I might e’en my joys lament?’

[011-273] “‘O son of woe,’ the pensive shade rejoin’d;

[011-274] ‘O most inured to grief of all mankind!

[011-275] ‘Tis not the queen of hell who thee deceives;

[011-276] All, all are such, when life the body leaves:

[011-277] No more the substance of the man remains,

[011-278] Nor bounds the blood along the purple veins:

[011-279] These the funereal flames in atoms bear,

[011-280] To wander with the wind in empty air:

[011-281] While the impassive soul reluctant flies,

[011-282] Like a vain dream, to these infernal skies.

[011-283] But from the dark dominions speed the way,

[011-284] And climb the steep ascent to upper day:

[011-285] To thy chaste bride the wondrous story tell,

[011-286] The woes, the horrors, and the laws of hell.’

[011-287] “Thus while she spoke, in swarms hell’s empress brings

[011-288] Daughters and wives of heroes and of kings;

[011-289] Thick and more thick they gather round the blood,

[011-290] Ghost thronged on ghost (a dire assembly) stood!

[011-291] Dauntless my sword I seize: the airy crew,

[011-292] Swift as it flash’d along the gloom, withdrew;

[011-293] Then shade to shade in mutual forms succeeds,

[011-294] Her race recounts, and their illustrious deeds.

[011-295] “Tyro began, whom great Salmoneus bred;

[011-296] The royal partner of famed Cretheus’ bed.

[011-297] For fair Enipeus, as from fruitful urns

[011-298] He pours his watery store, the virgin burns;

[011-299] Smooth flows the gentle stream with wanton pride,

[011-300] And in soft mazes rolls a silver tide.

[011-301] As on his banks the maid enamour’d roves,

[011-302] The monarch of the deep beholds and loves;

[011-303] In her Enipeus’ form and borrow’d charms

[011-304] The amorous god descends into her arms:

[011-305] Around, a spacious arch of waves he throws,

[011-306] And high in air the liquid mountain rose;

[011-307] Thus in surrounding floods conceal’d, he proves

[011-308] The pleasing transport, and completes his loves.

[011-309] Then, softly sighing, he the fair address’d,

[011-310] And as he spoke her tender hand he press’d.

[011-311] ‘Hail, happy nymph! no vulgar births are owed

[011-312] To the prolific raptures of a god:

[011-313] Lo! when nine times the moon renews her horn,

[011-314] Two brother heroes shall from thee be born;

[011-315] Thy early care the future worthies claim,

[011-316] To point them to the arduous paths of fame;

[011-317] But in thy breast the important truth conceal,

[011-318] Nor dare the secret of a god reveal:

[011-319] For know, thou Neptune view’st! and at my nod

[011-320] Earth trembles, and the waves confess their god.’

[011-321] “He added not, but mounting spurn’d the plain,

[011-322] Then plunged into the chambers of the main,

[011-323] “Now in the time’s full process forth she brings

[011-324] [[zeus|Jove]]’s dread vicegerents in two future kings;

[011-325] O’er proud Iolcos Pelias stretch’d his reign,

[011-326] And godlike Neleus ruled the Pylian plain:

[011-327] Then, fruitful, to her Cretheus’ royal bed

[011-328] She gallant Pheres and famed Aeson bred;

[011-329] From the same fountain Amythaon rose,

[011-330] Pleased with the din of scar; and noble shout of foes.

[011-331] “There moved Antiope, with haughty charms,

[011-332] Who bless’d the almighty Thunderer in her arms:

[011-333] Hence sprung Amphion, hence brave Zethus came,

[011-334] Founders of Thebes, and men of mighty name;

[011-335] Though bold in open field, they yet surround

[011-336] The town with walls, and mound inject on mound;

[011-337] Here ramparts stood, there towers rose high in air,

[011-338] And here through seven wide portals rush’d the war.

[011-339] “There with soft step the fair Alcmena trod,

[011-340] Who bore Alcides to the thundering god:

[011-341] And Megara, who charm’d the son of Jove,

[011-342] And soften’d his stern soul to tender love.

[011-343] “Sullen and sour, with discontented mien,

[011-344] Jocasta frown’d, the incestuous Theban queen;

[011-345] With her own son she join’d in nuptial bands,

[011-346] Though father’s blood imbrued his murderous hands

[011-347] The gods and men the dire offence detest,

[011-348] The gods with all their furies rend his breast;

[011-349] In lofty Thebes he wore the imperial crown,

[011-350] A pompous wretch! accursed upon a throne.

[011-351] The wife self-murder’d from a beam depends,

[011-352] And her foul soul to blackest hell descends;

[011-353] Thence to her son the choicest plagues she brings,

[011-354] And the fiends haunt him with a thousand stings.

[011-355] “And now the beauteous Chloris I descry,

[011-356] A lovely shade, Amphion’s youngest joy!

[011-357] With gifts unnumber’d Neleus sought her arms,

[011-358] Nor paid too dearly for unequall’d charms;

[011-359] Great in Orchomenos, in [[pylos|Pylos]] great,

[011-360] He sway’d the sceptre with imperial state.

[011-361] Three gallant sons the joyful monarch told,

[011-362] Sage [[nestor|Nestor]], Periclimenus the bold,

[011-363] And Chromius last; but of the softer race,

[011-364] One nymph alone, a myracle of grace.

[011-365] Kings on their thrones for lovely Pero burn;

[011-366] The sire denies, and kings rejected mourn.

[011-367] To him alone the beauteous prize he yields,

[011-368] Whose arm should ravish from Phylacian fields

[011-369] The herds of Iphyclus, detain’d in wrong;

[011-370] Wild, furious herds, unconquerably strong!

[011-371] This dares a seer, but nought the seer prevails,

[011-372] In beauty’s cause illustriously he fails;

[011-373] Twelve moons the foe the captive youth detains

[011-374] In painful dungeons, and coercive chains;

[011-375] The foe at last from durance where he lay,

[011-376] His heart revering, give him back to day;

[011-377] Won by prophetic knowledge, to fulfil

[011-378] The steadfast purpose of the Almighty will.

[011-379] “With graceful port advancing now I spied,

[011-380] Leda the fair, the godlike Tyndar’s bride:

[011-381] Hence Pollux sprung, who wields the furious sway

[011-382] The deathful gauntlet, matchless in the fray;

[011-383] And Castor, glorious on the embattled plain,

[011-384] Curbs the proud steeds, reluctant to the rein:

[011-385] By turns they visit this ethereal sky,

[011-386] And live alternate, and alternate die:

[011-387] In hell beneath, on earth, in heaven above,

[011-388] Reign the twin-gods, the favourite sons of Jove.

[011-389] “There Ephimedia trod the gloomy plain,

[011-390] Who charm’d the monarch of the boundless main:

[011-391] Hence Ephialtes, hence stern Otus sprung,

[011-392] More fierce than giants, more than giants strong;

[011-393] The earth o’erburden’d groan’d beneath their weight,

[011-394] None but Orion e’er surpassed their height:

[011-395] The wondrous youths had scarce nine winters told,

[011-396] When high in air, tremendous to behold,

[011-397] Nine ells aloft they rear’d their towering head,

[011-398] And full nine cubits broad their shoulders spread.

[011-399] Proud of their strength, and more than mortal size,

[011-400] The gods they challenge, and affect the skies:

[011-401] Heaved on [[olympus|Olympus]] tottering Ossa stood;

[011-402] On Ossa, Pelion nods with all his wood.

[011-403] Such were they youths! had they to manhood grown

[011-404] Almighty Jove had trembled on his throne,

[011-405] But ere the harvest of the beard began

[011-406] To bristle on the chin, and promise man,

[011-407] His shafts [[apollo|Apollo]] aim’d; at once they sound,

[011-408] And stretch the giant monsters o’er the ground.

## Section 31

[011-409] “There mournful Phaedra with sad Procris moves,

[011-410] Both beauteous shades, both hapless in their loves;

[011-411] And near them walk’d with solemn pace and slow,

[011-412] Sad Adriadne, partner of their woe:

[011-413] The royal Minos Ariadne bred,

[011-414] She Theseus loved, from Crete with Theseus fled:

[011-415] Swift to the Dian isle the hero flies,

[011-416] And towards his Athens bears the lovely prize;

[011-417] There Bacchus with fierce rage Diana fires,

[011-418] The goddess aims her shaft, the nymph expires.

[011-419] “There Clymene and Mera I behold,

[011-420] There Eriphyle weeps, who loosely sold

[011-421] Her lord, her honour, for the lust of gold.

[011-422] But should I all recount, the night would fail,

[011-423] Unequal to the melancholy tale:
