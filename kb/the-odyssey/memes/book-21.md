[021-001] BOOK XXI.

[021-002] ARGUMENT.

[021-003] THE BENDING OF ULYSSES’ BOW.

[021-004] [[penelope|Penelope]], to put an end to the solicitation of the suitors, proposes to

[021-005] marry the person who shall first bend the bow of [[odysseus|Ulysses]], and shoot

[021-006] through the ringlets. After their attempts have proved ineffectual,

[021-007] Ulysses, taking [[eumaeus|Eumaeus]] and Philaetius apart, discovers himself to

[021-008] them; then returning, desires leave to try his strength at the bow,

[021-009] which, though refused with indignation by the suitors, Penelope and

[021-010] [[telemachus|Telemachus]] cause it to be delivered to his hands. He bends it

[021-011] immediately, and shoots through all the rings. [[zeus|Jupiter]] at the same

[021-012] instant thunders from heaven; Ulysses accepts the omen, and gives a

[021-013] sign to Telemachus, who stands ready armed at his side.

[021-014] And Pallas now, to raise the rivals’ fires,

[021-015] With her own art Penelope inspires

[021-016] Who now can bend Ulysses’ bow, and wing

[021-017] The well-aim’d arrow through the distant ring,

[021-018] Shall end the strife, and win the imperial dame:

[021-019] But discord and black death await the game!

[021-020] The prudent queen the lofty stair ascends:

[021-021] At distance due a virgin-train attends;

[021-022] A brazen key she held, the handle turn’d,

[021-023] With steel and polish’d elephant adorn’d:

[021-024] Swift to the inmost room she bent her way,

[021-025] Where, safe reposed, the royal treasures lay:

[021-026] There shone high heap’d the labour’d brass and ore,

[021-027] And there the bow which great Ulysses bore;

[021-028] And there the quiver, where now guiltless slept

[021-029] Those winged deaths that many a matron wept.

[021-030] This gift, long since when [[sparta|Sparta]]’s shore he trod,

[021-031] On young Ulysses Iphitus bestowed:

[021-032] Beneath Orsilochus’ roof they met;

[021-033] One loss was private, one a public debt;

[021-034] Messena’s state from [[ithaca|Ithaca]] detains

[021-035] Three hundred sheep, and all the shepherd swains;

[021-036] And to the youthful prince to urge the laws,

[021-037] The king and elders trust their common cause.

[021-038] But Iphitus, employed on other cares,

[021-039] Search’d the wide country for his wandering mares,

[021-040] And mules, the strongest of the labouring kind;

[021-041] Hapless to search; more hapless still to find!

[021-042] For journeying on to Hercules, at length

[021-043] That lawless wretch, that man of brutal strength,

[021-044] Deaf to Heaven’s voice, the social rites transgress’d;

[021-045] And for the beauteous mares destroy’d his guest.

[021-046] He gave the bow; and on Ulysses’ part

[021-047] Received a pointed sword, and missile dart:

[021-048] Of luckless friendship on a foreign shore

[021-049] Their first, last pledges! for they met no more.

[021-050] The bow, bequeath’d by this unhappy hand,

[021-051] Ulysses bore not from his native land;

[021-052] Nor in the front of battle taught to bend,

[021-053] But kept in dear memorial of his friend.

[021-054] Now gently winding up the fair ascent,

[021-055] By many an easy step the matron went;

[021-056] Then o’er the pavement glides with grace divine

[021-057] (With polish’d oak the level pavements shine);

[021-058] The folding gates a dazzling light display’d,

[021-059] With pomp of various architrave o’erlaid.

[021-060] The bolt, obedient to the silken string,

[021-061] Forsakes the staple as she pulls the ring;

[021-062] The wards respondent to the key turn round;

[021-063] The bars fall back; the flying valves resound;

[021-064] Loud as a bull makes hill and valley ring,

[021-065] So roar’d the lock when it released the spring.

[021-066] She moves majestic through the wealthy room,

[021-067] Where treasured garments cast a rich perfume;

[021-068] There from the column where aloft it hung,

[021-069] Reach’d in its splendid case, the bow unstrung;

[021-070] Across her knees she laid the well-known bow,

[021-071] And pensive sate, and tears began to flow.

[021-072] To full satiety of grief she mourns,

[021-073] Then silent to the joyous hall returns,

[021-074] To the proud suitors bears in pensive state

[021-075] The unbended bow, and arrows winged with fate.

[021-076] Behind, her train the polish’d coffer brings,

[021-077] Which held the alternate brass and silver rings.

[021-078] Full in the portal the chaste queen appears,

[021-079] And with her veil conceals the coming tears:

[021-080] On either side awaits a virgin fair;

[021-081] While thus the matron, with majestic air:

[021-082] “Say you, when these forbidden walls inclose,

[021-083] For whom my victims bleed, my vintage flows:

[021-084] If these neglected, faded charms can move?

[021-085] Or is it but a vain pretence, you love?

[021-086] If I the prize, if me you seek to wife,

[021-087] Hear the conditions, and commence the strife.

[021-088] Who first Ulysses’ wondrous bow shall bend,

[021-089] And through twelve ringlets the fleet arrow send;

[021-090] Him will I follow, and forsake my home,

[021-091] For him forsake this loved, this wealthy dome,

[021-092] Long, long the scene of all my past delight,

[021-093] And still to last, the vision of my night!”

[021-094] Graceful she said, and bade Eumaeus show

[021-095] The rival peers the ringlets and the bow.

[021-096] From his full eyes the tears unbidden spring,

[021-097] Touch’d at the dear memorials of his king.

[021-098] Philaetius too relents, but secret shed

[021-099] The tender drops. [[antinous|Antinous]] saw, and said:

[021-100] “Hence to your fields, ye rustics! hence away,

[021-101] Nor stain with grief the pleasures of the day;

[021-102] Nor to the royal heart recall in vain

[021-103] The sad remembrance of a perish’d man.

[021-104] Enough her precious tears already flow—

[021-105] Or share the feast with due respect; or go

[021-106] To weep abroad, and leave to us the bow,

[021-107] No vulgar task! Ill suits this courtly crew

[021-108] That stubborn horn which brave Ulysses drew.

[021-109] I well remember (for I gazed him o’er

[021-110] While yet a child), what majesty he bore!

[021-111] And still (all infant as I was) retain

[021-112] The port, the strength, the grandeur of the man.”

## Section 52

[021-113] He said, but in his soul fond joys arise,

[021-114] And his proud hopes already win the prize.

[021-115] To speed the flying shaft through every ring,

[021-116] Wretch! is not thine: the arrows of the king

[021-117] Shall end those hopes, and fate is on the wing!

[021-118] Then thus Telemachus: “Some god I find

[021-119] With pleasing frenzy has possess’d my mind;

[021-120] When a loved mother threatens to depart,

[021-121] Why with this ill-timed gladness leaps my heart?

[021-122] Come then, ye suitors! and dispute a prize

[021-123] Richer than all the Achaian state supplies,

[021-124] Than all proud Argos, or Mycaena knows,

[021-125] Than all our isles or continents inclose;

[021-126] A woman matchless, and almost divine,

[021-127] Fit for the praise of every tongue but mine.

[021-128] No more excuses then, no more delay;

[021-129] Haste to the trial—Lo! I lead the way.

[021-130] “I too may try, and if this arm can wing

[021-131] The feather’d arrow through the destined ring,

[021-132] Then if no happier night the conquest boast,

[021-133] I shall not sorrow for a mother lost;

[021-134] But, bless’d in her, possess those arms alone,

[021-135] Heir of my father’s strength, as well as throne.”

[021-136] He spoke; then rising, his broad sword unbound,

[021-137] And cast his purple garment on the ground.

[021-138] A trench he open’d: in a line he placed.

[021-139] The level axes, and the points made fast

[021-140] (His perfect skill the wondering gazers eyed,

[021-141] The game as yet unseen, as yet untried).

[021-142] Then, with a manly pace, he took his stand:

[021-143] And grasp’d the bow, and twang’d it in his hand.

[021-144] Three times, with beating heart, he made essay:

[021-145] Three times, unequal to the task, gave way;

[021-146] A modest boldness on his cheek appear’d:

[021-147] And thrice he hoped, and thrice again he fear’d.

[021-148] The fourth had drawn it. The great sire with joy

[021-149] Beheld, but with a sign forbade the boy.

[021-150] His ardour straight the obedient prince suppress’d,

[021-151] And, artful, thus the suitor-train address’d:

[021-152] “O lay the cause on youth yet immature!

[021-153] (For heaven forbid such weakness should endure!)

[021-154] How shall this arm, unequal to the bow,

[021-155] Retort an insult, or repel a foe?

[021-156] But you! whom Heaven with better nerves has bless’d,

[021-157] Accept the trial, and the prize contest.”

[021-158] He cast the bow before him, and apart

[021-159] Against the polish’d quiver propp’d the dart.

[021-160] Resuming then his seat, Eupithes’ son,

[021-161] The bold Antinous, to the rest begun:

[021-162] “From where the goblet first begins to flow,

[021-163] From right to left in order take the bow;

[021-164] And prove your several strengths.” The princes heard

[021-165] And first Leiodes, blameless priest, appear’d:

[021-166] The eldest born of Œnops’ noble race,

[021-167] Who next the goblet held his holy place:

[021-168] He, only he, of all the suitor throng,

[021-169] Their deeds detested, and abjured the wrong.

[021-170] With tender hands the stubborn horn he strains,

[021-171] The stubborn horn resisted all his pains!

[021-172] Already in despair he gives it o’er:

[021-173] “Take it who will (he cries), I strive no more,

[021-174] What numerous deaths attend this fatal bow!

[021-175] What souls and spirits shall it send below!

[021-176] Better, indeed, to die, and fairly give

[021-177] Nature her debt, than disappointed live,

[021-178] With each new sun to some new hope a prey,

[021-179] Yet still to-morrow falser than to-day.

[021-180] How long in vain Penelope we sought!

[021-181] This bow shall ease us of that idle thought,

[021-182] And send us with some humbler wife to live,

[021-183] Whom gold shall gain, or destiny shall give.”

[021-184] Thus speaking, on the floor the bow he placed

[021-185] (With rich inlay the various floor was graced):

[021-186] At distance far the feather’d shaft he throws,

[021-187] And to the seat returns from whence he rose.

[021-188] To him Antinous thus with fury said:

[021-189] “What words ill-omen’d from thy lips have fled?

[021-190] Thy coward-function ever is in fear!

[021-191] Those arms are dreadful which thou canst not bear,

[021-192] Why should this bow be fatal to the brave?

[021-193] Because the priest is born a peaceful slave.

[021-194] Mark then what others can.” He ended there,

[021-195] And bade [[melanthius|Melanthius]] a vast pile prepare;

[021-196] He gives it instant flame, then fast beside

[021-197] Spreads o’er an ample board a bullock’s hide.

[021-198] With melted lard they soak the weapon o’er,

[021-199] Chafe every knot, and supple every pore.

[021-200] Vain all their art, and all their strength as vain;

[021-201] The bow inflexible resists their pain.

[021-202] The force of [[eurymachus|great Eurymachus]] alone

[021-203] And bold Antinous, yet untired, unknown:

[021-204] Those only now remain’d; but those confess’d

[021-205] Of all the train the mightiest and the best.

[021-206] Then from the hall, and from the noisy crew,

[021-207] The masters of the herd and flock withdrew.

[021-208] The king observes them, he the hall forsakes,

[021-209] And, past the limits of the court, o’ertakes.

[021-210] Then thus with accent mild Ulysses spoke:

[021-211] “Ye faithful guardians of the herd and flock!

[021-212] Shall I the secret of my breast conceal,

[021-213] Or (as my soul now dictates) shall I tell?

[021-214] Say, should some favouring god restore again

[021-215] The lost Ulysses to his native reign,

[021-216] How beat your hearts? what aid would you afford

[021-217] To the proud suitors, or your ancient lord?”

[021-218] Philaetius thus: “O were thy word not vain!

[021-219] Would mighty Jove restore that man again!

[021-220] These aged sinews, with new vigour strung,

[021-221] In his blest cause should emulate the young.”

[021-222] With equal vows Eumaeus too implored

[021-223] Each power above, with wishes for his lord.

[021-224] He saw their secret souls, and thus began:

[021-225] “Those vows the gods accord; behold the man!

[021-226] Your own Ulysses! twice ten years detain’d

[021-227] By woes and wanderings from this hapless land:

[021-228] At length he comes; but comes despised, unknown,

[021-229] And finding faithful you, and you alone.

[021-230] All else have cast him from their very thought,

[021-231] E’en in their wishes and their prayers forgot!

[021-232] Hear then, my friends: If Jove this arm succeed,

[021-233] And give yon impious revellers to bleed,

[021-234] My care shall be to bless your future lives

[021-235] With large possessions and with faithful wives;

[021-236] Fast by my palace shall your domes ascend,

[021-237] And each on young Telemachus attend,

[021-238] And each be call’d his brother and my friend.

[021-239] To give you firmer faith, now trust your eye;

[021-240] Lo! the broad scar indented on my thigh,

[021-241] When with Autolycus’ sons, of yore,

[021-242] On Parnass’ top I chased the tusky boar.”

[021-243] His ragged vest then drawn aside disclosed

[021-244] The sign conspicuous, and the scar exposed:

[021-245] Eager they view’d, with joy they stood amazed

[021-246] With tearful eyes o’er all their master gazed:

[021-247] Around his neck their longing arms they cast,

[021-248] His head, his shoulders, and his knees embraced;

[021-249] Tears followed tears; no word was in their power;

[021-250] In solemn silence fell the kindly shower.

[021-251] The king too weeps, the king too grasps their hands;

[021-252] And moveless, as a marble fountain, stands.

[021-253] Thus had their joy wept down the setting sun,

[021-254] But first the wise man ceased, and thus begun:

[021-255] “Enough—on other cares your thought employ,

[021-256] For danger waits on all untimely joy.

[021-257] Full many foes and fierce, observe us near;

[021-258] Some may betray, and yonder walls may hear.

[021-259] Re-enter then, not all at once, but stay

[021-260] Some moments you, and let me lead the way.

[021-261] To me, neglected as I am I know

[021-262] The haughty suitors will deny the bow;

[021-263] But thou, Eumaeus, as ’tis borne away,

[021-264] Thy master’s weapon to his hand convey.

[021-265] At every portal let some matron wait,

[021-266] And each lock fast the well-compacted gate:

[021-267] Close let them keep, whate’er invades their ear;

[021-268] Though arms, or shouts, or dying groans they hear.

[021-269] To thy strict charge, Philaetius, we consign

[021-270] The court’s main gate: to guard that pass be thine.”

[021-271] This said, he first return’d; the faithful swains

[021-272] At distance follow, as their king ordains.

[021-273] Before the flame Eurymachus now stands,

[021-274] And turns the bow, and chafes it with his hands

[021-275] Still the tough bow unmoved. The lofty man

[021-276] Sigh’d from his mighty soul, and thus began:

[021-277] “I mourn the common cause: for, oh, my friends,

[021-278] On me, on all, what grief, what shame attends!

[021-279] Not the lost nuptials can affect me more

[021-280] (For Greece has beauteous dames on every shore),

[021-281] But baffled thus! confess’d so far below

[021-282] Ulysses’ strength, as not to bend his bow!

[021-283] How shall all ages our attempt deride!

[021-284] Our weakness scorn!” Antinous thus replied:

[021-285] “Not so, Eurymachus: that no man draws

[021-286] The wondrous bow, attend another cause.

[021-287] Sacred to Phœbus is the solemn day,

[021-288] Which thoughtless we in games would waste away:

[021-289] Till the next dawn this ill-timed strife forego,

[021-290] And here leave fixed the ringlets in a row.

[021-291] Now bid the sewer approach, and let us join

[021-292] In due libations, and in rites divine,

[021-293] So end our night: before the day shall spring,

[021-294] The choicest offerings let Melanthius bring:

[021-295] Let then to Phœbus’ name the fatted thighs

[021-296] Feed the rich smokes high curling to the skies.

[021-297] So shall the patron of these arts bestow

[021-298] (For his the gift) the skill to bend the bow.”

[021-299] They heard well pleased: the ready heralds bring

[021-300] The cleansing waters from the limpid spring:

[021-301] The goblet high with rosy wine they crown’d,

[021-302] In order circling to the peers around.

[021-303] That rite complete, uprose the thoughtful man,

[021-304] And thus his meditated scheme began:

[021-305] “If what I ask your noble minds approve,

[021-306] Ye peers and rivals in the royal love!

[021-307] Chief, if it hurt not great Antinous’ ear

[021-308] (Whose sage decision I with wonder hear),

[021-309] And if Eurymachus the motion please:

[021-310] Give Heaven this day and rest the bow in peace.

[021-311] To-morrow let your arms dispute the prize,

[021-312] And take it he, the favour’d of the skies!

[021-313] But, since till then this trial you delay,

[021-314] [[pistis|Trust]] it one moment to my hands to-day:

[021-315] Fain would I prove, before your judging eyes,

[021-316] What once I was, whom wretched you despise:

[021-317] If yet this arm its ancient force retain;

[021-318] Or if my woes (a long-continued train)

[021-319] And wants and insults, make me less than man.”

[021-320] Rage flash’d in lightning from the suitors’ eyes,

[021-321] Yet mixed with terror at the bold emprise.

[021-322] Antinous then: “O miserable guest!

[021-323] Is common sense quite banish’d from thy breast?

[021-324] Sufficed it not, within the palace placed,

[021-325] To sit distinguish’d, with our presence graced,

[021-326] Admitted here with princes to confer,

[021-327] A man unknown, a needy wanderer?

[021-328] To copious wine this insolence we owe,

[021-329] And much thy betters wine can overthrow:

[021-330] The great Eurytion, when this frenzy stung,

[021-331] Pirithous’ roofs with frantic riot rung;

[021-332] Boundless the Centaur raged; till one and all

[021-333] The heroes rose, and dragg’d him from the hall;

[021-334] His nose they shorten’d, and his ears they slit,

[021-335] And sent him sober’d home, with better wit.

[021-336] Hence with long war the double race was cursed,

[021-337] Fatal to all, but to the aggressor first.

[021-338] Such fate I prophesy our guest attends,

[021-339] If here this interdicted bow he bends:

[021-340] Nor shall these walls such insolence contain:

[021-341] The first fair wind transports him o’er the main,

[021-342] Where Echetus to death the guilty brings

[021-343] (The worst of mortals, e’en the worst of kings).

[021-344] Better than that, if thou approve our cheer;

[021-345] Cease the mad strife and share our bounty here.”

[021-346] To this the queen her just dislike express’d:

[021-347] “‘Tis impious, prince, to harm the stranger-guest,

[021-348] Base to insult who bears a suppliant’s name,

[021-349] And some respect Telemachus may claim.

[021-350] What if the immortals on the man bestow

[021-351] Sufficient strength to draw the mighty bow?

[021-352] Shall I, a queen, by rival chiefs adored,

[021-353] Accept a wandering stranger for my lord?

[021-354] A hope so idle never touch’d his brain:

[021-355] Then ease your bosoms of a fear so vain.

[021-356] Far be he banish’d from this stately scene

[021-357] Who wrongs his princess with a thought so mean.”

[021-358] “O fair! and wisest of so fair a kind!

[021-359] (Respectful thus Eurymachus rejoin’d,)

[021-360] Moved by no weak surmise, but sense of shame,

[021-362] We dread the censure of the meanest slave,

[021-363] The weakest woman: all can wrong the brave.

[021-365] Of that brave chief whose bow they could not bend!

[021-366] In came a beggar of the strolling crew,

[021-367] And did what all those princes could not do.’

[021-368] Thus will the common voice our deed defame,

[021-369] And thus posterity upbraid our name.”

[021-440] And now his well-known bow the master bore,

[021-441] Turn’d on all sides, and view’d it o’er and o’er;

[021-442] Lest time or worms had done the weapon wrong,

[021-443] Its owner absent, and untried so long.

[021-444] While some deriding—“How he turns the bow!

[021-445] Some other like it sure the man must know,

[021-446] Or else would copy; or in bows he deals;

[021-447] Perhaps he makes them, or perhaps he steals.”

[021-448] “Heaven to this wretch (another cried) be kind!

[021-449] And bless, in all to which he stands inclined.

[021-450] With such good fortune as he now shall find.”

[021-451] Heedless he heard them: but disdain’d reply;

[021-452] The bow perusing with exactest eye.

[021-453] Then, as some heavenly minstrel, taught to sing

[021-454] High notes responsive to the trembling string,

[021-455] To some new strain when he adapts the lyre,

[021-456] Or the dumb lute refits with vocal wire,

[021-457] Relaxes, strains, and draws them to and fro;

[021-458] So the great master drew the mighty bow,

[021-459] And drew with ease. One hand aloft display’d

[021-460] The bending horns, and one the string essay’d.

[021-461] From his essaying hand the string, let fly,

[021-462] Twang’d short and sharp like the shrill swallow’s cry.

[021-463] A general horror ran through all the race,

[021-464] Sunk was each heart, and pale was every face,

[021-465] Signs from above ensued: the unfolding sky

[021-466] In lightning burst; Jove thunder’d from on high.

[021-467] Fired at the call of heaven’s almighty Lord,

[021-468] He snatch’d the shaft that glitter’d on the board

[021-469] (Fast by, the rest lay sleeping in the sheath,

[021-470] But soon to fly the messengers of death).

[021-471] Now sitting as he was, the cord he drew,

[021-472] Through every ringlet levelling his view:

[021-473] Then notch’d the shaft, released, and gave it wing;

[021-474] The whizzing arrow vanished from the string,

[021-475] Sung on direct, and threaded every ring.

[021-476] The solid gate its fury scarcely bounds;

[021-477] Pierced through and through the solid gate resounds,

[021-478] Then to the prince: “Nor have I wrought thee shame;

[021-479] Nor err’d this hand unfaithful to its aim;

[021-480] Nor prov’d the toil too hard; nor have I lost

[021-481] That ancient vigour, once my pride and boast.

[021-482] Ill I deserved these haughty peers’ disdain;

[021-483] Now let them comfort their dejected train,

[021-484] In sweet repast their present hour employ,

[021-485] Nor wait till evening for the genial joy:

[021-486] Then to the lute’s soft voice prolong the night;

[021-487] Music, the banquet’s most refined delight.”

[021-488] He said, then gave a nod; and at the word

[021-489] Telemachus girds on his shining sword.

[021-490] Fast by his father’s side he takes his stand:

[021-491] The beamy javelin lightens in his hand.
