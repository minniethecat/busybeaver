[008-001] BOOK VIII.

[008-002] [[alcinous|Alcinous]] calls a council, in which it is resolved to transport [[odysseus|Ulysses]]

[008-003] into his country. After which splendid entertainments are made, where

[008-004] the celebrated musician and poet, [[demodocus|Demodocus]], plays and sings to the

[008-005] guests. They next proceed to the games, the race, the wrestling,

[008-006] discus, &c., where Ulysses casts a prodigious length, to the admiration

[008-007] of all the spectators. They return again to the banquet and Demodocus

[008-008] sings the loves of Mars and Venus. Ulysses, after a compliment to the

[008-009] poet, desires him to sing the introduction of the wooden horse into

[008-010] [[troy|Troy]], which subject provoking his tears, Alcinous inquires of his guest

[008-011] his name, parentage, and fortunes.

[008-012] Now fair Aurora lifts her golden ray,

[008-013] And all the ruddy orient flames with day:

[008-014] Alcinous, and the chief, with dawning light,

[008-015] Rose instant from the slumbers of the night;

[008-016] Then to the council-seat they bend their way,

[008-017] And fill the shining thrones along the bay.

[008-018] Meanwhile [[athena|Minerva]], in her guardian care,

[008-019] Shoots from the starry vault through fields of air;

[008-020] In form, a herald of the king, she flies

[008-021] From peer to peer, and thus incessant cries;

[008-022] “Nobles and chiefs who rule Phæacia’s states,

[008-023] The king in council your attendance waits;

[008-024] A prince of grace divine your aid implores,

[008-025] O’er unknown seas arrived from unknown shores.”

[008-026] She spoke, and sudden with tumultuous sounds

[008-027] Of thronging multitudes the shore rebounds:

[008-028] At once the seats they fill; and every eye

[008-029] Glazed, as before some brother of the sky.

[008-030] Pallas with grace divine his form improves,

[008-031] More high he treads, and more enlarged he moves:

[008-032] She sheds celestial bloom, regard to draw;

[008-033] And gives a dignity of mien, to awe;

[008-034] With strength, the future prize of fame to play,

[008-035] And gather all the honours of the day.

[008-036] Then from his glittering throne Alcinous rose;

[008-037] “Attend (he cried) while we our will disclose.

[008-038] Your present aid this godlike stranger craves,

[008-039] Toss’d by rude tempest through a war of waves;

[008-040] Perhaps from realms that view the rising day,

[008-041] Or nations subject to the western ray.

[008-042] Then grant, what here all sons of woe obtain

[008-043] (For here affliction never pleads in vain);

[008-044] Be chosen youth prepared, expert to try

[008-045] The vast profound and bid the vessel fly;

[008-046] Launch the tall back, and order every oar;

[008-047] Then in our court indulge the genial hour.

[008-048] Instant, you sailors to this task attend;

[008-049] Swift to the palace, all ye peers ascend;

[008-050] Let none to strangers honours due disclaim:

[008-051] Be there Demodocus the bard of fame,

[008-052] Taught by the gods to please, when high he sings

[008-053] The vocal lay, responsive to the strings.”

[008-054] Thus spoke the prince; the attending peers obey;

[008-055] In state they move; Alcinous heads the way

[008-056] Swift to Demodocus the herald flies,

[008-057] At once the sailors to their charge arise;

[008-058] They launch the vessel, and unfurl the sails,

[008-059] And stretch the swelling canvas to the gales;

[008-060] Then to the palace move: a gathering throng,

[008-061] Youth, and white age, tumultuous pour along.

[008-062] Now all accesses to the dome are fill’d;

[008-063] Eight boars, the choicest of the herd, are kill’d;

[008-064] Two beeves, twelve fatlings, from the flock they bring

[008-065] To crown the feast; so wills the bounteous king.

[008-066] The herald now arrives, and guides along

[008-067] The sacred master of celestial song;

[008-068] Dear to the Muse! who gave his days to flow

[008-069] With mighty blessings, mix’d with mighty woe;

[008-070] With clouds of darkness quench’d his visual ray,

[008-071] But gave him skill to raise the lofty lay.

[008-072] High on a radiant throne sublime in state,

[008-073] Encircled by huge multitudes, he sate;

[008-074] With silver shone the throne; his lyre, well strung

[008-075] To rapturous sounds, at hand Poutonous hung.

[008-076] Before his seat a polish’d table shines,

[008-077] And a full goblet foams with generous wines;

[008-078] His food a herald bore; and now they fed;

[008-079] And now the rage of craving hunger fled.

## Section 22

[008-080] Then, fired by all the Muse, aloud he sings

[008-081] The mighty deeds of demigods and kings;

[008-082] From that fierce wrath the noble song arose,

[008-083] That made Ulysses and [[achilles|Achilles]] foes;

[008-084] How o’er the feast they doom the fall of Troy;

[008-085] The stern debate [[agamemnon|Atrides]] hears with joy;

[008-086] For Heaven foretold the contest, when he trod

[008-087] The marble threshold of the Delphic god,

[008-088] Curious to learn the counsels of the sky,

[008-089] Ere yet he loosed the rage of war on Troy.

[008-090] Touch’d at the song, Ulysses straight resign’d

[008-091] To soft affliction all his manly mind.

[008-092] Before his eyes the purple vest he drew,

[008-093] Industrious to conceal the falling dew;

[008-094] But when the music paused, he ceased to shed

[008-095] The flowing tear, and raised his drooping head;

[008-096] And, lifting to the gods a goblet crown’d,

[008-097] He pour’d a pure libation to the ground.

[008-098] Transported with the song, the listening train

[008-099] Again with loud applause demand the strain;

[008-100] Again Ulysses veil’d his pensive head.

[008-101] Again unmann’d, a shower of sorrows shed;

[008-102] Conceal’d he wept; the king observed alone

[008-103] The silent tear, and heard the secret groan;

[008-104] Then to the bard aloud—“O cease to sing,

[008-105] Dumb be thy voice and mute the harmonious string;

[008-106] Enough the feast has pleased, enough the power

[008-107] Of heavenly song has crown’d the genial hour!

[008-108] Incessant in the games your strength display,

[008-109] Contest, ye brave the honours of the day!

[008-110] That pleased the admiring stranger may proclaim

[008-111] In distant regions the Phæacian fame:

[008-112] None wield the gauntlet with so dire a sway,

[008-113] Or swifter in the race devour the way;

[008-114] None in the leap spring with so strong a bound,

[008-115] Or firmer, in the wrestling, press the ground.”

[008-116] Thus spoke the king; the attending peers obey;

[008-117] In state they move, Alcinous lends the way;

[008-118] His golden lyre Demodocus unstrung,

[008-119] High on a column in the palace hung;

[008-120] And guided by a herald’s guardian cares,

[008-121] Majestic to the lists of [[kleos|Fame]] repairs.

[008-122] Now swarms the populace: a countless throng,

[008-123] Youth and hoar age; and man drives man along.

[008-124] The games begin; ambitious of the prize,

[008-125] Acroneus, Thoon, and Eretmeus rise;

[008-126] The prize Ocyalus and Prymneus claim,

[008-127] Anchialus and Ponteus, chiefs of fame.

[008-128] There Proreus, Nautes, Eratreus, appear

[008-129] And famed Amphialus, Polyneus’ heir;

[008-130] [[euryalus|Euryalus]], like Mars terrific, rose,

[008-131] When clad in wrath he withers hosts of foes;

[008-132] Naubolides with grace unequall’d shone,

[008-133] Or equall’d by [[laodamas|Laodamas]] alone.

[008-134] With these came forth Ambasineus the strong:

[008-135] And three brave sons, from great Alcinous sprung.

[008-136] Ranged in a line the ready racers stand,

[008-137] Start from the goal, and vanish o’er the strand:

[008-138] Swift as on wings of winds, upborne they fly,

[008-139] And drifts of rising dust involve the sky.

[008-140] Before the rest, what space the hinds allow

[008-141] Between the mule and ox, from plough to plough,

[008-142] Clytonius sprung: he wing’d the rapid way,

[008-143] And bore the unrivall’d honours of the day.

[008-144] With fierce embrace the brawny wrestlers join;

[008-145] The conquest, great Euryalus, is thine.

[008-146] Amphialus sprung forward with a bound,

[008-147] Superior in the leap, a length of ground.

[008-148] From Elatreus’ strong arm the discus flies,

[008-149] And sings with unmatch’d force along the skies.

[008-150] And Laodam whirls high, with dreadful sway,

[008-151] The gloves of death, victorious in the fray.

[008-152] While thus the peerage in the games contends,

[008-153] In act to speak, Laodamas ascends.

[008-154] “O friends (he cries), the stranger seems well skill’d

[008-155] To try the illustrious labours of the field:

[008-156] I deem him brave: then grant the brave man’s claim,

[008-157] Invite the hero to his share of fame.

[008-158] What nervous arms he boasts! how firm his tread!

[008-159] His limbs how turn’d! how broad his shoulders spread!

[008-160] By age unbroke!—but all-consuming care

[008-161] Destroys perhaps the strength that time would spare:

[008-162] Dire is the ocean, dread in all its forms!

[008-163] Man must decay when man contends with storms.”

[008-164] “Well hast thou spoke (Euryalus replies):

[008-165] Thine is the guest, invite him thou to rise.”

[008-166] Swift as the word, advancing from the crowd,

[008-167] He made obeisance, and thus spoke aloud:

[008-168] “Vouchsafes the reverend stranger to display

[008-169] His manly worth, and share the glorious day?

[008-170] Father, arise! for thee thy port proclaims

[008-171] Expert to conquer in the solemn games.

[008-172] To fame arise! for what more fame can yield

[008-173] Than the swift race, or conflict of the field?

[008-174] Steal from corroding care one transient day,

[008-175] To glory give the space thou hast to stay;

[008-176] Short is the time, and lo! e’en now the gales

[008-177] Call thee aboard, and stretch the swelling sails.”

[008-178] To whom with sighs Ulysses gave reply:

[008-179] “Ah why the ill-suiting pastime must I try?

[008-180] To gloomy care my thoughts alone are free;

[008-181] Ill the gay sports with troubled hearts agree;

[008-182] Sad from my natal hour my days have ran,

[008-183] A much-afflicted, much-enduring man!

[008-184] Who, suppliant to the king and peers, implores

[008-185] A speedy voyage to his native shore.”

[008-186] “Wide wanders, Laodam, thy erring tongue

[008-187] The sports of glory to the brave belong

[008-188] (Retorts Euryalus): he bears no claim

[008-189] Among the great, unlike the sons of Fame.

[008-190] A wandering merchant he frequents the main

[008-191] Some mean seafarer in pursuit of gain;

[008-192] Studious of freight, in naval trade well skill’d,

[008-193] But dreads the athletic labours of the field.”

[008-194] Incensed, Ulysses with a frown replies:

[008-195] “O forward to proclaim thy soul unwise!

[008-196] With partial hands the gods their gifts dispense;

[008-197] Some greatly think, some speak with manly sense;

[008-198] Here Heaven an elegance of form denies,

[008-199] But wisdom the defect of form supplies;

[008-200] This man with energy of thought controls,

[008-201] And steals with modest violence our souls;

[008-202] He speaks reservedly, but he speaks with force,

[008-203] Nor can one word be changed but for a worse;

[008-204] In public more than mortal he appears,

[008-205] And as he moves, the praising crowd reveres;

[008-206] While others, beauteous as the etherial kind,

[008-207] The nobler portion went, a knowing mind,

[008-208] In outward show Heaven gives thee to excel.

[008-209] But Heaven denies the praise of thinking well

[008-210] I’ll bear the brave a rude ungovern’d tongue,

[008-211] And, youth, my generous soul resents the wrong.

[008-212] Skill’d in heroic exercise, I claim

[008-213] A post of honour with the sons of Fame.

[008-214] Such was my boast while vigour crown’d my days,

[008-215] Now care surrounds me, and my force decays;

[008-216] Inured a melancholy part to bear

[008-217] In scenes of death, by tempest and by war

[008-218] Yet thus by woes impair’d, no more I waive

[008-219] To prove the hero—slander stings the brave.”

[008-220] Then gliding forward with a furious bound

[008-221] He wrench’d a rocky fragment from the ground

[008-222] By far more ponderous, and more huge by far

[008-223] Than what Phæacia’s sons discharged in air.

[008-224] Fierce from his arm the enormous load he flings;

[008-225] Sonorous through the shaded air it sings;

[008-226] Couch’d to the earth, tempestuous as it flies,

[008-227] The crowd gaze upward while it cleaves the skies.

[008-228] Beyond all marks, with many a giddy round

[008-229] Down-rushing, it up-turns a hill of ground.

[008-230] That Instant Pallas, bursting from a cloud,

[008-231] Fix’d a distinguish’d mark, and cried aloud:

[008-232] “E’en he who, sightless, wants his visual ray

[008-233] May by his touch alone award the day:

[008-234] Thy signal throw transcends the utmost bound

[008-235] Of every champion by a length of ground:

[008-236] Securely bid the strongest of the train

[008-237] Arise to throw; the strongest throws in vain.”

[008-238] She spoke: and momentary mounts the sky:

[008-239] The friendly voice Ulysses hears with joy.

[008-240] Then thus aloud (elate with decent pride)

[008-241] “Rise, ye Phæacians, try your force (he cried):

[008-242] If with this throw the strongest caster vie,

[008-243] Still, further still, I bid the discus fly.

[008-244] Stand forth, ye champions, who the gauntlet wield,

[008-245] Or ye, the swiftest racers of the field!

[008-246] Stand forth, ye wrestlers, who these pastimes grace!

[008-247] I wield the gauntlet, and I run the race.

[008-248] In such heroic games I yield to none,

[008-249] Or yield to brave Laodamas alone:

[008-250] Shall I with brave Laodamas contend?

[008-251] A friend is sacred, and I style him friend.

[008-252] Ungenerous were the man, and base of heart,

[008-253] Who takes the kind, and pays the ungrateful part:

[008-254] Chiefly the man, in foreign realms confined,

[008-255] Base to his friend, to his own interest blind:

[008-256] All, all your heroes I this day defy;

[008-257] Give me a man that we our might may try.

[008-258] Expert in every art, I boast the skill

[008-259] To give the feather’d arrow wings to kill;

[008-260] Should a whole host at once discharge the bow,

[008-261] My well-aim’d shaft with death prevents the foe:

[008-262] Alone superior in the field of Troy,

[008-263] Great Philoctetes taught the shaft to fly.

[008-264] From all the sons of earth unrivall’d praise

[008-265] I justly claim; but yield to better days,

[008-266] To those famed days when great Alcides rose,

[008-267] And Eurytus, who bade the gods be foes

[008-268] (Vain Eurytus, whose art became his crime,

[008-269] Swept from the earth, he perish’d in his prime:

[008-270] Sudden the irremeable way he trod,

[008-271] Who boldly durst defy the bowyer god).

[008-272] In fighting fields as far the spear I throw

[008-273] As flies an arrow from the well-drawn bow.

[008-274] Sole in the race the contest I decline,

[008-275] Stiff are my weary joints, and I resign;

[008-276] By storms and hunger worn; age well may fail,

[008-277] When storms and hunger doth at once assail.”

[008-278] Abash’d, the numbers hear the godlike man,

[008-279] Till great Alcinous mildly thus began:

[008-280] “Well hast thou spoke, and well thy generous tongue

[008-281] With decent pride refutes a public wrong:

[008-282] Warm are thy words, but warm without offence;

[008-283] Fear only fools, secure in men of sense;

[008-284] Thy worth is known. Then hear our country’s claim,

[008-285] And bear to heroes our heroic fame:

[008-286] In distant realms our glorious deeds display,

[008-287] Repeat them frequent in the genial day;

[008-288] When, blest with ease, thy woes and wanderings end,

[008-289] Teach them thy consort, bid thy sons attend;

[008-290] How, loved of [[zeus|Jove]], he crown’d our sires with praise,

[008-291] How we their offspring dignify our race.

[008-292] “Let other realms the deathful gauntlet wield,

[008-293] Or boast the glories of the athletic field:

[008-294] We in the course unrivall’d speed display,

[008-295] Or through cerulean billows plough the way;

[008-296] To dress, to dance, to sing, our sole delight,

[008-297] The feast or bath by day, and love by night:

[008-298] Rise, then, ye skill’d in measures; let him bear

[008-299] Your fame to men that breathe a distant air;

[008-300] And faithful say, to you the powers belong

[008-301] To race, to sail, to dance, to chant the song.

[008-302] “But, herald, to the palace swift repair,

[008-303] And the soft lyre to grace our pastimes bear.”

[008-304] Swift at the word, obedient to the king,

[008-305] The herald flies the tuneful lyre to bring.

[008-306] Up rose nine seniors, chosen to survey

[008-307] The future games, the judges of the day

[008-308] With instant care they mark a spacious round

[008-309] And level for the dance the allotted ground:

[008-310] The herald bears the lyre: intent to play,

[008-311] The bard advancing meditates the lay.

[008-312] Skill’d in the dance, tall youths, a blooming band,

[008-313] Graceful before the heavenly minstrel stand:

[008-314] Light bounding from the earth, at once they rise,

[008-315] Their feet half-viewless quiver in the skies:

[008-316] Ulysses gazed, astonish’d to survey

[008-317] The glancing splendours as their sandals play.

[008-318] Meantime the bard, alternate to the strings,

[008-319] The loves of Mars and Cytherea sings:

[008-320] How the stern god, enamour’d with her charms

[008-321] Clasp’d the gay panting goddess in his arms,

[008-322] By bribes seduced; and how the sun, whose eye

[008-323] Views the broad heavens, disclosed the lawless joy.

[008-324] Stung to the soul, indignant through the skies

[008-325] To his black forge vindictive Vulcan flies:

[008-326] Arrived, his sinewy arms incessant place

[008-327] The eternal anvil on the massy base.

[008-328] A wondrous net he labours, to betray

[008-329] The wanton lovers, as entwined they lay,

[008-330] Indissolubly strong; Then instant bears

[008-331] To his immortal dome the finish’d snares:

[008-332] Above, below, around, with art dispread,

[008-333] The sure inclosure folds the genial bed:

[008-334] Whose texture even the search of gods deceives,

[008-335] Thin as the filmy threads the spider weaves,

[008-336] Then, as withdrawing from the starry bowers,

[008-337] He feigns a journey to the Lemnian shores,

[008-338] His favourite isle: observant Mars descries

[008-339] His wish’d recess, and to the goddess flies;

[008-340] He glows, he burns, the fair-hair’d queen of love

[008-341] Descends, smooth gliding from the courts of Jove,

[008-342] Gay blooming in full charms: her hand he press’d

[008-343] With eager joy, and with a sigh address’d:

## Section 23

[008-344] “Come, my beloved! and taste the soft delights:

[008-345] Come, to repose the genial bed invites:

[008-346] Thy absent spouse, neglectful of thy charms,

[008-347] Prefers his barbarous Sintians to thy arms!”

[008-348] Then, nothing loth, the enamour’d fair he led,

[008-349] And sunk transported on the conscious bed.

[008-350] Down rush’d the toils, inwrapping as they lay

[008-351] The careless lovers in their wanton play:

[008-352] In vain they strive; the entangling snares deny

[008-353] (Inextricably firm) the power to fly.

[008-354] Warn’d by the god who sheds the golden day,

[008-355] Stern Vulcan homeward treads the starry way:

[008-356] Arrived, he sees, he grieves, with rage he burns:

[008-357] Full horribly he roars, his voice all heaven returns.

[008-358] “O Jove (he cried) O all ye powers above,

[008-359] See the lewd dalliance of the queen of love!

[008-360] Me, awkward me, she scorns; and yields her charms

[008-361] To that fair lecher, the strong god of arms.

[008-362] If I am lame, that stain my natal hour

[008-363] By fate imposed; such me my parent bore.

[008-364] Why was I born? See how the wanton lies!

[008-365] Oh sight tormenting to a husband’s eyes!

[008-366] But yet, I trust, this once e’en Mars would fly

[008-367] His fair-one’s arms—he thinks her, once, too nigh.

[008-368] But there remain, ye guilty, in my power,

[008-369] Till Jove refunds his shameless daughter’s dower.

[008-370] Too dear I prized a fair enchanting face:

[008-371] Beauty unchaste is beauty in disgrace.”

[008-372] Meanwhile the gods the dome of Vulcan throng;

[008-373] [[apollo|Apollo]] comes, and [[poseidon|Neptune]] comes along;

[008-374] With these gay [[hermes|Hermes]] trod the starry plain;

[008-375] But modesty withheld the goddess train.

[008-376] All heaven beholds, imprison’d as they lie,

[008-377] And unextinguish’d laughter shakes the sky.

[008-378] Then mutual, thus they spoke: “Behold on wrong

[008-379] Swift vengeance waits; and art subdues the strong!

[008-380] Dwells there a god on all the Olympian brow

[008-381] More swift than Mars, and more than Vulcan slow?

[008-382] Yet Vulcan conquers, and the god of arms

[008-383] Must pay the penalty for lawless charms.”

[008-384] Thus serious they; but he who gilds the skies,

[008-385] The gay Apollo, thus to Hermes cries:

[008-386] “Wouldst thou enchain’d like Mars, O Hermes, lie

[008-387] And bear the shame like Mars to share the joy?”

[008-388] “O envied shame! (the smiling youth rejoin’d;)

[008-389] And thrice the chains, and thrice more firmly bind;

[008-390] Gaze all ye gods, and every goddess gaze,

[008-391] Yet eager would I bless the sweet disgrace.”

[008-392] Loud laugh the rest, e’en Neptune laughs aloud,

[008-393] Yet sues importunate to loose the god.

[008-394] “And free, (he cries) O Vulcan! free from shame

[008-395] Thy captives; I ensure the penal claim.”

[008-396] “Will Neptune (Vulcan then) the faithless trust?

[008-397] He suffers who gives surety for the unjust:

[008-398] But say, if that lewd scandal of the sky,

[008-399] To liberty restored, perfidious fly:

[008-400] Say, wilt thou bear the mulct?” He instant cries,

[008-401] “The mulct I bear, if Mars perfidious flies.”

[008-402] To whom appeased: “No more I urge delay;

[008-403] When Neptune sues, my part is to obey.”

[008-404] Then to the snares his force the god applies;

[008-405] They burst; and Mars to Thrace indignant flies:

[008-406] To the soft Cyprian shores the goddess moves,

[008-407] To visit Paphos and her blooming groves,

[008-408] Where to the Power an hundred altars rise,

[008-409] And breathing odours scent the balmy skies;

[008-410] Concealed she bathes in consecrated bowers,

[008-411] The Graces unguents shed, ambrosial showers,

[008-412] Unguents that charm the gods! she last assumes

[008-413] Her wondrous robes; and full the goddess blooms.

[008-414] Thus sung the bard: Ulysses hears with joy,

[008-415] And loud applauses read the vaulted sky.

[008-416] Then to the sports his sons the king commands,

[008-417] Each blooming youth before the monarch stands,

[008-418] In dance unmatch’d! A wondrous ball is brought

[008-419] (The work of Polypus, divinely wrought);

[008-420] This youth with strength enormous bids it fly,

[008-421] And bending backward whirls it to the sky;

[008-422] His brother, springing with an active bound,

[008-423] At distance intercepts it from the ground.

[008-424] The ball dismissed, in dance they skim the strand,

[008-425] Turn and return, and scarce imprint the sand.

[008-426] The assembly gazes with astonished eyes,

[008-427] And sends in shouts applauses to the skies.

[008-428] Then thus Ulysses: “Happy king, whose name

[008-429] The brightest shines in all the rolls of fame!

[008-430] In subjects happy with surprise I gaze;

[008-431] Thy praise was just; their skill transcends thy praise.”

[008-432] Pleas’d with his people’s fame, the monarch hears,

[008-433] And thus benevolent accosts the peers:

[008-434] “Since wisdom’s sacred guidance he pursues,

[008-436] Twelve princes in our realm dominion share,
