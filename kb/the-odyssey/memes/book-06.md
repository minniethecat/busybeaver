[006-001] BOOK VI.

[006-002] ARGUMENT.

[006-003] Pallas appearing in a dream in to [[nausicaa|Nausicaa]] (the daughter of [[alcinous|Alcinous]],

[006-004] king of Phæacia, commands her to descend to the river, and wash the

[006-005] robes of state, in preparation for her nuptials. Nausicaa goes with her

[006-006] handmaidens to the river; where, while the garments are spread on the

[006-007] bank, they divert themselves in sports. Their voices awaken [[odysseus|Ulysses]],

[006-008] who, addressing himself to the princess, is by her relieved and

[006-009] clothed, and receives directions in what manner to apply to the king

[006-010] and queen of the island.

[006-011] While thus the weary wanderer sunk to rest,

[006-012] And peaceful slumbers calmed his anxious breast,

[006-013] The martial maid from heaven’s aërial height

[006-014] Swift to Phæacia wing’d her rapid flight.

[006-015] In elder times the soft Phæacian train

[006-016] In ease possess’d the wide Hyperian plain;

[006-017] Till the Cyclopean race in arms arose,

[006-018] A lawless nation of gigantic foes;

[006-019] Then great Nausithous from Hyperia far,

[006-020] Through seas retreating from the sounds of war,

[006-021] The recreant nation to fair [[scheria|Scheria]] led,

[006-022] Where never science rear’d her laurell’d head;

[006-023] There round his tribes a strength of wall he raised;

[006-024] To heaven the glittering domes and temples blazed;

[006-025] Just to his realms, he parted grounds from grounds,

[006-026] And shared the lands, and gave the lands their bounds.

[006-027] Now in the silent grave the monarch lay,

[006-028] And wise Alcinous held the legal sway.

[006-029] To his high palace through the fields of air

[006-030] The goddess shot; Ulysses was her care.

[006-031] There, as the night in silence roll’d away,

[006-032] A heaven of charms divine Nausicaa lay:

[006-033] Through the thick gloom the shining portals blaze;

[006-034] Two nymphs the portals guard, each nymph a Grace,

[006-035] Light as the viewless air the warrior maid

[006-036] Glides through the valves, and hovers round her head;

[006-037] A favourite virgin’s blooming form she took,

[006-038] From Dymas sprung, and thus the vision spoke:

[006-039] “Oh Indolent! to waste thy hours away!

[006-040] And sleep’st thou careless of the bridal day!

[006-041] Thy spousal ornament neglected lies;

[006-042] Arise, prepare the bridal train, arise!

[006-043] A just applause the cares of dress impart,

[006-044] And give soft transport to a parent’s heart.

[006-045] Haste, to the limpid stream direct thy way,

[006-046] When the gay morn unveils her smiling ray;

[006-047] Haste to the stream! companion of thy care,

[006-048] Lo, I thy steps attend, thy labours share.

[006-049] Virgin, awake! the marriage hour is nigh,

[006-050] See from their thrones thy kindred monarchs sigh!

[006-051] The royal car at early dawn obtain,

[006-052] And order mules obedient to the rein;

[006-053] For rough the way, and distant rolls the wave,

[006-054] Where their fair vests Phæacian virgins lave,

[006-055] In pomp ride forth; for pomp becomes the great

[006-056] And majesty derives a grace from state.”

[006-057] Then to the palaces of heaven she sails,

[006-058] Incumbent on the wings of wafting gales;

[006-059] The seat of gods; the regions mild of peace,

[006-060] Full joy, and calm eternity of ease.

[006-061] There no rude winds presume to shake the skies,

[006-062] No rains descend, no snowy vapours rise;

[006-063] But on immortal thrones the blest repose;

[006-064] The firmament with living splendours glows.

[006-065] Hither the goddess winged the aërial way,

[006-066] Through heaven’s eternal gates that blazed with day.

[006-067] Now from her rosy car Aurora shed

[006-068] The dawn, and all the orient flamed with red.

[006-069] Up rose the virgin with the morning light,

[006-070] Obedient to the vision of the night.

[006-071] The queen she sought, the queen her hours bestowed

[006-072] In curious works; the whirling spindle glow’d

[006-073] With crimson threads, while busy damsels call

[006-074] The snowy fleece, or twist the purpled wool.

[006-075] Meanwhile Phæacia’s peers in council sate;

[006-076] From his high dome the king descends in state;

[006-077] Then with a filial awe the royal maid

[006-078] Approach’d him passing, and submissive said:

[006-079] “Will my dread sire his ear regardful deign,

[006-080] And may his child the royal car obtain?

[006-081] Say, with my garments shall I bend my way?

[006-082] Where through the vales the mazy waters stray?

[006-083] A dignity of dress adorns the great,

[006-084] And kings draw lustre from the robe of state.

[006-085] Five sons thou hast; three wait the bridal day.

[006-086] And spotless robes become the young and gay;

[006-087] So when with praise amid the dance they shine,

[006-088] By these my cares adorn’d that praise is mine.”

[006-089] Thus she: but blushes ill-restrain’d betray

[006-090] Her thoughts intentive on the bridal day,

[006-091] The conscious sire the dawning blush survey’d,

[006-092] And, smiling, thus bespoke the blooming maid

[006-093] “My child, my darling joy, the car receive;

[006-094] That, and whate’er our daughter asks, we give.”

[006-095] Swift at the royal nod the attending train

[006-096] The car prepare, the mules incessant rein,

[006-097] The blooming virgin with despatchful cares

[006-098] Tunics, and stoles, and robes imperial, bears.

[006-099] The queen, assiduous to her train assigns

[006-100] The sumptuous viands, and the flavorous wines.

[006-101] The train prepare a cruse of curious mould,

[006-102] A cruse of fragrance, form’d of burnish’d gold;

[006-103] Odour divine! whose soft refreshing streams

[006-104] Sleek the smooth skin, and scent the snowy limbs.
