[002-001] BOOK II.

[002-002] ARGUMENT.

[002-003] THE COUNCIL OF ITHACA.

[002-004] [[telemachus|Telemachus]] in the assembly of the lords of [[ithaca|Ithaca]] complains of the

[002-005] injustice done him by the suitors, and insists upon their departure

[002-006] from his palace; appealing to the princes, and exciting the people to

[002-007] declare against them. The suitors endeavour to justify their stay, at

[002-008] least till he shall send the queen to the court of [[icarius|Icarius]] her father;

[002-009] which he refuses. There appears a prodigy of two eagles in the sky,

[002-010] which an augur expounds to the ruin of the suitors. Telemachus then

[002-011] demands a vessel to carry him to [[pylos|Pylos]] and [[sparta|Sparta]], there to inquire of

[002-012] his father’s fortunes. Pallas, in the shape of [[mentor|Mentor]] (an ancient

[002-013] friend of [[odysseus|Ulysses]]), helps him to a ship, assists him in preparing

[002-014] necessaries for the voyage, and embarks with him that night; which

[002-015] concludes the second day from the opening of the poem. The scene

[002-016] continues in the palace of Ulysses, in Ithaca.

[002-017] Now reddening from the dawn, the morning ray

[002-018] Glow’d in the front of heaven, and gave the day

[002-019] The youthful hero, with returning light,

[002-020] Rose anxious from the inquietudes of night.

[002-021] A royal robe he wore with graceful pride,

[002-022] A two-edged falchion threaten’d by his side,

[002-023] Embroider’d sandals glitter’d as he trod,

[002-024] And forth he moved, majestic as a god.

[002-025] Then by his heralds, restless of delay,

[002-026] To council calls the peers: the peers obey.

[002-027] Soon as in solemn form the assembly sate,

[002-028] From his high dome himself descends in state.

[002-029] Bright in his hand a ponderous javelin shined;

[002-030] Two dogs, a faithful guard, attend behind;

[002-031] Pallas with grace divine his form improves,

[002-032] And gazing crowds admire him as he moves,

[002-033] His father’s throne he fill’d; while distant stood

[002-034] The hoary peers, and aged wisdom bow’d.

[002-035] ’Twas silence all. At last AEgyptius spoke;

[002-036] AEgyptius, by his age and sorrow broke;

[002-037] A length of days his soul with prudence crown’d,

[002-038] A length of days had bent him to the ground.

[002-039] His eldest hope in arms to [[troy|Ilion]] came,

[002-040] By great Ulysses taught the path to fame;

[002-041] But (hapless youth) the hideous Cyclops tore

[002-042] His quivering limbs, and quaff’d his spouting gore.

[002-043] Three sons remain’d; to climb with haughty fires

[002-044] The royal bed, Eurynomus aspires;

[002-045] The rest with duteous love his griefs assuage,

[002-046] And ease the sire of half the cares of age.

[002-047] Yet still his [[antiphus|Antiphus]] he loves, he mourns,

[002-048] And, as he stood, he spoke and wept by turns,

[002-049] “Since great Ulysses sought the Phrygian plains,

[002-050] Within these walls inglorious silence reigns.

[002-051] Say then, ye peers! by whose commands we meet?

[002-052] Why here once more in solemn council sit?

[002-053] Ye young, ye old, the weighty cause disclose:

[002-054] Arrives some message of invading foes?

[002-055] Or say, does high necessity of state

[002-056] Inspire some patriot, and demand debate?

[002-057] The present synod speaks its author wise;

[002-058] Assist him, [[zeus|Jove]], thou regent of the skies!”

[002-059] He spoke. Telemachus with transport glows,

[002-060] Embraced the omen, and majestic rose

[002-061] (His royal hand the imperial sceptre sway’d);

[002-062] Then thus, addressing to AEgyptius, said:

[002-063] “Reverend old man! lo here confess’d he stands

[002-064] By whom ye meet; my grief your care demands.

[002-065] No story I unfold of public woes,

[002-066] Nor bear advices of impending foes:

[002-067] Peace the blest land, and joys incessant crown:

[002-068] Of all this happy realm, I grieve alone.

[002-069] For my lost sire continual sorrows spring,

[002-070] The great, the good; your father and your king.

[002-071] Yet more; our house from its foundation bows,

[002-072] Our foes are powerful, and your sons the foes;

[002-073] Hither, unwelcome to the queen, they come;

[002-074] Why seek they not the rich Icarian dome?

[002-075] If she must wed, from other hands require

[002-076] The dowry: is Telemachus her sire?

[002-077] Yet through my court the noise of revel rings,

[002-078] And waste the wise frugality of kings.

[002-079] Scarce all my herds their luxury suffice;

[002-080] Scarce all my wine their midnight hours supplies.

[002-081] Safe in my youth, in riot still they grow,

[002-082] Nor in the helpless orphan dread a foe.

[002-083] But come it will, the time when manhood grants

[002-084] More powerful advocates than vain complaints.

[002-085] Approach that hour! insufferable wrong

[002-086] Cries to the gods, and vengeance sleeps too long.

[002-087] Rise then, ye peers! with virtuous anger rise;

[002-088] Your fame revere, but most the avenging skies.

[002-089] By all the deathless powers that reign above,

[002-090] By righteous Themis and by thundering Jove

[002-091] (Themis, who gives to councils, or denies

[002-092] Success; and humbles, or confirms the wise),

[002-093] Rise in my aid! suffice the tears that flow

[002-094] For my lost sire, nor add new woe to woe.

[002-095] If e’er he bore the sword to strengthen ill,

[002-096] Or, having power to wrong, betray’d the will,

[002-097] On me, on me your kindled wrath assuage,

[002-098] And bid the voice of lawless riot rage.

[002-099] If ruin to your royal race ye doom,

[002-100] Be you the spoilers, and our wealth consume.

[002-101] Then might we hope redress from juster laws,

[002-102] And raise all Ithaca to aid our cause:

[002-103] But while your sons commit the unpunish’d wrong,

[002-104] You make the arm of violence too strong.”

[002-105] While thus he spoke, with rage and grief he frown’d,

[002-106] And dash’d the imperial sceptre to the ground.

[002-107] The big round tear hung trembling in his eye:

[002-108] The synod grieved, and gave a pitying sigh,

[002-109] Then silent sate—at length [[antinous|Antinous]] burns

[002-110] With haughty rage, and sternly thus returns:

[002-111] “O insolence of youth! whose tongue affords

[002-112] Such railing eloquence, and war of words.

[002-113] Studious thy country’s worthies to defame,

[002-114] Thy erring voice displays thy mother’s shame.

[002-115] Elusive of the bridal day, she gives

[002-116] Fond hopes to all, and all with hopes deceives.

[002-117] Did not the sun, through heaven’s wide azure roll’d,

[002-118] For three long years the royal fraud behold?

[002-119] While she, laborious in delusion, spread

[002-120] The spacious loom, and mix’d the various thread:

[002-121] Where as to life the wondrous figures rise,

[002-122] Thus spoke the inventive queen, with artful sighs:

[002-123] “Though cold in death Ulysses breathes no more,

[002-124] Cease yet awhile to urge the bridal hour:

[002-125] Cease, till to great [[laertes|Laertes]] I bequeath

[002-126] A task of grief, his ornaments of death.

[002-127] Lest when [[moira|the Fates]] his royal ashes claim,

[002-128] The Grecian matrons taint my spotless fame;

[002-129] When he, whom living mighty realms obey’d,

[002-130] Shall want in death a shroud to grace his shade.’

[002-131] “Thus she: at once the generous train complies,

[002-132] Nor fraud mistrusts in virtue’s fair disguise.

[002-133] The work she plied; but, studious of delay,

[002-134] By night reversed the labours of the day.

[002-135] While thrice the sun his annual journey made,

[002-136] The conscious lamp the midnight fraud survey’d;

[002-137] Unheard, unseen, three years her arts prevail;

[002-138] The fourth her maid unfolds the amazing tale.

[002-139] We saw, as unperceived we took our stand,

[002-140] The backward labours of her faithless hand.

[002-141] Then urged, she perfects her illustrious toils;

[002-142] A wondrous monument of female wiles!

[002-143] “But you, O peers! and thou, O prince! give ear

[002-144] (I speak aloud, that every Greek may hear):

[002-145] Dismiss the queen; and if her sire approves

[002-146] Let him espouse her to the peer she loves:

[002-147] Bid instant to prepare the bridal train,

[002-148] Nor let a race of princes wait in vain.

[002-149] Though with a grace divine her soul is blest,

[002-150] And all [[athena|Minerva]] breathes within her breast,

[002-151] In wondrous arts than woman more renown’d,

[002-152] And more than woman with deep wisdom crown’d;

[002-153] Though Tyro nor Mycene match her name,

[002-154] Not great Alcmena (the proud boasts of fame);

[002-155] Yet thus by heaven adorn’d, by heaven’s decree

[002-156] She shines with fatal excellence, to thee:

[002-157] With thee, the bowl we drain, indulge the feast,

[002-158] Till righteous heaven reclaim her stubborn breast.

[002-159] What though from pole to pole resounds her name!

[002-160] The son’s destruction waits the mother’s fame:

[002-161] For, till she leaves thy court, it is decreed,

[002-162] Thy bowl to empty and thy flock to bleed.”

[002-163] While yet he speaks, Telemachus replies:

[002-164] “Ev’n nature starts, and what ye ask denies.

[002-165] Thus, shall I thus repay a mother’s cares,

[002-166] Who gave me life, and nursed my infant years!

[002-167] While sad on foreign shores Ulysses treads.

[002-168] Or glides a ghost with unapparent shades;

[002-169] How to Icarius in the bridal hour

[002-170] Shall I, by waste undone, refund the dower?

[002-171] How from my father should I vengeance dread!

[002-172] How would my mother curse my hated head!

[002-173] And while in wrath to vengeful fiends she cries,

[002-174] How from their hell would vengeful fiends arise!

[002-175] Abhorr’d by all, accursed my name would grow,

[002-176] The earth’s disgrace, and human-kind my foe.

[002-177] If this displease, why urge ye here your stay?

[002-178] Haste from the court, ye spoilers, haste away:

[002-179] Waste in wild riot what your land allows,

[002-180] There ply the early feast, and late carouse.

[002-181] But if to honour lost, ’tis still decreed

[002-182] For you my bowl shall flow, my flocks shall bleed;

[002-183] Judge, and assert my right, impartial Jove!

[002-184] By him, and all the immortal host above

[002-185] (A sacred oath), if heaven the power supply,

[002-186] [[poine|Vengeance]] I vow, and for your wrongs ye die.”

[002-187] With that, two eagles from a mountain’s height

[002-188] By Jove’s command direct their rapid flight;

[002-189] Swift they descend, with wing to wing conjoin’d,

[002-190] Stretch their broad plumes, and float upon the wind.

[002-191] Above the assembled peers they wheel on high,

[002-192] And clang their wings, and hovering beat the sky;

[002-193] With ardent eyes the rival train they threat,

[002-194] And shrieking loud denounce approaching fate.

[002-195] They cuff, they tear; their cheeks and neck they rend,

[002-196] And from their plumes huge drops of blood descend;

[002-197] Then sailing o’er the domes and towers, they fly,

[002-198] Full toward the east, and mount into the sky.

[002-199] The wondering rivals gaze, with cares oppress’d,

[002-200] And chilling horrors freeze in every breast,

[002-201] Till big with knowledge of approaching woes,

[002-202] The prince of augurs, [[halitherses|Halitherses]], rose:

[002-203] Prescient he view’d the aërial tracks, and drew

[002-204] A sure presage from every wing that flew.

[002-205] “Ye sons (he cried) of Ithaca, give ear;

[002-206] Hear all! but chiefly you, O rivals! hear.

[002-207] Destruction sure o’er all your heads impends

[002-208] Ulysses comes, and death his steps attends.

[002-209] Nor to the great alone is death decreed;

[002-210] We and our guilty Ithaca must bleed.

[002-211] Why cease we then the wrath of heaven to stay?

[002-212] Be humbled all, and lead, ye great! the way.

[002-213] For lo my words no fancied woes relate;

[002-214] I speak from science and the voice of fate.

[002-215] “When great Ulysses sought the Phrygian shores

[002-216] To shake with war proud Ilion’s lofty towers,

[002-217] Deeds then undone my faithful tongue foretold:

[002-218] Heaven seal’d my words, and you those deeds behold.

[002-219] I see (I cried) his woes, a countless train;

[002-220] I see his friends o’erwhelm’d beneath the main;

[002-221] How twice ten years from shore to shore he roams:

[002-222] Now twice ten years are past, and now he comes!”

[002-223] To whom [[eurymachus|Eurymachus]]—“Fly, dotard fly,

[002-224] With thy wise dreams, and fables of the sky.

[002-225] Go prophesy at home, thy sons advise:

[002-226] Here thou art sage in vain—I better read the skies

[002-227] Unnumber’d birds glide through the aërial way;

[002-228] Vagrants of air, and unforeboding stray.

[002-229] Cold in the tomb, or in the deeps below,

[002-230] Ulysses lies; oh wert thou laid as low!

[002-231] Then would that busy head no broils suggest,

[002-232] For fire to rage Telemachus’ breast,

[002-233] From him some bribe thy venal tongue requires,

[002-234] And interest, not the god, thy voice inspires.

[002-235] His guideless youth, if thy experienced age

[002-236] Mislead fallacious into idle rage,

[002-237] Vengeance deserved thy malice shall repress.

[002-238] And but augment the wrongs thou would’st redress,

[002-239] Telemachus may bid the queen repair

[002-240] To great Icarius, whose paternal care

[002-241] Will guide her passion, and reward her choice

[002-242] With wealthy dower, and bridal gifts of price.

[002-243] Till she retires, determined we remain,

[002-244] And both the prince and augur threat in vain:

[002-245] His pride of words, and thy wild dream of fate,

[002-246] Move not the brave, or only move their hate,

[002-247] Threat on, O prince! elude the bridal day.

[002-248] Threat on, till all thy stores in waste decay.

[002-249] True, Greece affords a train of lovely dames,

[002-250] In wealth and beauty worthy of our flames:

[002-251] But never from this nobler suit we cease;

[002-252] For wealth and beauty less than virtue please.”

[002-253] To whom the youth: “Since then in vain I tell

[002-254] My numerous woes, in silence let them dwell.

[002-255] But Heaven, and all the Greeks, have heard my wrongs;

[002-256] To Heaven, and all the Greeks, redress belongs;

[002-257] Yet this I ask (nor be it ask’d in vain),

[002-258] A bark to waft me o’er the rolling main,

[002-259] The realms of Pyle and Sparta to explore,

[002-260] And seek my royal sire from shore to shore;

[002-261] If, or to fame his doubtful fate be known,

[002-262] Or to be learn’d from oracles alone,

[002-263] If yet he lives, with patience I forbear,

[002-264] Till the fleet hours restore the circling year;

[002-265] But if already wandering in the train

[002-266] Of empty shades, I measure back the main,

[002-267] Plant the fair column o’er the mighty dead,

[002-268] And yield his consort to the nuptial bed.”

[002-269] He ceased; and while abash’d the peers attend,

[002-270] Mentor arose, Ulysses’ faithful friend:

[002-271] (When fierce in arms he sought the scenes of war,

[002-272] “My friend (he cried), my palace be thy care;

[002-273] Years roll’d on years my godlike sire decay,

[002-274] Guard thou his age, and his behests obey.”)

[002-275] Stern as he rose, he cast his eyes around,

[002-276] That flash’d with rage; and as spoke, he frown’d,

[002-277] “O never, never more let king be just,

[002-278] Be mild in power, or faithful to his trust!

[002-279] Let tyrants govern with an iron rod,

[002-280] Oppress, destroy, and be the scourge of God;

[002-281] Since he who like a father held his reign,

[002-282] So soon forgot, was just and mild in vain!

[002-283] True, while my friend is grieved, his griefs I share;

[002-284] Yet now the rivals are my smallest care:

[002-285] They for the mighty mischiefs they devise,

[002-286] Ere long shall pay—their forfeit lives the price.

[002-287] But against you, ye Greeks! ye coward train!

[002-288] Gods! how my soul is moved with just disdain!

[002-289] Dumb ye all stand, and not one tongue affords

[002-290] His injured prince the little aid of words.”

[002-291] While yet he spoke, Leocritus rejoined:

[002-292] “O pride of words, and arrogance of mind!

[002-293] Would’st thou to rise in arms the Greeks advise?

[002-294] Join all your powers? in arms, ye Greeks, arise!

[002-295] Yet would your powers in vain our strength oppose.

[002-296] The valiant few o’ermatch a host of foes.

[002-297] Should great Ulysses stern appear in arms,

[002-298] While the bowl circles and the banquet warms;

[002-299] Though to his breast his spouse with transport flies,

[002-300] Torn from her breast, that hour, Ulysses dies.

[002-301] But hence retreating to your domes repair.

[002-302] To arm the vessel, Mentor! be thy care,

[002-303] And Halitherses! thine: be each his friend;

[002-304] Ye loved the father: go, the son attend.

[002-305] But yet, I trust, the boaster means to stay

[002-306] Safe in the court, nor tempt the watery way.”

[002-307] Then, with a rushing sound the assembly bend

[002-308] Diverse their steps: the rival rout ascend

[002-309] The royal dome; while sad the prince explores

[002-310] The neighbouring main, and sorrowing treads the shores.

[002-311] There, as the waters o’er his hands he shed,

[002-312] The royal suppliant to Minerva pray’d:

[002-313] “O goddess! who descending from the skies

[002-314] Vouchsafed thy presence to my wondering eyes,

[002-315] By whose commands the raging deeps I trace,

[002-316] And seek my sire through storms and rolling seas!

[002-317] Hear from thy heavens above, O warrior maid!

[002-318] Descend once more, propitious to my aid.

[002-319] Without thy presence, vain is thy command:

[002-320] Greece, and the rival train, thy voice withstand.”

[002-321] Indulgent to his prayer, the goddess took

[002-322] Sage Mentor’s form, and thus like Mentor spoke:

[002-323] “O prince, in early youth divinely wise,

[002-324] Born, the Ulysses of thy age to rise

[002-325] If to the son the father’s worth descends,

[002-326] O’er the wide wave success thy ways attends

[002-327] To tread the walks of death he stood prepared;

[002-328] And what he greatly thought, he nobly dared.

[002-329] Were not wise sons descendant of the wise,

[002-330] And did not heroes from brave heroes rise,

[002-331] Vain were my hopes: few sons attain the praise

[002-332] Of their great sires, and most their sires disgrace.

[002-333] But since thy veins paternal virtue fires,

[002-334] And all [[penelope|Penelope]] thy soul inspires,

[002-335] Go, and succeed: the rivals’ aims despise;

[002-336] For never, never wicked man was wise.

[002-337] Blind they rejoice, though now, ev’n now they fall;

[002-338] Death hastes amain: one hour o’erwhelms them all!

[002-339] And lo, with speed we plough the watery way;

[002-340] My power shall guard thee, and my hand convey:

[002-341] The winged vessel studious I prepare,

[002-342] Through seas and realms companion of thy care.

[002-343] Thou to the court ascend: and to the shores

[002-344] (When night advances) bear the naval stores;

[002-345] Bread, that decaying man with strength supplies,

[002-346] And generous wine, which thoughtful sorrow flies.

[002-347] Meanwhile the mariners, by my command,

[002-348] Shall speed aboard, a valiant chosen band.

[002-349] Wide o’er the bay, by vessel vessel rides;

[002-350] The best I choose to waft then o’er the tides.”

[002-351] She spoke: to his high dome the prince returns,

[002-352] And, as he moves, with royal anguish mourns.

[002-353] ’Twas riot all, among the lawless train;

[002-354] Boar bled by boar, and goat by goat lay slain.

[002-355] Arrived, his hand the gay Antinous press’d,

[002-356] And thus deriding, with a smile address’d:

[002-357] “Grieve not, O daring prince! that noble heart;

[002-358] Ill suits gay youth the stern heroic part.

[002-359] Indulge the genial hour, unbend thy soul,

[002-360] Leave thought to age, and drain the flowing bowl.

[002-361] Studious to ease thy grief, our care provides

[002-362] The bark, to waft thee o’er the swelling tides.”

[002-363] “Is this (returns the prince) for mirth a time?

[002-364] When lawless gluttons riot, mirth’s a crime;

[002-365] The luscious wines, dishonour’d, lose their taste;

[002-366] The song is noise, and impious is the feast.

[002-367] Suffice it to have spent with swift decay

[002-368] The wealth of kings, and made my youth a prey.

[002-369] But now the wise instructions of the sage,

[002-370] And manly thoughts inspired by manly age,

[002-371] Teach me to seek redress for all my woe,

[002-372] Here, or in Pyle—in Pyle, or here, your foe.

[002-373] Deny your vessels, ye deny in vain:

[002-374] A private voyager I pass the main.

[002-375] Free breathe the winds, and free the billows flow;

[002-376] And where on earth I live, I live your foe.”

[002-377] He spoke and frown’d, nor longer deign’d to stay,

[002-378] Sternly his hand withdrew, and strode away.

[002-379] Meantime, o’er all the dome, they quaff, they feast,

[002-380] Derisive taunts were spread from guest to guest,

[002-381] And each in jovial mood his mate address’d:

[002-382] “Tremble ye not, O friends, and coward fly,

[002-383] Doom’d by the stern Telemachus to die?

[002-384] To Pyle or Sparta to demand supplies,

[002-385] Big with revenge, the mighty warrior flies;

[002-386] Or comes from Ephyre with poisons fraught,

[002-387] And kills us all in one tremendous draught!”

[002-388] “Or who can say (his gamesome mate replies)

[002-389] But, while the danger of the deeps he tries

[002-390] He, like his sire, may sink deprived of breath,

[002-391] And punish us unkindly by his death?

[002-392] What mighty labours would he then create,

[002-393] To seize his treasures, and divide his state,

[002-394] The royal palace to the queen convey,

[002-395] Or him she blesses in the bridal day!”

[002-396] Meantime the lofty rooms the prince surveys,

[002-397] Where lay the treasures of the Ithacian race:

[002-398] Here ruddy brass and gold refulgent blazed;

[002-399] There polished chests embroider’d vestures graced;

[002-400] Here jars of oil breathed forth a rich perfume;

[002-401] There casks of wine in rows adorn’d the dome

[002-402] (Pure flavorous wine, by gods in bounty given

[002-403] And worthy to exalt the feasts of heaven).

[002-404] Untouch’d they stood, till, his long labours o’er,

[002-405] The great Ulysses reach’d his native shore.

[002-406] A double strength of bars secured the gates;

[002-407] Fast by the door the wise Euryclea waits;

[002-408] Euryclea, who great Ops! thy lineage shared,

[002-409] And watch’d all night, all day, a faithful guard.

[002-410] To whom the prince: “O thou whose guardian care

[002-411] Nursed the most wretched king that breathes the air;

[002-412] Untouch’d and sacred may these vessels stand,

[002-413] Till great Ulysses views his native land.

[002-414] But by thy care twelve urns of wine be fill’d;

[002-415] Next these in worth, and firm these urns be seal’d;

[002-416] And twice ten measures of the choicest flour

[002-417] Prepared, ere yet descends the evening hour.

[002-418] For when the favouring shades of night arise,

[002-419] And peaceful slumbers close my mother’s eyes,

[002-420] Me from our coast shall spreading sails convey,

[002-421] To seek Ulysses through the watery way.”

[002-422] While yet he spoke, she fill’d the walls with cries,

[002-423] And tears ran trickling from her aged eyes.

[002-424] “O whither, whither flies my son (she cried)

[002-425] To realms; that rocks and roaring seas divide?

[002-426] In foreign lands thy father’s days decay’d.

[002-427] And foreign lands contain the mighty dead.

[002-428] The watery way ill-fated if thou try,

[002-429] All, all must perish, and by fraud you die!

[002-432] “Far hence (replied the prince) thy fears be driven:

[002-433] Heaven calls me forth; these counsels are of Heaven.

[002-434] But, by the powers that hate the perjured, swear,

[002-435] To keep my voyage from the royal ear,

[002-436] Nor uncompell’d the dangerous truth betray,

[002-437] Till twice six times descends the lamp of day,

[002-438] Lest the sad tale a mother’s life impair,

[002-439] And grief destroy what time awhile would spare.”

[002-440] Thus he. The matron with uplifted eyes

[002-441] Attests the all-seeing sovereign of the skies.

[002-442] Then studious she prepares the choicest flour,

[002-443] The strength of wheat and wines an ample store.

[002-444] While to the rival train the prince returns,

[002-445] The martial goddess with impatience burns;

[002-446] Like thee, Telemachus, in voice and size,

[002-447] With speed divine from street to street she flies,

[002-448] She bids the mariners prepared to stand,

[002-449] When night descends, embodied on the strand.

[002-450] Then to Noëmon swift she runs, she flies,

[002-451] And asks a bark: the chief a bark supplies.

[002-452] And now, declining with his sloping wheels,

[002-453] Down sunk the sun behind the western hills

[002-454] The goddess shoved the vessel from the shores,

[002-455] And stow’d within its womb the naval stores,

[002-456] Full in the openings of the spacious main

[002-457] It rides; and now descends the sailor-train,

[002-458] Next, to the court, impatient of delay.

[002-459] With rapid step the goddess urged her way;

[002-460] There every eye with slumberous chains she bound,

[002-461] And dash’d the flowing goblet to the ground.

[002-462] Drowsy they rose, with heavy fumes oppress’d,

[002-463] Reel’d from the palace, and retired to rest.

[002-464] Then thus, in Mentor’s reverend form array’d,

[002-465] Spoke to Telemachus the martial maid.

[002-466] “Lo! on the seas, prepared the vessel stands,

[002-467] The impatient mariner thy speed demands.”

[002-468] Swift as she spoke, with rapid pace she leads;

[002-469] The footsteps of the deity he treads.

[002-470] Swift to the shore they move along the strand;

[002-471] The ready vessel rides, the sailors ready stand.

[002-472] He bids them bring their stores; the attending train

[002-473] Load the tall bark, and launch into the main,

[002-474] The prince and goddess to the stern ascend;

[002-475] To the strong stroke at once the rowers bend.

[002-476] Full from the west she bids fresh breezes blow;

[002-477] The sable billows foam and roar below.

[002-478] The chief his orders gives; the obedient band

[002-479] With due observance wait the chief’s command;

[002-480] With speed the mast they rear, with speed unbind

[002-481] The spacious sheet, and stretch it to the wind.

[002-482] High o’er the roaring waves the spreading sails

[002-483] Bow the tall mast, and swell before the gales;

[002-484] The crooked keel the parting surge divides,

[002-485] And to the stern retreating roll the tides.

[002-486] And now they ship their oars, and crown with wine

[002-487] The holy goblet to the powers divine:

[002-488] Imploring all the gods that reign above,

[002-489] But chief the blue-eyed progeny of Jove.

[002-490] Thus all the night they stem the liquid way,

[002-491] And end their voyage with the morning ray.
