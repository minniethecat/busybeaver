# Immortals and Deities

The *Odyssey* is saturated with divine presence. The Olympian gods actively intervene in mortal affairs — Athena guides Telemachus and Odysseus, Poseidon obstructs the hero's return, Zeus arbitrates fate in the divine council. This page catalogues all immortal entries, organised by divine category.

The Greek pantheon in Homer operates through a network of alliances and rivalries. Athena and Poseidon represent the central divine tension of the *Odyssey* — wisdom versus wrath, civilisation versus the untamed sea.
