[012-001] BOOK XII.

[012-002] ARGUMENT.

[012-003] THE SIRENE, SCYLLA, AND CHARYBDIS.

[012-004] He relates how, after his return from the shades, he was sent by [[circe|Circe]]

[012-005] on his voyage, by the coast of the Sirens, and by the strait of [[scylla|Scylla]]

[012-006] and [[charybdis|Charybdis]]: the manner in which he escaped those dangers: how, being

[012-007] cast on the island [[the-cattle-of-the-sun|Trinacria]], his companions destroyed the oxen of the

[012-008] Sun: the vengeance that followed; how all perished by shipwreck except

[012-009] himself, who, swimming on the mast of the ship, arrived on the island

[012-010] of [[calypso|Calypso]]. With which his narration concludes.

[012-011] “Thus o’er the rolling surge the vessel flies,

[012-012] Till from the waves the [[circes-island|Ææa]]n hills arise.

[012-013] Here the gay Morn resides in radiant bowers,

[012-014] Here keeps her revels with the dancing Hours;

[012-015] Here Phœbus, rising in the ethereal way,

[012-016] Through heaven’s bright portals pours the beamy day.

[012-017] At once we fix our halsers on the land.

[012-018] At once descend, and press the desert sand:

[012-019] There, worn and wasted, lose our cares in sleep,

[012-020] To the hoarse murmurs of the rolling deep.

[012-021] “Soon as the morn restored the day, we paid

[012-022] Sepulchral honours to [[elpenor|Elpenor]]’s shade.

[012-023] Now by the axe the rushing forest bends,

[012-024] And the huge pile along the shore ascends.

[012-025] Around we stand, a melancholy train,

[012-026] And a loud groan re-echoes from the main.

[012-027] Fierce o’er the pyre, by fanning breezes spread,

[012-028] The hungry flames devour the silent dead.

[012-029] A rising tomb, the silent dead to grace,

[012-030] Fast by the roarings of the main we place;

[012-031] The rising tomb a lofty column bore,

[012-032] And high above it rose the tapering oar.

[012-033] “Meantime the goddess our return survey’d

[012-034] From the pale ghosts and hell’s tremendous shade.

[012-035] Swift she descends: a train of nymphs divine

[012-036] Bear the rich viands and the generous wine:

[012-037] In act to speak the power of magic stands,

[012-038] And graceful thus accosts the listening bands;

[012-039] “‘O sons of woe? decreed by adverse fates

[012-040] Alive to pass through hell’s eternal gates!

[012-041] All, soon or late, are doom’d that path to tread;

[012-042] More wretched you! twice number’d with the dead!

[012-043] This day adjourn your cares, exalt your souls,

[012-044] Indulge the taste, and drain the sparkling bowls;

[012-045] And when the morn unveils her saffron ray,

[012-046] Spread your broad sails, and plough the liquid way:

[012-047] Lo, I this night, your faithful guide, explain

[012-048] Your woes by land, your dangers on the main.’

[012-049] “The goddess spoke. In feasts we waste the day,

[012-050] Till Phœbus downward plunged his burning ray;

[012-051] Then sable night ascends, and balmy rest

[012-052] Seals every eye, and calms the troubled breast.

[012-053] Then curious she commands me to relate

[012-054] The dreadful scenes of Pluto’s dreary state.

[012-055] She sat in silence while the tale I tell,

[012-056] The wondrous visions and the laws of hell.

[012-057] “Then thus: ‘The lot of man the gods dispose;

[012-058] These ills are past: now hear thy future woes

[012-059] O prince attend; some favouring power be kind,

[012-060] And print the important story on thy mind!

[012-061] “‘Next, where the Sirens dwell, you plough the seas;

[012-062] Their song is death, and makes destruction please.

[012-063] Unblest the man, whom music wins to stay

[012-064] Nigh the cursed shore and listen to the lay.

[012-065] No more that wretch shall view the joys of life

[012-066] His blooming offspring, or his beauteous wife!

[012-067] In verdant meads they sport; and wide around

[012-068] Lie human bones that whiten all the ground:

[012-069] The ground polluted floats with human gore,

[012-070] And human carnage taints the dreadful shore

[012-071] Fly swift the dangerous coast: let every ear

[012-072] Be stopp’d against the song! ’tis death to hear!

[012-073] Firm to the mast with chains thyself be bound,

[012-074] Nor trust thy virtue to the enchanting sound.

[012-075] If, mad with transport, freedom thou demand,

[012-076] Be every fetter strain’d, and added band to band.

[012-077] “‘These seas o’erpass’d, be wise! but I refrain

[012-078] To mark distinct thy voyage o’er the main:

[012-079] New horrors rise! let prudence be thy guide,

[012-080] And guard thy various passage through the tide.

[012-081] “‘High o’er the main two rocks exalt their brow,’

[012-082] The boiling billows thundering roll below;

[012-083] Through the vast waves the dreadful wonders move,

[012-084] Hence named Erratic by the gods above.

[012-085] No bird of air, no dove of swiftest wing,

[012-086] That bears ambrosia to the ethereal king,

[012-087] Shuns the dire rocks: in vain she cuts the skies;

[012-088] The dire rocks meet, and crush her as she flies:

[012-089] Not the fleet bark, when prosperous breezes play,

[012-090] Ploughs o’er that roaring surge its desperate way;

[012-091] O’erwhelm’d it sinks: while round a smoke expires,

[012-092] And the waves flashing seem to burn with fires.

[012-093] Scarce the famed [[argo|Argo]] pass’d these raging floods,

[012-094] The sacred Argo, fill’d with demigods!

[012-095] E’en she had sunk, but [[zeus|Jove]]’s imperial bride

[012-096] Wing’d her fleet sail, and push’d her o’er the tide.

[012-097] “‘High in the air the rock its summit shrouds

[012-098] In brooding tempests, and in rolling clouds;

[012-099] Loud storms around, and mists eternal rise,

[012-100] Beat its bleak brow, and intercept the skies.

[012-101] When all the broad expansion, bright with day,

[012-102] Glows with the autumnal or the summer ray,

[012-103] The summer and the autumn glow in vain,

[012-104] The sky for ever lowers, for ever clouds remain.

[012-105] Impervious to the step of man it stands,

[012-106] Though borne by twenty feet, though arm’d with twenty hands;

[012-107] Smooth as the polish of the mirror rise

[012-108] The slippery sides, and shoot into the skies.

[012-109] Full in the centre of this rock display’d,

[012-110] A yawning cavern casts a dreadful shade:

[012-111] Nor the fleet arrow from the twanging bow,

[012-112] Sent with full force, could reach the depth below.

[012-113] Wide to the west the horrid gulf extends,

[012-114] And the dire passage down to hell descends.

[012-115] O fly the dreadful sight! expand thy sails,

[012-116] Ply the strong oar, and catch the nimble gales;

[012-117] Here Scylla bellows from the dire abodes,

[012-118] Tremendous pest, abhorr’d by man and gods!

[012-119] Hideous her voice, and with less terrors roar

[012-120] The whelps of lions in the midnight hour.

[012-121] Twelve feet, deform’d and foul, the fiend dispreads;

[012-122] Six horrid necks she rears, and six terrific heads;

[012-123] Her jaws grin dreadful with three rows of teeth;

[012-124] Jaggy they stand, the gaping den of death;

[012-125] Her parts obscene the raging billows hide;

[012-126] Her bosom terribly o’erlooks the tide.

[012-127] When stung with hunger she embroils the flood,

[012-128] The sea-dog and the dolphin are her food;

[012-129] She makes the huge leviathan her prey,

[012-130] And all the monsters of the watery way;

[012-131] The swiftest racer of the azure plain

[012-132] Here fills her sails, and spreads her oars in vain;

[012-133] Fell Scylla rises, in her fury roars,

[012-134] At once six mouths expands, at once six men devours.

[012-135] “‘Close by, a rock of less enormous height

[012-136] Breaks the wild waves, and forms a dangerous strait;

[012-137] Full on its crown a fig’s green branches rise,

[012-138] And shoot a leafy forest to the skies;

[012-139] Beneath, Charybdis holds her boisterous reign

[012-140] ’Midst roaring whirlpools, and absorbs the main;

[012-141] Thrice in her gulfs the boiling seas subside,

[012-142] Thrice in dire thunders she refunds the tide.

[012-143] Oh, if thy vessel plough the direful waves,

[012-144] When seas retreating roar within her caves,

[012-145] Ye perish all! though he who rules the main

[012-146] Lends his strong aid, his aid he lends in vain.

[012-147] Ah, shun the horrid gulf! by Scylla fly.

[012-148] ’Tis better six to lose, than all to die.’

[012-149] “I then: ‘O nymph propitious to my prayer,

[012-150] Goddess divine, my guardian power, declare,

[012-151] Is the foul fiend from human vengeance freed?

[012-152] Or, if I rise in arms, can Scylla bleed?’

[012-153] “Then she: ‘O worn by toils, O broke in fight,

[012-154] Still are new toils and war thy dire delight?

[012-155] Will martial flames for ever fire thy mind,

[012-156] And never, never be to Heaven resign’d?

[012-157] How vain thy efforts to avenge the wrong!

[012-158] Deathless the pest! impenetrably strong!

[012-159] Furious and fell, tremendous to behold!

[012-160] E’en with a look she withers all the bold!

[012-161] She mocks the weak attempts of human might;

[012-162] Oh, fly her rage! thy conquest is thy flight.

[012-163] If but to seize thy arms thou make delay,

[012-164] Again thy fury vindicates her prey;

[012-165] Her six mouths yawn, and six are snatch’d away.

[012-166] From her foul wound Crataeis gave to air

[012-167] This dreadful pest! To her direct thy prayer,

[012-168] To curb the monster in her dire abodes,

[012-169] And guard thee through the tumult of the floods.

[012-170] Thence to [[thrinacia|Trinacria]]’s shore you bend your way,

[012-171] Where graze thy herds, illustrious source of day!

[012-172] Seven herds, seven flocks enrich the sacred plains,

[012-173] Each herd, each flock full fifty heads contains;

[012-174] The wondrous kind a length of age survey,

[012-175] By breed increase not, nor by death decay.

[012-176] Two sister goddesses possess the plain,

[012-177] The constant guardian of the woolly train;

[012-178] Lampetie fair, and Phaethusa young,

[012-179] From Phœbus and the bright Neæra sprung;

[012-180] Here, watchful o’er the flocks, in shady bowers

[012-181] And flowery meads, they waste the joyous hours.

[012-182] Rob not the gods! and so propitious gales

[012-183] Attend thy voyage, and impel thy sails;

[012-184] But if thy impious hands the flocks destroy,

[012-185] The gods, the gods avenge it, and ye die!

[012-186] ’Tis thine alone (thy friends and navy lost)

[012-187] Through tedious toils to view thy native coast.’

[012-188] She ceased: and now arose the morning ray;

[012-189] Swift to her dome the goddess held her way.

[012-190] Then to my mates I measured back the plain,

[012-191] Climb’d the tall bark, and rush’d into the main;

[012-192] Then, bending to the stroke, their oars they drew

[012-193] To their broad breasts, and swift the galley flew.

[012-194] Up sprung a brisker breeze; with freshening gales

[012-195] The friendly goddess stretch’d the swelling sails;

[012-196] We drop our oars; at ease the pilot guides;

[012-197] The vessel light along the level glides.

[012-198] When, rising sad and slow, with pensive look,

[012-199] Thus to the melancholy train I spoke:

[012-200] “‘O friends, oh ever partners of my woes,

[012-201] Attend while I what Heaven foredooms disclose.

[012-202] Hear all! [[moira|Fate]] hangs o’er all; on you it lies

[012-203] To live or perish! to be safe, be wise!

[012-204] “‘In flowery meads the sportive Sirens play,

[012-205] Touch the soft lyre, and tune the vocal lay;

[012-206] Me, me alone, with fetters firmly bound,

[012-207] The gods allow to hear the dangerous sound.

[012-208] Hear and obey; if freedom I demand,

[012-209] Be every fetter strain’d, be added band to band.’

[012-210] “While yet I speak the winged galley flies,

[012-211] And lo! the Siren shores like mists arise.

[012-212] Sunk were at once the winds; the air above,

[012-213] And waves below, at once forgot to move;

[012-214] Some demon calm’d the air and smooth’d the deep,

[012-215] Hush’d the loud winds, and charm’d the waves to sleep.

[012-216] Now every sail we furl, each oar we ply;

[012-217] Lash’d by the stroke, the frothy waters fly.

[012-218] The ductile wax with busy hands I mould,

[012-219] And cleft in fragments, and the fragments roll’d;

[012-220] The aërial region now grew warm with day,

[012-221] The wax dissolved beneath the burning ray;

[012-222] Then every ear I barr’d against the strain,

[012-223] And from access of frenzy lock’d the brain.

[012-224] Now round the masts my mates the fetters roll’d,

[012-225] And bound me limb by limb with fold on fold.

[012-226] Then bending to the stroke, the active train

[012-227] Plunge all at once their oars, and cleave the main.

[012-228] “While to the shore the rapid vessel flies,

[012-229] Our swift approach the Siren choir descries;

[012-230] Celestial music warbles from their tongue,

[012-231] And thus the sweet deluders tune the song:

[012-232] “‘Oh stay, O pride of Greece! [[odysseus|Ulysses]], stay!

[012-233] Oh cease thy course, and listen to our lay!

[012-234] Blest is the man ordain’d our voice to hear,

[012-235] The song instructs the soul, and charms the ear.

[012-236] Approach! thy soul shall into raptures rise!

[012-237] Approach! and learn new wisdom from the wise!

[012-238] We know whate’er the kings of mighty name

[012-239] Achieved at [[troy|Ilion]] in the field of fame;

[012-240] Whate’er beneath the sun’s bright journey lies.

[012-241] Oh stay, and learn new wisdom from the wise!’

[012-242] “Thus the sweet charmers warbled o’er the main;

[012-243] My soul takes wing to meet the heavenly strain;

[012-244] I give the sign, and struggle to be free;

[012-245] Swift row my mates, and shoot along the sea;

[012-246] New chains they add, and rapid urge the way,

[012-247] Till, dying off, the distant sounds decay;

[012-248] Then scudding swiftly from the dangerous ground,

[012-249] The deafen’d ear unlock’d, the chains unbound.

[012-250] “Now all at once tremendous scenes unfold;

[012-251] Thunder’d the deeps, the smoky billows roll’d!

[012-252] Tumultuous waves embroil the bellowing flood,

[012-253] All trembling, deafen’d, and aghast we stood!

[012-254] No more the vessel plough’d the dreadful wave,

[012-255] Fear seized the mighty, and unnerved the brave;

[012-256] Each dropp’d his oar; but swift from man to man

[012-257] With looks serene I turn’d, and thus began:

[012-258] ‘O friends! O often tried in adverse storms!

[012-259] With ills familiar in more dreadful forms!

[012-260] Deep in the dire Cyclopean den you lay,

[012-261] Yet safe return’d—Ulysses led the way.

[012-262] Learn courage hence, and in my care confide;

[012-263] Lo! still the same Ulysses is your guide.

[012-264] Attend my words! your oars incessant ply;

[012-265] Strain every nerve, and bid the vessel fly.

[012-266] If from yon jostling rocks and wavy war

[012-267] Jove safety grants, he grants it to your care.

[012-268] And thou, whose guiding hand directs our way,

[012-269] Pilot, attentive listen and obey!

[012-270] Bear wide thy course, nor plough those angry waves

[012-271] Where rolls yon smoke, yon tumbling ocean raves;

[012-272] Steer by the higher rock; lest whirl’d around

[012-273] We sink, beneath the circling eddy drown’d.’

[012-274] While yet I speak, at once their oars they seize,

[012-275] Stretch to the stroke, and brush the working seas.

[012-276] Cautious the name of Scylla I suppress’d;

[012-277] That dreadful sound had chill’d the boldest breast.

[012-278] “Meantime, forgetful of the voice divine,

[012-279] All dreadful bright my limbs in armour shine;

[012-280] High on the deck I take my dangerous stand,

[012-281] Two glittering javelins lighten in my hand;

[012-282] Prepared to whirl the whizzing spear I stay,

[012-283] Till the fell fiend arise to seize her prey.

[012-284] Around the dungeon, studious to behold

[012-285] The hideous pest, my labouring eyes I roll’d;

[012-286] In vain! the dismal dungeon, dark as night,

[012-287] Veils the dire monster, and confounds the sight.

[012-288] “Now through the rocks, appall’d with deep dismay,

[012-289] We bend our course, and stem the desperate way;

[012-290] Dire Scylla there a scene of horror forms,

[012-291] And here Charybdis fills the deep with storms.

[012-292] When the tide rushes from her rumbling caves,

[012-293] The rough rock roars, tumultuous boil the waves;

[012-294] They toss, they foam, a wild confusion raise,

[012-295] Like waters bubbling o’er the fiery blaze;

[012-296] Eternal mists obscure the aërial plain,

[012-297] And high above the rock she spouts the main;

[012-298] When in her gulfs the rushing sea subsides,

[012-299] She drains the ocean with the refluent tides;

[012-300] The rock re-bellows with a thundering sound;

[012-301] Deep, wondrous deep, below appears the ground.

[012-302] “Struck with despair, with trembling hearts we view’d

[012-304] When lo! fierce Scylla stoop’d to seize her prey,

[012-305] Stretch’d her dire jaws, and swept six men away.

[012-316] In the wide dungeon she devours her food,

[012-319] Never, I never scene so dire survey’d!

[012-320] My shivering blood, congeal’d, forgot to flow;

[012-321] Aghast I stood, a monument of woe!

[012-323] And the hoarse din like distant thunder dies;

[012-324] To [[helios|Sol]]’s bright isle our voyage we pursue,

[012-325] And now the glittering mountains rise to view.

[012-332] Warn’d by their awful voice these shores to shun,

[012-334] “‘O friends! O ever exercised in care!

[012-335] Hear Heaven’s commands, and reverence what ye hear!

[012-336] To fly these shores the prescient Theban shade

[012-338] Some mighty woe relentless Heaven forebodes:

[012-339] Fly these dire regions, and revere the gods!’

[012-341] Through every breast, and spread from man to man,

[012-342] Till wrathful thus [[eurylochus|Eurylochus]] began:

[012-389] “There while the wild winds whistled o’er the main,

[012-390] Thus careful I address’d the listening train:

[012-391] “‘O friends, be wise! nor dare the flocks destroy

[012-392] Of these fair pastures: if ye touch, ye die.

[012-394] Holy the flocks, and dreadful is the god!

[012-432] And verdant leaves the flowery cake supplied.

[012-433] “With prayer they now address the ethereal train,

[012-434] Slay the selected beeves, and flay the slain;

[012-435] The thighs, with fat involved, divide with art,

[012-436] Strew’d o’er with morsels cut from every part.

[012-437] Water, instead of wine, is brought in urns,

[012-438] And pour’d profanely as the victim burns.

[012-439] The thighs thus offer’d, and the entrails dress’d,

[012-440] They roast the fragments, and prepare the feast.

[012-442] Back to the bark I speed along the main.

[012-443] When lo! an odour from the feast exhales,

[012-444] Spreads o’er the coast and scents the tainted gales;

[012-445] A chilly fear congeal’d my vital blood,

[012-446] And thus, obtesting Heaven, I mourn’d aloud;

[012-447] “‘O sire of men and gods, immortal Jove!
