[009-002] ARGUMENT.

[009-003] THE ADVENTURES OF THE CICONS, LOTOPHAGI AND CYCLOPS.

[009-004] [[odysseus|Ulysses]] begins the relation of his adventures: how, after the

[009-005] destruction of [[troy|Troy]], he with his companions made an incursion on the

[009-006] Cicons, by whom they were repulsed; and, meeting with a storm, were

[009-007] driven to the coast of the [[the-lotus-eaters|Lotophagi]]. From there they sailed to the

[009-008] land of [[polyphemus|the Cyclops]], whose manners and situation are particularly

[009-009] characterised. The giant Polyphemus and his cave described; the usage

[009-010] Ulysses and his companions met with there; and, lastly, the method and

[009-011] artifice by which he escaped.

[009-012] Then thus Ulysses: “Thou whom first in sway,

[009-013] As first in virtue, these thy realms obey;

[009-014] How sweet the products of a peaceful reign!

[009-015] The heaven-taught poet and enchanting strain;

[009-016] The well-filled palace, the perpetual feast,

[009-017] A land rejoicing, and a people bless’d!

[009-018] How goodly seems it ever to employ

[009-019] Man’s social days in union and in joy;

[009-020] The plenteous hoard high-heap’d with cates divine,

[009-021] And o’er the foaming bowl the laughing wine!

[009-022] “Amid these joys, why seeks thy mind to know

[009-023] The unhappy series of a wanderer’s woe?

[009-024] Rememberance sad, whose image to review,

[009-025] Alas, I must open all my wounds anew!

[009-026] And oh, what first, what last shall I relate,

[009-027] Of woes unnumbered sent by Heaven and [[moira|Fate]]?

[009-028] “Know first the man (though now a wretch distress’d)

[009-029] Who hopes thee, monarch, for his future guest.

[009-030] Behold Ulysses! no ignoble name,

[009-031] Earth sounds my wisdom and high heaven my fame.

[009-032] “My native soil is [[ithaca|Ithaca]] the fair,

[009-033] Where high [[the-mountain-of-neriton|Neritus]] waves his woods in air;

[009-034] [[dulichium|Dulichium]], [[same|Same]] and Zaccynthus crown’d

[009-035] With shady mountains spread their isles around.

[009-036] (These to the north and night’s dark regions run,

[009-037] Those to Aurora and the rising sun).

[009-038] Low lies our isle, yet bless’d in fruitful stores;

[009-039] Strong are her sons, though rocky are her shores;

[009-040] And none, ah none no lovely to my sight,

[009-041] Of all the lands that heaven o’erspreads with light.

[009-042] In vain [[calypso|Calypso]] long constrained my stay,

[009-043] With sweet, reluctant, amorous delay;

[009-044] With all her charms as vainly [[circe|Circe]] strove,

[009-045] And added magic to secure my love.

[009-046] In pomps or joys, the palace or the grot,

[009-047] My country’s image never was forgot;

[009-048] My absent parents rose before my sight,

[009-049] And distant lay contentment and delight.

[009-050] “Hear, then, the woes which mighty [[zeus|Jove]] ordain’d

[009-051] To wait my passage from the Trojan land.

[009-052] The winds from Ilion to the Cicons’ shore,

[009-053] Beneath cold [[ismarus|Ismarus]] our vessels bore.

[009-054] We boldly landed on the hostile place,

[009-055] And sack’d the city, and destroy’d the race,

[009-056] Their wives made captive, their possessions shared,

[009-057] And every soldier found a like reward

[009-058] I then advised to fly; not so the rest,

[009-059] Who stay’d to revel, and prolong the feast:

[009-060] The fatted sheep and sable bulls they slay,

[009-061] And bowls flow round, and riot wastes the day.

[009-062] Meantime the Cicons, to their holds retired,

[009-063] Call on the Cicons, with new fury fired;

[009-064] With early morn the gather’d country swarms,

[009-065] And all the continent is bright with arms;

[009-066] Thick as the budding leaves or rising flowers

[009-067] O’erspread the land, when spring descends in showers:

[009-068] All expert soldiers, skill’d on foot to dare,

[009-069] Or from the bounding courser urge the war.

[009-070] Now fortune changes (so the Fates ordain);

[009-071] Our hour was come to taste our share of pain.

[009-072] Close at the ships the bloody fight began,

[009-073] Wounded they wound, and man expires on man.

[009-074] Long as the morning sun increasing bright

[009-075] O’er heaven’s pure azure spreads the glowing light,

[009-076] Promiscuous death the form of war confounds,

[009-077] Each adverse battle gored with equal wounds;

[009-078] But when his evening wheels o’erhung the main,

[009-079] Then conquest crown’d the fierce Ciconian train.

[009-080] Six brave companions from each ship we lost,

[009-081] The rest escape in haste, and quit the coast,

[009-082] With sails outspread we fly the unequal strife,

[009-083] Sad for their loss, but joyful of our life.

[009-084] Yet as we fled, our fellows’ rites we paid,

[009-085] And thrice we call’d on each unhappy shade,

[009-086] “Meanwhile the god, whose hand the thunder forms,

[009-087] Drives clouds on clouds, and blackens heaven with storms:

[009-088] Wide o’er the waste the rage of Boreas sweeps,

[009-089] And night rush’d headlong on the shaded deeps.

[009-090] Now here, now there, the giddy ships are borne,

[009-091] And all the rattling shrouds in fragments torn.

[009-092] We furl’d the sail, we plied the labouring oar,

[009-093] Took down our masts, and row’d our ships to shore.

[009-094] Two tedious days and two long nights we lay,

[009-095] O’erwatch’d and batter’d in the naked bay.

[009-096] But the third morning when Aurora brings,

[009-097] We rear the masts, we spread the canvas wings;

[009-098] Refresh’d and careless on the deck reclined,

[009-099] We sit, and trust the pilot and the wind.

[009-100] Then to my native country had I sail’d:

[009-101] But, the cape doubled, adverse winds prevail’d.

[009-102] Strong was the tide, which by the northern blast

[009-103] Impell’d, our vessels on Cythera cast,

[009-104] Nine days our fleet the uncertain tempest bore

[009-105] Far in wide ocean, and from sight of shore:

[009-106] The tenth we touch’d, by various errors toss’d,

[009-107] The land of Lotus and the flowery coast.

[009-108] We climb’d the beach, and springs of water found,

[009-109] Then spread our hasty banquet on the ground.

[009-110] Three men were sent, deputed from the crew

[009-111] (A herald one) the dubious coast to view,

[009-112] And learn what habitants possess’d the place.

[009-113] They went, and found a hospitable race:

[009-114] Not prone to ill, nor strange to foreign guest,

[009-115] They eat, they drink, and nature gives the feast

[009-116] The trees around them all their food produce:

[009-117] Lotus the name: divine, nectareous juice!

[009-118] (Thence call’d Lotophagi); which whose tastes,

[009-119] Insatiate riots in the sweet repasts,

[009-120] Nor other home, nor other care intends,

[009-121] But quits his house, his country, and his friends.

[009-122] The three we sent, from off the enchanting ground

[009-123] We dragg’d reluctant, and by force we bound.

[009-124] The rest in haste forsook the pleasing shore,

[009-125] Or, the charm tasted, had return’d no more.

[009-126] Now placed in order on their banks, they sweep

[009-127] The sea’s smooth face, and cleave the hoary deep:

[009-128] With heavy hearts we labour through the tide,

[009-129] To coasts unknown, and oceans yet untried.

[009-130] “The land of Cyclops first, a savage kind,

[009-131] Nor tamed by manners, nor by laws confined:

[009-132] Untaught to plant, to turn the glebe, and sow,

[009-133] They all their products to free nature owe:

[009-134] The soil, untill’d, a ready harvest yields,

[009-135] With wheat and barley wave the golden fields;

[009-136] Spontaneous wines from weighty clusters pour,

[009-137] And Jove descends in each prolific shower,

[009-138] By these no statues and no rights are known,

[009-139] No council held, no monarch fills the throne;

[009-140] But high on hills, or airy cliffs, they dwell,

[009-141] Or deep in caves whose entrance leads to hell.

[009-142] Each rules his race, his neighbour not his care,

[009-143] Heedless of others, to his own severe.

[009-144] “Opposed to the Cyclopean coast, there lay

[009-145] An isle, whose hill their subject fields survey;

[009-146] Its name Lachaea, crown’d with many a grove,

[009-147] Where savage goats through pathless thickets rove:

[009-148] No needy mortals here, with hunger bold,

[009-149] Or wretched hunters through the wintry cold

[009-150] Pursue their flight; but leave them safe to bound

[009-151] From hill to hill, o’er all the desert ground.

[009-152] Nor knows the soil to feed the fleecy care,

[009-153] Or feels the labours of the crooked share;

[009-154] But uninhabited, untill’d, unsown,

[009-155] It lies, and breeds the bleating goat alone.

[009-156] For there no vessel with vermilion prore,

[009-157] Or bark of traffic, glides from shore to shore;

[009-158] The rugged race of savages, unskill’d

[009-159] The seas to traverse, or the ships to build,

[009-160] Gaze on the coast, nor cultivate the soil,

[009-161] Unlearn’d in all the industrious art of toil,

[009-162] Yet here all products and all plants abound,

[009-163] Sprung from the fruitful genius of the ground;

[009-164] Fields waving high with heavy crops are seen,

[009-165] And vines that flourish in eternal green,

[009-166] Refreshing meads along the murmuring main,

[009-167] And fountains streaming down the fruitful plain.

[009-168] “A port there is, inclosed on either side,

[009-169] Where ships may rest, unanchor’d and untied;

[009-170] Till the glad mariners incline to sail,

[009-171] And the sea whitens with the rising gale,

[009-172] High at the head, from out the cavern’d rock,

[009-173] In living rills a gushing fountain broke:

[009-174] Around it, and above, for ever green,

[009-175] The busy alders form’d a shady scene;

[009-176] Hither some favouring god, beyond our thought,

[009-177] Through all surrounding shade our navy brought;

[009-178] For gloomy night descended on the main,

[009-179] Nor glimmer’d Phoebe in the ethereal plain:

[009-180] But all unseen the clouded island lay,

[009-181] And all unseen the surge and rolling sea,

[009-182] Till safe we anchor’d in the shelter’d bay:

[009-183] Our sails we gather’d, cast our cables o’er,

[009-184] And slept secure along the sandy shore.

[009-185] Soon as again the rosy morning shone,

[009-186] Reveal’d the landscape and the scene unknown,

[009-187] With wonder seized, we view the pleasing ground,

[009-188] And walk delighted, and expatiate round.

[009-189] Roused by the woodland nymphs at early dawn,

[009-190] The mountain goats came bounding o’er the lawn:

[009-191] In haste our fellows to the ships repair,

[009-192] For arms and weapons of the sylvan war;

[009-193] Straight in three squadrons all our crew we part,

[009-194] And bend the bow, or wing the missile dart;

[009-195] The bounteous gods afford a copious prey,

[009-196] And nine fat goats each vessel bears away:

[009-197] The royal bark had ten. Our ships complete

[009-198] We thus supplied (for twelve were all the fleet).

[009-199] “Here, till the setting sun roll’d down the light,

[009-200] We sat indulging in the genial rite:

[009-201] Nor wines were wanting; those from ample jars

[009-202] We drain’d, the prize of our Ciconian wars.

[009-203] The land of Cyclops lay in prospect near:

[009-204] The voice of goats and bleating flocks we hear,

[009-205] And from their mountains rising smokes appear.

[009-206] Now sunk the sun, and darkness cover’d o’er

[009-207] The face of things: along the sea-beat shore

[009-208] Satiate we slept: but, when the sacred dawn

[009-209] Arising glitter’d o’er the dewy lawn,

[009-210] I call’d my fellows, and these words address’d

[009-211] ‘My dear associates, here indulge your rest;

[009-212] While, with my single ship, adventurous, I

[009-213] Go forth, the manners of yon men to try;

[009-214] Whether a race unjust, of barbarous might,

[009-215] Rude and unconscious of a stranger’s right;

[009-216] Or such who harbour pity in their breast,

[009-217] Revere the gods, and succour the distress’d,’

[009-218] “This said, I climb’d my vessel’s lofty side;

[009-219] My train obey’d me, and the ship untied.

[009-220] In order seated on their banks, they sweep

[009-221] [[poseidon|Neptune]]’s smooth face, and cleave the yielding deep.

[009-222] When to the nearest verge of land we drew,

[009-223] Fast by the sea a lonely cave we view,

[009-224] High, and with darkening laurels covered o’er;

[009-225] Where sheep and goats lay slumbering round the shore:

[009-226] Near this, a fence of marble from the rock,

[009-227] Brown with o’erarching pine and spreading oak.

[009-228] A giant shepherd here his flock maintains

[009-229] Far from the rest, and solitary reigns,

[009-230] In shelter thick of horrid shade reclined;

[009-231] And gloomy mischiefs labour in his mind.

[009-232] A form enormous! far unlike the race

[009-233] Of human birth, in stature, or in face;

[009-234] As some lone mountain’s monstrous growth he stood,

[009-235] Crown’d with rough thickets, and a nodding wood.

[009-236] I left my vessel at the point of land,

[009-237] And close to guard it, gave our crew command:

[009-238] With only twelve, the boldest and the best,

[009-239] I seek the adventure, and forsake the rest.

[009-240] Then took a goatskin fill’d with precious wine,

[009-241] The gift of [[maron|Maron]] of Evantheus’ line

[009-242] (The priest of Phœbus at the Ismarian shrine).

[009-243] In sacred shade his honour’d mansion stood

[009-244] Amidst [[apollo|Apollo]]’s consecrated wood;

[009-245] Him, and his house, Heaven moved my mind to save,

[009-246] And costly presents in return he gave;

[009-247] Seven golden talents to perfection wrought,

[009-248] A silver bowl that held a copious draught,

[009-249] And twelve large vessels of unmingled wine,

[009-250] Mellifluous, undecaying, and divine!

[009-251] Which now, some ages from his race conceal’d,

[009-252] The hoary sire in gratitude reveal’d.

[009-253] Such was the wine: to quench whose fervent steam

[009-254] Scarce twenty measures from the living stream

[009-255] To cool one cup sufficed: the goblet crown’d

[009-256] Breathed aromatic fragrances around.

[009-257] Of this an ample vase we heaved aboard,

[009-258] And brought another with provisions stored.

[009-259] My soul foreboded I should find the bower

[009-260] Of some fell monster, fierce with barbarous power;

[009-261] Some rustic wretch, who lived in Heaven’s despite,

[009-262] Contemning laws, and trampling on the right.

[009-263] The cave we found, but vacant all within

[009-264] (His flock the giant tended on the green):

[009-265] But round the grot we gaze; and all we view,

[009-266] In order ranged our admiration drew:

[009-267] The bending shelves with loads of cheeses press’d,

[009-268] The folded flocks each separate from the rest

[009-269] (The larger here, and there the lesser lambs,

[009-270] The new-fallen young here bleating for their dams:

[009-271] The kid distinguish’d from the lambkin lies);

[009-272] The cavern echoes with responsive cries.

[009-273] Capacious chargers all around were laid.

[009-274] Full pails, and vessels of the milking trade.

[009-275] With fresh provisions hence our fleet to store

[009-276] My friends advise me, and to quit the shore.

[009-277] Or drive a flock of sheep and goats away,

[009-278] Consult our safety, and put off to sea.

[009-279] Their wholesome counsel rashly I declined,

[009-280] Curious to view the man of monstrous kind,

[009-281] And try what social rites a savage lends:

[009-282] Dire rites, alas! and fatal to my friends

[009-283] “Then first a fire we kindle, and prepare

[009-284] For his return with sacrifice and prayer;

[009-285] The loaden shelves afford us full repast;

[009-286] We sit expecting. Lo! he comes at last,

[009-287] Near half a forest on his back he bore,

[009-288] And cast the ponderous burden at the door.

[009-289] It thunder’d as it fell. We trembled then,

[009-290] And sought the deep recesses of the den.

[009-291] New driven before him through the arching rock,

[009-292] Came tumbling, heaps on heaps, the unnumber’d flock.

[009-293] Big-udder’d ewes, and goats of female kind

[009-294] (The males were penn’d in outward courts behind);

[009-295] Then, heaved on high, a rock’s enormous weight

[009-296] To the cave’s mouth he roll’d, and closed the gate

[009-297] (Scarce twenty four-wheel’d cars, compact and strong,

[009-298] The massy load could bear, or roll along).

[009-299] He next betakes him to his evening cares,

[009-300] And, sitting down, to milk his flocks prepares;

[009-301] Of half their udders eases first the dams,

[009-302] Then to the mother’s teat submits the lambs;

[009-303] Half the white stream to hardening cheese be press’d,

[009-304] And high in wicker-baskets heap’d: the rest,

[009-305] Reserved in bowls, supplied his nightly feast.

[009-306] His labour done, he fired the pile, that gave

[009-307] A sudden blaze, and lighted all the cave.

[009-308] We stand discover’d by the rising fires;

[009-309] Askance the giant glares, and thus inquires:

[009-310] “‘What are ye, guests? on what adventure, say,

[009-311] Thus far ye wander through the watery way?

[009-312] Pirates perhaps, who seek through seas unknown

[009-313] The lives of others, and expose your own?’

[009-314] “His voice like thunder through the cavern sounds;

[009-315] My bold companions thrilling fear confounds,

[009-316] Appall’d at sight of more than mortal man!

[009-317] At length, with heart recover’d, I began:

[009-318] “‘From Troy’s famed fields, sad wanderers o’er the main,

[009-319] Behold the relics of the Grecian train:

[009-320] Through various seas, by various perils toss’d,

[009-321] And forced by storms, unwilling on your coast;

[009-322] Far from our destined course and native land,

[009-323] Such was our fate, and such high Jove’s command!

[009-324] Nor what we are befits us to disclaim,

[009-325] [[agamemnon|Atrides]]’ friends (in arms a mighty name),

[009-326] Who taught proud Troy and all her sons to bow;

[009-327] Victors of late, but humble suppliants now!

[009-328] Low at thy knee thy succour we implore;

[009-329] Respect us, human, and relieve us, poor.

[009-330] At least, some hospitable gift bestow;

[009-331] ’Tis what the happy to the unhappy owe;

[009-332] ’Tis what the gods require: those gods revere;

[009-333] The poor and stranger are their constant care;

[009-334] To Jove their cause, and their revenge belongs,

[009-335] He wanders with them, and he feels their wrongs.”

[009-336] “‘Fools that ye are (the savage thus replies,

[009-337] His inward fury blazing at his eyes),

[009-338] Or strangers, distant far from our abodes,

[009-339] To bid me reverence or regard the gods.

[009-340] Know then, we Cyclops are a race above

[009-341] Those air-bred people, and their goat-nursed Jove;

[009-342] And learn, our power proceeds with thee and thine,

[009-343] Not as he wills, but as ourselves incline.

[009-344] But answer, the good ship that brought ye o’er,

[009-345] Where lies she anchor’d? near or off the shore?’

[009-346] “Thus he. His meditated fraud I find

[009-347] (Versed in the turns of various human-kind):

[009-348] And, cautious thus: ‘Against a dreadful rock,

[009-349] Fast by your shore the gallant vessel broke.

[009-350] Scarce with these few I ’scaped; of all my train,

[009-351] Whom angry Neptune, whelm’d beneath the main,

[009-352] The scattered wreck the winds blew back again.’

[009-353] “He answer’d with his deed: his bloody hand

[009-354] Snatch’d two, unhappy! of my martial band;

[009-355] And dash’d like dogs against the stony floor:

[009-356] The pavement swims with brains and mingled gore.

[009-357] Torn limb from limb, he spreads his horrid feast,

[009-358] And fierce devours it like a mountain beast:

[009-359] He sucks the marrow, and the blood he drains,

[009-360] Nor entrails, flesh, nor solid bone remains.

[009-361] We see the death from which we cannot move,

[009-362] And humbled groan beneath the hand of Jove.

[009-363] His ample maw with human carnage fill’d,

[009-364] A milky deluge next the giant swill’d;

[009-365] Then stretch’d in length o’er half the cavern’d rock,

[009-366] Lay senseless, and supine, amidst the flock.

[009-367] To seize the time, and with a sudden wound

[009-368] To fix the slumbering monster to the ground,

[009-369] My soul impels me! and in act I stand

[009-370] To draw the sword; but wisdom held my hand.

[009-371] A deed so rash had finished all our fate,

[009-372] No mortal forces from the lofty gate

[009-373] Could roll the rock. In hopeless grief we lay,

[009-374] And sigh, expecting the return of day.

[009-375] Now did the rosy-fingered morn arise,

[009-376] And shed her sacred light along the skies;

[009-377] He wakes, he lights the fire, he milks the dams,

[009-378] And to the mother’s teats submits the lambs.

[009-379] The task thus finish’d of his morning hours,

[009-380] Two more he snatches, murders, and devours.

[009-381] Then pleased, and whistling, drives his flock before,

[009-382] Removes the rocky mountain from the door,

[009-383] And shuts again: with equal ease disposed,

[009-384] As a light quiver’s lid is oped and closed.

[009-385] His giant voice the echoing region fills:

[009-386] His flocks, obedient, spread o’er all the hills.

[009-387] “Thus left behind, even in the last despair

[009-388] I thought, devised, and Pallas heard my prayer.

[009-389] [[poine|Revenge]], and doubt, and caution, work’d my breast;

[009-390] But this of many counsels seem’d the best:

[009-391] The monster’s club within the cave I spied,

[009-392] A tree of stateliest growth, and yet undried,

[009-393] Green from the wood: of height and bulk so vast,

[009-394] The largest ship might claim it for a mast.

[009-395] This shorten’d of its top, I gave my train

[009-396] A fathom’s length, to shape it and to plane;

[009-397] The narrower end I sharpen’d to a spire,

[009-398] Whose point we harden’d with the force of fire,

[009-399] And hid it in the dust that strew’d the cave,

[009-400] Then to my few companions, bold and brave,

[009-401] Proposed, who first the venturous deed should try,

[009-402] In the broad orbit of his monstrous eye

[009-403] To plunge the brand and twirl the pointed wood,

[009-404] When slumber next should tame the man of blood.

[009-405] Just as I wished, the lots were cast on four:

[009-406] Myself the fifth. We stand and wait the hour.

[009-407] He comes with evening: all his fleecy flock

[009-408] Before him march, and pour into the rock:

[009-409] Not one, or male or female, stayed behind

[009-410] (So fortune chanced, or so some god designed);

[009-411] Then heaving high the stone’s unwieldy weight,

[009-412] He roll’d it on the cave and closed the gate.

[009-413] First down he sits, to milk the woolly dams,

[009-414] And then permits their udder to the lambs.

[009-415] Next seized two wretches more, and headlong cast,

[009-416] Brain’d on the rock; his second dire repast.

[009-417] I then approach’d him reeking with their gore,

[009-418] And held the brimming goblet foaming o’er;

[009-419] ‘Cyclop! since human flesh has been thy feast,

[009-420] Now drain this goblet, potent to digest;

[009-421] Know hence what treasures in our ship we lost,

[009-422] And what rich liquors other climates boast.

[009-423] We to thy shore the precious freight shall bear,

[009-424] If home thou send us and vouchsafe to spare.

[009-425] But oh! thus furious, thirsting thus for gore,

[009-426] The sons of men shall ne’er approach thy shore,

[009-427] And never shalt thou taste this nectar more,’

[009-428] “He heard, he took, and pouring down his throat,

[009-429] Delighted, swill’d the large luxurious draught,

[009-430] ‘More! give me more (he cried): the boon be thine,

[009-431] Whoe’er thou art that bear’st celestial wine!

[009-432] Declare thy name: not mortal is this juice,

[009-493] Of sleep, oppress thee, or by fraud or power?

[009-497] Of sleep, oppresses me with fraudful power.’

[009-498] ‘If no man hurt thee, but the hand divine

[009-499] Inflict disease, it fits thee to resign:

[009-503] Pleased with the effect of conduct and of art.

[009-513] Each scheme I turn’d, and sharpen’d every thought;

[009-514] This way and that I cast to save my friends,
