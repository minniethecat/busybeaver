[004-001] BOOK IV.

[004-002] ARGUMENT.

[004-003] THE CONFERENCE WITH MENELAUS.

[004-004] [[telemachus|Telemachus]] with [[pisistratus|Pisistratus]] arriving at [[sparta|Sparta]], is hospitably received

[004-005] by [[menelaus|Menelaus]] to whom he relates the cause of his coming, and learns from

[004-006] him many particulars of what befell the Greeks since the destruction of

[004-007] [[troy|Troy]]. He dwells more at large upon the prophecies of [[proteus|Proteus]] to him in

[004-008] his return; from which he acquaints Telemachus that [[odysseus|Ulysses]] is detained

[004-009] in the island of [[calypso|Calypso]].

[004-010] In the meantime the suitors consult to destroy Telemachus on the

[004-011] voyage home. [[penelope|Penelope]] is apprised of this; but comforted in a dream

[004-012] by Pallas, in the shape of her sister Iphthima.

[004-013] And now proud Sparta with their wheels resounds,

[004-014] Sparta whose walls a range of hills surrounds;

[004-015] At the fair dome the rapid labour ends;

[004-016] Where sate [[agamemnon|Atrides]] ’midst his bridal friends,

[004-017] With double vows invoking Hymen’s power,

[004-018] To bless his son’s and daughter’s nuptial hour.

[004-019] That day, to [[achilles|great Achilles]]’ son resign’d,

[004-020] Hermione, the fairest of her kind,

[004-021] Was sent to crown the long-protracted joy,

[004-022] Espoused before the final doom of Troy;

[004-023] With steeds and gilded cars, a gorgeous train

[004-024] Attend the nymphs to Phthia’s distant reign.

[004-025] Meanwhile at home, to Megapenthe’s bed

[004-026] The virgin choir Alector’s daughter led.

[004-027] Brave Megapenthes from a stolen amour

[004-028] To great Atrides’ age his handmaid bore;

[004-029] To [[helen|Helen]]’s bed the gods alone assign

[004-030] Hermione, to extend the regal line;

[004-031] On whom a radiant pomp of Graces wait,

[004-032] Resembling Venus in attractive state.

[004-033] While this gay friendly troop the king surround,

[004-034] With festival and mirth the roofs resound;

[004-035] A bard amid the joyous circle sings

[004-036] High airs attemper’d to the vocal strings;

[004-037] Whilst warbling to the varied strain, advance

[004-038] Two sprightly youths to form the bounding dance,

[004-039] ’Twas then, that issuing through the palace gate,

[004-040] The splendid car roll’d slow in regal state:

[004-041] On the bright eminence young [[nestor|Nestor]] shone,

[004-042] And fast beside him great Ulysses’ son;

[004-043] Grave Eteoneus saw the pomp appear,

[004-044] And speeding, thus address’d the royal ear;

[004-045] “Two youths approach, whose semblant features prove

[004-046] Their blood devolving from the source of [[zeus|Jove]]

[004-047] Is due reception deign’d, or must they bend

[004-048] Their doubtful course to seek a distant friend?”

[004-049] “Insensate! (with a sigh the king replies,)

[004-050] Too long, misjudging, have I thought thee wise

[004-051] But sure relentless folly steels thy breast,

[004-052] Obdurate to reject the stranger-guest;

[004-053] To those dear hospitable rites a foe,

[004-054] Which in my wanderings oft relieved my woe;

[004-055] Fed by the bounty of another’s board,

[004-056] Till pitying Jove my native realm restored—

[004-057] Straight be the coursers from the car released,

[004-058] Conduct the youths to grace the genial feast.”

[004-059] The seneschal, rebuked, in haste withdrew;

[004-060] With equal haste a menial train pursue:

[004-061] Part led the coursers, from the car enlarged,

[004-062] Each to a crib with choicest grain surcharged;

[004-063] Part in a portico, profusely graced

[004-064] With rich magnificence, the chariot placed;

[004-065] Then to the dome the friendly pair invite,

[004-066] Who eye the dazzling roofs with vast delight;

[004-067] Resplendent as the blaze of summer noon,

[004-068] Or the pale radiance of the midnight moon.

[004-069] From room to room their eager view they bend

[004-070] Thence to the bath, a beauteous pile, descend;

[004-071] Where a bright damsel train attends the guests

[004-072] With liquid odours, and embroider’d vests.

[004-073] Refresh’d, they wait them to the bower of state,

[004-074] Where, circled with his peers, Atrides sate;

[004-075] Throned next the king, a fair attendant brings

[004-076] The purest product of the crystal springs;

[004-077] High on a massy vase of silver mould,

[004-078] The burnish’d laver flames with solid gold,

[004-079] In solid gold the purple vintage flows,

[004-080] And on the board a second banquet rose.

[004-081] When thus the king, with hospitable port;

[004-082] “Accept this welcome to the Spartan court:

[004-083] The waste of nature let the feast repair,

[004-084] Then your high lineage and your names declare;

[004-085] Say from what sceptred ancestry ye claim,

[004-086] Recorded eminent in deathless fame,

[004-087] For vulgar parents cannot stamp their race

[004-088] With signatures of such majestic grace.”

[004-089] Ceasing, benevolent he straight assigns

[004-090] The royal portion of the choicest chines

[004-091] To each accepted friend; with grateful haste

[004-092] They share the honours of the rich repast.

[004-093] Sufficed, soft whispering thus to Nestor’s son,

[004-094] His head reclined, young Ithacus begun:

[004-095] “View’st thou unmoved, O ever-honour’d most!

[004-096] These prodigies of art, and wondrous cost!

[004-097] Above, beneath, around the palace shines

[004-098] The sunless treasure of exhausted mines;

[004-099] The spoils of elephants the roofs inlay,

[004-100] And studded amber darts the golden ray;

[004-101] Such, and not nobler, in the realms above

[004-102] My wonder dictates is the dome of Jove.”

[004-103] The monarch took the word, and grave replied:

[004-104] “Presumptuous are the vaunts, and vain the pride

[004-105] Of man, who dares in pomp with Jove contest,

[004-106] Unchanged, immortal, and supremely blest!

[004-107] With all my affluence, when my woes are weigh’d,

[004-108] Envy will own the purchase dearly paid.

[004-109] For eight slow-circling years, by tempests toss’d,

[004-110] From Cypress to the far Phoenician coast

[004-111] (Sidon the capital), I stretch’d my toil

[004-112] Through regions fatten’d with the flows of Nile.

[004-113] Next Aethiopia’s utmost bound explore,

[004-114] And the parch’d borders of the Arabian shore;

[004-115] Then warp my voyage on the southern gales,

[004-116] O’er the warm Lybian wave to spread my sails;

[004-117] That happy clime, where each revolving year

[004-118] The teeming ewes a triple offspring bear;

[004-119] And two fair crescents of translucent horn

[004-120] The brows of all their young increase adorn:

[004-121] The shepherd swains, with sure abundance blest,

[004-122] On the fat flock and rural dainties feast;

[004-123] Nor want of herbage makes the dairy fail,

[004-124] But every season fills the foaming pail.

[004-125] Whilst, heaping unwash’d wealth, I distant roam,

[004-126] The best of brothers, at his natal home,

[004-127] By the dire fury of a traitress wife,

[004-128] Ends the sad evening of a stormy life;

[004-129] Whence, with incessant grief my soul annoy’d,

[004-130] These riches are possess’d, but not enjoy’d!

[004-131] My wars, the copious theme of every tongue,

[004-132] To you your fathers have recorded long.

[004-133] How favouring Heaven repaid my glorious toils

[004-134] With a sack’d palace, and barbaric spoils.

[004-135] Oh! had the gods so large a boon denied

[004-136] And life, the just equivalent supplied

[004-137] To those brave warriors, who, with glory fired

[004-138] Far from their country, in my cause expired!

[004-139] Still in short intervals of pleasing woe.

[004-140] Regardful of the friendly dues I owe,

[004-141] I to the glorious dead, for ever dear!

[004-142] Indulge the tribute of a grateful tear.

[004-143] But oh! Ulysses—deeper than the rest

[004-144] That sad idea wounds my anxious breast!

[004-145] My heart bleeds fresh with agonizing pain;

[004-146] The bowl and tasteful viands tempt in vain;

[004-147] Nor sleep’s soft power can close my streaming eyes,

[004-148] When imaged to my soul his sorrows rise.

[004-149] No peril in my cause he ceased to prove,

[004-150] His labours equall’d only by my love:

[004-151] And both alike to bitter fortune born,

[004-152] For him to suffer, and for me to mourn!

[004-153] Whether he wanders on some friendly coast,

[004-154] Or glides in Stygian gloom a pensive ghost,

[004-155] No fame reveals; but, doubtful of his doom,

[004-156] His good old sire with sorrow to the tomb

[004-157] Declines his trembling steps; untimely care

[004-158] Withers the blooming vigour of his heir;

[004-159] And the chaste partner of his bed and throne

[004-160] Wastes all her widow’d hours in tender moan.”

[004-161] While thus pathetic to the prince he spoke,

[004-162] From the brave youth the streaming passion broke;

[004-163] Studious to veil the grief, in vain repress’d,

[004-164] His face he shrouded with his purple vest.

[004-165] The conscious monarch pierced the coy disguise,

[004-166] And view’d his filial love with vast surprise:

[004-167] Dubious to press the tender theme, or wait

[004-168] To hear the youth inquire his father’s fate.

[004-169] In this suspense bright Helen graced the room;

[004-170] Before her breathed a gale of rich perfume.

[004-171] So moves, adorn’d with each attractive grace,

[004-172] The silver shafted goddess of the chase!

[004-173] The seat of majesty Adraste brings,

[004-174] With art illustrious, for the pomp of kings;

[004-175] To spread the pall (beneath the regal chair)

[004-176] Of softest wool, is bright Alcippe’s care.

[004-177] A silver canister, divinely wrought,

[004-178] In her soft hands the beauteous Phylo brought;

[004-179] To Sparta’s queen of old the radiant vase

[004-180] Alcandra gave, a pledge of royal grace;

[004-181] For Polybus her lord (whose sovereign sway

[004-182] The wealthy tribes of Pharian Thebes obey),

[004-183] When to that court Atrides came, caress’d

[004-184] With vast munificence the imperial guest:

[004-185] Two lavers from the richest ore refined,

[004-186] With silver tripods, the kind host assign’d;

[004-187] And bounteous from the royal treasure told

[004-188] Ten equal talents of refulgent gold.

[004-189] Alcandra, consort of his high command,

[004-190] A golden distaff gave to Helen’s hand;

[004-191] And that rich vase, with living sculpture wrought,

[004-192] Which heap’d with wool the beauteous Phylo brought

[004-193] The silken fleece, impurpled for the loom,

[004-194] Rivall’d the hyacinth in vernal bloom.

[004-195] The sovereign seat then Jove born Helen press’d,

[004-196] And pleasing thus her sceptred lord address’d:

[004-197] “Who grace our palace now, that friendly pair,

[004-198] Speak they their lineage, or their names declare?

[004-199] Uncertain of the truth, yet uncontroll’d,

[004-200] Hear me the bodings of my breast unfold.

[004-201] With wonder wrapp’d on yonder check I trace

[004-202] The feature of the Ulyssean race:

[004-203] Diffused o’er each resembling line appear,

[004-204] In just similitude, the grace and air

[004-205] Of young Telemachus! the lovely boy,

[004-206] Who bless’d Ulysses with a father’s joy,

[004-207] What time the Greeks combined their social arms,

[004-208] To avenge the stain of my ill-fated charms!”

[004-209] “Just is thy thought, (the king assenting cries,)

[004-210] Methinks Ulysses strikes my wondering eyes;

[004-211] Full shines the father in the filial frame,

[004-212] His port, his features, and his shape the same;

[004-213] Such quick regards his sparkling eyes bestow;

[004-214] Such wavy ringlets o’er his shoulders flow

[004-215] And when he heard the long disastrous store

[004-216] Of cares, which in my cause Ulysses bore;

[004-217] Dismay’d, heart-wounded with paternal woes,

[004-218] Above restraint the tide of sorrow rose;

[004-219] Cautious to let the gushing grief appear,

[004-220] His purple garment veil’d the falling tear.”

[004-221] “See there confess’d (Pisistratus replies)

[004-222] The genuine worth of Ithacus the wise!

[004-223] Of that heroic sire the youth is sprung,

[004-224] But modest awe hath chain’d his timorous tongue.

[004-225] Thy voice, O king! with pleased attention heard,

[004-226] Is like the dictates of a god revered.

[004-227] With him, at Nestor’s high command, I came,

[004-228] Whose age I honour with a parent’s name.

[004-229] By adverse destiny constrained to sue

[004-230] For counsel and redress, he sues to you

[004-231] Whatever ill the friendless orphan bears,

[004-232] Bereaved of parents in his infant years,

[004-233] Still must the wrong’d Telemachus sustain,

[004-234] If, hopeful of your aid, he hopes in vain;

[004-235] Affianced in your friendly power alone,

[004-236] The youth would vindicate the vacant throne.”

[004-237] “Is Sparta blest, and these desiring eyes

[004-238] View my friend’s son? (the king exalting cries;)

[004-239] Son of my friend, by glorious toils approved,

[004-240] Whose sword was sacred to the man he loved;

[004-241] Mirror of constant faith, revered and mourn’d—

[004-242] When Troy was ruin’d, had the chief return’d,

[004-243] No Greek an equal space had ere possess’d,

[004-244] Of dear affection, in my grateful breast.

[004-245] I, to confirm the mutual joys we shared,

[004-246] For his abode a capital prepared;

[004-247] Argos, the seat of sovereign rule, I chose;

[004-248] Fair in the plan the future palace rose,

[004-249] Where my Ulysses and his race might reign,

[004-250] And portion to his tribes the wide domain,

[004-251] To them my vassals had resign’d a soil,

[004-252] With teeming plenty to reward their toil.

[004-253] There with commutual zeal we both had strove

[004-254] In acts of dear benevolence and love:

[004-255] Brothers in peace, not rivals in command,

[004-256] And death alone dissolved the friendly band!

[004-257] Some envious power the blissful scene destroys;

[004-258] Vanish’d are all the visionary joys;

[004-259] The soul of friendship to my hope is lost,

[004-260] Fated to wander from his natal coast!”

[004-261] He ceased; a gush of grief began to rise:

[004-262] Fast streams a tide from beauteous Helen’s eyes;

[004-263] Fast for the sire the filial sorrows flow;

[004-264] The weeping monarch swells the mighty woe;

[004-265] Thy cheeks, Pisistratus, the tears bedew,

[004-266] While pictured so thy mind appear’d in view,

[004-267] Thy martial brother; on the Phrygian plain

[004-268] Extended pale, by swarthy Memnon slain!

[004-269] But silence soon the son of Nestor broke,

[004-270] And melting with fraternal pity, spoke:

[004-271] “Frequent, O king, was Nestor wont to raise

[004-272] And charm attention with thy copious praise;

[004-273] To crowd thy various gifts, the sage assign’d

[004-274] The glory of a firm capacious mind;

[004-275] With that superior attribute control

[004-276] This unavailing impotence of soul,

[004-277] Let not your roof with echoing grief resound,

[004-278] Now for the feast the friendly bowl is crown’d;

[004-279] But when, from dewy shade emerging bright,

[004-280] Aurora streaks the sky with orient light,

[004-281] Let each deplore his dead; the rites of woe

[004-282] Are all, alas! the living can bestow;

[004-283] O’er the congenial dust enjoin’d to shear

[004-284] The graceful curl, and drop the tender tear.

[004-285] Then, mingling in the mournful pomp with you,

[004-286] I’ll pay my brother’s ghost a warrior’s due,

[004-287] And mourn the brave [[antilochus|Antilochus]], a name

[004-288] Not unrecorded in the rolls of fame;

[004-289] With strength and speed superior form’d, in fight

[004-290] To face the foe, or intercept his flight;

[004-291] Too early snatch’d by fate ere known to me!

[004-292] I boast a witness of his worth in thee.”

[004-293] “Young and mature! (the monarch thus rejoins,)

[004-294] In thee renew’d the soul of Nestor shines;

[004-295] Form’d by the care of that consummate sage,

[004-296] In early bloom an oracle of age.

[004-297] Whene’er his influence Jove vouchsafes to shower,

[004-298] To bless the natal and the nuptial hour;

[004-299] From the great sire transmissive to the race,

[004-300] The boon devolving gives distinguish’d grace.

[004-301] Such, happy Nestor! was thy glorious doom,

[004-302] Around thee, full of years, thy offspring bloom.

[004-303] Expert of arms, and prudent in debate;

[004-304] The gifts of Heaven to guard thy hoary state.

[004-305] But now let each becalm his troubled breast,

[004-306] Wash, and partake serene the friendly feast.

[004-307] To move thy suit, Telemachus, delay,

[004-308] Till heaven’s revolving lamp restores the day.”

[004-309] He said, Asphalion swift the laver brings;

[004-310] Alternate, all partake the grateful springs;

[004-311] Then from the rites of purity repair,

[004-312] And with keen gust the savoury viands share.

[004-313] Meantime, with genial joy to warm the soul,

[004-314] Bright Helen mix’d a mirth inspiring bowl;

[004-315] Temper’d with drugs of sovereign use, to assuage

[004-316] The boiling bosom of tumultuous rage;

[004-317] To clear the cloudy front of wrinkled Care,

[004-318] And dry the tearful sluices of Despair;

[004-319] Charm’d with that virtuous draught, the exalted mind

[004-320] All sense of woe delivers to the wind.

[004-321] Though on the blazing pile his parent lay,

[004-322] Or a loved brother groan’d his life away,

[004-323] Or darling son, oppress’d by ruffian force,

[004-324] Fell breathless at his feet, a mangled corse;

[004-325] From morn to eve, impassive and serene,

[004-326] The man entranced would view the dreadful scene.

[004-327] These drugs, so friendly to the joys of life,

[004-328] Bright Helen learn’d from Thone’s imperial wife;

[004-329] Who sway’d the sceptre, where prolific Nile

[004-330] With various simples clothes the fatten’d soil.

[004-331] With wholesome herbage mix’d, the direful bane

[004-332] Of vegetable venom taints the plain;

[004-333] From Paeon sprung, their patron-god imparts

[004-334] To all the Pharian race his healing arts.

[004-335] The beverage now prepared to inspire the feast,

[004-336] The circle thus the beauteous queen addressed:

[004-337] “Throned in omnipotence, supremest Jove

[004-338] Tempers the fates of human race above;

[004-339] By the firm sanction of his sovereign will,

[004-340] Alternate are decreed our good and ill.

[004-341] To feastful mirth be this white hour assign’d.

[004-342] And sweet discourse, the banquet of the mind

[004-343] Myself, assisting in the social joy,

[004-344] Will tell Ulysses’ bold exploit in Troy,

[004-345] Sole witness of the deed I now declare

[004-346] Speak you (who saw) his wonders in the war.

[004-347] “Seam’d o’er with wounds, which his own sabre gave,

[004-348] In the vile habit of a village slave,

[004-349] The foe deceived, he pass’d the tented plain,

[004-350] In Troy to mingle with the hostile train.

[004-351] In this attire secure from searching eyes,

[004-352] Till happily piercing through the dark disguise,

[004-353] The chief I challenged; he, whose practised wit

[004-354] Knew all the serpent mazes of deceit,

[004-355] Eludes my search; but when his form I view’d

[004-356] Fresh from the bath, with fragrant oils renew’d,

[004-357] His limbs in military purple dress’d,

[004-358] Each brightening grace the genuine Greek confess’d.

[004-359] A previous pledge of sacred faith obtain’d,

[004-360] Till he the lines and Argive fleet regain’d,

[004-361] To keep his stay conceal’d; the chief declared

[004-362] The plans of war against the town prepared.

[004-363] Exploring then the secrets of the state,

[004-364] He learn’d what best might urge the Dardan fate;

[004-365] And, safe returning to the Grecian host,

[004-366] Sent many a shade to Pluto’s dreary coast.

[004-367] Loud grief resounded through the towers of Troy,

[004-368] But my pleased bosom glow’d with secret joy:

[004-369] For then, with dire remorse and conscious shame

[004-370] I view’d the effects of that disastrous flame,

[004-371] Which, kindled by the imperious queen of love,

[004-372] Constrain’d me from my native realm to rove:

[004-373] And oft in bitterness of soul deplored

[004-374] My absent daughter and my dearer lord;

[004-375] Admired among the first of human race,

[004-376] For every gift of mind and manly grace.”

[004-377] “[[dike|Right]] well (replied the king) your speech displays

[004-378] The matchless merit of the chief you praise:

[004-379] Heroes in various climes myself have found,

[004-380] For martial deeds and depth of thought renown’d;

[004-381] But Ithacus, unrivall’d in his claim,

[004-382] May boast a title to the loudest fame:

[004-383] In battle calm he guides the rapid storm,

[004-384] Wise to resolve, and patient to perform.

[004-385] What wondrous conduct in the chief appear’d,

[004-386] When the vast fabric of the steed we rear’d!

[004-387] Some demon, anxious for the Trojan doom,

[004-388] Urged you with great Deiphobus to come,

[004-389] To explore the fraud; with guile opposed to guile.

[004-390] Slow-pacing thrice around the insidious pile,

[004-391] Each noted leader’s name you thrice invoke,

[004-392] Your accent varying as their spouses spoke!

[004-393] The pleasing sounds each latent warrior warm’d,

[004-394] But most Tydides’ and my heart alarm’d:

[004-395] To quit the steed we both impatient press

[004-396] Threatening to answer from the dark recess.

[004-397] Unmoved the mind of Ithacus remain’d;

[004-398] And the vain ardours of our love restrain’d;

[004-399] But Anticlus, unable to control,

[004-400] Spoke loud the language of his yearning soul:

[004-401] Ulysses straight, with indignation fired

[004-402] (For so the common care of Greece required),

[004-403] Firm to his lips his forceful hands applied,

[004-404] Till on his tongue the fluttering murmurs died.

[004-405] Meantime [[athena|Minerva]], from the fraudful horse,

[004-406] Back to the court of Priam bent your course.”

[004-407] “Inclement fate! (Telemachus replies,)

[004-408] Frail is the boasted attribute of wise:

[004-409] The leader mingling with the vulgar host,

[004-410] Is in the common mass of matter lost!

[004-411] But now let sleep the painful waste repair

[004-412] Of sad reflection and corroding care.”

[004-413] He ceased; the menial fair that round her wait,

[004-414] At Helen’s beck prepare the room of state;

[004-415] Beneath an ample portico they spread

[004-416] The downy fleece to form the slumberous bed;

[004-417] And o’er soft palls of purple grain unfold

[004-418] Rich tapestry, stiff with interwoven gold:

[004-419] Then, through the illumined dome, to balmy rest

[004-420] The obsequious herald guides each princely guest;

[004-421] While to his regal bower the king ascends,

[004-422] And beauteous Helen on her lord attends.

[004-423] Soon as the morn, in orient purple dress’d,

[004-424] Unbarr’d the portal of the roseate east,

[004-425] The monarch rose; magnificent to view,

[004-426] The imperial mantle o’er his vest he threw;

[004-427] The glittering zone athwart his shoulders cast,

[004-428] A starry falchion low-depending graced;

[004-429] Clasp’d on his feet the embroidered sandals shine;

[004-430] And forth he moves, majestic and divine,

[004-431] Instant to young Telemachus he press’d;

[004-432] And thus benevolent his speech addressed:

[004-433] “Say, royal youth, sincere of soul report

[004-434] Whit cause hath led you to the Spartan court?

[004-435] Do public or domestic care constrain

[004-436] This toilsome voyage o’er the surgy main?”

[004-437] “O highly-favour’d delegate of Jove!

[004-438] (Replies the prince) inflamed with filial love,

[004-439] And anxious hope, to hear my parent’s doom,

[004-440] A suppliant to your royal court I come:
