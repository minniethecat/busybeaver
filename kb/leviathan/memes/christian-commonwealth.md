# Christian Commonwealth

The Christian Commonwealth is Hobbes' solution to the problem of divided allegiance that, in his view, caused the English Civil War. By making the civil sovereign the "Supreme Pastor," Hobbes unifies temporal and spiritual authority in a single person — the defining feature of a Christian commonwealth.

## Role in Leviathan

Hobbes develops the concept of the Christian Commonwealth in Chapter XLII and Chapter XLIII. In a Christian commonwealth, "the Civill Soveraign is Supreme Pastor, to whose charge the whole flock of his Subjects is committed" (Chapter XLII (046-024)). This means that the sovereign — not the Pope, not presbyteries, not individual congregations — determines public doctrine, appoints ministers, and regulates worship. "There is no other Government in this life, neither of State, nor Religion, but Temporall" (Chapter XLII). The Christian Commonwealth is the institutional expression of Hobbes' Erastianism — the doctrine that the state has authority over the church in all temporal matters.

## Key References

Hobbes discusses the Christian Commonwealth in Chapters XLII–XLIII. The concept is the culmination of his biblical-political argument in Parts III and IV.
