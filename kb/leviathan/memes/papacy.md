# Papacy

The Papacy — the office and jurisdiction of the Pope as head of the Roman Catholic Church — is the primary institutional target of Hobbes' polemic in Parts III and IV. Hobbes' attack on the Papacy is not merely theological but political: the Pope claims a universal jurisdiction that, if valid, would make every civil sovereign subordinate to a foreign power.

## Role in Leviathan

Hobbes argues that the Papacy is a counterfeit of the Kingdom of God — a human institution that has appropriated divine authority through "darknesse" (ignorance and deception). "The Papacy, is no other, than the Ghost of the deceased Romane Empire, sitting crowned upon the grave thereof" (Chapter XLVII). The Pope's claim to depose kings, absolve subjects of their allegiance, and exercise temporal jurisdiction is, in Hobbes' reading, the principal cause of civil war in Christendom — including the English Civil War. Hobbes' detailed refutation of Cardinal Bellarmine in Chapter XLII is the most sustained engagement with a contemporary adversary in all of *Leviathan*.

## Key References

Hobbes discusses the Papacy in Chapters XLII, XLIV, XLVI, and XLVII. Chapter XLII contains his point-by-point refutation of Bellarmine's *De Summo Pontifice*. Chapter XLVII catalogues the institutional mechanisms through which the Papacy maintains its power.
