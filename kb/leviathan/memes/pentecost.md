# Pentecost

Pentecost, the descent of the Holy Spirit upon the Apostles, is interpreted by Hobbes as the empowering of the apostolic teaching mission — not as the founding of a church government with temporal authority independent of civil sovereigns.

## Role in Leviathan

Hobbes discusses Pentecost in Chapter XLII. The Holy Spirit descended upon the Apostles in the form of "cloven tongues, like as of fire" (Acts 2:3), enabling them to preach in diverse languages. Hobbes emphasizes that this gift was for teaching, not ruling: the Apostles received "the gift of Tongues, and the gift of Healing... but not the gift of Governing" (Chapter XLII). This is essential to his argument that ecclesiastical authority derives from civil sovereignty, not from apostolic succession.

## Key References

Hobbes discusses Pentecost in Chapter XLII, where he systematically distinguishes spiritual gifts (teaching, healing, prophecy) from temporal authority (legislation, judgment, punishment). The former were given to the Apostles; the latter remain with the civil sovereign.
