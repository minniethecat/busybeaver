# Babylon

Babylon functions in *Leviathan* as both a historical reality and an apocalyptic symbol. Historically, it was the empire that conquered Judah and destroyed Solomon's Temple. Apocalyptically, in the Book of Revelation, "Babylon" is the symbol of the Papacy — "the Whore of Babylon" — the great city that corrupts the earth with false worship.

## Role in Leviathan

Hobbes discusses Babylon in Chapters XXXIII, XXXVIII, and XLV. The Babylonian Captivity is the period during which God's prophetic kingdom (through the Kings of Judah) was suspended. In Chapter XLV, Hobbes identifies "the Kingdome of Darknesse" — the Papacy — with "the Kingdome of Babylon" from the Book of Revelation. This identification — the Pope as Antichrist, Rome as Babylon — was a standard Protestant trope, and Hobbes deploys it to devastating effect against Catholic claims.

## Key References
