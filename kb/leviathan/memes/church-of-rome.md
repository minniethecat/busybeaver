# Church of Rome

The Church of Rome — the Roman Catholic Church under the Pope — is the target of Hobbes' most sustained polemic in *Leviathan*. Hobbes treats the papacy not as a legitimate ecclesiastical institution but as "the Kingdome of Darknesse" — a system of spiritual deception that usurps the rightful authority of civil sovereigns.

## Role in Leviathan

Hobbes engages the Church of Rome throughout Parts III and IV, but most directly in Chapter XLVII ("Of the Benefit that proceedeth from such Darknesse"). He argues that the papacy's claim to temporal power is based on a systematic misinterpretation of scripture, reinforced by philosophical corruptions (Aristotelian metaphysics, the doctrine of separated essences) and institutional practices (the sacrament of penance, canon law, the Inquisition). "The Papacy, is no other, than the Ghost of the deceased Romane Empire, sitting crowned upon the grave thereof" (Chapter XLVII (051-021)). Hobbes presents the Papacy as the primary threat to civil peace and the principal cause of the English Civil War.

## Key References

Hobbes discusses the Church of Rome extensively in Chapters XLII, XLIV, XLVI, and XLVII. Chapter XLII contains his detailed refutation of Cardinal Bellarmine's *De Summo Pontifice*. Chapter XLVII is a comprehensive attack on the institutional machinery of papal power.
