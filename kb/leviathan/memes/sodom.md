# Sodom

Sodom, the city destroyed by fire from heaven in Genesis 19, appears in *Leviathan* as an example of God's direct exercise of sovereign power — punishment without the mediation of human law — and as a type of the judgement to come.

## Role in Leviathan

Hobbes references Sodom in his discussion of divine punishment and in his treatment of biblical history. The destruction of Sodom is an act of God's natural kingdom — his exercise of irresistible power as sovereign of all mankind, not through the institutions of his covenant kingdom. In Chapter XXXI, Hobbes references Sodom in the context of God's judgements: "the plagues of Egypt, the destruction of Sodome... were all done by the Power of God." Sodom represents the outer limit of divine sovereignty — what God may do to any people who are not under a special covenant.

## Key References

Hobbes references Sodom in Chapters XXXI and XXXVIII. The destruction of Sodom is one of several biblical examples Hobbes uses to illustrate the irresistible power that is the basis of all sovereignty.
