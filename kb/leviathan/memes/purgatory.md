# Purgatory

Hobbes treats the doctrine of purgatory as one of the principal instruments of "the Kingdome of Darknesse" — a fabricated doctrine without biblical warrant, designed to enrich the clergy and extend their power over the living through the fear of post-mortem suffering.

## Role in Leviathan

Hobbes attacks purgatory in Chapter XLIV ("Of Spirituall Darknesse, from Misinterpretation of Scripture"). He argues that "the Doctrine of Purgatory" is "grounded upon certain places of the New Testament, which are manifestly wrested from their proper sense" (Chapter XLIV). The doctrine was invented to support "the gain of the Clergy, and the ambition of the Pope." By claiming power over the intermediate state between death and resurrection — a state Hobbes denies exists — the clergy created an apparatus of indulgences, masses for the dead, and intercessory prayer that enriched the church and terrified the laity. Hobbes' attack on purgatory is part of his comprehensive rejection of any ecclesiastical power not explicitly grounded in scripture and authorized by the civil sovereign.

## Key References

Hobbes discusses purgatory in Chapter XLIV, in the context of "Spirituall Darknesse." The doctrine is one of four principal errors Hobbes identifies as instruments of ecclesiastical domination, alongside the doctrines of demons, the immortality of the soul, and the kingdom of darkness.
