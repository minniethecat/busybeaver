# Church

Hobbes' definition of the church is one of the most radical moves in *Leviathan*. By defining a church as "a company of men professing Christian Religion, united in the person of one Soveraign," he eliminates any possibility of a universal church with independent authority over civil sovereigns.

## Role in Leviathan

Hobbes develops his definition of the church in Chapter XXXIX ("Of the Signification in Scripture of the word Church"). He distinguishes between the church as the universal company of the elect (which is invisible and has no government on earth) and the church as a visible assembly of Christians in a particular commonwealth (which is a civil corporation under the sovereign). "A Church, is a company of men professing Christian Religion, united in the person of one Soveraign; at whose command they ought to assemble, and without whose authority they ought not to assemble" (Chapter XXXIX (043-001)). This definition means that "there is no Universall Church, which all Christians are bound to obey; because there is no power on earth, to which all other Common-wealths are subject" (Chapter XXXIX).

## Key References

Hobbes defines the church in Chapter XXXIX and develops the implications in Chapter XLII. The church as a civil corporation is entirely subject to the sovereign, who is "the Supreme Pastor" — the sovereign determines who may preach, what doctrines are taught, and when the church assembles.
