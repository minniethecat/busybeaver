# River Jordan

The River Jordan — the eastern boundary of Canaan and the site of John the Baptist's ministry — appears in *Leviathan* as a geographical landmark in Hobbes' biblical narrative.

## Role in Leviathan

Hobbes references the Jordan in Chapter XXXV and Chapter XLII. The crossing of the Jordan under Joshua marked the entry into the Promised Land — the territorial possession of God's kingdom. In the New Testament, John's baptism in the Jordan was a "Baptisme of Repentance" — not a sacrament of admission into Christ's kingdom, which was not yet established. Hobbes' treatment of the Jordan is brief but consistent with his broader geographical-political reading of biblical narrative.

## Key References

Hobbes references the Jordan in Chapters XXXV and XLII. The river serves as a boundary marker between the wilderness wandering and the possession of Canaan — the territorial foundation of God's kingdom.
