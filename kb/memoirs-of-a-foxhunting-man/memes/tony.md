# Tony

**Tony** (also referred to as "young Lewison") was a hunt member encountered by the narrator and [[stephen-colwood|Stephen Colwood]] during a day's hunting near [[basset-wood|Basset Wood]]. A "moody faced youth," he became the unwitting seller of his horse [[cockbird|Cockbird]] to the narrator in an impromptu negotiation conducted almost entirely by Stephen.

## Appearance and Character

The narrator's first sight of Tony is during a covert tour:

> Later on, while Stephen and I were touring the covert with our ears open, we overtook a moody faced youth on a handsome bay horse. "Hullo, Tony! I thought you'd parted with that conspicuous quad of yours at Tatts last week," exclaimed Stephen, riding robustly up alongside of him and giving the bay horse a friendly slap on his hind quarters. (021-021)

Tony's "moody faced" and "doleful" demeanour became something of a running characteristic. Stephen Colwood later remarked that "Tony never thinks of anything except getting home to his tea" (021-043).

## The Sale of Cockbird

Tony owned a horse named Cockbird, which the narrator had been riding as a hireling. The sale was arranged with characteristic briskness by Stephen Colwood. When young Lewison reappeared during the hunt and asked how the narrator liked the horse, Stephen intervened before Sherston could answer:

> Before I had time to answer Stephen interposed with "He makes a distinct noise, Tony, and his wind's bound to get worse. But my friend Sherston likes the feel of him and he'll give you fifty." (021-041)

The deal was evidently struck, for shortly afterwards:

> "We'll send you your saddle and bridle tomorrow," shouted Stephen, as Cockbird's ex-owner disappeared along the lane outside Basset Wood. "Tony never thinks of anything except getting home to his tea," he added. (021-043)

The transaction was typical of the informal horse-dealing that occurred among hunt members, with Stephen Colwood acting as the decisive intermediary.
