# Dumborough Park

## Location & Setting

Dumborough Park is a country estate twelve miles from Butley, seat of Lord Dumborough and headquarters of the Dumborough Hounds. The estate lies at the heart of the "Dumborough country," a hunting territory that Sherston describes as being on "the edge of" from the perspective of his Aunt Evelyn's house [005-001]. The park and its surrounding woodlands contained the kennels and stables of one of the principal hunt clubs in Sherston's early hunting life.

## Significance in the Memoir

Dumborough Park represents the aristocratic center of the Edwardian hunting world as Sherston first encountered it. His early hunting experiences were closely tied to the Dumborough Hounds, and the estate serves as a symbol of the landed gentry's role in organizing and presiding over foxhunting. It appears in the memoir's early chapters (Part 1: Early Days), establishing the social hierarchy that pervades the hunting scenes.

Lord Dumborough's subscription to the hounds from Dumborough Park [002-002] sets the tone for the patronage system that sustained hunt clubs in the period. The "conspicuous Dumborough Park contingent" [006-008] at hunt meets dominated the social landscape.

## Key Scenes

The narrator first encountered the Dumborough world through his Aunt Evelyn's neighbor, who "subscribed handsomely to Lord Dumborough's Hounds," noting that "Dumborough Park was twelve miles from where my aunt lived" [002-002].

During the Christmas season, the hunting intensified:

> He was staying at Dumborough Park for Christmas. He described his day's hunting... [005-042]

At hunt meets, the estate's influence was visible:

> ...the conspicuous Dumborough Park contingent, which was dominated by Lord Dumborough [006-008]
