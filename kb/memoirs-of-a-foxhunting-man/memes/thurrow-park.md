# Thurrow Park

## Location & Setting

Thurrow Park is the country estate of Mrs. Oakfield, situated in the "Saturday country" near Folesford Hall. The estate functions as a social center for the fashionable hunting set, with Mrs. Oakfield described as "a lady who made friends wherever she went" (032-006). On Fridays, the hunting community converged on Thurrow Park for what was known as "Fashionable Friday" (032-007), establishing the estate as a key node in the social geography of the hunting season.

## Significance in the Memoir

Thurrow Park appears in Part 8 ("Migration to the Midlands") as part of Sherston's introduction to a new hunting country. The estate represents the social dimension of foxhunting — the gatherings, the fashion, the networks of acquaintance that surrounded the actual sport. Mrs. Oakfield's hospitality at Thurrow Park contrasts with the more austere, ball-less Folesford Hall nearby, highlighting the variety of social styles among the hunting gentry.

The introduction of Mrs. Oakfield and her estate establishes Thurrow Park as a welcoming social hub, in contrast to the more formidable personalities of neighboring estates.

> Fashionable Friday contained all of them, and they all converged on Mrs. Oakfield at Thurrow Park. (032-007)

The convergence of the hunting community at Thurrow Park for "Fashionable Friday" illustrates how the social calendar of the hunting season revolved around country-house hospitality.
