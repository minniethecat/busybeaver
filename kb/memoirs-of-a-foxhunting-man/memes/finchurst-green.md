## Location

Finchurst Green is a village green in the Ringwell hunt country, used as a regular meeting place for the Ringwell Hunt during the Edwardian period. It lies within riding distance of the narrator's home near Butley.

## Role in Foxhunting

Finchurst Green served as one of the primary meet locations for the Ringwell Hounds. Tom Dixon, the narrator's groom and hunting mentor, would announce forthcoming meets there with enthusiasm:

> "They're meeting at Finchurst Green on Saturday," he announced with an air of one who communicates tidings of overwhelming importance [(005-009)](005-009).

The green was a gathering point where the hounds would cluster before moving off to draw nearby coverts:

> ...and there at last was Finchurst Green, with the hounds clustering in the road [(005-014)](005-014).

## The Narrator's Experience

For the young narrator, Finchurst Green was a place of both anticipation and anxiety. The seasoned hunting men who gathered there represented a standard of horsemanship he aspired to match:

> Hard-bitten hunting-men rattled up in dog-carts and converged on the meet with an air of imminent jeopardy [(007-009)](007-009).

The significance of a meet at Finchurst Green was such that even a casual mention carried weight:

> "I see they're meeting at Finchurst Green on Tuesday." The significance of this information was not lost on me [(014-028)](014-028).

The narrator's preparation for these meets was meticulous. Dixon ensured every detail of his turnout was correct:

> ...when I started for Finchurst Green, hung spurless on each side of my new knee-boots [(014-029)](014-029).
