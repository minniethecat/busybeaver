# Packlestone

## Location & Setting

Packlestone is a hunting country in the Midlands, notable for its dog-hounds and four-day-a-week hunting schedule. The Packlestone Kennels, where Tom Dixon served as huntsman, form the center of the local hunting world. The country is described as more demanding than the familiar Kentish territory, hunted four days a week [031-004].

## Significance in the Memoir

Packlestone represents the second major hunting phase in Sherston's life, following his early experiences with the Ringwell and Dumborough hunts in Kent. When Denis Milden was elected Master of the Packlestone [030-018], Dixon moved north to serve under him, and Sherston followed, spending "the best part of a month" at Packlestone [031-007].

The Packlestone chapters (31–32) portray a more professional, socially complex hunting world than the Kentish country. The "Packlestone people" are observed "with peculiar vividness" [031-011], and the social dynamics of the hunt—including meetings at Folesford Hall—occupy much of the narrative.
