## Location

Chartley Church stands in the rolling hunting country between the villages and coverts of the Ringwell territory. It served as a notable landmark for hunting men navigating the lanes and byways of the countryside.

## A Landmark for Riders

Church towers were essential navigation points in the Edwardian hunting landscape, and Chartley Church was no exception. Hunt followers used it to orient themselves when moving between draws:

> ...well to Wissenden, then on by Chartley Church and Henhurst down the hill and away to the vale... [(023-008)](023-008).

The church was a reassuring sight during long, solitary rides home after a blank day:

> ...but when I was jogging back by Chartley Church, with my coat collar turned up against the drizzle, I felt that I had earned my place... [(023-011)](023-011).

## Context

In the pre-motor age, church towers like Chartley's were among the few vertical reference points in the rural landscape. For hunting men covering long distances on horseback, they provided both practical navigation and a sense of the familiar in an otherwise featureless winter countryside.
