# Ashbridge

## Location & Setting

Ashbridge is a "somnolent town" [014-003] in the Weald, about ten miles across the countryside from Butley. It serves as the nearest market town for Sherston's everyday needs—a place of shops, vegetable vendors, and outfitters where a young man could purchase the necessities of Edwardian country life.

## Significance in the Memoir

Ashbridge appears at two distinct points in the memoir, both connected to Sherston's material preparations for his social and hunting life. During the Flower Show, the vegetable judges suspected one exhibitor of having "bought all the vegetables at Ashbridge" [010-029], a detail that captures the town's role as the local commercial center.

More significantly, Ashbridge becomes Sherston's destination when he needs proper hunting attire. His first pair of riding gaiters, purchased in Ashbridge, were "a source of considerable satisfaction" [014-026], and his ready-made boots from the town "remain vividly in my mind" [014-029] as the most serviceable he ever owned.
