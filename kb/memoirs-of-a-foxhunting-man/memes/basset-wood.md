# Basset Wood

## Location & Setting

Basset Wood is a woodland covert in the Ringwell hunt country, situated near the Bells, a wayside inn that served as a meeting point for the hunt. The wood lies within the Sussex landscape that forms the backdrop of Sherston's early hunting seasons. Tony, the Ringwell huntsman, described Basset Wood as "one of our werry best Wednesday coverts" (019-005), marking it as a reliable location for finding foxes on midweek hunting days.

## Significance in the Memoir

Basset Wood appears prominently in Part 5 ("At the Rectory") as one of the key coverts in the Ringwell hunting country. It serves as the setting for several memorable hunting episodes, including a hunt where the fox was viewed returning to the wood after being chased out (021-040), and another occasion when an old dog-fox was pushed out and killed after a fast forty-minute chase (028-015). The wood's reliability as a fox covert made it a staple of the Ringwell hunting calendar.

## Key Scenes

> "That's Basset Wood—one of our werry best Wednesday coverts" (019-005)

Tony's endorsement of Basset Wood establishes it as a trusted and productive hunting ground, central to the Wednesday hunting routine.

> And viewed our fox returning to Basset Wood. (021-040)

A moment of frustration as the fox evades the hounds by circling back to its familiar refuge, demonstrating the fox's intimate knowledge of its own territory.

> Pushed an old dog-fox out of Basset Wood and killed him after a fast forty minutes (028-015)

A successful hunt described in the hunting diary's characteristic understatement, with hounds stopped in Basset Wood at 4:25 as recorded in the concluding entry (029-003).
