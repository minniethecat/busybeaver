## Overview

Hoath Wood is a woodland covert in the Ringwell hunt country, notable as a reliable fox-hunting draw that rarely fails to produce a find. Situated approximately three miles from Major Gamble's preserves, it features in the narrator's early hunting experiences under the guidance of Tom Dixon.

## Location & Setting

Hoath Wood lies in the Sussex hunting country, reached after passing through several smaller spinneys on the rising ground beyond Major Gamble's land (005-027). Dixon describes it as "a sure find" and "a rare place to get a gallop from" (005-027), indicating its reputation among the local hunting community. The approach to the wood includes a notable fence — one that proved challenging for the narrator's mount on at least one occasion (006-011).

## In the Memoir

> It was about three miles to Hoath Wood; on the way several small spinneys were drawn blank, but Hoath Wood was a sure find, so Dixon said, and a rare place to get a gallop from. (005-027)

The covert is associated with Lord Dumborough's characteristic irascibility. When a keeper failed to stop earths at Major Gamble's preserves, Dumborough was heard "bawling 'Go on to Hoath Wood, Jack,' to the invisible huntsman" (005-024) — redirecting the hunt toward more reliable ground.

## Key Scenes

- **Lord Dumborough's outburst**: The covert serves as the fallback destination after a blank draw at Major Gamble's preserves (005-024, 005-027, 005-029)
- **The fence outside Hoath Wood**: A fellow rider, Colonel Hesmon, later teases the narrator about a fence jumped "a bit too big for you at that fence outside Hoath Wood" (006-011)
