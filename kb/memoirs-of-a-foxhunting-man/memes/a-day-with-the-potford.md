# A Day with the Potford

A day's hunting with the Potford Hounds forms the centerpiece of Part 4 of the memoir, spanning three chapters (16–18).

## Arrangements

Dixon, the narrator's groom, made the arrangements for the day, as the Potford Hounds were meeting not far off (017-004). The distance to the meet required preparation, and Dixon ensured everything was in order for the excursion (017-005).

## The Hunt

The narrator was curious to see the new Master of the Potford in action, about whom Dixon had heard favourable reports (017-009). The hunt took them into the only good stretch of vale in the Potford country (017-024).

The high point of the day was a successful scramble across the Potford Vale, which was both exhilarating and demanding (018-001). The exciting half-hour spent there surpassed anything the narrator's Aunt could imagine from his account (018-019).

## Aftermath

The narrator's account of the Potford gallop was well received by his circle. Stephen Colwood remarked that the narrator had "a regular turn for writing" after hearing the vivid description of the day's sport (019-003).

## Significance

This episode marks the narrator's deepening immersion in the hunting world, transitioning from observer to active participant. It also introduces the Potford country as a distinct hunting territory, separate from the Ringwell country, and establishes the social bonds formed through shared sporting experience.
