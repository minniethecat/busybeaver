# Folesford Hall

## Location & Setting

Folesford Hall is a country estate belonging to Sir Jocelyn Porteus-Porteous, situated in the middle of a park with its "chain of woodlands" (032-008). The estate lies in what the memoir describes as the "Saturday country" (032-007), a hunting territory associated with weekend meets. The hall is a substantial country house, prominent enough in local society that Sherston inquires about entertainments given there.

## Significance in the Memoir

Folesford Hall appears in Part 8 ("Migration to the Midlands") as the seat of Sir Jocelyn Porteus-Porteous, a figure of local importance. The hall serves as a social marker in the memoir — Sherston's polite inquiry about when Sir Jocelyn last gave a ball there (032-010) becomes the occasion for one of the memoir's quietly comic exchanges. Sir Jocelyn's sniffling response that there is "no record of any ball at Folesford" (032-011) reveals both the magnate's peculiar manner and the slightly absurd formality of the social rituals surrounding country-house life.

## Key Scenes

> Sir Jocelyn Porteus-Porteous of Folesford Hall. The Saturday country was his, and his manner of giving you the impression that he expected you to enjoy yourself in it was more than dignified. (032-007)

The introduction of Folesford Hall through its owner establishes the estate as an extension of Sir Jocelyn's imposing, slightly comical personality.

> "When did you last give a ball at Folesford, Sir Jocelyn?" I politely asked. "There is no record (sniff) of any ball at Folesford (sniff)," was his rejoinder. (032-010–032-011)

The famous ball-at-Folesford exchange, one of the memoir's most memorable comic moments, where the grandeur of the estate name contrasts with the total absence of the kind of social entertainment one might expect there.
