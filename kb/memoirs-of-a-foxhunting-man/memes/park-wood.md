## Geography

Park Wood is a substantial woodland covert in the Ringwell hunting country, visible from the surrounding fields as a "big green patch." It lies within the territory regularly drawn by the Ringwell Hounds and is close enough to the narrator's home to be reached on horseback.

## Hunting Significance

Park Wood was one of the coverts regularly drawn during meets in the Ringwell country. The narrator first encountered it during his early hunting days under the guidance of Tom Dixon. As the hunt procession approached the wood, he looked upward and identified it as their destination:

> ...and a big green patch called Park Wood quite near. I wondered what it contained [(007-003)](007-003).

The hunt field gathered outside the wood while hounds were put in to draw:

> The procession moved away toward Park Wood, but I looked upward and identified a solitary heron [(007-010)](007-010).

> ...when we pulled up outside Park Wood and I caught sight of Denis on his chestnut pony... [(007-011)](007-011).

## The Narrator's Early Hunt

The narrator spent much of his first serious hunting day following Dixon up and down Park Wood, learning the rhythm of the sport and the discipline of waiting while hounds worked the covert:

> ...following Dixon up and down Park Wood that my indiscretion was temporarily forgotten [(007-020)](007-020).
