# Baldock Wood

## Location & Setting

Baldock Wood is a railway station situated near Butley, serving as a local junction on the line. The station is described as being "exactly the" kind of small country station that marked the boundary between the narrator's familiar home territory and the wider hunting world beyond (008-001). It lies close enough to Butley to serve as the primary departure point for early-morning trains to distant meets.

## Significance in the Memoir

Baldock Wood Station appears at several key transitional moments in the memoir. It is from here that Sherston departs on hunting expeditions, the early train symbolizing both the dedication required of the hunting enthusiast and the broadening of his geographical horizons. The station also marks moments of personal significance — at Baldock Wood, Sherston's "personality somehow diluted" as he left his familiar world behind (016-003), and it was from here that he departed "by a very early train" to begin a new chapter in a remote part of the hunting country (023-002).

## Key Scenes

> Slowed up at Baldock Wood. The station was exactly the... (008-001)

The train's approach to Baldock Wood, a moment of anticipation as the narrator arrives at or departs from the familiar local station at the threshold of his hunting adventures.

> At Baldock Wood Station there had been no doubt about his identity; but already his personality somehow diluted. (016-003)

A reflection on how travel from Baldock Wood marked a shift in identity — leaving behind the known self for the wider, less defined world beyond.
