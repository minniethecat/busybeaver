# Sir Jocelyn Porteus-Porteous

**Sir Jocelyn Porteus-Porteous** was the "unmistakable magnate" of the Saturday hunting country, residing at [[folesford-hall|Folesford Hall]] (032-007). The Saturday country was the least popular of the four hunting divisions — well-wooded, hilly, and sporadically blemished by collieries — yet Sir Jocelyn dominated it as its principal figure (032-007).

## Personal Characteristics

He was a short, thickset, round-legged man with regular features and a moustache (032-008). He was known for a pompous manner punctuated by his unconscious habit of regularly recurrent sniffs, though his hauteur was described as "not unkindly" (032-008).

The narrator records a characteristic exchange: when politely asked when Sir Jocelyn had last given a ball at Folesford, the narrator gazed bashfully at one of his dangling top-boots (032-010). The answer, as the narrator later reflected, was that there had never been any balls at Folesford — a fact delivered with the characteristic "(sniff)" that defined Sir Jocelyn's conversational manner (032-012).

## Folesford Hall

Sir Jocelyn's ancestral seat, Folesford Hall, was the center of his domain. The Porteous family had occupied Folesford since Plantagenet times, and the hall was a Gothic nucleus with Tudor and Jacobean additions (032-008). Despite the industrial habitations and colliery chimneys that had cropped up outside his grandiose gateway — which provided lucrative mineral royalties — Sir Jocelyn maintained a lofty and impercipient attitude toward these developments, dismissing their occupants as "plebian upstarts" (032-008).

## Role in the Hunt

As the predominant landowner of the Saturday country, Sir Jocelyn held a commanding position in local hunting society. The narrator concluded that he was "the king of the Saturday country" (032-012), a title reflecting both his territorial dominance and his social pre-eminence among the hunting community.
