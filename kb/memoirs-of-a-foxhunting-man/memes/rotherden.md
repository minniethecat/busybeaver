# Rotherden

## Location & Setting

Rotherden is a village in the Weald of Kent, reachable by an early morning bicycle ride from Butley. It lies on the "unlimited" side of the Kentish cricket tradition, where the Rotherden eleven played with a relaxed, unhurried approach to the game. The landscape around Rotherden offered "certain lovely glimpses of the Weald, and the smell of mown hayfields" [009-020] during the summer cricket season.

## Significance in the Memoir

Rotherden is the setting for the Flower Show Match in Part 2, the pivotal cricket episode of Sherston's youth. The match between the Butley Flower Show team and the Rotherden eleven occupies chapters 8–10, and the village provides the backdrop for Sherston's brief moment of batting glory. The Flower Show itself takes place on the meadows near Rotherden, with the cricket ground adjoining the show-ground.

Rotherden also becomes significant later in the memoir, when Stephen Colwood returns from Hoadley Rectory to play a return fixture against Rotherden in chapter 11.

## Key Scenes

> The Rotherden men appeared to be in no great hurry to begin the game. [009-016]

The Rotherden eleven arrived in a two-horse brake, epitomizing the unhurried country approach to cricket that Sherston fondly recalls:

> Tom Seamark, the Rotherden umpire, is a red-faced sporting man who has no objection to a little friendly chaff. [010-002]

The match concluded with Rotherden all out for 183, and the Butley eleven won by a narrow margin [010-018–010-021]. A ribald Rotherden partisan called from a cart after a close appeal, adding to the spirited atmosphere of village cricket.
