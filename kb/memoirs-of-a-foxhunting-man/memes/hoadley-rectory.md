# Hoadley Rectory

## Location & Setting

Hoadley Rectory is situated in Sussex, with its garden divided from the churchyard by a cricket field [020-001]. The rectory stable forms an important part of the establishment, housing the horses that Stephen Colwood and his guests ride to meets. The rectory is approached by carriage, with Stephen pointing out local landmarks along the way [019-004].

## Significance in the Memoir

Hoadley Rectory serves as the setting for Part 5 ("At the Rectory," chapters 19–22) and represents a new phase in Sherston's social and hunting life. After his early hunting experiences in Kent under Dixon's tutelage, the rectory offers a more independent, young-man's hunting existence centered on Stephen Colwood's lively household.

The rectory is a place of "excellent" hunting companionship [018-024], where Sherston learns the social codes of Edwardian hunt society. His anxiety not to "disgrace the Rectory" [021-001] on his first hunt appearance reveals how the rectory serves as a social credential and identity for its residents and guests.
