## Overview

Cowslake Manor is the country seat of Mr. Bellerby, a newcomer to the local hunting scene. The manor and its owner feature in one of the most memorable comic episodes of the opening-day meet at Dumborough, where Bellerby's inelegant horsemanship and untidy appearance mark him out among the assembled field.

## Location & Setting

The manor is situated within riding distance of the Dumborough hunt country. Its precise location is not specified in detail, though Mr. Bellerby is seen "tit-tupping away to Cowslake Manor" (028-010) at the conclusion of the day's sport. Pacey's Plantation, where the fox was found on the opening day, lies nearby.

## In the Memoir

The manor's owner, Mr. Bellerby, is introduced at the opening meet:

> He presented me to an affable person on the other side of him — Mr. Bellerby, of Cowslake Manor. Mr. Bellerby was mounted on a fidgety, ewe-necked, weak-middled, dun-coloured mare. He had a straggling sandy beard and was untidily dressed in new clothes which looked all wrong. (028-005)

The narrator subsequently encounters Bellerby in a panicked gallop through Pacey's Plantation, where the narrator's timely intervention at a gate saves Bellerby from a collision (028-007). Bellerby later recounts the episode at length to Sir John Ruddimore, exaggerating the narrator's heroism before riding home to Cowslake Manor (028-010).

## Key Scenes

- **Introduction at the opening meet**: Bellerby of Cowslake Manor is presented to the narrator by Lord Dumborough (028-005)
- **The gate incident**: The narrator's skillful handling of Cockbird saves Bellerby from crashing into a gatepost (028-007)
- **Bellerby's departure**: After pressing the narrator to accept a memento, Bellerby departs for Cowslake Manor (028-010)
