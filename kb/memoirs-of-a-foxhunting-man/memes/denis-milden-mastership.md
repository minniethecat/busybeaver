# Denis Milden as Master

The appointment of Denis Milden as Master of Foxhounds (MFH) for the Ringwell Hunt is a key organizational event in the memoir, covered across four chapters of Part 7. It represents a generational shift in the leadership of the hunting country.

## Early Impressions

The narrator first encountered Denis Milden at supper, sitting opposite him at a gathering (006-008). Even at this early stage, Milden made a strong impression. The narrator's interest in hunting was as much shaped by the personal appeal of Denis Milden as by any abstract love of the sport (007-007).

## Reunion and the Mastership Idea

Years later, the narrator rediscovered Milden. When told that his name was Milden — Denis Milden — he immediately knew he had known it before (026-045). The new idea of Denis Milden as Master of the Ringwell Hunt took hold, reviving the narrator's early impressions of him (026-048).

During a casual moment while taking a turn with the spades, Denis Milden addressed the narrator directly, asking "Haven't we met before?", cementing their reconnection (027-016). The narrator recalled his early impressions of Denis Milden from when he was younger (027-004).

## The Mastership

Denis Milden brought a fresh energy to the Mastership. His predecessor, by contrast, was seen in a different light by the hunting community (029-009). Milden refused to lift his hounds under conditions his predecessor might have accepted (028-015). The narrator took the right amount of interest in Denis Milden, remembering him as "such a nice young fellow" (029-001).

## A Day at the Kennels

The narrator visited the Kennels, where Denis Milden blew a long wavering blast on his horn from just over a quarter of a mile away, a sound that symbolized the new Mastership (029-005).

## Significance

By the time the Mastership was established, it was a recognized fact that for Denis Milden the Ringwell country was only the beginning of what would be a distinguished career in hunting (030-018). The appointment marked a generational transition and injected new vitality into the Ringwell Hunt.
