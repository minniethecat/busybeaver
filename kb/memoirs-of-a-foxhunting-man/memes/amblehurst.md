# Amblehurst

## Location & Setting

Amblehurst was a village about four miles from Butley (013-004). It was notable for Amblehurst Priory, occupied by "new people" who arrived in the neighbourhood during Sherston's childhood (002-006). The village also had a nine-hole golf course, though Sherston admitted it was "in far from first-rate condition" (014-002).

## Significance in the Memoir

Amblehurst appears in Sherston's early memories as a destination for solitary recreation. He frequently bicycled over to play a round of golf on the Amblehurst course, often playing by himself in the late afternoon (013-003, 013-007). The priory and its new occupants represented the limited social horizons of Sherston's childhood world.

## Key Scenes

> "And those new people over at Amblehurst Priory." (002-006)

> "I shall probably bike over to Amblehurst after lunch for a round of golf." (013-003)

In his "spontaneous memories of Amblehurst," Sherston recalls "always playing by myself, in the late afternoon, when the shadows were lengthening" (013-007). The course itself was modest: "the Amblehurst course was in far from first-rate condition" (014-002), yet it provided a setting for youthful freedom before his hunting days began in earnest.
