# Downfield

## Location & Setting

Downfield was the county town, situated about three miles from the Ringwell hunt country (015-004). It served as a transport hub, with a railway station on the branch line connecting Dumbridge and the hunting districts. Whatman's "Hunting Box" in Downfield provided stabling and boxing services for hunters (026-002). The town had established solicitors' firms, including Croplady, the family firm of Nigel Croplady (021-017).

## Significance in the Memoir

Downfield functions as the logistical base for Sherston's hunting activities. He frequently travelled to Downfield to box his horses, stay overnight before early meets, and return by train after long days in the saddle. The town's hireling stables supplied "top-hole hirelings" at thirty-five shillings a day (018-023). It was also the home of various soldiers who hunted from the town (026-013).

As Sherston and Colwood rode out from Downfield on a two-horse charabanc, "the country became more attractive, with a pleasant prospect of woods and hills" (015-006). After hunting, Sherston habitually rode back toward Downfield to catch his train (028-020).
