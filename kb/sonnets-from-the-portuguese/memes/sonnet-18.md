[018-001] I never gave a lock of hair away

[018-002] To a man, Dearest, except this to thee,

[018-003] Which now upon my fingers thoughtfully

[018-004] I ring out to the full brown length, and say:

[018-005] “Take it.” My day of youth went yesterday;

[018-006] My hair no longer bounds to my foot’s glee,

[018-007] Nor plant I it from rose or myrtle-tree,

[018-008] As girls do, any more: it only may

[018-009] Now [[shadow|shade]] on two pale cheeks, the mark of [[tear-imagery|tears]],

[018-010] Taught drooping from the head that hangs aside

[018-011] Through [[grief-theme|sorrow]]’s trick. I thought the funeral shears

[018-012] Would take this first, but Love is justified:

[018-013] Take it thou—finding pure, from all those years,

[018-014] The [[kiss-imagery|kiss]] my mother left here when she died.
