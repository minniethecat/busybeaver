[029-001] I think of thee!—my thoughts do twine and bud

[029-002] [[About]] thee, as wild vines about a tree

[029-003] Put out broad leaves, and soon there’s nought to see

[029-004] Except the straggling green which hides the wood.

[029-005] Yet, O my palm-tree, be it understood

[029-006] I will not have my thoughts instead of thee

[029-007] Who art dearer, better! Rather, instantly

[029-008] Renew thy presence; as a strong tree should,

[029-009] Rustle thy boughs and set thy trunk all bare,

[029-010] And let these bands of greenery which insphere thee

[029-011] Drop heavily down—burst, shattered, everywhere!

[029-012] Because, in this deep joy to see and hear thee

[029-013] And breathe within thy [[shadow]] a new air,

[029-014] I do not think of thee—I am too near thee.
