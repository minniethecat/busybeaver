## Full Text

> A heavy heart, Beloved, have I borne [025-001]
> From year to year until I saw thy face, [025-002]
> And sorrow after sorrow took the place [025-003]
> Of all those natural joys as lightly worn [025-004]
> As the stringed pearls, each lifted in its turn [025-005]
> By a beating heart at dance-time. Hopes apace [025-006]
> Were changed to long despairs, till God's own grace [025-007]
> Could scarcely lift above the world forlorn [025-008]
> My heavy heart. Then *thou* didst bid me bring [025-009]
> And let it drop adown thy calmly great [025-010]
> Deep being! Fast it sinketh, as a thing [025-011]
> Which its own nature does precipitate, [025-012]
> While thine doth close above it, mediating [025-013]
> Betwixt the stars and the unaccomplished fate. [025-014]

— [[elizabeth-barrett-browning|Elizabeth Barrett Browning]], *[[sonnets-from-the-portuguese|Sonnets from the Portuguese]]*, Sonnet XXV [025-001]–[025-014]

## Analysis

Sonnet XXV of [[elizabeth-barrett-browning|Elizabeth Barrett Browning]]'s *[[sonnets-from-the-portuguese|Sonnets from the Portuguese]]* (1850) recounts the history of the speaker's heavy [[heart-imagery|heart]] — borne "from year to year" — and its release through the be[[love-theme|love]]d. The octave catalogs [[grief-theme|sorrow]]s replacing joys, hopes turning to despairs, until even divine grace could barely help.

The volta pivots on "Then *thou*" — the be[[love-theme|love]]d's invitation transforms everything. The speaker's [[heart-imagery|heart]], dropped into the be[[love-theme|love]]d's "calmly great / Deep being," sinks like a stone that "its own nature does precipitate." The final image of the be[[love-theme|love]]d's being closing above the [[heart-imagery|heart]], "mediating / Betwixt the stars and the unaccomplished fate," is one of the sequence's most cosmologically ambitious closures.

## Place in the Sequence

Sonnet XXV dramatizes the therapeutic and redemptive power of the be[[love-theme|love]]d. It follows the protective sanctuary of [[sonnet-xxiv|Sonnet XXIV]] with an image of the be[[love-theme|love]]d's being as a depth that absorbs and transforms the speaker's pain.
