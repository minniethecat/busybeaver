[005-001] I lift my heavy [[heart-imagery|heart]] up solemnly,

[005-002] As once Electra her sepulchral urn,

[005-003] And, looking in thine eyes, I overturn

[005-004] The ashes at thy feet. Behold and see

[005-005] What a great heap of [[grief-theme|grief]] lay hid in me,

[005-006] And how the red wild sparkles dimly burn

[005-007] Through the ashen greyness. If thy foot in scorn

[005-008] Could tread them out to darkness utterly,

[005-009] It might be well perhaps. But if instead

[005-010] Thou wait beside me for the wind to blow

[005-011] The grey dust up—those laurels on thine head,

[005-012] O my Beloved, will not shield thee so,

[005-013] That none of all the fires shall scorch and shred

[005-014] The hair beneath. Stand further off then! Go.
