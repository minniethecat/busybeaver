[004-001] Thou hast thy calling to some palace-floor,

[004-002] Most gracious singer of high poems! where

[004-003] The dancers will break footing, from the care

[004-004] Of watching up thy pregnant lips for more.

[004-005] And dost thou lift this house’s latch too poor

[004-006] For hand of thine? and canst thou think and bear

[004-007] To let thy [[music-theme|music]] drop here unaware

[004-008] In folds of golden fullness at my door?

[004-009] Look up and see the casement broken in,

[004-010] The bats and owlets builders in the roof!

[004-011] My cricket chirps against thy mandolin.

[004-012] Hush! call no echo up in further proof

[004-013] Of desolation! there’s a voice within

[004-014] That weeps as—thou must sing—alone, aloof.
