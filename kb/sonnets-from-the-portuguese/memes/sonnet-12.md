[012-001] Indeed this very [[love-theme|love]] which is my boast,

[012-002] And which, when rising up from breast to brow

[012-003] Doth crown me with a ruby large enow

[012-004] To draw men’s eyes and prove the inner cost—

[012-005] This love even, all my worth, to the uttermost,

[012-006] I should not love withal, unless that thou

[012-007] Hadst set me an example, shown me how,

[012-008] When first thine earnest eyes with mine were crossed,

[012-009] And love called love. And thus, I cannot speak

[012-010] Of love even, as a good thing of my own.

[012-011] Thy [[soul-theme|soul]] hath snatched up mine all faint and weak,

[012-012] And placed it by thee on a golden throne—

[012-013] And that I love, (O soul, we must be meek!)

[012-014] Is by thee only, whom I love alone.
