## Full Text

> When we met first and loved, I did not build [036-001]
> Upon the event with marble. Could it mean [036-002]
> To last, a love set pendulous between [036-003]
> Sorrow and sorrow? [036-004]
> Nay, I rather thrilled, [036-004]
> Distrusting every light that seemed to gild [036-005]
> The onward path, and feared to overlean [036-006]
> And, though I have grown serene [036-007]
> And strong since then, I think that God has willed [036-008]
> A still renewable fear—O love, O troth— [036-009]
> Lest these enclasped hands should never hold, [036-010]
> This mutual kiss drop down between us both [036-011]
> As an unowned thing, once the lips being cold. [036-012]
> And Love, be false! [036-013]
> if he, to keep one oath, [036-013]
> Must lose one joy, by his life’s star foretold. [036-014]

— [[elizabeth-barrett-browning|Elizabeth Barrett Browning]], *[[sonnets-from-the-portuguese|Sonnets from the Portuguese]]*, Sonnet XXXVI [036-001]–[036-016]

## Analysis

Sonnet XXXVI is one of the forty-four [[petrarchan-sonnet-form|Petrarchan sonnet]]s in [[elizabeth-barrett-browning|Elizabeth Barrett Browning]]'s *[[sonnets-from-the-portuguese|Sonnets from the Portuguese]]*, composed during her [[browning-courtship|courtship]] with [[robert-browning|Robert Browning]] (1845–1846). The sonnet follows the Petrarchan form (octave ABBA ABBA, sestet with varied pattern).

## Place in the Sequence

Sonnet XXXVI occupies its position within the forty-four-sonnet arc tracing the speaker's movement from initial disbelief through surrender to transcendent [[love-theme|love]].
