[044-001] Beloved, thou hast brought me many flowers

[044-002] Plucked in the garden, all the summer through

[044-003] And winter, and it seemed as if they grew

[044-004] In this close room, nor missed the sun and showers.

[044-005] So, in the like name of that [[love-theme|love]] of ours,

[044-006] Take back these thoughts which here unfolded too,

[044-007] And which on warm and cold days I withdrew

[044-008] From my [[heart-imagery|heart]]’s ground. Indeed, those beds and bowers

[044-009] Be overgrown with bitter weeds and rue,

[044-010] And wait thy weeding; yet here’s eglantine,

[044-011] Here’s ivy!—take them, as I used to do

[044-012] Thy flowers, and keep them where they shall not pine.

[044-013] Instruct thine eyes to keep their colours true,

[044-014] And tell thy [[soul-theme|soul]] their roots are left in mine.
