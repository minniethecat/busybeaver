[032-001] The first time that the sun rose on thine oath

[032-002] To [[love-theme|love]] me, I looked forward to the moon

[032-003] To slacken all those bonds which seemed too soon

[032-004] And quickly tied to make a lasting troth.

[032-005] Quick-loving hearts, I thought, may quickly loathe;

[032-006] And, looking on myself, I seemed not one

[032-007] For such man’s love!—more like an out-of-tune

[032-008] Worn viol, a good singer would be wroth

[032-009] To spoil his [[music-theme|song]] with, and which, snatched in haste,

[032-010] Is laid down at the first ill-sounding note.

[032-011] I did not wrong myself so, but I placed

[032-012] A wrong on *thee*. For perfect strains may float

[032-013] ’Neath master-hands, from instruments defaced—

[032-014] And great souls, at one stroke, may do and dote.
