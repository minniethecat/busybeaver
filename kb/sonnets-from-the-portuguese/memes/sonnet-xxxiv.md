## Full Text

> With the same heart, I said, I’ll answer thee [034-001]
> As those, when thou shalt call me by my name— [034-002]
> Lo, the vain promise! [034-003]
> Is the same, the same, [034-003]
> Perplexed and ruffled by life’s strategy? [034-004]
> When called before, I told how hastily [034-005]
> I dropped my flowers or brake off from a game. [034-006]
> To run and answer with the smile that came [034-007]
> At play last moment, and went on with me [034-008]
> Through my obedience. [034-009]
> When I answer now, [034-009]
> I drop a grave thought—break from solitude;— [034-010]
> Yet still my heart goes to thee—ponder how— [034-011]
> Not as to a single good, but all my good! [034-012]
> Lay thy hand on it, best one, and allow [034-013]
> That no child’s foot could run fast as this blood. [034-014]

— [[elizabeth-barrett-browning|Elizabeth Barrett Browning]], *[[sonnets-from-the-portuguese|Sonnets from the Portuguese]]*, Sonnet XXXIV [034-001]–[034-016]

## Analysis

Sonnet XXXIV is one of the forty-four [[petrarchan-sonnet-form|Petrarchan sonnet]]s in [[elizabeth-barrett-browning|Elizabeth Barrett Browning]]'s *[[sonnets-from-the-portuguese|Sonnets from the Portuguese]]*, composed during her [[browning-courtship|courtship]] with [[robert-browning|Robert Browning]] (1845–1846). The sonnet follows the Petrarchan form (octave ABBA ABBA, sestet with varied pattern).

## Place in the Sequence

Sonnet XXXIV occupies its position within the forty-four-sonnet arc tracing the speaker's movement from initial disbelief through surrender to transcendent [[love-theme|love]].
