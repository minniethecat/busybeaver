[017-001] My poet, thou canst touch on all the notes

[017-002] [[religious-faith|God]] set between His After and Before,

[017-003] And strike up and strike off the general roar

[017-004] Of the rushing worlds a [[music-theme|melody]] that floats

[017-005] In a serene air purely. Antidotes

[017-006] Of medicated music, answering for

[017-007] Mankind’s forlornest uses, thou canst pour

[017-008] From thence into their ears. [[god|God]]’s will devotes

[017-009] Thine to such ends, and mine to wait on thine!

[017-010] How, Dearest, wilt thou have me for most use?

[017-011] A hope, to sing by gladly? or a fine

[017-012] Sad memory, with thy songs to interfuse?

[017-013] A [[shadow|shade]], in which to sing—of palm or pine?

[017-014] A grave, on which to rest from singing? Choose.
