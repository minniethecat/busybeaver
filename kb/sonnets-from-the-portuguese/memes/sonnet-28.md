[028-001] My letters! all dead paper, mute and white!

[028-002] And yet they seem alive and quivering

[028-003] Against my tremulous hands which loose the string

[028-004] And let them drop down on my knee tonight.

[028-005] This said, he wished to have me in his sight

[028-006] Once, as a friend; this fixed a day in spring

[028-007] To come and touch my hand—a simple thing,

[028-008] Yet I wept for it!—this—the paper’s [[light-imagery|light]]—

[028-009] Said, *Dear, I [[love-theme|love]] thee*; and I sank and quailed

[028-010] As if [[religious-faith|God]]’s future thundered on my past.

[028-011] This said *I am thine*—and so its ink has paled

[028-012] With lying at my [[heart-imagery|heart]] that beat too fast;

[028-013] And this—O Love, thy words have ill availed,

[028-014] If what this said I dared repeat at last!
