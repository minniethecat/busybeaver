[001-001] I thought once how [[theocritus|Theocritus]] had sung

[001-002] Of the sweet years, the dear and wished-for years,

[001-003] Who each one in a gracious hand appears

[001-004] To bear a gift for mortals, old or young;

[001-005] And, as I mused it in his antique tongue,

[001-006] I saw, in gradual [[dream|vision]] through my [[tear-imagery|tears]],

[001-007] The sweet, sad years, the melancholy years,

[001-008] Those of my own life, who by turns had flung

[001-009] A [[shadow]] across me. Straightway I was ’ware,

[001-010] So weeping, how a mystic Shape did move

[001-011] Behind me, and drew me backward by the hair;

[001-012] And a voice said in mastery, while I strove:

[001-013] “Guess now who holds thee!”—“Death!” I said. But there,

[001-014] The silver answer rang: “Not Death, but Love.”
