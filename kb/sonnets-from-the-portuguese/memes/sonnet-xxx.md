## Full Text

> I see thine image through my tears tonight, [030-001]
> And yet today I saw thee smiling. How [030-002]
> Refer the cause?—Beloved, is it thou [030-003]
> Or I who makes me sad? The acolyte [030-004]
> Amid the chanted joy and thankful rite [030-005]
> May so fall flat, with pale insensate brow, [030-006]
> On the altar-stair. I hear thy voice and vow [030-007]
> Perplexed, uncertain, since thou art out of sight, [030-008]
> As he, in his swooning ears, the choir's amen. [030-009]
> Beloved, dost thou love? or did I see all [030-010]
> The glory as I dreamed, and fainted when [030-011]
> Too vehement light dilated my ideal [030-012]
> For my soul's eyes? Will that light come again [030-013]
> As now these tears come—falling hot and real? [030-014]

— [[elizabeth-barrett-browning|Elizabeth Barrett Browning]], *[[sonnets-from-the-portuguese|Sonnets from the Portuguese]]*, Sonnet XXX [030-001]–[030-014]

## Analysis

Sonnet XXX of [[elizabeth-barrett-browning|Elizabeth Barrett Browning]]'s *[[sonnets-from-the-portuguese|Sonnets from the Portuguese]]* (1850) dramatizes the oscillation between joy and doubt. The speaker sees the be[[love-theme|love]]d "smiling" by day but his "image through my [[tear-imagery|tears]]" by night, uncertain whether the sadness originates in herself or in him.

The acolyte metaphor in the octave — fainting on the altar-stair amid chanted joy — captures the speaker's emotional vulnerability. The sestet poses the central question: "Be[[love-theme|love]]d, dost thou [[love-theme|love]]? or did I see all / The glory as I [[dream]]ed?" The concluding question — "Will that [[light-imagery|light]] come again / As now these [[tear-imagery|tears]] come—falling hot and real?" — pairs [[soul-theme|spirit]]ual [[light-imagery|light]] with physical [[tear-imagery|tears]], refusing to separate the transcendent from the embodied.

## Place in the Sequence

Sonnet XXX marks a moment of doubt near the sequence's midpoint. After the triumph of [[sonnet-xxix|Sonnet XXIX]], this sonnet reminds us that doubt recurs, that the speaker's emotional arc is not linear.
