[024-001] Let the world’s sharpness like a clasping knife

[024-002] Shut in upon itself and do no harm

[024-003] In this close hand of Love, now soft and warm;

[024-004] And let us hear no sound of human strife

[024-005] After the click of the shutting. Life to life—

[024-006] I lean upon thee, Dear, without alarm,

[024-007] And feel as safe as guarded by a charm

[024-008] Against the stab of worldlings, who if rife

[024-009] Are weak to injure. Very whitely still

[024-010] The lilies of our lives may reassure

[024-011] Their blossoms from their roots, accessible

[024-012] Alone to heavenly dews that drop not fewer;

[024-013] Growing straight, out of man’s reach, on the hill.

[024-014] [[religious-faith|God]] only, who made us rich, can make us poor.
