[027-001] My own Beloved, who hast lifted me

[027-002] From this drear flat of earth where I was thrown,

[027-003] And in betwixt the languid ringlets blown

[027-004] A life-breath, till the forehead hopefully

[027-005] Shines out again, as all the [[angel-imagery|angels]] see,

[027-006] Before thy saving [[kiss-imagery|kiss]]! My own, my own,

[027-007] Who camest to me when the world was gone,

[027-008] And I who looked for only [[religious-faith|God]], found *thee*!

[027-009] I find thee: I am safe, and strong, and glad.

[027-010] As one who stands in dewless asphodel

[027-011] Looks backward on the tedious time he had

[027-012] In the upper life—so I, with bosom-swell,

[027-013] Make witness, here, between the good and bad,

[027-014] That Love, as strong as Death, retrieves as well.
