[014-001] If thou must [[love-theme|love]] me, let it be for nought

[014-002] Except for love’s sake only. Do not say

[014-003] “I love her for her smile—her look—her way

[014-004] Of speaking gently—for a trick of thought

[014-005] That falls in well with mine, and certes brought

[014-006] A sense of pleasant ease on such a day,”—

[014-007] For these things in themselves, Beloved, may

[014-008] Be changed, or change for thee—and love so wrought

[014-009] May be unwrought so. Neither love me for

[014-010] Thine own dear pity’s wiping my cheeks dry:

[014-011] A creature might forget to weep, who bore

[014-012] Thy comfort long, and lose thy love thereby.

[014-013] But love me for love’s sake, that evermore

[014-014] Thou mayst love on through love’s eternity.
