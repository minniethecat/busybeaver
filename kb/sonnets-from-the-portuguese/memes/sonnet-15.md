[015-001] Accuse me not, beseech thee, that I wear

[015-002] Too calm and sad a face in front of thine;

[015-003] For we two look two ways, and cannot shine

[015-004] With the same sunlight on our brow and hair.

[015-005] On me thou lookest with no doubting care,

[015-006] As on a bee shut in a crystalline;

[015-007] Since [[grief-theme|sorrow]] hath shut me safe in [[love-theme|love]]’s divine,

[015-008] And to spread wing and fly in the outer air

[015-009] Were most impossible failure, if I strove

[015-010] To fail so. But I look on thee—on thee—

[015-011] Beholding, besides love, the end of love,

[015-012] Hearing oblivion beyond memory;

[015-013] As one who sits and gazes from above,

[015-014] Over the rivers to the bitter sea.
