## Full Text

> Yes, call me by my pet name! let me hear [033-001]
> The name I used to run at, when a child, [033-002]
> From innocent play, and leave the cowslips plied, [033-003]
> To glance up in some face that proved me dear [033-004]
> With the look of its eyes. [033-005]
> I miss the clear [033-005]
> Fond voices, which, being drawn and reconciled [033-006]
> Into the music of Heaven’s undefiled, [033-007]
> Call me no longer. [033-008]
> Silence on the bier [033-008]
> While I call God—call God! [033-009]
> —so let thy mouth [033-009]
> Be heir to those who are now exanimate; [033-010]
> Gather the north flowers to complete the south, [033-011]
> And catch the early love up in the late! [033-012]
> Yes, call me by that name—and I, in truth, [033-013]
> With the same heart, will answer and not wait. [033-014]

— [[elizabeth-barrett-browning|Elizabeth Barrett Browning]], *[[sonnets-from-the-portuguese|Sonnets from the Portuguese]]*, Sonnet XXXIII [033-001]–[033-017]

## Analysis

Sonnet XXXIII is one of the forty-four [[petrarchan-sonnet-form|Petrarchan sonnet]]s in [[elizabeth-barrett-browning|Elizabeth Barrett Browning]]'s *[[sonnets-from-the-portuguese|Sonnets from the Portuguese]]*, composed during her [[browning-courtship|courtship]] with [[robert-browning|Robert Browning]] (1845–1846). The sonnet follows the Petrarchan form (octave ABBA ABBA, sestet with varied pattern).

## Place in the Sequence

Sonnet XXXIII occupies its position within the forty-four-sonnet arc tracing the speaker's movement from initial disbelief through surrender to transcendent [[love-theme|love]].
