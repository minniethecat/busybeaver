[021-001] Say over again, and yet once over again

[021-002] That thou dost [[love-theme|love]] me. Though the word repeated

[021-003] Should seem a “cuckoo-[[music-theme|song]],” as thou dost treat it,

[021-004] Remember, never to the hill or plain,

[021-005] Valley and wood, without her cuckoo-strain

[021-006] Comes the fresh Spring in all her green completed!

[021-007] Beloved, I, amid the darkness greeted

[021-008] By a doubtful [[soul-theme|spirit]]-voice, in that doubt’s pain

[021-009] Cry, “Speak once more—thou lovest!” Who can fear

[021-010] Too many stars, though each in heaven shall roll,

[021-011] Too many flowers, though each shall crown the year?

[021-012] Say thou dost love me, love me, love me—toll

[021-013] The silver iterance!—only minding, Dear,

[021-014] To love me also in [[silence]] with thy soul.
