[031-001] Thou comest! all is said without a word.

[031-002] I sit beneath thy looks, as children do

[031-003] In the noon-sun, with souls that tremble through

[031-004] Their happy eyelids from an unaverred

[031-005] Yet prodigal inward joy. Behold, I erred

[031-006] In that last doubt! and yet I cannot rue

[031-007] The sin most, but the occasion—that we two

[031-008] Should for a moment stand unministered

[031-009] By a mutual presence. Ah, keep near and close,

[031-010] Thou dove-like help! and when my fears would rise,

[031-011] With thy broad [[heart-imagery|heart]] serenely interpose:

[031-012] Brood down with thy divine sufficiencies

[031-013] These thoughts which tremble when bereft of those,

[031-014] Like callow birds left desert to the skies.
