[037-001] Pardon, oh, pardon, that my [[soul-theme|soul]] should make

[037-002] Of all that strong divineness which I know

[037-003] For thine and thee, an image only so

[037-004] Formed of the sand, and fit to shift and break.

[037-005] It is that distant years which did not take

[037-006] Thy sovereignty, recoiling with a blow,

[037-007] Have forced my swimming brain to undergo

[037-008] Their doubt and dread, and blindly to forsake

[037-009] Thy purity of likeness and distort

[037-010] Thy worthiest [[love-theme|love]] to a worthless counterfeit:

[037-011] As if a shipwrecked Pagan, safe in port,

[037-012] His guardian sea-god to commemorate,

[037-013] Should set a sculptured porpoise, gills a-snort

[037-014] And vibrant tail, within the temple-gate.
