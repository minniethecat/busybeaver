## Full Text

> If I leave all for thee, wilt thou exchange [035-001]
>  [035-002]
> And be all to me? Shall I never miss [035-003]
>  [035-004]
> Home-talk and blessing and the common kiss [035-005]
>  [035-006]
> That comes to each in turn, nor count it strange, [035-007]
>  [035-008]
> When I look up, to drop on a new range [035-009]
>  [035-010]
> Of walls and floors, another home than this? [035-011]
>  [035-012]
> Nay, wilt thou fill that place by me which is [035-013]
>  [035-014]
> Filled by dead eyes too tender to know change? [035-015]
>  [035-016]
> That’s hardest! If to conquer love has tried, [035-017]
>  [035-018]
> To conquer grief tries more, as all things prove; [035-019]
>  [035-020]
> For grief indeed is love and grief beside. [035-021]
>  [035-022]
> Alas, I have grieved so I am hard to love— [035-023]
>  [035-024]
> Yet love me—wilt thou? Open thy heart wide [035-025]
>  [035-026]
> And fold within, the wet wings of thy dove. [035-027]

— [[elizabeth-barrett-browning|Elizabeth Barrett Browning]], *[[sonnets-from-the-portuguese|Sonnets from the Portuguese]]*, Sonnet XXXV [035-001]–[035-027]

## Analysis

Sonnet XXXV is one of the forty-four [[petrarchan-sonnet-form|Petrarchan sonnet]]s in [[elizabeth-barrett-browning|Elizabeth Barrett Browning]]'s *[[sonnets-from-the-portuguese|Sonnets from the Portuguese]]*, composed during her [[browning-courtship|courtship]] with [[robert-browning|Robert Browning]] (1845–1846). The poem continues the sequence's exploration of [[love-theme|love]], devotion, and [[soul-theme|spirit]]ual transformation.

The sonnet follows the Petrarchan form (octave ABBA ABBA, sestet with varied pattern), employing [[elizabeth-barrett-browning|Barrett Browning]]'s characteristic blend of intimate address and rhetorical elevation.

## Place in the Sequence

Sonnet XXXV occupies its position within the forty-four-sonnet arc tracing the speaker's movement from initial disbelief through surrender to transcendent [[love-theme|love]]. Each sonnet contributes a distinct emotional or rhetorical development to this larger narrative.
