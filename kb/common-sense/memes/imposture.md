# Imposture

## Definition

Imposture is the central concept in Holbach's genetic account of religion. Religious doctrines are not [[dreams|revelations]] from [[god|God]] — they are fabrications by human [[existence|being]]s who claim [[divinity|divine]] authority in order to rule over others. "These ambitious [[charlatan|impostor]]s took exclusive possession of the minds of their votaries" (ch. II). The [[clergy|priests]] are not mistaken believers who happen to be wrong; they are knowing deceivers who exploit the [[belief|credulity]] of the masses for wealth and power.

Holbach's imposture thesis has three components: the [[charlatan|impostor]] (the priest who fabricates [[dreams|revelations]]), the victim (the credulous believer who accepts them), and the mechanism (the manipulation of fear, hope, and ignorance). "An [[charlatan|impostor]] can sometimes die with courage; he makes then, as is said, a [[morality|virtue]] of necessity" (ch. II). Even [[martyr|martyrdom]] proves nothing, because [[charlatan|impostor]]s can be as courageous as [[vice|sin]]cere believers — both are [[desire|drive]]n by the structural incentives of the priestly role.

The imposture thesis is Holbach's most radical argument, because it denies religion [[eve]]n the dignity of [[vice|sin]]cere error. [[religion|Religion]] is not a genuine attempt to understand the [[universe|cosmos]] that happens to be wrong — it is a [[certainty|confidence]] trick. "They discredit and treat each other as [[charlatan|impostor]]s and liars" (ch. I) — each religion recognizes the imposture in [[eve]]ry other religion but denies it in its own.

## Historical Context

The imposture thesis has ancient roots. Critias the Sophist (5th century BCE) argued that the gods were invented by a clever [[monarch|ruler]] to enforce morality through fear of invisible witnesses. Machiavelli praised Numa Pompilius for fabricating [[divinity|divine]] sanction for Roman laws. The 17th-century libertines revived the imposture thesis as a weapon against [[revelation|revealed religion]], and the discovery of the [[charlatan|fraud]]ulence of the *Do[[nations|nation]] of Constantine* gave it documentary force.

The radical Enlightenment made the imposture thesis systematic. The *Treatise of the Three Impostors* (anonymous, c. 1719) — which circulated clandestinely and was attributed to [[baruch-spinoza|Spinoza]] — argued that [[moses|Moses]], Jesus, and [[mohammed|Muhammad]] were the three great [[charlatan|impostor]]s who divided [[earth|the world]] between them. Holbach's originality was to combine the imposture thesis with [[john-locke|Locke]]an epistemology: religion is an imposture not merely because [[clergy|priests]] are wicked but because all knowledge must come through the senses, and revelation is not a sensory channel.

## Core Arguments

**1. [[priest|Priest]]s knowingly fabricate [[dreams|revelations]].** "These ambitious [[charlatan|impostor]]s took exclusive possession of the minds of their votaries, rendered them unsocial, and made them consider as outcasts all those who had not the same ideas." (ch. II). The fabrication of religion is a political act — a seizure of mental territory by a class of professional deceivers.

**2. Each religion exposes the imposture of [[eve]]ry other.** "They discredit and treat each other as [[charlatan|impostor]]s and liars" (ch. I). When a [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian dismisses the Koran as imposture and a Muslim dismisses the [[trinity|Trinity]] as idolatry, each is correct about the other. The conclusion neither draws is that all are impostures.

**3. [[miracle|Miracle]]s are the primary tool of imposture.** "Would be suspected of having been deceived by their own imagi[[nations|nation]], and of permitting themselves to be seduced by the illusions which skillful [[charlatan|impostor]]s performed before their eyes" (ch. II). [[miracle|Miracle]] claims are always the product of imagi[[nations|nation]], deception, or both — never of credible testimony.

**4. [[martyr|Martyr]]dom does not prove [[vice|sin]]cerity.** "An [[charlatan|impostor]] can sometimes die with courage; he makes then, as is said, a [[morality|virtue]] of necessity." (ch. II). The willingness to die for a belief proves only that humans can be intensely committed, not that what they beli[[eve]] is true.

## Relation to Other Concepts

Imposture is the mechanism by which [[Superstition]] is manufactured and maintained. It exploits [[Ignorance]] and [[Fear]] to produce [[Credulity]]. [[Persecution]] is the enforcement arm of imposture — the means by which [[charlatan|impostor]]s suppress those who might expose them. [[Priest]] is the professional role of the [[charlatan|impostor]], and [[Revelation]] is the [[charlatan|fraud]]ulent product. [[Reform]] and [[Progress]] require the exposure of imposture as the first step.
