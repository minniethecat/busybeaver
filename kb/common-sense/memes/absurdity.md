# Absurdity

## Definition

Absurdity, in Holbach's lexicon, is not mere falsehood or error — it is the extreme of irrationality, the point at which a claim becomes "a palpable absurdity, a manifest nonsense" (ch. CXI) that no properly functioning mind can entertain. The category of the absurd is central to Holbach's rhetorical and philosophical strategy: rather than merely arguing against religion, he aims to show that its doctrines are so self-evidently irrational that they would be laughed at if they were not enforced by habit and authority.

The absurd has a precise logical structure. A claim is absurd when it "implies contradiction" (ch. CIV) — when it asserts what cannot possibly be true because its very formulation violates the principle of non-contradiction. The doctrine of the [[trinity|Trinity]] is absurd because it asserts that one is three and three is one. The doctrine of transubstantiation is absurd because it asserts that bread is simultaneously bread and the body of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]. [[miracle|Miracle]]s are absurd because they assert the suspension of natural laws by a [[existence|being]] who supposedly established those laws.

But Holbach's use of absurdity goes beyond logical analysis into social critique. "Is it not the height of absurdity," he asks, to see [[nation|nations]] "torn by religious quarrels, and ready to cut each other's throats about opinions which neither of them understand?" (ch. XXII). The absurd is not just logically flawed — it is practically destructive. Whole societies organize themselves around absurd beliefs, with real consequences in blood and [[evil|suffering]].

## Historical Context

The category of the absurd has roots in classical philosophy. Aristotle's *reductio ad absurdum* was a [[model|standard]] method of refutation: show that a claim leads to an absurd consequence, and the claim is thereby refuted. The ancient skeptics used absurdity arguments against [[doctrine|dogma]]tic philosophers of all schools, [[demon|demons]]trating that [[eve]]ry positive philosophical system generated absurdities when pushed to its limits.

In early modern philosophy, "absurdity" was a term of strong condem[[nations|nation]]. [[john-locke|Locke]] argued that it was absurd to claim innate ideas when all ideas could be shown to originate in experience. [[david-hume|Hume]] called the doctrine of transubstantiation absurd because it contradicted the uniform [[evidence|testimony of the senses]]. The deists — Toland, Tindal, Collins — argued that the [[mysteries]] of [[revelation|revealed religion]] were absurd accretions on a simple, rational [[theism|natural religion]].

Holbach radicalizes this tradition. Where the deists distinguished between rational [[theism|natural religion]] and absurd [[revelation|revealed religion]], Holbach argues that all religion is absurd at its foundation. "Every religion is an absurdity" (ch. VII) — not just some versions of some religions, but the very project of positing supernatural [[existence|being]]s, forces, and [[dreams|revelations]] as expla[[nation|nations]] of [[phenomena|natural phenomena]].

## Core Arguments

**1. [[religion|Religion]] is absurd at its foundation.** The notion of an invisible, incomprehensible [[existence|being]] who created [[earth|the world]] and governs it by inscrutable decrees is itself absurd. "The notion of [[god|God]] is impossible" (ch. VIII) because it [[attribute|attributes]] incompatible properties to a [[vice|sin]]gle [[existence|being]] and then declares the result incomprehensible.

**2. Mysteries are absurdities in theological dress.** "A [[secrecy|mystery]] is nothing but a contradiction, a palpable absurdity, a manifest nonsense" (111-002). The church calls absurdities "[[mysteries]]" and demands that believers accept them as acts of [[religion|faith]]. This relabeling does not change the logical status of the claims.

**3. [[supernatural|Supernatural]] expla[[nation|nations]] are absurd because they explain nothing.** To explain a phenomenon by reference to "supernatural causes" is to explain it by something more obscure than the thing to be explained. "The absurdity of supernatural causes" (ch. CIV) is that they substitute one [[secrecy|mystery]] for another while claiming to provide [[knowledge|understanding]].

**4. The morality derived from absurd doctrines is itself absurd.** Religious morality produces "absurdity of the morality and of the religious [[morality|virtue]]s" (ch. CLXVIII) — [[morality|virtue]]s that are useless or harmful to society, such as monastic celibacy, self-mortification, and the hatred of [[heresy|heretic]]s.

## Relation to Other Concepts

Absurdity is the extreme form of [[Contradiction]] — a contradiction so flagrant that it cannot be disguised by theological subtlety. It is the endpoint of [[Superstition]], which begins with [[belief|credulity]] and ends with the acceptance of manifest nonsense. [[Mystery]] is absurdity rebranded as profundity. [[Reason]] is the faculty that detects absurdity; [[Faith]] is the disposition to accept it. [[Miracle]]s are absurdities presented as [[proof|evidence]], and [[Theology]] is the systematic defense of absurdity.
