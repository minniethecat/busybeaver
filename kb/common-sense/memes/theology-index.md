# Theology Index

This page aggregates all entries tagged with `[[religion|theology]]` — the [[guilt|sin]]gle largest thematic cluster in the wiki. Holbach's *Common Sense* is at its core a theological demolition project: it examines [[eve]]ry pillar of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian doctrine ([[god|God]], revelation, [[bible|scripture]], [[miracle|miracles]], [[sacrament|sacraments]], [[clergy|priesthood]]) and argues each is either logically incoherent, historically [[charlatan|fraud]]ulent, or morally corrupting. The entries below span abstract theological categories like [[theism]] and [[deism]], specific [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian doctrines like [[trinity]] and [[incarnation]], and the institutional apparatus that sustains belief — [[priestcraft]], [[hierarchy]], and [[superstition]].

## Key Sub-Themes

### Doctrine of God

Entries examining Holbach's critique of the [[attribute|divine attributes]]: [[omnipotence]], [[omniscience]], [[benevolence]], [[simplicity]], [[immutability]], and the logical problems they generate ([[problem-of-evil]], [[free-will]], [[divine-hiddenness]]).

### Revelation and Scripture

Holbach's historical-critical dismantling of the [[bible|Bible]] as a human document: [[revelation]], [[scripture]], [[inspiration]], [[canon]], [[prophecy]], and the contradictions between [[old-testament]] and [[new-testament]].

### Ritual and Worship

The materialist critique of religious practice: [[sacrament]], [[baptism]], [[eucharist]], [[mass]], [[prayer]], [[fasting]], [[penance]], and [[pilgrimage]] — all analyzed as empty ceremonies with no supernatural efficacy.

### Ecclesiastical Power

The institutional analysis of organized religion: [[priestcraft]], [[hierarchy]], [[papacy]], [[infallibility]], [[excommunication]], [[anathema]], and [[tithe]] — religion as a system of social control.
