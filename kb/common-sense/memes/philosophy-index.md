# Philosophy Index

This page aggregates all entries tagged with `philosophy` — the conceptual backbone of Holbach's Enlightenment project. *Common Sense* is not merely a polemic against religion; it is a systematic exposition of [[materialism|materialist philosophy]], drawing on the traditions of [[epicureanism]], [[stoicism]], [[spinozism]], and the French Enlightenment. The entries below range from foundational metaphysical commitments ([[materialism]], [[determinism]], [[necessity]]) through epistemological principles ([[empiricism]], [[skepticism]], [[reason]]) to practical [[morality|ethics]] ([[virtue]], [[utility]], [[happiness]]).

## Key Sub-Themes

### Metaphysics and Ontology

Holbach's universe is matter in motion, governed by necessary laws. Entries include [[materialism]], [[determinism]], [[necessity]], [[causality]], [[nature]], [[motion]], [[matter]], [[substance]], and [[essence]].

### Epistemology and Method

How we know what we know — and why religious claims fail the test. Entries include [[empiricism]], [[reason]], [[skepticism]], [[evidence]], [[demonstration]], [[probability]], and the critique of [[faith]] and [[revelation]].

### Philosophical Anthropology

The materialist account of human [[universe|nature]]. Entries include [[soul]], [[mind]], [[consciousness]], [[free-will]], [[passion]], [[desire]], [[self-interest]], [[habit]], and [[temperament]].

### Political Philosophy

The social and political implications of [[matter|materialism]]. Entries include [[natural-rights]], [[social-contract]], [[sovereignty]], [[toleration]], [[liberty]], [[equality]], and [[justice]].
