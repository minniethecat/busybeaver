# Epistemology Index

This page aggregates all entries tagged with `epistemology` — the theory of knowledge that underlies Holbach's entire critical project. Before *Common Sense* can demolish specific religious doctrines, it must first establish *how we know anything at all*. Holbach's answer is uncompromisingly empiricist: all knowledge comes from [[experience]] through the [[senses]], processed by [[reason]]. Claims based on [[faith]], [[revelation]], or [[tradition]] fail this epistemological test and are therefore not knowledge at all — they are [[prejudice]], [[credulity]], and [[superstition]].

## Key Sub-Themes

### Sources of Knowledge

Holbach's empiricist foundations: [[experience]], [[senses]], [[observation]], [[experiment]], [[reason]], and [[evidence]] — the only valid pathways to knowledge.

### Barriers to Knowledge

The cognitive obstacles that religion exploits: [[ignorance]], [[prejudice]], [[credulity]], [[superstition]], [[habit]], [[custom]], and the power of early [[education]] to shape lifelong beliefs.

### Faith vs. Reason

The central epistemological conflict of *Common Sense*: Holbach's sustained critique of [[faith]] as belief without [[proof|evidence]], [[revelation]] as an untestable claim, [[mystery]] as a cover for [[contradiction|incoherence]], and [[authority]] as a substitute for rational inquiry.

### Certainty and Doubt

How confident can we be? Entries include [[certainty]], [[probability]], [[doubt]], [[skepticism]], [[demonstration]], [[proof]], and [[evidence]] — the epistemological vocabulary of Enlightenment rationalism.
