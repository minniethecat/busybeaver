# Trinity

## Definition

In Holbach's critique of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian doctrine, the Trinity — the claim that [[god|God]] is three persons in one substance — serves as a paradigm case of theological [[absurdity|absurd]]ity. It is not merely false; it is logically incoherent in a way that exposes the irrational foundation of all [[revelation|revealed religion]]. By demanding belief in "the inconceivable [[secrecy|mystery]] of the Trinity," [[christianity|Christianity]] places itself in the same epistemological category as the pagan fables and Muslim [[dreams|revelations]] it dismisses.

Holbach does not offer a sustained theological analysis of Trinitarian doctrine. Instead, he uses the Trinity as a comparative weapon: the [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian who scoffs at transubstantiation or scoffs at the Koran beli[[eve]]s something equally [[absurdity|absurd]].

## Holbach's Use of the Trinity

**1. The Trinity as comparative [[absurdity|absurd]]ity.** Holbach's most devastating use of the Trinity appears in a passage comparing religious perspectives on each other's [[absurdity|absurd]]ities. The Protestant "beli[[eve]]s firmly in the inconceivable [[secrecy|mystery]] of the Trinity" yet "ridicules the Catholic [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian because the latter beli[[eve]]s in the [[secrecy|mystery]] of the transubstantiation." Each [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian [[sect|denomination]] mocks the other's "[[mysteries]]" while defending its own as [[creed|articles of faith]]. The objective observer sees only "[[absurdity|absurd]]ities" in both.

**2. The mutual judgment problem.** The [[mohammed|Mohammed]]an "treats the [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian as an idolater and a dog; he sees but [[absurdity|absurd]]ities in his religion; he imagines that nothing is more impious or more unreasonable than to worship a man or to beli[[eve]] in the Trinity." From outside [[christianity|Christianity]], the Trinity appears as irrational as the Koran appears to [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ians. Neither side has a principled basis for privileging its own r[[eve]]lation.

**3. The "ineffable [[secrecy|mystery]]" label is not an expla[[nations|nation]].** Holbach's broader critique of "[[secrecy|mystery]]" as an epistemological category applies directly to the Trinity. To call the Trinity an "ineffable [[secrecy|mystery]]" is to admit that the doctrine cannot be understood — which is not a reason to beli[[eve]] it but a reason to reject it. "[[nothingness|Nothing]] appears more ridiculous" than a [[god|God]] defined by [[contradiction|logical contradiction]].

## In the Text

On mutual religious judgment (ch. 111):

> "The Protestant Christian, who without scruple worships a man, and who believes firmly in the inconceivable mystery of the Trinity, ridicules the Catholic Christian because the latter believes in the mystery of the transubstantiation."
>
> (116-002)

On the [[mohammed|Mohammed]]an perspective:

> "The Mohammedan, on his side, treats the Christian as an idolater and a dog; he sees but absurdities in his religion; he imagines that nothing is more impious or more unreasonable than to worship a man or to believe in the Trinity."
>
> (116-002)

On the [[absurdity|absurd]]ity of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian [[mysteries]] from an outsider's view:

> "You consider the ineffable mystery of the Trinity very absurd. Nothing appears more ridiculous to you than a God who changes Himself into bread and who is eaten every day in a thousand different places."
>
> (118-003)

## Relation to Other Concepts

The Trinity is a specific instance of [[mystery]] — the theological category that Holbach systematically demolishes. It connects to [[transubstantiation]], [[incarnation]], and [[atonement]] — other [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian "[[mysteries]]" that demand belief in [[contradiction|logical contradiction]]. The comparative method links it to [[paganism]], [[islam]], and [[christianity]].
