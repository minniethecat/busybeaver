# Ethics Index

This page aggregates all entries tagged with `[[morality|ethics]]` — the practical heart of Holbach's philosophy. *Common Sense* argues that morality does not require religion; on the contrary, religion corrupts morality by substituting arbitrary [[divinity|divine]] commands for the natural principles of human welfare. The entries below cover the full spectrum of Holbach's naturalistic [[morality|ethics]]: from foundational concepts ([[utility]], [[happiness]], [[virtue]]) through specific [[morality|virtue]]s and vices ([[temperance]], [[justice]], [[cruelty]], [[fanaticism]]) to the social mechanisms that sustain moral life ([[education]], [[law]], [[public-opinion]]).

## Key Sub-Themes

### Foundations of Naturalistic Ethics

Holbach grounds morality in human [[universe|nature]] and [[utility|social utility]] rather than [[divinity|divine]] command: [[utility]], [[happiness]], [[pleasure]], [[pain]], [[self-interest]], [[sympathy]], and [[conscience]] as natural psychological phenomena.

### Virtues

The natural [[morality|virtue]]s that promote individual and social welfare: [[virtue]], [[temperance]], [[justice]], [[benevolence]], [[compassion]], [[gratitude]], [[honesty]], and [[courage]].

### Vices and Moral Corruption

The vices that undermine human welfare — many of them, Holbach argues, fostered or amplified by religion: [[vice]], [[cruelty]], [[fanaticism]], [[intolerance]], [[persecution]], [[hypocrisy]], and [[superstition]] as a moral poison.

### Moral Education and Social Reform

How societies [[worship|cult]]ivate or corrupt moral character: [[education]], [[habit]], [[custom]], [[law]], [[public-opinion]], [[praise]], [[blame]], [[reward]], and [[punishment]].
