# Sacred

## Definition

For Holbach, the sacred is not a property that anything possesses by [[universe|nature]] — it is a label applied by human [[existence|being]]s to shield certain ideas, institutions, and persons from exami[[nations|nation]]. "[[priest|Priest]]s in all ages have made it their sacred duty to [[torture|torment]], to persecute, to destroy those whose opinions differed from theirs" (ch. CXLVIII). The sacred is a cloak beneath which the worst actions are sheltered from moral judgment.

The concept operates as an epistemic blockade. To declare something sacred is to announce that it may not be questioned, analyzed, or criticized — that reason must halt at its threshold. "[[theology|Theology]] in [[eve]]ry age has been the bed of Procrustes" (ch. CCIII), cutting and stretching thought to fit pre-determined sacred conclusions. This is the opposite of free inquiry: where free inquiry follows [[proof|evidence]] wherever it leads, the sacred pre-selects the desti[[nations|nation]] and forbids deviation.

Holbach's critique of the sacred is not merely theological but political. "What horrors would have been spared this [[universe|world]], if some bold hand had dared to tear away this sacred cloak of which men make such a bad use?" (ch. CLIX). The sacred protects not [[god|God]] but [[clergy|priests]], not truth but power. It is the ultimate instrument of intellectual [[despotism|tyranny]] — the claim that certain questions may not be asked.

## Historical Context

The sociology of the sacred was developed most fully by Émile Durkheim a century after Holbach, but Holbach's analysis anticipates its core insight: the sacred is socially constructed and serves social functions. Durkheim would argue that the sacred is society worshipping itself; Holbach argued that the sacred is [[clergy|priesthood]] protecting itself.

In Holbach's immediate context, the sacred was the primary obstacle to Enlightenment. The [[catholic-church|Catholic Church]] claimed sacred authority for doctrines, texts, and institutions that could not withstand rational scrutiny. [[rene-descartes|Descartes]], [[malebranche|Malebranche]], and [[gottfried-leibniz|Leibniz]] — the greatest minds of the preceding century — had all been "compelled to invent hypotheses and evasions in order to reconcile their discoveries with the reveries and the blunders which religion had rendered sacred" (ch. CCIII). Even the most brilliant thinkers were forced to distort their own systems to avoid collision with the sacred.

[[enlightenment|The Enlightenment]]'s project of secularization was fundamentally a project of de-sacralization — removing the protective label from institutions and ideas so they could be examined by the same [[model|standard]]s as [[eve]]rything else.

## Core Arguments

**1. [[nothingness|Nothing]] is inherently sacred.** "Sacredness" is a human attribution, not a natural property. Objects, texts, and institutions become sacred only when human [[existence|being]]s agree to treat them as such — and this agreement is manufactured by those who benefit from it. The sacred oath, the sacred text, the sacred office — all are human constructions.

**2. The sacred is a shield for [[crime|wrongdoing]].** "What horrors would have been spared this [[universe|world]], if some bold hand had dared to tear away this sacred cloak?" (ch. CLIX). Under cover of the sacred, [[clergy|priests]] have persecuted [[heresy|heretic]]s, [[monarch|ruler]]s have crushed dissent, and ordinary moral inhibitions have been suspended. The sacred does not elevate conduct; it excuses the worst conduct.

**3. The sacred retards intellectual progress.** "How many subterfuges and mental gymnastics all the ancient and modern thinkers have employed, in order to avoid falling out with the ministers of the [[god|God]]s!" (ch. CCIII). The sacred forces thinkers to cripple their own reasoning — to stop where inquiry becomes dangerous, to prevaricate where honesty would offend.

**4. De-sacralization is liberation.** To remove the sacred label from an idea is not to destroy it but to subject it to the same [[model|standard]]s as all other ideas. [[reason|Reason]], not sacred authority, becomes the arbiter of truth. This is the precondition for intellectual and moral progress.

## Relation to Other Concepts

The sacred is the mechanism by which [[Authority]] is immunized against criticism. It is the antithesis of [[Reason]], which recognizes no forbidden questions. [[Dogma]] is sacred belief — doctrine that may not be examined. [[Priest]] is the profession that manufactures and polices the sacred. [[Tyranny]] is made possible when [[monarch|ruler]]s are declared sacred. [[Science]] is systematic de-sacralization — the refusal to exempt any claim from scrutiny.
