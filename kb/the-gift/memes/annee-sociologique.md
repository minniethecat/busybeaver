## Overview

**L'Année Sociologique** is a scholarly journal founded by [[emile-durkheim]] in 1898, which gave its name to the broader **Durkheimian school** (the "Année Sociologique school") — a collective of sociologists, anthropologists, economists, and historians who pursued [[emile-durkheim|Durkheim]]'s program of studying social phenomena as *things* (*choses sociales*).

The journal published systematic reviews of literature across the social sciences, organizing knowledge around the concept of the *fait social* (social fact). After [[emile-durkheim|Durkheim]]'s death in 1917, [[marcel-mauss]] became the central figure of the group and revived the journal in a second series (1925–1927).

## Intellectual Significance

The Année Sociologique school established sociology as an autonomous discipline in France. Its members applied [[emile-durkheim|Durkheim]]'s sociological method to diverse fields: religion, law, economics, language, and [[classification]] systems. The collaborative model of the journal — with its extensive review sections — created a shared intellectual framework that bridged sociology and anthropology.

[[marcel-mauss|Mauss]]'s work on the [[gift-economy]] exemplified the school's comparative method, drawing on ethnographic data from the [[potlatch]], [[kula-exchange]], and other [[total-prestation|total prestation]] systems.

## In the Text

[[marcel-mauss|Mauss]] dedicated *The Gift* to the memory of [[emile-durkheim|Durkheim]] and the collective enterprise:

> This work is part of the body of research undertaken by Durkheim and some of his disciples and ourselves. It is the result of the collaboration of the Année Sociologique group.
>
> （Mauss 1966:v）
