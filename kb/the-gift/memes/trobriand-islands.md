## Overview

The **Trobriand Islands** (also known as the Kiriwina Islands) are an archipelago of coral atolls located in the Solomon Sea, off the eastern coast of [[papua-new-guinea|Papua New Guinea]]. They are part of the Milne Bay Province of [[papua-new-guinea]] and belong to the Massim cultural region of [[melanesia]].

The Trobriands are of singular importance in the history of anthropology as the field site where [[bronislaw-malinowski]] conducted pioneering participant-observation fieldwork (1915–1918) and documented the [[kula-exchange]] system.

## Role in the Kula

The Trobriand Islands are a central node in the [[kula-exchange]] ring that links island communities across the Massim region. From the Trobriands:

- **Mwali** (arm-shells) travel south and west, moving counterclockwise around the ring
- **Soulava** (necklaces) travel north and east, moving clockwise

Trobriand men undertake perilous ocean voyages in outrigger canoes to exchange these valuables with partners on distant islands, sustaining a network of [[ceremonial-exchange|ceremonial trade]] that spans hundreds of miles.

## In the Text

[[bronislaw-malinowski|Malinowski]] describes the Trobriand setting:

> The Trobriand Islands are coral islands, flat and low, surrounded by a barrier reef... The villages are scattered over the island, hidden among the groves of fruit trees and palms.
>
> （Malinowski 1922:51）

[[marcel-mauss]] drew extensively on [[bronislaw-malinowski|Malinowski]]'s Trobriand ethnography in *The Gift*, using the Kula as a paradigmatic example of [[total-prestation]].
