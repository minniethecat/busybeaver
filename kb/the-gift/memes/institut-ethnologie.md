## Overview

The **Institut d'Ethnologie** (Institute of Ethnology) was established in 1925 at the University of Paris by [[marcel-mauss]], [[lucien-levy-bruhl]], and [[paul-rivet]]. It was the first academic institution in France dedicated to the professional training of ethnologists (cultural anthropologists).

The Institute's founding coincided with [[marcel-mauss|Mauss]]'s publication of *The Gift* (*Essai sur le don*) and reflected the growing institutionalization of anthropology as a discipline distinct from sociology and philosophy.

## Teaching and Legacy

[[marcel-mauss|Mauss]] taught **descriptive ethnology** at the Institute, training students in the comparative method that characterized the [[annee-sociologique|Année Sociologique]] school. His lectures on [[gift-economy|gift exchange]], [[total-prestation]], body techniques, and [[magic]] formed the core curriculum for a generation of French anthropologists.

Among the Institute's early students were several figures who later became prominent in French anthropology, including [[claude-levi-strauss]], who acknowledged [[marcel-mauss|Mauss]]'s profound influence on [[structuralism|structural anthropology]].

## Relationship to Mauss's Work

The Institute was the institutional vehicle through which [[marcel-mauss|Mauss]] disseminated his ideas about the [[gift-economy]] and the method of studying [[total-prestation|total social facts]]. His teaching emphasized meticulous ethnographic observation combined with broad comparative analysis — an approach exemplified in his own work on the [[potlatch]] and [[kula-exchange]].
