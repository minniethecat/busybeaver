# Neville Shandon

## Overview

Neville Shandon is the brother of Roger and Ernest Shandon, killed alongside Roger in the Whistlefield Maze. His death as a co-victim — rather than as the sole target — complicates the investigation's understanding of motive. If Roger was the intended victim, why was Neville killed as well? If Neville was the target, why Roger? The double murder suggests a motive that encompasses both brothers, narrowing the field of possible killers.

Neville, like Roger, exists in the novel primarily as an absence: he dies in Chapter II, and what we know of him comes through others' recollections. His role as co-victim is structurally significant — the double murder transforms the case from a single-victim crime (with inheritance as the obvious motive) to a more complex pattern requiring a more complex explanation.

## Role in the Plot

Neville's death alongside Roger creates the novel's central puzzle: who would want both brothers dead? The inheritance motive — equal division among four beneficiaries — would be served by Roger's death alone. Killing Neville as well suggests either a different motive or a killer who needed both brothers eliminated for non-financial reasons. Sir Clinton's ultimate solution accounts for both deaths within a single coherent framework.

## Relationships
