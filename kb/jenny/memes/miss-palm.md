Miss Palm is a minor character in Sigrid Undset's *[[jenny-winge|Jenny]]* (1911), identified as a Danish nurse within the Scandinavian expatriate community in [[rome|Rome]]. She appears primarily during the [[Campagna]] dinner party in Part I, Chapter VII, where her lively spirits provide a contrast to the evening's gathering emotional tensions.

## Appearances

### The Campagna Dinner

Miss Palm is part of the group that travels to an osteria on the [[via-appia-nuova|Via Appia Nuova]] for a dinner party: "[[ahlin|Ahlin]], Heggen, Gram, and Miss Palm, the Danish nurse, made up the party" (007-027). During the meal, she and [[gunnar-heggen|Gunnar Heggen]] engage in playful antics — "Heggen and Miss Palm were chasing each other round the tables" (007-053) — providing a moment of levity before the evening's emotional turn with [[ahlin|Ahlin]]'s breakdown.

After the dinner's darker moments, Miss Palm alone maintains her composure: "Miss Palm alone was in good spirits" (007-056). Her persistent cheerfulness in the face of the group's emotional turmoil marks her as an outsider to the deeper currents that bind the core characters — a reminder that the expatriate community includes ordinary, undramatic presences alongside its more turbulent personalities.
