Hjerrild is a composer in the Scandinavian expatriate artist circle in Sigrid Undset's *[[jenny-winge|Jenny]]* (1911). He appears most prominently in Part I, Chapter VI, where his sharp wit and political opinions enliven a dinner gathering at the [[via-vantaggio|Via Vantaggio]] studio. Hjerrild also has a brief romantic entanglement with [[cesca-jahrman|Francesca Cesca Jahrman]], making him part of the web of attractions and jealousies that characterizes the group's social dynamic.

## Biography

### Part I — The Rome Circle

Hjerrild is introduced at a dinner party hosted by Cesca, where he converses with [[helge-gram|Helge Gram]] and [[gunnar-heggen|Gunnar Heggen]] about art, socialism, and social class. His presence adds intellectual and political texture to the artist circle, which otherwise centers on painting and sculpture:

> "Much of a bully for me," said Hjerrild, "unpardonably rude, but perhaps not altogether wrong."

(006-036)

He is described as working on a book — "Hjerrild, I think, is working at a book" (020-023) — suggesting literary or scholarly ambitions beyond composition.

### The Campagna Affair

Hjerrild's most significant narrative function involves his brief romantic involvement with Cesca. During a period when Cesca is navigating her complicated feelings for [[ahlin|Ahlin]] and others, she goes to the [[campagna|Campagna]] with Hjerrild:

> She had been to the Campagna again with Hjerrild, and he had kissed her.

(007-026)

This episode reveals Hjerrild as one of several men drawn into Cesca's orbit — a pattern that underscores her magnetic but turbulent presence in the group.

### Part II and Beyond

Hjerrild's later mentions are sparse. He appears in Cesca's retrospective account of her romantic history, grouped with "[[hans|Hans Hermann]], [[douglas|Norman Douglas]], and Hjerrild, and all the rest of her achievements" (020-033). The dismissive tone of this reference suggests that Hjerrild, like others, was ultimately a footnote in Cesca's complex romantic life rather than a lasting attachment.

## Character Traits

- **Witty**: His dialogue is marked by sharp humor — "Mr. Heggen is a count, of many airy castles" (006-054)
- **Politically engaged**: Expresses socialist sympathies and critiques of class privilege
- **Intellectually curious**: Working on a book, suggesting creative ambitions beyond music
- **Minor romantic player**: His brief involvement with Cesca places him in the novel's network of desire and disappointment

## Relationships

- **[[cesca-jahrman|Francesca Cesca Jahrman]]**: Brief romantic involvement — a kiss on the [[campagna|Campagna]], quickly passed over
- **[[helge-gram|Helge Gram]]**: Conversational foil at the dinner party; Hjerrild's wit contrasts with Helge's insecurity
- **[[gunnar-heggen|Gunnar Heggen]]**: Intellectual peer; their banter about class and art enlivens the group dynamic
