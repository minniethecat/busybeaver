# Paris

**Paris** appears throughout Sigrid Undset's *Jenny* (1911) as the quintessential European art capital —
a city synonymous with cosmopolitanism, fashion, and artistic aspiration. Although never a direct setting,
Paris is referenced in conversation, memory, and aspiration by nearly every major character.

## Paris as Cultural Benchmark

For the Scandinavian artists of the novel, Paris represents the pinnacle of artistic culture. Gunnar Heggen's
Parisian experience becomes a mark of his worldliness:

> "It was natural for him to have been in Paris as in a restaurant in Christiania." [002-106]

Heggen himself captures the city's romantic aura:

> "In springtime and in Paris," said Heggen, with a smile. [002-116]

## A Shared Memory

Paris functions as a shared reference point for the expatriate community. Jenny reflects on her own time there:

> "I was in Paris for two months in the summer, and I was very pleased with it now." [002-176]

During the early, happier phase of her relationship with Helge, Paris becomes part of their intimate repertoire:

> "The lovely, peaceful time in Paris, when they had worked hard..." [008-042]

## Cultural Capital

Cesca and Jenny's Parisian purchases carry social weight back in Norway, where imported items become markers of status:

> "The dress she had told them she had bought in Paris was handled almost with reverence." [012-021]

The city also appears in Gunnar's suggestion for Jenny's escape after the death of her child:

> "She could take the child the same for her trip to Paris." [024-070]
