The Colosseum (Colosseo) — the Flavian Amphitheatre, ancient [[rome|Rome]]'s most iconic ruin — appears in Part III of Sigrid Undset's *[[jenny-winge|Jenny]]* (1911) as part of the landscape [[jenny-winge|Jenny Winge]] inhabits after her return to the city. Unlike the earlier Roman landmarks of Part I — which frame new love and creative possibility — the Colosseum belongs to the novel's elegiac final movement.

## In the Novel

The Colosseum appears in the novel's late chapters, when [[rome|Rome]] has emptied of tourists and the weather has turned:

> They entered a little piazza, where the ruins of the Colosseum, the Palatine, and the Forum lay. Very few tourists were about.

(032-002)

The grouping of the Colosseum with the [[palatine|Palatine]] and the Forum places it within the novel's mapping of ancient [[rome|Rome]] — a zone of ruins that [[jenny-winge|Jenny]] and her companion move through in the novel's diminished present.

Later, [[jenny-winge|Jenny]] is observed from a distance:

> She came up the street from the Piazza round the Colosseum, and looking in her direction he saw her approaching.

(032-009)

## Key Scenes

The Colosseum does not host a discrete scene — rather, it forms part of the ambient Roman landscape of Part III:

- **The empty city**: "Very few tourists" — [[rome|Rome]] in Part III has been stripped of the social bustle of Part I. The Colosseum stands in a depopulated city, its grandeur now a backdrop to [[jenny-winge|Jenny]]'s solitude.
- **Approach and distance**: [[jenny-winge|Jenny]] is seen "coming up the street" from the Colosseum area — she moves through the ruins rather than stopping at them, her trajectory suggesting a restlessness that will find its terminus in the novel's final pages.

## Significance

The Colosseum's role in *[[jenny-winge|Jenny]]* is primarily atmospheric:

- **The weight of antiquity**: Unlike the [[campagna|Campagna]] (a space of natural renewal) or the artist-frequented streets of Part I, the Colosseum represents [[rome|Rome]]'s permanent, indifferent past — stone that has outlasted empires and will outlast individual sorrow.
- **Return and diminishment**: In Part III, the grand monuments that once formed the backdrop to [[jenny-winge|Jenny]]'s romantic awakening now frame her isolation. The Colosseum — built for spectacle — witnesses only her quiet passage.
- **Contrast with Part I**: The [[trajan-forum|Trajan Forum]] hosted Helge's first kiss; the Colosseum area hosts only [[jenny-winge|Jenny]]'s solitary movement through a city that no longer feels like hers.
