# Berlin

**Berlin** appears in Sigrid Undset's *Jenny* (1911) as a waypoint in the Scandinavian expatriate circuit.
The German capital is mentioned in connection with Helge's travels, Cesca's movements,
and as one of several European cities that form the cultural backdrop to the novel's Roman center.

## In the Expatriate Network

Berlin belongs to the constellation of European cities — alongside Paris, Copenhagen, and Dresden — that the
novel's Scandinavian characters circulate through. Heggen mentions encountering Helge there:

> "I saw him in Berlin on my way here," Heggen said, connecting the German capital to the broader
artistic wanderings of the expatriate community. [006-029]

## Threshold City

The city functions as a threshold: Helge passes through Berlin en route to Italy, using it as a staging post
between northern Europe and the Mediterranean:

> "He went first to Dresden and then to Italy just about the time the boy died..." [027-008]

This geographical positioning — between Scandinavia and the south — mirrors Helge's own suspended state
between duty (Copenhagen) and desire (Rome).
