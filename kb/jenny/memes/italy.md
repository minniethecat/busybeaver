# Italy

**Italy** is the Mediterranean country that serves as the primary setting for Parts I and III of Sigrid Undset's *Jenny* (1911).
For the novel's expatriate artists, Italy — and Rome in particular — represents freedom, beauty, and escape from
Scandinavian provincialism.

## Artistic Magnetism

Italy exerts a powerful draw on the novel's painter characters. Jenny's first journey there is framed in terms of
romantic aspiration:

> "When I went abroad it was to the Italy of romance I turned my steps." [017-015]

Upon arrival, the reality of the Italian landscape shapes her artistic sensibility:

> "When I came to Italy the baroque took my heart and all its effectiveness." [017-017]

## Narrative Trajectory

Italy bookends the novel: Jenny arrives in Rome at the start (Part I), returns to Norway (Part II), and
comes back to Italy (Part III) after personal tragedy. Gunnar, writing from Italy, urges her return:

> "Gunnar wrote from Italy, advising her to go there." [028-010]

When Jenny finally arrives back, she writes to confirm her safe passage, closing the novel's geographical arc:

> "I have arrived safely in Italy and am quite comfortable." [028-014]
