Trajan's Forum (Forum Traiani) — one of the imperial fora of ancient [[rome|Rome]] — appears in Sigrid Undset's *[[jenny-winge|Jenny]]* (1911) as the setting for a pivotal early moment between [[jenny-winge|Jenny Winge]] and [[helge-gram|Helge Gram]]. The ruins of the ancient marketplace provide a backdrop of monumental decay against which a tentative romance begins.

## In the Novel

The scene at Trajan's Forum occurs in Part I, during one of [[jenny-winge|Jenny]] and Helge's early walks through [[rome|Rome]]:

> They had descended a staircase, leading to the Trajan Forum. On the last step he drew her to him and kissed her.

(010-001)

The architectural description — a staircase descending into the forum — mirrors the emotional movement of the moment: a descent from the everyday into a space of intimacy, framed by ancient stone. Later the same morning:

> They had passed the Forum in the morning and sat down among the ruins further on.

(010-011)

## Key Scenes

The Trajan Forum sequence is brief but structurally significant:

1. **The descent**: Helge and [[jenny-winge|Jenny]] walk down the staircase into the forum — a physical movement that prefigures the emotional descent into relationship.
2. **The kiss**: On the "last step," Helge draws [[jenny-winge|Jenny]] close — the forum's ruins witness the first explicitly physical moment between them.
3. **The aftermath**: After passing through the Forum, they sit among other ruins, the ancient city enfolding their new intimacy.

## Significance

The choice of Trajan's Forum as the site of the first kiss is not incidental:

- **Monumental backdrop**: The ruins — remnants of imperial power and centuries of decay — provide a contrast to the freshness of new love, a reminder of time's scale against human feeling.
- **The palimpsest city**: [[rome|Rome]] in *[[jenny-winge|Jenny]]* is a city built on layers of history. That [[jenny-winge|Jenny]] and Helge's romance begins among ruins aligns it with the novel's broader theme of the past's pressure on the present.
- **Public intimacy**: The Forum is a public space, and the kiss — though private — occurs in a place of civic memory, suggesting that this relationship will not remain hidden.
