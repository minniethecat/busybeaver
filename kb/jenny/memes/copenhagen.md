# Copenhagen

**Copenhagen** is the Danish capital and Helge Gram's city of origin in Sigrid Undset's *Jenny* (1911).
As the seat of the Gram family and Helge's academic background, the city represents the social
expectations and familial pressures that constrain Helge throughout the novel.

## Helge's Background

Helge's Copenhagen connections underscore his class positioning. He studied drawing there,
and his work reflects the formal academic training of the city's institutions:

> "You could see that Gram had studied in Copenhagen. The drawing was good and thorough." [013-034]

Earlier, Cesca had studied there as well, connecting the Scandinavian art world:

> "She was in Copenhagen two years ago studying." [006-020]

## Jenny's Journey

Copenhagen becomes a pivotal location in Part III when Jenny travels there after her child's death.
Helge arranges the visit, and the city becomes the stage for their final encounter:

> "Ten days later Jenny left for Copenhagen. Her mother and Bodil Berner saw her off." [026-001]

In Copenhagen, Jenny confronts the reality of her situation with Helge's family. The pressures of
respectability and the Gram family's expectations close in around her:

> "He was coming to Copenhagen, and she had to promise to stay." [026-014]

Jenny waits in Copenhagen, increasingly isolated:

> "She went back the same evening to Copenhagen. At last she was alone." [026-017]
