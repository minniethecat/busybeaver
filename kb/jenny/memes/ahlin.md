Ahlin is a Swedish sculptor living in [[rome|Rome]] as a member of the Scandinavian expatriate artist community in Sigrid Undset's novel *[[jenny-winge|Jenny]]* (1911). He is closely tied to [[cesca-jahrman|Francesca Cesca Jahrman]], whom he eventually marries, and to the broader circle of artists that includes [[jenny-winge|Jenny Winge]], [[gunnar-heggen|Gunnar Heggen]], and [[Lennart]]. Ahlin's emotional intensity — alternating between jovial camaraderie and sudden despair — makes him one of the novel's most vividly drawn supporting characters.

## Biography

### Part I — Rome

Ahlin is introduced at the novel's opening as part of the artist circle centered on the [[via-vantaggio|Via Vantaggio]] studio shared by [[jenny-winge|Jenny]] and Cesca. He is first mentioned alongside [[gunnar-heggen|Gunnar Heggen]] — "Mr. Heggen, artist, and Mr. Ahlin, sculptor" (001-071) — establishing him from the outset as a fixture of the group's social life.

Ahlin's personality is marked by a distinctive blend of warmth and volatility. At gatherings he is quick with a joke or a teasing remark, as when he interrupts a conversation with "the necklace was very fine" (002-026) or needles Cesca about her cigarette habit. Yet beneath this surface lies a deep emotional vulnerability. During a dinner in the [[campagna|Campagna]], he suddenly breaks down:

> "No, no—not if you don't care for me," he sobbed.

(007-052)

### Marriage to Cesca

Ahlin's relationship with Cesca Jahrman forms the emotional core of his arc. His devotion to her is unwavering — "Ahlin stood holding her cloak" (002-060), "Ahlin bought me quite a load of roses this morning" (004-035). He proposes in earnest, and by Part II they have married. However, the marriage proves complicated. Cesca later confides to [[jenny-winge|Jenny]] about the difficulties of their union, revealing Ahlin's jealousy, his illness, and the strain of their poverty. She describes him with protective tenderness:

> "Lennart was very weak after his illness, the doctor had ordered him to the south."

(024-055)

### Part III — Aftermath

By Part III, Ahlin and Cesca have settled in Sweden. [[jenny-winge|Jenny]] receives word from "Mrs. Ahlin, Varberg, Sweden" (026-037), and the couple remains a point of reference for the surviving characters. When [[gunnar-heggen|Gunnar Heggen]] returns to [[rome|Rome]], he asks "about Francesca Ahlin and other acquaintances" (032-044), confirming that the Ahlin-Jahrman marriage endures as a fixed point in the expatriate network.

## Character Traits

| Trait | Manifestation |
|-------|--------------|
| **Emotional intensity** | Prone to sudden tears and grand gestures; "Ahlin sobbed" after Cesca rebuffs him |
| **Protective devotion** | Constantly attending to Cesca's needs — holding her cloak, buying roses, escorting her home |
| **Artistic ambition** | A working sculptor, though his specific works are less detailed than his personality |
| **Social conviviality** | Present at nearly every group gathering, contributing banter and companionship |
| **Insecurity** | His jealousy and possessiveness toward Cesca suggest deep-seated anxiety about her affection |

## Relationships

- **[[cesca-jahrman|Francesca Cesca Jahrman]]**: The central relationship of Ahlin's life. He pursues Cesca with persistence, marries her, and their marriage — though strained — endures.
- **[[jenny-winge|Jenny Winge]]**: Ahlin is part of [[jenny-winge|Jenny]]'s social circle and is shown interacting with her regularly at gatherings and on the [[campagna|Campagna]] excursion.
- **[[gunnar-heggen|Gunnar Heggen]]**: Ahlin's male peer in the artist group. They appear together frequently — "Heggen and Ahlin were nearly always there" (006-003).
- **[[helge-gram|Helge Gram]]**: Ahlin does not like Helge; "he noticed that Ahlin did not like him" (006-006). This tension adds to Helge's sense of outsider status within the group.
- **[[lennart|Lennart]]**: Fellow Swedish artist; their fates mirror each other in their marriages to strong-willed women (Cesca and another woman respectively).
