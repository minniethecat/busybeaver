Spring functions as a recurring symbolic motif in *[[jenny-winge|Jenny]]*, appearing at moments of hope and transition. Undset uses seasonal imagery to contrast the natural world's cycles of renewal with the linear trajectory of human suffering. Spring represents possibility — but also the cruel gap between nature's indifference and [[jenny-winge|Jenny]]'s emotional devastation.

## Manifestation in the Novel

### Spring in Rome

The Roman spring provides the sensory backdrop for the novel's opening sections. Warmth, light, and blooming vegetation create an atmosphere of possibility that mirrors [[jenny-winge|Jenny]]'s optimism in Part I. The [[campagna|Campagna]] in springtime is a recurring site of both artistic inspiration and romantic encounters.

### Spring as Counterpoint

As the novel darkens, spring's persistence becomes ironic. The natural world renews itself while [[jenny-winge|Jenny]]'s life disintegrates. In Part III, the return to spring in [[rome|Rome]] after the tragedy in Norway underscores the gap between natural time and psychological time.
