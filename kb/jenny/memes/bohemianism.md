Bohemianism in *[[jenny-winge|Jenny]]* represents both a lifestyle and an ideology. The Scandinavian artists in [[rome|Rome]] live outside the norms of bourgeois society — they form casual relationships, prioritize art over economic security, and value experience over respectability. Undset presents this bohemianism sympathetically but without illusion, showing both its appeal and its costs.

## Manifestation in the Novel

### The Bohemian Circle

[[jenny-winge|Jenny]]'s social circle — Heggen, Cesca, [[ahlin|Ahlin]], [[lennart|Lennart]] — embodies bohemian values. They gather informally, speak freely about art and love, and support each other without judgment. This community provides the emotional and practical support that conventional family structures would offer in Norway.

### Freedom and Its Limits

The bohemian rejection of convention offers freedom, but it also removes protections. [[jenny-winge|Jenny]]'s pregnancy, which in a conventional marriage would bring social support, instead isolates her. The bohemian community can offer sympathy but not structural support.

### Male and Female Bohemianism

Undset subtly distinguishes between male and female bohemianism. Heggen and [[ahlin|Ahlin]] can be bohemians with few consequences — their unconventional lives do not threaten their social standing. [[jenny-winge|Jenny]] and Cesca, as women, face sharper judgment and greater vulnerability. The novel suggests that bohemian freedom is not equally available to all.
