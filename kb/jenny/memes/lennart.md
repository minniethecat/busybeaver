Lennart is a Swedish painter in Sigrid Undset's novel *[[jenny-winge|Jenny]]* (1911), part of the Scandinavian expatriate artist community in [[rome|Rome]]. Compared to the more flamboyant [[Ahlin]] or the intellectually formidable [[gunnar-heggen|Gunnar Heggen]], Lennart is a quieter figure — but his presence is woven through the novel in ways that illuminate the domestic and emotional lives of the central characters. His marriage to a woman described in Cesca's long confession reveals the hidden costs of the bohemian-artist ideal.

## Biography

### Part I — Rome

Lennart appears in the early chapters as part of the artist circle, present at the same gatherings, dinners, and [[campagna|Campagna]] excursions as the other members. While he has fewer spotlight moments than [[ahlin|Ahlin]] or Heggen, his steady presence contributes to the texture of expatriate life that Undset so carefully renders.

### Marriage and Domestic Life

The richest portrait of Lennart emerges indirectly, through Cesca Jahrman's long confession to [[jenny-winge|Jenny]] in Part II. Cesca describes her previous marriage to a man named [[hans|Hans]] and her subsequent relationship with Lennart, whom she married after leaving [[hans|Hans]]. Her account reveals Lennart as a man of patience and devotion — but also one who struggles to understand his wife's inner turmoil:

> "I don't know what Lennart thinks; he does not understand."

(024-030)

Cesca's narrative paints a picture of domestic strain: Lennart's illness, financial precarity, and the emotional distance between them. She speaks of him with a mixture of affection and despair:

> "To me he was the most beautiful thing on earth."

(024-038)

> "I do wish Lennart and I could be happy!"

(024-074)

### Part III — Return

By Part III, Lennart remains part of Cesca's life. Plans are made to convert a space into a studio for him, and messages of love are sent through [[jenny-winge|Jenny]] — "Lennart sends his love to both of you" (023-007). He endures as a steady, if somewhat tragic, figure in the expatriate network.

## Character Traits

| Trait | Manifestation |
|-------|--------------|
| **Patience** | Tolerates difficult circumstances and relationship strains without lashing out |
| **Devotion** | Cesca describes him as deeply loving, even when she is unable to reciprocate fully |
| **Quiet endurance** | Unlike [[ahlin|Ahlin]]'s emotional outbursts, Lennart suffers more silently |
| **Vulnerability** | His illness and Cesca's confession reveal a man bearing private pain |
| **Artistic commitment** | Continues to paint and plans for a studio, sustaining his creative identity |

## Relationships

- **[[cesca-jahrman|Francesca Cesca Jahrman]]**: Cesca's relationship with Lennart forms the emotional core of her confession. She married him after a previous failed relationship, and their marriage — while deeply strained — persists.
- **[[jenny-winge|Jenny Winge]]**: Lennart's wife Cesca is [[jenny-winge|Jenny]]'s closest female friend; [[jenny-winge|Jenny]] becomes a confidante to both of them.
- **[[ahlin|Ahlin]]**: Fellow Swedish artist in [[rome|Rome]], with whom Lennart shares a parallel trajectory — both marry women who struggle with the conventional roles expected of them.
- **[[gunnar-heggen|Gunnar Heggen]]**: Through Cesca, Lennart sends greetings to Gunnar and [[jenny-winge|Jenny]], indicating continued connection.
