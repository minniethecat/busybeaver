# Dinner Party in the Campagna

The **dinner party in the [[campagna|Campagna]]** took place on 17 January, [[jenny-winge|Jenny Winge]]'s twenty-eighth birthday, during her first stay in [[rome|Rome]]. Hosted by [[jenny-winge|Jenny]] and [[cesca-jahrman|francesca-cesca-jahrman]] at a small osteria on the [[via-appia-nuova|Via Appia Nuova]], the gathering brought together the core members of the Scandinavian artist community in [[rome|Rome]] and became a decisive moment in [[jenny-winge|Jenny]]'s romantic relationship with [[helge-gram|Helge Gram]].

## The Gathering

The party assembled on a day of early spring, with the brown [[campagna|Campagna]] showing its first greyish-green tinge and daisies spreading in silvery patches across the plain. The guests walked from the tram terminus in pairs along the sunny white road (007-028):

* **[[jenny-winge|Jenny Winge]]** — the host, radiant in a black silk dress that marked a departure from her usual grey working clothes (007-030)
* **[[helge-gram|Helge Gram]]** — the Danish archaeologist, who carried [[jenny-winge|Jenny]]'s dust-coat and walked with her (007-030)
* **[[cesca-jahrman|francesca-cesca-jahrman]]** — [[jenny-winge|Jenny]]'s friend and fellow painter, who supplied fruit and coffee (007-031)
* **[[ahlin|Ahlin]]** — the Swedish sculptor, who spent the afternoon in emotional turmoil [007-031, 007-050]
* **[[gunnar-heggen|Gunnar Heggen]]** — the Norwegian writer, who decorated the table with daisies alongside [[miss-palm|Miss Palm]] (007-031)
* **[[miss-palm|Miss Palm]]** — the Danish nurse, in lively spirits throughout [007-031, 007-056]

> The food was good and the wine was excellent; Cesca had brought fruit, and coffee, which she was going to make herself, to make sure it should be good. (007-031)

## Events of the Afternoon

### The Meal and Early Diversions

The party ate outdoors under a vine that cast a bluish net of shadow over the tablecloth. After the meal, [[miss-palm|Miss Palm]] and Heggen wandered off to examine marble reliefs and inscriptions built into the house's masonry, eventually disappearing around a corner (007-031). [[ahlin|Ahlin]] remained at the table, smoking with half-shut eyes (007-031).

### Cesca and Ahlin

During the walk, a dramatic scene unfolded. [[ahlin|Ahlin]], overcome with emotion, lay forward over the table in tears. Cesca bent over him, her arms around his neck (007-050):

> "Oh, don't, Lennart! Don't cry. I will love you. I will marry you—do you hear?—but you must not cry like that. I will marry you, and I think I can be fond of you, only don't be so miserable." (007-051)

[[jenny-winge|Jenny]], witnessing this, flushed deeply and turned away, leading Gram by another path through the orchard (007-053).

### Jenny and Helge

[[jenny-winge|Jenny]] and Helge walked up the slope of a small hill behind the osteria, then to the Bosco Sacro and the Egeria grotto (007-060). Sitting on a grassy slope, Helge declared his feelings for the first time (007-067):

> "I believe it is you, Jenny, I am in love with," he said suddenly. (007-067)

He confessed that he had noticed her first — crossing [[corso|the Corso]] on the day they met — and that his initial attraction to Francesca had been a fleeting confusion (007-070). When she sat beside him in the sun, he asked for a kiss (007-076), and [[jenny-winge|Jenny]], after a moment's hesitation, bent down and kissed his cheek (007-081).

> She bent suddenly down and kissed him. Only for a second did he feel her hair and lips brush his cheek, and he saw the movement of her body under the black silk as she bent down and rose again. (007-081)

### Return to the Osteria

The three couples dispersed across the [[campagna|Campagna]], losing sight of one another among the hills (007-057). By the time they reassembled for coffee and liqueurs, the atmosphere at the table was strained. Only [[miss-palm|Miss Palm]] remained in good spirits (007-056).

## Significance

The dinner party crystallized several relationship dynamics central to the novel's first part:

| Dynamic | Development |
|---------|------------|
| [[jenny-winge|Jenny]] and Helge | Helge declares his love; [[jenny-winge|Jenny]] responds with a kiss — the relationship shifts from friendship to romance |
| Cesca and [[ahlin|Ahlin]] | Cesca agrees to marry [[ahlin|Ahlin]] out of pity, foreshadowing future complications |
| Cesca and [[hjerrild|Hjerrild]] | Not present at the party, but Cesca had confided to [[jenny-winge|Jenny]] the previous night about his inappropriate advances (007-026) |
| The artist community | The gathering illustrates the close-knit but emotionally volatile circle of Scandinavian expatriates in [[rome|Rome]] |

Helge's declaration at the [[campagna|Campagna]] dinner set in motion the romantic relationship that would define the remainder of Part I and set the stage for the novel's central tragedy.
