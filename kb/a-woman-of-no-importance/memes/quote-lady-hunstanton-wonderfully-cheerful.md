# "Wonderfully cheerful, wonderfully cheerful."

> Wonderfully cheerful, wonderfully cheerful.
> (Spoken by Lady Hunstanton)

## Context

Lady Hunstanton's characteristic response to anything uncomfortable — a cheerful dismissal that exemplifies her role as the guardian of social surfaces.

## Analysis

This line exemplifies Wilde's ability to make dialogue do dramatic work. It is at once a piece of characterization — revealing something essential about Lady Hunstanton — and a contribution to the play's thematic architecture.
