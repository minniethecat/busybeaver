# "Don't call them the other sex. There is no such thing."

> Don't call them the other sex. There is no such thing.
> (by Mrs Allonby)

## Context

Mrs. Allonby delivers this witticism in Act II at Hunstanton Chase. The line challenges the Victorian assumption of fundamental difference between the sexes — an assumption that underlay the entire social order Wilde depicts.

## Analysis

By denying the existence of 'the other sex,' Mrs. Allonby is denying the logic of separate spheres and complementary natures. If there is no 'other sex,' then the differential treatment of men and women has no foundation.
