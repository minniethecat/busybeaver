# "What name shall I give at the club?"

> What name shall I give at the club?
> (Spoken by Gerald Arbuthnot)

## Context

The question that forces the final revelation — Gerald asks for a name, and receives a truth that changes everything.

## Analysis

This line exemplifies Wilde's ability to make dialogue do dramatic work. It is at once a piece of characterization — revealing something essential about Gerald Arbuthnot — and a contribution to the play's thematic architecture.
