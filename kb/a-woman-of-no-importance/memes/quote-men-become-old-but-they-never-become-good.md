# "Men become old, but they never become good."

> Men become old, but they never become good.
> (Spoken by Lady Caroline Pontefract)

## Context

Lady Caroline's cynical observation on the persistence of male moral failings across a lifetime.

## Analysis

This line exemplifies Wilde's ability to make dialogue do dramatic work. It is at once a piece of characterization — revealing something essential about Lady Caroline Pontefract — and a contribution to the play's thematic architecture.
