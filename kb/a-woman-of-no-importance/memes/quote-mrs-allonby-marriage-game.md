# "We have a much better time than they have."

> We have a much better time than they have.
> (Spoken by Mrs Allonby)

## Context

A superficially light observation that carries a serious claim: women, for all their constraints, may have more freedom within the marriage system than men do.

## Analysis

This line exemplifies Wilde's ability to make dialogue do dramatic work. It is at once a piece of characterization — revealing something essential about Mrs Allonby — and a contribution to the play's thematic architecture.
