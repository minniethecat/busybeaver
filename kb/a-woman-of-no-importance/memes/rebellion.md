# Rebellion

## Overview

The acts of rebellion in Wilde's play — from Hester's verbal defiance to Mrs. Arbuthnot's final refusal to accept the terms of a corrupt society.

## Manifestation in the Play

rebellion pervades the world of [[hunstanton-chase|Hunstanton Chase]] and the lives of its inhabitants. Wilde treats the theme with his characteristic combination of wit and penetration — the epigrams entertain, but the dramatic situations expose the human cost of the social structures being described.

## Key Characters

- **[[mrs-arbuthnot]]**: The rebel who acts by refusing
- **[[hester-worsley]]**: The rebel who speaks
- **[[lord-illingworth]]**: The false rebel whose defiance serves the system
