# "A very wonderful opening for so young a man as you are, Mr. Arbuthnot."

> That is a very wonderful opening for so young a man as you are, Mr. Arbuthnot.
> (001-044)

## Context

[[lady-hunstanton|Lady Hunstanton]] delivers this line in Act I on the [[hunstanton-terrace|terrace]] at [[hunstanton-chase|Hunstanton Chase]]. She has just announced that [[lord-illingworth|Lord Illingworth]] has appointed [[gerald-arbuthnot|Gerald Arbuthnot]] as his private secretary — a position that represents a dramatic elevation in Gerald's social and professional prospects.

## Analysis

This line is the play's plot engine. Lady Hunstanton's innocent celebration of Gerald's good fortune sets in motion the entire chain of events: [[mrs-arbuthnot|Mrs. Arbuthnot]]'s horror when she learns who has appointed her son, her journey to Hunstanton, and the eventual revelation of Gerald's parentage.

The dramatic irony is acute. Lady Hunstanton has no idea that the "wonderful opening" she celebrates is in fact the re-entry of [[lord-illingworth|Lord Illingworth]] into the life of the woman he wronged. Her cheerful ignorance of the forces she is setting in motion is characteristic of her role in the play — the well-meaning hostess whose social management perpetuates the system's cruelties.
