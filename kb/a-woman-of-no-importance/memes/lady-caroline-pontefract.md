# Lady Caroline Pontefract

## Character Overview

Lady Caroline Pontefract is the voice of conventional [[victorian-morality|morality]] at [[hunstanton-chase|Hunstanton Chase]] — and Wilde's satire of it. She judges everyone and everything by the standards of her class, and her judgments are invariably confident and invariably wrong. She is the character who most perfectly embodies the gap between moral pretension and moral perception that the play exists to expose.

## Role in the Play

Lady Caroline's function is to articulate the social code so that Wilde can demolish it. She is [[illegitimacy-and-reputation|scandal]]ized that [[hester-worsley|Hester Worsley]]'s father worked for a living — "one never met anyone in society who worked for their living" (001-017) — not recognizing that this very statement condemns the idle [[class-and-society|aristocracy]] she represents.

She presides over the [[marriage-and-courtship|marriage]] market [[wit-and-epigram|wit]]h the vigilance of a customs officer, assessing every young person's eligibility [[wit-and-epigram|wit]]h reference to birth, fortune, and [[illegitimacy-and-reputation|reputation]]. Her judgments on [[mrs-arbuthnot|Mrs. Arbuthnot]] would be devastating if she knew the truth; as it is, her obliviousness is both comic and damning.

## Relationships

- **[[lady-hunstanton|Lady Hunstanton]]**: Her friend and fellow arbiter of society.
- **[[sir-john-pontefract|Sir John Pontefract]]**: Her husband — a silent presence who seems to have learned that silence is the wisest response.
- **[[hester-worsley|Hester Worsley]]**: The American whose values Lady Caroline finds incomprehensible.

## Character Analysis

Lady Caroline is a comic character, but the comedy has a sharp edge. She is what [[mrs-arbuthnot|Mrs. Arbuthnot]] would have become had she not been ruined — a woman whose entire existence is defined by the social code she enforces. The difference is that [[mrs-arbuthnot|Mrs. Arbuthnot]], through suffering, has learned to see beyond that code; Lady Caroline never will.
