# Courtship and the Marriage Market

## Overview

The social rituals of courtship as depicted in the play — the marriage market that governs relations between young men and women.

## Manifestation in the Play

courtship and the marriage market pervades the world of [[hunstanton-chase|Hunstanton Chase]] and the lives of its inhabitants. Wilde treats the theme with his characteristic combination of wit and penetration — the epigrams entertain, but the dramatic situations expose the human cost of the social structures being described.

## Key Characters

- **[[gerald-arbuthnot]]**: The eligible young man
- **[[hester-worsley]]**: The American heiress on the marriage market
- **[[lady-hunstanton]]**: The hostess as matchmaker
