# Class Consciousness

## Overview

The awareness of class boundaries that pervades Wilde's play — who knows their place, who transgresses it, and what happens when class lines are crossed.

## Manifestation in the Play

class consciousness pervades the world of [[hunstanton-chase|Hunstanton Chase]] and the lives of its inhabitants. Wilde treats the theme with his characteristic combination of wit and penetration — the epigrams entertain, but the dramatic situations expose the human cost of the social structures being described.

## Key Characters

- **[[lady-caroline-pontefract]]**: The enforcer of class boundaries
- **[[gerald-arbuthnot]]**: The young man whose rise threatens class stability
- **[[mrs-arbuthnot]]**: The woman whose fall was also a class transgression
