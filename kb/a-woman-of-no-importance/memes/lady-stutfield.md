# Lady Stutfield

## Character Overview

Lady Stutfield is one of Wilde's comic miniatures — a woman whose defining characteristic is her breathless, repetitive manner of speech. Everything she says is qualified, softened, and repeated. Yet her naive questions sometimes strike unexpectedly close to the truth, as when she asks [[mrs-arbuthnot|Mrs. Arbuthnot]]: "Have you never forgiven him then? How sad that seems" (002-040).

## Role in the Play

Lady Stutfield appears in the Act II drawing-room scene, where her ingenuous questions serve as a foil to the sophisticated cynicism of [[mrs-allonby|Mrs. Allonby]] and [[lord-illingworth|Lord Illingworth]]. Her question to [[mrs-arbuthnot|Mrs. Arbuthnot]] about forgiveness — asked in complete ignorance of the situation — is the most emotionally direct moment in the scene.
