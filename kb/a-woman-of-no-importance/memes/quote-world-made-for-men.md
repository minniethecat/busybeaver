# "The world was made for men and not for women."

> Ah! The world was made for men and not for women.
> (001-063)

## Context

[[lady-stutfield|Lady Stutfield]] speaks this line in Act I at [[hunstanton-chase|Hunstanton Chase]], during the garden-party conversation about [[mrs-allonby|Mrs. Allonby]]'s theory of [[quote-playing-with-fire|playing with fire]] (001-061). [[lady-hunstanton|Lady Hunstanton]] has just said she doesn't know "how the world would get on with such a theory," and Lady Stutfield replies with this lament. It is one of her few lines that is not merely repetitive or vacuous.

## Analysis

Lady Stutfield is the play's most satirized female character — a woman whose conversational contribution consists almost entirely of repeating the word "quite" and agreeing with whoever spoke last. Yet Wilde gives her this one moment of lucidity. The line is all the more powerful for coming from a character who is otherwise a figure of fun: it suggests that even the most seemingly vacuous woman in the room can see the fundamental injustice of her position. [[mrs-allonby|Mrs. Allonby]] immediately deflects with a counter-epigram ("We have a much better time than they have"), but Lady Stutfield's simple statement lingers as the play's most direct articulation of its [[gender-roles|gender]] critique.
