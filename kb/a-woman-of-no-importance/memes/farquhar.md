# Farquhar

## Character Overview

Farquhar is the butler at [[hunstanton-chase|Hunstanton Chase]] (P03-007), mentioned several times in Acts I–III as the servant who announces guests and carries messages. He speaks no lines on stage but serves as the mechanism through which the play's entrances and exits are managed — a structural figure whose presence enables the drawing-room comedy's machinery of arrivals, departures, and intercepted letters.

## Role in the Play

Farquhar's function is entirely functional: he announces [[lord-illingworth|Lord Illingworth]]'s arrival at the conservatory (001-007), delivers notes to [[lady-hunstanton|Lady Hunstanton]], and carries out the household errands that keep the plot in motion. He appears briefly in Acts I, II, and III. His most consequential action is entirely offstage — he is the servant through whom the letter from [[mrs-arbuthnot|Mrs. Arbuthnot]] is delivered in Act II, an event that tightens the play's central dramatic knot.

## Relationships

- **[[lady-hunstanton|Lady Hunstanton]]**: His employer, who issues instructions [[wit-and-epigram|wit]]h the casual imperiousness of the country-house hostess.
- **[[francis|Francis]]**: The footman, his subordinate in the household hierarchy.
