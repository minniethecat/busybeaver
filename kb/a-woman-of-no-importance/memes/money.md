# Money

## Overview

The role of money in Wilde's social world — as a source of power, a measure of worth, and a factor that complicates gender dynamics.

## Manifestation in the Play

money pervades the world of [[hunstanton-chase|Hunstanton Chase]] and the lives of its inhabitants. Wilde treats the theme with his characteristic combination of wit and penetration — the epigrams entertain, but the dramatic situations expose the human cost of the social structures being described.

## Key Characters

- **[[hester-worsley]]**: The American heiress whose money gives her independence
- **[[lord-illingworth]]**: The aristocrat for whom money is assumed
- **[[gerald-arbuthnot]]**: The young man whose career represents aspiration
