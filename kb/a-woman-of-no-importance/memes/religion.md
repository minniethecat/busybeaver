# Religion

## Overview

The role of religion in Wilde's play — the Archdeacon, the moral language characters invoke, and the gap between religious profession and behaviour.

## Manifestation in the Play

religion pervades the world of [[hunstanton-chase|Hunstanton Chase]] and the lives of its inhabitants. Wilde treats the theme with his characteristic combination of wit and penetration — the epigrams entertain, but the dramatic situations expose the human cost of the social structures being described.

## Key Characters

- **[[archdeacon-daubeny]]**: The representative of the established Church
- **[[hester-worsley]]**: The Puritan voice of earnestness
- **[[lord-illingworth]]**: The cynic who dismisses religion while using its language
