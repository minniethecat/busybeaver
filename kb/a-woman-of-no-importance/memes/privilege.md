# Privilege

## Overview

The unearned advantages of birth and gender — embodied in Lord Illingworth's effortless social dominance and immunity from consequence.

## Manifestation in the Play

privilege pervades the world of [[hunstanton-chase|Hunstanton Chase]] and the lives of its inhabitants. Wilde treats the theme with his characteristic combination of wit and penetration — the epigrams entertain, but the dramatic situations expose the human cost of the social structures being described.

## Key Characters

- **[[lord-illingworth]]**: The supreme embodiment of privilege
- **[[lady-hunstanton]]**: The benign face of aristocratic privilege
- **[[hester-worsley]]**: The outsider who can see what privilege conceals
