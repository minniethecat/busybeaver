# Mrs. Arbuthnot's Cottage

Mrs. Arbuthnot's sitting room at [[wrockley|Wrockley]] is the setting for Act IV, the play's final and most emotionally concentrated act. The stage directions describe: "Sitting-room at Mrs. Arbuthnot's. Large open French window at back, looking on to garden. Doors R.C. and L.C."

## Dramatic Space

The cottage sitting room is the moral and spatial counterpoint to [[hunstanton-chase|Hunstanton Chase]]. Where Hunstanton is a space of public performance, social manoeuvre, and witty conversation, the cottage is a space of private truth, emotional intensity, and moral reckoning. The "large open French window at back, looking on to garden" provides a visual connection to nature that the enclosed rooms of Hunstanton lack — a spatial metaphor for the honesty and openness that [[mrs-arbuthnot|Mrs. Arbuthnot]] represents.

This is [[mrs-arbuthnot|Mrs. Arbuthnot]]'s domain — a space she has created and controls, in contrast to the borrowed spaces of Hunstanton where she is always a visitor, always subject to the social judgment of others. The cottage represents the life she has built for herself and her son, the respectable existence maintained at the cost of perpetual concealment.

## The Final Confrontation

Act IV in the cottage contains the play's climactic scenes. [[lord-illingworth|Lord Illingworth]] arrives to propose his marriage of convenience — a visit that inverts the spatial hierarchy of the earlier acts, bringing the aristocratic predator into the domestic space of the woman he wronged. Mrs. Arbuthnot's refusal of his proposal, delivered in her own sitting room, on her own terms, is the play's most powerful assertion of female agency.

The French window — open to the garden — provides the exit through which Mrs. Arbuthnot and [[hester-worsley|Hester]] depart together at the play's end, leaving [[gerald-arbuthnot|Gerald]] alone. This final spatial movement, from the enclosed room into the open garden, enacts the play's qualified optimism: the possibility of a life lived openly, without concealment, after the truth has been told.

## Key Events in This Space

- Gerald writes at his table before the visitors arrive
- [[lady-hunstanton|Lady Hunstanton]] and [[mrs-allonby|Mrs. Allonby]] visit, bringing news
- [[lord-illingworth|Lord Illingworth]] proposes marriage to Mrs. Arbuthnot
- Mrs. Arbuthnot reveals the truth to Gerald
- Hester's declaration of solidarity with Mrs. Arbuthnot
- The play's closing exit into the garden
