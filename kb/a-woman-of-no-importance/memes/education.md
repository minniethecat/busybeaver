# Education

## Overview

The role of education and intellectual culture in Wilde's play — classical references, literary allusions, and the educational background that marks class distinctions.

## Manifestation in the Play

education pervades the world of [[hunstanton-chase|Hunstanton Chase]] and the lives of its inhabitants. Wilde treats the theme with his characteristic combination of wit and penetration — the epigrams entertain, but the dramatic situations expose the human cost of the social structures being described.

## Key Characters

- **[[lord-illingworth]]**: The product of elite classical education
- **[[gerald-arbuthnot]]**: The young man whose education is his path upward
- **[[mr-kelvil]]**: The earnest improver who values education as moral uplift
