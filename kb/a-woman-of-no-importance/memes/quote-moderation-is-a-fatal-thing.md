# "Moderation is a fatal thing. Nothing succeeds like excess."

> Moderation is a fatal thing. Nothing succeeds like excess.
> (by Lord Illingworth)

## Context

Lord Illingworth delivers this witticism in Act II at Hunstanton Chase. It is a characteristic inversion of Victorian pieties: the age that celebrated moderation and the golden mean is dismissed in favour of excess and the refusal of limits.

## Analysis

The epigram works by substituting one value system for another. Where the Victorians saw moderation as a moral virtue, Illingworth sees it as timidity. Excess is the dandy's creed — success comes not from playing by the rules but from breaking them with style.
