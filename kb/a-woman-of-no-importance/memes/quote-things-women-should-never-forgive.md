# "There are many things women should never forgive"

> I am afraid… that there are many things women should never forgive.
> (003-100)

## Context

[[lady-hunstanton|Lady Hunstanton]] has just piously declared that "we women should forgive everything" (003-099). [[hester-worsley|Hester]]'s response is immediate and firm. The hesitation marked by "I am afraid…" is not uncertainty but politeness — she is about to contradict her hostess and knows it.

## Analysis

This single line is the moral fulcrum of *A Woman of No Importance*. Hester's declaration transforms forgiveness from a feminine duty into a moral choice. By insisting that some things should not be forgiven, she gives [[mrs-arbuthnot|Mrs. Arbuthnot]] the ethical framework she needs to refuse [[lord-illingworth|Lord Illingworth]]'s proposal. The line is also a rebuke to the entire Victorian ideology of feminine self-abnegation.
