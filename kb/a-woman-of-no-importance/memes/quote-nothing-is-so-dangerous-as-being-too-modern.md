# "Nothing is so dangerous as being too modern."

> Nothing is so dangerous as being too modern.
> (Spoken by Lord Illingworth)

## Context

A Wildean paradox: the advocate of aesthetic modernity warns against the dangers of being too modern, revealing the dandy's complex relationship to social change.

## Analysis

This line exemplifies Wilde's ability to make dialogue do dramatic work. It is at once a piece of characterization — revealing something essential about Lord Illingworth — and a contribution to the play's thematic architecture.
