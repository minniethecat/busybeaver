# Plato

**Plato** (c. 427–347 BCE) was an ancient Greek philosopher, a student of Socrates and teacher of Aristotle. In *Beyond Good and Evil*, Nietzsche identifies Platonism as the foundational dogmatic error of Western philosophy, the original split between appearance and reality.

## Overview

For Nietzsche, Plato represents the inception of a philosophical tradition that devalues the earthly in favor of the transcendent — what Nietzsche calls "the worst, most durable, and most dangerous of all errors." Platonism's invention of a "true world" beyond appearances sets the template for Christianity and all subsequent idealist philosophy.

## Relationship to Nietzsche's Philosophy

Nietzsche's entire project can be read as an "inversion of Platonism." Where Plato posits a true world of Forms beyond the sensible world, Nietzsche affirms the sensible world as the only world. Nietzsche's perspectivism, his critique of truth, and his revaluation of values are all directed against the Platonic-Christian tradition.
