## Overview

Nietzsche's treatment of democracy in *Beyond Good and Evil* is consistently critical. The democratic movement is not merely a political phenomenon but a symptom of a deeper cultural and physiological decline — a "Verkleinerungs-Form des Menschen" (diminution-form of humanity). Democracy, for Nietzsche, is the political expression of [[Herd Morality|herd morality]], a system that makes everyone equal by bringing everyone down.

Democracy appears in Nietzsche's text alongside his analyses of modernity, European culture, and the crisis of philosophy. It is connected to the rise of the scholar type, the decline of genuine philosophy, and the triumph of the "democratic taste" that prefers comfort, safety, and equality over danger, hierarchy, and greatness.

## Key Passages

- **[005-026]** — Nietzsche explicitly names "die demokratische Bewegung" as "nicht bloss als eine Verfalls-Form der politischen Organisation, sondern als Verfalls-, nämlich Verkleinerungs-Form des Menschen" (not merely a decay-form of political organization, but a decay-form — namely a diminution-form — of humanity). This is his most direct statement on democracy: it makes humans smaller.

- **[002-025]** — The so-called "free spirits" are denounced as "beredte und schreibfingrige Sklaven des demokratischen Geschmacks und seiner 'modernen Ideen'" (eloquent and scribble-fingered slaves of the democratic taste and its "modern ideas"). The free spirit is not democratic — the genuine free spirit stands *above* the herd, not with it.

- **[006-002]** — The scientific man's declaration of independence from philosophy "ist eine der feineren Nachwirkungen des demokratischen Wesens und Unwesens" (is one of the finer after-effects of the democratic essence and non-essence). The fragmentation of knowledge into specialized sciences mirrors the democratic fragmentation of values.

- **[000-003]** — Nietzsche groups democracy with Jesuitism as a failed attempt to relieve European cultural tension: "es ist schon zwei Mal im grossen Stile versucht worden, den Bogen abzuspannen, einmal durch den Jesuitismus, zum zweiten Mal durch die demokratische Aufklärung" (twice it has been attempted on a grand scale to relax the bow, once through Jesuitism, the second time through democratic enlightenment).

- **[007-029]** — The modern treatment of women "gehört zum demokratischen Hang und Grundgeschmack" (belongs to the democratic inclination and basic taste). Democracy flattens all distinctions, including the natural hierarchy between the sexes.

- **[006-018]** — The philosopher must pursue knowledge "mit verwegenen und schmerzhaften Versuchen weiter gehn, als es der weichmüthige und verzärtelte Geschmack eines demokratischen Jahrhunderts gut heissen kann" (go further with audacious and painful experiments than the soft-hearted and coddled taste of a democratic century can approve). The democratic century is too soft for genuine philosophy.

## Significance

1. **Democracy as Diminution**: Nietzsche's core charge against democracy is that it produces smaller human beings. By removing the conditions that produce greatness — danger, hierarchy, suffering, solitude — democracy makes greatness impossible (005-026).

2. **Democracy and Herd Morality**: Democracy is the political arm of [[Herd Morality|herd morality]]. Where herd morality values safety, equality, and pity, democracy builds institutions that enforce these values. The democratic state is the herd made sovereign (005-025, 002-025).

3. **Democracy vs. Philosophy**: The democratic age is hostile to genuine philosophy. Democracy produces scholars, not philosophers; it encourages the "objective man" who mirrors reality rather than the creator who transvalues it. The philosopher in a democratic age must be a counter-force (006-002, 006-018).

4. **Democracy and the Good European**: Nietzsche's vision of a post-national Europe is not democratic but aristocratic. The "good European" transcends the democratic nation-state not toward democratic internationalism but toward a higher aristocratic culture governed by [[Order of Rank|order of rank]] (000-004, 008-007).

## Related Concepts

- **[[Herd Morality]]** — Democracy is herd morality made political; the democratic movement is the herd's political triumph.
- **[[Europe]]** — Democracy is a European phenomenon, and Nietzsche's critique of it is inseparable from his diagnosis of European modernity.
- **[[Good European]]** — The post-national European identity Nietzsche envisions is explicitly anti-democratic, based on rank rather than equality.
- **[[Scholar]]** — The scholar (der Gelehrte) is democracy's intellectual type — specialized, objective, and hostile to the commanding philosopher.
- **[[Nobility]]** — Nobility and democracy are antonyms in Nietzsche's vocabulary; the noble type requires inequality, rank, and distance.
