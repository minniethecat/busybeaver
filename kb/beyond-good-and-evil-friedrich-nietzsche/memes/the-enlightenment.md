# The Enlightenment

The **Enlightenment** (German: *Aufklärung*) was an 18th-century European intellectual movement emphasizing reason, science, and individualism. In *Beyond Good and Evil*, Nietzsche engages with the Enlightenment as both a precursor to and a target of his philosophical project.

## Overview

Nietzsche's relationship to the Enlightenment is complex. He inherits the Enlightenment's critique of religion and its commitment to intellectual honesty, while rejecting its faith in reason, progress, and universal human rights. For Nietzsche, the Enlightenment represents an incomplete liberation — it freed humanity from religious dogma only to enshrine new dogmas of reason and equality.

## Significance in Nietzsche's Philosophy

Nietzsche positions himself as a radicalization of the Enlightenment project — extending its critical impulse to the point where Enlightenment values themselves become objects of critique. The "free spirit" of *Beyond Good and Evil* is an attempt to go beyond Enlightenment rationalism toward a more profound liberation of the intellect.
