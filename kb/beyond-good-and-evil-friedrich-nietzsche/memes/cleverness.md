# Cleverness

## Overview

In *Beyond Good and Evil*, Nietzsche treats cleverness (*Klugheit*) as a distinctive mark of the higher type — not the calculating prudence of the shopkeeper or the utilitarian, but the cunning grace of the noble soul that knows when to reveal and when to conceal. Humanity itself is characterized as an animal made "uncanny through cunning and cleverness" (*durch List und Klugheit unheimlich*). Cleverness belongs to the aesthetic repertoire of the noble type: it is "the art of grace, of play, of chasing away cares, of lightening and taking lightly" (*die Kunst, der Anmuth, des Spielens, Sorgen-Wegscheuchens, Erleichterns und Leicht-Nehmens*). It is intelligence in the service of life, knowledge deployed with style.

## Key Passages

- [009-035] Nietzsche describes the human being as "a manifold, mendacious, artificial and opaque animal, uncanny to other animals less through force than through cunning and cleverness" (*weniger durch Kraft, als durch List und Klugheit unheimlich*). Humanity invented the good conscience in order to enjoy its soul once as *simple*; the whole of morality is "a bold long falsification" (*eine beherzte lange Fälschung*) through which any enjoyment in the sight of the soul becomes possible. Under this perspective, "perhaps much more belongs in the concept 'art' than is commonly believed" (*gehört vielleicht viel Mehr in den Begriff „Kunst" hinein, als man gemeinhin glaubt*). Cleverness here is not instrumental rationality but the fundamental human capacity for self-fashioning through artifice.

- [007-025] In the context of Nietzsche's controversial analysis of woman, he warns of what happens when "the 'eternally-boring in woman'... dares to come forward! when it begins fundamentally and on principle to unlearn its cleverness and art, that of grace, of play, of chasing away cares, of lightening and taking lightly" (*seine Klugheit und Kunst, die der Anmuth, des Spielens, Sorgen-Wegscheuchens, Erleichterns und Leicht-Nehmens*). The passage reveals Nietzsche's broader concept of cleverness as an aesthetic virtue — not mere intelligence but the artful management of surfaces, the grace that makes social life bearable. To "unlearn" this cleverness in the name of seriousness or scientific rigor is to lose a form of power.

## Significance in Nietzsche's Philosophy

Cleverness belongs to Nietzsche's revaluation of intelligence against the morality of pure reason. Where Kantian ethics valorizes the good will that acts from duty alone, Nietzsche valorizes the cleverness that knows when rules apply and when they do not — the intelligence of the exception. The human animal's distinctiveness lies not in reason as such but in the cunning that makes morality itself possible as an artistic falsification. The good conscience, that apparently simple moral feeling, is revealed as the supreme achievement of cleverness: a self-deception so thorough that it becomes indistinguishable from truth. The philosopher must be cleverer still — must recognize the artifice without simply dismantling it, must use cleverness in the service of truthfulness rather than self-deception.
