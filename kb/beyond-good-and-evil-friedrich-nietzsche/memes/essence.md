# Essence

## Overview

*Wesen* in Nietzsche's usage does not refer to an Aristotelian essence — a fixed set of properties that make a thing what it is independently of time and circumstance. It refers rather to the *constitution of a being* as expressed through its drives, its valuations, and its mode of relating to others. What a being "is" is what it does, what it values, and what it commands. The noble soul has a different *Wesen* from the base soul, not because they instantiate different eternal forms, but because their drives are organized according to different principles of rank. This naturalization of essence is a key component of Nietzsche's anti-metaphysical project.

## Key Passages

- (001-005) — Nietzsche suggests that our most basic valuations — for example, that the determinate is worth more than the indeterminate, that "appearance" is worth less than "truth" — "could be foreground-valuations, a certain kind of *niaiserie*, such as may be necessary for the preservation of beings such as we are" (*eine bestimmte Art von niaiserie, wie sie gerade zur Erhaltung von Wesen, wie wir sind, noth thun mag*). The essence of our kind of being is revealed not in what we know but in what we must *believe* to survive.

- (001-009) — Nietzsche deploys the concept of *Wesen* in its fullest naturalistic sense when he writes: "Imagine a being such as nature is — wasteful beyond measure, indifferent beyond measure, without aims and considerations, without mercy and justice, fertile and desolate and uncertain at once — imagine indifference itself as a power" (*Denkt euch ein Wesen, wie es die Natur ist*). Nature as a *Wesen* is not a teleological order but a blind, indifferent force. To live "according to nature" is to live according to *this*, not according to the Stoic fantasy of a rational cosmos.

- (002-010) — In the discussion of the exoteric and esoteric, Nietzsche locates the *Wesentlichere* (the more essential difference) not in whether one stands outside or inside, but in the *direction* of one's seeing: "the exoteric sees things from below upward — the esoteric, however, from above downward!" (*der Exoteriker draussen steht und von aussen her... der Esoteriker aber von Oben herab*). Essence here is a function of *rank* — of the position from which one evaluates.

- (001-029) — The affects of hatred, envy, greed, and the drive to rule are described as "life-conditioning affects, something that must be present fundamentally and essentially in the total economy of life" (*im Gesammt-Haushalte des Lebens grundsätzlich und grundwesentlich vorhanden sein muss*). The "essence" of life includes what morality condemns; to condemn these affects is to condemn life itself.

## Significance in Nietzsche's Philosophy

By relocating essence from a metaphysical substrate to the organization of drives, Nietzsche performs the same operation on *Wesen* that he performs on the soul, the will, and the self: he *naturalizes* it without eliminating it. A being still has an essence, but that essence is not given in advance — it is the product of history, of the long discipline of valuations, of the triumph of some drives over others. The "essence" of the noble soul is the sediment of millennia of commanding; the "essence" of the herd animal is the sediment of millennia of obeying.

This conception of essence as *achieved* rather than *given* opens the door to Nietzsche's doctrine of self-overcoming: to change what one is, one must reorganize the hierarchy of one's drives. Essence becomes a task rather than a fate.
