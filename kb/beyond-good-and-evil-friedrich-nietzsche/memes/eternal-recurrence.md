# Eternal Recurrence

The **Eternal Recurrence** (German: *Ewige Wiederkunft des Gleichen*) is one of Nietzsche's most profound and challenging ideas.

## The Concept

Nietzsche presents the eternal recurrence as a thought experiment: what if a demon whispered to you that this life, exactly as you have lived it — every pain, every joy, every thought — will be repeated eternally, without variation?

Would this be a crushing weight, or the ultimate affirmation?

## Key Passages

- The "reversed ideal" — the most world-affirming human who wants it all again eternally, da capo (003-013)
- The demon's whisper of eternal return as the greatest weight (003-013)
- "Oh Lebens Mittag! Feierliche Zeit!" — the Aftersong as poetic expression of eternal recurrence (010-001)

## Significance

The eternal recurrence serves as Nietzsche's ultimate test of life-affirmation. Only those who can joyfully affirm the eternal return of all things have truly overcome nihilism and embraced life in its fullness.

The concept is closely tied to *amor fati* — the love of fate — and represents the highest formula of affirmation.
