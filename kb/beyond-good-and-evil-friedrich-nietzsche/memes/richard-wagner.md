# Richard Wagner

**Richard Wagner** (1813–1883) was a German composer, conductor, and theatre director, best known for his operas and the Bayreuth Festival. Once Nietzsche's close friend and mentor figure, Wagner became a target of Nietzsche's critique in *Beyond Good and Evil* and other late works.

## Overview

Wagner occupies a unique position in Nietzsche's thought. Nietzsche's early admiration for Wagner gave way to disillusionment, seeing Wagner's art as symptomatic of European decadence rather than its cure. In *Beyond Good and Evil*, Wagner is analyzed as a cultural phenomenon reflecting the condition of the modern German spirit.

## Relationship to Nietzsche's Philosophy

Nietzsche's break with Wagner was both personal and philosophical. Wagner represented the romantic ideal that *The Birth of Tragedy* had celebrated, but Nietzsche came to see Wagner's art as manipulative, nationalistic, and decadent. The critique of Wagner in Part 8 of *Beyond Good and Evil* is part of Nietzsche's broader analysis of German identity and modern culture.
