## Overview

The psychologist (*der Psychologe*) occupies a privileged position in *Beyond Good and Evil*. Nietzsche elevates psychology from a subordinate science to "die Herrin der Wissenschaften" (the queen of the sciences, 001-030), arguing that it is now the path to the fundamental problems of philosophy. The psychologist is not the clinical empiricist of the modern laboratory but the genealogical investigator of the human soul — one who sees through the surface of moral valuations to the instincts, drives, and power relations beneath.

Yet Nietzsche's psychologist is a figure of profound ambivalence. The depth of psychological insight comes at a cost: the psychologist needs healing, escape, and forgetfulness from what he has seen (009-022). He risks disgust at his own knowledge. The "psychological eye" that penetrates masks and exposes hidden motives is both a gift and a burden — a dangerous instrument that separates its possessor from the consolations of ordinary life.

## Key Passages

- **[001-030]** — Nietzsche makes his most programmatic claim for psychology: "der Psychologe, welcher dergestalt 'Opfer bringt' — es ist nicht das sacrifizio dell'intelletto, im Gegentheil! — wird zum Mindesten dafür verlangen dürfen, dass die Psychologie wieder als Herrin der Wissenschaften anerkannt werde" (The psychologist who thus "brings sacrifices" — it is not the sacrifice of the intellect, on the contrary! — will at least be permitted to demand that psychology be recognized again as the queen of the sciences). This is not a modest disciplinary claim but a reversal of the metaphysical tradition: the fundamental problems are psychological problems.

- **[009-022]** — Nietzsche exposes the psychologist's vulnerability: "Man wird fast bei jedem Psychologen eine verrätherische Vorneigung und Lust am Umgange mit alltäglichen und wohlgeordneten Menschen wahrnehmen: daran verräth sich, dass er immer einer Heilung bedarf" (One will almost always detect in a psychologist a revealing predilection for and pleasure in the company of everyday and well-ordered people: this reveals that he is always in need of healing). The psychologist flees from his own insights into the ordinary; the depth of his knowledge wounds him.

- **[007-005]** — Nietzsche issues a challenge: "studirt, ihr Psychologen, die Philosophie der 'Regel' im Kampfe mit der 'Ausnahme'" (Study, you psychologists, the philosophy of the "rule" in combat with the "exception"). The psychologist must attend to the exception — the rare, the anomalous, the dangerous — as the key to understanding the rule, rather than subsuming the exception under pre-established categories.

## Significance in Nietzsche's Philosophy

1. **Psychology as First Philosophy**: By declaring psychology the "queen of the sciences," Nietzsche inverts the traditional hierarchy that placed metaphysics or logic at the foundation. The question "what is truth?" becomes "what drive seeks truth, and why?"

2. **The Psychologist's Self-Knowledge**: The psychologist cannot exempt himself from his own analysis. The predilection for ordinary company (009-022) reveals that the psychologist's knowledge is not a secure possession but a wound that requires constant management.

3. **Methodological Imperative**: The study of the "exception" over the "rule" (007-005) defines Nietzsche's psychological method: one learns more about human nature from the rare and extreme than from the normal and average. This is a methodological principle that underlies the entire book.
