# Master-Morality

**Master-Morality** (German: *Herrenmoral*) is one of the two fundamental types of morality that Nietzsche distinguishes in *Beyond Good and Evil* and *On the Genealogy of Morals*.

## Characteristics

Master-morality arises from the noble, powerful, and self-affirming type of person. It is characterized by:

- Self-affirmation and pride in one's own strength
- Valuing courage, power, and nobility
- Creating values from within, rather than reacting to others
- The dichotomy "good vs. bad" (not "good vs. evil")

## Key Passages

- Two basic types of morality introduced — Herren-Moral and Sklaven-Moral (009-005)
- Noble type as value-determining, self-glorification of the powerful (009-006)
- The powerful honor strength and hardness, contempt for selflessness (009-007)
- Typical marks of noble/master morality — courage, pride, creating values (009-008)
