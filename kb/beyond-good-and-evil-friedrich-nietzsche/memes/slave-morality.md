# Slave-Morality

**Slave-Morality** (German: *Sklavenmoral*) is the reactive moral system that Nietzsche contrasts with master-morality in *Beyond Good and Evil*.

## Characteristics

Slave-morality arises from the oppressed, weak, and suffering. It is characterized by:

- Reactivity: defining "evil" first, then "good" as its opposite
- Valuing humility, sympathy, patience, and equality
- Rooted in *ressentiment* — the creative revenge of the powerless
- The dichotomy "good vs. evil" (in contrast to master "good vs. bad")

## Key Passages

- Two basic types of morality — Herren-Moral and Sklaven-Moral distinguished (009-005)
- Slave morality described — pessimism, suspicion against the powerful and their virtues (009-008)
- Slave-morality consequence — "good" and "stupid" approaching, the harmless man as ideal (009-009)

## Historical Influence

Nietzsche argues that slave-morality, through Christianity, triumphed over master-morality in Western civilization — a "transvaluation of values" he seeks to reverse.
