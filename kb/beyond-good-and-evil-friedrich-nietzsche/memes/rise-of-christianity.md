# Rise of Christianity

The **Rise of Christianity** represents for Nietzsche the most consequential moral transformation in Western history. In *Beyond Good and Evil*, Nietzsche analyzes Christianity not primarily as a religion but as a moral system that fundamentally reshaped European values.

## Overview

Nietzsche treats the rise of Christianity as the paradigmatic case of the "slave revolt in morals" — a transvaluation of values in which the weak, through resentment, inverted the noble morality of antiquity. Christianity's triumph represents the victory of slave-morality over master-morality on a civilizational scale.

## Significance in Nietzsche's Philosophy

The critique of Christianity is central to Nietzsche's project of the "revaluation of all values." Nietzsche sees Christianity as having bequeathed to modern Europe a morality that has outlived its theological foundations — a morality that continues to shape secular thought even after the "death of God."
