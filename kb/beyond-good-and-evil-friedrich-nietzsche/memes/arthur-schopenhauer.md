# Arthur Schopenhauer

**Arthur Schopenhauer** (1788–1860) was a German philosopher best known for his pessimistic philosophy and his concept of the metaphysical will. Nietzsche was profoundly influenced by Schopenhauer in his early years but came to critique and distance himself from Schopenhauer's pessimism in *Beyond Good and Evil*.

## Overview

Schopenhauer represents both a major influence on and a target of Nietzsche's philosophy. While Nietzsche inherited Schopenhauer's emphasis on the will and his atheism, he rejected Schopenhauer's pessimistic negation of life and his metaphysics of the will as thing-in-itself.

## Relationship to Nietzsche's Philosophy

Nietzsche's relationship with Schopenhauer is complex. He admired Schopenhauer's intellectual honesty and atheism, and his early work *The Birth of Tragedy* was deeply Schopenhauerian. However, in *Beyond Good and Evil*, Nietzsche criticizes Schopenhauer's metaphysical will as a disguised form of the old "thing-in-itself" and rejects his pessimism in favor of life-affirmation.
