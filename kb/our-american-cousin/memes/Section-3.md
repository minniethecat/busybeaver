(003-001) [[lord-dundreary|Dun]] Now he thinks he's a horse. I've heard of a great jackass, and I

(003-002) dreampt of a jackass, but I don't believe there is any such insect.

(003-003) [[florence-trenchard|Flo]] Well, cousin, I hope you made yourself comfortable.

(003-004) Asa Well, no, I can't say as I did. You see there was so many all-fired

(003-005) fixins in my room I couldn't find anything I wanted.

(003-006) Flo What was it you couldn't find in your room?

(003-007) Asa There as no soft soap.

(003-008) De B Soft soap!

(003-009) [[augustus-trenchard|Aug]] Soft soap!

(003-010) [[harry-vernon|Ver]] Soft soap!

(003-011) [[mrs-mountchessington|Mrs M]] Soft soap!

(003-012) Flo Soft soap!

(003-013) Geo [On sofa.] Soft soap!

(003-014) Dun Thoft thoap?

(003-015) Asa Yes, soft soap. I reckon you know what that is. However, I struck a

(003-016) pump in the kitchen, slicked my hair down a little, gave my boots a lick

(003-017) of grease, and now I feel quite handsome; but I'm everlastingly dry.

(003-018) Flo You'll find ale, wine and luncheon on the side-table.

(003-019) Asa Wal, I don't know as I've got any appetite. You see comin' along on

(003-020) the cars I worried down half a dozen ham sandwiches, eight or ten boiled

(003-021) eggs, two or three pumpkin pies and a string of cold sausages--and--Wal,

(003-022) I guess I can hold on till dinner-time.

(003-023) Dun Did that illustrious exile eat all that? I wonder where he put it?

(003-024) Asa I'm as dry as a sap-tree in August.

(003-025) [[binny|Binny]] [Throwing open, E. D.] Luncheon!

(003-026) Asa [Goes hastily up to table.] Wal, I don't want to speak out too

(003-027) plain, but this is an awful mean set out for a big house like this.

(003-028) Flo Why, what's wrong, sir?

(003-029) Asa Why, there's no mush!

(003-030) Dun No mush?

(003-031) Asa Nary slapjack.

(003-032) Dun Why, does he want [[mary-meredith|Mary]] to slap Jack?

(003-033) Asa No pork and beans!

(003-034) Dun Pork's been here, but he's left.

(003-035) Asa And where on airth's the clam chowder?

(003-036) Dun Where _is_ clam chowder? He's never here when he's wanted.

(003-037) Asa [Drinks and spits.] Here's your health, old hoss. Do you call that

(003-038) a drink? See here, cousin, you seem to be the liveliest critter here,

(003-039) so just hurry up the fixins, and I'll show this benighted aristocratic

(003-040) society what real liquor is. So hurry up the fixins.

(003-041) All Fixins?

(003-042) Flo What do you mean by fixins?

(003-043) Asa Why, brandy, rum, gin and whiskey. We'll make them all useful.

(003-044) Flo Oh, I'll hurry up the fixins. What fun! [Exit, R.]

(003-045) Dun Oh! I thought he meant the gas fixins.

(003-046) Asa Say, you, you Mr. Puffy, you run out and get me a bunch of mint

(003-047) and a bundle of straws; hurry up, old hoss. [Exit [[mr-binny|Binny]], L. 3 E.,

(003-048) indignantly.] Say, Mr. Sailor man, just help me down with this table.

(003-049) Oh! don't you get riley, you and I ran against each other when I came

(003-050) in, but we'll be friends yet. [Vernon helps him with table to C.]

(003-051) Enter Florence, followed by [[servants-and-service|servants]] in livery; they carry a case of

(003-052) decanters and water, on which are seven or eight glasses, two or three

(003-053) tin mixers and a bowl of sugar. Binny enters with a bunch of mint and a

(003-054) few straws.

(003-055) Flo Here, cousin, are the fixins.

(003-056) Asa That's yer sort. Now then, I'll give you all a drink that'll make

(003-057) you squeal. [To Binny] Here, Puffy, just shake that up, faster. I'll

(003-058) give that sick gal a drink that'll make her squirm like an eel on a mud

(003-059) bank.

(003-060) Dun [Screams.] What a horrible idea. [Runs about stage.]

(003-061) Flo Oh, don't mind him! That's only an American joke.

(003-062) Dun A joke! Do you call that a joke? To make a sick girl squirm like a

(003-063) mud bank on an eel's skin?

(003-064) Asa Yes, I'll give you a drink that'll make your whiskers return under

(003-065) your chin, which is their natural location. Now, ladies and gentlemen,

(003-066) what'll you have, Whiskey Skin, Brandy Smash, Sherry Cobbler, Mint Julep

(003-067) or Jersey Lightning?

(003-068) Aug Oh, I want a Mint Julep.

(003-069) De B Give me a Gin Cocktail.

(003-070) Flo I'll take a Sherry Cobbler.

(003-071) Ver Brandy Smash for me.

(003-072) Mrs M Give me a Whiskey Skin.

(003-073) Geo I'll take a Lemonade.

(003-074) Dun Give me a Jersey Lightning.

(003-075) Asa Give him a Jersey Lightning. [As Dundreary drinks] Warranted to kill

(003-076) at forty rods. [Dundreary falls back on Mrs. M. and Georgina.]

(003-077) Closed In.

(003-078) Scene 2--Library in [[trenchard-manor|Trenchard Manor]]. Oriel Window, L. C., with curtains.

(003-079) Two chairs and table brought on at change.

(003-080) Enter Binny and [[richard-coyle|Coyle]], L. 1 E.

(003-081) Binny Sir Hedward will see you directly, [[mr-coyle|Mr. Coyle]].

(003-082) Coyle Very well. House full of company, I see, Mr. Binny.

(003-083) Binny Cram full, Mr. Coyle. As one of the first families in the country

(003-084) we must keep up our position.

(003-085) Coyle [Rubbing his hands.] Certainly, certainly, that is as long as

(003-086) we can, Mr. Binny. Tell [[murcott|Murcott]], my clerk, to bring my [[letters-and-documents|papers]] in here.

(003-087) You'll find him in the servant's hall, and see that you keep your strong

(003-088) ale out of his way. People who serve me must have their senses about

(003-089) them.

(003-090) Binny [Aside.] I should say so, or 'e'd 'ave hevery tooth hout in their

(003-091) 'eds, the wiper. [Exit, L. 1 E.]

(003-092) Coyle And now to show this pompous baronet the precipice on which he

(003-093) stands.

(003-094) Enter Murcott, with green bag and papers.

(003-095) Coyle Are you sober, sirrah?

(003-096) Murcott Yes, Mr. Coyle.

(003-097) Coyle Then see you keep so.

(003-098) Mur I'll do my best, sir. But, oh! do tell them to keep liquor out of my

(003-099) way. I can't keep from it now, try as I will, and I try hard enough, God

(003-100) help me!

(003-101) Coyle Pshaw! Get out those mortgages and the letters from my [[london|London]]

(003-102) agent. [Murcott takes papers from bag and places then on table. Coyle

(003-103) [[beauty-and-appearance|looks]] off, R. 1 E.] So; here comes [[sir-edward-trenchard|Sir Edward]]. Go, but be within call. I

(003-104) may want you to witness a signature.

(003-105) Mur I will sir. [Aside.] I must have brandy, or my hand will not be

(003-106) steady enough to write. [Exit, L. 1 E.]

(003-107) Enter Sir Edward, R. 1 E. Coyle bows.

(003-108) Sir E Good morning, Coyle, good morning. [With affected ease.] There is

(003-109) a chair, Coyle. [They sit.] So you see those infernal tradespeople are

(003-110) pretty troublesome.

(003-111) Coyle My agent's letter this morning announces that Walter and Brass

(003-112) have got judgement and execution on their amount for repairing your town

(003-113) house last season. [Refers to papers.] Boquet and Barker announce their

(003-114) intention of taking this same course with the wine account. Handmarth

(003-115) is preparing for a settlement of his heavy demand for the stables.

(003-116) Then there is Temper for pictures and other things and Miss Florence

(003-117) Trenchard's account with Madame Pompon, and--

(003-118) Sir E Confound it, why harass me with details, these infernal

(003-119) particulars? Have you made out the total?

(003-120) Coyle Four thousand, eight hundred and thirty pounds, nine shillings and

(003-121) sixpence.

(003-122) Sir E Well, of course we must find means of settling this extortion.

(003-123) Coyle Yes, Sir Edward, if possible.

(003-124) Sir E If possible?

(003-125) Coyle I, as your agent, must stoop to detail, you must allow me to

(003-126) repeat, if possible.

(003-127) Sir E Why, you don't say there will be any difficulty in raising the

(003-128) [[fortune-and-wealth|money]]?

(003-129) Coyle What means would you suggest, Sir Edward.

(003-130) Sir E That, sir, is your business.

(003-131) Coyle A foretaste in the interest on the Fanhille & Ellenthrope

(003-132) mortgages, you are aware both are in the arrears, the mortgagees in

(003-133) fact, write here to announce their intentions to foreclose. [Shows

(003-134) papers.]

(003-135) Sir E Curse your impudence, pay them off.

(003-136) Coyle How, Sir Edward?

(003-137) Sir E Confound it, sir, which of us is the agent? Am I to find you

(003-138) brains for your own business?

(003-139) Coyle No, Sir Edward, I can furnish the brains, but what I ask of you is

(003-140) to furnish the [[money-and-wealth|money]].

(003-141) Sir E There must be money somewhere, I came into possession of one of

(003-142) the finest properties in Hampshire only twenty-six years ago, and now

(003-143) you mean to tell me I cannot raise 4,000 pounds?

(003-144) Coyle The fact is distressing, Sir Edward, but so it is.

(003-145) Sir E There's the Ravensdale property unencumbered.

(003-146) Coyle There, Sir Edward, you are under a mistake. The Ravensdale

(003-147) property is deeply encumbered, to nearly its full value.

(003-148) Sir E [Springing up.] Good heavens.

(003-149) Coyle I have found among my father's papers a [[debt-and-credit|mortgage]] of that very

(003-150) property to him.

(003-151) Sir E To your father! My father's agent?

(003-152) Coyle Yes, bearing date the year after the great contested election

(003-153) for the county, on which the late Sir Edward patriotically spent sixty

(003-154) thousand pounds for the honor of not being returned to Parliament.

(003-155) Sir E A mortgage on the Ravensdale estate. But it must have been paid

(003-156) off, Mr. Coyle, [anxiously,] have you looked for the release or the

(003-157) receipt?

(003-158) Coyle Neither exists. My father's sudden death explains sufficiently. I

(003-159) was left in ignorance of the transaction, but the seals on the deed and

(003-160) the stamps are intact, here it is, sir. [Shows it.]

(003-161) Sir E Sir, do you know that if this be true I am something like a

(003-162) beggar, and your father something like a thief.

(003-163) Coyle I see the first plainly, Sir Edward, but not the second.

(003-164) Sir E Do you forget sir, that your father was a charity boy, fed,

(003-165) clothed by my father?

(003-166) Coyle Well, Sir Edward?

(003-167) Sir E And do you mean to tell me, sir, that your father repaid that

(003-168) kindness by robbing his benefactor?

(003-169) Coyle Certainly not, but by advancing money to that benefactor when

(003-170) he wanted it, and by taking the security of one of his benefactor's

(003-171) estates, as any prudent man would under the circumstances.

(003-172) Sir E Why, then, sir, the benefactor's property is yours.

(003-173) Coyle Pardon me, the legal estate you have your equity of redemption.

(003-174) You have only to pay the money and the estate is yours as before.

(003-175) Sir E How dare you, sir, when you have just shown me that I cannot raise

(003-176) five hundred pounds in the world. Oh! Florence, why did I not listen to

(003-177) you when you warned me against this man?

(003-178) Coyle [Aside.] Oh! she warned you, did she? [Aloud.] I see one means, at

(003-179) least, of keeping the Ravensdale estate in the family.

(003-180) Sir E What is it?

(003-181) Coyle By marrying your daughter to the mortgagee.

(003-182) Sir E To you?

(003-183) Coyle I am prepared to settle the estate on Miss Trenchard the day she

(003-184) becomes Mrs. Richard Coyle.

(003-185) Sir E [Springing up.] You insolent scoundrel, how dare you insult me in

(003-186) my own house, sir. Leave it, sir, or I will have you kicked out by my

(003-187) servants.

(003-188) Coyle I never take an angry man at his word, Sir Edward. Give a few

(003-189) moments reflection to my offer, you can have me kicked out afterwards.

(003-190) Sir E [ Pacing stage.] A beggar, Sir Edward Trenchard a beggar, see my

(003-191) children reduced to labor for their bread, to misery perhaps; but the

(003-192) alternative, Florence detests him, still the match would save her,

(003-193) at least, from ruin. He might take the family name, I might retrench,

(003-194) retire, to the continent for a few years. Florence's health might

(003-195) serve as a pretence. Repugnant as the alternative is, yet it deserves

(003-196) consideration.

(003-197) Coyle [Who has watched.] Now, Sir Edward, shall I ring for the servants

(003-198) to kick me out?

(003-199) Sir E Nay Mr. Coyle, you must pardon my outburst, you know I am hasty,

(003-200) and----

(003-201) Flo [Without.] Papa, dear! [Enters gaily, starts on seeing Coyle.] Papa,

(003-202) pardon my breaking in on business, but our [[asa-trenchard|American cousin]] has come,

(003-203) such an original--and we are only waiting for you to escort us to the

(003-204) field.

(003-205) Sir E I will come directly, my love. Mr. Coyle, my dear, you did not see

(003-206) him.

(003-207) Flo [Disdainfully.] Oh! yes, I saw him, papa.

(003-208) Sir E Nay, Florence, your hand to Mr. Coyle. [Aside.] I insist.

(003-209) Flo Papa. [Frightened at his look, gives her hand. Coyle attempts to

(003-210) kiss it, she snatches it away and crosses to L.]

(003-211) Sir E [Crosses to L.] Come, Florence. Mr. Coyle, we will join you in the

(003-212) park. Come, my love, take my arm. [Hurries her off, L. 1 E.]
