# Themes & Concepts Index

This page indexes the thematic and conceptual content of *Our American Cousin*.
Taylor's comedy engages with social class, national identity, economic anxiety,
and the rituals of courtship and hospitality—all filtered through the lens of
transatlantic cultural collision.

## Thematic Landscape

The play's conceptual world is organized around the tension between English tradition
and American informality. Social rituals—drinking, dining, courtship, and the
maintenance of class distinctions—are repeatedly subjected to comic scrutiny,
while economic realities (mortgages, inheritances, financial desperation) provide
the underlying dramatic stakes.
