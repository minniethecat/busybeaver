# Mr. Buddicombe

Mr. Buddicombe is a servant at Trenchard Manor and Lord Dundreary's personal attendant in *Our American Cousin*, a minor character whose quiet competence and loyalty provide a grounding contrast to his master's celebrated absurdity.

## In the Play

Buddicombe is first "discovered on sofa reading newspaper" [001-043] in the play's opening domestic tableau — an image of servant leisure that immediately establishes Trenchard Manor as a household where even the staff enjoy a certain comfort. Binny introduces him with characteristic bombast as "Mr. Buddicombe, sir, my lord's hown man" [004-099] before summoning him with the comic cry "Turkey cock number one" [004-100], the nickname suggesting Buddicombe's strutting dignity.

As Dundreary's personal attendant, Buddicombe shadows his master through the play's complications. He enters alongside Dundreary [005-001], delivers letters on command [009-082], and is dismissed with a curt "You may go" [009-084] — a man whose entire dramatic function is to be available when needed and invisible when not. In the final act, he reports that "Skillet and I have made so bold, My lord" [010-054], participating in the servants' final-act initiative. The actor McDouall originated the role.

## Character Analysis

Buddicombe exemplifies the ideal Victorian servant: present but unobtrusive, capable but deferential. His association with Lord Dundreary is especially telling — Buddicombe's calm competence throws his master's fecklessness into sharper relief. Where Dundreary cannot complete a thought without tangling it, Buddicombe executes orders with wordless efficiency. Taylor understood that the surest way to make an eccentric aristocrat funnier is to give him a perfectly competent servant — the contrast writes its own comedy.

The nickname "turkey cock number one" hints at a private life beneath the servant's impassive exterior: Binny's mockery suggests Buddicombe is known among the staff for a certain pompous bearing, a personality trait the family never sees. In this, Buddicombe participates in the play's running joke that servants have richer interior lives than the aristocrats who employ them.
