(010-001) [[florence-trenchard|Flo]] You'll keep your promise and make Mr. [[murcott|Murcott]] your clerk, papa?

(010-002) [[sir-edward-trenchard|Sir E]] Yes, I can refuse nothing; I am so happy; I am so happy, I can

(010-003) refuse none anything to-day.

(010-004) Asa Can't you, Sir Edward! Now, that's awful lucky, for there's two gals

(010-005) want your consent mighty bad.

(010-006) Sir E Indeed; for what?

(010-007) Asa To get hitched.

(010-008) Sir E Hitched?

(010-009) Asa Yes to get spliced.

(010-010) Sir E Spliced?

(010-011) Asa Yes, to get married.

(010-012) Sir E They have it by anticipation. Who are they?

(010-013) Asa There's one on 'em. [Points to Florence.]

(010-014) Sir E Florence! and the other?

(010-015) Asa She's right outside. [Exit, hastily, R. 1. E.]

(010-016) Sir E Well, and who is the happy man, Lord [[lord-dundreary|Dun]]--

(010-017) Flo Lord Dundreary! No, papa--but [[harry-vernon|Harry Vernon]]. He's not poor now,

(010-018) though he's got a ship.

(010-019) Re-enter Asa, with [[mary-meredith|Mary]].

(010-020) Asa Here's the other one, Sir Edward.

(010-021) Sir E Mary? Who is the object of your choice?

(010-022) Mary Rough-spun, honest-hearted [[asa-trenchard|Asa Trenchard]].

(010-023) Sir E Ah! Mr. Trenchard you win a heart of gold.

(010-024) Flo And so does Mary, papa, believe me. [Crosses to Asa. Mary and Sir

(010-025) Edward go up.]

(010-026) Flo What's the matter?

(010-027) Asa You make me blush.

(010-028) Flo I don't see you blushing.

(010-029) Asa I'm blushing all the way down my back.

(010-030) Flo Oh, you go long. [Goes up stage.]

(010-031) Asa Hello! here's all the folks coming two by two, as if they were

(010-032) pairing for Noah's ark. Here's Mrs. Mountchestnut and the Sailor man.

(010-033) [Enter as Asa calls them off.] Here's [[capt-de-boots|De Boots]] and his gal, and darn

(010-034) me, if here ain't old setidy fetch it, and the sick gal, how are you,

(010-035) buttons? [Dundreary knocks against Asa, who is in C. of stage.]

(010-036) Dun There's that damned rhinocerous again. [Crosses to L. with Georgina,

(010-037) and seats her.]

(010-038) Asa Here comes turkey cock, number two, and his gal, and darn me, if

(010-039) here ain't Puffy and his gal.

(010-040) Sir E Mr. Vernon, take her, she's yours, though Heaven knows what I

(010-041) shall do without her.

(010-042) [[mrs-mountchessington|Mrs M]] [Rising.] Ah, Sir Edward, that is just my case; but you'll never

(010-043) know what it is to be a mother. [Comes down, L. C.] Georgina, [[augusta|Augusta]],

(010-044) my dears, come here. [They come down each side of her.] You'll sometimes

(010-045) think of your poor mamma, bless you. [Aside to them.] Oh, you couple of

(010-046) fools.

(010-047) [Bumps their foreheads. Dundreary has business with Georgina, then leads

(010-048) her to a seat, L.]

(010-049) De B [To Dundreary.] Why, Fred, we're all getting married!

(010-050) Dun Yes, it's catching, like the cholera.

(010-051) [[binny|Binny]] I 'ope, Sir Edward, there's no objections to my leading Miss

(010-052) [[sharpe|Sharpe]] to the hymenial halter.

(010-053) Sir E Certainly not, [[mr-binny|Mr. Binny]].

(010-054) Bud [To Dun.] And [[skillet|Skillet]] and I have made so bold, My lord--

(010-055) Dun Yes, you generally do make bold--but bless you, my children--bless

(010-056) you.

(010-057) Asa Say, you, lord, buttons, I say, whiskers.

(010-058) Dun Illustrious exile? [Comes down.]

(010-059) Asa They're a nice color, ain't they?

(010-060) Dun Yes, they're all wight now.

(010-061) Asa All wight? no, they're all black.

(010-062) Dun When I say wight I mean black.

(010-063) Asa Say, shall I tell that sick gal about that hair dye?

(010-064) Dun No, you needn't tell that sick gal about that hair dye!

(010-065) Asa Wal, I won't, if you don't want me to.

(010-066) Dun [Aside.] That man is a damned rattlesnake.

(010-067) [Goes up, sits in Georgina's lap--turns to apologize, sits in Augusta's

(010-068) lap--same business with Mrs. M, then goes back to Georgina.]

(010-069) Asa Miss Georgina. [She comes down.] How's your appetite? shall I tell

(010-070) that lord about the beefsteak and onions I saw you pitching into?

(010-071) Geo Please don't, Mr. Trenchard, I'm so delicate.

(010-072) Asa Wal, I won't, if you don't want me to.

(010-073) Geo Oh, thank you.

(010-074) [Backs up stage and sits in Dundreary's lap, who has taken her seat.]

(010-075) Asa Miss Gusty. [Augusta comes down.] Got your boots, hain't you?

(010-076) [[augustus-trenchard|Aug]] Yes, Mr. Trenchard.

(010-077) Asa How do they fit you? Say, shall I tell that fellow you were after me

(010-078) first?

(010-079) Aug [Extravagantly.] Not for the world, Mr. Trenchard.

(010-080) Asa [Mimicing.] Wal, I won't, if you don't want me to.

(010-081) Asa [To Mrs M.] Mrs. Mountchestnut.

(010-082) Dun [Coming down.] Sir, I haven't a chestnut to offer you, but if you'd

(010-083) like some of your native food, I'll order you a doughnut?

(010-084) Asa I dough not see it.

(010-085) Dun [Laughs.] That's good.

(010-086) Asa Yes, very good.

(010-087) Dun For you.

(010-088) Asa Oh, you get out, I mean the old lady.

(010-089) Dun Mrs. Mountchessington, this illustrious exile wishes to see you.

(010-090) [Mrs M. comes down.]

(010-091) Asa Wal, old woman?

(010-092) Mrs M Old woman, sir?

(010-093) Asa Got two of them gals off your hands, haven't you?

(010-094) Mrs M I'm proud to say, I have.

(010-095) Asa Shall I tell them fellows you tried to stick them on me first?

(010-096) Mrs M You'll please not mention the subject.

(010-097) Asa Wal, I won't, if you don't want me to. [Backs

(010-098) up;--curtseying;--knocks back against Dundreary, who is stooping to pick

(010-099) up a handkerchief. They turn and bunk foreheads.] Say, Mr. Puffy. [Binny

(010-100) comes down.] Shall I tell Sir Edward about your getting drunk in the

(010-101) wine cellar?

(010-102) Binny You need not--not if you don't like unto.

(010-103) Asa Wal, I won't, if you don't want me to.

(010-104) Binny Remember the hold hadage. ``A still tongue shows a wise ead.''

(010-105) Asa X Q's me.

(010-106) Binny O, I, C. [Goes up.]

(010-107) Flo [Comes down, L.] Well cousin, what have you to say to us? [Mary

(010-108) comes down R. of Asa.]

(010-109) Asa Wal, I ain't got no ring, to put in your noses, but I's got one to

(010-110) put on your finger. [To Mary.] And I guess the sailor man has one to put

(010-111) on yours, and I guess you two are as happy as clams at high water.

(010-112) Flo I am sure you must be very happy.

(010-113) Asa Wal, I am not so sure about my happiness.

(010-114) Flo Why, you ungrateful fellow. What do want to complete it?

(010-115) Asa [To Audience.] My happiness depends on you.

(010-116) Flo And I am sure you will not regret your kindness shown to Our

(010-117) American Cousin. But don't go yet, pray--for Lord Dundreary has a word

(010-118) to say. [Calls Dundreary.]

(010-119) Dun [Sneezes.] That's the idea.

(010-120) CURTAIN
