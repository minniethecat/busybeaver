(004-001) [[richard-coyle|Coyle]] Shallow, selfish fool. She warned you of me did she? And you did

(004-002) not heed her; you shall both pay dearly. She, for her suspicions, and

(004-003) you that you did not share them. [Walks up and down.] How lucky the

(004-004) seals were not cut from that [[debt-and-credit|mortgage]], when the release was given.

(004-005) 'Tis like the silly security of the Trenchard's. This mortgage makes

(004-006) Ravensdale mine, while the release that restores it to its owner lies in

(004-007) the recess of the bureau, whose secret my father revealed to me on

(004-008) his death bed. [Enter [[murcott|Murcott]], L. 1 E.] Write to the mortgagee of the

(004-009) Fanhill and Ellenthrope estates, to foreclose before the week is out,

(004-010) and tell Walters and Brass to put in execution to-day. We'll prick this

(004-011) wind-bag of a Baronet. Abel, we have both a bone to pick with him and

(004-012) his daughter. [Murcott starts.] Why, what's the matter?

(004-013) Mur Nothing, the dizziness I've had lately.

(004-014) [[mr-coyle|Coyle]] Brandy in the evening, brandy in the morning, brandy all night.

(004-015) What a fool you are, Murcott.

(004-016) Mur Who knows that as well as I do?

(004-017) Coyle If you would but keep the [[fortune-and-wealth|money]] out of your mouth, there's the

(004-018) making of a man in you yet.

(004-019) Mur No, no, it's gone too far, it's gone too far, thanks to the man

(004-020) who owns this house, you know all about it. How he found me a thriving,

(004-021) sober lad, flogging the village children through their spelling book.

(004-022) How he took a fancy to me as he called it, and employed me here to teach

(004-023) his son and Miss Florence. [His voice falters.] Then remember how I

(004-024) forgot who and what I was, and was cuffed out of the house like a dog.

(004-025) How I lost my school, my good name, but still hung about the place, they

(004-026) all looked askance at me, you don't know how that kills the heart of a

(004-027) man, then I took to drink and sank down, down, till I came to this.

(004-028) Coyle You owe [[sir-edward-trenchard|Sir Edward]] revenge, do you not? You shall have a rare

(004-029) revenge on him, that mortgage you found last week puts the remainder

(004-030) of the property in my reach, and I close my hand on it unless he will

(004-031) consent to my terms.

(004-032) Mur You can drive a hard bargain. I know.

(004-033) Coyle And a rare price I ask for his forbearance, Abel--his daughter's

(004-034) hand.

(004-035) Mur Florence?

(004-036) Coyle Yes, Florence marries Richard Coyle. Richard Coyle steps into Sir

(004-037) Edward's estates. There, you dog, will not that be a rare revenge. So

(004-038) follow me with those [[letters-and-documents|papers]]. [Crosses to L.] And now to lay the mine

(004-039) that will topple over the pride of the Trenchards. [Exit L. 1 E.]

(004-040) Mur He marry Florence! [[florence-trenchard|Florence Trenchard]]! My Florence. Mine! Florence

(004-041) _his wife_. No, no, better a thousand times she had been mine, low as

(004-042) I am, when I dreampt that dream, but it shan't be, it shan't be.

(004-043) [Tremblingly putting papers in bag.] If I can help her, sot though I

(004-044) am. Yes, I can help her, if the shock don't break me down. Oh! my poor

(004-045) muddled brain, surely there was a release with it when I found it. I

(004-046) must see Florence to warn her and expose Coyle's villainy. Oh! how my

(004-047) poor head throbs when I try to. I shall die if I don't have a drop of

(004-048) brandy, yes brandy. [Exit, L. 1 E.]

(004-049) Scene 3--Chamber in 3. at [[trenchard-manor|Trenchard Manor]]. Large shower bath near R. 3

(004-050) E. Toilet table with draw, L. 2 E. Small bottle in draw with red sealing

(004-051) wax on cork. Asa discovered seated, R. with foot on table, smoking a

(004-052) cigar. Valise on floor in front of him. [[binny|Binny]] discovered standing by his

(004-053) side.

(004-054) Asa Wal, I guess I begin to feel kinder comfortable here in this place,

(004-055) if it wan't for this tarnal fat critter. He don't seem to have any [[labor-and-worth|work]]

(004-056) to do, but swells out his big bosom like an old turkey-cock in

(004-057) laying time. I do wonder what he's here for? Do they think I mean to

(004-058) absquatulate with the spoons? [[[mr-binny|Binny]] attempts to take valise--Asa puts

(004-059) his foot on it.] Let that sweat. That's my plunder.

(004-060) Binny Will you have the kindness to give me your keys, hif you please,

(004-061) sir?

(004-062) Asa What do you want with my keys?

(004-063) Bin To put your things away in the wardrobe, sir.

(004-064) Asa Wal, I calculate if my two shirts, three bosoms, four collars, and

(004-065) two pair of socks were to get into that everlasting big bunk, they'd

(004-066) think themselves so all-fired small I should never be able to crawl into

(004-067) them again.

(004-068) Bin Will you take a baath before you dress?

(004-069) Asa Take a baath?

(004-070) Bin A baath.

(004-071) Asa I suppose you mean a bath. Wal, man, I calkalate I ain't going to

(004-072) expose myself to the shakes by getting into cold water in this cruel

(004-073) cold climate of yours, so make tracks.

(004-074) Bin Make what?

(004-075) Asa Vamose!

(004-076) Bin Make vamose!

(004-077) Asa Absquatulate.

(004-078) Bin Ab-- what sir?

(004-079) Asa Skedaddle.

(004-080) Bin Skedaddle?

(004-081) Asa Oh! get out.

(004-082) Bin Oh! [Going.] If you are going to dress you'll want some hassistance.

(004-083) Asa Assistance! what to get out of my unmentionables and into them

(004-084) again? Wal, 'spose I do, what then?

(004-085) Bin Just ring the bell, hi'll hattend you.

(004-086) Asa All right, come along. [Binny going.] Hold on, say, I may want

(004-087) to yawn presently and I shall want somebody to shut my mouth. [Binny

(004-088) hurries off, L. 1 E.] Wal, now I am alone, I can look about me and

(004-089) indulge the enquiring spirit of an American citizen. What an everlasting

(004-090) lot of things and fixins there is to be sure. [Opens table draw.] Here's

(004-091) a place will hold my plunder beautifully. [Sees bottle.] Hallo, what's

(004-092) this? [Comes down.] Something good to drink. [Smells bottle.] It smells

(004-093) awful bad. [Reads label.] Golden Fluid, one application turns the hair

(004-094) a beautiful brown, several applications will turn the hair a lustrous

(004-095) black. Well, if they keep on it may turn a pea green. I reckon this has

(004-096) been left here by some fellow who is ashamed of the natural color of his

(004-097) top knot. [Knock.] Come in.

(004-098) Enter Binny, L. 1 E.

(004-099) Bin [[buddicombe|Mr. Buddicombe]], sir, my lord's hown man.

(004-100) Asa Roll him in. [Binny beckons, enter Buddicombe.] Turkey cock number

(004-101) two, what is it?

(004-102) Bud My [[lord-dundreary|Lord Dundreary]]'s compliments and _have_ you seen a small _bottle_

(004-103) in the toilet table drawer?

(004-104) Asa Suppose I had, what then?

(004-105) Bud My lord wants it particly.

(004-106) Asa Was it a small bottle?

(004-107) Bud A small bottle.

(004-108) Bin Bottle small.

(004-109) Asa Blue label?

(004-110) Bin Label blue.

(004-111) Asa Red sealing wax on the top?

(004-112) Bud Red sealing wax.

(004-113) Bin Wax red.

(004-114) Asa Nice little bottle?

(004-115) Bin Little bottle nice.

(004-116) Asa Wal, I ain't seen it. [Aside.] If my lord sets a valley on it, guess

(004-117) it must be worth something.

(004-118) Bud Sorry to trouble you, sir.

(004-119) Bin [ Aside to Bud.] What his hit?

(004-120) Bud My lord's hair dye, the last bottle, and he turns red tomorrow.

(004-121) [Exit in haste.]

(004-122) Bin Orrable, what an hawful situation, to be sure.

(004-123) Asa [Aside.] So I've got my ring on that lord's nose, and if I don't

(004-124) make him dance to my tune it's a pity.

(004-125) Bin Miss Florence begged me to say she had borrowed a costume for you,

(004-126) for the harchery meeting, sir.

(004-127) Asa Hain't you dropped something?

(004-128) Bin Where?

(004-129) Asa What do you mean by the harchery meeting?

(004-130) Bin Where they shoot with bows and harrows.

(004-131) Asa There goes another of them, oh! you need'nt look for them, you can't

(004-132) find 'em when you want 'em. Now you just take my compliments to Miss

(004-133) Trenchard when I goes out shooting with injurious weapons I always wears

(004-134) my own genuine shooting costume. That's the natural buff tipped off with

(004-135) a little red paint.

(004-136) Bin Good gracious, he'd look like Hadam and Heve, in the garden of Eden.

(004-137) [Exit Binny.]

(004-138) Asa Wal, there's a queer lot of fixings. [Sees shower bath.] What on

(004-139) airth is that? Looks like a 'skeeter net, only it 'ain't long enough

(004-140) for a feller to lay down in unless he was to coil himself up like a

(004-141) woodchuck in a knot hole. I'd just like to know what the all-fired thing

(004-142) is meant for. [Calls.] Say Puffy, Puffy, Oh! he told me if I wanted him

(004-143) to ring the bell. [Looks round room.] Where on airth is the bell? [Slips

(004-144) partly inside shower bath, pulls rope, water comes down.] Murder! help!

(004-145) fire! Water! I'm drown.

(004-146) Enter [[skillet|Skillet]], [[sharpe|Sharpe]], R. 1 E. Binny, Buddicombe, L. 1 E., seeing Asa,

(004-147) all laugh, and keep it up till curtain falls.

(004-149) ACT II

(004-150) Scene 1.--Oriel Chamber in one.

(004-151) Enter [[mrs-mountchessington|Mrs. Mountchessington]] and [[augusta|Augusta]], L. 1 E., dressed for Archery

(004-152) Meeting.

(004-153) Mrs M No, my dear Augusta, you must be very careful. I don't by any

(004-154) means want you to give up [[capt-de-boots|De Boots]], his expectations are excellent, but,

(004-155) pray be attentive to this American savage, as I rather think he will

(004-156) prove the better match of the two, if what I hear of Mark Trenchard's

(004-157) property be correct.

(004-158) [[augustus-trenchard|Aug]] [Disdainfully.] Yes, ma.

(004-159) Mrs M And look more cheerful, my love.

(004-160) Aug I am so tired, ma, of admiring things I hate.

(004-161) Mrs. M Yes, my poor love, yet we must all make sacrifices to society.

(004-162) Look at your poor sister, with the appetite.

(004-163) Aug What am I to be enthusiastic about with that American, Ma?

(004-164) Mrs M Oh! I hardly know yet, my dear. We must study him. I think if

(004-165) you read up Sam Slick a little, it might be useful, and just dip into

(004-166) Bancroft's History of the [[united-states|United States]], or some of Russell's Letters;

(004-167) you should know something of George Washington, of whom the Americans

(004-168) are justly proud.

(004-169) Aug Here he comes, ma. What a ridiculous figure he [[beauty-and-appearance|looks]] in that dress,

(004-170) ha! ha!

(004-171) Mrs M Hush, my dear!

(004-172) Enter Asa, in Archery Dress.

(004-173) Aug Oh, [[asa-trenchard|Mr. Trenchard]], why did you not bring me one of those lovely

(004-174) Indian's dresses of your boundless prairie?

(004-175) Mrs M Yes, one of those dresses in which you hunt the buffalo.

(004-176) Aug [Extravagantly.] Yes, in which you hunt the buffalo.

(004-177) Asa [Imitating.] In which I hunt the buffalo. [Aside.] Buffaloes down in

(004-178) [[vermont|Vermont]]. [Aloud.] Wal, you see, them dresses are principally the nateral

(004-179) skin, tipped off with paint, and the indians object to parting with

(004-180) them.

(004-181) Both Ahem! ahem!

(004-182) Asa The first buffalo I see about here I shall hunt up for you.

(004-183) Mrs M Oh, you Americans are so clever, and so acute.

(004-184) Aug Yes, so 'cute.

(004-185) Asa Yes, we're 'cute, we are; know soft solder when we see it.

(004-186) Aug [Aside.] Ma, I do believe he's laughing at us.

(004-187) Mrs M Oh, no, my dear, you are mistaken. Oh! I perceive they are

(004-188) appearing for the archery practice. I suppose we shall see you on the

(004-189) ground, Mr. Trenchard.

(004-190) Asa Yes, I'll be there like a thousand of brick.

(004-191) Aug A thousand of brick!

(004-192) Mrs M Hush, my dear! that is doubtless some elegant American expression.

(004-193) Au revoir, Mr. Trenchard.

(004-194) Asa Which?

(004-195) Mrs M Au revoir. [Exit with Augusta, R.]

(004-196) Asa No, thank you, don't take any before dinner. No use their talking

(004-197) Dutch to me. Wal, I never see an old gal stand fire like that, she's

(004-198) a real old bison bull. I feel all-fired tuckered out riding in those

(004-199) keers. I'd like to have a snooze if I could find a place to lay down in.

(004-200) [Sees curtain on window, L. E.] Oh, this might do! [Pulls curtain, then

(004-201) starts back.] No you don't! One shower bath a day is enough for me.

(004-202) [Cautiously opens them.] No, I guess this is all right, I shall be just

(004-203) as snug in here as in a pew at meeting, or a private box at the Theatre.

(004-204) Hello! somebody's coming. [Goes into recess.]
