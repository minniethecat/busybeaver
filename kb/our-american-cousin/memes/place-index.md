This page catalogues all locations depicted or referenced in *Our American Cousin*.

## Setting and Dramatic Function

Tom Taylor uses location as a dramatic device throughout the play. Trenchard Manor represents
the established order of English society, while references to Vermont evoke the New World's
egalitarian spirit. Interior scenes—the drawing room, the dairy, the library—each serve as
stages for specific social encounters and revelations.
