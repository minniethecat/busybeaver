# Sheridan Apartments

## Overview

The Sheridan ap[[art-and-artists|art]]ment house is the primary setting of *The Small Bachelor*. Located in the he[[art-and-artists|art]] of [[new-york|New York]]'s [[art-and-artists|Bohemian]] and [[art-and-artists|artist]]ic qu[[art-and-artists|art]]er, it is home to [[George-Finch|George Finch]] and the center from which the novel's action radiates.

## In the Novel

The Sheridan is introduced early as the physical embodiment of the novel's [[art-and-artists|artist]]ic milieu:

> The Sheridan stands in the heart of New York's Bohemian and artistic quarter. If you threw a brick from any of its windows, you would be certain to brain some rising interior decorator, some Vorticist sculptor, or a writer of revolutionary vers libre. (002-002)

It is here that [[George-Finch|George]] and [[Hamilton-Beamish|Hamilton Beamish]] discuss [[love-and-romance|love]], [[art-and-artists|art]], and the complications of [[engagement-and-courtship|courtship]]. The ap[[art-and-artists|art]]ment serves as both home and refuge — a space where [[art-and-artists|Bohemian]] values hold sway against the [[class-and-society|social]] pressures of [[fifth-avenue|Fifth Avenue]].

## Significance

The Sheridan represents the [[art-and-artists|artist]]ic, unconventional world that [[George-Finch|George]] inhabits and that [[Mrs-Waddington|Mrs. Waddington]] despises. It is a symbolic counterpoint to the formal, [[fortune-and-inheritance|wealth]]y world of the Waddington residence, and the tension between these two spaces drives much of the novel's [[class-and-society|social]] [[humor-and-comedy|satire]].
