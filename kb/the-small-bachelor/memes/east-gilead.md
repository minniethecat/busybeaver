## A Stuffy Little Hamlet

East Gilead, Idaho, is [[George-Finch|George Finch]]'s hometown — and the novel's symbol of everything he fled when he came to [[new-york|New York]]:

> It was not precisely the way George would have described East Gilead, which was a stuffy little hamlet with a poorish water-supply and one of the worst soda-fountains in Idaho, but he nodded amiably. [011-029]

The "poorish water-supply" and the "worst soda-fountain in Idaho" are vintage Wodehouse details — small, specific, devastating. Through them, East Gilead becomes more than a place; it becomes a condition. It is provincialism, narrowness, the past that refuses to stay past.

## The Geography of the Past

East Gilead's narrative function is to provide [[George-Finch|George]] [[humor-and-comedy|wit]]h a history that complicates his present. Before he left for [[new-york|New York]], [[George-Finch|George]] had what the town calls an "[[engagement-and-courtship|understanding]]" [[humor-and-comedy|wit]]h a local girl named May:

> "I knew her in East Gilead," said George hopelessly. "We were sort of engaged." [034-013]

The qualifier "sort of" is the give-away. East Gilead's [[class-and-society|social]] customs — its "[[engagement-and-courtship|understanding]]s" — are provincial approximations of commitment, less binding than [[engagement-and-courtship|engagement]]s but more entangling than friendship. [[George-Finch|George]]'s hopelessness as he explains this to [[Hamilton-Beamish|Hamilton Beamish]] reveals his awareness that [[new-york|New York]] standards and East Gilead standards are incompatible:

> "Not where I come from. In East Gilead they have what they call understandings." [034-016]

## The Concept of "Understandings"

The East Gilead "[[engagement-and-courtship|understanding]]" is one of Wodehouse's finest comic inventions — a [[class-and-society|social]] institution that exists nowhere else, governed by rules that no one can quite [[art-and-artists|art]]iculate but everyone in East Gilead takes seriously. [[may-stubbs|May Stubbs]] — the girl [[humor-and-comedy|wit]]h whom [[George-Finch|George]] shared one — pursues him to [[new-york|New York]], threatening his [[love-and-romance|romance]] [[humor-and-comedy|wit]]h [[Molly-Waddington|Molly Waddington]]:

> "That is her name. By the way, she comes from your home town, East Gilead. Perhaps you know her?" [035-042]

[[Hamilton-Beamish|Beamish]]'s casual revelation that May has arrived in [[new-york|New York]] — and his suggestion that [[George-Finch|George]] might "know her" — is masterfully understated [[humor-and-comedy|comedy]]. [[George-Finch|George]] does indeed "know her," and the East Gilead [[engagement-and-courtship|understanding]] is about to collide [[humor-and-comedy|wit]]h his [[new-york|New York]] life.

## Hometown Time

Time itself moves differently in East Gilead. When a character remarks on how slowly minutes pass there, the observation is delivered [[humor-and-comedy|wit]]hout irony:

> "Yes. That's the way minutes do seem in East Gilead." [032-018]

This temporal slowness — the sense that nothing much happens and what does happen takes forever — is the antithesis of [[new-york|New York]]'s kaleidoscopic energy. It explains, as much as anything, why [[George-Finch|George]] left.

## Family and Roots

Yet East Gilead is not merely a place of entrapment. It is also home — the site of [[family-and-duty|family]] [[family-and-duty|obligation]]s that [[George-Finch|George]], for all his [[art-and-artists|artist]]ic pretensions, takes seriously. [[Molly-Waddington|Molly]] mentions returning:

> "I've got to jump on a train tomorrow and go visit the old folks back in East Gilead. It's Pop's birthday on Saturday, and I never miss it." [021-079]

Pop's birthday represents the claims of [[family-and-duty|family]], of the past, of the small-town values that [[George-Finch|George]] has not entirely abandoned. In the novel's final reflective passage, [[George-Finch|George]]'s thoughts circle back to origins:

> He thought of his youth in East Gilead, of his manhood in New York. [050-002]
