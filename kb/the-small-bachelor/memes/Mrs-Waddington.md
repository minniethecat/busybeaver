# Mrs. Waddington

## Overview

Mrs. Waddington is the principal antagonist of *The Small Bachelor*. Molly's mother, she judges all human beings — p[[art-and-artists|art]]icularly potential sons-in-[[crime-and-law|law]] — exclusively by t[[fortune-and-inheritance|heir]] [[fortune-and-inheritance|wealth]] and [[class-and-society|social]] standing. Her opposition to [[George-Finch|George Finch]] drives the novel's central conflict, and her sudden reversal upon learning of [[George-Finch|George]]'s [[fate-and-coincidence|fortune]] provides one of the novel's sharpest satirical moments.

## Role in the Novel

Mrs. Waddington functions as the obstacle between [[George-Finch|George]] and [[Molly-Waddington|Molly]]. She forbids the [[marriage-and-weddings|marriage]], dismissing [[George-Finch|George]] as a [[fate-and-coincidence|fortune]]-hunter:

> "Do you imagine I intend to allow you to ruin your life by marrying a despicable fortune-hunter?" (025-044)

When her daughter protests that [[George-Finch|George]] is not a [[fate-and-coincidence|fortune]]-hunter, Mrs. Waddington's response reveals her real objection — his profession and perceived poverty:

> "He is a penniless artist." (025-046)

## Character and Personality

Mrs. Waddington embodies a p[[art-and-artists|art]]icular type of [[class-and-society|social]] climber — one who judges all worth by the measure of [[money-and-wealth|money]]. Her prejudice against [[art-and-artists|artists]] is visceral rather than intellectual:

> And he was an artist! Mrs. Waddington shuddered. Of all the myriad individuals that went to make up the kaleidoscopic life of New York, she disliked artists most. They never had any money. (015-090)

As [[Hamilton-Beamish|Hamilton Beamish]] explains to [[George-Finch|George]], her worldview is entirely financial:

> "A woman of her type looks on artists as blots on the social scheme. I told you she judged her fellow-creatures solely by the extent of their bank balances." (018-012)

The satirical point becomes devastatingly clear when [[Hamilton-Beamish|Beamish]] reveals that [[George-Finch|George]] is actually [[fortune-and-inheritance|wealth]]y — and Mrs. Waddington's opposition vanishes instantly. Her principles were never about character or compatibility, only about [[money-and-wealth|money]] (025-096).
