# Giuseppe

## Identity and Role

Giuseppe is a waiter at the [[Purple-Chicken|Purple Chicken]] restaurant in *The Small Bachelor*. He appears during the novel's most chaotic scene — the tablecloth fracas in which [[George-Finch|George Finch]] sweeps a tablecloth over a policeman's head — and his response elevates an already absurd situation to sublime comic heights.

## The Tablecloth Fracas

When [[George-Finch|George]] brings the tablecloth down on the policeman's head "in a hideous whirl of apple-pie, ice water, bread, potatoes, salad and *poulet rôti*," the [[Purple-Chicken|Purple Chicken]]'s clientele responds with "interested cries." But it is Giuseppe who delivers the scene's most memorable line:

> "*Casta dimura salve e pura!* Attaboy! Soak him again." (047-085)

The juxtaposition of operatic Italian — or what sounds like operatic Italian — with the demotic American "Attaboy!" is pure Wodehouse. Giuseppe is described as being "convinced now that the man in the tablecloth was one who had not the best interests of the Purple Chicken at heart" — a masterpiece of understated characterization delivered through a single observation.

## Comic Function

Giuseppe belongs to the novel's gallery of minor characters who appear for a single scene and leave an indelible impression. His bilingual battle cry — half aria, half baseball game — captures the [[Purple-Chicken|Purple Chicken]]'s identity as a place where [[art-and-artists|Bohemian]] culture and American vernacular collide, and where even the waiters rise to the occasion of chaos with style.
