# The Purple Chicken

## Overview

The Purple Chicken is a [[food-and-dining|restaurant]] in *The Small Bachelor* that functions as an informal gathering place for the novel's characters. Its whimsical name captures the [[art-and-artists|Bohemian]] spirit of [[Greenwich-Village|Greenwich Village]] and provides a counterpoint to the formal [[food-and-dining|dining]] rooms of high [[class-and-society|society]]. The novel introduces it as one of "those numerous oases in this great city where, in spite of the law of Prohibition, you can still, so the cognoscenti whisper, 'always get it if they know you'" (002-002).

## In the Novel

Characters gather at the Purple Chicken to eat, drink, and conspire away from the watchful eyes of [[Mrs-Waddington|Mrs. Waddington]] and her [[class-and-society|social]] circle. The [[food-and-dining|restaurant]] represents a more democratic and relaxed [[class-and-society|social]] space — one where [[art-and-artists|artists]], [[efficiency-and-self-improvement|efficiency]] experts, and young [[love-and-romance|love]]rs can meet as equals. Officer Garroway warns that a raid is imminent because "the Purple Chicken, defying the Eighteenth Amendment, still purveys alcoholic liquors" (003-013).

On a typical night, the Purple Chicken "catered for the intelligentsia of the neighbourhood" — "wispy poets and slender futurist painters" were the usual clientele (047-002). But the restaurant also witnesses the novel's most chaotic scene when [[George-Finch|George Finch]], in a moment of desperation, sweeps a tablecloth off a table "in a hideous whirl of apple-pie, ice water, bread, potatoes, salad and *poulet rôti*" and brings it down on a policeman's head — an occurrence that even in the Purple Chicken was "unusual and calculated to excite remark" (047-082).

## Significance

The Purple Chicken is more than a [[food-and-dining|restaurant]] — it is a symbol of the unconventional, creative, and slightly absurd world that Wodehouse celebrates. Its very name defies the pretensions of high [[class-and-society|society]] and announces an alternative set of values: whimsy over formality, community over [[class-and-society|status]], and good company over expensive [[food-and-dining|cuisine]].
