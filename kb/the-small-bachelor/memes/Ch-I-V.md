# V

[006-001] “[[mullett|Mullett]],” said [[George-Finch|George]].

[006-002] “Sir?”

[006-003] “Have you pressed my [[fashion-and-appearance|dress]] clothes?”

[006-004] “Yes, sir.”

[006-005] “And brushed them?”

[006-006] “Yes, sir.”

[006-007] “My ties—are they laid out?”

[006-008] “In a neat row, sir.”

[006-009] George coughed.

[006-010] “Mullett!”

[006-011] “Sir?”

[006-012] “You recollect the little chat we were having just now?”

[006-013] “Sir?”

[006-014] “[[About]] the young lady I—er. …”

[006-015] “Oh, yes, sir.”

[006-016] “I understand you have seen her.”

[006-017] “Just a glimpse, sir.”

[006-018] George coughed again.

[006-019] “Ah—rather attractive, Mullett, didn’t you think?”

[006-020] “Extremely, sir. Very cuddly.”

[006-021] “The exact adjective I would have used myself, Mullett!”

[006-022] “Indeed, sir?”

[006-023] “Cuddly! A beautiful word.”

[006-024] “I think so, sir.”

[006-025] George coughed for the third time.

[006-026] “A lozenge, sir?” said Mullett solicitously.

[006-027] “No, thank you.”

[006-028] “Very good, sir.”

[006-029] “Mullett!”

[006-030] “Sir?”

[006-031] “I find that Mr. [[Hamilton-Beamish|Beamish]] is an intimate friend of this young lady.”

[006-032] “Fancy that, sir!”

[006-033] “He is going to introduce me.”

[006-034] “Very gratifying, I am sure, sir.”

[006-035] George sighed dreamily.

[006-036] “Life is very sweet, Mullett.”

[006-037] “For those that like it, sir—yes, sir.”

[006-038] “Lead me to the ties,” said George.
