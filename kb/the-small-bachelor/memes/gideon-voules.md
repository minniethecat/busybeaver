# Gideon Voules

## Identity and Role

The Reverend Gideon Voules is the clergyman summoned to perform the wedding ceremony in *The Small Bachelor*. Introduced by [[Molly-Waddington|Molly Waddington]] with the words "This is the Reverend Gideon Voules. He is going to marry us" (036-082), he is described as looking at [[George-Finch|George Finch]] "with a dull and poached-egg-like eye" (036-084). The narrator notes that he "did not seem to the latter to be a frightfully cheery sort of person: but, after all, when you're married, you're married, no matter how like a poached egg the presiding minister may look."

## Character

The Reverend Gideon later draws [[George-Finch|George]] into a corner and "endeavour[s] to make his better acquaintance," revealing himself as "a strong believer in presentiments" (036-112). His presence at the chaotic wedding — with its romantic complications, [[crime-and-law|police]] interruptions, and last-minute revelations — provides a fixed point of clerical dignity amid the surrounding [[humor-and-comedy|farce]].

In the novel's final reflections, [[George-Finch|George]] thinks of "the Reverend Gideon Voules and his white socks" alongside the other figures who populated his journey toward matrimony (050-002). The white socks, like the poached-egg eye, are vintage Wodehouse — the single, devastating physical detail that makes a minor character unforgettable.

## Significance

The Reverend Gideon Voules belongs to Wodehouse's gallery of comic clergymen — figures whose professional solemnity is perpetually undercut by physical absurdity. His poached-egg eye and white socks do not make him a bad clergyman; they simply make him a Wodehousian one.
