# Molly Waddington

## Overview

Molly Waddington is the romantic heroine of *The Small Bachelor*. The daughter of the [[class-and-society|social]]ly ambitious [[Mrs-Waddington|Mrs. Waddington]], Molly defies her mother's materialistic values by loving [[George-Finch|George Finch]] — a man her mother dismisses as "a penniless [[art-and-artists|artist]]." Her loyalty to [[George-Finch|George]], even before his [[fortune-and-inheritance|wealth]] is revealed, represents the novel's affirmation of genuine feeling over [[class-and-society|social]] calculation. As the emotional anchor of the story, Molly's steadfast [[love-and-romance|love]] provides the fixed point around which the comic machinery revolves.

## Role in the Novel

Molly is the object of [[George-Finch|George Finch]]'s [[beamish-bus-encounter|love at first sight]]. She returns his affection [[humor-and-comedy|wit]]h equal sincerity, disregarding the [[class-and-society|social]] and financial objections her mother raises. When [[George-Finch|George]] is revealed to be an [[art-and-artists|artist]] — a category her mother despises — Molly does not waver:

> "I am sure George is one of the cleverest artists living," cried Molly. (025-084)

Her steadfastness provides the emotional anchor of the novel. While [[George-Finch|George]] worries about [[class-and-society|social]] obstacles and [[Hamilton-Beamish|Hamilton Beamish]] schemes, Molly simply [[love-and-romance|love]]s — and her [[love-and-romance|love]] is the fixed point around which the comic machinery revolves.

When the plan to elope goes awry — the clergyman sprains his ankle and [[Mrs-Waddington|Mrs. Waddington]] and Molly must travel to Flushing to find a substitute — the confusion multiplies (035-014). Yet through every complication, Molly remains determined. Her reunion [[humor-and-comedy|wit]]h [[George-Finch|George]] at the climax is simple and direct:

> "Here we are, angel," said Molly. (036-079)

> "This is the Reverend Gideon Voules," said Molly. "He is going to marry us." (036-082)

## Character and Personality

Molly is more than a passive [[love-and-romance|love]] interest. She actively schemes to help [[George-Finch|George]], including the plan to visit [[madame-eulalie|Madame Eulalie]] the palmist to secure a favorable [[fate-and-coincidence|fortune]]-reading that would persuade her mother. Her willingness to defy [[family-and-duty|maternal]] authority and [[class-and-society|social]] convention reveals a character of genuine independence beneath the surface of the conventional romantic heroine.

Even when confronted by her mother, Molly shows resilience. After an emotional confrontation, she emerges "somewhat red about the eyes and distinctly mutinous about the mouth" (038-014) — wounded but unbowed, a young woman who has chosen [[love-and-romance|love]] over obedience.
