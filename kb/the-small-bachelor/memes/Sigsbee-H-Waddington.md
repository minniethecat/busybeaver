# Sigsbee H. Waddington

## Overview

Sigsbee H. Waddington is [[Molly-Waddington|Molly]]'s father in *The Small Bachelor*. A [[fortune-and-inheritance|wealth]]y but largely passive figure, he defers to his wife's [[class-and-society|social]] judgments while occasionally functioning as a more sympathetic presence. His [[fortune-and-inheritance|wealth]] is the foundation of the Waddington [[family-and-duty|family]]'s [[class-and-society|social]] standing, yet he himself is a background figure — a husband who has learned to let his wife dominate.

## Role in the Novel

Sigsbee H. functions primarily as a contrast to his wife. Where [[Mrs-Waddington|Mrs. Waddington]] is forceful, judgmental, and [[class-and-society|social]]ly aggressive, Sigsbee H. is mild, accommodating, and largely absent from the novel's conflicts. He appears at key moments — [[waddington-dinner-party|the dinner]] p[[art-and-artists|art]]y, the crisis over [[Molly-Waddington|Molly]]'s [[marriage-and-weddings|marriage]] — but rarely drives the action.

His [[fortune-and-inheritance|wealth]], inherited by [[George-Finch|George Finch]] through a different [[family-and-duty|family]] line, is the key that unlocks the plot. When [[George-Finch|George]]'s own [[fortune-and-inheritance|wealth]] is revealed, it is Sigsbee H. who confirms the transformation of the situation:

> "Molly, my dear," said Sigsbee H., "touching on that necklace. Now that this splendid young fellow turns out to be very rich, you will not want to sell it, of course?" (025-125)

## Character and Personality

Sigsbee H. Waddington represents the type of amiable, slightly bewildered [[fortune-and-inheritance|wealth]]y husband familiar from Wodehouse's fiction. He is not unintelligent, but he has long since learned that domestic peace requires deference to his wife. His few moments of independent action — such as his conversation [[humor-and-comedy|wit]]h [[Molly-Waddington|Molly]] about the necklace — suggest a warmer, more sympathetic character than his wife, one whom life [[humor-and-comedy|wit]]h [[Mrs-Waddington|Mrs. Waddington]] has subdued but not extinguished.
