# IV

[016-001] It was a fair sunny morning next day when [[George-Finch|George Finch]] trotted up the steps of Number 16, Seventy-Ninth Street, East, and pressed the bell. He was wearing his dove-grey suit, and under his arm was an enormous canvas wrapped in brown paper. After much thought he had decided to present [[Molly-Waddington|Molly]] with his favourite work, Hail, Jocund Spring!—a picture representing a young woman, scantily draped and obviously suffering from an advanced form of chorea, dancing with lambs in a flower-speckled field. At the moment which George had selected for her portrayal, she had—to judge from her expression—just stepped rather hard on a sharp stone. Still, she was George’s masterpiece, and he intended to present her to Molly.

[016-002] The door opened. Ferris, the butler, appeared.

[016-003] “All goods,” said Ferris, regarding George dispassionately, “must be delivered in the rear.”

[016-004] George blinked.

[016-005] “I want to see Miss Waddington.”

[016-006] “Miss Waddington is not at home.”

[016-007] “Can I see Mr. Waddington?” asked George, accepting the second-best.

[016-008] “Mr. Waddington is not at home.”

[016-009] George hesitated a moment before he spoke again. But [[love-and-romance|love]] conquers all.

[016-010] “Can I see Mrs. Waddington?”

[016-011] “Mrs. Waddington is not at home.”

[016-012] As the butler spoke, there proceeded from the upper regions of the house a commanding female voice that inquired of an unseen [[Sigsbee-H-Waddington|Sigsbee]] how many times the speaker had told him not to smoke in the drawing-room.

[016-013] “But I can hear her,” George pointed out.

[016-014] The butler shrugged his shoulders with an aloof gesture, as if disclaiming all desire to go into these mysteries. His look suggested that he thought George might possibly be psychic.

[016-015] “Mrs. Waddington is not at home,” he said once more.

[016-016] There was a pause.

[016-017] “Nice morning,” said George.

[016-018] “The weather appears to be clement,” agreed Ferris.

[016-019] George then tumbled backwards down the steps, and the interview concluded.
