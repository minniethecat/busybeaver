# United Beef

## Identity and Role

United Beef is a wealthy business magnate in *The Small Bachelor* — one of those characters Wodehouse names with a single brilliant stroke that makes further description almost unnecessary. He appears among the guests at the [[waddington-dinner-party|Waddington dinner party]] as part of a "consignment worth on the hoof between eighty and a hundred million," alongside Consolidated Popcorn and their respective families (008-010).

## The Fateful Revelation

United Beef's most significant contribution to the plot occurs at a later gathering when he casually reveals to [[Sigsbee-H-Waddington|Sigsbee H. Waddington]] that the [[finer-and-better-film-co|Finer and Better Motion Picture Company]] — in which [[Sigsbee-H-Waddington|Waddington]] declined to invest — has become enormously successful. His words are devastating in their offhandedness:

"Too bad. Too bad."

The narrator captures the moment with precision: "The solid outlines of United Beef shimmered uncertainly before Mr. Waddington's horrified eyes" (036-155). United Beef then twists the knife by observing, through a mouthful of caviar, that the missed opportunity represents "Only millions, that's all. Merely millions."

## Comic Function

United Beef represents the world of big business that exists at the periphery of the novel's romantic plot. He is not a character so much as a force — the embodiment of the [[fortune-and-inheritance|wealth]] that [[Sigsbee-H-Waddington|Waddington]] desperately pursues and consistently fails to capture. His very name suggests both scale and absurdity, and Wodehouse uses him sparingly but effectively: a few appearances, each one delivering another blow to Waddington's financial ambitions.
