# Greenwich Village

## Overview

Greenwich Village in the early twentieth century was the [[art-and-artists|Bohemian]] he[[art-and-artists|art]] of [[new-york|New York City]] — a neighborhood of [[art-and-artists|artists]], writers, and free spirits. In *The Small Bachelor*, Wodehouse uses the Village as both a physical setting and a [[class-and-society|social]] symbol, representing everything that respectable [[fifth-avenue|Fifth Avenue]] [[class-and-society|society]] fears and despises. The novel opens at the [[Sheridan-Apartments|Sheridan Apartment House]], "near Washington Square, New York" in the heart of the city's "Bohemian and artistic quarter" (002-002).

## In the Novel

[[George-Finch|George Finch]] lives in the Village, at the [[Sheridan-Apartments|Sheridan]] ap[[art-and-artists|art]]ment house. His identity as a Village [[art-and-artists|artist]] is the source of both his pride and his [[class-and-society|social]] difficulties. [[Mrs-Waddington|Mrs. Waddington]]'s horror at discovering that [[George-Finch|George]] is an [[art-and-artists|artist]] is inseparable from her horror at his Village associations:

> "Mr. Finch," she said, "though a very incompetent artist, has lived for a considerable time in the heart of Greenwich Village and mingled daily with Bohemians of both sexes and questionable morals." (025-112)

The neighborhood signifies a world of unconventional values that threatens her [[class-and-society|social]] order. She refuses to sympathise with [[Molly-Waddington|Molly]]'s romantic predicament because [[George-Finch|George]] has "deliberately and of his own free-will goes and lives near Washington Square" (025-114).

The Village serves as a haven where characters can escape the oppressive formality of high [[class-and-society|society]] and where more authentic relationships can develop.

## Significance

Greenwich Village in the novel functions as a geographic metaphor for [[art-and-artists|artist]]ic freedom and [[class-and-society|social]] nonconformity, in deliberate contrast to the [[fortune-and-inheritance|wealth]] and convention of [[fifth-avenue|Fifth Avenue]].
