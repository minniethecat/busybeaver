# Narrative Timeline of The Mill on the Floss

This page presents a book-by-book chronology of the major events in George Eliot's *The Mill on the Floss* (1860). The novel spans approximately fifteen years, tracing Maggie and Tom Tulliver from childhood to young adulthood.

## Book I — Boy and Girl

The novel opens at Dorlcote Mill, home of the Tulliver family. Mr. Tulliver decides to send his son Tom to a boarding school with Mr. Stelling, hoping to give him the education he never had. The young Maggie Tulliver, passionate and clever, is introduced in vivid contrast to her more conventional brother. Key events include Maggie's impulsive flight to the gypsies (see [[maggie-and-the-gypsies]]) and Mr. Tulliver's escalating conflict with Lawyer Wakem over water rights, culminating in the lawsuit (see [[the-lawsuit]]) that will ruin the family.

## Book II — School-Time

Tom is sent to study under the Reverend Mr. Stelling at King's Lorton (see [[tom-mr-stelling]]). There he meets Philip Wakem, the lawyer's sensitive and hunchbacked son. An unlikely friendship begins to form between Tom and Philip, complicated by the bitter enmity between their fathers. Meanwhile, Maggie, left at home, struggles with the constraints placed on girls and begins to develop her fierce inner life.

## Book III — The Downfall

Mr. Tulliver loses the lawsuit and faces bankruptcy (see [[ruin-and-bankruptcy]]). The Dodson family council (see [[dodson-family-council]]) convenes but offers limited help. The family's furniture and belongings are sold at auction (see [[the-auction]]). Mr. Tulliver, broken and humiliated, suffers a severe illness. Upon recovering, he makes Tom swear in the family Bible to repay the debts and seek revenge on Wakem. Shortly afterward, Mr. Tulliver dies (see [[mr-tulliver-death]]).

## Book IV — The Valley of Humiliation

A quieter book focused on the aftermath of loss. Tom goes to work to repay the family debts. Maggie, now a young woman, struggles with the narrowness of her life at the mill, finding solace in religion and the ascetic teachings of Thomas a Kempis.

## Book V — Wheat and Tares

Maggie renews her acquaintance with Philip Wakem during secret meetings in the Red Deeps (see [[maggie-and-philip-red-deeps]]). Philip, who has long loved Maggie, encourages her intellectual growth. Tom discovers the meetings and, in a scene of characteristic severity, forbids Maggie from seeing Philip again (see [[the-quarrel]]). Tom achieves his goal of repaying the family debts (see [[toms-redemption]]), restoring the Tulliver name.

## Book VI — The Great Temptation

Maggie visits her cousin Lucy Deane in St. Ogg's, entering a world of social elegance and leisure. She meets Stephen Guest, Lucy's suitor, and a powerful attraction develops between them (see [[stephen-guest-courtship]]). The tension culminates in a near-elopement (see [[the-near-elopement]]), which Maggie ultimately resists. However, her reputation is ruined, and she returns to St. Ogg's to face condemnation and social ostracism.

## Book VII — The Final Rescue

Maggie is shunned by the community of St. Ogg's (see [[maggie-return-to-st-oggs]]). Only her mother, Mrs. Tulliver, and the loyal Bob Jakin stand by her. A catastrophic flood sweeps through the valley (see [[the-flood]]). Maggie rows through the rising waters to rescue Tom from the mill. In the boat together, brother and sister are reconciled in a moment of transcendent love before the waters overwhelm them. Bob Jakin's rescue attempt (see [[bob-jakin-rescue]]) arrives too late.

## Aftermath

The epilogue records that the brother and sister were buried together in the churchyard, reunited in death. Dorlcote Mill is eventually taken over by a man named Jetsome. The landscape heals, and the Floss flows on.
