# Pride

**Pride** in *The Mill on the Floss* is primarily family pride—an attachment to clan identity that serves as both social armor and moral blindfold. The Dodson family motto, to act "as becomes a Dodson," elevates pride from personal vanity to collective creed.

## Definition

Dodson pride is not mere arrogance but a comprehensive social philosophy. It dictates behavior, governs economic decisions, and provides the standard by which all actions are judged. Mrs. Glegg embodies this pride in its purest form: every choice is measured against its effect on family standing. The Tullivers, by contrast, possess a more individualistic pride—Mr. Tulliver's pride in his mill and his independence, which proves his undoing when it leads him into a disastrous lawsuit.

## Context in the Novel

Family pride fuels the novel's central conflicts. The Dodson sisters' gatherings become arenas for the display and policing of family status (007-001). Mrs. Glegg's pronouncements on "our family" serve as both comfort and constraint, binding members to a collective identity while denying individual deviation.

Mr. Tulliver's pride operates differently: it is the pride of the self-made man who "never run i' my family" to bankruptcy (003-019), and this very pride blinds him to the financial risk of suing Lawyer Wakem. His fall is a fall of pride as much as of fortune. Tom inherits this pride, channeling it into his determination to repay the family debts—a noble but narrowing ambition that leaves no room for Maggie's more complex moral vision.

## Key Passages

- **Family pride as moral code** (003-005): Speaking disrespectfully of the family is the ultimate transgression in Mrs. Glegg's world.
- **Pride in family lineage** (003-019): Mr. Tulliver insists that bankruptcy "niver run i' my family," revealing pride's vulnerability.
- **The family party** (007-001): A Dodson gathering showcases pride as performance and mutual surveillance.
- **Pride of appearance** (008-001): The Dodsons "were certainly a handsome family," and this physical pride reinforces social standing.
