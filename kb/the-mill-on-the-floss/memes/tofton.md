# Tofton

A riverside village downstream from St. Ogg's on the Floss, accessible by both road and ferry, and the direction of Maggie Tulliver's final journey during the climactic flood.

## Description

Tofton lies on the River Floss, reached from St. Ogg's by the Tofton Road [013-006] or by crossing at the Tofton Ferry [044-019]. The village presents a picturesque prospect of "trees and houses" along the river, and contains at least one substantial residence — "the finest stone house" among the trees of Tofton [030-043].

## Significance

Tofton serves as the geographical endpoint of the novel's climactic flood sequence. Maggie Tulliver rows with desperate strength downstream past Tofton's trees and houses [058-038], struggling against the current until she passes "nearly to Tofton" before she can gain control of the boat [065-039]. The village is the last named landmark before the final tragedy, and its houses are the vantage from which observers witness the boat's danger [065-052][065-054].

Tofton is also associated with Lawyer Wakem, who directs Luke to take the Tofton Ferry route rather than passing through the town [044-019] — a detail that connects the village to the novel's themes of evasion and social avoidance.

## Key Events at This Location

- Maggie and Stephen Guest's boating excursion on the river past Tofton, during which the backward-flowing tide carries them beyond the point of return [058-038]
- Maggie's solo rowing journey during the flood, struggling to reach Dorlcote Mill as she passes Tofton [065-039][065-052]
- The final sighting of Maggie's boat from the Tofton houses as the flood danger becomes apparent [065-054]
