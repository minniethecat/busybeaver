# Garum Firs

Garum Firs is the home of Mr. and Mrs. Glegg, the most formidable of the Dodson households. Its carefully managed economy and atmosphere of strict propriety make it the architectural expression of Dodson values.

## Description

Garum Firs is presented as the model of Dodson domestic management: scrupulously clean, economically run, and maintained with an eye to respectability rather than comfort. Mrs. Glegg's thrift governs every detail of the household, from the carefully measured provisions to the well-preserved furnishings. The house is both her domain and her fortress.

## Significance

Garum Firs represents the Dodson ideal successfully realized. It is neither as precarious as the Tulliver mill nor as socially ambitious as the Deane household. Its solidity is the solidity of people who have never taken a risk and never made a mistake. Mrs. Glegg's authority within its walls is absolute, making it one of the few spaces in the novel where a woman exercises unquestioned power.
