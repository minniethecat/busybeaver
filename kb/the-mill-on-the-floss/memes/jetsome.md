# Jetsome

**Jetsome** is a minor character in George Eliot's *The Mill on the Floss*, hired by [[lawyer-wakem|Lawyer Wakem]] to manage [[dorlcote-mill|Dorlcote Mill]] after [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]]'s financial ruin. His incompetence and dissolute habits disappoint Wakem and contribute to the mill's decline under non-Tulliver management.

## Role in the Novel

Jetsome is introduced in Book 5 as the man Wakem installs at the mill. [[tom-tulliver|Tom Tulliver]] describes him dismissively as "a loose fish" who is "taking to drinking" [050-021], reflecting Tom's resentment at seeing a stranger occupy his family's ancestral workplace.

Despite Wakem's hopes, Jetsome proves unreliable. He is described as "that unfortunate young Jetsome" [057-002] — unfortunate both for himself and for Wakem's investment. By Book 6, Wakem experiences "disappointment in this young Jetsome, to whom, apparently, he was" attached in some paternal capacity [063-009], suggesting Wakem had taken a personal interest in the young man that went unreciprocated.

## Significance

Jetsome represents the disruption of the natural order at Dorlcote Mill. His failure as miller underscores the novel's theme that the mill is inseparable from the Tulliver family — it cannot thrive under hired management. In the novel's moral economy, Jetsome's incompetence is a form of poetic justice, as Wakem's takeover of the mill yields only frustration.
