# Laceham

An ancient market town on the River Floss, noted for its manufactured goods — particularly linen — and serving as a commercial destination for the novel's trading characters.

## Description

Laceham is an "ancient town" situated on the River Floss, distinguished by its position as a regional trading centre [022-001]. The town lies within coach-travel distance of St. Ogg's, with the Laceham coach arriving as a regular fixture of local travel [024-003][024-010]. Its character as a manufacturing town is reflected in the reference to the "Laceham Courier," a local newspaper carrying community news [058-019].

## Commercial Significance

Laceham's primary narrative role is as a source of high-quality manufactured goods. Bob Jakin serves as an intermediary for "Laceham goods," which he presents to the Dodson aunts as a standard of quality manufacture [039-027]. During his first independent trading venture, Bob rides into Laceham to procure goods for resale [039-085][039-115]. The goods are associated with quality and durability in the Dodson value system [039-027][039-106].

## Key Events at This Location

- Tom Tulliver's early memory of attending school in Laceham, recalled through the connection with his cousin Lucy [022-001]
- Bob Jakin's commercial journey to Laceham as a packman, marking his first step into independent trade [039-085][039-115]
- Mrs. Glegg's purchasing history with Laceham goods, referenced as a marker of respectable domestic economy [039-006][039-027]
