# Character Map of The Mill on the Floss

This page provides a relational overview of the major characters in George Eliot's *The Mill on the Floss*, organized by family affiliation and social connection. For a complete alphabetical index, see [[characters]].

## The Tulliver Family

The Tullivers are the central family of the novel, living at Dorlcote Mill on the River Floss.

- **[[maggie-tulliver|Maggie Tulliver]]** — The passionate, intelligent protagonist. Torn between duty and desire throughout the novel.
- **[[tom-tulliver|Tom Tulliver]]** — Maggie's older brother, rigid in his sense of justice and family honor.
- **[[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]]** — The father, miller by trade, whose lawsuit against Lawyer Wakem leads to the family's ruin.
- **[[mrs-tulliver|Mrs. Tulliver]]** (née Bessy Dodson) — The mother, preoccupied with domestic propriety and her Dodson lineage.
- **[[kezia|Kezia]]** — The loyal Tulliver family servant.

## The Dodson Family

The Dodsons represent provincial respectability and social order in St. Ogg's. Mrs. Tulliver is one of four Dodson sisters.

- **[[mrs-glegg|Mrs. Glegg]]** — The eldest Dodson sister, sharp-tongued but ultimately loyal to family.
- **[[mr-glegg|Mr. Glegg]]** — Mrs. Glegg's retired husband, mild-mannered and conciliatory.
- **[[mrs-pullet|Mrs. Pullet]]** — The second Dodson sister, hypochondriacal and weeping.
- **[[mr-pullet|Mr. Pullet]]** — A gentleman farmer, Mrs. Pullet's husband.
- **[[mrs-deane|Mrs. Deane]]** — The third Dodson sister, mother of Lucy.
- **[[mr-deane|Mr. Deane]]** — A successful businessman, partner in Guest & Co.
- **[[lucy-deane|Lucy Deane]]** — The pretty, kind-hearted cousin whom Stephen Guest courts.
- **[[miss-dodson|Miss Dodson]]** — An unmarried Dodson relative.
- **[[dodson-family|The Dodson Family]]** — Collective page on Dodson values and social influence.

## The Wakem Family

- **[[lawyer-wakem|Lawyer Wakem]]** — Mr. Tulliver's adversary in the lawsuit, a shrewd and not wholly unsympathetic figure.
- **[[philip-wakem|Philip Wakem]]** — The hunchbacked, sensitive son who loves Maggie and befriends both Tulliver children in different ways.

## The Guest Family

- **[[stephen-guest|Stephen Guest]]** — The handsome, charming suitor of Lucy Deane who falls passionately in love with Maggie.

## Other Key Characters

- **[[bob-jakin|Bob Jakin]]** — Tom's childhood friend turned loyal companion, a peddler with a good heart.
- **[[mrs-jakin|Mrs. Jakin]]** — Bob Jakin's mother.
- **[[mr-moss|Mr. Moss]]** — Mr. Tulliver's brother-in-law, a struggling farmer at Basset.
- **[[mrs-moss|Mrs. Moss]]** — Mr. Tulliver's sister, living in rural poverty at Basset.
- **[[mr-pivart|Mr. Pivart]]** — The upstream farmer whose irrigation practices trigger the lawsuit.
- **[[mr-stelling|Mr. Stelling]]** — The clergyman-schoolmaster who educates Tom.
- **[[dr-kenn|Dr. Kenn]]** — The wise, compassionate clergyman who offers Maggie refuge.
- **[[mr-riley|Mr. Riley]]** — The auctioneer who recommends Mr. Stelling as Tom's tutor.
- **Mr. Tulliver Sr.** — The elder Mr. Tulliver, grandfather to Tom and Maggie (mentioned only).
- **[[sally]]** — The Tullivers' maid at Dorlcote Mill.

## Key Relationships

- **Maggie ↔ Tom**: The central relationship of the novel — deep childhood love strained by adult conflicts, reconciled only in death.
- **Maggie ↔ Philip Wakem**: Intellectual and emotional kinship, forbidden by family enmity.
- **Maggie ↔ Stephen Guest**: Passionate attraction versus moral duty — the novel's central romantic crisis.
- **Mr. Tulliver ↔ Lawyer Wakem**: Lawsuit and revenge, the engine of the family's downfall.
