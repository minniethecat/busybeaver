# Dunlow

A rural district on the outskirts of St. Ogg's, encompassing Dunlow Common and Dunlow Lane, and notable in the novel as the location of the gypsy encampment to which the young Maggie Tulliver attempts to run away.

## Description

Dunlow is a rustic area reached from St. Ogg's by travelling "straight away" until one reaches Dunlow Common, an expanse expected to be visible from some distance [012-001][012-002]. The district includes Dunlow Lane, a country road leading to the common land where travelling people occasionally set up camp [012-056].

## Significance

Dunlow Common is the setting for one of Maggie Tulliver's most memorable childhood episodes. Facing the disapproval of her Dodson aunts and feeling that her family would be happier without her, the young Maggie resolves to run away to the gypsies and sets out toward Dunlow Common, where she believes she will find their encampment [012-001]. The episode captures Maggie's romantic imagination, her longing for a world outside the constraints of Dodson respectability, and her childish faith that she might be welcomed as a queen by the gypsies.

The common represents a symbolic threshold between the ordered world of St. Ogg's society and the nomadic freedom Maggie craves — a boundary she crosses in imagination but, in the end, only briefly in fact.

## Key Events at This Location

- Maggie Tulliver's attempted escape to join the gypsies encamped at Dunlow Common [012-001][012-002]
- The gypsy encampment at the far end of Dunlow Lane, where Maggie arrives expecting a warm welcome [012-056]
