# Music

**Music** in *The Mill on the Floss* functions as a register of emotional intensity and social distinction. Maggie Tulliver's powerful response to music reveals the depth of feeling that her culture cannot otherwise accommodate, while musical performance in drawing-rooms marks the boundary between cultivated sensibility and provincial limitation.

## Definition

Eliot uses music as a metaphor for emotional experience that exceeds language. Maggie is repeatedly moved to transport by music—her whole body responds to it in ways that propriety cannot approve. This responsiveness is both her gift and her vulnerability: music opens her to feelings that the Dodson code would suppress. In a novel about the conflict between passion and propriety, music is passion's native language.

## Context in the Novel

Maggie's musical sensitivity is established early and recurs at crucial moments. Her response to music distinguishes her from the other women of St. Ogg's: Lucy Deane can perform competently at the piano, but Maggie feels music in her body. This difference marks the gap between conventional accomplishment and genuine artistic sensibility.

The drawing-room performances that punctuate the novel's social scenes serve a double function: they display the cultivated status of the performers while providing occasions for emotional expression that conversation forbids. When Maggie and Stephen Guest sing together, music becomes the medium through which forbidden desire communicates itself—a language that bypasses the censorship of conscious intention.

## Key Passages

- **Maggie's love of music** (005-006): Maggie's childhood responsiveness to sensory experience, including music, as a mark of her passionate nature.
- **Music as emotional transport** (Book 6, Chapter 1): Maggie and Stephen's duet as the moment when music mediates forbidden attraction.
- **Performance and class** (Book 6, Chapter 6): Lucy's accomplished playing as a marker of her social position.
- **The piano as symbol** (Book 6, Chapter 7): The instrument that gathers characters around it, enabling and disguising emotional exchange.
