# Mr. Glegg

Mr. Glegg is the retired wool-stapler husband of [[Mrs. Glegg]], a kind-hearted, white-haired old gentleman whose mild and amiable disposition stands in gentle contrast to his wife's severe and thrifty nature.

## Role in the Novel

Mr. Glegg functions as a secondary figure within the Dodson family circle, present at key family gatherings and discussions. Though his wife dominates household discourse, Mr. Glegg's quiet decency and fondness for a jest offer moments of warmth and balance amid the Dodson family's harsher judgments [008-088]. He is notably one of the relatives consulted when [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]]'s financial troubles come to light, though his influence is overshadowed by the stronger personalities around him [008-104] [008-107].

## Character Analysis

Mr. Glegg represents a gentler strain of the Dodson character. Where his wife is sharp, critical, and relentlessly economical, Mr. Glegg is described as "a kind-looking, white-haired old gentleman" who enjoys a jest and harbours a genuine, if passive, goodwill toward his relations [008-088] [008-110]. His retirement from the wool trade has left him comfortably situated — a fact his wife frequently references with some bitterness, as she believes his fortune would have been greater had he attended more diligently to "risky" investments [008-011].

Throughout the novel, Mr. Glegg speaks little but meaningfully. His interjections at the family council reveal a man of good feeling who is willing to help his nephew by marriage but knows the limits of what his wife will permit [026-053] [026-095]. His character arc is static — he remains a kind, slightly henpecked figure — but his presence provides a moral counterweight to the Dodson family's otherwise relentless pragmatism.

## Relationships

- **[[Mrs. Glegg]]** — His wife, née Jane Dodson, whose formidable personality dominates the household. Mr. Glegg defers to her in most matters but retains his own quiet judgment [008-011].
- **[[Mr. Tulliver]]** — His nephew by marriage; Mr. Glegg shows genuine concern for [[mr-tulliver|Tulliver]]'s misfortunes and is willing to extend help [008-110] [026-095].
- **[[Tom Tulliver]]** — His great-nephew; Mr. Glegg participates in the family discussion about [[tom-tulliver|Tom]]'s education [007-001].
- **[[Maggie Tulliver]]** — His great-niece; he witnesses her childhood outbursts at the Dodson family gathering [008-088].

## Key Scenes

- **The Dodson [[family|Family]] Council** — Mr. Glegg attends the gathering at which [[mrs-glegg|Mrs. Glegg]], [[mrs-pullet|Mrs. Pullet]], and [[mrs-tulliver|Mrs. Tulliver]] discuss [[tom-tulliver|Tom]]'s future. He makes a playful remark intended to lighten the mood [008-088] [008-110].
- **The [[mr-tulliver|Tulliver]] Bankruptcy Discussion** — Mr. Glegg is the first to speak when the family discusses how to assist the ruined [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]], and later shakes his head involuntarily at the gravity of the situation [026-053] [026-095] [027-022].
