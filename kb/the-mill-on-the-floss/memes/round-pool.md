# The Round Pool

A deep, mysterious pool in the river near Dorlcote Mill, renowned in the Tulliver children's imagination as a place of excellent fishing and associated with the legend of a great flood that carved it out.

## Description

The Round Pool is a "wonderful pool" situated downstream from Dorlcote Mill, reached by walking along the riverbank [006-074]. It is distinguished by its depth and its circular shape, which local legend attributes to the erosive force of a great flood in the past [007-055].

## Significance

The Round Pool is the setting for a key childhood episode in which Tom Tulliver invites his sister Maggie to accompany him fishing [006-013]. Tom's invitation is a rare moment of sibling inclusion, and Maggie's eager acceptance reflects her deep desire for her brother's approval and companionship. The episode encapsulates the rhythms of the Tulliver children's outdoor life at the Mill before the family's financial ruin.

The pool's origin story — formed by a flood — also serves as a subtle piece of foreshadowing. The flood that shaped the Round Pool is a memory of a past catastrophe; the flood that ends the novel will sweep over the same landscape, uniting the childhood fishing spot with the novel's climactic tragedy.

## Key Events at This Location
