# Mrs. Glegg

Mrs. Glegg is the eldest of the Dodson sisters and the most forceful presence in the family. With her sharp tongue, her absolute certainty about how things ought to be done, and her unexpected capacity for generosity, she is one of Eliot's greatest comic creations—and one of her most psychologically acute portraits of provincial matriarchy.

## Role in the Novel

Mrs. Glegg functions as the voice of Dodson orthodoxy. Her pronouncements on family duty, proper conduct, and the correct way to manage money provide both comic relief and serious social commentary. She is the custodian of custom, and her judgments carry the weight of [[custom|tradition]].

## Character Analysis

Mrs. Glegg is a paradox: rigid and generous, harsh and loyal. She berates [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]] for his impracticality and then, when the family is ruined, contributes financially to their support. Her code is inflexible, but it includes real [[duty|obligation]]s. Eliot does not simplify her into either villain or saint; she is both comic and admirable, a woman whose limitations are also her strengths. Her insistence that "it used to be the way in our family" (008-004) is both suffocating and stabilizing.

## Relationships

- **[[mrs-tulliver|Mrs. Tulliver]]**: Mrs. Glegg's sister, whose impractical marriage to [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]] is a constant source of exasperation.
- **[[mrs-pullet|Mrs. Pullet]] and [[mrs-deane|Mrs. Deane]]**: The other Dodson sisters, with whom Mrs. Glegg maintains a complex dynamic of solidarity and competition.
- **[[maggie-tulliver|Maggie Tulliver]]**: Mrs. Glegg disapproves of [[maggie-tulliver|Maggie]]'s un[[custom|convention]]ality but ultimately offers her refuge when the community condemns her.
- **[[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]]**: Mrs. Glegg's brother-in-[[justice|law]], whose financial imprudence she despises.

## Key Scenes

- **The family party** (007-001): Mrs. Glegg holds court at the Dodson gathering, policing family standards.
- **Mrs. Glegg on family duty** (003-005): Her pronouncement that speaking disrespectfully of the family is the ultimate transgression.
- **Unexpected generosity**: Mrs. Glegg contributes to the [[mr-tulliver|Tulliver]]s' support after the [[justice|bankruptcy]].
