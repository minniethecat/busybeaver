# Mr. Askern

Mr. Askern is the surgeon who attends Tom Tulliver after the accident that breaks his foot during his first quarter at King's Lorton. His presence in the narrative, though brief, enables one of the novel's most important early relationships: it is during Tom's incapacitation under Mr. Askern's care that Philip Wakem, the hunchbacked son of Lawyer Wakem, shows Tom a kindness that Maggie will never forget.

## Role in the Novel

Mr. Askern appears only in reported speech, his name invoked by the anxious Maggie and the recuperating Tom. When Maggie first hears of Tom's injury, her immediate question is: "does Mr Askern say Tulliver will be lame?" — the fear of permanent damage revealing her intense attachment to her brother [021-002]. Philip Wakem, who has become Tom's regular visitor during the convalescence, reassures Tom: "Mr Askern says you'll soon be all right again, Tulliver, did you know?" [021-009]. The phrase is repeated, a refrain of medical authority that makes the sickroom bearable. Later, when Maggie visits and must leave, she tells Tom she will go "because I think Mr Askern's done with Tom's foot" — the surgeon's completion of his work marking the boundary between illness and recovery [021-036].

## Character Analysis

Mr. Askern is the kind of functional minor character at which Eliot excels — a name that carries a world of implication. As a surgeon (rather than a mere apothecary), he represents a certain level of professional competence available in the provincial world, and his optimistic prognosis — that Tom will "walk as well as ever again by-and-day" — provides the reassurance that allows the convalescent friendship between Tom and Philip to flourish. Without Mr. Askern's authority, Philip's reassurances would carry no weight; without the period of enforced stillness under the surgeon's care, Tom might never have received Philip's kindness at all. Mr. Askern thus enables, from the narrative's periphery, the first fragile bond between the Tullivers and the Wakems.

## Key Scenes

- **Maggie's anxious inquiry** [021-002]: Maggie, hearing of Tom's injury, asks whether Mr. Askern believes Tom will be lame — her first thought upon reaching King's Lorton.
- **Philip's reassurance** [021-009]: Philip visits Tom's bedside and delivers Mr. Askern's optimistic prognosis, embodying the kindness that will earn him Maggie's lasting gratitude.
- **The surgeon departs** [021-036]: Maggie tells Tom she will leave because Mr. Askern has finished with Tom's foot — the surgeon's exit marking the end of the acute phase of Tom's illness and the beginning of the narrative's next movement.
