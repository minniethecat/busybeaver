# Dunlow Common

The expanse of common land outside St. Ogg's where travelling gypsies occasionally set up camp, and the destination to which the young Maggie Tulliver flees in a memorable childhood episode of rebellion and romantic imagination.

## Description

Dunlow Common is a rural open space reached by travelling "straight away" from St. Ogg's, visible from a distance as one approaches [012-001]. The common is connected to the town by Dunlow Lane, at the far end of which gypsy encampments were known to be pitched [012-056].

## Significance

For Maggie Tulliver, Dunlow Common represents escape. Smarting from the criticism of her Dodson aunts and convinced her family would be happier without her, the nine-year-old Maggie resolves to run away to the gypsies she imagines encamped on the common. She sets out expecting to be welcomed as their queen, her head full of romantic notions drawn from books [012-001][012-002].

The episode is among the most vivid renderings of Maggie's childhood psychology — her impulsiveness, her hunger for recognition, and her naive belief in alternative worlds where she might belong. Dunlow Common is less a physical place than a threshold of the imagination, the point where Maggie's fantasy of freedom meets the reality of her dependence and young age.
