# Mr. Tulliver

Jeremy Tulliver, the miller of [[dorlcote-mill|Dorlcote Mill]], is the patriarch whose proud, impulsive nature initiates the novel's central conflict. His [[the-lawsuit|lawsuit]] against [[lawyer-wakem|Lawyer Wakem]] over water rights—a decision driven more by pride than by prudence—results in [[justice|bankruptcy]] and a vow of [[justice|revenge]] that binds his son [[tom-tulliver|Tom]] to a narrowing sense of duty.

## Role in the Novel

Mr. Tulliver's role is foundational: his character and decisions create the circumstances that shape [[maggie-tulliver|Maggie]] and [[tom-tulliver|Tom]]'s lives. His defeat in court transforms the family's material circumstances overnight, but it is the moral wound—his demand that [[tom-tulliver|Tom]] "make him and his feel" the consequences of [[lawyer-wakem|Wakem]]'s victory—that has the deepest and longest-lasting effect.

## Character Analysis

Mr. Tulliver is a man of strong feelings and weak judgment. He loves his [[childhood|children]] genuinely, especially [[maggie-tulliver|Maggie]], whose cleverness he recognizes and values in ways that others do not. But his pride—"that niver run i' my family, thank God!" (003-019)—blinds him to the risk of his [[the-lawsuit|lawsuit]]. He is incapable of backing down, even when prudence demands it.

His fall is both economic and psychological. After losing the mill, he becomes a broken man, his proud independence reduced to dependency on his son. The vow of [[justice|revenge]] he inscribes in the family Bible transforms personal grievance into inherited [[duty|obligation]]. In his final moments, he attacks [[lawyer-wakem|Lawyer Wakem]] physically, then dies—a violent, pathetic end to a life defined by the inability to master his own impulses.

## Relationships

- **[[maggie-tulliver|Maggie Tulliver]]**: Mr. Tulliver's favorite child, whose intelligence and spirit he appreciates in ways that [[mrs-tulliver|Mrs. Tulliver]] does not.
- **[[tom-tulliver|Tom Tulliver]]**: The son to whom Mr. Tulliver entrusts the family's [[justice|revenge]] and restoration.
- **[[mrs-tulliver|Mrs. Tulliver]] ([[mrs-tulliver|Bessy]])**: Mr. Tulliver's wife, a Dodson by birth whose family pride he both depends on and resents.
- **[[lawyer-wakem|Lawyer Wakem]]**: Mr. Tulliver's enemy, against whom he wages a self-destructive legal and personal war.
