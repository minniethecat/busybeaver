# Mr. Turnbull

**Mr. Turnbull** is a medical man in [[st-oggs|St Ogg's]] who attends [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]] during his illness and eventual death. Though a minor character, he plays a crucial role as the bearer of medical truth to the [[mr-tulliver|Tulliver]] family.

## Medical Role

Mr. Turnbull is first mentioned in connection with an early childhood illness of [[tom-tulliver|Tom Tulliver]]:

> "we paid Dr Turnbull for attending him" (003-007)

He later becomes the principal medical attendant for [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]] after the latter's financial and physical collapse.

## Mr. Tulliver's Final Illness

When [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]] falls seriously ill after his confrontation with [[lawyer-wakem|Wakem]], Mr. Turnbull is called in. His role is significant because he must deliver difficult news to the family:

> "The very quietness with which Mr Turnbull spoke had frightened her suspense into a keener edge" (024-015)

He is depicted as a serious, professional man whose measured manner communicates the gravity of the situation:

> "Mr Turnbull came now to meet her; a middle-aged man in professional costume" (024-013)

### Prognosis

Mr. Turnbull provides cautious but honest assessments of [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]]'s condition:

| Assessment | PN |
|------------|-----|
| Initial diagnosis of inflammation | 003-007 |
| Says there is "no immediate danger" at one point | 024-018 |
| Later delivers grave prognosis | 024-009 |

He does not offer false hope, stating plainly what the family must prepare for:

> "Mr Turnbull thinks he'll get better" — reported by Mrs. Tulliver, but this optimism proves temporary (026-073)

## His Function in the Narrative

Mr. Turnbull serves a narrative function beyond his medical duties. As a professional outsider to the [[mr-tulliver|Tulliver]]-Dodson family drama, he provides an objective, clinical perspective on [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]]'s decline. His presence marks the transition from the legal and financial crisis (the [[the-lawsuit|lawsuit]], the [[justice|bankruptcy]]) to the physical and mortal consequences of that crisis.

His professional detachment contrasts with the emotional intensity of the family:

1. **[[mrs-tulliver|Mrs. Tulliver]]** reacts with bewildered grief
2. **[[tom-tulliver|Tom]]** responds with grim determination to restore the family fortunes
3. **[[maggie-tulliver|Maggie]]** is consumed by guilt and emotional turmoil
4. **Mr. Turnbull** simply states the medical facts

## Significance

As a minor character, Mr. Turnbull represents the world of professional expertise that exists outside the insular concerns of the [[mr-tulliver|Tulliver]] and Dodson families. His presence reminds the reader that while the families are consumed by questions of pride, property, and social standing, the physical realities of illness and death operate by their own [[justice|law]]s, indifferent to social distinctions.
