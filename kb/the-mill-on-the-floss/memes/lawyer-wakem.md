# Lawyer Wakem

Lawyer Wakem is the [[mr-tulliver|Tulliver]] family's nemesis: the attorney whose victory in court costs [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]] his mill and his sanity. Yet Eliot refuses to make him a simple villain. Wakem is also a devoted father to his disabled son [[philip-wakem|Philip]], and his complexity is one of the novel's most effective subversions of reader expectation.

## Role in the Novel

> Mr. Wakem was not a man to be trusted with litigation; he was known to be a hard man, and a man who would always have his own way. [018-016]

Wakem serves as both plot catalyst and thematic counterpoint. His legal victory over [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]] sets the [[mr-tulliver|Tulliver]] family's downfall in motion. But his love for [[philip-wakem|Philip]] complicates the novel's moral scheme: the man [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]] hates is also the father of the man [[maggie-tulliver|Maggie]] loves.

## Character Analysis

Wakem is pragmatic, intelligent, and not without feeling. He genuinely loves [[philip-wakem|Philip]] and indulges his son's artistic ambitions—a striking contrast to [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]]'s more [[custom|convention]]al paternal expectations. His professional conduct is legally correct if morally indifferent; he is a sharp practitioner rather than a villain. The novel's final, surprising revelation of Wakem's generosity after [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]]'s death underscores Eliot's resistance to simple moral categorization.

## Relationships

- **[[philip-wakem|Philip Wakem]]**: Wakem's disabled son, whom he loves deeply and supports in his artistic pursuits.
- **[[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]]**: Wakem's legal opponent and sworn enemy, whose hatred of Wakem becomes pathological.
- **[[maggie-tulliver|Maggie Tulliver]]**: Wakem treats [[maggie-tulliver|Maggie]] with unexpected gentleness after [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]] attacks him.

## Key Scenes

- **The [[the-lawsuit|lawsuit]]** (Book 1): Wakem's legal victory that ruins the [[mr-tulliver|Tulliver]]s.
- **[[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]]'s attack** (Book 3, Chapter 9): The dying [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]] attacks Wakem physically, and Wakem's response reveals unexpected decency.
- **Wakem and [[philip-wakem|Philip]]**: Scenes between father and son that humanize the "villain."
