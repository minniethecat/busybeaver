# Mr. Pivart

Mr. Pivart is a [[property|wealth]]y [[property|land]]owner whose irrigation scheme on [[property|land]]s upstream of [[dorlcote-mill|Dorlcote Mill]] sets off the legal dispute that ultimately ruins [[Mr. Tulliver]]. Though he never appears directly in the novel's scenes, his actions drive the central plot crisis of the first two books.

## Role in the Novel

Mr. Pivart serves as the unseen catalyst of the [[mr-tulliver|Tulliver]] family's downfall. By diverting water from [[the-floss|the Floss]] for his irrigation works upstream, he provokes [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]] into filing a [[the-lawsuit|lawsuit]] — a suit that [[mr-tulliver|Tulliver]] loses, saddling him with ruinous costs and forcing [[the-auction|the sale]] of [[dorlcote-mill|Dorlcote Mill]] [017-005] [024-001]. Pivart's alliance with [[Lawyer Wakem]] — who acts as his agent — sharpens the novel's critique of a legal system that favours the [[property|wealth]]y and well-connected over small proprietors like [[mr-tulliver|Tulliver]].

## Character Analysis

Eliot keeps Pivart entirely offstage, a narrative choice that heightens his symbolic function. He is "a new name hereabout" — an outsider whose [[property|wealth]] and connections allow him to reshape the local [[nature|landscape]] to his advantage, indifferent to the consequences borne by families like the [[mr-tulliver|Tulliver]]s [017-006]. The local community's reaction is telling: "nobody ever heard of a Pivart meddling with the [[nature|river]]" before, and the supposition is that his money will prevail [017-007] [017-015].

Pivart's partnership with [[lawyer-wakem|Wakem]] links the novel's two primary antagonists: the [[property|wealth]]y outsider who has no attachment to the place he disrupts, and the sharp local [[justice|law]]yer who profits from that disruption. Together they represent the impersonal forces — economic, legal, social — against which [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]]'s stubborn independence is powerless.

## Relationships

- **[[Lawyer Wakem]]** — Pivart employs [[lawyer-wakem|Wakem]] as his legal agent, making the [[justice|law]]yer the visible face of Pivart's interests in [[st-oggs-town|St. Ogg's]] [022-002].
- **[[Mr. Tulliver]]** — Pivart's decision to divert the [[nature|river]] water leads to [[mr-tulliver|Tulliver]]'s [[the-lawsuit|lawsuit]] and, ultimately, his [[justice|bankruptcy]] [017-005] [024-001].
- **[[Dorlcote Mill]]** — Pivart's irrigation works threaten the water supply that powers the mill, the economic foundation of the [[mr-tulliver|Tulliver]] family [017-005] [017-020].

## Key Scenes

- **The Water Rights Dispute** — [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]] explains to his family that Pivart's upstream irrigation is stealing water from [[the-floss|the Floss]], and that he has been "obliged" to bring a [[the-lawsuit|lawsuit]]. The community at the Ripple fair discusses Pivart's [[property|wealth]] and the likely outcome [017-005] through [017-021].
- **The Verdict** — News arrives that the [[the-lawsuit|lawsuit]] has been decided against [[mr-tulliver|Tulliver]], and that "Pivart and [[lawyer-wakem|Wakem]] were triumphant." This moment marks the irreversible turn in the [[mr-tulliver|Tulliver]] family's fortunes [024-001].
