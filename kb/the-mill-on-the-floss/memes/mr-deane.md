# Mr. Deane

Mr. Deane is the manager at Guest & Co, the husband of [[Mrs. Deane]], and the father of [[Lucy Deane]]. He represents the successful Dodson archetype: a prudent businessman whose close application to commercial affairs has yielded prosperity and social standing in [[st-oggs-town|St. Ogg's]].

## Role in the Novel

Mr. Deane appears primarily at Dodson family gatherings and in scenes concerning [[tom-tulliver|Tom Tulliver]]'s career prospects. As the most commercially successful member of the Dodson clan, he commands a certain respect at family councils [008-049] [008-103]. His role is most significant when the question of [[tom-tulliver|Tom]]'s future arises: Mr. Deane provides the practical connection that eventually leads to [[tom-tulliver|Tom]]'s placement at Guest & Co [028-051]. He functions as a model of Dodson values — diligence, prudence, and steady accumulation — against which the more impulsive [[mr-tulliver|Tulliver]]s are implicitly measured.

## Character Analysis

Mr. Deane embodies the Dodson ideal of worldly success achieved through steady application. He takes snuff from an elegant box — a gift from a superior, as he likes to remind listeners — a detail that signals both his commercial achievements and his pride in them [008-052] [008-053]. His conversation is marked by judicious reserve rather than warmth; he speaks sparingly but with weight, laughing with "much enjoyment" at [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]]'s indiscretions rather than rebuking them openly [008-090] [008-129].

Unlike the more domineering Dodson women, Mr. Deane exercises influence through quiet authority. When [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]] announces his plan to send [[tom-tulliver|Tom]] to a clergyman's tutor, Mr. Deane's response — a vigorous pinch of snuff and a measured observation — conveys skepticism without confrontation [008-109]. His prudence makes him the natural person for [[tom-tulliver|Tom]] to approach when seeking a career path, and though his response is pragmatic rather than warm, it proves decisive [028-051] [028-061].

## Relationships

- **[[Mrs. Deane]]** — His wife, née Susan Dodson, a thin-lipped woman who shares his prudent nature [026-006].
- **[[Lucy Deane]]** — His daughter, whose beauty and sweetness he regards with quiet pride [007-006].
- **[[Mr. Tulliver]]** — His brother-in-[[justice|law]]; Mr. Deane is amused by [[mr-tulliver|Tulliver]]'s impractical outbursts but refrains from open criticism [008-090].
- **[[Tom Tulliver]]** — His nephew; [[tom-tulliver|Tom]] seeks Mr. Deane's guidance for a career, and Mr. Deane provides the connection to Guest & Co [028-051] [028-061].
- **[[Mrs. Glegg]]** — His sister-in-[[justice|law]]; [[mrs-glegg|Mrs. Glegg]] approves of Mr. Deane's commercial sense [008-004].

## Key Scenes

- **The Dodson [[family|Family]] Council** — Mr. Deane attends the family gathering where [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]] announces his plans for [[tom-tulliver|Tom]]'s education. He responds with characteristic prudence, taking snuff and offering measured observations [008-104] [008-109] [008-129].
- **[[tom-tulliver|Tom]]'s Career Enquiry** — When [[tom-tulliver|Tom]] visits his Uncle Deane to ask about a business career at Guest & Co, Mr. Deane assesses the young man practically and provides the necessary introduction [028-051] [028-061].
