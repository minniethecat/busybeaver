# Mr. Hyndmarsh

**Mr. Hyndmarsh** is a grocer in [[st-oggs-town|St Ogg's]], operating his shop on Danish Street near the offices of [[lawyer-wakem|Mr. Wakem]].

## Role in the Novel

Mr. Hyndmarsh appears indirectly through [[mrs-tulliver|Mrs. Tulliver's]] scheme to avert the sale of [[dorlcote-mill|Dorlcote Mill]]. Facing the impending auction, Mrs. Tulliver resolves to speak to Wakem personally, hoping her Dodson respectability might sway him. She uses the pretext of selling her pickles and ketchup to Mr. Hyndmarsh as cover for her real errand: "Mrs. Tulliver possessed, and which Mr. Hyndmarsh, the grocer, would certainly purchase if she could transact the business in a personal interview" [030-009].

The stratagem reveals Mrs. Tulliver's limited but genuine resourcefulness in crisis. She walks with Tom toward St Ogg's, turning toward Danish Street "where Mr. Hyndmarsh retailed his grocery, not far from the offices of Mr. Wakem" [030-009]. The grocer thus serves as both alibi and geographical marker in Mrs. Tulliver's desperate plan to protect her family.
