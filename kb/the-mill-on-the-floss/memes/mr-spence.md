# Mr. Spence

**Mr. Spence** is the managing clerk at Guest & Co, the shipping and trading firm in [[st-oggs-town|St Ogg's]] where [[mr-deane|Mr. Deane]] is a partner.

## Role in the Novel

Mr. Spence appears in the scene where [[tom-tulliver|Tom Tulliver]] visits his uncle Deane to ask about a position after the family's bankruptcy. He is described as one of the "sleek, prosperous men of business" whose "quiet, monotonous procedure" fills Tom with unease [028-015]. When Mr. Deane dismisses Spence to Torry's on an errand, the clock, which had seemed oppressively loud during the clerk's absorbed presence, "suddenly became less loud and deliberate in Tom's ears" [028-016].

When Spence returns later in the same scene, his presence constrains Tom, who "had not courage to ask another question, especially in the presence of Mr. Spence" [028-058]. This detail underscores Tom's sense of being an outsider in the world of commerce — even a clerk represents an intimidating competence that Tom, fresh from his failed classical education, cannot match.
