# Bob Jakin

Bob Jakin is one of the novel's most warmly drawn characters: [[tom-tulliver|Tom]]'s childhood friend, a former packman who becomes [[tom-tulliver|Tom]]'s business partner and a steadfast source of practical kindness. His cheerful resilience and natural generosity offer a counterexample to the rigid, rule-bound masculinity [[tom-tulliver|Tom]] embodies.

## Role in the Novel

Bob appears at crucial moments throughout the narrative, always offering practical help without moral judgment. He is the novel's embodiment of natural goodness—kind without self-consciousness, loyal without rigidity. His role in [[the-flood|the final flood]] sequence, where he rows with [[maggie-tulliver|Maggie]] to rescue [[tom-tulliver|Tom]], makes him an agent of the novel's resolution.

## Character Analysis

Bob is one of Eliot's finest portraits of working-class decency. Unlike the Dodson code of [[custom|propriety]], Bob's morality is instinctive rather than doctrinal. He helps because help is needed, not because duty requires it. His relationship with [[tom-tulliver|Tom]] is particularly revealing: where [[tom-tulliver|Tom]]'s understanding of loyalty is punitive and absolute, Bob's is flexible and forgiving. His cheerful freckled face appears at moments of crisis as a reminder that goodness need not be solemn.

## Relationships

- **[[tom-tulliver|Tom Tulliver]]**: Bob's childhood friend and later business partner, whose narrow rectitude Bob's warmth implicitly critiques.
- **[[maggie-tulliver|Maggie Tulliver]]**: Bob treats [[maggie-tulliver|Maggie]] with uncomplicated kindness, offering her practical help when the community condemns her.
- **[[mrs-glegg|Mrs. Glegg]]**: Bob's unlikely ally, whose respect he earns through his practical competence.

## Key Scenes

- **[[childhood|Childhood]] friendship** (Book 1): Bob's early appearance establishes him as [[tom-tulliver|Tom]]'s loyal but independent companion.
- **Return as partner**: Bob reappears as a successful packman and becomes [[tom-tulliver|Tom]]'s business partner.
- **The flood rescue** (Book 7, Chapter 5): Bob rows [[maggie-tulliver|Maggie]] into the flood to find [[tom-tulliver|Tom]].
