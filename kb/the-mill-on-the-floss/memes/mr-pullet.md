# Mr. Pullet

Mr. Pullet is a gentleman farmer, the husband of [[Mrs. Pullet]], and a member of the Dodson family by marriage. A kindly but perpetually confused man, he plays a minor role at family gatherings, where his good nature is more evident than his grasp of practical affairs.

## Role in the Novel

Mr. Pullet appears at the Dodson family council in Book 1 Chapter 8, where [[mrs-tulliver|Mrs. Tulliver]] consults her sisters about [[tom-tulliver|Tom]]'s education and [[mr-tulliver|Mr. Tulliver]]'s plans. His role is that of a background presence: he speaks little, and when he does, his remarks tend toward the obvious or the confused, offering comic relief amid the more earnest deliberations of the Dodson women [008-028] [008-032] [008-105].

## Character Analysis

Mr. Pullet is among the gentlest of the Dodson in-[[justice|law]]s, with a nature that inclines toward conciliation rather than criticism. Eliot describes him as having "the most confused idea of what was going on" in any business conversation, a trait that renders him harmless but also ineffectual [008-105]. His one clear instinct is to relieve tension: he tells his wife during the family conference that [[maggie-tulliver|Maggie]] and [[tom-tulliver|Tom]] are "so many of 'em" — not a helpful observation, but one intended to calm things down [008-118].

His relationship with his wife is marked by deference. [[mrs-pullet|Mrs. Pullet]]'s preoccupations — her health complaints, her mourning for departed acquaintances, her concern with funerals and wills — dominate the household, and Mr. Pullet accommodates them with a vague good will, occasionally venturing a remark that his wife ignores or corrects [008-028] [008-031] [008-032]. In this sense, he mirrors the dynamic between [[mr-glegg|Mr. Glegg]] and [[mrs-glegg|Mrs. Glegg]], though Mr. Pullet is still more ineffectual.

## Relationships

- **[[Mrs. Pullet]]** — His wife, whose hypochondria and preoccupation with funerals dominate their domestic life. Mr. Pullet bears her complaints with patient good nature [008-028] [008-031].
- **[[Mrs. Glegg]]** — His sister-in-[[justice|law]]; Mr. Pullet shares a neighborly rapport with [[mr-glegg|Mr. Glegg]], their shared position as Dodson in-[[justice|law]]s creating a tacit bond [008-124].
- **[[Tom Tulliver]]** and **[[Maggie Tulliver]]** — His great-nephew and great-niece; he regards them with avuncular kindness but little agency [008-091] [008-118].
- **[[Mr. Tulliver]]** — His brother-in-[[justice|law]]; Mr. Pullet can "hardly have been more completely at a loss" than during [[mr-tulliver|Tulliver]]'s business talk [008-105].

## Key Scenes

- **The Dodson [[family|Family]] Council** — Mr. Pullet attends the gathering at [[mrs-tulliver|Mrs. Tulliver]]'s house, where he contributes little to the discussion but much to the atmosphere of amiable confusion. His remark that [[maggie-tulliver|Maggie]] looks "very funny" after her hair-cutting incident and his attempt to steer conversation toward less contentious topics reveal his gentle, if ineffectual, character [008-028] [008-091] [008-118].
