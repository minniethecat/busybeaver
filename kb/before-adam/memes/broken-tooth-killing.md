## Overview

The Killing of Broken-Tooth is one of the most traumatic events in [[big-tooth|the narrator]]'s ancestral memory — the death of a child, witnessed from a tree branch, that introduces the lethal asymmetry between the Folk and the [[fire-people|Fire People]]. A [[fire-man|Fire-Man]] scout, armed with a [[bow-and-arrow|bow and arrow]], encountered three young Folk — Big-Tooth, [[lop-ear|Lop-Ear]], and [[broken-tooth|Broken-Tooth]] — hiding in a tree. The encounter ended with Broken-Tooth dead, an arrow through his chest, and Big-Tooth wounded in the leg.

The killing recurs throughout Big-Tooth's dream-memories and is the source of his childhood nightmares — the primal scene of a conflict that would culminate, years later, in the extermination of nearly the entire horde.

## The Encounter

Big-Tooth's first sight of a [[fire-man|Fire-Man]] came with the crackle of a dry twig. Looking down from his perch in the tree, he saw the scout creeping along the ground, peering upward. "At first I thought he was a wild animal, because he wore around his waist and over his shoulders a ragged piece of bearskin" (006-031).

The scout carried a [[bow-and-arrow|bow]] — a weapon unlike anything the Folk possessed. [[lop-ear|Lop-Ear]], who had previous experience with the Fire People, understood the danger immediately and kept the tree trunk between himself and the scout. Big-Tooth, innocent of the threat, viewed the exchange as a game.

## The Death

The scout's first arrow missed Lop-Ear, glancing off a limb. He then turned his attention to [[broken-tooth|Broken-Tooth]], who was "teetering slightly and involuntarily on the end of the branch." The first arrow struck upward into the foliage; the second found its mark.

> The bow-string twanged, the arrow leaped upward, and Broken-Tooth, uttering a terrible scream, fell off the branch. I saw him as he went down, turning over and over, all arms and legs it seemed, the shaft of the arrow projecting from his chest and appearing and disappearing with each revolution of his body. (006-042)

The Fire-Man ran forward with a stone and hammered Broken-Tooth on the head — finishing the child off (006-048). It was not enough to kill; the Fire-Man ensured the kill was final.

## The Aftermath

During the flight that followed, Big-Tooth was struck by one of the Fire-Man's arrows — the shaft embedded in his right leg, "protruding head and shaft from either side," causing burning pain and hampering his movement through the trees. When he could no longer keep up, [[lop-ear|Lop-Ear]] returned to help him remove the arrow — a moment of loyalty that cemented their friendship (006-069).

The killing transformed the Fire-Man from a curiosity into a mortal enemy in Big-Tooth's mental map. The arrow wound he received during the flight became a physical reminder of that transformation: the scar on his leg was also a scar on his understanding of the world.
