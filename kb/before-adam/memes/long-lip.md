# Long-Lip

**Long-Lip** is a young member of the [[fire-people|Folk]], the son of [[hairless-one|the Hairless One]] and the grandson of [[marrow-bone|Marrow-Bone]]. He is notable as the first designated water-carrier among the horde, a role that marked the emergence of specialized labor within the Folk's social structure.

## The First Water-Carrier

Long-Lip's water-carrying role emerged through a chain of delegation. When [[marrow-bone|Marrow-Bone]] — too old to fetch water himself — had sick spells, the Hairless One took over the task. Later, the Hairless One deputed the task to Long-Lip:

> The Hairless One deputed the task to Long-Lip, his son. And after that, even when Marrow-Bone was well again, Long-Lip continued carrying water for him.[^1]

This arrangement persisted and became institutionalized: "except on unusual occasions, the men never carried any water at all, leaving the task to the women and larger children."[^1] Long-Lip's service to his grandfather thus represents the earliest documented instance of a specialized caregiving role within the horde.

## The Fire People Ambush

Long-Lip was present at the morning meal when the [[fire-people|Fire People]] launched a surprise attack. He sat beside his father, the Hairless One, and his grandfather, Marrow-Bone. When arrows struck down the Hairless One and [[big-tooth|Big-Tooth]]'s sister, Long-Lip survived the initial volley.[^2]

During the retreat, Long-Lip waited for his elderly grandfather:

> Now that there was no immediate danger, Long-Lip waited for his grand-father, Marrow-Bone; and with the gap of a generation between them, the old fellow and the youth brought up our rear.[^3]

## Death

Long-Lip died during the Fire People's final assault on the caves. He was shot by an arrow while climbing the cliff face:

> I remember Long-Lip. He got as far as my ledge, crying piteously, an arrow clear through his chest, the feathered shaft sticking out behind, the bone head sticking out before, shot through the back as he climbed. He sank down on my ledge bleeding profusely at the mouth.[^4]

His death was one of many during the massacre, as the Folk were driven from their caves by fire and smoke.

## Family Line

Long-Lip is part of a three-generation lineage:
- Grandfather: [[marrow-bone|Marrow-Bone]], the oldest member of the Folk
- Father: [[hairless-one|the Hairless One]], cave owner
- Sister: [[singing-one|the Singing One]], claimed by [[red-eye|Red-Eye]]
