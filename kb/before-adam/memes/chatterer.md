# Chatterer

The **Chatterer** is a member of the [[fire-people|Folk]] and the stepfather of [[big-tooth|Big-Tooth]], the narrator of *Before Adam*. He is characterized by his persistent cruelty toward the young Big-Tooth and his fundamental cowardice. Big-Tooth gave him the name "Chatterer" as the most fitting description for "that precious stepfather of mine," reflecting both his constant verbal noise-making and his malicious nature.[^1]

[^1]: PN=004-076 — Big-Tooth explains his naming convention and calls the Chatterer "that precious stepfather of mine."

## Role in the Narrative

The Chatterer serves as the primary antagonist of Big-Tooth's early childhood, making "home-life a burden" for both the narrator and his mother through constant persecution.[^2] His mistreatment of Big-Tooth includes pinching, cuffing, and biting — behavior that forces the young narrator to flee the nest and begin his independent life in the forest.

> He persistently terrorized me. He was always pinching me and cuffing me, and on occasion he was not above biting me. Often my mother interfered, and the way she made his fur fly was a joy to see.[^3]

The Chatterer's most significant act is deliberately abandoning Big-Tooth from the family nest. Seizing an opportunity when Big-Tooth's mother was away at the [[blueberry-swamp|blueberry swamp]], the Chatterer returned alone, "roaring with self-induced rage," and chased the young boy out of the tree. Big-Tooth fell from the branches but survived, and this event marked the end of his childhood and the beginning of his independent existence.[^4]

Despite his viciousness, the Chatterer is consistently portrayed as a coward. When Big-Tooth climbed out onto thin twigs and leaves, the Chatterer refused to follow — "greater always than any anger he ever worked up was his caution."[^5] He celebrated Big-Tooth's fall with "a demoniacal chant of joy," teetering in satisfaction from the nest.[^6]

## Relationships

The Chatterer's relationship with Big-Tooth is defined by persecution. He is the narrator's stepfather and a source of constant torment during early childhood. Big-Tooth's mother frequently intervened to protect her son, engaging in physical fights with the Chatterer — encounters from which "his fur fly was a joy to see."[^3]

Later in the narrative, the Chatterer appears more peripherally. Big-Tooth catches glimpses of him near the caves and takes pleasure in "making faces at him and angering him from the mouth of my cave."[^7] The Chatterer also fathers a daughter — Big-Tooth's half-sister — who eventually mates with [[lop-ear|Lop-Ear]], and she is noted to have inherited "all the viciousness of the Chatterer."[^8]

## Traits and Characteristics

The Chatterer's defining traits are his malice and his cowardice. Big-Tooth describes him as:

> The Chatterer was ever a coward, and greater always than any anger he ever worked up was his caution.[^5]

He is persistently noisy, hence his name, and prone to theatrical displays of rage and joy — chanting, teetering, and roaring in what Big-Tooth characterizes as self-induced performances. His cruelty is spiteful rather than predatory; he takes pleasure in tormenting a child but backs down when confronted by an adult.

## Key Appearances

- **Chapter IV**: The Chatterer is introduced as Big-Tooth's stepfather and childhood tormentor. His abuse and the nest-abandonment incident are narrated in detail.
- **Chapter V**: Residing in the "suburban" area of the horde's territory with Big-Tooth's mother.
- **Chapter X**: The Chatterer briefly reappears when Big-Tooth's mother, now mated to him, reacts with inherited viciousness to [[lop-ear|Lop-Ear]].
- **Chapter XI**: The Chatterer has "come to tolerate" Lop-Ear in the group.
- **Chapter XV**: Big-Tooth notes that the Chatterer is the father of his half-sister, who mates with Lop-Ear.
- **Chapter XVII**: Big-Tooth briefly glimpses the Chatterer during the Fire People crisis.
