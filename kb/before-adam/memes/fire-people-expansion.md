## Overview

The Fire People Expansion was the population-driven territorial pressure that ultimately destroyed [[the-horde|the Folk]]. It was not a single dramatic event but a slow demographic phenomenon: the [[fire-people|Fire People]] outgrew their peninsula stronghold and pressed outward into the lands of their neighbors, carrying with them technological advantages that made resistance impossible.

The narrator recognized this expansion in hindsight as the force that ended his people's existence in the caves: "the migration of the Fire People, and the consequent migration of the survivors of the Folk, set back our evolution for centuries" (011-133-011-135). In evolutionary terms, it was an early instance of competitive exclusion — one hominin population displacing another through a combination of demographic pressure and technological superiority.

## The Peninsula Stronghold

The Fire People's original territory was a peninsula protected on three sides by the curving river — "a splendid strategic selection" that offered natural defense against ground-based predators (013-081-013-083). The security of this location allowed the Fire People to prosper and multiply. Their very success — population growth fueled by secure territory and abundant resources — became the engine of their expansion.

The narrator's analysis is remarkably sophisticated for a proto-human consciousness: "The Fire People must have increased in numbers until they pressed uncomfortably against the bounds of their habitat. They were expanding, and in the course of their expanding they drove the Folk before them, and settled down themselves in the caves and occupied the territory that we had occupied" (013-087-013-091).

## The Mechanism of Displacement

The expansion operated through a series of escalating encounters. First came reconnaissance: the wizened old hunter walked alone into the open space before the caves, studying the cliff-wall and run-ways (015-048-015-053). Then came harassment: the [[the-carrot-patch-ambush|Carrot Patch Ambush]] killed two Folk and wounded others (015-020-015-036), and Fire-Men began appearing "in twos and threes, creeping silently through the forest" (015-037-015-038). Finally came the full assault: the [[the-fire-people-massacre|Fire People Massacre]], a dawn attack that systematically exterminated the Folk from their caves using [[fire-as-weapon|fire as a weapon]] (017-001-017-210).

At each stage, the Folk's response was the same: individual flight, never collective defense. The expansion succeeded not only because the Fire People were technologically superior but because the Folk were socially undeveloped — they could feel collective rage but could not organize collective action.
