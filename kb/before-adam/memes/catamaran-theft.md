## Overview

The Catamaran Theft was the first successful appropriation of [[fire-people|Fire People]] technology by the Folk. After observing the Fire People's settlement from hiding, [[big-tooth|the narrator]] and [[lop-ear|Lop-Ear]] spotted a catamaran — a floating platform of logs lashed together — moored at the river's edge. They stole it, launched it into the current, and escaped downstream, laughing at the Fire People who had hunted them across half the known world (013-097-013-101).

The theft was significant beyond the immediate escape it provided. It was the first time the Folk had turned the Fire People's own technology against them — not by manufacturing it themselves but by recognizing its value and seizing the opportunity. The catamaran, built by Fire-Man hands, became the vehicle of Folk survival.

## The Escape

The catamaran carried them down the river in a great loop, passing familiar landmarks — the great drinking-place, the carrot patch — and eventually returning them to the territory of the caves. They had traveled farther and faster than any Folk had ever moved, propelled by a technology they had not invented but had learned to use.

The laughter that accompanied their escape — "we laughed as we drifted, and the Fire People on the bank grew smaller and smaller" (013-097-013-101) — was a reversal of the normal power dynamic. For once, the technologically superior Fire People were helpless, watching their own creation carry their enemies to safety. The catamaran, which the Fire People had built to extend their own range, had become the instrument of Folk survival.

## Technological Appropriation

The theft of the catamaran inaugurated a pattern that would define the Folk's relationship to Fire People technology: observation, imitation, and appropriation. The Folk could not invent the bow or control fire, but they could learn from those who did. Each stolen or imitated technology narrowed the gap between the two peoples, and each narrowing of the gap increased the Folk's chances of survival.

The catamaran was also the first technology that the Folk used without disastrous consequences. Unlike the [[fire-imitation|imitation of fire]], which had started a catastrophic forest blaze, the catamaran did exactly what it was supposed to do: it floated, it carried, and it delivered its passengers to safety.
