## Overview

The Ridge was a low crest of elevated ground near the [[caves]], rising just enough above the surrounding terrain to provide a vantage point. It was not a mountain or a cliff — the [[the-cliffs|cliffs]] themselves served those grander functions — but a subtler feature: a swell in the landscape from which the eye could sweep across the [[the-open-space|Open Space]], the [[the-plain|plain]], and the forest beyond.

The Folk, like their hominid ancestors, understood the value of high ground. The caves offered shelter within the rock; [[the-cliffs|the cliff]]s offered a defensive wall and a place to climb in danger (006-024)(006-041). But the Ridge offered something else: a place to stand and see.

## Geographic Context

The Ridge lay somewhere above the Open Space, likely part of the same geological formation that produced the bluffs into which the caves were carved (005-003). The [[run-way|run-ways]] — the n[[bow-and-arrow|arrow]], well-trodden [[run-way|path]]s used by the Folk to reach the [[drinking-place|drinking-places]] — led upward past the Ridge on their way to the higher ground above [[the-cliffs|the cliff]]s (005-016).

From the Ridge, a watcher could see the Open Space below, [[the-river|the river]] beyond, and the dark line of the forest on the far side. It was a natural lookout — not high enough to be dangerous, but high enough to be useful.

## Role in the Narrative

The Ridge appears in the narrative only indirectly — inferred from the terrain the Folk inhabited rather than named explicitly. The bluffs and high ground above the caves feature repeatedly in moments of crisis: when [[saber-tooth|Saber-Tooth]] entered the Open Space, the Folk scrambled up the bluff to the crevices (006-011)(006-024); when the [[saber-tooth|tiger]] climbed after them, the entire bluff-side became a battleground (006-041)(006-044).

The Ridge proper — the lower, more accessible crest — would have served a quieter purpose. In the daily rhythm of life, someone was always watching: for game returning to the forest, for enemies approaching across the plain, for the first sign of the foraging party coming home at twilight (005-180).

## Inhabitants and Usage

No one lived on the Ridge — it was a place of passing, a waypoint on the [[run-way|path]] up to the [[drinking-place|drinking-places]] and the higher bluffs. But its value as a lookout meant that someone was often there: a sentinel watching while others foraged, or simply a member of the Folk pausing on the climb to scan the territory below.

In the world of the Folk, where danger could appear at any moment — a [[saber-tooth|tiger]] walking into the Open Space, a strange hunter wandering in from the forest — the Ridge was an early-warning system. From its modest height, the watcher could raise the alarm, and the Folk below could scramble for the safety of [[the-cliffs|the cliff]]s.
