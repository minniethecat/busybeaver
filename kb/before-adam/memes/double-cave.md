The **Double-Cave** was a distinctive cave formation in the cliff face occupied by the [[the-folk|Folk]]. Unlike the single-entrance [[caves]] that most of the Folk inhabited, the double-cave had two separate mouths connected by a narrow internal passage (015-010-015-011).

## The Narrator's Residence

The narrator took up residence in the connecting passage of the double-cave after being displaced from his original cave by [[lop-ear|Lop-Ear]] and his new mate — the narrator's own sister, daughter of [[chatterer|the Chatterer]] (015-003-015-010).

> (015-010-015-012) I slept that night, and for many nights, in the connecting passage of the double-cave. From my experience it seemed reasonably safe.

The narrator reasoned that just as two Folk had evaded [[saber-tooth|old Saber-Tooth]] and he himself had dodged [[red-eye|Red-Eye]] by moving through the passage, he could similarly evade hunting animals by going back and forth between the two cave mouths (015-012-015-014).

## Vulnerability to Wild Dogs

The double-cave's safety was compromised by its very design. While the passage provided an escape route from large predators, it was accessible to smaller predators — specifically the [[wild-dogs|wild dogs]] that hunted the Folk. The dogs were small enough to enter any passage the narrator could squeeze through (015-016-015-017).

One night, the wild dogs tracked the narrator into the double-cave. He escaped by dashing out through the other entrance, though one dog caught him in mid-leap, sinking its teeth into his thigh. He managed to climb the cliff face with the dog still attached and throttled it once safely above the pack (015-017-015-028).

## Later Use

After the wild dog attack, the narrator briefly abandoned the double-cave. However, he later returned to sleeping in the passage, while [[lop-ear|Lop-Ear]] would sometimes sleep in the connecting passage as well, with his mate settled in one of the cave mouths. The double-cave remained part of the cave system occupied by the Folk until the [[the-fire-people-massacre|Fire People massacre]] forced their dispersal.
