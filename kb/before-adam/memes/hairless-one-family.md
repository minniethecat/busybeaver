## Overview

The Hairless One's Family is the most fully documented lineage in the horde, spanning three generations: old [[marrow-bone|Marrow-Bone]] (the inventor), his son the [[hairless-one|Hairless One]] (the cave-owner), and his grandchildren [[long-lip|Long-Lip]] (the water-carrier) and the [[singing-one|Singing One]] (the singer). Through this family, the novel traces the emergence of filial duty, task inheritance, and the earliest glimmer of patriarchal lineage among the Folk.

## Filial Duty

The Hairless One's relationship with his father represents the earliest documented instance of filial care among the Folk. Marrow-Bone, too old to fetch water for himself, depended on his son. The Hairless One filled his father's [[gourd|gourd]] and brought it to the cave where the old man resided in a corner (008-036).

This was not an instinctual behavior — the Folk had no concept of filial duty and no cultural expectation that children should care for aging parents. The Hairless One's care for Marrow-Bone was, in this sense, a moral innovation: the recognition that kinship carried obligations beyond childhood dependence.

## Task Inheritance

The delegation of water-carrying from Hairless One to [[long-lip|Long-Lip]] established a pattern that became institutionalized among the Folk. "The women and the larger children" became the designated water-carriers, a division of labor that the narrator traces directly back to this family (008-036).

This task inheritance — from Marrow-Bone's need, to Hairless One's duty, to Long-Lip's delegated responsibility — represents the first documented instance of family-based role transmission within the horde. It was not a formal institution but a pattern that, through the Folk's strong imitative instinct, spread through the community.

## The Family's Fate

The family's story ends tragically. The [[hairless-one|Hairless One]] was killed in the [[the-carrot-patch-ambush|Carrot Patch Ambush]], transfixed by [[bow-and-arrow|arrow]]s while making breakfast alongside his father and son (015-020-015-036). [[marrow-bone|Marrow-Bone]] was the first to die in the [[the-fire-people-massacre|Fire People Massacre]], smoked out of his cave and shot with a dozen arrows (017-041-017-054). The [[singing-one|Singing One]] was seized by [[red-eye|Red-Eye]] after the murder of her mate [[crooked-leg|Crooked-Leg]] (014-034-014-037).

Only [[long-lip|Long-Lip]] survived into the later chapters — he reached [[big-tooth|the narrator]]'s ledge during the massacre with an arrow through his chest before collapsing (017-090-017-094). The family's destruction mirrors the destruction of the horde itself: a whole lineage, carefully maintained across three generations, extinguished in a single season of violence.
