The **Mid-Pleistocene** is the geological epoch during which the ancestral memories recounted in the narrator's dreams are set. Dating from approximately 780,000 to 126,000 years ago, it represents the "Younger World" — a time when early hominids lived in constant peril from predators and the elements [ch00].

## The Younger World

The narrator describes the Mid-Pleistocene as a time when "the world was young" and fear was the dominant experience of life. His dreams transport him to this distant past, where his ancestral self — referred to as [[big-tooth|Big-Tooth]] — navigated a world filled with deadly threats [ch00].

> In short, the fear that reigned supreme in that period known as the Mid-Pleistocene.

The ancestral hominid of this period was a transitional creature — not fully arboreal, not fully terrestrial. As the narrator describes: "He fell from the trees but did not strike bottom" [ch00]. This captures the evolutionary moment when early humans were abandoning tree-dwelling for life on the ground, but had not yet fully adapted to either environment.

## Daily Perils

Life in the Mid-Pleistocene was defined by constant danger. The ancestral human was pursued by beasts of prey including [[saber-tooth|saber-tooth cats]] and [[wild-dogs|wild dogs]], struck at by deadly snakes, and surrounded by competitors like the [[fire-people|Fire People]] who possessed superior technology [ch00].

The narrator emphasizes that fear was not merely an occasional experience but the fundamental condition of existence during this epoch. Every sound in the darkness, every shadow in the forest, could conceal a predator.

## Climate and Environment

The Mid-Pleistocene was characterized by climatic instability and the advance of ice sheets from the north. The narrator references the "ice-sheet from farther north" that "crept down over the face of the land," though the [[the-folk|Folk]] of his ancestral memory never witnessed the ice sheet directly — it lay many generations in their future (008-020-008-025).

A particularly cold winter during the narrator's remembered lifetime, when ice was discovered on the drinking-place for the first time, may have been a fore-runner of the glacial advances to come (008-007-008-022).

## Evolutionary Context

The Mid-Pleistocene represents a critical period in human evolution. The narrator's ability to access ancestral memories from this epoch — through what he describes as dreams — suggests a biological continuity between the hominids of this period and modern humans. The [[the-folk|Folk]], the Fire People, and other groups coexisted in this epoch, representing different stages and branches of hominid development.
