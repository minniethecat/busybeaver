# Hair-Face

**Hair-Face** is a member of the [[fire-people|Folk]] who appears during the [[fire-people|Fire People]]'s campaign of extermination against the horde. He and his wife are among the refugees fleeing the massacre, and Hair-Face joins [[big-tooth|Big-Tooth]], [[lop-ear|Lop-Ear]], and [[swift-one|the Swift One]] during the desperate crossing of the great swamp.

## Flight From the Fire People

Hair-Face first appears during the afternoon of the Fire People's attack, fleeing eastward through the trees:

> In the afternoon, Hair-Face and his wife fled out from among the trees to the east, passed us, and were gone. They fled silently and swiftly, with alarm in their faces.[^1]

Behind them, the cries of the hunters and the screams of captured Folk could be heard — the Fire People were systematically hunting down the horde.

Hair-Face and his wife's flight path provided direction for Big-Tooth's group, who "followed on the heels of Hair-Face and his wife" to the edge of the great swamp — a territory the Folk had always avoided as a place of mystery and fear.[^2]

## The Swamp Crossing

At the swamp's edge, Hair-Face was the first to venture onto the dangerous terrain:

> Hair-Face ran out on the quaking morass and gained the firmer footing of a grass-hummock a dozen yards away. His wife did not follow. She tried to, but shrank back from the treacherous surface and cowered down.[^3]

His wife, driven by the approaching Fire-Men into panic, dashed after them but "ran blindly, without caution, and broke through the crust" — the attackers shot her with arrows as she sank into the mud.[^4] Hair-Face pressed on, joining the group of four (Big-Tooth, Lop-Ear, the Swift One, and himself) as they plunged "deeper and deeper into the swamp."[^4]

## Significance

Hair-Face represents the ordinary members of the Folk caught up in the catastrophe of the Fire People's territorial expansion. His brief appearance illustrates the terror of the extermination campaign and the desperate choices forced upon the horde — fleeing into unknown and deadly terrain rather than facing the more certain death of Fire People arrows.
