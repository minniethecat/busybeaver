## Overview

Red-Eye's Tyranny was the regime of fear that [[red-eye|Red-Eye]] imposed on [[the-horde|the Folk]] through pure physical dominance. As "the most powerful member of the horde" (006-012), he took what he wanted — food, mates, space — through violence rather than consent. His power was absolute within the limits of his physical reach, and it was enforced not by any social structure but by the simple calculus of terror: defy Red-Eye, and die.

The tyranny was made possible not by Red-Eye's strength alone but by the Folk's inability to organize collective opposition. They felt collective rage at his crimes — the murder of [[crooked-leg|Crooked-Leg]], the seizure of the [[singing-one|Singing One]] — but they could not translate that rage into collective action. Their [[hee-hee-council|hee-hee council]] produced noise without resolution, and Red-Eye continued to rule unchallenged.

## The Dead End

Red-Eye's tyranny represents, in London's evolutionary scheme, the dead end of governance by brute force. It was effective in the short term — Red-Eye got what he wanted — but it was ultimately self-defeating. A group governed by fear alone cannot innovate, cannot cooperate, and cannot adapt to external threats. When the [[fire-people|Fire People]] arrived, the Folk's inability to organize collective defense — an inability that Red-Eye's reign had reinforced — proved catastrophic.

Red-Eye himself survived the [[the-fire-people-massacre|massacre]], fighting his way free through an explosion of violence that proved his personal ferocity was undiminished. But his survival was individual, not collective. He fled alone into a thicket and was never seen again. The tyranny of the individual could produce a survivor; it could not produce a future.
