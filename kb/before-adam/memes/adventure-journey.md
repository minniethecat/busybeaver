The **Adventure-Journey** was an extended expedition undertaken by the narrator ([[big-tooth|Big-Tooth]]) and his companion [[lop-ear|Lop-Ear]] during the fall season. Lasting several months, it became a key chronological reference point for the [[the-folk|Folk]], with events dated as occurring before or after the journey (014-007-014-008).

## The Journey

The narrator provides few direct details of the journey itself, but its significance as a temporal marker is established when he and Lop-Ear return. The journey occurred during a year of abundant nuts, which the Folk gathered in [[gourd|gourds]] and cracked at pounding-places in the rock (014-003-014-006).

> (014-007-014-008) It was the fall of the year when Lop-Ear and I returned from our long adventure-journey, and the winter that followed was mild.

Upon returning from the journey, the narrator made frequent trips to his old home territory, searching the area between the [[blueberry-swamp]] and the slough where he and Lop-Ear had learned navigation (014-009-014-012). He was searching for [[swift-one|the Swift One]], driven by the hunger of mate-desire, but could find no trace of her (014-012-014-014).

## Narrative Significance

The adventure-journey served as a chronological anchor point in the narrator's storytelling. Key events are temporally located relative to it:

| Event | Timing Relative to Journey |
|-------|---------------------------|
| Lop-Ear's marriage | Second winter after the journey (015-001-015-002) |
| Narrator's search for the Swift One | Immediately upon return (014-009) |
| Narrator's double-cave period | Begins after Lop-Ear's marriage (015-010) |

The journey represented a period of wandering and exploration distinct from the settled cave-dwelling life of the Folk. It occurred during a period of relative peace and abundance, before the major conflicts and catastrophes that would later befall the group.

## Relationship to the Great Migration

The adventure-journey should be distinguished from the later [[the-great-migration|Great Migration]]. While the migration was a collective, survival-driven movement of the entire Folk away from the [[caves]], the adventure-journey was a voluntary, two-person expedition of exploration and perhaps mate-seeking. The journey predates the migration and occurred during a time of stability.
