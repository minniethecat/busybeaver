**Expedition** is a recurring narrative device in _Before Adam_ that drives both plot and world-building. The characters' exploratory ventures — whether a child's first solo journey, a scouting mission, or the epic [[adventure-journey|adventure-journey]] — expose them to new lands, new peoples, and new dangers. Each expedition expands the known world and advances the story.

## In the Novel

The narrator's first expedition is a childhood solo venture into the world outside his mother's immediate vicinity. He describes it as "this my first journey in the world" (004-185), a formative experience that establishes his independence and curiosity.

The central expedition of the novel is the [[adventure-journey]] undertaken by the narrator and [[lop-ear|Lop-Ear]]. Lasting many days, this expedition takes them far from the horde's territory, across rivers (010-008-010-017), through unfamiliar forests, and into encounters with the [[fire-people|Fire People]]. It is during this journey that they accidentally invent the [[catamaran]] while crossing a river on logs (010-119-010-122). The adventure-journey represents a rite of passage — the boys leave as children and return with knowledge and experience that elevates their status.

The [[the-great-migration|Great Migration]] is expedition at the collective scale — the entire horde moving across mountains, through starvation, toward an unknown destination (012-006-012-124). Unlike the adventure-journey, this was not voluntary exploration but survival-driven displacement. The narrator notes that "many children had survived the frightful journey" (018-040), implying heavy losses.

Later in life, the narrator continues to explore. Even after settling with the [[swift-one|Swift One]], he ventures out on solo scouting missions — one of which leads him to discover the [[fire-people|Fire People]] preparing for war, a discovery that saves his family.

> The days before Lop-Ear and I had gone on our adventure-journey. (016-017)
