## Overview

The caves above [[the-river|the river]] form the central dwelling place of [[big-tooth|the narrator]]'s horde. Located in limestone cliffs, these natural formations provide permanent shelter superior to the temporary [[shelter|tree-shelters]] constructed in the forest. The narrator remembers them as places of relative peace — "the cool caves in [[the-cliffs|the cliff]]s" offering respite from the constant danger of the forest floor (001-024).

## In the Novel

The narrator's first arrival at the caves marks a significant transitional moment (004-197). Before reaching them, the horde lived more precariously in [[shelter|tree-shelters]] scattered through the forest. The caves provide a degree of security that allows the group to establish a more stable territorial base. Different families or sub-groups within the horde occupy different cave openings in [[the-cliffs|the cliff]] face, creating a proto-village structure.

## Context and Significance

Cave occupation is one of the best-attested behaviors of early _Homo_, with sites like Zhoukoudian and Atapuerca providing evidence of hominin cave use spanning hundreds of thousands of years. The positioning of the caves — above a river and accessible only by climbing — reflects a strategic choice of defensible location common in early hominin settlement patterns.
