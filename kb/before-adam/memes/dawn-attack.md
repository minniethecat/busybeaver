## Overview

The Dawn Attack was the opening phase of the [[the-fire-people-massacre|Fire People Massacre]], launched in the "chill gray light" of early morning while most of the Folk were still asleep in their caves (017-002). It began with a stone-throwing counter-assault by the surprised Folk, escalated into a [[bow-and-arrow|arrow]]-volley that pinned the defenders inside their caves, and concluded with the deployment of [[fire-as-weapon|fire as a weapon]] — a tactic against which the Folk had no defense.

## The Surprise

The attack came without warning. [[big-tooth|The narrator]] and the [[swift-one|Swift One]], in the highest cave on [[the-cliffs|the cliff]], were awakened by "a pandemonium of screeching and gibbering" (017-002-017-003). Creeping to the mouth of their cave, they looked down and saw that "the open space was filled with the [[fire-people|Fire People]]" (017-004).

The Folk had grown complacent. It was a golden year — the turnip crop was large, nuts were heavy on the trees, and wild plums were larger and sweeter than usual (017-006-017-008). No one had expected an attack of this scale. There were no sentries posted, no coordinated defense plan.

## The Stone Counter-Attack

The Folk's first response was to fight back with their only ranged weapon: stones. From their positions on [[the-cliffs|the cliff]], they hurled rocks down at the [[fire-people|Fire People]] below. Their first volley was effective — three [[fire-man|Fire-Men]] were struck down, and the Folk finished them off with more rocks (017-010-017-011).

But the [[fire-people|Fire People]] adapted quickly. They drew back out of range of the stones and answered with [[bow-and-arrow|arrow]]s — a weapon against which the Folk had no equivalent and no defense. Within minutes, half a dozen of the Folk were killed and a score wounded (017-029-017-031).

## The Stalemate

After the initial exchange, the fighting settled into a deadlock. The Folk retreated into their caves and would not come out. The [[fire-people|Fire People]] would not come in. "We remained in our caves, and they in the open space. It was a siege" (017-035-017-037).

For a time, it seemed possible that the Folk might survive simply by staying hidden. But the deadlock was an illusion. The [[fire-people|Fire People]] had not come to besiege the caves — they had come to clear them.

## The Breaking

The deadlock broke when the wizened old hunter — the commander of the [[fire-people|Fire People]] — gave the order for fire. Parties of [[fire-man|Fire-Men]] returned from the forest carrying armloads of dry wood, leaves, and grass (017-019-017-020). They heaped this fuel at the mouths of the lowest tier of caves, and then — "the monster we feared" — they set it alight (017-027).

The attack had entered its most lethal phase. With [[fire-as-weapon|fire as a weapon]] now deployed, the cave-by-cave extermination began, and the Dawn Attack became the Fire People Massacre.
