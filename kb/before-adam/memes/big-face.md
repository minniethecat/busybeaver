# Big-Face

**Big-Face** is a member of the [[fire-people|Folk]] who appears in two significant moments: the gibbering council against [[red-eye|Red-Eye]], and the climactic fight with Red-Eye where he became a brief but crucial ally — only to be killed by [[saber-tooth|Saber-Tooth]].

## The Council Against Red-Eye

Big-Face participated in the proto-council where the Folk attempted to coordinate action against Red-Eye. After [[hairless-one|the Hairless One]]'s attempts at cooperative speech dissolved into gibberish, Big-Face took over:

> Then Big-Face, with brow-bristling and chest-pounding, began to chatter. One after another of us joined in the orgy of rage, until even old Marrow-Bone was mumbling and spluttering with his cracked voice and withered lips.[^1]

The council, though it failed to produce coherent collaborative planning, represented an unprecedented moment of collective will among the Folk. Someone seized a stick and began pounding a log in rhythm — the first rhythmic group expression — and Big-Face's energetic participation helped drive the shared emotional crescendo.[^1]

## The Fight With Red-Eye

Big-Face's most consequential act came during the fight between [[big-tooth|Big-Tooth]] and Red-Eye. As Big-Tooth held the Swift One's hair with one hand while grappling with Red-Eye:

> Encouraged by my conduct, Big-Face became a sudden ally. He charged in, sank his teeth in Red-Eye's arm, and ripped and tore at his face.[^2]

This was the moment for the rest of the Folk to join and destroy Red-Eye once and for all — but "they remained afraid in the trees,"[^2] paralyzed by their ingrained terror of the tyrant.

## Death

The fight was interrupted by the appearance of [[saber-tooth|Saber-Tooth]], aroused from his lair by the noise. At the moment of the predator's attack, "Big-Face screamed terribly" — and Big-Tooth caught only "a glimpse of striped fur" before springing for a tree.[^3] From the safety of the branches, the survivors heard "a snarling, and crunching of bones" — Saber-Tooth was "making his supper off of what had been Big-Face."[^4]

## Significance

Big-Face's brief alliance and sudden death encapsulate the brutal calculus of life among the Folk. His courage in joining the fight against Red-Eye — the first member of the horde to physically attack the tyrant alongside Big-Tooth — marks him as one of the braver members of the horde. But his death by Saber-Tooth, while Red-Eye watched from a nearby branch, underscores the ever-present danger of predation that made every confrontation potentially fatal.
