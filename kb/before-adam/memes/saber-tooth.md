## Overview

The Saber-Tooth tiger is the most feared predator in the world of [[the-horde|the Folk]]. Larger, faster, and more lethal than any other creature in their environment, it represents the ultimate threat that keeps [[the-horde|the Folk]] arboreal. The narrator describes encounters with the Saber-Tooth as moments of paralysing terror (006-008).

## In the Novel

The Saber-Tooth appears dramatically in Chapter 6, when it "walked into the open space" where [[the-horde|the Folk]] had gathered (006-008). The description emphasizes the creature's size and the immediate, instinctual fear it triggers. Despite the danger, [[the-horde|the Folk]] engage in a remarkable behavior: "tiger-baiting" — taunting a saber-tooth from the safety of tree branches overhead (008-132). This risky sport demonstrates both [[the-horde|the Folk]]'s arboreal confidence and their complex psychological relationship with the predator they most fear.

## Context and Significance

_Smilodon fatalis_ was one of the last and largest saber-toothed cats, extinct approximately 10,000 years ago. It inhabited [[younger-world|Pleistocene]] North and South America, where it preyed on megafauna such as bison, camels, and ground sloths. Its presence in London's narrative — though geographically improbable for an Old World setting — represents the archetypal predator that shaped hominin evolution through predation pressure.
