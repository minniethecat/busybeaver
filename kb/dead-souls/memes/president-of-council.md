# President of the Local Council

## Character Profile

[[the-president|The President of the Local Council]] is among the first [[officialdom|officials]]
[[chichikov|Chichikov]] inquires about upon arriving in the [[town-of-n|town of N.]] [001-004].
He is described as "a man of much amiability and good nature" who
receives his guests with uncommon warmth — even when they come two or
three at a time [001-009].

Gogol gives him one of the novel's most charming details: he can recite
the whole of Zhukovsky's "Liudmila" from memory — a poem of romantic
melancholy that seems incongruous with [[provincial-government|provincial administration]], and
yet the Council President recites it "with great feeling," moving his
listeners with passages that "made one's heart ache" [008-002].

He is a fixture at whist tables. [[manilov|Manilov]] recalls playing cards with
him until "the cock crowed" [002-041], and [[chichikov|Chichikov]] spends an evening
at his house where the company "had no sooner seated themselves at the
card-tables than they seemed to have been sitting there for years"
[001-010]. His relationship with [[chichikov|Chichikov]] proves useful: as an
"intimate acquaintance" he facilitates the registration of [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s
dubious [[serfdom|serf]] purchases [007-010].

## Role in Dead Souls

The Council President represents the **cultivated surface** of
provincial [[officialdom|official]]dom — a man whose literary sensibilities coexist
without friction with a system of routine administrative [[bribery|corruption]].
His ability to recite romantic poetry while processing fraudulent [[serfdom|serf]]
deeds is Gogol's quiet indictment: culture, in this society, is not a
conscience but a veneer.
