## Location

Gorokhovaia Street (Гороховая улица) is a major thoroughfare in St. Petersburg, Russia. In Gogol's time, it was one of the city's principal streets, running from the Admiralty to the outskirts of the capital.

## In Dead Souls

Gorokhovaia Street appears in the story of Captain Kopeikin (Chapter 10) as part of a sweeping description of St. Petersburg's grandeur. The wounded veteran, arriving in the capital after the 1812 campaign, is overwhelmed by the city's splendor:

> Before him there lay spread out the whole field of life, like a sort of Arabian Nights—a picture made up of the Nevski Prospect, Gorokhovaia Street, countless tapering spires, and a number of bridges apparently supported on nothing. [010-007]

The street is listed alongside the Nevski Prospect as a symbol of the imperial capital's magnificence — a stark contrast to Kopeikin's own impoverished condition.

## Significance

Gorokhovaia Street serves a narrative function in the Kopeikin episode: its mention helps establish the gulf between the dazzling imperial capital and the destitute war veteran seeking a pension. The street represents the unattainable world of wealth and power that Kopeikin can see but never enter.
