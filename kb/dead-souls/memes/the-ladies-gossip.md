# The Ladies' Gossip / The Talk of the Town

## Summary

The [[provincial-scandal|gossip]] that destroys [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s reputation erupts when a lady "in an elegant plaid cloak" hurries across town to communicate a piece of intelligence to her bosom friend, [[anna-grigorievna|Anna Grigorievna]] [009-001]. The visitor reports that Korobochka — "an old woman" and a [[landownership|landowner]] — arrived at [[father-cyril|the Archpriest]]'s house in terror, claiming that [[chichikov|Chichikov]] had appeared at her [[landownership|estate]] armed, demanding to purchase [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s [009-016]. As the two ladies embellish the story, the hostess draws a startling conclusion: [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s real object is not [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s at all but "to abduct [[governors-daughter|the Governor's daughter]]" [009-034]. The pair depart to spread the news, and within half an hour the entire town is aflame with speculation [009-048]. The women's party focuses on the alleged abduction while the men's party wrestles with the [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s, soon connecting the rumours to fears about a newly appointed [[governor|Governor]]-General and suspecting [[chichikov|Chichikov]] of being a government inspector or even a forger of rouble-notes [009-049].

## Participants

- The lady in the plaid cloak — the visitor who first relays Korobochka's story [009-001]
- [[anna-grigorievna|Anna Grigorievna]] — the hostess and "bosom friend" who deduces the abduction plot [009-034]
- [[korobotchka]] — the widowed [[landownership|landowner]] whose trip to town triggers the entire rumour cascade [009-016]
- [[father-cyril|Father Cyril]]'s wife ([[father-cyril|the Archpriest]]'s wife) — intermediary who relays Korobochka's story [009-014]
- The [[prosecutor|Public Prosecutor]] — enters mid-discussion and receives the full version of events [009-048]
- [[chichikov-paul-ivanovitch]] — the object of all speculation

## Significance

The Ladies' Gossip represents the pivotal narrative turn where [[purchase-of-dead-souls|Chichikov's scheme]], which has thus far proceeded smoothly, begins its catastrophic unravelling. Gogol uses the episode to satirise provincial society's mechanisms of information transmission: the competitive embellishment of narrative, the gendered division of interpretive frameworks, and the alacrity with which idle speculation hardens into accepted fact. The women's party proceeds with "method and caution" born of household management [009-049], inventing increasingly elaborate narratives about [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s romantic designs, while the men's party — "the more absolutely senseless of the two" [009-048] — plunges into suspicion of conspiracy, forgery, and espionage. The scene demonstrates that the [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s themselves are, to the townspeople, less interesting than the social drama they can be made to support.

## Context

The [[provincial-scandal|gossip]] is triggered by [[korobochkas-trip-to-town]], when the anxious widow arrives to verify the market price of [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s [009-016]. It follows the successful [[civil-court-registration]] of the [[dead-souls|souls]] in Chapter 7 and the [[governors-ball]] in Chapter 8, where [[chichikov|Chichikov]] had reached the peak of his social prestige. The rumours escalate across Chapters 9 and 10, culminating in [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s realisation that he must flee the town — the event narrated in [[chichikovs-flight]].
