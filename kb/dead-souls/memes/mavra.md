# Mavra

## Character Profile

Mavra is [[plushkin|Plushkin]]'s housekeeper, an elderly servant woman who has endured her master's pathological miserliness long enough to learn the art of patient endurance. She appears in Chapter 6 during [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s visit to [[plushkin|Plushkin]]'s decaying [[landownership|estate]], where she is the domestic factotum entrusted — however grudgingly — with the keys to the store-room [006-044].

Her relationship with [[plushkin|Plushkin]] is defined by suspicion and verbal abuse. When [[plushkin|Plushkin]] cannot find a sheet of clean writing paper, he summons Mavra with frantic cries and immediately accuses her of theft: "What have you done with my piece of writing paper, you pilferer?" [006-061]. Mavra defends herself with peasant directness: "Why should I make off with it? 'Twould be of no use to me, for I can neither read nor write." When [[plushkin|Plushkin]] threatens her with devils roasting her on iron spits on the Judgment Day, she stands her ground, declaring that she never stole anything — and moments later locates the paper sitting on the table before [[plushkin|Plushkin]]'s very eyes [006-073].

This small domestic scene reveals Mavra as a woman of quiet competence and undeserved resilience. She fetches sugar, candles, and matches as ordered, absorbs her master's paranoid accusations without open rebellion, and knows the household well enough to find what [[plushkin|Plushkin]] himself has mislaid.

## Role in Dead Souls

Mavra's function is twofold. First, she serves as a witness to the full extent of [[plushkin|Plushkin]]'s degeneration: his suspicion extends even to the servant entrusted with his store-room keys, and his accusations of theft over a sheet of paper reveal a mind that has lost all sense of proportion [006-059]. Second, her plain-spoken responses — "I can neither read nor write," the paper is "lying on the table" — provide one of the novel's rare moments where a servant speaks truth to a [[landownership|landowner]] without punishment [006-073]. Her ability to endure [[plushkin|Plushkin]]'s tirades without losing her composure contrasts with his frantic agitation, making him appear the more absurd.

## Appearances

- **Chapter 6**: Mavra is summoned by [[plushkin|Plushkin]] to fetch sugar for the samovar and receives detailed, paranoid instructions about scraping the loaf [006-044]. Later, she is accused of stealing writing paper, defends herself, and locates the missing sheet [006-073]. She is sent for a tallow candle and finally instructed to return unused sugar to the store-room [006-101].
