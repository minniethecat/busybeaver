# Korobochka's Trip to Town

## Summary

Anxious after [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s nocturnal visit to her [[landownership|estate]] and the transaction in which he purchased her [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s for fifteen roubles apiece [003-137], the widowed [[landownership|landowner]] Korobochka travels to town N. to verify that she has not been cheated. She arrives at the house of [[father-cyril|the Archpriest]] "all pale and trembling" [009-016], recounting a sensational version of events: [[chichikov|Chichikov]] appeared at dead of night, armed, shouting "Open the gates, or we will break them down!" and demanding that she sell him [[dead-souls|souls]] of the recently deceased [009-016]. When she protested that [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s could not be sold, [[chichikov|Chichikov]] supposedly insisted they were still alive, creating such a scene that the entire village gathered [009-020]. Korobochka also reports that [[chichikov|Chichikov]] "threw fifteen roubles at her" and forced her to sign "a worthless paper" [009-022]. [[father-cyril|Father Cyril]]'s wife relays this account to a lady of the town, who rushes to tell her bosom friend [[anna-grigorievna|Anna Grigorievna]] — and within half an hour, the entire town is consumed by speculation [009-048].

## Significance

Korobochka's trip to town is the narrative catalyst that transforms [[purchase-of-dead-souls|Chichikov's scheme]] from a private series of transactions into a public scandal. It is characteristic of Gogol's plotting that the unravelling originates not from any grand revelation but from the anxious curiosity of a minor character — the widowed Korobochka, whose defining traits are parsimony and a fear of being cheated. Her journey also exemplifies the novel's preoccupation with the speed and distortion of information in provincial society: Korobochka's account is already embellished in the telling, and each subsequent relay adds new layers of invention until the dead-[[dead-souls|souls]] scheme has been transfigured into a romantic abduction plot [009-034]. The episode demonstrates that in the closed world of town N., a single anxious widow can set in motion forces powerful enough to destroy even as adaptable a man as [[chichikov|Chichikov]].

## Context

The trip follows Korobochka's transaction with [[chichikov|Chichikov]] in Chapter 3, where she reluctantly sold him her [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s after protracted negotiation [003-137]. The immediate trigger for her journey is her fear that she may have undersold: she has never heard of a market for [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s and suspects [[chichikov|Chichikov]] may be exploiting her ignorance. The trip directly precipitates the events of [[the-ladies-gossip]], which in turn leads to the collapse of [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s social standing and his eventual flight from town in [[chichikovs-flight]].
