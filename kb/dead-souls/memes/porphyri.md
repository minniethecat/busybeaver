# Porphyri

## Character Profile

Porphyri is one of [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]'s household servants, appearing throughout Chapter 4 during [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s visit to [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]'s [[landownership|estate]]. He is introduced when [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]] summons him to display a puppy: "Hi, Porphyri! Bring here that puppy, you rascal!" [004-044]. Gogol describes him as "a fellow dressed like his master" [004-050], a detail that immediately establishes the blurred social boundaries of [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]'s chaotic household, where the master's informality extends to a kind of sartorial parity with his [[serfdom|serfs]].

Porphyri is physically robust — along with [[pavlushka|Pavlushka]], he is described as "a pair of sturdy [[serfdom|serfs]]" [004-274] — and he performs a wide range of duties. He brings the puppy for inspection, carries it away on command, lights candles in the parlour, serves a fresh bottle of wine, and fetches the chessboard when ordered [004-125] [004-201] [004-228]. His functions blur the line between house servant, kennel-hand, and general factotum. When reproached for not brushing the puppy properly, he protests: "I DID brush him" [004-055], one of the few moments a [[serfdom|serf]] servant speaks in his own voice in the novel.

## Role in Dead Souls

Porphyri's most dramatic appearance comes during the climactic fight scene, when [[chichikov|Chichikov]] attempts to flee [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]'s [[landownership|estate]]. [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]] shouts "Porphyri! [[pavlushka|Pavlushka]]!" and the two servants enter to restrain their master physically [004-273] [004-274]. This moment — servants holding back their own master at the behest of a guest — is a vivid illustration of the moral chaos of [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]'s world, where established hierarchies collapse under the pressure of the [[landownership|landowner]]'s own violent impulsiveness.

Porphyri serves a structural function in the chapter as well: his repeated entrances and exits (with the puppy, with candles, with wine, with the chessboard) mark the escalating rhythm of [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]'s hospitality-turned-hostility, providing a physical, almost theatrical punctuation to each phase of [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s ordeal.
