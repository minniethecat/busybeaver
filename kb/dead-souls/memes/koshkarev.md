# Colonel Koshkarev

## Character Profile

Colonel Koshkarev is one of Gogol's most memorable minor creations —
a retired military officer who has transformed his [[landownership|estate]] into a parody
of bureaucratic administration. When [[chichikov|Chichikov]] arrives, seeking to
purchase [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s, he finds that Koshkarev's household operates
entirely through written petitions, committees, and formal approvals.

Koshkarev's ruling passion is **procedure for its own sake**. Nothing
can be done without a properly formatted request submitted through
channels — even the simplest conversation must be processed through his
system of [[officialdom|official]]dom-in-miniature. [[selifan|Selifan]] the coachman, sent to
announce [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s arrival, encounters men who direct him to "the
office" and ask "whom have I the honour of addressing?" [014-030].

[[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s reaction — "A queer fellow, that Koshkarev!" [014-021] —
understates the absurdity. Koshkarev is what happens when the
bureaucratic mentality Gogol satirized throughout Part I is
concentrated into a single personality: the system becomes the man,
and the man becomes incomprehensible outside the system.

Koshkarev is a relative of [[general-betristchev|General Betristchev]], a connection that
places him in Part II's social network of military-gentry families
[014-169].

## Role in Dead Souls

Koshkarev is a **satirical intensification** of the bureaucratic
absurdity that pervades the novel. If the [[officialdom|officials]] of the [[town-of-n|town of N.]]
are unconsciously bureaucratic — their procedures are the water they
swim in — Koshkarev is consciously, zealously bureaucratic. He has
chosen to live this way, and his choice reveals the absurdity more
clearly than any amount of background satire could.

He also serves a practical narrative function: [[kostanzhoglo|Kostanzhoglo]] directs
[[chichikov|Chichikov]] to visit Koshkarev, and the visit's comic frustration propels
[[chichikov|Chichikov]] onward to the next stage of his Part II journey.
