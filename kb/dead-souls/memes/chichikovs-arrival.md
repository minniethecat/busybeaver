# Chichikov's Arrival in Town N.

## Summary

The novel opens with the arrival of a "smart britchka" at an inn in the [[town-of-n|provincial town]] of N. [001-001]. The occupant is a gentleman of deliberately indeterminate description — "though not handsome, was not ill-favoured, not over-fat, and not over-thin. Also, though not over-elderly, he was not over-young" [001-001]. His arrival produces no stir; two peasants at a dramshop briefly debate whether the carriage might travel as far as Moscow or Kazan before dismissing the matter [001-001]. At [[the-tavern|the inn]], [[chichikov|Chichikov]] is shown to a modest room and immediately sets about questioning the waiter on the town's [[officialdom|officials]], [[landownership|landowners]], and social conditions — inquiring about epidemic diseases, the governor, the president of the council, and the most significant [[landownership|landowners]] of the district [001-003]. Over the following days, he makes a series of social calls on the town's dignitaries, displaying a remarkable talent for agreeable conversation and flattery that quickly establishes him as a welcome figure in provincial society.

## Significance

[[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s arrival is a masterclass in narrative exposition through indirection. Gogol introduces his protagonist not through biography or internal monologue but through the external details of his equipage, his hotel room, and his pattern of inquiries. The famous description of [[chichikov|Chichikov]] as neither one thing nor another — not handsome but not ugly, not fat but not thin, not old but not young — establishes him immediately as a figure of strategic indeterminacy, a man whose social survival depends on being whatever each interlocutor wishes to see. The two peasants' speculation about whether the carriage will reach Moscow or Kazan is an apparently trivial detail that, in retrospect, prefigures the novel's concern with [[russian-empire|Russia]]'s vastness and the question of where its hero — and its national destiny — is ultimately headed. The arrival also prefigures [[chichikovs-flight|Chichikov's departure]] in [[chichikovs-flight]]: the novel begins and ends with a britchka in motion, framing the entire narrative as a journey.

## Context

The arrival is the first event of the novel and establishes [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s _modus operandi_: careful reconnaissance, social ingratiation, and the gathering of information about [[landownership|landowners]] and their [[serfdom|serfs]]. It directly precedes his visits to the surrounding [[landownership|estate]]s — [[manilovka]], [[korobotchkas-village]], and the others — where he will execute his scheme of purchasing [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s. The unremarkable nature of the arrival [001-001] contrasts starkly with the scandalous departure narrated in [[chichikovs-flight]].
