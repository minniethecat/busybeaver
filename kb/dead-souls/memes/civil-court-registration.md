# Registration of Dead Souls at the Civil Court

## Summary

After acquiring nearly four hundred [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s from the [[landownership|landowners]] of the district, [[chichikov|Chichikov]] proceeds to the municipal offices in town N. to formalise the deeds of purchase [007-010]. He has personally drawn up the legal documents rather than employ a lawyer, both to expedite the process and to save expense [007-001]. Accompanied by [[manilov|Manilov]], who insists on escorting him and producing a beautifully calligraphed list of his own [[serfdom|serfs]] bordered by his wife's handiwork [007-014], [[chichikov|Chichikov]] navigates the chaotic [[officialdom|bureaucracy]] of the Serf Department. The registration is completed under the supervision of [[ivan-antonovitch|Ivan Antonovitch]], the [[officialdom|functionary]] whose face is described as a "pitcher-mug" [007-040], and the transaction culminates in a celebratory gathering where Sobakevitch, though absent, is nevertheless toasted by the [[officialdom|officials]] [007-110].

## Participants

- [[chichikov-paul-ivanovitch]] — purchaser of the [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s, who personally drafts the deeds of indenture [007-001]
- [[manilov]] — accompanies [[chichikov|Chichikov]] to the offices and presents a handwritten list of his [[serfdom|serfs]] [007-012]
- [[ivan-antonovitch|Ivan Antonovitch]] — the [[officialdom|functionary]] in charge of the Serf Department, known for his "pitcher-mug" physiognomy [007-040]
- [[president-of-council|President of the Local Council]] — an intimate acquaintance of [[chichikov|Chichikov]] who facilitates the proceedings [007-010]

## Significance

The civil court registration marks the formal legal enactment of [[purchase-of-dead-souls|Chichikov's scheme]] and represents Gogol's extended satire of [[russian-empire|Russia]]n imperial [[officialdom|bureaucracy]]. The episode contrasts the gravity of the transaction — trafficking in [[dead-souls|souls]] of the deceased — with the absurd mundanity of the clerks' procedures, the petty curiosity of young [[officialdom|officials]], and the casual [[bribery|corruption]] that oils the administrative ma[[table-of-ranks|chin]]ery. [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s internal monologue while drafting the deeds [007-001] constitutes one of the novel's most philosophically resonant passages, as he meditates on the lives and deaths of the peasants whose names appear on his lists, momentarily humanising the abstract transaction before reverting to his practical concerns [007-009].

## Context

The registration follows [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s visits to the [[landownership|landowners]] [[manilov|Manilov]], Korobochka, [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]], Sobakevitch, and [[plushkin|Plushkin]], from whom he has purchased the [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s [007-001]. It precedes the [[governors-ball|Governor's Ball]] in Chapter 8, where [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s social standing reaches its zenith before the rumours that will destroy it begin to circulate. The successful registration provides [[chichikov|Chichikov]] with the legal documentation he needs to mortgage the "[[dead-souls|souls]]" as though they were living [[serfdom|serfs]], completing the paper phase of his scheme.
