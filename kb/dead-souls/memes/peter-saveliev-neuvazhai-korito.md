# Peter Saveliev Neuvazhai Korito

## Character Profile

Peter Saveliev Neuvazhai Korito is a **deceased [[serfdom|serf]]** who never appears in the novel as a living character — he is one of the "[[dead-souls|dead soul]]s" whose name survives only in [[officialdom|official]] [[revision-census|census]] records. His sole distinction is his name, which is the most elaborately comical among all the peasants that [[chichikov|Chichikov]] purchases.

When the widow Korobochka dictates her list of [[dead-souls|dead serfs]], [[chichikov|Chichikov]] is amazed by the peasants' names and surnames. "Especially did he halt before a certain 'Peter Saveliev Neuvazhai Korito.' 'What a string of titles!' involuntarily he ejaculated" （003-147）. Later, when [[chichikov|Chichikov]] is reviewing the deeds of purchase and basking in his new status as a [[landownership|landowner]], his eyes again halt "at one name in particular — that of the same Peter Saveliev Neuvazhai Korito who had once been the property of the widow [[korobotchka|Korobotchka]]. Once more he could not help exclaiming: 'What a series of titles! They occupy a whole line!'" （007-003）.

The name is rich in comic implication. "Neuvazhai" suggests "do not respect" in [[russian-empire|Russia]]n, and "Korito" means "trough" — together suggesting something like "Peter, son of Savel, the Disrespected Trough." The name's sheer length, occupying "a whole line" of text, is itself the joke: a [[serfdom|serf]] — a person legally treated as property — bearing a name more grandiose than any nobleman's.

[[chichikov|Chichikov]], reviewing the list in Chapter 7, addresses the dead [[serfdom|serf]] directly: "Peter Saveliev, I wonder whether you were an artisan or a plain muzhik. Also, I wonder how you came to meet your end; whether in a tavern, or whether through going to sleep in the middle of the road and being run over by a train of waggons" （007-004）. These imagined biographies — [[chichikov|Chichikov]] inventing lives for his phantom peasants — form one of the novel's most celebrated passages, in which the "[[dead-souls|dead soul]]s" briefly come alive through [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s imagination even as he reduces them to commodities.

## Role in Dead Souls

Peter Saveliev Neuvazhai Korito is a **minor but memorable presence** among [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s purchased [[dead-souls|souls]]. His function is primarily comic: the absurdly long name of a [[serfdom|serf]] is a Gogolian joke at the expense of [[russian-empire|Russia]]n naming conventions and the bureaucratic records that reduce living people to entries on a page. The name appears twice — once during the transaction with Korobochka （003-147） and again when [[chichikov|Chichikov]] reviews the deeds （007-003） — creating a small running gag.

The name also serves the novel's deeper theme of **identity and erasure**. All that remains of this man is a comically long name — no life story, no family, no trace of his actual existence beyond his entry in the [[revision-census|census]]. [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s attempt to imagine his biography in Chapter 7 is both an act of imaginative empathy and an illustration of how completely the [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s have been reduced to abstractions. The name that "occupies a whole line" takes up more space in the text than the man himself ever occupied in the world.
