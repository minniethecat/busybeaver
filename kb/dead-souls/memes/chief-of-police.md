# Chief of Police

## Character Profile

The Chief of Police of the [[town-of-n|town of N.]] is a man of remarkable social
gifts — described by Gogol as "a magician" who needs only to wink at
a fishmonger's shop to have the finest sturgeon and caviar appear on
his table [007-115]. He is, in effect, the town's master of ceremonies:
charismatic, genial, and universally liked, with an apparently
inexhaustible capacity for hospitality.

His residence is the ultimate social destination. Gogol notes that a
party at the Chief of Police's house is where "three hours after the
festivities had begun, the company had no sooner seated themselves at
the card-tables than they seemed to have been sitting there for years"
— such was the atmosphere of effortless conviviality he created
[001-010].

Unlike the [[governor|Governor]] (bland and [[officialdom|official]]) or the [[prosecutor|Prosecutor]] (blinking
and silent), the Chief of Police projects an air of capable warmth.
He is the [[officialdom|official]] everyone wants at their dinner party, the one who
can smooth over any awkwardness with a joke or a well-timed
refreshment.

## Role in Dead Souls

The Chief of Police represents the **performative charm** that sustains
provincial society. His "magic" is the grease that keeps the wheels of
[[officialdom|official]]dom turning — he can procure anything because every merchant
and shopkeeper owes him a favor, and those favors are the invisible
currency of local governance.

[[chichikov|Chichikov]] is naturally drawn to him. The Chief of Police is among the
first [[officialdom|officials]] [[chichikov|Chichikov]] visits, and his warm reception sets the tone
for [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s social conquest of the town [001-007]. Later, when the
[[chichikov|Chichikov]] scandal breaks, the Chief of Police hosts the gathering where
the [[officialdom|officials]] attempt to decode the mystery [010-011].

His administration is notably less moralistic than the [[governor|Governor]]'s and
less philosophical than the [[postmaster|Postmaster]]'s — he operates on the principle
of practical accommodation. This makes him, in Gogol's taxonomy, one of
the town's more effective [[officialdom|officials]], though his effectiveness is
entirely in the service of maintaining pleasant appearances.
