# Proshka

Proshka is a thirteen-year-old [[serfdom|serf]] boy serving in [[plushkin|Plushkin]]'s household [006-042]. He appears in Chapter 6 during [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s visit to [[plushkin|Plushkin]]'s decaying [[landownership|estate]], where he exemplifies the miserly [[landownership|landowner]]'s treatment of his servants.

## Character Profile

Proshka is a barefoot youngster who, when summoned to the house, must don the [[landownership|estate]]'s single universal pair of boots — kept in the hall for all servants to share [006-041]. The boots are described as being of such immense dimensions that they almost engulf his legs as he walks [006-042]. [[plushkin|Plushkin]] characterizes Proshka as stupid-faced but light-fingered, remarking to [[chichikov|Chichikov]] that "lay anything aside, and in a trice he will have stolen it" [006-042].

When [[plushkin|Plushkin]] gives him instructions, Proshka stands silent and unresponsive, prompting further scolding [006-043]. [[plushkin|Plushkin]] threatens him with a bir[[table-of-ranks|chin]]g should he dare enter the storeroom unsupervised, and warns that he will be watched from the window [006-043].

After Proshka departs with the boots, [[plushkin|Plushkin]] uses the boy's supposed untrustworthiness to justify his general distrust of servants [006-045]. Later, [[plushkin|Plushkin]] summons Proshka again to cancel the samovar order and return the sugar — a characteristic gesture of the [[landownership|landowner]]'s pathological stinginess [006-101].

## Role in Dead Souls

Proshka functions as a minor but vivid illustration of the conditions on [[plushkin|Plushkin]]'s [[landownership|estate]]. The single-pair-of-boots arrangement, the barefoot servants performing "saltatory feats worthy of the most vigorous of stage-dancers" to navigate the muddy courtyard [006-041], and the casual threats of physical punishment all paint a picture of the degradation that extreme miserliness inflicts on dependents. Proshka himself is barely individuated — he is less a character than a specimen of [[plushkin|Plushkin]]'s household management, his silence and apparent dullness likely products of the environment rather than innate traits.
