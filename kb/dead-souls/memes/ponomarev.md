# Ponomarev

Ponomarev is a wine merchant operating in the [[town-of-n|provincial town]] where the main action of _[[dead-souls|Dead Souls]]_ unfolds. He appears only through the boasting of [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]], who recounts his dealings with the merchant during a drinking session with [[Chichikov, Paul Ivanovitch|Chichikov]] and his son-in-law [[mizhuev|Mizhuev]] in Chapter 4.

## Character Profile

Ponomarev is described by [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]] in contradictory terms — simultaneously a rogue and a purveyor of superlative drink. [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]] declares that "Ponomarev, the wine merchant, did a fine trade indeed!" before immediately adding that "he is a rascal, you know, and ought not to be dealt with, for he puts all sorts of rubbish into his liquor — Indian wood and burnt cork and elderberry juice, the villain!" [004-021].

Yet [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]'s condemnation is immediately undercut by his own enthusiasm: "Nevertheless, get him to produce a bottle from what he calls his 'special cellar,' and you will fancy yourself in the seventh heaven of delight" [004-021]. Ponomarev also produced a French wine he called "Bonbon," which [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]] claims had "the bouquet of a rose garden, of anything else you like" [004-021].

Later, [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]] recounts that Ponomarev sold him a piece of sturgeon, declaring "This piece is just the thing for you. Even if you were to search the whole market, you would never find a better one" [004-042]. [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]] responds by telling Ponomarev outright that he and the Collector of Taxes are "the two greatest skinflints in the town" — to which the merchant merely "stroked his beard and smiled" [004-042].

## Role in Dead Souls

Ponomarev is a figure of pure hearsay, known only through [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]'s unreliable narration. His significance lies in what he reveals about the provincial commercial world: a marketplace where adulteration is expected, where every merchant has a "special cellar" for favoured customers, and where being called a rascal is an accepted part of doing business.

He is not a character in any dramatic sense but a feature of [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]'s anecdotes — part of the texture of carousing, consumption, and casual dishonesty that defines the [[landownership|landowner]]'s social world. The name "Ponomarev" appears in the novel's merchant-class background, alongside the references to commercial networks discussed in the [[merchant-guild]] page.
