# Nozdrev's Estate

## Description

[[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]'s [[landownership|estate]] presents a picture of chaos and restless energy. Upon arrival,
[[chichikov|Chichikov]] finds the dining room under haphazard renovation — peasants on wooden
trestles whitewashing the ceiling while drawling an endless song [004-001]. The
manor house itself is modest; what distinguishes the [[landownership|estate]] is the variety of
its outbuildings, each reflecting [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]'s erratic, boastful character.

The stables house two mares and a colt that [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]] extravagantly claims cost
ten thousand roubles, though his brother-in-law scoffs at the figure [004-002].
Empty stalls stand ready for "equally fine animals" that once occupied them.
A goat paces beneath the horses' noses — an old stable superstition — while
a young wolf, chained nearby, is fed only raw meat "to grow up as fierce as
possible" [004-003]. The pond allegedly holds fish requiring two men to lift
one out.

The kennel is the [[landownership|estate]]'s centerpiece: a small, neatly-built shed surrounded by
a fenced run, filled with dogs of every sort, size, and colour. [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]] moves
among them "like a father lording it over his family circle," and the dogs leap
up to greet visitors, one even licking [[chichikov|Chichikov]] on the lips [004-003].

The water-mill lacks its spindle-socket — the stone that should turn is missing,
a detail [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]] dismisses with a wave. The smithy completes the tour. Finally,
[[nozdrev|Nozdrev]] leads his guests across a boggy, molehill-studded field to show them a
boundary post, claiming ownership of the forest beyond it as well — supposedly
purchased three days earlier by his steward while he was at the fair [004-005].
The brother-in-law's raised eyebrow says everything.

Inside the study, not a single book or paper is to be found. Instead, swords
and guns ("this one worth three hundred roubles, that one about eight hundred"),
"real Turkish" daggers with a [[russian-empire|Russia]]n craftsman's name accidentally inscribed,
a barrel-organ that cannot finish a tune without a shrill pipe whistling
independently, and a collection of tobacco pipes in every conceivable material
fill the room [004-006].

## Role in Dead Souls

[[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]'s [[landownership|estate]] is the third stop on [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s circuit, following
[[manilovka|Manilovka]] and Korobochka's village. Unlike the sentimental dreaminess of
[[manilovka|Manilov's estate]] or the prudent frugality of Korobochka's, Nozdrev's property
embodies complete disorder — the outer manifestation of a character for whom no
boundary, literal or figurative, carries weight.

The [[landownership|estate]] tour serves a dual narrative function. Structurally, it delays the
[[dead-souls|dead soul]]s negotiation and builds comic tension. Thematically, it reveals a
property whose every feature — the unfinished mill, the disputed boundary, the
empty stalls, the broken barrel-organ — mirrors its owner's tendency to inflate,
fabricate, and abandon. When [[chichikov|Chichikov]] finally broaches the subject of dead
[[dead-souls|souls]], the ensuing negotiation degenerates into a brawl, forcing [[chichikov|Chichikov]] to
flee. The [[landownership|estate]] thus becomes the setting for the novel's most violent rejection
of [[purchase-of-dead-souls|Chichikov's scheme]] [004-007].
