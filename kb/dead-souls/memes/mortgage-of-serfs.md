# Mortgage of Serfs

## Explanation

Under the [[russian-empire|Russia]]n Imperial system of [[serfdom|serf]]dom, [[landownership|landowners]] could use their [[serfdom|serfs]] — registered as taxable "[[dead-souls|souls]]" in the periodic [[revision-census|census]] or [[revision-census|revision]] — as collateral for loans from state banks and the Board of Guardians [P03-005]. A [[landownership|landowner]] could mortgage his living [[serfdom|serfs]] and the land they worked on, receiving a substantial sum while retaining the use of both the land and the [[serfdom|serfs]]' labour. The bank held title to the [[serfdom|serfs]] on paper, but the [[landownership|landowner]] continued to profit from their work — paying only interest until the principal was redeemed [014-041]. This system meant that [[serfdom|serfs]] functioned not only as agricultural labour but as financial instruments: they could be bought, sold, mortgaged, or pledged, their value determined by the number of "[[dead-souls|souls]]" on the [[revision-census|revision]] list [011-033].

The legal fiction at the heart of the system was that [[serfdom|serfs]] were attached to the land — yet in practice, [[landownership|landowners]] routinely bought, sold, and mortgaged [[serfdom|serfs]] independently of land transfers. Gogol's narrator notes the absurdity: "it is illegal to buy or mortgage peasants without land, but I..." — trailing off suggestively [011-033]. This ambiguous legality was precisely what [[chichikov|Chichikov]] intended to exploit.

## Significance in Dead Souls

The serf mortgage system is the financial engine of [[purchase-of-dead-souls|Chichikov's scheme]]. His plan is not merely to acquire [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s as a curiosity but to present them to a bank as collateral for a loan — as though they were living [[serfdom|serfs]] with economic value [003-098]. The scheme rests on the bureaucratic gap between death and its registration: [[serfdom|serfs]] who died between [[revision-census|revision]]s remained on the tax rolls, and a [[revision-census|revision]] list entry was sufficient to establish ownership for mortgage purposes [P03-005].

This mechanism allows Gogol to layer satire upon satire. First, the absurdity that human beings — living or dead — can be reduced to entries on a ledger and pledged like real [[landownership|estate]]. Second, the complicity of the entire bureaucratic apparatus: [[officialdom|officials]] at every level process [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s transparently fraudulent deeds because the right social connections have been made [007-010]. Third, the moral blindness of the [[landownership|landowners]]: each one approaches the transaction differently, yet none sees through to the essential horror of treating the dead as financial assets. The mortgage system is Gogol's metaphor for a society that has mortgaged its own soul.

The scheme's viability is taken for granted by the narrator: "he would need to do would be to mortgage the [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s, and then to sell them" [014-274]. This matter-of-fact tone — as though mortgaging non-existent people were a routine business transaction — is the novel's darkest satirical register.

## Examples from the Text

- **The initial conceit**: The narrator explains that an owner might "mortgage to a bank for a considerable sum" his various [[serfdom|serfs]], including the dead ones still on the [[revision-census|revision]] list [P03-005].
- **[[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s pitch to Korobochka**: He argues that by selling [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s, she saves herself "paying the tax upon [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s as though they were living" [003-098].
- **The legal ambiguity**: [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s father-in-law reference: "it is illegal to buy or mortgage peasants without land, but I shall sell them as though consigning them for settlement" [011-033].
- **The burden of mortgages**: In Part II, a character laments "it is all mortgaged" — the universal condition of the gentry [014-041]. Another adds "nowadays everything is being mortgaged, or is going to be" [014-209].
- **[[kostanzhoglo|Kostanzhoglo]]'s critique**: The industrious [[landownership|landowner]] condemns mortgage culture: "every day the interest on a mortgage is due" — a life lived in financial servitude [014-154].
- **[[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s endgame**: The [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s would be "mortgaged" and then sold, completing the circle of paper wealth built on non-existent property [014-274].
