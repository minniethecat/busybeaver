# Antoshka

## Character Profile

Antoshka is a minor servant at the estate of Peter Petrovitch Pietukh, appearing briefly in Part II, Chapter 3 of *Dead Souls*. He is mentioned only once, alongside another servant Emelian, during the extravagant feast hosted by Pietukh for Chichikov and his companions [014-072].

## Role in the Novel

Antoshka appears when the host Pietukh, responding to Chichikov's complaint of weariness, declares "we will soon put weariness to flight." At that moment, Antoshka and Emelian enter the room bearing food, presumably the *zakuski* (appetizers) that precede the main meal [014-072]–[014-073].

The host refers to Antoshka dismissively as a "thief" and to Emelian as a "gawk." These epithets appear to be the landowner's habitual characterizations of his servants rather than specific accusations. The term "thief" in this context may reflect a general suspicion of household serfs rather than any known crime [014-072].

## Significance

Antoshka, along with Emelian, is among the most minor named characters in *Dead Souls*. His sole appearance serves to illustrate the bustling domestic economy of Pietukh's estate, where servants are summoned en masse to sustain the host's legendary hospitality. The casual insult "thief" directed at Antoshka also reflects the authoritarian dynamic between Russian landowners and their serfs — a theme that runs throughout the novel.
