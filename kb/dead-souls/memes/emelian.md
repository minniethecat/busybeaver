# Emelian

## Character Profile

Emelian is a minor servant at the estate of Peter Petrovitch Pietukh, appearing briefly in Part II, Chapter 3 of *Dead Souls*. He is mentioned only once, alongside another servant Antoshka, during the extravagant feast hosted by Pietukh for Chichikov and his companions [014-072].

## Role in the Novel

Emelian appears when the host Pietukh, responding to Chichikov's complaint of weariness, declares "we will soon put weariness to flight." At that moment, Emelian and Antoshka enter the room bearing food, presumably the *zakuski* (appetizers) that precede the main meal [014-072]–[014-073].

The host refers to Emelian dismissively as a "gawk" — a term suggesting foolishness or awkwardness — and to Antoshka as a "thief." These epithets appear to be the landowner's habitual characterizations of his servants rather than specific accusations [014-072].

## Significance

Emelian, along with Antoshka, is among the most minor named characters in *Dead Souls*. His sole appearance serves to illustrate the bustling domestic economy of Pietukh's estate, where even servants with disparaging nicknames are part of the elaborate hospitality machine that so overwhelms Chichikov. The pair's entrance with food underscores the host's determination to conquer weariness through consumption.
