# Chichikov's Dismissal from the Customs

## Summary

After leaving the Building Commission, [[chichikov|Chichikov]] secures a position in the Customs Department — a post he had long coveted. He applies himself to his new duties with extraordinary zeal, displaying "activity, perspicuity, and ubiquity" never before seen. Within four weeks, he masters every detail of Customs procedure: he can divine from an invoice the exact yardage of cloth, weigh a roll of fabric by instinct, and search suspected travelers with "the nose of a veritable bloodhound."

His incorruptibility at first seems absolute. He reduces Polish Jewish smugglers to despair and refuses even to convert confiscated goods into personal profit — conduct so scrupulous it attracts the attention of the authorities. After receiving promotion and unlimited search powers, however, [[chichikov|Chichikov]] signals to a well-organized smuggling ring that has long sought to [[bribery|bribe]] him: "The time is NOW." The scheme involves Spanish sheep transported across the frontier wearing double fleeces concealing Brabant lace worth millions of roubles. Within a year, [[chichikov|Chichikov]] and his accomplice each amass four hundred thousand roubles.

The enterprise collapses through a petty quarrel. For reasons variously attributed to drink, a woman, or mutual recrimination about being "priest's sons," [[chichikov|Chichikov]] quarrels with his accomplice. The offended partner submits an anonymous denunciation to the authorities. A complete exposure follows: the two [[officialdom|officials]] are interrogated, stripped of their property, and forced to confess. [[chichikov|Chichikov]], through charm, flattery, and strategic [[bribery|bribe]]ry, escapes criminal trial — but emerges with only ten thousand roubles hidden away, two dozen shirts, a small britchka, and his servants [[selifan|Selifan]] and [[petrushka|Petrushka]].

## Participants

- [[chichikov-paul-ivanovitch]] — the disgraced customs officer who rebuilds from ruin
- The accomplice — a fellow t[[table-of-ranks|chin]]ovnik, "of the type which, in spite of grey hairs, stands powerless against temptation," whose quarrel with [[chichikov|Chichikov]] triggers the exposure
- Polish Jewish smugglers — the first targets of [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s incorruptible zeal
- The smuggling association — a "well-found association for smuggling on regular, carefully prepared lines"
- Spanish sheep — the ovine couriers of contraband Brabant lace
- [[selifan]] and [[petrushka]] — [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s servants, acquired during this period

## Significance

The customs episode is the pivotal event in [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s backstory. It demonstrates the full arc of his moral economy: initial rectitude that is merely strategic patience, followed by audacious [[bribery|corruption]] once the risk-reward calculus shifts. His survival — escaping with ten thousand roubles and his liberty while his accomplice "sank into oblivion" — reveals the resilience and adaptability that define his character. The episode also establishes the pattern that will govern the [[purchase-of-dead-souls|dead souls scheme]]: identify a legal loophole, exploit it with elaborate organization, and be undone by human fallibility (here, a drunken quarrel; in the main plot, [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]'s loose tongue). [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s reflection that his losses constitute "suffering in the Service in the cause of Truth" is both self-serving and psychologically revealing — it shows a man constitutionally incapable of admitting genuine defeat.

## Context

The customs episode follows [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s earlier career in the Civil Service and the Building Commission, where he first learned to manipulate bureaucratic systems for personal gain. It directly precedes his invention of the [[purchase-of-dead-souls|dead souls scheme]]: stripped of wealth but not of energy, he surveys the ruined landscape of his fortunes and resolves to begin again. The episode also introduces [[selifan|Selifan]] and [[petrushka|Petrushka]], who will accompany him throughout the main narrative. Gogol structures the customs story as a miniature of the novel's larger arc: rise through ingenuity, collapse through human weakness, and ambiguous escape.
