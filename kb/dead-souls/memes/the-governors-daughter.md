# The Governor's Daughter

**The Governor's Daughter** is an unnamed character in Gogol's *Dead Souls* — a sixteen-year-old blonde maiden returning from boarding school who makes her sole appearance at the [[governors-ball|Governor's Ball]] in Chapter 8. Though she speaks barely a word in the novel, her effect on [[Chichikov, Paul Ivanovitch|Chichikov]] sets in motion the chain of events that leads to his downfall.

## Appearance at the Ball

[[Chichikov, Paul Ivanovitch|Chichikov]] first notices her when, seeking refuge from the crush of the ballroom, he retreats to a side room and sees the [[governor|Governor]]'s wife leading the girl by the arm. He is struck by her appearance:

> "She was a blonde, with delicate, regular features, a sharp little chin, and fascinatingly rounded oval of the face — the sort of face an artist would take as a model for a Madonna."

She is "just sixteen, and just out of a boarding-school" [008-030]. [[Chichikov, Paul Ivanovitch|Chichikov]] stands rooted to the spot, unable to speak — his usual social fluency deserts him entirely. He later tries unsuccessfully to engage her in conversation, but she yawns at his tales of [[kherson-province|Kherson]] villages and rural improvements [008-036].

## The Scandal

The ladies of the town immediately notice [[Chichikov, Paul Ivanovitch|Chichikov]]'s marked attention to the Governor's daughter while ignoring every other woman in the room. Offended, they retaliate by spreading malicious gossip. The rumour escalates from social slight to criminal fantasy: "The man's real object is — TO ABDUCT THE GOVERNOR'S DAUGHTER" [009-032]. The ladies lament the consequences of "educating girls like the Governor's daughter at school" and [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]] later volunteers to assist in the abduction scheme [009-033].

The scandal proves fatal: the [[prosecutor|Public Prosecutor]] dies of anxiety over the affair [010-020], and [[Chichikov, Paul Ivanovitch|Chichikov]] is forced to [[chichikovs-flight|flee town]].
