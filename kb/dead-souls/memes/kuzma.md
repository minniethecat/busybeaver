# Telepen Kuzma

## Character Profile

Telepen Kuzma is a kitchen servant at [[peter-petrovitch-pietukh|Pietukh]]'s [[landownership|estate]], appearing briefly in Part II of the novel. He is introduced during [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s visit when [[peter-petrovitch-pietukh|Pietukh]], eager to display his hospitality, orders a freshly caught sturgeon to be shown to his guest. Standing by the river with his cap held over his head, [[chichikov|Chichikov]] watches as [[peter-petrovitch-pietukh|Pietukh]] directs his peasants: "Cast off the net, Thoma Menshov, and pick up that sturgeon for the gentleman to see. Go and help him, Telepen Kuzma" [014-017].

Kuzma's role is confined to this single command — he helps lift the "veritable monster of a fish" for [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s inspection. The scene is characteristic of [[peter-petrovitch-pietukh|Pietukh]]'s exuberant, almost competitive hospitality, where the display of food (in this case a giant sturgeon "fresh run from the river") becomes a performance requiring multiple servants. Kuzma's patronymic-style name "Telepen" marks him as a peasant, and his function is purely physical: he is the extra pair of hands needed to hoist the enormous fish.

## Role in Dead Souls

Kuzma appears only in this moment, but like many of Gogol's minor servants, his brief presence contributes to the novel's immersive texture of [[landownership|estate]] life. [[peter-petrovitch-pietukh|Pietukh]]'s household runs on the labour of men like Kuzma and Thoma Menshov — [[serfdom|serfs]] whose names flicker into the narrative only when their physical strength is required, then vanish again. The giant sturgeon scene, with its cast of summoned peasants, is a miniature tableau of the seigneurial economy: the master displays abundance, the [[serfdom|serfs]] provide the muscle.
