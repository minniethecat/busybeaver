# Nozdrev

## Character Profile

Nozdrev is the third [[landownership|landowner]] [[chichikov|Chichikov]] encounters and by far the most dangerous. Where the other [[landownership|landowners]] are merely indifferent, greedy, or suspicious, Nozdrev is actively chaotic — a man who "could not sit still for a whole day together" and whose life is a relentless pursuit of sensation through gambling, fighting, drinking, and lying （004-001）. He is described as having "a plentiful supply of health and animal spirits" and a rosy complexion that suggests both physical vigor and a complete absence of inner reflection.

Nozdrev's [[landownership|estate]] reflects his character: he cares more about his kennels (where he is "like a father among his family") than his fields or [[serfdom|serfs]]. He has a genius for fabrication — he lies with such conviction and detail that it is impossible to tell where the truth ends. He will cheat at cards, then indignantly deny it; he will offer to sell you anything (horses, dogs, a barrel-organ, [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s), then refuse to conclude the deal.

## Role in Dead Souls

Nozdrev serves as the novel's **agent of chaos**. His encounter with [[chichikov|Chichikov]] is the only one that nearly turns violent — [[chichikov|Chichikov]] barely escapes being beaten. More importantly, Nozdrev is the one who exposes [[chichikov|Chichikov]] at the [[governors-ball|Governor's ball]], loudly revealing that [[chichikov|Chichikov]] has been buying [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s. This triggers the cascade of [[provincial-scandal|rumor]]s that ultimately drives [[chichikov|Chichikov]] out of town （ch08）.

Nozdrev represents a specific pathology of the [[russian-empire|Russia]]n gentry: the squandering of energy on nothing. He is all action and no purpose, all passion and no object. His vitality is real but entirely wasted — on card games he loses, on horses he cannot afford, on lies that convince no one.

## Quotations

> "Nozdrev was in his element. He talked so much that even Chichikov found himself powerless to get a word in edgewise." （004-015）
