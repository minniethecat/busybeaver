## Location

Naberezhnaia Street (Набережная улица, "Embankment Street") is a street in St. Petersburg, Russia. In the early 19th century, it was a fashionable residential area where high-ranking government officials maintained their residences.

## In Dead Souls

Naberezhnaia Street appears in the story of Captain Kopeikin (Chapter 10). The wounded veteran, seeking a pension from the government, inquires after the residence of the President of the Commission and is directed to this street:

> He asked where the President lived, and was told that his house was in Naberezhnaia Street. And you may be sure that it was no peasant's hut, with its glazed windows and great mirrors and statues and lacqueys and brass door handles! [010-007]

The description emphasizes the opulence of the government official's residence — a world utterly removed from Kopeikin's own circumstances.

## Significance

Naberezhnaia Street symbolizes the inaccessible world of bureaucratic power in imperial St. Petersburg. For Captain Kopeikin, the street represents both hope (the Commission that might grant his pension) and the crushing reality of a system indifferent to the sacrifices of ordinary soldiers. The juxtaposition of the grand residence with Kopeikin's wooden leg and poverty is one of the episode's sharpest satirical contrasts.
