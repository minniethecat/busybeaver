# The Town Square

## Description

The town square of the [[town-of-n|provincial town]] of N. is dominated by the municipal
offices — "a large, three-storied stone structure, all as white as chalk" — that
probably represents the construction of a government department in the
provinces: "stone by stone, brick by brick, with the usual architectural
features" — in short, the kind of building "whose object it is to house the
municipal offices, the house of correction, and the town council of the
district" [007-020].

The square itself is sufficiently spacious that [[selifan|Selifan]], driving [[chichikov|Chichikov]]
there, "debouch[es] upon" it, and [[chichikov|Chichikov]] enters the building with his fur
cap to complete the legal formalities of the [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s transfer [007-010].
The upper stories of the municipal offices are "dotted with the heads of
clerks, including that of Themis herself's votaries bent over documents"
[007-020].

The interior of the municipal offices exposes the ma[[table-of-ranks|chin]]ery of provincial
governance: a chaotic network of desks through which [[chichikov|Chichikov]] must navigate to
find the "desk for [[serfdom|serf]] affairs," encountering an elderly man whose "quills
scratched with a squeaking sound" and a general office where clerks "sat with
their heads bent over their desks" [007-020 – 007-030].

The square is also flanked by buildings associated with the town's social
and administrative life. From here [[chichikov|Chichikov]] proceeds to the notary's office
and later encounters [[officialdom|officials]] who will become pivotal in his unmasking.

## Role in Dead Souls

The town square serves as the bureaucratic theater of [[dead-souls|Dead Souls]]. It is where
[[chichikov|Chichikov]] transforms his verbal agreements with the [[landownership|landowners]] into legally
registered property. The square's municipal offices are the venue for the
novel's central bureaucratic set-piece: the elaborate procedure of registering
the [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s, with its desks, clerks, [[bribery|bribes]], and formalities — a scene that
both advances the plot and delivers Gogol's satire of [[russian-empire|Russia]]n [[officialdom|official]]dom.

The square represents the intersection of town and country in the novel's
world: [[landownership|landowners]] from the countryside ([[manilov|Manilov]], Sobakevitch) converge here to
have their transactions witnessed and notarized. It is also where [[chichikov|Chichikov]]
first encounters the [[chief-of-police|Chief of Police]], who "fill[s] the office of a sort of
father and benefactor in the town" [007-030].

The physical description of the municipal offices — the white chalk-like
facade, the clerks' heads bobbing in the windows, the scrat[[table-of-ranks|chin]]g quills —
functions as a visual synecdoche for the entire administrative system: imposing
from without, chaotic within.
