# Semen Ivanovitch

## Character Profile

Semen Ivanovitch is a minor [[officialdom|official]] in the [[town-of-n|town of N.]], introduced in a catalogue of bureaucrats in Chapter 10 of [[dead-souls|Dead Souls]]. He is defined by two peculiar details. First, "for some reason or another, he was never alluded to by his family name" [010-001] — an oddity in a society where family name and patronymic are the standard modes of address. Second, he wore "on his index finger a ring with which he was accustomed to dazzle his lady friends" [010-001] — a small, vain flourish that Gogol deploys with characteristic economy to sketch an entire personality.

The ring is the key to his miniature portrait. In a single detail, Gogol captures the provincial [[officialdom|official]] who compensates for obscurity with flash — a man whose social existence depends on a glittering accessory rather than any substance of rank or achievement. The fact that he is known only by his Christian name and patronymic, without the dignity of a family name, suggests a person of uncertain social standing, hovering somewhere between respectability and insignificance.

## Role in Dead Souls

Semen Ivanovitch appears in a darkly comic passage cataloguing the physical toll that the "[[dead-souls|dead soul]]s" scandal has taken on the town's [[officialdom|official]]dom. The arrival of a new [[governor|Governor]]-General, combined with rumours and "serious documents," has caused a general wasting-away among the bureaucrats: "more than one frockcoat had come to look too large for its wearer, and more than one frame had fallen away" [010-001]. The passage names specific [[officialdom|officials]] who have diminished in bulk — the President of the Council, the Director of the Medical Department, and the [[prosecutor|Public Prosecutor]] — before adding that "even a certain Semen Ivanovitch" had "diminished in bulk" [010-001].

The word "even" is crucial. Semen Ivanovitch is so minor, so peripheral to the ma[[table-of-ranks|chin]]ery of power, that his physical shrinkage becomes a measure of how thoroughly the panic has permeated the town. If even the obscure, ring-wearing Semen Ivanovitch is wasting away from anxiety, the terror must be universal.
