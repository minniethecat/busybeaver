# Mizhuev

## Character Profile

Mizhuev is Nozdrev's brother-in-law, a flaxen-haired [[landownership|landowner]] who first appears in Chapter 4 when [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]] encounters [[chichikov|Chichikov]] at the posting inn. [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]] introduces him with a casual gesture: "He is Mizhuev, my brother-in-law. He and I were talking of you only this morning." [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]] has just returned from the fair, cleaned out, and has transferred himself to Mizhuev's britchka for the journey home, having gambled away his own horses.

Physically, Mizhuev is distinguished by his flaxen hair — the narrator refers to him repeatedly as "the flaxen-haired man." His temperament stands in sharp contrast to [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]'s. Where [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]] boasts of drinking seventeen bottles of champagne at a sitting, Mizhuev quietly interjects: "But you were saying the same thing when last I met you. Yet, even though I lent you fifty roubles, you lost them all" [004-018]. Where [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]] insists he could have broken the bank, Mizhuev flatly contradicts him [004-018]. This pattern of skeptical correction runs through their entire interaction.

The narrator provides a brief character sketch of Mizhuev as "one of those people in whose character, at first sight, there seems to be a certain stubbornness" [004-074]. Such people, the narrator explains, will argue against what they know to be true and will ultimately agree to things they initially refused — a trait that explains why Mizhuev, despite his better judgment, continues to accompany his reckless brother-in-law.

## Role in Dead Souls

Mizhuev functions as a dramatic foil to [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]. Every extravagant claim [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]] makes — about gambling winnings, champagne consumption, or the quality of his possessions — is met with Mizhuev's quiet contradiction, grounding the scene in reality and signaling to the reader that [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]'s fabrications are recognized even within his own family [004-018]. His presence establishes the normalcy of [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]'s lying: those closest to him expect nothing else.

He also serves as a reluctant enabler. Despite repeatedly trying to leave, Mizhuev is drawn back by [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]'s insistence. When [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]] orders him to drink, he complies. When [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]] compels him to stay, he stays — illustrating the magnetic, if toxic, pull of [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]'s personality on those around him [004-111].

## Appearances

- **Chapter 4**: Mizhuev first appears at the posting inn, introduced to [[chichikov|Chichikov]] as Nozdrev's brother-in-law [004-004].
