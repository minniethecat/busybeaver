# Eremi Sorokoplechin

## Character Profile

Eremi Sorokoplechin is a deceased [[serfdom|serf]] praised by [[sobakevich|Sobakevich]] during his bargaining session with [[chichikov|Chichikov]] over the sale of [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s. [[sobakevich|Sobakevich]], seeking to inflate the value of his deceased peasants, launches into an elaborate catalogue of their skills. After boasting of the bricklayer [[milushkin|Milushkin]] and the bootmaker [[maksim-teliatnikov|Maksim Teliatnikov]], [[sobakevich|Sobakevich]] reaches his climax: "And Eremi Sorokoplechin, too! He was the best of the lot, and used to work at his trade in Moscow, where he paid a tax of five hundred roubles. Well, THERE'S an assortment of [[serfdom|serfs]] for you! — a very different assortment from what [[plushkin|Plushkin]] would sell you!" [005-087].

The five-hundred-rouble tax payment is a significant detail — it marks Sorokoplechin as an *obrok* peasant, one who paid quit-rent to his master while working independently in the city. Such a sum indicates a prosperous craftsman whose labour generated substantial income, making him the most economically valuable of [[sobakevich|Sobakevich]]'s listed [[serfdom|serfs]]. [[sobakevich|Sobakevich]]'s rhetorical strategy is transparent: by dwelling on the skills and earnings of the dead, he attempts to treat them as living commodities.

## Role in Dead Souls

Sorokoplechin appears solely as a name in [[sobakevich|Sobakevich]]'s catalogue of [[dead-souls|dead serfs]], but his brief mention carries thematic weight. He belongs to a group of deceased peasants whose talents [[sobakevich|Sobakevich]] lovingly enumerates — men who, in life, were skilled artisans contributing to [[russian-empire|Russia]]'s economy. [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s cynical observation that "a dead body is only good to prop a fence with" [005-089] stands in stark contrast to the vivid, almost tender specificity with which [[sobakevich|Sobakevich]] describes each man. Sorokoplechin, the "best of the lot," becomes a ghostly emblem of lost human potential, his five hundred roubles of tax a numeric epitaph.
