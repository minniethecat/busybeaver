# Pavlushka

## Character Profile

Pavlushka is one of [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]'s household servants, appearing in Chapter 4 during [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s visit. He is introduced at the climactic moment when [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]], enraged at [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s refusal to continue the card game, summons his servants to intervene: "[[porphyri|Porphyri]]! Pavlushka!" shouted [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]] as madly he strove to free himself [004-273]. Together with [[porphyri|Porphyri]], Pavlushka enters the room and is described as one of "a pair of stout rascals with whom it would be unwise to meddle" [004-274].

Pavlushka is a minor figure who appears only in this single scene, functioning as physical enforcer in [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]'s chaotic household. His physical description — "stout rascal" — suggests the kind of brawny [[serfdom|serf]] selected for his capacity to intimidate, a practical necessity in a household where the master's volatile temper regularly escalates to violence.

> **Note**: This Pavlushka (Nozdrev's servant, Chapter 4) is distinct from the childhood nickname "Pavlushka" used for Chichikov in Chapter 11. The two characters are unrelated.

## Role in Dead Souls

Pavlushka's brief appearance serves a structural function in the chapter's climax. When [[chichikov|Chichikov]] releases [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]'s arms upon seeing the servants enter, Pavlushka and [[porphyri|Porphyri]]'s presence instantly shifts the power dynamic — the guest who had been physically restraining the host now faces three opponents. This moment crystallises the danger of [[nozdrev|Nozdrev]]'s world, where conventional hospitality can collapse into physical threat at any moment, and where a "guest" may need to flee for his safety.
