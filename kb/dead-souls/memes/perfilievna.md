# Perfilievna

Perfilievna is the housekeeper on [[tientietnikov|Tientietnikov]]'s [[landownership|estate]], appearing in Part II of the novel [012-008]. Her sole appearance consists of a daily comic altercation with [[grigory|Grigory]], the [[landownership|estate]]'s butler, observed by their master from his window.

## Character Profile

Perfilievna and [[grigory|Grigory]] engage in a routine shouting match in the courtyard, a spectacle that forms part of the morning chaos of [[tientietnikov|Tientietnikov]]'s household [012-008]. In their exchange, [[grigory|Grigory]] calls her a "nuisance" and a "good-for-nothing," and accuses her of being "thick with that bailiff" — to which Perfilievna retorts that the bailiff is "as big a thief as you are" [012-012]. She then notices [[tientietnikov|Tientietnikov]] wat[[table-of-ranks|chin]]g from the window and alerts [[grigory|Grigory]]: "There — sitting by the window, and looking at us!" [012-014].

The squabble is presented without narrative resolution — it simply merges into the general din of the household, which includes a crying [[serfdom|serf]] child and a scalded borzoi puppy [012-014]. [[tientietnikov|Tientietnikov]], unable to concentrate amid the noise, eventually sends word for the commotion to cease.

## Role in Dead Souls

Perfilievna serves as part of Gogol's portrait of the idle, disorderly country [[landownership|estate]]. Together with [[grigory|Grigory]], [[mikhailo|Mikhailo]] the valet, and the general clamour of the household, she illustrates the aimless drift of [[tientietnikov|Tientietnikov]]'s domestic life — a microcosm of the [[landownership|landowner]]'s own paralysis of will. Her exchange with [[grigory|Grigory]], with its accusations of thievery and favouritism, hints at the petty [[bribery|corruption]] and mutual suspicion that pervade the [[serfdom|serf]]-run [[landownership|estate]].
