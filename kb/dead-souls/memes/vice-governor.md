# Vice-Governor

## Character Profile

The Vice-Governor is the second-ranking civil [[officialdom|official]] in the town of
N., notable primarily for his invisibility — he is mentioned far more
often than he appears, functioning as a kind of background constant in
provincial life. Gogol introduces him almost in passing: "next,
[[chichikov|Chichikov]] repaired to the Vice-Governor's" [001-007], and from that
point on he recurs as a name on invitation lists and dinner-party
roll-calls rather than as a distinct personality.

His most memorable characterization comes not from Gogol's narration
but from [[sobakevich|Sobakevich]]'s acid tongue: "The pair are just Gog and Magog"
— referring to the [[governor|Governor]] and Vice-Governor together as a matched
set of biblical rogues [005-030]. This is [[sobakevich|Sobakevich]]'s blanket
condemnation of [[officialdom|official]]dom: everyone is corrupt, and the Vice-Governor,
by virtue of his proximity to power, is doubly suspect.

[[mizhuev|Mizhuev]]'s wife is occasionally speculated to be the Vice-Governor's
wife, though this is presented as uncertain [[provincial-scandal|gossip]] — a testament to the
porous boundaries between private life and public [[provincial-scandal|rumor]] in the town of
N. [009-002].

## Role in Dead Souls

The Vice-Governor embodies the **institutional background** of
provincial [[russian-empire|Russia]] — the layer of [[officialdom|official]]dom that is present everywhere
and notable nowhere. He hosts dinner parties ([[chichikov|Chichikov]] attends one),
receives [[officialdom|official]] visits, and is counted among the notables — but his
individual character is so negligible that Gogol grants him almost no
direct speech or action.

This is itself a satirical point: the Vice-Governor is the ultimate
placeholder, a man whose function is to exist as the [[governor|Governor]]'s
substitute without ever needing to substitute. In the novel's taxonomy
of [[officialdom|official]] futility, he occupies the median: not as memorably blank
as the [[prosecutor|Prosecutor]], not as socially gifted as the [[chief-of-police|Chief of Police]], not
as philosophical as the [[postmaster|Postmaster]] — simply there.
