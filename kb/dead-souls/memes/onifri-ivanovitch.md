# Onifri Ivanovitch

Onifri Ivanovitch was a State Councillor in the [[imperial-civil-service|Imperial Russian Civil Service]] and the uncle of [[tientietnikov|Andrei Ivanovitch Tientietnikov]] [012-024]. His single appearance in the narrative comes through a flashback explaining the origins of his nephew's bureaucratic career.

## Character Profile

Onifri Ivanovitch instilled in the young [[tientietnikov|Tientietnikov]] the maxim that "the only means to success in the Service lay in good handwriting, and that, without that accomplishment, no one could ever hope to become a Minister or Statesman" [012-024]. Through his influence, he secured his nephew a posting to a Government Department in St. Petersburg.

His advice, while limited to the clerical virtue of penmanship, reflects the values of the mid-level imperial [[officialdom|bureaucracy]] — where form and presentation often mattered more than substance. [[tientietnikov|Tientietnikov]]'s subsequent disillusionment with the Service suggests the inadequacy of such counsel as a guide to a meaningful life.

## Appearances

- **Part II, Chapter 1** [012-024]: Mentioned as [[tientietnikov|Tientietnikov]]'s uncle, who provided both career advice and patronage to launch his nephew's [[imperial-civil-service|civil service]] career.
