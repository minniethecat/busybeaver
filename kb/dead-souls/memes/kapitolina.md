# Kapitolina

Kapitolina is a maidservant mentioned in a single line of _[[dead-souls|Dead Souls]]_. She appears only in [[sobakevich|Sobakevich]]'s list of deceased [[serfdom|serfs]], where she is recorded as the mother of a certain Thedotov.

## Character Profile

Kapitolina is known solely from the annotation on [[sobakevich|Sobakevich]]'s [[serfdom|serf]] registry. As [[chichikov|Chichikov]] scans the document in Chapter 7, the narrator notes that [[sobakevich|Sobakevich]] had taken unusual care in listing each deceased peasant — recording who had been the father and mother and how those parents had behaved themselves. Only against the name of a certain Thedotov was there inscribed: "Father unknown, Mother the maidservant Kapitolina, Morals and Honesty good" [007-001].

The brief notation says much in little. That the father is "unknown" while the mother is a named maidservant suggests Kapitolina was an unwed domestic [[serfdom|serf]] who bore a child outside marriage — a circumstance common enough in the world of the novel to require no further comment. The phrase "Morals and Honesty good" is the standard formula of a [[serfdom|serf]] registry, yet applied to a woman whose situation might in other contexts have invited moral censure, it carries a quiet irony.

## Role in Dead Souls

Kapitolina's single appearance serves a structural purpose in the Chapter 7 [[serfdom|serf]]-list scene. The narrator observes that the details in [[sobakevich|Sobakevich]]'s list — the names of parents, the notes on conduct — "communicated to the document a certain air of freshness, they seemed to connote that the peasants in question had lived but yesterday" [007-001]. As [[chichikov|Chichikov]] reads on, he feels "softened in spirit" and begins the extended meditation on the lives of the [[dead-souls|dead serfs]] that constitutes one of the novel's most celebrated passages.

Kapitolina thus exists at the threshold between bureaucratic record and human life. She is not a character but a documentary trace — one of the many brief, suggestive notations by which Gogol transforms a list of [[dead-souls|dead soul]]s into a meditation on mortality and the [[russian-empire|Russia]]n peasantry.
