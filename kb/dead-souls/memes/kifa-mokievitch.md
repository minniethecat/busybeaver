# Kifa Mokievitch

## Character Profile

Kifa Mokievitch is introduced in a philosophical digression in Chapter 11 of [[dead-souls|Dead Souls]] — a story within the story, told by the narrator to illustrate the peculiarities of [[russian-empire|Russia]]n character. He is described as "a good man named Kifa Mokievitch, and a man of kindly disposition" who "went through life in a dressing-gown, and paid no heed to his household" [011-037].

The reason for his domestic neglect is unusual: Kifa Mokievitch's "whole being was centred upon the province of speculation" [011-037]. He is preoccupied with a single philosophical question: "A beast is born naked. Now, why should that be? Why should not a beast be born as a bird is born — that is to say, through the process of being hatched from an egg?" [011-037]. Gogol adds drily: "Nature is beyond the understanding, however much one may probe her" [011-037]. Kifa Mokievitch represents a particular [[russian-empire|Russia]]n type — the man of abstract thought who is so absorbed in cosmic questions that he completely neglects the practical world around him.

## Role in Dead Souls

Kifa Mokievitch appears in a digression that Gogol deploys at a pivotal moment. His story immediately follows the narrator's reflection on the two incompatible tendencies of the [[russian-empire|Russia]]n character — the impulse toward lofty speculation and the impulse toward destructive, ungoverned energy. Kifa embodies the first tendency: benign, gentle, utterly absorbed in questions of no practical consequence, while his household and his troublesome son [[moki-kifovitch|Mofi Kifovitch]] run entirely out of control.

The digression serves a structural purpose. It follows the revelation of [[chichikov|Chichikov]]'s biography and his flight from town, and it functions as a comic parable about the gap between contemplation and action. Kifa Mokievitch, who "ponders the parturition of beasts" while his son "cracks someone's fingers in half," is a satirical portrait of a peculiarly [[russian-empire|Russia]]n intellectual type — the man whose philosophy touches nothing and changes nothing.
