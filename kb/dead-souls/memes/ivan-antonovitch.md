# Ivan Antonovitch, the Jug-Snout

## Character Profile

Ivan Antonovitch is a senior clerk in the Civil Court of the [[town-of-n|town of N.]],
memorable chiefly for the shape of his face, which "tended towards the
nose — it was what, in common parlance, is known as a jug-shaped jowl"
[007-011]. He is a man past forty, with thick black hair, and belongs
to that class of seasoned bureaucrats who have long since shed any
youthful impulse toward helpfulness or haste.

When [[chichikov|Chichikov]] and [[manilov|Manilov]] arrive at the court to register the [[serfdom|serf]]
purchase deeds, Ivan Antonovitch does not look up from his writing.
He continues scrat[[table-of-ranks|chin]]g away with his pen as if the visitors did not
exist — a studied indifference that [[chichikov|Chichikov]] instantly recognises as
the hallmark of an experienced [[officialdom|official]] who understands the value of
making petitioners wait [007-011].

His desk work is no idle pretense of efficiency. The narrative notes
that he was transcribing a report on the recovery of monies from a
property seizure, a document so swollen with bureaucratic verbiage that
Ivan Antonovitch himself, had it been read aloud, "would have found
nothing to object to in its style" — a detail that lampoons both the
document's vacuity and the clerk's complete identification with the
system [007-011].

## Role in Dead Souls

Ivan Antonovitch functions as the novel's **purest embodiment of
routine [[bribery|corruption]]**. He does not rage or scheme; he simply processes.
The [[bribery|bribe]] [[chichikov|Chichikov]] offers him — a banknote slipped under a book —
is accepted without a word, without so much as a glance. The clerk
"covered it with a book" and, when [[chichikov|Chichikov]] tried to show it again,
"with a movement of his head, Ivan Antonovitch signified that that was
unnecessary" [007-019]. The transaction requires no negotiation because
no negotiation is needed: [[bribery|bribe]]ry has been so thoroughly normalised that
it operates through gesture alone.

He is the gatekeeper [[chichikov|Chichikov]] must pass. Without Ivan Antonovitch's
tacit cooperation, the deeds would languish indefinitely. His power
lies in his immovability — he cannot be hurried, cannot be charmed,
and yields only to the one language the system recognises: money. When
he later appears among the witnesses for the deed registration, he
"comported himself with not a little address," handling the paperwork
with professional smoothness now that the proper inducements have been
made [007-095].
