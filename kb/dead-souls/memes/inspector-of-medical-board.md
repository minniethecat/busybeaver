# Inspector of the Medical Board

## Character Profile

The Inspector of the Medical Department (rendered as "Medical Board" in the queue entry) is one of the minor [[officialdom|officials]] of the [[town-of-n|provincial town]] of N. He belongs to that class of functionaries who populate the background of Gogol's provincial world — present at every formal occasion, named among the dignitaries, yet never developed into a full character.

He is first mentioned by Sobakevitch during the arrangement of the [[serfdom|serf]] deed registration. Needing witnesses for the transaction, Sobakevitch nominates the [[prosecutor|Public Prosecutor]] and the Inspector of the Medical Department as ideal candidates, observing that both are "men of leisure" [007-076].

Sobakevitch's recommendation is characteristically blunt. The [[prosecutor|Public Prosecutor]] "has little to do, and has even that little done for him by his chief clerk, Zolotucha" [007-076]. The Inspector, Sobakevitch implies, is similarly situated — a man who "cumbers the ground for nothing."

When the witnesses assemble, the Inspector arrives alongside the [[prosecutor|Public Prosecutor]], the constantly blinking [[officialdom|functionary]] who is his perpetual companion [007-102]. Both are listed among [[officialdom|officials]] [[chichikov|Chichikov]] barely knows — "substitutes and supernumeraries" pressed into service from the subordinate staff.

At the [[governors-ball|Governor's ball]], the Inspector participates in the great chain of greetings that engulfs [[chichikov|Chichikov]] [008-013]. [[chichikov|Chichikov]] is passed from [[officialdom|official]] to [[officialdom|official]] like a parcel — surrendered to the Inspector, handed to the Commissioner of Taxes, committed to the Town Architect. This comic relay reduces each dignitary to an interchangeable link in the bureaucratic chain.

## Role in Dead Souls

The Inspector exemplifies Gogol's technique of populating his provincial world through brief, functional mentions. The reader never learns his name, appearance, or medical qualifications. He exists only as a title and a function: to witness a deed, to complete a receiving line.

His utter interchangeability — handed from one [[officialdom|official]] to another like a parcel — is the point. In a novel about the deadness of provincial life, even the living [[officialdom|officials]] are ghostly.
