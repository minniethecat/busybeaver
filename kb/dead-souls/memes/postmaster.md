# Postmaster

## Character Profile

The Postmaster of the [[town-of-n|town of N.]] is a short, insignificant-looking man
with a penchant for philosophy and a more questionable habit: he reads
the letters that pass through his office, keeping particularly
interesting ones for his personal collection [010-006].

Gogol describes him as a man who "went in more for philosophy" than for
argument, spending his evenings absorbed in books — "Young's Night
Thoughts" is mentioned as a particular favorite [008-002].

Despite his philosophical leanings, the Postmaster is no deep thinker.
His analysis of the [[chichikov|Chichikov]] mystery — that [[chichikov|Chichikov]] must be [[captain-kopeikin|Captain]]
[[captain-kopeikin|Kopeikin]], a one-armed, one-legged war veteran — is a masterpiece of
comically wrong deduction. He delivers this theory with the conviction
of a man who has read one too many romantic tales.

## Role in Dead Souls

The Postmaster serves two important functions in the novel. First, he
is the **custodian of information** — literal and figurative. His habit
of reading letters mirrors the town's collective appetite for [[provincial-scandal|gossip]]
and surveillance. In a society where private communication is routinely
violated, the line between public [[officialdom|official]] and spy is conveniently
blurred.

Second, he is the **novel's embedded storyteller**. His narration of
the "Tale of [[captain-kopeikin|Captain Kopeikin]]" [010-006] is one of *[[dead-souls|Dead Souls]]*' most
famous digressions — a story-within-a-story about a disabled war
veteran denied a pension by callous St. Petersburg bureaucrats.

The tale is both a critique of Imperial indifference and a narrative
trap: the Postmaster's identification of [[chichikov|Chichikov]] as [[captain-kopeikin|Kopeikin]] is
absurd ([[captain-kopeikin|Kopeikin]] has one arm and one leg; [[chichikov|Chichikov]] is conspicuously
whole). Gogol uses this misidentification to satirize the town's
desperation to fit the inexplicable [[chichikov|Chichikov]] into a recognizable
narrative frame.
