## Overview

The travellers in "The Tour" journey through the urban landscape of the early Roman Empire — the great metropolis of [[alexandria|Alexandria]], the [[memphis|ancient Egyptian capital]]s of [[memphis|Memphis]] and [[thebes|Thebes]], Nile-side towns, [[arabian-gulf|Red Sea]] ports, and the distant cities of Arabia and Kush. This page indexes all cities and settlements by type, drawing from the novel's geography.

## Alexandria

The novel's primary setting, [[alexandria|Alexandria]], is treated in depth on its own dedicated page. As the intellectual and commercial capital of Roman Egypt under Augustus, it houses [[museum-of-alexandria|the Museum]], the [[great-harbour|Great Harbour]], the [[heptastadium|Heptastadium]], [[soma|the Soma]] of Alexander, and the diversoria where [[lucius|Lucius]] and his companions lodge. The city appears in nearly every chapter — as point of departure, place of return, and the gravitational centre of the travellers' world.
