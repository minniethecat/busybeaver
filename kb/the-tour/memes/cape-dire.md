# Cape Dire

Cape Dire is a headland on the [[arabian-gulf|Red Sea]] coast of Africa, notable as the site of [[caleb|Caleb]]'s branch guesthouse and the pillars of [[sesostris|Sesostris]]. It is the location of one of the novel's most dramatic scenes: the reunion of the travellers where [[lucius|Lucius]] learns that the emperor [[tiberius|Tiberius]] has confiscated all his possessions — the catastrophic news that transforms the nature of the entire tour from a pleasure expedition into a struggle for survival.

## The Guesthouse

When the travellers arrive at Cape Dire after their Ethiopian expedition, [[caleb|Caleb]] reveals a surprise:

> "Most noble lord, I wished to keep it for you as a surprise and would not tell you before, but this little diversorium at Cape Dire, overlooking my beloved native land, belongs to *us*, to [[ghizla|Ghizla]] and me, and is a small branch of our great [[hermes-house|Hermes House]] at [[alexandria|Alexandria]]; and tonight you need no longer sleep in a tent, but will have a worthy apartment and sleep on a soft couch of skins." (027-002)

The guesthouse, while modest compared to the [[alexandria|Alexandria]] establishment, is "comfortable and clean and it has bathrooms and kitchens" (027-002). It was built for "the accommodation of any noble lords who travel from [[alexandria|Alexandria]] to the pillars of [[sesostris|Sesostris]] or from the pillars of [[sesostris|Sesostris]] to [[alexandria|Alexandria]]" (027-002).

## The Pillars of Sesostris

At Cape Dire stand "the [[obelisk|obelisks]], the shafts, the pillars of [[sesostris|Sesostris]], whose sacred writings immortalized the remembrance of the passage of the Egyptian world-ruler who for nine years had linked conquest to conquest, even to Arabia, even to Bactriana, even to India" (027-001). These monuments mark the southernmost extent of [[sesostris|Sesostris]]'s legendary conquests and serve as a recurring geographical reference point for the entire tour — the ultimate destination that [[lucius|Lucius]] had set out to see.

## The Dramatic Reunion

At Cape Dire, after weeks of separation, the overland party ([[lucius|Lucius]], [[thrasyllus|Thrasyllus]], [[caleb|Caleb]], [[cora|Cora]], [[tarrar|Tarrar]]) reunites with [[catullus|Uncle Catullus]], who has sailed separately on the [[quadrireme]]. The reunion is freighted with tension:

> "But what was the anxiety that covered the faces of all on board, who were now preparing to walk across the gangway to the jetty? Why did Uncle Catullus lift his hands on high and shake his head, pinned round so comically in his travelling-veil?" (027-007)

[[catullus|Uncle Catullus]] cannot bring himself to speak the news and delegates to [[vettius|Vettius]] and [[rufus|Rufus]] the terrible announcement:

> "My lord, our gracious emperor, Augustus [[tiberius|Tiberius]], is wroth with you … And he has confiscated all your possessions … You are penniless, my lord, for you own nothing except what your ship contains." (027-040)

The news transforms the tour. From this point forward, [[lucius|Lucius]] is no longer a wealthy Roman noble travelling for pleasure, but a ruined man dependent on the loyalty of his household and the business acumen of [[caleb|Caleb]]. Cape Dire thus marks the structural pivot of the entire narrative — the moment when the outward journey of discovery becomes an inward journey of adaptation and survival.

## Caleb's Anxiety

[[caleb|Caleb]], who has been listening at the door, turns "very pale" at the news — "for there was still a long, long, long papyrus scroll of a bill awaiting payment, for the big hunts in the Ethiopian forests!" (027-043). His mercantile mind immediately calculates the financial implications, revealing both his commercial nature and the precariousness of the travellers' position.
