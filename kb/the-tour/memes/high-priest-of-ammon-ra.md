# High Priest of Ammon-Râ

## Overview

The High Priest of Ammon-Râ is a centenarian holy man whom [[lucius|Lucius]] encounters at the temple of [[ammon-ra|Ammon-Râ]] in [[thebes|Thebes]]. Ancient and serene, he represents the purest form of Egyptian religious [[philosophy|wisdom]] in the novel — a figure who has devoted a century to the contemplation of the divine. His conversation with [[lucius|Lucius]] marks one of the novel's most spiritually significant encounters, offering counsel that transcends the syncretic temple cults of [[alexandria|Alexandria]] and [[canopus|Canopus]].

## In the Narrative

The high priest is introduced in chapter XXI, described as "the centenarian high-priest" (021-008) who approaches [[lucius|Lucius]] with the dignity of immense age. He addresses [[lucius|Lucius]] with paternal tenderness: "My son?" (021-009), and ordains his spiritual path: "Go, my son, to the House of the Sun" (021-011). He leads [[lucius|Lucius]] to the temple of the "god with the bull's head" (021-012), where he speaks of deep theological matters.

Later, [[lucius|Lucius]] reflects on the priest's words during a moment of solitary contemplation: "He went down into his own thoughts, down to the old high-priest of [[ammon-ra|Ammon-Râ]], who spoke to me" (023-015), suggesting that the encounter has left a lasting impression on his spiritual consciousness.
