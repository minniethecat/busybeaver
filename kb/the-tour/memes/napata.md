# Napata

Napata is an Ethiopian city on the upper Nile, encountered by [[lucius|Lucius]] and his party as they travel south beyond the Egyptian frontier. It is the first "entirely native, barbarian town" they see — a place where "there was not a word of Latin spoken" and the travellers could not have made themselves understood without [[tarrar|Tarrar]] and [[caleb|Caleb]] (025-015).

## The Emerald-Mines and Topaz-Rocks

Napata's most striking feature is its mineral wealth. As the caravan approaches, the travellers encounter "the Ethiopian emerald-mines and topaz-rocks" (025-014):

> "The emerald-mines were like marvellous green, magic caves, in which thousands of slaves were working; the topaz-rocks were visited at night time: the stones, because of their yellow sheen, are almost invisible by day, but glitter in the dark night; then little metal tubes are planted over each stone that is found, so as to make it easier to recognize the stones in the daytime and to grub them out. In former ages, the Egyptian and Ethiopian kings maintained separate guards around these mines and rocks." (025-014)

The mining operation described is on a massive scale — thousands of slaves labouring in "marvellous green, magic caves" — and the ingenious nighttime prospecting method demonstrates sophisticated mineral-extraction technology.

## The Town

At Napata the travellers encounter Ethiopian civilization for the first time:

- The inhabitants "wore no clothing save the skin of some animal round their waist" (025-015).
- They were notably small in stature: "Everything about them was small: their houses built of palm-leaves and bamboo, their oxen and goats and sheep" (025-015).
- [[thrasyllus|Thrasyllus]] speculated "that the legend of the Pygmaei, or nations of dwarfs, had originated because of Ethiopia" (025-015).
- The natives' diet consisted mainly of "vegetables and fruits, or young shoots of trees, or they would suck reed-stalks and [[lotus|lotus-flower]]s. But they also took blood and milk and cheese; and there was no other food" (025-016).

## The Liqueurs of Napata

Napata is famous throughout Egypt for its date-wines and topaz-yellow liqueurs, which are served at the finest [[alexandria|Alexandria]]n entertainments. At the festival banquet in [[alexandria|Alexandria]], "the intoxicating liqueurs of Napata" flow alongside [[lake-mareotis|Mareotis]] wines (010-066). [[caleb|Caleb]] boasts that "they're drinking *our* wines … all the way to Napata!" (013-024).

However, when the travellers finally reach Napata and Ethiopia, [[lucius|Lucius]] discovers a commercial deception:

> "Lucius discovered that the famous date-wines and topaz-yellow liqueurs of Napata and Meroe were a sheer hoax, that there was no wine or liqueur whatever distilled in Ethiopia and that the delicious drinks with which Master [[ghizla|Ghizla]] and Caleb had provided him and Uncle Catullus came from no farther than [[lake-mareotis|Lake Mareotis]] at Alexandria!" (025-016)

This revelation adds a layer of irony to [[caleb|Caleb]]'s earlier boasts and reveals the mercantile savvy of the [[saba|Saba]]ean brothers — the "exotic" Ethiopian liqueurs are in fact local [[alexandria|Alexandria]]n products, rebranded with an exotic provenance to command higher prestige and price.

## Route to Meroë

From Napata, the travellers continue south to [[meroe|Meroë]], the Ethiopian capital on the island of the same name (025-016).
