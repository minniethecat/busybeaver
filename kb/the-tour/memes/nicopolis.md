## Overview

Nicopolis ("City of Victory") was an eastern suburb of [[alexandria|Alexandria]], founded or re-dedicated by Augustus following his victory over Mark Antony and [[cleopatra|Cleopatra]] in 30 BC. Situated on the road from [[alexandria|Alexandria]] to [[canopus|Canopus]], it was best known for its amphitheatre and stadium, where the quinquennial games (held every four years) were instituted in honor of the emperor [005-014]. The settlement lay along the broader of two roads leading east from [[alexandria|Alexandria]] [008-015].

## In the Narrative

As the narrator travels eastward from [[alexandria|Alexandria]], Nicopolis appears along the route past the Nile's edge. The broader road passes Nicopolis on the way to [[canopus|Canopus]] [008-015]. A character points out the suburb to [[catullus|Catullus]], noting its amphitheatre and stadium [008-017]. The place functions in the narrative primarily as a geographical marker — a recognizable landmark along the eastern approach — rather than a site of extended action.

## Historical Context

Nicopolis was established by Augustus after the Roman conquest of Egypt. The quinquennial games held there were modeled on the Olympic Games and were part of Augustus's broader program of founding Nicopoleis ("Victory Cities") across the eastern empire to commemorate his triumphs. The suburb served both as a recreational destination for [[alexandria|Alexandria]]ns and as an imperial statement of Roman dominance over the Ptolemaic capital.
