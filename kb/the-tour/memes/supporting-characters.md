## Overview

Beyond the central travellers — [[lucius|Lucius]], [[catullus|Catullus]], [[thrasyllus|Thrasyllus]], and [[caleb|Caleb]] — "The Tour" is peopled by a rich supporting cast: Egyptian priests and temple servants, Roman officials, Greek scholars, [[saba|Saba]]ean merchants, slaves, and the ordinary inhabitants of [[alexandria|Alexandria]], [[memphis|Memphis]], and the Nile villages. This page indexes all supporting characters.

## The Novel's Social World

Louis Couperus populates his narrative with a cross-section of early Imperial society. Supporting characters are not merely background — they embody the ethnic diversity, social hierarchy, and religious complexity of Roman Egypt. Slaves serve Greek masters, Egyptian priests interpret [[oracle|oracles]] for Roman visitors, and [[saba|Saba]]ean traders carry goods across the [[arabian-gulf|Red Sea]].
