# Carus

## Overview

Carus the Cypriote is an offstage antagonist in *The Tour*, a sailor with whom [[ilia|Ilia]] — [[lucius|Lucius]]'s beloved slave — allegedly fled from the villa at [[baiae|Baiae]]. He never appears directly in the narrative but is invoked by the household as the explanation for [[ilia|Ilia]]'s disappearance. Whether [[ilia|Ilia]] truly left with Carus, or whether this is merely the assumption of the household, remains ambiguous throughout the novel.

## In the Narrative

Carus is first mentioned when the household speculates about [[ilia|Ilia]]'s disappearance: "She ran away with Carus the Cypriote, the sailor! Every one knew it in the house … and she ran away with Carus the Cypriote!" (002-012-013). The household's conviction is absolute: "She left a master like [[lucius|Lucius]] for a scoundrel like Carus!" (002-013). The name "Carus the Cypriote" recurs with the rhythmic insistence of gossip.

Later, the narrator confirms that Carus was known to have been present near the time of [[ilia|Ilia]]'s disappearance: "It was the Cypriote, the sailor, Carus … who, shortly before [[ilia|Ilia]]'s disappearance, had been seen at [[baiae|Baiae]]" (018-003). [[lucius|Lucius]] himself acknowledges the possibility — or the accusation — when he says simply: "That Carus stole [[ilia|Ilia]] from me" (023-012).

The entire question — Did [[ilia|Ilia]] go willingly? Was she taken? Is she dead? — revolves around the shadowy figure of Carus, who remains as absent from the narrative as [[ilia|Ilia]] herself.
