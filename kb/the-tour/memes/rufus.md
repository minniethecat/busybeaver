# Rufus

## Overview

Rufus is [[lucius|Lucius]]'s under-steward, subordinate to [[vettius|Vettius]] in managing the household's travel arrangements. He appears briefly during the arrival at [[alexandria|Alexandria]], where he and [[vettius|Vettius]] receive the travellers after having "worked to good purpose, conveying furniture, boxes and packing-cases on camels and mules" (003-097).

## Role in the Household

In a Roman household of the scale depicted in the narrative, the steward (Latin: *dispensator* or *vilicus*) was responsible for the day-to-day management of the *familia* — supervising slaves, provisioning the household, managing accounts, and coordinating logistics. A senior steward such as [[vettius|Vettius]] might have an under-steward such as Rufus to handle specific aspects of the operation — in this case, the complex task of transporting an entire household's furnishings from the port to the [[alexandria|Alexandria]]n residence. The division of labour between [[vettius|Vettius]] (who oversees strategy) and Rufus (who executes the physical transport of goods) reflects the hierarchical structure typical of elite Roman domestic administration. Rufus is not merely a servant but a trusted functionary: the task of "conveying furniture, boxes and packing-cases on camels and mules" (003-097) required considerable organisational skill, negotiating with local transporters, ensuring nothing was lost or damaged, and reassembling the household on arrival.

## In the Narrative

Rufus is mentioned alongside [[vettius|Vettius]] as part of the efficient domestic machinery. At the quay, "Rufus, the under-steward, had not even thought of preparing his master's own litter" before [[ghizla|Ghizla]] had already arranged [[alexandria|Alexandria]]n transport (003-024). Despite this minor lapse, Rufus has otherwise performed well: "They had worked to good purpose" (003-097). He is later described as standing among the assembled household slaves when [[lucius|Lucius]] enters the prepared triclinium (003-101). Rufus's presence in this domestic tableau — the assembled *familia* awaiting the master's inspection — underscores the hierarchical order that [[lucius|Lucius]] brings with him from Rome and transplants into the Egyptian setting.
