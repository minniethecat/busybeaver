# Berenice

Berenice (Berenice Troglodytica) was a Ptolemaic port city on the [[arabian-gulf|Red Sea]] coast of Egypt, situated on the Bay of Acathantus (modern Foul Bay). It was one of the principal [[arabian-gulf|Red Sea]] harbours of ancient Egypt, connected to the Nile by the Berenice Canal. In *The Tour*, Berenice serves as the crucial rendezvous where [[catullus|Uncle Catullus]] learns of [[lucius|Lucius]]'s ruin and where the travellers' plans are irrevocably altered.

## In the Narrative

Berenice first enters the story through [[caleb|Caleb]]'s alternative travel plan, proposed when [[catullus|Uncle Catullus]] grows weary of the Egyptian expedition and expresses reluctance to continue through Ethiopia:

> "I would propose that the thalamegus take you from Apollonopolis Magna, by the canal, to Berenice, on the Bay of Acathantus, in the Arabian Gulf. At Berenice you will meet the quadrireme, which has gone by Pelusium and the Nechao Canal and is ascending the Arabian Gulf to fetch us at the pillars of Sesostris. In this way you will do the journey without inconvenience and yet with enjoyment, for the Berenice Canal passes along the Smaragdis Mountains and they are a dream, my lord; my lord, they're a *dream*!" (024-036)

[[caleb|Caleb]]'s ulterior motive in proposing this route is that the princely apartments at the [[hermes-house|Hermes House]] in [[alexandria|Alexandria]] would remain unoccupied and could be let to Persian grandees travelling in the opposite direction — a detail that reveals his ever-present commercial calculations.

The plan is adopted: [[catullus|Uncle Catullus]] boards the thalamegus and travels from [[apollonopolis-magna|Apollonopolis Magna]] by canal to Berenice, while [[lucius|Lucius]], [[thrasyllus|Thrasyllus]], and [[caleb|Caleb]] take a barge to [[syene|Syene]] (025-001).

The critical scene at Berenice occurs when [[vettius|Vettius]] arrives with catastrophic news. Having bribed the authorities at [[pelusium|Pelusium]], [[vettius|Vettius]] slipped through the [[nechao-canal|Nechao Canal]] to [[arsinoe|Arsinoe]] and then to Berenice:

> "By bribing the authorities at Pelusium with the money that remained in my hands, I managed secretly to pass through the Nechao Canal to Arsinoe; and at Berenice we met your Uncle Catullus and informed him of the terrible news." (027-040)

This news — that Emperor [[tiberius|Tiberius]] has confiscated all of [[lucius|Lucius]]'s possessions — transforms the narrative. Berenice thus marks the geographic point where the leisurely tour of Egypt becomes a desperate struggle for survival.

The name Berenice reappears in the closing chapters when [[caleb|Caleb]] sends his letter to [[lucius|Lucius]] via a merchant ship also named *Berenice*, whose master hails from [[alexandria|Alexandria]] (029-001, 030-004) — a subtle reminder of the port's ongoing role in the commercial networks of the [[arabian-gulf|Red Sea]].

## The Berenice Canal

The canal connecting the Nile at [[apollonopolis-magna|Apollonopolis Magna]] to Berenice on the [[arabian-gulf|Red Sea]] was a vital trade artery. Its route passed along the Smaragdis Mountains (the "Emerald Mountains" of the Eastern Desert), celebrated by [[caleb|Caleb]] for their dreamlike beauty. This canal, together with the [[nechao-canal|Nechao Canal]] connecting the Nile to the [[arabian-gulf|Red Sea]] via [[arsinoe|Arsinoë]], formed the infrastructure of Egypt's [[arabian-gulf|Red Sea]] trade under the Ptolemies and Romans.
