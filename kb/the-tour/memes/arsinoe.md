# Arsinoë

Arsinoë is a port city on the [[arabian-gulf|Red Sea]] coast of Egypt, situated at the eastern end of the [[nechao-canal|Nechao Canal]] (the ancient canal connecting the Nile to the [[arabian-gulf|Red Sea]]). It serves as a crucial logistical waypoint in the closing chapters of the narrative, enabling [[vettius|Vettius]] to save [[lucius|Lucius]]'s last remaining asset — the [[quadrireme]].

## In the Narrative

Arsinoë appears in the account of how [[vettius|Vettius]] preserved [[lucius|Lucius]]'s [[quadrireme]] from imperial confiscation:

> "If I had not succeeded in keeping [[tiberius|Tiberius]]' displeasure secret by means of a precipitate flight and by continuing to drift about in the Great Sea and the Arabian Gulf, your quadrireme also would have been seized at Alexandria and you would now have been without your ship, without your rowers, without your slaves, without a single penny. By bribing the authorities at [[pelusium|Pelusium]] with the money that remained in my hands, I managed secretly to pass through the Nechao Canal to Arsinoe; and at [[berenice|Berenice]] we met your Uncle Catullus and informed him of the terrible news." (027-040)

The canal passage from [[pelusium|Pelusium]] (on the Mediterranean) through to Arsinoë (on the [[arabian-gulf|Red Sea]]) was the crucial escape route that saved the quadrireme — and by extension, [[lucius|Lucius]]'s last remaining asset. This transit demonstrates the strategic importance of the [[nechao-canal|Nechao Canal]] as a military and commercial artery connecting the Mediterranean world to the [[arabian-gulf|Red Sea]] and Indian Ocean trade routes.

## Name and Identity

The name Arsinoë was used for several cities in Ptolemaic Egypt, named after Arsinoë II, the sister-wife of [[ptolemy|Ptolemy]] II Philadelphus. This can cause ambiguity:

- **Arsinoë on the [[arabian-gulf|Red Sea]]** (this page): the port at the eastern terminus of the [[nechao-canal|Nechao Canal]], near modern Suez.
- **Arsinoë-Crocodilopolis**: a city in the Fayum, also mentioned in the text (024-006) when [[lucius|Lucius]] travels "a hundred stadia farther, to Arsinoe-Crocodilopolis."
- **Temple of [[aphrodite|Aphrodite]] Arsinoë**: a shrine near [[canopus|Canopus]] on the Mediterranean coast, dedicated to the deified queen (008-017).

The multiplicity of Arsinoë-named locations reflects the Ptolemaic dynasty's practice of honouring its queens through civic nomenclature, and the name's recurrence across Egypt's Mediterranean, riverine, and [[arabian-gulf|Red Sea]] regions maps the geographic reach of Ptolemaic infrastructure.
