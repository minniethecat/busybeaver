## Overview

The Egypt of "The Tour" is a land of innumerable gods. As the travellers journey from [[alexandria|Alexandria]] to the first cataract, they encounter the cults of [[isis|Isis]], [[serapis|Serapis]], [[apis|Apis]], Thoth, [[ammon-ra|Ammon]], and many lesser-known deities. This page indexes all Egyptian deity concepts that appear in the novel.

## The Divine Landscape

Egyptian religion in the Roman period was both ancient and adaptive. Traditional cults like that of [[isis|Isis]] had spread across the Mediterranean, while new syncretic forms like [[serapis|Serapis]] blended Greek and Egyptian theology. The novel captures this moment of religious transition: the old gods still receive their offerings, but the questions travellers bring to their [[oracle|oracles]] — about fate, love, and meaning — are timelessly human.
