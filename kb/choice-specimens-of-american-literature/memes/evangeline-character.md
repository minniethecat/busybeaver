## Character Overview

Evangeline Bellefontaine is the heroine of Longfellow's epic poem "Evangeline: A Tale of Acadie" (1847). Separated from her fiance on her wedding day during the Acadian deportation, she spends her life wandering across America searching for him, finally finding him on his deathbed in Philadelphia.

## Role in American Literature

Evangeline embodies faithfulness, endurance, and the tragedy of displacement. Longfellow's poem was immensely popular and contributed to the romanticization of Acadian history.
