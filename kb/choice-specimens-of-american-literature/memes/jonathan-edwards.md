# Jonathan Edwards

## Biography

Jonathan Edwards (1703-1758) was an American sermon, active during the Colonial / Great Awakening period. Theologian and philosopher, a central figure of the Great Awakening, author of "Sinners in the Hands of an Angry God" and "Freedom of the Will."

## Literary Career

Associated with the Puritan / Evangelical movement, Jonathan Edwards worked primarily in sermon, theology, philosophy. His/her writings contributed to the development of a distinctly American literary voice during a formative period in the nation's cultural history.

## Critical Reception

Jonathan Edwards has been recognized as one of the significant voices in American literary history. Contemporary reception reflected the literary standards of the Colonial / Great Awakening era, and modern scholarship continues to study Jonathan Edwards's contributions to Puritan / Evangelical and American letters more broadly.
