# Henry Wadsworth Longfellow

## Biography

Henry Wadsworth Longfellow (1807-1882) was an American poetry, active during the 19th Century period. The most popular American poet of the 19th century, author of "Evangeline," "The Song of Hiawatha," and "Paul Revere's Ride."

## Literary Career

Associated with the Fireside Poets movement, Henry Wadsworth Longfellow worked primarily in poetry, translation. His/her writings contributed to the development of a distinctly American literary voice during a formative period in the nation's cultural history.

## Critical Reception

Henry Wadsworth Longfellow has been recognized as one of the significant voices in American literary history. Contemporary reception reflected the literary standards of the 19th Century era, and modern scholarship continues to study Henry Wadsworth Longfellow's contributions to Fireside Poets and American letters more broadly.
