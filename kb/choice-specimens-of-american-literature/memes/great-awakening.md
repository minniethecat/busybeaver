## Overview

The Great Awakening was a wave of religious revivals in the 1730s and 1740s. Preachers like Jonathan Edwards drew enormous crowds with emotional sermons emphasizing personal conversion and divine grace.

## Literary Significance

The Awakening produced powerful American prose. Edwards's sermons combined rigorous Calvinist theology with vivid imagery. The revival democratized religious authority, expanding the range of American voices in print.
