## Overview

New England is the birthplace of American literature. From the Puritan sermons of John Winthrop and Jonathan Edwards through the Transcendentalist essays of Ralph Waldo Emerson to the dark romances of Nathaniel Hawthorne, New England provided both setting and intellectual context for foundational American texts.

## Literary Significance

The region's combination of Puritan theological traditions, educational institutions (Harvard, Yale), and reformist energy produced a concentration of literary activity unmatched elsewhere in early America.
