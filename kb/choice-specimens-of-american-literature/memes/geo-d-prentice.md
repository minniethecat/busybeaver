## Biography

George D. Prentice (1802–1869) was an American journalist and poet. Born in Connecticut, he moved to
Kentucky in 1830 to edit the *Louisville Journal*, which under his leadership became one of the most
influential newspapers in the American West. Prentice was known for his sharp wit, political commentary,
and literary sensibility.

## Literary Career

Prentice is represented in *Choice Specimens of American Literature* by a selection from his poem
"The Mammoth Cave" (001-206). The poem reflects the Romantic fascination with the natural wonders of
the American landscape. Prentice's verse is characterized by lyrical grace and a keen eye for natural
imagery:

> The snow-flake that the cliff receives —
> The diamonds of the showers —
> Spring's tender blossoms, buds, and leaves —
> The sisterhood of flowers —
> Morn's early beam, eve's balmy breeze —
> Her purity define; —
> But Ida's dearer far than these
> To this fond breast of mine. (068-034)

## Critical Reception

As editor of the *Louisville Journal*, Prentice exercised considerable influence over literary culture
in the antebellum West. His newspaper published original poetry and literary criticism, helping to
foster a distinct Western literary identity.
