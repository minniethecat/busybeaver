# John Winthrop

## Biography

John Winthrop (1587-1649) was an American sermon, active during the Colonial period. First governor of the Massachusetts Bay Colony, author of "A Model of Christian Charity" and key figure in early American Puritan literature.

## Literary Career

Associated with the Puritan movement, John Winthrop worked primarily in sermon, journal, political writing. His/her writings contributed to the development of a distinctly American literary voice during a formative period in the nation's cultural history.

## Critical Reception

John Winthrop has been recognized as one of the significant voices in American literary history. Contemporary reception reflected the literary standards of the Colonial era, and modern scholarship continues to study John Winthrop's contributions to Puritan and American letters more broadly.
