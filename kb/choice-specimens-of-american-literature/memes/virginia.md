## Overview

Virginia, the first permanent English settlement in America (Jamestown, 1607), was the largest of the original colonies. Its planter aristocracy produced revolutionary leaders whose writings shaped American political thought.

## Literary Significance

Virginia's contribution to American letters includes Revolutionary oratory (Patrick Henry), political philosophy (Jefferson's Declaration of Independence), and historical writing (William Stith).
