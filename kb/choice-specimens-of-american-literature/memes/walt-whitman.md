# Walt Whitman

## Biography

Walt Whitman (1819-1892) was an American poetry, active during the 19th Century period. Poet of democracy and the self, author of "Leaves of Grass" (1855), a revolutionary work in American poetry.

## Literary Career

Associated with the Transcendentalism / Free Verse movement, Walt Whitman worked primarily in poetry, essay, journalism. His/her writings contributed to the development of a distinctly American literary voice during a formative period in the nation's cultural history.

## Critical Reception

Walt Whitman has been recognized as one of the significant voices in American literary history. Contemporary reception reflected the literary standards of the 19th Century era, and modern scholarship continues to study Walt Whitman's contributions to Transcendentalism / Free Verse and American letters more broadly.
