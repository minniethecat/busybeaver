# Benjamin Franklin

## Biography

Benjamin Franklin (1706-1790) was an American essay, active during the Colonial / Revolutionary period. Founding Father, printer, scientist, and diplomat, author of "Poor Richard's Almanack" and the "Autobiography."

## Literary Career

Associated with the Enlightenment movement, Benjamin Franklin worked primarily in essay, autobiography, political writing. His/her writings contributed to the development of a distinctly American literary voice during a formative period in the nation's cultural history.

## Critical Reception

Benjamin Franklin has been recognized as one of the significant voices in American literary history. Contemporary reception reflected the literary standards of the Colonial / Revolutionary era, and modern scholarship continues to study Benjamin Franklin's contributions to Enlightenment and American letters more broadly.
