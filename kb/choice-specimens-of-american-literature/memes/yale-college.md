## Overview

Yale College, founded in 1701 in New Haven, Connecticut, was the third college established in the American colonies. It became a major center of theological education and, by the 19th century, a leading institution for American literature and the arts.

## Literary Significance

Yale educated Jonathan Edwards (class of 1720), Timothy Dwight (who became its president), and generations of American writers and clergy. The "Yale school" of theology and letters shaped New England intellectual culture for over a century.
