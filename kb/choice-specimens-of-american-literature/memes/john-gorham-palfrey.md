# John Gorham Palfrey

## Biography

John Gorham Palfrey (1796–1881) was an American historian, Unitarian minister, and politician. Born in Boston, Massachusetts, he graduated from Harvard College in 1815 and from Harvard Divinity School in 1818. He served as pastor of the Brattle Street Church in Boston before turning to academic and literary pursuits. Palfrey held the Dexter Professorship of Sacred Literature at Harvard and served as editor of the *North American Review*, one of the most influential periodicals of the era. He also represented Massachusetts in the United States House of Representatives as a Free-Soil candidate. His most enduring contribution, however, was his multi-volume *History of New England*, a work of careful scholarship that remained authoritative well into the twentieth century.

## Literary Career

Palfrey's literary career was defined by his commitment to historical scholarship. His *History of New England* (1858–1890), published in five volumes, was a landmark achievement — a meticulously researched account of the region from its earliest colonial settlements through the seventeenth century. Palfrey also published *Lectures on the Evidences of Christianity* and numerous articles and reviews in the *North American Review*. His prose style is characterized by clarity, moral seriousness, and a deep sympathy for the Puritan founders of New England — qualities that distinguish his historical writing from both the dry annalistic tradition and the more romanticized treatments of his contemporaries.

## Critical Reception

Palfrey's *History of New England* was praised by contemporaries for its thoroughness and judicious tone. Twentieth-century historians, while inevitably superseding some of his interpretations, have acknowledged his foundational role in establishing the scholarly study of early New England. His work is notable for its sympathetic yet balanced treatment of the Puritan experiment, avoiding both hagiography and skepticism. As an early editor of the *North American Review*, Palfrey helped shape the development of American literary and intellectual culture in the antebellum period.
