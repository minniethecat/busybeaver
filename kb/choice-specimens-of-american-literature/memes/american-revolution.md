## Overview

The American Revolution (1775-1783) was the conflict through which thirteen British colonies won independence and established the United States. It was a political revolution grounded in Enlightenment ideas of natural rights and republican self-government.

## Literary Significance

The Revolution generated foundational American texts: Patrick Henry's oratory, the Declaration of Independence, the Federalist Papers, and the correspondence of Washington, Adams, and Jefferson.
