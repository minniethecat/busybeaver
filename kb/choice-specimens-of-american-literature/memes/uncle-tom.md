## Character Overview

Uncle Tom is the central figure of Harriet Beecher Stowe's "Uncle Tom's Cabin" (1852). An enslaved man of deep Christian faith, Tom is sold away from his family and passes through a series of owners, maintaining his dignity and faith. His death at the hands of the brutal Simon Legree becomes a martyrdom that galvanized anti-slavery sentiment.

## Role in American Literature

Uncle Tom is arguably the most consequential fictional character in American history. The novel moved millions of readers and intensified the sectional conflict that led to the Civil War.
