# Harriet Beecher Stowe

## Biography

Harriet Beecher Stowe (1811-1896) was an American fiction, active during the Antebellum / Civil War period. Abolitionist author of "Uncle Tom's Cabin" (1852), the most influential American novel of the 19th century.

## Literary Career

Associated with the Abolitionist Literature movement, Harriet Beecher Stowe worked primarily in fiction, essay. His/her writings contributed to the development of a distinctly American literary voice during a formative period in the nation's cultural history.

## Critical Reception

Harriet Beecher Stowe has been recognized as one of the significant voices in American literary history. Contemporary reception reflected the literary standards of the Antebellum / Civil War era, and modern scholarship continues to study Harriet Beecher Stowe's contributions to Abolitionist Literature and American letters more broadly.
