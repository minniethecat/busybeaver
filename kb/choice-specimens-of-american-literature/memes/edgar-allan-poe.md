# Edgar Allan Poe

## Biography

Edgar Allan Poe (1809-1849) was an American poetry, active during the Antebellum period. Poet, short story writer, and literary critic, known for "The Raven," "The Tell-Tale Heart," and his pioneering work in detective fiction.

## Literary Career

Associated with the Dark Romanticism / Gothic movement, Edgar Allan Poe worked primarily in poetry, fiction, literary criticism. His/her writings contributed to the development of a distinctly American literary voice during a formative period in the nation's cultural history.

## Critical Reception

Edgar Allan Poe has been recognized as one of the significant voices in American literary history. Contemporary reception reflected the literary standards of the Antebellum era, and modern scholarship continues to study Edgar Allan Poe's contributions to Dark Romanticism / Gothic and American letters more broadly.
