# Charles Brockden Brown

**Charles Brockden Brown** (1771–1810) was an [[america|American]] novelist, historian, and editor, recognized as the first professional [[america|American]] author and the earliest significant novelist of the [[america|United States]].

## Literary Career

Brown began his career as a novelist at the close of the eighteenth century. (044-020) His early works were tentative contributions to [[america|American]] fiction, which was still in its formative stages during this period.

His major novels include *Wieland* (1798), *Arthur Mervyn* (1799–1800), *Ormond* (1799), and *Edgar Huntly* (1799). These works are noted for their Gothic elements, psychological complexity, and engagement with the intellectual currents of the early republic.

## Influence

Brown is often regarded as a precursor to later [[america|American]] Gothic and Romantic writers. His use of [[america|American]] settings and psychological themes influenced authors such as [[Edgar Allan Poe]] and [[Nathaniel Hawthorne]].
