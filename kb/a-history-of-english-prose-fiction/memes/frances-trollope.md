## Literary Significance

Mrs. Trollope, the mother of a more celebrated son, contributed largely to the English domestic novel (042-009). She is counted among the considerable group of women novelists who made the study of domestic life the most considerable subdivision of the [[novel|English novel]], a department for which women were particularly fitted and in which they were eminently successful (042-008). Her son, [[Anthony Trollope]], became one of the most prominent [[the-nineteenth-century-novel|Victorian novel]]ists, achieving for the novel a recognized social influence and respectability (040-028).
