## Life

Charles Kingsley was born in Holne, Devon, in 1819. He was educated at King's College [[london|London]] and Magdalene College, Cambridge, and was ordained as a clergyman in the Church of [[england|England]]. Kingsley served as rector of Eversley in Hampshire and later became a canon of Chester and Westminster. He was also a professor of modern history at Cambridge. His Christian faith, his passion for social reform, and his love of the outdoors all found expression in his fiction.

## Literary Significance

Charles Kingsley was among the foremost [[the-nineteenth-century-novel|Victorian novel]]ists who elevated the novel to a position of respectability and dignity. In the hands of writers such as Scott, Austen, Dickens, Thackeray, as Charles Kingsley, and as [[anthony-trollope|Mr. Anthony Trollope]], the novel achieved for itself a position of respectability and dignity (040-028). Kingsley's works span both historical fiction and the novel of purpose.

In historical fiction, Kingsley's *Hypatia* and *Westward Ho!* are among the most prominent of recent historical novels (046-003). The latter aimed at describing the time of Elizabeth. As a novelist of social purpose, Kingsley described the grievances of mechanics in *Alton Locke*, a work in which the artistic elements are much subordinated to the didactic (046-010).

However, Kingsley was not always a reliable critic of fiction. Regarding [[henry-brooke|Henry Brooke]]'s *The Fool of Quality*, it must be admitted that Charles Kingsley's judgment was seriously at fault (036-004). He saw both its qualities and its faults, but he did not realize that a good purpose will not make up for a poor execution (036-004). Kingsley wrote an enthusiastic preface for a reprint of the novel, claiming he had learnt from it more which is pure, sacred, and eternal, than from any which has been published since Spenser's *Fairy Queen* (036-004).

## Major Works

- **Alton Locke** (1850) — A social-problem novel depicting the struggles of working-class mechanics and the Chartist movement; Kingsley's most explicitly didactic work.
- **Hypatia** (1853) — A historical novel set in fifth-century Alexandria, dramatizing the conflict between early Christianity and classical philosophy.
- **Westward Ho!** (1855) — An Elizabethan historical adventure novel following the exploits of English seafarers in the age of exploration.
- **The Water-Babies** (1863) — A children's fantasy novel with strong moral and social themes, satirizing child labor and advocating for environmental reform.
