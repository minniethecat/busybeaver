# William Black

**William Black** (1841–1898) was a [[scottish-fiction|Scottish novel]]ist and journalist of the late Victorian period.

## Literary Career

Black was among the writers who followed the tradition of Sir [[walter-scott|Walter Scott]] in depicting [[scotland|Scottish]] life and manners in fiction. (041-015) His novels contributed to the regional fiction tradition in late 19th-century British literature.

His works include *A Daughter of Heth* (1871), *The Strange Adventures of a Phaeton* (1872), and *A Princess of Thule* (1873), which were popular in their time for their romantic treatment of [[scotland|Scottish]] and Hebridean settings.

## Context

Black was a contemporary of [[Margaret Oliphant]], and both were noted for their contributions to the [[scotland|Scottish]] regional novel tradition. His fiction appeared during a period when regional and dialect literature was gaining prominence across [[england|Britain]].
