## Works

Thomas Love Peacock was an [[novel|English novel]]ist best known for his satirical "conversation novels" including *Headlong Hall* (1816), *Nightmare Abbey* (1818), and *Crotchet Castle* (1831). His works satirize intellectual and literary fashions of the day through witty dialogue and eccentric characters.

## Reception in Tuckerman

Tuckerman's mention of Peacock is minimal; he appears in a footnote listing contributors "to the English domestic novel" alongside other nineteenth-century writers (044-015).
