# John Banim

## Literary Significance

John Banim (1798–1842) was an [[irish-fiction|Irish novel]]ist who, together with [[Samuel Lover]] and [[William Carleton]], contributed to the tradition of [[ireland|Irish]] fiction in the early nineteenth century. Tuckerman notes that "the novels of [[samuel-lover|Samuel Lover]] and of John Banim are still well known" (042-004). He was among the writers who, following [[Maria Edgeworth]]'s pioneering depictions, studied and portrayed [[ireland|Irish]] life and manners in prose fiction. (042-004)
