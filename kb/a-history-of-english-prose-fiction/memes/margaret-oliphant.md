## Life

Margaret Oliphant Wilson Oliphant (1828–1897) was a [[scottish-fiction|Scottish novel]]ist, biographer, and literary critic of extraordinary productivity. Born in Wallyford, near Edinburgh, she published her first novel in 1849 and went on to write more than one hundred books, including novels, biographies, histories, and literary criticism. Following her husband's early death, she supported her extended family entirely through her writing. Oliphant's career spanned the entire Victorian period, and her work reflects the evolving tastes and concerns of [[the-nineteenth-century-novel|nineteenth-century fiction]].

## Literary Significance

Following the great Sir [[walter-scott|Walter Scott]] in the description of [[scotland|Scottish]] life and manners, many well-known writers contributed to the regional tradition. The talents of [[susan-ferrier|Miss Ferrier]], of Mrs. Oliphant, and of Mr. [[william-black|William Black]] kept up the interest which the world learned to take in every thing appertaining to the land which Sir [[walter-scott|Walter Scott]] taught it to know and love so well (041-015). Oliphant thus belongs to the distinguished line of [[scotland|Scottish]] regional novelists who followed in Scott's wake, bringing the distinctive manners, speech, and landscapes of [[scotland|Scotland]] to a wide English-speaking readership.

## Major Works

- **_The Chronicles of Carlingford_** (1863–1876) — A series of novels set in a provincial English town, often compared to [[anthony-trollope|Anthony Trollope]]'s Barsetshire series for their acute observation of clerical and community life.
- **_Salem Chapel_** (1863) — One of the most admired of the Carlingford novels, depicting the struggles of a young dissenting minister.
- **_Miss Marjoribanks_** (1866) — A comic masterpiece of the Carlingford series, centered on the indomitable Lucilla Marjoribanks.
- **_The Beleaguered City_** (1880) — An unusual supernatural tale that influenced later writers of ghost stories.
