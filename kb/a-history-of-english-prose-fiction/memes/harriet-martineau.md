# Harriet Martineau

Harriet Martineau (1802–1876) was an English social theorist and novelist who made very successful use of fiction in conveying her ideas on political economy. (046-007)

## Life and Works

Martineau's most notable contribution to fiction was her *Illustrations of Political Economy* (1832–1834), a series of tales that dramatized the principles of classical economics for a general readership. This work placed her within the tradition of the [[novel-of-purpose|Novel of Purpose]], where fiction served as a vehicle for social, political, or religious argument. (046-007)

Tuckerman notes that Martineau's works belong to a category of fiction "written in the interest of religious sects or special tenets," alongside writers of the early Victorian period such as Elizabeth M. Sewell, who advocated High Church doctrines. (046-007)
