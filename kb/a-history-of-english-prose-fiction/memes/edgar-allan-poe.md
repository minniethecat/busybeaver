# Edgar Allan Poe

## Literary Significance

Edgar Allan Poe (1809–1849) was an [[america|American]] writer whose short stories exercised a significant influence on the development of fiction. Tuckerman notes that Poe's "'Tales of the Grotesque and the Arabesque,' are remarkable for intensity and vividness of conception, combined with a circumstantial invention almost equal to that of [[daniel-defoe|Defoe]]" (045-011). This comparison to [[Daniel Defoe]]'s celebrated realism places Poe among the notable [[america|American]] contributors to prose fiction in the nineteenth century.
