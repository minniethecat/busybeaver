# Tobias Smollett

Tobias Smollett (1721–1771) was, with [[Henry Fielding|Henry Fielding]]
and [[Samuel Richardson|Samuel Richardson]], one of the three founding masters
of the eighteenth-century English [[novel]].
Tuckerman groups "Fielding, Smollett, and the other novelists" as the writers who described
the full range of eighteenth-century life with an unflinching eye (033-006).

Where Richardson explored the inner life of sentiment and Fielding the broad comedy of manners,
Smollett brought to English fiction a picaresque energy, a surgeon's eye for physical detail,
and a [[scotland|Scottish]] outsider's satirical perspective on English society.

## Life

Smollett was born in Dalquhurn, Dunbartonshire, [[scotland|Scotland]], and studied medicine
at the University of Glasgow before moving to [[london|London]] in 1739.
He served as a surgeon's mate in the Royal Navy during the War of Jenkins' Ear,
an experience that provided the raw material for his first novel, *Roderick Random*.

Smollett "entered the field" of fiction at a moment when [[Samuel Richardson|Richardson]]
and [[Henry Fielding|Fielding]] had already "established" the novel in popularity (031-009).
He supplemented his novel-writing with journalism, translation, and historical compilation.

## Literary Significance

Smollett is, in Tuckerman's judgment, "plainly an imitator" of the Fielding model,
yet he brought distinctive qualities to the form (031-010).
He presents a "different group of characters" — sailors, Scotsmen, provincial adventurers —
absent from Richardson's drawing rooms and Fielding's country estates (031-011).

His gift for the picaresque drives novels that follow a young hero
through a succession of grotesque characters and absurd adventures.
The "scene of misery and degradation" that Smollett mingled with comic incident
gave his fiction a dark edge unusual in eighteenth-century comic narrative (032-001).

Of all Smollett's works, *Humphry Clinker* is universally considered his masterpiece (031-013).
Written in his final year, it abandons the single-hero picaresque structure for an epistolary form
in which five correspondents each record their impressions of a tour through [[england|England]] and [[scotland|Scotland]].

Smollett inserted the scandalous memoirs of Lady Vane into *Peregrine Pickle* (032-002, 032-009).
That he could "declare that the miserable" circumstances were true
demonstrated the novel's growing power as a vehicle for social truth-telling (032-003).

## Major Works

- **Roderick Random** (1748) — A picaresque novel drawing on Smollett's naval experiences.
- **Peregrine Pickle** (1751) — A comic novel interweaving the adventures of Peregrine
  with the scandalous memoirs of Lady Vane (032-009).
- **Ferdinand Count Fathom** (1753) — An experiment in villain-as-hero.
- **Humphry Clinker** (1771) — Smollett's masterpiece, an epistolary novel (031-013).
