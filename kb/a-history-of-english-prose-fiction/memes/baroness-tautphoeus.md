The Baroness Tautphoeus (1807–1893), born Jemima Montgomery in [[ireland|Ireland]], was a novelist who achieved distinction for her portrayals of English and German life. Tuckerman singles her out for particular praise, noting that she "described English and German life in the particularly fascinating novels, 'Quits,' 'At Odds,' and 'The Initials'" (042-010). Her name also appears in the index to the volume (048-001).

In 1838 Jemima Montgomery married Cajetan Josef von Tautphoeus, a Bavarian nobleman, and thereafter lived primarily in Germany. Her experience of both British and German society gave her a unique vantage point from which to compare the manners, customs, and social conventions of the two cultures—a theme that runs through all her major works.

Her three principal novels all explore the contrasts between English and Continental life. *The Initials* (1850), her most celebrated work, is a story of German family life told through the letters and journals of its characters. *Quits* (1857) and *At Odds* (1863) continued in a similar vein, combining courtship plots with sharp social observation. Tuckerman's description of her work as "particularly fascinating" reflects the esteem in which she was held by contemporaries for her cross-cultural insight and narrative skill.
