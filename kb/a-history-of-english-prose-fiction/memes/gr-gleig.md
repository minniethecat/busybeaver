# G.R. Gleig

George Robert Gleig (1796–1888) was a [[scottish-fiction|Scottish novel]]ist, military writer, and chaplain-general to the British Army.

## Life

Gleig served in the British Army during the Peninsular War before taking holy orders. He became chaplain-general to the forces and was a prolific author. (044-011)

## Works

Gleig contributed to the military novel tradition of the early 19th century, alongside authors such as [[william-godwin|William Godwin]] and Captain Chamier. His works include *The Subaltern* (1825), a fictionalized account of his Peninsular War experiences, and several historical and religious writings. (044-011)
