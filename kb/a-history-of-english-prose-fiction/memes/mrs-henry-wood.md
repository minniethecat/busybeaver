# Mrs. Henry Wood

**Mrs. Henry Wood** (1814–1887), born Ellen Price, was an [[novel|English novel]]ist of the Victorian period, best known for her bestselling sensation novel *East Lynne* (1861).

## Literary Career

Mrs. Henry Wood was the author of *East Lynne*, one of the most popular novels of the Victorian era. (042-010) The novel was a landmark work of the sensation fiction genre, combining domestic drama with elements of crime, bigamy, and disguise.

Her other notable works include *The Channings* (1862) and *Mrs. Halliburton's Troubles* (1862). Wood also owned and edited the magazine *The Argosy*, in which many of her novels were first serialized.

## Sensation Fiction

*East Lynne* was a defining work of the 1860s sensation novel movement, alongside works by [[Wilkie Collins]] (*The Woman in White*) and [[Mary Elizabeth Braddon]] (*Lady Audley's Secret*). The genre was characterized by its sensational plots, domestic settings, and challenges to Victorian social norms.
