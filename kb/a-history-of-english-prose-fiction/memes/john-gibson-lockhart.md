# John Gibson Lockhart

**John Gibson Lockhart** (1794–1854) was a [[scotland|Scottish]] writer, editor, and biographer, best known as the biographer of Sir [[Walter Scott]] and as a contributor to *Blackwood's Magazine*.

## Literary Career

Lockhart wrote the historical novel *Valerius* (1821), a work of fiction set in ancient Rome. (046-004) The novel reflects the 19th-century interest in classical antiquity as a setting for fiction, a tradition also pursued by [[Wilkie Collins]] in his novel *Antonina*.

His other fictional works include *Adam Blair* (1822), *Reginald Dalton* (1823), and *Matthew Wald* (1824). However, Lockhart's most enduring contribution to literary history is his *Memoirs of the Life of Sir [[walter-scott|Walter Scott]]* (1837–1838), a monumental biography of his father-in-law.

## Connection to Walter Scott

Lockhart married Sophia Scott, the eldest daughter of Sir [[walter-scott|Walter Scott]], in 1820. His close relationship with Scott gave him unique access to materials for his celebrated biography.
