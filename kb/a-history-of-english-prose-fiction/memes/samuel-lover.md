# Samuel Lover

## Literary Significance

Samuel Lover (1797–1868) was an [[irish-fiction|Irish novel]]ist and songwriter whose fiction contributed to the tradition of [[ireland|Irish]] prose in the early nineteenth century. Tuckerman records that "the novels of Samuel Lover and of [[John Banim]] are still well known" (042-004). Alongside [[William Carleton]] and other students of [[ireland|Irish]] life, Lover followed in the path of [[Maria Edgeworth]] in depicting [[ireland|Irish]] manners and character in fiction. (042-004)
