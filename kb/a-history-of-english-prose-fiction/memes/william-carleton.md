# William Carleton

## Literary Significance

William Carleton (1794–1869) was an [[irish-fiction|Irish novel]]ist whose work forms an important part of the nineteenth-century [[ireland|Irish]] literary tradition. Tuckerman identifies him among the "several students of [[ireland|Irish]] life" who followed [[Maria Edgeworth]]'s example, noting that his "'Traits and Stories of the [[ireland|Irish]] Peasantry,' the novels of [[samuel-lover|Samuel Lover]] and of [[john-banim|John Banim]] are still well known" (042-004). His depictions of the [[ireland|Irish]] peasantry contributed to the broader current of regional and national fiction in the period.
