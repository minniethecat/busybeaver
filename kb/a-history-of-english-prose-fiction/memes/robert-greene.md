# Robert Greene

Robert Greene (1560–1592) was an Elizabethan pamphleteer, poet, and dramatist whose works influenced the development of English prose fiction.

## Life

Born in Norfolk in 1560, Greene studied at Cambridge and received the degree of Master of Arts. After wasting his property in [[italy|Italy]] and [[spain|Spain]], he returned to [[london|London]] to earn his bread by the pen. As a pamphleteer, as a poet, and especially as a dramatist, Greene achieved a considerable reputation. (012-006)

But his improvident habits and a life of constant debauchery brought his career to a close, amidst poverty and remorse, at the early age of thirty-two. He died in a drunken brawl, leaving in his works the evidence of talents and qualities which the degradation of his life had failed to destroy. (012-007)

## Works

Greene's most famous prose work is *Pandosto* (1588), a pastoral [[romance-of-chivalry|romance]] which later served as the source for Shakespeare's *The Winter's Tale*. His writings were strongly influenced by the euphuistic style of [[John Lyly]], whose "Euphues" had recently achieved great popularity. (012-006)
