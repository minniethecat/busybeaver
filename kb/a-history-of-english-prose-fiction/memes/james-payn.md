## Works

James Payn was a prolific [[novel|English novel]]ist whose most famous works include *Lost Sir Massingberd* (1864), a sensation novel of a different stripe, and *By Proxy* (1878). He also served as editor of *Chambers's Journal* and *The Cornhill Magazine*.

## Reception in Tuckerman

Tuckerman mentions Payn only in a passing footnote, listing him among "many other well-known writers" who "have contributed to the English domestic novel" (044-016).
