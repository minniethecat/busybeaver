## Works

Rhoda Broughton was a popular [[novel|English novel]]ist whose early works, including *Not Wisely, But Too Well* (1867), *Red as a Rose Is She* (1870), and *Joan* (1876), caused controversy for their frank treatment of romantic relationships.

## Reception in Tuckerman

Tuckerman mentions Broughton in his critique of what he views as vulgar and immoral tendencies in contemporary fiction. He writes that "if Rhoda Broughton or '[[ouida|Ouida]]' were to cease being vulgar in print, they would be obliged to stop writing altogether, a public benefit which we can hardly expect them to confer" (047-008). Her novels *Not Wisely, But Too Well*, *Red as a Rose Is She*, and *Joan* are listed among objectionable works in his footnote of "nasty books" (047-013).
