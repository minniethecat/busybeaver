## Literary Significance

Herman Melville is listed among the [[america|American]] writers of fiction in Tuckerman's survey, alongside R.B. Kimball, Dr. R. Bird, John Neal, H.W. Longfellow, and others (045-011). The survey noted that [[america|American]] fiction in the nineteenth century was exceptionally rich, with notable contributions across diverse forms including the novel of Western life, juvenile fiction, and tales of imagination (045-010).
