# Thomas Deloney

Thomas Deloney (c. 1543–1600) was a famous ballad-maker of the 16th century and an early [[novel|English novel]]ist.

## Works

Deloney's most important work was the "Pleasant Historic of Thomas of Reading; or, The Sixe Worthie Yeomen of the West." It is the narrative of the life and fortunes of a worthy clothier of Henry the First's time, telling how he rose to wealth and prosperity, and was finally murdered by an innkeeper. There is interwoven a relation of the unhappy loves of the "faire Margaret," daughter of the exiled Earl of Shrewsbury, and of Duke Robert, the King's brother. (008-003)

## Style

The story illustrates some curious old customs, and is written in an unaffected and easy style, which makes it still very readable. Deloney's work belongs to the tradition of stories relating to the yeomanry, appearing alongside the popular tales of Robin Hood and George-a-Green in the early development of English prose fiction. (008-003)
