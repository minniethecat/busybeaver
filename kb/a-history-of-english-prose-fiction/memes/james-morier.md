# James Morier

**James Justinian Morier** (1780–1849) was a British diplomat and novelist, known for his contributions to the genre of Oriental fiction in English literature.

## Diplomatic Career

Morier served as a British diplomat in Persia (modern Iran), traveling extensively in the Middle East. His firsthand experience of Persian culture and society provided the material for his later literary work.

## Literary Career

Morier's most significant work is *The Adventures of Hajji Baba of Ispahan* (1824), a picaresque novel set in Persia. (044-012) The novel belongs to the tradition of Oriental fiction, which became a recognized department of English fiction through the great interest felt by English readers in Eastern manners and customs.

*Hajji Baba* was followed by a sequel, *The Adventures of Hajji Baba of Ispahan in [[england|England]]* (1828). Morier also wrote *Ayesha, the Maid of Kars* (1834) and several other works drawing on his Eastern experiences.

## Oriental Novel Tradition

The Oriental novel as a genre flourished in the 18th and 19th centuries, fueled by British imperial expansion and popular curiosity about Eastern cultures. Morier's work is notable for its satirical elements and its relatively authentic depiction of Persian life, informed by his diplomatic service.
