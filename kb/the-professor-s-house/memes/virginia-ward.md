## Role in the Novel

Virginia Ward appears briefly in Book II ("[[tom-outland|Tom Outland]]'s Story") as a sympathetic figure in Tom's [[washington-dc|Washington]] narrative. A clerk at the Smithsonian Institution, she represents the ordinary human kindness that exists even within the indifferent bureaucracy that ultimately fails Tom. Her presence highlights the contrast between personal decency and institutional coldness — she, as an individual, encourages Tom and expresses genuine disappointment at his treatment, but has no power to [[loss-and-change|change]] the outcome.

## Character Portrait

Cather sketches Virginia Ward with economy. She is introduced simply: "Her name was Virginia Ward" (023-005). Her kindness to Tom is practical and personal: she goes to the theatre with him and offers to "cheer him up" when the Director's slow pace discourages him (023-017). When Tom's hopes are finally dashed, she is "almost as much disappointed as I" (023-022). She has a sharp eye for institutional hypocrisy, telling Tom bluntly that [[dr-ripley|Dr. Ripley]] "only really cared about was getting a free trip to Europe and acting on a jury, and maybe getting a decoration" (023-022).

## Key Relationships

- **[[tom-outland|Tom Outland]]** — Virginia befriends Tom at the Smithsonian, going to lunch and the theatre with him. She is the only person in [[washington-dc|Washington]] who shows him genuine personal warmth (023-017, 023-022)
- **[[dr-ripley|Dr. Ripley]]** — Virginia's superior at the Smithsonian, whom she criticizes as self-interested, telling Tom he doesn't "care much about dead and gone Indians" (023-022)

## Key Scenes

- **Meeting Tom**: Virginia Ward introduces herself to Tom at the Smithsonian, beginning their brief friendship (023-005)
- **The theatre outing**: She goes to the theatre with Tom and reassures him about the slow pace of bureaucracy (023-017)
- **The let-down lunch**: After Tom's proposal is rejected, Virginia lunches with him and admits he "had been let down," criticizing [[dr-ripley|Dr. Ripley]]'s motives (023-022)
