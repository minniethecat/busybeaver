France is the European country most deeply woven into the fabric of [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|Godfrey St. Peter]]'s identity. It is the source of his education, his wife's [[family-and-domesticity|family]], his cultural tastes, and his scholarly preoccupations. While [[paris|Paris]] represents specific moments of romance and [[death-and-mortality|loss]], France in its broader reach represents the entire alternative to [[hamilton|Hamilton]]'s provincial Midwestern life.

## Family Roots

The connection is ancestral. "His education in France had been an accident" (025-001), the novel notes — yet that accident shaped everything. The young [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|St. Peter]] "who went to France to try his luck" (026-004) was a more adventurous figure than the settled professor he became.

He was connected to "the Thierault [[family-and-domesticity|family]] in France" (001-045), a tie that linked him across the Atlantic. When he stayed in [[paris|Paris]], he lived at Versailles, and "when he was in France their [[home-and-belonging|home]]s were his" (009-031). The Thieraults in Marseilles provided a net[[work-and-vocation|work]] of [[home-and-belonging|belonging]] that [[hamilton|Hamilton]] never fully replicated.

## Scholarship and Spain

France is paired throughout the novel with [[spain|Spain]], the other country central to [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]]'s scholarly [[work-and-vocation|work]]. The two countries represent different modes of engagement: [[spain|Spain]] for [[scholarship-and-knowledge|research]] and records, France for living and being.

His multi-volume [[work-and-vocation|work]], [[spanish-adventurers-in-north-america|Spanish Adventurers in North America]], required "two summers in [[spain|Spain]] studying records" (001-037) and [[scholarship-and-knowledge|knowledge]] of both French and Spanish colonial histories.

## The Marsellus Summer Tour

[[louie-marsellus|Louie Marsellus]] proposes the great summer expedition: "We're going to France for the summer" (009-044). The trip is planned in detail — "Doctor and [[lillian-st-peter|Mrs. St. Peter]] to France for the summer. [[louie-marsellus|Louie]] had decided" (015-002) — with a schedule of restaurants and introductions.

[[louie-marsellus|Louie]] can claim "the pleasure of knowing Anatole France" (015-006), the Nobel laureate whose name becomes a punning sign of cosmopolitan access. [[lillian-st-peter|Lillian]] and [[louie-marsellus|Louie]] "alternated" letters from France (027-016), keeping [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]] informed of a world he chose not to join.

> "He liked Paris, and he liked Louie. But one couldn't do one's own Paris with Louie." (015-015)

The refusal applies to France as a whole. The [[louie-marsellus|Marsellus]] version — luxurious, social, name-dropping — is not the France of the young solitary scholar, of the Thieraults, of [[tom-outland|Tom Outland]] who never made it there.

## The Unmade Journey

Several connections to France remain open or broken. [[tom-outland|Tom Outland]] "ought to have taken it to France with him" (011-022) — a reference to his [[science-and-discovery|invention]], whose European patent journey was cut short by war and [[death-and-mortality|death]]. Before one trip, a character "made a will in her favour; before he went to France" (002-026), the legal gesture underscoring the crossing's gravity.

The Professor "always brought [[home-and-belonging|home]] from France in great numbers" (005-016) objects of taste and beauty — the material culture that furnished [[the-old-house|his study]] and [[the-garden|his garden]].

> "The Marselluses sailed for France early in May. The Professor, released from his winter lectures, settled down to the most enjoyable season of the year." (017-001)

Their dep[[art-and-religion|art]]ure and his staying behind summarize the novel's deepest tension: France is where others go; [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]] remains.
