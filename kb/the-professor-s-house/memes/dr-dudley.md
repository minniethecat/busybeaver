## Role in the Novel

Dr. Dudley is a minor character in Book III of the novel, appearing only in relation to [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|Professor St. Peter]]'s declining health. As the local [[hamilton|Hamilton]] physician, he serves a functional role in the narrative — his medical examination provides concrete evidence of [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]]'s physical deterioration, which runs parallel to his spiritual and psychological crisis.

## Character Portrait

Dr. Dudley appears only through brief references, and Cather provides no physical description of him. His role is strictly [[work-and-vocation|profession]]al: he examines [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]], asks clinical questions, and offers a medical opinion. When [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]] requests an examination, he does not disclose "the real reason for his asking" (027-015), suggesting a certain [[work-and-vocation|profession]]al distance between doctor and patient. Dudley's diagnosis that [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]] has "not been melancholy" (029-024) is an important piece of evidence in the novel's ambiguity about the nature of [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|St. Peter]]'s crisis — whether it is depression, spiritual exhaustion, or something else.

## Key Relationships

- **[[professor-godfrey-st-peter|Professor St. Peter]]** — Dudley is [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]]'s physician, called to examine him after a summer of low spirits and again after [[tom-outlands-invention|the gas]] leak incident (027-004, 029-010)

## Key Scenes

- **The medical examination**: [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|Professor St. Peter]] visits Dr. Dudley's office and shrugs off the doctor's concerns, keeping his real reasons private (027-004, 027-015)
- **The gas leak emergency**: After [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|Professor St. Peter]] is found unconscious from a gas leak, someone telephones for Dr. Dudley (029-010)
- **The clinical assessment**: Dudley records that [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]], despite low spirits, "had not been melancholy" — a distinction that matters to the novel's portrayal of [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|St. Peter]]'s state (029-024)
