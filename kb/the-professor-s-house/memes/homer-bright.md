## Role in the Novel

Homer Bright is a minor but pointedly drawn character whose legal incompetence becomes a plot concern in the [[outland|Outland]] patent dispute. He is the brother of Mrs. Crane and, by extension, the brother-in-law of [[dr-crane|Dr. Crane]], who contemplates legal action related to [[tom-outland|Tom Outland]]'s patent. [[rosamond-marsellus|Rosamond]] describes him bluntly as "that dreadful shyster brother of hers" (004-029).

## Character Portrait

Homer Bright was once a student of [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|Professor St. Peter]], who remembers him as "about the greatest bluffer I ever had in my classes" (004-029). His reputation for rhetorical flourish over legal substance is a [[family-and-domesticity|family]] concern: when [[dr-crane|Dr. Crane]] considers bringing a lawsuit, [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]] urgently tries to dissuade him, pointing out that Homer Bright "is not" a sound lawyer (013-027).

The Professor's assessment is sharp: "Homer Bright's rhetoric might [[power|influence]] a jury in a rape or bigamy case, but it would antagonize a judge in an [[justice|equity]] court" (013-030). This characterization — of Bright as a flashy but unsound courtroom performer — indirectly affirms the novel's broader preference for substance over show.

## Key Relationships

- **[[dr-crane|Dr. Crane]]** — Homer Bright's brother-in-law. The Professor fears Bright will be "tempted by the publicity" (012-032) of a patent lawsuit and will lead Crane into a losing case.
- **[[rosamond-marsellus|Rosamond Marsellus]]** — Bright's sister (or sister-in-law), who dismisses him as a "dreadful shyster brother" (004-029).
- **[[professor-godfrey-st-peter|Professor St. Peter]]** — Bright was once [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|St. Peter]]'s student; [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]] remembers him as the greatest bluffer in his classes (004-029).

## Key Scenes

- **Rosamond's warning**: Rosamond mentions Homer Bright as the lawyer advising Mrs. Crane, prompting [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]]'s characterization of him as a "bluffer" (004-029).
- **The Crane lawsuit concern**: [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|Professor St. Peter]] tries to persuade [[dr-crane|Dr. Crane]] not to involve Homer Bright in the [[outland|Outland]] patent dispute, warning that Bright's courtroom style would fail in [[justice|equity]] court (012-032, 013-027, 013-030).
