## Role in the Novel

Barney Shea appears briefly in Book II ([[tom-outland|Tom Outland]]'s Story) as a minor figure at the Ruby Light saloon in Pardee. He is present during the scene where Blake returns drunk from a card game, and is later identified by [[tom-outland|Tom Outland]] as the one person among the saloon crowd he does not trust. His role is minimal — two brief mentions — but he serves to characterize the rough frontier atmosphere of Pardee and the tensions surrounding Blake's last night alive (018-008).

## Character Portrait

Barney Shea is described as "little" and is presented as verbally aggressive. His single line of dialogue is an insult shouted after [[rodney-blake|Rodney Blake]]:

> "Manners of a hog, and a dirty hog!" (018-008)

This outburst, directed at Blake after he returns from a card game with a large sum of [[money-and-materialism|money]], paints Shea as coarse and hostile. Cather gives him no physical description beyond his small stature, and his character is conveyed entirely through this one aggressive action.

## Key Relationships

- **[[rodney-blake]]**: Antagonist. Shea insults Blake as he leaves the Ruby Light, shouting after him about his manners (018-008).
- **[[tom-outland]]**: Distrusted. Tom identifies Shea as the one person among the Ruby Light crowd he does not trust, suspecting he "might try to pull something off on a stranger, down in Mexican town" (018-012).

## Critical Significance

Barney Shea is a background figure who contributes to the atmosphere of danger and roughness in Pardee. [[tom-outland|Tom Outland]]'s suspicion of him underscores the precariousness of Blake's situation after winning a significant sum gambling — surrounded by men who might rob a stranger. Shea's brief appearance also serves to highlight Blake's stoicism: when insulted, Blake "hitched up one shoulder, but didn't turn or make a sound" (018-008).

## Key Scenes

- **The Ruby Light insult**: Shea yells an insult at Blake as Blake leaves the saloon after a card game (018-008).
- **Tom's vigil**: Tom identifies Shea as the one person he distrusts while watching over Blake's body and winnings through the night (018-012).
