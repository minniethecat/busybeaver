## Definition

Ambition in The Professor's House takes two contrasting forms. [[louie-marsellus|Louie Marsellus]] embodies commercial ambition — the drive to convert [[tom-outland|Tom Outland]]'s [[science-and-discovery|invention]] into [[money-and-materialism|wealth]], to build an estate named [[outland|Outland]], to succeed in terms the world recognizes. The Professor embodies intellectual ambition — the quieter drive to complete his eight-volume history, to be a great teacher, to live a life of the mind. The novel is skeptical of both: [[louie-marsellus|Louie]]'s success alienates the [[family-and-domesticity|family]], while [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]]'s intellectual achievements leave him isolated and exhausted. Cather asks what success is worth if it costs the things that made the striving meaningful.

## In the Novel

[[louie-marsellus|Louie]]'s ambition is presented with Cather's characteristic ambivalence — he is generous, energetic, and fundamentally decent, but his enthusiasm for commercializing Tom's [[fatherhood-and-legacy|legacy]] disturbs [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]]:

> "In me youth it was me ambition to go to Egypt and see the tombs of the Pharaohs." (020-019)

This line, spoken by an Irish contractor [[louie-marsellus|Louie]] hires, echoes the novel's theme: ambition takes many forms, from archaeological wonder to real-estate development. The ambition that built the cliff [[home-and-belonging|dwelling]]s is separated by centuries from the ambition that builds "[[outland|Outland]]" in suburban [[chicago|Chicago]].

[[lillian-st-peter|Lillian]]'s "clear-cut ambitions" (028-013) drive much of the novel's domestic tension — her [[love-and-desire|desire]] for social position, for the new [[home-and-belonging|house]], for a life beyond [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]]'s academic world.

## Character Connections

- **[[louie-marsellus]]**: The novel's most visible ambitious figure — he turns [[tom-outlands-invention|Tom's invention]] into a fortune and names his estate after a man he never met. Cather treats him with sympathy even as she critiques his values.

- **[[professor-godfrey-st-peter]]**: His ambition was intellectual — the Spanish Adventurers, the European history chair. But by the novel's present, his ambition has burned low; he is post-ambition, living in the aftermath of his achievements.

- **[[lillian-st-peter]]**: Her social ambition — for the new [[home-and-belonging|house]], for European travel, for a life beyond [[hamilton|Hamilton]] — drives the [[family-and-domesticity|family]]'s relocation and much of its tension.

- **[[rosamond-marsellus]]**: Inherits both Tom's fortune and [[louie-marsellus|Louie]]'s ambition, becoming the novel's emblem of success that has lost its connection to what made it possible.

## Critical Context

Cather wrote during the 1920s boom, and The Professor's House registers anxiety about American [[money-and-materialism|commercialism]] at its peak. The novel contrasts [[tom-outland|Tom Outland]]'s pure [[science-and-discovery|discovery]] — done for [[love-and-desire|love]], not profit — with the commercial machinery that converts his [[science-and-discovery|invention]] into [[louie-marsellus|Louie]]'s fortune. Critics read this as Cather's critique of a culture that had lost the distinction between value and price. The Professor's [[escape|withdrawal]] in Book III is, among other things, a refusal to p[[art-and-religion|art]]icipate in an economy of ambition he no longer believes in.

## Related Concepts

- **[[money-and-materialism]]**: Ambition in the novel is almost always measured in financial terms — the [[outland|Outland]] fortune is success made visible.
- **[[work-and-vocation]]**: The Professor's intellectual ambition is a [[work-and-vocation|vocation]]; [[louie-marsellus|Louie]]'s commercial ambition is a career. The novel values the former over the latter.
- **[[outland]]**: [[outland|Tom Outland's legacy]] is the object of everyone's ambition — but Tom himself had no commercial ambition at all.
- **[[scholarship-and-knowledge]]**: The Professor's scholarly ambition is the novel's counter-model to commercial success.
- **[[academic-life]]**: The university is an arena of ambition too — the expansion of the student body, the faculty politics, the competition for resources.
