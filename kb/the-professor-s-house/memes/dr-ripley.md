## Role in the Novel

Dr. Ripley is a minor but symbolically significant figure in Book II ("[[tom-outland|Tom Outland]]'s Story"). As the Smithsonian's [[power|authority]] on prehistoric Indian remains, he is the institutional gatekeeper who could have enabled the excavation of the [[blue-mesa|Blue Mesa]] cliff city. His failure to secure funding — and the motives attributed to him — crystallize the novel's critique of institutional indifference to genuine [[science-and-discovery|discovery]]. [[father-duchene|Father Duchene]] had anticipated Ripley's conclusions, and Ripley's own assessment confirms the importance of Tom's find, which makes the funding refusal all the more damning.

## Character Portrait

Tom's initial impression of Ripley is positive: "Dr. Ripley asked the right sort of questions, and evidently knew his business" (023-021). But the outcome tells a different story. Ripley tells Tom that "the sum Congress had allowed the Smithsonian wouldn't cover an expedition to the Southwest" (023-021). [[virginia-ward|Virginia Ward]] offers a harsher assessment: "the only thing Dr. Ripley really cared about was getting a free trip to Europe and acting on a jury, and maybe getting a decoration" (023-022). [[father-duchene|Father Duchene]] had earlier noted that Ripley, "in [[washington-dc|Washington]], afterward surmised" the same conclusion about the cliff city's fate — that the tribe had been exterminated in their summer camp, not in their stronghold (022-003).

## Key Relationships

- **[[tom-outland|Tom Outland]]** — Ripley met with Tom, asked the right questions, but ultimately informed him that no funding was available (023-021)
- **[[virginia-ward|Virginia Ward]]** — Ripley's subordinate at the Smithsonian, who criticized his motives as self-interested (023-022)
- **[[father-duchene|Father Duchene]]** — Ripley's later archaeological conclusions aligned with what [[father-duchene|Father Duchene]] had already surmised about the cliff city's fate (022-003)

## Key Scenes

- **The Smithsonian interview**: Tom meets Ripley, who "asked the right sort of questions" and seemed [[scholarship-and-knowledge|knowledge]]able (023-021)
- **The funding refusal**: Ripley tells Tom the Smithsonian cannot fund an expedition to the Southwest (023-021)
- **Virginia's verdict**: [[virginia-ward|Virginia Ward]] tells Tom that Ripley only cares about European travel and decorations, not "dead and gone Indians" (023-022)
