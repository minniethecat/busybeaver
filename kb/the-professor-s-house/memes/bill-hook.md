## Role in the Novel

Bill Hook is a minor character in Book II ("[[tom-outland|Tom Outland]]'s Story"), appearing only in [[tom-outland|Tom Outland]]'s first-person narrative. The liveryman at Tarpin, New Mexico, Hook becomes a loyal supporter of Tom and [[rodney-blake|Roddy Blake]]'s excavation of the [[cliff-dweller-civilization|Blue Mesa ruins]]. His practical assistance — transporting [[work-and-vocation|work]]men, hauling supplies, and providing free lodging — exemplifies the everyday decency that contrasts with the institutional indifference Tom later encounters in [[washington-dc|Washington]] (021-011).

## Character Portrait

Cather presents Hook through brief but vivid strokes. He is a man of modest means who extends generosity to those in need: he sheltered [[henry-atkins|old Henry Atkins]] when Henry was "down and out" (021-011). When Tom returns to Tarpin after his disastrous trip to [[washington-dc|Washington]], Hook greets him warmly: "He gave me a glad hand and said Blake was out on the mesa" (023-025). Though uneducated, Hook is shrewd enough to recognize practical worth — and honest enough to show Tom that Roddy had sold his horse to the barn for sixty dollars (024-001).

## Key Relationships

- **[[tom-outland|Tom Outland]]** — Hook was a "good friend" to Tom, providing hauling services at "a bottom price" knowing the young men's expenses were heavy (021-011)
- **[[rodney-blake|Rodney Blake]]** — Hook transported Blake's [[work-and-vocation|work]]men and supplies to the mesa; later, he showed Tom that Blake had sold his horse before dep[[art-and-religion|art]]ing (024-001)
- **[[henry-atkins|Henry Atkins]]** — Hook had "sheltered old Henry when he was down and out," suggesting a long-standing pattern of generosity toward the vulnerable (021-011)

## Key Scenes

- **Provisioning the mesa expedition**: Hook got Tom and Roddy's [[work-and-vocation|work]]men back and forth, brought supplies up on pack-mules, and let them sleep in his hay barn to save hotel costs (021-011)
- **Tom's return from [[washington-dc|Washington]]**: When Tom arrived at Tarpin to find Roddy gone, he "went straight to the livery stable to ask Bill Hook for news of Blake" (023-025)
- **The sold horse**: Hook showed Tom Roddy's horse, revealing that Blake had sold it to the livery barn for sixty dollars — a concrete sign of Blake's permanent dep[[art-and-religion|art]]ure (024-001)
