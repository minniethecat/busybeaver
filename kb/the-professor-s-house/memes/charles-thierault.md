## Role in the Novel

Charles Thierault appears only indirectly in _The Professor's House_ — he is a figure from [[memory-and-the-past|the past]], the foster-brother from [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|Professor St. Peter]]'s student years in [[france|France]]. Yet his significance extends into the novel's present: when [[louie-marsellus|Louie Marsellus]] needed commercial expertise to navigate the patent for [[tom-outland|Tom Outland]]'s [[science-and-discovery|invention]], it was Charles Thierault whom they consulted. [[louie-marsellus|Louie]] tells [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]], "Your foster-brother, Charles Thierault" will help with the patent matters (015-003).

## Character Portrait

Charles Thierault is the son — "fils" — of the Thierault mercantile [[family-and-domesticity|family]] in Versailles, where the young [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|Godfrey St. Peter]] lived as a tutor during his student years. The Thierault [[family-and-domesticity|household]], with its "happy student years" (001-045), represents a formative period in [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|St. Peter]]'s life — his introduction to European culture, refinement, and [[family-and-domesticity|family]] life beyond America. Charles became, in effect, [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|St. Peter]]'s French brother.

The Thierault [[family-and-domesticity|family]]'s mercantile [[home-and-belonging|house]] "did a business with [[spain|Spain]] in cork" (009-025), and Charles "is still interested in a shipping line" (015-010). His commercial connections bridged the Old World and the New, and proved practically useful when the [[outland|Outland]] patent required international navigation.

## Key Relationships

- **[[professor-godfrey-st-peter|Professor St. Peter]]** — Charles is [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]]'s foster-brother from the Versailles years. The Thieraults took [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|St. Peter]] in "as tutor to their boys" (009-025). Decades later, [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|St. Peter]] plans to visit Charles in [[france|France]].
- **[[louie-marsellus|Louie Marsellus]]** — [[louie-marsellus|Louie]] consulted Charles Thierault regarding the commercial aspects of [[tom-outland|Tom Outland]]'s patent (015-003).
- **Madame Thierault** — Charles's mother, who managed the [[family-and-domesticity|household]]. [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|St. Peter]] remembered that "he had found Madame Thierault more sympathetic than anyone else in his life" (009-031).

## Key Scenes

- **Planning the French trip**: [[louie-marsellus|Louie]] announces that Charles Thierault will help with patent matters and that the [[family-and-domesticity|family]] will spend the summer in [[france|France]] (015-003, 015-008).
- **Remembering Versailles**: The Professor's [[memory-and-the-past|recollection]]s of the Thierault [[family-and-domesticity|household]] in his student days form a key strand of his [[memory-and-the-past|memory]] of [[france|France]] (001-045, 009-025).
