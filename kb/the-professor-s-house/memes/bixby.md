## Role in the Novel

The Bixbys appear in Book II ("[[tom-outland|Tom Outland]]'s Story") as Tom's landlords during his stay in [[washington-dc|Washington]]. Their [[family-and-domesticity|household]] provides a bitterly comic counterpoint to Tom's high-minded mission to secure Smithsonian funding for the [[blue-mesa|Blue Mesa]] excavation. Where Tom's life on the mesa was defined by [[science-and-discovery|discovery]], friendship, and ancient beauty, the Bixbys' world is one of social scheming, financial anxiety, and s[[art-and-religion|art]]orial disaster — a miniature portrait of the [[washington-dc|Washington]] that fails him.

## Character Portrait

Mr. and Mrs. Bixby are presented as a couple consumed by social aspiration. Their lives revolve around securing invitations to official receptions, and when one finally arrives, "the terrible question of what Mrs. Bixby should wear" consumes their [[family-and-domesticity|household]] (023-018). Bixby borrows twenty-five dollars from Tom — taking his lunch hour to go shopping with his wife for dress satin (023-019). Their triumph is short-lived: at the reception, claret-cup is spilled on Mrs. Bixby's dress, and the evening ends with "her weeping and reproaching him for having been so upset about it, and looking at nothing but her ruined dress all evening" (023-019). Tom's verdict is damning: "Living with the Bixbys gave me a kind of low-spiritedness I had never known before" (023-020).

## Key Relationships

- **[[tom-outland|Tom Outland]]** — Tom boarded with the Bixbys and lent Bixby twenty-five dollars. Their [[family-and-domesticity|household]] became, for Tom, a symbol of the pettiness that pervaded his [[washington-dc|Washington]] experience (023-018–020)
- **Mrs. Bixby** — Bixby's wife, whose obsession with her reception dress and subsequent humiliation define the couple's role in the narrative (023-019)

## Key Scenes

- **The invitation**: The Bixbys' scheming for a reception invitation finally succeeds, sending them into a frenzy of preparation (023-018)
- **The dress**: Bixby borrows [[money-and-materialism|money]] from Tom and spends his lunch hour shopping for satin with his wife (023-019)
- **The reception disaster**: Mrs. Bixby's dress is ruined by spilled claret-cup, and the evening descends into tears and recrimination (023-019)
- **Tom's judgment**: Tom reflects that living with the Bixbys gave him "a kind of low-spiritedness I had never known before" (023-020)
