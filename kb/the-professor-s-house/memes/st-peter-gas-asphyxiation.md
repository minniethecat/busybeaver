## Context

By late summer, [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|Professor St. Peter]] has spent months living [[solitude-and-creativity|alone]] in the [[the-old-house|old house]], his [[family-and-domesticity|family]] away in [[france|France]]. The [[home-and-belonging|house]] has been stripped of furnishings — only his study in the attic sewing-room remains, with a box-couch, [[augusta|Augusta]]'s dress forms, and the rusty gas stove that has been a hazard all along.

The stove had no flue and required the window to stay open for ventilation (001-038). Throughout the novel, characters warn [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]] about it: [[lillian-st-peter|Lillian]] calls it "miserable" (008-026), and [[augusta|Augusta]] cautions him repeatedly (029-006). The Professor has been cultivating a detached, almost passive relationship to life — a "novel mental dissipation" (026-003) — and has been indifferent to his own safety.

> "There are only a few years, at the last, in which man can consider his estate, and he thought he might be quite as near the end of his road as his grandfather had been in those days." (026-008)

## The Event

On a stormy night, [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]] lights the stove and lies down on his box-couch. The storm blows the stove out and shuts the window. The room fills with gas (028-018—028-019). The Professor loses consciousness.

> "He was cold and numb, felt sick and weak. He vaguely realized that the storm had blown the stove out and the window shut. The room was pitch-black and full of gas." (028-019)

[[augusta|Augusta]], returning late from [[work-and-vocation|work]] and having received [[lillian-st-peter|Mrs. St. Peter]]'s letter asking for the keys to the new [[home-and-belonging|house]], comes to the [[the-old-house|old house]] in the storm. She smells gas at the front door and rushes upstairs.

> "When I opened the front door I smelled gas, and I knew that stove had been up to its old tricks. I supposed you'd gone out and forgot to turn it off. When I got to the second floor I heard a fall overhead, and it flashed across me that you were up here and had been overcome." (029-008)

She drags [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]] into the hallway, opens the windows, turns off the stove, and calls [[dr-dudley|Dr. Dudley]] from next door. The Professor regains consciousness, "[[escape|flight]]y" and disoriented.

## Key Participants

- **[[professor-godfrey-st-peter]]**: The near-victim. His passive acceptance of the accident — "he had felt no will to resist, but had let chance take its way" (029-024) — reveals his existential crisis and detachment from life.
- **[[augusta]]**: The rescuer. Her practical competence, presence of mind, and willingness to act in crisis contrast with [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]]'s passivity. She represents the "bloomless side of life" that he has always avoided but now finds indispensable (029-020).

## Aftermath and Significance

The near-[[death-and-mortality|death]] experience proves transformative. The Professor reflects:

> "His temporary release from consciousness seemed to have been beneficial. He had let something go — and it was gone: something very precious, that he could not consciously have relinquished, probably." (029-025)

The asphyxiation is the novel's existential pivot. The Professor does not attempt suicide — he "had no more thought of suicide than he had thought of embezzling" (029-024) — but he also does not resist [[death-and-mortality|death]]. After surviving, he finds himself [[loss-and-change|change]]d, the "original" Kansas boy within him having dis[[home-and-belonging|place]]d the social self built over decades of career and [[family-and-domesticity|family]] (026-005—026-007).

[[augusta|Augusta]] stays with him through the night, reading her religious book. He recognizes in her a "corrective, a remedial [[power|influence]]" (029-020). The event crystallizes the novel's central theme: the tension between the life one builds for others and the life one might have lived for oneself. The Professor ends the novel lying on his couch, having accepted that he must "learn to live without delight" (029-023) — but still alive, still grounded, facing the return of his [[family-and-domesticity|family]] on the *Berengaria*.
