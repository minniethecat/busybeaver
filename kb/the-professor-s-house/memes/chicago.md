Chicago appears throughout the novel as the great city beyond [[hamilton|Hamilton]] — a [[home-and-belonging|place]] of commerce, culture, and obligation. Unlike [[hamilton|the university town]] where [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|Professor St. Peter]] lives embedded in academic routine, Chicago represents the demands of the wider world: [[money-and-materialism|shopping expeditions]], lecture engagements, business meetings, and the [[family-and-domesticity|family]]'s accelerating immersion in [[ambition-and-success|worldly success]].

## A City of Commerce

Chicago enters the novel first through material objects. The wrought-iron door fittings of [[the-old-house|the old house]] were sourced from Chicago: "We found just the right sort of wrought-iron door fittings from Chicago" (002-019). The city is a market[[home-and-belonging|place]], a [[home-and-belonging|place]] where things are acquired.

[[lillian-st-peter|Lillian St. Peter]] and her daughters treat Chicago as a destination for consumption. When [[rosamond-marsellus|Rosamond]] needed a trousseau, "[[professor-godfrey-st-peter|St. Peter]] went to Chicago with Rosamond to help her buy things for her trousseau" (014-001). [[louie-marsellus|Louie Marsellus]] "accompanied them to Chicago, where he was to join his brother" (014-002), folding the shopping trip into his net[[work-and-vocation|work]] of business obligations.

During that same expedition, Rosamond announced she was "going to buy a new fur coat in Chicago" (014-012), a purchase that distressed [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]] with its casual extravagance. [[lillian-st-peter|Lillian]] had urged [[kathleen-mcgregor|Kathleen]] "to go to Chicago and study in the life classes at the Art Institute" (004-034), framing the city as a destination for cultivation.

## The University of Chicago

[[professor-godfrey-st-peter|St. Peter]]'s connection is [[work-and-vocation|profession]]al. His "lecture engagement at the University of Chicago is coincident with" his daughter's birthday (006-007), and "the following week [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|St. Peter]] went to Chicago to give his lectures" (008-001). The city draws him away from [[the-old-house|his study]] and [[the-garden|his garden]] into the obligations of his public scholarly identity.

Before those lectures, he met [[louie-marsellus|Louie]] "at the Blackstone" hotel for tea (008-001), where the visiting professors encountered a side of [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|Godfrey St. Peter]] they had not seen in [[hamilton|Hamilton]].

> "The professors went away from the Blackstone that night respecting Godfrey St. Peter more than they had ever done before." (008-023)

The hotel lobby becomes a stage where [[ambition-and-success|worldly accomplishment]] and academic reputation meet.

## A World Beyond Hamilton

Chicago functions as the urban counterpoint to [[hamilton|Hamilton]]'s provinciality. Its distance is measured in train schedules: [[lillian-st-peter|Lillian]] waits "anxiously for her husband. The Chicago train was usually punctual" (014-010). [[louie-marsellus|Louie]]'s restless energy makes him "cab across Chicago" (008-010) rather than settle into one neighborhood.

For [[tom-outland|Tom Outland]], Chicago is remote — a [[home-and-belonging|place]] [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]] imports delicacies from during their bachelor summer: "a choice assortment of cheeses and light Italian wines from a discriminating importer in Chicago" (017-019). The importer is "discriminating" in a way [[hamilton|Hamilton]] cannot be, linking Chicago to the cultivated European taste [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]] treasures.

## Key Scenes

- **The wrought-iron fittings**: Chicago's material reach extends to [[the-old-house]] (002-019)
- **Kathleen's [[art-and-religion|art]] education**: [[lillian-st-peter|Lillian]] urges Chicago study as a path to refinement (004-034)
- **The lecture engagement**: [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|St. Peter]]'s [[work-and-vocation|profession]]al identity pulls him away from [[hamilton|Hamilton]] (006-007, 008-001)
- **The Blackstone tea**: [[louie-marsellus|Louie]] hosts the visiting professors, transforming their view of [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|St. Peter]] (008-023)
- **The trousseau trip**: [[rosamond-marsellus|Rosamond]]'s shopping expedition, with [[louie-marsellus|Louie]] in attendance (014-001–014-012)
- **The importer's delicacies**: Chicago enables cultivated taste in provincial [[hamilton|Hamilton]] (017-019)
