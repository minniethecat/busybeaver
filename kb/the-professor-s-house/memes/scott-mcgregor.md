## Role in the Novel

Scott McGregor is a minor but structurally important character in _The Professor's House_, appearing primarily in Book I. A young journalist with a modest salary, he marries [[kathleen-mcgregor|Kathleen St. Peter]], [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]]'s younger daughter, at a time when the [[family-and-domesticity|family]] expected the elder daughter [[rosamond-marsellus|Rosamond]] to marry first. His humble circumstances and unpretentious character provide a quiet foil to the [[money-and-materialism|wealth]] and [[ambition-and-success|ambition]] of [[louie-marsellus|Louie Marsellus]].

Scott's role in the novel is largely relational — he is [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]]'s son-in-law and [[kathleen-mcgregor|Kathleen]]'s husband. Unlike [[louie-marsellus|Louie]], who dominates social occasions with talk of business and building, Scott tends to fall silent when conversation turns to topics beyond his experience, as when [[sir-edgar-spilling|Sir Edgar Spilling]] and [[louie-marsellus|Louie]] discuss topics "beyond his little circle of interests" (002-015).

## Character Portrait

Scott is young and just beginning his career when he marries Kathleen. Cather presents him as unremarkable but decent — a man whose salary was "not large enough for two people to live upon" (004-036), a fact the [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|St. Peter]]s hoped would delay the [[love-and-desire|marriage]]. [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|Professor St. Peter]] had initially "thought that Scott McGregor was the sort of fellow [[lillian-st-peter|Lillian]] always found tiresome" (006-014), yet after the [[love-and-desire|marriage]], arch and confidential relations developed between Scott and his mother-in-law.

Scott [[work-and-vocation|work]]s as a journalist and travels for his newspaper. He appears at [[family-and-domesticity|family]] gatherings, including the dinner p[[art-and-religion|art]]y for [[sir-edgar-spilling|Sir Edgar Spilling]] and the Country Club luncheon for the electrical engineers (010-001). His presence is steady if unremarkable, and he treats his father-in-law with casual warmth, offering him rides and checking in on him.

## Key Relationships

- **[[kathleen-mcgregor|Kathleen St. Peter]]** — Scott's wife, [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]]'s younger daughter. Their [[love-and-desire|marriage]], which came before Rosamond's, surprised the [[family-and-domesticity|family]] (004-036).
- **[[professor-godfrey-st-peter|Professor St. Peter]]** — Scott's father-in-law, with whom he maintains easy, informal relations. Scott picks him up at the university and drives him [[home-and-belonging|home]] (010-001).
- **[[louie-marsellus|Louie Marsellus]]** — Scott's brother-in-law by [[love-and-desire|marriage]] to Rosamond. The contrast between [[louie-marsellus|Louie]]'s [[money-and-materialism|wealth]] and Scott's modest means is a recurring subtext (002-015).
- **[[lillian-st-peter|Lillian St. Peter]]** — Scott's mother-in-law, with whom he developed unexpectedly warm relations after his [[love-and-desire|marriage]] (006-014).

## Key Scenes

- **Dinner with Sir Edgar**: Scott attends the dinner p[[art-and-religion|art]]y for the visiting English scholar and finds himself excluded from conversations beyond his [[scholarship-and-knowledge|knowledge]] (002-011, 002-015)
- **Campus encounter**: Scott catches up with [[lillian-st-peter|Lillian]] on a warm September day on the university campus (005-001)
- **Country Club luncheon**: Scott covers the electrical engineers' luncheon as a newspaper man (010-001)
- **Train encounter**: Scott finds his father-in-law on the Blue Bird Express, returning from a business trip (014-004)
