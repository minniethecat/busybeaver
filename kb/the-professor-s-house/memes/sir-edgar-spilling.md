## Role in the Novel

Sir Edgar Spilling is an English scholar who appears in Book I at a dinner p[[art-and-religion|art]]y hosted by the [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|St. Peter]]s, providing a narrative occasion for [[louie-marsellus|Louie Marsellus]] to explain the [[outland|Outland]] [[fatherhood-and-legacy|legacy]] to an outsider. His presence brings an international dimension to the otherwise insular [[hamilton|Hamilton]] academic world, and his genuine interest in [[tom-outland|Tom Outland]]'s scientific [[science-and-discovery|discovery]] — which he learns of by chance over dinner — temporarily diverts him from his original purpose of discussing Spanish manuscripts with [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|Professor St. Peter]].

Spilling also serves a secondary function later in the novel, when [[scott-mcgregor|Scott McGregor]] tells [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]] that Spilling visited the newspaper office after the dinner, seeking information about [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]]'s [[ambition-and-success|achievement]]s (005-018).

## Character Portrait

Cather introduces Spilling as "a gaunt, rugged, large-boned man of fifty, with long legs and arms, a pear-shaped face, and a drooping, prewar moustache" (002-011). He is "so anxious to do the usual thing in America that he wore a morning street suit" (002-011). His specialty is Spanish history, and he has come to [[hamilton|Hamilton]] to discuss "manuscripts shut up in certain mouldering monasteries in [[spain|Spain]]" (002-030).

At dinner, Spilling is good-natured but slightly bewildered by [[louie-marsellus|Louie Marsellus]]'s enthusiastic monologues about building, interior decoration, and the [[outland|Outland]] [[fatherhood-and-legacy|legacy]]. Yet when he learns that the [[outland|Outland]] engine — which he knows from his Air Service experience — originated with a young man connected to this [[family-and-domesticity|family]], he becomes "as much interested as he was mystified" (002-030).

## Key Relationships

- **[[professor-godfrey-st-peter|Professor St. Peter]]** — Spilling has come to consult [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]] about Spanish historical sources. After dinner, [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|St. Peter]] takes Spilling's arm and leads him to the library for a private discussion (002-031).
- **[[louie-marsellus|Louie Marsellus]]** — [[louie-marsellus|Louie]] takes charge of Spilling socially, regaling him with talk of [[home-and-belonging|house]]-building, Chinese acquaintances, and the [[outland|Outland]] story. Spilling listens with polite bewilderment that gradually turns to genuine interest (002-017–002-030).
- **[[scott-mcgregor|Scott McGregor]]** — Scott is skeptical of the "baronet with walrus moustaches" (002-018) and feels excluded from the cosmopolitan conversation.

## Key Scenes

- **The dinner p[[art-and-religion|art]]y**: Spilling arrives at the [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|St. Peter]] [[home-and-belonging|home]] and is seated at [[lillian-st-peter|Lillian St. Peter]]'s right. [[louie-marsellus|Louie Marsellus]] dominates the conversation, eventually revealing that their [[home-and-belonging|house]] is named "[[outland|Outland]]" for [[tom-outland|Tom Outland]] (002-011–002-031).
- **The library consultation**: After dinner, [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|St. Peter]] leads Spilling to the library to discuss the Spanish manuscripts that were Spilling's original purpose in visiting (002-031).
- **Visit to the Herald**: Spilling later visits [[scott-mcgregor|Scott McGregor]]'s newspaper office seeking more information about [[professor-godfrey-st-peter|the Professor]]'s accomplishments (005-018).
