[072-001] In the year when Ciaran the son of the Carpenter died, the same year

[072-002] when Tuathal Maelgariv was killed and the year when Diarmait the son of

[072-003] Cerrbel became king of all Ireland, the year 538 of our era in short, it

[072-004] happened that there was a great gathering of the men of Ireland at the

[072-005] Hill of Uisneach in [[meath|Royal Meath]].

[072-006] In addition to the Council which was being held, there were games and

[072-007] tournaments and brilliant deployments of troops, and universal feastings

[072-008] and enjoyments. The gathering lasted for a week, and on the last day

[072-009] of the week [[mongan|Mongan]] was moving through the crowd with seven guards, his

[072-010] story-teller [[cairide|Cairide]]’, and his wife.

[072-011] It had been a beautiful day, with brilliant sunshine and great sport,

[072-012] but suddenly clouds began to gather in the sky to the west, and others

[072-013] came rushing blackly from the east. When these clouds met the world went

[072-014] dark for a space, and there fell from the sky a shower of hailstones, so

[072-015] large that each man wondered at their size, and so swift and heavy that

[072-016] the women and young people of the host screamed from the pain of the

[072-017] blows they received.

[072-018] Mongan’s men made a roof of their shields, and the hailstones battered

[072-019] on the shields so terribly that even under them they were afraid. They

[072-020] began to move away from the host looking for shelter, and when they had

[072-021] gone apart a little way they turned the edge of a small hill and a knoll

[072-022] of trees, and in the twinkling of an eye they were in fair weather.

[072-023] One minute they heard the clashing and bashing of the hailstones, the

[072-024] howling of the venomous wind, the screams of women and the uproar of the

[072-025] crowd on the Hill of Uisneach, and the next minute they heard nothing

[072-026] more of those sounds and saw nothing more of these sights, for they had

[072-027] been permitted to go at one step out of the world of men and into the

[072-028] world of [[faery|Faery]].
