## Overview

Fiachna Finn, the son of Baltan and grandson of Murchertach, was a King of
[[ulster|Ulster]] and the mortal father of [[mongan]]. His reign and death are central to
the political drama that opens *[[mongan|Mongan's Frenzy]]*.

## In the Tales

### King of Ulster

Fiachna Finn was elected king following the death of the previous ruler.
He was "the son of Baltan, the son of Murchertach" [074-003], and his father
had fought alongside Eolgarg's father in past conflicts [074-007]. His
elevation to the throne brought him into conflict with neighbouring rulers,
particularly Fiachna Duv, the ruler of another province.

### Father of Mongan

On the same night that Fiachna Finn's wife bore a son — a child who would be
named [[mongan|Mongan]] — an attendant in his palace called An Dáv also had a son, who
was named mac an Dáv [077-008]–[077-011]. The two infants would be raised
together, and [[mongan|Mongan]] would grow up to succeed his father.

### Encounter at the Tree

Fiachna Finn once found himself besieged in a tree by a flock of monstrous
sheep. When a mysterious stranger appeared and offered to fight them, Fiachna
declared he would face them himself [075-044], but his men dissuaded him.
The stranger proved impervious to harm [076-016], and in gratitude Fiachna
granted him extraordinary rewards.

### Death and Succession

Following a campaign of warfare waged by Fiachna Duv [077-018]–[077-020],
Fiachna Finn was killed in battle. Fiachna Duv crowned himself King of
[[ulster|Ulster]] in his place, and the young [[mongan|Mongan]] was left as heir to a contested
throne.
