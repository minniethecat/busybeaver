[079-001] “There is nothing to marvel about in this [[duv-laca|Duv Laca]],” said the Flame Lady

[079-002] scornfully. “She has got married, and she has been beaten at chess. It

[079-003] has happened before.”

[079-004] “Let us keep to the story,” said [[mongan|Mongan]], and, having taken some few

[079-005] dozen deep draughts of the wine, he became even more jovial than before.

[079-006] Then he recommenced his tale:

[079-007] It happened on a day that Mongan had need of treasure. He had many

[079-008] presents to make, and he had not as much gold and silver and cattle as

[079-009] was proper for a king. He called his nobles together and discussed what

[079-010] was the best thing to be done, and it was arranged that he should visit

[079-011] the provincial kings and ask boons from them.

[079-012] He set out at once on his round of visits, and the first province he

[079-013] went to was [[leinster|Leinster]].

[079-014] The King of Leinster at that time was [[branduv|Branduv]], the son of Echach. He

[079-015] welcomed Mongan and treated him well, and that night Mongan slept in his

[079-016] palace.

[079-017] When he awoke in the morning he looked out of a lofty window, and he

[079-018] saw on the sunny lawn before the palace a herd of cows. There were fifty

[079-019] cows in all, for he counted them, and each cow had a calf beside her,

[079-020] and each cow and calf was pure white in colour, and each of them had red

[079-021] ears.

[079-022] When Mongan saw these cows, he fell in love with them as he had never

[079-023] fallen in love with anything before.

[079-024] He came down from the window and walked on the sunny lawn among the

[079-025] cows, looking at each of them and speaking words of affection and

[079-026] endearment to them all; and while he was thus walking and talking and

[079-027] looking and loving, he noticed that some one was moving beside him. He

[079-028] looked from the cows then, and saw that the King of Leinster was at his

[079-029] side.

[079-030] “Are you in love with the cows?” Branduv asked him.

[079-031] “I am,” said Mongan.

[079-032] “Everybody is,” said the King of Leinster.

[079-033] “I never saw anything like them,” said Mongan.

[079-034] “Nobody has,” said the King of Leinster.

[079-035] “I never saw anything I would rather have than these cows,” said Mongan.

[079-036] “These,” said the King of Leinster, “are the most beautiful cows

[079-037] in Ireland, and,” he continued thoughtfully, “Duv Laca is the most

[079-038] beautiful woman in Ireland.”

[079-039] “There is no lie in what you say,” said Mongan.

[079-040] “Is it not a queer thing,” said the King of Leinster, “that I should

[079-041] have what you want with all your soul, and you should have what I want

[079-042] with all my heart?”

[079-043] “Queer indeed,” said Mongan, “but what is it that you do want?”

[079-044] “Duv Laca, of course,” said the King of Leinster.

[079-045] “Do you mean,” said Mongan, “that you would exchange this herd of fifty

[079-046] pure white cows having red ears--”

[079-047] “And their fifty calves,” said the King of Leinster--

[079-048] “For Duv Laca, or for any woman in the world?”

[079-049] “I would,” cried the King of Leinster, and he thumped his knee as he

[079-050] said it.

[079-051] “Done,” roared Mongan, and the two kings shook hands on the bargain.

[079-052] Mongan then called some of his own people, and before any more words

[079-053] could be said and before any alteration could be made, he set his men

[079-054] behind the cows and marched home with them to [[ulster|Ulster]].
