[081-001] [[duv-laca|Duv Laca]] and the King of [[leinster|Leinster]] went apart then to speak together, and

[081-002] the eye of the king seemed to be as big as a plate, so fevered was

[081-003] it and so enlarged and inflamed by the look of Duv Laca. He was so

[081-004] confounded with joy also that his words got mixed up with his teeth, and

[081-005] Duv Laca did not know exactly what it was he was trying to say, and

[081-006] he did not seem to know himself. But at last he did say something

[081-007] intelligible, and this is what he said.

[081-008] “I am a very happy man,” said he.

[081-009] “And I,” said Duv Laca, “am the happiest woman in the world.”

[081-010] “Why should you be happy?” the astonished king demanded.

[081-011] “Listen to me,” she said. “If you tried to take me away from this place

[081-012] against my own wish, one half of the men of [[ulster|Ulster]] would be dead before

[081-013] you got me and the other half would be badly wounded in my defence.”

[081-014] “A bargain is a bargain,” the King of Leinster began.

[081-015] “But,” she continued, “they will not prevent my going away, for they all

[081-016] know that I have been in love with you for ages.”

[081-017] “What have you been in with me for ages?” said the amazed king.

[081-018] “In love with you,” replied Duv Laca.

[081-019] “This is news,” said the king, “and it is good news.”

[081-020] “But, by my word,” said Duv Laca, “I will not go with you unless you

[081-021] grant me a boon.”

[081-022] “All that I have,” cried [[branduv|Branduv]], “and all that every-body has.”

[081-023] “And you must pass your word and pledge your word that you will do what

[081-024] I ask.”

[081-025] “I pass it and pledge it,” cried the joyful king.

[081-026] “Then,” said Duv Laca, “this is what I bind on you.”

[081-027] “Light the yolk!” he cried.

[081-028] “Until one year is up and out you are not to pass the night in any house

[081-029] that I am in.”

[081-030] “By my head and hand!” Branduv stammered.

[081-031] “And if you come into a house where I am during the time and term of

[081-032] that year, you are not to sit down in the chair that I am sitting in.”

[081-033] “Heavy is my doom!” he groaned.

[081-034] “But,” said Duv Laca, “if I am sitting in a chair or a seat you are

[081-035] to sit in a chair that is over against me and opposite to me and at a

[081-036] distance from me.”

[081-037] “Alas!” said the king, and he smote his hands together, and then he beat

[081-038] them on his head, and then he looked at them and at everything about,

[081-039] and he could not tell what anything was or where anything was, for his

[081-040] mind was clouded and his wits had gone astray.

[081-041] “Why do you bind these woes on me?” he pleaded.

[081-042] “I wish to find out if you truly love me.”

[081-043] “But I do,” said the king. “I love you madly and dearly, and with all my

[081-044] faculties and members.”

[081-045] “That is the way! love you,” said Duv Laca. “We shall have a notable

[081-046] year of courtship and joy. And let us go now,” she continued, “for I am

[081-047] impatient to be with you.”

[081-048] “Alas!” said Branduv, as he followed her. “Alas, alas!” said the King of

[081-049] Leinster.
