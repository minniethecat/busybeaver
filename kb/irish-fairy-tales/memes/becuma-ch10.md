[069-001] He went forward in his light bark, and at some moment found that he

[069-002] had parted from those seas and was adrift on vaster and more turbulent

[069-003] billows. From those dark-green surges there gaped at him monstrous

[069-004] and cavernous jaws; and round, wicked, red-rimmed, bulging eyes stared

[069-005] fixedly at the boat. A ridge of inky water rushed foaming mountainously

[069-006] on his board, and behind that ridge came a vast warty head that gurgled

[069-007] and groaned. But at these vile creatures he thrust with his lengthy

[069-008] spear or stabbed at closer reach with a dagger.

[069-009] He was not spared one of the terrors which had been foretold. Thus, in

[069-010] the dark thick oak forest he slew the seven hags and buried them in the

[069-011] molten lead which they had heated for him. He climbed an icy mountain,

[069-012] the cold breath of which seemed to slip into his body and chip off

[069-013] inside of his bones, and there, until he mastered the sort of climbing

[069-014] on ice, for each step that he took upwards he slipped back ten steps.

[069-015] Almost his heart gave way before he learned to climb that venomous hill.

[069-016] In a forked glen into which he slipped at night-fall he was surrounded

[069-017] by giant toads, who spat poison, and were icy as the land they lived

[069-018] in, and were cold and foul and savage. At Sliav Saev he encountered the

[069-019] long-maned lions who lie in wait for the beasts of the world, growling

[069-020] woefully as they squat above their prey and crunch those terrified

[069-021] bones. He came on Ailill of the Black Teeth sitting on the bridge that

[069-022] spanned a torrent, and the grim giant was grinding his teeth on a pillar

[069-023] stone. Art drew nigh unobserved and brought him low.

[069-024] It was not for nothing that these difficulties and dangers were in his

[069-025] path. These things and creatures were the invention of Dog Head, the

[069-026] wife of Morgan, for it had become known to her that she would die on the

[069-027] day her daughter was wooed. Therefore none of the dangers encountered

[069-028] by Art were real, but were magical chimeras conjured against him by the

[069-029] great witch.

[069-030] Affronting all, conquering all, he came in time to Morgan’s [[dun]], a place

[069-031] so lovely that after the miseries through which he had struggled he

[069-032] almost wept to see beauty again.

[069-033] Delvcaem knew that he was coming. She was waiting for him, yearning for

[069-034] him. To her mind Art was not only love, he was freedom, for the poor

[069-035] girl was a captive in her father’s home. A great pillar an hundred feet

[069-036] high had been built on the roof of Morgan’s palace, and on the top of

[069-037] this pillar a tiny room had been constructed, and in this room Delvcaem

[069-038] was a prisoner.

[069-039] She was lovelier in shape than any other princess of the Many-Coloured

[069-040] Land. She was wiser than all the other women of that land, and she

[069-041] was skilful in music, embroidery, and chastity, and in all else that

[069-042] pertained to the knowledge of a queen.

[069-043] Although Delvcaem’s mother wished nothing but ill to Art, she yet

[069-044] treated him with the courtesy proper in a queen on the one hand and

[069-045] fitting towards the son of the King of Ireland on the other. Therefore,

[069-046] when Art entered the palace he was met and kissed, and he was bathed and

[069-047] clothed and fed. Two young girls came to him then, having a cup in

[069-048] each of their hands, and presented him with the kingly drink, but,

[069-049] remembering the warning which Credl had given him, he drank only from

[069-050] the right-hand cup and escaped the poison. Next he was visited by

[069-051] Delvcaem’s mother, Dog Head, daughter of the King of the Dog Heads, and

[069-052] Morgan’s queen. She was dressed in full armour, and she challenged Art

[069-053] to fight with her.

[069-054] It was a woeful combat, for there was no craft or sagacity unknown to

[069-055] her, and Art would infallibly have perished by her hand but that her

[069-056] days were numbered, her star was out, and her time had come. It was her

[069-057] head that rolled on the ground when the combat was over, and it was

[069-058] her head that grinned and shrivelled on the vacant spike which she had

[069-059] reserved for Art’s.

[069-060] Then Art liberated Delvcaem from her prison at the top of the pillar

[069-061] and they were affianced together. But the ceremony had scarcely been

[069-062] completed when the tread of a single man caused the palace to quake and

[069-063] seemed to jar the world.

[069-064] It was Morgan returning to the palace.

[069-065] The gloomy king challenged him to combat also, and in his honour Art

[069-066] put on the battle harness which he had brought from Ireland. He wore a

[069-067] breastplate and helmet of gold, a mantle of blue satin swung from his

[069-068] shoulders, his left hand was thrust into the grips of a purple

[069-069] shield, deeply bossed with silver, and in the other hand he held the

[069-070] wide-grooved, blue hilted sword which had rung so often into fights and

[069-071] combats, and joyous feats and exercises.

[069-072] Up to this time the trials through which he had passed had seemed so

[069-073] great that they could not easily be added to. But if all those trials

[069-074] had been gathered into one vast calamity they would not equal one half

[069-075] of the rage and catastrophe of his war with Morgan.

[069-076] For what he could not effect by arms Morgan would endeavour by guile,

[069-077] so that while Art drove at him or parried a crafty blow, the shape of

[069-078] Morgan changed before his eyes, and the monstrous king was having at him

[069-079] in another form, and from a new direction.

[069-080] It was well for the son of the Ard-Ri’ that he had been beloved by the

[069-081] poets and magicians of his land, and that they had taught him all that

[069-082] was known of [[shapeshifting|shape-changing]] and words of power.

[069-083] He had need of all these.

[069-084] At times, for the weapon must change with the enemy, they fought with

[069-085] their foreheads as two giant stags, and the crash of their monstrous

[069-086] onslaught rolled and lingered on the air long after their skulls had

[069-087] parted. Then as two lions, long-clawed, deep-mouthed, snarling, with

[069-088] rigid mane, with red-eyed glare, with flashing, sharp-white fangs, they

[069-089] prowled lithely about each other seeking for an opening. And then as two

[069-090] green-ridged, white-topped, broad-swung, overwhelming, vehement billows

[069-091] of the deep, they met and crashed and sunk into and rolled away from

[069-092] each other; and the noise of these two waves was as the roar of all

[069-093] ocean when the howl of the tempest is drowned in the league-long fury of

[069-094] the surge.

[069-095] But when the wife’s time has come the husband is doomed. He is required

[069-096] elsewhere by his beloved, and Morgan went to rejoin his queen in the

[069-097] world that comes after the [[faery|Many-Coloured Land]], and his victor shore that

[069-098] knowledgeable head away from its giant shoulders.

[069-099] He did not tarry in the Many-Coloured Land, for he had nothing further

[069-100] to seek there. He gathered the things which pleased him best from among

[069-101] the treasures of its grisly king, and with Delvcaem by his side they

[069-102] stepped into the coracle.

[069-103] Then, setting their minds on Ireland, they went there as it were in a

[069-104] flash.

[069-105] The waves of all the world seemed to whirl past them in one huge, green

[069-106] cataract. The sound of all these oceans boomed in their ears for one

[069-107] eternal instant. Nothing was for that moment but a vast roar and pour

[069-108] of waters. Thence they swung into a silence equally vast, and so sudden

[069-109] that it was as thunderous in the comparison as was the elemental rage

[069-110] they quitted. For a time they sat panting, staring at each other,

[069-111] holding each other, lest not only their lives but their very souls

[069-112] should be swirled away in the gusty passage of world within world; and

[069-113] then, looking abroad, they saw the small bright waves creaming by the

[069-114] rocks of [[ben-edair|Ben Edair]], and they blessed the power that had guided and

[069-115] protected them, and they blessed the comely land of Ir.

[069-116] On reaching [[tara|Tara]], Delvcaem, who was more powerful in art and [[enchantment|magic]] than

[069-117] Becuma, ordered the latter to go away, and she did so.

[069-118] She left the king’s side. She came from the midst of the counsellors and

[069-119] magicians. She did not bid farewell to any one. She did not say good-bye

[069-120] to the king as she set out for Ben Edair.

[069-121] Where she could go to no man knew, for she had been ban-ished from the

[069-122] Many-Coloured Land and could not return there. She was forbidden entry

[069-123] to the Shi’ by [[angus-og|Angus Og]], and she could not remain in Ireland. She went

[069-124] to Sasana and she became a queen in that country, and it was she who

[069-125] fostered the rage against the Holy Land which has not ceased to this

[069-126] day.
