## Overview

Royal Meath is the central province of mythical Ireland, containing some of the island's most sacred sites. In Stephens' tales it is the burial-place of Conn's queen Eithne at Tailltin, where nine days of funeral games were held [061-015]–[061-018]. It is also the site of the Hill of Uisneach, where "a great gathering of the men of Ireland" convened for council, games, tournaments, "and universal feastings" [072-005]–[072-008]. Meath is the political heartland: where kings bury their dead, hold court, and assemble the nation.

## Location

Meath occupies the center of Ireland, bordering all four other provinces ([[ulster|Ulster]], [[leinster|Leinster]], [[connacht|Connacht]], [[munster|Munster]]). Its central position made it the natural seat of the High Kingship at [[tara|Tara]], which lies within its territory.

- **Region**: Meath
- **Type**: Royal seat (province containing [[tara|Tara]] and Uisneach)
- **Modern location**: Co. Meath, Ireland

## In the Tales

### Becuma of the White Skin

When Conn's wife Eithne died, he chose Tailltin in Royal Meath for her burial. Stephens enumerates the three great burial-places of Ireland — the Brugh of [[boyne|the Boyne]] in [[ulster|Ulster]], the [[sidhe-mound|sídhe]] mound of Cruachan Ahi in [[connacht|Connacht]], and "Tailltin, in Royal Meath. It was in this last, the sacred place of his own lordship, that Conn laid his wife to rest" [061-013]–[061-018]. The funeral games that followed lasted nine days, a testament to Meath's role as the ceremonial center of the High Kingship.

### The Wooing of Becfola

The Hill of Uisneach in Royal Meath hosts a national gathering: "a great gathering of the men of Ireland … In addition to the Council which was being held, there were games and tournaments and brilliant deployments of troops, and universal feastings" [072-005]–[072-008]. Such assemblies — political, martial, and festive — define Meath's function in the mythic landscape as the place where the nation comes together.

## Mythological Significance

Meath is the province of kingship. While [[ulster|Ulster]] has the great burial-mound of [[boyne|the Boyne]] and [[connacht|Connacht]] the [[sidhe-mound|sídhe]] of Cruachan Ahi, Meath contains both [[tara|Tara]] (the seat of the High King) and Tailltin (the royal burial ground). The province's central geography mirrors its political centrality: it is the axis around which the other four provinces turn.
