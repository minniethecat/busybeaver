[042-001] In the morning as they sat at breakfast four clerics were announced, and

[042-002] when they entered the king looked on them with stern disapproval.

[042-003] “What is the meaning of this journey on Sunday?” he demanded.

[042-004] A lank-jawed, thin-browed brother, with uneasy, intertwining fingers,

[042-005] and a deep-set, venomous eye, was the spokesman of those four.

[042-006] “Indeed,” he said, and the fingers of his right hand strangled and did

[042-007] to death the fingers of his left hand, “indeed, we have transgressed by

[042-008] order.”

[042-009] “Explain that.”

[042-010] “We have been sent to you hurriedly by our master, Molasius of

[042-011] Devenish.”

[042-012] “A pious, a saintly man,” the king interrupted, “and one who does not

[042-013] countenance transgressions of the Sunday.”

[042-014] “We were ordered to tell you as follows,” said the grim cleric, and he

[042-015] buried the fingers of his right hand in his left fist, so that one could

[042-016] not hope to see them resurrected again. “It was the duty of one of

[042-017] the Brothers of Devenish,” he continued, “to turn out the cattle this

[042-018] morning before the dawn of day, and that Brother, while in his duty, saw

[042-019] eight comely young men who fought together.”

[042-020] “On the morning of Sunday,” Dermod exploded.

[042-021] The cleric nodded with savage emphasis.

[042-022] “On the morning of this self-same and instant sacred day.”

[042-023] “Tell on,” said the king wrathfully.

[042-024] But terror gripped with sudden fingers at [[becfola|Becfola]]’s heart.

[042-025] “Do not tell horrid stories on the Sunday,” she pleaded. “No good can

[042-026] come to any one from such a tale.”

[042-027] “Nay, this must be told, sweet lady,” said the king. But the cleric

[042-028] stared at her glumly, forbiddingly, and resumed his story at a gesture.

[042-029] “Of these eight men, seven were killed.”

[042-030] “They are in hell,” the king said gloomily.

[042-031] “In hell they are,” the cleric replied with enthusiasm.

[042-032] “And the one that was not killed?”

[042-033] “He is alive,” that cleric responded.

[042-034] “He would be,” the monarch assented. “Tell your tale.”

[042-035] “Molasius had those seven miscreants buried, and he took from their

[042-036] unhallowed necks and from their lewd arms and from their unblessed

[042-037] weapons the load of two men in gold and silver treasure.”

[042-038] “Two men’s load!” said Dermod thoughtfully.

[042-039] “That much,” said the lean cleric. “No more, no less. And he has sent us

[042-040] to find out what part of that hellish treasure belongs to the Brothers

[042-041] of Devenish and how much is the property of the king.”

[042-042] Becfola again broke in, speaking graciously, regally, hastily: “Let

[042-043] those Brothers have the entire of the treasure, for it is Sunday

[042-044] treasure, and as such it will bring no luck to any one.”

[042-045] The cleric again looked at her coldly, with a harsh-lidded, small-set,

[042-046] grey-eyed glare, and waited for the king’s reply.

[042-047] Dermod pondered, shaking his head as to an argument on his left side,

[042-048] and then nodding it again as to an argument on his right.

[042-049] “It shall be done as this sweet queen advises. Let a reliquary be formed

[042-050] with cunning workmanship of that gold and silver, dated with my date and

[042-051] signed with my name, to be in memory of my grandmother who gave birth to

[042-052] a lamb, to a salmon, and then to my father, the Ard-Ri’. And, as to the

[042-053] treasure that remains over, a pastoral staff may be beaten from it in

[042-054] honour of Molasius, the pious man.”

[042-055] “The story is not ended,” said that glum, spike-chinned cleric.

[042-056] The king moved with jovial impatience.

[042-057] “If you continue it,” he said, “it will surely come to an end some time.

[042-058] A stone on a stone makes a house, dear heart, and a word on a word tells

[042-059] a tale.”

[042-060] The cleric wrapped himself into himself, and became lean and menacing.

[042-061] He whispered: “Besides the young man, named Flann, who was not slain,

[042-062] there was another person present at the scene and the combat and the

[042-063] transgression of Sunday.”

[042-064] “Who was that person?” said the alarmed monarch.

[042-065] The cleric spiked forward his chin, and then butted forward his brow.

[042-066] “It was the wife of the king,” he shouted. “It was the woman called

[042-067] Becfola. It was that woman,” he roared, and he extended a lean,

[042-068] inflexible, unending first finger at the queen.

[042-069] “Dog!” the king stammered, starting up.

[042-070] “If that be in truth a woman,” the cleric screamed.

[042-071] “What do you mean?” the king demanded in wrath and terror.

[042-072] “Either she is a woman of this world to be punished, or she is a woman

[042-073] of the Shi’ to be banished, but this holy morning she was in the Shi’,

[042-074] and her arms were about the neck of Flann.”

[042-075] The king sank back in his chair stupefied, gazing from one to the other,

[042-076] and then turned an unseeing, fear-dimmed eye towards Becfola.

[042-077] “Is this true, my pulse?” he murmured.

[042-078] “It is true,” Becfola replied, and she became suddenly to the king’s eye

[042-079] a whiteness and a stare. He pointed to the door.

[042-080] “Go to your engagement,” he stammered. “Go to that Flann.”

[042-081] “He is waiting for me,” said Becfola with proud shame, “and the thought

[042-082] that he should wait wrings my heart.”

[042-083] She went out from the palace then. She went away from [[tara|Tara]]: and in all

[042-084] Ireland and in the world of living men she was not seen again, and she

[042-085] was never heard of again.
