[085-001] They met him near the place where the games were played.

[085-002] “Good my soul, Tibraide’!” cried the King of [[leinster|Leinster]], and he gave

[085-003] [[mongan|Mongan]] a kiss. Mongan kissed him back again.

[085-004] “Amen, amen,” said mac an Da’v.

[085-005] “What for?” said the King of Leinster.

[085-006] And then mac an Da’v began to sneeze, for he didn’t know what for.

[085-007] “It is a long time since I saw you, Tibraide’,” said the king, “but at

[085-008] this minute I am in great haste and hurry. Go you on before me to the

[085-009] fortress, and you can talk to the queen that you’ll find there, she that

[085-010] used to be the King of [[ulster|Ulster]]’s wife. Kevin Cochlach, my charioteer,

[085-011] will go with you, and I will follow you myself in a while.”

[085-012] The King of Leinster went off then, and Mongan and his servant went with

[085-013] the charioteer and the people.

[085-014] Mongan read away out of the book, for he found it interesting, and he

[085-015] did not want to talk to the charioteer, and mac an Da’v cried amen,

[085-016] amen, every time that Mongan took his breath. The people who were going

[085-017] with them said to one another that mac an Da’v was a queer kind of

[085-018] clerk, and that they had never seen any one who had such a mouthful of

[085-019] amens.

[085-020] But in a while they came to the fortress, and they got into it without

[085-021] any trouble, for Kevin Cochlach, the king’s charioteer, brought them in.

[085-022] Then they were led to the room where [[duv-laca|Duv Laca]] was, and as he went into

[085-023] that room Mongan shut his eyes, for he did not want to look at Duv Laca

[085-024] while other people might be looking at him.

[085-025] “Let everybody leave this room, while I am talking to the queen,” said

[085-026] he; and all the attendants left the room, except one, and she wouldn’t

[085-027] go, for she wouldn’t leave her mistress.

[085-028] Then Mongan opened his eyes and he saw Duv Laca, and he made a great

[085-029] bound to her and took her in his arms, and mac an Da’v made a savage and

[085-030] vicious and terrible jump at the attendant, and took her in his arms,

[085-031] and bit her ear and kissed her neck and wept down into her back.

[085-032] “Go away,” said the girl, “unhand me, villain,” said she.

[085-033] “I will not,” said mac an Da’v, “for I’m your own husband, I’m your own

[085-034] mac, your little mac, your macky-wac-wac.” Then the attendant gave a

[085-035] little squeal, and she bit him on each ear and kissed his neck and wept

[085-036] down into his back, and said that it wasn’t true and that it was.
