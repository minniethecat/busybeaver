## Overview

Slieve Bloom is not a court or a battlefield — it is the hidden place. In the tale of Fionn's boyhood, "the youngster was brought to the woods of Slieve Bloom and was nursed there in secret" [012-025]–[012-027], hidden from the clann-Morna who had killed his father and would kill the son if they found him. It is the wilderness cradle of the greatest hero of the [[fianna|Fianna]] cycle, and its obscurity is its power.

## Location

The Slieve Bloom Mountains rise on the border of modern-day Co. Laois and Co. Offaly, in the central south of Ireland. In the mythic geography they sit at the edge of [[leinster|Leinster]], remote enough to provide concealment but still within reach of the wider world.

- **Region**: [[leinster|Leinster]]
- **Type**: Hill (wooded mountain wilderness)
- **Modern location**: Slieve Bloom Mountains, Co. Laois/Offaly, Ireland

## In the Tales

### The Boyhood of Fionn

After Fionn's father was killed by the [[clann-morna|sons of Morna]], his mother [[muirne|Muirne]] sent the infant away for protection. "The youngster was brought to the woods of Slieve Bloom and was nursed there in secret" [012-025]–[012-027]. The women who raised him were "fond of him, for other than Fionn there was no life in the house" [012-028]–[012-029]. The woods provided a sanctuary where the boy could grow strong away from the political violence that had claimed his father.

Later, after his first adventures abroad, Fionn "was brought home again to the woods of Slieve Bloom, but he had gathered great knowledge and new supplenesses" [018-040]–[018-042]. Slieve Bloom was the home base to which the boy returned between his wanderings, absorbing what he had learned before setting out again. It is this rhythm — refuge, adventure, return — that shapes the young hero.

## Mythological Significance

Slieve Bloom is the hidden place in the hero's journey — the wilderness that shelters, the obscurity that protects. Without Slieve Bloom there is no Fionn: the woods were the necessary condition for the hero's survival through childhood. In the larger geography of the tales, it provides the counterpoint to the public seats of power like [[tara|Tara]] and [[allen|Allen]] — the place where greatness gestates in secret.
