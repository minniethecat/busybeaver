[064-001] The High King did not know where exactly he should look for such a

[064-002] saviour, but he was well educated and knew how to look for whatever was

[064-003] lacking. This knowledge will be useful to those upon whom a similar duty

[064-004] should ever devolve.

[064-005] He went to [[ben-edair|Ben Edair]]. He stepped into a coracle and pushed out to the

[064-006] deep, and he permitted the coracle to go as the winds and the waves

[064-007] directed it.

[064-008] In such a way he voyaged among the small islands of the sea until he

[064-009] lost all knowledge of his course and was adrift far out in ocean. He was

[064-010] under the guidance of the stars and the great luminaries.

[064-011] He saw black seals that stared and barked and dived dancingly, with the

[064-012] round turn of a bow and the forward onset of an arrow. Great whales came

[064-013] heaving from the green-hued void, blowing a wave of the sea high

[064-014] into the air from their noses and smacking their wide flat tails

[064-015] thunder-ously on the water. Porpoises went snorting past in bands and

[064-016] clans. Small fish came sliding and flickering, and all the outlandish

[064-017] creatures of the deep rose by his bobbing craft and swirled and sped

[064-018] away.

[064-019] Wild storms howled by him so that the boat climbed painfully to the sky

[064-020] on a mile-high wave, balanced for a tense moment on its level top, and

[064-021] sped down the glassy side as a stone goes furiously from a sling.

[064-022] Or, again, caught in the chop of a broken sea, it stayed shuddering and

[064-023] backing, while above his head there was only a low sad sky, and around

[064-024] him the lap and wash of grey waves that were never the same and were

[064-025] never different.

[064-026] After long staring on the hungry nothingness of air and water he would

[064-027] stare on the skin-stretched fabric of his boat as on a strangeness, or

[064-028] he would examine his hands and the texture of his skin and the stiff

[064-029] black hairs that grew behind his knuckles and sprouted around his ring,

[064-030] and he found in these things newness and wonder.

[064-031] Then, when days of storm had passed, the low grey clouds shivered and

[064-032] cracked in a thousand places, each grim islet went scudding to the

[064-033] horizon as though terrified by some great breadth, and when they had

[064-034] passed he stared into vast after vast of blue infinity, in the depths

[064-035] of which his eyes stayed and could not pierce, and wherefrom they could

[064-036] scarcely be withdrawn. A sun beamed thence that filled the air with

[064-037] sparkle and the sea with a thousand lights, and looking on these he was

[064-038] reminded of his home at [[tara|Tara]]: of the columns of white and yellow bronze

[064-039] that blazed out sunnily on the sun, and the red and white and yellow

[064-040] painted roofs that beamed at and astonished the eye.

[064-041] Sailing thus, lost in a succession of days and nights, of winds and

[064-042] calms, he came at last to an island.

[064-043] His back was turned to it, and long before he saw it he smelled it and

[064-044] wondered; for he had been sitting as in a daze, musing on a change that

[064-045] had seemed to come in his changeless world; and for a long time he could

[064-046] not tell what that was which made a difference on the salt-whipped wind

[064-047] or why he should be excited. For suddenly he had become excited and his

[064-048] heart leaped in violent expectation.

[064-049] “It is an October smell,” he said.

[064-050] “It is apples that I smell.”

[064-051] He turned then and saw the island, fragrant with apple trees, sweet with

[064-052] wells of wine; and, hearkening towards the shore, his ears, dulled yet

[064-053] with the unending rhythms of the sea, distinguished and were filled

[064-054] with song; for the isle was, as it were, a nest of birds, and they sang

[064-055] joyously, sweetly, triumphantly.

[064-056] He landed on that lovely island, and went forward under the darting

[064-057] birds, under the apple boughs, skirting fragrant lakes about which were

[064-058] woods of the sacred hazel and into which the nuts of knowledge fell and

[064-059] swam; and he blessed the gods of his people because of the ground that

[064-060] did not shiver and because of the deeply rooted trees that could not gad

[064-061] or budge.
