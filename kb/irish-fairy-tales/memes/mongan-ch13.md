[082-001] “I think,” said the Flame Lady, “that whoever lost that woman had no

[082-002] reason to be sad.”

[082-003] [[mongan|Mongan]] took her chin in his hand and kissed her lips.

[082-004] “All that you say is lovely, for you are lovely,” said he, “and you are

[082-005] my delight and the joy of the world.”

[082-006] Then the attendants brought him wine, and he drank so joyously of that

[082-007] and so deeply, that those who observed him thought he would surely burst

[082-008] and drown them. But he laughed loudly and with enormous delight, until

[082-009] the vessels of gold and silver and bronze chimed mellowly to his peal

[082-010] and the rafters of the house went creaking.

[082-011] Said he:

[082-012] Mongan loved [[duv-laca|Duv Laca of the White Hand]] better than he loved his life,

[082-013] better than he loved his honour. The kingdoms of the world did not weigh

[082-014] with him beside the string of her shoe. He would not look at a sunset

[082-015] if he could see her. He would not listen to a harp if he could hear her

[082-016] speak, for she was the delight of ages, the gem of time, and the wonder

[082-017] of the world till Doom.

[082-018] She went to [[leinster|Leinster]] with the king of that country, and when she had

[082-019] gone Mongan fell grievously sick, so that it did not seem he could ever

[082-020] recover again; and he began to waste and wither, and he began to look

[082-021] like a skeleton, and a bony structure, and a misery.

[082-022] Now this also must be known.

[082-023] Duv Laca had a young attendant, who was her foster-sister as well as her

[082-024] servant, and on the day that she got married to Mongan, her attendant

[082-025] was married to mac an Da’v, who was servant and foster-brother to

[082-026] Mongan. When Duv Laca went away with the King of Leinster, her servant,

[082-027] mac an Da’v’s wife, went with her, so there were two wifeless men

[082-028] in [[ulster|Ulster]] at that time, namely, Mongan the king and mac an Da’v his

[082-029] servant.

[082-030] One day as Mongan sat in the sun, brooding lamentably on his fate, mac

[082-031] an Da’v came to him.

[082-032] “How are things with you, master?” asked Mac an Da’v.

[082-033] “Bad,” said Mongan.

[082-034] “It was a poor day brought you off with Mananna’n to the Land of

[082-035] Promise,” said his servant.

[082-036] “Why should you think that?” inquired Mongan.

[082-037] “Because,” said mac an Da’v, “you learned nothing in the [[faery|Land of Promise]]

[082-038] except how to eat a lot of food and how to do nothing in a deal of

[082-039] time.”

[082-040] “What business is it of yours?” said Mongan angrily.

[082-041] “It is my business surely,” said mac an Da’v, “for my wife has gone off

[082-042] to Leinster with your wife, and she wouldn’t have gone if you hadn’t

[082-043] made a bet and a bargain with that accursed king.”

[082-044] Mac an Da’v began to weep then.

[082-045] “I didn’t make a bargain with any king,” said he, “and yet my wife has

[082-046] gone away with one, and it’s all because of you.”

[082-047] “There is no one sorrier for you than I am,” said Mongan.

[082-048] “There is indeed,” said mac an Da’v, “for I am sorrier myself.”

[082-049] Mongan roused himself then.

[082-050] “You have a claim on me truly,” said he, “and I will not have any one

[082-051] with a claim on me that is not satisfied. Go,” he said to mac an Da’v,

[082-052] “to that fairy place we both know of. You remember the baskets I left

[082-053] there with the sod from Ireland in one and the sod from Scotland in the

[082-054] other; bring me the baskets and sods.”

[082-055] “Tell me the why of this?” said his servant.

[082-056] “The King of Leinster will ask his wizards what I am doing, and this is

[082-057] what I will be doing. I will get on your back with a foot in each of the

[082-058] baskets, and when [[branduv|Branduv]] asks the wizards where I am they will tell him

[082-059] that I have one leg in Ireland and one leg in Scotland, and as long as

[082-060] they tell him that he will think he need not bother himself about me,

[082-061] and we will go into Leinster that way.”

[082-062] “No bad way either,” said mac an Da’v.

[082-063] They set out then.
