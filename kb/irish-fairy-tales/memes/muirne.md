# Muirne

## Overview

Muirne (also Muireann), known as "beautiful, long-haired Muirne," is the mother of [[fionn-mac-uail|Fionn mac Uail]] and a pivotal figure in the Fenian cycle. She was the wife of Cumhal, leader of the [[fianna|Fianna]], and after his death at the hands of [[clann-morna|Clann Morna]], she became the protector of their infant son — hiding him from his father's killers, arranging his fosterage with druid women and warriors, and visiting him in secret throughout his boyhood. Her beauty was legendary, and her second marriage to the King of [[kerry|Kerry]] linked Fionn's lineage to the royal houses of southern Ireland.

## Biography

Muirne was the wife of Cumhal (also called Uall mac Balsc), the leader of the [[fianna|Fianna]], and bore him a son — Fionn [019-111]. After Cumhal was killed by the [[clann-morna|sons of Morna]], Muirne faced an impossible choice: keep her son with her and risk his death at the hands of his father's enemies, or send him away and preserve his life. She chose the latter.

"Beautiful, long-haired Muirne" is the epithet that follows her through the tales [012-014][013-027], marking her as a figure of almost otherworldly beauty — a trait shared with many mothers of heroes in Irish myth. When her first lord Uall died, "Muirne got married again to the King" of [[kerry|Kerry]] [012-022], creating a political alliance that would later support Fionn's rise. The narrative reflects on her feelings: "as for Muirne she must have loved her lord" [019-118], a rare moment of emotional interiority in a genre that normally dwells on external action.

## Role in Fionn's Boyhood

Muirne's defining act is the concealment of her son. Understanding that the [[clann-morna|sons of Morna]] would kill any heir of Cumhal, she sent Fionn to be raised in the wilderness by the women [[druid|druids]] [[bovmall|Bovmall]] and [[lia-luachra|Lia Luachra]], and by the warriors who guarded him. She visited him "secretly, for she feared the [[clann-morna|sons of Morna]]" [013-028], slipping away before dawn — a mother who could only love her son in the shadows.

When Fionn grew older and began to reclaim his birthright, Muirne came to visit him at [[allen|wide Allen of Leinster]], bringing her young sister [[tuiren|Tuiren]] with her [026-030]. This visit marked the beginning of Fionn's public acknowledgment and the first step toward his assuming leadership of the [[fianna|Fianna]].

## Significance

Muirne belongs to the archetype of the threatened mother in heroic narrative — like Thetis hiding Achilles, or Rhea hiding Zeus. Her role is structurally essential: without her decision to part with her son, there would be no [[fionn-mac-uail|Fionn mac Uail]], no Fenian cycle, no great tales of the [[fianna|Fianna]]. Her second marriage to the King of [[kerry|Kerry]] also grounds Fionn's lineage in the political geography of Ireland, connecting the hero to both northern (Cumhal's) and southern ([[kerry|Kerry]]'s) royal lines. She embodies the quiet, private cost of heroic destiny — the mother who gives up her child so that the hero may live.
