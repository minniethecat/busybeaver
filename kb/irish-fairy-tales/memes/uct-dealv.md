# Uct Dealv

## Identity

Uct Dealv, whose name means "Fair Breast," is a lady of the Shi' — one of the fairy folk who dwell in the Otherworld beneath the *sidhe* mounds of Ireland. She is of the Tuatha De Danann kindred whose people retreated underground after the Milesian conquest, living in a parallel realm that intersects with the mortal world at certain times and places.

Before the events of *The Wooing of Becfola*, Uct Dealv had been the lover of [[iollan-eachtach|Iollan Eachtach]], a warrior of the [[fianna|Fianna]], and they had been sweethearts for years. Their bond, however, was disrupted when Iollan's affections turned elsewhere, setting in motion a chain of jealousy and magical revenge that would entangle the High King of Ireland himself.

## In the Tales

### The Lover Spurned

Before Iollan Eachtach ever joined the Fianna, he and Uct Dealv had been devoted to one another. "Before he joined the Fianna he had been in love with a lady of the Shi', named Uct Dealv (Fair Breast), and they had been sweethearts for years." [^1] Their love was the enchanted kind that crosses the boundary between the mortal and fairy realms — a theme that recurs throughout the Irish mythological tradition, from the love of [[fionn-mac-uail|Fionn]] and [[saeve|Saeve]] to that of [[mongan|Mongan]] and the Flame Lady.

But when Iollan fell in love with [[becfola|Becfola]], the young wife of the High King [[conn-of-the-hundred-battles|Conn of the Hundred Battles]], Uct Dealv's jealousy turned to fury. She railed bitterly against the woman who had supplanted her in Iollan's heart, and sought to punish both her unfaithful lover and his new beloved.

### The Abduction of Becfola

In her rage, Uct Dealv used her fairy powers to seize Becfola. "As they paced along Uct Dealv railed bitterly against the hound, and shook and jerked her chain." [^1] The hound — a enchanted beast — was her instrument in the abduction, dragging Becfola away from the mortal world. Uct Dealv's cry of "Ah, supplanter! Ah, taker of another girl's sweetheart!" [^1] reveals the raw emotional wound beneath the magical act: she is not merely a villain but a betrayed lover, her grievance as human as it is supernatural.

She intended to deliver Becfola to [[fergus-true-lips|Fergus True-Lips]], perhaps as a twisted gift or a further twist in her revenge, declaring, "It is to Fergus I shall bring you." [^1] But her scheme was ultimately foiled when Iollan, guessing her involvement, tracked her to her fairy hill. "He knew the way, and in no great time he came to the hill where Uct Dealv was." [^1]

## The Fairy Lover Type

Uct Dealv belongs to a distinct type in Irish myth: the fairy lover scorned. This figure appears across the corpus with remarkable consistency — a woman of the [[tuatha-de-danann|Sidhe]] who has given her love to a mortal man, only to be abandoned when his affections turn elsewhere. The pattern is not merely a romantic plot device but a narrative mechanism for exploring the asymmetrical relationship between the immortal and mortal worlds.

The fairy lover possesses powers that make her grievance dangerous — Uct Dealv's hazel rod can transform, her knowledge of the [[faery|Shi']] paths allows her to abduct — but she cannot command love. This is the crucial asymmetry: for all her magic, she is subject to the same emotional vulnerabilities as any mortal woman. Her rage is proportionate to her powerlessness in the one domain that matters to her.

Compare her to three other fairy lovers in the Irish tradition:

- **[saeve|Saeve]]**, loved by [[fionn-mac-uail|Fionn mac Uail]], was transformed into a fawn by the Dark Druid's jealousy — a victim of supernatural power, not its wielder. Saeve's tragedy is passive; Uct Dealv's is active.
- **[[becuma|Becuma of the White Skin]]**, exiled from the [[many-coloured-land|Many-Coloured Land]], sought mortal love as refuge and ambition. Where Uct Dealv fights to recover what she has lost, Becuma schemes to acquire what she has never had.
- **The [[flame-lady|Flame Lady]]**, also a mortal woman loved by a man of mixed heritage, channels her passion into fierce loyalty rather than destructive jealousy. She is Uct Dealv's mirror image — the lover who keeps what the other has lost.

Uct Dealv is thus the most human of the Sidhe women in the Stephens corpus: her magic is real but her motivation is utterly mortal. She does not seek power or revenge in the abstract — she wants back the man she loves, and when she cannot have him, she wants the woman who took him to suffer. Her story acknowledges, with an honesty rare in mythological narrative, that the boundary between the fairy and the human is not a line between reason and passion, but between different expressions of the same fundamental drives.

## The Hazel Rod

The hazel rod that Uct Dealv draws from beneath her mantle [027-072] is not merely a wand but an object dense with Irish mythological significance. [[hazel-rod|The hazel]] is the tree of wisdom in Irish tradition — the nine hazels of Segais dropped their nuts into the [[well-of-segais|Well of Segais]], where the [[salmon-of-knowledge|Salmon of Knowledge]] ate them and gained all wisdom. A hazel rod in a woman's hand thus signals access to Otherworld knowledge, the kind that can transform perception and reshape reality.

When Uct Dealv uses the rod, the effect is specifically perceptual: it changes how Becfola is seen, or how the world appears to her. This is hazel magic in its purest form — not the creation of illusions but the alteration of the faculty through which reality is apprehended. The hazel rod does not make things other than they are; it makes the perceiver other than she was. In this it embodies the Sidhe's characteristic mode of power: not violence but transformation, not coercion but the rewiring of perception itself.

## Significance

Uct Dealv represents a common figure in Irish myth: the fairy lover scorned, whose supernatural powers make her personal grievance a matter of mortal danger. She is a mirror to other Otherworld women in the Stephens corpus — [[becuma|Becuma of the White Skin]], exiled from the Many-Coloured Land for her own transgressions, and [[saeve|Saeve]], transformed by the Dark Druid's jealousy. Where Becuma is ambitious and Saeve is a victim, Uct Dealv is a figure of active, dangerous heartbreak — a reminder that the Sidhe, for all their magic, are driven by passions as fierce and fallible as any mortal's.
