# Bran

## Identity

Bran was one of the two legendary wolfhounds of [[fionn-mac-uail|Fionn mac Uail]], paired always with his brother [[sceolan|Sceolan]]. They were not ordinary dogs but were born of a Sidhe enchantment — whelps of a woman of the [[tuatha-de-danann|Tuatha De Danann]] who had been transformed into a hound. Bran and Sceolan were Fionn's constant companions in the hunt and among his most beloved attachments, ranking in his affections alongside his son [[oisin|Oisin]] and his closest warriors. [^1]

## Origin

The birth of Bran and Sceolan is one of the more remarkable episodes in the Fenian cycle. Their mother was a woman of the Sidhe, the sister of Uct Dealv ([[uct-dealv|Fair Breast]]), who had been transformed into a hound by the enchantment of a rival. In this form she gave birth to two whelps — Bran and Sceolan — who inherited both the keen senses of the Sidhe and the fierce loyalty of hounds. The enchantment could not be broken, and so the two whelps remained in their hound-form, eventually coming into Fionn's possession. [^1]

## In the Tales

Bran appears throughout the Fenian tales as Fionn's most trusted hound. In *The Birth of Bran*, his origin is told in full. In *The Little Brawl at Allen*, Bran and Sceolan accompany Fionn to the [[wide-allen-of-leinster|Hill of Allen]]. In *The Enchanted Cave of Cesh Corran*, it is Bran and Sceolan whose furious barking alerts Fionn to the danger of the cave. [^1]

Bran's intelligence and loyalty were legendary. He could track any quarry across any terrain, and his bond with Fionn was such that he understood his master's commands before they were fully spoken. A thorn driven into Bran's paw was said to cause Fionn as much pain as if it had pierced his own flesh — so close was their connection. [^1]

> Fionn loved a chase at any hour, and, with Bran and Sceo'lan, he outstripped the men and dogs of his troop. [031-001]

## Significance

Bran represents the deep bond between the heroic world of the Fianna and the animal realm — a bond that, in Irish mythology, is never merely domestic. The great hounds of the heroes are kin to the Sidhe, their origins tangled in enchantment and transformation. Bran's dual nature — Sidhe-born yet hound-formed, magical yet fiercely loyal — makes him a bridge figure between the mortal and Otherworld domains, just as the Fianna themselves patrol the boundary between civilization and wilderness.
