[080-001] [[duv-laca|Duv Laca]] wanted to know where the cows came from, and [[mongan|Mongan]] told her

[080-002] that the King of [[leinster|Leinster]] had given them to him. She fell in love with

[080-003] them as Mongan had done, but there was nobody in the world could have

[080-004] avoided loving those cows: such cows they were! such wonders! Mongan

[080-005] and Duv Laca used to play chess together, and then they would go out

[080-006] together to look at the cows, and then they would go in together and

[080-007] would talk to each other about the cows. Everything they did they did

[080-008] together, for they loved to be with each other.

[080-009] However, a change came.

[080-010] One morning a great noise of voices and trampling of horses and rattle

[080-011] of armour came about the palace. Mongan looked from the window.

[080-012] “Who is coming?” asked Duv Laca.

[080-013] But he did not answer her.

[080-014] “The noise must announce the visit of a king,” Duv Laca continued.

[080-015] But Mongan did not say a word. Duv Laca then went to the window.

[080-016] “Who is that king?” she asked.

[080-017] And her husband replied to her then.

[080-018] “That is the King of Leinster,” said he mournfully.

[080-019] “Well,” said Duv Laca surprised, “is he not welcome?”

[080-020] “He is welcome indeed,” said Mongan lamentably.

[080-021] “Let us go out and welcome him properly,” Duv Laca suggested.

[080-022] “Let us not go near him at all,” said Mongan, “for he is coming to

[080-023] complete his bargain.”

[080-024] “What bargain are you talking about?” Duv Laca asked. But Mongan would

[080-025] not answer that.

[080-026] “Let us go out,” said he, “for we must go out.”

[080-027] Mongan and Duv Laca went out then and welcomed the King of Leinster.

[080-028] They brought him and his chief men into the palace, and water was

[080-029] brought for their baths, and rooms were appointed for them, and

[080-030] everything was done that should be done for guests.

[080-031] That night there was a feast, and after the feast there was a banquet,

[080-032] and all through the feast and the banquet the King of Leinster stared

[080-033] at Duv Laca with joy, and sometimes his breast was delivered of great

[080-034] sighs, and at times he moved as though in perturbation of spirit and

[080-035] mental agony.

[080-036] “There is something wrong with the King of Leinster,” Duv Laca

[080-037] whispered.

[080-038] “I don’t care if there is,” said Mongan.

[080-039] “You must ask what he wants.”

[080-040] “But I don’t want to know it,” said Mongan. “Nevertheless, you musk ask

[080-041] him,” she insisted.

[080-042] So Mongan did ask him, and it was in a melancholy voice that he asked

[080-043] it.

[080-044] “Do you want anything?” said he to the King of Leinster.

[080-045] “I do indeed,” said [[branduv|Branduv]].

[080-046] “If it is in [[ulster|Ulster]] I will get it for you,” said Mongan mournfully.

[080-047] “It is in Ulster,” said Branduv.

[080-048] Mongan did not want to say anything more then, but the King of Leinster

[080-049] was so intent and everybody else was listening and Duv Laca was nudging

[080-050] his arm, so he said: “What is it that you do want?” “I want Duv Laca.”

[080-051] “I want her too,” said Mongan.

[080-052] “You made your bargain,” said the King of Leinster, “my cows and their

[080-053] calves for your Duv Laca, and the man that makes a bargain keeps a

[080-054] bargain.”

[080-055] “I never before heard,” said Mongan, “of a man giving away his own

[080-056] wife.”

[080-057] “Even if you never heard of it before, you must do it now,” said Duv

[080-058] Laca, “for honour is longer than life.”

[080-059] Mongan became angry when Duv Laca said that. His face went red as a

[080-060] sunset, and the veins swelled in his neck and his forehead.

[080-061] “Do you say that?” he cried to Duv Laca.

[080-062] “I do,” said Duv Laca.

[080-063] “Let the King of Leinster take her,” said Mongan.
