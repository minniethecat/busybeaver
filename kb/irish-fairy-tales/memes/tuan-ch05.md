[005-001] “Tell on, my love,” [[finnian|Finnian]] murmured

[005-002] “I was alone,” said Tuan. “I was so alone that my own shadow frightened

[005-003] me. I was so alone that the sound of a bird in flight, or the creaking

[005-004] of a dew-drenched bough, whipped me to cover as a rabbit is scared to

[005-005] his burrow.

[005-006] “The creatures of the forest scented me and knew I was alone. They stole

[005-007] with silken pad behind my back and snarled when I faced them; the long,

[005-008] grey wolves with hanging tongues and staring eyes chased me to my cleft

[005-009] rock; there was no creature so weak but it might hunt me, there was no

[005-010] creature so timid but it might outface me. And so I lived for two tens

[005-011] of years and two years, until I knew all that a beast surmises and had

[005-012] forgotten all that a man had known.

[005-013] “I could pad as gently as any; I could run as tirelessly. I could be

[005-014] invisible and patient as a wild cat crouching among leaves; I could

[005-015] smell danger in my sleep and leap at it with wakeful claws; I could bark

[005-016] and growl and clash with my teeth and tear with them.”

[005-017] “Tell on, my beloved,” said Finnian, “you shall rest in God, dear

[005-018] heart.”

[005-019] “At the end of that time,” said Tuan, “Nemed the son of Agnoman came to

[005-020] Ireland with a fleet of thirty-four barques, and in each barque there

[005-021] were thirty couples of people.”

[005-022] “I have heard it,” said Finnian.

[005-023] “My heart leaped for joy when I saw the great fleet rounding the land,

[005-024] and I followed them along scarped cliffs, leaping from rock to rock like

[005-025] a wild goat, while the ships tacked and swung seeking a harbour. There I

[005-026] stooped to drink at a pool, and I saw myself in the chill water.

[005-027] “I saw that I was hairy and tufty and bristled as a savage boar; that I

[005-028] was lean as a stripped bush; that I was greyer than a badger; withered

[005-029] and wrinkled like an empty sack; naked as a fish; wretched as a starving

[005-030] crow in winter; and on my fingers and toes there were great curving

[005-031] claws, so that I looked like nothing that was known, like nothing that

[005-032] was animal or divine. And I sat by the pool weeping my loneliness and

[005-033] wildness and my stern old age; and I could do no more than cry and

[005-034] lament between the earth and the sky, while the beasts that tracked me

[005-035] listened from behind the trees, or crouched among bushes to stare at me

[005-036] from their drowsy covert.

[005-037] “A storm arose, and when I looked again from my tall cliff I saw that

[005-038] great fleet rolling as in a giant’s hand. At times they were pitched

[005-039] against the sky and staggered aloft, spinning gustily there like

[005-040] wind-blown leaves. Then they were hurled from these dizzy tops to the

[005-041] flat, moaning gulf, to the glassy, inky horror that swirled and whirled

[005-042] between ten waves. At times a wave leaped howling under a ship, and with

[005-043] a buffet dashed it into air, and chased it upwards with thunder stroke

[005-044] on stroke, and followed again, close as a chasing wolf, trying with

[005-045] hammering on hammering to beat in the wide-wombed bottom and suck out

[005-046] the frightened lives through one black gape. A wave fell on a ship and

[005-047] sunk it down with a thrust, stern as though a whole sky had tumbled at

[005-048] it, and the barque did not cease to go down until it crashed and sank in

[005-049] the sand at the bottom of the sea.

[005-050] “The night came, and with it a thousand darknesses fell from the

[005-051] screeching sky. Not a round-eyed creature of the night might pierce an

[005-052] inch of that multiplied gloom. Not a creature dared creep or stand. For

[005-053] a great wind strode the world lashing its league-long whips in cracks

[005-054] of thunder, and singing to itself, now in a world-wide yell, now in an

[005-055] ear-dizzying hum and buzz; or with a long snarl and whine it hovered

[005-056] over the world searching for life to destroy.

[005-057] “And at times, from the moaning and yelping blackness of the sea, there

[005-058] came a sound--thin-drawn as from millions of miles away, distinct as

[005-059] though uttered in the ear like a whisper of confidence--and I knew that

[005-060] a drowning man was calling on his God as he thrashed and was battered

[005-061] into silence, and that a blue-lipped woman was calling on her man as her

[005-062] hair whipped round her brows and she whirled about like a top.

[005-063] “Around me the trees were dragged from earth with dying groans; they

[005-064] leaped into the air and flew like birds. Great waves whizzed from the

[005-065] sea: spinning across the cliffs and hurtling to the earth in monstrous

[005-066] clots of foam; the very rocks came trundling and sidling and grinding

[005-067] among the trees; and in that rage, and in that horror of blackness I

[005-068] fell asleep, or I was beaten into slumber.”
