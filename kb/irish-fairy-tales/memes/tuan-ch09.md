[009-001] “I became the king of the salmon, and, with my multitudes, I ranged on

[009-002] the tides of the world. Green and purple distances were under me: green

[009-003] and gold the sunlit regions above. In these latitudes I moved through a

[009-004] world of amber, myself amber and gold; in those others, in a sparkle

[009-005] of lucent blue, I curved, lit like a living jewel: and in these again,

[009-006] through dusks of ebony all mazed with silver, I shot and shone, the

[009-007] wonder of the sea.

[009-008] “I saw the monsters of the uttermost ocean go heaving by; and the long

[009-009] lithe brutes that are toothed to their tails: and below, where gloom

[009-010] dipped down on gloom, vast, livid tangles that coiled and uncoiled, and

[009-011] lapsed down steeps and hells of the sea where even the salmon could not

[009-012] go.

[009-013] “I knew the sea. I knew the secret caves where ocean roars to ocean; the

[009-014] floods that are icy cold, from which the nose of a salmon leaps back as

[009-015] at a sting; and the warm streams in which we rocked and dozed and were

[009-016] carried forward without motion. I swam on the outermost rim of the great

[009-017] world, where nothing was but the sea and the sky and the salmon; where

[009-018] even the wind was silent, and the water was clear as clean grey rock.

[009-019] “And then, far away in the sea, I remembered [[ulster|Ulster]], and there came on

[009-020] me an instant, uncontrollable anguish to be there. I turned, and through

[009-021] days and nights I swam tirelessly, jubilantly; with terror wakening in

[009-022] me, too, and a whisper through my being that I must reach Ireland or

[009-023] die.

[009-024] “I fought my way to Ulster from the sea.

[009-025] “Ah, how that end of the journey was hard! A sickness was racking in

[009-026] every one of my bones, a languor and weariness creeping through my every

[009-027] fibre and muscle. The waves held me back and held me back; the soft

[009-028] waters seemed to have grown hard; and it was as though I were urging

[009-029] through a rock as I strained towards Ulster from the sea.

[009-030] “So tired I was! I could have loosened my frame and been swept away;

[009-031] I could have slept and been drifted and wafted away; swinging on

[009-032] grey-green billows that had turned from the land and were heaving and

[009-033] mounting and surging to the far blue water.

[009-034] “Only the unconquerable heart of the salmon could brave that end of

[009-035] toil. The sound of the rivers of Ireland racing down to the sea came to

[009-036] me in the last numb effort: the love of Ireland bore me up: the gods of

[009-037] the rivers trod to me in the white-curled breakers, so that I left

[009-038] the sea at long, long last; and I lay in sweet water in the curve of a

[009-039] crannied rock, exhausted, three parts dead, triumphant.”
