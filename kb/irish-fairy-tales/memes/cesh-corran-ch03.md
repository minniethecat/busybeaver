[056-001] BY his arts [[conaran|Conaran]] changed the sight of Fionn’s eyes, and he did the

[056-002] same for Cona’n.

[056-003] In a few minutes Fionn stood up from his place on the mound. Everything

[056-004] was about him as before, and he did not know that he had gone into

[056-005] [[faery|Faery]]. He walked for a minute up and down the hillock. Then, as by

[056-006] chance, he stepped down the sloping end of the mound and stood with his

[056-007] mouth open, staring. He cried out:

[056-008] “Come down here, Cona’n, my darling.”

[056-009] Cona’n stepped down to him.

[056-010] “Am I dreaming?” Fionn demanded, and he stretched out his finger before

[056-011] him.

[056-012] “If you are dreaming,” said Congn, “I’m dreaming too. They weren’t here

[056-013] a minute ago,” he stammered.

[056-014] Fionn looked up at the sky and found that it was still there. He stared

[056-015] to one side and saw the trees of Kyle Conor waving in the distance. He

[056-016] bent his ear to the wind and heard the shouting of hunters, the yapping

[056-017] of dogs, and the clear whistles, which told how the hunt was going.

[056-018] “Well!” said Fionn to himself.

[056-019] “By my hand!” quoth Cona’n to his own soul.

[056-020] And the two men stared into the hillside as though what they were

[056-021] looking at was too wonderful to be looked away from.

[056-022] “Who are they?” said Fionn.

[056-023] “What are they?” Cona’n gasped. And they stared again.

[056-024] For there was a great hole like a doorway in the side of the mound, and

[056-025] in that doorway the daughters of Conaran sat spinning. They had three

[056-026] crooked sticks of holly set up before the cave, and they were reeling

[056-027] yarn off these. But it was enchantment they were weaving.

[056-028] “One could not call them handsome,” said Cona’n.

[056-029] “One could,” Fionn replied, “but it would not be true.”

[056-030] “I cannot see them properly,” Fionn complained. “They are hiding behind

[056-031] the holly.”

[056-032] “I would be contented if I could not see them at all,” his companion

[056-033] grumbled.

[056-034] But the Chief insisted.

[056-035] “I want to make sure that it is whiskers they are wearing.”

[056-036] “Let them wear whiskers or not wear them,” Cona’n counselled. “But let

[056-037] us have nothing to do with them.”

[056-038] “One must not be frightened of anything,” Fionn stated.

[056-039] “I am not frightened,” Cona’n explained. “I only want to keep my good

[056-040] opinion of women, and if the three yonder are women, then I feel sure I

[056-041] shall begin to dislike females from this minute out.”

[056-042] “Come on, my love,” said Fionn, “for I must find out if these whiskers

[056-043] are true.”

[056-044] He strode resolutely into the cave. He pushed the branches of holly

[056-045] aside and marched up to Conaran’s daughters, with Cona’n behind him.
