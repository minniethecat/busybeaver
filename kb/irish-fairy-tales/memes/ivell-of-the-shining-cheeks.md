# Ivell of the Shining Cheeks

## Overview

Ivell of the Shining Cheeks is a character in [[mongan|Mongan's Frenzy]] who is, in truth, the Hag of the Mill transformed by [[mongan|Mongan]]'s [[enchantment|magic]]. Through [[shapeshifting|shape-changing]], [[mongan|Mongan]] turned the ancient, wizened hag into a woman of such beauty that one dared scarcely look upon her — so lovely and young she seemed but sixteen. This illusion was central to [[mongan|Mongan]]'s plan to recover his wife [[duv-laca|Duv Laca]] from [[branduv|King Branduv of Leinster]]. [^1]

## The Transformation

The Hag of the Mill was a bony, thin creature with mismatched feet, misplaced facial features, and whiskers on her nose, dressed in rags and accompanied by a toothless dog, a knock-kneed mare, and a sick hen. When [[mongan|Mongan]] asked what she would wish for, she replied she would sooner be a lovely young girl than a skinned eel — and would happily marry [[mongan|Mongan]], the King of [[leinster|Leinster]], or both. [^1]

[[mongan|Mongan]] touched her with his finger, and all dilapidation and age fell away. She became so beautiful and young that she seemed sixteen. He named her Ivell of the Shining Cheeks, daughter of the King of [[munster|Munster]]. Simultaneously he transformed her animals — the dog became a silky lapdog, the mare a brisk palfrey — and changed himself into the likeness of Ae, the son of the King of [[connacht|Connaught]], who had just married the real Ivell. [^1]

The choice of identity is characteristically cunning: [[mongan|Mongan]] does not invent a fictional person but assumes the identity of a real couple — Ae the Beautiful and his new bride Ivell — who have just been married in [[connacht|Connaught]]. By impersonating them, [[mongan|Mongan]] exploits [[branduv|Branduv]]'s likely ignorance of distant provincial affairs. The real Ivell, daughter of the King of [[munster|Munster]], provides cover that would withstand casual inquiry, while the hag's transformation provides the physical beauty necessary to captivate the Leinster king.

## The Deception at Leinster

Arriving at [[branduv|Branduv]]'s fortress as Ae the Beautiful and his wife Ivell, [[mongan|Mongan]] claimed they had been banished from [[connacht|Connaught]] and sought protection. [[branduv|Branduv]] came to the door himself and the moment he saw Ivell, he was captivated. [^2] Throughout the feast and banquet that followed, [[branduv|Branduv]] could not stop looking at her — every time he drank, he toasted Ivell across the brim of his goblet. He grew so enamoured that he declared there was not a bone in his body that was not filled with love and longing for her.

## The Exchange

[[mongan|Mongan]], as Ae, coached the hag to resist [[branduv|Branduv]]'s advances while extracting gifts — first the king's drinking-horn, then his girdle, treasures that [[mac-an-dav|mac an Dáv]] quietly pocketed. Finally, [[branduv|Branduv]] offered an exchange: his own intended bride, [[duv-laca|Duv Laca of the White Hand]] — [[mongan|Mongan]]'s true wife — for Ivell. After a show of reluctance, [[mongan|Mongan]] agreed. He brought Ivell to the king, kissed her three times "so the king would not suspect," and gave the hag over. That night, [[mongan|Mongan]] and [[mac-an-dav|mac an Dáv]] fled with [[duv-laca|Duv Laca]] and [[mac-an-dav|mac an Dáv]]'s wife towards [[ulster|Ulster]]. [^2]

The three kisses are a masterful detail: each one seals the illusion, binding the transformed hag more tightly to her assumed identity while also performing the public affection expected of a husband reluctantly giving up his wife. The performance is so convincing that [[branduv|Branduv]] never questions it — the king's desire blinds him to the possibility of deception.

In the morning, the servants discovered the hag's true face — whiskered nose, mismatched feet, and all — lying beside the horrified king, who screeched and leaped from the bed. The comic denouement, in which the mighty King of Leinster finds himself in bed with a whiskered crone, is one of the richest moments of humiliation in the Stephens corpus, and it serves a double purpose: it punishes [[branduv|Branduv]] for his sexual greed while celebrating [[mongan|Mongan]]'s cleverness as a trickster-hero.

## Significance

Ivell of the Shining Cheeks is a fiction within the fiction — her name, beauty, and royal lineage are all [[enchantment|magic]]al constructs serving [[mongan|Mongan]]'s clever scheme. Her story illustrates [[mongan|Mongan]]'s mastery of [[shapeshifting|shape-changing]] and his cunning as a trickster-hero. More broadly, the episode exemplifies a pattern in Irish myth in which disguise and transformation — rather than force of arms — achieve what warriors cannot: the recovery of a wife, the humiliation of an enemy, and the restoration of the rightful order without bloodshed.
