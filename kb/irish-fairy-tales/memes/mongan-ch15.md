[084-001] The road was now not so crowded as it had been. Minutes would pass and

[084-002] only a few travellers would come, and minutes more would go when nobody

[084-003] was in sight at all.

[084-004] Then two men came down the road: they were clerics.

[084-005] “I never saw that kind of uniform before,” said mac an Da’v.

[084-006] “Even if you didn’t,” said [[mongan|Mongan]], “there are plenty of them about. They

[084-007] are men that don’t believe in our gods,” said he.

[084-008] “Do they not, indeed?” said mac an Da’v. “The rascals!” said he. “What,

[084-009] what would Mananna’n say to that?”

[084-010] “The one in front carrying the big book is Tibraide’. He is the priest

[084-011] of Cell Camain, and he is the chief of those two.”

[084-012] “Indeed, and indeed!” said mac an Da’v. “The one behind must be his

[084-013] servant, for he has a load on his back.”

[084-014] The priests were reading their offices, and mac an Da’v marvelled at

[084-015] that.

[084-016] “What is it they are doing?” said he.

[084-017] “They are reading.”

[084-018] “Indeed, and indeed they are,” said mac an Da’v. “I can’t make out a

[084-019] word of the language except that the man behind says amen, amen, every

[084-020] time the man in front puts a grunt out of him. And they don’t like our

[084-021] gods at all!” said mac an Da’v.

[084-022] “They do not,” said Mongan.

[084-023] “Play a trick on them, master,” said mac an Da’v. Mongan agreed to play

[084-024] a trick on the priests.

[084-025] He looked at them hard for a minute, and then he waved his hand at them.

[084-026] The two priests stopped, and they stared straight in front of them, and

[084-027] then they looked at each other, and then they looked at the sky. The

[084-028] clerk began to bless himself, and then Tibraide’ began to bless himself,

[084-029] and after that they didn’t know what to do. For where there had been a

[084-030] road with hedges on each side and fields stretching beyond them, there

[084-031] was now no road, no hedge, no field; but there was a great broad river

[084-032] sweeping across their path; a mighty tumble of yellowy-brown waters,

[084-033] very swift, very savage; churning and billowing and jockeying among

[084-034] rough boulders and islands of stone. It was a water of villainous depth

[084-035] and of detestable wetness; of ugly hurrying and of desolate cavernous

[084-036] sound. At a little to their right there was a thin uncomely bridge that

[084-037] waggled across the torrent.

[084-038] Tibraide’ rubbed his eyes, and then he looked again. “Do you see what I

[084-039] see?” said he to the clerk.

[084-040] “I don’t know what you see,” said the clerk, “but what I see I never did

[084-041] see before, and I wish I did not see it now.”

[084-042] “I was born in this place,” said Tibraide’, “my father was born here

[084-043] before me, and my grandfather was born here before him, but until this

[084-044] day and this minute I never saw a river here before, and I never heard

[084-045] of one.”

[084-046] “What will we do at all?” said the clerk. “What will we do at all?”

[084-047] “We will be sensible,” said Tibraide’ sternly, “and we will go about our

[084-048] business,” said he. “If rivers fall out of the sky what has that to do

[084-049] with you, and if there is a river here, which there is, why, thank God,

[084-050] there is a bridge over it too.”

[084-051] “Would you put a toe on that bridge?” said the clerk. “What is the

[084-052] bridge for?” said Tibraide’ Mongan and mac an Da’v followed them.

[084-053] When they got to the middle of the bridge it broke under them, and they

[084-054] were precipitated into that boiling yellow flood.

[084-055] Mongan snatched at the book as it fell from Tibraide’s hand.

[084-056] “Won’t you let them drown, master?” asked mac an Da’v.

[084-057] “No,” said Mongan, “I’ll send them a mile down the stream, and then they

[084-058] can come to land.”

[084-059] Mongan then took on himself the form of Tibraide’ and he turned mac an

[084-060] Da’v into the shape of the clerk.

[084-061] “My head has gone bald,” said the servant in a whisper.

[084-062] “That is part of it,” replied Mongan. “So long as we know,” said mac an

[084-063] Da’v.

[084-064] They went on then to meet the King of [[leinster|Leinster]].
