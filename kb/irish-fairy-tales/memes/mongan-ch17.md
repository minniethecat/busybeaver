[086-001] But they were not alone, although they thought they were. The hag that

[086-002] guarded the jewels was in the room. She sat hunched up against the wail,

[086-003] and as she looked like a bundle of rags they did not notice her. She

[086-004] began to speak then.

[086-005] “Terrible are the things I see,” said she. “Terrible are the things I

[086-006] see.”

[086-007] [[mongan|Mongan]] and his servant gave a jump of surprise, and their two wives

[086-008] jumped and squealed. Then Mongan puffed out his cheeks till his face

[086-009] looked like a bladder, and he blew a [[enchantment|magic]] breath at the hag, so that

[086-010] she seemed to be surrounded by a fog, and when she looked through that

[086-011] breath everything seemed to be different to what she had thought. Then

[086-012] she began to beg everybody’s pardon.

[086-013] “I had an evil vision,” said she, “I saw crossways. How sad it is that I

[086-014] should begin to see the sort of things I thought I saw.”

[086-015] “Sit in this chair, mother,” said Mongan, “and tell me what you thought

[086-016] you saw,” and he slipped a spike under her, and mac an Da’v pushed her

[086-017] into the seat, and she died on the spike.

[086-018] Just then there came a knocking at the door. Mac an Da’v opened it, and

[086-019] there was Tibraide, standing outside, and twenty-nine of his men were

[086-020] with him, and they were all laughing.

[086-021] “A mile was not half enough,” said mac an Da’v reproachfully.

[086-022] The Chamberlain of the fortress pushed into the room and he stared from

[086-023] one Tibraide’ to the other.

[086-024] “This is a fine growing year,” said he. “There never was a year

[086-025] when Tibraide’s were as plentiful as they are this year. There is a

[086-026] Tibraide’ outside and a Tibraide’ inside, and who knows but there are

[086-027] some more of them under the bed. The place is crawling with them,” said

[086-028] he.

[086-029] Mongan pointed at Tibraide’.

[086-030] “Don’t you know who that is?” he cried.

[086-031] “I know who he says he is,” said the Chamberlain.

[086-032] “Well, he is Mongan,” said Mongan, “and these twenty-nine men are

[086-033] twenty-nine of his nobles from [[ulster|Ulster]].”

[086-034] At that news the men of the household picked up clubs and cudgels and

[086-035] every kind of thing that was near, and made a violent and woeful attack

[086-036] on Tibraide’s men The King of [[leinster|Leinster]] came in then, and when he was

[086-037] told Tibraide’ was Mongan he attacked them as well, and it was with

[086-038] difficulty that Tibraide’ got away to Cell Camain with nine of his men

[086-039] and they all wounded.

[086-040] The King of Leinster came back then. He went to [[duv-laca|Duv Laca]]’s room.

[086-041] “Where is Tibraide’?” said he.

[086-042] “It wasn’t Tibraide was here,” said the hag who was still sitting on

[086-043] the spike, and was not half dead, “it was Mongan.”

[086-044] “Why did you let him near you?” said the king to Duv Laca.

[086-045] “There is no one has a better right to be near me than Mongan has,” said

[086-046] Duv Laca, “he is my own husband,” said she.

[086-047] And then the king cried out in dismay: “I have beaten Tibraide’s

[086-048] people.” He rushed from the room.

[086-049] “Send for Tibraide’ till I apologise,” he cried. “Tell him it was all a

[086-050] mistake. Tell him it was Mongan.”
