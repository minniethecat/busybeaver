[007-001] “Then my soul rose to the height of Doom, and I intended all that might

[007-002] happen to me, and agreed to it.

[007-003] “‘To-morrow,’ I said, ‘I will go out among ye, and I will die,’ and at

[007-004] that the wolves howled joyfully, hungrily, impatiently.

[007-005] “I slept, and I saw myself changing into a boar in dream, and I felt in

[007-006] dream the beating of a new heart within me, and in dream I stretched my

[007-007] powerful neck and braced my eager limbs. I awoke from my dream, and I

[007-008] was that which I had dreamed.

[007-009] “The night wore away, the darkness lifted, the day came; and from

[007-010] without the cave the wolves called to me: “‘Come out, O Skinny Stag.

[007-011] Come out and die.’

[007-012] “And I, with joyful heart, thrust a black bristle through the hole of

[007-013] the cave, and when they saw that wriggling snout, those curving tusks,

[007-014] that red fierce eye, the wolves fled yelping, tumbling over each other,

[007-015] frantic with terror; and I behind them, a wild cat for leaping, a giant

[007-016] for strength, a devil for ferocity; a madness and gladness of lusty,

[007-017] unsparing life; a killer, a champion, a boar who could not be defied.

[007-018] “I took the lordship of the boars of Ireland.

[007-019] “Wherever I looked among my tribes I saw love and obedience: whenever

[007-020] I appeared among the strangers they fled away. And the wolves feared me

[007-021] then, and the great, grim bear went bounding on heavy paws. I charged

[007-022] him at the head of my troop and rolled him over and over; but it is not

[007-023] easy to kill the bear, so deeply is his life packed under that stinking

[007-024] pelt. He picked himself up and ran, and was knocked down, and ran again

[007-025] blindly, butting into trees and stones. Not a claw did the big bear

[007-026] flash, not a tooth did he show, as he ran whimpering like a baby, or

[007-027] as he stood with my nose rammed against his mouth, snarling up into his

[007-028] nostrils.

[007-029] “I challenged all that moved. All creatures but one. For men had again

[007-030] come to Ireland. Semion, the son of Stariath, with his people, from whom

[007-031] the men of Domnann and the Fir Bolg and the Galiuin are descended. These

[007-032] I did not chase, and when they chased me I fled.

[007-033] “Often I would go, drawn by my memoried heart, to look at them as they

[007-034] moved among their fields; and I spoke to my mind in bitterness: ‘When

[007-035] the people of Partholon were gathered in counsel my voice was heard; it

[007-036] was sweet to all who heard it, and the words I spoke were wise. The eyes

[007-037] of women brightened and softened when they looked at me. They loved to

[007-038] hear him when he sang who now wanders in the forest with a tusky herd.’”
