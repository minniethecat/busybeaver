## Overview

Cesh Corran is both a hill of the [[fianna|Fianna]] and a threshold to [[faery|Faery]]. Its summit carries a hunting-mound where Fionn would sit and watch his warriors range the countryside [054-025]–[054-033]. Beneath the hill lies the [[sidhe-mound|sídhe]] of [[conaran|Conaran]], a hostile [[faery|Faery]] king whose daughters captured Fionn and several of his men in the tale of the Enchanted Cave [055-001]–[055-010]. Cesh Corran is thus a place where the mortal world of hunting and heroism brushes against the treacherous beauty of [[faery|Faery]].

## Location

Cesh Corran rises in the territory of the [[fianna|Fianna]]'s [[connacht|Connacht]] ranges. From its summit Fionn could watch his men "beating the coverts in Legney and Brefny, ranging the fastnesses of Glen Dallan, creeping in the nut and beech forests of Carbury, spying among the woods of Kyle Conor, and ranging the wide plain of Moy Conal" [054-027]–[054-033] — a commanding vantage over one of the [[fianna|Fianna]]'s richest hunting territories.

- **Region**: [[connacht|Connacht]]
- **Type**: Sídhe mound (hill with a [[faery|Faery]] dwelling beneath)
- **Modern location**: Keshcorran, Co. Sligo, Ireland

## In the Tales

### The Enchanted Cave of Cesh Corran

The entire tale of *The Enchanted Cave of Cesh Corran* takes its name from this location. Fionn, with [[conan-the-swearer|Cona'n the Swearer]] and his hounds [[bran-and-sceolan|Bran]] and Sceo'lan, was "sitting on the hunting-mound at the top of Cesh Corran" while the [[fianna|Fianna]] hunted below [054-025]–[054-027]. Unknown to Fionn, "the king of the Shi' of Cesh Corran, [[conaran|Conaran]], son of Imidel, was also watching the hunt" [055-001]–[055-003]. [[conaran|Conaran]] bore Fionn a grudge — "we do not know what Fionn had done to [[conaran|Conaran]], but it must have been bad enough" [055-013]–[055-014] — and seeing the champion alone, he sent his three daughters, enchanted hags, to lure the [[fianna|Fianna]] into the cave and bind them with [[enchantment|magic]]al ropes.

### The Little Brawl at Allen

Fionn also mentions Cesh Corran as a landmark of the [[fianna|Fianna]]'s territory when searching for Caelte mac Romin. Setting out from [[ben-edair|Ben Edair]], he thinks: "if he is not there, then I shall find him at Cesh Corran of the [[fianna|Fianna]]" [046-111]. This passing reference establishes Cesh Corran as one of the recognized gathering-places of the warrior-band — a fixture in the [[fianna|Fianna]]'s mental map of Ireland.

## Mythological Significance

Cesh Corran is a classic Irish [[sidhe-mound|sídhe]] site: a hill that is simultaneously a natural landmark, a royal hunting station, and a portal to the [[faery|Faery]] realm beneath. Its dual nature — a place of [[fianna|Fianna]] strength and a place of [[faery|Faery]] entrapment — mirrors the recurring theme in the tales that the greatest heroes are most vulnerable at the threshold between worlds. [[goll-mac-morna|Goll mac Morna]] was needed to rescue Fionn from the cave, underscoring the irony that the Captain of the [[fianna|Fianna]] could be undone at his own hunting-mound by forces from the realm that exists alongside the mortal world but hidden from it.
