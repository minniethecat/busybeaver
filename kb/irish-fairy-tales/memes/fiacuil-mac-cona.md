# Fiacuil mac Cona

## Overview

Fiacuil mac Cona was a notorious robber and warrior in the time of the broken Clann Baiscne. He was the husband of [[bovmall|Bovmall]], the aunt of [[fionn-mac-uail|Fionn mac Cumhaill]], and ran a hidden fighting school in the wilderness where young hunters trained. When Fionn sought to learn the warrior arts, Fiacuil's camp became his school, and it was Fiacuil who provided the venom-tipped spear of [[aillen-mac-midna|Aillen mac Midna]] that would prove decisive in Fionn's first great deed. [017-030][018-026]

## Identity

Fiacuil mac Cona was a human warrior of fearsome reputation. He killed the poets—"chopped them up and chopped them down"—in an episode whose details are only hinted at in the surviving tales. He wielded a great spear with thirty rivets of Arabian gold in its socket, a weapon so precious and perilous it had to be kept wrapped and hidden. [017-030]

When Clann Baiscne was broken and scattered, Fiacuil took to the wilds. He married [[bovmall|Bovmall]], who was Fionn's aunt, and together with her and [[lia-luachra|Lia Luachra]] they formed the hidden household in the [[slieve-bloom|woods of Slieve Bloom]] where the young Fionn was raised in secret from his father's enemies. [018-026]

## In the Tales

Fiacuil appears in *The Boyhood of Fionn*. He ran a fighting school deep in the wilderness—a rough camp where young men came to train in combat. This school was Fionn's true education in the warrior arts; the boy gathered great knowledge and new skills during his time there. [017-030]

His most important contribution to Fionn's story came on the eve of [[samhain|Samhain]], when Fionn needed a defence against the sleep-[[enchantment|spell]] of [[aillen-mac-midna|Aillen mac Midna]]. Fiacuil whispered to Fionn that he possessed Aillen's own spear—the Birgha—tipped with a venom so foul that merely pressing its point to the forehead would keep a man awake through any enchantment. He gave the spear to Fionn, warning him to be as frightened of it as he was of the fire-breather himself. [024-040][024-074]

Fiacuil also advised on strategy against Aillen, and after Fionn's victory, the boy returned to the woods with the fame and standing that would lead him to the leadership of the [[fianna|Fianna]]. [018-026]

## Significance

Fiacuil mac Cona is a transitional figure in the Fenian Cycle—a representative of the chaotic, outlaw world into which Fionn was born, and a bridge to the heroic life Fionn would build. His fighting school gave Fionn the martial foundation that no royal court could provide, and his possession of Aillen's spear made possible the deed that won Fionn his name and his command.
