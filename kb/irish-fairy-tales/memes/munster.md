# Munster

## Overview

Munster is the southern province of Ireland, appearing in Stephens' *Irish Fairy Tales* as the territory of kings and the setting of the Carl's journey. It encompasses the [[slieve-luachra|Slieve Luachra]] mountain range, the Hill of the Rushes, and the royal house whose daughter, [[ivell-of-the-shining-cheeks|Ivell of the Shining Cheeks]], becomes a figure of desire in "[[becuma|Becuma of the White Skin]]."

## Location

- **Region**: Munster (southern Ireland)
- **Modern equivalent**: The province of Munster covers six counties in southwest Ireland — Cork, [[kerry|Kerry]], Limerick, Tipperary, Clare, and Waterford
- **Type of place**: Ancient province and kingdom, containing mountains ([[slieve-luachra|Slieve Luachra]]), royal courts, and major roads

## In the Tales

Munster appears in "[[the-carl|The Carl]] of the Drab Coat" when the Carl calculates distances for his companion: "to the Hill of the Rushes, [[slieve-luachra|Slieve Luachra]] of Munster, is exactly sixty miles from this place" [048-026]. The two set out and "went off to [[slieve-luachra|Slieve Luachra]] now" [048-029], finally reaching the destination as "the sun was setting they reached [[slieve-luachra|Slieve Luachra]] and prepared to spend the night there" [048-032]–[048-033].

In "The [[wooing-of-becfola|Wooing of Becfola]]," a Munster lord appears as one of the many travelers on the roads to [[tara|Tara]]: "a Munster lord. On another road a vat of seasoned yew, monstrous as a palace on the axle" [022-017]. This single mention establishes Munster as one of the provinces sending its nobility to the High King's gatherings.

In "[[becuma|Becuma of the White Skin]]," Conn's son Art encounters "[[ivell-of-the-shining-cheeks|Ivell of the Shining Cheeks]], daughter of the King of Munster" [088-073] during his quest, suggesting Munster's royal house was known throughout the island.

## Mythological Significance

Munster, while less central to Stephens' tales than [[ulster|Ulster]] or [[meath|Meath]], provides the geographical anchor for the [[slieve-luachra|Slieve Luachra]] episode in "[[the-carl|The Carl]] of the Drab Coat." The sixty-mile journey to Munster's hills tests the Carl's supernatural endurance and frames the mythical geography of the tale.

The King of Munster's unnamed daughter, Ivell, represents the province's connection to the royal marriage alliances that weave through Irish myth. Munster's mention alongside the other provinces — [[connacht|Connacht]], [[ulster|Ulster]], [[leinster|Leinster]], and [[meath|Meath]] — reinforces the five-fold division of ancient Ireland.
