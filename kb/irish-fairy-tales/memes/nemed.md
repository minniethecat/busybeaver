# Nemed mac Agnoman

**Nemed**, son of Agnoman, was the leader of the second legendary invasion of Ireland, arriving after the race of Partholon had been wiped out by plague. His story, like Partholon's, was preserved in the long memory of Tuan mac Cairill.

## Arrival in Ireland

After the death of Partholon's people, the land lay empty until Nemed's coming. Tuan mac Cairill, witnessing events in his beast-forms, recalled the arrival with joy:

> At the end of that time, Nemed the son of Agnoman came to Ireland with a fleet of thirty-four barques, and in each barque there were thirty couples of people.

For Tuan, who had lived long in animal solitude, the sight of human beings again stirred powerful emotions.

## A Savage and Active People

The Nemedians differed from their predecessors. Where Partholon's people had been gentle but witless, Nemed's folk were fierce and vigorous:

> The people of Nemed had small minds but great activity. They were savage fighters and hunters.

Nemed and four couples had been saved from a fierce storm, and from that small remnant they increased and multiplied until four thousand couples lived and laughed and were riotous in the sun.

## Mysterious End

Like Partholon's people before them, the race of Nemed met a sudden and unexplained fate. When Tuan returned to observe them, drawn by his memories:

> But one time I came, drawn by that intolerable anguish of memory, and all of these people were gone: the place that knew them knew them no longer.

The disappearance of the Nemedians remains one of the mysteries of the mythological cycle, with later traditions connecting their scattered remnants to the Fir Bolg and the Tuatha De Danann.

## Legacy

Nemed occupies a crucial place in the _Lebor Gabála Érenn_ (Book of Invasions) tradition, representing the second wave of settlement. Through Tuan mac Cairill's unique testimony — a being who lived through the ages of stag, boar, eagle, and man — Nemed's story bridges the gap between the Flood and the coming of later peoples to Ireland.
