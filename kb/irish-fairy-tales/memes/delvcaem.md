# Delvcaem

**Delvcaem** (meaning "Fair Shape") was the daughter of Morgan, a great magician of the Many-Coloured Land. She became the object of Art mac Conn's quest when Becuma of the White Skin imposed a binding geasa upon him.

## Captive in Her Father's Dun

Despite her beauty and accomplishments, Delvcaem lived as a prisoner. Her father Morgan had constructed a hundred-foot pillar atop his palace, and at its summit a tiny room had been built where Delvcaem was confined:

> Delvcaem knew that he was coming. She was waiting for him, yearning for him. To her mind Art was not only love, he was freedom, for the poor girl was a captive in her father's home.

She was lovelier in shape than any other princess of the Many-Coloured Land. She was wiser than all the other women of that land, and she was skilful in music, embroidery, and chastity.

## Art's Quest

The quest for Delvcaem began when Becuma, having been defeated at chess by Art, bound him with a geasa:

> "I bind you," said Becuma, "to eat no food in Ireland until you have found Delvcaem, the daughter of Morgan."

Art embarked from Ireland and roamed from island to island in enchanted waters, asking all people how he might come to Delvcaem. He received warnings of the perils that awaited him, including Delvcaem's fearsome mother:

> "There is yet a danger," she called. "Beware of Delvcaem's mother, Dog Head, daughter of the King of the Dog Heads. Beware of her."

## Rescue and Legacy

Art mac Conn overcame all obstacles, confronting the dangers of Morgan's realm and the monstrous Dog Head, and at last reached Delvcaem. Upon meeting her, he asked her name:

> "I am called Delvcaem (Fair Shape) and I am the daughter of Morgan," she replied.

Through her union with Art mac Conn, Delvcaem was freed from captivity, and their marriage brought together the mortal realm of Ireland and the enchanted Many-Coloured Land.
