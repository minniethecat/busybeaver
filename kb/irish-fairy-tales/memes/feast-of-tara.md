## Overview

The Feast of Tara (Feis Temrach) is the great gathering of Irish mythological society — an annual assembly held at [[samhain|Samhain]] when the divisions between worlds were thinnest and all the forces of the kingdom converged at the High King's seat. In James Stephens' *Irish Fairy Tales*, the feast is the default setting for significant action: this is where quarrels erupt into battle, where supernatural visitors appear, and where the bonds between king, warriors, and poets are ritually renewed.

## Context

> "It was Samhain-tide, and the feast of Tara was being held, at which all the Fianna of Ireland were present." — [022-005]

[[samhain|Samhain]] (November 1) was the most significant festival of the Irish year — marking the transition from summer to winter, the end of the fighting season, and the night when the boundary between the mortal world and the [[land-of-promise|Otherworld]] grew permeable. The feast at [[tara|Tara]] was the secular counterpart to the sacred dimensions of [[samhain|Samhain]]: a political and social assembly that gathered every significant actor in the kingdom.

| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Social Renewal | All [[fianna|Fianna]] warriors gathered to reaffirm loyalty to the High King |
| Political Arena | Disputes between clans were aired and resolved |
| Judicial Court | The king rendered judgements on the most serious matters |

## Narrative

The feast serves multiple functions in the tales:

**As setting for conflict**: The [[brawl-at-allen|Little Brawl at Allen]] begins at the feast of [[tara|Tara]], where a seemingly minor insult — a blow struck by Fionn's brother Cairell against [[conan-mac-morna|Conan mac Morna]] — escalates into a factional battle. The feast's atmosphere of heightened emotion and copious drinking lowers the inhibitions that ordinarily keep clan rivalries in check.

**As arena for judgement**: After the brawl, the great hall of [[tara|Tara]] is cleansed and transformed into a court of law, where [[fergus-true-lips|Fergus True-Lips]] delivers his sworn testimony before the High King [044-138]–[045-025]. The same space that hosts celebration becomes the stage for the most solemn acts of justice.

**As threshold for the supernatural**: Supernatural visitors — Becuma arriving from [[faery|Faery]], [[becfola|Becfola]] arriving as a mysterious stranger — choose the feast as their moment of entry. The gathered crowd at [[tara|Tara]], representing the entire power structure of Ireland, provides the maximum audience for their interventions.

> "Friends and heroes," said Conn, "Aillen, the son of Midna, will come tonight from the Shi' of Finnachy." — [023-026]

It is at the feast that Conn announces the supernatural threat of Aillen, the fire-breathing Sidhe attacker whose defeat will mark Fionn's first great triumph. The feast is thus not merely celebration but vigil — the moment when the kingdom's defenders must be most alert, for it is when they are gathered that they are most vulnerable.

## Outcome

Each feast of [[tara|Tara]] that appears in the tales is a turning point. The [[samhain|Samhain]] gathering is where the status quo is both celebrated and disrupted — where the kingdom's unity is performed even as its fractures are exposed. The dual nature of the feast — at once a ritual of cohesion and a tinderbox of conflict — reflects the broader theme of the Fenian Cycle: the [[fianna|Fianna]] is Ireland's glory, but its internal tensions carry the seeds of its own dissolution.
