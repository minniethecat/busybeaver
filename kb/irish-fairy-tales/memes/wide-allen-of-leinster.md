# Wide Allen of Leinster

The **Wide Allen of Leinster** (*Mag nAlmaine Laigen* in Irish) was the
great plain in the [[leinster|province of Leinster]], upon which the
[[allen|Hill of Allen]] rose. It was the territory where [[fionn-mac-uail|Fionn mac Uail]]
and the [[fianna|Fianna]] established their headquarters, and for generations it
served as the beating heart of the Fenian warrior-band — a place of
assembly, training, feasting, and departure for the great adventures
that fill the Fenian cycle.

## Geography and Name

The name *Mag nAlmaine* means "the Plain of Almha," with Almha being the
Irish name for the [[allen|Hill of Allen]]. The epithet "Wide" reflects the
plain's expanse — a stretch of relatively flat, fertile land in what is
now County Kildare, suitable for the grazing of horses and the
gathering of large bodies of warriors.

In the topography of Irish legend, the Wide Allen occupied a strategic
position: close enough to [[tara|Tara]], the seat of the High Kings, for the
[[fianna|Fianna]] to answer royal summons, yet far enough to maintain their
distinct identity as a warrior society governed by its own code.

## In the Tales

### Fionn's Homecoming

After [[fionn-mac-uail|Fionn]] had proven himself — defeating [[aillen-mac-midna|Aillen mac Midna]]
at [[tara|Tara]], claiming leadership of the [[fianna|Fianna]] — he established his
seat at the [[allen|Hill of Allen]] on this plain. It was here that his mother
[[muirne|Muirne]] came to visit him, traveling to "wide Allen of Leinster" to see
the son she had not raised, now the most celebrated warrior in Ireland
[^026-029].

### Place of Assembly

The plain served as the gathering ground for the [[fianna|Fianna]]'s great
assemblies. Warriors from across Ireland would converge on the Wide
Allen, and the text captures the joy of such homecomings:

> that joyous company he came to wide Allen of Leinster, where the
> [[fianna|Fianna]] were assembled [^031-066]

These gatherings were both practical — mustering for campaigns,
distributing spoils, settling disputes — and ceremonial, reinforcing the
bonds that held the warrior-band together.

### Life on the Plain

While the [[allen|Hill of Allen]] itself was the fortified [[dun|stronghold]] where
[[fionn-mac-uail|Fionn]] and his inner circle dwelt, the Wide Allen was where the
full life of the [[fianna|Fianna]] unfolded. The plain provided grazing lands
for the war-horses, open ground for weapons-training and martial
contests, and space for the great feasting-halls and temporary camps
that sprang up when the full strength of the warrior-band was assembled.

The rhythm of Fianna life on the Wide Allen followed the seasons: summer
hunting in the forests, autumn feasting on the plain, winter quarters in
the [[dun|stronghold]], and the spring muster that sent bands of warriors
riding out to meet whatever challenges the new year would bring.

## Legacy

The Wide Allen of Leinster has passed from geography into legend. The
plain still exists — the countryside around the [[allen|Hill of Allen]] in
modern County Kildare — but in Irish tradition it is remembered as the
home-ground of heroes, a place where the boundaries between the mortal
world and the supernatural grew thin, and where the [[fianna|Fianna]] rode out
to meet their destinies.
