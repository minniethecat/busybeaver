## Overview

"[[wide-allen-of-leinster|Wide Allen of Leinster]]" [026-029] is more than a hill — it is the tribal home of the [[fianna|Fianna]], the place to which its members return after adventures and from which they set out. In Stephens' retelling, Allen appears as both a hill-fort (the Dun) with its heights surveyed by guards, and a plain from which the hunt departs and to which warriors return "swiftly … for he could not bear to be one unnecessary day parted from [[saeve|Saeve]]" [033-012]. It is the center of gravity for the entire [[fianna|Fianna]] world.

## Location

Allen lies in the [[leinster|province of Leinster]], in the eastern heartland of Ireland. The Dun of Allen commanded views over the surrounding plain, and its guards could watch for returning warriors from its heights [034-002]. The plain itself served as the [[fianna|Fianna]]'s hunting grounds and gathering space.

- **Region**: [[leinster|Leinster]]
- **Type**: Stronghold (hill-fort with surrounding plain)
- **Modern location**: Hill of Allen, Co. Kildare, Ireland

## In the Tales

### The Birth of Bran

Fionn's mother, [[muirne|Muirne]], "went to wide Allen of Leinster to visit her son, and she brought her young sister [[tuiren|Tuiren]] with her" [026-029]. This visit initiates the chain of events that leads to the birth of [[bran-and-sceolan|Bran]] and Sceo'lan — the enchanted hounds that become Fionn's inseparable companions. Allen is the stage on which the domestic and the [[enchantment|magic]]al meet: the mother visiting her son, the sister transformed by a jealous rival, and the hounds born from fairy enchantment.

### Oisin's Mother

After his victory at the battle described in the tale, Fionn "returned swiftly to the plain of Allen, for he could not bear to be one unnecessary day parted from [[saeve|Saeve]]" [033-012]–[033-014]. [[saeve|Saeve]], the Flower of Allen, is named for this place — "the Flower of Allen" [034-002] — and it is at the Dun of Allen that she waits for Fionn's return. When she is lured away by a false vision of Fionn, it is from "the heights of the Dun" that the guards watch [034-002], and it is to "the gate of the Dun" that the transformed fawn flees [034-022]–[034-025].

### The Little Brawl at Allen

An entire tale is named for this location. The brawl — really a conflict of privilege between [[goll-mac-morna|Goll mac Morna]] and the [[fianna|Fianna]]'s foreign guests — takes place at Allen, underscoring its role as the arena where the [[fianna|Fianna]]'s internal politics and personal rivalries play out.

## Mythological Significance

Allen is not a [[enchantment|magic]]al place in the manner of [[faery|Faery]] or the [[sidhe-mound|sídhe]] mounds — it is the solid, earthly anchor of the [[fianna|Fianna]]'s world. In the geography of the tales, Allen serves as home base: adventures depart from it, warriors return to it, and relationships are maintained or fractured within its walls. Its hill-fort (the Dun) is one of the few built structures in the tales not associated with kingship — it belongs to a warrior-band, making it a unique type of seat in Irish myth.
