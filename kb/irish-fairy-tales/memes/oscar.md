## Overview

Oscar is the son of the poet-warrior Oisin and the grandson of [[fionn-mac-uail|Fionn mac Uail]], captain of the [[fianna|Fianna]]. He appears as a formidable warrior in two tales: "The [[brawl-at-allen|Little Brawl at Allen]]," where his intervention escalates the banquet brawl into a full battle, and "[[the-carl|The Carl]] of the Drab Coat," where he accompanies Fionn and Oisin on their dealings with the mysterious Carl. Fiercely loyal to his family, Oscar is a champion of irresistible physical force — though not, perhaps, of irresistible judgement.

## Identity

Oscar belongs to the royal line of the [[fianna|Fianna]]: his grandfather is [[fionn-mac-uail|Fionn mac Uail]] and his father is Oisin mac Fionn, the celebrated poet of the Fenian Cycle [043-027]. He is the father of at least two sons who are old enough to participate in battle [044-076]. Oscar is consistently portrayed as a hammer-wielding warrior of explosive action: "Oscar himself leaped up, and with a hammer in either hand he went battering into the melee" [044-078].

## In the Tales

### The Little Brawl at Allen

Oscar is among the notables who attend Fionn's great banquet at [[allen|Allen]]: "There was Goll mor mac Morna and his people; Fionn's son Oisi'n and his grandson Oscar" [043-027]. After the poet [[fergus-true-lips|Fergus True-Lips]] sings of Fionn's ancestors, "Oisi'n and Oscar and mac Lugac of the Terrible Hand gave him rare and costly presents" [043-056].

When the banquet erupts into violence between Cairell Whiteskin and Cona'n mac Morna, Oscar's sons are the first of Fionn's household to join the fight: "Two of Oscar's sons could not bear to see their uncle being worsted, and they leaped at Cona'n" [044-076]. Oscar himself then enters the fray, leaping up with a hammer in each hand [044-078]. Cona'n greets him with grim relish: "I thank the gods for the chance of killing yourself, Oscar" [044-081].

Oscar and Cona'n meet directly, and "Oscar knocked a groan of distress out of Cona'n" [044-082]. But Art og mac Morna comes to Cona'n's aid and wounds Oscar [044-084], drawing Oscar's father Oisin into the combat [044-085]. At the subsequent judgement at [[tara|Tara]], [[fergus-true-lips|Fergus True-Lips]] testifies that "Oscar went to help Cairell, and with that Fionn's people and the clann-Morna rose at each other" [045-022]–[045-023].

### The Carl of the Drab Coat

Oscar appears alongside Fionn and Oisin when they encounter the Carl of the Drab Coat at [[tara|Tara]]. "Oisi'n and Oscar and mac Lugac came, with the nobles of clann-Baiscne" [057-035]. Later, when the Carl's wife appears as promised, "in a short time Fionn and Oisi'n and Oscar" were there to witness the strange events [058-059]. When the situation grows tense, Fionn turns to his grandson in disbelief: "Fionn was astounded. 'Oscar,' he said, 'will you kill me this great trouble?'" Oscar can only stammer back, "I would not be able to," defeated by the uncanny power of the Carl [059-018]–[059-020].

## Significance

Oscar represents the younger generation of the [[fianna|Fianna]] — the grandsons and sons who inherit the warrior tradition but not always the wisdom of their elders. His immediate resort to physical violence in the [[allen|Allen]] banquet hall, while brave, helps transform a private quarrel into a massacre. In the Carl of the Drab Coat, he stands alongside Fionn in bafflement before supernatural forces beyond his comprehension. Oscar's role in the tales bridges the generations — between his father Oisin the poet and his grandfather Fionn the captain, he is the pure warrior, quick to act and slow to calculate.
