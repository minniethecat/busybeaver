## Overview

Becfola is the enigmatic central figure of "The [[wooing-of-becfola|Wooing of Becfola]]," a queen whose origins are unknown even to the storyteller. Her flight from [[tara|Tara]] sets in motion a tale of enchantment, violence, and self-discovery that explores the boundaries between the mortal world and the [[sidhe-mound|sídhe]], and between duty and desire.

## Identity

"We do not know where Becfola came from. Nor do we know for certain her real name," the tale begins [038-001]–[038-002]. The name "Becfola" itself is a mystery, and she is called "Becfola the Dowerless" [039-004] — a woman who arrived at the High King's court with nothing behind her name. She is married to the High King at [[tara|Tara]], but "the mind of Becfola was not satiated with peaceful happiness" [039-010]. Her restlessness, her lack of origin, and her susceptibility to the lure of the [[land-of-promise|Otherworld]] mark her as a liminal figure, caught between worlds.

## In the Tales

### The Flight from Tara

Becfola's tale begins with her decision to flee: "Becfola should fly from [[tara|Tara]]" in response to a summons or impulse she cannot resist [039-024]. She leaves the royal seat and journeys into the wilderness, where she promptly becomes lost — "the country went astray" before her eyes [039-085].

### The Enchanted Island

Lost in the forest, Becfola encounters a troop of strange beings leading her toward an island monastery. She follows a wounded young man who leads her across a causeway and into an otherworldly [[dun|stronghold]] [040-028]–[040-044]. There she witnesses a brutal fight in which the youth is killed before her eyes. "Why did you fight that fight?" she cries in anguish [040-062]. Though horrified, Becfola finds herself drawn into the rhythms of this strange place, and "reluctant feet" carry her through "dim corridors" [040-080]–[041-004].

### The Hidden Truth of Tara

The tale takes a strange turn when Becfola returns to [[tara|Tara]] and staggers into the king's chamber, stammering and unable to speak coherently [041-033]–[041-038]. Her experience in the [[land-of-promise|Otherworld]] has left her transformed — or perhaps revealed. The tale frames her journey as a kind of ordeal through which truths that were concealed at court come into the open. The "apprehension was lifted from Becfola's heart" in the end [041-044], suggesting that her flight from duty was also a flight toward self-knowledge.

## Significance

Becfola is one of the most unusual protagonists in the Irish fairy tale corpus: a woman of unknown origin, a queen who abandons her station, a mortal who stumbles through the [[land-of-promise|Otherworld]] and returns changed. Her story tests the boundaries of the "wooing" tale tradition — there is a marriage, but the central relationship is between Becfola and the unknown, not Becfola and a suitor. Her namelessness and placelessness make her a universal figure: the restless soul who cannot find satisfaction within the walls of the palace.
