# Oisin

## Overview

Oisin (also spelled Oisín or Ossian) is the son of [[fionn-mac-uail|Fionn mac Uail]] and [[saeve|Saeve]], born under extraordinary circumstances after his mother was transformed into a fawn by the [[fear-doirche|Dark Druid]]. He is renowned as one of the greatest warrior-poets of the [[fianna|Fianna]], combining martial prowess with the gift of eloquence and song.

## Identity

- **Species**: Human (born of a mortal father and an enchanted mother)
- **Affiliation**: [[fianna|Fianna]] (the warrior band led by his father)
- **Parents**: [[fionn-mac-uail|Fionn mac Uail]] and [[saeve|Saeve]] (the Flower of Allen)
- **Associated cycles**: Fenian
- **Associated tales**: Oisin's Mother

## Birth and Early Life

The tale of Oisin begins before his birth, with the tragic enchantment of his mother. [[saeve|Saeve]], wife of [[fionn-mac-uail|Fionn]], was lured from the Dun by a false apparition created by the [[fear-doirche|Dark Druid]], who transformed her into a fawn with a [[hazel-rod|hazel rod]] [034-029]. Fionn searched for Saeve across Ireland but could not find her.

Years later, while hunting on [[ben-gulbain|Ben Gulbain]], Fionn and his hounds [[bran-and-sceolan|Bran and Sceolan]] encountered a boy raised in the wild. The hounds, who were themselves of enchanted origin, recognized the child as Fionn's own son and refused to harm him. Fionn named the boy Oisin (meaning "little fawn"), acknowledging his mother's transformed state. The bond was immediate: "where before he could not be parted from Saeve, so now he could not be parted from Oisin" [036-001]–[036-002].

Oisin later recounted his early life in a cliff-walled valley of the [[faery|Shi']], where he lived with a deer that loved him and that he loved — his mother in her transformed shape [037-010]–[037-013]. The [[fear-doirche|Fear Doirche]] came repeatedly to the valley, speaking "gently and angrily" to the deer, until at last he struck her with a hazel rod and forced her to follow him away [037-015]–[037-026]. The deer wept and looked back at her son until she was gone, and the boy woke in the mortal world on the mountain among the hounds [037-030]–[037-031].

## Warrior and Poet

Oisin grew to become a celebrated member of the [[fianna|Fianna]], a warrior of distinction and a poet of rare gift. He fought alongside his father in many of the Fianna's battles, including against the [[lochlann|Lochlannachs]]. In one encounter, Rough Hair mac Morna wounded Oisin in combat before being tumbled by the warrior [[conan-the-swearer|Conán the Swearer]] [044-086].

His dual heritage — mortal father, enchanted mother — marks him as a bridge between the human world and the supernatural realm of the [[tuatha-de-danann|Tuatha Dé Danann]]. His name is attached to the title of the tale "Oisin's Mother" [030-059], recognizing that his birth story is the central event of that narrative.

## Significance

Oisin occupies a unique position in Irish mythology as both warrior and poet, embodying the Fianna's ideal of the complete man — skilled in arms and gifted in words. His mysterious birth from an enchanted mother and his recognition by the hounds [[bran-and-sceolan|Bran and Sceolan]] connect him to the [[shapeshifting|shape-shifting]] and transformation themes that run throughout the Fenian cycle. His story also preserves the memory of his mother [[saeve|Saeve]], whose beauty and tragedy are immortalised through her son's name. In the broader Ossianic tradition beyond Stephens' text, Oisin survives into the Christian era and recounts the deeds of the Fianna to Saint Patrick — though this later material is not included in the *Irish Fairy Tales* corpus.
