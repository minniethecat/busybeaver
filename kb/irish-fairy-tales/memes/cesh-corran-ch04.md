[057-001] The instant they passed the holly a strange weakness came over the

[057-002] heroes. Their fists seemed to grow heavy as lead, and went dingle-dangle

[057-003] at the ends of their arms; their legs became as light as straws and

[057-004] began to bend in and out; their necks became too delicate to hold

[057-005] anything up, so that their heads wibbled and wobbled from side to side.

[057-006] “What’s wrong at all?” said Cona’n, as he tumbled to the ground.

[057-007] “Everything is,” Fionn replied, and he tumbled beside him.

[057-008] The three sisters then tied the heroes with every kind of loop and twist

[057-009] and knot that could be thought of.

[057-010] “Those are whiskers!” said Fionn.

[057-011] “Alas!” said [[conan-the-swearer|Conan]].

[057-012] “What a place you must hunt whiskers in?” he mumbled savagely. “Who

[057-013] wants whiskers?” he groaned.

[057-014] But Fionn was thinking of other things.

[057-015] “If there was any way of warning the [[fianna|Fianna]] not to come here,” Fionn

[057-016] murmured.

[057-017] “There is no way, my darling,” said Caevo’g, and she smiled a smile that

[057-018] would have killed Fionn, only that he shut his eyes in time.

[057-019] After a moment he murmured again:

[057-020] “Cona’n, my dear love, give the warning whistle so that the Fianna will

[057-021] keep out of this place.”

[057-022] A little whoof, like the sound that would be made by a baby and it

[057-023] asleep, came from Cona’n.

[057-024] “Fionn,” said he, “there isn’t a whistle in me. We are done for,” said

[057-025] he.

[057-026] “You are done for, indeed,” said Cuillen, and she smiled a hairy and

[057-027] twisty and fangy smile that almost finished Cona’n.

[057-028] By that time some of the Fianna had returned to the mound to see why

[057-029] [[bran-and-sceolan|Bran]] and Sceo’lan were barking so outrageously. They saw the cave and

[057-030] went into it, but no sooner had they passed the holly branches than

[057-031] their strength went from them, and they were seized and bound by the

[057-032] vicious hags. Little by little all the members of the Fianna returned to

[057-033] the hill, and each of them was drawn into the cave, and each was bound

[057-034] by the sisters.

[057-035] Oisi’n and [[oscar|Oscar]] and mac Lugac came, with the nobles of clann-Baiscne,

[057-036] and with those of clann-Corcoran and clann-Smo’l; they all came, and

[057-037] they were all bound.

[057-038] It was a wonderful sight and a great deed this binding of the Fianna,

[057-039] and the three sisters laughed with a joy that was terrible to hear and

[057-040] was almost death to see. As the men were captured they were carried by

[057-041] the hags into dark mysterious holes and black perplexing labyrinths.

[057-042] “Here is another one,” cried Caevo’g as she bundled a trussed champion

[057-043] along.

[057-044] “This one is fat,” said Cuillen, and she rolled a bulky Fenian along

[057-045] like a wheel.

[057-046] “Here,” said Iaran, “is a love of a man. One could eat this kind of

[057-047] man,” she murmured, and she licked a lip that had whiskers growing

[057-048] inside as well as out.

[057-049] And the corded champion whimpered in her arms, for he did not know

[057-050] but eating might indeed be his fate, and he would have preferred to be

[057-051] coffined anywhere in the world rather than to be coffined inside of that

[057-052] face. So far for them.
