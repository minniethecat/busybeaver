[041-001] IT was so early that not even a bird was yet awake, and the dull grey

[041-002] light that came from the atmosphere enlarged and made indistinct all

[041-003] that one looked at, and swathed all things in a cold and livid gloom.

[041-004] As she trod cautiously through dim corridors [[becfola|Becfola]] was glad that,

[041-005] saving the guards, no creature was astir, and that for some time yet

[041-006] she need account to no person for her movements. She was glad also of

[041-007] a respite which would enable her to settle into her home and draw about

[041-008] her the composure which women feel when they are surrounded by the walls

[041-009] of their houses, and can see about them the possessions which, by the

[041-010] fact of ownership, have become almost a part of their personality.

[041-011] Sundered from her belongings, no woman is tranquil, her heart is not

[041-012] truly at ease, however her mind may function, so that under the broad

[041-013] sky or in the house of another she is not the competent, precise

[041-014] individual which she becomes when she sees again her household in order

[041-015] and her domestic requirements at her hand.

[041-016] Becfola pushed the door of the king’s sleeping chamber and entered

[041-017] noiselessly. Then she sat quietly in a seat gazing on the recumbent

[041-018] monarch, and prepared to consider how she should advance to him when

[041-019] he awakened, and with what information she might stay his inquiries or

[041-020] reproaches.

[041-021] “I will reproach him,” she thought. “I will call him a bad husband

[041-022] and astonish him, and he will forget everything but his own alarm and

[041-023] indignation.”

[041-024] But at that moment the king lifted his head from the pillow and looked

[041-025] kindly at her. Her heart gave a great throb, and she prepared to speak

[041-026] at once and in great volume before he could formulate any question.

[041-027] But the king spoke first, and what he said so astonished her that the

[041-028] explanation and reproach with which her tongue was thrilling fled

[041-029] from it at a stroke, and she could only sit staring and bewildered and

[041-030] tongue-tied.

[041-031] “Well, my dear heart,” said the king, “have you decided not to keep that

[041-032] engagement?”

[041-033] “I--I--!” Becfola stammered.

[041-034] “It is truly not an hour for engagements,” Dermod insisted, “for not

[041-035] a bird of the birds has left his tree; and,” he continued maliciously,

[041-036] “the light is such that you could not see an engagement even if you met

[041-037] one.”

[041-038] “I,” Becfola gasped. “I---!”

[041-039] “A Sunday journey,” he went on, “is a notorious bad journey. No good can

[041-040] come from it. You can get your smocks and diadems to-morrow. But at this

[041-041] hour a wise person leaves engagements to the bats and the staring owls

[041-042] and the round-eyed creatures that prowl and sniff in the dark. Come back

[041-043] to the warm bed, sweet woman, and set on your journey in the morning.”

[041-044] Such a load of apprehension was lifted from Becfola’s heart that she

[041-045] instantly did as she had been commanded, and such a bewilderment had yet

[041-046] possession of her faculties that she could not think or utter a word on

[041-047] any subject.

[041-048] Yet the thought did come into her head as she stretched in the warm

[041-049] gloom that Crimthann the son of Ae must be now attending her at

[041-050] Cluain da chaillech, and she thought of that young man as of something

[041-051] wonderful and very ridiculous, and the fact that he was waiting for

[041-052] her troubled her no more than if a sheep had been waiting for her or a

[041-053] roadside bush.

[041-054] She fell asleep.
