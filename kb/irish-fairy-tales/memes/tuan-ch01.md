[001-001] [[finnian|Finnian]], the Abbott of [[moville|Moville]], went southwards and eastwards in great

[001-002] haste. News had come to him in [[donegal|Donegal]] that there were yet people in his

[001-003] own province who believed in gods that he did not approve of, and the

[001-004] gods that we do not approve of are treated scurvily, even by saintly

[001-005] men.

[001-006] He was told of a powerful gentleman who observed neither Saint’s day nor

[001-007] Sunday.

[001-008] “A powerful person!” said Finnian.

[001-009] “All that,” was the reply.

[001-010] “We shall try this person’s power,” said Finnian.

[001-011] “He is reputed to be a wise and hardy man,” said his informant.

[001-012] “We shall test his wisdom and his hardihood.”

[001-013] “He is,” that gossip whispered--“he is a magician.”

[001-014] “I will magician him,” cried Finnian angrily. “Where does that man

[001-015] live?”

[001-016] He was informed, and he proceeded to that direction without delay.

[001-017] In no great time he came to the [[dun|stronghold]] of the gentleman who followed

[001-018] ancient ways, and he demanded admittance in order that he might preach

[001-019] and prove the new God, and exorcise and terrify and banish even the

[001-020] memory of the old one; for to a god grown old Time is as ruthless as to

[001-021] a beggarman grown old.

[001-022] But the [[ulster|Ulster]] gentleman refused Finnian admittance. He barricaded

[001-023] his house, he shuttered his windows, and in a gloom of indignation and

[001-024] protest he continued the practices of ten thousand years, and would

[001-025] not hearken to Finnian calling at the window or to Time knocking at his

[001-026] door.

[001-027] But of those adversaries it was the first he redoubted.

[001-028] Finnian loomed on him as a portent and a terror; but he had no fear of

[001-029] Time. Indeed he was the foster-brother of Time, and so disdainful of the

[001-030] bitter god that he did not even disdain him; he leaped over the scythe,

[001-031] he dodged under it, and the sole occasions on which Time laughs is when

[001-032] he chances on Tuan, the son of [[cairill|Cairill]], the son of Muredac Red-neck.
