[052-001] “What do you see?” Fionn demanded of the watcher.

[052-002] “Nothing,” that man replied.

[052-003] “Look again,” said Fionn.

[052-004] The eagle-eyed man lifted a face, thin and sharp as though it had been

[052-005] carven on the wind, and he stared forward with an immobile intentness.

[052-006] “What do you see?” said Fionn.

[052-007] “Nothing,” the man replied.

[052-008] “I will look myself,” said Fionn, and his great brow bent forward and

[052-009] gloomed afar.

[052-010] The watcher stood beside, staring with his tense face and unwinking,

[052-011] lidless eye.

[052-012] “What can you see, O Fionn?” said the watcher.

[052-013] “I can see nothing,” said Fionn, and he projected again his grim, gaunt

[052-014] forehead. For it seemed as if the watcher stared with his whole face,

[052-015] aye, and with his hands; but Fionn brooded weightedly on distance with

[052-016] his puckered and crannied brow.

[052-017] They looked again.

[052-018] “What can you see?” said Fionn.

[052-019] “I see nothing,” said the watcher.

[052-020] “I do not know if I see or if I surmise, but something moves,” said

[052-021] Fionn. “There is a trample,” he said.

[052-022] The watcher became then an eye, a rigidity, an intense out-thrusting and

[052-023] ransacking of thin-spun distance. At last he spoke.

[052-024] “There is a dust,” he said.

[052-025] And at that the champions gazed also, straining hungrily afar, until

[052-026] their eyes became filled with a blue darkness and they could no longer

[052-027] see even the things that were close to them.

[052-028] “I,” cried Cona’n triumphantly, “I see a dust.”

[052-029] “And I,” cried another.

[052-030] “And I.”

[052-031] “I see a man,” said the eagle-eyed watcher.

[052-032] And again they stared, until their straining eyes grew dim with tears

[052-033] and winks, and they saw trees that stood up and sat down, and fields

[052-034] that wobbled and spun round and round in a giddily swirling world.

[052-035] “There is a man,” Cona’n roared.

[052-036] “A man there is,” cried another.

[052-037] “And he is carrying a man on his back,” said the watcher.

[052-038] “It is [[cael|Cael of the Iron]] carrying the Carl on his back,” he groaned.

[052-039] “The great pork!” a man gritted.

[052-040] “The no-good!” sobbed another.

[052-041] “The lean-hearted.”

[052-042] “Thick-thighed.”

[052-043] “Ramshackle.”

[052-044] “Muddle-headed.”

[052-045] “Hog!” screamed a champion.

[052-046] And he beat his fists angrily against a tree.

[052-047] But the eagle-eyed watcher watched until his eyes narrowed and became

[052-048] pin-points, and he ceased to be a man and became an optic.

[052-049] “Wait,” he breathed, “wait until I screw into one other inch of sight.”

[052-050] And they waited, looking no longer on that scarcely perceptible speck in

[052-051] the distance, but straining upon the eye of the watcher as though they

[052-052] would penetrate it and look through it.

[052-053] “It is the Carl,” he said, “carrying something on his back, and behind

[052-054] him again there is a dust.”

[052-055] “Are you sure?” said Fionn in a voice that rumbled and vibrated like

[052-056] thunder.

[052-057] “It is the Carl,” said the watcher, “and the dust behind him is Cael of

[052-058] the Iron trying to catch him up.”

[052-059] Then the [[fianna|Fianna]] gave a roar of exultation, and each man seized his

[052-060] neighbour and kissed him on both cheeks; and they gripped hands about

[052-061] Fionn, and they danced round and round in a great circle, roaring with

[052-062] laughter and relief, in the ecstasy which only comes where grisly fear

[052-063] has been and whence that bony jowl has taken itself away.
