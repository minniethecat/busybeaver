[077-001] “You have not told me one word about [[duv-laca|Duv Laca]],” said the Flame Lady

[077-002] reproachfully.

[077-003] “I am coming to that,” replied [[mongan|Mongan]].

[077-004] He motioned towards one of the great vats, and wine was brought to him,

[077-005] of which he drank so joyously and so deeply that all people wondered at

[077-006] his thirst, his capacity, and his jovial spirits.

[077-007] “Now, I will begin again.”

[077-008] Said Mongan: There was an attendant in [[fiachna-finn|Fiachna Finn]]’s palace who was

[077-009] called An Da’v, and the same night that Fiachna’s wife bore a son, the

[077-010] wife of An Da’v gave birth to a son also. This latter child was called

[077-011] mac an Da’v, but the son of Fiachna’s wife was named Mongan.

[077-012] “Ah!” murmured the Flame Lady.

[077-013] The queen was angry. She said it was unjust and presumptuous that the

[077-014] servant should get a child at the same time that she got one herself,

[077-015] but there was no help for it, because the child was there and could not

[077-016] be obliterated.

[077-017] Now this also must be told.

[077-018] There was a neighbouring prince called Fiachna Duv, and he was the ruler

[077-019] of the Dal Fiatach. For a long time he had been at enmity and spiteful

[077-020] warfare with Fiachna [[fionn-mac-uail|Finn]]; and to this Fiachna Duv there was born in

[077-021] the same night a daughter, and this girl was named Duv Laca of the White

[077-022] Hand.

[077-023] “Ah!” cried the Flame Lady.

[077-024] “You see!” said Mongan, and he drank anew and joyously of the fairy

[077-025] wine.

[077-026] In order to end the trouble between Fiachna Finn and Fiachna Duv the

[077-027] babies were affianced to each other in the cradle on the day after they

[077-028] were born, and the men of Ireland rejoiced at that deed and at that

[077-029] news. But soon there came dismay and sorrow in the land, for when the

[077-030] little Mongan was three days old his real father, Mananna’n the son

[077-031] of Lir, appeared in the middle of the palace. He wrapped Mongan in his

[077-032] green cloak and took him away to rear and train in the [[faery|Land of Promise]],

[077-033] which is beyond the sea that is at the other side of the grave.

[077-034] When Fiachna Duv heard that Mongan, who was affianced to his daughter

[077-035] Duv Laca, had disappeared, he considered that his compact of peace was

[077-036] at an end, and one day he came by surprise and attacked the palace.

[077-037] He killed Fiachna Finn in that battle, and be crowned himself King of

[077-038] [[ulster|Ulster]].

[077-039] The men of Ulster disliked him, and they petitioned Mananna’n to bring

[077-040] Mongan back, but Mananna’n would not do this until the boy was sixteen

[077-041] years of age and well reared in the wisdom of the [[land-of-promise|Land of Promise]]. Then

[077-042] he did bring Mongan back, and by his means peace was made between Mongan

[077-043] and Fiachna Duv, and Mongan was married to his cradle-bride, the young

[077-044] Duv Laca.
