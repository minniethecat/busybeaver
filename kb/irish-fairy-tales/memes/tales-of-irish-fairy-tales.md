## Overview

James Stephens' *Irish Fairy Tales* (1920) collects ten classic tales from Irish mythology, drawing primarily from the Fenian Cycle. The tales range from the mythic prehistory of Ireland (The Story of Tuan Mac Cairill) to the boyhood adventures and mature exploits of [[fionn-mac-uail|Fionn mac Uail]], the great hero of the [[fianna|Fianna]].

## Cycles Represented

The collection draws primarily from the **Fenian Cycle** (tales 2–8, 10), concerning [[fionn-mac-uail|Fionn mac Uail]] and the [[fianna|Fianna]]. The opening tale (Tuan Mac Cairill) belongs to the **Mythological Cycle**, recounting the ancient invasions and transformations of Ireland. The closing tales (Becuma, Mongan) bridge the Fenian and Mythological cycles, linking the world of heroes to the realm of the [[tuatha-de-danann|Tuatha Dé Danann]].
