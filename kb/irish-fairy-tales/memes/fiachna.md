# Fiachna Finn

## Identity

Fiachna Finn — "Fiachna the Fair" — was the son of Baltan and an Ulster king elected to the kingship of Ireland. He is most significant in the Irish mythological corpus as the mortal father of [[mongan|Mongan]], whose conception and birth involved the direct intervention of [[manannan-mac-lir|Manannan mac Lir]], the sea god of the [[tuatha-de-danann|Tuatha De Danann]]. Fiachna Finn's reign was marked by ceaseless warfare with his rival and namesake, Fiachna Duv ([[fiachna-duv|Fiachna Duv]]), the ruler of the northeast of Ireland. [^1]

## The Kingship

Fiachna Finn was elected king in the traditional Irish manner — not by primogeniture but by the assembly of the freemen and nobles of the land. His election followed the death of the previous king, and he became one of the several provincial kings who held the high kingship in rotation during this period of Irish legendary history. [^1]

> Fiachna Finn, had been elected king in the place of the king who died. [074-003]

## In the Tales

### The Divine Intervention

During his reign, Fiachna Finn was engaged in constant warfare with Fiachna Duv. In the midst of this conflict, a stranger appeared to Fiachna Finn — none other than [[manannan-mac-lir|Manannan]], the son of Lir, who had come from the [[many-coloured-land|Many-Coloured Land]] with a proposition. Manannan offered to aid Fiachna in his battles in exchange for a single day with Fiachna's wife. From this supernatural union, [[mongan|Mongan]] was conceived and born — a child of two worlds, mortal and divine. [^1]

### The Rivalry with Fiachna Duv

The conflict between Fiachna Finn and [[fiachna-duv|Fiachna Duv]] was the defining political struggle of Fiachna Finn's reign. Fiachna Duv, ruler of the northeastern territory, waged unremitting war against Fiachna Finn in an attempt to seize the kingship. Their enmity divided the province of Ulster and drew in allies from across Ireland. [^1]

> There was a neighbouring prince called Fiachna Duv, and he was the ruler of the northeast of Ireland, and he was in warfare with Fiachna Finn. [077-008]

The war between the two Fiachnas culminated in a final battle in which Fiachna Finn was killed by his rival. Fiachna Duv then crowned himself king in Fiachna Finn's place — an act that would eventually bring him into conflict with the grown Mongan, who would return to reclaim his father's inheritance. [^1]

## Significance

Fiachna Finn occupies a pivotal position in the Mongan cycle as the mortal father whose kingship and death create the conditions for Mongan's extraordinary destiny. He is not a tragic figure so much as a transitional one — the last of an old order whose death clears the stage for a hero born of both this world and the Otherworld. His election, his warfare, his divine pact, and his death in battle form the narrative foundation upon which the whole of *Mongan's Frenzy* is built.
