[074-001] Said [[mongan|Mongan]]:

[074-002] In the days of long ago and the times that have disappeared for ever,

[074-003] there was one [[fiachna-finn|Fiachna Finn]] the son of Baltan, the son of Murchertach,

[074-004] the son of Muredach, the son of Eogan, the son of Neill. He went from

[074-005] his own country when he was young, for he wished to see the land of

[074-006] Lochlann, and he knew that he would be welcomed by the king of that

[074-007] country, for Fiachna’s father and Eolgarg’s father had done deeds in

[074-008] common and were obliged to each other.

[074-009] He was welcomed, and he stayed at the Court of Lochlann in great ease

[074-010] and in the midst of pleasures.

[074-011] It then happened that Eolgarg Mor fell sick and the doctors could not

[074-012] cure him. They sent for other doctors, but they could not cure him, nor

[074-013] could any one say what he was suffering from, beyond that he was wasting

[074-014] visibly before their eyes, and would certainly become a shadow and

[074-015] disappear in air unless he was healed and fattened and made visible.

[074-016] They sent for more distant doctors, and then for others more distant

[074-017] still, and at last they found a man who claimed that he could make a

[074-018] cure if the king were supplied with the medicine which he would order.

[074-019] “What medicine is that?” said they all.

[074-020] “This is the medicine,” said the doctor. “Find a perfectly white cow

[074-021] with red ears, and boil it down in the lump, and if the king drinks that

[074-022] rendering he will recover.”

[074-023] Before he had well said it messengers were going from the palace in all

[074-024] directions looking for such a cow. They found lots of cows which were

[074-025] nearly like what they wanted, but it was only by chance they came on

[074-026] the cow which would do the work, and that beast belonged to the most

[074-027] notorious and malicious and cantankerous female in Lochlann, the Black

[074-028] Hag. Now the Black Hag was not only those things that have been said;

[074-029] she was also whiskered and warty and one-eyed and obstreperous, and she

[074-030] was notorious and ill-favoured in many other ways also.

[074-031] They offered her a cow in the place of her own cow, but she refused to

[074-032] give it. Then they offered a cow for each leg of her cow, but she would

[074-033] not accept that offer unless Fiachna went bail for the payment. He

[074-034] agreed to do so, and they drove the beast away.

[074-035] On the return journey he was met by messengers who brought news from

[074-036] Ireland. They said that the King of [[ulster|Ulster]] was dead, and that he,

[074-037] Fiachna [[fionn-mac-uail|Finn]], had been elected king in the dead king’s place. He at once

[074-038] took ship for Ireland, and found that all he had been told was true, and

[074-039] he took up the government of Ulster.
