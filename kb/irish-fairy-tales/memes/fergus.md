# Fergus Fionnliath

**Fergus Fionnliath** was a man living near the harbour of Galway who was famous throughout Ireland for his intense dislike of dogs. His reputation for canine hatred made him a pivotal figure in the events surrounding the transformation of Tuiren, Fionn's aunt, into a hound.

## Character and Reputation

Fergus Fionnliath was known as the unfriendliest man in the world towards a dog. His reaction to any barking was immediate and violent:

> This gentleman's name was Fergus Fionnliath, and his stronghold was near the harbour of Galway. Whenever a dog barked he would leap out of his seat, and he would throw everything that he owned out of the window in the direction of the bark.

His character stood in stark contrast to that of Fionn mac Uail, who delighted in dogs and understood them from "the setting of the first little white tooth to the rocking of the last long yellow one."

## Role in Tuiren's Story

When Uct Dealv sought revenge against Tuiren (whom she had transformed into a hound), she deliberately chose Fergus as the hound's destination precisely because of his reputation for mistreating dogs. She did not want a good home for the transformed Tuiren; she wanted the worst home that could be found.

When Uct Dealv arrived at Fergus's stronghold with the hound, the servant at the door commanded her to leave the dog outside. Fergus himself came to the door and declared:

> "By my faith," he cried in amazement, "it is a dog."

Yet despite his reputation, the events that followed did not proceed as Uct Dealv had planned, for the inherent nobility of the hound (who was truly the transformed Tuiren) moved even Fergus's hardened heart.

## Legacy

Fergus Fionnliath serves as a notable counterpoint to the dog-loving culture of the Fianna, where hounds like Bran and Sceolan were cherished companions. His eventual treatment of Tuiren's hound-form contributed to the chain of events that led to the birth of Bran and Sceolan, Fionn's two most beloved hounds.
