[051-001] It would be hard to tell of the terror that was in Fionn’s breast and

[051-002] in the hearts of the [[fianna|Fianna]] while they attended the conclusion of that

[051-003] race.

[051-004] They discussed it unendingly, and at some moment of the day a man

[051-005] upbraided Fionn because he had not found Caelte the son of Rona’n as had

[051-006] been agreed on.

[051-007] “There is no one can run like Caelte,” one man averred.

[051-008] “He covers the ground,” said another.

[051-009] “He is light as a feather.”

[051-010] “Swift as a stag.” “Lunged like a bull.”

[051-011] “Legged like a wolf.”

[051-012] “He runs!”

[051-013] These things were said to Fionn, and Fionn said these things to himself.

[051-014] With every passing minute a drop of lead thumped down into every heart,

[051-015] and a pang of despair stabbed up to every brain.

[051-016] “Go,” said Fionn to a hawk-eyed man, “go to the top of this hill and

[051-017] watch for the coming of the racers.”

[051-018] And he sent lithe men with him so that they might run back in endless

[051-019] succession with the news.

[051-020] The messengers began to run through his tent at minute intervals calling

[051-021] “nothing,” “nothing,” “nothing,” as they paused and darted away.

[051-022] And the words, “nothing, nothing, nothing,” began to drowse into the

[051-023] brains of every person present.

[051-024] “What can we hope from that Carl?” a champion demanded savagely.

[051-025] “Nothing,” cried a messenger who stood and sped.

[051-026] “A clump!” cried a champion.

[051-027] “A hog!” said another.

[051-028] “A flat-footed.”

[051-029] “Little-wlnded.”

[051-030] “Big-bellied.”

[051-031] “Lazy-boned.”

[051-032] “Pork!”

[051-033] “Did you think, Fionn, that a whale could swim on land, or what did you

[051-034] imagine that lump could do?”

[051-035] “Nothing,” cried a messenger, and was sped as he spoke.

[051-036] Rage began to gnaw in Fionn’s soul, and a red haze danced and flickered

[051-037] before his eyes. His hands began to twitch and a desire crept over him

[051-038] to seize on champions by the neck, and to shake and worry and rage among

[051-039] them like a wild dog raging among sheep.

[051-040] He looked on one, and yet he seemed to look on all at once.

[051-041] “Be silent,” he growled. “Let each man be silent as a dead man.”

[051-042] And he sat forward, seeing all, seeing none, with his mouth drooping

[051-043] open, and such a wildness and bristle lowering from that great glum brow

[051-044] that the champions shivered as though already in the chill of death, and

[051-045] were silent.

[051-046] He rose and stalked to the tent-door.

[051-047] “Where to, O Fionn?” said a champion humbly.

[051-048] “To the hill-top,” said Fionn, and he stalked on.

[051-049] They followed him, whispering among themselves, keeping their eyes on

[051-050] the ground as they climbed.
