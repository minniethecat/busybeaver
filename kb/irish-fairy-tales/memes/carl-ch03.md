[048-001] When they came among the host the men of Ireland gathered about the vast

[048-002] stranger; and there were some who hid their faces in their mantles so

[048-003] that they should not be seen to laugh, and there were some who rolled

[048-004] along the ground in merriment, and there were others who could only hold

[048-005] their mouths open and crook their knees and hang their arms and stare

[048-006] dumbfoundedly upon the stranger, as though they were utterly dazed.

[048-007] [[cael|Cael of the Iron]] came also on the scene, and he examined the stranger

[048-008] with close and particular attention.

[048-009] “What in the name of the devil is this thing?” he asked of Fionn.

[048-010] “Dear heart,” said Fionn, “this is the champion I am putting against you

[048-011] in the race.”

[048-012] Cael of the Iron grew purple in the face, and he almost swallowed his

[048-013] tongue through wrath.

[048-014] “Until the end of eternity,” he roared, “and until the very last moment

[048-015] of doom I will not move one foot in a race with this greasy, big-hoofed,

[048-016] ill-assembled resemblance of a beggarman.”

[048-017] But at this the Carl burst into a roar of laughter, so that the eardrums

[048-018] of the warriors present almost burst inside of their heads.

[048-019] “Be reassured, my darling, I am no beggarman, and my quality is not more

[048-020] gross than is the blood of the most delicate prince in this assembly.

[048-021] You will not evade your challenge in that way, my love, and you shall

[048-022] run with me or you shall run to your ship with me behind you. What

[048-023] length of course do you propose, dear heart?”

[048-024] “I never run less than sixty miles,” Cael replied sullenly.

[048-025] “It is a small run,” said the Carl, “but it will do. From this place

[048-026] to the Hill of the Rushes, [[slieve-luachra|Slieve Luachra]] of [[munster|Munster]], is exactly sixty

[048-027] miles. Will that suit you?”

[048-028] “I don’t care how it is done,” Cael answered.

[048-029] “Then,” said the Carl, “we may go off to Slieve Luachra now, and in the

[048-030] morning we can start our race there to here.”

[048-031] “Let it be done that way,” said Cael.

[048-032] These two set out then for Munster, and as the sun was setting they

[048-033] reached Slieve Luachra and prepared to spend the night there.
