# Kerry

## Overview

Kerry is the southwestern region of Ireland, appearing in Stephens' *Irish Fairy Tales* as the birthplace and fosterage-ground of Fionn Mac Uail. It is where Fionn's mother [[muirne|Muirne]] gave birth, where the druid-women [[bovmall|Bovmall]] and [[lia-luachra|Lia Luachra]] raised the infant hero in hiding, and where the King of Kerry later employed the young Fionn.

## Location

- **Region**: Part of the historic province of [[munster|Munster]]
- **Modern equivalent**: County Kerry, in the southwest of Ireland
- **Type of place**: Kingdom and region, characterized by remote woodlands suitable for concealment

## In the Tales

Kerry is central to the early chapters of "The Boyhood of Fionn." After [[muirne|Muirne]], Fionn's mother, gave birth to the child, she entrusted him to two women [[druid|druids]] for safekeeping: "of Kerry. She gave the child to [[bovmall|Bovmall]] and [[lia-luachra|Lia Luachra]] to rear" [012-023]. This act of concealment was necessary because Fionn's father Uail had been killed by the clann-Morna, who now threatened the infant heir to the leadership of the [[fianna|Fianna]].

When the young Fionn was grown, his foster-mother [[bovmall|Bovmall]] sent him away from the [[slieve-bloom|Slieve Bloom]] woods, and [[muirne|Muirne]] "went in sorrowful and lonely ways to her lord in Kerry" [013-045]. Kerry thus represents [[muirne|Muirne]]'s own place of origin and her destination after years of hiding.

Later in his youth, Fionn "went southwards and was next in the employment of the King of Kerry" [019-068], working in service to gain experience before his eventual return to the [[fianna|Fianna]]. The King of Kerry is one of several provincial rulers who employed the wandering young hero.

## Mythological Significance

Kerry functions in the Fionn cycle as the hidden cradle of the hero — the remote region where the rightful heir to the [[fianna|Fianna]] was preserved in secret while the usurping clann-Morna held power. The druid-women [[bovmall|Bovmall]] and [[lia-luachra|Lia Luachra]], deeply connected to the forests and wild places of Kerry, represent the nurturing but hidden forces that protect the destined hero.

The region's association with fosterage — the Irish custom of sending noble children to be raised by other families — reflects the mythological geography of concealment and preparation that surrounds Fionn's early years.
