[015-001] He was given good training by the women in running and leaping and

[015-002] swimming.

[015-003] One of them would take a thorn switch in her hand, and Fionn would

[015-004] take a thorn switch in his hand, and each would try to strike the other

[015-005] running round a tree.

[015-006] You had to go fast to keep away from the switch behind, and a small boy

[015-007] feels a switch. Fionn would run his best to get away from that prickly

[015-008] stinger, but how he would run when it was his turn to deal the strokes!

[015-009] With reason too, for his nurses had suddenly grown implacable. They

[015-010] pursued him with a savagery which he could not distinguish from hatred,

[015-011] and they swished him well whenever they got the chance.

[015-012] Fionn learned to run. After a while he could buzz around a tree like

[015-013] a maddened fly, and oh, the joy, when he felt himself drawing from the

[015-014] switch and gaining from behind on its bearer! How he strained and panted

[015-015] to catch on that pursuing person and pursue her and get his own switch

[015-016] into action.

[015-017] He learned to jump by chasing hares in a bumpy field. Up went the hare

[015-018] and up went Fionn, and away with the two of them, hopping and popping

[015-019] across the field. If the hare turned while Fionn was after her it was

[015-020] switch for Fionn; so that in a while it did not matter to Fionn which

[015-021] way the hare jumped for he could jump that way too. Long-ways, sideways

[015-022] or baw-ways, Fionn hopped where the hare hopped, and at last he was the

[015-023] owner of a hop that any hare would give an ear for.

[015-024] He was taught to swim, and it may be that his heart sank when he fronted

[015-025] the lesson. The water was cold. It was deep. One could see the bottom,

[015-026] leagues below, millions of miles below. A small boy might shiver as he

[015-027] stared into that wink and blink and twink of brown pebbles and murder.

[015-028] And these implacable women threw him in!

[015-029] Perhaps he would not go in at first. He may have smiled at them, and

[015-030] coaxed, and hung back. It was a leg and an arm gripped then; a swing for

[015-031] Fionn, and out and away with him; plop and flop for him; down into chill

[015-032] deep death for him, and up with a splutter; with a sob; with a grasp

[015-033] at everything that caught nothing; with a wild flurry; with a raging

[015-034] despair; with a bubble and snort as he was hauled again down, and down,

[015-035] and down, and found as suddenly that he had been hauled out.

[015-036] Fionn learned to swim until he could pop into the water like an otter

[015-037] and slide through it like an eel.

[015-038] He used to try to chase a fish the way he chased hares in the bumpy

[015-039] field--but there are terrible spurts in a fish. It may be that a fish

[015-040] cannot hop, but he gets there in a flash, and he isn’t there in another.

[015-041] Up or down, sideways or endways, it is all one to a fish. He goes and

[015-042] is gone. He twists this way and disappears the other way. He is over

[015-043] you when he ought to be under you, and he is biting your toe when you

[015-044] thought you were biting his tail.

[015-045] You cannot catch a fish by swimming, but you can try, and Fionn tried.

[015-046] He got a grudging commendation from the terrible women when he was able

[015-047] to slip noiselessly in the tide, swim under water to where a wild duck

[015-048] was floating and grip it by the leg.

[015-049] “Qu--,” said the duck, and he disappeared before he had time to get the

[015-050] “-ack” out of him.

[015-051] So the time went, and Fionn grew long and straight and tough like a

[015-052] sapling; limber as a willow, and with the flirt and spring of a young

[015-053] bird. One of the ladies may have said, “He is shaping very well, my

[015-054] dear,” and the other replied, as is the morose privilege of an aunt,

[015-055] “He will never be as good as his father,” but their hearts must have

[015-056] overflowed in the night, in the silence, in the darkness, when they

[015-057] thought of the living swiftness they had fashioned, and that dear fair

[015-058] head.
