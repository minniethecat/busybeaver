# Aillen mac Midna

## Overview

Aillen mac Midna was a fairy being of the [[tuatha-de-danann|Tuatha Dé Danann]] who dwelt in the [[sidhe-mound|sídhe]]-mound of [[fionn-mac-uail|Finn]]achy. Every year at [[samhain|Samhain]], he would come to [[tara|Tara]] and put all its defenders to sleep with enchanted music, then burn the royal seat with a blast of fire from his mouth. He terrorised [[tara|Tara]] for nine years during the reign of [[conn-of-the-hundred-battles|Conn of the Hundred Battles]], until the boy [[fionn-mac-uail|Fionn mac Cumhaill]] defeated him—winning leadership of the [[fianna|Fianna]] as his reward. [023-006][024-060]

## Identity

Aillen mac Midna belonged to the [[tuatha-de-danann|Tuatha Dé Danann]], the ancient divine race that retreated into the [[sidhe-mound|fairy mound]]s of Ireland. He came from the Shí of [[fionn-mac-uail|Finn]]achy, a [[sidhe-mound|sídhe]]-dwelling in the [[land-of-promise|Otherworld]], and crossed each [[samhain|Samhain]] into the mortal realm to work his destruction. [018-015][023-006]

His weapon was his own enchanted spear, tipped with the poison of a creature called Birgha, and he wielded fire as his primary means of attack—blowing a dart of blue flame from his mouth. [024-074][024-085] The hill where he stood to loose his fire upon [[tara|Tara]] became known as the Ard of Fire. [025-049]

## In the Tales

Aillen appears in *The Boyhood of Fionn*, where his annual burning of [[tara|Tara]] sets the stage for Fionn's great deed. For nine years, at the feast of [[samhain|Samhain]], Aillen would arrive at [[tara|Tara]] and lull every warrior and guardsman to sleep with bewitching fairy music. When all lay unconscious, he would open his mouth and hiss a fierce blue flame that set the royal buildings ablaze. [024-060][025-049]

[[conn-of-the-hundred-battles|Conn of the Hundred Battles]], the High King, was powerless against him; no man could resist the sleep-[[enchantment|spell]]. The young Fionn, however, learned of a means to stay awake—by pressing the point of Aillen's own poison-spear against his forehead whenever drowsiness threatened. Armed with this spear, which [[fiacuil-mac-cona|Fiacuil mac Cona]] had obtained, Fionn stood watch on the night of [[samhain|Samhain]]. [024-085][024-094]

When Aillen arrived and began his music, Fionn used the spear to keep himself alert. As Aillen paused his playing and unleashed his fire, Fionn caught the flame in his crimson cloak and smothered it against the ground. Aillen, finding his fire extinguished and his sleep-music defied, retreated towards the [[sidhe-mound|sídhe]]. Fionn pursued and, with a cast of the spear, struck Aillen dead at the very threshold of the [[sidhe-mound|fairy mound]]. [025-049]

## Significance

Aillen mac Midna represents one of the archetypal fairy antagonists in the Fenian Cycle—a being of the [[land-of-promise|Otherworld]] whose defeat by a mortal hero marks the hero's coming of age. His slaying at the hands of the young Fionn is Fionn's first great deed, the act that wins him command of the [[fianna|Fianna]] from the High King, and symbolically represents the passing of supernatural dread into an age of human heroes.
