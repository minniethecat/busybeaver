[078-001] One day [[mongan|Mongan]] and [[duv-laca|Duv Laca]] were playing chess in their palace. Mongan

[078-002] had just made a move of skill, and he looked up from the board to see

[078-003] if Duv Laca seemed as discontented as she had a right to be. He saw

[078-004] then over Duv Laca’s shoulder a little black-faced, tufty-headed cleric

[078-005] leaning against the door-post inside the room.

[078-006] “What are you doing there?” said Mongan.

[078-007] “What are you doing there yourself?” said the little black-faced cleric.

[078-008] “Indeed, I have a right to be in my own house,” said Mongan.

[078-009] “Indeed I do not agree with you,” said the cleric.

[078-010] “Where ought I be, then?” said Mongan.

[078-011] “You ought to be at Dun Fiathac avenging the murder of your father,”

[078-012] replied the cleric, “and you ought to be ashamed of yourself for not

[078-013] having done it long ago. You can play chess with your wife when you have

[078-014] won the right to leisure.”

[078-015] “But how can I kill my wife’s father?” Mongan exclaimed. “By starting

[078-016] about it at once,” said the cleric. “Here is a way of talking!” said

[078-017] Mongan.

[078-018] “I know,” the cleric continued, “that Duv Laca will not agree with a

[078-019] word I say on this subject, and that she will try to prevent you from

[078-020] doing what you have a right to do, for that is a wife’s business, but a

[078-021] man’s business is to do what I have just told you; so come with me now

[078-022] and do not wait to think about it, and do not wait to play any more

[078-023] chess. Fiachna Duv has only a small force with him at this moment,

[078-024] and we can burn his palace as he burned your father’s palace, and

[078-025] kill himself as he killed your father, and crown you King of [[ulster|Ulster]]

[078-026] rightfully the way he crowned himself wrongfully as a king.”

[078-027] “I begin to think that you own a lucky tongue, my black-faced friend,”

[078-028] said Mongan, “and I will go with you.”

[078-029] He collected his forces then, and he burned Fiachna Duv’s fortress, and

[078-030] he killed Fiachna Duv, and he was crowned King of Ulster.

[078-031] Then for the first time he felt secure and at liberty to play chess.

[078-032] But he did not know until afterwards that the black-faced, tufty-headed

[078-033] person was his father Mananna’n, although that was the fact.

[078-034] There are some who say, however, that Fiachna the Black was killed in

[078-035] the year 624 by the lord of the Scot’s Dal Riada, Condad Cerr, at the

[078-036] battle of Ard Carainn; but the people who say this do not know what they

[078-037] are talking about, and they do not care greatly what it is they say.
