# Germany

Germany is central to [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]'s _Philosophical Theory of [[state|the State]]_ as the home of German idealism—[[immanuel-kant|Kant]], [[johann-gottlieb-fichte|Fichte]], and above all [[g-w-f-hegel|Hegel]]—the philosophical tradition from which [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]'s own theory most directly descends. [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] discusses Germany not primarily as a political entity but as the context within which the most systematic philosophical account of the state was developed.

## Germany as Intellectual Context

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] notes that the "literary intercourse of [[france|France]], [[england|England]], and Germany was far closer" in the eighteenth century than is sometimes recognized, emphasizing the transnational character of [[europe|European]] intellectual life (009-005). The "path which had been opened in Germany by the translation" of [[england|English]] and [[france|French]] works contributed to the development of German philosophical culture, which in turn deeply influenced [[england|British]] idealism (009-007).

The German contribution to political philosophy came not only from "the great philosophers" but also from "the poets and sentimentalists of Germany," indicating the broad cultural context within which philosophical ideas about the state developed (009-010).

## The Prussian State

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] addresses the common criticism that "[[g-w-f-hegel|Hegel]] found his ideal in the [[prussia|Prussian]] bureaucracy," defending [[g-w-f-hegel|Hegel]] against the charge that his philosophy of the state is merely an apology for [[prussia|Prussian]] absolutism (009-051). The [[prussia|Prussian state]] of [[g-w-f-hegel|Hegel]]'s time, [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] argues, was not the militaristic autocracy of later reputation but a state in the process of reform and modernization. [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] notes the exclamation of "Hardenberg, the [[prussia|Prussian]] minister," as evidence of the reformist spirit within the [[prussia|Prussian state]] (009-055).
