# France

France appears in [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]'s _Philosophical Theory of [[state|the State]]_ primarily as the home of [[jean-jacques-rousseau|Rousseau]] and the [[french-revolution|French Revolution]]ary tradition—a major source of the modern democratic ideas that [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]'s theory both draws upon and seeks to transcend. French political thought, particularly [[jean-jacques-rousseau|Rousseau]]'s _[[contrat-social|Contrat Social]]_, is central to [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]'s argument about the [[general-will|general will]].

## Revolutionary France

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] discusses the impact of [[jean-jacques-rousseau|Rousseau]]'s ideas on "the minds of Revolutionary France," noting how [[jean-jacques-rousseau|Rousseau]]'s concepts "gave to the aspirations of the Revolution a form which was serviceable" for political action (001-025). The translation of philosophical ideas into political practice—however imperfect that translation may be—is itself a significant phenomenon for the philosophical theory of the state.

The [[french-revolution|French Revolution]] represents both the power and the danger of political ideas. [[jean-jacques-rousseau|Rousseau]]'s concept of the [[general-will|general will]], distorted by the pressures of revolutionary politics, could become a justification for terror. [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]'s careful interpretation of [[jean-jacques-rousseau|Rousseau]] aims to recover the philosophical core of the [[general-will|general will]] from its revolutionary misappropriations.

## France in European Context

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] notes that "the literary intercourse of France, [[england|England]], and [[germany|Germany]] was far closer" in the eighteenth century than national boundaries might suggest, emphasizing the [[europe|European]] context within which French political thought developed and exercised its influence (009-005). The French contribution to political philosophy cannot be understood in isolation from the broader [[europe|European]] conversation about sovereignty, rights, and the nature of [[community|political community]].
