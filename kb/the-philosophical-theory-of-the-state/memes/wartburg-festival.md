# Wartburg Festival

The **Wartburg Festival** (Wartburgfest) was a gathering of [[germany|German]] nationalist student fraternities (*Burschenschaften*) held in October 1817 at Wartburg Castle in Eisenach. The students celebrated the 300th anniversary of the Reformation and the recent victory at the Battle of Leipzig (1813). [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] discusses the event as part of the political context in which [[g-w-f-hegel|Hegel]] published the *[[philosophy-of-right|Philosophy of Right]]*.

## Political Context

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] situates the Wartburg demonstration within a period of anti-constitutional reaction in the [[germany|German states]] after the Napoleonic Wars:

> "The anti-constitutional reaction had recently declared itself. The demonstration at the Wartburg, celebrating the anniversary of the Reformation, and of the Battle of Leipzig, took place in October, 1817." [009-053]

The festival alarmed conservative authorities. The student demonstrations, which included book-burnings of works deemed un-[[germany|German]], prompted the [[germany|German]] Confederation to issue the Carlsbad Decrees (1819), imposing press censorship and university surveillance.

## Hegel's Involvement

The event directly figures in [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]'s narrative of [[g-w-f-hegel|Hegel]]'s career. [[g-w-f-hegel|Hegel]]'s *[[philosophy-of-right|Philosophy of Right]]* (published 1820, though dated 1821) contained a preface attacking **Jakob Friedrich Fries**, a philosopher who had spoken at the Wartburg Festival:

> "The publication of Hegel's book with a preface attacking Fries for some expressions used by him at the Wartburg festival, took place, as we said, in 1820, and Hegel had moved from Heidelberg to Berlin shortly before." [009-055]

This attack on Fries — who advocated for a more democratic, emotionally-grounded politics against [[g-w-f-hegel|Hegel]]'s rationalist approach — shaped the reception of [[g-w-f-hegel|Hegel]]'s political philosophy and contributed to the perception of [[g-w-f-hegel|Hegel]] as a conservative defender of the [[prussia|Prussian state]].

## Significance for Bosanquet

For [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]], the Wartburg episode illustrates the fraught political atmosphere in which [[germany|German]] political philosophy developed. The tension between nationalist-democratic sentiment (represented by Fries and the Burschenschaften) and the rationalist-organic conception of the state (represented by [[g-w-f-hegel|Hegel]]) was not merely academic — it played out in real political confrontations of the post-Napoleonic era.
