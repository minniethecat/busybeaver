# New Science

*Scienza Nuova* (*The New Science*, 1725, revised 1744) by [[giambattista-vico|Giambattista Vico]] is a pioneering work in the philosophy of history and an early attempt to establish a systematic science of [[society|human society]]. [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] references [[giambattista-vico|Vico]]'s *New Science* in a single but significant context: as one of the earliest attempts in [[europe|modern Europe]] to "inaugurate the Science of [[society|Society]] as a new departure" [001-013].

## A Precursor to Social Science

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] situates [[giambattista-vico|Vico]]'s *New Science* at the beginning of a tradition of attempts to develop a systematic science of society — a tradition that runs through the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries and includes the work of [[montesquieu|Montesquieu]], [[auguste-comte|Comte]], and [[herbert-spencer|Spencer]]. For [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]], [[giambattista-vico|Vico]]'s effort is noteworthy as an early recognition that [[society|human society]] requires its own form of scientific understanding, distinct from both natural science and traditional political philosophy [001-013].

## Bosanquet's Use of This Work

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]'s reference to [[giambattista-vico|Vico]] is brief, occurring in his introductory discussion of the emergence of social theory as a distinct mode of inquiry. Unlike his sustained engagement with works such as [[herbert-spencer|Spencer]]'s *[[man-vs-state|Man v. State]]* or [[jean-jacques-rousseau|Rousseau]]'s *[[contrat-social|Du Contrat Social]]*, [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] invokes [[giambattista-vico|Vico]] primarily as a historical marker, acknowledging the *New Science* as a forerunner of the sociological tradition he both draws upon and critiques [001-013].
