# Ancient Rome

Ancient Rome appears in [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]'s _Philosophical Theory of [[state|the State]]_ as a significant example in the historical development of political and legal [[institution|institutions]]. Roman political experience—particularly the development of Roman law and the transition from republic to empire—provides important material for [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]'s analysis of the nature of [[community|political community]].

## Roman Law and Institutions

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] discusses the "foreigners' court at Rome which obtained for itself the important function of creating a law for aliens, and thereby a type of law founded upon reason and common to all men" (001-013). This development of the _jus gentium_—the law of nations—represents a crucial step in the emergence of universal legal concepts. Roman legal innovation created institutional forms that transcended the particular customs of any single community.

The "Foreigners Court at Rome" is cited in the Index as a specific institutional example, suggesting its importance for [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]'s argument about the development of rational legal forms (012-140). The Roman capacity to create legal [[institution|institutions]] that could accommodate diverse populations within a single framework anticipates, in embryonic form, the universal character of the modern state.

## Rome as Historical Example

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] groups Rome alongside "[[sparta|Sparta]] and republican Rome" as examples of ancient republics that later political thinkers have invoked (004-017). The Roman Republic provided a model of non-monarchical government that influenced modern republican thought from Machiavelli through to the [[america|American]] founders.

The contrast between the ancient city-state—whether [[greece|Greek]] or Roman—and the modern nation-state is fundamental to [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]'s historical framework. Roman political experience, like [[greece|Greek]], provides essential concepts for political philosophy while also revealing the limitations of the ancient form of [[community|political community]].
