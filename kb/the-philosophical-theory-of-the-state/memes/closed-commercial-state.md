# The Closed Commercial State

*Der geschlossene Handelsstaat* (The Closed Commercial State) is an 1800 treatise by **[[johann-gottlieb-fichte|Johann Gottlieb Fichte]]** that advocates for state control of foreign trade, price regulation, and occupational assignment. [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] discusses it in Chapter 9 as an early instance of state socialism arising from the organic theory of the state taken to an extreme.

## Fichte's Organic Conception

[[johann-gottlieb-fichte|Fichte]] claimed to be the first to apply the simile of an organism to the whole civic relation. In a passage [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] quotes at length, [[johann-gottlieb-fichte|Fichte]] writes:

> "Just as, in the natural product, every part can be what it is only in this combination, and out of this combination simply would not be this...: just so it is only in the combination of the State that man attains a definite position in the series of things, a point of rest in nature; and each attains this determinate position towards others, and towards Nature, only through the fact that he is in this determinate combination. … In the organic body every part continually maintains the whole, and while it maintains it, is itself maintained thereby; just such is the citizen's relation to the State." [009-046]

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] notes that in this organic vision "we seem to be back with [[plato|Plato]] and [[aristotle|Aristotle]]" [009-046].

## Bosanquet's Critique

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]'s criticism centres on [[johann-gottlieb-fichte|Fichte]]'s failure to distinguish between two distinct functions:

> "The distinction between maintenance of the citizen's determinate activity, and maintenance of the general conditions of such activity, being destroyed by Fichte in his desire to make State action positive and not negative, the conclusion necessarily arises that the citizen must be secured and maintained in his definite activity or occupation, and from this springs the notion of the closed commercial State; 'closed' against foreign trade in order that the government may be able to determine prices and assign occupations." [009-046]

For [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]], [[johann-gottlieb-fichte|Fichte]]'s error lies in collapsing the distinction between the state maintaining *general conditions* for [[individuality|individual]] activity and the state determining the *specific content* of that activity — the latter being, on [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]'s view, a form of overreach incompatible with genuine [[liberty|freedom]].

## Historical Significance

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] characterises the work as "perhaps the earliest document of something approaching [[state|State]] socialism" [009-046]. The work represents an important transitional moment in [[germany|German]] political philosophy, where the idea of contract (central to [[immanuel-kant|Kant]] and the early [[johann-gottlieb-fichte|Fichte]]) transmutes into the idea of an organic whole — a transition that [[g-w-f-hegel|Hegel]] would later complete with greater philosophical sophistication.
