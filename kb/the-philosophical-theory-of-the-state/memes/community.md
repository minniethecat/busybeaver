# Community

In [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]'s _Philosophical Theory of [[state|the State]]_, **Community** is the foundational category of social and political life. Before [[state|the State]], before the social contract, before rights and laws, there is the community — the living tissue of shared consciousness, custom, and mutual recognition in which human beings become fully human.

## The Community as the Ground of Human Life

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] opens his work with the question of "the nature and value of the community in which man finds himself to be a member" (001-003). This is not a secondary question about political arrangements but the primary question about human existence itself:

> The community which has a youth, a maturity, and an old age, like a living being — this is the object which the political philosopher must first endeavour to understand. (001-006)

The community is the medium in which [[individuality|individual]] minds develop. A human being "would not freely find himself in the community unless he was capable of reproducing within himself the life of the community" (001-010) — community membership is not external to the self but constitutive of it.

## Community and the Common Life

The "common life" is [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]'s term for the shared fabric of customs, [[institution|institutions]], language, and mutual understanding that makes [[individuality|individual]] existence meaningful. In "every class of persons in the community — the statesman, the soldier, the artist, the thinker — the common life takes a distinctive form" (001-009), yet it is recognisably the same life.

This common life is not static but historical. Communities have "a youth, a maturity, and an old age" (001-006), passing through phases of growth, flourishing, and decline — a conception [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] draws from the ancient [[greece|Greek]]s, for whom the polis was a living thing with its own biography.

## Community and Coercion

One of [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]'s most important arguments concerns the relation between community and coercion. The "cohesive forces of any community whatever" (003-056) include not only law and [[force|enforcement]] but custom, sympathy, and shared ideals:

> The cohesive forces of any community include the whole range of influences by which men are moulded into a common type. Force is only one of these, and not the most important. (003-056)

This insight has direct implications for the theory of [[state|the State]]: [[state|the State]] does not create community out of nothing; it builds upon, organises, and rationalises the pre-existing common life. The [[state|State]]'s coercive power is legitimate only because it is "regularly accountable to the community" (003-058).

## Community, Individual, and Self-Realisation

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]'s idealism reaches its fullest expression in his account of the [[individuality|individual]]'s relation to the community:

| View of Individual | Relation to Community | Result |
|-------------------|----------------------|--------|
| Atomistic | External, contractual | Isolated will, partial [[liberty|freedom]] |
| Idealist ([[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]) | Internal, constitutive | Self-realisation through common life |

> The individual, if the community is penetrated by a sense of universal interest, becomes himself a more fully developed self. (003-016)

This is not a sacrifice of [[individuality|individual]]ity but its fulfilment. The community provides the conditions — language, [[institution|institutions]], [[morality|moral]] ideals — within which [[individuality|individual]]ity can develop to its fullest extent.

## From Community to State

The [[state|State]], for [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]], is the community become self-conscious and rationally organised. It is the "community of minds" (001-008) raised to the level of explicit principle. The transition from community to [[state|State]] is the transition from custom to law, from implicit unity to explicit self-government:

> The State is the community in its full and proper life in a community of minds, or more strictly in a community of self-conscious beings who know their common good and will it. (001-008)
