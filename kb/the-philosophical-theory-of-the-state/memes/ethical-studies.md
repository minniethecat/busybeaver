# Ethical Studies

_Ethical Studies_ (1876) is [[f-h-bradley|F.H. Bradley]]'s principal work in [[morality|moral]] philosophy and a foundational text of [[england|British]] idealism. [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] engages with [[f-h-bradley|Bradley]]'s ethical theory at several points in _The Philosophical Theory of [[state|the State]]_, treating [[f-h-bradley|Bradley]]'s account of self-realization and "my station and its duties" as philosophical resources for his own theory.

## Core Arguments

[[f-h-bradley|Bradley]]'s central thesis is that [[morality|moral]]ity is a matter of self-realization—the [[individuality|individual]] realizes their true self not through the satisfaction of particular desires but through identification with the concrete ethical life of their community. His famous doctrine of "my station and its duties" holds that [[morality|moral obligation]]s arise from the [[individuality|individual]]'s social roles and positions, not from abstract universal principles.

[[f-h-bradley|Bradley]] criticizes both utilitarian hedonism and [[immanuel-kant|Kant]]ian formalism, arguing that neither can adequately account for the concrete [[morality|moral]] experience of persons embedded in determinate social relations. The [[morality|moral]] self is not an abstract choosing agent but a person whose identity is constituted by their place within a system of social practices and [[institution|institutions]].

## Bosanquet's Use

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] draws on [[f-h-bradley|Bradley]]'s ethical theory to support his own account of the relation between [[individuality|individual]] and society. [[f-h-bradley|Bradley]]'s argument that the self is realized through its social roles—that "the [[individuality|individual]] is what he is through his membership of a community"—is central to [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]'s theory of the state as the institutional condition for [[individuality|individual]] self-realization. The state, for [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] as for [[f-h-bradley|Bradley]], is not external to the [[morality|moral]] life of [[individuality|individual]]s but is the framework within which [[morality|moral]] personality develops and finds expression.
