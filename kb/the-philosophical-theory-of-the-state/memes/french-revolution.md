# French Revolution

The French Revolution (1789–1799) appears in [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]'s _Philosophical Theory of [[state|the State]]_ as a crucial moment in the development of modern political consciousness. [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] treats it not as a historical narrative but as a philosophical event—the point at which [[jean-jacques-rousseau|Rousseau]]'s theory of the [[general-will|general will]] entered practical politics.

## In Bosanquet's Analysis

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] raises the question of [[jean-jacques-rousseau|Rousseau]]'s influence on the Revolution directly: the "ideas which inspired the leaders of the French Revolution" cannot be understood without reference to [[jean-jacques-rousseau|Rousseau]]'s political philosophy (001-021). The Revolution, for [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]], represents the attempt to translate the philosophical concept of the [[general-will|general will]] into political reality.

## Rousseau's Influence

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] notes that the Revolution "drew its formulae" from [[jean-jacques-rousseau|Rousseau]]'s writings (009-002). The Declaration of the [[rights|Rights]] of Man and of the Citizen, the abolition of feudal privileges, and the assertion of [[sovereignty|popular sovereignty]] all bear the mark of [[jean-jacques-rousseau|Rousseau]]'s _[[contrat-social|Du Contrat Social]]_. [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] is careful to distinguish [[jean-jacques-rousseau|Rousseau]]'s philosophical intentions from the Revolution's political outcomes, while acknowledging the historical connection between them.

## Significance for State Theory

The French Revolution marks the transition from the _ancien régime_—in which sovereignty resided in the monarch—to the modern conception of the state as the expression of the people's will. For [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]], this transition raises the central problem of political philosophy: how can the state be both the expression of [[individuality|individual]] [[liberty|freedom]] and the source of binding obligation?
