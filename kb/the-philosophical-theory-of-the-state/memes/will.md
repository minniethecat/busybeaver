# Will

In [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]'s _Philosophical Theory of [[state|the State]]_, **Will** is the central psychological and metaphysical category. It is not mere desire or caprice but the self-determining activity of a rational being — "the will that wills itself" (006-064). The entire theory of [[state|the State]] rests on understanding how [[individuality|individual]] wills are transformed and fulfilled within the rational structure of political life.

## The Social Will

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] argues that the will is inherently social. Just as an [[individuality|individual]]'s will evolves from day to day, the "social will" represents the continuous, self-correcting rational tendency of a community:

> The social will, like the will of any one of us from day to day, is a living and growing thing, not a fixed sum of decisions once for all arrived at. (002-039)

This dynamic character of will is crucial: it means that [[state|the State]]'s authority cannot be reduced to any single vote or majority decision. The real will of society is the underlying rational direction, not the momentary aggregate of opinions.

## Will and the Community

The will of the [[individuality|individual]] finds its object not in isolation but in community. [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] follows [[g-w-f-hegel|Hegel]] in holding that will achieves genuine content only through its objects, and the highest object is the rational community itself:

> The imperative claim of the will that wills itself is our own inmost demand, and it finds its satisfaction only in a world which is itself a system of self-conscious wills. (006-069)

In [[family|the family]] "at its best the will has an object which is not alien to it" (006-068). The [[state|State]] extends this principle — it is the institutional form in which the will encounters an object adequate to its rational nature.

## General Will and Real Will

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] distinguishes between three dimensions of will:

| Form of Will | Character | Relation to [[state|State]] |
|-------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Actual will | Momentary desire or preference | May oppose [[state|the State]] |
| Real will | The will's true rational end | Coincides with [[state|the State]] |
| General will | The [[real-will|rational will]] of the whole | Embodied in [[state|the State]] |

The [[general-will|General Will]] "generalises the will" (005-026) — it is not the sum of [[individuality|individual]] preferences (the "Will of All") but the rational principle that makes each [[individuality|individual]] will genuinely free. The Will of All "aims at the interests of [[individuality|individual]]s as such," while the [[general-will|General Will]] aims at the [[common-good|common interest]] which is the true interest of each (005-026).

## Freedom of the Will

For [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]], [[liberty|freedom]] is not the absence of determination but [[autonomy|self-determination]] by reason. The famous [[g-w-f-hegel|Hegel]]ian formula — "The free will is the will that wills itself" (006-064) — means that a will is free when its object is its own rational nature, not something external and alien.

This has direct political implications: the [[individuality|individual]] is most free when participating in the rational life of [[state|the State]], because [[state|the State]]'s laws are the expression of the [[individuality|individual]]'s own real will, not an external constraint.

## The Logic of the Will

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] speaks of "the living logic of the will, which alone can create a unity of experience" (008-009). This logic is not formal deduction but the inherent [[reason|rationality]] of purposive action. The will, by its nature, seeks coherence and system — it cannot rest in contradiction:

> The means at its disposal... are shaped by the fundamental logic of the will. (008-015)

This teleological structure of the will is what makes [[state|the State]] possible as a rational institution. Because wills inherently seek rational coherence, they converge on a common rational order — [[state|the State]].
