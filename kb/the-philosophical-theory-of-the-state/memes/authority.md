# Authority

**Political authority** is the right to make and enforce law (004-001)—the [[morality|moral]] power that distinguishes legitimate government from mere coercion. In _The Philosophical Theory of [[state|the State]]_, [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] dedicates Chapter IV to analyzing the authority of the state and grounding it in the [[general-will|general will]].

## The Problem of Authority

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] frames the problem sharply: by what right does the state compel obedience? The liberal tradition answers "consent," but [[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] finds [[consent|consent theory]] inadequate. [[consent|Consent]] presupposes the very [[morality|moral]] personality that the state makes possible. The authority of the state cannot rest on a historical act of agreement but must be grounded in the ongoing rational relationship between the [[individuality|individual]] and the [[community|political community]] (004-041).

## Authority and the General Will

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]]'s solution is that the authority of the state is the authority of the [[general-will|general will]]—the [[real-will|rational will]] of the community directed toward the common good. "The essence of this '[[social-contract|social pact]]' is further reducible to the following formula: Each of us puts into the common stock his person and his entire powers under the supreme direction of the [[general-will|general will]]" (004-032).

Authority, on this account, is not an external claim against the [[individuality|individual]] but the expression of their own rational nature. The state has authority because—and to the extent that—it embodies the [[general-will|general will]].

## Authority and Force

[[bernard-bosanquet|Bosanquet]] distinguishes authority from mere force. A tyrant may compel obedience through fear, but compulsion is not authority. Authority involves a claim to rightness that the subject can recognize as valid. The state's monopoly on legitimate force is justified only because that force serves the [[general-will|general will]]—it removes obstacles to rational [[liberty|freedom]] rather than imposing an alien power.
