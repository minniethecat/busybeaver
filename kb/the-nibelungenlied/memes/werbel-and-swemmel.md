Werbel and Swemmel (also Schwemmelein) are King Etzel's minstrels (Spielleute), who serve as royal messengers and envoys. They play a pivotal role in the Nibelungenlied by delivering Kriemhild's invitation to the Burgundian court — the invitation that sets the final tragedy in motion.

## The Mission to Worms

Kriemhild, seeking revenge for Siegfried's death, asks Etzel to send messengers to Worms inviting her brothers to visit Hunland. Werbel and Swemmel are chosen for this mission (022-153). They are described as the king's own minstrels, trusted servants who travel with the authority of the Hunnish crown.

> "Des Königs Spielleute, Werbel und Schwemmelein" (022-153)

## At the Burgundian Court

The minstrels arrive at Worms and deliver the invitation. Werbel, described as "the swift" (024-022), acts as the primary spokesman. They convey Kriemhild's message with diplomatic skill, and the Burgundians receive them with honor despite the underlying tension (024-034).

> "Gen Worms in die Veste, Werbel und Schwemmelein" (024-034)

After the Burgundians accept the invitation, Werbel and Swemmel return to Hunland with news of the acceptance. Their successful mission seals the fate of the Burgundians, as the journey they have arranged will end in catastrophe.

## Legacy

As Etzel's minstrels, Werbel and Swemmel represent the diplomatic machinery that, ironically, enables the destruction. Their seemingly routine mission as messengers becomes the mechanism through which Kriemhild's revenge plot is set in motion.
