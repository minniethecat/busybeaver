Eckewart is a Burgundian margrave (Markgraf) introduced alongside [[gere|Gere]] as one of the two margraves of the Burgundian realm (001-035). He is distinguished from Gere by his deeper personal bond with [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]: he becomes her chamberlain (Kämmerer), accompanies her to [[hun-court|Hunland]], and serves as her guardian and protector at [[etzels-court|Etzel's court]]. His most significant narrative function is as the loyal watchman who warns the arriving Burgundians of the dangers awaiting them in Hunland.

## At the Burgundian Court

Eckewart is first introduced alongside Gere: "Die beiden Markgrafen Gere und Eckewart" (001-035). The two margraves are frequently paired throughout the epic, appearing together in ceremonial and military contexts. At the Burgundian court, Eckewart participates in the governance and ceremonial life of [[worms|Worms]] under King [[gunther|Gunther]].

## Kriemhild's Chamberlain and Guardian

Eckewart's most defining role is his service to Kriemhild. When Kriemhild departs for Hunland to marry King [[etzel|Etzel]], Eckewart is among the margraves who accompany her: "Eckewart der Markgraf zog mit Kriemhilden" (011-044). He remains in her service in Hunland, becoming her chamberlain — a position of intimate trust within the royal household.

> "Ihr Kämmerer war Eckewart: drum ward er gern gesehn" (023-047)

Unlike many Burgundians who return home, Eckewart stays with Kriemhild permanently: "bei ihr im Lande der Markgraf Eckewart" (019-002). This enduring loyalty places him in a unique position — a Burgundian insider who has become part of the Hunnish court, and who understands the tensions between the two worlds.

## The Warning at the Border

Eckewart's most crucial narrative moment occurs in Adventure 26, when the Burgundian army arrives at the border of Hunland. As Kriemhild's trusted man, he rides out to meet them and delivers a grave warning. He tells them plainly that they have not been invited and that danger awaits (026-185, 026-189).

> "Eckewart geheißen war dieser Ritter gut" (026-185)

When the Burgundians dismiss his warning, Eckewart grows more urgent. He warns of the shame and destruction to come (026-189, 026-197), and offers to guide them to a safe lodging:

> "Kommt Eckewart in Eile, Kriemhildens Untertan" (026-227)

He bluntly states the reality of their situation: "Geladet hat uns Niemand" — no one has invited us (026-233). Eckewart's warning, though ignored, marks him as one of the few characters who perceives the impending catastrophe and tries to prevent it.

## Character and Significance

Eckewart embodies the ideal of the loyal retainer whose allegiance transcends political boundaries. His dual identity — Burgundian by birth, Hunnish by service — gives him a perspective that most characters lack. Where Hagen and the Burgundian kings see only the surface of Etzel's hospitality, Eckewart recognizes the undercurrent of Kriemhild's vengeful design. His warning scene in Adventure 26 functions as a narrative hinge: the last moment when the tragedy might have been averted, had the Burgundians heeded him.

His title "der getreue Eckewart" (the faithful Eckewart) — though not used directly in the Nibelungenlied text — would later become proverbial in German literature for the loyal guardian who warns in vain.
