# Vosges

The Vosges Mountains (Wasgenwald) in the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied serve as the setting for [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s murder — a wilderness space beyond the courtly world where treachery replaces hospitality. The epic's hunters travel into these forested highlands for sport, and it is there, at a forest spring, that [[hagen|Hagen]] spears [[siegfried|Siegfried]] through his one vulnerable spot.

## Location & Geography

- **Type**: [[horses-and-mounts|Mount]]ain range and forest (hunting wilderness east of the [[rhine|Rhine]])
- **Ruler**: None — unclaimed wilderness under the nominal authority of the Burgundian kings
- **Real-world counterpart**: The Vosges Mountains (northeastern France) and the [[odenwald|Odenwald]] (Germany), traditionally identified as the location of the hunt
- **Associated characters**: [[siegfried|Siegfried]], [[hagen|Hagen]], [[gunther|Gunther]], [[gernot|Gernot]], [[geiselher|Geiselher]], [[volker|Volker]]

### Strategic Significance

The Vosges represent the wilderness that lies just beyond the ordered world of the Burgundian court. It is a space where the normal rules of hospitality do not apply — a fact [[hagen|Hagen]] exploits to commit murder without witnesses. The forest setting is essential to the narrative: it isolates [[siegfried|Siegfried]] from his allies, provides the pretext of a hunting accident, and creates the physical distance from [[worms|Worms]] that allows the conspiracy to succeed. The spring where [[siegfried|Siegfried]] dies becomes one of the epic's most iconic locations.

## Key Events at This Location

- [[hagen|Hagen]] arranges a hunt in the Vosges mountains as the pretext for [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s murder (016-178, 016-209)
- [[siegfried|Siegfried]], [[hagen|Hagen]], and the Burgundian nobles hunt bears, boars, and other game in the forest (016-178, 016-188)
- [[hagen|Hagen]] withholds the wine supply, forcing the hunters to seek a spring (016-209, 016-217)
- [[siegfried|Siegfried]] races [[hagen|Hagen]] to the spring and is speared through his vulnerable spot as he bends to drink (016-236, 016-242)
- [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s body is carried back to [[worms|Worms]] on a shield (016-353)

## Historical Notes

- The Wasgenwald (Vosges) was a heavily forested region in the early medieval period, forming the boundary between Lotharingia and the Duchy of Swabia.
- The [[odenwald|Odenwald]], located east of the [[rhine|Rhine]] between [[worms|Worms]] and the Main River, is sometimes identified as the actual setting based on topographical details in the epic.
- Medieval hunting expeditions were significant social events for the nobility, involving large retinues, specialized huntsmen, and elaborate protocols — [[hagen|Hagen]]'s manipulation of these conventions underscores his cunning.
