# Bechelaren

Bechelaren is the seat of [[rudiger|Margrave Rüdiger]] on the [[danube|Danube]], a prosperous and hospitable march that serves as the critical waypoint between the Burgundian realm and [[etzel|Etzel]]'s court. In the epic's narrative architecture, Bechelaren stands as the last moment of peace before the massacre — the place where the Burgundians are welcomed with genuine warmth by [[rudiger|Rüdiger]] and his wife [[gotelind|Gotelind]], and where their daughter is betrothed to [[geiselher|Giselher]] in a marriage that will never be consummated.

## Location & Geography

- **Type**: Fortified castle and margraviate (markgraveschaft) on the [[danube|Danube]]
- **Ruler**: [[rudiger|Margrave Rüdiger]] ([[rudiger|Rüdiger of Bechelaren]])
- **Real-world counterpart**: Pöchlarn, Lower Austria — a town on the southern bank of the [[danube|Danube]] in the Mostviertel region
- **Associated characters**: [[rudiger|Rüdiger]], [[gotelind|Gotelind]] ([[rudiger|Rüdiger]]'s wife), [[rudiger|Rüdiger]] and [[gotelind|Gotelind]]'s daughter (betrothed to [[geiselher|Giselher]]), [[geiselher|Giselher]]

### Strategic Significance

Bechelaren occupies the most tragic position in the epic's geography. Located on the [[danube|Danube]] between the land of the Burgundians and the land of the Huns, it is a border march — a place that belongs to [[etzel|Etzel]]'s realm but shares cultural and feudal ties with the Burgundian world. [[rudiger|Rüdiger]] is [[etzel|Etzel]]'s vassal, but he has personally escorted [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] from [[worms|Worms]] and is bound by ties of friendship and impending marriage to the Burgundian kings. When the massacre at [[etzel|Etzel]]'s court forces him to choose between loyalty to his lord and his guests, the choice destroys him. Bechelaren's tragedy is that it represents everything the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied's violence destroys: hospitality, friendship, the hope of peaceful alliance through marriage.

## Historical Notes

- Bechelaren corresponds to the modern town of Pöchlarn in Lower Austria, on the southern bank of the [[danube|Danube]]. The name "Bechlaren" derives from a Celtic or early Germanic toponym.
- Pöchlarn was an important river crossing and a margraviate in the Bavarian Eastern March (Ostarrîchi, the origin of Austria) during the High Middle Ages.
- The historical [[rudiger|Rüdiger]] may reflect the figure of a Bavarian border margrave serving under the Carolingian or early Árpád dynasties, though no specific historical margrave directly corresponds to the epic's character.
- The route from [[worms|Worms]] to [[etzel|Etzel]]'s court described in the epic — through Bavaria, past Passau and the Inn river confluence, along the [[danube|Danube]] — closely follows the medieval [[danube|Danube]] trading route, with Bechelaren/Pöchlarn as a known stopping point.
