# Saxony and Denmark

Saxony (Sachsenland) and Denmark (Dänemark) appear in the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied as the allied northern kingdoms that declare war on Burgundy in Adventure 4. Ruled by the brothers-in-arms Liudeger (Saxon king) and Liudegast (Danish king), these realms launch an invasion that [[siegfried|Siegfried]] repels, capturing both enemy kings and cementing his heroic status at the Burgundian court.

## Location & Geography

- **Type**: Two allied kingdoms north of Burgundy
- **Ruler**: King Liudeger of Saxony, King Liudegast of Denmark
- **Real-world counterpart**: Saxony (the medieval Duchy of Saxony, northern Germany) and Denmark (the medieval Kingdom of Denmark, Jutland and the Danish islands)
- **Associated characters**: Liudeger (Saxon king), Liudegast (Danish king), their allied forces

### Strategic Significance

The Saxon-Danish war serves a crucial narrative function: it is [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s first opportunity to demonstrate his martial superiority in the service of Burgundy. Until this point, he is known only by reputation. The victory over Saxony and Denmark proves that [[siegfried|Siegfried]] is not merely a boastful stranger but the greatest warrior of his age — "the hero of the [[netherlands|Netherlands]]" who fights "with great strength" (004-192). The captured kings Liudeger and Liudegast are later released without ransom when they acknowledge [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s supremacy (Adventure 5), demonstrating his magnanimity.

## Historical Notes

- The medieval Duchy of Saxony was a powerful realm in northern Germany, often in conflict with the southern and western territories of the Holy Roman Empire.
- Denmark in the 12th-13th century was undergoing expansion and Christianization, with frequent wars along its southern border with Saxony.
- The alliance of Liudeger and Liudegast in the epic may reflect historical alliances between Saxon and Danish rulers during the 12th century.
