# Siegmund

Siegmund (Middle High German: Sigemunt) is the King of the [[netherlands|Netherlands]], father of the epic's hero [[siegfried|Siegfried]]. A wise and loving parent, he rules [[xanten|Xanten]] alongside his wife Sieglind, reluctantly permits his son's journey to Burgundy, and endures the unimaginable: outliving his heroic son.

## Identity & Status

- **Title/Role**: King of the [[netherlands|Netherlands]] (Niderland), ruler of [[xanten|Xanten]]
- **Affiliation**: Court of [[xanten|Xanten]] (independent sovereign)
- **Family**: Sieglind (wife, Queen of the [[netherlands|Netherlands]]), [[siegfried|Siegfried]] (son), [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] (daughter-in-law)
- **First Appearance**: Adventure 02 — introduced alongside his wife as [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s parents (002-002)

## Role in the Narrative

### Part I: The Reluctant Father (Adventures 2-19)

Siegmund is first mentioned at [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s knighthood ceremony in [[xanten|Xanten]] (002-002). When [[siegfried|Siegfried]] announces his intention to journey to [[worms|Worms]] to woo [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]], Siegmund protests — he knows the Burgundian court's reputation and fears for his son's safety (003-025, 003-049). Despite his objections, he supports [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s quest, sending knights and provisions (003-063, 003-090). After [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s successful knighting and the Saxon war, Siegmund welcomes [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] to [[xanten|Xanten]] with joy (011-057, 011-077). When news of [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s murder reaches [[xanten|Xanten]], Siegmund's grief is overwhelming — he demands revenge and leads a party to [[worms|Worms]] to recover [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s body (017-035), but is ultimately persuaded to accept the peace settlement.

### Part II (Adventures 20-39)

Siegmund does not appear in Part II. He returns to [[xanten|Xanten]], accepting that he cannot avenge his son, and fades from the narrative — the last mention being after [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s funeral (017+).

## Character Analysis

### Motivations

Siegmund is motivated by paternal love and royal responsibility. He opposes [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s reckless quest not from weakness but from foresight. After [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s death, his desire for revenge is checked by the political reality that the Burgundian kings outrank him in power — a feudal calculation that underscores the epic's tragic theme.

### Virtues & Flaws

Siegmund represents the wise king — cautious, loving, and dignified. His inability to protect his son or avenge him is not a personal failing but a structural tragedy: even a king cannot shield his child from fate.

## Scholarly Notes

- Siegmund is a minor figure who nonetheless carries the poem's most poignant paternal grief. His counterpart in the Old Norse tradition (Sigmund in the Völsunga saga) is a far more active hero — suggesting the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied poet deliberately reduced Siegmund's role to emphasize [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s autonomy.
- The name Siegmund (victory-protection) parallels [[siegfried|Siegfried]] (victory-peace), reinforcing the dynastic naming pattern.
