# Lindger and Lindgast

Lindger (Lüdeger, king of the Saxons) and Lindgast (Lüdegast, king of the Danes) were allied rulers who declared war on the [[burgundian-court|Burgundian kingdom]] in Adventure 4. Their invasion was decisively repelled by [[siegfried|Siegfried]] in his first military campaign at the Burgundian court.

## The Saxon-Danish Invasion (Adventure 4)

Messengers arrived at [[worms|Worms]] announcing that Lüdeger of Saxony and Lüdegast of Denmark intended to attack Burgundy (004-020). The narrator names them as a united threat:

> Lüdegast and Lüdeger, they seek to attack your land. (004-020)

King [[gunther|Gunther]] was troubled by the declaration, but [[siegfried|Siegfried]] — eager to prove his worth — volunteered to lead the Burgundian defense. He raised a force and marched to meet the invaders.

In the ensuing campaign, Siegfried personally captured King Lüdegast on the battlefield (004-202), taking him prisoner. He then confronted Lüdeger, the stronger of the two, in fierce combat:

> Siegfried the bold and King Lüdeger fought fiercely. (004-299)

Lüdeger too was defeated, and the Burgundian victory was complete (004-395).

## Aftermath

Both kings were brought as captives to Worms, where Gunther treated them with the formal courtesies owed to royal prisoners. After pledging peace and offering tribute, they were eventually released. The victory established Siegfried's military reputation at the Burgundian court independently of his relationship with [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]].

## Narrative Function

The Saxon-Danish invasion episode performs an important structural role: it demonstrates Siegfried's prowess and his indispensable value to Gunther BEFORE the bridal quest to [[isenstein|Isenstein]]. Without this episode, Siegfried's integration into Burgundian society would rest solely on his marriage — the invasion provides a public, martial proof of his heroic status.
