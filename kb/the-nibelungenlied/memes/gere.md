Gere is a Burgundian margrave (Markgraf) who serves at the court of Worms under King Gunther. He functions as a marshal, a diplomatic envoy, and a trusted companion throughout the Nibelungenlied. He is introduced alongside his fellow margrave [[eckewart|Eckewart]] and is revealed to be a cousin (Vetter) of [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]].

## Role at the Burgundian Court

Gere is first introduced alongside Eckewart as one of the two margraves of the Burgundian realm, both described as "die beiden Markgrafen" (001-035). He serves as a marshal (Marschall) and participates in courtly ceremonial duties, most notably leading Kriemhild's horse by the bridle during a royal procession (010-013).

> "Der Markgraf Gere führte am Zaum Kriemhildens Pferd" (010-013)

He is frequently paired with [[eckewart|Eckewart]] in battle arrays and ceremonial contexts. The two margraves appear together in the lists of Burgundian nobility, as seen when they are enumerated among the "edeln Ritter gut" (020-339).

## Diplomatic Envoy to Isenstein

Gere plays a prominent role during Gunther's bridal quest to [[isenstein|Isenstein]]. He is one of the foremost messengers and heralds, alongside Ortwein. As the Burgundians arrive in Brunhild's land, Gere is seen standing in the courtyard and is the first to be noticed by the Isenstein court (012-070).

> "Die sah den kühnen Gere auf dem Hofe stehn" (012-070)

He is received with honor and lodged appropriately (012-081, 012-086). Unlike other members of the retinue, Gere is specifically noted as Kriemhild's cousin ("Gere war ihr Vetter," 012-121), a kinship tie that enhances his standing. During the diplomatic exchanges at Isenstein, Gere speaks directly as a representative of Burgundy, answering queries about Gunther's intentions and the purpose of the journey (012-105, 012-113).

> "Antwort gab der Markgraf Gere, ein Ritter gut" (012-105)

## The Journey to Hunland and Later Appearances

Gere accompanies the Burgundians on later campaigns and journeys. He is present during the events surrounding Etzel's courtship of Kriemhild, where he appears alongside Geiselher during the arrival scenes (020-181). He takes an active speaking role as an envoy, offering to deliver messages (020-289).

Gere's epithet "der schnelle Gere" (the swift Gere) appears in the later adventures (020-585), emphasizing his continued role as a messenger and man of action. He remains in the Burgundian retinue through the later adventures, appearing alongside Geiselher, Gernot, and Ortwein (024-285).

## Character and Significance

Gere is depicted throughout the epic as a model vassal: loyal, swift to act, and eloquent in diplomatic settings. His dual role as both warrior-margrave and courtly envoy reflects the ideal of the medieval German courtier. His kinship with Kriemhild places him within the inner circle of the Burgundian royal house, while his frequent partnership with Eckewart establishes a recognizable pair of noble companions who frame the Burgundian courtly identity.
