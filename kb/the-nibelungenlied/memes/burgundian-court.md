# Burgundian Court

The Burgundian Court is the central political organization of the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied's first part. Ruled by King [[gunther|Gunther]] with his brothers [[gernot|Gernot]] and [[geiselher|Geiselher]], seated at [[worms|Worms]] on the [[rhine|Rhine]], it is introduced in the epic's opening stanzas: "Es wuchs in Burgunden solch edel Mägdelein" (001-005).

The court is the stage for the epic's defining events — [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s arrival, the wooing of [[brunhild|Brunhild]], the [[queens-quarrel|quarrel of the queens]], and the murder of [[siegfried|Siegfried]]. Its destruction at [[etzels-court|Etzel's court]] in the second part marks the epic's catastrophic conclusion.

## Overview

- **Type**: Royal Court
- **Ruler**: King [[gunther|Gunther]], with his brothers [[gernot|Gernot]] and [[geiselher|Geiselher]] (001-013)
- **Seat**: [[worms|Worms]] on the [[rhine|Rhine]]
- **Era**: Active throughout the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied (Adventures 1–39)

### Relationships

The Burgundian Court maintains complex relationships with other powers. It is allied with [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s [[netherlands|Netherlands]] through marriage and military alliance. It is connected to [[brunhild|Brunhild]]'s [[isenstein|Isenstein]] through [[gunther|Gunther]]'s marriage.

The court is in tension with the [[nibelungenland|Nibelung kingdom]] — [[siegfried|Siegfried]] rules both realms, creating an ambiguous hierarchy. After [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s murder, the court becomes the target of [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s [[revenge]], ultimately leading to its destruction at [[etzels-court|Etzel's court]].

## Role in the Narrative

### Major Actions

- Receives [[siegfried|Siegfried]] at [[worms|Worms]] and forms an alliance through his marriage to [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] (Adventures 3–10)
- Wages war against the [[saxon-invasion|Saxons]] with [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s crucial assistance (Adventure 4) (004-257)
- Undertakes the [[bridal-quest|expedition to Isenstein]] to win [[brunhild|Brunhild]] for [[gunther|Gunther]] (Adventures 6–7)
- [[hagen|Hagen]] murders [[siegfried|Siegfried]] to restore Burgundian [[ere|honor]] after the queens' quarrel (Adventure 16)
- [[hagen|Hagen]] seizes and sinks the [[nibelung-treasure|Nibelung treasure]] in the [[rhine|Rhine]] (Adventure 19)
- Departs for [[etzel|Etzel]]'s court despite [[hagen|Hagen]]'s warnings, knowing it is a death trap (Adventure 27)

### Narrative Function

The Burgundian Court serves as the epic's center of gravity in Part I — the stable political order into which [[siegfried|Siegfried]] arrives and which his presence destabilizes.

In Part II, it becomes a mobile tragedy: the entire court travels eastward to its doom. The court's destruction represents the collapse of the heroic world order. "Nach den Burgunden war ihr Land genannt" (001-019) — after the Burgundians their land was named, and after their fall, the land is empty.

## Historical / Legendary Notes

- The historical Burgundians were an East Germanic tribe that established a kingdom in the [[rhine|Rhine]]land in the early 5th century.
- The historical Burgundian kingdom was destroyed by the Huns under [[etzel|Attila]] in 437 CE — the event that the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied transforms into the epic's catastrophic finale.
- The epic relocates the Burgundians from their historical location (around [[worms|Worms]]) to a literary court that merges historical memory with 12th-century courtly culture.
