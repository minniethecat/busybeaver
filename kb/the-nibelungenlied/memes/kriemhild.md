# Kriemhild

Kriemhild (Middle High German: Kriemhilt) is the female protagonist of the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied, undergoing the most dramatic transformation of any character in medieval German epic. Introduced as a princess "who kept herself free of [[minne|Minne]]" (001-069), she becomes [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s devoted wife, then his grieving widow, and finally the architect of the apocalyptic revenge that consumes the Burgundian court. Her arc from innocent maiden to "she-devil" (vâlendinne) is the spine of the epic's two-part structure.

## Identity & Status

- **Title/Role**: Princess of Burgundy; Queen of [[xanten|Xanten]] and [[nibelungenland|Nibelungenland]] (by first marriage); Queen of the Huns (by second marriage)
- **Affiliation**: [[burgundian-court|Burgundian Court]] (by birth), [[hun-court|Hun Court]] (by marriage to [[etzel|Etzel]])
- **Family**: [[ute|Ute]] (mother, Queen of Burgundy), [[gunther|Gunther]], [[gernot|Gernot]], [[geiselher|Geiselher]] (brothers, Burgundian kings), [[siegfried|Siegfried]] (first husband), [[etzel|Etzel]] (second husband, King of the Huns)
- **First Appearance**: Adventure 01 — introduced as the beautiful maiden of Burgundy (001-069)

## Role in the Narrative

### Part I: The Beloved (Adventures 1–19)

Kriemhild begins the epic as the idealized object of courtly desire. [[siegfried|Siegfried]] journeys to [[worms|Worms]] to woo her based on reputation alone. Their meeting at the Pentecost festival (Adventure 05) is the emotional high point of Part I — "they looked upon each other with longing [[minne|Minne]]'s need" (005-118). Their marriage is depicted as genuinely happy.

But Kriemhild is not merely passive. She reveals to [[hagen|Hagen]] the secret of [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s vulnerable spot, trusting [[hagen|Hagen]]'s feigned concern for [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s safety — an act of misplaced trust that enables the murder. After [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s death, her grief is portrayed with harrowing intensity. She refuses to see [[gunther|Gunther]] until he publicly acknowledges the murder, and she secures the Nibelung treasure as her inheritance (019-070) — only for [[hagen|Hagen]] to steal and sink it.

### Part II: The Avenger (Adventures 20–39)

Kriemhild's transformation in Part II is total. After years of mourning, she accepts [[etzel|Etzel]]'s marriage proposal — not for love but for power. As Queen of the Huns, she commands an army. After thirteen years, she invites her brothers to [[etzel|Etzel]]'s court, ostensibly for reconciliation, actually for revenge.

The final adventures depict Kriemhild orchestrating the destruction of the Burgundian army. She demands the return of the Nibelung treasure from [[hagen|Hagen]]; when he refuses, she orders the hall set ablaze. In the climactic scene, she beheads [[hagen|Hagen]] with [[balmung|Balmung]] — [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s own sword — before [[hildebrand|Hildebrand]], Dietrich von Bern's master-at-arms and an Amelung exile, kills her in horror at a woman wielding a sword against a bound man (039-172).

## Character Analysis

### Motivations

Kriemhild's primary motivation is [[triuwe|Triuwe]] — loyalty to her murdered husband. In a world where the feudal system protects [[hagen|Hagen]] from justice, Kriemhild's personal [[triuwe|Triuwe]] to [[siegfried|Siegfried]] overrides all other obligations: to her brothers, to her second husband, to her own life. Her revenge is horrifying in its scope but comprehensible in its origin — the failure of the Burgundian court to deliver justice for [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s murder.

### Virtues & Flaws

Kriemhild's virtues and flaws are the same qualities in different contexts. Her loyalty ([[triuwe|Triuwe]]) to [[siegfried|Siegfried]] is admirable; the same loyalty becomes monstrous when it demands the destruction of her entire family. Her intelligence — she plans the revenge over thirteen years — becomes cunning. Her capacity for love becomes the capacity for hatred. The [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied does not judge Kriemhild as much as it traces the logic by which love becomes destruction.

## Death

Kriemhild is killed by [[hildebrand|Hildebrand]] in the final scene of the epic (Adventure 39). After she beheads the bound [[hagen|Hagen]] with [[balmung|Balmung]], [[hildebrand|Hildebrand]] — the aged Amelung warrior and [[dietrich|Dietrich von Bern]]'s master-at-arms — is so appalled by the sight of a woman executing a bound man that he strikes her down with his sword. Her death completes the epic's vision of total destruction: the avenger, the avenged, and the entire world that contained them all lie dead.

## Scholarly Notes

- The Old Norse tradition (Völsunga saga, Atlakviða) names her Gudrun and presents her revenge differently — she kills Atli ([[etzel|Etzel]]) for murdering her brothers, reversing the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied's direction of vengeance.
- Kriemhild's transformation has been read as a critique of the revenge ethic, a misogynistic portrayal of female power, and a proto-feminist narrative of a woman who refuses to accept male-controlled justice.
- The name "Kriemhild" may derive from "grim" (mask/helmet) + "hild" (battle), suggesting "battle-mask" — a fitting name for a character who hides her intentions behind a mask of queenship.
