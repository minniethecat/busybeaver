# Dankwart

Dankwart is a Burgundian warrior and [[hagen|Hagen]]'s younger brother. As marshal of the Burgundian court, he is a skilled fighter first introduced alongside the other Burgundian knights. His death at [[etzel|Etzel]]'s court — preceded by his killing of [[bloedel|Bloedel]] — is the immediate trigger for the epic's final massacre.

## Identity & Status

- **Title/Role**: Marshal (Marschall) of the Burgundian court; Burgundian knight
- **Affiliation**: [[burgundian-court|Burgundian Court]] — vassal of [[gunther|King Gunther]]
- **Family**: Aldrian (father), [[hagen|Hagen of Tronje]] (brother)
- **First Appearance**: Adventure 01 — listed as marshal among the Burgundian court officials (001-034, 001-041)

## Role in the Narrative

### Part I: The Secondary Warrior (Adventures 1-19)

Dankwart is introduced alongside his brother [[hagen|Hagen]] and other Burgundian knights (001-034, 001-041). He fights with distinction in the Saxon war (Adventure 04), where his prowess earns him recognition (004-139, 004-158, 004-252, 004-290, 004-304). He accompanies the Burgundians to [[isenstein|Isenstein]] when [[brunhild|Brunhild]] is wooed (006-095, 006-164, 007-053, 007-166, 007-221, 007-246). When [[siegfried|Siegfried]] and [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] visit [[worms|Worms]], Dankwart serves as [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s designated knight (008-136). He also appears during the Nibelung treasure transfer (013-091).

### Part II: The Catalyst of the Final Battle (Adventures 20-39)

Dankwart travels to [[etzel|Etzel]]'s court with the Burgundian party (020-182, 024-233, 025-092). When the quarrel at the banquet erupts, Dankwart kills [[bloedel|Bloedel]] ([[bloedel|Blödelin]]), [[etzel|Etzel]]'s brother, after [[bloedel|Bloedel]] attacks the Burgundian retinue (032-013, 032-025). This killing turns a private quarrel into total war, forcing the Hunnish court to join the conflict. Dankwart is then overwhelmed and killed himself alongside the other Burgundians.

## Character Analysis

### Motivations

Dankwart is motivated by kin loyalty ([[triuwe|Triuwe]] to his brother [[hagen|Hagen]]) and feudal obligation to the Burgundian kings. He is a straightforward warrior character — loyal, brave, and uncomplicated in his loyalties.

### Virtues & Flaws

Dankwart's defining virtue is his courage in battle. His role as the catalyst of the final battle gives him disproportionate narrative importance — his killing of [[bloedel|Bloedel]] is the single action that makes the epic's general slaughter inevitable.

## Notable Passages

> "Dankwart der Schnelle, von Metz Herr Ortwein." (Dankwart the Swift, Lord Ortwein of Metz.) (001-034)

> "Damit schlug er Blödeln einen schwinden Schwertesschlag." (With that he struck Bloedel a swift sword-blow.) (032-025)

## Death

Dankwart kills [[bloedel|Bloedel]] in the banquet hall after being attacked (032-025). This act, committed in self-defense, brings the Hunnish army down upon the Burgundians. Dankwart is then killed in the ensuing battle, dying alongside his brother [[hagen|Hagen]] and his lords.

## Scholarly Notes

- Dankwart's role as the catalyst of the final battle is structurally important: the poet uses a minor character's action to trigger the catastrophe rather than having [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] or [[etzel|Etzel]] order the attack directly.
- Dankwart's relationship to [[hagen|Hagen]] is never fully explained in the epic beyond "[[hagen|Hagen]]'s brother" — they share a father (Aldrian) but their mother is unnamed.
