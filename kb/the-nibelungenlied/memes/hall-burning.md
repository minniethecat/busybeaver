# The Hall Burning

The hall burning (Adventure 36) is the most shocking episode of the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied's final battle. [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]], finding her warriors unable to dislodge the Burgundians from [[etzel|Etzel]]'s great hall, orders the building set on fire. The flames consume the hall and the wounded — but the surviving Burgundians, trapped and parched, drink the blood of the fallen to stay alive, emerging from the inferno even more terrible than before.

## Overview

- **Adventure(s)**: 36
- **Location**: [[etzel|Etzel]]'s great hall, [[etzels-court|Etzelnburg]]
- **Key Participants**: [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] (who orders the fire), [[hagen|Hagen]] (who leads the Burgundians), the Burgundian army, [[etzel|Etzel]] (powerless witness), Dietrich (who negotiates safe passage)
- **Outcome**: The hall burns but the Burgundians survive by drinking blood; the battle continues

## Narrative Context

### Prelude

The battle in the hall has been raging, with the Burgundians holding their position against wave after wave of Hunnish attackers. [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]], frustrated by her warriors' inability to defeat the entrenched Burgundians, decides on a drastic measure: she will burn the hall with the Burgundians inside.

### The Event

[[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] orders fire set to the four corners of the hall (036-150). The flames spread rapidly; the wounded burn alive; the smoke threatens to suffocate the defenders. [[hagen|Hagen]], seeing his men dying of thirst, commands the Burgundians to drink the blood of the fallen (036-175). The act — horrifying to medieval sensibilities — restores their strength and saves them. When the fire subsides, the Burgundians emerge from the smoldering hall, covered in blood and ash, more fearsome than before. [[etzel|Etzel]], watching, is appalled: these are not men, he cries, but devils.

### Aftermath

The hall burning fails to destroy the Burgundians but transforms the nature of the battle. After drinking blood, the Burgundians are no longer ordinary knights — they have crossed into a realm beyond human limits. [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s escalation has not ended the battle but made it more savage. The hall itself, once the seat of feasting and hospitality, becomes a tomb.

## Thematic Analysis

### Significance to the Narrative

The hall burning marks the point at which [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s revenge transcends all bounds. She is willing to destroy her own husband's hall, kill her own warriors, and burn her own kingdom to reach her enemies. The blood-drinking episode transforms the Burgundians from heroes into something monstrous — they have passed beyond human suffering into a realm of desperation.

### Medieval Themes

Blood-drinking in medieval literature is associated with the monstrous and the demonic. The Burgundians' act — forced by necessity — turns them into figures of horror. The fire itself evokes hell imagery: the hall becomes an inferno, and the warriors within it are tormented souls. [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s willingness to burn her own home reflects the medieval understanding of revenge as a fire that consumes everything.
