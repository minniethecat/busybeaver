# Nibelung Castle

The Nibelung Castle is the legendary mountain fortress of the [[nibelungen|Nibelung people]], a subterranean stronghold where the immense treasure of the Nibelung kings was stored before [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s conquest. Located deep within the mountains of [[nibelungenland|Nibelungenland]], the castle was guarded by the dwarf [[alberich|Alberich]] and contained not only gold and gems but also the magical sword [[balmung|Balmung]] and the cloak of invisibility ([[tarnkappe|Tarnkappe]]).

## Location & Geography

- **Type**: [[horses-and-mounts|Mount]]ain fortress / subterranean stronghold
- **Ruler**: [[siegfried|Siegfried]] (after his conquest), formerly the Nibelung kings Schilbung and Nibelung
- **Real-world counterpart**: Legendary/Mythical — a symbolic underground realm associated with dwarfish mining and treasure hoarding in Germanic tradition
- **Associated characters**: [[siegfried|Siegfried]], [[alberich|Alberich]], Schilbung, Nibelung, the Nibelung warriors

### Strategic Significance

The Nibelung Castle functions as the physical source of [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s extraordinary status. Unlike the Burgundian kings, whose power rests on inherited land and titles, [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s wealth is personally won from a legendary stronghold. The castle's subterranean nature links it to chthonic Germanic myth — the treasure is not mined but found within the earth, guarded by a dwarf, suggesting a connection to the underworld. The castle's mountain setting, described as a "mountain shaft" from which the treasure was drawn (019-093), emphasizes the vertical dimension of heroic achievement: [[siegfried|Siegfried]] descends into the underworld and emerges with its wealth.

## Historical Notes

- The Nibelung mountain stronghouse draws on Germanic mythological traditions of subterranean dwarf kingdoms where treasures were forged and stored.
- In Wagner's Ring cycle, Nibelheim is explicitly an underground realm of dwarfs and smiths, ruled by [[alberich|Alberich]], expanding on the medieval epic's hints.
- The motif of a dwarf-guarded treasure mountain appears in the Völsunga saga and the Poetic Edda, where Andvari's gold is similarly kept in a cave.
