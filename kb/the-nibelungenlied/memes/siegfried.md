# Siegfried

Siegfried (Middle High German: Sivrit) is the hero of the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied's first part. The prince of [[xanten|Xanten]] who won the [[nibelung-treasure|Nibelung treasure]] and kingdom through his extraordinary strength, he journeys to [[worms|Worms]] to woo [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]].

His service to King [[gunther|Gunther]], his marriage to [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]], and his murder by [[hagen|Hagen]] form the arc around which the epic's tragedy revolves. Though dead, Siegfried dominates the second half — his unavenged murder drives [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s [[revenge]].

## Identity & Status

- **Title/Role**: Prince of [[xanten|Xanten]]; King of the Nibelungs
- **Affiliation**: Court of [[xanten|Xanten]] (birth), [[nibelungenland]] (conquest), [[burgundian-court|Burgundian Court]] (marriage and alliance)
- **Family**: [[siegmund|Siegmund]] (father), Sieglind (mother), [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] (wife)
- **First Appearance**: Adventure 02 (002-009)

## Role in the Narrative

### Part I: The Hero's Arc (Adventures 1–19)

Siegfried arrives at the Burgundian court in [[worms|Worms]], having heard of [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s beauty. After a tense initial confrontation, he proves his worth by defeating the [[saxon-invasion|Saxon invaders]] (Adventure 04).

In return for his service, [[gunther|Gunther]] grants him [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s hand — but only after Siegfried, wearing the [[tarnkappe|Tarnkappe]], helps [[gunther|Gunther]] defeat [[brunhild|Brunhild]] at [[isenstein|Isenstein]] (Adventure 07) and subdues her in the bridal chamber (Adventure 10).

Siegfried's marriage to [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] is happy. But the deception of [[brunhild|Brunhild]] festers. At the [[queens-quarrel|church door]], [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] reveals the truth: that Siegfried, not [[gunther|Gunther]], took [[brunhild|Brunhild]]'s virginity (Adventure 14). This seals Siegfried's fate.

[[hagen|Hagen]] orchestrates the murder. Pretending a second invasion, he learns from [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] the one vulnerable spot on Siegfried's body — between his shoulder blades, where a linden leaf fell as he bathed in dragon's blood. During a hunt in the [[vosges|Vosges]], [[hagen|Hagen]] spears him through this spot at a spring (Adventure 16).

> "Siegfried was called the noble warrior — in the Netherlands, the son of King Siegmund." (002-009)

> "He won there with his father's sword, which was called Balmung — a broad, splendid weapon." (003-203)

### Part II: The Absent Center (Adventures 20–39)

Though dead, Siegfried dominates the epic's second half. [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s revenge is conducted entirely in his name. [[hagen|Hagen]]'s possession of Siegfried's sword [[balmung|Balmung]] (016-157) becomes a symbol of the unavenged murder.

At [[etzels-court|Etzel's court]], [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] demands [[hagen|Hagen]] return what he took — both Siegfried's life and the Nibelung treasure. Siegfried is the absent center around which all of Part II's violence revolves, culminating in the [[fall-of-the-burgundians|catastrophe]] that destroys both kingdoms.

## Character Analysis

### Motivations

Siegfried is driven by [[ere|Êre]] (honor) and [[minne|Minne]] (courtly love). His desire for [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] — whom he has never met — motivates his journey to [[worms|Worms]].

His willingness to serve [[gunther|Gunther]] stems from both feudal obligation and the promise of [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s hand. His tragedy lies in failing to recognize that his very excellence makes him a threat the Burgundian court cannot tolerate.

### Virtues & Flaws

Siegfried embodies the heroic ideal: courage, strength, [[triuwe|loyalty]], and generosity. Yet his virtues contain their corresponding flaws.

His strength breeds arrogance; his loyalty to [[gunther|Gunther]] leads him into a fatal deception; his openness — telling [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] the secret of his vulnerability — provides [[hagen|Hagen]] the knowledge he needs. He is the epic's supreme hero and its first great victim: the two are inseparable.

## Death

Siegfried is murdered by [[hagen|Hagen]] during a hunt in the [[vosges|Vosges]] (Adventure 16). [[hagen|Hagen]] has [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] mark the vulnerable spot on Siegfried's garment.

As Siegfried bends to drink at a forest spring, [[hagen|Hagen]] drives a spear through the marked spot. His last words curse the Burgundians for their treachery. His body, brought back to [[worms|Worms]] and laid at [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s door, transforms her from grieving widow to agent of vengeance.

## Scholarly Notes

- Siegfried's invulnerability is mentioned obliquely; fuller versions appear in the Völsunga saga.
- The historical Siegfried remains debated — candidates include Arminius and Merovingian figures.
- His murder at a spring may connect to ritual sacrifice motifs in Indo-European mythology.
- His relationship with [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] bridges the epic's two halves.
