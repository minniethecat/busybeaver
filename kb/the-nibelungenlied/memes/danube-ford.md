# Danube Ford

The Danube Ford (also the ferryman's landing at Möringen) is the crossing point on the [[danube|Danube River]] where the Burgundian army enters the eastern lands in Adventure 25-26. It is the site of two of the epic's most memorable episodes: the prophecy of the water nymphs, who foretell the death of all the Burgundians, and the killing of the ferryman Else, which seals the irreversible nature of the crossing.

## Location & Geography

- **Type**: River ford and ferry landing
- **Ruler**: None — border territory within the lands of Margrave Gelfrat and his brother Else
- **Real-world counterpart**: The [[danube|Danube]] crossing in Bavaria, possibly near Möringen (Möhringen an der [[danube|Donau]]) in the vicinity of the Lech-[[danube|Danube]] confluence
- **Associated characters**: [[hagen|Hagen]], the water nymphs (Hadburg and Sieglind), the ferryman Else, Gelfrat, the Burgundian expedition

### Strategic Significance

The Danube Ford is the most symbolically charged geographical location in the epic's second part. It is where the epic's spatial threshold — the boundary between the Burgundian western world and the Hunnish east — becomes a fatal line. [[hagen|Hagen]]'s encounter with the water nymphs transforms the ford from a simple crossing point into a site of prophetic revelation. The ferryman's death and the chaplain's near-drowning confirm the prophecy: the Burgundians will not return. Every detail of the crossing episode reinforces the theme of irreversible fate.

## Key Events at This Location

- [[hagen|Hagen]] finds the water nymphs Hadburg and Sieglind bathing in a spring feeding the [[danube|Danube]] (025-126, 025-129)
- [[hagen|Hagen]] seizes the nymphs' clothing and forces them to prophesy (025-132)
- Hadburg prophesies that all the Burgundians will die at [[etzel|Etzel]]'s court except the chaplain (025-133)
- [[hagen|Hagen]] summons the ferryman Else by using the name Amelrich (025-191, 025-203)
- [[hagen|Hagen]] kills the ferryman when Else recognizes the deception (025-213, 025-225)
- The Burgundians cross the [[danube|Danube]] at Möringen and enter the eastern lands (026-021)
- [[hagen|Hagen]] throws the chaplain overboard to test the prophecy; the chaplain survives, confirming it (025-168)

## Historical Notes

- Möringen (modern Möhringen an der [[danube|Donau]]) was a known crossing point on the upper [[danube|Danube]] in the medieval period, part of the route east from the [[rhine|Rhine]] valley.
- The ferryman's fee (a gold armband or buckle offered by [[hagen|Hagen]], 025-195) was a typical medieval payment for river crossing.
- The motif of water spirits prophesying doom at a river crossing appears in multiple Germanic traditions, including the Völsunga saga.
