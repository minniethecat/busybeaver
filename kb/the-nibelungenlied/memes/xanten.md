# Xanten

Xanten is the royal seat of the [[netherlands|Netherlands]] ([[netherlands|Niederlande]]) in the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied, the birthplace and home of the hero [[siegfried|Siegfried]]. Situated on the lower [[rhine|Rhine]], it is the starting point of [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s journey into the narrative and the location of his burial after his murder. Though Xanten appears directly in only a few passages, it defines [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s identity as the "Hero of the [[netherlands|Netherlands]]."

## Location & Geography

- **Type**: City (royal residence on the Lower [[rhine|Rhine]])
- **Ruler**: King Sigemund ([[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s father), Queen Sigelind ([[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s mother)
- **Real-world counterpart**: Xanten, North [[rhine|Rhine]]-Westphalia, Germany — one of the oldest German cities, founded as a Roman colony (Colonia Ulpia Traiana) and the site of the medieval St. Victor's Cathedral
- **Associated characters**: [[siegfried|Siegfried]], Sigemund, Sigelind

### Strategic Significance

Xanten functions in the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied as the narrative mirror of [[worms|Worms]]: both are royal cities on the [[rhine|Rhine]], both are seats of powerful kings, and the marriage of [[siegfried|Siegfried]] and [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] symbolically unites the two courts. While [[worms|Worms]] is the epic's center of political action, Xanten represents [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s origin and legacy — the place he comes from and the place he returns to in death. The epic's geography treats the [[netherlands|Netherlands]] as a distinct kingdom with its own court, underlining [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s status as a foreign prince, not a Burgundian retainer.

## Key Events at This Location

- [[siegfried|Siegfried]] grows up in Xanten, where he receives his knightly training and is invested with the sword (002-001, 002-056)
- [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] leaves [[worms|Worms]] to become queen of Xanten after her marriage to [[siegfried|Siegfried]] (011-055)
- [[siegfried|Siegfried]] is laid to rest in Xanten; his father Sigemund later returns there with the Nibelung treasure (Adventure 17)

## Historical Notes

- Xanten's history dates to the Roman period: the Colonia Ulpia Traiana was founded under Emperor Trajan and was one of the most important Roman settlements in Germania Inferior.
- The medieval city grew around the canons of St. Victor, whose cathedral (the "Xantener Dom") remains a landmark today.
- The Middle High German form "Santen" (attested 011-076) reflects the local pronunciation and is the form used throughout much of the medieval textual tradition.
- Xanten's identification as [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s birthplace in the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied likely reflects its 12th-century prestige as an imperial city rather than a specific folk-memory of the legendary hero.
