# Superhuman Strength

Superhuman strength (Middle High German: starke Stärke, grôze Kraft) is a recurring motif in the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied that distinguishes the epic's central figures from ordinary warriors. [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s strength "led him into many a foreign land" (002-011); by youth he "was in his strength such that he well bore weapons" (002-033). [[brunhild|Brunhild]]'s strength is so formidable that only [[siegfried|Siegfried]] — aided by the [[tarnkappe|Tarnkappe]] — can match her. The motif of extraordinary physical power serves both to elevate the heroes and to create the conditions for the epic's central deception.

## Definition

In the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied, superhuman strength is not a supernatural gift but an extreme intensification of the heroic quality of Kraft (strength/power). [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s strength is innate — it defines him from his first introduction. [[brunhild|Brunhild]]'s strength is similarly natural, a personal attribute rather than magical enhancement. [[alberich|Alberich]] recognizes [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s "great strength" (003-216) before submitting, and [[siegfried|Siegfried]] tells [[gunther|Gunther]]: "If you win it by strength, it shall be subject to you" (003-284). Strength in the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied is the currency of heroic legitimacy.

### The Paradox of Strength

The [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied presents strength as both the supreme heroic quality and a source of vulnerability. [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s strength wins him a kingdom, a treasure, and a wife — but it also makes him a threat to the Burgundian court, ultimately contributing to his murder. [[brunhild|Brunhild]]'s strength makes her unmarriageable by normal means, requiring the deception that triggers the epic's catastrophe. Strength, the epic suggests, isolates as much as it elevates.

## Thematic Significance

### Medieval Context

Physical strength in medieval heroic literature is an external manifestation of inner worth. The hero's strength is not merely physical but moral — it proves his fitness to rule. The [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied participates in this tradition but complicates it by showing strength's destructive potential.

### In the Nibelungenlied

The epic traces strength's trajectory from heroic asset to tragic liability. [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s strength makes him invaluable to [[gunther|Gunther]] in the Saxon war and the [[brunhild|Brunhild]] expedition — but it also makes him dangerous. A hero whose strength cannot be matched cannot be safely integrated into the feudal order. The [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied suggests that transcendence of normal limits, however admirable, is incompatible with the collective security that society requires.
