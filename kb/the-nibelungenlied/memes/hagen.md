# Hagen

[[Hagen]] of [[tronje|Tronje]] is the dark heart of the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied — [[gunther|Gunther]]'s chief vassal, [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s murderer, and the man whose actions drive the epic's narrative across both parts.

He is simultaneously the most loyal and the most treacherous character in the poem: loyal to [[gunther|Gunther]] to the point of murder, treacherous to [[siegfried|Siegfried]] to the point of violating every norm of hospitality. His unwavering adherence to the warrior code of [[triuwe]] and [[ere|Êre]] transforms him from a mere antagonist into the poem's most compelling figure.

> "Das war von Tronje Hagen, und der Bruder sein — Dankwart, der schnelle" (001-033)

## Identity & Status

- **Title/Role**: Lord of [[tronje|Tronje]]; Chief Vassal of the [[burgundian-march|Burgundian]] kings
- **Affiliation**: [[burgundian-court|Burgundian Court]] — [[gunther|Gunther]]'s most trusted advisor
- **Family**: Aldrian (father); [[dankwart|Dankwart]] (brother, fellow Burgundian warrior)
- **First Appearance**: Adventure 01 (001-033) — named among the court officials

## Role in the Narrative

### Part I: The Loyal Executioner (Adventures 1–19)

Hagen's role in Part I is defined by his absolute [[triuwe|loyalty]] to the Burgundian crown — and his willingness to do what his king cannot.

When [[brunhild|Brunhild]]'s honor is wounded by [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] at the [[queens-quarrel|church door]], Hagen declares that [[siegfried|Siegfried]] must die. [[gunther|Gunther]] hesitates; Hagen acts. He tricks [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] into revealing [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s vulnerability, then murders him at a spring in the [[vosges|Vosges]] (Adventure 16).

> "Da sprach von Tronje Hagen: 'Das dünkt mich nicht gut; soll Siegfried länger leben, so wird uns nimmer wohl'" (004-049)

But Hagen's loyalty goes further. He recognizes that the [[nibelung-treasure|Nibelung treasure]] would let [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] raise an army for [[revenge]].

Hagen seizes the hoard and sinks it in the [[rhine|Rhine]] at [[lochheim|Lochheim]] (019-158, 019-161). This act — denying a widow her inheritance to protect the political order — makes [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s revenge absolute: without treasure, only blood can settle the account.

> "Vor Hagen von Tronje also barg man den Hort — sie schwuren einen Eid, dass er verborgen bliebe" (019-161)

### Part II: The Defiant Captive (Adventures 20–39)

In Part II, Hagen is the object of [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s pursuit. He goes to [[etzels-court|Etzel's court]] knowing it is a death trap.

At the [[danube-crossing|Danube]], water-sprites prophesy doom. Hagen tests the prophecy, then destroys the boat — cutting off all retreat.

> "Da sprach von Tronje Hagen: 'Frau, euch sei bekannt: wir lassen euch den Kaplan'" (008-157)

At [[etzel|Etzel]]'s court, he is the last Burgundian standing. [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] demands the treasure; Hagen refuses while any of his lords live.

After [[gunther|Gunther]] is killed, [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] beheads Hagen with [[balmung|Balmung]] — [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s sword (Adventure 39). His death ends the [[fall-of-the-burgundians|catastrophe]] before [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] herself is killed.

## Character Analysis

### Motivations

Hagen is driven by an absolutist conception of [[triuwe|Triuwe]] (feudal loyalty). His loyalty to [[gunther|Gunther]] justifies any act — murder, theft, the destruction of an entire army.

Hagen represents the dark logic of [[feudal-bond|feudal obligation]]: when loyalty to one's lord conflicts with every other moral consideration, loyalty wins. He is terrifying not because he is evil but because he is principled — and his principles permit atrocity.

### Virtues & Flaws

Hagen is courageous, intelligent, and unfailingly loyal — and each virtue becomes a flaw. His courage becomes ruthlessness; his intelligence becomes cunning; his loyalty becomes fanaticism.

His grasp of [[ere|Êre]] — the heroic honor code — is perversely complete. He understands that honor demands vengeance for any slight, and he executes that logic to its bloody end.

## Death

Hagen is the last Burgundian to die at [[etzel|Etzel]]'s court (Adventure 39) — the climax of the [[fall-of-the-burgundians|fall of the Burgundians]].

After [[gunther|Gunther]] is killed on [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s orders, Hagen defies her final demand. She draws [[balmung|Balmung]] and beheads him. Hagen dies defiant, loyal to the end — and his death precipitates [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s own.

## Scholarly Notes

- Hagen may reflect tensions between the Burgundian royal house and its warrior aristocracy.
- In Old Norse tradition, Högni is [[gunther|Gunnar]]'s brother, not vassal — heightening the [[feudal-bond|feudal]] dimension.
- Hagen's refusal to reveal the treasure has been read as vassalic independence.
- The boat destruction at the [[danube|Danube]] (Adventure 25) is a symbolically charged moment.
