# Worms

Worms is the seat of the Burgundian kings and the central setting of the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied's first part. On the left bank of the [[rhine|Rhine]], it stages the epic's key events: [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s arrival, the wooing of [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] and [[brunhild|Brunhild]], and the [[queens-quarrel|quarrel of the queens]].

The city embodies Burgundian [[ere|Êre]] and royal authority. When the entire male line departs for [[etzels-court|Etzel's court]], the empty seat foreshadows the [[fall-of-the-burgundians|dynasty's destruction]].

## Location & Geography

- **Type**: City (fortified royal seat on the [[rhine|Rhine]])
- **Ruler**: King [[gunther|Gunther]], with his brothers [[gernot|Gernot]] and [[geiselher|Geiselher]] (001-013)
- **Real-world counterpart**: Worms, [[rhine|Rhine]]land-Palatinate, Germany — one of Europe's oldest cities
- **Associated characters**: [[gunther|Gunther]], [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]], [[gernot|Gernot]], [[geiselher|Geiselher]], [[ute|Ute]], [[hagen|Hagen]], [[siegfried|Siegfried]], [[brunhild|Brunhild]]

### Strategic Significance

Worms is the political and symbolic center of Burgundian power. Its location on the [[rhine|Rhine]] makes it both a natural fortress and a hub for travel — [[siegfried|Siegfried]] arrives by river, and the expeditions to [[isenstein|Isenstein]] and later to [[etzels-court|Etzel's court]] both depart from here.

The city's fall in a narrative sense — when the entire male line of Burgundy leaves for [[etzel|Etzel]]'s court — foreshadows the [[fall-of-the-burgundians|dynasty's destruction]]. Worms, the center from which all action radiates in Part I, becomes the empty seat from which none return in Part II.

## Historical Notes

- Worms has been continuously inhabited since the Neolithic period.
- It was an important Roman city (Civitas Vangionum) founded in 14 BCE.
- The Worms Cathedral is one of the three great imperial cathedrals of the [[rhine|Rhine]].
- The historical Burgundian destruction (c. 437 CE) occurred far from Worms.
