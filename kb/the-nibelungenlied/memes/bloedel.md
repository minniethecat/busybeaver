# Bloedel

Bloedel (Middle High German: Blödel/Blödelein) is the younger brother of [[etzel|King Etzel of the Huns]]. Though a minor figure, he plays a pivotal role in the epic's final act: his attack on the Burgundian retinue and his death at [[dankwart|Dankwart]]'s hand turn [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s private revenge into an all-out war that destroys both armies.

## Identity & Status

- **Title/Role**: Prince of the Huns; Degen (warrior) in [[etzel|Etzel]]'s retinue
- **Affiliation**: [[hun-court|Hun Court]] — brother of King [[etzel|Etzel]]
- **Family**: [[etzel|Etzel]] (brother, King of the Huns)
- **First Appearance**: Adventure 22 — arrives with [[etzel|Etzel]]'s army (022-042)

## Role in the Narrative

### Part I (Adventures 1-19)

Bloedel does not appear in Part I.

### Part II: The Catalyst (Adventures 20-39)

Bloedel is first mentioned alongside [[etzel|Etzel]]'s forces welcoming the Burgundians (022-042, 022-064, 022-149). He becomes significant in Adventure 31 when [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] approaches him directly, promising him land and a noble bride ([[rudiger|Rüdiger]]'s daughter) if he attacks the Burgundian retinue (031-121, 031-238, 031-241, 031-245, 031-249, 031-257, 031-265). Bloedel agrees and attacks the Burgundian feast in Adventure 32 (032-001). He confronts [[dankwart|Dankwart]] at the tables (032-005, 032-007, 032-009, 032-013) and demands satisfaction. [[dankwart|Dankwart]] kills him with a swift sword-blow (032-025). Bloedel's death is immediately seen by his men (032-033), and the full-scale massacre erupts (032-051, 033-014).

## Character Analysis

### Motivations

Bloedel is motivated by greed and ambition. [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s promise of land and a noble marriage is sufficient to turn him against the Burgundian guests despite the sacred obligation of hospitality.

### Virtues & Flaws

Bloedel is depicted as a proud, ambitious, and gullible warrior. His willingness to break the peace of [[etzel|Etzel]]'s court for material gain marks him as morally compromised — yet his directness in attacking rather than scheming aligns with the warrior ethos.

## Notable Passages

> "Da kam der Degen Blödel mit dreitausend gar." (Then the warrior Bloedel came with three thousand men.) (022-042)

> "Damit schlug er Blödeln einen schwinden Schwertesschlag." (With that he struck Bloedel a swift sword-blow.) (032-025)

## Death

Bloedel attacks the Burgundian retinue at the banquet hall on [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s instigation. He confronts [[dankwart|Dankwart]] and the Burgundian knights, and in the ensuing fight is killed by [[dankwart|Dankwart]] with a single swift sword-blow (032-025). His death is immediately marked by his men: "They saw that their lord had been killed" (032-033). This act transforms the skirmish into a full-scale massacre.

## Scholarly Notes

- Bloedel's name (Blödel/Blödelein) may derive from a diminutive form suggesting "little brother" — fitting for [[etzel|Etzel]]'s younger sibling.
- The bribery of Bloedel by [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] (land + a bride) is a calculated inversion of feudal loyalty: [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] uses the tools of the very system that protected [[hagen|Hagen]] to undo the Burgundians.
- In some manuscript traditions, Bloedel's role varies: the C version of the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied slightly expands his characterization, while the B version (often considered the oldest) keeps his role brief.
