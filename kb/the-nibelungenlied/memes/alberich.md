# Alberich

Alberich is the dwarf ([[dwarf-folk|Gezwerg]]) who guards the Nibelung treasure in the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied. Described as "Alberich the strong" (003-224) and "Alberich the bold, a wild dwarf" (008-046), he is defeated by [[siegfried|Siegfried]] after the hero conquers the Nibelung kings. Unlike the Norse tradition where the dwarf Andvari curses the gold, Alberich becomes [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s loyal servant — "he must swear oaths to serve him as his man" (003-225) — and administers the treasure chamber thereafter.

## Definition

Alberich occupies a liminal position in the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied's taxonomy of beings. He is an individual named character with a distinct personality, yet he also functions as an archetypal figure: the dwarf guardian of subterranean wealth. His strength is formidable — "Alberich was courageous, also strong enough" (008-049) — but it is ultimately no match for [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s. His transformation from enemy to loyal servant distinguishes the German [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied from the Norse tradition where the dwarf's treasure carries a curse.

### Guardian and Servant

Alberich's narrative role is paradoxical: he is the original possessor of the [[tarnkappe|Tarnkappe]] (006-071), yet after defeat he becomes the most reliable guardian of [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s treasure. He swears oaths of fealty (003-225) and apparently keeps them — there is no mention of Alberich betraying [[siegfried|Siegfried]]. In an epic defined by broken oaths, Alberich's fidelity is a quiet counter-example.

## Thematic Significance

### Medieval Context

[[dwarf-folk|Dwarves]] ([[dwarf-folk|Gezwerg]]e) in medieval Germanic literature are ambivalent figures: associated with subterranean wealth and magical craftsmanship, they can be hostile, helpful, or both. Alberich represents the "tamed dwarf" — supernatural power brought into the service of the heroic order. His name (Alberich = "elf-ruler") connects him to the broader Germanic elf/dwarf tradition.

## Comparative Notes

- In Old Norse tradition, the dwarf Andvari (not Alberich) guards the cursed gold — Alberich's role in the German poem is more benign, lacking the curse motif entirely.
- Wagner's Ring cycle merges Alberich with the Norse Andvari, making him the original thief of the [[rhine|Rhine]]gold and the opera's central villain — a significant departure from the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied's loyal chamberlain.
