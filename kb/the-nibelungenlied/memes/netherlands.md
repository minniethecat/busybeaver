# Netherlands

The Netherlands (Middle High German: Niederlande, "Lower Lands") in the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied is the kingdom of [[siegmund|Siegmund]] and Sieglind on the lower [[rhine|Rhine]], the homeland of the hero [[siegfried|Siegfried]]. Though the epic refers to it consistently as a distinct political realm, its geography is defined entirely by the lower [[rhine|Rhine]] valley rather than by the modern national boundaries of the Netherlands.

## Location & Geography

- **Type**: Kingdom (Lower [[rhine|Rhine]] region)
- **Ruler**: King [[siegmund|Siegmund]], with Queen Sieglind
- **Real-world counterpart**: The Lower [[rhine|Rhine]] region around [[xanten|Xanten]], in modern North [[rhine|Rhine]]-Westphalia, Germany — the historical Duchy of Cleves and the adjacent Netherlandish lands
- **Associated characters**: [[siegfried|Siegfried]], [[siegmund|Siegmund]], Sieglind, [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] (as [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s wife and queen of the Netherlands)

### Strategic Significance

The Netherlands functions as [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s defining identity throughout the epic. He is consistently called "the hero of the Netherlands" or "the lord of the Netherlands," marking him as a foreign prince at the Burgundian court — a distinction that shapes both his power (he owes no feudal allegiance to [[gunther|Gunther]]) and his vulnerability (he is always an outsider). The Netherlands also serves as the narrative mirror of Burgundy: both are [[rhine|Rhine]] kingdoms with royal courts, and the marriage of [[siegfried|Siegfried]] and [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] symbolically unites them. The kingdom's distance from [[worms|Worms]] — "down by the [[rhine|Rhine]]" at the river's mouth — emphasizes the epic's geographical scope.

## Historical Notes

- The "Niederlande" in Middle High German refers literally to "lower lands" (low-lying regions) at the mouth of the [[rhine|Rhine]] — a geographic rather than political designation.
- In the 12th-13th century when the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied was composed, the Lower [[rhine|Rhine]] region was part of the Duchy of Lower Lorraine, with [[xanten|Xanten]] as a significant imperial city.
- The epic's distinction between the Netherlands and Burgundy reflects the political geography of the Hohenstaufen period, when the Lower and Upper [[rhine|Rhine]] were divided into multiple competing territories.
