# Hun Court

The Hun Court is the royal court of King [[etzel|Etzel]] ([[etzel|Attila]] the Hun), the most powerful ruler in the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied's world. Seated at [[etzels-court|Etzelnburg]] on the [[danube|Danube]], it spans a vast domain encompassing many subject peoples and vassal kingdoms.

The Hun Court becomes the stage for the epic's second part. [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] marries [[etzel|Etzel]] thirteen years after [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s murder, gaining access to the resources of the most powerful court in the epic — resources she systematically deploys to destroy her own kin, the [[burgundian-court|Burgundian Court]], in the [[fall-of-the-burgundians|catastrophic finale]].

## Overview

- **Type**: Royal Court (multi-ethnic empire)
- **Ruler**: King [[etzel|Etzel]] ([[etzel|Attila]] the Hun)
- **Seat**: [[etzels-court|Etzelnburg]] on the [[danube|Danube]]
- **Era**: Active throughout Part II (Adventures 20–39)

### Relationships

The Hun Court is the dominant power in the epic's eastern world. Its reach extends to [[bechelaren|Bechelaren]] ([[rudiger|Rüdiger]]'s march), the exiled court of [[dietrich|Dietrich von Bern]], and numerous subject territories.

The court's relationship with the Burgundians is initially one of alliance through marriage — [[etzel|Etzel]] marries [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] with the hope of peaceful union. But [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s hidden purpose transforms the Hun Court from a place of hospitality into an instrument of destruction.

## Role in the Narrative

### Major Actions

- Receives [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] as [[etzel|Etzel]]'s bride after her long period of mourning (022-007)
- Hosts the Burgundian court at a feast — orchestrated by [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] as a trap (Adventures 27–33)
- The hall battle erupts when [[bloedel|Bloedel]] attacks the Burgundian squires (033-001)
- [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] orders the hall set ablaze, consuming Burgundians and Huns alike (036-003)
- The court becomes a charnel house; [[etzel|Etzel]] survives but is powerless to stop the slaughter (039-003)

### Narrative Function

The Hun Court serves as the dark mirror of the [[burgundian-court|Burgundian Court]]. Where the Burgundian court at [[worms|Worms]] is defined by [[ere|honor]] and order, the Hun Court becomes defined by chaos and destruction — not through its own nature, but through [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s manipulation of its power.

[[etzel|Etzel]] himself is a tragic figure — the most powerful king in the world, yet unable to protect his guests, his wife, or his court from destruction. The Hun Court's fate illustrates the epic's central theme: that power without restraint leads only to annihilation.

## Historical / Legendary Notes

- [[etzel|Etzel]] is the literary representation of [[etzel|Attila]] the Hun (c. 406–453 CE), whose historical empire stretched from the [[danube|Danube]] to the Caucasus.
- The historical [[etzel|Attila]] died of a hemorrhage on his wedding night in 453 CE — the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied transforms this into [[etzel|Etzel]]'s survival as witness to catastrophe.
- The court at [[etzels-court|Etzelnburg]] blends historical memory of the Hunnic Empire with the 12th-century courtly culture familiar to the epic's audience.
- Unlike the Burgundians, the Huns survive the catastrophe — [[etzel|Etzel]] and Dietrich are among the few who remain standing at the epic's end.
