# Lochheim

Lochheim is the site on the [[rhine|Rhine]], likely a landing or riverbank settlement near [[worms|Worms]], where [[hagen|Hagen]] sinks the Nibelung treasure in the depths of the river. The act occurs in Adventure 19, after [[hagen|Hagen]] seizes the hoard from [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] and determines that it must be hidden forever to prevent her from using its wealth to raise an army against the Burgundians.

## Location & Geography

- **Type**: Riverbank landing or small settlement on the [[rhine|Rhine]]
- **Ruler**: None — within the territory of the Burgundian kings
- **Real-world counterpart**: Uncertain precise location on the upper [[rhine|Rhine]] near [[worms|Worms]]; the name may be topographically descriptive rather than denoting a known medieval town
- **Associated characters**: [[hagen|Hagen]], [[gunther|Gunther]], [[gernot|Gernot]], [[geiselher|Geiselher]], [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]

### Strategic Significance

Lochheim is the site of the epic's most consequential act of concealment. [[hagen|Hagen]]'s sinking of the Nibelung treasure transforms the hoard from an active political force — one that [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] was using to build alliances through generous gifts (019-117, 019-121) — into a permanently inaccessible object. The act sets in motion the revenge plot: without the treasure, [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s only remaining resource for justice is violence. Lochheim thus marks the point where the epic's conflict shifts from inheritance dispute to mortal revenge.

## Key Events at This Location

- [[hagen|Hagen]] convinces [[gunther|Gunther]] that [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s control of the treasure threatens Burgundian security (019-121, 019-129)
- [[hagen|Hagen]] seizes the treasure from [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] and has it transported to the [[rhine|Rhine]] (019-129, 019-158)
- [[hagen|Hagen]] sinks the entire Nibelung hoard in the [[rhine|Rhine]] at Lochheim (019-161)
- [[hagen|Hagen]] swears an oath never to reveal the treasure's location as long as any of the Burgundians live (019-158)

## Notable Descriptions

> "Hagen of Tronje had taken the whole treasure; before he sank it, they swore an oath that it should remain hidden as long as any of them lived." (019-158, 019-161)

> "Den Schatz versenkt' er in des Rheines Grund bei Lochheim in die Tiefe, dem Wasser zu guter Stund." — "The treasure he sank in the Rhine's depths at Lochheim in the deep, to the waters he consigned it." (019-161)

## Historical Notes

- The exact location of Lochheim in the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied is debated; it may be a descriptive place name rather than a reference to a known medieval settlement.
- "Loch" in German means "hole" — Lochheim may denote a "deep place" in the river suitable for sinking treasure.
- The motif of treasure sunk in rivers appears in multiple Germanic legends, possibly reflecting Migration Period practices of ritual deposition in water.
