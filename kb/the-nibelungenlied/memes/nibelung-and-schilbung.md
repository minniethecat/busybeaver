Nibelung and Schilbung are the dwarf brothers who originally possess the Nibelung treasure (Nibelungenhort) and rule Nibelungenland. They are slain by Siegfried in Adventure 3 during the hero's acquisition of the treasure and the land, establishing one of the foundational episodes of the Nibelungenlied's backstory.

## The Brothers and Their Treasure

Nibelung and Schilbung are introduced as "rich young king's sons" (003-175). They rule Nibelungenland and possess an immense treasure of red gold, which later becomes known as the Nibelungenhort. The treasure is described as so vast that it could not be carried away by a hundred wagons (003-181).

> "Der Hort König Nibelungs ward hervorgetragen" (003-181)

## Siegfried's Conquest

When Siegfried arrives in Nibelungenland, he encounters the brothers. A dispute over the division of the treasure leads to conflict — Siegfried, wielding the sword Balmung which the brothers had given him as a reward, slays them both along with their retinue of giants and dwarfs (003-174).

> "Die kühnen Nibelungen schlug des Helden Hand" (003-174)

Following their deaths, Siegfried seizes the treasure and Nibelungenland itself, forcing seven hundred warriors to swear allegiance to him (003-204, 003-208). The land and treasure remain associated with Siegfried thereafter, and news of his conquest spreads throughout Nibelungenland (008-044).

## Legacy

The treasure taken from Nibelung and Schilbung becomes a central motif in the epic. After Siegfried's death, the treasure is brought to Worms, where it eventually becomes the object of Hagen's theft and the cause of the final conflict. The brothers' brief appearance in Adventure 3 sets in motion the chain of events that culminates in the destruction of the Burgundians.
