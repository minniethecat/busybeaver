# Hagen's Oath

[[hagen|Hagen]]'s oath (Adventure 19) is the act by which the Nibelung treasure disappears from the epic's world — but remains as a mystery that drives the final confrontation. When [[hagen|Hagen]], fearing that [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s generosity with the treasure is building a power base, persuades [[gunther|Gunther]] to seize the hoard, the two men sink it in the [[rhine|Rhine]] and swear an oath never to reveal its location to anyone.

## Overview

- **Adventure(s)**: 19
- **Location**: [[worms|Worms]] and a secret location on the [[rhine|Rhine]]
- **Key Participants**: [[hagen|Hagen]] (architect of the seizure), [[gunther|Gunther]] (reluctant accomplice), [[gernot|Gernot]] (opposes the seizure), [[geiselher|Geiselher]] (opposes the seizure), [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] (victim of the theft)
- **Outcome**: The treasure sunk in the [[rhine|Rhine]]; its location known only to [[hagen|Hagen]] and [[gunther|Gunther]]

## Narrative Context

### Prelude

[[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] has brought the Nibelung treasure to [[worms|Worms]] and begun distributing it generously to knights and nobles throughout the kingdom, winning allies and influence. [[hagen|Hagen]] watches with growing alarm as [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s power expands. He warns [[gunther|Gunther]] that the treasure will make her too powerful — that she will use it to turn the Burgundian nobles against her brothers.

### The Event

[[hagen|Hagen]] approaches [[gunther|Gunther]] and persuades him that the treasure must be neutralized. On [[gunther|Gunther]]'s authority, [[hagen|Hagen]] seizes the keys to the treasure chamber. He and [[gunther|Gunther]] transport the hoard to a secret location on the [[rhine|Rhine]], where they sink it in the river (019-210). Upon their return, they swear an oath to never reveal the location. [[gernot|Gernot]] and [[geiselher|Geiselher]], who opposed the seizure, cannot undo what has been done. [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]], discovering the theft, is outraged — she has lost not only her husband but now her inheritance.

### Aftermath

The treasure's disappearance becomes the epic's longest-running mystery. When, in the final scene of the epic, [[hagen|Hagen]] is captured and [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] demands the treasure's location, [[hagen|Hagen]]'s refusal is his last act of defiance. He taunts her: as long as [[gunther|Gunther]] lives, he will say nothing. [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] has [[gunther|Gunther]] executed — but even then, [[hagen|Hagen]] refuses, and she beheads him with [[balmung|Balmung]], taking the secret to the grave. The treasure remains at the bottom of the [[rhine|Rhine]], lost forever.

## Thematic Analysis

### Significance to the Narrative

The oath creates the mystery that drives the epic's final scene. Without the hidden treasure, [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] would have no reason to keep [[hagen|Hagen]] alive, and the final confrontation — her demand, his defiance, her fury — would lose its dramatic logic. The oath also cements [[hagen|Hagen]]'s character: he is a man of absolute secrecy, capable of taking a truth to the grave.

### Medieval Themes

The oath ([[oath|Eid]]) in medieval Germanic tradition is the most sacred of bonds. [[hagen|Hagen]]'s oath of secrecy, sworn to protect the kingdom, is technically honorable — but it is also theft. The tension between the oath's form (sacred) and its content (unjust) reflects the epic's broader concern with the corruption of social bonds. The treasure's loss also resonates with the medieval topos of the lost golden age.
