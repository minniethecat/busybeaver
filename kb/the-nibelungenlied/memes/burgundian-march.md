# Burgundian March

The Burgundian March (the northern border territories of the Burgundian kingdom) is the setting of the decisive battle against [[saxon-land|Saxony and Denmark]] in Adventure 4. Though the epic does not name a specific location for the battlefield, it describes the Saxon invasion as entering the Burgundian "land" and "marches," where [[siegfried|Siegfried]] intercepts the enemy forces before they can reach [[worms|Worms]].

## Location & Geography

- **Type**: Border territory / defensive march
- **Ruler**: [[gunther|King Gunther]] of Burgundy (as overlord of the border territories)
- **Real-world counterpart**: The northern frontier of the Burgundian kingdom in the Upper [[rhine|Rhine]] region, likely the area between the Main River and the borders of Saxony
- **Associated characters**: [[siegfried|Siegfried]], [[gunther|Gunther]], [[hagen|Hagen]], [[gernot|Gernot]], [[geiselher|Geiselher]], Liudeger, Liudegast

### Strategic Significance

The Burgundian March functions as the epic's primary military frontier — the liminal space between the ordered court at [[worms|Worms]] and the hostile kingdoms beyond. Unlike the city settings ([[worms|Worms]], [[xanten|Xanten]]) or the symbolic spaces (the [[rhine|Rhine]], the forest), the march is purely a space of military action. The invasion narrative follows the classic pattern: the enemy approaches from beyond the border, the defender intercepts them in the march, and the decisive battle determines the fate of the kingdom. [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s success in defending the march proves his value to Burgundy beyond his reputation as a foreign prince.

## Key Events at This Location

- Liudeger and Liudegast lead their combined forces into the Burgundian march (004-020, 004-023)
- [[siegfried|Siegfried]] marches from [[worms|Worms]] at the head of the Burgundian army to intercept the invasion (004-119)
- The Burgundian forces meet the Saxon-Danish army in a pitched battle in the border territory (004-150, 004-167)
- [[siegfried|Siegfried]] fights with "great strength" against the Saxon champion, capturing Liudeger (004-192, 004-194)
- The Burgundian victory secures the march, and the defeated armies withdraw beyond the border (004-281, 004-303)

## Historical Notes

- The concept of a "march" (from Old High German "marka," boundary) was central to medieval defensive organization — border provinces under military lords who could respond to invasion without waiting for royal authorization.
- The Upper [[rhine|Rhine]] frontier between Burgundy and Saxony was an active defensive zone in the 10th-12th centuries, with fortified castles and watchtowers.
- The epic's depiction of a northern invasion threatening [[worms|Worms]] reflects the historical vulnerability of the Upper [[rhine|Rhine]] region to raids from Saxony and Thuringia.
