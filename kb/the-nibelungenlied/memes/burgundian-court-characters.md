## Overview

The Burgundian Court at Worms is the central political institution of the Nibelungenlied's first half. Ruled by King Gunther and his co-ruling brothers Gernot and Geiselher, with their sister Kriemhild and mother Ute, the court hosts Siegfried's bridal quest (003-050), the Saxon war (004-001), and the fateful visit of Siegfried and Kriemhild that culminates in the queens' quarrel (014-001). After Kriemhild's marriage to Etzel, the Burgundian court's journey to Hunland becomes the epic's second half, ending in the court's destruction at Etzel's hall (038-066).

## Narrative Arc

The Burgundian Court moves through four distinct phases in the poem:

1. **Splendor (Adventures 1–13)** : The prosperous court at Worms, host to festivals and diplomatic missions. Siegfried arrives seeking Kriemhild's hand (003-050).

2. **Crisis (Adventures 14–19)** : The queens' quarrel (014-001) and Siegfried's murder (016-157) fracture the court's moral order. The Nibelung treasure is brought to Worms, then sunk in the Rhine by Hagen (019-161).

3. **Decline (Adventures 20–27)** : Kriemhild departs for Hunland (022-001). The invitation to Etzel's court arrives. The Burgundians decide to journey east despite Hagen's warnings (024-001).

4. **Destruction (Adventures 28–39)** : The journey across the Danube, the stay at Bechelaren, and the final catastrophe at Etzel's hall. Every major Burgundian character dies except Hagen and Gunther, who are executed by Kriemhild (038-066, 039-001).
