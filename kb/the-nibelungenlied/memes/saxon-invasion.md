# Saxon Invasion

The Saxon invasion (Adventure 04) is the military crisis that transforms [[siegfried|Siegfried]] from a visiting prince into the proven defender of Burgundy. When Saxon and Danish armies march against [[gunther|Gunther]]'s kingdom, [[siegfried|Siegfried]] takes command of the Burgundian forces, leads a decisive counterattack, and personally captures both enemy kings — earning the gratitude of the court and, crucially, securing [[gunther|Gunther]]'s promise of [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s hand.

## Overview

- **Adventure(s)**: 04
- **Location**: Burgundy and the Saxon March
- **Key Participants**: [[siegfried|Siegfried]] (commander), [[gunther|Gunther]] (king), [[hagen|Hagen]], [[gernot|Gernot]], [[geiselher|Geiselher]] (Burgundian warriors), Liudeger (Saxon king), Liudegast (Danish king)
- **Outcome**: Decisive Burgundian victory; enemy kings captured; [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s status elevated

## Narrative Context

### Prelude

[[church-and-religion|Messe]]ngers arrive at [[worms|Worms]] announcing that Liudeger of Saxony and Liudegast of [[saxon-land|Denmark]] have raised armies against Burgundy. [[gunther|Gunther]], uncertain of his forces' strength, turns to [[siegfried|Siegfried]] for counsel. [[siegfried|Siegfried]] volunteers to lead the defense — his first opportunity to demonstrate his value beyond his reputation.

### The Event

[[siegfried|Siegfried]] leads the Burgundian army into battle. The fighting is fierce but decisive. [[siegfried|Siegfried]] personally engages Liudegast, wounding and capturing him. He then pursues Liudeger, overcoming the Saxon king after a desperate struggle. The capture of both enemy kings ends the war. [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s performance is peerless — he fights "with great strength" (004-194) and proves himself the warrior his reputation promised.

### Aftermath

The victory transforms [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s position at the Burgundian court. He is no longer a foreign prince of uncertain worth but the proven savior of the kingdom. [[gunther|Gunther]], in gratitude, grants [[siegfried|Siegfried]] the right to see [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] — the first step toward their marriage. The Saxon invasion thus serves as the narrative mechanism by which [[siegfried|Siegfried]] earns his place in Burgundian society through martial service.

## Thematic Analysis

### Significance to the Narrative

The Saxon invasion establishes the feudal contract between [[siegfried|Siegfried]] and [[gunther|Gunther]]: martial service in exchange for [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s hand. This contract will later be violated by [[gunther|Gunther]]'s complicity in [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s murder, making the invasion a crucial reference point for the epic's exploration of broken obligations.

### Medieval Themes

The invasion embodies the heroic code in its most straightforward form: military threat, heroic response, decisive victory. It is the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied's clearest depiction of warfare as a theater of honor — before the epic darkens into treachery and revenge.
