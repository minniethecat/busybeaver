Irnfried is a hero from Thuringia who appears in the later adventures of the Nibelungenlied. He arrives at Etzel's court for Kriemhild's wedding and later fights alongside the Burgundians in the catastrophic final battle, where he meets his death.

## Arrival at Etzel's Court

Irnfried is introduced among the guests at Etzel's wedding feast, described as "a handsome man from Thuringia" (022-039). He is received with honor and joins the assembly of heroes gathered at the Hunnish court.

> "Von Thüringen Irnfried, ein waidlicher Mann" (022-039)

## The Final Battle

During the great battle at Etzel's hall, Irnfried fights with distinction. He engages Gernot, one of the Burgundian kings, in single combat (029-187). He rides into the buhurt alongside Hawart, the Danish hero (031-117), and enters the main battle with his Thuringian warriors (035-014).

> "Irnfried nahm Gernoten, diesen kühnen Degen" (029-187)

## Death

Irnfried and Hawart fight fiercely side by side, but both are ultimately overcome. Irnfried is slain by the minstrel Volker in the climactic fighting (035-169, 035-173). His death is part of the cascading slaughter that consumes the heroes gathered at Etzel's court.

> "Irnfried der kühne lief den Spielmann an" (035-173)
