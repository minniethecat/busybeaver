# Nibelungen

A mythical kingdom and warrior people whose treasure (the [[nibelung-treasure|Nibelungenhort]]) and lands were conquered by [[siegfried|Siegfried]]. The Nibelungen appear throughout the epic as [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s vassals and warriors, forming a thousand-strong retinue that accompanies him to [[worms|Worms]].

## Overview

- **Type**: Mythical Kingdom / Warrior People
- **Rulers**: Kings Schilbung and Nibelung (original); [[siegfried|Siegfried]] (after conquest) (003-175, 003-204)
- **Seat**: [[nibelung-castle|Nibelung Castle]] (die Veste) in [[nibelungenland|Nibelungenland]] (008-012, 012-062)
- **Era**: Prior to and during the events of the Nibelungenlied
- **Key Possession**: The [[nibelung-treasure|Nibelungenhort]] — a vast treasure of red gold, guarded by the dwarf [[alberich|Alberich]] (003-195, 008-106)

### Relationships

The Nibelungen, once an independent kingdom, became subjugated to [[siegfried|Siegfried]] after he defeated their kings. Following the conquest, a thousand Nibelungen warriors served as [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s personal retinue (008-079, 010-076). After [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s marriage to [[Kriemhild]], the Nibelungen treasure was transported to [[worms|Worms]], where it became a source of conflict between [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] and the [[burgundian-court]], ultimately leading to its sinking in the [[rhine|Rhine]] (019-076).

## Role in the Narrative

### Major Actions

- The Nibelungen kings Schilbung and Nibelung sought [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s help to divide their inheritance, offering him the sword [[Balmung]] as payment (003-189)
- [[siegfried|Siegfried]] slew the two kings, seven hundred Nibelungen knights, and twelve giants, then subdued [[alberich|Alberich]] and claimed the [[nibelung-treasure|Nibelungenhort]] and [[nibelungenland|Nibelungenland]] (003-174, 003-204, 003-208)
- A thousand Nibelungen warriors accompanied [[siegfried|Siegfried]] to [[worms|Worms]] for his wedding to [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]] (010-076, 010-160)
- After [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s death, [[alberich|Alberich]] and the Nibelungen were called upon to transport the treasure to [[worms|Worms]] (019-076)

### Narrative Function

The Nibelungen serve as the source of [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s wealth and military power, establishing his status before he arrives at the Burgundian court. Their treasure — the [[nibelung-treasure|Nibelungenhort]] — becomes the central catalyst for the epic's second half: [[kriemhild|Kriemhild]]'s use of the treasure to recruit allies, [[hagen|Hagen]]'s decision to sink it in the [[rhine|Rhine]], and the eventual fatal conflict. The Nibelungen thus function as both a foundation of [[siegfried|Siegfried]]'s heroic identity and the origin of the material wealth that drives the tragedy forward.

## Historical / Legendary Notes

The Nibelungen originate in Germanic legendary tradition, possibly connected to the historical Burgundian kingdom on the [[rhine|Rhine]] and the Nibelung dynasty of the Frankish empire. In the Norse *Völsunga saga*, the equivalent figures are the Niflungar. The name "Nibelungen" may derive from Old High German *nibul* ("mist" or "darkness"), linking them to the mythical underworld (*Niflheim*). In the *Nibelungenlied*, the Nibelungen are presented as a historical people with a tangible kingdom, rather than purely mythical dwarfs — though the presence of [[alberich|Alberich]] and the giants retains the supernatural elements of the older legend.
