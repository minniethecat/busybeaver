# Volker

Volker von Alzey is one of the most distinctive figures in the [[nibelungen|Nibelungen]]lied — a noble knight who is also a master minstrel (Spielmann). He is [[hagen|Hagen]]'s closest companion among the Burgundian warriors, and his fiddle-playing before the final battle becomes the epic's most haunting image of defiance in the face of certain death.

## Identity & Status

- **Title/Role**: Knight of Alzey; known as the Minstrel (Spielmann, Fiedelmann)
- **Affiliation**: [[burgundian-court|Burgundian Court]] — vassal of [[gunther|Gunther]], [[gernot|Gernot]], and [[geiselher|Geiselher]]
- **Family**: Unnamed in the epic; origin in Alzey ([[rhine|Rhine]]land-Palatinate)
- **First Appearance**: Adventure 01 — listed among the Burgundian court (001-036)

## Role in the Narrative

### Part I: The Minor Knight (Adventures 1-19)

Volker is introduced among the Burgundian retinue at [[worms|Worms]] (001-036, 004-095, 004-134, 004-249). He fights in the Saxon war, bearing the Burgundian banner, and is identified as a bold (kühn) warrior alongside [[dankwart|Dankwart]]. His minstrel identity is not emphasized in Part I.

### Part II: The Fiddler of Doom (Adventures 20-39)

Volker rises to prominence in Part II. When the Burgundians travel to [[etzel|Etzel]]'s court, Volker serves as their quartermaster (Marschall) (020-588). At [[etzel|Etzel]]'s hall, his defiance of the Huns is constant (024-237, 024-276, 024-340). His most famous moment comes in Adventure 27-28, where he plays his fiddle before the battle — not for joy but as a war-cry. He stands guard with [[hagen|Hagen]] against the Huns, and in the final battle he fights alongside [[hagen|Hagen]] with ferocious skill (027-032, 027-096, 027-221, 028-077). Volker dies alongside the other Burgundian warriors in the hall.

## Character Analysis

### Motivations

Volker is driven by [[triuwe|Triuwe]] (loyalty) to his lords and comrades, particularly his bond with [[hagen|Hagen]]. His minstrel's art serves the warrior's ethos — his fiddle becomes a weapon of psychological warfare, taunting the Huns and rallying the Burgundians.

### Virtues & Flaws

Volker embodies the fusion of courtly refinement and martial prowess. His music is a symbol of civilization that becomes an instrument of destruction. His flaw, if any, is a joyful embrace of violence that makes him complicit in the epic's slaughter.

## Notable Passages

> "Da kam der kühne Volker, ein edler Spielmann." (Then the bold Volker came, a noble minstrel.) (024-237)

> "Die sollt ihr führen, Volker, mit euch in der Heunen Land." (You shall lead them, Volker, with you into the land of the Huns.) (027-228)

## Death

Volker is killed in the final battle at [[etzel|Etzel]]'s court, fighting alongside [[hagen|Hagen]]. His death is not individually described in the epic's concluding massacre — he falls among the last Burgundian defenders, loyal to the end.

## Scholarly Notes

- Volker's dual identity as knight and minstrel is unique in medieval German epic. He has no clear parallel in the Old Norse tradition (where [[gunther|Gunnar]]'s harp-playing in the snake-pit is a different motif).
- The name may be historical: Alzey was a real town in [[rhine|Rhine]]land-Palatinate.
- Volker and [[hagen|Hagen]] as an inseparable pair anticipate the "warrior comrade" trope later developed in Germanic literature.
