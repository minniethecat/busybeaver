# The Common-Room Dinner

During the investigation into Derek's disappearance, Nigel Burtell attended a dinner at the High Table of Simon Magus College —
a scene of "A Common-Room Dinner" that revealed the strain the investigation was placing on him and the impressions of those who had known both cousins at Oxford.

## Summary

The dinner at Simon Magus brought Nigel back to the college where both he and Derek had been undergraduates. By this time, Derek was missing and Nigel was under suspicion.
The dons who had known them both — who had seen Derek's "lumpish unimaginativeness" and Nigel's "unendurable affectations" (001-004) — now watched Nigel with new eyes.
The dinner conversation, with its undercurrents of suspicion and its surface of academic politeness, became a scene of psychological tension.

## Timeline

- **Before dinner**: Nigel returns to Oxford, ostensibly for normal business
- **During dinner**: Conversation touches on Derek, the investigation, the river
- **After dinner**: The dons' private discussion of Nigel's character and the likelihood of his guilt

## Participants

| Character | Role | Significance |

## Location

The dinner took place in the Senior Common Room or Hall of Simon Magus College, Oxford — the same college where "men cared little what their neighbours did, short of the bagpipes" (001-008).
The college setting is rich with memory: the walls where Nigel hung his blank cartridge paper, the rooms where he offered absinthe, the quads where he was mistaken for his cousin.

## Evidence & Clues

- **Nigel's composure**: His behavior under the scrutiny of men who knew both cousins intimately
- **The dons' recollections**: Their memories of Derek and Nigel as undergraduates — the Centaur and the aesthete
- **Unguarded remarks**: What Nigel said, or failed to say, about his missing cousin
- **The college grapevine**: How news of the investigation was received in Oxford

## Impact on the Investigation

The dinner scene illustrates the social dimension of the investigation. Oxford was the world that had shaped both cousins.
The dons who had tolerated Nigel's affectations now wondered whether they had tolerated something darker.
The dinner also establishes Nigel's state of mind during the investigation — his characteristic performance under pressure, his inability to drop the pose even when his freedom was at stake.
