# Millington Bridge

A location on the upper Thames above Shipcote, significant primarily for its inn, where Derek Burtell stayed on the night before the fatal canoe expedition.
The timeline of Derek's movements through Millington Bridge is central to reconstructing the events leading to his disappearance.

## Location

Millington Bridge is situated on the Thames above Shipcote Lock, at a point where there is both a bridge crossing and an inn [009-012].
The inn is described as an establishment where travelers can stay overnight, making it a natural stopping point on a journey down the river.

## Derek's Stay

Derek Burtell slept at the Millington Bridge inn on the Sunday night before the canoe incident [011-015]. This fact is established through inquiries made by Bredon and Leyland during their investigation.
The landlady at Millington Bridge provided key information: "the landlady at Millington Bridge told her that they didn't come..." [011-019], suggesting a discrepancy in the account of Derek's movements.

The stay at Millington Bridge is significant because:
- It places Derek at a specific location on the night before the incident
- It helps establish the timeline of his journey down the river
- The testimony of the landlady becomes part of the investigative record

## Role in the Investigation

Millington Bridge forms part of the chain of locations along the Thames that Bredon and Inspector Leyland must reconstruct to understand Derek's final movements.
The impressions left at the inn contribute to the broader picture of Derek's state of mind and intentions before setting out in the canoe.

## Quotations

> That inn above Shipcote, that at Millington Bridge. Everywhere the impressions left were of a man in no particular hurry. [009-012]

> I understand that Derek Burtell slept at Millington Bridge last Sunday night, the night before the canoe incident. [011-015]
