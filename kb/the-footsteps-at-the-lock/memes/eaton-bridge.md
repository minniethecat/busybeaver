# Eaton Bridge

A bridge on the upper Thames, the designated meeting point for the cousins on the day of Derek's disappearance. "How long will it take you to get down to Eaton Bridge?
A couple of hours?" Nigel called back to Derek from the lock bank (002-009). It was the last time anyone heard Nigel speak to Derek alive.

## Location

Eaton Bridge lies approximately three miles downstream from Shipcote Lock, "close to a disused boat-house" (003-009).
It stands on the stretch of the Thames between Oxford and Lechlade where the river flows through isolation — "the villages stand to one side and let it pass" (002-002).
The bridge marks the limit of the lock channel: below the lock, the canalised channel runs straight for nearly a mile before rejoining the weir-stream after "unnecessary windings" (002-005).

The Gudgeon Inn sits near Eaton Bridge (004-041), providing the only accommodation for river travellers in the vicinity. The towpath runs along the bank, accessible from the bridge.

## Description

Eaton Bridge is one of the "rare, purposeless iron bridges" that punctuate the upper Thames (002-002).
It crosses the navigation channel at a point where the river emerges from the straight lock-cut into its natural meandering course.
The bridge is a simple iron structure, typical of the Thames Conservancy's lock-and-bridge system.

## Role in the Story

Eaton Bridge is where Nigel Burtell was supposed to meet Derek after taking his viva voce examination at Oxford.
The plan was for Nigel to travel by train to Oxford, sit the examination, then take a taxi out to the bridge — arriving, he estimated, by about eleven o'clock (002-010). "Oh yes, quite a decent sort of pub, it looked.
Wait for me there if you like," he told Derek (002-011).

The bridge becomes critical to the investigation because:
1. It is the next downstream landmark after Shipcote Lock — the stretch of river between them is where the crime occurred
2. The abandoned canoe was discovered floating near here on July 4 (003-008)
3. Bredon and Angela stay at the inn by Eaton Bridge during their investigation (004-041)
4. The "familiar distance between Shipcote and Eaton Bridge had lengthened" on the fateful night of the return journey (021-018), suggesting the tension and danger of the passage

## Significance to the Mystery

Eaton Bridge is the terminus of the critical stretch of river. Everything that happened to Derek happened between Shipcote Lock and Eaton Bridge.
The bridge was the intended rendezvous that never took place — Nigel never arrived, claiming his examination detained him.
The bridge is also where Bredon bases his investigation, using the inn as his headquarters while he reconstructs the events on the river.
