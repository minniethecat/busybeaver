# The Indescribable

"Human ingenuity has still failed to imagine any form or any degree of danger which the Indescribable are not prepared (for a consideration) to underwrite" (001-013).
The insurance company that insured Derek Burtell's life — and, when he disappeared, dispatched Miles Bredon to find out why.

## Overview

The Indescribable is an insurance agency of vast scope and unconventional methods. Unlike ordinary insurers, they will underwrite any risk for the right premium.
A millionaire flying to Nova Zèmbla paid "a shilling per second by way of insurance money" (001-013).
Derek Burtell, an alcoholic with a failing heart, was "not legitimate business" — but the Indescribable backed him anyway, "for a very reasonable premium" (001-013).

The company maintains its own doctor — "a matter of honour, and indeed of income" — who refuses all other practice. When a claim is suspicious, they do not simply refuse payment; they investigate.
And for investigations, they employ men like Miles Bredon.

## Role in the Story

The Indescribable is the engine of the plot. Without their insurance of Derek's life, there would be no investigation — the police had no body, no evidence, and "were in a difficulty" (010-002).
It is the Indescribable that sends Bredon to the upper Thames, that pays for his inn at Eaton Bridge, that gives him the mandate to solve a crime the official investigation could not touch.

The company also embodies the story's moral ambiguity. They insured a dying man for profit, knowing he was unlikely to survive.
When he disappeared, they sent an investigator not to seek justice but to protect their financial interests. And yet that investigation — selfish in motive — became the instrument of justice.

## Significance

The Indescribable represents the commercialization of risk — a company that profits from the boundary between life and death.
In a novel structured around the question of whether Derek is alive or dead, the Indescribable is the institution that has placed a financial value on that question.
They are not good, but their self-interest drives the pursuit of truth.
