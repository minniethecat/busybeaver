# The Discovery of the Canoe

The moment when Derek's abandoned canoe was found at Eaton Bridge, transforming a possible accident into a confirmed mystery (003-009).

## Summary

After the canoe drifted through Shipcote Lock, Nigel ran along the towpath but lost sight of it.
The following day, the canoe was discovered at Eaton Bridge, three miles downstream, "close to a disused boat-house" (003-009). It was intact, showing "no mark of any collision" (002-005). Derek was gone.

## Timeline

- Canoe disappears through Shipcote Lock gates (002-008)
- Nigel searches downstream; finds nothing (003-007)
- Next day: canoe discovered at Eaton Bridge (003-009)
- Police notified; river search begins (003-010)
- No body found after extensive dragging

## Evidence

The canoe's condition was the first piece of physical evidence: no damage, no sign of struggle, no personal effects left behind.
This suggested either a clean accident (Derek fell silently into the water) or a deliberate abandonment (he left the canoe intentionally).

## Impact

The discovery transformed the case from a search-and-rescue to a possible crime investigation.
With the canoe found but no body, the police faced the corpus delicti problem: no corpse meant no certainty about whether a crime had occurred.
