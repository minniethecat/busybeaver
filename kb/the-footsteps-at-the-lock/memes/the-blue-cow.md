# The Blue Cow

An inn situated on the bank of the upper Thames, above Millington Bridge.
It becomes a point of inquiry during Bredon's investigation into Derek Burtell's disappearance, as its position on the river makes it a potential source of observations about river traffic.

## Location

The Blue Cow is located on the side of the river above Millington Bridge, "just close to the Blue Cow" on the riverside [017-007].
There is no regular ferry service at this point on the river [017-008], making it a relatively isolated establishment accessible primarily by the river or country roads.

## Role in the Investigation

Bredon visits the Blue Cow during his inquiries, seeking information about any unusual activity on the river around the time of the disappearance.
The inn's position on the upper Thames makes it a natural place to ask whether anyone was seen on the river — particularly anyone heading downstream toward Shipcote Lock.

The visit to the Blue Cow is part of Bredon's methodical canvassing of riverside establishments, each of which might have observed something relevant to the timeline.
The absence of a regular ferry at this point [017-008] means river traffic past the inn would be notable to those present.
