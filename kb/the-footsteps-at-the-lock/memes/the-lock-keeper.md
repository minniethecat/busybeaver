## Character

The lock-keeper is a figure of the river — a man whose life is governed by the rhythms of water and boats, whose knowledge is local and practical rather than abstract.
His [[the-oath-and-testimony|testimony]] reflects this: it is concrete, sensory, based on what he saw and heard.
But [[miles-bredon|Bredon]]'s questioning reveals that what [[the-shipcote-lock-keeper|the lock-keeper]] saw may have been what someone wanted him to see (004-043).

## Significance

The lock-keeper represents the ordinary [[the-oath-and-testimony|witness]] — the person who happens to be present when something significant occurs.
His [[the-oath-and-testimony|testimony]] is valuable but fallible, precise about some details and vague about others.
