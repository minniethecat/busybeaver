# Uncle Robert

A relative of the Burtell-[[miles-bredon|Bredon]] family and a member of the Salisbury College Common-room at [[oxford|Oxford]], whose unannounced appearances are a source of mild exasperation to his relations.

## Role

Uncle Robert is a minor family figure who appears in the [[oxford|Oxford]] episodes of the investigation.
He is a fellow or associate of Salisbury College and belongs to its Common-room, where he is regarded as a "supernumerary member" and "a bore at that" [008-022].
His role in the narrative is to complicate [[miles-bredon|Bredon]]'s movements during the [[oxford|Oxford]] phase of the inquiry by imposing an unwanted social obligation.

## Relationships

- - **[[miles-bredon|Miles Bredon]]**: [[miles-bredon|Bredon]] is waylaid by Uncle Robert on the street in [[oxford|Oxford]] and coerced into dining with him at the Common-room,
- a distraction from [[miles-bredon|Bredon]]'s investigative errand [008-001].
- **[[angela-bredon|Angela Bredon]]**: [[angela-bredon|Angela]] cites Uncle Robert as the family exemplar of turning up when not wanted, suggesting a shared family exasperation with his intrusions [009-027].
- **[[nigel-burtell|Nigel Burtell]]**: The family connection extends to [[nigel-burtell|Nigel]]; [[angela-bredon|Angela]]'s remark about Uncle Robert arises in conversation about the Burtell family's habits.

## Key Appearances

- **Chapter 8: The Chance Encounter**: While [[miles-bredon|Bredon]] is delayed in traffic at Carfax, Uncle Robert hails him from the pavement and insists on his company at dinner in the Common-room that evening.
-   [[miles-bredon|Bredon]] accepts under duress, compelled to send a warning telegram to [[angela-bredon|Angela]] [008-001].
- **Chapter 8: [[the-common-room-dinner|The Common-Room Dinner]]**: [[miles-bredon|Bredon]] endures a tense formal dinner at Salisbury College.
-   Uncle Robert is described as a peripheral figure in [[simon-magus-college-org|the college]] society whose guests are met with silent stares from the other members [008-022].
- - **Chapter 9: A Family Trait**: [[angela-bredon|Angela]], discussing the Burtell family's tendency to appear uninvited, points to Uncle Robert as the definitive example: "In *our* family,
- we're always appearing when we're not wanted to, [[the-oath-and-testimony|witness]] Uncle Robert" [009-027].
-   She later wonders whether Uncle Robert might have inadvertently revealed the [[miles-bredon|Bredon]]s' whereabouts to [[inspector-leyland|Inspector Leyland]] [009-029].

## Character Traits

Uncle Robert is characterized by his untimely social intrusions and his indifference to the inconvenience he causes.
The narrative presents him as a figure of mild comic irritation — the universal uncle whose presence in a university [[london-place|town]] becomes a social hazard for visiting relations.
His status in the Common-room is marginal; the other fellows tolerate rather than welcome him.

## Significance to the Mystery

Uncle Robert's contribution to the plot is circumstantial.
His detention of [[miles-bredon|Bredon]] in [[oxford|Oxford]] delays the investigation slightly,
and [[angela-bredon|Angela]]'s later speculation that he may have inadvertently disclosed their location to [[inspector-leyland|Leyland]] introduces a small element of uncertainty about how the detective located the [[miles-bredon|Bredon]]s.
His primary function, however, is to add texture to the [[oxford|Oxford]] episode and to illustrate the kind of family network within which the main characters move.
