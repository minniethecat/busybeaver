# The Oxford Examination

Nigel Burtell's viva voce examination at Oxford became the linchpin of his alibi.
He claimed that on the morning of July 3, he left the canoe at Shipcote Lock, caught the 9:14 train to Oxford, and sat his examination — arriving just in time to meet the examiners (002-010).
But Bredon's investigation revealed that the examination timing could not account for all of Nigel's movements.

## Summary

"If the examiners take me early, and don't show an indecent curiosity about the extent of my knowledge, I ought to be clear by eleven" (002-010). So Nigel told Derek before leaving the lock.
According to his account, he would take the train, sit the examination, then take a taxi to Eaton Bridge to rejoin his cousin.

The examination was real enough — Nigel did sit a viva voce that morning. But the timing was wrong.
Between the 9:14 train, the examination, and the taxi to Eaton Bridge, there were gaps in Nigel's timeline that the examination could not fill.
The examiners' testimony about when Nigel arrived and when he left became critical to breaking the alibi.

## Timeline

- **~8:55 AM**: Nigel leaves Shipcote Lock, walks to Shipcote Station (002-013)
- **9:14 AM**: Train departs Shipcote for Oxford (002-018)
- **~9:45 AM**: Nigel arrives at Oxford station (002-022)
- **Morning**: Nigel sits his viva voce examination
- **~11:00 AM**: Nigel claims to have finished and taken a taxi to Eaton Bridge (002-010)
- **But**: The time between the examination's end and Nigel's confirmed arrival at Oxford station was unaccounted for — and the examiners' memories of his exact arrival time were vague

## Participants

| Character | Role | Significance |

| Miles Bredon | Investigator | Tested the alibi against railway timetables and witness accounts |

## Location

The examination took place at Oxford University — likely in the Examination Schools on the High Street.
The location is significant because of the distance from the railway station and the time required to travel between the two.

## Evidence & Clues

- **The 9:14 train**: Confirmed by Shipcote Station staff and Nigel's ticket (009-036)
- **The examination timing**: When did the viva actually begin? When did it end?
- **The taxi**: Did Nigel take a taxi to Eaton Bridge, or did he use the time for another purpose?
- **The Bradshaw cipher**: Nigel's annotated railway timetable was a coded message — the cipher correspondence (003-001)

## Impact on the Investigation

The examination alibi was the wall Nigel built around himself. If the wall held, he was innocent — he was in Oxford, sitting an exam, while Derek drifted to his death. Bredon broke the wall by showing that:
1. The examination timing left gaps in Nigel's account
2. Those gaps were sufficient for Nigel to have returned to the river
3. The Bradshaw cipher was a coded communication about the crime, not a harmless puzzle
