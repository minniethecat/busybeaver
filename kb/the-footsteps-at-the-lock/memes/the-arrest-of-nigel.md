# The Arrest of Nigel Burtell

The formal detention of Nigel Burtell on charges related to the staged disappearance of his cousin Derek. The arrest marks the transition from investigation to legal process.

## Summary

After Bredon broke the Bradshaw cipher and accumulated sufficient evidence, Inspector Leyland arrested Nigel on suspicion of conspiracy to defraud the Indescribable.
The arrest was based on the deciphered messages, the manipulated camera evidence, and the implausibility of Nigel's alibi.

## Evidence Leading to Arrest

- The Bradshaw cipher proved pre-arranged communication (016-010)
- The camera film showed deliberate manipulation (007-044)
- Nigel's "perfect alibi" was shown to be too perfect (006-013)
- Financial records traced post-disappearance payments

## Impact

The arrest crystallized the case against Nigel but did not end it. The legal process required more than Bredon's deductions — it required evidence admissible in court.
The novel's final chapters explore the gap between detection and prosecution.
