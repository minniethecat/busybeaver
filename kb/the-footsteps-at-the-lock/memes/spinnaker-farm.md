# Spinnaker Farm

A farm on the field path between Shipcote Lock and Shipcote Station: "You'll see Spinnaker's Farm across on the left, but don't you take no notice of that, you keep straight on" (002-016).
It is a landmark on the route Nigel Burtell took on the morning Derek disappeared.

## Location

Spinnaker Farm lies on the left-hand side of the field path that runs from Shipcote Lock to Shipcote Station.
The path crosses the weir bridge, then keeps "straight on across the field, with the hedge on your left" (002-016). The farm is visible from the path but the walker is instructed not to turn toward it.
It is "maybe a quarter of an hour's walk" from the lock to the station across the fields (002-016).

## Description

Spinnaker Farm is a working farm of the upper Thames valley, set among the hayfields and hedgerows.
The farmhouse and outbuildings stand "across on the left" as one walks the field path from the lock toward Shipcote Station (002-016).
It is one of the isolated habitations in a landscape where "the villages stand to one side and let [the river] pass" (002-002).

The farm is substantial enough to be a local reference point — the lock-keeper uses it as a landmark when giving directions.

## Role in the Story

The farm appears in the lock-keeper's directions to Nigel: "You want to cross the bridge, see, over the weir yonder, and keep straight on across the field, with the hedge on your left.
You'll see Spinnaker's Farm across on the left, but don't you take no notice of that" (002-016).

Later, during Bredon's investigation, the farm takes on greater significance. The path past Spinnaker Farm is the route Nigel would have taken — or claimed to have taken — from the lock to the station.
Bredon must establish whether Nigel actually walked this path or took some other route, possibly returning to the river unseen.

Bredon has "a word with Spinnaker Farm" during his investigation (005-037). The inhabitants may have seen something — or someone — on the morning of July 3.
The farm also appears in the account of the man seen "going through Spinnaker Farm" (005-035), potentially a witness or a suspect.

## Significance to the Mystery

The path past Spinnaker Farm is the critical connection between the river and the railway. If Nigel took this path as he claimed, his timeline holds.
If he deviated — returning to the river instead — the entire alibi collapses.
The farm's inhabitants are potential witnesses to Nigel's movements, and Bredon's interview with them is a key step in verifying or disproving the alibi.
