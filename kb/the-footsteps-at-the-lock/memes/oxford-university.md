# Oxford University

"Oxford is a broad stream, in which the varied regatta of life can be managed without jostling" (001-009).
The University is the world that shaped Derek and Nigel Burtell — and the institution whose examination halls provided Nigel's alibi.

## Overview

The University of Oxford, "the older University" (001-008), is the backdrop against which the cousins' estrangement develops.
Both matriculated at Simon Magus College — Derek first, spending two years there as a "normal creature, though morose in disposition" (001-004); Nigel following, transforming his rooms with mauve walls and absinthe.

The University "tolerates everything" (001-008), and at Simon Magus "men cared little what their neighbours did,
short of the bagpipes." This tolerance allowed Nigel to flourish as an aesthete and Derek to drift as a centaur — neither challenged, neither reformed.

## Role in the Story

Oxford serves three functions in the narrative:
1. **Character formation**: It is where the cousins' personalities crystallize and their mutual resentment festers
2. **Alibi**: Nigel's viva voce examination provides his cover for the morning of July 3
3. **Social world**: The dons, the scouts, the undergraduate circles — Oxford's microcosm reflects the larger world's indifference to the cousins' fate

## Significance

Oxford is the institution of tradition and tolerance that produces, without judgment, both a Derek and a Nigel.
The University does not make them what they are — but it provides the stage on which their characters are revealed. And it is an Oxford examination that becomes, paradoxically, the key to a murder investigation.
