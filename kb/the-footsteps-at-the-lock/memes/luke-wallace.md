# Luke Wallace

A mysterious stranger who appeared at [[shipcote-lock|Shipcote Lock]] under the assumed name "Luke Wallace," providing a Coventry address (014-004).
His sudden appearance, his punt tied at [[shipcote-lock|the lock]],
and his subsequent involvement in [[the-canoe-expedition|the canoe]] expedition with [[derek-burtell|Derek Burtell]] make him a central figure in the investigation of [[the-disappearance|Derek's disappearance]] (014-027).

## Role

Luke Wallace serves as one of the most enigmatic figures in the investigation — a man whose very identity is a fabrication.
He appears at [[shipcote-lock|the lock]], joins [[derek-burtell|Derek Burtell]] in [[the-canoe-expedition|the canoe]], and then vanishes after [[the-canoe-expedition|the canoe]] [[accident-or-design|accident]].
His role oscillates between possible accomplice, potential murderer, and unwitting participant in events beyond his control (014-033).
[[miles-bredon|Miles Bredon]] later identifies him as operating under "an assumed name, that of Mr. Luke Wallace, to be exact" (022-006).

## Relationships

- **[[derek-burtell|Derek Burtell]]**: Wallace joins [[derek-burtell|Derek]]'s canoe expedition at [[shipcote-lock|Shipcote Lock]] on the night of [[the-disappearance|Derek's disappearance]].
-   The two were seen together on the river in the hours before [[the-canoe-expedition|the canoe]] [[accident-or-design|accident]] (014-033).
- **[[angela-bredon|Angela Bredon]]**: [[angela-bredon|Angela]] has a strong negative reaction to the name "Luke Wallace," exclaiming "I don't want Luke Wallace here one little bit. He spoils things" (023-055).
- **[[miles-bredon|Miles Bredon]]**: [[miles-bredon|Bredon]] investigates Wallace's movements, including his visit to Witney, and pursues "inquiry into the whereabouts of Mr. Wallace" (024-012, 024-036).

## Key Appearances

- **Arrival at the Lock**: Wallace gives his name as Luke Wallace and an address in Coventry, approximately a fortnight before [[the-canoe-expedition|the canoe]] [[accident-or-design|accident]] (014-004).
- **[[the-canoe-expedition|The Canoe Expedition]]**: Wallace joins [[derek-burtell|Derek]] in [[the-canoe-expedition|the canoe]], having tied his punt at [[shipcote-lock|the lock]].
-   The two paddle together in what becomes [[derek-burtell|Derek]]'s final journey (014-027, 014-033).
- **The Letter-Rack Discovery**: The words "L. Wallace, 41 Digby Road, Coventry" are discovered on a letter-rack, revealing the address Wallace had given (023-053).
- **The Witney Visit**: [[miles-bredon|Bredon]] investigates "Mr. Luke Wallace's visit to Witney" as a key lead in the case (024-012).
- **The Package**: The name "Luke Wallace" is seen on a package, providing another piece of the puzzle (024-024).

## Character Traits

Wallace is described as someone who "had attracted attention" but of whom few could "remember much" (023-058).
He is skilled at misdirection — the address he gave proved to be a dead end, and "no incidental picture survived of 'Mr. Wallace'" (016-003).
His ability to appear and disappear without leaving traces suggests either professional training or careful premeditation. [[miles-bredon|Bredon]]'s team suspected "there was something fishy about Mr.
Wallace, or he wouldn't have hidden the punt" (017-007).

## Significance to the Mystery

Wallace's assumed identity is a linchpin of the mystery. The question of who "Luke Wallace" really was — and why he needed a false name — drives much of the investigation's middle phase (022-006).
The revelation that Wallace was "the name he'd given" rather than his real name fundamentally changes the direction of the case (014-016).
His role in [[the-canoe-expedition|the canoe]] [[accident-or-design|accident]] — whether he was perpetrator, victim, or something in between — forms one of the central questions [[miles-bredon|Bredon]] must resolve.
