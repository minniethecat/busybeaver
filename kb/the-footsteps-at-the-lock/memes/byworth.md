# Byworth

A village on the Thames close to Shipcote Lock, on the opposite bank from the lock itself.
Byworth figures prominently in the reconstruction of the crime as the possible point of origin and escape for the person responsible for Derek Burtell's death.

## Location

Byworth is situated on the Thames, on the bank opposite to Shipcote Lock, approximately "half a mile from the scene of the accident" [003-010].
The village is described as being close enough to the river that its inhabitants would naturally use the lock and ferry crossings in their daily movements.
"The village of Byworth, close to the scene of the accident" [003-008] establishes its proximity to the central events.

The "Byworth bank" [024-033] is referenced as a specific geographic feature — the riverside on the Byworth side of the Thames —
and becomes a tactical location during the investigation when Bredon and Leyland plan to "go across on to the Byworth bank, and lie up in those bushes" [021-010].

## Role in the Investigation

Byworth becomes significant as the investigation narrows on the question of access and escape.
The theory that the murderer "had come from Byworth, and had made off in the Byworth direction" [025-005] makes the village a critical point in the geographical reconstruction of the crime.
The investigators trace movements "afterwards in the direction of Byworth" [020-030], and the question of who might have crossed "the main stream to the Byworth bank" [024-033] becomes central to the timeline.

The short distance between Byworth and Shipcote Lock — about half a mile — means that someone coming from Byworth could reach the lock quickly and on foot, without needing to use the river.
