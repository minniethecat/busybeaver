# Mrs. Burgess

The wife of the [[shipcote-lock|Shipcote Lock]] [[the-lock-keeper|lock-keeper]], [[mr-burgess|Mr. Burgess]] (who also works for [[the-indescribable-company|the Indescribable Company]]). Mrs.
Burgess provides tea, keeps hens, and maintains a domestic presence at [[shipcote-lock|the lock]] that contrasts with the dark events unfolding on the river (005-008).

## Role

Mrs.
Burgess is a background figure whose domestic activities — making tea, tending hens, maintaining [[the-shipcote-lock-keeper|the lock-keeper]]'s cottage —
create the everyday texture of life at [[shipcote-lock|Shipcote Lock]]. Her presence serves as a reminder of the ordinary world that the investigation intrudes upon.
When [[miles-bredon|Miles Bredon]] visits [[shipcote-lock|the lock]], he "rightly conjectured that Mrs. Burgess was the lock-keeper's wife" (005-008).

## Relationships

-   - **[[mr-burgess|Mr. Burgess]]**: Her husband, the [[shipcote-lock|Shipcote]] [[the-lock-keeper|lock-keeper]] and [[the-indescribable|Indescribable Company]] employee.
-   The two maintain [[shipcote-lock|the lock]] together as a household (005-007).
- **[[miles-bredon|Miles Bredon]]**: [[miles-bredon|Bredon]] encounters Mrs.
-   Burgess during his visits to [[shipcote-lock|Shipcote Lock]] and correctly identifies her role in [[the-shipcote-lock-keeper|the lock-keeper]]'s household (005-008).
- **The Lock Community**: Mrs. Burgess is part of the small community centered on [[shipcote-lock|Shipcote Lock]], along with her husband and the various boaters and visitors who pass through (005-025).

## Key Appearances

- **Offering Tea**: When a visitor arrives at [[shipcote-lock|the lock]] in a hurry, the suggestion is made to "ask Mrs. Burgess if she'd put the pot on" — establishing her as the provider of hospitality (005-007).
- **The Hens**: Mrs. Burgess keeps hens at [[shipcote-lock|the lock]], which occasionally get loose and require attention — a mundane detail that anchors [[shipcote-lock|the lock]] in ordinary rural life (005-025).
- **The Tea Arrangement**: [[mr-burgess|Mr. Burgess]] defers to "see Mrs. Burgess about the tea" before continuing a conversation, indicating her management of the household's domestic affairs (005-036).
- **The Drugget**: A later reference mentions someone who might "covet Mrs. Burgess' best piece of drugget," a floor covering that represents her pride in her home (020-015).

## Character Traits

Mrs. Burgess is defined by her domestic competence and her role as keeper of [[shipcote-lock|the lock]]'s household.
Her hens, her tea service, and her "best piece of drugget" (020-015) paint a portrait of a woman who takes pride in maintaining a comfortable home despite the isolated setting of [[shipcote-lock|the lock]].
She is a figure of warmth and normalcy in a story otherwise dominated by mystery and suspicion.

## Significance to the Mystery

While not directly involved in the investigation, Mrs. Burgess represents the domestic sphere at [[shipcote-lock|Shipcote Lock]] — the everyday life that continues around and despite the mystery.
Her presence at [[shipcote-lock|the lock]] establishes it as a lived-in place rather than merely a crime scene, and her connection to [[mr-burgess|Mr.
Burgess]] links the domestic and investigatory aspects of [[shipcote-lock|the lock]]'s role in the story.
