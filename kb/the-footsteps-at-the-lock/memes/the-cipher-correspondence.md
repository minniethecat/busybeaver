# The Cipher Correspondence

The coded messages between Derek Burtell and an accomplice that Bredon deciphers using a Bradshaw railway guide. This breakthrough transforms the investigation by proving premeditation (016-006).

## Summary

The cipher was discovered among Nigel's papers after his arrest — a newspaper personal column clipping whose irregular spacing "clearly indicated a cipher" (016-006).
Bredon recognized it as a word-cipher requiring a key text.

Recalling Quirk's mention that "most country hotels don't keep Bradshaw" (016-037), Bredon obtained a railway timetable and broke the cipher, revealing pre-arranged communication between the conspirators.

## Participants

| Character | Role |
|-----------|------|
| Miles Bredon | Decipherer |
| Angela Bredon | Companion during the night-long decipherment |
| Inspector Leyland | Observer; recipient of the solution |
| Mr. Quirk | Unwittingly provided the key insight about Bradshaw |

## Evidence

The deciphered messages proved Derek communicated with his accomplice after the supposed date of his death — meaning the Indescribable was not liable, and the disappearance was staged (017-013).

## Impact

The cipher's solution was the intellectual climax of the novel. It transformed Bredon's theory from speculation to proof and made prosecution possible.
