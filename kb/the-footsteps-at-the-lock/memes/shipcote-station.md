# Shipcote Station

The railway station serving the Shipcote area on the upper Thames.
In the investigation into Derek Burtell's disappearance, the station becomes a crucial checkpoint — its timetable, ticket records,
and the observations of its staff provide fixed time references against which other movements can be measured.

## Location

Shipcote Station is situated near Shipcote Lock but at some distance from it, connected by a field path.
The path between the lock and the station passes through or near Spinnaker Farm, making the station-lock route a known and observable corridor.
Bredon uses this route himself: "I went to Shipcote Station, and took the field path to the lock" [008-013].

## Role in the Investigation

The station features in the investigation in several ways:

- **Witness evidence**: "They remembered, at Shipcote Station, a gentleman catching the nine..." — the railway staff's recollection provides a time marker [010-011].
- **Nigel Burtell's location**: "Nigel Burtell was at Shipcote Station, or close to it" at a critical time on the day of the incident [009-033], a fact that bears directly on his alibi.
- **Route tracing**: The station is one terminus of the lock-station route, and anyone traveling between them would be observable.
-   The question of whether someone could have been "at Shipcote Station" and also at the lock within a certain time window is examined closely.

## The Field Path

The path connecting Shipcote Station to the lock is a significant route in the geographical reconstruction.
Bredon walks it himself to time the journey, and the possibility that evidence might have been hidden along this path — "hiding in a hedge somewhere near Shipcote Station" [007-046] — is considered during the search.
