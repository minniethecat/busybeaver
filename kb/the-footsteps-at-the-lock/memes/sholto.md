# Sholto

A senior figure at [[the-indescribable-company|the Indescribable Company]] who serves as the liaison between the corporation and its private detective.
His friendship with [[miles-bredon|Bredon]] personalizes the institutional relationship between the [[the-indescribable|Indescribable]] and its investigator (004-003).

## Role

Sholto briefs [[miles-bredon|Bredon]] on the Burtell case in the novel's fourth chapter, establishing the terms of the investigation.
"It's about this Burtell business," he says, launching [[miles-bredon|Bredon]] into the case that will occupy the rest of the novel (004-004).

As "an important cog in the business and a personal friend," Sholto occupies a dual role: he represents the [[the-indescribable|Indescribable]]'s institutional interests while also maintaining a personal relationship with [[miles-bredon|Bredon]] (004-003).
This duality reflects the [[the-indescribable|Indescribable]]'s distinctive corporate culture — a blend of formality and intimacy, of vast resources and personal connections.

## Character

Sholto is sketched with economy. He offers [[miles-bredon|Bredon]] "something to smoke.
I suppose a two-and-sixpenny cigar" (004-004) — a detail that conveys both the [[the-indescribable|Indescribable]]'s wealth and its careful accounting.
He delivers the company's instructions with a blend of authority and camaraderie that reflects his position at the intersection of friendship and employment.

## Significance

Sholto embodies the [[the-indescribable|Indescribable]]'s human face. The company is vast, anonymous, and "indescribable" — but Sholto gives it a voice, a manner, a personal relationship with its detective.
Without Sholto, the [[the-indescribable|Indescribable]] would be a purely abstract force. His presence humanizes the institution while also reminding us that institutions act through individuals.
