# Mr. Carmichael

A Fellow at [[simon-magus-college|Simon Magus College]], described as a "man called Carmichael. Always full of odd ideas" (008-026).
He is a member of the [[oxford|Oxford]] circle drawn into the investigation of [[derek-burtell|Derek Burtell]]'s disappearance, contributing his own distinctive theories to the case.

## Role

Carmichael serves as a voice of alternative hypotheses within the [[oxford|Oxford]] investigation circle.
He participates in discussions at [[simon-magus-college-org|the college]] and contributes to the intellectual atmosphere of the case, offering theories that range from the ingenious to the improbable.
His most notable contribution is the "soap dish theory" — a speculative explanation for [[the-disappearance|Derek's disappearance]] that becomes a recurring reference point in the investigation (013-015).

## Relationships

- **[[miles-bredon|Miles Bredon]]**: Carmichael is part of the [[oxford|Oxford]] cohort assisting [[miles-bredon|Bredon]]'s investigation.
-   [[miles-bredon|Bredon]] tolerates Carmichael's theories with a mixture of amusement and genuine interest (008-037).
- - **[[nigel-burtell|Nigel Burtell]]**: Carmichael interacts with [[nigel-burtell|Nigel]] at [[simon-magus-college|Simon Magus College]],
- where [[nigel-burtell|Nigel]] turns to his "Uncle" figure for advice about the investigation (008-026).
- **[[simon-magus-college|Simon Magus College]] Fellows**: Carmichael is one of several college members involved in the case, including a "junior Fellow" who chuckles at Carmichael's enthusiasm (008-031).

## Key Appearances

- **The College Discussion**: Carmichael breaks into a conversation about detectives at [[the-common-room-dinner|the college dinner]] table, offering his perspective on the investigation (008-030).
- **The Soap Dish Theory**: Carmichael proposes his signature theory involving a soap dish as a key piece of [[the-oath-and-testimony|evidence]] in understanding [[the-disappearance|Derek's disappearance]] (013-015).
- - **The Inn Visit**: Carmichael advises someone to "Give your name as Carmichael, and say you want to look in" at a local inn —
- suggesting he is known in the area and familiar with local investigative techniques (009-009).

## Character Traits

Carmichael is characterized by his intellectual energy and his penchant for unconventional thinking.
The description "always full of odd ideas" (008-026) captures both his creative approach to problem-solving and his tendency to propose theories that others find eccentric.
A "junior Fellow" at [[simon-magus-college-org|the college]] reacts to Carmichael's contributions with a chuckle, suggesting he is viewed with affectionate amusement by his academic peers (008-031).
Despite his quirks, Carmichael is taken seriously enough to be included in [[the-gudgeon-inn|the inn]]er circle of the investigation.

## Significance to the Mystery

Carmichael's soap dish theory, while perhaps fanciful,
serves an important narrative function: it illustrates the range of speculative thinking that the case generated among [[miles-bredon|Bredon]]'s [[oxford|Oxford]] associates. [[miles-bredon|Bredon]] reflects on "Mr.
Carmichael, and his theory of the soap dish" as one of several hypotheses that must be weighed against the [[the-oath-and-testimony|evidence]] (013-015).
His presence also grounds the investigation in the intellectual culture of [[simon-magus-college|Simon Magus College]].
