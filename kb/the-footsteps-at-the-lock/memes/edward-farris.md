# Edward Farris

Edward Farris is a pivotal figure in the [[the-burtell-legacy|inheritance]] plot of the novel. Orphaned in infancy, he was taken in by [[mrs-coolman|Mrs.
Coolman]] and raised at Brimley House, Wallingford, serving as her secretary and companion (015-003). Under the terms of [[mrs-coolman|Mrs.
Coolman]]'s will, [[farris|Farris]] is named as the alternate heir — should [[derek-burtell|Derek Burtell]] fail to meet the conditions set for his [[the-burtell-legacy|inheritance]],
the entire estate passes to Edward Farris, giving him a powerful financial motive in the mystery of [[the-disappearance|Derek's disappearance]].

## Role

[[farris|Farris]] occupies the structural position of the "alternative heir" in the novel's [[the-burtell-legacy|inheritance]] drama. [[mrs-coolman|Mrs. Coolman]]'s will, made after [[derek-burtell|Derek]] and [[nigel-burtell|Nigel]] effected a "reconciliation and adoption" with her, names [[farris|Farris]] as [[derek-burtell|Derek]]'s substitute (015-004). This creates a clear motive for [[farris|Farris]] to wish [[derek-burtell|Derek]] harm — a fact that [[miles-bredon|Bredon]] and [[inspector-leyland|Leyland]] must weigh carefully in their investigation. [[farris|Farris]] himself remains largely offstage until later in the narrative, his presence felt through the legal documents that establish his claim.

## Relationships

-   Having been orphaned in infancy, he was adopted by her and lived at Brimley House as her companion (015-003). She named him her heir as [[derek-burtell|Derek]]'s alternative.
- **[[derek-burtell|Derek Burtell]]**: As the alternate heir to [[mrs-coolman|Mrs.
-   Coolman]]'s estate, [[farris|Farris]] stands in direct financial opposition to [[derek-burtell|Derek]] — if [[derek-burtell|Derek]] fails to claim the [[the-burtell-legacy|inheritance]],
-   [[farris|Farris]] benefits (015-005).
- **[[nigel-burtell|Nigel Burtell]]**: The reconciliation between [[mrs-coolman|Mrs.
-   Coolman]] and the Burtell grandsons directly affects [[farris|Farris]]'s prospects, as [[the-burtell-inheritance|the will]] names him "as [[derek-burtell|Derek]]'s alternative" (015-004).
-   [[nigel-burtell|Nigel]] would tell [[farris|Farris]] the news about the [[the-burtell-legacy|inheritance]].
- **[[miles-bredon|Miles Bredon]]**: [[miles-bredon|Bredon]] investigates [[farris|Farris]]'s role and motives, recognizing the financial incentive as a significant line of inquiry (022-001).

## Key Appearances

- **The Will's Revelation**: [[farris|Farris]] is first introduced through the terms of [[mrs-coolman|Mrs.
-   Coolman]]'s will, which names him as the alternate heir — establishing his stake in the [[the-burtell-legacy|inheritance]] before he appears in person (015-003–015-005).
- **Physical Description**: When [[farris|Farris]] does appear, he is described as having a "vigorous physique" despite his youth, suggesting a capable and potentially formidable young man (022-001).
- **The Investigation Focus**: [[miles-bredon|Bredon]] turns his attention to [[farris|Farris]] as a person of interest, recognizing that the financial motive makes him a significant figure in the case (021-023).

## Character Traits

Despite being orphaned and dependent on [[mrs-coolman|Mrs. Coolman]]'s charity, [[farris|Farris]] is described as possessing a "vigorous physique" (022-001) that be[[the-lie|lies]] his dependent status.
The narrative notes that "Edward Farris was human" — suggesting that his motivations, while perhaps self-interested, are understandable rather than villainous (015-004).
He is a young man who has risen from orphan to potential heir through a combination of circumstance and, presumably, personal effort in serving [[mrs-coolman|Mrs. Coolman]].

## Significance to the Mystery

Edward Farris provides the [[the-burtell-legacy|inheritance]] plot with its central tension.
As the alternate heir, he gives the mystery a clear financial dimension: if [[derek-burtell|Derek Burtell]] has been harmed or has disappeared permanently,
[[farris|Farris]] stands to gain the entire [[mrs-coolman|Coolman]] estate.
This motive makes him a person of interest in [[miles-bredon|Bredon]]'s investigation and creates a counter-narrative to other theories about [[the-disappearance|Derek's disappearance]].

His existence also raises questions about whether the apparent [[accident-or-design|accident]] at [[shipcote-lock|Shipcote Lock]] was truly [[accident-or-design|accident]]al,
or whether someone with a financial interest might have arranged events.
