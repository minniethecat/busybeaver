# The Bredon Reconstruction

Miles Bredon's masterstroke — the physical re-enactment of the crime that exposed Nigel Burtell's guilt. "We'll go up to Shipcote Lock, and you can act as the corpse" (018-039).
With Inspector Leyland playing the role of Derek, Bredon retraced every step of the fatal journey, demonstrating that the alibi was a tissue of impossibilities.

## Summary

Bredon's method was to turn deduction into demonstration.
Having worked out what must have happened — Nigel killing Derek above the lock, passing through with a live accomplice, scuttling the canoe below — he recreated the entire sequence on the actual river.
The reconstruction proved that:
1. Nigel could not have caught the 9:14 train and also committed the crime — **unless** the crime happened before the train
2. Derek was already dead when the canoe passed through Shipcote Lock
3. The photograph of the lock was a fabrication — it showed a different lock entirely

## Location

The reconstruction traced the exact route: Shipcote Lock → Eaton Bridge → the river between them. The lock, the weir, the island, the towpath — every geographical feature played a role.
The "familiar distance between Shipcote and Eaton Bridge had lengthened" in the darkness of the original journey; in daylight, Bredon could measure it precisely (021-018).

## Evidence & Clues

- **Timing**: How long does it take to paddle from Shipcote Lock to Eaton Bridge? Does it match Nigel's account?
- **The current**: How fast does the river flow? Can a canoe drift at the speed Nigel claimed?
- **The lock passage**: Can one person operate the lock alone? Could Derek, "lying motionless," have done anything to assist?
- **The photograph comparison**: Two locks, one photograph — "it's a common type, you know" (024-021)

## Impact on the Investigation

The reconstruction is the climax of the investigation.
Everything Bredon has learned — from the lock-keeper, the station staff, Angela, the moneylenders, the Indescribable files — converges in this single physical demonstration.
It is not enough to have a theory; you must be able to **show** it. The reconstruction is the proof made visible.
