# The Upper Thames Canal

The canalized section above Oxford: "a narrow, artificial waterway, with an unambitious lock at intervals of a mile or two" (002-002).

## Description

Created by the Thames Conservancy Board, the canal section disciplines the river into a navigable channel.
But the discipline is incomplete — currents still run, gates can be half-open, a canoe can drift out of control (002-004).

## Significance

The canal's engineered character makes it both navigable and dangerous. Human attempts to control nature are shown to be fragile — a theme that mirrors the novel's treatment of human attempts to control fate.
