# The Man in the Punt

The mysterious stranger who passed through [[Shipcote Lock]] in a punt just before [[Derek Burtell]] and [[Nigel Burtell|Nigel Burtell's]] canoe on the morning of the disappearance.
His inexplicable vanishing between Shipcote and the next lock upstream, and his later unmasking as [[Edward Farris]] disguised as [[Luke Wallace]],
make him one of the most important figures in [[the-police-investigation|the investigation]] (010-020).

## Summary

On the morning Derek Burtell vanished, a man in a punt passed through Shipcote Lock shortly before the Burtell cousins arrived. The lock-keeper saw him go up, but never saw him return (010-020).
When [[inspector-leyland|Leyland]] and [[Miles Bredon|Bredon]] began investigating, they discovered that the punt had apparently vanished: it had not passed through the next lock upstream,
nor had it returned through Shipcote (014-018). The man and his punt had simply disappeared from the river between the two locks.

The investigation into the man in the punt consumes multiple chapters. Leyland devotes an entire day to tracing his movements (013-001), and [[mr-quirk|Mr.
Quirk]] suggests tracking purchases the man might have made in [[Oxford]] before reaching the river (016-001).
The mystery deepens when Bredon and Leyland find a notecase at [[Eaton Bridge]] — marked "F.N.2" — which the man had taken at Eaton (020-038).

Ultimately, the man in the punt is revealed as [[Edward Farris]], [[mrs-coolman|Mrs.
Coolman]]'s protégé, who had disguised himself as the solicitor [[Luke Wallace]] to investigate the Burtell affair for his own purposes.

## Location

The man in the punt's journey began at [[Oxford]], where he likely purchased provisions for a river tour (016-001).
He passed upstream through [[Shipcote Lock]] just before the Burtells, witnessed by the lock-keeper (010-020).
He then apparently vanished: the lock-keeper at the next lock upstream was "quite positive that no punt had come up at the time mentioned" (014-018).
[[mr-burgess|Burgess]] confirmed the punt never came back through Shipcote.

The mystery of the vanishing punt was resolved when Bredon and Leyland found the notecase at [[Eaton Bridge]], confirming that the man had portaged his punt out of the river at Eaton —
explaining how he disappeared between the two locks (020-038).

## Impact on the Investigation

The man in the punt is initially treated as a potential witness — someone who passed through the scene shortly before the disappearance. But the discovery that he vanished without a trace transforms him into a suspect.
Leyland remarks that "it's still important to find the man in the punt before we find Nigel Burtell" (017-042), elevating the punt-man to primary investigative priority.

The revelation that the man was [[Edward Farris]] in disguise reframes the entire case. Farris, as [[mrs-coolman|Mrs.
Coolman]]'s alternate heir, had a motive to investigate (and possibly sabotage) the Burtell inheritance.
His elaborate disguise as [[Luke Wallace]] — including the punt journey — demonstrates the lengths to which the Coolman faction went to control the narrative of Derek's disappearance.

The notecase at Eaton (F.N.2) is one of the pivotal pieces of physical evidence in the case, alongside [[the-photograph-of-the-lock|the photograph]] and the [[the-cipher-correspondence|cipher correspondence]].
It ties the man in the punt directly to the Coolman household and provides the first concrete link between the river mystery and the inheritance plot.
