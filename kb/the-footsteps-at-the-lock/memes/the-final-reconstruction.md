# The Final Reconstruction

Bredon's comprehensive account of what actually happened — how Derek staged his death with Nigel's assistance, and how the conspiracy was maintained until the cipher broke it open [020-004].

## Summary

In the novel's penultimate chapter, Bredon lays out the complete sequence of events: Derek, facing financial ruin over the fifty thousand pounds [001-012], conspired with Nigel to stage his death.
The canoe trip was a performance; the camera was planted; the cipher was their means of communication [016-006]. Derek went into hiding while Nigel maintained the fiction of a tragic accident.

## Key Elements of the Reconstruction

- The canoe was deliberately allowed to drift through the lock gates
- Nigel's camera was placed to create false evidence
- The cipher proved Derek was alive after the supposed date of death [016-006]
- Derek's hiding place was identified through financial records

## Impact

The reconstruction saved the Indescribable fifty thousand pounds [004-001], established the truth of Derek's fate, and brought the investigation to a close.
But it also left scars: a family destroyed, a fortune dissipated, and the melancholy recognition that justice and truth are not always the same thing.
