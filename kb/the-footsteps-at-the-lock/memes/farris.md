# Farris

The solicitor who managed the Burtell family's legal affairs, including [[mr-burtell-the-grandfather|old Mr.
Burtell]]'s will and the trust provisions governing [[derek-burtell|Derek]]'s [[the-burtell-legacy|inheritance]].
His knowledge of the [[the-burtell-legacy|legacy]]'s terms — and his potential conflicts of interest — make him a significant figure in the investigation (015-028).

## Role

Farris occupies the uneasy position of a family solicitor whose clients' interests diverge. He administered the trust that held [[derek-burtell|Derek]]'s [[the-burtell-legacy|inheritance]].
He knew the terms of [[the-burtell-inheritance|the will]] and the consequences of [[the-death-of-derek-burtell|Derek's death]].
He was, in some sense, the custodian of the financial incentives that drove the plot (015-020).

[[miles-bredon|Bredon]]'s investigation reveals that Farris may have been more than a passive administrator. The question of whether Farris colluded with one party or another — or merely did his professional duty — is one of the novel's sub-puzzles.

## Character

As a solicitor, Farris is defined by professional discretion.
He knows things that he cannot disclose, represents interests that may conflict, and maintains the opacity that is both the virtue and the vice of legal professionalism.
[[miles-bredon|Bredon]]'s challenge in dealing with Farris is to extract information without triggering the solicitor's instinct for concealment (015-028).

## Significance

Farris represents the legal dimension of the novel's exploration of institutional complicity.
The Burtell [[the-burtell-legacy|legacy]] was not merely a family matter but a legal construction — a document interpreted and administered by professionals whose own interests were entangled with their clients'.
Farris's role raises uncomfortable questions about how much of the tragedy was produced by the legal structure of the [[the-burtell-legacy|legacy]] itself.
