# Jeff Tucker Drugging

Jeff Tucker, the colored caretaker of the Topham bungalow, is given alcohol by the robbers to incapacitate him, allowing them to steal his keys and ransack the property using a moving van.

## Summary

Jeff Tucker is employed as the caretaker of the Topham bungalow on Moon Lake (012-073, 014-027). When Nancy visits the bungalow during her investigation, she finds it deserted with no sign of Jeff Tucker (014-027, 014-035). She later encounters him face to face (017-001), and he reveals that men had given him alcohol to get him drunk before stealing his keys (021-061). The robbers used the stolen keys to enter the bungalow and load stolen goods onto their moving van.

Jeff's disarmingly simple explanation of the drugging — that he was given liquor until he could no longer stand — underscores both his trusting nature and the robbers' cold calculation. After recovering, Jeff followed the robbers' trail to a garage, standing outside and gazing mournfully at the place where the moving van was hidden (018-022, 018-024).

## Investigation Context

This event forms part of the larger pattern of criminal activity that Nancy Drew systematically uncovers. Each incident reveals another layer of the conspiracy surrounding the Crowley estate, building toward the eventual resolution of the case.

## Plot Significance

The drugging of Jeff Tucker is a key enabling event for the bungalow robbery. Without incapacitating the caretaker, the robbers would not have been able to enter the bungalow undetected. The incident demonstrates the robbers' calculated criminal methods: rather than breaking in violently, they used deception and drugs to neutralize the lone guard.

Jeff Tucker's testimony later becomes critical evidence when Nancy confronts the robbers. His account of the stolen keys directly links the thieves to the bungalow burglary, and his subsequent tracking of the van to the garage provides Nancy with the lead she needs to locate the stolen goods and the [[crowley-clock]].
