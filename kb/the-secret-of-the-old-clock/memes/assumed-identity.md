# Assumed Identity

**[[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]** used the assumed name "Josiah Harkston" to rent **[[safety-deposit-box-148|safety deposit box number 148]]** at the **[[masonville-national-bank|Masonville National Bank]]**, the box in which he placed his **[[the-lost-will|second will]]**. This concealment is the central device that kept the will hidden.

## The Discovery

When **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]** finds **[[crowley-notebook|Crowley's notebook]]** inside the **[[crowley-clock|family clock]]**, she reads the critical entry:

> "My last will and testament will be found in safety deposit box number 148 in the Masonville National Bank. The box is under the name of Josiah Harkston."

Nancy immediately grasps the significance: "Who would have ever thought of looking for it in a safety deposit box under the name of Josiah Harkston?"

## Crowley's Method

The use of a pseudonym is consistent with Crowley's "queer ways" — his habit of secreting objects in places so safe that even he could not find them. His choice of an assumed name for the deposit box ensured that even if someone searched for a box under his real name, they would find nothing.

## Verification

At the bank, the president initially states they "have never had dealings with a man by the name of Josiah Crowley." When **[[carson-drew|Carson Drew]]** mentions Harkston, the banker recalls the name and produces the signature card. The handwriting matches Crowley's "cramped hand," confirming the identity.

The use of an assumed identity nearly defeated Crowley's own purpose — if Nancy had not been so persistent, the will might never have been found.
