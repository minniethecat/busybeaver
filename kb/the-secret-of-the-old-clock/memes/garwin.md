# Garwin

A small town situated along the lefthand road from a fork in the highway — notable primarily as the intended destination of the robbers who stole [[josiah-crowley]]'s clock. Nancy Drew's pursuit of the thieves toward Garwin forms the climactic chase sequence of the novel.

## Location

Garwin lies at the end of the lefthand fork of a road branching from the main highway. At the fork, Nancy confirms with the marshal:

> "Doesn't the lefthand road lead to Garwin?" The marshal nodded. (018-032)

The town is close enough to River Heights that a van or automobile can reach it within the span of a pursuit, but far enough that a criminal reaching town limits could evade capture:

> She realized that already the robbers had a big start, and that if once they reached Garwin she could not hope to overtake them. (018-038)

The road to Garwin passes through countryside, and the highway on the lefthand fork is "smooth" with "a clear stretch ahead." (018-038)

## Description

Garwin is described only in passing as a town toward which characters travel. No detailed physical description of the town itself appears in the text — it functions primarily as a directional waypoint in the narrative's geography. Its significance lies entirely in its role as the robbers' intended destination and the direction of the pursuit.

> "Doesn't the lefthand road lead to Garwin?" The marshal nodded. (018-032)

## Role in the Mystery

Garwin serves as the directional anchor for the pursuit sequence that recovers the stolen Crowley property. After the robbers steal the clock and luggage from the Horner farm, they flee toward Garwin, hoping to reach the town limits before law enforcement can intercept them.

At the fork in the road, Nancy makes a strategic decision:

> "I'll tell you what we'll do," Nancy proposed, as she had an inspiration. "You take the righthand road and I'll head for Garwin." (018-035)

Nancy takes the lefthand road. She discovers the robbers' van:

> She paused long enough to make certain that the van had taken the road to Garwin. Then, clutching the precious clock in her arms, she turned and ran. (020-021)

The van continues on without noticing her:

> As Nancy darted into the woods, she cast an anxious glance over her shoulder, but to her intense relief she saw that she was not being followed. There was no one to be seen in the vicinity of the roadhouse, and the big moving van was proceeding slowly on its way toward Garwin, the occupants oblivious that their whereabouts had been discovered. (020-022)

Later, Nancy warns the marshal:

> "Quick!" she called to the marshal. "The robbers have taken the road to Garwin! If you hurry you can overtake them! Go on and I'll follow!" (021-014)

The marshal races toward Garwin:

> Following close behind the police car, the girl sped down the road in pursuit of the robbers. The two automobiles passed the roadhouse beyond the fork and raced toward Garwin. (021-017)

The successful interception of the robbers before they reach Garwin allows for the recovery of all stolen items and their arrest.

> "If those men reach Garwin before the marshal overtakes them, there won't be one chance in a hundred of capturing them," Nancy thought. (021-009)
