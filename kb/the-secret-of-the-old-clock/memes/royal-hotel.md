# Royal Hotel

A hotel in downtown [[river-heights|River Heights]] where [[carson-drew|Carson Drew]] arranged a luncheon meeting with lawyer Henry Rolsted — the occasion where [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] first heard professional confirmation that [[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]] had likely made a second will. (002-055) This meeting transforms the novel's premise from [[nancy-drew|Nancy]]'s intuition to an evidence-based investigation.

## Location

The Royal Hotel was located less than a block from [[carson-drew|Carson Drew]]'s law office in downtown [[river-heights|River Heights]]. Nancy and her father walked the short distance together, meeting Mr. Rolsted in the lobby before proceeding to the dining room. (002-059) Its proximity to the legal district made it a natural venue for professional luncheons.

## Description

The hotel had a lobby and a formal dining room where tables could be reserved in advance. Carson Drew secured a table for three, positioning the luncheon as both a social occasion and a strategic information-gathering opportunity. (002-059) (002-060)

The luncheon conversation followed a deliberate arc. It began with general legal topics — a professional veneer — before [[carson-drew|Carson Drew]] skillfully steered it toward the [[crowley-will-document|Crowley inheritance]] case. His approach was indirect: rather than interrogating Rolsted, he created conversational space for the older lawyer to volunteer what he knew.

> "It would not surprise me if another will should come to light. The Tophams treated Crowley more shabbily than anyone else." — Henry Rolsted (002-071)

This single sentence, spoken over lunch in the Royal Hotel dining room, is arguably the most consequential line of dialogue in the novel. It confirms three things: that Crowley had motive to change his will, that the Tophams' mistreatment was widely known in legal circles, and that a new will was not merely possible but probable. [[nancy-drew|Nancy]], listening intently, absorbed every word — the luncheon gave her factual grounding for the investigation she would pursue.

## Role in the Mystery

The Royal Hotel luncheon is the first turning point in [[nancy-drew|Nancy]]'s investigation. Before this meeting, the existence of a second will was merely [[nancy-drew|Nancy]]'s intuition — a reasonable suspicion but not a fact. Henry Rolsted's disclosure — that he had advised [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]] on drawing up a will deliberately unfavorable to [[topham-family|the Tophams]] — gave Nancy the factual basis she needed to pursue the case in earnest. (002-071) (002-073)

The setting matters: a respectable hotel dining room, a professional luncheon, an established lawyer speaking in confidence. Rolsted's words carry weight because of where and how they are delivered — this is not rumor or gossip but a colleague's professional recollection, shared in a setting that signals discretion and reliability.

The Royal Hotel thus functions as a narrative gateway: Nancy enters as a curious daughter humoring her father's invitation, and leaves as an investigator with a confirmed lead.
