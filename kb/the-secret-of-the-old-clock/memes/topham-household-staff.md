# Topham Household Staff

The **[[topham-family|Topham family]]** employs domestic staff at their pretentious **[[topham-residence|residence]]** on the avenue in **[[river-heights|River Heights]]**.

## The Butler

The most visible member of the staff is the uniformed butler who answers the door when **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]** visits to sell **[[charity-dance-tickets|charity dance tickets]]**. The butler "condescendingly waited for Nancy to state her mission," reflecting the household's atmosphere of affected superiority.

## Role in Nancy's Visit

When Nancy arrives, she instructs the butler to "Tell Mrs. Topham that Miss Drew is calling" and explains she is selling tickets for "one of the most important social functions of the year." The butler conveys the message and returns with permission for her to enter.

After ushering Nancy into the living room, the butler leaves her alone — giving her the opportunity to glance about and notice the modern mantel clock that is NOT the **[[crowley-clock|Crowley clock]]** she seeks. This brief moment alone proves valuable for her investigation.

## Contrast

The Tophams' employment of a uniformed butler reflects **[[cora-topham|Mrs. Topham]]**'s social ambitions. However, the butler's "condescending" manner mirrors his employers' **[[snobbery|snobbery]]**, suggesting that the household's values permeate its entire structure.
