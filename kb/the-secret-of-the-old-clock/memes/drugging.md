# Drugging

The **[[bungalow-robbery|robbery of the Topham bungalow]]** at **[[moon-lake|Moon Lake]]** is facilitated by the drugging of **[[jeff-tucker|Jeff Tucker]]**, the caretaker.

## The Scheme

A member of the **[[robber-leader|robber gang]]** — a "tall an' slick-lookin'" white man driving a large sedan — approaches Jeff at the bungalow. Recognizing Jeff's loneliness, he invites him for a ride and offers him drinks. Jeff later testifies: "Dey gives me some kind of a sleepin' powdah and pahks me in a ho‑tel."

## Jeff's Account

Jeff describes his experience to **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]**: "Den I had one and some mo' too I guess, cause aftah while I didn't feel like I was ridin' in no automobeel at all. I thought dat de chariot had done gone an' swung low an' I was a-bein' wafted off to some place in de gen'ral direction o' heaven!"

He wakes up in a hotel, feeling "pow'fui sickish-like and discouraged," and discovers his keys to the bungalow have been stolen.

## The Robbery

With Jeff incapacitated, the robbers use the stolen keys to enter the bungalow and strip it of furniture, rugs, draperies, and valuables — including the **[[crowley-clock|Crowley clock]]**. The robbery would have succeeded if **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]** had not arrived and witnessed the aftermath, leading to her being **[[nancy-trapped-in-closet|locked in the closet]]**, the recovery of the clock, and the eventual **[[capturing-the-robbers|capture of the robbers]]**.
