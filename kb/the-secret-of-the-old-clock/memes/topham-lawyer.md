# Topham Lawyer

The unnamed lawyer who accompanies the four [[richard-topham|Topham]] family members to the reading of [[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]'s [[crowley-will-document|second will]] at the [[drew-household|Drew home]]. His professional honesty — acknowledging that [[carson-drew|Carson Drew]]'s legal assessment is correct — costs him his job within minutes of the reading's conclusion.

## Role in the Story

As [[carson-drew|Carson Drew]] had predicted, "[[topham-family|the Tophams]] will bring a lawyer with them" to the will reading [024-004]. The lawyer arrives with [[richard-topham|Richard Topham]], [[cora-topham|Mrs. Cora Topham]], [[ada-topham|Ada]], and [[isabel-topham|Isabel]], and seats himself "uneasily" beside [[richard-topham|Richard Topham]] as the reading begins [024-026]. His discomfort is telling — he already senses he represents clients whose claim is indefensible.

When [[carson-drew|Carson Drew]] warns that contesting the will would be "only a waste of your time and [[fortune|money]]" and suggests the Tophams "ask your own lawyer" for a second opinion, the lawyer promptly agrees: "[[carson-drew|Mr. Drew]] is right." [024-064] [024-065] His response is swift and unqualified — he makes no attempt to argue or negotiate.

His honesty proves fatal to his employment. [[cora-topham|Mrs. Topham]] immediately dismisses him: "If that's all you know about law, you're discharged! We'll get another lawyer and we'll fight to the last ditch!" [024-066] The irony is pointed: she fires him for knowing the law too well.

After the Tophams storm out, the lawyer rises, picks up his briefcase, and remarks to [[carson-drew|Carson Drew]]: "Well, I can't say I'm sorry to lose the case." [024-068] He then takes his leave with dignity [024-068].

## Role in the Mystery

The Topham lawyer serves an important function in the novel's final scene: his independent legal opinion confirms the validity of the [[crowley-will-document|second will]]. By agreeing with [[carson-drew|Carson Drew]]'s assessment without hesitation, he provides impartial verification that the Tophams have no legal ground to contest [[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]'s wishes. His dismissal underscores the Tophams' irrational refusal to accept reality.

His role also highlights a subtle class distinction within the legal profession: while [[carson-drew|Carson Drew]] is a "noted criminal and mystery-case lawyer" with the freedom to choose his cases, the unnamed Topham lawyer must serve whoever pays his fees — however distasteful the client.

## Narrative Significance

The lawyer's brief appearance demonstrates the novel's moral economy: even the Tophams' own hired counsel cannot support their claim. His quiet departure represents the collapse of the [[richard-topham|Topham]] family's legal position and, symbolically, their social standing. The fact that he is never named — he is simply "the Topham lawyer" — reinforces his function as an objective mirror reflecting the Tophams' moral bankruptcy. When even your own lawyer is relieved to be rid of you, the narrative suggests, your cause is truly lost.
