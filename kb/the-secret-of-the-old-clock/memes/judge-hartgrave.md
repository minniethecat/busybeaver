# Judge Hartgrave

A judge in [[masonville|Masonville]] — a colleague of [[carson-drew|Carson Drew]] who receives legal documents delivered by [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] and extends hospitality to her, demonstrating the professional network that surrounds and supports the [[drew-household|Drew family]]. (003-002) (003-022)

## Role in the Story

Judge Hartgrave appears in Chapter 3 when [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] delivers "a number of legal documents which must be delivered to Judge Hartgrave at [[masonville|Masonville]]" on her father's behalf. (003-002) This errand takes [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] on the road trip that brings her through the rural countryside and ultimately to [[moon-lake|Moon Lake]], where the key events of the [[detective-work|investigation]] unfold. When [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] arrives at his office and learns he has gone to the courthouse, she follows — and Judge Hartgrave, knowing she is "the daughter of [[carson-drew|Carson Drew]], insisted that she take luncheon with him." (003-022) This professional courtesy reflects the respect [[carson-drew|Carson Drew]] commands in legal circles and [[nancy-drew|Nancy]]'s own standing within that network.

## Character Traits

- Hospitable: insists [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] take lunch with him upon learning her identity (003-022)
- Respected: holds a judicial position in [[masonville|Masonville]]
- Connected to the Drew network: a colleague and friend of [[carson-drew|Carson Drew]]
- Courteous: treats the sixteen-year-old [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] with professional respect

## Role in the Mystery

Judge Hartgrave does not participate directly in the mystery but his presence serves a narrative function: his errand sends [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] onto the road trip that brings her through the countryside, past the [[horner-farm|Horner farm]], and ultimately into contact with the key witnesses at [[moon-lake|Moon Lake]]. His professional respect for [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] — treating her as a responsible agent, not merely [[carson-drew|Carson Drew]]'s daughter — reinforces her status as a capable young adult.

## Narrative Significance

Judge Hartgrave represents the established legal order that provides the backdrop for [[nancy-drew|Nancy]]'s amateur [[detective-work|investigation]]. The fact that [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] is trusted to deliver legal documents and is welcomed as an equal by a judge underscores the novel's theme that competence and character — not age or professional credentials — are what matter.
