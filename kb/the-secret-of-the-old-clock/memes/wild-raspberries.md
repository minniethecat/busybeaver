# Wild Raspberries

Wild raspberries gathered by **[[allie-horner|Allie]]** and **[[grace-horner|Grace Horner]]** from the woods near their farm represent the sisters' ability to find small pleasures despite their **[[poverty|poverty]]**.

## The Gathering

When **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]** visits the Horner farm for the second time, she finds the sisters returning from the woods with "a tin pail which was filled nearly to the brim with raspberries." Their arms are covered with "ugly scratches which had been caused by brambles and sharp thorns."

## Hospitality

Despite having almost nothing, Grace urges Nancy to come inside for "a dish of berries" served "with sugar and cream." Allie adds with characteristic humor: "If you don't have them too often." This simple offering exemplifies the sisters' generous spirit — they share what little they have with warmth and grace.

## Symbolism

The wild raspberries symbolize the Horner sisters' resilience and resourcefulness. Unable to afford luxuries, they find nature's bounty in the woods. The image of the two sisters, scratched but cheerful, returning with their filled pail captures their determination to find joy and offer hospitality despite their hardship. This spirit of **[[gratitude|gratitude]]** and generosity is ultimately rewarded when they receive their **[[inheritance|inheritance]]**.
