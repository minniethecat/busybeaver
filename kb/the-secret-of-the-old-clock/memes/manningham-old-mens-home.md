## Overview

The Manningham Old Men's Home is a charitable institution for elderly men, mentioned in [[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]'s second will as the recipient of the residual balance of his estate.

## Role in the Novel

The Manningham Old Men's Home appears only in the final chapter, during the reading of Crowley's second will at the [[drew-household|Drew home]].

> [024-056] "That is all there is except that there is a proviso to pay all his just debts, including his funeral expenses and that what little balance is left—maybe three or four thousand dollars—goes to the Manningham Old Men's Home."

[[carson-drew|Carson Drew]] explained that after all specific bequests to Crowley's relatives and friends were distributed, and after paying debts and funeral expenses, the remaining balance — estimated at three or four thousand dollars — would go to the home.

## Significance

The Manningham Old Men's Home serves a narrative function in the will's structure: it is the **residual beneficiary**, ensuring that no portion of Crowley's estate would revert to the [[topham-family|Tophams]] or go unallocated. Its inclusion underscores Crowley's charitable intentions, in contrast to the Tophams' greed.
