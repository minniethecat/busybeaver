# Manilla Envelope

The manilla envelope containing legal documents is the narrative device that places **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]** on the **[[river-road|River Road]]** during the **[[storm-on-river-road|storm]]**, leading to her **[[chance-meeting|chance meeting]]** with the **[[horner-sisters|Horner sisters]]**.

## The Errand

**[[carson-drew|Carson Drew]]** asks Nancy to deliver "a number of legal documents" to **[[judge-hartgrave|Judge Hartgrave]]** at **[[masonville|Masonville]]**. The envelope is "fat" and the papers are described as important. Nancy agrees willingly: "It's such a wonderful day, I'll enjoy the trip."

## The Journey

Nancy drives to Masonville, delivers the envelope to Judge Hartgrave at the courthouse, and accepts his invitation to luncheon. On her return trip, she decides to take the scenic **[[river-road|River Road]]** — a "beautiful drive" but a fateful choice.

The storm catches her on the isolated road, forcing her to seek shelter at the **[[horner-farm|Horner farm]]** barn. There she meets **[[allie-horner|Allie]]** and **[[grace-horner|Grace Horner]]**, learning of their connection to **[[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]** and their exclusion from his will.

## Catalytic Function

The manilla envelope is thus the catalyst for the entire mystery's resolution. Without this simple errand, Nancy would never have met the Horner sisters, never learned of their plight, and never been motivated to find the **[[the-lost-will|missing will]]**. It exemplifies how seemingly minor events can have far-reaching consequences.
