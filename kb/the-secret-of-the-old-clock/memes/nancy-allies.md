# Nancy's Allies

Throughout *The Secret of the Old Clock*, Nancy Drew does not work alone. She is supported by a network of allies — her father's legal expertise, her housekeeper's steady counsel, and the informants she cultivates among the very relatives whose cause she champions. This page catalogs every character who aids Nancy's investigation.

## The Inner Circle

**Carson Drew** — Nancy's father, a noted criminal lawyer and former district attorney. He provides legal guidance, professional contacts, and unwavering support. Though he initially doubts a second will can be proven, he encourages Nancy's investigation and connects her with key figures like Mr. Rolsted.

**Hannah Gruen** — The Drew household's housekeeper and a maternal figure to Nancy. She provides emotional support and practical assistance, keeping the household running while Nancy pursues the case.

## Allies in the Field

**Helen Corning** — Nancy's friend who accompanies her on several investigative trips, including the visit to Abigail Rowen's cottage.

**Grace and Allie Horner** — The Horner sisters are not just beneficiaries but active allies, providing Nancy with crucial information about the Crowley family history, the existence of a second will, and directing her to Abigail Rowen.

**The Turner Sisters** — Edna and Matilda Turner share what they know of Crowley's final intentions and suggest Nancy consult Abigail Rowen, calling her the person who "knew more about his queer ways than any other living person" (009-055).

**William and Fred Mathews** — Once assured Nancy is not a friend of the Tophams, William Mathews "talked frankly of Josiah Crowley and told all that he knew concerning the will" (009-031).

**Abigail Rowen** — Though initially skeptical, she becomes a critical ally, revealing Crowley's secret notebook that ultimately leads Nancy to the hidden will.
