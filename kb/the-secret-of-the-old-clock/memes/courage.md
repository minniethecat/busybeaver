# Courage

**[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]** demonstrates both physical and moral courage throughout her investigation of the **[[the-lost-will|Crowley will mystery]]**.

## Moral Courage

Nancy repeatedly stands up to injustice. At the department store, she testifies truthfully against **[[ada-topham|Ada Topham]]** when Ada falsely accuses a saleswoman of breaking an expensive vase, despite knowing it will deepen the Tophams' enmity toward her. She visits the **[[topham-residence|Topham residence]]** despite having no friendly relationship with the family, using the pretext of selling charity tickets to gather information about the **[[crowley-clock|Crowley clock]]**.

## Physical Courage

Nancy's physical bravery is most evident at the **[[topham-bungalow|Topham bungalow]]** on **[[moon-lake|Moon Lake]]**. When she discovers the cottage has been robbed, she enters anyway. Confronted by the **[[robber-leader|robber leader]]**, she defiantly tells him, "You're nothing but a common thief." After being **[[nancy-trapped-in-closet|locked in a closet]]** and left to starve, she resourcefully uses a closet rod as a lever to pry the door from its hinges.

At **[[the-black-horse|The Black Horse]]** roadhouse, she risks entering a disreputable establishment, locates the robbers' moving van, and retrieves the Crowley clock while the thieves drink nearby. Her father later acknowledges her risk-taking, saying "You took a real risk when you encountered those robbers."

## Recognition

The marshal of **[[melborne|Melborne]]** tells Nancy, "Not many girls would have used their brains the way you did." Her courage is integral to bringing the robbers to justice and ensuring the **[[finding-the-missing-will|recovery of the will]]**.
