# River Heights High School

The high school in **[[river-heights|River Heights]]** is where the social dynamics among the novel's young characters were established. **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]**, **[[helen-corning|Helen Corning]]**, and the **[[topham-family|Topham sisters]]** were all students there.

## Social History

At school, Nancy was "very popular and she boasted many friends." She made "one particular chum, Helen Corning," who would become her close friend and camping companion at **[[camp-avondale|Camp Avondale]]** on **[[moon-lake|Moon Lake]]**.

She also made "two enemies, the Topham sisters." The animosity originated when Nancy "caught Ada in wrongdoing in class and both sisters had placed the blame on Nancy." However, "Helen and two others had come to Nancy's rescue." Since that incident, **[[ada-topham|Ada]]** and **[[isabel-topham|Isabel Topham]]** had been "more hateful to Nancy than ever."

## Character Contrast

The school setting establishes the character contrast that drives much of the novel's conflict: Nancy is honest, popular, and respected, while the Topham sisters are "stupid, as well as arrogant" and "had boasted few friends." These school-age dynamics carry forward into the **[[the-lost-will|Crowley will case]]**, where Nancy's integrity again puts her at odds with the Tophams' dishonesty.
