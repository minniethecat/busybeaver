# Fred Mathews

Fred Mathews is a nephew of [[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]] who, together with his brother [[william-mathews|William]], operates a farm near [[masonville|Masonville]] and [[inheritance|inherit]]s a portion of [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]]'s [[fortune|estate]] under the contested [[will-legal-document|will]] (008-042).

## Role in the Story

Fred Mathews appears as one of the legitimate heirs to [[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]'s [[fortune|estate]]. He and his brother [[william-mathews|William]] are nephews of the deceased, and they are among the relatives who were remembered in [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]]'s true [[will-legal-document|will]] with a bequest of $20,000 (024-041). Together with his brother, Fred expresses genuine surprise at the amount left to them (024-042).

## Character Traits

- Farmer by occupation, working the family farm alongside his brother [[william-mathews|William]] (008-058)
- Modest and hardworking — the farm bears the family name "Mathews" on its mailbox (008-057)
- Unassuming expectations about the [[inheritance|inherit]]ance; the brothers declare they "didn't expect that much" upon learning of their bequest (024-042)

## Relationships

- [[william-mathews]] — Brother and farming partner; the two are always mentioned together (008-042)
- [[josiah-crowley]] — Uncle and benefactor, who left them $20,000 in his [[will-legal-document|will]] (024-041)

## Role in the Mystery

Fred Mathews is one of the rightful heirs whom the true [[will-legal-document|will]] was designed to benefit. Unlike the [[richard-topham|Topham]] family, who claimed the entire [[fortune|estate]] under an earlier [[will-legal-document|will]], Fred and his brother were entirely excluded until [[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]] uncovered the later document. Their inclusion in the rediscovered [[will-legal-document|will]] serves as narrative evidence of [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]]'s true intentions — to distribute his [[fortune|wealth]] among multiple relatives rather than concentrating it with [[topham-family|the Tophams]] (024-041).

> ...the Mathews brothers, William and Fred, arrived. (024-019)

## Narrative Significance

Fred Mathews, together with his brother [[william-mathews|William]], represents the class of modest, rural relatives whom [[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]] genuinely wished to provide for. The brothers' surprise at the size of their bequest underscores the contrast between their humble expectations and [[topham-family|the Tophams]]' sense of entitlement. Their presence in the resolution scene (Chapter XXIV) provides closure to the [[inheritance|inherit]]ance plotline, affirming that [[fairness|justice]] has been served for all of [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]]'s intended beneficiaries (024-042).
