# Determination

**[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]**'s determination is the driving force behind the resolution of the **[[the-lost-will|Crowley will mystery]]**. Despite repeated setbacks and discouragement from others, she refuses to give up until justice is served.

## Early Setbacks

Nancy faces skepticism from the start. Her father, **[[carson-drew|Carson Drew]]**, warns her that finding the will is as difficult as "looking for a needle in a haystack." After interviewing the **[[crowley-relatives|Crowley relatives]]** and learning nothing definitive, Nancy admits to feeling discouraged: "I'm no closer to finding the will than I ever was."

## Perseverance

Rather than abandon her quest, Nancy pushes forward. She visits **[[abigail-rowen|Abigail Rowen]]** as a "last hope" and, through her compassionate care of the injured woman, unlocks the vital clue about the **[[crowley-notebook|notebook]]** and the **[[crowley-clock|family clock]]**.

Nancy's determination is further tested at **[[moon-lake|Moon Lake]]**, where she endures being **[[nancy-trapped-in-closet|locked in a closet]]** by robbers, escapes using a wooden rod as a lever, and then pursues the thieves to recover the clock. At the **[[the-black-horse|Black Horse]]** roadhouse, she risks discovery to retrieve the clock from the moving van.

## Resolution

Nancy's persistence pays off when she finds **[[crowley-notebook|Josiah Crowley's notebook]]** hidden inside the clock, leading to the **[[finding-the-missing-will|recovery of the will]]** from the **[[masonville-national-bank|Masonville National Bank]]**. Her father acknowledges her achievement, telling her he "couldn't have done better myself."
