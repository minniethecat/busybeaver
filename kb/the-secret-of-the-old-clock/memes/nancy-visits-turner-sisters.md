# Nancy Visits the Turner Sisters

After visiting the **[[mathews-farm|Mathews brothers]]** along the **[[river-road|River Road]]**, **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]** continues to **[[masonville|Masonville]]** to interview **[[matilda-turner|Matilda]]** and **[[edna-turner|Edna Turner]]**, two of **[[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]**'s cousins.

## The Visit

The Turner sisters, "regular old maids, but awfully nice," live together at the outskirts of Masonville. They greet Nancy cordially when they learn the purpose of her visit. Although "not in poverty, they did not have any more money than they needed."

Nancy spends nearly an hour at their home. The sisters answer her questions willingly but, like the **[[william-mathews|Mathews brothers]]**, cannot provide information with "a direct bearing on the missing will."

## Key Contribution

While the Turners cannot locate the will, they reinforce the **[[crowley-relatives|relatives]]**' conviction that Crowley intended to disinherit the **[[topham-family|Tophams]]**. More importantly, they suggest Nancy visit **[[abigail-rowen|Abigail Rowen]]**, telling her: "She took care of Josiah once when he was sick, and he thought a lot of her. She knew more about his queer ways than any other living person."

This recommendation proves crucial — the visit to Abigail leads Nancy to the **[[crowley-clock|clock]]** and **[[crowley-notebook|notebook]]** clues.
