# Antagonists

Nancy Drew's investigation in *The Secret of the Old Clock* faces opposition from multiple quarters. The Topham family, having manipulated Josiah Crowley into leaving them his fortune, forms the primary antagonistic force. But as the mystery deepens, a second set of adversaries emerges — the robbers who have been systematically ransacking the homes of Crowley's relatives, searching for the very will Nancy seeks.

## The Topham Family — Legal Antagonists

The Tophams represent institutional opposition: they hold the legal claim to the fortune and will use every advantage of their wealth and position to defend it.

- **Richard Topham** — The patriarch, described as "an old skinflint who made his money by gambling on the stock exchange" (001-021). He orchestrated the scheme to secure Crowley's will.
- **Cora Topham** — Richard's wife, "a vapid social climber" (001-021) whose ambition drives the family's grasping behavior.
- **Isabel Topham** — The elder daughter, "rather pretty in a vapid sort of way" but "lacked character" (007-015). She has "acquired an artificial manner of speaking which was both irritating and amusing."
- **Ada Topham** — The younger daughter, equally haughty and dismissive of those beneath her social standing.

## The Robbers — Criminal Antagonists

A second, more dangerous set of adversaries emerges in the novel's second half: a gang of robbers who have been breaking into the homes of Crowley's relatives, searching for the hidden will or anything of value connected to the inheritance. Their leader orchestrates the thefts while his accomplices carry out the actual burglaries.

- **The Robber Leader** — Directs the criminal operation targeting Crowley relatives' homes
- **Jeff Tucker** — Initially presented as suspicious but ultimately revealed to be a victim of circumstance

## Minor Adversaries

- **The Topham Butler** — Loyal to his employers, he obstructs Nancy's investigation at the Topham residence
- **The Topham Lawyer** — Provides legal cover for the family's claim to the fortune

## Thematic Role

The antagonists serve two narrative functions: the Tophams represent the corruption of wealth and social climbing, while the robbers provide the physical danger that makes the mystery a genuine adventure. Together, they ensure Nancy must fight on both legal and physical fronts to secure justice for the deserving relatives.
