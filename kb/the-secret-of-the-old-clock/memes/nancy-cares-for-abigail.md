# Nancy Cares for Abigail Rowen

When **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]** visits **[[abigail-rowen|Abigail Rowen]]**'s dilapidated cottage on the **[[west-lake-road|West Lake Road]]**, she finds the elderly woman in desperate circumstances. Abigail has fallen down the cellar stairs, injuring her hip and spraining her ankle, and has been lying on her couch for at least a day without medical attention.

## Nancy's Response

Finding Abigail unable to walk and in pain, Nancy immediately takes charge. She:
- Arranges pillows to make Abigail more comfortable
- Drives to the nearest store and purchases nearly ten dollars' worth of groceries
- Stops at a drug store for bandages and liniment
- Bathes and bandages Abigail's swollen ankle
- Makes tea and a nourishing meal
- Raises the drawn shades to let in sunshine

## The Vital Disclosure

While Nancy cares for her, Abigail gradually reveals what she knows about **[[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]**'s affairs. She confirms seeing the second will "with her own eyes" and recalls that Crowley mentioned writing its location in a little notebook. When the **[[mantel-clock-abigail|mantel clock]]** chimes three, the sound triggers Abigail's memory: the will's location is connected to a clock and a **[[crowley-notebook|notebook]]**.

This revelation is the critical breakthrough that leads Nancy to the **[[crowley-clock|Crowley family clock]]** and ultimately to the **[[finding-the-missing-will|recovery of the will]]**.
