# Crowley Clock Discovery

The moment when Nancy Drew realizes that the Crowley family clock — mentioned cryptically by Abigail Rowen — contains not only the hidden will but a notebook revealing Josiah Crowley's safe deposit box alias. (010-013) (022-023)

## Summary

Abigail Rowen had been the first to mention the clock, whispering the word during one of Nancy's visits to her rundown [[abigail-rowen-cottage|cottage]]. (010-001) But it was not the mantel clock she initially suspected — she studied it and realized it was the wrong one. (010-012) This misdirection is key: Crowley owned multiple clocks, and only one held his secret.

> "The clock," Abigail whispered so softly that Nancy could scarcely hear. The old woman's eyes were distant, as though she were looking at something far beyond the walls of her cottage. (010-001)

Nancy later wonders "if the notebook had been hidden in the clock," connecting Abigail's clue with the physical object recovered from the [[truck-pursuit|moving van]]. (010-013) Her intuition proves correct. When the clock is finally examined after being recovered from the robbers' van, it yields not only the second will — dated later than the one held by the Tophams — but a notebook containing the name "Josiah Harkston." (022-023)

> The clock's casing, when carefully pried open, revealed a compartment that had been sealed for years. Inside were the documents that would change everything. (022-023)

"Josiah Harkston" was Crowley's alias — the name he used for his safe deposit box at the Masonville National Bank. This discovery is the culmination of Nancy's detective work: every clue, from Abigail's whispered memories to the investigation of the [[bungalow-robbery]], has led to this moment. The notebook also confirms the testimony of Doctor Nesbitt, who had witnessed the second will. (023-060)

## Location

The clock itself was recovered from the robbers' moving van near [[garwin-road]] (see [[truck-pursuit]]). The safe deposit box it revealed was at the Masonville National Bank.

## Participants

- [[nancy-drew]] — Makes the discovery and connects the clues

- [[josiah-crowley]] — Hid the will and notebook in the clock before his death
- [[doctor-nesbitt]] — Witnessed the second will; his testimony is validated by the notebook

## Investigation Significance

The clock discovery represents the detective's moment of synthesis — when scattered clues coalesce into a single solution. Nancy had heard about the clock from Abigail, learned of the alias from bank records, and recovered the physical clock from the robbers. Only by holding all three threads simultaneously could she see that they were one thread: the clock contained the alias that opened the deposit box that held the second will.

## Plot Significance

The clock discovery is the pivot point of the entire novel. It transforms Nancy's investigation from a search based on hearsay and suspicion to one grounded in physical, irrefutable evidence. The clock itself becomes symbolic — this ordinary household object, overlooked by the Tophams who had claimed everything of value from Crowley's estate, contains the document that will strip them of their fortune.

Nancy's decision to keep the clock as a "trophy of my first venture as a detective" at the novel's end closes the narrative loop: the object of the search becomes the symbol of its success. (025-039)
