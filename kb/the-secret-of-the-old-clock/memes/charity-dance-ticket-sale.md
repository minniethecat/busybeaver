# Charity Dance Ticket Sale

Nancy Drew's cover operation for her investigation — selling tickets for a charity dance allows her to visit the Topham bungalow and other Moon Lake locations while gathering information. (010-042) (011-006)

## Summary

Nancy takes on the task of selling tickets for a charity dance benefiting a local cause, which provides a legitimate — and ingenious — reason to visit the [[topham-family|Topham family]] at their Moon Lake bungalow. (010-042) The charity is genuine, but Nancy's motives are dual-purpose: she needs to observe the Tophams up close, and a ticket sale is the perfect pretext.

> She "regarded the charity tickets meditatively" — seeing them as both a genuine charitable effort and a useful investigative cover. (010-063)

At the Topham bungalow, Nancy makes her pitch with practiced charm: "I am selling tickets for the charity dance. It's one of the most important social events of the season." (011-006) The Topham sisters — Ada and Isabel — receive her with the cold indifference they show to anyone they consider beneath them. Their response is dismissive and revealing:

> "Give it to the charity," one of the sisters said, handing over a small sum with visible contempt, as though the act of giving were beneath her dignity. (011-039)

The Tophams' attitude toward charity — treating it as an annoyance rather than a duty — tells Nancy more about their character than any direct questioning could have. Despite the rudeness, Nancy successfully uses the ticket sales as entrée into Moon Lake society, allowing her to observe the Tophams' lifestyle and gather information from neighbors and witnesses.

[[helen-corning]], Nancy's friend who accompanies her on some of the sales visits, provides both companionship and a second set of eyes. Together they canvass the Moon Lake area, and Nancy shares the results of her investigation with Helen afterward. (012-053)

## Investigative Value

The charity ticket sale is more than a convenient plot device — it is a lesson in Nancy's investigative philosophy. Rather than sneaking around or resorting to deception, she creates a legitimate reason to be where she needs to be. The approach is ethical (the charity is real and she does sell tickets), effective (she gains access to the Topham bungalow), and character-revealing (the Tophams' response to charity speaks volumes).

The information Nancy gathers during the ticket sales — about the Tophams' lifestyle, their reputation among neighbors, and their dismissive attitude toward the community — helps her build the psychological profile that will later help her predict their moves in the will dispute.

## Plot Significance

The charity dance ticket sale is the novel's most elegant plotting device. It gives Nancy a legitimate reason to approach the Tophams, observe their lifestyle, and enter Moon Lake society — all while appearing to do nothing more than sell tickets for a good cause. The Tophams' dismissive response to the charity request further damns their character in the reader's eyes.

The ticket sale thus functions simultaneously as plot mechanism (gaining access), character revelation (the Tophams' contempt for charity), and thematic reinforcement (the contrast between Nancy's community spirit and the Tophams' selfishness). It is a masterclass in how to structure an investigation scene.
