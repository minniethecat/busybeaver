# Clock Recovery from the Robbers

Nancy Drew locates the robbers' moving van hidden in a barn, sneaks inside, and recovers the Crowley mantel clock — a daring retrieval that secures the case's most crucial piece of evidence.

## Summary

After tracking the moving van to a barn near Garwin, Nancy Drew searches the vehicle for the Crowley clock (019-010). She finds the mantel clock wrapped in a protective blanket on a center table at the very front of the van (019-027). Nancy scrambles over furniture to reach it, snatching the clock and blanket (019-028). Carrying the clock, she flees into the nearby woods just as the robbers return, narrowly escaping discovery (020-021, 020-023).

Later, examining the clock, she shakes it and hears a rattling sound, then removes the clock face to find a tiny blue notebook attached to a hook inside (020-035, 020-040). This notebook contains the alias "Josiah Harkston" — the key that unlocks the mystery of the safe deposit box and the missing second will.

## Investigation Context

This event forms part of the larger pattern of criminal activity that Nancy Drew systematically uncovers. Each incident reveals another layer of the conspiracy surrounding the Crowley estate, building toward the eventual resolution of the case.

## Plot Significance

The physical recovery of the Crowley clock is the most dangerous action Nancy takes in the investigation. By retrieving the clock before the robbers can dispose of it, she preserves the evidence that ultimately cracks the case. The tiny blue notebook found inside the clock contains the alias "Josiah Harkston," leading to the safe deposit box and the missing will.

This event is the turning point of the entire mystery. Before the clock recovery, Nancy had only suspicions and circumstantial clues. After finding the notebook, she has concrete evidence that leads directly to the resolution. The clock itself — an ordinary mantel clock that the robbers likely considered worthless — becomes the case's most valuable object through its hidden contents.
