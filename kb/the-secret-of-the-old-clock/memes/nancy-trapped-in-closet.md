# Nancy Trapped in the Closet

A tense episode during Nancy's investigation of the Topham bungalow in which she hides in a bedroom closet to avoid detection, only to be discovered and locked inside by the burglars. The incident leads to her temporary imprisonment in the bungalow and the suspenseful chapters that follow.

## Summary

While searching the Topham bungalow for evidence related to the Crowley inheritance, Nancy hears someone approaching. Finding no better hiding place, she squeezes into a bedroom closet. As the intruder — one of the burglars — moves about the room, Nancy tries to remain silent. However, she inadvertently sneezes, revealing her location. The burglar flings open the closet door, confronts her, and after a tense interrogation, shoves her back into the closet and locks her in. Nancy is left trapped in the dark closet, unable to escape, setting up the desperate situation of the following chapter. (015-009) (015-026) (015-027) (015-031) (015-050)

## Plot Significance

The closet trapping represents the point in the story where Nancy's investigation takes a dangerous turn from observation to personal peril. Her discovery by the burglars escalates the conflict: she is no longer a distant observer but a captive who has seen the criminals' faces and heard their plans. This event directly leads to the "Left to Starve" chapter, where Nancy and Helen Corning (who later arrives and is also captured) must find a way to escape the bungalow and alert the authorities. The closet scene also demonstrates Nancy's courage — even when discovered and physically threatened, she does not reveal what she has learned about the Crowley clock or the burglars' identities.
