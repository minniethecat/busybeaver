# Closet Rod

The closet rod is a narrow wooden rod suspended overhead in the bedroom closet of the **[[topham-bungalow|Topham bungalow]]** at **[[moon-lake|Moon Lake]]**. Originally used for hanging clothes, it becomes **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]**'s means of escape after the **[[robber-leader|robber leader]]** locks her in the closet.

## The Escape

After being **[[nancy-trapped-in-closet|trapped in the closet]]** and left to starve, Nancy searches desperately for a way out. Her attempts to pick the lock with a hairpin fail. Then her head strikes the rod overhead.

She tears it down and discovers it is "too long to use as a ram" but can be used as a wedge. Inserting it "in the crack between the hinges," she throws all her weight against it. The hinges begin to give way.

## Success

The first hinge tears from the casing and the door sags. As a second hinge gives way, Nancy hears footsteps — **[[jeff-tucker|Jeff Tucker]]**, the caretaker, has returned. He throws himself against the door, momentarily stunning her, but after recognizing her feminine voice, he lets her out (though he briefly misplaces the key).

## Symbolism

The closet rod symbolizes Nancy's **[[courage|courage]]** and resourcefulness under extreme pressure. Trapped in darkness with no obvious escape, she uses observation, physical strength, and knowledge of basic physics (the lever principle) to free herself.
