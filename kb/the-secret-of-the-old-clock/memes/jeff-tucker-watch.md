# Jeff Tucker's Pocket Watch

**[[jeff-tucker|Jeff Tucker]]**'s pocket watch is "a large, silver, open-face watch" that, like its owner, has proven unreliable. The watch serves as a comic symbol of Jeff's negligent caretaking of the **[[topham-bungalow|Topham bungalow]]** at **[[moon-lake|Moon Lake]]**.

## The Watch

Jeff proudly displays the watch during his conversation with **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]** after the **[[bungalow-robbery|robbery]]**. He punctuates his remarks by "taking a large, silver, open-face watch from his pocket and holding it to his ear." The watch, he discovers, has run down: "She's done run down too. I reckon dat I was in dat ho‑tel when I should o' been tendin' to dis watch."

## Symbolism

The stopped watch parallels Jeff's failure as caretaker. Just as he neglected to wind his timepiece, he neglected his duty to protect the Topham property. The watch's condition is the result of the same lapse that enabled the robbery — Jeff's **[[drugging|drunken absence]]** from the bungalow.

## Comic Relief

Jeff's watch provides a moment of comic relief in the tense aftermath of Nancy's closet ordeal. His character, with his dialect, his "rye-laden breath," and his lament over the unwound watch, brings levity to the story while reinforcing the lesson about the consequences of neglecting one's responsibilities.
