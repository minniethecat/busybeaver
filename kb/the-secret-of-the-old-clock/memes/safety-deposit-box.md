# Safety Deposit Box 148

Safety deposit box number 148 at the **[[masonville-national-bank|Masonville National Bank]]** in **[[masonville|Masonville]]** is the hiding place of **[[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]**'s **[[the-lost-will|second will]]**.

## The Concealment

Crowley rented the box under the **[[assumed-identity|assumed name]]** "Josiah Harkston," a pseudonym that prevented anyone searching for a box under his real name from finding it. **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]** later reflected: "Who would have ever thought of looking for it in a safety deposit box under the name of Josiah Harkston?"

The rent on the box had gone unpaid for the past year, and the bank had been trying to contact "Harkston" without success.

## Discovery

The box's existence and contents are revealed in the **[[crowley-notebook|Crowley notebook]]** found inside the **[[crowley-clock|family clock]]**. The notebook entry reads: "My last will and testament will be found in safety deposit box number 148 in the Masonville National Bank. The box is under the name of Josiah Harkston."

## Access

**[[carson-drew|Carson Drew]]** obtains a **[[court-order|court order]]** to access the box. Crowley had left a sealed envelope with one of the two keys at the bank. The bank president, after verifying the court order, releases the key to Drew, and together they open the box in the vault. Inside they find only one document: the second will, comprising several pages written in Crowley's cramped hand.

The box thus contains the instrument that overturns the Topham inheritance and delivers **[[justice|justice]]** to the deserving relatives.
