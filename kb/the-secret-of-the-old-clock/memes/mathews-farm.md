# Mathews Farm

The farm on River Road belonging to William and Fred Mathews, nephews of [[josiah-crowley]] who "everyone thought would get something" from his estate. [[nancy-drew]] visited the farm during her round of interviews with Crowley's relatives, and the brothers were ultimately vindicated when the discovered will awarded them twenty thousand dollars each. (008-044)

## Location

The Mathews farm is situated on River Road, approximately five miles from the [[horner-farm|Horner farm]] in the direction of Masonville. A mailbox bearing the name "Mathews" marked the property, and the farmhouse was set back a short distance from the road, reached by a narrow lane. (008-059)

## Description

The farmhouse was modest — typical of the poor farmland along the River Road, where spring flooding from the Muskoka River made agriculture unreliable and kept land values low. William Mathews, the farmer who greeted Nancy, was initially distrustful of her but warmed once he confirmed she was no friend of the [[topham-family]]. (008-059) (008-060)

## Role in the Mystery

The Mathews farm was one stop in Nancy's systematic investigation of all of Josiah Crowley's relatives. William Mathews, though initially distrustful, confirmed the family's belief that Crowley had made a second will and that the Tophams had no rightful claim to the entire estate. Like the other relatives, the Mathews brothers had filed a complaint with the courthouse but lacked proof. Their inclusion in the discovered will — $20,000 each — validated their claims and Nancy's investigation. (008-060) (008-061) (024-059)
