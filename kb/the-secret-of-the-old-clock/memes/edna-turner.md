# Edna Turner

One of the two Turner sisters — elderly neighbors of [[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]] who, like the [[horner-sisters|Horner sisters]] and [[abigail-rowen|Abigail Rowen]], were promised portions of his [[fortune|estate]] and can testify that [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]] intended to change his [[will-legal-document|will]]. (008-041) (008-071)

## Role in the Story

Edna Turner and her sister [[matilda-turner|Matilda]] are mentioned as fellow deserving heirs — "regular old maids, but awfully nice" — who, like the [[horner-sisters|Horner sisters]], were promised a [[portion|share]] of [[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]'s fortune. (008-041) [[allie-horner|Allie Horner]] mentions them as potential witnesses: [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] "must see the Turner sisters" who "insisted that [[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]] had promised them a portion of his [[fortune|money]]." (008-071) Their similarity to the [[horner-sisters|Horner sisters]] — elderly, poor, and morally deserving — reinforces the pattern of [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]]'s true intentions and the breadth of the in[[fairness|justice]] caused by the first [[will-legal-document|will]]. Edna and [[matilda-turner|Matilda]] are part of the chorus of witnesses whose collective testimony establishes that the first [[will-legal-document|will]] did not reflect [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]]'s final wishes.

## Character Traits

- Deserving and kind: described as "regular old maids, but awfully nice" (008-041)
- Victim of in[[fairness|justice]]: promised a [[portion|share]] of [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]]'s [[fortune|estate]] but received nothing
- Neighborly: had a long-standing relationship with [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]] as neighbors

## Role in the Mystery

Edna Turner and her sister [[matilda-turner|Matilda]] provide corroborating testimony. They are part of the pattern: multiple independent witnesses — the [[horner-sisters|Horner sisters]], [[abigail-rowen|Abigail Rowen]], the Turner sisters — all report that [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]] intended to change his [[will-legal-document|will]]. This pattern of consistent testimony from unrelated sources is what gives [[nancy-drew|Nancy]]'s [[detective-work|investigation]] its evidentiary weight and convinces her that the [[crowley-will-document|second will]] must exist.

## Narrative Significance

The Turner sisters, like the [[horner-sisters|Horner sisters]] and [[abigail-rowen|Abigail Rowen]], represent the deserving poor whose claims the legal system has failed. Their multiplication — not one deserving heir but several — reinforces the scale of the in[[fairness|justice]] and the importance of [[nancy-drew|Nancy]]'s work.
