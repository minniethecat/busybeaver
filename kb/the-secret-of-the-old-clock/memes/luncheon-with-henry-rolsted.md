# Luncheon with Henry Rolsted

At **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]**'s urging, **[[carson-drew|Carson Drew]]** invites **[[henry-rolsted|Henry Rolsted]]**, an attorney specializing in wills and legal documents, to luncheon at the **[[royal-hotel|Royal Hotel]]** in **[[river-heights|River Heights]]**.

## The Setup

Nancy had learned that her father once overheard **[[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]** discussing a will with Rolsted at the **[[first-national-bank|First National Bank]]**. Carson Drew, an "old friend and an old college classmate" of Rolsted, is reluctant to pry into professional confidences but Nancy's persistence prevails.

## The Luncheon

Over coffee, Carson Drew skillfully steers the conversation toward "strange cases" and mentions the Crowley estate. Rolsted hesitates, then, after being assured of Nancy's discretion, reveals critical information:

- About a year before his death, Crowley came to Rolsted's office wanting to "draw up a new will"
- Crowley "indicated that he intended to cut the Tophams off without a cent"
- He "expressed a desire to write the will himself" and asked Rolsted for guidance
- Rolsted told him exactly how to proceed but never saw the completed document — Crowley "never came back"

## Significance

This luncheon confirms Nancy's intuition that a second will existed. Rolsted's testimony provides the strongest evidence yet that Crowley intended to disinherit the **[[topham-family|Tophams]]**. The information launches Nancy's investigation in earnest, leading her to interview the **[[crowley-relatives|other relatives]]**.
