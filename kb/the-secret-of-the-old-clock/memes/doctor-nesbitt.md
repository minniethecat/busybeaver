# Doctor Nesbitt

A physician who served as one of two witnesses to [[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]'s [[crowley-will-document|second will]] — his death soon after the signing created a legal obstacle to proving [[crowley-will-document|the will]]'s authenticity. (023-060)

## Role in the Story

Doctor Nesbitt appears only as a name in the novel, but his role as a witness is crucial to the plot. When [[judge-hartgrave|Judge Hartgrave]] examines the newly discovered [[crowley-will-document|second will]], he "picked up the last page of [[crowley-will-document|the will]] and studied it closely" and noted: "I see Doctor Nesbitt signed as a witness." (023-060) The fact that Nesbitt is deceased complicates the legal validation of the document, as surviving witnesses are typically called upon to testify to the authenticity of a contested [[will-legal-document|will]]. His death, combined with the anonymity of the second witness ([[thomas-wackley|Thomas Wackley]]), adds tension to the question of whether the [[crowley-will-document|second will]] can be upheld in court.

## Character Traits

- A professional man of sufficient standing to serve as a [[witness|legal witness]]
- Deceased by the time [[crowley-will-document|the will]] is discovered, depriving the court of his testimony
- His role as witness implies he had contact with [[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]] in his final days

## Relationships

- [[josiah-crowley]] — The testator whose [[will-legal-document|will]] he [[witness|witnessed]]
- [[thomas-wackley]] — The other witness to [[crowley-will-document|the will]]
- [[judge-hartgrave]] — The judge who examines [[crowley-will-document|the will]] and notes Nesbitt's signature

## Role in the Mystery

Doctor Nesbitt's signature on the [[crowley-will-document|second will]] is a key piece of evidence. His death before [[crowley-will-document|the will]]'s discovery creates legal uncertainty — [[topham-family|the Tophams]] might have challenged [[crowley-will-document|the will]] on the grounds that no living witness could authenticate it. However, the combination of both witnesses' signatures, along with [[crowley-will-document|the will]] being found in [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]]'s safety deposit box under his alias "[[josiah-harkston|Josiah Harkston]]," provides sufficient corroboration.

> "I see Doctor Nesbitt signed as a witness." — Judge Hartgrave, examining the will. (023-060)

## Narrative Significance

Doctor Nesbitt represents one of the many small but essential links in the chain of evidence that [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] assembles. His posthumous role underscores the fragility of legal [[evidence|proof]] — how easily a just outcome can be lost when key witnesses die before they can speak.
