# Estate

The total property, assets, and debts left by Josiah Crowley upon his death — the settlement of this estate is the legal process that occupies the lawyers, the Tophams, and Nancy Drew throughout the novel. (001-026)

## Definition

An estate is the net worth of a person at any point in time, alive or dead. In the context of probate law, it is the collection of assets and liabilities left by a deceased person, which must be settled — debts paid, assets distributed — before heirs can receive their inheritances. Carson Drew describes the Crowley estate as "a considerable one," and the Tophams are impatient for its settlement: "The estate hasn't been settled, but they're counting on the fortune already." (001-026) (002-047)

## Role in the Story

The unsettled estate creates vulnerability and urgency. Until the estate is settled, the Tophams cannot legally access all the funds, yet they are already spending against their expected inheritance. (002-047) The estate's size — "nearly half of the estate" going to the Horners under the second will — demonstrates the magnitude of what is at stake. (024-034) The Tophams' desperation to "speed up the settlement of the estate" reveals their financial precariousness, while the eventual settlement under the second will restores justice. (022-043)
