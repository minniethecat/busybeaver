# Drew Home

The Drew family residence in River Heights — the stable domestic center from which Nancy launches her investigation, maintained by the loyal housekeeper Hannah Gruen. (002-010)

## Location

The Drew home is located in River Heights, within walking distance of the "business section" of town. (006-005) It is a comfortable middle-class residence appropriate to a successful lawyer's family.

## Description

The Drew household employs "one servant, Hannah Gruen, an elderly maid of all work who had been with the family since before the death of Mrs. Drew." (002-011) The home has a library where Nancy and her father discuss the Crowley case, a kitchen where Hannah works, and a telephone — a crucial tool for Nancy's investigation. The home operates as a well-functioning unit, with Nancy consulting Hannah "concerning the work of the day" before leaving on her adventures. (002-010)

## Role in the Mystery

The Drew home is the command center of Nancy's investigation. All strategic discussions with her father happen here, and her departures and returns mark the rhythm of the investigation. The home's stability — a loving father, a loyal housekeeper, a comfortable library — provides the secure base from which Nancy can venture into the uncertainties of detective work.
