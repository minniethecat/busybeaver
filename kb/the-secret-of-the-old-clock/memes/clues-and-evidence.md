# Clues and Evidence

Nancy Drew's investigation in *The Secret of the Old Clock* is built on the systematic collection and interpretation of clues. This page catalogs the key pieces of evidence — physical objects, documents, witness testimony, and circumstantial findings — that together unravel the mystery of Josiah Crowley's missing will.

## The Core Evidence Chain

Nancy's case follows a logical chain of evidence, each discovery building on the last:

| # | Clue | Discovery | Chapter | Significance |
|---|------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| 1 | **Testimony of the Horner Sisters** | Interview at their home | II–IV | First confirmation that a second will existed |
| 2 | **Mr. Rolsted's Account** | Legal consultation | V | Crowley consulted a lawyer about writing a new will |
| 3 | **Testimony of the Turner Sisters** | Interview in Masonville | IX | Further confirmation; direction to Abigail Rowen |
| 4 | **Testimony of the Mathews Brothers** | Interview at their farm | IX | Details of Crowley's final intentions |
| 5 | **Abigail Rowen's Disclosure** | Interview at her cottage | X | The critical lead — Crowley's secret notebook |
| 6 | **[[crowley-notebook]]** | Found through Abigail's guidance | XIV | Cryptic entries pointing to the will's location |
| 7 | **[[crowley-clock]]** | Found during the search | XX | The physical hiding place of the true will |
| 8 | **[[crowley-will-document]]** | Inside the clock | XXIV | The true will, read aloud by Carson Drew |

## Types of Evidence

**Documentary Evidence:**
- The Topham will (the initial, disputed document)
- [[crowley-will-document]] (the true will, hidden in the clock)
- [[crowley-notebook]] (Crowley's personal notes with cryptic entries)

**Physical Evidence:**
- [[crowley-clock]] (the antique clock concealing the will)
- The moving van used by the robbers
- Items stolen from relatives' homes

**Testimonial Evidence:**
- Statements from each of the Crowley relatives
- Mr. Rolsted's legal testimony about Crowley's intentions
- Jeff Tucker's account of the robbery operation

**Circumstantial Evidence:**
- The Tophams' suspicious behavior and sudden "change of heart"
- The pattern of break-ins at relatives' homes
- The robbers' persistent search for something of value
