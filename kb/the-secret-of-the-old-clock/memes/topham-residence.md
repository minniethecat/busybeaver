# Topham Residence

The River Heights home of the [[topham-family]] on Highland Boulevard — a large, pretentious mansion whose ostentatious appearance mirrors the family's combination of wealth and poor taste. [[nancy-drew]] visited the house under the pretext of selling subscriptions in order to gain entry and search for clues. (011-002) (011-003)

## Location

The Topham residence stands on Highland Boulevard in River Heights, set back from the street and elevated above its neighbors. The address is well-known in River Heights, though the family is not warmly received in the community's better homes. (011-002) (011-017)

## Description

The house was a large, pretentious affair of nondescript architectural type. It was set back from the street and seemed to look down aloofly upon the surrounding homes. The lawn had been aggressively "landscaped" — crowded with sundials, benches, bird houses, and statues in an effort to "do it in proper style." (011-003)

> "She surveyed the structure with distaste. The house was a large, pretentious affair of nondescript type." (011-002) (011-003)

Inside, the front hall was where [[ada-topham]] and [[isabel-topham]] were overheard complaining about their social calls, unaware of Nancy's presence as a visitor. The home also contained the furniture that had belonged to [[josiah-crowley]] before his death — furniture the Tophams had "practically confiscated" when they induced him to live with them. (011-023) (024-021)

## Role in the Mystery

The Topham residence is central to the mystery in two ways. First, it was [[josiah-crowley]]'s final home — the Tophams induced him to live with them three years before his death, and reportedly persuaded him to make a will in their favor. After his death, they "practically confiscated" his furniture, which included [[crowley-clock|the old clock]] where Nancy eventually suspected the will might be hidden. (001-005) (024-021)

Second, Nancy's visit to the house under a sales ruse was her attempt to gain physical access to search for the clock and any hidden will — a risky gambit that required her to directly deceive [[ada-topham|Ada]] and [[isabel-topham]]. (011-002) (011-023)
