# William Mathews

William Mathews is a nephew of [[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]], a farmer near [[masonville|Masonville]], and one of the heirs in [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]]'s true [[will-legal-document|will]]. [[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]] visits his farm during her [[detective-work|investigation]] of the [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]] relatives (008-058).

## Role in the Story

William Mathews, together with his brother [[fred-mathews|Fred]], is identified by [[grace-horner|Grace Horner]] as one of [[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]'s nephews who should have been remembered in [[crowley-will-document|the will]] (008-042). [[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]] visits the Mathews farm to interview the brothers, finding a farmstead marked by the family name (008-057). Learning that the farmer was William Mathews, "[[nancy-drew|Nancy]] quickly explained her mission" (008-058). At the resolution of the case, William and [[fred-mathews|Fred]] arrive to hear the reading of the true [[will-legal-document|will]], where they are awarded $20,000 (024-041).

## Character Traits

- A working farmer who maintains the Mathews family farm with his brother (008-057)
- Cooperative with [[nancy-drew|Nancy]]'s [[detective-work|investigation]] — he listens to her explanation of her mission (008-058)
- Humble and unassuming; like his brother, he did not expect the generous bequest (024-042)

## Role in the Mystery

William Mathews is part of the network of [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]] relatives whom [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] must investigate to trace the [[crowley-will-document|missing will]]. His farm visit (Chapter VIII) is part of [[nancy-drew|Nancy]]'s systematic effort to interview all of [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]]'s known relatives and potential heirs. The fact that William and [[fred-mathews|Fred]] were entirely cut out of the earlier [[will-legal-document|will]] — which gave everything to [[topham-family|the Tophams]] — helps establish the in[[fairness|justice]] that the true [[will-legal-document|will]] was designed to correct (024-041).

> Learning that the farmer was William Mathews, Nancy quickly explained her mission. (008-058)

## Narrative Significance

The Mathews brothers represent the rural, working-class side of [[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]'s family — relatives who labored on a farm rather than living in town [[fortune|wealth]] like [[topham-family|the Tophams]]. Their inclusion in the true [[will-legal-document|will]], and their humble reaction to it, reinforces the novel's thematic contrast between entitled greed ([[topham-family|the Tophams]]) and deserving modesty (the Mathews brothers and [[edna-turner|Turner sisters]]). William's brief but cooperative interaction with [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] also illustrates her methodical approach to [[detective-work|investigation]] — leaving no relative uninterviewed (008-058).
