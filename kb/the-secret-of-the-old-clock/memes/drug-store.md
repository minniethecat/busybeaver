# Drug Store

The drug store in the nearest town to **[[abigail-rowen|Abigail Rowen]]**'s cottage on the **[[west-lake-road|West Lake Road]]** is where **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]** purchases medical supplies during her **[[nancy-cares-for-abigail|emergency visit to Abigail]]**.

## The Visit

After finding Abigail injured and unable to walk following her fall down the cellar stairs, Nancy insists on proper medical supplies. She drives to "the nearest drug store" and purchases "bandages and liniments." She also buys groceries from a nearby general store, spending nearly ten dollars of her own money since Abigail's cash jar contains "less than five dollars" — "every cent poor Abigail has in the world."

## Abigail's Care

With these supplies, Nancy "bathed the swollen ankle and bound it neatly with the clean bandage." She also builds a fire, makes tea, and prepares a nourishing meal. Though Abigail refuses a doctor because she "can't afford it," Nancy's care significantly improves her condition.

## Significance

The drug store visit illustrates Nancy's practical compassion and resourcefulness. Her willingness to spend her own money and time to help a stranger in need reflects the moral values that drive her investigation of the **[[the-lost-will|Crowley will mystery]]** — a commitment to helping those who cannot help themselves.
