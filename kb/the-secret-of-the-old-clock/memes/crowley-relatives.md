# Crowley Relatives

The extended family network of Josiah Crowley, comprising multiple households who variously provided him care and were intended to receive portions of his fortune before the Topham family manipulated the will in their own favor.

## Definition

The term "Crowley relatives" refers to the several families related to Josiah Crowley by blood or marriage — specifically the Horner sisters, the Turner sisters, the Mathews brothers, and Abigail Rowen — who were the intended beneficiaries of Crowley's fortune. After his wife died during the influenza epidemic following World War I, Crowley "made his home with various relatives" and "preferred to 'visit around'" (001-025). These relatives, though in modest circumstances, provided Crowley with genuine care and hospitality at times when the wealthy Tophams showed no interest in him.

## Role in the Story

The Crowley relatives are the moral counterweight to the Topham family, driving the novel's central conflict over the inheritance. When Crowley had nowhere else to go, "kindly relatives who were scarcely able to have him with them" took him in, and he "openly declared that he intended to make his will in their favor" (001-027). The Tophams' subsequent scheme — feigning interest in Crowley to manipulate him into changing his will — deprived the deserving relatives of what was rightfully theirs. Nancy Drew's investigation systematically visits each relative household, gathering evidence that a second will existed and building the case that the Tophams' claim to the fortune was fraudulent.

## The Relatives

**The Horner Sisters** (Grace and Allie) — Two sisters living in modest circumstances on the River Road. As children they had been "great pets of Crowley's" and maintained a warm relationship with him throughout his life. They filed a claim asserting a later will was made in their favor (004-013).

**The Turner Sisters** (Edna and Matilda) — Cousins of Josiah Crowley living in Masonville "at the outskirts of the city," not in poverty but with no more than they needed (009-051). Matilda Turner suggested Nancy speak with Abigail Rowen, calling her the person who "knew more about his queer ways than any other living person" (009-055).

**The Mathews Brothers** (William and Fred) — Nephews of Josiah Crowley. William Mathews ran a farm, and he "talked frankly of Josiah Crowley" once assured Nancy was not a friend of the Tophams (009-031). Both brothers were named as beneficiaries in the true will.

**Abigail Rowen** — Though not a blood relative, she "took care of Josiah once when he was sick, and he thought a lot of her" (009-053). She was the final relative Nancy visited in her investigation, living in a rundown cottage on West Lake Road.
