# Court Order

The court order is a legal document obtained by **[[carson-drew|Carson Drew]]** from a county judge, authorizing him to open **[[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]**'s **[[safety-deposit-box|safety deposit box]]** at the **[[masonville-national-bank|Masonville National Bank]]**.

## Necessity

When Nancy discovers the **[[crowley-notebook|notebook]]**'s reference to the deposit box, her father points out a legal obstacle: "We haven't authority to open the safety deposit box. It may be necessary to get a court order." Without this document, the bank would not permit access to a box rented under a different name.

## Acquisition

Carson Drew visits the judge early the next morning. After "explaining everything to him," the judge issues the order. Drew keeps it in an inside pocket and presents it at the bank when the president initially refuses access.

The president "examined it for a minute" and then declared: "You are welcome to open the safety deposit box — of course in the presence of a bank official."

## Significance

The court order represents the intersection of Nancy's detective work and formal legal procedure. While Nancy's **[[determination|persistence]]** and **[[detective-intuition|intuition]]** uncovered the will's location, it is her father's legal expertise that enables the final recovery. Together, they successfully retrieve the **[[the-lost-will|second will]]**.
