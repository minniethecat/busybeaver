# Abigail Rowen's Cottage

The dilapidated home of [[abigail-rowen]] on West Lake Road — a square, one-story frame building where the elderly woman lived alone in poverty. It was here that [[nancy-drew]] received the critical information that [[josiah-crowley]] had hidden the location of his will inside [[crowley-clock|an old clock]]. (009-003) (009-004)

## Location

The cottage is located on West Lake Road, several miles from the Horner farm on River Road. Neighbors along the road could direct visitors to it, noting it as "the most rundown house along the road." Nancy drove out from River Heights early one morning in her final quest for information about the Crowley will. (009-005)

## Description

The cottage was a square, one-story frame building standing dejectedly in the center of a yard overgrown with weeds and dandelions. It was sadly in need of paint, with pickets missing from the sagging fence. A rotting boardwalk led to the front porch, offering treacherous footing. (009-003)

> "The place looks deserted, but I'm sure Abigail Rowen must live here." — Nancy Drew (009-004)

Inside, the living room was dreary. [[abigail-rowen]] lay huddled under a ragged old shawl on a couch, her withered face drawn with pain from a fall down the cellar stairs. She had no doctor, no visitors, and had been lying helpless and alone. (009-012) (009-017) (009-019)

## Role in the Mystery

Abigail Rowen's cottage is the location of the single most important clue in the novel. After Nancy tended to the injured Abigail — making her tea, caring for her sprained ankle, and showing her the first kindness she had received in a long time — the old woman, prompted by the mantel clock striking three, began to recall what [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]] had told her about the clock. (009-062) (009-063)

The following day, Abigail finally whispered the words that redirected [[nancy-drew|Nancy]]'s entire investigation: "The clock! That was it! The clock!" (010-001)
