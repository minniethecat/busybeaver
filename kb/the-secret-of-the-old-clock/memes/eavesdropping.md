# Eavesdropping

Eavesdropping serves as a crucial investigative technique for **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]** in her quest to find **[[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]**'s **[[the-lost-will|missing second will]]**. On multiple occasions, Nancy overhears conversations that provide vital clues.

## Key Instances

### Overhearing the Topham Sisters

While walking through the public park in **[[river-heights|River Heights]]**, Nancy spots **[[ada-topham|Ada]]** and **[[isabel-topham|Isabel Topham]]** seated on a bench. By creeping through bushes behind their bench, she overhears their conversation about the **[[the-lost-will|will]]**. Ada expresses worry that **[[carson-drew|Carson Drew]]** might "dig up another will," and Isabel darkly hints that their father would "take care of it" if one surfaced. This conversation reveals to Nancy that the Tophams have not destroyed a second will and are anxious about its possible discovery.

### At the Department Store

While shopping, Nancy overhears **[[ada-topham|Ada]]** telling **[[isabel-topham|Isabel]]** that the Tophams are "counting on the fortune already" and are "mightily worried for fear somebody will show up with a later will." This information, relayed by a saleswoman, confirms Nancy's suspicions.

## Significance

Nancy's eavesdropping yields two critical pieces of information: first, that the Tophams have not destroyed any later will (meaning one may still exist), and second, that they are worried about the claims of the **[[crowley-relatives|other relatives]]**. These insights motivate Nancy to continue her investigation, ultimately leading to the discovery of **[[crowley-notebook|Crowley's notebook]]** and the **[[finding-the-missing-will|recovery of the will]]**.
