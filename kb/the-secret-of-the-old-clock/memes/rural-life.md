# Rural Life

Rural farm life along the **[[river-road|River Road]]** serves as both setting and thematic counterpoint to the urban affluence of **[[river-heights|River Heights]]** in _The Secret of the Old Clock_.

## The Horner Farm

The **[[horner-sisters|Horner sisters]]**, **[[allie-horner|Allie]]** and **[[grace-horner|Grace]]**, live on a rundown farm inherited from their parents. The house is "large and rambling, but apparently it had not been painted in years," with shingles falling from the roof and sagging porches. The sisters survive through hard work: Grace does dressmaking in **[[masonville|Masonville]]** when she can get work, while Allie raises chickens.

The farm's isolation is emphasized — sometimes "a whole week will go by, and we won't see anyone but the postman." The land along the **[[river-road|River Road]]** is considered poor because the **[[muskoka-river|Muskoka River]]** floods in spring, destroying crops. Only "poor folk" live there.

## The Mathews Farm

**[[william-mathews|William]]** and **[[fred-mathews|Fred Mathews]]** operate another farm on the same road, about five miles from the Horners. They too are modest farmers who expected a small inheritance from **[[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]**.

## Transformation

After receiving their **[[inheritance|inheritance]]**, the Horner farm undergoes a dramatic transformation: a fresh coat of white paint, a new green roof, well-kept gardens, new chicken houses, incubators, and a thriving flock of white Leghorns. The farm becomes a symbol of honest work rewarded.
