# Stock Market Speculation

**[[richard-topham|Richard Topham]]** is described as having made his money through "gambling on the stock exchange." His speculative habits play a significant role in the Topham family's eventual downfall.

## Topham's Investments

Throughout the novel, Mr. Topham is frequently absorbed in market reports. When **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]** visits the **[[topham-residence|Topham residence]]** to sell charity dance tickets, Topham immediately "buried himself in the stock page" after making a show of generosity.

## Financial Ruin

After the **[[finding-the-missing-will|discovery of the second will]]** cuts the Tophams out of the **[[fortune|Crowley fortune]]**, **[[carson-drew|Carson Drew]]** reveals that Richard Topham had been "losing heavily in the stock market" and had been obtaining credit from banks "on the strength of the inheritance." He was depending on **[[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]**'s money to cover his losses.

With the loss of the inheritance, the banks reduce Topham's credit. He is forced to give up the **[[topham-residence|family home]]** on the avenue, and the Tophams move to a much smaller house. The speculative approach to wealth — trying to get rich through market gambling and inheritance schemes — is contrasted with the honest labor of the **[[horner-sisters|Horner sisters]]** and the quiet dignity of **[[abigail-rowen|Abigail Rowen]]**.
