# The Hidden Staircase (Foreshadowing)

At the conclusion of _The Secret of the Old Clock_, the narrative looks forward to Nancy's next adventure, creating a bridge to the series' second volume. This metafictional moment suggests that Nancy's life as a detective is just beginning.

## The Final Paragraph

As **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]** gazes at the **[[crowley-clock|Crowley clock]]** — her chosen reward — the narration reveals: "She could not know that before many months had passed, she would be involved in a mystery far more baffling than the one she had just solved. Her adventures, recounted in the next volume of this series, The Hidden Staircase, were before her."

## Narrative Function

The foreshadowing serves multiple purposes:
- It establishes the serial nature of the Nancy Drew series, encouraging readers to continue with subsequent volumes
- It frames the present mystery as merely the first of many, positioning Nancy's detective career as an ongoing pursuit
- It reinforces the theme that life continues beyond the resolution — mysteries are not endings but transitions

## Nancy's Reflection

Nancy herself seems to sense this: "I wonder if I'll ever have another half so thrilling?" The question is immediately answered by the narrator's revelation, confirming that her **[[detective-intuition|intuition]]** about the future is as sharp as her investigative instincts.
