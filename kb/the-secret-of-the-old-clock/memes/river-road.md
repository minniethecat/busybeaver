# River Road

A scenic but sparsely populated road running along the river — Nancy's chosen route to Masonville that takes her through the rural landscape where the Horner sisters and other key witnesses live. (003-024)

## Location

The River Road runs between River Heights and the surrounding countryside, providing an alternate route to Masonville. It is a "beautiful drive" but also isolated: "Few persons lived along the River Road, for the land was not considered fertile." (003-027) (003-033)

## Description

The River Road is scenic but treacherous in bad weather — "perfectly safe in dry weather" but hazardous when it rains, as Nancy discovers when a storm hits during her drive. (003-032) The land along the road is poor farmland, explaining why "the land was held by poor folk" — including the Horner sisters. (003-033) Nancy takes pride in the "fertility of the soil" of the broader Midwest, contrasting with this marginal farmland. (003-018)

> "It's a beautiful drive on the River Road. But the trip will take longer." — Judge Hartgrave. (003-024)

## Role in the Mystery

The River Road is the physical connection between the worlds of the novel — River Heights' middle-class stability and the rural poverty of the deserving heirs. Nancy's storm-plagued drive along this road is her passage from the theoretical injustice discussed at home to the lived reality of the people she will help.
