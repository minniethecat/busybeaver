# Social Class

Throughout _The Secret of the Old Clock_, social class distinctions shape character motivations and conflicts. The novel contrasts wealthy, snobbish families like the **[[topham-family|Tophams]]** with impoverished but virtuous individuals such as the **[[horner-sisters|Horner sisters]]** and **[[abigail-rowen|Abigail Rowen]]**.

## The Tophams' Pretensions

**[[richard-topham|Richard Topham]]** and his wife **[[cora-topham|Cora Topham]]** are well-to-do but lack genuine social acceptance in **[[river-heights|River Heights]]**. Mrs. Topham's obsession with "social standing" is evident in her desperate purchase of charity dance tickets from **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]**, hoping to elevate the family's position. The Tophams' efforts at landscaping and home decoration are described as tasteless and ostentatious, reflecting their inability to buy class.

## Poverty and Pride

The **[[horner-sisters|Horner sisters]]**, **[[allie-horner|Allie]]** and **[[grace-horner|Grace]]**, live in poverty on a rundown farm along the **[[river-road|River Road]]**. Despite their circumstances, they maintain dignity and refuse charity. Grace earns money through dressmaking, while Allie raises chickens. Their pride repeatedly leads them to decline financial help from **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]**, even when their chickens are dying.

**[[abigail-rowen|Abigail Rowen]]**, an elderly woman living in a dilapidated cottage on **[[west-lake-road|West Lake Road]]**, also refuses charity despite having less than five dollars to her name and suffering from a serious fall.

## The Social Critique

**[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]** embodies the novel's ideal: she is comfortably middle-class but judges people by character rather than wealth. She befriends the Horner sisters and Abigail Rowen while standing up to the Tophams. The narrative rewards the humble and kind with **[[inheritance|inheritance]]** from **[[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]**'s **[[the-lost-will|lost will]]**, while the grasping Tophams are cut off entirely.

## Resolution

After the **[[finding-the-missing-will|discovery of the second will]]**, the deserving relatives receive their inheritances. The Tophams, meanwhile, face financial ruin when **[[richard-topham|Richard Topham]]**'s stock market losses coincide with their loss of the **[[fortune|Crowley fortune]]**.
