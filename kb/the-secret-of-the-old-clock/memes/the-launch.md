# The Launch

A small motor launch kept at the Moon Lake girls' camp, serving as the primary watercraft for campers exploring the lake. Nancy Drew uses the launch both for pleasure rides and for clandestine reconnaissance of the Topham bungalow. A mechanical failure leaves her stranded and drifting, creating one of the story's suspenseful sequences.

## Description

The launch is a small powered boat capable of carrying several girls at once. It is equipped with a motor that requires starting and responds to a tiller or wheel steering. The craft is light enough to be maneuvered close to shore for observation of lakeside properties. (012-067) (013-018)

> As the little launch turned out into the lake, it seemed to Nancy the perfect vantage point for studying the shoreline cottages. (012-069)

## Role in the Mystery

The launch serves a dual role in Nancy's investigation. First, it provides her with waterborne access to observe the Topham bungalow from the lake side — a perspective she could not obtain from the road. During the group ride, she notes the bungalow's location, its dock, and its general condition. Second, the launch's mechanical failure and the resulting drifting incident create one of the story's personal jeopardy sequences, testing Nancy's resourcefulness and strengthening her connection to Helen Corning, who becomes increasingly involved in the case. (012-069) (013-029) (013-039)

> The little launch plowed through the water toward the distant dock, and Nancy breathed a sigh of relief as she finally brought it safely to shore. (013-039)

## Owner

The launch belongs to the Moon Lake girls' camp and is available for campers' use. [[camp-avondale|Camp Avondale]] (or the camp where Helen stays) maintains it.
