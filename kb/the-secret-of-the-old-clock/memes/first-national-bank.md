# First National Bank

The financial institution in Masonville where crucial events in the Crowley case unfold — Henry Rolsted witnessed Crowley discussing a new will here, and Crowley later used the bank's safe deposit box under a false name. (001-041) (022-023)

## Description

The First National Bank (also referred to as the Masonville National Bank) is a conventional small-town financial institution. It provides standard banking services — accounts, safe deposit boxes — that become plot-critical when Crowley uses the bank to conceal his plans. Henry Rolsted was at the bank when "Crowley came in with Henry Rolsted" and made an appointment to discuss a will, providing the key testimony that a second will existed. (001-041) (001-043) Later, Nancy discovers that Crowley rented "box 148" at this bank under the alias "Josiah Harkston." (023-028) (023-031)

## Role in the Story

The First National Bank is the institutional link in the chain of evidence. Rolsted's sighting of Crowley at the bank provides witness testimony; the safe deposit box provides physical evidence. The bank's record-keeping — "According to our record a Josiah Harkston rented box 148 from us" — validates Nancy's investigation through institutional documentation rather than personal recollection. (023-031) The bank thus represents the intersection of personal testimony (Rolsted's sighting) and institutional proof (the rental records) that together make the case for the second will's existence.

> "According to our record a Josiah Harkston rented box 148 from us." — The banker, confirming Crowley's alias. (023-031)
