# Melborne Police Department

The police department of **[[melborne|Melborne]]**, led by the town marshal and his deputies, plays a crucial role in the **[[capturing-the-robbers|capture of the Topham bungalow robbers]]**. Located at the intersection of Central Avenue and Maple Street, the station is where **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]** reports the **[[bungalow-robbery|robbery]]**.

## The Marshal

The marshal is described as "mighty persnickitty about how folks acts" by **[[jeff-tucker|Jeff Tucker]]**, who has been "run in mo'n once" for enjoying himself. Despite Jeff's familiarity with the jail, the marshal proves competent when given a real case.

## The Pursuit

After Nancy reports the robbery and provides a description of the tire tracks, the marshal organizes a pursuit. He and his deputies pile into the police car, buckling on holsters. The police car's engine is difficult to start ("after several unsuccessful attempts he started the motor"), but once underway, the pursuit is determined.

At the fork in the road, Nancy suggests splitting up — she takes the left fork toward **[[garwin|Garwin]]** while the marshal takes the right. After Nancy locates the robbers at **[[the-black-horse|The Black Horse]]** roadhouse and recovers the **[[crowley-clock|Crowley clock]]**, she reunites with the marshal and directs him toward the robbers.

## The Capture

The police car overtakes the moving van on the road to Garwin. When the robbers refuse to halt and fire the first shot, the marshal and deputies return fire, shooting out a front tire. The van topples into a ditch, and the three robbers are disarmed, searched, and handcuffed. The marshal tells Nancy they will "get ten or twenty years for this" on multiple charges.

The marshal is impressed by Nancy's bravery and intelligence, telling her: "Not many girls would have used their brains the way you did."
