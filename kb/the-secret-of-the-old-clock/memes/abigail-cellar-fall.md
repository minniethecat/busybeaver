# Abigail Rowen's Cellar Stairs Fall

The day before **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]**'s visit, **[[abigail-rowen|Abigail Rowen]]** fell down the cellar stairs of her cottage on the **[[west-lake-road|West Lake Road]]**. The accident left her helpless, alone, and in pain for more than a day.

## The Injury

Abigail tells Nancy: "Yesterday I fell down the cellar stairs. I hurt my hip and sprained my ankle. I can't take a step without it nearly killing me." She has been lying on her couch under a ragged old shawl with no one to help. Her hip is not broken — she can "walk a little, but it hurts something awful."

## Isolation

"No one has been near her" since the fall. Abigail has been unable to reach food, water, or medical supplies. She refuses Nancy's offer to call a doctor: "I can't afford it. I'd rather die in my bed." Her pride and poverty combine to create a potentially fatal situation.

## Nancy's Aid

Nancy's **[[nancy-cares-for-abigail|compassionate response]]** — purchasing groceries, bandages, and liniment, bathing and wrapping the ankle, and making tea — not only saves Abigail from further suffering but also creates the trusting environment in which Abigail shares the vital clue about the **[[crowley-notebook|notebook]]**.

Without this fall, Nancy might never have visited Abigail. Without Nancy's care, Abigail might never have disclosed what she knew. The accident thus becomes an unlikely catalyst for solving the **[[the-lost-will|mystery]]**.
