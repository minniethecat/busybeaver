# Charity Dance Tickets

The charity dance tickets serve as **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]**'s "passport to the impregnable Topham fortress." When her friend **[[helen-corning|Helen Corning]]** struggles to sell the remaining six tickets for an upcoming charity dance, Nancy sees an opportunity.

## Nancy's Plan

Helen is anxious to sell the tickets so she can leave for **[[camp-avondale|Camp Avondale]]** at **[[moon-lake|Moon Lake]]**. Nancy buys two tickets, then offers to sell the remaining four — but her real motive is to gain entry to the **[[topham-residence|Topham residence]]** and learn the whereabouts of the **[[crowley-clock|Crowley clock]]**.

She tells herself: "That's once when I killed two birds with one stone! These tickets will serve as my passport to the impregnable Topham fortress!"

## At the Topham Residence

Nancy presents herself as selling tickets for "one of the most important social functions of the year." **[[cora-topham|Mrs. Topham]]** initially dismisses her rudely, but upon learning it is Carson Drew's daughter, becomes hesitant to offend. **[[ada-topham|Ada]]** and **[[isabel-topham|Isabel Topham]]** urge their mother not to buy.

**[[richard-topham|Richard Topham]]** intervenes, grandly buying all four tickets with a twenty-dollar bill and declaring, "Keep the change. Give it to the charity." His motive is strategic: "This donation will get our names into the paper."

## Success

During the visit, Nancy cleverly steers the conversation to the clock on the mantel, learning that it is NOT the Crowley clock — the Tophams sent that "old-fashioned thing" to their **[[topham-bungalow|bungalow on Moon Lake]]**. The tickets thus achieve their hidden purpose.
