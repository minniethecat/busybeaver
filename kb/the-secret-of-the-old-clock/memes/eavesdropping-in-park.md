# Eavesdropping in the Park

Nancy Drew overhears Ada and Isabel Topham discussing "the will" on a park bench, sparking her suspicion and becoming the first major clue that the Crowley will was hidden from them.

## Summary

While walking through the public park after an encounter with the Topham sisters at a department store, Nancy Drew accidentally overhears Ada and Isabel Topham in earnest conversation (006-052). As she passes their bench, the phrase "the will" reaches her ears, immediately arousing her suspicions (006-056). She later recounts the overheard conversation to her father, [[carson-drew]] (007-023), and reflects on it throughout her investigation (010-035, 020-032).

The overheard conversation gains significance when Nancy learns that the Topham sisters have been living comfortably at the [[crowley-house|Crowley homestead]] while other relatives received nothing (005-019). This contrast between the sisters' lifestyle and the missing will deepens her determination to investigate.

## Background

Before the park encounter, Nancy had an unpleasant run-in with the Topham sisters at a department store, where Ada and Isabel treated a saleswoman rudely (002-040). Nancy observed their haughty behavior and learned they were staying at the [[royal-hotel]] (002-044). This earlier interaction made the overheard words in the park all the more striking.

> "But how I dread to see the Topham sisters come into the store! They are so overbearing and hard to please." — saleswoman at the department store (002-044)

## Investigation Impact

The overheard conversation transforms Nancy from a curious bystander into an active investigator. Before this moment, she had only a general awareness of the Crowley estate controversy (002-053). Hearing the words "the will" spoken in secret by the very people who stood to benefit from the first will gives her a concrete direction. She immediately shares the information with her father, who advises caution but agrees the matter warrants investigation (007-023, 007-030).

> "I think I should find out what became of the Crowley will," Nancy told her father. "If those sisters are hiding something, someone ought to know about it." (002-086)

## Plot Significance

This overheard conversation is the first direct clue suggesting that the Topham sisters may have concealed the existence of [[josiah-crowley]]'s second will (006-056). Nancy's suspicion, first ignited in the park, drives her subsequent investigation into the [[crowley-clock]] and the missing notebook. She returns to this overheard conversation several times throughout the case, using it as a reference point when evaluating new evidence (010-035, 020-032).

The encounter illustrates Nancy's method of investigation: careful observation combined with strategic follow-up. Rather than confronting the sisters directly, she shares the information with her father and continues gathering evidence independently.
