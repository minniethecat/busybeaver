# False Accusation

False accusation appears as a motif reflecting the **[[topham-family|Topham family]]**'s dishonesty and moral bankruptcy. The most striking instance occurs at a **[[river-heights|River Heights]]** department store.

## The Vase Incident

**[[ada-topham|Ada Topham]]** accidentally knocks an expensive imported vase from a counter, smashing it. When a saleswoman asks her to pay, Ada not only refuses but falsely accuses the saleswoman of breaking the vase herself. She enlists her sister **[[isabel-topham|Isabel Topham]]** to corroborate the lie.

**[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]**, who witnessed the accident, steps forward to defend the truth. Her intervention saves the saleswoman from having to pay fifty dollars she could not afford and likely from being discharged.

## Broader Pattern

The Tophams' willingness to make false accusations extends beyond this incident. After the **[[finding-the-missing-will|second will]]** is discovered, the Tophams claim it is "a forged document" and "a conspiracy" — accusations they cannot prove. The case is ultimately thrown out of court.

The novel contrasts the Tophams' dishonesty with Nancy's commitment to **[[fairness|fairness]]** and truth, reinforcing the moral framework that the honest and kind deserve their **[[inheritance|inheritance]]** while the dishonest lose everything.
