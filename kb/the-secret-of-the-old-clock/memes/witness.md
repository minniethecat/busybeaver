# Witness

A person who is present at the execution of a legal document and signs it to attest to its authenticity — in the novel, the identity of the witness to Crowley's second will is a key piece of the puzzle. (009-052) (023-060)

## Definition

In legal terms, a witness to a will is a disinterested person who observes the testator signing the document and attests by their own signature that the signing was voluntary and authentic. Abigail Rowen recalls Crowley asking whether a handwritten will was valid: "Are you sure it's legal to write it yourself?" — and the assurance that it was "all right just so long as I had it witnessed." (009-052) The witness requirement is what makes the second will legally binding, and the discovery that Doctor Nesbitt signed as a witness is a pivotal moment. (023-060)

## Role in the Story

The witness question drives a critical part of the investigation. When Abigail Rowen reports Crowley's claim about having a will witnessed, Nancy immediately asks "Do you know who witnessed the will?" — recognizing that finding the witness is essential to establishing the document's validity. (009-053) Much later, Nancy discovers that Doctor Nesbitt served as the witness — explaining "No wonder the will never came to light": the witness had died, leaving no one alive who knew where the document was hidden. (023-060)
