# Robbery

The criminal act of taking property from another person by force or threat — a spate of thefts around Moon Lake intersects with Nancy's will investigation and tests her detective skills. (014-033) (014-034)

## Definition

Robbery is the crime of stealing goods or property, distinguished from burglary by the direct confrontation with victims. In the novel, the Moon Lake bungalow is ransacked, and valuable items — including furniture and silverware belonging to the Tophams — are stolen. Nancy initially dismisses it as "a case of ordinary robbery" but soon realizes it is connected to the larger mystery. (014-033) (015-003)

## Role in the Story

The robbery subplot serves multiple narrative functions. It establishes the criminal element at Moon Lake beyond the civil dispute over the will, giving Nancy an urgent practical problem to solve. It introduces Jeff Tucker as a key witness — he was at the bungalow before the robbery and provides crucial testimony. (017-036) Nancy's decision to "report the robbery to the police" demonstrates her respect for legal process, while her parallel investigation shows her amateur detective initiative. (015-003) (018-010) The robbery's resolution — capturing the thieves — is Nancy's first tangible success as a detective, foreshadowing the larger triumph of finding the will.
