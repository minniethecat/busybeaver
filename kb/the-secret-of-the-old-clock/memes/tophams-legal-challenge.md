# Tophams' Legal Challenge

After being disinherited by **[[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]**'s **[[the-lost-will|second will]]**, the **[[topham-family|Tophams]]** mount a legal challenge in an attempt to recover the **[[fortune|fortune]]**.

## The Claim

The Tophams "put forth the claim that the will Nancy had unearthed was a forged document." This accusation follows the pattern established earlier — **[[ada-topham|Ada Topham]]**'s **[[false-accusation|false accusation]]** against the department store saleswoman, and the family's broader pattern of dishonesty.

## The Outcome

The challenge fails decisively. **[[carson-drew|Carson Drew]]** had carefully reviewed the will and found it "technically perfect." Despite being "peculiar in some ways," Crowley "was no one's fool" and had written a legally sound document. The case is "thrown out of court."

## Consequences

The failed legal challenge compounds the Tophams' troubles. **[[richard-topham|Richard Topham]]** had been counting on the inheritance to cover his **[[stock-market-speculation|stock market losses]]**. With the will upheld and his credit reduced, he is "forced to give up his home on the avenue," and the family moves to "a small house."

The Tophams' legal defeat serves as the final confirmation of **[[justice|justice]]** in the novel — their greed, **[[snobbery|snobbery]]**, and cruelty to Crowley are repaid with the complete loss of everything they schemed to obtain.
