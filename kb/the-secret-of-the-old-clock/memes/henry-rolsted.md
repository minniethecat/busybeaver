# Henry Rolsted

An attorney and old friend of [[carson-drew|Carson Drew]] — his eyewitness account of [[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]] discussing a new [[will-legal-document|will]] at the [[first-national-bank|First National Bank]] provides the strongest evidence that a [[crowley-will-document|second will]] existed. (001-041) (001-043)

## Role in the Story

Henry Rolsted is the novel's key [[witness|legal witness]]. He was with [[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]] at the [[first-national-bank|First National Bank]] when [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]] made an appointment to "call at Rolsted's office the following day" to discuss a [[will-legal-document|will]]. (001-043) [[carson-drew|Carson Drew]], who describes Rolsted as "an old friend," arranges for [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] to hear Rolsted's account, which confirms [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]] "had made up his mind to write a new [[will-legal-document|will]]." (001-048) (001-044) This testimony is crucial — it establishes that [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]] intended to change his [[will-legal-document|will]] after making the one favoring [[topham-family|the Tophams]], providing the legal foundation for the search for the second document.

## Character Traits

- Trusted legal professional: an attorney respected enough to be [[carson-drew|Carson Drew]]'s old friend (001-048)
- Observant: noticed and remembered the details of [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]]'s bank visit (001-041)
- Cooperative: [[will-legal-document|will]]ing to [[portion|share]] his knowledge with [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] and [[carson-drew|Carson Drew]]
- Discreet: professional discretion about client matters

## Role in the Mystery

Henry Rolsted is the chain of evidence that connects [[nancy-drew|Nancy]]'s suspicion to legal fact. Without his testimony, the existence of a [[crowley-will-document|second will]] would be mere rumor. His professional credibility — as an attorney and [[carson-drew|Carson Drew]]'s friend — makes his account admissible in the court of the novel's logic, giving [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] the confidence to pursue the [[detective-work|investigation]].

> "Crowley made an appointment to call at Rolsted's office the following day." — Carson Drew, relaying the crucial evidence. (001-043)

## Narrative Significance

Henry Rolsted represents the professional network that supports amateur [[detective-work|detective]] work. [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] is not operating in isolation — her father's connections provide access to information that would otherwise be unavailable, demonstrating how social capital complements individual initiative in the pursuit of [[fairness|justice]].
