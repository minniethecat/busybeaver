# Hidden

The state of being concealed from view or knowledge — the defining condition of Josiah Crowley's second will, which he hid somewhere known only to Abigail Rowen, transforming Nancy Drew's investigation into a treasure hunt. (005-041) (010-025)

## Definition

To hide something is to place it where others cannot find it. In the novel, hiding operates at multiple levels: Crowley physically hid the will (possibly inside a clock), Abigail Rowen hid her knowledge of its location out of fear and confusion, and Nancy Drew's skill lies in "unearthing hidden facts" that others have overlooked or concealed. (004-070) (010-025)

## Role in the Story

The hidden will is the novel's central puzzle. Abigail Rowen reveals that Crowley "might have hidden his will inside the family clock," giving Nancy a specific physical location to search. (010-025) Earlier, Nancy asks a key question: "What led you to believe that he had hidden a will?" — trying to separate fact from conjecture. (005-041) The hidden status of the will creates dramatic irony: readers know it exists, but characters must prove it. Nancy's reputation as "a wonder at unearthing hidden facts" is both validated by the plot and essential to its resolution. (004-070)
