# Danger

**Danger** permeates *The Secret of the Old Clock* as the element that transforms Nancy Drew's investigation from a curious inquiry into a genuine adventure. Each stage of her pursuit introduces new peril, escalating from environmental hazards to life-threatening confrontations.

## Environmental Danger

The novel's first brush with danger is environmental. Racing home through a storm on the River Road, Nancy knows "that a little rain would make it dangerously slippery" (003-034). The road "was little traveled, and in case of accident she could not depend upon being found for hours" (003-034). This early scene establishes Nancy's willingness to accept risk in pursuit of her goals.

> Although the River Road was perfectly safe in dry weather, Nancy knew that a little rain would make it dangerously slippery. Then, too, the road was little traveled, and in case of accident she could not depend upon passing motorists for aid. (003-034)
## Psychological Danger

In the Topham bungalow, the danger shifts from physical to psychological. Locked in the closet, Nancy feels "an unseen danger lurked in wait for her" (015-003). "The very walls seemed to smirk and grimace at her and there was an ominous quality about the creaking boards" (015-003). Left to starve, Nancy confronts the danger of isolation and helplessness — a test of her resilience as much as her physical endurance.

## Confronting Armed Criminals

The climax brings the most direct danger: facing three armed robbers. The marshal's deputies "searched their pockets for other dangerous weapons" (021-035) before handcuffing them, underscoring the genuine threat Nancy has been pursuing. Her courage in tracking and identifying these men, despite the risk, completes her arc from curious amateur to capable investigator.

## Danger and Character

Throughout the novel, danger serves as a crucible that reveals character. Nancy does not seek danger, but neither does she retreat from it. Her father worries about the risks she takes: "Oh, do you think there is any danger it won't be?" Nancy asks anxiously about the second will's validity "(023)" — but the danger she fears is not physical harm but the possibility that justice might fail.
