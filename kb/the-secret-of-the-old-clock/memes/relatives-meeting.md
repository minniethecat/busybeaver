# Reading of the Second Will

The reading of **[[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]**'s second will at the **[[drew-home|Drew home]]** is the climactic resolution of the mystery. **[[carson-drew|Carson Drew]]** convenes all interested parties for the formal disclosure.

## The Gathering

Nancy arranges chairs in the living room as the beneficiaries arrive: the **[[horner-sisters|Horner sisters]]**, **[[matilda-turner|Matilda]]** and **[[edna-turner|Edna Turner]]**, and **[[william-mathews|William]]** and **[[fred-mathews|Fred Mathews]]**. **[[abigail-rowen|Abigail Rowen]]** is absent due to her health.

The **[[topham-family|Tophams]]** arrive last — **[[richard-topham|Richard]]**, **[[cora-topham|Cora]]**, **[[ada-topham|Ada]]**, and **[[isabel-topham|Isabel Topham]]** — accompanied by their lawyer. Mrs. Topham immediately protests: "It's preposterous! Josiah Crowley made only one will and in that he left everything to us!" Ada calls it "a conspiracy."

## The Will's Provisions

Carson Drew reads the key provisions:
- **[[allie-horner|Allie]]** and **[[grace-horner|Grace Horner]]**: $75,000 each, plus Crowley's household furniture
- **[[abigail-rowen|Abigail Rowen]]**: $75,000
- **[[william-mathews|William]]** and **[[fred-mathews|Fred Mathews]]**: $20,000 each
- **[[matilda-turner|Matilda]]** and **[[edna-turner|Edna Turner]]**: $20,000 each
- The Tophams: nothing

## The Tophams' Exit

The Tophams are devastated. Mrs. Topham storms out, declaring "We'll fight to the last ditch!" Ada accuses Nancy directly: "You had something to do with this, Nancy Drew." Their lawyer, when consulted, confirms Mr. Drew's assessment that the will is legally sound — and is promptly discharged.

The Tophams' later legal challenge is "thrown out of court." **[[justice|Justice]]** is fully served.
