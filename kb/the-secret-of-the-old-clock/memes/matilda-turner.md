# Matilda Turner

Matilda Turner is a cousin of [[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]] and the sister of [[edna-turner|Edna Turner]]. Together with her sister, she is among the relatives who [[inheritance|inherit]] $20,000 under [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]]'s rediscovered [[will-legal-document|will]] (024-043).

## Role in the Story

Matilda Turner, together with her sister [[edna-turner|Edna]], is identified by [[allie-horner|Allie Horner]] as one of [[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]'s relatives whom [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] should visit during her [[detective-work|investigation]] (008-041). [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] subsequently visits the [[edna-turner|Turner sisters]] at their home, where they live alone (008-069). The sisters insist that [[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]] had always promised to remember them in his [[will-legal-document|will]] (008-071). During [[nancy-drew|Nancy]]'s visit, Matilda suggests that [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] speak with [[abigail-rowen|Abigail Rowen]] for additional information (008-072). At the story's resolution, Matilda and [[edna-turner|Edna]] arrive to hear the reading of the true [[will-legal-document|will]] and receive $20,000 as [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]]'s cousins (024-043).

## Character Traits

- Lives quietly with her sister [[edna-turner|Edna]] in their own home near [[masonville|Masonville]] (008-069)
- Helpful to [[nancy-drew|Nancy]]'s [[detective-work|investigation]] — she proactively suggests interviewing [[abigail-rowen|Abigail Rowen]] (008-072)
- Faithful to [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]]'s promises; she and [[edna-turner|Edna]] "insisted that [[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]] had always promised to remember them" (008-071)

## Role in the Mystery

Matilda Turner is one link in the chain of [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]] relatives whom [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] must locate and interview. Her most significant contribution to the [[detective-work|investigation]] is her suggestion that [[nancy-drew|Nancy]] speak with [[abigail-rowen|Abigail Rowen]] — a [[clue|lead]] that advances [[nancy-drew|Nancy]]'s search for the [[crowley-will-document|missing will]] (008-072). As a cousin of [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]] who was entirely excluded from the earlier [[richard-topham|Topham]]-favoring [[will-legal-document|will]], Matilda's testimony about [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]]'s promises helps establish the existence of a later, [[fairness|fair]]er document (008-071).

> "Why don't you talk with Abigail Rowen?" Matilda suggested at the conclusion of Nancy's visit. (008-072)

## Narrative Significance

Matilda Turner and her sister [[edna-turner|Edna]] represent the elderly, dependent relatives whom [[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]] genuinely intended to support. Their quiet faith in [[josiah-crowley|Crowley]]'s promises — contrasted with [[topham-family|the Tophams]]' aggressive claim to the entire [[fortune|estate]] — reinforces the novel's moral framework: the true [[will-legal-document|will]] rewards loyalty and patience while the false [[will-legal-document|will]] serves greed. Matilda's small but effective contribution to [[nancy-drew|Nancy]]'s [[detective-work|investigation]] (suggesting the [[abigail-rowen|Abigail Rowen]] [[clue|lead]]) also demonstrates how even minor characters can advance the [[detective-work|detective]] plot through a single well-placed piece of information (008-072).
