# Secret

Information deliberately kept hidden from others — Nancy Drew's investigation is fundamentally an effort to uncover the secrets surrounding Josiah Crowley's estate, from the location of the hidden will to the true intentions behind his changed testament. (009-059) (010-032)

## Definition

A secret is knowledge intentionally concealed from those who do not have authorization to know it. In the novel, secrets operate at multiple levels: Crowley's decision to change his will was kept secret from the Tophams; the location of the second will was "secreted" in a safe place known only to Abigail Rowen; and Carson Drew's private pride — "it was his secret boast that he had taught her everything she knew" — reveals a softer side of the professional lawyer. (001-034) (010-032)

## Role in the Story

The entire novel is structured around uncovering a secret: the existence and location of Crowley's second will. Abigail Rowen "held the secret of the Crowley will locked in her heart," and Nancy's patient questioning gradually unlocks it. (009-059) The secrecy surrounding the will creates dramatic tension — if the will is too well hidden, it may never be found, and injustice will prevail. Nancy also learns that Crowley "secreted it in a safe place," information that guides her search. (010-032)
