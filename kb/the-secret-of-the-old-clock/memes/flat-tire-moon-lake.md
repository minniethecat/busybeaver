# Flat Tire on Moon Lake Road

While driving to **[[camp-avondale|Camp Avondale]]** on **[[moon-lake|Moon Lake]]**, **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]**'s **[[nancy-roadster|blue roadster]]** develops a flat tire on the rough, isolated road.

## The Problem

Nancy notices the roadster pulling to the left. Stopping to investigate, she discovers a rear tire is flat. She murmurs in disgust: "If that isn't just my luck! Oh, well, I suppose I must fix it myself, because there won't be another car along for an hour on this road."

## Self-Reliance

"It was not the first time Nancy Drew had changed a tire, but she never relished the task." She jacks up the rear axle, loosens the lugs, and tugs at the heavy balloon tire. After several attempts, it comes off suddenly and she falls "backwards into a sitting posture in the road."

She has the spare on within minutes, but the accident delays her nearly an hour.

## Significance

This minor incident demonstrates Nancy's practical competence and self-reliance — qualities that serve her well throughout her investigation. She is comfortable handling mechanical problems that many girls her age would find daunting, a skill that proves essential when she must later start the camp launch engine and drive in pursuit of the **[[truck-pursuit|robbers' van]]**.
