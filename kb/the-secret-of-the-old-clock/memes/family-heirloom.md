# Family Heirloom

The **[[crowley-clock|Crowley family mantel clock]]** serves as the central physical artifact in _The Secret of the Old Clock_, embodying the concept of the family heirloom as a vessel of hidden history. While the **[[topham-family|Tophams]]** dismiss it as "old junk" unworthy of their "modern home," the clock contains the key to the entire **[[the-lost-will|mystery]]**.

## Description

The clock is described by **[[abigail-rowen|Abigail Rowen]]** as "an ordinary mantel clock, tall, and with a square face" — "more elaborate" than her own, with "a moon or something on top." When **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]** finally sees it, she notes it has "a square face and the top was ornamented with a crescent."

## The Tophams' Dismissal

The Tophams considered the clock "entirely out of place in our modern home" and sent it to their **[[topham-bungalow|bungalow on Moon Lake]]**. **[[cora-topham|Mrs. Topham]]** calls it "that old-fashioned thing," and **[[isabel-topham|Isabel Topham]]** dismisses it as part of "Crowley's old junk." Their contempt for the heirloom reflects their inability to see value in anything that cannot be monetized.

## Hidden Treasure

Inside the clock, behind its cardboard face and attached to a hook, hangs the tiny blue **[[crowley-notebook|notebook]]** containing **[[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]**'s notation about the location of his **[[the-lost-will|second will]]**. The clock thus transforms from a dismissed heirloom into the key that unlocks justice.

## Nancy's Reward

At the novel's end, when the **[[horner-sisters|Horner sisters]]** offer Nancy a reward, she asks only for "the Crowley clock for my very own." She prizes it as "a trophy of my first venture as a detective" and "a pleasant reminder of a thrilling adventure," valuing its personal significance far above monetary worth.
