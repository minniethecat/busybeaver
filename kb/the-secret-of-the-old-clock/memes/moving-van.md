# Moving Van

A large, heavy moving van used by the thieves who robbed the Topham bungalow at Moon Lake — the vehicle Nancy Drew tracks across the countryside in her pursuit of the criminals and their stolen goods. (014-024) (015-003)

## Description

The vehicle is described as a "large moving van" (018-043) and a "heavy moving van" (015-003), suggesting a substantial covered truck suitable for transporting furniture and household goods. It leaves distinctive tire tracks on the road — tracks that Nancy identifies as having been "made by a heavy truck" (014-015) — and is large enough to carry the extensive stolen items from the bungalow. The van moves slowly due to its weight: "they couldn't travel very fast with a heavy moving van" (015-003), which gives Nancy hope of catching up. At one point it is described as "the big moving van" proceeding "slowly on its way" (020-022).

## Ownership

- Original owner: The robbers (unnamed)
- Appears in: Ch. 14–21

## Story Role

The moving van is central to the Moon Lake robbery subplot. After the Topham bungalow is burglarized, the robbers use the van to transport the stolen furniture and silverware. Nancy pursues the van, following its tire tracks (014-015) (018-029) and questioning locals: "Have you seen a large moving van on this road?" (018-043). She eventually discovers it parked at a roadhouse (019-009), examines it with her flashlight (019-012), and it later becomes key evidence when the authorities search it — the police officer "turned toward the moving van. 'I'll just have a look inside'" (021-054). The van's disappearance (021-006) and the difficulty of locating it (021-018) drive much of the chase narrative in the middle chapters.
