# Nancy Visits the Mathews Brothers

During her tour of the **[[crowley-relatives|Crowley relatives]]**, **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]** visits the farm of **[[william-mathews|William]]** and **[[fred-mathews|Fred Mathews]]** on the **[[river-road|River Road]]**.

## The Visit

Nancy drives about five miles beyond the **[[horner-farm|Horner farm]]** and spots a mailbox bearing the name "Mathews." She drives down a narrow lane to the farmhouse. **[[william-mathews|William Mathews]]** comes out to greet her, initially distrustful until he confirms she is not a friend of the **[[topham-family|Tophams]]**.

## The Interview

William confirms that he and his brother have "filed a complaint with the proper authorities at the courthouse" regarding the estate. Like the other relatives, he believes Crowley made a second will: "Uncle Josiah always said that he intended to leave us something."

He offers a significant lead: "My brother and I will be glad to offer a substantial reward to anyone who can produce it." This is the first mention of a reward for finding the will.

## Outcome

As with the **[[nancy-visits-turner-sisters|Turner sisters]]**, the Mathews cannot provide information that directly locates the will. However, their conviction that Crowley intended to disinherit the Tophams reinforces Nancy's **[[determination|determination]]** to continue her search. The visit also underscores how many lives the missing will affects — honest farmers and hardworking relatives all left waiting while the Tophams attempt to seize everything.
