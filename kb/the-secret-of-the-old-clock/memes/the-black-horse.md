## Location

The Black Horse was a roadhouse inn located less than a mile from the Topham family's summer bungalow, on the road near Melborne [018-049]. The inn was positioned at a fork in the road, between Melborne and the rural area where the Topham property stood [021-017].

## Description

A sign on the gate identified the establishment: "The Black Horse" [018-052]. From outside the inn, Nancy could see the robbers' moving van parked beyond the building [018-052]. The roadhouse was situated at a distance that made it a plausible stop for travelers on the rural roads outside Melborne [018-050].

## Role in the Story

After Nancy spotted the moving van containing the robbers' stolen goods, she tracked the vehicle past the roadhouse. When the robbers failed to stop there, she became suspicious [018-050]. The roadhouse served as a landmark in the pursuit that led to the eventual capture of the three robbers near Melborne [021-017].

The roadhouse and its surroundings were searched by authorities after the robbers' arrest, as the moving van had been seen in the vicinity [020-022].
