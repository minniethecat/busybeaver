# Detective Intuition

**[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]** possesses a natural gift for detective work that her father, **[[carson-drew|Carson Drew]]**, calls her "intuitions." This intuitive sense repeatedly guides her investigation of the **[[the-lost-will|Crowley will mystery]]**.

## Early Signs

From the beginning, Nancy has "a certain feeling that a mystery lurked behind" the Crowley case, even though her father is not directly involved. She insists, "I just seem to know that Josiah Crowley made a second will," long before any evidence confirms it.

## Key Intuitive Leaps

Several of Nancy's intuitive deductions prove critical:

- After meeting the **[[horner-sisters|Horner sisters]]** during the **[[storm-on-river-road|storm]]**, she immediately senses "she had encountered something unusual" and connects their plight to the Crowley estate
- When **[[abigail-rowen|Abigail Rowen]]** mentions a clock while discussing the **[[crowley-notebook|notebook]]**, Nancy's "keen mind jumped to the conclusion that a clock had something to do with the missing Crowley will"
- She deduces that the robbers at **[[moon-lake|Moon Lake]]** must have taken the clock along with the other furniture, and correctly guesses their route toward **[[garwin|Garwin]]**

## Father's Assessment

**[[carson-drew|Carson Drew]]** acknowledges Nancy's talent: "More than once her father had found her suggestions, or 'intuitions' as he called them, extremely helpful." He remarks that she goes at things "like a detective" and has "a naturally clever mind."
