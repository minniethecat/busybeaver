# Portion

A share or part of a whole, specifically the part of Josiah Crowley's estate allocated to a particular beneficiary — the Horner sisters and Abigail Rowen each expected a portion but received nothing under the first will. (001-032) (005-020)

## Definition

A portion in inheritance law is the specific share of an estate designated for a particular heir. The novel repeatedly emphasizes that multiple relatives "deserve a portion of the fortune" — not the whole, but a fair share. (001-032) (005-060) The Horner sisters were told they would receive "a portion of the Crowley estate," creating expectations that the first will brutally disappointed. (005-020) At the resolution, "Nancy had arranged that the Horner girls and Abigail Rowen receive a portion of their inheritance," restoring the portions Crowley intended. (025-007)

## Role in the Story

The concept of portion defines the difference between greed and justice. The Tophams want everything; the Horners and Abigail Rowen only want their promised portions. The second will formalizes these portions, and when it is read, it reveals "the portions which have to do with the disposition of the estate." (024-029) The portion concept also distinguishes the novel's morality from simplistic "all or nothing" thinking — justice is not about taking everything from the Tophams, but about ensuring each deserving person receives their fair portion.
