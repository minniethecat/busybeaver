Camp Avondale is a summer camp in the Moon Lake area. Its specific function in the novel is minor — it is mentioned as a destination for young people, part of the social geography of the region. The camp represents the network of institutions (schools, camps, clubs, charities) that form the social fabric within which Nancy's investigation takes place.
