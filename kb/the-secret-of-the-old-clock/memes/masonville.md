# Masonville

A neighboring town to River Heights, approximately fifteen miles away — serving as a key administrative and commercial center where crucial legal proceedings in the [[josiah-crowley]] estate case unfold, culminating in the discovery of the missing will at the Masonville National Bank.

## Location

Masonville is the nearest town to River Heights, accessible by roadster within a morning's drive. When Carson Drew needs legal documents delivered to Judge Hartgrave there, Nancy readily accepts the errand:

> "I have a number of legal documents which must be delivered to Judge Hartgrave at Masonville some time before noon. I would take them myself, but I have several important appointments this morning." (003-002)

Nancy responds:

> "I'll be glad to do the errand for you," Nancy promised willingly. "I can run over to Masonville in the roadster. It isn't more than a fifteen mile jaunt." (003-003)

The road between River Heights and Masonville passes through open country, and the route is familiar enough that Nancy notes she has "frequently driven as far as Masonville." (003-017)

## Description

Masonville is a modest commercial town with at least a bank, a courthouse, various shops, and a dressmaker. Grace Horner "helps a dressmaker at Masonville whenever she can get work." (004-036) It is large enough to support professional services but small enough that residents of River Heights visit only occasionally:

> "We don't see a girl of our own age once in a month unless we go to Masonville, and our visits there aren't very frequent, Miss Drew." (004-022)

The town's key institution is the Masonville National Bank, which maintains safety deposit boxes — a detail that proves crucial to the resolution of the mystery. The Turner sisters, cousins who were kind to Josiah Crowley, live "at the outskirts of the city":

> Upon reaching Masonville, Nancy made inquiry and was directed to the home of the Turner sisters. They lived alone at the outskirts of the city. Although not in poverty, they did not have any more money than they needed. (008-069)

Nancy's first trip to Masonville illustrates the legal connections the town hosts. After arriving:

> Nancy took her time on the road and it was nearly eleven o'clock when she finally drove into Masonville. She went at once to Judge Hartgrave's office but was informed that he had gone to the courthouse. Nancy knew that the legal papers which she had been sent to deliver were important, and rather than leave them with the office girl she set off in search of the judge. (003-021)

## Role in the Mystery

Masonville plays a pivotal role in the resolution of the Crowley inheritance mystery. The Masonville National Bank is where Josiah Crowley, under the alias "Josiah Harkston," rented safety deposit box 148. The revelation comes when Nancy discovers a note inside the old clock:

> "To whom it may concern: My last will and testament will be found in safety deposit box number 148 in the Masonville National Bank. The box is under the name of Josiah Harkston." (022-023)

The trip to recover the will is described with efficiency:

> The trip to Masonville was quickly accomplished, and in a short time Nancy stopped the roadster in front of the Masonville National Bank. (023-023)

The discovery of this second will at the Masonville bank overturns the Topham family's claim to the entire estate:

> After locating the Crowley will in a safety deposit box at the Masonville National Bank in the town of Masonville, Carson Drew and his daughter had read it carefully. Without disclosing the contents to anyone, they had called a special meeting of the relatives at the Drew home. (024-007)

Carson Drew later tells the assembled relatives:

> "As you have probably learned by this time," Mr. Drew began, "a second will of the late Josiah Crowley has been found in a safety deposit box in a Masonville bank. The will is unusually long, and with your permission I will read only the portions which have to do with the disposal of the property." (024-029)

The bank represents Crowley's characteristic caution — even while using an assumed name, he took care to secure the will in a formal institution outside of River Heights, ensuring it could not be easily discovered or destroyed by the Tophams, who had treated him unkindly.
