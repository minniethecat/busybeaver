# Moon Lake Investigation

Nancy Drew's undercover trip to Moon Lake, where she poses as a visitor to a girls' camp while secretly investigating the Topham family's bungalow and the string of thefts in the area. This multi-chapter operation represents one of Nancy's most elaborate investigative stratagems.

## Summary

When Nancy learns that the Tophams have a bungalow on Moon Lake — where stolen goods from local robberies might be stored — she devises a cover story. A friend, Helen Corning, is attending a girls' camp on Moon Lake, providing Nancy with a natural reason to visit. Under the guise of a social trip, Nancy travels to Moon Lake, explores the area by boat, locates the Topham bungalow, and gathers crucial intelligence about the robbery ring operating from the lake. The investigation lays the groundwork for her later discovery of the Crowley clock at the bungalow and the eventual capture of the thieves. (012-001) (012-002) (012-011)

> "If the Tophams learn I have gone to Moon Lake they may become suspicious," Nancy reflected, knowing she must maintain her cover. (012-002)

## Plot Significance

The Moon Lake investigation is a turning point in Nancy's detective work. Previously, she had relied on interviews and document searches; this operation marks her first use of undercover tactics and field surveillance. By physically visiting the scene and observing the Topham bungalow, Nancy transitions from gathering testimony to gathering physical evidence. The intelligence she collects — the layout of the bungalow, the pattern of thefts, the Tophams' presence on the lake — directly enables her later break-in, the clock discovery, and the identification of the burglary ring.
