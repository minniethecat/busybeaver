# Crowley Notebook

The notebook concealed alongside the will inside the Crowley family clock — its contents, including the alias "Josiah Harkston," lead Nancy to Crowley's safe deposit box and the full documentation of his final intentions. (010-013) (022-023)

## Description

The notebook is a small, handwritten document hidden with the second will inside the Crowley clock. (010-013) It contains the name "Josiah Harkston" — an alias Crowley used to rent a safe deposit box at the Masonville National Bank. The notebook reveals: "To whom it may concern: My last will and testament will be found in safety deposit box number 148 in the Masonville National Bank. The box is under the name of Josiah Harkston." (022-023) The date of this entry was recent, and the ink had not faded as in the earlier entries — a detail that immediately signaled to Nancy that this was the crucial clue. (022-024)

The notebook demonstrates Crowley's careful planning: he created a false name to protect his true intentions, knowing the Tophams would try to suppress any evidence of a second will. When Nancy discovered the entry, she exclaimed aloud: "Then there was another will! Oh, I'm sure it must be in favor of the Horner girls, too!" (022-025) But although she carefully examined every page in the book, there was no other mention of the will — the notebook gave her just enough to find the document, not enough to know its contents. (022-026)

At the bank, Nancy and her father confirmed the alias: "According to our record a Josiah Harkston rented box 148 from us, but the past year the rent has not been paid. Here is his signature if you care to look at it." They saw that the writing was in the cramped hand of Josiah Crowley. (023-031) (023-032)

## Story Role

The notebook is the secondary puzzle within the primary mystery. Finding the will is one achievement; finding the notebook that explains Crowley's alias is another. The notebook demonstrates the depth of Crowley's planning — he anticipated that the Tophams would search for a will under his own name and took measures to protect his documents. The alias "Josiah Harkston" is an additional layer of concealment that Nancy must penetrate, testing her detective skills beyond the initial discovery.

## Plot Significance

The notebook transforms the will discovery from a single find into a chain of discoveries. It reveals that Crowley was not merely hiding a document but constructing a careful legal strategy — using an alias, a safe deposit box, and a trusted confidante (Abigail Rowen) to ensure his true intentions would eventually come to light. The notebook validates Nancy's thoroughness as a detective: she did not stop at finding the will but pursued every lead until the full picture emerged.
