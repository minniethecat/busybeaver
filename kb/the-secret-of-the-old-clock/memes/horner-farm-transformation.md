# Horner Farm Transformation

Following their inheritance of seventy-five thousand dollars each from **[[josiah-crowley|Josiah Crowley]]**'s **[[the-lost-will|second will]]**, the **[[horner-sisters|Horner sisters]]** dramatically transform their rundown farm.

## Before

The farm on the **[[river-road|River Road]]** was in desperate condition: the house was "large and rambling, but apparently it had not been painted in years," shingles were falling from the roof, porches sagged, and the barn had barely survived the **[[storm-on-river-road|storm]]**. The sisters survived on Grace's sporadic dressmaking and Allie's small chicken operation.

## The Transformation

When **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]** visits weeks after the will reading, she is astonished:
- The house has "a fresh coat of white paint and a bright green roof"
- The yard is "well kept and beds of hardy plants had been set out"
- "Many new hen houses were going up" in the barnyard
- A "large number of chickens" are in evidence — all white Leghorns

## Allie's Enterprise

**[[allie-horner|Allie Horner]]** is "in her element," proudly showing Nancy her new **[[white-leghorn-chickens|white Leghorn flock]]** and incubators. She plans to "raise nothing but high-grade fowls." The farm has been transformed from a place of poverty into a thriving agricultural enterprise.

The transformation serves as the novel's final visual testament to **[[justice|justice]]** served — honest work and kindness rewarded with the means to build a better life.
