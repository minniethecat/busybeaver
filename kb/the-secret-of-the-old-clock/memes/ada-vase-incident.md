# Ada Topham Breaks the Vase

During a shopping trip to a **[[river-heights|River Heights]]** department store, **[[ada-topham|Ada Topham]]** accidentally knocks a fragile imported vase from a counter with her sleeve, smashing it to pieces. Rather than taking responsibility, Ada attempts to walk away and then falsely accuses the saleswoman of breaking the vase herself. Her sister **[[isabel-topham|Isabel Topham]]** corroborates the lie.

## Nancy's Intervention

**[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]**, who had been browsing in the store and witnessed the accident, steps forward to testify on behalf of the bewildered saleswoman. She quietly tells the floor manager, "I am certain the saleswoman did not break the bowl, for I saw the accident myself."

When Ada demands to know what business it is of Nancy's, Nancy responds that she "can't permit you to accuse this girl of something she didn't do."

## Aftermath

The floor manager informs Ada that the vase was imported from Italy and costs fifty dollars. After initially refusing to pay, Ada relents and writes a check, but warns both the manager and Nancy that "the matter won't be dropped here."

This incident deepens the enmity between the **[[topham-family|Tophams]]** and Nancy Drew. The saleswoman expresses gratitude, telling Nancy she could not have paid for the bowl and would likely have been discharged. This encounter reinforces Nancy's commitment to **[[fairness|fairness]]** and **[[justice|justice]]**, values that drive her investigation of the **[[the-lost-will|Crowley will]]**.
