# White Leghorn Chickens

White Leghorn chickens are central to **[[allie-horner|Allie Horner]]**'s livelihood and aspirations. Before receiving her **[[inheritance|inheritance]]**, Allie's chicken operation provides the **[[horner-sisters|Horner sisters]]**' "only means of support" when dressmaking work declines.

## The Struggle

Allie explains to **[[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]]** that she could make the chicken business pay "if only I could afford to start a flock of white leghorns." The market for eggs has dropped, and a **[[chicken-deaths|strange disease]]** kills many of her birds — "ten dead" in one morning alone. The losses devastate Allie, who has "worked so hard with her chickens" and considers many of them "pets."

## After the Inheritance

With her inheritance, Allie fulfills her dream. The **[[horner-farm-transformation|transformed farm]]** features "many new hen houses," incubators, and a large flock of Leghorns. Nancy observes: "I never saw so many chickens in all my life." Allie declares she will "raise nothing but high-grade fowls."

## Symbolism

The chickens represent the dignity of honest work. Unlike the **[[topham-family|Tophams]]**, who seek wealth through inheritance schemes and **[[stock-market-speculation|stock market speculation]]**, Allie's ambition is to build a productive enterprise through her own labor. The inheritance simply provides the capital to realize this dream.
