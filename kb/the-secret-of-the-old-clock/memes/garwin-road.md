# Garwin Road

The road leading from the fork near Melborne to the town of [[garwin]] — the escape route taken by the robbers who stole [[crowley-clock|the Crowley clock]]. [[nancy-drew]]'s decision to pursue the thieves down this road, while sending the Melborne marshal down the opposite fork, was the critical tactical move that led to the recovery of the clock and, ultimately, the discovery of [[josiah-crowley]]'s missing will. (018-032) (018-035)

## Location

Garwin Road branches off as the lefthand fork from the main highway near Melborne. The road leads to the town of Garwin, and at the time of the chase it was a smooth highway with a clear stretch ahead, allowing for rapid driving. (018-032) (018-038)

## Description

The road was smooth enough to permit fast driving — Nancy "drove rapidly" once committed to the pursuit. The robbers' moving van was already well ahead, having taken the Garwin road after departing the abandoned roadhouse where they had temporarily stashed the clock. (018-038) (018-038)

The fork where the marshal and Nancy split up was a critical decision point. The marshal took the righthand road while Nancy "headed for Garwin" down the lefthand fork — an inspired guess that proved correct. (018-035)

## Role in the Mystery

The Garwin Road represents the final chase sequence of the novel. After [[nancy-drew]] escaped from the abandoned roadhouse with the Crowley clock, the robbers' moving van was proceeding slowly toward Garwin. Nancy's decision to split from the marshal and pursue independently down this road was a gamble — had she chosen wrong, the robbers would have escaped. Instead, her pursuit led to the marshal catching up and the eventual recovery of both the clock and the will hidden inside it. (018-035) (018-038) (021-013)
