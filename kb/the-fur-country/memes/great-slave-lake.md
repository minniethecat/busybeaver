# Great Slave Lake

The Great Slave Lake is a major subarctic lake that forms the southern anchor of the [[hudsons-bay-company|Hudson's Bay Company]]'s northern trading network in *The Fur Country*. It marks the starting point of [[jaspar-hobson|Lieutenant Jaspar Hobson]]'s expedition to the [[arctic-ocean|Polar Sea]].

## Geography

The lake is one of the largest bodies of water in the far north of America:

> [004-002] One of the largest of the lakes beyond the 61st parallel is that called the Great Slave Lake; it is two hundred and fifty miles long by fifty across, and is situated exactly at 61° 25′ N. lat. and 114° W. long. The surrounding districts slope down to it, and it completely fills a vast natural hollow.

The lake is dotted with little islands of granite and gneiss. Its northern banks are clothed with thick forests, forming a boundary between habitable land and the barren "Cursed Land" beyond.

## Strategic Position

The lake's position at the centre of the hunting districts made it a crucial hub for the [[hudsons-bay-company|Hudson's Bay Company]]:

| Fort | Location | Role |
|------|----------|------|
| [[fort-providence|Fort Providence]] | North shore | Trading post |
| Fort Resolution | South shore | Trading post |
| [[fort-reliance|Fort Reliance]] | Northeast extremity | Regional headquarters |

> [004-002] The position of the lake in the very centre of the hunting districts, once swarming with game, early attracted the attention of the Company. Numerous streams either take their rise from it or flow into it — the Mackenzie, the Athabasca, etc.

[[fort-reliance|Fort Reliance]], situated on the northeastern extremity, was about three hundred miles from the Chesterfield Inlet on Hudson's Bay.

## Flora and Climate

Despite its far northern latitude, the lake's shores supported remarkable vegetation:

> [004-004] The trees on the northern shores of the lake form magnificent forests. We need not be astonished at meeting with such fine vegetation in this remote district. The Great Slave Lake is not really in a higher latitude than Stockholm or Christiania.

The forests consisted almost entirely of poplar, pine, and birch, providing essential timber for [[hudsons-bay-company|the Company]]'s forts. In summer, [[fort-reliance|Fort Reliance]] was "surrounded with masses of verdure, refreshing to the sight after the long dreary winter."

## Expedition Departure Point

The lake served as the starting point for Hobson's expedition:

> [005-007] On the morning of the 16th April Lieutenant Jaspar Hobson and his party were ready to start. The route across the known districts, between the Slave Lake and that of the Great Bear beyond the Arctic Circle, was already determined.

From [[fort-reliance|Fort Reliance]], the expedition would travel northwest toward [[great-bear-lake|Great Bear Lake]], then onward to the Arctic coast via the [[coppermine-river|Coppermine River]].
