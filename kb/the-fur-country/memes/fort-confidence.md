# Fort Confidence

**Fort Confidence** is a [[hudsons-bay-company|Hudson's Bay Company]] post on the northern extremity of the [[great-bear-lake|Great Bear Lake]] — the last outpost of [[hudsons-bay-company|the Company]]'s established network before the unknown territory of the Arctic coast. For [[jaspar-hobson|Lieutenant Hobson]]'s expedition, it represents the frontier: beyond it, there are no more factories, no more resupply points, only the uncharted wilderness stretching north to the [[arctic-ocean|Polar Sea]]. Although less prominent than Fort Hope in the narrative, the fort's mention establishes the network of HBC outposts that form the logistical backbone of the Company's Arctic operations. Its position on the southern shore of the lake placed it at a critical junction between the subarctic forests and the barren grounds to the north.

## In the Novel

### Overview

The expedition reaches Fort Confidence after the long overland journey from [[fort-reliance|Fort Reliance]]. Hobson is anxious to arrive there, hoping to gather intelligence about the districts beyond — intelligence that only the men who live on the edge of the unknown can provide:

> [007-003] "He was anxious to get to Fort Confidence, on the further side of the Great Bear Lake, where he hoped to obtain some useful information."

The fort itself, when they reach it, is described with Verne's characteristic attention to commercial geography. It is "a most important position," built at the lake's northern extremity, its location chosen so that the frozen waters of winter and the navigable waters of summer both serve its purposes. It resembles other Company factories further south, but its isolation gives it a different character — a frontier post where the familiar routines of the fur trade meet the vast silence of the north.

> [008-004] "Fort Confidence was a most important position, built on the northern extremity of the lake, close to its waters, which being frozen over in winter, and navigable in summer, afforded easy access to Fort Franklin and other posts."

The fort's "fleet" consists of a single fishing boat — the vessel that will take Hobson and [[paulina-barnett|Paulina Barnett]] across the lake to the Indian encampment, and into the storm that nearly claims their lives. The modesty of this fleet underscores the thinness of [[hudsons-bay-company|the Company]]'s presence at the frontier: one boat, a handful of men, and the endless lake.

> [008-020] "Here was anchored the fleet of Fort Confidence, consisting of a single fishing-boat — the very one which was to take Mrs. Barnett and Hobson to the Indian encampment the next day."

## Historical Note

Fort Confidence was a real [[hudsons-bay-company|Hudson's Bay Company]] post, established in 1837 on the Dease Arm of [[great-bear-lake|Great Bear Lake]]. It served as a base for the expeditions of Peter Warren Dease and Thomas Simpson, who mapped much of the Arctic coastline between 1837 and 1839. The fort was abandoned in the 1840s, well before the novel's 1859 setting.
