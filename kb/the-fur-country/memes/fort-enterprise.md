# Fort Enterprise

**Fort Enterprise** is a [[hudsons-bay-company|Hudson's Bay Company]] trading post that serves as a critical waypoint on [[jaspar-hobson|Lieutenant Jaspar Hobson]]'s expedition from [[fort-reliance|Fort Reliance]] to the [[great-bear-lake|Great Bear Lake]] in Jules Verne's *The Fur Country*. The overland journey between the two forts covers approximately two hundred miles of challenging subarctic terrain.  Fort Enterprise occupies a significant place in the geography of Arctic exploration, its name evoking the spirit of commercial and territorial ambition that drove the Hudson's Bay Company's expansion into the northern wilderness. As a waypoint on the route to the Arctic coast, it represents one link in the chain of outposts that made Hobson's expedition logistically possible.

## The Journey from Fort Reliance

### Overview

The expedition's movement from [[fort-reliance|Fort Reliance]] to Fort Enterprise occupies Chapter V of the novel, and the chapter title — "From [[fort-reliance|Fort Reliance]] to Fort Enterprise" — signals the route's importance in the narrative geography. The journey began on the morning of 16th April and lasted fifteen days, arriving at Fort Enterprise on 1st May.

> [005-007] "On the morning of the 16th April Lieutenant Jaspar Hobson and his party were ready to start. The route across the known districts, between the Slave Lake and that of the Great Bear beyond the Arctic Circle, was already determined. Jaspar Hobson was to make for Fort Confidence, on the northern extremity of the lake."

> [005-052] "No incident worth mentioning occurred during the next fifteen days. The weather continued favourable, the cold was not too severe, and on the 1st May the expedition arrived at Fort Enterprise."

## Geographic Context

Fort Enterprise occupies a strategic position in the chain of [[hudsons-bay-company|HBC]] posts stretching northward toward the Arctic:

| Fort | Position | Role |
|------|----------|------|
| [[fort-reliance|Fort Reliance]] | Southern start point | Expedition's departure base |
| **Fort Enterprise** | Intermediate waypoint | Rest and resupply |
| [[fort-confidence|Fort Confidence]] | Northern extremity of [[great-bear-lake|Great Bear Lake]] | Intelligence-gathering post |
| [[fort-hope|Fort Hope]] | Arctic coast | Expedition's final destination |

## Narrative Role

The journey to Fort Enterprise is notable for its relative calm — "no incident worth mentioning" — which stands in sharp contrast to the dramatic events that follow. This period of smooth travel serves as a narrative breathing space before the expedition crosses the [[arctic-circle|Arctic Circle]] and enters the more perilous northern territories. The arrival at Fort Enterprise also marks the point at which the characters have covered two hundred miles, with the expedition's true hardships still ahead.

> [006-002] "Two hundred miles had been traversed since the expedition left Fort Reliance. The travellers, taking advantage of the long twilight, pressed on day and night, and were literally overcome with fatigue."
