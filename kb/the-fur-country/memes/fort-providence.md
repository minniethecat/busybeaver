# Fort Providence

Fort Providence is a [[hudsons-bay-company|Hudson's Bay Company]] outpost situated on the northern shore of the [[great-slave-lake|Great Slave Lake]]. It served as one of several important forts ringing the lake and was a key waypoint for travellers and traders journeying between [[hudsons-bay-company|the Company]]'s southern districts and the far northern territories.

## Location and Role

The fort was one of several built on the shores of the [[great-slave-lake|Great Slave Lake]] after [[hudsons-bay-company|the Company]] recognised the lake's strategic position at the centre of the hunting districts:

> [004-002] Numerous streams either take their rise from it or flow into it — the Mackenzie, the Athabasca, etc.; and several important forts have been constructed on its shores — Fort Providence on the north, and Fort Resolution on the south.

Fort Providence, on the northern shore, was positioned to intercept furs and travellers moving between the lake's northern reaches and the great rivers flowing toward the [[arctic-ocean|Arctic Ocean]].

## Notable Events

[[paulina-barnett|Mrs. Paulina Barnett]] and [[jaspar-hobson|Lieutenant Hobson]] narrowly escaped death near Fort Providence when their boat was torn apart by a violent storm on the [[great-slave-lake|Great Slave Lake]]. They were rescued by three Eskimo kayakers:

> [009-066] Half an hour afterwards, the shipwrecked travellers were lying on the sandy beach three miles above Fort Providence.

The fort provided refuge for the exhausted and half-drowned travellers, demonstrating the vital role these remote outposts played as sanctuaries in a hostile environment.
