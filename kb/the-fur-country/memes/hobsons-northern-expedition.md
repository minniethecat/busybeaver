# Hobson's Northern Expedition

Hobson's Northern Expedition of 1859 is the central narrative framework of *The Fur Country*. Led by Lieutenant [[jaspar-hobson|Jaspar Hobson]], the expedition departed [[fort-reliance|Fort Reliance]] in April 1859 with a triple mission: to explore the lands north of the [[great-bear-lake|Great Bear Lake]], to establish a new [[hudsons-bay-company|Hudson's Bay Company]] fort on the Arctic coast, and to escort [[thomas-black|the astronomer]] [[thomas-black|Thomas Black]] to a suitable observation point for the [[solar-eclipse-1860|solar eclipse of July 18, 1860]].

## Mission Objectives

The expedition was conceived by the [[hudsons-bay-company|Hudson's Bay Company]] in response to the northward migration of fur-bearing animals:

> [001-029] The object of this enterprise was to found a fort, or factory, to the north of the limits already reached by the hunters of furs, in order to afford them some degree of protection in the heart of these rich preserves.

Hobson, a "child of [[hudsons-bay-company|the Company]]" born at Fort Assiniboin, was chosen for his proven merit, zeal, and intrepidity. He had distinguished himself during the Oregon struggles and rapidly rose to the rank of lieutenant.

## Route

The expedition followed a carefully planned route northward:

| Stage | Location | Date |
|-------|----------|------|
| 1 | [[fort-reliance|Fort Reliance]] ([[great-slave-lake|Great Slave Lake]]) | April 16 departure |
| 2 | [[fort-enterprise|Fort Enterprise]] (Snare Lake) | Revictualling stop |
| 3 | [[fort-confidence|Fort Confidence]] ([[great-bear-lake|Great Bear Lake]]) | Late May |
| 4 | [[coppermine-river|Coppermine River]] valley | June |
| 5 | [[coronation-gulf|Coronation Gulf]] (Arctic coast) | Early July |
| 6 | [[victoria-island|Victoria Land]] ([[cape-bathurst|Cape Bathurst]]) | Mid-July — [[fort-hope|Fort Hope]] founded |

## Founding of Fort Hope

Upon reaching [[cape-bathurst|Cape Bathurst]], Hobson selected an admirably suited site and oversaw the [[founding-fort-hope|construction of Fort Hope]], a substantial wooden factory with palisades, warehouses, and living quarters. The fort was intended to become [[hudsons-bay-company|the Company]]'s northernmost establishment — a purpose tragically subverted by the discovery that it had been built on a foundation of ice.
