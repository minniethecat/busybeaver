# Fort Franklin

Fort Franklin is a [[hudsons-bay-company|Hudson's Bay Company]] outpost situated on the southern shore of the [[great-bear-lake|Great Bear Lake]]. It forms part of a network of forts around the lake that facilitated [[hudsons-bay-company|the Company]]'s fur-trading operations in the far north.

## Location and Access

The fort's position on the southern shore of the [[great-bear-lake|Great Bear Lake]] provided convenient access to the lake's waters, which were frozen in winter and navigable in summer:

> [008-004] Fort Confidence was a most important position, built on the northern extremity of the lake, close to its waters, which being frozen over in winter, and navigable in summer, afforded easy access to Fort Franklin, on the southern shores, and promoted the coming and going of the Indian hunters with their daily spoils.

This seasonal duality — ice highway in winter, waterway in summer — made the lake forts an integrated system rather than isolated posts.

## Namesake

The fort was named after **[[franklin|Sir John Franklin]]** (1786–18[[TOC|47]]), the British Royal Navy officer and Arctic explorer who charted over 5,550 miles of the North American coastline between 1819 and 1822. [[franklin|Franklin]]'s tragic later expedition to find the Northwest Passage, in which he and all his men perished, was a well-known event at the time of the novel's setting.

## Role in the Fur Trade

Together with [[fort-confidence|Fort Confidence]], [[fort-hope|Fort Good Hope]], and [[fort-reliance|Fort Reliance]], Fort Franklin formed the [[hudsons-bay-company|Hudson's Bay Company]]'s northernmost operational ring. These forts served as:
- Trading posts for furs brought in by Indian hunters
- Supply depots for expeditions pushing further north
- Resting places for convoys transporting furs south
