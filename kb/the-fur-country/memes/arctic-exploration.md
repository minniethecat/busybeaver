# Arctic Exploration

**Arctic exploration** forms the historical and thematic context of Jules Verne's *The Fur Country*. The novel's central expedition — [[jaspar-hobson|Lieutenant Jaspar Hobson]]'s journey to establish [[fort-hope|Fort Hope]] on the Arctic coast — takes place within a broader tradition of polar discovery that had captured the European imagination throughout the nineteenth century. Verne weaves this historical context into the narrative through references to real explorers and expeditions.  The novel draws upon the rich historical context of mid-nineteenth-century Arctic exploration, including the Franklin expedition's disappearance and the subsequent search efforts that captured the Victorian public's imagination. Verne transforms this historical backdrop into the foundation for his fictional narrative, blending real geographical knowledge with speculative adventure.

## Historical Context

### Overview

The mid-nineteenth century was the heroic age of Arctic exploration. European powers, particularly Britain, dispatched expedition after expedition to map the northern coast of North America, to locate the [[northwest-passage|Northwest Passage]], and — after 1845 — to search for [[franklin|Sir John Franklin]]'s lost expedition.

> [010-020] "But the Northwest Passage—that is to say, the direct communication by sea between the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans—was not then discovered?"

## The Novel's Place in the Tradition

*The Fur Country* belongs to a tradition of Arctic fiction that includes Mary Shelley's *Frankenstein* (1818) and, later, the works of Jack London. Verne's distinctive contribution is his fusion of commercial logic ([[hudsons-bay-company|Hudson's Bay Company]] interests) with geographical discovery. Hobson's expedition is not a voyage of pure science like those of Parry or [[franklin|Franklin]] — it is a business venture that happens to push the boundaries of the known world.

> [007-011] "Yes, madam," replied Jaspar Hobson; "but I think the hyperborean regions will longer resist thorough exploration."

> [010-034] "Everyone shared the Lieutenant's impatience, and resolutely resisted fatigue in order to reach the Arctic Ocean with the least possible delay. They were drawn onwards by an indefinable attraction."
