# Earthquake of January 8

The earthquake of **January 8, 1860** is the pivotal geological catastrophe of *The Fur Country*. This violent subterranean convulsion severed the isthmus connecting [[victoria-island|Victoria Land]] to the North American continent, transforming what had been a peninsula into a drifting ice-island that carried [[fort-hope|Fort Hope]] and all its inhabitants across the [[arctic-ocean|Arctic Ocean]].

## The Event

The earthquake struck [[cape-bathurst|Cape Bathurst]] with tremendous force:

> [024-002] The peninsula of Victoria, which the best maps of English America join to the American continent, had been torn suddenly away from it. This peninsula was in fact nothing but an immense piece of ice, five hundred square miles in extent, converted by successive deposits of sand and earth into apparently solid ground well clothed with vegetation. Connected with the mainland for thousands of centuries, the earthquake of the 8th of January had dragged it away from its moorings.

## Mechanism

The earthquake was connected to volcanic activity in the region — a chain of igneous hills and an active volcano lay near [[cape-bathurst|Cape Bathurst]]. Hobson later understood that:

> [024-003] The isthmus — that is to say, the neck of land which connected the peninsula of Victoria with the mainland — had been snapped in two by a subterranean convulsion connected with the eruption of the volcano some months before.

The frozen Arctic winter masked the effects for months. With the sea frozen solid, the broken isthmus was not immediately apparent. Only when the spring thaw arrived and the ice fields melted did the full extent of the catastrophe become visible.

| Phase | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| Winter | Frozen sea holds the severed island in place, masking the break |
| Spring thaw | Ice fields melt, island begins drifting on the current |
| Summer | Island carried westward toward [[bering-sea|Bering Strait]] |

## Consequences

The earthquake's effects were profound and far-reaching:

- **Geological**: The crest of [[cape-bathurst|Cape Bathurst]] broke off; cliffs collapsed onto the beach
- **Topographical**: The soil on the west was depressed, the east elevated — the horizontal ground became a westward slope
- **Hydrological**: When the thaw came, Lake Barnett and Paulina River waters threatened to inundate the western coast
- **Existential**: The colonists found themselves adrift on an ice-island, 500 square miles in extent, heading toward the Pacific

Hobson later reflected grimly on the discovery:

> The wandering Island might at any moment settle down in consequence of a change in its specific gravity, or break up into more or less numerous islets, the duration of which must necessarily be ephemeral.
