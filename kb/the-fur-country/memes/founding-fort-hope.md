# Founding of Fort Hope

The founding of [[fort-hope|Fort Hope]] in July 1859 represents the triumphant culmination of [[hobsons-northern-expedition|Lieutenant Hobson's northern expedition]] — and the tragic irony at the heart of *The Fur Country*. The fort was built on what appeared to be the solid ground of [[cape-bathurst|Cape Bathurst]], only to be revealed months later as an enormous sheet of ice adrift on the [[arctic-ocean|Arctic Ocean]].

## Site Selection

Upon reaching the Arctic coast in early July 1859, Hobson carefully selected a site at [[cape-bathurst|Cape Bathurst]]:

> [012-026] The site was indeed admirably suited for the foundation of a new settlement. The wooded heights surrounding the lagoon would supply plenty of pine, birch, and fir for the construction of the house. Game abounded in the neighbourhood, and the lake and river promised good returns to the fishermen.

The site offered a natural harbour, a freshwater lagoon (later named Lake Barnett), and a small river (Paulina River) teeming with salmon and other fish. The surrounding woods provided ample timber for construction.

## Construction

The carpenter **[[mac-nab|Mac-Nab]]** directed the construction with characteristic Highland efficiency:

| Element | Material | Method |
|---------|----------|--------|
| Foundation | Leveled and beaten earth/sand | Ash layer from burnt brushwood for damp-proofing |
| Framework | Scotch fir timbers (~20 ft) | Mortise and tenon joinery, ends hardened in fire |
| Walls | Planks and beams | Upright posts sunk several feet into ground |
| Roof | Horizontal beams with trusses | Overhanging eaves like a chalet |
| Interior | Partitioned rooms | Wooden flooring over ash base |

The construction was completed rapidly through [[mac-nab|Mac-Nab]]'s skilled workmanship and the ready availability of timber.

## Layout

[[fort-hope|Fort Hope]] was built as a substantial wooden house with:

- Ground floor and upper storey
- A central courtyard enclosed by palisades
- Storehouses for furs and provisions
- A separate building for the soldiers
- A magazine for gunpowder
- A dog kennel for the sledge teams

## The Ironic Legacy

The founding of [[fort-hope|Fort Hope]] took on a devastating irony when Hobson later realised:

> [024-003] Yes, Fort Hope was built upon ice! The isthmus — the neck of land which connected the peninsula of Victoria with the mainland — had been snapped in two by a subterranean convulsion.

The factory, built with such care and hope, was destined to drift across the [[arctic-ocean|Arctic Ocean]] on a foundation of ice, its purpose as a permanent trading post irreversibly compromised. Hobson's instructions had been "to build upon a rock," but as he ruefully acknowledged: "he had not even built upon sand."
