# Cape Krusenstern

Cape Krusenstern is a prominent cape on the Arctic coast of North America, situated at the point where the coastline turns abruptly northward beyond the mouth of the [[coppermine-river|Coppermine River]]. It was passed by [[jaspar-hobson|Lieutenant Jaspar Hobson]]'s expedition as they reached the [[arctic-ocean|Polar Sea]].

## Geographical Position

The cape marks a critical turning point in the Arctic coastline:

> [011-002] On the other side the coast turned abruptly to the north beyond the mouth of the Coppermine River, and ended in Cape Krusenstern.

To the south lies the broad estuary of [[coronation-gulf|Coronation Gulf]], dotted with the islands of the Duke of York Archipelago. Cape Krusenstern anchors the northern limit of the coast visible from the gulf's eastern reach.

## Namesake

The cape was named after **Adam Johann von Krusenstern** (1770–1846), a Baltic German admiral and explorer in Russian service who led the first Russian circumnavigation of the globe (1803–1806). His name appears on geographical features across the Arctic and North Pacific, reflecting the era's practice of honouring explorers through toponymy.

## Position in Hobson's Journey

After reaching [[coronation-gulf|Coronation Gulf]], Hobson's expedition continued along the coast past Cape Krusenstern in search of a suitable site for the new Company fort. The cape represented one of the last named geographical features on the known maps before the expedition ventured into the truly unknown territory that would become [[victoria-island|Victoria Land]].
