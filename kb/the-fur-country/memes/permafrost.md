## Definition

Permafrost is ground — soil, gravel, or bedrock — that remains at or below the freezing point of water for two or more consecutive years. In the [[arctic-circle|Arctic region]]s of *The Fur Country*, permafrost extends to depths of several hundred feet, thawing only in a thin "active layer" at the surface during the brief summer. This frozen foundation dictates what can be built, where, and for how long.

## Practical Implications

For the [[hudsons-bay-company|Hudson's Bay Company]]'s operations, permafrost is both curse and ally. The frozen ground makes digging foundations and burying stores nearly impossible without fire and pickaxe. Yet it also provides a stable, rock-hard surface for [[dog-sledging|dog sledging]] through the long winter months, when thawed ground would be impassable bog.

At [[fort-hope|Fort Hope]], the construction of the palisade, barracks, and storehouses must account for the thermal properties of the soil. Any structure that transmits heat into the ground risks melting the permafrost beneath it, causing uneven settling, collapse, or the formation of treacherous thermokarst depressions.

## Survival Context

The permafrost plays a quiet but decisive role in the survival arithmetic of the novel. It locks away organic material — there is no soil to farm, no deep roots to anchor timber. The party at [[fort-hope|Fort Hope]] is entirely dependent on imported provisions and hunted game because the ground itself cannot sustain agriculture. When the ice breakup isolates the fort in Part II, the permafrost ensures that no alternative food source can be cultivated from the land.
