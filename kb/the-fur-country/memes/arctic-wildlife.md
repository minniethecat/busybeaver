# Arctic Wildlife

The wildlife of the [[arctic-circle|Arctic region]]s is both the economic foundation of the fur trade and the means of survival for the colonists in *The Fur Country*. From the [[polar-bear-encounter|polar bear]]s that menace [[fort-hope|Fort Hope]] to the reindeer that provide meat and hides, animals are a constant presence throughout the narrative.

## Fur-Bearing Animals

The [[fur-trade|fur trade]] depended on the rich populations of fur-bearing animals that inhabited the northern forests and tundra:

| Animal | Fur Value | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Otter (inland) | £50–£60 each | Prized in China, decreasing demand |
| Beaver | High | Foundation of the Canadian fur trade |
| Marten | Moderate | Abundant near [[fort-hope|Fort Hope]] |
| Fox | Varies | Multiple species, trapped extensively |
| Ermine | High | Used for luxurious garments |
| Polecat | Moderate | Hunted near the fort |
| Muskrat | Lower | Widespread, easily trapped |

The novel notes that the fur-bearing animals had been driven further north by over-hunting, which was the very reason [[hudsons-bay-company|the Company]] needed to establish new forts beyond the [[arctic-circle|Arctic Circle]].

## Hunted Game

Edible game sustained the colonists:

- **Reindeer (Caribou)**: The primary source of fresh meat. Hobson's hunters brought in regular supplies from the herds that migrated near the fort.
- **Polar Hares**: Abundant and "underwent every variety of culinary treatment from [[mrs-joliffe|Mrs. Joliffe]]."
- **Waterfowl**: Ducks, grouse (willow-fowl, *Tetrao tetrix*), and other birds flocked to the lagoon.
- **Fish**: Trout, pike, salmon, whitebait, and smelts teemed in Lake Barnett and Paulina River.

## Predators

The [[polar-bear-encounter|polar bear]] was the most feared predator of the Arctic:

> The colonists' encounters with polar bears provided some of the most dramatic moments at Fort Hope. After the [[earthquake-january-8|earthquake]], a period of bear attacks threatened the settlement, and the bears seemed to vanish as mysteriously as they had appeared — perhaps frightened away by the same seismic disturbance.

Other predators included Arctic foxes, wolves (mentioned in passing), and the occasional grampus or whale passing in the offing.

## The Seal Question

Seals abounded on the western shore of [[victoria-island|Victoria Island]], but Hobson forbade their capture at first, wanting to determine how best to use them. Seal oil would have been an invaluable resource for lighting and fuel during the polar night.
