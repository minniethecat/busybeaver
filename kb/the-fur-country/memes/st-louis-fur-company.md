# St. Louis Fur Company

The St. Louis Fur Company (also referred to as the American Fur Company) is the principal commercial rival of the [[hudsons-bay-company|Hudson's Bay Company]] in the North American fur trade. While it plays only a peripheral role in *The Fur Country*, its existence represents the competitive pressure that drove [[hudsons-bay-company|the Company]]'s relentless northward expansion.

## Competitive Context

By the mid-nineteenth century, the [[hudsons-bay-company|Hudson's Bay Company]] had absorbed or outlasted most of its rivals, but the American company remained:

> [002-023] Now the only rival of this important association is the American St. Louis Fur Company.

The Company's vast domain — extending over 3,700,000 square miles — was built through decades of competition with fur-trading enterprises of various nationalities.

## A Shadowy Presence

The St. Louis Fur Company appears in the narrative only indirectly. During the struggle in Oregon, the [[hudsons-bay-company|Hudson's Bay Company]] had clashed with "the rival companies of the Union" — a reference to American fur interests operating out of St. Louis and the Pacific Northwest.

One brief encounter occurs when a stranger arrives at [[fort-hope|Fort Hope]], initially suspected of being a rival agent:

> [016-042] "To the St. Louis Fur Company," replied the stranger with a bow.

## Historical Context

The real-world St. Louis fur trade was dominated by several companies and prominent families throughout the nineteenth century. St. Louis, situated at the confluence of the Missouri and Mississippi Rivers, served as the gateway to the western fur trade. Companies like the **American Fur Company** (founded by John Jacob Astor in 1808) and the **Missouri Fur Company** (founded 1809) competed fiercely with the [[hudsons-bay-company|Hudson's Bay Company]] for control of the rich fur territories of the American West.

## Relationship to the Hudson's Bay Company

| Aspect | [[hudsons-bay-company|Hudson's Bay Company]] | St. Louis Fur Company |
|--------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Nationality | British | American |
| Territory | British North America, Rupert's Land | United States, Oregon Country |
| Headquarters | Fort York (Nelson River) | St. Louis, Missouri |
| Period | Founded 1670 | Active early–mid 19th century |

The novel presents the [[hudsons-bay-company|Hudson's Bay Company]] as the dominant force, with its American rival serving mainly as a reminder that [[hudsons-bay-company|the Company]]'s monopoly was never absolute.
