# Dog Sledging

Dog sledging was the indispensable technology of Arctic travel in the era of *The Fur Country*. The [[hudsons-bay-company|Hudson's Bay Company]]'s entire northern operation — from the movement of furs to the provisioning of remote forts — depended on teams of dogs pulling loaded sledges across the frozen landscape.

## The Dog Teams

The novel provides vivid descriptions of dog sledges in action. At [[fort-reliance|Fort Reliance]], a typical sledge team consisted of six dogs:

> [003-006] At last the door swung open, and the Sergeant was almost upset by a sledge, drawn by six dogs, which dashed past him like a thunderbolt.

The dogs were hardy Arctic breeds, capable of pulling heavy loads at considerable speed over snow and ice. Their ability to travel when all other means of transport were impossible made them more valuable than horses in the far north.

## The Expedition's Sledges

For [[hobsons-northern-expedition|Hobson's expedition]], [[hudsons-bay-company|the Company]] had assembled an impressive sledge train:

> [004-048] A dozen sledges, with their teams of dogs, had been procured from the outlying stations, and brought together at Fort Reliance.

| Equipment | Quantity | Purpose |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| Sledges | 12+ | Transport of men, provisions, and equipment |
| Dogs | 72+ (6 per team) | Motive power |
| Harnesses | Complete sets | Dog control |

The sledges carried everything needed for an expedition expected to last many months: provisions, ammunition, tools, clothing, scientific instruments, and trade goods for encounters with native peoples.

## Seasonal Constraints

Dog sledging was strictly a winter activity. The thaw transformed the landscape:

- Frozen rivers became impassable torrents
- Smooth snow surfaces became rough, uneven ground
- The sledges' runners could no longer glide
- Lakes became liquid barriers requiring detours

Hobson noted that the expedition must reach its destination before the spring melt, observing that "winter is the time to visit the hyperborean regions, for they are then far easier to traverse."

## Speed and Efficiency

Under good conditions, the dog teams could cover about **fifteen miles a day** — the rate Hobson maintained during the expedition's first weeks. When conditions were favourable, they could travel even faster, as when the dogs advanced "at full gallop" down the Coppermine valley toward the [[arctic-ocean|Arctic Ocean]].
