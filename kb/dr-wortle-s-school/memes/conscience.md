## Overview

Conscience in *Dr. Wortle's School* is the ultimate court of appeal. When social convention, ecclesiastical authority, and even the law fail to provide clear guidance, characters turn to conscience as the arbiter of right action. The novel consistently privileges the verdict of individual conscience over the judgments of institutions.

## Dr. Wortle's Conscience

[[jeffrey-wortle|Dr. Wortle]] grounds his most consequential decisions in conscience rather than ecclesiastical obedience or social prudence. When [[the-bishop-of-broughton|the Bishop]] demands that he dismiss [[mr-peacocke|Mr. Peacocke]], Wortle refuses on the grounds that conscience forbids it:

> "My first duty would be to my parish and to my conscience." (012-024)

This elevation of conscience above institutional authority is the novel's most radical moral claim. It places the individual's internal moral sense above the collective judgment of church and society.

## Conscience vs. Reputation

The novel repeatedly tests whether conscience can withstand the pressure of reputation. Characters who follow conscience risk social ruin; those who follow social convention risk inner corruption. [[mrs-wortle|Mrs. Wortle]] embodies the quiet victory of conscience—she arrives at the right moral conclusion not through theological reasoning but through an unshakeable inner conviction.

## The Bishop's Failure

The Bishop represents the failure of institutional conscience. He acts not from genuine moral conviction but from a desire to manage appearances and protect ecclesiastical authority. His conscience is outsourced to procedure. The novel's judgment is clear: a conscience that merely ratifies institutional convenience is no conscience at all.

## Conscience and Action

Conscience in Trollope's world is not passive. It demands action, often costly action. Mr. Peacocke's journey to America, Dr. Wortle's defiance of the Bishop, and Mrs. Peacocke's refusal of shame are all acts of conscience—decisions made at personal cost because the inner voice will not permit otherwise.
