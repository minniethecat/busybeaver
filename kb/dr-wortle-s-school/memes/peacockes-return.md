## Overview

[[mr-peacocke|Mr. Peacocke]]'s return to Bowick from his American journey is the novel's moment of resolution. Armed with documentary proof of [[ferdinand-lefroy|Ferdinand Lefroy]]'s death, Peacocke returns to reclaim his position, his wife, and his good name. The return transforms the novel's emotional register from anxiety and conflict to relief and restoration.

## The Proof

Peacocke returns not with mere testimony but with documents—death certificates, witness statements, legal affidavits. The novel insists on the materiality of proof: truth must be written down, signed, and sealed before it can defeat rumour. This emphasis on documentation reflects Trollope's lawyerly sensibility and his understanding of how reputation is constructed and reconstructed in a bureaucratic society.

The proof of Ferdinand Lefroy's death is the key that unlocks every closed door in the novel. Once established, it validates the Peacockes' marriage, removes the legal cloud over their relationship, and deprives [[mrs-stantiloup|Mrs. Stantiloup]] and her allies of their ammunition.

## The Reunion

The reunion between Peacocke and [[mrs-peacocke|Mrs. Peacocke]] is rendered with characteristic Trollopian restraint. There are no dramatic embraces or declarations—instead, a quiet restoration of the domestic order that the scandal had disrupted. [[jeffrey-wortle|Dr. Wortle]]'s welcome is equally understated but deeply felt: his faith in Peacocke has been vindicated, and the school can return to its proper business.

## Vindication

The return is also a public vindication of [[jeffrey-wortle|Dr. Wortle]]'s judgment. The schoolmaster who defied the Bishop, the press, and public opinion is proven right. The novel's resolution affirms that personal faith, grounded in knowledge of character, is a more reliable guide than institutional procedure:

> "I have never been wont to be ashamed of myself;—nor do I think that I shall now begin." (028-054)

The return scene, and the novel's closing chapters, present a world restored to order—but an order now strengthened by having survived the challenge to it.
