## Identity

Don Dionisio Alcalá Galiano is a historical figure who appears in the novel as one of the Spanish commanders at the Battle of Trafalgar. He is a brigadier in the [[affiliation::armada-espanola]] and an accomplished cartographer and navigator, known for his expedition to the Strait of Magellan (006-005). In the narrative, he commands the [[vessel::bahama]], a 74-gun ship of the line in the combined Franco-Spanish fleet. He is portrayed as a man of learning, severity tempered by justice, and unyielding courage.

## Role in the Narrative

Galiano first appears at the council of war held aboard Villeneuve's flagship on 8 October 1805. Alongside [[friend::churruca]], Gravina, and other Spanish commanders, he is among those who unanimously oppose the French admiral's plan to take the fleet to sea (008-018). The debate grows so heated that Galiano nearly fights a duel with the French Rear-Admiral Magon:

> "Villeneuve having expressed his wish to go out to sea, we Spaniards unanimously opposed it. The discussion was warm and eager, and Alcalá Galiano and Magon exchanged such hard words that it must have come to a duel if we had not intervened to pacify them." (008-019)

His judgment proves prophetic. When Villeneuve overrides the Spanish council and orders the fleet to sea, Galiano fights aboard the Bahama in the thick of the action. A crewman who survives describes him as "the bravest seaman in the fleet" and recounts his final hours: Galiano reviews the crew at noon, declares the flag nailed to the mast, and directs his guns through a head wound until a cannonball strikes him dead (016-009)-(016-015).

After his death, the Bahama's crew loses heart and the ship surrenders. She founders before reaching Gibraltar (016-025).

## Historical Context

Dionisio Alcalá Galiano (1760--1805) was a distinguished Spanish naval officer and hydrographer. In 1792, commanding the schooners *Sutil* and *Mejicana*, he completed a celebrated survey expedition of the Strait of Magellan alongside Cayetano Valdés -- an achievement referenced twice in the novel (006-005), (008-018). His scientific reputation was widely recognized. At Trafalgar, he commanded the Bahama and was killed in action on 21 October 1805.
