# Bucentaure

The French flagship at Trafalgar, a powerful 80-gun ship of the line from whose quarterdeck Admiral Villeneuve directed the combined fleet in the hours before the British attack shattered his formation.

---

## Overview

The *Bucentaure* was a French 80-gun ship of the line, launched in 1803 and among the newest major warships in the combined fleet at Trafalgar. As Villeneuve's flagship, she occupied the center of the Franco-Spanish line and was the nerve center from which tactical signals were issued — the "Bucentaure signalled that we were to put about" (010-002) and the "admiral signalled that the ships were to form in five lines" (010-001).

Her position made her a natural target for Nelson's attack. The *Victory* passed close under her stern, and the combination of British fire and the general collapse of the combined fleet's formation left the flagship isolated and eventually captured.

## Design and Armament

The *Bucentaure* was an 80-gun two-decker, a design that represented a compromise between the firepower of a three-decker and the handling qualities of a 74. She carried 30 × 36-pounders on her lower deck and 32 × 24-pounders on her upper deck, with additional lighter guns on her quarterdeck and forecastle. Her crew numbered approximately 700 men.

## At Trafalgar

As Villeneuve's flagship, the *Bucentaure* was the command center of the combined fleet. Her signal flags conveyed the admiral's orders to the fleet — first forming "in five lines — the van, centre, and rear" (010-001), then ordering the fleet to "put about" (010-002) as Nelson's attack developed. The failure of the fleet to execute these maneuvers effectively was not the flagship's fault — poor crew training, light winds, and the sheer difficulty of coordinating 33 ships of two nationalities made Villeneuve's orders largely aspirational.

When the British columns struck, the *Bucentaure* was in the center of the line. Nelson's *Victory* passed under her stern at close range, and the *Bucentaure* suffered heavy casualties. Villeneuve survived, but his flagship was captured, and the admiral himself was taken prisoner. The ship went down in the storm that followed the battle.
