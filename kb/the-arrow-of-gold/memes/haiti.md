# Haiti

Haiti is a Caribbean nation occupying the western portion of the island of Hispaniola. In *The Arrow of Gold*, it appears in a single conversational reference by Monsieur George, but this brief mention opens a revealing window into Captain Blunt's character and the racial attitudes of the novel's world.

## Location

- **Geography**: Caribbean Sea, western third of Hispaniola, east of Cuba
- **Coordinates in the Novel**: Referenced at the Café de la Cannebière, Marseilles, during the Carnival-night conversation
- **Real-World Counterpart**: Real — Haiti declared independence from France in 1804; Conrad's reference is literal

## Role in the Narrative

When Captain Blunt, in the midst of a discussion about his South American travels, asks George what he knows of South American republics, George replies modestly: "Wandering about the Gulf of Mexico I had a look-in here and there; and amongst others I had a few days in Haiti which was of course unique, being a negro republic" (PN=001-051). This remark serves as a hinge: it prompts Blunt to launch into an extended and knowledgeable discourse on "negroes at large" — a performance of expertise so unexpected from the boulevardier-exterior Captain that it surprises George and prompts Mills to reveal Blunt's South Carolina origins.

The Haiti reference thus does double narrative work: it establishes George's genuine seafaring credentials while simultaneously triggering the revelation of Blunt's hidden depth and Southern American background.

## Symbolic Dimension

Haiti appears in the novel as a signifier of the exotic and the exceptional — "of course unique, being a negro republic." For Conrad's European characters, it represents a political anomaly: a black-governed state in a world of white empires. The reference also subtly positions George as a man who has seen the wider world beyond Europe, reinforcing his identity as a wanderer whose experience makes him simultaneously useful to and alienated from the legitimist circles he now moves in.

> "Wandering about the Gulf of Mexico I had a look-in here and there; and amongst others I had a few days in Haiti which was of course unique, being a negro republic." ((PN=001-051))
