# Bastia

Bastia is a port town on the northeastern coast of Corsica. In *The Arrow of Gold*, it appears as the practical destination of Dominic's mission to acquire the smuggling vessel that will make the Carlist gun-running operation possible.

## Location

- **Geography**: Northeastern coast of Corsica, on the Tyrrhenian Sea, facing Italy
- **Coordinates in the Novel**: Accessible by mail-boat from Marseilles; Dominic departs "in the morning" after the late-night planning session
- **Real-World Counterpart**: Real — Bastia is the second-largest city of Corsica and its principal commercial port

## Role in the Narrative

After George and Dominic agree on the purchase of a fast sailing craft for the Carlist arms-smuggling operation, Dominic knows exactly where to find one: "He knew of one suitable but she was in Corsica. Offered to start for Bastia by mail-boat in the morning" (PN=007-007). This laconic declaration takes place in Madame Léonore's café on the Marseilles quay, with the mature and handsome Léonore looking on.

The Bastia mission is a turning point: it transforms the conspiracy from talk into action. Dominic's matter-of-fact competence — he knows the boat, knows where she is, and will leave at dawn — contrasts with the abstract political discussions of the legitimist salons. The mail-boat to Bastia represents the first concrete step in the gun-running scheme.

## Symbolic Dimension

Bastia embodies the operational reality beneath the political romance. While Mills speaks of the Principle of Legitimacy and Blunt of his sword, Dominic quietly books passage to Bastia to secure a boat. The town's very name — matter-of-fact, unglamorous — belongs to the world of mail-boats and harbour schedules, not to the world of salons and royalist gush. It is the geography of getting things done.
