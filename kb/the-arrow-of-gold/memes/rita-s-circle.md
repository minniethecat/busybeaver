# Doña Rita's Circle

The informal social network that forms around Doña Rita in Marseilles — a fluid assembly of exiles, adventurers, and legitimist sympathizers whose personal relationships are inseparable from their political operations.

## Role in the Narrative

The circle functions as the novel's social infrastructure. It is through this network that the gun-running operation is organized, that information flows, and that the personal dramas of rivalry and desire play out. The circle's membership includes Mills, Blunt, the narrator, and a shifting cast of Carlist agents and European exiles. Its social character — at once intimate and political, passionate and calculating — defines the novel's distinctive atmosphere.

The circle is held together by Rita's personal magnetism rather than by any formal structure. When she is present, it coheres; when she withdraws, it threatens to dissolve. This makes it the perfect social form for a novel about the entanglement of the personal and the political.
