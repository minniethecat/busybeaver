# Lord X

An unnamed English aristocrat who serves as a background financier of the Carlist conspiracy. Though he never appears in person, his wealth and social position enable key elements of the plot.

## Identity

- **Full Name**: Never revealed; referred to only as "Lord X"
- **Nationality / Origin**: English
- **Affiliation**: Carlist cause (indirect); relative of Mills
- **First Mention**: Introduced as Mills's relation at a legitimist gathering in Marseilles (002-010)

## Role in the Narrative

Lord X functions as the financial backbone of the Carlist arms-smuggling operation in the novel's backstory. When Mills recounts how he came to be involved with the Carlist cause, he describes joining "a small steamer chartered by a relative of his, 'a very wealthy man'" — and the narrator immediately connects this to Lord X: "(probably Lord X, I thought)" (002-010). This steamer was carrying "arms and other supplies to the Carlist army" before being chased ashore by a Republican ironclad.

In Parisian royalist circles, Lord X is spoken of with admiration. The banker's wife in Marseilles goes into "ecstasies over 'ce cher Monsieur Mills' and that magnificent Lord X" (026-036), indicating his social standing among Legitimist sympathisers.
