# Baron H.

A shadowy Carlist operative who functions as a connective figure between the novel's social world in Marseilles and the military reality of the Carlist campaign in Spain.

## Identity

- **Full Name**: Baron H. (full name never revealed)
- **Nationality / Origin**: Unclear; possibly Spanish with a European title
- **Affiliation**: Carlist cause; operates between France and Spain
- **First Appearance**: Mentioned among the officials who visited Doña Rita at Tolosa (003-031)

## Role in the Narrative

Baron H. appears in the narrative only through references, but these references establish him as an important node in the Carlist network. During Doña Rita's visit to the Carlist headquarters at Tolosa, "Baron H., a fellow with a pretty wife, who had made so many sacrifices for the cause, raised a great to-do about seeing her and she consented to receive him for a moment" (003-031). The detail about his "pretty wife" and "sacrifices for the cause" sketches a man whose personal and political lives are intertwined.

Later, Baron H. reappears as a practical operative: messages are carried "from Baron H. to the Quartel Real in Tolosa" (020-005), and arrangements are made according to "the Baron's wish, as a matter of prudence" (020-007). The narrator proposes to "arrange with Baron H. that he should be sent away" (020-026), suggesting that the Baron functions as a dispatcher or coordinator of Carlist personnel.
