# Portrait of Rita

The painting of Doña Rita that functions in the novel as a meditation on the nature of beauty and the relationship between a person and their representation.

## Role in the Narrative

The portrait serves as a material embodiment of the novel's central concern with appearance and reality. Like Rita herself, the portrait is an object of fascination — something that can be possessed visually but never fully captured. The narrator's description of Rita throughout the novel is itself a kind of portraiture, an attempt to render in words what the painted portrait renders in pigment.
