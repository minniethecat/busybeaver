# Allègre's Art Collection

The collection of art and precious objects assembled by the wealthy connoisseur Henry Allègre, which upon his death passes to Doña Rita and provides the material setting for much of the novel's action.

## Role in the Narrative

The collection functions as more than décor. It represents the world of cultivated taste and wealth that Allègre built and that Rita now inhabits, serving as both inheritance and burden. The objects in the collection — paintings, sculptures, rare books — create an atmosphere of refined luxury that contrasts with the rough world of gun-running and political conspiracy taking place within its walls.

The collection also symbolizes the transformation Allègre effected in Rita herself: from a goatherd in the Basque hills to the mistress of a world-class collection of art. The objects surrounding her are material evidence of a life that was given to her, but that she must now sustain on her own terms.
