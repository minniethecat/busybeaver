# Portsmouth

Town on [[ohio-river|the Ohio]] River in the state of [[ohio|Ohio]], significant as a site of narrow escape during [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]]'s flight from slavery. Portsmouth represented the first free-state town [[josiah-henson|Henson]] reached after crossing from [[kentucky|Kentucky]].

## Location & Geography

Portsmouth is located in Scioto County, [[ohio|Ohio]], at the confluence of the Scioto and [[ohio-river|Ohio River]]s. During [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s escape, it was a river port on the border between the slave state of [[kentucky|Kentucky]] and the free state of [[ohio|Ohio]].

## Role in the Narrative

Portsmouth marks a critical moment in [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s escape journey. Upon arriving at Portsmouth, [[ohio|Ohio]], [[josiah-henson|Henson]] had a very narrow escape from detection. He was carrying bricks in a handkerchief as part of his disguise as a laborer, and the unusual nature of his baggage attracted suspicion [017-003].

During his short stay in Portsmouth, several persons accosted him, appearing very anxious to learn details about him — a dangerous situation for a fugitive. [[josiah-henson|Henson]] managed to wait until the afternoon steamboat, timing his departure to arrive further upriver after dark [017-003].

The passage through Portsmouth illustrates the persistent danger fugitive slaves faced even after reaching free-state territory, where slave catchers and suspicious citizens could still threaten their [[freedom|liberty]].

## Key Passages

> On arriving at Portsmouth, in the State of Ohio, I had a very narrow escape. I was carrying bricks in a handkerchief, and this, together with my somewhat suspicious appearance, attracted the attention of several persons. [017-003]

> I reached Portsmouth in the morning, and waited until two in the afternoon for the steamboat, so that I might not arrive in Maysville till after dark. [017-003]
