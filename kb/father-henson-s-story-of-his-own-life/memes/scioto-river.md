# Scioto River

River in central and southern [[ohio|Ohio]], encountered by [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]] and his family during their [[escape-to-canada|escape from slavery]] in 1830. At the Scioto, the fugitives located the beginning of General Hull's military road, which allowed them to travel by day with renewed hope.

## Location & Geography

The Scioto River flows southward through [[ohio|Ohio]] from its headwaters near present-day Auglaize County to its mouth at [[ohio-river|the Ohio]] River at [[portsmouth|Portsmouth]]. During [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s escape journey, his party reached the Scioto after travelling north from [[ohio-river|the Ohio]] River through southern [[ohio|Ohio]].

## Role in the Narrative

[[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s family had been travelling by night and resting by day — the standard precaution for fugitives — when they reached the Scioto. They had been told that at this river they would "strike the military road of General Hull, in the last war with [[england|Great Britain]], and might then safely travel by day" [013-004].

The family found the road "by the large sycamore and elms which marked its beginning, and entered upon it with fresh spirits early in the day" [013-004]. The discovery of the road at the Scioto marked a turning point in their journey — they could now make faster progress northward. However, the road proved difficult: "Nobody had told us that it was cut through the wilderness, and I had neglected to provide any food" [013-004]. The family spent two hungry days traversing the rough road before reaching aid.

The road through the wilderness proved a harsh test:

> We travelled on all day without seeing one [habitation], and lay down at night, hungry and weary enough. The wolves were howling around us, and though too cowardly to approach, their noise terrified my poor wife and children.
>
> (013-004)
