# Sandusky

City on the shore of [[lake-erie|Lake Erie]] in northern [[ohio|Ohio]], significant as a destination on [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]]'s escape route and a well-known terminus of [[underground-railroad|Underground Railroad]] routes leading to [[canada|Canada]].

## Location & Geography

Sandusky is located on Sandusky Bay, an arm of [[lake-erie|Lake Erie]], in Erie County, [[ohio|Ohio]]. In the era of [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s narrative, the area south and west of Sandusky was described as a wilderness of cut-down trees, with the village itself consisting of small houses [013-007]. The city served as a port for steamships crossing [[lake-erie|Lake Erie]] to Canadian ports.

## Role in the Narrative

Sandusky appears at two key moments in [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s story. During his initial escape to [[canada|Canada]], [[josiah-henson|Henson]] and his companions passed through Sandusky, finding it a modest settlement on the edge of the great lake [013-007]. Here they encountered a captain who agreed to transport them across [[lake-erie|Lake Erie]] to [[canada|Canada]] — a moment of providential [[freedom|deliverance]].

Later, during the planning of his return journey to rescue his family, [[josiah-henson|Henson]] identified Sandusky as the destination for the overland stage from [[cincinnati|Cincinnati]]. The route was sixty-five miles from [[maysville|Maysville]] to [[cincinnati|Cincinnati]], followed by a stage to Sandusky on [[lake-erie|Lake Erie]] [017-006]. However, the leaky steamboat disrupted this plan.

## Key Passages

> We reached the neighborhood of Sandusky, a city on Lake Erie. The houses of the village were in sight, but we were still in the wilderness of trees which had been cut down. [013-007]

> We thought we could reach Cincinnati before daylight, and then take the stage for Sandusky. Our boat sprung a leak before we had gone half the distance. [017-006]
