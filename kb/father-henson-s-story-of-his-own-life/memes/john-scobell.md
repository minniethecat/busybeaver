John Scobell was the Secretary of the [[british-and-foreign-anti-slavery-society|British and Foreign Anti-Slavery Society]] who played a crucial role in vindicating [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]]'s reputation during his 1851 visit to [[england|England]]. When charges of imposture were leveled against [[josiah-henson|Henson]], Scobell served on the oversight committee and was subsequently dispatched as an agent to [[canada|Canada]] to personally investigate the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn Settlement]].

## The Committee of Twelve

When [[josiah-henson|Henson]] arrived in [[england|England]] in 1851, he faced a printed circular accusing him of being "an impostor, obtaining money under false pretences." In response, [[josiah-henson|Henson]] requested the formation of a committee to oversee his fundraising. Scobell was appointed to this committee in his capacity as Secretary of the [[british-and-foreign-anti-slavery-society|Anti-Slavery Society]]:

> This committee had been appointed, and consisted of Samuel Gurney, Samuel Gurney, Junior, Samuel Marley, Esq., George Hitchcock, Esq., Rev. James Sherman, Rev. Thomas Binney, Rev. John Branch, Eusebius Smith, Esq., John Scobell, Secretary of the British and Foreign Anti-slavery Society, Lord Ashley (now Earl of Shaftsbury), George Sturge, and Thomas Sturge. [020-007]

Scobell was further appointed to the sub-committee of three — alongside [[john-branch|Rev. John Branch]] and [[eusebius-smith|Eusebius Smith]] — tasked with the detailed oversight of [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s finances:

> The sub-committee of three were, John Scobell, Rev. John Branch, and Eusebius Smith, who appointed Samuel Gurney, Junior, treasurer. [020-007]

## The Canadian Investigation

To put the public controversy to rest, the committee resolved at their own expense to send Scobell to [[canada|Canada]] to conduct a thorough inquiry into [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s character and the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn Settlement]]:

> They were pleased to assure me of their entire satisfaction; but to give perfect quiet to the public they determined, at their own expense, to send an agent to Canada, to make a full inquiry into the matter, and advised me to accompany him. Accordingly John Scobell and myself started for Canada immediately. [020-011]

Upon arriving in [[canada|Canada]], Scobell examined the settlement, interviewed residents, and convened a public convention in [[detroit|Detroit]] to hear testimony. The convention unanimously repudiated the charges, and Scobell returned to [[england|England]] with a favorable report that enabled [[josiah-henson|Henson]] to clear the entire debt of the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn]] institution within months.
