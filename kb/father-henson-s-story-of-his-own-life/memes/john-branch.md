John Branch was a [[london|London]] clergyman who served on [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]]'s oversight committee during the 1851 [[england|England]] fundraising campaign. Appointed to both the full committee of twelve and the sub-committee of three, he was among the British religious leaders who safeguarded [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s financial integrity against charges of imposture.

## The Oversight Committee

When [[josiah-henson|Henson]] arrived in [[england|England]] to raise funds for the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn Settlement]] manual labor school, he was publicly attacked in a printed circular accusing him of being an impostor. In response, [[josiah-henson|Henson]] requested that a committee of British gentlemen be formed to oversee all contributions. Branch was among those appointed:

> This committee had been appointed, and consisted of Samuel Gurney, Samuel Gurney, Junior, Samuel Marley, Esq., George Hitchcock, Esq., Rev. James Sherman, Rev. Thomas Binney, Rev. John Branch, Eusebius Smith, Esq., John Scobell, Secretary of the British and Foreign Anti-slavery Society, Lord Ashley (now Earl of Shaftsbury), George Sturge, and Thomas Sturge. [020-007]

Branch was further selected as one of the three members of the sub-committee responsible for the detailed management and disbursement of funds, alongside [[john-scobell|John Scobell]] and [[eusebius-smith|Eusebius Smith]]:

> The sub-committee of three were, John Scobell, Rev. John Branch, and Eusebius Smith, who appointed Samuel Gurney, Junior, treasurer. Many of the above names are known throughout the world. [020-007]

The committee's oversight proved effective: within a few months, the entire debt of the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn]] institution was cancelled, and [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s reputation was fully vindicated through the investigation led by his fellow sub-committee member [[john-scobell|John Scobell]].
