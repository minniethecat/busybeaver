James Lightfoot was an enslaved man whose emotional plea at a religious meeting in [[fort-erie|Fort Erie]] moved [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]] to undertake a dangerous rescue mission to liberate his family from slavery in [[kentucky|Kentucky]]. His story appears in [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s narrative as an example of the risks taken to assist fugitives.

## The Fort Erie Meeting

[[josiah-henson|Henson]] first encountered James Lightfoot while preaching at a large gathering of colored people at [[fort-erie|Fort Erie]]. Lightfoot was in the congregation, and his visible distress — born of having left his family behind in [[slavery|bondage]] — stirred [[josiah-henson|Henson]] to action:

> I was once attending a very large meeting at Fort Erie, at which a great many colored people were present. In the course of my preaching I tried to impress upon them the importance of the obligations they were under; first, to God, for their deliverance; and then, secondly, to their fellow-men, to do all that was in their power for those who were still in bondage. In the congregation was a man named James Lightfoot, who was deeply agitated by the earnestness with which I had spoken. [016-002]

## The Rescue Mission

[[josiah-henson|Henson]] promised to assist Lightfoot's family to escape, and in the following autumn he set out on the long journey south to fulfill that promise:

> I remained at home, working on my farm, until the next autumn, soon after which time I had promised to assist in the restoring to liberty the friends of James Lightfoot, the individual who had excited my sympathy at the meeting at Fort Erie. [017-001]

The rescue journey was arduous. [[josiah-henson|Henson]] traveled hundreds of miles, navigating dangers including detection, ice-filled river crossings, and wolves. Upon reaching the other side, a remarkable coincidence occurred:

> On landing a wonderful providence happened to me. The second person I met in the street was Jefferson Lightfoot, brother of the James Lightfoot previously mentioned, and one of the party who had promised to escape if I would assist them. [017-004]

The escape was successful, though one of the Lightfoot brothers fell gravely ill during the journey, causing great distress. Despite these hardships, [[josiah-henson|Henson]] brought the family to freedom in [[canada|Canada]]. The narrative later records that [[francis-master-frank-riley|Mr. Frank]] Taylor, the Lightfoots' owner, fell ill after discovering their escape and was eventually persuaded to free the remaining family members, who were subsequently brought to [[canada|Canada]].
