The **meeting with [[john-rolfe|John Rolfe]]** was a public inquiry held at the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn Settlement]] in 1851, presided over by [[john-rolfe|Rev. John Rolfe]] of [[toronto|Toronto]]. The mass meeting was convened by English supporters of [[josiah-henson|Henson]] to investigate accusations made against him regarding the management of the [[dawn-settlement|Manual Labor School]] and its finances. [[josiah-henson|Henson]] was fully exonerated, and the inquiry cleared the way for the cancellation of [[slavery|the institution]]'s debt.

## Background

[[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s visit to [[visit-to-england|England]] in 1851 was motivated by the financial needs of the [[dawn-settlement|Manual Labor School]] at [[dawn-settlement|Dawn]], which carried a debt of seven thousand five hundred dollars. [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s plan was to exhibit black walnut lumber from the settlement at the [[london|Great Exhibition]] in [[london|London]] to raise funds.[020-004]

> My project was to go to England, carrying with me some of the best specimens of black walnut boards our farm would produce, and to exhibit them in the great World's Industrial Exhibition, then in session in London.
> [020-004]

While in [[england|England]], [[josiah-henson|Henson]] preached in prominent pulpits, including those of [[thomas-binney|Rev. Thomas Binney]] and Rev. [[samuel-gurney|Samuel Gurney]], and was cordially received by British abolitionists. Funds were raised to cancel the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn Settlement]]'s debt.[020-007]

## The Accusation

During [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s time in [[england|England]], an accusation was made against him — likely by individuals with sectarian motives who sought to gain control of the settlement's property. [[josiah-henson|Henson]] described these people as pursuing "an unjust and unchristian course":[020-006]

> It was undoubtedly the plan of certain individuals of the party who assumed the care of the school, probably from unworthy sectarian feelings, to obtain entire possession of the property of the association.
> [020-006]

A meeting of [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s English supporters was called, and his accuser — who was in [[england|England]] — was asked to face [[josiah-henson|Henson]] directly.[020-008]

## The English Inquiry

Before a company of English gentlemen, [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s accuser presented his case. [[josiah-henson|Henson]] responded by restating the facts of his management and reminded the investigators of the property and funds he had personally contributed to the settlement over the years:[020-010]

> I simply re-stated to them all the facts I had previously made known. I reminded them of the condition of the property when I first took charge of it, and what it now was.
> [020-010]

The English gentlemen expressed their entire satisfaction with [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s account. However, to ensure complete transparency and public [[trust|confidence]], they resolved to send an agent to [[canada|Canada]] at their own expense for a full investigation.[020-011]

## The Mass Meeting at Dawn

The investigation culminated in a public mass meeting held at the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn Settlement]]:

> A mass meeting, of all interested in the matter, was called in the institution on the premises. A large assemblage met, and Rev. John Rolfe of Toronto, presided. A thorough examination into the records of the institution was made.
> [020-012]

[[john-rolfe|Rev. John Rolfe]], a respected minister from [[toronto|Toronto]], chaired the proceedings. The examination was thorough, scrutinizing [[slavery|the institution]]'s financial records. The result was a complete vindication:

> The result was fatal to my accusers. My innocence was fully proved, and my persecutors put to shame.
> [020-012]

## Aftermath

With his name cleared and public [[trust|confidence]] restored, [[josiah-henson|Henson]] was able to raise substantial funds:

> The ground was now prepared for me, and I reaped an abundant harvest. The whole debt of the institution was cancelled in a few months.
> [020-013]

The cancellation of the debt secured the future of the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn Settlement]]'s educational mission. [[josiah-henson|Henson]] was recalled to [[canada|Canada]] shortly after by the fatal illness of his wife.[020-013]

## Significance

The mass meeting at [[dawn-settlement|Dawn]] represents a rare instance in which an escaped slave and black community leader was afforded due process and public vindication against his accusers. The support of English abolitionists and the impartial chairmanship of [[john-rolfe|Rev. John Rolfe]] demonstrated the transnational networks that sustained the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn Settlement]] and other Canadian freedom communities.
