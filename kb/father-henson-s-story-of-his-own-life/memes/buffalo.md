# Buffalo

City on the eastern shore of [[lake-erie|Lake Erie]] in western [[new-york|New York]]. Buffalo was the final [[us-america|America]]n location [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]] and his family reached before crossing the Niagara River into [[canada|Canada]] and freedom. It was a key terminus on the [[underground-railroad|Underground Railroad]].

## Location & Geography

Buffalo is located at the eastern end of [[lake-erie|Lake Erie]], where the lake flows into the Niagara River. During the era of [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s escape (1830), it was a growing port city. Its location directly across [[mississippi-river|the river]] from [[canada|Canada]] made it a natural crossing point for freedom-seekers.

## Role in the Narrative

[[josiah-henson|Henson]] and his family reached Buffalo after a boat journey from [[sandusky|Sandusky]], [[ohio|Ohio]]. The captain of their vessel, having learned of their fugitive status, treated them with sympathy and assistance. The party arrived in the evening, but "it was too late to cross [[mississippi-river|the river]] that night" [013-011]. The next morning they crossed safely into [[canada|Canada]] and found themselves on free soil.

## Significance

Buffalo represents the final [[us-america|America]]n location in the [[henson-family-tree|Henson family]]'s [[escape-to-canada|escape journey]], making it a pivotal [[underground-railroad|Underground Railroad]] site. Its geographical proximity to [[canada|Canada]] made it one of the most important crossing points for freedom-seekers in the Great Lakes region.

The captain's parting words to [[josiah-henson|Henson]] captured the threshold moment between slavery and freedom:

> "You see those trees," said the noble hearted captain next morning, pointing to a group in the distance; "they grow on free soil, and as soon as your feet touch that you're a man. I want to see you go and be a freeman. I'm poor myself, and have nothing to give you; I only sail the boat for wages; but I'll see you across."
>
> (013-011)
