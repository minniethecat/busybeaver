Rev. Ephraim Peabody was a [[boston|Boston]] clergyman who served as the crucial connector between [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]] and the network of [[boston|Boston]] philanthropists whose financial backing made the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn Settlement]] possible. His introduction of [[josiah-henson|Henson]] to [[samuel-eliot|Samuel Eliot]] set in motion the fundraising chain that sustained [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s work in [[canada|Canada]].

## Introduction to Boston Philanthropy

When [[josiah-henson|Henson]] traveled through New [[england|England]] seeking support for the manual labor school and sawmill he was building at [[dawn-settlement|Dawn]], he needed a credible introduction to [[boston|Boston]]'s philanthropic elite. Peabody provided that introduction:

> Rev. Ephraim Peabody introduced me to Samuel Eliot, Esq., who was kind enough to examine carefully into all my representations, and to draw up a sketch of them, which was afterwards presented to Amos Lawrence, Esq., and others. (019-004)

This single introduction proved transformative. Eliot's sketch of [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s project was circulated among [[boston|Boston]]'s leading citizens, resulting in a collection of funds that provided the initial capital for [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s sawmill enterprise — which would become the economic engine of the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn Settlement]].

## The Chain of Support

Peabody's role can be understood as the first link in a philanthropic chain:

| Step | Actor | Action |
|------|-------|--------|
| 1 | **Rev. Ephraim Peabody** | Introduced [[josiah-henson|Henson]] to [[samuel-eliot|Samuel Eliot]] |
| 2 | [[samuel-eliot|Samuel A. Eliot]] | Examined [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s claims, wrote sketch |
| 3 | [[amos-lawrence|Amos Lawrence]] | Endorsed bank note, provided financial backing |
| 4 | [[boston|Boston]] donors | Collected funds for the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn]] sawmill |

Each subsequent link depended on the credibility conferred by the preceding one, making Peabody's initial act of introduction disproportionately consequential.

## Boston's Clerical Abolitionism

Peabody represented the clerical wing of [[boston|Boston]]'s [[abolition|abolitionist]] community — ministers who advanced the [[abolition|anti-slavery]] cause not primarily through public agitation but through institutional channels: introductions, character references, and fundraising among their congregations. This approach complemented the more confrontational activism represented by figures like [[boston-riot|William Lloyd Garrison]].

Peabody's willingness to vouch for a formerly enslaved Black man seeking support for a self-help enterprise also reflected the Unitarian commitment to individual moral agency — the belief that每一个人的尊严 (each person's dignity) should be recognized through practical support, not merely abstract sympathy.

## Historical Significance

While Peabody appears only briefly in [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s narrative, his intervention illustrates how the success of Black-led initiatives in the antebellum period often depended on white allies willing to open doors within their own networks. Without Peabody's introduction, [[josiah-henson|Henson]] might never have gained access to the capital that transformed [[dawn-settlement|Dawn]] from an aspiration into a functioning community.

Peabody served as [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s bridge to [[boston|Boston]]'s philanthropic circles:

> Rev. Ephraim Peabody introduced me to Samuel Eliot, Esq., who was kind enough to examine carefully into all my representations, and to draw up a sketch of them, which was afterwards presented to Amos Lawrence, Esq., and others. By means of this a collection of money to aid me was made, to which many of the leading gentleman of Boston contributed, amounting to about fourteen hundred dollars.
>
> (019-004)
