## Role in Publishing Henson's Autobiography

John Lobb was a prominent British lay preacher and businessman who became a close ally of [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]]. When [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s autobiography — _[[josiah-henson|Father Henson]]'s Story of His Own Life_ — was first printed in [[london|London]] in 1858, Lobb wrote the preface to the first edition.

In his preface, Lobb attested to [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s character and the truth of his account, lending the book the endorsement of a respected English [[christianity|Christian]]. The first edition had an initial run of 2,000 copies and served as both an antislavery testimony and a fundraising instrument for the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn Settlement]].

## Relationship with Henson

Lobb was among a circle of British evangelical and abolitionist supporters who championed [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s cause in [[england|England]]. His willingness to publicly endorse [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s narrative was significant at a time when [[josiah-henson|Henson]] faced accusations from detractors who sought to undermine his credibility.
