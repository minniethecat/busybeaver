Pennsylvania was one of the free states through which [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]] traveled while conducting fugitive slaves to [[canada|Canada]] via the [[underground-railroad|Underground Railroad]]. (016-003)

The state also figured in [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s experience during his first visit to [[england|England]]. At a public meeting in [[exeter-hall|Exeter Hall]], [[london|London]], an eminent man from Pennsylvania addressed the anniversary of a [[sabbath|Sunday]] School Union, boasting of the great benefits of [[sabbath|Sunday]] Schools in the [[us-america|United States]] and asserting that all classes indiscriminately enjoyed their blessings. [[josiah-henson|Henson]] felt bound to contradict him. (022-001)

## The Exeter Hall Incident

During his first visit to [[england|England]], [[josiah-henson|Henson]] attended a [[sabbath|Sunday]] School anniversary at [[exeter-hall|Exeter Hall]] where a speaker from Pennsylvania made claims about universal access to religious education in [[us-america|America]]. [[josiah-henson|Henson]] challenged those claims directly:

> On one occasion, I recollect, an eminent man from Pennsylvania, was addressing the anniversary of a Sabbath School Union, who boasted of the great benefits of Sunday Schools in the United States, and asserted that all classes indiscriminately enjoyed their blessings. I felt bound to contradict him, and after putting to the speaker a few questions which he stammeringly answered, I told the immense meeting that in the Southern States, the great body of the colored people were almost entirely neglected, and in many places they were excluded altogether; and that even in the most of the Northern States, the great mass of the colored children were not sought out and gathered into Sunday Schools.
>
> (022-001)

[[josiah-henson|Henson]] noted that this contradiction "created some little storm," but his personal observation and experience carried conviction to the people. (022-001)
