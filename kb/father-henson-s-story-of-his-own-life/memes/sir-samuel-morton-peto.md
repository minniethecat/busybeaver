Sir Samuel Morton Peto (1809–1889) was a prominent British civil engineer, railway contractor, and Member of Parliament who lent his public standing to the abolitionist cause. He was among the distinguished speakers at [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]]'s farewell gathering in [[london|London]] during the summer of 1852.

## The Farewell Speech

Peto appeared at a celebratory dinner organized to honor [[josiah-henson|Henson]] before his return to [[canada|Canada]] after a successful fundraising and speaking tour of [[visit-to-england|England]]. He spoke alongside other notables including [[reverend-william-brock|Rev. William Brock]]:

> Among the distinguished persons who made speeches on this joyous occasion, I will mention the names of Rev. William Brock, Hon. Samuel M. Peto, and a Mr. Bess, brother-in-law of Mr. Peto, with his accomplished and beautiful lady. Thus ended one of the pleasantest days of my life. (022-009)

The presence of Peto — a sitting MP and one of the wealthiest contractors in Britain — signaled the breadth of support [[josiah-henson|Henson]] had attracted across British society, from clergymen to industrialists to parliamentarians.

## Abolitionist Engagement

Peto's involvement with [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s cause was part of a broader pattern of British elite support for abolitionist work in North [[us-america|America]]. British abolitionists, particularly within Nonconformist religious circles and the Liberal Party, saw the Canadian fugitive slave settlements as a practical demonstration that formerly enslaved people could build self-sustaining, educated communities when given access to land and resources.

The 1852 farewell dinner came just after the [[publishing-autobiography|publication]] of [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s narrative in [[london|London]], which had drawn wide attention from the British public. Peto's presence helped legitimize [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s mission in the eyes of potential donors and political allies.

Peto was among the distinguished guests at the [[lord-john-russell-dinner|Lord John Russell dinner]]:

> Among the distinguished persons who made speeches on this joyous occasion, I will mention the names of Rev. William Brock, Hon. Samuel M. Peto, and a Mr. Bess, brother-in-law of Mr. Peto, with his accomplished and beautiful lady. Thus ended one of the pleasantest days of my life.
