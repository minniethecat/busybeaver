# Richmond, Indiana

Town in eastern [[indiana|Indiana]] settled by [[quaker|Quakers]], known as a station on the [[underground-railroad|Underground Railroad]]. [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]] and a large party of fugitive slaves passed through [[indiana|Richmond]] and received critical assistance there during one of his rescue journeys from [[kentucky|Kentucky]] to [[canada|Canada]].

## Location & Geography

[[indiana|Richmond]] is located in east-central [[indiana|Indiana]], near [[ohio-river|the Ohio]] border. During [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s time, it lay on a route northward from [[cincinnati|Cincinnati]] toward [[lake-erie|Lake Erie]] and [[canada|Canada]]. The town had been settled by members of the Religious [[quaker|Society of Friends]] ([[quaker|Quakers]]), known for their antislavery convictions and active participation in the [[underground-railroad|Underground Railroad]].

## Role in the Narrative

After successfully crossing [[ohio-river|the Ohio]] River and arriving in [[cincinnati|Cincinnati]] with a party of fugitives, [[josiah-henson|Henson]] procured assistance and, "after stopping a short time to rest, we started for Richmond, Indiana" [016-006]. The choice of [[indiana|Richmond]] was deliberate — it was "a town which had been settled by [[quaker|Quakers]], and there we found friends indeed, who at once helped us on our way, without loss of time" [016-006].

The [[quaker|Quaker]] aid proved essential, as the party still faced a difficult journey ahead: "after a difficult journey of two weeks, through the wilderness, we reached [[toledo|Toledo]], [[ohio|Ohio]]" [016-006]. [[indiana|Richmond]] and its [[quaker|Quaker]] community represented a critical link in the chain of assistance that enabled [[josiah-henson|Henson]] to conduct fugitives safely through the free states to [[canada|Canada]].

[[josiah-henson|Henson]] described the [[quaker|Quaker]] welcome at [[indiana|Richmond]]:

> We started for Richmond, Indiana. This is a town which had been settled by Quakers, and there we found friends indeed, who at once helped us on our way, without loss of time.
>
> (016-006)
