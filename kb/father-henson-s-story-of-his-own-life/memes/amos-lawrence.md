Amos Lawrence was a [[boston|Boston]] merchant and philanthropist who was among the group of influential New [[england|England]] supporters that endorsed [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]]'s character and backed his business ventures. His interventions — endorsing a bank note during a financial crisis, providing funds to ship lumber to market, and holding a draft for [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s return journey to [[england|England]] — were instrumental at several critical junctures in [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s post-escape career.

## Boston Philanthropic Circle

Lawrence was part of a network of [[boston|Boston]] gentlemen who supported [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s endeavors after [[education|learning]] of his work at the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn Settlement]]. [[josiah-henson|Henson]] first approached these men during a journey of observation through [[new-york|New York]] and New [[england|England]], seeking support for the manual labor school and sawmill he was building in [[canada|Canada]]:

> On reaching Boston, Mass. I made known these facts and my feelings to some philanthropic gentlemen with whom I had become acquainted. (019-003)

Among this group were [[rev-ephraim-peabody|Rev. Ephraim Peabody]], [[samuel-eliot|Samuel A. Eliot]], and [[h-ingersoll-bowditch|H. Ingersoll Bowditch]] — all prominent [[boston|Boston]] figures who would vouch for [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s integrity.

## Endorsement During Financial Crisis

Lawrence's most consequential intervention came when [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s sawmill project faced a funding shortfall after the frame was completed but before operations could begin. [[josiah-henson|Henson]] returned to [[boston|Boston]], where his supporters listened to his accounting:

> I immediately returned to my Boston friends. Amos Lawrence, H. Ingersoll Bowditch, and Samuel A. Elliot, Esqs., listened to me again, and gave me to understand that they deemed me an honest man. They encouraged me in my business enterprise, and the approval of such men was like balm to my soul. They endorsed a note for me and put it into the bank. (019-006)

This endorsement was both practical and symbolic: the bank note provided immediate financial relief, while the moral validation from men of Lawrence's standing restored [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s credibility at a moment when his entire enterprise might otherwise have collapsed.

## Assistance with the Lumber Shipment

When [[josiah-henson|Henson]] attempted to ship 80,000 feet of black walnut lumber from [[canada|Canada]] to the [[boston|Boston]] market, a shipping agent forwarded the cargo only as far as [[new-york|New York]] and then abandoned it. Lawrence intervened:

> There great efforts were made to cheat me out of the lumber, but, by the good friendship of Mr. Lawrence, of Boston, who furnished me the means of having it re-shipped, I succeeded in bringing the whole eighty thousand feet safe to Boston, where I sold it to Mr. Jonas Chickering for forty-five dollars per thousand feet. (019-008)

The proceeds from this sale covered all [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s expenses and nearly canceled his debts. Without Lawrence's timely financial assistance for re-shipping, the lumber — and the investment it represented — would have been lost.

## Facilitator of England Fundraising

Lawrence also served as a reference and financial facilitator for [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s fundraising journeys to [[visit-to-england|England]]. When [[josiah-henson|Henson]] sought introductions for his first trip, Lawrence's name appeared alongside other prominent figures as a recommender, giving [[josiah-henson|Henson]] access to "the very best society in the kingdom" (020-007).

Later, when a British supporter named [[john-scobell|Mr. Scobell]] wished to fund [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s return to [[england|England]] for a second fundraising campaign, the draft was deposited with Lawrence:

> Mr. Scobell... sent me a letter, informing me that whenever I should see fit to return to England, I should find in the hands of Amos Lawrence, Esq., of Boston, a draft to defray the expenses of the journey. (020-013)

## Character and Significance

Lawrence's role exemplifies the type of practical, financially-grounded abolitionism practiced by [[boston|Boston]]'s mercantile elite. Unlike the more publicly visible [[abolition|abolitionist]] orators, Lawrence worked through business channels — bank endorsements, shipping funds, letters of credit. His interventions came at moments when [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s enterprises faced ruin, and each time the combination of financial backing and moral endorsement proved decisive.
