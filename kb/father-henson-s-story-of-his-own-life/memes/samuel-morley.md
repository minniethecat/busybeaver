## Henson's English Preaching Tour

During [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]]'s visit to [[england|England]] in 1851, he preached in the pulpits of several leading Congregational ministers in [[london|London]]. Among these was Samuel Morley, alongside other prominent divines including [[thomas-binney|Thomas Binney]], Baptist Noel, [[reverend-william-brock|William Brock]], [[james-sherman|James Sherman]], and George Smith. (020-007)

## Role in the Abolitionist Network

Morley was part of the network of British evangelical ministers who provided platforms for [[josiah-henson|Henson]] to speak about the condition of enslaved people in the [[us-america|United States]]. These preaching opportunities were instrumental in introducing [[josiah-henson|Henson]] to the British public and building the support base that would prove crucial to his fundraising for the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn Settlement]]. (020-007)

Morley's willingness to open his pulpit to a formerly enslaved Black preacher was characteristic of the British Congregationalist commitment to the antislavery cause, which continued long after the abolition of slavery in the [[england|British Empire]].
