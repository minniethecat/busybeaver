# Maysville

River town on [[ohio-river|the Ohio]] River in [[kentucky|Kentucky]], significant as a waypoint and near-confrontation site during [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]]'s [[escape-to-canada|escape from slavery]].

## Location & Geography

Maysville is located on [[ohio-river|the Ohio]] River in Mason County, [[kentucky|Kentucky]]. In [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s narrative, it is described as a steamboat landing on [[mississippi-river|the river]] route between the slave states of [[kentucky|Kentucky]] and the free city of [[cincinnati|Cincinnati]] [016-002]. The town served as a river port connecting the interior of [[kentucky|Kentucky]] to [[ohio-river|the Ohio]] River transportation network.

## Role in the Narrative

Maysville appears at a critical juncture in [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s escape journey. After leaving his enslaver's property in Davies County, [[josiah-henson|Henson]] traveled toward [[ohio-river|the Ohio]] River and arrived in the vicinity of Maysville. He was acutely aware of the danger of being recognized as a fugitive slave in this [[kentucky|Kentucky]] river town, where slave catchers were active [017-003].

[[josiah-henson|Henson]] carefully timed his arrival in Maysville to occur after dark, deliberately avoiding daytime exposure when he might be recognized. Despite his precautions, he was accosted by several individuals during his passage through the town, requiring him to maintain his composure under scrutiny [017-003]. He successfully boarded a steamboat at the Maysville landing and proceeded upriver toward [[cincinnati|Cincinnati]].

The distance from Maysville to [[cincinnati|Cincinnati]] — approximately sixty-five miles — represented the final overland leg of [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s journey out of slave territory [017-006].

## Key Passages

> I took care to wait for the steamboat, so that I might not arrive in Maysville till after dark. While in the town I was obliged to encounter several who scrutinized me closely, but I passed through without detection. [017-003]

> It was sixty-five miles from Maysville to Cincinnati, and we thought we could reach that city before night if we walked briskly. [017-006]
