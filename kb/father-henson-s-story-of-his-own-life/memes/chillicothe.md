# Chillicothe

Chillicothe, [[ohio|Ohio]], was the location where [[ohio-river|the Ohio]] Conference of the [[methodist-episcopal-church|Methodist Episcopal Church]] held its annual session, an event that drew [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]] during the early years of his religious ministry.

## The Ohio Conference

In the narrative, [[josiah-henson|Henson]] recounts traveling to Chillicothe to attend the Conference session. This journey was significant because it occurred during a period when [[josiah-henson|Henson]] was an exhorter and preacher, traveling considerable distances to participate in church governance and fellowship:

> "I felt it my duty to go, as usual, towards Chillicothe, to attend the session of the Ohio Conference of the Methodist Episcopal Church." (007-005)

## A Traveling Preacher

[[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s journey to Chillicothe followed a remarkably successful fundraising campaign. He had been traveling with a companion, preaching in towns and cities, and had raised one hundred and sixty dollars — a substantial sum:

> "In three or four days I left the city with no less a sum than one hundred and sixty dollars in my pockets, and with a soul jubilant with thanksgiving, and high in hope, directed my steps towards Chillicothe." (007-005)

With the funds raised, [[josiah-henson|Henson]] purchased a decent suit of clothes and an excellent horse, transforming himself into a traveling preacher of respectable appearance. The contrast between this treatment and the abuse of [[plantation|plantation life]] was profound:

> "The contrast between the respect with which I was treated and the ordinary abuse, or at best insolent familiarity, of plantation life, gratified me in the extreme, as it must any one who has within him one spark of personal dignity as a man." (007-006)

## Religious Context

The [[methodist-episcopal-church|Methodist Episcopal Church]] was the dominant religious institution in [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s early life. Its conference system brought together ministers and exhorters from across the region, providing governance, fellowship, and the assignment of preaching circuits. For an enslaved man like [[josiah-henson|Henson]], attendance at such a gathering represented both religious commitment and a rare measure of mobility and recognition within the church structure.

Chillicothe, as [[ohio|Ohio]]'s first capital (1803–1810), was an established town with the infrastructure to host such regional gatherings. The town's location in south-central [[ohio|Ohio]] made it accessible to delegates traveling from multiple circuits.

## Liberty Through Purchase

[[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s attendance at the Chillicothe conference was part of a larger mission: he was raising money to purchase his own freedom. Despite the allure of escape while in free territory, he remained committed to the legal path:

> "Still, dear as was the thought of liberty, I still clung to my determination to gain it in one way only — by purchase. The cup of my affliction was not yet full enough to lead me to disregard all terms with my master." (007-007)

This determination to purchase his freedom rather than simply flee was a defining characteristic of [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s early approach to [[freedom|emancipation]], and the funds raised on his preaching tour — including at Chillicothe — were directed toward that goal.
