**Speaking in England** refers to [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]]'s public speaking and preaching engagements during his visit to [[england|England]] in 1851. While his primary purpose was fundraising for the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn Settlement]] through exhibiting black walnut lumber at the [[worlds-fair-london|World's Fair in London]], [[josiah-henson|Henson]] was frequently invited to speak at public meetings, preach in prominent pulpits, and address charitable institutions. His appearances brought him into contact with leading figures of English society, including the Archbishop of Canterbury and [[lord-grey|Lord Grey]].

## Arrival and Reception

[[josiah-henson|Henson]] arrived in [[england|England]] with a dual purpose: to exhibit lumber at the [[london|Great Exhibition]] and to raise funds to clear the debt of the [[dawn-settlement|Manual Labor School]] at [[dawn-settlement|Dawn]].[020-004] He was warmly received by English abolitionists:

> Much to my astonishment, therefore, when I had arrived in England, and had been cordially received by the men above mentioned, and had preached in the pulpits of such men as Rev. Messrs. Thomas Binney and Samuel Gurney, I found myself in the midst of a storm of opposition.
> [020-007]

The "storm of opposition" referred to accusations made against [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s management of the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn Settlement]] — charges that were eventually investigated and dismissed at the [[meeting-john-rolfe|mass meeting presided over by Rev. John Rolfe]].[020-012]

## Ragged School Addresses

[[josiah-henson|Henson]] took particular interest in the Ragged School movement, a network of free schools for destitute children in [[england|England]]:

> While in England I was frequently called upon to speak at public meetings of various kinds. I was deeply interested in the Ragged School enterprise, and frequently addressed the schools, and also public meetings called in their aid.
> [022-001]

He saw a parallel between these schools and his own educational mission at [[dawn-settlement|Dawn]], and his addresses on their behalf helped build his reputation as a speaker and advocate.

## Meeting with Lord Grey

[[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s growing public profile led to a notable proposal from [[lord-grey|Lord Grey]], a prominent British statesman:

> Lord Grey made a proposition to me, which, if circumstances had permitted, I should have been glad to attempt to carry out. He said he had long been looking for a person of my capabilities to go to India, to introduce the culture of cotton there.
> [022-002]

The proposal would have sent [[josiah-henson|Henson]] to India to teach cotton cultivation — a remarkable testament to the reputation he had built among English reformers. [[josiah-henson|Henson]] declined, bound by his commitment to the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn Settlement]] and his family in [[canada|Canada]].[022-002]

## Audience with the Archbishop of Canterbury

One of the most notable encounters of [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s time in [[england|England]] was his meeting with the Archbishop of Canterbury. Invited to spend a day at the Archbishop's estate, [[josiah-henson|Henson]] was treated as an honored guest:

> On my second visit to England, I had an invitation, in company with a large number of Sabbath School Teachers, to spend a day on the premises of the Archbishop of Canterbury.
> [022-005]

At the conclusion of the visit, the Archbishop presented [[josiah-henson|Henson]] with a gift — a large quarto volume documenting the [[london|Great Exhibition]], inscribed with recognition of [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s contribution.[021-010]

## Significance

[[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s speaking engagements in [[england|England]] served several important purposes:

1. **Fundraising**: The public exposure translated directly into financial support — the entire debt of the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn Settlement]] was cancelled within months of his speaking campaign.[020-013]

2. **International abolitionist network**: [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s appearances strengthened the transatlantic ties between British abolitionists and Canadian freedom settlements.

3. **Personal transformation**: The respect and recognition [[josiah-henson|Henson]] received in [[england|England]] — being invited to preach in prominent pulpits, dine with aristocrats, and address large public meetings — stood in stark contrast to his former condition as a slave and fugitive.

4. **Model of black capability**: [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s eloquent speaking and dignified presence challenged racial stereotypes and presented a powerful argument for the capacity of black people to achieve at the highest levels of society.
