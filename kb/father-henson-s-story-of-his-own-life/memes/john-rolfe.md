## Role in Henson's England Visit

Rev. John Rolfe of [[toronto|Toronto]] was among the prominent men who furnished [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]] with complimentary letters of introduction before his departure for [[england|England]] in 1851. These letters, along with those from Chief Justice Robinson, Sir Allen McNab, and others, secured [[josiah-henson|Henson]] a cordial reception in English society. (020-004)

## Defense of Henson's Character

During [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s time in [[england|England]], a printed circular was circulated accusing him of being an impostor who obtained money under false pretenses. When news of this slander reached [[toronto|Toronto]], a mass meeting of all interested parties was called at the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn]] institution. Rev. John Rolfe presided over this assembly, which conducted a thorough examination of [[slavery|the institution]]'s records. (020-007)

The convention unanimously repudiated the false charges against [[josiah-henson|Henson]]. The originator of the slander, when confronted, denied having made it, and the accusation was proved upon him. (020-007)

[[josiah-henson|Henson]] recorded the outcome of this meeting, at which Rolfe presided:

> A mass meeting, of all interested in the matter, was called in the institution on the premises. A large assemblage met, and Rev. John Rolfe of Toronto, presided. A thorough examination into the records of the institution was made. The originator of the slander against me denied having made it; it was proved upon him, and the whole convention unanimously repudiated the false charges.
>
