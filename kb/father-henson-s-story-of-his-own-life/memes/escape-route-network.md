# Escape Route Network

The geographical network of locations and routes by which [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]] escaped from slavery in [[kentucky|Kentucky]] to freedom in [[canada|Canada]], traversing [[ohio-river|the Ohio]] River, [[indiana|Indiana]], [[ohio|Ohio]], [[lake-erie|Lake Erie]], and ultimately reaching the Canadian shore.

## Definition

In the context of [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s narrative, the escape route network refers to the interconnected chain of safe locations, rivers, lakes, and land routes that formed his path from [[slavery|enslavement]] in the [[us-america|America]]n South to freedom in [[canada|British Canada]]. This network was part of the broader [[underground-railroad|Underground Railroad]] system that helped thousands of enslaved people reach free territory in the decades before the [[us-america|America]]n Civil War.

## Henson's Route

[[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s escape journey began in [[kentucky|Kentucky]], where he was enslaved by [[amos-riley|Amos Riley]] in [[kentucky|Davis County]] (007-001). After resolving to escape, he crossed [[ohio-river|the Ohio]] River into the free state of [[ohio|Ohio]] (016-006), traveling at night through [[indiana|Indiana]] to avoid detection (012-002). He reached [[cincinnati|Cincinnati]], a major waypoint on the [[underground-railroad|Underground Railroad]] (017-009), and continued northward through [[ohio|Ohio]], passing near [[lake-erie|Lake Erie]] where escaped slaves were especially vulnerable to recapture (013-006).

The final legs of his journey brought him to [[buffalo|Buffalo, New York]] (013-011), and across [[lake-erie|Lake Erie]] to the Canadian shore, where he famously threw himself to the ground in joy upon touching free soil (013-012).

## Key Locations

The route encompassed multiple geographic points that served distinct functions in the escape:

- **[[kentucky|Kentucky]]** ([[kentucky|Davis County]]): Point of origin, where [[josiah-henson|Henson]] was enslaved and from which he fled
- **[[ohio-river|Ohio River]]**: The critical boundary between slave state and free territory, crossed under cover of darkness (016-006)
- **[[cincinnati|Cincinnati]]**: Major [[underground-railroad|Underground Railroad]] hub where fugitives could find temporary shelter (017-009)
- **[[ohio|Ohio]]**: Free state traversed on foot, marked by encounters with both sympathetic and hostile residents (013-006)
- **[[buffalo|Buffalo]]**: Final [[us-america|America]]n crossing point before reaching [[canada|Canada]] (013-011)
- **[[lake-erie|Lake Erie]]**: The water boundary between the [[us-america|United States]] and [[canada|Canada]], crossed by ferry
- **[[canada|Canada]]**: Destination of freedom, where [[josiah-henson|Henson]] first touched free soil (013-012)

## Henson's Perspective

[[josiah-henson|Henson]] described the escape as a carefully planned endeavor: "I matured it... I devised a plan of escape" (012-002). The journey was marked by close calls, including a "very narrow escape from being detected" in the region of [[ohio|Ohio]] near the lake (017-003). Upon reaching [[canada|Canada]], he experienced an overwhelming emotional release: "I threw myself on the ground, rolled in the sand, seized handfuls of it and kissed them" (013-012).

## Historical Context

The route [[josiah-henson|Henson]] traveled was typical of the [[underground-railroad|Underground Railroad]]'s western corridor, which funneled escapees from [[kentucky|Kentucky]] and the border states through [[ohio|Ohio]] and across [[lake-erie|Lake Erie]] to [[canada|Canada]]. The [[ohio-river|Ohio River]] served as the symbolic and practical boundary between slavery and freedom. After the [[fugitive-slave-law|Fugitive Slave Law of 1850]], [[canada|Canada]] became the only truly safe destination, as even free northern states were required to return escaped slaves to their owners.

> "When my feet first touched the Canada shore, I threw myself on the ground, rolled in the sand, seized handfuls of it and kissed them, and danced around, till in the eyes of the several bystanders I passed for a madman." (013-012)
