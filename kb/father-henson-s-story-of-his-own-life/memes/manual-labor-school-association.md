# Manual Labor School Association

The [[Manual Labor School Association]] was the organization that established and operated the manual-labor school at the [[Dawn Settlement]] in [[canada|Canada]] West (now [[canada|Ontario]]). The school was a key institution of the settlement, providing education not only to Black children but also to white and Indigenous youth.

## Origins

The school's origins trace to a convention of Black delegates held in [[london|London]], [[canada|Upper Canada]], in June 1838, called by [[Josiah Henson]] and [[Hiram Wilson]]. At this convention, [[josiah-henson|Henson]] proposed using donated funds to establish a manual-labor school, arguing that education and vocational training were essential for the community's long-term independence:

> "I urged the appropriation of the money to the establishment of a manual-labor school, where our children could be taught those elements of knowledge which are usually the occupations of a grammar-school; and where the boys could be taught, in addition, the practice of some mechanic art, and the girls could be instructed in those domestic arts which are the proper occupation and ornament of their sex." (018-005)

After three days of discussion, the proposal was unanimously adopted, and a committee of three was appointed to select a site. [[josiah-henson|Henson]] and Wilson were the active members of this committee.

## The Mill and School

The association's operations included a sawmill, which [[josiah-henson|Henson]] completed with borrowed funds. [[josiah-henson|Henson]] made clear that the mill was not his private property but belonged to the association:

> "I ought here to add, that the mill was not my own private property, but belonged to an association, which established an excellent manual-labor school, where many children and youth of both sexes have been educated. The school was well attended by both colored children, whites, and some Indians." (019-006)

## Later Developments

The school faced financial challenges over the years. [[josiah-henson|Henson]] later traveled to [[england|England]] to raise funds, and the voluntar​ily-assumed debt he incurred on behalf of the school was eventually discharged through his successful fundraising mission (021-005).
