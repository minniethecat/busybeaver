James Sherman was a [[london|London]] minister and abolitionist who was among the British religious leaders that received [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]] during his 1851 visit to [[england|England]]. He opened his pulpit to [[josiah-henson|Henson]] and served on the oversight committee that managed his fundraising for the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn Settlement]].

## Henson's England Reception

[[josiah-henson|Henson]] traveled to [[england|England]] in 1851 with the dual purpose of exhibiting black walnut lumber at the World's Industrial Exhibition in [[london|London]] and raising funds for the manual labor school at [[dawn-settlement|Dawn]]. Armed with letters of introduction, he was received by a circle of [[london|London]] clergy who gave him access to their pulpits. James Sherman was among them:

> Much to my astonishment, therefore, when I had arrived in England, and had been cordially received by the men above mentioned, and had preached in the pulpits of such men as Rev. Messrs. Thomas Binney, Baptist Noel, William Brock, James Sherman, George Smith, Dr. Burns, in London, and had already introduced my enterprise before a portion of the British public... [020-007]

## Committee Service

When a printed circular was distributed accusing [[josiah-henson|Henson]] of being an impostor, he asked his British supporters to form a committee to receive and disburse all contributions. Sherman served on this committee alongside [[samuel-gurney|Samuel Gurney]], [[thomas-binney|Thomas Binney]], [[john-branch|John Branch]], [[john-scobell|John Scobell]], and other prominent figures:

> This committee had been appointed, and consisted of Samuel Gurney, Samuel Gurney, Junior, Samuel Marley, Esq., George Hitchcock, Esq., Rev. James Sherman, Rev. Thomas Binney, Rev. John Branch, Eusebius Smith, Esq., John Scobell, Secretary of the British and Foreign Anti-slavery Society, Lord Ashley (now Earl of Shaftsbury), George Sturge, and Thomas Sturge. [020-007]

The committee's formation and the subsequent investigation by [[john-scobell|John Scobell]] were decisive in clearing [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s name and enabling him to discharge the entire debt of the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn]] institution within a few months.
