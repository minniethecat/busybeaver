# Toronto

Major city in [[canada|Upper Canada]] (present-day [[canada|Ontario]]), home to influential supporters of [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]] including [[john-rolfe|Rev. John Rolfe]], who presided over an important public meeting examining [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s record.

## Location & Geography

Toronto is situated on the northern shore of Lake [[canada|Ontario]]. In the mid-nineteenth century, it was the capital of the Province of [[canada|Canada]] and a growing center of political, religious, and abolitionist activity.

## Role in the Narrative

Toronto enters [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s story through his connections with prominent Canadian figures who supported his work. [[john-rolfe|Rev. John Rolfe]] of Toronto served as a character witness for [[josiah-henson|Henson]], providing a letter of recommendation to the [[us-america|America]]n Minister to [[england|England]] that attested to [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s integrity [020-004].

Later, a large public assemblage met in Toronto with Rev. Rolfe presiding, to conduct a thorough examination of [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s record and the management of the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn Settlement]] [020-012]. This meeting served as an important public vindication of [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s character and leadership.

## Key Passages

> Among the numerous letters recommending me to the confidence of the British public, I received letters from the American Minister to England, from Rev. John Rolfe, of Toronto, Chief Justice Robinson, Sir Allen McNab, Col. John Prince, and many other distinguished persons of Canada. [020-004]

> A large assemblage met, and Rev. John Rolfe of Toronto, presided. A thorough examination into the record and management of the Dawn Settlement was made. [020-012]
