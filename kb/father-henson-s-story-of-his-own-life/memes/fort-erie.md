# Fort Erie

Town on the Canadian shore of [[lake-erie|Lake Erie]], across from [[buffalo|Buffalo]], [[new-york|New York]]. Site of a significant religious meeting attended by [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]], where he encountered a fugitive slave whose family he later helped rescue.

## Location & Geography

Fort Erie is located on the Niagara Peninsula in present-day [[canada|Ontario]], [[canada|Canada]], on the northeastern shore of [[lake-erie|Lake Erie]]. During the period of [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s narrative, it was part of [[canada|Upper Canada]] and lay on a key route for freedom-seekers crossing from the [[us-america|United States]].

## Role in the Narrative

Fort Erie was the site of a very large religious meeting attended by many Black settlers and freedom-seekers. It was at this gathering that [[josiah-henson|Henson]] encountered an individual whose story of family separation in slavery deeply moved him [016-002]. This encounter led [[josiah-henson|Henson]] to promise assistance, and he later returned to the Fort Erie area to begin another journey southward to rescue the man's family still held in [[slavery|bondage]] [017-001].

The meeting at Fort Erie exemplifies the role of religious gatherings as organizing spaces for the Black community in [[canada|Canada]], where information about those still enslaved could be shared and rescue plans formulated.

## Key Passages
