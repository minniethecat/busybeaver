# Ohio Conference of the Methodist Episcopal Church

The [[Ohio Conference of the Methodist Episcopal Church]] was a regional governing body of the [[Methodist Episcopal Church]] that held its annual sessions in [[Chillicothe]], [[ohio|Ohio]]. [[Josiah Henson]] attended one of these sessions during the period of his early ministry, a journey that proved significant for both his spiritual development and his path to freedom.

## Henson's Attendance

Following a remarkably successful preaching and fundraising tour, [[josiah-henson|Henson]] traveled to [[chillicothe|Chillicothe]] to attend the Conference session. He had been traveling with a companion, preaching in towns and cities, and had raised one hundred and sixty dollars — a substantial sum for a man recently enslaved:

> "I felt it my duty to go, as usual, towards Chillicothe, to attend the session of the Ohio Conference of the Methodist Episcopal Church." (007-005)

> "In three or four days I left the city with no less a sum than one hundred and sixty dollars in my pockets, and with a soul jubilant with thanksgiving, and high in hope, directed my steps towards Chillicothe, to attend the session of the Ohio Conference of the Methodist Episcopal Church." (007-005)

## Significance

The [[methodist-episcopal-church|Ohio Conference]] connection was important to [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s story in several ways. The [[methodist-circuit-preaching|Methodist circuit]] system gave [[josiah-henson|Henson]] — then still legally enslaved — a degree of mobility and respect unusual for his station. His attendance at the Conference placed him among the church leadership of the western jurisdiction, exposing him to networks of people engaged in aiding fugitive slaves. These contacts would later prove crucial in planning his family's escape to [[Canada]].
