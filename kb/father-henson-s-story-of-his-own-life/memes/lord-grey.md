## Proposition to Henson

After [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]] was introduced to the British public through his preaching and speaking engagements, he became well acquainted with many of the leading men of [[england|England]]. Among them, Lord Grey made a significant proposition: that [[josiah-henson|Henson]] go to India and superintend efforts by the British government to introduce the culture of cotton on the [[us-america|America]]n plan. (022-002)

Lord Grey promised [[josiah-henson|Henson]] an appointment to an office with a good salary. [[josiah-henson|Henson]] stated that, had it not been for his deep commitment to the Canadian enterprise at [[dawn-settlement|Dawn]], he would have accepted the proposal. (022-002)

## The India Proposal in Context

Lord Grey's offer must be understood against the backdrop of British imperial economics in the mid-nineteenth century. The British textile industry was heavily dependent on [[us-america|America]]n cotton, and the government was actively seeking alternative sources — particularly in India — to reduce this dependency. [[josiah-henson|Henson]], with his firsthand knowledge of [[us-america|America]]n cotton cultivation gained through a lifetime of [[plantation]] labor, represented a uniquely qualified candidate for such an enterprise.

> Lord Grey made a proposition to me, which, if circumstances had permitted, I should have been glad to attempt. It was to go to India and there superintend some great efforts made by the government to introduce the culture of cotton on the American plan. He promised to me an appointment to an office, and a good salary. Had it not been for my warm interest in my Canadian enterprise, I should have accepted his proposal.
>
> (022-002)

## Significance

Lord Grey's offer represents one of several instances during [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s English travels where his abilities and character were recognized by the highest levels of British society. The proposal to send a formerly enslaved man to India in an official government capacity underscores the remarkable distance [[josiah-henson|Henson]] had traveled from his origins in [[slavery]]. (022-002)
