Eusebius Smith was a British gentleman who served on [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]]'s oversight committee during the 1851 [[england|England]] fundraising campaign. Appointed to both the full committee of twelve and the sub-committee of three, he helped safeguard [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s financial integrity during a period when charges of imposture threatened to derail support for the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn Settlement]].

## The Oversight Committee

When [[josiah-henson|Henson]] arrived in [[england|England]] in 1851 to raise funds for the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn Settlement]] manual labor school, he was publicly attacked in a printed circular. In response, [[josiah-henson|Henson]] requested that a committee of British gentlemen oversee all contributions. Smith was among those appointed:

> This committee had been appointed, and consisted of Samuel Gurney, Samuel Gurney, Junior, Samuel Marley, Esq., George Hitchcock, Esq., Rev. James Sherman, Rev. Thomas Binney, Rev. John Branch, Eusebius Smith, Esq., John Scobell, Secretary of the British and Foreign Anti-slavery Society, Lord Ashley (now Earl of Shaftsbury), George Sturge, and Thomas Sturge. [020-007]

Smith was also one of the three members of the sub-committee entrusted with direct oversight of fund disbursement:

> The sub-committee of three were, John Scobell, Rev. John Branch, and Eusebius Smith, who appointed Samuel Gurney, Junior, treasurer. [020-007]
