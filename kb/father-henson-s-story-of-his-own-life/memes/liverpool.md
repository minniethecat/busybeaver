# Liverpool

Major port city in [[england|England]], significant as the embarkation point for [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]]'s return voyage across the Atlantic after his speaking and fundraising tour of [[england|Great Britain]].

## Location & Geography

Liverpool is a major port city on the northwest coast of [[england|England]], situated on the River Mersey. In the mid-nineteenth century, it was one of the world's busiest ports and the primary departure point for transatlantic steamship voyages to North [[us-america|America]].

## Role in the Narrative

Liverpool appears at the conclusion of [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s British journey. After traveling from [[london|London]], [[josiah-henson|Henson]] arrived in Liverpool and boarded the steamer *[[canada|Canada]]* bound for [[boston|Boston]] [023-001]. The brief interval in Liverpool carried deep personal anxiety: just before embarking, [[josiah-henson|Henson]] received a letter with troubling news about his wife, compounding the emotional weight of his return journey.

> I was on my way from London to Liverpool, and embarked from Liverpool on the fifth, in the steamer Canada, bound for Boston. I had not forgotten the communication contained in the letter which reached me at Liverpool. I knew not whether my dear wife, the mother of my thirteen children, was living or dead. [023-001]

## Key Passages
