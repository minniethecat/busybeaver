# Charlotte Henson

**Charlotte Henson** was the wife of [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]] for over 40 years. She appears throughout the narrative as a steadfast companion through [[slavery|enslavement]], escape, and life in freedom in [[canada|Canada]].

## Marriage and Family

Charlotte was married to [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]] during their [[slavery|enslavement]]. Together they had children, whom Charlotte cared for during the family's ordeal in slavery and through the harrowing escape journey to [[canada|Canada]] in 1830.

## Discovery of the Freedom-Purchase Fraud

Charlotte played a critical role in uncovering the scheme by which [[isaac-riley|Isaac Riley]] and [[amos-riley|Amos Riley]] attempted to defraud [[josiah-henson|Henson]] of his freedom. When [[josiah-henson|Henson]] returned to [[kentucky|Kentucky]] after traveling to raise money for his freedom, Charlotte informed him of what she had heard from letters at the "great house":

> It was not long before Charlotte began to question me, with much excitement, about how I raised the money. She evidently thought I had stolen it. Her opinion of my powers as a preacher was not exalted enough to permit her to believe I had gained it as I really did. (009-001)

Charlotte told [[josiah-henson|Henson]] that "[[amos-riley|Master Amos]] said I had paid three hundred and fifty dollars down, and when I had made up six hundred and fifty more I was to have my free papers" — revealing the fraudulent additional debt that the [[isaac-riley|Riley]]s had invented.

## The Escape Journey

Charlotte and the children accompanied Josiah on the escape journey from [[kentucky|Kentucky]] to [[canada|Canada]] in 1830. The journey involved traveling by night through woods, crossing [[ohio-river|the Ohio]] River in a small skiff, and enduring extreme hardships. As [[josiah-henson|Henson]] recorded:

> In consequence of the great exposures we had been through, my wife and all the children fell sick; and it was not without extreme peril that they escaped death. (014-002)

## Life in Canada

After reaching [[canada|Canada]] on October 28, 1830, Charlotte helped establish the family's new life in freedom. She lived with Josiah at the [[dawn-settlement|Dawn Settlement]] and in various Canadian locations.

## Death

Charlotte Henson died in approximately 1852, shortly after Josiah's return from his fundraising tour in [[england|England]]. Her death, after more than 40 years of marriage, was a profound loss recorded in [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s narrative.
