# Fugitive Slave Law of 1850

The Fugitive Slave Law of 1850 was a federal statute that required the return of enslaved people who had escaped to free states. It dramatically impacted [[josiah-henson|Josiah Henson]]'s life and work, as it made the northern [[us-america|United States]] unsafe for fugitive slaves and drove many, including [[josiah-henson|Henson]], to seek refuge in [[canada|Canada]].

## Background and Passage

The law was part of the Compromise of 1850, a package of legislation designed to resolve tensions between slave and free states. It strengthened the original Fugitive Slave Act of 1793 by requiring citizens to assist in the capture of escaped slaves and denying alleged fugitives the right to a jury trial.

## Impact on Henson

The passage of the Fugitive Slave Law profoundly affected [[josiah-henson|Henson]]'s sense of security. After escaping to [[canada|Canada]] in 1830, [[josiah-henson|Henson]] had occasionally traveled to the northern [[us-america|United States]] for speaking engagements and fundraising. The new law made such travel extremely dangerous:

> The passage of the Fugitive Slave Law in 1850 made it unsafe for any colored man, however free in reality, to remain in the Northern States. The law was executed with great rigor, and many who had lived in freedom for years were seized and carried back into slavery. [013-005]

## Flight to Safety in Canada

In response to the law, [[josiah-henson|Henson]] assisted many fugitive slaves who fled northward. The [[underground-railroad|Underground Railroad]] became more critical than ever:

> The effect of this law was to send a great many fugitives into Canada. Some estimates placed the number who passed through the hands of the Anti-Slavery Society in Boston alone at two thousand in a single year. [013-005]

## Significance

The Fugitive Slave Law had the unintended effect of energizing the [[abolition|abolitionist movement]] in the North. Many who had been indifferent to slavery were outraged by the requirement to participate in the capture of escaped slaves. The law contributed to the tensions that eventually led to the [[us-america|America]]n Civil War, and for [[josiah-henson|Henson]] personally, it reinforced [[canada|Canada]]'s importance as a haven of freedom.
