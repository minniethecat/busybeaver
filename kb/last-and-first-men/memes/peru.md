# Peru

A region of western South America whose ancient indigenous population — the "Peruvian stock" — re-emerged as a significant racial element after the decline of European influence following the First World State's fall.

## Location

### Type

Region

### Context

Located in western South America, along the Pacific coast and Andean highlands. After the fall of the First World State, the European element in this region dwindled, and the ancient "Indian" and Peruvian stock came into dominance [005-011].

## Physical Description

Peru encompasses the Andean mountain range and the Pacific coastal region. Its geography made it a distinct cultural zone within South America, contributing to the diverse racial conditions that made South America favourable for post-apocalyptic civilization.

## Historical Significance

### Era(s) of Importance

Peru was significant during the post-World State era when its indigenous population reasserted dominance after centuries of European colonial influence.

### Key Events

The fall of the First World State led to a demographic transformation in South America. The European element dwindled and ancient indigenous stocks — including the Peruvian — came back into dominance [005-011]. This racial reconfiguration contributed to the conditions in South America that were more favourable for civilizational recovery than elsewhere. Peruvian architectural modes were among the diverse styles that later human species would emulate — the Eighteenth Men's traveller would find modes "he would be tempted to call Gothic, Classical, Egyptian, Peruvian, Chinese, or American" [015-005].

## Species & Civilizations

The First Men inhabited Peru. The Peruvian indigenous population, along with other ancient "Indian" stocks of South America, contributed significantly to the racial makeup of the Patagonian civilization [005-011]. The Patagonian civilization later organized the region into its community of autonomous provinces [005-016].

## Changes Over Time

Peru experienced cycles of European colonization, indigenous re-emergence, and incorporation into the Patagonian civilization. Its architectural legacy persisted as an influence on later human species in the far future.
