# First World State

The **First World State** was humanity's inaugural experiment in global governance, established roughly three hundred and eighty terrestrial years after the [[European War]]. It arose from the exhaustion of the [[nation-state system]] and the [[First Men]]'s desperate longing for peace [004-001].

For approximately four thousand years it maintained a fragile stability, presided over by a [[President of the World]] and the [[World Financial Directorate]], before collapsing into the chaos of the [[First Dark Age]].

Though its material achievements were staggering — [[flying|ritual aviation]], three-mile-high residential pylons, continent-wide [[climate engineering]], and the near-elimination of [[disease]] — the World State was hollow at its core.

[[Intelligence]] declined across its lifespan, and philosophical inquiry was suppressed. The entire civilization became enslaved to the fanatical worship of [[Gordelpus]], the Prime Mover.

Its fall, precipitated by the exhaustion of [[Antarctic coal]], unleashed a century of [[American Madness|plague]], civil war, and cannibalism that extinguished civilization for a [[First Dark Age|hundred thousand years]].

## Overview

What distinguished the First World State from every civilization that preceded it was the sheer scale of its ambition — and the shallowness of its soul. For the only time in the history of the [[first-men|First Men]], a single political order governed all continents simultaneously.

The [[Sahara]] became a lake district crowded with holiday resorts; the [[arctic]] islands of [[Canada]] were warmed by redirected tropical currents; [[Antarctica]]'s coasts were permanently inhabited [004-025]. Yet this planetary artificialization was not matched by any deepening of the human spirit.

> Nothing half so artificial and intricate and prosperous had ever before existed. An earlier age, indeed, had held before itself some such ideal as this; but its nationalistic mania prevented it from attaining the necessary economic unity.
> — [004-026]

The World State solved the ancient problem of war between nations, but replaced it with a vast spiritual emptiness. The race substituted the pursuit of activity for the pursuit of meaning, losing the capacity even to recognize the difference.

Understanding the World State is essential to comprehending the broader [[Age of the First Men]]: it was simultaneously the zenith of [[first-men|First Men]] material power and the nadir of their intellectual vitality.

## Dominant Species

The First World State was exclusively a civilization of the **[[First Men]]** — the original human species, now deep in species-wide decline. By the time the World State was founded, the race had passed its intellectual zenith.

The [[first-men|First Men]] who built this civilization were biologically the same species that produced [[Newton]], [[Einstein]], and [[Socrates]], but their capacities had been systematically blunted by thousands of years of anti-intellectual religious conditioning [004-039].

A telling physiological detail: under the World State, human youth — which in the days of [[Einstein]] lasted roughly twenty-five years — was artificially doubled through medical intervention.

This was achieved not through genuine [[longevity|rejuvenation]] but by suppressing visible signs of aging, concealing the accelerating "general senescence of the species" that would doom the [[first-men|First Men]] after the collapse [005-012].

The World State's treatment of racial minorities revealed deep contradictions in [[first-men|First Men]] culture. The [[Negroes]] became a sacred caste — simultaneously outcast and holy, entrusted with ritual dancing but denied full citizenship.

The [[Jews]], having preserved a furtive respect for intelligence when it had become disreputable in others, controlled much of the financial apparatus while being regarded with "universal hate and even physical repulsion" as embodiments of satanic cunning [004-026].

## Political Structure

The World State's governance was a hybrid of [[guild socialism]] and plutocratic oligarchy. Each industry was, in theory, democratically governed by its members; in practice, control rested with dominant individuals. Coordination was achieved through a [[World Industrial Council]], where each industry held status determined partly by raw economic power and partly by public prestige [004-011].

An inner ring of "noble industries" emerged, ranked by prestige:

| Rank | Industry | Role in Governance |
|------|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | [[Finance]] | Real seat of power; [[world-financial-directorate|Financial Directorate]] of ~12 millionaires |
| 2 | [[flying|Flying]] | Supreme religious and economic activity |
| 3 | [[Engineering]] | Maintained planetary infrastructure |
| 4 | Surface Locomotion | Land transport and freight |
| 5 | [[Chemical Industry]] | Materials and manufacturing |
| 6 | Professional [[Athletics]] | Ritual and cultural significance |

Beneath this formal structure, true authority resided in the **[[world-financial-directorate|Financial Directorate]]** — a dozen millionaires headed by the [[President of the World|American President]] and the Chinese Vice-President [004-015].

The Directorate's power was sustained not by democratic mandate but by the population's eagerness for peace and terror of renewed national [[war|conflict]]. For four millennia, save for occasional local [[war|conflict]]s easily quelled by the [[World Police]], the system held.

## Culture

### Religion and the Worship of Activity

The dominant cultural force was the fanatical worship of movement. **[[Gordelpus]]**, the Prime Mover, demanded of his worshippers swift and intricate activity. The individual's prospect of [[immortality|eternal life]] depended on fulfilling this obligation — "those who justified themselves in action were preserved eternally, by special miracle, in the swift spirit of [[gordelpus|Gordelpus]]" [004-026].

Three occupations were honoured almost as much as the [[Sacred Order of Scientists]]: [[flying]], dancing, and athletics. [[flying|Flying]] held unique prestige — it was the swiftest locomotion and therefore the supreme act of worship.

The form of the [[aeroplane]], reminiscent of the [[Christian cross]], lent it additional mystical significance in a culture that had preserved the symbols of [[Christianity]] while losing its spirit.

> The general revulsion from intelligence was a corollary of the adoration of instinct, and this in turn was an aspect of the worship of activity. Since the unconscious source of human vigour was the divine energy, spontaneous impulse must so far as possible never be thwarted.
> — [004-039]

### The Repression of Intellect

By the fourth millennium of the World State, civilization had become so intricately stereotyped that novel situations never arose. Reasoning was permitted within official work, but never beyond it. Specialists could not experiment without an expensive license, granted only if the goal could be shown to increase world activity [004-039].

The only honoured intellectual pursuit besides [[finance]] was mathematical calculation — the vast enterprise of describing all ritual movements and natural phenomena in formulae, which were filed in sacred archives and never consulted again.

### Material Achievement

The physical accomplishments were nonetheless remarkable. Residential pylons rose to heights of three miles, scattered over every continent like "an open forest of lopped tree-trunks, gigantic in stature" [004-026]. The sky was perpetually swarming with aircraft up to five miles high.

Perhaps the greatest achievement was in [[medicine|preventive medicine]]. [[Cancer]], [[tuberculosis]], rheumatic diseases, nervous disorders, and microbic epidemics were eliminated. Childbirth ceased to be an ordeal.

Only senility remained, and even this could be alleviated by physiological rejuvenation. "The removal of all these ancient sources of weakness and misery brought about now a pervading buoyancy and optimism impossible to earlier peoples" [004-026].

## Fate

The collapse was triggered by the sudden exhaustion of [[Antarctic coal]] — the civilization's primary energy source. Despite centuries of warning, a superstition had arisen that the southern coalfields were inexhaustible.

When the truth could no longer be suppressed, the [[Sacred Order of Scientists]] declared the crisis a divine test and demanded that ritual flying — which consumed more power than all industry combined — be increased rather than reduced [005-002].

What followed was catastrophe on every level. Psychologically, the race suffered an unprecedented crisis: after centuries of orthodoxy and the systematic repression of curiosity, men were possessed by a doubt they regarded as diabolical. "No one said a word of it; but in each man's own mind the fiend raised a whispering head, and each was haunted by the troubled eyes of his fellows" [005-003].

The political order disintegrated. [[China]] and [[India]] declared themselves free states but could not achieve internal unity. The [[America|American]] government, historic stronghold of science and religion, resorted to poison gas and microbes.

The ancient "[[American Madness]]," once used as a biological weapon against China, now devastated [[america|America]] itself. Whole populations vanished in cannibalism [005-005].

> Far from recovering, man's estate rapidly deteriorated till it had sunk into abject savagery. Four thousand years of routine had deprived human nature of all its suppleness. To expect these beings to refashion their whole behaviour, were scarcely less unreasonable than to expect ants, when their nest was flooded, to assume the habits of water beetles.
> — [005-002]

The collapse inaugurated the **[[First Dark Age]]** — a hundred thousand years of barbarism during which the [[first-men|First Men]] forgot almost everything they had known. Only the ruined pylons remained, standing over the hovels of latter-day savages like monuments of senile domination [005-001].

From this darkness, a new civilization would eventually emerge: the **[[Patagonian Civilization]]**, which would rediscover the records of the World State and grapple with the tragic lesson of its predecessor's glory and madness.
