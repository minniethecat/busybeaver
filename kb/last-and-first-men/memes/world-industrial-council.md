# World Industrial Council

The **World Industrial Council** was a governing institution of the [[first-world-state|First World State]] that coordinated global industrial policy. It served as the primary interface between the state and the industrial sectors, bringing together the leaders of each major industry under governmental supervision.

## Structure

The Council was organized along sectoral lines, with each major industry represented by its leadership:

> "Industrial Council, whereon the leaders of each industry discussed the common policy under the eye of the Government." [004-003]

This structure reflected the World State's approach to economic governance: private industrial management operating within a framework of state oversight. The Council's deliberations shaped global production, resource allocation, and labor conditions.

## Role in the Energy Crisis

During the terminal phase of the First World State, the Council became a focal point of the escalating conflict between industrial needs and religious obligations. The worship of [[gordelpus|Gordelpus]] demanded ever-increasing ritual flight, consuming far more of the community's resources than all productive industry combined.

When [[agriculture|agricultural]] workers in China struck to obtain reasonable electrical power for tillage, and industrial workers demanded more flour, the Council's inability to reconcile competing demands contributed to the broader collapse of authority. The [[sacred-order-of-scientists|Sacred Order of Scientists]], whose promised energy secret might have resolved the crisis, proved unable to deliver.

## Legacy

The World Industrial Council represented one of the earliest attempts at global economic coordination. Its failure — caught between the irrational demands of state religion and the physical realities of resource depletion — illustrated the vulnerability of rational institutions to systemic irrationality. Later human species, including the [[second-men|Second Men]], would develop more sophisticated forms of economic organization.
