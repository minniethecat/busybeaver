# Venerian Civilization

The **Venerian Civilization** was humanity's second planetary home, established after the [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]] migrated from Earth to [[venus|Venus]] to escape the deteriorating conditions of their native world. The sojourn on Venus lasted somewhat longer than man's entire career on Earth [013-001].

## Overview

Transplanted into a foreign environment, humanity initially withered almost to the root before slowly readjusting to Venusian conditions [013-002]. The [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]] had genetically engineered themselves for adaptability, and on Venus their biological type remained more constant than on Earth, though culturally the period was scarcely less variegated [013-001].

## Civilization Phases

The Venerian age passed through many successive cultural flowerings and collapses. Unlike the relatively straightforward narrative of Earth, Venus saw humanity burgeon "season by season, with leaf and flower of many successive forms" [013-002]. The [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]] were joined by later human species who developed on Venus, each building distinct civilizations atop the ruins of their predecessors.

## The Solar Catastrophe

The Venerian phase ended when astronomical observations revealed that the solar system faced an impending catastrophe. The discovery that the sun would soon enter a phase making both Earth and Venus uninhabitable triggered the planning for humanity's next migration — to [[neptune|Neptune]] [014-001].

## Legacy

The Venerian Civilization represented humanity's first successful adaptation to an alien world. The lessons of biological and cultural adjustment learned on Venus directly enabled the far more ambitious Neptunian colonization. The Venerian period also saw the emergence of the [[eighth-men|Eighth Men]], who would lead the migration to Neptune.
