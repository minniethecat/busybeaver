# European Confederacy

The **European Confederacy** was a political union of European nations formed under [[china|Chinese]] leadership during the period between the era of nation-states and the establishment of the [[first-world-state|First World State]]. It represented an intermediate step in the long movement from national sovereignty toward global governance.

## Formation

The Confederacy emerged in the aftermath of the devastating [[european-war|European War]] and the [[anglo-french-war|Anglo-French War]], which had exhausted the traditional European powers. Chinese influence, growing steadily through the twentieth and twenty-first centuries, provided the organizational framework for European unification:

> "European Confederacy, under Chinese leadership." [003-001]

This arrangement reflected China's unique position as the world's most stable and administratively sophisticated civilization, whose [[nationalist-party-of-china|Nationalist Party]] had achieved a form of governance approaching the Platonic ideal of philosopher-kings.

## Function

The Confederacy served primarily as an administrative and economic coordinating body, standardizing trade, law, and infrastructure across the formerly warring European states. It preserved a degree of cultural autonomy for member nations while subordinating their foreign policy and military forces to a central authority under Chinese supervision.

## Transition to World State

The European Confederacy was one of several regional blocs (alongside the American sphere and the Asian sphere) that eventually merged into the First World State. This merger, however, was not an equal partnership — American cultural and economic dominance, combined with the [[american-madness|American Madness]] cult of vitality, ultimately subordinated both European and Chinese traditions within the new global order.
