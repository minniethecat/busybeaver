# Interglacial Period

The **Interglacial Period** was a geologically brief window — merely fifteen thousand years — between the [[first-dark-age|First Dark Age]] and the [[second-dark-age|Second Dark Age]]. During this interval, humanity struggled once more from savagery into civilization, achieving a renaissance that would prove tragically short-lived [006-001].

## Overview

After a hundred thousand years of eclipse following the fall of the [[first-world-state|First World State]], complex climatic changes rendered southern South America a fit nursery for civilization. A vast warping of the Earth's crust connected South America with Antarctica, creating new lands and altering ocean currents [005-010]. In this transformed geography, the [[patagonian-civilization|Patagonian Civilization]] arose.

## The Patagonian Achievement

The renaissance was driven by the resurgence of ancient "Indian" and Peruvian stock, whose pre-Columbian culture had lain dormant through the previous civilization like "a seed in winter" [005-011]. From the interaction of this ancient culture with residual cosmopolitan elements, a new civil life began. In a sense, the Incas triumphed at last over their Spanish conquerors.

## The Second Dark Age

The Interglacial Period ended catastrophically. The Patagonian Civilization's industrial exploitation triggered a chain reaction in the planet's unstable geology. Massive volcanic eruptions spewed ash and gases that rendered Earth's atmosphere toxic, extinguishing nearly all life. The [[second-dark-age|Second Dark Age]] that followed lasted ten million years — during which the planet was so seriously damaged that mind lay in deep slumber [006-001].

## Legacy

Though brief, the Interglacial Period demonstrated humanity's resilience — the capacity to rise from utter barbarism to complex civilization in mere millennia. Its catastrophic end served as a warning, echoed in later epochs, about the fragility of civilized life on a geologically active planet.
