# Martian Civilization

The indigenous civilization of [[mars-place|Mars]], built by the cloud-jelly [[martians|Martians]] over hundreds of thousands of years. It was a unified planetary society whose crowning achievement — vast scientific and technological mastery — was ultimately turned toward the invasion and attempted colonization of [[earth-place|Earth]].

## Origins & Development

The [[martians|Martians]] had three sexes and reproduced through a form of subdivision, with each new Martian cloud arising from the division of a parent organism [008-019]. At the zenith of Martian evolution, the whole planet was effectively a single biological and social organism, with individual cloudlets functioning as semi-autonomous units within the greater whole [008-020]. The geological played a less tyrannical part in Martian than in terrestrial history, allowing their civilization to develop without the catastrophic environmental disruptions that repeatedly shattered human societies [008-012].

## Political Structure

Martian society was organized as a unified global civilization without the national divisions that plagued terrestrial humanity. Individual [[martians|Martians]] could produce eyes of every variety, extending their perception across vast distances — a biological capacity that made collective decision-making fundamentally different from human political processes [008-023]. This unity, however, was not democracy in any human sense; the superb loyalty of the [[martians|Martians]] was squandered upon purposes that individual [[martians|Martians]] could neither question nor resist [008-028].

## Science & Technology

The vast corpus of Martian knowledge had taken many thousands of centuries to accumulate, representing one of the most comprehensive scientific achievements in solar system history [008-032]. Martian civilization provided an outlet for the intellectual and practical energies of beings whose physical form — free-floating cloud-jelly — was radically unlike the tool-manipulating human body [008-029]. At the time of their invasion of [[earth-place|Earth]], the [[martians|Martians]] were still advancing intellectually, their science continuing to deepen even as their planet's environment deteriorated [008-033].

## Military & Expansion

The deterioration of Martian environmental conditions — particularly the loss of water-vapor — drove the [[martians|Martians]] to seek a new home [008-014]. Their invasion of [[earth-place|Earth]] was not an act of conquest for its own sake but a desperate migration born of planetary necessity. The [[martians|Martians]] deployed beam radiation against terrestrial populations with devastating effect, their attack suggesting purposeful and hostile intent on a scale humanity had never encountered [009-004].

## Culture & Society

The Martian mind was of a very different temper from the human [008-025]. The most distinctive feature of the Martian, compared with man, was the radical difference in individual identity: Martian cloudlets merged, divided, and reformed in ways that made human notions of selfhood irrelevant [008-026]. These differences between the Martian and the human psyche entailed profound consequences for their art, their social bonds, and their understanding of existence [008-027]. The [[martians|Martians]] had strong impulses of curiosity and a drive toward knowledge that rivaled or exceeded the human scientific spirit [008-031].

## Decline & Legacy

The Martian civilization met its end on [[earth-place|Earth]], where their invasion was eventually repelled by human resistance. Their still-visible remains on [[mars-place|Mars]] would later be explored by human successors [012-030]. The tragedy of Martian civilization lay in the mismatch between their intellectual achievement and the cosmic circumstances that drove them to destruction. They had mastered knowledge to a degree that their terrestrial contemporaries could scarcely imagine yet could not save their own world or secure a new one.
