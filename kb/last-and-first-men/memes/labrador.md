# Labrador

**Labrador** was the site of one of two surviving human settlements during the Second Dark Age, alongside [[siberia|Siberia]]. Unlike the Siberian colony, the Labrador settlement failed to preserve human culture, sinking into animalistic existence over millions of years.

## Settlement

The Labrador settlers were refugees from the [[patagonian-civilization|Patagonian civilization]], driven across the Atlantic by catastrophic geological upheavals:

> "Those who stayed behind heard no more of the wanderers, and finally concluded that they had come to grief. But in fact they were driven west and southwest past Iceland, now a cluster of volcanoes, to Labrador." [006-018]

The voyage was devastating. The refugees lost nearly half their number to storms and oceanic convulsions before wrecking on the coast.

## Environment and Decline

Labrador's climate proved far more punishing than Siberia's:

> "These found themselves in a climate far more sultry than Siberia; but like Siberia, Labrador contained uplands of luxuriant vegetation." [006-019]

The sweltering conditions, combined with isolation and the absence of a cultural mission, eroded the settlers' humanity:

> "That of Labrador, oppressed by a more sweltering climate, and unsupported by the Siberian will to preserve human culture, sank into animality; but ultimately it peopled the whole West with swarming tribes." [006-032]

## Legacy

While Siberia preserved a small seed population that eventually gave rise to the [[second-men|Second Men]], Labrador's descendants regressed into subhuman tribes that spread across the western hemisphere. The contrast between the two settlements — one maintaining cultural purpose, the other abandoning it — became a defining parable of the [[second-dark-age|Second Dark Age]]. The Labrador tribes persisted for millions of years, eventually providing biological stock from which later human species drew genetic diversity.
