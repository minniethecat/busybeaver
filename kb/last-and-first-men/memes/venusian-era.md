# Venusian Era

The **Venusian Era** was the epoch of human civilization on [[venus|Venus]], spanning a period longer than humanity's entire prior career on Earth [013-001]. It began with the migration of the [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]] from a dying Earth and ended with the discovery of the solar catastrophe that forced the next migration to [[neptune|Neptune]].

## Overview

The transplantation of humanity to Venus was traumatic. The species initially "withered almost to the root" before slowly readjusting to the alien environment [013-002]. The biological type of humanity remained more constant on Venus than on Earth, but culturally the period was "scarcely less variegated" — successive civilizations rose and fell like seasonal growth [013-001].

## Cultural Phases

The Venusian Era passed through many distinct cultural flowerings. Unlike the relatively linear narrative of Earth's history, Venus saw humanity burgeon "season by season, with leaf and flower of many successive forms" [013-002]. Each phase built upon or reacted against its predecessor, creating a layered archaeological record of immense complexity.

## Species Succession

While the [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]] initiated the Venusian colonization, they were eventually succeeded by later human species who evolved on Venus itself. The [[eighth-men|Eighth Men]] emerged as particularly significant, developing the astronomical knowledge that would reveal the impending solar catastrophe.

## End

The era concluded when astronomical observations confirmed that the sun would enter a phase making the inner solar system uninhabitable. This triggered the planning and execution of humanity's final migration — to Neptune [014-001].
