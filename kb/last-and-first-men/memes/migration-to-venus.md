# Migration to Venus

The **Migration to Venus** was the forced exodus of humanity from [[earth-place|Earth]] to [[venus|Venus]] during the era of the [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]]. The migration was necessitated by the impending collision of the Moon with Earth — a catastrophe that would render the home planet uninhabitable.

## Impetus

Astronomical calculations had confirmed that the Moon's orbit was decaying, and that collision with Earth was inevitable:

> "On the other hand, either the migration to Venus must go forward, or humanity must be destroyed; for it seemed quite certain by now that the moon would fall, and at no very distant date." [012-041]

The moon's approach had already caused devastating tides and weather disruptions, damaging civilization's infrastructure and spoiling the habitability of Earth.

## The Venerian Conflict

Venus was not an empty world. It was inhabited by the [[venerians|Venerians]] — intelligent deep-sea organisms whose mental life was incomprehensible to humans. The Venerians actively resisted human colonization, hurling explosives at island settlements and undermining them from tunnels.

This created a profound moral dilemma for the Fifth Men:

> "What right had man to interfere in a world already possessed by beings who were obviously intelligent, even though their mental life was incomprehensible to man? Long ago man himself had suffered at the hands of Martian invaders, who doubtless regarded themselves as more noble than the human race." [012-041]

Ultimately, humanity judged the Venerians to be mentally inferior — a judgement that paralleled the reasoning of the [[martian-invasions|Martians]] who had invaded Earth ages earlier.

## The Exodus

When the time came, the scale of the operation was immense:

> "Some centuries passed before the migration was completed, before Venus had received, not only the whole remaining human population, but also representatives of many other species of organisms, and all the most precious treasure of man's culture." [012-048]

The migration was not merely an evacuation — it was an attempt to transplant the entire terrestrial biosphere and cultural heritage to a new world.
