# France

A dominant European nation-state during the First Men era that briefly held complete mastery of the European continent and was distinguished by its Latin temperament in contrast to Nordic Germany.

## Location

### Type

Nation-state

### Context

France occupied Western Europe. It was a leading power characterized by a real difference of temperament between "Latin-France" and Nordic Germany [001-007]. French territory extended into North Africa, and France sought to expand its African holdings.

## Physical Description

France was a European nation with Mediterranean and Atlantic coasts. Its southern region was characterized as a place of leisure — an idle and neurotic Englishwoman traveled to the south of France during the uneasy interwar period [001-015].

## Historical Significance

### Era(s) of Importance

France rose to continental dominance in the era between the European War and the rise of the American-Chinese global rivalry. It was a leading power in Europe before the continent's eclipse.

### Key Events

A short and grotesque struggle broke out between France and Italy — the first outbreak and less disastrous for Europe [001-011]. Italy, hungry for French territory in Africa and jealous of French leadership of the Latin races, attacked France. After a single sharp engagement, the relentless armies of France pressed into North Italy, and the new Italian government made a hasty peace, ceding frontier territory to France [001-012]. France gained complete mastery of the continent of Europe, but "having much to lose, she behaved arrogantly and nervously" [001-014]. The long rivalry between France and England soon threatened to erupt into war [001-015]. France, along with England, had "chiefly inspired the intellectual integrity which was the rarest and brightest thread of Western culture" [001-031].

## Species & Civilizations

The First Men inhabited France. French intellectual confidence was shaken through progressive economic eclipse at the hands of America and Germany [001-038]. The spirit of Europe, including France's contribution, lived on only in a piecemeal and dislocated manner in the minds of Americans, Chinese, Indians, and others after Europe's eclipse [002-032].

## Changes Over Time

France rose to European mastery, then declined as global power shifted to America and China. The intellectual tradition that France cultivated survived in dispersed form across the globe.
