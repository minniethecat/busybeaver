# President of the World

The President of the World was the executive leader of the [[first-world-state|First World State]], an [[america|America]]n who rose to global prominence and shaped the union of science and religion in early human civilization.

## Identity

### Species

The President belonged to the [[first-men|First Men]], the original human species of [[earth-place|Earth]].[PN=004-004]

### Era

He served during the era of the [[first-world-state|First World State]], approximately two centuries after its formation.[PN=004-015]

## Biography

### Rise to Power

The President rose from humble origins, described as having gone "from garage boy to World President."[PN=003-048]

### Political Scandal

A scandal threatened his presidency when the Vice-President published his connection with a woman known as the Daughter of Man. Rather than deny it, the President embraced the revelation, claiming that this experience had enlarged his mind and made him fit to lead the world rather than remain "simply an [[america|America]]n."[PN=004-004]

### Policy Toward China

The President's enlarged perspective led to unexpectedly tactful policy toward the East. He moderated [[america|America]]n demands for the immediate [[america|America]]nization of China and persuaded the Chinese to welcome policies they initially regarded with suspicion.[PN=004-006]

## Key Actions

### Union of Science and Religion

[[About]] two centuries after the formation of the first World State, the President declared that the time was ripe for a formal union of science and religion. He called a conference of the leaders of both disciplines on an island in the Pacific that had become the Mecca of cosmopolitan sentiment.[PN=004-015]

### Sacred Order of Scientists

The President established the Sacred Order of Scientists, a select body nominated by the International College of [[science-concept|Science]] and sanctified by the Church, to be custodians of "the supreme secret" and devoted wholly to the service of science.[PN=004-017]

## In the Narrative

[[olaf-stapledon|Stapledon]] uses the President as an exemplar of [[first-men|First Men]] political leadership — flawed yet capable of growth, whose personal transformation enabled a pivotal synthesis of science and religion in the World State.
