# America

The dominant power of the late [[first-men|First Men]] era, whose economic and cultural reach reshaped the entire planet before succumbing to the forces of decline that consumed its European rivals.

> After the eclipse of Europe, the allegiance of men gradually crystallized into two great national or racial sentiments, the American and the Chinese. Little by little all other patriotisms became mere local variants of one or other of these two major loyalties.

## Location

America occupies the northern continent of the Western Hemisphere on [[Earth]], bounded by the Atlantic and Pacific oceans. In the era of [[first-men|First Men]] global civilization, American capital and culture reached every inhabited region of the planet.

## Geopolitical Role

### Rise to Dominance

Following the collapse of European powers in the devastating wars of the twentieth and twenty-first centuries, America emerged as the world's preeminent superpower.

> This occurred within a century after the eclipse of Europe. The second century completed the process of crystallization into two systems, political and mental. On the one hand was the wealthy and close-knit American Continental Federation, with its personal ideal of racial aggrandizement.

The American Federation consolidated the Western Hemisphere under its influence, establishing economic hegemony over vast territories.

### America–China Rivalry

The global order of the late First Men era was defined by the bipolar rivalry between America and [[china|China]].

> China, owing to her relative weakness and the irritation caused by the tentacles of American industry within her, was at this time more nationalistic than her rival. America, indeed, professed to have outgrown nationalism, and to stand for political cosmopolitanism.

While America claimed to represent a universal, post-national ideal, its economic power was experienced by others as a new form of domination — a tension that would contribute to the instability of the late First Men world order.

## Cultural Influence

American culture became a global export, reshaping values and aspirations worldwide. The distinctive American blend of materialism, individualism, and spiritual yearning was absorbed and reinterpreted by cultures across the planet.

> His thought also was largely a Mongolian variant of American thought. For instance, since his was a non-metaphysical mind, but since also some kind of metaphysics is unavoidable, he accepted the naively materialistic metaphysics which had been popular in America a generation earlier.

American intellectual trends — from Behaviorism to various forms of spiritualized materialism — spread through global networks, creating a shared cultural vocabulary even among geopolitical rivals.

> There is no need to recount in detail the transition from rival national sovereignties to unitary control by the World Financial Directorate. Suffice it that by concerted action in America and China the military governments found themselves hamstrung by the passive resistance of cosmopolitan big business.

## Legacy

America's global dominance was relatively brief in evolutionary terms — a few centuries — but its influence on the political and cultural shape of late First Men civilization was profound. When the First Dark Age descended, the ruins of American pylons stood alongside those of Europe and China, monuments to a vanished world order.
