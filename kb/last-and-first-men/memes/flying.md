# Flying

Flight is a recurring motif throughout humanity's two-billion-year [[biological evolution]] in *Last and [[first-men|First Men]]*, manifesting across multiple [[species]] in mechanical, biological, and spiritual forms.

From the [[First Men]] who pioneered powered aviation on [[Earth]], to the bat-winged [[Seventh Men]] who lived entire civilizations in the air above [[Venus]], to the [[Last Men]] who wore personal flying-suits on [[Neptune]] — flight traces an arc from crude technology to transcendent liberation [013-015].

The concept of flying in [[Olaf Stapledon]]'s cosmos is never merely about transportation. For the [[Sixth Men]], flight was a sacred obsession embodied in bird-deities; for the [[Seventh Men]] it was the very medium of spiritual ecstasy.

For the [[Last Men]] it represented the body's integration with cosmic freedom. This page surveys the biological origins, technological expressions, and cultural significance of flight across the [[species|eighteen human species]].

## Definition

"Flying" encompasses all forms of unaided and technologically-assisted [[aerial locomotion]] achieved by human species — from mechanical aircraft to biologically evolved wings, to radiation-powered personal flight suits (013-015, [015-006].

The term carries both literal and metaphorical-spiritual dimensions: for the [[Seventh Men]], "it was not flight itself, but the spiritual aspect of flight which obsessed the species" [013-020].

Flight serves as a metric of [[biological evolution|evolutionary advancement]] within the narrative. Species that achieved flight attained greater freedom and environmental integration than earthbound predecessors such as the [[First Men]] and [[Second Men]] (013-022, [015-006].

## Origin

### Sixth Men and the God-Bird

The systematic pursuit of biological flight originated with the [[Sixth Men]] on [[Venus]], who inherited the pedestrian limitation of all prior species from the [[First Men]] through the [[Fifth Men]].

Throughout their career, "the [[sixth-men|Sixth Men]] had often been fascinated by the idea of flight. The bird was again and again their most sacred symbol" [013-014].

Their [[religion|monotheism]] evolved through successive conceptions of a divine bird — "now as the divine sea eagle, winged with power, now as the giant swift, winged with mercy" — culminating in a "bird-god that became man to endow the human race with flight, physical and spiritual" [013-014].

### Engineering the Seventh Men

The [[Sixth Men]] developed mechanical flying machines comparable to those of the [[First Men]], but regarded them as "only a makeshift" [013-015]. With the advance of biological science, they reshaped the human organism itself.

After many failed attempts across successive [[civilization|civilizations]], "the most enduring and brilliant of all the civilizations of the [[sixth-men|Sixth Men]] actually attained the goal" [013-015], creating the [[Seventh Men]] — the only humans to achieve natural flight through deliberate biological design.

### The Last Men's Synthesis

On [[Neptune]], the [[Last Men]] achieved a synthesis of biology and technology. Their flying-suits, fitted with radiation-generators, enabled "a kind of aerial swimming" [015-006].

Where the [[Seventh Men]] relied on biological gift, the [[Last Men]]'s suits fused evolutionary refinement with technological mastery — a union the [[Fifth Men]]'s wingless [[aerial locomotion|aerial boats]] had only dimly prefigured [011-039].

## Biological Flight

### Anatomy of the Seventh Men

The [[Seventh Men]] were engineered entirely for aerial existence by their [[Sixth Men]] creators. "A leathery membrane spread from the foot to the tip of the immensely elongated and strengthened 'middle finger'" — the three outer fingers served as ribs to the membrane, while index and thumb remained free for manipulation [013-016].

Their bodies assumed "the streamlines of a bird" and were covered in "a deep quilt of feathery wool" whose colouring varied between individuals. On the ground, membranes folded "like exaggerated sleeves"; in flight, legs extended as a flattened tail [013-016].

Internally, they possessed a keeled breastbone anchoring flight muscles, hollow bones whose internal surfaces served as supplementary lungs, and a feverish metabolic rate [013-016].

Their brains included "a system of reflexes for aerial balance, and a true, though artificial, instinctive aptitude for flight" [013-017]. This specialization cost them reduced brain volume and a lifespan of merely fifty years [013-017].

### The Aerial Phase

The most distinctive feature of the [[Seventh Men]] was the psychological transformation accompanying flight. "In the air and on the ground the [[seventh-men|Seventh Men]] were different beings. Whenever they exercised themselves in flight they suffered a remarkable change of spirit" [013-021].

On the ground they were "sober folk, mildly bored," but airborne their constitution altered:

> In the air the flying man's heart beat more powerfully. His temperature rose. His sensation became more vivid and more discriminate, his intelligence more agile and penetrating. He experienced a more intense pleasure or pain in all that happened to him... the most remarkable feature of the aerial phase was that this enhanced power of appreciation was dispassionate. [013-022]

This dual nature meant the [[Seventh Men]] "grudged every moment that was passed on the ground" [013-024]. Extended ground confinement induced "acute melancholia" and death — a deliberate design, as their [[Sixth Men]] creators "had so contrived them that with the onset of any very great pain or misery their hearts should stop" [013-021].

### The Last Men's Wings

The [[Last Men]] on [[Neptune]] possessed natural wings as part of their bodily form [015-001]. Unlike the [[Seventh Men]], their flight was augmented by radiation-generating suits compensating for [[Neptune]]'s powerful gravity, making flight "a kind of aerial swimming" [015-006].

This symbiosis of biology and technology echoed the [[First World State]]'s earlier ambition to merge organic and mechanical aerial mastery.

## Technological Flight

### First Men Aviation

The [[First Men]] pioneered mechanical flight on [[Earth]], developing aircraft, aerial [[war|warfare]], and the demand for "complete aerial disarmament" — a political crisis that triggered the [[anglo-french-war|Anglo-French War]] [001-015].

The [[First World State]] later developed elaborate aerial transportation networks and "vast mechanical aerial choreography" that the [[Seventh Men]] would echo with living bodies [013-026]. The [[Patagonian Civilization]] also contributed to the [[First Men]]'s aerial heritage before their decline.

### Fifth Men and Beyond

By the era of the [[Fifth Men]], aerial technology on [[Venus]] had advanced to wingless vessels prefiguring the [[Last Men]]'s personal flight: "not an aeroplane, but either a wingless aerial boat, or a mere suit of overalls" [011-039].

The [[Eighth Men]], inheriting [[Venus]] after the [[Seventh Men]]'s extinction, developed aircraft that "treated with contempt" the perennial hurricanes of their world [013-033] — technological mastery contrasting sharply with the biological fragility of their winged predecessors.

### Last Men Flying-Suits

The technological pinnacle appears among the [[Last Men]]. In every home on [[Neptune]]:

> Near the outer door would be hanging a number of flying-suits, and in a garage attached to the house would be the private air-boats, gaily coloured torpedo-shaped objects of various sizes. [015-013]

Operating on radiation-generation rather than aerodynamics, these suits made flight the [[Last Men]]'s "ordinary means of locomotion" [015-006] despite [[Neptune]]'s crushing gravity.

Where the [[Seventh Men]] achieved aerial existence through biological sacrifice — reduced brains, a fifty-year lifespan — the [[Last Men]] flew while perfecting their full capacities, including [[telepathy]] and the [[group-mind]].

## Cultural Significance

### Flight as Civilization

For the [[Seventh Men]], flight was not merely locomotion but the organizing principle of [[civilization]] itself. "The social order of the [[seventh-men|Seventh Men]] was in essence neither utilitarian, nor humanistic, nor religious, but aesthetic. Every act and every institution were to be justified as contributing to the perfect form of the community" [013-025].

The following table illustrates how every cultural domain derived from aerial existence:

| Cultural Domain | Aerial Expression | Terrestrial Counterpart |
|-----------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| **Food** | Browsing sky-pastures of wind-borne plants "like swallows" [013-024] | Fishing, agriculture on islands |
| **Art** | "the supreme art of winged dance," aerial choreography, epic verse [013-024] | [[music-concept|Music]], verbal tradition |
| **Work** | Gathering manna in flight, hunting by stooping like hawks [013-026] | Industry in pylons, ground labour |
| **Love** | Aerial courtship — "spires, cascades, and truelove knots of flight" [013-026] | Domestic life on the ground |
| **Death** | Mass suicide diving into a volcano [013-032] | Heart failure from ground-bound grief |

This civilization endured for "close on a hundred million terrestrial years" with "little change" [013-026], sustained by the perfect fit between [[species]] and environment. "Generation succeeded generation, and each was too short-lived to outlast its young delight and discover boredom" [013-027].

### Flight and Spirituality

Flight acquired distinct spiritual meanings across [[species]]. The [[Sixth Men]] conceived of a "bird-god that became man to endow the human race with flight, physical and spiritual" [013-014].

For the [[Seventh Men]], flight was the supreme spiritual experience — "in the air they assumed a very different and more heroic nature" characterized by "detached aesthetic delight" even facing death [013-022].

The [[First World State]] practiced "devotional aerial acrobatics" as state [[religion]] [004-032], anticipating the aerial spirituality of [[Venus]].

The [[Last Men]] integrated flight into their astronomical consciousness. From their "cloud-piercing" pylons on [[Neptune]], they launched into the air as "a swarm of midges, which would turn out to be human fliers" [015-006]. Their flight expressed their spiritual capacity to transcend individual limitation through the [[group-mind]] and racial consciousness.

### The End of Flying Man

The extinction of the [[Seventh Men]] illustrates the fragility of extreme biological specialization. When a crucial marine salt became exhausted, "infants were more often deformed than true to type" [013-028]. Flightless cripples, spared for research, eventually usurped power from the winged majority.

Facing subjugation to the emergent [[Eighth Men]], the remaining [[seventh-men|Flying Men]] chose racial suicide:

> Their leaders, conferring together, saw clearly that the day of Flying Man was done, and that it would be more fitting for a high-souled race to die at once than to drag on in subjection... The leaders pressed on towards its ruddy smoke plume; and unflinchingly, couple by couple, the whole multitude darted into its fiery breath and vanished. So ended the career of Flying Man. [013-032]

This ending — a million winged beings diving into a volcano on [[Venus]] — is among the most striking images in the chronicle, encapsulating the [[Seventh Men]]'s conviction that "death on the wing was more excellent than prolonged life on the ground" [013-025].
