# American Madness

The **American Madness** was a catastrophic plague of pulmonary and nervous diseases that ravaged the American continent during the final collapse of the [[first-world-state|First World State]], and subsequently spread to Asia and Africa, extinguishing the last remnants of the First World Civilization.

## Origins

The plague originated from biological weapons deployed by the American government during the civil wars that followed the global energy crisis. The government, increasingly desperate to maintain authority, resorted to microbes after poison gas proved insufficient:

> "Finally it made the mistake of using not merely poison gas, but microbes; and such was the decayed state of medical science that no one could invent a means of restraining their ravages. The whole American continent succumbed to a plague of pulmonary and nervous diseases. The ancient 'American Madness,' which long ago had been used against China, now devastated America." [004-057]

The reference to the disease as "ancient" indicates that this pathogen — or a related strain — had been weaponized earlier in history, likely during the conflicts between America and China in the era of nation-states.

## Effects

The Madness produced both pulmonary and neurological symptoms, attacking the respiratory system and the nervous system simultaneously. The combination proved universally fatal in a society where medical knowledge had severely deteriorated.

The societal collapse was total:

> "The great stations of waterpower and wind-power were wrecked by lunatic mobs who sought vengeance upon anything associated with authority. Whole populations vanished in an orgy of cannibalism." [004-057]

## Global Spread

Initially confined to the Americas, the plague soon crossed oceans:

> "In Asia and Africa, some semblance of order was maintained for a while. Presently, however, the American Madness spread to these continents also, and very soon all living traces of their civilization vanished." [004-058]

This global dissemination marked the definitive end of the First World State and ushered in the [[first-dark-age|First Dark Age]].

## Legacy

The American Madness was the terminal event of First Men civilization. Combined with the energy collapse caused by the depletion of coal and oil, the overconsumption of resources for ritual flying in worship of [[gordelpus|Gordelpus]], and the failure of the [[sacred-order-of-scientists|Sacred Order of Scientists]] to deliver on their promised energy secret, the plague ensured that recovery was impossible. For the next hundred thousand years, humanity descended into barbarism.
