# Water

Water is a fundamental planetary resource in *Last and [[first-men|First Men]]* — a determinant of evolutionary possibility, an energy source for civilization, and the scarcity that drove the [[mars-place|Martian]] invasion of [[earth-place|Earth]].

## Definition

In the [[olaf-stapledon|Stapledon]]ian cosmos, water is both a biological necessity and a civilizational energy source. Its abundance on Earth and its scarcity on Mars shaped the evolutionary trajectories of two planets, ultimately bringing them into catastrophic conflict. Waterpower, alongside wind, provided the renewable energy that sustained human civilization through its darkest interregna.

## Origin

### Water as Planetary Determinant

On [[mars-place|Mars]], "earlier in the history of Mars, when there were more air, more water, and a higher temperature from internal heat, life had appeared in the coastal waters of the seas, and evolution had proceeded in much the same manner as on earth" [008-013]. But "a rapid loss of atmosphere, and especially of water-vapour, was changing Martian conditions beyond the limits of adaptability" [008-014]. By the time of the Martian civilization, "water also was comparatively rare" [008-012].

The Martian subvital units evolved specialized functions: "some for absorbing and storing water" [008-022], "some of the units had specialized for carrying minute globules of water" [008-023]. Water scarcity shaped every aspect of Martian biology.

### The Martian Invasion

The Martians came to Earth seeking precisely what their planet lacked. "The Martians sought water and vegetable matter; but they came also in a crusading spirit, to 'liberate' the terrestrial diamonds" [009-001]. On Earth, "as was expected, there was rich vegetation, and water was even too abundant" [009-004]. The Martians deployed "myriads of the subvital units, specialized for transport of water and food materials," each "loaded each with a few molecules of the treasure, and dispatched to the home planet" [009-006].

## Details

### Waterpower and Civilization

On Earth, water served as a primary renewable energy source across multiple civilizations. During the [[second-men|Second Men]]'s era, "wind- and waterpower was converted into electricity to supplement human and animal labour" [007-032]. In the [[patagonia|Patagonian]] civilization after the collapse, "wind- and waterpower began to be used for the generation of electricity" [005-037], and "in wind and water they had all that was needed" [005-038].

The [[first-world-state|First World State]] harnessed water extensively: "every considerable fall of water forced its way through turbines" [004-025]. When that civilization collapsed, "the great stations of waterpower and wind-power were wrecked by lunatic mobs who sought vengeance upon anything associated with authority" [004-057].

## Role in the Narrative

### Significance

Water embodies the book's theme of planetary determinism — the physical conditions of a world shape the biology, culture, and destiny of its inhabitants. That water scarcity on Mars led to an interplanetary invasion, and that water abundance on Earth sustained civilization through multiple collapses, illustrates how the most basic chemical compound drives the grandest historical events.
