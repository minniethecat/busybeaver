# Isaac Newton

Isaac Newton is referenced in the narrative as a historical milestone in human scientific development, marking the pre-atomic age of physics.

## Identity

### Species

Newton belonged to the [[first-men|First Men]].[PN=005-001]

### Era

He lived in seventeenth-century Europe, long before the formation of the [[first-world-state|First World State]].

## Role in the Narrative

### Scientific Benchmark

The narrative places events "a thousand years after the life of Newton," using him as a temporal and scientific reference point for the development of human knowledge.[PN=005-001]

### Pre-Atomic Science

Newton's physics represents the pre-atomic baseline. Even during the [[patagonia|Patagonia]]n civilization following the [[first-dark-age|First Dark Age]], scientific knowledge was described as "more advanced than that of pre-Newtonian Europe," indicating that Newton's era served as the starting point for measuring scientific progress.[PN=005-033]
