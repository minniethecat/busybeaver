# Third Men

The third of the eighteen human species, succeeding the [[second-men|Second Men]] after a period of evolutionary transformation. The Third Men were shorter-lived but more emotionally vivid than their predecessors, with a culture dominated by music, religion, and a distinctive form of egoism.

> Scarcely more than half the stature of their predecessors, these beings were proportionately slight and lithe. Their skin was of a sunny brown, covered with a luminous halo of red-gold hairs, which on the head became a russet mop. Their golden eyes, it is said, were bewilderingly beautiful.

## Biology

### Physical Form

The most distinctive feature of the Third Men was their great lean hands, each bearing six fingers — a refinement that gave them exceptional manual dexterity [010-004]. In bodily proportions they were similar to their predecessors but generally slighter and more gracile. Their sensory apparatus was keen, well-suited to a species that began as hunters and later evolved into pastoralists [010-008].

> But though in some respects the third human species fell short of the high standard of its predecessor, especially in certain of the finer mental capacities, it was by no means simply degenerate. The admirable sensory equipment of the second species was retained, and indeed refined, especially in respect of musical sensibility.

### Lifespan and Reproduction

Unlike the longer-lived [[second-men|Second Men]], the Third Men were short-lived.

> Unlike their predecessors, the Third Men were short-lived. They had a brief childhood and a brief maturity, followed (in the natural course) by a decade of senility, and death at about sixty. But such was their abhorrence of decrepitude, that they seldom allowed themselves to reach the natural term. Long before the end, they would put an end to themselves.

Their compressed lifespan contributed to a sense of urgency in all their endeavors, and their reproductive patterns shaped the rapid turnover of generations.

## Psychology

The psychology of the Third Men was distinct from all their predecessors.

> Mentally the Third Men were indeed very unlike their predecessors. Their intelligence was in some ways no less agile; but it was more cunning than intellectual, more practical than theoretical. They were interested more in the world of sense-experience than in the abstract.

Though they lacked the systematic intellectual rigor of the Second Men, they possessed an intensity of emotional and aesthetic experience that their predecessors had never known.

## Culture

### Music and Art

Music was the supreme art of the Third Men, woven into every aspect of their lives.

> Though the Third Men lacked interest in systematic thought, their minds were often concerned with matters outside the fields of private and social economy. They experienced not only aesthetic but mystical cravings.

### Domestication and Breeding

In their primitive phases the Third Men were keen hunters, but their strong parental impulses drew them toward domestication.

> As herdsmen and shepherds the Third Men very early practised selective breeding; and increasingly they became absorbed in the perfecting and enriching of all types of animals and plants.

This passion for biological refinement eventually led to their most audacious project: the deliberate breeding of a successor species.

## Transition to Great Brains

The Third Men's fascination with biological manipulation culminated in their greatest and most fateful creation: the [[great-brains|Great Brains]], or Fourth Men. This engineered successor species would surpass them intellectually while abandoning the emotional richness that defined Third Men existence — a transition that marked one of the most radical transformations in human evolutionary history.
