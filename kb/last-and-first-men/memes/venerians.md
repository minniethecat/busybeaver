# Venerians

The native intelligent species of [[venus-place|Venus]], whose civilization flourished in the depths of the Venerian ocean long before human arrival. The Venerians were not a single species but comprised several related species that had attained considerable civilization on the ocean floor [012-038].

## Biology

### Physical Form

The Venerians were marine organisms adapted to the extreme conditions of the [[venus-place|Venus]]ian ocean floor. Their bodies were shaped by the pressures of deep-sea existence, and their life processes depended on the delicate chemical balance of their aquatic environment [012-042]. Unlike the terrestrial human species that would later colonize [[venus-place|Venus]], the Venerians could not survive outside their oceanic habitat.

### Lifespan & Reproduction

At the bottom of the Venerian ocean, these creatures thronged in vast numbers, their population sustained by the rich marine ecosystem [012-039]. Several Venerian species had evolved, each representing a distinct branch of intelligent marine life [012-038].

## Psychology

### Mental Traits

The Venerians possessed a sophisticated intelligence that allowed them to build a complex civilization beneath the waves. They were capable of recognizing the threat posed by the encroaching Terrestrial species and mounted increasingly energetic resistance as human exploitation of their world intensified [012-041]. Their psychology was fundamentally shaped by their aquatic existence and the communal imperatives of ocean-floor life.

### Special Abilities

The Venerians' principal adaptation was their complete integration with the marine environment. They had mastered the art of civilization without fire, metal, or any of the technologies that surface-dwelling species take for granted. Their science and culture developed along entirely independent lines from those of terrestrial humanity.

## Culture & Society

### Social Structure

Venerian society was organized around the demands of oceanic existence. Their civilization was concentrated on the ocean floor, where they built structures suited to the pressures and chemistry of the deep [012-039]. When the human exploitation of [[venus-place|Venus]] began, the Venerians mounted collective resistance, their surface waters becoming strewn with thousands of their dead in the ensuing [[war|conflict]] [012-043].

### Achievements

The Venerians achieved a fully developed civilization entirely independent of terrestrial influence. Their greatest technological legacy, ironically, was posthumous: in their desperate final phase, the last Venerian men designed and fashioned the biological template for the [[ninth-men|Ninth Men]], the human species that would carry intelligence beyond the solar system [014-006].

## Historical Role

### Origin

The Venerians arose through indigenous evolutionary processes on [[venus-place|Venus]], developing intelligence in the planet's ocean basins. Their evolutionary history paralleled that of terrestrial humanity but followed a radically different biological trajectory shaped by the aquatic environment.

### Fate

The arrival of terrestrial humans on [[venus-place|Venus]] proved catastrophic for the Venerians. Human exploitation of the planet's resources, particularly the depletion of vital compounds from the ocean, triggered [[war|conflict]]. The Venerians resented the steady depletion of their life-giving environment [012-037]. Despite their resistance, the Venerians were systematically exterminated. Man's self-confidence was profoundly shaken by the extermination of the Venerians — it marked humanity's first knowing destruction of an entire intelligent species [013-004].
