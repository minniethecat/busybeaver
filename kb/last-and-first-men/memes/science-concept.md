# Science

Science in *Last and [[first-men|First Men]]* is the engine of human transformation — the systematic accumulation of knowledge that enables each successive species to surpass its predecessors. From the [[first-men|First Men]]'s discovery of Antarctica's energy reserves to the [[last-men|Last Men]]'s apprehension of cosmic law, science is the thread that connects two billion years of human striving.

## Definition

Science in the [[olaf-stapledon|Stapledon]]ian cosmos encompasses both the practical mastery of nature and the theoretical understanding of cosmic order. It is the method by which humanity transcends biological limitation — first through technology, then through the deliberate redesign of the human organism itself.

## Origin

### Developed By

The [[first-men|First Men]] laid the foundations of modern science, applying scientific principles to revolutionize their material circumstances [004-010]. The [[first-world-state|First World State]] brought science and religion into formal union, establishing institutions that preserved knowledge through the subsequent Dark Ages [004-017]. The [[great-brains|Great Brains]] ([[great-brains|Fourth Men]]) achieved the purest scientific civilization in human history, discovering principles that had remained hidden from all previous species [011-008]. The [[eighth-men|Eighth Men]] made the astronomical discovery that the sun was dying, redirecting human destiny toward the outer solar system [013-034].

### Era

Science developed continuously across all eras, though its institutional forms varied dramatically. The [[first-world-state|First World State]] maintained science as a state function, with the Government preserving scientific institutions even through periods of religious dominance [004-057]. During the Dark Ages, scientific knowledge was preserved in distorted mythological forms, only to be recovered and extended by subsequent civilizations.

## Details

### Mechanism / Principles

Science in the early eras was indistinguishable from technology — the practical mastery of energy, materials, and biological processes. The [[first-world-state|First World State]] drew the power needed to keep civilization running from the burning of Antarctica's coal deposits, an achievement of applied science on a planetary scale [004-025]. The [[great-brains|Great Brains]] transformed science into pure intellection, pursuing knowledge for its own sake in their mountain towers. The [[eighth-men|Eighth Men]] turned science toward species survival, designing the [[ninth-men|Ninth Men]] for life in the outer solar system.

### Role in the Narrative

Science is the instrument of humanity's cosmic education. Each scientific discovery — from the principles of energy extraction to the astronomical discovery of solar death — forces humanity to confront a larger reality and adapt or perish. The arc from the [[first-men|First Men]]'s industrial science to the [[last-men|Last Men]]'s cosmic science is the arc of human maturation: from tool-making animal to cosmic intelligence.
