# Americas

The Western Hemisphere landmass comprising North and South America, which became the heartland of the dominant American Continental Federation during the First Men era.

## Location

### Type

Continental landmass

### Context

The Americas span the Western Hemisphere, from the Arctic to the Antarctic. North America, through a process compared to the ancient American Civil War, incorporated within its federal unity the whole continent and the greater part of South America [003-001]. A major war between North and South America occurred, during which an attempt was made to recreate the League of Nations as a Cosmopolitan Sovereignty [003-003].

## Physical Description

The Americas encompass two large continents connected by a narrow isthmus. North America was the wealthier, dominant partner. The southern part of South America was climatically transformed to become a nursery for civilization [005-010]. Geological upheavals later connected South America to Antarctica and eventually to Africa [006-031].

## Historical Significance

### Era(s) of Importance

The Americas dominated global affairs after Europe's eclipse, becoming one of the two great poles of world power alongside the Asia-Africa bloc.

### Key Events

The American Continental Federation incorporated much of the Western Hemisphere. America was first in exploiting Antarctic fuel, seeing it as a means for "Americanizing the planet" [003-021]. The Americas became an economic powerhouse through control of resources. After the period of American dominance, the continent eventually declined as global civilization shifted. South America later collapsed into hollows blasted beneath it during the moonfall catastrophe, though a new land rose connecting Brazil to West Africa [006-031].

## Species & Civilizations

The First Men inhabited the Americas. The American Continental Federation was the dominant political entity. In South America, the Patagonian civilization emerged as a distinct cultural development. Later human species inhabited the continents in different eras.

## Changes Over Time

The Americas transformed from a collection of nations into a unified federation, then suffered catastrophic geological collapse during the moonfall era. The landmass was dramatically reshaped, with South America partially collapsing and new lands emerging.
