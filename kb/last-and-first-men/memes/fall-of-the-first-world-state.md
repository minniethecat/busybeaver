# Fall of the First World State

The **Fall of the First World State** was the catastrophic collapse of the first planet-wide human civilization, marking the end of the [[first-men|First Men]]'s civilized era and the beginning of the [[first-dark-age|First Dark Age]], a period of savagery lasting approximately one hundred thousand years.

## Causes

### Energy Depletion

The primary structural cause was the exhaustion of accessible energy resources:

> "The sane policy would have been to abolish the huge expense of power on ritual flying, which used more of the community's resources than the whole of productive industry." [004-048]

Coal and oil had long been depleted. The world ran on wind, water, and limited electrical power, yet the dominant religion of [[gordelpus|Gordelpus]] demanded ever-increasing ritual flight — an enormously energy-intensive practice.

### The Gordelpus Deception

Central to the collapse was the belief that the [[sacred-order-of-scientists|Sacred Order of Scientists]] possessed a divine secret — the "mystery of Gordelpus" — that would unlock subatomic energy. In truth, the secret had been lost centuries earlier. When the energy crisis became acute and scientists could not deliver, the deception unraveled:

> "Immediately the hideous truth, which few hitherto had dared to admit even to themselves, was blurted out upon the ether by a prominent Jew: the whole hoary legend of the divine secret was a lie, else why were the physicists temporizing?" [004-057]

### Civil War and Plague

The revelation triggered global uprisings. Governments resorted to increasingly brutal suppression, culminating in biological warfare. The [[american-madness|American Madness]] — a plague of pulmonary and nervous diseases — devastated America and spread to Asia and Africa, extinguishing civilization entirely.

## The Fall

The collapse was remarkably complete and lasting:

> "It might have been expected that, after the downfall of the First World State, recovery would have occurred within a few generations. Historians have, indeed, often puzzled over the cause of this surprisingly complete and lasting degradation." [005-002]

The generation that brought down civilization was followed by generations wholly incapable of rebuilding it. Scientific knowledge was rapidly lost, replaced by folklore and superstition. The "First Dark Age" had begun.

## Legacy

The fall left a permanent scar on human racial psychology. The symbol of the falling aeroplane — originally a rebel emblem — became the swastika, a sacred memento of lost glory used continuously by humanity across all subsequent species. The conviction of irreversible decay haunted human cultures for millennia.
