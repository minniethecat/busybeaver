# Martian Era

The Martian Era marks humanity's first traumatic encounter with non-terrestrial intelligence — the cloud-like beings of [[Mars]] whose invasion of [[Earth]] reshaped the trajectory of human evolution.

Occurring roughly ten million years after the collapse of the [[First Men]] and during the ascendancy of the [[Second Men]], this era was defined by a [[war|conflict]] between two utterly incompatible forms of life.

On one side stood the solid, bipedal, terrestrially-evolved [[Second Men]]. On the other, the floating, electromagnetically-organized [[Martians]] whose entire physiology was "so different from the terrestrial that the two kinds of life could never live in the same world" [008-016].

The Martian invasions were not a single event but a series of increasingly desperate assaults, driven by the slow desiccation of [[mars-place|Mars]]. Ultimately, the era concluded with the near-annihilation of the Martian species and the profound psychological scarring of humanity — a trauma that would influence human civilization for millennia afterward.

## Time Span

The [[Martian Era]] unfolded over several centuries, positioned approximately ten million years after the fall of the [[First Men]]'s civilizations. By this time, the [[Second Men]] had established a mature global civilization on [[Earth]], having emerged from the long darkness of the [[First Dark Age]] that followed the [[patagonia|Patagonia]]n catastrophe.

The [[Martians]] had been watching [[Earth]] for millennia. As early as the [[First Men]]'s era, "[[martians|Martians]], already watching the earth" observed terrestrial affairs [005-047].

Direct contact and invasion did not occur until the [[Second Men]]'s civilization had reached a sufficient level of development to represent both a threat and a potential resource to the dying [[Mars|Martian world]].

## Environmental Conditions

### Mars in Decline

The driving force behind the [[Martian Era]] was environmental catastrophe — but not on [[Earth]]. [[Mars]], the [[Martians]]' home world, was slowly losing its atmosphere and [[water]].

The planet's conditions were changing "beyond the limits within which the Martian organism could flourish" [008-014]. As their world became increasingly uninhabitable, the [[Martians]] turned their attention to the blue-green planet next door, whose abundant water and atmosphere represented salvation.

### Earth During the Invasions

[[Earth]] during this period was dominated by the [[Second Men]], a species physically superior to their [[First Men]] ancestors — taller, more graceful, with greater neural integration.

The [[Second Men]] had built a global civilization that, while less technologically flamboyant than the [[First World State]], was more stable and intellectually mature. Yet nothing in their experience had prepared them for an enemy that was not solid, not bipedal, and not comprehensible in [[human]] terms.

## Human-Martian Psychology

The psychological dimension of the [[Martian Era]] was perhaps its most significant legacy. The [[Second Men]], for all their enhanced neural organization, confronted a truly alien mind for the first time.

> The Martian mind was of a very different order from the mind of any terrestrial organism. [008-025]

The [[Martians]] were not merely intelligent — they were intelligent in a mode that human cognition could barely model. Their "free-floating ultramicroscopic units" functioned collectively as a single distributed consciousness [008-017], a [[group-mind]] far more integrated than even the most advanced [[Telepathy|telepathic]] communion.

This encounter with radical otherness deeply unsettled the [[Second Men]]'s philosophical certainties and forced a fundamental reconsideration of what mind and life could mean.

## The Martian Invasions

### First Contact

The [[Martians]] "were first seen by men" on an early summer morning, appearing as shimmering cloud-like forms descending through [[Earth]]'s atmosphere [008-001].

Initial attempts at communication failed catastrophically — the [[Martians]]' electromagnetic mode of perception and communication had no common ground with [[human]] speech or gesture. What humans perceived as hostile attack may have been, from the [[Martians|Martian]] perspective, something closer to terraforming or resource extraction.

### The Nature of the Enemy

The [[Martians]] were unlike anything in terrestrial evolutionary history. Their very biology was organized around electromagnetic fields rather than solid tissue:

> The Martian organism depended, so to speak, upon the maintenance of a certain electromagnetic character, a certain "field" of subtle forces which was itself the organism's essential nature. [008-016]

This meant that the [[Martians]] could not simply coexist with terrestrial life — their electromagnetic nature disrupted the conditions required for [[Earth]]'s biology.

"Terrestrial organisms, and Martian organisms of the terrestrial type" could not share the same environment [008-015]. The war was existential: one form of life had to yield the planet to the other.

## Aftermath and Legacy

The [[Martian Era]] concluded with the defeat of the invaders, but at immense cost. The final phase saw the [[Martians]]' near-total extinction. Their fate was rendered poignant by their status as desperate refugees from a dying [[Mars]].

The era's legacy included:

- **Technological acceleration**: The [[war|conflict]] drove rapid advances in electromagnetic science and atmospheric manipulation.

- **Psychological trauma**: The species-level trauma of facing an incomprehensible alien intelligence became a permanent element of [[human]] cultural memory.

- **Cosmic perspective**: For the first time, [[human|humanity]] understood itself as one form of life among potentially many — a humbling recognition that would echo through later eras.

- **Precedent for later contact**: The [[Martian Era]] established patterns of inter-species relations that would recur throughout [[Solar]] System history.

As the [[Last Men]] would later reflect, the [[Martians]] were not merely destroyers — they were also unwitting teachers, forcing [[human|humanity]] to transcend its terrestrial parochialism and begin the long journey toward cosmic maturity.

The subsequent rise of the [[Third Men]] marked the beginning of a new era, one shaped profoundly by the scars and lessons of this first interplanetary war.
