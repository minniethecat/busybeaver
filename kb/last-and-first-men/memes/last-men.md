# Last Men

The Last Men are the eighteenth and final human species, dwelling on [[neptune-place|Neptune]] two billion years in the future.

They represent the culmination of all human evolution. These beings of perfect telepathy, natural [[flying|flight]], and cosmic consciousness faced the end of humanity with serene acceptance.

> We, the Last Men, have all their zest in private and in racial life, whether it fares well or ill. [015-039]

## Physical Form

The Last Men's appearance would have shocked any earlier human. They possessed natural wings, a pair of occipital eyes at the back of the head, and an upward-looking astronomical eye on the crown — a feature "peculiar to the Last Men" [015-002].

> The pair of occipital eyes, too, would shock him; so would the upward-looking astronomical eye on the crown, which is peculiar to the Last Men. [015-002]

Their bodies were adapted to [[neptune-place|Neptune]]'s environment — a planet that could comfortably house "the million million of the Last Men" [015-005]. Beyond flight, they had perfected telepathy so that any individual could communicate directly with any person on the entire planet [015-012].

## Mind and Consciousness

The Last Men were "astronomically minded," their consciousness oriented toward the cosmos rather than mere planetary concerns. They had transcended books entirely. Pocket instruments generated a complex flow of telepathic language to transmit knowledge. Their psychology was defined by profound integration — unlike earlier species torn by internal division, the Last Men achieved wholeness of being, enabling them to contemplate extinction without panic or despair.

> It has become very difficult to reach you, and still more difficult to speak to you; for already the Last Men are not the men they were. [016-026]

## Culture and Values

The Last Men's civilization was the highest refinement of human culture across two billion years. Art, philosophy, and science fused into a single enterprise of cosmic understanding. They valued the contemplative life as the deepest form of engagement with reality. They rejected the crude equation of material development with civilization that characterized the [[first-men|First Men]].

> To us, the Last Men, there is an extreme pathos and even comicality, not only in this most thorough confusion of material development with civilization, but also in the actual paucity of the vaunted achievements. [004-021]

## The Final Generation

When stellar catastrophe made extinction certain, the Last Men responded with two great projects. First, they explored the entirety of human racial memory through perfected [[telepathy|telepathic]] powers. Second, they seeded life across the cosmos in a Supreme Moment that dispersed consciousness to other worlds.

> He is young, the last born of the Last Men; for he was the latest to be conceived before we learned man's doom, and put an end to all conceiving. [016-038]

The last born embodied the tragic dignity of their end — not despair but a serene acceptance of humanity's completed purpose.
