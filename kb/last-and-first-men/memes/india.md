India was one of the great civilizational centers of the [[first-men|First Men]], representing a distinct mentality — one inclined "to seek behind appearances for some ultimate reality, of which this life, they said, was but a passing aspect" [003-005]. Alongside Greek tragic art and Hebrew worship of divine law, the Indian tradition of resignation was among humanity's earliest intimations of "an alien and supernal beauty" [001-005].

## Location

India occupied the South Asian subcontinent, bounded to the north by the [[hindu-kush|Hindu Kush]] and to the south by the Indian Ocean. It was part of the broader Asian cultural sphere, paired with [[china|China]] as the two great Eastern civilizations.

## Cultural and Philosophical Tradition

### The Indian Mentality

Within the [[first-world-state|First World State]], the primary cultural distinction was "the difference between the Chinese and Indian mentalities." The Chinese were interested "in appearances, in the sensory, the urbane, the practical," while "the Indians inclined to seek behind appearances for some ultimate reality" [003-005]. This metaphysical orientation meant "the average Indian never took to heart the practical social problem in all its seriousness," having been taught "that this world was mere shadow" [003-005].

### Spiritual Heritage

The Indian tradition of resignation, alongside Greek tragedy and Hebrew law, represented one of humanity's earliest spiritual achievements: "In Greek tragic art and Hebrew worship of divine law, as also in the Indian resignation, man experienced, at first very obscurely, that vision of an alien and supernal beauty, which was to exalt and perplex him again and again throughout his whole career" [001-005].

## Historical Significance

### Tensions with America

India's relations with [[america|America]] were fraught. A scandal over child labor in Indian factories — with "boys and girls under twelve being badly sweated" [003-022] — escalated into a diplomatic crisis. When America protested "in terms which assumed that America was the guardian of the world's morals," India "replied to America as to a busybody" [003-022]. The resulting tensions contributed to the outbreak of the [[war|War]] between East and West.

### Alliance with China

Despite cultural differences, India and [[china|China]] were drawn together by "earnest hate of that strange blend of the commercial traveller, the missionary, and the barbarian conqueror, which was the American abroad" [003-005].

### Legacy in Patagonia

Descendants of ancient Indian stock played a crucial role in the rebirth of civilization. In [[patagonia|Patagonia]], the convergence of "ancient Indian and Peruvian peoples mingled with European cosmopolitan remnants" [005-011] produced the population of renewed vigour that ended the [[first-dark-age|First Dark Age]].
