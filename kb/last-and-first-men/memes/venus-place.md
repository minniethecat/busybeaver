# Venus

Venus became the second home of humanity after the decline of terrestrial civilization. Its dense atmosphere and unique environment shaped the evolution of multiple human species, from the [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]] to the [[eighth-men|Eighth Men]], and it was here that humanity first confronted the moral cost of interplanetary colonization.

## Geography & Environment

Venus possessed a dense, cloud-filled atmosphere quite unlike [[earth-place|Earth]]'s. The planet's surface was largely oceanic, with the deep Venerian seas covering most of the globe. This aquatic environment had given rise to its own indigenous intelligent life — the [[venerians|Venerians]] — who inhabited the ocean floor in vast numbers [012-039]. The Venerian nights were long and dark, and human colonists adapted to conditions radically different from those of their terrestrial homeland.

## Indigenous Inhabitants

Before human arrival, Venus was home to the [[venerians|Venerians]] — several species of intelligent marine organisms that had attained considerable civilization on the ocean floor [012-038]. The [[venerians|Venerians]] resented the steady depletion of their life-giving environment as human exploitation of the planet intensified [012-037]. The subsequent extermination of the [[venerians|Venerians]] represented humanity's second destruction of an intelligent species, following the Martian genocide [013-004].

## Human Colonization

The [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]] established the first major human presence on Venus, adapting their biology and culture to the Venerian environment. The planet's conditions favored the evolution of radically different human forms: the pygmy bat-like [[seventh-men|Seventh Men]] who mastered powered [[flying|flight]] in the dense atmosphere, and the giant long-headed [[eighth-men|Eighth Men]] who made the astronomical discoveries that would determine humanity's cosmic future [013-034]. The [[seventh-men|Seventh Men]]'s aerial culture, in particular, was uniquely suited to Venus — their joy in [[flying|flight]] was made possible by the planet's thick, buoyant atmosphere.

## Legacy

Venus was the crucible in which humanity transformed from a terrestrial to a spacefaring species. The [[eighth-men|Eighth Men]]'s discovery of the solar catastrophe — and their recognition that they themselves could never survive on the outer worlds — led to the design of the [[ninth-men|Ninth Men]] from the Venerian biological template [013-036]. Venus thus became the launch point for humanity's colonization of the outer solar system, though at the cost of two intelligent species: the [[martians|Martians]] and the [[venerians|Venerians]].
