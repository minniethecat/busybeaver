# Immortality

The concept of immortality evolves dramatically across the two billion years of human history, from a religious belief among the [[first-men|First Men]] to a partial biological reality and ultimately a cosmic reconciliation with mortality.

## First Men Era

Among the [[first-men|First Men]], belief in personal immortality was widespread but was "destroyed" as an "intrinsic attribute of the individual mind" when examined critically [004-031]. The [[first-dark-age|First Dark Age]] philosophers concluded that only by merging with the collective — "only by defining can he know" — could the individual approach immortality [004-046].

## Later Species

The [[third-men|Third Men]] experienced a "craving for personal immortality" driven by their brief, precarious lives [010-018]. By the era of the [[last-men|Last Men]], immortality had transformed from a spiritual aspiration into a technologically-mediated reality — the [[last-men|Last Men]] achieved practical immortality through mastery of biology and the ability to project consciousness across time.
