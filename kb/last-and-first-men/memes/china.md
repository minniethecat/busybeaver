# China

A powerful nation-state on Earth during the First Men era that emerged as one of two great global powers (alongside America) after the eclipse of European civilization.

## Location

### Type

Nation-state / Civilization centre

### Context

China occupied the Far East of Asia. It was bordered by Russia to the north and faced the Pacific to the east. American interests in Russian Asiatic oil competed with Chinese-controlled supplies [001-042].

## Physical Description

China extended across East Asia, characterized by rice lands that were patiently improved over centuries, supported by railways that were pushed forward into the interior [002-002]. The nation developed extensive infrastructure and industrial capacity.

## Historical Significance

### Era(s) of Importance

China rose to global prominence after the eclipse of Europe. The allegiance of humanity gradually crystallized into two great national sentiments: the American and the Chinese [003-001]. China was viewed as the "natural enemy" of America and remained within the League of Nations, hoping to use it against her rival [001-046].

### Key Events

China began to mobilize her forces in response to American militarization [001-051]. During the Russo-German War, China favoured Germany while America favoured Russia [003-002]. The League of Nations was dominated by China and no longer stood for cosmopolitanism [003-002]. A young Chinese physicist played a pivotal role in the international scientific crisis, expressing a plan to the assembled scientists at Plymouth with "quaintly stilted formality" characteristic of educated Chinese (002-010, [002-015]. Ultimately, the Chinese scientist's one hostile act doomed England [002-029].

## Species & Civilizations

China was inhabited by the First Men. Its society was already "half American, though largely Russian and wholly Eastern" [002-002]. During its attainment of unity and independence, China had learned much from militant Bolshevism, though it did not adopt communist theory or practice — instead entrusting itself to a vigorous, devoted leadership class [002-002].

## Changes Over Time

China evolved from a traditional Eastern society into a modernized, semi-Americanized power. After the cataclysms that reshaped the planet, China's role in global civilization gave way to new centres of power.
