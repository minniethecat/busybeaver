# Venus

The second planet of the Solar System, serving as humanity's principal home for a period longer than the entire career of the human race on [[earth|Earth]]. Venus was colonized by the [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]] and hosted successive human species before its eventual decline.

## Physical Description

Venus was a cloud-covered world with a global ocean dotted by archipelagos of islands. The atmosphere was dense and warm, presenting both opportunities and dangers for human habitation.

> "The first human settlers on Venus knew well that life would be difficult." [013-003]

The planet's ocean teemed with native life. A sentient aquatic species — the Venusian marine organisms — occupied the oceans and periodically clashed with the human colonists. As human activities expanded, "the Venusian marine organisms began to react against the terrestrial invasion" [012-041].

Venus was "far better supplied than Earth" in terms of mineral and energy resources, making it suitable for industrial civilization [012-042]. However, "it would be impossible for Venus ever to support the huge population that had been planned" [012-047], imposing natural limits on human expansion.

## Historical Significance

### Colonization by the Fifth Men

When [[mars|Mars]] and [[earth|Earth]] both became uninhabitable, the [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]] turned to Venus as their only viable option.

> "There was nothing for it, then, but to attack Venus." [012-032]

The colonization was an immense engineering project. "The polar surfaces of that planet had first to be rendered habitable" [012-032]. The Fifth Men established electrolysis stations to terraform the atmosphere and prepared the archipelagos for settlement.

### The Great Migration

The migration from Earth to Venus was one of the most momentous events in human history — "before Venus had received, not only the whole human population of the Earth, but the cultural achievements of the whole terrestrial era" [012-048].

> "Man's sojourn on Venus lasted somewhat longer than his whole career on the Earth." [013-001]

During the Venusian era, humanity evolved through multiple species. The planet witnessed the rise and fall of civilizations, the evolution of [[flying|flight]] as a dominant mode of existence, and the development of aerial human species adapted to the Venusian atmosphere.

### Decline and Abandonment

Over geological timescales, Venus gradually cooled and became less habitable. The planet "would probably be frozen and uninhabitable" within thousands of years [013-034]. This decline prompted the migration to [[neptune|Neptune]], with vegetation and ecosystems being "shipped to Neptune and established in the warm areas" [013-038].

## Species & Civilizations

The [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]] first colonized Venus. Subsequent species included the Sixth through Seventeenth human forms, many adapted to the aerial environment of Venus. The planet was also the stage for the [[flying|aerial civilizations]] that characterized mid-period human history.

## Changes Over Time

Venus was progressively terraformed from a hostile cloud-world to a habitable archipelago planet, then gradually deteriorated as the Solar System aged. "Venus turned into an engineer's paradise" [013-033] during the height of human mastery, but the planet's eventual fate was sealed by cosmic forces beyond human control.
