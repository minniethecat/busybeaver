# Fourth Men

The Fourth Men, also known as the Great Brains, were an artificial human species created by the [[third-men|Third Men]]. They were massive disembodied brains — sessile, specialized intellects that pursued knowledge with obsessive devotion. After three thousand years of triumph, their purely intellectual nature led to existential crisis [011-010].

## Biology

The Fourth Men were brains enlarged to immense proportions, housed in specialized structures. Some ten thousand individuals were created, each adapted to a specific environment — astronomers on mountain peaks, geologists in the planet's interior, all linked by telepathic communication [011-010]. They had no mobile bodies; their servants of the Third Men species performed all physical tasks.

## Psychology

The Great Brains valued only the intellective process. They cared nothing for companionship, art, or physical experience — regarding other beings merely as tools for research [011-012]. Their single-minded pursuit of knowledge, when finally thwarted by the limits of pure intellect, gave way to jealousy of the balanced natural species and to cruel experimentation on captive [[third-men|Third Men]] specimens [011-014].

## Society

Ten thousand Great Brains formed a worldwide research network, while the remaining Third Men population was degraded to a menial servant class. Resistance was suppressed by the Fourth Men's overwhelming prestige and persuasive power [011-010]. The whole civilization was organized as a single enterprise of intellection.

## Historical Role

### Origin

The Fourth Men were deliberately designed by the [[third-men|Third Men]] as "walking brains" — specialized intellects meant to surpass all previous human understanding [011-005].

### Fate

After three thousand years of research, the Great Brains reached an intellectual dead end. Recognizing their emotional and physical incompleteness, they redirected their efforts to designing a more balanced successor — the [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]] [011-025]. This act of self-surpassing creation was their last and greatest achievement.
