# Brazil

A region of eastern South America that was incorporated into the Patagonian civilization and later became connected to West Africa through cataclysmic geological transformation.

## Location

### Type

Region

### Context

Located in eastern South America. After the geological upheavals that reshaped the globe, the Patagonian civilization spread north and west into the Brazilian highlands [005-015]. Following the catastrophic moonfall, South America mostly collapsed into hollows, but a new land rose to join Brazil with West Africa [006-031].

## Physical Description

Brazil encompasses the eastern highlands of South America. The region's terrain includes elevated plateaus (the Brazilian highlands) that were accessible to the expanding Patagonian people from their coastal origin.

## Historical Significance

### Era(s) of Importance

Brazil was significant during the Patagonian civilization's expansion and during the geological transformation that reshaped the planet during the moonfall era.

### Key Events

The Patagonian people, advancing century by century from their origin east of Bahía Blanca, eventually reached and incorporated the Brazilian highlands [005-015]. The region became part of the vast Patagonian community of autonomous provinces. After the moonfall catastrophe, South America largely collapsed, but Brazil became linked to West Africa via new land [006-031].

## Species & Civilizations

The Patagonian civilization — a sober, intellectually oriented First Men culture — incorporated Brazil into its organized community of autonomous provinces (005-015, [005-016].

## Changes Over Time

Brazil transitioned from a region within the Patagonian civilization to a geographical link between South America and Africa after the moonfall. The region was fundamentally reshaped by the same forces that collapsed much of South America.
