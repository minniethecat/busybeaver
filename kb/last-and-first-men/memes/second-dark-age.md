# Second Dark Age

The Second Dark Age was a vast period of barbarism and biological degeneration lasting approximately ten million years, following the collapse of the [[first-world-state|First World State]] [005-001]. It was the longest sustained regression in human history, during which the achievements of earlier civilization were almost entirely lost and humanity was reduced to scattered, struggling tribes.

## Duration & Scope

The Second Dark Age spanned roughly ten million years — a duration so vast that it dwarfs all subsequent human history [006-032]. During this period, geological changes compounded human misery: an incursion of the sea cut off populations, reshaped continents, and isolated communities from one another, preventing any coordinated recovery [006-032]. The sheer scale of time involved meant that the memory of the [[first-world-state|First World State]] faded into myth, and the very idea of civilization became almost incomprehensible to the scattered remnants of humanity.

## Conditions & Causes

The collapse that initiated the Second Dark Age was triggered by the failure of the global energy system upon which the [[first-world-state|First World State]] depended. Without power, the intricate machinery of world-civilization seized up, and the densely populated urban centers became death traps. Those who survived fled into the countryside, where they found themselves unable to maintain even basic technological infrastructure. The severity of the struggle for survival in the Dark Ages systematically eliminated any excess energy that might have been directed toward cultural or intellectual pursuits [005-014].

## Biological & Cultural Impact

The prolonged barbarism of the Second Dark Age had profound biological effects on the human species. Without the selective pressures of civilization, physical and mental traits that had been artificially sustained under the World State began to deteriorate. Yet, paradoxically, the Dark Age also served as an evolutionary crucible: it was from these harsh conditions that the [[second-men|Second Men]] eventually emerged, adapted to a simpler but more resilient mode of existence.

The tradition of myth-making persisted even through the deepest periods of the Dark Age, providing psychological consolation to scattered communities and preserving, in distorted form, fragments of pre-collapse knowledge [005-016].

## Transition & Legacy

[[patagonia|Patagonia]] eventually brought the Second Dark Age to an end. The great theme of recovery centered on this remote southern region, where conditions favored the emergence of the [[second-men|Second Men]] and the [[patagonian-civilization|Patagonian Civilization]] [005-012]. The ten million years of darkness gave way to a new dawn, but the legacy of the Second Dark Age — in the genetic memory of the species and the mythic structures of subsequent cultures — endured for hundreds of millions of years.
