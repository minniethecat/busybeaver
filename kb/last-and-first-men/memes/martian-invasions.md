# Martian Invasions

The **Martian Invasions** were a series of extraterrestrial attacks by the [[martians|Martian]] species against [[second-men|humanity]] during the era of the Second Men. Driven by economic need and religious zeal, the Martians repeatedly descended upon Earth to extract water resources and to destroy human civilization.

## Background

The Martians were cloud-like organisms composed of subvital units capable of forming quasi-solid structures. By the time of the first invasion, Mars was fully populated and reproduction had been restricted, but the species faced existential threats from environmental changes and a cancer-like plague:

> "By the time of the first invasion, however, reproduction had been greatly restricted; for the planet was fully populated, and each individual cloudlet was potentially immortal." [008-030]

The Martians also harbored a fanatical religious veneration of rigid materials — particularly diamonds — which they believed should be displayed on sacred pinnacles and exposed to sunlight. The thought that Earth might contain diamonds not properly treated was one motive for invasion.

## The First Invasion

The first invasion ended in Martian defeat:

> "Thus ended the first invasion of the Earth from Mars." [008-009]

Human defensive radiation proved effective against the early Martian forms. However, the Martians learned from this defeat and adapted their biotechnology for subsequent assaults.

## Subsequent Invasions

The Martians returned in far greater force, now largely immune to human radiation weapons:

> "Some years after the first invasion, the Martians appeared again, and in far greater force. This time, moreover, they were almost immune from man's offensive radiation." [009-006]

Operating from alpine regions, they dried up great rivers at their sources, stripped vegetation from entire countries, and formed gigantic cloud-jellies that knocked down cities and masticated human beings. The motives were both economic and religious: water was desperately needed on Mars, and the destruction of human civilization was a holy crusade.

> "During each invasion the Martians contrived to dispatch a considerable bulk of water to Mars. And every time, not satisfied with this material gain, they stayed too long crusading, until man had found a weapon to circumvent their new defences; and then they were routed." [009-015]

## Outcome

Each invasion left humanity weaker, while Mars — despite losing large portions of its population — was ultimately invigorated by the stolen water. The invasions were later remembered as traumatic episodes in human racial memory, accessible through the [[great-brains|Great Brain]] experiments of the [[fourth-men|Fourth Men]]:

> "Another child was chosen... After an hour of the trance, he woke and became terribly agitated, but forced himself to describe an episode which the historians assigned to the age of the Martian invasions." [012-012]
