## Overview

Chemistry plays a crucial but often understated role in "Last and First Men." From the industrial chemistry that powered the First Men's global civilization to the revolutionary chemical processes developed by later species, chemistry is the invisible foundation upon which technological progress rests.

## Industrial Chemistry of the First Men

The First Men's civilization was built on chemical industry. As their global conflict escalated, "physics and chemistry advanced in Antarctica" [005-036], where the Second Men secretly developed their superior technology. Chemistry, combined with physics, was the scientific engine of military and economic power.

The energy infrastructure of the First World State depended on chemistry: "under the influence of physics and chemistry it began to change" [005-037], referring to the transformation of energy systems from coal to wind and water power — a chemical and physical revolution.

## Chemistry in Second Men Education

The Second Men placed chemistry at the core of their educational system. Every child learned "the outlines of physics, chemistry, biology, psychology, and geometry" [006-022]. This comprehensive scientific education produced a society where chemical knowledge was universally distributed, not confined to specialists.

## The Deep Chemistry of Later Eras

As humanity advanced, the boundaries between sciences blurred. "In physics, astronomy, chemistry, and even in the chemistry of life itself" [008-031], the Martians and later species discovered deeper unities. Chemistry was no longer a separate discipline but merged with physics and biology.

The most profound transformation came when biology began "to give rise to chemistry and physics" [010-029] — a reversal of the reductionist hierarchy. Understanding life's deepest processes unlocked "the deeper regions of chemistry and physics" [010-033], suggesting that the ultimate chemical truths were biological in origin.
