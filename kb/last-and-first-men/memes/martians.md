# Martians

**Cloud-borne electromagnetic beings from [[Mars]] — the first non-terrestrial intelligence encountered by [[humanity]]**, whose repeated invasions over fifty thousand years shaped the fate of the [[Second Men]], precipitated the [[First Dark Age|Third Dark Age]], and left a permanent mark on the [[biology|biological]] and [[psychology|psychological]] evolution of [[Earth]].

First sighted by the [[Second Men]] in the mountains that were once the Hindu Kush, the Martians were not solid creatures but animate green cloudlets — organisms composed of ultramicroscopic **subvital units** bound not by material contact but by radiant "wireless" communication. Their encounter with humanity was a tragic collision of two minds so alien to each other that mutual understanding proved impossible across millennia of [[war|conflict]]. The Martians were at once intellectually brilliant and spiritually crippled, masters of [[physics]] and [[astronomy]] yet wholly lacking in [[philosophy|philosophical]] insight, capable of perfect racial unity yet devoid of the [[individuality]] and [[love]] that enriched human [[civilization]].

## Biology

### Cloud-Form Constitution

The Martian organism was radically unlike any terrestrial [[life]]. Where [[Earth]] creatures relied on continuous material contact — nerves, tissues, a central [[brain]] — the Martian body was a [[Group Mind|group-mind]] of millions of free-floating ultramicroscopic subvital units, each smaller than a terrestrial [[bacteria|bacterium]] (PN [008-016]. These units communicated not by "telephone wires" of nerves but by transmitting and receiving ethereal vibrations on different wavelengths, like a vast crowd of mobile wireless stations. The whole organism was thus a **cloudlet** — a coherent swarm whose consistency was "something between a smoke-cloud and a very tenuous jelly" with a "definite though ever-changing contour and resistant surface" (PN [008-017].

> "The Martian organism depended, so to speak, not on 'telephone' wires, but on an immense crowd of mobile 'wireless stations,' transmitting and receiving different wavelengths according to their function." (PN [008-016]

The Martians possessed **three distinct physical formations**, each suited to different purposes (PN [008-018]:

| Formation | Description | Function |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Open Order** | Independent tenuous cloudlets in "[[telepathy|telepathic]]" communication | Everyday existence, exploration, [[Group Mind|group-mind]] unity |
| **Corporate Cloud** | More concentrated, less vulnerable mass | Coordinated action, transport |
| **Cloud-Jelly** | Extremely concentrated, formidable opaque mass | Combat, heavy manipulation, crushing force |

### Electromagnetic Physiology

Rather than muscles, Martians used **magnetic fields** for all physical action. Each subvital unit could generate a magnetic field to repel or attract its neighbours, giving the cloudlet cohesion, locomotion, and manipulative power. In its most concentrated cloud-jelly form, a Martian mass could "bring to bear immense forces" capable of flattening buildings while maintaining delicate control (PN [008-017]. Martians could also spontaneously produce **optical organs** — forming aqueous lenses as large as an ox's eye by holding water droplets in a scaffolding of units, allowing them to create eyes, [[telescope|telescopes]], and [[microscope|microscopes]] at will (PN [008-023].

The Martian organism uniquely combined **animal and vegetable functions**. By day it absorbed sunlight through [[photosynthesis]]; by night it rested on the ground like "a knee-high mist on terrestrial meadows," projecting root-like tubular groups into the soil to absorb chemicals (PN [008-024]. This dual-mode metabolism — combined with the fact that subvital units retained the power of growth and subdivision — made the Martian cloudlet **potentially [[Immortality|immortal]]**. There was no "natural death" through senility; disease alone, particularly a cancer-like plague where units lost radiant sensitivity, could end a Martian life (PN [008-029].

Martian [[reproduction]] occurred when myriads of specialized subvital units subdivided within the parent cloud and were ejected as a new individual. [[Sexuality]] was wholly absent; there were no partners in reproduction, and parental impulses were "scarcely worthy of the name" (PN [008-029]. Each cloudlet was an organization of specialized groups — some for attraction and repulsion, some for chemical operations, some for storing the sun's [[energy]], some for emitting [[radiation]], others for sensitivity to pressure, temperature, or light (PN [008-016].

## Psychology

### The Alien Mind

The Martian mind was simultaneously more **collective** and more **fragile** than the human. A Martian cloudlet could at any moment "wake up to be the intelligent mind of his race," perceiving through the sense-organs of all other individuals and experiencing the pooled thoughts and desires of the whole [[species]] (PN [008-026]. At the zenith of Martian [[evolution]], the entire planet sometimes constituted "a single biological and psychological individual" (PN [008-020]. Yet this unity was achieved at a severe cost:

> "The Martians, moreover, were hampered by being almost identical in character. They possessed perfect harmony; but only through being almost wholly in temperamental unison. They were all hobbled by their sameness to one another." (PN [008-027]

The Martians were immune to humanity's "inveterate selfishness and spiritual isolation," but they also lacked the concentrated attention, rigorous self-criticism, and rich diversity of personal character that even the [[First Men]] possessed in some degree (PN [008-027]. They were devoid of the passion of **love**; their [[loyalty]] was given only to the vaguely conceived "spirit of the race." Tragically, their revered super-individual was "not greater than themselves in mental calibre, but only in mere bulk" — the pooled [[consciousness]] of the race never rose to a higher order of mentality than that of any individual cloudlet (PN [008-028].

In their private phases, Martians experienced impulses of [[fear]] and [[pugnacity]], strong [[curiosity]], and an instinctive [[gregariousness]] that was developed "at the expense of instinctive self-assertion" (PN 008-030, PN [008-031]. Martian [[civilization]] provided outlets for their physical cravings through [[agriculture]] and intricate, emotionally charged aerial [[dance|dances]] — "flinging out wild rhythmical streamers, intertwining with one another in spirals, concentrating into opaque spheres, cubes, cones, and all sorts of fantastical volumes" (PN [008-029].

### The Diamond Delusion

The most bizarre feature of Martian [[psychology]] was their **religious obsession with rigidity and crystals**. In their history, the capacity for radiative unity had been mistaken for spiritual worth. Rigidity — the state in which Martians were physically most potent — became endowed with "peculiar sanctity" (PN [008-038]. This evolved into a fanatical veneration of **diamonds**, which they regarded as "supreme embodiments of the tense energy and eternal equilibrium of the cosmos." On [[Mars]], all known diamonds were displayed on the pinnacles of sacred buildings to receive sunlight. One motive of the [[invasion]] of [[Earth]] was the crusading desire to "liberate" terrestrial diamonds from improper treatment (PN [009-001].

This delusion was the symptom of a **deep spiritual sickness** — a "dual personality" in which every Martian privately harboured thoughts and impulses that contradicted the orthodox public mind, yet lived in terror of acknowledging them (PN [008-034]. "This was the great tragedy of the spirit on [[mars-place|Mars]]," records the chronicle. The Martians were "extremely well equipped for mental progress and for true spiritual adventure, but through a trick of fortune which had persuaded them to prize above all else unity and uniformity, they were driven to thwart their own struggling spirits at every turn" (PN [008-038].

### Scientific Prowess, Spiritual Poverty

In the natural [[science|sciences]] — [[physics]], [[astronomy]], [[chemistry]], even the chemistry of life — the Martians had nothing to learn from [[humanity]]. Their interplanetary voyage was an achievement of theoretical physics: by dissipating into their ultramicroscopic units, they learned to "get a purchase on the gravitational fields of the solar system" and sail on sunlight to [[Earth]] (PN [008-033]. Yet in "spiritual knowledge" they were "the most naive of self-deceivers," wholly lacking in [[philosophy|philosophical]] interest, never critical of their own ideals, and incapable of recognizing obligation toward beings outside the Martian radiant system (PN [008-034]. Their vast [[knowledge]] was recorded on immense scrolls of paper stored in stone libraries; they had become great masons, covering [[Mars]] with buildings "of feathery and toppling design" impossible on [[earth-place|Earth]] (PN [008-032].

## The Martian Invasion

### First Contact

The first Martian invasion occurred during the era of the [[Second Men]], shortly after a summer dawn. Observers in the mountains of [[Asia]] noticed "the sky had an unaccountably greenish tinge, and that the climbing sun, though free from cloud, was wan" (PN [008-001]. The green concentrated into a thousand tiny cloudlets — each smaller than the moon's disc, changing shape rapidly, "strongly reminiscent of primitive amoeboid organisms seen through a microscope" (PN [008-001].

The initial encounter was catastrophic in its mutual incomprehension. The Martians, encountering terrestrial "beam wireless" transmissions, interpreted them as the crude [[radiation]] of a low-order intelligence. Human beings, being solid creatures insensitive to radiation, were dismissed as unconscious — mere cattle of whatever hidden entity generated the planet's radio noise. The Martians' first act upon descending into a human village in the [[Hindu Kush]] was to envelop and crush its inhabitants, their **cloud-jelly** form elbowing through the settlement like "a lava-stream" (PN [008-001].

The Martians' motives were both **economic and religious**: they sought terrestrial [[water]] and vegetation to sustain their arid homeworld, but they came also "in a crusading spirit, to 'liberate' the terrestrial diamonds" (PN [009-001]. [[Earth]]'s dense atmosphere constricted them painfully, excessive [[oxygen]] induced violent fever, and heavy [[rain]] disrupted their physiology — yet they returned again and again across more than fifty thousand years, each time fortified against whatever weapon [[humanity]] had last deployed (PN [009-008].

During each invasion, the Martians operated with devastating efficiency. They spread over the countryside as an impalpable mist, absorbing [[water]] and stripping every leaf from jungle and agricultural land. For the destruction of [[civilization]], they became "armies of gigantic cloud-jellies" far bigger than the brute that had formed during the first invasion. Cities were knocked down and flattened, "human beings masticated into pulp" (PN [009-037]. After each Martian defeat, their disintegrated subvital units dispersed across [[Earth]] as a pulmonary plague, crippling human populations and reducing the [[Second Men]] to "timid invalidity" (PN [009-037].

Yet the [[Second Men]] endured. They developed countermeasures — high-tension [[electricity]] in the form of artificial lightning — and rebuilt their [[civilization]] again and again. They even attempted diplomatic overtures, but since the Martians regarded human beings as unconscious instruments of a hidden terrestrial intelligence, "the envoys were always either ignored or destroyed" (PN [009-037]. The cycle of invasion, devastation, plague, suicide of the stricken, and slow recovery continued for millennia, gradually wearing down the spiritual reserves of the most noble human species.

The [[war|conflict]] finally ended in mutual annihilation. A human [[bacteriologist]] bred a virus of "peculiar deadliness and transmissibility" that destroyed the Martian colony on [[Earth]] and spread to [[Mars]] itself, annihilating the entire Martian population. The [[Second Men]] also succumbed, falling into the [[First Dark Age|Third Dark Age]] — a racial trance that would last thirty million years (PN [009-037].

## Legacy

The Martians were destroyed — first on [[Earth]] by human countermeasures, and finally on [[Mars]] itself. Yet their impact far outlasted their physical existence:

- **Biological legacy**: Martian subvital units, dispersed across [[Earth]] as a pulmonary plague, eventually entered into symbiosis with terrestrial mammals. Millions of years later, the [[Great Brains|Fourth Men]] would deliberately incorporate a specially bred strain of these Martian parasites into their brain tissue to achieve direct sensitivity to [[radiation]] and "[[Telepathy|telepathic]]" communication (PN [011-044].
- **Psychological legacy**: The Martian episode shaped the [[Second Men]]'s understanding of cosmic tragedy, contributed to their spiritual decline, and ultimately informed the [[philosophy]] of later human species including the [[Fifth Men]] and the [[Last Men]].
- **Cautionary tale**: The Martians stand as a warning of what happens when a species prizes unity above truth, confuses a technological capacity with spiritual worth, and silences the private critical [[conscience]] in favour of an unchallengeable public mind.

The anonymous chronicler captured the tragedy with precision: the Martians were beings "in some ways so inferior yet in others definitely superior to the human species which they encountered" (PN [008-010]. Their story is that of an intelligence that reached the stars while remaining imprisoned within itself.
