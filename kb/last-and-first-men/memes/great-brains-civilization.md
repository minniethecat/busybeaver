# Great Brains Civilization

The **Great Brains Civilization** was the regime of the [[great-brains|Fourth Men]], enormous brain-entities who achieved dominance after supplanting the [[third-men|Third Men]]. Through advanced bioengineering, they designed a perfected human species with carefully calibrated physiology and temperament [011-032].

## Overview

The [[great-brains|Great Brains]] were not conventional rulers but biological engineers who saw humanity as raw material for improvement. They used centuries of chemical research to design far-reaching improvements in glandular secretions, aiming to produce a being with balanced emotions and perfect physiological equilibrium [011-032].

## Creation of the Fifth Men

The Great Brains' crowning achievement was engineering the [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]]. From a single pair of individuals, a new population gradually arose, fostered by their creators [011-037]. But the creators failed piteously to understand the mentality of their creatures. The Great Brains attempted to tyrannize over the Fifth Men, and for a period the planet was divided between the two species in conflict [011-037].

## Political Structure

The civilization was structured around the Great Brains as a ruling caste, with domesticated populations of [[third-men|Third Men]] serving as labor. The Brains themselves — enormous disembodied cerebral organisms — pursued pure research and biological experimentation, disconnected from the emotional and social needs of their subjects.

## Decline

The civilization collapsed when the [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]] asserted independence and proved more capable than their creators. The Great Brains' failure was ultimately one of empathy: they could engineer bodies and brains but could not understand the minds they created [011-037].

## Legacy

Despite their tyranny, the Great Brains established the foundation for all subsequent human evolution. The [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]], their greatest creation, would go on to become one of the most successful human species, eventually migrating to Venus and beyond.
