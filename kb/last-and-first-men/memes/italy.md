# Italy

**Italy** was a major European nation-state during the era of the [[first-men|First Men]]. Its rivalry with [[france|France]] over colonial territory in [[africa|Africa]] and national prestige triggered the opening hostilities of the [[anglo-french-war|European War]].

## National Character

By the era of the First Men, Italy had a complex national identity shaped by the nineteenth-century unification:

> "Since the fall of ancient Rome, the Italians had excelled more in art and literature than in martial achievement. But the heroic liberation of Italy in the nineteenth Christian century had made Italians peculiarly sensitive to national prestige." [001-011]

This sensitivity to national honor and perceived slights made Italy prone to international confrontation.

## The Franco-Italian Conflict

Italy's primary grievance was against [[france|France]], driven by territorial ambition and status rivalry:

> "Italy, hungry for French territory in Africa, jealous of French leadership of the Latin races, indignant at the protection afforded to Italian 'traitors' in France, became increasingly prone to quarrel with the most assertive of her late allies." [001-012]

A frontier incident involving an alleged "insult to the Italian flag" ultimately triggered a short but grotesque war between the two nations. Although brief, this Franco-Italian conflict was the first outbreak of what would escalate into the broader [[anglo-french-war|European War]].

## Role in the European War

The Franco-Italian War served as the spark that ignited the wider conflict. When [[england|England]] became embroiled in its own dispute with France, the Italian front had already demonstrated the destructive potential of modern aerial warfare. The Italian experience with military [[aeroplane|aeroplanes]] and the vulnerability of civilian populations to air attack presaged the devastation that would consume all of Europe.
