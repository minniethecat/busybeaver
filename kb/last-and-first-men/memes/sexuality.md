# Sexuality

A fundamental human drive whose manipulation, repression, and expression figured prominently in the politics, religion, and mass psychology of the First Men.

## Overview

Sexuality in "Last and First Men" functions as a lens through which Stapledon examines the First Men's civilization. Far from a private matter, sexual energy was deliberately exploited by power-holders — the press, the military, the state — to inflame public sentiment. Religious movements sought to reform it; political movements harnessed it; and the media traded upon it.

## Sexuality as Political Weapon

The First Men's governments and media understood sexuality's power over the masses. In the Anglo-French crisis, "the magnates of the English Press could not resist this opportunity of trading upon the public's sexuality, tribalism, and self-righteousness" [001-015]. A princess became "an overwhelmingly potent sexual symbol and emblem of tribalism, slaughtered and exposed before the eyes of her adorers" [001-011]. News was "distorted of course in such a manner as to admit no doubt that this act was the crowning devilry of sexual fiends beyond the Channel" [001-015].

The British Government itself was "probably confused by its own sexual repressions" [001-008] in handling the diplomatic crisis — a remarkable diagnosis of how unconscious drives shaped international relations.

## Religious Reform and Sexual Discipline

Religious movements targeted sexuality as a site of moral reform. The American-influenced religious revival promised to "see also to the reform of your people's sexual frivolity in which you squander so much of the Divine Energy" [003-012]. Sexual energy was conceived as a spiritual resource to be conserved and redirected toward higher purposes.

At the same time, a competing ideal promoted "continuous advance in aeronautical prowess, legal sexual freedom, and millionaireship" [004-016] — sexuality liberalized and commercialized as part of a materialist vision of human flourishing.

## The Daughter of Man Cult

The most elaborate fusion of sexuality and politics was the cult of the Daughter of Man. "In that moment of sexual triumph, he said, a great truth had been revealed to him" [004-009]. The World President's sexual union with this symbolic woman was presented as a holy mystery — the embodiment of spiritual-political unity. The cult demonstrated how the First Men's sexuality could be sublimated into collective worship.

## Later Species

Later human species channeled sexual energy differently — less explosively, more integratively. The Second Men achieved a more harmonious relationship between individual erotic life and communal existence. The more radically transformed species experienced forms of connection and reproduction that the First Men would not have recognized as sexual at all.
