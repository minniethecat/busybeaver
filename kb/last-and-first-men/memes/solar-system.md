# Solar System

The planetary system centered on the Sun that serves as the cosmic theater for the entire two-billion-year drama of human evolution, from the First Men to the Eighteenth Men.

## Location

### Type

Cosmic setting / Star system

### Context

The Solar System comprises the Sun, its planets, and their moons. The inner planets — Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars — are the primary stages of human activity, while the outer system holds significance for later human species. Some distance from the Solar System, astronomers detected a volume of non-luminous gas [013-035], and eventually the centre of the Solar System became "a star nearly as wide as the old orbit of Mercury" as the system was transformed [013-038].

## Physical Description

The Solar System contains Earth as its inhabited core world, with Mars and Venus as additional homes for human species. The scale is such that the all-embracing cosmos would "bulk as a sphere whose diameter was some twenty times greater than that of the lunar orbit in your day" [014-003].

## Historical Significance

### Era(s) of Importance

The entire history of humanity unfolds within the Solar System. The narrator explicitly frames the chronicle as concerning "the history of mind in the Solar System" [001-050].

### Key Events

The Solar System witnessed the rise and fall of 18 distinct human species. The Martian invasion brought an alien presence into the inner system. The Fifth Men's space-faring civilization dissipated into ultramicroscopic units and "contrived to get a purchase on the gravitational fields of the solar system, as a boat's sails get a purchase on the wind" . The Ninth Men endured a strange and thorough metamorphosis within the system . The outer regions became accessible in later eras — literature of the period "reverberates with the novelty of such experiences, with the sense that man had at last learned true flight, and acquired the freedom of the whole solar system" [012-030]. Eventually, astronomers detected interstellar phenomena and the system itself was transformed when the Sun expanded dramatically (013-035, [013-038].

## Species & Civilizations

Every human species from the First Men to the Eighteenth Men lived within the Solar System. Martians also occupied parts of the inner system. Later human species achieved full interplanetary civilization.

## Changes Over Time

The Solar System transformed from a conventional planetary system into a radically different configuration, with the Sun eventually expanding to fill the orbit of Mercury and fundamentally reshaping the inner system [013-038].
