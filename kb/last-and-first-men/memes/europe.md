# Europe

The continent that served as the primary stage for the First Men's early history — the site of devastating wars, the source of Western intellectual culture, and ultimately a region whose spirit survived only in fragments after its political eclipse.

## Location

### Type

Continent

### Context

Europe occupies the western peninsula of the Eurasian landmass. Its nations were the dominant global powers before their eclipse by America and China. European territorial conflicts extended into Africa, and European influence reached across the globe [001-012].

## Physical Description

Europe was a densely populated continent of competing nation-states. Poison gas warfare contaminated every country of Europe and Western Asia, with whiffs of poison continuing to be blown by fickle winds [001-050]. The continent's geography made it a natural theatre for intense inter-state rivalry.

## Historical Significance

### Era(s) of Importance

Europe dominated the earliest chapters of First Men history. The European War — "called at the time the War to End War" — was the first and least destructive of the world conflicts that displayed the fundamental incapacity of the First Men [001-007]. The peace that followed this war "epitomizes both the dawning vision and the self-destructive frenzy of the species" [001-008].

### Key Events

After the European War, the defeated nation became the most pacific and a stronghold of enlightenment [001-010]. Yet one brief but tragic incident, occurring within a century after the European War, sealed the fate of the First Men [001-009]. France achieved complete mastery of the continent, but having much to lose, behaved arrogantly and nervously [001-014]. Europe did eventually become something like a single nation in sentiment, though "this unity was brought about less by a common loyalty than by a common fear of America" [001-047]. In the end, the spirit of Europe "lived henceforth only in a piecemeal and dislocated manner in the minds of Americans, Chinese, Indians, and the rest" [002-032].

## Species & Civilizations

The First Men built their earliest civilizations in Europe. France, England, Germany, Italy, and Russia were the major powers. The intellectual integrity which was "the rarest and brightest thread of Western culture" was chiefly inspired by France and England, radiating throughout Europe and America [001-031].

## Changes Over Time

Europe passed from the dominant global centre to a subordinate region between the American and Asian blocs. The continent was devastated by wars and poison gas, and its cultural legacy dispersed across the globe.
