# Antarctica

Earth's southernmost continent, which served as a critical energy resource base for the First Men's global civilization and later hosted an advanced Nordic culture.

## Location

### Type

Continent

### Context

Located at Earth's southern pole. The coasts of Antarctica, thawed by climate change, were permanently inhabited by those engaged in exploiting the mineral wealth of the hinterland [004-024]. An immense warping of the earth's crust connected South America with Antarctica by way of the former Falkland Islands and South Georgia [005-010].

## Physical Description

Antarctica's coasts became temperate and habitable, while the interior contained amazingly rich coal seams [004-025]. The continent's mineral wealth included huge deposits of fuel that were profitably worked [003-021].

## Historical Significance

### Era(s) of Importance

Antarctica was of paramount economic importance during the World State era. America was first in the field of Antarctic exploitation and saw Antarctic fuel as a means for her advancement and for the self-imposed duty of "Americanizing the planet" [003-021]. Control of Antarctica was so critical that nations threatened war to secure cosmopolitan governance of the continent [003-051].

### Key Events

After the foundation of the World State, Antarctic fuel was very carefully husbanded, but the supply of oil gave out in less than two centuries [004-025]. Even the rich coalfields of Antarctica would not last forever [004-025]. For some thousands of years, the main energy supply came from Antarctica [004-047]. The more vigorous Antarctic coasts eventually became "modern" — developing an almost Nordic culture that outshone the ancient Patagonian centre [005-016]. Several wars erupted as physics and chemistry advanced in Antarctica and the Southerners devised superior engines of war [005-036]. More than fifty thousand years after the Martians' first appearance, they secured a permanent footing on the Antarctic tableland and overran Australia [009-016]. In later eras, epidemics of malignant tumours reduced the southern people to a mere remnant [016-029].

## Species & Civilizations

The First Men exploited Antarctica's resources. The Patagonian civilization's Antarctic offshoot developed a Nordic culture superior to the parent civilization. During the Martian invasion, Antarctica served as a Martian foothold. In later eras, the Antarctic nations persisted as distinct political entities but suffered from disease and social revolution [016-032].

## Changes Over Time

Antarctica transformed from an uninhabitable ice sheet to a temperate, inhabited continent. Its coasts supported permanent populations and its fuel resources powered global civilization. Over millennia, the population shifted from resource workers to a sophisticated Nordic culture, then declined through disease and conflict.
