# Group Mind

The group mind is a form of collective consciousness in which individual identities merge into a unified psychical entity. In [[olaf-stapledon|Stapledon]]'s cosmos, it appears in two forms: the inferior Martian group-mind and the more sophisticated mental communion of the [[last-men|Last Men]].

## Martian Group Mind

The [[martians|Martians]] achieved a true group-mind through electromagnetic radiation — "a single psychical process embodied in the electromagnetic radiation of the whole race." However, this collective mind was paradoxically inferior to individual Martian minds, suppressing all that was "distinctive of an individual at his best" [011-044].

## Human Telepathy vs. Group Mind

The [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]] deliberately avoided the group-mind path. Their telepathy was "only a means of intercourse between individuals" — there was "no true group-mind." This preserved individual identity while enabling deep communion [011-044]. The [[last-men|Last Men]] achieved something between pure individualism and group-mind, with individuals capable of direct mental contact while maintaining distinct personhood.
