# Siberia

A northern coastal region of Earth that became humanity's last surviving habitable zone during the moonfall catastrophe and later the birthplace of the Second Men.

## Location

### Type

Region (coastal settlement)

### Context

Siberia occupies the northern coast of Asia. During the moonfall era, for millions of years the planet was uninhabitable save for a fringe of Siberian coast [006-020]. The Siberian coast remained a tropical land, bounded on the south by a forest of volcanoes [006-028].

## Physical Description

After the moonfall catastrophe reshaped Earth's climate, the Siberian coast became a tropical land. Volcanoes formed a southern barrier. The region's climate was far cooler than the sweltering Labrador — the other surviving human refuge [006-019].

## Historical Significance

### Era(s) of Importance

Siberia was critically important during the transition from the First Men to the Second Men. The First Men crept along the Siberian coast before finding a tolerable resting-place at the mouth of one of the great rivers [006-004]. This small Siberian settlement became the last repository of human culture, hard-pressed but single-minded in its determination to preserve civilization [006-020].

### Key Events

During the Siberian exile, the First Men acquired a thick hairy covering — most races of the Second Men retained something of this blonde hirsute quality [007-002]. A ship's crew sailed from Siberia millions of years earlier, and their subhuman descendants were later encountered by the Second Men [007-014]. The biologist leader of the Siberian party recorded much of the sacred text of the Life of the Divine Boy, which was later discovered by the Second Men as a store of scientific and philosophical knowledge (007-036, [007-037].

## Species & Civilizations

The First Men's last survivors inhabited Siberia. The Second Men emerged from this Siberian seed population. Unlike the Labrador settlement, which was oppressed by a more sweltering climate and unsupported by the Siberian will to preserve human culture, the Siberian colony maintained its cultural mission [006-032].

## Changes Over Time

Before the catastrophe, Siberia was a cold northern region. After lunar disruption, the coast became tropical, bounded by volcanoes. The region served as a multi-million-year refuge, and eventually the Siberian population evolved into the Second Men.
