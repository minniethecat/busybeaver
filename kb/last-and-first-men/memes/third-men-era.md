# Age of the Third Men

The **Age of the Third Men** began after the [[second-men|Second Men]] fell into a thirty-million-year racial trance. Geological isolation on an island continent in the former North Atlantic triggered an epidemic of biological variation, from which the [[third-men|Third Men]] emerged as the dominant species [010-003].

## Overview

The Third Men were scarcely more than half the stature of their predecessors, with golden eyes, six-fingered hands, and expressive bat-like ears [010-004]. They were short-lived, seldom reaching sixty, and preferred suicide to senility. Their civilization was not a unified political order but a scattered network of pastoral communities united by a shared biocentric worldview.

## Culture

The Third Men possessed an uncanny sympathy with all living things. They tamed the great leonine wolf and developed relationships with animals that blended economic cooperation with religious symbolism [010-008]. Selective breeding became an obsession — every chieftain boasted of his territory's uniquely noble bears, birds, and plants [010-009].

## Religion

The era's dominant religion worshipped Life as an all-pervading spirit. The deity was imagined as a composite animal with human hands, eyes, and sexual organs holding the world between divine fingers [010-012]. This faith was complicated by a strain of sadism — pain was regarded as a superior mode of apprehending the sacred [010-014].

## End

The era concluded when the Third Men, through their own biological manipulation, created the [[great-brains|Great Brains]] — enormous cerebral entities that would eventually supplant their creators and inaugurate the Age of the Great Brains.
