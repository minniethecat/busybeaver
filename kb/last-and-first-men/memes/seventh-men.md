# Seventh Men

The Seventh Men were a pygmy flying species, scarcely heavier than the largest terrestrial flying birds [013-016]. Often called the Flying Men, they were probably the most carefree of all human species, their existence defined by the freedom of the air [013-018].

## Biology

### Physical Form

The Seventh Men were diminutive bat-like beings, their bodies adapted for powered [[flying|flight]] in the dense [[venus-place|Venus]]ian atmosphere. Their wings allowed them to navigate the skies with natural grace, and their small size made sustained [[flying|flight]] energetically feasible. In the air and on the ground, the Seventh Men were effectively different beings, their behavior and consciousness shifting dramatically between the two realms [013-021].

### Lifespan & Reproduction

The high-strung nature of the Seventh Men had consequences for their reproduction: infants were more often deformed than in other human species, a vulnerability that their culture learned to accommodate [013-028]. Despite this fragility, their species endured for an extraordinarily long period.

## Psychology

### Mental Traits

The Seventh Men were characterized by their devotion to the aerial life. They grudged every moment that was not spent in [[flying|flight]], and their psychology was fundamentally shaped by the experience of three-dimensional movement through the sky [013-024]. They were chiefly concerned with matters of the air — the patterns of wind, the textures of cloud, the social choreography of flocking [[flying|flight]] [013-020]. On the ground, they became subdued and practical, reserving their true vitality for the sky.

### Special Abilities

The Seventh Men possessed the unique ability among human species of powered [[flying|flight]]. Their mastery of the air was not technological but biological — a natural endowment that liberated them from the constraints of surface-bound existence. This freedom shaped every aspect of their culture and consciousness.

## Culture & Society

### Social Structure

The social order of the Seventh Men was in essence neither utilitarian nor hierarchical in any conventional sense [013-025]. Their society was organized around the rhythms of [[flying|flight]], with communities forming and reforming in the air. During the long Venerian nights, they slept crowded together like roosting birds, their bodies packed closely for warmth [013-026].

### Achievements

It was scarcely credible that the culture of the Seventh Men should have lasted so long, given its apparent fragility [013-027]. Their civilization endured for millions of years, sustained by the joy of [[flying|flight]] itself. They produced little in the way of material technology or monumental art, but their aerial culture represented a unique experiment in human happiness — a society built around freedom of movement and the exhilaration of the sky.

## Historical Role

### Origin

The Seventh Men evolved on [[venus-place|Venus]] as a biological adaptation to the planet's dense atmosphere, which made powered [[flying|flight]] possible for human-sized organisms. They succeeded the [[sixth-men|Sixth Men]], whose terrestrial existence they transcended through their aerial transformation.

### Fate

The Seventh Men were eventually succeeded by the [[eighth-men|Eighth Men]], a giant long-headed species that made the startling astronomical discovery that would redirect human destiny toward the outer solar system [013-034]. The Seventh Men's unique experiment in aerial joy gave way to a species defined by cosmic ambition.
