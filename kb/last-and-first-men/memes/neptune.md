# Neptune

The eighth and outermost planet of the Solar System, and humanity's final planetary home. Neptune was colonized after [[venus|Venus]] became uninhabitable, serving as the stage for the last chapters of human evolution, including the emergence of the [[last-men|Last Men]].

## Physical Description

Neptune is a massive outer planet with powerful gravity. Its physical conditions posed unique challenges for human habitation: "Neptune's year was at this time about 165 Earth-years" [014-009], and its powerful gravitational field made movement difficult.

> "Jumping was no easy matter on Neptune, the catlike types were all more or less disabled." [014-011]

Despite the harsh environment, Neptune offered a habitable zone for terrestrial life. The planet was prepared for colonization through the establishment of vegetation shipped from Venus: "vegetation was shipped to Neptune and established in the warm areas" [013-038]. The planet's atmosphere and seas supported the transplanted Earth-Venus biosphere.

## Historical Significance

### Colonization

The colonization of Neptune was a desperate measure undertaken as [[venus|Venus]] approached its end as a habitable world. The Fifth Men had already determined that the outer planets offered the only long-term refuge.

> "The three planets beyond Neptune were too cold and too small to support life; but Neptune itself might be made habitable." [013-035]

Humanity's migration to Neptune involved the transfer of not only population but entire ecosystems. The Last Men, as the Eighteenth human species, were "the Eighteenth, and certainly the last, human species" [014-004], having evolved specifically for the Neptunian environment.

### The Last Men's Civilization

On Neptune, human civilization reached heights never before attained. The [[last-men-commonwealth|Last Men Commonwealth]] represented a mature, world-spanning society with profound spiritual and philosophical achievements.

> "For the first time on Neptune, conceived, as an enduring racial enterprise, the religion of love." [014-018]

The planet's vast size — "there is plenty of room on Neptune even for the million million persons of the last human species" [015-005] — allowed a population and cultural complexity unprecedented in human history.

### The End

Neptune was destined to be humanity's final stage. The [[last-men|Last Men]] foresaw the eventual end of the solar system and the extinction of human life. Their final acts were not of despair but of contemplation, having achieved a philosophical comprehension that gave meaning to humanity's two-billion-year journey.

## Species & Civilizations

The [[last-men|Last Men]] (Eighteenth Men) were the dominant species on Neptune, having evolved from the human lineages that preceded them. Their [[last-men-commonwealth|Commonwealth]] was a unified world-state encompassing the entire planet, representing the culmination of all human social organization.

## Changes Over Time

Neptune's habitable period was finite. The planet gradually cooled as the Sun aged, and the Last Men understood that even Neptune would eventually become uninhabitable. Their civilization's final phase was marked by this awareness and the philosophical reconciliation with humanity's ultimate extinction.
