# Longevity

Longevity — the natural capacity for greatly extended lifespan — was one of the hallmarks of advanced human species in [[olaf-stapledon|Stapledon]]'s future history, reaching its peak in the [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]] who lived for thousands of years.

## Origin

The [[second-men|Second Men]] showed "much greater natural longevity" compared to the [[first-men|First Men]], setting the evolutionary trend [007-033].

## The Fifth Men

Longevity reached its zenith in the [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]], whose natural lifespan extended to many thousands of years. Combined with telepathy and their extremely subtle brain-structure, their longevity "afforded each individual an immense number of intimate friendships, and some slight acquaintance actually with the whole race" [011-043].

## Cultural Impact

The Last Terrestrials, inheriting this trait, were "blessed also with longevity and philosophic detachment," enabling a civilization of deep contemplation [012-006]. Long periods of life allowed for the accumulation of wisdom, artistic mastery, and scientific understanding impossible for shorter-lived species.
