# Regenerate Christian Brotherhood

The **Regenerate Christian Brotherhood** was the most powerful of the Western Christian churches during the era of the [[first-world-state|First World State]]. It played a central role in the unification of world religions under the worship of [[gordelpus|Gordelpus]] and participated in the great conference that established the [[sacred-order-of-scientists|Sacred Order of Scientists]].

## Theology

The Brotherhood reinterpreted Christian doctrine in terms of the scientific religion of the age. Central to their theology was the figure of the Discoverer — the Chinese physicist and saint who had allegedly unlocked the mystery of Gordelpus:

> "The Regenerate Christian Brotherhood, by now overwhelmingly the most powerful of the Western Churches, regarded the Discoverer as the Son of God, who, in this his Second Coming, had proposed to bring about the millennium by publishing the secret of divine power; but, finding the peoples still unable to put in practice even the more primitive gospel of love which was announced at his First Coming, he had suffered martyrdom for man's sake, and had entrusted his secret to the Scientists." [004-011]

This theology positioned the Discoverer as a Christ-figure whose Second Coming had already occurred — but who, finding humanity unworthy, had chosen martyrdom and entrusted divine power to the scientific elite rather than revealing it publicly.

## Role in Religious Unity

The Brotherhood participated in the historic religious conference on the Pacific island that had become the "Mecca of cosmopolitan sentiment":

> "Upon that island in the Pacific... the heads of Buddhism, Mohammedanism, Hinduism, the Regenerate Christian Brotherhood, and the Modern Catholic Church in South America, agreed that their differences were but differences of expression." [004-017]

This conference formally unified all major religions under the shared worship of Divine Energy and paved the way for the merger of science and religion into the Sacred Order of Scientists.

## Legacy

The Brotherhood represented the final evolutionary stage of Christianity before its absorption into the state religion of Gordelpus. Though the spirit of Christianity was ultimately lost, many of its symbols — including the cross-like form of the aeroplane — were preserved and repurposed in the new faith.
