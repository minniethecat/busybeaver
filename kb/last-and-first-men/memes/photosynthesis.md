## Overview

Photosynthesis appears throughout "Last and First Men" as a fundamental energy-capture mechanism, evolving from the natural photosynthesis of Martian organisms to the artificial photosynthesis systems that sustained the Last Men. It represents the bridge between biology and energy technology.

## Martian Photosynthesis

The Martian cloudlets — subvital units floating in the Martian atmosphere — "absorbed sunlight by a photosynthesis almost identical with that of terrestrial plants" [008-014]. This was their primary mode of energy acquisition, making them fundamentally photosynthetic organisms despite their radically alien biology.

## Venusian Adaptation

On Venus, photosynthesis took on new forms. Organisms "engaged solely in photosynthesis" in the "upper air" [013-024] of the Venusian atmosphere. The human population on Venus became dependent on these photosynthetic organisms as the base of their food chain, adapting human civilization to an entirely new planetary ecology.

## Artificial Photosynthesis and the Last Men

The Eighteenth Men achieved the ultimate technological application: artificial photosynthesis. On Neptune, energy was "produced by artificial photosynthesis on the broiling planet Jupiter" [015-007], capturing solar energy at planetary scale and transmitting it across space. This represented the culmination of the photosynthetic principle — from biological process to cosmic energy infrastructure.
