# Russo-German War

The Russo-German War was a decisive [[war|conflict]] in the early history of the [[first-men|First Men]], occurring in the turbulent period before the establishment of the [[first-world-state|First World State]]. It consolidated rival powers and reshaped the geopolitical landscape of Europe, setting in motion the chain of events that would eventually lead to global unification [001-048].

## Background

The war emerged from the chronic disunity that characterized [[first-men|First Men]] civilization in its industrial phase. The European powers, having emerged from the earlier [[european-war|European War]], remained locked in competitive rivalries that made large-scale [[war|conflict]] almost inevitable. Russia and Germany, representing distinct civilizational traditions — one Eastern and expansive, the other Central European and industrial — found their interests incompatible.

## The Conflict

The Russo-German War was brutal and transformative. The upshot of the [[war|conflict]] was that Europe's political map was fundamentally redrawn [001-052]. The specific territorial and political outcomes are not recorded in detail in the [[last-men|Last Men]]'s chronicle, but the war's effects radiated far beyond its immediate participants. The disaster that followed the [[war|conflict]] scarred the European psyche, creating a generation determined to prevent such devastation from recurring [002-008].

## Aftermath & Significance

The consolidation of power that followed the Russo-German War was one of the critical steps toward world unity. The war demonstrated, as had the earlier [[european-war|European War]], that the old system of competing nation-states was incompatible with industrial civilization. The Russo-German disaster convinced the surviving powers that some form of international order was necessary — a conviction that would eventually find expression in the [[league-of-nations|League of Nations]] and, ultimately, the [[first-world-state|First World State]] [003-002].

## Legacy

The Russo-German War, like the [[european-war|European War]] before it, was remembered by subsequent generations as a cautionary example of the destructive potential of nationalist rivalry in an age of advanced technology. The [[last-men|Last Men]]'s chronicle notes it as one of the key [[war|conflict]]s that paradoxically advanced the cause of world unity by demonstrating the intolerable cost of division.
