# Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein is invoked in the narrative as a temporal reference point, marking the era from which one of the book's two narrators speaks.

## Identity

### Species

Einstein belonged to the [[first-men|First Men]], the original human species.[PN=P03-001]

### Era

He lived in the early twentieth century, before the formation of the [[first-world-state|First World State]].

## Role in the Narrative

### Temporal Anchor

The book describes itself as having two authors: one contemporary with its readers, and one from the distant future. The brain that "conceives and writes these sentences lives in the time of Einstein," while the true inspirer of the book inhabits an age far in the remote future.[PN=P03-001]

### Longevity Baseline

Einstein's era serves as a reference for human lifespan. In his time, an individual's youth lasted approximately twenty-five years, a figure that served as a baseline against which later changes in human longevity were measured.[PN=005-012] Under the World State, youth was artificially doubled, and after the collapse of civilization, it contracted dramatically.
