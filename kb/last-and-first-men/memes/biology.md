## Overview

Biology evolves dramatically across the eighteen human species in "Last and First Men." Beginning as a nascent observational science among the First Men, it becomes the central transformative discipline — enabling later species to deliberately reshape their own bodies, minds, and evolutionary trajectories.

## Biology Among the First Men

The First Men developed biology alongside physics and psychology as part of their scientific revolution. The "established formulae of physics, biology, psychology" [003-015] formed the intellectual foundation of their civilization, though their biological knowledge remained limited compared to what later species would achieve.

## The Second Men's Biological Foundation

The Second Men inherited and deepened biological understanding. Their education included "the outlines of physics, chemistry, biology, psychology, and geometry" [006-022] as essential knowledge for every citizen. Biology was not merely an academic discipline but a practical science informing their entire civilization.

## Martian Biology

Martian biology was radically alien. To describe it required an entirely new framework — "to describe the biology, psychology, and history of a race so alien to humanity" [008-011] pushed human comprehension to its limits. The Martians' subvital units and cloudlet-based existence challenged every Earth-born biological assumption.

## Biology as Transformative Force

For the later human species, biology became the tool for conscious self-evolution. It "did biology itself give rise to chemistry and physics" [010-029], indicating that the deeper penetration into biological processes unlocked new physical sciences. "It was from biology that the first hint had come" [012-010] of possibilities that transformed human existence.

The Eighth Men, or Great Brains, achieved the ultimate synthesis — "a sufficient biology, and a human psychology surpassing our own" [013-024] — enabling them to guide humanity's adaptation to new worlds.
