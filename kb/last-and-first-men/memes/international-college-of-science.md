# International College of Science

The **International College of Science** was the global corporate body of scientific workers during the era of the [[first-world-state|First World State]]. It controlled access to scientific research and served as the precursor to the [[sacred-order-of-scientists|Sacred Order of Scientists]].

## Overview

The College functioned as a close corporation that regulated who could practice science. It maintained a monopoly over scientific work, granting the title of "Scientist" only to those who passed its examinations and paid substantial fees. Membership was a prerequisite for most lucrative professional positions.

## Structure

Entry into the College was deliberately exclusive:

> "The scientific workers of the world had long ago organized themselves as a close corporation. Entrance to the International College of Science was to be obtained only by examination and the payment of high fees." [004-012]

Membership conferred the right to perform experiments and was an essential qualification for prestigious posts. The College was also rumored to maintain secret rites accessible only to its inner circle.

## Role in the Sacred Order

The College's most consequential act was its role in the formation of the [[sacred-order-of-scientists|Sacred Order of Scientists]]. During the great conference of science and religion, the [[president-of-the-world|President of the World]] called upon the College to nominate a select body from its membership:

> "He, therefore, called upon the International College of Science to nominate from amongst themselves a select body, which should be sanctified by the Church, and called the Sacred Order of Scientists." [004-017]

This effectively merged the scientific establishment with the [[first-world-state|First World State]]'s state religion of [[gordelpus|Gordelpus]], transforming the College's elite into a sanctified priesthood.

## Legacy

The International College of Science represented the institutionalization of scientific knowledge under the First World State. Its transformation into the Sacred Order of Scientists marked the fusion of science and religion that characterized the era's intellectual life — and ultimately contributed to the crisis of legitimacy that led to the [[fall-of-the-first-world-state|Fall of the First World State]].
