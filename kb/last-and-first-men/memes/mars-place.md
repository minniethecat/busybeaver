# Mars

Mars was the home world of the intelligent cloud-jelly [[martians|Martians]] — the first alien species encountered by humanity. The planet's deteriorating environment drove the [[martians|Martians]] to invade [[earth-place|Earth]], setting in motion one of the central tragedies of solar system history.

## Geography & Environment

Mars was a smaller, colder world than [[earth-place|Earth]], with a thin atmosphere and limited surface water. The planet's most distinctive environmental feature was the steady loss of water-vapor from its atmosphere, a process that was changing Martian conditions beyond the limits within which the native life forms could survive [008-014]. The coastal waters of the Martian seas had been the cradle of Martian evolution, but as the planet desiccated, the intelligent cloud-jelly organisms found their habitat shrinking toward extinction [008-013].

## Native Life

Mars evolved a radically different form of life from [[earth-place|Earth]]'s. Terrestrial organisms had been constrained by the tyranny of solid bodies and fixed forms, but Martian life — at its most advanced — took the form of free-floating ultramicroscopic units that could combine into cloud-like superorganisms [008-017]. At the zenith of Martian evolution, the whole planet was effectively a single biological entity, with individual cloudlets functioning as semi-autonomous units within the planetary organism [008-020].

## The Martian Invasion

The deterioration of the Martian environment drove the [[martians|Martians]] to seek a new home. Their invasion of [[earth-place|Earth]] was not an act of malevolence but a desperate migration born of planetary necessity. The [[martians|Martians]] deployed beam radiation against terrestrial populations, their attack suggesting purposeful and hostile intent on a scale humanity had never encountered [009-004]. The invasion failed, and the [[martians|Martians]] were eventually driven to extinction — humanity's first knowing destruction of an intelligent species.

## Legacy

After the extinction of the [[martians|Martians]], Mars became a world of ruins. Later human explorers groped through the still-visible remains of Martian civilization, studying the cloud-shrouded structures and pondering the tragedy of a species that had mastered vast knowledge yet could not save itself [012-030]. Mars remained a haunting presence in human memory — a reminder of the fragility of intelligence in an indifferent cosmos.
