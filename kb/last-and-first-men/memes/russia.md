Russia was the immense Eurasian power whose [[bolshevik-revolution|Bolshevik Revolution]] transformed it into a radical state, unleashing an unprecedented cultural and intellectual ferment. Its organizational model — the disciplined party-state — eventually influenced governance across Eurasia and beyond.

## Location

Russia stretched across northern Eurasia, spanning "the immense tract of corn and forest" [001-033] from Eastern Europe to the Pacific. It bordered [[germany|Germany]] to the west — described as "the eastern neighbour 'beyond Germany'" — and [[asia|Asia]] to the east, with territories reaching into Siberia.

## Historical Significance

### The Bolshevik Revolution

The [[bolshevik-revolution|Bolshevik Revolution]] was as much a cultural as a political event. It created "a new element" in Russian culture, "one which had not been known before in any modern state" [001-034]. From the "scattered towns of this immense tract of corn and forest, and still more in the metropolis," there arose "an original mode of art and thought" [001-033] whose influence radiated outward across continents.

### Cultural Renaissance

The revolution unleashed a cultural renaissance unprecedented in Russian history. The new Russia began "to grasp human experience in one unifying act of apprehension" [001-036], an intellectual ambition that distinguished it from all previous civilizations. This synthesis united Western technique with Eastern contemplative traditions.

### The Russo-German War

Russia's rivalry with [[germany|Germany]] eventually erupted into the [[russo-german-war|Russo-German War]], a continental conflict that involved biological weapons and contributed to European mental deterioration [001-052].

## Organizational Innovation

The Bolsheviks' most enduring contribution was the disciplined elite party as an instrument of governance. "Modelled originally on the Bolshevik Party of Russia, it had also drawn inspiration from the native and literary civil service of old China" [003-010]. This fusion created a powerful new political technology capable of governing vast, diverse populations.
