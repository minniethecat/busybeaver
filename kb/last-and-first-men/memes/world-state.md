## Overview

The World State is a recurring political form in "Last and First Men." Across multiple human species and eras, the dream (and nightmare) of planetary unification is realized, collapses, and is rebuilt in new forms. Each World State reflects the biological and psychological nature of the species that creates it.

## The First World State

The First Men achieved world unification through conflict and economic integration. "The attainment of the world state might have been regarded, not as an end, but as the starting point of a new and more difficult adventure" [001-009]. However, the First World State quickly became a vehicle for American cultural and economic dominance rather than genuine human unity.

The "financial organization of the world state, having far outstripped the old national organizations" [004-037], reveals that economic forces — not political ideals — drove unification. The World State "defined all the processes of the world state and the ritual of the established church" [004-046], fusing political power with a quasi-religious ideology of Americanism.

## The Second Men's World State

The Second Men inherited and reformed the concept of world governance. Their approach was more rational and less nationalistic. Unlike the First Men's World State, the Second Men's administration was built on scientific principles rather than commercial interests.

## Later Planetary Administrations

Later species developed their own forms of planetary governance. The Martians "encouraged this policy" [010-043] of selective breeding among human populations, exerting a form of biological world governance over the human species. The Eighth Men and subsequent species achieved forms of world administration that integrated biological management with political organization, far surpassing the crude nation-state unification of the First Men.
