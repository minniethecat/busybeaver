# World Financial Directorate

The concealed seat of real power within the [[First World State]], a small council of millionaires who governed humanity for four millennia through control of planetary finance.

## Overview

Though the [[First World State]] presented itself as a vast system of guild socialism, actual authority lay elsewhere. Each industry was in theory democratically governed and coordinated through a [[World Industrial Council]], but "the real seat of power was not the Council, not even the inner ring of the Council, but the Financial Directorate" [004-003].

This body of roughly twelve individuals wielded ultimate authority over the planetary economy for the entire lifespan of the World State.

## Details

### Founded

The Directorate emerged from the transition that created the [[First World State]], approximately three hundred and eighty years after the [[European War]].

It was established through "concerted action in [[america|America]] and China" whereby cosmopolitan big business, through passive resistance, hamstrung the military governments of the old nation-state order [004-002]. In China the process was bloodless; in [[america|America]] there was disorder for weeks, but the population, desperate for peace, offered no sustained resistance.

### Structure

The [[First World State]] was formally organized as a guild-socialist federation. Industries were coordinated through a [[World Industrial Council]], where each guild's status reflected economic power and public prestige.

An inner ring of "noble industries" dominated the Council: [[Finance]] held first place, followed by [[flying|Flying]], Engineering, Surface Locomotion, Chemical Industry, and Professional Athletics [004-011].

But even this inner ring was subordinate to the Financial Directorate proper -- "a dozen millionaires, with the [[america|America]]n President and the Chinese Vice-President at their head" [004-003]. The Directorate's authority was sustained not by democratic mandate but by the population's eagerness for peace and their terror of renewed national [[war|conflict]].

## History

### Rise

The Directorate's rise was inseparable from the founding of the [[First World State]]. As military governments collapsed under the weight of recurrent world wars, the financiers imposed their will and the generals capitulated.

### Key Actions

Under Directorate governance, the World State achieved staggering material triumphs: the [[Sahara]] transformed into a lake district, Arctic islands warmed by redirected currents, Antarctica's coasts permanently inhabited, pylons rising three miles high [004-025].

Yet the Directorate presided over an empire hollow at its core. Scientific inquiry was suppressed by the [[Sacred Order of Scientists]], the worship of [[Gordelpus]] became state religion, and intelligence steadily declined across the race [004-039].

### Collapse

The Directorate fell with the [[First World State]] itself. When [[Antarctic coal]] exhaustion precipitated the terminal energy crisis, the Directorate proved incapable of responding. The collapse unleashed the [[First Dark Age]] -- a hundred thousand years of savagery.

## Legacy

The World Financial Directorate is an archetype of a recurring pattern: nominal democracy concealing actual plutocracy. Its four-millennium reign demonstrated that a system sustained by material abundance and popular exhaustion could endure far longer than one built on consent -- but also that such a system, when its material basis failed, would collapse utterly.
