# Nationalist Party of China

The **Nationalist Party of China** was the governing organization of [[china|China]] during the era preceding the formation of the [[first-world-state|First World State]]. Combining elements of civil service, religious order, and revolutionary party, it represented one of the most sophisticated systems of governance achieved by the [[first-men|First Men]].

## Character and Structure

The Party was unique among the governing institutions of its time:

> "The Party was an intensely practical yet idealistic organization, half civil service, half religious order, though violently opposed to every kind of religion." [003-012]

Modelled originally on the [[bolshevik-revolution|Bolshevik Party]] of Russia, it drew inspiration from the traditional Chinese literary civil service and the administrative integrity of British Imperialism in the East. Through this synthesis, it approached the classical ideal of [[plato|Platonic]] governors — philosopher-administrators devoted to the common good.

## Admission and Standards

Membership required both intellectual and practical qualification:

> "In order to be admitted to the Party, it was necessary to do two things, to pass a very strict written examination on Western and Chinese social theory, and to come through a five years apprenticeship in actual administrative work." [003-012]

This dual requirement produced a governing class of unusual competence and integrity. The Party set "a brilliant example of self-oblivious devotion" that was unprecedented in Chinese history. Its members were genuinely interested in social rather than private matters, earning them widespread trust.

## Impact and Limitations

Despite its virtues, the Party governed only China proper. Outside its administration, corruption, peculation, and nepotism persisted. The Party's ultimate loyalty was not to individual Chinese people — whom party members regarded with near-indifference — but to "the corporate unity and culture of the race."

The Party's model of governance influenced the later administrative structures of the First World State, particularly in the [[european-confederacy|European Confederacy]] formed under Chinese leadership. However, the subsequent dominance of American cultural and economic power submerged Chinese organizational principles within the broader global order.
