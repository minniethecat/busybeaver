# First Dark Age

The First Dark Age followed the catastrophic wars that destroyed [[first-men|First Men]] civilization. It was a period of radical environmental and social collapse lasting roughly one hundred thousand years, during which the surviving human populations were reduced to barbarism and forced to adapt to a poisoned and devastated [[earth-place|Earth]].

> It might have been expected that, after the downfall of the First World State, recovery would have occurred within a few generations. Historians have, indeed, often puzzled over the cause of this surprisingly complete and lasting degradation.

The collapse was not caused by any single factor but by a confluence of disasters that reinforced one another, creating a downward spiral from which recovery proved nearly impossible for millennia.

## Environmental Conditions

The wars left the planet scarred. Chemical weapons had poisoned vast territories, and the global climate was disrupted. Surviving human populations were forced into subsistence in the least contaminated regions. Natural resources had been exhausted — coal, oil, and mineral wealth had been stripped from the accessible crust by the industrial civilization that preceded the collapse.

> These latter-day barbarians were hampered by living in a used planet. Not only were coal and oil no more, but almost no mineral wealth of any kind remained within reach of their feeble instruments and wits.

The psychological weight of decline was as crushing as the physical devastation.

> The idea of irresistible decay obsessed the race at this time. The generation which brought about the downfall of the World State oppressed its juniors with stories of past amenities and marvels, and hugged to itself the knowledge that the young men had not the wit to rebuild such complexity.

## Key Events

The collapse occurred in stages: the [[european-war|European War]] was followed by the [[anglo-french-war|Anglo-French War]] and the [[russo-german-war|Russo-German War]], each further destabilizing global civilization. By the end, all major nation-states had collapsed, and humanity was reduced to scattered tribes [005-005].

> Even at the close of the First Dark Age the ruins of the ancient residential pylons still characterized every landscape, often with an effect of senile domination over the hovels of latter-day savages.

The survivors lived in the shadow of their ancestors' achievements, unable to replicate the technology or social organization that had come before.

## The Patagonian Recovery

The First Dark Age was eventually brought to an end by the emergence of the [[patagonian-civilization|Patagonian civilization]] in South America.

> Various causes, then, combined in South America, and especially in the new and virgin plains of Patagonia, to bring the First Dark Age to an end. The great theme of mind began to repeat itself. But in a minor key.

The Patagonians were a sober and determined people who, despite the biological disadvantage of shortened youth, slowly rebuilt civilization through steady, cumulative effort across countless generations.
