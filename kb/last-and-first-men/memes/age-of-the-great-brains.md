# Age of the Great Brains

The **Age of the Great Brains** was the epoch in which the [[great-brains|Fourth Men]] — enormous disembodied cerebral organisms — dominated Earth. This era saw the most radical experiments in biological engineering in human history, culminating in the creation of the [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]] [011-032].

## Overview

After the decline of the [[third-men|Third Men]], the Great Brains established themselves as the planet's ruling intelligence. Unlike their predecessors, they were not a natural evolutionary development but the product of deliberate biological manipulation — the Third Men had, in effect, engineered their own successors. The Great Brains existed primarily as vast brain-entities, sustained by mechanical life-support systems and served by populations of domesticated Third Men [011-037].

## Achievements

The Great Brains' greatest achievement was the design and creation of the [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]]. Through centuries of chemical and biological research, they calibrated every aspect of the new species: glandular secretions for emotional balance, tissue structure for longevity, and neural architecture for superior cognition [011-032]. From a single pair of engineered individuals, the Fifth Men population arose, initially fostered by their creators but ultimately asserting independence.

## Decline

The era ended in conflict when the Fifth Men, whom the Great Brains had attempted to dominate, rebelled and established their own civilization. The Great Brains failed not through lack of intelligence but through lack of empathy — they could engineer bodies and brains but could not comprehend the minds they created [011-037].

## Legacy

The Age of the Great Brains represents humanity's first experiment in conscious self-evolution. Though the Great Brains' tyranny was eventually overthrown, their biological template for the Fifth Men shaped all subsequent human species.
