# Earth

The cradle of humanity and the primary stage for human history from the emergence of the [[first-men|First Men]] through the [[age-of-the-fifth-men|Fifth Men]]. Over two billion years, Earth witnessed the rise and fall of multiple human species, devastating wars, and radical geological transformations.

## Physical Description

Earth was a temperate rocky planet in the inner Solar System. During the era of the First Men, its continents included the familiar landmasses of [[europe|Europe]], [[asia|Asia]], [[africa|Africa]], and the [[americas|Americas]], though their shapes and climates were repeatedly altered by geological upheavals.

> "During the zenith of Western Civilization, therefore, the subnormal were sterilized." [004-038]

The planet's crust was unstable over geological timescales. Major warping raised new landmasses — the ocean floor east of [[south-america|South America]] was thrust upward, connecting it with [[antarctica|Antarctica]] [005-010]. [[south-america|South America]] itself "mostly collapsed into the hollow of the Pacific" during later transformations [006-031].

## Historical Significance

### Cradle of Human Species

Earth was the birthplace of the [[first-men|First Men]], who emerged from earlier primates and built the first human civilizations. After the extinction of the First Men, Earth remained dormant through the [[second-dark-age|Second Dark Age]] until the [[second-men|Second Men]] arose from the survivors' descendants [005-037].

The planet subsequently hosted the [[third-men|Third Men]], [[fourth-men|Fourth Men]] (the Great Brains), and the [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]] before becoming uninhabitable. Each species left its mark on the planet's surface and biological heritage.

### The Martian Invasion

Earth was invaded from [[mars|Mars]] in a catastrophic event that reshaped the planet's destiny. The [[martians|Martians]], cloud-like composite organisms, descended upon Earth seeking water and resources.

> "Thus ended the first invasion of the Earth from Mars." [008-009]

The invasion lasted generations, with the Martians exploiting Earth's oceans and atmosphere while human civilization crumbled. When the Martians finally withdrew, Earth was left in a devastated state that contributed to the decline of the First Men.

### Final Abandonment

By the era of the Fifth Men, Earth had become increasingly inhospitable. The [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]] ultimately colonized [[venus|Venus]] as their new home, completing a migration that transferred the entirety of human civilization to a new world [012-047]. After this exodus, Earth entered a long period of dormancy — "once more Earth slept" [007-030].

## Species & Civilizations

The planet hosted the [[first-men|First Men]] and their civilizations, including the [[first-world-state|First World State]] and the [[patagonian-civilization|Patagonian Civilization]]. Later, the [[second-men|Second Men]], [[third-men|Third Men]], [[fourth-men|Fourth Men]], and [[fifth-men|Fifth Men]] all made Earth their home before humanity's eventual dispersal to other planets.

## Changes Over Time

Earth's geography was repeatedly transformed by crustal warping, climate change, and tectonic activity. [[south-america|South America]] collapsed into the Pacific, [[siberia|Siberia]] became tropical, and the [[sahara|Sahara]] became a lake district during the World State era [004-024]. Over millions of years, the planet's surface bore little resemblance to the continents known to the First Men.
