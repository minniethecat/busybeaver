# Asia

Earth's largest continent, which along with Africa formed a geopolitical counterweight to the American Continental Federation during the First Men era's great power rivalry.

## Location

### Type

Continent

### Context

Asia occupies the eastern landmass of Earth. In the depths of Asia, a hardy and ever-growing population developed, influenced by the new ways of life spreading from townsfolk to peasants [001-035]. Through intercourse with peasant culture and with the peoples of Asia, a new Russia began to grasp human experience in one unifying act of apprehension [001-036].

## Physical Description

Asia stretched from the Urals to the Pacific, from the Siberian tundra to the Indian Ocean. Western Asia was affected by the poison gas warfare that contaminated Europe, with whiffs of poison continuing to be blown by fickle winds into every country of Europe and Western Asia [001-050].

## Historical Significance

### Era(s) of Importance

Asia rose to geopolitical significance after the eclipse of Europe, becoming one pole in the bipolar world order. The allegiance of humanity crystallized into American and Asian spheres [003-001].

### Key Events

Asia and Africa formed one side of the global rivalry, while America led the other [003-004]. During the Russo-German War, Asia's geopolitical alignment favoured China and Germany. The kind of cosmopolitanism championed by America and Europe was regarded by Asia and Africa "without sympathy" [003-006]. In the major global conflict, Asia and North Africa formed the geographically more compact system of combatants, though America and her dependents were economically more organized [003-023]. American interests competed with "still prolific sources under Chinese control" for the exploitation of Russian Asiatic oil [001-042].

## Species & Civilizations

The First Men inhabited Asia. Chinese civilization dominated East Asia, while Russian influence extended across Northern Asia. The continent was a patchwork of civilizations, with the "peoples of Asia" contributing to Russia's cultural development toward a more unified apprehension of human experience.

## Changes Over Time

Asia's political landscape shifted from a mosaic of traditional societies to a China-dominated bloc opposing American hegemony. Over geological timescales, the continent's geography was reshaped by the same forces that transformed the rest of Earth.
