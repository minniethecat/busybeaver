# Section 36

[036-001]Corpang’s face became swollen with a kind of coarse anger. “Life is

[036-002]clearly false—I have been seeking Thire for a lifetime, and now I

[036-003]find—this.”

[036-004]“You have nothing to reproach yourself with. Crystalman has had eternity

[036-005]to practice his cunning in, so it’s no wonder if a man can’t see

[036-006]straight, even with the best intentions. What have you decided to do?”

[036-007]“The drumming seems to be moving away. Will you follow it, Maskull?”

[036-008]“Yes.”

[036-009]“But where will it take us?”

[036-010]“Perhaps out of Threal altogether.”

[036-011]“It sounds to me more real than reality,” said Corpang. “Tell me, who is

[036-012]Surtur?”

[036-013]“Surtur’s world, or Muspel, we are told, is the original of which this

[036-014]world is a distorted copy. Crystalman is life, but Surtur is other than

[036-015]life.”

[036-016]“How do you know this?”

[036-017]“It has sprung together somehow—from inspiration, from experience, from

[036-018]conversation with the wise men of your planet. Every hour it grows truer

[036-019]for me and takes a more definite shape.”

[036-020]Corpang stood up squarely, facing the three Figures with a harsh,

[036-021]energetic countenance, stamped all over with resolution. “I believe you,

[036-022]Maskull. No better proof is required than that. Thire is not the

[036-023]highest; he is even in a certain sense the lowest. Nothing but the

[036-024]thoroughly false and base could stoop to such deceits.... I am coming

[036-025]with you—but don’t play the traitor. These signs may be for you, and not

[036-026]for me at all, and if you leave me—”

[036-027]“I make no promises. I don’t ask you to come with me. If you prefer to

[036-028]stay in your little world, or if you have any doubts about it, you had

[036-029]better not come.”

[036-030]“Don’t talk like that. I shall never forget your service to me... Let us

[036-031]make haste, or we shall lose the sound.”

[036-032]Corpang started off more eagerly than Maskull. They walked fast in the

[036-033]direction of the drumming. For upward of two miles the path went along

[036-034]the ledge without any change of level. The mysterious radiance gradually

[036-035]departed, and was replaced by the normal light of Threal. The rhythmical

[036-036]beats continued, but a very long way ahead—neither was able to diminish

[036-037]the distance.

[036-038]“What kind of man are you?” Corpang suddenly broke out.

[036-039]“In what respect?”

[036-040]“How do you come to be on such terms with the Invisible? How is it that

[036-041]I’ve never had this experience before I met you, in spite of my never-

[036-042]ending prayers and mortifications? In what way are you superior to me?”

[036-043]“To hear voices perhaps can’t be made a profession,” replied Maskull. “I

[036-044]have a simple and unoccupied mind—that may be why I sometimes hear

[036-045]things that up to the present you have not been able to.”

[036-046]Corpang darkened, and kept silent; and then Maskull saw through to his

[036-047]pride.

[036-048]The ledge presently began to rise. They were high above the platform on

[036-049]the opposite side of the gulf. The road then curved sharply to the

[036-050]right, and they passed over the abyss and the other ledge as by a

[036-051]bridge, coming out upon the top of the opposite cliffs. A new line of

[036-052]precipices immediately confronted them. They followed the drumming along

[036-053]the base of these heights, but as they were passing the mouth of a large

[036-054]cave the sound came from its recesses, and they turned their steps

[036-055]inward.

[036-056]“This leads to the outer world,” remarked Corpang. “I’ve occasionally

[036-057]been there by this passage.”

[036-058]“Then that’s where it is taking us, no doubt. I confess I shan’t be

[036-059]sorry to see sunlight once more.”

[036-060]“Can you find time to think of sunlight?” asked Corpang with a rough

[036-061]smile.

[036-062]“I love the sun, and perhaps I’m rather lacking in the spirit of a

[036-063]zealot.”

[036-064]“Yet, for all that, you may get there before me.”

[036-065]“Don’t be bitter,” said Maskull. “I’ll tell you another thing. Muspel

[036-066]can’t be willed, for the simple reason that Muspel does not concern the

[036-067]will. To will is a property of this world.”

[036-068]“Then what is your journey for?”

[036-069]“It’s one thing to walk to a destination, and to linger over the walk,

[036-070]and quite another to run there at top speed.”

[036-071]“Perhaps I’m not so easily deceived as you think,” said Corpang with

[036-072]another smile.

[036-073]The light persisted in the cave. The path narrowed and became a steep

[036-074]ascent. Then the angle became one of forty-five degrees, and they had to

[036-075]climb. The tunnel grew so confined that Maskull was reminded of the

[036-076]confined dreams of his childhood.

[036-077]Not long afterward, daylight appeared. They hastened to complete the

[036-078]last stage. Maskull rushed out first into the world of colours and, all

[036-079]dirty and bleeding from numerous scratches, stood blinking on a

[036-080]hillside, bathed in the brilliant late-afternoon sunshine. Corpang

[036-081]followed closely at his heels. He was obliged to shield his eyes with

[036-082]his hands for a few minutes, so unaccustomed was he to Branchspell’s

[036-083]blinding rays.

[036-084]“The drum beats have stopped!” he exclaimed suddenly.

[036-085]“You can’t expect music all the time,” answered Maskull dryly. “We

[036-086]mustn’t be luxurious.”

[036-087]“But now we have no guide. We’re no better off than before.”

[036-088]“Well, Tormance is a big place. But I have an infallible rule, Corpang.

[036-089]As I come from the south, I always go due north.”

[036-090]“That will take us to Lichstorm.”

[036-091]Maskull gazed at the fantastically piled rocks all around them. “I saw

[036-092]these rocks from Matterplay. The mountains look as far off now as they

[036-093]did then, and there’s not much of the day left. How far is Lichstorm

[036-094]from here?”

[036-095]Corpang looked away to the distant range. “I don’t know, but unless a

[036-096]miracle happens we shan’t get there tonight.”

[036-097]“I have a feeling,” said Maskull, “that we shall not only get there

[036-098]tonight, but that tonight will be the most important in my life.”

[036-099]And he sat down passively to rest.

[036-101]While Maskull sat, Corpang walked restlessly to and fro, swinging his

[036-102]arms. He had lost his staff. His face was inflamed with suppressed

[036-103]impatience, which accentuated its natural coarseness. At last he stopped

[036-104]short in front of Maskull and looked down at him. “What do you intend to

[036-105]do?”

[036-106]Maskull glanced up and idly waved his hand toward the distant mountains.

[036-107]“Since we can’t walk, we must wait.”

[036-108]“For what?”

[036-109]“I don’t know... How’s this, though? Those peaks have changed colour,

[036-110]from red to green.”

[036-111]“Yes, the lich wind is travelling this way.”

[036-112]“The lich wind?”

[036-113]“It’s the atmosphere of Lichstorm. It always clings to the mountains,

[036-114]but when the wind blows from the north it comes as far as Threal.”

[036-115]“It’s a sort of fog, then?”

[036-116]“A peculiar sort, for they say it excites the sexual passions.”

[036-117]“So we are to have lovemaking,” said Maskull, laughing.

[036-118]“Perhaps you won’t find it so joyous,” replied Corpang a little grimly.

[036-119]“But tell me—these peaks, how do they preserve their balance?”

[036-120]Corpang gazed at the distant, overhanging summits, which were fast

[036-121]fading into obscurity.

[036-122]“Passion keeps them from falling.”

[036-123]Maskull laughed again; he was feeling a strange disturbance of spirit.

[036-124]“What, the love of rock for rock?”

[036-125]“It is comical, but true.”

[036-126]“We’ll take a closer peep at them presently. Beyond the mountains is

[036-127]Barey, is it not?”

[036-128]“Yes.”

[036-129]“And then the Ocean. But what is the name of that Ocean?”

[036-130]“That is told only to those who die beside it.”

[036-131]“Is the secret so precious, Corpang?”

[036-132]Branchspell was nearing the horizon in the west; there were more than

[036-133]two hours of daylight remaining. The air all around them became murky.

[036-134]It was a thin mist, neither damp nor cold. The Lichstorm Range now

[036-135]appeared only as a blur on the sky. The air was electric and tingling,

[036-136]and was exciting in its effect. Maskull felt a sort of emotional

[036-137]inflammation, as though a very slight external cause would serve to

[036-138]overturn his self-control. Corpang stood silent with a mouth like iron.

[036-139]Maskull kept looking toward a high pile of rocks in the vicinity.

[036-140]“That seems to me a good watchtower. Perhaps we shall see something from

[036-141]the top.”

[036-142]Without waiting for his companion’s opinion, he began to scramble up the

[036-143]tor, and in a few minutes was standing on the summit. Corpang joined

[036-144]him.

[036-145]From their viewpoint they saw the whole countryside sloping down to the

[036-146]sea, which appeared as a mere flash of far-off, glittering water.

[036-147]Leaving all that, however, Maskull’s eyes immediately fastened

[036-148]themselves on a small, boat-shaped object, about two miles away, which

[036-149]was travelling rapidly toward them, suspended only a few feet in the

[036-150]air.

[036-151]“What do you make of that?” he asked in a tone of astonishment.

[036-152]Corpang shook his head and said nothing.

[036-153]Within two minutes the flying object, whatever it was, had diminished

[036-154]the distance between them by one half. It resembled a boat more and

[036-155]more, but its flight was erratic, rather than smooth; its nose was

[036-156]continually jerking upward and downward, and from side to side. Maskull

[036-157]now made out a man sitting in the stern, and what looked like a large

[036-158]dead animal lying amidships. As the aerial craft drew nearer, he

[036-159]observed a thick, blue haze underneath it, and a similar haze behind,

[036-160]but the front, facing them, was clear.

[036-161]“Here must be what we are waiting for, Corpang. But what on earth

[036-162]carries it?”

[036-163]He stroked his beard contemplatively, and then, fearing that they had

[036-164]not been seen, stepped onto the highest rock, bellowed loudly, and made

[036-165]wild motions with his arm. The flying-boat, which was only a few hundred

[036-166]yards distant, slightly altered its course, now heading toward them in a

[036-167]way that left no doubt that the steersman had detected their presence.

[036-168]The boat slackened speed until it was travelling no faster than a

[036-169]walking man, but the irregularity of its movements continued. It was

[036-170]shaped rather queerly. About twenty feet long, its straight sides

[036-171]tapered off from a flat bow, four feet broad, to a sharp-angled stern.

[036-172]The flat bottom was not above ten feet from the ground. It was undecked,

[036-173]and carried only one living occupant; the other object they had

[036-174]distinguished was really the carcass of an animal, of about the size of

[036-175]a large sheep. The blue haze trailing behind the boat appeared to

[036-176]emanate from the glittering point of a short upright pole fastened in

[036-177]the stern. When the craft was within a few feet of them, and they were

[036-178]looking down at it in wonder from above, the man removed this pole and

[036-179]covered the brightly shining tip with a cap. The forward motion then

[036-180]ceased altogether, and the boat began to drift hither and thither, but

[036-181]still it remained suspended in the air, while the haze underneath

[036-182]persisted. Finally the broad side came gently up against the pile of

[036-183]rocks on which they were standing. The steersman jumped ashore and

[036-184]immediately clambered up to meet them.

[036-185]Maskull offered him a hand, but he refused it disdainfully. He was a

[036-186]young man, of middle height. He wore a close-fitting fur garment. His

[036-187]limbs were quite ordinary, but his trunk was disproportionately long,

[036-188]and he had the biggest and deepest chest that Maskull had ever seen in a

[036-189]man. His hairless face was sharp, pointed, and ugly, with protruding

[036-190]teeth, and a spiteful, grinning expression. His eyes and brows sloped

[036-191]upward. On his forehead was an organ which looked as though it had been

[036-192]mutilated—it was a mere disagreeable stump of flesh. His hair was short

[036-193]and thin. Maskull could not name the colour of his skin, but it seemed

[036-194]to stand in the same relation to jale as green to red.

[036-195]Once up, the stranger stood for a minute or two, scrutinising the two

[036-196]companions through half-closed lids, all the time smiling insolently.

[036-197]Maskull was all eagerness to exchange words, but did not care to be the

[036-198]first to speak. Corpang stood moodily, a little in the background.

[036-199]“What men are you?” demanded the aerial navigator at last. His voice was

[036-200]extremely loud, and possessed a most unpleasant timbre. It sounded to

[036-201]Maskull like a large volume of air trying to force its way through a

[036-202]narrow orifice.
