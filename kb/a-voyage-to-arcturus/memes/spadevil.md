# Spadevil

## Role

Spadevil appears in Chapter 12 as a stern, imposing figure who transforms [[maskull|Maskull]]'s moral consciousness. By covering [[maskull|Maskull]]'s sorb (forehead organ), Spadevil causes "the new law" to flow into [[maskull|Maskull]]'s consciousness — a philosophy of pure duty that transcends both pleasure and pain.

## Appearances

Spadevil stands in [[maskull|Maskull]]'s path in the forest (Ch. 12): "A man stood, with crossed arms, right in his path" (012-005). He is associated with the sorb organ and the philosophy of duty as the highest moral principle.

## Philosophical Significance

Spadevil represents the Kantian dimension of Lindsay's philosophy — duty as the supreme moral law that transcends all personal inclination. His "new law" is an ethical absolute that transforms [[maskull|Maskull]]'s relationship to the world: previously things existed for him, now he exists for them.
