# Threal

## Location

Threal is a region in the far north of Tormance, lying beyond Barey and the Mornstab Pass. It is visible from elevated vantage points as a distant land, and is sought by Maskull as his final terrestrial destination before the novel's cosmic conclusion.

From a distance, Threal appears as a cluster of elevations — "these were probably Threal" [034-008] — behind which the landscape stretches toward the horizon. Krag, Maskull's mysterious guide, proposes that they "search for Threal" together [034-101], marking it as the culminating destination of their shared journey.

## Description

Threal is described as "a land of mystics" [029-065] — a region where the deepest spiritual truths are not taught but innate. Its inhabitants are said to "learn the mystery" of existence not through study or revelation, but as part of their natural development: "In Threal, where I was born and brought up, we learn the mystery" [029-101].

Earthrid, the death-musician of Swaylone's Island, is said to have come from Threal [015-027], though his art represents a perversion of whatever Threal's native wisdom might be. The connection between Threal and Earthrid suggests that even the purest spiritual origins can produce corrupted expressions.

Unlike other regions that Maskull passes through, Threal remains partially veiled — approached but perhaps never fully entered within the novel's narrative frame. This incompleteness is itself significant: the ultimate mystical destination can be sought but perhaps never fully attained within the confines of mortal journeying.

## Narrative Events

Maskull first learns of Threal from a character who tells him: "Threal comes next, as you go north. They say it's a land of mystics" [029-065]. The name and its reputation lodge in Maskull's mind as he continues his journey.

The origin of Earthrid in Threal adds a layer of complexity: if Threal is a land of mystics, why does one of its sons produce music that kills? This tension reflects Lindsay's recurring theme that spiritual truth is easily distorted when brought into contact with the world of action and expression [015-027].

In the novel's final terrestrial passages, Krag proposes: "Together we will search for Threal" [034-101]. Threal thus becomes the symbolic endpoint of the earthly quest — the last destination before the journey transcends the material plane entirely.

## Symbolic Significance

Threal represents the mystical endpoint in Lindsay's spiritual geography — a place where knowledge of ultimate reality is not earned through suffering or choice (as in Ifdawn or Sant) but is simply present from birth. In a novel structured around Maskull's acquisition of ever-deeper understanding, Threal is the counterpoint: a state where understanding is innate.

The name "Threal" — suggesting "the real" or perhaps a fusion of "three" and "real" — points toward the ultimate reality that Lindsay's philosophical system posits beyond the world of appearances. Threal is the real that lies beyond the illusions of Lusion Plain, the harsh truths of Ifdawn, and even the revelations of Sant.
