# Digrung

## Role

Digrung is a traveller encountered by [[maskull]] on the road to Poolingdred. He introduces himself in a "dry, manly, nervous voice: 'I am Digrung. I have arrived here from Matterplay'" [015]. His most striking feature is his changing colour, which reminds [[maskull|Maskull]] of [[joiwind]] — suggesting a shared nature or a capacity for emotional transparency that most beings on [[tormance|Tormance]] lack.

## In the Novel

Digrung is lost: "Perhaps you're going to Poolingdred, Digrung?" [[maskull|Maskull]] asks, and Digrung confirms he is — "if I can find my way out of this accursed country" [015]. His disorientation is not merely geographical but existential. He questions [[maskull|Maskull]] sharply about the woman following him: "Who is this woman, why are you following at her heels like a slave? She looks insane to me" [015].

Digrung's directness borders on aggression — [[maskull|Maskull]] asks, "Why do you make this unprovoked attack on me, Digrung?" [015] — but his questioning serves a narrative function. He is the stranger who sees clearly because he is not invested in [[maskull|Maskull]]'s drama. His changing colour and his resemblance to [[joiwind|Joiwind]] suggest that he belongs to the same order of being: naturally good, transparent, and unsettled by the moral complexities he witnesses.

## Philosophical Significance

Digrung represents the self in transition — the traveller who is between destinations, between identities. His changing colour literalizes the fluidity of identity that [[maskull|Maskull]] himself is undergoing through his organ transformations. Digrung is a mirror in which [[maskull|Maskull]] can see his own unsettled state reflected.

The connection to [[joiwind|Joiwind]] is significant: both represent a natural transparency of being that makes deception impossible. But where [[joiwind|Joiwind]] is settled and wise, Digrung is lost and nervous — the same essence, but not yet arrived.
