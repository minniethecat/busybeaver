# Branchspell

## Definition

Branchspell is the name given by the inhabitants of Tormance to their primary sun — the star that Earth astronomers call Arcturus. It is a white star whose light dominates the Tormance sky, providing heat and illumination of an intensity far beyond that of Earth's sun. Its rays are described as "insufferable" at their peak [008-051], and its presence governs the fundamental rhythms of life on the planet.

## In the Novel

Branchspell is introduced early in Maskull's journey on Tormance. When Joiwind first explains the world to him, she distinguishes Branchspell from Arcturus itself:

> [007-117] "Is Branchspell another name for Arcturus?"

The sun's character is established through Maskull's physical experience of it. Unlike the gentle, filtered light of Earth, Branchspell's rays are direct and overwhelming — the novel repeatedly notes the heat [009-138] and the way the light affects every part of one's being [009-010]. Maskull's journey is paced by Branchspell's daily cycle: it rises at dawn [012-176], blazes through the zenith at midday [025-006], declines through the afternoon [011-084], and sinks in the west at evening [012-063].

The sun's white light serves as a neutral backdrop against which the novel's invented colors — [[jale]] and [[ulfire]] — become visible through the new organs that grow on Maskull's body.

## Philosophical Meaning

Branchspell represents the ordinary light of material existence in Lindsay's allegorical system. It is the light by which the physical world of Tormance — Shaping's creation — is perceived. Its white, "insufferable" quality suggests the harshness of purely material existence, unmediated by higher perception.

In the novel's metaphysical hierarchy, Branchspell's light is intermediate. Below it is the twilight phenomenon of [[blodsombre]]; above it is the transformative blue radiance of [[Alppain]], the second sun whose light reveals deeper truths. The progression from Branchspell's white light through the new organs to Alppain's blue glow mirrors Maskull's spiritual evolution from material perception toward metaphysical vision.

Branchspell is also associated with pleasure and sensory life — it is the star under which Shaping's world of forms and desires flourishes. When characters speak of transcending the material world, they are implicitly turning away from what Branchspell illuminates.
