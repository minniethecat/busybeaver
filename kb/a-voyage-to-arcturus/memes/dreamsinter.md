# Dreamsinter

**Dreamsinter** is a mysterious, unnamed-at-first being encountered by [[maskull|Maskull]] in Chapter 24 of [[David Lindsay]]'s *A Voyage to Arcturus*. He appears suddenly, identifies himself as a servant of [[surtur|Surtur]], and leads [[maskull|Maskull]] through a musical-visionary experience before vanishing as abruptly as he came. His name — given only when asked — suggests one who enters and interprets the space between waking and dreaming.

## Role

Dreamsinter functions as a guide and initiator in [[maskull|Maskull]]'s journey. Unlike the philosophical interlocutors [[maskull|Maskull]] has encountered before ([[panawe|Panawe]], [[corpang|Corpang]]), Dreamsinter does not argue or explain — he *shows*. He offers [[maskull|Maskull]] a fruit to eat and leads him in pursuit of mysterious music, creating a sensory experience that transcends rational understanding. His declaration that he serves [[surtur|Surtur]] places him within the novel's central metaphysical conflict, aligning him with the transformative fire against the static material world of [[crystalman|Crystalman]].

## Appearances

Dreamsinter appears solely in Chapter 24. His entrance is abrupt and unexplained: [[maskull|Maskull]] simply encounters him, and "the name he gave was 'Dreamsinter'" (024-018). When [[maskull|Maskull]] asks whom he serves, Dreamsinter answers without hesitation: "[[surtur|Surtur]]" (024-020) — a rare direct identification of allegiance in a novel where such loyalties are usually ambiguous.

Dreamsinter immediately takes physical hold of [[maskull|Maskull]]: "Dreamsinter caught hold of him, bent down, and peered into his face" (024-024). He offers [[maskull|Maskull]] a fruit, saying "Eat this. Then we will chase the sound together" (024-029). He breaks the fruit with his hands and shares it. The two then pursue a mysterious music together — "[[maskull|Maskull]] and Dreamsinter put themselves in motion" (024-057) — in what becomes a visionary passage where "the music pulsated in his blood" (024-071).

As suddenly as he appeared, Dreamsinter vanishes. [[maskull|Maskull]] realizes "Dreamsinter had vanished" (024-094) and reflects that "Dreamsinter was one" — one of the beings aligned with [[surtur|Surtur]]'s purpose (024-097). The encounter ends with [[maskull|Maskull]] asking: "What did Dreamsinter mean by his 'Not you, but [[nightspore|Nightspore]]'?" (024-108) — a cryptic statement that points toward the novel's climactic revelation about the relationship between [[maskull|Maskull]] and [[nightspore|Nightspore]].

## Philosophical Significance

Dreamsinter represents the initiatory function in Lindsay's esoteric framework — the guide who does not teach through doctrine but through direct experience. The fruit, the music, and the chase are all elements of a visionary ordeal that bypasses rational analysis. His alignment with [[surtur|Surtur]] is significant: [[surtur|Surtur]]'s method is always transformation through fire and pain, and Dreamsinter's gentler approach (offering fruit, sharing music) suggests that [[surtur|Surtur]]'s way can also operate through beauty and wonder.

His cryptic final statement — "Not you, but [[nightspore|Nightspore]]" — is one of the novel's key foreshadowings of the [[maskull|Maskull]]-[[nightspore|Nightspore]] identity mystery. Dreamsinter perceives what [[maskull|Maskull]] cannot yet see about himself: that the seeker and the knower are not two separate beings.

## Relationships

Dreamsinter's only relationship is with [[maskull|Maskull]], whom he guides through a single intense visionary episode. He declares allegiance to [[surtur|Surtur]], making him one of the few characters in the novel who explicitly name their metaphysical loyalty. His cryptic reference to [[nightspore|Nightspore]] connects the encounter to the novel's central mystery — the relationship between [[maskull|Maskull]] and his Earth companion.
