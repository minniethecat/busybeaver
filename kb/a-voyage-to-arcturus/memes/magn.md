# Magn

## Definition

The magn is one of the new sensory organs that Maskull grows during his journey on Tormance. It enables direct emotional perception — the ability to feel the inner emotional states of other beings without verbal communication. Like all Tormantic organs, it represents an expansion of consciousness beyond ordinary human limitations.

## In the Novel

The magn first appears or is activated in the early chapters of Maskull's journey. In Chapter 9, Maskull's magn is described as touching Joiwind and drawing "from her person a stream of love and joy" (009-018). This demonstrates the organ's function: it creates direct emotional connection between beings, bypassing the barriers of language and individual isolation.

The magn is part of a suite of new organs that collectively transform Maskull's perception. Along with the poigns (which explore the "inward nature" of things) and the breve (which enables silent thought exchange), the magn represents a new modality of consciousness.

## Philosophical Meaning

The magn embodies Lindsay's theme of expanded perception as spiritual growth. It suggests that true understanding between beings requires more than intellectual communication — it requires direct emotional participation. The magn's ability to draw "a stream of love and joy" from another person represents the ideal of empathic connection that transcends ego boundaries.
