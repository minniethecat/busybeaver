# Section 39

[039-001]“But why this errand at all?” asked Corpang quietly. “Can’t you men

[039-002]exercise self-control till you have arrived out of danger?”

[039-003]Haunte fixed him with wild eyes. “No. The phantoms come trooping in on

[039-004]me already.”

[039-005]He sat down moodily, but the next minute was up again.

[039-006]“And I cannot wait.... the game is started.”

[039-007]Soon afterward, by silent consent, they began to walk the ledge, Haunte

[039-008]in front. It was narrow, ascending, and slippery, so that extreme

[039-009]caution was demanded. The way was lighted by the self-luminous snow and

[039-010]rocks.

[039-011]When they had covered about half a mile, Maskull, who went second of the

[039-012]party, staggered, caught the cliff, and finally sat down.

[039-013]“The drink works. My old sensations are returning, but worse.”

[039-014]Haunte turned back. “Then you are a doomed man.”

[039-015]Maskull, though fully conscious of his companions and situation,

[039-016]imagined that he was being oppressed by a black, shapeless, supernatural

[039-017]being, who was trying to clasp him. He was filled with horror, trembled

[039-018]violently, yet could not move a limb. Sweat tumbled off his face in

[039-019]great drops. The waking nightmare lasted a long time, but during that

[039-020]space it kept coming and going. At one moment the vision seemed on the

[039-021]point of departing; the next it almost took shape—which he knew would be

[039-022]his death. Suddenly it vanished altogether—he was free. A fresh spring

[039-023]breeze fanned his face; he heard the slow, solitary singing of a sweet

[039-024]bird; and it seemed to him as if a poem had shot together in his soul.

[039-025]Such flashing, heartbreaking joy he had never experienced before in all

[039-026]his life! Almost immediately that too vanished.

[039-027]Sitting up, he passed his hand across his eyes and swayed quietly, like

[039-028]one who has been visited by an angel.

[039-029]“Your colour changed to white,” said Corpang. “What happened?”

[039-030]“I passed through torture to love,” replied Maskull simply.

[039-031]He stood up. Haunte gazed at him sombrely. “Will you not describe that

[039-032]passage?”

[039-033]Maskull answered slowly and thoughtfully. “When I was in Matterplay, I

[039-034]saw heavy clouds discharge themselves and change to coloured, living

[039-035]animals. In the same way, my black, chaotic pangs just now seemed to

[039-036]consolidate themselves and spring together as a new sort of joy. The joy

[039-037]would not have been possible without the preliminary nightmare. It is

[039-038]not accidental; Nature intends it so. The truth has just flashed through

[039-039]my brain.... You men of Lichstorm don’t go far enough. You stop at the

[039-040]pangs, without realising that they are birth pangs.”

[039-041]“If this is true, you are a great pioneer,” muttered Haunte.

[039-042]“How does this sensation differ from common love?” interrogated Corpang.

[039-043]“This was all that love is, multiplied by wildness.”

[039-044]Corpang fingered his chin awhile. “The Lichstorm men, however, will

[039-045]never reach this stage, for they are too masculine.”

[039-046]Haunte turned pale. “Why should we alone suffer?”

[039-047]“Nature is freakish and cruel, and doesn’t act according to justice....

[039-048]“I’ll see,” muttered Haunte. “Perhaps I will.”

[039-049]“Have we far to go, to Sullenbode?” inquired Maskull.

[039-050]“No, her home’s under the hanging cap of Sarclash.”

[039-051]“What is to happen tonight?” Maskull spoke to himself, but Haunte

[039-052]answered him.

[039-053]“Don’t expect anything pleasant, in spite of what has just occurred. She

[039-054]is not a woman, but a mass of pure sex. Your passion will draw her out

[039-055]into human shape, but only for a moment. If the change were permanent,

[039-056]you would have endowed her with a soul.”

[039-057]“Perhaps the change might be made permanent.”

[039-058]“To do that, it is not enough to desire her; she must desire you as

[039-059]well. But why should she desire you?”

[039-060]“Nothing turns out as one expects,” said Maskull, shaking his head. “We

[039-061]had better get on again.”

[039-062]They resumed the journey. The ledge still rose, but, on turning a corner

[039-063]of the cliff, Haunte quitted it and began to climb a steep gully, which

[039-064]mounted directly to the upper heights. Here they were compelled to use

[039-065]both hands and feet. Maskull thought all the while of nothing but the

[039-066]overwhelming sweetness he had just experienced.

[039-067]The flat ground on top was dry and springy. There was no more snow, and

[039-068]bright plants appeared. Haunte turned sharply to the left.

[039-069]“This must be under the cap,” said Maskull.

[039-070]“It is; and within five minutes you will see Sullenbode.”

[039-071]When he spoke his words, Maskull’s lips surprised him by their tender

[039-072]sensitiveness. Their action against each other sent thrills throughout

[039-073]his body.

[039-074]The grass shone dimly. A huge tree, with glowing branches, came into

[039-075]sight. It bore a multitude of red fruit, like hanging lanterns, but no

[039-076]leaves. Underneath this tree Sullenbode was sitting. Her beautiful

[039-077]light—a mingling of jale and white—gleamed softly through the darkness.

[039-078]She sat erect, on crossed legs, asleep. She was clothed in a singular

[039-079]skin garment, which started as a cloak thrown over one shoulder, and

[039-080]ended as loose breeches terminating above the knees. Her forearms were

[039-081]lightly folded, and in one hand she held a half-eaten fruit.

[039-082]Maskull stood over her and looked down, deeply interested. He thought he

[039-083]had never seen anything half so feminine. Her flesh was almost melting

[039-084]in its softness. So undeveloped were the facial organs that they looked

[039-085]scarcely human; only the lips were full, pouting, and expressive. In

[039-086]their richness, these lips seemed like a splash of vivid will on a

[039-087]background of slumbering protoplasm. Her hair was undressed. Its colour

[039-088]could not be distinguished. It was long and tangled, and had been tucked

[039-089]into her garment behind, for convenience.

[039-090]Corpang looked calm and sullen, but both the others were visibly

[039-091]agitated. Maskull’s heart was hammering away under his chest. Haunte

[039-092]pulled him, and said, “My head feels as if it were being torn from my

[039-093]shoulders.”

[039-094]“What can that mean?”

[039-095]“Yet there’s a horrible joy in it,” added Haunte, with a sickly smile.

[039-096]He put his hand on the woman’s shoulder. She awoke softly, glanced up at

[039-097]them, smiled, and then resumed eating her fruit. Maskull did not imagine

[039-098]that she had intelligence enough to speak. Haunte suddenly dropped on

[039-099]his knees, and kissed her lips.

[039-100]She did not repulse him. During the continuance of the kiss, Maskull

[039-101]noticed with a shock that her face was altering. The features emerged

[039-102]from their indistinctness and became human, and almost powerful. The

[039-103]smile faded, a scowl took its place. She thrust Haunte away, rose to her

[039-104]feet, and stared beneath bent brows at the three men, each one in turn.

[039-105]Maskull came last; his face she studied for quite a long time, but

[039-106]nothing indicated what she thought.

[039-107]Meanwhile Haunte again approached her, staggering and grinning. She

[039-108]suffered him quietly; but the instant lips met lips the second time, he

[039-109]fell backward with a startled cry, as though he had come in contact with

[039-110]an electric wire. The back of his head struck the ground, and he lay

[039-111]there motionless.

[039-112]Corpang sprang forward to his assistance. But, when he saw what had

[039-113]happened, he left him where he was.

[039-114]“Maskull, come here quickly!”

[039-115]The light was perceptibly fading from Haunte’s skin, as Maskull bent

[039-116]over. The man was dead. His face was unrecognisable. The head had been

[039-117]split from the top downward into two halves, streaming with strange-

[039-118]coloured blood, as though it had received a terrible blow from an axe.

[039-119]“This couldn’t be from the fall,” said Maskull.

[039-120]“No, Sullenbode did it.”

[039-121]Maskull turned quickly to look at the woman. She had resumed her former

[039-122]attitude on the ground. The momentary intelligence had vanished from her

[039-123]face, and she was again smiling.

[039-124]Chapter 19. SULLENBODE

[039-125]Sullenbode’s naked skin glowed softly through the darkness, but the

[039-126]clothed part of her person was invisible. Maskull watched her senseless,

[039-127]smiling face, and shivered. Strange feelings ran through his body.

[039-128]Corpang spoke out of the night. “She looks like an evil spirit filled

[039-129]with deadliness.”

[039-130]“It was like deliberately kissing lightning.”

[039-131]“Haunte was insane with passion.”

[039-132]“So am I,” said Maskull quietly. “My body seems full of rocks, all

[039-133]grinding against one another.”

[039-134]“This is what I was afraid of.”

[039-135]“It appears I shall have to kiss her too.”

[039-136]Corpang pulled his arm. “Have you lost all manliness?”

[039-137]But Maskull impatiently shook himself free. He plucked nervously at his

[039-138]beard, and stared at Sullenbode. His lips kept twitching. After this had

[039-139]gone on for a few minutes, he stepped forward, bent over the woman, and

[039-140]lifted her bodily in his arms. Setting her upright against the rugged

[039-141]tree trunk, he kissed her.

[039-142]A cold, knifelike shock passed down his frame. He thought that it was

[039-143]death, and lost consciousness.

[039-144]When his sense returned, Sullenbode was holding him by the shoulder with

[039-145]one hand at arm’s length, searching his face with gloomy eyes. At first

[039-146]he failed to recognise her; it was not the woman he had kissed, but

[039-147]another. Then he gradually realised that her face was identical with

[039-148]that which Haunte’s action had called into existence. A great calmness

[039-149]came upon him; his bad sensations had disappeared.

[039-150]Sullenbode was transformed into a living soul. Her skin was firm, her

[039-151]features were strong, her eyes gleamed with the consciousness of power.

[039-152]She was tall and slight, but slow in all her gestures and movements. Her

[039-153]face was not beautiful. It was long, and palely lighted, while the mouth

[039-154]crossed the lower half like a gash of fire. The lips were as voluptuous

[039-155]as before. Her brows were heavy. There was nothing vulgar in her—she

[039-156]looked the kingliest of all women. She appeared not more than twenty-

[039-157]five.

[039-158]Growing tired, apparently, of his scrutiny, she pushed him a little way

[039-159]and allowed her arm to drop, at the same time curving her mouth into a

[039-160]long, bowlike smile. “Whom have I to thank for this gift of life?”

[039-161]Her voice was rich, slow, and odd. Maskull felt himself in a dream.

[039-162]“My name is Maskull.”

[039-163]She motioned to him to come a step nearer. “Listen, Maskull. Man after

[039-164]man has drawn me into the world, but they could not keep me there, for I

[039-165]did not wish it. But now you have drawn me into it for all time, for

[039-166]good or evil.”

[039-167]Maskull stretched a hand toward the now invisible corpse, and said

[039-168]quietly, “What have you to say about him?”

[039-169]“Who was it?”

[039-170]“Haunte.”

[039-171]“So that was Haunte. The news will travel far and wide. He was a famous

[039-172]man.”

[039-173]“It’s a horrible affair. I can’t think that you killed him

[039-174]deliberately.”

[039-175]“We women are endowed with terrible power, but it is our only

[039-176]protection. We do not want these visits; we loathe them.”

[039-177]“I might have died, too.”

[039-178]“You came together?”

[039-179]“There were three of us. Corpang still stands over there.”

[039-180]“I see a faintly glimmering form. What do you want of me, Corpang?”

[039-181]“Nothing.”

[039-182]“Then go away, and leave me with Maskull.”

[039-183]“No need, Corpang. I am coming with you.”

[039-184]“This is not that pleasure, then?” demanded the low, earnest voice, out

[039-185]of the darkness.

[039-186]“No, that pleasure has not returned.”

[039-187]Sullenbode gripped his arm hard. “What pleasure are you speaking of?”

[039-188]“A presentiment of love, which I felt not long ago.”

[039-189]“But what do you feel now?”

[039-190]“Calm and free.”

[039-191]Sullenbode’s face seemed like a pallid mask, hiding a slow, swelling sea

[039-192]of elemental passions. “I do not know how it will end, Maskull, but we

[039-193]will still keep together a little. Where are you going?”

[039-194]“To Adage,” said Corpang, stepping forward.

[039-195]“But why?”

[039-196]“We are following the steps of Lodd, who went there years ago, to find

[039-197]Muspel-light.”

[039-198]“What light is that?”

[039-199]“It’s the light of another world.”

[039-200]“The quest is grand. But cannot women see that light?”

[039-201]“On one condition,” said Corpang. “They must forget their sex. Womanhood

[039-202]and love belong to life, while Muspel is above life.”

[039-203]“I give you all other men,” said Sullenbode. “Maskull is mine.”

[039-204]“No. I am not here to help Maskull to a lover but to remind him of the

[039-205]existence of nobler things.”
