# Tydomin

## Role

Tydomin is the title character of Chapter 10, encountered by [[maskull]] and [[oceaxe]] in the Disscourn region. She is a figure of complex moral authority who forces [[maskull|Maskull]] to confront the consequences of his actions — specifically his killing of [[oceaxe|Oceaxe]] and, through her, the death of her lover. Tydomin embodies the novel's recurring theme that moral transformation requires the painful acknowledgment of guilt.

## In the Novel

[[maskull|Maskull]] first sees Tydomin when [[oceaxe|Oceaxe]], frightened, mutters her name and moves away from him [010-6417]. Tydomin's presence immediately exerts moral pressure: her words "ring in [[maskull|Maskull]]'s head like an alarm bell" [010-6435], signaling that she represents a conscience he has tried to suppress.

Tydomin confronts [[maskull|Maskull]] directly about [[oceaxe|Oceaxe]]'s death: "So you are guilty of two deaths" [010-6611], referring both to [[oceaxe|Oceaxe]] and to the lover [[maskull|Maskull]] killed earlier. She demands, "Who gave you the right to kill him?" [010-6619], forcing [[maskull|Maskull]] into an ethical accounting he has thus far avoided. Her judgment is not merely punitive — it is transformative. Through her, [[maskull|Maskull]] begins to understand that his journey across [[tormance|Tormance]] is not just a physical pilgrimage but a moral one.

The encounter with Tydomin takes place on the edge of a precipice — a fitting symbol for the moral brink [[maskull|Maskull]] stands upon. Tydomin's outstretched finger pointing over the edge [010-6547] marks the threshold between evasion and accountability.

## Philosophical Significance

Tydomin represents the necessity of guilt in moral growth. Unlike [[joiwind]], who embodies unconditional love, or [[spadevil]], who preaches impersonal duty, Tydomin operates through direct moral confrontation. She does not offer comfort but demands reckoning — a function that places her among the novel's most ethically serious figures.

In Lindsay's allegorical scheme, Tydomin corresponds to the stage of spiritual development where the seeker can no longer evade responsibility for his actions. She is the voice that says: you cannot move forward until you acknowledge what you have done.
