# Section 41

[041-001]“To me you seem like a lonely giant, searching for you know not what....

[041-002]The grandest that life holds.... You at least have no cause to look up

[041-003]to women.”

[041-004]“Thanks, Sullenbode!” he responded, with a troubled smile.

[041-005]“When Maskull passes, let people watch. Everyone is thrown out of your

[041-006]road. You go on, looking neither to right nor left.”

[041-007]“Take care that you are not thrown as well,” said Corpang gravely.

[041-008]“Maskull shall do with me whatever he pleases, old skull! And for

[041-009]whatever he does, I will thank him.... In place of a heart you have a

[041-010]bag of loose dust. Someone has described love to you. You have had it

[041-011]described to you. You have heard that it is a small, fearful, selfish

[041-012]joy. It is not that—it is wild, and scornful, and sportive, and

[041-013]bloody.... How should you know.”

[041-014]“Selfishness has far too many disguises.”

[041-015]“If a woman wills to give up all, what can there be selfish in that?”

[041-016]“Only do not deceive yourself. Act decisively, or fate will be too swift

[041-017]for you both.”

[041-018]Sullenbode studied him through her lashes. “Do you mean death—his death

[041-019]as well as mine?”

[041-020]“You go too far, Corpang,” said Maskull, turning a shade darker. “I

[041-021]don’t accept you as the arbiter of our fortunes.”

[041-022]“If honest counsel is disagreeable to you, let me go on ahead.”

[041-023]The woman detained him with her slow, light fingers. “I wish you to stay

[041-024]with us.”

[041-025]“Why?”

[041-026]“I think you may know what you are talking about. I don’t wish to bring

[041-027]harm to Maskull. Presently I’ll leave you.”

[041-028]“That will be best,” said Corpang.

[041-029]Maskull looked angry. “I shall decide—Sullenbode, whether you go on, or

[041-030]back, I stay with you. My mind is made up.”

[041-031]An expression of joyousness overspread her face, in spite of her efforts

[041-032]to conceal it. “Why do you scowl at me, Maskull?”

[041-033]He returned no answer, but continued walking onward with puckered brows.

[041-034]After a dozen paces or so, he halted abruptly. “Wait, Sullenbode!”

[041-035]The others came to a standstill. Corpang looked puzzled, but the woman

[041-036]smiled. Maskull, without a word, bent over and kissed her lips. Then he

[041-037]relinquished her body, and turned around to Corpang.

[041-038]“How do you, in your great wisdom, interpret that kiss?”

[041-039]“It requires no great wisdom to interpret kisses, Maskull.”

[041-040]“Hereafter, never dare to come between us. Sullenbode belongs to me.”

[041-041]“Then I say no more; but you are a fated man.”

[041-042]From that time forward he spoke not another word to either of the

[041-043]others.

[041-044]A heavy gleam appeared in the woman’s eyes. “Now things are changed,

[041-045]Maskull. Where are you taking me?”

[041-046]“Choose, you.”

[041-047]“The man I love must complete his journey. I won’t have it otherwise.

[041-048]You shall not stand lower than Corpang.”

[041-049]“Where you go, I will go.”

[041-050]“And I—as long as your love endures, I will accompany you—even to

[041-051]Adage.”

[041-052]“Do you doubt its lasting?”

[041-053]“I wish not to.... Now I will tell you what I refused to tell you

[041-054]before. The term of your love is the term of my life. When you love me

[041-055]no longer, I must die.”

[041-056]“And why?” asked Maskull slowly.

[041-057]“Yes, that’s the responsibility you incurred when you kissed me for the

[041-058]first time. I never meant to tell you.”

[041-059]“Do you mean that if I had gone on alone, you would have died?”

[041-060]“I have no other life but what you give me.”

[041-061]He gazed at her mournfully, without attempting to reply, and then slowly

[041-062]placed his arms around her body. During this embrace he turned very

[041-063]pale, but Sullenbode grew as white as chalk.

[041-064]A few minutes later the journey toward Adage was resumed.

[041-065]They had been walking for two hours. Teargeld was higher in the sky and

[041-066]nearer the south. They had descended many hundred feet, and the

[041-067]character of the ridge began to alter for the worse. The thin snow

[041-068]disappeared, and gave way to moist, boggy ground. It was all little

[041-069]grassy hillocks and marshes. They began to slip about and become

[041-070]draggled with mud. Conversation ceased; Sullenbode led the way, and the

[041-071]men followed in her tracks. The southern half of the landscape grew

[041-072]grander. The greenish light of the brilliant moon, shining on the

[041-073]multitude of snow-green peaks, caused it to appear like a spectral

[041-074]world. Their nearest neighbour towered high above them on the other side

[041-075]of the valley, due south, some five miles distant. It was a slender,

[041-076]inaccessible, dizzy spire of black rock, the angles of which were too

[041-077]steep to retain snow. A great upward-curving horn of rock sprang out

[041-078]from its topmost pinnacle. For a long time it constituted their cheif

[041-079]landmark.

[041-080]The whole ridge gradually became saturated with moisture. The surface

[041-081]soil was spongy, and rested on impermeable rock; it breathed in the damp

[041-082]mists by night, and breathed them out again by day, under Branchspell’s

[041-083]rays. The walking grew first unpleasant, then difficult, and finally

[041-084]dangerous. None of the party could distinguish firm ground from bog.

[041-085]Sullenbode sank up to her waist in a pit of slime; Maskull rescued her,

[041-086]but after this incident took the lead himself. Corpang was the next to

[041-087]meet with trouble. Exploring a new path for himself, he tumbled into

[041-088]liquid mud up to his shoulders, and narrowly escaped a filthy death.

[041-089]After Maskull had got him out, at great personal risk, they proceeded

[041-090]once more; but now the scramble changed from bad to worse. Each step had

[041-091]to be thoroughly tested before weight was put upon it, and even so the

[041-092]test frequently failed. All of them went in so often, that in the end

[041-093]they no longer resembled human beings, but walking pillars plastered

[041-094]from top to toe with black filth. The hardest work fell to Maskull. He

[041-095]not only had the exhausting task of beating the way, but was continually

[041-096]called upon to help his companions out of their difficulties. Without

[041-097]him they could not have got through.

[041-098]After a peculiarly evil patch, they paused to recruit their strength.

[041-099]Corpang’s breathing was difficult, Sullenbode was quiet, listless, and

[041-100]depressed.

[041-101]Maskull gazed at them doubtfully. “Does this continue?” he inquired.

[041-102]“No. I think,” replied the woman, “we can’t be far from the Mornstab

[041-103]Pass. After that we shall begin to climb again, and then the road will

[041-104]improve perhaps.”

[041-105]“Can you have been here before?”

[041-106]“Once I have been to the Pass, but it was not so bad then.”

[041-107]“You are tired out, Sullenbode.”

[041-108]“What of it?” she replied, smiling faintly. “When one has a terrible

[041-109]lover, one must pay the price.”

[041-110]“We cannot get there tonight, so let us stop at the first shelter we

[041-111]come to.”

[041-112]“I leave it to you.”

[041-113]He paced up and down, while the others sat. “Do you regret anything?” he

[041-114]demanded suddenly.

[041-115]“No, Maskull, nothing. I regret nothing.”

[041-116]“Your feelings are unchanged?”

[041-117]“Love can’t go back—it can only go on.”

[041-118]“Yes, eternally on. It is so.”

[041-119]“No, I don’t mean that. There is a climax, but when the climax has been

[041-120]reached, love if it still wants to ascend must turn to sacrifice.”

[041-121]“That’s a dreadful creed,” he said in a low voice, turning pale beneath

[041-122]his coating of mud.

[041-123]“Perhaps my nature is discordant.... I am tired. I don’t know what I

[041-124]feel.”

[041-125]In a few minutes they were on their feet again, and the journey

[041-126]recommenced. Within half an hour they had reached the Mornstab Pass.

[041-127]The ground here was drier; the broken land to the north served to drain

[041-128]off the moisture of the soil. Sullenbode led them to the northern edge

[041-129]of the ridge, to show them the nature of the country. The pass was

[041-130]nothing but a gigantic landslip on both sides of the ridge, where it was

[041-131]the lowest above the underlying land. A series of huge broken terraces

[041-132]of earth and rock descended toward Barey. They were overgrown with

[041-133]stunted vegetation. It was quite possible to get down to the lowlands

[041-134]that way, but rather difficult. On either side of the landslip, to east

[041-135]and west, the ridge came down in a long line of sheer, terrific cliffs.

[041-136]A low haze concealed Barey from view. Complete stillness was in the air,

[041-137]broken only by the distant thundering of an invisible waterfall.

[041-138]Maskull and Sullenbode sat down on a boulder, facing the open country.

[041-139]The moon was directly behind them, high up. It was almost as light as an

[041-140]Earth day.

[041-141]“Tonight is like life,” said Sullenbode.

[041-142]“How so?”

[041-143]“So lovely above and around us, so foul underfoot.”

[041-144]Maskull sighed. “Poor girl, you are unhappy.”

[041-145]“And you—are you happy?”

[041-146]He thought a while, and then replied—“No. No, I’m not happy. Love is not

[041-147]happiness.”

[041-148]“What is it, Maskull?”

[041-149]“Restlessness—unshed tears—thoughts too grand for our soul to think...”

[041-150]“Yes,” said Sullenbode.

[041-151]After a time she asked, “Why were we created, just to live for a few

[041-152]years and then disappear?”

[041-153]“We are told that we shall live again.”

[041-154]“Yes, Maskull?”

[041-155]“Perhaps in Muspel,” he added thoughtfully.

[041-156]“What kind of life will that be?”

[041-157]“Surely we shall meet again. Love is too wonderful and mysterious a

[041-158]thing to remain uncompleted.”

[041-159]She gave a slight shiver, and turned away from him. “This dream is

[041-160]untrue. Love is completed here.”

[041-161]“How can that be, when sooner or later it is brutally interrupted by

[041-162]Fate?”

[041-163]“It is completed by anguish.... Oh, why must it always be enjoyment for

[041-164]us? Can’t we suffer—can’t we go on suffering, forever and ever? Maskull,

[041-165]until love crushes our spirit, finally and without remedy, we don’t

[041-166]begin to feel ourselves.”

[041-167]Maskull gazed at her with a troubled expression. “Can the memory of love

[041-168]be worth more than its presence and reality?”

[041-169]“You don’t understand. Those pangs are more precious than all the rest

[041-170]beside.” She caught at him. “Oh, if you could only see inside my mind,

[041-171]Maskull! You would see strange things.... I can’t explain. It is all

[041-172]confused, even to myself.... This love is quite different from what I

[041-173]thought.”

[041-174]He sighed again. “Love is a strong drink. Perhaps it is too strong for

[041-175]human beings. And I think that it overturns our reason in different

[041-176]ways.”

[041-177]They remained sitting side by side, staring straight before them with

[041-178]unseeing eyes.

[041-179]“It doesn’t matter,” said Sullenbode at last, with a smile, getting up.

[041-180]“Soon it will be ended, one way or another. Come, let us be off!”

[041-181]Maskull too got up.

[041-182]“Where’s Corpang?” he asked listlessly.

[041-183]They both looked across the ridge in the direction of Adage. At the

[041-184]point where they stood it was nearly a mile wide. It sloped perceptibly

[041-185]toward the southern edge, giving all the earth the appearance of a heavy

[041-186]list. Toward the west the ground continued level for a thousand yards,

[041-187]but then a high, sloping, grassy hill went right across the ridge from

[041-188]side to side, like a vast billow on the verge of breaking. It shut out

[041-189]all further view beyond. The whole crest of this hill, from one end to

[041-190]the other, was crowned by a long row of enormous stone posts, shining

[041-191]brightly in the moonlight against a background of dark sky. There were

[041-192]about thirty in all, and they were placed at such regular intervals that

[041-193]there was little doubt that they had been set there by human hands. Some

[041-194]were perpendicular, but others dipped so much that an aspect of extreme

[041-195]antiquity was given to the entire colonnade. Corpang was seen climbing

[041-196]the hill, not far from the top.

[041-197]“He wishes to arrive,” said Maskull, watching the energetic ascent with

[041-198]a rather cynical smile.

[041-199]“The heavens won’t open for Corpang,” returned Sullenbode. “He need not

[041-200]be in such a hurry.... What do these pillars seem like to you?”

[041-201]“They might be the entrance to some mighty temple. Who can have planted

[041-202]them there?”

[041-203]She did not answer. They watched Corpang gain the summit of the hill,

[041-204]and disappear through the line of posts.
