# Section 13

[013-001]Before he bathed, he viewed the mountains of the Ifdawn Marest. In the

[013-002]morning sunlight they stood out pictorially. He guessed that they were

[013-003]from five to six thousand feet high. The lofty, irregular, castellated

[013-004]line seemed like the walls of a magic city. The cliffs fronting him were

[013-005]composed of gaudy rocks—vermilion, emerald, yellow, ulfire, and black.

[013-006]As he gazed at them, his heart began to beat like a slow, heavy drum,

[013-007]and he thrilled all over—indescribable hopes, aspirations, and emotions

[013-008]came over him. It was more than the conquest of a new world which he

[013-009]felt—it was something different....

[013-010]He bathed and drank, and as he was reclothing himself, Oceaxe strolled

[013-011]indolently up.

[013-012]He could now perceive the colour of her skin—it was a vivid, yet

[013-013]delicate mixture of carmine, white, and jale. The effect was startlingly

[013-014]unearthly. With these new colors she looked like a genuine

[013-015]representative of a strange planet. Her frame also had something curious

[013-016]about it. The curves were womanly, the bones were characteristically

[013-017]female—yet all seemed somehow to express a daring, masculine underlying

[013-018]will. The commanding eye on her forehead set the same puzzle in plainer

[013-019]language. Its bold, domineering egotism was shot with undergleams of sex

[013-020]and softness.

[013-021]She came to the river’s edge and reviewed him from top to toe. “Now you

[013-022]are built more like a man,” she said, in her lovely, lingering voice.

[013-023]“You see, the experiment was successful,” he answered, smiling gaily.

[013-024]Oceaxe continued looking him over. “Did some woman give you that

[013-025]ridiculous robe?”

[013-026]“A woman did give it to me”—dropping his smile—“but I saw nothing

[013-027]ridiculous in the gift at the time, and I don’t now.”

[013-028]“I think I’d look better in it.”

[013-029]As she drawled the words, she began stripping off the skin, which suited

[013-030]her form so well, and motioned to him to exchange garments. He obeyed,

[013-031]rather shamefacedly, for he realised that the proposed exchange was in

[013-032]fact more appropriate to his sex. He found the skin a freer dress.

[013-033]Oceaxe in her drapery appeared more dangerously feminine to him.

[013-034]“I don’t want you to receive gifts at all from other women,” she

[013-035]remarked slowly.

[013-036]“Why not? What can I be to you?”

[013-037]“I have been thinking about you during the night.” Her voice was

[013-038]retarded, scornful, viola-like. She sat down on the trunk of a fallen

[013-039]tree, and looked away.

[013-040]“In what way?”

[013-041]She returned no answer to his question, but began to pull off pieces of

[013-042]the bark.

[013-043]“Last night you were so contemptuous.”

[013-044]“Last night is not today. Do you always walk through the world with your

[013-045]head over your shoulder?”

[013-046]It was now Maskull’s turn to be silent.

[013-047]“Still, if you have male instincts, as I suppose you have, you can’t go

[013-048]on resisting me forever.”

[013-049]“But this is preposterous,” said Maskull, opening his eyes wide.

[013-050]“Granted that you are a beautiful woman—we can’t be quite so primeval.”

[013-051]Oceaxe sighed, and rose to her feet. “It doesn’t matter. I can wait.”

[013-052]“From that I gather that you intend to make the journey in my society. I

[013-053]have no objection—in fact I shall be glad—but only on condition that you

[013-054]drop this language.”

[013-055]“Yet you do think me beautiful?”

[013-056]“Why shouldn’t I think so, if it is the fact? I fail to see what that

[013-057]has to do with my feelings. Bring it to an end, Oceaxe. You will find

[013-058]plenty of men to admire—and love you.”

[013-059]At that she blazed up. “Does love pick and choose, you fool? Do you

[013-060]imagine I am so hard put to it that I have to hunt for lovers? Is not

[013-061]Crimtyphon waiting for me at this very moment?”

[013-062]“Very well. I am sorry to have hurt your feelings. Now carry the

[013-063]temptation no farther—for it is a temptation, where a lovely woman is

[013-064]concerned. I am not my own master.”

[013-065]“I’m not proposing anything so very hateful, am I? Why do you humiliate

[013-066]me so?”

[013-067]Maskull put his hands behind his back. “I repeat, I am not my own

[013-068]master.”

[013-069]“Then who is your master?”

[013-070]“Yesterday I saw Surtur, and from today I am serving him.”

[013-071]“Did you speak with him?” she asked curiously.

[013-072]“I did.”

[013-073]“Tell me what he said.”

[013-074]“No, I can’t—I won’t. But whatever he said, his beauty was more

[013-075]tormenting than yours, Oceaxe, and that’s why I can look at you in cold

[013-076]blood.”

[013-077]“Did Surtur forbid you to be a man?”

[013-078]Maskull frowned. “Is love such a manly sport, then? I should have

[013-079]thought it effeminate.”

[013-080]“It doesn’t matter. You won’t always be so boyish. But don’t try my

[013-081]patience too far.”

[013-082]“Let us talk about something else—and, above all, let us get on our

[013-083]road.”

[013-084]She suddenly broke into a laugh, so rich, sweet, and enchanting, that he

[013-085]grew half inflamed, and half wished to catch her body in his arms. “Oh,

[013-086]Maskull, Maskull—what a fool you are!”

[013-087]“In what way am I a fool?” he demanded, scowling—not at her words, but

[013-088]at his own weakness.

[013-089]“Isn’t the whole world the handiwork of innumerable pairs of lovers? And

[013-090]yet you think yourself above all that. You try to fly away from nature,

[013-091]but where will you find a hole to hide yourself in?”

[013-092]“Besides beauty, I now credit you with a second quality: persistence.”

[013-093]“Read me well, and then it is natural law that you’ll think twice and

[013-094]three times before throwing me away.... And now, before we go, we had

[013-095]better eat.”

[013-096]“Eat?” said Maskull thoughtfully.

[013-097]“Don’t you eat? Is food in the same category as love?”

[013-098]“What food is it?”

[013-099]“Fish from the river.”

[013-100]Maskull recollected his promise to Joiwind. At the same time, he felt

[013-101]hungry.

[013-102]“Is there nothing milder?”

[013-103]She pulled her mouth scornfully. “You came through Poolingdred, didn’t

[013-104]you? All the people there are the same. They think life is to be looked

[013-105]at, and not lived. Now that you are visiting Ifdawn, you will have to

[013-106]change your notions.”

[013-107]“Go catch your fish,” he returned, pulling down his brows.

[013-108]The broad, clear waters flowed past them with swelling undulations, from

[013-109]the direction of the mountains. Oceaxe knelt down on the bank, and

[013-110]peered into the depths. Presently her look became tense and

[013-111]concentrated; she dipped her hand in and pulled out some sort of little

[013-112]monster. It was more like a reptile than a fish, with its scaly plates

[013-113]and teeth. She threw it on the ground, and it started crawling about.

[013-114]Suddenly she darted all her will into her sorb. The creature leaped into

[013-115]the air, and fell down dead.

[013-116]She picked up a sharp-edged slate, and with it removed the scales and

[013-117]entrails. During this operation, her hands and garment became stained

[013-118]with the light scarlet blood.

[013-119]“Find the drude, Maskull,” she said, with a lazy smile. “You had it last

[013-120]night.”

[013-121]He searched for it. It was hard to locate, for its rays had grown dull

[013-122]and feeble in the sunlight, but at last he found it. Oceaxe placed it in

[013-123]the interior of the monster, and left the body lying on the ground.

[013-124]“While it’s cooking, I’ll wash some of this blood away, which frightens

[013-125]you so much. Have you never seen blood before?”

[013-126]Maskull gazed at her in perplexity. The old paradox came back—the

[013-127]contrasting sexual characteristics in her person. Her bold, masterful,

[013-128]masculine egotism of manner seemed quite incongruous with the

[013-129]fascinating and disturbing femininity of her voice. A startling idea

[013-130]flashed into his mind.

[013-131]“In your country I’m told there is an act of will called ‘absorbing.’

[013-132]What is that?”

[013-133]She held her red, dripping hands away from her draperies, and uttered a

[013-134]delicious, clashing laugh. “You think I am half a man?”

[013-135]“Answer my question.”

[013-136]“I’m a woman through and through, Maskull—to the marrowbone. But that’s

[013-137]not to say I have never absorbed males.”

[013-138]“And that means...”

[013-139]“New strings for my harp, Maskull. A wider range of passions, a stormier

[013-140]heart...”

[013-141]“For you, yes—But for them?...”

[013-142]“I don’t know. The victims don’t describe their experiences. Probably

[013-143]unhappiness of some sort—if they still know anything.”

[013-144]“This is a fearful business!” he exclaimed, regarding her gloomily. “One

[013-145]would think Ifdawn a land of devils.”

[013-146]Oceaxe gave a beautiful sneer as she took a step toward the river.

[013-147]“Better men than you—better in every sense of the word—are walking about

[013-148]with foreign wills inside them. You may be as moral as you like,

[013-149]Maskull, but the fact remains, animals were made to be eaten, and simple

[013-150]natures were made to be absorbed.”

[013-151]“And human rights count for nothing!”

[013-152]She had bent over the river’s edge, to wash her arms and hands, but

[013-153]glanced up over her shoulder to answer his remark. “They do count. But

[013-154]we only regard a man as human for just as long as he’s able to hold his

[013-155]own with others.”

[013-156]The flesh was soon cooked, and they breakfasted in silence. Maskull cast

[013-157]heavy, doubtful glances from time to time toward his companion. Whether

[013-158]it was due to the strange quality of the food, or to his long

[013-159]abstention, he did not know, but the meal tasted nauseous, and even

[013-160]cannibalistic. He ate little, and the moment he got up he felt defiled.

[013-161]“Let me bury this drude, where I can find it some other time,” said

[013-162]Oceaxe. “On the next occasion, though, I shall have no Maskull with me,

[013-163]to shock.... Now we have to take to the river.”

[013-164]They stepped off the land onto the water. It flowed against them with a

[013-165]sluggish current, but the opposition, instead of hindering them, had the

[013-166]contrary effect—it caused them to exert themselves, and they moved

[013-167]faster. They climbed the river in this way for several miles. The

[013-168]exercise gradually improved the circulation of Maskull’s blood, and he

[013-169]began to look at things in a far more cheerful way. The hot sunshine,

[013-170]the diminished wind, the marvellous cloud scenery, the quiet, crystal

[013-171]forests—all was soothing and delightful. They approached nearer and

[013-172]nearer to the gaily painted heights of Ifdawn.

[013-173]There was something enigmatic to him in those bright walls. He was

[013-174]attracted by them, yet felt a sort of awe. They looked real, but at the

[013-175]same time very supernatural. If one could see the portrait of a ghost,

[013-176]painted with a hard, firm outline, in substantial colors, the feelings

[013-177]produced by such a sight would be exactly similar to Maskull’s

[013-178]impressions as he studied the Ifdawn precipices.

[013-179]He broke the long silence. “Those mountains have most extraordinary

[013-180]shapes. All the lines are straight and perpendicular—no slopes or

[013-181]curves.”

[013-182]She walked backward on the water, in order to face him. “That’s typical

[013-183]of Ifdawn. Nature is all hammer blows with us. Nothing soft and

[013-184]gradual.”

[013-185]“I hear you, but I don’t understand you.”

[013-186]“All over the Marest you’ll find patches of ground plunging down or

[013-187]rushing up. Trees grow fast. Women and men don’t think twice before

[013-188]acting. One may call Ifdawn a place of quick decisions.”

[013-189]Maskull was impressed. “A fresh, wild, primitive land.”

[013-190]“How is it where you come from?” asked Oceaxe.

[013-191]“Oh, mine is a decrepit world, where nature takes a hundred years to

[013-192]move a foot of solid land. Men and animals go about in flocks.

[013-193]Originality is a lost habit.”

[013-194]“Are there women there?”

[013-195]“As with you, and not very differently formed.”

[013-196]“Do they love?”

[013-197]He laughed. “So much so that it has changed the dress, speech, and

[013-198]thoughts of the whole sex.”

[013-199]“Probably they are more beautiful than I?”

[013-200]“No, I think not,” said Maskull.

[013-201]There was another rather long silence, as they travelled unsteadily

[013-202]onward.

[013-203]“What is your business in Ifdawn?” demanded Oceaxe suddenly.

[013-204]He hesitated over his answer. “Can you grasp that it’s possible to have

[013-205]an aim right in front of one, so big that one can’t see it as a whole?”

[013-206]She stole a long, inquisitive look at him, “What sort of aim?”

[013-207]“A moral aim.”

[013-208]“Are you proposing to set the world right?”
