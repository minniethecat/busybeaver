# Gangnet

## Role

Gangnet is a native of the land of Barey whom [[maskull]] and [[krag]] encounter near the beginning of Chapter 43. He appears waiting for them at the edge of a forest, having anticipated their arrival since sunrise. Gangnet serves as a foil to [[krag|Krag]] — where [[krag|Krag]] is harsh, mocking, and unsparing, Gangnet is gentle, prescient, and emotionally attuned to others. His "man-woman" nature, as [[krag|Krag]] derisively calls it, marks him as a being who integrates what [[tormance|Tormance]]'s dualistic world typically keeps separate.

## Appearance

Gangnet is described as small, dark, and beardless, still in early manhood. He wears a dark blue, loosely flowing robe and a broad-brimmed slouch hat. His face bears no disfigurement from the special organs (breve, poigns, magn, sorb) characteristic of many [[tormance|Tormance]] inhabitants, making him one of the more recognisably human figures [[maskull|Maskull]] meets. His pale, earnest, and grave face is described as "remarkably pleasing."

> He was small, dark, and beardless, and was still in early manhood. He was clothed in a dark blue, loosely flowing robe, and wore a broad-brimmed slouch hat. His face, which was not disfigured by any special organs, was pale, earnest, and grave, yet somehow remarkably pleasing. [043-031]

Gangnet's voice is a "vibrating baritone" with "strangely womanish" modulations, and his smile is said to make his face "very handsome" [043-032]. When he arrives, his first act is to warmly grasp [[maskull|Maskull]]'s hand before any words are exchanged [043-030].

## Nature and Philosophy

Gangnet's defining philosophical position is his belief that beauty and affection justify existence. When [[krag|Krag]] challenges the freedom of the world, Gangnet responds with a quiet affirmation of [[maskull|Maskull]]'s autonomy:

> "Freedom from pain and ugliness should be every man's privilege. Maskull is quite within his rights." [043-038]

His tenderness toward [[maskull|Maskull]] is physical and emotional — he presses [[maskull|Maskull]]'s arm "affectionately" and speaks of him with evident care [043-041]. This tenderness extends to his view of the world itself. After learning of [[sullenbode]]'s tragic fate, Gangnet is moved to compassion:

> "Poor Sullenbode!" said Gangnet, sighing. [043-044]

And he draws from her existence a cosmological conclusion:

> "If Sullenbode can exist, the world cannot be a bad place." [043-045]

This is the opposite of [[krag|Krag]]'s cosmology. Where [[krag|Krag]] sees pain and ugliness as proof of [[crystalman]]'s corrupting influence, Gangnet sees beauty and love as evidence of the world's essential goodness.

## Relationship with Krag

Gangnet and [[krag|Krag]] recognise each other instantly and share a deep mutual antipathy:

> "Haven't you a greeting for me too — Gangnet?" asked Krag, thrusting his forbidding features almost into the other's face.
>
> "I know you, Krag. There are few places where you are welcome." [043-033]

[[krag|Krag]] calls Gangnet "you man-woman," a slur that captures the gendered dualism Gangnet embodies [043-034]. [[krag|Krag]] dismisses Gangnet's role as "the shadow" while claiming "the substance" for himself [043-035]. When Gangnet challenges [[krag|Krag]]'s peculiar theories, [[krag|Krag]] responds with open mockery — "So Gangnet thinks!" [043-048] — but Gangnet does not retaliate in kind.

## Farewell

Gangnet accompanies [[maskull|Maskull]] and [[krag|Krag]] for a time through Barey, leading the way through country [[maskull|Maskull]] does not know [043-039]. He presses [[maskull|Maskull]]'s arm affectionately and offers what comfort he can before [[maskull|Maskull]] continues on toward the Ocean. His parting is not recorded in detail; he fades from the narrative as [[maskull|Maskull]] presses deeper into the final stages of his journey.
