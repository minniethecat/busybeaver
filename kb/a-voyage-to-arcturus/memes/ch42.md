# Section 42

[042-001]Maskull turned again to Sullenbode. “Now we two are alone in a lonely

[042-002]world.”

[042-003]She regarded him steadily. “Our last night on this earth must be a grand

[042-004]one. I am ready to go on.”

[042-005]“I don’t think you are fit to go on. It will be better to go down the

[042-006]pass a little, and find shelter.”

[042-007]She half smiled. “We won’t study our poor bodies tonight. I mean you to

[042-008]go to Adage, Maskull.”

[042-009]“Then at all events let us rest first, for it must be a long, terrible

[042-010]climb, and who knows what hardships we shall meet?”

[042-011]She walked a step or two forward, half turned, and held out her hand to

[042-012]him. “Come, Maskull!”

[042-013]*****

[042-014]When they had covered half the distance that separated them from the

[042-015]foot of the hill, Maskull heard the drum taps. They came from behind the

[042-016]hill, and were loud, sharp, almost explosive. He glanced at Sullenbode,

[042-017]but she appeared to hear nothing. A minute later the whole sky behind

[042-018]and above the long chain of stone posts on the crest of the hill began

[042-019]to be illuminated by a strange radiance. The moonlight in that quarter

[042-020]faded; the posts stood out black on a background of fire. It was the

[042-021]light of Muspel. As the moments passed, it grew more and more vivid,

[042-022]peculiar, and awful. It was of no colour, and resembled nothing—it was

[042-023]supernatural and indescribable. Maskull’s spirit swelled. He stood fast,

[042-024]with expanded nostrils and terrible eyes.

[042-025]Sullenbode touched him lightly.

[042-026]“What do you see, Maskull?”

[042-027]“Muspel-light.”

[042-028]“I see nothing.”

[042-029]The light shot up, until Maskull scarcely knew where he stood. It burned

[042-030]with a fiercer and stranger glare than ever before. He forgot the

[042-031]existence of Sullenbode. The drum beats grew deafeningly loud. Each beat

[042-032]was like a rip of startling thunder, crashing through the sky and making

[042-033]the air tremble. Presently the crashes coalesced, and one continuous

[042-034]roar of thunder rocked the world. But the rhythm persisted—the four

[042-035]beats, with the third accented, still came pulsing through the

[042-036]atmosphere, only now against a background of thunder, and not of

[042-037]silence.

[042-038]Maskull’s heart beat wildly. His body was like a prison. He longed to

[042-039]throw it off, to spring up and become incorporated with the sublime

[042-040]universe which was beginning to unveil itself.

[042-041]Sullenbode suddenly enfolded him in her arms, and kissed

[042-042]him—passionately, again and again. He made no response; he was unaware

[042-043]of what she was doing. She unclasped him and, with bent head and

[042-044]streaming eyes, went noiselessly away. She started to go back toward the

[042-045]Mornstab Pass.

[042-046]A few minutes afterward the radiance began to fade. The thunder died

[042-047]down. The moonlight reappeared, the stone posts and the hillside were

[042-048]again bright. In a short time the supernatural light had entirely

[042-049]vanished, but the drum taps still sounded faintly, a muffled rhythm,

[042-050]from behind the hill. Maskull started violently, and stared around him

[042-051]like a suddenly awakened sleeper.

[042-052]He saw Sullenbode walking slowly away from him, a few hundred yards off.

[042-053]At that sight, death entered his heart. He ran after her, calling

[042-054]out.... She did not look around. When he had lessened the distance

[042-055]between them by a half, he saw her suddenly stumble and fall. She did

[042-056]not get up again, but lay motionless where she fell.

[042-057]He flew toward her, and bent over her body. His worst fears were

[042-058]realised. Life had departed.

[042-059]Beneath its coating of mud, her face bore the vulgar, ghastly Crystalman

[042-060]grin, but Maskull saw nothing of it. She had never appeared so beautiful

[042-061]to him as at that moment.

[042-062]*****

[042-063]He remained beside her for a long time, on his knees. He wept—but,

[042-064]between his fits of weeping, he raised his head from time to time, and

[042-065]listened to the distant drum beats.

[042-066]An hour passed—two hours. Teargeld was now in the south-west. Maskull

[042-067]lifted Sullenbode’s dead body on to his shoulders, and started to walk

[042-068]toward the Pass. He cared no more for Muspel. He intended to look for

[042-069]water in which to wash the corpse of his beloved, and earth in which to

[042-070]bury her.

[042-071]When he had reached the boulder overlooking the landslip, on which they

[042-072]had sat together, he lowered his burden, and, placing the dead girl on

[042-073]the stone, seated himself beside her for a time, gazing over toward

[042-074]Barey.

[042-075]After that, he commenced his descent of the Mornstab Pass.

[042-076]Chapter 20. BAREY

[042-077]The day had already dawned, but it was not yet sunrise when Maskull

[042-078]awoke from his miserable sleep. He sat up and yawned feebly. The air was

[042-079]cool and sweet. Far away down the landslip a bird was singing; the song

[042-080]consisted of only two notes, but it was so plaintive and heartbreaking

[042-081]that he scarcely knew how to endure it.

[042-082]The eastern sky was a delicate green, crossed by a long, thin band of

[042-083]chocolate-coloured cloud near the horizon. The atmosphere was blue-

[042-084]tinted, mysterious, and hazy. Neither Sarclash nor Adage was visible.

[042-085]The saddle of the Pass was five hundred feet above him; he had descended

[042-086]that distance overnight. The landslip continued downward, like a huge

[042-087]flying staircase, to the upper slopes of Barey, which lay perhaps

[042-088]fifteen hundred feet beneath. The surface of the Pass was rough, and the

[042-089]angle was excessively steep, though not precipitous. It was above a mile

[042-090]across. On each side of it, east and west, the dark walls of the ridge

[042-091]descended sheer. At the point where the pass sprang outward they were

[042-092]two thousand feet from top to bottom, but as the ridge went upward, on

[042-093]the one hand toward Adage, on the other toward Sarclash, they attained

[042-094]almost unbelievable heights. Despite the great breadth and solidity of

[042-095]the pass, Maskull felt as though he were suspended in midair.

[042-096]The patch of broken, rich, brown soil observable not far away marked

[042-097]Sullenbode’s grave. He had interred her by the light of the moon, with a

[042-098]long, flat stone for a spade. A little lower down, the white steam of a

[042-099]hot spring was curling about in the twilight. From where he sat he was

[042-100]unable to see the pool into which the spring ultimately flowed, but it

[042-101]was in that pool that he had last night washed first of all the dead

[042-102]girl’s body, and then his own.

[042-103]He got up, yawned again, stretched himself, and looked around him dully.

[042-104]For a long time he eyed the grave. The half-darkness changed by

[042-105]imperceptible degrees to full day; the sun was about to appear. The sky

[042-106]was nearly cloudless. The whole wonderful extent of the mighty ridge

[042-107]behind him began to emerge from the morning mist... there was a part of

[042-108]Sarclash, and the ice-green crest of gigantic Adage itself, which he

[042-109]could only take in by throwing his head right back.

[042-110]He gazed at everything in weary apathy, like a lost soul. All his

[042-111]desires were gone forever; he wished to go nowhere, and to do nothing.

[042-112]He thought he would go to Barey.

[042-113]He went to the warm pool, to wash the sleep out of his eyes. Sitting

[042-114]beside it, watching the bubbles, was Krag.

[042-115]Maskull thought that he was dreaming. The man was clothed in a skin

[042-116]shirt and breeches. His face was stern, yellow, and ugly. He eyed

[042-117]Maskull without smiling or getting up.

[042-118]“Where in the devil’s name have you come from, Krag?”

[042-119]“The great point is, I am here.”

[042-120]“Where’s Nightspore?”

[042-121]“Not far away.”

[042-122]“It seems a hundred years since I saw you. Why did you two leave me in

[042-123]such a damnable fashion?”

[042-124]“You were strong enough to get through alone.”

[042-125]“So it turned out, but how were you to know?.... Anyway, you’ve timed it

[042-126]well. It seems I am to die today.”

[042-127]Krag scowled. “You will die this morning.”

[042-128]“If I am to, I shall. But where have you heard it from?”

[042-129]“You are ripe for it. You have run through the gamut. What else is there

[042-130]to live for?”

[042-131]“Nothing,” said Maskull, uttering a short laugh. “I am quite ready. I

[042-132]have failed in everything. I only wondered how you knew.... So now

[042-133]you’ve come to rejoin me. Where are we going?”

[042-134]“Through Barey.”

[042-135]“And what about Nightspore?”

[042-136]Krag jumped to his feet with clumsy agility. “We won’t wait for him.

[042-137]He’ll be there as soon as we shall.”

[042-138]“Where?”

[042-139]“At our destination.... Come! The sun’s rising.”

[042-140]*****

[042-141]As they started clambering down the pass side by side, Branchspell, huge

[042-142]and white, leaped fiercely into the sky. All the delicacy of the dawn

[042-143]vanished, and another vulgar day began. They passed some trees and

[042-144]plants, the leaves of which were all curled up, as if in sleep.

[042-145]Maskull pointed them out to his companion.

[042-146]“How is it the sunshine doesn’t open them?”

[042-147]“Branchspell is a second night to them. Their day is Alppain.”

[042-148]“How long will it be before that sun rises?”

[042-149]“Some time yet.”

[042-150]“Shall I live to see it, do you think?”

[042-151]“Do you want to?”

[042-152]“At one time I did, but now I’m indifferent.”

[042-153]“Keep in that humour, and you’ll do well. Once for all, there’s nothing

[042-154]worth seeing on Tormance.”

[042-155]After a few minutes Maskull said, “Why did we come here, then?”

[042-156]“To follow Surtur.”

[042-157]“True. But where is he?”

[042-158]“Closer at hand than you think, perhaps.”

[042-159]“Do you know that he is regarded as a god here, Krag?... There is

[042-160]supernatural fire, too, which I have been led to believe is somehow

[042-161]connected with him.... Why do you keep up the mystery? Who and what is

[042-162]Surtur?”

[042-163]“Don’t disturb yourself about that. You will never know.”

[042-164]“Do you know?”

[042-165]“I know,” snarled Krag.

[042-166]“The devil here is called Krag,” went on Maskull, peering into his face.

[042-167]“As long as pleasure is worshiped, Krag will always be the devil.”

[042-168]“Here we are, talking face to face, two men together.... What am I to

[042-169]believe of you?”

[042-170]“Believe your senses. The real devil is Crystalman.”

[042-171]They continued descending the landslip. The sun’s rays had grown

[042-172]insufferably hot. In front of them, down below in the far distance,

[042-173]Maskull saw water and land intermingled. It appeared that they were

[042-174]travelling toward a lake district.

[042-175]“What have you and Nightspore been doing during the last four days,

[042-176]Krag? What happened to the torpedo?”

[042-177]“You’re just about on the same mental level as a man who sees a brand-

[042-178]new palace, and asks what has become of the scaffolding.”

[042-179]“What palace have you been building, then?”

[042-180]“We have not been idle,” said Krag. “While you have been murdering and

[042-181]lovemaking, we have had our work.”

[042-182]“And how have you been made acquainted with my actions?”

[042-183]“Oh, you’re an open book. Now you’ve got a mortal heart wound on account

[042-184]of a woman you knew for six hours.”

[042-185]Maskull turned pale. “Sneer away, Krag! If you lived with a woman for

[042-186]six hundred years and saw her die, that would never touch your leather

[042-187]heart. You haven’t even the feelings of an insect.”

[042-188]“Behold the child defending its toys!” said Krag, grinning faintly.

[042-189]Maskull stopped short. “What do you want with me, and why did you bring

[042-190]me here?”

[042-191]“It’s no use stopping, even for the sake of theatrical effect,” said

[042-192]Krag, pulling him into motion again. “The distance has got to be

[042-193]covered, however often we pull up.”

[042-194]When he touched him, Maskull felt a terrible shooting pain through his

[042-195]heart.

[042-196]“I can’t go on regarding you as a man, Krag. You’re something more than

[042-197]a man—whether good or evil, I can’t say.”

[042-198]Krag looked yellow and formidable. He did not reply to Maskull’s remark,

[042-199]but after a pause said, “So you’ve been trying to find Surtur on your

[042-200]own account, during the intervals between killing and fondling?”

[042-201]“What was that drumming?” demanded Maskull.

[042-202]“You needn’t look so important. We know you had your ear to the keyhole.

[042-203]But you could join the assembly, the music was not playing for you, my

[042-204]friend.”
