# Section 24

[024-001]The gloom of the forest settled down upon his spirits. He felt

[024-002]despondent, tired, and savage. He had not heard the drum beats for some

[024-003]while, and was half inclined to discontinue the pursuit.

[024-004]Passing around a great, columnar tree trunk, he almost stumbled against

[024-005]a man who was standing on the farther side. He was leaning against the

[024-006]trunk with one hand, in an attitude of repose. His other hand was

[024-007]resting on a staff. Maskull stopped short and stared at him.

[024-008]He was nearly naked, and of gigantic build. He over-topped Maskull by a

[024-009]head. His face and body were faintly phosphorescent. His eyes—three in

[024-010]number—were pale green and luminous, shining like lamps. His skin was

[024-011]hairless, but the hair of his head was piled up in thick, black coils,

[024-012]and fastened like a woman’s. His features were absolutely tranquil, but

[024-013]a terrible, quiet energy seemed to lie just underneath the surface.

[024-014]Maskull addressed him. “Did the drumming come from you?”

[024-015]The man shook his head.

[024-016]“What is your name?”

[024-017]He replied in a strange, strained, twisted voice. Maskull gathered that

[024-018]the name he gave was “Dreamsinter.”

[024-019]“What is that drumming?”

[024-020]“Surtur,” said Dreamsinter.

[024-021]“Is it advisable for me to follow it?”

[024-022]“Why?”

[024-023]“Perhaps he intends me to. He brought me here from Earth.”

[024-024]Dreamsinter caught hold of him, bent down, and peered into his face.

[024-025]“Not you, but Nightspore.”

[024-026]This was the first time that Maskull had heard Nightspore’s name since

[024-027]his arrival on the planet. He was so astonished that he could frame no

[024-028]more questions.

[024-029]“Eat this,” said Dreamsinter. “Then we will chase the sound together.”

[024-030]He picked something up from the ground and handed it to Maskull. He

[024-031]could not see distinctly, but it felt like a hard, round nut, of the

[024-032]size of a fist.

[024-033]“I can’t crack it.”

[024-034]Dreamsinter took it between his hands, and broke it into pieces. Maskull

[024-035]then ate some of the pulpy interior, which was intensely disagreeable.

[024-036]“What am I doing in Tormance, then?” he asked.

[024-037]“You came to steal Muspel-fire, to give a deeper life to men—never

[024-038]doubting if your soul could endure that burning.”

[024-039]Maskull could hardly decipher the strangled words.

[024-040]“Muspel.... That’s the name I’ve been trying to remember ever since I

[024-041]awoke.”

[024-042]Dreamsinter suddenly turned his head sideways, and appeared to listen

[024-043]for something. He motioned with his hand to Maskull to keep quiet.

[024-044]“Is it the drumming?”

[024-045]“Hush! They come.”

[024-046]He was looking toward the upper forest. The now familiar drum rhythm was

[024-047]heard—this time accompanied by the tramp of marching feet.

[024-048]Maskull saw, marching through the trees and heading toward them, three

[024-049]men in single file separated from one another by only a yard or so. They

[024-050]were travelling down hill at a swift pace, and looked neither to left

[024-051]nor right. They were naked. Their figures were shining against the black

[024-052]background of the forest with a pale, supernatural light—green and

[024-053]ghostly. When they were abreast of him, about twenty feet off, he

[024-054]perceived who they were. The first man was himself—Maskull. The second

[024-055]was Krag. The third man was Nightspore. Their faces were grim and set.

[024-056]The source of the drumming was out of sight. The sound appeared to come

[024-057]from some point in front of them. Maskull and Dreamsinter put themselves

[024-058]in motion, to keep up with the swiftly moving marchers. At the same time

[024-059]a low, faint music began.

[024-060]Its rhythm stepped with the drum beats, but, unlike the latter, it did

[024-061]not seem to proceed from any particular quarter of the forest. It

[024-062]resembled the subjective music heard in dreams, which accompanies the

[024-063]dreamer everywhere, as a sort of natural atmosphere, rendering all his

[024-064]experiences emotional. It seemed to issue from an unearthly orchestra,

[024-065]and was strongly troubled, pathetic and tragic. Maskull marched, and

[024-066]listened; and as he listened, it grew louder and stormier. But the pulse

[024-067]of the drum interpenetrated all the other sounds, like the quiet beating

[024-068]of reality.

[024-069]His emotion deepened. He could not have said if minutes or hours were

[024-070]passing. The spectral procession marched on, a little way ahead, on a

[024-071]path parallel with his own and Dreamsinter’s. The music pulsated

[024-072]violently. Krag lifted his arm, and displayed a long, murderous-looking

[024-073]knife. He sprang forward and, raising it over the phantom Maskull’s

[024-074]back, stabbed him twice, leaving the knife in the wound the second time.

[024-075]Maskull threw up his arms, and fell down dead. Krag leaped into the

[024-076]forest and vanished from sight. Nightspore marched on alone, stern and

[024-077]unmoved.

[024-078]The music rose to crescendo. The whole dim, gigantic forest was roaring

[024-079]with sound. The tones came from all sides, from above, from the ground

[024-080]under their feet. It was so grandly passionate that Maskull felt his

[024-081]soul loosening from its bodily envelope.

[024-082]He continued to follow Nightspore. A strange brightness began to glow in

[024-083]front of them. It was not daylight, but a radiance such as he had never

[024-084]seen before, and such as he could not have imagined to be possible.

[024-085]Nightspore moved straight toward it. Maskull felt his chest bursting.

[024-086]The light flashed higher. The awful harmonies of the music followed hard

[024-087]one upon another, like the waves of a wild, magic ocean.... His body was

[024-088]incapable of enduring such shocks, and all of a sudden he tumbled over

[024-089]in a faint that resembled death.

[024-091]The morning slowly passed. Maskull made some convulsive movements, and

[024-092]opened his eyes. He sat up, blinking. All was night-like and silent in

[024-093]the forest. The strange light had gone, the music had ceased,

[024-094]Dreamsinter had vanished. He fingered his beard, clotted with Tydomin’s

[024-095]blood, and fell into a deep muse.

[024-096]“According to Panawe and Catice, this forest contains wise men. Perhaps

[024-097]Dreamsinter was one. Perhaps that vision I have just seen was a specimen

[024-098]of his wisdom. It looked almost like an answer to my question.... I

[024-099]ought not to have asked about myself, but about Surtur. Then I would

[024-100]have got a different answer. I might have learned something... I might

[024-101]have seen him.”

[024-102]He remained quiet and apathetic for a bit.

[024-103]“But I couldn’t face that awful glare,” he proceeded. “It was bursting

[024-104]my body. He warned me, too. And so Surtur does really exist, and my

[024-105]journey stands for something. But why am I here, and what can I do? Who

[024-106]is Surtur? Where is he to be found?”

[024-107]Something wild came into his eyes.

[024-108]“What did Dreamsinter mean by his ‘Not you, but Nightspore’? Am I a

[024-109]secondary character—is he regarded as important; and I as unimportant?

[024-110]Where is Nightspore, and what is he doing? Am I to wait for his time and

[024-111]pleasure—can I originate nothing?”

[024-112]He continued sitting up, with straight-extended legs.

[024-113]“I must make up my mind that this is a strange journey, and that the

[024-114]strangest things will happen in it. It’s no use making plans, for I

[024-115]can’t see two steps ahead—everything is unknown. But one thing’s

[024-116]evident: nothing but the wildest audacity will carry me through, and I

[024-117]must sacrifice everything else to that. And therefore if Surtur shows

[024-118]himself again, I shall go forward to meet him, even if it means death.”

[024-119]Through the black, quiet aisles of the forest the drum beats came again.

[024-120]The sound was a long way off and very faint. It was like the last

[024-121]mutterings of thunder after a heavy storm. Maskull listened, without

[024-122]getting up. The drumming faded into silence, and did not return.

[024-123]He smiled queerly, and said aloud, “Thanks, Surtur! I accept the omen.”

[024-124]When he was about to get up, he found that the shrivelled skin that had

[024-125]been his third arm was flapping disconcertingly with every movement of

[024-126]his body. He made perforations in it all around, as close to his chest

[024-127]as possible, with the fingernails of both hands; then he carefully

[024-128]twisted it off. In that world of rapid growth and ungrowth he judged

[024-129]that the stump would soon disappear. After that, he rose and peered into

[024-130]the darkness.

[024-131]The forest at that point sloped rather steeply and, without thinking

[024-132]twice about it, he took the downhill direction, never doubting it would

[024-133]bring him somewhere. As soon as he started walking, his temper became

[024-134]gloomy and morose—he was shaken, tired, dirty, and languid with hunger;

[024-135]moreover, he realised that the walk was not going to be a short one. Be

[024-136]that as it may, he determined to sit down no more until the whole dismal

[024-137]forest was at his back.

[024-138]One after another the shadowy, houselike trees were observed, avoided,

[024-139]and passed. Far overhead the little patch of glowing sky was still

[024-140]always visible; otherwise he had no clue to the time of day. He

[024-141]continued tramping sullenly down the slope for many damp, slippery

[024-142]miles—in some places through bogs. When, presently, the twilight seemed

[024-143]to thin, he guessed that the open world was not far away. The forest

[024-144]grew more palpable and grey, and now he saw its majesty better. The tree

[024-145]trunks were like round towers, and so wide were the intervals that they

[024-146]resembled natural amphitheatres. He could not make out the colour of the

[024-147]bark. Everything he saw amazed him, but his admiration was of the

[024-148]growling, grudging kind. The difference in light between the forest

[024-149]behind him and the forest ahead became so marked that he could no longer

[024-150]doubt that he was on the point of coming out.

[024-151]Real light was in front of him; looking back, he found he had a shadow.

[024-152]The trunks acquired a reddish tint. He quickened his pace. As the

[024-153]minutes went by, the bright patch ahead grew luminous and vivid; it had

[024-154]a tinge of blue. He also imagined that he heard the sound of surf.

[024-155]All that part of the forest toward which he was moving became rich with

[024-156]colour. The boles of the trees were of a deep, dark red; their leaves,

[024-157]high above his head, were ulfire-hued; the dead leaves on the ground

[024-158]were of a colour he could not name. At the same time he discovered the

[024-159]use of his third eye. By adding a third angle to his sight, every object

[024-160]he looked at stood out in greater relief. The world looked less

[024-161]flat—more realistic and significant. He had a stronger attraction toward

[024-162]his surroundings; he seemed somehow to lose his egotism, and to become

[024-163]free and thoughtful.

[024-164]Now through the last trees he saw full daylight. Less than half a mile

[024-165]separated him from the border of the forest, and, eager to discover what

[024-166]lay beyond, he broke into a run. He heard the surf louder. It was a

[024-167]peculiar hissing sound that could proceed only from water, yet was

[024-168]unlike the sea. Almost immediately he came within sight of an enormous

[024-169]horizon of dancing waves, which he knew must be the Sinking Sea. He fell

[024-170]back into a quick walk, continuing to stare hard. The wind that met him

[024-171]was hot, fresh and sweet.

[024-172]When he arrived at the final fringe of forest, which joined the wide

[024-173]sands of the shore without any change of level, he leaned with his back

[024-174]to a great tree and gazed his fill, motionless, at what lay in front of

[024-175]him. The sands continued east and west in a straight line, broken only

[024-176]here and there by a few creeks. They were of a brilliant orange colour,

[024-177]but there were patches of violet. The forest appeared to stand sentinel

[024-178]over the shore for its entire length. Everything else was sea and sky—he

[024-179]had never seen so much water. The semicircle of the skyline was so vast

[024-180]that he might have imagined himself on a flat world, with a range of

[024-181]vision determined only by the power of his eye. The sea was unlike any

[024-182]sea on Earth. It resembled an immense liquid opal. On a body colour of

[024-183]rich, magnificent emerald-green, flashes of red, yellow, and blue were

[024-184]everywhere shooting up and vanishing. The wave motion was extraordinary.

[024-185]Pinnacles of water were slowly formed until they attained a height of

[024-186]perhaps ten or twenty feet, when they would suddenly sink downward and

[024-187]outward, creating in their descent a series of concentric rings for long

[024-188]distances around them. Quickly moving currents, like rivers in the sea,

[024-189]could be seen, racing away from land; they were of a darker green and

[024-190]bore no pinnacles. Where the sea met the shore, the waves rushed over

[024-191]the sands far in, with almost sinister rapidity—accompanied by a weird,

[024-192]hissing, spitting sound, which was what Maskull had heard. The green

[024-193]tongues rolled in without foam.
