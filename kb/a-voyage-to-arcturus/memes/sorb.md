# Sorb

## Definition

The sorb is a forehead organ grown by Maskull that is associated with moral perception. When covered or activated, it enables the reception of the "new law" — Spadevil's philosophy of duty. Unlike the breve (thought exchange) or magn (emotional perception), the sorb has a specifically moral and ethical function.

## In the Novel

The sorb is introduced in connection with Spadevil's philosophy. In Chapter 12, Spadevil places an iron hand on Maskull's forehead, covering his sorb, and "the new law quietly flowed into his consciousness, like a smooth-running stream of clean water which had hitherto been dammed by his obstructive will. The law was duty" (012-004).

## Philosophical Meaning

The sorb represents the capacity for moral perception and the reception of ethical law. Unlike the other organs which expand sensory or communicative capacity, the sorb specifically opens the individual to duty — a transcendent moral order that exists beyond personal preference or pleasure. This aligns with Lindsay's theme of self-transcendence through submission to higher principles.
