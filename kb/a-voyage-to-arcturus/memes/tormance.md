# Tormance

**Tormance** is the inhabited planet orbiting the star Arcturus where virtually the entire narrative of [[David Lindsay]]'s *A Voyage to Arcturus* takes place. First named by [[krag|Krag]] at the [[starkness|Starkness]] observatory — "There you see Arcturus, and Tormance is its one inhabited planet" (003-052) — it is the created world of [[crystalman|Crystalman]], the domain of [[shaping|Shaping]]'s formative power, and the arena where [[maskull|Maskull]] undergoes his transformative pilgrimage.

## Location

Tormance orbits the star Arcturus (Alpha Boötis), visible from Earth as one of the brightest stars in the northern sky. Krag points it out to Maskull from the Starkness observatory: "There you see Arcturus, and Tormance is its one inhabited planet" (003-052). The journey from Earth to Tormance is accomplished through mysterious means — not technological spaceflight but something closer to metaphysical translation.

## Description

Tormance is a world of radical sensory and philosophical diversity. Its inhabitants possess organs unknown on Earth — the breve (telepathy), poigns (moral perception), magn (enlarged vision), sorb (taste-based knowledge) — and its landscape is shaped by forces both physical and metaphysical.

When Maskull first sees Tormance through a crystal window, it appears "like a continent in relief" and "like a landscape" (005-069), but Krag dismisses these visions as "illusions en route." Upon arrival, Maskull's first question — "Where am I? Is this Tormance?" (006-189) — underscores his disorientation in a world where perception itself is unreliable.

The planet's day-night cycle is marked by two suns: Branchspell, a white sun, and Alppain, a blue sun described as "the wonder of wonders in Tormance" whose light reveals dimensions invisible under ordinary illumination.

## Narrative Events

Tormance is the stage for Maskull's entire pilgrimage. He arrives disoriented in Chapter 6, grows his first organ (the breve) through contact with Joiwind in Chapters 7–8, and proceeds across the planet's varied regions. Each encounter — with Panawe, Crimtyphon, Oceaxe, Tydomin, Spadevil, Catice, Sant, Corpang, and many others — takes place in a distinct Tormantic landscape that reflects the philosophical character of its inhabitants.

Maskull marks time by Tormance days: "Maskull's second day on Tormance dawned" (012-176); "He awoke to his third day on Tormance" (023-126). By the novel's end, Maskull has traversed the planet and experienced the full spectrum of its philosophical possibilities.

## Philosophical Significance

Tormance is not merely a setting but a philosophical construct — the material world created by the Demiurge (Crystalman/Shaping) that simultaneously reveals and conceals the higher reality of Muspel. Its inhabitants' affection for Crystalman reflects humanity's attachment to material existence; its strange organs and landscapes represent modes of perception beyond ordinary human capacity.

The planet is explicitly identified as "Crystalman's world" (026-028), distinct from "Surtur's world." The journey across Tormance is thus a journey through the material creation toward what lies beyond it — a pilgrimage that can only be completed by passing through suffering, strangeness, and ultimately death.
