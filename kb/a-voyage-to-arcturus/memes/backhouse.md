# Backhouse

**Backhouse** is the professional medium in the opening séance scene of [[David Lindsay]]'s *A Voyage to Arcturus*. Hired by [[faull|Montague Faull]], he conducts the ritual that results in the appearance of a supernatural being — and the subsequent transportation of [[maskull|Maskull]] and [[nightspore|Nightspore]] to the planet [[tormance|Tormance]].

## Role

Backhouse serves as the narrative gatekeeper between the mundane world of Earth and the metaphysical reality of [[tormance|Tormance]]. As the medium, he is the technical operator of the séance — the figure who, through his professional expertise, makes the supernatural manifestation possible. Yet his role is entirely instrumental: he does not understand the true nature of what he facilitates, and he vanishes from the narrative once the transition occurs. He embodies the paradox of the technician who opens a door without comprehending what lies beyond it.

## Appearances

Backhouse appears exclusively in [[chapter/index|Chapters 1 and 2]], the framing séance sequence set on Earth. He is introduced as "a fast-looking, shady individual" with a "foxy, disagreeable face" (001-026), in contrast to the respectable businessmen who constitute the rest of the gathering. His professional demeanor is cold and detached — when [[faull|Faull]] describes the poetic environment of Hampstead, Backhouse responds that he "will be delighted" with palpable insincerity (001-072).

During the séance preparations, Backhouse is methodical and defensive about his craft. He insists on "full light" and "every opportunity for examination" (001-120), positioning himself as a transparent professional rather than a charlatan — though his shifty appearance undermines this self-presentation. Once the supernatural visitor appears, Backhouse is as awestruck as the others: "Backhouse also stood up, and stared hard at the supernatural youth" (002-030). He enters "on his task" as the medium (002-066) but is ultimately a bystander to the cosmic drama that engulfs [[maskull|Maskull]] and [[nightspore|Nightspore]].

## Philosophical Significance

Backhouse represents the limitations of technical expertise in Lindsay's metaphysical framework. He is competent at the mechanics of spirit communication but has no insight into the nature of the forces he channels. This makes him a foil to both [[maskull|Maskull]] (the naïve seeker) and [[nightspore|Nightspore]] (the knowing initiate): Backhouse knows *how* but not *what*, while [[maskull|Maskull]] knows neither and [[nightspore|Nightspore]] knows both.

His characterization as "shady" and "foxy" also introduces Lindsay's critique of professional spiritualism — the medium is a paid technician, and the entire séance is framed as a wealthy man's entertainment. The genuine cosmic event that occurs is indifferent to the commercial transaction that summoned it.

## Relationships

Backhouse's professional relationship with [[faull|Montague Faull]] is that of hired specialist to patron — [[faull|Faull]] pays, Backhouse performs. With [[trent|Mrs. Trent]], he shares the role of séance facilitator, though their domains are distinct (she provides the venue and social connections; he provides the spiritualist technique). His interactions with [[jameson|Mrs. Jameson]] and the other attendees are coolly professional. He has no direct interaction with [[maskull|Maskull]] or [[nightspore|Nightspore]], who arrive as observers and become the séance's true subjects.
