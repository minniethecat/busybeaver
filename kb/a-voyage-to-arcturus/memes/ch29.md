# Section 29

[029-001]“You may call it music,” remarked Maskull thoughtfully, “but to me it

[029-002]bears a closer resemblance to actual life.”

[029-003]“If Shaping’s plans had gone straight, life would have been like that

[029-004]other sort of music. He who seeks can find traces of that intention in

[029-005]the world of nature. But as it has turned out, real life resembles my

[029-006]music and mine is the true music.”

[029-007]“Shall we see living shapes?”

[029-008]“I don’t know what my mood will be,” returned Earthrid. “But when I have

[029-009]finished, you shall adventure your tune, and produce whatever shapes you

[029-010]please—unless, indeed, the tune is out of your own big body.”

[029-011]“The shocks you are preparing may kill us,” said Gleameil, in a low,

[029-012]taut voice, “but we shall die, seeing beauty.”

[029-013]Earthrid looked at her with a dignified expression.

[029-014]“Neither you, nor any other person, can endure the thoughts which I put

[029-015]into my music. Still, you must have it your own way. It needed a woman

[029-016]to call it ‘beauty.’ But if this is beauty, what is ugliness?”

[029-017]“That I can tell you, Master,” replied Gleameil, smiling at him.

[029-018]“Ugliness is old, stale life, while yours every night issues fresh from

[029-019]the womb of nature.”

[029-020]Earthrid stared at her, without response. “Teargeld is rising,” he said

[029-021]at last. “And now you shall see—though not for long.”

[029-022]As the words left his mouth, the full moon peeped over the hills in the

[029-023]dark eastern sky. They watched it in silence, and soon it was wholly up.

[029-024]It was larger than the moon of Earth, and seemed nearer. Its shadowy

[029-025]parts stood out in just as strong relief, but somehow it did not give

[029-026]Maskull the impression of being a dead world. Branchspell shone on the

[029-027]whole of it, but Alppain only on a part. The broad crescent that

[029-028]reflected Branchspell’s rays alone was white and brilliant; but the part

[029-029]that was illuminated by both suns shone with a greenish radiance that

[029-030]had almost solar power, and yet was cold and cheerless. On gazing at

[029-031]that combined light, he felt the same sense of disintegration that the

[029-032]afterglow of Alppain had always caused in him; but now the feeling was

[029-033]not physical, but merely aesthetic. The moon did not appear romantic to

[029-034]him, but disturbing and mystical.

[029-035]Earthrid rose, and stood quietly for a minute. In the bright moonlight,

[029-036]his face seemed to have undergone a change. It lost its loose, weak,

[029-037]disagreeable look, and acquired a sort of crafty grandeur. He clapped

[029-038]his hands together meditatively two or three times, and walked up and

[029-039]down. The others stood together, watching him.

[029-040]Then he sat down by the side of the lake, and, leaning on his side,

[029-041]placed his right hand, open palm downward, on the ground, at the same

[029-042]time stretching out his right leg, so that the foot was in contact with

[029-043]the water.

[029-044]While Maskull was in the act of staring at him and at the lake, he felt

[029-045]a stabbing sensation right through his heart, as though he had been

[029-046]pierced by a rapier. He barely recovered himself from falling, and as he

[029-047]did so he saw that a spout had formed on the water, and was now

[029-048]subsiding again. The next moment he was knocked down by a violent blow

[029-049]in the mouth, delivered by an invisible hand. He picked himself up; and

[029-050]observed that a second spout had formed. No sooner was he on his legs,

[029-051]than a hideous pain hammered away inside his brain, as if caused by a

[029-052]malignant tumour. In his agony, he stumbled and fell again; this time on

[029-053]the arm Krag had wounded. All his other mishaps were forgotten in this

[029-054]one, which half stunned him. It lasted only a moment, and then sudden

[029-055]relief came, and he found that Earthrid’s rough music had lost its power

[029-056]over him.

[029-057]He saw him still stretched in the same position. Spouts were coming

[029-058]thick and fast on the lake, which was full of lively motion. But

[029-059]Gleameil was not on her legs. She was lying on the ground, in a heap,

[029-060]without moving. Her attitude was ugly, and he guessed she was dead. When

[029-061]he reached her, he discovered that she was dead. In what state of mind

[029-062]she had died, he did not know, for her face wore the vulgar Crystalman

[029-063]grin. The whole tragedy had not lasted five minutes.

[029-064]He went over to Earthrid and dragged him forcibly away from his playing.

[029-065]“You have been as good as your word, musician,” he said. “Gleameil is

[029-066]dead.”

[029-067]Earthrid tried to collect his scattered senses.

[029-068]“I warned her,” he replied, sitting up. “Did I not beg her to go away?

[029-069]But she died very easily. She did not wait for the beauty she spoke

[029-070]about. She heard nothing of the passion, nor even of the rhythm. Neither

[029-071]have you.”

[029-072]Maskull looked down at him in indignation, but said nothing.

[029-073]“You should not have interrupted me,” went on Earthrid. “When I am

[029-074]playing, nothing else is of importance. I might have lost the thread of

[029-075]my ideas. Fortunately, I never forget. I shall start over again.”

[029-076]“If music is to continue, in the presence of the dead, I play next.”

[029-077]The man glanced up quickly.

[029-078]“That can’t be.”

[029-079]“It must be,” said Maskull decisively. “I prefer playing to listening.

[029-080]Another reason is that you will have every night, but I have only

[029-081]tonight.”

[029-082]Earthrid clenched and unclenched his fist, and began to turn pale. “With

[029-083]your recklessness, you are likely to kill us both. Irontick belongs to

[029-084]me, and until you have learned how to play, you would only break the

[029-085]instrument.”

[029-086]“Well, then, I will break it; but I am going to try.”

[029-087]The musician jumped to his feet and confronted him. “Do you intend to

[029-088]take it from me by violence?”

[029-089]“Keep calm! You will have the same choice that you offered us. I shall

[029-090]give you time to go away somewhere.”

[029-091]“How will that serve me, if you spoil my lake? You don’t understand what

[029-092]you are doing.”

[029-093]“Go, or stay!” responded Maskull. “I give you till the water gets smooth

[029-094]again. After that, I begin playing.”

[029-095]Earthrid kept swallowing. He glanced at the lake and back to Maskull.

[029-096]“Do you swear it?”

[029-097]“How long that will take, you know better than I; but till then you are

[029-098]safe.”

[029-099]Earthrid cast him a look of malice, hesitated for an instant, and then

[029-100]moved away, and started to climb the nearest hill. Halfway up he glanced

[029-101]over his shoulder apprehensively, as if to see what was happening. In

[029-102]another minute or so, he had disappeared over the crest, travelling in

[029-103]the direction of the shore that faced Matterplay.

[029-104]Later, when the water was once more tranquil, Maskull sat down by its

[029-105]edge, in imitation of Earthrid’s attitude. He knew neither how to set

[029-106]about producing his music, nor what would come of it. But audacious

[029-107]projects entered his brain and he willed to create physical shapes—and,

[029-108]above all, one shape, that of Surtur.

[029-109]Before putting his foot to the water, he turned things over a little in

[029-110]his mind.

[029-111]He said, “What themes are in common music, shapes are in this music. The

[029-112]composer does not find his theme by picking out single notes; but the

[029-113]whole theme flashes into his mind by inspiration. So it must be with

[029-114]shapes. When I start playing, if I am worth anything, the undivided

[029-115]ideas will pass from my unconscious mind to this lake, and then,

[029-116]reflected back in the dimensions of reality, I shall be for the first

[029-117]time made acquainted with them. So it must be.”

[029-118]The instant his foot touched the water, he felt his thoughts flowing

[029-119]from him. He did not know what they were, but the mere act of flowing

[029-120]created a sensation of joyful mastery. With this was curiosity to learn

[029-121]what they would prove to be. Spouts formed on the lake in increasing

[029-122]numbers, but he experienced no pain. His thoughts, which he knew to be

[029-123]music, did not issue from him in a steady, unbroken stream, but in

[029-124]great, rough gushes, succeeding intervals of quiescence. When these

[029-125]gushes came, the whole lake broke out in an eruption of spouts.

[029-126]He realised that the ideas passing from him did not arise in his

[029-127]intellect, but had their source in the fathomless depths of his will. He

[029-128]could not decide what character they should have, but he was able to

[029-129]force them out, or retard them, by the exercise of his volition.

[029-130]At first nothing changed around him. Then the moon grew dimmer, and a

[029-131]strange, new radiance began to illuminate the landscape. It increased so

[029-132]imperceptibly that it was some time before he recognised it as the

[029-133]Muspel-light which he had seen in the Wombflash Forest. He could not

[029-134]give it a colour, or a name, but it filled him with a sort of stern and

[029-135]sacred awe. He called up the resources of his powerful will. The spouts

[029-136]thickened like a forest, and many of them were twenty feet high.

[029-137]Teargeld looked faint and pale; the radiance became intense; but it cast

[029-138]no shadows. The wind got up, but where Maskull was sitting, it was calm.

[029-139]Shortly afterward it began to shriek and whistle, like a full gale. He

[029-140]saw no shapes, and redoubled his efforts.

[029-141]His ideas were now rushing out onto the lake so furiously that his whole

[029-142]soul was possessed by exhilaration and defiance. But still he did not

[029-143]know their nature. A huge spout shot up and at the same moment the hills

[029-144]began to crack and break. Great masses of loose soil were erupted from

[029-145]their bowels, and in the next period of quietness, he saw that the

[029-146]landscape had altered. Still the mysterious light intensified. The moon

[029-147]disappeared entirely. The noise of the unseen tempest was terrifying,

[029-148]but Maskull played heroically on, trying to urge out ideas which would

[029-149]take shape. The hillsides were cleft with chasms. The water escaping

[029-150]from the tops of the spouts, swamped the land; but where he was, it was

[029-151]dry.

[029-152]The radiance grew terrible. It was everywhere, but Maskull fancied that

[029-153]it was far brighter in one particular quarter. He thought that it was

[029-154]becoming localised, preparatory to contracting into a solid form. He

[029-155]strained and strained....

[029-156]Immediately afterward the bottom of the lake subsided. Its waters fell

[029-157]through, and his instrument was broken.

[029-158]The Muspel-light vanished. The moon shone out again, but Maskull could

[029-159]not see it. After that unearthly shining, he seemed to himself to be in

[029-160]total blackness. The screaming wind ceased; there was a dead silence.

[029-161]His thoughts finished flowing toward the lake, and his foot no longer

[029-162]touched water, but hung in space.

[029-163]He was too stunned by the suddenness of the change to either think or

[029-164]feel. While he was still lying dazed, a vast explosion occurred in the

[029-165]newly opened depths beneath the lakebed. The water in its descent had

[029-166]met fire. Maskull was lifted bodily in the air, many yards high, and

[029-167]came down heavily. He lost consciousness....

[029-168]When he came to his senses again, he saw everything. Teargeld was

[029-169]gleaming brilliantly. He was lying by the side of the old lake, but it

[029-170]was now a crater, to the bottom of which his eyes could not penetrate.

[029-171]The hills encircling it were torn, as if by heavy gunfire. A few

[029-172]thunderclouds were floating in the air at no great height, from which

[029-173]branched lightning descended to the earth incessantly, accompanied by

[029-174]alarming and singular crashes.

[029-175]He got on his legs, and tested his actions. Finding that he was

[029-176]uninjured, he first of all viewed the crater at closer quarters, and

[029-177]then started to walk painfully toward the northern shore.

[029-178]When he had attained the crest above the lake, the landscape sloped

[029-179]gently down for two miles to the sea. Everywhere he passed through

[029-180]traces of his rough work. The country was carved into scarps, grooves,

[029-181]channels, and craters. He arrived at the line of low cliffs overlooking

[029-182]the beach, and found that these also were partly broken down by

[029-183]landslips. He got down onto the sand and stood looking over the moonlit,

[029-184]agitated sea, wondering how he could contrive to escape from this island

[029-185]of failure.
