# Section 5

[005-001]“As we can’t get any further,” said Maskull, “I presume this is your Gap

[005-002]of Sorgie?”

[005-003]“Yes,” answered his friend, first dropping on his knees and then lying

[005-004]at full length, face downward. He drew his head and shoulders over the

[005-005]edge and began to stare straight down at the water.

[005-006]“What is there interesting down there, Nightspore?”

[005-007]Receiving no reply, however, he followed his friend’s example, and the

[005-008]next minute was looking for himself. Nothing was to be seen; the gloom

[005-009]had deepened, and the sea was nearly invisible. But, while he was

[005-010]ineffectually gazing, he heard what sounded like the beating of a drum

[005-011]on the narrow strip of shore below. It was very faint, but quite

[005-012]distinct. The beats were in four-four time, with the third beat slightly

[005-013]accented. He now continued to hear the noise all the time he was lying

[005-014]there. The beats were in no way drowned by the far louder sound of the

[005-015]surf, but seemed somehow to belong to a different world....

[005-016]When they were on their feet again, he questioned Nightspore. “We came

[005-017]here solely to hear that?”

[005-018]Nightspore cast one of his odd looks at him. “It’s called locally ‘The

[005-019]Drum Taps of Sorgie.’ You will not hear that name again, but perhaps you

[005-020]will hear the sound again.”

[005-021]“And if I do, what will it imply?” demanded Maskull in amazement.

[005-022]“It bears its own message. Only try always to hear it more and more

[005-023]distinctly.... Now it’s growing dark, and we must get back.”

[005-024]Maskull pulled out his watch automatically, and looked at the time. It

[005-025]was past six.... But he was thinking of Nightspore’s words, and not of

[005-026]the time.

[005-028]Night had already fallen by the time they regained the tower. The black

[005-029]sky was glorious with liquid stars. Arcturus was a little way above the

[005-030]sea, directly opposite them, in the east. As they were passing the base

[005-031]of the tower, Maskull observed with a sudden shock that the gate was

[005-032]open. He caught hold of Nightspore’s arm violently. “Look! Krag is

[005-033]back.”

[005-034]“Yes, we must make haste to the house.”

[005-035]“And why not the tower? He’s probably in there, since the gate is open.

[005-036]I’m going up to look.”

[005-037]Nightspore grunted, but made no opposition.

[005-038]All was pitch-black inside the gate. Maskull struck a match, and the

[005-039]flickering light disclosed the lower end of a circular flight of stone

[005-040]steps. “Are you coming up?” he asked.

[005-041]“No, I’ll wait here.”

[005-042]Maskull immediately began the ascent. Hardly had he mounted half a dozen

[005-043]steps, however, before he was compelled to pause, to gain breath. He

[005-044]seemed to be carrying upstairs not one Maskull, but three. As he

[005-045]proceeded, the sensation of crushing weight, so far from diminishing,

[005-046]grew worse and worse. It was nearly physically impossible to go on; his

[005-047]lungs could not take in enough oxygen, while his heart thumped like a

[005-048]ship’s engine. Sweat coursed down his face. At the twentieth step he

[005-049]completed the first revolution of the tower and came face to face with

[005-050]the first window, which was set in a high embrasure.

[005-051]Realising that he could go no higher, he struck another match, and

[005-052]climbed into the embrasure, in order that he might at all events see

[005-053]something from the tower. The flame died, and he stared through the

[005-054]window at the stars. Then, to his astonishment, he discovered that it

[005-055]was not a window at all but a lens.... The sky was not a wide expanse of

[005-056]space containing a multitude of stars, but a blurred darkness, focused

[005-057]only in one part, where two very bright stars, like small moons in size,

[005-058]appeared in close conjunction; and near them a more minute planetary

[005-059]object, as brilliant as Venus and with an observable disk. One of the

[005-060]suns shone with a glaring white light; the other was a weird and awful

[005-061]blue. Their light, though almost solar in intensity, did not illuminate

[005-062]the interior of the tower.

[005-063]Maskull knew at once that the system of spheres at which he was gazing

[005-064]was what is known to astronomy as the star Arcturus.... He had seen the

[005-065]sight before, through Krag’s glass, but then the scale had been smaller,

[005-066]the colors of the twin suns had not appeared in their naked reality....

[005-067]These colors seemed to him most marvellous, as if, in seeing them

[005-068]through earth eyes, he was not seeing them correctly.... But it was at

[005-069]Tormance that he stared the longest and the most earnestly. On that

[005-070]mysterious and terrible earth, countless millions of miles distant, it

[005-071]had been promised him that he would set foot, even though he might leave

[005-072]his bones there. The strange creatures that he was to behold and touch

[005-073]were already living, at this very moment.

[005-074]A low, sighing whisper sounded in his ear, from not more than a yard

[005-075]away. “Don’t you understand, Maskull, that you are only an instrument,

[005-076]to be used and then broken? Nightspore is asleep now, but when he wakes

[005-077]you must die. You will go, but he will return.”

[005-078]Maskull hastily struck another match, with trembling fingers. No one was

[005-079]in sight, and all was quiet as the tomb.

[005-080]The voice did not sound again. After waiting a few minutes, he

[005-081]redescended to the foot of the tower. On gaining the open air, his

[005-082]sensation of weight was instantly removed, but he continued panting and

[005-083]palpitating, like a man who has lifted a far too heavy load.

[005-084]Nightspore’s dark form came forward. “Was Krag there?”

[005-085]“If he was, I didn’t see him. But I heard someone speak.”

[005-086]“Was it Krag?”

[005-087]“It was not Krag—but a voice warned me against you.”

[005-088]“Yes, you will hear these voices too,” said Nightspore enigmatically.

[005-090]When they returned to the house, the windows were all in darkness and

[005-091]the door was ajar, just as they had left it; Krag presumably was not

[005-092]there. Maskull went all over the house, striking matches in every

[005-093]room—at the end of the examination he was ready to swear that the man

[005-094]they were expecting had not even stuck his nose inside the premises.

[005-095]Groping their way into the library, they sat down in the total darkness

[005-096]to wait, for nothing else remained to be done. Maskull lit his pipe, and

[005-097]began to drink the remainder of the whisky. Through the open window

[005-098]sounded in their ears the trainlike grinding of the sea at the foot of

[005-099]the cliffs.

[005-100]“Krag must be in the tower after all,” remarked Maskull, breaking the

[005-101]silence.

[005-102]“Yes, he is getting ready.”

[005-103]“I hope he doesn’t expect us to join him there. It was beyond my

[005-104]powers—but why, heaven knows. The stairs must have a magnetic pull of

[005-105]some sort.”

[005-106]“It is Tormantic gravity,” muttered Nightspore.

[005-107]“I understand you—or, rather, I don’t—but it doesn’t matter.”

[005-108]He went on smoking in silence, occasionally taking a mouthful of the

[005-109]neat liquor. “Who is Surtur?” he demanded abruptly.

[005-110]“We others are gropers and bunglers, but he is a master.”

[005-111]Maskull digested this. “I fancy you are right, for though I know nothing

[005-112]about him his mere name has an exciting effect on me.... Are you

[005-113]personally acquainted with him?”

[005-114]“I must be... I forget...” replied Nightspore in a choking voice.

[005-115]Maskull looked up, surprised, but could make nothing out in the

[005-116]blackness of the room.

[005-117]“Do you know so many extraordinary men that you can forget some of

[005-118]them?... Perhaps you can tell me this... will we meet him, where we are

[005-119]going?”

[005-120]“You will meet death, Maskull.... Ask me no more questions—I can’t

[005-121]answer them.”

[005-122]“Then let us go on waiting for Krag,” said Maskull coldly.

[005-123]Ten minutes later the front door slammed, and a light, quick footstep

[005-124]was heard running up the stairs. Maskull got up, with a beating heart.

[005-125]Krag appeared on the threshold of the door, bearing in his hand a feebly

[005-126]glimmering lantern. A hat was on his head, and he looked stern and

[005-127]forbidding. After scrutinising the two friends for a moment or so, he

[005-128]strode into the room and thrust the lantern on the table. Its light

[005-129]hardly served to illuminate the walls.

[005-130]“You have got here, then, Maskull?”

[005-131]“So it seems—but I shan’t thank you for your hospitality, for it has

[005-132]been conspicuous by its absence.”

[005-133]Krag ignored the remark. “Are you ready to start?”

[005-134]“By all means—when you are. It is not so entertaining here.”

[005-135]Krag surveyed him critically. “I heard you stumbling about in the tower.

[005-136]You couldn’t get up, it seems.”

[005-137]“It looks like an obstacle, for Nightspore informs me that the start

[005-138]takes place from the top.”

[005-139]“But your other doubts are all removed?”

[005-140]“So far, Krag, that I now possess an open mind. I am quite willing to

[005-141]see what you can do.”

[005-142]“Nothing more is asked.... But this tower business. You know that until

[005-143]you are able to climb to the top you are unfit to stand the gravitation

[005-144]of Tormance?”

[005-145]“Then I repeat, it’s an awkward obstacle, for I certainly can’t get up.”

[005-146]Krag hunted about in his pockets, and at length produced a clasp knife.

[005-147]“Remove your coat, and roll up your shirt sleeve,” he directed.

[005-148]“Do you propose to make an incision with that?”

[005-149]“Yes, and don’t start difficulties, because the effect is certain, but

[005-150]you can’t possibly understand it beforehand.”

[005-151]“Still, a cut with a pocket-knife—” began Maskull, laughing.

[005-152]“It will answer, Maskull,” interrupted Nightspore.

[005-153]“Then bare your arm too, you aristocrat of the universe,” said Krag.

[005-154]“Let us see what your blood is made of.”

[005-155]Nightspore obeyed.

[005-156]Krag pulled out the big blade of the knife, and made a careless and

[005-157]almost savage slash at Maskull’s upper arm. The wound was deep, and

[005-158]blood flowed freely.

[005-159]“Do I bind it up?” asked Maskull, scowling with pain.

[005-160]Krag spat on the wound. “Pull your shirt down, it won’t bleed any more.”

[005-161]He then turned his attention to Nightspore, who endured his operation

[005-162]with grim indifference. Krag threw the knife on the floor.

[005-164]Maskull’s body, and he began to doubt whether he would not have to

[005-166]a gnawing ache in the injured arm, just strong enough to make life one

[005-168]“That’s finished,” said Krag. “Now you can follow me.”

[005-174]While they were walking swiftly across to the tower, Maskull caught his

[005-175]arm. “I heard a voice up those stairs.”

[005-176]“What did it say?”

[005-177]“That I am to go, but Nightspore is to return.”

[005-181]“I don’t know what I want. But I thought the thing was curious enough to

[005-183]“It is not a bad thing to hear voices,” said Krag, “but you mustn’t for

[005-186]When they had arrived at the open gateway of the tower, he immediately

[005-193]At the lowest window Krag went straight ahead without stopping, but
