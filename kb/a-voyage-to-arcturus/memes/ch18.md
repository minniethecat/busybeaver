# Section 18

[018-001]“I fail to comprehend.”

[018-002]“Listen, then. I wish to start a new existence in your body. I wish to

[018-003]be a male. I see it isn’t worth while being a woman. I mean to dedicate

[018-004]my own body to Crimtyphon. I shall tie his body and mine together, and

[018-005]give them a common funeral in the burning lake. That’s the sacrifice I

[018-006]offer you. As I said, it’s a hard one.”

[018-007]“So you do ask me to die. Though how you can make use of my body is

[018-008]difficult to understand.”

[018-009]“No, I don’t ask you to die. You will go on living.”

[018-010]“How is it possible without a body?”

[018-011]Tydomin gazed at him earnestly. “There are many such beings, even in

[018-012]your world. There you call them spirits, apparitions, phantoms. They are

[018-013]in reality living wills, deprived of material bodies, always longing to

[018-014]act and enjoy, but quite unable to do so. Are you noble-minded enough to

[018-015]accept such a state, do you think?”

[018-016]“If it’s possible, I accept it,” replied Maskull quietly. “Not in spite

[018-017]of its heaviness, but because of it. But how is it possible?”

[018-018]“Undoubtedly there are very many things possible in our world of which

[018-019]you have no conception. Now let us wait till we get home. I don’t hold

[018-020]you to your word, for unless it’s a free sacrifice I will have nothing

[018-021]to do with it.”

[018-022]“I am not a man who speaks lightly. If you can perform this miracle, you

[018-023]have my consent, once for all.”

[018-024]“Then we’ll leave it like that for the present,” said Tydomin sadly.

[018-025]They proceeded on their way. Owing to the subsidence, Tydomin seemed

[018-026]rather doubtful at first as to the right road, but by making a long

[018-027]divergence they eventually got around to the other side of the newly

[018-028]formed chasm. A little later on, in a narrow copse crowning a miniature,

[018-029]insulated peak, they fell in with a man. He was resting himself against

[018-030]a tree, and looked tired, overheated, and despondent. He was young. His

[018-031]beardless expression bore an expression of unusual sincerity, and in

[018-032]other respects he seemed a hardy, hardworking youth, of an intellectual

[018-033]type. His hair was thick, short, and flaxen. He possessed neither a sorb

[018-034]nor a third arm—so presumably he was not a native of Ifdawn. His

[018-035]forehead, however, was disfigured by what looked like a haphazard

[018-036]assortment of eyes, eight in number, of different sizes and shapes. They

[018-037]went in pairs, and whenever two were in use, it was indicated by a

[018-038]peculiar shining—the rest remained dull, until their turn came. In

[018-039]addition to the upper eyes he had the two lower ones, but they were

[018-040]vacant and lifeless. This extraordinary battery of eyes, alternatively

[018-041]alive and dead, gave the young man an appearance of almost alarming

[018-042]mental activity. He was wearing nothing but a sort of skin kilt. Maskull

[018-043]seemed somehow to recognise the face, though he had certainly never set

[018-044]eyes on it before.

[018-045]Tydomin suggested to him to set down the corpse, and both sat down to

[018-046]rest in the shade.

[018-047]“Question him, Maskull,” she said, rather carelessly, jerking her head

[018-048]toward the stranger.

[018-049]Maskull sighed and asked aloud, from his seat on the ground, “What’s

[018-050]your name, and where do you come from?”

[018-051]The man studied him for a few moments, first with one pair of eyes, then

[018-052]with another, then with a third. He next turned his attention to

[018-053]Tydomin, who occupied him a still longer time. He replied at last, in a

[018-054]dry, manly, nervous voice. “I am Digrung. I have arrived here from

[018-055]Matterplay.” His colour kept changing, and Maskull suddenly realised of

[018-056]whom he reminded him. It was of Joiwind.

[018-057]“Perhaps you’re going to Poolingdred, Digrung?” he inquired, interested.

[018-058]“As a matter of fact I am—if I can find my way out of this accursed

[018-059]country.”

[018-060]“Possibly you are acquainted with Joiwind there?”

[018-061]“She’s my sister. I’m on my way to see her now. Why, do you know her?”

[018-062]“I met her yesterday.”

[018-063]“What is your name, then?”

[018-064]“Maskull.”

[018-065]“I shall tell her I met you. This will be our first meeting for four

[018-066]years. Is she well, and happy?”

[018-067]“Both, as far as I could judge. You know Panawe?”

[018-068]“Her husband—yes. But where do you come from? I’ve seen nothing like you

[018-069]before.”

[018-070]“From another world. Where is Matterplay?”

[018-071]“It’s the first country one comes to beyond the Sinking Sea.”

[018-072]“What is it like there—how do you amuse yourselves? The same old murders

[018-073]and sudden deaths?”

[018-074]“Are you ill?” asked Digrung. “Who is this woman, why are you following

[018-075]at her heels like a slave? She looks insane to me. What’s that

[018-076]corpse—why are you dragging it around the country with you?”

[018-077]Tydomin smiled. “I’ve already heard it said about Matterplay, that if

[018-078]one sows an answer there, a rich crop of questions immediately springs

[018-079]up. But why do you make this unprovoked attack on me, Digrung?”

[018-080]“I don’t attack you, woman, but I know you. I see into you, and I see

[018-081]insanity. That wouldn’t matter, but I don’t like to see a man of

[018-082]intelligence like Maskull caught in your filthy meshes.”

[018-083]“I suppose even you clever Matterplay people sometimes misjudge

[018-084]character. However, I don’t mind. Your opinion’s nothing to me, Digrung.

[018-085]You’d better answer his questions, Maskull. Not for his own sake—but

[018-086]your feminine friend is sure to be curious about your having been seen

[018-087]carrying a dead man.”

[018-088]Maskull’s underlip shot out. “Tell your sister nothing, Digrung. Don’t

[018-089]mention my name at all. I don’t want her to know about this meeting of

[018-090]ours.”

[018-091]“Why not?”

[018-092]“I don’t wish it—isn’t that enough?”

[018-093]Digrung looked impassive.

[018-094]“Thoughts and words,” he said, “which don’t correspond with the real

[018-095]events of the world are considered most shameful in Matterplay.”

[018-096]“I’m not asking you to lie, only to keep silent.”

[018-097]“To hide the truth is a special branch of lying. I can’t accede to your

[018-098]wish. I must tell Joiwind everything, as far as I know it.”

[018-099]Maskull got up, and Tydomin followed his example.

[018-100]She touched Digrung on the arm and gave him a strange look. “The dead

[018-101]man is my husband, and Maskull murdered him. Now you’ll understand why

[018-102]he wishes you to hold your tongue.”

[018-103]“I guessed there was some foul play,” said Digrung. “It doesn’t matter—I

[018-104]can’t falsify facts. Joiwind must know.”

[018-105]“You refuse to consider her feelings?” said Maskull, turning pale.

[018-106]“Feelings which flourish on illusions, and sicken and die on realities,

[018-107]aren’t worth considering. But Joiwind’s are not of that kind.”

[018-108]“If you decline to do what I ask, at least return home without seeing

[018-109]her; your sister will get very little pleasure out of the meeting when

[018-110]she hears your news.”

[018-111]“What are these strange relations between you?” demanded Digrung, eying

[018-112]him with suddenly aroused suspicion.

[018-113]Maskull stared back in a sort of bewilderment. “Good God! You don’t

[018-114]doubt your own sister. That pure angel!”

[018-115]Tydomin caught hold of him delicately. “I don’t know Joiwind, but,

[018-116]whoever she is and whatever she’s like, I know this—she’s more fortunate

[018-117]in her friend than in her brother. Now, if you really value her

[018-118]happiness, Maskull, you will have to take some firm step or other.”

[018-119]“I mean to. Digrung, I shall stop your journey.”

[018-120]“If you intend a second murder, no doubt you are big enough.”

[018-121]Maskull turned around to Tydomin and laughed. “I seem to be leaving a

[018-122]wake of corpses behind me on this journey.”

[018-123]“Why a corpse? There’s no need to kill him.”

[018-124]“Thanks for that!” said Digrung dryly. “All the same, some crime is

[018-125]about to burst. I feel it.”

[018-126]“What must I do, then?” asked Maskull.

[018-127]“It is not my business, and to tell the truth I am not very

[018-128]interested.... If I were in your place, Maskull, I would not hesitate

[018-129]long. Don’t you understand how to absorb these creatures, who set their

[018-130]feeble, obstinate wills against yours?”

[018-131]“That is a worse crime,” said Maskull.

[018-132]“Who knows? He will live, but he will tell no tales.”

[018-133]Digrung laughed, but changed colour. “I was right then. The monster has

[018-134]sprung into the light of day.”

[018-135]Maskull laid a hand on his shoulder. “You have the choice, and we are

[018-136]not joking. Do as I ask.”

[018-137]“You have fallen low, Maskull. But you are walking in a dream, and I

[018-138]can’t talk to you. As for you, woman—sin must be like a pleasant bath to

[018-139]you....”

[018-140]“There are strange ties between Maskull and myself; but you are a

[018-141]passer-by, a foreigner. I care nothing for you.”

[018-142]“Nevertheless, I shall not be frightened out of my plans, which are

[018-143]legitimate and right.”

[018-144]“Do as you please,” said Tydomin. “If you come to grief, your thoughts

[018-145]will hardly have corresponded with the real events of the world, which

[018-146]is what you boast about. It is no affair of mine.”

[018-147]“I shall go on, and not back!” exclaimed Digrung, with angry emphasis.

[018-148]Tydomin threw a swift, evil smile at Maskull. “Bear witness that I have

[018-149]tried to persuade this young man. Now you must come to a quick decision

[018-150]in your own mind as to which is of the greatest importance, Digrung’s

[018-151]happiness or Joiwind’s. Digrung won’t allow you to preserve them both.”

[018-152]“It won’t take me long to decide, Digrung, I gave you a last chance to

[018-153]change your mind.”

[018-154]“As long as it’s in my power I shall go on, and warn my sister against

[018-155]her criminal friends.”

[018-156]Maskull again clutched at him, but this time with violence. Instructed

[018-157]in his actions by some new and horrible instinct, he pressed the young

[018-158]man tightly to his body with all three arms. A feeling of wild, sweet

[018-159]delight immediately passed through him. Then for the first time he

[018-160]comprehended the triumphant joys of “absorbing.” It satisfied the hunger

[018-161]of the will, exactly as food satisfies the hunger of the body. Digrung

[018-162]proved feeble—he made little opposition. His personality passed slowly

[018-163]and evenly into Maskull’s. The latter became strong and gorged. The

[018-164]victim gradually became paler and limper, until Maskull held a corpse in

[018-165]his arms. He dropped the body, and stood trembling. He had committed his

[018-166]second crime. He felt no immediate difference in his soul, but...

[018-167]Tydomin shed a sad smile on him, like winter sunshine. He half expected

[018-168]her to speak, but she said nothing. Instead, she made a sign to him to

[018-169]pick up Crimtyphon’s corpse. As he obeyed, he wondered why Digrung’s

[018-170]dead face did not wear the frightful Crystalman mask.

[018-171]“Why hasn’t he altered?” he muttered to himself.

[018-172]Tydomin heard him. She kicked Digrung lightly with her little foot. “He

[018-173]isn’t dead—that’s why. The expression you mean is waiting for your

[018-174]death.”

[018-175]“Then is that my real character?”

[018-176]She laughed softly. “You came here to carve a strange world, and now it

[018-177]appears you are carved yourself. Oh, there’s no doubt about it, Maskull.

[018-178]You needn’t stand there gaping. You belong to Shaping, like the rest of

[018-179]us. You are not a king, or a god.”

[018-180]“Since when have I belonged to him?”

[018-181]“What does that matter? Perhaps since you first breathed the air of

[018-182]Tormance, or perhaps since five minutes ago.”

[018-183]Without waiting for his response, she set off through the copse, and

[018-184]strode on to the next island. Maskull followed, physically distressed

[018-185]and looking very grave.

[018-186]The journey continued for half an hour longer, without incident. The

[018-187]character of the scenery slowly changed. The mountaintops became loftier

[018-188]and more widely separated from one another. The gaps were filled with

[018-189]rolling, white clouds, which bathed the shores of the peaks like a

[018-190]mysterious sea. To pass from island to island was hard work, the

[018-191]intervening spaces were so wide—Tydomin, however, knew the way. The

[018-192]intense light, the violet-blue sky, the patches of vivid landscape,

[018-193]emerging from the white vapour-ocean, made a profound impression on

[018-194]Maskull’s mind. The glow of Alppain was hidden by the huge mass of

[018-195]Disscourn, which loomed up straight in front of them.
