# Section 35

[035-001]“How do you explain that?”

[035-002]“When everything is darkest, he may be nearest, Maskull.”

[035-003]“But this is troubling you?”

[035-004]“My days are spent in torture.”

[035-005]“You still persist, though? This day darkness can’t be the ultimate

[035-006]state?”

[035-007]“My questions will be answered.”

[035-008]A silence ensued.

[035-009]“What do you propose to show me?” asked Maskull.

[035-010]“The land is about to grow wilder. I am taking you to the Three Figures,

[035-011]which were carved and erected by an earlier race of men. There, we will

[035-012]pray.”

[035-013]“And what then?”

[035-014]“If you are truehearted, you will see things you will not easily

[035-015]forget.”

[035-016]They had been walking slightly uphill in a sort of trough between two

[035-017]parallel, gently sloping downs. The trough now deepened, while the hills

[035-018]on either side grew steeper. They were in an ascending valley and, as it

[035-019]curved this way and that, the landscape was shut off from view. They

[035-020]came to a little spring, bubbling up from the ground. It formed a

[035-021]trickling brook, which was unlike all other brooks in that it was

[035-022]flowing up the valley instead of down. Before long it was joined by

[035-023]other miniature rivulets, so that in the end it became a fair-sized

[035-024]stream. Maskull kept looking at it, and puckering his forehead.

[035-025]“Nature has other laws here, it seems?”

[035-026]“Nothing can exist here that is not a compound of the three worlds.”

[035-027]“Yet the water is flowing somewhere.”

[035-028]“I can’t explain it, but there are three wills in it.”

[035-029]“Is there no such thing as pure Thire-matter?”

[035-030]“Thire cannot exist without Amfuse, and Amfuse cannot exist without

[035-031]Faceny.”

[035-032]Maskull thought this over for some minutes. “That must be so,” he said

[035-033]at last. “Without life there can be no love, and without love there can

[035-034]be no religious feeling.”

[035-035]In the half light of the land, the tops of the hills containing the

[035-036]valley presently attained such a height that they could not be seen. The

[035-037]sides were steep and craggy, while the bed of the valley grew narrower

[035-038]at every step. Not a living organism was visible. All was unnatural and

[035-039]sepulchral.

[035-040]Maskull said, “I feel as if I were dead, and walking in another world.”

[035-041]“I still do not know what you are doing here,” answered Corpang.

[035-042]“Why should I go on making a mystery of it? I came to find Surtur.”

[035-043]“That name I’ve heard—but under what circumstances?”

[035-044]“You forget?”

[035-045]Corpang walked along, his eyes fixed on the ground, obviously troubled.

[035-046]“Who is Surtur?”

[035-047]Maskull shook his head, and said nothing.

[035-048]The valley shortly afterward narrowed, so that the two men, touching

[035-049]fingertips in the middle, could have placed their free hands on the rock

[035-050]walls on either side. It threatened to terminate in a cul-de-sac, but

[035-051]just when the road seemed least promising, and they were shut in by

[035-052]cliffs on all sides, a hitherto unperceived bend brought them suddenly

[035-053]into the open. They emerged through a mere crack in the line of

[035-054]precipices.

[035-055]A sort of huge natural corridor was running along at right angles to the

[035-056]way they had come; both ends faded into obscurity after a few hundred

[035-057]yards. Right down the centre of this corridor ran a chasm with

[035-058]perpendicular sides; its width varied from thirty to a hundred feet, but

[035-059]its bottom could not be seen. On both sides of the chasm, facing one

[035-060]another, were platforms of rock, twenty feet or so in width; they too

[035-061]proceeded in both directions out of sight. Maskull and Corpang emerged

[035-062]onto one of these platforms. The shelf opposite was a few feet higher

[035-063]than that on which they stood. The platforms were backed by a double

[035-064]line of lofty and unclimbable cliffs, whose tops were invisible.

[035-065]The stream, which had accompanied them through the gap, went straight

[035-066]forward, but, instead of descending the wall of the chasm as a

[035-067]waterfall, it crossed from side to side like a liquid bridge. It then

[035-068]disappeared through a cleft in the cliffs on the opposite side.

[035-069]To Maskull’s mind, however, even more wonderful than this unnatural

[035-070]phenomenon was the absence of shadows, which was more noticeable here

[035-071]than on the open plain. It made the place look like a hall of phantoms.

[035-072]Corpang, without delay, led the way along the shelf to the left. When

[035-073]they had walked about a mile, the gulf widened to two hundred feet.

[035-074]Three large rocks loomed up on the ledge opposite; they resembled three

[035-075]upright giants, standing motionless side by side on the extreme edge of

[035-076]the chasm. Corpang and Maskull drew nearer, and then Maskull saw that

[035-077]they were statues. Each was about thirty feet high, and the workmanship

[035-078]was of the rudest. They represented naked men, but the limbs and trunks

[035-079]had been barely chipped into shape—the faces alone had had care bestowed

[035-080]on them, and even these faces were merely generalised. It was obviously

[035-081]the work of primitive artists. The statues stood erect with knees closed

[035-082]and arms hanging straight down their sides. All three were exactly

[035-083]alike.

[035-084]As soon as they were directly opposite, Corpang halted.

[035-085]“Is this a representation of your three Beings?” asked Maskull, awed by

[035-086]the spectacle in spite of his constitutional audacity.

[035-087]“Ask no questions, but kneel,” replied Corpang. He dropped onto his own

[035-088]knees, but Maskull remained standing.

[035-089]Corpang covered his eyes with one hand, and prayed silently. After a few

[035-090]minutes the light sensibly faded. Then Maskull knelt as well, but he

[035-091]continued looking.

[035-092]It grew darker and darker, until all was like the blackest night. Sight

[035-093]and sound no longer existed; he was alone with his own spirit.

[035-094]Then one of the three Colossi came slowly into sight again. But it had

[035-095]ceased to be a statue—it was a living person. Out of the blackness of

[035-096]space a gigantic head and chest emerged, illuminated by a mystic, rosy

[035-097]glow, like a mountain peak bathed by the rising sun. As the light grew

[035-098]stronger Maskull saw that the flesh was translucent and that the glow

[035-099]came from within. The limbs of the apparition were wreathed in mist.

[035-100]Before long the features of the face stood out distinctly. It was that

[035-101]of a beardless youth of twenty years. It possessed the beauty of a girl

[035-102]and the daring force of a man; it bore a mocking, cryptic smile. Maskull

[035-103]felt the fresh, mysterious thrill of mingled pain and rapture of one who

[035-104]awakes from a deep sleep in midwinter and sees the gleaming, dark,

[035-105]delicate colours of the half-dawn. The vision smiled, kept still, and

[035-106]looked beyond him. He began to shudder, with delight—and many emotions.

[035-107]As he gazed, his poetic sensibility acquired such a nervous and

[035-108]indefinable character that he could endure it no more; he burst into

[035-109]tears.

[035-110]When he looked up again the image had nearly disappeared, and in a few

[035-111]moments more he was plunged back into total darkness.

[035-112]Shortly afterward a second statue reappeared. It too was transfigured

[035-113]into a living form, but Maskull was unable to see the details of its

[035-114]face and body, because of the brightness of the light that radiated from

[035-115]them. This light, which started as pale gold, ended as flaming golden

[035-116]fire. It illumined the whole underground landscape. The rock ledges, the

[035-117]cliffs, himself and Corpang on their knees, the two unlighted

[035-118]statues—all appeared as if in sunlight, and the shadows were black and

[035-119]strongly defined. The light carried heat with it, but a singular heat.

[035-120]Maskull was unaware of any rise in temperature, but he felt his heart

[035-121]melting to womanish softness. His male arrogance and egotism faded

[035-122]imperceptibly away; his personality seemed to disappear. What was left

[035-123]behind was not freedom of spirit or lightheartedness, but a passionate

[035-124]and nearly savage mental state of pity and distress. He felt a

[035-125]tormenting desire to serve. All this came from the heat of the statue,

[035-126]and was without an object. He glanced anxiously around him, and fastened

[035-127]his eyes on Corpang. He put a hand on his shoulder and aroused him from

[035-128]his praying.

[035-129]“You must know what I am feeling, Corpang.”

[035-130]Corpang smiled sweetly, but said nothing.

[035-131]“I care nothing for my own affairs any more. How can I help you?”

[035-132]“So much the better for you, Maskull, if you respond so quickly to the

[035-133]invisible worlds.”

[035-134]As soon as he had spoken, the figure began to vanish, and the light to

[035-135]die away from the landscape. Maskull’s emotion slowly subsided, but it

[035-136]was not until he was once more in complete darkness that he became

[035-137]master of himself again. Then he felt ashamed of his boyish exhibition

[035-138]of enthusiasm, and thought ruefully that there must be something wanting

[035-139]in his character. He got up onto his feet.

[035-140]The very moment that he arose, a man’s voice sounded, not a yard from

[035-141]his ear. It was hardly raised above a whisper, but he could distinguish

[035-142]that it was not Corpang’s. As he listened he was unable to prevent

[035-143]himself from physically trembling.

[035-144]“Maskull, you are to die,” said the unseen speaker.

[035-145]“Who is speaking?”

[035-146]“You have only a few hours of life left. Don’t trifle the time away.”

[035-147]Maskull could bring nothing out.

[035-148]“You have despised life,” went on the low-toned voice. “Do you really

[035-149]imagine that this mighty world has no meaning, and that life is a joke?”

[035-150]“What must I do?”

[035-151]“Repent your murders, commit no fresh ones, pay honour to...”

[035-152]The voice died away. Maskull waited in silence for it to speak again.

[035-153]All remained still, however, and the speaker appeared to have taken his

[035-154]departure. Supernatural horror seized him; he fell into a sort of

[035-155]catalepsy.

[035-156]At that moment he saw one of the statues fading away, from a pale, white

[035-157]glow to darkness. He had not previously seen it shining.

[035-158]In a few more minutes the normal light of the land returned. Corpang got

[035-159]up, and shook him out of his trance.

[035-160]Maskull looked around, but saw no third person. “Whose statue was the

[035-161]last?” he demanded.

[035-162]“Thire’s.”

[035-163]“Did you hear me speaking?”

[035-164]“I heard your voice, but no one else’s.”

[035-165]“I’ve just had my death foretold, so I suppose I have not long to live.

[035-166]Leehallfae prophesied the same thing.”

[035-167]Corpang shook his head. “What value do you set on life?” he asked.

[035-168]“Very little. But it’s a fearful thing all the same.”

[035-169]“Your death is?”

[035-170]“No, but this warning.”

[035-171]They stopped talking. A profound silence reigned. Neither of the two men

[035-172]seemed to know what to do next, or where to go. Then both of them heard

[035-173]the sound of drumming. It was slow, emphatic, and impressive, a long way

[035-174]off and not loud, but against the background of quietness, very marked.

[035-175]It appeared to come from some point out of sight, to the left of where

[035-176]they were standing, but on the same rock shelf. Maskull’s heart beat

[035-177]quickly.

[035-178]“What can that sound be?” asked Corpang, peering into the obscurity.

[035-179]“It is Surtur.”

[035-180]“Once again, who is Surtur?”

[035-181]Maskull clutched his arm and pressed him to silence. A strange radiance

[035-182]was in the air, in the direction of the drumming. It increased in

[035-183]intensity and gradually occupied the whole scene. Things were no longer

[035-184]seen by Their’s light, but by this new light. It cast no shadows.

[035-185]Corpang’s nostrils swelled, and he held himself more proudly. “What fire

[035-186]is that?”

[035-187]“It is Muspel-light.”

[035-188]They both glanced instinctively at the three statues. In the strange

[035-189]glow they had undergone a change. The face of each figure was clothed in

[035-190]the sordid and horrible Crystalman mask.

[035-191]Corpang cried out and put his hand over his eyes. “What can this mean?”

[035-192]he asked a minute later.

[035-193]“It must mean that life is wrong, and the creator of life too, whether

[035-194]he is one person or three.”

[035-195]Corpang looked again, like a man trying to accustom himself to a

[035-196]shocking sight. “Dare we believe this?”

[035-197]“You must,” replied Maskull. “You have always served the highest, and

[035-198]you must continue to do so. It has simply turned out that Thire is not

[035-199]the highest.”
