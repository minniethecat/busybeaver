# Section 27

[027-001]“Something strong is attracting her, and I can’t hold her back. I don’t

[027-002]think I shall see you again, wife, but the lads are now nearly old

[027-003]enough to fend for themselves.”

[027-004]“Don’t take dejected views,” replied Gleameil sternly. She was not

[027-005]eating. “I shall come back, and make amends to you. It’s only for a

[027-006]night.”

[027-007]Maskull gazed from one to the other in perplexity. “Let me go alone. I

[027-008]would be sorry if anything happened.”

[027-009]Gleameil shook her head.

[027-010]“Don’t regard this as a woman’s caprice,” she said. “Even if you hadn’t

[027-011]passed this way, I would have heard that music soon. I have a hunger for

[027-012]it.”

[027-013]“Haven’t you any such feeling, Polecrab?”

[027-014]“No. A woman is a noble and sensitive creature, and there are

[027-015]attractions in nature too subtle for males. Take her with you, since she

[027-016]is set on it. Maybe she’s right. Perhaps Earthrid’s music will answer

[027-017]your questions, and hers too.”

[027-018]“What are your questions, Gleameil?”

[027-019]The woman shed a strange smile. “You may be sure that a question which

[027-020]requires music for an answer can’t be put into words.”

[027-021]“If you are not back by the morning,” remarked her husband, “I will know

[027-022]you are dead.”

[027-023]The meal was finished in a constrained silence. Polecrab wiped his

[027-024]mouth, and produced a seashell from a kind of pocket.

[027-025]“Will you say goodbye to the boys? Shall I call them?” She considered a

[027-026]moment.

[027-027]“Yes—yes, I must see them.”

[027-028]He put the shell to his mouth, and blew; a loud, mournful noise passed

[027-029]through the air.

[027-030]A few minutes later there was a sound of scurrying footsteps, and the

[027-031]boys were seen emerging from the forest. Maskull looked with curiosity

[027-032]at the first children he had seen on Tormance. The oldest boy was

[027-033]carrying the youngest on his back, while the third trotted some distance

[027-034]behind. The child was let down, and all the three formed a semicircle in

[027-035]front of Maskull, standing staring up at him with wide-open eyes.

[027-036]Polecrab looked on stolidly, but Gleameil glanced away from them, with

[027-037]proudly raised head and a baffling expression.

[027-038]Maskull put the ages of the boys at about nine, seven, and five years,

[027-039]respectively; but he was calculating according to Earth time. The eldest

[027-040]was tall, slim, but strongly built. He, like his brothers, was naked,

[027-041]and his skin from top to toe was ulfire-colored. His facial muscles

[027-042]indicated a wild and daring nature, and his eyes were like green fires.

[027-043]The second showed promise of being a broad, powerful man. His head was

[027-044]large and heavy, and drooped. His face and skin were reddish. His eyes

[027-045]were almost too sombre and penetrating for a child’s.

[027-046]“That one,” said Polecrab, pinching the boy’s ear, “may perhaps grow up

[027-047]to be a second Broodviol.”

[027-048]“Who was that?” demanded the boy, bending his head forward to hear the

[027-049]answer.

[027-050]“A big, old man, of marvellous wisdom. He became wise by making up his

[027-051]mind never to ask questions, but to find things out for himself.”

[027-052]“If I had not asked this question, I should not have known about him.”

[027-053]“That would not have mattered,” replied the father.

[027-054]The youngest child was paler and slighter than his brothers. His face

[027-055]was mostly tranquil and expressionless, but it had this peculiarity

[027-056]about it, that every few minutes, without any apparent cause, it would

[027-057]wrinkle up and look perplexed. At these times his eyes, which were of a

[027-058]tawny gold, seemed to contain secrets difficult to associate with one of

[027-059]his age.

[027-060]“He puzzles me,” said Polecrab. “He has a soul like sap, and he’s

[027-061]interested in nothing. He may turn out to be the most remarkable of the

[027-062]bunch.”

[027-063]Maskull took the child in one hand, and lifted him as high as his head.

[027-064]He took a good look at him, and set him down again. The boy never

[027-065]changed countenance.

[027-066]“What do you make of him?” asked the fisherman.

[027-067]“It’s on the tip of my tongue to say, but it just escapes me. Let me

[027-068]drink again, and then I shall have it.”

[027-069]“Go and drink, then.”

[027-070]Maskull strode over to the tree, drank, and returned. “In ages to come,”

[027-071]he said, speaking deliberately, “he will be a grand and awful tradition.

[027-072]A seer possibly, or even a divinity. Watch over him well.”

[027-073]The eldest boy looked scornful. “I want to be none of those things. I

[027-074]would like to be like that big fellow.” And he pointed his finger at

[027-075]Maskull.

[027-076]He laughed, and showed his white teeth through his beard. “Thanks for

[027-077]the compliments old warrior!” he said.

[027-078]“He’s great and brawny,” continued the boy, “and can hold his own with

[027-079]other men. Can you hold me up with one arm, as you did that child?”

[027-080]Maskull complied.

[027-081]“That is being a man!” exclaimed the boy. “Enough!” said Polecrab

[027-082]impatiently. “I called you lads here to say goodbye to your mother. She

[027-083]is going away with this man. I think she may not return, but we don’t

[027-084]know.”

[027-085]The second boy’s face became suddenly inflamed. “Is she going of her own

[027-086]choice?” he inquired.

[027-087]“Yes,” replied the father.

[027-088]“Then she is bad.” He brought the words out with such force and emphasis

[027-089]that they sounded like the crack of a whip.

[027-090]The old man cuffed him twice. “Is it your mother you are speaking of?”

[027-091]The boy stood his ground, without change of expression, but said

[027-092]nothing.

[027-093]The youngest child spoke, for the first time. “My mother will not come

[027-094]back, but she will die dancing.”

[027-095]Polecrab and his wife looked at one another.

[027-096]“Where are you going to, Mother?” asked the eldest lad.

[027-097]Gleameil bent down, and kissed him. “To the Island.”

[027-098]“Well then, if you don’t come back by tomorrow morning, I will go and

[027-099]look for you.”

[027-100]Maskull grew more and more uneasy in his mind. “This seems to me to be a

[027-101]man’s journey,” he said. “I think it would be better for you not to

[027-102]come, Gleameil.”

[027-103]“I am not to be dissuaded,” she replied.

[027-104]He stroked his beard in perplexity. “Is it time to start?”

[027-105]“It wants four hours to sunset, and we shall need all that.”

[027-106]Maskull sighed. “I’ll go to the mouth of the creek, and wait there for

[027-107]you and the raft. You will wish to make your farewells, Gleameil.”

[027-108]He then clasped Polecrab by the hand. “Adieu, fisherman!”

[027-109]“You have repaid me well for my answers,” said the old man gruffly. “But

[027-110]it’s not your fault, and in Shaping’s world the worst things happen.”

[027-111]The eldest boy came close to Maskull, and frowned at him. “Farewell, big

[027-112]man!” he said. “But guard my mother well, as well as you are well able

[027-113]to, or I shall follow you, and kill you.”

[027-114]Maskull walked slowly along the creek bank till he came to the bend. The

[027-115]glorious sunshine, and the sparkling, brilliant sea then met his eyes

[027-116]again; and all melancholy was swept out of his mind. He continued as far

[027-117]as the seashore, and issuing out of the shadows of the forest, strolled

[027-118]on to the sands, and sat down in the full sunlight. The radiance of

[027-119]Alppain had long since disappeared. He drank in the hot, invigorating

[027-120]wind, listened to the hissing waves, and stared over the coloured sea

[027-121]with its pinnacles and currents, at Swaylone’s Island.

[027-122]“What music can that be, which tears a wife and mother away from all she

[027-123]loves the most?” he meditated. “It sounds unholy. Will it tell me what I

[027-124]want to know? Can it?”

[027-125]In a little while he became aware of a movement behind him, and, turning

[027-126]his head, he saw the raft floating along the creek, toward the open sea.

[027-127]Polecrab was standing upright, propelling it with a rude pole. He passed

[027-128]by Maskull, without looking at him, or making any salutation, and

[027-129]proceeded out to sea.

[027-130]While he was wondering at this strange behaviour, Gleameil and the boys

[027-131]came in sight, walking along the bank of the inlet. The eldest-born was

[027-132]holding her hand, and talking; and the other two were behind. She was

[027-133]calm and smiling, but seemed abstracted.

[027-134]“What is your husband doing with the raft?” asked Maskull.

[027-135]“He’s putting it in position and we shall wade out and join it,” she

[027-136]answered, in her low-toned voice.

[027-137]“But how shall we make the island, without oars or sails?”

[027-138]“Don’t you see that current running away from land? See, he is

[027-139]approaching it. That will take us straight there.”

[027-140]“But how can you get back?”

[027-141]“There is a way; but we need not think of that today.”

[027-142]“Why shouldn’t I come too?” demanded the eldest boy.

[027-143]“Because the raft won’t carry three. Maskull is a heavy man.”

[027-144]“It doesn’t matter,” said the boy. “I know where there is wood for

[027-145]another raft. As soon as you have gone, I shall set to work.”

[027-146]Polecrab had by this time manoeuvred his flimsy craft to the position he

[027-147]desired, within a few yards of the current, which at that point made a

[027-148]sharp bend from the east. He shouted out some words to his wife and

[027-149]Maskull. Gleameil kissed her children convulsively, and broke down a

[027-150]little. The eldest boy bit his lip till it bled, and tears glistened in

[027-151]his eyes; but the younger children stared wide-eyed, and displayed no

[027-152]emotion.

[027-153]Gleameil now walked into the sea, followed by Maskull. The water covered

[027-154]first their ankles, then their knees, but when it came as high as their

[027-155]waists, they were close on the raft. Polecrab let himself down into the

[027-156]water, and assisted his wife to climb over the side. When she was up,

[027-157]she bent down and kissed him. No words were exchanged. Maskull scrambled

[027-158]up on to the front part of the raft. The woman sat cross-legged in the

[027-159]stern, and seized the pole.

[027-160]Polecrab shoved them off toward the current, while she worked her pole

[027-161]until they had got within its power. The raft immediately began to

[027-162]travel swiftly away from land, with a smooth, swaying motion.

[027-163]The boys waved from the shore. Gleameil responded; but Maskull turned

[027-164]his back squarely to land, and gazed ahead. Polecrab was wading back to

[027-165]the shore.

[027-166]For upward of an hour Maskull did not change his position by an inch. No

[027-167]sound was heard but the splashing of the strange waves all around them,

[027-168]and the streamlike gurgle of the current, which threaded its way

[027-169]smoothly through the tossing, tumultuous sea. From their pathway of

[027-170]safety, the beautiful dangers surrounding them were an exhilarating

[027-171]experience. The air was fresh and clean, and the heat from Branchspell,

[027-172]now low in the west, was at last endurable. The riot of sea colors had

[027-173]long since banished all sadness and anxiety from his heart. Yet he felt

[027-174]such a grudge against the woman for selfishly forsaking those who should

[027-175]have been dear to her that he could not bring himself to begin a

[027-176]conversation.

[027-177]But when, over the now enlarged shape of the dark island, he caught

[027-178]sight of a long chain of lofty, distant mountains, glowing salmon-pink

[027-179]in the evening sunlight, he felt constrained to break the silence by

[027-180]inquiring what they were.

[027-181]“It is Lichstorm,” said Gleameil.

[027-182]Maskull asked no questions about it; but in turning to address her, his

[027-183]eyes had rested on the rapidly receding Wombflash Forest, and he

[027-184]continued to stare at that. They had travelled about eight miles, and

[027-185]now he could better estimate the enormous height of the trees.

[027-186]Overtopping them, far away, he saw Sant; and he fancied, but was not

[027-187]quite sure, that he could distinguish Disscourn as well.

[027-188]“Now that we are alone in a strange place,” said Gleameil, averting her

[027-189]head, and looking down over the side of the raft into the water, “tell

[027-190]me what you thought of Polecrab.”

[027-191]Maskull paused before answering. “He seemed to me like a mountain

[027-192]wrapped in cloud. You see the lower buttresses, and think that is all.

[027-193]But then, high up, far above the clouds, you suddenly catch sight of

[027-194]more mountain—and even then it is not the top.”
