# Section 34

[034-001]“The sick must have their way,” said Maskull. Lifting aer bodily in his

[034-002]arms, he walked quickly along for another hundred yards or so. They then

[034-003]emerged from the tunnel and faced a world the parallel of which he had

[034-004]never set eyes upon before.

[034-005]“Set me down!” directed Leehallfae feebly. “Here I’ll die.”

[034-006]Maskull obeyed, and laid aer down at full length on the rocky ground.

[034-007]The phaen raised aerself with difficulty on one arm, and stared with

[034-008]fast-glazing eyes at the mystic landscape.

[034-009]Maskull looked too, and what he saw was a vast, undulating plain,

[034-010]lighted as if by the moon—but there was of course no moon, and there

[034-011]were no shadows. He made out running streams in the distance. Beside

[034-012]them were trees of a peculiar kind; they were rooted in the ground, but

[034-013]the branches also were aerial roots, and there were no leaves. No other

[034-014]plants could be seen. The soil was soft, porous rock, resembling pumice.

[034-015]Beyond a mile or two in any direction the light merged into obscurity.

[034-016]At their back a great rocky wall extended on either hand; but it was not

[034-017]square like a wall, but full of bays and promontories like an indented

[034-018]line of sea cliffs. The roof of this huge underworld was out of sight.

[034-019]Here and there a mighty shaft of naked rock, fantastically weathered,

[034-020]towered aloft into the gloom, doubtless serving to support the roof.

[034-021]There were no colours—every detail of the landscape was black, white, or

[034-022]grey. The scene appeared so still, so solemn and religious, that all his

[034-023]feelings quieted down to absolute tranquillity.

[034-024]Leehallfae fell back suddenly. Maskull dropped on his knees, and

[034-025]helplessly watched the last flickerings of aer spirit, going out like a

[034-026]candle in foul air. Death came.... He closed the eyes. The awful grin of

[034-027]Crystalman immediately fastened upon the phaen’s dead features.

[034-028]While Maskull was still kneeling, he became conscious of someone

[034-029]standing beside him. He looked up quickly and saw a man, but did not at

[034-030]once rise.

[034-031]“Another phaen dead,” said the newcomer in a grave, toneless, and

[034-032]intellectual voice.

[034-033]Maskull got up.

[034-034]The man was short and thickset but emaciated. His forehead was not

[034-035]disfigured by any organs. He was middle-aged. The features were

[034-036]energetic and rather coarse—yet it seemed to Maskull as though a pure,

[034-037]hard life had done something toward refining them. His sanguine eyes

[034-038]carried a twisted, puzzled look; some unanswerable problem was

[034-039]apparently in the forefront of his brain. His face was hairless; the

[034-040]hair of his head was short and manly; his brow was wide. He was clothed

[034-041]in a black, sleeveless robe, and bore a long staff in his hand. There

[034-042]was an air of cleanness and austerity about the whole man that was

[034-043]attractive.

[034-044]He went on speaking dispassionately to Maskull, and, while doing so,

[034-045]kept passing his hand reflectively over his cheeks and chin. “They all

[034-046]find their way here to die. They come from Matterplay. There they live

[034-047]to an incredible age. Partly on that account, and partly because of

[034-048]their spontaneous origin, they regard themselves as the favoured

[034-049]children of Faceny. But when they come here to find him, they die at

[034-050]once.”

[034-051]“I think this one is the last of the race. But whom do I speak to?”

[034-052]“I am Corpang. Who are you, where do you come from, and what are you

[034-053]doing here?”

[034-054]“My name is Maskull. My home is on the other side of the universe. As

[034-055]for what I am doing here—I accompanied Leehallfae, that phaen, from

[034-056]Matterplay.”

[034-057]“But a man doesn’t accompany a phaen out of friendship. What do you want

[034-058]in Threal?”

[034-059]“Then this is Threal?”

[034-060]“Yes.”

[034-061]Maskull remained silent.

[034-062]Corpang studied his face with rough, curious eyes. “Are you ignorant, or

[034-063]merely reticent, Maskull?”

[034-064]“I came here to ask questions, and not to answer them.”

[034-065]The stillness of the place was almost oppressive. Not a breeze stirred,

[034-066]and not a sound came through the air. Their voices had been lowered, as

[034-067]though they were in a cathedral.

[034-068]“Then do you want my society, or not?” asked Corpang.

[034-069]“Yes, if you can fit in with my mood, which is—not to talk about

[034-070]myself.”

[034-071]“But you must at least tell me where you want to go to.”

[034-072]“I want to see what is to be seen here, and then go on to Lichstorm.”

[034-073]“I can guide you through, if that’s all you want. Come, let us start.”

[034-074]“First let’s do our duty and bury the dead, if possible.”

[034-075]“Turn around,” directed Corpang.

[034-076]Maskull looked around quickly. Leehallfae’s body had disappeared.

[034-077]“What does this mean—what has happened?”

[034-078]“The body has returned to whence it came. There was nowhere here for it

[034-079]to be, so it has vanished. No burial will be required.”

[034-080]“Was the phaen an illusion, then?”

[034-081]“In no sense.”

[034-082]“Well, explain quickly, then, what has taken place. I seem to be going

[034-083]mad.”

[034-084]“There’s nothing unintelligible in it, if you’ll only listen calmly. The

[034-085]phaen belonged, body and soul, to the outside, visible world—to Faceny.

[034-086]This underworld is not Faceny’s world, but Thire’s, and Faceny’s

[034-087]creatures cannot breathe its atmosphere. As this applies not only to

[034-088]whole bodies, but even to the last particles of bodies, the phaen has

[034-089]dissolved into Nothingness.”

[034-090]“But don’t you and I belong to the outside world too?”

[034-091]“We belong to all three worlds.”

[034-092]“What three worlds—what do you mean?”

[034-093]“There are three worlds,” said Corpang composedly. “The first is

[034-094]Faceny’s, the second is Amfuse’s, the third is Thire’s. From him Threal

[034-095]gets its name.”

[034-096]“But this is mere nomenclature. In what sense are there three worlds?”

[034-097]Corpang passed his hand over his forehead. “All this we can discuss as

[034-098]we go along. It’s a torment to me to be standing still.”

[034-099]Maskull stared again at the spot where Leehallfae’s body had lain, quite

[034-100]bewildered at the extraordinary disappearance. He could scarcely tear

[034-101]himself away from the place, so mysterious was it. Not until Corpang

[034-102]called to him a second time did he make up his mind to follow him.

[034-103]They set off from the rock wall straight across the airlit plain,

[034-104]directing their course toward the nearest trees. The subdued light, the

[034-105]absence of shadows, the massive shafts, springing grey-white out of the

[034-106]jetlike ground, the fantastic trees, the absence of a sky, the deathly

[034-107]silence, the knowledge that he was underground—the combination of all

[034-108]these things predisposed Maskull’s mind to mysticism, and he prepared

[034-109]himself with some anxiety to hear Corpang’s explanation of the land and

[034-110]its wonders. He already began to grasp that the reality of the outside

[034-111]world and the reality of this world were two quite different things.

[034-112]“In what sense are there three worlds?” he demanded, repeating his

[034-113]former question.

[034-114]Corpang smote the end of his staff on the ground. “First of all,

[034-115]Maskull, what is your motive for asking? If it’s mere intellectual

[034-116]curiosity, tell me, for we mustn’t play with awful matters.”

[034-117]“No, it isn’t that,” said Maskull slowly. “I’m not a student. My journey

[034-118]is no holiday tour.”

[034-119]“Isn’t there blood on your soul?” asked Corpang, eying him intently.

[034-120]The blood rose steadily to Maskull’s face, but in that light it caused

[034-121]it to appear black.

[034-122]“Unfortunately there is, and not a little.”

[034-123]The other’s face was all wrinkles, but he made no comment.

[034-124]“And so you see,” went on Maskull, with a short laugh, “I’m in the very

[034-125]best condition for receiving your instruction.”

[034-126]Corpang still paused. “Underneath your crimes I see a man,” he said,

[034-127]after a few minutes. “On that account, and because we are commanded to

[034-128]help one another, I won’t leave you at present, though I little thought

[034-129]to be walking with a murderer.... Now to your question.... Whatever a

[034-130]man sees with his eyes, Maskull, he sees in three ways—length, breadth,

[034-131]depth. Length is existence, breadth is relation, depth is feeling.”

[034-132]“Something of the sort was told me by Earthrid, the musician, who came

[034-133]from Threal.”

[034-134]“I don’t know him. What else did he tell you?”

[034-135]“He went on to apply it to music. Continue, and pardon the

[034-136]interruption.”

[034-137]“These three states of perception are the three worlds. Existence is

[034-138]Faceny’s world, relation is Amfuse’s world, feeling is Thire’s world.”

[034-139]“Can’t we come down to hard facts?” said Maskull, frowning. “I

[034-140]understand no more than I did before what you mean by three worlds.”

[034-141]“There are no harder facts than the ones I am giving you. The first

[034-142]world is visible, tangible Nature. It was created by Faceny out of

[034-143]nothingness, and therefore we call it Existence.”

[034-144]“That I understand.”

[034-145]“The second world is Love—by which I don’t mean lust. Without love,

[034-146]every individual would be entirely self-centred and unable deliberately

[034-147]to act on others. Without love, there would be no sympathy—not even

[034-148]hatred, anger, or revenge would be possible. These are all imperfect and

[034-149]distorted forms of pure love. Interpenetrating Faceny’s world of Nature,

[034-150]therefore, we have Amfuse’s world of Love, or Relation.”

[034-151]“What grounds have you for assuming that this so-called second world is

[034-152]not contained in the first?”

[034-153]“They are contradictory. A natural man lives for himself; a lover lives

[034-154]for others.”

[034-155]“It may be so. It’s rather mystical. But go on—who is Thire?”

[034-156]“Length and breadth together without depth give flatness. Life and love

[034-157]without feeling produce shallow, superficial natures. Feeling is the

[034-158]need of men to stretch out toward their creator.”

[034-159]“You mean prayer and worship?”

[034-160]“I mean intimacy with Thire. This feeling is not to be found in either

[034-161]the first or second world, therefore it is a third world. Just as depth

[034-162]is the line between object and subject, feeling is the line between

[034-163]Thire and man.”

[034-164]“But what is Thire himself?”

[034-165]“Thire is the afterworld.”

[034-166]“I still don’t understand,” said Maskull. “Do you believe in three

[034-167]separate gods, or are these merely three ways of regarding one God?”

[034-168]“There are three gods, for they are mutually antagonistic. Yet they are

[034-169]somehow united.”

[034-170]Maskull reflected a while. “How have you arrived at these conclusions?”

[034-171]“None other are possible in Threal, Maskull.”

[034-172]“Why in Threal—what is there peculiar here?”

[034-173]“I will show you presently.”

[034-174]They walked on for above a mile in silence, while Maskull digested what

[034-175]had been said. When they came to the first trees, which grew along the

[034-176]banks of a small stream of transparent water, Corpang halted.

[034-177]“That bandage around your forehead has long been unnecessary,” he

[034-178]remarked.

[034-179]Maskull removed it. He found that the line of his brow was smooth and

[034-180]uninterrupted, as it had never yet been since his arrival in Tormance.

[034-181]“How has this come about—and how did you know it?”

[034-182]“They were Faceny’s organs. They have vanished, just as the phaen’s body

[034-183]vanished.”

[034-184]Maskull kept rubbing his forehead. “I feel more human without them. But

[034-185]why isn’t the rest of my body affected?”

[034-186]“Because its living will contains the element of Thire.”

[034-187]“Why are we stopping here?”

[034-188]Corpang broke off the tip of one of the aerial roots of a tree, and

[034-189]proffered it to him. “Eat this, Maskull.”

[034-190]“For food, or something else?”

[034-191]“Food for body and soul.”

[034-192]Maskull bit into the root. It was white and hard; its white sap was

[034-193]bleeding. It had no taste, but after eating it, he experienced a change

[034-194]of perception. The landscape, without alteration of light or outline,

[034-195]became several degrees more stern and sacred. When he looked at Corpang

[034-196]he was impressed by his aspect of Gothic awfulness, but the perplexed

[034-197]expression was still in his eyes.

[034-198]“Do you spend all your time here, Corpang?”

[034-199]“Occasionally I go above, but not often.”

[034-200]“What fastens you to this gloomy world?”

[034-201]“The search for Thire.”

[034-202]“Then it’s still a search?”

[034-203]“Let us walk on.”

[034-204]As they resumed their journey across the dim, gradually rising plain,

[034-205]the conversation became even more earnest in character than before.

[034-206]“Although I was not born here,” proceeded Corpang, “I’ve lived here for

[034-207]twenty-five years, and during all that time I have been drawing nearer

[034-208]to Thire, as I hope. But there is this peculiarity about it—the first

[034-209]stages are richer in fruit and more promising than the later ones. The

[034-210]longer a man seeks Thire, the more he seems to absent himself. In the

[034-211]beginning he is felt and known, sometimes as a shape, sometimes as a

[034-212]voice, sometimes an overpowering emotion. Later on all is dry, dark, and

[034-213]harsh in the soul. Then you would think that Thire was a million miles

[034-214]off.”
