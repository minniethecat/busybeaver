# Section 46

[046-001]Nightspore felt a sickening shame in his soul as he looked on at that

[046-002]spectacle. His exaltation had long since vanished. He bit his nails, and

[046-003]understood why Krag was waiting for him below.

[046-004]He mounted slowly to the fifth window. The pressure of air against him

[046-005]was as strong as a full gale, divested of violence and irregularity, so

[046-006]that he was not for an instant suffered to relax his efforts.

[046-007]Nevertheless, not a breath stirred.

[046-008]Looking through the window, he was startled by a new sight. The sphere

[046-009]was still there, but between it and the Muspel-world in which he was

[046-010]standing he perceived a dim, vast shadow, without any distinguishable

[046-011]shape, but somehow throwing out a scent of disgusting sweetness.

[046-012]Nightspore knew that it was Crystalman. A flood of fierce light—but it

[046-013]was not light, but passion—was streaming all the time from Muspel to the

[046-014]Shadow, and through it. When, however, it emerged on the other side,

[046-015]which was the sphere, the light was altered in character. It became

[046-016]split, as by a prism, into the two forms of life which he had previously

[046-017]seen—the green corpuscles and the whirls. What had been fiery spirit but

[046-018]a moment ago was now a disgusting mass of crawling, wriggling

[046-019]individuals, each whirl of pleasure-seeking will having, as nucleus, a

[046-020]fragmentary spark of living green fire. Nightspore recollected the back

[046-021]rays of Starkness, and it flashed across him with the certainty of truth

[046-022]that the green sparks were the back rays, and the whirls the forward

[046-023]rays, of Muspel. The former were trying desperately to return to their

[046-024]place of origin, but were overpowered by the brute force of the latter,

[046-025]which wished only to remain where they were. The individual whirls were

[046-026]jostling and fighting with, and even devouring, each other. This created

[046-027]pain, but, whatever pain they felt, it was always pleasure that they

[046-028]sought. Sometimes the green sparks were strong enough for a moment to

[046-029]move a little way in the direction of Muspel; the whirls would then

[046-030]accept the movement, not only without demur, but with pride and

[046-031]pleasure, as if it were their own handiwork—but they never saw beyond

[046-032]the Shadow, they thought that they were travelling toward it. The

[046-033]instant the direct movement wearied them, as contrary to their whirling

[046-034]nature, they fell again to killing, dancing, and loving.

[046-035]Nightspore had a foreknowledge that the sixth window would prove to be

[046-036]the last. Nothing would have kept him from ascending to it, for he

[046-037]guessed that the nature of Crystalman himself would there become

[046-038]manifest. Every step upward was like a bloody life-and-death struggle.

[046-039]The stairs nailed him to the ground; the air pressure caused blood to

[046-040]gush from his nose and ears; his head clanged like an iron bell. When he

[046-041]had fought his way up a dozen steps, he found himself suddenly at the

[046-042]top; the staircase terminated in a small, bare chamber of cold stone,

[046-043]possessing a single window. On the other side of the apartment another

[046-044]short flight of stairs mounted through a trap, apparently to the roof of

[046-045]the building. Before ascending these stairs, Nightspore hastened to the

[046-046]window and stared out.

[046-047]The shadow form of Crystalman had drawn much closer to him, and filled

[046-048]the whole sky, but it was not a shadow of darkness, but a bright shadow.

[046-049]It had neither shape, nor colour, yet it in some way suggested the

[046-050]delicate tints of early morning. It was so nebulous that the sphere

[046-051]could be clearly distinguished through it; in extension, however, it was

[046-052]thick. The sweet smell emanating from it was strong, loathsome, and

[046-053]terrible; it seemed to spring from a sort of loose, mocking slime

[046-054]inexpressibly vulgar and ignorant.

[046-055]The spirit stream from Muspel flashed with complexity and variety. It

[046-056]was not below individuality, but above it. It was not the One, or the

[046-057]Many, but something else far beyond either. It approached Crystalman,

[046-058]and entered his body—if that bright mist could be called a body. It

[046-059]passed right through him, and the passage caused him the most exquisite

[046-060]pleasure. _The Muspel-stream was Crystalman’s food_. The stream emerged

[046-061]from the other side on to the sphere, in a double condition. Part of it

[046-062]reappeared intrinsically unaltered, but shivered into a million

[046-063]fragments. These were the green corpuscles. In passing through

[046-064]Crystalman they had escaped absorption by reason of their extreme

[046-065]minuteness. The other part of the stream had not escaped. Its fire had

[046-066]been abstracted, its cement was withdrawn, and, after being fouled and

[046-067]softened by the horrible sweetness of the host, it broke into

[046-068]individuals, which were the whirls of living will.

[046-069]Nightspore shuddered. He comprehended at last how the whole world of

[046-070]will was doomed to eternal anguish in order that one Being might feel

[046-071]joy.

[046-072]Presently he set foot on the final flight leading to the roof; for he

[046-073]remembered vaguely that now only that remained.

[046-074]Halfway up, he fainted—but when he recovered consciousness he persisted

[046-075]as though nothing had happened to him. As soon as his head was above the

[046-076]trap, breathing the free air, he had the same physical sensation as a

[046-077]man stepping out of water. He pulled his body up, and stood expectantly

[046-078]on the stone-floored roof, looking round for his first glimpse of

[046-079]Muspel.

[046-080]There was nothing.

[046-081]He was standing upon the top of a tower, measuring not above fifteen

[046-082]feet each way. Darkness was all around him. He sat down on the stone

[046-083]parapet, with a sinking heart; a heavy foreboding possessed him.

[046-084]Suddenly, without seeing or hearing anything, he had the distinct

[046-085]impression that the darkness around him, on all four sides, was

[046-086]grinning.... As soon as that happened, he understood that he was wholly

[046-087]surrounded by Crystalman’s world, and that Muspel consisted of himself

[046-088]and the stone tower on which he was sitting.

[046-089]Fire flashed in his heart.... Millions upon millions of grotesque,

[046-090]vulgar, ridiculous, sweetened individuals—once Spirit—were calling out

[046-091]from their degradation and agony for salvation from Muspel.... To answer

[046-092]that cry there was only himself... and Krag waiting below... and

[046-093]Surtur—But where was Surtur?

[046-094]The truth forced itself on him in all its cold, brutal reality. Muspel

[046-095]was no all-powerful Universe, tolerating from pure indifference the

[046-096]existence side by side with it of another false world, which had no

[046-097]right to be. Muspel was fighting for its life—against all that is most

[046-098]shameful and frightful—against sin masquerading as eternal beauty,

[046-099]against baseness masquerading as Nature, against the Devil masquerading

[046-100]as God....

[046-101]Now he understood everything. The moral combat was no mock one, no

[046-102]Valhalla, where warriors are cut to pieces by day and feast by night;

[046-103]but a grim death struggle in which what is worse than death—namely,

[046-104]spiritual death—inevitably awaited the vanquished of Muspel.... By what

[046-105]means could he hold back from this horrible war!

[046-106]During those moments of anguish, all thoughts of Self—the corruption of

[046-107]his life on Earth—were scorched out of Nightspore’s soul, perhaps not

[046-108]for the first time.

[046-109]After sitting a long time, he prepared to descend. Without warning, a

[046-110]strange, wailing cry swept over the face of the world. Starting in awful

[046-111]mystery, it ended with such a note of low and sordid mockery that he

[046-112]could not doubt for a moment whence it originated. It was the voice of

[046-113]Crystalman.

[046-114]*****

[046-115]Krag was waiting for him on the island raft. He threw a

[046-116]stern glance at Nightspore.

[046-117]“Have you seen everything?”

[046-118]“The struggle is hopeless,” muttered Nightspore.

[046-119]“Did I not say I am the stronger?”

[046-120]“You may be the stronger, but he is the mightier.”

[046-121]“I am the stronger and the mightier. Crystalman’s Empire is but a shadow

[046-122]on the face of Muspel. But nothing will be done without the bloodiest

[046-123]blows.... What do you mean to do?”

[046-124]Nightspore looked at him strangely. “Are you not Surtur, Krag?”

[046-125]“Yes.”

[046-126]“Yes,” said Nightspore in a slow voice, without surprise. “But what is

[046-127]your name on Earth?”

[046-128]“It is pain.”

[046-129]“That, too, I must have known.”

[046-130]He was silent for a few minutes; then he stepped quietly onto the raft.

[046-131]Krag pushed off, and they proceeded into the darkness.
