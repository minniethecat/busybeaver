# Catice

## Role

Catice is introduced by [[spadevil]] as the successor to Maulger: "This is not Maulger, but Catice" [021]. Where [[spadevil|Spadevil]] preaches the new law of duty, Catice enforces it through direct physical challenge. He is the skeptical tester — the one who demands that faith prove itself in the body. His voice has an even harsher accent than [[spadevil|Spadevil]]'s, so that "the tympanum of [[maskull|Maskull]]'s ear was affected painfully" [021].

## In the Novel

Catice's most significant act is the flinting of [[maskull]]'s probe — striking out one of the two sensory organs with a flint stone. Before doing so, he challenges [[maskull|Maskull]] with terse, probing questions: "Is that the whole?" [021], "Do you destroy [[hator|Hator]], and all his generations, with a single word?" [021]. His skepticism is not hostile but exacting — he will not accept verbal profession without physical proof.

Catice picks up a flint and declares his intention: "With this stone I strike out one of your two probes. When you have but one, you will see with me, and you will recollect with [[spadevil|Spadevil]]. Choose you then the superior faith, and I shall obey your choice" [022]. The act is brutal but purposeful — Catice believes that truth emerges only through reduction and ordeal, not through comfort or consensus.

## Philosophical Significance

Catice represents the necessary role of skepticism in spiritual development. Unlike [[spadevil]], who offers doctrine, Catice offers a test. His function is to ensure that [[maskull|Maskull]]'s commitment is real — that it can survive pain and loss. In Lindsay's allegory, Catice is the gatekeeper who will not open the gate until the seeker has demonstrated genuine willingness to be transformed.
