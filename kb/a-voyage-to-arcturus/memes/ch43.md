# Section 43

[043-001]Maskull smiled rather bitterly. “At all events, I listen through no more

[043-002]keyholes. I have finished with life. I belong to nobody and nothing any

[043-003]more, from this time forward.”

[043-004]“Brave words, brave words! We shall see. Perhaps Crystalman will make

[043-005]one more attempt on you. There is still time for one more.”

[043-006]“Now I don’t understand you.”

[043-007]“You think you are thoroughly disillusioned, don’t you? Well, that may

[043-008]prove to be the last and strongest illusion of all.”

[043-009]The conversation ceased. They reached the foot of the landslip an hour

[043-010]later. Branchspell was steadily mounting the cloudless sky. It was

[043-011]approaching Sarclash, and it was an open question whether or not it

[043-012]would clear its peak. The heat was sweltering. The long, massive,

[043-013]saucer-shaped ridge behind them, with its terrific precipices, was

[043-014]glowing with bright morning colours. Adage, towering up many thousands

[043-015]of feet higher still, guarded the end of it like a lonely Colossus. In

[043-016]front of them, starting from where they stood, was a cool and enchanting

[043-017]wilderness of little lakes and forests. The water of the lakes was dark

[043-018]green; the forests were asleep, waiting for the rising of Alppain.

[043-019]“Are we now in Barey?” asked Maskull.

[043-020]“Yes—and there is one of the natives.”

[043-021]There was an ugly glint in his eye as he spoke the words, but Maskull

[043-022]did not see it.

[043-023]A man was leaning in the shade against one of the first trees,

[043-024]apparently waiting for them to come up. He was small, dark, and

[043-025]beardless, and was still in early manhood. He was clothed in a dark

[043-026]blue, loosely flowing robe, and wore a broad-brimmed slouch hat. His

[043-027]face, which was not disfigured by any special organs, was pale, earnest,

[043-028]and grave, yet somehow remarkably pleasing.

[043-029]Before a word was spoken, he warmly grasped Maskull’s hand, but even

[043-030]while he was in the act of doing so he threw a queer frown at Krag. The

[043-031]latter responded with a scowling grin.

[043-032]When he opened his mouth to speak, his voice was a vibrating baritone,

[043-033]but it was at the same time strangely womanish in its modulations and

[043-034]variety of tone.

[043-035]“I’ve been waiting for you here since sunrise,” he said. “Welcome to

[043-036]Barey, Maskull! Let’s hope you’ll forget your sorrows here, you over-

[043-037]tested man.”

[043-038]Maskull stared at him, not without friendliness. “What made you expect

[043-039]me, and how do you know my name?”

[043-040]The stranger smiled, which made his face very handsome. “I’m Gangnet. I

[043-041]know most things.”

[043-042]“Haven’t you a greeting for me too—Gangnet?” asked Krag, thrusting his

[043-043]forbidding features almost into the other’s face.

[043-044]“I know you, Krag. There are few places where you are welcome.”

[043-045]“And I know you, Gangnet—you man-woman.... Well, we are here together,

[043-046]and you must make what you can of it. We are going down to the Ocean.”

[043-047]The smile faded from Gangnet’s face. “I can’t drive you away, Krag—but I

[043-048]can make you the unwelcome third.”

[043-049]Krag threw back his head, and gave a loud, grating laugh. “That bargain

[043-050]suits me all right. As long as I have the substance, you may have the

[043-051]shadow, and much good may it do you.”

[043-052]“Now that it’s all arranged so satisfactorily,” said Maskull, with a

[043-053]hard smile, “permit me to say that I don’t desire any society at all at

[043-054]present.... You take too much for granted, Krag. You have played the

[043-055]false friend once already.... I presume I’m a free agent?”

[043-056]“To be a free man, one must have a universe of one’s own,” said Krag,

[043-057]with a jeering look. “What do you say, Gangnet—is this a free world?”

[043-058]“Freedom from pain and ugliness should be every man’s privilege,”

[043-059]returned Gangnet tranquilly. “Maskull is quite within his rights, and if

[043-060]you’ll engage to leave him I’ll do the same.”

[043-061]“Maskull can change face as often as he likes, but he won’t get rid of

[043-062]me so easily. Be easy on that point, Maskull.”

[043-063]“It doesn’t matter,” muttered Maskull. “Let everyone join in the

[043-064]procession. In a few hours I shall finally be free, anyhow, if what they

[043-065]say is true.”

[043-066]“I’ll lead the way,” said Gangnet. “You don’t know this country, of

[043-067]course, Maskull. When we get to the flat lands some miles farther down,

[043-068]we shall be able to travel by water, but at present we must walk, I

[043-069]fear.”

[043-070]“Yes, you fear—you fear!” broke out Krag, in a highpitched, scraping

[043-071]voice. “You eternal loller!”

[043-072]Maskull kept looking from one to the other in amazement. There seemed to

[043-073]be a determined hostility between the two, which indicated an intimate

[043-074]previous acquaintance.

[043-075]They set off through a wood, keeping close to its border, so that for a

[043-076]mile or more they were within sight of the long, narrow lake that flowed

[043-077]beside it. The trees were low and thin; their dolm-coloured leaves were

[043-078]all folded. There was no underbrush—they walked on clean, brown earth, A

[043-079]distant waterfall sounded. They were in shade, but the air was

[043-080]pleasantly warm. There were no insects to irritate them. The bright lake

[043-081]outside looked cool and poetic.

[043-082]Gangnet pressed Maskull’s arm affectionately. “If the bringing of you

[043-083]from your world had fallen to me, Maskull, it is here I would have

[043-084]brought you, and not to the scarlet desert. Then you would have escaped

[043-085]the dark spots, and Tormance would have appeared beautiful to you.”

[043-086]“And what then, Gangnet? The dark spots would have existed all the

[043-087]same.”

[043-088]“You could have seen them afterward. It makes all the difference whether

[043-089]one sees darkness through the light, or brightness through the shadows.”

[043-090]“A clear eye is the best. Tormance is an ugly world, and I greatly

[043-091]prefer to know it as it really is.”

[043-092]“The devil made it ugly, not Crystalman. These are Crystalman’s

[043-093]thoughts, which you see around you. He is nothing but Beauty and

[043-094]Pleasantness. Even Krag won’t have the effrontery to deny that.”

[043-095]“It’s very nice here,” said Krag, looking around him malignantly. “One

[043-096]only wants a cushion and half a dozen houris to complete it.”

[043-097]Maskull disengaged himself from Gangnet. “Last night, when I was

[043-098]struggling through the mud in the ghastly moonlight—then I thought the

[043-099]world beautiful.”

[043-100]“Poor Sullenbode!” said Gangnet, sighing.

[043-101]“What! You knew her?”

[043-102]“I know her through you. By mourning for a noble woman, you show your

[043-103]own nobility. I think all women are noble.”

[043-104]“There may be millions of noble women, but there’s only one Sullenbode.”

[043-105]“If Sullenbode can exist,” said Gangnet, “the world cannot be a bad

[043-106]place.”

[043-107]“Change the subject.... The world’s hard and cruel, and I am thankful to

[043-108]be leaving it.”

[043-109]“On one point, though, you both agree,” said Krag, smiling evilly.

[043-110]“Pleasure is good, and the cessation of pleasure is bad.”

[043-111]Gangnet glanced at him coldly. “We know your peculiar theories, Krag.

[043-112]You are very fond of them, but they are unworkable. The world could not

[043-113]go on being, without pleasure.”

[043-114]“So Gangnet thinks!” jeered Krag.

[043-115]They came to the end of the wood, and found themselves overlooking a

[043-116]little cliff. At the foot of it, about fifty feet below, a fresh series

[043-117]of lakes and forests commenced. Barey appeared to be one big mountain

[043-118]slope, built by nature into terraces. The lake along whose border they

[043-119]had been travelling was not banked at the end, but overflowed to the

[043-120]lower level in half a dozen beautiful, threadlike falls, white and

[043-121]throwing off spray. The cliff was not perpendicular, and the men found

[043-122]it easy to negotiate.

[043-123]At the base they entered another wood. Here it was much denser, and they

[043-124]had nothing but trees all around them. A clear brook rippled through the

[043-125]heart of it; they followed its bank.

[043-126]“It has occurred to me,” said Maskull, addressing Gangnet, “that Alppain

[043-127]may be my death. Is that so?”

[043-128]“These trees don’t fear Alppain, so why should you? Alppain is a

[043-129]wonderful, life-bringing sun.”

[043-130]“The reason I ask is—I’ve seen its afterglow, and it produced such

[043-131]violent sensations that a very little more would have proved too much.”

[043-132]“Because the forces were evenly balanced. When you see Alppain itself,

[043-133]it will reign supreme, and there will be no more struggling of wills

[043-134]inside you.”

[043-135]“And that, I may tell you beforehand, Maskull,” said Krag, grinning, “is

[043-136]Crystalman’s trump card.”

[043-137]“How do you mean?”

[043-138]“You’ll see. You’ll renounce the world so eagerly that you’ll want to

[043-139]stay in the world merely to enjoy your sensations.”

[043-140]Gangnet smiled. “Krag, you see, is hard to please. You must neither

[043-141]enjoy, nor renounce. What are you to do?”

[043-142]Maskull turned toward Krag. “It’s very odd, but I don’t understand your

[043-143]creed even yet. Are you recommending suicide?”

[043-144]Krag seemed to grow sallower and more repulsive every minute. “What,

[043-145]because they have left off stroking you?” he exclaimed, laughing and

[043-146]showing his discoloured teeth.

[043-147]“Whoever you are, and whatever you want,” said Maskull, “you seem very

[043-148]certain of yourself.”

[043-149]“Yes, you would like me to blush and stammer like a booby, wouldn’t you!

[043-150]That would be an excellent way of destroying lies.”

[043-151]Gangnet glanced toward the foot of one of the trees. He stooped and

[043-152]picked up two or three objects that resembled eggs.

[043-153]“To eat?” asked Maskull, accepting the offered gift.

[043-154]“Yes, eat them; you must be hungry. I want none myself, and one mustn’t

[043-155]insult Krag by offering him a pleasure—especially such a low pleasure.”

[043-156]Maskull knocked the ends off two of the eggs, and swallowed the liquid

[043-157]contents. They tasted rather alcoholic. Krag snatched the remaining egg

[043-158]out of his hand and flung it against a tree trunk, where it broke and

[043-159]stuck, a splash of slime.

[043-160]“I don’t wait to be asked, Gangnet.... Say, is there a filthier sight

[043-161]than a smashed pleasure?”

[043-162]Gangnet did not reply, but took Maskull’s arm.

[043-163]After they had alternately walked through forests and descended cliffs

[043-164]and slopes for upward of two hours, the landscape altered. A steep

[043-165]mountainside commenced and continued for at least a couple of miles,

[043-166]during which space the land must have dropped nearly four thousand feet,

[043-167]at a practically uniform gradient. Maskull had seen nothing like this

[043-168]immense slide of country anywhere. The hill slope carried an enormous

[043-169]forest on its back. This forest, however, was different from those they

[043-170]had hitherto passed through. The leaves of the trees were curled in

[043-171]sleep, but the boughs were so close and numerous that, but for the fact

[043-172]that they were translucent, the rays of the sun would have been

[043-173]completely intercepted. As it was, the whole forest was flooded with

[043-174]light, and this light, being tinged with the colour of the branches, was

[043-175]a soft and lovely rose. So gay, feminine, and dawnlike was the

[043-176]illumination, that Maskull’s spirits immediately started to rise,

[043-177]although he did not wish it.

[043-178]He checked himself, sighed, and grew pensive.

[043-179]“What a place for languishing eyes and necks of ivory, Maskull!” rasped

[043-180]Krag mockingly. “Why isn’t Sullenbode here?”

[043-181]Maskull gripped him roughly and flung him against the nearest tree. Krag

[043-182]recovered himself, and burst into a roaring laugh, seeming not a whit

[043-183]discomposed.

[043-184]“Still what I said—was it true or untrue?”

[043-185]Maskull gazed at him sternly. “You seem to regard yourself as a

[043-186]necessary evil. I’m under no obligation to go on with you any farther. I

[043-187]think we had better part.”

[043-188]Krag turned to Gangnet with an air of grotesque mock earnestness.

[043-189]“What do you say—do we part when Maskull pleases, or when I please?”

[043-190]“Keep your temper, Maskull,” said Gangnet, showing Krag his back. “I

[043-191]know the man better than you do. Now that he has fastened onto you

[043-192]there’s only one way of making him lose his hold, by ignoring him.

[043-193]Despise him—say nothing to him, don’t answer his questions. If you

[043-194]refuse to recognise his existence, he is as good as not here.”

[043-195]“I’m beginning to be tired of it all,” said Maskull. “It seems as if I

[043-196]shall add one more to my murders, before I have finished.”
