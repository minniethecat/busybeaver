# Faceny

## Definition

Faceny is a metaphysical entity discussed in the later chapters of the novel, described as the ultimate source of all individual streams of life. According to the teachings revealed to Maskull, all existence flows from Faceny's thoughts — "Life is the thoughts of Faceny" [032-182] — and the entire world of Tormance is understood as Faceny's creation: "There is only this one world — Faceny's" [032-137]. Each individual being is a partial expression of Faceny's mind, a "little Faceny" [033-010].

## In the Novel

Faceny is introduced through Maskull's conversation with [[Leehallfae]], the phantom-being of the third sex, in the later chapters of the journey. Leehallfae describes a lifetime spent searching for Faceny:

> [032-060] …the streams spring direct from Faceny. My whole life has been spent trying to find Faceny himself. I've hunted so long…

The concept develops through a series of revelations about the nature of existence on Tormance. All individual lives are understood as thoughts emanating from Faceny's face [033-012], and the physical world itself is the visible shape of Faceny's consciousness. The search for Faceny is thus the search for the origin and meaning of one's own existence.

In the novel's final philosophical passages [033-027], Maskull himself is described as approaching Faceny — the moment of drawing near to the source from which all individual being flows.

## Philosophical Meaning

Faceny represents the principle of emanation — the idea that all individual existence flows from a single divine source. In Lindsay's metaphysical system, Faceny stands alongside [[Shaping]] (the demiurgic creator of forms and pleasure) and [[Crystalman]] (the principle of material illusion) as one of the fundamental metaphysical entities that structure the reality of Tormance.

Unlike Shaping, who creates the world of sensory forms, or Crystalman, who embodies the material world's illusory nature, Faceny appears to be the most fundamental source — the consciousness from which existence itself emanates. The imagery of streams flowing from a single source, and of individual beings as "thoughts" in a divine mind, draws on Neoplatonic and Gnostic traditions that were influential in early twentieth-century philosophical literature.

The concept that each person is a "little Faceny" [033-010] suggests a participatory metaphysics in which every conscious being shares in the nature of the divine source. This resonates with the novel's broader theme that authentic existence requires turning away from Shaping's illusory pleasures and toward the original source of being.
