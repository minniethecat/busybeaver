# Section 44

[044-001]“I smell murder in the air,” exclaimed Krag, pretending to sniff. “But

[044-002]whose?”

[044-003]“Do as I say, Maskull. To bandy words with him is to throw oil on fire.”

[044-004]“I’ll say no more to anyone.... When do we get out of this accursed

[044-005]forest?”

[044-006]“It’s some way yet, but when we’re once out we can take to the water,

[044-007]and you will be able to rest, and think.”

[044-008]“And brood comfortably over your sufferings,” added Krag.

[044-009]None of the three men said anything more until they emerged into the

[044-010]open day. The slope of the forest was so steep that they were forced to

[044-011]run, rather than walk, and this would have prevented any conversation,

[044-012]even if they had otherwise felt inclined toward it. In less than half an

[044-013]hour they were through. A flat, open landscape lay stretched in front of

[044-014]them as far as they could see.

[044-015]Three parts of this country consisted of smooth water. It was a

[044-016]succession of large, low-shored lakes, divided by narrow strips of tree-

[044-017]covered land. The lake immediately before them had its small end to the

[044-018]forest. It was there about a third of a mile wide. The water at the

[044-019]sides and end was shallow, and choked with dolm-colored rushes; but in

[044-020]the middle, beginning a few yards from the shore, there was a

[044-021]perceptible current away from them. In view of this current, it was

[044-022]difficult to decide whether it was a lake or a river. Some little

[044-023]floating islands were in the shallows.

[044-024]“Is it here that we take to the water?” inquired Maskull.

[044-025]“Yes, here,” answered Gangnet.

[044-026]“But how?”

[044-027]“One of those islands will serve. It only needs to move it into the

[044-028]stream.”

[044-029]Maskull frowned. “Where will it carry us to?”

[044-030]“Come, get on, get on!” said Krag, laughing uncouthly. “The morning’s

[044-031]wearing away, and you have to die before noon. We are going to the

[044-032]Ocean.”

[044-033]“If you are omniscient, Krag, what is my death to be?”

[044-034]“Gangnet will murder you.”

[044-035]“You lie!” said Gangnet. “I wish Maskull nothing but good.”

[044-036]“At all events, he will be the cause of your death. But what does it

[044-037]matter? The great point is you are quitting this futile world.... Well,

[044-038]Gangnet, I see you’re as slack as ever. I suppose I must do the work.”

[044-039]He jumped into the lake and began to run through the shallow water,

[044-040]splashing it about. When he came to the nearest island, the water was up

[044-041]to his thighs. The island was lozenge-shaped, and about fifteen feet

[044-042]from end to end. It was composed of a sort of light brown peat; there

[044-043]was no form of living vegetation on its surface. Krag went behind it,

[044-044]and started shoving it toward the current, apparently without having

[044-045]unduly to exert himself. When it was within the influence of the stream

[044-046]the others waded out to him, and all three climbed on.

[044-047]The voyage began. The current was not travelling at more than two miles

[044-048]an hour. The sun glared down on their heads mercilessly, and there was

[044-049]no shade or prospect of shade. Maskull sat down near the edge, and

[044-050]periodically splashed water over his head. Gangnet sat on his haunches

[044-051]next to him. Krag paced up and down with short, quick steps, like an

[044-052]animal in a cage. The lake widened out more and more, and the width of

[044-053]the stream increased in proportion, until they seemed to themselves to

[044-054]be floating on the bosom of some broad, flowing estuary.

[044-055]Krag suddenly bent over and snatched off Gangnet’s hat, crushing it

[044-056]together in his hairy fist and throwing it far out into the stream.

[044-057]“Why should you disguise yourself like a woman?” he asked with a harsh

[044-058]guffaw—“Show Maskull your face. Perhaps he has seen it somewhere.”

[044-059]Gangnet did remind Maskull of someone, but he could not say of whom. His

[044-060]dark hair curled down to his neck, his brow was wide, lofty, and noble,

[044-061]and there was an air of serious sweetness about the whole man that was

[044-062]strangely appealing to the feelings.

[044-063]“Let Maskull judge,” he said with proud composure, “whether I have

[044-064]anything to be ashamed of.”

[044-065]“There can be nothing but magnificent thoughts in that head,” muttered

[044-066]Maskull, staring hard at him.

[044-067]“A capital valuation. Gangnet is the king of poets. But what happens

[044-068]when poets try to carry through practical enterprises?”

[044-069]“What enterprises?” asked Maskull, in astonishment.

[044-070]“What have you got on hand, Gangnet? Tell Maskull.”

[044-071]“There are two forms of practical activity,” replied Gangnet calmly.

[044-072]“One may either build up, or destroy.”

[044-073]“No, there’s a third species. One may steal—and not even know one is

[044-074]stealing. One may take the purse and leave the money.”

[044-075]Maskull raised his eyebrows. “Where have you two met before?”

[044-076]“I’m paying Gangnet a visit today, Maskull, but once upon a time Gangnet

[044-077]paid me a visit.”

[044-078]“Where?”

[044-079]“In my home—whatever that is. Gangnet is a common thief.”

[044-080]“You are speaking in riddles, and I don’t understand you. I don’t know

[044-081]either of you, but it’s clear that if Gangnet is a poet, you’re a

[044-082]buffoon. Must you go on talking? I want to be quiet.”

[044-083]Krag laughed, but said no more. Presently he lay down at full length,

[044-084]with his face to the sun, and in a few minutes was fast asleep, and

[044-085]snoring disagreeably. Maskull kept glancing over at his yellow,

[044-086]repulsive face with strong disfavour.

[044-087]Two hours passed. The land on either side was more than a mile distant.

[044-088]In front of them there was no land at all. Behind them, the Lichstorm

[044-089]Mountains were blotted out from view by a haze that had gathered

[044-090]together. The sky ahead, just above the horizon, began to be of a

[044-091]strange colour. It was an intense jale-blue. The whole northern

[044-092]atmosphere was stained with ulfire.

[044-093]Maskull’s mind grew disturbed. “Alppain is rising, Gangnet.”

[044-094]Gangnet smiled wistfully. “It begins to trouble you?”

[044-095]“It is so solemn—tragical, almost—yet it recalls me to Earth. Life was

[044-096]no longer important—but this is important.”

[044-097]“Daylight is night to this other daylight. Within half an hour you will

[044-098]be like a man who has stepped from a dark forest into the open day. Then

[044-099]you will ask yourself how you could have been blind.”

[044-100]The two men went on watching the blue sunrise. The entire sky in the

[044-101]north, halfway up to the zenith, was streaked with extraordinary

[044-102]colours, among which jale and dolm predominated. Just as the principal

[044-103]character of an ordinary dawn is mystery, the outstanding character of

[044-104]this dawn was wildness. It did not baffle the understanding, but the

[044-105]heart. Maskull felt no inarticulate craving to seize and perpetuate the

[044-106]sunrise, and make it his own. Instead of that, it agitated and tormented

[044-107]him, like the opening bars of a supernatural symphony.

[044-108]When he looked back to the south, Branchspell’s day had lost its glare,

[044-109]and he could gaze at the immense white sun without flinching. He

[044-110]instinctively turned to the north again, as one turns from darkness to

[044-111]light.

[044-112]“If those were Crystalman’s thoughts that you showed me before, Gangnet,

[044-113]these must be his feelings. I mean it literally. What I am feeling now,

[044-114]he must have felt before me.”

[044-115]“He is all feeling, Maskull—don’t you understand that?”

[044-116]Maskull was feeding greedily on the spectacle before him; he did not

[044-117]reply. His face was set like a rock, but his eyes were dim with the

[044-118]beginning of tears. The sky blazed deeper and deeper; it was obvious

[044-119]that Alppain was about to lift itself above the sea. The island had by

[044-120]this time floated past the mouth of the estuary. On three sides they

[044-121]were surrounded by water. The haze crept up behind them and shut out all

[044-122]sight of land. Krag was still sleeping—an ugly, wrinkled monstrosity.

[044-123]Maskull looked over the side at the flowing water. It had lost its dark

[044-124]green colour, and was now of a perfect crystal transparency.

[044-125]“Are we already on the Ocean, Gangnet?”

[044-126]“Yes.”

[044-127]“Then nothing remains except my death.”

[044-128]“Don’t think of death, but life.”

[044-129]“It’s growing brighter—at the same time, more sombre. Krag seems to be

[044-130]fading away....”

[044-131]“There is Alppain!” said Gangnet, touching his arm.

[044-132]The deep, glowing disk of the blue sun peeped above the sea. Maskull was

[044-133]struck to silence. He was hardly so much looking, as feeling. His

[044-134]emotions were unutterable. His soul seemed too strong for his body. The

[044-135]great blue orb rose rapidly out of the water, like an awful eye watching

[044-136]him.... it shot above the sea with a bound, and Alppain’s day commenced.

[044-137]“What do you feel?” Gangnet still held his arm.

[044-138]“I have set myself against the Infinite,” muttered Maskull.

[044-139]Suddenly his chaos of passions sprang together, and a wonderful idea

[044-140]swept through his whole being, accompanied by the intensest joy.

[044-141]“Why, Gangnet—I am nothing.”

[044-142]“No, you are nothing.”

[044-143]The mist closed in all around them. Nothing was visible except the two

[044-144]suns, and a few feet of sea. The shadows of the three men cast by

[044-145]Alppain were not black, but were composed of white daylight.

[044-146]“Then nothing can hurt me,” said Maskull with a peculiar smile.

[044-147]Gangnet smiled too. “How could it?”

[044-148]“I have lost my will; I feel as if some foul tumour had been scraped

[044-149]away, leaving me clean and free.”

[044-150]“Do you now understand life, Maskull?”

[044-151]Gangnet’s face was transfigured with an extraordinary spiritual beauty;

[044-152]he looked as if he had descended from heaven.

[044-153]“I understand nothing, except that I have no self any more. But this is

[044-154]life.”

[044-155]“Is Gangnet expatiating on his famous blue sun?” said a jeering voice

[044-156]above them. Looking up, they saw that Krag had got to his feet.

[044-157]They both rose. At the same moment the gathering mist began to obscure

[044-158]Alppain’s disk, changing it from blue to a vivid jale.

[044-159]“What do you want with us, Krag?” asked Maskull with simple composure.

[044-160]Krag looked at him strangely for a few seconds. The water lapped around

[044-161]them.

[044-162]“Don’t you comprehend, Maskull, that your death has arrived?”

[044-163]Maskull made no response. Krag rested an arm lightly on his shoulder,

[044-164]and suddenly he felt sick and faint. He sank to the ground, near the

[044-165]edge of the island raft. His heart was thumping heavily and queerly; its

[044-166]beating reminded him of the drum taps. He gazed languidly at the

[044-167]rippling water, and it seemed to him as if he could see right through

[044-168]it... away, away down... to a strange fire....

[044-169]The water disappeared. The two suns were extinguished. The island was

[044-170]transformed into a cloud, and Maskull—alone on it—was floating through

[044-171]the atmosphere.... Down below, it was all fire—the fire of Muspel. The

[044-172]light mounted higher and higher, until it filled the whole world....

[044-173]He floated toward an immense perpendicular cliff of black rock, without

[044-174]top or bottom. Halfway up it Krag, suspended in midair, was dealing

[044-175]terrific blows at a blood-red spot with a huge hammer. The rhythmical,

[044-176]clanging sounds were hideous.

[044-177]Presently Maskull made out that these sounds were the familiar drum

[044-178]beats. “What are you doing, Krag?” he asked.

[044-179]Krag suspended his work, and turned around.

[044-180]“Beating on your heart, Maskull,” was his grinning response.

[044-181]*****

[044-182]The cliff and Krag vanished. Maskull saw Gangnet struggling in the

[044-183]air—but it was not Gangnet—it was Crystalman. He seemed to be trying to

[044-184]escape from the Muspel-fire, which kept surrounding and licking him,

[044-185]whichever way he turned. He was screaming.... The fire caught him. He

[044-186]shrieked horribly. Maskull caught one glimpse of a vulgar, slobbering

[044-187]face—and then that too disappeared.

[044-188]*****

[044-189]He opened his eyes. The floating island was still faintly illuminated by

[044-190]Alppain. Krag was standing by his side, but Gangnet was no longer there.

[044-191]“What is this Ocean called?” asked Maskull, bringing out the words with

[044-192]difficulty.

[044-193]“Surtur’s Ocean.”

[044-194]Maskull nodded, and kept quiet for some time. He rested his face on his

[044-195]arm. “Where’s Nightspore?” he asked suddenly.

[044-196]Krag bent over him with a grave expression. “You are Nightspore.”
