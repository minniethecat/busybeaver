# Section 25

[025-001]About twenty miles distant, as he judged, directly opposite him, a long,

[025-002]low island stood up from the sea, black and not distinguished in

[025-003]outline. It was Swaylone’s Island. Maskull was less interested in that

[025-004]than in the blue sunset that glowed behind its back. Alppain had set,

[025-005]but the whole northern sky was plunged into the minor key by its

[025-006]afterlight. Branchspell in the zenith was white and overpowering, the

[025-007]day was cloudless and terrifically hot; but where the blue sun had sunk,

[025-008]a sombre shadow seemed to overhang the world. Maskull had a feeling of

[025-009]disintegration—just as if two chemically distinct forces were

[025-010]simultaneously acting upon the cells of his body. Since the afterglow of

[025-011]Alppain affected him like this, he thought it more than likely that he

[025-012]would never be able to face that sun itself, and go on living. Still,

[025-013]some modification might happen to him that would make it possible.

[025-014]The sea tempted him. He made up his mind to bathe, and at once walked

[025-015]toward the shore. The instant he stepped outside the shadow line of the

[025-016]forest trees, the blinding rays of the sun beat down on him so savagely

[025-017]that for a few minutes he felt sick and his head swam. He trod quickly

[025-018]across the sands. The orange-coloured parts were nearly hot enough to

[025-019]roast food, he judged, but the violet parts were like fire itself. He

[025-020]stepped on a patch in ignorance, and immediately jumped high into the

[025-021]air with a startled yell.

[025-022]The sea was voluptuously warm. It would not bear his weight, so he

[025-023]determined to try swimming. First of all he stripped off his skin

[025-024]garment, washed it thoroughly with sand and water, and laid it in the

[025-025]sun to dry. Then he scrubbed himself as well as he could and washed out

[025-026]his beard and hair. After that, he waded in a long way, until the water

[025-027]reached his breast, and took to swimming—avoiding the spouts as far as

[025-028]possible He found it no pastime. The water was everywhere of unequal

[025-029]density. In some places he could swim, in others he could barely save

[025-030]himself from drowning, in others again he could not force himself

[025-031]beneath the surface at all. There were no outward signs to show what the

[025-032]water ahead held in store for him. The whole business was most

[025-033]dangerous.

[025-034]He came out, feeling clean and invigorated. For a time he walked up and

[025-035]down the sands, drying himself in the hot sunshine and looking around

[025-036]him. He was a naked stranger in a huge, foreign, mystical world, and

[025-037]whichever way he turned, unknown and threatening forces were glaring at

[025-038]him. The gigantic, white, withering Branchspell, the awful, body-

[025-039]changing Alppain, the beautiful, deadly, treacherous sea, the dark and

[025-040]eerie Swaylone’s Island, the spirit-crushing forest out of which he had

[025-041]just escaped—to all these mighty powers, surrounding him on every side,

[025-042]what resources had he, a feeble, ignorant traveller from a tiny planet

[025-043]on the other side of space, to oppose, to avoid being utterly

[025-044]destroyed?... Then he smiled to himself. “I’ve already been here two

[025-045]days, and still I survive. I have luck—and with that one can balance the

[025-046]universe. But what is luck—a verbal expression, or a thing?”

[025-047]As he was putting on his skin, which was now dry, the answer came to

[025-048]him, and this time he was grave. “Surtur brought me here, and Surtur is

[025-049]watching over me. That is my ‘luck.’... But what is Surtur in this

[025-050]world?... How is he able to protect me against the blind and

[025-051]ungovernable forces of nature? Is he stronger than Nature?...”

[025-052]Hungry as he was for food, he was hungrier still for human society, for

[025-053]he wished to inquire about all these things. He asked himself which way

[025-054]he should turn his steps. There were only two ways; along the shore,

[025-055]either east or west. The nearest creek lay to the east, cutting the

[025-056]sands about a mile away. He walked toward it.

[025-057]The forest face was forbidding and enormously high. It was so squarely

[025-058]turned to the sea that it looked as though it had been planed by tools.

[025-059]Maskull strode along in the shade of the trees, but kept his head

[025-060]constantly turned away from them, toward the sea—there it was more

[025-061]cheerful. The creek, when he reached it, proved to be broad and flat-

[025-062]banked. It was not a river, but an arm of the sea. Its still, dark green

[025-063]water curved around a bend out of sight, into the forest. The trees on

[025-064]both banks overhung the water, so that it was completely in shadow.

[025-065]He went as far as the bend, beyond which another short reach appeared. A

[025-066]man was sitting on a narrow shelf of bank, with his feet in the water.

[025-067]He was clothed in a coarse, rough hide, which left his limbs bare. He

[025-068]was short, thick, and sturdy, with short legs and a long, powerful arms,

[025-069]terminating in hands of an extraordinary size. He was oldish. His face

[025-070]was plain, slablike, and expressionless; it was full of wrinkles, and

[025-071]walnut-coloured. Both face and head were bald, and his skin was tough

[025-072]and leathery. He seemed to be some sort of peasant, or fisherman; there

[025-073]was no trace in his face of thought for others, or delicacy of feeling.

[025-074]He possessed three eyes, of different colors—jade-green, blue, and

[025-075]ulfire.

[025-076]In front of him, riding on the water, moored to the bank, was an

[025-077]elementary raft, consisting of the branches of trees, clumsily corded

[025-078]together.

[025-079]Maskull addressed him. “Are you another of the wise men of the Wombflash

[025-080]Forest?”

[025-081]The man answered him in a gruff, husky voice, looking up as he did so.

[025-082]“I’m a fisherman. I know nothing about wisdom.”

[025-083]“What name do you go by?”

[025-084]“Polecrab. What’s yours?”

[025-085]“Maskull. If you’re a fisherman, you ought to have fish. I’m famishing.”

[025-086]Polecrab grunted, and paused a minute before answering.

[025-087]“There’s fish enough. My dinner is cooking in the sands now. It’s easy

[025-088]enough to get you some more.”

[025-089]Maskull found this a pleasant speech.

[025-090]“But how long will it take?” he asked.

[025-091]The man slid the palms of his hands together, producing a shrill,

[025-092]screeching noise. He lifted his feet from the water, and clambered onto

[025-093]the bank. In a minute or two a curious little beast came crawling up to

[025-094]his feet, turning its face and eyes up affectionately, like a dog. It

[025-095]was about two feet long, and somewhat resembled a small seal, but had

[025-096]six legs, ending in strong claws.

[025-097]“Arg, go fish!” said Polecrab hoarsely.

[025-098]The animal immediately tumbled off the bank into the water. It swam

[025-099]gracefully to the middle of the creek and made a pivotal dive beneath

[025-100]the surface, where it remained a great while.

[025-101]“Simple fishing,” remarked Maskull. “But what’s the raft for?”

[025-102]“To go to sea with. The best fish are out at sea. These are eatable.”

[025-103]“That arg seems a highly intelligent creature.”

[025-104]Polecrab grunted again. “I’ve trained close on a hundred of them. The

[025-105]bigheads learn best, but they’re slow swimmers. The narrowheads swim

[025-106]like eels, but can’t be taught. Now I’ve started interbreeding them—he’s

[025-107]one of them.”

[025-108]“Do you live here alone?”

[025-109]“No, I’ve got a wife and three boys. My wife’s sleeping somewhere, but

[025-110]where the lads are, Shaping knows.”

[025-111]Maskull began to feel very much at home with this unsophisticated being.

[025-112]“The raft’s all crazy,” he remarked, staring at it. “If you go far out

[025-113]in that, you’ve got more pluck than I have.”

[025-114]“I’ve been to Matterplay on it,” said Polecrab.

[025-115]The arg reappeared and started swimming to shore, but this time

[025-116]clumsily, as if it were bearing a heavy weight under the surface. When

[025-117]it landed at its master’s feet, they saw that each set of claws was

[025-118]clutching a fish—six in all. Polecrab took them from it. He proceeded to

[025-119]cut off the heads and tails with a sharp-edged stone which he picked up;

[025-120]these he threw to the arg, which devoured them without any fuss.

[025-121]Polecrab beckoned to Maskull to follow him and, carrying the fish,

[025-122]walked toward the open shore, by the same way that he had come. When

[025-123]they reached the sands, he sliced the fish, removed the entrails, and

[025-124]digging a shallow hole in a patch of violet sand, placed the remainder

[025-125]of the carcasses in it, and covered them over again. Then he dug up his

[025-126]own dinner. Maskull’s nostrils quivered at the savoury smell, but he was

[025-127]not yet to dine.

[025-128]Polecrab, turning to go with the cooked fish in his hands, said, “These

[025-129]are mine, not yours. When yours are done, you can come back and join me,

[025-130]supposing you want company.”

[025-131]“How soon will that be?”

[025-132]“About twenty minutes,” replied the fisherman, over his shoulder.

[025-133]Maskull sheltered himself in the shadows of the forest, and waited. When

[025-134]the time had approximately elapsed, he disinterred his meal, scorching

[025-135]his fingers in the operation, although it was only the surface of the

[025-136]sand which was so intensely hot. Then he returned to Polecrab.

[025-137]In the warm, still air and cheerful shade of the inlet, they munched in

[025-138]silence, looking from their food to the sluggish water, and back again.

[025-139]With every mouthful Maskull felt his strength returning. He finished

[025-140]before Polecrab, who ate like a man for whom time has no value. When he

[025-141]had done, he stood up.

[025-142]“Come and drink,” he said, in his husky voice.

[025-143]Maskull looked at him inquiringly.

[025-144]The man led him a little way into the forest, and walked straight up to

[025-145]a certain tree. At a convenient height in its trunk a hole had been

[025-146]tapped and plugged. Polecrab removed the plug and put his mouth to the

[025-147]aperture, sucking for quite a long time, like a child at its mother’s

[025-148]breast. Maskull, watching him, imagined that he saw his eyes growing

[025-149]brighter.

[025-150]When his own turn came to drink, he found the juice of the tree somewhat

[025-151]like coconut milk in flavour, but intoxicating. It was a new sort of

[025-152]intoxication, however, for neither his will not his emotions were

[025-153]excited, but only his intellect—and that only in a certain way. His

[025-154]thoughts and images were not freed and loosened, but on the contrary

[025-155]kept labouring and swelling painfully, until they reached the full

[025-156]beauty of an aperçu, which would then flame up in his consciousness,

[025-157]burst, and vanish. After that, the whole process started over again. But

[025-158]there was never a moment when he was not perfectly cool, and master of

[025-159]his senses. When each had drunk twice, Polecrab replugged the hole, and

[025-160]they returned to their bank.

[025-161]“Is it Blodsombre yet?” asked Maskull, sprawling on the ground, well

[025-162]content.

[025-163]Polecrab resumed his old upright sitting posture, with his feet in the

[025-164]water. “Just beginning,” was his hoarse response.

[025-165]“Then I must stay here till it’s over.... Shall we talk?”

[025-166]“We can,” said the other, without enthusiasm.

[025-167]Maskull glanced at him through half-closed lids, wondering if he were

[025-168]exactly what he seemed to be. In his eyes he thought he detected a wise

[025-169]light.

[025-170]“Have you travelled much, Polecrab?”

[025-171]“Not what you would call travelling.”

[025-172]“You tell me you’ve been to Matterplay—what kind of country is that?”

[025-173]“I don’t know. I went there to pick up flints.”

[025-174]“What countries lie beyond it?”

[025-175]“Threal comes next, as you go north. They say it’s a land of mystics...

[025-176]I don’t know.”

[025-177]“Mystics?”

[025-178]“So I’m told.... Still farther north there’s Lichstorm.”

[025-179]“Now we’re going far afield.”

[025-180]“There are mountains there—and altogether it must be a very dangerous

[025-181]place, especially for a full-blooded man like you. Take care of

[025-182]yourself.”

[025-183]“This is rather premature, Polecrab. How do you know I’m going there?”

[025-184]“As you’ve come from the south, I suppose you’ll go north.”

[025-185]“Well, that’s right enough,” said Maskull, staring hard at him. “But how

[025-186]do you know I’ve come from the south?”

[025-187]“Well, then, perhaps you haven’t—but there’s a look of Ifdawn about

[025-188]you.”
