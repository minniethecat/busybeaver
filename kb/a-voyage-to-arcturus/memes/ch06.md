[006-110]IT WAS DENSE NIGHT when Maskull awoke from his profound sleep. A wind

[006-111]was blowing against him, gentle but wall-like, such as he had never

[006-112]experienced on earth. He remained sprawling on the ground, as he was

[006-113]unable to lift his body because of its intense weight. A numbing pain,

[006-114]which he could not identify with any region of his frame, acted from now

[006-115]onward as a lower, sympathetic note to all his other sensations. It

[006-116]gnawed away at him continuously; sometimes it embittered and irritated

[006-117]him, at other times he forgot it.

[006-118]He felt something hard on his forehead. Putting his hand up, he

[006-119]discovered there a fleshy protuberance the size of a small plum, having

[006-120]a cavity in the middle, of which he could not feel the bottom. Then he

[006-121]also became aware of a large knob on each side of his neck, an inch

[006-122]below the ear.

[006-123]From the region of his heart, a tentacle had budded. It was as long as

[006-124]his arm, but thin, like whipcord, and soft and flexible.

[006-125]As soon as he thoroughly realised the significance of these new organs,

[006-126]his heart began to pump. Whatever might, or might not, be their use,

[006-127]they proved one thing—that he was in a new world.

[006-128]One part of the sky began to get lighter than the rest. Maskull cried

[006-129]out to his companions, but received no response. This frightened him. He

[006-130]went on shouting out, at irregular intervals—equally alarmed at the

[006-131]silence and at the sound of his own voice. Finally, as no answering hail

[006-133]lay quiet, waiting in cold blood for what might happen.

[006-134]In a short while he perceived dim shadows around him, but these were not

[006-135]his friends.
