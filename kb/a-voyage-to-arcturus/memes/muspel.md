# Muspel

## Definition

Muspel is the transcendent realm of ultimate reality in Lindsay's metaphysical system — a world of pure light and truth that exists beyond and above Crystalman's material creation. It is the destination toward which the novel's spiritual journey points, and the source of true being.

## In the Novel

Muspel is first named by Catice in Chapter 23: "Muspel" (023-109). The concept deepens in Chapter 24 when it is revealed that Krag came "to steal Muspel-fire, to give a deeper life to men" (024-037). Maskull himself puzzles over the name: "Muspel.... That's the name I've been trying to pronounce ever since I awoke" (024-040).

In Chapter 26, the narrator explains that many beings on Tormance contain "vestiges of Muspel which Shaping did not know how to destroy" (026-085), and in Chapter 28, these vestiges are described as compounded remnants that survived Crystalman's creative process (028-051).

## Philosophical Meaning

Muspel represents the Gnostic Pleroma — the fullness of divine reality from which the material world (Crystalman's creation) is a fall. The "Muspel-fire" that Krag seeks to bring to humanity is gnosis — direct knowledge of the divine — which alone can liberate beings from the illusion of material existence.

The "vestiges of Muspel" that survive in Tormance's inhabitants represent the divine spark present in all beings, which Lindsay's allegory suggests must be awakened through suffering and self-overcoming.
