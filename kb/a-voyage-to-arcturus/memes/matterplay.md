# Matterplay

## Location

Matterplay is a region of Tormance situated between Ifdawn and Sant, encountered by Maskull during his northward journey. It is described as a waypoint on the route to Sant, its inhabitants encountered as travelers or mentioned in connection with other regions.

## Description

Matterplay is characterized primarily through its cultural distinctiveness rather than its physical geography. The inhabitants of Matterplay are known for their unusual moral perspective: "I've already heard it said about Matterplay, that if you want to do something new, you must first unlearn what you know" [018-091].

The most striking feature of Matterplay's culture is its systematic inversion of values: "events of the world are considered most shameful in Matterplay" that would elsewhere be considered honorable, and vice versa [018-099]. This deliberate reversal of moral categories makes Matterplay one of Lindsay's most direct illustrations of philosophical relativism.

Digrung, a traveler Maskull encounters on the road to Sant, is from Matterplay [018-022]. His character exemplifies the region's distinctive outlook — a kind of innocent moral contrarianism that Maskull finds both puzzling and unsettling.

## Narrative Events

Maskull does not visit Matterplay directly; he encounters its influence through Digrung, a Matterplay native traveling toward Poolingdred. Through their conversation, Maskull learns of Matterplay's inverted moral system and is forced to confront the possibility that his own ethical framework is merely one among many possible arrangements [018-022].

Tydomin, ever the sharp commentator, remarks on the Matterplay people's reputation: "I suppose even you clever Matterplay people sometimes misjudge" [018-095] — suggesting both the intellectualism of Matterplay's culture and its limitations.

## Symbolic Significance

Matterplay is Lindsay's philosophical laboratory for the transvaluation of values — the Nietzschean concept that moral systems are human constructions that can be deliberately inverted. The region's name itself ("matter" + "play") suggests a playful approach to material reality, in contrast to the deadly seriousness with which other regions treat their moral codes.

The fact that Maskull only meets Matterplay's emissary (Digrung) rather than visiting the region itself is significant: Matterplay's influence is portable, philosophical rather than geographical. Its ideas travel with its people, challenging Maskull's assumptions wherever he encounters them.

This makes Matterplay different from other regions whose philosophy is tied to their physical environment (Poolingdred's clarity, Ifdawn's harshness, Wombflash's contemplative depths). Matterplay's philosophy is purely intellectual — a system of thought that can be carried, discussed, and contested anywhere.
