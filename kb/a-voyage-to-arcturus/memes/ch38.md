# Section 38

[038-001]“This is Sarclash—the second highest mountain in the land.”

[038-002]“Which is the highest, then?”

[038-003]“Adage. Between Sarclash and Adage there is a long ridge—very difficult

[038-004]in places. About halfway along the ridge, at the lowest point, lies the

[038-005]top of the Mornstab Pass, which goes through to Barey. Now you know the

[038-006]lay of the land.”

[038-007]“Does the woman Sullenbode live near here?”

[038-008]“Near enough.” Haunte grinned.

[038-009]He leaped out of the boat and, pushing past the others without ceremony,

[038-010]walked straight into the cave.

[038-011]Maskull followed, with Corpang at his heels. A few stone steps led to a

[038-012]doorway, curtained by the skin of some large beast. Their host pushed

[038-013]his way in, never offering to hold the skin aside for them. Maskull made

[038-014]no comment, but grabbed it with his fist and tugged it away from its

[038-015]fastenings to the ground. Haunte looked at the skin, and then stared

[038-016]hard at Maskull with his disagreeable smile, but neither said anything.

[038-017]The place in which they found themselves was a large oblong cavern, with

[038-018]walls, floor, and ceiling of natural rock. There were two doorways: that

[038-019]by which they had entered, and another of smaller size directly

[038-020]opposite. The cave was cold and cheerless; a damp draft passed from door

[038-021]to door. Many skins of wild animals lay scattered on the ground. A

[038-022]number of lumps of sun-dried flesh were hanging on a string along the

[038-023]wall, and a few bulging liquor skins reposed in a corner. There were

[038-024]tusks, horns, and bones everywhere. Resting against the wall were two

[038-025]short hunting spears, having beautiful crystal heads.

[038-026]Haunte set down the two male stones on the ground, near the farther

[038-027]door; thire light illuminated the whole cave. He then walked over to the

[038-028]meat and, snatching a large piece, began to gnaw it ravenously.

[038-029]“Are we invited to the feast?” asked Maskull.

[038-030]Haunte pointed to the hanging flesh and to the liquor skins, but did not

[038-031]pause in his chewing.

[038-032]“Where’s a cup?” inquired Maskull, lifting one of the skins.

[038-033]Haunte indicated a clay goblet lying on the floor. Maskull picked it up,

[038-034]undid the neck of the skin, and, resting it under his arm, filled the

[038-035]cup. Tasting the liquor, he discovered it to be raw spirit. He tossed

[038-036]off the draught, and then felt much better.

[038-037]The second cupful he proffered to Corpang. The latter took a single sip,

[038-038]swallowed it, and then passed the cup back without a word. He refused to

[038-039]drink again, as long as they were in the cave. Maskull finished the cup,

[038-040]and began to throw off care.

[038-041]Going to the meat line, he took down a large double handful, and sat

[038-042]down on a pile of skins to eat at his ease. The flesh was tough and

[038-043]coarse, but he had never tasted anything sweeter. He could not

[038-044]understand the flavour, which was not surprising in a world of strange

[038-045]animals. The meal proceeded in silence. Corpang ate sparingly, standing

[038-046]up, and afterward lay down on a bundle of furs. His bold eyes watched

[038-047]all the movements of the other two. Haunte had not drunk as yet.

[038-048]At last Maskull concluded his meal. He emptied another cup, sighed

[038-049]pleasantly, and prepared to talk.

[038-050]“Now explain further about your women, Haunte.”

[038-051]Haunte fetched another skin of liquor and a second cup. He tore off the

[038-052]string with his teeth, and poured out and drank cup after cup in quick

[038-053]succession. Then he sat down, crossed his legs, and turned to Maskull.

[038-054]“Well?”

[038-055]“So they are objectionable?”

[038-056]“They are deadly.”

[038-057]“Deadly? In what way can they possibly be deadly?”

[038-058]“You will learn. I was watching you in the boat, Maskull. You had some

[038-059]bad feelings, eh?”

[038-060]“I don’t conceal it. There were times when I felt as if I were

[038-061]struggling with a nightmare. What caused it?”

[038-062]“The female atmosphere of Lichstorm. Sexual passion.”

[038-063]“I had no passion.”

[038-064]“That was passion—the first stage. Nature tickles your people into

[038-065]marriage, but it tortures us. Wait till you get outside. You’ll have a

[038-066]return of those sensations—only ten times worse. The drink you’ve had

[038-067]will see to that.... How do you suppose it will all end?”

[038-068]“If I knew, I wouldn’t be asking you questions.”

[038-069]Haunte laughed loudly. “Sullenbode.”

[038-070]“You mean it will end in my seeking Sullenbode?”

[038-071]“But what will come of it, Maskull? What will she give you? Sweet,

[038-072]fainting, white-armed, feminine voluptuousness?”

[038-073]Maskull coolly drank another cup. “And why should she give all that to a

[038-074]passerby?”

[038-075]“Well, as a matter of fact, she hasn’t it to give. No, what she will

[038-076]give you, and what you’ll accept from her, because you can’t help it,

[038-077]is—anguish, insanity, possibly death.”

[038-078]“You may be talking sense, but it sounds like raving to me. Why should I

[038-079]accept insanity and death?”

[038-080]“Because your passion will force you to.”

[038-081]“What about yourself?” Maskull asked, biting his nails.

[038-082]“Oh, I have my male stones. I am immune.”

[038-083]“Is that all that prevents you from being like other men?”

[038-084]“Yes, but don’t attempt any tricks, Maskull.”

[038-085]Maskull went on drinking steadily, and said nothing for a time. “So men

[038-086]and women here are hostile to each other, and love is unknown?” he

[038-087]proceeded at last.

[038-088]“That magic word.... Shall I tell you what love is, Maskull? Love

[038-089]between male and female is impossible. When Maskull loves a woman, it is

[038-090]Maskull’s female ancestors who are loving her. But here in this land the

[038-091]men are pure males. They have drawn nothing from the female side.”

[038-092]“Where do the male stones come from?”

[038-093]“Oh, they are not freaks. There must be whole beds of the stuff

[038-094]somewhere. It is all that prevents the world from being a pure female

[038-095]world. It would be one big mass of heavy sweetness, without individual

[038-096]shapes.”

[038-097]“Yet this same sweetness is torturing to men?”

[038-098]“The life of an absolute male is fierce. An excess of life is dangerous

[038-099]to the body. How can it be anything else than torturing?”

[038-100]Corpang now sat up suddenly, and addressed Haunte. “I remind you of your

[038-101]promise to tell about Muspel.”

[038-102]Haunte regarded him with a malevolent smile. “Ha! The underground man

[038-103]has come to life.”

[038-104]“Yes, tell us,” put in Maskull carelessly.

[038-105]Haunte drank, and laughed a little. “Well, the tale’s short, and hardly

[038-106]worth telling, but since you’re interested.... A stranger came here five

[038-107]years ago, inquiring after Muspel-light. His name was Lodd. He came from

[038-108]the east. He came up to me one bright morning in summer, outside this

[038-109]very cave. If you ask me to describe him—I can’t imagine a second man

[038-110]like him. He looked so proud, noble, superior, that I felt my own blood

[038-111]to be dirty by comparison. You can guess I don’t have this feeling for

[038-112]everyone. Now that I am recalling him, he was not so much superior as

[038-113]different. I was so impressed that I rose and talked to him standing. He

[038-114]inquired the direction of the mountain Adage. He went on to say, ‘They

[038-115]say Muspel-light is sometimes seen there. What do you know of such a

[038-116]thing?’ I told him the truth—that I knew nothing about it, and then he

[038-117]went on, ‘Well, I am going to Adage. And tell those who come after me on

[038-118]the same errand that they had better do the same thing.’ That was the

[038-119]whole conversation. He started on his way, and I’ve never seen him or

[038-120]heard of him since.”

[038-121]“So you didn’t have the curiosity to follow him?”

[038-122]“No, because the moment he had turned his back all my interest in the

[038-123]man somehow seemed to vanish.”

[038-124]“Probably because he was useless to you.”

[038-125]Corpang glanced at Maskull. “Our road is marked out for us.”

[038-126]“So it would appear,” said Maskull indifferently.

[038-127]The talk flagged for a time. Maskull felt the silence oppressive, and

[038-128]grew restless.

[038-129]“What do you call the colour of your skin, Haunte, as I saw it in

[038-130]daylight? It struck me as strange.”

[038-131]“Dolm,” said Haunte.

[038-132]“A compound of ulfire and blue,” explained Corpang.

[038-133]“Now I know. These colours are puzzling for a stranger.”

[038-134]“What colours have you in your world?” asked Corpang.

[038-135]“Only three primary ones, but here you seem to have five, though how it

[038-136]comes about I can’t imagine.”

[038-137]“There are two sets of three primary colours here,” said Corpang, “but

[038-138]as one of the colours—blue—is identical in both sets, altogether there

[038-139]are five primary colours.”

[038-140]“Why two sets?”

[038-141]“Produced by the two suns. Branchspell produces blue, yellow, and red;

[038-142]Alppain, ulfire, blue, and jale.”

[038-143]“It’s remarkable that explanation has never occurred to me before.”

[038-144]“So here you have another illustration of the necessary trinity of

[038-145]nature. Blue is existence. It is darkness seen through light; a

[038-146]contrasting of existence and nothingness. Yellow is relation. In yellow

[038-147]light we see the relation of objects in the clearest way. Red is

[038-148]feeling. When we see red, we are thrown back on our personal

[038-149]feelings.... As regards the Alppain colours, blue stands in the middle

[038-150]and is therefore not existence, but relation. Ulfire is existence; so it

[038-151]must be a different sort of existence.”

[038-152]Haunte yawned. “There are marvellous philosophers in your underground

[038-153]hole.”

[038-154]Maskull got up and looked about him.

[038-155]“Where does that other door lead to?”

[038-156]“Better explore,” said Haunte.

[038-157]Maskull took him at his word, and strolled across the cave, flinging the

[038-158]curtain aside and disappearing into the night. Haunte rose abruptly and

[038-159]hurried after him.

[038-160]Corpang too got to his feet. He went over to the untouched spirit skins,

[038-161]untied the necks, and allowed the contents to gush out on to the floor.

[038-162]Next he took the hunting spears, and snapped off the points between his

[038-163]hands. Before he had time to resume his seat, Haunte and Maskull

[038-164]reappeared. The host’s quick, shifty eyes at once took in what had

[038-165]happened. He smiled, and turned pale.

[038-166]“You haven’t been idle, friend.”

[038-167]Corpang fixed Haunte with his bold, heavy gaze. “I thought it well to

[038-168]draw your teeth.”

[038-169]Maskull burst out laughing. “The toad’s come into the light to some

[038-170]purpose, Haunte. Who would have expected it?”

[038-171]Haunte, after staring hard at Corpang for two or three minutes, suddenly

[038-172]uttered a strange cry, like an evil spirit, and flung himself upon him.

[038-173]The two men began to wrestle like wildcats. They were as often on the

[038-174]floor as on their legs, and Maskull could not see who was getting the

[038-175]better of it. He made no attempt to separate them. A thought came into

[038-176]his head and, snatching up the two male stones, he ran with them,

[038-177]laughing, through the upper doorway, into the open night air.

[038-178]The door overlooked an abyss on another face of the mountain. A narrow

[038-179]ledge, sprinkled with green snow, wound along the cliff to the right; it

[038-180]was the only available path. He pitched the pebbles over the edge of the

[038-181]chasm. Although hard and heavy in his hand, they sank more like feathers

[038-182]than stones, and left a long trail of vapour behind. While Maskull was

[038-183]still watching them disappear, Haunte came rushing out of the cavern,

[038-184]followed by Corpang. He gripped Maskull’s arm excitedly.

[038-185]“What in Krag’s name have you done?”

[038-186]“Overboard they have gone,” replied Maskull, renewing his laughter.

[038-187]“You accursed madman!”

[038-188]Haunte’s luminous colour came and went, just as though his internal

[038-189]light were breathing. Then he grew suddenly calm, by a supreme exertion

[038-190]of his will.

[038-191]“You know this kills me?”

[038-192]“Haven’t you been doing your best this last hour to make me ripe for

[038-193]Sullenbode? Well then, cheer up, and join the pleasure party!”

[038-194]“You say it as a joke, but it is the miserable truth.”

[038-195]Haunte’s jeering malevolence had completely vanished. He looked a sick

[038-196]man—yet somehow his face had become nobler.

[038-197]“I would be very sorry for you, Haunte, if it did not entail my being

[038-198]also very sorry for myself. We are now all three together on the same

[038-199]errand—which doesn’t appear to have struck you yet.”
