# Section 9

[009-001]“We call it ‘gnawl water’,” said Joiwind. “This is not quite pure, as

[009-002]you can see by the colour. At Poolingdred it is crystal clear. But we

[009-003]would be ungrateful if we complained. After this you’ll find we’ll get

[009-004]along much better.”

[009-005]Maskull now began to realise his environment, as it were for the first

[009-006]time. All his sense organs started to show him beauties and wonders that

[009-007]he had not hitherto suspected. The uniform glaring scarlet of the sands

[009-008]became separated into a score of clearly distinguished shades of red.

[009-009]The sky was similarly split up into different blues. The radiant heat of

[009-010]Branchspell he found to affect every part of his body with unequal

[009-011]intensities. His ears awakened; the atmosphere was full of murmurs, the

[009-012]sands hummed, even the sun’s rays had a sound of their own—a kind of

[009-013]faint Aeolian harp. Subtle, puzzling perfumes assailed his nostrils. His

[009-014]palate lingered over the memory of the gnawl water. All the pores of his

[009-015]skin were tickled and soothed by hitherto unperceived currents of air.

[009-016]His poigns explored actively the inward nature of everything in his

[009-017]immediate vicinity. His magn touched Joiwind, and drew from her person a

[009-018]stream of love and joy. And lastly by means of his breve he exchanged

[009-019]thoughts with her in silence. This mighty sense symphony stirred him to

[009-020]the depths, and throughout the walk of that endless morning he felt no

[009-021]more fatigue.

[009-022]When it was drawing near to Blodsombre, they approached the sedgy margin

[009-023]of a dark green lake, which lay underneath Poolingdred.

[009-024]Panawe was sitting on a dark rock, waiting for them.

[009-026]The husband got up to meet his wife and their guest. He was clothed in

[009-027]white. He had a beardless face, with breve and poigns. His skin, on face

[009-028]and body alike, was so white, fresh, and soft, that it scarcely looked

[009-029]skin at all—it rather resembled a new kind of pure, snowy flesh,

[009-030]extending right down to his bones. It had nothing in common with the

[009-031]artificially whitened skin of an over-civilised woman. Its whiteness and

[009-032]delicacy aroused no voluptuous thoughts; it was obviously the

[009-033]manifestation of a cold and almost cruel chastity of nature. His hair,

[009-034]which fell to the nape of his neck, also was white; but again, from

[009-035]vigour, not decay. His eyes were black, quiet and fathomless. He was

[009-036]still a young man, but so stern were his features that he had the

[009-037]appearance of a lawgiver, and this in spite of their great beauty and

[009-038]harmony.

[009-039]His magn and Joiwind’s intertwined for a single moment and Maskull saw

[009-040]his face soften with love, while she looked exultant. She put him in her

[009-041]husband’s arms with gentle force, and stood back, gazing and smiling.

[009-042]Maskull felt rather embarrassed at being embraced by a man, but

[009-043]submitted to it; a sense of cool, pleasant languor passed through him in

[009-044]the act.

[009-045]“The stranger is red-blooded, then?”

[009-046]He was startled by Panawe’s speaking in English, and the voice too was

[009-047]extraordinary. It was absolutely tranquil, but its tranquillity seemed

[009-048]in a curious fashion to be an illusion, proceeding from a rapidity of

[009-049]thoughts and feelings so great that their motion could not be detected.

[009-050]How this could be, he did not know.

[009-051]“How do you come to speak in a tongue you have never heard before?”

[009-052]demanded Maskull.

[009-053]“Thought is a rich, complex thing. I can’t say if I am really speaking

[009-054]your tongue by instinct, or if you yourself are translating my thoughts

[009-055]into your tongue as I utter them.”

[009-056]“Already you see that Panawe is wiser than I am,” said Joiwind gaily.

[009-057]“What is your name?” asked the husband.

[009-058]“Maskull.”

[009-059]“That name must have a meaning—but again, thought is a strange thing. I

[009-060]connect that name with something—but with what?”

[009-061]“Try to discover,” said Joiwind.

[009-062]“Has there been a man in your world who stole something from the Maker

[009-063]of the universe, in order to ennoble his fellow creatures?”

[009-064]“There is such a myth. The hero’s name was Prometheus.”

[009-065]“Well, you seem to be identified in my mind with that action—but what it

[009-066]all means I can’t say, Maskull.”

[009-067]“Accept it as a good omen, for Panawe never lies, and never speaks

[009-068]thoughtlessly.”

[009-069]“There must be some confusion. These are heights beyond me,” said

[009-070]Maskull calmly, but looking rather contemplative.

[009-071]“Where do you come from?”

[009-072]“From the planet of a distant sun, called Earth.”

[009-073]“What for?”

[009-074]“I was tired of vulgarity,” returned Maskull laconically. He

[009-075]intentionally avoided mentioning his fellow voyagers, in order that

[009-076]Krag’s name should not come to light.

[009-077]“That’s an honourable motive,” said Panawe. “And what’s more, it may be

[009-078]true, though you spoke it as a prevarication.”

[009-079]“As far as it goes, it’s quite true,” said Maskull, staring at him with

[009-080]annoyance and surprise.

[009-081]The swampy lake extended for about half a mile from where they were

[009-082]standing to the lower buttresses of the mountain. Feathery purple reeds

[009-083]showed themselves here and there through the shallows. The water was

[009-084]dark green. Maskull did not see how they were going to cross it.

[009-085]Joiwind caught his arm. “Perhaps you don’t know that the lake will bear

[009-086]us?”

[009-087]Panawe walked onto the water; it was so heavy that it carried his

[009-088]weight. Joiwind followed with Maskull. He instantly started to slip

[009-089]about—nevertheless the motion was amusing, and he learned so fast, by

[009-090]watching and imitating Panawe, that he was soon able to balance himself

[009-091]without assistance. After that he found the sport excellent.

[009-092]For the same reason that women excel in dancing, Joiwind’s half falls

[009-093]and recoveries were far more graceful and sure than those of either of

[009-094]the men. Her slight, draped form—dipping, bending, rising, swaying,

[009-095]twisting, upon the surface of the dark water—this was a picture Maskull

[009-096]could not keep his eyes away from.

[009-097]The lake grew deeper. The gnawl water became green-black. The crags,

[009-098]gullies, and precipices of the shore could now be distinguished in

[009-099]detail. A waterfall was visible, descending several hundred feet. The

[009-100]surface of the lake grew disturbed—so much so that Maskull had

[009-101]difficulty in keeping his balance. He therefore threw himself down and

[009-102]started swimming on the face of the water. Joiwind turned her head, and

[009-103]laughed so joyously that all her teeth flashed in the sunlight.

[009-104]They landed in a few more minutes on a promontory of black rock. The

[009-105]water on Maskull’s garment and body evaporated very quickly. He gazed

[009-106]upward at the towering mountain, but at that moment some strange

[009-107]movements on the part of Panawe attracted his attention. His face was

[009-108]working convulsively, and he began to stagger about. Then he put his

[009-109]hand to his mouth and took from it what looked like a bright-coloured

[009-110]pebble. He looked at it carefully for some seconds. Joiwind also looked,

[009-111]over his shoulder, with quickly changing colors. After this inspection,

[009-112]Panawe let the object—whatever it was—fall to the ground, and took no

[009-113]more interest in it.

[009-114]“May I look?” asked Maskull; and, without waiting for permission, he

[009-115]picked it up. It was a delicately beautiful egg-shaped crystal of pale

[009-116]green.

[009-117]“Where did this come from?” he asked queerly.

[009-118]Panawe turned away, but Joiwind answered for him. “It came out of my

[009-119]husband.”

[009-120]“That’s what I thought, but I couldn’t believe it. But what is it?”

[009-121]“I don’t know that it has either name or use. It is merely an

[009-122]overflowing of beauty.”

[009-123]“Beauty?”

[009-124]Joiwind smiled. “If you were to regard nature as the husband, and Panawe

[009-125]as the wife, Maskull, perhaps everything would be explained.”

[009-126]Maskull reflected.

[009-127]“On Earth,” he said after a minute, “men like Panawe are called artists,

[009-128]poets, and musicians. Beauty overflows into them too, and out of them

[009-129]again. The only distinction is that their productions are more human and

[009-130]intelligible.”

[009-131]“Nothing comes from it but vanity,” said Panawe, and, taking the crystal

[009-132]out of Maskull’s hand, he threw it into the lake.

[009-133]The precipice they now had to climb was several hundred feet in height.

[009-134]Maskull was more anxious for Joiwind than for himself. She was evidently

[009-135]tiring, but she refused all help, and was in fact still the nimbler of

[009-136]the two. She made a mocking face at him. Panawe seemed lost in quiet

[009-137]thoughts. The rock was sound, and did not crumble under their weight.

[009-138]The heat of Branchspell, however, was by this time almost killing, the

[009-139]radiance was shocking in its white intensity, and Maskull’s pain

[009-140]steadily grew worse.

[009-141]When they got to the top, a plateau of dark rock appeared, bare of

[009-142]vegetation, stretching in both directions as far as the eye could see.

[009-143]It was of a nearly uniform width of five hundred yards, from the edge of

[009-144]the cliffs to the lower slopes of the chain of hills inland. The hills

[009-145]varied in height. The cup-shaped Poolingdred was approximately a

[009-146]thousand feet above them. The upper part of it was covered with a kind

[009-147]of glittering vegetation which he could not comprehend.

[009-148]Joiwind put her hand on Maskull’s shoulder, and pointed upward. “Here

[009-149]you have the highest peak in the whole land—that is, until you come to

[009-150]the Ifdawn Marest.”

[009-151]On hearing that strange name, he experienced a momentary unaccountable

[009-152]sensation of wild vigour and restlessness—but it passed away.

[009-153]Without losing time, Panawe led the way up the mountainside. The lower

[009-154]half was of bare rock, not difficult to climb. Halfway up, however, it

[009-155]grew steeper, and they began to meet bushes and small trees. The growth

[009-156]became thicker as they continued to ascend, and when they neared the

[009-157]summit, tall forest trees appeared.

[009-158]These bushes and trees had pale, glassy trunks and branches, but the

[009-159]small twigs and the leaves were translucent and crystal. They cast no

[009-160]shadows from above, but still the shade was cool. Both leaves and

[009-161]branches were fantastically shaped. What surprised Maskull the most,

[009-162]however, was the fact that, as far as he could see, scarcely any two

[009-163]plants belonged to the same species.

[009-164]“Won’t you help Maskull out of his difficulty?” said Joiwind, pulling

[009-165]her husband’s arm.

[009-166]He smiled. “If he’ll forgive me for again trespassing in his brain. But

[009-167]the difficulty is small. Life on a new planet, Maskull, is necessarily

[009-168]energetic and lawless, and not sedate and imitative. Nature is still

[009-169]fluid—not yet rigid—and matter is plastic. The will forks and sports

[009-170]incessantly, and thus no two creatures are alike.”

[009-171]“Well, I understand all that,” replied Maskull, after listening

[009-172]attentively. “But what I don’t grasp is this—if living creatures here

[009-173]sport so energetically, how does it come about that human beings wear

[009-174]much the same shape as in my world?”

[009-175]“I’ll explain that too,” said Panawe. “All creatures that resemble

[009-176]Shaping must of necessity resemble one another.”

[009-177]“Then sporting is the blind will to become like Shaping?”

[009-178]“Exactly.”

[009-179]“It is most wonderful,” said Maskull. “Then the brotherhood of man is

[009-180]not a fable invented by idealists, but a solid fact.”

[009-181]Joiwind looked at him, and changed colour. Panawe relapsed into

[009-182]sternness.

[009-183]Maskull became interested in a new phenomenon. The jale-coloured

[009-184]blossoms of a crystal bush were emitting mental waves, which with his

[009-185]breve he could clearly distinguish. They cried out silently, “To me! To

[009-186]me!” While he looked, a flying worm guided itself through the air to one

[009-187]of these blossoms and began to suck its nectar. The floral cry

[009-188]immediately ceased.

[009-189]They now gained the crest of the mountain, and looked down beyond. A

[009-190]lake occupied its crater-like cavity. A fringe of trees partly

[009-191]intercepted the view, but Maskull was able to perceive that this

[009-192]mountain lake was nearly circular and perhaps a quarter of a mile

[009-193]across. Its shore stood a hundred feet below them.

[009-194]Observing that his hosts did not propose to descend, he begged them to

[009-195]wait for him, and scrambled down to the surface. When he got there, he

[009-196]found the water perfectly motionless and of a colourless transparency.

[009-197]He walked onto it, lay down at full length, and peered into the depths.

[009-198]It was weirdly clear: he could see down for an indefinite distance,

[009-199]without arriving at any bottom. Some dark, shadowy objects, almost out

[009-200]of reach of his eyes, were moving about. Then a sound, very faint and

[009-201]mysterious, seemed to come up through the gnawl water from an immense

[009-202]depth. It was like the rhythm of a drum. There were four beats of equal

[009-203]length, but the accent was on the third. It went on for a considerable

[009-204]time, and then ceased.
