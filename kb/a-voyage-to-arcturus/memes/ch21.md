# Section 21

[021-001]“What is the name of those organs, and what is their function?”

[021-002]“They are probes, and they are the gates opening into a new world.”

[021-003]Maskull lingered no longer, but permitted Spadevil to cover his sorb.

[021-004]While the iron hand was still pressing his forehead, the new law quietly

[021-005]flowed into his consciousness, like a smooth-running stream of clean

[021-006]water which had hitherto been dammed by his obstructive will. The law

[021-007]was duty.

[021-008]Chapter 12. SPADEVIL

[021-009]Maskull found that his new organs had no independent function of their

[021-010]own, but only intensified and altered his other senses. When he used his

[021-011]eyes, ears, or nostrils, the same objects presented themselves to him,

[021-012]but his judgment concerning them was different. Previously all external

[021-013]things had existed for him; now he existed for them. According to

[021-014]whether they served his purpose or were in harmony with his nature, or

[021-015]otherwise, they had been pleasant or painful. Now these words “pleasure”

[021-016]and “pain” simply had no meaning.

[021-017]The other two watched him, while he was making himself acquainted with

[021-018]his new mental outlook. He smiled at them.

[021-019]“You were quite right, Tydomin,” he said, in a bold, cheerful voice. “We

[021-020]have been fools. So near the light all the time, and we never guessed

[021-021]it. Always buried in the past or future—systematically ignoring the

[021-022]present—and now it turns out that apart from the present we have no life

[021-023]at all.”

[021-024]“Thank Spadevil for it,” she answered, more loudly than usual.

[021-025]Maskull looked at the man’s dark, concrete form. “Spadevil, now I mean

[021-026]to follow you to the end. I can do nothing less.”

[021-027]The severe face showed no sign of gratification—not a muscle relaxed.

[021-028]“Watch that you don’t lose your gift,” he said gruffly.

[021-029]Tydomin spoke. “You promised that I should enter Sant with you.”

[021-030]“Attach yourself to the truth, not to me. For I may die before you, but

[021-031]the truth will accompany you to your death. However, now let us journey

[021-032]together, all three of us.”

[021-033]The words had not left his mouth before he put his face against the

[021-034]fine, driving snow, and pressed onward toward his destination. He walked

[021-035]with a long stride; Tydomin was obliged to half run in order to keep up

[021-036]with him. The three travelled abreast; Spadevil in the middle. The fog

[021-037]was so dense that it was impossible to see a hundred yards ahead. The

[021-038]ground was covered by the green snow. The wind blew in gusts from the

[021-039]Sant highlands and was piercingly cold.

[021-040]“Spadevil, are you a man, or more than a man?” asked Maskull.

[021-041]“He that is not more than a man is nothing.”

[021-042]“Where have you now come from?”

[021-043]“From brooding, Maskull. Out of no other mother can truth be born. I

[021-044]have brooded, and rejected; and I have brooded again. Now, after many

[021-045]months’ absence from Sant, the truth at last shines forth for me in its

[021-046]simple splendour, like an upturned diamond.”

[021-047]“I see its shining,” said Maskull. “But how much does it owe to ancient

[021-048]Hator?”

[021-049]“Knowledge has its seasons. The blossom was to Hator, the fruit is to

[021-050]me. Hator also was a brooder—but now his followers do not brood. In Sant

[021-051]all is icy selfishness, a living death. They hate pleasure, and this

[021-052]hatred is the greatest pleasure to them.”

[021-053]“But in what way have they fallen off from Hator’s doctrines?”

[021-054]“For him, in his sullen purity of nature, all the world was a snare, a

[021-055]limed twig. Knowing that pleasure was everywhere, a fierce, mocking

[021-056]enemy, crouching and waiting at every corner of the road of life, in

[021-057]order to kill with its sweet sting the naked grandeur of the soul, he

[021-058]shielded himself behind pain. This also his followers do, but they do

[021-059]not do it for the sake of the soul, but for the sake of vanity and

[021-060]pride.”

[021-061]“What is the Trifork?”

[021-062]“The stem, Maskull, is hatred of pleasure. The first fork is

[021-063]disentanglement from the sweetness of the world. The second fork is

[021-064]power over those who still writhe in the nets of illusion. The third

[021-065]fork is the healthy glow of one who steps into ice-cold water.”

[021-066]“From what land did Hator come?”

[021-067]“It is not said. He lived in Ifdawn for a while. There are many legends

[021-068]told of him while there.”

[021-069]“We have a long way to go,” said Tydomin. “Relate some of these legends,

[021-070]Spadevil.”

[021-071]The snow had ceased, the day brightened, Branchspell reappeared like a

[021-072]phantom sun, but bitter blasts of wind still swept over the plain.

[021-073]“In those days,” said Spadevil, “there existed in Ifdawn a mountain

[021-074]island separated by wide spaces from the land around it. A handsome

[021-075]girl, who knew sorcery, caused a bridge to be constructed across which

[021-076]men and women might pass to it. Having by a false tale drawn Hator on to

[021-077]this rock, she pushed at the bridge with her foot until it tumbled into

[021-078]the depths below. ‘You and I, Hator, are now together, and there is no

[021-079]means of separating. I wish to see how long the famous frost man can

[021-080]withstand the breath, smiles and perfume of a girl.’ Hator said no word,

[021-081]either then or all that day. He stood till sunset like a tree trunk, and

[021-082]thought of other things. Then the girl grew passionate, and shook her

[021-083]curls. She rose from where she was sitting she looked at him, and

[021-084]touched his arm; but he did not see her. She looked at him, so that all

[021-085]the soul was in her eyes; and then she fell down dead. Hator awoke from

[021-086]his thoughts, and saw her lying, still warm, at his feet, a corpse. He

[021-087]passed to the mainland; but how, it is not related.”

[021-088]Tydomin shuddered. “You too have met your wicked woman, Spadevil; but

[021-089]your method is a nobler one.”

[021-090]“Don’t pity other women,” said Spadevil, “but love the right. Hator also

[021-091]once conversed with Shaping.”

[021-092]“With the Maker of the World?” said Maskull thoughtfully.

[021-093]“With the Maker of Pleasure. It is told how Shaping defended his world,

[021-094]and tried to force Hator to acknowledge loveliness and joy. But Hator,

[021-095]answering all his marvellous speeches in a few concise, iron words,

[021-096]showed how this joy and beauty was but another name for the bestiality

[021-097]of souls wallowing in luxury and sloth. Shaping smiled, and said, ‘How

[021-098]comes it that your wisdom is greater than that of the Master of wisdom?’

[021-099]Hator said, ‘My wisdom does not come from you, nor from your world, but

[021-100]from that other world, which you, Shaping, have vainly tried to

[021-101]imitate.’ Shaping replied, ‘What, then, do you do in my world?’ Hator

[021-102]said, ‘I am here falsely, and therefore I am subject to your false

[021-103]pleasures. But I wrap myself in pain—not because it is good, but because

[021-104]I wish to keep myself as far from you as possible. For pain is not

[021-105]yours, neither does it belong to the other world, but it is the shadow

[021-106]cast by your false pleasures.’ Shaping then said, ‘What is this faraway

[021-107]other world of which you say “This is so—this is not so?” How happens it

[021-108]that you alone of all my creatures have knowledge of it?’ But Hator spat

[021-109]at his feet, and said, ‘You lie, Shaping. All have knowledge of it. You,

[021-110]with your pretty toys, alone obscure it from our view.’ Shaping asked,

[021-111]‘What, then, am I?’ Hator answered, ‘You are the dreamer of impossible

[021-112]dreams.’ And then the story goes that Shaping departed, ill pleased with

[021-113]what had been said.”

[021-114]“What other world did Hator refer to?” asked Maskull.

[021-115]“One where grandeur reigns, Maskull, just as pleasure reigns here.”

[021-116]“Whether grandeur or pleasure, it makes no difference,” said Maskull.

[021-117]“The individual spirit that lives and wishes to live is mean and

[021-118]corrupt-natured.”

[021-119]“Guard you your pride!” returned Spadevil. “Do not make law for the

[021-120]universe and for all time, but for yourself and for this small, false

[021-121]life of yours.”

[021-122]“In what shape did death come to that hard, unconquerable man?” asked

[021-123]Tydomin.

[021-124]“He lived to be old, but went upright and free-limbed to his last hour.

[021-125]When he saw that death could not be staved off longer he determined to

[021-126]destroy himself. He gathered his friends around him; not from vanity,

[021-127]but that they might see to what lengths the human soul can go in its

[021-128]perpetual warfare with the voluptuous body. Standing erect, without

[021-129]support, he died by withholding his breath.”

[021-130]A silence followed, which lasted for perhaps an hour. Their minds

[021-131]refused to acknowledge the icy winds, but the current of their thoughts

[021-132]became frozen.

[021-133]When Branchspell, however, shone out again, though with subdued power,

[021-134]Maskull’s curiosity rose once more. “Your fellow countrymen, then,

[021-135]Spadevil, are sick with self-love?”

[021-136]“The men of other countries,” said Spadevil, “are the slaves of pleasure

[021-137]and desire, knowing it. But the men of my country are the slaves of

[021-138]pleasure and desire, not knowing it.”

[021-139]“And yet that proud pleasure, which rejoices in self-torture, has

[021-140]something noble in it.”

[021-141]“He who studies himself at all is ignoble. Only by despising soul as

[021-142]well as body can a man enter into true life.”

[021-143]“On what grounds do they reject women?”

[021-144]“Inasmuch as a woman has ideal love, and cannot live for herself. Love

[021-145]for another is pleasure for the loved one, and therefore injurious to

[021-146]him.”

[021-147]“A forest of false ideas is waiting for your axe,” said Maskull. “But

[021-148]will they allow it?”

[021-149]“Spadevil knows, Maskull,” said Tydomin, “that be it today or be it

[021-150]tomorrow, love can’t be kept out of a land, even by the disciples of

[021-151]Hator.”

[021-152]“Beware of love—beware of emotion!” exclaimed Spadevil. “Love is but

[021-153]pleasure once removed. Think not of pleasing others, but of serving

[021-154]them.”

[021-155]“Forgive me, Spadevil, if I am still feminine.”

[021-156]“Right has no sex. So long, Tydomin, as you remember that you are a

[021-157]woman, so long you will not enter into divine apathy of soul.”

[021-158]“But where there are no women, there are no children,” said Maskull.

[021-159]“How came there to be all these generations of Hator men?”

[021-160]“Life breeds passion, passion breeds suffering, suffering breeds the

[021-161]yearning for relief from suffering. Men throng to Sant from all parts,

[021-162]in order to have the scars of their souls healed.”

[021-163]“In place of hatred of pleasure, which all can understand, what simple

[021-164]formula do you offer?”

[021-165]“Iron obedience to duty,” answered Spadevil.

[021-166]“And if they ask ‘How far is this consistent with hatred of pleasure?’

[021-167]what will your pronouncement be?”

[021-168]“I do not answer them, but I answer you, Maskull, who ask the question.

[021-169]Hatred is passion, and all passion springs from the dark fires of self.

[021-170]Do not hate pleasure at all, but pass it by on one side, calm and

[021-171]undisturbed.”

[021-172]“What is the criterion of pleasure? How can we always recognise it, in

[021-173]order to avoid it?”

[021-174]“Rigidly follow duty, and such questions will not arise.”

[021-175]Later in the afternoon, Tydomin timidly placed her fingers on Spadevil’s

[021-176]arm.

[021-177]“Fearful doubts are in my mind,” she said. “This expedition to Sant may

[021-178]turn out badly. I have seen a vision of you, Spadevil, and myself lying

[021-179]dead and covered in blood, but Maskull was not there.”

[021-180]“We may drop the torch, but it will not be extinguished, and others will

[021-181]raise it.”

[021-182]“Show me a sign that you are not as other men—so that I may know that

[021-183]our blood will not be wasted.”

[021-184]Spadevil regarded her sternly. “I am not a magician. I don’t persuade

[021-185]the senses, but the soul. Does your duty call you to Sant, Tydomin? Then

[021-186]go there. Does it not call you to Sant? Then go no farther. Is not this

[021-187]simple? What signs are necessary?”

[021-188]“Did I not see you dispel those spouts of lightning? No common man could

[021-189]have done that.”

[021-190]“Who knows what any man can do? This man can do one thing, that man can

[021-191]do another. But what all men can do is their duty; and to open their

[021-192]eyes to this, I must go to Sant, and if necessary lay down my life. Will

[021-193]you not still accompany me?”
