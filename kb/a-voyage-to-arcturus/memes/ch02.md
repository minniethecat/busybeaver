# Section 2

[002-001]The signal was for the hidden orchestra to begin playing. A murmur of

[002-002]surprise passed through the audience as, without previous warning, the

[002-003]beautiful and solemn strains of Mozart’s “temple” music pulsated through

[002-004]the air. The expectation of everyone was raised, while, beneath her

[002-005]pallor and composure, it could be seen that Mrs. Trent was deeply moved.

[002-006]It was evident that aesthetically she was by far the most important

[002-007]person present. Faull watched her, with his face sunk on his chest,

[002-008]sprawling as usual.

[002-009]Backhouse stood up, with one hand on the back of his chair, and began

[002-010]speaking. The music instantly sank to pianissimo, and remained so for as

[002-011]long as he was on his legs.

[002-012]“Ladies and gentlemen, you are about to witness a materialisation. That

[002-013]means you will see something appear in space that was not previously

[002-014]there. At first it will appear as a vaporous form, but finally it will

[002-015]be a solid body, which anyone present may feel and handle—and, for

[002-016]example, shake hands with. For this body will be in the human shape. It

[002-017]will be a real man or woman—which, I can’t say—but a man or woman

[002-018]without known antecedents. If, however, you demand from me an

[002-019]explanation of the origin of this materialised form—where it comes from,

[002-020]whence the atoms and molecules composing its tissues are derived—I am

[002-021]unable to satisfy you. I am about to produce the phenomenon; if anyone

[002-022]can explain it to me afterward, I shall be very grateful.... That is all

[002-023]I have to say.”

[002-024]He resumed his seat, half turning his back on the assembly, and paused

[002-025]for a moment before beginning his task.

[002-026]It was precisely at this minute that the manservant opened the door and

[002-027]announced in a subdued but distinct voice: “Mr. Maskull, Mr.

[002-028]Nightspore.”

[002-029]Everyone turned round. Faull rose to welcome the late arrivals.

[002-030]Backhouse also stood up, and stared hard at them.

[002-031]The two strangers remained standing by the door, which was closed

[002-032]quietly behind them. They seemed to be waiting for the mild sensation

[002-033]caused by their appearance to subside before advancing into the room.

[002-034]Maskull was a kind of giant, but of broader and more robust physique

[002-035]than most giants. He wore a full beard. His features were thick and

[002-036]heavy, coarsely modelled, like those of a wooden carving; but his eyes,

[002-037]small and black, sparkled with the fires of intelligence and audacity.

[002-038]His hair was short, black, and bristling. Nightspore was of middle

[002-039]height, but so tough-looking that he appeared to be trained out of all

[002-040]human frailties and susceptibilities. His hairless face seemed consumed

[002-041]by an intense spiritual hunger, and his eyes were wild and distant. Both

[002-042]men were dressed in tweeds.

[002-043]Before any words were spoken, a loud and terrible crash of falling

[002-044]masonry caused the assembled party to start up from their chairs in

[002-045]consternation. It sounded as if the entire upper part of the building

[002-046]had collapsed. Faull sprang to the door, and called to the servant to

[002-047]say what was happening. The man had to be questioned twice before he

[002-048]gathered what was required of him. He said he had heard nothing. In

[002-049]obedience to his master’s order, he went upstairs. Nothing, however, was

[002-050]amiss there, neither had the maids heard anything.

[002-051]In the meantime Backhouse, who almost alone of those assembled had

[002-052]preserved his sangfroid, went straight up to Nightspore, who stood

[002-053]gnawing his nails.

[002-054]“Perhaps you can explain it, sir?”

[002-055]“It was supernatural,” said Nightspore, in a harsh, muffled voice,

[002-056]turning away from his questioner.

[002-057]“I guessed so. It is a familiar phenomenon, but I have never heard it so

[002-058]loud.”

[002-059]He then went among the guests, reassuring them. By degrees they settled

[002-060]down, but it was observable that their former easy and good-humoured

[002-061]interest in the proceedings was now changed to strained watchfulness.

[002-062]Maskull and Nightspore took the places allotted to them. Mrs. Trent kept

[002-063]stealing uneasy glances at them. Throughout the entire incident,

[002-064]Mozart’s hymn continued to be played. The orchestra also had heard

[002-065]nothing.

[002-066]Backhouse now entered on his task. It was one that began to be familiar

[002-067]to him, and he had no anxiety about the result. It was not possible to

[002-068]effect the materialisation by mere concentration of will, or the

[002-069]exercise of any faculty; otherwise many people could have done what he

[002-070]had engaged himself to do. His nature was phenomenal—the dividing wall

[002-071]between himself and the spiritual world was broken in many places.

[002-072]Through the gaps in his mind the inhabitants of the invisible, when he

[002-073]summoned them, passed for a moment timidly and awfully into the solid,

[002-074]coloured universe.... He could not say how it was brought about.... The

[002-075]experience was a rough one for the body, and many such struggles would

[002-076]lead to insanity and early death. That is why Backhouse was stern and

[002-077]abrupt in his manner. The coarse, clumsy suspicion of some of the

[002-078]witnesses, the frivolous aestheticism of others, were equally obnoxious

[002-079]to his grim, bursting heart; but he was obliged to live, and, to pay his

[002-080]way, must put up with these impertinences.

[002-081]He sat down facing the wooden couch. His eyes remained open but seemed

[002-082]to look inward. His cheeks paled, and he became noticeably thinner. The

[002-083]spectators almost forgot to breathe. The more sensitive among them began

[002-084]to feel, or imagine, strange presences all around them. Maskull’s eyes

[002-085]glittered with anticipation, and his brows went up and down, but

[002-086]Nightspore appeared bored.

[002-087]After a long ten minutes the pedestal of the statue was seen to become

[002-088]slightly blurred, as though an intervening mist were rising from the

[002-089]ground. This slowly developed into a visible cloud, coiling hither and

[002-090]thither, and constantly changing shape. The professor half rose, and

[002-091]held his glasses with one hand further forward on the bridge of his

[002-092]nose.

[002-093]By slow stages the cloud acquired the dimensions and approximate outline

[002-094]of an adult human body, although all was still vague and blurred. It

[002-095]hovered lightly in the air, a foot or so above the couch. Backhouse

[002-096]looked haggard and ghastly. Mrs. Jameson quietly fainted in her chair,

[002-097]but she was unnoticed, and presently revived. The apparition now settled

[002-098]down upon the couch, and at the moment of doing so seemed suddenly to

[002-099]grow dark, solid, and manlike. Many of the guests were as pale as the

[002-100]medium himself, but Faull preserved his stoical apathy, and glanced once

[002-101]or twice at Mrs. Trent. She was staring straight at the couch, and was

[002-102]twisting a little lace handkerchief through the different fingers of her

[002-103]hand. The music went on playing.

[002-104]The figure was by this time unmistakably that of a man lying down. The

[002-105]face focused itself into distinctness. The body was draped in a sort of

[002-106]shroud, but the features were those of a young man. One smooth hand fell

[002-107]over, nearly touching the floor, white and motionless. The weaker

[002-108]spirits of the company stared at the vision in sick horror; the rest

[002-109]were grave and perplexed. The seeming man was dead, but somehow it did

[002-110]not appear like a death succeeding life, but like a death preliminary to

[002-111]life. All felt that he might sit up at any minute.

[002-112]“Stop that music!” muttered Backhouse, tottering from his chair and

[002-113]facing the party. Faull touched the bell. A few more bars sounded, and

[002-114]then total silence ensued.

[002-115]“Anyone who wants to may approach the couch,” said Backhouse with

[002-116]difficulty.

[002-117]Lang at once advanced, and stared awestruck at the supernatural youth.

[002-118]“You are at liberty to touch,” said the medium.

[002-119]But Lang did not venture to, nor did any of the others, who one by one

[002-120]stole up to the couch—until it came to Faull’s turn. He looked straight

[002-121]at Mrs. Trent, who seemed frightened and disgusted at the spectacle

[002-122]before her, and then not only touched the apparition but suddenly

[002-123]grasped the drooping hand in his own and gave it a powerful squeeze.

[002-124]Mrs. Trent gave a low scream. The ghostly visitor opened his eyes,

[002-125]looked at Faull strangely, and sat up on the couch. A cryptic smile

[002-126]started playing over his mouth. Faull looked at his hand; a feeling of

[002-127]intense pleasure passed through his body.

[002-128]Maskull caught Mrs. Jameson in his arms; she was attacked by another

[002-129]spell of faintness. Mrs. Trent ran forward, and led her out of the room.

[002-130]Neither of them returned.

[002-131]The phantom body now stood upright, looking about him, still with his

[002-132]peculiar smile. Prior suddenly felt sick, and went out. The other men

[002-133]more or less hung together, for the sake of human society, but

[002-134]Nightspore paced up and down, like a man weary and impatient, while

[002-135]Maskull attempted to interrogate the youth. The apparition watched him

[002-136]with a baffling expression, but did not answer. Backhouse was sitting

[002-137]apart, his face buried in his hands.

[002-138]It was at this moment that the door was burst open violently, and a

[002-139]stranger, unannounced, half leaped, half strode a few yards into the

[002-140]room, and then stopped. None of Faull’s friends had ever seen him

[002-141]before. He was a thick, shortish man, with surprising muscular

[002-142]development and a head far too large in proportion to his body. His

[002-143]beardless yellow face indicated, as a first impression, a mixture of

[002-144]sagacity, brutality, and humour.

[002-145]“Aha-i, gentlemen!” he called out loudly. His voice was piercing, and

[002-146]oddly disagreeable to the ear. “So we have a little visitor here.”

[002-147]Nightspore turned his back, but everyone else stared at the intruder in

[002-148]astonishment. He took another few steps forward, which brought him to

[002-149]the edge of the theatre.

[002-150]“May I ask, sir, how I come to have the honour of being your host?”

[002-151]asked Faull sullenly. He thought that the evening was not proceeding as

[002-152]smoothly as he had anticipated.

[002-153]The newcomer looked at him for a second, and then broke into a great,

[002-154]roaring guffaw. He thumped Faull on the back playfully—but the play was

[002-155]rather rough, for the victim was sent staggering against the wall before

[002-156]he could recover his balance.

[002-157]“Good evening, my host!”

[002-158]“And good evening to you too, my lad!” he went on, addressing the

[002-159]supernatural youth, who was now beginning to wander about the room, in

[002-160]apparent unconsciousness of his surroundings. “I have seen someone very

[002-161]like you before, I think.”

[002-162]There was no response.

[002-163]The intruder thrust his head almost up to the phantom’s face. “You have

[002-164]no right here, as you know.”

[002-165]The shape looked back at him with a smile full of significance, which,

[002-166]however, no one could understand.

[002-167]“Be careful what you are doing,” said Backhouse quickly.

[002-168]“What’s the matter, spirit usher?”

[002-169]“I don’t know who you are, but if you use physical violence toward that,

[002-170]as you seem inclined to do, the consequences may prove very unpleasant.”

[002-171]“And without pleasure our evening would be spoiled, wouldn’t it, my

[002-172]little mercenary friend?”

[002-173]Humour vanished from his face, like sunlight from a landscape, leaving

[002-174]it hard and rocky. Before anyone realised what he was doing, he

[002-175]encircled the soft, white neck of the materialised shape with his hairy

[002-176]hands and, with a double turn, twisted it completely round. A faint,

[002-177]unearthly shriek sounded, and the body fell in a heap to the floor. Its

[002-178]face was uppermost. The guests were unutterably shocked to observe that

[002-179]its expression had changed from the mysterious but fascinating smile to

[002-180]a vulgar, sordid, bestial grin, which cast a cold shadow of moral

[002-181]nastiness into every heart. The transformation was accompanied by a

[002-182]sickening stench of the graveyard.

[002-183]The features faded rapidly away, the body lost its consistence, passing

[002-184]from the solid to the shadowy condition, and, before two minutes had

[002-185]elapsed, the spirit-form had entirely disappeared.

[002-186]The short stranger turned and confronted the party, with a long, loud

[002-187]laugh, like nothing in nature.

[002-188]The professor talked excitedly to Kent-Smith in low tones. Faull

[002-189]beckoned Backhouse behind a wing of scenery, and handed him his check

[002-190]without a word. The medium put it in his pocket, buttoned his coat, and

[002-191]walked out of the room. Lang followed him, in order to get a drink.
