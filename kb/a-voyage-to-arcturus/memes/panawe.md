# Panawe

## Role

Panawe is the title character of Chapter 7, the husband of [[joiwind]], and one of the first beings [[maskull]] encounters on [[tormance|Tormance]]. Where [[joiwind|Joiwind]] embodies love and self-sacrifice, Panawe represents their complementary qualities: pure wisdom, chastity, and spiritual detachment. He is described as incapable of falsehood — "Panawe never lies, and never speaks without knowledge" [007-3421] — making him a figure of absolute epistemic reliability in a world where [[maskull|Maskull]]'s perceptions are constantly shifting.

## In the Novel

[[maskull|Maskull]] first sees Panawe sitting on a dark rock, waiting for them with quiet patience [007-3335]. The first surprise is Panawe's voice — he speaks in English, startling [[maskull|Maskull]] [007-3379]. [[joiwind|Joiwind]] frames this as evidence of his wisdom: "Already you see that Panawe is wiser than I am" [007-3399].

Panawe's most striking action is walking on water. He steps onto the surface of a lake, and the water is "so heavy that it carried his weight" [007-3461]. [[maskull|Maskull]] imitates him and is soon able to balance himself on the water's surface [007-3467] — a moment that literalizes the theme of spiritual levitation and transcendence of material limitations.

Panawe's movements and expressions convey an inner stillness. His face reflects "the inner movements of his heart" [007-3501], suggesting a transparency of being that contrasts with the moral opacity [[maskull|Maskull]] will encounter in later characters.

## Philosophical Significance

Panawe embodies the ideal of purity achieved through detachment rather than struggle. Unlike [[spadevil]] (who preaches duty), [[tydomin]] (who demands guilt), or [[corpang]] (who studies and questions), Panawe simply _is_ what he is — wise, truthful, and calm. His walking on water is the novel's most direct image of spiritual transcendence: the pure soul is not bound by material laws.

In Lindsay's taxonomy, Panawe and [[joiwind|Joiwind]] together represent the highest possibility of existence within the natural order of [[tormance|Tormance]] — love and wisdom united in marriage. They are the baseline of goodness against which [[maskull|Maskull]]'s subsequent, more turbulent encounters are measured.
