# Section 37

[037-001]“I am Maskull; my friend is Corpang. He comes from Threal, but where I

[037-002]come from, don’t ask.”

[037-003]“I am Haunte, from Sarclash.”

[037-004]“Where may that be?”

[037-005]“Half an hour ago I could have shown it to you, but now it has got too

[037-006]murky. It is a mountain in Lichstorm.”

[037-007]“Are you returning there now?”

[037-008]“Yes.”

[037-009]“And how long will it take to get there in that boat?”

[037-010]“Two—three hours.”

[037-011]“Will it accommodate us too?”

[037-012]“What, are you for Lichstorm as well? What can you want there?”

[037-013]“To see the sights,” responded Maskull with twinkling eyes. “But first

[037-014]of all, to dine. I can’t remember having eaten all day. You seem to have

[037-015]been hunting to some purpose, so we won’t lack for food.”

[037-016]Haunte eyed him quizzically. “You certainly don’t lack impudence.

[037-017]However, I’m a man of that sort myself, and it is the sort I prefer.

[037-018]Your friend, now, would probably rather starve than ask a meal of a

[037-019]stranger. He looks to me just like a bewildered toad dragged up out of a

[037-020]dark hole.”

[037-021]Maskull took Corpang’s arm, and constrained him to silence.

[037-022]“Where have you been hunting, Haunte?”

[037-023]“Matterplay. I had the worst luck—I speared one wold horse, and there it

[037-024]lies.”

[037-025]“What is Lichstorm like?”

[037-026]“There are men there, and there are women there, but there are no men-

[037-027]women, as with you.”

[037-028]“What do you call men-women?”

[037-029]“Persons of mixed sex, like yourself. In Lichstorm the sexes are pure.”

[037-030]“I have always regarded myself as a man.”

[037-031]“Very likely you have; but the test is, do you hate and fear women?”

[037-032]“Why, do you?”

[037-033]Haunte grinned and showed his teeth. “Things are different in

[037-034]Lichstorm.... So you want to see the sights?”

[037-035]“I confess I am curious to see your women, for example, after what you

[037-036]say.”

[037-037]“Then I’ll introduce you to Sullenbode.”

[037-038]He paused a moment after making this remark, and then suddenly uttered a

[037-039]great, bass laugh, so that his chest shook.

[037-040]“Let us share the joke,” said Maskull.

[037-041]“Oh, you’ll understand it later.”

[037-042]“If you play pranks with me, I won’t stand on ceremony with you.”

[037-043]Haunte laughed again. “I won’t be the one to play pranks. Sullenbode

[037-044]will be deeply obliged to me. If I don’t visit her myself as often as

[037-045]she would like, I’m always glad to serve her in other ways.... Well, you

[037-046]shall have your boat ride.”

[037-047]Maskull rubbed his nose doubtfully. “If the sexes hate one another in

[037-048]your land, is it because passion is weaker, or stronger?”

[037-049]“In other parts of the world there is soft passion, but in Lichstorm

[037-050]there is hard passion.”

[037-051]“But what do you call hard passion?”

[037-052]“Where men are called to women by pain, and not pleasure.”

[037-053]“I intend to understand, before I’ve finished.”

[037-054]“Yes,” answered Haunte, with a taunting look, “it would be a pity to let

[037-055]the chance slip, since you’re going to Lichstorm.”

[037-056]It was now Corpang’s turn to take Maskull by the arm. “This journey will

[037-057]end badly.”

[037-058]“Why so?”

[037-059]“Your goal was Muspel a short while ago; now it is women.”

[037-060]“Let me alone,” said Maskull. “Give luck a slack rein. What brought this

[037-061]boat here?”

[037-062]“What is this talk about Muspel?” demanded Haunte.

[037-063]Corpang caught his shoulder roughly, and stared straight into his eyes.

[037-064]“What do you know?”

[037-065]“Not much, but something, perhaps. Ask me at supper. Now it is high time

[037-066]to start. Navigating the mountains by night isn’t child’s play, let me

[037-067]tell you.”

[037-068]“I shall not forget,” said Corpang.

[037-069]Maskull gazed down at the boat. “Are we to get in?”

[037-070]“Gently, my friend. It’s only canework and skin.”

[037-071]“First of all, you might enlighten me as to how you have contrived to

[037-072]dispense with the laws of gravitation.”

[037-073]Haunte smiled sarcastically. “A secret in your ear, Maskull. All laws

[037-074]are female. A true male is an outlaw—outside the law.”

[037-075]“I don’t understand.”

[037-076]“The great body of the earth is continually giving out female particles,

[037-077]and the male parts of rocks and living bodies are equally continually

[037-078]trying to reach them. That’s gravitation.”

[037-079]“Then how do you manage with your boat?”

[037-080]“My two male stones do the work. The one underneath the boat prevents it

[037-081]from falling to the ground; the one in the stern shuts it off from solid

[037-082]objects in the rear. The only part of the boat attracted by any part of

[037-083]the earth is the bow, for that’s the only part the light of the male

[037-084]stones does not fall on. So in that direction the boat travels.”

[037-085]“And what are these wondrous male stones?”

[037-086]“They really are male stones. There is nothing female in them; they are

[037-087]showering out male sparks all the time. These sparks devour all the

[037-088]female particles rising from the earth. No female particles are left

[037-089]over to attract the male parts of the boat, and so they are not in the

[037-090]least attracted in that direction.”

[037-091]Maskull ruminated for a minute.

[037-092]“With your hunting, and boatbuilding, and science, you seem a very

[037-093]handy, skilful fellow, Haunte.... But the sun’s sinking, and we’d better

[037-094]start.”

[037-095]“Get down first, then, and shift that carcass farther forward. Then you

[037-096]and your gloomy friend can sit amidships.”

[037-097]Maskull immediately climbed down, and dropped himself into the boat; but

[037-098]then he received a surprise. The moment he stood on the frail bottom,

[037-099]still clinging to the rock, not only did his weight entirely disappear,

[037-100]as though he were floating in some heavy medium, like salt water, but

[037-101]the rock he held onto drew him, as by a mild current of electricity, and

[037-102]he was able to withdraw his hands only with difficulty.

[037-103]After the first moment’s shock, he quietly accepted the new order of

[037-104]things, and set about shifting the carcass. Since there was no weight in

[037-105]the boat this was effected without any great labour. Corpang then

[037-106]descended. The astonishing physical change had no power to disturb his

[037-107]settled composure, which was founded on moral ideas. Haunte came last;

[037-108]grasping the staff which held the upper male stone, he proceeded to

[037-109]erect it, after removing the cap. Maskull then obtained his first near

[037-110]view of the mysterious light, which, by counteracting the forces of

[037-111]Nature, acted indirectly not only as elevator but as motive force. In

[037-112]the last ruddy gleams of the great sun, its rays were obscured, and it

[037-113]looked little more impressive than an extremely brilliant, scintillating

[037-114]blue-white jewel, but its power could be gauged by the visible, coloured

[037-115]mist that it threw out for many yards around.

[037-116]The steering was effected by means of a shutter attached by a cord to

[037-117]the top of the staff, which could be so manipulated that any segment of

[037-118]the male stone’s rays, or all the rays, or none at all, could be shut

[037-119]off at will. No sooner was the staff raised than the aerial vessel

[037-120]quietly detached itself from the rock to which it had been drawn, and

[037-121]passed slowly forward in the direction of the mountains. Branchspell

[037-122]sank below the horizon. The gathering mist blotted out everything

[037-123]outside a radius of a few miles. The air grew cool and fresh.

[037-124]Soon the rock masses ceased on the great, rising plain. Haunte withdrew

[037-125]the shutter entirely, and the boat gathered full speed.

[037-126]“You say that navigation among the mountains is difficult at night,”

[037-127]exclaimed Maskull. “I would have thought it impossible.”

[037-128]Haunte grunted. “You will have to take risks, and think yourself

[037-129]fortunate if you come off with nothing worse than a cracked skull. But

[037-130]one thing I can tell you—if you go on disturbing me with your chitchat

[037-131]we shan’t get as far as the mountains.”

[037-132]Thereafter Maskull was silent.

[037-133]The twilight deepened; the murk grew denser. There was little to look

[037-134]at, but much to feel. The motion of the boat, which was due to the

[037-135]never-ending struggle between the male stones and the force of

[037-136]gravitation, resembled in an exaggerated fashion the violent tossing of

[037-137]a small craft on a choppy sea. The two passengers became unhappy.

[037-138]Haunte, from his seat in the stern, gazed at them sardonically with one

[037-139]eye. The darkness now came on rapidly.

[037-140]About ninety minutes after the commencement of the voyage they arrived

[037-141]at the foothills of Lichstorm. They began to mount. There was no

[037-142]daylight left to see by. Beneath them, however, on both sides of them

[037-143]and in the rear, the landscape was lighted up for a considerable

[037-144]distance by the now vivid blue rays of the twin male stones. Ahead,

[037-145]where these rays did not shine, Haunte was guided by the self-luminous

[037-146]nature of the rocks, grass, and trees. These were faintly

[037-147]phosphorescent; the vegetation shone out more strongly than the soil.

[037-148]The moon was not shining and there were no stars; Maskull therefore

[037-149]inferred that the upper atmosphere was dense with mist. Once or twice,

[037-150]from his sensations of choking, he thought that they were entering a

[037-151]fogbank, but it was a strange kind of fog, for it had the effect of

[037-152]doubling the intensity of every light in front of them. Whenever this

[037-153]happened, nightmare feelings attacked him; he experienced transitory,

[037-154]unreasoning fright and horror.

[037-155]Now they passed high above the valley that separated the foothills from

[037-156]the mountains themselves. The boat began an ascent of many thousands of

[037-157]feet and, as the cliffs were near, Haunte had to manoeuvre carefully

[037-158]with the rear light in order to keep clear of them. Maskull watched the

[037-159]delicacy of his movements, not without admiration. A long time went by.

[037-160]It grew much colder; the air was damp and drafty. The fog began to

[037-161]deposit something like snow on their persons. Maskull kept sweating with

[037-162]terror, not because of the danger they were in, but because of the cloud

[037-163]banks that continued to envelop them.

[037-164]They cleared the first line of precipices. Still mounting, but this time

[037-165]with a forward motion, as could be seen by the vapours illuminated by

[037-166]the male stones through which they passed, they were soon altogether out

[037-167]of sight of solid ground. Suddenly and quite unexpectedly the moon broke

[037-168]through. In the upper atmosphere thick masses of fog were seen crawling

[037-169]hither and thither, broken in many places by thin rifts of sky, through

[037-170]one of which Teargeld was shining. Below them, to their left, a gigantic

[037-171]peak, glittering with green ice, showed itself for a few seconds, and

[037-172]was then swallowed up again. All the rest of the world was hidden by the

[037-173]mist. The moon went in again. Maskull had seen quite enough to make him

[037-174]long for the aerial voyage to end.

[037-175]The light from the male stones presently illuminated the face of a new

[037-176]cliff. It was grand, rugged, and perpendicular. Upward, downward, and on

[037-177]both sides, it faded imperceptibly into the night. After coasting it a

[037-178]little way, they observed a shelf of rock jutting out. It was square,

[037-179]measuring about a dozen feet each way. Green snow covered it to a depth

[037-180]of some inches. Immediately behind it was a dark slit in the rock, which

[037-181]promised to be the mouth of a cave.

[037-182]Haunte skilfully landed the boat on this platform. Standing up, he

[037-183]raised the staff bearing the keel light and lowered the other; then

[037-184]removed both male stones, which he continued to hold in his hand. His

[037-185]face was thrown into strong relief by the vivid, sparkling blue-white

[037-186]rays. It looked rather surly.

[037-187]“Do we get out?” inquired Maskull.

[037-188]“Yes. I live here.”

[037-189]“Thanks for the successful end of a dangerous journey.”

[037-190]“Yes, it has been touch-and-go.”

[037-191]Corpang jumped onto the platform. He was smiling coarsely. “There has

[037-192]been no danger, for our destinies lie elsewhere. You are merely a

[037-193]ferryman, Haunte.”

[037-194]“Is that so?” returned Haunte, with a most unpleasant laugh. “I thought

[037-195]I was carrying men, not gods.”

[037-196]“Where are we?” asked Maskull. As he spoke, he got out, but Haunte

[037-197]remained standing a minute in the boat.
