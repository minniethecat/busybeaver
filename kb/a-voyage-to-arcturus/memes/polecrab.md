# Polecrab

## Role

Polecrab is the title character of Chapter 14, a rough fisherman encountered by [[maskull]] in the region of Matterplay. He represents the simplest mode of being in Lindsay's taxonomy: the life of pure material practicality, untroubled by philosophy, morality, or spiritual ambition. Polecrab trains sea-creatures to fish for him and eats with the unhurried contentment of "a man for whom time has no value" [014-9927].

## In the Novel

[[maskull|Maskull]] meets Polecrab by the shore, where the fisherman introduces himself simply: "Polecrab. What's yours?" [014-9815]. Polecrab's manner is brusque and physical — he grunts, pauses, and speaks in short, functional sentences. His relationship to the world is entirely practical: he has "trained close on a hundred" sea-creatures to fish for him [014-9855], and he has traveled "to Matterplay" on his raft [014-9875].

Polecrab's creatures — six in all — clutch fish from the water, and he takes the catch from them with practiced efficiency [014-9883]. He cooks the fish on intensely hot sand and shares the meal with [[maskull|Maskull]] [014-9919]. The scene is one of elemental simplicity: a man, his trained beasts, the catch, the fire, the food.

[[maskull|Maskull]]'s encounter with Polecrab is brief but significant. It offers a glimpse of a life lived entirely at the material level — not evil, not corrupt, simply untouched by the spiritual questions that drive [[maskull|Maskull]]'s journey. Polecrab does not need to transcend because he has never aspired.

## Philosophical Significance

In Lindsay's allegorical scheme, Polecrab represents the baseline of embodied existence. He is not a philosophy to be debated but a fact to be observed: there are beings who live entirely within the material world and are content there. His training of sea-creatures mirrors [[crystalman]]'s shaping of the material world itself — both operate through practical mastery rather than spiritual understanding.

Polecrab's function in the narrative is to show [[maskull|Maskull]] (and the reader) what is _left behind_ by the spiritual journey. The seeker cannot remain at Polecrab's level, but neither is Polecrab's life condemned — it is simply limited. The chapter serves as a rest stop in [[maskull|Maskull]]'s accelerating transformation, a moment of physical animal satisfaction before the intensifying spiritual trials to come.
