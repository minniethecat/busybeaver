# Section 20

[020-001]A faint sound of drumming came up. He listened intently, and as he did

[020-002]so his heart quickened and the black cares rolled away from his soul.

[020-003]All the world and its accidents seemed at that moment false, and without

[020-004]meaning....

[020-005]He climbed abstractedly to his feet. Tydomin was talking to her dead

[020-006]husband. She was peering into the hideous face of ivory, and fondling

[020-007]his violet hair. When she perceived Maskull, she hastily kissed the

[020-008]withered lips, and got up from her knees. Lifting the corpse with all

[020-009]three arms, she staggered with it to the extreme edge of the gulf and,

[020-010]after an instant’s hesitation, allowed it to drop into the lava. It

[020-011]disappeared immediately without sound; a metallic splash came up. That

[020-012]was Crimtyphon’s funeral.

[020-013]“Now I am ready, Maskull.”

[020-014]He did not answer, but stared past her. Another figure was standing,

[020-015]erect and mournful, not far behind her. It was Joiwind. Her face was

[020-016]wan, and there was an accusing look in her eyes. Maskull knew that it

[020-017]was a phantasm, and that the real Joiwind was miles away, at

[020-018]Poolingdred.

[020-019]“Turn around, Tydomin,” he said oddly, “and tell me what you see behind

[020-020]you.”

[020-021]“I don’t see anything,” she answered, looking around.

[020-022]“But I see Joiwind.”

[020-023]Just as he was speaking, the apparition vanished.

[020-024]“Now I present you with your life, Tydomin. She wishes it.”

[020-025]The woman fingered her chin thoughtfully.

[020-026]“I little expected I should ever be beholden for my life to one of my

[020-027]own sex—but so be it. What really happened to you in my cavern?”

[020-028]“I really saw Krag.”

[020-029]“Yes, some miracle must have taken place.” She suddenly shivered. “Come,

[020-030]let us leave this horrible spot. I shall never come here again.”

[020-031]“Yes,” said Maskull, “it stinks of death and dying. But where are we to

[020-032]go—what are we to do? Take me to Sant. I must get away from this hellish

[020-033]land.”

[020-034]Tydomin remained standing, dull and hollow-eyed. Then she gave an

[020-035]abrupt, bitter little laugh. “We make our journey together in singular

[020-036]stages. Rather than be alone, I’ll come with you—but you know that if I

[020-037]set foot in Sant they will kill me.”

[020-038]“At least set me on the way. I wish to get there before night. Is it

[020-039]possible?”

[020-040]“If you are willing to take risks with nature. And why should you not

[020-041]take risks today? Your luck holds. But someday or other it won’t

[020-042]hold—your luck.”

[020-043]“Let us start,” said Maskull. “The luck I’ve had so far is nothing to

[020-044]brag about.”

[020-045]Blodsombre was over when they set off; it was early afternoon, but the

[020-046]heat seemed more stifling than ever. They made no more pretence at

[020-047]conversation; both were buried in their own painful thoughts. The land

[020-048]fell away from Disscourn in all other directions, but toward Sant there

[020-049]was a gentle, persistent rise. Its dark, distant plateau continued to

[020-050]dominate the landscape, and after walking for an hour they seemed none

[020-051]the nearer to it. The air was stale and stagnant.

[020-052]By and by, an upright object, apparently the work of man, attracted

[020-053]Maskull’s notice. It was a slender tree stem, with the bark still on,

[020-054]imbedded in the stony ground. From the upper end three branches sprang

[020-055]out, pointing aloft at a sharp angle. They were stripped to twigs and

[020-056]leaves and, getting closer, he saw that they had been artificially

[020-057]fastened on, at equal distances from each other.

[020-058]As he stared at the object, a strange, sudden flush of confident vanity

[020-059]and self-sufficiency seemed to pass through him, but it was so momentary

[020-060]that he could be sure of nothing.

[020-061]“What may that be, Tydomin?”

[020-062]“It is Hator’s Trifork.”

[020-063]“And what is its purpose?”

[020-064]“It’s a guide to Sant.”

[020-065]“But who or what is Hator?”

[020-066]“Hator was the founder of Sant—many thousands of years ago. He laid down

[020-067]the principles they all live by, and that trifork is his symbol. When I

[020-068]was a little child my father told me the legends, but I’ve forgotten

[020-069]most of them.”

[020-070]Maskull regarded it attentively.

[020-071]“Does it affect you in any way?”

[020-072]“And why should it do that?” she said, dropping her lip scornfully. “I

[020-073]am only a woman, and these are masculine mysteries.”

[020-074]“A sort of gladness came over me,” said Maskull, “but perhaps I am

[020-075]mistaken.”

[020-076]They passed on. The scenery gradually changed in character. The solid

[020-077]parts of the land grew more continuous, the fissures became narrower and

[020-078]more infrequent. There were now no more subsidences or upheavals. The

[020-079]peculiar nature of the Ifdawn Marest appeared to be giving place to a

[020-080]different order of things.

[020-081]Later on, they encountered a flock of pale blue jellies floating in the

[020-082]air. They were miniature animals. Tydomin caught one in her hand and

[020-083]began to eat it, just as one eats a luscious pear plucked from a tree.

[020-084]Maskull, who had fasted since early morning, was not slow in following

[020-085]her example. A sort of electric vigour at once entered his limbs and

[020-086]body, his muscles regained their elasticity, his heart began to beat

[020-087]with hard, slow, strong throbs.

[020-088]“Food and body seem to agree well in this world,” he remarked smiling.

[020-089]She glanced toward him. “Perhaps the explanation is not in the food, but

[020-090]in your body.”

[020-091]“I brought my body with me.”

[020-092]“You brought your soul with you, but that’s altering fast, too.”

[020-093]In a copse they came across a short, wide tree, without leaves, but

[020-094]possessing a multitude of thin, flexible branches, like the tentacles of

[020-095]a cuttlefish. Some of these branches were moving rapidly. A furry

[020-096]animal, somewhat resembling a wildcat, leaped about among them in the

[020-097]most extraordinary way. But the next minute Maskull was shocked to

[020-098]realise that the beast was not leaping at all, but was being thrown from

[020-099]branch to branch by the volition of the tree, exactly as an imprisoned

[020-100]mouse is thrown by a cat from paw to paw.

[020-101]He watched the spectacle a while with morbid interest.

[020-102]“That’s a gruesome reversal of rôles, Tydomin.”

[020-103]“One can see you’re disgusted,” she replied, stifling a yawn. “But that

[020-104]is because you are a slave to words. If you called that plant an animal,

[020-105]you would find its occupation perfectly natural and pleasing. And why

[020-106]should you not call it an animal?”

[020-107]“I am quite aware that, as long as I remain in the Ifdawn Marest, I

[020-108]shall go on listening to this sort of language.”

[020-109]They trudged along for an hour or more without talking. The day became

[020-110]overcast. A thin mist began to shroud the landscape, and the sun changed

[020-111]into an immense ruddy disk which could be stared at without flinching. A

[020-112]chill, damp wind blew against them. Presently it grew still darker, the

[020-113]sun disappeared and, glancing first at his companion and then at

[020-114]himself, Maskull noticed that their skin and clothing were coated by a

[020-115]kind of green hoarfrost.

[020-116]The land was now completely solid. About half a mile, in front of them,

[020-117]against a background of dark fog, a moving forest of tall waterspouts

[020-118]gyrated slowly and gracefully hither and thither. They were green and

[020-119]self-luminous, and looked terrifying. Tydomin explained that they were

[020-120]not waterspouts at all, but mobile columns of lightning.

[020-121]“Then they are dangerous?”

[020-122]“So we think,” she answered, watching them closely.

[020-123]“Someone is wandering there who appears to have a different opinion.”

[020-124]Among the spouts, and entirely encompassed by them, a man was walking

[020-125]with a slow, calm, composed gait, his back turned toward Maskull and

[020-126]Tydomin. There was something unusual in his appearance—his form looked

[020-127]extraordinarily distinct, solid, and real.

[020-128]“If there’s danger, he ought to be warned,” said Maskull.

[020-129]“He who is always anxious to teach will learn nothing,” returned the

[020-130]woman coolly. She restrained Maskull by a pressure of the arm, and

[020-131]continued to watch.

[020-132]The base of one of the columns touched the man. He remained unharmed,

[020-133]but turned sharply around, as if for the first time made aware of the

[020-134]proximity of these deadly waltzers. Then he raised himself to his full

[020-135]height, and stretched both arms aloft above his head, like a diver. He

[020-136]seemed to be addressing the columns.

[020-137]While they looked on, the electric spouts discharged themselves, with a

[020-138]series of loud explosions. The stranger stood alone, uninjured. He

[020-139]dropped his arms. The next moment he caught sight of the two, and stood

[020-140]still, waiting for them to come up. The pictorial clarity of his person

[020-141]grew more and more noticeable as they approached; his body seemed to be

[020-142]composed of some substance heavier and denser than solid matter.

[020-143]Tydomin looked perplexed.

[020-144]“He must be a Sant man. I have seen no one quite like him before. This

[020-145]is a day of days for me.”

[020-146]“He must be an individual of great importance,” murmured Maskull.

[020-147]They now came up to him. He was tall, strong, and bearded, and was

[020-148]clothed in a shirt and breeches of skin. Since turning his back to the

[020-149]wind, the green deposit on his face and limbs had changed to streaming

[020-150]moisture, through which his natural colour was visible; it was that of

[020-151]pale iron. There was no third arm. His face was harsh and frowning, and

[020-152]a projecting chin pushed the beard forward. On his forehead there were

[020-153]two flat membranes, like rudimentary eyes, but no sorb. These membranes

[020-154]were expressionless, but in some strange way seemed to add vigour to the

[020-155]stern eyes underneath. When his glance rested on Maskull, the latter

[020-156]felt as though his brain were being thoroughly travelled through. The

[020-157]man was middle-aged.

[020-158]His physical distinctness transcended nature. By contrast with him,

[020-159]every object in the neighbourhood looked vague and blurred. Tydomin’s

[020-160]person suddenly appeared faint, sketch-like, without significance, and

[020-161]Maskull realised that it was no better with himself. A queer, quickening

[020-162]fire began running through his veins.

[020-163]He turned to the woman. “If this man is going to Sant, I shall bear him

[020-164]company. We can now part. No doubt you will think it high time.”

[020-165]“Let Tydomin come too.”

[020-166]The words were delivered in a rough, foreign tongue, but were as

[020-167]intelligible to Maskull as if spoken in English.

[020-168]“You who know my name, also know my sex,” said Tydomin quietly. “It is

[020-169]death for me to enter Sant.”

[020-170]“That is the old law. I am the bearer of the new law.”

[020-171]“Is it so—and will it be accepted?”

[020-172]“The old skin is cracking, the new skin has been silently forming

[020-173]underneath, the moment of sloughing has arrived.”

[020-174]The storm gathered. The green snow drove against them, as they stood

[020-175]talking, and it grew intensely cold. None noticed it.

[020-176]“What is your name?” asked Maskull, with a beating heart.

[020-177]“My name, Maskull, is Spadevil. You, a voyager across the dark ocean of

[020-178]space, shall be my first witness and follower. You, Tydomin, a daughter

[020-179]of the despised sex, shall be my second.”

[020-180]“The new law? But what is it?”

[020-181]“Until eye sees, of what use it is for ear to hear?.... Come, both of

[020-182]you, to me!”

[020-183]Tydomin went to him unhesitatingly. Spadevil pressed his hand on her

[020-184]sorb and kept it there for a few minutes, while he closed his own eyes.

[020-185]When he removed it, Maskull observed that the sorb was transformed into

[020-186]twin membranes like Spadevil’s own.

[020-187]Tydomin looked dazed. She glanced quietly about for a little while,

[020-188]apparently testing her new faculty. Then the tears started to her eyes

[020-189]and, snatching up Spadevil’s hand, she bent over and kissed it hurriedly

[020-190]many times.

[020-191]“My past has been bad,” she said. “Numbers have received harm from me,

[020-192]and none good. I have killed—and worse. But now I can throw all that

[020-193]away, and laugh. Nothing can now injure me. Oh, Maskull, you and I have

[020-194]been fools together!”

[020-195]“Don’t you repent your crimes?” asked Maskull.

[020-196]“Leave the past alone,” said Spadevil, “it cannot be reshaped. The

[020-197]future alone is ours. It starts fresh and clean from this very minute.

[020-198]Why do you hesitate, Maskull? Are you afraid?”
