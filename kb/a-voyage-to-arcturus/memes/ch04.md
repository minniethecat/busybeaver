# Section 4

[004-001]He passed through the open gate, followed by Nightspore, and knocked

[004-002]vigorously at the front door. The knocker was thick with dust and had

[004-003]obviously not been used for a long time. He put his ear to the door, but

[004-004]could hear no movements inside the house. He then tried the handle; the

[004-005]door was looked.

[004-006]They walked around the house, looking for another entrance, but there

[004-007]was only the one door.

[004-008]“This isn’t promising,” growled Maskull. “There’s no one here..... Now

[004-009]you try the shed, while I go over to that tower.”

[004-010]Nightspore, who had not spoken half a dozen words since leaving the

[004-011]train, complied in silence, and started off across the yard. Maskull

[004-012]passed out of the gate again. When he arrived at the foot of the tower,

[004-013]which stood some way back from the cliff, he found the door heavily

[004-014]padlocked. Gazing up, he saw six windows, one above the other at equal

[004-015]distances, all on the east face—that is, overlooking the sea. Realising

[004-016]that no satisfaction was to be gained here, he came away again, still

[004-017]more irritated than before. When he rejoined his friend, Nightspore

[004-018]reported that the workshop was also locked.

[004-019]“Did we, or did we not, receive an invitation?” demanded Maskull

[004-020]energetically.

[004-021]“The house is empty,” replied Nightspore, biting his nails. “Better

[004-022]break a window.”

[004-023]“I certainly don’t mean to camp out till Krag condescends to come.”

[004-024]He picked up an old iron bolt from the yard and, retreating to a safe

[004-025]distance, hurled it against a sash window on the ground floor. The lower

[004-026]pane was completely shattered. Carefully avoiding the broken glass,

[004-027]Maskull thrust his hand through the aperture and pushed back the frame

[004-028]fastening. A minute later they had climbed through and were standing

[004-029]inside the house.

[004-030]The room, which was a kitchen, was in an indescribably filthy and

[004-031]neglected condition. The furniture scarcely held together, broken

[004-032]utensils and rubbish lay on the floor instead of on the dust heap,

[004-033]everything was covered with a deep deposit of dust. The atmosphere was

[004-034]so foul that Maskull judged that no fresh air had passed into the room

[004-035]for several months. Insects were crawling on the walls.

[004-036]They went into the other rooms on the lower floor—a scullery, a barely

[004-037]furnished dining room, and a storing place for lumber. The same dirt,

[004-038]mustiness, and neglect met their eyes. At least half a year must have

[004-039]elapsed since these rooms were last touched, or even entered.

[004-040]“Does your faith in Krag still hold?” asked Maskull. “I confess mine is

[004-041]at vanishing point. If this affair isn’t one big practical joke, it has

[004-042]every promise of being one. Krag never lived here in his life.”

[004-043]“Come upstairs first,” said Nightspore.

[004-044]The upstairs rooms proved to consist of a library and three bedrooms.

[004-045]All the windows were tightly closed, and the air was insufferable. The

[004-046]beds had been slept in, evidently a long time ago, and had never been

[004-047]made since. The tumbled, discoloured bed linen actually preserved the

[004-048]impressions of the sleepers. There was no doubt that these impressions

[004-049]were ancient, for all sorts of floating dirt had accumulated on the

[004-050]sheets and coverlets.

[004-051]“Who could have slept here, do you think?” interrogated Maskull. “The

[004-052]observatory staff?”

[004-053]“More likely travellers like ourselves. They left suddenly.”

[004-054]Maskull flung the windows wide open in every room he came to, and held

[004-055]his breath until he had done so. Two of the bedrooms faced the sea; the

[004-056]third, the library, the upward-sloping moorland. This library was now

[004-057]the only room left unvisited, and unless they discovered signs of recent

[004-058]occupation here Maskull made up his mind to regard the whole business as

[004-059]a gigantic hoax.

[004-060]But the library, like all the other rooms, was foul with stale air and

[004-061]dust-laden. Maskull, having flung the window up and down, fell heavily

[004-062]into an armchair and looked disgustedly at his friend.

[004-063]“Now what is your opinion of Krag?”

[004-064]Nightspore sat on the edge of the table which stood before the window.

[004-065]“He may still have left a message for us.”

[004-066]“What message? Why? Do you mean in this room?—I see no message.”

[004-067]Nightspore’s eyes wandered about the room, finally seeming to linger

[004-068]upon a glass-fronted wall cupboard, which contained a few old bottles on

[004-069]one of the shelves and nothing else. Maskull glanced at him and at the

[004-070]cupboard. Then, without a word, he got up to examine the bottles.

[004-071]There were four altogether, one of which was larger than the rest. The

[004-072]smaller ones were about eight inches long. All were torpedo-shaped, but

[004-073]had flattened bottoms, which enabled them to stand upright. Two of the

[004-074]smaller ones were empty and unstoppered, the others contained a

[004-075]colourless liquid, and possessed queer-looking, nozzle-like stoppers

[004-076]that were connected by a thin metal rod with a catch halfway down the

[004-077]side of the bottle. They were labelled, but the labels were yellow with

[004-078]age and the writing was nearly undecipherable. Maskull carried the

[004-079]filled bottles with him to the table in front of the window, in order to

[004-080]get better light. Nightspore moved away to make room for him.

[004-081]He now made out on the larger bottle the words “Solar Back Rays”; and on

[004-082]the other one, after some doubt, he thought that he could distinguish

[004-083]something like “Arcturian Back Rays.”

[004-084]He looked up, to stare curiously at his friend. “Have you been here

[004-085]before, Nightspore?”

[004-086]“I guessed Krag would leave a message.”

[004-087]“Well, I don’t know—it may be a message, but it means nothing to us, or

[004-088]at all events to me. What are ‘back rays’?”

[004-089]“Light that goes back to its source,” muttered Nightspore.

[004-090]“And what kind of light would that be?”

[004-091]Nightspore seemed unwilling to answer, but, finding Maskull’s eyes still

[004-092]fixed on him, he brought out: “Unless light pulled, as well as pushed,

[004-093]how would flowers contrive to twist their heads around after the sun?”

[004-094]“I don’t know. But the point is, what are these bottles for?”

[004-095]While he was still talking, with his hand on the smaller bottle, the

[004-096]other, which was lying on its side, accidentally rolled over in such a

[004-097]manner that the metal caught against the table. He made a movement to

[004-098]stop it, his hand was actually descending, when—the bottle suddenly

[004-099]disappeared before his eyes. It had not rolled off the table, but had

[004-100]really vanished—it was nowhere at all.

[004-101]Maskull stared at the table. After a minute he raised his brows, and

[004-102]turned to Nightspore with a smile. “The message grows more intricate.”

[004-103]Nightspore looked bored. “The valve became unfastened. The contents have

[004-104]escaped through the open window toward the sun, carrying the bottle with

[004-105]them. But the bottle will be burned up by the earth’s atmosphere, and

[004-106]the contents will dissipate, and will not reach the sun.”

[004-107]Maskull listened attentively, and his smile faded. “Does anything

[004-108]prevent us from experimenting with this other bottle?”

[004-109]“Replace it in the cupboard,” said Nightspore. “Arcturus is still below

[004-110]the horizon, and you would succeed only in wrecking the house.”

[004-111]Maskull remained standing before the window, pensively gazing out at the

[004-112]sunlit moors.

[004-113]“Krag treats me like a child,” he remarked presently. “And perhaps I

[004-114]really am a child.... My cynicism must seem most amusing to Krag. But

[004-115]why does he leave me to find out all this by myself—for I don’t include

[004-116]you, Nightspore.... But what time will Krag be here?”

[004-117]“Not before dark, I expect,” his friend replied.

[004-119]It was by this time past three o’clock. Feeling hungry, for they had

[004-120]eaten nothing since early morning, Maskull went downstairs to forage,

[004-121]but without much hope of finding anything in the shape of food. In a

[004-122]safe in the kitchen he discovered a bag of mouldy oatmeal, which was

[004-123]untouchable, a quantity of quite good tea in an airtight caddy, and an

[004-124]unopened can of ox tongue. Best of all, in the dining-room cupboard he

[004-125]came across an uncorked bottle of first-class Scotch whisky. He at once

[004-126]made preparations for a scratch meal.

[004-127]A pump in the yard ran clear after a good deal of hard working at it,

[004-128]and he washed out and filled the antique kettle. For firewood, one of

[004-129]the kitchen chairs was broken up with a chopper. The light, dusty wood

[004-130]made a good blaze in the grate, the kettle was boiled, and cups were

[004-131]procured and washed. Ten minutes later the friends were dining in the

[004-132]library.

[004-133]Nightspore ate and drank little, but Maskull sat down with good

[004-134]appetite. There being no milk, whisky took the place of it; the nearly

[004-135]black tea was mixed with an equal quantity of the spirit. Of this

[004-136]concoction Maskull drank cup after cup, and long after the tongue had

[004-137]disappeared he was still imbibing.

[004-138]Nightspore looked at him queerly. “Do you intend to finish the bottle

[004-139]before Krag comes?”

[004-140]“Krag won’t want any, and one must do something. I feel restless.”

[004-141]“Let us take a look at the country.”

[004-142]The cup, which was on its way to Maskull’s lips, remained poised in the

[004-143]air. “Have you anything in view, Nightspore?”

[004-144]“Let us walk out to the Gap of Sorgie.”

[004-145]“What’s that?”

[004-146]“A showplace,” answered Nightspore, biting his lip.

[004-147]Maskull finished off the cup, and rose to his feet. “Walking is better

[004-148]than soaking at any time, and especially on a day like this.... How far

[004-149]is it?”

[004-150]“Three or four miles each way.”

[004-151]“You probably mean something,” said Maskull, “for I’m beginning to

[004-152]regard you as a second Krag. But if so, so much the better. I am growing

[004-153]nervous, and need incidents.”

[004-154]They left the house by the door, which they left ajar, and immediately

[004-155]found themselves again on the moorland road that had brought them from

[004-156]Haillar. This time they continued along it, past the tower.

[004-157]Maskull, as they went by, regarded the erection with puzzled interest.

[004-158]“What is that tower, Nightspore?”

[004-159]“We sail from the platform on the top.”

[004-160]“Tonight?”—throwing him a quick look.

[004-161]“Yes.”

[004-162]Maskull smiled, but his eyes were grave. “Then we are looking at the

[004-163]gateway of Arcturus, and Krag is now travelling north to unlock it.”

[004-164]“You no longer think it impossible, I fancy,” mumbled Nightspore.

[004-165]After a mile or two, the road parted from the sea coast and swerved

[004-166]sharply inland, across the hills. With Nightspore as guide, they left it

[004-167]and took to the grass. A faint sheep path marked the way along the cliff

[004-168]edge for some distance, but at the end of another mile it vanished. The

[004-169]two men then had some rough walking up and down hillsides and across

[004-170]deep gullies. The sun disappeared behind the hills, and twilight

[004-171]imperceptibly came on. They soon reached a spot where further progress

[004-172]appeared impossible. The buttress of a mountain descended at a steep

[004-173]angle to the very edge of the cliff, forming an impassable slope of

[004-174]slippery grass. Maskull halted, stroked his beard, and wondered what the

[004-175]next step was to be.

[004-176]“There’s a little scrambling here,” said Nightspore. “We are both used

[004-177]to climbing, and there is not much in it.”

[004-178]He indicated a narrow ledge, winding along the face of the precipice a

[004-179]few yards beneath where they were standing. It averaged from fifteen to

[004-180]thirty inches in width. Without waiting for Maskull’s consent to the

[004-181]undertaking, he instantly swung himself down and started walking along

[004-182]this ledge at a rapid pace. Maskull, seeing that there was no help for

[004-183]it, followed him. The shelf did not extend for above a quarter of a

[004-184]mile, but its passage was somewhat unnerving; there was a sheer drop to

[004-185]the sea, four hundred feet below. In a few places they had to sidle

[004-186]along without placing one foot before another. The sound of the breakers

[004-187]came up to them in a low, threatening roar.

[004-188]Upon rounding a corner, the ledge broadened out into a fair-sized

[004-189]platform of rock and came to a sudden end. A narrow inlet of the sea

[004-190]separated them from the continuation of the cliffs beyond.
