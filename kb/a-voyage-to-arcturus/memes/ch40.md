# Section 40

[040-001]“You are a good man. But you two alone will never strike the road to

[040-002]Adage.”

[040-003]“Are you acquainted with it?”

[040-004]Again the woman gripped Maskull’s arm. “What is love—which Corpang

[040-005]despises?”

[040-006]Maskull looked at her attentively. Sullenbode went on, “Love is that

[040-007]which is perfectly willing to disappear and become nothing, for the sake

[040-008]of the beloved.”

[040-009]Corpang wrinkled his forehead. “A magnanimous female lover is new in my

[040-010]experience.”

[040-011]Maskull put him aside with his hand, and said to Sullenbode, “Are you

[040-012]contemplating a sacrifice?”

[040-013]She gazed at her feet, and smiled. “What does it matter what my thoughts

[040-014]are? Tell me, are you starting at once, or do you mean to rest first?

[040-015]It’s a rough road to Adage.”

[040-016]“What’s in your mind?” demanded Maskull.

[040-017]“I will guide you a little. When we reach the ridge between Sarclash and

[040-018]Adage, perhaps I shall turn back.”

[040-019]“And then?”

[040-020]“Then if the moon shines perhaps you will arrive before daybreak, but if

[040-021]it is dark it’s hardly likely.”

[040-022]“That’s not what I meant. What will become of you after we have parted

[040-023]company?”

[040-024]“I shall return somewhere—perhaps here.”

[040-025]Maskull went close up to her, in order to study her face better. “Shall

[040-026]you sink back into—the old state?”

[040-027]“No, Maskull, thank heaven.”

[040-028]“Then how will you live?”

[040-029]Sullenbode calmly removed the hand which he had placed on her arm. There

[040-030]was a sort of swirling flame in her eyes. “And who said I would go on

[040-031]living?”

[040-032]Maskull blinked at her in bewilderment. A few moments passed before he

[040-033]spoke again. “You women are a sacrificing lot. You know I can’t leave

[040-034]you like this.”

[040-035]Their eyes met. Neither withdrew them, and neither felt embarrassed.

[040-036]“You will always be the most generous of men, Maskull. Now let us go....

[040-037]Corpang is a single-minded personage, and the least we others—who aren’t

[040-038]so single-minded—can do is to help him to his destination. We mustn’t

[040-039]inquire whether the destination of single-minded men is as a rule worth

[040-040]arriving at.”

[040-041]“If it is good for Maskull, it will be good for me.”

[040-042]“Well, no vessel can hold more than its appointed measure.”

[040-043]Corpang gave a wry smile. “During your long sleep you appear to have

[040-044]picked up wisdom.”

[040-045]“Yes, Corpang, I have met many men, and explored many minds.”

[040-046]As they moved off, Maskull remembered Haunte.

[040-047]“Can we not bury that poor fellow?”

[040-048]“By this time tomorrow we shall need burial ourselves. But I do not

[040-049]include Corpang.”

[040-050]“We have no tools, so you must have your way. You killed him, but I am

[040-051]the real murderer. I stole his protecting light.”

[040-052]“Surely that death is balanced by the life you have given me.” They left

[040-053]the spot in the direction opposite to that by which the three men had

[040-054]arrived. After a few steps, they came to green snow again. At the same

[040-055]time the flat ground ended, and they started to traverse a steep,

[040-056]pathless mountain slope. The snow and rocks glimmered, their own bodies

[040-057]shone; otherwise everything was dark. The mists swirled around them, but

[040-058]Maskull had no more nightmares. The breeze was cold, pure, and steady.

[040-059]They walked in file, Sullenbode leading; her movements were slow and

[040-060]fascinating. Corpang came last. His stern eyes saw nothing ahead but an

[040-061]alluring girl and a half-infatuated man.

[040-062]For a long time they continued crossing the rough and rocky slope,

[040-063]maintaining a slightly upward course. The angle was so steep that a

[040-064]false step would have been fatal. The high ground was on their right.

[040-065]After a while, the hillside on the left hand changed to level ground,

[040-066]and they seemed to have joined another spur of the mountain. The

[040-067]ascending slope on the right hand persisted for a few hundred yards

[040-068]more. Then Sullenbode bore sharply to the left, and they found level

[040-069]ground all around them.

[040-070]“We are on the ridge,” announced the woman, halting.

[040-071]The others came up to her, and at the same instant the moon burst

[040-072]through the clouds, illuminating the whole scene.

[040-073]Maskull uttered a cry. The wild, noble, lonely beauty of the view was

[040-074]quite unexpected. Teargeld was high in the sky to their left, shining

[040-075]down on them from behind. Straight in front, like an enormously wide,

[040-076]smoothly descending road, lay the great ridge which went on to Adage,

[040-077]though Adage itself was out of sight. It was never less than two hundred

[040-078]yards wide. It was covered with green snow, in some places entirely, but

[040-079]in other places the naked rocks showed through like black teeth. From

[040-080]where they stood they were unable to see the sides of the ridge, or what

[040-081]lay underneath. On the right hand, which was north, the landscape was

[040-082]blurred and indistinct. There were no peaks there; it was the distant,

[040-083]low-lying land of Barey. But on the left hand appeared a whole forest of

[040-084]mighty pinnacles, near and far, as far as the eye could see in

[040-085]moonlight. All glittered green, and all possessed the extraordinary

[040-086]hanging caps that characterised the Lichstorm range. These caps were of

[040-087]fantastic shapes, and each one was different. The valley directly

[040-088]opposite them was filled with rolling mist.

[040-089]Sarclash was a mighty mountain mass in the shape of a horseshoe. Its two

[040-090]ends pointed west, and were separated from each other by a mile or more

[040-091]of empty space. The northern end became the ridge on which they stood.

[040-092]The southern end was the long line of cliffs on that part of the

[040-093]mountain where Haunte’s cave was situated. The connecting curve was the

[040-094]steep slope they had just traversed. One peak of Sarclash was invisible.

[040-095]In the south-west many mountains raised their heads. In addition, a few

[040-096]summits, which must have been of extraordinary height, appeared over the

[040-097]south side of the horseshoe.

[040-098]Maskull turned round to put a question to Sullenbode, but when he saw

[040-099]her for the first time in moonlight the words he had framed died on his

[040-100]lips. The gashlike mouth no longer dominated her other features, and the

[040-101]face, pale as ivory and most femininely shaped, suddenly became almost

[040-102]beautiful. The lips were a long, womanish curve of rose-red. Her hair

[040-103]was a dark maroon. Maskull was greatly disturbed; he thought that she

[040-104]resembled a spirit, rather than a woman.

[040-105]“What puzzles you?” she asked, smiling.

[040-106]“Nothing. But I would like to see you by sunlight.”

[040-107]“Perhaps you never will.”

[040-108]“Your life must be most solitary.”

[040-109]She explored his features with her black, slow-gleaming eyes. “Why do

[040-110]you fear to speak your feelings, Maskull?”

[040-111]“Things seem to open up before me like a sunrise, but what it means I

[040-112]can’t say.”

[040-113]Sullenbode laughed outright. “It assuredly does not mean the approach of

[040-114]night.”

[040-115]Corpang, who had been staring steadily along the ridge, here abruptly

[040-116]broke in. “The road is plain now, Maskull. If you wish it, I’ll go on

[040-117]alone.”

[040-118]“No, we’ll go on together. Sullenbode will accompany us.”

[040-119]“A little way,” said the woman, “but not to Adage, to pit my strength

[040-120]against unseen powers. That light is not for me. I know how to renounce

[040-121]love, but I will never be a traitor to it.”

[040-122]“Who knows what we shall find on Adage, or what will happen? Corpang is

[040-123]as ignorant as myself.”

[040-124]Corpang looked him full in the face. “Maskull, you are quite well aware

[040-125]that you never dare approach that awful fire in the society of a

[040-126]beautiful woman.”

[040-127]Maskull gave an uneasy laugh. “What Corpang doesn’t tell you,

[040-128]Sullenbode, is that I am far better acquainted with Muspel-light than

[040-129]he, and that, but for a chance meeting with me, he would still be saying

[040-130]his prayers in Threal.”

[040-131]“Still, what he says must be true,” she replied, looking from one to the

[040-132]other.

[040-133]“And so I am not to be allowed to—”

[040-134]“So long as I am with you, I shall urge you onward, and not backward,

[040-135]Maskull.”

[040-136]“We need not quarrel yet,” he remarked, with a forced smile. “No doubt

[040-137]things will straighten themselves out.”

[040-138]Sullenbode began kicking the snow about with her foot. “I picked up

[040-139]another piece of wisdom in my sleep, Corpang.”

[040-140]“Tell it to me, then.”

[040-141]“Men who live by laws and rules are parasites. Others shed their

[040-142]strength to bring these laws out of nothing into the light of day, but

[040-143]the law-abiders live at their ease—they have conquered nothing for

[040-144]themselves.”

[040-145]“It is given to some to discover, and to others to preserve and perfect.

[040-146]You cannot condemn me for wishing Maskull well.”

[040-147]“No, but a child cannot lead a thunderstorm.”

[040-148]They started walking again along the centre of the ridge. All three were

[040-149]abreast, Sullenbode in the middle.

[040-150]The road descended by an easy gradient, and was for a long distance

[040-151]comparatively smooth. The freezing point seemed higher than on Earth,

[040-152]for the few inches of snow through which they trudged felt almost warm

[040-153]to their naked feet. Maskull’s soles were by now like tough hides. The

[040-154]moonlit snow was green and dazzling. Their slanting, abbreviated shadows

[040-155]were sharply defined, and red-black in colour. Maskull, who walked on

[040-156]Sullenbode’s right hand, looked constantly to the left, toward the

[040-157]galaxy of glorious distant peaks.

[040-158]“You cannot belong to this world,” said the woman. “Men of your stamp

[040-159]are not to be looked for here.”

[040-160]“No, I have come here from Earth.”

[040-161]“Is that larger than our world?”

[040-162]“Smaller, I think. Small, and overcrowded with men and women. With all

[040-163]those people, confusion would result but for orderly laws, and therefore

[040-164]the laws are of iron. As adventure would be impossible without

[040-165]encroaching on these laws, there is no longer any spirit of adventure

[040-166]among the Earthmen. Everything is safe, vulgar, and completed.”

[040-167]“Do men hate women there, and women men?”

[040-168]“No, the meeting of the sexes is sweet, though shameful. So poignant is

[040-169]the sweetness that the accompanying shame is ignored, with open eyes.

[040-170]There is no hatred, or only among a few eccentric persons.”

[040-171]“That shame surely must be the rudiment of our Lichstorm passion. But

[040-172]now say—why did you come here?”

[040-173]“To meet with new experiences, perhaps. The old ones no longer

[040-174]interested me.”

[040-175]“How long have you been in this world?”

[040-176]“This is the end of my fourth day.”

[040-177]“Then tell me what you have seen and done during those four days. You

[040-178]cannot have been inactive.”

[040-179]“Great misfortunes have happened to me.”

[040-180]He proceeded briefly to relate everything that had taken place from the

[040-181]moment of his first awakening in the scarlet desert. Sullenbode

[040-182]listened, with half-closed eyes, nodding her head from time to time.

[040-183]Only twice did she interrupt him. After his description of Tydomin’s

[040-184]death, she said, speaking in a low voice—“None of us women ought by

[040-185]right of nature to fall short of Tydomin in sacrifice. For that one act

[040-186]of hers, I almost love her, although she brought evil to your door.”

[040-187]Again, speaking of Gleameil, she remarked, “That grand-souled girl I

[040-188]admire the most of all. She listened to her inner voice, and to nothing

[040-189]else besides. Which of us others is strong enough for that?”

[040-190]When his tale was quite over, Sullenbode said, “Does it not strike you,

[040-191]Maskull, that these women you have met have been far nobler than the

[040-192]men?”

[040-193]“I recognise that. We men often sacrifice ourselves, but only for a

[040-194]substantial cause. For you women almost any cause will serve. You love

[040-195]the sacrifice for its own sake, and that is because you are naturally

[040-196]noble.”

[040-197]Turning her head a little, she threw him a smile so proud, yet so sweet,

[040-198]that he was struck into silence.

[040-199]They tramped on quietly for some distance, and then he said, “Now you

[040-200]understand the sort of man I am. Much brutality, more weakness, scant

[040-201]pity for anyone—Oh, it has been a bloody journey!”

[040-202]She laid her hand on his arm. “I, for one, would not have it less

[040-203]rugged.”

[040-204]“Nothing good can be said of my crimes.”
