# Section 19

[019-001]The green snow on the top of the gigantic pyramid had by now completely

[019-002]melted away. The black, gold, and crimson of its mighty cliffs stood out

[019-003]with terrific brilliance. They were directly beneath the bulk of the

[019-004]mountain, which was not a mile away. It did not appear dangerous to

[019-005]climb, but he was unaware on which side of it their destination lay.

[019-006]It was split from top to bottom by numerous straight fissures. A few

[019-007]pale-green waterfalls descended here and there, like narrow, motionless

[019-008]threads. The face of the mountain was rugged and bare. It was strewn

[019-009]with detached boulders, and great, jagged rocks projected everywhere

[019-010]like iron teeth. Tydomin pointed to a small black hole near the base,

[019-011]which might be a cave. “That is where I live.”

[019-012]“You live here alone?”

[019-013]“Yes.”

[019-014]“It’s an odd choice for a woman—and you are not unbeautiful, either.”

[019-015]“A woman’s life is over at twenty-five,” she replied, sighing. “And I am

[019-016]far older than that. Ten years ago it would have been I who lived

[019-017]yonder, and not Oceaxe. Then all this wouldn’t have happened.”

[019-018]*****

[019-019]A quarter of an hour later they stood within the mouth of the cave. It

[019-020]was ten feet high, and its interior was impenetrably black.

[019-021]“Put down the body in the entrance, out of the sun,” directed Tydomin.

[019-022]He did so.

[019-023]She cast a keenly scrutinising glance at him. “Does your resolution

[019-024]still hold, Maskull?”

[019-025]“Why shouldn’t it hold? My brains are not feathers.”

[019-026]“Follow me, then.”

[019-027]They both stepped into the cave. At that very moment a sickening crash,

[019-028]like heavy thunder just over their heads, set Maskull’s weakened heart

[019-029]thumping violently. An avalanche of boulders, stones, and dust, swept

[019-030]past the cave entrance from above. If their going in had been delayed by

[019-031]a single minute, they would have been killed.

[019-032]Tydomin did not even look up. She took his hand in hers, and started

[019-033]walking with him into the darkness. The temperature became as cold as

[019-034]ice. At the first bend the light from the outer world disappeared,

[019-035]leaving them in absolute blackness. Maskull kept stumbling over the

[019-036]uneven ground, but she kept tight hold of him, and hurried him along.

[019-037]The tunnel seemed of interminable length. Presently, however, the

[019-038]atmosphere changed—or such was his impression. He was somehow led to

[019-039]imagine that they had come to a larger chamber. Here Tydomin stopped,

[019-040]and then forced him down with quiet pressure. His groping hand

[019-041]encountered stone and, by feeling it all over, he discovered that it was

[019-042]a sort of stone slab, or couch, raised a foot or eighteen inches from

[019-043]the ground. She told him to lie down.

[019-044]“Has the time come?” asked Maskull.

[019-045]“Yes.”

[019-046]He lay there waiting in the darkness, ignorant of what was going to

[019-047]happen. He felt her hand clasping his. Without perceiving any gradation,

[019-048]he lost all consciousness of his body; he was no longer able to feel his

[019-049]limbs or internal organs. His mind remained active and alert. Nothing

[019-050]particular appeared to be taking place.

[019-051]Then the chamber began to grow light, like very early morning. He could

[019-052]see nothing, but the retina of his eyes was affected. He fancied that he

[019-053]heard music, but while he was listening for it, it stopped. The light

[019-054]grew stronger, the air grew warmer; he heard the confused sound of

[019-055]distant voices.

[019-056]Suddenly Tydomin gave his hand a powerful squeeze. He heard someone

[019-057]scream faintly, and then the light leaped up, and he saw everything

[019-058]clearly.

[019-059]He was lying on a wooden couch, in a strangely decorated room, lighted

[019-060]by electricity. His hand was being squeezed, not by Tydomin, but by a

[019-061]man dressed in the garments of civilisation, with whose face he was

[019-062]certainly familiar, but under what circumstances he could not recall.

[019-063]Other people stood in the background—they too were vaguely known to him.

[019-064]He sat up and began to smile, without any especial reason; and then

[019-065]stood upright.

[019-066]Everybody seemed to be watching him with anxiety and emotion—he wondered

[019-067]why. Yet he felt that they were all acquaintances. Two in particular he

[019-068]knew—the man at the farther end of the room, who paced restlessly

[019-069]backward and forward, his face transfigured by stern, holy grandeur; and

[019-070]that other big, bearded man—who was himself. Yes—he was looking at his

[019-071]own double. But it was just as if a crime-riddled man of middle age were

[019-072]suddenly confronted with his own photograph as an earnest, idealistic

[019-073]youth.

[019-074]His other self spoke to him. He heard the sounds, but did not comprehend

[019-075]the sense. Then the door was abruptly flung open, and a short, brutish-

[019-076]looking individual leaped in. He began to behave in an extraordinary

[019-077]manner to everyone around him; and after that came straight up to

[019-078]him—Maskull. He spoke some words, but they were incomprehensible. A

[019-079]terrible expression came over the newcomer’s face, and he grasped his

[019-080]neck with a pair of hairy hands. Maskull felt his bones bending and

[019-081]breaking, excruciating pains passed through all the nerves of his body,

[019-082]and he experienced a sense of impending death. He cried out, and sank

[019-083]helplessly on the floor, in a heap. The chamber and the company

[019-084]vanished—the light went out.

[019-085]Once more he found himself in the blackness of the cave. He was this

[019-086]time lying on the ground, but Tydomin was still with him, holding his

[019-087]hand. He was in horrible bodily agony, but this was only a setting for

[019-088]the despairing anguish that filled his mind.

[019-089]Tydomin addressed him in tones of gentle reproach. “Why are you back so

[019-090]soon? I’ve not had time yet. You must return.”

[019-091]He caught hold of her, and pulled himself up to his feet. She gave a low

[019-092]scream, as though in pain. “What does this mean—what are you doing,

[019-093]Maskull?”

[019-094]“Krag—” began Maskull, but the effort to produce his words choked him,

[019-095]so that he was obliged to stop.

[019-096]“Krag—what of Krag? Tell me quickly what has happened. Free my arm.”

[019-097]He gripped her arm tighter.

[019-098]“Yes, I’ve seen Krag. I’m awake.”

[019-099]“Oh! You are awake, awake.”

[019-100]“And you must die,” said Maskull, in an awful voice.

[019-101]“But why? What has happened?...”

[019-102]“You must die, and I must kill you. Because I am awake, and for no other

[019-103]reason. You blood-stained dancing mistress!”

[019-104]Tydomin breathed hard for a little time. Then she seemed suddenly to

[019-105]regain her self-possession.

[019-106]“You won’t offer me violence, surely, in this black cave?”

[019-107]“No, the sun shall look on, for it is not a murder. But rest assured

[019-108]that you must die—you must expiate your fearful crimes.”

[019-109]“You have already said so, and I see you have the power. You have

[019-110]escaped me. It is very curious. Well, then, Maskull, let us come

[019-111]outside. I am not afraid. But kill me courteously, for I have also been

[019-112]courteous to you. I make no other supplication.”

[019-113]Chapter 11. ON DISSCOURN

[019-114]BY THE TIME that they regained the mouth of the cavern, Blodsombre was

[019-115]at its height. In front of them the scenery sloped downward—a long

[019-116]succession of mountain islands in a sea of clouds. Behind them the

[019-117]bright, stupendous crags of Disscourn loomed up for a thousand feet or

[019-118]more. Maskull’s eyes were red, and his face looked stupid; he was still

[019-119]holding the woman by the arm. She made no attempt to speak, or to get

[019-120]away. She seemed perfectly gentle and composed.

[019-121]After gazing at the country for a long time in silence, he turned toward

[019-122]her. “Whereabouts is the fiery lake you spoke of?”

[019-123]“It lies on the other side of the mountain. But why do you ask?”

[019-124]“It is just as well if we have some way to walk. I shall grow calmer,

[019-125]and that’s what I want. I wish you to understand that what is going to

[019-126]happen is not a murder, but an execution.”

[019-127]“It will taste the same,” said Tydomin.

[019-128]“When I have gone out of this country, I don’t wish to feel that I have

[019-129]left a demon behind me, wandering at large. That would not be fair to

[019-130]others. So we will go to the lake, which promises an easy death for

[019-131]you.”

[019-132]She shrugged her shoulders. “We must wait till Blodsombre is over.”

[019-133]“Is this a time for luxurious feelings? However hot it is now, we will

[019-134]both be cool by evening. We must start at once.”

[019-135]“Without doubt, you are the master, Maskull.... May I not carry

[019-136]Crimtyphon?”

[019-137]Maskull looked at her strangely.

[019-138]“I grudge no man his funeral.”

[019-139]She painfully hoisted the body on her narrow shoulders, and they stepped

[019-140]out into the sunlight. The heat struck them like a blow on the head.

[019-141]Maskull moved aside, to allow her to precede him, but no compassion

[019-142]entered his heart. He brooded over the wrongs the woman had done him.

[019-143]The way went along the south side of the great pyramid, near its base.

[019-144]It was a rough road, clogged with boulders and crossed by cracks and

[019-145]water gullies; they could see the water, but could not get at it. There

[019-146]was no shade. Blisters formed on their skin, while all the water in

[019-147]their blood seemed to dry up.

[019-148]Maskull forgot his own tortures in his devil’s delight at Tydomin’s.

[019-149]“Sing me a song!” he called out presently. “A characteristic one.”

[019-150]She turned her head and gave him a long, peculiar look; then, without

[019-151]any sort of expostulation, started singing. Her voice was low and weird.

[019-152]The song was so extraordinary that he had to rub his eyes to ascertain

[019-153]whether he was awake or dreaming. The slow surprises of the grotesque

[019-154]melody began to agitate him in a horrible fashion; the words were pure

[019-155]nonsense—or else their significance was too deep for him.

[019-156]“Where, in the name of all unholy things, did you acquire that stuff,

[019-157]woman?”

[019-158]Tydomin shed a sickly smile, while the corpse swayed about with ghastly

[019-159]jerks over her left shoulder. She held it in position with her two left

[019-160]arms. “It’s a pity we could not have met as friends, Maskull. I could

[019-161]have shown you a side of Tormance which now perhaps you will never see.

[019-162]The wild, mad side. But now it’s too late, and it doesn’t matter.”

[019-163]They turned the angle of the mountain, and started to traverse the

[019-164]western base.

[019-165]“Which is the quickest way out of this miserable land?” asked Maskull.

[019-166]“It is easiest to go to Sant.”

[019-167]“Will we see it from anywhere?”

[019-168]“Yes, though it is a long way off.”

[019-169]“Have you been there?”

[019-170]“I am a woman, and interdicted.”

[019-171]“True. I have heard something of the sort.”

[019-172]“But don’t ask me any more questions,” said Tydomin, who was becoming

[019-173]faint.

[019-174]Maskull stopped at a little spring. He himself drank, and then made a

[019-175]cup of his hand for the woman, so that she might not have to lay down

[019-176]her burden. The gnawl water acted like magic—it seemed to replenish all

[019-177]the cells of his body as though they had been thirsty sponge pores,

[019-178]sucking up liquid. Tydomin recovered her self-possession.

[019-179]About three-quarters of an hour later they worked around the second

[019-180]corner, and entered into full view of the north aspect of Disscourn.

[019-181]A hundred yards lower down the slope on which they were walking, the

[019-182]mountain ended abruptly in a chasm. The air above it was filled with a

[019-183]sort of green haze, which trembled violently like the atmosphere

[019-184]immediately over a furnace.

[019-185]“The lake is underneath,” said Tydomin.

[019-186]Maskull looked curiously about him. Beyond the crater the country sloped

[019-187]away in a continuous descent to the skyline. Behind them, a narrow path

[019-188]channelled its way up through the rocks toward the towering summit of

[019-189]the pyramid. Miles away, in the north-east quarter, a long, flat-topped

[019-190]plateau raised its head far above all the surrounding country. It was

[019-191]Sant—and there and then he made up his mind that that should be his

[019-192]destination that day.

[019-193]Tydomin meanwhile had walked straight to the gulf, and set down

[019-194]Crimtyphon’s body on the edge. In a minute or two, Maskull joined her;

[019-195]arrived at the brink, he immediately flung himself at full length on his

[019-196]chest, to see what could be seen of the lake of fire. A gust of hot,

[019-197]asphyxiating air smote his face and set him coughing, but he did not get

[019-198]up until he had stared his fill at the huge sea of green, molten lava,

[019-199]tossing and swirling at no great distance below, like a living will.
