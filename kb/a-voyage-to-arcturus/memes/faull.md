# Montague Faull

## Role

Montague Faull is the host of the séance at Prolands, his Hampstead residence, which opens the novel. As the framing-narrative figure who brings together [[backhouse]] ([[backhouse|the medium]]), [[nightspore]], and the other guests on that March evening, Faull is the proximate cause of the entire journey that follows. Though he appears only in Chapter 1, his role as the mundane gateway to the cosmic drama makes him structurally significant: he represents the world of ordinary curiosity that makes contact with forces beyond its comprehension.

## Appearance and Manner

Faull is introduced with a vivid physical description that emphasises his worldly, metallic quality:

> Faull's prominent, clear-cut features, metallic-looking skin, and general air of bored impassiveness, did not seem greatly to impress the medium. [001-003]

He is a "South American merchant" — a man of commerce and wealth, accustomed to comfort and control. His manner is languid and drawling; he "sprawled in his chair" [001-011] and regards [[backhouse|Backhouse]] through "half closed lids and the smoke of a cigar" [001-003].

The visual detail of "metallic-looking skin" is striking. In a novel where physical appearance often encodes metaphysical nature, Faull's metallic complexion suggests something hardened, inorganic, and sealed-off — a man for whom the spiritual world exists as a curiosity to be purchased, not a reality to be lived.

> "Do you smoke?" drawled Faull, by way of starting the conversation. "No? Then will you take a drink?" [001-005]

His opening conversational gambits are the rituals of a man comfortable with material pleasures but awkward with the immaterial.

## The Séance

Faull has organised the séance with the enthusiasm of a theatrical producer. He has "the decoration of the séance room, the music, and so forth" in mind [001-013], treating the supernatural as a performance to be staged. [[backhouse|Backhouse]]'s curt response — "But this is not a theatrical performance" [001-014] — rebukes Faull's approach, but Faull persists in his impresario role.

He has a check written out for [[backhouse|Backhouse]]'s services [001-008] and has personally invited the guests, including [[nightspore|Nightspore]] — whose presence he seems to find faintly disreputable but intriguing. When his sister arrives as an unexpected guest, Faull gives her "a queer look" [001-031], suggesting an undercurrent of family tension beneath the social surface.

At the climactic moment, Faull "stepped up to the apparatus" [001-042], positioning himself as master of ceremonies. When things go wrong — when the séance produces [[krag]] instead of the expected spirit — Faull's reaction is to try to maintain control:

> Faull preserved his stoical apathy, and glanced once at the medium. [001-058]

His "stoical apathy" is both admirable composure and a kind of spiritual deadness. The genuinely supernatural has entered his drawing room, and his response is to glance at [[backhouse|the medium]] to see if this is part of the show.

## Philosophical Significance

Faull embodies the secular, materialist approach to the spiritual: he wants it packaged, controlled, and entertaining. He is the novel's representative of what we might call the *tourist* attitude toward transcendence — interested, willing to pay, but ultimately insulated by wealth and social position from any genuine encounter with the unknown.

His "metallic-looking skin" takes on symbolic weight in retrospect. If [[tormance|Tormance]] is the world of [[crystalman]] — a world of surfaces and distortions — then Faull, in his metallic impassiveness, is already a citizen of that world without having left Earth. The séance he hosts is not an escape from the [[crystalman|Crystalman]] reality but an expression of it: a commodified, managed, safely bounded encounter with "the beyond."

The fact that Faull disappears from the narrative after Chapter 1 is itself meaningful. The cosmic journey leaves him behind; he is not called, not chosen, not transformed. He remains in Hampstead, presumably to host other evenings, while [[maskull]] (who was not even present at the séance) becomes the vehicle for the journey Faull tried to purchase.
