# Section 33

[033-001]“This is a cheerless philosophy,” said Maskull. “But who is Faceny

[033-002]himself, then, and why does he think at all?”

[033-003]Leehallfae gave another wrinkled smile. “That I’ll explain too. Faceny

[033-004]is of this nature. He faces Nothingness in all directions. He has no

[033-005]back and no sides, but is all face; and this face is his shape. It must

[033-006]necessarily be so, for nothing else can exist between him and

[033-007]Nothingness. His face is all eyes, for he eternally contemplates

[033-008]Nothingness. He draws his inspirations from it; in no other way could he

[033-009]feel himself. For the same reason, phaens and even men love to be in

[033-010]empty places and vast solitudes, for each one is a little Faceny.”

[033-011]“That rings true,” said Maskull.

[033-012]“Thoughts flow perpetually from Faceny’s face backward. Since his face

[033-013]is on all sides, however, they flow into his interior. A draught of

[033-014]thought thus continuously flows from Nothingness to the inside of

[033-015]Faceny, which is the world. The thoughts become shapes, and people the

[033-016]world. This outer world, therefore, which is lying all around us, is not

[033-017]outside at all, as it happens, but inside. The visible universe is like

[033-018]a gigantic stomach, and the real outside of the world we shall never

[033-019]see.”

[033-020]Maskull pondered deeply for a while.

[033-021]“Leehallfae, I fail to see what you personally have to hope for, since

[033-022]you are nothing more than a discarded, dying thought.”

[033-023]“Have you never loved a woman?” asked the phaen, regarding him fixedly.

[033-024]“Perhaps I have.”

[033-025]“When you loved, did you have no high moments?”

[033-026]“That’s asking the same question in other words.”

[033-027]“In those moments you were approaching Faceny. If you could have drawn

[033-028]nearer still, would you not have done so?”

[033-029]“I would, regardless of the consequences.”

[033-030]“Even if you personally had nothing to hope for?”

[033-031]“But I would have that to hope for.”

[033-032]Leehallfae walked on in silence.

[033-033]“A man is the half of Life,” ae broke out suddenly. “A woman is the

[033-034]other half of life, but a phaen is the whole of life. Moreover, when

[033-035]life becomes split into halves, something else has dropped out of

[033-036]it—something that belongs only to the whole. Between your love and mine

[033-037]there is no comparison. If even your sluggish blood is drawn to Faceny,

[033-038]without stopping to ask what will come of it, how do you suppose it is

[033-039]with me?”

[033-040]“I don’t question the genuineness of your passion,” replied Maskull,

[033-041]“but it’s a pity you can’t see your way to carry it forward into the

[033-042]next world.”

[033-043]Leehallfae gave a distorted grin, expressing heaven knows what emotion.

[033-044]“Men think what they like, but phaens are so made that they can see the

[033-045]world only as it really is.”

[033-046]That ended the conversation.

[033-047]The sun was high in the sky, and they appeared to be approaching the

[033-048]head of the ravine. Its walls had still further closed in and, except at

[033-049]those moments when Branchspell was directly behind them, they strode

[033-050]along all the time in deep shade; but still it was disagreeably hot and

[033-051]relaxing. All life had ceased. A beautiful, fantastic spectacle was

[033-052]presented by the cliff faces, the rocky ground, and the boulders that

[033-053]choked the entire width of the gorge. They were of a snow-white

[033-054]crystalline limestone, heavily scored by veins of bright, gleaming blue.

[033-055]The rivulet was no longer green, but a clear, transparent crystal. Its

[033-056]noise was musical, and altogether it looked most romantic and charming,

[033-057]but Leehallfae seemed to find something else in it—aer features grew

[033-058]more and more set and tortured.

[033-059]About half an hour after all the other life forms had vanished, another

[033-060]plant-animal was precipitated out of space, in front of their eyes. It

[033-061]was as tall as Maskull himself, and had a brilliant and vigorous

[033-062]appearance, as befitted a creature just out of Nature’s mint. It started

[033-063]to walk about; but hardly had it done so when it burst silently asunder.

[033-064]Nothing remained of it—the whole body disappeared instantaneously into

[033-065]the same invisible mist from which it had sprung.

[033-066]“That bears out what you said,” commented Maskull, turning rather pale.

[033-067]“Yes,” answered Leehallfae, “we have now come to the region of terrible

[033-068]life.”

[033-069]“Then, since you’re right in this, I must believe all that you’ve been

[033-070]telling me.”

[033-071]As he uttered the words, they were just turning a bend of the ravine.

[033-072]There now loomed up straight ahead a perpendicular cliff about three

[033-073]hundred feet in height, composed of white, marbled rock. It was the head

[033-074]of the valley, and beyond it they could not proceed.

[033-075]“In return for my wisdom,” said the phaen, “you will now lend me your

[033-076]luck.”

[033-077]They walked up to the base of the cliff, and Maskull looked at it

[033-078]reflectively. It was possible to climb it, but the ascent would be

[033-079]difficult. The now tiny brook issued from a hole in the rock only a few

[033-080]feet up. Apart from its musical running, not a sound was to be heard.

[033-081]The floor of the gorge was in shadow, but about halfway up the precipice

[033-082]the sun was shining.

[033-083]“What do you want me to do?” demanded Maskull.

[033-084]“Everything is now in your hands, and I have no suggestions to make.

[033-085]Now it’s your luck that must help us.”

[033-086]Maskull continued gazing up a little while longer. “We had better wait

[033-087]till the afternoon, Leehallfae. I’ll probably have to climb to the top,

[033-088]but it’s too hot at present—and besides, I’m tired. I’ll snatch a few

[033-089]hours’ sleep. After that, we’ll see.”

[033-090]Leehallfae seemed annoyed, but raised no opposition.

[033-092]Maskull did not awaken till long after Blodsombre. Leehallfae was

[033-093]standing by his side, looking down at him. It was doubtful whether ae

[033-094]had slept at all.

[033-095]“What time is it?” Maskull asked, rubbing his eyes and sitting up.

[033-096]“The day is passing,” was the vague reply.

[033-097]Maskull got on to his feet, and gazed up at the cliff. “Now I’m going to

[033-098]climb that. No need for both of us to risk our necks, so you wait here,

[033-099]and if I find anything on top I’ll call you.”

[033-100]A phaen glanced at him strangely. “There’s nothing up there except a

[033-101]bare hillside. I’ve been there often. Have you anything special in

[033-102]mind?”

[033-103]“Heights often bring me inspiration. Sit down, and wait.”

[033-104]Refreshed by his sleep, Maskull immediately attacked the face of the

[033-105]cliff, and took the first twenty feet at a single rush. Then it grew

[033-106]precipitous, and the ascent demanded greater circumspection and

[033-107]intelligence. There were few hand- or footholds: he had to reflect

[033-108]before every step. On the other hand, it was sound rock, and he was no

[033-109]novice at the sport. Branchspell glared full on the wall, so that it

[033-110]half blinded him with its glittering whiteness.

[033-111]After many doubts and pauses he drew near the top. He was hot, sweating

[033-112]copiously, and rather dizzy. To reach a ledge he caught hold of two

[033-113]projecting rocks, one with each hand, at the same time scrambling

[033-114]upward, his legs between the rocks. The left-hand rock, which was the

[033-115]larger of the two, became dislodged by his weight, and, flying like a

[033-116]huge, dark shadow past his head, crashed down with a terrifying sound to

[033-117]the foot of the precipice, followed by an avalanche of smaller stones.

[033-118]Maskull steadied himself as well as he could, but it was some moments

[033-119]before he dared to look down behind him.

[033-120]At first he could not distinguish Leehallfae. Then he caught sight of

[033-121]legs and hindquarters a few feet up the cliff from the bottom. He

[033-122]perceived that the phaen had aer head in a cavity and was scrutinising

[033-123]something, and waited for aer to reappear.

[033-124]Ae emerged, looked up to Maskull, and called out in aer hornlike voice,

[033-125]“The entrance is here!”

[033-126]“I’m coming down!” roared Maskull. “Wait for me!”

[033-127]He descended swiftly—without taking too much care, for he thought he

[033-128]recognised his “luck” in this discovery—and within twenty minutes was

[033-129]standing beside the phaen.

[033-130]“What happened?”

[033-131]“The rock you dislodged struck this other rock just above the spring. It

[033-132]tore it out of its bed. See—now there’s room for us to get in!”

[033-133]“Don’t get excited!” said Maskull. “It’s a remarkable accident, but we

[033-134]have plenty of time. Let me look.”

[033-135]He peered into the hole, which was large enough to admit a big man

[033-136]without stooping. Contrasted with the daylight outside it was dark, yet

[033-137]a peculiar glow pervaded the place, and he could see well enough. A rock

[033-138]tunnel went straight forward into the bowels of the hill, out of sight.

[033-139]The valley brook did not flow along the floor of this tunnel, as he had

[033-140]expected, but came up as a spring just inside the entrance.

[033-141]“Well Leehallfae, not much need to deliberate, eh? Still, observe that

[033-142]your stream parts company with us here.”

[033-143]As he turned around for an answer he noticed that his companion was

[033-144]trembling from head to foot.

[033-145]“Why, what’s the matter?”

[033-146]Leehallfae pressed a hand to aer heart. “The stream leaves us, but what

[033-147]makes the stream what it is continues with us. Faceny is there.”

[033-148]“But surely you don’t expect to see him in person? Why are you shaking?”

[033-149]“Perhaps it will be too much for me after all.”

[033-150]“Why? How is it affecting you?”

[033-151]The phaen took him by the shoulder and held him at arm’s length,

[033-152]endeavouring to study him with aer unsteady eyes. “Faceny’s thoughts are

[033-153]obscure. I am his lover, you are a lover of women, yet he grants to you

[033-154]what he denies to me.”

[033-155]“What does he grant to me?”

[033-156]“To see him, and go on living. I shall die. But it’s immaterial.

[033-157]Tomorrow both of us will be dead.”

[033-158]Maskull impatiently shook himself free. “Your sensations may be reliable

[033-159]in your own case, but how do you know I shall die?”

[033-160]“Life is flaming up inside you,” replied Leehallfae, shaking aer head.

[033-161]“But after it has reached its climax—perhaps tonight—it will sink

[033-162]rapidly and you’ll die tomorrow. As for me, if I enter Threal I shan’t

[033-163]come out again. A smell of death is being wafted to me out of this

[033-164]hole.”

[033-165]“You talk like a frightened man. I smell nothing.”

[033-166]“I am not frightened,” said Leehallfae quietly—ae had been gradually

[033-167]recovering aer tranquillity—“but when one has lived as long as I have,

[033-168]it is a serious matter to die. Every year one puts out new roots.”

[033-169]“Decide what you’re going to do,” said Maskull with a touch of contempt,

[033-170]“for I’m going in at once.”

[033-171]The phaen gave an odd, meditative stare down the ravine, and after that

[033-172]walked into the cavern without another word. Maskull, scratching his

[033-173]head, followed close at aer heels.

[033-174]The moment they stepped across the bubbling spring, the atmosphere

[033-175]altered. Without becoming stale or unpleasant, it grew cold, clear and

[033-176]refined, and somehow suggested austere and tomblike thoughts. The

[033-177]daylight disappeared at the first bend in the tunnel. After that,

[033-178]Maskull could not say where the light came from. The air itself must

[033-179]have been luminous, for though it was as light as full moon on Earth,

[033-180]neither he nor Leehallfae cast a shadow. Another peculiarity of the

[033-181]light was that both the walls of the tunnel and their own bodies

[033-182]appeared colourless. Everything was black and white, like a lunar

[033-183]landscape. This intensified the solemn, funereal feelings created by the

[033-184]atmosphere.

[033-185]After they had proceeded for about ten minutes, the tunnel began to

[033-186]widen out. The roof was high above their heads, and six men could have

[033-187]walked side by side. Leehallfae was visibly weakening. Ae dragged

[033-188]aerself along slowly and painfully, with sunken head.

[033-189]Maskull caught hold of aer. “You can’t go on like that. Better let me

[033-190]take you back.”

[033-191]The phaen smiled, and staggered. “I’m dying.”

[033-192]“Don’t talk like that. It’s only a passing indisposition. Let me take

[033-193]you back to the daylight.”

[033-194]“No, help me forward. I wish to see Faceny.”
