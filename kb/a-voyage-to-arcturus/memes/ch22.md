# Section 22

[022-001]“Yes,” said Tydomin, “I will follow you to the end. It is all the more

[022-002]essential, because I keep on displeasing you with my remarks, and that

[022-003]means I have not yet learned my lesson properly.”

[022-004]“Do not be humble, for humility is only self-judgment, and while we are

[022-005]thinking of self, we must be neglecting some action we could be planning

[022-006]or shaping in our mind.”

[022-007]Tydomin continued to be uneasy and preoccupied.

[022-008]“Why was Maskull not in the picture?” she asked.

[022-009]“You dwell on this foreboding because you imagine it is tragical. There

[022-010]is nothing tragical in death, Tydomin, nor in life. There is only right

[022-011]and wrong. What arises from right or wrong action does not matter. We

[022-012]are not gods, constructing a world, but simple men and women, doing our

[022-013]immediate duty. We may die in Sant—so you have seen it; but the truth

[022-014]will go on living.”

[022-015]“Spadevil, why do you choose Sant to start your work in?” asked Maskull.

[022-016]“These men with fixed ideas seem to me the least likely of any to follow

[022-017]a new light.”

[022-018]“Where a bad tree thrives, a good tree will flourish. But where no tree

[022-019]at all can be found, nothing will grow.”

[022-020]“I understand you,” said Maskull. “Here perhaps we are going to

[022-021]martyrdom, but elsewhere we should resemble men preaching to cattle.”

[022-022]Shortly before sunset they arrived at the extremity of the upland plain,

[022-023]above which towered the black cliffs of the Sant Levels. A dizzy,

[022-024]artificially constructed staircase, of more than a thousand steps of

[022-025]varying depth, twisting and forking in order to conform to the angles of

[022-026]the precipices, led to the world overhead. In the place where they stood

[022-027]they were sheltered from the cutting winds. Branchspell, radiantly

[022-028]shining at last, but on the point of sinking, filled the cloudy sky with

[022-029]violent, lurid colors, some of the combinations of which were new to

[022-030]Maskull. The circle of the horizon was so gigantic, that had he been

[022-031]suddenly carried back to Earth, he would by comparison have fancied

[022-032]himself to be moving beneath the dome of some little, closed-in

[022-033]cathedral. He realised that he was on a foreign planet. But he was not

[022-034]stirred or uplifted by the knowledge; he was conscious only of moral

[022-035]ideas. Looking backward, he saw the plain, which for several miles past

[022-036]had been without vegetation, stretching back away to Disscourn. So

[022-037]regular had been the ascent, and so great was the distance, that the

[022-038]huge pyramid looked nothing more than a slight swelling on the face of

[022-039]the earth.

[022-040]Spadevil stopped, and gazed over the landscape in silence. In the

[022-041]evening sunlight his form looked more dense, dark, and real than ever

[022-042]before. His features were set hard in grimness.

[022-043]He turned around to his companions. “What is the greatest wonder, in all

[022-044]this wonderful scene?” he demanded.

[022-045]“Acquaint us,” said Maskull.

[022-046]“All that you see is born from pleasure, and moves on, from pleasure to

[022-047]pleasure. Nowhere is right to be found. It is Shaping’s world.”

[022-048]“There is another wonder,” said Tydomin, and she pointed her finger

[022-049]toward the sky overhead.

[022-050]A small cloud, so low down that it was perhaps not more than five

[022-051]hundred feet above them, was sailing along in front of the dark wall of

[022-052]cliff. It was in the exact shape of an open human hand, with downward-

[022-053]pointing fingers. It was stained crimson by the sun; and one or two tiny

[022-054]cloudlets beneath the fingers looked like falling drops of blood.

[022-055]“Who can doubt now that our death is close at hand?” said Tydomin. “I

[022-056]have been close to death twice today. The first time I was ready, but

[022-057]now I am more ready, for I shall die side by side with the man who has

[022-058]given me my first happiness.”

[022-059]“Do not think of death, but of right persistence,” replied Spadevil. “I

[022-060]am not here to tremble before Shaping’s portents; but to snatch men from

[022-061]him.”

[022-062]He at once proceeded to lead the way up the staircase. Tydomin gazed

[022-063]upward after him for a moment, with an odd, worshiping light in her

[022-064]eyes. Then she followed him, the second of the party. Maskull climbed

[022-065]last. He was travel stained, unkempt, and very tired; but his soul was

[022-066]at peace. As they steadily ascended the almost perpendicular stairs, the

[022-067]sun got higher in the sky. Its light dyed their bodies a ruddy gold.

[022-068]They gained the top. There they found rolling in front of them, as far

[022-069]as the eye could see, a barren desert of white sand, broken here and

[022-070]there by large, jagged masses of black rock. Tracts of the sand were

[022-071]reddened by the sinking sun. The vast expanse of sky was filled by evil-

[022-072]shaped clouds and wild colors. The freezing wind, flurrying across the

[022-073]desert, drove the fine particles of sand painfully against their faces.

[022-074]“Where now do you take us?” asked Maskull.

[022-075]“He who guards the old wisdom of Sant must give up that wisdom to me,

[022-076]that I may change it. What he says, others will say. I go to find

[022-077]Maulger.”

[022-078]“And where will you seek him, in this bare country?”

[022-079]Spadevil struck off toward the north unhesitatingly.

[022-080]“It is not so far,” he said. “It is his custom to be in that part where

[022-081]Sant overhangs the Wombflash Forest. Perhaps he will be there, but I

[022-082]cannot say.”

[022-083]Maskull glanced toward Tydomin. Her sunken cheeks, and the dark circles

[022-084]beneath her eyes told of her extreme weariness.

[022-085]“The woman is tired, Spadevil,” he said.

[022-086]She smiled. “It’s but another step into the land of death. I can manage

[022-087]it. Give me your arm, Maskull.”

[022-088]He put his arm around her waist, and supported her along that way.

[022-089]“The sun is now sinking,” said Maskull. “Will we get there before dark?”

[022-090]“Fear nothing, Maskull and Tydomin; this pain is eating up the evil in

[022-091]your nature. The road you are walking cannot remain unwalked. We shall

[022-092]arrive before dark.”

[022-093]The sun then disappeared behind the far-distant ridges that formed the

[022-094]western boundary of the Ifdawn Marest. The sky blazed up into more vivid

[022-095]colors. The wind grew colder.

[022-096]They passed some pools of colourless gnawl water, round the banks of

[022-097]which were planted fruit trees. Maskull ate some of the fruit. It was

[022-098]hard, bitter, and astringent; he could not get rid of the taste, but he

[022-099]felt braced and invigorated by the downward-flowing juices. No other

[022-100]trees or shrubs were to be seen anywhere. No animals appeared, no birds

[022-101]or insects. It was a desolate land.

[022-102]A mile or two passed, when they again approached the edge of the

[022-103]plateau. Far down, beneath their feet, the great Wombflash Forest began.

[022-104]But daylight had vanished there; Maskull’s eyes rested only on a vague

[022-105]darkness. He faintly heard what sounded like the distant sighing of

[022-106]innumerable treetops.

[022-107]In the rapidly darkening twilight, they came abruptly on a man. He was

[022-108]standing in a pool, on one leg. A pile of boulders had hidden him from

[022-109]their view. The water came as far up as his calf. A trifork, similar to

[022-110]the one Maskull had seen on Disscourn, but smaller, had been stuck in

[022-111]the mud close by his hand.

[022-112]They stopped by the side of the pond, and waited. Immediately he became

[022-113]aware of their presence, the man set down his other leg, and waded out

[022-114]of the water toward them, picking up his trifork in doing so.

[022-115]“This is not Maulger, but Catice,” said Spadevil.

[022-116]“Maulger is dead,” said Catice, speaking the same tongue as Spadevil,

[022-117]but with an even harsher accent, so that the tympanum of Maskull’s ear

[022-118]was affected painfully.

[022-119]The latter saw before him a bowed, powerful individual, advanced in

[022-120]years. He wore nothing but a scanty loincloth. His trunk was long and

[022-121]heavy, but his legs were rather short. His face was beardless, lemon-

[022-122]coloured, and anxious-looking. It was disfigured by a number of

[022-123]longitudinal ruts, a quarter of an inch deep, the cavities of which

[022-124]seemed clogged with ancient dirt. The hair of his head was black and

[022-125]sparse. Instead of the twin membranous organs of Spadevil, he possessed

[022-126]but one; and this was in the centre of his brow.

[022-127]Spadevil’s dark, solid person stood out from the rest like a reality

[022-128]among dreams.

[022-129]“Has the trifork passed to you?” he demanded.

[022-130]“Yes. Why have you brought this woman to Sant?”

[022-131]“I have brought another thing to Sant. I have brought the new faith.”

[022-132]Catice stood motionless, and looked troubled. “State it.”

[022-133]“Shall I speak with many words, or few words?”

[022-134]“If you wish to say what is not, many words will not suffice. If you

[022-135]wish to say what is, a few words will be enough.”

[022-136]Spadevil frowned.

[022-137]“To hate pleasure brings pride with it. Pride is a pleasure. To kill

[022-138]pleasure, we must attach ourselves to duty. While the mind is planning

[022-139]right action, it has no time to think of pleasure.”

[022-140]“Is that the whole?” asked Catice.

[022-141]“The truth is simple, even for the simplest man.”

[022-142]“Do you destroy Hator, and all his generations, with a single word?”

[022-143]“I destroy nature, and set up law.”

[022-144]A long silence followed.

[022-145]“My probe is double,” said Spadevil. “Suffer me to double yours, and you

[022-146]will see as I see.”

[022-147]“Come you here, you big man!” said Catice to Maskull. Maskull advanced a

[022-148]step closer.

[022-149]“Do you follow Spadevil in his new faith?”

[022-150]“As far as death,” exclaimed Maskull.

[022-151]Catice picked up a flint. “With this stone I strike out one of your two

[022-152]probes. When you have but one, you will see with me, and you will

[022-153]recollect with Spadevil. Choose you then the superior faith, and I shall

[022-154]obey your choice.”

[022-155]“Endure this little pain, Maskull, for the sake of future men,” said

[022-156]Spadevil.

[022-157]“The pain is nothing,” replied Maskull, “but I fear the result.”

[022-158]“Permit me, although I am only a woman, to take his place, Catice,” said

[022-159]Tydomin, stretching out her hand.

[022-160]He struck at it violently with the flint, and gashed it from wrist to

[022-161]thumb; the pale carmine blood spouted up. “What brings this kiss-lover

[022-162]to Sant?” he said. “How does she presume to make the rules of life for

[022-163]the sons of Hator?”

[022-164]She bit her lip, and stepped back. “Well then, Maskull, accept! I

[022-165]certainly should not have played false to Spadevil; but you hardly can.”

[022-166]“If he bids me, I must do it,” said Maskull. “But who knows what will

[022-167]come of it?”

[022-168]Spadevil spoke. “Of all the descendants of Hator, Catice is the most

[022-169]wholehearted and sincere. He will trample my truth underfoot, thinking

[022-170]me a demon sent by Shaping, to destroy the work of this land. But a seed

[022-171]will escape, and my blood and yours, Tydomin, will wash it. Then men

[022-172]will know that my destroying evil is their greatest good. But none here

[022-173]will live to see that.”

[022-174]Maskull now went quite close to Catice, and offered his head. Catice

[022-175]raised his hand, and after holding the flint poised for a moment,

[022-176]brought it down with adroitness and force upon the left-hand probe.

[022-177]Maskull cried out with the pain. The blood streamed down, and the

[022-178]function of the organ was destroyed.

[022-179]There was a pause, while he walked to and fro, trying to staunch the

[022-180]blood.

[022-181]“What now do you feel, Maskull? What do you see?” inquired Tydomin

[022-182]anxiously.

[022-183]He stopped, and stared hard at her. “I now see straight,” he said

[022-184]slowly.

[022-185]“What does that mean?”

[022-186]He continued to wipe the blood from his forehead. He looked troubled.

[022-187]“Henceforward, as long as I live, I shall fight with my nature, and

[022-188]refuse to feel pleasure. And I advise you to do the same.”

[022-189]Spadevil gazed at him sternly. “Do you renounce my teaching?”

[022-190]Maskull, however, returned the gaze without dismay. Spadevil’s image-

[022-191]like clearness of form had departed for him; his frowning face he knew

[022-192]to be the deceptive portico of a weak and confused intellect.
