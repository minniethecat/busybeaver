# Mr. Bender

Mr. Bender was [[mary-bender|Mary Bender]]'s father and the original owner of the Bender stamp collection, described as "one of the greatest collections of postage stamps in the world" (011-039). He died before the events of the novel, but his stamp collection drives much of the mystery's backstory.

## Role in the Story

Though deceased, Mr. Bender is a pivotal figure in the novel's backstory. His refusal to sell the stamp collection to [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]] set in motion the chain of events that ultimately leads to the stamps' disappearance and the mystery the Hardy boys must solve (011-041). [[amos-grice|Amos Grice]] recounts his history to [[frank-hardy|Frank]] and [[joe-hardy|Joe]] during their visit to his store (011-034) (011-041) (011-043).

## Character Traits

- **Collector**: Mr. Bender was a serious stamp collector who assembled one of the world's finest private postage stamp collections (011-039).
- **Determined**: He refused to sell his prized collection to [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]] despite [[elroy-jefferson|Jefferson]]'s persistent offers, evidently valuing the collection above money (011-041).
- **Father**: He was [[mary-bender|Mary Bender]]'s father; after his death, his daughter inherited the stamp collection (011-041).

## Key Events (Backstory)

- **Building the collection**: Mr. Bender assembled what became known as the Bender stamp collection, recognized internationally for its quality and rarity (011-039).
- **Refusing [[elroy-jefferson|Jefferson]]**: [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]], a wealthy collector of antiques and stamps, repeatedly tried to purchase the collection from Mr. Bender, but was refused (011-041) (011-043).
- **Death and inheritance**: After Mr. Bender died, his daughter Mary inherited the stamps. She later married [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]] — a union that [[amos-grice|Amos Grice]] suspects was motivated by [[elroy-jefferson|Jefferson]]'s desire for the collection (011-034) (011-041).

## Relationships

- [[mary-bender]]: Mr. Bender's daughter, who inherited his stamp collection upon his death (011-041).
- [[elroy-jefferson]]: [[elroy-jefferson|Jefferson]] persistently tried to buy the stamp collection from Mr. Bender. After Mr. Bender's death, [[elroy-jefferson|Jefferson]] married [[mary-bender|Mary Bender]] (011-043).
- [[amos-grice]]: Grice knew of Mr. Bender and his stamp collection, and recounts the history to the Hardy boys (011-034).

## Significance

Mr. Bender's stamp collection is the central MacGuffin of the novel. His refusal to sell the stamps to [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]], followed by his death and the stamps' subsequent disappearance via [[john-sparewell|John Sparewell]], creates the mystery that the Hardy boys must unravel. Without Mr. Bender's history as a collector and his fateful refusal, there would be no mystery on [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]].
