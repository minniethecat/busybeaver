# The Chimney Collapses

During a violent hurricane on [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]], a falling tree strikes the cabin's great stone chimney, triggering a collapse that turns into an unexpected interrogation — as [[frank-hardy|Frank Hardy]] seizes the chaos to confront [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] about [[john-sparewell|John Sparewell]] and the stolen stamp collection.

## The Storm

The storm that strikes [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] is the most violent yet. "The cabin was creaking and swaying in the violent wind" (023-009), and the snow is "so heavy that the boys could not see more than a few feet beyond the window" (023-015). [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]], injured with a bad ankle, is resting in the outer room where the great stone chimney rises.

## The Collapse

Without warning, a tree blown over by the hurricane strikes the chimney. "The chimney is caving in!" [[frank-hardy|Frank]] shouts (023-002). There follows "another crash. Stones and rocks went bumping and rolling down the roof, and more debris came tumbling into the fireplace" (023-006). The chimney "was no longer breaking up and the steady thump and clatter of rocks on the roof gradually subsided" (023-015), but the threat remains: "If that other tree blows over and hits it, the chimney will be wrecked" (023-012).

## Hanleigh's Reaction

The boys notice something strange about [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]]'s response to the disaster: "Instead of being frightened, the man actually appeared glad of the mishap" (023-011). When [[frank-hardy|Frank]] expresses concern about additional damage, "there was a look of undisguised elation in his swarthy face" (023-013). [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]]'s strange satisfaction with the destruction hints at a secret connected to the chimney itself.

## The Interrogation

With [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] disoriented by the collapse and the storm, [[frank-hardy|Frank]] seizes the moment. "Quite calmly, he said: 'When did [[john-sparewell|John Sparewell]] die?'" (023-019). "[[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] was taken completely off his guard by the sudden question" (023-020). He begins to answer before catching himself, revealing that [[john-sparewell|Sparewell]] died "about eighteen months ago" (023-021).

The interrogation escalates rapidly. [[frank-hardy|Frank]] confronts [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] with what they know: "We know he was your uncle. And we know he disappeared from [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]]'s home with the rosewood box fifteen years ago" (023-022). [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] realizes they found the notebook (023-023) and defiantly claims the cipher is unsolvable: "I defy you to solve it!" (023-028). [[joe-hardy|Joe]]'s revelation that "we solved the cipher" (023-033) breaks [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]]'s confidence — "consternation was written on [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]]'s face. He groaned" (023-034). His panicked question — "you haven't found it?" (023-035) — confirms that something valuable is hidden, though [[frank-hardy|Frank]] truthfully answers "we have found nothing, so far" (023-040).

## Significance

The chimney collapse serves as both a physical crisis and the turning point in the [[detective-work|investigation]]. Under [[cabin-cove|the cove]]r of chaos, [[frank-hardy|Frank]] extracts critical information from [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]]: the connection to [[john-sparewell|John Sparewell]], the importance of the cipher, and confirmation that something of value remains hidden on the island.
