# The Hardy Iceboat

The iceboat built by [[frank-hardy|Frank]] and [[joe-hardy|Joe Hardy]] is both a practical vehicle and a symbol of their resourcefulness. Constructed over several weeks, it carries the boys through every stage of the [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] adventure.

## Description

The iceboat was "homemade" — the product of "several weeks' hard work on the part of himself and his brother [[joe-hardy|Joe]]" (001-003). Despite its amateur construction, the craft was "staunch and stoutly built" (001-003), a testament to the boys' mechanical skill. [[frank-hardy|Frank]] proudly declared "I don't think there's a faster boat on the bay" (001-006). The boat was small enough to hold four people — [[frank-hardy|Frank]] noted "the iceboat won't hold any more, anyway" (006-025) — but large enough to carry camping supplies and equipment.

## In the Novel

The iceboat appears throughout the story as the boys' primary means of transportation. It carries them to [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] for their initial reconnaissance, transports their camping gear for the expedition, and serves as their escape vehicle when danger threatens. The boat's maneuverability, demonstrated when it "swung around the point" (001-038), proves crucial in the collision incident. Before the expedition, the boys spent time "putting the iceboat in readiness and accumulating supplies" (006-034), showing the care they invested in their craft.
