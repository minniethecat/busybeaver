Hanleigh's notebook is a crucial piece of [[evidence]] in the [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] mystery. Left behind by [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]], it contains a [[the-cipher|cipher message]] that, once decoded, leads the Hardy boys directly to the hiding place of the [[rosewood-box|rosewood box]] in [[the-chimney|the chimney]].

## Discovery

The notebook is discovered as part of the boys' search of the cabin on [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]]. Among [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]]'s belongings left in the cabin, they find papers that include the notebook with its cryptic message. [[frank-hardy|Frank]], with his characteristic attention to detail, immediately recognizes the importance of the written material [012-034] [012-045].

## Contents

The notebook contains a message written in cipher. The key line, once decoded, reads:

> "Cabin Island Chimney Left Front Nine Feet High" [018-031]

This message provides precise instructions for locating the hidden [[rosewood-box|rosewood box]]: examine the chimney on the left-hand side, approximately nine feet above the ground, at the front of the chimney structure [018-034].

## Role in the Investigation

### Decoding the Cipher

[[frank-hardy|Frank]] takes the lead in decoding the notebook's message. The cipher is a substitution code, and through careful analysis, Frank is able to decipher its meaning. His success demonstrates the detective skills inherited from his father, [[fenton-hardy|Fenton Hardy]] [013-008] [013-022].

### Interpreting the Message

Once decoded, the message's meaning is initially puzzling. [[frank-hardy|Frank]] reads it aloud and immediately interprets its practical significance:

"What good does it do? Don't you understand? This message refers to the chimney right in this very cabin. All we have to do now is examine a part of the chimney on the left-hand side, about nine feet up, somewhere at the front." [018-034]

### Failed Initial Search

Despite the clear instructions in the notebook, the boys' initial search of [[the-chimney|the chimney]] proves unsuccessful. Both [[frank-hardy|Frank]] and [[joe-hardy|Joe]] climb into the chimney and examine it thoroughly with flashlights, but find nothing. The notebook's message is correct — but the hiding place was sealed with mortar and could not be detected through normal inspection [018-075] [019-006].

### Vindication

The notebook's instructions are ultimately vindicated when the chimney partially collapses during a hurricane. The destruction breaks the mortar seal, revealing the hollow hiding place exactly where the cipher had indicated. [[frank-hardy|Frank]] finds the [[rosewood-box|rosewood box]] "right at the place mentioned in the cipher" [024-028].

## Ownership

The notebook belongs to [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]], the mysterious figure who had been staying on [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]]. [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] had been attempting to locate the hidden [[rosewood-box|rosewood box]] himself, using the same cipher directions. His earlier behavior — measuring the chimney with a stick — shows he had already decoded the notebook's message and was trying to find the exact location [008-017] [008-020].

## Significance

Hanleigh's notebook represents the intellectual challenge at the heart of the [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] mystery. Unlike physical clues such as the stolen supplies or the iceboat collision, the notebook requires mental effort to unlock — it rewards the Hardy boys' [[detective-work|detective skills]] rather than their physical courage. The notebook also serves as tangible evidence of [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]]'s involvement, confirming that he was actively searching for the hidden stamps and was not merely a passive occupant of the island.
