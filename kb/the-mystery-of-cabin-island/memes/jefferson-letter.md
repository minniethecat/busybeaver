# Jefferson's Permission Letter

A formal note from [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]] to the Hardy boys granting permission to camp on [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]], whose strangely worded tone becomes an early clue that something is amiss.

## Summary

After the boys seek permission to camp on [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]], they receive a letter from [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]]. The note "which puzzled the Hardy boys was as follows" (004-001) — suggesting that its wording or tone was unexpected. [[fenton-hardy|Fenton Hardy]] immediately notices something off: "That doesn't sound like [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]]" (004-017), expressing doubt that the letter genuinely reflects [[elroy-jefferson|Jefferson]]'s character. The chauffeur who delivered it confirms it came from [[elroy-jefferson|Jefferson]] (003-044), but the discrepancy between the letter's tone and [[elroy-jefferson|Jefferson]]'s known personality plants a seed of suspicion.

## Key Moments
- The puzzling letter arrives (004-001)
