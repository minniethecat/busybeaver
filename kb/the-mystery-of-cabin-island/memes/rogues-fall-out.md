# Rogues Fall Out

In a dramatic turn late in the mystery, [[Hanleigh]]'s hired henchmen [[Tad Carson]] and [[Ike Nash]] turned against their employer, demanding more money and threatening to expose him. When [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] refused, the rogues' alliance collapsed — and [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] retaliated by [[theft|stealing]] Carson and Nash's iceboat, marooning them on [[Cabin Island]] while he made his escape.

## Summary

[[Tad Carson]] and [[Ike Nash]], the two unsavory young men whom [[Hanleigh]] had employed to guard [[Cabin Island]] and intimidate intruders, confronted their employer in the cabin on the island (020-001). Dissatisfied with their pay and suspicious that [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] was concealing the true value of what he sought, they demanded more money — a demand [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] angrily rejected as "blackmail" and a "holdup" (020-034). When [[Tad Carson|Carson]] and [[Ike Nash|Nash]] pressed their demands further, [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] appeared to capitulate, telling them to return the following day for payment (020-076). After they left, however, [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] located their iceboat — the very craft they relied on for transport across [[Barmet Bay]] — and stole it, stranding his former henchmen on the island (020-079).

## Participants

- [[Hanleigh]]: The antagonist who turned the tables on his would-be blackmailers, [[theft|stealing]] their iceboat to escape (020-079)
- [[Tad Carson]]: One of [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]]'s hired henchmen; led the demand for more money, accusing [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] of running "some crooked game" (020-049)
- [[Ike Nash]]: Carson's partner in [[theft|crime]]; accompanied Carson in the confrontation and the subsequent retreat (020-076)

## Location

The confrontation took place inside the cabin on [[Cabin Island]], where [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] had been conducting his mysterious search (020-001). After the confrontation, [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] walked to the shore where Carson and Nash's iceboat was moored on [[Barmet Bay]] (020-079).

## Narrative Significance

This event marks the collapse of [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]]'s support network and his transition from a position of relative strength to desperate isolation. The falling-out between the rogues demonstrated that [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]]'s scheme was unraveling from within — his own hired men no longer trusted him and were willing to turn on him. By [[theft|stealing]] their iceboat, [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] bought himself temporary freedom but made two more enemies, setting the stage for Carson and Nash's later attempt at revenge (025-001). The episode also confirmed that whatever [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] was searching for on [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] was valuable enough to justify theft and betrayal.

## Lead-up

[[Tad Carson]] and [[Ike Nash]] had been employed by [[Hanleigh]] since early in the story to patrol [[Cabin Island]] and keep intruders away (002-035, 007-054). They had already clashed with the Hardy boys on [[Barmet Bay]] in an iceboat collision (003-005–027) and had been seen meeting with [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] at the cabin (008-057). Carson and Nash had grown increasingly dissatisfied with their arrangement, feeling they were being underpaid for the risks they were taking.

> "We don't risk our necks running that iceboat out here just for our health," said Carson. "Every time we go there we have to dodge the harbor police" (020-029).

## Aftermath

Having stolen the iceboat, [[Hanleigh]] returned to [[Bayport]], leaving [[Tad Carson|Carson]] and [[Ike Nash|Nash]] stranded on [[Cabin Island]] overnight. The two henchmen were forced to wait until they could find alternative transport. Their anger at [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]]'s betrayal later contributed to the final resolution of the mystery, as Carson and Nash sought revenge against their former employer (025-001). The iceboat theft also meant that [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] was now operating alone, without accomplices to run interference — a vulnerability the Hardy boys would soon exploit.
