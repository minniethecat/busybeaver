# Christmas Gift

The **Christmas Gift** was an [[hardy-iceboat|iceboat]] presented to [[Frank Hardy]] and [[Joe Hardy]] as a [[christmas-holidays|Christmas]] present from Mr. Hooper, the father of their close friend [[Biff Hooper]] (007-001). This timely gift provided the boys with their primary means of transportation across the frozen waters of [[Barmet Bay]], making their winter expedition to [[Cabin Island]] possible.

## Summary

On [[christmas-holidays|Christmas]] morning, among the presents exchanged at the Hardy household, one gift stood out: an [[hardy-iceboat|iceboat]] from Mr. Hooper, given through [[Biff Hooper]] to [[Frank Hardy|Frank]] and [[Joe Hardy]] (007-001). The iceboat was a sturdy, homemade craft — "staunch and stoutly built" — capable of carrying several passengers across the frozen bay (001-003). This gift arrived at a critical moment: the boys had been planning a [[christmas-holidays|Christmas]]-holiday camping trip, and [[fenton-hardy|Mr. Hardy]] had given his conditional approval for an outing to [[Cabin Island]] (005-039). The iceboat transformed a vague plan into a practical expedition, giving the boys independent transport across [[Barmet Bay]] in the depths of winter.

## Participants

- [[Frank Hardy]]: Co-recipient of the iceboat; served as primary helmsman during the expedition
- [[Joe Hardy]]: Co-recipient; shared piloting duties and navigated the iceboat across [[Barmet Bay]]
- [[Biff Hooper]]: Delivered the gift on behalf of his father, who had built the iceboat (007-001)
- [[Chet Morton]]: Accompanied the boys on the iceboat during the expedition to [[Cabin Island]]

## Location

The [[christmas-holidays|Christmas]] gift was presented at the Hardy Home in [[Bayport]] on [[christmas-holidays|Christmas]] morning, following the family's [[christmas-holidays|Christmas]] dinner and the ceremony of opening presents (006-044–047). The iceboat itself would soon be launched from the shores of [[Barmet Bay]], carrying the boys toward [[Cabin Island]].

## Narrative Significance

The [[christmas-holidays|Christmas]] gift of the iceboat serves as the narrative key that unlocks the entire adventure. Without independent winter transport, the boys could not have reached [[Cabin Island]] — the frozen bay made conventional boating impossible, and overland routes were impractical. The iceboat is introduced in the novel's very first paragraph ("a trim little iceboat went scudding over the frozen surface of [[barmet-bay|Barmet Bay]]") and remains central to the plot throughout (001-001). The gift also establishes the Hooper family's generosity and the close bonds between the boys, reinforcing the series' themes of friendship and mutual support.

## Lead-up

In the days before [[christmas-holidays|Christmas]], [[Frank Hardy|Frank]] and [[Joe Hardy|Joe]] had been eager to plan an outdoor adventure during their [[christmas-holidays|Christmas]] holidays (004-047–050). [[Fenton Hardy|Mr. Hardy]] initially expressed reluctance — he wanted his sons home for [[christmas-holidays|Christmas]] — but was ultimately persuaded by their enthusiasm and gave permission for the trip (005-039). The boys knew they would need some form of transport to reach [[Cabin Island]] across the frozen bay, but had not yet solved this problem. The iceboat gift arrived at precisely the right moment.

> Among Biff's gifts was an iceboat from his father, and this the boys promptly claimed as their own, with Biff's hearty approval (007-001).

## Aftermath

With the iceboat in their possession, the boys finalized their plans for the [[Cabin Island Expedition]]. They provisioned the craft and sailed from [[Bayport]] across [[Barmet Bay]] shortly after [[christmas-holidays|Christmas]], navigating through the winter winds toward the island (001-036–047). The iceboat would go on to play a role in multiple adventures throughout the story: it was used to chase suspicious vessels (001-020), explore the shoreline of [[Cabin Island]] (001-036), and transport supplies between the island and the mainland. The craft also became a target — [[Tad Carson]] and [[Ike Nash]] would later attempt to interfere with the boys' iceboat activities, leading to the dramatic [[Iceboat Collision on Barmet Bay]] (003-005–027).
