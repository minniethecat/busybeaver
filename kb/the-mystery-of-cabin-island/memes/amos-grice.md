Amos Grice is the proprietor of the general store near [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] who provides the Hardy boys with vital background on [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]], the stolen stamp collection, and the connection to [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]]. His local knowledge makes him a key informant in the [[detective-work|investigation]].

## Role in the Story

Amos Grice serves as a local informant — a village elder who knows the history of every resident and every rumor in the area around [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]]. When the boys visit his store, Grice provides the essential link between the stamp theft and [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]], transforming what was a food-theft [[detective-work|investigation]] into a deeper mystery involving a valuable collection [011-012] [015-034].

## Character Traits

- **Appearance**: No detailed physical description is given in the text beyond his role as an older storekeeper, but his mannerisms — particularly his habit of "wagging his head" — are repeatedly noted [011-034] [011-060].
- **Personality**: Warm and sympathetic ("clucking sympathetically" over the boys' stolen food), garrulous, and possessed of a theatrical storytelling style: Grice delivers information "with great effect" [011-045], enjoying the drama of his revelations. He is generous, offering the boys supplies and even firearms when they explain their plan [015-050] [015-054].
- **Knowledge**: Grice possesses detailed local knowledge about [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]]'s history, the stamp collection, and the mysterious goings-on at [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]]. His store functions as the village information hub [011-012] [015-039].

## Key Appearances

- **First Encounter at the Store**: The boys visit Grice's general store for camping supplies. Grice launches into the story of [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]]'s stamp collection and its mysterious disappearance, providing the crucial lead [011-012] [011-045] [011-053].
- **The Stamp Revelation**: "Then," said Amos Grice, with great effect, "the stamps disappeared too." His account of the stamp theft plants the seed of the larger mystery [011-045].
- **Post-Food-[[theft|Theft]] Context**: After the boys' food is stolen at the cabin, the conversation with Grice takes on new urgency. [[joe-hardy|Joe]] recalls Grice's words and connects them to their current situation [012-068] [012-072] [015-003].
- **Second Visit — New Intelligence**: Returning to Grice's store, the boys learn about recent suspicious activity: "There's been a couple of things happened around here lately" [015-039]. Grice also mentions mysterious tracks [015-042].
- **Equipping the [[adventure|Expedition]]**: Grice lends the boys rifles and ammunition for their expedition back to [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]], actively supporting their [[detective-work|investigation]] [015-050] [015-054] [015-055].

## Relationships

- [[frank-hardy|Frank Hardy]]: Grice is responsive to [[frank-hardy|Frank]]'s questioning and provides detailed local intelligence

## Significance

Amos Grice functions as the narrative's key node of local knowledge. Without his information about [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]] and the stamp collection, the boys would have no basis to connect the food theft at [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] to the larger stamp mystery. He is the storyteller who frames the historical context: his account of how the stamp collection came to be hidden at [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] and how it relates to [[elroy-jefferson|Jefferson]]'s personal history provides the backstory that drives the second half of the [[detective-work|investigation]] [011-045] [011-053] [015-039].

## In the Hardy Boys Series

Amos Grice does not appear in other Hardy Boys mysteries. He is a single-book supporting character — a local source type that appears throughout the series in various guises (village informants, small-town storekeepers with crucial gossip).
