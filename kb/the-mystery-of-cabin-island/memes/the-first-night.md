# The First Night on Cabin Island

The first night on [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] marks the transition from anticipation to reality. After the journey across [[barmet-bay|Barmet Bay]], after the [[permission-granted|meeting with Jefferson]], after the [[the-christmas-surprise|early Christmas celebration]], the boys finally arrive at the island they have been discussing for days and begin their adventure in earnest.

## Arrival and Setup

The boys spend the greater part of the day installing themselves in the cabin and getting "everything shipshape" (009-001). The cabin, unused for years, requires significant effort to make habitable: sweeping out accumulated debris, checking the chimney's draw, arranging sleeping quarters, and organizing supplies.

The great stone fireplace — the same chimney that will later collapse to reveal the [[rosewood-box|rosewood box]] — dominates the single long room. The boys build a fire, and the flickering light transforms the abandoned structure into something approaching a home. The cabin, "dark and austere" (001-039) from the outside, becomes warm and intimate within.

## The Rising Storm

As darkness falls, the weather turns. The rising wind becomes a storm:

> As darkness fell, the snow began beating against the cabin windows and the gale howled down the great chimney. (009-007)

The boys' first night on [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] is not peaceful but dramatic — the snow, the wind, and the isolation combining to create an atmosphere of adventure tinged with danger. The cabin, rather than being a refuge, becomes an outpost: four boys, alone, facing the winter night with only each other and the fire for comfort.

## The Night's Mood

[[chet-morton|Chet Morton]] provides the comic relief that balances the tension. His attempts to hide his socks in the woodpile are discovered by [[biff-hooper|Biff Hooper]], leading to "much scuffling about" (009-020) before the boys finally retire. This lightness — the ordinary foolery of teenage boys — grounds the adventure in recognizable humanity. They are not hardened explorers but friends on a camping trip, however unusual the circumstances.

## The Threshold

The first night serves as a narrative threshold. Before it, the adventure is anticipation and preparation. After it, the boys are committed — residents of [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]], with all the mystery and danger that entails. The night's storm is both literal and metaphorical: the forces that will test the boys have been unleashed, and there is no going back until the mystery is solved.
