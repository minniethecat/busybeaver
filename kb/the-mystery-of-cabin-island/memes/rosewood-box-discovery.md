# The Discovery of the Rosewood Box

The climactic moment of the [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] mystery: after the chimney's partial collapse during a hurricane, [[frank-hardy|Frank Hardy]] climbs into the damaged fireplace and discovers the long-hidden rosewood box — containing the stamp collection that had been missing for fifteen years — concealed in a hollow stone mortared into the chimney.

## The Search

The morning after the chimney's partial collapse, the boys clear the rubble from the fireplace. "The tall chimney having collapsed, he could see the white snow swirling just a few yards above" (024-010). [[frank-hardy|Frank]] switches on his flashlight and examines the interior of the damaged chimney.

> He switched on his flashlight and examined the interior. Then he gave an exclamation of satisfaction. "It's all cracked and broken," he reported. "I'm going up." (024-011–024-012)

Despite [[biff-hooper|Biff]]'s nervous warning to "be careful" (024-013), [[frank-hardy|Frank]] scrambles up into the fireplace. The others wait anxiously as his struggling dislodges more stones (024-014). There is silence, then a muffled shout.

## The Discovery

> "I have it!" yelled Frank, in excitement. "It's here!" (024-017)

After more struggling and a shower of stones and mortar (024-018), [[frank-hardy|Frank]] emerges from the fireplace "black with soot, his clothes were ruined" (024-020) — but bearing a beautiful object. [[joe-hardy|Joe]] immediately identifies it: "The rosewood box!" (024-021).

> Frank held the box up. It was a beautiful object, and although it had been hidden in the chimney for many years, its rosewood surface was almost as lustrous as on the day it was first concealed. (024-024)

Inside the box, "neatly arranged on sheets, were the rare stamps that had been [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]]'s pride — the stamps that were worth a fortune!" (024-026). The mystery of the chimney is solved.

[[frank-hardy|Frank]] explains how the box was hidden: it "had been hidden in a hollow right in the middle of one of the stones, and it had been mortared up when they were building the chimney" (024-028). Only the violent shaking from the hurricane — which brought a tree crashing onto the roof — had broken the mortar and dislodged the concealing stone.

## Hanleigh's Confrontation

The celebration is interrupted by a harsh voice from the kitchen doorway:

> "What's that? You found it? Give it here! That box is mine!" (024-030)

[[hanleigh|Hanleigh]], his face "livid with rage" (024-031), tries to claim the box. [[frank-hardy|Frank]] stands firm: "It belongs to [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]], and we are going to return it to him" (024-032). When [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] threatens legal action, [[frank-hardy|Frank]] counters with the evidence they discovered — the letter from lawyers instructing [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] to return the box (024-039).

Cornered, [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]]'s defiance crumbles. "I guess the game is up," he admits heavily (024-044).

## The Hidden History

Pressed by [[frank-hardy|Frank]], [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] reveals the full story of the box's concealment. His uncle, [[john-sparewell|John Sparewell]], was a trusted servant in [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]]'s household who stole the stamp collection during a moment of financial desperation (024-049).

[[john-sparewell|Sparewell]] discovered he could not sell the stamps — dealers were alerted and police were watching (024-051). He decided to return them but found [[elroy-jefferson|Jefferson]] had gone to Europe. Fearing arrest, he fled to [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]], where the cabin was under construction:

> "The masons had the chimney just about half completed. As it was a Sunday, the island was deserted that day. Fearing that he might be trapped on the island, with the box in his possession, he hid it in a hollow of one of the stones and covered it over with mortar, intending to come back for it later." (024-057)

When [[john-sparewell|Sparewell]] returned the next day, the masons had completed the chimney — sealing the box inside solid masonry. Despite repeated attempts over the years, he never recovered it (024-059).

## Significance

The discovery of the rosewood box is the payoff to the cipher that the Hardy boys solved (see [[the-cipher]]), the confession they extracted during the chimney collapse (see [[chimney-collapse]]), and the notebook they recovered from the cabin (see [[hanleigh-notebook]]). The event transforms the boys from investigators into heroes, recovering a treasure that had eluded professional detectives for fifteen years.
