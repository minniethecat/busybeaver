# Jefferson's Permission Letter

The physical letter from [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]] granting the Hardy boys permission to camp on [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]], a document whose unusual wording makes it an object of suspicion and an early clue in the mystery.

## Description

The letter is a formal note from [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]], delivered by his chauffeur to the Hardy household. It grants permission for the boys' camping trip but is written in a tone that puzzles its recipients. The note "which puzzled the Hardy boys was as follows" (004-001), suggesting its wording was unexpectedly cold or impersonal. It was delivered by "[[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]]'s chauffeur" (003-044), confirming its apparent authenticity despite the odd tone.

## In the Novel

The letter serves dual functions. On the surface, it is a practical document enabling the adventure — without it, the boys cannot legally camp on [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]]. But the discrepancy between the letter's tone and [[elroy-jefferson|Jefferson]]'s personality becomes an early clue. [[fenton-hardy|Fenton Hardy]] immediately notices: "That doesn't sound like [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]]" (004-017). The letter is either a forgery or evidence that something — or someone — was influencing [[elroy-jefferson|Jefferson]] at the time it was written.
