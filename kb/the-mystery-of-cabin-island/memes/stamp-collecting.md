# Stamp Collecting

The hobby of acquiring, organizing, and studying postage stamps, known as philately, which serves as the MacGuffin driving the central mystery of _The Mystery of [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]]_.

## Definition

Stamp collecting is one of the world's most popular hobbies, involving the acquisition and categorization of postage stamps from various countries and historical periods. In the early 20th century — the era in which the Hardy Boys stories are set — stamp collecting was a widespread and respected pastime. Collections of significant value were assembled by wealthy enthusiasts, and rare stamps commanded prices comparable to fine art or antiques.

## In the Novel

The stamp collection at the center of the mystery belongs to the Bender family. It is first mentioned when [[frank-hardy|Frank]] and [[joe-hardy|Joe]] learn of the Bender postage stamp collection (011-034) that had been in the possession of the Bender household. The collection is described as extraordinarily valuable: "You've heard of the Bender stamp collection, haven't you?" (011-037), with the implication that it is famous even among non-collectors.

The stamps disappeared under mysterious circumstances. A man named [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] "used to call at the Bender home frequently" and "took a great interest in [[mary-bender|Mrs. Bender]] entirely on account of the stamps" (011-043). After [[mary-bender|Mrs. Bender]]'s death, "the stamps disappeared" (011-045). The missing collection — worth a fortune — becomes the object of a search that drives much of the novel's suspense. [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]], the owner of [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]], is ultimately connected to the stamp collection's fate, and the resolution involves the stamps being returned to their rightful owner to his great "treasure" (025-018).
