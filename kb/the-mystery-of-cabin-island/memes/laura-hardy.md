Laura Hardy is the mother of [[frank-hardy|Frank Hardy]] and [[joe-hardy|Joe Hardy]], and the wife of [[fenton-hardy|Fenton Hardy]]. Though she appears only briefly in the narrative, she plays an important supporting role in enabling the boys' adventure to [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]].

## Permission for the Expedition

When the boys propose their winter camping trip to [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]], Mrs. Hardy is initially reluctant. She agrees only after consideration, "although at first she was reluctant in view of the fact that the boys would be absent from the family circle over [[christmas-holidays|Christmas]]" (006-010). Her willingness to permit the expedition, despite her reservations, demonstrates her trust in her sons' judgment and capabilities.

## Christmas Surprise

Mrs. Hardy actively participates in the [[christmas-holidays|Christmas]] preparations that surprise the boys. She and her husband "had taken liberties with the calendar," arranging for an early [[christmas-holidays|Christmas]] celebration so the boys could receive their gifts — including the new [[hardy-iceboat|iceboat]] — before departing for [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] (006-038). When the boys come home that night, "they found, to their unbounded delight and astonishment," that [[christmas-holidays|Christmas]] had arrived early.

## Role in the Hardy Household

As the mother in the Hardy household, Mrs. Hardy provides a stable home environment. She sends the boys on errands — [[frank-hardy|Frank]] and [[joe-hardy|Joe]] are "on an errand downtown for their mother" when they encounter [[callie-shaw|Callie Shaw]] and [[iola-morton|Iola Morton]] (004-021). She also prepares food for the expedition, including "the [[christmas-holidays|Christmas]] pudding, which Mrs. Hardy had prepared before the boys left [[bayport|Bayport]]" (014-049), ensuring the boys have a taste of home even on the remote island.
