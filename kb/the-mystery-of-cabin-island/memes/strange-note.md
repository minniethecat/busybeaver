# The Strange Note

The mysterious note from [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]] is the catalyst that launches the Hardy boys into the [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] mystery. Delivered during the Christmas holidays, the note's brevity and ambiguity puzzle even [[fenton-hardy|Fenton Hardy]], the noted detective — and its true significance becomes clear only at the novel's conclusion.

## The Note

The note that puzzled the Hardy boys read as follows:

> Messrs. Frank and Joseph Hardy,
> Bayport.
> Dear Sirs:
> "If it is convenient for you to call upon me at my residence tomorrow afternoon at two o'clock, I wish to discuss a matter of business with you."
> Yours truly,
> Elroy Jefferson (004-001)

The message is remarkable for what it omits: no explanation of the "matter of business," no hint of how [[elroy-jefferson|Jefferson]] knows the boys, and no context for the unusual request that two high school students meet with a wealthy stranger.

## The Hardy Family's Reaction

When the boys bring the note to their father, even the experienced detective is stumped:

> Fenton Hardy read the note. He looked puzzled. Then he handed it back to the boys. (003-052)

> "I can't say, I'm sure," he said. "It's a strange note. Still, I suppose you had better do as he asks, and then you'll know more about it later." (003-053)

The admission that even the famous detective cannot decipher the note's purpose heightens the mystery. [[fenton-hardy|Fenton Hardy]]'s decision to let his sons attend the meeting — despite his own uncertainty — shows his trust in their judgment and his understanding that real detective work begins with the unknown.

## The Boys' Speculation

The next morning, "although the boys had discussed the note from Mr. Jefferson many times, they had still failed to arrive at any satisfactory conclusion regarding its meaning" (004-020). The note has become an intellectual puzzle — a cipher of a different kind than the one they will later decode in the cabin chimney.

The boys eventually connect the note to their earlier encounter with [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] on [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]], and to the auto theft case on [[shore-road|Shore Road]]: "Well, we recovered his automobile for him from the Shore Road thieves" (004-003). The note, they realize, may be [[elroy-jefferson|Jefferson]]'s way of repaying a debt.

## The Revelation

The meeting with [[elroy-jefferson|Jefferson]] transforms the cryptic note into a clear proposition: the boys are offered the use of [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] for their winter camping trip — and a hundred-dollar reward if they can solve the mystery of the island's unwelcome visitor. What began as a puzzling note becomes the key to the entire adventure.

## Significance

The Strange Note exemplifies a recurring theme in the Hardy Boys series: the ordinary and the mysterious are often the same thing, seen from different angles. A brief business letter — polite, formal, and utterly unremarkable in style — conceals within it the seeds of a mystery involving stolen stamps, hidden treasure, a desperate criminal, and a winter island adventure.

The note also establishes the boys' relationship with [[elroy-jefferson|Jefferson]] as one of earned trust: they are not random teenagers but proven young detectives whose earlier success on the [[shore-road|Shore Road]] auto theft case has earned them a wealthy man's confidence.
