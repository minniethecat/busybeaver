The village is a small settlement "that nestled in a cove on the southern shore" of [[barmet-bay|Barmet Bay]], approximately two miles from [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] (010-007). It serves as the nearest civilization to the boys' isolated camp and becomes a crucial resupply and intelligence-gathering point during their expedition.

## Discovery

The boys discover the village during an exploratory iceboat trip on their first full day on [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]]. They venture "as far as a little village that nestled in a cove on the southern shore, about two miles from the island" (010-007). The discovery is significant — it means that even though [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] feels remote and isolated, help and supplies are within reach.

## Role in the Story

### Emergency Resupply

After the theft of their food supplies from [[the-cabin|the cabin]], the village becomes the boys' lifeline. [[frank-hardy|Frank]] proposes the solution: "We can go down to that little village and buy some more food" (010-053). [[joe-hardy|Joe]] supports the plan, turning what could have been a crisis that ended the expedition into a manageable setback.

The following morning, [[frank-hardy|Frank]] and [[joe-hardy|Joe]] take their [[hardy-iceboat|iceboat]] to the village: "Right away. Joe and I will take our iceboat and go down to the village. You and Biff had better stay here" (011-004).

### Intelligence Gathering at Grice's Store

The village is the location of [[grice-store|Grice's Store]], where the boys encounter [[amos-grice|Amos Grice]]. What begins as a simple supply run transforms into a major breakthrough in their [[detective-work|investigation]] when Grice reveals the history of [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]]'s stolen [[bender-stamp-collection|stamp collection]] and its connection to [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] [011-012] [011-045].

### The Fox Hunt Connection

On a subsequent visit to the village, the boys learn from [[amos-grice|Amos Grice]] about foxes that have been raiding local chicken coops. This intelligence leads to [[the-fox-hunt|the fox hunt]] — a successful interlude in which the boys eliminate the nuisance and earn Grice's lasting gratitude [015-034] [016-026].

## Location and Access

The village is situated approximately two miles from [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] across the frozen surface of [[barmet-bay|Barmet Bay]] (010-007). In winter, access is primarily by iceboat across the ice, making the Hardy boys' [[hardy-iceboat|iceboat]] an essential link between their isolated camp and civilization. The village's cove location provides shelter from the winds that sweep across the open bay.

## Key Locations

- **[[grice-store|Grice's Store]]**: The village general store, owned and operated by [[amos-grice|Amos Grice]]. The primary destination for the boys' supply runs and the source of critical information about the stamp mystery
- **The Cove**: The sheltered inlet where the village is situated, providing natural protection for small boats

## Significance

The village serves as the story's link between wilderness and civilization. While [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] represents isolation, mystery, and danger, the village represents community, knowledge, and support. The boys' trips to the village punctuate the narrative, providing both practical resupply and crucial plot information. Without the village — and specifically without [[grice-store|Grice's Store]] — the Hardy boys would have had no way to connect the events on [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] to the larger mystery of the stolen [[bender-stamp-collection|stamp collection]].
