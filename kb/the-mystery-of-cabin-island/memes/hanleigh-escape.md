# Hanleigh's Escape

[[hanleigh|Hanleigh]]'s return to the cabin and his narrow escape from the Hardy boys represents the most direct confrontation between the young investigators and their elusive adversary — a missed opportunity that prolongs the [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] mystery.

## Hanleigh Returns

[[frank-hardy|Frank]] spots [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] moving among the trees near the cabin. Recognizing him as "the man who had stolen their supplies and hidden them, evidently to get the boys to leave the island" (017-001), [[frank-hardy|Frank]] hurries back to warn [[joe-hardy|Joe]]. He reasons that "if he thinks we don't see him, he may give himself away" (017-003).

The brothers devise a plan: "I think he intends to come here. He imagines we're all out hunting for him. Let's hide and find out what he wants" (017-010). They hide in the bushes and watch as [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] enters the cabin.

## The Search

Through the window, [[frank-hardy|Frank]] and [[joe-hardy|Joe]] observe [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]]'s frantic behavior. "He was looking at the fireplace. He stepped toward it, then apparently changed his mind" (017-019). His objective becomes clear: "he was searching for something" (017-020). [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] tears through the cabin — "he went over to the beds and flung blankets, pillows and even mattresses on the floor" (017-020) — hunting for his missing notebook. The boys later confirm "we heard him say that it didn't really matter, because we wouldn't be able to solve the cipher anyway" (018-006).

## The Confrontation

When the moment is right, the boys move to capture [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]]. But their quarry is alert and desperate. A struggle ensues, and rifle shots ring out. [[chet-morton|Chet]] and [[biff-hooper|Biff]], "who were widely separated at the time, heard the rifle shots and returned posthaste to the cabin, but they did not meet the fugitive" (018-002).

## The Escape

Despite their best efforts, "[[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] made good his escape" (018-001). [[frank-hardy|Frank]] makes a tactical decision not to pursue: "By the time we got one of the iceboats out, he would be on the shore, and we'd never find him there. Let him go. We learned something, at any rate" (018-003).

## Aftermath

Though the capture attempt fails, the confrontation yields valuable intelligence. [[frank-hardy|Frank]] remains confident: "I don't think we've seen the last of him. There is something mighty important about that notebook, and I'm sure he is not the man to give up as easily as all that" (018-009). The escape, while frustrating, confirms that [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] is desperate — and that the notebook and cipher contain information worth fighting for.
