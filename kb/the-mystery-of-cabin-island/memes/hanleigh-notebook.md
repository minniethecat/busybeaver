# The Notebook

A small notebook discovered among the stolen supplies on [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] provides the first tangible link between the mystery on the island and the disappearance of [[john-sparewell|John Sparewell]] and his valuable stamp collection.

## Discovery

After recovering their stolen food supplies from beneath a large rock on the shore of [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]], [[joe-hardy|Joe Hardy]] finds an unfamiliar object among the boxes: "I wonder what this is" (012-047). He picks it up — "the object was a small notebook" (012-048). Glancing through its pages, [[joe-hardy|Joe]] finds that most are blank but "there was a certain amount of writing on the opening sheets" (012-048). [[joe-hardy|Joe]] hands the notebook to his brother: "[[joe-hardy|Joe]] handed him the notebook" (012-050). None of the boys claims ownership: "I'm sure none of us had a notebook like this" (012-051).

## The Inscription

[[frank-hardy|Frank]] examines the notebook and finds a crucial clue on the fly leaf — a man's name written "in a bold, flowing script" (012-061): **[[john-sparewell|J. Sparewell]]**. This is not the name of their suspected adversary [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]]. "It wasn't [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]], after all" (012-062), [[chet-morton|Chet]] remarks in surprise. [[frank-hardy|Frank]] puzzles over the name: "Where have I heard that name before?" (012-063). [[joe-hardy|Joe]] suggests a connection: "Perhaps [[john-sparewell|Sparewell]] and [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] are the same man" (012-067).

## Connection to the Bender Collection

[[frank-hardy|Frank]] suddenly recalls where he heard the name [[john-sparewell|Sparewell]] — from [[amos-grice|Amos Grice]], who told them about the missing postage stamp collection. "[[john-sparewell|Sparewell]] was the man who disappeared from [[bayport|Bayport]] years ago" (012-068). [[joe-hardy|Joe]] confirms: "[[john-sparewell|John Sparewell]]! That was the name. I remember now!" (012-069). The discovery transforms the notebook from a minor find into a critical piece of evidence. "The Bender collection! [[john-sparewell|John Sparewell]]'s disappearance!" [[joe-hardy|Joe]] exclaims (012-073).

## Significance

The notebook establishes a direct connection between the events on [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] and the long-unsolved disappearance of [[john-sparewell|John Sparewell]], linking the present mystery to the theft of the valuable Bender stamp collection. [[frank-hardy|Frank]]'s earlier hypothesis that "if this is his notebook, we have positive proof that he stole our supplies" (012-058) is now superseded by a much larger discovery — the notebook ties their adversary to a major historical [[theft|crime]].
