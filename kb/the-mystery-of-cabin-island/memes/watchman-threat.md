# The Watchman's Threat

The menacing encounter on [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] in which a "tough-looking" guard orders the Hardy boys away, representing the first direct human threat they face in the mystery.

## Summary

During their initial exploration of [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]], the Hardy boys are confronted by a watchman who orders them to leave. The boys report the incident to their father: "He keeps such a tough-looking watchman on [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]]" (004-014). [[fenton-hardy|Fenton Hardy]] is surprised by the revelation — "A watchman?" (004-015) — and finds it puzzling that [[elroy-jefferson|Jefferson]] would employ a guard on an uninhabited island in winter: he "can't imagine why he would have a watchman there in the winter, anyway" (004-019). The watchman's presence and threatening behavior raise the first serious questions about what is really happening on [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]].
