The Boathouse is a small structure located on the shore of [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] (007-044). From the outside it is an unremarkable building that "would not have attracted a second glance from any passerby" (007-045), yet it serves as a pivotal location where the Hardy boys conduct surveillance and uncover critical information about the island's occupants.

## Location and Appearance

The boathouse sits on the shoreline of [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]], visible from the bay. [[frank-hardy|Frank Hardy]] first spots it while surveying the island from a distance, noting "a small boathouse" farther down the shore (007-044). Its modest appearance belies its importance to the plot — it is precisely because the building seems so ordinary that it becomes an effective place for covert observation.

## Pre-Expedition Meeting Point

Before the boys depart for [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]], they use their own boathouse in [[bayport|Bayport]] as a gathering place. After receiving [[christmas-holidays|Christmas]] gifts from their parents, the lads meet "at the boathouse half an hour later" to make final preparations for their departure (007-001). This boathouse serves as the staging ground for the expedition.

## Surveillance and Discovery

The [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]] boathouse becomes central to the [[detective-work|investigation]] when [[frank-hardy|Frank Hardy]] leads his companions toward it upon their arrival. [[frank-hardy|Frank]] notices "a distinct line of footprints in the snow leading toward the rear door" and observes that the prints "were those of two people and that they led toward the boathouse — not away from it" (007-045), indicating that someone was currently inside.

Positioning themselves "in the lee of the boathouse," the boys pause to listen (007-048). Through the wall, they overhear a conversation that reveals [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]]'s presence on the island (007-047–007-055). This eavesdropping confirms that the island is occupied and that [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]] is involved in suspicious activity. After gathering this intelligence, the boys move away, "in the deep snow they moved silently from the boathouse" (007-069).

## The Watchman's Visit

Later, the boys observe from an elevated position as [[watchman|the watchman]] approaches the boathouse. After "evidently deciding that further opposition was useless," the watchman goes "on down the path that led toward the boathouse at the base of the cliff" (008-056). The boys watch until he reaches the boathouse, and continue observing "until he emerged again with [[tad-carson|Tad Carson]] and [[ike-nash|Ike Nash]]" (008-057), revealing that [[hanleigh|Hanleigh]]'s two henchmen had been hiding there.

## Post-Theft Investigation

After the theft of the boys' supplies, the boathouse becomes part of the search effort. Although "the snow had obliterated any tracks the thief might have made in the immediate vicinity of the cabin," the boys find "several footprints" down by the boathouse, "on the side sheltered from the wind" (011-001). They search "down in the boathouse, under the trees, all around the cabin" in hopes of recovering their stolen food, but without success (012-006).

## Significance

The boathouse exemplifies how seemingly minor locations in the novel serve critical narrative functions. It is the site where the boys first confirm the presence of antagonists on the island, where hidden associates are revealed, and where crucial physical evidence (footprints) survives the weather. Without the boathouse as a listening post, the Hardy boys would have entered the island unaware of the danger awaiting them.
