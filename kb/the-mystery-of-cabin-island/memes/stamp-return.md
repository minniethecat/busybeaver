# Return of the Stamp Collection

The climactic resolution of _The Mystery of [[cabin-island|Cabin Island]]_, in which the Hardy boys succeed where professional detectives failed and return the treasured stamp collection to its rightful owner.

## Summary

After their [[detective-work|investigation]] uncovers the truth behind the stamp theft, the Hardy boys are able to recover the Bender stamp collection. [[elroy-jefferson|Elroy Jefferson]] is "pleased at the return of his treasured stamp collection" (025-018), a moment of emotional resolution after years of loss. [[elroy-jefferson|Jefferson]] acknowledges that he had "engaged professional detectives, who failed" (025-019), making the boys' success all the more remarkable. He tells them "you lads helped a lot" and credits them because "they did the detective work" (025-033). The return of the stamps represents not just the recovery of valuable property but the restoration of [[elroy-jefferson|Jefferson]]'s connection to his late wife [[mary-bender|Mary Bender]].

## Key Moments
