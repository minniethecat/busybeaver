# Malcolm's Return

Malcolm's return from England to Scotland with the invading army represents the play's decisive turn from tyranny toward restoration. Gathering English support under Siward and the exiled Scottish thanes, Malcolm marches from Birnam Wood to Dunsinane, fulfilling the Apparitions' prophecies and reversing the usurpation that began with Duncan's murder.

## The Gathering of Forces

In Act V, Malcolm appears at the head of an English army, joined by Macduff and the Scottish thanes who have defected from Macbeth. His return is first reported by Menteith: "The English power is near, led on by Malcolm, / His uncle Siward, and the good Macduff" (022-001-002). The collective nature of Malcolm's return — a coalition of English and Scottish forces — contrasts with the isolation of Macbeth, who by this point commands only "those that should be ours" (028-003).
