# Siward

Siward is a character in William Shakespeare's tragedy *Macbeth*.

## Overview

Siward, the Earl of Northumberland, is an English general who leads the army supporting Malcolm's claim to the Scottish throne. He is described as "warlike Siward" (017-003), a veteran commander lent by "pious Edward," the English king, to aid the Scottish rebels (020-048).

## Role in the Play

Siward commands ten thousand English soldiers who join Malcolm and Macduff in their campaign against Macbeth (020-022). He appears with Malcolm, Macduff, and the Scottish thanes in the final acts as they march toward Dunsinane (024-001)(026-001). His son, Young Siward, confronts Macbeth in battle and is slain (027-010). When told of his son's death, Siward stoically accepts it, asking only whether his wounds were in the front — a mark of honorable combat. Siward survives the battle and appears in the final scene alongside Malcolm, who is proclaimed King of Scotland (028-013).
