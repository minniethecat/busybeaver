# Macbeth's Second Meeting with the Witches

Macbeth's Second Meeting with the Witches occurs in Act IV, Scene I of William Shakespeare's tragedy. Desperate to secure his crown against mounting threats, Macbeth seeks out the Weird Sisters in their cavern. They conjure the Apparitions — supernatural visions whose deceptive reassurances drive Macbeth to his final ruin.

## Overview

Macbeth enters the Witches' cavern demanding answers: "I conjure you, by that which you profess, / Howe'er you come to know it, answer me" (018-018). The Witches are brewing a hellish potion — "Double, double, toil and trouble; / Fire, burn; and cauldron, bubble" (018-006) — into which they throw "eye of newt and toe of frog, / Wool of bat and tongue of dog" (018-012).

Hecate, the goddess of witchcraft, appears briefly to commend the Witches' work: "O, well done! I commend your pains; / And every one shall share i' th' gains" (018-012). Then the Apparitions rise from the cauldron.

The First Apparition, an Armed Head, warns: "Macbeth! Macbeth! Macbeth! Beware Macduff; / Beware the Thane of Fife" (018-030). Macbeth resolves to kill Macduff. The Second Apparition, a Bloody Child, declares: "Be bloody, bold, and resolute. Laugh to scorn / The power of man, for none of woman born / Shall harm Macbeth" (018-036). Reassured, Macbeth decides to spare Macduff — then changes his mind again, resolving to kill him anyway to "make assurance double sure" (018-038).

The Third Apparition, a Crowned Child with a tree in his hand, promises: "Macbeth shall never vanquish'd be, until / Great Birnam wood to high Dunsinane hill / Shall come against him" (018-045). Buoyed, Macbeth asks one final, desperate question: "shall Banquo's issue ever / Reign in this kingdom?" (018-053). The Witches show him a vision — "A show of eight Kings, the last with a glass in his hand; Banquo's Ghost following" (018-055) — confirming that Banquo's line will indeed inherit the throne.

Macbeth leaves the cavern a transformed man. The prophecies have given him false confidence, and he immediately orders the slaughter of Macduff's family. From this point forward, his tyranny accelerates unchecked — until the prophecies' double meanings are exposed on the battlefield.
