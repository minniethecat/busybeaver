# Glamis

Glamis is a location in William Shakespeare's tragedy *Macbeth*.

## Overview

Glamis appears in the play primarily as Macbeth's original title — Thane of Glamis — which he holds before the Witches' prophecy elevates him to Thane of Cawdor and eventually to King.

## Significance in the Play

The title "Thane of Glamis" is Macbeth's birthright and his identity at the start of the play. The Witches first hail him as "Thane of Glamis" (003-018), acknowledging the title he already holds before prophesying greater honors. Macbeth himself confirms: "By Sinel's death I know I am Thane of Glamis" (003-029). The title becomes part of his self-identification: "Glamis, and Thane of Cawdor: The greatest is behind" (003-044). After Macbeth becomes king, the title is still associated with him — Lady Macbeth's waiting-gentlewoman reports his guilt-ridden cry: "Glamis hath murder'd sleep, and therefore Cawdor Shall sleep no more" (009-024). Banquo later observes that Macbeth now holds "King, Cawdor, Glamis, all" (012-002), noting the fulfillment of the Witches' prophecies.
