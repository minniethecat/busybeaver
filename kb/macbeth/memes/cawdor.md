# Cawdor

Cawdor is a location in William Shakespeare's tragedy *Macbeth*.

## Overview

Cawdor appears in the play primarily as a title — the Thane of Cawdor — rather than as a physical location. The title passes from a traitorous nobleman to Macbeth, fulfilling the Witches' prophecy and setting the tragedy in motion.

## Historical Significance

The title "Thane of Cawdor" is central to Macbeth's rise. The original Thane of Cawdor is executed as a traitor by King Duncan after rebelling against the crown (002-017). Duncan declares that the thane "shall deceive Our bosom interest" no more (002-020). The Witches then hail Macbeth as "Thane of Cawdor" (003-019), a title he does not yet hold. When Ross and Angus arrive to confer the title upon him, Macbeth is stunned: "The Thane of Cawdor lives: why do you dress me In borrow'd robes?" (003-042). The swift fulfillment of this prophecy convinces Macbeth that the Witches' prediction of kingship may also come true (003-044)(003-046).
