# Death & Violence

*Macbeth* is Shakespeare's most violent tragedy. From the opening battle report to Macbeth's severed head, death is never far from the surface — and when it arrives, it is rendered with unnerving intimacy. This index gathers pages that explore how the play thinks about killing, dying, and what comes after.
