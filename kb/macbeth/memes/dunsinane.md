# Dunsinane

Dunsinane is a location in William Shakespeare's tragedy *Macbeth*.

## Overview

Dunsinane is Macbeth's fortified castle and the site of the play's climactic battle. It is inextricably linked to the Witches' prophecy that Macbeth shall not be vanquished until Birnam Wood comes to Dunsinane Hill.

## Significance in the Play

Dunsinane becomes Macbeth's seat of power after he is crowned king. As his tyranny grows, he retreats there and "strongly fortifies" the castle against the approaching English army (022-007). The confident tyrant "Keeps still in Dunsinane" (024-008), believing himself invincible because of the prophecy: "Till Birnam wood remove to Dunsinane I cannot taint with fear" (023-002). The prophecy is fulfilled when Malcolm's army uses branches from Birnam Wood as camouflage, and the wood appears to march toward the castle (025-015). The final battle takes place at Dunsinane, where Macbeth falls despite the prophecy: "Though Birnam wood be come to Dunsinane, And thou oppos'd" (028-011).
