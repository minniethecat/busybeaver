# The English Forces

The English forces are the army raised in England by [[malcolm]] with the support of King Edward the Confessor, commanded by [[siward]], Earl of Northumberland.

This military force invades Scotland in Act V to depose the tyrant [[macbeth]] and restore Malcolm to the Scottish throne.

## Nature & Role

The English army serves as the instrument of legitimate restoration.

After fleeing to the English court following his father's murder, Malcolm raises an army with English support.

Siward, a seasoned commander and Malcolm's uncle, leads the operation as "General of the English Forces" (P01-013).

As the army marches toward Dunsinane, the Scottish thanes who have defected from Macbeth report its approach:

> The English power is near, led on by Malcolm,
> His uncle Siward, and the good Macduff.
> Revenges burn in them; for their dear causes
> Would to the bleeding and the grim alarm
> Excite the mortified man.
>
> (022-002)

The army's approach is first reported to Macbeth by a servant: "The English force, so please you" (023-007).

[[macbeth]]'s dismissive response — "Would scour these English hence?" (023-031) — reveals his overconfidence, as he still trusts the Witches' prophecy that no man of woman born can harm him.

## Political & Military Significance

The English forces represent the restoration of legitimate political order through military intervention.

[[malcolm]] tests [[macduff]]'s loyalty before revealing the army's readiness, ensuring that the English-backed invasion serves Scottish liberation rather than foreign conquest.

As the army marches through Birnam Wood, each soldier carries a bough to disguise their numbers.

[[malcolm]]'s order — "Let every soldier hew him down a bough" (024-006) — unwittingly fulfills the Witches' prophecy that Macbeth will not fall until Birnam Wood comes to Dunsinane.

The English army's approach thus becomes both a military strategy and a supernatural inevitability.

The army's victory vindicates the play's political theology: a tyrant's rule cannot stand when legitimate authority is backed by righteous force.
