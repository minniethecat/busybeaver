The **trumpet** is a recurring sound motif in _Macbeth_ that marks key dramatic moments — from divine judgment to earthly murder to military climax. Each appearance shifts the instrument's symbolic register, moving from sacred to profane to martial.

## Appearances

### Trumpet-Tongued Angels

In Act I, Scene VII, as Macbeth weighs the consequences of killing Duncan, he imagines Duncan's virtues pleading against the murder:

> his virtues / Will plead like angels, trumpet-tongued, against / The deep damnation of his taking-off (007-002)

The compound "trumpet-tongued" fuses sound and speech: the trumpet here is the instrument of divine judgment, a voice so clarion that it will reach heaven itself. The image draws on the biblical tradition of trumpets announcing the Last Judgment, casting Duncan's murder as a cosmic rather than merely political crime.

### The Hideous Trumpet

When Macduff discovers Duncan's body, he attempts to wake the household:

> What's the business, / That such a hideous trumpet calls to parley / The sleepers of the house? Speak, speak! (010-043)

Here the trumpet is inverted. Macduff imagines that a "hideous trumpet" has been sounded — one loud enough to wake the dead. The irony is that the trumpet (Macduff's own cry of horror) wakes the living but cannot restore the dead king. The adjective "hideous" marks the shift from the sacred register of Act I to the grotesque reality of the murdered body.

### Battle Trumpets

In the play's final act, Macduff commands: "Make all our trumpets speak; give them all breath" (026-004). The trumpet returns to its military function, but now in the service of rightful vengeance rather than Macbeth's tyranny. Where earlier trumpets announced judgment and horror, this final blast signals the restoration of order.

## Symbolic Arc

The trumpet's trajectory through the play mirrors Macbeth's moral arc: from divine trumpet (potential redemption) → hideous trumpet (the murder's horror) → battle trumpet (retributive justice). The instrument that once pleaded for Duncan's soul finally sounds the charge that destroys his murderer.
