# Dr. Matignon

Dr. J. J. Matignon was a French physician attached to the French Legation in [[peking|Peking]] during the Boxer Rebellion period. He is cited in Katherine Carl's endnotes as an authority on the causes of the 1900 uprising, offering a notably self-critical European perspective.

## Role in the Boxer Period

Matignon served in Peking during the siege of the Legations in 1900. His medical service during the crisis gave him firsthand experience of both the violence of the uprising and the complexities of Chinese-European relations.

## In Carl's Account

Matignon appears in a single but significant reference in Carl's endnotes. Quoted in the French account of the siege by F. Laur, Matignon offers a strikingly honest assessment of European responsibility for the Boxer crisis:

> "C'est l'Europe tout entière qu'il faut mettre en cause. C'est parce qu'elle n'a pas compris les Chinois, c'est parce qu'elle a prétendu leur imposer ses idées que le movement xénophobe de 1900 a déterminé cette explosion de haine" (036-067).

Translation: "It is all of Europe that must be called into question. It is because she did not understand the Chinese, because she claimed to impose her ideas upon them, that the xenophobic movement of 1900 produced this explosion of hatred."

## Significance

Matignon's analysis represents a rare European voice of self-criticism in the aftermath of the Boxer Rebellion. His argument—that European incomprehension and cultural imperialism were root causes of the uprising—anticipates modern historiographical perspectives and provides an important counterpoint to the more common narratives of Chinese barbarism.
