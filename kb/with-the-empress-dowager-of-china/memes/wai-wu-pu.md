# Wai-Wu-Pu

The Wai-Wu-Pu (Waiwubu), or Chinese Foreign Office, was the Qing government ministry responsible for foreign affairs, established in 1901 as part of the late Qing reforms. It replaced the earlier Tsung Li Yamen (030-018).

## Role

Carl interacted with the Wai-Wu-Pu throughout her residency. It was through this office that "Mrs. Conger received an official notification from the Wai-Wu-Pu (Chinese Foreign Office) requesting 'Her Excellency Mrs. Conger to present the American artist, Miss Carl, to Her Imperial Majesty'" [P04-002]. The Wai-Wu-Pu provided a "Chinese Guard of Honor" to escort Carl's party to the Summer Palace (001-004). After the portrait was completed, "several members of the Wai-Wu-Pu were present as interpreters" (032-013) and "the portrait was removed to the Wai-Wu-Pu (Foreign Office)" where officials could view it (034-008).

## In Carl's Account

Carl found the Wai-Wu-Pu's processes typically Chinese: "the 'mills of' Chinese Officialdom 'grind slowly'" [P04-002]. She waited from April to July 1903 for the official notification arranging her audience with the Empress Dowager. Carl sometimes met "attaches of the Wai-Wu-Pu and different Yamens hurrying thither" (025-005) in the Palace precincts. Despite the delays, the office ultimately facilitated her unprecedented access to the Inner Court, and "a railway had been laid from the Wai-Wu-Pu to the station" (034-009) to transport the finished portrait for its journey to the St. Louis Exposition.
