# Imperial Plowing Ceremony

The Imperial Plowing Ceremony was an annual agricultural rite performed by the [[emperor-kwang-hsu|Emperor]] at the [[temple-of-agriculture|Temple of Agriculture]] in [[peking|Peking]]. The ceremony symbolized the Emperor's role as the guarantor of agricultural prosperity and connected the Imperial house to the most fundamental of Chinese economic activities.

## The Ceremony

Carl describes the public ceremony at the Temple of Agriculture: "The annual plowing of a furrow and sowing of the first seeds of the year by the Emperor" (029-021). This public ritual, held at the great temple complex near the Altar of Heaven, was one of the most important state ceremonies of the agricultural calendar.

The Empress Dowager performed a complementary rite: "the planting of a mulberry tree (to nourish the silkworms) by the Empress Dowager" (029-021). Together, these ceremonies—plowing and planting, grain and silk—represented the dual foundation of Chinese civilization.

The annual agricultural rites performed by the Emperor and Empress connected the throne to the land:

> The annual plowing of a furrow and sowing of the first seeds of the year by the Emperor, the planting of a mulberry tree (to nourish the silkworms) by the Empress, are other touches of Nature which show how near the Chinese are to the heart of things.
>
> (029-021)

## The Private Plowing

In addition to the public ceremony, Carl witnessed a private version held within the Palace grounds: "I was to see the private plowing, done in the Palace grounds and viewed only by the Princes of the Imperial Family and the highest Manchu nobles" (033-012). This inner-court ritual was restricted to the innermost circle of the Imperial clan, emphasizing the ceremony's intimate connection to Manchu identity.

## In Carl's Account

Carl situates the plowing ceremony within her discussion of Chinese religious rites, linking it to the worship of nature. She observes that it demonstrates how "all their conventions and all their forms [are] very near the heart itself of Nature" (029-021). The ceremony was part of a seasonal cycle that included the [[spring-awakening-ceremony|Spring Awakening Ceremony]] and the [[temple-of-agriculture|Temple of Agriculture]] rites.

## Significance

The Imperial Plowing Ceremony represents the ancient Chinese conception of the Emperor as the intermediary between Heaven and Earth, responsible for ensuring the fertility of the land. Its continuation at the late Qing Court, even as the dynasty faced existential threats, testifies to the enduring power of these ritual traditions.
