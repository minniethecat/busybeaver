# Ancestor Worship

Ancestor worship was a central practice in Qing dynasty China, rooted in
Confucian principles of filial piety and propriety (*li*, 礼).
It permeated all levels of society — from the Emperor's grand ceremonial
sacrifices to the household rites of ordinary families.

## At the Imperial Court

At the Qing court, ancestor worship took its most elaborate form
in the rites performed before the ancestral tablets.
Carl observed that the Empress Dowager's birthday celebrations included
"rites and worship of the ancestral tablets" (023-008),
and that sacrifices at the ancestral tablets were a recurring
feature of court religious ceremonies (028-012).


> The Emperor of China, with the usual Chinese tolerance — and the Chinese are the most tolerant people in the world as to religious faith — is not only the head of one church, but of all the churches in China. He is, as Emperor, the Great High Priest of Heaven, the High Priest of Buddhism and Taoism, and is, of course, a Confucian; though this is a philosophy rather than a religion.
>
> (013-004)
The Emperor himself was required to perform the Autumnal Sacrifices
to his ancestors, a duty so important that the timing of his own
birthday celebration was adjusted to accommodate the sacrificial calendar.
The Emperor's birthday was observed two days before the actual anniversary
because he "must make the Autumnal Sacrifices to his Ancestors three days
before his birthday" (009-007).

## Role in the Social Order

Ancestor worship was intertwined with the Confucian social hierarchy.
The ascendancy of the ancestor in Chinese culture was such that
"the Emperor could not sit while his Ancestor, the Empress Dowager, stood"
(030-012), and tradition dictated that "she, being his 'ancestor,'
must always take the first place" (030-015).

The practice also reinforced the preference for male heirs:
"A daughter not being able to sacrifice to the ancestors,
even Manchus prefer a son" (026-005). The desecration of ancestral tombs
was considered among the gravest of offenses (028-014).
