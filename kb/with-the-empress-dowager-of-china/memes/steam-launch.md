## 蒸汽船与御用画舫

At the [[summer-palace|Summer Palace]], the Imperial party took their excursions on the lake in two types of vessels: the traditional, picturesque Imperial barge and the modern steam-launch. The [[empress-dowager|Empress Dowager]] appreciated both — she was "artistic and conservative enough to like the old-fashioned barge" but also "intelligent enough to appreciate the advantages of other modes of locomotion, and has no prejudices; in fact, she rather likes trying new things." (013-002)

When the days were long and the air soft, with the lake "engirdled with its chain of blooming lotus," Her Majesty preferred the barge. But when the shorter, cooler days came and the lotus were no longer in bloom, she ordered the steam-launch for the promenades, seeming to "like its swift and noisy progress as much as she had before enjoyed the softly gliding motion of the barge." (013-002)

## 御座与布置

Her Majesty's Throne on the launch was positioned on the prow, just outside and above the cabin. The Princesses and Court Ladies sat within the luxuriously fitted cabin with its lounges and tables. The Empress Dowager, however, "always wanted the fresh air and the view, and never went inside." (013-002) The young Empress and the Ladies remained seated within the cabin.

## 首次汽船事故

The first day the party went out in the steam-launch, the engineer appeared not to have it fully under control, and the launch soon ran aground in a field of water-plants near the island. (013-003) There was "great consternation among the eunuchs" when the launch could not advance even with full steam. The engineer "didn't seem to know what to do." (013-003) Her Majesty ordered the engines reversed, but it was some time before the launch moved.

Despite the mishap, the Empress Dowager remained "perfectly unconcerned, and laughed at their fears for her safety. She said it would be no great matter for her to walk over to the island. It would only mean one pair of shoes the less!" (013-003) When the Chief Eunuch, fearing another mishap, wanted to return, Her Majesty would not hear of it and insisted upon completing the excursion as planned. When the launch ran aground again and no effort could free it, she calmly ordered the barge brought alongside and finished the tour in the traditional vessel — demonstrating both her adaptability and her determination. (013-003)

## 节庆日的蒸汽船

On special occasions, the steam-launches added a modern note to the otherwise traditional spectacle of the Imperial lake procession. During the [[european-circus-palace|European circus at the Palace]], the lake was described as "gay with beautiful barges, great houseboats, and numbers of flat boats for the eunuchs," all "picturesquely decorated with flying banners, pennants, and tasseled wands." Two steam-launches, "puffing away, gave an air of modernity to this most Oriental fleet." (021-005)

One of the launches was splendidly decorated with yellow banners, with gorgeous yellow [[silk-court-dress|silk]] scarfs festooned around the cabin and the Imperial flag flying above it. The Empress Dowager and the [[emperor-kwang-hsu|Emperor]] descended the marble steps to this gaily decked launch and started off for the far end of the lake, the Imperial banners and colors flying. (021-005)
