## 定义与特征

The Pekingese is a breed of pug dog that was a great favorite at the Chinese imperial court. The author describes them as having "magnificent specimens" with "perfectly symmetrical" spots, "beautifully long and silky" hair, and "wonderful intelligence." (006-006) These dogs were bred with exceptional care within the Palace precincts.

The breed's historical significance extends beyond China: the author notes that "the King Charles spaniels are said to have been bred out of the first of these dogs ever carried to Europe" (006-006), making the Pekingese an ancestor of one of Europe's most famous toy breeds.

## 在清宫中的地位

The [[empress-dowager|Empress Dowager Cixi]] was particularly devoted to her Pekingese dogs. She kept "dozens of these pets" in a beautiful pavilion near her Throne-room at the [[summer-palace|Summer Palace]], where they lived in luxury — "with marble floors," "silken cushions to sleep on," and "special eunuchs to attend them." (006-009) They were given daily outdoor exercise and regular baths.

She had favorites among them — one was a "splendid, fawn-colored Pekingese pug, with large, pale-brown, liquid eyes" whom she called "Shadza" (meaning "fool"), because "he was not easily taught, even as a puppy." (006-006) Despite his name, he was "devoted to her, and she [was] very fond of him."

## 袖犬与 Empress Dowager 的态度

The Empress Dowager strongly disapproved of the "sleeve-dog" — a miniature variety of the Pekingese that was stunted by being "fed only on sweets and wines." (006-007) The author reports that Her Majesty "hates the thought of their being stunted" and "cannot understand animals being deformed, at man's pleasure" (006-007), showing a notable compassion for animal welfare that was unusual for the era.

## Me-lah：作者得到的礼物

The author, [[katherine-carl|Katherine Carl]], was herself given a Pekingese by the Empress Dowager. She describes receiving "a beautiful white-and-amber-colored Pekingese pug" which Her Majesty named "Me-lah" (Golden Amber), "from the color of his spots." (006-012) The dog became the author's "constant companion and faithful friend" during her stay at the Palace, and also appears in a later chapter when Shadza "resented any such accoutrement" as a hat being placed near his ears. (020-003)

> She has some magnificent specimens of Pekingese pugs and of a sort of Skye terrier. The pugs are bred with great care and have reached a high state of perfection, their spots being perfectly symmetrical and their hair beautifully long and silky, and they are of wonderful intelligence.
>
> (006-006)
