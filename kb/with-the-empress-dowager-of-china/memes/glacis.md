# Glacis

The Glacis is an open area in the Legation Quarter of [[peking|Peking]], situated between the enclosure of the [[british-legation|British Legation]] and the [[marble-bridge|Marble Bridge]]. Katherine Carl crossed it daily on her journey from the [[american-legation|United States Legation]] to the [[imperial-city|Imperial City]] (025-003).

## Location and Context

The Glacis lies in the [[tartar-city|Tartar City]], forming part of the route between the diplomatic compounds of the Legation Quarter and the walls of the Imperial City. Carl described her morning journey:

> My cart rattled down the road, running parallel to the canal, past the splendid enclosure of the English Legation to the "Glacis," and across the Marble Bridge, that traverses it, to the narrow street under the great red walls of the Imperial City. (025-003)

## Description

The term "glacis" in military architecture refers to a gently sloping open area before a fortification, designed to expose attackers to defensive fire. In Peking's context, the Glacis was an open space within the Legation Quarter that had likely been cleared for defensive purposes after the Boxer Rebellion, ensuring clear lines of sight around the diplomatic compounds.

## Role in Court Life

The Glacis formed part of Carl's daily commute from the Legation Quarter to her studio within the Imperial City. Her passage across it was one of the "picturesque" elements of her morning journey through Peking (025-003).

## In the Narrative

The Glacis appears in Chapter 25 as a waypoint on Carl's route through Peking. Its mention provides geographic texture to her description of the journey from the diplomatic quarter to the Palace gates.

## Historical Significance

The presence of a glacis in the Legation Quarter reflects the militarization of the diplomatic enclave after the Boxer Rebellion. Under the Boxer Protocol of 1901, the Legation Quarter was fortified and defended by foreign troops, and the creation of open defensive spaces like the Glacis was part of this transformation of urban Peking.
