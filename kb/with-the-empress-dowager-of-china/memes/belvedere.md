# Belvedere

A belvedere — an open-sided pavilion commanding a view — stands on Coal Hill in Peking, marking the spot where the last emperor of the Ming dynasty committed suicide when Beijing fell to rebel forces in 1644 (010-017).

## Location and Context

The belvedere is situated on Coal Hill (Jingshan), an artificial hill at the northern edge of the Imperial City. From the marble terrace of a lama temple south of the hill, visitors could see both the Dagoba at the summit and the belvedere below it (010-017).

## Description

The belvedere is one of the structures crowning the eminences of Peking's imperial landscape. In the vocabulary of the period, "belvedere" referred to an open-sided pavilion or summerhouse designed to command a scenic view. Carl encountered similar belvederes and summerhouses throughout the imperial grounds, noting that "all the eminences, where views could be had, were surmounted by charming summerhouses and belvederes" (003-004).

Carl explored the Palace grounds each morning and discovered their design:

> The Park was enclosed by high walls, for the Chinese are jealous of their privacy. Parts of the grounds were gently undulating, and all the eminences, where views could be had, were surmounted by charming summerhouses and belvederes.
>
> (003-004)

## Historical Significance

The belvedere on Coal Hill derives its historical importance from the death of the Chongzhen Emperor, the last ruler of the Ming dynasty. In April 1644, as rebel forces under Li Zicheng breached the walls of Peking, the emperor fled to Coal Hill and hanged himself at this spot. His death marked the end of the 276-year Ming dynasty and the beginning of a turbulent transition period that would eventually lead to the Qing conquest of China.

## In the Narrative

Carl views the belvedere from the terrace of a Buddhist temple during an excursion by boat along the palace lakes. From that vantage, she takes in the panoramic view of the Imperial City, noting both the Dagoba atop Coal Hill and the historically freighted belvedere below (010-017).
