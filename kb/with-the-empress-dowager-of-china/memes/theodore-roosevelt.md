## Role in the Narrative

[[theodore-roosevelt|President Theodore Roosevelt]] appears in the narrative only at its conclusion, when the portrait of the [[empress-dowager|Empress Dowager Cixi]] — painted by [[katherine-carl|Katharine Carl]] for the St. Louis Exposition — arrived in Washington. His Excellency Sir [[sir-chentung-liang-cheng|Chentung Liang Cheng]], the Chinese Minister to Washington, attended by his Secretaries, made a formal presentation of the portrait to the President, "which Mr. Roosevelt received on behalf of the United States Government." (034-015)

The presentation marked the culmination of Carl's nearly year-long commission at the Chinese Court and represented a significant diplomatic gesture between China and the United States.
