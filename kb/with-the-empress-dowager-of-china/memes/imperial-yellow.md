# Imperial Yellow

Imperial yellow (明黄, *mínghuáng*) was the color reserved exclusively for the Emperor, Empress Dowager, and their immediate family at the Qing Court — a sumptuary law violation could carry severe penalties. (007-006)

## Description

Carl first encountered Imperial yellow on Her Majesty's portrait gown — "a gown of Imperial yellow, brocaded in the wistaria vine in realistic colors and richly embroidered in pearls" (001-011). The Emperor also wore yellow: "a yellow gown, tightly belted in around his slender waist" (009-004). Even furniture followed the color code — the Empress Dowager used "a yellow, throne-like chair" (002-013).

Carl observed Imperial yellow deployed across the Palace during the Emperor's Birthday festivities:

> The slanting and projecting, upturned roofs of the different buildings forming the Palaces were decorated with scarfs of varicolored silk, knotted into a curious sort of fringe of rosettes, about two feet long; yellow, the Imperial color, and red, the festive color, predominating, but other colors were introduced into the color-scheme to accentuate these.
>
> (007-006)

## Cultural Significance

Yellow symbolized the center (中, *zhōng*) in Chinese cosmology and was associated with the Yellow River, the cradle of Chinese civilization. Under Qing sumptuary law, the specific shade of "Imperial yellow" was forbidden to anyone outside the Imperial family. (004-002) Lower ranks could wear related shades like apricot or pale yellow, but the bright, saturated Imperial yellow was the exclusive prerogative of the throne. (015-020)

## In Carl's Account

As a painter, Carl was alert to color symbolism. She noted the visual impact of the Empress Dowager in Imperial yellow against the dark throne room background. The color appeared repeatedly: in the Empress Dowager's gowns, the Emperor's robes, and the ceremonial decorations at the Birthday celebration where "there was also a jade ruyie in each tray, such as are presented to their Majesties" alongside other Imperial-colored objects (009-026).
