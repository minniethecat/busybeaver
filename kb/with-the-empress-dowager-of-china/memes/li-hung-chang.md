# Li Hung-chang

Li Hung-chang (李鴻章, 1823–1901) was the most prominent Chinese statesman of the late 19th century. As Viceroy of Zhili, Commissioner of the Northern Ports, and China's chief diplomat, he dominated Chinese foreign relations for a generation. Though he died two years before Katherine Carl's arrival in Peking, his presence pervades her memoir.

## Career and Significance

Li rose to prominence during the Taiping Rebellion and became the architect of China's Self-Strengthening Movement, promoting military modernization, industrial development, and diplomatic engagement with Western powers. He founded the Beiyang Fleet, negotiated the Treaty of Shimonoseki after the Sino-Japanese War, and represented China in its dealings with the powers throughout the late 19th century.

His final diplomatic act was negotiating the Boxer Protocol of 1901, the settlement that ended the [[boxer-rebellion|Boxer Rebellion]]. The negotiations exhausted him, and he died shortly after their conclusion.

## In Carl's Account

Li appears in Carl's memoir primarily through his physical legacy. Her first lodging in Peking was at the [[hsien-liang-hsu|Hsien-Liang-Hsu]], described as "the former residence of Li-Hung-Chang" (010-010). The grand compound, which had housed one of China's greatest officials, was now repurposed as a guest residence for the American portrait artist.

Carl also references Li in connection with the [[temple-of-agriculture|Temple of Agriculture]], another site associated with his legacy. The Viceroy's vast influence over late Qing affairs meant that his shadow fell across many of the institutions and places Carl encountered, even though she never met him.

> The next morning at seven, a green official chair with its bearers came to take me to the Sea Palace. I was first carried to the Hsien-Liang-Hsu, the "Temple of the loyal and virtuous," where Li-Hung-Chang formerly had his home in Peking, and a part of which the Yu-Kengs had arranged for their home after their return from their mission at Paris.
>
> (010-010)
