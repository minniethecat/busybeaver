# Chang Chih-tung

Chang Chih-tung (1837–1909) was one of the most prominent Viceroys and scholar-officials of the late Qing dynasty. A leader of the Self-Strengthening Movement, Chang advocated for selective modernization while preserving Chinese cultural traditions. He is cited in Katherine Carl's endnotes for his ode to the [[emperor-kwang-hsu|Emperor Kwang-Hsu]].

## Official Career

Chang served as Viceroy of Liangguang (Guangdong and Guangxi) and later as Viceroy of Huguang (Hunan and Hubei), where he promoted industrial development, modern education, and military reform. His slogan "Chinese learning for fundamental principles, Western learning for practical application" encapsulated the moderate reformist position.

## In Carl's Account

Carl cites Chang's famous ode to the Emperor in her discussion of [[filial-piety|filial piety]]: "In the Viceroy Chang-Chih-Tung's famous ode to the Emperor, he speaks of this filial piety as one of the Emperor's greatest qualities: 'Who does not admire the filial reverence and piety with which he waits upon his august mother?'" (036-052).

The ode serves Carl's purpose of presenting the Emperor—and by extension the Imperial family—in a favorable light. By citing a respected Chinese scholar-official's praise of the Emperor's filial devotion, Carl grounds her own positive portrayal in Chinese cultural values rather than Western judgments.

## Significance

Chang Chih-tung represents the traditional scholar-official ideal at the highest level of Qing government. His endorsement of the Emperor's filial piety carries weight precisely because it is expressed in Confucian terms that any educated Chinese reader would recognize. Carl's citation of his ode demonstrates her memoir's strategy of validating Western observations through Chinese authorities.

> In the Viceroy Chang-Chih-Tung's famous ode to the Emperor, he speaks of this filial piety as one of the Emperor's greatest qualities: "Who does not admire the filial reverence and piety with which he waits upon his august mother?
