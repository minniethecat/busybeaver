# Emperor Kwang-Hsu

Emperor Kwang-Hsu (光绪帝, *Guāngxù*), referred to by Carl as "His Majesty Kwang-Hsu," was the twelfth Emperor of the Qing Dynasty's "Great Purity" reign (008-003).

## Role at Court

Carl describes the Emperor as a figure whose authority was shared with -- and largely overshadowed by -- the Empress Dowager (008-003). He "sat beside" Her Majesty during official functions, and Carl observed that "when the Empress Dowager sits upon the throne with him, I think he will not assert himself" (008-002). His reign had begun at the age of five under the co-regency of the Empress Dowager (008-003).

## Personal Characteristics

Carl found the Emperor "an interesting study, but not to the degree of Her Majesty the Empress Dowager, who has charm and is so fascinating" (008-004). He was "nearing the completion of his thirty-second year" when Carl was first presented to him (008-004). She describes his dress -- "a yellow gown, tightly belted in around his slender waist with a handsome belt buckle of jade" (009-004).

Carl's physical description of the Emperor reveals her painter's eye for detail:

> In person he is of slight and elegant figure, not more than five feet four in height. He has a well-shaped head, with the intellectual qualities well developed, a high brow, with large brown eyes and rather drooping lids, not at all Chinese in form or setting. His nose is high and, like most members of the Imperial Family, is of the so-called "noble" type.
>
> (008-004)

## In Carl's Account

Carl observed the Emperor at the morning salutation ceremony, where the Ladies of his family made their formal greetings (009-004). She witnessed him receiving ceremonial gifts, including "a jade emblem, called by the Chinese a *ruyie*" (009-011), and participating in the elaborate Birthday celebrations (009-029).
