# Empress Dowager Cixi

Empress Dowager Cixi (慈禧太后), referred to by Carl as "Her Majesty the Empress Dowager" and "the Great Empress Dowager, Tze-Shi," was the de facto ruler of China for nearly half a century (001-017).

## Role at Court

By the time of Carl's visit in 1903–1904, the Empress Dowager had consolidated power as the supreme authority at the Qing Court (001-017). Carl describes her as receiving officials, presiding over ceremonies, and managing both domestic and foreign affairs from the Summer Palace. She sat upon the Dragon Throne for official functions and shared authority with the Emperor Kwang-Hsu, who "sat beside" her rather than independently (008-002).

## Personal Characteristics

Carl's portrait sessions gave her unusual access to the Empress Dowager's personality. She describes Her Majesty as animated, curious about Western art, and keenly interested in the progress of her portrait (001-002). Despite being "not more than five feet tall," the Empress Dowager commanded the room through presence and the added height of "Manchu shoes with six-inch-high, stiff white soles" (001-014). She changed her elaborate gowns daily and never repeated an outfit during Carl's entire residency (004-007).

## In Carl's Account

Carl received Her Majesty "in private Audience" on August 5, 1903, at the Summer Palace [P04-002] (001-002). She found the Empress Dowager "kindly" and "very gracious," a stark contrast to the fearsome reputation that preceded her [P04-003]. Over months of sittings, Carl painted four portraits of the Empress Dowager -- the first for the St. Louis Exposition (028-006) and three additional ones.

Carl describes the moment she began painting:

> The inscrutable eyes of the wonderful woman I was to paint, fixed piercingly upon me, were also disconcerting; but just then the eighty-five clocks in this particular Throne-room began to chime, play airs, and strike the hour in eighty-five different ways. The auspicious moment had come! I raised my charcoal and put the first stroke upon the canvas of the first portrait that had ever been painted of the Empress Dowager of Great China, the powerful Tze-Shi.
>
> (001-017)
