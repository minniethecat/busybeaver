## In the Narrative

[[laotze|Lao-tze]] (Laozi), the ancient Chinese philosopher and founder of [[taoism|Taoism]], is referenced once in Katharine Carl's memoir, in a passage discussing the religious character of the Chinese people. The author observes that while the Chinese participate in both Buddhist and Taoist rites, "the doctrines of Buddha and Laotze have become so encrusted with error in China as to afford no moral or ideal help to their followers." (029-011)

The passage contrasts the formal religious practices of Buddhism and Taoism with the ethical philosophy of [[confucius|Confucius]], which Carl identifies as the true moral foundation of Chinese life: "They get all their moral support from the writings of Confucius and all their ideals from communion with Nature." (029-011)
