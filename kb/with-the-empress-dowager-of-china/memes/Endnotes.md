[036-001]1. > In the firmament of the Son of Heaven

[036-002]>

[036-003]> A brilliant new star has risen!—

[036-004]>

[036-005]> Supple as the neck of the swan

[036-006]>

[036-007]> Is the charm of her graceful form.

[036-008]>

[036-009]> From the firm contour of charming chin

[036-010]>

[036-011]> Springs the faultless oval of her fair face,

[036-012]>

[036-013]> Crowned by the harmonious arch

[036-014]>

[036-015]> Of a broad and noble brow.

[036-016]>

[036-017]> The stately profile, chiseled clear,

[036-018]>

[036-019]> Is dominated by the pure line of noble nose

[036-020]>

[036-021]> Straight and slender and singularly mobile,

[036-022]>

[036-023]> Sensitive to all the impressions of the soul.

[036-024]>

[036-025]> Dewy lips with gracious curves

[036-026]>

[036-027]> Are the portals of a dainty mouth

[036-028]>

[036-029]> Where often blooms the sweet flower

[036-030]>

[036-031]> Of a most alluring smile.

[036-032]>

[036-033]> Her face is lit by black and sparkling eyes,

[036-034]>

[036-035]> Whose flames, in hours of ease,

[036-036]>

[036-037]> With oblique caress, envelop and thrill

[036-038]>

[036-039]> That happy mortal allowed to see.

[036-040]>

[036-041]> When stern circumstance demands,

[036-042]>

[036-043]> Her graceful form an attitude of firmness takes,

[036-044]>

[036-045]> The soft glow of her brilliant eyes

[036-046]>

[036-047]> Grows penetrating and holds one with proud authority.

[036-048]>

[036-049]> O beauty Supreme! O brilliant Star

[036-050]>

[036-051]> Shining but for the Son of Heaven!

[036-052]>

[036-053]> From thy glowing soul radiate

[036-054]>

[036-055]> Love, daring, hope, intellect, ambition, power!

[036-056]>

[036-063]6. It has been said by foreigners, that Her Majesty the Empress Dowager obliges the Emperor and Empress to make the prostrations before her on her Birthday as an indignity to them and to show her authority. The truth is, that every son in China kneels before his parents on their birthdays, and should the Emperor fail to do so, the whole of China would be horrified and cry out against his unfilial conduct. Her Majesty is not only the wife of his uncle, the [[emperor-hsien-feng|Emperor Hsien-Feng]], but the sister of his mother, and, more than all else, the Empress Dowager is the Emperor’s adopted mother. The duties of an adopted child to his adopted parents are the same, in China, as to his own parents. In the Viceroy Chang-Chih-Tung’s famous ode to the Emperor, he speaks of this filial piety as one of the Emperor’s greatest qualities: “Who does not admire the filial reverence and piety with which he waits upon his august mother? Setting a brilliant example to all, he inquires early and late after her well-being and watches over her meals in person. Let us now add a new ode, extolling to the skies our Emperor’s fidelity to his Imperial mother.” [↩︎](chapter-23.xhtml#noteref-6)

[036-064]7. Her Majesty said my individuality was not lost in these costumes, and that I was clothed in attire suitable to the Chinese interior. She had now devised a costume for me which was really in harmony with my new environment. Our rough tweeds and somber garments, outlining and defining the figure, looked mesquin and out of place in these great halls. The bright colors and simple lines of the gowns of the Chinese ladies are much more in keeping with their interiors. Her Majesty’s artistic taste had divined this, and she had made several attempts to devise something for me that was in harmony with the Chinese milieu and at the same time comfortable.
