# Legation Quarter

The Legation Quarter was the fortified diplomatic enclave in [[peking|Peking]] that housed the foreign legations during the late Qing period. Established in the aftermath of the [[boxer-rebellion|Boxer Rebellion]] of 1900, the Quarter was a self-contained European settlement within the Chinese capital, with its own walls, guards, and extraterritorial administration.

## History

Prior to the Boxer Rebellion, the foreign legations were scattered across a district of Peking south of the [[imperial-city|Imperial City]]. During the rising of 1900, the legations were besieged for 55 days, an experience that led to the fortification and consolidation of the diplomatic quarter under the terms of the Boxer Protocol of 1901.

## In Carl's Account

The Legation Quarter serves as the starting point for Carl's journey to the [[chinese-court|Chinese Court]]. She departed from the [[american-legation|American Legation]] in the early morning hours for the three-hour drive to the [[summer-palace|Summer Palace]]:

> The day of my first Audience at the Chinese Court, August 5th, we were up betimes at the American Legation, for it takes full three hours to drive out to the Summer Palace from Peking; and punctuality is the etiquette of Oriental as well as of Occidental potentates. Our audience was for half-past ten o'clock, and the portrait of the Empress Dowager was to be begun at eleven.
>
> (001-002)

Carl also references the Legation Quarter in connection with diplomatic social life: the garden parties, receptions, and informal gatherings that constituted the European community's social calendar. The Quarter's [[legation-street|Legation Street]] was the artery connecting the various national compounds.
