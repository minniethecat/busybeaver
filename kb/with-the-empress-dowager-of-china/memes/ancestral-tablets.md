# Ancestral Tablets

Ancestral tablets were sacred wooden plaques, inscribed with the names of deceased ancestors, that formed the focal point of the most solemn rituals at the Qing Court. Katherine Carl witnessed the [[emperor-kwang-hsu|Emperor]] and Empress kneeling before them in the Sacred Hall of the [[summer-palace|Summer Palace]].

## The Sacred Hall

Carl observed the conclusion of the [[emperor-birthday|Emperor's Birthday]] ceremonies, when the Imperial procession "moved through several sunlit courts until it finally came to the entrance of the Sacred Hall, containing the Ancestral tablets" (009-029).

The [[empress-dowager|Empress Dowager]] stopped at the threshold "until His Majesty and the young Empress had passed within, to complete the ceremonies of the day by worshiping and kneeling together before the tablets of their Ancestors" (009-029).

## Ancestor Worship

Ancestor veneration was fundamental to Chinese religious practice and to the legitimacy of the Imperial house. The tablets represented the continuing presence of the ancestors, whose blessings were essential for dynastic prosperity. The Emperor, as head of both the nation and the Imperial family, performed these rites on behalf of the entire realm.
