# Western Palace

The Western Palace was one of the two principal palace compounds within the Imperial precincts of [[peking|Peking]]. As the historical residence of [[empress-dowager|the Empress Dowager Cixi]], it was the effective center of political power in late Qing China.

## Historical Context

The Western Palace derived its name from its position within the Forbidden City complex and its association with the Western Empress Dowager title. Cixi, originally a concubine of the Xianfeng Emperor, became the Western Empress Dowager upon her son's accession as the Tongzhi Emperor in 1861. Carl records that Emperor Kwang-Hsu "reigned over China, of the Dynasty of the 'Great Purity'... His reign began at the age of five years, under the Co-Regency of the Empress of the Eastern and Empress of the Western Palaces. The former died in 1881, and from that time on Her Majesty, the present Empress Dowager, ruled alone as Regent" (008-003).

## The Western Empress Dowager

Carl describes the Empress of the Western Palace — the future Empress Dowager Cixi — as possessing "stronger executive ability" than her co-regent (015-009). When China faced crisis after the death of Emperor Hsien-Feng, "the young Empress of the Western Palace, absolutely unversed in Statecraft... showed wonderful perspicacity and rare judgment in her keen grasp of the situation" (022-009). She "repudiated the anti-foreign party and joined forces with Prince Kung, whose name was then synonymous with Progress in China" (022-009).

## In Carl's Account

Carl occasionally uses the title "Empress of the Western Palace" when referring to Cixi's historical position, noting the distinction between the two Empress Dowagers of the Tongzhi period. The Western Palace was part of the complex of Imperial residences that Carl navigated during her nine-month sojourn at the Court.
