## Definition

The Manchu Banner (八旗, *Bāqí*) was the foundational military and social organization of the Manchu people under the Qing Dynasty, combining military units with hereditary social classification and administrative functions for the ruling Manchu elite. The Banner system divided Manchu society into eight ranked divisions, each identified by a colored flag. (005-002)

## Structure and Hierarchy

The eight Banners were organized in a strict hierarchy of rank and precedence. The Yellow Banner held the highest position, headed by the Emperor of China himself. The White Banner ranked second in precedence, followed by the remaining six banners in descending order. The lower-ranking banners — particularly the Seventh and Eighth Banners — occupied the lowest social tier within the Manchu nobility, though they still held privileged status above the Han Chinese population. (005-015)(030-002)

At the time of the Manchu conquest of China, a fierce struggle for supremacy occurred between the Yellow and White Banner families. The Yellow Banner ultimately prevailed, establishing its primacy within the Qing power structure. (030-002)

## Military Function

Each Banner functioned as a military corps under the command of a General drawn from the Manchu nobility. The position of Lieutenant-General of the Manchu forces was attainable only by members of the highest Manchu nobility, and Banner Corps Generals were typically close relatives of the Imperial family. (005-002)(030-002)

[[manchu-dynasty|Manchu]] Banner quarters were located within the [[imperial-city|Imperial City]] of [[peking|Peking]], near the [[winter-palace|Winter Palace]] at the foot of the [[coal-hill|Coal Hill]]. These quarters housed the guards and soldiers who served the Imperial household. (024-005)

## Social Function

The Banner system also governed the selection of palace servants and maids. Each spring, the daughters of families belonging to the lowest Banners — the Seventh and Eighth — were brought to the Palace to be reviewed by the Head Eunuch and chosen for ten-year terms of service by Her Majesty. Those who served satisfactorily received a marriage dowry and outfit upon completion, enabling advantageous marriages they could not otherwise secure. (005-015)

## Imperial Connections

The [[empress-dowager|Empress Dowager Cixi]] herself belonged to the family of the White Banner, the second most prestigious banner after the Yellow Banner. Her father held the rank of Lieutenant-General of the Manchu forces, a position reserved for the highest Manchu nobility. (030-002)

The young [[empress]], first Lady of the Court after the Empress Dowager, was the daughter of the Duke Chow, General of one of the Manchu Banner Corps and a brother of the reigning Empress Dowager. (005-002)
