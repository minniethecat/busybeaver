# Western Hills

The Western Hills (西山, *Xīshān*) were the low mountain range rising to the west of [[peking|Peking]], forming the natural backdrop to the [[summer-palace|Summer Palace]] and the [[sea-palace|Sea Palace]]. In Katherine Carl's account, they were a constant presence — the distant horizon visible from the palace gardens where she walked and worked.

## In Carl's Account

The Western Hills are mentioned in Carl's description of the landscape surrounding the Imperial palaces. The drive from Peking to the Summer Palace passed through fertile fields "with here and there the somber note of a clump of arborvitae, out of which rose the walls of a temple," and "the distant hills, where lay the Summer Palace, were delicately limned against a soft blue-gray sky" (001-001).

## The Imperial Landscape

The Western Hills were not merely scenic — they were integral to the Imperial landscape. Temples and monasteries dotted their slopes, and the Summer Palace was deliberately sited to take advantage of their beauty. The hills provided a sense of seclusion and natural grandeur that contrasted with the flat terrain of Peking itself.

From a high vantage in the Summer Palace grounds, Carl witnessed the Empress Dowager's proprietary pride in the landscape:

> When we reached our objective point, one of the highest eminences in the grounds, with the whole panorama of the Western Hills spread out beneath us, and the setting sun glowing over all in brilliant splendor, it was a glorious scene. She called me up to her side and made a graceful, sweeping gesture of the hand that said, "This is all mine, but you may share it with me."
>
> (012-011)
