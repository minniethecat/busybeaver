# First Audience with the Empress Dowager

On August 5, 1903, Katherine Carl was presented to the Empress Dowager Cixi at the Summer Palace in "private Audience" -- an unprecedented event that broke with centuries of Chinese Imperial tradition.

## Background

Carl had received Mrs. Conger's invitation in April 1903 while visiting Shanghai (P04-001). After months of waiting for official approval from the Wai-Wai-Pu, she traveled to Peking in late July. "I was cordially received, on my arrival in Peking, by Mr. and Mrs. Conger at the American Legation, and on the fifth of August was presented by Mrs. Conger to Her Majesty the Empress Dowager at the Summer Palace in private Audience" (P04-001). The audience was scheduled "for half-past ten o'clock" (001-002), and Carl was escorted by a "Chinese Guard of Honor sent by the Wai-Wu-Pu (Foreign Office)" (001-004).

## The Audience

Carl approached the audience with considerable anxiety. She "did not know whether I would have one sitting or ten, and no one else seemed to have any more definite information" (P04-001). She feared she was "going into the Palace on trial" -- but the Empress Dowager's gracious reception transformed her apprehension into confidence.

After greeting Mrs. Conger, the Empress Dowager turned to Carl:

> After greeting Mrs. Conger, the Empress Dowager looked toward me, and I advanced with a reverence. She met me halfway and extended her hand with another brilliant smile which quite won me, and I spontaneously raised her dainty fingers to my lips. This was not in the protocol program. It was an involuntary and surprised tribute on my part to her unexpected charm.
>
> (001-008)

## Significance

This audience marked the first time a Western artist was permitted to paint the Empress Dowager's portrait. It was "a great innovation in Chinese customs and a breaking away from long-established tradition" (P04-001), representing a moment of cultural opening in the final decade of the Qing dynasty.
