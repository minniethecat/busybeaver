The Imperial Gourd was a specially cultivated variety of gourd grown within the Palace grounds, known across China for its exceptional perfection. Under the personal supervision of the [[empress-dowager|Empress Dowager Cixi]], these gourds attained their highest state of cultivation and became one of the distinctive artifacts of Palace life during her reign.

## Cultural Significance

The gourd is much esteemed by the Chinese. It is emblematic of Fruitfulness and Prosperity, and was a special favorite of the Empress Dowager. Those cultivated at the Palace, and known all over China as the "Imperial Gourd," have long been famous. (011-012)

> The gourd is much esteemed by the Chinese. It is emblematic of Fruitfulness and Prosperity, and is a special favorite of Her Majesty's. Those cultivated at the Palace, and known all over China as the "Imperial Gourd," have long been famous; but have reached a greater state of perfection than ever before, under the special care and training given them during Her Majesty the Empress Dowager's reign.

> (011-012)

## Cultivation

The gourds were grown on trellises about seven feet high, and the vines were carefully trained so that each of the much-prized fruit might attain its best development and have its proper quota of light and sun. They were of one shape only, with a contracted neck and two equal parts above and below. They came in all sizes, from one to twelve inches, the one-inch size being as perfect as the larger ones. (011-012)

The Empress Dowager took a personal interest in their cultivation. During promenades in the Palace grounds, she would stop at the gourd-arbors to examine the fruit, testing whether they were properly ripe — for they must be pulled at a certain time or they would not dry well. (011-013)

## The Scraping Art

After harvesting, the gourds were scraped to remove the outer skin, a delicate art practiced by the most skilled members of the Court. The Empress Dowager herself was proficient at this craft, using a sharpened piece of bamboo. Her principal Lady-in-waiting and the Chief Eunuch [[li-lien-ying|Li Lien Ying]] were also recognized as experts. (011-015)
