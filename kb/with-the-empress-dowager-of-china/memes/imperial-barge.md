The Imperial Barge was the [[empress-dowager|Empress Dowager]]'s personal watercraft on the lake at the [[summer-palace|Summer Palace]], forming the centerpiece of the Palace fleet that lay moored beneath the white marble terrace. Described as "picturesque" and traditional, it was the primary vessel for the Imperial party's daily lake excursions until gradually supplemented by the modern [[steam-launch]]. (013-002)

## Physical Description

The barge featured a raised platform amidships, upon which sat Her Majesty's yellow, throne-like chair — the color yellow being sacred to Their Majesties and never used by anyone else. (002-013) The young [[empress|Empress]], Princesses, and [[lady-of-the-court|Court Ladies]] took their places on cushions placed upon the carpeted floor of the raised platform, their positions "decreed by centuries-old tradition." (002-013)

The barge was not self-propelled but was attached by great yellow ropes to two boats, each manned by twenty-four rowers, which towed it across the lake. (002-017) Two additional rowers stood on the barge itself with long oars to guide it, but the boatmen of the towing vessels did the actual work of propulsion. Critically, all the Palace boatmen stood to their oars — they could not sit in the presence of Her Majesty, "even though not upon the Imperial barge." (002-017)

## Court Etiquette on the Barge

The Imperial Barge was governed by strict hierarchies. Only eunuchs of the highest rank — Her Majesty's personal attendants — went on the barge with her. They stood at the back of the Empress Dowager's chair, carrying her extra wraps, bonbons, cigarettes, and water-pipes. (002-017)

The barge was the one place where the formalities of seating were partially relaxed: "it is only on the barge that the Empress and Ladies sit in the presence of the Empress Dowager without being invited by her to do so." (002-017) When landing, however, protocol was strictly observed — the Princesses and Ladies disembarked first and stood to receive the Empress Dowager when she came ashore. (002-019)

A number of flat boats followed the Imperial barge carrying the "army of eunuchs" that made up the Imperial train. One boat was dedicated to portable stoves and all the necessary arrangements for making tea, which was "taken so frequently by Her Majesty and the Ladies, it may be called for at any time." (002-018)

## Role in Palace Life

### Daily Excursions

The barge was central to the rhythm of life at the Summer Palace. After Her Majesty's afternoon sitting for her portrait, she would frequently "order the boats" and the Imperial party would proceed to the marble terrace. The barge, drawn by its two towing boats, "glided as gently as a swan over the still waters of the lake." (004-009) The excursions were accompanied by eunuchs singing — "the minor chords of a curious air mingled their rhythm with the soft swish of the water" — while the [[western-hills|Western Hills]] formed an ever-changing backdrop of "soft, warm violet" and "clear, deep blue, as if hewn out of lapis lazuli." (004-009)

[[katherine-carl|Katharine Carl]], the author and portrait painter, frequently accompanied the Imperial party on these excursions, seated at Her Majesty's right hand while the young Empress sat on her left. (002-013)

### Festival Night Processions

During festivals such as the [[festival-of-the-harvest-moon|Harvest Moon]], the barge took on a spectacular illuminated character. After the ceremonies, the lantern-lit procession followed Their Majesties to the banks of the lake, "where the whole Palace fleet, brilliantly illuminated, lay moored beneath the marble terrace." (018-008) Eunuchs holding gleaming lanterns knelt along the marble steps leading into the water. On two of the boats, at either side of the Imperial barge, the eunuchs held their lanterns to form the characters for "Peace" and "Prosperity." (018-008) The waters of the lake glowed with the reflections of myriad lanterns, while the great arc-lights on the Imperial landing-place sent their reflections "shimmering, in long, wavy lines, far out into the lake." (018-008)

On other occasions, the Empress Dowager's barge did not lead the Palace fleet. During the [[garden-party-summer-palace|garden party at the Summer Palace]], three great houseboats each carried a yellow-covered seat for Her Majesty, which — though she never used them — could never be occupied by anyone else. (019-012)

### The Emperor's Presence

His Majesty the [[emperor-kwang-hsu|Emperor Kwang-Hsu]] went out in the Imperial barge on festival days, walking with Her Majesty and the Ladies through the Palace promenades. Carl observed, however, that "he did not seem to enjoy them, and when they were finished he would return with his own attendants to his own Palace." (008-017) While he assisted Her Majesty when she entertained Foreign Representatives, "one, who knew him, could plainly see that he was bored by these Audiences" and would slip away at the first opportunity — not from objection to the foreigners, but because "these state functions were not to his taste." (008-017)

## Comparison with the Steam-Launch

When shorter and cooler days came and the lotus were no longer in bloom, the Empress Dowager ordered the steam-launch for the lake promenades in place of the traditional barge. She seemed to "like its swift and noisy progress as much as she had before enjoyed the softly gliding motion of the barge." (013-002) The barge remained her preference, however, when the days were long, the air soft, and "the bosom of the lake engirdled with its chain of blooming lotus." (013-002)

The Empress Dowager's flexibility in adopting the steam-launch while retaining affection for the barge revealed an important aspect of her character. Carl observed that she was "artistic and conservative enough to like the old-fashioned barge; but she is also intelligent enough to appreciate the advantages of other modes of locomotion, and has no prejudices; in fact, she rather likes trying new things." (013-002)

## The Fleet at Special Occasions

During the [[european-circus-palace|European circus at the Palace]], the lake came alive with an extravagant display of watercraft. "The lake was gay with beautiful barges, great houseboats, and numbers of flat boats for the eunuchs," all "picturesquely decorated with flying banners, pennants, and tasseled wands." (021-005) Two steam-launches, "puffing away, gave an air of modernity to this most Oriental fleet," while the Empress Dowager and the Emperor descended the marble steps to a gaily decked launch flying the Imperial flag to proceed across the lake. (021-005)
