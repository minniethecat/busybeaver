# Marble Boat

The Marble Boat (石舫, *Shí Fǎng*) was one of the most distinctive landmarks of the [[summer-palace|Summer Palace]] — a lakeside pavilion constructed entirely of white marble in the form of a two-story paddle steamer. It stood immobile at the water's edge, a whimsical architectural folly that embodied the Empress Dowager's taste for the novel and the picturesque.

## Architecture

The structure was built in a hybrid style combining Chinese roof forms with Western steamboat details — paddle wheels carved in marble, a superstructure with European-style window openings, and traditional Chinese roof ornaments. It was not a functioning boat but a stationary pleasure pavilion where the Court could enjoy the lake views in comfort.

## In Carl's Account


> She gave me a short sitting after her nap this second day and then ordered the boats for a row on the lakes. Attended by the young Empress and Princesses, and with the usual train of attendants and eunuchs, we went out into the court of the Throne-room, passed through a small pavilion opening directly upon the beautiful white marble terrace, with its balustrade of lotus design, beneath which lay the fleet of boats that the Empress Dowager maintains on the lovely lake.
>
> (002-013)
Carl does not describe the Marble Boat directly in her memoir, but it was one of the most recognizable landmarks of the Summer Palace grounds where she resided. The structure was a favorite spot of the [[empress-dowager|Empress Dowager Cixi]], who had it refurbished with funds allocated for the Imperial Navy — a decision that became a symbol of her prioritizing personal pleasure over national defense.

## Historical Legacy

The Marble Boat survived the destruction of the Summer Palace by foreign troops in 1860 and again in 1900, its marble construction impervious to fire. It remains one of the most photographed sites in the restored Summer Palace park today.
