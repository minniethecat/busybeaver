# Grain-bearing Canal

The Grain-bearing Canal was a waterway in Peking, crossed by a stone bridge on the approach to the Winter Palace. Its name reflects its function in transporting grain to the imperial capital (024-005).

## Location and Context

The canal lay along the route from the American Legation to the Winter Palace. Carl describes "crossing the stone bridge that spans 'the Grain-bearing Canal'" shortly before arriving at the palace gate, which stood at the foot of Coal Hill, near the quarters of the Manchu bannermen (024-005).

## Description

The canal is part of the extensive network of waterways that characterized Peking's landscape. Carl noted on her arrival that the city was visible as "a place with its hills, valleys, canals, and lakes" (001-004). Canals served both practical and aesthetic functions in imperial Peking. The canal from Peking fed the lakes of the Sea Palace, and other canals ran past the American Legation compound and the walls of the Imperial City (017-007) (025-002) (025-003).

## In the Narrative

The Grain-bearing Canal appears in the narrative as a landmark on the journey from the Legation Quarter to the Winter Palace. The stone bridge crossing it was a waypoint along the route, described in Carl's account of her first visit to Her Majesty's palace (024-005).

Carl described the approach to the Winter Palace:

> After passing through one of the great gates, in the wall surrounding the Imperial City, and crossing the stone bridge that spans "the Grain-bearing Canal," we soon came in sight of the splendid walls and lofty gates of the Palace enclosure.
>
> (024-005)
