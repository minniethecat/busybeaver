# Prince Adalbert of Prussia

Prince Adalbert of Prussia (1884–1948) was a member of the German Imperial family, the third son of Kaiser Wilhelm II. During his world tour as a young naval officer, he visited [[peking|Peking]] and was received in a special audience by the [[empress-dowager|Empress Dowager]] and the [[emperor-kwang-hsu|Emperor]].

## Visit to Peking

Adalbert's visit occurred during a period of active German naval and colonial expansion under Wilhelm II. The Prince's reception at the Chinese Court was a diplomatic event of some significance, reflecting Germany's interest in maintaining and extending its influence in China following the Boxer Rebellion.

## In Carl's Account

Carl describes the Prince's audience in the context of the Court's evolving protocol for foreign visitors: "When the young Prince Adalbert of Prussia was received in special Audience by Their Majesties, on his visit to Peking, he was accompanied not only by the German Minister and his suite, but by all the members of the Foreign Legations in Peking" (031-022).

The presence of the entire diplomatic corps at the audience—unusual for a private visit—suggests the significance attached to the occasion. The young Prince's reception demonstrated the Court's willingness to honor foreign royalty with exceptional ceremony, part of the broader pattern of diplomatic engagement that characterized the post-Boxer period.

## Significance

Prince Adalbert's audience represents the internationalization of the late Qing Court. The presence of European royalty in the Imperial audience hall—unthinkable a generation earlier—testified to the transformation of China's foreign relations and the Empress Dowager's personal role in that transformation.

> When the young Prince Adalbert of Prussia was received in special Audience by Their Majesties, on his visit to Peking, he was accompanied not only by the German Minister and his staff, but by a number of officers as his personal escort. This made an unusually large number of presentations necessary.
