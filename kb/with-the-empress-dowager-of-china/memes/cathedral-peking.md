# First Catholic Cathedral of Peking

The first Catholic Cathedral of Peking was built on land granted to the Catholic mission by the Chinese Emperor. Its towers, however, overlooked the Palace grounds, and the Cathedral was subsequently purchased by the Emperor and incorporated within the walls of the [[sea-palace|Sea Palace]] (010-020).

## Location and Context

The Cathedral is situated within the walls of the Sea Palace, visible from every part of the grounds of the two Peking Palaces. Its location within the Imperial precinct was the result of an unusual transaction: after the Cathedral's completion, its towers were found to overlook the Palace grounds, which was deemed unacceptable. The Emperor purchased the Cathedral and provided land further on for a new one to be built (010-020).

## Description

The Cathedral's towers are a prominent feature of the Sea Palace landscape. Katherine Carl observed them from the Marble Bridge, describing how "the towers of the old Cathedral were strongly silhouetted" against the golden glow of the sunset sky, their arches turning a "beautiful, deep violet hue" in the evening light (010-022).

The presence of a Christian church within the precincts of an Oriental potentate's palace struck Carl as anomalous:

> It seems a strange anomaly to see this Christian Church within the Precincts of the Palace of an Oriental potentate, who is one of the representatives on earth of the "Great Buddha." (010-020)

## Role in Court Life

The Cathedral did not serve an active religious function for the Court. Rather, it stood as a silent architectural presence within the Palace grounds, a consequence of the extraterritorial concessions made to foreign powers. The [[empress-dowager|Empress Dowager Cixi]] and the Court passed near it during their movements between the Sea Palace and the [[summer-palace|Summer Palace]], but it played no role in Court ritual or ceremony (010-020).

## In the Narrative

The Cathedral appears in Chapter 10 during Carl's day-trip to the Sea Palace. After visiting the temple on the heights, Carl and her party descended and crossed the [[marble-bridge|Marble Bridge]]. From the bridge, they saw the Cathedral's towers nearby. Carl later reflected on the incongruity of its presence as the sun set and the towers stood silhouetted against the brilliant sky (010-022).

## Historical Significance

The first Catholic Cathedral of Peking represents the complex history of Christian missionary activity in late imperial China. The fact that it was built on Imperial land, then repurchased by the Emperor due to its intrusive sightlines, illustrates the tensions between Chinese sovereignty and foreign religious presence. After the Cathedral was acquired by the Throne, the mission received land elsewhere to build a replacement, and the original structure became a curious landmark within the Imperial enclosure—a Christian church in the heart of the Buddhist and Confucian Court (010-020).
