# Official Beads

Official beads (朝珠, *cháozhū*) were the ceremonial necklaces that formed an essential part of Qing Court dress, worn by Princes, high Officials, and their wives on all formal occasions. Katherine Carl documented their use during her residency at the Palace.

## In Court Costume

Carl observed the official beads as part of the elaborate [[court-costume|Court costume]] system: "the official beads that are always worn in Court dress by Princes and Officials and their wives" (009-006). The beads hung in a prescribed manner, and their arrangement was subject to the scrutiny of the [[empress-dowager|Empress Dowager]].

## Distinguished Absence

Significantly, Carl noted that "the Emperor and Empress Dowager are the only members of the Court who wear neither the [[double-dragon|Double Dragon]] on their Court dress, nor the official beads" (009-006). This absence was itself a marker of supreme rank — Their Majesties were above the symbols of rank that defined everyone else.

## Enforcement of Detail

The Empress Dowager was meticulous about the proper wearing of official beads. Carl described how she would scrutinize the Ladies during ceremonial occasions, "calling their attention to the way their official beads hung and signaling any little deviation from traditional forms that she noticed in their attire" (009-005).

## Original Text

> After this she turned to the Ladies and, with a quick glance, took in all the details of their Court costumes, calling their attention to the way their official beads hung and signaling any little deviation from traditional forms that she noticed in their attire. She was extremely rigid as to all the details of Court dress.
>
> (009-005)
