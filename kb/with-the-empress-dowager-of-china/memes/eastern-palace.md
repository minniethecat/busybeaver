# Eastern Palace

The Eastern Palace was one of the two principal palace compounds within the Imperial precincts of [[peking|Peking]], paired with the [[western-palace|Western Palace]]. Together, these two palaces formed the inner core of the Imperial residence, housing the principal consorts and serving as centers of court life.

## Historical Context

In Qing court tradition, the Eastern Palace was the residence of the senior Empress Dowager. During the Tongzhi reign, the Eastern Empress Dowager Ci'an (慈安) occupied this palace, while [[empress-dowager|the Empress Dowager Cixi]], then the Western Empress Dowager, resided in the Western Palace. After Ci'an's death in 1881, Cixi became the sole Empress Dowager and exercised authority over both palaces. Carl notes that Emperor Kwang-Hsu's "reign began at the age of five years, under the Co-Regency of the Empress of the Eastern and Empress of the Western Palaces" (008-003).

## The Eastern Empress Dowager

The Empress of the Eastern Palace, Ci'an, was known as the "Literary Empress" for her devotion to scholarly pursuits (015-009). Carl records that "all State affairs were left to the stronger executive ability of the Empress of the Western Palace; while she of the Eastern Palace gave herself up to literary pursuits and led the life of a student" (015-009). She was "a woman of such fine literary ability that she, herself, sometimes examined the essays of the aspirants for the highest literary honors in the University of Peking" (015-009).

## In Carl's Account

Carl's memoir references the palace geography of the Imperial precincts, distinguishing between the various compounds that housed the Court. The Empress Dowager moved between palaces according to season: the [[summer-palace|Summer Palace]] for warm months, the [[sea-palace|Sea Palace]] for transitional periods, and the [[winter-palace|Winter Palace]] within the Imperial City for the coldest months. The Eastern Palace was part of this larger complex of Imperial residences.

## Original Text

> When Her Majesty the Empress Dowager was Empress of the Western Palace, Co-Regent with the Empress of the Eastern Palace, who died in 1881, the Empress of the Eastern Palace was known as the "Literary Empress." All State affairs were left to the stronger executive ability of the Empress of the Western Palace; while she of the Eastern Palace gave herself up to literary pursuits and led the life of a student. She was a woman of such fine literary ability that she, herself, sometimes examined the essays of the aspirants for the highest literary honors in the University of Peking. She was also a writer of distinction.
>
> (015-002)
