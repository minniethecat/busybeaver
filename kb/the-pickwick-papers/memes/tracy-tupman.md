## Overview

Tracy Tupman is, by his own frank admission, the "susceptible" member of [[pickwick-club|the Pickwick Club]]: a middle-aged bachelor whose susceptibility to female charms is matched only by his invariable failure to secure them. (002-068) With a figure that "in his younger days, had presented all the appearance of a pin, but now presented that of a very respectable barrel," Tupman is the novel's most genial portrait of romantic self-delusion. He pursues love with the same dogged enthusiasm with which [[Mr. Pickwick]] pursues knowledge, and with roughly the same rate of empirical success.

## Role in the Novel

Tupman is the [[pickwick-club|Pickwickians]]' resident romantic, the member whose narrative function is to fall in love and be disappointed — a cycle he repeats with admirable resilience. His principal adventure is his courtship of Rachael [[mr-wardle|Wardle]] at [[Manor Farm]], a romance conducted in bowers and over picnic hampers, which collapses spectacularly when he is discovered embracing her by [[joe-the-fat-boy|the fat boy]]. (002-131) Tupman's romantic misadventures are not merely comic relief: they enact in miniature the novel's larger theme of the gap between feeling and circumstance, the way the world conspires against even the most sincere intentions. He is also, crucially, a loyal friend — his presence at [[Mr. Pickwick]]'s side during the [[Fleet Prison]] episode demonstrates that his sentimentality extends beyond the romantic to the genuinely humane. (016-080)

## Relationships

- **[[Mr. Pickwick]]** — Tupman's leader and friend. He follows [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] with unswerving loyalty, even when that loyalty leads him into courtrooms and debtors' prisons. (003-022, 016-080)
- **[[Augustus Snodgrass]]** — Tupman's frequent companion in sentiment and seating arrangements. The two bachelor [[pickwick-club|Pickwickians]] form a kind of comic chorus to the novel's principal action. (007-010, 007-049)
- **[[Nathaniel Winkle]]** — The third [[gentleman|Pickwickian]], with whom Tupman shares the bond of being perpetually out of his depth. (002-050)
