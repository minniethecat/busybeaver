## Overview

Mr. Simmery, also referred to as Frank Simmery, Esquire, appears in a single extended scene in Chapter 27 of _The [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] Papers_. He is a city man who lounges about the counting-house of [[mr-flasher|Wilkins Flasher]], stockbroker, killing flies with a ruler and casually wagering on whether a ruined colleague named Boffer will take his own life. Dickens uses Simmery — and his mirror-image [[mr-flasher|Flasher]] — to satirize the moral vacancy of the financial district, where human suffering is reduced to betting odds and recorded in "little books with gold pencil-cases" (027-097).

## Role in the Novel

Mr. Simmery functions as a moral exhibit in the novel's gallery of [[london|London]] types. His role is brief but sharply pointed: he demonstrates, through his languid cruelty, the deadening of sympathy that Dickens associated with speculative finance. He and [[mr-flasher|Wilkins Flasher]] together form a diptych of callous city gentlemen, their identical accessories (gold pencil-cases, open waistcoats, large guard chains) suggesting an interchangeable, hollow affluence. The scene serves as a darkly comic interlude during the financial machinations surrounding [[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]]'s settlement.

## Key Scenes

**The Wager on Boffer (Chapter 27)**
Mr. Simmery is discovered in the office of [[mr-flasher|Wilkins Flasher]], Esquire, where he is lounging over a desk "killing flies with a ruler" (027-090). The two men discuss a former colleague:

> "I see there's a notice up this morning about Boffer," observed Mr. Simmery. "Poor devil, he's expelled the house!" (027-092)

What follows is a chilling display of moral indifference. [[mr-flasher|Flasher]] bets ten guineas to five that Boffer will cut his throat; Simmery counters by narrowing the timeframe:

> "Confound it, no;" rejoined Mr. Simmery, stopping for an instant to smash a fly with the ruler. "Say a week." (027-099)

They eventually split the difference at ten days (027-101). The wager is formalized in the "little books": if Boffer kills himself within ten days, Simmery pays [[mr-flasher|Flasher]] five guineas; if he survives, [[mr-flasher|Flasher]] pays Simmery ten (027-102). The casual cruelty of the bet — conducted between fly-swatting and pencil-twirling — is underscored by [[mr-flasher|Flasher]]'s valedictory: "Capital dinners he gave" (027-107).
