## Overview

Mr. Tom Roker is the gentleman turnkey who first conducts [[Mr. Pickwick]] into [[debtors-prison|the Fleet]] Prison, and who, in a few brilliantly economical pages, becomes the novel's most complete embodiment of institutionalised indifference. (013-002) Fat, cheerful, and entirely at home in the prison's grotesque economy, Roker treats [[debtors-prison|the Fleet]] as a hotel, its inmates as guests, and its degradations as amenities. His guided tour of the prison is one of the great set-pieces of Dickens's early career: a tour conducted with the professional pride of a concierge showing off a particularly well-appointed establishment.

## Role in the Novel

Roker's role is that of the institutional gatekeeper — the man who translates the prison's bewildering rules into the language of commerce. He explains the system of "chummage" (the fee paid by new prisoners to share a room), the hierarchy of accommodation from the "poor side" to the comparatively comfortable quarters of gentlemen who can pay, and the casual brutality of a system in which "One of 'em takes his twelve turns of the yard of a night, and another takes his dose of opium." (013-007–027) Roker is not cruel — he is something more troubling: a man for whom the prison's degradations are simply facts of life, as unremarkable as the weather. His practical accommodation of [[Mr. Pickwick]], arranging for him to share a room with a "chummage ticket," demonstrates both the limited humanity of the system and its thoroughgoing corruption. (014-034–038)

## Relationships

- **[[Mr. Pickwick]]** — Roker's most distinguished temporary resident. Their relationship is purely transactional, yet Roker's cheerful professionalism is, in its way, one of the few comforts [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] finds in [[debtors-prison|the Fleet]]. (013-002)
- **The [[fleet-prison|Fleet Prison]] inmates** — Roker relates to the prisoners with the genial indifference of a man who has seen every variety of human misery and classified it by its ability to pay rent. (013-007)
- **[[Fleet Prison]]** — Roker is the human face of the institution, embodying its strange blend of commerce, coercion, and routine.
