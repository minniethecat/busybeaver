## Overview

The Honourable Mr. Crushton is a minor figure in the [[bath|Bath]] chapters of _The [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] Papers_, where he appears as a satellite of the aristocracy — specifically, as the "bosom friend" of [[lord-mutanhed|Lord Mutanhed]]. (007-126) Dickens describes him with one memorable physical detail: a "large moustache" (007-126) which, together with his obsequious manner, brands him as a comic type of the hanger-on. He drifts through the Pump Room and the card-rooms of [[bath|Bath]] in [[lord-mutanhed|Lord Mutanhed]]'s wake, his conversation consisting largely of agreeable monosyllables and solicitous inquiries about his patron's refreshment.

## Role in the Novel

Mr. Crushton's function in the novel is to populate the social landscape of [[bath|Bath]] — the fashionable spa town where the [[pickwick-club|Pickwickians]] encounter the rituals of upper-class sociability. He is less a character than a specimen: the "Honourable" prefix to his name and his attachment to [[lord-mutanhed|Lord Mutanhed]] serve to illustrate the hierarchies and dependencies of Regency high life. His obsequiousness toward his social superior — "through that of the obsequious Mr. Crushton, [[lord-mutanhed|Lord Mutanhed]] walked away" (007-135) — is Dickens's shorthand for an entire class of aristocratic attendants.

## Key Scenes

**The Pump Room Introduction (Chapter 7)**
Crushton is introduced in a single swift gesture by [[angelo-cyrus-bantam|Angelo Cyrus Bantam]], the Master of Ceremonies:

> "That gentleman with the large moustache, is the Honourable Mr. Crushton, his bosom friend. How do you do?" (007-126)

The reply is a single word: "Tipsy," assented the Honourable Mr. Crushton. (007-129) — responding to [[angelo-cyrus-bantam|Bantam]]'s question about how someone is "this morning."

**Attending [[lord-mutanhed|Lord Mutanhed]]**
Crushton's principal activity is attending to his patron. After a short pause, "the Honourable Mr. Crushton" inquires: "Shall I try a quarter of an hour's sparklimation?" (007-130) — a comic periphrasis for some fashionable restorative. Moments later, he concurs that something is "complete" (007-133), and then — through him — "[[lord-mutanhed|Lord Mutanhed]] walked away." (007-135) Crushton appears again the following day among the party that includes "the [[lady-snuphanuph|Dowager Lady Snuphanuph]], [[colonel-wugsby|Mrs. Colonel Wugsby]], and all the great people of [[bath|Bath]]." (008-004)
