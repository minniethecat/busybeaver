## Overview

Dodson and Fogg, of Freeman's Court, Cornhill, in [[london|the City]] of [[London]], are the solicitors who represent [[mrs-bardell|Mrs. Bardell]] in her breach-of-promise suit against [[Mr. Pickwick]]. They are introduced in Chapter III when their clerk, [[mr-jackson|Mr. Jackson]], delivers the fateful summons to Goswell Street. (003-005) The firm embodies Dickens's earliest sustained satire of the legal profession: its partners are "capital fellows" whose "excellent ideas of effect" consist in manipulating vulnerable clients, inflating costs, and extracting fees from both sides of a lawsuit. Their role in the [[Bardell v. Pickwick Trial]] and its aftermath makes them the novel's principal antagonists — not villains of melodrama, but petitfogging exploiters of legal machinery.

## Nature and Purpose

Dodson and Fogg are solicitors — the branch of the English legal profession that dealt directly with clients, as opposed to barristers (like [[serjeant-buzfuz|Serjeant Buzfuz]]) who appeared in court. As solicitors, they controlled the initiation and management of lawsuits. Their business model, as depicted by Dickens, depends on speculative litigation: taking cases on a contingent-fee basis (no win, no fee), inflating the costs bill, and recovering their expenses from whichever party proves solvent. This model is explicitly condemned by [[Mr. Pickwick]]:

> "It has gone into the pockets of Dodson and Fogg. That is my deliberate and irreversible determination." (003-107)

The firm operates from Freeman's Court, Cornhill, in the legal quarter of [[london|the City]] of [[London]]. (003-005) Their clerk, [[mr-jackson|Mr. Jackson]], serves as process-server and intermediary, delivering summonses with an air of insolent familiarity.

| Poetic Justice | 006-242 | [[mrs-bardell|Mrs. Bardell]] is herself arrested for costs owed to Dodson and Fogg |

The firm's arc ends in poetic justice: having manipulated [[mrs-bardell|Mrs. Bardell]] into the lawsuit, they ultimately arrest her for non-payment of their own costs when [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] refuses to pay the damages. The solicitors are left with nothing — a conclusion that satisfies comic justice while leaving the systemic critique intact.
