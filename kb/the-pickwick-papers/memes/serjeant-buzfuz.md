## Overview

Serjeant Buzfuz is the barrister engaged by [[mrs-bardell|Mrs. Bardell]]'s solicitors, [[dodson-and-fogg|Dodson and Fogg]], to prosecute her claim against [[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]] for [[breach-of-promise|breach of promise of marriage]]. His opening address to the jury is a masterpiece of forensic theatre: a dazzling edifice of insinuation, rhetorical exaggeration, and sentimental manipulation that transforms a trivial misunderstanding into a narrative of "premeditated, cold-blooded villainy." (006-018) Dickens's portrait of Buzfuz is at once a savage satire of the legal profession and an acknowledgement of the barrister's prodigious skill — Buzfuz may be a charlatan, but he is a supremely effective one.

## Role in the Novel

Buzfuz appears only in the trial scenes of the novel's middle chapters but exerts an influence disproportionate to his page-time. As the principal antagonist of the [[bardell-v-pickwick-trial|Bardell v. Pickwick trial]], he is the instrument by which [[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]] is unjustly convicted and ordered to pay damages of seven hundred and fifty pounds — a judgment that ultimately leads to [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]]'s principled imprisonment in the [[fleet-prison|Fleet Prison]]. Buzfuz personifies the adversarial excess that Dickens saw as the central defect of the common-law system: a mechanism in which rhetorical skill, not truth, determines outcomes. (006-053–073)

## Relationships

- **[[Mrs. Bardell]]** — Buzfuz's client, whom he represents with forensic passion, transforming her domestic disappointment into a narrative of grievous wrong. He refers to her as "the plaintiff" and treats her tears as evidentiary exhibits. (006-043)
- **[[Mr. Pickwick]]** — The defendant and the object of Buzfuz's rhetorical assault. Buzfuz looks through [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] "as if he did not know him" while simultaneously subjecting him to the most devastating character assassination of the novel. (006-066)
- **[[Mr. Perker]]** — Buzfuz's professional adversary, the solicitor representing [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]], who identifies Buzfuz with grim admiration. (006-017)
- **[[Mrs. Cluppins]]** — A witness for the plaintiff whom Buzfuz examines with theatrical solemnity, extracting corroborating testimony. (006-073)
