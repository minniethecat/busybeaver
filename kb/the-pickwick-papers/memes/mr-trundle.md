## Overview

Mr. Trundle is a peripheral but recurring figure in the [[mr-wardle|Wardle]] family circle, introduced early in the novel at the [[manor-farm|Manor Farm]] [[christmas|Christmas]] festivities and reappearing at key domestic milestones. He marries Isabella ([[arabella-allen|Bella]]) [[mr-wardle|Wardle]], daughter of old [[Mr. Wardle]], and by the novel's close has become one of the young fathers who petition [[Mr. Pickwick]] to stand as godfather. Though he never emerges as a fully drawn character, his presence helps populate the novel's domestic world and anchors the [[mr-wardle|Wardle]] family's expansion across the narrative arc.

## Role in the Novel

Mr. Trundle functions as part of the warm, bustling domestic backdrop of the [[mr-wardle|Wardle]] household. His first appearance, at the [[christmas|Christmas]] skating party, establishes him as one of the young men of the circle — a practical sort who owns "a couple of pair" of skates (002-067). His marriage to [[arabella-allen|Bella]] [[mr-wardle|Wardle]] is mentioned as an established fact by the time old [[mr-wardle|Wardle]] discusses [[Arabella Allen]]'s engagement (026-046). In the final chapters, he and his wife are drawn into the elaborate preparations for [[Emily Wardle]]'s wedding; [[mrs-trundle|Mrs. Trundle]], "being in a delicate state of health," receives the news through her husband rather than directly (029-017). The novel's last pages record that Mr. Trundle, along with [[Augustus Snodgrass]] and [[Mr. Winkle]], repeatedly applies to [[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]] to act as godfather to their offspring — a duty [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] eventually takes up "as a matter of course" (029-038).

## Key Scenes

**The Skating Party at [[manor-farm|Manor Farm]]** (002-067): When [[Mr. Winkle]] claims he would be happy to skate but has no skates, it is Trundle who provides the solution: "Trundle had a couple of pair, and [[joe-the-fat-boy|the fat boy]] announced that there were half a dozen more down-stairs." This brief mention establishes him as a practical and good-natured member of the [[mr-wardle|Wardle]] party.

**[[arabella-allen|Bella]]'s Marriage Discussion** (026-046): [[mr-wardle|Old Wardle]], explaining the family dynamics to [[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]] during the [[arabella-allen|Arabella Allen]] affair, casually refers to "my daughter [[arabella-allen|Bella]] — [[arabella-allen|Bella]], who married young Trundle, you know" — indicating that Trundle has been quietly absorbed into the family offstage, between the novel's major narrative blocks.

**The Wedding Preparations** (029-017): [[mrs-trundle|Mrs. Trundle]] must be informed of the "mighty preparations" for [[emily-wardle|Emily Wardle]]'s wedding in [[london|London]]. Because she is in a delicate condition, the news is relayed through Mr. Trundle "lest the news should be too much for her." Rather than being overcome, [[mrs-trundle|Mrs. Trundle]] promptly orders a new cap and black satin gown and resolves to attend — a characteristically Dickensian comic touch in which the husband's protective caution proves entirely unnecessary. The Trundles duly appear among the wedding party at Dulwich Church.

**Godfather Duties** (029-038): In the novel's closing summary, Mr. Trundle is listed alongside [[augustus-snodgrass|Snodgrass]] and [[nathaniel-winkle|Winkle]] as one of the young fathers who repeatedly apply to [[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]] to stand as godfather to their children — a detail that suggests the continued flourishing of the [[mr-wardle|Wardle]] family circle and [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]]'s place at its centre.

## Relationships

- **[[Mr. Wardle]]** — Father-in-law; the jovial owner of [[manor-farm|Manor Farm]], [[dingley-dell|Dingley Dell]]
- **[[Emily Wardle]]** — Sister-in-law; her marriage to [[augustus-snodgrass|Mr. Snodgrass]] occasions the Trundles' trip to [[london|London]]
- **[[Mr. Pickwick]]** — The central figure of the novel, whom Trundle repeatedly asks to serve as godfather
- **[[Augustus Snodgrass]]** — Fellow petitioner for [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]]'s godfather services
- **[[Mr. Winkle]]** — Fellow petitioner; Trundle supplied him with skates at the [[manor-farm|Manor Farm]] party
