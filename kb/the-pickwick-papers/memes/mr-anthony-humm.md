## Overview

Mr. Anthony Humm is a minor comic character in _The [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] Papers_ who appears in Chapter 5 as the president of the [[brick-lane-temperance-association|United Grand Junction Ebenezer Temperance Association]]. A "converted fireman" who has exchanged extinguishing literal fires for extinguishing the metaphorical fires of intemperance, Humm presides over a temperance meeting attended by [[Mr. Pickwick]] and his companions. His character allows Dickens to satirize the moralistic excesses of the temperance movement — particularly its self-righteous rhetoric and its tendency to substitute one form of excess (oratory) for another (drink).

## Role in the Novel

Humm's appearance is confined to a single episode set in Brick Lane, Whitechapel, where the [[pickwick-club|Pickwickians]] attend a temperance meeting. As president of the association, Humm chairs the proceedings, delivers moralistic pronouncements, and leads the assembly in temperance songs — all while [[Mr. Stiggins]], the red-nosed "deputy shepherd," provides a hypocritical counterpoint. The scene is one of Dickens's earliest extended satires on organized moral reform in the novel.

## Key Scenes

**The Temperance Meeting at Brick Lane (Chapter 5)**

Mr. Anthony Humm is introduced as "the straight-walking Mr. Anthony Humm, a converted fireman, now a schoolmaster, and occasionally an itinerant preacher." (005-116) He is immediately nominated to the chair:

> "I move our excellent brother, Mr. Anthony Humm, into the chair." (005-124)

The motion being carried, Humm assumes the presidency. He later puts forward a motion for the assembly to "drink" tea rather than stronger beverages (005-133), and when the tea arrives, he leads the company in song. The narrative describes how, amid the maidens present, "he (Mr. Anthony Humm), however unworthily, was 'fired' with a high and noble purpose" (005-139) — a punning reference to his former profession as a fireman.

The song is delivered in the traditional temperance-meeting style, with Humm giving out the lines two at a time for the congregation to repeat. (005-143) When [[Mr. Stiggins]] appears inattentive, Humm whispers to him:

> "Brother Stiggins?" whispered Mr. Anthony Humm. (005-151)

[[mr-stiggins|Stiggins]]'s terse reply causes Humm to retreat:

> "Oh, very well," rejoined Mr. Anthony Humm, retreating a few paces. (005-153)

This brief exchange reveals the uneasy hierarchy between the two temperance leaders — Humm the institutional president, [[mr-stiggins|Stiggins]] the charismatic "deputy shepherd" whose moral authority (or lack thereof) Humm dare not challenge.
