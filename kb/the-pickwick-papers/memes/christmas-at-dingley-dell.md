## Overview

[[christmas|Christmas]] at [[Dingley Dell|Dingley Dell]] ([[manor-farm|Manor Farm]]) is one of the most vividly rendered episodes in *The [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] Papers*. [[mr-wardle|Mr. Wardle]] hosts the [[pickwick-club|Pickwickians]] for a country [[christmas|Christmas]] that includes skating on the frozen pond, substantial feasting, and the first appearance of [[arabella-allen|Arabella Allen]] — whose romantic subplots with [[Nathaniel Winkle|Mr. Winkle]] and [[Augustus Snodgrass|Mr. Snodgrass]] will thread through the remainder of the novel. (002-003)

The [[christmas|Christmas]] chapters establish the novel's pattern of alternating [[london|London]] legal entanglements with rural festivities, and they introduce the warmth of the [[mr-wardle|Wardle]] household as a counterweight to the cold formality of the [[Bardell v. Pickwick Trial|courtroom]].

> "Still frosty?" — Mr. Pickwick, Christmas morning (002-003)

The skating sequence is a masterpiece of physical comedy: [[Nathaniel Winkle|Winkle]], who has claimed expertise at skating, is revealed to know nothing of it, and his successive falls on the ice are described with Dickens's gift for slapstick. (002-114)

## Participants

- **[[Mr. Pickwick]]** — guest of honour, participates in skating with disastrous results
- **[[Mr. Wardle]]** — host, jovial country squire
- **[[Nathaniel Winkle|Mr. Winkle]]** — claims skating expertise, fails comically
- **[[Arabella Allen]]** — [[mr-wardle|Wardle]]'s niece, first meeting with [[nathaniel-winkle|Winkle]]
- **[[Bob Sawyer|Mr. Bob Sawyer]]** — medical student, boisterous guest
- **[[Benjamin Allen|Mr. Benjamin Allen]]** — Arabella's brother

## Aftermath

The [[christmas|Christmas]] party breaks up the following morning (002-133), but the connections forged there — particularly between [[Nathaniel Winkle|Winkle]] and [[Arabella Allen]] — set in motion the romantic plots that culminate in the [[wedding-at-dingley-dell|Wedding at Dingley Dell]] near the novel's end. The party also introduces [[Bob Sawyer]], whose [[lant-street|Lant Street]] lodgings become the scene of another memorable party. (004-002)

## Significance

The Christmas at Dingley Dell chapters exemplify Dickens's gift for festive writing — the warmth, abundance, and good fellowship of the country [[christmas|Christmas]] would become a Dickensian trademark, culminating in *A [[christmas|Christmas]] Carol*. The skating episode, in particular, has been praised as one of the most purely joyful passages in English fiction.
