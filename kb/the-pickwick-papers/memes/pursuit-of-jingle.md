## Overview

The pursuit of [[Alfred Jingle]] across southern England is one of the most breathless sequences in *The [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] Papers*: a madcap chase from [[London]] to [[Bristol]] as [[Mr. Pickwick]] and [[Sam Weller]] attempt to intercept the scheming strolling actor who has eloped with Miss Rachael [[mr-wardle|Wardle]], the wealthy spinster sister of [[Mr. Wardle]]. (009-157)

The pursuit is conducted by coach and post-chaise, exploiting the [[Coaching Era]] transport network at its peak, and it showcases [[Sam Weller]]'s resourcefulness as a detective and manoeuvrer.

## Background

[[Alfred Jingle]], having ingratiated himself with the [[pickwick-club|Pickwickians]] at the [[cricket-match-at-muggleton|Muggleton cricket match]], has charmed Miss Rachael [[mr-wardle|Wardle]], [[mr-wardle|Mr. Wardle]]'s unmarried sister, into an elopement. The couple has fled towards [[Bristol]]. [[Mr. Pickwick]], alerted by [[Mr. Wardle]], resolves to pursue.

[[Sam Weller]] proves indispensable throughout, his Cockney shrewdness matching [[Alfred Jingle|Jingle]]'s trickery at every turn. The chase is conducted at breakneck speed through the coaching inns and posting-houses of the era.

## Aftermath

[[Alfred Jingle|Jingle]] is bought off — paid to abandon the elopement — and Miss Rachael returns to her brother. But [[alfred-jingle|Jingle]] and his servant [[Job Trotter]] will reappear later in the novel, at the [[Fleet Prison]], where [[Mr. Pickwick]]'s forgiveness of his former tormentor marks the novel's final moral turn. (019-056)

The pursuit establishes [[Sam Weller]] as more than a comic servant: he is the novel's most capable man of action, as comfortable on the coaching road as in the [[lant-street|Borough]].

## Significance

The [[alfred-jingle|Jingle]] pursuit is Dickens's first extended travel sequence, exploiting the speed and drama of the coaching network. It also introduces the theme of the "rescue" that recurs throughout the novel — [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] as protector of vulnerable women, however comically mismatched he may be to the role.
