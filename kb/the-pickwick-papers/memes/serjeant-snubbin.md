## Overview

Serjeant Snubbin is a senior barrister retained by [[mr-perker|Mr. Perker]] to represent [[Mr. Pickwick]] in the breach of promise case brought by [[Mrs. Bardell]]. He is described as "a lantern-faced, sallow-complexioned" figure (003-125), a leading practitioner at the top of his profession whose time is highly sought after and expensive.

## Role in the Novel

Serjeant Snubbin serves as the primary defence counsel in the trial of *Bardell v. [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]]*, one of the novel's most consequential legal episodes. He is introduced through [[mr-perker|Mr. Perker]], who describes him as "at the very top of his profession" and someone whose retention is "a great coup" (003-089). Despite his professional eminence, Snubbin's performance at the trial is marked by a pragmatic recognition of the case's weakness — he ultimately advises against cross-examining certain witnesses and his defence fails to prevent the verdict against [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]].

## Key Scenes

**Retention at Serjeant's Inn** (003-087–003-161): [[mr-perker|Mr. Perker]] escorts [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] to the chambers of Serjeant Snubbin at Serjeant's Inn to brief him on the case. Snubbin listens with an air of "haughty condescension" (003-152) while studying the brief. During the consultation he assigns his junior, [[Mr. Phunky]], to handle preliminaries, and after delivering his opinion relapses into "abstraction" (003-150), leaving [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] uncertain about his prospects.

**The Trial — Opening** (006-015–006-048): Serjeant Snubbin appears at the Guildhall for the trial, accompanied by [[mr-mallard|Mr. Mallard]]. He announces himself as counsel "for the defendant" (006-045) and introduces [[mr-phunky|Mr. Phunky]] as his junior. The judge, [[mr-justice-stareleigh|Mr. Justice Stareleigh]], has difficulty with Snubbin's arguments about procedural technicalities, but Snubbin persists with professional courtesy.

**Cross-examination of [[nathaniel-winkle|Mr. Winkle]]** (006-147–006-164): [[mr-phunky|Mr. Phunky]] conducts the initial examination of [[Nathaniel Winkle]], but Serjeant Snubbin directs the strategy — winking at [[mr-phunky|Phunky]] to signal when he should sit down (006-156). Snubbin's cross-examination exposes inconsistencies in [[nathaniel-winkle|Winkle]]'s testimony about the circumstances of the [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]]–Bardell misunderstanding.

**Examination of [[sam-weller|Sam Weller]]** (006-220–006-222): Serjeant Snubbin examines [[Sam Weller]] with good humour, laughing during the testimony (006-220). He attempts to extract testimony favourable to the defence, though [[sam-weller|Sam]]'s clever evasions prove challenging to manage in court.
