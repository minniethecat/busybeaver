## Overview

The wedding at [[Dingley Dell]] brings *The [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] Papers* to its romantic conclusion. After the dissolution of the [[pickwick-club|Pickwick Club]], two of the original [[pickwick-club|Pickwickians]] — [[Augustus Snodgrass|Mr. Snodgrass]] and [[Nathaniel Winkle|Mr. Winkle]] — settle into matrimony at [[manor-farm|Manor Farm]], the [[mr-wardle|Wardle]] family seat. (029-032)

The double wedding is the novel's final festive set-piece, balancing the [[christmas-at-dingley-dell|Christmas at Dingley Dell]] that opened the country sequence. Where [[christmas|Christmas]] was about community and conviviality, the wedding is about individual happiness and the novel's movement from club solidarity to domestic settlement.

## Background

The romantic threads that conclude at the wedding have been woven throughout the novel:

- [[Augustus Snodgrass|Snodgrass]] and [[Arabella Allen]] first met at the [[christmas-at-dingley-dell|Christmas party]] (002-054)
- Their correspondence, conducted in secret, is discovered when [[mr-wardle|Wardle]] intercepts a letter (026-056)
- [[Nathaniel Winkle|Winkle]]'s courtship of Arabella's companion parallels [[augustus-snodgrass|Snodgrass]]'s romance

> "Snodgrass! Since last Christmas!" were the first broken words that issued from the lips of Wardle. (026-058)

## The Event

The double wedding is modest, without bridesmaids — "that there should have been a wedding without bridesmaids, that I may never see another!" (026-034) — but joyful. Key elements:

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Setting | [[manor-farm|Manor Farm]], [[dingley-dell|Dingley Dell]] |
| Grooms | [[augustus-snodgrass|Mr. Snodgrass]] and [[nathaniel-winkle|Mr. Winkle]] |
| Brides | [[arabella-allen|Arabella Allen]] and her companion |
| Witnesses | [[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]], [[mr-wardle|Mr. Wardle]], [[sam-weller|Sam Weller]] |
| Aftermath | The [[augustus-snodgrass|Snodgrass]]es settle at [[dingley-dell|Dingley Dell]] (029-032) |

[[Mr. Pickwick]], now released from the [[Fleet Prison]] and having dissolved the Club, presides as the benign godfather figure — no longer the bumbling adventurer of the early chapters but a man of quiet wisdom who has been transformed by his experiences.

## Aftermath

The wedding closes the novel's romantic subplots and signals the final dispersal of the original [[pickwick-club|Pickwick Club]]:

- [[Augustus Snodgrass|Snodgrass]] and [[Arabella Allen|Arabella]] settle at [[dingley-dell|Dingley Dell]], where they "purchased and cultivated a small farm" (029-032)
- [[Nathaniel Winkle|Winkle]] and his bride live nearby
- [[Mr. Pickwick]] retires to Dulwich, a contented observer of his friends' happiness

## Participants

- **[[Augustus Snodgrass|Mr. Snodgrass]]** — groom, poet of the Club
- **[[Arabella Allen]]** — bride, [[mr-wardle|Wardle]]'s niece
- **[[Nathaniel Winkle|Mr. Winkle]]** — groom, the reluctant sportsman
- **[[Mr. Pickwick]]** — presiding figure of benevolence
- **[[Mr. Wardle]]** — host, the jovial country squire
