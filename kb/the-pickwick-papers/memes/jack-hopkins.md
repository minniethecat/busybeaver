## Overview

Jack Hopkins is a medical student at St. Bartholomew's Hospital and a friend of [[Bob Sawyer]]. He appears in a single chapter — Sawyer's raucous party in his Borough lodgings — where he serves as one of the evening's principal entertainers. Hopkins arrives late, wearing "a black velvet waistcoat, with thunder-and-lightning buttons; and a blue striped shirt, with a white false collar," and immediately launches into a series of graphic medical stories that alternately horrify and fascinate [[Mr. Pickwick]]. His combination of professional sang-froid, grim humour, and personal loyalty makes him one of the more vividly sketched minor figures in the novel's medical subplot. (004-040)

## Role in the Novel

Hopkins functions as a representative of the young medical student type — worldly, irreverent, and inured to human suffering. His casual discussion of operations and accidents before the fastidious [[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]] creates a comic contrast between the genteel world of [[pickwick-club|the Pickwick Club]] and the rougher, more knowing milieu of the [[london|London]] teaching hospitals. When the party is threatened with forcible dissolution by the landlady [[mrs-raddle|Mrs. Raddle]], Hopkins reveals a warmer side: he offers to "step up-stairs and pitch into the landlord," ring the bell, or groan on the staircase — any service, he tells [[bob-sawyer|Bob Sawyer]], that his friend might command. This loyalty, expressed in the language of a man who would happily brawl for a mate, rounds out what might otherwise be a purely grotesque comic turn. (004-100)

## Key Scenes

**The Arrival** (004-045–004-050): Hopkins enters late, apologising that he has been "detained at Bartholomew's." His news — "a man fallen out of a four pair of stairs' window" — he pronounces "a very fair case — very fair case indeed," meaning, to [[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]]'s dismay, that the patient is unlikely to survive. Hopkins's professional enthusiasm for the "splendid operation" to follow, especially "if Slasher does it," establishes his medical-student nonchalance towards others' misfortune. (004-045)

**The Necklace Story** (004-068–004-076): Hopkins's signature set piece is the tale of a child who swallowed a necklace, bead by bead, over the course of a week, until the twenty-five wooden beads rattled audibly in his stomach — a sound the family mistook for a hailstorm. The father, shaking the child into obedience, produced "such a rattling ensued as nobody ever heard before," and ran him to the hospital, the beads jolting all the way. Hopkins delivers the story with the timing of a practised raconteur, pausing to needle [[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]] — "Not all at once, you know, that would be too much — *you* couldn't swallow that, if the child did — eh, [[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]], ha! ha!" The boy, he adds, is still in hospital, "obliged to be muffled in a watchman's coat, for fear he should wake the patients." The anecdote encapsulates Hopkins's mode: clinical data wrapped in music-hall storytelling. (004-076)

**Defending [[bob-sawyer|Bob Sawyer]]** (004-098–004-100): When [[mrs-raddle|Mrs. Raddle]] erupts and [[bob-sawyer|Bob Sawyer]] concedes that the party must break up, Hopkins offers himself as his friend's champion: "Shall I step up-stairs and pitch into the landlord? or keep on ringing the bell, or go and groan on the staircase? You may command me, Bob." The offer, in its escalating absurdity, is at once comic and genuinely generous — a glimpse of the bond beneath the bravado. (004-100)

**The Song** (004-066): As the party threatens to flag after a dispute between [[mr-noddy|Noddy]] and [[mr-gunter|Gunter]], Hopkins proposes "just to set us going again, Bob, I don't mind singing a song," and launches into "The King, God bless him," sung to "a novel air, compounded of the 'Bay of Biscay,' and 'A Frog he would.'" The chorus — each gentleman joining in to his own preferred tune — produces an effect that the narrator dryly calls "very striking indeed." (004-066)

## Relationships

- **[[Bob Sawyer]]** — Hopkins's close friend and fellow medical student; Hopkins is loyal to Sawyer even when the latter's landlady threatens to clear the house
- **[[Mr. Pickwick]]** — The fastidious guest whose reaction to Hopkins's grisly tales provides the comic counterpoint
- **[[Benjamin Allen]]** — A fellow medical student present at the party, who greets Hopkins upon his late arrival
- **[[mr-noddy|Mr. Noddy]]** and **[[mr-gunter|Mr. Gunter]]** — Fellow guests whose near-duel Hopkins attempts to dissolve by starting a song
