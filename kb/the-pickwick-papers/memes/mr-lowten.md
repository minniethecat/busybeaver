## Overview

Mr. Lowten is a law clerk employed by [[Mr. Perker]] at Gray's Inn. He serves as an intermediary between [[mr-perker|Perker]] and his clients, managing the practical details of legal business with a mixture of efficiency and disreputable good humour. Lowten is a comic figure whose fondness for drink and irreverent commentary provide relief amid the novel's legal proceedings.

## Role in the Novel

Lowten appears as a recurring supporting figure in the legal episodes of the novel. As [[mr-perker|Perker]]'s clerk, he facilitates contact between [[Mr. Pickwick]] and his solicitor, often serving as gatekeeper to [[mr-perker|Perker]]'s chambers. His role expands during [[debtors-prison|the Fleet]] Prison chapters, where he becomes involved in the practical arrangements for [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]]'s confinement and the subsequent negotiations for his release.

## Key Scenes

**At [[mr-perker|Perker]]'s Office** (003-081–003-083): Lowten is first encountered at [[mr-perker|Perker]]'s chambers, where [[mr-perker|Perker]] instructs him not to "neglect" a particular matter (003-081). He is presented as a familiar fixture of the office, trusted with the solicitor's routine business.

**The Trial Day** (006-008): Lowten accompanies the [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] party to Guildhall for the trial of *Bardell v. [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]]*, following behind with "the blue bag" (006-008) containing case documents, alongside [[sam-weller|Sam Weller]].

**[[magpie-and-stump|The Magpie and Stump]]** (012-093): Lowten is found at the [[magpie-and-stump|Magpie and Stump]] tavern, observing legal proceedings with the cynical eye of an insider. He remarks on a barrister's likely fees: "How much do you think he'd make, sir" (012-093), displaying the clerk's characteristic interest in the financial side of the law.

**[[fleet-prison|Fleet Prison]] Arrangements** (019-003–019-007): When [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] is committed to [[debtors-prison|the Fleet]], Lowten must be "ferreted out" (019-003) to assist. He takes up his hat and accompanies the party to the prison, where he and [[Job Trotter]] are found "looking very much at home" (019-007) in the dining-room — a detail that underscores Lowten's adaptable nature.

**Negotiations at the Inn** (025-016–025-160): Lowten features prominently in the chapters dealing with the aftermath of the trial and the negotiations over costs. He extracts the plug from a brandy bottle (025-020), dispenses cynical commentary, and mediates between [[mr-perker|Perker]] and the various parties pressing for settlement. When [[mr-perker|Perker]] calls out "Make haste, Mr. Lowten" (025-159), Lowten is found "washing his hands" (025-160) — a recurring image of his unhurried approach to duty.
