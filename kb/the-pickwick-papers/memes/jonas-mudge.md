## Overview

Jonas Mudge is the secretary of the [[brick-lane-temperance-association|Brick Lane Branch]] of the [[brick-lane-temperance-association|United Grand Junction Ebenezer Temperance Association]], a position that combines record-keeping with the delicate art of managing a meeting composed largely of tea-drinking enthusiasts and reformed drunkards. He is a chandler's-shop keeper by trade (005-116), and his presence at the temperance meeting — pious, earnest, and slightly hapless — embodies the lower-middle-class respectability that Dickens treats with a blend of amusement and affection. When the monthly meeting convenes with [[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]] in attendance, it is Mudge who reads the reports and who, at a critical moment, sneezes "in a very imprudent manner" (005-126).

## Role in the Novel

Mudge appears in a single extended scene: the monthly meeting of the [[brick-lane-temperance-association|Brick Lane Branch]], where [[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]] and his companions observe the rituals of organised temperance. As secretary, Mudge is responsible for the administrative formalities — reading reports, recording resolutions — that give the gathering its air of institutional gravity. [[sam-weller|Sam Weller]], watching from the gallery, immediately identifies him as a target for mockery: "If that 'ere secretary fellow keeps on for only five minutes more, he'll blow hisself up with depravity and rum-and-water" (005-120). The joke, characteristically, is that [[sam-weller|Sam]] sees drunkenness where the teetotallers see virtue — and Mudge, the most visibly pious of the lot, becomes the unwitting butt of it.

The [[brick-lane-temperance-association|Brick Lane Branch]] meeting (005-110–168) is one of the novel's most sustained comic set-pieces, featuring speeches by [[mr-stiggins|Mr. Stiggins]], the red-nosed deputy shepherd whose own fondness for spirits undermines the proceedings, and resolutions that praise the members for abstaining from drink while inadvertently revealing how much they previously consumed.
