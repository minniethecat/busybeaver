## Overview

Lant Street in the Borough is the modest lodging of [[Bob Sawyer|Mr. Bob Sawyer]], the boisterous medical student whose acquaintance the [[pickwick-club|Pickwickians]] make early in their travels. (002-139) The street is near Guy's Hospital, the teaching hospital where Sawyer trained.

> "There is a repose about Lant Street, in the Borough, which sheds a gentle melancholy upon the soul." (004-002)

## Location

Lant Street lies in the Borough — the area of Southwark south of [[london|London]] Bridge. In the 1830s this was a district of mixed character: the grandeur of Guy's Hospital alongside modest lodging-houses for students and clerks. (004-002)

The street's proximity to Guy's Hospital was its chief advantage for medical students like [[Bob Sawyer]], who described it as "near Guy's, and handy for me." (002-139)

## In the Novel

The [[pickwick-club|Pickwickians]] visit [[bob-sawyer|Bob Sawyer]] at his Lant Street lodgings in Chapter 4, a visit that becomes one of the novel's most chaotic comic set-pieces:

- **The Party**: Mr. [[jack-hopkins|Jack Hopkins]] arrives with tales of hospital eccentricities. (004-003)
- **[[mrs-raddle|Mrs. Raddle]]**: The landlady, [[mrs-raddle|Mrs. Raddle]], storms in demanding unpaid rent — one of Dickens's finest comic harridan portraits. (004-044)
- **Medical Humour**: The gathering of medical students at Sawyer's lodgings allows Dickens to satirise the medical profession of his day. (004-053)

Later, Sawyer's connection to Guy's Hospital is mentioned again: "formerly of Guy's Hospital in the Borough, with a private residence in Lant Street." (010-011)
