## Overview

George Nupkins, Esquire, is the magistrate and justice of the peace for the borough of [[ipswich|Ipswich]]. He presides over the legal proceedings when [[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]] and his companions are forcibly brought before him after the midnight incident at the Great White Horse Inn. Nupkins represents provincial legal authority in the novel, a counterpart to the [[london|London]]-based legal machinery of [[Dodson and Fogg]] and the Bardell v. [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] trial.

## Role in the Novel

Mr. Nupkins appears as a figure of municipal authority in [[ipswich|Ipswich]]. His household employs [[mary-housemaid|Mary]], the pretty housemaid who becomes [[sam-weller|Sam Weller]]'s Valentine, creating one of the novel's romantic subplots. As a magistrate, Nupkins handles the charge brought against [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] — though the encounter is more farcical than judicial, typical of Dickens's satirical treatment of provincial officialdom. The connection between Nupkins's household and the [[pickwick-club|Pickwickians]] runs through [[mary-housemaid|Mary]], whose presence at the [[ipswich|Ipswich]] inn momentarily unsettles [[sam-weller|Sam Weller]]. (006-163)

## Key Scenes

**Employment of [[mary-housemaid|Mary]]** (005-097): [[sam-weller|Sam Weller]]'s father writes a letter addressed "To [[mary-housemaid|Mary]], Housemaid, at Mr. Nupkins's Mayor's, [[ipswich|Ipswich]], Suffolk," folded in a very intricate manner and wafered for the post. This establishes the household as a node in the Weller family's social network.

**The Magistrate's Bench** (006-163): After the midnight disturbance at the inn, the entire [[gentleman|Pickwickian]] party is "forcibly carried before George Nupkins, Esq., magistrate and justice of the peace for the borough of [[ipswich|Ipswich]]." The proceeding arises from [[nathaniel-winkle|Mr. Winkle]]'s faltering explanation that [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] was found in a lady's sleeping apartment at midnight — an event that had "terminated, he believed, in the breaking off of the projected marriage of the lady in question."

**[[mary-housemaid|Mary]] Recognized** (011-034): At the inn in [[ipswich|Ipswich]], [[sam-weller|Sam Weller]] encounters [[mary-housemaid|Mary]] unexpectedly. She is described as "the pretty housemaid from Mr. Nupkins's," and [[sam-weller|Sam]] is "scarcely less staggered" than she is at the recognition. The scene marks the reunion of the two after their Valentine's Day correspondence.
