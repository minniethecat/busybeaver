## Overview

Mr. Solomon Pell is an attorney of the Insolvent Court who handles the affairs of [[Tony Weller]] in a celebrated comic scene that serves as Dickens's satire of professional self-importance and legal bureaucracy. First introduced as "one of this learned body" — the phrasing itself dripping with the irony that colours the entire episode — Pell presides over a ritual of debt-discharge that is simultaneously a farce and an exact representation of how the law actually operated. (015-007) His chambers, filled with parchments, schedules, and the arcane paraphernalia of insolvency practice, provide the setting for one of the novel's most sustained comic set-pieces.

## Role in the Novel

Pell's function in the novel is limited but precise: he administers the legal machinery by which [[Tony Weller]]'s worldly goods are transferred to [[Mr. Pickwick]] to protect them from the claims of [[Mrs. Weller (Susan)]], Tony's second wife, a widow and publican who married Weller for his money. (015-010) Pell is thus both a facilitator of the Wellers' domestic strategy and a comic exhibit of the legal type — the man who talks in technicalities, laughs at his own jokes, and extracts comfortable fees from human misfortune. His role intersects with the novel's larger concern with the [[Legal System]]: like [[Mr. Perker]] and [[Dodson and Fogg]], Pell represents another facet of the profession, though his is the comedy of bureaucracy rather than the tragedy of exploitation.

## Relationships

- **[[Tony Weller]]** — Pell's client. The relationship is professional but coloured by Pell's awareness that Weller, despite his shrewdness, is entirely at sea in the legal world Pell commands. (015-025)
- **[[Mr. Pickwick]]** — Present at the consultation as Weller's friend and the recipient of the transferred assets. Pell treats [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] with the professional deference due to a gentleman of means. (015-025)
- **[[Job Trotter]]** — Dispatched to deliver documents to Pell, connecting the insolvency plot to the larger legal narrative. (019-091)
