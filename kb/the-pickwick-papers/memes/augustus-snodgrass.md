## Overview

Augustus Snodgrass is the youngest member of [[pickwick-club|the Pickwick Club]]'s travelling quartet: a gentle, slightly ridiculous young man who carries a notebook and professes himself a poet, though the novel never reveals a single line of his verse. (002-050, 003-017) Where [[Tracy Tupman]] chases romance and [[Nathaniel Winkle]] stumbles into adventure, Snodgrass drifts through the narrative in a state of mild artistic abstraction, observing and recording — yet his observations, like his poetry, remain forever unseen. His love affair with [[Emily Wardle]], conducted in whispers and meaningful glances across the lawns of [[manor-farm|Manor Farm]], is the quietest and most straightforward of the novel's several romantic plots.

## Role in the Novel

Snodgrass's role in [[pickwick-club|the Pickwick Club]] is officially that of documentarian — his notebook is meant to preserve the Club's discoveries for posterity. (002-069) In practice, the narrative consistently undercuts this pretension: the notebook remains stubbornly blank, and Snodgrass's "artistic temperament" serves mainly as a gentle running joke. He is the least distinctive of the four [[pickwick-club|Pickwickians]], which is itself part of his function — in a novel crowded with eccentrics, Snodgrass represents the ordinary young man of sensibility, the type who means well and feels deeply but produces nothing. His presence at [[Manor Farm]] supplies one thread of the novel's romantic tapestry, his courtship of [[emily-wardle|Emily]] providing a quiet counterpoint to the more eventful pairing of [[nathaniel-winkle|Winkle]] and [[Arabella Allen]].

## Relationships

- **[[Mr. Pickwick]]** — Snodgrass's patron and leader. Snodgrass is loyal to [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] throughout, though his loyalty is expressed more in feeling than in effective action. (002-050)
- **[[Tracy Tupman]]** — Fellow [[gentleman|Pickwickian]] and companion in sentiment. The two are frequently paired, sharing benches, dinner tables, and romantic disappointments. (007-010)
- **[[Nathaniel Winkle]]** — The third [[gentleman|Pickwickian]]. Snodgrass and [[nathaniel-winkle|Winkle]] grasp each other's hands in moments of crisis — a gesture that is as close to action as either of them reliably gets. (002-119)
- **[[Emily Wardle]]** — The object of Snodgrass's affections. Their romance is conducted almost entirely in reported whispers and parental speculation. (002-143)
