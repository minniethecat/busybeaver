## Overview

Bob Sawyer is a young medical student who appears in the [[dingley-dell|Dingley Dell]] chapters of the novel, introduced alongside his friend [[ben-allen|Ben Allen]] at the [[mr-wardle|Wardle]] family's [[christmas|Christmas]] festivities. He is characterised by his coarse clothing, prodigious drinking, and an infectious high-spiritedness that culminates in the ill-fated ice-skating expedition. (002-024) Though his appearances are limited to the early chapters, Sawyer represents the boisterous, convivial side of Dickens's medical student archetype — a figure who would reappear in later novels.

## Role in the Novel

Bob Sawyer functions as a comic supporting character whose primary dramatic contribution is his participation in the skating party where [[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]] suffers a memorable fall through the ice. As [[ben-allen|Ben Allen]]'s companion, Sawyer adds to the atmosphere of masculine camaraderie and youthful excess that contrasts with [[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]]'s more sedate temperament. His professional identity as a medical student also provides a pretext for the mock-medical consultation following the skating accident. (002-120)

## Relationships

- **[[Ben Allen]]** — Sawyer's closest friend and fellow medical student; the two are inseparable throughout the [[dingley-dell|Dingley Dell]] episodes, drinking together, skating together, and conferring on medical matters with equal confidence.
- **[[Mr. Pickwick]]** — Sawyer regards [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] with a mixture of deference and professional interest, particularly after the skating accident when he and [[ben-allen|Ben Allen]] debate whether to bleed the unconscious philosopher.
- **[[Nathaniel Winkle]]** — Sawyer and [[nathaniel-winkle|Winkle]] exchange "mutual distrust" during the skating expedition, each suspecting the other of incompetence on the ice. (002-056)
