## Overview

Mr. Phunky is a junior barrister who serves as [[Serjeant Snubbin]]'s assistant in the defence of [[Mr. Pickwick]] during the breach of promise trial. He is young, inexperienced, and visibly nervous in the presence of his eminent senior. His name — "Phunky" — is misheard by the judge as "Monkey" (006-048), and his performance at the trial provides some of the novel's sharpest satire of legal ineptitude.

## Role in the Novel

Phunky appears in two episodes: the pre-trial consultation at Serjeant's Inn and the trial itself. His primary dramatic function is to serve as a foil to [[serjeant-snubbin|Serjeant Snubbin]] — his nervous deference highlights Snubbin's haughty eminence, while his blunders in court expose the casual incompetence that Dickens saw in the legal profession. Phunky's examination of [[Nathaniel Winkle]] is an extended comic set-piece in which the junior barrister repeatedly misses opportunities to extract favourable testimony, to the visible frustration of his senior.

## Key Scenes

**Introduction at Serjeant's Inn** (003-145–003-161): Phunky is summoned to Snubbin's chambers to meet the client. His entrance is marked by a nervous "bow" (003-153), and Snubbin acknowledges him with "haughty condescension" (003-152), noting that he has not had "the pleasure of seeing you before" (003-152). Snubbin asks whether Phunky has "read the papers" (003-156), and Phunky's anxious response is described as tactically inept: rather than admitting ignorance, "Mr. Phunky should have professed to have forgotten all about the merits of the case" (003-157) but instead reveals his inexperience through awkward silence. Despite his discomfort, Phunky "would not hear of passing through the door before [[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]]" (003-161), showing a basic courtesy beneath his professional anxiety.

**At the Trial** (006-015–006-158): Phunky arrives at Guildhall and takes his seat as junior counsel (006-015). When Snubbin introduces him, the judge mishears: the court reporter writes "for the defendant, [[serjeant-snubbin|Serjeant Snubbin]] and Mr. Monkey" (006-048). Phunky "bowed and smiled" (006-050) at the judge, who remarks that he has not "had the pleasure of hearing that gentleman's name before" (006-050).

**Examination of [[nathaniel-winkle|Mr. Winkle]]** (006-147–006-158): This is Phunky's defining scene. He rises to examine [[nathaniel-winkle|Winkle]], asking whether [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] "is not a very old friend of yours" (006-148). His questioning meanders without purpose, and he pursues a line about whether [[nathaniel-winkle|Winkle]] considered the circumstances "at all suspicious" (006-156). [[serjeant-snubbin|Serjeant Snubbin]] winks at him to sit down, but Phunky misses the signal: "if the unfortunate Mr. Phunky had sat down when [[serjeant-snubbin|Serjeant Snubbin]] winked at him, or if [[serjeant-buzfuz|Serjeant Buzfuz]] had stopped this irregular cross-examination at the outset, the unfortunate defendant's case might have been less prejudiced" (006-158). His inept questioning inadvertently damages [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]]'s case.

## Relationships

- **[[Serjeant Snubbin]]** — Senior barrister; Phunky is his junior, treated with condescension
- **[[Mr. Pickwick]]** — The defendant whom Phunky is retained to help defend
- **[[Nathaniel Winkle]]** — Witness examined (badly) by Phunky
- **[[Mr. Justice Stareleigh]]** — The judge who mishears his name
- **[[Serjeant Buzfuz]]** — Opposing counsel who benefits from Phunky's mistakes
