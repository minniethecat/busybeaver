## Overview

Mrs. Dowler is a minor character who appears during the [[bath|Bath]] episodes of _The [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] Papers_ (Chapters 7–8). Married to the bombastic [[Mr. Dowler]], she is a figure of some consequence in [[bath|Bath]] society—described as "no less illustrious" than her husband. Her most memorable contribution to the novel is as the unwitting centrepiece of the famous sedan-chair incident, in which [[Mr. Winkle]], fleeing a compromising situation, accidentally climbs into her sedan-chair late at night, producing a scene of exquisite comic confusion.

## Role in the Novel

Mrs. Dowler's narrative function is twofold. First, she and her husband represent [[bath|Bath]]'s self-important social milieu, the world of Assembly Rooms, Master of Ceremonies, and carefully cultivated gentility that [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]]'s party encounters on their travels. Second, she serves as the catalyst for one of the novel's purest farcical set-pieces: the sedan-chair episode in Chapter 8, where her late return from an evening party sets off a chain of misunderstandings culminating in [[nathaniel-winkle|Mr. Winkle]]'s nocturnal flight through [[bath|Bath]].

## Key Scenes

**Introduction at the Assembly Rooms (Chapter 7)**
Mrs. Dowler first appears at the [[bath|Bath]] Assembly Rooms on her husband's arm:
> Mr. Dowler, with Mrs. Dowler on his arm, led the little party into the tea-room. (007-115)

She is complimented by [[Angelo Cyrus Bantam]], the Master of Ceremonies:
> "Mrs. Dowler, you embellish the rooms." (007-116)

This introduction establishes her as an ornament of [[bath|Bath]] society, a woman of recognised consequence in the resort town's carefully ordered social world.

**The Sedan-Chair Incident (Chapter 8)**
Mrs. Dowler attends an evening party while [[mr-dowler|Mr. Dowler]] sits up awaiting her return. (008-008) Her arrival in a sedan-chair late at night sets the stage for calamity. The chair, borne by a short fat man and an exceptionally tall one, arrives at the crescent:

> "Dear me!" said Mrs. Dowler. "You must knock again, if you please." (008-058)

When the knocker proves to be "only a handle" with a broken wire (008-061), a comic sequence ensues in which the chairmen struggle with the door. In the confusion, [[Mr. Winkle]]—fleeing from a separate misunderstanding involving [[mr-dowler|Mr. Dowler]]—stumbles into the sedan-chair occupied by Mrs. Dowler:

> Winkle in his confusion stepped into the sedan-chair where Mrs. Dowler was. (008-079)

The resulting scene, with Mrs. Dowler shrieking inside the chair while [[nathaniel-winkle|Winkle]] attempts to escape, is one of the novel's most perfectly constructed farcical moments.

**Later Reference (Chapter 10)**
Mrs. Dowler is mentioned in passing in connection with a vow she made, preserving a comic echo of her earlier prominence. (010-087)
