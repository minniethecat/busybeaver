## Overview

Mr. Smangle is a [[fleet-prison|Fleet Prison]] inmate who, together with his companion [[Mr. Mivins]], insinuates himself into [[Mr. Pickwick]]'s room shortly after [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]]'s arrival at the prison. With his whiskers, his gentlemanly affectations, and his tirelessly inventive conversation, Smangle embodies a particular type of Dickensian rogue: the debtor who has so thoroughly adapted to his circumstances that imprisonment has become a kind of perverse liberation. "Paper has been my ruin," he declares (013-084) — a claim that, in the context of his evident comfort within the prison walls, reads less as confession than as credential. His rapid exploitation of [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]]'s good nature — commissioning sherry, cigars, and an audience for his "romantic adventures" (013-102) — provides a darkly comic illustration of the moral ecology of the [[Fleet Prison]].

## Role in the Novel

Smangle's role is to dramatise the world into which [[Mr. Pickwick]] has fallen: a society of debtors where gentlemanly codes are simultaneously invoked and inverted. He calls himself a gentleman, speaks of "the feelings of a gentleman" (013-077), and organises the consumption of [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]]'s sherry according to "a fair and gentlemanlike division of labour" (013-090) — yet his every action belies the code he invokes. His elaborate tales of "a thorough-bred horse, and a magnificent Jewess, both of surpassing beauty" (013-102) are so transparently fabricated that they function less as lies than as performance: Smangle is an artist of self-presentation, and [[debtors-prison|the Fleet]] is his stage. Together with [[Mr. Mivins]] and later [[Zephyr]] and [[Tom Martin]], Smangle represents the prison's social order, which [[Sam Weller]] memorably describes as "a reg'lar, properly conwivial, and knock-down sort of place" (013-110). Smangle's reappearance in Chapter XIV, described in his borrowed finery as "the stately figure of the dashing Mr. Smangle" (014-003), confirms that the prison has not humbled but rather adorned him.

## Relationships

- **[[Mr. Pickwick]]** — Smangle's unwitting patron. [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]]'s gentlemanly reluctance to refuse hospitality makes him the perfect mark for Smangle's schemes. The dynamic exposes the vulnerability of genuine gentlemanliness in the prison's moral environment.
- **[[Mr. Mivins]]** — Smangle's partner in [[debtors-prison|the Fleet]]. Their friendship is a study in mutual convenience: Smangle directs, [[mr-mivins|Mivins]] fetches, and both drink at [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]]'s expense. Smangle describes [[mr-mivins|Mivins]] as "a devilish pleasant gentlemanly dog" before adding a "but—" and shaking his head dubiously (013-094).
- **[[Sam Weller]]** — [[sam-weller|Sam]] sees through Smangle immediately and intervenes to protect [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]]'s interests, using his Cockney wit as a shield against Smangle's gentlemanly imposture.
- **[[Zephyr]]** and **[[Tom Martin]]** — Fellow inmates of [[debtors-prison|the Fleet]] who populate the prison's social world alongside Smangle.
