# Bristol

**Bristol** is a city in the West of England that appears in *The [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] Papers* as a destination in the pursuit of [[Alfred Jingle]]. The city serves as a waypoint in the novel's geography of coaching routes and provincial towns, its distance from [[london|London]] emphasizing the lengths to which [[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]] and [[sam-weller|Sam Weller]] will go in their pursuit of the scheming stroller.

## Role in the Narrative

Bristol enters the narrative during the extended pursuit sequence that follows [[alfred-jingle|Jingle]]'s abduction of Rachael [[mr-wardle|Wardle]]. When word reaches [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] that [[alfred-jingle|Jingle]] has "gone over to Bristol that morning, by the branch coach" (009-145), the [[pickwick-club|Pickwickians]] immediately set out in pursuit. The journey to Bristol — a considerable distance from [[london|London]] — demonstrates [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]]'s determination and sets the pace for the novel's episodic travel structure.

[[sam-weller|Sam Weller]] is dispatched to Bristol "immediately, in pursuit of the fugitives" (009-157), and the city becomes a temporary stage in the cat-and-mouse game between [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] and [[alfred-jingle|Jingle]]. The coaching route to Bristol passes through [[bath|Bath]], linking two of the novel's significant provincial settings.

## Bristol in the Coaching Era

Bristol's appearance in the novel reflects its importance in the coaching network of early 19th-century England. As a major port and commercial centre in the West Country, Bristol was a regular destination for stage-coaches from [[london|London]], and the "branch coach" mentioned in the narrative was part of an extensive system of routes connecting the provinces to the capital.

The journey to Bristol, undertaken at speed in the hope of intercepting [[alfred-jingle|Jingle]], exemplifies the novel's dependence on coaching culture for its narrative momentum. The [[pickwick-club|Pickwickians]]' travels from [[london|London]] to [[rochester|Rochester]], [[dingley-dell|Dingley Dell]], [[eatanswill|Eatanswill]], [[bath|Bath]], [[ipswich|Ipswich]], and Bristol constitute a geography of movement that is central to the novel's structure.
