# The Eatanswill Gazette

The **Eatanswill Gazette** is a provincial newspaper serving the borough of [[eatanswill|Eatanswill]], edited by [[Mr. Pott]]. It is the principal Whig organ in the town and plays a prominent role in the famously corrupt and partisan [[eatanswill|Eatanswill]] election chronicled in *The [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] Papers*. The Gazette's editorial policy is one of uncompromising party loyalty, and its pages are filled with fierce denunciations of the rival Blue interest and its organ, the [[Eatanswill Independent]].

## Editorial Character

The Gazette is distinguished by the virulence of its political rhetoric. [[mr-pott|Mr. Pott]], its editor, writes editorials of extraordinary invective, denouncing his opponents in language that, as [[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]] observes, is more notable for its energy than its restraint. The Gazette's leading articles have appeared at intervals over the preceding three years (023-065) and have become notorious for their personal attacks on the rival editor, [[mr-slurk|Mr. Slurk]] of the Independent.

A sample of the Gazette's style is provided when [[mr-pott|Mr. Pott]] proudly reads aloud his latest editorial, which refers to the Independent as:

> a scurrilous print, whose columns are a receptacle for the lowest scurrility and the most malignant falsehoods (023-088)

The Gazette also engages in what [[mr-pott|Pott]] describes as "a searching review of the public and private character" of its adversaries — a phrase that, as events prove, is a euphemism for character assassination.

## The Eatanswill Election

The Gazette's coverage of the [[eatanswill|Eatanswill]] election (Chapter 23) exemplifies the town's partisan press culture. It serves as the mouthpiece for the Honourable [[sam-weller|Sam]]uel [[mr-slumkey|Slumkey]], the Buff (Whig) candidate, and its editorials work to sway public opinion through a combination of flattery for the Buff interest and vituperation for the Blues.

The circulation and influence of the Gazette are such that it shapes the public discourse of [[eatanswill|Eatanswill]] (023-094), and its rivalry with the Independent forms a subplot within the election narrative.

## The Gazette and the Independent

The Gazette's counterpart and sworn enemy is the [[Eatanswill Independent]], edited by [[mr-slurk|Mr. Slurk]]. The two papers maintain a state of permanent warfare, each regarding the other as a publication beneath contempt. Their editors mirror this hostility in their personal relations: when [[mr-pott|Pott]] and Slurk encounter one another at the Peacock Inn, their professional antagonism escalates into a physical altercation, with [[mr-pott|Pott]] wielding a carpet bag and Slurk a fire-shovel.

This rivalry, while comic, also serves Dickens's satirical purpose. The interchangeable virulence of the two papers — each calling the other a "scurrilous print" — exposes the pointlessness of such partisan journalism, in which party loyalty entirely replaces any commitment to truth.

## Mr. Pott

[[Mr. Pott]] is the editor and public face of the Gazette. A man of elaborate self-regard, [[mr-pott|Pott]] regards his editorials as works of literary distinction. He entertains [[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]] at his home during the election and takes evident pride in showing off both his paper and his command of political language. His wife, Mrs. [[mr-pott|Pott]], is less impressed, and her eventual flight from [[eatanswill|Eatanswill]] with [[nathaniel-winkle|Mr. Winkle]] suggests a domestic life that the Gazette's pages do not record.
