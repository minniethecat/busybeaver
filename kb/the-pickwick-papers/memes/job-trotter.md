## Overview

Job Trotter is the servant and accomplice of [[Alfred Jingle]], a man whose extraordinary gift for weeping on command makes him, in [[Sam Weller]]'s admiring phrase, possessed of "the gift o' the gab wery much developed" (017-130). First glimpsed as a barely noticed "familiar feature" in the debtor's prison (014-116), Job emerges as a comic grotesque whose gaunt frame, sanctimonious manner, and alarming fluency in deceit make him one of Dickens's most vivid minor creations. His redemption, parallel to [[alfred-jingle|Jingle]]'s, transforms him from a figure of farce into one of pathos: the reformed Job Trotter becomes, in time, "a worthy member of society" in the colonies. (029-038)

## Role in the Novel

Job Trotter functions as [[alfred-jingle|Jingle]]'s operational arm — the servant who executes the schemes his master devises. His signature technique is the tearful imposture: he poses as a wronged innocent, a distressed servant, or a moral reformer with such conviction that even the suspicious [[Sam Weller]] is momentarily deceived. (017-139–192) Job's skill at extracting information and sympathy is the dark mirror of [[sam-weller|Sam]]'s street-smart honesty; together, the two servants represent competing models of working-class intelligence. His imprisonment alongside [[alfred-jingle|Jingle]] in the [[Fleet Prison]] and his eventual reformation demonstrate that Dickens's forgiveness extends even to the novel's minor villains. (025-074, 029-038)

## Relationships

- **[[Alfred Jingle]]** — Master and partner in crime. Job's devotion to [[alfred-jingle|Jingle]] is absolute; he follows him into the [[Fleet Prison]] and into reformation. (017-170–174)
- **[[Sam Weller]]** — [[sam-weller|Sam]] recognises Job as a kindred spirit of a darker shade, diagnosing him as "a reg'lar thorough-bred dish-onorary" with professional admiration. (017-171)
- **[[Mr. Pickwick]]** — The agent of Job's redemption; [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]]'s forgiveness extends to Job as fully as to [[alfred-jingle|Jingle]].
- **[[Mr. Perker]]** — The attorney who arranges the practical details of Job's emigration. (019-093)
