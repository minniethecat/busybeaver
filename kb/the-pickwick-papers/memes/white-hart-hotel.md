# The White Hart Hotel

The **White Hart Hotel** is a coaching inn in the city of [[bath|Bath]], situated opposite the Great Pump Room. It serves as [[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]]'s headquarters during his visit to [[bath|Bath]] in Chapter 7 of *The [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] Papers*, and is the setting for several encounters that advance the novel's romantic and comic subplots.

## Location and Description

The White Hart occupies a prime location in [[bath|Bath]], directly opposite the Great Pump Room (007-065). [[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]] and his companions take "private sitting-rooms" at the hotel, establishing it as a respectable establishment catering to gentlemen travellers. The inn's proximity to the Pump Room — the social heart of [[bath|Bath]] — places the [[pickwick-club|Pickwickians]] at the centre of the city's fashionable life.

## Role in the Narrative

The White Hart serves as the stage for several key scenes during the [[bath|Bath]] chapters:

- **Arrival in [[bath|Bath]]** (007-065): [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]], [[nathaniel-winkle|Winkle]], and [[augustus-snodgrass|Snodgrass]] establish themselves at the hotel after arriving by coach.
- **[[sam-weller|Sam Weller]]'s Errand** (007-082): [[sam-weller|Sam]] is dispatched from the White Hart on a mission that takes him into the city, a journey that brings him into contact with the lower orders of [[bath|Bath]] society.
- **[[mr-pickwick|Mr. Pickwick]]'s Departure** (007-150): Having soothed his feelings after the complications of the [[bath|Bath]] episode, [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] returns to the White Hart and prepares to leave the city.

## Bath as a Setting

The White Hart's location places it within the broader social geography of [[bath|Bath]], a city that Dickens presents as a theatre of fashion, hypocrisy, and social ambition. The hotel, as a space of transience — where travellers arrive and depart — mirrors the novel's episodic structure, in which each location hosts a self-contained narrative before the characters move on.

## The Coaching Inn in Dickens

The White Hart belongs to a recurring Dickensian type: the coaching inn. Like the [[george-and-vulture|George and Vulture]] in [[london|London]] or the inn at [[dingley-dell|Dingley Dell]], it functions as a waypoint in the [[pickwick-club|Pickwickians]]' peripatetic adventures. These inns provide temporary homes for the travellers and serve as microcosms of the society through which they move.
