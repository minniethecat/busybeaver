## Overview

The duel at [[Rochester]] is an early comic episode in *The [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] Papers*, in which [[Nathaniel Winkle|Mr. Winkle]] — the [[pickwick-club|Pickwickian]] who claims to be a sportsman in all fields — finds himself challenged to a duel by Dr. Slammer of the 97th Regiment. The challenge arises from a misunderstanding at a military ball, and the confrontation is defused only when the seconds recognize each other.

The episode is Dickens's satire of the duelling code: the elaborate ritual of honour, seconds, and pistols at dawn, rendered absurd when neither combatant truly wishes to fight.

## Background

At a ball in [[Rochester]], [[Nathaniel Winkle|Mr. Winkle]] — wearing [[pickwick-club|the Pickwick Club]] uniform of bright green coat with brass buttons — is mistaken by Dr. Slammer for a man who has insulted a lady. Slammer sends a challenge. [[nathaniel-winkle|Winkle]], who has boasted of his sporting credentials, cannot refuse without admitting cowardice.

## The Event

The duel is arranged for the following morning at Fort Pitt, [[Rochester]]. Key moments:

- **The Challenge**: Dr. Slammer's second delivers the formal notice
- **[[nathaniel-winkle|Winkle]]'s Terror**: [[nathaniel-winkle|Winkle]] confesses to [[Augustus Snodgrass|Snodgrass]] that he has never fired a pistol in his life
- **The Confrontation**: At Fort Pitt, the seconds meet and discover they are friends
- **Resolution**: Mutual recognition dissolves the feud; no shots are fired

> "Never fired a pistol in my life," admitted Winkle. (002-010)

The comedy lies in the gap between [[Nathaniel Winkle|Winkle]]'s pretensions and reality. He has claimed expertise in shooting, skating, and riding — but in each case, his claims collapse under the slightest test. The duel is the first of many episodes in which [[nathaniel-winkle|Winkle]]'s "sporting character" is comically dismantled.

## Participants

- **[[Nathaniel Winkle|Mr. Winkle]]** — the reluctant duellist
- **Dr. Slammer** — surgeon of the 97th Regiment, challenger
- **[[Augustus Snodgrass|Mr. Snodgrass]]** — [[nathaniel-winkle|Winkle]]'s second
- **[[Tracy Tupman|Mr. Tupman]]** — fellow [[gentleman|Pickwickian]]

## Significance

The duel establishes the novel's recurring pattern of deflated pretension. [[nathaniel-winkle|Winkle]]'s claims to sporting prowess — repeated throughout the novel at the skating party (002-068) and elsewhere — are always exposed. The episode also introduces the military world that hovers at the novel's margins, from the [[rochester|Rochester]] garrison to the officers who appear in later episodes.
