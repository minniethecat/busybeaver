## Overview

Mr. Pott is the editor of the _[[eatanswill-gazette|Eatanswill Gazette]]_, the "blue" (Whig) organ in the fiercely partisan world of [[eatanswill|Eatanswill]] politics. With his "profound and thoughtful features" (023-042) and a conviction of his own "intellectual superiority" (023-079), Pott personifies the self-importance and intellectual vacuity of provincial newspaper journalism as Dickens satirises it. His appearance in the novel coincides with the [[Eatanswill Election]] subplot, where his newspaper war with the rival _Independent_ and its editor [[Mr. Slurk]] provides the backdrop for [[Mr. Pickwick]]'s brief immersion in provincial political life. Pott's grand pronouncements about serving "my country's good" (023-081) are consistently undercut by the pettiness of his actual behaviour and the absurdity of his journalistic methods.

## Role in the Novel

Pott functions primarily as a satirical exhibit: through him, Dickens lampoons the pretensions of the press and the empty rhetoric of political partisanship. His character is inseparable from the [[Eatanswill Election]] episode, which occupies Chapter XXIII and serves as the novel's most concentrated political satire. Pott's rivalry with the "buff" (Tory) _Independent_ mirrors the larger political divisions Dickens portrays as essentially meaningless — a conflict of colours rather than principles. His journalistic practice, revealed when he proudly explains that his critic "crammed" for a review by reading entries under "M" and "C" in the Encyclopædia Britannica (023-078), exposes the intellectual bankruptcy behind the grandiose rhetoric. Pott's connection to [[Newspapers and Publishing]] makes him part of the novel's broader commentary on the power and irresponsibility of the press.

## Relationships

- **[[Mr. Pickwick]]** — Pott is eager to cultivate [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] as a social and political ally, inviting him to "club dinners together" (023-053). [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]] treats Pott with polite bemusement.
- **[[Sam Weller]]** — [[sam-weller|Sam]] is the first to encounter Pott and serves as intermediary between him and [[mr-pickwick|Pickwick]]'s party. [[sam-weller|Sam]]'s Cockney practicality is the perfect foil to Pott's windy rhetoric.
- **[[Mr. Slurk]]** — Pott's great rival, editor of the _[[eatanswill-independent|Eatanswill Independent]]_. Slurk is the "buff" to Pott's "blue," and their mutual animosity provides the comic energy of the [[eatanswill|Eatanswill]] chapters.
- **[[Bob Sawyer]]** and **[[Ben Allen]]** — The medical students are subjected to Pott's political catechism, with [[ben-allen|Ben Allen]] being denounced as "a waverer" (023-065) for his insufficient commitment to blue principles.
