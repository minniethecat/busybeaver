## Overview

Mr. John Smauker is a footman in [[bath|Bath]] who serves as [[Sam Weller]]'s introduction to the local servants' social circle. He is an affected and self-important figure who takes great pride in the exclusivity of the [[bath|Bath]] servants' society, treating [[sam-weller|Sam]] with a mixture of condescension and patronage.

## Role in the Novel

Smauker appears exclusively in the [[bath|Bath]] episode (Chapter 9 of Volume 2). His function is to provide comic relief through his pretentious manner and to serve as [[sam-weller|Sam Weller]]'s guide into the elaborate social rituals of [[bath|Bath]]'s servant class. Through Smauker, Dickens satirises the affectation of rank and fashion among domestic servants, who mimic the social hierarchies of their masters.

## Key Scenes

**The Invitation** (009-010–009-011): Smauker writes to [[sam-weller|Sam Weller]], addressing him as a fellow gentleman's gentleman, and invites him to a "swarry" (soirée) of select [[bath|Bath]] footmen. The letter is signed with elaborate formality: "(Signed) John Smauker" (009-011).

**The Meeting at the Pump Room** (009-014–009-038): Smauker meets [[sam-weller|Sam]] at the Pump Room, "leaning his powdered head against a door-post" (009-014). He greets [[sam-weller|Sam]] with affected grace, "raising his hat gracefully with one hand" (009-015). Throughout their conversation Smauker complains about the toll that high living takes on his health, comparing himself to "bad wine" (009-019) and delivering opinions with a world-weary sigh.

**The Walk to the Swarry** (009-043–009-060): Smauker escorts [[sam-weller|Sam]] to the greengrocer's shop where the servants' society meets. He reassures [[sam-weller|Sam]] not to be "alarmed" (009-043) by the assembled company and explains the social protocols. He introduces [[sam-weller|Sam]] with a flourish: "Gentlemen, my friend Mr. Weller" (009-060).

**At the Servants' Society** (009-107–009-123): Smauker presides as [[sam-weller|Sam]]'s sponsor, calling for "Order" (009-107) when the gathering becomes rowdy. He delivers pronouncements with "an alarming frown" (009-109) and takes evident satisfaction in his role as a senior member of the society. His friendship with [[sam-weller|Sam]] serves as a "sufficient letter of recommendation" (009-119) for the evening.
