## Overview

Arabella Allen is the sister of [[Ben Allen]] and the romantic interest of [[Nathaniel Winkle]], one of the original [[pickwick-club|Pickwickians]]. First mentioned in passing during the [[christmas|Christmas]] festivities at [[manor-farm|Manor Farm]] (002-133), Arabella emerges as a central figure in the novel's later domestic plot, where her secret marriage to [[nathaniel-winkle|Winkle]] — conducted without the knowledge or consent of [[nathaniel-winkle|Winkle]]'s father — creates a crisis that tests the loyalties of the [[pickwick-club|Pickwickian]] circle. Arabella's eventual reconciliation with the elder [[Nathaniel Winkle|Mr. Winkle]] in Chapter XXVIII, mediated by her own gentle dignity and the old gentleman's grudging admiration, provides one of the novel's most satisfying comic resolutions. (028-131–162)

## Role in the Novel

Arabella's narrative function is that of the romantic heroine whose personal crisis brings the novel's marriage plot to its climax. Unlike [[Mrs. Bardell]], whose breach-of-promise suit against [[Mr. Pickwick]] drives the legal plot, Arabella represents the genuinely affectionate — if imprudent — marriage of inclination that Dickens endorses. Her situation mirrors the novel's broader concern with [[Marriage and Courtship]]: she and [[nathaniel-winkle|Winkle]] marry for love but without money, a predicament that the elder [[nathaniel-winkle|Mr. Winkle]] condemns as "foolish, romantic, unbusiness-like" (028-153) before relenting. Arabella's characterisation as "a nice little girl, [[nathaniel-winkle|Winkle]], with black eyes" (010-054) emphasises her sweetness rather than her agency, but her composed handling of the elder [[nathaniel-winkle|Mr. Winkle]]'s unexpected visit — answering his pointed questions with honesty and tears, then winning him over with a smile (028-156) — reveals a quiet strength that transcends the passive heroine type.

## Relationships

- **[[Nathaniel Winkle]]** — Arabella's husband. Their secret marriage drives the novel's domestic crisis. [[nathaniel-winkle|Winkle]] describes his attachment as "disinterested affection" (028-151), and Arabella bears the elder [[nathaniel-winkle|Mr. Winkle]]'s reproaches with loyalty, insisting "It was my fault; all my fault, sir" (028-154).
- **[[Ben Allen]]** — Arabella's brother, a medical student and friend of [[Bob Sawyer]]. Ben is devoted to his sister, though his tobacco-scented embraces (026-152) provide more comedy than comfort. It is Ben who first describes Arabella to [[nathaniel-winkle|Winkle]] (010-054).
- **[[Mr. Wardle]]** — Arabella's connection to the [[mr-wardle|Wardle]] circle. [[mr-wardle|Wardle]] brings [[Emily Wardle|Emily]] to [[london|London]] "under pretence of bringing [[emily-wardle|Emily]] to see Arabella" (026-069), facilitating the reunion of the young couple.
- **[[Emily Wardle]]** — Close friend and confidante. [[emily-wardle|Emily]]'s note announcing her arrival brings Arabella to the Adelphi (026-084), and the two young women conspire together during the dinner-party revelations (026-093).
- **[[Mary]]** — Arabella's housemaid, who serves as a go-between in the love plot and is herself courted by [[Sam Weller]].
