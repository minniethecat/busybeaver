# Hampstead

Hampstead is a district of North [[london|London]], historically associated with artists, writers, and intellectuals. In the novel, it appears only once — "[[helen-denny|Helen]]'s artist pals at Hampstead" (003-021) — but that single reference carries the weight of an entire alternative [[london|London]]. Where [[billiter-avenue|Billiter Avenue]] is grey limestone and glass, Hampstead is color and conversation; where [[perriam|Perriam]] judges, the artists discuss.

## In the Novel

Hampstead exists in the novel exclusively as [[jim-colet|Colet]]'s imagination of [[helen-denny|Helen]]'s world. He thinks of what "would put it across [[helen-denny|Helen]]'s artist pals at Hampstead" (003-021) — his own social performance aimed at an audience he has never met. Hampstead is not a place [[jim-colet|Colet]] visits but a place he imagines, and the gap between imagination and reality is the gap that defines his [[london|London]] life.

## Significance

Hampstead matters as [[london|London]]'s counter-[[london|London]]: the place where different values apply, where art matters more than commerce, where conversation is an end in itself. That [[jim-colet|Colet]] never actually goes there — that it remains a name, a projection, a possibility — is the point. Hampstead is what [[london|London]] could be but isn't, at least not for him.
