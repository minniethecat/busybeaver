# The Encounter with Mr. Ah Loi

[[jim-colet|Colet]]'s encounter with [[ah-loi|Mr. Ah Loi]] in [[penang|Penang]] is the novel's most concentrated dramatization of the colonial encounter — and of its limits. Armed with a chit and the hotel people's directions, [[jim-colet|Colet]] finds a man whose "unctuous and cool" composure (024-014) resists every attempt at reading. [[ah-loi|Ah Loi]] is not hostile, not friendly, not deferential, not assertive. He is simply opaque — and his opacity is the novel's acknowledgment that the East exceeds European understanding.

## The Event

The encounter unfolds across Chapter 24. [[ah-loi|Ah Loi]] appears behind [[jim-colet|Colet]] — "[[ah-loi|Ah Loi]] was behind him. Interested in" (024-007) — the suddenness of his presence suggesting a surveillance that is never acknowledged. He handles books with "unctuous and cool" deliberation (024-014). He is amused by [[jim-colet|Colet]]'s impatience: "[[ah-loi|Ah Loi]] was amused. 'Wait till [[norrie|Norrie]]'" (024-021). His questions — "Well, [[ah-loi|Mr. Ah Loi]], I should like to know" (024-031) — are met with answers that deepen rather than dispel the mystery.

## Significance

The encounter with [[ah-loi|Ah Loi]] is the novel's counterpart to [[perriam|Perriam]]'s judgment. Where [[perriam|Perriam]]'s judgment is devastatingly clear — you are being tried — [[ah-loi|Ah Loi]]'s assessment is devastatingly unreadable. [[jim-colet|Colet]] cannot tell whether he has passed or failed, whether [[ah-loi|Ah Loi]] is friend or functionary, whether the encounter was transaction or test. This opacity is not a failure of representation but its point.
