# The Straits of Malacca

The Straits of Malacca are the narrow channel of water between the Malay Peninsula and the island of Sumatra — one of the world's great maritime [[time|passage]]s, through which ships have passed for centuries on their way between the [[the-sea|Indian Ocean]] and the China Sea. In the novel, they are more implicit than named: the waters the *Altair* would have navigated, the [[time|passage]] through which [[jim-colet|Colet]] moves from one world to another.

## In the Novel

The Straits are not explicitly named in the novel, but their presence is felt in every description of the maritime [[time|passage]] east. The narrowing of waters, the approach of land, the intensification of heat and light — these are the sensory markers of the Straits, the geographical correlative of the novel's progressive stripping-away of [[london|London]]'s certainties.

## Significance

The Straits of Malacca represent the novel's geography of transition. They are neither the open [[the-sea|ocean]] nor the enclosed harbor, neither [[london|London]] nor [[penang|Penang]], neither departure nor arrival. They are the [[time|passage]] itself — the space between, where the old world has receded and the new world has not yet appeared.
