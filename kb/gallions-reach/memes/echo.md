# Echo

The echo motif in H. M. Tomlinson's *[[gallions-reach|Gallions Reach]]* (1927) carries the novel's meditation on memory and the persistence of things past. An echo is a sound that outlasts its cause — and in this novel about departure and transformation, echoes mark the spaces where human presence has receded, leaving only its reverberation.

## In the Novel

The echo first appears in the empty [[london|London]] office at dusk: "Mary Axe chimed a quarter-past, half past; and its echo in the empty office where the shadows were deepening was like the memory of things gone in a place where the stir of men would be seen no more" (005-003). Here the echo is explicitly linked to memory — the sound of the clock persists after its striking, just as the memory of the departed workers lingers in the darkened office. This passage establishes the echo as the novel's figure for what remains when presence has withdrawn.

In the streets of [[london|London]] at night, [[jim-colet|Colet]] "made less noise" because "the echoes of his footsteps might shake them down" (006-046) — where "them" refers ambiguously to the tenement buildings but also to the fragile reality of the city itself. The echo threatens to collapse what it touches, suggesting that memory and reverberation carry a destructive potential.

In the jungle, the echo transforms from urban metaphor to geological fact. The caves and rock formations produce real echoes: "its recesses were capricious, and the disturbance of a rock by the invaders awoke echoes in lofty transepts and high vaultings unseen" (031-004). These jungle echoes suggest a hidden architecture within the natural world — unseen spaces that reveal themselves only through sound.

The sound of the [[the-harlow|Malays]]' poles in the water "clinked, splashed, and echoed" (036-011), marking the final transition — echo no longer as memory of the departed, but as the real reverberation of the living moving through the world.

## Key Passages

> "Mary Axe chimed a quarter-past, half past; and its echo in the empty office where the shadows were deepening was like the memory of things gone in a place where the stir of men would be seen no more." (005-003)

> "The echoes of his footsteps might shake them down, so he made less noise." (006-046)

> "Its recesses were capricious, and the disturbance of a rock by the invaders awoke echoes in lofty transepts and high vaultings unseen." (031-004)

## Related Concepts

- **[[noise|Noise]]** — The original sound; echo is what noise becomes when its source has vanished.
- **[[silence|Silence]]** — The medium through which echo travels; the novel's silences are the spaces where echoes are heard.
- **[[memory|Memory]]** — The echo's psychological counterpart; both are forms of persistence after departure.
- **[[time|Time]]** — The clock at Mary Axe that produces the novel's first echo is also its principal symbol of time's passage.
