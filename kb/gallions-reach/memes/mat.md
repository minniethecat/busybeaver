# Mat

A Malayan sailor serving aboard the *Harlow*, noted for his competence and reliability during the voyage and subsequent jungle expedition.

## Role in the Novel

Mat is a crew member of the *Harlow*, the vessel captained by Mr. Sinclair that transports Jim Colet from London to the East. He is "decidedly a good man" whose steady competence keeps the work moving briskly (035-006). During the jungle expedition on the island, Mat serves as a guide and assistant to Colet, leading the way through difficult terrain and managing the practicalities of disembarkation and movement through the forest.

## Character Traits

Mat is portrayed as practical, capable, and quietly perceptive. He maintains a respectful but frank manner with the Europeans — he "told Colet frankly that his hope and his rheumatism would keep him going" (037-002). In the jungle, he displays a mixture of wonder and wariness, murmuring his observations to Colet as they ascend into unfamiliar territory (037-005). He is also shown to be deferential, glancing "respectfully" at Colet after a successful moment (037-009), yet he leads decisively when action is required, "at once" leading down precipitous paths (037-019).

## Key Appearances

- **Chapter 35** — Introduced as a crew member; helps Colet prepare gear for the island landing (035-006, 035-012)
- **Chapter 36** — Oversees the disembarkation from the *Harlow* and roves about the landing site (036-005, 036-014)
- **Chapter 37** — Guides Colet through the jungle ascent, comments on the strangeness of the environment, and leads the way down steep terrain (037-002, 037-005, 037-007, 037-009, 037-013, 037-019)
- **Chapter 38** — Continues the jungle trek with Colet; his judgment about the difficulty of the route proves correct (038-001, 038-005, 038-011, 038-017)

## Relationships

- **[[Jim Colet]]** — Mat acts as Colet's guide and assistant during the island expedition, sharing his local knowledge and observations
- **[[Mr. Sinclair]]** — Mat serves under Captain Sinclair aboard the *Harlow*

## Significance

Mat represents the capable local crew without whom the European characters' ventures would be impossible. His knowledge of the terrain and practical skills contrast with Colet's urban background, and his quiet observations — including moments of wonder at the jungle — add a layer of perspective to the narrative that the European characters alone could not provide.
