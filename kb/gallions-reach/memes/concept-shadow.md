## Overview

Shadow in Gallions Reach is both a physical phenomenon and a psychological symbol. Characters edge closer to shadows for comfort or recoil from them in fear. A man afraid of his own shadow becomes the subject of remark — the shadow as the projected self.

> He heard waters muttering, and edged closer to the shadow of his mother. (001-003)

## Shadow and Fear

To be afraid of one's own shadow is to fear what follows you — the inescapable self. The text uses shadow to externalize internal dread, making visible what haunts from within.

> "Yes, but he was afraid of his own shadow." (001-013)
