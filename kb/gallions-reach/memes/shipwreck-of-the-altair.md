# The Shipwreck of the *Altair*

The sinking of the *Altair* is the novel's central event — the catastrophe that divides [[jim-colet|Colet]]'s life into before and after. It occurs in the [[china-sea|China Sea]], in the midst of a storm that has been building through the novel's maritime chapters, and it transforms every character it touches. The ship that carried [[jim-colet|Colet]] from [[london|London]] cannot carry him to [[the-jungle|the jungle]]; the vessel that defined his [[time|passage]] must be destroyed for the [[time|passage]] to continue.

## The Event

The storm builds through Chapters 12–15, registered in [[captain-hale|Captain Hale]]'s barometer readings and [[steward|the steward]]'s warnings. "The steward had warned [[jim-colet|Colet]] that they were" in for weather (011-001), and the weather, when it arrives, exceeds all warning. [[captain-hale|Captain Hale]] releases "sheet after sheet" of log entries (015-019); [[sinclair|Mr. Sinclair]] glares at [[gillespie|Gillespie]] and shouts for the engine-room (013-014); [[steward|the steward]] "hurriedly balances his way" across the deck (015-032).

The shipwreck itself is rendered through the ordered chaos of maritime crisis. "Your boat, [[collins|Collins]], keep her away" (018-063) — the orders are terse, professional, the hierarchy holding even as [[the-altair|the ship]] founders. In the lifeboat, [[collins|Collins]] sits "mute and observant" (019-010), [[gillespie|Gillespie]] "gently pressed his knee" (019-033), and [[jim-colet|Colet]] becomes a survivor rather than a passenger.

## Aftermath

The shipwreck deposits [[jim-colet|Colet]] in [[penang|Penang]], where the novel shifts from maritime to terrestrial. The loss of the *Altair* is also the loss of [[london|London]]'s last claim on [[jim-colet|Colet]] — with [[the-altair|the ship]] goes the commercial identity, the measured time, the father's definition. What remains is [[the-jungle|the jungle]].

The lifeboat scene crystallizes the novel's method: catastrophe rendered not through melodrama but through precise physical detail — [[collins|Collins]] mute, [[gillespie|Gillespie]] pressing a knee, [[steward|the steward]] balancing — small human gestures against the immensity of [[china-sea|the sea]].

## Significance

The shipwreck is the novel's structural fulcrum. Everything before it — [[london|London]], [[perriam|Perriams]], [[billiter-avenue|Billiter Avenue]] — is preparation; everything after it — [[penang|Penang]], [[the-jungle|the jungle]], [[return-to-gallions-reach|the return]] — is consequence. It is the event that makes the novel's central question urgent: what remains of a man when the structures that defined him are destroyed?
