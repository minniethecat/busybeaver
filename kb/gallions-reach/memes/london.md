# London

London is the novel's point of departure — geographical, psychological, and symbolic. It is the city toward which [[jim-colet|Colet]]'s father points from the darkness of [[gallions-reach|Gallions Reach]]: "See it? London, my boy" (001-005), and it is the city [[jim-colet|Colet]] must leave to become himself. More than any single location, London is the world the novel pushes against.

## In the Novel

London dominates the novel's first six chapters before receding into memory and contrast. It is first seen from the water — "There's the smoke of it. London" (001-002) — its presence announced by its industrial exhalation before any building is visible. This inaugural vision establishes London as a city defined by what it produces and consumes rather than what it is.

[[jim-colet|Colet]]'s London is specific: [[billiter-avenue|Billiter Avenue]], where [[perriam|Perriams, Limited]] occupies "the first floor of a region of grey limestone and glass" (002-001); the [[british-museum|British Museum]], where [[jim-colet|Colet]] lingers at bookshop windows "searching its windows for insinuations and conjectural words" (003-012); [[brixton|Brixton]], where the [[perriam|Perriams]]' privet hedge marks the boundary between commercial and domestic life. It is a city of clerks and magnates, of morning entrances and evening desertions — "one of the multitude which entered this region every morning at nine o'clock and deserted it about six in the evening" (002-003).

## Atmosphere

London's atmosphere is grey — "grey limestone and glass" (002-001) — and its rhythms are mechanical. The clerks enter at nine and leave at six, indistinguishable from thousands of others. Even at [[darkness|night]] the city exhales "the smell, on a still [[darkness|night]], of London's pavements that had been heated by the day's traffic" (004-048).

Yet [[jim-colet|Colet]] also perceives London as cosmic: "London was like the dream in which you stood by yourself at [[darkness|night]] and saw all the stars break loose and stream down the sky" (003-019). This doubleness — London as both the grey city of commerce and the visionary city of [[dream|dreams]] — runs through the novel's treatment of the capital.

## Significance

London in *[[gallions-reach|Gallions Reach]]* is what the City is in so much interwar fiction: the measured world, the world of clocks and ledgers and "being tried" (002-018). To work in London is to be one of a multitude; to leave London is to risk becoming singular. The novel does not condemn London so much as outgrow it. The city's values — commercial calculation, social performance, measured time — are insufficient. [[the-jungle|The jungle]] and [[the-sea|the sea]] do not refute London; they reveal its limits.
