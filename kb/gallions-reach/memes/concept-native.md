## Overview

"Native" in *Gallions Reach* is charged with the asymmetry of colonial encounter. It designates those who belong to the tropical world the Europeans only pass through — labourers, passengers on deck, figures in amateurish drawings. But Tomlinson also uses the word in its older, richer sense: what is "native to" a place, what belongs there naturally. The tension between these meanings runs through the novel.

## Native Labour

The colonial economy rests on extraction by native labour: "rubber, copra, nutmegs, tea, gums, pepper, sugar, rattans, tortoiseshell and much else which can be induced by native labour out of tropical prodigality disciplined by western accountancy" (002-004). The sentence is structured as a list of commodities followed by the mechanism that produces them — native bodies, disciplined by western ledgers. The native is at once central to the system and invisible within it.

> It is rubber, copra, nutmegs, tea, gums, pepper, sugar, rattans, tortoiseshell and much else which can be induced by native labour out of tropical prodigality disciplined by western accountancy. (002-004)

## Native as Familiar

Tomlinson sometimes uses "native" in its ecological sense, to mean what belongs: "She was nothing to him; she was only a familiar, native to the desert" (005-003). Here "native" loses its colonial charge and becomes a term of natural belonging — what is at home in its environment, as the European observer is not.

> She was nothing to him; she was only a familiar, native to the desert. (005-003)

## The Native Gaze

The European protagonist is repeatedly made aware of his difference by the presence of natives: "the seclusion compelled by his difference from that crowd of noisy native passengers on the deck below" (022-016). The native crowd is noisy, vital, at ease — and the European's silence is a function of his estrangement. In another scene, a character jokes that someone "will want to sit down and become a native" (034-045) — the idea of crossing the boundary is treated as absurd, yet the possibility lingers.

> The informal little steamer, the quiet, the radiant day, and the seclusion compelled by his difference from that crowd of noisy native passengers on the deck below, brought him back to a central loneliness. (022-016)
