## Overview

Invasion appears in Gallions Reach not as military conquest but as a metaphor for intrusion — the violation of silence, solitude, and private space. A character sitting in a chair, an unwelcome thought, outsiders entering a hamlet: each is figured as an invasion, reflecting the colonial anxiety about boundaries and belonging.

> Jimmy took a wicker-chair, which whined so loudly under his invasion that he thought it better for the silence if he did not move. (008-004)

## Psychological Dimension

The concept extends inward — unwelcome thoughts themselves become invasions. The text frames certain ideas as intrusive presences that disrupt mental composure, linking the outer world of colonial intrusion with the inner world of psychological disturbance.

> Wasteful to hurry over a sudden flavour of the richness of the earth, as though it were the invasion of a licentious and inappropriate thought. (027-003)
