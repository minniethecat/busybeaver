## Overview

Secrets in *Gallions Reach* operate at the boundary between the visible world of commerce and the hidden currents of inner life. The financial district itself is built upon secrets — treasures visible only as "symbols in ledgers" — while characters harbour private reservations that even they only dimly understand.

> This secluded corner of the city must be traversed on foot … but nobody ever goes into it except they who are concerned with the secrets of its caves. (002-001)

## The Commerce of Secrets

The commercial world of Billiter Avenue is structured around hidden knowledge. Its wealth is "never visible" except to "confidential initiates" who read it in ledgers and bills of lading (002-001). The Gridiron restaurant thrives on a similar logic of exclusivity — "its frequenters are so pleased with the secret of its choice attractions" (004-001) that they guard its location as a badge of belonging.

> No outside symbol betrays the Gridiron. Its frequenters are so pleased with the secret of its choice attractions that they take their friends to it. (004-001)

## The Inner Reservation

For Colet, the secret is internal. Despite outward success, he senses "some kind of secret reservation, only dimly felt by himself" that warns him he is "not in his element" (002-021). This private knowledge — that commerce asks "for only half of a man, and that the worst and cleverest half" — is the secret that propels his eventual break from the office world.

> Some kind of secret reservation, only dimly felt by himself, warned him that he was not in his element. Never had been in his element. (002-021)
