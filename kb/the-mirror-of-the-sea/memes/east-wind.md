# East Wind

The **East Wind** is the prevailing wind of the tropical latitudes, ruling from the east between the Tropics. In Conrad's elemental geography, he is the counterpart and antagonist of the [[west-wind|West Wind]] — a spare, subtle Southerner whose "cold craftiness and profound duplicity" make him the most treacherous of the winds that govern the world's oceans. Where the West Wind is frank and open in his violence, the East Wind strikes from concealment.

## In Conrad's Words

### The Character of the East Wind

Conrad establishes the East Wind's personality through sustained contrast with his northern rival:

> The East Wind, an interloper in the dominions of Westerly weather, is an impassive-faced tyrant with a sharp poniard held behind his back for a treacherous stab. (028-004)

> As a ruler, the East Wind has a remarkable stability; as an invader of the high latitudes lying under the tumultuous sway of his great brother, the Wind of the West, he is extremely difficult to dislodge, by the reason of his cold craftiness and profound duplicity. (025-003)

### Deceiving the Instruments

One of the East Wind's most characteristic traits is his ability to deceive even the most reliable nautical instruments, lending his meteorological behaviour a quality of deliberate deception:

> Its duplicity is such that it will deceive a scientific instrument. No barometer will give warning of an Easterly gale, were it ever so wet. It would be an unjust and ungrateful thing to say that a barometer is a stupid contrivance. It is simply that the wiles of the East Wind are too much for its fundamental honesty. (029-001)

### The East Wind's Malice

Conrad treats the East Wind's character as consistently malicious across all regions of the globe:

> It is strange that the winds which men are prone to style capricious remain true to their character in all the various regions of the earth. To us here, for instance, the East Wind comes across a great continent, sweeping over the greatest body of solid land upon this earth. For the Australian east coast the East Wind is the wind of the ocean, coming across the greatest body of water upon the globe; and yet here and there its characteristics remain the same with a strange consistency in everything that is vile and base. (029-003)

### Raiding the High Latitudes

When the East Wind ventures north from his tropical dominions, he does so as an interloper — a raider who exploits his brother's moments of melancholy weakness:

> But the other, crafty and unmoved, nursing his shaven chin between the thumb and forefinger of his slim and treacherous hand, thinks deep within his heart full of guile: "Aha! our brother of the West has fallen into the mood of kingly melancholy. He is tired of playing with circular gales, and blowing great guns, and unrolling thick streamers of fog in wanton sport at the cost of his own poor, miserable subjects. Their fate is most pitiful. Let us make a foray upon the dominions of that noisy barbarian, a great raid from Finisterre to Hatteras, catching his fishermen unawares, baffling the fleets that trust to his power, and shooting sly arrows into the livers of men who court his good graces." (028-003)

> For six weeks did the robber sheik hold the trade route of the earth, while our liege lord, the West Wind, slept profoundly like a tired Titan, or else remained lost in a mood of idle sadness known only to frank natures. (028-005)

## Related Concepts

The East Wind is one of four ruling winds in Conrad's scheme: the [[west-wind|West Wind]] (King of the North Atlantic), the [[wind|Wind]] (the elemental force itself), and the subordinate winds of the north and south. A [[gale]] is the wind at its most personal — and an easterly gale is the most treacherous of all.
