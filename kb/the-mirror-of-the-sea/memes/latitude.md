## Definition

Latitude is the angular distance of a point north or south of the equator, expressed in degrees. To the practical seaman of Conrad's era, latitude was far more than an abstract coördinate — it was a shorthand for the character of the sea in a given region. High latitudes meant long summer days and the threat of ice; low latitudes meant heat, calm belts, and hurricane seasons. The latitude determined not only the course to be steered but the very nature of the passage.

## In Conrad's Words

The latitude of a ship at sea was a constant preoccupation, noted in logs and reported in formal communications:

> Evidently they didn't believe in ghosts, for on arrival into port her captain unromantically reported having sighted a disabled steamer in latitude so-and-so and longitude so-and-so. (019-004)

High latitudes possessed a particular, savage beauty. Conrad's description of a gale under moonlight in northern waters captures this quality exactly:

> For a true expression of dishevelled wildness there is nothing like a gale in the bright moonlight of a high latitude. (024-002)

The Western King — Conrad's personification of the prevailing westerlies — ruled a vast domain defined by its latitudes, from the Azores in the south to the northern reaches of the Atlantic:

> It was a favour of the Western King, who rules masterfully the North Atlantic from the latitude of the Azores to the latitude of Cape Farewell. (026-008)
