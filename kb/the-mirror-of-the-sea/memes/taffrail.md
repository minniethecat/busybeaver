## Definition

The taffrail is the rail surrounding the stern deck of a vessel, forming the aftermost boundary of the ship. In Conrad's accounts, the taffrail was more than a structural guard — it was the place where men stood in contemplation, watching the ship's wake, holding quiet conferences, or surveying the sea astern for pursuing vessels.

## In Conrad's Words

> And as we stood near the taffrail side by side, my captain and I, looking at it, hardly discernible already, but still quite close-to on our quarter, he remarked in a meditative tone... (017-008)

> They stand on the mud of pavements, these degraded sea-dogs, with long lines of railway-trucks clanking their couplings behind their backs, and run malevolent glances over your ship from headgear to taffrail, only wishing to tyrannize over the poor creature under the hypocritical cloak of benevolence. (033-002)
