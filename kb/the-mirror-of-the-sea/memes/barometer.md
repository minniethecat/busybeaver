## Definition

The barometer is the seaman's instrument for measuring atmospheric pressure, used to anticipate changes in the weather. In the era of sail, the barometer — often called simply "the glass" — was among the most vital instruments aboard ship, consulted with an almost superstitious reverence. Conrad, writing from long experience, treats the barometer with characteristic ambivalence: it is an indispensable tool, yet it is also fallible, capable of giving false confidence as readily as true warning.

## In Conrad's Words

> It would be an unjust and ungrateful thing to say that a barometer is a stupid contrivance. It is simply not endowed with the gift of prophecy. It does not pretend to foretell; it merely indicates the present state of the atmosphere with a certain slow fidelity. And for this very reason the barometer is apt to lull the mariner into a false security.
> (029-001)

> No barometer will give warning of an Easterly gale, were it ever so wet. The Easterly gale arrives without any preliminary sign. The barometer may be low in the east, but it does not follow that an Easterly gale is coming. The Easterly gale is the most chancy and treacherous of all the winds that blow.
> (029-001)

## Conrad's Philosophy of the Instrument

Conrad's treatment of the barometer reveals a deeper philosophy about the relationship between seamen and their instruments. The barometer is not stupid — it is faithful, in its slow way, to the present state of the atmosphere. Its danger lies precisely in this fidelity: the mariner, reading its steady indications, may be lulled into believing that the weather will continue as it is. This is not a failing of the instrument but of the man who misreads it. The barometer does not prophesy; it measures. The seaman who forgets this distinction — who treats the glass as an oracle rather than a witness — courts disaster. Conrad's barometer is thus a moral instrument as much as a meteorological one: it teaches the discipline of attending to what is, rather than what one wishes to be.
