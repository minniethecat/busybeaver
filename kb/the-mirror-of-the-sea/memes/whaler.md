## Definition

A whaler is a vessel fitted out for the hunting and processing of whales, typically operating in polar or sub-polar waters. In *The Mirror of the Sea*, the whaler appears not as the subject of the narrative but as a chance observer--a ship of the old maritime order, itself a relic, that sights a disabled steamer in the vast emptiness of the Southern Ocean. Conrad uses the whaler to evoke a vanishing world of sail-driven enterprise, a ship whose crew still possess the mariner's instinct to look outward, to see, and to report.

## In Conrad's Words

> And she would have been posted really as "overdue," or maybe as "missing," had she not been sighted in a snowstorm, vaguely, like a strange rolling island, by a whaler going north from her Polar cruising ground. (019-002)

> She must have looked ghostly enough, that broken-down steamer, rolling in that snowstorm--a dark apparition in a world of white snowflakes to the staring eyes of that whaler's crew. Evidently they didn't believe in ghosts, for on arrival into port her captain unromantically reported having sighted a disabled steamer in latitude somewhere about 50 degrees S. and a longitude still more uncertain. (019-004)
