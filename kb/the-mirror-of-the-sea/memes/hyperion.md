## Vessel Identity

The *Hyperion* appears briefly but memorably in *The Mirror of the Sea* — a barque laid up in the London docks whose "aristocratic" bearing among the captive vessels caught Conrad's eye. She represents, in his meditation on ships removed from their element, the nobility that even the finest vessel cannot maintain when she is tied up, stripped of her sails, and subjected to the indignities of dock life.

## Conrad's Account

> It was a sight. The humblest craft that floats makes its appeal to a seaman by the faithfulness of her life; and this was the place where one beheld the aristocracy of ships. (035-003)

> The elderly, respectable seaman, withdrawing his gaze from that multitude of spars, gave me a glance to make sure of our fellowship in the craft and mystery of the sea. (035-004)
