## Identity

Captain MacW—— was the master of the ship in which Conrad served his first voyage as chief mate. He appears in *The Mirror of the Sea* as the exemplar of a peculiar type of commander — the captain who, once the ship has departed, retreats into absolute seclusion, leaving the running of the vessel entirely to his officers. Conrad treats this withdrawal with a mixture of professional criticism and amused tolerance, recognising in it a form of command by absence that was, if not admirable, at least harmless.

## Conrad's Account

> On my first voyage as chief mate with good Captain MacW—— I remember that I felt quite flattered, and went blithely about my duties, myself a commander for all practical purposes. Still, whatever the greatness of my illusion, the fact remained that the real commander was there, backing up my self-confidence, though invisible to my eyes behind a white-wooden bulkhead. (002-003)

> The good MacW—— would not even come out to his meals, and fed solitarily in his holy of holies from a tray covered with a white napkin. Our steward used to bend an ironic glance at the perfectly empty plates. (002-005)

> That is the time, after your Departure is taken, when the spirit of your commander communes with you in a muffled voice, as if from the sanctum sanctorum of a temple; because, call her a temple or a "hell afloat" — as some ships have been called — the captain's state-room is the august place in every vessel. (002-004)

## The Reclusive Commander

Conrad uses Captain MacW—— to introduce a meditation on the two pathological types of command he encountered at sea: the captain who vanished into his cabin and the captain who could not stay out of it — the latter driven by "sheer anxiety" to a craving for stimulants. MacW—— represented the benign form of the type: a man who trusted his officers to run the ship while he remained in seclusion, neither a tyrant nor an alcoholic, merely absent. The steward's ironic glance at the untouched plates became, in Conrad's memory, the symbol of a command exercised entirely through delegated competence.
