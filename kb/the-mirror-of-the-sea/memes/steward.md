The **steward** aboard a sailing ship is the domestic officer of the afterguard — responsible for the captain's table, the officers' meals, and the maintenance of the cabin. In Conrad's hands, the steward is never a heroic figure, but he is a revealing one: his relationship with the captain, his bearing in storm and calm, and his silent absorption of the ship's grievances make him a quiet barometer of life aboard.

## The empty plate

Conrad deploys the steward with a dry, observational irony. In the hungry times of a protracted passage, the steward's role is bleakly comic: "Our steward used to bend an ironic glance at the perfectly empty plates he was bringing out from there" (002-005). The steward is the one who must serve nothing, day after day, while maintaining the fiction of a properly set table.

> Our steward used to bend an ironic glance at the perfectly empty plates he was bringing out from there. (002-005)

## The steward as lightning-rod

When the captain's temper runs short, the steward is the nearest target. Conrad records one such scene: "Then, raising his head, he attacked grumpily the steward, a lanky, anxious youth with a long, pale face and two big front teeth" (021-011). The steward — young, anxious, and powerless — absorbs the captain's displeasure as part of his office. The charge was "so outrageous that the steward for all answer only dropped his eyelids bashfully" (021-013). This silent endurance is the steward's occupational fate.

> Then, raising his head, he attacked grumpily the steward, a lanky, anxious youth with a long, pale face and two big front teeth. (021-011)

> The charge was so outrageous that the steward for all answer only dropped his eyelids bashfully. (021-013)

## The steward and the sea

Conrad's stewards, unlike the deck officers, are men of the interior — creatures of the cabin, not the weather deck. "While I was still busy about the decks forward I heard the steward at my elbow saying: 'The captain asks whether you mean to come down to dinner, sir'" (021-005). The steward is the messenger between the world of sea and the world of hierarchy below.
