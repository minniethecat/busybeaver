## Overview

The Cat is one of the three [[animal-helpers|animal companions]] purchased by the merchant's son in "The Charmed Ring" (Chapter 12). Together with the Dog and the Snake, the Cat becomes the young man's faithful friend after he spends his inheritance to save them from being killed (012-001). When the charmed ring is stolen by an [[supernatural-beings|ogres]]s and the hero's wife and house vanish, the Cat proves to be the most intelligent and resourceful of the companions, devising and executing a daring plan to recover the ring — even retrieving it twice after the Dog carelessly drops it (012-018–012-031).

## Role in the Tale

### Rescue and Companionship

The Cat is first found by the merchant's son among people quarrelling over whether to kill it. The young man pays one hundred rupees to save its life (012-001). The Cat, along with the Dog and the Snake, becomes his constant companion, sleeping by his feet at night and following him during the day (012-004).

### The Quest for the Ring

When the [[supernatural-beings|ogres]]s steals the ring and the hero's wife and house vanish, the Cat and Dog volunteer to recover them (012-016). The Cat takes the lead throughout the quest:

> "We may have some difficulty here. Look, the king has taken our master's wife and house for himself. You stay here. I will go to the house and try to see her." (012-018)

The Cat climbs into the princess's room, learns from her that the ring is in the [[supernatural-beings|ogres]]s's stomach, and promptly forms a plan (012-021–012-023).

### The Rat's Wedding

The Cat's most clever stratagem involves the rat community. Lying pretended-dead by a rat-hole during a rat prince's wedding, the Cat seizes the bridegroom and demands the ring as ransom (012-024):

> "Not unless you do something for me. Listen. The ogress, who lives in that house with the prince and his wife, has swallowed a ring, which I very much want. If you will procure it for me, I will allow the rat to depart unharmed." (012-024)

The rats comply: at midnight, one rat inserts its tail into the sleeping [[supernatural-beings|ogres]]s's throat, causing her to cough up the ring (012-026).

### Recovering the Ring — Twice

The Cat's patience is tested twice by the Dog's carelessness. First, the Dog drops the ring into a stream where a fish swallows it. The Cat hides inside a dead lamb to catch a nadhar bird, which then forces the king of fishes to find the ring (012-028–012-031). [[the-dog|The Dog]] then drops the ring a second time, and a kite carries it off. The Cat climbs the tree, kills the kite, and recovers the ring once more (012-031). Finally, the Cat delivers the ring safely to their master, restoring his wife and house (012-031).

## Key Deeds

- **Loyalty**: Accompanies the merchant's son throughout his adventures and volunteers for the dangerous ring-recovery quest (012-004, 012-016).
- **Intelligence**: Devises the entire plan — from infiltrating the palace to coercing the rats — and adapts when the plan goes awry (012-018–012-024).
- **Persistence**: Recovers the ring three times (from the [[supernatural-beings|ogres]]s, from the fish, and from the kite), never giving up despite the Dog's repeated failures (012-026, 012-031).
- **Leadership**: Directs the Dog throughout the quest, giving clear instructions and solving problems as they arise (012-018, 012-028, 012-031).

## Relationships

- **[[the-dog|The Dog]]** (the-dog) — Fellow companion of the merchant's son. [[the-dog|The Dog]] helps the Cat but is jealous and careless, twice dropping the ring and causing delays. The Cat forgives the Dog each time and continues their quest (012-027–012-031).
- **[[the-snake|The Snake]]** (the-snake) — The third animal companion, actually the son of [[king|Raja]] Indrasha, who rewards the merchant's son with the charmed ring (012-005–012-012).
- **[[the-merchant|The Merchant]]'s Son** — The Cat's master, who saved its life for one hundred rupees. The Cat repays this kindness many times over through its loyalty and cleverness (012-001, 012-016).
- **The Ogress** — The antagonist from whom the Cat must recover the ring. The Cat outwits her by enlisting the rats (012-023–012-026).
- **[[the-rat|The Rat]] King** — Allied through coercion: the Cat seizes the rat prince during his wedding and demands the ring as ransom (012-024–012-026).

## Cultural Significance

The Cat in this tale exemplifies the helper-animal motif common in Indian folklore: creatures saved by a hero repay the debt through service and cleverness. The Cat's intelligence and resourcefulness form the core of the tale's second half, transforming what could be a simple loss-and-recovery story into an elaborate chain of animal alliances — cats coercing rats, cats catching birds, birds commanding fish — that [[supernatural-beings|demons]]trates the interconnectedness of the natural world in folk narrative.
