## Overview

Sun is the eldest child of Star and a central figure in the etiological tale "How Sun, [[moon|Moon]], and [[wind|Wind]] Went Out to Dinner" (027-001-027-009). Along with his siblings [[moon|Moon]] and [[wind|Wind]], Sun goes to dine with their uncle and aunt Thunder and Lightning. His selfishness — [[food-and-feasting|feasting]] without bringing anything back for his waiting mother — earns him a curse that explains why the sun's rays are hot and scorching to this day (027-004-027-005).

## Role in the Tale

Sun, [[moon|Moon]], and [[wind|Wind]] are invited to dine with their uncle and aunt Thunder and Lightning. Their mother, Star (one of the most distant stars in the sky), waits alone at home for her children's return (027-001). Sun, being both [[greed-in-folklore|greedy]] and selfish, enjoys the feast without any thought of saving anything for his mother (027-002). In contrast, the gentle [[moon|Moon]] secretly places a portion of every dish under her long fingernails to share with Star (027-002).

When the children return, Star asks what they have brought. Sun boldly declares: "I have brought nothing home for you. I went out to enjoy myself with my friends — not to fetch a dinner for my mother!" (027-003). Enraged by this ingratitude, Star curses Sun: from that moment, his rays shall be "ever hot and scorching, and shall burn all that they touch," and people shall hate him and cover their heads when he appears (027-004). The tale notes that this is why the sun is so hot to this day (027-005).

## Key Deeds

- **Feasting without thought**: Sun enjoys the grand feast at Thunder and Lightning's home without saving any food for his mother (027-002).
- **Bold selfishness**: When asked what he brought back, Sun arrogantly declares he went out for his own pleasure, not to fetch dinner for his mother (027-003).
- **Receiving the curse**: Star's curse transforms Sun's nature, making his rays forever scorching as punishment for his ingratitude (027-004).

## Relationships

- **Star** — Sun's mother, a distant star who waits alone for her children while they feast. She curses Sun for his selfishness (027-001, 027-004).
- **[[moon|Moon]]** — Sun's sister, who contrasts sharply with him by saving portions of every dish for their mother. She is blessed with cool, calm, beautiful light (027-002, 027-003, 027-008).
- **[[wind|Wind]]** — Sun's sibling, equally [[greed-in-folklore|greedy]] and selfish, who is similarly cursed to "parch and shrivel all living things" (027-002, 027-003, 027-006).
- **Thunder and Lightning** — Sun's uncle and aunt who host the feast that sets the story in motion (027-001).

## Moral and Cultural Significance

Sun in this tale is an etiological figure — his character and punishment explain a natural phenomenon (why the sun is hot) through narrative. The story belongs to the universal genre of "origin myths" found across cultures, where the characteristics of celestial bodies are explained through family drama. The moral is explicitly about filial piety: Sun's failure to remember his mother results in a permanent alteration of his nature. The tale also establishes a cosmic hierarchy of virtue, where [[moon|Moon]] (the dutiful daughter) is blessed with pleasant light while Sun and [[wind|Wind]] (the selfish sons) are cursed with harsh, damaging qualities.

> "Because you went out to amuse yourself with your friends, and feasted and enjoyed yourself, without any thought of your mother at home — you shall be cursed. Henceforth, your rays shall ever be hot and scorching." (027-004)
