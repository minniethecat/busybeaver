## Overview

The Rat community plays a brief but crucial role in "The Charmed Ring" (Chapter 12). When the Cat needs to recover the charmed ring from the stomach of a sleeping [[supernatural-beings|ogres]]s, she lies pretended-dead by a rat-hole where the rat prince's wedding is taking place. Seizing the bridegroom, the Cat forces the rats to retrieve the ring as ransom — a task they accomplish by having one rat insert its tail into the [[supernatural-beings|ogres]]s's throat at midnight, causing her to cough up the precious object (012-024–012-026).

## Role in the Tale

### The Wedding

The rats appear at a critical juncture in the quest. The charmed ring has been swallowed by an [[supernatural-beings|ogres]]s, and the Cat needs a way to retrieve it from inside her stomach. Conveniently, "a great wedding chanced to be going on among the rat community of that place, and all the rats of the neighbourhood were assembled in that one particular mine by which the cat had lain down" (012-024). The eldest son of the king of the rats is about to be married, and the entire community pours forth from the hole "squealing and jumping in honour of the occasion" (012-024).

### The Capture

[[the-cat|The Cat]] seizes the opportunity — and the bridegroom:

> Consequently, when the procession poured forth from the hole squealing and jumping in honour of the occasion, it immediately spotted the bridegroom and pounced down on him. (012-024)

The terrified rat and his entire community beg for his release, but the Cat makes a hard bargain: the ring in exchange for the prince's life.

### The Midnight Mission

The rats accept the terms with remarkable boldness:

> "Very well, we agree," said they all. "Nay, if we do not get the ring for you, devour us all." (012-024)

At midnight, their plan is executed. One rat climbs onto the sleeping [[supernatural-beings|ogres]]s's face and inserts its tail into her throat. The [[supernatural-beings|ogres]]s coughs violently, and the ring rolls onto the floor:

> At midnight, when the ogress was sound asleep, one of the rats went to her bedside, climbed up on her face, and inserted its tail into her throat; whereupon the ogress coughed violently, and the ring came out and rolled on to the floor. (012-026)

The rat immediately seizes the ring and delivers it to the Rat King, who in turn gives it to the Cat, securing the release of his son (012-026).

## Key Deeds

- **Collective Bravery**: The entire rat community agrees to the dangerous mission rather than abandon their prince (012-024).
- **The Midnight Theft**: A single rat executes the delicate task of extracting the ring from the [[supernatural-beings|ogres]]s's stomach, requiring stealth and precision (012-026).
- **Honoring the Bargain**: The Rat King promptly delivers the ring to the Cat upon its recovery, keeping the community's word (012-026).

## Relationships

- **The Rat Prince** — The bridegroom whose capture by the Cat triggers the entire rat community's involvement in the quest. His life is the stakes of the bargain (012-024).
- **The Rat King** — The father of the bridegroom, who organizes the recovery effort and delivers the ring to the Cat (012-024, 012-026).
- **[[the-cat|The Cat]]** (the-cat) — The antagonist-turned-ally who seizes the Rat Prince and coerces the community into helping. The relationship is purely transactional, but the terms are honored by both sides (012-024–012-026).
- **The Ogress** — The unwitting victim of the rats' midnight operation; she sleeps through the extraction of the ring from her stomach (012-026).

## Cultural Significance

The rat episode exemplifies the chain-of-alliances motif in Indian folklore, where even the smallest creatures can play decisive roles in a hero's quest. The rats' bold offer — "if we do not get the ring for you, devour us all" — adds a note of comic bravado to the tale. Their midnight infiltration of the [[supernatural-beings|ogres]]s's bedroom, with a rat's tail down a throat, is one of the story's most vividly physical and humorous images, connecting the tale to the broader tradition of animal [[trickster-figure|trickster]] stories where small, weak creatures use cleverness and stealth to defeat larger, more powerful adversaries.
