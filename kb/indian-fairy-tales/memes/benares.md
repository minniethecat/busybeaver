# Benares

## Location

Benares is a great royal city of ancient northern India and the most frequently named city in the collection. In the tales it is the seat of a kingdom ruled by King Brahma-datta (001-001), and it functions as a fixed point on the map of the story-world: characters journey *to* it and *from* it along established routes. In [[the-soothsayers-son]] the hero sets out for Benares and reaches it, living there "for more than twelve years" (010-003, 010-008), while the city of [[ujjain|Ujjaini]] is placed "twenty kas to the south" of a point on the same road (010-007). In [[the-demon-with-the-matted-hair]] the Bodhisatta, having completed his education at Takkasila, "began his journey to Benares" (025-010), confirming Benares as the southern terminus of the great northern highway from Takkasila (Taxila). Benares corresponds to the modern city of Varanasi on the Ganges in Uttar Pradesh.

## Appearances in Tales

- [[the-lion-and-the-crane]] — The tale opens by establishing that "[[king-brahmadatta|Brahmadatta]] was at that time reigning in Benares" while the Bodhisatta was born as a white crane in the [[the-himalayas|Himavanta]] region (001-001).
- [[the-talkative-tortoise]] — Set when "Brahma-datta was reigning in Benares"; the Bodhisatta is born there and becomes the king's minister, and the action reaches "[[the-palace|the king's palace]] in the city of Benares" (013-001, 013-009).
- [[a-lesson-for-kings]] — "While Brahma-datta was reigning in Benares, the [[bodhisatta|future Buddha]] returned to life" as his son; the tale repeatedly refers to "the king of Benares" and "the kingdom of Benares" ruled by Brahma-datta, culminating when the crowd "made way for the king of Benares" (017-001, 017-008, 017-031).
- [[the-ass-in-the-lions-skin]] — Opens with the formula "While Brahma-datta was reigning in Benares, the [[bodhisatta|future Buddha]] was born" into a farming family (020-001).
- [[the-demon-with-the-matted-hair]] — "When [[king-brahmadatta|Brahmadatta]] was King of Benares, the Bodhisatta was born" as his son; after study at Takkasila the hero travels "on to Benares, armed with his five weapons" (025-004, 025-024).
- [[the-soothsayers-son]] — The hero's elder brother "started for Benares" and the hero reaches it and settles there (010-003, 010-008), later returning past it (010-024).

## Significance

Benares is the connective tissue of the Jataka material in this collection. The recurring opening formula — some version of "while Brahma-datta was reigning in Benares, the [[bodhisatta|future Buddha]] was born" — signals to the reader that a Buddhist birth-story is beginning and anchors the tale in a shared, semi-historical setting (013-001, 017-001, 020-001, 025-004). Because [[king-brahmadatta|King Brahmadatta]] rules Benares in each of these tales, the city serves as a stable royal court against which the Bodhisatta's many incarnations — crane, minister's son, prince, farmer's child — play out their moral lessons (001-001, 025-004). In [[the-soothsayers-son]], a non-Jataka tale, Benares instead functions as a real destination and place of residence, showing that its authority as a landmark extends beyond the Buddhist stories (010-008).

## Cultural or Historical Notes

Benares — modern Varanasi, on the banks of the Ganges — is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world and among the holiest sites in Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism. In the classical Jataka tradition of Pali Buddhist literature, an enormous proportion of the birth-stories open with the stock phrase that a king named [[king-brahmadatta|Brahmadatta]] was reigning in Benares (Baranasi), which established the setting so conventionally that it became a genre marker rather than a specific historical reference. The tales in this collection preserve that convention faithfully, and their use of Benares as the Bodhisatta's habitual birthplace reflects the city's real status as a center of learning and religion in ancient India.
