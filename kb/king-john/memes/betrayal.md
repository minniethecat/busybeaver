## Overview

Betrayal is the primary motor of political action in *[[king-john|King John]]*. Nearly every major character betrays or is betrayed: [[king-philip-ii|King Philip]] abandons [[arthur|Arthur]]'s cause for a marriage alliance; [[king-john|King John]] orders the death of his nephew; the English lords [[rebellion|revolt]] against their king; [[lewis-the-dauphin|Lewis the Dauphin]] plans to execute the English lords who helped him. (001-197) The [[commodity|commodity]] soliloquy provides the philosophical diagnosis — [[commodity|self-interest]] drives every betrayal (004-180) — but the play's relentless accumulation of broken faiths creates a political world in which [[faith|loyalty]] is always provisional. (004-215)
