# Anjou

## Location Overview

French territory contested between [[england|England]] and [[france|France]], part of the Angevin inheritance disputed in the play. (003-024)

## Role in the Play

### Strategic Significance

Anjou serves as a key location in the play's geography of [[war|conflict]]. Its position within the french sphere of influence shapes the military and political dynamics that drive the action.

### Dramatic Function

The setting of Anjou provides the physical space for encounters that define the play's central themes — legitimacy contested, alliances forged and broken, loyalties tested.

### Associated Characters

Anjou is most closely associated with the characters whose fates are tied to it, and the location's significance is inseparable from their stories. (002-004)

> You louing men of Angiers, Arthurs subiects,
> Our Trumpet call'd you to this gentle parle.
>
> (003-081)

## Historical Background

Anjou was the ancestral homeland of the Plantagenet dynasty — the name "Angevin" derives from Anjou. Henry II, John's father, inherited Anjou through his father Geoffrey Plantagenet, making it the geographic heart of what became the Angevin Empire. In the play, the territory's contested status reflects the collapse of that empire under John's reign. Chatillon's opening embassy lists "Aniowe" among the lands claimed by [[arthur|Arthur]] in right of his father Geoffrey, John's deceased elder brother (001-087).

The capital city of Anjou, [[angiers|Angiers]], serves as the setting for the pivotal siege and diplomatic confrontation of Act 2, where the citizens of Angiers force [[king-john|King John]] and [[king-philip-ii|King Philip]] into a temporary alliance sealed by the [[marriage-of-lewis-and-blanche|marriage of Lewis and Blanche]] (003-081). By Act 4, John surrenders Anjou — "Poyctiers and Aniow" — as part of the five provinces gifted to the Dauphin to secure French recognition of his crown (004-138). The trajectory of Anjou across the play, from claimed inheritance to diplomatic gift, mirrors the wider collapse of Plantagenet power under John.
