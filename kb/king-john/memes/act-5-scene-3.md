## Section 10

[010-001] Sal. Vpon our sides it neuer shall be broken.

[010-002] And Noble Dolphin, albeit we sweare

[010-003] A voluntary zeale, and an vn-urg'd [[faith|Faith]]

[010-004] To your proceedings: yet beleeue me Prince,

[010-005] I am not glad that such a sore of Time

[010-006] Should seeke a plaster by contemn'd reuolt,

[010-007] And heale the inueterate Canker of one wound,

[010-008] By making many: Oh it grieues my soule,

[010-009] That I must draw this mettle from my side

[010-010] To be a widdow-maker: oh, and there

[010-011] Where [[honour|honourable]] rescue, and defence

[010-012] Cries out vpon the name of [[salisbury|Salisbury]].

[010-013] But such is the infection of the time,

[010-014] That for the health and Physicke of our right,

[010-015] We cannot deale but with the very hand

[010-016] Of sterne Iniustice, and confused wrong:

[010-017] And is't not pitty, (oh my grieued friends)

[010-018] That we, the sonnes and children of this Isle,

[010-019] Was borne to see so sad an houre as this,

[010-020] Wherein we step after a stranger, march

[010-021] Vpon her gentle bosom, and fill vp

[010-022] Her Enemies rankes? I must withdraw, and weepe

[010-023] Vpon the spot of this inforced cause,

[010-024] To grace the Gentry of a Land remote,

[010-025] And follow vnacquainted colours heere:

[010-026] What heere? O Nation that thou couldst remoue,

[010-027] That Neptunes Armes who clippeth thee about,

[010-028] Would beare thee from the knowledge of thy selfe,

[010-029] And cripple thee vnto a Pagan shore,

[010-030] Where these two Christian Armies [[power|might]] combine

[010-031] The bloud of malice, in a vaine of league,

[010-032] And not to spend it so vn-neighbourly

[010-033] Dolph. A noble temper dost thou shew in this,

[010-034] And great affections wrastling in thy bosome

[010-035] Doth make an earth-quake of Nobility:

[010-036] Oh, what a noble [[war|combat]] hast fought

[010-037] Between compulsion, and a braue respect:

[010-038] Let me wipe off this honourable dewe,

[010-039] That siluerly doth progresse on thy cheekes:

[010-040] My heart hath melted at a Ladies teares,

[010-041] Being an ordinary Inundation:

[010-042] But this effusion of such manly drops,

[010-043] This showre, blowne vp by tempest of the soule,

[010-044] Startles mine eyes, and makes me more amaz'd

[010-045] Then had I seene the vaultie top of heauen

[010-046] Figur'd quite ore with burning Meteors.

[010-047] Lift vp thy brow (renowned Salisburie)

[010-048] And with a great heart heaue away this storme:

[010-049] Commend these waters to those baby-eyes

[010-050] That neuer saw the giant-world enrag'd,

[010-051] Nor met with [[fortune|Fortune]], other then at feasts,

[010-052] Full warm of blood, of mirth, of gossipping:

[010-053] Come, come; for thou shalt thrust thy hand as deepe

[010-054] Into the purse of rich prosperity

[010-055] As Lewis himselfe: so (Nobles) shall you all,

[010-056] That knit your sinewes to the strength of mine.

[010-057] Enter Pandulpho.

[010-058] And euen there, methinkes an Angell spake,

[010-059] Looke where the holy Legate comes apace,

[010-060] To giue vs warrant from the hand of heauen,

[010-061] And on our actions set the name of right

[010-062] With holy breath

[010-063] Pand. Haile noble Prince of [[france|France]]:

[010-064] The next is this: King Iohn hath reconcil'd

[010-065] Himselfe to [[rome|Rome]], his spirit is come in,

[010-066] That so stood out against the holy Church,

[010-067] The great Metropolis and Sea of Rome:

[010-068] Therefore thy threatning Colours now winde vp,

[010-069] And tame the sauage spirit of wilde warre,

[010-070] That like a Lion fostered vp at hand,

[010-071] It may lie gently at the foot of peace,

[010-072] And be no further harmefull then in shewe

[010-073] Dol. Your [[grace|Grace]] shall pardon me, I will not backe:

[010-074] I am too high-borne to be proportied

[010-075] To be a secondary at controll,

[010-076] Or vsefull seruing-man, and Instrument

[010-077] To any Soueraigne State throughout the world.

[010-078] Your breath first kindled the dead coale of warres,

[010-079] Betweene this chastiz'd kingdome and my selfe,

[010-080] And brought in matter that should feed this fire;

[010-081] And now 'tis farre too huge to be blowne out

[010-082] With that same weake winde, which enkindled it:

[010-083] You taught me how to know the face of right,

[010-084] Acquainted me with interest to this Land,

[010-085] Yea, thrust this enterprize into my heart,

[010-086] And come ye now to tell me Iohn hath made

[010-087] His peace with Rome? what is that peace to me?

[010-088] I (by the honour of my marriage bed)

[010-089] After yong [[arthur|Arthur]], claime this Land for mine,

[010-090] And now it is halfe conquer'd, must I backe,

[010-091] Because that Iohn hath made his peace with Rome?

[010-092] Am I Romes slaue? What penny hath Rome borne?

[010-093] What men prouided? What munition sent

[010-094] To vnder-prop this Action? Is't not I

[010-095] That vnder-goe this charge? Who else but I,

[010-096] And such as to my claime are liable,

[010-097] Sweat in this businesse, and maintaine this warre?

[010-098] Haue I not heard these Islanders shout out

[010-099] Viue le Roy, as I haue bank'd their Townes?

[010-100] Haue I not heere the best Cards for the game

[010-101] To winne this easie match, plaid for a Crowne?

[010-102] And shall I now giue ore the yeelded Set?

[010-103] No, no, on my soule it neuer shall be said

[010-104] Pand. You looke but on the out-side of this worke

[010-105] Dol. Out-side or in-side, I will not returne

[010-106] Till my attempt so much be glorified,

[010-107] As to my ample hope was promised,

[010-108] Before I drew this gallant head of warre,

[010-109] And cull'd these fiery spirits from the world

[010-110] To out-looke Conquest, and to winne renowne

[010-111] Euen in the iawes of danger, and of death:

[010-112] What lusty Trumpet thus doth summon vs?

[010-113] Enter Bastard.

[010-114] Bast. According to the faire-play of the world,

[010-115] Let me haue audience: I am sent to speake:

[010-116] My holy Lord of Millane, from the King

[010-117] I come to learne how you haue dealt for him:

[010-118] And, as you answer, I doe know the scope

[010-119] And warrant limited vnto my tongue

[010-120] Pand. The Dolphin is too wilfull opposite

[010-121] And will not temporize with my intreaties:

[010-122] He flatly saies, hee'll not lay downe his Armes

[010-123] Bast. By all the bloud that euer fury breath'd,

[010-124] The youth saies well. Now heare our English King,

[010-125] For thus his Royaltie doth speake in me:

[010-126] He is prepar'd, and reason to he should,

[010-127] This apish and vnmannerly approach,

[010-128] This harness'd Maske, and vnaduised Reuell,

[010-129] This vn-heard sawcinesse and boyish Troopes,

[010-130] The King doth smile at, and is well prepar'd

[010-131] To whip this dwarfish warre, this Pigmy Armes

[010-132] From out the circle of his Territories.

[010-133] That hand which had the strength, euen at your dore,

[010-134] To cudgell you, and make you take the hatch,

[010-135] To diue like Buckets in concealed Welles,

[010-136] To crowch in litter of your stable plankes,

[010-137] To lye like pawnes, lock'd vp in chests and truncks,

[010-138] To hug with swine, to seeke sweet safety out

[010-139] In vaults and prisons, and to thrill and shake,

[010-140] Euen at the crying of your Nations crow,

[010-141] Thinking this voyce an armed Englishman.

[010-142] Shall that victorious hand be feebled heere,

[010-143] That in your Chambers gaue you chasticement?

[010-144] No: know the gallant Monarch is in Armes,

[010-145] And like an Eagle, o're his ayerie towres,

[010-146] To sowsse annoyance that comes neere his Nest;

[010-147] And you degenerate, you ingrate Reuolts,

[010-148] You bloudy Nero's, ripping vp the wombe

[010-149] Of your deere Mother-[[england|England]]: blush for shame:

[010-150] For your owne Ladies, and pale-visag'd Maides,

[010-151] Like Amazons, come tripping after drummes:

[010-152] Their thimbles into armed Gantlets change,

[010-153] Their Needl's to Lances, and their gentle hearts

[010-154] To fierce and bloody inclination

[010-155] Dol. There end thy braue, and turn thy face in peace,

[010-156] We grant thou canst out-scold vs: Far thee well,

[010-157] We hold our time too precious to be spent

[010-158] with such a brabler

[010-159] Pan. Giue me leaue to speake

[010-160] Bast. No, I will speake

[010-161] Dol. We will attend to neyther:

[010-162] Strike vp the drummes, and let the tongue of warre

[010-163] Pleade for our interest, and our being heere

[010-164] Bast. Indeede your drums being beaten, wil cry out;

[010-165] And so shall you, being beaten: Do but start

[010-166] An eccho with the clamor of thy drumme,

[010-167] And euen at hand, a drumme is readie brac'd,

[010-168] That shall reuerberate all, as lowd as thine.

[010-169] Sound but another, and another shall

[010-170] (As lowd as thine) rattle the Welkins eare,

[010-171] And mocke the deepe mouth'd Thunder: for at hand

[010-172] (Not trusting to this halting Legate heere,

[010-173] Whom he hath vs'd rather for sport, then neede)

[010-174] Is warlike Iohn: and in his fore-head sits

[010-175] A bare-rib'd death, whose office is this day

[010-176] To feast vpon whole thousands of the French

[010-177] Dol. Strike vp our drummes, to finde this danger out

[010-178] Bast. And thou shalt finde it (Dolphin) do not doubt

[010-179] Exeunt.

[010-180] Scaena Tertia.

[010-181] Alarums. Enter Iohn and [[hubert|Hubert]].

[010-182] Iohn. How goes the day with vs? oh tell me Hubert

[010-183] Hub. Badly I feare; how fares your Maiesty?

[010-184] Iohn. This Feauer that hath troubled me so long,

[010-185] Lyes heauie on me: oh, my heart is sicke.

[010-186] Enter a Messenger.

[010-187] Mes. My Lord: your valiant kinsman Falconbridge,

[010-188] Desires your Maiestie to leaue the field,

[010-189] And send him word by me, which way you go

[010-190] Iohn. Tell him toward Swinsted, to the Abbey there

[010-191] Mes. Be of good comfort: for the great supply

[010-192] That was expected by the Dolphin heere,

[010-193] Are wrack'd three nights ago on [[goodwin-sands|Goodwin sands]].

[010-194] This newes was brought to Richard but euen now,

[010-195] The French fight coldly, and retyre themselues

[010-196] Iohn. Aye me, this tyrant Feauer burnes mee vp,

[010-197] And will not let me welcome this good newes.

[010-198] Set on toward Swinsted: to my Litter straight,

[010-199] Weaknesse possesseth me, and I am faint.

[010-200] Exeunt.

[010-201] Scena Quarta.

[010-202] Enter Salisbury, [[pembroke|Pembroke]], and [[bigot|Bigot]].

[010-203] Sal. I did not thinke the King so stor'd with friends

[010-204] Pem. Vp once againe: put spirit in the French,

[010-205] If they miscarry: we miscarry too

[010-206] Sal. That misbegotten diuell Falconbridge,

[010-207] In spight of spight, alone vpholds the day

[010-208] Pem. They say King Iohn sore sick, hath left the field.

[010-209] Enter Meloon wounded.

[010-210] Mel. Lead me to the Reuolts of England heere

[010-211] Sal. When we were happie, we had other names

[010-212] Pem. It is the Count Meloone

[010-213] Sal. Wounded to death

[010-214] Mel. Fly Noble English, you are bought and sold,

[010-215] Vnthred the rude eye of [[rebellion|Rebellion]],

[010-216] And welcome home againe discarded faith,

[010-217] Seeke out King Iohn, and fall before his feete:

[010-218] For if the French be Lords of this loud day,

[010-219] He meanes to recompence the paines you take,

[010-220] By cutting off your heads: Thus hath he sworne,

[010-221] And I with him, and many moe with mee,

[010-222] Vpon the Altar at S[aint]. Edmondsbury,

[010-223] Euen on that Altar, where we swore to you

[010-224] Deere Amity, and euerlasting loue

[010-225] Sal. May this be possible? May this be true?

[010-226] Mel. Haue I not hideous death within my view,

[010-227] Retaining but a quantity of life,

[010-228] Which bleeds away, euen as a forme of waxe

[010-229] Resolueth from his figure 'gainst the fire?

[010-230] What in the world should make me now deceiue,

[010-231] Since I must loose the vse of all deceite?

[010-232] Why should I then be false, since it is true

[010-233] That I must dye heere, and liue hence, by [[truth|Truth]]?

[010-234] I say againe, if Lewis do win the day,

[010-235] He is forsworne, if ere those eyes of yours

[010-236] Behold another day breake in the East:

[010-237] But euen this night, whose blacke contagious breath

[010-238] Already smoakes about the burning Crest

[010-239] Of the old, feeble, and day-wearied Sunne,

[010-240] Euen this ill night, your breathing shall expire,

[010-241] Paying the fine of rated Treachery,

[010-242] Euen with a treacherous fine of all your liues:

[010-243] If Lewis, by your assistance win the day.

[010-244] Commend me to one Hubert, with your King;

[010-245] The loue of him, and this respect besides

[010-246] (For that my Grandsire was an Englishman)

[010-247] Awakes my Conscience to confesse all this.

[010-248] In lieu whereof, I pray you beare me hence

[010-249] From forth the noise and rumour of the Field;

[010-250] Where I may thinke the remnant of my thoughts

[010-251] In peace: and part this bodie and my soule

[010-252] With contemplation, and deuout desires

[010-253] Sal. We do beleeue thee, and beshrew my soule,

[010-254] But I do loue the fauour, and the forme

[010-255] Of this most faire occasion, by the which

[010-256] We will vntread the steps of damned flight,

[010-257] And like a bated and retired Flood,

[010-258] Leauing our ranknesse and irregular course,

[010-259] Stoope lowe within those bounds we haue ore-look'd,

[010-260] And calmely run on in obedience

[010-261] Euen to our Ocean, to our great King Iohn.

[010-262] My arme shall giue thee helpe to beare thee hence,

[010-263] For I do see the cruell pangs of death

[010-264] Right in thine eye. Away, my friends, new flight,

[010-265] And happie newnesse, that intends old right.
