# Austria

## Character Overview

The Duke of Austria, a French ally introduced at the siege of Angiers (002-128), whose death at [[philip-the-bastard|the Bastard]]'s hands (006-055) is one of the play's most satisfying moments.

During the siege of [[angiers|Angiers]], Austria and the French king plan a joint artillery assault on the city. [[philip-the-bastard|the Bastard]], ever the cynic, observes that their cannon will fire directly into one another's lines:

> O prudent discipline! From North to South:
> Austria and France shoot in each others mouth.
>
> (004-019)

## Role in the Play

### Political Role

As a member of the french [[truth|fact]]ion, Austria participates in the play's central [[war|conflict]]s of legitimacy and [[faith|loyalty]]. The character's actions reflect the broader political dynamics of alliance, betrayal, and the cost of choosing sides in a contested kingdom.

### Dramatic Significance

Austria contributes to the play's exploration of how individuals are caught in the machinery of political [[war|conflict]]. After the breaking of the league between [[king-john|John]] and [[king-philip-ii|Philip]], [[constance|Lady Constance]] unleashes a furious condemnation upon Austria for his complicity in the betrayal of her son [[arthur|Arthur]]'s cause:

> War, war, no peace, peace is to me a warre:
> O Lymoges, O Austria, thou dost shame
> That bloudy spoyle: thou slaue, thou wretch, y coward,
> Thou little valiant, great in villanie,
> Thou euer strong vpon the stronger side;
>
> (005-077)

The Bastard later kills Austria in battle and takes his head as a trophy, declaring "Austrias head lye there" (006-058), a moment of grim satisfaction that punctuates the character's role.

### Key Scenes

Austria appears at crucial moments in the play's action, and the character's choices — or failures to choose — shape the trajectory of the larger political drama that unfolds across the five acts.
