# Blanche of Castile

## Character Overview

Princess of Castile, married to Lewis as a political pawn (002-192), whose tragedy is taking the marriage seriously when no one else does.

[[king-john|King John]] offers Blanche to [[lewis-the-dauphin|Lewis the Dauphin]] as part of the peace settlement before [[angiers|Angiers]]. His speech praising her qualities is a masterclass in political rhetoric — beauty, virtue, and birth are all invoked, not for Blanche's sake but to make the territorial deal palatable:

> If lustie loue should go in quest of beautie,
> Where should he finde it fairer, then in Blanch:
> If zealous loue should go in search of vertue,
> Where should he finde it purer then in Blanch?
> If loue ambitious, sought a match of birth,
> Whose veines bound richer blood then Lady Blanch?
>
> (004-028)
