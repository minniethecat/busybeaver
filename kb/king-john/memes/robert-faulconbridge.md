# Robert Faulconbridge

## Character Overview

The legitimate son of [[sir-robert-faulconbridge|Sir Robert Faulconbridge]], whose inheritance claim triggers the revelation of [[philip-the-bastard|the Bastard]]'s royal parentage. He enters with his brother Philip in the play's opening act to dispute their father's estate (001-128), and his accusation — that Philip is not their father's true son — sets in motion the revelation of the Bastard's Plantagenet lineage (001-131).

[[king-john|King John]]'s first question cuts to the legal heart of the dispute, while [[philip-the-bastard|Philip]]'s response already hints at the wit that will define his character:

> K.Iohn. Is that the elder, and art thou the heyre?
> You came not of one mother then it seemes
>
> Philip. Most certain of one mother, mighty King,
> That is well knowne, and as I thinke one father:
> But for the certaine knowledge of that truth,
> I put you o're to heauen, and to my mother;
> Of that I doubt, as all mens children may
>
> (001-136)
