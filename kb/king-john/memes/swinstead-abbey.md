# Swinstead Abbey

## Location Overview

A religious house where [[king-john|King John]] is poisoned and dies, marking the play's final location and the site of [[king-john|John]]'s decline. The abbey (spelled "Swinsted" in the Folio text) is first mentioned when John, burning with fever, directs his attendants to carry him there: "Tell him toward Swinsted, to the Abbey there" (010-190).

> Iohn. Tell him toward Swinsted, to the Abbey there
>
> Mes. Be of good comfort: for the great supply
> That was expected by the Dolphin heere,
> Are wrack'd three nights ago on Goodwin sands.
>
> Iohn. Aye me, this tyrant Feauer burnes mee vp,
> And will not let me welcome this good newes.
> Set on toward Swinsted: to my Litter straight,
> Weaknesse possesseth me, and I am faint.
>
> (010-190–199)

## Role in the Play

### Dramatic Function

Swinstead Abbey is the unseen location to which [[king-john|John]] retreats as his body fails him. Unlike the battlefield of [[angiers|Angiers]] or the court at [[northampton|Northampton]], the abbey is a place of withdrawal and diminishment — not a seat of power but a sickroom. It is here that John is poisoned by a monk, and here that he dies, his final moments reported to [[prince-henry|Prince Henry]] in the orchard.

### Thematic Significance

The choice of a religious house as the site of John's death is thematically loaded. Throughout the play, John has defied [[the-papacy|the Papacy]], been [[johns-excommunication|excommunicated]], and seized Church property — the Bastard reports "the summes I haue collected among the Clergy men" (008-121). That John dies in an abbey, poisoned by a monk, suggests a form of poetic justice: the Church he plundered becomes the instrument of his end.

### Associated Characters

Swinstead Abbey is most closely associated with [[king-john|King John]], whose final journey there marks his physical and political decline. [[pembroke|Pembroke]] reports on John's condition from the abbey, and [[philip-the-bastard|the Bastard]], returning from battle, learns of his king's death at this place.
