# Bigot

## Character Overview

An English lord who joins the rebellion against [[king-john|John]], completing the trio of defecting nobles.

## Role in the Play

### Political Role

As a member of the english [[truth|fact]]ion, Bigot participates in the play's central [[war|conflict]]s of legitimacy and [[faith|loyalty]]. The character's actions reflect the broader political dynamics of alliance, betrayal, and the cost of choosing sides in a contested kingdom.

### Dramatic Significance

Bigot contributes to the play's exploration of how individuals are caught in the machinery of political [[war|conflict]]. Whether through action or inaction, the character illuminates the play's central question: what is the relationship between personal [[faith|loyalty]] and political necessity?

### Key Scenes

Bigot appears at crucial moments in the play's action, and the character's choices — or failures to choose — shape the trajectory of the larger political drama that unfolds across the five acts.
