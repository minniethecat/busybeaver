# Maine

## Location Overview

French territory disputed between the English and French crowns, listed among the continental holdings claimed by [[arthur|Arthur]]'s supporters (001-087).

## Role in the Play

### Strategic Significance

Maine serves as a key location in the play's geography of [[war|conflict]]. The Dauphin [[lewis-the-dauphin|Lewis]] formally claims "Angiers, Toraine, Maine" in right of [[arthur|Arthur]] (003-024), and the territory is later named among the spoils of the French-English peace negotiation (004-093).

> England and Ireland, Angiers, Toraine, Maine,
> In right of Arthur doe I claime of thee:
> Wilt thou resigne them, and lay downe thy Armes?
>
> (003-024)

### Dramatic Function

The setting of Maine provides the physical space for encounters that define the play's central themes — legitimacy contested, alliances forged and broken, loyalties tested.

### Associated Characters

Maine is most closely associated with the characters whose fates are tied to the contested Angevin territories, and the location's significance is inseparable from their stories.

## Diplomatic Currency

Maine functions in the play as diplomatic currency — a territory whose ownership shifts with every political wind. In the opening embassy, Chatillon names Maine among the continental holdings that [[arthur|Arthur]] claims through his father Geoffrey, John's elder brother (001-087). The Dauphin [[lewis-the-dauphin|Lewis]] repeats the demand more aggressively before [[angiers|Angiers]]: "England and Ireland, Angiers, Toraine, Maine, / In right of Arthur doe I claime of thee" (003-024–025). John's defiant reply — "My life as soone: I doe defie thee France" — sets the military confrontation of Act 2 in motion (003-026).

By Act 4, Maine has become a bargaining chip rather than a war aim. John, seeking to purchase peace with [[france|France]], grants "Volquessen, Toraine, Maine, / Poyctiers and Aniow, these fiue Prouinces" to the Dauphin along with [[blanche-of-castile|Blanche]]'s hand and thirty thousand marks of English coin (004-137–138). The transaction reveals how thoroughly the Plantagenet inheritance has been reduced to movable assets in John's desperate effort to secure his [[crown|crown]].
