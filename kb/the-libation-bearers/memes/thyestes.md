# Thyestes

Thyestes is the remote ancestor whose wrongs echo through every scene of [[aeschylus|Aeschylus]]' *Choephoroe*. His brother Atreus slaughtered Thyestes' children and served them to him at a feast — the originating atrocity that set the curse upon the [[house-of-atreus|House of Atreus]] in motion.

## Identity

- **Play**: Referenced in the *Choephoroe* but does not appear onstage; his story is the mythological background of the [[the-trilogy-form|trilogy]]
- **Family**: Son of Pelops; brother of Atreus; father of [[aigisthos|Aigisthos]] (the avenger of his wrongs) and of the murdered children
- **Status**: Mortal — the wronged ancestor whose suffering initiates the curse
- **Mythological role**: The victim whose cry for [[dike|justice]] his son [[aigisthos|Aigisthos]] answers by murdering [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]

## Role in the Play

Thyestes never appears in the *Choephoroe* — he is a figure from the previous generation — but his curse is the play's mythological foundation. [[aigisthos|Aigisthos]], his son and avenger, is explicitly identified as "[[aigisthos|son of Thyestes]], blood-foe to [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]" (P02-011). The pattern that the *Choephoroe* re-enacts — a son avenging a father's wrongs — was established by Thyestes' story a generation earlier.

The curse traced through Thyestes links the play's action to the larger mythological framework. [[orestes|Orestes]]' [[divine-vengeance|vengeance]] for [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]] is structurally identical to [[aigisthos|Aigisthos]]' [[divine-vengeance|vengeance]] for Thyestes' children — and the play's implicit question is whether [[orestes|Orestes]]' act will merely continue the inherited cycle or somehow break it.

## The Banquet of Thyestes

The foundational atrocity of the [[house-of-atreus|House of Atreus]] — Atreus' slaughter of Thyestes' children and his serving of their flesh to their father at a reconciliation feast — is not directly narrated in the *Choephoroe* but is the unspoken horror behind every reference to the [[curse-of-the-house-of-atreus|family curse]]. The Erinyes (Furies) were said to have been born from the blood of the murdered children, making Thyestes' suffering the literal origin of the [[divine-vengeance|vengeance]] spirits that pursue [[orestes|Orestes]].

## Across the Trilogy

- **[[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]**: The [[the-chorus|Chorus]] recounts the story of Thyestes and the origins of the curse.
- **Choephoroe**: Thyestes' son [[aigisthos|Aigisthos]] rules [[argos|Argos]] as the avenger of his father's wrongs.
- **Eumenides**: The curse originating with Thyestes' children is finally resolved through Athena's court.

## Related Characters

- [[agamemnon]] — the son of his enemy Atreus
