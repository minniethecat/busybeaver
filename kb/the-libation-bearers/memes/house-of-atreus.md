The House of Atreus is the royal palace in [[argos|Argos]], seat of the dynasty founded by Atreus and passed to his son [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]. In *The [[sacrifice-and-offering|Libation]] Bearers*, the house is not merely a setting but a character — a place saturated with past crimes, where the stones themselves seem to cry out for blood.

## The Cursed Palace

The house is "broken in twain" by [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]'s murder. The [[the-chorus|Chorus]]'s lament makes the house itself a victim: "O hearthstone wet with tears! / O pillars of a house broken in twain! / Without sun, without love, / Murk in the heart thereof and mist above, / For a lord slain!" (001-002). The dynastic centre has become a tomb. [[aigisthos|Aigisthos]], "[[aigisthos|son of Thyestes]], blood-foe to [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]], and now Tyrant of [[argos|Argos]]" (P02-011), occupies a seat that is both throne and crime scene.

## Physical Description

The Endnotes provide what little archaeological precision is possible: the "castle of the Atreidae" (002-086) stood at [[argos|Argos]], and in [[aeschylus|Aeschylus]]'s time "there was probably no actual palace" (002-086) — the building is mythical architecture. Yet [[aeschylus|Aeschylus]] gives it vivid physical detail: "the bronzen door / On darkness gloateth and on gold" (001-004), suggesting thresholds that conceal and reveal. The Endnotes note that "an ordinary great house would be thrown open" (002-087) at moments of celebration, but this house remains sealed — its doors are barriers between public appearance and private horror.

## The House as Moral Space

The palace's physical structure mirrors its moral condition. [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] first appears at its door — the threshold between the public world of mourning and the private world of power. When [[orestes|Orestes]] enters the house to kill [[aigisthos|Aigisthos]], the act happens inside, unseen — the house conceals its crimes. Only at the play's climax do the doors open to reveal [[orestes|Orestes]] standing over the bodies, "the amazing scene at the end with [[the-net-and-the-robe|the bloodstained robe]], the gathering of the unseen Furies" (P01-008).

The house is also the space where the curse propagates across generations. The Endnotes describe "the castle of the Atreidae" (002-086) in front of which the action takes place — the [[the-chorus|Chorus]], slave women, stand outside the palace, neither fully of the house nor fully free of it, positioned to witness but powerless to intervene.

## Context in the Trilogy

- **[[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]**: The house receives the conquering king — and his murderer. The famous "carpet scene" marks the threshold as a site of [[hubris|hybris]].
- **The [[sacrifice-and-offering|Libation]] Bearers**: The house is the enemy fortress [[orestes|Orestes]] must infiltrate. His [[guile-and-justice|deception]] of [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] gains him entry; the killing of [[aigisthos|Aigisthos]] and [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] is house-work.
- **Eumenides**: The house is left behind entirely — the action moves to [[delphi|Delphi]] and Athens, suggesting that resolution requires leaving the cursed palace for institutions of civic [[dike|justice]].
