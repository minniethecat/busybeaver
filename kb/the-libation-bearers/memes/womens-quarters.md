# The Women's Quarters

The Women's Quarters (Greek: *gynaikonitis*) is a distinct section of the royal palace at [[argos|Argos]], represented on stage by a separate door in the *skene* facade.
In *The [[sacrifice-and-offering|Libation]] Bearers*, this architectural division carries dramatic significance — it demarcates [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]]'s domain from the main palace where [[aigisthos|Aigisthos]] holds court (001-003).

## Location

- **Region**: [[argos|Argos]], the [[argos|Argive]] plain
- **Place type**: palace — the Women's Quarters (*gynaikonitis*) within the royal residence
- **Mythological association**: The [[house-of-atreus|House of Atreus]] — the palace is the seat of the cursed dynasty

## Dramatic Significance

The Women's Quarters is more than a background setting — it functions as an active dramatic space.
The stage directions specify "one door leading to the main palace, another to the Women's House" (001-003), and characters enter and exit through these distinct portals throughout the play.

[[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]]'s movements between the Women's Quarters and the main palace trace her shifting power.
She emerges to receive [[orestes|Orestes]]' false report, then retreats "back into the Women's House" (001-004) — a spatial withdrawal that mirrors her impending loss of control.
The Women's Quarters is where [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] has her terrifying dream of the serpent (P01-008), and it is from this space that the [[the-nurse|Nurse]] emerges with her errand.

The separate door also enables the play's climactic staging: after [[orestes|Orestes]] kills [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]], the *ekkyklema* reveals the bodies, with the palace doors framing the tableau.
