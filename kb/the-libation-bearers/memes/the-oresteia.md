# The Oresteia

The only complete Greek tragic trilogy to survive antiquity. Produced at the City Dionysia in 458 BCE, the Oresteia traces the curse of the House of Atreus from Agamemnon's murder to Orestes' acquittal, transforming the archaic law of blood-vengeance into the civic institution of justice at Athens.

## The Three Plays

**Agamemnon**: The king returns from Troy and is murdered by his wife Clytemnestra and her lover Aegisthus. The play ends with Clytemnestra triumphant, standing over the bodies of Agamemnon and Cassandra.

**The Libation Bearers (Choephoroe)**: Orestes, the exiled son, returns to avenge his father. With his sister Electra, he kills Aegisthus and Clytemnestra — but the matricide awakens the Furies, who pursue him from the stage.

**The Eumenides**: Orestes flees to Delphi, then to Athens, where Athena establishes the Areopagus court. She casts the deciding vote for acquittal and persuades the Furies to become the Eumenides — "the Kindly Ones" — guardians of Athens.

## Central Arc

The trilogy's formal arc mirrors its thematic one. From the private chamber of Agamemnon's murder to the public court of the Areopagus; from the language of vengeance to the language of law; from the Furies' ancient claim ("on him that doeth shall be done" [001-002]) to Athena's civic reason — the Oresteia tells the story of civilization itself.
