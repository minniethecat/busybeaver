# Revenge and Justice

the central thematic dialectic of the Oresteia — the irreducible tension between personal vengeance and impersonal justice that drives the trilogy's action.

## In The Libation Bearers

This theme is embodied in the play's central dramatic situation. Orestes is commanded by Apollo to kill his mother — an act that is simultaneously justice (avenging Agamemnon) and the most terrible crime (matricide). The play refuses to distinguish between the two, forcing the audience to inhabit the same moral uncertainty that torments its protagonist.

## The Oresteia's Arc

The trilogy's great achievement is tracing the transformation of this theme from the private violence of the *Agamemnon* through the crisis of *The Libation Bearers* to the civic resolution of the *Eumenides*. Aeschylus shows that the archaic law of blood-vengeance cannot simply be obeyed or denied — it must be transformed.
