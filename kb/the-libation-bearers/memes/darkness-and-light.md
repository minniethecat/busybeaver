Darkness and Light imagery runs through *The [[sacrifice-and-offering|Libation]] Bearers* as a structuring opposition, mapping the play's moral and spiritual landscape. Darkness characterises the [[house-of-atreus|House of Atreus]], the tomb, and the spiritual blindness of blood-guilt; light represents [[dike|justice]], knowledge, and the hoped-for resolution glimpsed in the [[the-trilogy-form|trilogy]]'s final play.

## Manifestation in the Play

From its earliest moments, the play establishes darkness as the condition of the House. The [[the-chorus|Chorus]] sings of "the bronzen door / On darkness gloateth and on gold" (001-004), characterising the palace as a place where darkness is not merely absence but active presence — something that "gloateth." The dead [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]] lies in "darkness" (P01-005); his children's prayers strain "through the hood / Of darkness" (001-002) toward the ears of the dead. Darkness is the medium of the tomb, of concealment, of the fear that keeps the enslaved [[the-chorus|Chorus]] from speaking freely.

Light, by contrast, is prayed for. [[electra|Electra]] appeals to the gods to "smitten swift in the eyes of light" (001-002) those who have wronged her father. [[orestes|Orestes]], at the play's climax, calls on [[hermes|Hermes]] to "help the House to light out of darkness" (002-100). Light is the condition of [[dike|justice]] realised — a state the play itself cannot achieve but gestures toward.

## The Shadow of the Coming Darkness

Gilbert Murray identifies a progressive darkening even within [[orestes|Orestes]] himself: "from quite the early scenes… the shadow of the coming darkness begins to show itself" (P01-005). This internal darkness — the madness that will overtake [[orestes|Orestes]] after the [[killing-of-clytemnestra|matricide]] — parallels the physical darkness of the house and tomb. The "flight of the would-be Saviour as one Accursed, never to rest again" (P01-008) is a movement into darkness that will take the entire third play of the [[the-trilogy-form|trilogy]] to reverse.

## Light as Resolution Deferred

The play does not end in light. The Furies — glimpsed by [[orestes|Orestes]] alone as "the gathering of the unseen Furies" (P01-008) — are creatures of darkness. Light arrives only in *Eumenides*, where Athena's establishment of the Areopagus transforms the Furies from chthonic darkness-dwellers to "Beneficent Beings" who will be honoured "from 'Erinyes' to 'Eumenides'" (P01-001). The imagery of light is thus a promise that the play itself cannot keep — a reach toward resolution that will be fulfilled only when [[divine-vengeance|blood-vengeance]] yields to civic law.
