# Death of Aigisthos

The killing of [[aigisthos|Aigisthos]] is the first act of [[orestes|Orestes]]' [[divine-vengeance|vengeance]] at the climax of *The [[sacrifice-and-offering|Libation]] Bearers*.
[[orestes|Orestes]] slays the usurper who, together with [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]], murdered his father [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]] and seized the throne of [[argos|Argos]] (P01-001).

## Summary

[[aigisthos|Aigisthos]], [[aigisthos|son of Thyestes]] and blood-foe to the [[house-of-atreus|House of Atreus]] (P02-011), rules [[argos|Argos]] alongside [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] after their murder of [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]] (P02-008).
[[orestes|Orestes]], [[exile-and-return|return]]ing from [[exile-and-return|exile]], enters the palace in disguise. When the [[the-nurse|Nurse]] is sent to summon [[aigisthos|Aigisthos]] to meet the "strangers" who bring news of [[orestes|Orestes]]' death (001-004), [[aigisthos|Aigisthos]] arrives at the palace unprepared for the confrontation.
[[orestes|Orestes]] kills him offstage — a death that precedes and precipitates the climactic confrontation with [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] (002-106).

- **Position in the play**: climax

## Participants

- [[orestes|Orestes]] — the avenger, [[orestes|son of Agamemnon]], who executes the killing
- [[aigisthos|Aigisthos]] — the victim, usurper and co-murderer of [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]
- [[the-chorus|The Chorus of Libation-Bearers]] — witness and commentator on the act of [[divine-vengeance|vengeance]]
- [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] — summoned by the commotion; her confrontation follows immediately

## Dramatic Significance

The death of [[aigisthos|Aigisthos]] is the first fulfilment of [[apollo|Apollo]]'s command to [[orestes|Orestes]]. As the [[aigisthos|son of Thyestes]] and the seducer of [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]], [[aigisthos|Aigisthos]] represents the human dimension of the usurpation — he is the one who "hated" [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]] and seized power through adultery and conspiracy.
His death clears the path for the more morally complex act: the killing of [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]], [[orestes|Orestes]]' own mother (P01-001).

The offstage nature of the killing follows Greek tragic convention — violent deaths occur out of sight, reported by messengers or witnesses.
[[aeschylus|Aeschylus]] places the emphasis not on the physical act but on its moral and religious consequences.

The [[the-chorus|Chorus]], who "hate [[aigisthos|Aigisthos]] and love the memory of [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]" (002-014), respond to the death with vindication, but the moment is brief — [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]]'s entrance transforms the scene into the play's true moral crisis.

## The House of Atreus Context

[[aigisthos|Aigisthos]] is the [[aigisthos|son of Thyestes]], whose feud with his brother Atreus cursed the entire House. The Endnotes note that [[aigisthos|Aigisthos]]'s own father [[thyestes|Thyestes]] was wronged by Atreus, and that "a large blood-price from [[aigisthos|Aigisthos]]" would not have been accepted for the crime (002-038).
His death is thus one link in the chain of retributive violence that defines the [[house-of-atreus|House of Atreus]] — violence that [[aeschylus|Aeschylus]] confronts directly rather than eliding (P01-001).

## In Other Versions

In [[euripides|Euripides]]' *[[electra|Electra]]*, the killing of [[aigisthos|Aigisthos]] receives extended treatment — it occurs during a sacrifice, with [[aigisthos|Aigisthos]] as the host and [[orestes|Orestes]] striking him down in an ironic reversal of hospitality.
[[sophocles|Sophocles]]' *[[electra|Electra]]* places the death inside the palace, with the Paedagogus's false report of [[orestes|Orestes]]' death serving as the [[guile-and-justice|deception]] that draws [[aigisthos|Aigisthos]] out.
[[aeschylus|Aeschylus]]'s treatment is more compressed — [[aigisthos|Aigisthos]]'s death is swift, a necessary prelude to the mother-murder that is the play's true dramatic and religious centre.
