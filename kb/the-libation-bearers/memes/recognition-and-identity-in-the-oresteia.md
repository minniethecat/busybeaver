# Recognition and Identity in the Oresteia

the anagnorisis (recognition) scene between Orestes and Electra — one of the great recognition scenes in Greek tragedy, employing tokens, reasoning, and emotional reunion.

## In The Libation Bearers

This theme manifests through the play's central dramatic situation. Aeschylus constructs his tragedy around it, using the formal resources of Greek drama — the kommos, the stichomythia, the choral odes — to explore its depths.

## Dramatic Significance

The play's power derives in part from its refusal to offer easy resolution. The ambiguity — is Orestes an agent of justice or a matricide? — is sustained until the trilogy's final play, and the *Choephoroe* gains its tragic intensity from the audience's awareness that the answer is not yet given.

## Across the Trilogy

The Oresteia traces the transformation of this theme from the private chamber of the *Agamemnon* through the crisis of *The Libation Bearers* to the civic resolution of the *Eumenides*.
