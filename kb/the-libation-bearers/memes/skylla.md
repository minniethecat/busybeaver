# Skylla

the sea monster Scylla (Σκύλλα) — invoked in the play's imagery as a figure of consuming destruction, part of the mythological language the Chorus uses to express the horror of the House of Atreus' crimes.

## In The Libation Bearers

This reference appears in *The Libation Bearers* at a crucial moment. Aeschylus draws on the full resources of Greek mythological and literary tradition to deepen the play's resonance — each allusion carries a weight of associations that his original audience would have immediately recognized.

## Dramatic Significance

The invocation of this figure or concept is not incidental. In Aeschylus' compressed and intense dramaturgy, every reference serves a purpose — to heighten the emotional stakes, to connect the particular action to the larger mythological world, or to suggest an interpretation that the dialogue alone cannot state directly.
