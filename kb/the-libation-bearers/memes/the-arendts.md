# The Areopagus

the hill of Ares in Athens, where the first homicide court was established by Athena and where Orestes is tried in the *Eumenides* — the site that transforms private vengeance into civic justice.

## In The Libation Bearers

This place plays a crucial role in the structure of *The Libation Bearers*. Aeschylus builds his tragedy around it with characteristic economy and intensity — every element of the play's architecture serves the central dramatic purpose.

## Dramatic Significance

The play is remarkable for its concentrated power. With what the Preface calls "both an archaic beauty and a stark grimness of speech," Aeschylus creates moments of extraordinary theatrical impact from the simplest of dramatic elements.

## Across the Trilogy

The Oresteia traces the transformation of private vengeance into civic justice. Each element of the play's setting and structure contributes to this arc, from the private tomb of the *Choephoroe* to the public court of the *Eumenides*.
