# Singapore

Singapore is an island port in the Straits Settlements — a coaling station and commercial hub of the British Empire — where the [[rangoon|Rangoon]] stops briefly on its voyage from [[calcutta|Calcutta]] to [[hong-kong|Hong Kong]]. Though [[phileas-fogg|Phileas Fogg]] and his party barely pause there, Singapore serves as a waypoint on the [[around-the-world-route|circumnavigation]] and a reminder of the imperial networks that make the journey possible.

## In the Novel

The Rangoon reaches Singapore on schedule, anchoring for only a few hours to take on coal. Fogg, ever precise, goes ashore briefly with [[aouda|Aouda]] and [[passepartout|Passepartout]], but does not linger — the steamer's stay is too brief for sightseeing. The stop is notable primarily as a transit point: from Singapore, a telegraphic wire connects to Hong Kong, and Fix uses the opportunity to communicate with his superiors in London. (PN: 017-005)

Singapore appears in the novel as a microcosm of the British Empire's global reach. The harbour is filled with vessels of every nation, and the passengers who board include Indians, Ceylonese, Chinamen, and Malays — a diversity of peoples that reflects the trade routes converging on the port. (PN: 017-020)

> "The island of Singapore is not imposing in aspect, for there are no mountains; yet its appearance is not without attractions. It is a park checkered by pleasant highways and avenues." (017-008)

## Singapore as a Coaling Station

Singapore's role in Fogg's journey is primarily logistical. The island's strategic position at the tip of the Malay Peninsula made it an essential coaling station for steamers traveling between India and China. Without such stations — spaced at regular intervals along the route — the [[steamer-travel|steam navigation]] that Fogg depends on would be impossible. At Singapore, the Rangoon takes on coal and departs the next morning at four a.m., continuing eastward toward Hong Kong. (PN: 017-020)
