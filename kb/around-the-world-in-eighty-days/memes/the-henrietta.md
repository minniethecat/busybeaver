# Henrietta (Steamship)

The Henrietta is an iron-hulled, wood-built steamship that becomes — in one of the novel's most audacious sequences — Phileas Fogg's improvised transport across the Atlantic Ocean. Having missed the Liverpool steamer at New York, Fogg purchases the Henrietta outright and redirects it from its Bordeaux course, burning the ship's wooden fittings to fuel the final leg of his eighty-day race.

## In the Novel

Fogg discovers the Henrietta at the New York docks on the 12th of December, a fast vessel of eleven to twelve knots normally plying the New York to Bordeaux route. Its captain, [[captain-speedy|Andrew Speedy]], is a man of fixed habits and fixed destinations — and Bordeaux, not Liverpool, is his scheduled port. (PN: 028-055)

When Speedy refuses to alter course, Fogg takes the extraordinary step of purchasing the vessel from its owners, confining Speedy to his cabin, and assuming command. The Henrietta's crew, bribed with Fogg's money, cooperate in the transformation of their merchant steamer into an Atlantic racer. (PN: 028-065)

> The "Henrietta," iron-hulled, wood-built above. Fogg ascended to the deck. The captain was not on board; the mate received him. Fogg looked at the steamer; it was a vessel of eight hundred tons, a fast sailer, and, as he was soon informed, capable of making eleven to twelve knots. (028-038)

## The Atlantic Crossing

Under Fogg's direction, the Henrietta makes good speed across the Atlantic. But as the voyage progresses and coal runs low, Fogg escalates his improvisation: he purchases the ship's wooden superstructure from Speedy — masts, deckhouses, cabins, and fittings — and feeds it into the furnaces. Passepartout climbs the rigging to adjust sails, and the Henrietta ploughs across the waves at increasing speed. (PN: 028-130)

An hour after departure, the Henrietta passes the lighthouse that marks the entrance to New York Harbour, and the open Atlantic stretches ahead. The crew, now serving a new master, work the vessel as it was never intended to be worked — racing not against another ship, but against the clock. (PN: 028-107)

## Arrival at Queenstown

The Henrietta enters [[queenstown|Queenstown]] Harbour at one o'clock in the morning, its wooden fittings consumed, its decks stripped bare. Fogg's calculation proves sound: by docking at Queenstown rather than continuing to Liverpool, he gains twelve hours through the Irish mail express to Dublin and the fast steamer onward to Liverpool. (PN: 029-124)

The Henrietta's enforced service in Fogg's cause is one of the novel's most vivid set pieces — a demonstration that in Fogg's world, everything, including a merchant steamer, can be repurposed in the service of precision and punctuality.
