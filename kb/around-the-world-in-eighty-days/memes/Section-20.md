[020-001] What happened when the pilot-boat came in sight of Shanghai will be

[020-002] easily guessed. The signals made by the “Tankadere” had been seen by

[020-003] [[captain-speedy|the captain]] of the [[yokohama|Yokohama]] [[steamer-travel|steamer]], who, espying the flag at

[020-004] half-mast, had directed his course towards the little craft. Phileas

[020-005] [[phileas-fogg|Fogg]], after paying the stipulated price of his passage to John Busby,

[020-006] and rewarding that worthy with the additional sum of five hundred and

[020-007] fifty pounds, ascended the steamer with [[aouda|Aouda]] and [[detective-fix|Fix]]; and they started

[020-008] at once for Nagasaki and Yokohama.

[020-009] They reached their destination on the morning of the 14th of November.

[020-010] Phileas Fogg lost no time in going on board the “Carnatic,” where he

[020-011] learned, to Aouda’s great delight—and perhaps to his own, though he

[020-012] betrayed no emotion—that [[passepartout|Passepartout]], a Frenchman, had really arrived

[020-013] on her the day before.

[020-014] The [[san-francisco|San Francisco]] steamer was announced to leave that very evening, and

[020-015] it became necessary to find Passepartout, if possible, without delay.

[020-016] Mr. Fogg applied in vain to the French and English consuls, and, after

[020-017] wandering through the streets a long time, began to despair of finding

[020-018] his missing servant. Chance, or perhaps a kind of presentiment, at last

[020-019] led him into the Honourable Mr. Batulcar’s theatre. He certainly would

[020-020] not have recognised Passepartout in the eccentric mountebank’s costume;

[020-021] but the latter, lying on his back, perceived his master in the gallery.

[020-022] He could not help starting, which so changed the position of his nose

[020-023] as to bring the “pyramid” pell-mell upon the stage.

[020-024] All this Passepartout learned from Aouda, who recounted to him what had

[020-025] taken place on the voyage from [[hong-kong|Hong Kong]] to Shanghai on the

[020-026] “Tankadere,” in company with one [[fix-detective|Mr. Fix]].

[020-027] Passepartout did not change countenance on hearing this name. He

[020-028] thought that the time had not yet arrived to divulge to his master what

[020-029] had taken place between the detective and himself; and, in the account

[020-030] he gave of his absence, he simply excused himself for having been

[020-031] overtaken by drunkenness, in smoking opium at a tavern in Hong Kong.

[020-032] Mr. Fogg heard this narrative coldly, without a word; and then

[020-033] furnished his man with funds necessary to obtain clothing more in

[020-034] harmony with his position. Within an hour the Frenchman had cut off his

[020-035] nose and parted with his wings, and retained nothing about him which

[020-036] recalled the sectary of the god Tingou.

[020-037] The steamer which was about to depart from Yokohama to San Francisco

[020-038] belonged to the [[pacific-mail|Pacific Mail Steamship Company]], and was named the

[020-039] “General Grant.” She was a large paddle-wheel steamer of two thousand

[020-040] five hundred tons; well equipped and very fast. The massive

[020-041] walking-beam rose and fell above the deck; at one end a piston-rod

[020-042] worked up and down; and at the other was a connecting-rod which, in

[020-043] changing the rectilinear motion to a circular one, was directly

[020-044] connected with the shaft of the paddles. The “General Grant” was rigged

[020-045] with three masts, giving a large capacity for sails, and thus

[020-046] materially aiding the steam power. By making twelve miles an hour, she

[020-047] would cross the ocean in twenty-one days. Phileas Fogg was therefore

[020-048] justified in hoping that he would reach San Francisco by the 2nd of

[020-049] December, [[new-york|New York]] by the 11th, and [[london|London]] on the 20th—thus gaining

[020-050] several hours on the fatal date of the 21st of December.

[020-051] There was a full complement of passengers on board, among them English,

[020-052] many Americans, a large number of coolies on their way to California,

[020-053] and several East Indian officers, who were spending their vacation in

[020-054] making the tour of the world. Nothing of moment happened on the voyage;

[020-055] the steamer, sustained on its large paddles, rolled but little, and the

[020-056] “[[the-pacific-crossing|Pacific]]” almost justified its name. Mr. Fogg was as calm and taciturn

[020-057] as ever. His young companion felt herself more and more attached to him

[020-058] by other ties than gratitude; his silent but generous nature impressed

[020-059] her more than she thought; and it was almost unconsciously that she

[020-060] yielded to emotions which did not seem to have the least effect upon

[020-061] her protector. Aouda took the keenest interest in his plans, and became

[020-062] impatient at any incident which seemed likely to retard his journey.

[020-063] She often chatted with Passepartout, who did not fail to perceive the

[020-064] state of the lady’s heart; and, being the most faithful of domestics,

[020-065] he never exhausted his eulogies of Phileas Fogg’s honesty, generosity,

[020-066] and devotion. He took pains to calm Aouda’s doubts of a successful

[020-067] termination of the journey, telling her that the most difficult part of

[020-068] it had passed, that now they were beyond the fantastic countries of

[020-069] Japan and China, and were fairly on their way to civilised places

[020-070] again. A [[railway-travel|railway]] train from San Francisco to New York, and a

[020-071] transatlantic steamer from New York to [[liverpool|Liverpool]], would doubtless bring

[020-072] them to the end of this impossible journey round the world within the

[020-073] period agreed upon.

[020-074] On the ninth day after leaving Yokohama, Phileas Fogg had traversed

[020-075] exactly one half of the terrestrial globe. The “General Grant” passed,

[020-076] on the 23rd of November, the one hundred and eightieth meridian, and

[020-077] was at the very antipodes of London. Mr. Fogg had, it is true,

[020-078] exhausted fifty-two of the eighty days in which he was to complete the

[020-079] tour, and there were only twenty-eight left. But, though he was only

[020-080] half-way by the difference of meridians, he had really gone over

[020-081] two-thirds of the whole journey; for he had been obliged to make long

[020-082] circuits from London to Aden, from Aden to [[bombay|Bombay]], from [[calcutta|Calcutta]] to

[020-083] Singapore, and from Singapore to Yokohama. Could he have followed

[020-084] without deviation the fiftieth parallel, which is that of London, the

[020-085] whole distance would only have been about twelve thousand miles;

[020-086] whereas he would be forced, by the irregular methods of locomotion, to

[020-087] traverse twenty-six thousand, of which he had, on the 23rd of November,

[020-088] accomplished seventeen thousand five hundred. And now the course was a

[020-089] straight one, and Fix was no longer there to put obstacles in their

[020-090] way!

[020-091] It happened also, on the 23rd of November, that Passepartout made a

[020-092] joyful discovery. It will be remembered that the obstinate fellow had

[020-093] insisted on keeping his famous family [[precision-and-timekeeping|watch]] at London time, and on

[020-094] regarding that of the countries he had passed through as quite false

[020-095] and unreliable. Now, on this day, though he had not changed the hands,

[020-096] he found that his watch exactly agreed with the ship’s chronometers.

[020-097] His triumph was hilarious. He would have liked to know what Fix would

[020-098] say if he were aboard!

[020-099] “The rogue told me a lot of stories,” repeated Passepartout, “about the

[020-100] meridians, the sun, and the moon! Moon, indeed! moonshine more likely!

[020-101] If one listened to that sort of people, a pretty sort of time one would

[020-102] keep! I was sure that the sun would some day regulate itself by my

[020-103] watch!”

[020-104] Passepartout was ignorant that, if the face of his watch had been

[020-105] divided into twenty-four hours, like the Italian clocks, he would have

[020-106] no reason for exultation; for the hands of his watch would then,

[020-107] instead of as now indicating nine o’clock in the morning, indicate nine

[020-108] o’clock in the evening, that is, the twenty-first hour after midnight

[020-109] precisely the difference between London time and that of the one

[020-110] hundred and eightieth meridian. But if Fix had been able to explain

[020-111] this purely physical effect, Passepartout would not have admitted, even

[020-112] if he had comprehended it. Moreover, if the detective had been on board

[020-113] at that moment, Passepartout would have joined issue with him on a

[020-114] quite different subject, and in an entirely different manner.

[020-115] Where was Fix at that moment?

[020-116] He was actually on board the “General Grant.”

[020-117] On reaching Yokohama, the detective, leaving Mr. Fogg, whom he expected

[020-118] to meet again during the day, had repaired at once to the English

[020-119] consulate, where he at last found the warrant of arrest. It had

[020-120] followed him from Bombay, and had come by the “Carnatic,” on which

[020-121] steamer he himself was supposed to be. Fix’s disappointment may be

[020-122] imagined when he reflected that the warrant was now useless. Mr. Fogg

[020-123] had left English ground, and it was now necessary to procure his

[020-124] extradition!

[020-125] “Well,” thought Fix, after a moment of anger, “my warrant is not good

[020-126] here, but it will be in England. The rogue evidently intends to return

[020-127] to his own country, thinking he has thrown the police off his track.

[020-128] Good! I will follow him across the Atlantic. As for the money, heaven

[020-129] grant there may be some left! But the fellow has already spent in

[020-130] travelling, rewards, trials, bail, elephants, and all sorts of charges,

[020-131] more than five thousand pounds. Yet, after all, the Bank is rich!”

[020-132] His course decided on, he went on board the “General Grant,” and was

[020-133] there when Mr. Fogg and Aouda arrived. To his utter amazement, he

[020-134] recognised Passepartout, despite his theatrical disguise. He quickly

[020-135] concealed himself in his cabin, to avoid an awkward explanation, and

[020-136] hoped—thanks to the number of passengers—to remain unperceived by Mr.

[020-137] Fogg’s servant.

[020-138] On that very day, however, he met Passepartout face to face on the

[020-139] forward deck. The latter, without a word, made a rush for him, grasped

[020-140] him by the throat, and, much to the amusement of a group of Americans,

[020-141] who immediately began to [[the-wager|bet]] on him, administered to the detective a

[020-142] perfect volley of blows, which proved the great superiority of French

[020-143] over English pugilistic skill.

[020-144] When Passepartout had finished, he found himself relieved and

[020-145] comforted. Fix got up in a somewhat rumpled condition, and, looking at

[020-146] his adversary, coldly said, “Have you done?”

[020-147] “For this time—yes.”

[020-148] “Then let me have a word with you.”

[020-149] “But I—”

[020-150] “In your master’s interests.”

[020-151] Passepartout seemed to be vanquished by Fix’s coolness, for he quietly

[020-152] followed him, and they sat down aside from the rest of the passengers.

[020-153] “You have given me a thrashing,” said Fix. “Good, I expected it. Now,

[020-154] listen to me. Up to this time I have been Mr. Fogg’s adversary. I am

[020-155] now in his game.”

[020-156] “Aha!” cried Passepartout; “you are convinced he is an honest man?”

[020-157] “No,” replied Fix coldly, “I think him a rascal. Sh! don’t budge, and

[020-158] let me speak. As long as Mr. Fogg was on English ground, it was for my

[020-159] interest to detain him there until my warrant of arrest arrived. I did

[020-160] everything I could to keep him back. I sent the Bombay priests after

[020-161] him, I got you intoxicated at Hong Kong, I separated you from him, and

[020-162] I made him miss the Yokohama steamer.”

[020-163] Passepartout listened, with closed fists.

[020-164] “Now,” resumed Fix, “Mr. Fogg seems to be going back to England. Well,

[020-165] I will follow him there. But hereafter I will do as much to keep

[020-166] obstacles out of his way as I have done up to this time to put them in

[020-167] his path. I’ve changed my game, you see, and simply because it was for

[020-168] my interest to change it. Your interest is the same as mine; for it is

[020-169] only in England that you will ascertain whether you are in the service

[020-170] of a criminal or an honest man.”

[020-171] Passepartout listened very attentively to Fix, and was convinced that

[020-172] he spoke with entire good faith.

[020-173] “Are we friends?” asked the detective.

[020-174] “Friends?—no,” replied Passepartout; “but allies, perhaps. At the least

[020-175] sign of treason, however, I’ll twist your neck for you.”

[020-176] “Agreed,” said the detective quietly.

[020-177] Eleven days later, on the 3rd of December, the “General Grant” entered

[020-178] the bay of the Golden Gate, and reached San Francisco.

[020-179] Mr. Fogg had neither gained nor lost a single day.

[020-180] CHAPTER XXV.

[020-181] IN WHICH A SLIGHT GLIMPSE IS HAD OF SAN FRANCISCO

[020-182] It was seven in the morning when Mr. Fogg, Aouda, and Passepartout set

[020-183] foot upon the American continent, if this name can be given to the

[020-184] floating quay upon which they disembarked. These quays, rising and

[020-185] falling with the tide, thus facilitate the loading and unloading of

[020-186] vessels. Alongside them were clippers of all sizes, steamers of all

[020-187] nationalities, and the steamboats, with several decks rising one above

[020-188] the other, which ply on the Sacramento and its tributaries. There were

[020-189] also heaped up the products of a commerce which extends to Mexico,

[020-190] Chili, Peru, Brazil, Europe, Asia, and all the Pacific islands.
