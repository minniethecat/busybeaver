# Carnatic (Steamship)

The Carnatic is a [[p-and-o-company|P&O]] steamship scheduled to sail from [[hong-kong|Hong Kong]] to [[yokohama|Yokohama]]. It becomes one of the novel's most consequential plot devices when [[passepartout|Passepartout]] boards it alone — drugged, separated from [[phileas-fogg|Phileas Fogg]], and entirely unaware that his master has missed the departure. The Carnatic episode is the hinge on which the novel's second act turns: it separates master from servant, strands Passepartout in Yokohama, and sets in motion the chain of improvisations that will carry Fogg across the Pacific.

## In the Novel

The Carnatic is announced to depart Hong Kong at high tide on the evening following Fogg's arrival. Fogg, having learned of an earlier departure than published, sends Passepartout to engage three cabins. However, [[detective-fix|Fix]] — desperate to detain Fogg on British soil until an arrest warrant arrives — intercepts Passepartout in a Hong Kong tavern and drugs him with opium, leaving the valet unconscious while the ship prepares to sail ([PN=018-045]–[PN=019-030]).

Passepartout, barely conscious, manages to stagger aboard the Carnatic at the last moment. He collapses in a cabin and does not fully wake until the steamer is well at sea — by which time he realizes that Fogg and [[aouda]] are not on board. He is alone, without money, headed for Japan, while his master, having missed the Carnatic, has been forced to charter the [[tankadere|Tankadere]] to pursue the vessel to Shanghai ([PN=019-080]–[PN=020-035]).

The Carnatic episode is a masterpiece of narrative engineering. Fix's desperate gamble to keep Fogg in Hong Kong backfires: rather than detaining his quarry, Fix succeeds only in separating master from servant and propelling Fogg into one of his most audacious improvisations — the typhoon chase aboard the Tankadere ([PN=020-035]–[PN=020-100]).

## Passepartout's Journey

Passepartout's voyage aboard the Carnatic — alone, penniless, and still groggy from Fix's opium — is among the novel's most disorienting passages. The valet who had sought a quiet life in London finds himself on a steamer bound for Japan, unable to pay for a single meal, with no knowledge of where his master has gone or whether the wager can possibly survive this disaster.

His arrival in Yokohama transforms the comic disaster into an opportunity for the novel's most surreal sequence: Passepartout, to earn passage money, joins [[batulcar|Batulcar's]] Japanese acrobatic troupe, performing as a "human pyramid" in a Yokohama circus — a humiliation that becomes the unlikely route to his reunion with Fogg ([PN=021-024]–[PN=025-018]).

## Relationship to Other Vessels

The Carnatic is one of several P&O steamers that carry Fogg and his party across the oceans:

| Vessel | Route | Role |
|--------|-------|------|
| [[mongolia]] | Suez → Bombay | Carries Fogg and party to India ([PN=004]) |
| [[rangoon]] | Calcutta → Hong Kong | Carries Fogg across the Bay of Bengal ([PN=012]) |
| **Carnatic** | Hong Kong → Yokohama | Separates Passepartout from Fogg ([PN=018–025]) |
| [[tankadere]] | Hong Kong → Shanghai | Pilot-boat that chases the Carnatic ([PN=020]) |

The Carnatic is thus part of a larger pattern: each P&O vessel represents not just a mode of transport but a narrative stage, with its own delays, complications, and chain of consequences for the wager's fragile schedule.
