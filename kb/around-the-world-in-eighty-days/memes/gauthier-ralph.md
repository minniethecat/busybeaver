# Gauthier Ralph

Gauthier Ralph was a Director of the [[bank-of-england|Bank of England]] and a member of the [[reform-club|Reform Club]]. He was one of five gentlemen who gathered daily at the Reform Club for a game of whist with [[phileas-fogg|Phileas Fogg]], and was present on the evening when Fogg wagered twenty thousand pounds that he could circumnavigate the globe in eighty days. (PN: 003-025)

## Role in the Wager

Along with [[andrew-stuart|Andrew Stuart]], [[john-sullivan|John Sullivan]], Samuel Fallentin, and [[thomas-flanagan|Thomas Flanagan]], Ralph formed the inner circle of Fogg's whist partners — all rich and highly respectable personages in a club that comprised the princes of English trade and finance. When conversation turned to a newspaper article calculating the theoretical minimum time for a round-the-world journey, Ralph was among the voices of skepticism that provoked Fogg into making his historic wager. (PN: 003-028)

## The Eighty-Day Countdown

As the eighty days drew to a close, Ralph joined his fellow whist players in daily speculation about Fogg's chances. On the final evening, when the clock struck twenty minutes past eight, Ralph noted precisely that the next train from Liverpool did not arrive until ten minutes after twelve — a detail that reinforced the growing conviction that Fogg had failed. (PN: 030-143)

When the Reform Club members prepared to present Fogg's cheque at Barings, Ralph observed pragmatically that they had nothing to do but collect on the wager — a pronouncement made moments before Fogg's dramatic and triumphant appearance at the club. (PN: 030-157)
