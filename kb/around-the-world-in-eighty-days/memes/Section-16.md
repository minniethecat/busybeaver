[016-001] It was then eight o’[[precision-and-timekeeping|clock]]; at half-past nine, it being then high tide,

[016-002] the “Carnatic” would leave the harbour. [[phileas-fogg|Mr. Fogg]] and [[aouda|Aouda]] got into the

[016-003] palanquin, their luggage being brought after on a wheelbarrow, and half

[016-004] an hour later stepped upon the quay whence they were to embark. Mr.

[016-005] Fogg then learned that the “Carnatic” had sailed the evening before. He

[016-006] had expected to find not only the [[steamer-travel|steamer]], but his domestic, and was

[016-007] forced to give up both; but no sign of disappointment appeared on his

[016-008] face, and he merely remarked to Aouda, “It is an accident, madam;

[016-009] nothing more.”

[016-010] At this moment a man who had been observing him attentively approached.

[016-011] It was [[detective-fix|Fix]], who, bowing, addressed Mr. Fogg: “Were you not, like me,

[016-012] sir, a passenger by the ‘Rangoon,’ which arrived yesterday?”

[016-013] “I was, sir,” replied Mr. Fogg coldly. “But I have not the honour—”

[016-014] “Pardon me; I thought I should find your servant here.”

[016-015] “Do you know where he is, sir?” asked Aouda anxiously.

[016-016] “What!” responded [[fix-detective|Fix]], feigning surprise. “Is he not with you?”

[016-017] “No,” said Aouda. “He has not made his appearance since yesterday.

[016-018] Could he have gone on board the ‘Carnatic’ without us?”

[016-019] “Without you, madam?” answered the detective. “Excuse me, did you

[016-020] intend to sail in the ‘Carnatic’?”

[016-021] “Yes, sir.”

[016-022] “So did I, madam, and I am excessively disappointed. The ‘Carnatic’,

[016-023] its repairs being completed, left [[hong-kong|Hong Kong]] twelve hours before the

[016-024] stated time, without any notice being given; and we must now wait a

[016-025] week for another steamer.”

[016-026] As he said “a week” Fix felt his heart leap for joy. Fogg detained at

[016-027] Hong Kong for a week! There would be time for the warrant to arrive,

[016-028] and fortune at last favoured the representative of the law. His horror

[016-029] may be imagined when he heard Mr. Fogg say, in his placid voice, “But

[016-030] there are other vessels besides the ‘Carnatic,’ it seems to me, in the

[016-031] harbour of Hong Kong.”

[016-032] And, offering his arm to Aouda, he directed his steps toward the docks

[016-033] in search of some craft about to start. Fix, stupefied, followed; it

[016-034] seemed as if he were attached to Mr. Fogg by an invisible thread.

[016-035] Chance, however, appeared really to have abandoned the man it had

[016-036] hitherto served so well. For three hours Phileas Fogg wandered about

[016-037] the docks, with the determination, if necessary, to charter a vessel to

[016-038] carry him to [[yokohama|Yokohama]]; but he could only find vessels which were

[016-039] loading or unloading, and which could not therefore set sail. Fix began

[016-040] to hope again.

[016-041] But Mr. Fogg, far from being discouraged, was continuing his search,

[016-042] resolved not to stop if he had to resort to Macao, when he was accosted

[016-043] by a sailor on one of the wharves.

[016-044] “Is your honour looking for a boat?”

[016-045] “Have you a boat ready to sail?”

[016-046] “Yes, your honour; a pilot-boat—No. 43—the best in the harbour.”

[016-047] “Does she go fast?”

[016-048] “Between eight and nine knots the hour. Will you look at her?”

[016-049] “Yes.”

[016-050] “Your honour will be satisfied with her. Is it for a sea excursion?”

[016-051] “No; for a voyage.”

[016-052] “A voyage?”

[016-053] “Yes, will you agree to take me to Yokohama?”

[016-054] The sailor leaned on the railing, opened his eyes wide, and said, “Is

[016-055] your honour joking?”

[016-056] “No. I have missed the ‘Carnatic,’ and I must get to Yokohama by the

[016-057] 14th at the latest, to take the boat for [[san-francisco|San Francisco]].”

[016-058] “I am sorry,” said the sailor; “but it is impossible.”

[016-059] “I offer you a hundred pounds per day, and an additional reward of two

[016-060] hundred pounds if I reach Yokohama in time.”

[016-061] “Are you in earnest?”

[016-062] “Very much so.”

[016-063] The pilot walked away a little distance, and gazed out to sea,

[016-064] evidently struggling between the anxiety to gain a large sum and the

[016-065] fear of venturing so far. Fix was in mortal suspense.

[016-066] Mr. Fogg turned to Aouda and asked her, “You would not be afraid, would

[016-067] you, madam?”

[016-068] “Not with you, Mr. Fogg,” was her answer.

[016-069] The pilot now returned, shuffling his hat in his hands.

[016-070] “Well, pilot?” said Mr. Fogg.

[016-071] “Well, your honour,” replied he, “I could not risk myself, my men, or

[016-072] my little boat of scarcely twenty tons on so long a voyage at this time

[016-073] of year. Besides, we could not reach Yokohama in time, for it is

[016-074] sixteen hundred and sixty miles from Hong Kong.”

[016-075] “Only sixteen hundred,” said Mr. Fogg.

[016-076] “It’s the same thing.”

[016-077] Fix breathed more freely.

[016-078] “But,” added the pilot, “it might be arranged another way.”

[016-079] Fix ceased to breathe at all.

[016-080] “How?” asked Mr. Fogg.

[016-081] “By going to Nagasaki, at the extreme south of Japan, or even to

[016-082] Shanghai, which is only eight hundred miles from here. In going to

[016-083] Shanghai we should not be forced to sail wide of the Chinese coast,

[016-084] which would be a great advantage, as the currents run northward, and

[016-085] would aid us.”

[016-086] “Pilot,” said Mr. Fogg, “I must take the American steamer at Yokohama,

[016-087] and not at Shanghai or Nagasaki.”

[016-088] “Why not?” returned the pilot. “The San Francisco steamer does not

[016-089] start from Yokohama. It puts in at Yokohama and Nagasaki, but it starts

[016-090] from Shanghai.”

[016-091] “You are sure of that?”

[016-092] “Perfectly.”

[016-093] “And when does the boat leave Shanghai?”

[016-094] “On the 11th, at seven in the evening. We have, therefore, four days

[016-095] before us, that is ninety-six hours; and in that time, if we had good

[016-096] luck and a south-west wind, and the sea was calm, we could make those

[016-097] eight hundred miles to Shanghai.”

[016-098] “And you could go—”

[016-099] “In an hour; as soon as provisions could be got aboard and the sails

[016-100] put up.”

[016-101] “It is a bargain. Are you the master of the boat?”

[016-102] “Yes; John Bunsby, master of the ‘Tankadere.’”

[016-103] “Would you like some earnest-money?”

[016-104] “If it would not put your honour out—”

[016-105] “Here are two hundred pounds on account sir,” added Phileas Fogg,

[016-106] turning to Fix, “if you would like to take advantage—”

[016-107] “Thanks, sir; I was about to ask the favour.”

[016-108] “Very well. In half an hour we shall go on board.”

[016-109] “But poor [[passepartout|Passepartout]]?” urged Aouda, who was much disturbed by the

[016-110] servant’s disappearance.

[016-111] “I shall do all I can to find him,” replied Phileas Fogg.

[016-112] While Fix, in a feverish, nervous state, repaired to the pilot-boat,

[016-113] the others directed their course to the police-station at Hong Kong.

[016-114] Phileas Fogg there gave Passepartout’s description, and left a sum of

[016-115] money to be spent in the search for him. The same formalities having

[016-116] been gone through at the French consulate, and the palanquin having

[016-117] stopped at the hotel for the luggage, which had been sent back there,

[016-118] they returned to the wharf.

[016-119] It was now three o’clock; and pilot-boat No. 43, with its crew on

[016-120] board, and its provisions stored away, was ready for departure.

[016-121] The “Tankadere” was a neat little craft of twenty tons, as gracefully

[016-122] built as if she were a racing yacht. Her shining copper sheathing, her

[016-123] galvanised iron-work, her deck, white as ivory, betrayed the pride

[016-124] taken by John Bunsby in making her presentable. Her two masts leaned a

[016-125] trifle backward; she carried brigantine, foresail, storm-jib, and

[016-126] standing-jib, and was well rigged for running before the wind; and she

[016-127] seemed capable of brisk speed, which, indeed, she had already proved by

[016-128] gaining several prizes in pilot-boat races. The crew of the “Tankadere”

[016-129] was composed of John Bunsby, the master, and four hardy mariners, who

[016-130] were familiar with the Chinese seas. John Bunsby, himself, a man of

[016-131] forty-five or thereabouts, vigorous, sunburnt, with a sprightly

[016-132] expression of the eye, and energetic and self-reliant countenance,

[016-133] would have inspired confidence in the most timid.

[016-134] Phileas Fogg and Aouda went on board, where they found Fix already

[016-135] installed. Below deck was a square cabin, of which the walls bulged out

[016-136] in the form of cots, above a circular divan; in the centre was a table

[016-137] provided with a swinging lamp. The accommodation was confined, but

[016-138] neat.

[016-139] “I am sorry to have nothing better to offer you,” said Mr. Fogg to Fix,

[016-140] who bowed without responding.

[016-141] The detective had a feeling akin to humiliation in profiting by the

[016-142] kindness of Mr. Fogg.

[016-143] “It’s certain,” thought he, “though rascal as he is, he is a polite

[016-144] one!”

[016-145] The sails and the English flag were hoisted at ten minutes past three.

[016-146] Mr. Fogg and Aouda, who were seated on deck, cast a last glance at the

[016-147] quay, in the hope of espying Passepartout. Fix was not without his

[016-148] fears lest chance should direct the steps of the unfortunate servant,

[016-149] whom he had so badly treated, in this direction; in which case an

[016-150] explanation the reverse of satisfactory to the detective must have

[016-151] ensued. But the Frenchman did not appear, and, without doubt, was still

[016-152] lying under the stupefying influence of the opium.

[016-153] John Bunsby, master, at length gave the order to start, and the

[016-154] “Tankadere,” taking the wind under her brigantine, foresail, and

[016-155] standing-jib, bounded briskly forward over the waves.

[016-156] CHAPTER XXI.

[016-157] IN WHICH THE MASTER OF THE “TANKADERE” RUNS GREAT RISK OF LOSING A

[016-158] REWARD OF TWO HUNDRED POUNDS

[016-159] This voyage of eight hundred miles was a perilous venture on a craft of

[016-160] twenty tons, and at that season of the year. The Chinese seas are

[016-161] usually boisterous, subject to terrible gales of wind, and especially

[016-162] during the equinoxes; and it was now early November.

[016-163] It would clearly have been to the master’s advantage to carry his

[016-164] passengers to Yokohama, since he was paid a certain sum per day; but he

[016-165] would have been rash to attempt such a voyage, and it was imprudent

[016-166] even to attempt to reach Shanghai. But John Bunsby believed in the

[016-167] “Tankadere,” which rode on the waves like a seagull; and perhaps he was

[016-168] not wrong.

[016-169] Late in the day they passed through the capricious channels of Hong

[016-170] Kong, and the “Tankadere,” impelled by favourable winds, conducted

[016-171] herself admirably.

[016-172] “I do not need, pilot,” said Phileas Fogg, when they got into the open

[016-173] sea, “to advise you to use all possible speed.”

[016-174] “Trust me, your honour. We are carrying all the sail the wind will let

[016-175] us. The poles would add nothing, and are only used when we are going

[016-176] into port.”

[016-177] “It’s your trade, not mine, pilot, and I confide in you.”

[016-178] Phileas Fogg, with body erect and legs wide apart, standing like a

[016-179] sailor, gazed without staggering at the swelling waters. The young

[016-180] woman, who was seated aft, was profoundly affected as she looked out

[016-181] upon the ocean, darkening now with the twilight, on which she had

[016-182] ventured in so frail a vessel. Above her head rustled the white sails,

[016-183] which seemed like great white wings. The boat, carried forward by the

[016-184] wind, seemed to be flying in the air.

[016-185] Night came. The moon was entering her first quarter, and her

[016-186] insufficient light would soon die out in the mist on the horizon.

[016-187] Clouds were rising from the east, and already overcast a part of the

[016-188] heavens.

[016-189] The pilot had hung out his lights, which was very necessary in these

[016-190] seas crowded with vessels bound landward; for collisions are not

[016-191] uncommon occurrences, and, at the speed she was going, the least shock

[016-192] would shatter the gallant little craft.

[016-193] Fix, seated in the bow, gave himself up to meditation. He kept apart

[016-194] from his fellow-travellers, knowing Mr. Fogg’s taciturn tastes;

[016-195] besides, he did not quite like to talk to the man whose favours he had

[016-196] accepted. He was thinking, too, of the future. It seemed certain that

[016-197] Fogg would not stop at Yokohama, but would at once take the boat for

[016-198] San Francisco; and the vast extent of America would ensure him impunity

[016-199] and safety. Fogg’s plan appeared to him the simplest in the world.

[016-200] Instead of sailing directly from England to the United States, like a

[016-201] common villain, he had traversed three quarters of the globe, so as to

[016-202] gain the American continent more surely; and there, after throwing the

[016-203] police off his track, he would quietly enjoy himself with the fortune

[016-204] stolen from the bank. But, once in the United States, what should he,

[016-205] Fix, do? Should he abandon this man? No, a hundred times no! Until he

[016-206] had secured his extradition, he would not lose sight of him for an

[016-207] hour. It was his duty, and he would fulfil it to the end. At all

[016-208] events, there was one thing to be thankful for; Passepartout was not

[016-209] with his master; and it was above all important, after the confidences

[016-210] Fix had imparted to him, that the servant should never have speech with

[016-211] his master.
