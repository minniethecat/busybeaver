# John Bunsby

John Bunsby was the master of the *Tankadere*, a swift pilot-boat that carried [[phileas-fogg|Phileas Fogg]], [[passepartout|Passepartout]], and [[aouda|Aouda]] from [[hong-kong|Hong Kong]] to Shanghai during a critical leg of Fogg's eighty-day journey. A man of about forty-five, vigorous and sunburnt, Bunsby was known for his skill in navigating the challenging waters of the Chinese seas. (PN: 011-177)

## The Tankadere

Bunsby took great pride in the *Tankadere*, a small but elegant vessel of some twenty tons. Its deck was white as ivory, its two masts leaned slightly backward, and it carried brigantine, foresail, and storm-jib rigging. The *Tankadere* had earned several prizes in pilot-boat races, and its master's confidence in her seaworthiness was absolute. (PN: 011-179)

The crew consisted of Bunsby and four hardy mariners who, like their master, knew the Chinese seas intimately. Together they formed a team capable of handling the vessel in conditions that would have deterred a larger ship. (PN: 011-183)

## The Hong Kong–Shanghai Passage

When Fogg and his party missed the *Carnatic* at Hong Kong, Bunsby agreed to take them to Shanghai — a voyage of some eight hundred miles across open sea. Though the journey bordered on reckless, Bunsby believed utterly in the *Tankadere*, which rode the waves like a seagull. Throughout the passage he remained in high hope, repeatedly assuring Fogg that they would reach Shanghai in time. (PN: 011-195)

The voyage was beset by storms and rough weather, but Bunsby's seamanship proved equal to the challenge. His intimate knowledge of tacking against the wind and his steady nerve kept the small craft on course when larger vessels might have been forced to turn back. (PN: 012-215)
