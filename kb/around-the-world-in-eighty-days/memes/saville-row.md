# Saville Row

Saville Row, Burlington Gardens, was the London residence of [[phileas-fogg|Phileas Fogg]], and the house in which the celebrated playwright and politician Richard Brinsley Sheridan died in 1814. It served as both the departure point and final destination of Fogg's circumnavigation of the globe. (PN: 001-159)

## Fogg's Residence

The mansion at No. 7 Saville Row, though not sumptuous, was exceedingly comfortable. Its occupant's methodical habits demanded little of the household beyond precision and order: Fogg spent ten hours out of every twenty-four in the house, either sleeping or preparing his toilet. At exactly half-past eleven each morning he would quit Saville Row and make his way to the [[reform-club|Reform Club]]. (PN: 001-293)

The house had known a very different character under Sheridan — described as a temple of disorder and unrest — but under Fogg's tenure it became a model of cosiness and regimented calm. (PN: 001-347)

## Role in the Journey

It was from Saville Row that [[passepartout|Passepartout]] first discovered the precise regularity of his new master's life, scouring the house from cellar to garret on his first day. When Fogg departed on his eighty-day journey, the house was left in Passepartout's care — a duty abruptly interrupted when master and servant departed for [[london|Charing Cross station]] within hours of Passepartout's arrival. (PN: 002-551)

Upon Fogg's return to London after his circumnavigation, the house in Saville Row briefly held [[aouda|Aouda]] as a guest before the party's final dash to the Reform Club brought the adventure to its triumphant conclusion. (PN: 031-699)
