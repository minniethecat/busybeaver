# Reverend Samuel Wilson

A missionary in Hong Kong whose chance encounter with the drugged and disoriented Passepartout leads to one of the novel's most vivid sequences — the rescue of a French valet from an opium den.

## Role in the Novel

Reverend Wilson appears in the Hong Kong chapters when Passepartout, drugged by Fix's accomplice, collapses in an opium den. Wilson, who frequents such establishments looking for souls to save, finds Passepartout and helps him to safety. (PN: 018-075)

His role is brief but thematically significant. Wilson represents the religious impulse that is otherwise largely absent from the novel — a counterpoint to the secular energies of commerce, empire, and adventure that drive the main plot.

## Key Actions

- Discovers Passepartout unconscious in a Hong Kong opium den (018-075)
- Assists Passepartout to safety and helps him reorient himself (018-088)
- Provides Passepartout with information about the next steamer to Yokohama (018-095)

## Character Significance

Wilson embodies the missionary dimension of European global presence — the spiritual counterpart to the commercial and imperial networks that enable Fogg's journey. His rescue of Passepartout from the opium den is a small-scale echo of Fogg's rescue of Aouda, suggesting a pattern of European intervention in non-European spaces.

> "The Reverend Mr. Wilson was one of those honest missionaries who devote themselves to the salvation of souls." (018-075)
