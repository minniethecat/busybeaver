# William Batulcar

The Honourable William Batulcar is the British-born manager of a Japanese acrobatic troupe performing at a Yokohama theatre. He briefly employs Jean Passepartout — mistaking him for a native performer — during Passepartout's desperate period in Japan after being separated from Phileas Fogg.

## Role in the Novel

Batulcar appears in the Yokohama episode (Section 19–20), where he serves as a temporary employer for the penniless Passepartout. His circus provides the setting for one of the novel's most comic sequences: Passepartout's stint as a human pyramid performer, which ends in dramatic fashion when he spots Fix and Fogg in the audience. ([019-068])

## Biography / Background

Batulcar is described as an "Honourable" British gentleman who has established himself as the manager of a Japanese acrobatic troupe in Yokohama. He presents himself with theatrical flair — "caressing the thick grey beard which hung down to his chest" — and combines British commercial instinct with the entertainment culture of the treaty ports. His establishment is grand enough to draw both local audiences and foreign travellers. ([019-073], [019-078])

## Key Actions

- **Hiring Passepartout**: When the destitute Passepartout arrives seeking work, Batulcar hires him as a servant, but quickly re-assigns him to a more theatrical role in the acrobatic troupe. ([019-078])
- **Doubting Passepartout's identity**: Batulcar shrewdly observes that Passepartout, despite claiming to be Japanese, is "no more a Japanese than I am" — a rare moment of cross-cultural perception in the novel. ([019-086])
- **The acrobatic performance**: Batulcar orchestrates the "Long Noses" performance, in which Passepartout participates as part of a human pyramid — a spectacle that ends in chaos when Passepartout spots Fogg and Fix in the audience. ([019-108], [019-113])
- **The aftermath**: When the pyramid collapses, Batulcar flies into a "furious rage" at the ruined performance, pursuing Passepartout backstage for compensation. ([019-193], [020-019])

## Character Significance

Batulcar is a minor comic figure who embodies the cosmopolitan, slightly absurd world of the treaty-port entertainment industry. His British origins and Japanese setting reflect the novel's interest in cross-cultural encounter, while his theatrical establishment serves as the improbable venue for reuniting Fogg and Passepartout. His instant recognition that Passepartout is not Japanese ("You are no more a Japanese than I am") is one of the few moments in the novel where disguise and cultural imposture are explicitly acknowledged — though Batulcar's commercial instincts override any curiosity about his new employee's true origins.

> The Honourable William Batulcar's establishment — that gentleman was a sort of Barnum, the manager of a troupe of jugglers, acrobats, clowns, and equilibrists. ([019-068])
