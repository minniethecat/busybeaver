Salt Lake City is the capital of the Mormon settlement in Utah, situated on the shores of the Great Salt Lake. In the novel, the transcontinental railway carries Fogg's party through this remarkable colony, and a Mormon elder boards the train to deliver an impromptu lecture on Mormon history and doctrine — one of the novel's few extended digressions into regional culture and religion.

## In the Novel

Fogg's party traverses the Great Salt Lake region by rail after crossing the Rocky Mountains. The train passes through the singular Mormon colony, and Elder William Hitch, a Mormon missionary, takes advantage of the captive audience in car No. 117 to deliver a lecture on Mormonism ([PN=022-138]–[PN=022-158]).

The party briefly visits Salt Lake City itself during a stop, though their observations are limited: "they had time to pay a visit to Salt Lake City, and to take a hasty survey of its streets and buildings" ([PN=023-071]). Passepartout is particularly struck by the city and, with typical French curiosity, is the only passenger to follow the Mormon elder's full lecture ([PN=022-163]).

## The Great Salt Lake

The Great Salt Lake, seventy miles long and thirty wide, is described in the novel as a remarkable remnant of an inland sea. Verne notes its high salinity — containing about one-fourth solid matter in its water — and its altitude of four thousand feet above sea level ([PN=023-057]). The surrounding landscape belongs to one of the most remarkable territories that the United States can boast.

## Significance

Salt Lake City serves as a narrative interlude. While the journey is fundamentally a race against time, Verne periodically pauses to present geographic and cultural observations — the Mormon settlement being one of the most extended. The episode reflects the Victorian fascination with American religious movements and the recently settled West, while also providing comic relief through Passepartout's earnest engagement with the elder's lecture, contrasting with the other passengers' indifference.

The city also marks the midpoint of the American transcontinental segment: from here, the train continues across Nevada toward the Sierra Nevada and ultimately [[sacramento]].
