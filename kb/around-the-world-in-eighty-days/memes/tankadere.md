# Tankadere

The *Tankadere* was a twenty-ton pilot-boat, commanded by John Bunsby, that carried [[phileas-fogg|Phileas Fogg]], [[aouda|Aouda]], and [[passepartout|Passepartout]] from Hong Kong toward Shanghai after they missed the departure of the *Carnatic*. The vessel's perilous voyage through a typhoon in the South China Sea is one of the most dramatic episodes of the novel.

## The Vessel

The *Tankadere* was "a neat little craft of twenty tons, as gracefully built as if she were a racing yacht." Its crew consisted of John Bunsby, the master, and four hands — "bold sailors" who knew every inlet and current of the China seas. Bunsby himself had gained several prizes in pilot-boat races, and Fogg's companions regarded him with confidence: "so far as the sea was concerned, there was perhaps no reason to fear." (PN: 016-121)

The vessel's compact size and speed made her ideal for the task. Under her brigantine, foresail, and standing-jib, the *Tankadere* "rode on the waves like a seagull" — a description that Verne returns to repeatedly as the weather worsens and the comparison with a seabird becomes ever more precarious. (PN: 016-167)

## The Voyage

Fogg chartered the *Tankadere* after Fix, conspiring to delay him in Hong Kong, got Passepartout drunk and separated from his master. When Fogg arrived at the dock, the *Carnatic* had already sailed for Yokohama. Rather than wait for the next steamer — which would have meant certain defeat — Fogg approached the sailors on the quay and negotiated passage with Bunsby to carry the party to Shanghai, where they might intercept the San Francisco steamer. (PN: 016-102)

The voyage of approximately eight hundred miles was supposed to take six days. But a typhoon transformed it into a struggle for survival. The *Tankadere* "was lifted like a feather by the wind" as Bunsby fought to keep her afloat. The storm drove the vessel northward at extraordinary speed while threatening, at every moment, to capsize her. Fogg, characteristically imperturbable, observed the storm with the same equanimity with which he observed everything. (PN: 017-011)

## A Turning Point

The *Tankadere* voyage marks a turning point in the journey's logic. Until Hong Kong, Fogg had relied on scheduled transportation — steamers and railways running to publicly posted timetables. The *Tankadere* represents the first major improvisation, the moment when the race against the clock required abandoning the predictable in favor of the desperate.

It also marks the moment when the journey becomes a collective enterprise. Bunsby and his crew, though hired for a fee, risk their lives for Fogg's cause. Aouda, who had joined the party in India, proves her fortitude during the storm. And Fix, though still scheming against Fogg, is physically aboard the same vessel — the detective and the fugitive now sharing the same fragile craft on the same dangerous sea.

> "She rode on the waves like a seagull." (016-167)
