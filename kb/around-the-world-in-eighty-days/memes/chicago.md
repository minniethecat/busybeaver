# Chicago

Chicago is the great railway hub of the American Midwest, where the transcontinental journey from [[san-francisco|San Francisco]] connects to the eastern rail network bound for [[new-york|New York]]. In the novel, Fogg passes through Chicago on the tenth of December as he races to complete his circumnavigation within the eighty-day limit.

## Role in the Novel

Chicago appears as a waypoint on the American leg of Fogg's journey. The train from [[omaha|Omaha]] arrives at Chicago at four o'clock in the evening on the 10th of December. The city is described not in terms of its architecture or inhabitants, but as a node in the railway network — a place where connections are made and schedules are kept or broken. (PN: 027-164)

> Nine hundred miles separated Chicago from New York; but trains are not wanting at Chicago. The locomotive of the Pittsburgh, Fort Wayne, and Chicago Railway left at full speed, as if it fully comprehended that the gentleman had no time to lose. (027-167)

The railway connection from Omaha to Chicago is provided by the Chicago and Rock Island Railroad, a historic line that linked the Midwest to the transcontinental Union Pacific at Omaha. For Fogg, Chicago represents the midpoint of his American crossing — approximately halfway between the Pacific coast and the Atlantic seaboard. (PN: 027-155)

## The American Railway Network

In the 1870s setting of the novel, Chicago had already established itself as the dominant railway center of North America. Five main trunk lines connected the city eastward to New York, while the Chicago and Rock Island provided the westward link to Omaha and the Pacific Railway beyond. This network made Fogg's transcontinental crossing feasible within the compressed timeframe his wager demanded.
