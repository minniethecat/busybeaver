# Technology and Innovation

A collection of pages exploring the technological innovations of the 1870s that make Phileas Fogg's journey possible. Steam-powered locomotives and vessels shrink travel times across continents and oceans. The global telegraph network — newly expanded — races ahead of Fogg, spreading news of his wager faster than he can travel. Precision timekeeping, from chronometers to railway timetables, turns circumnavigation from a years-long expedition into a calculated eighty-day sprint. These technologies are not merely background; they are central characters in Verne's novel of modern speed.
