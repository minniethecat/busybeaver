# Scotland Yard

The Metropolitan Police headquarters that dispatches Detective Fix on the global pursuit of a bank robber — a mission that Fix executes with more persistence than accuracy.

## Role in the Novel

This institution is part of the infrastructure — social, financial, legal, or commercial — that makes Phileas Fogg's eighty-day journey possible. Without the institutions of Victorian Britain and its empire, there would be no wager, no pursuit, no steamers, and no global itinerary.

## Key Functions

The organization represents one facet of the interconnected systems that Fogg navigates: the gentleman's club that provides the social framework for the wager, the bank whose robbery triggers Fix's pursuit, the shipping lines whose scheduled steamers cross the oceans, and the police force whose detective chases Fogg around the globe.
