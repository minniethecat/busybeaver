# Fort Kearney

Fort Kearney was a United States Army outpost and station on the Pacific Railroad in Nebraska, established to protect travelers and railway operations on the western frontier. It became a critical point during [[phileas-fogg|Phileas Fogg]]'s transcontinental crossing when the train came under attack from a band of Sioux warriors. (PN: 022-281)

## The Sioux Attack

As Fogg's train approached Fort Kearney, it was ambushed by a mounted band of Sioux warriors. The fort's garrison, only two miles distant, represented the passengers' sole hope of rescue. The train had to be stopped before it passed Fort Kearney station — for beyond it, the Sioux would be masters of the railway until the next station, and the attack would likely end in disaster. (PN: 022-283)

The commanding officer of Fort Kearney was present at the station with a hundred soldiers positioned to defend it. The fort's military presence allowed the train's passengers to mount a defense until the engine could be brought to a halt and the attackers repelled. (PN: 022-291)

## Aftermath

The train eventually stopped some twenty miles beyond Fort Kearney, having run low on fuel during the chaos of the attack. Despite the violence of the engagement — in which [[passepartout|Passepartout]] was briefly taken captive — the presence of Fort Kearney's garrison ensured that the situation did not end in catastrophe. For [[detective-fix|Detective Fix]], the stop at Fort Kearney marked a moment of deep discouragement, as he felt an almost overwhelming urge to abandon his pursuit of Fogg and return home. (PN: 023-305)
