# Samuel Fallentin

Samuel Fallentin is a London banker and member of the Reform Club, one of the five gentlemen who play whist with Phileas Fogg and participate in the famous wager on Fogg's ability to circumnavigate the globe in eighty days.

## Role in the Novel

Fallentin appears at the Reform Club during the wager scene (Section 2) and again at the novel's conclusion (Section 31). As one of Fogg's regular whist partners, he belongs to the inner circle of the Reform Club and contributes to the debate about whether a circumnavigation is mathematically feasible. ([002-132])

## Biography / Background

A banker by profession, Fallentin represents the financial and commercial interests of the Reform Club. Along with John Sullivan (also a banker), he brings a banker's calculating mindset to the wager — viewing Fogg's proposal not as an adventure but as a calculation of risk and probability. ([002-132])

## Key Actions

- **Participating in the wager**: Fallentin is among the five Reform Club members who agree to the £20,000 wager with Fogg. ([002-132])
- **Reconvening at the novel's end**: Fallentin appears at the Reform Club on the final day, alongside Sullivan and the other whist partners, to see whether Fogg will arrive in time. ([031-031])
- **Urging caution**: When the whist partners grow impatient as the deadline approaches without Fogg's appearance, Fallentin counsels prudence: "Let us be too hasty." ([031-044])

## Character Significance

Fallentin embodies the financial establishment of Victorian London — the world of bankers who assess risk in pounds sterling. His presence at the Reform Club underscores that Fogg's wager is not a romantic flight of fancy but a transaction evaluated by serious men of business. His measured response at the climax — urging the others not to be "too hasty" — reflects the banker's temperament: cautious, patient, unwilling to rush to judgment even when the evidence seems conclusive.

> John Sullivan and Samuel Fallentin, the bankers, were among the five who had taken up Fogg's wager. ([031-031])
