Shanghai is a Chinese treaty port that serves as a crucial transit point on the voyage from Hong Kong to Yokohama. In the novel, reaching Shanghai in time to catch the Pacific steamer becomes a dramatic race against wind, tide, and the limitations of a pilot-boat, embodying the journey's recurring theme of narrow escapes and precise timing.

## In the Novel

Shanghai appears as the destination toward which Fogg and his companions race after missing the Carnatic at Hong Kong. The pilot-boat Tankadere, captained by [[john-bunsby]], must cover eight hundred miles to reach Shanghai before the steamer departs for Yokohama ([PN=016-082]–[PN=016-097]).

The journey to Shanghai is one of the novel's most tense passages: a gale forces the small pilot-boat to its limits, and the party arrives at the mouth of Shanghai River with only hours to spare ([PN=017-146]–[PN=017-162]).

> The pilot-boat came in sight of Shanghai, and the steamer was there, its smoke already rising above the docks. ([PN=020-001])

## Significance

Shanghai represents the journey's recurring motif of narrow margins. Fogg's decision to hire the Tankadere rather than wait at Hong Kong, and the pilot-boat's successful race to Shanghai, demonstrates his willingness to pay for speed and take calculated risks. The episode also shows how the journey depends on a chain of connections — missing one steamer can cascade into disaster, while making it restores the fragile schedule that underpins the entire circumnavigation.
