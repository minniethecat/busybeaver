# Detective Pursuit

The parallel journey of [[fix-detective|Detective Fix]], who shadows [[phileas-fogg|Phileas Fogg]] across continents and oceans, convinced that the gentleman traveler is in fact the mastermind behind the theft of fifty-five thousand pounds from the Bank of England.

## The Telegraphic Trigger

Fix's pursuit begins with a telegraphic dispatch from London to Suez, informing the British consul that a suspected bank robber — described as "a well-dressed man" — is believed to be traveling on the *Mongolia*. Fix, stationed at Suez as a detective on the lookout for the thief, identifies Fogg as matching the description and begins his pursuit. The global telegraphic network, emblem of imperial infrastructure, becomes the instrument of the chase.

## A Hidden Journey

While Fogg's journey is public — reported in newspapers, wagered upon at the Reform Club — Fix's parallel journey is hidden. He follows Fogg from Suez to Bombay, from Bombay to Calcutta, from Calcutta to Hong Kong, and onward. His presence creates dramatic irony: the reader knows that Fix is pursuing an innocent man, while Fogg remains oblivious to the detective at his heels.

Fix is not merely an observer. He actively interferes: delaying Fogg in Calcutta by having him arrested, attempting to detain him in Hong Kong, and — in his most consequential intervention — getting Passepartout drunk and separated from his master, causing Fogg to miss the *Carnatic*. Each act of pursuit paradoxically threatens the very departure Fix needs to maintain if he is to eventually arrest Fogg on British soil.

## Narrative Function

The detective pursuit serves multiple functions in the novel's architecture. It provides a second thread of suspense independent of the race against the clock. It grounds the fantastical journey in the mundane machinery of law enforcement, creating a counterpoint to Fogg's mechanical precision. It introduces moral complexity: Fix is not a villain but a conscientious officer doing his duty, and his mistake is entirely plausible. And it sets up the novel's resolution, where Fix's arrest of Fogg in Liverpool — occurring just as the deadline passes — becomes the final obstacle before the date-line revelation.
