# Rangoon (Steamship)

The Rangoon is a [[p-and-o-company|P&O]] steamer plying the route from [[calcutta|Calcutta]] to [[hong-kong|Hong Kong]] via the Straits of Malacca and [[singapore|Singapore]]. It carries [[phileas-fogg|Phileas Fogg]], [[aouda|Aouda]], [[passepartout|Passepartout]], and the ever-present [[detective-fix|Fix]] on the second major sea leg of the [[around-the-world-route|circumnavigation]].

## In the Novel

Fogg and his party board the Rangoon at Calcutta after their overland journey across India — which included the [[the-elephant-journey|elephant journey]] and the [[the-rescue-from-suttee|rescue of Aouda from suttee]]. The steamer is moored half a mile off in the harbour, and Fogg boards with characteristic precision, having calculated the Rangoon's schedule as carefully as every other connection. (PN: 016-010)

The voyage from Calcutta to Hong Kong is eventful. The Rangoon passes through the Straits of Malacca, calls briefly at Singapore for coal, and then encounters a typhoon in the South China Sea. The storm forces the steamer to reduce speed and even to reef all sails, threatening Fogg's carefully calculated timetable. (PN: 019-025)

> "The 'Rangoon' — one of the Peninsular and Oriental Company's boats plying in the Chinese and Japanese seas — was a screw steamer, built of iron, weighing about seventeen hundred and seventy tons, and with engines of four hundred horse-power." (016-010)

## Passengers and Plot

The Rangoon carries a diverse complement of passengers — Indians, Ceylonese, Chinamen, Malays, and Europeans — and serves as the setting for key plot developments. Fix, who has managed to embark at Calcutta despite losing track of Fogg in India, is astonished to find himself once again on the same vessel as his quarry. The voyage also sees the deepening of the bond between Fogg and Aouda, who is traveling to Hong Kong to seek refuge with a relative. (PN: 018-015)
