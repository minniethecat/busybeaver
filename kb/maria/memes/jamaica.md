# Jamaica

Jamaica is the novel's other lost paradise — the island where [[maria|María]] was born to [[salomon|Salomón]] and his wife, where both her parents died, and where the legal documents of her inheritance reside. Salomón married in Jamaica in May 1820 (007-001), and the island remains a presence in the novel's wider geography, evoked in stories of hunting "stags in Jamaica" (015-028), and in a later chapter María is urged to go to her grandmother in Kingston (015-039).

## Role in the Novel

Jamaica functions as a double of the Cauca Valley — another fertile, beautiful place shadowed by death. María's connection to Jamaica makes her slightly exotic within the [[the-hacienda|hacienda]] world, her origins hinting at a wider geography of trade and migration. Its mention alongside Cuba (022-034) places the Cauca Valley within a Caribbean network that Isaacs knew from personal experience.
