# María's Epileptic Seizure

María's first witnessed epileptic seizure is a scene of clinical horror and emotional devastation. The body Efraín loves is convulsed by forces beyond control, and the threat of death — previously abstract, discussed in medical terms — becomes viscerally present. The seizure marks the point at which María's illness can no longer be discussed calmly; it must be confronted as embodied reality (015-032)(015-039)(015-044).

## Role in the Novel

This event is the novel's turn from romance toward tragedy. Before the seizure, María's epilepsy is a condition spoken of — a reason for caution, a justification for delay. After the seizure, it is an undeniable fact, visible to everyone (015-032)(015-044). The scene's clinical precision reflects Isaacs's own medical knowledge, while its emotional devastation reflects his personal investment in the character based on his sister. A later seizure confirms the inexorable progression of the disease (056-021).
