# Julian

Julian is an enslaved man who serves as the captain of the hacienda's work gang. He appears during the wedding celebration of [[Bruno]] and [[Remigia]], where he directs the musicians to play their best tune in honor of the master. (005-020, 005-021)

> When we arrived Julian, the slave captain of the gang, came out to help us dismount and to care for our horses. He was in his Sunday's best, and there hung from his belt the long knife with silver-plated sheath which was the sign of his office.
>
> (005-020)

## Role in the Novel

Julian appears briefly but his role as gang captain reflects the hierarchical structure within the enslaved population at the hacienda. He is entrusted with organizing labor and, in the wedding scene, takes initiative to honor the master, showing both his authority among the workers and his awareness of social obligations. (005-020)(005-021)

## Key Scenes

- Julian, as captain of the work gang, arrives with music for the wedding celebration. (005-020)
- Julian instructs the musicians to play their best tune because the celebration is for the master. (005-021)
