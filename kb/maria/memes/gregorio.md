# Gregorio

Gregorio is among the minor characters whose presence enriches the novel's portrait of hacienda society. He appears primarily in the river journey sequence, where he rows alongside Laurean on the Dagua — Isaacs's willingness to name and briefly characterize even peripheral figures gives *María* its documentary texture (051-011).

## Role in the Novel

Gregorio is among the minor characters whose presence enriches the novel's portrait of hacienda society. He is the more talkative of the boatmen (051-023), given to commentary on songs and stories, and his shout upon reaching shore arouses the whole post (051-045). Isaacs's willingness to name and briefly characterize even peripheral figures — workers, servants, neighbors — gives *María* its documentary texture, distinguishing it from novels that reduce secondary characters to mere plot functions.
