# The Tiger Hunt

The tiger hunt is the novel's most extended action sequence and its most sustained encounter with wild nature. The expedition begins when José and his nephew Braulio come to find Efraín, their eagerness to begin the hunt palpable — a moment that shifts the novel's tone from domestic intimacy to adventurous anticipation (020-002). Led by the skilled hunter José, the expedition takes Efraín and his companions into the mountains in pursuit of a tiger that has been killing livestock. José and Braulio's impatience to start the hunt underscores the seriousness of the predator threat to the hacienda's livestock (020-034). The hunt reveals dimensions of character invisible in the hacienda's domestic spaces — courage, skill, and the capacity to face danger (030-003) (030-062).

## Role in the Novel

The hunt serves as a masculine counterpoint to the novel's dominant romantic register. In the mountains, the values are different: physical courage replaces emotional sensitivity, practical skill replaces poetic sensibility. The encounter with the sublime — nature as overwhelming and dangerous — provides a necessary contrast to the beautiful (the garden, María's face). Yet even here, love intrudes: Efraín's thoughts return to María even in the wilderness.
