# Cuba

Cuba is mentioned in passing in the novel, part of the network of Caribbean and Latin American places that contextualize the Cauca Valley within a wider geographical frame. The comparison is made through Havana: "in Cuba nature is very much like what it is in [[cauca-valley|Cauca]]" (022-034).

## Role in the Novel

Cuba's presence reinforces the novel's awareness of a world beyond Colombia — a Spanish American geography connected by trade, migration, and shared colonial history. The comparison between Cuban and Cauca Valley landscapes suggests both the specificity and the typicality of the novel's natural setting (022-034).

## Key Passages

> ...in Cuba nature is very much like what it is in Cauca... (022-034)
