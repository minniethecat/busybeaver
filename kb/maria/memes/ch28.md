[028-001] The arrival of the mails, and the visit of the M——s, had accumulated a great deal to do in my [[efrains-father|father]]’s study. We worked steadily all the next day, but when we were with the family in the dining-room, [[maria|María]]’s smiles made me sweet promises for the hour of rest; in their strength I could go cheerfully to the most wearisome labor.

[028-002] At eight in the evening I went with my father to his bedroom, and responding to my usual goodnight, he added, “We have accomplished something, but there is still much to be done; so, then, early tomorrow morning.”

[028-003] On such days as this María was always waiting for me in the evening, talking with [[emma|Emma]] and my mother in the parlor, reading to the latter a chapter of the *Imitation* or teaching the children prayers.

[028-004] “Did you see your friend this morning?” she asked.

[028-005] “Yes; why do you ask that now?”

[028-006] “Because I have not been able to ask it before.”

[028-007] “And why are you interested in knowing?”

[028-008] “Did he ask you to [[travel-and-journeys|return]] his visit?”

[028-009] “Yes.”

[028-010] “And you are going to, aren’t you?”

[028-011] “Certainly.”

[028-012] “He is very fond of you, isn’t he?”

[028-013] “I have always believed so.”

[028-014] “And you still believe it?”

[028-015] “Why not?”

[028-016] “Are you as fond of him as when you were together in college?”

[028-017] “Yes; but what makes you talk about this today?”

[028-018] “It is because I want you always to be his friend, and hope he will keep on being yours. But you didn’t tell him anything?”

[028-019] “Anything of what?”

[028-020] “Why, of that.”

[028-021] “What do you mean by ‘that’?”

[028-022] “You know what I mean. You didn’t tell him, did you?”

[028-023] “This is the first time I ever failed to understand you.”

[028-024] “Oh, goodness! of course you understand. It is if you told him about what …”

[028-025] As I sat looking at her, while she smiled at me in an almost childish eagerness, she continued, “Very well; you need not tell me now.”

[028-026] She began to build little towers with backgammon-counters, with which we were playing.

[028-027] “If you don’t look at me,” I said, “I will not confess to you what I told [[carlos|Carlos]].”

[028-028] “Well, then, let’s see; now tell,” she replied, trying to do what I asked.

[028-029] “I told him everything.”

[028-030] “Oh, no, not everything?”

[028-031] “Did I do wrong?”

[028-032] “As if you could. But, then, why didn’t you tell him before he came?”

[028-033] “My father did not want me to.”

[028-034] “I know; but then he would not have come, and wouldn’t that have been better?”

[028-035] “Undoubtedly, but I could not do it; and now he is satisfied that I did not.”

[028-036] “He will keep on, then, being your friend?”

[028-037] “There is no reason why he should not.”

[028-038] “True, and I do not want him just for this—”

[028-039] “Carlos will thank you as much as I do for that.”

[028-040] “So you parted as usual? And he went away happy?”

[028-041] “As happy as he could be under the circumstances.”

[028-042] “Then I was not to blame, was I?”

[028-043] “No, María, and he will not think the less of you for what you have done.”

[028-044] “If he truly cares for you it ought to be so.”

[028-045] “But mamma is going,” she added, “and Emma is falling asleep. It’s time now, isn’t it?”

[028-046] “Must you go?”

[028-047] “And what am I going to do? Will you have to write so much tomorrow?”

[028-048] “Very likely.”

[028-049] “And when [[transito|Tránsito]] comes?”

[028-050] “When is she coming?”

[028-051] “At twelve; so she sent word.”

[028-052] “By that time we shall have finished. Goodbye till tomorrow.”

[028-053] She took leave of me with the same words, but was surprised that I kept the handkerchief which was in the hand she gave me to press. María did not understand that this scented handkerchief was my treasure for a whole night.
