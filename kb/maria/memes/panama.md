# Panama

Panama appears in the novel as a transit point — the isthmus crossed on the journey between Colombia and Europe. Before the Panama Canal, travelers crossed the isthmus by land; the journey was arduous and marked a definitive break with home (050-001). Efraín forwarded letters to Panama during his stay in [[kingston|Kingston]], and letters from [[maria|María]] reached him there, making the city a relay point for the correspondence that sustained his connection to the [[cauca-valley|Cauca Valley]] (050-034).

## Role in the Novel

Panama's narrative function is transitional — it is neither home nor destination, but the space between, the geographical embodiment of Efraín's liminal state as he moves between worlds (050-003). His voyage from [[buenaventura|Buenaventura]] through Panama to [[efrains-journey-to-europe|Europe]] traces the arc of separation that defines the novel's second half.

## Key Passages

> Among the many letters I forwarded to you at Panama. (050-034)
