Matilde is Carlos's love interest, a woman in Bogotá who occupies his thoughts during his time on the hacienda. She is mentioned in conversation between Carlos and Efraín as a parallel to Efraín's own romantic preoccupation with María — both young men are distracted by thoughts of women who are far away (042-005). (022-011)

## Role in the Novel

Matilde never appears directly in the narrative but serves as a foil to María — while Efraín's love is present on the hacienda, Carlos's Matilde is in distant Bogotá. Carlos jokes that Matilde "belongs to Bogotá," suggesting the tension between city and country life that runs through the novel (042-015). (042-005)

## Relationships

- **[[Carlos]]** — Carlos is romantically involved with Matilde and thinks of her during his time away from Bogotá (042-012).

## Key Scenes

- Carlos mentions "some desperate whim of Matilde's" in conversation with Efraín, revealing the emotional toll of long-distance love (042-005).
- Carlos imagines that Matilde is "actually my wife" to cope with the separation (042-013).
