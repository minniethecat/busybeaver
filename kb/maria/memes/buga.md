# Buga

Buga is a town in the Cauca Valley, known in the novel as a social destination for local families and a site of religious pilgrimage. It is referenced by characters as a place for Christmas gatherings and devotion to the *milagroso* (miraculous image), reflecting the regional culture of 19th-century Colombia. (042-007) (018-049)

> They all wanted me to go to Buga to spend Christmas, and in order to stay at home, I had to pretend that I had sprained my ankle.
>
> (042-007)

## Geography

Buga is located in the Cauca Valley region, accessible from the hacienda and from neighboring towns like [[Cali]]. Its position in the valley makes it part of the social and economic network that connects the landed families of the region. (018-049) (042-007)

## Cultural Significance

The town is associated with two distinct aspects of valley life: social recreation and religious devotion. Young landowners visit Buga for Christmas festivities, while others invoke the *milagroso of Buga* — a miraculous image venerated in the town's sanctuary — for protection during journeys. (042-007) (043-117)

## Key Appearances

- [[emigdio|Emigdio]] jokes that his cousin Zoila, who lives in San Pedro, rarely comes to Buga. (018-049)
- [[efrain|Efraín]]'s friend mentions that everyone wanted him to go to Buga for Christmas, which he avoided by pretending to have sprained his ankle. (042-007)
- Efraín's *comadre* promises to recommend him to the *milagroso* of Buga for a safe journey. (043-117)
