# Return to the Cauca Valley

Efraín returns to the Cauca Valley after years abroad, but the home he left no longer exists. María is dead, and the landscape that once promised happiness now serves only as the setting for grief. The return is not a reunion but a reckoning (059-001).

## Role in the Novel

This final return completes the novel's circular structure of departure and return. Each earlier return brought Efraín closer to María; this one takes him to her grave. The Cauca Valley, the novel's primary setting of love and beauty, is transformed by loss into a landscape of mourning.

## Key Passages

> I arrived in the city of Cali, intending to pass by the cemetery where was María's grave. (059-001)
