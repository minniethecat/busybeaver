# Remigia

Remigia is a household servant at the family hacienda whose wedding to [[Bruno]] is arranged by the patriarch. Her brief but memorable appearance illustrates the close, patriarchal oversight that the hacienda owner exercised over the lives of those who worked the estate. (005-009)

> Remigia, urged on by her husband and by the captain, at last agreed to dance a few moments with my father; but she dared not lift her eyes while she was doing it, and her dancing was rather constrained.
>
> (005-021)

## Role in the Novel

Remigia appears in a single chapter alongside Bruno, but her story serves an important thematic purpose. The arranged wedding, the preparations for confession, and the communal celebration demonstrate the integration of work, religion, and family life within the hacienda's social structure. (005-021) (005-020)

## Relationships

- **[[Bruno]]** — Remigia is married to Bruno in a ceremony arranged by the hacienda owner. (005-020)
- **[[efrains-father|Efraín's Father]]** — The patriarch who arranges the wedding and instructs the couple to go to confession. (005-009)

## Key Scenes

- The hacienda owner instructs Remigia and Bruno to go to confession before their wedding. (005-009)
- Remigia and Bruno are married the following Saturday, with music and celebration. (005-020)
- Remigia, urged by her husband and others, participates in the festivities prepared for the master. (005-021)
