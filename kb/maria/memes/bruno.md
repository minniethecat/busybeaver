# Bruno

Bruno is a worker at the family hacienda whose wedding to [[Remigia]] is arranged by the family patriarch. His brief but memorable appearance in the novel illustrates the patriarchal structure of hacienda life, where the landowners oversaw the personal affairs of those who worked their estate. (005-005)

## Role in the Novel

Bruno appears in a single chapter but serves an important thematic function: his arranged wedding demonstrates the authority of the hacienda owner over the lives of workers and the integration of social and economic relationships in 19th-century Colombian rural society. (005-016)

## Relationships

- **[[Remigia]]** — Bruno is married to Remigia in a wedding arranged by the hacienda owner. (005-020)
- **[[efrains-father|Efraín's Father]]** — The hacienda patriarch who arranges Bruno's wedding, reflecting his role as both employer and social authority. (005-005)
- **[[efrain|Efraín]]** — The young narrator who witnesses and recounts Bruno's story. (005-005)

## Key Scenes

- The hacienda owner informs Bruno that his wedding to Remigia has been arranged, framing it as a settled matter. (005-005)
- Bruno laughs good-naturedly at the owner's remarks, revealing his agreeable disposition. (005-016)
- Bruno and Remigia are married the following Sunday, marking a moment of communal celebration. (005-020)

Bruno is introduced through his wedding plans:

> As we met a group of slaves, my father said to a young negro of remarkably pleasant appearance, "Well, Bruno, your wedding is all arranged for the day after tomorrow?"
>
> (005-005)
