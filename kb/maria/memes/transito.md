# Tránsito

Tránsito is a young woman of the hacienda community whose courtship and marriage to José offers the novel's most complete portrait of love successfully realized. Her story serves as a structural counterpoint to Efraín and María's tragedy: where the central romance is thwarted by illness and separation, Tránsito and José's union proceeds uncomplicatedly to its happy conclusion (030-015) (020-014).

## Key Scenes

- **The wedding**: Tránsito's marriage to José is a moment of communal celebration — a rare instance of uncomplicated joy in the novel (030-015).\n- **The courtship**: Her romance with José unfolds in the background of the main plot, a quiet counter-narrative of love without obstacles (020-014).
