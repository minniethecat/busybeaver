Juntas is a village on the Dagua River, situated along the route from the Cauca Valley to the Pacific port of Buenaventura. Efraín passes through Juntas during his journey, and it serves as a waypoint where mule trains and travelers stop for rest and resupply (052-008). (052-008)

## Role in the Novel

Juntas appears in the travel sequence of the novel, representing the small settlements that dot the route between the highlands and the coast. The urgency of Efraín's journey is underscored by the decision not to spend the night there, pressing on instead toward the next stop (053-002). (052-015)

## Key Scenes

- The Dagua River is described as flowing below the village of Juntas (052-008).
- The traveling party exerts themselves to reach Juntas before dark (052-015).
- Efraín arranges with his guide not to spend the night in Juntas, pressing onward (053-002).
- Justo arrives at Juntas with the mules, and Lucía's boy reports on the condition of the animals (053-006).

> Urged on by Lorenzo and the extra pay he had promised them, the boatmen exerted themselves to get me to Juntas before dark. Soon after we passed the little plain of Sombrerillo, whose verdure was in contrast with the barrenness of the mountains which shaded it on the south. It was four when we passed by the foot of the dangerous rocks of Media-luna. Shortly afterwards we emerged from the dreaded Credo, and at last we made a happy end of our strange trip, leaping out on the shore at Juntas.
>
> (052-015)
