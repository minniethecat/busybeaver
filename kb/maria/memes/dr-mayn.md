# Dr. Mayn

Dr. Mayn is the physician consulted about María's epilepsy, and his medical judgment carries decisive weight in the novel's plot. He is first summoned during a storm in Chapter 14, when a family member rides out to fetch him — an early indication of the seriousness of María's condition (014-008). It is his diagnosis that informs Efraín's father's decision to make marriage conditional on María's health — a medically sound judgment that, tragically, ensures the lovers will never marry. Dr. Mayn represents the authority of 19th-century medicine: observational, diagnostic, but ultimately powerless to cure (015-039).

## Role in the Novel

Dr. Mayn is the physician consulted about María's epilepsy. He diagnoses her condition as epilepsy and is "almost positive" that she will not survive if subjected to emotional strain — this medical opinion becomes the basis for the father's marriage conditions (015-032). His warning about the dangers of emotional distress directly shapes the novel's central conflict (016-008). Even late in the novel, when María's condition worsens, the family still believes that consulting Mayn again might help — underscoring his role as the medical authority figure throughout the story (035-014). Dr. Mayn represents the authority of 19th-century medicine: observational, diagnostic, but ultimately powerless to cure (015-039).
