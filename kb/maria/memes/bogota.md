# Bogotá

Bogotá is the distant capital where [[efrain|Efraín]] is sent to study at the College. It represents the world beyond the Cauca Valley — urban, institutional, and impersonal — and Efraín's time there constitutes his first extended separation from [[maria|María]] and from home (001-001), an event detailed in the account of [[efrains-departure-to-bogota|his departure to Bogotá]]. Upon his return, his family asks him "a thousand things about Bogotá" and he must describe the city to them (004-004).

## Role in the Novel

Bogotá functions as the anti-Cauca: a place of duty rather than love, of study rather than feeling. Efraín's education there initiates the pattern of separation that structures the novel. While he was away, his father made significant improvements to the hacienda (005-002). Efraín brings back gifts from Bogotá — a "toilet-glass" for María, precisely like one he had given her before (006-002). The city also enters the novel's social texture: Efraín speaks of "the beautiful women of Bogotá" (011-001), and [[emigdio|Emigdio]] later talks of their Bogotá friends and neighbors (021-077).

## Key Passages

> I was still a mere boy when sent away from home to study in ——— College, founded a few years before in Bogotá... (001-001)
