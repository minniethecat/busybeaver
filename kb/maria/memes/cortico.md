Cortico is a boatman on the Dagua River who, together with Laurean, navigates Efraín's river passage. Also known by his given name Gregorio, Cortico sings the second verses of couplets while Laurean sings the first, their call-and-response filling the river with music as they travel (051-020). He is vigilant on the water, once shouting a warning about smugglers passing by (051-039). (051-008)

## Role in the Novel

Cortico is a minor character whose singing and banter with Laurean enliven the river travel scenes. Along with Laurean, he represents the skilled boatmen who ply the Dagua River, connecting the highlands to the Pacific port of Buenaventura. His alternate name, Gregorio, suggests the fluidity of identity among the river workers (051-011).

## Relationships

- **[[Laurean]]** — Cortico's fellow boatman and singing partner, with whom he trades couplets in call-and-response (051-020).
- **[[Bibiano]]** — Cortico accompanies Bibiano during the river journey (051-061).

## Key Scenes

- Cortico and Laurean are called by name to take good care of Efraín during the river passage (051-008).
- Cortico replies with the second couplet while Laurean sings the first, their voices alternating over the water (051-020).
- Cortico is offered a drink and urged to sing, his voice carrying across the river (051-013).
- Cortico remarks on the night dew as the boatmen navigate, his commentary adding to the river atmosphere (051-015).
- Cortico shouts a warning as smugglers pass by on the river (051-039).
