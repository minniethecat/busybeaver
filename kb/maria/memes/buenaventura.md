# Buenaventura

Buenaventura is Colombia's Pacific port, mentioned in the novel as a point of maritime departure. It represents the threshold between Colombia and the outside world — the place from which ships sail for [[panama|Panama]], Europe, and beyond. (036-040)

## Role in the Novel

Buenaventura functions as a liminal space — neither fully Colombian nor foreign, it is the place of embarkation, the last Colombian soil [[efrain|Efraín]] treads before departing for Europe on [[efrains-journey-to-europe|his journey abroad]]. (050-001) The port is also a place of anxious waiting — no letters arrived for Efraín during his two weeks there (050-010), deepening the novel's theme of separation and uncertainty. Letters from [[kingston|Kingston]] were meant to reach him here, making Buenaventura a node in the correspondence network that sustained his connection to [[maria|María]] and the [[cauca-valley|Cauca Valley]] (050-034).

## Key Passages

> The Emilia López on board of which I had come from Panama, anchored in the Bay of Buenaventura. (050-001)
