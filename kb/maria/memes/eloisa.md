Eloisa is one of Efraín's younger sisters, a child who plays cards with him and Felipe during his return to the hacienda (008-002). She is among the family members who take turns caring for the sick during times of illness, showing the closeness of the family unit (055-007).

> My mother, Eloisa, and Emma took turns that night in watching by my bed.
>
> (055-007)

## Role in the Novel

Eloisa is a minor character who contributes to the family portrait at the heart of the novel. Along with Felipe and the other younger children, she represents the lively domestic world that Efraín is both part of and, increasingly, separated from by his adult concerns and romantic tragedy. She "would have liked to have me keep on talking always" about Bogotá (011-001), and runs out excitedly to meet the family when something important is happening at the house (035-001).

## Relationships

- **[[efrain|Efraín]]** — Eloisa's older brother, who returns from school to the family home. She plays cards with him and enjoys hearing him talk (011-001).
- **[[felipe|Felipe]]** — Eloisa's brother, with whom she plays cards and shares childhood activities (008-002).
- **[[emma|Emma]]** — Eloisa's sister, with whom she takes turns caring for the sick (055-007).

## Key Scenes

- Felipe and Eloisa play cards with Efraín during his return to the hacienda (008-002).
- Eloisa runs out to meet the family, making signs about something happening inside the house (035-001).
- Eloisa, along with her mother and Emma, takes turns watching over the sick during the night (055-007).
