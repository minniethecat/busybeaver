# Andrea

Donna Andrea is the mother of [[Emigdio]] and a neighbor of [[efrain|Efraín]]'s family in the Cauca Valley. She is described as an agreeable hostess who welcomes Efraín during his visit to her household. (018-022)

## Role in the Novel

Andrea appears briefly as part of the social fabric of the valley. Her hospitality during Efraín's visit to Emigdio's home illustrates the cordial relations between neighboring landowning families and provides a glimpse of domestic life beyond the central hacienda. (018-022) (018-024)

## Relationships

- **[[Emigdio]]** — Andrea is Emigdio's mother, presiding over the household where Efraín visits. (018-022)
- **[[efrain|Efraín]]** — Andrea hosts Efraín during his visit, and he takes polite leave of her. (018-024)

## Key Scenes

- Efraín finds Donna Andrea entirely agreeable during his visit to Emigdio's home. (018-022)
- Efraín takes his leave of Señora Andrea at eleven o'clock after a pleasant visit. (018-024)

> Breakfast was served shortly, and I made myself entirely agreeable to Donna Andrea, Emigdio's mother, in the quarter of an hour we were together.
>
> (018-022)
