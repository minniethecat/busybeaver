# Emigdio

Emigdio is a cheerful, good-natured young man who is a close friend of [[efrain|Efraín]] and a neighbor of the family hacienda. Described as "a good boy," he is a landowner with a romantic disposition who provides companionship and local color in the novel's early chapters. (018-001) (016-030)

> Emigdio was a good boy. A year before my return to Cauca his father had sent him to Bogotá, with the purpose of putting him, as the excellent man said, in the way of becoming a merchant and man of affairs.
>
> (018-001)

## Role in the Novel

Emigdio appears primarily in the early chapters as Efraín's friend and confidant. His function is to illustrate the social world of the Cauca Valley — the friendships, visits, and amusements of young landowners. He also provides an early contrast to Efraín's more introspective and romantic nature. (017-003)

## Relationships

- **[[efrain|Efraín]]** — Emigdio is Efraín's boyhood friend. Efraín visits him upon returning to the valley, and the two share hunting and horseback riding. (018-002)
- **[[efrains-father|Efraín's Father]]** — Emigdio's father interacts with Efraín's father in business matters. (017-026)
- **Donna Andrea** — Emigdio's mother, mentioned as agreeable and hospitable. (018-022)

## Key Scenes

- Efraín arrives at Emigdio's house and the two friends embrace warmly. (018-002)
- Emigdio sits down hesitantly in Efraín's presence, showing his respectful nature. (018-005)
- Emigdio chooses a good swimming spot by the river during Efraín's visit. (018-041)
- Efraín takes his leave of Emigdio before departing for his journey, showing the depth of their friendship. (042-026)

## Character Analysis

Emigdio represents the uncomplicated life of the valley — hunting, riding, and socializing. Unlike [[efrain|Efraín]], whose education in Bogotá has given him a more reflective temperament, Emigdio lives fully in the present. His romantic reasoning about a girl — "How could she help being a lady if I loved her?" — reveals both his simplicity and his genuine warmth. (018-045) (017-008)
