# Antioquia

Antioquia is a Colombian province referenced in the novel, contributing to the geographical precision that distinguishes Isaacs's fiction. Its mention connects the Cauca Valley setting to the broader national territory. (020-014)

## Role in the Novel

Antioquia's presence in the novel reinforces its documentary quality — Isaacs names real places, grounding his romantic fiction in actual Colombian geography. (030-022) The region's distinctive cultural identity is further evoked through its folk songs, part of the broader theme of [[music-and-song|music and song]] in the novel (030-033). The character [[transito|Tránsito]]'s beloved came from Antioquia two months earlier, bringing the region's presence into the novel's intimate social world (020-014).

## Key Passages

> Two months before he had come from Antioquia to live with his uncle, and was already madly in love with his cousin, Tránsito. (020-014)
