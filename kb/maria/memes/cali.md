# Cali

Cali appears in the novel as a geographical reference point, one of the real Colombian places that anchor the fiction in actual geography. Its presence contributes to the novel's documentary precision — Isaacs names real towns, rivers, and regions, giving his love story the weight of specific place. [[efrain|Efraín]] is expected to arrive there on the 30th of January as part of the plan for his European departure (see [[efrains-journey-to-europe]]) (036-018)(036-040).

## Role in the Novel

Cali functions as a waypoint — a place passed through on journeys to and from [[the-hacienda|the hacienda]]. Its mention adds to the geographical texture that distinguishes *María* from more abstractly located Romantic fiction. The city also enters the novel's social world: the convent at Cali is mentioned as a possible place to confine a woman (042-071), and at one point "the family is in Cali" (054-006).

## Key Passages

> ...to get to Cali earlier in the morning. (057-004)
