# Lucía

Lucía is one of the girls of the hacienda household, frequently appearing alongside Tránsito in scenes of domestic life. She dresses "with more than usual care" for the return of Efraín (009-009) and receives a "pretty rosary" from Luisa along with Tránsito (009-014). Her presence enriches the novel's portrait of a household full of young women whose lives orbit the central drama.

## Role in the Novel

Lucía appears in key domestic and social scenes throughout the novel. She interrupts conversations with playful news — "We let the first melon of the year for you" (020-021) — and helps prepare breakfast when guests arrive (020-031). During the hunting party, she comes up to ask about Efraín's rifle (020-085) and joins in urging him to tell stories (020-090). She and Tránsito improvise a bed for Efraín (020-093), and later she is teased about her fear of horses (030-020).
