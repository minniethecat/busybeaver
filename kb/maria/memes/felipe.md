Felipe is one of Efraín's younger brothers, a lively and talkative boy who appears in family scenes throughout the novel. Already mounted on his little horse, Felipe chatters without pause during family outings, providing a child's perspective on the events that surround the central love story (034-072). He plays cards with Efraín and Eloisa (008-002).

## Role in the Novel

As a minor character, Felipe contributes to the domestic texture of the novel, representing the lively, noisy world of the younger children that contrasts with the intense, silent passion of Efraín and María. His presence in family scenes reminds the reader of the wider household that exists alongside the central romantic tragedy. During the tiger hunt episode, he urgently warns "Don't open the door, for brother is going to kill him" (024-002), showing a protective childish concern.

## Relationships

- **[[efrain|Efraín]]** — Felipe's older brother, whose return from school is a significant family event. Felipe is among the children who greet him (023-010).
- **[[maria|María]]** — Felipe participates in a family outing to the mountains that includes María, Efraín, and their mother (034-001).
- **[[emma|Emma]]** — Felipe's sister, who along with him is part of the bustling household.

## Key Scenes

- During Efraín's return, Felipe advises against opening a door, showing a protective, if childish, concern for his older brother's privacy (024-002). He breaks into the conversation with characteristic impatience (024-006).
- Felipe is called to come and get his coffee during a family morning scene, establishing the rhythm of daily hacienda life (034-061).
- Mounted on his little horse, Felipe chatters without stopping during a long ride, filling the silence that Efraín maintains (034-097).

Felipe appears as a playful younger brother:

> Felipe and Eloisa, who had got me to play cards with them, accused me of falling asleep. Felipe had in vain besought my mother to be allowed to go with me up the mountain in the morning, and on this account he went away unhappy.
>
> (008-002)
