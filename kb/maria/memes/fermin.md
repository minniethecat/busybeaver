Fermín is a young boy who works on the hacienda, described as a "little gossip" who helps carry gourds and runs errands. He assists [[salome|Salome]] with chores and is alternately scolded and relied upon by the older servants, embodying the rhythms of [[hacienda-life|hacienda life]] (043-002). (042-092)

## Role in the Novel

Fermín is a minor character who appears in the domestic scenes of the hacienda, contributing to the portrayal of daily life on the estate. His youth and talkative nature add a child's perspective to the working world of the servants, a world shaped by the hierarchies of [[slavery-and-servitude|slavery and servitude]] in the novel (042-092). (042-123)

## Relationships

- **[[salome|Salome]]** — Fermín helps Salome carry gourds and runs errands for her (043-111).

## Key Scenes

- Fermín is called for, and someone asks where he has hidden himself (042-092).
- Fermín, loaded down with gourds, walks alongside Salome during the chores (042-123).
- Salome comments that Fermín is "such a little gossip," characterizing his talkative nature (043-002).
- Fermín returns with Salome, both loaded with gourds, as the day's work concludes (043-111).
