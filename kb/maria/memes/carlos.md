# Carlos

Carlos is a secondary figure from the hacienda's social world, mentioned among the hacendados of the region, such as those at the [[hunting-expedition|hunting expedition]]. His name appears alongside others like Don Ignacio, Don Jerónimo, and Anselmo in the social landscape of the Cauca Valley. (020-034)

## Role in the Novel

Carlos represents the broader social network within which the hacienda operates. He is part of the world of landowners and neighbors who form the economic and social fabric of the region — figures who appear at gatherings, hunts, and visits that structure hacienda life.

## Key Scenes

- Present at the hunting expedition on Don Jerónimo's Chagra estate (020-034)
- Part of the social circle of landowners in the Cauca Valley

## The Marriage Proposal

Carlos's most significant narrative function in the novel is as Efraín's rival for [[maria|María]]'s hand. [[efrains-father|Efraín's father]] reveals the proposal in a tense conversation with his son, testing his commitment to María:

> "Four days ago I received a letter from Señor M——, asking María's hand for his son, Carlos."
>
> (015-040)

The proposal sends [[efrain|Efraín]] into jealous anguish. For ten days afterward he avoided the house, "not trusting myself to adopt the bearing with María which my father desired, and sorrowfully reflecting on the proposal of marriage made for Carlos" (016-001). The episode is a catalyst in the novel's central love plot — crystallizing the threat of separation and the social forces (parental authority, wealth, geographical distance) that press against Efraín and María's relationship. Carlos himself is not a villain; he is an unwitting rival whose claim to María's hand is advanced by his father, [[don-jeronimo|Don Jerónimo]], and the conventions of hacienda society (015-040).
