# Rufina

Rufina, affectionately called "ña' Rufina," is a woman who lives at a river settlement along the Dagua River. She is the companion of [[Gregorio]] and hosts [[efrain|Efraín]] during his journey through the tropical lowlands, providing food, shelter, and warm conversation. (051-054)

> Rufina, who had come down to greet me, remarked with laughter that they had sometimes killed larger ones.
>
> (051-066)

## Role in the Novel

Rufina appears in the river-journey chapters as part of the human landscape Efraín encounters while traveling through the Dagua region. Her domestic presence — cooking, chatting with [[Lorenzo]], calling out to her companion — adds texture and warmth to the travel narrative. (051-073) (051-066)

## Relationships

- **[[Gregorio]]** — Rufina is Gregorio's companion; he calls out to her as they approach the settlement. (051-054)
- **[[efrain|Efraín]]** — Rufina greets Efraín and hosts him during his overnight stay at the settlement. (051-066)
- **[[Lorenzo]]** — Rufina gossips with Lorenzo while he prepares provisions. (051-073)

## Key Scenes

- Gregorio shouts "ña' Rufina!" as the party approaches the river settlement. (051-054)
- Rufina comes down to greet Efraín upon his arrival at the settlement. (051-066)
- Rufina gossips with Lorenzo while he brings in provisions for the journey. (051-073)
- Rufina calls out from her room, speaking to her companion in a moment of domestic intimacy. (051-077)
- Rufina's voice is fittingly the last thing heard before the next stage of the journey. (051-081)
