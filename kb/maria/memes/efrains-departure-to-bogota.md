# Efraín's Departure to Bogotá

The novel opens with Efraín's departure from the family hacienda to study in Bogotá. This childhood separation is the primal loss that prefigures all subsequent departures. His sisters' tears, his mother's embrace, and María's blushing farewell establish the emotional stakes that will define the entire narrative (001-001) (001-003) (001-004).

## Role in the Novel

This departure establishes the novel's fundamental pattern: leaving the Cauca Valley means leaving love. Each subsequent departure — for Bogotá again, for Europe — repeats and intensifies this original loss. The scene also introduces the novel's integration of landscape and emotion: the fading murmur of the Zabaletas River becomes the acoustic signature of separation.

## Key Passages

> I was still a mere boy when sent away from home to study in ——— College... (001-001)\n\n> I threw a last glance backward: María was behind the creeper that climbed up by the windows of my mother's room. (001-004)
