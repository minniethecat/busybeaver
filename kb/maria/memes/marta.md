# Marta

Marta is the elderly cook at the family hacienda, a long-serving member of the household whose presence adds warmth and familiarity to domestic life. She appears in scenes of family gatherings, where her reactions and interactions reflect the deep bonds between the family and its servants. (020-036)

> Even the pet of Marta, the cook, Palomo, whom the very hares knew to be stone-blind, offered his neck to be counted among the able-bodied dogs.
>
> (020-036)

## Role in the Novel

Marta is a minor domestic character whose primary function is to embody the continuity and intimacy of hacienda life. As the cook, she is part of the daily rhythm of the household, and her presence at family moments — including the return of [[efrain|Efraín]] — reinforces the sense of community that defines the estate. (020-088) (020-085)

## Relationships

- **[[efrain|Efraín]]** — Marta is among those who welcome Efraín upon his return to the hacienda. (020-085)
- **[[jose|José]]** — Marta interacts with José, the family patriarch, in domestic settings. (020-088)
- **Palomo** — Marta's pet, mentioned affectionately as part of the household's daily life. (020-036)

## Key Scenes

- Marta's pet Palomo is mentioned during a pleasant domestic scene, showing her integration into hacienda life. (020-036)
- Old Marta, with half-plucked chicken in hand, joins others in welcoming the family. (020-085)
- Marta replies to José with characteristic familiarity, reflecting her established place in the household. (020-088)
