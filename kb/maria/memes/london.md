# London

London represents the ultimate distance from home — the European metropolis where [[efrain|Efraín]] completes his medical studies and where news of [[maria|María]]'s death reaches him. It is the place of exile at its most extreme: not just geographically distant but culturally and emotionally alien (017-023) (053-003).

## Role in the Novel

London is barely described — it exists in the novel primarily as absence, the place where Efraín is when María dies. Its narrative function is to be not-the-Cauca, the place where love cannot survive (048-001). Efraín left for London two hours after receiving word (049-020), and nights there, as on the high seas, never brought peace (051-075).

## Key Passages

> I had been two weeks in London, when one night I received letters from home. (048-001)
