# Luisa

Luisa is [[efrain|Efraín]]'s mother, the wife of José and mistress of the family hacienda. She is a gentle, maternal presence in the novel, characterized by her warmth, devotion to her family, and her role in maintaining the domestic tranquility of the household. (009-011)

A figure of steady affection, Luisa appears throughout the novel as the emotional heart of the home, receiving Efraín with joy upon his return and tending to the wellbeing of all who live under her roof. (020-016)

> Luisa, his wife, somewhat more fortunate than he in the contest with time, preserved in her manner of dress something of the city style, and her vivacity and pleasant ways made it clear that she was contented with her lot.
>
> (009-011)

## Role in the Novel

Luisa serves as the domestic center of the hacienda. She is not merely a background figure but an active presence who manages household affairs, cares for her children, and provides emotional support to her husband José. Her reactions to events — particularly her concern for Efraín's health and her grief over [[maria|María]]'s decline — reflect the novel's deeper emotional currents. (009-014) (016-003)

## Relationships

- **[[efrain|Efraín]]** — Luisa is Efraín's devoted mother, deeply concerned for his wellbeing and overjoyed at his return from Bogotá. (020-018)
- **[[jose|José]]** — Luisa is José's wife and partner in managing the hacienda. (009-011)
- **[[maria|María]]** — Luisa treats María as a daughter, adorning her room and caring for her during her illness. (045-023)
- **[[transito|Tránsito]]** — Luisa's relationship with her daughter Tránsito reflects the warmth of the family. (020-023)

## Key Scenes

- Luisa receives Efraín upon his return from Bogotá, expressing both joy and concern for his health. (020-015)
- Luisa adorns María's room with furniture and decorations, showing her love for the young woman she treats as a daughter. (045-023)
- Luisa attends the family breakfast after María's room is prepared, her demeanor reflecting the emotional weight of the household's circumstances. (045-025)
- Luisa receives gifts and a reliquary, highlighting her connection to family traditions and religious devotion. (020-001)
