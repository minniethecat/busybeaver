Bibiano is a river-dwelling worker who lives along the Dagua River route. He is the father of a young black girl who prepares stew for Efraín's traveling party, and he sleeps in the same room with the travelers during their overnight stay (051-061).

## Role in the Novel

Bibiano is a minor character who appears in the river travel sequence, representing the Afro-Colombian river communities that Efraín encounters on his journey. His presence, along with his daughter's hospitality, adds texture to the travel narrative. He shares the room with the travelers at night, and Efraín questions him about a mysterious flying creature in the darkness (051-082). (051-071)

## Relationships

- **[[Cortico]]** — Bibiano accompanies Cortico during the river passages (051-061).

## Key Scenes

- Cortico asks whether "Uncle Bibiano" is present, establishing Bibiano as a familiar figure among the river workers (051-061).
- Bibiano's daughter prepares stew for the traveling party, providing hospitality at the river stop (051-073).
- Bibiano sleeps in the same room as the travelers, and Efraín asks him about a strange flying thing in the night (051-077).
- Bibiano responds to the mysterious sound in the night, asking "Do you hear that, man?" (051-080).
- Efraín questions Bibiano about the flying creature, almost imagining it to be something supernatural (051-082).
- Bibiano and his daughter commend the travelers to God as they depart at dawn (052-001).

Bibiano is introduced with a vivid physical description:

> Bibiano, the father of the young negress, who was a boatman more than fifty years old, already laid aside by rheumatism, came out to welcome me, hat in hand, and leaning upon a thick cane. His pantaloons were of yellow baize, and he wore a blue striped shirt outside them.
>
> (051-071)
