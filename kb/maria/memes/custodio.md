# Custodio

Custodio is a minor but vividly rendered figure in the hacienda community. He serves multiple roles — "the combined functions of swineherd, bird-catcher, and gardener" (042-031) — and his presence helps Isaacs populate his fictional world with convincing particularity. Efraín encounters him emerging from the forest (042-031), and later visits his house (042-084). The novel's secondary characters may not drive the plot, but they create the sense of a real, functioning society — a world in which the central love story is embedded and against which its exceptional intensity is measured.

## Role in the Novel

Custodio is a compadre of Emigdio (042-026) and a recognizable figure in the hacienda's social world. His practical skills — herding swine, catching birds, tending the garden — embody the self-sufficient economy of the estate. Isaacs gives even this minor character moments of distinct personality: Custodio has "reason enough to be jealous" (042-116), and his name appears in conversations about the community's social dynamics (043-135).
