# Tiburcio

Tiburcio is a mulatto hunter, a peon from the Chagra hacienda who participates in the tiger hunt alongside [[efrain|Efraín]], [[jose|José]], [[braulio]], and [[lucas|Lucas]]. He is one of "the five hunters" who set out that day (020-035), and throughout the hunt he proves himself a capable and steady companion.

## Role in the Novel

Tiburcio appears primarily in the tiger hunt sequence, where his skills are displayed in practical tasks — climbing to a vantage point with [[jose|José]] and [[efrain|Efraín]] (020-049), turning "olive color" at a moment of danger (020-058), rushing to the fallen animal (020-061), and flaying the jaguar "with wonderful dexterity" after the kill (020-080). He also wraps up the skin with [[braulio]] (020-092) and fills the hunters' cups with water (020-078). His presence exemplifies the multi-racial character of the hacienda's working community and the competence of its laborers.

## Relationships

- [[braulio]] — Fellow hunter.
- [[efrains-father|Efraín's Father]] — The patriarch who employs him.

## Key Scenes

- **The tiger hunt**: Tiburcio is one of five hunters, staying with Efraín at a key moment (020-046)(020-049). He turns pale at danger (020-058) and rushes to the kill (020-061).
- **Skinning the jaguar**: Tiburcio flays the dead animal with remarkable skill (020-080), then wraps up the skin with [[braulio]] (020-092).
