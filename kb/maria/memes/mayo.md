Mayo is [[efrain|Efraín]]'s loyal hunting dog, a constant companion who follows him through the forests and fields of the [[cauca-valley|Cauca Valley]]. A trusty dog who waits attentively at his master's feet, Mayo appears throughout the novel as a quiet but faithful presence, accompanying Efraín on hunts, visits to neighbors, and solitary walks through the estate (009-001)(009-013).

## Role in the Narrative

Mayo serves as Efraín's silent companion during moments of both action and reflection. The dog follows Efraín through dangerous terrain — during the tiger hunt, Mayo runs by Efraín in "great fright" at the scent of the predator (021-007). His presence marks Efraín's integration into the masculine world of the hacienda: hunting, riding, and managing the land. Even after a long day, Mayo is found "licking off the burrs" from his coat (010-002), and his faithfulness is noted as he follows Efraín everywhere (020-001).

## Key Scenes

- Efraín takes his rifle and gives a signal to Mayo, the trusty dog seated on his hind-legs watching fixedly (009-001). Mayo sits at Efraín's feet with attentive expectation (009-013).
- At José's house, Mayo howls "in cowardly fashion" when confronted by the barking dogs (009-004), showing a timid side despite his hunting role.
- During the tiger hunt, Mayo runs past Efraín in great fright at the predator's scent (021-007).

> I took my rifle; gave a signal to Mayo, the trusty dog who was seated on his hind-legs watching me fixedly, his forehead wrinkled in the excess of his anxiety to perceive the first order; leaped over the stone wall, and took the mountain path.
>
> (009-001)
