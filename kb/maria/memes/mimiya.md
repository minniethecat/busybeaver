Mimiya is an affectionate family nickname, most closely associated with Juan, the youngest child on the hacienda. The name is used in playful exchanges between Efraín and Juan, and it represents the warmth and intimacy of family life on the estate (044-002). (030-075)

## Role in the Novel

Mimiya appears as a term of endearment that reveals the tenderness of family relationships. When Efraín asks Juan "what have you done today with your Mimiya," the question carries the affectionate teasing that characterizes adult-child interactions in the novel (044-002). (044-002)

## Key Scenes

- A character jokes that "it is only a joke of your Mimiya," using the nickname affectionately (030-075).
- Efraín asks Juan about his day with "your Mimiya" (044-002).
- Juan explains that "Mimiya loves mamma more than me," revealing a child's perception of family affection (044-009).

> "No, no, my little man," said she, "it is only a joke of your Mimiya." And she kissed him.
>
> (030-075)
