Lucas is a worker on the family hacienda who joins the tiger hunt expedition. Known for his boastful nature, Lucas is described as "boastful" and a "braggart" by the other hunters, and his courage fails him at a critical moment during the chase (020-004). His comic cowardice provides a moment of levity amid the tension of the hunt. (020-004)

## Role in the Novel

Lucas is a minor character whose role in the tiger hunt episode illustrates the social hierarchy of the hacienda and provides comic relief. While the principal hunters — José, Braulio, and Tiburcio — demonstrate courage and skill, Lucas's flight from danger serves as a foil to their bravery (020-058). (020-034)

## Relationships

- **[[jose|José]]** — The lead hunter who calls out for Lucas after he disappears during the jaguar chase (020-069).
- **[[Braulio]]** — A fellow hunter who emerges from the undergrowth with Lucas during the hunt (020-051).
- **[[Tiburcio]]** — Another hunter who participates in the tiger pursuit alongside Lucas.

## Key Scenes

- Lucas carries the hamper prepared by Luisa as the hunting party sets out, establishing him as a helper rather than a lead hunter (020-034).
- During the tiger hunt, Lucas disappears at the critical moment when the jaguar charges, earning him mockery from José and the other hunters (020-058).
- José shouts "Lucas, you rogue!" into the mountains, and the echoes return his contempt for Lucas's cowardice (020-075).
