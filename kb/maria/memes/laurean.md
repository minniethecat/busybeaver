Laurean is a boatman who navigates the Dagua River during Efraín's journey through the Pacific lowlands. With an athletic body silhouetted against the river landscape, Laurean is a skilled riverman who sings couplets with his companion Cortico and looks after Efraín's safety on the water (051-011). (051-011)

> The athletic body of Laurean was sketched, like the profile of a giant, against the last flush of the vanishing moon.
>
> (051-011)

## Role in the Novel

Laurean appears in the travel passages of the novel, representing the working men of the river who facilitate Efraín's journey. Along with Cortico and Gregorio, he provides safe passage and local knowledge, while his singing and banter bring life to the river scenes (051-008). (051-015)

## Relationships

- **[[Cortico]]** — Laurean's fellow boatman, with whom he trades couplets and shares the work of navigating the river (051-020).
- **[[Gregorio]]** — Another companion on the river journey, who shares the boat with Laurean (051-044).

## Key Scenes

- Cortico calls out to Laurean, identifying him as the boatman tasked with caring for Efraín during the river passage (051-008).
- Laurean samples brandy "like an expert" before the journey, establishing his character as a seasoned riverman (051-018).
- Laurean and Gregorio, in their breeches, accompany Efraín while he secures a safe-conduct for the journey (051-044).
