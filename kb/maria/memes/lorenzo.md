# Lorenzo

Lorenzo is a faithful servant and river guide who accompanies the narrator [[efrain|Efraín]] on his journey through the [[dagua-river|Dagua River]] region. Though not a slave, he is devoted to the family and serves as both guide and caretaker during Efraín's travels. (050-011)

A loyal and resourceful character, Lorenzo appears primarily in the river-journey chapters, where his practical knowledge, warm personality, and dedication to Efraín's wellbeing are consistently demonstrated. (050-006) (050-019)

> Lorenzo was not a slave. A faithful companion of my father, during the time of his frequent business journeys, he was loved by the whole family, and enjoyed in our home the rights of an overseer and the privileges of a friend.
>
> (050-011)

## Role in the Novel

Lorenzo is a supporting character whose primary function is to facilitate Efraín's journey through the tropical lowlands. He serves as a boatman, guide, and host, arranging lodging, preparing meals, and navigating the dangerous river passages. His presence grounds the narrative in the physical reality of 19th-century Colombian travel. (051-011) (050-028)

## Relationships

- **[[efrain|Efraín]]** — Lorenzo is Efraín's companion and guide during the river journey, showing unwavering loyalty and care for the protagonist. (051-052)
- **The boatmen** — Lorenzo coordinates with the local boatmen, paying them and managing the river travel logistics. (052-015)

## Key Scenes

- Lorenzo recognizes Efraín upon his arrival and prepares for the river journey. (050-006)
- Lorenzo arranges a bed for Efraín in a rustic shelter during the night. (051-011)
- Lorenzo runs to investigate cries heard on the river, demonstrating his alertness. (051-074)
- Lorenzo brings Efraín a riding suit and helps him transition from river to land travel. (053-002)
