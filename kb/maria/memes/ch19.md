[019-001] My mother and [[emma|Emma]] came out to the corridor to welcome me. My [[efrains-father|father]] had mounted his horse and gone to oversee the farm-work.

[019-002] Very soon they called me to the dining-room, and I promptly went there because I thought I should meet [[maria|María]]; but I was disappointed. When I asked my mother for her, she said, “As those people are coming tomorrow, the girls are very anxious to have some fine dishes ready; but I think that they must have finished by this time, and that they will soon be here.”

[019-003] Just as I was rising from the table, [[jose|José]], who was going up the mountain, driving two mules loaded with cane, paused at a point from which he could see into the house, and called to me:

[019-004] “Good afternoon! I can’t stop, because I have a big load and night is coming on. I’ve left a little present for you with the children. Get up early tomorrow, for it will be a sure thing.”

[019-005] “Very well,” I replied, “I will start very early. My regards to all.”

[019-006] “Don’t forget the buckshot.”

[019-007] He waved his hat to me, and passed on.

[019-008] I went to my room to get my rifle ready; not that it really needed cleaning, but I wanted a pretext for leaving the dining-room, since María was not to be found there. I had opened and held in my hand a little box of cartridges, when I saw María coming, bringing me my coffee, which she was stirring with a spoon. My cartridges fell all over the floor as soon as she drew near. Without looking at me, she said, “Good afternoon,” and put down the cup with a rather unsteady hand; then for an instant she let her timid eyes rest upon mine; she smiled, and dropping to her knees, began to pick up the cartridges.

[019-009] “Don’t you do that,” said I; “I will do it afterwards.”

[019-010] “I have good sharp eyes,” she replied, “to find little things like these. Where is the box?”

[019-011] She reached out her hand to take it, and exclaimed, as she saw it, “What! every one dropped!”

[019-012] “It wasn’t full,” I said, beginning to help her.

[019-013] “And what do you want of these tomorrow?” she asked, blowing the dust off some of the cartridges which she held in one of her pink hands.

[019-014] “What do you know about tomorrow and about these things?”

[019-015] “Well, I know that this [[hunting-and-horses|hunting]] is dangerous, and I imagine that it is a dreadful thing to miss a shot, and, besides, I know by the box that these are the cartridges which the [[illness-and-medicine|doctor]] gave you, and said that they were English, and very good ones.”

[019-016] “You hear everything.”

[019-017] “Sometimes I would have given a good deal not to hear. Perhaps you had better give up this hunt—José left a present with us for you.”

[019-018] “Do you wish me not to go?”

[019-019] “How could I ask that?”

[019-020] “Why not?”

[019-021] She looked at me, but did not reply.

[019-022] “I think that must be all,” said she, rising to her feet, and looking around the floor. “I must go now. Your coffee will be cold.”

[019-023] “You taste it.”

[019-024] “But don’t stop to load your rifle now. It’s good,” she added, handing me the cup.

[019-025] “Well, I’ll leave the rifle now, and take the coffee; but don’t go away.” I had gone into my room and come out again.

[019-026] “Is there much for you to do in there?”

[019-027] “Oh yes,” I answered, “getting ready for tomorrow. But must you go?”

[019-028] She shrugged her shoulders, and leaned her head on one side, as if to say, “As you wish.”

[019-029] “I owe you an explanation,” said I, going nearer to her. “Will you hear me?”

[019-030] “Didn’t I tell you that there are things which I do not want to hear?” she replied, rattling the box of cartridges.

[019-031] “But I thought that I …”

[019-032] “It’s surely that you are going to speak of … what you thought.”

[019-033] “What?”

[019-034] “That I ought to listen to you; but I ought not this time.”

[019-035] “How bad an opinion you must have had of me lately!”

[019-036] She did not reply, and busied herself reading the lettering on the box.

[019-037] “I’ll say nothing, then. But tell me, what did you think?”

[019-038] “Why should I tell you now?”

[019-039] “Won’t you allow me to apologize to you?”

[019-040] “What I should like to know is why you did that; yet I dread to know it, because I can’t imagine what motive you had, and I have always thought that you must have had one which I ought not to know. But, as it seems that you are happy again, why, I am happy too.”

[019-041] “I don’t deserve that you should be so good to me.”

[019-042] “Perhaps I am the one who do not deserve …”

[019-043] “I treated you unfairly, and if you will allow it, I will go down on my knees to ask your pardon.”

[019-044] Her eyes, which had been veiled for a few moments, now shone with all their [[beauty-and-aesthetics|beauty]], and she exclaimed: “Good heavens! no. I have forgotten it all; do you hear? All.”

[019-045] “But on one condition,” she added, after a short pause.

[019-046] “Whatever you wish.”

[019-047] “If ever I say or do anything that displeases you, you are to tell me of it; and I will not say or do it again. That’s very easy, isn’t it?”

[019-048] “And I, can’t I ask the same thing of you?”

[019-049] “No, because I am not able to give you advice, nor always to know if what I think is best; besides, you know what I am going to say to you before I say it.”

[019-050] “Is that true? Then you must be sure that I [[romantic-love|love]] you with all my heart,” said I, in a low voice, and strongly moved.

[019-051] “Yes, yes,” she replied, softly, and almost touching my lips with her hand to tell me I must be silent took a few steps towards the parlor.

[019-052] “What are you going to do?” I asked.

[019-053] “Don’t you hear Juan calling me, and crying because he can’t find me?”

[019-054] Hesitating for a moment, there was in her smile such sweetness and amorous languor that after she was gone I could still see her face.
