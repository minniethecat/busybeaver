# Tránsito's Wedding

Tránsito's marriage to José is the novel's most complete portrait of love successfully realized. The wedding is a communal celebration — family, workers, and neighbors gathered in joy — and it stands in deliberate contrast to the deferred, ultimately thwarted union of Efraín and María. Where the central romance is shadowed by illness and separation, Tránsito and José's wedding is straightforward happiness (030-015)(034-001).

## Role in the Novel

The wedding serves as structural counterpoint — proof that happy love is possible in this world, which makes the central tragedy more poignant rather than less (030-015)(056-023). It also provides one of the novel's few scenes of unalloyed communal celebration, a reminder that the hacienda world contains joy as well as grief (034-001).

## Key Passages

> We tried to find out when the marriage was to be. José, to relieve his daughter's anxiety, had to confess that it would be very soon. (030-015)
