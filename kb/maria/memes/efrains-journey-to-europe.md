# Efraín's Journey to Europe

Efraín's departure for Europe is the novel's definitive separation — the journey that makes tragedy inevitable. Unlike his earlier departures for Bogotá, from which return was always anticipated, the European voyage carries the weight of years: five years of medical study, five years of absence, five years during which María's health will deteriorate past the point of recovery (057-004). The plan had been introduced earlier: Efraín's father had spoken to him "of sending me to Europe to study medicine" (005-022), and the promise is reiterated as the date approaches (034-130).

## Role in the Novel

This journey is the fulfillment of the father's plan and the death of the lovers' hopes. Efraín leaves because he must — filial duty and practical necessity demand it — but the novel makes clear that this is a journey from which there is no true return. Señor A—— tells him he is to start "under four months" (036-016), and conversations among the hacienda community revolve around "your voyage to Europe" (037-014). Even as late as his time in London, the question follows him: "You mean to go back to Europe, then?" (051-005). By the time he comes back, María will be dead, and the Cauca Valley he returns to will be a landscape of loss.
