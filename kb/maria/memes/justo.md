Justo is a head mule-driver (arriero) who meets Efraín during his journey through the mountains. He arrives at Juntas with Efraín's mother's mules, and his capable management of the mule train and camp demonstrates the skill of the mountain workers who keep the hacienda's trade routes operating (053-006). (053-006)

## Role in the Novel

Justo appears in the travel sequence of the novel, embodying the rugged independence of the mountain muleteers. His practical competence — lighting candles, preparing camp, handling tired mules — provides a contrast to Efraín's more contemplative journey (053-018). (053-010)

## Relationships

- **[[Lorenzo]]** — Justo calls Lorenzo back to give him instructions during the mountain camp scene (053-032).
- **[[efrain|Efraín]]** — Justo welcomes Efraín at the camp, offering him chocolate and brandy as refreshment (053-029).

## Key Scenes

- Justo arrives at Juntas with the mules, announced by the sound of the lead mule's bell (053-015).
- Justo lights a candle-end and sticks it into a crack, illuminating the mountain camp as night falls (053-022).
- After sharing brandy with Efraín and his men, Justo calls Lorenzo back for a quiet word (053-030).

> A tall and thin mulatto came out from among the tobacco-sacks which closed up the two sides of the canvas where it did not reach the ground. It was the head-driver, Justo.
