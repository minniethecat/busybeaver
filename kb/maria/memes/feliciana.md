# Feliciana

Feliciana is a longtime servant of the family hacienda, the mother of [[juan-angel|Juan Ángel]], and a figure whose illness and eventual death form a poignant episode in the novel. Her relationship with the family reflects the deep, multigenerational bonds between servants and landowners in the world of the hacienda (021-021). (021-021)

## Role in the Novel

Feliciana serves as a domestic presence in the household, having been a nurse to the family children. Her illness becomes a narrative thread in the later chapters, where [[efrain|Efraín]] and others attend to her, and her death is marked with tenderness. Her character illustrates the familial bonds that transcend social class within the hacienda's domestic sphere. When news of her worsening condition arrives, the family sends affectionate messages back to her (037-011). (032-011)

## Relationships

- **[[juan-angel|Juan Ángel]]** — Feliciana is Juan Ángel's mother. He is present at her bedside during her final illness (038-002).
- **[[Dr. Mayn]]** — The doctor who initially treats Feliciana's illness (037-010).
- **[[efrain|Efraín]]** — Efraín is concerned for Feliciana's welfare and visits her during her illness (038-002).
- **The hacienda family** — Feliciana has been a nurse and servant to the family for many years (021-021).

## Key Scenes

- Feliciana is introduced as the mother of the intractable young boy, having been a nurse in the household (021-021).
- News arrives that Feliciana's condition has worsened, prompting concern from the family (037-010).
- The family sends affectionate messages to Feliciana through the messenger (037-011).
- [[Dr. Mayn]]'s replacement informs Efraín that Feliciana's case has been lost (038-001).
- Efraín and Juan Ángel visit Feliciana in her room during her final illness (038-002).
- A cross is left on Feliciana's grave, marking the solemn conclusion of her story (038-015).

Feliciana is described through her role as a nurse in the family:

> Feliciana, his mother, who had been a nurse in the family, and enjoyed all the consideration attached to that position, always hoped he would turn out a good valet for me.
>
> (021-021)
