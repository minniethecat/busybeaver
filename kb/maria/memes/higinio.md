# Higinio

Higinio is the overseer at the family hacienda, a trusted worker responsible for managing field labor, assigning living quarters, and carrying out the patriarch's instructions. He appears across multiple chapters, from the early wedding scenes to the later illness episodes, reflecting his enduring role in hacienda life. (005-004)

## Role in the Novel

As overseer, Higinio occupies an intermediate position in the hacienda hierarchy — above the field workers and enslaved people, but firmly under the authority of the patriarch. He supervises work, assigns cabins to couples like [[Bruno]] and [[Remigia]], and communicates important news to the family, such as [[Feliciana]]'s worsening condition. (005-013)

## Relationships

- **[[efrains-father|Efraín's Father]]** — The patriarch who gives Higinio direct orders regarding estate management. (005-017)
- **[[efrain|Efraín]]** — The narrator who interacts with Higinio, especially regarding [[juan-angel|Juan Ángel]]'s care. (038-009)
- **[[Bruno]]** — Higinio assigns the cabin for Bruno and Remigia's new life together. (005-013)
- **[[Feliciana]]** — Higinio writes to inform the family of Feliciana's worsening illness. (037-010)
- **[[juan-angel|Juan Ángel]]** — Higinio is tasked with watching over Juan Ángel, who escapes his vigilance. (038-009)

## Key Scenes

- Higinio returns from the fields with the patriarch and Efraín. (005-004)
- The patriarch asks Higinio about the cabin given to Bruno, showing Higinio's role in worker housing. (005-013)
- Higinio is instructed to attend to wedding preparations for Bruno and Remigia. (005-017)
- Higinio writes to inform the family that Feliciana's condition has worsened. (037-010)
- Juan Ángel escapes Higinio's vigilance to search for his mother. (038-011)

Higinio appears in an evening scene at the hacienda:

> One evening, just at sunset, we were coming back with Higinio, the overseer, from the fields to the mill. They were talking about the work to be done; I was occupied with less serious things: I was thinking about the days of my childhood.
>
> (005-004)
