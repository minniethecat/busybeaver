# Pedro

Pedro is an enslaved man at the family hacienda, described by [[efrain|Efraín]] as his "faithful friend and servant." His role, though brief, is significant: he tells a story that enters Efraín's dreams and foreshadows the novel's central tragedy. (004-001)

> I slept as I used to when a child, after being lulled to slumber by a fairy story told me by the slave Pedro.
>
> (004-001)

## Role in the Novel

Pedro appears in the early chapters as part of Efraín's childhood world. His story — which Efraín recounts dreaming about — involves [[maria|María]] and prefigures the loss that will define the novel's second half. Pedro thus functions as an unwitting oracle, his tale carrying symbolic weight beyond his social station. (004-001) (005-002)

## Relationships

- **[[efrain|Efraín]]** — Pedro is a childhood companion and faithful servant to Efraín. His story lingers in Efraín's memory. (005-002)
- **[[maria|María]]** — Pedro's story features María entering Efraín's room, a scene that haunts the narrator. (004-001)

## Key Scenes

- Pedro tells a story to the young Efraín that becomes the basis of a prophetic dream about María. (004-001)
- Efraín reflects that he was not to meet Pedro again, suggesting the passage of time and change. (005-002)
