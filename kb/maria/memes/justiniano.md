Justiniano is a young worker who is in love with Salome, a servant woman on the hacienda. His courtship of Salome provides a subplot that mirrors the novel's central romance, as various characters on the estate conspire to bring the couple together (042-052). (042-052)

## Role in the Novel

Justiniano's pursuit of Salome represents a more practical, earthbound version of romantic love in contrast to the idealized passion of Efraín and María. His story, told through the matchmaking efforts of other characters, adds a comic touch to the novel's exploration of love and courtship (043-130). (042-057)

## Relationships

- **[[Salome]]** — Justiniano is deeply in love with Salome, and the novel follows the efforts to bring them together. Salome is described as being as much in love with Justiniano as he is with her (043-130).

## Key Scenes

- Carlos asks Efraín whether he is a friend of Justiniano, the brother of Don Carlitos, introducing the character through his social connections (042-052).
- A discussion about how Salome treats Justiniano reveals the dynamics of their relationship (042-057).
- It is revealed that Salome is as much in love with Justiniano as he is with her, confirming the mutual nature of their affection (043-130).

> "Well, Salome is as much in love with Justiniano as I am with her. You must judge that girl at her true worth, which, luckily for you, is great. You have offended her with your jealousy; yet if you go and try to please her, she will forgive you, and love you more than ever."
>
> (043-130)
