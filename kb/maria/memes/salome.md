Salome is a young servant woman on the family hacienda in the Cauca Valley. She is hardworking and honest, and her story intersects with the main narrative through the romantic pursuit of Justiniano, a neighboring worker who seeks to marry her (042-038). Salome is portrayed as proper and honest, which complicates the matchmaking efforts of those around her (042-041). (042-038)

> She does needlework which is a sight to see, and helps in everything. Her mother's own daughter.
>
> (042-039)

## Role in the Novel

Salome is a supporting character whose brief storyline provides a parallel to the novel's central romantic themes. While the main plot follows the idealized love between Efraín and María, Salome's courtship with Justiniano represents a more practical, earthy version of romance within the hacienda's social hierarchy (042-047). Her presence helps establish the social texture of the estate, showing the lives of the workers alongside the family drama. (042-039)

## Relationships

- **[[Justiniano]]** — A worker who is in love with Salome and seeks to marry her. Salome initially resists his advances, and the match requires the intervention of others on the hacienda to bring them together (042-057).
- **Candelaria** — A fellow servant who assists in the efforts to arrange meetings between Salome and Justiniano (042-086).

## Key Scenes

- Salome's money-box is broken open to pay for a purchase, revealing her thrifty nature and the trust others place in her (042-038).
- Salome is called from the river, where she has been working, to meet Efraín — demonstrating her diligence as a laborer (042-097).
- Salome makes an "expressive little face" at Efraín when she realizes she has been drawn into a matchmaking scheme with Justiniano (042-115).
