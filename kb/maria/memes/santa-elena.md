Santa Elena is a small settlement in the Cauca Valley, visible from the road as Efraín rides through the countryside. Its cabins can be seen on the right-hand side of the path (014-011). Santa Elena is known as a source of good hunting dogs — Braulio orders two dogs from there for the tiger hunt (024-016). (014-011)

> I saw the cabins of Santa Elena on my right; soon I was out of hearing of the barking of their dogs.
>
> (014-011)

## Role in the Novel

Santa Elena is a minor location that contributes to the geographical texture of the Cauca Valley. Its significance lies in its association with hunting — the dogs brought from Santa Elena play a role in the tiger hunt, one of the novel's most dramatic episodes (025-005). (024-016)

## Key Scenes

- Efraín sees the cabins of Santa Elena on his right as he rides out of the valley (014-011).
- Braulio announces he has ordered two good dogs from Santa Elena for the tiger hunt (024-016).
- The servant arrives with the other dogs from Santa Elena, completing the hunting party (025-005).
