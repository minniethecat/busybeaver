# Braulio

Braulio is a worker on the hacienda, the nephew of José, who appears in several key scenes. About Efraín's age, he had come from Antioquia two months earlier to live with his uncle (020-014). His courtship of Tránsito — whom he was already "madly in love with" (020-014) — provides a counterpoint to Efraín and María's romance: a humble but happy love that ends in marriage rather than tragedy.

## Role in the Novel

Braulio represents the loyal, capable labor that sustains the estate's daily life. He and José come to meet Efraín on his return (020-002), and he participates eagerly in the tiger hunt alongside Efraín, José, and Lucas (020-034)(020-035). His character contributes to the novel's portrait of hacienda society as an integrated, hierarchical community — a world in which masters and workers are bound by mutual obligation and shared daily rhythms.

## Relationships

- **[[jose|José]]** — His uncle and fellow worker on the estate.
- **[[efrains-father|Efraín's Father]]** — His employer, the hacienda patriarch.
- **[[transito|Tránsito]]** — His love interest, whom he eventually marries (020-014)(020-031).

## Key Scenes

- **Meeting Efraín**: Braulio and José come to find Efraín on his return to the hacienda. José promises "this will be a great day for Braulio" (020-002)(020-006).
- **The Tiger Hunt**: Braulio participates in the hunt on the Chagra hacienda alongside Lucas, José, and Efraín — all carrying rifles (020-034)(020-035).
- **Courtship of Tránsito**: Braulio's tender glances and his relationship with Tránsito provide warmth and normalcy (020-031).
