Religious Mass is a recurring ritual that marks the rhythm of community and family life in the Cauca Valley. The family attends Mass together, and the ceremony also appears at moments of heightened emotion — notably as a Mass for the dead after a major loss (038-011).

Efraín and María attend Mass during their excursion, and the shared religious experience binds the community together (034-135). (003-003)

> We all heard mass, and as we left the church, Braulio told us that while we were mounting they would set out from the village; but that we should overtake them not very far away.
>
> (034-135)

## Role in the Narrative

Mass serves as a social and spiritual anchor in the novel, representing the Catholic faith that permeates 19th-century Colombian rural life. The shared experience of attending Mass, saying prayers, and participating in religious rituals connects the characters across social classes and marks the passage of time (042-064). (022-008)

## Key Events

- The family hears Mass together during their mountain excursion, and Efraín and María leave the church together afterward (034-135).
- A Mass for the dead is held, followed by prayers for the deceased (038-011).
- Salome is described as being as attentive as if she were at Mass, using the ritual as a metaphor for focused devotion (042-064).
- Juan Ángel says his evening prayer, leaning sleepily against María, showing how religious practice is woven into daily family life (030-083).
