# Paris

Paris is mentioned as part of Efraín's European medical education — the cultural capital of the 19th-century world and the ultimate symbol of the civilization that draws young Latin Americans away from home. The novel mentions it only in passing: "He had just arrived in Paris" (049-004), a statement whose brevity underscores the emotional distance between the European destination and the Cauca Valley.

## Role in the Novel

Paris exists in the novel as an idea rather than a described place — it is where Efraín might go, the destination that takes him farthest from María. Its minimal description reflects the novel's emotional geography: Paris matters only because it is not the Cauca Valley (049-004).

## Key Passages
