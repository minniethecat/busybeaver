Estefana is a household servant on the hacienda who assists with domestic tasks, including preparing tea, running errands, and helping to care for the younger children. She is trusted with household duties and appears in several domestic scenes (031-025).

## Role in the Novel

As a minor domestic character, Estefana contributes to the detailed portrait of hacienda life that forms the backdrop of the novel. Her presence in the household helps establish the rhythm of daily work and the social hierarchy of the estate. She announces visitors to Efraín's mother (037-015) and accompanies young Juan to the bath (040-002).

## Key Scenes

- María suggests leaving "the rest for Estefana," indicating her reliability with household tasks (031-025).
- Estefana brings in the teapot during a family scene (036-041).
- Estefana comes in to announce that Efraín's mother is calling for him (037-015).
- Estefana accompanies Juan to the bath, helping with childcare duties (040-002).

Estefana is a reliable domestic presence:

> She scarcely dared look at him, but seeing that breakfast was ready, she said, rising, "Breakfast is served, Señor." Then, addressing Emma, "Let us leave the rest for Estefana; she will do it well."
>
> (031-025)
