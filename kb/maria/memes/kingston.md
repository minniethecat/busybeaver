# Kingston

Kingston, [[jamaica|Jamaica]], is a crucial offstage location in the novel. It is where [[maria|María]] was born, where her parents met and married in May 1820, and where her father [[salomon|Salomón]] lived and died (007-001). The sealed packet of legal documents sent to Kingston represents the novel's most tangible connection to this distant place (007-007) (057-004).

## Role in the Novel

Kingston represents the world beyond Colombia — the place of María's origins and her parents' deaths. It connects the [[cauca-valley|Cauca Valley]] love story to a wider Caribbean geography of trade, migration, and loss.

## Key Passages

> ...telling of Salomón's death. I recall but one expression of grief... (007-007)

> It is to go to Kingston. It is Salomón's last will... (057-004)
