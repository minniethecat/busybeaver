## Overview

Caen Wood (later known as Kenwood) was Lord Mansfield's country residence, situated between [[hampstead|Hampstead]] and [[highgate|Highgate]] to the north of [[london|London]]. During the [[gordon-riots-1780|Gordon Riots of 1780]], the house became a target of the anti-Catholic mob after it was reported that a party of rioters, meeting others engaged in similar destruction elsewhere, united and marched upon the estate with the intent of burning it down (066-027).

## Description

The house stood on elevated ground between [[hampstead|Hampstead]] and [[highgate|Highgate]], positioned such that any great fire lit there "from that height should be seen all over [[london|London]]" (066-027). The rioters' plan, however, was thwarted when a party of horse arrived before them, forcing a hasty retreat back to town.

> The scouts reported further, that this party meeting with some others who had been at similar work elsewhere, they all united into one, and drafting off a few men with the killed and wounded, marched away to Lord Mansfield's country seat at Caen Wood, between Hampstead and Highgate; bent upon destroying that house likewise, and lighting up a great fire there, which from that height should be seen all over London. But in this, they were disappointed, for a party of horse having arrived before them, they retreated faster than they went, and came straight back to town. (066-027)
