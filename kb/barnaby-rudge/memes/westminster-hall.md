## Overview

Westminster Hall is the oldest part of the [[westminster|Palace of Westminster]] in [[london|London]], built in 1097. In *[[barnaby-rudge|Barnaby Rudge]]*, it appears as a law court and a key route for the [[lord-george-gordon|Gordon]] Rioters as they marched toward the [[parliament|Houses of Parliament]] to present their petition.

## Description

Dickens describes Westminster Hall as a space of legal gravity, where the rioters' presence creates a stark contrast between civil order and mob violence. The hall's function as a passage between the street and Palace Yard places it at the threshold of the political crisis.

> …intending to pass through Westminster Hall into Palace Yard, and there they found the ground covered with a great concourse of people, who had assembled to witness the presentation of the petition. (043-009)

The interior of the hall on the day of the petition is depicted with scattered groups of onlookers:

> …knots and groups of persons in Westminster Hall: some few looking upward at its noble roof with an expression of pleasure, and some gazing from the windows into Palace Yard without. (043-010)

## Role in the Narrative

Westminster Hall serves primarily as the architectural context for the presentation of the [[protestant-association|Protestant Association]]'s petition to [[parliament|Parliament]] (043-009). The rioters' movement through this historic hall, with its associations of law and royal authority, underscores the symbolic challenge the [[gordon-riots-1780|Gordon Riots]] posed to established order.

Later, during the riots themselves, the area around Westminster Hall becomes a tactical flashpoint. The military deploys cavalry nearby to defend the [[parliament|Houses of Parliament]] against the advancing mob (067-003).

In the novel's closing pages, the hall reappears in a reflective passage concerning the legal aftermath, reinforcing its identity as a site where law and history converge (081-042).

## Characters Associated

- [[lord-george-gordon]] — Led the [[protestant-association|Protestant Association]] march through Westminster Hall to present the petition
- The rioters — Passed through Westminster Hall en route to Palace Yard during the petition presentation

## Historical Basis

The Westminster Hall of *[[barnaby-rudge|Barnaby Rudge]]* is the actual Westminster Hall, built under William II (Rufus) and boasting the largest medieval timber roof in Northern Europe. During the [[gordon-riots-1780|Gordon Riots]] of June 1780, the area around Westminster Hall and the [[parliament|Houses of Parliament]] was the initial focal point of the protest. Troops were stationed here, and the presentation of [[lord-george-gordon|Lord George Gordon]]'s petition on 2 June 1780 drew an enormous crowd to Palace Yard. Dickens's depiction of the hall as a liminal space between civil order and [[mob-violence|mob rule]] is historically grounded.
