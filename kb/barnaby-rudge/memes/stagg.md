Stagg is a blind informer who moves through the novel's [[london|London]] underworld. Though his role is relatively brief, he serves as a connecting thread between the 'Prentice Knights conspiracy and the later revelations about the Rudge family murder. His blindness is both a physical fact and a metaphor for the moral opacity of the world he inhabits.

## Introduction: The 'Prentice Knights

Stagg first appears in Chapter 8 as a member of [[simon-tappertit|Simon Tappertit]]'s secret society, the 'Prentice Knights. His blindness is immediately established as a defining trait — he is introduced being roused from sleep (008-021):

> "Even Stagg hath been asleep," said the leader.

Despite his disability, Stagg displays an uncanny awareness of the room and its occupants, claiming he can sense when someone's eyes are upon him (008-026):

> "He's got his eyes on me!" cried Stagg, stopping short on his way back.

His subservience to the "[[simon-tappertit|captain]]" (Tappertit) is theatrical and self-abasing (008-028–029):

> "I obey you, captain," cried Stagg, drawing close to him and filling out a very dirty dram-glass.

## Association with Tappertit

Stagg reappears in Chapter 18, meeting Tappertit on the road at dawn. Their exchange reveals the informal network of spies and informers that operates beneath the surface of [[london|London]] life. Tappertit uses Stagg as a source of information about people's movements and activities, treating him with a mixture of contempt and utility (018-013):

> "What a fool you are, Stagg!" said Mr. Tappertit.

Stagg's physical helplessness is emphasized in comic yet unsettling passages — he clasps Tappertit's ankle in a gesture both obsequious and desperate (018-014):

> "His precious limbs!" cried Stagg, clasping one of his ankles.

## The Rudge Family Connection

Stagg's most significant function comes in Chapters 45–46, where he visits [[mrs-rudge|Mrs. Rudge]] at her cottage. Introducing himself with studied politeness (045-042):

> "Madam, my name is Stagg. A friend of mine who has designs upon this place has sent me here to know..."

His visit reveals the shadowy surveillance network that has been tracking [[mrs-rudge|Mrs. Rudge]] and her son. Stagg's apparent harmlessness — the blind man who needs a guiding hand — masks his role as an agent of darker forces. His encounters with [[mrs-rudge|Mrs. Rudge]] expose the fear and [[deception|secrecy]] that have governed her life since her husband's disappearance.

## Final Appearances

Stagg appears briefly in the novel's closing chapters (62, 69), where his connections to the conspiracy become more explicit. His final lines express fatalistic acceptance of his circumstances.
