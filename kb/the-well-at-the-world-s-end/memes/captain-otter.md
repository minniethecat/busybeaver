# Captain Otter

**Captain Otter**, commonly referred to as **Otter**, is the captain of the guard at [[utterbol|Utterbol]].
A thrall who was once a knight, he serves [[gandolf-lord-of-utterbol|Lord Gandolf]] and oversees the
combat trials into which [[ralph-of-upmeads|Ralph]] is pressed.

## Role at Utterbol

Otter holds the position of captain of the guard under the Lord of Utterbol.
He is responsible for managing the fighting men and organising the combats
in which prisoners and newcomers are tested. When Ralph arrives at Utterbol,
it is Otter who escorts him to the lists and explains the stakes of the
coming joust.

> "Well, Knight, thou hast seen them this morn, even if ye die ere nightfall."
> [032-044]

Although Otter carries out his lord's commands without question, Ralph finds
his company not entirely disagreeable. Riding together on the road, they speak
at length, and Ralph notes that Otter seems to value a man's life no more than
a rabbit's when his lord bids slay or let live.

## Background

Otter reveals to Ralph that he was once a free knight before becoming a thrall.
This shared knightly past creates a subtle bond between the two men, though
Otter is clear about the limits of his sympathy: he will not risk his own
position for Ralph's sake. His practical, unsentimental view of his servitude
contrasts with Ralph's determination to remain free.

## Relationship with Ralph

During the journey toward the mountains, Otter rides beside Ralph and answers
his questions about the Wall of the World. He speaks of legends concerning the
[[the-well-at-the-worlds-end|Well at the World's End]], showing that even the
Lord of Utterbol's hardened captain is not entirely untouched by the wider
world's mysteries.

Otter's laughter and blunt warnings give Ralph a clear picture of his
predicament: fail the combat and become a serving-thrall, or succeed and remain
a valued—but still captive—fighter. His honesty, though harsh, helps Ralph
navigate the dangers of Utterbol.
