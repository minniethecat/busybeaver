The Queen of Utterbol is the unnamed ruler of the strong-thieves who hold the region around Utterbol and Utterness. She is one of the most feared figures in the world of the narrative, described by those who oppose her as a sorceress of great and terrible power.

A franklin of the region tells Ralph that she is "their goddess, their mawmet, their devil, the very heart and soul of their wickedness." He speaks with grim satisfaction of a day when they shall "have her body and soul," at which point "her body shall have but an evil day of it till she dieth in this world."

The Queen's power is linked to sorcery. Roger of the Ropewalk notes that the Men of the Dry Tree "speak but of one woman amongst them," but that there are "many others who are like unto her in one way or other," making it difficult to lay hands on "the very Queen of them all."

Her association with the Land of the Tower is significant: the Men of the Dry Tree recall a fair woman who was Queen of the Land of the Tower fifty years before the main narrative, whom they had hoped would "come back and dwell with us and be our Lady and Queen." This Queen vanished, and a band of one-and-twenty champions of the Dry Tree swore to seek her. The relationship between this earlier Queen of the Tower and the present Queen of Utterbol remains ambiguous in the text.
