# The Land of the Tower

The **Land of the Tower** is a fair kingdom over which the Lady of Abundance once reigned as Queen. It lies somewhere beyond the wilderness and desert lands, and its history is intertwined with the Lady's story and the fellowship of the Dry Tree.

## The Lady's Queenship

> The Lady tells Ralph of her return to the Land of the Tower: "when at last we came out from the twilight of the woods into the sunny fields of the Land of the Tower, there was no man to naysay us; nay, the gates of the strong places flew open before the wind of our banners, and the glittering of our spears drew the folk together toward the places of rejoicing" (031-025).

After emerging from the wilderness with her champion, the Lady found that her return to the Land of the Tower was welcomed without resistance. The strong places opened their gates and the people gathered in celebration.

## The People's Discontent

Despite the initial rejoicing, over time murmurs and "blind causeless hatred" began to grow against the Lady. Tales spread among the people that "I was not only a sorceress, but even one foredoomed from of old and sent by the lords of hell to wreck that fair Land of the Tower and make it unhappy and desolate" (031-028). These accusations ultimately forced her to abandon her queenship and return to the Dry Tree.

## The Dry Tree's Connection

The Men of the Dry Tree held a deep connection to the Land of the Tower through the Lady. Fifty years after her departure, old Geoffrey speaks of "a fair woman who was Queen of the Land of the Tower and whom we loved sorely because we had dwelt together with her amidst tribulation in the desert and the wildwood" (032-010). The fellowship had hoped she would return to dwell among them.

## The Desert Connection

The Lady notes that "this desert is verily a part" of the Land of the Tower, "but was desert still in the days when I was Queen of the Land" (034-003). This suggests the Land of the Tower encompasses both fertile fields and desolate wilderness.
