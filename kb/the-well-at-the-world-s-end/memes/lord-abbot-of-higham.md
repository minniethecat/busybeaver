# The Lord Abbot of Higham

**The Lord Abbot of Higham** is the Abbot of the Abbey of St. Mary at
[[higham-on-the-way|Higham-on-the-Way]]. A wealthy and powerful ecclesiastical lord,
he governs extensive lands and commands the loyalty of many men-at-arms. He is
initially presented as a beneficent ruler but later becomes an obstacle to
[[ralph-of-upmeads|Ralph]]'s campaign.

## Wealth and Power

The Abbot is described as "an exceeding mighty lord" with vast wealth derived
from the lands of St. Mary's Abbey. He rebuilt and expanded the Great Church
at Higham, adorning it with gilded imagery. His vassals and villeins maintain
balefires across the countryside, each representing a band of armed men ready
to serve. During the Midsummer festival at Higham, the Abbot appeared "in
garments all of gold" beneath a canopy of rich cloth, bearing a precious mitre
and crozier as if he were a patriarch.

## Relationship with Ralph

When Ralph first arrived at Higham, a monk encouraged him to take service with
the Abbot, praising the security and prosperity of life under Holy Church's
protection. Ralph attended the Abbot's blessing at the Midsummer festival but
chose not to enter his service.

Later, when Ralph returned to Higham at the head of his company of the Dry Tree,
the Abbot's men refused him entry. The armed guards at the barriers threatened
Ralph's forces with bows, and a knight of the town declared that Ralph had
"stolen" a man from the Abbot's service. This confrontation marked the Abbot
as an antagonist in the final chapters.

## Lands and Conflicts

The Abbot's lands included Bourton Abbas and other territories. His forces had
previously clashed with the [[the-knights-of-the-dry-tree|Champions of the Dry Tree]],
who raided his lands as far as Bear Castle. The Abbot's men-at-arms had special
devotion to St. George, whom they regarded as their patron and protector.
