# Upmeads

A small, peaceful kingdom ruled by King Peter, situated near a forest where the king's father and eldest son died in battle long ago (001-010). Upmeads is the homeland of Ralph of Upmeads and the starting point of the novel's quest narrative.

## Overview

Upmeads is described as "a little land" over which a "regulus or kinglet" ruled (001-002). Its small size and peaceful nature stand in contrast to the wider, more dangerous world beyond. The kingdom's modest scale and King Peter's pacifism create the conditions that drive his four sons to seek adventure elsewhere.

## Geography

Upmeads lies near a forest where King Peter's father died in battle (001-010). The land is small enough that its ruler is called a kinglet rather than a king, suggesting a modest territory. Each of the four sons rides out in a different direction — north, west, east, and south — indicating Upmeads is situated at a crossroads (001-021).

## Significance to the Narrative

Upmeads serves as the narrative's starting point and the "ordinary world" from which the hero departs. Its peace and smallness establish Ralph's motivation for seeking adventure and provide a contrast to the wider, more perilous world he explores.
