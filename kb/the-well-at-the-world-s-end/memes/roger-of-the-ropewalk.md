# Roger of the Ropewalk

A woodland man who proved a steadfast ally to Ralph in the perilous lands about the Burg of the Four Friths.

## Overview

Roger of the Ropewalk was a man of the woodlands who aided Ralph when he was pursued by men of the Dry Tree. Skilled in woodland craft and fiercely independent, Roger guided Ralph to safety near the Burg of the Four Friths, earning his trust through swift and decisive action.

## Biography

### The Rescue

When Ralph was pursued through the woodland by armed men, Roger appeared as an unexpected ally. Under fire from a Turkish bow, Roger turned in his saddle and cried defiance at his pursuers:

> Ha, thieves! I am Roger of the Ropewalk, I go to twist a rope for the necks of you! (009-015)

His boldness and skill — riding at a hand-gallop through thinning woodland — helped Ralph escape the immediate danger. Once clear of pursuit, Roger reassured his companion: "Thou mayst breathe thy nag now, and ride single, for we are amidst friends" (009-017).

### Guide to the Burg

Roger led Ralph through the contested lands between the Wood Perilous and the Burg of the Four Friths. When they approached the Burg's gate, it was Roger who spoke for Ralph, directing him to lodgings: "My lord shall go to the Flower de Luce, which is in the big square" (009-025). His knowledge of the town — its streets, its inns, its dangers — marked him as a seasoned traveller in these borderlands.

### Character

Roger embodied the self-reliant woodland dweller common in Morris's medieval landscape. His name — "of the Ropewalk" — suggests a trade or a place of origin, and his fierce independence (shaking his fist at armed pursuers while shouting threats) marked him as a man who lived by his wits and courage in dangerous territory.
