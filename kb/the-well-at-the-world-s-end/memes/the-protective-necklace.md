# The Protective Necklace

A magical talisman given to Ralph of Upmeads by a carline (wise woman) early in his journey. The necklace consists of blue and green stones with gold knobs between them, "like a pair of beads; albeit neither pope nor priest had blessed them," and is tied to a little box of gold with something hidden inside (003-018).

## Overview

The carline presents the necklace to Ralph with the promise that wearing it will be "salvation to thee in peril, and good luck to thee in the time of questing; so that it shall be to thee as if thou hadst drunk of the **Well at the World's End**" (003-018). The necklace thus serves as a portable, symbolic substitute for the distant Well.

## Properties

The necklace is explicitly described as unblessed by religious authority — "neither pope nor priest had blessed them" (003-018) — emphasizing that its power is innate and natural rather than mediated through institutional religion. This reflects Morris's secular humanist values.

## Significance

The necklace functions as both a protective charm and a narrative device that introduces the concept of the Well at the World's End. Through the carline's gift, Ralph first learns of the Well's existence and its legendary properties.
