# Giles

**Giles**, often called **old Giles**, is an old carle of the Down-country who
aids [[ralph-of-upmeads|Ralph of Upmeads]] during the final campaign to liberate
Upmeads. He serves as a guide, messenger, and advisor, proving an invaluable
ally despite his humble station.

## Messenger from Martha

Giles brought Ralph a message from Martha, the wise woman of the Shepherds.
The message contained both prophetic vision and practical counsel: Martha saw
Ralph and his champions in Bear Castle, the Shepherds rising like a wood, and
a valiant man at Wulstead awaiting the call to arms. She bid Ralph make haste
but not overmuch, warning that excessive speed could worsen his chances. The
message urged him to gather his allies before striking.

## Guide in the Campaign

Giles rode at Ralph's side as the company moved toward Upmeads, guiding them
through the Down-country. His knowledge of the terrain and the local folk proved
essential. When they encountered a company of Burg men near Garton, Giles
identified them as foes and counseled Ralph to attack, which led to a swift
victory and the capture of prisoners.

Giles also helped organize the Shepherds who flocked to Ralph's standard at
Bear Castle. Donning a hauberk and mail-coif, the old carle "looked a proper
man-at-arms" and was appointed to help array the Shepherds into companies of
tens and hundreds. He advised Ralph to shed his heavy armour before the long
march, showing practical wisdom born of experience.
