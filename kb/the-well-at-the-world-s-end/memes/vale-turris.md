# Vale Turris

A valley settlement with a prominent tower, situated on the route between the Burg of the Four Friths and Utterness. Its name derives from the Latin _turris_ (tower), referring to the tower that dominates the dale.

## Overview

Vale Turris lies in the upland country on the approach to Utterness, north of the Burg of the Four Friths. It is first encountered when Ralph rides with David and the Queen's company from the Burg toward Utterness [063-026]. The dale opens onto plain meads below the Tower, large enough to accommodate a considerable encampment [090-004].

## Geography & Description

The valley opens onto level meadowland below the Tower. Beyond it, the road climbs into rough and shaggy upland, from which the great wall of mountains is visible in the distance [070-004]. The surrounding country is wooded enough that the Sage, who knows the forest well, can shorten the journey from his dwelling to four days [090-004].

## Inhabitants

Vale Turris serves as a waypoint and encampment ground rather than a permanent settlement. The Queen of Utterbol's retinue passes through it, and Ralph's growing company uses it as a mustering ground. Bull Shockhead and Otter are among those who join Ralph's fellowship here or nearby [093-009].

## Role in the Narrative

### The Queen's Passage

Ralph first comes to Vale Turris riding with David in the company of the Queen of Utterbol. Here he notices that the woman he sees is not the same Queen he had glimpsed before — a moment of revelation about the shifting nature of appearances and identities in the realm of Utterbol [064-004].

### The Tournament

During their stay at Vale Turris, a tournament is held. Redhead later recalls his bout with Ralph: "Dost thou remember how sorry a job I made of it, when we met in the lists at Vale Turris that other day?" [094-012]. This tournament serves as a bonding rite among Ralph's fellowship.

### Return After Winter

After wintering with the Sage, Ralph and Ursula return to Vale Turris in May to find the plain meadows bright with tents and booths, a great gathering assembled there [090-004]. From Vale Turris they ride onward with their full company, including Redhead, Bull, and Otter [093-001][093-009].

### Significance

Vale Turris represents a threshold place — a gathering ground where Ralph's fellowship coalesces before the final stages of the quest. It is neither a hostile stronghold (like Utterbol) nor a place of rest (like the Sage's dwelling), but a staging point where allies are mustered and loyalties tested in the lists.
