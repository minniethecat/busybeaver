# Goldburg

A prosperous city that Ralph passes through on his journey. Goldburg is a center of trade and information — Morfinn the Minstrel hears of Ralph's inquiries there, demonstrating the city's role as a nexus where travelers' paths and stories intersect.

## Overview

Goldburg appears as a wealthy trade city where news travels and connections are made. It is in Goldburg that Morfinn the Minstrel learns of Ralph's questions, leading to their meeting and the exchange of information about Utterness. The city's name suggests prosperity built on commerce.

## Geography

Goldburg lies on the route between the settled lands and the territories of Utterbol and Utterness. Its position makes it a natural gathering point for travelers, merchants, and minstrels moving between regions.

## Significance

Goldburg represents the commercial civilization that exists alongside the chivalric and mythic elements of the narrative. It is a place of exchange — of goods, news, and stories — that connects the different regions of Morris's imagined world.
