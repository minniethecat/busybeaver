# The Fellowship of the Dry Tree

A band of robbers and reivers dwelling at Hampton, whose name derives from the legendary Dry Tree — the landmark that marks the way to the Well at the World's End. The Fellowship is mentioned early in the narrative as a known danger on the path.

## Overview

The Fellowship is first mentioned when a carle tells Ralph about the route to the Well, mentioning the Dry Tree (003-034). A damsel later elaborates that the Fellowship dwells at Hampton and confirms their reputation as "robbers and reivers," but adds that "the tale tells that the way to the Well at the World's End is by the Dry Tree" (010-016), linking them to the quest geography.

## Reputation

The Fellowship is described with ambivalence — they are acknowledged as dangerous outlaws, yet their association with the Dry Tree gives them a mythic significance beyond mere brigandage. The damsel notes they "will perchance be little worse than the others" (010-016), suggesting a world where moral distinctions are blurred.

## Significance

The Fellowship represents one of the dangers and waypoints on the quest for the Well. Their existence at Hampton, associated with the Dry Tree, makes them an integral part of the narrative geography Morris constructs.
