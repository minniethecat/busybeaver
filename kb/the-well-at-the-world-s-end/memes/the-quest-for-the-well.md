# The Quest for the Well at the World's End

The Quest for the Well at the World's End is the central narrative framework of William Morris's 1896 fantasy novel. Multiple characters undertake perilous journeys across strange lands, driven by the promise that drinking from the Well grants youth, strength, and good fortune.

## Overview

The quest is introduced gradually. Early references describe the Well as a distant, almost mythical destination accessible only by passing through the Wood Perilous and finding the Dry Tree (003-034). As Ralph journeys further, the Well shifts from background legend to his explicit goal.

## Key Participants

- [[ralph-of-upmeads|Ralph of Upmeads]] — the protagonist, who eventually makes the quest his own
- A mysterious woman who declares she seeks the Well "to find it and live, or to find it not, and die" (010-006)

## The Path

The way to the Well is consistently described as passing through dangerous territory: the Wood Perilous, beyond which lies the Dry Tree, which marks the way to the Well (003-034) (010-016).

## Motivation

Those who seek the Well are driven by varied motives — escape from suffering, desire for strength, or simply the call of adventure. The carline tells Ralph his protective necklace will be "as if thou hadst drunk of the Well at the World's End" (003-018), establishing the Well's legendary power.
