# Bear Castle

A fortified stronghold in the upland country associated with the Champions of the Dry Tree. It serves as a military staging point during Ralph's campaign against the Burgers.

## Overview

Bear Castle stands in hilly country, approached by steep and winding ways. It is first glimpsed from a hill brow, its ramparts visible against the landscape [004-005]. The castle is associated with the Champions of the Dry Tree, who used it before building their greater stronghold of the Scaur [031-018].

## Geography & Description

The castle is situated on high ground, approached by a steep winding road with a "hollow cup of the hills" below [004-005]. It possesses ramparts and fortifications substantial enough to serve as a military base [110-010]. Garton, a settlement that provides footmen for Ralph's company, lies nearby [110-010].

## Inhabitants

Bear Castle is held by or allied with the Champions of the Dry Tree. During Ralph's campaign, it serves as a base where prisoners are taken and from which sorties are launched against the Burgers [110-001]. Men from Garton, both mounted and on foot, assemble here to join Ralph's forces [110-010].

## Role in the Narrative

### Early Allusions

The old shepherd on the downs speaks of it in connection with an oath sworn by Ralph, hinting that the castle may offer aid to those who keep their word [004-026].

### The Champions' Earlier Stronghold

The Lady's tale reveals that before the Champions of the Dry Tree built their fortress on the Scaur, they made their stand at Bear Castle and other woodland strongholds [031-018].

### The Campaign Against the Burgers

In the final campaign, Ralph and his company ride to Bear Castle to consolidate their forces and hold prisoners taken from the enemy [110-001][110-010]. The castle's position allows them to command the surrounding country and coordinate the final push against the Burgers' stronghold in the Wood Debateable.

### Significance

Bear Castle represents the network of strongholds that the forces of good maintain against the lawless elements of the world. It is a place of martial gathering and righteous force, contrasting with the wild and contested Wood Debateable.
