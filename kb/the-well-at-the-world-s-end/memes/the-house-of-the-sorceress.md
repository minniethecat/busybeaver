# The House of the Sorceress

A solitary dwelling on the edge of the Dry Desert, inhabited by the Sorceress who guarded the approach to the Well at the World's End. It is also called the Desert-House, and its chamber is named the Chamber of Love in the Wilderness.

## Overview

The House of the Sorceress stands at the boundary between the peopled lands and the Dry Desert, beyond which lies the Well at the World's End. It serves as the final waypoint and preparation ground for those who seek the Well, and as a place of refuge and learning for those who abide there [031-042].

## Geography & Description

The House lies in a stony waste beyond the woodland. Travellers from the east must ride through long leagues of forest, pleasant in spring and early summer, before the trees thin out into the waste where the House stands [081-004][082-009]. The surrounding country is described as a "lonesomest of deserts," once a fair land of castles and homesteads now passed away [2007]. Within the House is an ark containing hallowed raiment, which seekers don before descending to the Well [086-004].

## Inhabitants

The House is the dwelling of the Sorceress, a woman of great wisdom and power who studied under the Teacher of Lore. She can send dreams and visions, foresee things to come, and practice what the common folk call sorcery [031-042]. She provides guidance and aid to those who seek the Well, sending way-leaders to accompany travellers through the final stages of their journey [079-006].

## Role in the Narrative

### The Lady's Sojourn

The Lady (Ursula's mother) tells Ralph how she and her companions abode in the House of the Sorceress for years before daring to seek the Well. During this time she "waxed stronger of body and fairer" as though receiving a foretaste of the Well's power, and learned wisdom and sorcery from the Teacher of Lore [031-042].

### The Parting

After the Lady's companion departed through the wildwood, she remained at the House alone, "waiting on the wearing of the days" until she could set out on her own quest. She later declared she would not see the House again until she returned "hand in hand with thee, beloved" — a prophecy of her eventual reunion with Ralph [032-006][032-023].

### Ralph and Ursula's Arrival

Ralph and Ursula, guided by elders sent from the Sage, reach the House of the Sorceress at the fullness of June after a long woodland journey. They receive the hallowed raiment from the ark and make their final preparations for the descent to the Well [081-001][081-004][086-004].

### Return from the Well

After drinking from the Well and achieving their quest, Ralph and Ursula return to the House of the Sorceress, crossing the Dry Desert in ease compared to their outward journey [088-002].

### Significance

The House of the Sorceress represents the threshold between the ordinary world and the realm of transcendence. It is a place of preparation, purification, and waiting — those who would reach the Well must first abide here, learning what they need and shedding what they cannot carry forward.
