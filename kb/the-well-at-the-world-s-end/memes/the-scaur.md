# The Scaur

A towering cliff or escarpment in the eastern lands, upon whose height stands the castle of the Champions of the Dry Tree. The town of Hampton lies below it, hence its full name: Hampton under Scaur. The Scaur is both a natural fortress and the seat of the Lady who rules the Champions.

## Overview

The Scaur is first mentioned when Ralph, newly departed from Upmeads, asks the way eastward. A forester tells him of a road that leads to Hampton under Scaur, "where the folk are peaceable and friendly" [009-018]. But the Scaur itself holds a more formidable presence: upon its height is built the great castle of the Champions of the Dry Tree [032-016]. The Lady who once ruled the Land of Abundance became, after war and upheaval, "Lady and Queen of the Champions of Hampton under the Scaur" [070-021].

## Geography & Description

The Scaur is a sheer cliff or rock face rising above a small town. The castle is built "upon the height of the said Scaur," making it a nearly impregnable stronghold [032-016]. Below, the town of Hampton nestles under the cliff's shadow — described by one who knew it as "Heaven, wherein men sin not" compared to the harsher strongholds of the east [011-013]. Yet even this seeming innocence is qualified: the folk of Hampton "be verily as harmless and innocent as sheep; but they be under evil lords who are not their true lords" [011-015].

The road to the Scaur leads through woodland — a hazel copse gives way to thicker forest, and a path branches off toward the castle on the rock [026-006].

## Inhabitants

The height of the Scaur is held by the Champions of the Dry Tree, a fellowship of warriors who serve the Lady as their Queen [070-021]. These Champions were once based elsewhere but, after losing their original stronghold, established themselves upon the Scaur [104-020].

The town of Hampton below is populated by peaceable folk, though they live under lords not of their own choosing [011-015]. The Lady taught Ursula something of significance "in the wood by Hampton under Scaur" [068-036], suggesting she maintained a presence both in the castle above and the woodland below.

## Role in the Narrative

### Key Events

Ralph first learns of Hampton under Scaur from a forester near the Burg of the Four Friths, who recommends it as a destination of goodwill [009-018]. The Lady of the Castle of Abundance later directs Roger to bring Ralph there, saying she will meet him at the castle [025-024].

Roger reveals his own connection to the place — he is a rebel and runaway from Hampton under Scaur, having been driven from his land and his friends slain [012-032]. Despite this, he agrees to guide Ralph thither [016-005].

The Lady tells Ralph the full story of how she came to rule at the Scaur: after war devastated the Land of Abundance, she and her lord "set ourselves to heal the wounds which war had made in the land" [031-027]. Later, she became Queen of the Champions who dwell upon the rock [070-021].

### Significance

The Scaur represents a transformed stronghold — what was once a robber-fastness becomes, under the Lady's rule, a seat of order in a lawless land. The contrast between the peaceful town below and the armed fortress above mirrors the tension between innocence and power that runs through the narrative.
