# The Knight of the Sun

**The Knight of the Sun** (also called the **Sun-Knight**) is a mighty baron
who bears a golden sun device on a blue surcoat. He appears in the wilderness
threatening [[the-lady-of-abundance|the Lady of Abundance]] and is slain in
single combat by [[ralph-of-upmeads|Ralph]].

## Appearance and Bearing

The Knight of the Sun is described as a very big man, clad in armour and
wearing a blue surcoat upon which is beaten a great golden sun. His companion
in the encounter is a knight dressed in black. When Ralph first sees them,
the Sun-Knight has just struck down the black knight and stands over him,
sword bloodied to the hilt, his face white as chalk and scowling.

## Combat with Ralph

The Knight of the Sun turns his aggression upon the Lady of Abundance,
demanding to know who will help her in the desert. At her signal, Ralph
steps forward, sword in hand. The Sun-Knight briefly kneels before the Lady,
clasping his hands in prayer and asking her pardon, but then springs to his
feet and rushes furiously upon Ralph.

The combat is fierce. The Knight of the Sun presses through Ralph's strokes
despite his mail being rent, closing within Ralph's guard to deliver a heavy
blow. But Ralph ultimately prevails and slays him:

> "Thou mayst not slay him; he was a great and mighty man, a baron who bore
> a golden sun on a blue field."
> [022-033]

## Significance

The Knight of the Sun is the first man Ralph kills in armed combat, marking
a turning point in his journey from innocent youth to seasoned warrior. The
encounter also deepens Ralph's bond with the Lady of Abundance, whom he has
now defended at mortal risk. In later chapters, when Ralph recounts the deed,
an old man recognises the Sun-Knight's device and confirms his stature—a
great baron whose death at Ralph's hands carries weight in the wider world.
