# Bull Shockhead

A carle — a free yeoman — whom Ralph encounters in the Wood Perilous. Bull Shockhead bargains his way into Ralph's company, proving himself a rough but loyal companion in the dangerous wood.

## Overview

Bull Shockhead introduces himself with characteristic bluntness when Ralph asks his name: "Bull Shockhead" (022). He is a plain-spoken man of the woods, a fighter by necessity, who recognizes Ralph's quality and offers his service in exchange for protection and companionship.

## Biography

### Origins

Bull's origins are humble and obscure. He is a carle — not a knight or noble, but a free man of the yeoman class. His presence in the Wood Perilous suggests a life lived on the margins of settled society, where survival depends on strength and shrewdness.

### Journey / Arc

Bull meets Ralph in the Wood Perilous and strikes a bargain to join him. Their exchange reveals Bull's character: when Ralph threatens him, Bull responds pragmatically — "That is the bargain, then, and I yeasay it" (022). He becomes one of Ralph's companions in the dangerous wood, contributing his strength and woodcraft to the fellowship.

## Character Analysis

### Traits & Personality

Bull is defined by his directness. His name — Shockhead — suggests wild, unkempt appearance. He bargains for his life and position with neither groveling nor arrogance, accepting terms straightforwardly. His physical strength and knowledge of the wood make him a valuable companion, contrasting with Ralph's more courtly background.

### Relationships

Bull's relationship with Ralph is that of retainer to lord, but with the rough equality of the wilderness. He bargains rather than swears fealty, reflecting the free yeoman's independence. His loyalty, once given, appears steadfast — the bargain struck, he abides by it.

### Themes

Bull Shockhead represents the common man of Morris's medieval world — neither noble nor thrall, but a free yeoman whose worth is measured in strength, loyalty, and plain dealing. His presence in the narrative broadens the social range beyond kings and knights.
