# Nicholas Longshanks

Wise and lettered squire to Ralph of Upmeads, known to friend and foe alike across the lands.

## Overview

Nicholas Longshanks was the squire assigned by King Peter to his youngest son Ralph at the Four-want-way drawing of cuts. Described as having "more words than I have, yet hath he more wisdom," Nicholas was a man lettered and far-travelled who loved the House of Upmeads right well (001-045). He served as Ralph's guardian and mentor, attempting to follow his young lord when Ralph departed on his quest.

## Biography

### Appointment as Squire

When King Peter assigned squires to his four sons, Nicholas Longshanks was given to Ralph — the youngest son who drew the shortest straw and was meant to stay at home. The king said of him:

> I have none left to follow thee, Ralph, save Nicholas Longshanks; but though he hath more words than I have, yet hath he more wisdom, and is a man lettered and far-travelled, and loveth our house right well. (001-045)

After the brothers departed, Nicholas mounted his hackney and led Ralph's warhorse home to King Peter's House (001-048).

### Pursuit of Ralph

When Ralph left Upmeads against his father's wishes, Nicholas pursued him. Clement Chapman, in whose house Ralph stopped, muttered to himself: "Thou art pressed lest Nicholas be here presently to fetch thee back; and to say sooth I would his hand were on thy shoulder even now" (002-029). The Lady of Abundance also spoke of setting "old Nicholas on thine heels when he cometh after thee, as come he will full surely" (005-042), acknowledging his determination to follow and protect his young lord.

### Reputation

Nicholas's reputation extended beyond Upmeads. When Ralph encountered the company of the Dry Tree in the Wood Perilous, old Oliver recognized Ralph's connection to Nicholas:

> "How fareth Long Nicholas, my lord?" ... Old Oliver laughed and said: "Well, Nicholas and I have been friends in a way, as well as foes; and for the sake of the old days his name shall help thee, young lord." (008-578)

This intervention — Oliver's respect for Nicholas — helped protect Ralph from being detained by the Knight of the Dry Tree.

## Character Analysis

Nicholas embodies the loyal retainer archetype: older, wiser, and more experienced than his charge, yet bound by duty and affection to serve. His "many words" (noted by King Peter) suggest a man of counsel and learning, while his far travels imply a depth of worldly knowledge uncommon in the little kingdom of Upmeads.
