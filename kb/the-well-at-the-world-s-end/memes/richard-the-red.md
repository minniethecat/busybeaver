# Richard the Red

Fierce squire to Blaise of Upmeads, a veteran warrior who journeyed east and later reunited with Ralph at Whitwall.

## Overview

Richard the Red was the squire assigned by King Peter to his eldest son Blaise. Though "somewhat stricken in years," Richard was known as "a fierce carle and a doughty, and knoweth well all feats of arms" (001-045). He journeyed eastward with Blaise after the drawing of cuts, and later appeared at Whitwall where he joyfully reunited with Ralph.

## Biography

### Appointment to Blaise

At the Four-want-way, King Peter declared:

> Richard the Red shall go with Blaise; for though he be somewhat stricken in years, and wise, yet is he a fierce carle and a doughty, and knoweth well all feats of arms. (001-045)

Richard was the most martial of the four squires, a seasoned warrior contrasted with Lancelot Longtongue (the courteous logician who served Hugh), Clement the Black (the careful man of few words who served Gregory), and Nicholas Longshanks (the wise counsellor who served Ralph).

### The Eastern Journey

Richard accompanied Blaise eastward after the drawing of cuts. Ralph later encountered them on the road — Blaise clad in a black coat welted with gold, riding a good grey palfrey, and "Richard was armed well and knightly" (014-050). The reunion was joyful: Blaise cast his arms about Ralph, crying "O happy day! when two of the Upmeads kindred meet thus in an alien land!"

### Reunion at Whitwall

Richard appeared again at Whitwall, where Ralph and Ursula rode through the market-day crowds. Richard — described as "a man wide-shouldered, grey-haired, blue-eyed, and ruddy of countenance — a man warrior-like to look on, and girt with a long sword" — cried out in astonished joy upon seeing Ralph. The people of Whitwall, who knew Richard well, clapped and cheered: "Hail to the friends long parted, and now united!" (066-023).

### Return to Upmeads

Richard fought alongside Ralph in the final battle for Upmeads. The wild-man of the woods, dying, acknowledged both "Child of Upmeads" and "Richard the Red" as warriors "naught lighthanded" (100-034). After the victory, Ralph lodged Ursula at "the house of Richard the Red" before leading her into his father's hall (103-020).
