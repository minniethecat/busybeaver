# The Burg of the Four Friths

A significant walled city that Ralph enters after traversing the first part of the Wood Perilous. The Burg is organized around its Four Friths — wards or guilds that govern different quarters of the city.

## Overview

The Burg of the Four Friths represents Ralph's first encounter with a major urban center beyond Upmeads. Its organization around the four friths suggests a guild-based or ward-based governance structure. Ralph spends several days in the Burg, gathering information about the wider world and the path to the Well.

## Geography

The Burg lies on the far side of the Wood Perilous, serving as a waypoint between the settled lands and the wilder territories beyond. Its walls and organized governance mark it as a bastion of civilization at the edge of dangerous territory.

## Significance

The Burg represents urban civilization in Morris's imagined world — a place of trade, information, and social complexity that contrasts with both the simplicity of Upmeads and the wildness of the Wood Perilous. Ralph's time in the Burg expands his understanding of the world before he ventures deeper into the unknown.
