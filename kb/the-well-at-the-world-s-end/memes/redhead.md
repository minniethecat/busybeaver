# Redhead

**Redhead** is a soldier in the service of [[gandolf-lord-of-utterbol|Lord Gandolf]]
at [[utterbol|Utterbol]]. A big, rough-looking man, he owes his life to
[[ralph-of-upmeads|Ralph]]'s intercession and repays the debt with a
warning that shapes the course of Ralph's journey.

## Saved by Ralph

Redhead first appears when [[captain-otter|Captain Otter]] brings him to
Lord Gandolf's attention as a man who has displeased his master. The Lord
orders Redhead to be set up as a live target for the bowmen—head down and
buttocks aloft—to be shot at until killed. Ralph successfully pleads for
Redhead's life, and the Lord, amused by Ralph's boldness, grants the
pardon.

When Redhead next encounters Ralph, he recognises his benefactor and
takes Ralph's hand in gratitude:

> "Fair lord, I would that I might do something for thine avail, and
> perchance I may."
> [037-054]

## The Warning

Redhead, knowing he cannot be seen speaking privately with Ralph, waits
until they are unobserved and then delivers an urgent warning: Ralph must
not go to Utterbol. He reveals the Lord's true intention—Ralph has been
purchased as a gift for the Lord's wife, to serve as her toy and minion.
Once she tires of him, his life will be worth nothing.

He tells Ralph plainly that there is no honour to be found at Utterbol,
only degradation for those still capable of honour. His advice is
practical: Ralph, who can move freely about the camp, should slip away by
night or in the grey of morning and never look back.

## Character

Though rough in appearance and manner, Redhead is no rascal. He possesses
a rough integrity and a clear-eyed understanding of the court's corruption.
His warning to Ralph is an act of genuine courage—if discovered, it would
mean his death. That he takes the risk anyway marks him as one of the few
people at Utterbol who acts from gratitude rather than calculation.
