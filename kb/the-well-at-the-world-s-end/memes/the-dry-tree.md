# The Dry Tree

A legendary landmark — a dead, withered tree that stands as a way-marker on the path to the Well at the World's End. The Dry Tree is consistently paired with the Well in the narrative: "the tale tells that the way to the Well at the World's End is by the Dry Tree" (010-016).

## Overview

The Dry Tree is first mentioned when a carle tells Ralph about the route to the Well: the way lies "down to the Wood Perilous, and beyond it" where the Dry Tree stands (003-034). It serves as both a geographical landmark and a symbolic threshold — passing the Dry Tree means committing to the quest.

## Geography

The Dry Tree stands beyond the Wood Perilous, on the path toward the Well. Its exact location is known to those versed in quest-lore. The Fellowship of the Dry Tree at Hampton takes its name from this landmark, suggesting its fame extends across the narrative world.

## Significance

The Dry Tree and the Well form a paired symbolism: death and life, ending and renewal, the withered and the life-giving. The dead tree marking the way to the water of life is a powerful image that runs through medieval and romance traditions. The Fellowship of the Dry Tree — a band of robbers who take the tree's name — adds a layer of danger to the landmark's associations.
