# David the Sage

**David**, called **David the Sage**, is the chief counsellor of
[[gandolf-lord-of-utterbol|Lord Gandolf]] at [[utterbol|Utterbol]].
An old man clad in scarlet who wears spectacles, he is among the most
trusted servants of the Lord, though his position grants him only the
freedom to agree.

## Position at Court

David stands at the Lord of Utterbol's right hand during audiences.
When [[ralph-of-upmeads|Ralph]] is brought before Lord Gandolf, it is
David who is asked to assess the newcomer's quality. After putting on his
spectacles and examining Ralph, David pronounces him a fine catch with
no room for disagreement:

> "There are no two words to be said about it; he is a goodly young man."
> [031-042]

Lord Gandolf relies on David to handle practical arrangements, ordering
him to pay [[morfinn-the-minstrel|Morfinn]] for bringing Ralph and to
ensure the captive is properly fed and rested.

## Treatment of Ralph

David takes Ralph to a tent and makes much of him, providing meat, drink,
and comfort. He warns Ralph plainly not to attempt escape, lest he be
killed. Though his words are those of a jailer, his manner is pleasant,
and Ralph deems him a great man at Utterbol.

Ralph soon understands the limits of David's position: he is the Lord's
chief counsellor chiefly because he never counsels anything the Lord
would not wish to hear. David's amiability is genuine, but it is the
amiability of a man who has long since learned to survive by never
opposing power.

## Character

David's spectacles mark him as a man of learning in a court otherwise
defined by brute force. His wry grin when discussing Ralph's prospects
suggests a dry awareness of the absurdity of his own position—a wise man
whose wisdom must always bend to his master's whims. He speaks pleasantly
with Ralph, eats and drinks with him, and sees him comfortably bedded,
all while knowing that Ralph's fate rests entirely with Lord Gandolf's
moods.
