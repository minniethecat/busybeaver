# Morfinn the Minstrel

A wandering minstrel encountered by Ralph, bearing a let-pass from Gandolf, Lord of Utterbol. Known also as "Morfinn the Unmanned," he serves as an informant about events in Utterness, offering Ralph a "privy word" concerning a woman.

## Overview

Morfinn introduces himself: "Morfinn the Minstrel I hight, to serve thee, fair lord. Or some call me Morfinn the Unmanned" (029). He carries a scroll from the Lord of Utterbol that guarantees safe passage — without which the road is "deadly" (029). His epithet "the Unmanned" carries ambiguous connotations: perhaps a eunuch, perhaps a man rendered powerless.

## Biography

### Origins

Morfinn's origins are obscure. He travels the roads between Goldburg and Utterness, making his living as a minstrel. His let-pass from Utterbol — dated within three months — marks him as someone who operates within Gandolf's system, whether willingly or under duress.

### Journey / Arc

Morfinn meets Ralph and offers him information: "I heard of thee asking many questions thereof in Goldburg, and I said to myself, now may I, who am bound for Utterness, do a good turn to this fair young lord" (029). His "privy word" concerns a woman — almost certainly Ursula — making him a crucial link in Ralph's chain of informants. He expects reward for his information but also hopes for future reciprocation.

## Character Analysis

### Traits & Personality

Morfinn is a survivor — the let-pass, the willingness to trade information for reward, the epithet "Unmanned" all suggest a man who has navigated dangerous powers through adaptation rather than resistance. He is "blithe and merry" (029) despite his circumstances, suggesting resilience or performance.

### Relationships

Morfinn's relationship with Ralph is transactional but not hostile. He offers information for reward, framing it as mutual benefit: "there is no doubt but he will reward me well at once for any help I may give him; and also it may be that he will do me a good turn hereafter" (029). His connection to the Lord of Utterbol, through the let-pass, marks him as operating within the tyrant's system.

### Themes

Morfinn represents the intermediary figure — neither hero nor villain, but a man who navigates between powers. His "Unmanned" epithet suggests the cost of surviving under tyranny. As a minstrel, he embodies the role of information-carrier in a world without mass communication.
