# Sir Aymer

Sir Aymer is a knight serving under Old Oliver, encountered by Ralph of Upmeads in the Wood Perilous during his early journey eastward.

## Overview

Sir Aymer appears in a single scene, when Ralph, newly arrived in the Wood Perilous, is brought before Oliver's company. Aymer is present as Oliver's subordinate knight and participates in the questioning of the young traveler.

## Biography

### The Encounter in the Wood Perilous

Sir Aymer is first addressed by Oliver, who confirms to him that he knows Ralph: "Yea, Sir Aymer, and now meseems I know him better than he knoweth me" (008-017). Aymer is described as "the Knight" who initially regards Ralph with some suspicion.

When Oliver recognizes Ralph as being associated with Long Nicholas, Aymer hears Oliver's judgment. Oliver tells Aymer of Ralph: "he is of a goodly house and an ancient; but thou hearest how he adjureth me. Ye shall let his name alone" (008-022), instructing Aymer not to take hostile action.

### Questioning Ralph

After receiving Oliver's instruction, the Knight (Aymer) looks silently at Ralph for a while before speaking. He asks Ralph whether he intends to "wend with us to the Burg of the Four Friths" and what business brings him to the Wood Perilous (008-023). This questioning serves as a test of Ralph's intentions.

## Character

Sir Aymer appears as a dutiful knight, loyal to Oliver's command. His initial wariness toward Ralph is tempered by Oliver's recognition of the young man's lineage. Though a minor figure with only two mentions, he represents the atmosphere of watchfulness and testing that Ralph encounters at the threshold of the Wood Perilous.
