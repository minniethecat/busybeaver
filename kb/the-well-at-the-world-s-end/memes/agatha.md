# Agatha

Agatha is the dark, slender, and cunning handmaiden of the Lady of [[gandolf-lord-of-utterbol|Utterbol]], a thrall who uses her sharp wits and understanding of court intrigue to navigate the dangerous household of the Lord of Utterbol. She serves as her mistress's confidante, strategist, and agent in the scheme to win the affections of [[ralph-of-upmeads|Ralph of Upmeads]].

## Overview

Agatha first appears in Chapter 60, walking with the Lady outside the pavilion at Tower Dale after the Lady has seen Ralph for the first time (060-004). In a remarkable extended dialogue that occupies much of the chapter, Agatha demonstrates her defining qualities: perceptiveness, boldness bordering on insolence, strategic cunning, and a clear-eyed understanding of the power dynamics at Utterbol. Unlike the typical fearful thrall, Agatha speaks to her mistress with surprising frankness — teasing, chiding, and ultimately guiding her. She devises the plan to have [[ursula|Joyce]] sit in the Lady's seat at the tournament, setting in motion events that will shape Ralph's path.

## Biography

### Origins

Agatha's origins are not explicitly given, but she is consistently described as a thrall — a bonded servant — in the household of the Lord of Utterbol. Her dark complexion and slender build mark her as physically distinct from the fair-skinned Lady and the Northern women of the court (060-005). Her intimate knowledge of court politics and human nature suggests long experience in the dangerous corridors of Utterbol.

### Journey / Arc

Agatha's narrative function crystallises in the chapters set at [[goldburg|Tower Dale]] and Utterbol (Chapters 60–66). When the Lady becomes infatuated with Ralph, it is Agatha who both diagnoses the malady and prescribes the cure. She negotiates immunity from the Lord on the edge of his sword — demonstrating remarkable nerve — and then executes a multi-layered scheme:

1. She persuades the Lady to feign illness and stay away from the tournament (060-017)
2. She arranges for Joyce (Ursula in disguise) to sit in the Lady's seat, creating a decoy (060-021)
3. She spreads rumours and lays groundwork for the Lady's larger plan (063-004)
4. She later approaches Ralph directly at the Lady's behest, attempting to persuade him (066-007)

> "Easy is this. What more hast thou?"
>
> (060-016, Agatha to the Lady, after securing immunity)

Her relationship with the Lady is complex — part servant, part co-conspirator, part mirror. When the Lady weeps at her own perceived coarseness, Agatha "looked on her curiously, but smiling no more" (060-012), revealing a capacity for genuine empathy beneath the cunning exterior.

### Fate

Agatha's fate is revealed indirectly in Chapter 93, where [[bull-shockhead|Redhead]] — who loved her — speaks of her death. She was killed, it seems, in the violent upheaval at Utterbol that followed Ralph's escape with Ursula. Redhead describes how she had spread tales of her own sorcery as a form of protection:

> She had got it spread abroad that she was cunning in sorcery, and that her spell would not end when her life ended; nay, that he to whom her ghost should bear ill-will, and more especially such an one as might compass her death, should never thrive.
>
> (093-017)

That Agatha felt the need for such a defence speaks to her precarious position — a thrall whose only weapons were her wits and her reputation. Redhead also exacted revenge on the Captain involved in her death, binding him and casting him into a deep woodland pool (093-018).

## Character Analysis

### Traits and Personality

Agatha is defined by a constellation of traits rare in medieval romance's servant class:

| Trait | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| **Perceptiveness** | Immediately discerns both the Lady's infatuation and Ralph's nature (060-005) |
| **Cunning** | Devises the multi-part scheme with Joyce as decoy (060-017–060-021) |
| **Boldness** | Speaks to the Lady with teasing familiarity; negotiates directly with the Lord (060-024) |
| **Pragmatism** | "My Lady, when thou hast sent me to the White Pillar, or the Red, or the Black, my stripes will not mend the matter for thee" (060-006) |
| **Genuine loyalty** | Despite her manipulation, she truly serves the Lady's interests |

Her physical description — "dark and sleek and lithe" (066-007), "slender body swaying like a willow branch" (060-024) — marks her as both alluring and other, a figure who uses appearance as a tool in a world where thralls have few others.

### Relationships

| Character | Relationship | Significance |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **The Lady of Utterbol** | Mistress and confidante | Agatha serves, teases, and genuinely cares for the Lady |
| **The Lord of Utterbol** | Master | Agatha manipulates him through calculated humility and flattery |
| **Ralph** | Object of the Lady's scheme | Agatha approaches Ralph as agent and later confesses her role |
| **Redhead (Bull Shockhead)** | Lover | In death, revealed to have been loved by Redhead, who avenges her |

### Themes

Agatha embodies the theme of **wit as survival** in a brutal world. Born a thrall in the Lord of Utterbol's household — a place where disobedience means the whip or death — she has learned to survive and even thrive through intelligence, manipulation, and performance. Her self-mythologising as a sorceress is a particularly telling detail: a woman with no formal power creates a supernatural reputation as her shield.

Morris does not judge Agatha. Though she schemes and lies, she operates within constraints that offer her no moral high ground. Her death — mourned by Redhead, avenged by violence — is treated with a gravity that elevates her from stock "wily servant" to tragic figure.
