# Dame Katherine

**Dame Katherine** is the wife of [[clement-chapman|Clement Chapman]], a wealthy merchant,
and the godmother of [[ralph-of-upmeads|Ralph of Upmeads]]. She is "a very goodly woman
of twoscore years" who came "of no mean kindred of the Kingdom of Upmeads" [003-002].
Her gift of a chaplet of beads to Ralph becomes a recurring talisman throughout his
quest, and her own backstory — a journey to the fabled city of Sarras — reveals hidden
depths beneath her domestic exterior.

## Appearance and Character

Katherine is introduced as the mistress of Clement's household in the Cheaping Town,
presiding over a chamber "panelled with wainscot well carven" with "a cupboard of no
sorry vessels of silver and latten" — a setting "no king's might be better" [003-002].
She is warm-hearted and motherly, particularly toward her godson Ralph, having held
him at the font when she was "a slim damsel new wedded."

She has a sharp tongue when needed. When Clement tries to discourage Ralph from his
journey, Katherine rebukes him:

> "Hold thy peace, Clement! What hast thou to meddle or make in the matter? If our
> young lord hath will to ride out and see the world, why should we let him? Yea,
> why should his father let him, if it come to that? Take my word for it that my
> gossip shall go through the world and come back to those that love him, as goodly
> as he went forth." [003-002]

## The Chaplet of Beads

Katherine's most significant contribution to the story is the chaplet of beads she
gives Ralph at his departure. This gift proves far more than a token of affection:
the beads are matched by an identical pair carried by a mysterious monk at Higham,
and later by the collar worn by the [[the-lady-of-abundance|Lady of Abundance]].
The beads function as a thread of connection, linking Ralph's humble beginnings in
Upmeads to the wider world of magic and destiny.

Ralph carries the chaplet throughout his journey. When lost in the wilderness,
"he thought of her quest, and the chaplet of dame Katherine, his gossip, which he
yet bore on his neck, and he deemed that he had naught to choose but to go forward"
[022-018].

## The Journey to Sarras

In the closing chapters, Katherine reveals an extraordinary episode from her youth.
Compelled by a "madness" to seek wisdom, she journeyed with Clement to **Sarras the
Holy**, "a city right ancient, and fulfilled of marvels" in the eastern parts of the
earth. There she sought out a Wise Woman who dwelt "all by herself in a nook of an
ancient ruined palace, erst the house of the ancientest of all the kings of Sarras."

The Wise Woman — "cheerful of countenance and courteous of demeanour" — received
Katherine kindly and gave her the pair of beads. One chaplet Katherine kept; the
other, she was told, would one day belong to a woman who would be "dearer to thee
than thine own self." This prophecy is fulfilled when the matching beads appear on
the Lady of Abundance.

> "In the days when I was first wedded to Master Clement yonder, he found his bed
> cold without me, for he was a hot lover; therefore would he often have me with
> him on his journeys, how hard soever or perilous the way might be." [028-001]

## The Christening Feast

In the final chapter, Katherine presides over the christening feast of Ralph and
[[ursula|Ursula]]'s children. Her reunion with Ralph is emotional:

> She turned and saw Ralph at the door, and she cried aloud and ran towards him
> with arms outspread. But when she drew nigh to him and beheld him closely, she
> withheld her, and falling down on her knees before him took his hand and fell to
> kissing it and weeping and crying out, "O my lord, my lord, thou art come again
> to us!" [027-001]

Katherine's telling of the children's story at the feast serves as a framing device
that brings the narrative full circle, from Ralph's departure to the establishment
of his own line.
