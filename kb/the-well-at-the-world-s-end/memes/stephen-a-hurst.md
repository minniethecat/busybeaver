# Stephen a-Hurst

**Stephen a-Hurst** is a man-at-arms stationed at [[higham-on-the-way|Higham-on-the-Way]].
An old comrade-in-arms of [[roger-of-the-ropewalk|Roger of the Ropewalk]],
he becomes one of [[ralph-of-upmeads|Ralph of Upmeads]]'s most trusted lieutenants
and oath-sworn companions during the final campaign to liberate Upmeads.

## Meeting Ralph

When Ralph and his company approached Higham, Roger recognized Stephen among the
guards at the barriers and called out to him as an old friend. Stephen greeted Roger
warmly and, upon learning of Ralph's identity as the Child of Upmeads, offered his
assistance. He directed Ralph's party to the Flower de Luce inn and conferred with
Roger about how to rally support.

## Oath-sworn Companion

Stephen brought Roger to meet with fellow veterans of the Dry Tree, and together
they persuaded the Captain of the Burg to provide men for Ralph's campaign. Stephen
was chosen as one of the leaders of the company alongside Roger and [[richard-the-red|Richard]].

At the Castle of the Scaur, Stephen administered the oath of fealty on behalf of
the Dry Tree's champions. Taking Ralph's sword, he kissed the blade and hilts and
swore to follow Ralph for a year and a day, calling upon God and Allhallows as
witness. He later revealed that the Dry Tree intended to grant Ralph the Castle of
the Scaur if he proved worthy.

## The Final Campaign

Stephen rode at Ralph's side throughout the campaign into Upmeads. When the company
was denied entry at Higham by the Lord Abbot's men, Stephen laughed roughly at their
demands but Ralph counseled patience. Stephen was present at the final muster and
the victorious battle that restored Ralph to his inheritance.
