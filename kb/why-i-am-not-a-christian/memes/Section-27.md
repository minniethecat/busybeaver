[026-001]_Question_.  If you take away the idea of eternal punishment, how


[026-002]do you propose to restrain men; in what way will you influence


[026-003]conduct for good?


[026-004]_Answer_.  Well, the trouble with religion is that it postpones


[026-005]punishment and reward to another world.  Wrong is wrong, because


[026-006]it breeds unhappiness.  Right is right, because it tends to the


[026-007]happiness of man.  These facts are the basis of what I call the


[026-008]religion of this world.  When a man does wrong, the consequences


[026-009]follow, and between the cause and effect, a Redeemer cannot step.


[026-010]Forgiveness cannot form a breastwork between act and consequence.


[026-011]There should be a religion of the body--a religion that will prevent


[026-012]deformity, that will refuse to multiply insanity, that will not


[026-013]propagate disease--a religion that is judged by its consequences in


[026-014]this world.  Orthodox Christianity has taught, and still teaches,


[026-015]that in this world the difference between the good and the bad is


[026-016]that the bad enjoy themselves, while the good carry the cross of


[026-017]virtue with bleeding brows bound and pierced with the thorns of


[026-018]honesty and kindness.  All this, in my judgment, is immoral.  The


[026-019]man who does wrong carries a cross.  There is no world, no star,


[026-020]in which the result of wrong is real happiness.  There is no world,


[026-021]no star, in which the result of doing right is unhappiness.  Virtue


[026-022]and vice must be the same everywhere.


[026-023]Vice must be vice everywhere, because its consequences are evil;


[026-024]and virtue must be virtue everywhere, because its consequences are


[026-025]good.  There can be no such thing as forgiveness.  These facts are


[026-026]the only restraining influences possible--the innocent man cannot


[026-027]suffer for the guilty and satisfy the law.


[026-028]_Question_.  How do you answer the argument, or the fact, that the


[026-029]church is constantly increasing, and that there are now four hundred


[026-030]millions of Christians?


[026-031]_Answer_.  That is what I call the argument of numbers.  If that


[026-032]argument is good now, it was always good.  If Christians were at


[026-033]any time in the minority, then, according to this argument,


[026-034]Christianity was wrong.  Every religion that has succeeded has


[026-035]appealed to the argument of numbers.  There was a time when Buddhism


[026-036]was in a majority.  Buddha not only had, but has more followers


[026-037]then Christ.  Success is not a demonstration.  Mohammed was a


[026-038]success, and a success from the commencement.  Upon a thousand


[026-039]fields he was victor.  Of the scattered tribes of the desert, he


[026-040]made a nation, and this nation took the fairest part of Europe from


[026-041]the followers of the cross.  In the history of the world, the


[026-042]success of Mohammed is unparalleled, but this success does not


[026-043]establish that he was the prophet of God.


[026-044]Now, it is claimed that there are some four hundred millions of


[026-045]Christians.  To make that total I am counted as a Christian; I am


[026-046]one of the fifty or sixty millions of Christians in the United


[026-047]States--excluding Indians, not taxed.  By this census report, we


[026-048]are all going to heaven--we are all orthodox.  At the last great


[026-049]day we can refer with confidence to the ponderous volumes containing


[026-050]the statistics of the United States.  As a matter of fact, how many


[026-051]Christians are there in the United States--how many believers in


[026-052]the inspiration of the Scriptures--how many real followers of


[026-053]Christ?  I will not pretend to give the number, but I will venture


[026-054]to say that there are not fifty millions.  How many in England?


[026-055]Where are the four hundred millions found?  To make this immense


[026-056]number, they have counted all the Heretics, all the Catholics, all


[026-057]the Jews, Spiritualists, Universalists and Unitarians, all the


[026-058]babes, all the idiotic and insane, all the Infidels, all the


[026-059]scientists, all the unbelievers.  As a matter of fact, they have


[026-060]no right to count any except the orthodox members of the orthodox


[026-061]churches.  There may be more "members" now than formerly, and this


[026-062]increase of members is due to a decrease of religion.  Thousands


[026-063]of members are only nominal Christians, wearing the old uniform


[026-064]simply because they do not wish to be charged with desertion.  The


[026-065]church, too, is a kind of social institution, a club with a creed


[026-066]instead of by-laws, and the creed is never defended unless attacked


[026-067]by an outsider.  No objection is made to the minister because he


[026-068]is liberal, if he says nothing about it in his pulpit.  A man like


[026-069]Mr. Beecher draws a congregation, not because he is a Christian,


[026-070]but because he is a genius; not because he is orthodox, but because


[026-071]he has something to say.  He is an intellectual athlete.  He is


[026-072]full of pathos and poetry.  He has more description than divinity;


[026-073]more charity than creed, and altogether more common sense than


[026-074]theology.  For these reasons thousands of people love to hear him.


[026-075]On the other hand, there are many people who have a morbid desire


[026-076]for the abnormal--for intellectual deformities--for thoughts that


[026-077]have two heads.  This accounts for the success of some of Mr.


[026-078]Beecher's rivals.


[026-079]Christians claim that success is a test of truth.  Has any church


[026-080]succeeded as well as the Catholic?  Was the tragedy of the Garden


[026-081]of Eden a success?  Who succeeded there?  The last best thought is


[026-082]not a success, if you mean that only that is a success which has


[026-083]succeeded, and if you mean by succeeding, that it has won the assent


[026-084]of the majority.  Besides there is no time fixed for the test.  Is


[026-085]that true which succeeds to-day, or next year, or in the next


[026-086]century?  Once the Copernican system was not a success.  There is


[026-087]no time fixed.  The result is that we have to wait.  A thing to


[026-088]exist at all has to be, to a certain extent, a success.  A thing


[026-089]cannot even die without having been a success.  It certainly


[026-090]succeeded enough to have life.  Presbyterians should remember,


[026-091]while arguing the majority argument, and the success argument, that


[026-092]there are far more Catholics than Protestants, and that the Catholics


[026-093]can give a longer list of distinguished names.


[026-094]My answer to all this, however, is that the history of the world


[026-095]shows that ignorance has always been in the majority.  There is


[026-096]one right road; numberless paths that are wrong.  Truth is one;


[026-097]error is many.  When a great truth has been discovered, one man


[026-098]has pitted himself against the world.  A few think; the many believe.


[026-099]The few lead; the many follow.  The light of the new day, as it


[026-100]looks over the window sill of the east, falls at first on only one


[026-101]forehead.


[026-102]There is another thing.  A great many people pass for Christians


[026-103]who are not.  Only a little while ago a couple of ladies were


[026-104]returning from church in a carriage.  They had listened to a good


[026-105]orthodox sermon.  One said to the other:  "I am going to tell you


[026-106]something--I am going to shock you--I do not believe in the Bible."


[026-107]And the other replied:  "Neither do I."


[026-108]--_The News_, Detroit, Michigan, January 6, 1884.


[026-109]POLITICS, MORMONISM AND MR. BEECHER


[026-110]_Question_.  What will be the main issues in the next presidential


[026-111]campaign?


[026-112]_Answer_.  I think that the principal issues will be civil rights


[026-113]and protection for American industries.  The Democratic party is


[026-114]not a unit on the tariff question--neither is the Republican; but


[026-115]I think that a majority of the Democrats are in favor of free trade


[026-116]and a majority of Republicans in favor of a protective tariff.


[026-117]The Democratic Congressmen will talk just enough about free trade


[026-118]to frighten the manufacturing interests of the country, and probably


[026-119]not quite enough to satisfy the free traders.  The result will be


[026-120]that the Democrats will talk about reforming the tariff, but will


[026-121]do nothing but talk.  I think the tariff ought to be reformed in


[026-122]many particulars; but as long as we need to raise a great revenue


[026-123]my idea is that it ought to be so arranged as to protect to the


[026-124]utmost, without producing monopoly in American manufacturers.  I


[026-125]am in favor of protection because it multiplies industries; and I


[026-126]am in favor of a great number of industries because they develop


[026-127]the brain, because they give employment to all and allow us to


[026-128]utilize all the muscle and all the sense we have.  If we were all


[026-129]farmers we would grow stupid.  If we all worked at one kind of


[026-130]mechanic art we would grow dull.  But with a variety of industries,


[026-131]with a constant premium upon ingenuity, with the promise of wealth


[026-132]as the reward of success in any direction, the people become


[026-133]intelligent, and while we are protecting our industries we develop


[026-134]our brains.  So I am in favor of the protection of civil rights by


[026-135]the Federal Government, and that, in my judgment, will be one of


[026-136]the great issues in the next campaign.


[026-137]_Question_.  I see that you say that one of the great issues in


[026-138]the coming campaign will be civil rights; what do you mean by that?


[026-139]_Answer_.  Well, I mean this.  The Supreme Court has recently


[026-140]decided that a colored man whose rights are trampled upon, in a


[026-141]State, cannot appeal to the Federal Government for protection.


[026-142]The decision amounts to this:  That Congress has no right until a


[026-143]State has acted, and has acted contrary to the Constitution.  Now,


[026-144]if a State refuses to do anything upon the subject, what is the


[026-145]citizen to do?  My opinion is that the Government is bound to


[026-146]protect its citizens, and as a consideration for this protection,


[026-147]the citizen is bound to stand by the Government.  When the nation


[026-148]calls for troops, the citizen of each State is bound to respond,


[026-149]no matter what his State may think.  This doctrine must be maintained,


[026-150]or the United States ceases to be a nation.  If a man looks to his


[026-151]State for protection, then he must go with his State.  My doctrine


[026-152]is, that there should be patriotism upon the one hand, and protection


[026-153]upon the other.  If a State endeavors to secede from the Union, a


[026-154]citizen of that State should be in a position to defy the State


[026-155]and appeal to the Nation for protection.  The doctrine now is, that


[026-156]the General Government turns the citizen over to the State for


[026-157]protection, and if the State does not protect him, that is his


[026-158]misfortune; and the consequence of this doctrine will be to build


[026-159]up the old heresy of State Sovereignty--a doctrine that was never


[026-160]appealed to except in the interest of thieving or robbery.  That


[026-161]doctrine was first appealed to when the Constitution was formed,


[026-162]because they were afraid the National Government would interfere


[026-163]with the slave trade.  It was next appealed to, to uphold the


[026-164]Fugitive Slave Law.  It was next appealed to, to give the territories


[026-165]of the United States to slavery.  Then it was appealed to, to


[026-166]support rebellion, and now out of this doctrine they attempt to


[026-167]build a breastwork, behind which they can trample upon the rights


[026-168]of free colored men.


[026-169]I believe in the sovereignty of the Nation.  A nation that cannot


[026-170]protect its citizens ought to stop playing nation.  In the old


[026-171]times the Supreme Court found no difficulty in supporting slavery


[026-172]by "inference," by "intendment," but now that liberty has become


[026-173]national, the Court is driven to less than a literal interpretation.


[026-174]If the Constitution does not support liberty, it is of no use.  To


[026-175]maintain liberty is the only legitimate object of human government.


[026-176]I hope the time will come when the judges of the Supreme Court will


[026-177]be elected, say for a period of ten years.  I do not believe in


[026-178]the legal monk system.  I believe in judges still maintaining an


[026-179]interest in human affairs.


[026-180]_Question_.  What do you think of the Mormon question?


[026-181]_Answer_.  I do not believe in the bayonet plan.  Mormonism must


[026-182]be done away with by the thousand influences of civilization, by


[026-183]education, by the elevation of the people.  Of course, a gentleman


[026-184]would rather have one noble woman than a hundred females.  I hate


[026-185]the system of polygamy.  Nothing is more infamous.  I admit that


[026-186]the Old Testament upholds it.  I admit that the patriarchs were


[026-187]mostly polygamists.  I admit that Solomon was mistaken on that


[026-188]subject.  But notwithstanding the fact that polygamy is upheld by


[026-189]the Jewish Scriptures, I believe it to be a great wrong.  At the


[026-190]same time if you undertake to get the idea out of the Mormons by


[026-191]force you will not succeed.  I think a good way to do away with


[026-192]that institution would be for all the churches to unite, bear the


[026-193]expense, and send missionaries to Utah; let these ministers call


[026-194]the people together and read to them the lives of David, Solomon,


[026-195]Abraham and other patriarchs.  Let all the missionaries be called


[026-196]home from foreign fields and teach these people that they should


[026-197]not imitate the only men with whom God ever condescended to hold


[026-198]intercourse.  Let these frightful examples be held up to these


[026-199]people, and if it is done earnestly, it seems to me that the result


[026-200]would be good.
