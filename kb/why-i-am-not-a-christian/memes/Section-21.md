[020-001]No, I do not believe there will be any more Star Route trials.


[020-002]There is so much talk about the last one, there will not be time


[020-003]for another.


[020-004]_Question_.  Did you anticipate a verdict?


[020-005]_Answer_.  I did anticipate a verdict, and one of acquittal.  I


[020-006]knew that the defendants were entitled to such a verdict.  I knew


[020-007]that the Government had signally failed to prove a case.  There


[020-008]was nothing but suspicion, from which malice was inferred.  The


[020-009]direct proof was utterly unworthy of belief.  The direct witness


[020-010]was caught with letters he had forged.  This one fact was enough


[020-011]to cover the prosecution with confusion.  The fact that Rerdell


[020-012]sat with the other defendants and reported to the Government from


[020-013]day to day satisfied the jury as to the value of his testimony,


[020-014]and the animus of the Department of Justice.  Besides, Rerdell had


[020-015]offered to challenge such jurors as the Government might select.


[020-016]He handed counsel for defendants a list of four names that he wanted


[020-017]challenged.  At that time it was supposed that each defendant would


[020-018]be allowed to challenge four jurors.  Afterward the Court decided


[020-019]that all the defendants must be considered as one party and had


[020-020]the right to challenge four and no more.  Of the four names on


[020-021]Rerdell's list the Government challenged three and Rerdell tried


[020-022]to challenge the other.  This was what is called a coincidence.


[020-023]Another thing had great influence with the jury--the evidence of


[020-024]the defendants was upon all material points so candid and so natural,


[020-025]so devoid of all coloring, that the jury could not help believing.


[020-026]If the people knew the evidence they would agree with the jury.


[020-027]When we remember that there were over ten thousand star routes, it


[020-028]is not to be wondered at that some mistakes were made--that in some


[020-029]instances too much was paid and in others too little.


[020-030]_Question_.  What has been the attitude of President Arthur?


[020-031]_Answer_.  We asked nothing from the President.  We wanted no help


[020-032]from him.  We expected that he would take no part--that he would


[020-033]simply allow the matter to be settled by the court in the usual


[020-034]way.  I think that he made one very serious mistake.  He removed


[020-035]officers on false charges without giving them a hearing.  He deposed


[020-036]Marshal Henry because somebody said that he was the friend of the


[020-037]defendants.  Henry was a good officer and an honest man.  The


[020-038]President removed Ainger for the same reason.  This was a mistake.


[020-039]Ainger should have been heard.  There is always time to do justice.


[020-040]No day is too short for justice, and eternity is not long enough


[020-041]to commit a wrong.  It was thought that the community could be


[020-042]terrorized:--


[020-043]_First_.  The President dismissed Henry and Ainger.


[020-044]_Second_.  The Attorney-General wrote a letter denouncing the


[020-045]defendants as thieves and robbers.


[020-046]_Third_.  Other letters from Bliss and MacVeagh were published.


[020-047]_Fourth_.  Dixon, the foreman of the first jury, was indicted.


[020-048]_Fifth_.  Members of the first jury voting "guilty" were in various


[020-049]ways rewarded.


[020-050]_Sixth_.  Bargains were made with Boone and Rerdell.  The cases


[020-051]against Boone were to be dismissed and Rerdell was promised immunity.


[020-052]Under these circumstances the second trial commenced.  But of all


[020-053]the people in this country the citizens of Washington care least


[020-054]for Presidents and members of the Cabinets.  They know what these


[020-055]officers are made of.  They know that they are simply folks--that


[020-056]they do not hold office forever--that the Jupiters of to-day are


[020-057]often the pygmies of to-morrow.  They have seen too many people


[020-058]come in with trumpets and flags and go out with hisses and rags to


[020-059]be overawed by the deities of a day.  They have seen Lincoln and


[020-060]they are not to be frightened by his successors.  Arthur took part


[020-061]to the extent of turning out men suspected of being friendly to


[020-062]the defence.  Arthur was in a difficult place.  He was understood


[020-063]to be the friend of Dorsey and, of course, had to do something.


[020-064]Nothing is more dangerous than a friend in power.  He is obliged


[020-065]to show that he is impartial, and it always takes a good deal of


[020-066]injustice to establish a reputation for fairness.


[020-067]_Question_.  Was there any ground to expect aid or any different


[020-068]action on Arthur's part?


[020-069]_Answer_.  All we expected was that Arthur would do as the soldier


[020-070]wanted the Lord to do at New Orleans--"Just take neither side."


[020-071]_Question_.  Why did not Brewster speak?


[020-072]_Answer_.  The Court would not allow two closings.  The Attorney-


[020-073]General did not care to speak in the "middle."  He wished to close,


[020-074]and as he could not do that without putting Mr. Merrick out, he


[020-075]concluded to remain silent.  The defendants had no objection to


[020-076]his speaking, but they objected to two closing arguments for the


[020-077]Government, and the Court decided they were right.  Of course, I


[020-078]understand nothing about the way in which the attorneys for the


[020-079]prosecution arranged their difficulties.  That was nothing to me;


[020-080]neither do I care what money they received--all that is for the


[020-081]next Congress.  It is not for me to speak of those questions.


[020-082]_Question_.  Will there be other trials?


[020-083]_Answer_.  I think not.  It does not seem likely that other attorneys


[020-084]will want to try, and the old ones have.  My opinion is that we


[020-085]have had the last of the Star Route trials.  It was claimed that


[020-086]the one tried was the strongest.  If this is so the rest had better


[020-087]be dismissed.  I think the people are tired of the whole business.


[020-088]It now seems probable that all the time for the next few years will


[020-089]be taken up in telling about the case that was tried.  I see that


[020-090]Cook is telling about MacVeagh and James and Brewster and Bliss;


[020-091]Walsh is giving his opinion of Kellogg and Foster; Bliss is saying


[020-092]a few words about Cook and Gibson; Brewster is telling what Bliss


[020-093]told him; Gibson will have his say about Garfield and MacVeagh,


[020-094]and it now seems probable that we shall get the bottom facts about


[020-095]the other jury--the actions of Messrs. Hoover, Bowen, Brewster


[020-096]Cameron and others.  Personally I have no interest in the business.


[020-097]_Question_.  How does the next campaign look?


[020-098]_Answer_.  The Republicans are making all the mistakes they can,


[020-099]and the only question now is, Can the Democrats make more?  The


[020-100]tariff will be one of the great questions, and may be the only one


[020-101]except success.  The Democrats are on both sides of the question.


[020-102]They hate to give up the word "only."  Only for that word they


[020-103]might have succeeded in 1880.  If they can let "only" alone, and


[020-104]say they want "a tariff for revenue" they will do better.  The fact


[020-105]is the people are not in favor of free trade, neither do they want


[020-106]a tariff high enough to crush a class, but they do want a tariff


[020-107]to raise a revenue and to protect our industries.  I am for protection


[020-108]because it diversifies industries and develops brain--allows us to


[020-109]utilize all the muscle and brain we have.  A party attacking the


[020-110]manufacturing interests of this country will fail.  There are too


[020-111]many millions of dollars invested and too many millions of people


[020-112]interested.  The country is becoming alike interested in this


[020-113]question.  We are no longer divided, as in slavery times, into


[020-114]manufacturing and agricultural districts or sections.  Georgia,


[020-115]Alabama, Tennessee, Louisiana and Texas have manufacturing interests.


[020-116]And the Western States believe in the protection of their industries.


[020-117]The American people have a genius for manufacturing, a genius for


[020-118]invention.  We are not the greatest painters or sculptors or


[020-119]scientists, but we are without doubt the greatest inventors.  If


[020-120]we were all engaged in one business we would become stupid.


[020-121]Agricultural countries produce great wealth, but are never rich.


[020-122]To get rich it is necessary to mix thought with labor.  To raise


[020-123]the raw material is a question of strength; to manufacture, to put


[020-124]it in useful and beautiful forms, is a question of mind.  There is


[020-125]a vast difference between the value of, say, a milestone and a


[020-126]statue, and yet the labor expended in getting the raw material is


[020-127]about the same.  The point, after all, is this:  First, we must


[020-128]have revenue; second, shall we get this by direct taxation or shall


[020-129]we tax imports and at the same time protect American labor?  The


[020-130]party that advocates reasonable protection will succeed.*


[020-131][* At this point, with far away peals of thunder, the storm


[020-132]ceased, the sun reappeared and a vault of heavenly blue


[020-133]swung overhead. "Let us get out," said Colonel Ingersoll.


[020-134]Suiting the action to the word, the Colonel struck out


[020-135]lustily for the beach, on which, hard as a rock and firm as


[020-136]flint, he soon planted his sturdy form. And as he lumbered


[020-137]across the sand to the side door of his comfortable cottage,


[020-138]some three hundred feet from the surf, the necessarily


[020-139]suggested contrast between Ingersoll in court and Ingersoll


[020-140]in soaked flannels was illustrated with forcible comicality.


[020-141]Half an hour later he was found in the cozy library puffing


[020-142]a high flavored Havana, and listening to home-made music of


[020-143]delicious quality.  Ingersoll at home is pleasant to


[020-144]contemplate.  His sense of personal freedom is there aptly


[020-145]pictured.  Loving wife and affectionate daughters form, with


[020-146]happy-faced and genial-hearted father, a model circle into


[020-147]which friends deem it a privilege to enter and a pleasure to


[020-148]remain.

[020-150]_Question_.  In view of all this, where do you think the presidential


[020-151]candidate will come from?


[020-152]_Answer_.  From the West.


[020-153]_Question_.  Why so?


[020-154]_Answer_.  The South and East must compromise.  Both can trust the


[020-155]West.  The West represents the whole country.  There is no


[020-156]provincialism in the West.  The West is not old enough to have the


[020-157]prejudice of section; it is too prosperous to have hatred, too


[020-158]great to feel envy.


[020-159]_Question_.  You do not seem to think that Arthur has a chance?


[020-160]_Answer_.  No Vice-President was ever made President by the people.


[020-161]It is natural to resent the accident that gave the Vice-President


[020-162]the place.  They regard the Vice-President as children do a


[020-163]stepmother.  He is looked upon as temporary--a device to save the


[020-164]election--a something to stop a gap--a lighter--a political raft.


[020-165]He holds the horse until another rider is found.  People do not


[020-166]wish death to suggest nominees for the presidency.  I do not believe


[020-167]it will be possible for Mr. Arthur, no matter how well he acts, to


[020-168]overcome this feeling.  The people like a new man.  There is some


[020-169]excitement in the campaign, and besides they can have the luxury


[020-170]of believing that the new man is a great man.


[020-171]_Question_.  Do you not think Arthur has grown and is a greater


[020-172]man than when he was elected?


[020-173]_Answer_.  Arthur was placed in very trying circumstances, and, I


[020-174]think, behaved with great discretion.  But he was Vice-President,


[020-175]and that is a vice that people will not pardon.


[020-176]_Question_.  How do you regard the situation in Ohio?


[020-177]_Answer_.  I hear that the Republicans are attacking Hoadly, saying


[020-178]that he is an Infidel.  I know nothing about Mr. Hoadly's theological


[020-179]sentiments, but he certainly has the right to have and express his


[020-180]own views.  If the Republicans of Ohio have made up their minds to


[020-181]disfranchise the Liberals, the sooner they are beaten the better.


[020-182]Why should the Republican party be so particular about religious


[020-183]belief?  Was Lincoln an orthodox Christian?  Were the founders of


[020-184]the party--the men who gave it heart and brain--conspicuous for


[020-185]piety?  Were the abolitionists all believers in the inspiration of


[020-186]the Bible?  Is Judge Hoadly to be attacked because he exercises


[020-187]the liberty that he gives to others?  Has not the Republican party


[020-188]trouble enough with the spirituous to let the spiritual alone?  If


[020-189]the religious issue is made, I hope that the party making it will


[020-190]be defeated.  I know nothing about the effect of the recent decision


[020-191]of the Supreme Court of Ohio.  It is a very curious decision and


[020-192]seems to avoid the Constitution with neatness and despatch.  The


[020-193]decision seems to rest on the difference between the words tax and


[020-194]license--_I. e._, between allowing a man to sell whiskey for a tax


[020-195]of one hundred dollars or giving him a license to sell whiskey and


[020-196]charging him one hundred dollars.  In this, the difference is in


[020-197]the law instead of the money.  So far all the prohibitory legislation


[020-198]on the liquor question has been a failure.  Beer is victorious,


[020-199]and Gambrinus now has Olympus all to himself.  On his side is the


[020-200]"bail"--
