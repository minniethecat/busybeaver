[030-001]The expectation of a farthing in this world was supposed to outweigh


[030-002]the fear of God's wrath in the next.  All the pangs, pains, and


[030-003]penalties of perdition were considered as nothing when compared


[030-004]with pounds, shillings and pence in this world.


[030-005]_Question_.  You know that in nearly all deliberative bodies--in


[030-006]parliaments and congresses--an oath or an affirmation is required


[030-007]to support what is called the Constitution; and that all officers


[030-008]are required to swear or affirm that they will discharge their


[030-009]duties; do these oaths and affirmations, in your judgment, do any


[030-010]good?


[030-011]_Answer_.  Men have sought to make nations and institutions immortal


[030-012]by oaths.  Subjects have sworn to obey kings, and kings have sworn


[030-013]to protect subjects, and yet the subjects have sometimes beheaded


[030-014]a king; and the king has often plundered the subjects.  The oaths


[030-015]enabled them to deceive each other.  Every absurdity in religion,


[030-016]and all tyrannical institutions, have been patched, buttressed,


[030-017]and reinforced by oaths; and yet the history of the world shows


[030-018]the utter futility of putting in the coffin of an oath the political


[030-019]and religious aspirations of the race.


[030-020]Revolutions and reformations care little for "So help me God."


[030-021]Oaths have riveted shackles and sanctified abuses.  People swear


[030-022]to support a constitution, and they will keep the oath as long as


[030-023]the constitution supports them.  In 1776 the colonists cared nothing


[030-024]for the fact that they had sworn to support the British crown.


[030-025]All the oaths to defend the Constitution of the United States did


[030-026]not prevent the Civil War.  We have at last learned that States


[030-027]may be kept together for a little time, by force; permanently only


[030-028]by mutual interests.  We have found that the Delilah of superstition


[030-029]cannot bind with oaths the secular Samson.


[030-030]Why should a member of Parliament or of Congress swear to maintain


[030-031]the Constitution?  If he is a dishonest man, the oath will have no


[030-032]effect; if he is an honest patriot, it will have no effect.  In


[030-033]both cases it is equally useless.  If a member fails to support


[030-034]the Constitution the probability is that his constituents will


[030-035]treat him as he does the Constitution.  In this country, after all


[030-036]the members of Congress have sworn or affirmed to defend the


[030-037]Constitution, each political party charges the other with a deliberate


[030-038]endeavor to destroy that "sacred instrument."  Possibly the political


[030-039]oath was invented to prevent the free and natural development of


[030-040]a nation.  Kings and nobles and priests wished to retain the property


[030-041]they had filched and clutched, and for that purpose they compelled


[030-042]the real owners to swear that they would support and defend the


[030-043]law under color of which the theft and robbery had been accomplished.


[030-044]So, in the church, creeds have been protected by oaths.  Priests


[030-045]and laymen solemnly swore that they would, under no circumstances,


[030-046]resort to reason; that they would overcome facts by faith, and


[030-047]strike down demonstrations with the "sword of the spirit."  Professors


[030-048]of the theological seminary at Andover, Massachusetts, swear to


[030-049]defend certain dogmas and to attack others.  They swear sacredly


[030-050]to keep and guard the ignorance they have.  With them, philosophy


[030-051]leads to perjury, and reason is the road to crime.  While theological


[030-052]professors are not likely to make an intellectual discovery, still


[030-053]it is unwise, by taking an oath, to render that certain which is


[030-054]only improbable.


[030-055]If all witnesses sworn to tell the truth, did so, if all members


[030-056]of Parliament and of Congress, in taking the oath, became intelligent,


[030-057]patriotic, and honest, I should be in favor of retaining the


[030-058]ceremony; but we find that men who have taken the same oath advocate


[030-059]opposite ideas, and entertain different opinions, as to the meaning


[030-060]of constitutions and laws.  The oath adds nothing to their


[030-061]intelligence; does not even tend to increase their patriotism, and


[030-062]certainly does not make the dishonest honest.


[030-063]_Question_.  Are not persons allowed to testify in the United States


[030-064]whether they believe in future rewards and punishments or not?


[030-065]_Answer_.  In this country, in most of the States, witnesses are


[030-066]allowed to testify whether they believe in perdition and paradise


[030-067]or not.  In some States they are allowed to testify even if they


[030-068]deny the existence of God.  We have found that religious belief


[030-069]does not compel people to tell the truth, and than an utter denial


[030-070]of every Christian creed does not even tend to make them dishonest.


[030-071]You see, a religious belief does not affect the senses.  Justice


[030-072]should not shut any door that leads to truth.  No one will pretend


[030-073]that, because you do not believe in hell, your sight is impaired,


[030-074]or your hearing dulled, or your memory rendered less retentive.


[030-075]A witness in a court is called upon to tell what he has seen, what


[030-076]he has heard, what he remembers, not what he believes about gods


[030-077]and devils and hells and heavens.  A witness substantiates not a


[030-078]faith, but a fact.  In order to ascertain whether a witness will


[030-079]tell the truth, you might with equal propriety examine him as to


[030-080]his ideas about music, painting or architecture, as theology.  A


[030-081]man may have no ear for music, and yet remember what he hears.  He


[030-082]may care nothing about painting, and yet is able to tell what he


[030-083]sees.  So he may deny every creed, and yet be able to tell the


[030-084]facts as he remembers them.


[030-085]Thomas Jefferson was wise enough so to frame the Constitution of


[030-086]Virginia that no person could be deprived of any civil right on


[030-087]account of his religious or irreligious belief.  Through the


[030-088]influence of men like Paine, Franklin and Jefferson, it was provided


[030-089]in the Federal Constitution that officers elected under its authority


[030-090]could swear or affirm.  This was the natural result of the separation


[030-091]of church and state.


[030-092]_Question_.  I see that your Presidents and Governors issue their


[030-093]proclamations calling on the people to assemble in their churches


[030-094]and offer thanks to God.  How does this happen in a Government


[030-095]where church and state are not united?


[030-096]_Answer_.  Jefferson, when President, refused to issue what is


[030-097]known as the "Thanksgiving Proclamation," on the ground that the


[030-098]Federal Government had no right to interfere in religious matters;


[030-099]that the people owed no religious duties to the Government; that


[030-100]the Government derived its powers, not from priests or gods, but


[030-101]from the people, and was responsible alone to the source of its


[030-102]power.  The truth is, the framers of our Constitution intended that


[030-103]the Government should be secular in the broadest and best sense;


[030-104]and yet there are thousands and thousands of religious people in


[030-105]this country who are greatly scandalized because there is no


[030-106]recognition of God in the Federal Constitution; and for several


[030-107]years a great many ministers have been endeavoring to have the


[030-108]Constitution amended so as to recognize the existence of God and


[030-109]the divinity of Christ.  A man by the name of Pollock was once


[030-110]superintendent of the mint of Philadelphia.  He was almost insane


[030-111]about having God in the Constitution.  Failing in that, he got the


[030-112]inscription on our money, "In God we Trust."  As our silver dollar


[030-113]is now, in fact, worth only eighty-five cents, it is claimed that


[030-114]the inscription means that we trust in God for the other fifteen


[030-115]cents.


[030-116]There is a constant effort on the part of many Christians to have


[030-117]their religion in some way recognized by law.  Proclamations are


[030-118]now issued calling upon the people to give thanks, and directing


[030-119]attention to the fact that, while God has scourged or neglected


[030-120]other nations, he has been remarkably attentive to the wants and


[030-121]wishes of the United States.  Governors of States issue these


[030-122]documents written in a tone of pious insincerity.  The year may or


[030-123]may not have been prosperous, yet the degree of thankfulness called


[030-124]for is always precisely the same.


[030-125]A few years ago the Governor of Iowa issued an exceedingly rhetorical


[030-126]proclamation, in which the people were requested to thank God for


[030-127]the unparalleled blessings he had showered upon them.  A private


[030-128]citizen, fearing that the Lord might be misled by official


[030-129]correspondence, issued his proclamation, in which he recounted with


[030-130]great particularity the hardships of the preceding year.  He insisted


[030-131]that the weather had been of the poorest quality; that the spring


[030-132]came late, and the frost early; that the people were in debt; that


[030-133]the farms were mortgaged; that the merchants were bankrupt; and


[030-134]that everything was in the worst possible condition.  He concluded


[030-135]by sincerely hoping that the Lord would pay no attention to the


[030-136]proclamation of the Governor, but would, if he had any doubt on


[030-137]the subject, come down and examine the State for himself.


[030-138]These proclamations have always appeared to me absurdly egotistical.


[030-139]Why should God treat us any better than he does the rest of his


[030-140]children?  Why should he send pestilence and famine to China, and


[030-141]health and plenty to us?  Why give us corn, and Egypt cholera?


[030-142]All these proclamations grow out of egotism and selfishness, of


[030-143]ignorance and superstition, and are based upon the idea that God


[030-144]is a capricious monster; that he loves flattery; that he can be


[030-145]coaxed and cajoled.


[030-146]The conclusion of the whole matter with me is this:  For truth in


[030-147]courts we must depend upon the trained intelligence of judges, the


[030-148]right of cross-examination, the honesty and common sense of jurors,


[030-149]and upon an enlightened public opinion.  As for members of Congress,


[030-150]we will trust to the wisdom and patriotism, not only of the members,


[030-151]but of their constituents.  In religion we will give to all the


[030-152]luxury of absolute liberty.


[030-153]The alchemist did not succeed in finding any stone the touch of


[030-154]which transmuted baser things to gold; and priests have not invented


[030-155]yet an oath with power to force from falsehood's desperate lips


[030-156]the pearl of truth.


[030-157]--_Secular Review_, London, England, 1884.


[030-158]WENDELL PHILLIPS, FITZ JOHN PORTER AND BISMARCK.


[030-159]_Question_.  Are you seeking to quit public lecturing on religious


[030-160]questions?


[030-161]_Answer_.  As long as I live I expect now and then to say my say


[030-162]against the religious bigotry and cruelty of the world.  As long


[030-163]as the smallest coal is red in hell I am going to keep on.  I never


[030-164]had the slightest idea of retiring.  I expect the church to do the


[030-165]retiring.


[030-166]_Question_.  What do you think of Wendell Phillips as an orator?


[030-167]_Answer_.  He was a very great orator--one of the greatest that


[030-168]the world has produced.  He rendered immense service in the cause


[030-169]of freedom.  He was in the old days the thunderbolt that pierced


[030-170]the shield of the Constitution.  One of the bravest soldiers that


[030-171]ever fought for human rights was Wendell Phillips.


[030-172]_Question_.  What do you think of the action of Congress on Fitz


[030-173]John Porter?


[030-174]_Answer_.  I think Congress did right.  I think they should have


[030-175]taken this action long before.  There was a question of his guilt,


[030-176]and he should have been given the benefit of a doubt.  They say he


[030-177]could have defeated Longstreet.  There are some people, you know,


[030-178]who would have it that an army could be whipped by a good general


[030-179]with six mules and a blunderbuss.  But we do not regard those


[030-180]people.  They know no more about it than a lady who talked to me


[030-181]about Porter's case.  She argued the question of Porter's guilt


[030-182]for half an hour.  I showed her where she was all wrong.  When she


[030-183]found she was beaten she took refuge with "Oh, well, anyhow he had


[030-184]no genius."  Well, if every man is to be shot who has no genius,


[030-185]I want to go into the coffin business.


[030-186]_Question_.  What, in your judgment, is necessary to be done to


[030-187]insure Republican success this fall?


[030-188]_Answer_.  It is only necessary for the Republican party to stand


[030-189]by its principles.  We must be in favor of protecting American


[030-190]labor not only, but of protecting American capital, and we must be


[030-191]in favor of civil rights, and must advocate the doctrine that the


[030-192]Federal Government must protect all citizens.  I am in favor of a


[030-193]tariff, not simply to raise a revenue--that I regard as incidental.


[030-194]The Democrats regard protection as incidental.  The two principles


[030-195]should be, protection to American industry and protection to American


[030-196]citizens.  So that, after all, there is but one issue--protection.


[030-197]As a matter of fact, that is all a government is for--to protect.


[030-198]The Republican party is stronger to-day than it was four years ago.


[030-199]The Republican party stands for the progressive ideas of the American


[030-200]people.  It has been said that the administration will control the


[030-201]Southern delegates. I do not believe it.  This administration has


[030-202]not been friendly to the Southern Republicans, and my opinion is


[030-203]there will be as much division in the Southern as in the Northern


[030-204]States.  I believe Blaine will be a candidate, and I do not believe


[030-205]the Prohibitionists will put a ticket in the field, because they


[030-206]have no hope of success.
