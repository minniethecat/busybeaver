[091-001]_Question_.  Has not Spiritualism added to the world's stock of


[091-002]hope?  And in what way has not Spiritualism done good?


[091-003]_Answer_.  The mother holding in her arms her dead child, believing


[091-004]that the babe has simply passed to another life, does not weep as


[091-005]bitterly as though she thought that death was the eternal end.  A


[091-006]belief in Spiritualism must be a consolation.  You see, the


[091-007]Spiritualists do not believe in eternal pain, and consequently a


[091-008]belief in immortality does not fill their hearts with fear.


[091-009]Christianity makes eternal life an infinite horror, and casts the


[091-010]glare of hell on almost every grave.


[091-011]The Spiritualists appear to be happy in their belief.  I have never


[091-012]known a happy orthodox Christian.


[091-013]It is natural to shun death, natural to desire eternal life.  With


[091-014]all my heart I hope for everlasting life and joy--a life without


[091-015]failures, without crimes and tears.


[091-016]If immortality could be established, the river of life would overflow


[091-017]with happiness.  The faces of prisoners, of slaves, of the deserted,


[091-018]of the diseased and starving would be radiant with smiles, and the


[091-019]dull eyes of despair would glow with light.


[091-020]If it could be established.


[091-021]Let us hope.


[091-022]--_The Journal_, New York, July 26, 1896.


[091-023]A LITTLE OF EVERYTHING.


[091-024]_Question_.  What is your opinion of the position taken by the


[091-025]United States in the Venezuelan dispute?  How should the dispute


[091-026]be settled?


[091-027]_Answer_.  I do not think that we have any interest in the dispute


[091-028]between Venezuela and England.  It was and is none of our business.


[091-029]The Monroe doctrine was not and is not in any way involved.  Mr.


[091-030]Cleveland made a mistake and so did Congress.


[091-031]_Question_.  What should be the attitude of the church toward the


[091-032]stage?


[091-033]_Answer_.  It should be, what it always has been, against it.  If


[091-034]the orthodox churches are right, then the stage is wrong.  The


[091-035]stage makes people forget hell; and this puts their souls in peril.


[091-036]There will be forever a conflict between Shakespeare and the Bible.


[091-037]_Question_.  What do you think of the new woman?


[091-038]_Answer_.  I like her.


[091-039]_Question_.  Where rests the responsibility for the Armenian


[091-040]atrocities?


[091-041]_Answer_.  Religion is the cause of the hatred and bloodshed.


[091-042]_Question_.  What do you think of international marriages, as


[091-043]between titled foreigners and American heiresses?


[091-044]_Answer_.  My opinion is the same as is entertained by the American


[091-045]girl after the marriages.  It is a great mistake.


[091-046]_Question_.  What do you think of England's Poet Laureate, Alfred


[091-047]Austin?


[091-048]_Answer_.  I have only read a few of his lines and they were not


[091-049]poetic.  The office of Poet Laureate should be abolished.  Men


[091-050]cannot write poems to order as they could deliver cabbages or beer.


[091-051]By poems I do not mean jingles of words.  I mean great thoughts


[091-052]clothed in splendor.


[091-053]_Question_.  What is your estimate of Susan B. Anthony?


[091-054]_Answer_.  Miss Anthony is one of the most remarkable women in the


[091-055]world.  She has the enthusiasm of youth and spring, the courage


[091-056]and sincerity of a martyr.  She is as reliable as the attraction


[091-057]of gravitation.  She is absolutely true to her conviction,


[091-058]intellectually honest, logical, candid and infinitely persistent.


[091-059]No human being has done more for women than Miss Anthony.  She has


[091-060]won the respect and admiration of the best people on the earth.


[091-061]And so I say:  Good luck and long life to Susan B. Anthony.


[091-062]_Question_.  Which did more for his country, George Washington or


[091-063]Abraham Lincoln?


[091-064]_Answer_.  In my judgment, Lincoln was the greatest man ever


[091-065]President.  I put him above Washington and Jefferson.  He had the


[091-066]genius of goodness; and he was one of the wisest and shrewdest of


[091-067]men.  Lincoln towers above them all.


[091-068]_Question_.  What gave rise to the report that you had been converted


[091-069]--did you go to church somewhere?


[091-070]_Answer_.  I visited the "People's Church" in Kalamazoo, Michigan.


[091-071]This church has no creed.  The object is to make people happy in


[091-072]this world.  Miss Bartlett is the pastor.  She is a remarkable


[091-073]woman and is devoting her life to good work.  I liked her church


[091-074]and said so.  This is all.


[091-075]_Question_.  Are there not some human natures so morally weak or


[091-076]diseased that they cannot keep from sin without the aid of some


[091-077]sort of religion?


[091-078]_Answer_.  I do not believe that the orthodox religion helps anybody


[091-079]to be just, generous or honest.  Superstition is not the soil in


[091-080]which goodness grows.  Falsehood is poor medicine.


[091-081]_Question_.  Would you consent to live in any but a Christian


[091-082]community?  If you would, please name one.


[091-083]_Answer_.  I would not live in a community where all were orthodox


[091-084]Christians.  I would rather dwell in Central Africa.  If I could


[091-085]have my choice I would rather live among people who were free, who


[091-086]sought for truth and lived according to reason.  Sometime there


[091-087]will be such a community.


[091-088]_Question_.  Is the noun "United States" singular or plural, as


[091-089]you use English?


[091-090]_Answer_.  I use it in the singular.


[091-091]_Question_.  Have you read Nordau's "Degeneracy"?  If so, what do


[091-092]you think of it?


[091-093]_Answer_.  I think it is substantially insane.


[091-094]_Question_.  What do you think of Bishop Doane's advocacy of free


[091-095]rum as a solution of the liquor problem?


[091-096]_Answer_.  I am a believer in liberty.  All the temperance legislation,


[091-097]all the temperance societies, all the agitation, all these things


[091-098]have done no good.


[091-099]_Question_.  Do you agree with Mr. Carnegie that a college education


[091-100]is of little or no practical value to a man?


[091-101]_Answer_.  A man must have education.  It makes no difference where


[091-102]or how he gets it.  To study the dead languages is time wasted so


[091-103]far as success in business is concerned.  Most of the colleges in


[091-104]this country are poor because controlled by theologians.


[091-105]_Question_.  What suggestion would you make for the improvement of


[091-106]the newspapers of this country?


[091-107]_Answer_.  Every article in a newspaper should be signed by the


[091-108]writer.  And all writers should do their best to tell the exact


[091-109]facts.


[091-110]_Question_.  What do you think of Niagara Falls?


[091-111]_Answer_.  It is a dangerous place.  Those great rushing waters--


[091-112]there is nothing attractive to me in them.  There is so much noise;


[091-113]so much tumult.  It is simply a mighty force of nature--one of


[091-114]those tremendous powers that is to be feared for its danger.  What


[091-115]I like in nature is a cultivated field, where men can work in the


[091-116]free open air, where there is quiet and repose--no turmoil, no


[091-117]strife, no tumult, no fearful roar or struggle for mastery.  I do


[091-118]not like the crowded, stuffy workshop, where life is slavery and


[091-119]drudgery.  Give me the calm, cultivated land of waving grain, of


[091-120]flowers, of happiness.


[091-121]_Question_.  What is worse than death?


[091-122]_Answer_.  Oh, a great many things.  To be dishonored.  To be


[091-123]worthless.  To feel that you are a failure.  To be insane.  To be


[091-124]constantly afraid of the future.  To lose the ones you love.


[091-125]--_The Herald_, Rochester, New York, February 25, 1896.


[091-126]IS LIFE WORTH LIVING--CHRISTIAN SCIENCE AND POLITICS.


[091-127]_Question_.  With all your experiences, the trials, the responsibilities,


[091-128]the disappointments, the heartburnings, Colonel, is life worth


[091-129]living?


[091-130]_Answer_.  Well, I can only answer for myself.  I like to be alive,


[091-131]to breathe the air, to look at the landscape, the clouds and stars,


[091-132]to repeat old poems, to look at pictures and statues, to hear music,


[091-133]the voices of the ones I love.  I like to talk with my wife, my


[091-134]girls, my grandchildren.  I like to sleep and to dream.  Yes, you


[091-135]can say that life, to me, is worth living.


[091-136]_Question_.  Colonel, did you ever kill any game?


[091-137]_Answer_.  When I was a boy I killed two ducks, and it hurt me as


[091-138]much as anything I ever did.  No, I would not kill any living


[091-139]creature.  I am sometimes tempted to kill a mosquito on my hand,


[091-140]but I stop and think what a wonderful construction it has, and shoo


[091-141]it away.


[091-142]_Question_.  What do you think of political parties, Colonel?


[091-143]_Answer_.  In a country where the sovereignty is divided among the


[091-144]people, that is to say, among the men, in order to accomplish


[091-145]anything, many must unite, and I believe in joining the party that


[091-146]is going the nearest your way.  I do not believe in being the slave


[091-147]or serf or servant of a party.  Go with it if it is going your


[091-148]road, and when the road forks, take the one that leads to the place


[091-149]you wish to visit, no matter whether the party goes that way or


[091-150]not.  I do not believe in belonging to a party or being the property


[091-151]of any organization.  I do not believe in giving a mortgage on


[091-152]yourself or a deed of trust for any purpose whatever.  It is better


[091-153]to be free and vote wrong than to be a slave and vote right.  I


[091-154]believe in taking the chances.  At the same time, as long as a


[091-155]party is going my way, I believe in placing that party above


[091-156]particular persons, and if that party nominates a man that I despise,


[091-157]I will vote for him if he is going my way.  I would rather have a


[091-158]bad man belonging to my party in place, than a good man belonging


[091-159]to the other, provided my man believes in my principles, and to


[091-160]that extent I believe in party loyalty.


[091-161]Neither do I join in the general hue and cry against bosses.  There


[091-162]has always got to be a leader, even in a flock of wild geese.  If


[091-163]anything is to be accomplished, no matter what, somebody takes the


[091-164]lead and the others allow him to go on.  In that way political


[091-165]bosses are made, and when you hear a man howling against bosses at


[091-166]the top of his lungs, distending his cheeks to the bursting point,


[091-167]you may know that he has ambition to become a boss.


[091-168]I do not belong to the Republican party, but I have been going with


[091-169]it, and when it goes wrong I shall quit, unless the other is worse.


[091-170]There is no office, no place, that I want, and as it does not cost


[091-171]anything to be right, I think it better to be that way.


[091-172]_Question_.  What is your idea of Christian Science?


[091-173]_Answer_.  I think it is superstition, pure and unadulterated.  I


[091-174]think that soda will cure a sour stomach better than thinking.  In


[091-175]my judgment, quinine is a better tonic than meditation.  Of course


[091-176]cheerfulness is good and depression bad, but if you can absolutely


[091-177]control the body and all its functions by thought, what is the use


[091-178]of buying coal?  Let the mercury go down and keep yourself hot by


[091-179]thinking.  What is the use of wasting money for food?  Fill your


[091-180]stomach with think.  According to these Christian Science people


[091-181]all that really exists is an illusion, and the only realities are


[091-182]the things that do not exist.  They are like the old fellow in


[091-183]India who said that all things were illusions.  One day he was


[091-184]speaking to a crowd on his favorite hobby.  Just as he said "all


[091-185]is illusion" a fellow on an elephant rode toward him.  The elephant


[091-186]raised his trunk as though to strike, thereupon the speaker ran


[091-187]away.  Then the crowd laughed.  In a few moments the speaker


[091-188]returned.  The people shouted:  "If all is illusion, what made you


[091-189]run away?"  The speaker replied:  "My poor friends, I said all is


[091-190]illusion.  I say so still.  There was no elephant.  I did not run


[091-191]away.  You did not laugh, and I am not explaining now.  All is


[091-192]illusion."


[091-193]That man must have been a Christian Scientist.


[091-194]--_The Inter-Ocean_, Chicago, November, 1897.


[091-195]VIVISECTION.


[091-196]_Question_.  Why are you so utterly opposed to vivisection?


[091-197]_Answer_.  Because, as it is generally practiced, it is an unspeakable


[091-198]cruelty.  Because it hardens the hearts and demoralizes those who


[091-199]inflict useless and terrible pains on the bound and helpless.  If


[091-200]these vivisectionists would give chloroform or ether to the animals


[091-201]they dissect; if they would render them insensible to pain, and


[091-202]if, by cutting up these animals, they could learn anything worth


[091-203]knowing, no one would seriously object.
