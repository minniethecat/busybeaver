[034-001]Some people say that God existed from eternity, and has created


[034-002]eternity.  It is impossible to conceive of an act co-equal with


[034-003]eternity.  If you say that God has existed forever, and has always


[034-004]acted, then you make the universe eternal, and you make the universe


[034-005]as old as God; and if the universe be as old as God, he certainly


[034-006]did not create it.


[034-007]These questions of origin and destiny--of infinite gods--are beyond


[034-008]the powers of the human mind.  They cannot be solved.  We might as


[034-009]well try to travel fast enough to get beyond the horizon.  It is


[034-010]like a man trying to run away from his girdle.  Consequently, I


[034-011]believe in turning our attention to things of importance--to


[034-012]questions that may by some possibility be solved.  It is of no


[034-013]importance to me whether God exists or not.  I exist, and it is


[034-014]important to me to be happy while I exist.  Therefore I had better


[034-015]turn my attention to finding out the secret of happiness, instead


[034-016]of trying to ascertain the secret of the universe.


[034-017]I say with regard to God, I do not know; and therefore I am accused


[034-018]of being arrogant and egotistic.  Religious papers say that I do


[034-019]know, because Webster told me.  They use Webster as a witness to


[034-020]prove the divinity of Christ.  They say that Webster was on the


[034-021]God side, and therefore I ought to be.  I can hardly afford to take


[034-022]Webster's ideas of another world, when his ideas about this were


[034-023]so bad.  When bloodhounds were pursuing a woman through the tangled


[034-024]swamps of the South--she hungry for liberty--Webster took the side


[034-025]of the bloodhounds.  Such a man is no authority for me.  Bacon


[034-026]denied the Copernican system of astronomy; he is an unsafe guide.


[034-027]Wesley believed in witches; I cannot follow him.  No man should


[034-028]quote a name instead of an argument; no man should bring forward


[034-029]a person instead of a principle, unless he is willing to accept


[034-030]all the ideas of that person.


[034-031]_Question_.  Is not a pleasant illusion preferable to a dreary


[034-032]truth--a future life being in question?


[034-033]_Answer_.  I think it is.  I think that a pleasing illusion is


[034-034]better then a terrible truth, so far as its immediate results are


[034-035]concerned.  I would rather think the one I love living, than to


[034-036]think her dead.  I would rather think that I had a large balance


[034-037]in bank than that my account was overdrawn.  I would rather think


[034-038]I was healthy than to know that I had a cancer.  But if we have an


[034-039]illusion, let us have it pleasing.  The orthodox illusion is the


[034-040]worst that can possibly be conceived.  Take hell out of that


[034-041]illusion, take eternal pain away from that dream, and say that the


[034-042]whole world is to be happy forever--then you might have an excuse


[034-043]for calling it a pleasant illusion; but it is, in fact, a nightmare


[034-044]--a perpetual horror--a cross, on which the happiness of man has


[034-045]been crucified.


[034-046]_Question_.  Are not religion and morals inseparable?


[034-047]_Answer_.  Religion and morality have nothing in common, and yet


[034-048]there is no religion except the practice of morality.  But what


[034-049]you call religion is simply superstition.  Religion as it is now


[034-050]taught teaches our duties toward God--our obligations to the


[034-051]Infinite, and the results of a failure to discharge those obligations.


[034-052]I believe that we are under no obligations to the Infinite; that


[034-053]we cannot be.  All our obligations are to each other, and to sentient


[034-054]beings.  "Believe in the Lord Jesus Christ, and thou shalt be


[034-055]saved," has nothing to do with morality.  "Do unto other as ye


[034-056]would that others should do unto you" has nothing to do with


[034-057]believing in the Lord Jesus Christ.  Baptism has nothing to do with


[034-058]morality.  "Pay your honest debts."  That has nothing to do with


[034-059]baptism.  What is called religion is simple superstition, with


[034-060]which morality has nothing to do.


[034-061]The churches do not prevent people from committing natural offences,


[034-062]but restrain them from committing artificial ones.  As for instance,


[034-063]the Catholic Church can prevent one of its members from eating meat


[034-064]on Friday, but not from whipping his wife.  The Episcopal Church


[034-065]can prevent dancing, it may be, in Lent, but not slander.  The


[034-066]Presbyterian can keep a man from working on Sunday, but not from


[034-067]practicing deceit on Monday.  And so I might go through the churches.


[034-068]They lay the greater stress upon the artificial offences.  Those


[034-069]countries that are the most religious are the most immoral.  When


[034-070]the world was under the control of the Catholic Church, it reached


[034-071]the very pit of immorality, and nations have advanced in morals


[034-072]just in proportion that they have lost Christianity.


[034-073]_Question_.  It is frequently asserted that there is nothing new


[034-074]in your objections against Christianity.  What is your reply to


[034-075]such assertions?


[034-076]_Answer_.  Of course, the editors of religious papers will say


[034-077]this; Christians will say this.  In my opinion, an argument is new


[034-078]until it has been answered.  An argument is absolutely fresh, and


[034-079]has upon its leaves the dew of morning, until it has been refuted.


[034-080]All men have experienced, it may be, in some degree, what we call


[034-081]love.  Millions of men have written about it.  The subject is of


[034-082]course old.  It is only the presentation that can be new.  Thousands


[034-083]of men have attacked superstition.  The subject is old, but the


[034-084]manner in which the facts are handled, the arguments grouped--these


[034-085]may be forever new.  Millions of men have preached Christianity.


[034-086]Certainly there is nothing new in the original ideas.  Nothing can


[034-087]be new except the presentation, the grouping.  The ideas may be


[034-088]old, but they may be clothed in new garments of passion; they may


[034-089]be given additional human interest.  A man takes a fact, or an old


[034-090]subject, as a sculptor takes a rock; the rock is not new.  Of this


[034-091]rock he makes a statue; the statue is new.  And yet some orthodox


[034-092]man might say there is nothing new about that statue:  "I know the


[034-093]man that dug the rock; I know the owner of the quarry."  Substance


[034-094]is eternal; forms are new.  So in the human mind certain ideas, or


[034-095]in the human heart certain passions, are forever old; but genius


[034-096]forever gives them new forms, new meanings; and this is the perpetual


[034-097]originality of genius.


[034-098]_Question_.  Do you consider that churches are injurious to the


[034-099]community?


[034-100]_Answer_.  In the exact proportion that churches teach falsehood;


[034-101]in the exact proportion that they destroy liberty of thought, the


[034-102]free action of the human mind; in the exact proportion that they


[034-103]teach the doctrine of eternal pain, and convince people of its


[034-104]truth--they are injurious.  In the proportion that they teach


[034-105]morality and justice, and practice kindness and charity--in that


[034-106]proportion they are a benefit.  Every church, therefore, is a mixed


[034-107]problem--part good and part bad.  In one direction it leads toward


[034-108]and sheds light; in the other direction its influence is entirely


[034-109]bad.


[034-110]Now, I would like to civilize the churches, so that they will be


[034-111]able to do good deeds without building bad creeds.  In other words,


[034-112]take out the superstitious and the miraculous, and leave the human


[034-113]and the moral.


[034-114]_Question_.  Why do you not respond to the occasional clergyman


[034-115]who replies to your lectures?


[034-116]_Answer_.  In the first place, no clergyman has ever replied to my


[034-117]lectures.  In the second place, no clergyman ever will reply to my


[034-118]lectures.  He does not answer my arguments--he attacks me; and the


[034-119]replies that I have seen are not worth answering.  They are far


[034-120]below the dignity of the question under discussion.  Most of them


[034-121]are ill-mannered, as abusive as illogical, and as malicious as


[034-122]weak.  I cannot reply without feeling humiliated.  I cannot use


[034-123]their weapons, and my weapons they do not understand.  I attack


[034-124]Christianity because it is cruel, and they account for all my


[034-125]actions by putting behind them base motives.  They make it at once


[034-126]a personal question.  They imagine that epithets are good enough


[034-127]arguments with which to answer an Infidel.  A few years ago they


[034-128]would have imprisoned me.  A few years before that they would have


[034-129]burned me.  We have advanced.  Now they only slander; and I


[034-130]congratulate myself on the fact that even that is not believed.


[034-131]Ministers do not believe each other about each other.  The truth


[034-132]has never yet been ascertained in any trial by a church.  The longer


[034-133]the trial lasts, the obscurer is the truth.  They will not believe


[034-134]each other, even on oath; and one of the most celebrated ministers


[034-135]of this country has publicly announced that there is no use in


[034-136]answering a lie started by his own church; that if he does answer


[034-137]it--if he does kill it--forty more lies will come to the funeral.


[034-138]In this connection we must remember that the priests of one religion


[034-139]never credit the miracles of another religion.  Is this because


[034-140]priests instinctively know priests?  Now, when a Christian tells


[034-141]a Buddhist some of the miracles of the Testament, the Buddhist


[034-142]smiles.  When a Buddhist tells a Christian the miracles performed


[034-143]by Buddha, the Christian laughs.  This reminds me of an incident.


[034-144]A man told a most wonderful story.  Everybody present expressed


[034-145]surprise and astonishment, except one man.  He said nothing; he


[034-146]did not even change countenance.  One who noticed that the story


[034-147]had no effect on this man, said to him:  "You do not seem to be


[034-148]astonished in the least at this marvelous tale."  The man replied,


[034-149]"No; I am a liar myself."


[034-150]You see, I am not trying to answer individual ministers.  I am


[034-151]attacking the whole body of superstition.  I am trying to kill the


[034-152]entire dog, and I do not feel like wasting any time killing fleas


[034-153]on that dog.  When the dog dies, the fleas will be out of provisions,


[034-154]and in that way we shall answer them all at once.


[034-155]So, I do not bother myself answering religious newspapers.  In the


[034-156]first place, they are not worth answering; and in the second place,


[034-157]to answer would only produce a new crop of falsehoods.  You know,


[034-158]the editor of a religious newspaper, as a rule, is one who has


[034-159]failed in the pulpit; and you can imagine the brains necessary to


[034-160]edit a religious weekly from this fact.  I have known some good


[034-161]religious editors.  By some I mean one.  I do not say that there


[034-162]are not others, but I do say I do not know them.  I might add,


[034-163]here, that the one I did know is dead.


[034-164]Since I have been in this city there have been some "replies" to


[034-165]me.  They have been almost idiotic.  A Catholic priest asked me


[034-166]how I had the impudence to differ with Newton.  Newton, he says,


[034-167]believed in a God; and I ask this Catholic priest how he has the


[034-168]impudence to differ with Newton.  Newton was a Protestant.  This


[034-169]simply shows the absurdity of using men's names for arguments.


[034-170]This same priest proves the existence of God by a pagan orator.


[034-171]Is it possible that God's last witness died with Cicero?  If it is


[034-172]necessary to believe in a God now, the witnesses ought to be on


[034-173]hand now.


[034-174]Another man, pretending to answer me, quotes Le Conte, a geologist;


[034-175]and according to this geologist we are "getting very near to the


[034-176]splendors of the great white throne."  Where is the great white


[034-177]throne?  Can any one, by studying geology, find the locality of


[034-178]the great white throne?  To what stratum does it belong?  In what


[034-179]geologic period was the great white throne formed?  What on earth


[034-180]has geology to do with the throne of God?


[034-181]The truth is, there can be no reply to the argument that man should


[034-182]be governed by his reason; that he should depend upon observation


[034-183]and experience; that he should use the faculties he has for his


[034-184]own benefit, and the benefit of his fellow-man.  There is no answer.


[034-185]It is not within the power of man to substantiate the supernatural.


[034-186]It is beyond the power of evidence.
