[009-001]If we take the Old Testament for authority, man is not immortal.


[009-002]The Old Testament shows man how he lost immortality.  According to


[009-003]Genesis, God prevented man from putting forth his hand and eating


[009-004]of the Tree of Life.  It is there stated, had he succeeded, man


[009-005]would have lived forever.  God drove him from the garden, preventing


[009-006]him eating of this tree, and in consequence man became mortal; so


[009-007]that if we go by the Old Testament we are compelled to give up


[009-008]immortality.  The New Testament has but little on the subject.  In


[009-009]one place we are told to seek for immortality.  If we are already


[009-010]immortal, it is hard to see why we should go on seeking for it.


[009-011]In another place we are told that they who are worthy to obtain


[009-012]that world and the resurrection of the dead, are not given in


[009-013]marriage.  From this one would infer there would be some unworthy


[009-014]to be raised from the dead.  Upon the question of immortality, the


[009-015]Old Testament throws but little satisfactory light.  I do not deny


[009-016]immortality, nor would I endeavor to shake the belief of anybody


[009-017]in another life.  What I am endeavoring to do is to put out the


[009-018]fires of hell.  If we cannot have heaven without hell, I am in


[009-019]favor of abolishing heaven.  I do not want to go to heaven if one


[009-020]soul is doomed to agony.  I would rather be annihilated.


[009-021]My opinion of immortality is this:


[009-022]First.--I live, and that of itself is infinitely wonderful.


[009-023]Second.--There was a time when I was not, and after I was not, I


[009-024]was.  Third.--Now that I am, I may be again; and it is no more


[009-025]wonderful that I may be again, if I have been, than that I am,


[009-026]having once been nothing.  If the churches advocated immortality,


[009-027]if they advocated eternal justice, if they said that man would be


[009-028]rewarded and punished according to deeds; if they admitted that


[009-029]some time in eternity there would be an opportunity given to lift


[009-030]up souls, and that throughout all the ages the angels of progress


[009-031]and virtue would beckon the fallen upward; and that some time, and


[009-032]no matter how far away they might put off the time, all the children


[009-033]of men would be reasonably happy, I never would say a solitary word


[009-034]against the church, but just as long as they preach that the majority


[009-035]of mankind will suffer eternal pain, just so long I shall oppose


[009-036]them; that is to say, as long as I live.


[009-037]_Question_.  Do you believe in a God; and, if so, what kind of a


[009-038]God?


[009-039]_Answer_.  Let me, in the first place, lay a foundation for an


[009-040]answer.


[009-041]First.--Man gets all food for thought through the medium of the


[009-042]senses.  The effect of nature upon the senses, and through the


[009-043]senses upon the brain, must be natural.  All food for thought,


[009-044]then, is natural.  As a consequence of this, there can be no


[009-045]supernatural idea in the human brain.  Whatever idea there is must


[009-046]have been a natural product.  If, then, there is no supernatural


[009-047]idea in the human brain, then there cannot be in the human brain


[009-048]an idea of the supernatural.  If we can have no idea of the


[009-049]supernatural, and if the God of whom you spoke is admitted to be


[009-050]supernatural, then, of course, I can have no idea of him, and I


[009-051]certainly can have no very fixed belief on any subject about which


[009-052]I have no idea.


[009-053]There may be a God for all I know.  There may be thousands of them.


[009-054]But the idea of an infinite Being outside and independent of nature


[009-055]is inconceivable.  I do not know of any word that would explain my


[009-056]doctrine or my views upon the subject.  I suppose Pantheism is as


[009-057]near as I could go.  I believe in the eternity of matter and in


[009-058]the eternity of intelligence, but I do not believe in any Being


[009-059]outside of nature.  I do not believe in any personal Deity.  I do


[009-060]not believe in any aristocracy of the air.  I know nothing about


[009-061]origin or destiny.  Between these two horizons I live, whether I


[009-062]wish to or not, and must be satisfied with what I find between


[009-063]these two horizons.  I have never heard any God described that I


[009-064]believe in.  I have never heard any religion explained that I


[009-065]accept.  To make something out of nothing cannot be more absurd


[009-066]than that an infinite intelligence made this world, and proceeded


[009-067]to fill it with crime and want and agony, and then, not satisfied


[009-068]with the evil he had wrought, made a hell in which to consummate


[009-069]the great mistake.


[009-070]_Question_.  Do you believe that the world, and all that is in it


[009-071]came by chance?


[009-072]_Answer_.  I do not believe anything comes by chance.  I regard


[009-073]the present as the necessary child of a necessary past.  I believe


[009-074]matter is eternal; that it has eternally existed and eternally will


[009-075]exist.  I believe that in all matter, in some way, there is what


[009-076]we call force; that one of the forms of force is intelligence.  I


[009-077]believe that whatever is in the universe has existed from eternity


[009-078]and will forever exist.


[009-079]Secondly.--I exclude from my philosophy all ideas of chance.  Matter


[009-080]changes eternally its form, never its essence.  You cannot conceive


[009-081]of anything being created.  No one can conceive of anything existing


[009-082]without a cause or with a cause.  Let me explain; a thing is not


[009-083]a cause until an effect has been produced; so that, after all,


[009-084]cause and effect are twins coming into life at precisely the same


[009-085]instant, born of the womb of an unknown mother.  The Universe in


[009-086]the only fact, and everything that ever has happened, is happening,


[009-087]or will happen, are but the different aspects of the one eternal


[009-088]fact.


[009-089]--_The Dispatch_, Pittsburg, Pa., December 11, 1880.


[009-090]THE POLITICAL OUTLOOK.


[009-091]_Question_.  What phases will the Southern question assume in the


[009-092]next four years?


[009-093]_Answer_.  The next Congress should promptly unseat every member


[009-094]of Congress in whose district there was not a fair and honest


[009-095]election.  That is the first hard work to be done.  Let notice, in


[009-096]this way, be given to the whole country, that fraud cannot succeed.


[009-097]No man should be allowed to hold a seat by force or fraud.  Just


[009-098]as soon as it is understood that fraud is useless it will be


[009-099]abandoned.  In that way the honest voters of the whole country can


[009-100]be protected.


[009-101]An honest vote settles the Southern question, and Congress has the


[009-102]power to compel an honest vote, or to leave the dishonest districts


[009-103]without representation.  I want this policy adopted, not only in


[009-104]the South, but in the North.  No man touched or stained with fraud


[009-105]should be allowed to hold his seat.  Send such men home, and let


[009-106]them stay there until sent back by honest votes.  The Southern


[009-107]question is a Northern question, and the Republican party must


[009-108]settle it for all time.  We must have honest elections, or the


[009-109]Republic must fall.  Illegal voting must be considered and punished


[009-110]as a crime.


[009-111]Taking one hundred and seventy thousand as the basis of representation,


[009-112]the South, through her astounding increase of colored population,


[009-113]gains three electoral votes, while the North and East lose three.


[009-114]Garfield was elected by the thirty thousand colored votes cast in


[009-115]New York.


[009-116]_Question_.  Will the negro continue to be the balance of power,


[009-117]and if so, will it inure to his benefit?


[009-118]_Answer_.  The more political power the colored man has the better


[009-119]he will be treated, and if he ever holds the balance of power he


[009-120]will be treated as well as the balance of our citizens.  My idea


[009-121]is that the colored man should stand on an equality with the white


[009-122]before the law; that he should honestly be protected in all his


[009-123]rights; that he should be allowed to vote, and that his vote should


[009-124]be counted.  It is a simple question of honesty.  The colored people


[009-125]are doing well; they are industrious; they are trying to get an


[009-126]education, and, on the whole, I think they are behaving fully as


[009-127]well as the whites.  They are the most forgiving people in the


[009-128]world, and about the only real Christians in our country.  They


[009-129]have suffered enough, and for one I am on their side.  I think more


[009-130]of honest black people than of dishonest whites, to say the least


[009-131]of it.


[009-132]_Question_.  Do you apprehend any trouble from the Southern leaders


[009-133]in this closing session of Congress, in attempts to force pernicious


[009-134]legislation?


[009-135]_Answer_.  I do not.  The Southern leaders know that the doctrine


[009-136]of State Sovereignty is dead.  They know that they cannot depend


[009-137]upon the Northern Democrat, and they know that the best interests


[009-138]of the South can only be preserved by admitting that the war settled


[009-139]the questions and ideas fought for and against.  They know that


[009-140]this country is a Nation, and that no party can possibly succeed


[009-141]that advocates anything contrary to that.  My own opinion is that


[009-142]most of the Southern leaders are heartily ashamed of the course


[009-143]pursued by their Northern friends, and will take the first opportunity


[009-144]to say so.


[009-145]_Question_.  In what light do you regard the Chinaman?


[009-146]_Answer_.  I am opposed to compulsory immigration, or cooley or


[009-147]slave immigration.  If Chinamen are sent to this country by


[009-148]corporations or companies under contracts that amount to slavery


[009-149]or anything like it or near it, then I am opposed to it.  But I am


[009-150]not prepared to say that I would be opposed to voluntary immigration.


[009-151]I see by the papers that a new treaty has been agreed upon that


[009-152]will probably be ratified and be satisfactory to all parties.  We


[009-153]ought to treat China with the utmost fairness.  If our treaty is


[009-154]wrong, amend it, but do so according to the recognized usage of


[009-155]nations.  After what has been said and done in this country I think


[009-156]there is very little danger of any Chinaman voluntarily coming


[009-157]here.  By this time China must have an exceedingly exalted opinion


[009-158]of our religion, and of the justice and hospitality born of our


[009-159]most holy faith.


[009-160]_Question_.  What is your opinion of making ex-Presidents Senators


[009-161]for life?


[009-162]_Answer_.  I am opposed to it.  I am against any man holding office


[009-163]for life.  And I see no more reason for making ex-Presidents


[009-164]Senators, than for making ex-Senators Presidents.  To me the idea


[009-165]is preposterous.  Why should ex-Presidents be taken care of?  In


[009-166]this country labor is not disgraceful, and after a man has been


[009-167]President he has still the right to be useful.  I am personally


[009-168]acquainted with several men who will agree, in consideration of


[009-169]being elected to the presidency, not to ask for another office


[009-170]during their natural lives.  The people of this country should


[009-171]never allow a great man to suffer.  The hand, not of charity, but


[009-172]of justice and generosity, should be forever open to those who have


[009-173]performed great public service.


[009-174]But the ex-Presidents of the future may not all be great and good


[009-175]men, and bad ex-Presidents will not make good Senators.  If the


[009-176]nation does anything, let it give a reasonable pension to ex-


[009-177]Presidents.  No man feels like giving pension, power, or place to


[009-178]General Grant simply because he was once President, but because he


[009-179]was a great soldier, and led the armies of the nation to victory.


[009-180]Make him a General, and retire him with the highest military title.


[009-181]Let him grandly wear the laurels he so nobly won, and should the


[009-182]sky at any time be darkened with a cloud of foreign war, this


[009-183]country will again hand him the sword.  Such a course honors the


[009-184]nation and the man.


[009-185]_Question_.  Are we not entering upon the era of our greatest


[009-186]prosperity?


[009-187]_Answer_.  We are just beginning to be prosperous.  The Northern


[009-188]Pacific Railroad is to be completed.  Forty millions of dollars


[009-189]have just been raised by that company, and new States will soon be


[009-190]born in the great Northwest.  The Texas Pacific will be pushed to


[009-191]San Diego, and in a few years we will ride in a Pullman car from


[009-192]Chicago to the City of Mexico.  The gold and silver mines are


[009-193]yielding more and more, and within the last ten years more than


[009-194]forty million acres of land have been changed from wilderness to


[009-195]farms.  This country is beginning to grow.  We have just fairly


[009-196]entered upon what I believe will be the grandest period of national


[009-197]development and prosperity.  With the Republican party in power;


[009-198]with good money; with unlimited credit; with the best land in the


[009-199]world; with ninety thousand miles of railway; with mountains of


[009-200]gold and silver; with hundreds of thousands of square miles of coal


[009-201]fields; with iron enough for the whole world; with the best system


[009-202]of common schools; with telegraph wires reaching every city and


[009-203]town, so that no two citizens are an hour apart; with the telephone,


[009-204]that makes everybody in the city live next door, and with the best


[009-205]folks in the world, how can we help prospering until the continent


[009-206]is covered with happy homes?
