[086-001]_Question_.  What do you think of the Congress of Religions, to be


[086-002]held in Chicago during the World's Fair?


[086-003]_Answer_.  It will do good, if they will honestly compare their


[086-004]creeds so that each one can see just how foolish all the rest are.


[086-005]They ought to compare their sacred books, and their miracles, and


[086-006]their mythologies, and if they do so they will probably see that


[086-007]ignorance is the mother of them all.  Let them have a Congress, by


[086-008]all means, and let them show how priests live on the labor of those


[086-009]they deceive.  It will do good.


[086-010]_Question_.  Do you think that Cleveland's course as to appointments


[086-011]has strengthened him with the people?


[086-012]_Answer_.  Patronage is a two-edged sword with very little handle.


[086-013]It takes an exceedingly clever President to strengthen himself by


[086-014]its exercise.  When a man is running for President the twenty men


[086-015]in every town who expect to be made postmaster are for him heart


[086-016]and soul.  Only one can get the office, and the nineteen who do


[086-017]not, feel outraged, and the lucky one is mad on account of the


[086-018]delay.  So twenty friends are lost with one place.


[086-019]_Question_.  Is the Age of Chivalry dead?


[086-020]_Answer_.  The "Age of Chivalry" never existed except in the


[086-021]imagination.  The Age of Chivalry was the age of cowardice and


[086-022]crime.


[086-023]There is more chivalry to-day than ever.  Men have a better, a


[086-024]clearer idea of justice, and pay their debts better, and treat


[086-025]their wives and children better than ever before.  The higher and


[086-026]better qualities of the soul have more to do with the average life.


[086-027]To-day men have greater admiration and respect for women, greater


[086-028]regard for the social and domestic obligations than their fathers


[086-029]had.


[086-030]_Question_.  What led you to begin lecturing on your present subject,


[086-031]and what was your first lecture?


[086-032]_Answer_.  My first lecture was entitled "Progress."  I began


[086-033]lecturing because I thought the creeds of the orthodox church false


[086-034]and horrible, and because I thought the Bible cruel and absurd,


[086-035]and because I like intellectual liberty.


[086-036]--New York, May 5, 1893.


[086-037]SUNDAY A DAY OF PLEASURE.


[086-038]_Question_.  What do you think of the religious spirit that seeks


[086-039]to regulate by legislation the manner in which the people of this


[086-040]country shall spend their Sundays?


[086-041]_Answer_.  The church is not willing to stand alone, not willing


[086-042]to base its influence on reason and on the character of its members.


[086-043]It seeks the aid of the State.  The cross is in partnership with


[086-044]the sword.  People should spend Sundays as they do other days; that


[086-045]is to say, as they please.  No one has the right to do anything on


[086-046]Monday that interferes with the rights of his neighbors, and everyone


[086-047]has the right to do anything he pleases on Sunday that does not


[086-048]interfere with the rights of his neighbors.  Sunday is a day of


[086-049]rest, not of religion.  We are under obligation to do right on all


[086-050]days.


[086-051]Nothing can be more absurd than the idea that any particular space


[086-052]of time is sacred.  Everything in nature goes on the same on Sunday


[086-053]as on other days, and if beyond nature there be a God, then God


[086-054]works on Sunday as he does on all other days.  There is no rest in


[086-055]nature.  There is perpetual activity in every possible direction.


[086-056]The old idea that God made the world and then rested, is idiotic.


[086-057]There were two reasons given to the Hebrews for keeping the Sabbath


[086-058]--one because Jehovah rested on that day, the other because the


[086-059]Hebrews were brought out of Egypt.  The first reason, we know, is


[086-060]false, and the second reason is good only for the Hebrews.  According


[086-061]to the Bible, Sunday, or rather the Sabbath, was not for the world,


[086-062]but for the Hebrews, and the Hebrews alone.  Our Sunday is pagan


[086-063]and is the day of the sun, as Monday is the day of the moon.  All


[086-064]our day names are pagan.  I am opposed to all Sunday legislation.


[086-065]_Question_.  Why should Sunday be observed otherwise than as a day


[086-066]of recreation?


[086-067]_Answer_.  Sunday is a day of recreation, or should be; a day for


[086-068]the laboring man to rest, a day to visit museums and libraries, a


[086-069]day to look at pictures, a day to get acquainted with your wife


[086-070]and children, a day for poetry and art, a day on which to read old


[086-071]letters and to meet friends, a day to cultivate the amenities of


[086-072]life, a day for those who live in tenements to feel the soft grass


[086-073]beneath their feet.  In short, Sunday should be a day of joy.  The


[086-074]church endeavors to fill it with gloom and sadness, with stupid


[086-075]sermons and dyspeptic theology.


[086-076]Nothing could be more cowardly than the effort to compel the


[086-077]observance of the Sabbath by law.  We of America have outgrown the


[086-078]childishness of the last century; we laugh at the superstitions of


[086-079]our fathers.  We have made up our minds to be as happy as we can


[086-080]be, knowing that the way to be happy is to make others so, that


[086-081]the time to be happy is now, whether that now is Sunday or any


[086-082]other day in the week.


[086-083]_Question_.  Under a Federal Constitution guaranteeing civil and


[086-084]religious liberty, are the so-called "Blue Laws" constitutional?


[086-085]_Answer_.  No, they are not.  But the probability is that the


[086-086]Supreme Courts of most of the States would decide the other way.


[086-087]And yet all these laws are clearly contrary to the spirit of the


[086-088]Federal Constitution and the constitutions of most of the States.


[086-089]I hope to live until all these foolish laws are repealed and until


[086-090]we are in the highest and noblest sense a free people.  And by free


[086-091]I mean each having the right to do anything that does not interfere


[086-092]with the rights or with the happiness of another.  I want to see


[086-093]the time when we live for this world and when all shall endeavor


[086-094]to increase, by education, by reason, and by persuasion, the sum


[086-095]of human happiness.


[086-096]--_New York Times_, July 21, 1893.


[086-097]THE PARLIAMENT OF RELIGIONS.


[086-098]_Question_.  The Parliament of Religions was called with a view to


[086-099]discussing the great religions of the world on the broad platform


[086-100]of tolerance.  Supposing this to have been accomplished, what effect


[086-101]is it likely to have on the future of creeds?


[086-102]_Answer_.  It was a good thing to get the representatives of all


[086-103]creeds to meet and tell their beliefs.  The tendency, I think, is


[086-104]to do away with prejudice, with provincialism, with egotism.  We


[086-105]know that the difference between the great religions, so far as


[086-106]belief is concerned, amounts to but little.  Their gods have


[086-107]different names, but in other respects they differ but little.


[086-108]They are all cruel and ignorant.


[086-109]_Question_.  Do you think likely that the time is coming when all


[086-110]the religions of the world will be treated with the liberality that


[086-111]is now characterizing the attitude of one sect toward another in


[086-112]Christendom?


[086-113]_Answer_.  Yes, because I think that all religions will be found


[086-114]to be of equal authority, and because I believe that the supernatural


[086-115]will be discarded and that man will give up his vain and useless


[086-116]efforts to get back of nature--to answer the questions of whence


[086-117]and whither?  As a matter of fact, the various sects do not love


[086-118]one another.  The keenest hatred is religious hatred.  The most


[086-119]malicious malice is found in the hearts of those who love their


[086-120]enemies.


[086-121]_Question_.  Bishop Newman, in replying to a learned Buddhist at


[086-122]the Parliament of Religions, said that Buddhism had given to the


[086-123]world no helpful literature, no social system, and no heroic virtues.


[086-124]Is this true?


[086-125]_Answer_.  Bishop Newman is a very prejudiced man.  Probably he


[086-126]got his information from the missionaries.  Buddha was undoubtedly


[086-127]a great teacher.  Long before Christ lived Buddha taught the


[086-128]brotherhood of man.  He said that intelligence was the only lever


[086-129]capable of raising mankind.  His followers, to say the least of


[086-130]them, are as good as the followers of Christ.  Bishop Newman is a


[086-131]Methodist--a follower of John Wesley--and he has the prejudices of


[086-132]the sect to which he belongs.  We must remember that all prejudices


[086-133]are honest.


[086-134]_Question_.  Is Christian society, or rather society in Christian


[086-135]countries, cursed with fewer robbers, assassins, and thieves,


[086-136]proportionately, then countries where "heathen" religions


[086-137]predominate?


[086-138]_Answer_.  I think not.  I do not believe that there are more


[086-139]lynchings, more mob murders in India or Turkey or Persia than in


[086-140]some Christian States of the great Republic.  Neither will you find


[086-141]more train robbers, more forgers, more thieves in heathen lands


[086-142]than in Christian countries.  Here the jails are full, the


[086-143]penitentiaries are crowded, and the hangman is busy.  All over


[086-144]Christendom, as many assert, crime is on the increase, going hand


[086-145]in hand with poverty.  The truth is, that some of the wisest and


[086-146]best men are filled with apprehension for the future, but I believe


[086-147]in the race and have confidence in man.


[086-148]_Question_.  How can society be so reconstructed that all this


[086-149]horrible suffering, resultant from poverty and its natural associate,


[086-150]crime, may be abolished, or at least reduced to a minimum?


[086-151]_Answer_.  In the first place we should stop supporting the useless.


[086-152]The burden of superstition should be taken from the shoulders of


[086-153]industry.  In the next place men should stop bowing to wealth


[086-154]instead of worth.  Men should be judged by what they do, by what


[086-155]they are, instead of by the property they have.  Only those able


[086-156]to raise and educate children should have them.  Children should


[086-157]be better born--better educated.  The process of regeneration will


[086-158]be slow, but it will be sure.  The religion of our day is supported


[086-159]by the worst, by the most dangerous people in society.  I do not


[086-160]allude to murderers or burglars, or even to the little thieves.


[086-161]I mean those who debauch courts and legislatures and elections--


[086-162]those who make millions by legal fraud.


[086-163]_Question_.  What do you think of the Theosophists?  Are they


[086-164]sincere--have they any real basis for their psychological theories?


[086-165]_Answer_.  The Theosophists may be sincere.  I do not know.  But


[086-166]I am perfectly satisfied that their theories are without any


[086-167]foundation in fact--that their doctrines are as unreal as their


[086-168]"astral bodies," and as absurd as a contradiction in mathematics.


[086-169]We have had vagaries and theories enough.  We need the religion of


[086-170]the real, the faith that rests on fact.  Let us turn our attention


[086-171]to this world--the world in which we live.


[086-172]--_New York Herald_, September, 1893.


[086-173]CLEVELAND'S HAWAIIAN POLICY.


[086-174]_Question_.  Colonel, what do you think about Mr. Cleveland's


[086-175]Hawaiian policy?


[086-176]_Answer_.  I think it exceedingly laughable and a little dishonest


[086-177]--with the further fault that it is wholly unconstitutional.  This


[086-178]is not a one-man Government, and while Liliuokalani may be Queen,


[086-179]Cleveland is certainly not a king.  The worst thing about the whole


[086-180]matter, as it appears to me, is the bad faith that was shown by


[086-181]Mr. Cleveland--the double-dealing.  He sent Mr. Willis as Minister


[086-182]to the Provisional Government and by that act admitted the existence,


[086-183]and the rightful existence, of the Provisional Government of the


[086-184]Sandwich Islands.


[086-185]When Mr. Willis started he gave him two letters.  One was addressed


[086-186]to Dole, President of the Provisional Government, in which he


[086-187]addressed Dole as "Great and good friend," and at the close, being


[086-188]a devout Christian, he asked "God to take care of Dole."  This was


[086-189]the first letter.  The letter of one President to another; of one


[086-190]friend to another.  The second letter was addressed to Mr. Willis,


[086-191]in which Mr. Willis was told to upset Dole at the first opportunity


[086-192]and put the deposed Queen back on her throne.  This may be diplomacy,


[086-193]but it is no kin to honesty.


[086-194]In my judgment, it is the worst thing connected with the Hawaiian


[086-195]affair.  What must "the great and good" Dole think of our great


[086-196]and good President?  What must other nations think when they read


[086-197]the two letters and mentally exclaim, "Look upon this and then upon


[086-198]that?"  I think Mr. Cleveland has acted arrogantly, foolishly, and


[086-199]unfairly.  I am in favor of obtaining the Sandwich Islands--of


[086-200]course by fair means.  I favor this policy because I want my country


[086-201]to become a power in the Pacific.  All my life I have wanted this


[086-202]country to own the West Indies, the Bermudas, the Bahamas and


[086-203]Barbadoes.  They are our islands.  They belong to this continent,


[086-204]and for any other nation to take them or claim them was, and is,


[086-205]a piece of impertinence and impudence.
