[041-001]_Question_.  Do you think that the orthodox church gets its ideas


[041-002]of the Sabbath from the teachings of Christ?


[041-003]_Answer_.  I do not hold Christ responsible for these idiotic ideas


[041-004]concerning the Sabbath.  He regarded the Sabbath as something made


[041-005]for man--which was a very sensible view.  The holiest day is the


[041-006]happiest day.  The most sacred day is the one in which have been


[041-007]done the most good deeds.  There are two reasons given in the Bible


[041-008]for keeping the Sabbath.  One is that God made the world in six


[041-009]days, and rested on the seventh.  Now that all the ministers admit


[041-010]that he did not make the world in six days, but that he made it in


[041-011]six "periods," this reason is no longer applicable.  The other


[041-012]reason is that he brought the Jews out of Egypt with a "mighty


[041-013]hand."  This may be a very good reason still for the observance of


[041-014]the Sabbath by the Jews, but the real Sabbath, that is to say, the


[041-015]day to be commemorated, is our Saturday, and why should we commemorate


[041-016]the wrong day?  That disposes of the second reason.


[041-017]Nothing can be more inconsistent than the theories and practice of


[041-018]the churches about the Sabbath.  The cars run Sundays, and out of


[041-019]the profits hundreds of ministers are supported.  The great iron


[041-020]and steel works fill with smoke and fire the Sabbath air, and the


[041-021]proprietors divide the profits with the churches.  The printers of


[041-022]the city are busy Sunday afternoons and evenings, and the presses


[041-023]during the nights, so that the sermons of Sunday can reach the


[041-024]heathen on Monday.  The servants of the rich are denied the privileges


[041-025]of the sanctuary.  The coachman sits on the box out-doors, while


[041-026]his employer kneels in church preparing himself for the heavenly


[041-027]chariot.  The iceman goes about on the holy day, keeping believers


[041-028]cool, they knowing at the same time that he is making it hot for


[041-029]himself in the world to come.  Christians cross the Atlantic,


[041-030]knowing that the ship will pursue its way on the Sabbath.  They


[041-031]write letters to their friends knowing that they will be carried


[041-032]in violation of Jehovah's law, by wicked men.  Yet they hate to


[041-033]see a pale-faced sewing girl enjoying a few hours by the sea; a


[041-034]poor mechanic walking in the fields; or a tired mother watching


[041-035]her children playing on the grass.  Nothing ever was, nothing ever


[041-036]will be, more utterly absurd and disgusting than a Puritan Sunday.


[041-037]Nothing ever did make a home more hateful than the strict observance


[041-038]of the Sabbath.  It fills the house with hypocrisy and the meanest


[041-039]kind of petty tyranny.  The parents look sour and stern, the children


[041-040]sad and sulky.  They are compelled to talk upon subjects about


[041-041]which they feel no interest, or to read books that are thought good


[041-042]only because they are so stupid.


[041-043]_Question_.  What have you to say about the growth of Catholicism,


[041-044]the activity of the Salvation Army, and the success of revivalists


[041-045]like the Rev. Samuel Jones?  Is Christianity really gaining a strong


[041-046]hold on the masses?


[041-047]_Answer_.  Catholicism is growing in this country, and it is the


[041-048]only country on earth in which it is growing.  Its growth here


[041-049]depends entirely upon immigration, not upon intellectual conquest.


[041-050]Catholic emigrants who leave their homes in the Old World because


[041-051]they have never had any liberty, and who are Catholics for the same


[041-052]reason, add to the number of Catholics here, but their children's


[041-053]children will not be Catholics.  Their children will not be very


[041-054]good Catholics, and even these immigrants themselves, in a few


[041-055]years, will not grovel quite so low in the presence of a priest.


[041-056]The Catholic Church is gaining no ground in Catholic countries.


[041-057]The Salvation Army is the result of two things--the general belief


[041-058]in what are known as the fundamentals of Christianity, and the


[041-059]heartlessness of the church.  The church in England--that is to


[041-060]say, the Church of England--having succeeded--that is to say, being


[041-061]supported by general taxation--that is to say, being a successful,


[041-062]well-fed parasite--naturally neglected those who did not in any


[041-063]way contribute to its support.  It became aristocratic.  Splendid


[041-064]churches were built; younger sons with good voices were put in the


[041-065]pulpits; the pulpit became the asylum for aristocratic mediocrity,


[041-066]and in this way the Church of England lost interest in the masses


[041-067]and the masses lost interest in the Church of England.  The neglected


[041-068]poor, who really had some belief in religion, and who had not been


[041-069]absolutely petrified by form and patronage, were ready for the


[041-070]Salvation Army.  They were not at home in the church.  They could


[041-071]not pay.  They preferred the freedom of the street.  They preferred


[041-072]to attend a church where rags were no objection.  Had the church


[041-073]loved and labored with the poor the Salvation Army never would have


[041-074]existed.  These people are simply giving their idea of Christianity,


[041-075]and in their way endeavoring to do what they consider good.  I


[041-076]don't suppose the Salvation Army will accomplish much.  To improve


[041-077]mankind you must change conditions.  It is not enough to work simply


[041-078]upon the emotional nature.  The surroundings must be such as


[041-079]naturally produce virtuous actions.  If we are to believe recent


[041-080]reports from London, the Church of England, even with the assistance


[041-081]of the Salvation Army, has accomplished but little.  It would be


[041-082]hard to find any country with less morality.  You would search long


[041-083]in the jungles of Africa to find greater depravity.


[041-084]I account for revivalists like the Rev. Samuel Jones in the same


[041-085]way.  There is in every community an ignorant class--what you might


[041-086]call a literal class--who believe in the real blood atonement; who


[041-087]believe in heaven and hell, and harps and gridirons; who have never


[041-088]had their faith weakened by reading commentators or books harmonizing


[041-089]science and religion.  They love to hear the good old doctrine;


[041-090]they want hell described; they want it described so that they can


[041-091]hear the moans and shrieks; they want heaven described; they want


[041-092]to see God on a throne, and they want to feel that they are finally


[041-093]to have the pleasure of looking over the battlements of heaven and


[041-094]seeing all their enemies among the damned.  The Rev. Mr. Munger


[041-095]has suddenly become a revivalist.  According to the papers he is


[041-096]sought for in every direction.  His popularity seems to rest upon


[041-097]the fact that he brutally beat a girl twelve years old because she


[041-098]did not say her prayers to suit him.  Muscular Christianity is what


[041-099]the ignorant people want.  I regard all these efforts--including


[041-100]those made by Mr. Moody and Mr. Hammond--as evidence that Christianity,


[041-101]as an intellectual factor, has almost spent its force.  It no longer


[041-102]governs the intellectual world.


[041-103]_Question_.  Are not the Catholics the least progressive?  And are


[041-104]they not, in spite of their professions to the contrary, enemies


[041-105]to republican liberty?


[041-106]_Answer_.  Every church that has a standard higher than human


[041-107]welfare is dangerous.  A church that puts a book above the laws


[041-108]and constitution of its country, that puts a book above the welfare


[041-109]of mankind, is dangerous to human liberty.  Every church that puts


[041-110]itself above the legally expressed will of the people is dangerous.


[041-111]Every church that holds itself under greater obligation to a pope


[041-112]than to a people is dangerous to human liberty.  Every church that


[041-113]puts religion above humanity--above the well-being of man in this


[041-114]world--is dangerous.  The Catholic Church may be more dangerous,


[041-115]not because its doctrines are more dangerous, but because, on the


[041-116]average, its members more sincerely believe its doctrines, and


[041-117]because that church can be hurled as a solid body in any given


[041-118]direction.  For these reasons it is more dangerous than other


[041-119]churches; but the doctrines are no more dangerous than those of


[041-120]the Protestant churches.  The man who would sacrifice the well-


[041-121]being of man to please an imaginary phantom that he calls God, is


[041-122]also dangerous.  The only safe standard is the well-being of man


[041-123]in this world.  Whenever this world is sacrificed for the sake of


[041-124]another, a mistake has been made.  The only God that man can know


[041-125]is the aggregate of all beings capable of suffering and of joy


[041-126]within the reach of his influence.  To increase the happiness of


[041-127]such beings is to worship the only God that man can know.


[041-128]_Question_.  What have you to say to the assertion of Dr. Deems


[041-129]that there were never so many Christians as now?


[041-130]_Answer_.  I suppose that the population of the earth is greater


[041-131]now than at any other time within the historic period.  This being


[041-132]so, there may be more Christians, so-called, in this world than


[041-133]there were a hundred years ago.  Of course, the reverend doctor,


[041-134]in making up his aggregate of Christians, counts all kinds and


[041-135]sects--Unitarians, Universalists, and all the other "ans" and "ists"


[041-136]and "ics" and "ites" and "ers."  But Dr. Deems must admit that only


[041-137]a few years ago most of the persons he now calls Christians would


[041-138]have been burnt as heretics and Infidels.  Let us compare the


[041-139]average New York Christian with the Christian of two hundred years


[041-140]ago.  It is probably safe to say that there is not now in the city


[041-141]of New York a genuine Presbyterian outside of an insane asylum.


[041-142]Probably no one could be found who will to-day admit that he believes


[041-143]absolutely in the Presbyterian Confession of Faith.  There is


[041-144]probably not an Episcopalian who believes in the Thirty-nine


[041-145]Articles.  Probably there is not an intelligent minister in the


[041-146]city of New York, outside of the Catholic Church, who believes that


[041-147]everything in the Bible is true.  Probably no clergyman, of any


[041-148]standing, would be willing to take the ground that everything in


[041-149]the Old Testament--leaving out the question of inspiration--is


[041-150]actually true.  Very few ministers now preach the doctrine of


[041-151]eternal punishment.  Most of them would be ashamed to utter that


[041-152]brutal falsehood.  A large majority of gentlemen who attend church


[041-153]take the liberty of disagreeing with the preacher.  They would have


[041-154]been very poor Christians two hundred years ago.  A majority of


[041-155]the ministers take the liberty of disagreeing, in many things, with


[041-156]their Presbyteries and Synods.  They would have been very poor


[041-157]preachers two hundred years ago.  Dr. Deems forgets that most


[041-158]Christians are only nominally so.  Very few believe their creeds.


[041-159]Very few even try to live in accordance with what they call Christian


[041-160]doctrines.  Nobody loves his enemies.  No Christian when smitten


[041-161]on one cheek turns the other.  Most Christians do take a little


[041-162]thought for the morrow.  They do not depend entirely upon the


[041-163]providence of God.  Most Christians now have greater confidence in


[041-164]the average life-insurance company than in God--feel easier when


[041-165]dying to know that they have a policy, through which they expect


[041-166]the widow will receive ten thousand dollars, than when thinking of


[041-167]all the Scripture promises.  Even church-members do not trust in


[041-168]God to protect their own property.  They insult heaven by putting


[041-169]lightning rods on their temples.  They insure the churches against


[041-170]the act of God.  The experience of man has shown the wisdom of


[041-171]relying on something that we know something about, instead of upon


[041-172]the shadowy supernatural.  The poor wretches to-day in Spain,


[041-173]depending upon their priests, die like poisoned flies; die with


[041-174]prayers between their pallid lips; die in their filth and faith.


[041-175]_Question_.  What have you to say on the Mormon question?


[041-176]_Answer_.  The institution of polygamy is infamous and disgusting


[041-177]beyond expression.  It destroys what we call, and all civilized


[041-178]people call, "the family."  It pollutes the fireside, and, above


[041-179]all, as Burns would say, "petrifies the feeling."  It is, however,


[041-180]one of the institutions of Jehovah.  It is protected by the Bible.


[041-181]It has inspiration on its side.  Sinai, with its barren, granite


[041-182]peaks, is a perpetual witness in its favor.  The beloved of God


[041-183]practiced it, and, according to the sacred word, the wisest man


[041-184]had, I believe, about seven hundred wives.  This man received his


[041-185]wisdom directly from God.  It is hard for the average Bible worshiper


[041-186]to attack this institution without casting a certain stain upon


[041-187]his own book.
