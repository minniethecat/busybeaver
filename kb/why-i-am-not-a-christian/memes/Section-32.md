[031-001]_Question_.  What do you think generally of the revival of the


[031-002]bloody shirt?  Do you think the investigations of the Republicans


[031-003]of the Danville and Copiah massacres will benefit them?


[031-004]_Answer_.  Well, I am in favor of the revival of that question just


[031-005]as often as a citizen of the Republic is murdered on account of


[031-006]his politics.  If the South is sick of that question, let it stop


[031-007]persecuting men because they are Republicans.  I do not believe,


[031-008]however, in simply investigating the question and then stopping


[031-009]after the guilty ones are found.  I believe in indicting them,


[031-010]trying them, and convicting them.  If the Government can do nothing


[031-011]except investigate, we might as well stop, and admit that we have


[031-012]no government.  Thousands of people think that it is almost vulgar


[031-013]to take the part of the poor colored people in the South.  What


[031-014]part should you take if not that of the weak?  The strong do not


[031-015]need you.  And I can tell the Southern people now, that as long as


[031-016]they persecute for opinion's sake they will never touch the reins


[031-017]of political power in this country.


[031-018]_Question_.  How do you regard the action of Bismarck in returning


[031-019]the Lasker resolutions?  Was it the result of his hatred of the


[031-020]Jews?


[031-021]_Answer_.  Bismarck opposed a bill to do away with the disabilities


[031-022]of the Jews on the ground that Prussia is a Christian nation,


[031-023]founded for the purpose of spreading the gospel of Jesus Christ.


[031-024]I presume that it was his hatred of the Jews that caused him to


[031-025]return the resolutions.  Bismarck should have lived several centuries


[031-026]ago.  He belongs to the Dark Ages.  He is a believer in the sword


[031-027]and the bayonet--in brute force.  He was loved by Germany simply


[031-028]because he humiliated France.  Germany gave her liberty for revenge.


[031-029]It is only necessary to compare Bismarck with Gambetta to see what


[031-030]a failure he really is.  Germany was victorious and took from France


[031-031]the earnings of centuries; and yet Germany is to-day the least


[031-032]prosperous nation in Europe.  France was prostrate, trampled into


[031-033]the earth, robbed, and yet, guided by Gambetta, is to-day the most


[031-034]prosperous nation in Europe.  This shows the difference between


[031-035]brute force and brain.


[031-036]--_The Times_, Chicago, Illinois, February 21, 1884.


[031-037]GENERAL SUBJECTS.


[031-038]_Question_.  Do you enjoy lecturing?


[031-039]_Answer_.  Of course I enjoy lecturing.  It is a great pleasure to


[031-040]drive the fiend of fear out of the hearts of men women and children.


[031-041]It is a positive joy to put out the fires of hell.


[031-042]_Question_.  Where do you meet with the bitterest opposition?


[031-043]_Answer_.  I meet with the bitterest opposition where the people


[031-044]are the most ignorant, where there is the least thought, where


[031-045]there are the fewest books.  The old theology is becoming laughable.


[031-046]Very few ministers have the impudence to preach in the old way.


[031-047]They give new meanings to old words.  They subscribe to the same


[031-048]creed, but preach exactly the other way.  The clergy are ashamed


[031-049]to admit that they are orthodox, and they ought to be.


[031-050]_Question_.  Do liberal books, such as the works of Paine and


[031-051]Infidel scientists sell well?


[031-052]_Answer_.  Yes, they are about the only books on serious subjects


[031-053]that do sell well.  The works of Darwin, Buckle, Draper, Haeckel,


[031-054]Tyndall, Humboldt and hundreds of others, are read by intelligent


[031-055]people the world over.  Works of a religious character die on the


[031-056]shelves.  The people want facts.  They want to know about the world,


[031-057]about all forms of life.  They want the mysteries of every day


[031-058]solved.  They want honest thoughts about sensible questions.  They


[031-059]are tired of the follies of faith and the falsehoods of superstition.


[031-060]They want a heaven here.  In a few years the old theological books


[031-061]will be sold to make paper on which to print the discoveries of


[031-062]science.


[031-063]_Question_.  In what section of the country do you find the most


[031-064]liberality?


[031-065]_Answer_.  I find great freedom of thought in Boston, New York,


[031-066]Chicago, San Francisco, in fact, all over what we call the North.


[031-067]The West of course is liberal.  The truth is that all the intelligent


[031-068]part of the country is liberal.  The railroad, the telegraph, the


[031-069]daily paper, electric light, the telephone, and freedom of thought


[031-070]belong together.


[031-071]_Question_.  Is it true that you were once threatened with a criminal


[031-072]prosecution for libel on religion?


[031-073]_Answer_.  Yes, in Delaware.  Chief Justice Comegys instructed the


[031-074]grand jury to indict me for blasphemy.  I have taken by revenge on


[031-075]the State by leaving it in ignorance.  Delaware is several centuries


[031-076]behind the times.  It is as bigoted as it is small.  Compare Kansas


[031-077]City with Wilmington and you will see the difference between


[031-078]liberalism and orthodoxy.


[031-079]_Question_.  This is Washington's birthday.  What do you think of


[031-080]General Washington?


[031-081]_Answer_.  I suppose that Washington was what was called religious.


[031-082]He was not very strict in his conduct.  He tried to have church


[031-083]and state united in Virginia and was defeated by Jefferson.  It


[031-084]should make no difference with us whether Washington was religious


[031-085]or not.  Jefferson was by far the greater man.  In intellect there


[031-086]was no comparison between Washington and Franklin.  I do not prove


[031-087]the correctness of my ideas by names of dead people.  I depend upon


[031-088]reason instead of gravestones.  One fact is worth a cemetery full


[031-089]of distinguished corpses.  We ask not for the belief of somebody,


[031-090]but for evidence, for facts.  The church is a beggar at the door


[031-091]of respectability.  The moment a man becomes famous, the church


[031-092]asks him for a certificate that the Bible is true.  It passes its


[031-093]hat before generals and presidents, and kings while they are alive.


[031-094]It says nothing about thinkers and real philosophers while they


[031-095]live, except to slander them, but the moment they are dead it seeks


[031-096]among their words for a crumb of comfort.


[031-097]_Question_.  Will Liberalism ever organize in America?


[031-098]_Answer_.  I hope not.  Organization means creed, and creed means


[031-099]petrifaction and tyranny.  I believe in individuality.  I will not


[031-100]join any society except an anti-society society.


[031-101]_Question_.  Do you consider the religion of Bhagavat Purana of


[031-102]the East as good as the Christian?


[031-103]_Answer_.  It is far more poetic.  It has greater variety and shows


[031-104]vastly more thought.  Like the Hebrew, it is poisoned with


[031-105]superstition, but it has more beauty.  Nothing can be more barren


[031-106]than the theology of the Jews and Christians.  One lonely God, a


[031-107]heaven filled with thoughtless angels, a hell with unfortunate


[031-108]souls.  Nothing can be more desolate.  The Greek mythology is


[031-109]infinitely better.


[031-110]_Question_.  Do you think that the marriage institution is held in


[031-111]less respect by Infidels than by Christians?


[031-112]_Answer_.  No; there was never a time when marriage was more believed


[031-113]in than now.  Never were wives treated better and loved more; never


[031-114]were children happier than now.  It is the ambition of the average


[031-115]American to have a good and happy home.  The fireside was never


[031-116]more popular than now.


[031-117]_Question_.  What do you think of Beecher?


[031-118]_Answer_.  He is a great man, but the habit of his mind and the


[031-119]bent of his early education oppose his heart.  He is growing and


[031-120]has been growing every day for many years.  He has given up the


[031-121]idea of eternal punishment, and that of necessity destroys it all.


[031-122]The Christian religion is founded upon hell.  When the foundation


[031-123]crumbles the fabric falls.  Beecher was to have answered my article


[031-124]in the _North American Review_, but when it appeared and he saw


[031-125]it, he agreed with so much of it that he concluded that an answer


[031-126]would be useless.


[031-127]--_The Times_, Kansas City, Missouri, February 23, 1884.


[031-128]REPLY TO KANSAS CITY CLERGY.


[031-129]_Question_.  Will you take any notice of Mr. Magrath's challenge?


[031-130]_Answer_.  I do not think it worth while to discuss with Mr. Magrath.


[031-131]I do not say this in disparagement of his ability, as I do not know


[031-132]the gentleman.  He may be one of the greatest of men.  I think,


[031-133]however, that Mr. Magrath might better answer what I have already


[031-134]said.  If he succeeds in that, then I will meet him in public


[031-135]discussion.  Of course he is an eminent theologian or he would not


[031-136]think of discussing these questions with anybody.  I have never


[031-137]heard of him, but for all that he may be the most intelligent of


[031-138]men.


[031-139]_Question_.  How have the recently expressed opinions of our local


[031-140]clergy impressed you?


[031-141]_Answer_.  I suppose you refer to the preachers who have given


[031-142]their opinion of me.  In the first place I am obliged to them for


[031-143]acting as my agents.  I think Mr. Hogan has been imposed upon.


[031-144]Tacitus is a poor witness--about like Josephus.  I say again that


[031-145]we have not a word about Christ written by any human being who


[031-146]lived in the time of Christ--not a solitary word, and Mr. Hogan


[031-147]ought to know it.


[031-148]The Rev. Mr. Matthews is mistaken.  If the Bible proves anything,


[031-149]it proves that the world was made in six days and that Adam and


[031-150]Eve were built on Saturday.  The Bible gives the age of Adam when


[031-151]he died, and then gives the ages of others down to the flood, and


[031-152]then from that time at least to the return from the captivity.  If


[031-153]the genealogy of the Bible is true it is about six thousand years


[031-154]since Adam was made, and the world is only five days older than


[031-155]Adam.  It is nonsense to say that the days were long periods of


[031-156]time.  If that is so, away goes the idea of Sunday.  The only reason


[031-157]for keeping Sunday given in the Bible is that God made the world


[031-158]in six days and rested on the seventh.  Mr. Mathews is not candid.


[031-159]He knows that he cannot answer the arguments I have urged against


[031-160]the Bible.  He knows that the ancient Jews were barbarians, and


[031-161]that the Old Testament is a barbarous book.  He knows that it


[031-162]upholds slavery and polygamy, and he probably feels ashamed of what


[031-163]he is compelled to preach.


[031-164]Mr. Jardine takes a very cheerful view of the subject.  He expects


[031-165]the light to dawn on the unbelievers.  He speaks as though he were


[031-166]the superior of all Infidels.  He claims to be a student of the


[031-167]evidences of Christianity.  There are no evidences, consequently


[031-168]Mr. Jardine is a student of nothing.  It is amazing how dignified


[031-169]some people can get on a small capital.


[031-170]Mr. Haley has sense enough to tell the ministers not to attempt to


[031-171]answer me.  That is good advice.  The ministers had better keep


[031-172]still.  It is the safer way.  If they try to answer what I say,


[031-173]the "sheep" will see how foolish the "shepherds" are.  The best


[031-174]way is for them to say, "that has been answered."


[031-175]Mr. Wells agrees with Mr. Haley.  He, too, thinks that silence is


[031-176]the best weapon.  I agree with him.  Let the clergy keep still;


[031-177]that is the best way.  It is better to say nothing than to talk


[031-178]absurdity.  I am delighted to think that at last the ministers have


[031-179]concluded that they had better not answer Infidels.


[031-180]Mr. Woods is fearful only for the young.  He is afraid that I will


[031-181]hurt the children.  He thinks that the mother ought to stoop over


[031-182]the cradle and in the ears of the babe shout, Hell!  So he thinks


[031-183]in all probability that the same word ought to be repeated at the


[031-184]grave as a consolation to mourners.


[031-185]I am glad that Mr. Mann thinks that I am doing neither good nor


[031-186]harm.  This gives me great hope.  If I do no harm, certainly I


[031-187]ought not to be eternally damned.  It is very consoling to have an


[031-188]orthodox minister solemnly assert that I am doing no harm.  I wish


[031-189]I could say as much for him.


[031-190]The truth is, all these ministers have kept back their real thoughts.


[031-191]They do not tell their doubts--they know that orthodoxy is doomed


[031-192]--they know that the old doctrine excites laughter and scorn.  They


[031-193]know that the fires of hell are dying out; that the Bible is ceasing


[031-194]to be an authority; and that the pulpit is growing feebler and


[031-195]feebler every day.  Poor parsons!
