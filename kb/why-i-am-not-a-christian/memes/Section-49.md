[048-001]--_The Mail and Express_, New York, November 3, 1887.


[048-002]THE STAGE AND THE PULPIT.


[048-003]_Question_.  What do you think of the Methodist minister at Nashville,


[048-004]Tenn., who, from his pulpit, denounced the theatrical profession,


[048-005]without exception, as vicious, and of the congregation which passed


[048-006]resolutions condemning Miss Emma Abbott for rising in church and


[048-007]contradicting him, and of the Methodist bishop who likened her to


[048-008]a "painted courtesan," and invoked the aid of the law "for the


[048-009]protection of public worship" against "strolling players"?


[048-010]_Answer_.  The Methodist minister of whom you speak, without doubt


[048-011]uttered his real sentiments.  The church has always regarded the


[048-012]stage as a rival, and all its utterances have been as malicious as


[048-013]untrue.  It has always felt that the money given to the stage was


[048-014]in some way taken from the pulpit.  It is on this principle that


[048-015]the pulpit wishes everything, except the church, shut up on Sunday.


[048-016]It knows that it cannot stand free and open competition.


[048-017]All well-educated ministers know that the Bible suffers by a


[048-018]comparison with Shakespeare.  They know that there is nothing within


[048-019]the lids of what they call "the sacred book" that can for one moment


[048-020]stand side by side with "Lear" or "Hamlet" or "Julius Caesar" or


[048-021]"Antony and Cleopatra" or with any other play written by the immortal


[048-022]man.  They know what a poor figure the Davids and the Abrahams and


[048-023]the Jeremiahs and the Lots, the Jonahs, the Jobs and the Noahs cut


[048-024]when on the stage with the great characters of Shakespeare.  For


[048-025]these reasons, among others, the pulpit is malicious and hateful


[048-026]when it thinks of the glories of the stage.  What minister is there


[048-027]now living who could command the prices commanded by Edwin Booth


[048-028]or Joseph Jefferson; and what two clergymen, by making a combination,


[048-029]could contend successfully with Robson and Crane?  How many clergymen


[048-030]would it take to command, at regular prices, the audiences that


[048-031]attend the presentation of Wagner's operas?


[048-032]It is very easy to see why the pulpit attacks the stage.  Nothing


[048-033]could have been in more wretched taste than for the minister to


[048-034]condemn Miss Emma Abbott for rising in church and defending not


[048-035]only herself, but other good women who are doing honest work for


[048-036]an honest living.  Of course, no minister wishes to be answered;


[048-037]no minister wishes to have anyone in the congregation call for the


[048-038]proof.  A few questions would break up all the theology in the


[048-039]world.  Ministers can succeed only when congregations keep silent.


[048-040]When superstition succeeds, doubt must be dumb.


[048-041]The Methodist bishop who attacked Miss Abbott simply repeated the


[048-042]language of several centuries ago.  In the laws of England actors


[048-043]were described as "sturdy vagrants," and this bishop calls them


[048-044]"strolling players."  If we only had some strolling preachers like


[048-045]Garrick, like Edwin Forrest, or Booth or Barrett, or some crusade


[048-046]sisters like Mrs. Siddons, Madam Ristori, Charlotte Cushman, or


[048-047]Madam Modjeska, how fortunate the church would be!


[048-048]_Question_.  What is your opinion of the relative merits of the


[048-049]pulpit and the stage, preachers and actors?


[048-050]_Answer_.  We must remember that the stage presents an ideal life.


[048-051]It is a world controlled by the imagination--a world in which the


[048-052]justice delayed in real life may be done, and in which that may


[048-053]happen which, according to the highest ideal, should happen.  It


[048-054]is a world, for the most part, in which evil does not succeed, in


[048-055]which the vicious are foiled, in which the right, the honest, the


[048-056]sincere, and the good prevail.  It cultivates the imagination, and


[048-057]in this respect is far better than the pulpit.  The mission of the


[048-058]pulpit is to narrow and shrivel the human mind.  The pulpit denounces


[048-059]the freedom of thought and of expression; but on the stage the mind


[048-060]is free, and for thousands of years the poor, the oppressed, the


[048-061]enslaved, have been permitted to witness plays wherein the slave


[048-062]was freed, wherein the oppressed became the victor, and where the


[048-063]downtrodden rose supreme.


[048-064]And there is another thing.  The stage has always laughed at the


[048-065]spirit of caste.  The low-born lass has loved the prince.  All


[048-066]human distinctions in this ideal world have for the moment vanished,


[048-067]while honesty and love have triumphed.  The stage lightens the


[048-068]cares of life.  The pulpit increases the tears and groans of man.


[048-069]There is this difference:  The pretence of honesty and the honesty


[048-070]of pretence.


[048-071]_Question_.  How do you view the Episcopalian scheme of building


[048-072]a six-million-dollar untaxed cathedral in this city for the purpose


[048-073]of "uniting the sects," and, when that is accomplished, "unifying


[048-074]the world in the love of Christ," and thereby abolishing misery?


[048-075]_Answer_.  I regard the building of an Episcopal cathedral simply


[048-076]as a piece of religious folly.  The world will never be converted


[048-077]by Christian palaces and temples.  Every dollar used in its


[048-078]construction will be wasted.  It will have no tendency to unite


[048-079]the various sects; on the contrary, it will excite the envy and


[048-080]jealousy of every other sect.  It will widen the gulf between the


[048-081]Episcopalian and the Methodist, between the Episcopalian and the


[048-082]Presbyterian, and this hatred will continue until the other sects


[048-083]build a cathedral just a little larger, and then the envy and the


[048-084]hatred will be on the other side.


[048-085]Religion will never unify the world, and never will give peace to


[048-086]mankind.  There has been more war in the last eighteen hundred


[048-087]years than during any similar period within historic times.  War


[048-088]will be abolished, if it ever is abolished, not by religion, but


[048-089]by intelligence.  It will be abolished when the poor people of


[048-090]Germany, of France, of Spain, of England, and other countries find


[048-091]that they have no interest in war.  When those who pay, and those


[048-092]who do the fighting, find that they are simply destroying their


[048-093]own interests, wars will cease.


[048-094]There ought to be a national court to decide national difficulties.


[048-095]We consider a community civilized when the individuals of that


[048-096]community submit their differences to a legal tribunal; but there


[048-097]being no national court, nations now sustain, as to each other,


[048-098]the relation of savages--that is to say, each one must defend its


[048-099]rights by brute force.  The establishment of a national court


[048-100]civilizes nations, and tends to do away with war.


[048-101]Christianity caused so much war, so much bloodshed, that Christians


[048-102]were forced to interpolate a passage to account for their history,


[048-103]and the interpolated passage is, "I came not to bring peace, but


[048-104]a sword."  Suppose that all the money wasted in cathedrals in the


[048-105]Middle Ages had been used for the construction of schoolhouses,


[048-106]academies, and universities, how much better the world would have


[048-107]been!  Suppose that instead of supporting hundreds of thousands of


[048-108]idle priests, the money had been given to men of science, for the


[048-109]purpose of finding out something of benefit to the human race here


[048-110]in this world.


[048-111]_Question_.  What is your opinion of "Christian charity" and the


[048-112]"fatherhood of God" as an economic polity for abolishing poverty


[048-113]and misery?


[048-114]_Answer_.  Of course, the world is not to be civilized and clothed


[048-115]and fed through charity.  Ordinary charity creates more want than


[048-116]it alleviates.  The greatest possible charity is the greatest


[048-117]possible justice.  When proper wages are paid, when every one is


[048-118]as willing to give what a thing is worth as he is now willing to


[048-119]get it for less, the world will be fed and clothed.


[048-120]I believe in helping people to help themselves.  I believe that


[048-121]corporations, and successful men, and superior men intellectually,


[048-122]should do all within their power to keep from robbing their fellow-


[048-123]men.  The superior man should protect the inferior.  The powerful


[048-124]should be the shield of the weak.  To-day it is, for the most part,


[048-125]exactly the other way.  The failures among men become the food of


[048-126]success.


[048-127]The world is to grow better and better through intelligence, through


[048-128]a development of the brain, through taking advantage of the forces


[048-129]of nature, through science, through chemistry, and through the


[048-130]arts.  Religion can do nothing except to sow the seeds of discord


[048-131]between men and nations.  Commerce, manufactures, and the arts tend


[048-132]to peace and the well-being of the world.  What is known as religion


[048-133]--that is to say, a system by which this world is wasted in


[048-134]preparation for another--a system in which the duties of men are


[048-135]greater to God than to his fellow-men--a system that denies the


[048-136]liberty of thought and expression--tends only to discord and


[048-137]retrogression.  Of course, I know that religious people cling to


[048-138]the Bible on account of the good that is in it, and in spite of


[048-139]the bad, and I know that Freethinkers throw away the Bible on


[048-140]account of the bad that is in it, in spite of the good.  I hope


[048-141]the time will come when that book will be treated like other books,


[048-142]and will be judged upon its merits, apart from the fiction of


[048-143]inspiration.  The church has no right to speak of charity, because


[048-144]it is an object of charity itself.  It gives nothing; all it can


[048-145]do is to receive.  At best, it is only a respectable beggar.  I


[048-146]never care to hear one who receives alms pay a tribute to charity.


[048-147]The one who gives alms should pay this tribute.  The amount of


[048-148]money expended upon churches and priests and all the paraphernalia


[048-149]of superstition, is more than enough to drive the wolves from the


[048-150]doors of the world.


[048-151]_Question_.  Have you noticed the progress Catholics are making in


[048-152]the Northwest, discontinuing public schools, and forcing people to


[048-153]send their children to the parochial schools; also, at Pittsburg,


[048-154]Pa., a Roman Catholic priest has been elected principal of a public


[048-155]school, and he has appointed nuns as assistant teachers?


[048-156]_Answer_.  Sectarian schools ought not to be supported by public


[048-157]taxation.  It is the very essence of religious tyranny to compel


[048-158]a Methodist to support a Catholic school, or to compel a Catholic


[048-159]to support a Baptist academy.  Nothing should be taught in the


[048-160]public schools that the teachers do not know.  Nothing should be


[048-161]taught about any religion, and nothing should be taught that can,


[048-162]in any way, be called sectarian.  The sciences are not religion.


[048-163]There is no such thing as Methodist mathematics, or Baptist botany.


[048-164]In other words, no religion has anything to do with facts.  The


[048-165]facts are all secular; the sciences are all of this world.  If


[048-166]Catholics wish to establish their own schools for the purpose of


[048-167]preserving their ignorance, they have the right to do so; so has


[048-168]any other denomination.  But in this country the State has no right


[048-169]to teach any form of religion whatever.  Persons of all religions


[048-170]have the right to advocate and defend any religion in which they


[048-171]believe, or they have the right to denounce all religions.  If the


[048-172]Catholics establish parochial schools, let them support such schools;


[048-173]and if they do, they will simply lessen or shorten the longevity


[048-174]of that particular superstition.  It has often been said that


[048-175]nothing will repeal a bad law as quickly as its enforcement.  So,


[048-176]in my judgment, nothing will destroy any church as certainly, and


[048-177]as rapidly, as for the members of that church to live squarely up


[048-178]to the creed.  The church is indebted to its hypocrisy to-day for


[048-179]its life.  No orthodox church in the United States dare meet for


[048-180]the purpose of revising the creed.  They know that the whole thing


[048-181]would fall to pieces.


[048-182]Nothing could be more absurd than for a Roman Catholic priest to


[048-183]teach a public school, assisted by nuns.  The Catholic Church is


[048-184]the enemy of human progress; it teaches every man to throw away


[048-185]his reason, to deny his observation and experience.


[048-186]_Question_.  Your opinions have frequently been quoted with regard


[048-187]to the Anarchists--with regard to their trial and execution.  Have


[048-188]you any objection to stating your real opinion in regard to the


[048-189]matter?
