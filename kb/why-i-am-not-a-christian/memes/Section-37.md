[036-001]_Question_.  Why did you not take part in the campaign?


[036-002]_Answer_.  Well, I was afraid of frightening the preachers away.


[036-003]I might have done good by scaring one, but I did not know Burchard


[036-004]until it was too late.  Seriously, I did not think that I was


[036-005]needed.  I supposed that Blaine had a walkover, that he was certain


[036-006]to carry New York.  I had business of my own to attend to and did


[036-007]not want to interfere with the campaign.


[036-008]_Question_.  What do you think of the policy of nominating Blaine


[036-009]in 1888, as has been proposed?


[036-010]_Answer_.  I think it too early to say what will be done in 1888.


[036-011]Parties do not exist for one man.  Parties have certain ends in


[036-012]view and they choose men as instruments to accomplish these ends.


[036-013]Parties belong to principles, not persons.  No party can afford to


[036-014]follow anybody.  If in 1888 Mr. Blaine should appear to be the best


[036-015]man for the party then he will be nominated, otherwise not.  I know


[036-016]nothing about any intention to nominate him again and have no idea


[036-017]whether he has that ambition.  The Whig party was intensely loyal


[036-018]to Henry Clay and forgot the needs of the country, and allowed the


[036-019]Democrats to succeed with almost unknown men.  Parties should not


[036-020]belong to persons, but persons should belong to parties.  Let us


[036-021]not be too previous--let us wait.


[036-022]_Question_.  What do you think of the course pursued by the Rev.


[036-023]Drs. Ball and Burchard?


[036-024]_Answer_.  In politics the preacher is somewhat dangerous.  He has


[036-025]a standard of his own; he has queer ideas of evidence, great reliance


[036-026]on hearsay; he is apt to believe things against candidates, just


[036-027]because he wants to.  The preacher thinks that all who differ with


[036-028]him are instigated by the Devil--that their intentions are evil,


[036-029]and that when they behave themselves they are simply covering the


[036-030]poison with sugar.  It would have been far better for the country


[036-031]if Mr. Ball had kept still.  I do not pretend to say that his


[036-032]intentions were not good.  He likely thought it his duty to lift


[036-033]a warning voice, to bawl aloud and to spare not, but I think he


[036-034]made a mistake, and he now probably thinks so himself.  Mr. Burchard


[036-035]was bound to say a smart thing.  It sounded well, and he allowed


[036-036]his ears to run away with his judgment.  As a matter of fact, there


[036-037]is no connection between rum and Romanism.  Catholic countries do


[036-038]not use as much alcohol as Protestant.  England has far more


[036-039]drunkards than Spain.  Scotland can discount Italy or Portugal in


[036-040]good, square drinking.  So there is no connection between Romanism


[036-041]and rebellion.  Ten times as many Methodists and twenty times as


[036-042]many Baptists went into the Rebellion as Catholics.  Thousands of


[036-043]Catholics fought as bravely as Protestants for the preservation of


[036-044]the Union.  No doubt Mr. Burchard intended well.  He thought he


[036-045]was giving Blaine a battle-cry that would send consternation into


[036-046]the hearts of the opposition.  My opinion is that in the next


[036-047]campaign the preachers will not be called to the front.  Of course


[036-048]they have the same right to express their views that other people


[036-049]have, but other people have the right to avoid the responsibility


[036-050]of appearing to agree with them.  I think though that it is about


[036-051]time to let up on Burchard.  He has already unloaded on the Lord.


[036-052]_Question_.  Do you think Cleveland will put any Southern men in


[036-053]his Cabinet?


[036-054]_Answer_.  I do.  Nothing could be in worse taste than to ignore


[036-055]the section that gave him three-fourths of his vote.  The people


[036-056]have put the Democratic party in power.  They intended to do what


[036-057]they did, and why should the South not be recognized?  Garland


[036-058]would make a good Attorney-General; Lamar has the ability to fill


[036-059]any position in the Cabinet.  I could name several others well


[036-060]qualified, and I suppose that two or three Southern men will be in


[036-061]the Cabinet.  If they are good enough to elect a President they


[036-062]are good enough to be selected by a President.


[036-063]_Question_.  What do you think of Mr. Conkling's course?


[036-064]_Answer_.  Mr. Conkling certainly had the right to keep still.  He


[036-065]was under no obligation to the party.  The Republican papers have


[036-066]not tried to secure his services.  He has been very generally and


[036-067]liberally denounced ever since his quarrel with Mr. Garfield, and


[036-068]it is only natural to resent what a man feels to be an injustice.


[036-069]I suppose he has done what he honestly thought was, under the


[036-070]circumstances, his duty.  I believe him to be a man of stainless


[036-071]integrity, and he certainly has as much independence of character


[036-072]as one man can carry.  It is time to put the party whip away.


[036-073]People can be driven from, but not to, the Republican party.  If we


[036-074]expect to win in 1888 we must welcome recruits.


[036-075]--_The Plain Dealer_, Cleveland, Ohio, Dec. 11, 1884.


[036-076]RELIGIOUS PREJUDICE.


[036-077]_Question_.  Will a time ever come when political campaigns will


[036-078]be conducted independently of religious prejudice?


[036-079]_Answer_.  As long as men are prejudiced, they will probably be


[036-080]religious, and certainly as long as they are religious they will


[036-081]be prejudiced, and every religionist who imagines the next world


[036-082]infinitely more important than this, and who imagines that he gets


[036-083]his orders from God instead of from his own reason, or from his


[036-084]fellow-citizens, and who thinks that he should do something for


[036-085]the glory of God instead of for the benefit of his fellow-citizens


[036-086]--just as long as they believe these things, just so long their


[036-087]prejudices will control their votes.  Every good, ignorant, orthodox


[036-088]Christian places his Bible above laws and constitutions.  Every


[036-089]good, sincere and ignorant Catholic puts pope above king and


[036-090]president, as well as above the legally expressed will of a majority


[036-091]of his countrymen.  Every Christian believes God to be the source


[036-092]of all authority.  I believe that the authority to govern comes


[036-093]from the consent of the governed.  Man is the source of power, and


[036-094]to protect and increase human happiness should be the object of


[036-095]government.  I think that religious prejudices are growing weaker


[036-096]because religious belief is growing weaker.  And these prejudices


[036-097]--should men ever become really civilized--will finally fade away.


[036-098]I think that a Presbyterian, to-day, has no more prejudice against


[036-099]an Atheist than he has against a Catholic.  A Catholic does not


[036-100]dislike an Infidel any more than he does a Presbyterian, and I


[036-101]believe, to-day, that most of the Presbyterians would rather see


[036-102]and Atheist President than a pronounced Catholic.


[036-103]_Question_.  Is Agnosticism gaining ground in the United States?


[036-104]_Answer_.  Of course, there are thousands and thousands of men who


[036-105]have now advanced intellectually to the point of perceiving the


[036-106]limit of human knowledge.  In other words, at last they are beginning


[036-107]to know enough to know what can and cannot be known.  Sensible men


[036-108]know that nobody knows whether an infinite God exists or not.


[036-109]Sensible men know that an infinite personality cannot, by human


[036-110]testimony, be established.  Sensible men are giving up trying to


[036-111]answer the questions of origin and destiny, and are paying more


[036-112]attention to what happens between these questions--that is to say,


[036-113]to this world.  Infidelity increases as knowledge increases, as


[036-114]fear dies, and as the brain develops.  After all, it is a question


[036-115]of intelligence.  Only cunning performs a miracle, only ignorance


[036-116]believes it.


[036-117]_Question_.  Do you think that evolution and revealed religion are


[036-118]compatible--that is to say, can a man be an evolutionist and a


[036-119]Christian?


[036-120]_Answer_.  Evolution and Christianity may be compatible, provided


[036-121]you take the ground that Christianity is only one of the links in


[036-122]the chain, one of the phases of civilization.  But if you mean by


[036-123]Christianity what is generally understood, of course that and


[036-124]evolution are absolutely incompatible.  Christianity pretends to


[036-125]be not only the truth, but, so far as religion is concerned, the


[036-126]whole truth.  Christianity pretends to give a history of religion


[036-127]and a prophecy of destiny.  As a philosophy, it is an absolute


[036-128]failure.  As a history, it is false.  There is no possible way by


[036-129]which Darwin and Moses can be harmonized.  There is an inexpressible


[036-130]conflict between Christianity and Science, and both cannot long


[036-131]inhabit the same brain.  You cannot harmonize evolution and the


[036-132]atonement.  The survival of the fittest does away with original sin.


[036-133]_Question_.  From your knowledge of the religious tendency in the


[036-134]United States, how long will orthodox religion be popular?


[036-135]_Answer_.  I do not think that orthodox religion is popular to-day.


[036-136]The ministers dare not preach the creed in all its naked deformity


[036-137]and horror.  They are endeavoring with the vines of sentiment to


[036-138]cover up the caves and dens in which crawl the serpents of their


[036-139]creed.  Very few ministers care now to speak of eternal pain.  They


[036-140]leave out the lake of fire and brimstone.  They are not fond of


[036-141]putting in the lips of Christ the loving words, "Depart from me,


[036-142]ye cursed."  The miracles are avoided.  In short, what is known as


[036-143]orthodoxy is already unpopular.  Most ministers are endeavoring to


[036-144]harmonize what they are pleased to call science and Christianity,


[036-145]and nothing is now so welcome to the average Christian as some work


[036-146]tending to show that, after all, Joshua was an astronomer.


[036-147]_Question_.  What section of the United States, East, West, North,


[036-148]or South, is the most advanced in liberal religious ideas?


[036-149]_Answer_.  That section of the country in which there is the most


[036-150]intelligence is the most liberal.  That section of the country


[036-151]where there is the most ignorance is the most prejudiced.  The


[036-152]least brain is the most orthodox.  There possibly is no more


[036-153]progressive city in the world, no more liberal, than Boston.


[036-154]Chicago is full of liberal people.  So is San Francisco.  The brain


[036-155]of New York is liberal.  Every town, every city, is liberal in the


[036-156]precise proportion that it is intelligent.


[036-157]_Question_.  Will the religion of humanity be the religion of the


[036-158]future?


[036-159]_Answer_.  Yes; it is the only religion now.  All other is


[036-160]superstition.  What they call religion rests upon a supposed relation


[036-161]between man and God.  In what they call religion man is asked to


[036-162]do something for God.  As God wants nothing, and can by no possibility


[036-163]accept anything, such a religion is simply superstition.  Humanity


[036-164]is the only possible religion.  Whoever imagines that he can do


[036-165]anything for God is mistaken.  Whoever imagines that he can add to


[036-166]his happiness in the next world by being useless in this, is also


[036-167]mistaken.  And whoever thinks that any God cares how he cuts his


[036-168]hair or his clothes, or what he eats, or whether he fasts, or rings


[036-169]a bell, or puts holy water on his breast, or counts beads, or shuts


[036-170]his eyes and says words to the clouds, is laboring under a great


[036-171]mistake.


[036-172]_Question_.  A man in the Swaim Court Martial case was excluded as


[036-173]a witness because he was an Atheist.  Do you think the law in the


[036-174]next decade will permit the affirmative oath?


[036-175]_Answer_.  If belief affected your eyes, your ears, any of your


[036-176]senses, or your memory, then, of course, no man ought to be a


[036-177]witness who had not the proper belief.  But unless it can be shown


[036-178]that Atheism interferes with the sight, the hearing, or the memory,


[036-179]why should justice shut the door to truth?


[036-180]In most of the States of this Union I could not give testimony.


[036-181]Should a man be murdered before my eyes I could not tell a jury


[036-182]who did it.  Christianity endeavors to make an honest man an outlaw.


[036-183]Christianity has such a contemptible opinion of human nature that


[036-184]it does not believe a man can tell the truth unless frightened by


[036-185]a belief in God.  No lower opinion of the human race has ever been


[036-186]expressed.
