[092-001]The trouble is that these doctors, these students, these professors,


[092-002]these amateurs, do not give anesthetics.  They insist that to render


[092-003]the animal insensible does away with the value of the experiment.


[092-004]They care nothing for the pain they inflict.  They are so eager to


[092-005]find some fact that will be of benefit to the human race, that they


[092-006]are utterly careless of the agony endured.


[092-007]Now, what I say is that no decent man, no gentleman, no civilized


[092-008]person, would vivisect an animal without first having rendered


[092-009]that animal insensible to pain.  The doctor, the scientist, who


[092-010]puts his knives, forceps, chisels and saws into the flesh, bones


[092-011]and nerves of an animal without having used an anesthetic, is a


[092-012]savage, a pitiless, heartless monster.  When he says he does this


[092-013]for the good of man, because he wishes to do good, he says what is


[092-014]not true.  No such man wants to do good; he commits the crime for


[092-015]his own benefit and because he wishes to gratify an insane cruelty


[092-016]or to gain a reputation among like savages.


[092-017]These scientists now insist that they have done some good.  They


[092-018]do not tell exactly what they have done.  The claim is general in


[092-019]its character--not specific.  If they have done good, could they


[092-020]not have done just as much if they had used anesthetics?  Good is


[092-021]not the child of cruelty.


[092-022]_Question_.  Do you think that the vivisectionists do their work


[092-023]without anesthetics?  Do they not, as a rule, give something to


[092-024]deaden pain?


[092-025]_Answer_.  Here is what the trouble is.  Now and then one uses


[092-026]chloroform, but the great majority do not.  They claim that it


[092-027]interferes with the value of the experiment, and, as I said before,


[092-028]they object to the expense.  Why should they care for what the


[092-029]animals suffer?  They inflict the most horrible and useless pain,


[092-030]and they try the silliest experiments--experiments of no possible


[092-031]use or advantage.


[092-032]For instance:  They flay a dog to see how long he can live without


[092-033]his skin.  Is this trifling experiment of any importance?  Suppose


[092-034]the dog can live a week or a month or a year, what then?  What must


[092-035]the real character of the scientific wretch be who would try an


[092-036]experiment like this?  Is such a man seeking the good of his fellow-


[092-037]men?


[092-038]So, these scientists starve animals until they slowly die; watch


[092-039]them from day to day as life recedes from the extremities, and


[092-040]watch them until the final surrender, to see how long the heart


[092-041]will flutter without food; without water.  They keep a diary of


[092-042]their sufferings, of their whinings and moanings, of their insanity.


[092-043]And this diary is published and read with joy and eagerness by


[092-044]other scientists in like experiments.  Of what possible use is it


[092-045]to know how long a dog or horse can live without food?


[092-046]So, they take animals, dogs and horses, cut through the flesh with


[092-047]the knife, remove some of the back bone with the chisel, then divide


[092-048]the spinal marrow, then touch it with red hot wires for the purpose


[092-049]of finding, as they say, the connection of nerves; and the animal,


[092-050]thus vivisected, is left to die.


[092-051]A good man will not voluntarily inflict pain.  He will see that


[092-052]his horse has food, if he can procure it, and if he cannot procure


[092-053]the food, he will end the sufferings of the animal in the best and


[092-054]easiest way.  So, the good man would rather remain in ignorance as


[092-055]to how pain is transmitted than to cut open the body of a living


[092-056]animal, divide the marrow and torture the nerves with red hot iron.


[092-057]Of what use can it be to take a dog, tie him down and cut out one


[092-058]of his kidneys to see if he can live with the other?


[092-059]These horrors are perpetrated only by the cruel and the heartless


[092-060]--so cruel and so heartless that they are utterly unfit to be


[092-061]trusted with a human life.  They inoculate animals with a virus


[092-062]of disease; they put poison in their eyes until rottenness destroys


[092-063]the sight; until the poor brutes become insane.  They given them


[092-064]a disease that resembles hydrophobia, that is accompanied by the


[092-065]most frightful convulsions and spasms.  They put them in ovens to


[092-066]see what degree of heat it is that kills.  They also try the effect


[092-067]of cold; they slowly drown them; they poison them with the venom


[092-068]of snakes; they force foreign substances into their blood, and, by


[092-069]inoculation, into their eyes; and then watch and record their


[092-070]agonies; their sufferings.


[092-071]_Question_.  Don't you think that some good has been accomplished,


[092-072]some valuable information obtained, by vivisection?


[092-073]_Answer_.  I don't think any valuable information has been obtained


[092-074]by the vivisection of animals without chloroform that could not


[092-075]have been obtained with chloroform.  And to answer the question


[092-076]broadly as to whether any good has been accomplished by vivisection,


[092-077]I say no.


[092-078]According to the best information that I can obtain, the vivisectors


[092-079]have hindered instead of helped.  Lawson Tait, who stands at the


[092-080]head of his profession in England, the best surgeon in Great Britain,


[092-081]says that all this cutting and roasting and freezing and torturing


[092-082]of animals has done harm instead of good.  He says publicly that


[092-083]the vivisectors have hindered the progress of surgery.  He declares


[092-084]that they have not only done no good, but asserts that they have


[092-085]done only harm.  The same views according to Doctor Tait, are


[092-086]entertained by Bell, Syme and Fergusson.


[092-087]Many have spoken of Darwin as though he were a vivisector.  This


[092-088]is not true.  All that has been accomplished by these torturers of


[092-089]dumb and helpless animals amounts to nothing.  We have obtained


[092-090]from these gentlemen Koch's cure for consumption, Pasteur's factory


[092-091]of hydrophobia and Brown-Sequard's elixir of life.  These three


[092-092]failures, gigantic, absurd, ludicrous, are the great accomplishment


[092-093]of vivisection.


[092-094]Surgery has advanced, not by the heartless tormentors of animals,


[092-095]but by the use of anesthetics--that is to say, chloroform, ether


[092-096]and cocaine.  The cruel wretches, the scientific assassins, have


[092-097]accomplished nothing.  Hundreds of thousands of animals have suffered


[092-098]every pain that nerves can feel, and all for nothing--nothing except


[092-099]to harden the heart and to make criminals of men.


[092-100]They have not given anesthetics to these animals, but they have


[092-101]been guilty of the last step in cruelty.  They have given curare,


[092-102]a drug that attacks the centers of motion, that makes it impossible


[092-103]for the animal to move, so that when under its influence, no matter


[092-104]what the pain may be, the animal lies still.  This curare not only


[092-105]destroys the power of motion, but increases the sensitiveness of


[092-106]the nerves.  To give this drug and then to dissect the living animal


[092-107]is the extreme of cruelty.  Beyond this, heartlessness cannot go.


[092-108]_Question_.  Do you know that you have been greatly criticized for


[092-109]what you have said on this subject?


[092-110]_Answer_.  Yes; I have read many criticisms; but what of that.  It


[092-111]is impossible for the ingenuity of man to say anything in defence


[092-112]of cruelty--of heartlessness.  So, it is impossible for the defenders


[092-113]of vivisection to show any good that has been accomplished without


[092-114]the use of anesthetics.  The chemist ought to be able to determine


[092-115]what is and what is not poison.  There is no need of torturing the


[092-116]animals.  So, this giving to animals diseases is of no importance


[092-117]to man--not the slightest; and nothing has been discovered in


[092-118]bacteriology so far that has been of use or that is of benefit.


[092-119]Personally, I admit that all have the right to criticise; and my


[092-120]answer to the critics is, that they do not know the facts; or,


[092-121]knowing them, they are interested in preventing a knowledge of


[092-122]these facts coming to the public.  Vivisection should be controlled


[092-123]by law.  No animal should be allowed to be tortured.  And to cut


[092-124]up a living animal not under the influence of chloroform or ether,


[092-125]should be a penitentiary offence.


[092-126]A perfect reply to all the critics who insist that great good has


[092-127]been done is to repeat the three names--Koch, Pasteur and Brown-


[092-128]Sequard.


[092-129]The foundation of civilization is not cruelty; it is justice,


[092-130]generosity, mercy.


[092-131]--_Evening Telegram_, New York, September 30, 1893.


[092-132]DIVORCE.


[092-133]_Question_.  The _Herald_ would like to have you give your ideas


[092-134]on divorce.  On last Sunday in your lecture you said a few words


[092-135]on the subject, but only a few.  Do you think the laws governing


[092-136]divorce ought to be changed?


[092-137]_Answer_.  We obtained our ideas about divorce from the Hebrews--


[092-138]from the New Testament and the church.  In the Old Testament woman


[092-139]is not considered of much importance.  The wife was the property


[092-140]of the husband.


[092-141]"Thou shalt not covet thy neighbor's ox or his wife."  In this


[092-142]commandment the wife is put on an equality with other property, so


[092-143]under certain conditions the husband could put away his wife, but


[092-144]the wife could not put away her husband.


[092-145]In the New Testament there is little in favor of marriage, and


[092-146]really nothing as to the rights of wives.  Christ said nothing in


[092-147]favor of marriage, and never married.  So far as I know, none of


[092-148]the apostles had families.  St. Paul was opposed to marriage, and


[092-149]allowed it only as a choice of evils.  In those days it was imagined


[092-150]by the Christians that the world was about to be purified by fire,


[092-151]and that they would be changed into angels.


[092-152]The early Christians were opposed to marriage, and the "fathers"


[092-153]looked upon woman as the source of all evil.  They did not believe


[092-154]in divorces.  They thought that if people loved each other better


[092-155]than they did God, and got married, they ought to be held to the


[092-156]bargain, no matter what happened.


[092-157]These "fathers" were, for the most part, ignorant and hateful


[092-158]savages, and had no more idea of right and wrong than wild beasts.


[092-159]The church insisted that marriage was a sacrament, and that God,


[092-160]in some mysterious way, joined husband and wife in marriage--that


[092-161]he was one of the parties to the contract, and that only death


[092-162]could end it.


[092-163]Of course, this supernatural view of marriage is perfectly absurd.


[092-164]If there be a God, there certainly have been marriages he did not


[092-165]approve, and certain it is that God can have no interest in keeping


[092-166]husbands and wives together who never should have married.


[092-167]Some of the preachers insist that God instituted marriage in the


[092-168]Garden of Eden.  We now know that there was no Garden of Eden, and


[092-169]that woman was not made from the first man's rib.  Nobody with any


[092-170]real sense believes this now.  The institution of marriage was not


[092-171]established by Jehovah.  Neither was it established by Christ, not


[092-172]any of his apostles.


[092-173]In considering the question of divorce, the supernatural should be


[092-174]discarded.  We should take into consideration only the effect upon


[092-175]human beings.  The gods should be allowed to take care of


[092-176]themselves.


[092-177]Is it to the interest of a husband and wife to live together after


[092-178]love has perished and when they hate each other?  Will this add to


[092-179]their happiness?  Should a woman be compelled to remain the wife


[092-180]of a man who hates and abuses her, and whom she loathes?  Has


[092-181]society any interest in forcing women to live with men they hate?


[092-182]There is no real marriage without love, and in the marriage state


[092-183]there is no morality without love.  A woman who remains the wife


[092-184]of a man whom she despises, or does not love, corrupts her soul.


[092-185]She becomes degraded, polluted, and feels that her flesh has been


[092-186]soiled.  Under such circumstances a good woman suffers the agonies


[092-187]of moral death.  It may be said that the woman can leave her husband;


[092-188]that she is not compelled to live in the same house or to occupy


[092-189]the same room.  If she has the right to leave, has she the right


[092-190]to get a new house?  Should a woman be punished for having married?


[092-191]Women do not marry the wrong men on purpose.  Thousands of mistakes


[092-192]are made--are these mistakes sacred?  Must they be preserved to


[092-193]please God?
