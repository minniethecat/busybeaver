[090-001]Suppose God should answer the prayers and convert me, how would he


[090-002]bring the conversion about?  In the first place, he would have to


[090-003]change my brain and give me more credulity--that is, he would be


[090-004]obliged to lessen my reasoning power.  Then I would believe not


[090-005]only without evidence, but in spite of evidence.  All the miracles


[090-006]would appear perfectly natural.  It would then seem as easy to


[090-007]raise the dead as to waken the sleeping. In addition to this, God


[090-008]would so change my mind that I would hold all reason in contempt


[090-009]and put entire confidence in faith.  I would then regard science


[090-010]as the enemy of human happiness, and ignorance as the soil in which


[090-011]virtues grow.  Then I would throw away Darwin and Humboldt, and


[090-012]rely on the sermons of orthodox preachers.  In other words, I would


[090-013]become a little child and amuse myself with a religious rattle and


[090-014]a Gabriel horn.  Then I would rely on a man who has been dead for


[090-015]nearly two thousand years to secure me a seat in Paradise.


[090-016]After conversion, it is not pretended that I will be any better so


[090-017]far as my actions are concerned; no more charitable, no more honest,


[090-018]no more generous.  The great difference will be that I will believe


[090-019]more and think less.


[090-020]After all, the converted people do not seem to be better than the


[090-021]sinners.  I never heard of a poor wretch clad in rags, limping into


[090-022]a town and asking for the house of a Christian.


[090-023]I think that I had better remain as I am.  I had better follow the


[090-024]light of my reason, be true to myself, express my honest thoughts,


[090-025]and do the little I can for the destruction of superstition, the


[090-026]little I can for the development of the brain, for the increase of


[090-027]intellectual hospitality and the happiness of my fellow-beings.


[090-028]One world at a time.


[090-029]--_New York Journal_, December 15, 1895.


[090-030]SPIRITUALISM.


[090-031]There are several good things about Spiritualism.  First, they are


[090-032]not bigoted; second, they do not believe in salvation by faith;


[090-033]third, they don't expect to be happy in another world because Christ


[090-034]was good in this; fourth, they do not preach the consolation of


[090-035]hell; fifth, they do not believe in God as an infinite monster;


[090-036]sixth, the Spiritualists believe in intellectual hospitality.  In


[090-037]these respects they differ from our Christian brethren, and in


[090-038]these respects they are far superior to the saints.


[090-039]I think that the Spiritualists have done good.  They believe in


[090-040]enjoying themselves--in having a little pleasure in this world.


[090-041]They are social, cheerful and good-natured.  They are not the slaves


[090-042]of a book.  Their hands and feet are not tied with passages of


[090-043]Scripture.  They are not troubling themselves about getting


[090-044]forgiveness and settling their heavenly debts for a cent on the


[090-045]dollar.  Their belief does not make then mean or miserable.


[090-046]They do not persecute their neighbors.  They ask no one to have


[090-047]faith or to believe without evidence.  They ask all to investigate,


[090-048]and then to make up their minds from the evidence.  Hundreds and


[090-049]thousands of well-educated, intelligent people are satisfied with


[090-050]the evidence and firmly believe in the existence of spirits.  For


[090-051]all I know, they may be right--but----


[090-052]_Question_.  The Spiritualists have indirectly claimed, that you


[090-053]were in many respects almost one of them.  Have you given them


[090-054]reason to believe so?


[090-055]_Answer_.  I am not a Spiritualist, and have never pretended to


[090-056]be.  The Spiritualists believe in free thought, in freedom of


[090-057]speech, and they are willing to hear the other side--willing to


[090-058]hear me.  The best thing about the Spiritualists is that they


[090-059]believe in intellectual hospitality.


[090-060]_Question_.  Is Spiritualism a religion or a truth?


[090-061]_Answer_.  I think that Spiritualism may properly be called a


[090-062]religion.  It deals with two worlds--teaches the duty of man to


[090-063]his fellows--the relation that this life bears to the next.  It


[090-064]claims to be founded on facts.  It insists that the "dead" converse


[090-065]with the living, and that information is received from those who


[090-066]once lived in this world.  Of the truth of these claims I have no


[090-067]sufficient evidence.


[090-068]_Question_.  Are all mediums impostors?


[090-069]_Answer_.  I will not say that all mediums are impostors, because


[090-070]I do not know.  I do not believe that these mediums get any


[090-071]information or help from "spirits."  I know that for thousands of


[090-072]years people have believed in mediums--in Spiritualism.  A spirit


[090-073]in the form of a man appeared to Samson's mother, and afterward to


[090-074]his father.


[090-075]Spirits, or angels, called on Abraham.  The witch of Endor raised


[090-076]the ghost of Samuel.  An angel appeared with three men in the


[090-077]furnace.  The handwriting on the wall was done by a spirit.  A


[090-078]spirit appeared to Joseph in a dream, to the wise men and to Joseph


[090-079]again.


[090-080]So a spirit, an angel or a god, spoke to Saul, and the same happened


[090-081]to Mary Magdalene.


[090-082]The religious literature of the world is filled with such things.


[090-083]Take Spiritualism from Christianity and the whole edifice crumbles.


[090-084]All religions, so far as I know, are based on Spiritualism--on


[090-085]communications received from angels, from spirits.


[090-086]I do not say that all the mediums, ancient and modern, were, and


[090-087]are, impostors--but I do think that all the honest ones were, and


[090-088]are, mistaken.  I do not believe that man has ever received any


[090-089]communication from angels, spirits or gods.  No whisper, as I


[090-090]believe, has ever come from any other world.  The lips of the dead


[090-091]are always closed.  From the grave there has come no voice.  For


[090-092]thousands of years people have been questioning the dead.  They


[090-093]have tried to catch the whisper of a vanished voice.  Many say that


[090-094]they have succeeded.  I do not know.


[090-095]_Question_.  What is the explanation of the startling knowledge


[090-096]displayed by some so-called "mediums" of the history and personal


[090-097]affairs of people who consult them?  Is there any such thing as


[090-098]mind-reading or thought-transference?


[090-099]_Answer_.  In a very general way, I suppose that one person may


[090-100]read the thought of another--not definitely, but by the expression


[090-101]of the face, by the attitude of the body, some idea may be obtained


[090-102]as to what a person thinks, what he intends.  So thought may be


[090-103]transferred by look or language, but not simply by will.  Everything


[090-104]that is, is natural.  Our ignorance is the soil in which mystery


[090-105]grows.  I do not believe that thoughts are things that can been


[090-106]seen or touched.  Each mind lives in a world of its own, a world


[090-107]that no other mind can enter.  Minds, like ships at sea, give signs


[090-108]and signals to each other, but they do not exchange captains.


[090-109]_Question_.  Is there any such thing as telepathy?  What is the


[090-110]explanation of the stories of mental impressions received at long


[090-111]distances?


[090-112]_Answer_.  There are curious coincidences.  People sometimes happen


[090-113]to think of something that is taking place at a great distance.


[090-114]The stories about these happenings are not very well authenticated,


[090-115]and seem never to have been of the least use to anyone.


[090-116]_Question_.  Can these phenomena be considered aside from any


[090-117]connection with, or form of, superstition?


[090-118]_Answer_.  I think that mistake, emotion, nervousness, hysteria,


[090-119]dreams, love of the wonderful, dishonesty, ignorance, grief and


[090-120]the longing for immortality--the desire to meet the loved and lost,


[090-121]the horror of endless death--account for these phenomena.  People


[090-122]often mistake their dreams for realities--often think their thoughts


[090-123]have "happened."  They live in a mental mist, a mirage.  The boundary


[090-124]between the actual and the imagined becomes faint, wavering and


[090-125]obscure.  They mistake clouds for mountains.  The real and the


[090-126]unreal mix and mingle until the impossible becomes common, and the


[090-127]natural absurd.


[090-128]_Question_.  Do you believe that any sane man ever had a vision?


[090-129]_Answer_.  Of course, the sane and insane have visions, dreams.


[090-130]I do not believe that any man, sane or insane, was ever visited by


[090-131]an angel or spirit, or ever received any information from the dead.


[090-132]_Question_.  Setting aside from consideration the so-called physical


[090-133]manifestations of the mediums, has Spiritualism offered any proof


[090-134]of the immortality of the soul?


[090-135]_Answer_.  Of course Spiritualism offers what it calls proof of


[090-136]immortality.  That is its principal business.  Thousands and


[090-137]thousands of good, honest, intelligent people think the proof


[090-138]sufficient.  They receive what they believe to be messages from


[090-139]the departed, and now and then the spirits assume their old forms


[090-140]--including garments--and pass through walls and doors as light


[090-141]passes through glass.  Do these things really happen?  If the


[090-142]spirits of the dead do return, then the fact of another life is


[090-143]established.  It all depends on the evidence.  Our senses are easily


[090-144]deceived, and some people have more confidence in their reason than


[090-145]in their senses.


[090-146]_Question_.  Do you not believe that such a man as Robert Dale Owen


[090-147]was sincere?  What was the real state of mind of the author of


[090-148]"Footfalls on the Boundaries of Another World"?


[090-149]_Answer_.  Without the slightest doubt, Robert Dale Owen was sincere.


[090-150]He was one of the best of men.  His father labored all his life


[090-151]for the good of others.  Robert Owen, the father, had a debate, in


[090-152]Cincinnati, with the Rev. Alexander Campbell, the founder of the


[090-153]Campbellite Church.  Campbell was no match for Owen, and yet the


[090-154]audience was almost unanimously against Owen.


[090-155]Robert Dale Owen was an intelligent, thoughtful, honest man.  He


[090-156]was deceived by several mediums, but remained a believer.  He wanted


[090-157]Spiritualism to be true.  He hungered and thirsted for another


[090-158]life.  He explained everything that was mysterious or curious by


[090-159]assuming the interference of spirits.  He was a good man, but a


[090-160]poor investigator.  He thought that people were all honest.


[090-161]_Question_.  What do you understand the Spiritualist means when he


[090-162]claims that the soul goes to the "Summer land," and there continues


[090-163]to work and evolute to higher planes?


[090-164]_Answer_.  No one pretends to know where "heaven" is.  The celestial


[090-165]realm is the blessed somewhere in the unknown nowhere.  So far as


[090-166]I know, the "Summer land" has no metes and bounds, and no one


[090-167]pretends to know exactly or inexactly where it is.  After all, the


[090-168]"Summer land" is a hope--a wish.  Spiritualists believe that a soul


[090-169]leaving this world passes into another, or into another state, and


[090-170]continues to grow in intelligence and virtue, if it so desires.


[090-171]Spiritualists claim to prove that there is another life.  Christians


[090-172]believe this, but their witnesses have been dead for many centuries.


[090-173]They take the "hearsay" of legend and ancient gossip; but Spiritualists


[090-174]claim to have living witnesses; witnesses that can talk, make music;


[090-175]that can take to themselves bodies and shake hands with the people


[090-176]they knew before they passed to the "other shore."


[090-177]_Question_.  Has Spiritualism, through its mediums, ever told the


[090-178]world anything useful, or added to the store of the world's knowledge,


[090-179]or relieved its burdens?


[090-180]_Answer_.  I do not know that any medium has added to the useful


[090-181]knowledge of the world, unless mediums have given evidence of


[090-182]another life.  Mediums have told us nothing about astronomy, geology


[090-183]or history, have made no discoveries, no inventions, and have


[090-184]enriched no art.  The same may be said of every religion.


[090-185]All the orthodox churches believe in Spiritualism.  Every now and


[090-186]then the Virgin appears to some peasant, and in the old days the


[090-187]darkness was filled with evil spirits.  Christ was a Spiritualist,


[090-188]and his principal business was the casting out of devils.  All of


[090-189]his disciples, all of the church fathers, all of the saints were


[090-190]believers in Spiritualism of the lowest and most ignorant type.


[090-191]During the Middle Ages people changed themselves, with the aid of


[090-192]spirits, into animals.  They became wolves, dogs, cats and donkeys.


[090-193]In those day all the witches and wizards were mediums.  So animals


[090-194]were sometimes taken possession of by spirits, the same as Balaam's


[090-195]donkey and Christ's swine.  Nothing was too absurd for the


[090-196]Christians.
