## 生平

Jesus Christ (c. 4 BC – c. 30 AD), also known as Jesus of Nazareth, is the central figure of Christianity. According to Christian tradition, he was born in Bethlehem and preached in Galilee and Judea before being crucified under Pontius Pilate. Ingersoll references Jesus primarily in the context of Christian doctrine, particularly the requirement of belief in Christ for salvation (005-012).

## 主要观点与贡献

### 基督教信仰的核心

Ingersoll discusses the Christian doctrine that salvation depends on belief in Jesus Christ. He quotes the biblical injunction to "believe in the Lord Jesus Christ, and thou shalt be saved" (034-054), critiquing the notion that eternal destiny hinges on theological belief rather than moral conduct.

### 耶稣的品格与神迹

Ingersoll notes that some ministers have paid "tribute to the character of Jesus Christ" (014-015) while simultaneously condemning honorable freethinkers. He questions the logical consistency of this position. On the subject of miracles, Ingersoll argues that claims about the "superiority of Jesus Christ" rest on miraculous accounts (067-091) whose credibility he challenges.

### 批评性视角

Throughout the text, Ingersoll examines the historical and theological claims surrounding Jesus from a skeptical standpoint. He questions whether the gospel narratives are reliable historical records and challenges the moral framework that demands belief in Christ as a condition for divine favor (074-143).
