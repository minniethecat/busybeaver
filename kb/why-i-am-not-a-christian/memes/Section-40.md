[039-001]_Question_.  Have you ever been misrepresented in interviews?


[039-002]_Answer_.  Several times.  As a general rule, the clergy have


[039-003]selected these misrepresentations when answering me.  I never blamed


[039-004]them, because it is much easier to answer something I did not say.


[039-005]Most reporters try to give my real words, but it is difficult to


[039-006]remember.  They try to give the substance, and in that way change


[039-007]or destroy the sense.  You remember the Frenchman who translated


[039-008]Shakespeare's great line in Macbeth--"Out, brief candle!"--into


[039-009]"Short candle, go out!"  Another man, trying to give the last


[039-010]words of Webster--"I still live"--said "I aint dead yit."  So that


[039-011]when they try to do their best they often make mistakes.  Now and


[039-012]then interviews appear not one word of which I ever said, and


[039-013]sometimes when I really had an interview, another one has appeared.


[039-014]But generally the reporters treat me well, and most of them succeed


[039-015]in telling about what I said.  Personally I have no cause for


[039-016]complaint.


[039-017]_Question_.  What do you think of the administration of President


[039-018]Cleveland?


[039-019]_Answer_.  I know but very little about it.  I suppose that he is


[039-020]doing the best he can.  He appears to be carrying out in good faith


[039-021]the principles laid down in the platform on which he was elected.


[039-022]He is having a hard road to travel.  To satisfy an old Democrat


[039-023]and a new mugwump is a difficult job.  Cleveland appears to be the


[039-024]owner of himself--appears to be a man of great firmness and force


[039-025]of character.  The best thing that I have heard about him is that


[039-026]he went fishing on Sunday.  We have had so much mock morality, dude


[039-027]deportment and hypocritical respectability in public office, that


[039-028]a man with courage enough to enjoy himself on Sunday is a refreshing


[039-029]and healthy example.  All things considered I do not see but that


[039-030]Cleveland is doing well enough.  The attitude of the administration


[039-031]toward the colored people is manly and fair so far as I can see.


[039-032]_Question_.  Are you still a Republican in political belief?


[039-033]_Answer_.  I believe that this is a Nation.  I believe in the


[039-034]equality of all men before the law, irrespective of race, religion


[039-035]or color.  I believe that there should be a dollar's worth of silver


[039-036]in a silver dollar.  I believe in a free ballot and a fair count.


[039-037]I believe in protecting those industries, and those only, that need


[039-038]protection.  I believe in unrestricted coinage of gold and silver.


[039-039]I believe in the rights of the State, the rights of the citizen,


[039-040]and the sovereignty of the Nation.  I believe in good times, good


[039-041]health, good crops, good prices, good wages, good food, good clothes


[039-042]and in the absolute and unqualified liberty of thought.  If such


[039-043]belief makes a Republican, than that is what I am.


[039-044]_Question_.  Do you approve of John Sherman's policy in the present


[039-045]campaign with reference to the bloody shirt, which reports of his


[039-046]speeches show that he is waving?


[039-047]_Answer_.  I have not read Senator Sherman's speech.  It seems to


[039-048]me that there is a better feeling between the North and South than


[039-049]ever before--better than at any time since the Revolutionary war.


[039-050]I believe in cultivating that feeling, and in doing and saying what


[039-051]we can to contribute to its growth.  We have hated long enough and


[039-052]fought enough.  The colored people never have been well treated


[039-053]but they are being better treated now than ever before.  It takes


[039-054]a long time to do away with prejudices that were based upon religion


[039-055]and rascality--that is to say, inspiration and interest.  We must


[039-056]remember that slavery was the crime of the whole country.  Now, if


[039-057]Senator Sherman has made a speech calculated to excite the hatreds


[039-058]and prejudices of the North and South, I think that he has made a


[039-059]mistake.  I do not say that he has made such a speech, because I


[039-060]have not read it.  The war is over--it ended at Appomattox.  Let


[039-061]us hope that the bitterness born of the conflict died out forever


[039-062]at Riverside.  The people are tired almost to death of the old


[039-063]speeches.  They have been worn out and patched, and even the patches


[039-064]are threadbare.  The Supreme Court decided the Civil Rights Bill


[039-065]to be unconstitutional, and the Republican party submitted.  I


[039-066]regarded the decision as monstrous, but the Republican party when


[039-067]in power said nothing and did nothing.  I most sincerely hope that


[039-068]the Democratic party will protect the colored people at least as


[039-069]well as we did when we were in power.  But I am out of politics


[039-070]and intend to keep politics out of me.


[039-071]_Question_.  We have been having the periodical revival of interest


[039-072]in Spiritualism.  What do you think of "Spiritualism," as it is


[039-073]popularly termed?


[039-074]_Answer_.  I do not believe in the supernatural.  One who does not


[039-075]believe in gods would hardly believe in ghosts.  I am not a believer


[039-076]in any of the "wonders" and "miracles" whether ancient or modern.


[039-077]There may be spirits, but I do not believe there are.  They may


[039-078]communicate with some people, but thus far they have been successful


[039-079]in avoiding me.  Of course, I know nothing for certain on the


[039-080]subject.  I know a great many excellent people who are thoroughly


[039-081]convinced of the truth of Spiritualism.  Christians laugh at the


[039-082]"miracles" to-day, attested by folks they know, but believe the


[039-083]miracles of long ago, attested by folks that they did not know.


[039-084]This is one of the contradictions in human nature.  Most people


[039-085]are willing to believe that wonderful things happened long ago and


[039-086]will happen again in the far future; with them the present is the


[039-087]only time in which nature behaves herself with becoming sobriety.


[039-088]In old times nature did all kinds of juggling tricks, and after a


[039-089]long while will do some more, but now she is attending strictly to


[039-090]business, depending upon cause and effect.


[039-091]_Question_.  Who, in your opinion, is the greatest leader of the


[039-092]"opposition" yclept the Christian religion?


[039-093]_Answer_.  I suppose that Mr. Beecher is the greatest man in the


[039-094]pulpit, but he thinks more of Darwin than he does of David and has


[039-095]an idea that the Old Testament is just a little too old.  He has


[039-096]put evolution in the place of the atonement--has thrown away the


[039-097]Garden of Eden, snake, apples and all, and is endeavoring to save


[039-098]enough of the orthodox wreck to make a raft.  I know of no other


[039-099]genius in the pulpit.  There are plenty of theological doctors and


[039-100]bishops and all kinds of titled humility in the sacred profession,


[039-101]but men of genius are scarce.  All the ministers, except Messrs.


[039-102]Moody and Jones, are busy explaining away the contradiction between


[039-103]inspiration and demonstration.


[039-104]_Question_.  What books would you recommend for the perusal of a


[039-105]young man of limited time and culture with reference to helping


[039-106]him in the development of intellect and good character?


[039-107]_Answer_.  The works of Darwin, Ernst Haeckel, Draper's "Intellectual


[039-108]Development of Europe," Buckle's "History of Civilization in


[039-109]England," Lecky's "History of European Morals," Voltaire's


[039-110]"Philosophical Dictionary," Buechner's "Force and Matter," "The


[039-111]History of the Christian Religion" by Waite; Paine's "Age of Reason,"


[039-112]D'Holbach's "System of Nature," and, above all, Shakespeare.  Do


[039-113]not forget Burns, Shelley, Dickens and Hugo.


[039-114]_Question_.  Will you lecture the coming winter?


[039-115]_Answer_.  Yes, about the same as usual.  Woe is me if I preach


[039-116]not my gospel.


[039-117]_Question_.  Have you been invited to lecture in Europe?  If so do


[039-118]you intend to accept the "call"?


[039-119]_Answer_.  Yes, often.  The probability is that I shall go to


[039-120]England and Australia.  I have not only had invitations but most


[039-121]excellent offers from both countries.  There is, however, plenty


[039-122]to do here.  This is the best country in the world and our people


[039-123]are eager to hear the other side.


[039-124]The old kind of preaching is getting superannuated.  It lags


[039-125]superfluous in the pulpit.  Our people are outgrowing the cruelties


[039-126]and absurdities of the ancient Jews.  The idea of hell has become


[039-127]shocking and vulgar.  Eternal punishment is eternal injustice.  It


[039-128]is infinitely infamous.  Most ministers are ashamed to preach the


[039-129]doctrine, and the congregations are ashamed to hear it preached.


[039-130]It is the essence of savagery.


[039-131]--_Plain Dealer_, Cincinnati, Ohio, September 5, 1885.


[039-132]MY BELIEF.


[039-133]_Question_.  It is said that in the past four or five years you


[039-134]have changed or modified your views upon the subject of religion;


[039-135]is this so?


[039-136]_Answer_.  It is not so.  The only change, if that can be called


[039-137]a change, is, that I am more perfectly satisfied that I am right--


[039-138]satisfied that what is called orthodox religion is a simple


[039-139]fabrication of mistaken men; satisfied that there is no such thing


[039-140]as an inspired book and never will be; satisfied that a miracle


[039-141]never was and never will be performed; satisfied that no human


[039-142]being knows whether there is a God or not, whether there is another


[039-143]life or not; satisfied that the scheme of atonement is a mistake,


[039-144]that the innocent cannot, by suffering for the guilty, atone for


[039-145]the guilt; satisfied that the doctrine that salvation depends on


[039-146]belief, is cruel and absurd; satisfied that the doctrine of eternal


[039-147]punishment is infamously false; satisfied that superstition is of


[039-148]no use to the human race; satisfied that humanity is the only true


[039-149]and real religion.


[039-150]No, I have not modified my views.  I detect new absurdities every


[039-151]day in the popular belief.  Every day the whole thing becomes more


[039-152]and more absurd.  Of course there are hundreds and thousands of


[039-153]most excellent people who believe in orthodox religion; people for


[039-154]whose good qualities I have the greatest respect; people who have


[039-155]good ideas on most other subjects; good citizens, good fathers,


[039-156]husbands, wives and children--good in spite of their religion.  I


[039-157]do not attack people.  I attack the mistakes of people.  Orthodoxy


[039-158]is getting weaker every day.


[039-159]_Question_.  Do you believe in the existence of a Supreme Being?


[039-160]_Answer_.  I do not believe in any Supreme personality or in any


[039-161]Supreme Being who made the universe and governs nature.  I do not


[039-162]say that there is no such Being--all I say is that I do not believe


[039-163]that such a Being exists.  I know nothing on the subject, except


[039-164]that I know that I do not know and that nobody else knows.  But if


[039-165]there is such a Being, he certainly never wrote the Old Testament.


[039-166]You will understand my position.  I do not say that a Supreme Being


[039-167]does not exist, but I do say that I do not believe such a Being


[039-168]exists.  The universe--embracing all that is--all atoms, all stars,


[039-169]each grain of sand and all the constellations, each thought and


[039-170]dream of animal and man, all matter and all force, all doubt and


[039-171]all belief, all virtue and all crime, all joy and all pain, all


[039-172]growth and all decay--is all there is.  It does not act because it


[039-173]is moved from without.  It acts from within.  It is actor and


[039-174]subject, means and end.


[039-175]It is infinite; the infinite could not have been created.  It is


[039-176]indestructible and that which cannot be destroyed was not created.


[039-177]I am a Pantheist.


[039-178]_Question_.  Don't you think the belief of the Agnostic is more


[039-179]satisfactory to the believer than that of the Atheist?


[039-180]_Answer_.  There is no difference.  The Agnostic is an Atheist.


[039-181]The Atheist is an Agnostic.  The Agnostic says:  "I do not know,


[039-182]but I do not believe there is any God."  The Atheist says the same.


[039-183]The orthodox Christian says he knows there is a God; but we know


[039-184]that he does not know.  He simply believes.  He cannot know.  The


[039-185]Atheist cannot know that God does not exist.


[039-186]_Question_.  Haven't you just the faintest glimmer of a hope that


[039-187]in some future state you will meet and be reunited to those who


[039-188]are dear to you in this?


[039-189]_Answer_.  I have no particular desire to be destroyed.  I am


[039-190]willing to go to heaven if there be such a place, and enjoy myself


[039-191]for ever and ever.  It would give me infinite satisfaction to know


[039-192]that all mankind are to be happy forever.  Infidels love their


[039-193]wives and children as well as Christians do theirs.  I have never


[039-194]said a word against heaven--never said a word against the idea of


[039-195]immortality.  On the contrary, I have said all I could truthfully


[039-196]say in favor of the idea that we shall live again.  I most sincerely


[039-197]hope that there is another world, better than this, where all the


[039-198]broken ties of love will be united.  It is the other place I have


[039-199]been fighting.  Better that all of us should sleep the sleep of


[039-200]death forever than that some should suffer pain forever.  If in


[039-201]order to have a heaven there must be a hell, then I say away with


[039-202]them both.  My doctrine puts the bow of hope over every grave; my


[039-203]doctrine takes from every mother's heart the fear of hell.  No good


[039-204]man would enjoy himself in heaven with his friends in hell.  No


[039-205]good God could enjoy himself in heaven with millions of his poor,


[039-206]helpless mistakes in hell.  The orthodox idea of heaven--with God


[039-207]an eternal inquisitor, a few heartless angels and some redeemed


[039-208]orthodox, all enjoying themselves, while the vast multitude will


[039-209]weep in the rayless gloom of God's eternal dungeon--is not calculated


[039-210]to make man good or happy.  I am doing what I can to civilize the


[039-211]churches, humanize the preachers and get the fear of hell out of


[039-212]the human heart.  In this business I am meeting with great success.
