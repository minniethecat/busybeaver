# Conte Zurla

Conte Zurla (also referred to as Zurla-Contarini) is the Minister of the Interior at the Court of Parma during the reign of Ranuccio-Ernesto IV. He is a minor but recurring figure in the novel's depiction of court politics.

## Role at Court

Zurla holds the portfolio of Minister of the Interior. He is initially given the position, according to the Marchesa Balbi, because he is "an imbecile who is easily managed" [[Balbi]]. Despite this unflattering characterization, he remains in office and is a fixture at court gatherings.

## In the Narrative

Zurla appears at several key court moments. After Fabrizio's arrest, the Prince sends Zurla to deal with matters at the Citadel. At a court ball, he is seen among the gathered nobility, "everyone gathered round them" as political factions jostle for position.

Zurla represents the mediocrity of court functionaries—men who hold power not through ability but through maneuverability and connections.
