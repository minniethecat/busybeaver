# Political Factions

The court of Parma is a crucible of competing factions. Liberals and reactionaries, Francophiles and Austrian loyalists, the Raversi cabal and the Sanseverina circle — political allegiance in Stendhal's world is personal, passionate, and often deadly. This page indexes the political organisations and groups that shape the novel's power dynamics.

## Court Factions

The Parma court operates through informal networks of patronage, intrigue, and personal loyalty. The *Raversi faction* opposes the *Sanseverina–Mosca axis*, while the *Liberal party* represents reformist sentiment.

## Cross-Border Politics

The political landscape spans multiple jurisdictions — Austrian Lombardy, the Duchy of Parma, the Papal States, and Bourbon Naples — each with its own factional dynamics.
