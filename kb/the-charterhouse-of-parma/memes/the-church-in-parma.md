# The Church in Parma

The Catholic Church permeates every level of Stendhal's novel. Fabrizio's ecclesiastical career — from theology student to Archbishop's coadjutor — structures the plot, while parish priests like Priore Blanès serve as moral compasses. The Church is simultaneously a spiritual institution, a political power, and a vehicle for personal ambition.

## The Spiritual Dimension

Beyond institutional politics, the novel explores genuine spiritual questions — Blanès's astrology and prophecy, Fabrizio's sermon at the Charterhouse, and the tension between worldly ambition and religious vocation.
