# La Ricciarda

La Ricciarda is a small farmstead (podere) located about a league from Casalmaggiore, in Lombardy. It belongs to the del Dongo family and is offered to [[Fabrizio-del-Dongo]] by his mother as a place of refuge.

## Fabrizio's Retreat

After Fabrizio is forced to flee Parma, the [[Marchesa-del-Dongo]] offers him "the small podere of La Ricciarda, a league from Casalmaggiore." Fabrizio, however, initially refuses: "I cannot accept the podere of La Ricciarda. I have the honour to return it."

His refusal reflects his pride and his reluctance to burden his mother, but eventually he takes possession of the property. The farmer at La Ricciarda owes the family "three or four thousand lire," which provides Fabrizio with some income.

## Significance

La Ricciarda represents the pastoral alternative to court life—a quiet place of retreat far from the intrigues of Parma. Fabrizio's eventual acceptance of the farm signals his growing maturity and willingness to accept help from those who love him.
