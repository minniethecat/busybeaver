# Honour

## Definition

Honour in *The Charterhouse of Parma* appears in multiple, often contradictory forms. There is the military honour that drives men to risk their lives for a cause; the aristocratic honour that governs duels, debts, and social standing; the family honour that the Marchesa del Dongo fears losing; and the personal honour that Gina invokes as her last and most precious possession. Stendhal treats honour with characteristic irony — it is both genuinely felt and cynically exploited, a noble principle and a "false point of honour" that leads men to destruction.

## Historical Context

In early 19th-century Italy, honour was a social code with real practical force. Duels were fought over perceived slights; families could be ruined by scandal; a man's "word of honour" was a binding contract. The Napoleonic era had introduced new conceptions of honour rooted in merit and patriotism rather than birth, creating tensions that the novel dramatises. The Restoration brought back older, more hierarchical codes.

## Role in the Novel

Honour motivates many of the novel's critical actions. Fabrizio is driven by a sense of honour to fight at Waterloo, even though he is a naive volunteer with no military experience. His father's refusal to support him is framed as a question of family honour (and its absence). The Duchessa's ultimate threat to the Prince — "The day on which I shall have lost my honour will be also the last day on which I shall set eyes on you" (027-010) — reveals that honour is, for her, synonymous with self-respect and moral independence.

Yet Stendhal also exposes honour's dark side. Giletti's "false point of honour" leads him to attack Fabrizio, resulting in his own death (026-012). The Prince's fear of being dishonoured by Fabrizio's escape drives him to persecute the young man more harshly. Conte Mosca's brother acts on a "false point of honour" and feels "bound to avenge himself" — to his ruin. Throughout the novel, honour is a double-edged sword: it can elevate or destroy, protect or imprison.
