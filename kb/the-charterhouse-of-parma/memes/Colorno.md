# Colorno

**Colorno** is the grand ducal palace of the Prince of Parma, described by Stendhal as "the Versailles of the Prince of Parma" [011-022]. Situated near Sanguigna in the Duchy of [[Parma]], the palace serves as one of the principal residences of the [[Ernesto-IV|court]] and a symbol of princely magnificence.

## Location

The palace is situated on the farther side of [[Colorno]], on a hill commanding the [[Po]] River valley [021-001]. Its elevated position offers strategic views of the surrounding plain. The location is opposite [[Sanguigna]], a small locality referenced in connection with the palace grounds [011-022].

## Role in the Novel

Colorno appears as a backdrop to the courtly life of Parma. The palace's comparison to Versailles underscores the ambitions and pretensions of the miniature court of [[Ernesto-IV|Ranuccio-Ernesto IV]], who modelled his sovereignty on the grander monarchies of Europe.

## Architecture

The palace, formally known as the Reggia di Colorno, is a Baroque ducal residence with extensive gardens. Though Stendhal provides little architectural detail, the Versailles comparison conveys its scale and ornamental character within the modest Duchy of [[Parma]].
