# Vasi

Vasi is a dealer in barometers from Menaggio, a town on Lake Como. He plays a small but pivotal role in Fabrizio del Dongo's journey by providing him with a passport that allows him to cross into Switzerland and eventually reach Waterloo.

## Role in Fabrizio's Escape

When Fabrizio decides to join Napoleon's army, his first obstacle is obtaining travel documents. His aunt Gina Sanseverina arranges for him to "go and get the passport from Vasi" (PN 008-028). The passport proves essential — Fabrizio stays overnight at Menaggio at the house of "my friend Vasi, the dealer in barometers" (PN 002-029), and the next morning crosses into Switzerland.

## The Vasi Alias

Fabrizio adopts "Vasi" as his assumed name during the Waterloo campaign. When questioned by the cantinière about his identity, he replies: "'I'm called Vasi,' replied Fabrizio, with a curious smile" (PN 004-055). The alias serves him throughout the battle, as he tells others: "My name is Vasi and I come from Genoa; my sister is a famous beauty in Paris" (PN 004-060).

The alias becomes a recurring motif. Fabrizio's protectors instruct him to "say that your real name: say that you're Vasi" (PN 004-081), and later in Parma, Fabio Conti notes "a dealer in barometers, named Vasi" (PN 005-097) as part of the identity Fabrizio used.

## Significance

Vasi represents the network of ordinary people who assist Fabrizio's improbable journey. Though Vasi himself never appears directly in the narrative, his name — borrowed for a passport and an alias — enables the entire Waterloo episode. The merchant's unremarkable existence in Menaggio stands in contrast to the extraordinary chain of events his passport sets in motion.
