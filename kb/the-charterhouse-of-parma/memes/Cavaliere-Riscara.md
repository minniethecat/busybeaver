## Overview

Cavaliere Riscara is a minor but vivid figure in the Parma court, an ally of the Marchesa Raversi and a participant in the conspiracy against the Duchessa Sanseverina (Gina). His small, darting eyes and cold, indifferent manner make him one of Stendhal's memorable sketches of court malice.

## Character

Riscara is defined by two physical and behavioral traits: his "little eyes" that "sparkled" [014-107] and his habit of speaking in an "indifferent tone" even when delivering threatening news [014-108]. He is a court creature — entirely dependent on proximity to power for his status. His hatred of Rassi the Fiscal General [020-076] suggests he distinguishes himself from the more vulgar forms of court advancement, though the distinction is one of style rather than morality.

## Role in the Conspiracy

Riscara appears at a critical moment in Chapter 14, when the Marchesa Raversi is orchestrating the Duchessa Sanseverina's downfall. He and "three more of her friends" [014-094] gather the day following Gina's disgrace to plan their next moves. Riscara's role is that of a reliable lieutenant — he carries messages, assesses information ("presumably he knows you also" [014-100]), and ultimately "got into a post-chaise by himself" [014-106] to execute some errand. His efficiency makes him dangerous despite his relatively low rank.
