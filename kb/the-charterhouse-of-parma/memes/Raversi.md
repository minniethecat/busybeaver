# Marchesa Raversi

## Role in the Novel

The Marchesa Raversi is one of the principal antagonists at the court of Parma, serving as the leader of the faction opposed to [[Conte-Mosca]]. Immensely wealthy and politically adroit, she represents the dangerous world of court intrigue that Fabrizio must navigate. Her machinations drive much of the political tension in the novel's middle chapters.

## Biography

The Marchesa Raversi is introduced as a powerful figure at court, immensely rich and possessed of formidable political skills. She is the niece of a prominent court figure and has built a substantial political following.

> "If he succeeded, there was nothing to fear from the party of the Marchesa Raversi, who was immensely rich." (006-031)

Her party seizes upon the history of Fabrizio's life to attack Conte Mosca's position. When Mosca appears to be politically broken, the Raversi faction stands on the verge of triumph.

> "The Marchesa Raversi, a consummate intriguer who had the whole of Parma ringing." (006-059)

The Raversi is described as Gina Sanseverina's principal enemy at court, and she relentlessly circulates damaging rumors. She pretends that Conte Mosca has been paid by foreign powers, attempting to undermine his credibility with the Prince.

## Political Influence

Raversi's power derives from three sources: her immense personal wealth, her extensive network of allies at court, and her skill as an intriguer. She operates through a well-organized party that includes many of the court's most influential figures. Her tactics include spreading rumors, forging alliances with other powerful nobles, and exploiting any weakness in Mosca's political position.

## Relationships

- **[[Conte-Mosca]]**: Her principal political rival; she seeks to destroy his influence
- **[[Gina-Sanseverina]]**: Her enemy at court; the two women vie for influence
- **[[Prince-of-Parma]]**: She attempts to manipulate the Prince against Mosca
- **[[Fabrizio-del-Dongo]]**: She uses his history to attack Mosca's position
