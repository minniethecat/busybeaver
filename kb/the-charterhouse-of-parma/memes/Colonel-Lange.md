# Colonel Lange

## Role in the Novel

Colonel Lange is a military officer who succeeds General [[Fabio-Conti]] as the commanding officer of the Citadel of Parma. His ascension to this position occurs at a critical moment in the novel, when the political fallout from Fabrizio del Dongo's imprisonment and Clelia Conti's involvement reaches its climax. Lange represents the institutional continuity of the Parma military apparatus — the Citadel does not crumble when its governor is removed, but passes smoothly to a successor.

## Biography

Colonel Lange appears late in the novel when he is called upon to succeed Fabio Conti as commander of the Citadel [025-087]. The transition comes after the poisoning scandal and the exposure of the relationship between [[Fabrizio-del-Dongo]] and [[Clelia-Conti]], which has made General Conti's position untenable. Lange's appointment signifies the Prince's determination to restore order at the fortress without disrupting the military chain of command.

Lange's report on the poisoning incident is referenced in the narrative [025-089], indicating that he conducted an official inquiry into the events surrounding Fabrizio's imprisonment. Unlike his predecessor, who was personally invested in the outcome through his daughter Clelia, Lange is presented as a professional soldier executing his duties without personal entanglement in the court intrigues.

## Key Appearances

- **Chapter 25**: Lange is called upon to replace Fabio Conti as Citadel commander [025-087] and submits his report on the poisoning case [025-089].
