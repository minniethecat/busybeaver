Bellagio is a village on [[Lake-Como|Lake Como]] in [[Lombardy]], northern Italy. In *The Charterhouse of Parma*, it appears as one of the lakeside settlements that form the backdrop to the early chapters of the novel, set in the [[Lake-Como|Lake Como]] region around the del Dongo family's castle at [[Grianta]].

## Location and Description

Bellagio occupies a celebrated position on the promontory where [[Lake-Como|Lake Como]] divides into its two southern branches. The village is known for its dramatic views across the lake and its position at the intersection of the lake's three arms. In Stendhal's description, it is mentioned alongside other villages: "contadini returning to Bellagio, Menaggio and other villages situated on the shores of the lake of Como" [009-012].

The village lies on the eastern shore of the lake, across the water from [[Menaggio]] on the western shore. Together, these villages form the fabric of [[Lake-Como|Lake Como]]'s inhabited shoreline — a world of fishermen, contadini, and the country houses of the Lombard aristocracy that Stendhal evokes in the novel's opening chapters.

## Role in the Novel

Bellagio appears only in passing, as part of the geographical texture of the [[Lake-Como|Lake Como]] region where [[Fabrizio-del-Dongo|Fabrizio del Dongo]] spends his childhood and from which he later flees. The reference at [009-012] places Bellagio within the network of lakeside villages that define the novel's northern Italian landscape — a world of natural beauty that stands in sharp contrast with the political prisons (such as the [[Spielberg]]) that threaten Fabrizio's liberty.

The village's fleeting mention nevertheless contributes to the novel's detailed evocation of place. Stendhal meticulously maps the geography of [[Fabrizio-del-Dongo|Fabrizio]]'s world, and Bellagio, like [[Menaggio]] and [[Grianta]], is a fixed point in that map.

## Associated Characters

- **[[Fabrizio-del-Dongo|Fabrizio del Dongo]]** — childhood in the Lake Como region that includes Bellagio and surrounding villages

## Historical Background

Bellagio has been settled since Roman times and was already renowned in the early nineteenth century for its scenic beauty. The village was part of Austrian-ruled [[Lombardy]] during the period of the novel. Its famed location at the junction of [[Lake-Como|Lake Como]]'s branches has made it one of the most visited destinations on the lake, a reputation that has only grown since Stendhal's time.
