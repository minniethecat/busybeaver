# Cadenabbia

**Cadenabbia** is a small village on the western shore of [[Lake Como]] (Lago di Como), near [[Grianta]] in the Lombardy region. In Stendhal's novel, it is home to young contadini (peasant boys) who interacted with the [[The-del-Dongo-Family|del Dongo family]] estate at Grianta [002-005].

## Location and Setting

Cadenabbia lies along the lakeshore road connecting [[Grianta]] to other villages of the Como region. The village is situated on an embankment built by the marchese, extending in the direction of Cadenabbia [002-019]. It forms part of the pastoral landscape of Austrian-ruled [[Lombardy]].

## Role in the Novel

The village appears early in the novel as part of the childhood world of [[Fabrizio-del-Dongo]]. Local boys from Cadenabbia and [[Grianta]] procured a number of things for young Fabrizio, reflecting the close-knit lakeside community [002-005]. The marchese del Dongo's embankment, built towards Cadenabbia, hints at the family's landowning presence extending beyond the immediate grounds of Grianta [002-019].
