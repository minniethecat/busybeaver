# Bishop of Piacenza

The Bishop of Piacenza is an ecclesiastical figure mentioned in connection with the del Dongo family's church interests and Fabrizio's ecclesiastical career.

## Ecclesiastical Authority

The del Dongo family holds significant influence in the diocese of Piacenza. Fabrizio's great-uncle, the Archbishop of Parma, "filled himself with joy at the prospect of having a del Dongo under his orders" (PN 011-021), indicating the family's extensive church connections that include the Bishop of Piacenza. The Archbishop boasts that "we have had as Archbishops of Parma three members of your family" (PN 006-071), underscoring the dynastic reach of the del Dongo ecclesiastical network.

## Fabrizio's Career Path

The Bishop of Piacenza figures in the plans for Fabrizio's advancement in the Church hierarchy. Fabrizio's ecclesiastical ambitions include the hope that the Prince "would deign to allow me a minor Bishopric to begin with" (PN 011-016). The Bishop of Piacenza, as the ordinary of a neighboring diocese, represents the kind of ecclesiastical career path envisioned for the young del Dongo.

## Church Politics

The Bishop of Piacenza exemplifies the intertwining of church and aristocratic politics that characterizes the novel's Parma. In a world where "the Prince made a great point of not dismissing" (PN 006-031) even his enemies, the episcopal bench serves as an extension of court patronage. The Bishop of Piacenza, though barely glimpsed directly, stands as part of the ecclesiastical infrastructure through which families like the del Dongo maintain power across generations.
