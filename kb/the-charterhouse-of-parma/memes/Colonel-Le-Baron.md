# Colonel Le Baron

## Role in the Novel

Colonel Le Baron is a French cavalry officer commanding the 6th Dragoons who appears in the aftermath of the Battle of Waterloo. He is present at the inn where the wounded and scattered French soldiers regroup, and his appearance marks one of Fabrizio del Dongo's early encounters with the formal military hierarchy. As a professional cavalry commander, he represents the established military order that young Fabrizio, an eager but untrained volunteer, aspires to join.

## Biography

Colonel Le Baron is first mentioned when hussars point out to Fabrizio that "Colonel Le Baron is in the inn over there" [004-105], encouraging him to seek out a superior officer. He is identified as commanding the 6th Dragoons [004-109]. During the chaotic aftermath of the battle, the Colonel is said to have been "summoned by the pistol shots" [004-117] to intervene in a situation, indicating his authority amid the disorder. His character plays a minor but illustrative role in showing the disorganized state of the French army after Waterloo — even senior officers are found in inns rather than with their units.

Stendhal himself provided additional context on the character, with a note about Colonel Le Baron appearing in the first volume of *Les Soirées du Stendhal Club* [029-004], suggesting the character may have been drawn from the author's personal knowledge of Napoleonic officers.

## Key Appearances

- **Chapter 4 (After Waterloo)**: Colonel Le Baron is at the inn where Fabrizio and other scattered soldiers gather [004-105, 004-109]. He is summoned during a confrontation [004-117].
