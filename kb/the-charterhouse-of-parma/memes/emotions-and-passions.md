# Emotions and Passions

Stendhal is renowned for his psychological acuity, and *The Charterhouse of Parma* is above all a novel of the passions. Love, jealousy, ambition, honour, and the pursuit of happiness drive every major character. This page indexes the emotional and affective concepts that structure the novel's inner world.

## Stendhalian Psychology

The novel's emotional vocabulary is distinctively Stendhalian: *cristallisation* (the lover's idealisation), *espagnolisme* (the fiery Southern temperament), and the pursuit of *bonheur* (happiness) as life's ultimate aim.
