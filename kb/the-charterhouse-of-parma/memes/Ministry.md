# Ministry

The **Ministry** in [[The-Charterhouse-of-Parma]] represents the bureaucratic apparatus through which the Prince's absolutist rule is administered. Unlike the personal and capricious nature of court politics, the ministries represent the institutional dimension of [[Power]] in the Duchy of [[Parma]].

## The Ministries of Parma

The novel references several distinct ministries that together constitute the government. The Ministry of Finance, held by Conte [[Mosca]], is the most frequently mentioned, reflecting its centrality to the state's functioning. The Ministry of the Interior, responsible for internal security and administration, features prominently in [[Fabrizio-del-Dongo]]'s imprisonment. The Ministry of Justice, under [[Rassi]], represents the regime's repressive apparatus.

> [006-118] …here only by that burden of a Ministry of Finance which I was stupid enough to accept.

> [013-069] …of having him brought to the Ministry of the Interior.

## The Ultra Ministry

The novel refers to Conte Mosca's government as the "Ultra Ministry" (012-032), indicating its alignment with the most conservative, pro-absolutist faction at court. The term "Ultra" (ultra-royalist) places Parma's government within the broader European reaction against Napoleonic [[Liberals|liberalism]] following the Congress of Vienna.

> [012-032] …about the fall of the Ultra Ministry, and of its leader, Conte Mosca.

## Ministry as Burden

Conte Mosca repeatedly speaks of his ministerial position as a burden rather than a privilege. His willingness to resign from the Ministry forms a recurring theme, reflecting the precariousness of political life under an absolute [[Sovereign]]. The ministry is both the summit of political ambition and a source of constant anxiety.

> [006-032] …his resignation, to leave the Ministry and to come and spend the rest of his life quietly at Naples or in Paris.

> [016-048] …to resign from the Ministry if the rigour shown Fabrizio were not mitigated.
