# Captain Teulier

Captain Teulier was an officer in the 4th Hussars of the French Army who was killed at the Battle of Waterloo. His name becomes significant when Fabrizio del Dongo assumes it as a protective alias during the battle's aftermath.

## Death at Waterloo

Teulier's fate is established early in the Waterloo episode. When Fabrizio inquires about officers in his regiment, he is asked: "And if Captain Teulier has been killed, where will his things go?" (PN 003-089). The question confirms Teulier's death, placing him among the many casualties of the battle.

## The Teulier Alias

Fabrizio adopts Teulier's identity as part of his survival strategy. When captured and interrogated by Prussian hussars, he declares: "'Teulier, Captain in the 4th Hussars'" (PN 004-064). The alias is risky — a fellow hussar later recognizes the name: "'I know you, you're in Captain Henriot's squadron'" (PN 004-119). Fabrizio is forced to improvise, replying that "Captain Henriot was killed yesterday" (PN 004-120).

## Significance

Captain Teulier represents one of the novel's recurring motifs — borrowed identity. Like Vasi, the barometer dealer whose name Fabrizio also uses, Teulier is an absent figure whose name becomes a tool of survival. The dead captain's identity, assumed by a young Italian volunteer who barely understands what he is doing on a Belgian battlefield, embodies the chaos and impersonality of war. Teulier himself — his life, his squadron, his death — remains entirely offstage, known only through the use of his name.
