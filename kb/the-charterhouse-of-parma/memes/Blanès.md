# Priore Blanès

## Role in the Novel

Priore Blanès is the parish priest of Grianta and serves as Fabrizio del Dongo's earliest spiritual mentor. An amateur astrologer of deep personal integrity, he introduces Fabrizio to ideas about fate, the stars, and the hidden patterns of life. His death in Chapter 8 marks the end of Fabrizio's childhood and his final break with his life at Grianta.

## Biography

Blanès is the good parish priest of Grianta, entrusted by the Marchesa del Dongo with Fabrizio's early education. A man of honesty, virtue, and simple goodness, he is nonetheless regarded by the local contadini with superstitious awe as a great magician.

> "The contadini looked upon Priore Blanès with awe as a great magician." (002-004)

Blanès shares Fabrizio's interest in the stars and omens. The boy spends many nights studying astrology with the old priest in the bell tower of the Grianta church. Fabrizio regards Blanès as his true father, a spiritual counterweight to his absent biological father.

> "Priore Blanès was his true father." (008-031)

At eighty-three, Blanès senses his approaching death and summons Fabrizio back from exile. Their final conversation on the night of his death is one of the novel's most poignant scenes.

> "I expected you," said Blanès, after the first warm words of greeting. "Death is at hand," said Priore Blanès. (008-032–033)

## Astrology and Philosophy

Blanès practices a form of astrological divination, reading the stars to predict the future. His predictions, though often ambiguous, create a sense of fate and destiny that haunts Fabrizio throughout the novel. The Marchesa del Dongo herself is drawn to the primitive spirituality that Blanès represents.
