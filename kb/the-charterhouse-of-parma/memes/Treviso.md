# Treviso

**Treviso** is a city in the Veneto region of northern Italy, east of the [[Alps|Alpine]] chain. In Stendhal's novel, it is noted for the vast panoramic view it affords of the Alpine range, from the Treviso plain to the distant peaks.

## Landscape

From Treviso, the eye sweeps across the chain of the [[Alps]] towards the city itself [018-005]. The panorama extends along the entire northern horizon, from [[Treviso]] to [[Monviso]], described as "the endless chain of the Alps" — a view that underscores the grandeur of the natural world against which the novel's human dramas unfold [018-013].

## Role in the Novel

Treviso appears as part of the broader geographical imagination of the novel. Its mention, alongside the Alpine panorama, situates characters within the physical and political geography of northern Italy during the Napoleonic and post-Napoleonic periods. The view from Treviso towards [[Monviso]] represents one of the novel's moments of landscape reverie.
