# Marchesa Balbi

The Marchesa Balbi is a young courtier at the court of Parma, described as "a young woman of five-and-twenty" (PN 006-057) who "might still have passed for the most perfect beauty" (PN 006-057). She serves as a mistress to the Prince and is an art collector.

## Role at Court

Balbi is part of the Raversi faction at court. She is dismissively described by the Conte Mosca as "that doll of a Marchesa Balbi, who moreover robs my poor subjects by refusing to pay for what she buys" (PN 014-023), indicating her character as both frivolous and corrupt.

She is also noted for her "agreeable thinness" (PN 014-100), which is remarked upon when she leaves a dinner party early.

## Relationship with the Prince

Balbi is identified as "the late Prince's mistress" (PN 028-020), confirming her role as a royal favorite. Her position at court is significant enough that after a change in the political landscape, courtiers remark "There is the Balbi of this new reign" (PN 024-025), suggesting she adapts to shifting power structures.

## Art Collection

Balbi is known for her "magnificent collection of pictures" (PN 028-020), a mark of her wealth and status as a court figure who benefits from her proximity to power. Her art collection is mentioned in connection with Gonzo, a functionary at court.

Despite her minor role, Balbi represents the type of court figure — young, beautiful, self-interested — who navigates the Parma court's complex personal politics through strategic relationships.
