# Conte Baldi

Conte Baldi is a courtier at the court of Parma, renowned as "the handsomest man at court" (PN 006-059). He serves as the reigning lover of the Marchesa Raversi, one of the principal antagonists at court.

## Role at Court

Baldi is described as "a young man of admirable appearance" (PN 014-094). He is closely allied with the Raversi faction, which opposes the Conte Mosca and the Duchessa Sanseverina. His physical attractiveness is repeatedly emphasized — he is called "that handsome man" (PN 019-031) and is noted for his "fine eyes" (PN 014-099).

## Relationship with Raversi

The Marchesa Raversi, "although definitely ugly" (PN 006-059), maintains Baldi as her lover. He is referred to as "the Raversi's reigning lover" (PN 017-032) and "the former friend of the Marchesa" (PN 024-011), indicating the relationship persists throughout the novel's middle chapters.

## Political Involvement

Baldi participates in court intrigues on behalf of the Raversi. During the political maneuvering around Fabrizio's imprisonment, Baldi is directed by Sanseverina's faction in a scheme: "You, Conte Baldi, sit down at my desk and write what I shall dictate" (PN 014-095). He is also mentioned as "presenting himself and failing" when Sanseverina tests the loyalty of various courtiers (PN 015-046).

Baldi's role is minor but illustrative — he embodies the court's vanity and the transactional nature of its personal relationships, where beauty and political convenience intertwine.
