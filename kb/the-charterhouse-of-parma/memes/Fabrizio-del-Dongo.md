# Fabrizio del Dongo

## Role in the Novel

Fabrizio del Dongo is the young protagonist of Stendhal's *The Charterhouse of Parma* — a passionate, impulsive Milanese nobleman whose romantic adventures and political entanglements drive the narrative. His journey traces an arc from the battlefield of [[Battle-of-Waterloo|Waterloo]] to the [[Citadel-of-Parma|Farnese Tower]] to the [[The-Charterhouse-of-Parma-Novel|Charterhouse of Parma]], making him one of literature's most memorable charm-driven protagonists. Through Fabrizio, Stendhal explores the gulf between romantic idealism and the realities of power, love, and mortality in post-Napoleonic Italy.

## Biography

Fabrizio del Dongo is born in Milan, the younger son of the reactionary [[Marchesa-del-Dongo|Marchese del Dongo]] and his liberal wife. Raised amid the revolutionary fervour of Napoleonic Italy, he runs away at seventeen to fight at [[Battle-of-Waterloo|Waterloo]] — a formative experience that strips him of romantic illusions about war:

> The little cart halted; Fabrizio awoke with a start. The sun had set some time back; he was quite astonished to see that it was almost night. The troops were running in all directions; they seemed to be in a great hurry.
> [004-003]

Returning to Italy, he enters the Church under the protection of his aunt [[Gina-Sanseverina]], now the Duchessa Sanseverina, and her lover [[Conte-Mosca]], Prime Minister of [[Parma]]. At the court of Parma, his charm and imprudence make him both a favourite and a target. His affair with the actress [[Marietta]] leads to a duel with her protector [[Giletti]], whom Fabrizio kills:

> On leaving the Archbishop's Palace, Fabrizio hastened to see little Marietta; he could hear from the street the loud voice of Giletti who had sent out for wine and was regaling himself with his favourite *barbera*…
> [011-001]

Imprisoned in the [[Citadel-of-Parma|Farnese Tower]], he falls transcendentally in love with [[Clelia-Conti]], the governor's daughter. After a daring escape arranged by [[Gina-Sanseverina]], he returns voluntarily to prison to be near her. Eventually freed, he becomes a revered preacher under the name Monsignor del Dongo. He and Clelia maintain a secret relationship, but tragedy strikes when their son dies. Fabrizio retreats to the [[The-Carthusian-Order|Charterhouse of Parma]], where he dies in contemplative solitude within a year.

> The whole of this adventure had not lasted a minute. Fabrizio's wounds were nothing; they tied up his arm with bandages torn from the colonel's shirt. They wanted to make up a bed for him upstairs in the inn.
> [005-001]

## Character Analysis

Fabrizio is one of literature's most charming protagonists — a character defined more by what happens to him than by what he does. He is impulsive but not foolish, passionate but not cruel, and his fundamental innocence persists despite every misadventure. Stendhal uses him to explore the gulf between romantic idealism and reality. Fabrizio's arc from eager Waterloo volunteer to contemplative monk is a compressed spiritual biography: he seeks glory in war, love in prison, and meaning in solitude. His appeal lies in his sincerity — in a novel full of calculation, Fabrizio is the one character who never calculates.

## Relationships

### Family
- [[Marchesa-del-Dongo]] — his mother, the one constant source of unconditional love
- [[Gina-Sanseverina]] — his aunt, protector, and the novel's most important relationship
- [[Pietranera|Contessa Pietranera]] — his aunt, whose death sets early events in motion

### Romantic
- [[Clelia-Conti]] — his greatest love, discovered in the Farnese Tower; their secret relationship and the death of their child defines his final tragedy
- [[Marietta]] — a young actress; their affair triggers the duel with Giletti
- [[Fausta]] — a singer at La Scala; a brief entanglement

### Political and Social
- [[Conte-Mosca]] — Prime Minister of Parma; his political protector, devoted to Gina
- [[Prince-of-Parma]] — Ranuce-Erneste IV; his persecutor, motivated by jealousy over Gina
- [[Rassi]] — the Prince's fiscal officer; instrumental in Fabrizio's prosecution
- [[General-Fabio-Conti]] — governor of the Citadel and Clelia's father
