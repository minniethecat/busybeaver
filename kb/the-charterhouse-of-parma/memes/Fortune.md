# Fortune

## Definition

Fortune in *The Charterhouse of Parma* is a secular, almost pagan force — not divine providence but sheer luck, the unpredictable conjunction of circumstances that determines human fate. Stendhal's characters constantly invoke fortune: they are its beneficiaries and its victims. The concept reflects both the Napoleonic belief in merit and the Restoration reality of arbitrary power. In a world without reliable justice, fortune is the only real sovereign.

## Historical Context

The concept of *fortuna* has deep roots in Italian culture, from Machiavelli to the Renaissance. For Stendhal's generation, the rise and fall of Napoleon was the supreme example of fortune's power. The novel's characters live in a world where a chance encounter can make a career and a random suspicion can destroy a life — a world Stendhal knew intimately from his own experience as a Napoleonic administrator and later consul.

## Role in the Novel

Fortune is the novel's hidden protagonist. Fabrizio's entire life is shaped by fortune: his chance birth into nobility, his accidental presence at Waterloo, his random killing of Giletti, his imprisonment and improbable escape. The Duchessa's political success depends on fortune's favour. Conte Mosca, for all his calculation, knows that fortune can undo his careful plans in an instant. Stendhal's point is not fatalism but realism: in a world of arbitrary power, prudence matters but so does luck.
