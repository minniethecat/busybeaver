# Lieutenant Robert

## Role in the Novel

Lieutenant Robert is the first character to appear in the novel's narrative frame, serving as the catalyst for the story's opening. His arrival at the del Dongo household in Milan — assigned there by a billeting order during the French occupation — provides the occasion for the Marchesa del Dongo's social awakening and, through the stories he tells, introduces the themes of war, adventure, and fortune that will define Fabrizio's life.

## Biography

Robert is a young French lieutenant serving in Napoleon's army during the Italian campaign. He receives a billeting order to stay at the del Dongo palace in Milan, where he is hosted by the [[Marchesa-del-Dongo]].

> "A lieutenant, for instance, named Robert, received a billeting order on the del Dongo palace." (001-008)

During his stay, Robert entertains the Marchesa and her household with stories of military life. His tales of the French army's adventures capture the imagination of the household and mark the beginning of the Marchesa's fascination with all things French.

> "Lieutenant Robert's story was more or less that of all the French soldiers in Italy." (001-015)

Robert later participates in an expedition to a remote castle, where he distinguishes himself. Following this action, he is promoted to the rank of Captain, his career advancing with Napoleon's fortunes.

> "When the army drew near to Milan, Lieutenant Robert, who had been promoted to the rank of Captain." (001-023)

## Narrative Function

Robert serves a structural purpose in the novel: his stories establish the atmosphere of the Napoleonic era and plant in the young Fabrizio — who grows up hearing these tales — the dream of military glory that will lead him to Waterloo. The Marchesa's admiration for Robert foreshadows her later relationship with French officers and her estrangement from her reactionary husband.

## Relationships

- **[[Marchesa-del-Dongo]]**: His hostess; her admiration for him signals her Francophile sympathies
- **[[Fabrizio-del-Dongo]]**: Though Robert never meets Fabrizio directly, his stories inspire the boy's martial ambitions
