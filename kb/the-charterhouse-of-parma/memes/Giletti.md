# Giletti

## Role in the Novel

Marietta's jealous protector whose fatal confrontation with Fabrizio on the road sets in motion the novel's central crisis — a minor character whose death has major consequences.

## Biography

Giletti is a travelling actor and Marietta's protector. He encounters Fabrizio on the road and, suspecting that the young nobleman has designs on Marietta, challenges him. The confrontation escalates into violence, and Fabrizio kills Giletti in self-defence — though the circumstances are ambiguous enough to be interpreted as murder. Giletti's death is the event that leads to Fabrizio's arrest and imprisonment in the Farnese Tower, making him one of the most consequential minor characters in the novel. His death is investigated by the partisan legal system of Parma and becomes the pretext for the Prince's persecution of Fabrizio.

## Character Analysis

Giletti appears in only a few pages but his death structures the novel's second half. He is an example of Stendhal's skill with minor characters: vividly sketched, memorable, and narratively essential. His confrontation with Fabrizio is one of the novel's most suspenseful scenes — a moment when Fabrizio's aristocratic carelessness collides with the violent realities of the road. Giletti is not sympathetic — he is brutish and jealous — but his death is not justified either. It is simply what happens when passion, pride, and poor judgment converge on a lonely road.
