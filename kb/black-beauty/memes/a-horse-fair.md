# A Horse Fair

The horse fair is the pivotal marketplace scene in *Black Beauty* where [[Black Beauty]], having been repeatedly sold after his accident, finally finds a kind new owner in [[Jerry Barker]]. The fair serves as both a literal turning point in Beauty's fortunes and a symbolic panorama of the Victorian horse economy in all its variety and cruelty. (035-001)

## 概述

After being "ruined and going downhill" — passed through a succession of owners who worked him harder and cared for him less — Beauty finds himself at a large horse fair. The scene opens with a sweeping description: long strings of young horses fresh from the marshes, droves of shaggy Welsh ponies, hundreds of cart horses, and handsome high-bred animals fallen into the middle class through accident or blemish. (035-001)

Among the wretched animals in the background — horses with knuckling knees, dejected old creatures with "no more pleasure in life" — Beauty stands out as still handsome despite his damaged knees. It is here that Jerry Barker, a London cab driver, notices him and decides to make a bid. (035-003)

## 事件经过

The fair was a chaotic marketplace where horses of every description were bought and sold. Beauty observes the spectacle with the dispassionate eye of one who has seen the worst of human nature: "a good many like myself, handsome and high-bred, but fallen into the middle class, through some accident or blemish." (035-001)

A prospective buyer remarked on Beauty's battered knees, interpreting them as a sign of a fall. The seller admitted the scar but vouched for Beauty's good temper and gentleness. (035-003) Jerry, who had been looking for a horse with exactly those qualities, was impressed — not by flawless appearance, but by the horse's calm, intelligent eye. He bought Beauty for a modest sum, and the two began their partnership as a London cab horse and his driver. (035-003) (035-005)

> No doubt a horse fair is a very amusing place to those who have nothing to lose; but to the horses themselves it is a very different matter. There is more trickery and more false representation at a horse fair than a clever man could give an account of.
>
> (035-003)

## 后果与影响

The horse fair marks the beginning of Beauty's life as a London cab horse — the third major phase of his working life after his idyllic years at Birtwick Park and his suffering at Earlshall. Under Jerry's kind and principled care, Beauty experiences a version of the good horsemanship he knew under John Manly, adapted to the harsher conditions of city work. (035-005)

## 主题意义

The horse fair encapsulates the novel's critique of the Victorian horse trade as a system. The contrast between the "splendid animals quite in their prime" and the "poor things, sadly broken down with hard work" in the background of the same fair is Sewell's visual shorthand for the fate that awaits every horse in a system that values them only for their labor. That Beauty is rescued here — by a man who looks past surface blemishes to see character — is the novel's argument that individual compassion can, in some cases, interrupt the downward spiral.
