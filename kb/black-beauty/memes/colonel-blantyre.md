# Colonel Blantyre

A military gentleman and guest at [[Earlshall Park|Earlshall]], whose quick thinking and skilled horsemanship helped save [[Lady Anne]] after a near-fatal riding accident. He is one of the few male characters in the novel who demonstrates genuine concern for a horse's welfare.

## 角色概述

Colonel Blantyre was a guest at [[Earlshall Park|Earlshall]], the estate of [[Lord W]], during the spring when most of the family had gone to London. (026-003) Unlike many gentlemen of his class who treated horses as mere equipment, Blantyre showed a careful and considerate attitude toward horses — he warned Lady Anne against riding the nervous mare [[Lizzie]], and after the accident, he visited [[Black Beauty]] to personally thank and praise him. (026-032)

## 故事经历

### 警告与坚持

When Lady Anne declared her intention to ride Lizzie — a "charming creature" that the gentlemen admired for her "fine action and lively spirit" — Blantyre immediately protested. (026-003) He warned her plainly: "She is a charming creature, but she is too nervous for a lady. I assure you, she is not perfectly safe; let me beg you to have the saddles changed." (026-006) His protest was overruled by Lady Anne, who laughed off his concern, declaring herself an experienced horsewoman who had "followed the hounds a great many times." (026-007)

### 事故与救援

The party rode to the village to deliver a message for [[Lady Harriet]]. Blantyre dismounted at the doctor's gate, hanging Black Beauty's rein on a spike while he went inside. (026-010) While he was gone, some colts blundered against Lizzie, startling her into a headlong gallop with Lady Anne on her back. Hearing Black Beauty's neighs for help, Blantyre came running, saw the distant figure, and "in an instant he sprang to the saddle." (026-013)

What followed was a frantic pursuit across a mile and a half of road, then onto a rough common. Blantyre guided Black Beauty with "a light hand and a practiced eye" over the treacherous ground, steadily gaining on the runaway. (026-016) At a wide dike, Lizzie stumbled and fell, throwing Lady Anne motionless to the heather. Blantyre gathered Black Beauty and "with one determined leap cleared both dike and bank." (026-017)

Finding Lady Anne unconscious, Blantyre knelt beside her, calling her name. When she did not respond, he acted swiftly — checking her pulse, loosening her collar, and sending a turf-cutter on Black Beauty to fetch the doctor and the carriage. (026-018, 026-023)

### 结局

Two days after the accident, Blantyre visited Black Beauty in his stable, patted him, and praised him to [[Lord George]], saying "the horse knew of Annie's danger as well as he did" and that Lady Anne "ought never to ride any other horse." (026-032) Lady Anne recovered from the accident, and Black Beauty's role in the rescue cemented his reputation at Earlshall.

## 性格特征

- **谨慎而有远见**：He alone foresaw the danger of letting Lady Anne ride Lizzie and spoke up, even when overruled
- **果断且勇敢**：When the crisis came, he acted without hesitation — mounting in "an instant" and riding at full speed across dangerous ground
- **骑术精湛**：He guided Black Beauty over the rough common with a "light hand and a practiced eye," gaining ground without punishing the horse
- **感恩与尊重**：He personally visited Black Beauty after the accident to thank and praise him, an uncommon gesture toward a horse

## 主题意义

Colonel Blantyre represents a rare type in the novel: a gentleman who combines military bearing with genuine concern for equine welfare. His warning about Lizzie — and its tragic vindication — reinforces one of [[Anna Sewell]]'s central arguments: that ignoring a horse's nature and temperament, whether through fashion or overconfidence, leads to disaster. His praise of Black Beauty after the rescue also shows that he judges horses not by their breeding or price, but by their character and performance — a perspective Sewell clearly endorsed.
