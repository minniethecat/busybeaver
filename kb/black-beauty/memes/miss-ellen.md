# Miss Ellen

One of the three ladies who formed [[Black Beauty]]'s final and happiest home, the first of the household to take him out for a ride and the one who confirmed his suitability as a lady's horse.

## 角色概述

Miss Ellen was, along with Miss Blomefield, one of the ladies at the final home where [[Black Beauty]] found lasting peace and happiness after his long odyssey through many owners. (053-003) She appears only in the novel's closing chapter, but her role is symbolically significant: she represents the kind, discerning owner who recognizes a good horse's worth not by his pedigree or appearance, but by his character and behavior under saddle.

## 故事经历

### 早期经历

Nothing is known of Miss Ellen's life before she enters the narrative in the final chapter. She was part of the household of three ladies — presumably sisters or relatives — who lived together and were in need of a safe, reliable horse. When [[Mr. Thoroughgood]] and his grandson [[Willie]] brought [[Black Beauty]] to them for consideration, it was Miss Blomefield or Miss Ellen who came to the door. (053-003)

### 关键转折

The ladies were cautious. Having been warned by the farmer that the horse had seen hard times and needed gentle treatment, they agreed to try Beauty for a period before committing to purchase him. Miss Ellen was the one chosen to take the first trial ride. (053-016) The choice was significant — it was the ultimate test of Beauty's suitability as a lady's horse, and his calm, willing performance under Miss Ellen's hand demonstrated that despite all he had suffered, his fundamental gentleness remained intact.

### 结局

Miss Ellen's successful ride sealed Beauty's fate. The ladies kept him, and in their quiet, caring household, Beauty finally found the permanent home he had been seeking since his early days at [[Birtwick Park]]. (053-016) Miss Ellen and her companions gave Beauty the life that every horse in the novel deserves but few receive: steady work without cruelty, kindness without sentimentality, and the security of knowing he would never be sold again.

## 性格特征

- Gentle and discerning — recognized a good horse when she rode one
- Cautious but not suspicious — willing to give a worn-down horse a fair trial
- Represents the ideal of the considerate woman rider that Sewell championed
- Quiet and domestic — belongs to the private sphere of home rather than the public world of commerce and sport

## 主题意义

In the novel's moral geography, Miss Ellen's household represents the ideal destination — a quiet, kind, permanent home where horses are treated with respect rather than exploited for profit, fashion, or sport. That Miss Ellen is the one to take the first ride is thematically important: where earlier in the novel young women like Lady Anne rode spirited horses for excitement and nearly died as a result, Miss Ellen rides a calm, experienced horse for practical transportation and finds satisfaction in his reliability. She embodies Sewell's vision of proper horsemanship as a partnership between a thoughtful rider and a willing horse, free from the vanity and recklessness that caused so much suffering throughout the novel.
