# Old Daniel

Old Daniel was the man who took over the care of the colts at [[Farmer Grey]]'s farm after the plowboy Dick was dismissed for throwing stones at the young horses. He later appeared at [[Birtwick Park]], where he held Beauty's head gently while the new bridle was fitted. (002-011)(004-005)

## A Gentle Hand

When Dick was caught stoning the colts and dismissed on the spot, Old Daniel took his place. The change in caretaker was, for the young horses, a change in the quality of their daily lives — from a boy who saw them as targets for stones to a man who understood them as creatures deserving of quiet care. (002-011)

Daniel's gentle manner was evident again when Beauty was being trained to accept the saddle. Farmer Grey placed the saddle on Beauty's back while Old Daniel held the young horse's head, "patting and talking to me all the time." (004-005) His steady presence — calm hands and a soothing voice — was part of the patient, humane training method that Farmer Grey practiced, and that the novel holds up as the proper way to gentle a young horse.

> My master put it on my back very gently, while old Daniel held my head; he then made the girths fast under my body, patting and talking to me all the time.
>
> (004-005)
