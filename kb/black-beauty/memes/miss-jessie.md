# Miss Jessie

One of [[Squire Gordon]]'s young daughters at [[Birtwick Park]], known for her fondness for the pony [[Merrylegs]] and her gentle treatment of the horses. (005-016)

## 角色概述

Miss Jessie and her sister [[Miss Flora]] were the young ladies of Birtwick Park. They were "very fond" of Merrylegs and often rode the gentle pony around the estate. (005-016) Black Beauty noted that they were kind to all the horses, treating them with the respect that their father modeled.

## 故事经历

### 早期经历

Miss Jessie grew up at Birtwick Park surrounded by horses. She and Miss Flora would ride Merrylegs and [[Sir Oliver]] under supervision, and were trusted to handle the gentle pony on their own. (006-024)

### 关键转折

When the village school children visited the park, Miss Jessie and Flora played with them around the stables, showing an easy, democratic spirit unusual for their class. (010-001) Merrylegs once recounted a conversation where he was asked if he had ever thrown Miss Jessie — a suggestion he indignantly denied, as the girls were kind and never gave him reason to. (010-006)

### 结局

Miss Jessie and Miss Flora eventually went away, presumably to school, marking the beginning of changes at Birtwick Park. Their departure was felt by the horses, who missed the cheerful presence of the young ladies. (022-003)

## 性格特征

- **Kind to animals**: Treated the horses with gentleness and affection. (005-016)
- **Cheerful and playful**: Enjoyed riding and playing around the stables. (010-001)
- **Well-raised**: Reflected her father Squire Gordon's values of kindness and respect for animals.

## 主题意义

Miss Jessie represents the next generation of humane horse owners. Through her, Sewell shows that kindness to animals is taught — Squire Gordon's values are passed down to his daughters, who in turn treat the horses well. Her presence also adds warmth and domestic happiness to the Birtwick Park chapters, establishing the estate as the novel's moral and emotional high point.
