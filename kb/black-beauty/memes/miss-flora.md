# Miss Flora

One of [[Squire Gordon]]'s daughters at [[Birtwick Park]], a gentle young girl who, together with her sister [[Miss Jessie]], showed affection for the horses and represented the virtues of a kind household. (005-016)

## 角色概述

Miss Flora was the daughter of Squire Gordon, the kindly master of Birtwick Park. Along with her sister [[Miss Jessie]], she formed part of the Gordon family circle that made Birtwick such a pleasant home for both humans and horses.

The two sisters were known for their gentle treatment of the animals, often visiting the stables and showing kindness to [[Black Beauty]], [[Merrylegs]], and [[Ginger]]. (005-016)

## 故事经历

### 早期经历

Miss Flora appears early in the novel as part of the happy domestic scene at Birtwick Park. She and her sister were described as being "very good to me," according to Black Beauty's own testimony, indicating that the children of the household shared their parents' benevolent attitude toward animals. (005-016)

### 关键转折

Miss Flora's most memorable moment involves a minor accident — while playing with the horses, she or her sister was thrown and her arm was made to bleed. (005-016) This incident, rather than leading to punishment of the horse, was handled with the understanding and compassion characteristic of the Gordon household.

Later, when [[Black Beauty]] had fallen and injured his knees, Squire Gordon's anxious question — "Did you throw Miss Jessie or Miss Flora?" — revealed how deeply he trusted the horse and how central his daughters' safety was to his concerns. (010-006)

### 结局

When Squire Gordon was compelled to leave England for his health, Miss Flora and the entire Gordon family departed Birtwick Park, marking the end of Black Beauty's happiest period and his sale to [[Earlshall Park]].

## 性格特征

- **Gentle and affectionate**: Known for her kindness to the horses. (005-016)
- **Part of a virtuous household**: Embodied the compassion that defined the Gordon family.
- **Beloved by her father**: Squire Gordon's concern for her safety reflected deep paternal love. (010-006)

## 主题意义

Miss Flora, along with her sister Miss Jessie, represents the ideal of a compassionate household where kindness toward animals is passed from parents to children.

The Gordon daughters are not developed as individual personalities but serve as key indicators of the moral atmosphere at Birtwick Park — a place where even the youngest members of the family treated horses with gentleness and respect.

The brief mention of a riding accident handled without cruelty reinforces Sewell's contrast between the Gordon household and later, harsher environments where horses were punished rather than understood.
