# Mr. Bond

A farrier and horse-doctor who attended [[Black Beauty]] on two separate occasions, providing skilled medical care that saved the horse's life and restored him to health.

## 角色概述

Mr. Bond is a recurring veterinary figure in [[Black Beauty]]'s life, appearing at critical moments of injury and illness. (003-014) (020-001) He belongs to the novel's small but significant cast of competent professionals — men whose skill and diligence directly counterbalance the neglect and cruelty that Beauty endures elsewhere. Unlike the vain grooms or cruel owners, Bond simply does his job, and does it well.

## 故事经历

### 第一次治疗：狩猎事故之后

When young Squire Gordon's son fell from his horse during a hunt, riders were dispatched in all directions — to the doctor for the boy, and to Mr. Bond, the farrier, to attend to the injured horse, [[Rob Roy]]. (003-014) This early mention establishes Bond's role as the trusted expert called upon in equine emergencies. The hunt accident resulted in [[Rob Roy]]'s death, a sobering introduction to the fragility of a horse's life.

### 第二次治疗：蹄部感染

During [[Black Beauty]]'s time at [[Mr. Barry]]'s livery stable, the neglect of groom [[Alfred Smirk]] caused Beauty to develop a severe hoof infection. (020-001) Mr. Bond was called in and attended Beauty daily throughout his illness. (020-001) His treatment — which included ordering all soiled litter removed from Beauty's stall and the floor kept scrupulously clean — reveals a practitioner who understood that recovery depended as much on hygiene and care as on medicine. (033-016)

### 结局

Mr. Bond's competent care brought [[Black Beauty]] through his illness, and [[Mr. Barry]], disgusted by the groom's negligence, resolved to give up keeping a horse at a livery stable. Bond's role as a healer is quiet but essential: in a novel filled with suffering, he is one of the few characters who reliably makes things better.

## 性格特征

- Skilled and diligent, a consummate professional
- Trusted by the gentry for equine emergencies
- Represents the positive potential of human–horse relationships when grounded in knowledge and care
- Unobtrusive — appears only when needed, then fades from the narrative

## 主题意义

Mr. Bond belongs to a category of character that *Black Beauty* quietly celebrates: the competent professional who makes equine suffering his business to relieve. In a novel structured around the many ways humans fail horses, Bond and the few like him — [[John Manly]], [[Jerry Barker]], [[Mr. Thoroughgood]] — form a counter-tradition of care. Bond never gives speeches about animal welfare; he simply shows up, diagnoses correctly, and treats effectively. His presence in the novel argues implicitly that much equine suffering could be prevented not by grand moral reform but by the simple, consistent application of professional competence.
