# Dr. White

Dr. White was the physician called upon when Mrs. Gordon fell seriously ill, requiring [[John Manly]] to ride [[Black Beauty]] through the night to fetch him. His house was three miles from the inn where Beauty was given rest after the desperate ride. When John arrived, Dr. White responded promptly to the urgent summons. (019-002)(019-005)

## The Midnight Summons

The call came in the dead of night. Squire Gordon handed John a note with the urgent command: "Ride for your life — that is, for your mistress' life; there is not a moment to lose." (019-002) Beauty galloped eight miles through frosty moonlight, through village and wood, uphill and down, until they reached the sleeping town. The church clock struck three as they drew up at Dr. White's door. (019-005)

John rang the bell twice and knocked like thunder. A window was thrown up, and Dr. White — in his nightcap — put his head out. Upon hearing that Mrs. Gordon was dangerously ill, he dressed at once. (019-005)(019-007) His own horse was spent from the day's work, so John offered Beauty for the return journey, and the doctor rode back to Birtwick without delay. (019-009)(019-010)
