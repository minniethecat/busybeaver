# Mr. Clay

A livery stable keeper in London, briefly encountered during Black Beauty's time as a cab horse.

## 角色概述

Mr. Clay is a minor figure who runs a livery stable where horses are kept and hired out. He appears only briefly in the narrative, mentioned in connection with the livery stable operations. His role, though small, illustrates the commercial infrastructure of horse-keeping in Victorian London. (021-007)

## 故事经历

### 背景

Mr. Clay operates a livery stable, a business that boards horses for owners or rents them out to customers. Such establishments were common in Victorian cities for those who could not afford to keep their own stable. (021-009)

### 在书中的出现

Mr. Clay is encountered when someone arrives at his livery stable looking for him. The scene provides a glimpse into the working-class horse economy that forms the backdrop of Beauty's later chapters. (021-007) (021-009)

### 结局

Mr. Clay does not reappear after his brief mention. His role is purely contextual, serving to illustrate the network of horse-related businesses in Victorian London.

## 主题意义

Mr. Clay represents the commercial horse-keeping economy that existed alongside the private ownership model. His livery stable is part of the broader Victorian horse industry that Anna Sewell depicts throughout the novel, showing how horses moved between different types of ownership and service.
