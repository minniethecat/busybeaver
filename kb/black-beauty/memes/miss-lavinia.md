# Miss Lavinia

One of the three Blomefield sisters who gave [[Black Beauty]] his final, happy home. Her initial nervousness about riding a horse with broken knees gave way to trust, and her household became the place where Beauty's "troubles are all over."

## 角色概述

Miss Lavinia was the sister of [[Miss Blomefield]] and [[Miss Ellen]], living with them in a "pretty, low house, with a lawn and shrubbery" a mile or two from the village. (053-003) When [[Mr. Thoroughgood]] brought Black Beauty to them on trial, Miss Lavinia was the most cautious of the three sisters — but also the one whose eventual acceptance sealed Beauty's fate.

## 故事经历

### 初次见面的犹豫

When Mr. Thoroughgood arrived at the Blomefield house with Black Beauty, he was met by all three sisters. Miss Lavinia's first reaction was cautious: "she said that she should always be nervous in riding behind a horse that had once been down, as I might come down again, and if I did she should never get over the fright." (053-003)

Mr. Thoroughgood gently pushed back, explaining that "many first-rate horses have had their knees broken through the carelessness of their drivers without any fault of their own" — a description that fit Black Beauty's situation precisely. (053-004) Miss Blomefield, the eldest and stateliest sister, deferred to Lavinia's judgment: "If my sister Lavinia sees no objection we will accept your offer of a trial, with thanks." (053-005)

### 建立信任

Beauty was taken on trial, and [[Miss Ellen]] — the most enthusiastic of the sisters — was the first to drive him, finding him a good and safe horse. Over the following week, Beauty was driven every day, and his consistently steady behavior gradually won Miss Lavinia's confidence. "As I appeared to be quite safe, Miss Lavinia at last ventured out in the small close carriage." (053-019)

### 结局

Miss Lavinia's trial ride was the final test. It passed without incident, and the sisters decided to keep Black Beauty, calling him by his old name — a symbolic restoration of his identity. (053-019) It was in their household, with [[Joe Green]] as groom, that Beauty spent his final years, with the promise that he "shall never be sold." (053-022)

## 性格特征

- **谨慎但非固执**：Her initial hesitation came from genuine concern, not prejudice, and she was willing to be convinced by evidence
- **理性的判断力**：She trusted observation over first impressions, letting Beauty prove himself over a week of daily trials
- **善良**：Once convinced, she fully accepted Beauty into her home and agreed to the promise of permanent care

## 主题意义

Miss Lavinia's arc — from fear to trust — mirrors the reader's own journey through the novel. If Black Beauty's broken knees represent the visible scars of a hard life, Miss Lavinia's willingness to look past those scars represents the compassion that Sewell believed every horse deserved. Her cautious, evidence-based approach to judging Beauty — a week of daily trials rather than a snap decision — also models a thoughtful alternative to the impulsive buying and selling that caused so much suffering throughout the novel.
