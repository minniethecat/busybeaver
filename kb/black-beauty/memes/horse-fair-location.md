# Horse Fair

The horse fair is where Black Beauty is sold to Jerry Barker. It represents the commercial heart of the Victorian horse trade — a place where horses are examined, appraised, and bargained over, their fates decided by the character and motives of each buyer.

## 地点描述

The fair is a bustling, rough environment where dealers, buyers, and horses mingle. A salesman appraises Beauty for a potential buyer: "if you want him for cab work he's a bargain" (035-009). The transaction is swift: "The money was paid on the spot" (035-010). Beauty is led away to begin his new life as a cab horse.

## 在故事中的角色

The horse fair is a turning point in Beauty's life — the transition from gentleman's horse to working cab horse. The scene emphasizes the randomness of a horse's fate: a kind buyer means a good life; a cruel one means suffering. Beauty is fortunate to be bought by Jerry Barker, but the fair itself offers no guarantees.

## 原文引用

Beauty describes the spectacle of the horse fair:

> No doubt a horse fair is a very amusing place to those who have nothing to lose; at any rate, there is plenty to see.
>
> (035-001)

The fair reveals the full range of equine fortune:

> Long strings of young horses out of the country, fresh from the marshes; and droves of shaggy little Welsh ponies, no higher than Merrylegs; and hundreds of cart horses of all sorts, some of them with their long tails braided up and tied with scarlet cord; and a good many like myself, handsome and high-bred, but fallen into the middle class, through some accident or blemish, unsoundness of wind, or some other complaint.
>
> (035-002)
