# Highwood

A wooded area near [[Birtwick Park]], serving as a landmark along riding routes and a site for country sports.

## 概述

The Highwood lay beyond [[Birtwick Common|birtwick-common]] on one of the riding routes from Birtwick Park (006-002). It was also a spot where shooting parties hunted rabbits (006-007). The wood is mentioned only in passing but forms part of the rural landscape surrounding Squire Gordon's estate.


> Go by the common and the Highwood, and back by the watermill and the river.
>
> (006-002)

## 地理位置

The Highwood was situated beyond Birtwick Common, along a circular riding route that passed the common, the Highwood, the watermill, and the river before returning to Birtwick Park (006-002).

## 叙事功能

The Highwood illustrates how Anna Sewell uses landscape not as decoration but as narrative infrastructure. The wood, the common, the watermill, and the river form a mapped circuit — a geography the reader can follow. This precision gives the novel's world a tactile reality.

When a shooting party near the Highwood startles Beauty with a gunshot (006-007), the moment serves a dual purpose. It establishes Beauty's steady temperament — he startles but does not bolt — and it quietly registers the proximity of human sport to animal space. The gunshot that is recreation for men is a moment of fear for horses.
