# Joe Green

A young stable boy who began his career at [[Birtwick Park]] with a near-fatal mistake, but who later redeemed himself through diligence and became a skilled, trusted groom. (018-005) (053-013)

## 角色概述

Joe Green, sometimes called "Little Joe Green," joined the Birtwick Park staff as a very young boy. [[John Manly]] himself noted his youth, emphasizing the risk of entrusting a horse to someone so inexperienced. (018-006) Through guidance and experience, Joe grew into a capable horseman who genuinely cared for the animals in his charge. (021-001)

## 故事经历

### 早期经历

Joe first appeared at Birtwick Park as a child at the lodge, recommended by John Manly to help with the horses. (018-005) His youth and inexperience were immediately noted by the head groom. (018-006)

### 关键转折

Joe's most significant moment came early in his career, during [[Black Beauty]]'s serious illness. After a hard ride to fetch the doctor, Black Beauty was left hot and sweating. Joe, not knowing better, failed to properly cool the horse down and gave him cold water — a mistake that nearly proved fatal. Black Beauty's life was saved by [[John Manly]]'s intervention, but the incident became a defining lesson for young Joe. (021-001)

Under John Manly's patient instruction, Joe "went on very well" and "learned his business." (021-001) He developed into a competent groom who understood the importance of proper horse care.

### 结局

Years later, Joe Green reappeared in the novel's final chapter. By then a grown man and an experienced groom, he recognized the now-aged Black Beauty at a horse sale and was moved to tears. (053-013) The reunion was a joyful one — Black Beauty, who remembered Joe and bore him no grudge for the childhood mistake, "put my nose up to him" in recognition. (053-014) Joe's emotional response confirmed that the lesson he learned as a boy had shaped him into the kind of horseman Anna Sewell hoped to inspire.

## 性格特征

- **Eager to learn**: Despite his early mistake, Joe absorbed John Manly's teachings and became skilled. (021-001)
- **Kind-hearted**: His tearful reunion with Black Beauty years later showed genuine love for the horse. (053-013)
- **Remorseful**: He carried the memory of his near-fatal error and clearly learned from it.
- **Growth-minded**: Joe's arc from careless boy to responsible groom is one of the novel's few redemptive human stories.

## 关键引文

## 主题意义

Joe Green represents the novel's hope for the future. His arc from ignorant boy to compassionate groom embodies Sewell's educational mission — that with proper instruction, even those who begin with serious mistakes can become forces for good in the lives of horses. The reunion scene (053-013) is one of the novel's most emotionally resonant moments, demonstrating that kindness leaves a lasting mark on both horse and human. Joe's tears confirm that the novel's moral lessons are not merely intellectual but deeply felt.
