# Thomas Green

Thomas Green was the father of [[Joe Green]], a young stable boy at [[Birtwick Park]]. He came in to help [[John Manly]] when Beauty needed a draught of medicine after his near-fatal illness caused by Joe Green's mistake. (020-002)

## A Father's Plea

In the quiet of the stable, while Beauty lay feverish and near death, Thomas Green sat with John Manly on a bench in Merrylegs' old stall. The two men kept vigil through the night, waiting to see if the medicine would take effect. (020-002)

After a long silence, Thomas spoke — not about the horse, but about his son. Joe, he said, was "quite broken-hearted; he can't eat his meals, and he can't smile." The boy knew it was his fault, done in ignorance, and feared that if Beauty died no one would ever speak to him again. (020-004) Thomas asked John, with a father's gentle persistence, to say a kind word to the boy.

His plea worked. John, though still sore over Beauty's suffering, agreed to give Joe a good word — provided Beauty survived the night. (020-005) The conversation that followed, in which John and Tom discussed the terrible cost of ignorance, revealed both men to be thoughtful and compassionate. (020-008)(020-009)
