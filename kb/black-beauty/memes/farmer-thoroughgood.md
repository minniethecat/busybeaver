# Thoroughgood Farm

Thoroughgood Farm is the country property of Farmer Thoroughgood and his grandson Willie, where Black Beauty spends his final years in peaceful retirement. It represents the novel's closing argument that kindness and redemption remain possible even after great suffering.

## 地点描述

The farm is modest but well-kept. Farmer Thoroughgood is knowledgeable about horses and recognizes Beauty's quality despite his broken-down condition: "he might have been anything when he was young; look at his nostrils and his ears, the shape of his neck and shoulder; there's a deal of breeding about that horse" (052-005).

## 在故事中的角色

The farm is the setting for the novel's redemptive conclusion. Willie, the farmer's grandson, pleads for Beauty's purchase: "Poor old fellow! see, grandpapa, how well he understands kindness. Could not you buy him and make him young again?" (052-006). Under the farmer's care, Beauty recovers his health and finds peace — a quiet affirmation that kindness can still be found.
