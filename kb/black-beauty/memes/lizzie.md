# Lizzie

Lizzie is a "bright bay mare" (026-002) at [[Earlshall Park]], praised for her beauty but known for her nervous temperament. Her bolting with [[Lady Anne]] creates one of the most dangerous episodes in Black Beauty, illustrating the risks of mismatching horse and rider.

## 角色概述

Lizzie is a handsome horse — "a bright bay" (026-002) — who attracts praise from the gentlemen at Earlshall. But beneath her attractive exterior is a high-strung, easily startled animal. [[Black Beauty]] observes the crucial difference between Lizzie and [[Ginger]]: while Ginger's spirit has been shaped by mistreatment, Lizzie's nervousness appears innate, making her unpredictable even under kind handling.

## 故事经历

### 在 Earlshall

Lizzie is regularly ridden at Earlshall alongside Ginger and Black Beauty. A gentleman "always rode Lizzie, and praised her so much" (026-003), contributing to Lady Anne's desire to try the mare herself. Lizzie's reputation as "charming" (026-005) obscures the warning signs of her temperament.

### Lady Anne 的骑行事故

Lady Anne insists on riding Lizzie despite her cousin's concerns. The ride begins calmly: "Lizzie was standing quietly by the side" (026-013) when the riders pause to enjoy a view. But "it was so sudden" (026-013) — something startles Lizzie, and she bolts at a gallop. Lady Anne is "nearly unseated" (026-013) as Lizzie tears through the countryside.

The chase is desperate. The soft ground "had very much lessened Lizzie's speed" (026-015), giving pursuers hope of catching her. But "with scarcely a pause Lizzie took the leap, stumbled among the rough ground" (026-017), and Lady Anne is thrown. Lizzie continues running wild, eventually spotted by turf-cutters who see "Lizzie running wild without a rider" (026-019).

### 事故之后

Lizzie is eventually caught, but the damage is done — Lady Anne lies injured. The accident permanently marks Lizzie as a dangerous horse, though the novel suggests her behavior stems from temperament rather than malice.

## 性格特征

- **Nervous** — easily startled and prone to bolting
- **Beautiful** — praised for her bright bay appearance
- **Unpredictable** — calm one moment, panicked the next
- **High-strung** — unlike Ginger, her nervousness is innate rather than the result of mistreatment

## 主题意义

Lizzie represents a subtle point in Sewell's argument about animal welfare: not all dangerous behavior in horses comes from cruelty. Her innate nervousness shows that some horses require more experienced handling regardless of how kindly they are treated. The accident also demonstrates the consequences of ignoring warnings — Lady Anne was cautioned about Lizzie but chose to ride her anyway — making this a cautionary tale about matching horse to rider.
