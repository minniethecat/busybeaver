# Captain

A large-boned cab horse who once served as a war horse in the Crimean War. Now old, he works alongside [[Black Beauty]] in [[Jerry Barker]]'s London cab service. (036-001)

## 角色概述

Captain was a "large-boned animal" who had grown old in service. (036-001) Despite his age and the hardships of cab work, Captain maintained a dignified and philosophical outlook. He became [[Black Beauty]]'s closest friend during their time in the London cab stables. (047-001)

## 故事经历

### 早期经历

Captain began his career as a war horse, trained for cavalry service in the Crimean War. He was "broken in and trained for an army horse" and experienced the chaos and danger of battle firsthand. (037-001) His war experiences gave him a unique perspective on human nature and suffering.

### 关键转折

After his military service, Captain ended up pulling a cab in London — a harsh fall from the glory of cavalry service. He worked alongside Black Beauty under [[Jerry Barker]], one of the kindest cab owners in London. (036-005) Captain would pause in his storytelling, drawing "a long breath" as he recalled the horrors of war. (037-008)

Captain's friendship with Black Beauty was a source of comfort during the difficult London period. The two old horses found solace in each other's company. (047-001) A younger cab horse named Jack sometimes brought humor to their days. (038-003)

### 结局

Captain's end came tragically. While pulling the cab, he was "dragged down" when the cab was thrown over, suffering severe injuries. (047-003) Though he initially seemed to recover, his health declined. When Black Beauty returned from being fitted with new shoes, Captain was gone — either sold or dead. (047-013) His loss was deeply felt by the entire household.

## 性格特征

- **Dignified**: Despite his fall from war horse to cab horse, Captain maintained his composure and pride. (036-001)
- **Stoic**: He endured the hardships of war and the cruelty of London streets without bitterness.
- **Wise**: His war experiences gave him deep insight into human courage and folly. (037-008)
- **Loyal friend**: Formed a strong bond with Black Beauty, becoming his closest companion in London. (047-001)

## 主题意义

Captain's story arc — from war horse to cab horse to tragic death — is one of the novel's most poignant narratives. It illustrates the fate of military horses after their service: no pension, no retirement, just continued labor until they drop. Captain's quiet dignity in the face of this decline embodies Sewell's message about the nobility of working horses and the ingratitude of the society that exploits them. His death from a cab accident also highlights the daily dangers faced by urban working horses in Victorian London.
