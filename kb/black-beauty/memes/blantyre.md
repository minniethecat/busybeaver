# Blantyre

Blantyre was a gentleman staying at [[Earlshall Park]] who frequently rode [[Lizzie]], a spirited chestnut mare, and accompanied [[Lady Anne]] on her rides. He appears in the chapter "The Lady Anne, or a Runaway Horse," where he tries to prevent the riding accident that left Lady Anne injured. (026-003)

## 角色概述

Blantyre is introduced as "a gentleman of the name of Blantyre staying at the hall." He always rode Lizzie and praised her so highly that Lady Anne was persuaded to try the mare herself. When the riding party was about to set out, Blantyre seemed genuinely uneasy about Lady Anne riding Lizzie, warning "I am sure she is too spirited for you." But Lady Anne, confident in her horsemanship, insisted. (026-003)

## 故事经历

### 试图阻止事故

When Lady Anne ordered the side-saddle put on Lizzie, Blantyre was uneasy but deferred to her authority. As they rode, he positioned himself to assist — at the gate, he dismounted to open it for her. Lady Anne, however, insisted on opening it herself from horseback. (026-010)

> Blantyre alighted at the gate, and was going to open it for Lady Anne, but she said, "I will wait, but don't get off."
>
> (026-010)

When Lizzie bolted moments later, Blantyre acted instantly:

> Scarcely drawing the rein, Blantyre shouted, "Which way?" and hearing "To the common!" he gave Beauty his head and galloped after Lizzie.
>
> (026-014)

He rode desperately after the runaway horse, giving Beauty his head on the highroad and then showing skilled horsemanship through the rough ground at the common, "nerving the horse" to navigate the rabbit holes and gravel pits that made the chase so dangerous. (026-016)

### 事故之后

After Lady Anne was thrown, Blantyre helped carry her to safety and sent for help. The accident was not his fault — he had warned against the ride, offered to open the gate, and pursued Lizzie with courage and skill. The blame fell squarely on the spirited mare, the rough ground, and Lady Anne's overconfidence.

## 性格特征

- **Cautious and prudent**: He warned Lady Anne that Lizzie was too spirited for her
- **Chivalrous**: He dismounted to open the gate and risked his own neck chasing the runaway
- **Skilled rider**: His pursuit through dangerous terrain shows real horsemanship
- **Deferential to rank**: Despite his misgivings, he could not overrule a lady of the household
