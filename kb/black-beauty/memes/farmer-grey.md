# Farmer Grey

The farmer who bred [[Black Beauty]] and was his first master. His kind treatment during Beauty's early years laid the foundation for the horse's gentle nature. (006-019)

## 角色概述

Farmer Grey raised Black Beauty from a foal on his farm, where Beauty spent his first four years in green meadows alongside his mother, Duchess. (006-019) He was a respected figure in the community and a friend of [[Squire Gordon]], with whom he "had worked together" on matters of horse breeding and local affairs. (012-002)

## 故事经历

### 早期经历

Farmer Grey's farm was the idyllic setting of Black Beauty's earliest memories — "the green pleasant meadow" that Beauty would recall throughout his life as a standard of happiness. (027-009) He bred Duchess, Beauty's mother, and ensured the young colt received gentle handling from the start.

### 关键转折

When Black Beauty reached four years old, Farmer Grey sold him to Squire Gordon at Birtwick Park. This was not an act of indifference but a natural progression — the young horse was ready for training and a more active life. The fact that Farmer Grey and Squire Gordon were friends ensured a good home.

### 结局

Black Beauty remembered Farmer Grey with gratitude throughout his life, counting him alongside Squire Gordon as one of the best masters a horse could have. (031-022)

## 性格特征

- **Kind and gentle**: Raised horses with patience and kindness, creating well-tempered animals.
- **Trusted breeder**: His horses, including Duchess and Black Beauty, were sought after by quality owners.
- **Community-minded**: Worked cooperatively with Squire Gordon on local matters. (012-002)

## 主题意义

Farmer Grey represents the ideal of humane animal husbandry. His kind, natural approach to raising horses — allowing them to run free in meadows with their mothers — contrasts sharply with the harsh training methods and urban exploitation that Black Beauty later encounters. His brief but foundational role establishes the novel's baseline for what good treatment looks like.
