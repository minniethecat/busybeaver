# Jerry's New Year

On New Year's Eve, after a near-accident with a drunken fare, [[Jerry Barker]] makes a life-changing resolution: to leave the London cab trade and move his family to the country — a decision that sets [[Black Beauty]] on the path to his final home.

## 概述

After years of honest labor on the London cab stand, Jerry Barker's health was declining. On New Year's Eve, he was called to take a group of rowdy, drunken men who demanded dangerous speed through icy streets.

When they reached their destination and the men refused to pay, Jerry realized the toll that cab work was taking on his health, his family, and his horses (045-012). That night, he resolved to accept an offer from a gentleman who had long admired him: a position in the country that would allow him to leave the cab trade forever.

## 背景

Jerry had been a cab driver for many years. He was respected by his regular customers for his honesty, his refusal to work Sundays, and the excellent condition of his horses.

But the London cab trade was grueling — long hours in all weather, exposure to abusive customers, and the constant physical strain were wearing Jerry down. His wife [[Polly Barker]] and children [[Harry Barker|Harry]] and [[Dolly Barker|Dolly]] had long hoped he would find a healthier occupation.

## 后果与影响

Jerry's decision to leave the trade was a turning point for the entire Barker family — and for Beauty. Jerry moved his family to the country, where he took a position as a coachman for a gentleman.

Beauty, however, could not accompany them: he was sold to a corn dealer and then to a cab owner who worked him hard (047-001). This period of hardship — Beauty's lowest point — would end only when [[Mr. Thoroughgood]] noticed him at a horse fair and bought him, leading to Beauty's final happy retirement at [[Thoroughgood Farm]] (048-020).

## 主题意义

"Jerry's New Year" closes the London cab horse arc of the novel with a note of hard-won hope. Jerry is rewarded for his integrity — the gentleman who offers him the country position has observed his character over years of service.

But the episode also reminds readers that a working man's improvement in circumstances does not automatically benefit his animals: Beauty is sold on, entering the worst period of his life before his final rescue.

The New Year's Eve timing is symbolic — it is a moment of resolution and renewal, the turning of a calendar page that mirrors the turning of Beauty's fortunes from the cab stand toward his eventual happy ending.
