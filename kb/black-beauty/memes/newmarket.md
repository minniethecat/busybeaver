# Newmarket

A historic racecourse town in Suffolk, England, mentioned in *Black Beauty* as the site of prestigious horse races. [[Black Beauty]]'s grandfather won the cup at the Newmarket races for two consecutive years.

## 概述

Newmarket is referenced twice in the novel as a benchmark of equine excellence. Beauty's mother tells him that his grandfather "won the cup two years at the Newmarket races" and that his grandmother "had the sweetest temper of any horse I ever knew" (002-006).

Later, during the midnight ride to fetch the doctor, the narrator reflects that "not even the grandfather, who won the race at Newmarket, could have gone faster" (019-005). Newmarket thus serves as a symbolic reference point for thoroughbred quality and speed.

## 地理位置

Newmarket is a real town in Suffolk, England, approximately 65 miles north of London. It has been the historic center of British horse racing since the 17th century.

## 象征意义

Newmarket functions in the novel as more than a geographical reference — it is a symbol of inherited excellence. Beauty carries the bloodline of a Newmarket champion. The two invocations of the name bracket his identity as a thoroughbred.

Yet Sewell's use of Newmarket is quietly subversive. The grandfather's victories establish pedigree, but the novel values temperament over speed. Beauty's mother pairs the Newmarket cups with the observation that his grandmother had "the sweetest temper of any horse I ever knew" (002-006). It is the sweet temper, not the racing blood, that carries Beauty through his trials.
