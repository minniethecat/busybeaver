# Rory's Accident

Rory's accident is a violent gig collision in Chapter 29 ("Cockneys") of [[Black Beauty]] that leaves the gentle paired horse with a torn-open chest, a long convalescence, and a final fate in coal-carting. The incident is Anna Sewell's sharp illustration of the damage caused by incompetent, indifferent drivers.

## 背景

Rory was the horse that usually partnered [[Black Beauty]] for pair work at the Bath livery stables — "a good honest fellow he was" (031-007). He and Beauty were returning home at twilight after a pleasant spring day with their own considerate driver.


> There was poor Rory with his flesh torn open and his body cut all over by the shafts of the other gig.
>
> (031-008)

## 事件经过

The road made a sharp left turn, with a high hedge blocking visibility. Beauty and Rory's driver kept to the correct side, but another gig came rapidly down the hill toward them. The oncoming driver was "one of those random, ignorant fellows, who don't even know which is their own side of the road" (031-008). He made no attempt to pull over before reaching the corner.

When the two vehicles met, Beauty was spared by the hedge. Rory, on the left side of the pole with no shaft to protect him, took the full impact: "The gig shaft ran right into the chest, making him stagger back with a cry that I shall never forget" (031-007).

The aftermath was gruesome: "there was poor Rory with his flesh torn open and bleeding, and the blood streaming down. They said if it had been a little more to one side it would have killed him" (031-008).

## 后果与影响

Rory's wound took a long time to heal. Once recovered, he was sold for coal-carting — one of the hardest, most punishing forms of horse labour. Beauty's reflection on the coal-carting horses he later saw — negotiating steep hills with "a heavily loaded two-wheel cart behind him, on which no brake could be placed" (031-009) — makes clear that Rory's fate after the accident was grim.

Rory's injury also had a ripple effect: Beauty was partnered with [[Peggy]] the short-legged mare, whose anxious shuffling gait revealed her own history of mistreatment.

## 涉及角色

- [[Black Beauty]] — eyewitness, paired with Rory during the collision
- **Rory** — the injured horse, "a good honest fellow"
- **The ignorant driver** — a random, careless Cockney who caused the crash
- **The considerate driver** — Beauty and Rory's own driver, who drove correctly

## 主题意义

Rory's accident is a case study in [[Anna Sewell]]'s critique of incompetent horsemanship. The responsible driver did nothing wrong; the accident was entirely caused by ignorance and indifference. The chapter title "Cockneys" frames the problem as one of urban drivers who lack the knowledge and care that country horsemen possess.

The injury also destroys a good horse's useful life and sends him into the coal-carting trade — a downward mobility pattern that runs through the novel.
