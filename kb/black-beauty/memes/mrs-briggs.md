# Mrs. Briggs

The wife of Mr. Briggs, a regular client of Jerry Barker's cab service whose steadfast preference for Jerry's careful driving affirms the reward of honest and humane work.

## 角色概述

Mrs. Briggs is the wife of Mr. Briggs, a gentleman client of Jerry Barker. She features in a subplot about Jerry's Sunday cab work, where her employer's desire for her to be driven by Jerry specifically — and her own willingness to pay generously for the service — illustrates how a reputation for careful, humane driving can earn loyalty and financial reward. (039-002) (039-005)

## 故事经历

### 背景

Mrs. Briggs first appears in connection with an arrangement for her to be driven regularly to church on Sundays. Mr. Briggs approaches Jerry to secure his services, explaining that Mrs. Briggs has a strong preference for Jerry's driving over other cabmen. This preference speaks to Jerry's reputation for careful and considerate handling of both horse and passenger. (039-002)

### 关键转折

The arrangement becomes a moral dilemma for Jerry. Driving Mrs. Briggs to church on Sundays would mean working on the day of rest — a practice Jerry has strong principles against. Mr. Briggs offers financial incentives, noting that Mrs. Briggs would give a sovereign every Sunday for the service. Jerry's wife Polly and his friend Governor Grant weigh in on the decision, and ultimately Jerry agrees to take the job, reasoning that the horse would benefit from the extra rest during the rest of the week. (039-005) (039-014) (039-015) (039-017)

### 结局

The arrangement proves successful. Mrs. Briggs sends her servant to confirm the booking, and she becomes a regular and valued client. After this, she consistently requests Jerry's cab, demonstrating that quality service — driving that respects both horse and passenger — earns lasting customer loyalty. (040-002) (040-006)

## 性格特征

- Discerning in her choice of transport, preferring careful and humane driving
- Generous with payment, willing to compensate Jerry well for his Sunday service
- Loyal as a client, returning to Jerry's cab consistently
- A background figure whose preferences shape the decisions of the men around her

## 主题意义

Mrs. Briggs's role, though small, reinforces a key theme of the novel: that honest, humane work is ultimately rewarded. Her preference for Jerry is not based on price or speed but on the quality of his driving — his care for his horse, his punctuality, and his respectful manner. This suggests that a reputation for ethical conduct has tangible value in the marketplace, a quiet but pointed argument for the economic viability of humane treatment of working animals.
