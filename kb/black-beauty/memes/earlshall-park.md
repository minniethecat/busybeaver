# Earlshall Park

Earlshall Park is the estate where Black Beauty and Ginger are sent after Squire Gordon must sell his horses. Unlike Birtwick Park, where kindness governs, Earlshall prioritizes fashion over welfare — making it the novel's case study in how good horses are ruined by bad systems.

## 地点描述

At Earlshall, the stables are well-appointed but the ethos is wrong. The lady of the house insists on the fashionable bearing rein, and the coachman York, though not cruel by nature, enforces it. "Day by day, hole by hole, our bearing reins were shortened, and instead of looking forward with pleasure to having my harness put on, I began to dread it" (024-020).

## 在故事中的角色

Earlshall demonstrates that physical facilities are not enough — the character of the people determines a horse's welfare. The bearing rein, tightened progressively at the lady's insistence, drives Ginger to rebellion and injury, leading to her sale and eventual death. Beauty too suffers, and his dread of the harness marks a psychological turning point.
