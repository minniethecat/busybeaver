# Horse Trade

The horse trade was the commercial system through which horses were bought, sold, and circulated in Victorian England. In *Black Beauty*, the narrator passes through many hands — kind and cruel — revealing the horse trade as a marketplace where an animal's fate depends on the character and motives of each successive buyer.

## 定义

The horse trade encompassed breeders, dealers, auctioneers, fair vendors, and private sellers. Horses changed hands at horse fairs, through dealers, at auctions, and in private sales. A horse might be sold for many reasons: a change in the owner's circumstances, dissatisfaction with the horse's performance, or simply profit. Dealers often disguised a horse's faults to secure a higher price, and many horses passed rapidly through multiple owners, each transaction degrading their condition.

## 在书中的体现

Black Beauty is sold multiple times throughout the narrative — from Squire Gordon to Earlshall, then to a livery stable, a horse fair, a cab owner, and finally to a kind farmer. Each sale marks a change in fortune: "The money was paid on the spot" (035-010) at the horse fair where Beauty is purchased by Jerry Barker, one of the few transactions that leads to good care.

Ginger describes being sold by a dealer after being driven without a check-rein: the dealer "sold me as a perfectly quiet horse" (009-006), concealing the circumstances that made her appear docile. Such deception was common practice.

### 关键场景

**The horse fair.** Beauty's experience at the horse fair (035-009) reveals the rough, commercial nature of horse trading. Horses are examined, appraised, and bargained over — treated as commodities rather than living beings.

**Dealers and their methods.** Throughout the novel, horse dealers appear as ambivalent figures. Some are openly dishonest; others, like the one who sold Ginger, are merely indifferent to the horse's welfare once the sale is complete.

**The final sale.** Beauty's last sale — to Farmer Thoroughgood and his grandson — is the novel's redemptive conclusion. The farmer recognizes Beauty's quality despite his poor condition and restores him to health, demonstrating that the horse trade can also be a channel for kindness.
