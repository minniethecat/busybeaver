# Jerry Barker

Jerry Barker is a London cab driver who purchases Black Beauty at a horse fair and gives him several years of kind and dignified service. More than any other character, Jerry articulates the novel's ethical philosophy: that witnessing cruelty without intervening makes one complicit.

## 角色概述

Jerry is a workingman of modest means but firm principles. He maintains a six-days' license for his cab, refusing to work on Sundays so that both he and his horses may rest. His wife Polly is his equal in principle: "I would not have you a seven-days' license for all the money in London" (039-015). Together they create a household where kindness governs every decision.

## 故事经历

### 早期经历

Jerry purchases Black Beauty at a horse fair, where a salesman notes: "if you want him for cab work he's a bargain" (035-009). Jerry gives Beauty the name "Jack" and treats him with consistent care, grooming him well and providing "as much change of food as he could" (036-015).

### 关键转折

When a wealthy customer, Mrs. Briggs, offers Jerry a regular Sunday fare, he refuses on principle: "I have only taken out a six-days' license, and therefore I could not take a fare on a Sunday" (039-003). His wife supports him, and the decision costs him business — but he holds firm. His fellow driver Truman articulates the broader principle: "If workingmen don't stick to their Sunday, they'll soon have none left; it is every man's right and every beast's right." (039-020)

Jerry's moral doctrine is stated most clearly after intervening to stop a carter from beating his horses: "If we see cruelty or wrong that we have the power to stop, and do nothing, we make ourselves sharers in the guilt." (041-017)

### 结局

Jerry eventually falls ill from the harsh conditions of cab work and is advised by his doctor to move to the country. He sells his cab and horses, finding Beauty a kind final home.

## 性格特征

- **Principled** — adheres to his values even at economic cost
- **Kind** — treats both his family and his horses with consistent gentleness
- **Brave** — willing to intervene when he witnesses cruelty
- **Religious without hypocrisy** — observes Sunday as both religious duty and practical rest

## 主题意义

Jerry Barker is Sewell's ideal workingman — proof that kindness is not a luxury of the wealthy but a choice available to everyone. His doctrine of intervention (041-017) transforms animal welfare from a private virtue into a public responsibility.
