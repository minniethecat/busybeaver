# Polly Barker

Polly Barker is the wife of cab driver [[Jerry Barker]] and mother of [[Harry Barker]] and Dolly Barker. She appears throughout Black Beauty's years as a London cab horse, embodying the same kindness and moral firmness as her husband.

## 角色概述

Polly is Jerry's equal partner in both household and principle. On first meeting Black Beauty, she and her daughter Dolly "came into the yard to see me and make friends" (036-002), treating the new cab horse as a member of the family. When a customer urges Jerry to work on Sunday, Polly firmly supports his refusal: "I would not have you a seven-days' license for all the money in London" (039-015).

## 故事经历

### 日常照料

Polly regularly tends to the horses alongside her domestic duties. She "used to come in the evening to look at the horses" (038-003) when Jerry was cleaning them, and brings food to Jerry at the cab stand. Beauty notes that "Polly, his wife, was just as good a match as a man could have" (036-001).

### 周日原则

When Mrs. Briggs presses Jerry to work on Sunday, Polly stands with him against the financial temptation. Though "she had begun to cry" (039-016) at the thought of losing business, Jerry reassures her, and she accepts the sacrifice without complaint.

### 结局

Polly remains steadfast through Jerry's illness, caring for him at home while Harry manages the stable. Her resilience helps sustain the family through their most difficult period.

## 性格特征

- **Kind-hearted** — treats both family and horses with consistent warmth
- **Principled** — supports Jerry's moral stands even under economic pressure
- **Resilient** — holds the household together during Jerry's illness
- **Maternal** — nurturing toward her children and the horses alike

## 主题意义

Polly embodies the role of women in Sewell's moral vision — not as passive dependents but as active partners whose principles shape the household economy. Her support of Jerry's Sunday observance demonstrates that moral decisions are made in families, not by individuals alone.
