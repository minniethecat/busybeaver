# Justice

A roan cob who lived in the stable at [[Birtwick Park]], known for his calm temperament and thoughtful contributions to the horses' discussions about animal welfare and human behavior. (006-025) (011-016)

## 角色概述

Justice was a roan cob — a sturdy, practical type of horse — who shared the Birtwick Park stable with [[Black Beauty]], [[Ginger]], and [[Merrylegs]]. He was distinguished by his calm, measured way of speaking and his willingness to think through issues rather than react emotionally. His name itself suggested the quality that defined him: a steady, fair-minded perspective on the world. (011-016)

## 故事经历

### 早期经历

Justice was established at Birtwick Park before Black Beauty's arrival, stabled alongside him from the beginning. (006-025) He was a riding horse, used for carrying messages and transporting members of the household on shorter journeys.

### 关键转折

Justice participated in the stable's famous discussions about animal treatment. When the horses debated whether humans had the right to control animals, Justice offered his characteristic measured perspective: "They are supposed," he said in his calm way, to govern us for our own good and theirs. (011-016) His moderate tone stood in contrast to [[Ginger]]'s fiery indignation at human cruelty.

Justice played a practical role in the household's operations as well. On at least one occasion, he was sent out by [[James Howard]] on an errand toward the woods. (013-016) He also pulled the luggage cart when [[John Manly]] needed to transport goods. (021-001)

### 结局

When the Gordon family departed Birtwick Park and the household was broken up, Justice's fate — like that of most horses sold from the estate — is not explicitly recorded. His quiet dignity suggests he would have adapted to whatever new situation he found himself in.

## 性格特征

- **Calm and measured**: Known for his steady, unflappable temperament. (011-016)
- **Philosophical**: Engaged thoughtfully in the stable debates about animal welfare.
- **Practical**: Served reliably as a riding horse and cart horse. (013-016) (021-001)
- **Fair-minded**: His name reflected his balanced perspective on human-horse relations.

## 主题意义

Justice represents the moderate, reasoned voice in the novel's debates about animal welfare. Unlike [[Ginger]], whose suffering fueled righteous anger, or [[Merrylegs]], whose charm deflected difficult questions, Justice approached the moral questions of human dominion over animals with measured reflection. His name is pointed — Sewell suggests that true justice in human-animal relations lies not in extremes of rebellion or submission but in the kind of fair-minded stewardship that the Gordon household exemplified. Justice the horse embodies what Justice the principle requires: calm, consistent, proportionate treatment.
