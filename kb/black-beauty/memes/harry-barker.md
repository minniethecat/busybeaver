# Harry Barker

Harry Barker is the son of [[Jerry Barker]] and [[Polly Barker]], and brother of [[Dolly Barker]]. He appears throughout the London cab chapters as a diligent boy who inherits his parents' kindness toward horses.

## 角色概述

Harry is "as clever at stable-work as an old ostler" (038-003), helping his father groom and feed the horses after school. He treats Black Beauty with consistent gentleness: "Harry had been helping his father since the first day when he came into the yard to see me and make friends" (036-002).

## 故事经历

### 日常帮助

Harry assists Jerry with daily stable tasks — feeding, grooming, and cleaning. On Beauty's first day in the cab, "Harry came in after school to feed me and give me water" (036-005). His competence at stable work is noted early: he is as capable as a professional ostler despite his youth.

### 父亲生病期间

When Jerry falls seriously ill, Harry becomes the family's main support: "he cleaned us and fed us, and swept out the stable" (048-012). He manages the horses alone, reporting to the doctor on his father's condition (048-015, 048-017). Despite the strain, he maintains "a hopeful way of looking at things" (048-022).

### 品格考验

Harry demonstrates his father's moral values when he confronts a "ragamuffin" boy mistreating a horse, then rejects his own initial impulse toward violence, reflecting: "I am ashamed of myself for being angry" (045-017).

## 性格特征

- **Diligent** — takes his stable duties seriously from a young age
- **Kind** — treats horses gently, reflecting his upbringing
- **Responsible** — steps up during family crisis
- **Self-reflective** — capable of examining his own anger and correcting it

## 主题意义

Harry represents the transmission of moral values across generations. His kindness toward horses is not innate but learned from his parents, demonstrating Sewell's belief that ethical treatment of animals is taught through example. His self-correction after anger shows that moral growth is an active, conscious process.
