Northumberland, the northernmost county of [[england|England]], appears in the industrial biography of the period primarily through the patronage of the Duke of Northumberland, who took an active interest in mechanical invention and engineering.

## The Duke of Northumberland and Invention

The Duke of Northumberland was known as a patron of mechanical ingenuity. He commissioned the construction of a set of Northumberland bagpipes, an elaborate musical automaton that an ingenious craftsman finished to his specification. (041-197) The Duke, being a great admirer of mechanical skill, supported and encouraged inventive work on his estate. (042-143)

## Industrial Connections

While Northumberland itself was not a primary centre of the iron and engineering trades described in [[Samuel Smiles]]'s account, it lay adjacent to the industrial districts of [[Newcastle upon Tyne]] and the Durham coalfield. The Duke of Northumberland's patronage of engineering reflected the broader eighteenth-century aristocratic interest in mechanical arts that helped fuel the [[Industrial Revolution]].
