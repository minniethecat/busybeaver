# Bryan Donkin

Bryan Donkin (1768–1855) was an English engineer and inventor whose work spanned paper-making machinery, printing, and coinage. He was associated with the inventions of Fourdrinier and Donkin, and his engineering firm became one of the most respected in [[london|London]]. (034-095)

## 职业生涯

### 造纸机

Donkin's most notable contribution was in the development of the Fourdrinier paper-making machine, which mechanised the production of continuous paper — a transformative innovation for the printing and publishing industries. The inventions of Fourdrinier and Donkin in this area established the foundation of modern paper manufacturing. (034-095)

### 皇家铸币厂与精密工程

Donkin also worked with the Royal Mint, applying his engineering skill to the machinery of coin production. His reputation for precision and reliability brought him commissions from government and industry alike. (040-101) In 1806, he worked in conjunction with others on machinery for lifting, demonstrating the breadth of his engineering capabilities. (041-119)

### 印刷与计算

Subsequent improvements by the Messrs. Donkin extended into printing machinery, and his firm also contributed to the construction of calculating machines. The Scheutz calculating machine of Stockholm was referred to in connection with Donkin's workshop, indicating the high regard in which his precision engineering was held. (045-032) (045-022)

## 影响与评价

Donkin's career exemplified the versatility of the early nineteenth-century engineer — moving between paper-making, minting, and precision machinery with equal facility. His firm continued to be a leading name in [[london|London]] engineering long after his death.
