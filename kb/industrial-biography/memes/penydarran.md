# Penydarran

Penydarran was one of the four great ironworks of the [[merthyr-tydfil|Merthyr Tydfil]] district in [[south-wales|South Wales]], alongside [[cyfarthfa-ironworks|Cyfarthfa]], [[dowlais-ironworks|Dowlais]], and Plymouth. Established by [[samuel-homfray|Samuel Homfray]], Penydarran played a significant role in the early development of steam locomotion and the Welsh [[iron-industry|iron trade]]. (022-083) (023-127)

## Industrial Significance

Under [[samuel-homfray|[[samuel-homfray|Samuel Homfray]]'s]] direction, Penydarran became a centre of innovation as well as production. Homfray was a keen promoter of steam power and commissioned [[richard-trevithick|Richard Trevithick]] to build the first [[locomotive|steam locomotive]] to run on rails, which was tested at Penydarran in 1804. This experiment, though not a commercial success at the time, was a landmark in the history of the [[locomotive|railway locomotive]]. (023-154)

> "In connection with [[samuel-homfray|Mr. Homfray]] of the Penydarran Works, he planned and constructed the first locomotive that ever ran on a railway." (023-154)

## Role in the Welsh Iron Trade

Penydarran was part of the extraordinary concentration of ironworks in the Merthyr district that made [[south-wales|South Wales]] the dominant iron-producing region of Britain in the early 19th century. Together with Cyfarthfa, [[dowlais-ironworks|Dowlais]], and Plymouth, Penydarran turned out thousands of tons of pig and [[wrought-iron|bar iron]] annually, shipped to markets across Britain and the world. (023-127)

## Key Figures

- [[samuel-homfray|Samuel Homfray]] — founder and proprietor of Penydarran
- [[richard-trevithick|Richard Trevithick]] — built the first rail locomotive at Penydarran (023-154)
