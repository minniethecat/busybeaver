The **House of Commons** was the elected lower house of the British Parliament and the principal legislative body governing industrial Britain. Throughout the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]], its committees and debates shaped the development of iron manufacturing, patent policy, and naval procurement.

## [[iron-industry|Iron Industry]] Oversight

The Journals of the House of Commons record extensive engagement with the iron industry. As early as June 1642, the Commons addressed matters of iron supply during the Civil War period (012-001), and on 2 November 1652 recorded deliberations on [[iron-industry|iron manufacture]] (012-009). A committee inquiry of July 1648 dealt with ironworks and ordnance (014-121), demonstrating the House's long-standing oversight of strategic metal production.

In the late eighteenth century, [[[john-wilkinson|John Wilkinson]]](/john-wilkinson) reported to a House of Commons committee that he was making ten thousand tons of bar-iron yearly (023-144), and a subsequent Commons committee report confirmed the quality of British iron production (024-070).

## Debates on Industrial Britain

A member speaking in the House of Commons observed that the nation's industrial strength "grow[s] out of the skilled labour of the men working in metals. They are at the foundation of our manufacturing supremacy" (056-091). This 1862 debate (057-042) captured the Commons' recognition of iron workers as central to British economic power.
