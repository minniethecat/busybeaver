# Richard Reynolds

Richard Reynolds (1735–1816) was an ironmaster and partner at the [[coalbrookdale-company|Coalbrookdale Company]], where he made significant contributions to the iron industry, including the early use of iron rails and improvements in iron manufacturing quality. (016-076)

## 早年生活

Richard Reynolds was born at [[bristol|Bristol]] in 1735. His early life prepared him for a career in commerce and industry, and he eventually became connected with the [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] ironworks, the most celebrated iron-manufacturing establishment of the eighteenth century. (016-076)

## 职业生涯

### 加入 Coalbrookdale

Richard Reynolds joined the [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] firm and married into the Darby family, becoming a partner in the works. His management brought a new level of care and principle to the operations. (001-176) It was while Richard Reynolds had charge of the works that significant improvements in [[iron-industry|iron manufacture]] were achieved. (016-108)

### 铁轨先驱

One of Richard Reynolds's most notable contributions was the early use of iron rails — an innovation that anticipated the railway age. His sagacious anticipations in this matter were remarkable for their time. (017-106) (017-144)

### 倡导与政策

Richard Reynolds strongly urged upon Mr. Pitt the removal of the tax on pit-coal, demonstrating his understanding of how fiscal policy affected industrial development. (017-113)

## 影响与评价

> Richard Reynolds was a man of principle whose management of the Coalbrookdale works was marked by both commercial success and moral integrity.

The wrought iron made under his supervision was of exceptional quality, and any failures in the iron were no fault of Richard Reynolds, but were attributable to circumstances beyond his control. (016-180) His son later confirmed the high standards maintained during his tenure. (016-172)

### 退休与离世

Richard Reynolds finally left [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] in capable hands, retiring after a long and distinguished career. He was laid to rest with the respect and admiration of all who knew him. (018-002) (018-108)
