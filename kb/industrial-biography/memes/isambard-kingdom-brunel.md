# Isambard Kingdom Brunel

Isambard Kingdom Brunel (1806–1859) was one of the most prolific British civil and mechanical engineers of the 19th century, son of the renowned engineer [[marc-isambard-brunel|Marc Isambard Brunel]]. While his father features prominently in [[samuel-smiles|Samuel Smiles]]' *Industrial Biography*, Isambard Kingdom Brunel established his own legacy through groundbreaking projects including the [[great-western-railway|Great Western Railway]], the Clifton Suspension Bridge, and the SS *Great Britain* — the first iron-hulled, propeller-driven steamship to cross the Atlantic. (002-046)

## Early Life and Career

Isambard Kingdom Brunel was born in [[portsmouth|Portsmouth]] in 1806, the son of [[marc-isambard-brunel|Marc Isambard Brunel]] and Sophia Kingdom. He received his early education in France and later worked alongside his father on the [[thames-tunnel|[[thames|Thames]] Tunnel]] project, where he narrowly survived a serious flooding accident in 1828.

## Major Projects

Brunel's major works include the Great Western Railway ([[london|London]] to [[bristol|Bristol]], completed 1841), the Clifton Suspension Bridge in Bristol, and a series of pioneering steamships culminating in the SS *Great Eastern* (launched 1858). His designs combined bold engineering with aesthetic vision.

## Legacy

Isambard Kingdom Brunel died in 1859 at the age of 53. He was voted the second greatest Briton in a 2002 BBC poll, reflecting his enduring status as an icon of British engineering and the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]].
