Sir Marc Isambard Brunel (1769–1849) was a French-born engineer and prolific inventor who settled in [[england|England]] and designed the celebrated block-making machinery at [[portsmouth-dockyard|[[portsmouth|Portsmouth]] Dockyard]].

His career intersected with many of the leading engineers of the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]], including [[henry-maudslay|Henry Maudslay]] and [[samuel-bentham|Samuel Bentham]]. (002-047) (038-004)

## Early Life

Brunel was born in France in 1769. During the French Revolution, his royalist sympathies placed him in danger — his father was condemned to death — and Brunel fled to the United States before eventually settling in [[england|England]]. (038-032)

## Career

### Block Machinery

Brunel's most celebrated achievement was the invention of the block-making machinery at [[portsmouth|Portsmouth]], a system of machine tools that automated the production of ships' blocks.

This was extensively used in constructing Brunel's [[block-machinery|Block Machinery]] at the Royal Dockyard, where [[henry-maudslay|Henry Maudslay]] executed the designs. (002-046) (038-005) Brunel invented his block-machines at a time when the [[admiralty|Admiralty]] required vast quantities of pulley blocks for the naval fleet. (031-210)

### Collaboration with Maudslay

Though Brunel had a good knowledge of mechanics, he required the practical skill of a first-rate machinist to realise his designs. He was introduced to [[henry-maudslay|Henry Maudslay]], who undertook the construction of the block-making machinery. (002-050) (038-116) Brunel was on intimate terms with Maudslay, who communicated his methods to him with characteristic candour and generosity. (038-133)

### Other Inventions

Brunel was a prolific inventor. During his career he also devised machinery for saw-mills, and in later years was involved with the launching of the Great Eastern steamship. (038-065) (033-124) His ingenuity extended across many branches of engineering, and his methods received official recognition when submitted to the [[admiralty|Admiralty]]. (038-107)

## Influence and Legacy

> "There is one thing to invent," said Sir Marc Brunel, "and another thing to make the invention work." (031-172)

Brunel's career exemplified the partnership between theoretical invention and practical execution that characterised the Industrial Revolution. His designs, realised through the skilled hands of Maudslay and his workmen, transformed naval manufacturing. A memoir of his life was published by Beamish. (032-135)
