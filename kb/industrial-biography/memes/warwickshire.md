Warwickshire is a county in the West Midlands of [[england|England]], notable in industrial history as an early centre of iron-working activity. The Roman settlement on the Icknild Street provided a foundation for metal-working traditions that persisted through the medieval period and beyond.

## Iron-Working and Needle Manufacture

The town of Alcester, situated on the Icknild Street, was originally a Roman settlement where the art of working in iron was practised from an early period. (009-004) Originally called Alauna, it lay on the River Alne in Warwickshire. (009-006) The area remained a seat of the needle manufacture, continuing a tradition of precision metal-working that drew upon the region's long history of iron craftsmanship. (009-006)
