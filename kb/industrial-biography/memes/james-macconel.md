# James MacConnel

James MacConnel was a textile machine maker and manufacturer in [[manchester|Manchester]], best known as the partner of [[john-kennedy|John Kennedy]] in the firm of MacConnel and Kennedy — one of the leading [[mule-spinning|cotton-spinning]] establishments of the early nineteenth century. (054-096) (055-075)

## 职业生涯

### 与 Kennedy 的合作

James MacConnel became the partner of [[john-kennedy|John Kennedy]] in the firm of MacConnel and Kennedy, cotton spinners and machine makers in [[manchester|Manchester]]. Kennedy and MacConnel afterwards occupied a little factory, where they carried on business on a larger scale, combining machine-making with cotton spinning. (055-008) (054-121)

### 早期工作

Before forming the partnership, MacConnel had worked alongside other leading figures in the [[manchester|Manchester]] textile trade. He, along with [[adam-murray|Adam Murray]] and several others, had been among the skilled mechanics who laid the foundations of [[manchester|Manchester]]'s machine-making industry. (054-192) Their business was machine-making, and the firm became known for the quality of its [[mule-spinning|cotton-spinning]] machinery. (055-003)

### MacConnel and Kennedy

The firm of MacConnel and Kennedy grew to be one of the largest [[mule-spinning|cotton-spinning]] concerns in [[manchester|Manchester]]. In 1818, the machinery of the firm was described as among the finest in the trade, reflecting the technical skill of both partners. (055-075) The partnership exemplified the close connection between machine-making and [[mule-spinning|cotton-spinning]] that characterised [[manchester|Manchester]]'s industrial development. (054-096)
