Darlington, a market town in County Durham, was notable in the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]] both for early textile machinery innovation and as the starting point of the world's first public steam railway.

## Flax-Spinning Machinery

Darlington holds a place in the history of textile machinery through the work of John Kendrew, an ingenious self-taught mechanic of the town. It was at Darlington that the spinning of flax by machinery was first practically demonstrated. (047-136)

Kendrew invented one of the first successful machines for spinning flax by machinery — an important advance that mechanised the production of linen thread. (047-137) His work preceded the more famous flax-spinning innovations of [[john-marshall|John Marshall]] of [[leeds|Leeds]] and contributed to the development of the [[leeds|Leeds]] linen industry.

When a manufacturer refused to pay the patent right, Kendrew commenced an action against him. However, his claim was disputed and he lost his action. It is admitted that the invention was afterwards taken up and improved by others. (047-141) (047-143)

Despite this setback, Kendrew had flax-machinery in working order, demonstrating the practical viability of mechanised flax-spinning at Darlington. (047-147)

The spinning of flax by machinery at Darlington demonstrated the spread of mechanised textile production beyond the cotton districts of [[lancashire|Lancashire]] and into the north-east of [[england|England]].

## The [[stockton-on-tees|Stockton]] and Darlington Railway

Darlington is most famous in industrial history as the northern terminus of the Stockton and Darlington Railway, opened in 1825. This was the world's first public railway to use [[locomotive|steam locomotive]]s for both freight and passenger traffic.

The railway, engineered by [[george-stephenson|George Stephenson]], connected the Durham coal fields with the port of [[stockton-on-tees|Stockton-on-Tees]]. It demonstrated the viability of steam railways for long-distance transport, and its success triggered the rapid expansion and adoption of railway locomotion across Britain. (017-107) (033-001)

## Notable Figures

[[matthew-murray|Matthew Murray]], the Leeds machine-tool builder and locomotive pioneer, was born at Stockton-on-Tees, near Darlington, in 1765. (045-114) His early years in the region connected him to the emerging industrial culture of south Durham and north [[yorkshire|Yorkshire]].
