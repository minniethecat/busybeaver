Glamorganshire, in [[south-wales|South Wales]], attracted ironmasters from [[Sussex]] during the eighteenth century who sought to exploit the region's abundant coal and iron-ore deposits, transforming South Wales into one of Britain's principal iron-producing districts.

## Migration of Sussex Ironmasters

In the early 1700s, some Sussex ironmasters migrated to Glamorganshire because of the superior fuel and ore resources available in South Wales. (008-141) These Sussex masters settled in Glamorganshire to continue the [[iron-industry|iron trade]] they had practised in the Weald, bringing their skills and capital with them. (008-147) The migration was significant enough that the episode became known in industrial history as the "Sussex Ironmasters in Glamorganshire." (009-072)

## Industrial Significance

The influx of skilled ironmasters turned Glamorganshire into a thriving iron-producing region. The combination of Welsh coal seams and local ironstone provided the raw materials that the transplanted Sussex furnaces needed, and the region grew rapidly as an industrial centre during the eighteenth century.
