# London Bridge

London Bridge is a historic crossing of the River [[thames|Thames]] in central London. During the early modern period, the area just above the bridge served as a key hub for the iron and steel import trade into [[england|England]].

## 地理位置

London Bridge spans the River Thames in central London, connecting the City of London with Southwark. The area of Upper Thames Street, situated a short distance above the bridge, was a commercial centre for foreign merchants during the Tudor period.

## 工业历史

### 早期贸易中心

The Steelyard Company of Foreign Merchants established their base in Upper Thames Street, a little above London Bridge. From this location they imported large quantities of iron and steel from foreign countries, principally from continental Europe. (009-101)

This import trade was significant because England's domestic iron production was insufficient to meet demand during this period. The iron and steel brought in via London Bridge supplied the capital's artisans, smiths, and manufacturers.

## 重要人物与企业

The Steelyard merchants, a Hanseatic trading guild, were the primary operators of the iron import trade at London Bridge. Their presence made the area one of the most important entry points for foreign iron into England before the rise of domestic iron production in the 18th century.
