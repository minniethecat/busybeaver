Cumberland is a historic county in north-west [[england|England]], bordering [[scotland|Scotland]] and encompassing much of the Lake District. Its communities long preserved a distinct local culture shaped by the region's agricultural and industrial rhythms.

## Local Traditions

A well-known saying in Cumberland held that when children reached a certain age, their future path was determined by a practical test: those who stuck on the house-rigg were made thatchers of, while those who fell off were sent to St. Bees to be made parsons of. (041-169) This proverb, noted by [[samuel-smiles|Samuel Smiles]], reflected the region's pragmatic approach to placing young people in trades — including the iron-working and smithing trades for which the north-west was known — or, failing that, sending them into the Church.
