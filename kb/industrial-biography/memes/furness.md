# Furness

Furness, a district in [[lancashire|Lancashire]], was recognised as an iron-producing region from an early period. Its mineral wealth made it both a valuable industrial asset and, in times of border conflict, a target for raiding. (005-074)

## Industrial History

### Early Iron Production

Long before the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]] transformed British ironmaking, the district of Furness was already known as an iron-producing district. The bloomery forges of Furness worked local ores with charcoal fuel, producing wrought iron for tools, weapons, and agricultural implements. (005-074)

### Scottish Raids

The value of Furness iron was such that during the periods of border conflict between [[england|England]] and [[scotland|Scotland]], the district was frequently ravaged by marauding Scottish incursions. These raids were undertaken with the specific object of seizing iron — a testament to the strategic importance of Furness as a source of the metal. (005-073) (005-074)

The Scottish raiders obtained by these incursions into [[england|England]] what their own land could not supply in sufficient quantity. The long history of Furness as an iron district thus connected it to the broader political and military history of the border regions. (005-073)
