# Ellesmere Canal

Canal project connecting the Mersey, [[severn|Severn]], and Dee river systems along the Wales–[[shropshire|Shropshire]] border. The Ellesmere Canal is notable for the Pontcysyllte aqueduct, an early and celebrated use of [[pig-iron|cast iron]] in civil engineering.

## Establishment

The Ellesmere Canal was planned in the 1790s as part of Britain's canal-building era, linking the growing industrial centres of the Welsh borderlands with the major river networks. [[Thomas Telford]] was appointed as engineer for the project.

## Engineering Achievement

### The Pontcysyllte Aqueduct

The most remarkable feature of the Ellesmere Canal was the iron aqueduct carrying it over the River Dee at Pontcysyllte. [[William Reynolds]] of Coalbrookdale provided valuable assistance in planning the iron aqueduct and executing the necessary castings (017-174). The structure demonstrated the growing capabilities of the iron industry in producing large-scale engineering components.
