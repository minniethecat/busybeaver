# Yorkshire

Yorkshire was one of the great industrial counties of [[england|England]], with a history of iron-working stretching back to the medieval period. The county was home to [[leeds|Leeds]], a major centre of engineering and tool manufacturing, and [[sheffield|Sheffield]], the world capital of steel. From early iron-smelting by Cistercian monks to the 19th-century engineering works that produced locomotives and machine tools, Yorkshire's industrial story was inseparable from iron and steel. (001-113) (007-023)

## Historical Development

### Early Iron-Smelting

Early iron-smelting in Yorkshire dated from the monastic period. The remains of iron furnaces discovered at [[leeds|Leeds]], in Yorkshire, during the digging of house foundations, showed that the monks were well acquainted with the art of iron-making. (001-113) (007-035) The neighbourhood of [[leeds|Leeds]] — at Middleton, Whitkirk, and Horsforth — all within the borough, contained extensive evidence of medieval iron-working, the slag heaps at Horsforth being found in conglomerated masses. (007-048) (007-049)

### Steel and the [[sheffield|Sheffield]] Trade

The steel manufactures of Yorkshire achieved international renown. Early [[sheffield|Sheffield]] manufactures gained a reputation for quality, and the practical skill of the [[sheffield|Sheffield]] artisans was widely acknowledged. (001-188) (001-203) The French metallurgist Le Play documented the steel-making processes of Yorkshire in his study *Sur la Fabrication de l'Acier en Yorkshire*, recognising the distinguished merit of the Yorkshire steel manufacturers in advancing their craft to a level unmatched elsewhere. (019-012) (019-114) (021-070)

> "Le Play documented the steel-making processes of Yorkshire, recognising the distinguished merit of advancing the steel manufactures of Yorkshire to their high state of perfection." (019-114)

### The Movers and Shakers

The manufacture of steel in Yorkshire attracted visitors from across Europe, who paid visits to [[sheffield|Sheffield]] to study its methods. (020-080) The county produced a remarkable lineage of industrial figures, among them [[matthew-murray|Matthew Murray]] of [[leeds|Leeds]], the great locomotive and machine-tool builder, and others such as [[peter-fairbairn|Peter Fairbairn]] of [[leeds|Leeds]]. (031-154) (045-049)
