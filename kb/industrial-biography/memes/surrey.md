# Surrey

Surrey is a historic county in south-east [[england|England]], adjoining [[london|London]] to the south. During the 16th and 17th centuries it formed part of the Wealden iron-producing district, one of the earliest centres of [[iron-industry|iron manufacture]] in Britain.

## 地理位置

Surrey lies south of the [[thames|River Thames]], bordering Kent to the east and Sussex to the south. Its northern parts are close to London, while the southern areas extend into the Weald.

## 工业历史

### 铁器时代的冶铁业

In the late 16th century, Surrey was home to numerous iron furnaces and forges. The commentator Norden observed that there were, in Sussex and Surrey adjoining, three or four glass-houses alongside the iron-works. (008-078) These early industries consumed large quantities of charcoal from the surrounding woodlands.

### 产业限制与衰退

By the early 17th century, concerns over timber depletion led [[house-of-commons|Parliament]] to restrict the number of iron-works in Kent, Surrey, and Sussex, particularly within twenty-two miles of London. (008-137) Similar enactments were made in subsequent Parliaments with the same objective.

Andrew Yarranton, writing in 1676, noted "the many iron-works laid down in Kent, Sussex, Surrey, and in the north of England" — indicating that by the late 17th century, many Surrey iron-works had already been abandoned due to foreign competition. (015-099)

### 后续发展

As iron-making declined, Surrey became a residential county for London professionals. In 1765, for instance, the navy agent [[henry-cort|Henry Cort]]'s father-in-law was established in Surrey Street, Strand, carrying on business in the capital. (021-093)

## 重要人物与企业
