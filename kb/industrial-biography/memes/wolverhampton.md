# Wolverhampton

Wolverhampton is an industrial town in Staffordshire, in the West Midlands of [[england|England]]. It formed part of the Black Country, a region renowned for its iron-working and metal manufacturing during the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]].

## 地理位置

Wolverhampton lies in the West Midlands, northwest of [[Birmingham]]. The town sits within the South Staffordshire coalfield, which provided the fuel and raw materials that supported a dense concentration of iron furnaces, forges, and metal-working shops. (007-179)

## 工业历史

### Iron-Working Heritage

The iron industry in Wolverhampton developed as part of the broader Staffordshire iron district. By the 18th century, the iron-manufacturing ardour of Wolverhampton and Birmingham men had become proverbial. The willingness of established families to engage in iron production was noted by contemporaries: even the most ancient families of the nobility — the Nevilles, Howards, Percys, Stanleys, Montagues, Pelhams, Ashburnhams, Sidneys, Sackvilles, Dacres, and Finches — prosecuted the manufacture of iron with all the ardour of Birmingham and Wolverhampton men in modern times. (007-179)

### Industrial Expansion

Wolverhampton's industrial base grew through the 18th and 19th centuries, specialising in iron founding, lock-making, and metal goods. Its proximity to the coal and ironstone deposits of the Black Country ensured a steady supply of raw materials, while the canal network connected the town to national markets.

## 重要人物与企业

Wolverhampton was home to numerous ironmasters and manufacturers. The town's industry was closely linked to that of neighbouring [[Birmingham]], with which it shared technical expertise and commercial networks. Firms in the area produced a wide range of iron and steel goods, from heavy castings to precision tools.
