# Lancashire

Lancashire was the beating heart of the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]] in [[england|England]]. The county was home to the cotton spinning industry that transformed global trade, and its towns — [[manchester|Manchester]], [[liverpool|Liverpool]], and dozens of smaller mill towns — became synonymous with industrial enterprise. The Lancashire proverb "Twice clogs, once boots" captured the rapid rise in living standards that industry brought. (008-025) (054-100)

## Industrial Significance

### Cotton and Engineering

The cotton spinning industry drove Lancashire's growth in the late 18th and early 19th centuries. Thousands of workers poured into South Lancashire, and manufacturers started with very modest capital, building the mills and machinery that would make Britain the workshop of the world. (054-100) (054-106)

> "The Lancashire manufacturers started very nearly at the bottom of the ladder, with the poorest capital." (054-106)

### Iron and Innovation

Lancashire was not only a textile county. The iron-producing district of [[furness|Furness]] in the north of the county had been active since medieval times, and the engineering workshops of [[manchester|Manchester]] and its satellite towns produced steam engines, machine tools, and locomotives for markets across the empire. (005-074) (054-111)

### Labour and Migration

The demand for skilled workers in Lancashire drew artisans from across Britain. Many of the celebrated engineers and machine-makers who built their reputations in [[manchester|Manchester]] and the surrounding towns had come to serve apprenticeships in the county's workshops. (054-189)
