# Dud [[dudley|Dudley]]

Dud Dudley was the natural son of Edward Sutton, 5th Baron [[dudley|Dudley]]. He claimed to have successfully smelted iron using coke around 1620 and wrote Metallum Martis, a treatise on iron-making with pit-coal. His works were repeatedly destroyed by floods and rioting charcoal ironworkers, and he never achieved commercial sustainability. (001-129)

## Early Life and Career

Born in 1600, Dud Dudley entered the industrial world during a period of rapid change in British manufacturing.

## Major Contributions

Dud Dudley's principal contributions established new methods that influenced subsequent generations of engineers and industrial workers.

## Legacy

Dud Dudley died in 1684. Their work contributed to the transformation of British industry.
