Devon contributed to British iron-making and marine engineering through the Devon Iron Works and the naval installations at Devonport, where steam-powered dock facilities were constructed in the nineteenth century.

## Devon Iron Works

The Devon Iron Works, managed alongside [[Clyde Cleugh]], [[Muirkirk]], and other Scottish iron-producing sites, formed part of a network of nineteenth-century iron-making enterprises. (025-146) These works supplied iron for engineering and construction projects across Britain.

## Devonport Steam Dock

The great steam dock at Devonport was a major marine engineering project where the results of advanced steam-powered pumping and construction techniques were demonstrated. (050-098) Devonport, as a major [[admiralty|Royal Navy]] dockyard facility, linked Devon's industrial capabilities to Britain's naval power during the age of steam.
