# Industrial Revolution

The Industrial Revolution was the transformation of British manufacturing, transport, and society between roughly 1760 and 1840.

Driven by interconnected innovations in iron-making, steam power, textile machinery, and machine tools, it reshaped every aspect of economic life. The iron industry was central, providing the material foundation for mechanised production.

## Iron and the Industrial Revolution

Iron was the fundamental material of the Industrial Revolution. The period could be characterized as "the Age of Iron" — a phrase used by the Danish archaeologist Worsaae to describe the transformative power of iron in civilisation. (019-006) (056-069)

The substitution of [[coke-smelting|coke]] for charcoal in blast furnaces began at [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]]. (015-145) [[abraham-darby-i|Abraham Darby]] first successfully smelted iron with coke there in 1709, freeing iron production from dependence on dwindling forests.

The invention of the [[puddling-process|[[puddling-process|puddling]] process]] and the application of [[steam-power|steam power]] to blast furnaces and forges further accelerated production. These innovations combined to increase British iron production from roughly 17,000 tons in 1740 to over 2 million tons by 1850.

It may be averred that as certainly as the age of iron superseded that of bronze, so will the age of steel supersede that of iron. (019-006)

## The [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] Pioneers

[[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale Iron Works]] under the Darbys and Reynoldses became the cradle of the Industrial Revolution. (001-166) [[abraham-darby-i|Abraham Darby I]], a Quaker ironmaster, relocated to Coalbrookdale and began the dynasty that would transform iron-making. (001-172) (015-151)

[[richard-reynolds|Richard Reynolds]] later joined the Coalbrookdale firm, extending the works and contributing to its growth. (001-176) (001-181)

[[abraham-darby-iii|Abraham Darby III]], the grandson of the founder, constructed the world's first iron bridge across the [[severn|Severn]] at [[ironbridge|Ironbridge]], a structure that symbolised the new age of iron. (001-180)

## The Factory System

The mechanisation of textile production created the factory system that transformed British society. Inventions such as the [[spinning-jenny|spinning jenny]], the [[water-frame|water frame]], and the [[mule-spinning|spinning mule]] drove this change.

Mills powered by water and later steam concentrated production in urban centres such as [[manchester|Manchester]], [[leeds|Leeds]], and [[glasgow|Glasgow]]. The [[power-loom|power loom]] further accelerated the shift to factory-based manufacturing.
