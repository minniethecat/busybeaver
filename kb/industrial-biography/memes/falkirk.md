# Falkirk

Town in Stirlingshire, Scotland, best known in industrial history as the location of the [[Carron Iron Works]], the first large-scale iron-smelting works in Scotland, founded by Dr. [[John Roebuck]] in 1759.

## Geography

Falkirk lies near the River Carron in central Scotland, situated between [[edinburgh|Edinburgh]] and [[glasgow|Glasgow]]. The surrounding area provided an abundant supply of water, iron ore, coal, and limestone — the essential raw materials for iron production (001-235).

## Industrial History

### Early Development

The area around Falkirk was chosen by Dr. John Roebuck after careful examination of both sides of the River Forth. The site on the banks of the Carron offered an inexhaustible supply of natural resources needed for [[iron-industry|iron manufacture]] (001-235).

### The Carron Works

The [[Carron Iron Works]] and its successor the [[Carron Company]] became one of the most significant iron manufacturing establishments in Britain. Under the management of [[William Cadell]] and later partners, the works produced ordnance, iron goods, and the famous "Carronades" — short naval guns that saw extensive use.
