# Henry Cort

Henry Cort was an English ironmaster whose inventions transformed the [[iron-industry|British iron industry]]. At [[fontley-forge|Fontley Forge]] in Hampshire, he developed the [[puddling-process|puddling]] process for converting pig iron into wrought iron using a reverberatory furnace fired with coal, and grooved rollers for mechanically shaping the iron. His patents of 1783 and 1784 established the integrated coke-based iron production system. Though his inventions made Britain the world leader in iron production, Cort died in poverty after his patents were challenged. (021-156)

## Early Life and Career

Born in 1740, Henry Cort entered the industrial world during a period of rapid change in British manufacturing.
