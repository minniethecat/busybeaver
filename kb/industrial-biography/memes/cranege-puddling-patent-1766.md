# Cranege Puddling Patent 1766

In 1766, Thomas and [[cranege-brothers|George Cranege]] patented their reverberatory furnace for converting pig iron using pit-coal. Acting on the advice of [[richard-reynolds|Richard Reynolds]], their invention was the first to use the term '[[puddling-process|puddling]]' and demonstrated the feasibility of coal-based iron conversion. (016-166)

## Context

This event occurred during the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]] and contributed to the development of iron and engineering technology.
