# Royal Mint

The Royal Mint is the official mint of the United Kingdom, responsible for the production of coinage. During the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]] it became a centre for precision metalworking and was closely associated with leading engineers of the period.

## 地理位置

The Royal Mint was historically located at [[tower-of-london|the Tower]] of London before moving to separate premises. It has been a London institution for centuries.

## 工业历史

### 精密工程中心

During the late 18th and early 19th centuries, the Royal Mint employed some of the leading engineers and metalworkers of the day. Mr. Barton of the Royal Mint was associated with [[bryan-donkin|Mr. Donkin]] the engineer in ascertaining and applying precision measurement techniques. (040-101)

Barton was also consulted by the sculptor Sir Francis Chantrey about the casting of his bronze statuary, indicating the Mint's broader role in metallurgical expertise. (040-100)

### 硬币铸造技术

The Royal Mint was an early adopter of steam-powered coinage. Matthew Boulton's Soho Manufactory supplied steam-powered coining presses to the Mint, revolutionising the production of British currency. The precision engineering techniques developed at the Mint influenced wider industrial practice.

## 重要人物与企业

- **Mr. Barton** — engineer at the Royal Mint, expert in precision metalworking (040-101)
- **[[Bryan Donkin]]** — engineer associated with Barton in measurement work
- **[[Matthew Boulton]]** — supplied steam-powered coinage technology to the Mint
- **[[Soho Manufactory]]** — Boulton's [[birmingham|Birmingham]] works that produced coining machinery
