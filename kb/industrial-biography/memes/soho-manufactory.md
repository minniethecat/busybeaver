# Soho Manufactory

The Soho Manufactory at Handsworth near [[birmingham|Birmingham]] was founded by [[matthew-boulton|Matthew Boulton]] in 1761. It became the model factory of the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]], producing buttons, silver plate, and from 1775 [[james-watt|James Watt]]'s steam engines. The partnership of [[boulton-and-watt|Boulton and Watt]] made Soho the most famous industrial works in the world.

## Industrial Significance

Soho Manufactory played a significant role in the industrial development of Britain during the 18th and 19th centuries.
