# Derby

Derby was a significant centre of mechanical engineering and iron-working during the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]], noted particularly for the [[james-fox|Fox of Derby]] firm of machine-tool makers and the pioneering silk mill of [[john-lombe|John Lombe]]. Its location in the iron-rich North [[derbyshire|Derbyshire]] region provided the raw materials that sustained its industrial growth. (004-113)

## Industrial History

### Early Manufacturing

The town's industrial foundations were laid in the early eighteenth century when Mr. Lombe of Derby established a successful silk-throwing mill, one of the first factory-scale enterprises in [[england|England]]. (014-177) The surrounding North [[derbyshire|Derbyshire]] countryside, with hill-sides long known for their ironstone deposits, had supported iron-smelting from an earlier period. (004-113)

### The Fox of Derby

The most celebrated Derby engineering enterprise was the firm founded by [[james-fox|James Fox]], which became one of Britain's leading manufacturers of machine tools. Fox, who originally worked as a butler before turning to mechanics, established works that produced planing machines, lathes, and other precision tools essential to the engineering trades. (002-100) (045-053)

The neighbourhood of Derby furnished Fox with sufficient skilled workmen, and his machine tools gained a reputation for excellence that extended well beyond the Midlands. (045-064) The firm of Fox of Derby was counted among the foremost machine-tool makers of the age, alongside [[matthew-murray|Matthew Murray]] of [[leeds|Leeds]] and [[richard-roberts|Richard Roberts]] of [[manchester|Manchester]]. (031-153) (045-049)

### Later Years

The present firm of Fox of Derby continued to carry on the engineering tradition through the nineteenth century, and was followed by notable successors including Nasmyth and Whitworth. (045-074) (051-191) The town also produced distinguished figures such as [[william-strutt|William Strutt]], the father of Lord Belper, who contributed to fire-proof mill construction. (046-151)
