# William Reynolds

William Reynolds was an ironmaster and inventor associated with [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] and Coed-du, best known for his invention of inclined planes for working canals in 1788. He was a member of the Reynolds family that played a leading role in the [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] ironworks. (001-064) (001-182)

## 职业生涯

### Coalbrookdale 与 Coed-du

William Reynolds of Coed-du was closely connected with the [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] ironworks, collaborating with the Darby family and his kinsman [[richard-reynolds|Richard Reynolds]]. He was also involved with ironworks in [[south-wales|South Wales]] alongside other prominent ironmasters such as [[richard-crawshay|Richard Crawshay]] and [[samuel-homfray|Samuel Homfray]]. (022-084) (017-160) A grandson later confirmed details of his work and contributions. (018-162)

### Inclined Plane 发明

In 1788, William Reynolds invented inclined planes for working canals. The system consisted of parallel railways on which boats could be raised and lowered between different water levels. (017-157) (001-182)

This innovation was a significant advance in canal engineering, enabling more efficient transport of coal and iron. Reynolds constructed the first inclined plane and introduced boats of five tons capacity on the system. (017-168)

## 影响与评价

William Reynolds's inclined plane system demonstrated the practical ingenuity characteristic of the [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] ironmasters. The assistance he provided in planning and constructing these works was acknowledged by his contemporaries, and his invention represented an important step in the evolution of transport infrastructure during the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]]. (017-173)
