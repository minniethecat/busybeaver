# Bridgewater [[foundry|Foundry]]

The Bridgewater Foundry was an engineering works established by James Nasmyth at Patricroft, near Manchester, in 1836. It became one of the most important centres of mechanical engineering in Britain during the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]], renowned for manufacturing steam hammers and precision machine tools.

## 地理位置

The foundry was located at Patricroft, on the [[bridgewater-canal|Bridgewater Canal]] near Manchester, chosen by Nasmyth for its transport links and proximity to the engineering markets of [[lancashire|Lancashire]].

## 工业历史

### 建立

James Nasmyth, after gaining experience under Henry Maudslay in [[london|London]], returned north and selected the Patricroft site for his own engineering works. The foundry was rapidly constructed: within a month the wood sheds were erected, and the ring of the hammer on the smith's anvil was soon heard all over the place. (049-020) The Bridgewater Foundry was fairly under way, and Nasmyth toiled there right heartily until December of that year. (049-021)

### 鼎盛时期

The foundry grew to employ hundreds of men and produced a wide range of engineering products. Its most famous invention was Nasmyth's steam hammer, which revolutionised iron forging. The works also manufactured locomotives, bridge components, and heavy machinery for railways and shipbuilding.

## 重要人物与企业

- **[[James Nasmyth]]** — founder and chief engineer of the Bridgewater Foundry
- **[[Henry Maudslay]]** — Nasmyth's former employer and mentor in precision engineering
