# Greenock

Port town on the Firth of [[clyde|Clyde]] in Renfrewshire, Scotland. Greenock served as a key transport hub during the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]], connecting Scottish manufacturing centres to Liverpool and beyond by steamboat.

## Geography

Greenock lies at the mouth of the [[clyde|River Clyde]], west of [[glasgow|Glasgow]]. Its deep-water port made it a natural departure point for industrial goods and migrant workers travelling between Scotland and [[england|England]] (050-174).

## Industrial Role

### Transport Hub

When [[James Nasmyth]] relocated his skilled workforce and their families from Scotland to [[Patricroft]] near [[manchester|Manchester]], the entire party — with their wives, families, chests of drawers, and eight-day clocks — was transported by a specially-hired steamboat from Greenock to [[Liverpool]] (050-174).

### Shipbuilding

Greenock later became a significant centre for shipbuilding on the Clyde, continuing Scotland's long tradition of marine engineering that began with the early steamboat services on which Nasmyth's workers travelled.
