# Chemical Society

The Chemical Society of [[london|London]], founded in 1841, was one of the leading scientific bodies of the nineteenth century, dedicated to the advancement of chemistry and its industrial applications.

## Connection to Industrial Biography

[[James Nasmyth]], the inventor of the [[Steam Hammer]], was honoured by many eminent bodies during his lifetime. The Chemical Society joined the [[Institution of Civil Engineers]] and other institutions in recognising Nasmyth's contributions, with his election as a Member of the [[Royal Society]] in 1846 representing the highest of these honours (028-174).

## Significance

The recognition of Nasmyth by the Chemical Society reflects the interdisciplinary nature of nineteenth-century industrial science. Although Nasmyth was primarily a mechanical engineer, his work in metallurgy, iron-working, and manufacturing processes intersected with the chemical sciences of the period.
