# Kent

Kent is a historic county in south-east [[england|England]], bordering the [[thames|Thames]] Estuary and the English Channel. It was part of the Wealden iron-producing region and played an important role in the early iron industry of Britain, as well as later becoming a centre for shipbuilding and engineering.

## 地理位置

Kent occupies the south-eastern corner of England, with London to the north-west and Sussex to the south-west. The county's northern parts, known as North Kent, border the Thames Estuary.

## 工业历史

### 冶铁业的兴起

Kent was part of the great iron-producing region of the Weald. The county was renowned for its oak and beech woodlands, which provided charcoal essential for iron-smelting. In the early 17th century, it was described as "the grand nursery especially of oake and beech." (006-149) The abundance of timber made Kent an ideal location for early iron furnaces.

Norden's survey noted that of some 800 mills across the region, "there are foure hundred milnes in Surry, Kent, and Sussex" — a substantial concentration of water-powered industry. (008-067)

### 产业限制

[[house-of-commons|Parliament]]ary concern over the depletion of timber for shipbuilding led to restrictions on iron-works in Kent, Surrey, and Sussex. (008-137) The water-mills of North Kent, originally built for fulling cloth, were increasingly employed to work iron instead of driving fulling-mills. (008-177)

### 衰退

[[andrew-yarranton|Andrew Yarranton]] recorded in 1676 that "many iron-works laid down in Kent, Sussex, Surrey, and in the north of England" had ceased operation, as cheaper iron from Sweden, Flanders, and Spain displaced domestic production. (015-099)

### 工程与造船

In later centuries, Kent became a centre for marine engineering. The county's riverside towns along the Thames and Medway developed shipyards and engineering works. The view from Shooter's Hill offered an "out-look over Kent," encompassing the river and its industrial activity. (041-042)

## 重要人物与企业

Several notable figures retired to or worked in Kent. The engineer [[henry-maudslay|Henry Maudslay]] settled down at his rural retreat in Kent after his active engineering career. (051-016)
