The **[[woolwich|[[woolwich|Woolwich]] Arsenal]]** (formally the **Royal Arsenal**) was a major British military-industrial complex on the [[thames|Thames]] at [[woolwich|Woolwich]], southeast [[london|London]]. It served as the nation's principal ordnance factory and was a breeding ground for engineering talent during the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]].

## Maudslay's Starting Point

[[[henry-maudslay|Henry Maudslay]]](/henry-maudslay) began his career at Woolwich Arsenal, employed as a powder-boy before advancing to the blacksmiths' shop (002-029). It was at the [[woolwich|Woolwich]] smithy that Maudslay's extraordinary mechanical talent first emerged. The master smithy at [[woolwich|Woolwich]], where Maudslay worked, was the proving ground where his skills caught the attention of those who later recommended him to [[[joseph-bramah|Joseph Bramah]]](/joseph-bramah) (036-003). Maudslay's transfer "from the smithy in Woolwich Arsenal to a sphere more suitable for the development of his mechanical ability" (035-169) marked the beginning of his ascent as the father of machine-tool engineering.

## Bramah's [[hydraulic-press|Hydraulic Press]] Demonstration

[[[joseph-bramah|Joseph Bramah]]](/joseph-bramah) constructed an early version of his [[Bramah Hydraulic Press 1795|hydraulic press]] at Woolwich Arsenal, where it remained in efficient use long after its installation (034-050). The press's axis design was first demonstrated there. The Arsenal also housed a museum containing early ordnance specimens (012-007), preserving industrial history alongside active production.
