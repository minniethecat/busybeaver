James Hargreaves (c.1720–1778) was an English weaver and carpenter from Stanhill, near Blackburn in [[lancashire|Lancashire]]. He is remembered as the inventor of the spinning jenny, a multi-spindle spinning frame that dramatically increased the productivity of cotton spinning in the 1760s.

## The Spinning Jenny

Hargreaves's spinning jenny, developed around 1764, enabled a single operator to spin multiple threads simultaneously—initially eight, and later as many as eighty or more. The machine used a simple hand-operated wheel to drive multiple spindles arranged in a row, drawing out and twisting the cotton rovings into yarn.

The invention met fierce opposition. Like [[john-kay|John Kay]] of the fly-shuttle before him and [[richard-arkwright|Richard Arkwright]] of the spinning frame after him, Hargreaves had to flee from [[lancashire|Lancashire]] when hand-spinners, fearing for their livelihoods, broke into his house and destroyed his machine. (055-030)

## Significance

The spinning jenny, together with Arkwright's water frame and [[samuel-crompton|Samuel Crompton]]'s [[mule-spinning|spinning mule]], formed the trio of mechanical inventions that revolutionized cotton spinning and created the [[industrial-revolution|factory system]] of the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]]. While the jenny was initially hand-powered and suitable for cottage industry, its principle of multi-spindle spinning was eventually incorporated into power-driven factory machinery.
