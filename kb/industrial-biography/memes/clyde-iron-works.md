# [[clyde|Clyde]] Iron Works

Clyde Iron Works near [[glasgow|Glasgow]] was the site of the first successful trial of [[james-beaumont-neilson|James Beaumont Neilson]]'s hot blast in 1828. This trial proved the dramatic fuel savings the technology could deliver and transformed the Scottish iron industry.

## Early History

Founded in 1786, the Clyde Iron Works initially operated with only two blast furnaces. The works were situated on the rich coal and ironstone fields of the Scottish lowlands. When [[david-mushet|David Mushet]] joined the staff at the age of nineteen as accountant, the works were still a relatively modest enterprise. (025-160) (025-161)

## The [[hot-blast|Hot Blast]] Trial

The hot blast was first tried at Clyde in 1828. Neilson induced Mr. Charles Macintosh of Crossbasket and Mr. Colin Dunlop of the Clyde Iron Works to allow the trial. The results were immediate: fuel consumption dropped sharply and the quality of iron produced was improved. (028-069) (028-070)

[[david-mushet|David Mushet]], who later became famous for his metallurgical researches, was working at Clyde at the time. He described the period of his hot blast experiments: "Thus terminated my researches at the [[clyde-iron-works|Clyde Iron Works]]. It happened at a time when I was interested — and I had been two years previously occupied — in an attempt to convert..." (026-059) (026-060)

## Economic Impact

The success at Clyde spread rapidly. The first trials were made at Clyde and Calder, and from there the use of the hot blast gradually extended to other iron-mining districts. Within a few years, the annual saving in fuel costs across [[scotland|Scotland]] alone was estimated at over £200,000. (028-122) (028-123)

The hot blast proved particularly transformative for the Scottish iron industry, which had previously been negligible compared to English and Welsh production. The ability to use local raw coal — previously unsuitable for coking — gave Scottish ironworks a decisive cost advantage that propelled Scotland into the front rank of world iron producers within two decades.
