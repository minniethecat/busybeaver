# Staffordshire

Staffordshire was one of the principal iron-producing counties of [[england|England]] in the 17th and 18th centuries. The county's ironmasters were among the pioneers who developed coal-based smelting techniques, and Staffordshire ironworks supplied forgings to the growing industries of the Midlands. The county rivalled [[south-wales|South Wales]] and [[shropshire|Shropshire]] as a centre of the [[iron-industry|iron trade]]. (008-005) (027-044)

## Industrial History

### The Foley Ironworks

The [[foley-family|Foley family]] established some of the earliest and most successful ironworks in Staffordshire. Their operations at Cradley and elsewhere in the county demonstrated the viability of large-scale iron production using coal fuel, paving the way for the wider adoption of [[coke-smelting|coke smelting]] across Britain. (010-052)

### Experimental Ironmaking

Staffordshire was a centre of metallurgical experimentation. Dr. Plot recorded early attempts at coal-based iron smelting in his 'History of Staffordshire' (1686), and the county's ironmasters were among the first to successfully transition from charcoal to mineral fuel. (015-071) (018-111)
