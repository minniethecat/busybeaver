# [[portsmouth|[[portsmouth|Portsmouth]] Dockyard]]

Portsmouth Dockyard was the principal naval base of the [[admiralty|Royal Navy]] and the site of one of the most remarkable engineering achievements of the early 19th century: the block-making machinery designed by [[marc-isambard-brunel|Marc Isambard Brunel]] and built by [[henry-maudslay|Henry Maudslay]]. This installation was among the earliest examples of automated mass production using machine tools. (038-105)

## The [[block-machinery|Block Machinery]]

[[marc-isambard-brunel|Brunel's]] block-making machinery at [[portsmouth|Portsmouth]] represented a revolution in manufacturing. A system of 43 machines, driven by a steam engine, could produce 130,000 pulley blocks per year — sufficient for the entire [[admiralty|Royal Navy]] — with a workforce of ten unskilled men, replacing the previous requirement of 110 skilled craftsmen. (038-105) (039-003)

> "The machinery at Portsmouth Dockyard was regularly employed for upwards of fifty years, a testament to the soundness of its design." (039-003)

## Industrial Significance

The [[portsmouth|Portsmouth]] block-making installation was a landmark in the history of [[machine-tool|machine tools]] and mass production. It demonstrated that complex wooden components could be manufactured to precise, interchangeable standards using [[henry-maudslay|Maudslay's]] precision [[lathe|lathes]] and other specialised machinery. The techniques developed at [[portsmouth|Portsmouth]] influenced manufacturing practice across British industry. (038-092)

## Key Figures

- [[marc-isambard-brunel|Marc Isambard Brunel]] — designed the block-making system (038-105)
- [[henry-maudslay|Henry Maudslay]] — built the machinery, establishing his reputation as Britain's foremost machine-tool maker (038-092)
- [[samuel-bentham|Samuel Bentham]] — Inspector-General of Naval Works who championed mechanisation at the dockyards
