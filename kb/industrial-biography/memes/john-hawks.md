# William Hawks

William Hawks was a blacksmith and [[iron-industry|iron manufacture]]r who founded the iron works of Hawks, Crawshay and Co. at [[newcastle-upon-tyne|Newcastle]]-upon-[[river-tyne|Tyne]] in 1754, establishing a firm that would become one of the leading iron businesses in the north of [[england|England]].

## Early Career

William Hawks began his career as a blacksmith, with his principal trade consisting in making claw-hammers for joiners. His skill and industry enabled him to build a thriving business. (023-177)

## Founding of Hawks, Crawshay and Co.

In 1754, Hawks established iron works at Newcastle-upon-Tyne. He became a successful and prosperous man, eventually growing into a large manufacturer of bar-iron. Over time, partners joined the business, and through the changes wrought by time, one of the [[Crawshay family|Richard Crawshay]] family members became a principal partner in the firm in 1842. (023-176, 023-178, 023-180)

By the mid-19th century, the works employed about 1,300 men. At a Christmas party given to the workforce, the proprietor declared that it was "a great privilege to stand at the head of the trade." (023-173, 023-174, 023-175)

## The Hawks-Crawshay Partnership

The firm of Hawks, Crawshay and Co. represented the union of a northern ironmaking enterprise with the great Welsh iron dynasty of the Crawshay family. The Newcastle works benefited from the capital and commercial connections that the Crawshay name brought, while contributing its own established reputation in bar-iron manufacture.

## Legacy

William Hawks exemplified the path from skilled artisan to industrial manufacturer that characterized the early [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]]. From making claw-hammers as a blacksmith, he built an enterprise that would sustain over a thousand workers and connect with one of Britain's most powerful ironmaking families.
