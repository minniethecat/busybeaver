# London

London was the commercial and financial capital of industrial Britain. While the city itself was not a centre of heavy industry like [[manchester|Manchester]] or [[south-wales|South Wales]], it was where provincial engineers and ironmasters came to sell their goods, secure patents, and establish their reputations. The city's docks and wharves handled the import of raw materials and the export of finished iron goods to markets across the empire. (008-133) (009-101)

## Commercial Hub

The concentration of capital and commerce in London made it the natural marketplace for the products of Britain's industrial districts. Iron from [[shropshire|Shropshire]], [[staffordshire|Staffordshire]], and [[south-wales|South Wales]] was traded through London merchants. The [[london-bridge|London Bridge]] area became a centre for the [[iron-industry|iron trade]], with merchants importing Swedish and Russian iron alongside British production. (009-101)

## Engineering and Invention

London attracted ambitious engineers from across the country. [[henry-maudslay|Henry Maudslay]] established his celebrated machine-tool works in London, and [[marc-isambard-brunel|Marc Isambard Brunel]] designed his [[thames-tunnel|[[thames|Thames]] Tunnel]] and [[block-making-machinery|block-making machinery]] there. The city's workshops and dockyards employed thousands of skilled mechanics. (002-117) (002-136)

> "He went to London, and worked at Maudslay's — the great school for mechanics." (002-117)

## Notable Institutions

- **The [[royal-society|Royal Society]]** — centre of scientific and engineering discourse
- **The [[tower-of-london|Tower of London]]** — the royal armoury, which commissioned iron ordnance from provincial ironworks (010-095)
- **[[portsmouth-dockyard|[[portsmouth|Portsmouth]] Dockyard]]** — though at [[portsmouth|Portsmouth]], its block-making machinery was designed in London by Brunel

## Key Figures

- [[henry-maudslay|Henry Maudslay]] — whose London workshop became the training ground for a generation of engineers (002-117)
- [[marc-isambard-brunel|Marc Isambard Brunel]] — worked in London on the [[thames-tunnel|Thames Tunnel]] and block machinery (002-117)
- [[joseph-bramah|Joseph Bramah]] — London-based inventor of the [[hydraulic-press|hydraulic press]]
