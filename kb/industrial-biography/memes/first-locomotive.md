The **first locomotive** to run upon a railroad was constructed by [[[richard-trevithick|Richard Trevithick]]](/richard-trevithick) for [[[samuel-homfray|Samuel Homfray]]](/samuel-homfray) in 1803 (024-107). The engine was employed to bring down metal from the furnaces to the Old Forge at [[penydarran|Penydarran]] in [[south-wales|South Wales]], demonstrating for the first time that steam could replace horses for rail haulage.

## Trevithick's 1803 Locomotive

Trevithick's pioneering locomotive ran on the tramroad at [[penydarran|Penydarran]], where Homfray's ironworks provided the industrial setting for this revolutionary trial. The high-pressure steam engine — Trevithick's own invention — was mounted on wheels and coupled to loaded wagons. Though the cast-iron plateway proved too fragile for the engine's weight, the principle was proven: steam locomotion on rails was viable (024-107).

## Middleton Colliery (1812)

A second first-locomotive milestone followed at Middleton Colliery near [[leeds|Leeds]], where a locomotive erected by Trevithick for John Blenkinsop became the first to regularly work upon a railway. This engine began running in 1812, hauling coal from the Middleton [[coal-mining|colliery]] to [[leeds|Leeds]] (045-166). Blenkinsop's rack-and-pinion system used a toothed rail to overcome the limited adhesion of smooth wheels on iron rails.

## Legacy

Trevithick's demonstrations paved the way for the later locomotive developments of [[[george-stephenson|George Stephenson]]](/george-stephenson) and his son [[[robert-stephenson|Robert Stephenson]]](/robert-stephenson). The [[Rainhill Trials]] of 1829, where Stephenson's [[Rocket]] won the competition for the [[Liverpool and [[manchester|Manchester]] Railway]], directly descended from Trevithick's experiments. The [[Locomotive]] became the defining transport technology of the nineteenth century.
