The **Admiralty** was the British government department responsible for the command of the Royal Navy. During the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]], its procurement decisions and technical evaluations had a decisive influence on the adoption of iron and steam-powered machinery.

## Role in Iron and Engineering

The Admiralty served as a crucial early customer and validator for industrial iron technologies. [[[henry-cort|Henry Cort]]](/henry-cort)'s wrought iron was formally approved by the Admiralty after testing (001-216), and the Admiralty's favorable assessment of Cort's process against imported Oregrounds iron in 1787 led to the discontinuation of the latter (022-103).

[[[henry-maudslay|Henry Maudslay]]](/henry-maudslay)'s block-making machinery — one of the earliest examples of automated mass production — was developed for the Admiralty's [[portsmouth|Portsmouth]] dockyard. Maudslay offered his invention to the naval authorities, and the system was completed under [[[samuel-bentham|Sir Samuel Bentham]]](/samuel-bentham), Inspector-General of Naval Works, in 1801 (038-163).

## Steam and Naval Innovation

The Admiralty also evaluated [[[james-nasmyth|James Nasmyth]]](/james-nasmyth)'s steam hammer design (047-075), and Nasmyth later presented plans for elongated rifle projectiles to the Secretary of War in 1855 (047-075).
