Chepstow is a town in [[monmouthshire|Monmouthshire]], Wales, situated on the River Wye near its confluence with the [[severn|River Severn]]. Its Norman castle served various functions through English history, including as a place of detention for political prisoners during the seventeenth-century upheavals.

## Seventeenth-Century Imprisonment

Major Wildman, one of the chiefs of the Republican party during the Commonwealth period, was seized at Exton, near Marlborough, in 1654. (012-027) Following his capture, he was imprisoned in Chepstow Castle. (012-028) Wildman was a significant figure aligned with the republican faction, and his detention at Chepstow reflects the castle's continuing role as a secure Crown fortress during the turbulent politics of the mid-seventeenth century.
