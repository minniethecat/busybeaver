The **British Museum**, founded in 1753, was Britain's national museum and a repository of scientific and industrial collections. Its holdings and scholarly staff intersected with the history of engineering in several notable ways.

## Industrial and Scientific Collections

The museum preserved significant industrial artifacts. [[[joseph-bramah|Joseph Bramah]]](/joseph-bramah)'s [[Bramah Lock|challenge lock]], which remained unpicked for over sixty years, was eventually deposited in the British Museum (014-147), where it stood as a testament to British precision engineering. The museum's collections also included early ordnance alongside those at the [[Woolwich Arsenal]] and the [[Tower of London]].

The museum's scholarly staff contributed to the documentation of engineering history. Dr. Gray of the British Museum claimed to be the inventor of a postal system later credited to Sir Rowland Hill (031-119), demonstrating the museum's role as a center of intellectual innovation as well as preservation.
