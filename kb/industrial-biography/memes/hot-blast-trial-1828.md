# [[hot-blast|Hot Blast]] Trial 1828

In 1828, [[james-beaumont-neilson|James Beaumont Neilson]] successfully demonstrated his hot blast process at [[clyde-iron-works|[[clyde|Clyde]] Iron Works]] near [[glasgow|Glasgow]]. Fuel consumption fell by over 50 percent, and the process spread rapidly through the Scottish and then [[iron-industry|British iron industry]]. (027-172)

## Context

This event occurred during the transformative era of the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]] in Britain.

## Significance

The event contributed to the advancement of iron-making and engineering technology.
