# Richard Arkwright

Richard Arkwright (1732–1792) was the inventor of the spinning-frame and one of the central figures in the mechanisation of the British cotton industry. His machinery, along with that of Hargreaves and Crompton, transformed textile manufacturing in the late eighteenth century. (030-034)

## 职业生涯

### 发明 Spinning-Frame

Arkwright's principal contribution to the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]] was the spinning-frame, a machine that revolutionised cotton spinning by producing stronger yarn than was possible with the spinning-jenny. Alongside the inventions of Hargreaves, Higley, and others, Arkwright's spinning-frame formed the technological basis of the [[industrial-revolution|factory system]]. (031-125)

### 工厂体系

Arkwright's machinery was widely adopted in the cotton districts of [[lancashire|Lancashire]] and beyond. The great firms of Arkwright and his licensees became the model for the factory system that would spread throughout British industry. (029-055) Employment from the great firms of Arkwright provided livelihoods for thousands of operatives in the expanding cotton towns. (045-068)

## 影响与评价

Arkwright's spinning-frame was one of the key inventions that shifted textile production from the cottage to the factory. His machinery was in the cotton districts, and the employment from the great firms of Arkwright provided livelihoods for thousands of operatives. (029-055) (045-068) His business acumen — securing patents, licensing his technology, and organising production on an industrial scale — made him as much an entrepreneur as an inventor, and he was knighted in recognition of his contributions to British industry.
