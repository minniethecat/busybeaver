# Aberdare

Town in the Cynon Valley of [[glamorgan|Glamorganshire]], South Wales, noted in the Industrial Biography as one of the earliest sites of [[iron-industry|iron manufacture]] in Wales, where [[sussex|Sussex]] ironmasters established forges in the sixteenth and seventeenth centuries.

## 地理位置

Aberdare lies in the Cynon Valley in the upland region of northern [[glamorgan|Glamorgan]]shire, approximately 4 miles south-west of Merthyr Tydfil and 20 miles north of [[cardiff|Cardiff]]. The valley is drained by the River Cynon, a tributary of the Taff, and the surrounding hills contained abundant timber and ironstone deposits. These natural resources — wood for charcoal and iron ore for smelting — attracted ironmasters from the Weald of Sussex when their own local supplies began to run short. (008-143)

## 工业历史

### 早期铁业迁移

The iron industry at Aberdare began as an offshoot of the Sussex [[iron-industry|iron trade]]. Some Sussex ironmasters migrated to Glamorganshire in South Wales specifically because of the abundance of timber and ironstone in the district. They set up their forges at Aberdare and Merthyr Tydfil, transplanting the skills, capital, and organisation of the Wealden iron industry into the Welsh valleys. (008-143)

Mr. Llewellin published an account of these early ironworking settlers and their proceedings at Aberdare, providing descriptions of their works, the remains of which still existed at Llwydcoed, Pontyryns, and other places in the Aberdare valley. Among the Sussex masters who settled in Glamorganshire for the purpose of carrying on the iron manufacture was Walter Burrell, a member of a prominent Sussex iron-making family. (008-146)

### 繁荣与转型

The forges of the Aberdare valley operated on the charcoal method throughout the seventeenth century. As charcoal gave way to coke smelting in the eighteenth century, the focus of the iron industry shifted northward towards Merthyr Tydfil, where the great works of [[dowlais-ironworks|Dowlais]], Cyfarthfa, and Penydarran came to dominate Welsh iron production. Aberdare itself transformed in the nineteenth century into a coal-mining centre, with numerous collieries worked in the surrounding hills.

## 重要人物与企业

- Walter Burrell — Sussex ironmaster who settled in Glamorganshire to continue iron manufacture
- The Sussex ironmasters — collective name for the English families who established the early Welsh iron industry
- Early ironworks at Llwydcoed, Pontyryns, and locations in the Aberdare valley
