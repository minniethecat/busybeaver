# Low Moor Iron Works

Low Moor Iron Works was a major iron-making establishment located near [[bradford|Bradford]] in Yorkshire, [[england|England]]. Known for producing high-quality iron, the works operated from the late 18th century and became an important supplier of iron for engineering and railway applications during the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]].

## Founding

The Low Moor Iron Works was established around 1790, taking advantage of the rich coal and ironstone deposits of the Yorkshire coalfield. The works grew steadily through the early 19th century, producing a reputation for quality iron that was widely recognised across British industry.

## Products and Innovation

### Steam Hammer Innovation

Low Moor Iron Works is particularly noted for its collaboration with the engineer James Nasmyth in the development of improved steam hammer controls. Smiles records that "Mr. Nasmyth has lately introduced, with the assistance of Mr. Wilson of the Low Moor Iron Works, a new, exceedingly ingenious, and very simple contrivance for working the hammer." (051-148)

This innovation allowed precise control of the hammer's stroke length, striking force, and variation — all operated through a single lever. It represented a significant improvement in the versatility and precision of the steam hammer, one of the key machine tools of the industrial age.

## Legacy

Low Moor Iron Works continued production well into the 20th century, with its iron known for strength and consistency. It supplied materials for railways, bridges, and heavy engineering projects. The works exemplified the Yorkshire iron industry's contribution to Britain's industrial dominance in the 19th century.
