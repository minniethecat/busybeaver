# Gloucester

Gloucester is a cathedral city in South West [[england|England]], situated on the River [[severn|Severn]]. During the medieval period, it was a centre for iron-working, and it later featured in the military campaigns of the English Civil War.

## 地理位置

Gloucester lies on the eastern bank of the River Severn, at a crossing point that made it strategically important for trade and military movements. Its position connected the iron-producing Forest of Dean with the Bristol Channel, facilitating the transport of iron goods. (006-180)

## 工业历史

### 早期发展

Gloucester's iron-working tradition dates back to the Anglo-Saxon period. During the reign of Edward the Confessor, the city's tribute consisted almost entirely of iron rods manufactured to a size suitable for making nails for the king's ships. (006-180)

### Civil War Period

During the English Civil War, Gloucester was a [[house-of-commons|Parliament]]arian stronghold besieged by Royalist forces. Dud [[dudley|Dudley]], the ironmaster and military engineer, was present at the siege in 1643. After escaping from captivity, Dudley passed through Gloucester on crutches, travelling via [[worcestershire|Worcester]] and Tewkesbury en route to Bristol. (011-007) (011-065)

## 重要人物与企业

Gloucester's iron industry was connected to the broader Severn valley [[iron-industry|iron trade]]. The Forest of Dean, a major iron-producing region, lay nearby, and the city served as a commercial hub for iron goods moving downriver toward Bristol and export markets.
