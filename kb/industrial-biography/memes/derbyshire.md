# [[derby|Derby]]shire

Derbyshire was one of the earliest iron-smelting districts in Britain. The heaps of mining refuse left by early smelters in the valleys and along the hill-sides of North Derbyshire bear witness to centuries of iron production that long predated the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]]. (004-113)

## Industrial History

### Ancient Ironworks

The mining heaps of North Derbyshire are still spoken of by country people as "old man" or the "old man's work." These remains are the visible legacy of bloomery smelters who worked the local ores with charcoal fuel, centuries before the age of coal. (004-113) (004-114)

The traces of early British ironworkers extend far beyond Derbyshire. From Dartmoor to the Moray Firth, the plough continues to turn up fresh evidence of their activity. But the Derbyshire remains are among the most notable and well-documented in the country. (004-115)

### Regional Context

Derbyshire shared in the early iron-making tradition of the English Midlands, alongside [[staffordshire|Staffordshire]] and [[shropshire|Shropshire]]. The county's iron deposits and woodland for charcoal made it a natural centre for the bloomery process, before the shift to [[coke-smelting|coke smelting]]. (004-113)
