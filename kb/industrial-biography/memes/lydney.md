Lydney is a settlement on the western edge of the [[forest-of-dean|Forest of Dean]] in [[gloucester|Gloucester]]shire, a region with one of the longest continuous histories of iron-working in Britain. The area's iron industry was already well established by the thirteenth century.

## Medieval Iron Forges

The Earl of Warwick had forges at work in his woods at Lydney during the medieval period. (007-031) In 1282, as many as 72 forges were leased from the Crown by various iron-smelters in the same Forest of Dean, indicating the scale and intensity of iron production in the district at that time. (007-032) The Crown's iron-working rights in the forest had earlier been commuted in 1258 for Abbot's Wood of 872 acres, which was held by the abbey until its dissolution in the reign of Henry VIII. (007-028)
