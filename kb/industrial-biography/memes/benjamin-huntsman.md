# Benjamin Huntsman

Benjamin Huntsman was born in Lincolnshire to German immigrant parents. Trained as a clockmaker, he became dissatisfied with available steel for clock springs and began experiments. Around 1740 he perfected the [[cast-steel|crucible steel]] process, melting blister steel in sealed clay crucibles at extreme temperatures to produce homogeneous cast steel. [[sheffield|Sheffield]] cutlers initially rejected his steel, forcing him to export to France. (019-107)
