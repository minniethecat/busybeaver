The **[[liverpool|Liverpool]] and Manchester Railway**, opened in 1830, was the world's first inter-city railway to carry both passengers and freight by [[locomotive|steam locomotive]]. Its opening marked the birth of modern railway engineering and created an entirely new field for mechanical industry.

## Significance to Engineering

The railway's opening had a profound effect on the engineering profession. [[[richard-roberts|Richard Roberts]]](/richard-roberts) recognized the Liverpool and Manchester Railway as marking one of the chief branches of future mechanical engineering (047-007). He seized the opportunity presented by this new field, contributing to locomotive design and construction at his [[manchester|Manchester]] works.

The railway's locomotives were built by various engineering firms. By the end of the railway's early expansion period, locomotives were received from the railway's own workshops and outside contractors (049-017). [[[george-stephenson|George Stephenson]]](/george-stephenson) served as the railway's engineer, and his [[Rocket]] locomotive won the [[Rainhill Trials]] in 1829 that selected the railway's motive power.
