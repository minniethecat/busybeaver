# [[cranege-brothers|Thomas Cranege]]

Thomas Cranege and his brother George, working at [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] under the advice of [[richard-reynolds|Richard Reynolds]], patented a reverberatory furnace in 1766 that converted pig iron using pit-coal without the fuel contacting the metal. The term [[puddling-process|puddling]] was first used in their patent specification. (016-166)

## Early Life and Career

Thomas Cranege worked during the transformative era of British industrialisation.

## Major Contributions

Their work advanced industrial technology and manufacturing methods.
