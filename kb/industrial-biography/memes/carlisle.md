# Carlisle

Ancient border city and county town of [[cumberland|Cumberland]] in North West [[england|England]], appearing in the Industrial Biography as a workplace of engineer Joseph Clement and a staging post on the industrial travellers' route between [[lancashire|Lancashire]] and Scotland.

## 地理位置

Carlisle occupies a strategic position on the River Eden near the Solway Firth, approximately 10 miles south of the Scottish border. Its location at the western end of the Anglo-Scottish border made it a natural garrison town and market centre. During the industrial period, Carlisle served as a key waypoint on the route between the manufacturing districts of Lancashire and the growing industrial centres of Glasgow and [[edinburgh|Edinburgh]]. (054-218)

## 工业历史

### 纺织工程

Carlisle became an important centre for textile engineering in the early nineteenth century. The firm of Forster and Sons employed the young engineer Joseph Clement for two years, during which he worked on the construction of [[power-loom|power loom]]s. Clement had previously worked at Kirby Stephen making similar machinery, and his time at Carlisle represented a step in his progression towards becoming one of the most skilled draughtsmen and toolmakers of his generation. (042-021)

The textile industry in Carlisle was built on the cotton and linen trades, with several mills established along the River Caldew and River Eden. The city's engineering workshops supplied machinery not only to local mills but also to manufacturers in Scotland and the north of England.

### 旅行者之路

Carlisle's position on the great road to Scotland made it a regular stopping place for industrial travellers. The narrative of the two Chowbent mechanics who walked from Lancashire to Glasgow in search of work in the 1790s records them reaching Carlisle after stages through Preston and Chorley. At Carlisle, a Mr. Smith — whose firm had supplied carding engines and spinning jennies to a small mill in the north — assisted the travellers with provisions and directions for the onward journey into Scotland. (054-218)
