# Bristol

Bristol was an important port and centre of the [[iron-industry|iron trade]] in the seventeenth and early eighteenth centuries, playing a key role in the early career of the [[abraham-darby|Darby]] family before their epoch-making move to [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]]. The city's foundries produced cast-iron pots and other goods shipped nationally and abroad. (001-171) (011-065)

## Industrial History

### Early Iron Trade

The manufacture of cast-iron pots at Bristol was well established by the late 1600s, with metal brought from distant furnaces and smelted at the city's port-side works. Iron was sent from the [[forest-of-dean|Forest of Dean]] and other producing regions to Bristol, where it was refined into finished goods for domestic and colonial markets. (004-132) (011-065) The iron was transported by river and road — pigs of iron from [[gloucester|Gloucester]]shire were carried to Bristol with considerable difficulty before the improvement of turnpike roads. (011-069)

### Abraham Darby's Bristol Years

[[abraham-darby|Abraham Darby]], a smith of the city of Bristol, was apprenticed to a malster in [[birmingham|Birmingham]] but returned to Bristol where he was joined by three partners to establish a brass and [[foundry|iron foundry]]. (015-152) (015-179) The Bristol firm prospered, but Darby soon realised the limitations of the charcoal-fueled processes available at the port. In 1709 he abandoned the Bristol firm and moved to [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] in [[shropshire|Shropshire]], where he successfully smelted iron with coke — an innovation that transformed the iron industry. (016-005)

### Later Industry

Other iron-working enterprises continued at Bristol after Darby's departure. An attempt was made to erect a new furnace for the Bristol works, and other works were erected near Bristol and in the Forest of Kingswood. (011-103) (011-108) (011-114) The city remained an active port for the [[iron-industry|iron trade]], though its relative importance declined as inland centres like [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] and [[sheffield|Sheffield]] rose to dominance.
