# Bramah [[hydraulic-press|Hydraulic Press]] 1795

In 1795, [[joseph-bramah|Joseph Bramah]] patented his Hydrostatic or [[hydraulic-press|Hydraulic Press]], the first practical machine based on Pascal's principle of fluid pressure. The self-sealing leather collar was the key innovation that made hydraulic power practical. (033-103)

## Context

This event occurred during the transformative era of the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]] in Britain.

## Significance

The event contributed to the advancement of iron-making and engineering technology.
