# Bolton

Manufacturing town in Lancashire, known in the Industrial Biography as the family home of the Maudslays and as a centre of textile engineering with firms such as Hick and Eckersley supplying machinery across Britain.

## 地理位置

Bolton lies in south-central Lancashire, approximately 10 miles north-west of Manchester. The town is situated on the River Croal, a tributary of the Irwell, and sits on the Lancashire coalfield, which provided fuel for its early industrial development. Its position within a network of canals and turnpike roads connected Bolton to Manchester, [[liverpool|Liverpool]], and the wider manufacturing districts of the North West. (035-097)

## 工业历史

### 纺织工程

Bolton emerged as a significant centre of textile manufacture and engineering during the late eighteenth and early nineteenth centuries. The town was home to numerous [[mule-spinning|cotton-spinning]] mills, bleaching works, and machine shops. Engineering firms such as Benjamin Hick and Rushton and Eckersley of Bolton gained national reputations for the quality of their millwork, steam engines, and machinery. (049-142)

James Nasmyth sent illustrated sketches of his steam-hammer design to firms across Britain, including "Rushton and Eckersley of Bolton," seeking a manufacturer willing to take on the project. The Bolton firm was among a select group of engineering works capable of executing such advanced designs — a testament to the town's engineering capabilities. (049-142)

### Maudslay 家族的家乡

The family of Henry Maudslay, the great [[london|London]] machine-tool pioneer, had deep roots in the Bolton district. Maudslay's father, William Maudslay, belonged to the neighbourhood of Bolton, where he worked as a joiner. The family had been scattered from their earlier home in the north of [[england|England]], with several members becoming skilled workmen. William Maudslay's trade in Lancashire consisted principally of making the wooden framing for cotton machinery — work that required precision joinery and an understanding of mechanical principles. (035-097)

### 自学与互助文化

Bolton was notable for the culture of self-improvement among its working men. John Hick, a prominent civil engineer of Bolton, delivered lectures on "Self Help" before the Holy Trinity Working Men's Association of the town. These lectures, inspired by the example of self-taught mechanics like [[George Stephenson]], promoted the idea that working men could raise themselves through diligence and study — a philosophy that resonated strongly in the manufacturing towns of Lancashire. (051-003)

## 重要人物与企业

- Benjamin Hick — civil engineer and manufacturer of Bolton
