# Samuel Homfray

Samuel Homfray was one of the great Welsh ironmasters of the late eighteenth and early nineteenth centuries. As proprietor of the [[penydarran|Penydarran Ironworks]] in [[south-wales|South Wales]], he adopted the innovations of [[henry-cort|Henry Cort]] and was among the first to employ [[richard-trevithick|Richard Trevithick]]'s [[locomotive|steam locomotive]] for industrial use. (024-080) (022-083)

## 职业生涯

### Penydarran 铁厂

Samuel Homfray of [[penydarran|Penydarran]] was a contemporary and rival of [[richard-crawshay|Richard Crawshay]] of Cyfartha. Together with other ironmasters, he adopted the [[puddling-process|puddling]] and rolling inventions of [[henry-cort|Henry Cort]], applying them on an industrial scale in [[south-wales|South Wales]]. (022-083) (001-215) Homfray was equally prompt in adopting new methods, and under his management the [[penydarran|Penydarran Works]] became one of the most productive iron manufacturing establishments in the district. (022-094)

### Trevithick 机车

In 1803, Homfray employed [[richard-trevithick|Richard Trevithick]] to construct a [[locomotive|steam locomotive]] for use at the [[penydarran|Penydarran Works]] — one of the earliest applications of steam power to railway transport. The locomotive was constructed by Trevithick for Mr. Homfray, marking an important milestone in the development of railway locomotion. (024-108) In conjunction with Mr. Homfray of the [[penydarran|Penydarran Works]], Trevithick pressed forward with his experiments in high-pressure steam. (023-154)

## 影响与评价

Homfray's willingness to back new technologies — from Cort's [[puddling-process|puddling]] process to Trevithick's locomotive — made him one of the most forward-looking ironmasters of his generation. The [[penydarran|Penydarran Works]] under his direction exemplified the spirit of innovation that made [[south-wales|South Wales]] the leading iron-producing region of Britain.
