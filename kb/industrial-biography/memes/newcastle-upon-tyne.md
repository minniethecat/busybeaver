# Newcastle upon [[river-tyne|Tyne]]

Newcastle upon Tyne was one of the great industrial cities of northern [[england|England]], built on coal and engineering. The city's collieries supplied fuel to [[london|London]] and the south, while its workshops produced some of the earliest [[locomotive|steam locomotive]]s and iron bridges. (023-176)

## Industrial History

### Coal and Iron

Newcastle's prosperity was founded on the [[northumberland|Northumberland]] coalfield, which was mined from the Middle Ages and shipped from the [[river-tyne|Tyne]] to [[london|London]] and beyond. By the 18th century, the region was also a centre of iron production, supplying the raw material for bridges, rails, and machinery. (010-161)

### Engineering and [[locomotive|Locomotive]]s

The workshops of Newcastle became famous for locomotive building. [[george-stephenson|George Stephenson]] was not a native, but he acknowledged that he "owed almost everything to Newcastle" — it was there that he learned his trade as an engine-wright and built his earliest locomotives at the [[stephenson-works|Killingworth colliery]]. (056-170) (056-171)

> "Although not a native of Newcastle, he owed almost everything to Newcastle. He got the rudiments of his education there, and there he built his first locomotive." (056-170)

### High Level Bridge

The High Level Bridge at Newcastle, one of the most important early railway bridges, was designed by [[robert-stephenson|Robert Stephenson]] and completed in 1849. It carried the railway across the [[river-tyne|Tyne]] at a height that allowed shipping to pass beneath, a landmark of Victorian engineering. (050-100)
