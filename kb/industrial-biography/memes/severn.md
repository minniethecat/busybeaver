# River Severn

The River Severn, flowing from the Welsh mountains through [[shropshire|Shropshire]] to the [[bristol|Bristol]] Channel, was the principal transport artery of the early [[iron-industry|British iron industry]]. Iron from the furnaces of [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] and the surrounding districts was carried down the Severn to [[bristol|Bristol]] and beyond. (014-073)

## Industrial Significance

### Iron Transport

The Severn's navigable waters enabled the [[shropshire|Shropshire]] ironmasters to ship their products to distant markets. The river gave access to the iron-furnaces of the Severn Gorge, where [[abraham-darby|Abraham Darby]] and his successors established the works that produced the first iron bridge. (014-073) (014-163)

Loading-places along the river Severn were busy with barges carrying pig iron, castings, and [[wrought-iron|bar iron]] to [[bristol|Bristol]] and other ports for export. (017-005)

### The First [[iron-bridge|Iron Bridge]]

The most celebrated engineering achievement on the Severn was the [[iron-bridge|Iron Bridge]], thrown across the river by [[abraham-darby|Abraham Darby III]] and the [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] ironmasters in 1779. This was the world's first [[iron-bridge|cast-iron bridge]], a structure that demonstrated the potential of iron as a building material and became an icon of the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]]. (017-021)

The iron bridge over the Severn was found altogether adequate to the demands placed upon it, and it inspired a generation of engineers to build iron bridges across rivers throughout Britain. (017-026)

### Canal Connections

The Severn was connected by canal to the [[thames|Thames]] and [[trent|Trent]] river systems, creating an integrated water-transport network across the English Midlands. (013-002) (014-163) The [[staffordshire|Staffordshire]] and [[worcestershire|Worcestershire]] canal, which connected to the Severn at Stourport, was among the successful water communications that extended the river's reach. (013-151) (012-148)
