# Blackfriars Bridge

Blackfriars Bridge spans the [[thames|River Thames]] in [[london|London]] and was, in the early 19th century, the site of the engineering works of [[john-rennie|John Rennie]], the celebrated engineer. Rennie's establishment at the south end of Blackfriars Bridge became an important training ground for young engineers and millwrights. (053-076)

## Industrial Significance

### Rennie's Works

The engineering works situated at the south end of Blackfriars Bridge were among the most important in [[london|London]] during the early 1800s. [[john-rennie|John Rennie]], already celebrated for his work on canals, docks, and bridges, operated from this location and employed a generation of skilled mechanics. (053-076)

### Training Ground for Engineers

Rennie's Blackfriars works served as a practical school of engineering. When young aspirants such as [[james-nasmyth|James Nasmyth]] sought employment, Rennie sent them to his foreman at the Blackfriars works with instructions to set them to work. (053-076)

The foreman referred the newcomers to the secretary of the [[millwright|Millwright]]s' Society, introducing them into the organised world of [[london|London]]'s mechanical trades. Through this gateway passed many of the engineers who would shape Britain's industrial infrastructure. (053-076)

### Bridge Design Competitions

Blackfriars was also the site of bridge engineering competitions. One of the early independent efforts of [[william-fairbairn|William Fairbairn]] was the preparation of a design for a [[iron-bridge|cast-iron bridge]] over the Irwell at Blackfriars, for which a prize was offered. The attempt was unsuccessful, but the effort proved the beginning of many successful designs. (054-038)
