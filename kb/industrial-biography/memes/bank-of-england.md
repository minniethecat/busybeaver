The **Bank of [[england|England]]**, founded in 1694, was the central financial institution of Britain during the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]]. Beyond its monetary role, the Bank played a notable part in the development of precision engineering through its demand for secure banknote printing machinery.

## Engineering Connections

The Bank's most significant engineering contribution came in 1806 when it approached [[[joseph-bramah|Joseph Bramah]]](/joseph-bramah) to construct a machine for more accurately and expeditiously printing the numbers and date lines on banknotes (034-080). Bramah, already renowned for his [[Bramah Lock]] and [[Bramah [[hydraulic-press|Hydraulic Press]] 1795|hydraulic press]], designed a numbering machine that continued in regular use at the Bank for decades (034-086). The machine's precision demonstrated the practical value of machine tools in document security.

The Bank had earlier intervened in industrial affairs when it petitioned against [[[andrew-yarranton|Andrew Yarranton]]](/andrew-yarranton)'s scheme for ironworks development, leading to the proposal being dropped (015-008).
