# South Wales

South Wales emerged as Britain's foremost iron-producing region during the late 18th century. The combination of abundant ironstone, coal, and limestone within a compact geographical area made [[glamorgan|Glamorganshire]] and [[monmouthshire|Monmouthshire]] the natural centre of the [[iron-industry|iron trade]]. (008-141)

## Industrial History

### Early Development

The iron industry in South Wales grew from modest beginnings. Early ironmasters were drawn to [[glamorgan|Glamorgan]]shire by the easy availability of raw materials — iron ore, coal for fuel, and limestone for flux — all found in close proximity. (053-170)

> "The mineral wealth of the district was its making; the [[iron-industry|iron trade]] took root and flourished there as nowhere else in the kingdom." (053-174)

### Prosperity Period

By the early 1800s, South Wales was the dominant iron-producing region of Britain. The great ironworks of [[cyfarthfa-ironworks|Cyfarthfa]], [[dowlais-ironworks|Dowlais]], and [[penydarran|Penydarran]] turned out vast quantities of pig iron and [[wrought-iron|bar iron]]. Families such as the [[richard-crawshay|Crawshays]], Guests, and [[samuel-homfray|Homfrays]] built industrial empires in the Welsh valleys. (022-084) (026-157) (029-042)

### Later Years

The region maintained its dominance through the railway age, with South Wales iron shipped to markets across Britain and abroad. The transition from charcoal to coke smelting, and later the adoption of the [[neilson-hot-blast|hot blast]], kept the Welsh ironmasters competitive. (029-042)

## Key Figures and Enterprises

- [[richard-crawshay|Richard Crawshay]] — the "great ironmaster" of [[cyfarthfa-ironworks|Cyfarthfa]]
- [[samuel-homfray|Samuel Homfray]] — proprietor of [[penydarran|[[penydarran|Penydarran]] Ironworks]]
- [[john-josiah-guest|John Josiah Guest]] — master of [[dowlais-ironworks|Dowlais]], the largest ironworks in the world
- [[william-reynolds|William Reynolds]] — [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] partner whose inclined-plane invention was adopted in South Wales (023-111)
