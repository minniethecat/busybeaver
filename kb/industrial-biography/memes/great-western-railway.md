# Great Western Railway

The Great Western Railway (GWR) was a major British railway company founded in 1833 and engineered by Isambard Kingdom Brunel. It linked [[london|London]] with the West Country, [[south-wales|South Wales]], and the West Midlands, and became known as "God's Wonderful Railway" for its engineering excellence.

## Founding

The GWR was established by a group of [[bristol|Bristol]] merchants who sought a rail link to London. Brunel was appointed as chief engineer in 1833, and he made two key technical decisions: the route would follow a gentle gradient west from London, and the track would use a broad gauge of 7 ft 1⁄4 in (2,140 mm) — significantly wider than the 4 ft 8 1⁄2 in standard gauge used elsewhere. Brunel argued that the broader gauge allowed for larger, more stable locomotives capable of higher speeds.

## Engineering and Innovation

### Locomotive Works

The GWR built its own locomotive works at Swindon, which became one of the largest railway engineering facilities in the world. The railway was a major customer for locomotive builders, including James Nasmyth, who developed his "Steam Arm" planing machine specifically to handle a large order of locomotives for the Great Western Railway. (050-126)

### Broad Gauge

Brunel's broad gauge enabled the GWR to operate some of the fastest trains of the era. The debate between broad and standard gauge came to a head in the "Gauge War" of the 1840s, and the Railway Regulation (Gauge) Act of 1846 effectively mandated standard gauge for new lines. The GWR continued operating broad gauge on its core network until the final conversion to standard gauge in 1892.

## Legacy

The Great Western Railway remained an independent company until nationalisation in 1948, when it became part of British Railways. The GWR name was revived in 2015 when First Great Western adopted the Great Western Railway brand for its franchise operations. Brunel's engineering — including the Box Tunnel, Maidenhead Bridge, and Paddington Station — remains among the most celebrated achievements of the railway age.
