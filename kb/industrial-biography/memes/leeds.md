# Leeds

Leeds was one of the foremost centres of mechanical engineering and machine-making in northern [[england|England]] during the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]]. The town produced some of the age's most celebrated engineers and was at the heart of the [[yorkshire|Yorkshire]] coal and iron district. (001-057) (001-059)

## Industrial History

### Early Coal and Iron

The neighbourhood of Leeds had been a site of iron-working from an early period. Iron-smelting was carried on in the district using bloomeries, and coal was mined at Middleton near Leeds from the early seventeenth century. (007-035) (007-048) The Thoresby collection records that iron was dug north-east of Leeds, providing the raw material for local smiths and founders. (007-056) By the mid-eighteenth century, coal-mining had become extensive, with the Middleton [[coal-mining|colliery]] railway opened as one of the earliest in the kingdom. (045-169)

### Engineering and Machine-Making

[[matthew-murray|Matthew Murray]] of Leeds was one of the great figures of early nineteenth-century mechanical engineering. Starting as a blacksmith, Murray rose to become a leading designer of [[steam-engine]]s and textile machinery, with works at the Round [[foundry|Foundry]] that rivalled those of [[james-watt|Boulton and Watt]]. (031-154) (045-102) Murray's firm also supplied locomotives, and it was on the Middleton [[coal-mining|colliery]] railway near Leeds that his engines began to run in 1812. (045-169)

Later, the firm of [[peter-fairbairn|Peter Fairbairn]] of Leeds continued the town's engineering tradition, specialising in flax-spinning machinery and machine tools. (045-049) The town also became a centre of locomotive building, and [[matthew-murray|Matthew Murray]] of Leeds was listed among the foremost engineers of his generation alongside [[richard-roberts|Richard Roberts]] of [[manchester|Manchester]] and others. (045-103)

### Modern Industry

By the mid-nineteenth century, Leeds had diversified into many branches of manufacture, large quantities of machinery and locomotives being turned out for both the home market and export. (045-186)
