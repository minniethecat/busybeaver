## Biography

Peter Fairbairn was a machine-tool maker based in [[leeds|Leeds]], part of the northern school of mechanical engineers who transformed British manufacturing during the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]]. He was the younger brother of the renowned engineer [[William Fairbairn]].

## Career

Peter Fairbairn established himself as a machine-tool manufacturer in Leeds, alongside other notable figures such as Matthew Murray. His firm contributed to the development of precision engineering in the north of [[england|England]], and he was counted among the leading mechanical engineers of his generation, alongside figures such as [[james-fox|James Fox]] of [[derby|Derby]], Richard Roberts, Joseph Whitworth, and [[james-nasmyth|James Nasmyth]] of [[manchester|Manchester]].

## Civic Role and Honours

Fairbairn's prominence extended beyond engineering. He was knighted, becoming Sir Peter Fairbairn, and served as mayor of Leeds, reflecting his standing in both industrial and civic life.

## Family Connection

Peter Fairbairn was part of a remarkable engineering family. His elder brother, [[William Fairbairn]], became one of the most influential mechanical engineers and shipbuilders of the nineteenth century. In William Fairbairn's early years, he famously wheeled his little brother Peter about the family farm in a homemade vehicle.
