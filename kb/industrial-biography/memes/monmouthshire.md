# [[newport|Monmouthshire]]

County in South Wales, significant in early British industrial history as the location where tin-plate manufacturing was first established in Britain.

## Industrial History

### Tin-Plate Manufacture

Before the establishment of a tin-plate industry in Britain, all tin-plates were imported from Germany. Around 1720, Capel Hanbury established the first British tin-plate manufactory at [[Pontypool]] in Monmouthshire (013-108). The works continued to operate successfully for many decades, marking the beginning of Britain's domestic tin-plate industry.

### Iron and Coal

Monmouthshire formed part of the South Wales iron and coal district, together with neighbouring [[Glamorgan]] and [[Brecon]]. The region's geological wealth — coal measures and ironstone — made it one of Britain's principal iron-producing areas throughout the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries.
