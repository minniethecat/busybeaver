# Cwmbran

Cwmbran in [[south-wales|South Wales]] was part of the great Welsh iron belt that included [[merthyr-tydfil|Merthyr Tydfil]], Ebbw Vale, and Blaenavon. It became an important iron-making centre in the 19th century.

## Regional Context

The [[south-wales|South Wales]] iron district transformed from a remote hill region of difficult access into one of the world's greatest iron-producing centres in the late 18th and early 19th centuries. The district around Merthyr Tydvil was at once the richest and the dirtiest in all Britain during the height of its industrial activity. (023-158) (023-159)

The growth of the Welsh iron industry was fuelled by the abundant local coal and ironstone. In 1765, the entire mineral rights under forty square miles around Merthyr were leased for only £200 a year — a measure of how little value was then placed on the coal and iron that would fuel an industrial revolution. (023-120) (023-123)

## Industrial Development

Cwmbran and the surrounding valleys benefited from the transport improvements that opened up South Wales. The canal from Merthyr to [[cardiff|Cardiff]], completed in 1795 by [[richard-crawshay|Richard Crawshay]] in partnership with [[samuel-homfray|Samuel Homfray]], gave an immense impetus to the [[iron-industry|iron trade]] of the neighbourhood. (023-154) (023-156)

Numerous iron works became established across the region, and the Welsh iron industry grew to rival any in the world. The hot blast, adopted by many Welsh furnaces, further accelerated production by enabling the use of local coal directly in smelting.
