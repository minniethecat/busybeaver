# Joseph Bramah

Born in [[yorkshire|Yorkshire]], Joseph Bramah trained as a carpenter before establishing himself in [[london|London]]. His unpickable lock (1784) made his reputation, and his hydraulic press (1795) was the first practical application of Pascal's principle. His workshop became a training ground for [[henry-maudslay|Henry Maudslay]], and his inventions spanned locks, pumps, machine tools, and hydraulic machinery. (033-103)
