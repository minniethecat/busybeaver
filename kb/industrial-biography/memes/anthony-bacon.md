# Anthony Bacon

Anthony Bacon was an ironmaster and entrepreneur whose lease of mineral rights around Merthyr Tydfil in 1765 laid the foundation for what would become the greatest concentration of ironworks in Wales.

## The Merthyr Tydfil Lease

In 1765, at a time when land containing coal and iron was deemed of very little value, Anthony Bacon demonstrated remarkable foresight. He took a lease from Lord Talbot for ninety-nine years of the minerals under forty square miles of country surrounding the then insignificant hamlet of Merthyr Tydfil, at the trifling rental of £200 a year. (023-120, 023-122, 023-123)

## Iron Works and Government Contracts

On this mineral tract, Bacon erected iron works and supplied the British Government with considerable quantities of cannon and iron for various purposes. His enterprise helped establish [[south-wales|South Wales]] as a major centre of iron production. (023-123, 023-124)

## Retirement and Legacy

Having earned a competency from his ironmaking ventures, Bacon retired from business in 1782. He sublet his mineral tract in four divisions: the [[dowlais-ironworks|Dowlais]], the [[penydarran|Penydarran]], the Cyfartha, and the Plymouth Works — situated north, east, west, and south of Merthyr Tydfil respectively. (023-125, 023-127)

The domain became known as what [[David Mushet]] called "the Cyfartha flitch of the great Bacon domain." [[Richard Crawshay]] subsequently became the lessee of the Cyfartha division and carried on the works with increased vigor. (023-129, 023-130, 023-131)

## Significance

Anthony Bacon's 1765 lease was a pivotal moment in the industrialization of South Wales. The four ironworks that emerged from his original holding — Dowlais, Penydarran, Cyfartha, and Plymouth — became among the most important iron manufacturing centres in the world during the 19th century.
