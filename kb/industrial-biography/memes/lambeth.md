# Lambeth

Lambeth is a district on the south bank of the [[thames|River Thames]] in [[london|London]]. In the early 19th century, it became an important site for precision engineering, most notably as the location of [[Henry Maudslay]]'s engineering works.

## 地理位置

Lambeth lies on the south bank of the [[thames|Thames]], opposite Westminster. In the early 1800s, the area around Westminster Road was undeveloped marshland, with bulrushes and willows that attracted snipe and waterfowl. An enterprising riding-master had erected a riding-school on part of the marsh before Maudslay acquired land there for his expanding engineering business. (039-021)

## 工业历史

### Maudslay's Lambeth Works

After the success of his block-making machinery for [[Portsmouth Dockyard]], Henry Maudslay found his premises in Margaret Street too limited. He acquired a piece of ground in Westminster Road, Lambeth, and established what became known as the Lambeth Works. The location was convenient to the Thames, enabling transport of heavy machinery by water. (039-021)

The Lambeth Works became a centre of precision engineering, producing some of the finest machine tools of the age. Among the specimens preserved there was a micrometer screw of Maudslay's own design, used for dividing scales for astronomical purposes, which produced divisions so fine they could barely be detected without a magnifier. The screw was exhibited to the [[Society of Arts]] and remained carefully preserved at the Lambeth Works as a testament to Maudslay's skill. (039-142)

Lambeth also supported a community of skilled artisans and mechanics. In one account, a young workman at the Lambeth Works contrived a small cooking apparatus made by a local tinsmith in Lambeth at a cost of six shillings, enabling him to keep his weekly eating expenses within three shillings and sixpence. (048-149)
