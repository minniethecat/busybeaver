# Leicester

Leicester, a Midlands county and market town, was noted in the early modern period for its framework knitting industry and its place in the regional metalworking economy. The county was representative of the dispersed industrial activity that preceded the [[industrial-revolution|factory system]]. (006-109) (012-005)

## Industrial Character

### Early Industry

Leicester and its surrounding county were known for small-scale manufacturing and trades connected to the wool and knitting industries. The framework knitting machine, later developed by figures such as [[william-lee|William Lee]], found its earliest adoption in the East Midlands counties including Leicestershire. The region's industrial character was shaped by its position between the coal and iron districts of the West Midlands and the textile centres of the East. (012-005)

### Tools and Trades

The reference to Queen Elizabeth's gift of a whetstone tipped with gold to the Earl of Leicester indicates the cultural importance of edged tools and metalworking in the region. Whetstones were essential to the cutlery and metalworking trades that flourished in the Midlands during the 16th and 17th centuries. (006-109)
