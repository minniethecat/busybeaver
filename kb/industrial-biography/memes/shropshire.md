# Shropshire

Shropshire served as the cradle of the [[iron-industry|British iron industry]] in the 18th century. The county's rich seams of iron-ore and coal, combined with the enterprise of the [[Darby]] and [[Reynolds]] families at [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] and [[ketley|Ketley]], transformed the [[severn|Severn]] valley into the most important iron-producing district in Britain. (016-006) (017-167)

## Industrial Significance

### The [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] Works

The story of Shropshire iron begins with [[abraham-darby|Abraham Darby]], who removed to [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] in Shropshire in 1709 and took the lease of a small furnace to prosecute the iron-smelting business on his own account. (016-006) The castings of [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] gradually acquired a reputation, and the works expanded under successive generations of the Darby family, until [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] became the centre of British iron founding. (016-040)

> "Ford, of Coalbrookdale in Shropshire, from iron-ore and coal, both got in the same dale, makes iron brittle or tough as he pleases." (016-068)

### [[richard-reynolds|Richard Reynolds]] and Innovation

In 1763, [[richard-reynolds|Richard Reynolds]] removed to [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] to take charge of the works. Under his management, the [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] Works enjoyed the highest prosperity, and many important improvements were effected there. (016-087) (016-089) Reynolds notably adopted iron rails for the first time in the [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] tram-roads, an innovation that would later prove fundamental to railway development. (017-002)

### The [[iron-bridge|Iron Bridge]]

One of the most celebrated products of Shropshire iron was the world's first iron bridge, cast and made at the [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] Works. The bridge was executed in 1777 by Abraham Darby and became one of the wonders of the age. (017-016) (017-086)

### The Inclined Plane

At [[ketley|Ketley]] in Shropshire, [[william-reynolds|William Reynolds]] constructed the first inclined plane for canal boats, introducing boats of five tons where previously only smaller craft could navigate the steep slopes. (017-167) This invention was later acknowledged by [[thomas-telford|Thomas Telford]] as a precedent for his own canal innovations. (017-172)

## Key Figures

- [[abraham-darby|Abraham Darby]] — founder of the [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]] ironworks (016-006)
- [[richard-reynolds|Richard Reynolds]] — manager and partner at [[coalbrookdale|Coalbrookdale]], philanthropist (016-087)
- [[william-reynolds|William Reynolds]] — of [[ketley|Ketley]], inventor of the canal inclined plane (017-167)
