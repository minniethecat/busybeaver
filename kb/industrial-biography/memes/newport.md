# Newport

Newport is a town in [[monmouthshire|Monmouthshire]], South Wales, situated on the River Usk near its confluence with the [[severn|Severn]] Estuary. It played a role in both the English Civil War and the iron-making economy of South Wales.

## 地理位置

Newport is located on the South Wales coast, at the mouth of the River Usk. The town served as a gateway to the industrial valleys of Monmouthshire and was connected to Cardiff and the broader South Wales iron district by coastal routes. (053-174)

## 工业历史

### 早期历史

In the 17th century, Newport was the site of a Royalist defeat during the English Civil War. Dud [[dudley|Dudley]], the ironmaster who had been appointed general of artillery, was present at the rout of Royalist forces at Newport in the mid-1640s. (011-010)

### Nineteenth Century

By the early 19th century, Newport was a regular stopping point for travelling mechanics and engineers making their way through South Wales. In 1813, one young mechanic journeyed on foot through the region, spending a few days each at Newport, Llandaff, and Cardiff before taking ship for Dublin. (053-174)

## 重要人物与企业

Newport's proximity to the ironworks of Monmouthshire connected it to the broader South Wales industrial economy. The town served as a waypoint between the iron-producing valleys and the coastal shipping routes that carried Welsh iron to markets across Britain and Ireland.
