# Nottingham

Nottingham was one of the principal centres of the East Midlands textile industry, specialising in hosiery, framework knitting, and lace-making. The town's industrial growth was closely linked to the mechanical ingenuity of [[derby|Derby]]-based engineers who supplied it with the specialised machinery that drove its prosperity. (045-064)

## Industrial Significance

### Framework Knitting and Lace

The growth and extension of the cotton, silk, and lace trades in the 18th century furnished Nottingham's artisans with ample opportunities for mechanical innovation. (045-060)

[[james-fox|James Fox]] of [[derby|Derby]] — a butler's son whose instinct for handicraft was so strong it could not be repressed — supplied his celebrated lace machinery largely to the neighbouring town of Nottingham. Fox also obtained considerable employment from the great firms of [[richard-arkwright|Arkwright]] and [[william-strutt|Strutt]], the founders of the modern cotton manufacture. (045-053) (045-064) (045-065)

Fox's lathes were of excellent quality and maintained their high reputation long after his death. Besides making for the home demand, he exported much machinery abroad, to France, Russia, and the Mauritius. (045-067) (045-068)

### The [[planing-machine|Planing Machine]]

Among the mechanical innovations associated with Nottingham's supply chain was the claim that Fox of [[derby|Derby]] made the first planing machine in 1814 — an invention of profound importance for the engineering trades. (045-073)

### Royalist Stronghold

During the English Civil War, Nottingham held strategic importance as the place where the royal standard was raised at the outbreak of hostilities. The town was one of several Midlands locations — alongside Coventry — that became flashpoints in the conflict. (010-168)

## Key Figures

- [[richard-arkwright|Richard Arkwright]] — whose [[mule-spinning|cotton-spinning]] innovations stimulated Nottingham's textile trade (045-065)
