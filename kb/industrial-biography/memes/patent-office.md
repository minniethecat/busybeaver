The **Patent Office**, formally the **Great Seal Patent Office**, was the British government body responsible for registering and publishing patents. Established in its modern form in 1852, it played a crucial documentary role during the [[industrial-revolution|Industrial Revolution]] by preserving detailed specifications of iron and engineering inventions.

## Patent Documentation

The Patent Office published patent specifications that became invaluable historical records of British industrial innovation. Smiles's *Industrial Biography* frequently references these published specifications as primary sources (011-165). The reprinted specifications provided by the Patent Office in 1858 (011-166) documented inventions spanning centuries of ironworking, from early patentees such as Sturtevant and Rovenzon (011-166) through to the Victorian era.

The office's records reveal the breadth of industrial invention — not all patentees filed complete specifications: when [[[joseph-clement|Joseph Clement]]](/joseph-clement) attempted to locate the specification of [[[james-watt|James Watt]]](/james-watt)'s parallel motion at the Patent Office, he found none had been filed (043-142), leading him to publish his own account of the invention.
