# Richard Crawshay

Richard Crawshay (1739–1810) was the first of the great ironmasters of [[south-wales|South Wales]], described in contemporary accounts as "the great ironmaster" of Cyfartha. His career marked the beginning of the Crawshay dynasty that dominated Welsh ironmaking for generations. (001-221) (023-072)

## 早年生活

Richard Crawshay was born in 1739. As a young man, he was very attentive and industrious, qualities that would serve him well in his future career in the [[iron-industry|iron trade]]. (023-077) (023-092)

## 职业生涯

### 崛起

Crawshay began his working life in [[london|London]], where he was given an interest in the ironmongery business. When his employer retired, Crawshay was left in charge of the cast-[[iron-industry|iron trade]], and he soon demonstrated his enterprise and business acumen. (023-100) (023-102) (023-104)

### Cyfartha 铁厂

By 1787, Richard Crawshay had become the lessee of the Cyfartha ironworks in [[south-wales|South Wales]]. Under his management, the works expanded rapidly. [[henry-cort|Henry Cort]]'s inventions in [[puddling-process|puddling]] and rolling were adopted by Crawshay, who applied them on an industrial scale. (023-129) (023-134) (001-215) Crawshay found a ready market for all the iron he could produce, and the total produce of the Crawshay furnaces grew to be among the largest in Britain. (023-138) (023-147)

### 基础设施建设

Recognising that the badness of the roads hindered the transport of iron to market, Crawshay set himself to overcome this obstacle, investing in infrastructure improvements that benefited the entire district. (023-152)

## 影响与评价

> Richard Crawshay, the first of the great ironmasters of South Wales, transformed the Cyfartha works into the most productive iron-manufacturing district in all Britain. (023-072) (023-159)

Crawshay became known throughout the [[iron-industry|iron trade]] as a figure of immense energy and enterprise. The Crawshays followed one another in the ironworks for generations, and by 1842 a Crawshay descendant had become one of the wealthiest industrial figures in Wales. (023-171) (023-175) (023-180)
