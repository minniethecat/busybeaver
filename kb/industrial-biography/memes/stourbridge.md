# Stourbridge

Market town on the River Stour in [[worcestershire|Worcester]]shire, part of the Black Country industrial district, noted in the Industrial Biography for its early [[iron-industry|iron manufacture]] and glass-making traditions dating from the seventeenth century.

## 地理位置

Stourbridge sits on the River Stour in the western part of the West Midlands, between Kidderminster to the south-west and Dudley to the north-east. The town lies within the South [[staffordshire|Staffordshire]] coalfield, and its position on a navigable river gave it access to both raw materials — coal, ironstone, and fireclay — and transport routes for finished goods. The underlying geology of coal measures and the proximity of iron ore made the Stourbridge district a natural centre for metalworking. (010-069)

## 工业历史

### 早期铁业

Dud Dudley, the seventeenth-century ironmaster and illegitimate son of Lord Dudley, described Stourbridge as a market town in his memoir *Metallum Martis*. During the English Civil War era, ironworks in the Stourbridge district were vulnerable to flooding, and Dudley recounts an incident where he rushed to warn the inhabitants of impending danger after a sudden inundation threatened the forges and mills along the Stour. (010-069)

### 运河与交通

The improvement of navigation on the River Stour was a significant factor in the town's industrial growth. A navigable waterway was constructed from Stourbridge to Kidderminster, enabling the transport of coal in large quantities. This development allowed the ironworks and glass-houses of the district to ship their products more economically than the previous overland routes, which depended on packhorses and wagons on poor roads. (016-072)

### 玻璃制造

By the later seventeenth century, the neighbourhood of Stourbridge had become a noted centre of glass manufacture. The glass-houses upon the Stour produced window glass, bottles, and specialty glassware, taking advantage of the local availability of fireclay for furnace linings and coal for fuel. The glass-making tradition at Stourbridge persisted for centuries, with the town becoming one of [[england|England]]'s principal centres for fine crystal and cut glass. (016-090)

## 重要人物与企业

- [[Dud Dudley]] — seventeenth-century ironmaster who documented Stourbridge ironworks in his memoir
- The Foley family — ironmasters with interests in the Stourbridge district
- Stourbridge glass-houses — producers of window glass, bottles, and later fine crystal
