# Stockton-on-Tees

Market and port town on the River Tees in County Durham, North East [[england|England]], known in the Industrial Biography as the birthplace of engineer Matthew Murray and an early hub of the railway age.

## 地理位置

Stockton-on-Tees lies on the north bank of the River Tees, approximately 20 miles south of [[newcastle-upon-tyne|Newcastle]] upon [[river-tyne|Tyne]]. Its position at the head of the Tees estuary made it a natural port for the export of coal from the Durham coalfield and a market centre for the surrounding agricultural district. The town's location on navigable water contributed to its emergence as an industrial settlement in the late eighteenth century. (045-114)

## 工业历史

### 早期发展

Stockton was an ancient market town and port, but its industrial character emerged most visibly in the late eighteenth century when shipping and shipbuilding trades expanded along the Tees. The town's shipyards and associated metal trades — smithing, founding, and fitting — provided employment for a growing population of skilled artisans. It was into this working-class environment that Matthew Murray was born in 1763. (045-114)

### Matthew Murray 与机械工程

Stockton's most significant contribution to industrial history was as the birthplace of Matthew Murray (1763–1826), who would become one of the foremost machine makers and locomotive builders of the early nineteenth century. Murray was born to working-class parents and received a basic education before being apprenticed to a local blacksmith. Trade was slack at Stockton, compelling him to seek work in Leeds, where he later established the renowned Round [[foundry|Foundry]] and became a rival to Boulton & Watt. (045-114) (045-120)

After establishing himself in Leeds, Murray sent for his wife from Stockton, bringing her to the neighbouring village of Beeston near Leeds, where they settled and raised a family. His Stockton origins and early training in the town's metal trades shaped his practical approach to engineering. (045-135)

### 铁路时代

Stockton gained permanent fame in the railway age as the eastern terminus of the Stockton and Darlington Railway, opened in 1825 — the world's first public railway to use [[locomotive|steam locomotive]]s. Though this event falls slightly after the period covered in Smiles's Industrial Biography, the town's importance in railway history is directly linked to the engineering traditions established in its shipyards and metal workshops during the late 1700s.

## 重要人物与企业

- [[Matthew Murray]] — locomotive and machine-tool pioneer, born at Stockton in 1763
- Stockton and Darlington Railway — first public steam railway, eastern terminus at Stockton
- Local shipbuilding yards and iron foundries employed many of the town's mechanics
