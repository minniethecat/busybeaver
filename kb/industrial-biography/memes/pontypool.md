Pontypool is a town in [[monmouthshire|Monmouthshire]], [[south-wales|South Wales]], notable in industrial history as the site where the English tin-plate industry originated. The manufactory established there by Capel Hanbury broke Britain's dependence on imported tin-plates from Germany. (013-107)

## Tin-Plate Manufactory

Capel Hanbury established a tin-plate manufactory at Pontypool in Monmouthshire, which has since continued to be successfully carried on. (013-107) This enterprise directly challenged the German monopoly on tin-plate production. Prior to its establishment, [[england|England]] had relied on imported tin-plates from Germany for about sixty years. (013-106)
