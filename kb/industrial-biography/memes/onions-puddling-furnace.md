# Onions [[puddling-furnace|Puddling Furnace]]

A reverberatory furnace for [[puddling-process|puddling]] iron patented by [[peter-onions|Peter Onions]] of [[merthyr-tydfil|Merthyr Tydfil]] in 1783, which improved upon [[cranege-brothers|the Craneges]]' design by incorporating a blast to maintain combustion

## Background
The Cranege brothers had demonstrated that pig iron could be converted with pit-coal in a reverberatory furnace, but their process was inconsistent. [[peter-onions|Peter Onions]] of [[merthyr-tydfil|Merthyr Tydfil]] sought to improve the furnace design to make the process more reliable.

## Inventor
- **Inventor**: [[peter-onions|Peter Onions]] of [[merthyr-tydfil|Merthyr Tydfil]]
- **Patent**: 1783
- **Improver**: [[henry-cort|Henry Cort]] (incorporated Onions' principles)

## How It Works
Onions' furnace was bound with iron work and well annealed. The doors were luted with sand to seal the furnace. A blast was admitted underneath the grate to keep up combustion of the fuel. The workman opened the door and stirred the metal with a bar until it thickened into a froth, then collected the iron particles into a lump for forging. (021-143)

## Significance
Onions' patent provided the first detailed description of the manual [[puddling-process|puddling]] operation. Although [[henry-cort|Henry Cort]] is more famous, he was anticipated by Onions in the process of [[puddling-process|puddling]]. Cort combined Onions' [[puddling-process|puddling]] method with his grooved rollers to create the complete bar-iron production system. (021-156)
