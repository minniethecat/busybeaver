# Kidderminster

Kidderminster is a town in [[worcestershire|Worcestershire]], situated on the River Stour. The town played a role in the early development of inland navigation in [[england|England]], featuring in ambitious canal proposals during the 17th century.

## 地理位置

Kidderminster lies on the River Stour, between Stourport and Stourbridge. Its position on a navigable river made it a candidate for early canal and navigation improvement schemes aimed at connecting the industrial Midlands. (012-157) (012-181)

## 工业历史

### Canal Schemes

In the 1660s, the engineer and projector Andrew Yarranton proposed opening up the navigation of the River Stour, passing by Stourport and Kidderminster, and connecting it by an artificial cut with the [[river-trent|River Trent]]. The scheme was far in advance of its age, but it came to a standstill for want of money. More than a hundred years passed before it was carried out by the great canal maker [[James Brindley]]. (012-157)

### Yarranton's Later Work

Yarranton returned to the Stour navigation later in life, and despite earlier failures he successfully made the river completely navigable from [[Stourbridge]] to Kidderminster. He reported carrying down many hundred tons of coal and laying out nearly £1,000 before the work was obstructed for want of money. (012-181)

## 重要人物与企业

Kidderminster's navigation improvements were the work of [[Andrew Yarranton]], one of the earliest English canal promoters. His partnership with Wall and his later independent efforts demonstrated the economic potential of inland water transport for the coal and [[iron-industry|iron trade]]s of the West Midlands.
