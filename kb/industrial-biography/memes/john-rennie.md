# John Rennie

John Rennie (1761–1821) was a Scottish civil engineer whose works included bridges, canals, docks, and harbours across Britain. His sons [[george-rennie|George Rennie]] and John Rennie the Younger continued the family engineering practice. Smiles refers to [[george-rennie|George Rennie]] as "the celebrated engineer, whose works were situated at the south end of [[blackfriars-bridge|Blackfriars Bridge]]." (053-075)

## Early Life and Career

Born in Phantassie, East Lothian, Rennie studied at the University of [[edinburgh|Edinburgh]] and worked for [[boulton-and-watt|Boulton and Watt]] before establishing his own engineering practice. He earned a reputation for precision and innovative use of materials.

## Major Projects

Rennie's notable works include Waterloo Bridge and [[london-bridge|[[london|London]] Bridge]] (completed after his death by his sons), the Kennet and [[river-avon|Avon]] Canal, the Crinan Canal, and extensive dock systems in London, [[liverpool|Liverpool]], and Hull. He also designed the bell tower of St. Mary's Church in Edinburgh.

## Legacy

John Rennie died in 1821 and is buried in St. Paul's Cathedral. His sons [[george-rennie|George Rennie]] and Sir John Rennie carried forward his practice, and his bridges and canals remain landmarks of British civil engineering.
