# Dowlais Ironworks

The Dowlais Ironworks near [[merthyr-tydfil|Merthyr Tydfil]] was one of the four great ironworks established on the mineral domain leased by [[anthony-bacon|Anthony Bacon]] in 1765. It was among the first to adopt Neilson's hot blast, dramatically improving its efficiency.

## Historical Background

The Dowlais works originated from the extraordinary mineral lease taken by [[anthony-bacon|Anthony Bacon]] in 1765. He secured the minerals under forty square miles of country surrounding Merthyr Tydvil from Lord Talbot for 99 years, at a rental of only £200 a year. (023-120) (023-123)

After supplying the Government with cannon and iron, Bacon retired from business in 1782. He sublet his vast mineral tract in four divisions — the Dowlais, the [[penydarran|Penydarran]], the Cyfartha, and the Plymouth Works — situated north, east, west, and south of Merthyr Tydvil. (023-126) (023-127) (023-128)

## Industrial Significance

Dowlais became a centre of technological innovation. In 1804, [[richard-trevithick|Richard Trevithick]]'s high-pressure [[locomotive|steam locomotive]] — the first ever to run on rails — hauled ten tons of iron and seventy men along the [[penydarran|Penydarran]] tramroad from Dowlais. Though commercially unsuccessful, it proved the principle of the steam locomotive and laid the groundwork for later railway development.
