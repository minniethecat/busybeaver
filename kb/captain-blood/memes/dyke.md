# Nicholas Dyke

## Overview

Nicholas Dyke is a former petty officer in the Royal Navy who joins Peter Blood's company of escaped convicts and becomes one of his reliable buccaneer crewmen. He appears consistently throughout the later chapters of the novel, participating in councils of war and sailing expeditions. Dyke represents the capable seamen who form the backbone of Blood's crew — men with naval training who transfer their skills to the buccaneer life.

## In the Novel

Dyke is introduced as "Nicholas Dyke, who had been a petty officer" in the Royal Navy before his transportation to the Caribbean ([006-330]). He is among the first to agree to join Blood's escape from Barbados, listed alongside Hagthorpe and Ogle as having "agreed to join" the venture ([006-414]).

Throughout the narrative, Dyke is consistently named among Blood's inner circle. He participates in councils of war alongside Hagthorpe, Wolverstone, and other senior crew members ([011-143]). When Blood captures the Spanish ship, Dyke is entrusted with guarding the Spanish prisoners ([012-037]). He later appears in the company of Yberville, Wolverstone, and Ogle during the buccaneer campaigns ([017-312], [017-328]).

In the later chapters, Dyke chooses to sail with Blood on a specific expedition, demonstrating his continued loyalty ([025-122]). His interaction with a one-eyed character — who "rolled his single eye over Dyke" — provides a moment of narrative colour, with Dyke shown as persistent in his questioning despite being laughed at ([025-125], [025-133], [025-138], [025-142]). Near the novel's conclusion, Dyke is mentioned alongside Pitt as one of the men whose "outlawry, like Blood's" remains a concern ([031-107]), and he is present in moments of tension with Blood's inner circle ([029-048]).
