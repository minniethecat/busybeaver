[012-001] “In less than half-an-hour,” said Blood presently, “we shall have her

[012-002] athwart our hawse, sweeping our decks with her guns.”

[012-003] “We can fight,” said the one-eyed giant with an oath.

[012-004] “Fight!” sneered Blood. “Undermanned as we are, mustering a bare twenty

[012-005] men, in what case are we to fight? No, there would be only one way. To

[012-006] persuade her that all is well aboard, that we are Spaniards, so that she

[012-007] may leave us to continue on our course.”

[012-008] “And how is that possible?” [[hagthorpe|Hagthorpe]] asked.

[012-009] “It isn't possible,” said Blood. “If it....” And then he broke off,

[012-010] and stood musing, his eyes upon the green water. Ogle, with a bent for

[012-011] sarcasm, interposed a suggestion bitterly.

[012-012] “We might send [[don-diego|Don Diego de Espinosa]] in a boat manned by his Spaniards

[012-013] to assure his brother the Admiral that we are all loyal subjects of his

[012-014] Catholic Majesty.”

[012-015] The Captain swung round, and for an instant looked as if he would have

[012-016] struck the gunner. Then his expression changed: the light of inspiration

[012-017] Was in his glance.

[012-018] “Bedad! ye've said it. He doesn't fear death, this damned pirate; but

[012-019] his son may take a different view. Filial piety's mighty strong in

[012-020] Spain.” He swung on his heel abruptly, and strode back to the knot of

[012-021] men about his prisoner. “Here!” he shouted to them. “Bring him below.”

[012-022] And he led the way down to the waist, and thence by the booby hatch to

[012-023] the gloom of the 'tween-decks, where the air was rank with the smell

[012-024] of tar and spun yarn. Going aft he threw open the door of the spacious

[012-025] wardroom, and went in followed by a dozen of the hands with the pinioned

[012-026] Spaniard. Every man aboard would have followed him but for his sharp

[012-027] [[authority|command]] to some of them to remain on deck with Hagthorpe.

[012-028] In the ward-room the three stern chasers were in position, loaded,

[012-029] their muzzles thrusting through the open ports, precisely as the Spanish

[012-030] gunners had left them.

[012-032] thrusting forward through the little throng of gaping men, Blood pointed

[012-033] to the middle chaser; “Have that gun hauled back,” he ordered.

[012-034] When this was done, Blood beckoned those who held Don Diego.

[012-035] “Lash him across the mouth of it,” he bade them, and whilst, assisted by

[012-036] another two, they made haste to obey, he turned to the others. “To the

[012-037] roundhouse, some of you, and fetch the Spanish prisoners. And you, Dyke,

[012-057] displayed the proper degree of filial piety.

[012-134] “I... I accept,” he answered at last, and swung to the Spaniards. “And

[012-136] sake and for your own--for all our sakes. If you do not, this man will

[012-138] Since he yielded, and their leader himself counselled no resistance,

[012-139] why should they encompass their own destruction by a gesture of futile

[012-140] heroism? They answered without much hesitation that they would do as was

[012-141] required of them.

[012-142] Blood turned, and advanced to Don Diego.

[012-143] “I am sorry to inconvenience you in this fashion, but...” For a second

[012-145] after that scarcely perceptible pause, he continued, “but I do not think

[012-148] no answer.

[012-150] back.
