## Overview

St. Nicholas (Saint-Nicolas) is a French colonial port on the northwest coast of Hispaniola (present-day Haiti). In *Captain Blood*, it serves as a buccaneer gathering point and the setting for Blood's fateful encounter with the French pirate Levasseur and Mademoiselle Madeleine d'Ogeron ([018-229]).

## In the Novel

Captain Blood sailed the *Arabella* to St. Nicholas after learning that the French buccaneer Levasseur had made port there ([018-298]). The town functioned as a French colonial outpost and a natural harbor for privateers and buccaneers operating in the Caribbean. Mademoiselle d'Ogeron, the niece of the French Governor of Tortuga, had arrived at St. Nicholas some months earlier on a vessel bound for France ([018-302]).

At St. Nicholas, Blood's path crossed with Levasseur in a waterside tavern, where the French pirate's lieutenant, Cahusac, was also present ([018-411]). The encounter proved pivotal — Blood gathered intelligence about Levasseur's activities and later confronted him over his treatment of Mademoiselle d'Ogeron. The references to St. Nicholas in later chapters confirm that the events there had lasting consequences, shaping Blood's reputation among the buccaneers ([024-253]).

The *Mary* was warped out of St. Nicholas as Blood departed, and by the time they were out of sight of the port, an understanding had formed between Blood and those who would become his closest allies ([018-330]).
