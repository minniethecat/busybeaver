# Arabella (ship)

## Overview

The *Arabella* is Peter Blood's flagship — a captured Spanish frigate that he renames in honour of Arabella Bishop. Under Blood's command, this vessel becomes the most feared ship in the Caribbean, known for its speed, its captain's tactical brilliance, and its distinctive code of honour that distinguishes it from common pirate vessels.

## In the Novel

### The Capture and Naming

Blood captures the vessel from the Spanish and renames it — an act that is both practical (disguising the ship's origin) and romantic (the name is a tribute to the woman Blood loves). The naming itself is significant: a Spanish warship, captured by an Irish rebel, flying under an English name — the *Arabella* embodies the novel's themes of transformation and crossing boundaries.

### The Campaigns

The *Arabella* serves as Blood's command ship throughout his buccaneering career, participating in all his major naval engagements. Its "freedom of the ship" ((019-088)) is extended to honoured prisoners — a mark of Blood's distinctive approach to naval warfare.

## Significance

- Blood's flagship and primary vessel
- Named for Arabella Bishop — symbol of Blood's romantic motivation
- Represents Blood's transformation from slave to captain
