# Vice-Admiral Craufurd

**Vice-Admiral Craufurd** is a senior Royal Navy officer whose flagship, the *Imperator*, serves as the vessel upon which [[colonel-bishop|Colonel Bishop]] and [[lord-julian-wade|Lord Julian Wade]] sail to the Caribbean in pursuit of [[peter-blood|Captain Blood]].

## In the Novel

When the Jamaica fleet puts to sea following Lord Julian's arrival in Barbados, Lord Julian sails with Colonel Bishop aboard Craufurd's flagship [024-338]. The narrative notes that there is no operational need for either man to make the voyage — the Deputy-Governor's duties actually demand that he remain ashore [024-339, 024-340]. Their presence aboard the *Imperator* is driven by Bishop's obsessive determination to personally witness Blood's capture.

> When the Jamaica fleet put to sea some few days later, Lord Julian sailed with Colonel Bishop in Vice-Admiral Craufurd's flagship. Not only was there no need for either of them to go, but the Deputy-Governor's duties actually demanded that he should remain ashore. [024-338, 024-340]

Aboard the *Imperator*, Bishop's confidence reaches its peak. In the great cabin of Craufurd's flagship, the Deputy-Governor gets drunk celebrating his conviction that Captain Blood's career is finally ending [025-060, 025-061]. This drunken celebration — occurring as it does aboard a Royal Navy warship — underscores both Bishop's arrogance and his profound misjudgment of the man he pursues.

> In the great cabin of Vice-Admiral Craufurd's flagship, the Imperator, the Deputy-Governor got drunk that night to celebrate his conviction that the sands of Captain Blood's career were running out. [025-060, 025-062]

Craufurd himself remains a background figure — a facilitator of the chase rather than an active participant in the narrative. His flagship provides the setting for Bishop's hubris, but the Vice-Admiral's own perspective on the mission goes unrecorded.
