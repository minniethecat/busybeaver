[006-001] Now Blood had no cause to love Spaniards. His two years in a Spanish

[006-002] prison and his subsequent campaigning in the Spanish Netherlands had

[006-003] shown him a side of the Spanish character which he had found anything

[006-004] but admirable. Nevertheless he performed his doctor's duties zealously

[006-005] and painstakingly, if emotionlessly, and even with a certain superficial

[006-006] friendliness towards each of his patients. These were so surprised at

[006-007] having their wounds healed instead of being summarily hanged that they

[006-008] manifested a docility very unusual in their kind. They were shunned,

[006-009] however, by all those charitably disposed inhabitants of Bridgetown who

[006-010] flocked to the improvised hospital with gifts of fruit and flowers and

[006-011] delicacies for the injured English seamen. Indeed, had the wishes of

[006-012] some of these inhabitants been regarded, the Spaniards would have been

[006-013] left to die like vermin, and of this [[peter-blood|Peter Blood]] had an example almost

[006-014] at the very outset.

[006-015] With the assistance of one of the negroes sent to the shed for the

[006-016] purpose, he was in the act of setting a broken leg, when a deep, gruff

[006-017] voice, that he had come to know and dislike as he had never disliked the

[006-018] voice of living man, abruptly challenged him.

[006-019] “What are you doing there?”

[006-021] voice, as I have said.

[006-023] labours.
