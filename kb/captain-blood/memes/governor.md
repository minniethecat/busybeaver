# Governor

## Overview

The colonial governor is the highest authority in the Caribbean world of *[[peter-blood|Captain Blood]]*. From [[colonel-bishop|Colonel Bishop]], the governor of [[barbados|Barbados]], to the governorship of [[jamaica|Jamaica]] that is ultimately offered to Blood himself, the office represents [[crown|the Crown]]'s authority in the New World — an authority that is often exercised with the arbitrariness of petty tyranny. Governors in the novel levy taxes, [[authority|command]] militias, administer justice, and, in [[colonel-bishop|Colonel Bishop]]'s case, pursue personal vendettas under the color of official duty. The novel's trajectory from Blood's condemnation by a corrupt judicial system to his elevation as Governor of [[jamaica|Jamaica]] traces a complete inversion: the condemned rebel becomes [[crown|the Crown]]'s representative, suggesting that legitimate authority is won through demonstrated worth rather than inherited station.

## Historical Background

[[colonial|Colonial]] governors in the 17th-century Caribbean were appointed by [[crown|the Crown]] and wielded extensive [[authority|power]]s — they [[authority|command]]ed local militias, administered justice, collected revenues, and represented the monarch in all official functions. The governorship of [[jamaica|Jamaica]], established after the English conquest of the island from Spain in 1655, was a significant post in the [[colonial|colonial administration]]. Governors were expected to suppress piracy, maintain the profitability of [[plantation-system|plantation]]s, and defend the colony against Spanish and French aggression. The salary and perquisites of the office made it a coveted appointment, often granted as political patronage rather than on the basis of administrative competence.

## In the Novel

Blood's path "to accept the [[governorship|deputy-governorship]] of [[jamaica|Jamaica]]" (021-011) represents his ultimate vindication. Offered the post by a [[crown|Crown]] that once condemned him, Blood transitions from fugitive to governor — the hunted becoming the hunter of other pirates. The governorship is first mentioned through [[colonel-bishop|Colonel Bishop]]'s "fashion of most [[colonial|Colonial]] governors" (005-353), a dismissive characterization that suggests the mediocrity of typical appointees. Blood's elevation thus serves as a rebuke to the system that produced men like Bishop.
