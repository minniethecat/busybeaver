# Trinity College Dublin

**Trinity College Dublin** was the university in Ireland where Peter Blood received his medical education. Founded in 1592 by Queen Elizabeth I, Trinity College was the sole constituent college of the University of Dublin and served as the principal institution of higher learning for Irish Protestants during the 17th century.[^1]

## Peter Blood's Education

Peter Blood was a graduate of Trinity College, Dublin, where he earned his qualification as a physician. His medical training at Trinity provided him with the knowledge and skills he would later employ both as a practicing doctor in Bridgewater and as a surgeon to the buccaneers of the Caribbean.[^2]

Blood's time at Trinity College shaped his intellectual character. He was known to quote classical authors and display the broad education characteristic of a university-trained physician of his era. His medical expertise, rooted in his Trinity education, proved invaluable during his years of slavery in Barbados, where his skills as a physician gained him preferential treatment from Colonel Bishop and Governor Steed.[^3]

## Historical Context

As a Catholic born in Ireland, Blood's attendance at Trinity College was somewhat unusual for the period, as the college was officially a Protestant institution. However, Catholics were not formally barred from attending until later in its history. Blood's Irish Catholic background and Trinity education together placed him in a complex position within the religious and political conflicts of the late 17th century.[^4]

## Significance

Trinity College Dublin represents the foundation of Peter Blood's professional identity before his transformation into a soldier, slave, and buccaneer. His medical degree from Trinity was the credential he invoked at his trial before the Lords Commissioners, and it was the skill set that ultimately saved his life in Barbados.[^5]
