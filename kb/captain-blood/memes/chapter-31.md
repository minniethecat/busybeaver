[031-004] CHAPTER XXXI. HIS EXCELLENCY THE GOVERNOR

[031-005] When the cost of that victory came to be counted, it was found that of

[031-006] three hundred and twenty buccaneers who had left [[cartagena|Cartagena]] with Captain

[031-007] Blood, a bare hundred remained sound and whole. The Elizabeth had

[031-008] suffered so seriously that it was doubtful if she could ever again be

[031-009] rendered seaworthy, and [[hagthorpe|Hagthorpe]], who had so gallantly commanded her

[031-010] in that last action, was dead. Against this, on the other side of the

[031-011] account, stood the facts that, with a far inferior force and by sheer

[031-012] skill and desperate valour, Blood's buccaneers had saved [[jamaica|Jamaica]] from

[031-013] bombardment and pillage, and they had captured the fleet of M. de

[031-014] [[de-rivarol|Rivarol]], and seized for the benefit of King William the splendid

[031-015] treasure which she carried.

[031-016] It was not until the evening of the following day that van der Kuylen's

[031-017] truant fleet of nine ships came to anchor in the harbour of [[port-royal|Port Royal]],

[031-018] and its officers, Dutch and English, were made acquainted with their

[031-019] Admiral's true opinion of their worth.

[031-020] Six ships of that fleet were instantly refitted for sea. There were

[031-021] other West Indian settlements demanding the visit of inspection of the

[031-022] new Governor-General, and Lord Willoughby was in haste to sail for the

[031-023] Antilles.

[031-024] “And meanwhile,” he complained to his Admiral, “I am detained here by

[031-025] the absence of this fool of a Deputy-Governor.”

[031-026] “So?” said van der Kuylen. “But vhy should dad dedain you?”

[031-027] “That I may break the dog as he deserves, and appoint his successor

[031-028] in some man gifted with a sense of where his duty lies, and with the

[031-029] ability to perform it.”

[031-030] “Aha! But id is not necessary you remain for dat. And he vill require no

[031-031] insdrucshons, dis one. He vill know how to make Port Royal safe, bedder

[031-032] nor you or me.”

[031-033] “You mean Blood?”

[031-034] “Of gourse. Could any man be bedder? You haf seen vhad he can do.”

[031-035] “You think so, too, eh? Egad! I had thought of it; and, rip me, why not?

[031-036] He's a better man than Morgan, and Morgan was made Governor.”

[031-037] Blood was sent for. He came, spruce and debonair once more, having

[031-038] exploited the resources of Port Royal so to render himself. He was a

[031-039] trifle dazzled by the honour proposed to him, when Lord Willoughby made

[031-040] it known. It was so far beyond anything that he had dreamed, and he was

[031-041] assailed by doubts of his capacity to undertake so onerous a charge.

[031-042] “Damme!” snapped Willoughby, “Should I offer it unless I were satisfied

[031-043] of your capacity? If that's your only objection....”

[031-044] “It is not, my lord. I had counted upon going home, so I had. I am

[031-045] hungry for the green lanes of England.” He sighed. “There will be

[031-046] apple-blossoms in the orchards of Somerset.”

[031-047] “Apple-blossoms!” His lordship's voice shot up like a rocket, and

[031-048] cracked on the word. “What the devil...? Apple-blossoms!” He looked at

[031-049] van der Kuylen.

[031-050] The Admiral raised his brows and pursed his heavy lips. His eyes

[031-051] twinkled humourously in his great face.

[031-052] “So!” he said. “Fery boedical!”

[031-053] My lord wheeled fiercely upon [[peter-blood|Captain Blood]]. “You've a past score to

[031-054] wipe out, my man!” he admonished him. “You've done something towards it,

[031-055] I confess; and you've shown your quality in doing it. That's why I offer

[031-056] you the governorship of Jamaica in His Majesty's name--because I account

[031-057] you the fittest man for the office that I have seen.”

[031-058] Blood bowed low. “Your lordship is very good. But....”

[031-059] “Tchah! There's no 'but' to it. If you want your past forgotten, and

[031-060] your future assured, this is your chance. And you are not to treat it

[031-061] lightly on account of apple-blossoms or any other damned sentimental

[031-062] nonsense. Your duty lies here, at least for as long as the war lasts.

[031-063] When the war's over, you may get back to Somerset and cider or your

[031-064] native Ireland and its potheen; but until then you'll make the best of

[031-065] Jamaica and rum.”

[031-066] Van der Kuylen exploded into laughter. But from Blood the pleasantry

[031-067] elicited no smile. He remained solemn to the point of glumness. His

[031-068] thoughts were on [[arabella-bishop|Miss Bishop]], who was somewhere here in this very house

[031-069] in which they stood, but whom he had not seen since his arrival. Had she

[031-070] but shown him some [[mercy|compassion]]....

[031-071] And then the rasping voice of Willoughby cut in again, upbraiding him

[031-072] for his hesitation, pointing out to him his incredible stupidity in

[031-073] trifling with such a golden opportunity as this. He stiffened and bowed.

[031-074] “My lord, you are in the right. I am a fool. But don't be accounting

[031-075] me an ingrate as well. If I have hesitated, it is because there are

[031-076] considerations with which I will not trouble your lordship.”

[031-077] “Apple-blossoms, I suppose?” sniffed his lordship.

[031-078] This time Blood laughed, but there was still a lingering wistfulness in

[031-079] his eyes.

[031-080] “It shall be as you wish--and very gratefully, let me assure your

[031-081] lordship. I shall know how to earn His Majesty's approbation. You may

[031-082] depend upon my loyal service.

[031-083] “If I didn't, I shouldn't offer you this governorship.”

[031-084] Thus it was settled. Blood's commission was made out and sealed in

[031-085] the presence of Mallard, the Commandant, and the other officers of

[031-086] the garrison, who looked on in round-eyed astonishment, but kept their

[031-087] thoughts to themselves.

[031-088] “Now ve can aboud our business go,” said van der Kuylen.

[031-089] “We sail to-morrow morning,” his lordship announced.

[031-090] Blood was startled.

[031-091] “And [[colonel-bishop|Colonel Bishop]]?” he asked.

[031-092] “He becomes your affair. You are now the Governor. You will deal with

[031-093] him as you think proper on his return. Hang him from his own yardarm. He

[031-094] deserves it.”

[031-095] “Isn't the task a trifle invidious?” wondered Blood.

[031-096] “Very well. I'll leave a letter for him. I hope he'll like it.”

[031-097] Captain Blood took up his duties at once. There was much to be done to

[031-098] place Port Royal in a proper state of defence, after what had happened

[031-099] there. He made an inspection of the ruined fort, and issued instructions

[031-100] for the work upon it, which was to be started immediately. Next he

[031-101] ordered the careening of the three French vessels that they might

[031-102] be rendered seaworthy once more. Finally, with the sanction of Lord

[031-103] Willoughby, he marshalled his buccaneers and surrendered to them one

[031-104] fifth of the captured treasure, leaving it to their choice thereafter

[031-105] either to depart or to enrol themselves in the service of King William.

[031-106] A score of them elected to remain, and amongst these were [[jeremy-pitt|Jeremy Pitt]],

[031-107] Ogle, and Dyke, whose outlawry, like Blood's, had come to an end with

[031-108] the downfall of King James. They were--saving old [[wolverstone|Wolverstone]], who

[031-109] had been left behind at Cartagena--the only survivors of that band of

[031-110] rebels-convict who had left [[barbados|Barbados]] over three years ago in the Cinco

[031-111] Llagas.

[031-112] On the following morning, whilst van der Kuylen's fleet was making

[031-113] finally ready for sea, Blood sat in the spacious whitewashed room that

[031-114] was the Governor's office, when Major Mallard brought him word that

[031-115] Bishop's homing squadron was in sight.

[031-116] “That is very well,” said Blood. “I am glad he comes before Lord

[031-117] Willoughby's departure. The orders, Major, are that you place him under

[031-118] arrest the moment he steps ashore. Then bring him here to me. A moment.”

[031-119] He wrote a hurried note. “That to Lord Willoughby aboard Admiral van der

[031-120] Kuylen's flagship.”

[031-121] Major Mallard saluted and departed. Peter Blood sat back in his chair

[031-122] and stared at the ceiling, frowning. Time moved on. Came a tap at the

[031-123] door, and an elderly negro slave presented himself. Would his excellency

[031-124] receive Miss Bishop?

[031-125] His excellency changed colour. He sat quite still, staring at the negro

[031-126] a moment, conscious that his pulses were drumming in a manner wholly

[031-127] unusual to them. Then quietly he assented.

[031-128] He rose when she entered, and if he was not as pale as she was, it was

[031-129] because his tan dissembled it. For a moment there was silence between

[031-130] them, as they stood looking each at the other. Then she moved forward,

[031-131] and began at last to speak, haltingly, in an unsteady voice, amazing in

[031-132] one usually so calm and deliberate.

[031-133] “I... I... Major Mallard has just told me....”

[031-134] “Major Mallard exceeded his duty,” said Blood, and because of the effort

[031-135] he made to steady his voice it sounded harsh and unduly loud.

[031-136] He saw her start, and stop, and instantly made amends. “You alarm

[031-137] yourself without reason, Miss Bishop. Whatever may lie between me and

[031-138] your uncle, you may be sure that I shall not follow the example he has

[031-139] set me. I shall not abuse my position to prosecute a private vengeance.

[031-140] On the contrary, I shall abuse it to protect him. Lord Willoughby's

[031-141] recommendation to me is that I shall treat him without mercy. My own

[031-142] intention is to send him back to his [[slavery|plantation]] in Barbados.”

[031-143] She came slowly forward now. “I... I am glad that you will do that.

[031-144] Glad, above all, for your own sake.” She held out her hand to him.

[031-145] He considered it critically. Then he bowed over it. “I'll not presume to

[031-146] take it in the hand of a thief and a pirate,” said he bitterly.

[031-147] “You are no longer that,” she said, and strove to smile.

[031-148] “Yet I owe no thanks to you that I am not,” he answered. “I think

[031-149] there's no more to be said, unless it be to add the assurance that Lord

[031-150] Julian Wade has also nothing to apprehend from me. That, no doubt, will

[031-151] be the assurance that your peace of mind requires?”

[031-152] “For your own sake--yes. But for your own sake only. I would not have

[031-153] you do anything mean or dishonouring.”

[031-154] “Thief and pirate though I be?”

[031-155] She clenched her hand, and made a little gesture of despair and

[031-156] impatience.

[031-157] “Will you never forgive me those words?”

[031-158] “I'm finding it a trifle hard, I confess. But what does it matter, when

[031-159] all is said?”

[031-160] Her clear hazel eyes considered him a moment wistfully. Then she put out

[031-161] her hand again.

[031-162] “I am going, Captain Blood. Since you are so generous to my uncle,

[031-163] I shall be returning to Barbados with him. We are not like to meet

[031-164] again--ever. Is it impossible that we should part friends? Once I

[031-165] wronged you, I know. And I have said that I am sorry. Won't you... won't

[031-166] you say 'good-bye'?”

[031-167] He seemed to rouse himself, to shake off a mantle of deliberate

[031-168] harshness. He took the hand she proffered. Retaining it, he spoke, his

[031-169] eyes sombrely, wistfully considering her.

[031-170] “You are returning to Barbados?” he said slowly. “Will [[lord-julian-wade|Lord Julian]] be

[031-171] going with you?”

[031-172] “Why do you ask me that?” she confronted him quite fearlessly.

[031-173] “Sure, now, didn't he give you my message, or did he bungle it?”

[031-174] “No. He didn't bungle it. He gave it me in your own words. It touched me

[031-175] very deeply. It made me see clearly my error and my [[justice|injustice]]. I owe

[031-176] it to you that I should say this by way of amend. I judged too harshly

[031-177] where it was a presumption to judge at all.”

[031-178] He was still holding her hand. “And Lord Julian, then?” he asked, his

[031-179] eyes watching her, bright as sapphires in that copper-coloured face.

[031-180] “Lord Julian will no doubt be going home to England. There is nothing

[031-181] more for him to do out here.”

[031-182] “But didn't he ask you to go with him?”

[031-183] “He did. I forgive you the impertinence.”

[031-184] A wild hope leapt to life within him.

[031-185] “And you? Glory be, ye'll not be telling me ye refused to become my

[031-186] lady, when....”

[031-187] “Oh! You are insufferable!” She tore her hand free and backed away from

[031-188] him. “I should not have come. Good-bye!” She was speeding to the door.

[031-189] He sprang after her, and caught her. Her face flamed, and her eyes

[031-190] stabbed him like daggers. “These are pirate's ways, I think! Release

[031-191] me!”

[031-192] “Arabella!” he cried on a note of pleading. “Are ye meaning it? Must I

[031-193] release ye? Must I let ye go and never set eyes on ye again? Or will ye

[031-194] stay and make this exile endurable until we can go home together? Och,

[031-195] ye're crying now! What have I said to make ye cry, my dear?”

[031-196] “I... I thought you'd never say it,” she mocked him through her tears.

[031-197] “Well, now, ye see there was Lord Julian, a fine figure of a....”

[031-198] “There was never, never anybody but you, Peter.”

[031-199] They had, of course, a deal to say thereafter, so much, indeed, that

[031-200] they sat down to say it, whilst time sped on, and Governor Blood forgot

[031-201] the duties of his office. He had reached home at last. His odyssey was

[031-202] ended.

[031-203] And meanwhile Colonel Bishop's fleet had come to anchor, and the Colonel

[031-204] had landed on the mole, a disgruntled man to be disgruntled further yet.

[031-205] He was accompanied ashore by Lord Julian Wade.

[031-206] A corporal's guard was drawn up to receive him, and in advance of

[031-207] this stood Major Mallard and two others who were unknown to the

[031-208] Deputy-Governor: one slight and elegant, the other big and brawny.

[031-209] Major Mallard advanced. “Colonel Bishop, I have orders to arrest you.

[031-210] Your sword, sir!”

[031-211] “By order of the Governor of Jamaica,” said the elegant little man

[031-212] behind Major Mallard. Bishop swung to him.

[031-213] “The Governor? Ye're mad!” He looked from one to the other. “I am the

[031-214] Governor.”

[031-215] “You were,” said the little man dryly. “But we've changed that in your

[031-216] absence. You're broke for abandoning your post without due cause, and

[031-217] thereby imperiling the settlement over which you had charge. It's a

[031-218] serious matter, Colonel Bishop, as you may find. Considering that you

[031-219] held your office from the Government of King James, it is even possible

[031-220] that a charge of treason might lie against you. It rests with your

[031-221] successor entirely whether ye're hanged or not.”

[031-222] Bishop rapped out an oath, and then, shaken by a sudden fear: “Who the

[031-223] devil may you be?” he asked.

[031-224] “I am Lord Willoughby, Governor General of His Majesty's colonies in the

[031-225] West Indies. You were informed, I think, of my coming.”

[031-226] The remains of Bishop's anger fell from him like a cloak. He broke into

[031-227] a sweat of fear. Behind him Lord Julian looked on, his handsome face

[031-228] suddenly white and drawn.

[031-229] “But, my lord...” began the Colonel.

[031-230] “Sir, I am not concerned to hear your reasons,” his lordship interrupted

[031-231] him harshly. “I am on the point of sailing and I have not the time. The

[031-232] Governor will hear you, and no doubt deal justly by you.” He waved to

[031-233] Major Mallard, and Bishop, a crumpled, broken man, allowed himself to be

[031-234] led away.

[031-235] To Lord Julian, who went with him, since none deterred him, Bishop

[031-236] expressed himself when presently he had sufficiently recovered.

[031-237] “This is one more item to the account of that scoundrel Blood,” he

[031-238] said, through his teeth. “My God, what a reckoning there will be when we

[031-239] meet!”

[031-240] Major Mallard turned away his face that he might conceal his smile, and

[031-241] without further words led him a prisoner to the Governor's house, the

[031-242] house that so long had been Colonel Bishop's own residence. He was left

[031-243] to wait under guard in the hall, whilst Major Mallard went ahead to

[031-244] announce him.

[031-245] Miss Bishop was still with Peter Blood when Major Mallard entered. His

[031-246] announcement startled them back to realities.

[031-247] “You will be merciful with him. You will spare him all you can for my

[031-248] sake, Peter,” she pleaded.

[031-249] “To be sure I will,” said Blood. “But I'm afraid the circumstances

[031-250] won't.”

[031-251] She effaced herself, escaping into the garden, and Major Mallard fetched

[031-252] the Colonel.

[031-253] “His excellency the Governor will see you now,” said he, and threw wide

[031-254] the door.

[031-255] Colonel Bishop staggered in, and stood waiting.

[031-256] At the table sat a man of whom nothing was visible but the top of a

[031-257] carefully curled black head. Then this head was raised, and a pair of

[031-258] blue eyes solemnly regarded the prisoner. Colonel Bishop made a noise

[031-259] in his throat, and, paralyzed by amazement, stared into the face of his

[031-260] excellency the Deputy-Governor of Jamaica, which was the face of the man

[031-261] he had been hunting in [[tortuga|Tortuga]] to his present undoing.

[031-262] The situation was best expressed to Lord Willoughby by van der Kuylen as

[031-263] the pair stepped aboard the Admiral's flagship.

[031-264] “Id is fery boedigal!” he said, his blue eyes twinkling. “Cabdain Blood

[031-265] is fond of boedry--you remember de abble-blossoms. So? Ha, ha!”
