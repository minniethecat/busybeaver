# Lord Julian Wade

## Overview

Lord Julian Wade is the English aristocrat sent to the Caribbean "to put down piracy" ((019-079)) who becomes Peter Blood's most important ally. As a representative of the Crown that condemned Blood, Lord Julian initially embodies everything Blood opposes. But his intelligence, sense of justice, and growing recognition of Blood's true character transform him from antagonist to advocate. Lord Julian bridges the world of legitimate authority and Blood's outlaw existence, ultimately facilitating Blood's restoration to honour.

## In the Novel

### The Anti-Piracy Mission

Lord Julian arrives in the Caribbean with a royal commission to suppress buccaneering. His first encounters with Blood are adversarial — a representative of the law confronting an outlaw. But Lord Julian is not Bishop or Jeffreys; he is capable of seeing beyond legal categories to moral reality.

### The Advocate

As Lord Julian learns more about Blood's story, his position shifts. He recognizes that Blood was unjustly condemned and that his piracy has been conducted with "such honour as is consistent with a gentleman of fortune." Lord Julian becomes Blood's advocate before the Crown, arguing for his pardon and rehabilitation.

### The Bridge to Legitimacy

Lord Julian's most important function in the novel is structural: he provides the bridge between Blood's outlaw existence and legitimate authority. Without a figure like Lord Julian — capable of recognizing honour outside the law — Blood could never be restored. His advocacy makes Blood's eventual governorship possible.

## Relationships

- **Peter Blood** — From adversary to advocate; their relationship embodies the novel's argument that honour transcends legal status
- **Arabella Bishop** — His romantic interest and ally in recognizing Blood's virtue
- **Colonel Bishop** — A fellow representative of Crown authority whose cruelty contrasts with Lord Julian's justice
