# Barbados

## Overview

Barbados is the British Caribbean colony where Peter Blood is transported as a slave following his conviction for treason. The wealthiest of England's sugar colonies, Barbados represents both the economic engine of empire and the site of Blood's degradation. It is here that Blood is purchased by Colonel Bishop, endures plantation slavery, meets Arabella Bishop, and ultimately escapes to begin his buccaneering career.

## In the Novel

### The Arrival

Blood and his fellow rebels-convict arrive in Barbados as "human merchandise" ((004-055)), sold at auction to plantation owners. The "dulness of life in Barbados" ((005-150)) — both its physical monotony and its social emptiness — is the backdrop against which Blood's intelligence and wit burn all the brighter.

### Colonel Bishop's Domain

Bishop, as Deputy Governor, dominates Barbados society. His plantation, worked by "the stockade which the plantation slaves inhabited" ((005-138)), represents the brutality that underlies the island's wealth. Blood observes that "all things considered I prefer Barbados" ((005-214)) — a sardonic assessment that the island, for all its horrors, is at least known territory.

### The Escape

Barbados is the prison Blood must escape. His medical skills earn him the freedom of movement that makes escape possible, and his seizure of a Spanish ship from Barbados waters marks the transition from slave to captain.

## Significance

- Site of Blood's enslavement and escape
- Seat of Colonel Bishop's authority
- Setting for Blood's first encounters with Arabella
