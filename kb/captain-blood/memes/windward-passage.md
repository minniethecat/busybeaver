## Overview

The Windward Passage is a strait in the Caribbean Sea, separating the islands of Cuba and Hispaniola. In the 17th century, it was a vital shipping route for Spanish treasure fleets, French merchant vessels, and the buccaneers who preyed upon them. In *Captain Blood*, the Windward Passage features prominently as a theater of naval action and strategic navigation ([013-133]).

## In the Novel

Captain Blood's naval campaigns frequently brought him through the Windward Passage. During his pursuit by Don Miguel de Espinosa's Spanish fleet, there was "a fight in the Windward" Islands region ([013-133]). Blood's intimate knowledge of Caribbean waters, extending "from the Bahamas to the Windward Isles, from New Providence to" the Spanish Main, established him as a formidable naval commander ([013-145]).

The passage served as a critical navigation point. Blood was recorded "beating up for the Windward Passage" before intermittent winds slowed his advance ([018-219]), and later he was "making for the Windward" Passage on another voyage ([019-095]). The passage's challenging conditions — unpredictable winds and currents — tested even experienced mariners. On one return journey, Blood's ship endured "suffering the diurnal" hardships of the Windward Passage ([024-348]).
