[010-001] The planter waved the matter aside. Almost it seemed to offend him.

[010-002] “Tush! Tush! After this splendid deed of yours, do you suppose I can be

[010-003] thinking of such things?”

[010-004] “I'm glad ye feel like that about it. But I'm thinking it's mighty lucky

[010-005] for me the Spaniards didn't come to-day instead of yesterday, or it's

[010-006] in the same plight as [[jeremy-pitt|Jeremy Pitt]] I'd be this minute. And in that case

[010-007] where was the genius that would have turned the tables on these rascally

[010-008] Spaniards?”

[010-009] “Why speak of it now?”

[010-010] Mr. Blood resumed: “ye'll please to understand that I must, Colonel,

[010-011] darling. Ye've worked a deal of wickedness and cruelty in your time, and

[010-012] I want this to be a lesson to you, a lesson that ye'll remember--for

[010-013] the sake of others who may come after us. There's Jeremy up there in the

[010-014] round-house with a back that's every colour of the rainbow; and the poor

[010-015] lad'll not be himself again for a month. And if it hadn't been for the

[010-016] Spaniards maybe it's dead he'd be by now, and maybe myself with him.”

[010-017] [[hagthorpe|Hagthorpe]] lounged forward. He was a fairly tall, vigorous man with a

[010-018] clear-cut, attractive face which in itself announced his breeding.

[010-019] “Why will you be wasting words on the hog?” wondered that sometime

[010-020] officer in the [[royal-navy|Royal Navy]]. “Fling him overboard and have done with him.”

[010-021] The Colonel's eyes bulged in his head. “What the devil do you mean?” he

[010-022] blustered.

[010-023] “It's the lucky man ye are entirely, Colonel, though ye don't guess the

[010-024] source of your good fortune.”

[010-025] And now another intervened--the brawny, one-eyed [[wolverstone|Wolverstone]], less

[010-026] mercifully disposed than his more gentlemanly fellow-convict.

[010-027] “String him up from the yardarm,” he cried, his deep voice harsh and

[010-028] angry, and more than one of the slaves standing to their arms made echo.

[010-029] [[colonel-bishop|Colonel Bishop]] trembled. Mr. Blood turned. He was quite calm.

[010-030] “If you please, Wolverstone,” said he, “I conduct affairs in my own way.

[010-031] That is the pact. You'll please to remember it.” His eyes looked along

[010-032] the ranks, making it plain that he addressed them all. “I desire that

[010-033] Colonel Bishop should have his life. One reason is that I require him as

[010-034] a hostage. If ye insist on [[execution|hanging]] him, ye'll have to hang me with him,

[010-035] or in the alternative I'll go ashore.”

[010-036] He paused. There was no answer. But they stood hang-dog and

[010-037] half-mutinous before him, save Hagthorpe, who shrugged and smiled

[010-038] wearily.

[010-039] Mr. Blood resumed: “Ye'll please to understand that aboard a ship there

[010-040] is one captain. So.” He swung again to the startled Colonel. “Though

[010-041] I promise you your life, I must--as you've heard--keep you aboard as a

[010-042] hostage for the good behaviour of Governor Steed and what's left of the

[010-043] fort until we put to sea.”

[010-044] “Until you...” Horror prevented Colonel Bishop from echoing the

[010-045] remainder of that incredible speech.

[010-046] “Just so,” said [[peter-blood|Peter Blood]], and he turned to the officers who had

[010-047] accompanied the Colonel. “The boat is waiting, gentlemen. You'll have

[010-048] heard what I said. Convey it with my compliments to his excellency.”

[010-049] “But, sir...” one of them began.

[010-050] “There is no more to be said, gentlemen. My name is Blood--Captain

[010-051] Blood, if you please, of this ship the Cinco Llagas, taken as a prize of

[010-052] war from [[don-diego|Don Diego de Espinosa]] y Valdez, who is my prisoner aboard.

[010-053] You are to understand that I have turned the tables on more than the

[010-054] Spaniards. There's the ladder. You'll find it more convenient than being

[010-055] heaved over the side, which is what'll happen if you linger.”

[010-056] They went, though not without some hustling, regardless of the

[010-057] bellowings of Colonel Bishop, whose monstrous rage was fanned by terror

[010-058] at finding himself at the mercy of these men of whose cause to hate him

[010-059] he was very fully conscious.

[010-060] A half-dozen of them, apart from Jeremy Pitt, who was utterly

[010-061] incapacitated for the present, possessed a superficial knowledge

[010-062] of seamanship. Hagthorpe, although he had been a fighting officer,

[010-063] untrained in navigation, knew how to handle a ship, and under his

[010-064] directions they set about getting under way.

[010-065] The anchor catted, and the mainsail unfurled, they stood out for the

[010-066] open before a gentle breeze, without interference from the fort.

[010-067] As they were running close to the headland east of the bay, Peter

[010-068] Blood returned to the Colonel, who, under guard and panic-stricken, had

[010-069] dejectedly resumed his seat on the coamings of the main batch.

[010-070] “Can ye swim, Colonel?”

[010-071] Colonel Bishop looked up. His great face was yellow and seemed in that

[010-072] moment of a preternatural flabbiness; his beady eyes were beadier than

[010-073] ever.

[010-074] “As your doctor, now, I prescribe a swim to cool the excessive heat

[010-075] of your humours.” Blood delivered the explanation pleasantly, and,

[010-076] receiving still no answer from the Colonel, continued: “It's a mercy for

[010-077] you I'm not by nature as bloodthirsty as some of my friends here. And

[010-078] it's the devil's own labour I've had to prevail upon them not to be

[010-079] vindictive. I doubt if ye're worth the pains I've taken for you.”

[010-080] He was lying. He had no doubt at all. Had he followed his own wishes and

[010-081] instincts, he would certainly have strung the Colonel up, and accounted

[010-082] it a meritorious deed. It was the thought of [[arabella-bishop|Arabella Bishop]] that had

[010-083] urged him to mercy, and had led him to oppose the natural vindictiveness

[010-084] of his fellow-slaves until he had been in danger of precipitating a

[010-085] [[treason|mutiny]]. It was entirely to the fact that the Colonel was her uncle,

[010-086] although he did not even begin to suspect such a cause, that he owed

[010-087] such mercy as was now being shown him.

[010-088] “You shall have a chance to swim for it,” Peter Blood continued. “It's

[010-089] not above a quarter of a mile to the headland yonder, and with ordinary

[010-090] luck ye should manage it. Faith, you're fat enough to float. Come on!

[010-091] Now, don't be hesitating or it's a long voyage ye'll be going with us,

[010-092] and the devil knows what may happen to you. You're not loved any more

[010-093] than you deserve.”

[010-094] Colonel Bishop mastered himself, and rose. A merciless despot, who had

[010-095] never known the need for restraint in all these years, he was doomed by

[010-096] ironic fate to practise restraint in the very moment when his feelings

[010-097] had reached their most violent intensity.

[010-098] Peter Blood gave an order. A plank was run out over the gunwale, and

[010-099] lashed down.

[010-100] “If you please, Colonel,” said he, with a graceful flourish of

[010-101] invitation.

[010-102] The Colonel looked at him, and there was hell in his glance. Then,

[010-103] taking his resolve, and putting the best face upon it, since no other

[010-104] could help him here, he kicked off his shoes, peeled off his fine coat

[010-105] of biscuit-coloured taffetas, and climbed upon the plank.

[010-106] A moment he paused, steadied by a hand that clutched the ratlines,

[010-107] looking down in terror at the green water rushing past some

[010-108] five-and-twenty feet below.

[010-109] “Just take a little walk, Colonel, darling,” said a smooth, mocking

[010-110] voice behind him.

[010-111] Still clinging, Colonel Bishop looked round in hesitation, and saw the

[010-112] bulwarks lined with swarthy faces--the faces of men that as lately as

[010-113] yesterday would have turned pale under his frown, faces that were now

[010-114] all wickedly agrin.

[010-115] For a moment rage stamped out his fear. He cursed them aloud venomously

[010-116] and incoherently, then loosed his hold and stepped out upon the plank.

[010-117] Three steps he took before he lost his balance and went tumbling into

[010-118] the green depths below.

[010-119] When he came to the surface again, gasping for air, the Cinco Llagas

[010-120] was already some furlongs to leeward. But the roaring cheer of mocking

[010-121] valediction from the rebels-convict reached him across the water, to

[010-122] drive the iron of impotent rage deeper into his soul.

[010-123] CHAPTER X. DON DIEGO

[010-124] Don Diego [[don-miguel|de Espinosa]] y Valdez awoke, and with languid eyes in aching

[010-125] head, he looked round the cabin, which was flooded with sunlight from

[010-126] the square windows astern. Then he uttered a moan, and closed his eyes

[010-127] again, impelled to this by the monstrous ache in his head. Lying thus,

[010-128] he attempted to think, to locate himself in time and space. But between

[010-129] the pain in his head and the confusion in his mind, he found coherent

[010-130] thought impossible.

[010-131] An indefinite sense of alarm drove him to open his eyes again, and once

[010-132] more to consider his surroundings.

[010-133] There could be no doubt that he lay in the great cabin of his own

[010-134] ship, the Cinco Llagas, so that his vague disquiet must be, surely,

[010-135] ill-founded. And yet, stirrings of memory coming now to the assistance

[010-136] of reflection, compelled him uneasily to insist that here something was

[010-137] not as it should be. The low position of the sun, flooding the cabin

[010-138] with golden light from those square ports astern, suggested to him at

[010-139] first that it was early morning, on the assumption that the vessel was

[010-140] headed westward. Then the alternative occurred to him. They might be

[010-141] sailing eastward, in which case the time of day would be late afternoon.

[010-142] That they were sailing he could feel from the gentle forward heave of

[010-143] the vessel under him. But how did they come to be sailing, and he, the

[010-144] master, not to know whether their course lay east or west, not to be

[010-145] able to recollect whither they were bound?

[010-146] His mind went back over the adventure of yesterday, if of yesterday it

[010-147] was. He was clear on the matter of the easily successful raid upon the

[010-148] Island of [[barbados|Barbados]]; every detail stood vividly in his memory up to the

[010-149] moment at which, returning aboard, he had stepped on to his own deck

[010-150] again. There memory abruptly and inexplicably ceased.

[010-151] He was beginning to torture his mind with conjecture, when the door

[010-152] opened, and to Don Diego's increasing mystification he beheld his best

[010-153] suit of clothes step into the cabin. It was a singularly elegant and

[010-154] characteristically Spanish suit of black taffetas with silver lace that

[010-155] had been made for him a year ago in Cadiz, and he knew each detail of it

[010-156] so well that it was impossible he could now be mistaken.

[010-157] The suit paused to close the door, then advanced towards the couch on

[010-158] which Don Diego was extended, and inside the suit came a tall, slender

[010-159] gentleman of about Don Diego's own height and shape. Seeing the wide,

[010-160] startled eyes of the Spaniard upon him, the gentleman lengthened his

[010-161] stride.

[010-162] “Awake, eh?” said he in Spanish.

[010-163] The recumbent man looked up bewildered into a pair of light-blue eyes

[010-164] that regarded him out of a tawny, sardonic face set in a cluster of

[010-165] black ringlets. But he was too bewildered to make any answer.

[010-166] The stranger's fingers touched the top of Don Diego's head, whereupon

[010-167] Don Diego winced and cried out in pain.

[010-168] “Tender, eh?” said the stranger. He took Don Diego's wrist between thumb

[010-169] and second finger. And then, at last, the intrigued Spaniard spoke.

[010-170] “Are you a doctor?”

[010-171] “Among other things.” The swarthy gentleman continued his study of the

[010-172] patient's pulse. “Firm and regular,” he announced at last, and dropped

[010-173] the wrist. “You've taken no great harm.”

[010-174] Don Diego struggled up into a sitting position on the red velvet couch.

[010-175] “Who the devil are you?” he asked. “And what the devil are you doing in

[010-176] my clothes and aboard my ship?”

[010-177] The level black eyebrows went up, a faint smile curled the lips of the

[010-178] long mouth.

[010-179] “You are still delirious, I fear. This is not your ship. This is my

[010-180] ship, and these are my clothes.”

[010-181] “Your ship?” quoth the other, aghast, and still more aghast he added:

[010-182] “Your clothes? But... then....” Wildly his eyes looked about him. They

[010-183] scanned the cabin once again, scrutinizing each familiar object. “Am I

[010-184] mad?” he asked at last. “Surely this ship is the Cinco Llagas?”

[010-185] “The Cinco Llagas it is.”

[010-186] “Then....” The Spaniard broke off. His glance grew still more troubled.

[010-187] “Valga me Dios!” he cried out, like a man in anguish. “Will you tell me

[010-188] also that you are Don Diego de Espinosa?”
