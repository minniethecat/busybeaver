## Overview

The Antilles, stretching in a great arc from Puerto Rico to the coast of South America, form the eastern rim of the Caribbean Sea. In *Captain Blood*, the islands of the Antilles serve as waypoints, resupply stations, and occasional battlegrounds as Blood's buccaneer squadron navigates the complex geography and politics of the late seventeenth-century Caribbean.

## In the Novel

### Geographical Context

The Antilles provide the framework within which much of the novel's Caribbean action unfolds. When Blood's ships sail "into the Caribbean," they are passing through the passages between the Antilles islands — the gateways between the Atlantic Ocean and the Caribbean Sea proper. The islands themselves represent the European colonial presence in the New World: Spanish, French, English, and Dutch settlements dot the archipelago, each with its own interests and allegiances.

### Colonial Chessboard

The Antilles in Sabatini's novel are not a unified region but a patchwork of rival jurisdictions. French buccaneers operate from the northern coasts of Hispaniola and the small island of Tortuga; English planters dominate Barbados and Jamaica; Spanish authorities cling to their vast but decaying American empire. The islands of the Antilles are the stage upon which European rivalries play out in miniature:

> ...the French boucanhunters from Northern Hispaniola, men who had good cause to hate the Spanish...
> ([008-08334])

### Navigation and Strategy

The geography of the Antilles shapes the novel's naval action. Passages between the islands — the Mona Passage, the Windward Passage — are chokepoints where ships can be intercepted and where battles are fought. Blood's knowledge of Antilles geography, gained during his months of servitude in Barbados, becomes a strategic asset that Spanish commanders consistently underestimate.

The islands also serve as sources of intelligence. News of Spanish fleet movements, of colonial governors' attitudes toward buccaneers, and of potential targets all flow through the port towns of the Antilles:

> ...the Antilles, watching the narrow seas between the islands...
> ([016-16938])
