# Court of St. James

## In the Novel

The Court of St. James, the royal court of England, appears in *Captain Blood* as the seat of diplomatic authority that responds to complaints about Blood's buccaneering in the Caribbean. As Blood's reputation grows and his exploits disrupt colonial trade, diplomatic pressure mounts on the English Crown.

> "An echo of it had reached Europe, and at the Court of St. James's angry representations were made by the Spanish Ambassador" ([013-146])

The Court's involvement illustrates the international dimension of Blood's actions — his private war against Spain creates diplomatic friction between England and Spain. The Court of St. James also features in the legal apparatus that condemned Blood after the Monmouth Rebellion, as "the trouble between the courts of St. James's and the Escurial" ([005-351]) frames the broader Anglo-Spanish tensions that Blood exploits to his advantage.
