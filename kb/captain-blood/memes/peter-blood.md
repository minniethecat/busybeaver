# Peter Blood

## Overview

Peter Blood is the protagonist of Rafael Sabatini's *Captain Blood* (1922). An Irish physician and former soldier, Blood is swept up in the aftermath of the Monmouth Rebellion, unjustly convicted of treason, and transported as a slave to Barbados. His escape from slavery, his transformation into the most feared buccaneer in the Caribbean, and his eventual restoration to honour as Governor of Jamaica trace one of the great arcs in adventure fiction. Blood is defined by his wit, his medical skill, his seamanship, and above all his unshakeable sense of honour — qualities that distinguish him from both the corrupt aristocrats who condemn him and the common pirates who become his crew.

## In the Novel

### The Physician Condemned

Blood first appears as a prisoner awaiting trial after the Battle of Sedgemoor. His "crime" was not fighting for Monmouth but dressing the wounds of a dying rebel — an act of medical compassion that Judge Jeffreys construes as treason. Blood's defiant laughter in the courtroom establishes his fundamental character: he will not be intimidated by power wielded unjustly.

### Slave and Escapist

Transported to Barbados and purchased by Colonel Bishop, Blood endures the degradation of slavery with sardonic wit. His medical skills earn him privileges that make escape possible. Leading a desperate band of fellow rebels-convict, Blood seizes a Spanish ship and begins his career as a buccaneer.

### The Gentleman Pirate

As Captain Blood, he develops a reputation unique among Caribbean pirates: he maintains a code of honour, refuses to attack English vessels, treats prisoners with dignity, and operates with strategic brilliance rather than brute force. His capture of Spanish ships and his ransom operations make him wealthy and feared.

### From Outlaw to Governor

Blood's service to William of Orange during the Glorious Revolution transforms his status. The condemned traitor of James II becomes the honored servant of William III, ultimately appointed Governor of Jamaica — a restoration of the honour and position that were stolen from him.

## Relationships

- **Arabella Bishop** — Niece of Colonel Bishop, initially despises Blood as a rebel and pirate, eventually becomes his love interest and wife
- **Colonel Bishop** — The brutal Barbados planter who purchases Blood as a slave and becomes his nemesis
- **Jeremy Pitt** — Blood's loyal first mate and fellow escapee from slavery
- **Lord Julian Wade** — English nobleman sent to suppress piracy who becomes Blood's advocate and friend
- **Don Diego de Espinosa** — Spanish admiral and Blood's recurring adversary
- **Judge Jeffreys** — The Lord Chief Justice who condemns Blood, representing the corruption of Stuart justice
