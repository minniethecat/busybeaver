# Carlisle Bay

## Overview

Carlisle Bay is the natural harbour on the southwest coast of Barbados, serving the capital Bridgetown. In the novel, the bay is the entry point for Blood's enslavement, the backdrop for his escape, and later the scene of Spanish privateer incursions. Its "sunlit waters" ([000-2820]) contrast with the brutal reality of plantation slavery.

## In the Novel

### Arrival of the Convicts

The transport ship carrying Blood and the other condemned rebels enters Carlisle Bay and "put ashore the forty-two surviving rebels-convict" ([000-2220]). From the bay, Blood is taken to the Bridgetown auction block where Colonel Bishop purchases him.

### View from the Plantation

Jeremy Pitt, working on Colonel Bishop's plantation, looks out "over Carlisle Bay, of which this plateau commanded a clear view from the fort on one side to the long low arm of land on the other" ([000-4738]). The bay is a constant presence — a reminder of the sea and freedom just out of reach.

### Blood's Escape

Carlisle Bay is the stage for Blood's daring escape. The Spanish privateer that enters the bay under false colours provides the opportunity Blood seizes to liberate himself and his fellow convicts.

### Later Privateer Actions

After Blood becomes a buccaneer, Carlisle Bay remains significant. A "wounded, battered English ship, the Pride of Devon" crawls into the bay after an encounter with Blood's fleet ([000-3036]). Later, a Spanish privateer enters "Carlisle Bay under her false colours" ([000-5136]), part of the ongoing retribution against the Brethren of the Coast.

## Historical Context

Carlisle Bay has been Bridgetown's natural harbour since the founding of the Barbados colony in 1627. In the 17th century, it was one of the busiest ports in the English Caribbean, central to the sugar trade and the transatlantic slave economy. The bay's strategic importance made it a frequent target of privateers and naval actions throughout the colonial period.
