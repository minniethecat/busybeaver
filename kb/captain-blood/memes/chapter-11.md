[011-002] handsome suit of clothes, is mine by right of conquest. Just as you, Don

[011-003] Diego, are my prisoner.”

[011-004] Startling as was the explanation, yet it proved soothing to [[don-diego|Don Diego]],

[011-005] being so much less startling than the things he was beginning to

[011-006] imagine.

[011-007] “But... Are you not Spanish, then?”

[011-008] “You flatter my Castilian accent. I have the honour to be Irish. You

[011-009] were thinking that a miracle had happened. So it has--a miracle wrought

[011-010] by my genius, which is considerable.”

[011-011] Succinctly now Captain Blood dispelled the mystery by a relation of

[011-012] the facts. It was a narrative that painted red and white by turns the

[011-013] Spaniard's countenance. He put a hand to the back of his head, and there

[011-014] discovered, in confirmation of the story, a lump as large as a pigeon's

[011-015] egg. Lastly, he stared wild-eyed at the sardonic Captain Blood.

[011-016] “And my son? What of my son?” he cried out. “He was in the boat that

[011-017] brought me aboard.”

[011-018] “Your son is safe; he and the boat's crew together with your gunner and

[011-019] his men are snugly in irons under hatches.”

[011-021] the tawny face above him. He composed himself. After all, he possessed

[011-022] the stoicism proper to his desperate trade. The dice had fallen against

[011-023] him in this venture. The tables had been turned upon him in the very

[011-024] moment of success. He accepted the situation with the fortitude of a

[011-025] fatalist.

[011-027] “And now, Senior Capitan?”

[011-029] assumed--“being a humane man, I am sorry to find that ye're not dead

[011-030] from the tap we gave you. For it means that you'll be put to the trouble

[011-031] of dying all over again.”
