# Arabella Bishop

## Overview

Arabella Bishop is the niece of Colonel William Bishop and the romantic interest of Peter Blood. More than a conventional adventure-novel heroine, Arabella undergoes a significant moral journey: she begins by despising Blood as a convicted rebel and pirate, gradually recognizes his honour and the injustice of his condemnation, and ultimately becomes his advocate and wife. Her transformation mirrors the novel's larger argument about the gap between legal judgment and moral truth.

## In the Novel

### First Impressions

Arabella first encounters Blood as a slave on her uncle's Barbados plantation. Her initial contempt is class-based and political — she sees a convicted traitor, not a physician unjustly condemned. The "dulness of life in Barbados to a lady of her social aspirations" ((005-150)) makes Blood's wit and medical skill initially invisible to her.

### The Awakening

Arabella's growing recognition of Blood's qualities — his competence, his honour, his refusal to grovel — creates tension with her uncle. Bishop warns her that "you are talking treason" ((005-196)) when she defends Blood. This moment crystallizes the novel's central irony: the more Arabella sees Blood clearly, the more she herself becomes implicated in his "treason."

### Recognition and Regret

Later in the novel, Arabella confronts the "monstrous injustice" ((023-379)) done to Blood. Her realization that "you do him an injustice" ((024-157)) completes her moral arc. She moves from judge to advocate, from Colonel Bishop's niece to Blood's partner.

## Relationships

- **Peter Blood** — Her eventual husband; their relationship traces the novel's moral arc from prejudice to recognition
- **Colonel Bishop** — Her uncle, whose cruelty she must ultimately reject
- **Lord Julian Wade** — Her suitor and ally in recognizing Blood's true character
