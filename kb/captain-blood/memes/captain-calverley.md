# Captain Calverley

## Overview

Captain Calverley is a Royal Navy officer who appears late in the novel as the commander of a frigate despatched by Colonel Bishop to capture or kill Captain Blood. His encounter with Blood aboard the *Arabella* delivers one of the novel's richest comic reversals — arriving with haughty contempt to demand a pirate's surrender, Calverley is instead confronted with the revelation that Blood now holds a commission in the King's service, signed by Lord Sunderland himself.

## In the Novel

Calverley is introduced as he boards the *Arabella* with "truculent, and disdainful" bearing, expecting to confront a desperate buccaneer cornered into surrender ([022-019]). He finds instead a man whose demeanour confounds every expectation. Blood greets him pleasantly: "Good-day to you, sir. I have the honour to give you welcome aboard the Arabella. My name is Blood — Captain Blood, at your service. You may have heard of me" ([022-019]).

"Captain Calverley stared hard. The airy manner of this redoubtable buccaneer was hardly what he had looked for in a desperate fellow, compelled to ignominious surrender" ([022-027]). Dismissing Blood's manner as bravado, Calverley delivers a contemptuous reply: "You'll ruffle it to the gallows, no doubt. I suppose that is after the fashion of your kind. Meanwhile it's your surrender I require, my man, not your impudence" ([022-048]).

The reversal comes swiftly. Lord Julian Wade steps forward and, with cold formality, informs Calverley that "Captain Blood holds a commission in the King's service under the seal of my Lord Sunderland, His Majesty's Secretary of State" ([022-048]). The news lands like a thunderclap: "Captain Calverley's face empurpled; his eyes bulged. The buccaneers in the background chuckled and crowed and swore among themselves in their relish of this comedy" ([022-048]).

Calverley's bewilderment deepens when he learns the identity of the elegantly dressed gentleman before him — Lord Julian Wade, the King's envoy and Sunderland's near kinsman. "Light broke upon the darkness of Calverley's mind. 'I see. I understand'" — though Lord Julian acidly replies: "I will take leave to doubt it" ([022-090]). Swept "from surprise to surprise, Captain Calverley could do no more than stare" as the full implications of the changed political situation became clear ([022-090]).

The episode serves a dual narrative purpose: it marks the moment when Blood's transformation from outlaw to Crown servant becomes publicly acknowledged, and it delivers a satisfying comeuppance to the arrogance of colonial authority — Calverley arriving as an instrument of Bishop's vengeance, departing as a humbled officer forced to recognise the new order.

## Relationships

- **Colonel Bishop** — The Governor of Barbados who despatches Calverley on his mission
- **Lord Julian Wade** — The King's envoy whose presence aboard the *Arabella* upends Calverley's assumptions
- **Peter Blood** — The man Calverley expected to capture, who instead confronts him with a King's commission
