# Tortuga

## Overview

Tortuga (Île de la Tortue) is the buccaneer stronghold off the coast of Hispaniola that serves as Peter Blood's primary base of operations during his pirate career. A lawless port where French, English, and Dutch buccaneers mingle, Tortuga represents the alternative society Blood builds — one governed by competence and honour rather than by rank and legal title.

## In the Novel

### The Pirate Haven

Tortuga is the natural home of the Caribbean buccaneers. Blood's crew repairs and resupplies there, dividing spoils and planning new expeditions. The island's lawlessness is both its attraction and its danger — there is no authority beyond the captains' personal codes.

### Blood's Operations

From Tortuga, Blood launches his expeditions against Spanish shipping. The island's strategic location — near the Windward Passage, through which Spanish treasure ships must sail — makes it an ideal base for Caribbean operations. Blood's presence in Tortuga establishes him as a major figure in the buccaneering community.

## Significance

- Primary base of Blood's buccaneering operations
- Symbol of the alternative society of the Caribbean pirates
