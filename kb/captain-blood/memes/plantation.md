# Plantation

## Overview

The [[plantation-system|plantation]] is the economic foundation of the colonial Caribbean and the primary site of suffering in *[[peter-blood|Captain Blood]]*. It is to the "southern [[plantation-system|plantation]]s" of [[jamaica|Jamaica]], [[barbados|Barbados]], and the Leeward Islands ((004-064)) that the convicted rebels are transported. The [[plantation-system|plantation]] system — dependent on slave labor, producing sugar for European markets — represents the brutal intersection of imperial economics and human misery that drives Blood's escape to the sea.

## Historical Background

The 17th-century Caribbean [[plantation-system|sugar plantation]] was one of the most profitable economic institutions in the world — and one of the most deadly. Sugar cultivation required intensive labor in tropical conditions, and mortality rates among enslaved workers were catastrophic. The transition from [[slavery|indentured servitude]] to African chattel slavery accelerated during this period as planters sought more controllable labor forces. [[barbados|Barbados]], where Blood is sent, was England's wealthiest colony, its sugar planters among the richest men in the empire.

## In the Novel

### Transportation to the Plantations

The novel's fourth chapter details the economic logic of transportation: "Slaves were urgently required in the [[plantation-system|plantation]]s, and a healthy, vigorous man could be reckoned worth at least from ten to fifteen pounds a head" ((004-070)–(004-071)). The rebels are "human merchandise" ((004-055)), their lives valued by the labor they can perform.

### Plantation Life

Blood's experience on [[colonel-bishop|Colonel Bishop]]'s [[barbados|Barbados]] [[plantation-system|plantation]] is degrading but not typical — his medical skills earn him privileges denied to common field slaves. Yet the [[plantation-system|plantation]]'s reality is ever-present: "the stockade which the [[plantation-system|plantation]] slaves inhabited" ((005-138)), the brutal sun, and the knowledge that escape means death. The [[plantation-system|plantation]] is "this hell" ((006-222)) from which Blood must escape.

### Escape and Memory

Blood's escape from the [[plantation-system|plantation]] transforms him from slave to pirate, but the [[plantation-system|plantation]] remains the measure of his alternative: better to die at sea than return to the [[plantation-system|plantation]]. When his crew faces recapture, "they were likely to exchange the slavery from which they had so lately escaped for a slavery still worse" ((011-339)). The [[plantation-system|plantation]] is the baseline horror against which all other risks are measured.
