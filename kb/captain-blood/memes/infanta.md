## Overview

The **Infanta** was a small Spanish vessel captured by Captain Peter Blood's buccaneer fleet. She was the smaller and more fragile of two Spanish prizes taken in the same action, the other being the *San Felipe* ([016-278]). Due to her light construction, the Infanta suffered considerable damage during her capture and subsequent service.

## In the Novel

The Infanta was taken together with the *San Felipe* in an engagement where neither Spanish ship offered much resistance. The Infanta's crew was "bewildered" by shots from the buccaneer vessels and quickly surrendered ([017-017]). By Blood's own assessment, neither the San Felipe nor the Infanta "offered much resistance" — they were taken with relative ease ([017-024]).

Following her capture, the Infanta was manned by a prize crew and incorporated into Blood's fleet ([017-042]). However, the ship proved problematic. Her light, "frailer" construction meant she fared "even worse" than her consort, suffering damage that left her barely seaworthy ([017-058]). At one point she was described as "merely" able to put to sea ([017-085]), indicating a vessel of limited combat utility.

Despite her fragility, the Infanta continued to sail with Blood's squadron. She was observed leading side by side with the *Elizabeth* during fleet manoeuvres ([018-076]).

## Captain & Crew

After capture, the Infanta was operated by a prize crew of buccaneers under Blood's overall command ([017-042]). No specific captain is named for this vessel in the novel.

## Fate

The Infanta's ultimate fate is not explicitly stated in the novel. Given her frail construction and the damage sustained during capture, she was likely either disposed of or relegated to support duties within Blood's fleet.
