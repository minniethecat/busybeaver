# Spanish Crown

The **Spanish Crown** (also referred to as the **Catholic King**) was the monarchy of Spain under King Charles II, which claimed sovereignty over vast colonial territories in the Americas during the late 17th century. The Crown's interests in the Caribbean brought it into direct conflict with English and French buccaneers, most notably Captain Peter Blood.[^1]

## Authority and Governance

The Spanish Crown governed its colonial possessions through a hierarchical system culminating in the Supreme Council of Castile, which acted as the administrative and judicial authority for colonial affairs. The Crown's naval forces in the Caribbean operated under the command of admirals such as Don Miguel de Espinosa, who was styled "the Admiral of the Navies of the Catholic King."[^2]

The Crown's authority extended over Spanish treasure fleets, colonial settlements, and naval operations throughout the Caribbean. Buccaneers who preyed upon Spanish shipping were considered enemies of the Crown and subject to severe punishment if captured.[^3]

## Conflict with Captain Blood

Captain Blood's career as a buccaneer brought him into repeated conflict with the Spanish Crown. His attacks on Spanish ships and settlements caused "troubles to the subjects of the Catholic King," provoking diplomatic complaints lodged at the English court.[^4]

The Supreme Council of Castile formally condemned Blood, and the Spanish Crown demanded that its navy bring him to justice. Don Miguel de Espinosa, as Admiral of the Navies of the Catholic King, took personal responsibility for pursuing Blood, vowing to God that the pirates would be brought to account.[^5]

## Diplomatic Significance

The Spanish Crown's complaints against English buccaneers created diplomatic tension between Spain and England during a period of uneasy peace. The Crown's protests to the Court of St. James and the Escurial placed pressure on the English government to restrain its nationals, though with limited practical effect in the lawless waters of the Caribbean.[^6]
