## Overview

James Nuttall is a shipwright residing in Bridgetown, Barbados, who becomes entangled in [[peter-blood|Peter Blood]]'s escape plot from the island. A debtor by circumstance, Nuttall is recruited by [[dr-whacker|Dr. Whacker]] to procure a boat for Blood and his fellow convicts. Though well-intentioned, Nuttall proves to be a nervous and hapless conspirator whose panic nearly jeopardizes the entire enterprise.

## In the Novel

Nuttall first appears in Chapter VI ("Plans of Escape"), where Dr. Whacker recommends him to Peter Blood as a potential ally: "There's a fellow Nuttall, now, who follows the trade of a shipwright, whom I happen to know would welcome such a chance as you might afford him" ([006-370]). Blood meets with Nuttall and finds him "as disposed to the business as Dr. Whacker had predicted" ([006-395]).

Over the following three weeks, Nuttall works closely with Blood, meeting him daily to arrange the escape. He locates a serviceable wherry for sale at twenty-two pounds, and secretly stocks a shed with provisions: bread, cheese, water, Canary wine, a compass, quadrant, chart, and carpenter's tools ([006-410]). Nuttall also conceals the stores needed for the voyage, demonstrating his practical skills as a shipwright.

On the day planned for the escape, Nuttall departs to purchase the boat and bring it to the wharf. However, the plan unravels when Colonel Bishop detains Blood at Government House to attend Governor Steed's gout. The next morning, a bureaucratic official arrives at Nuttall's "miserable hovel" demanding a ten-pound surety for the boat purchase ([006-510]). Nuttall, who "was a thin, pale, small-featured, man with weak eyes that now blinked desperately" ([006-505]), is thrown into a state of "livid panic" ([006-498]). He had not anticipated the surety requirement and lacks the funds to pay it.

In Chapter VII, Nuttall frantically searches for Blood to resolve the crisis. He blunders through Bridgetown and the plantation fields, lying to the overseer Kent about his presence and eventually finding Blood working in the sugarcane fields. His panicked demeanor and indiscreet behavior — "Nuttall had no sense" ([007-140]) — further endangers the already precarious plot.

Blood ultimately secures the ten pounds from [[arabella-bishop|Arabella Bishop]], resolving the immediate crisis. Nuttall's role in the narrative, though brief, illustrates the network of debtors and small tradesmen in Barbados whose desperation made them willing accomplices to Blood's designs.

## Relationships

- **[[peter-blood|Peter Blood]]** — Nuttall's primary contact in the escape plot. Blood supplies the money and direction, while Nuttall provides the maritime expertise and local knowledge. Blood regards Nuttall with a mixture of reliance and exasperation at his timidity.
- **[[dr-whacker|Dr. Whacker]]** — The physician who first recommends Nuttall to Blood, knowing the shipwright's circumstances and willingness to escape.
- **[[jeremy-pitt|Jeremy Pitt]]** — Blood instructs Nuttall to find Pitt if Blood himself cannot be reached, making Pitt the secondary point of contact for the conspiracy.
