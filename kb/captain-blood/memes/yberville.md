# Yberville

**Yberville** is a young French buccaneer captain who joins [[peter-blood|Captain Blood]]'s fleet during the Maracaybo campaign and becomes one of his most steadfast and capable officers. Though still young, Yberville has already earned a reputation as an experienced sea-wolf, and his loyalty to Blood proves unwavering through the shifting fortunes of the buccaneer enterprise.

## Service Under Blood

### Joining the Fleet

Yberville is introduced as a sea-wolf of considerable experience despite his youth [016-128]. His reputation precedes him among the buccaneer captains gathered for the Maracaybo expedition. When the council debates whether to accept Blood's audacious plan, Yberville's voice carries weight.

> A sea-wolf named Yberville, who, though still young, had already earned a name for himself. [016-128]

At the crucial moment when the captains waver between following Blood or [[levasseur|Levasseur]], it is Yberville who speaks in manifest scorn of those who would break faith [016-188]. His intervention helps solidify the coalition behind Blood's leadership.

### The Battle of Maracaybo

During the naval engagement at [[maracaybo|Maracaybo]], Yberville proves his tactical acumen. When the Spanish fleet attempts to break through the buccaneer blockade, Yberville orders prompt action that prevents a coordinated enemy assault [017-043, 017-061]. His experienced judgment in the heat of battle distinguishes him among Blood's captains.

> The prompt action of the experienced Yberville in ordering her [017-061]

Yet even Yberville's skill cannot prevent the chaos that ensues when the Spanish fire-ship bears down on the buccaneer line. The *Arabella* is nearly lost despite all that Yberville can do [017-236]. In the aftermath, Blood calls together his council of officers, and Yberville takes his place alongside [[hagthorpe|Hagthorpe]], [[wolverstone|Wolverstone]], and [[jeremy-pitt|Pitt]] [017-312].

### Loyalty Through Crisis

Yberville's loyalty to Blood is tested when the buccaneers debate whether to abandon their captain. While others — [[dyke|Dyke]], Ogle, and even Pitt — waver in their allegiance, Yberville stands firm [017-328]. His support, along with that of Hagthorpe and Wolverstone, proves decisive in keeping the crew united.

When Blood returns to the fleet after his capture and escape, Yberville is among the first to greet him. Alongside Hagthorpe and Christian, Yberville is on the jetty to welcome Blood back [025-082]. Later, during the discussion of terms for Blood's proposed pardon, Yberville participates in the debate over the buccaneers' future [025-352].

## Command of the Lachesis

In the final phase of Blood's buccaneering career, Yberville commands the *Lachesis*, one of the ships in Blood's squadron [026-018]. He stands alongside Wolverstone at a critical moment when Blood's authority is challenged [026-013], a silent but formidable presence that reinforces Blood's position.

> Captain Yberville of the Lachesis. [026-018]

When Blood proposes the raid on [[cartagena|Cartagena]], Yberville, who has remained silent through much of the council, finally speaks his approval [027-071]. His endorsement carries weight with the other captains, who have learned to respect his judgment.

## Character Assessment

Yberville exemplifies the younger generation of buccaneer captains — men who combine practical seamanship with a code of loyalty that transcends mere self-interest. Where [[cahusac|Cahusac]] represents the older, more mercenary breed of buccaneer, Yberville embodies the disciplined professionalism that Blood seeks to cultivate in his followers.

His youth, mentioned several times in the narrative, is offset by experience that belies his years. Yberville speaks rarely in council but acts decisively in action, earning him the trust of both his captain and his peers [028-263]. In the final accounting of Blood's buccaneer fleet, Yberville and Wolverstone stand as the two most capable and loyal of Blood's subordinate commanders.
