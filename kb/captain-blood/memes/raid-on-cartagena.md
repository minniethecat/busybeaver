# Raid on Cartagena

## Overview

The Raid on Cartagena was a joint Franco-buccaneer expedition launched in 1689 against the wealthy Spanish colonial city of Cartagena ([026-300]). Under the command of Baron de Rivarol, and with Captain Blood's buccaneer squadron providing naval support, the expedition succeeded in capturing the city after a brief siege and extracted an enormous ransom of forty million livres in plunder ([026-325], [027-010]).

## In the Novel

### The Decision

Following their victory over Don Miguel de Espinosa's Spanish squadron, Blood's buccaneer fleet rendezvoused with M. de Rivarol's French force. Rivarol proposed an attack on Cartagena, describing it as a target rich with gold — a proposition that appealed to the buccaneers' appetite for plunder ([026-363]). Blood, aware of the city's formidable defences, counselled caution but ultimately agreed to participate in the expedition as naval support.

### The Siege

The allied fleet arrived off Cartagena in April 1689 ([027-010]). M. de Rivarol summoned the city to surrender, but the Spanish governor refused ([027-026]). Despite Blood's limited knowledge of the city's landward defences — derived only from hearsay — the buccaneers were tasked with the shore assault while the French ships blockaded the harbour ([027-044]).

### The Capture and Aftermath

The city fell after a determined resistance. The spoils — gold, silver, and merchandise valued at forty million livres — were loaded aboard Rivarol's ships. The distribution of this treasure became a point of contention: the French commander attempted to withhold the buccaneers' agreed share, setting the stage for the climactic confrontation at Port Royal.

## Key Participants

- **M. de Rivarol** — French commander who led the expedition
- **Captain Blood** — Provided buccaneer naval support and tactical counsel
- **The Spanish Governor of Cartagena** — Refused the initial summons to surrender

## Significance

The raid enriched the French crown and rivarol's personal fortune while simultaneously creating the grievance that would lead Blood to confront Rivarol at Port Royal — the action that secured Blood's redemption and elevation to the governorship of Jamaica.
