## Overview

The **Santo Nino** was a Spanish vessel that fell into the hands of Captain Peter Blood's buccaneer fleet. She was intercepted while en route to join Blood's forces at La Guayra, and her crew chose surrender over combat ([018-121]).

## In the Novel

The Santo Nino was originally bound to join Blood's fleet from La Guayra ([016-229]). Communications had been sent ahead on the Santo Nino to reach Blood at La Guayra, indicating the ship served as a dispatch vessel before its capture ([017-130]).

Blood's fleet eventually "came upon the belated Santo Nino, speeding under full sail" ([018-116]). The encounter was decisive: rather than engage the buccaneer squadron, "the Santo Nino chose the better part of valour" and surrendered without a fight ([018-121]). The decision was prudent — facing Blood's superior force, resistance would have been futile.

Following the capture, Cahusac, one of Blood's captains, was involved with the Santo Nino and "had narrowly escaped" some unspecified danger in connection with the vessel ([018-147]).

## Captain & Crew

The Santo Nino's original Spanish crew surrendered the vessel to Blood's buccaneers. After capture, the ship was operated by a prize crew under Blood's overall command.

## Fate

The Santo Nino's ultimate fate is not detailed in the novel. Like other captured Spanish prizes, she was likely either incorporated into Blood's fleet or disposed of as circumstances dictated.
