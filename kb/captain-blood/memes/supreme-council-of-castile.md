# Supreme Council of Castile

## In the Novel

The Supreme Council of Castile appears in *Captain Blood* as the Spanish governing authority that condemns Peter Blood and his crew. After Blood's escape from Spanish captivity, the Council formally declares him a pirate rather than a prisoner of war.

> "Supreme Council of the Catholic King that Peter Blood had got away from the island of Santa Maria del Carmen" ([018-105])

The Council's condemnation carries real weight — it authorizes the Spanish Admiral Don Miguel de Espinosa to pursue Blood with deadly force. The Council's authority extends across the Spanish Caribbean, making its judgment against Blood a threat that follows him throughout the later chapters. The Supreme Council of Castile "might anon condemn" Blood and his men "in his turn" ([018-194]), suggesting the Council's reach and its role in the imperial justice system.
