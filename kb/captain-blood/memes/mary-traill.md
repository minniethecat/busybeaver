## Overview

Mary Traill is a young woman who serves as Arabella Bishop's companion in *Captain Blood*. She appears in several key dramatic scenes, including Arabella's night visit to warn Peter Blood and the Spanish raid on Bridgetown. Described as having "childlike" qualities, Mary Traill provides a foil to Arabella's more forceful personality and serves as a witness to critical events in the novel.

## In the Novel

### The Warning Visit

Mary Traill accompanies Arabella Bishop on her clandestine night visit to Colonel Bishop's plantation, where Arabella warns Peter Blood of the impending Spanish attack on Bridgetown. When they arrive, it is Mary Traill who calls out to announce their presence: "Arabella! It is I, Mary Traill" ([009-048]). After delivering the warning, Blood "lifted the slight weight of Mary Traill to her horse" as the women depart ([009-096]). The last Blood hears of them that night is "Mary Traill's childlike voice calling back" a final farewell ([009-101]).

### Witness to the Spanish Raid

During the Spanish raid on Bridgetown, Mary Traill is present and later described by Arabella as having "fled in horror" before the attacking Spanish forces ([012-062]). Her terror underscores the violence and chaos of the raid.

### Aftermath

In the aftermath of the raid, Arabella references Mary Traill as a witness who can corroborate the events: "Mary Traill would tell you of it" ([023-167]). This establishes Mary Traill as a reliable observer whose account can verify Arabella's testimony about Blood's role in defending Bridgetown.

## Relationships

- **Arabella Bishop** — Mary Traill's close companion and patron. Mary follows Arabella through the dangerous events surrounding Blood's escape and the Spanish raid
- **Peter Blood** — interacts briefly with Mary Traill during the warning visit, lifting her onto her horse with characteristic gallantry
