[009-001] What he saw was fetching him in haste and white-faced out of that hell

[009-002] again, when in a narrow street a girl hurtled into him, wild-eyed, her

[009-003] unbound hair streaming behind her as she ran. After her, laughing and

[009-004] cursing in a breath, came a heavy-booted Spaniard. Almost he was upon

[009-005] her, when suddenly Mr. Blood got in his way. The doctor had taken a

[009-006] sword from a dead man's side some little time before and armed himself

[009-007] with it against an emergency.

[009-009] livid gleam of that sword which Mr. Blood had quickly unsheathed.

[009-010] “Ah, perro ingles!” he shouted, and flung forward to his death.

[009-011] “It's hoping I am ye're in a fit state to meet your Maker,” said Mr.

[009-012] Blood, and ran him through the body. He did the thing skilfully: with

[009-013] the combined skill of swordsman and surgeon. The man sank in a hideous

[009-014] heap without so much as a groan.

[009-016] wall. He caught her by the wrist.

[009-017] “Come!” he said.

[009-018] But she hung back, resisting him by her weight. “Who are you?” she

[009-019] demanded wildly.

[009-022] that Spanish ruffian. “Come,” he urged again. And this time, reassured

[009-023] perhaps by his clear English speech, she went without further questions.
