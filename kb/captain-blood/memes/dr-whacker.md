# Dr. Whacker

## Overview

Dr. Whacker is a physician in Barbados who attempts to recruit Peter Blood into an escape plot. His approach to Blood reveals the complex social dynamics of the plantation colony: Whacker appeals to Blood's professional identity as a physician while pursuing his own self-interest. He is a minor but significant character — one of several figures who test Blood's judgment about whom to trust.

## In the Novel

### The Approach

Whacker approaches Blood under the guise of professional sympathy: "This slavery in which you find yourself must be singularly irksome to a man of parts such as yourself" ((006-209)). His flattery is calculated — he recognizes Blood's value and seeks to recruit him as an asset.

### The Manipulation

Whacker's manipulation involves painting a picture of what Blood could achieve if he could "escape from this hell of slavery" ((006-222)) and practice medicine as a free man. "The world is large. There are many nations besides England where a man of your parts would be warmly welcomed" ((006-224)). The appeal to professional ambition is effective — but Blood sees through the manipulation.

### The Revelation

Blood's sharp intelligence pierces Whacker's schemes. He recognizes that Whacker and his colleague "desired to be rid of one who threatened" their position — that Blood's medical skill makes him a rival rather than merely a recruit. Blood's ability to read Whacker's true motives demonstrates his superior judgment.
