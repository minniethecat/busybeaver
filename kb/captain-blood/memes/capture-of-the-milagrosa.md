# Capture of the Milagrosa

## Overview

The Capture of the Milagrosa was a naval engagement in September 1688 in which Captain Blood's frigate *Arabella* defeated and captured the Spanish galleon *Milagrosa*, flagship of Don Miguel de Espinosa y Valdez, the Spanish Admiral of the West Indies ([018-222]). The action was notable for Blood's tactical brilliance in overcoming a superior vessel through rapid boarding rather than conventional gunnery.

## In the Novel

### The Encounter

Sailing in the waters off Hispaniola, the *Milagrosa* — a great Spanish galleon carrying the vindictive Don Miguel de Espinosa — sighted Blood's ships ([018-225]). Don Miguel, still smarting from his earlier humiliation at Blood's hands, eagerly gave chase. An unlucky shot from the *Milagrosa* struck among powder stores aboard one of Blood's consorts, causing damage ([018-467]).

### The Battle

Blood maneuvered the *Arabella* into position abeam of the *Milagrosa*, closing to half a cable's length ([019-144]). As the Spanish gunners raised their weapons, Blood gave the order to board ([019-154]). The buccaneer crew swarmed across, sweeping the *Milagrosa* from stem to stern with devastating speed ([019-187]). The Spanish crew, caught unprepared for the ferocity of the assault, were quickly overcome.

### Aftermath

Don Miguel de Espinosa was taken prisoner. The *Milagrosa*, renamed and incorporated into Blood's growing fleet, became one of his most valuable prizes. The victory cemented Blood's reputation as the most formidable buccaneer captain in the Caribbean.

## Key Participants

- **Captain Blood** — Commander of the *Arabella*, led the boarding action
- **Don Miguel de Espinosa** — Spanish Admiral aboard the *Milagrosa*, taken prisoner
- **The crew of the Arabella** — Buccaneers who executed the boarding

## Significance

The capture demonstrated Blood's preferred tactical doctrine: close rapidly, board decisively, and overwhelm the enemy before conventional naval tactics could be brought to bear. The *Milagrosa* herself became both a symbol of Blood's prowess and a material addition to his fleet.
