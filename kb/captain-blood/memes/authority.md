# Authority

## Overview

Authority in *[[peter-blood|Captain Blood]]* is a contested concept. Who has the right to command? The novel presents a spectrum of authority figures — judges, governors, admirals, ship captains — and evaluates each not by their title but by their conduct. Legitimate authority derives from competence and character, not from royal [[letters-of-marque|commission]]. Blood, who begins the novel stripped of all legal authority, ends it as the supreme authority in [[jamaica|Jamaica]] — not because [[crown|the Crown]] changed, but because authority naturally flows to those who deserve it.

## Historical Background

The late 17th century saw a crisis of authority in the English-speaking world. The "Glorious Revolution" of 1688 deposed a legitimate monarch and replaced him with another, raising profound questions about the source of political authority. In the colonies, authority was even more contested: [[governor|colonial governor]]s, [[admiral|naval commander]]s, and local assemblies all claimed overlapping jurisdictions. The Caribbean, with its mix of English, French, Spanish, and Dutch claims, was a crucible of conflicting authorities.

## In the Novel

### Corrupted Authority

[[judge-jeffreys|Judge Jeffreys]] embodies authority corrupted beyond recognition. His "authority" is merely the power to destroy — a "brutally spiteful" exercise of will dressed in judicial robes ((004-008)). [[colonel-bishop|Colonel Bishop]]'s authority over slaves in [[barbados|Barbados]] is similarly abusive: "the stockade which the [[plantation-system|plantation]] slaves inhabited" ((005-138)) is maintained by force alone. These figures demonstrate that legal authority without moral legitimacy is tyranny.

### Shipboard Authority

At sea, authority is more direct and more democratic. Blood's authority as captain derives from demonstrated competence and shared purpose — his crew follows him because he wins battles and divides spoils fairly. When his authority is challenged (as in the [[treason|mutiny]] reference at (023-213)), the challenge is personal and political. Shipboard authority prefigures the more legitimate form Blood will later exercise as governor.

### Restored Authority

Blood's final role as [[governor|Governor]] completes the arc: from rebel to governor, from the condemned to the condemner. The novel suggests that authority, properly understood, is not about rank but about responsibility — and that those who have suffered under unjust authority are best equipped to exercise it justly.
