## Overview

The **San Felipe** was a Spanish ship taken as a prize by Captain Peter Blood's buccaneer fleet. She was captured together with the smaller *Infanta*, and though she offered little resistance, she sustained significant damage that left her barely seaworthy ([017-086]).

## In the Novel

The San Felipe and the Infanta were captured in the same action, both being "smaller vessels" compared to the other ships in the engagement ([016-278]). During the attack, the buccaneer forces subdued the San Felipe with practiced efficiency — one group of boarders "was doing the like by the San Felipe" while another handled the Infanta ([017-019]).

Neither Spanish vessel mounted a serious defence. In Blood's assessment, "neither the San Felipe nor the Infanta offered much resistance" ([017-024]). The ease of capture belied the damage sustained: the San Felipe was "almost as sorely damaged" as the Infanta, kept "afloat by artifice" rather than sound seamanship ([017-086]).

## Captain & Crew

The vessel's original Spanish crew surrendered without significant combat. After capture, the San Felipe was crewed by buccaneer prize crews under Blood's command.

## Fate

The San Felipe's damaged state — "kept afloat by artifice" ([017-086]) — suggests she was in poor condition following her capture. Her ultimate disposition is not specified in the novel, but her severe damage likely limited her usefulness to Blood's fleet.
