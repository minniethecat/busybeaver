# Last Fight of the Arabella

The **Last Fight of the Arabella** was the climactic naval engagement in which Captain Peter Blood's flagship, the Arabella, confronted the Spanish fleet commanded by Admiral Don Miguel de Espinosa. The battle represented the culmination of Blood's long and bitter conflict with the Spanish naval forces that had pursued him across the Caribbean since his escape from Barbados.[^1]

## Prelude

The Spanish Admiral Don Miguel de Espinosa had long sworn to bring Blood to justice. As the Lord Admiral of Spain's Caribbean fleet, Don Miguel commanded substantial naval resources and enjoyed the full backing of the Spanish Crown. His flagship, the Milagrosa, was a powerful vessel — "the flagship of Don Miguel de Espinosa, the Lord Admiral of Spain's Caribbean fleet."[^2]

The Arabella, formerly the Spanish frigate Cinco Llagas and renamed after Arabella Bishop, had been Blood's flagship throughout his buccaneering career. The irony that Blood's most famous ship had been stolen from Spain was not lost on Don Miguel, whose pursuit of Blood carried a personal dimension beyond professional duty.[^3]

> "Don Miguel is my brother," the Spanish admiral's nephew had said, "and Don Miguel de Espinosa, the Admiral of Spain, through the mouth of Don Miguel, did not spare her threats."[^4]

## The Battle

The engagement was fought against overwhelming odds. The Arabella, already damaged from previous actions, faced the concentrated force of the Spanish fleet under Don Miguel. Despite the desperate circumstances, Blood's conduct of the battle was remarkable:

> "He conducted the engagement with a skill of which he might justly be proud."[^5]

Blood's tactical prowess — honed through years of service under de Ruyter in European waters and refined through countless Caribbean engagements — was on full display. The battle demonstrated the naval expertise that had made Blood the most formidable buccaneer captain in Caribbean waters.[^5]

The Arabella, penned in by "her implacable enemy, Don Miguel de Espinosa," fought with the desperation of a crew that knew no quarter would be given. The Spanish admiral had made it clear that "Spain, through the mouth of Don Miguel, did not spare her threats" against Blood and his followers.[^6]

## Aftermath

The outcome of the Last Fight of the Arabella marked the end of an era in Blood's buccaneer career. Whether the Arabella survived or succumbed to the Spanish fleet, the battle represented the final chapter in Blood's long struggle against the Spanish naval forces that had pursued him from Barbados to Maracaybo and back again.[^7]

The engagement cemented Blood's reputation as a commander of extraordinary skill and courage. Even his enemies acknowledged his abilities: Don Miguel himself, after the battle, is said to have "opened his arms to his nephew" in a gesture that mixed respect with frustration at his inability to defeat the elusive buccaneer.[^8]
