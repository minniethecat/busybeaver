## Overview

Andrew Baynes is a yeoman who is arrested by [[captain-hobart|Captain Hobart]]'s dragoons at Oglethorpe's Farm alongside [[peter-blood|Peter Blood]], [[jeremy-pitt|Jeremy Pitt]], and the wounded [[lord-gildoy|Lord Gildoy]]. Charged with harbouring rebels, Baynes is tried at the Bloody Assizes before [[judge-jeffreys|Judge Jeffreys]] and transported to Barbados as a convict. He later becomes one of the men who escape with Blood and serve aboard his ship.

## In the Novel

Baynes first appears in Chapter III ("The Lord Chief Justice") during the raid on Oglethorpe's Farm. When Captain Hobart's dragoons discover that the farm has sheltered the wounded rebel Lord Gildoy, Baynes is among those seized. Hobart orders his men: "And make fast that fellow also," pointing to Baynes, adding, "We'll show him what it means to harbour and comfort rebels" ([003-115]). Baynes struggles against the troopers who seize him, protesting vehemently ([003-117]).

Thrust into the courtyard with Pitt and Blood, Baynes is bound to a trooper's stirrup leather for the march to Bridgewater. As the dragoons sack the farm, the sound of a woman's screams causes Baynes to check in his stride — "his face ashen" — and he is dragged by the stirrup rope before the trooper strikes him with the flat of a sword ([003-178]).

At the Bloody Assizes, Baynes is formally identified as "Andrew Baynes" when called to plead ([003-346]). Unlike Pitt, who confesses his guilt, Baynes pleads not guilty. Blood later attempts to call Baynes as a witness in his defence: "There is also Master Baynes here, who can answer to it" ([003-476]), but Jeffreys dismisses the offer with contempt, remarking that Baynes "will have to answer for himself" and will be "greatly exercised to save his own neck from a halter" ([003-477]).

Following his conviction, Baynes is sentenced to transportation and shipped to Barbados as a rebel convict, sold into plantation slavery. [[jeremy-pitt|Jeremy Pitt]]'s page records him as a "fellow escapee and crew member," indicating that Baynes was among the small group of men who successfully escaped Barbados with Blood and subsequently served as part of his buccaneer crew.

## Relationships

- **[[peter-blood|Peter Blood]]** — Fellow prisoner and later captain. Blood attempts to use Baynes as a defence witness at trial.
- **[[jeremy-pitt|Jeremy Pitt]]** — Fellow arrestee at Oglethorpe's Farm and later shipmate. Pitt and Baynes are tried together and transported together.
- **[[captain-hobart|Captain Hobart]]** — The dragoon officer who arrests Baynes at the farm.
- **[[judge-jeffreys|Judge Jeffreys]]** — The Lord Chief Justice who presides over Baynes's trial and dismisses him as a credible witness.
