## Overview

Gibraltar is a Spanish colonial town on the eastern shore of Lake Maracaibo in present-day Venezuela. In *Captain Blood*, it is the target of a major buccaneer raid led by Captain Blood in partnership with the French pirate Levasseur. The town was known for its tobacco trade with the Sacerdotes islands ([016-291]).

## In the Novel

After capturing Maracaybo, Captain Blood proposed extending the raid to Gibraltar, a larger and wealthier town further inland on Lake Maracaibo ([016-105]). Blood's plan involved taking the town by surprise, but the overland march took three days, giving the inhabitants time to hear of the approaching buccaneers and prepare defenses or flee ([016-151]).

Upon arrival, the buccaneers found that much of the population had evacuated with their valuables. Nevertheless, the expedition yielded significant plunder — two sloops were captured at Gibraltar ([016-283]) and a vessel assigned to collect ransom payments was stationed there ([016-303]). The raid on Gibraltar demonstrated Blood's strategic ambition, extending beyond coastal targets into inland settlements. Prisoners taken at Gibraltar included prominent Spanish citizens who were subsequently transported to Maracaybo ([017-149]).

Blood's lieutenant, Wolverstone, expressed characteristic enthusiasm for the enterprise, noting that they would "make him pay big ransom for Gibraltar" ([016-106]).
