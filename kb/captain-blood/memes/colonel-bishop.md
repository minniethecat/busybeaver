# Colonel Bishop

## Overview

Colonel William Bishop is the Deputy Governor of Barbados and the novel's primary human antagonist. A wealthy sugar planter and commander of the Barbados Militia, Bishop purchases Peter Blood at the slave auction and becomes the instrument of Blood's degradation. His implacable hatred for Blood — driven partly by professional jealousy and partly by Blood's romantic threat regarding Arabella — makes him the personal face of the unjust system that condemned the Monmouth rebels.

## In the Novel

### The Slave Auction

Bishop first appears at the auction of the transported rebels, his "beady eyes" appraising the human merchandise with cold calculation. He recognizes Blood's medical training as an asset — "likely to be of much value in the plantation" ((004-178)) — and purchases him accordingly. Bishop embodies the economic logic of slavery: human beings are assets to be acquired and exploited.

### The Barbados Tyrant

As Deputy Governor, Bishop rules his plantation with authority backed by "the Barbados Militia" ((004-139)). His treatment of Blood mixes grudging respect for his medical skill with the casual cruelty of a man who has never been challenged. When Blood escapes, Bishop's fury is that of a property owner whose investment has absconded.

### The Pursuer

Bishop's pursuit of Blood extends across the Caribbean. He appears at inconvenient moments — during diplomatic missions, naval engagements — always representing the law that Blood has rejected. His authority derives from the same Stuart regime that condemned Blood, making their conflict personal and political simultaneously.

## Relationships

- **Arabella Bishop** — His niece, whom he attempts to control and whose growing sympathy for Blood enrages him
- **Peter Blood** — His slave, then his nemesis; Bishop's hatred of Blood defines his character
- **Lord Julian Wade** — His superior as Governor, with whom he clashes over Blood's fate

## Quotations

> "A slave is a slave, and has no power to shape his fate." ((005-085))
