# Council of War

## In the Novel

The Council of War is the deliberative body Peter Blood convenes aboard his flagship, the *Arabella*, to make strategic decisions with his senior officers. Rather than ruling as an autocratic pirate captain, Blood brings major questions of strategy before this council for collective deliberation.

> "Here that council of war, composed of six men in all, deliberated until late into the night" ([016-259])

The council represents Blood's preference for reasoned debate and collective decision-making over the despotic command style of other pirate captains. Its members include his most trusted lieutenants — Pitt, Hagthorpe, Wolverstone, Yberville, and Dyke. The council debates major operations such as whether to attack the Spanish or negotiate with colonial authorities, and its deliberations reflect Blood's dual nature as both a military commander and a reluctant outlaw seeking legitimacy.
