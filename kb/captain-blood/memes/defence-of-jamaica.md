# Defence of Jamaica

## Overview

The Defence of Jamaica was Captain Blood's decisive naval action to protect Port Royal from the French fleet of M. de Rivarol, who had arrived to bombard the undefended colony. Fighting against overwhelming odds, Blood's tactical brilliance — delaying engagement until the Port Royal fort had damaged the French ships, then delivering a final boarding assault — resulted in the capture of Rivarol's flagship and the salvation of Jamaica ([030-004], [030-040], [030-058]).

## In the Novel

### The Threat

Following the raid on Cartagena, M. de Rivarol sailed for Port Royal, intending to capture the colony for France. Colonel Bishop, the Deputy-Governor, had abandoned his post to hunt Blood, leaving Jamaica defenceless. Blood, now carrying a commission from the new King William III through Lord Willoughby, raced to intercept the French fleet ([029-080]).

### The Battle

Blood deliberately held back the *Arabella* and the Dutch squadron while Port Royal's shore batteries engaged the French ships ([030-040]). When Lord Willoughby and Admiral van der Kuylen expressed impatience, Blood explained his strategy: let the fort weaken the enemy before committing his own forces ([030-058]). The tactic proved sound.

Once the French vessels had sustained damage from the fort, Blood closed with Rivarol's flagship, the *Victorieuse*. In a desperate boarding action, Blood's buccaneer crew overwhelmed the French defenders ([030-216]). The impossible victory was watched in breathless amazement by Lord Willoughby and Admiral van der Kuylen from the *Arabella's* quarter-deck ([030-288], [030-306]).

### The Reward

Following the battle, Lord Willoughby apologized to Blood for the second time that day, declaring he had "never before seen the impossible made possible by resource and valour" ([030-378]). With Colonel Bishop absent and in disgrace, Willoughby appointed Blood as Governor of Jamaica, comparing him favourably to the legendary buccaneer-turned-governor Henry Morgan ([031-066]).

## Key Participants

- **Captain Blood** — Commander of the defending force, architect of the victory
- **Lord Willoughby** — Governor-General, witness to Blood's triumph, appointed him Governor
- **Admiral van der Kuylen** — Dutch Admiral whose squadron supported the defence
- **M. de Rivarol** — Commander of the attacking French fleet
- **Colonel Bishop** — Absent Deputy-Governor whose dereliction enabled the crisis

## Significance

The Defence of Jamaica represents the climax of Blood's redemption arc. By saving the colony from French attack, he transformed from outlaw into hero, earning the governorship that would make him the most powerful man in Jamaica.
