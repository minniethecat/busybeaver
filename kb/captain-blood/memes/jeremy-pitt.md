# Jeremy Pitt

## Overview

Jeremy Pitt is Peter Blood's most trusted companion and his first mate throughout the buccaneering career. A young shipmaster from Somerset, Pitt was condemned alongside Blood after the Monmouth Rebellion and transported to Barbados. His seamanship, loyalty, and quiet competence make him indispensable to Blood's operations. Pitt represents the capable common man — skilled, brave, and unswervingly faithful — who forms the backbone of Blood's crew.

## In the Novel

### Condemned Together

Pitt is introduced alongside Blood at the trial and transportation. A "young Somersetshire shipmaster" ((005-003)), his nautical skills are as valuable to Blood as Blood's medical skills are to him. The two form a partnership of mutual respect that survives slavery, piracy, and war.

### The Loyal First Mate

Throughout Blood's career, Pitt serves as first mate — navigating ships, commanding boarding parties, and maintaining discipline among the crew. His loyalty is absolute; when Blood's authority is challenged, Pitt is the first to enforce it. In a world where pirate crews routinely mutinied against their captains, Pitt's steadfast loyalty is Blood's greatest asset.

### The Professional Seaman

Pitt's competence as a seaman complements Blood's strategic genius. Where Blood plans campaigns and negotiates with enemies, Pitt executes — sailing the ship, managing the crew, and fighting in the rigging. Their partnership models the ideal relationship between commander and subordinate: mutual respect, complementary skills, and shared purpose.
