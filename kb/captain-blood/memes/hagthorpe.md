# Hagthorpe

## Overview

Nicholas (Nathaniel) Hagthorpe is one of Peter Blood's most trusted crew members, a gentleman by birth who had served in the Royal Navy before being enslaved in Barbados. Among Blood's inner circle, Hagthorpe stands out for his calm demeanour, diplomatic instincts, and unwavering loyalty — qualities that make him Blood's natural lieutenant in moments of crisis. His "sometime commission in the King's Navy" ((009-168)) signals that he brings professional naval experience to Blood's company, complementing Blood's own military background.

## In the Novel

### A Gentleman Among Pirates

Hagthorpe is introduced as "a gentleman who had served in the late King's Navy" ((006-329)), immediately distinguishing him from the common seamen in Blood's company. His bearing and education make him a natural officer: when tension rises, "Hagthorpe lounged forward" with the easy confidence of a man who has seen worse ((010-017)). He is "a fairly tall, athletic man with a pleasant, humorous face" ((010-017)), suggesting a temperament that balances the rougher edges of men like Wolverstone.

### The Loyal Lieutenant

When Blood faces a near-mutinous crew, Hagthorpe alone "shrugged and smiled" — a gesture of unshakeable confidence in his captain ((010-037)). This moment defines their relationship: Hagthorpe follows Blood not from fear or greed, but from genuine respect. He is consistently among those who step forward when Blood needs volunteers, joining Blood, Wolverstone, and Dyke as one of the core leaders who "elected to support him" at critical junctures ((011-143)).

### The Diplomatic Prisoner

Hagthorpe's finest hour comes during the captivity aboard the Cinco Llagas. When Don Esteban strikes him across the face, Hagthorpe's measured response — "I am not concerned to insult" ((012-309)) — demonstrates a self-possession that Blood himself might envy. Even under physical assault, Hagthorpe maintains the composure of a gentleman, "nursing his cheek" while continuing the negotiation ((012-314)). His firm declaration that "We do not break faith" ((012-301)) articulates the moral code that distinguishes Blood's company from common pirates.

### The Fighting Seaman

Despite his gentlemanly manner, Hagthorpe is a capable fighter. His "experience of seamanship" and his past as a "fighting man" in the Navy ((010-062)) make him valuable in action. When the escape from Barbados is planned, "Hagthorpe, Dyke, and Ogle had agreed to take the foremost risks" ((006-414)), placing themselves in the most dangerous position to ensure the plan's success.

## Character Analysis

Hagthorpe represents the ideal of the gentleman-adventurer — a man who has fallen from his proper station through political misfortune (his Royal Navy commission suggests Cavalier sympathies) but who retains the bearing and principles of his class. Unlike Wolverstone, who is pure fighting instinct, or Pitt, who is passionate devotion, Hagthorpe brings measured judgment. He is Blood's diplomatic arm, the man who can face down Spanish admirals with the same equanimity he shows to mutinous buccaneers.

## Relationships

- **Peter Blood** — His captain; Hagthorpe's loyalty is based on respect and shared values, not fear
- **Wolverstone**, **Dyke**, **Ogle** — Fellow members of Blood's inner circle; with Wolverstone and Dyke, "elected to support" Blood at critical moments ((011-143))
- **Jeremy Pitt** — Fellow crew member and first mate
- **Don Esteban** — Spanish commander who struck Hagthorpe during the Cinco Llagas captivity ((012-309))
