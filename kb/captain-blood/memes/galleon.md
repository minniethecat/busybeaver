# Galleon

## Overview

The Spanish galleon is the great antagonist-vessel of *[[peter-blood|Captain Blood]]* — the formidable warship that Blood must outfight and outthink to build his [[honour|reputation]]. These "mighty galleon(s) of forty-eight great guns" (016-276) dominate the Caribbean seascape, serving both as treasure transports and as warships of the Spanish [[crown|Crown]]. Blood's career is measured by the galleons he captures: the *[[cinco-llagas|Cinco Llagas]]*, the *[[milagrosa|Milagrosa]]*, and the ships of the Spanish [[treasure|treasure fleet]]. Each captured galleon not only enriches his fleet but demonstrates his tactical genius — the ability to defeat, with smaller and fewer ships, the most [[authority|power]]ful vessels afloat.

## Historical Background

Spanish galleons were the backbone of the Carrera de Indias — the trade route linking Spain to its American colonies. Typically displacing 500–1,200 tons and carrying 40–60 guns, galleons combined cargo capacity with formidable armament. The annual [[treasure|treasure fleet]] (*flota*) was composed primarily of galleons carrying silver from Potosí, gold from New Granada, and goods from Manila. English, French, and Dutch [[privateering|privateer]]s and pirates devoted enormous resources to intercepting these galleons, whose capture could make a [[authority|command]]er's fortune — and whose resistance could end his career. The galleon's high castles fore and aft made it a stable gun platform but a poor sailer compared to the smaller, faster vessels favored by [[buccaneer|buccaneers]].

## In the Novel

### First Encounters

Blood's career as a buccaneer begins with "a Spanish galleon" encountered "at the outset" (013-134), an engagement that results in the capture of the vessel that becomes the *[[cinco-llagas|Cinco Llagas]]*. A "boarding party from the Spanish galleon that had invaded the English" ship provides Blood's first opportunity to demonstrate his tactical skill (005-363). From these early encounters, Blood learns the galleon's weaknesses — its slow maneuverability, its vulnerability to boarding from smaller, quicker vessels, and the predictable tactics of its Spanish [[authority|command]]ers.

### The Milagrosa

The *[[milagrosa|Milagrosa]]*, "a mighty galleon of forty-eight great guns" (016-276), represents the pinnacle of Spanish naval [[authority|power]] in the novel. Its capture by Blood is his greatest single feat of [[navigation|seamanship]] — accomplished not through brute force but through cunning, using fireships to break the Spanish formation and isolate the great vessel. The *[[milagrosa|Milagrosa]]* later becomes Blood's flagship, a "great Spanish galleon" now serving under English colors (018-222), symbolizing the transfer of naval [[authority|power]] from Spain to the [[buccaneer|buccaneers]].

### The Fleet Engagement

The novel's major fleet action involves "four galleons" (018-109) under [[don-miguel|Don Miguel de Espinosa]] confronting Blood's [[fleet|squadron]]. The galleons, despite their superior fire[[authority|power]], are outmaneuvered by Blood's lighter vessels. "Aboard the rudely awakened galleon all was confused hurrying" (016-354) as Blood's attack catches the Spanish unprepared. The engagement demonstrates that the age of the galleon as the unchallenged master of Caribbean waters is ending — resourceful [[authority|command]]ers with smaller ships can defeat the great vessels of the Spanish [[crown|Crown]].
