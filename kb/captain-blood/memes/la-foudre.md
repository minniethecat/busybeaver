## Overview

**La Foudre** (French for "The Thunderbolt") was a vessel in Captain Peter Blood's buccaneer fleet during his Caribbean privateering campaign. Originally a French ship, it was captured and renamed by Blood, who gave it its flamboyant French name ([014-079]). The ship served alongside the *Arabella* as one of Blood's principal vessels before being lost near La Guayra.

## In the Novel

La Foudre first appears when Blood captures it from the French. With characteristic flair, Blood bestowed upon it the name *La Foudre* — "The Thunderbolt" — and integrated it into his growing fleet ([014-079]).

The ship operated in concert with the *Arabella*, Blood's flagship. On one occasion, both ships were observed beating out to sea together within the hour ([014-165]). La Foudre proved an effective hunter: at dawn on one pursuit, she was found "close on the Dutchman's heels, not a mile astern" ([014-184]), demonstrating her speed as a corsair vessel.

In combat, La Foudre engaged directly with enemy ships. During one action, she grappled an opposing vessel, her grapnels securing the enemy while buccaneers prepared to board ([014-196]). The ship sustained damage during these engagements — her shrouds were damaged by whistling wind during one chase ([014-192]) — and required periodic careening and repair ([015-021]).

## Captain & Crew

La Foudre was commanded by **Cahusac**, a French buccaneer who served as one of Blood's trusted captains ([016-057]). Cahusac operated La Foudre with a crew of buccaneers, sharing in the fleet's prize money — at one point receiving a share of three-fifths of a captured haul ([015-271]).

## Fate

La Foudre was ultimately lost through grounding. Blood later confronted Cahusac about running the ship aground on the coast near La Guayra ([016-147]). The loss was compounded when a Spanish *guarda-costa* captured the stranded vessel, removing it permanently from Blood's fleet ([016-156]).
