# Jamaica

## Overview

Jamaica appears in *Captain Blood* as the ultimate destination of Blood's journey — from condemned rebel to colonial governor. Mentioned early as one of "His Majesty's southern plantations" ((004-064)) to which rebels are transported, Jamaica represents the endpoint of the arc: the place where justice is finally restored and Blood receives the authority that was stolen from him.

## In the Novel

Jamaica is referenced as part of the plantation complex that receives transported rebels, alongside Barbados and the Leeward Islands. Its significance grows at the novel's conclusion, when Blood is appointed Governor of Jamaica under King William III — a restoration of honour that transforms the colony from a place of punishment into a seat of legitimate authority.

## Significance

- Destination of transported rebels
- Site of Blood's ultimate restoration as Governor
