# Cahusac

**Cahusac** is a Breton buccaneer who serves as one of [[peter-blood|Captain Blood]]'s lieutenants aboard the captured Spanish ship *Cinco Llagas*. A burly, callous man, Cahusac frequently challenges Blood's decisions, particularly regarding the disposition of prisoners and the division of prizes, embodying the more pragmatic and self-interested wing of Blood's buccaneer crew.

## Service Under Blood

Cahusac first appears as Blood's lieutenant during the capture of the Spanish galleon [014-141]. His physical presence is imposing — a broad-faced Breton of burly build and callous disposition. From the outset, he proves himself a capable officer but one whose priorities lean toward immediate material gain rather than the long-term discipline Blood seeks to instil [014-144].

> A broad face of his lieutenant Cahusac, a burly, callous [014-141]

When dealing with the spoils of captured ships, Cahusac takes charge with efficiency born of experience [014-242]. Yet his thoroughness in inspecting the prize is matched by an ill-humour when the results disappoint him [014-249]. He is a man who values tangible reward above abstract principle.

### Tensions with Blood

Cahusac's relationship with Blood is marked by recurring tension. He repeatedly attempts to press Blood toward courses of action that serve immediate buccaneer interests [014-176], and his pessimism often runs counter to Blood's more strategic vision [014-359].

> But the pessimism of Cahusac abated nothing. [014-359]

During the confrontation with the Spanish prisoners, Cahusac watches carefully, tripping up a prisoner who attempts to rush Blood [014-239]. He binds the captives' wrists as a precaution [014-264], demonstrating a practical ruthlessness that contrasts with Blood's occasional shows of mercy.

## Campaigns

### The Spanish Encounter

When a Spanish ship appears on the horizon, Cahusac brings the news to Blood with characteristic grimness [014-327, 014-334]. He urges decisive action, demanding to know what Blood intends [014-344]. When Blood's response strikes him as counsels of despair, Cahusac is openly contemptuous, spitting on the deck to mark his displeasure [014-346].

> "Counsels of despair." Cahusac was contemptuous. [014-346]

Yet ultimately, he follows Blood's orders. When Blood goes ashore in a boat, Cahusac accompanies him with two other men [014-374].

### The Maracaybo Episode

At the council of buccaneer captains off [[maracaybo|Maracaybo]], Cahusac represents Blood's interests, though not without friction [015-038]. He shrugs at the political manoeuvring between the French and English captains [015-125], and when Blood proposes the attack on Maracaybo, Cahusac's grunt of approval comes with a characteristic caveat about the terms of engagement [015-227].

> "Good!" grunted Cahusac. "On that understanding all are agreed." [015-227]

During the confrontation with [[levasseur|Levasseur]], Cahusac adds his voice in support of Blood [015-233], recognizing — however grudgingly — that Blood's leadership serves the common interest. Later, when Blood demands the return of Levasseur's stolen pearls, Cahusac clutches them possessively before surrendering them [015-264, 015-284].

Cahusac dives after the pearls when they scatter [015-293]. He watches Levasseur's death with cynical detachment, considering the twitching body before his eyes shift to Blood [015-317, 015-319].

## Command of La Foudre

After the buccaneers capture the Spanish frigate *La Foudre*, Cahusac assumes command of her [016-057]. This promotion does not ease the tension between him and Blood. When Blood's strategic decisions run counter to Cahusac's instincts, the Breton's uneasiness erupts into open conflict [016-069, 016-075].

[[wolverstone|Wolverstone]] laughs at Cahusac's outbursts, but Cahusac is not daunted by mockery [016-115, 016-121]. When Blood himself confronts him with wry amusement, the Breton's fury only deepens [016-139]. He insists that Don Miguel, the Spanish prisoner, must be compelled to speak [016-183], his face purple with rage when Blood refuses to use harsh measures [016-196, 016-205].

> Cahusac glowered at him in silence. [016-205]

## Character Assessment

Cahusac represents the practical, often ruthless face of seventeenth-century buccaneering. Where Blood tempers force with intelligence and occasional mercy, Cahusac embodies the simpler calculus of profit and power. His Breton stubbornness makes him a difficult subordinate but an effective enforcer of buccaneer discipline when Blood's authority is challenged by force rather than words.

His relationship with Blood is fundamentally one of uneasy respect: Cahusac recognizes Blood's superior intelligence and leadership, but he cannot reconcile those qualities with his own instinct toward direct, often brutal action. This tension recurs throughout their association, from the capture of the *Cinco Llagas* to the final encounters before Blood's pardon.
