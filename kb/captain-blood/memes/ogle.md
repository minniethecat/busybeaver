# Ogle

## Overview

Ogle is a former gunner who joins Peter Blood's crew and rises to become one of his most capable commanders. His expertise with naval artillery proves decisive in several engagements, and he is consistently portrayed as resolute and dependable — a man who "continued to give proof of his mettle" even under the most punishing fire. Among Blood's inner circle, Ogle stands out for his laconic professionalism and unshakeable nerve.

## In the Novel

Ogle is introduced as "a man named Ogle" who "had been a gunner" before joining Blood's company ([006-331]). He is among the first to agree to join the escape from Barbados, alongside Hagthorpe and Dyke ([006-414]).

Ogle's gunnery expertise becomes a critical asset when Blood's crew takes to the sea. He is given command of a gun crew and "was making excellent practice with his guns" during naval combat ([009-276]). When the fighting intensifies, "Ogle, however, continued to give proof of his mettle" — a tribute to his steadiness under fire ([009-292]). Blood recognises Ogle's capabilities, placing him in command positions; at one point Blood addresses him directly with the words "Here, Ogle, is work for you" before assigning him a critical gunnery task ([012-031]).

Ogle participates in the inner circle's councils, sitting with Hagthorpe and Wolverstone "in silence" as they discuss their course of action ([011-364]). He appears alongside other senior crew members throughout the campaigns ([017-328]). In the novel's final chapters, Ogle is among those whose "outlawry, like Blood's" remains unresolved ([031-107]).
