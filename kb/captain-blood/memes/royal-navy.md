# Royal Navy

## Overview

The Royal Navy appears in *Captain Blood* as the institutional embodiment of English maritime power — and the force that Blood must alternately evade and serve. Under James II, the Navy hunts Blood as a pirate. Under William III, it becomes the instrument of Blood's rehabilitation, providing the commissions and authority that transform him from outlaw to governor.

## In the Novel

Blood's relationship with the Navy is defined by the political context. Lord Julian Wade arrives in the Caribbean as a representative of Crown naval authority, sent "to put down piracy" ((019-079)). Yet the Navy's legitimacy depends on the monarch it serves — and when the monarch changes, so does Blood's relationship to naval authority.

## Historical Background

The Royal Navy of the 1680s was undergoing significant expansion, becoming the dominant naval force in European waters. The Caribbean station was a key theater, tasked with protecting English commerce and suppressing piracy — often with limited resources and ambiguous instructions.
