[025-001] Weeks passed, and every ship from home brought additional news. William

[025-002] had crossed to England, and in March of that year 1689 they learnt in

[025-003] [[jamaica|Jamaica]] that he had accepted the crown and that James had thrown himself

[025-004] into the arms of France for rehabilitation.

[025-005] To a kinsman of Sunderland's this was disquieting news, indeed. It was

[025-006] followed by letters from King William's Secretary of State informing

[025-007] [[colonel-bishop|Colonel Bishop]] that there was war with France, and that in view of its

[025-008] effect upon the Colonies a Governor-General was coming out to the

[025-009] West Indies in the person of Lord Willoughby, and that with him came a

[025-010] squadron under the [[authority|command]] of Admiral van der Kuylen to reenforce the

[025-011] Jamaica fleet against eventualities.

[025-012] Bishop realized that this must mean the end of his supreme authority,

[025-013] even though he should continue in [[port-royal|Port Royal]] as Deputy-Governor. Lord

[025-014] Julian, in the lack of direct news to himself, did not know what it

[025-015] might mean to him. But he had been very close and confidential with

[025-016] Colonel Bishop regarding his hopes of [[arabella-bishop|Arabella]], and Colonel Bishop more

[025-017] than ever, now that political events put him in danger of being retired,

[025-018] was anxious to enjoy the advantages of having a man of [[lord-julian-wade|Lord Julian]]'s

[025-019] eminence for his relative.

[025-020] They came to a complete understanding in the matter, and Lord Julian

[025-021] disclosed all that he knew.

[025-022] “There is one obstacle in our path,” said he. “[[peter-blood|Captain Blood]]. The girl

[025-023] is in love with him.”

[025-024] “Ye're surely mad!” cried Bishop, when he had recovered speech.

[025-025] “You are justified of the assumption,” said his lordship dolefully. “But

[025-026] I happen to be sane, and to speak with knowledge.”

[025-027] “With knowledge?”

[025-028] “Arabella herself has confessed it to me.”

[025-029] “The brazen baggage! By God, I'll bring her to her senses.” It was the

[025-030] slave-driver speaking, the man who governed with a whip.

[025-031] “Don't be a fool, Bishop.” His lordship's contempt did more than

[025-032] any argument to calm the Colonel. “That's not the way with a girl of

[025-033] Arabella's spirit. Unless you want to wreck my chances for all time,

[025-034] you'll hold your tongue, and not interfere at all.”

[025-035] “Not interfere? My God, what, then?”

[025-036] “Listen, man. She has a constant mind. I don't think you know your

[025-037] niece. As long as Blood lives, she will wait for him.”

[025-038] “Then with Blood dead, perhaps she will come to her silly senses.”

[025-039] “Now you begin to show intelligence,” Lord Julian commended him. “That

[025-040] is the first essential step.”

[025-041] “And here is our chance to take it.” Bishop warmed to a sort of

[025-042] enthusiasm. “This war with France removes all restrictions in the matter

[025-043] of [[tortuga|Tortuga]]. We are free to invest it in the service of the Crown. A

[025-044] victory there and we establish ourselves in the favour of this new

[025-045] government.”

[025-046] “Ah!” said Lord Julian, and he pulled thoughtfully at his lip.

[025-047] “I see that you understand,” Bishop laughed coarsely. “Two birds with

[025-048] one stone, eh? We'll hunt this rascal in his lair, right under the

[025-049] beard of the King of France, and we'll take him this time, if we reduce

[025-050] Tortuga to a heap of ashes.”

[025-051] On that expedition they sailed two days later--which would be some three

[025-052] months after Blood's departure--taking every ship of the fleet, and

[025-053] several lesser vessels as auxiliaries. To Arabella and the world in

[025-054] general it was given out that they were going to raid French Hispaniola,

[025-055] which was really the only expedition that could have afforded Colonel

[025-056] Bishop any sort of justification for leaving Jamaica at all at such

[025-057] a time. His sense of duty, indeed, should have kept him fast in

[025-058] Port Royal; but his sense of duty was smothered in hatred--that most

[025-059] fruitless and corruptive of all the emotions. In the great cabin of

[025-060] Vice-Admiral Craufurd's flagship, the Imperator, the Deputy-Governor got

[025-061] drunk that night to celebrate his conviction that the sands of Captain

[025-062] Blood's career were running out.

[025-064] Meanwhile, some three months before Colonel Bishop set out to reduce

[025-065] Tortuga, Captain Blood, bearing hell in his soul, had blown into its

[025-066] rockbound harbour ahead of the winter gales, and two days ahead of the

[025-067] frigate in which [[wolverstone|Wolverstone]] had sailed from Port Royal a day before

[025-068] him.

[025-069] In that snug anchorage he found his fleet awaiting him--the four ships

[025-070] which had been separated in that gale off the Lesser Antilles, and some

[025-071] seven hundred men composing their crews. Because they had been beginning

[025-072] to grow anxious on his behalf, they gave him the greater welcome. Guns

[025-073] were fired in his honour and the ships made themselves gay with bunting.

[025-074] The town, aroused by all this noise in the harbour, emptied itself

[025-075] upon the jetty, and a vast crowd of men and women of all creeds and

[025-076] nationalities collected there to be present at the coming ashore of the

[025-077] great buccaneer.

[025-078] Ashore he went, probably for no other reason than to obey the general

[025-079] expectation. His mood was taciturn; his face grim and sneering. Let

[025-080] Wolverstone arrive, as presently he would, and all this hero-worship

[025-081] would turn to execration.

[025-084] short their greetings, and when they plagued him with questions of where

[025-085] he had tarried, he bade them await the coming of Wolverstone, who would

[025-086] satisfy their curiosity to a surfeit. On that he shook them off, and

[025-095] way alone to the fine house of M. d'Ogeron, there to pay his respects to

[025-096] his friends, the Governor and the Governor's family.

[025-111] have been an explosion. When, however, the Old Wolf cast anchor in the

[025-112] bay two days later, it was to him all turned for the explanation they

[025-113] were about to demand of Blood.

[025-115] one eye than do most men with two; and despite his grizzled head--so

[025-116] picturesquely swathed in a green and scarlet turban--he had the sound

[025-117] heart of a boy, and in that heart much love for Peter Blood.

[025-119] as he sailed round the rocky headland that bore the fort. He rubbed his

[025-120] single eye clear of any deceiving film and looked again. Still he could

[025-121] not believe what it saw. And then a voice at his elbow--the voice of

[025-122] Dyke, who had elected to sail with him--assured him that he was not

[025-123] singular in his bewilderment.

[025-124] “In the name of Heaven, is that the Arabella or is it the ghost of her?”

[025-125] The Old Wolf rolled his single eye over Dyke, and opened his mouth to

[025-126] speak. Then he closed it again without having spoken; closed it tightly.

[025-127] He had a great gift of caution, especially in matters that he did not

[025-128] understand. That this was the Arabella he could no longer doubt. That

[025-129] being so, he must think before he spoke. What the devil should the

[025-130] Arabella be doing here, when he had left her in Jamaica? And was Captain

[025-131] Blood aboard and in command, or had the remainder of her hands made off

[025-132] with her, leaving the Captain in Port Royal?

[025-133] Dyke repeated his question. This time Wolverstone answered him.

[025-134] “Ye've two eyes to see with, and ye ask me, who's only got one, what it

[025-135] is ye see!”

[025-136] “But I see the Arabella.”

[025-137] “Of course, since there she rides. What else was you expecting?”

[025-138] “Expecting?” Dyke stared at him, open-mouthed. “Was you expecting to
