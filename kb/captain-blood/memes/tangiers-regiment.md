# Tangiers Regiment

## In the Novel

The Tangiers Regiment is the British army unit where Peter Blood served prior to his career as a physician and his subsequent adventures in the Caribbean. His military past with this regiment is revealed during his trial for treason.

> "Colonel of the Tangiers Regiment a deadly despatch which might have saved the lives of these poor fellows" ([003-206])

Blood's service at Tangiers is a key part of his backstory — it explains his competence with a sword and his military discipline, traits that distinguish him from the common rebels he is tried alongside. Colonel Kirke, who commands the regiment, is a figure from Blood's past who reappears during the legal proceedings. The regiment's name comes from Tangiers, the North African port that was briefly an English possession (1661–1684), placing Blood's military service in a specific historical context.
