# Taunton

## Overview

Taunton is a town in Somerset, England, synonymous with the Monmouth Rebellion of 1685. In the novel, Taunton appears in two key roles: first as a symbol of the popular enthusiasm for Monmouth's cause, and second as the site of Taunton Castle, where Peter Blood and his companions are tried by Judge Jeffreys during the Bloody Assizes.

## In the Novel

### The Banners of Liberty

Taunton is celebrated for its fervent support of Monmouth. The novel references "the banners of liberty — the banners woven by the virgins of Taunton, the girls from the seminaries of Miss Blake and Mrs. Musgrove" ([000-210]). This image of Taunton's maidens weaving Monmouth's flags exemplifies the romantic idealism that swept the West Country — and that swept up the reluctant Peter Blood.

### Taunton Castle and the Bloody Assizes

After Sedgemoor, "those who were too sorely wounded to march were conveyed in carts, into which they were brutally crowded, their wounds undressed" to Taunton ([000-1294]). It is in "the great hall of Taunton Castle" that Peter Blood steps forward "to take his trial" ([000-1394]). With him stand Jeremy Pitt and the yeoman Baynes — all three facing the brutal justice of the Bloody Assizes.

## Historical Context

Taunton was one of the most pro-Monmouth towns in the West Country. The historical "maids of Taunton" presented Monmouth with a banner and a Bible before his march. After the rebellion, Taunton Castle hosted Judge Jeffreys' Bloody Assizes in September 1685, where hundreds of rebels were sentenced to death or transportation — the sentence that sends Peter Blood to Barbados as a slave.
