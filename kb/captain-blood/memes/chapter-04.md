[004-001] The absence of that dazed jury was a brief one. The verdict found the

[004-002] three prisoners guilty. [[peter-blood|Peter Blood]] looked round the scarlet-hung court.

[004-003] For an instant that foam of white faces seemed to heave before him. Then

[004-004] he was himself again, and a voice was asking him what he had to say

[004-005] for himself, why sentence of death should not be passed upon him, being

[004-006] convicted of [[treason|high treason]].

[004-007] He laughed, and his laugh jarred uncannily upon the deathly stillness

[004-008] of the court. It was all so grotesque, such a [[justice|mockery of justice]]

[004-009] administered by that wistful-eyed jack-pudding in scarlet, who was

[004-010] himself a mockery--the venal instrument of a brutally spiteful and

[004-011] vindictive king. His laughter shocked the austerity of that same

[004-012] jack-pudding.

[004-013] “Do you laugh, sirrah, with the rope about your neck, upon the very

[004-014] threshold of that eternity you are so suddenly to enter into?”

[004-015] And then Blood took his revenge.

[004-016] “Faith, it's in better case I am for mirth than your lordship. For I

[004-017] have this to say before you deliver judgment. Your lordship sees me--an

[004-018] innocent man whose only offence is that I practised charity--with a

[004-019] halter round my neck. Your lordship, being the justiciar, speaks with

[004-020] knowledge of what is to come to me. I, being a physician, may speak with

[004-021] knowledge of what is to come to your lordship. And I tell you that I

[004-022] would not now change places with you--that I would not exchange this

[004-023] halter that you fling about my neck for the stone that you carry in

[004-024] your body. The death to which you may doom me is a light pleasantry by

[004-025] contrast with the death to which your lordship has been doomed by that

[004-026] Great Judge with whose name your lordship makes so free.”

[004-027] The Lord Chief Justice sat stiffly upright, his face ashen, his lips

[004-028] twitching, and whilst you might have counted ten there was no sound in

[004-029] that paralyzed court after Peter Blood had finished speaking. All those

[004-030] who knew Lord Jeffreys regarded this as the lull before the storm, and

[004-031] braced themselves for the explosion. But none came.

[004-032] Slowly, faintly, the colour crept back into that ashen face. The scarlet

[004-033] figure lost its rigidity, and bent forward. His lordship began to speak.

[004-034] In a muted voice and briefly--much more briefly than his wont on such

[004-035] occasions and in a manner entirely mechanical, the manner of a man

[004-036] whose thoughts are elsewhere while his lips are speaking--he delivered

[004-037] sentence of death in the prescribed form, and without the least

[004-038] allusion to what Peter Blood had said. Having delivered it, he sank back

[004-039] exhausted, his eyes half-closed, his brow agleam with sweat.

[004-040] The prisoners filed out.

[004-041] Mr. Pollexfen--a Whig at heart despite the position of Judge-Advocate

[004-042] which he occupied--was overheard by one of the jurors to mutter in the

[004-043] ear of a brother counsel:

[004-044] “On my soul, that swarthy rascal has given his lordship a scare. It's a

[004-045] [[mercy|pity]] he must hang. For a man who can frighten Jeffreys should go far.”

[004-046] CHAPTER IV. HUMAN MERCHANDISE

[004-047] Mr. Pollexfen was at one and the same time right and wrong--a condition

[004-048] much more common than is generally supposed.

[004-049] He was right in his indifferently expressed thought that a man whose

[004-050] mien and words could daunt such a lord of terror as Jeffreys, should by

[004-051] the dominance of his nature be able to fashion himself a considerable

[004-052] destiny. He was wrong--though justifiably so--in his assumption that

[004-053] Peter Blood must hang.

[004-054] I have said that the tribulations with which he was visited as a result

[004-055] of his errand of mercy to Oglethorpe's Farm contained--although as yet

[004-056] he did not perceive it, perhaps--two sources of thankfulness: one that

[004-057] he was tried at all; the other that his trial took place on the 19th

[004-058] of September. Until the 18th, the sentences passed by the court of the

[004-059] Lords Commissioners had been carried out literally and expeditiously.

[004-060] But on the morning of the 19th there arrived at Taunton a courier from

[004-061] Lord Sunderland, the Secretary of State, with a letter for Lord Jeffreys

[004-062] wherein he was informed that His Majesty had been graciously pleased

[004-063] to [[authority|command]] that eleven hundred rebels should be furnished for

[004-064] transportation to some of His Majesty's southern plantations, [[jamaica|Jamaica]],

[004-065] [[barbados|Barbados]], or any of the Leeward Islands.

[004-066] You are not to suppose that this command was dictated by any sense of

[004-067] mercy. Lord Churchill was no more than just when he spoke of the King's

[004-068] heart as being as insensible as marble. It had been realized that in

[004-069] these wholesale hangings there was taking place a reckless waste of

[004-070] valuable material. Slaves were urgently required in the plantations,

[004-071] and a healthy, vigorous man could be reckoned worth at least from ten

[004-072] to fifteen pounds. Then, there were at court many gentlemen who had some

[004-073] claim or other upon His Majesty's bounty. Here was a cheap and ready way

[004-074] to discharge these claims. From amongst the convicted rebels a certain

[004-075] number might be set aside to be bestowed upon those gentlemen, so that

[004-076] they might dispose of them to their own profit.

[004-077] My Lord Sunderland's letter gives precise details of the royal

[004-078] munificence in human flesh. A thousand prisoners were to be distributed

[004-079] among some eight courtiers and others, whilst a postscriptum to his

[004-080] lordship's letter asked for a further hundred to be held at the disposal

[004-081] of the Queen. These prisoners were to be transported at once to His

[004-082] Majesty's southern plantations, and to be kept there for the space of

[004-083] ten years before being restored to liberty, the parties to whom they

[004-084] were assigned entering into security to see that transportation was

[004-085] immediately effected.

[004-086] We know from Lord Jeffreys's secretary how the Chief Justice inveighed

[004-087] that night in drunken frenzy against this misplaced clemency to which

[004-088] His Majesty had been persuaded. We know how he attempted by letter to

[004-089] induce the King to reconsider his decision. But James adhered to it. It

[004-090] was--apart from the indirect profit he derived from it--a clemency

[004-091] full worthy of him. He knew that to spare lives in this fashion was to

[004-092] convert them into living deaths. Many must succumb in torment to the

[004-093] horrors of West Indian slavery, and so be the envy of their surviving

[004-094] companions.

[004-095] Thus it happened that Peter Blood, and with him [[jeremy-pitt|Jeremy Pitt]] and Andrew

[004-096] Baynes, instead of being hanged, drawn, and quartered as their sentences

[004-097] directed, were conveyed to Bristol and there shipped with some fifty

[004-098] others aboard the Jamaica Merchant. From close [[prison|confinement]] under

[004-099] hatches, ill-nourishment and foul water, a sickness broke out amongst

[004-100] them, of which eleven died. Amongst these was the unfortunate yeoman

[004-101] from Oglethorpe's Farm, brutally torn from his quiet homestead amid

[004-102] the fragrant cider orchards for no other sin but that he had practised

[004-103] mercy.

[004-104] The mortality might have been higher than it was but for Peter Blood.

[004-105] At first the master of the Jamaica Merchant had answered with oaths and

[004-106] threats the doctor's expostulations against permitting men to perish

[004-107] in this fashion, and his insistence that he should be made free of the

[004-108] medicine chest and given leave to minister to the sick. But presently

[004-109] Captain Gardner came to see that he might be brought to task for

[004-110] these too heavy losses of human merchandise and because of this he was

[004-111] belatedly glad to avail himself of the skill of Peter Blood. The doctor

[004-112] went to work zealously and zestfully, and wrought so ably that, by his

[004-113] ministrations and by improving the condition of his fellow-captives, he

[004-114] checked the spread of the disease.

[004-115] Towards the middle of December the Jamaica Merchant dropped anchor in

[004-116] Carlisle Bay, and put ashore the forty-two surviving rebels-convict.

[004-117] If these unfortunates had imagined--as many of them appear to have

[004-118] done--that they were coming into some wild, savage country, the

[004-119] prospect, of which they had a glimpse before they were hustled over

[004-120] the ship's side into the waiting boats, was enough to correct the

[004-121] impression. They beheld a town of sufficiently imposing proportions

[004-122] composed of houses built upon European notions of architecture, but

[004-123] without any of the huddle usual in European cities. The spire of a

[004-124] church rose dominantly above the red roofs, a fort guarded the entrance

[004-125] of the wide harbour, with guns thrusting their muzzles between the

[004-126] crenels, and the wide facade of Government House revealed itself

[004-127] dominantly placed on a gentle hill above the town. This hill was vividly

[004-128] green as is an English hill in April, and the day was such a day as

[004-129] April gives to England, the season of heavy rains being newly ended.

[004-130] On a wide cobbled space on the sea front they found a guard of

[004-131] red-coated militia drawn up to receive them, and a crowd--attracted by

[004-132] their arrival--which in dress and manner differed little from a crowd in

[004-133] a seaport at home save that it contained fewer women and a great number

[004-134] of negroes.

[004-135] To inspect them, drawn up there on the mole, came Governor Steed,

[004-136] a short, stout, red-faced gentleman, in blue taffetas burdened by a

[004-137] prodigious amount of gold lace, who limped a little and leaned heavily

[004-138] upon a stout ebony cane. After him, in the uniform of a colonel of the

[004-139] [[barbados-militia|Barbados Militia]], rolled a tall, corpulent man who towered head and

[004-140] shoulders above the Governor, with malevolence plainly written on his

[004-141] enormous yellowish countenance. At his side, and contrasting oddly with

[004-142] his grossness, moving with an easy stripling grace, came a slight young

[004-143] lady in a modish riding-gown. The broad brim of a grey hat with scarlet

[004-144] sweep of ostrich plume shaded an oval face upon which the climate of

[004-145] the Tropic of Cancer had made no impression, so delicately fair was its

[004-146] complexion. Ringlets of red-brown hair hung to her shoulders. Frankness

[004-147] looked out from her hazel eyes which were set wide; commiseration

[004-148] repressed now the mischievousness that normally inhabited her fresh

[004-149] young mouth.

[004-150] Peter Blood caught himself staring in a sort of amazement at that

[004-151] piquant face, which seemed here so out of place, and finding his stare

[004-152] returned, he shifted uncomfortably. He grew conscious of the sorry

[004-153] figure that he cut. Unwashed, with rank and matted hair and a

[004-154] disfiguring black beard upon his face, and the erstwhile splendid suit

[004-155] of black camlet in which he had been taken prisoner now reduced to rags

[004-156] that would have disgraced a scarecrow, he was in no case for inspection

[004-157] by such dainty eyes as these. Nevertheless, they continued to inspect

[004-158] him with round-eyed, almost childlike wonder and pity. Their owner put

[004-159] forth a hand to touch the scarlet sleeve of her companion, whereupon

[004-160] with an ill-tempered grunt the man swung his great bulk round so that he

[004-161] directly confronted her.

[004-162] Looking up into his face, she was speaking to him earnestly, but the

[004-163] Colonel plainly gave her no more than the half of his attention. His

[004-164] little beady eyes, closely flanking a fleshly, pendulous nose, had

[004-165] passed from her and were fixed upon fair-haired, sturdy young Pitt, who

[004-166] was standing beside Blood.

[004-167] The Governor had also come to a halt, and for a moment now that little

[004-168] group of three stood in conversation. What the lady said, Peter could

[004-169] not hear at all, for she lowered her voice; [[colonel-bishop|the Colonel]]'s reached him

[004-170] in a confused rumble, but the Governor was neither considerate nor

[004-171] indistinct; he had a high-pitched voice which carried far, and believing

[004-172] himself witty, he desired to be heard by all.

[004-173] “But, my dear Colonel Bishop, it is for you to take first choice from

[004-174] this dainty nosegay, and at your own price. After that we'll send the

[004-175] rest to auction.”

[004-176] Colonel Bishop nodded his acknowledgment. He raised his voice in

[004-177] answering. “Your excellency is very good. But, faith, they're a weedy

[004-178] lot, not likely to be of much value in the [[slavery|plantation]].” His beady eyes

[004-179] scanned them again, and his contempt of them deepened the malevolence of

[004-180] his face. It was as if he were annoyed with them for being in no better

[004-181] condition. Then he beckoned forward Captain Gardner, the master of the

[004-182] Jamaica Merchant, and for some minutes stood in talk with him over a

[004-183] list which the latter produced at his request.
