## Overview

Petit Goave is a French colonial port on the southern coast of Hispaniola (modern-day Haiti) that serves as the designated rendezvous point for the buccaneer fleet in the later chapters of *Captain Blood*. It is here that Captain Blood anchors his ships and meets M. le Baron de Rivarol, the French commander who has been commissioned to lead a joint French-buccaneer expedition against the Spanish stronghold of Cartagena.

## In the Novel

### The Rendezvous Point

Under Governor d'Ogeron's arrangement with the French Crown, Petit Goave is designated as the gathering place for buccaneer captains who have agreed to join the French fleet. The articles drawn up between the buccaneers and the French authorities specify that "the buccaneers were to be at Petit Goave by the end of January," when Baron de Rivarol had announced he might be expected ([025-360]).

### Blood's Indifference

Blood arrives at Petit Goave in a dark, sardonic mood. Having received Arabella Bishop's letter and believing she has married Lord Julian, he is entirely indifferent to his fate. When Hagthorpe ventures a remonstrance about the terms of the French agreement, Blood makes it clear "whether they went to Petit Goave or to Hades, and whether they entered the service of Louis XIV or of Satan" mattered little to him ([025-373]).

Blood comes to his moorings "in the bay of Petit Goave" still in that same dark humour, where he greets Baron de Rivarol when the French fleet of five men-of-war drops anchor alongside the buccaneer ships in the middle of February ([026-004]).

### The Castle Interview

The key meeting between Blood and Rivarol takes place at the Castle of Petit Goave. Summoned to attend the Baron, Blood presents himself before the French commander—"a tall, hawk-faced man of forty, very cold and distant of manner"—who measures Captain Blood "with an eye of obvious disapproval" ([026-010]). Despite the friction between the proud buccaneer captain and the aristocratic French officer, the alliance is settled, and the combined fleet prepares to sail for Cartagena.

## Historical Context

Petit-Goâve was a French colonial settlement on the southern coast of Saint-Domingue (modern Haiti), established in the mid-17th century. During the period of the novel, it served as an administrative centre for the French colony and a frequent port of call for buccaneers operating under French letters of marque. The town's strategic position made it a natural staging point for naval expeditions against Spanish territories in the Caribbean, including the 1697 raid on Cartagena led by French forces with buccaneer support—the historical event that loosely parallels the novel's climactic French-buccaneer expedition.
