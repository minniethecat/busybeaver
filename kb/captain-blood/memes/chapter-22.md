[022-001] She left him, and thereafter with [[wolverstone|Wolverstone]], leaning upon the rail,

[022-002] he watched the approach of that boat, manned by a dozen sailors, and

[022-003] commanded by a scarlet figure seated stiffly in the stern sheets. He

[022-004] levelled his telescope upon that figure.

[022-005] “It'll not be Bishop himself,” said Wolverstone, between question and

[022-006] assertion.

[022-007] “No.” Blood closed his telescope. “I don't know who it is.”

[022-008] “Ha!” Wolverstone vented an ejaculation of sneering mirth. “For all

[022-009] his eagerness, Bishop'd be none so willing to come, hisself. He's been

[022-010] aboard this hulk afore, and we made him swim for it that time. He'll

[022-011] have his memories. So he sends a deputy.”

[022-012] This deputy proved to be an officer named Calverley, a vigorous,

[022-013] self-sufficient fellow, comparatively fresh from England, whose manner

[022-014] made it clear that he came fully instructed by [[colonel-bishop|Colonel Bishop]] upon the

[022-015] matter of how to handle the pirates.

[022-016] His air, as he stepped into the waist of [[arabella-ship|the Arabella]], was haughty,

[022-017] truculent, and disdainful.

[022-018] Blood, the King's commission now in his pocket, and [[lord-julian-wade|Lord Julian]] standing

[022-019] beside him, waited to receive him, and Captain Calverley was a little

[022-020] taken aback at finding himself confronted by two men so very different

[022-021] outwardly from anything that he had expected. But he lost none of his

[022-022] haughty poise, and scarcely deigned a glance at the swarm of fierce,

[022-023] half-naked fellows lounging in a semicircle to form a background.

[022-024] “Good-day to you, sir,” Blood hailed him pleasantly. “I have the honour

[022-025] to give you welcome aboard the [[arabella-bishop|Arabella]]. My name is Blood--Captain

[022-026] Blood, at your service. You may have heard of me.”

[022-027] Captain Calverley stared hard. The airy manner of this redoubtable

[022-028] buccaneer was hardly what he had looked for in a desperate fellow,

[022-029] compelled to ignominious surrender. A thin, sour smile broke on the

[022-030] officer's haughty lips.

[022-031] “You'll ruffle it to the gallows, no doubt,” he said contemptuously.

[022-032] “I suppose that is after the fashion of your kind. Meanwhile it's your

[022-033] surrender I require, my man, not your impudence.”

[022-034] [[peter-blood|Captain Blood]] appeared surprised, pained. He turned in appeal to Lord

[022-035] Julian.

[022-036] “D'ye hear that now? And did ye ever hear the like? But what did I tell

[022-037] ye? Ye see, the young gentleman's under a misapprehension entirely.

[022-038] Perhaps it'll save broken bones if your lordship explains just who and

[022-039] what I am.”

[022-040] Lord Julian advanced a step and bowed perfunctorily and rather

[022-041] disdainfully to that very disdainful but now dumbfounded officer. [[jeremy-pitt|Pitt]],

[022-042] who watched the scene from the quarter-deck rail, tells us that his

[022-043] lordship was as grave as a parson at a [[execution|hanging]]. But I suspect this

[022-044] gravity for a mask under which Lord Julian was secretly amused.

[022-045] “I have the honour to inform you, sir,” he said stiffly, “that Captain

[022-046] Blood holds a commission in the King's service under the seal of my Lord

[022-047] Sunderland, His Majesty's Secretary of State.”

[022-048] Captain Calverley's face empurpled; his eyes bulged. The buccaneers in

[022-049] the background chuckled and crowed and swore among themselves in their

[022-050] relish of this comedy. For a long moment Calverley stared in silence

[022-051] at his lordship, observing the costly elegance of his dress, his air of

[022-052] calm assurance, and his cold, fastidious speech, all of which savoured

[022-053] distinctly of the great world to which he belonged.

[022-054] “And who the devil may you be?” he exploded at last.

[022-055] Colder still and more distant than ever grew his lordship's voice.

[022-056] “You're not very civil, sir, as I have already noticed. My name is

[022-057] Wade--Lord Julian Wade. I am His Majesty's envoy to these barbarous

[022-058] parts, and my Lord Sunderland's near kinsman. Colonel Bishop has been

[022-059] notified of my coming.”

[022-060] The sudden change in Calverley's manner at Lord Julian's mention of his

[022-061] name showed that the notification had been received, and that he had

[022-062] knowledge of it.

[022-063] “I... I believe that he has,” said Calverley, between doubt and

[022-064] suspicion. “That is: that he has been notified of the coming of Lord

[022-065] Julian Wade. But... but... aboard this ship...?” The officer made a

[022-066] gesture of helplessness, and, surrendering to his bewilderment, fell

[022-067] abruptly silent.

[022-068] “I was coming out on the Royal Mary....”

[022-069] “That is what we were advised.”

[022-070] “But the Royal Mary fell a victim to a Spanish privateer, and I might

[022-071] never have arrived at all but for the gallantry of Captain Blood, who

[022-072] rescued me.”

[022-073] Light broke upon the darkness of Calverley's mind. “I see. I

[022-074] understand.”

[022-075] “I will take leave to doubt it.” His lordship's tone abated nothing of

[022-076] its asperity. “But that can wait. If Captain Blood will show you

[022-077] his commission, perhaps that will set all doubts at rest, and we may

[022-078] proceed. I shall be glad to reach [[port-royal|Port Royal]].”

[022-079] Captain Blood thrust a parchment under Calverley's bulging eyes. The

[022-080] officer scanned it, particularly the seals and signature. He stepped

[022-081] back, a baffled, impotent man. He bowed helplessly.

[022-082] “I must return to Colonel Bishop for my orders,” he informed them.

[022-083] At that moment a lane was opened in the ranks of the men, and through

[022-084] this came Miss Bishop followed by her octoroon woman. Over his shoulder

[022-085] Captain Blood observed her approach.

[022-086] “Perhaps, since Colonel Bishop is with you, you will convey his niece

[022-087] to him. Miss Bishop was aboard the Royal Mary also, and I rescued her

[022-088] together with his lordship. She will be able to acquaint her uncle with

[022-089] the details of that and of the present state of affairs.”

[022-090] Swept thus from surprise to surprise, Captain Calverley could do no more

[022-091] than bow again.

[022-092] “As for me,” said Lord Julian, with intent to make Miss Bishop's

[022-093] departure free from all interference on the part of the buccaneers,

[022-094] “I shall remain aboard the Arabella until we reach Port Royal. My

[022-095] compliments to Colonel Bishop. Say that I look forward to making his

[022-096] acquaintance there.”

[022-097] CHAPTER XXII. HOSTILITIES

[022-098] In the great harbour of Port Royal, spacious enough to have given

[022-099] moorings to all the ships of all the navies of the world, the Arabella

[022-100] rode at anchor. Almost she had the air of a prisoner, for a quarter of a

[022-101] mile ahead, to starboard, rose the lofty, massive single round tower

[022-102] of the fort, whilst a couple of cables'-length astern, and to larboard,

[022-103] rode the six men-of-war that composed the [[jamaica|Jamaica]] squadron.

[022-104] Abeam with the Arabella, across the harbour, were the flat-fronted white

[022-105] buildings of that imposing city that came down to the very water's edge.

[022-106] Behind these the red roofs rose like terraces, marking the gentle slope

[022-107] upon which the city was built, dominated here by a turret, there by a

[022-108] spire, and behind these again a range of green hills with for ultimate

[022-109] background a sky that was like a dome of polished steel.

[022-110] On a cane day-bed that had been set for him on the quarter-deck,

[022-111] sheltered from the dazzling, blistering sunshine by an improvised awning

[022-112] of brown sailcloth, lounged Peter Blood, a calf-bound, well-thumbed copy

[022-113] of Horace's Odes neglected in his hands.

[022-114] From immediately below him came the swish of mops and the gurgle of

[022-115] water in the scuppers, for it was still early morning, and under the

[022-116] directions of Hayton, the bo'sun, the swabbers were at work in the

[022-117] waist and forecastle. Despite the heat and the stagnant air, one of the

[022-118] toilers found breath to croak a ribald [[piracy|buccaneering]] ditty:

[022-119] “For we laid her board and board,

[022-120] And we put her to the sword,

[022-121] And we sank her in the deep blue sea.

[022-122] So It's heigh-ho, and heave-a-ho!

[022-123] Who'll sail for the Main with me?”

[022-124] Blood fetched a sigh, and the ghost of a smile played over his lean,

[022-125] sun-tanned face. Then the black brows came together above the vivid

[022-126] blue eyes, and thought swiftly closed the door upon his immediate

[022-127] surroundings.

[022-128] Things had not sped at all well with him in the past fortnight since his

[022-129] acceptance of the King's commission. There had been trouble with Bishop

[022-130] from the moment of landing. As Blood and Lord Julian had stepped ashore

[022-131] together, they had been met by a man who took no pains to dissemble his

[022-132] chagrin at the turn of events and his determination to change it. He

[022-133] awaited them on the mole, supported by a group of officers.

[022-134] “You are Lord Julian Wade, I understand,” was his truculent greeting.

[022-135] For Blood at the moment he had nothing beyond a malignant glance.

[022-136] Lord Julian bowed. “I take it I have the honour to address Colonel

[022-137] Bishop, Deputy-Governor of Jamaica.” It was almost as if his lordship

[022-138] were giving the Colonel a lesson in deportment. The Colonel accepted it,

[022-139] and belatedly bowed, removing his broad hat. Then he plunged on.

[022-140] “You have granted, I am told, the King's commission to this man.” His

[022-141] very tone betrayed the bitterness of his rancour. “Your motives were

[022-142] no doubt worthy... your gratitude to him for delivering you from the

[022-143] Spaniards. But the thing itself is unthinkable, my lord. The commission

[022-144] must be cancelled.”

[022-145] “I don't think I understand,” said Lord Julian distantly.

[022-146] “To be sure you don't, or you'd never ha' done it. The fellow's bubbled

[022-147] you. Why, he's first a rebel, then an escaped slave, and lastly a bloody

[022-148] pirate. I've been hunting him this year past.”

[022-149] “I assure you, sir, that I was fully informed of all. I do not grant the

[022-150] King's commission lightly.”

[022-151] “Don't you, by God! And what else do you call this? But as His Majesty's

[022-152] Deputy-Governor of Jamaica, I'll take leave to correct your mistake in

[022-153] my own way.”

[022-154] “Ah! And what way may that be?”

[022-155] “There's a gallows waiting for this rascal in Port Royal.”

[022-156] Blood would have intervened at that, but Lord Julian forestalled him.

[022-157] “I see, sir, that you do not yet quite apprehend the circumstances. If

[022-158] it is a mistake to grant Captain Blood a commission, the mistake is not

[022-159] mine. I am acting upon the instructions of my Lord Sunderland; and with

[022-160] a full knowledge of all the facts, his lordship expressly designated

[022-161] Captain Blood for this commission if Captain Blood could be persuaded to

[022-162] accept it.”

[022-163] Colonel Bishop's mouth fell open in surprise and dismay.

[022-164] “Lord Sunderland designated him?” he asked, amazed.

[022-165] “Expressly.”

[022-166] His lordship waited a moment for a reply. None coming from the

[022-167] speechless Deputy-Governor, he asked a question: “Would you still

[022-168] venture to describe the matter as a mistake, sir? And dare you take the

[022-169] risk of correcting it?”

[022-170] “I... I had not dreamed....”

[022-171] “I understand, sir. Let me present Captain Blood.”

[022-172] Perforce Bishop must put on the best face he could [[authority|command]]. But that it

[022-173] was no more than a mask for his fury and his venom was plain to all.

[022-174] From that unpromising beginning matters had not improved; rather had

[022-175] they grown worse.

[022-176] Blood's thoughts were upon this and other things as he lounged there on

[022-177] the day-bed. He had been a fortnight in Port Royal, his ship virtually

[022-178] a unit now in the Jamaica squadron. And when the news of it reached

[022-179] [[tortuga|Tortuga]] and the buccaneers who awaited his return, the name of Captain

[022-180] Blood, which had stood so high among the Brethren of the Coast, would

[022-181] become a byword, a thing of execration, and before all was done his life

[022-182] might pay forfeit for what would be accounted a treacherous defection.

[022-183] And for what had he placed himself in this position? For the sake of

[022-184] a girl who avoided him so persistently and intentionally that he must

[022-185] assume that she still regarded him with aversion. He had scarcely been

[022-186] vouchsafed a glimpse of her in all this fortnight, although with that in

[022-187] view for his main object he had daily haunted her uncle's residence, and

[022-188] daily braved the unmasked hostility and baffled rancour in which Colonel

[022-189] Bishop held him. Nor was that the worst of it. He was allowed plainly

[022-190] to perceive that it was the graceful, elegant young trifler from St.

[022-191] James's, Lord Julian Wade, to whom her every moment was devoted. And

[022-192] what chance had he, a desperate adventurer with a record of outlawry,

[022-193] against such a rival as that, a man of parts, moreover, as he was bound

[022-194] to admit?
