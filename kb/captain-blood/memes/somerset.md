## Overview

Somerset is a county in South West England, central to the early chapters of Rafael Sabatini's *Captain Blood*. It is the home of Peter Blood, a former soldier and physician residing in the town of Bridgewater, and the site of key events surrounding the Monmouth Rebellion of 1685. The county's orchards, cider, and rural landscape provide a pastoral backdrop that contrasts sharply with Blood's later adventures in the Caribbean ([001-067]).

## In the Novel

Peter Blood, an Irish-born physician, settled in Bridgewater, Somerset, where he practiced medicine and lived quietly before being drawn into the Monmouth Rebellion ([001-039]). The county's volunteers, including men from Bridgewater and Taunton, "yielded generously" to the Duke of Monmouth's cause ([001-054]). After the rebellion's defeat at Sedgemoor, Somerset became the scene of the brutal Bloody Assizes, and Blood himself was arrested and condemned.

Blood's identity as a "Somersetshire shipmaster" is noted by his Spanish captors, who recognize his competence at sea despite his medical profession ([005-003]). Throughout his Caribbean adventures, Blood's Somerset origins are frequently referenced — his crewmate calls him a "young Somerset mariner" ([028-290]), and even in moments of nostalgia, Blood dreams of returning to "Somerset and cider" after his privateering days are over ([031-063]).

## Historical Context

In the late 17th century, Somerset was an agricultural county known for its apple orchards and cider production ([031-046]). The county played a significant role in the Monmouth Rebellion of 1685 — the Duke of Monmouth landed at Lyme Regis and gathered support across Somerset before his forces were defeated at the Battle of Sedgemoor on 6 July 1685. The subsequent Bloody Assizes, presided over by Judge Jeffreys, saw hundreds of Somerset men executed or transported to the Caribbean.
