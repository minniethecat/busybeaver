## Summary

The [[letter]] episode of Chapter X centres on [[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald Cromarty]]'s receipt of a business [[letter]] from [[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]] informing him that Castleknowe Farm would be let to a tenant named Shearer — contrary to [[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald]]'s express wishes. The baronet responded with a sharp [[letter]] of his own, and [[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]]'s reply promised a personal call the following morning. That morning visit became the occasion of the procurator fiscal's presence at [[keldale-house|Keldale House]] on the day of [[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald]]'s murder.

## Context

The morning began innocuously at [[keldale-house|Keldale House]] with [[cicely-farmond|Cicely Farmond]] reading a local newspaper and asking about the recently deceased [[george-rattar|George Rattar]], twin brother of the procurator fiscal [[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]]. [[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald]] recounted the history of the Rattar brothers: George had been Simon's law partner, had swindled multiple people (including [[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald]] himself), and had later been imprisoned for forgery — with Simon giving evidence against him "without mercy" (010-018).

The conversation shifted when [[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald]] noticed an advertisement in the same paper: [[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]] was offering a reward of one pound for the return of a lost signet ring. [[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald]] mocked the meagre sum: "If he had offered a penny more, I should have taken my business out of his hands! A pound is exactly his figure; a respectable sum, but not extravagant" (010-029). Cicely nearly revealed she had seen [[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]] without his ring recently, but caught herself and changed the subject (010-032, 010-034).

## Sequence

The morning post brought a [[letter]] from [[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]] that immediately darkened [[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald]]'s mood:

> It took the form of a business letter from Mr. Simon Rattar, a letter on the surface perfectly innocuous and formally polite (010-039).

The [[letter]]'s content was a notification that Castleknowe Farm, one of the Cromarty estate properties under [[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]]'s legal stewardship, would be let to a tenant named Shearer — against [[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald]]'s explicit instructions:

> "Simon Rattar has the impudence to tell me he is letting the farm of Castleknowe to that fellow Shearer after all!" (010-042)

The baronet's anger escalated rapidly. When [[lady-cromarty|Lady Cromarty]] asked what Rattar had thought of [[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald]]'s previous objection, her husband replied with mounting fury: "He gave no opinion whatever. He simply sat like a stuck pig" (010-046). [[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald]] declared his intention to respond forcefully: "I shall send Mr. Simon a [[letter]] that will make him sit up!" (010-048).

Throughout breakfast, [[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald]] glanced repeatedly at Rattar's [[letter]], "and each time his brows contracted" (010-051). [[lady-cromarty|Lady Cromarty]] counselled restraint — "Don't be too hasty with [[simon-rattar|Mr. Rattar]], Reggie!" (010-052) — and asked to review the reply before it was sent. [[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald]] appeared to agree, "but when she next saw him the [[letter]] had gone; and from the baronet's somewhat crusty explanation, she suspected that it was a little sharper than he knew she would have approved" (010-057).

[[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]]'s reply came swiftly: "[[simon-rattar|Mr. Rattar]] had sent a brief reply that he was too busy to come out that afternoon, but he would call on [[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald]] in the morning" (010-058).

## Key Participants

- **[[sir-reginald-cromarty]]** — The baronet, whose pride and quick temper made him determined to confront his procurator fiscal over what he saw as professional insubordination.
- **[[simon-rattar]]** — The procurator fiscal (in reality the impostor [[george-rattar|George Rattar]]) whose [[letter]] was "on the surface perfectly innocuous and formally polite" (010-039) but whose professional defiance provoked the baronet's wrath.
- **[[lady-cromarty]]** — The voice of moderation who counselled against hasty retaliation and whose attempts to review the [[letter]] before dispatch were circumvented.

## Outcome / Significance

The [[letter]] episode is significant for three reasons:

1. **Timing and proximity to the murder**: [[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]]'s promised morning visit to discuss the Castleknowe dispute placed him at [[keldale-house|Keldale House]] on the very day [[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald]] was found murdered. The [[letter]] establishes a documented, adversarial motive for the meeting.

2. **Character revelation**: The episode exposes two facets of the Rattar family history in a single chapter — the backstory of [[george-rattar|George Rattar]]'s crimes (swindling, forgery, imprisonment) and [[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]]'s escalating conflict with [[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald]]. The reader is invited to see the procurator fiscal through two lenses simultaneously, without yet knowing they are the same man.

3. **[[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald]]'s fatal flaw**: The baronet's refusal to let [[lady-cromarty|Lady Cromarty]] review his reply, and his dispatch of a [[letter]] sharper than she would have approved, illustrates the impulsive pride that placed him in direct, unmediated conflict with a dangerous man.
