# Procurator Fiscal's Office

The Procurator Fiscal's Office was the principal legal authority in the unnamed Scottish county where the events of the novel unfold. Held by [[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]], the office made him "the setter in motion of all criminal processes, and generalissimo, so to speak, of the police" (002-003). This dual role — combining prosecutorial authority with control over police investigations — placed Rattar at the centre of the criminal justice system, a position he exploited to devastating effect.

## Role and Authority

The Procurator Fiscal in Scotland is a unique legal institution, combining the functions of public prosecutor and director of criminal investigations. When [[the-cromarty-murder|Sir Reginald Cromarty was murdered]], it was the Fiscal's office that took charge:

> "It was in his ain library he was killed! The Fiscal and Superintendent is there now and we've been going into the circumstantial evidence." (011-015)

The office worked in tandem with the [[county-police|County Police]], directing their inquiries and determining what evidence to pursue. [[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]] himself described the office's professional scope to [[ned-cromarty|Ned Cromarty]]:

> "I am the Procurator Fiscal in charge of this case. But I am also lawyer and factor to the Cromarty family, and my father was before me." (017-040)

This dual identity — prosecutor and family lawyer — created the structural conflict of interest that the murderer exploited.

## The Impostor Fiscal

The central tragedy of the investigation was that the Fiscal's office was occupied by an impostor. [[george-rattar|George Rattar]], having murdered and [[the-impersonation|impersonated]] his brother Simon, controlled the very investigation into [[the-cromarty-murder|the murder he himself had committed]]. As [[f-t-carrington|F. T. Carrington]] observed, the official inquiry was compromised by George Rattar's control of the fiscal's office — a circumstance that "fatally" handicapped the pursuit of justice.

This perversion of legal authority is one of the novel's darkest ironies: the man responsible for prosecuting crime was himself the criminal, and the institution designed to protect the public became the instrument of its deception.
