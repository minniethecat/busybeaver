# Cromarty Estate

The **Cromarty Estate** is the landed property of the [[the-cromarty-family|Cromarty family]], centred on [[keldale-house|Keldale House]]. It is a significant holding in the northern county, comprising the mansion, its policies, and associated farmland and properties — including [[castleknowe-farm|Castleknowe Farm]]. The estate passes through the male line of the Cromarty family, with [[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]] serving as its lawyer and factor.

## Management

[[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]] is both "lawyer and factor to the Cromarty family," a role he inherited from his father before him (017-040). As factor, he manages the estate's legal and financial affairs, including the administration of [[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald]]'s will and the distribution of legacies. The "Cromarty estate letter book" (003-056) is a key administrative record that Rattar consults during the investigation:

> "Get me the Cromarty estate letter book," he commanded. (003-056)

## Inheritance structure

The estate's complex inheritance arrangements are central to the novel's plot. As [[simon-rattar|the lawyer]] explained:

> "In the first place, Lady Cromarty is life rented in the mansion and property, less certain sums to be paid to other people." (014-054)

The key provisions included:

- **[[lady-cromarty|Lady Cromarty]]** is "life rented in the mansion and property," meaning she has the right to occupy and receive income from the estate during her lifetime (014-054).
- **[[malcolm-cromarty|Sir Malcolm Cromarty]]** receives GBP 1,000 per year during Lady Cromarty's lifetime and "afterwards falls heir to the whole estate" (014-056).
- Various legacies are payable to servants and others upon Sir Reginald's death, including GBP 300 to [[james-bisset|James Bisset]], the butler.

## Role in the narrative

The estate's inheritance structure creates potential motives for [[the-cromarty-murder|the murder]]. The distribution of legacies and the question of who stood to benefit from the crime lie at the heart of the investigation. [[simon-rattar|Rattar]]'s dual role as both factor to the estate and [[procurator-fiscals-office|Procurator Fiscal]] investigating the murder creates a fundamental conflict of interest that shapes much of the novel's tension.

Rattar's intimate knowledge of the estate's affairs — gained through his position as factor — enables him to manipulate the investigation and protect his own interests as the disguised [[george-rattar|George Rattar]].
