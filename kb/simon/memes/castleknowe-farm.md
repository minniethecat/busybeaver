# Castleknowe Farm

Castleknowe Farm was a tenanted agricultural holding on the [[cromarty-estate|Cromarty estate]]. Its proposed letting to a tenant named Shearer became the subject of a sharp dispute between [[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald Cromarty]] and his factor [[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]], exposing the friction in their professional relationship shortly before the murder.

## The Letting Dispute

The matter of Castleknowe's tenancy arose over breakfast at Keldale House, when Sir Reginald received a business letter from Simon Rattar. The letter, outwardly "perfectly innocuous and formally polite," informed the baronet that the letting to Shearer was proceeding — despite Sir Reginald having countermanded those instructions in a private conversation ten days earlier.

Sir Reginald's reaction was explosive:

> "Simon Rattar has the impudence to tell me he is letting the farm of Castleknowe to that fellow Shearer after all!" [010-042]

Lady Cromarty was puzzled, since she knew her husband had previously intended to let the farm to Shearer. But Sir Reginald explained that he had since learned unfavourable things about the man:

> "I don't believe the man is solvent, in the first place; and in the second place he's a socialistic, quarrelsome, mischievous fellow!" [010-045]

## The Factor's Insolence

What angered Sir Reginald most was not the letting itself but Rattar's evasiveness. The factor had appeared to agree with the baronet's objections during their private conversation, yet now proceeded as if that conversation had never occurred — citing only Sir Reginald's earlier written instructions "of the 8th" and making no reference to the subsequent verbal countermand.

> "Now he tells me that the matter is settled as per my instructions of the 8th. That's weeks ago, and not a word does he say about our conversation cancelling the whole instructions!" [010-046]

The dispute revealed a deeper pattern: Simon Rattar, who had managed the Cromarty estate for so long that he "thinks it's his own property" (010-048), was increasingly acting with autonomy that bordered on insubordination. Sir Reginald resolved to write a sharp letter in reply, declaring "He's got to alter the arrangement somehow" (010-047).

## Narrative Significance

The Castleknowe incident serves a dual narrative function. On the surface, it is a minor estate-business squabble — the kind of routine friction between landlord and factor that any large property might generate. But in the architecture of the plot, it plants an early seed of the antagonism between Sir Reginald and Simon Rattar, an antagonism that would shortly escalate from a dispute over tenant farmers to a matter of life and death.

The episode also illuminates Sir Reginald's character: a man of "kindly temper and hearty spirits" who was nevertheless "easily ruffled" and whose "tempers were hot while they lasted" (010-040). His impulsive indignation over Castleknowe prefigures — in miniature — the pattern of direct confrontation that would prove fatal when he turned his attention to Rattar's management of the Cromarty inheritance.

## On the Cromarty Estate

As part of the Cromarty estate, Castleknowe Farm fell under the management of the estate's factor — a role held by the firm of [[firm-of-s-and-g-rattar|S. and G. Rattar]]. The farm represented one of many agricultural holdings whose rents sustained the estate's economy and provided income for the Cromarty family. The tenancy dispute illustrated how the factor's control over such holdings gave him considerable practical power, even against the nominal authority of the landowner.
