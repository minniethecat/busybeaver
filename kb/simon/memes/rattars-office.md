# Rattar's Office

The law office of the firm of S. and G. Rattar is the professional domain of [[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]] — the space where his legal practice, his fiscal duties, and, after the murder, the impostor's performance of respectability all converge. Described in unflattering terms as resembling a "rabbit warren," the office's physical character reflects the opacity of its proprietor's affairs.

## Description

The office is a space of documents and routine. "His [[letter]]s were always laid out on his desk" (003-002), and the instruction to "bring the [[letter]] book" (003-003) establishes the systematic record-keeping that defines the legal practice. The "[[letter]] book and ledger" (003-009) are the documentary infrastructure of the firm — and potentially, a repository of evidence that the impostor must control.

## Significance

The office is where the professional identity that George has stolen is most visibly performed. Client consultations, the management of estates, the conduct of fiscal business — all take place within these walls, and the impostor's ability to sustain his performance in this professional setting is the measure of his deception's success. The clerk [[james-ison|James Ison]], whose daily proximity to the office's routines makes him sensitive to deviation, is the professional equivalent of [[james-bisset|Bisset]] the butler: the institutional witness whose observations contribute to the impostor's eventual exposure.
