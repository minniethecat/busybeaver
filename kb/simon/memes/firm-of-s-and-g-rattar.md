The Firm of S. and G. Rattar was the legal practice operated by [[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]] in the unnamed Scottish county town. The firm served as "lawyer to several of the larger landowners and factor on their estates, and lawyer and adviser also to many other people in various stations in life" (002-003). Its work combined conventional legal services with estate management — the role of "factor" — giving Simon Rattar intimate knowledge of the financial affairs and private lives of the county's most prominent families.

## Structure and Staff

The firm occupied [[rattars-office|Rattar's Office]], described as a "rabbit warren" of interconnected rooms. Its head clerk was [[james-ison|James Ison]], who served as Simon Rattar's principal subordinate and was entrusted with confidential matters. The firm's work ranged from drafting [[the-reading-of-simon-rattars-will|wills]] to managing estate tenancies — notably the [[cromarty-estate|Cromarty Estate]], over which Rattar served as factor.

## George Rattar's Departure

The firm's name — "S. and G. Rattar" — retained the initial of [[george-rattar|George Rattar]] long after his departure. As [[superintendent-sutherland|Superintendent Sutherland]] informed [[f-t-carrington|Carrington]]: "Mr. George Rattar was out of the firm, sir, years ago" (032-012). The circumstances of this separation are never fully detailed, but it speaks to a rupture between the twin brothers that predated George's fateful return and the crimes that followed. The firm continued under Simon's sole direction, its reputation for probity — "above reproach" in Sutherland's words — forming a stark contrast with the darkness concealed within.

## The Firm During the Impersonation

During [[the-impersonation|George Rattar's impersonation of Simon]], the firm continued to operate — but under the control of a murderer. George, posing as Simon, conducted legal business as normal, including the reading of Simon's own will. The firm thus became another stage upon which the imposture was enacted, its institutional respectability providing cover for criminality.
