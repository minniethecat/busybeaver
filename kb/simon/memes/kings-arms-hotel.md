# Kings Arms Hotel

The Kings Arms Hotel is the commercial and social hub of the unnamed Scottish town where much of the novel's action takes place. As Carrington's base of operations, it serves as the investigation's unofficial headquarters — a neutral ground where the London enquiry agent can gather information without the constraints of official channels.

## Description

The hotel is managed by [[miss-peterkin|Miss Peterkin]], whose combination of commercial competence and conversational availability makes her Carrington's most valuable informant. It is here that Carrington's name is first noted — "the boots noted the name of [[f-t-carrington|F. T. Carrington]]" (020-005) — marking his arrival in the town. The hotel's public rooms — the bar where "another whisky and soda" (020-022) is ordered, the spaces where [[miss-peterkin|Miss Peterkin]] holds forth — are the stages on which Carrington performs his sympathetic-listener act.

## Significance

The Kings Arms represents the neutral territory of commerce, distinct from both the gentry world of [[keldale-house|Keldale]] and the professional world of Rattar's office. As an outsider, Carrington operates from this commercial base, using its social routines — the taking of rooms, the ordering of drinks, the exchange of gossip — to conduct an investigation that official channels cannot pursue.

## The Public Bar as Intelligence Hub

The public bar of the Kings Arms functions as an informal intelligence-gathering network. Unlike the formal spaces of the procurator fiscal's office or the drawing rooms of Keldale, the bar operates on principles of commercial egalitarianism: anyone with the price of a drink may speak and be heard. It is here that [[sandy-donaldson|Sandy Donaldson]], the town drunkard, unwittingly supplies Carrington with crucial fragments of local knowledge. The bar's role in the novel reflects a broader pattern in detective fiction: the hotel as liminal space where class boundaries dissolve and information flows more freely than in strictly hierarchical settings.

## Miss Peterkin's Role

[[miss-peterkin|Miss Peterkin]] is more than a hotel manageress — she is the Kings Arms's institutional memory and its primary intelligence asset. Her long tenure and commanding personality give her access to the town's collective memory, and her proprietorial pride in the hotel makes her a willing collaborator with Carrington's discreet enquiries. She represents a distinct type in the novel's social ecology: the commercially independent woman whose economic position grants her a social authority that transcends her formal class standing.
