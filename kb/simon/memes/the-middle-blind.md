# The Middle Blind

The middle blind of the library window at [[keldale-house|Keldale House]] was discovered to have been out of order on the night of [[the-cromarty-murder|Sir Reginald Cromarty's murder]]. This seemingly minor detail of domestic disrepair became one of the crucial pieces of physical evidence in the investigation, forming part of a chain of material facts that contradicted the official account.

## Discovery

[[james-bisset|James Bisset]] discovered the faulty blind during his meticulous examination of the crime scene. His excitement at the finding was evident:

> "The middle blind wasn't working!" he cried. "When I pulled up the blinds this morning the middle one of the library windows wouldn't go up properly. Now would it have gone up properly last night, or would it not?" (024-082)

Bisset's question was pointed: if the blind had been functional the previous day but was broken now, someone had tampered with it during the night of the murder. This made it a potential piece of evidence about the killer's movements and method of entry or exit.

## Investigative Context

The middle blind's significance depended on its relationship to other physical evidence. [[f-t-carrington|Carrington]] worked to establish whether the blinds had been down on the night of the murder:

> "Bisset... had a good view of the room, if the blinds were not down. Were the blinds down?" (024-080)

The state of the blinds was critical for determining whether an outsider could have seen into the library — or whether someone inside could have passed through the window undetected.

## Simon Rattar's Pattern of Dismissal

As with the [[the-hearth-brush|hearth brush]] evidence, [[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]] attempted to minimize the significance of the broken blind. His pattern of dismissing physical evidence that pointed toward the true killer became, in hindsight, one of the strongest indications of his guilt.

## At the Resolution

By the novel's conclusion, the broken blind was cited alongside the mud on the floor and the displaced hearth brush as part of the chain of material proof:

> "Adding that fact to the unfastened window, the mud on the floor, and the hearth brush, the odds seemed heavy on entrance by the library window." (040-009)

The middle blind represented the kind of evidence that makes sense only in retrospect — a small anomaly that, combined with other small anomalies, told a story the killer had tried to conceal.
