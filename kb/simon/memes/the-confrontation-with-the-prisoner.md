## Summary

On a dark and windy night, [[ned-cromarty|Ned Cromarty]] and [[f-t-carrington|F. T. Carrington]] executed a coordinated plan to confront and arrest the impostor occupying the office of procurator fiscal. While Ned kept "[[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]]" occupied in conversation at his villa, Carrington and [[superintendent-sutherland|Superintendent Sutherland]] dug up the real [[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]]'s body from the garden flower bed. Carrington then entered the villa and announced the arrest — revealing that the man who had been conducting the murder investigation was in fact [[george-rattar|George Rattar]], the twin brother he had murdered and impersonated.

## Context

Following the discovery of [[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]]'s body buried in the garden flower bed (see [[the-discovery-in-the-garden]]), Carrington had assembled the evidence necessary to prove that the living "[[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]]" was an impostor. The plan required two simultaneous actions: Cromarty would engage the suspect in conversation to prevent flight or evidence destruction, while Carrington and the police would dig up the body and then enter to make the arrest.

Carrington gave Cromarty a revolver as a precaution but advised restraint: "You're going to visit the most respectable citizen of the town" (038-011). He instructed Cromarty to "keep him in his chair for you" (038-014) — to sustain the suspect's attention until the evidence was in hand.

## Sequence

### The Approach

Cromarty and Carrington left their hotel shortly after eight o'clock, walking quickly through the silent town under a dark boisterous sky (038-016). They scarcely passed a soul. At the quiet road where [[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]]'s villa stood, [[superintendent-sutherland|Superintendent Sutherland]] and a constable emerged from the shadows of a gateway to join them, each carrying "something long-shaped wrapped up loosely in sacking" (038-016) — spades for the excavation.

At the villa gates the party divided. Carrington whispered "Good luck!" and Cromarty strode up the drive alone to confront the impostor (038-021, 038-022).

### Cromarty's Confrontation

Inside the villa, Cromarty studied the man before him and observed the toll of recent days. Each day since Carrington's arrival "seemed to have aged his face and changed it for the worse: a haggard, ugly, malicious face" (038-029). The impostor's composure was visibly cracking.

Cromarty pressed the advantage, revealing that Carrington had deliberately tricked "Simon" into revealing his ignorance of details the real procurator fiscal would have known (038-053). When "Simon" asked what Carrington accused him of, Cromarty deflected, maintaining the tension while buying time for the excavation outside.

The suspect's agitation grew. He attempted to summon his housekeeper [[mary-maclean|Mary]] to serve as a witness or buffer, but Cromarty's "towering form and formidable voice" sent her out and shut the door (038-070).

### The Arrest

Footsteps sounded on the gravel and the doorbell rang (038-078). [[superintendent-sutherland|Superintendent Sutherland]] entered first, then the constable, and Carrington last. [[superintendent-sutherland|Sutherland]] announced:

> "I arrest you in the King's name!" (038-081)

When [[superintendent-sutherland|Sutherland]] began to describe the charges, Carrington corrected him with the climactic revelation:

> "The superintendent has omitted part of the usual formalities. This person should have been introduced as Mr. George Rattar." (038-088)

Carrington then presented the evidence directly: "We have just dug up your brother's body from that flower bed. Do you recognise his ring?" (038-092).

### The Aftermath

[[george-rattar|George Rattar]], now exposed, did not resist. In Chapter XXXIX, he gave a full confession from custody, beginning: "I needn't trouble you with my adventures before I came down here to visit brother Simon, for you know them well enough" (039-002). He recounted how he had rung Simon's bell, been admitted, and made his brother a "sporting proposition" — which Simon rejected, leading to the murder and impersonation (039-005, 039-009).

## Key Participants

- **[[ned-cromarty]]** — The unconventional [[america|America]]n-Scottish laird who confronted the impostor directly, maintaining the pretence of a social visit while Carrington completed the excavation outside.
- **[[f-t-carrington]]** — The private enquiry agent who orchestrated the dual-pronged operation — excavation and arrest — and delivered the dramatic unmasking.
- **[[george-rattar]]** — The impostor procurator fiscal, exposed as the murderer of his twin brother Simon and of [[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald Cromarty]].
- **[[superintendent-sutherland|Superintendent Sutherland]]** — The local police superintendent who formally made the arrest.
- **[[mary-maclean|Mary]]** — The housekeeper at [[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]]'s villa, dismissed from the room before the arrest.

## Outcome / Significance

The confrontation with [[george-rattar|George Rattar]] was the climactic resolution of the novel's central mystery. Three crimes were solved in a single night:

1. **The murder of [[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]]**: George had killed his twin brother and buried him in the garden, then assumed his identity as procurator fiscal.
2. **The murder of [[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald Cromarty]]**: George, in his impostor role, had killed the baronet to silence him — the Castleknowe dispute [[letter]] (see [[the-letter-chapter-x]]) had brought [[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald]] dangerously close to the truth.
3. **The impersonation**: For the entire duration of the murder investigation, the man directing it was himself the murderer.

The arrest and confession (see [[george-rattars-confession]]) cleared the false suspicions against [[malcolm-cromarty|Malcolm Cromarty]] and [[cicely-farmond|Cicely Farmond]], and vindicated Carrington's independent investigation (see [[carringtons-investigation]]).
