# The Cromarty Family

The Cromarty family is one of the central families in J. Storer Clouston's *Simon*, representing the old landed gentry of the fictional Scottish setting. [[sir-reginald-cromarty|Sir Reginald Cromarty]], the head of the family at the novel's opening, belongs to the oldest branch of the Cromarty line. The family's estate, history, and legal affairs provide much of the novel's narrative engine — and their multi-generational relationship with the Rattar legal firm places the family at the center of the murder plot.

## Family Standing

Sir Reginald Cromarty was acutely conscious of his family's position and pedigree. He regarded [[keldale-house|Keldale House]] not merely as a residence but as the ancestral seat of a lineage that carried obligations:

> "It's the oldest branch of the whole Cromarty family and it's my duty to live in the place." — Sir Reginald Cromarty (004-026)

This sense of ancestral duty shaped Sir Reginald's decisions regarding the estate, his will, and ultimately the circumstances that led to his murder.

## Family History and Branches

The Cromarty family history extended beyond the immediate generation. Sir Reginald took pride in the family's past, and those who knew him could draw him into extended conversation on the subject. [[cicely-farmond|Cicely Farmond]], curious about her own connection to the family, applied to [[james-bisset|Bisset]] for information:

> "I simply drew him on the subject of the Cromarty family history and among other things — that didn't so much interest me — I found that Mr. Alfred Cromarty was never married and seemed to have had rather a gay reputation." — Cicely Farmond (005-054)

### Alfred Cromarty

Alfred Cromarty was Sir Reginald's brother, long deceased by the time of the novel's events. Cicely Farmond discovered his photograph in an old album and was struck by the resemblance to her own father:

> "I pulled myself together and asked Sir Reginald in a very ordinary voice who that was, and I could see that both he and Lady Cromarty jumped a little. He had to tell me it was his brother Alfred and I discovered he had long been dead." (005-051)

The implication — that Cicely was Alfred's illegitimate daughter — hung over her relationship with the family, and particularly with [[malcolm-cromarty|Sir Malcolm Cromarty]], Sir Reginald's heir.

She was described as "somebody's something — Alfred Cromarty's, I believe" (007-091), a cryptic reference to her ambiguous place in the family structure.

## Legal Entanglements

The Cromarty family's legal affairs were managed for generations by the [[firm-of-s-and-g-rattar|firm of S. and G. Rattar]]:

> "I am also lawyer and factor to the Cromarty family, and my father was before me." — Simon Rattar (017-040)

This multi-generational legal relationship placed the Rattar firm at the center of the family's confidential affairs, including [[the-cromarty-inheritance|the inheritance]] and Sir Reginald's will. The firm's intimate knowledge of the family's finances and secrets made it uniquely positioned to exploit them — which [[simon-rattar|Simon Rattar]] ultimately did.
