# Tsalal Island

The mysterious black-soil island near the [[the-south-pole|South Pole]] encountered by [[arthur-gordon-pym|Arthur Gordon Pym]] and the [[the-jane|Jane]] — home to black-skinned natives, site of a devastating [[the-iceberg-collision|catastrophe]], and the focal point of [[captain-len-guy|Captain Len Guy]]'s rescue expedition aboard the [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]].

## Overview

Tsalal Island lies at latitude 83° 20' S, nearly five hundred and fifty miles beyond the limits of previous Antarctic exploration. It is the central geographical anchor of both Edgar Allan Poe's *The Narrative of [[arthur-gordon-pym|Arthur Gordon Pym]] of Nantucket* and Jules Verne's *An Antarctic Mystery*. The island serves as the destination of two successive expeditions eleven years apart: first the *[[the-jane|Jane]]* under Captain [[william-guy|William Guy]] in 1828, and later the *[[the-halbrane|Halbrane]]* under [[captain-len-guy|Captain Len Guy]] in 1839.

When [[arthur-gordon-pym|Arthur Pym]] and the crew of the *[[the-jane|Jane]]* first arrived, Tsalal was a strange but vibrant land. Its soil was black, "as though the clay were made of lava-dust" [019-015], and it was inhabited by a population of black-skinned natives — "the almost naked men, armed with clubs and lances, the tall, well-made, upstanding women, endowed with grace and freedom of bearing not to be found in a civilized society" [019-037].

The natives, led by the chief Too-Wit, lived in the village of Klock-Klock, situated among wooded hills with streams of peculiarly veined water that the crew of the *[[the-jane|Jane]]* learned not to drink.

> "There is, then, no doubt that we are on Tsalal Island?" asked Jeorling. "None, Mr. Jeorling, if Tsalal Island lies where Arthur Pym places it." [019-026–027]

## The Two Visits

| Expedition | Year | Ship | Captain | Outcome |
|------------|------|------|---------|---------|
| First visit | 1828 | *[[the-jane|Jane]]* | [[william-guy|William Guy]] | Ship destroyed by native ambush; crew scattered or killed |
| Second visit | 1839 | *[[the-halbrane|Halbrane]]* | [[captain-len-guy|Len Guy]] | Island found utterly devastated, unrecognisable |

Eleven years separate the two visits, and the Tsalal that the [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]] expedition found in 1839 bore no resemblance to the land [[arthur-gordon-pym|Pym]] described. [[captain-len-guy|Captain Len Guy]] and his crew, guided by the enigmatic half-breed [[dirk-peters|Dirk Peters]] (disguised as [[hunt|Hunt]]), landed to find a landscape utterly transformed. Instead of wooded hills, streams, and the village of Klock-Klock, they encountered "absolute, awful drought" [019-029] — a blackened, cindery wasteland.

> "Not a tree, not a shrub, not a plant was visible in the landscape. There was no sign of the wooded hills between which the village of Klock-Klock ought to lie, or of the streams from which the crew of the *Jane* had not ventured to drink." [019-028]

## The Cataclysm

[[jeorling|Jeorling]], the narrator, was the first to grasp what had happened: "An earthquake! Yes, two or three of those terrible shocks, so common in these regions where the sea penetrates by infiltration, and a day comes when the quantity of accumulated vapour makes its way out and destroys everything on the surface" [019-038]. The earthquake had not only devastated the landscape but had apparently destroyed or dispersed the native population: "Where were the multitude of natives, with black skins, black hair, black teeth, who regarded white colour with deadly terror?" [019-037].

The only physical evidence recovered was the skeleton of [[arthur-gordon-pym|Pym]]'s dog Tiger, which confirmed that "Tiger had been saved from the shipwreck like [[arthur-gordon-pym|Arthur Pym]], had escaped the landslip of the Klock-Klock hill, and had come to his death at last in the catastrophe which had destroyed a portion of the population of Tsalal" [019-067].

## Strategic Significance

Tsalal Island was the critical waypoint that enabled the [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]] expedition to push further south. It was the last known position of the *[[the-jane|Jane]]*'s survivors, and [[patterson|Patterson]]'s ice-borne note-book entries stated explicitly that he "had left his companions on Tsalal Island seven months previously" [019-056]. From Tsalal, the [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]] sailed two degrees further south [023-050], ultimately reaching [[halbrane-land|Halbrane Land]] and the [[the-south-pole|South Pole]] itself.

The island also belonged to a group: "according to [[arthur-gordon-pym|Arthur Pym]]'s narrative, Tsalal belonged to a group of islands which extended towards the west" [019-046]. This archipelago proved important, as [[dirk-peters|Dirk Peters]] later revealed that the surviving Tsalal natives had fled there after the earthquake [020-119].
