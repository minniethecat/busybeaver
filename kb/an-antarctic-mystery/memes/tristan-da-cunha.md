# Tristan da Cunha

**Tristan da Cunha** is a remote volcanic island in the South Atlantic Ocean, the first port of call for the [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]] after departing the [[kerguelen-islands|Kerguelens]]. It is here, at the inn of a man named Glass, that [[captain-len-guy|Captain Len Guy]] and [[jeorling|Jeorling]] receive the testimony that transforms their understanding of the expedition's purpose — and that of the reader.

## Geography

Tristan da Cunha is described as a towering volcanic island visible from far out at sea. [[jeorling|Jeorling]] records that "in the morning, the towering volcano of the chief island was signalized" (010-004). The island's "thick forests, which clothed the volcano up to the middle of the central peak" present a striking contrast to the barren [[kerguelen-islands|Kerguelens]] (010-019).

The island lies approximately fifteen hundred miles west-southwest of the Cape of Good Hope, in a region of the South Atlantic frequented by whalers and trading vessels.

## The Testimony of Glass

The innkeeper Glass, described as "an English artilleryman" who "reigned over a little colony of twenty-eight subjects" (010-008), is the novel's most important minor character. It is Glass who confirms that the [[the-jane|Jane]] was a real vessel, that [[william-guy|Captain William Guy]] had indeed called at Tristan da Cunha, and that Poe's narrative — however embellished — had a basis in fact.

Glass had known [[william-guy|William Guy]] personally. He had seen the _Jane_ and spoken with her crew. His testimony provides the first independent corroboration of events that [[jeorling|Jeorling]] had dismissed as fiction.

> "English artillery, named Glass, reigned over a little colony of twenty-eight subjects." (010-008)

## The Pivot Point

The _Halbrane_ had been scheduled to "set sail again for Tristan d'Acunha, where she was to discharge her cargo" (001-106). But the visit becomes far more than a commercial call. After speaking with Glass, [[captain-len-guy|Captain Len Guy]] alters his plans decisively. The voyage is no longer a trading expedition but a rescue mission.

From Tristan da Cunha, the _Halbrane_ sails for the [[falkland-islands|Falklands]], and from there into the Antarctic unknown. The island thus serves as the narrative pivot — the point at which commercial motive yields to humanitarian quest.
