# The Magnetic Pole

**The Magnetic Pole** is a scientific concept explored in _An Antarctic Mystery_ as the [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]] approaches the extreme southern latitudes. Verne uses the magnetic pole's effects to heighten the sense of entering an unknown and hostile realm [026-012].

## In the Novel

As the Halbrane sails deeper into Antarctic waters, the compass begins to behave erratically — "gone wild at the approach to the magnetic pole" [027-015]. This phenomenon, while scientifically explicable, contributes to the growing atmosphere of disorientation and peril that characterizes the later chapters of the expedition.

The magnetic pole serves a dual function in the novel: it is both a genuine scientific curiosity that Verne — ever the educator — explains to his readers, and a narrative device that signals the expedition's passage beyond the boundaries of the known world [027-015].

At the place where the magnetic meridians cross, the primary observable phenomenon is "the vertical position of the magnetic needle" — a detail that reflects Verne's commitment to grounding his fiction in authentic scientific observation [039-005].
