# The Rescue of Pym

**The Rescue of Pym** was the stated mission of the [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]] expedition — to locate and rescue [[arthur-gordon-pym|Arthur Gordon Pym]] and any surviving crew of the [[the-jane|Jane]], believed by [[captain-len-guy|Captain Len Guy]] to be alive somewhere in the high Antarctic latitudes. The mission, driven by the captain's unshakeable conviction, drove the expedition beyond all known waters to the [[the-south-pole|South Pole]] and beyond.

## The Mission

[[captain-len-guy|Captain Len Guy]] believed — against all evidence and the scepticism of every rational observer — that his brother [[william-guy|William Guy]] and [[arthur-gordon-pym|Arthur Gordon Pym]] had survived the native uprising on [[tsalal-island|Tsalal Island]] and remained alive somewhere in the Antarctic interior. This belief, which [[jeorling|Jeorling]] initially dismissed as monomania, proved to be partially correct: survivors of the [[the-jane|Jane]] had indeed lived for some time after the attack, and evidence of their presence was discovered at multiple locations.

## The Outcome

The rescue mission succeeded and failed in equal measure. It succeeded in locating [[william-guy|William Guy]] and several of his companions, who were found alive on [[halbrane-land|Halbrane Land]] and reunited with [[captain-len-guy|Captain Len Guy]] in a moment of extraordinary emotion (039-034). But it failed in its ultimate objective: [[arthur-gordon-pym|Arthur Gordon Pym]] was found not alive but dead, his body frozen at the foot of the magnetic sphinx, where he had lain for eleven years (039-019).

## The Cost

The rescue mission exacted a terrible toll. Of the thirty-eight souls who sailed from the [[kerguelen-islands|Kerguelens]] aboard the [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]], only twelve survived to return (039-030). Among the dead were [[dirk-peters|Dirk Peters]], whose faithful heart broke at the sight of his lost companion, and the mutineer [[hearne|Hearne]], whose attempt to abandon the mission led him directly into the magnetic death trap of the sphinx.
