# The Ariel

The **_Ariel_** is a small two-decked sloop belonging to the [[arthur-gordon-pym|Pym]] family of Nantucket, featured in the backstory of Edgar Allan Poe's _The Narrative of [[arthur-gordon-pym|Arthur Gordon Pym]] of Nantucket_ and referenced in Jules Verne's _An Antarctic Mystery_. The vessel is the setting for [[arthur-gordon-pym|Arthur Gordon Pym's]] first maritime misadventure — a reckless nocturnal sailing attempt with his friend [[augustus-barnard|Augustus Barnard]] that nearly costs both boys their lives and foreshadows the more catastrophic voyages to come [001-060] [001-062].

> The first exploit of Augustus Barnard and Arthur Pym was an excursion on board a little sloop, the Ariel, a two-decked boat which belonged to the Pyms. [001-060]

## The Voyage

### The Drunken Departure

The adventure aboard the _Ariel_ begins, characteristically for [[arthur-gordon-pym|Pym]]'s early exploits, with intoxication. [[jeorling|Jeorling]] recounts the episode: "One evening the two youths, both being very tipsy, embarked secretly, in cold October weather, and boldly set sail in a strong breeze from the south-west" [001-060]. Neither boy had any serious sailing experience, and the October weather off Nantucket was already turning cold and stormy.

The _Ariel_, aided by the ebb tide, quickly lost sight of land. Within hours, a violent storm descended on the small vessel — far beyond what the inexperienced crew could handle [001-061].

> The Ariel, aided by the ebb tide, had already lost sight of land when a violent storm arose. [001-061]

### The Wreck

The sloop was no match for the storm. The _Ariel_ was dismasted and the two boys were washed overboard, left clinging to wreckage. In a moment of grim irony, a great ship appeared on the horizon — but instead of rescuing the castaways, it ran directly over the _Ariel's_ remains: "Then came a great ship which passed over the Ariel as the Ariel would have passed over a floating feather" [001-062].

[[arthur-gordon-pym|Pym]] and [[augustus-barnard|Barnard]] were eventually rescued by a passing vessel, the _Penguin_, which returned them to Nantucket. But the experience — a brush with death that began in drunken folly — established the pattern of reckless adventure and narrow survival that would define [[arthur-gordon-pym|Pym]]'s subsequent voyages.

> Then came a great ship which passed over the Ariel as the Ariel would have passed over a floating feather. [001-062]

## Significance in the Narrative

### Foreshadowing

The _Ariel_ episode serves as prologue to the narrative world that Verne inherits from Poe. It introduces [[arthur-gordon-pym|Arthur Pym]] as a character defined by impetuousness — a quality that will lead him, years later, to stow away on the [[the-grampus|Grampus]], survive mutiny and shipwreck, and ultimately vanish into the Antarctic unknown.

The episode also establishes the maritime world of Nantucket in the early nineteenth century: a place where even teenage boys have access to sailing vessels, where the sea is both playground and grave, and where survival depends on luck as much as skill.

### Connection to the Expedition

In _An Antarctic Mystery_, [[jeorling|Jeorling]] recounts the _Ariel_ story as part of his summary of [[arthur-gordon-pym|Pym]]'s earlier adventures, establishing the context for [[captain-len-guy|Captain Len Guy]]'s belief that [[arthur-gordon-pym|Pym]]'s narrative is factual rather than fictional [001-060].

The _Ariel_ is never directly relevant to the [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]]'s expedition, but it forms part of the dense web of maritime history that gives [[arthur-gordon-pym|Pym]]'s story its plausibility — and that convinces [[captain-len-guy|Len Guy]] to risk everything on the search for survivors.

### Narrative Contrast

The _Ariel_ — a two-decked family sloop lost in a Nantucket storm — stands in stark contrast to the vessels that define the novel's main action: the sturdy [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]], the doomed [[the-jane|Jane]], and the mysterious [[the-grampus|Grampus]]. Where those ships carry crews into Antarctic waters on missions of discovery and rescue, the _Ariel_ carries two drunk teenagers into a squall. It is the alpha point of [[arthur-gordon-pym|Pym]]'s maritime career — the first of many near-fatal encounters with the sea.
