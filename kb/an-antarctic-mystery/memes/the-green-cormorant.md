## Overview

The Green Cormorant was the inn and tavern at [[christmas-harbour|Christmas Harbour]] on the [[kerguelen-islands|Kerguelen Islands]], operated by the American [[atkins|Atkins]] and his family. It was the only such establishment at the harbour, and served as the primary supply point and gathering place for whaling and sealing vessels that called at the archipelago.

## The Atkins Establishment

The inn was run by [[atkins|Atkins]], described by [[jeorling|Jeorling]] as a "worthy American" who had become completely acclimatized to life on the Kerguelens. His wife was described as "a valiant woman of the Scriptural type," and his sons worked as carpenters, sailmakers, and fishers, hunting amphibians in the creeks during the hot season. The family lived as the penguins lived in their rookeries — hardy, self-sufficient, and well adapted to the harsh conditions [001-091].

The Green Cormorant had the custom of all the ships — whalers and others — that put in at Kerguelen. Atkins supplied them with everything they required, and no second inn existed at [[christmas-harbour|Christmas Harbour]].

## Jeorling's Stay

The narrator [[jeorling|Jeorling]] stayed at the Green Cormorant for approximately two months during the austral winter, having been set down at Christmas Harbour by the Chilian two-master *Peñas*. During his stay, he went sealing with Atkins's people, visited penguin and albatross rookeries, and was served occasional dishes of petrel seasoned by Atkins himself. He described the welcome at the inn as friendly and obliging [001-080].

It was at the Green Cormorant that [[jeorling|Jeorling]] first met [[captain-len-guy|Captain Len Guy]], who took his supplies there, and where [[atkins|Atkins]] first raised the possibility of Jeorling securing passage aboard the *[[the-halbrane|Halbrane]]* [001-102].

## Role in the Narrative

The Green Cormorant serves as the physical and narrative starting point of the novel. It is where [[jeorling|Jeorling]], the first-person narrator, is introduced in his idle state at the Kerguelens, and where the connection to the *[[the-halbrane|Halbrane]]* and her mysterious mission is first established. The inn represents the last outpost of civilization before the [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]]'s voyage into the unknown Antarctic waters.
