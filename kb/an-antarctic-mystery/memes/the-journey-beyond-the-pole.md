# The Journey Beyond the Pole

**The Journey Beyond the Pole** is one of the most remarkable episodes in Jules Verne's _An Antarctic Mystery_. After the [[the-iceberg-collision|destruction of the Halbrane]], the surviving crew find themselves adrift on a fragment of the iceberg, carried southward by polar currents beyond the [[the-south-pole|South Pole]] itself — becoming the first human beings ever to traverse the interior of the Antarctic continent.

## The Drift

The journey began in catastrophe. When the iceberg upon which the [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]] had been hauled overturned, the schooner was crushed. The survivors — twelve men huddled on a fragment of ice — had no control over their destination. The current carried them inexorably southward.

> "It was now beyond a doubt that we were moving between the two halves of a continent, one on the east, the other on the west, which formed the vast antarctic region." (033-012)

This discovery — made not through deliberate exploration but through enforced drift — represented a significant advance in polar geography. The survivors confirmed, through direct observation, that the Antarctic consisted of a continent bisected by a navigable strait.

## The Conditions

The journey was one of extreme privation. The Antarctic summer was ending, and "the sun was on the verge of the horizon" (038-008). Temperatures fell to twenty-three degrees below freezing. The survivors subsisted on penguin meat and what provisions they had salvaged from the wreck.

The psychological toll was equally severe. [[jeorling|Jeorling]], the narrator, experienced a growing identification with [[arthur-gordon-pym|Arthur Gordon Pym]], feeling himself "under the dominion of a sort of hallucination, one of those hallucinations which must have troubled the mind of Arthur Pym" (033-001).

## Navigation Without Instruments

With compasses rendered useless by proximity to the [[the-magnetic-disturbances|magnetic pole]], the survivors navigated by dead reckoning and the stars. The [[the-southern-cross|Southern Cross]] and the [[the-polar-aurora|polar aurora]] were their only guides. The journey was a testament to the seamanship of [[james-west|James West]] and the unyielding authority of [[captain-len-guy|Captain Len Guy]].

## The Destination

The drift ultimately carried the survivors to the Land of the Sphinx, where they encountered the colossal magnetic mountain and discovered the frozen body of [[arthur-gordon-pym|Arthur Gordon Pym]], preserved for eleven years in the Antarctic cold (039-019) (039-024). The journey beyond the pole thus delivered them to the novel's ultimate revelation — the resolution of the mystery that had driven the expedition from its inception.
