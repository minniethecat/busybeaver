## Overview

Ned Holt was a sailor aboard the *[[the-grampus|Grampus]]* and the brother of [[martin-holt|Martin Holt]], who served as sailing-master aboard the *[[the-halbrane|Halbrane]]*. His death at the hands of [[dirk-peters|Dirk Peters]] during the shipwreck ordeal of the *Grampus* is one of the dark secrets that haunts the narrative of *An Antarctic Mystery*.

## Identity Concealment in Pym's Narrative

In [[edgar-allan-poe|Edgar Allan Poe]]'s *The Narrative of Arthur Gordon Pym*, the name "Ned Holt" was changed to "Parker" — a deliberate concealment by [[arthur-gordon-pym|Arthur Gordon Pym]] to protect the identities of those involved in the cannibalism episode. [[jeorling|Jeorling]] later explains this to his companions:

> I informed my companions of the reason why the name of Ned Holt had been changed to that of Parker in Arthur Pym's narrative, and of the circumstances under which the half-breed had apprised me of the fact. [035-044]

## The Grampus Tragedy

When the *Grampus* was wrecked and its survivors faced starvation, the crew drew lots to determine who would be sacrificed to feed the others. Ned Holt drew the fatal lot, and [[dirk-peters|Dirk Peters]] carried out the killing. The parallel between the two brothers — Ned and [[martin-holt|Martin]] — is repeatedly emphasized:

> The two brothers are so like each other. In Martin Holt I seemed to see Ned Holt. I was afraid, I got away from him. [032-045]

This resemblance was so striking that [[dirk-peters|Dirk Peters]], haunted by his act, avoided [[martin-holt|Martin Holt]] throughout the early part of the *Halbrane* voyage, despite sailing together for nearly three months.

## Significance to Martin Holt

[[martin-holt|Martin Holt]] believed his brother perished in the wreck of the *Grampus* with the rest — unaware of the true circumstances of his death. [[hearne|Hearne]], the mutinous sealer, exploited this knowledge, repeatedly talking to [[martin-holt|Martin Holt]] about his brother in an apparent attempt to turn the sailing-master against [[dirk-peters|Dirk Peters]].

The tension between [[dirk-peters|Dirk Peters]] and [[martin-holt|Martin Holt]] over Ned's death is one of the submerged psychological dramas of the novel, revealed only gradually to [[jeorling|Jeorling]] and the reader.
