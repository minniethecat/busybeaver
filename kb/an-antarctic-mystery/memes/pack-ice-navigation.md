# Pack Ice Navigation

**Pack Ice Navigation** refers to the techniques and hazards involved in sailing a vessel through the fields of floating ice that encircle the Antarctic continent. This practical challenge is a constant presence throughout the [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]]'s voyage in _An Antarctic Mystery_ [003-012].

## In the Novel

The Halbrane's progress southward is repeatedly obstructed by ice — from the initial ice-floes encountered at relatively low latitudes to the formidable [[the-ice-barrier|Ice Barrier]] that guards the approach to the polar sea [003-012]. The crew's skill in navigating these obstacles — finding leads in the ice, avoiding [[iceberg|icebergs]], and reading the signs of changing conditions — is essential to the expedition's survival.

Verne draws on contemporary accounts of Antarctic navigation to depict the ice as both obstacle and guide. The behavior of ice — its colors, sounds, and movements — becomes a language that experienced mariners like [[captain-len-guy|Captain Len Guy]] and [[james-west|James West]] must interpret correctly to keep their vessel and crew alive.
