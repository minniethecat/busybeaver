# The Grampus

The **_Grampus_** is an American brig that plays a crucial role in the backstory of Jules Verne's _An Antarctic Mystery_. It was aboard the _Grampus_ that [[arthur-gordon-pym|Arthur Gordon Pym]], [[augustus-barnard|Augustus Barnard]], [[dirk-peters|Dirk Peters]], and a sailor named Parker (revealed to be Ned [[martin-holt|Holt]]) suffered shipwreck and the horrors of cannibalism at sea — events that would haunt the survivors for the rest of their lives and shape the course of the [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]] expedition.

## The Wreck

The _Grampus_ was lost in the South Atlantic in June 1827. After the ship was "rolled and pitched" by a violent storm, "the cargo of the _Grampus_ having shifted," the vessel capsized (007-042). The four survivors — [[arthur-gordon-pym|Pym]], [[augustus-barnard|Barnard]], [[dirk-peters|Peters]], and Parker — found themselves trapped in the overturned hull, adrift on the open sea.

The details of the _Grampus_ disaster, as recounted in Poe's _The Narrative of Arthur Gordon Pym_, form the darkest chapter of the novel's backstory. Verne's characters revisit these events with a mixture of horror and grim necessity, for the secret of what happened aboard the _Grampus_ threatens to destroy the fragile cohesion of the [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]] expedition.

## The Cannibalism Episode

As starvation set in, the four survivors drew lots to determine who would be sacrificed so the others might live. Parker drew the fatal short straw. [[dirk-peters|Dirk Peters]] — who "had drawn the first lot, meaning he might himself have been the victim had the drawing gone differently" — rushed upon Parker and stabbed him, an act that haunted him for the rest of his life (024-061) (035-044).

This secret, when later exposed aboard the [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]], nearly destroyed [[dirk-peters|Peters]] and opened a wound between him and [[martin-holt|Martin Holt]], the sailing-master whose brother Parker (Ned Holt) had been the victim.

## Legacy

The _Grampus_ is never seen in Verne's novel — it exists only in memory and in the fragmentary accounts preserved by the survivors. Yet its shadow falls across the entire narrative. The bond forged between [[arthur-gordon-pym|Pym]] and [[dirk-peters|Peters]] in the overturned hull of the _Grampus_ is the bond that drives Peters, eleven years later, to join the [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]] in search of his lost companion. And the secret of the cannibalism — the "serious thing" that Peters can never fully escape — provides the novel with its darkest moral undertow.

> "It was a serious thing to do — to acknowledge that I was Dirk Peters, he who had killed Parker!" (024-067)
