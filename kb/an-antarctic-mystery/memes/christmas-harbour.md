# Christmas Harbour

**Christmas Harbour** is the anchorage in the [[kerguelen-islands|Kerguelen Islands]] where the [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]] lies moored at the opening of Jules Verne's _An Antarctic Mystery_. It is here that the narrator [[jeorling|Jeorling]] first encounters the schooner, her captain, and her crew — the place where the Antarctic expedition is conceived, debated, and ultimately launched.

## The Setting

Christmas Harbour is described as a sheltered anchorage on the coast of the main Kerguelen island. The inn of [[atkins|Atkins]], where [[jeorling|Jeorling]] lodges, overlooks the harbour, providing a view of the [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]] at anchor. The harbour is frequented by whalers and sealers, and the atmosphere is one of rough maritime commerce — "the nucleus of the Kerguelen population at the date of the narrative" (001-065).

The harbour takes its name from the day of its discovery, reflecting the tradition of European explorers naming geographical features after the liturgical calendar.

## Role in the Novel

Christmas Harbour serves as the novel's opening stage. All the principal characters — [[jeorling|Jeorling]], [[captain-len-guy|Captain Len Guy]], [[james-west|James West]], [[hurliguerly|Hurliguerly]], and the mysterious [[hunt|Hunt]] — are introduced here. The harbour is the setting for [[jeorling|Jeorling]]'s decision to join the expedition: "The idea of leaving the Kerguelens on the _Halbrane_ had full possession of me" (003-026).

The _Halbrane_ sails from Christmas Harbour on 15 August, bound first for [[tristan-da-cunha|Tristan d'Acunha]]. The departure is a moment of irreversible commitment — the point at which the expedition passes from conception to execution.
