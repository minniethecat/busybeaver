# Bennet Islet

**Bennet Islet** is a small island in the [[kerguelen-islands|Kerguelen]] archipelago, noted in the opening chapters of Jules Verne's _An Antarctic Mystery_ as a location frequented by the crew of the [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]] during their preparations for departure.

## Location and Description

Bennet Islet lies within the complex of fjords and islets that characterize the Kerguelen archipelago. The Kerguelens "possess hundreds of other fjords" beyond Christmas Harbour, their shores "cut sharply, like the margin of a rock-bound coast" (001-067). Bennet Islet is among these numerous satellite islands, its rocky shores typical of the sub-Antarctic landscape.

## Role in the Novel

The islet appears in the context of the [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]] crew's daily activities during the period of preparation before the ship's departure. The crew, under the direction of [[james-west|James West]], make use of the surrounding waters and islets for provisioning and routine maritime tasks.

Like many of the specific locations named in the novel's opening, Bennet Islet contributes to Verne's characteristic geographical precision — the sense that the narrative is anchored in real, named places rather than generic settings. This precision was a hallmark of Verne's method, grounding his fantastic voyages in authentic geographical detail.
