# Jane Sound

**Jane Sound** is a body of water in the Antarctic region, named after the schooner _Jane_ — the vessel that, in [[edgar-allan-poe|Edgar Allan Poe]]'s narrative, carried [[arthur-gordon-pym|Arthur Gordon Pym]] toward his fateful encounter with the mysteries of the South Pole [006-001].

## Significance

The name connects Verne's novel directly to Poe's antecedent work. The _Jane_ was captained by William Guy, and its connection to [[captain-len-guy|Captain Len Guy]] — who is the brother of William Guy — provides the personal motivation for the [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]] expedition [006-001].

The Sound represents one of the geographical features that the Halbrane expedition encounters as it pushes deeper into Antarctic waters, following the trail left by Pym and the crew of the Jane.
