# Captain James Cook

**Captain James Cook** (1728–1779) is a historical figure whose Antarctic explorations serve as the benchmark against which all subsequent southern voyages — including that of the [[the-halbrane|Halbrane]] — are measured in _An Antarctic Mystery_ [001-003].

## In the Novel

Verne's characters repeatedly invoke Cook's achievements. Cook reached the seventy-first parallel before being "effectually barred by ice-floes" [001-003]. [[captain-len-guy|Captain Len Guy]] recites Cook's latitude among the roll of prior navigators, noting that even Cook could not attain the latitudes claimed in [[arthur-gordon-pym|Pym]]'s narrative [011-015].

Cook believed — correctly — that a polar continent existed farther south, a theory debated by Verne's characters throughout their journey [013-060]. His famous pronouncement that Antarctic lands would never be seen by human eyes hangs over the novel as an implicit challenge that the Halbrane expedition ultimately answers.
