# West Dale

The western valley of the country known as the Dales, lying on the western bank of the [[sundering-flood|Sundering Flood]]. It was home to [[elfhild|Elfhild]] and the western church of [[allhallows|Allhallows]] at the [[cloven-mote|Cloven Mote]], and was generally poorer and less populous than its eastern counterpart (031-002).

## Geography

The West Dale ran along the western side of the Sundering Flood, opposite the [[wethermel|East Dale]]. The two Dales met at the [[cloven-mote|Cloven Mote]], where the East and West Dales held their great assemblies. The river Sundering Flood divided West Dale from East Dale along its entire length, making direct travel between them impossible — all crossing had to be made far to the north at the [[city-of-the-sundering-flood|City of the Sundering Flood]] (031-005).

## The Western Church of Allhallows

At the Cloven Mote stood two churches of [[allhallows|Allhallows]] — one on the eastern bank and one on the western. The western church served the folk of West Dale, and it was there that [[elfhild|Elfhild]]'s kinswoman was buried: "we brought her to earth by the church of Allhallows the West, hard by the place of the Cloven Mote" (031-012). The burial was described as "a stately one as for a poor house in the West Dale" (031-012), indicating the relative poverty of the region.

## Society and Character

The West Dale was described as less wealthy than the East Dale. The people were primarily herders and farmers, living in scattered steads rather than towns. Conversations between [[osberne|Osberne]] and [[elfhild|Elfhild]] often touched on the differences between their two Dales. Elfhild's house was "neither grand nor great" but bigger than needed for the two women who dwelt there (031-014).

## Relationship to East Dale

The East and West Dales were closely linked through the institution of the [[cloven-mote|Cloven Mote]], where representatives from both sides gathered for their great feasts and councils. Despite being separated by the impassable Sundering Flood, the two communities shared a common culture, worship of Allhallows, and legal traditions. The trysting-place where [[osberne|Osberne]] and [[elfhild|Elfhild]] met as children lay near the place where the East and West Dales held the Cloven Mote (028-003).
