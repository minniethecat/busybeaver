# Red Lad

The martial epithet by which Osberne of [[wethermel]] was known among the fighting men of the Dales and beyond, earned during the wars against the Barons' League and their allies.

## Nature

The Red Lad is a personal epithet — a war-name bestowed upon or earned by Osberne as he rose from shepherd boy to renowned warrior. It is the name by which soldiers, knights, and strangers came to know him in the field of battle (039-021).

## Description

The name "Red Lad" became Osberne's common designation among the war-band of the Dales. Men drank to his health by that name, and it followed him through the campaigns (039-022). [[sir-godrick|The Knight of Longshaw]]aw]], a fellow commander, rode often beside the Red Lad and spoke much with him during the marches (040-002). In the heat of battle, warriors would call out to him by this name — urging him to loose his [[dwarfs|dwarf-wrought]] bow, or warning him of approaching danger (040-009, 040-010).

Even in councils of war, the Red Lad's voice carried weight. When a dispute arose over prisoners, one captain turned to him and asked his judgment directly: "How sayest thou, Red Lad?" (041-004). He was summoned to the front lines when the enemy pressed close, treated as an equal by seasoned warriors (041-007). Lords and knights recognized his banner and addressed him by this name (043-002, 044-003).

## Significance

The epithet "Red Lad" marks Osberne's transformation from a lonely shepherd boy of Wethermel into a leader of men. The name carries the affection and respect of his comrades, and its use by knights and lords signals that Osberne has earned his place in a world far larger than the Dale of his birth. The "Red" in his name may refer to his scarlet war-gear, or perhaps to the fiery spirit he showed in battle.
