# Waywearer

Waywearer is the alias under which Steelhead first appears at Wethermel, presenting himself as a wandering traveller who brings gifts and prophecy to Osberne's household (012-006). The name "Waywearer" — one who is wearied by travel — conceals the true identity of Steelhead, who later reveals himself as a powerful and mysterious figure.

## First Appearance at Wethermel

Waywearer arrives at Wethermel as a traveller seeking hospitality. He introduces himself plainly: "ye must know that I am called Waywearer, and that I wish increase of..." (012-006). He carries a sealed bundle which he entrusts to the household before departing (013-004).

## The Gifts

Before leaving, Waywearer bestows valuable gifts upon Wethermel. He left the sealed bundle with instructions, and it was later discovered hidden beneath a thorn-bush (013-006). The horse on which Waywearer had ridden away was sent back to Wethermel the following morning with a message (014-007) (014-009).

These gifts proved of lasting value. In subsequent seasons, "the gifts of Waywearer had stood them in good stead" at Wethermel, sustaining the household through difficult times (019-001).

## Revelation of Identity

Steelhead's true name is revealed when he discards the Waywearer persona: "nd down the clough, and there Waywearer, that is to say, Steelhead, d..." (013-005). The dual identity serves as a narrative device — the humble wanderer who is in truth a being of great power and mystery.

## Significance

The Waywearer episode establishes the motif of disguised benefactors that runs through the novel. Steelhead's choice to appear first as a weary traveller tests the hospitality of Wethermel, and the gifts he leaves become the foundation of the household's prosperity.
