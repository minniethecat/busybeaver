Sir Godrick of Longshaw is a noble knight and one of the most capable
military leaders among the [[Dalesmen]]. He becomes a close friend and ally
to [[Osberne]] after their first meeting at [[Eastcheaping]], and together
they wage war against the [[Black Skinners]] and the [[Barons' League]].

## Identity

- **Titles**: Knight, Lord of Longshaw
- **Epithets**: "the good Knight," "Sir Godrick of Longshaw"
- **Domain**: Longshaw, the southernmost great house of the Dales

## Role in the Story

Sir Godrick serves as Osberne's principal military ally and mentor in the
arts of war and lordship. After Osberne proves himself at Eastcheaping
(039-002), Sir Godrick takes the young [[red-lad]] under his wing. He functions
as the commander who organizes the Dalesmen's defense and counter-offensive
against external threats.

## Biography

### Origin and Domain

Sir Godrick holds Longshaw, a fortified seat "a long way south of the
Dales" (039-004). He is a peer of the Dales but maintains an independent
military capacity — "he is here with me partly for the furtherance of his
own matter" (039-004). His lands border regions threatened by marauding
bands, placing him at the frontier of Dalesman territory.

### Meeting Osberne

Sir Godrick first encounters Osberne at Eastcheaping, where the young
warrior is presented to him as a champion. Struck by Osberne's bearing,
Sir Godrick looks him "in the face, and ever the more he looked the more
he liked the looks of him" (039-005). He tests Osberne in a parley with
hostile envoys, finding him both bold and prudent.

### The Campaign Against the Black Skinners

Sir Godrick leads the expedition into the wilderness against the Black
Skinners, a feared enemy tribe. Upon approaching a ruined thorp, he cries:
"Prick on, good men of mine!" (042-001). He directs the rescue of
captives and the routing of the Skinner force. When Osberne recounts a
tale of loss to the Skinners, Sir Godrick shows compassion: "Poor lad,
and this was the woman thou wert to wed?" (043-012).

### The Barons' War

Sir Godrick faces a coalition of hostile barons who "hated Sir Godrick
sorely because he withheld them from the spoil of the Dales" (045-005).
Despite being outnumbered "three to Sir Godrick's one" (045-008), he
prevails through superior strategy and the loyalty of his men. When the
defeated barons sue for peace, Sir Godrick exacts just terms, accepting
submission but refusing to plunder.

### Osberne's Companion

Throughout Osberne's sojourn at Longshaw — "three years while Osberne
abode with Sir Godrick" (045-002) — the Knight becomes more than a
commander. He is "not only a wise man of war but a man of lore also"
(045-002), educating Osberne in governance, strategy, and the customs
of the broader world beyond the Dales.
