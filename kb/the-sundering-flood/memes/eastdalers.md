# Eastdalers

The **Eastdalers** are the folk dwelling in East Dale, the eastern side of the Sundering Flood. They are a proud, warrior-like people of the dales, and under Osberne's captaincy they transform from scattered pastoral communities into a disciplined fighting force.

## Community and Governance

The Eastdalers are bound together by the Cloven Motes, communal gatherings held at a stead some seven miles below Wethermel, where folk from both sides of the eastern dale meet to settle disputes and share news [002-003]. This institution gives the Eastdalers a cohesion that the Westdalers lack.

## Osberne's Captaincy

When warriors from Eastcheaping ride into the Dale seeking aid against the Baron of Deepdale, Osberne is chosen as captain of the Dalesmen — a recognition of his prowess and the respect he commands among the Eastdalers [023-001]. Under his leadership, the Eastdalers learn the art of the stave:

> Osberne taught his folk the use of the long staves, and they had soon many of them who were well skilled therein, and the fame thereof went abroad through all the country of the Eastdalers [036-001].

## Military Campaigns

The Eastdalers fight in several key engagements:

1. **Skirmish in the Marshes** — against the Baron of Deepdale's forces [024-001]
2. **Siege of Eastcheaping** — where they bring the Baron to parley and peace [026-001, 027-001]
3. **March to the City** — where Osberne leads them to aid Sir Godrick against the Barons' League [045-001]

In the great hosting against the Barons' League, the Eastdalers prove themselves formidable: "the Red Lad scatters the host of the Barons" [045-001].

## Character

The Eastdalers are described as "both proud and warrior-like, as if they were earls' kindred" [058-006]. Though they are husbandmen and shepherds by trade, they carry themselves with the bearing of free folk who owe no allegiance to any lord or baron. This independence is both their strength and their difference from the feudal society of the City and the Barons' League.
