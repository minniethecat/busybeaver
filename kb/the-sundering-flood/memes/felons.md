# The Felons

The **Felons** are criminals operating in and around the City of the Sundering Flood who beguile and capture Osberne in Chapter 51: "Osberne Is Beguiled by Felons" [051-001].

## The Beguiling

After the battle in the square and the establishment of Sir Godrick as Burgreve, Osberne is at the height of his fame as the Red Lad. But his very success makes him a target. The felons contrive a scheme to lure him into a trap, using false pretences and guile to overcome a warrior they could not defeat in open combat [051-001].

One of the felons poses as a chapman's servant — a disguise that allows him to approach Osberne without arousing suspicion [051-002]. Through this deception, they manage to capture the Red Lad, setting up the crisis that will ultimately lead to his reunion with Elfhild.

## The Irony

The felons' capture of Osberne is a dark irony: the hero who scattered the Barons' host and won the battle in the square falls not to armed foes but to criminals using trickery. This echoes a theme found in many medieval romances — the hero's vulnerability to guile rather than force, and the necessity of fate's intervention to set things right.

## Aftermath

Osberne's capture by the felons creates the circumstances that lead to his discovery by Elfhild and their reunion. In Chapter 52, "The Meeting of Osberne and Elfhild," the lovers are brought together through the very crisis the felons' treachery created [052-001]. Thus the felons, like the chapman before them, serve as unwitting agents of fate — their malice turned to purpose by the larger design of the tale.
