# Westdalers

The **Westdalers** are the folk who dwell in West Dale, the western side of the Sundering Flood. Unlike their eastern counterparts, the Westdalers appear in the tale as a scattered and vulnerable community, lacking the organisation and martial leadership that Osberne brings to the Eastdalers.

## Geography

West Dale lies on the western bank of the Sundering Flood, across the great river from East Dale and Wethermel. The Westdalers' steadings are fewer and more scattered than those of the eastern bank, and they seem to lack the communal gatherings of the Cloven Motes that bind the Eastdalers together [002-003].

## Vulnerability to Raiders

The Westdalers suffer greatly from raiders and foemen during the course of the tale. In Chapter 32, "Foemen Among the Westdalers," a band of raiders descends upon the West Dale, burning steads and driving off folk. It is during this raid that Elfhild's kinswoman dies and Elfhild herself is taken [019-005, 032-001].

This vulnerability contrasts sharply with the Eastdalers, who under Osberne's captaincy become an organised fighting force capable of defeating the Baron of Deepdale and marching to the aid of the City [023-001].

## Elfhild's People

Elfhild is the most notable Westdaler in the tale. She dwells with an old kinswoman on the western bank until the kinswoman's death during the winter of Chapter 19 [019-005]. After the raiders sweep through West Dale, Elfhild is taken and eventually sold to the Blue Knight, beginning her long journey eastward that ultimately reunites her with Osberne.

## Contrast with Eastdalers

The Westdalers and Eastdalers form a deliberate contrast in the tale:

| Westdalers | Eastdalers |
|-----------|-----------|
| Scattered, vulnerable | Organised under Osberne |
| No common defence | Stave-trained militia |
| Victims of raiders | Victors over the Barons |
| Passive, pastoral | Active, martial |

This east-west asymmetry mirrors the Sundering Flood itself — the western bank is the unknown, the desired, the vulnerable; the eastern bank is the home of action and defence.
