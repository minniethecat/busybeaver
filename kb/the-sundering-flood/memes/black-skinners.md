The Black Skinners are a marauding tribe who dwell in the wild lands
beyond the borders of the Dales. They are the primary antagonists of
the novel — feared raiders who "burned and slew" (042-001), capturing
[[Elfhild]] and holding her captive for years. Led by [[Osberne]] and
[[Sir Godrick]], the [[Dalesmen]] wage war against them to free their
captives and secure the borders.

## Nature

The Skinners are organized as tribes — the Black Skinners and the Red
Skinners are of "the same kindred" but "of another tribe" (043-011).
They are nomadic raiders who live by plunder, described by Sir Godrick
as practicing "an evil custom" (041-003). Their name likely derives
from their practice of taking skins and spoils from their victims.

## Territory

The Skinners inhabit the wilderness regions south and east of the Dales,
beyond the settled lands. Their territory includes ruined thorps and
burned settlements — evidence of their raiding way of life. The
Dalesmen venture into this territory at great risk.

## In the Story

### Raiders and Slavers

The Skinners raid the Dales' borderlands, burning steadings and taking
captives. When [[Elfhild]] is taken from her home by the Sundering
Flood, she falls into the hands of Skinner-aligned groups who carry
her eastward through the wilderness.

### The Rescue Campaign

Sir Godrick identifies the threat immediately: "These be the Black
Skinners, and if we make not the more haste, they will have
burned all this countryside" (042-001). Osberne and Sir Godrick
pursue the Skinners through the wilderness, routing them from a
burned thorp and freeing captives.

### The Enemy Defeated

The Skinners are eventually scattered by the [[longshaw]] forces.
Osberne's personal stake in the campaign — his lost Elfhild —
drives the pursuit with particular intensity. The defeat of the
Skinners secures the Dales' southern border and ends the era of
raids.

## Significance

The Black Skinners represent the chaotic, predatory forces that
surround the ordered world of the Dales. Unlike the [[Barons'
League]], who are political enemies with claims and grievances,
the Skinners are pure antagonists — destroyers who exist only
to plunder. Their defeat marks the establishment of Dalesman
hegemony over the borderlands.
