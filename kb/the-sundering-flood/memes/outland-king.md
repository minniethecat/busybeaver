# The Outland King

A mighty foreign conqueror from oversea who launched an invasion of the [[city-of-the-sundering-flood|City of the Sundering Flood]] and its surrounding lands, only to be thwarted by [[sir-godrick|Sir Godrick of Longshaw]]. Though initially a fearsome threat, he proved unable to gain any lasting foothold and ultimately fled back to his own country (049-004).

## The First Threat

The Outland King first appeared as a distant menace: he "had sent heralds defying the King" of the City, and his host was expected shortly (048-007). This external threat was a key factor driving the City King and the [[porte-of-eastcheaping|Porte]] toward peace with [[sir-godrick|Sir Godrick]], as both recognized that a divided city could not withstand a foreign invasion. Sir Godrick agreed to lend the swords of [[longshaw|Longshaw]] to the City's defense against the Outland men (048-007).

## The Abortive First Campaign

The Outland King's first campaign never materialized: "after all the Outland king came not at all that year, whereas he had fallen sick when he was just at point to take ship with his host" (049-002). This delay gave Sir Godrick a full year to strengthen the City's defenses, which he did thoroughly, making the City "exceeding strong, so that it might scarce be stronger" (049-002).

## The Second Campaign

When spring came, the Outland King arrived with a mighty host. But the campaign was a failure: "there he conquered nothing, but was held aloof at all points, save here and there he was suffered to break through to his great scathe" (049-004). The host was so large it hung about until autumn, but achieved almost nothing militarily (049-004).

## Alliance with the City King

The Outland King's sole gain was political: the [[city-king|City King]] fled to him and became his ally. The two kings plotted to attack the city together the following year, a scheme that prompted the City to abolish the monarchy altogether (049-006).

## Final Defeat

In the decisive battle chosen by Sir Godrick, the allied kings suffered a catastrophic overthrow from which "they might not draw to a head again" (049-012). While the [[city-king|City King]] was slain by [[osberne|the Red Lad]], the Outland King escaped by sharp riding under the protection of his best knights. He fled to his ships, sailed away, and "came back again never to trouble the City of the Sundering Flood" (049-012).

## Character Significance

The Outland King functions as an externalized threat that paradoxically strengthens the communities along the Sundering Flood. His invasion forces the City to unite, accept Sir Godrick's leadership, and ultimately abolish its monarchy. He is a classic Morris antagonist — vast in power but ultimately hollow, defeated by the cohesion of free communities under wise leadership.
