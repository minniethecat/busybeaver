# Bight of the Cloven Knoll

The **Bight of the Cloven Knoll** is a bight — a bend, curve, or bay — in the Sundering Flood near the Cloven Knoll. It is one of the river-landmarks along the great watercourse and gives its name to Chapter 9: "The Bight of the Cloven Knoll" [009-001].

## Geography

A "bight" in geographical terms refers to a curve or recess in a coastline or riverbank. The Bight of the Cloven Knoll is a place where the Sundering Flood curves around or near the Cloven Knoll, creating a distinctive landscape feature visible from both banks.

The Cloven Knoll itself is a cloven (split or cleft) hill near the river, and the bight is the river's response to this landform — the water swinging around the base of the knoll in a broad curve.

## Role in the Tale

The bight is mentioned as a place where the young Osberne takes the sheep to pasture near the water. It is one of the named locations along the Sundering Flood that form the geography of Osberne's childhood world — a world bounded by the river on one side and the mountains on the other.
