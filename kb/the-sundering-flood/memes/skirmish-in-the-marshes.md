# Skirmish in the Marshes

The **Skirmish in the Marshes** is an early military engagement between the Dalesmen under Osberne's captaincy and the forces of the Baron of Deepdale. It takes place in the marshy lands near Deepdale and is described in Chapter 24: "A Skirmish with the Baron of Deepdale in the Marshes" [024-001].

## Context

After Osberne is chosen as captain of the Dalesmen, warriors from Eastcheaping have ridden into the Dale seeking aid against the Baron of Deepdale, who threatens the trading town [023-001]. The Dalesmen march forth to meet this threat, and the skirmish in the marshes is their first significant engagement.

## The Battle

The skirmish demonstrates Osberne's tactical acumen and the effectiveness of the stave-fighting techniques he has taught the Dalesmen. Though the terrain — marshy ground — is difficult for conventional warfare, the Dalesmen's mobility and skill with the long staves give them an advantage against the Baron's heavily armed men-at-arms.

## Aftermath

The skirmish is a success for the Dalesmen and sets the stage for the larger campaign that follows. It establishes Osberne's reputation as a war-captain and proves that the free Dalesmen can stand against feudal forces. The subsequent events — including Stephen's adventure in the foemen's camp, the bringing of the Baron to Eastcheaping, and the parley from the walls — all flow from this initial engagement [025-001, 026-001, 027-001].
