# The North Gate

The **North Gate** is one of the gates of the City of the Sundering Flood, serving as a northern entry point into the walled city. It appears in the tale primarily in military contexts, as forces move through it during the conflict with the Barons' League.

## Military Significance

The North Gate is mentioned in connection with troop movements and the defence of the City. During the broader conflict, the disposition of forces at the city's gates — including the North Gate — becomes a matter of strategic importance [046-001].

The gate also features in Osberne's arrival at the City. As the Red Lad, he enters through one of the city gates, and the North Gate is among those through which allied forces and messengers pass during the critical days of the Barons' League crisis [046-004].

## Setting

The City of the Sundering Flood is a walled settlement, and its gates — like the North Gate — are the points where the countryside meets the urban world of guilds, portes, and kings. The gates serve as the boundary between the pastoral world of the Dales and the political world of the City.
