# Deepdale

Deepdale is the territorial domain of the [[baron-of-deepdale|Baron of Deepdale]], a feudal lordship lying in proximity to [[Eastcheaping]] in the eastern reaches beyond the Dales. The territory was a significant regional power, encompassing "wide lands" with "towns, tolls, and markets" under the Baron's authority (028-001).

## Geography

The lordship of Deepdale lay near enough to Eastcheaping that the Baron could mount a military campaign against the town (021-002). The territory extended over sufficient land to support a substantial military force of stout men-at-arms, who were "well known to them of Eastcheaping and were a terror to them" (025-001). The land beyond Deepdale was less familiar to the Dalesmen, described as being "of the fashion of them beyond Deepdale" (025-008).

## Role in the Story

### The Deepdale Conflict

The territory first enters the story when the Baron of Deepdale sent the Porte of Eastcheaping his challenge over a dispute concerning truage, initiating the [[deepdale-conflict|Deepdale Conflict]] (021-002). By late spring, the Baron "had waxed so mighty that he gathered a great host" and marched against Eastcheaping (025-004).

During the siege, the men of Deepdale proved fierce opponents. In the fighting at the North Gate, they cried "Deepdale, Deepdale!" as their battle-shout until "scarce a man of them was left un slain" (024-004). Osberne himself taunted them: "Go back, ye swine, to Deepdale" (024-005), then "entered among the Deepdale folk and smote right and left" (024-006).

### Capture and Aftermath

Osberne and Stephen the Eater raided the Baron's camp by night, capturing him from his Great Bastide and bringing "the lordship of Deepdale to the door" (026-007). After the Baron's capture, "the stout men-at-arms of Deepdale began to flow away from before Eastcheaping" (027-017), and the Baron signed a deed of peace the following day (028-001).

### Later in the Story

The territory reappears in the later war arc when Sir Godrick sought passage through Deepdale during the war against the [[barons-league|Barons' League]]. Osberne wore "goodly armour of the spoils of Deepdale" into that later conflict (031-001), a trophy of his earlier victory.
