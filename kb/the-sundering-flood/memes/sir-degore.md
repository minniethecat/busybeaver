# Sir Degore

A knight of Deepdale, described as a hoar (grey-haired) man and very wise, who served as a commander under the [[baron-of-deepdale]] during the conflict with Eastcheaping (027-003).

## Role in the Deepdale Conflict

Sir Degore stood before the Eastcheaping forces as the spokesman for the Deepdale host after the Baron's capture. When there was great doubt and turmoil in the leaguer because the Baron could not be found (027-003), Sir Degore, as the chiefest of the captains, crossed over their dyke bearing a white banner and stood unhelmed before Sir Medard (027-003). He introduced himself saying: "I am Sir Degore, of whom thou wilt have heard; under my lord the Baron of Deepdale I am the leader of this host" (027-004).

When Osberne and his companions brought the captured Baron before Sir Medard, Sir Degore confronted them and introduced himself: "I am Sir Degore, of whom thou wilt have heard" (027-004). He claimed the Baron was sick that morning and could not rise, attempting to conceal the capture (027-005).

His composure was notable — when questioned, he answered "without tarrying the word one moment," claiming a great aid and refreshment had come out of the East country (027-007). This quick, confident lie showed his diplomatic experience under pressure.

## Defeat

The ruse was short-lived. When the Baron was brought forth and took off his hat, Sir Degore and all they below knew him for their lord (027-010). The Baron bade Sir Degore and a five of his counsellors come to him to abide with him for his aid and service while he tarried in Eastcheaping (027-012). Sir Degore and the other Deepdalers were abashed when they understood that their Baron had been captured (027-013).

Sir Degore asked permission to speak with his lord privily, and when admitted, went to the Baron and bent the knee, unable to refrain his tears (027-014) (027-015). Yet the Baron laughed, though somewhat hardly (027-015). After this confrontation, Sir Degore and his leaders went back sadly to the leaguer and fell to work to undo all they had done the six months past (027-017).
