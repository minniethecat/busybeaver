# East Dale

The eastern valley of the country known as the Dales, lying on the eastern bank of the [[sundering-flood|Sundering Flood]]. It was the home of [[osberne|Osberne of Wethermel]], [[sir-godrick|Sir Godrick of Longshaw]], and contained the richer and more populous communities of the Dale-country (031-002).

## Geography and Settlements

The East Dale stretched along the eastern side of the Sundering Flood. Key settlements included [[wethermel|Wethermel]], a small stead in the upper Dale where Osberne grew up; [[longshaw|Longshaw]], Sir Godrick's fortified seat; and the eastern church of [[allhallows|Allhallows]] at the [[cloven-mote|Cloven Mote]]. The East Dale was described as the more prosperous of the two Dales, with better pasturage and richer steads.

## The Eastern Church of Allhallows

At the Cloven Mote stood the eastern church of [[allhallows|Allhallows]], paired with its western counterpart across the Flood. This church served the folk of East Dale and was the site of the great assemblies and feasts at which the Dalesmen gathered to worship, trade, and deliberate (034-003).

## The Errand of Peace

After the victory at [[eastcheaping|Eastcheaping]], [[osberne|Osberne]] rode with [[sir-medard|Sir Medard]] through the mid and lower Dales to spread word of the peace and gather support. They "sped their errand well at most of the steads of the mid and lower Dales" and made stay at a stead called Woodneb, "which was some little way up the river from the place where the East and West Dales held the Cloven Mote" (028-003).

## Society

The East Dale was home to a yeoman society of free farmers and herdsmen, organized around steads and family holdings. Unlike [[eastcheaping|Eastcheaping]], it had no towns or guilds, but its people were fiercely independent. Their chief gathering was the [[cloven-mote|Cloven Mote]], which served as both religious festival and political assembly. The East Dale produced notable warriors including [[osberne|Osberne]] and [[steelhead|Steelhead]], as well as leaders like [[sir-godrick|Sir Godrick]].

## Relationship to West Dale

Though the East Dale was richer, the two Dales were bound by shared customs, the [[cloven-mote|Cloven Mote]] assembly, and the common worship of [[allhallows|Allhallows]]. The love between [[osberne|Osberne]] of East Dale and [[elfhild|Elfhild]] of West Dale symbolized the unity of the two valleys despite their separation by the impassable Flood.
