# Council of the Lesser Crafts

A civic governing body in [[city-of-the-sundering-flood|the City]], composed of representatives of the lesser trades and crafts, convened by Sir Godrick of Longshaw to deliberate on matters of war and city governance (048-003).

## Context

After the successful campaign against the Barons' League, Sir Godrick turned his attention to the governance of the City. The men of the Small Crafts and the lesser commons had risen against [[porte-of-eastcheaping|the Porte]] and the King, seizing the North Gate and the quarter of the city around it (046-001). The City had a complex civic structure including a Porte (city gate and its governing body), a Great Council, and a Burgreve (chief magistrate) (049-005). The Council of the Lesser Crafts was a representative body drawn from the artisan and trade [[organizations-index|guilds]], distinct from the merchant elite (048-003). Its council-chamber was located within the North Gate (048-005).

## Narrative

Sir Godrick met with the Council of the Lesser Crafts in divers [[cloven-mote|mote]]s along with [[osberne]] and other captains of the Longshaw host to determine how the crafts should be represented on the Great Council of the City (048-003). It was decided that the lesser crafts should sit on the Great Council alongside the wealthier merchant interests, giving the artisans and tradespeople a voice in the city's governance (048-006). The Lesser Crafts also demanded that the Burgreve be chosen by all the Gilds of Craft voting together, rather than by the Great Council alone -- a provision that effectively gave the naming of the Burgreve into the hands of the Lesser Crafts, who were more numerous than the great ones, though far less rich and mighty (048-006).

The Great Council, acting with the advice of the Burgreve, held authority to determine civic matters (049-006). This structure balanced the power of the Porte (the fortified gate and its military garrison) with civilian representation (049-005). The inclusion of the lesser crafts on the Great Council represented a shift toward broader civic participation, secured as a key term of the peace between Sir Godrick and the King's party (048-006).

## Outcome / Aftermath

Peace was signed and sealed on the terms the Lesser Crafts had demanded, and three days thereafter the Porte and the Crafts chose Sir Godrick as Burgreve -- both big crafts and little would have none but he (048-007). The Great Council continued to govern the City with representation from both the merchant elite and the craft guilds (049-005). The Burgreve executed the decisions of the Great Council, maintaining order within the city walls (049-006). In time, the office of King was abolished altogether, and the City was governed through the Porte, the Great Council, and the Burgreve (049-005).
