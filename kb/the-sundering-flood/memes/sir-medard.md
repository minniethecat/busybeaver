Sir Medard is the knight in command of [[Eastcheaping]]'s
defenses during the war with the [[Barons' League]]. An older
and experienced commander, he takes [[Osberne]] under his wing,
teaching him "the beginnings of the lore of war" (023-010)
and entrusting him with increasing responsibility.

## Identity

- **Titles**: Knight, Captain of Eastcheaping
- **Role**: Military commander and civic leader
- **Character**: Wise, measured, and generous with young talent

## Biography

### The Captain of Eastcheaping

Sir Medard appears as the established military authority in
Eastcheaping. When the [[dalesmen]] arrive to help defend the town,
he is the figure they must deal with: "Sir Medard turned the
matter over in his mind" (024-002), weighing strategy carefully.

### Mentoring Osberne

Recognizing the young man's potential, Sir Medard takes Osberne
aside for private counsel: "he had a word with the Knight Sir
Medard, apart" (023-008). He provides Osberne with "the
beginnings of the lore of war" (023-010), forming the young
hero's military education.

### The Siege Commander

During the Barons' siege of Eastcheaping, Sir Medard directs
the defense. His banner flies alongside [[porte-of-eastcheaping|the Porte]]'s, and his
tactical decisions determine the outcome. The battle-cry
incorporates his name: "A Medard, A Medard!" (024-008), showing
the soldiers' trust in his leadership.

### The Parley

When the [[Baron of Deepdale]] is captured, Sir Medard conducts
the parley with characteristic wit. "Sir Medard laughed" (027-006)
at the Baron's pretensions, negotiating from a position of
strength. He handles [[Sir Degore]]'s request for safe passage
with political astuteness (027-014).
