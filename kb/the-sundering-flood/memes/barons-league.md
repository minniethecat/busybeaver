# Barons' League

The Barons' League was a far-reaching military alliance of robber-barons formed to subjugate the free communities along the [[sundering-flood]], and to destroy Sir Godrick of Longshaw. The League was the primary antagonist force in the latter half of the novel, drawing in barons from across the eastern and northern lands, including the Lord of Brookside and the Baron of Deepdale, and seeking to enlist the King of the [[city-of-the-sundering-flood]] (059-002).

## History

### Formation

The Barons of the lands that lay about Longshaw would not endure Sir Godrick's ways and his independence, and levied war against him. They formed a League, swearing the undoing of Sir Godrick and the House of Longshaw forever (045-005). The League was already most mightily manned and far-reaching, drawing in barons from further west and north than Brookside (059-002).

### The Great Battle

At the great battle fought on the first of May, the Barons' League outnumbered Sir Godrick three to one, yet were utterly defeated (045-008). Sir Godrick's forces broke the League's host through superior strategy: he sent Osberne on a flanking errand to seek the hosting of the Barons' League who dwelt furthest north, to fall upon them fast and fierce so they could not be on his flank when he marched against the main host (045-006).

### Defeat and Capitulation

Within a half-month of the great battle, the Barons' League sent a herald to Longshaw praying for peace, but Sir Godrick would grant peace only when they had delivered up all their strengths into his hands (045-010). He led a host against the castle of the greatest of the Barons and took it in ten days. Before winter, he had most of the Barons captive at Longshaw (045-010). After Yule, what was left of the Barons' League sent a herald through the snow craving peace, and Sir Godrick granted terms: he would keep what he had won, and ransom his captives duly — or they might dwell at Longshaw all their lives if they would (045-011).

### Aftermath

The League was broken so completely that it never reformed. All men saw what would have befallen if the Barons' League had not been so utterly broken up (048-003). The destruction of the League allowed the free dales and the City to establish lasting peace, and Sir Godrick's consolidated authority secured the region for years to come (059-002).

## Structure

The Barons' League was a coalition of independent lords bound by mutual military advantage rather than a formal feudal hierarchy. Each baron retained command of his own forces, gathered his own men and stuff for the hosting (060-001), and the League coordinated through heralds and messengers (059-003). The hosting was called at a designated place east of the water and far to the south, a month before Marymass (059-003).

The League conducted itself with the protocols of medieval warfare, sending formal heralds to enemies (045-010) and dispatching knights with special messages to member lords to summon them to the hosting (059-003).

## Territory

The League's territory extended east and north of Longshaw (045-005), in lands less settled than the free dales. Its reach was far — the League sought to draw the King of the City of the Sundering Flood into their alliance (059-002). Member lords held scattered strongholds and castles across this region, the greatest of which Sir Godrick besieged and took in ten days during his campaign of submission (045-010).

## Role in the Novel

### As Antagonist

The Barons' League represents the chief military threat to the free communities along the Sundering Flood. The war against the League is the defining conflict of the novel's latter portion, providing Osberne with his greatest test as the [[red-lad]] and Sir Godrick's champion (045-002).

### The Butchers' Guild Ceremony

The Butchers' guild of Eastcheaping performed civic ceremonies that referenced the threat from the Barons' League, indicating how deeply the conflict permeated daily life even in towns far from the front lines (028-002).

### Strategic Intimidation

The League sought to crush Sir Godrick through sheer weight of numbers and fear. False news spread that the Barons' League had driven Sir Godrick out of the field by the mere terror of their host and the wind of its banners (061-001). The League's psychological warfare extended to the City itself, where the King had brought his host into the field to help the Barons — though ultimately to little effect (061-001).

### Political Reach

The League was no mere band of brigands but a serious political force. They sought to draw the King of the City of the Sundering Flood into their confederation (059-002), and counted among their members knights who feared Sir Godrick's power and sought protection in numbers (057-002).

## Key Members

- [[baron-of-deepdale|Baron of Deepdale]] — one of the most powerful member barons, whose territory lay near Eastcheaping and who had previously waged his own war against [[porte-of-eastcheaping|the Porte]]
- [[blue-knight|Sir Mark, the Blue Knight]] — Lord of Brookside, a reluctant member who pledged to the League but feared for [[elfhild]]'s safety should he fall in the war (059-002, 060-006)
- [[hardcastle|Hardcastle]] — robber-knight slain by Osberne before the League's formation, but of the same type; his death foreshadowed the fall of greater robber-barons
- [[sir-degore]] — commander under the Baron of Deepdale during the Deepdale Conflict, later serving in the League's forces
- Sir Alwyn — a knight of the League, present at the feast where Elfhild observed the knights' fear of Sir Godrick (057-002)
