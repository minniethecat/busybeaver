# The Burgreve

The **Burgreve** (or burgrave) is the governor and military commander of the City of the Sundering Flood. The office becomes significant when Sir Godrick of Longshaw is chosen as Burgreve after the battle in the square, marking the transfer of effective power from the feathered-headed King to a capable war-leader [048-001].

## The Office

In medieval usage, a burgrave (from German *Burggraf*) is the military governor of a castle or walled city, responsible for its defence and civil order. In the City of the Sundering Flood, the Burgreve holds the practical reins of power — commanding the city's forces and managing its defences — while the King retains ceremonial authority.

## Sir Godrick's Election

Chapter 48 is titled "Sir Godrick Is Chosen Burgreve of the City" [048-001]. After the Red Lad (Osberne) scatters the host of the Barons and the battle in the square is won, the city's leading men recognise Sir Godrick's wisdom and martial skill by electing him to the office. This represents a shift toward meritocratic governance in place of the ineffective hereditary kingship.

Under Sir Godrick's burgrevate, the Small Crafts gain representation on the Great Council [048-006], the city's defences are strengthened, and the alliance with the Dalesmen is cemented.

## Significance

The Burgreve represents practical governance in Morris's political vision. While the King holds the title, the Burgreve does the work — a distinction that mirrors Morris's socialist critique of hereditary power. Sir Godrick's elevation to the office is the tale's clearest statement that competence and character, not birth, should determine who governs.
