# Osberne

Osberne of [[wethermel]], also known as the Red Lad, is the protagonist of William Morris's The [[sundering-flood]] (1897). A shepherd boy who becomes a renowned warrior, his life is shaped by three great forces: his mysterious mentor Steelhead, his love for Elfhild across the Sundering Flood, and his rise to leadership among the [[dalesmen]].

## Early Life

Osberne was a little lad of twelve winters, living at the re[[cloven-mote|mote]] stead of Wethermel on the east side of the Sundering Flood (002-003). He was a bold and spirited child, given to adventurous play (002-006, 002-007).

## Gifts of Steelhead

Steelhead, a mysterious warrior, became Osberne's friend and mentor, giving him gifts that would shape his destiny: a [[dwarfs|dwarf-wrought]] bow (007-014, 007-015) and the great sword Boardcleaver (013-009). The name Steelhead gave as his own, promising Osberne would know him by it when they met again (007-017).

## The Red Lad

In the wars against the [[barons-league]], Osberne earned the war-name "the Red Lad" and became known among knights and fighting men for his courage in battle (039-021, 039-022, 040-002). He wielded Boardcleaver and his dwarf-wrought bow to deadly effect (040-009).

## Love Across the Flood

Osberne's bond with Elfhild, a maiden dwelling on the far side of the Sundering Flood, is the heart of the tale. They held converse across the great river at the [[cloven-knoll|Bight of the Cloven Knoll]], exchanging words and gifts that defied the physical separation (009-015, 010-010, 011-001).
