# Longryggs

A farmstead in the [[westdale|West Dale]], located about five miles south of [[hartshaw|Hartshaw Knolls]] — "a good homestead, Longryggs to wit" (033-007). Longryggs was the first substantial target of the [[black-skinners|Black Skinners]] during their raid on the West Dale and became the site of a fierce battle between the raiders and the Westdalers.

## The Raid

When the [[black-skinners|Black Skinners]] swept down the West Dale, they fell upon Longryggs. Unlike [[hartshaw|Hartshaw Knolls]], where the two women were simply taken captive, Longryggs saw a more fortunate outcome: "the womenfolk of the stead had saved their lives by flight, and the carles thereof were in our company fighting valiantly" (034-010). The men of Longryggs joined the Westdale defenders and fought alongside them against the raiders.

## The Battle of Longryggs

The second phase of the West Dale battle was "foughten all about the garth and the house of Longryggs" (034-010). While [[osberne|Osberne]] and the Eastdalers engaged one moiety of the raiders at the [[bight-of-the-cloven-knoll|Bight of the Cloven Knoll]], the Westdalers pursued the other band to Longryggs. The battle was hard-fought, costing the Westdalers "two score and six of our best men" (034-010), but they buried "over seven-score" of the raiders. The survivors fled, "many of them grievously hurt" (034-010).

## Aftermath

After the battle, Wulfstan of Coldburne reported to Osberne that "the thieves have wasted no other stead save Longryggs" — meaning that except for Longryggs and Hartshaw Knolls, the West Dale escaped widespread destruction (034-010). The folk of Longryggs survived through flight, and the men fought bravely in the battle that reclaimed their land. The stead's survival of its people, if not its buildings, stood in contrast to the mysterious disappearance of [[elfhild|Elfhild]] and [[the-carline|the Carline]] from Hartshaw.

## Significance

Longryggs represents the human cost of the Skinners' raids — the destruction of homes and livelihoods, and the loss of life in the battles fought to repel them. Unlike the symbolic and personal tragedy of Hartshaw (where Elfhild was taken), Longryggs illustrates the broader communal impact of the raid, with the community rallying to fight off the invaders at great cost.
