The Blue Knight — **Sir Mark**, also called "the Blue Knight" throughout the tale — is a chivalrous knight and lord of the Castle of Brookside who rescues [[Elfhild]] from the Chapman and escorts her to safety. Acting as her protector and host, he treats her with all honour at Brookside.

## Identity

- **Name**: Sir Mark, known as "the Blue Knight"
- **Nature**: A knight of the Barons' League, lord of Brookside, friend to Sir Alwyn of Warding Knowe
- **Character**: Honourable, chivalrous, loyal to his friends, protective of those under his care

## Biography

### The Purchase

The Blue Knight encounters the Chapman who has bought Elfhild from the Red Skinners. "The Blue Knight buys the Maiden of the Chapman" [055-001] — he purchases her freedom, paying a fair price. His motives are chivalric rather than mercenary: he asks her consent: "Tell me, fair damsel, is it thy will to go with me?" [055-015].

### The Journey to Brookside

The Blue Knight leads Elfhild and the Carline on a journey of many days toward his home at Brookside. When they approach a walled and moated grange, he is recognised: "Welcome, Sir Mark!" [056-004]. He rides at the foremost of the company when danger threatens, placing himself between the Maiden and harm [056-012]. At Warding Knowe, he is warmly embraced by his old friend Sir Alwyn [057-001].

### At Brookside

When they arrive at Brookside, Sir Mark raises his hand and points to his castle: "Lady, yonder is Brookside, my poor house, where I would have thee dwell so long as it pleases thee" [057-009]. He sounds his horn with its distinctive music, and his household rides out to greet them. His two squires, Roland and James, receive him joyfully.

Sir Mark's mother, the Lady of Brookside, questions him pointedly about the Maiden — "What wilt thou do with her? Wilt thou wed her with priest and ring?" — and he answers firmly: "She shall abide here in all honour and kindness so long as she will" [058-006].

### The Call to War

When tidings come of Longshaw and the Hosting of the Barons' League, Sir Mark gathers men from Brookside and departs to join the war [060-001]. Before his departure, he leaves word that if danger threatens, Elfhild should seek refuge with the Grey Sisters — counsel that proves vital when the Maiden and the Carline must flee [061-001].
