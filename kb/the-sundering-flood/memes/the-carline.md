The Carline is a wise woman who becomes [[Elfhild]]'s companion and protector
during her captivity among the [[Black Skinners]] and the long journey that
follows. Skilled in lore and cunning, she serves as a surrogate mother
figure, gathering intelligence and guiding the Maiden through peril.

## Identity

- **Name**: Known only as "the Carline" — an archaic term for an old woman or wise-woman
- **Domain**: The house in the Wood Masterless, later the journey eastward
- **Gift**: Wisdom in lore and the ability to gather secret knowledge

## Role in the Story

The Carline is the steadying presence in Elfhild's life after the maiden's
separation from Osberne. Where Elfhild despairs, the Carline counsels
patience. Where danger threatens, she gathers intelligence and plans
escape. She is the narrative counterweight to Elfhild's emotional
intensity — practical, watchful, and quietly effective.

## Biography

### Arrival at the Wood Masterless

The Carline comes to dwell with Elfhild in the Wood Masterless house,
seemingly by chance. "It is like that the Carline came to dwell with her
because she had been sent by the wisdom of some friend of Osberne's"
(054-001). Her origin is obscure, but she brings with her a store of
traditional knowledge.

### Wise in Lore

The Carline "was somewhat wise in lore, and could tell the Maiden many
things of the woods and the waters and the heavens" (054-002). She
teaches Elfhild herbal remedies, weather-reading, and the old tales
of the Dales, becoming both teacher and guardian.

### Comforting the Maiden

When Elfhild cries out in grief for her lost life and love, "at such
times was the Carline sore grieved for her, and would set her wits to
work to comfort her" (054-005). She listens to Elfhild's sorrows without
dismissing them, offering what consolation she can.

### Facing the Black Skinners

When a raiding party of Red Skinners arrives at the house, "the Carline
stood in the door to meet them" (054-008). She faces them down with
unflinching courage, and the raiders "stare at the Carline as if they
were mazed" (054-010). Though struck with a spear-shaft on the shoulder
(054-015), she endures and protects the Maiden.

### Gathering Intelligence

During the journey under the Black Skinners' control, the Carline
secretly listens to conversations, "having well considered the land"
(055-011), storing up information that will prove vital. She "set herself
diligently to learn" (059-001) from the well-traveled men around her,
assembling a body of knowledge about the wider world.

### The Great City

In the City of the [[sundering-flood]], the Carline shares in the "merry
days" (058-009) of relative freedom. She continues her quiet work of
intelligence-gathering, hearing tidings "that they had for the most part
good hopes" (059-002). When Elfhild grieves the possible loss of her
friend [[blue-knight]], the Carline comes to her and "they lay in each
other's arms and wept together" (060-005).
