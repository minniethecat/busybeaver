# Castle of Brookside

The **Castle of Brookside** is the dwelling of Sir Mark, the Blue Knight. Situated on a low hill before a little river bridged with a goodly stone bridge, it is described as "a long house, defensible by reason of its towers and walls, yet no mere stronghold, but a goodly dwelling" [057-009].

## Architecture and Setting

The castle stands on a hill nowise high, with a river running before it. When Sir Mark brings Elfhild there for the first time, they approach through "a land of little hills, with no little woodland betwixt the meadows" [057-009]. A banner of blue and white waves flies from a high tower, and a score of folk ride out to meet the returning lord.

The great hall is described in detail:

> "Long was the said hall and great, but not very high, and its pillars thick and big, and its arches beetling; and that the folk loved better than flower-fair building, for very ancient it was and of all honour. Ancient withal were its adornments, and its halling was of the story of Troy, and stern and solemn looked out from it the stark woven warriors and kings, as they wended betwixt sword and shield on the highway of Fate." [057-019]

This ancient hall, decorated with the Trojan epic, places Brookside within the chivalric tradition that Morris weaves throughout the tale.

## Elfhild's Refuge

Elfhild dwells at Brookside under the Blue Knight's protection for an extended period — "peaceful days" that stretch through Chapter 58 [058-001]. She is treated with all honour, though the Lady, Sir Mark's hawk-nosed and stern mother, looks upon her with suspicion, asking pointedly whether Sir Mark intends to wed "a churl's daughter" [058-006].

The two young squires, Roland and James, do all they might to be with the Maiden and have speech of her, and she suffers them frankly, seeing no harm in their attention [058-007].

## Military Significance

Brookside is no mere peaceful dwelling. When tidings come of Longshaw and the Hosting of the Barons' League, the Blue Knight gathers men from Brookside and departs to join the war [060-001]. Later, when the Maiden and the Carline flee, they escape from Brookside to seek refuge with the Grey Sisters [061-001].
