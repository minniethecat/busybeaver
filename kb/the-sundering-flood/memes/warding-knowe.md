# Warding Knowe

A "fair white house garnished with towers on a knoll, round about which ran a little river" (056-006), the stronghold of Sir Alwyn and a way-station on the [[blue-knight|Blue Knight]]'s journey bringing [[elfhild|the Maiden]] to [[brookside|Brookside]].

## Description

Warding Knowe lay five days' ride from [[brookside|Brookside]], situated in a grassy fair valley. The castle was defensible, with towers and walls, and was described as a "fair white house" visible from a mile away (056-006). A little river ran around the knoll on which it stood, serving as a natural moat. The stronghold served as "a bar against the strong-thieves of the forest and thereabout" (057-011), protecting the surrounding countryside.

## Sir Alwyn's Hospitality

The lord of Warding Knowe was **Sir Alwyn**, a knight "well on in years, more than grizzled, but a stout and stark knight" and a dear friend of Sir Mark, the [[blue-knight|Blue Knight]] (057-001). When the Blue Knight arrived with the [[the-maiden|Maiden]] and her company, Sir Alwyn stood at the gate "bidding them a free and fair welcome" (057-001). He treated the Maiden with all honour, leading her in personally and later consulting the Blue Knight to ensure he had shown her appropriate respect.

## Counsel on the War

During the Maiden's stay at Warding Knowe, Sir Alwyn and the Blue Knight discussed the political situation. Sir Alwyn shared tidings of the [[city-of-the-sundering-flood|City of the Sundering Flood]] and assessed the [[city-king|King]] as "of little account" — "feathered-headed, stubborn in wrong, and hard-hearted" — compared to [[sir-godrick|Sir Godrick of Longshaw]] (057-003). He warned that unless the King allied with "the best men of the City, as the Porte and the masters of the Great Crafts, he was undone" (057-003).

Sir Alwyn also spoke of the rising [[eastcheaping-crafts|Small Crafts]], who had "in their hearts to rise against both Porte and King," noting that "if they may have Sir Godrick on their side, which is not wholly unlike, they will perchance come to their above" (057-004). This conversation provided crucial foreshadowing of the [[council-of-the-lesser-crafts|Council of the Lesser Crafts]] and the eventual reformation of the City's governance.

## Role in the Narrative

Warding Knowe serves two narrative functions. First, it provides the Maiden with an introduction to the world of knighthood and castle life, preparing her for her arrival at Brookside. Second, through the conversation between Sir Alwyn and the Blue Knight, the reader receives critical political intelligence about the wider world — the balance of power between King, Porte, Crafts, and Sir Godrick — that sets up the conflicts of the later chapters.
