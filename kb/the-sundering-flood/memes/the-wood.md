# The Wood

**The Wood** is the woodland and forest region that borders the settled lands along the Sundering Flood. It is a liminal, wild space — the dwelling-place of Dwarf-kin, a hunting-ground, and a realm of adventure and peril that stands in contrast to the pastoral Dale and the walled City.

## Geography

The Wood lies primarily on the eastern side of the Dale, stretching up toward the mountains. Near Wethermel, "towards the crown of the bent was a wood of bushes good for firewood and charcoal" [002-001]. Beyond the agricultural lands of the Dale, the Wood becomes denser and more extensive, culminating in the Wood Masterless — a particularly wild and untamed region.

## The Dwarf-Kin's Domain

The Wood is strongly associated with the Dwarf-kin. The young Osberne's encounter with the Dwarf takes place in a rocky dwelling within the wooded uplands, and the Dwarf tells him "tales about the woods where big trees grow, and how his kindred had used to dwell therein, and fashioned most fair things in smith's work of gold and silver and iron" [002-006].

It is in the Wood that the Dwarfs dwelt before the children of Adam supplanted them — a theme of displacement that echoes through the tale [002-002].

## The Wood Masterless

The Wood Masterless is the most dangerous and uncharted portion of the Wood. When Osberne rides with Sir Godrick, they "come to the edge of the Wood Masterless" [043-001]. It is a region that lies beyond the reach of any lord's authority — hence "masterless" — and is home to outlaws, wild beasts, and older things.

## Symbolism

In Morris's medieval fantasy, the Wood represents the wild margin of civilisation — a place of danger but also of transformation. Osberne's childhood encounters with the Dwarf in the Wood bestow upon him the gifts (the knife and the promised sword Boardcleaver) that will define his heroic journey. The Wood is both threat and benefactor, much like the Dwarf-kin who dwell within it.
