# Cloven Mote

A traditional gathering and festival of the Dalesmen, held twice yearly at midwinter and midsummer, where the folk of the East and West Dales came together for worship, trade, and social bonding.

## Nature

The Cloven Mote is a social custom — a regular assembly of the Dalesmen from both sides of the Sundering Flood, combining religious observance with communal festivity (002-003). It was one of the few occasions when the scattered households of the Dales gathered in one place, making it a cornerstone of their social and cultural life.

## Description

The Cloven Mote was held at a fixed place between the East and West Dales (022-002). The midwinter gathering included the Christmass service, a religious observance that drew folk from across the region (006-002). By tradition, the Dalesmen and their families would travel to the appointed place, sometimes from great distances across the lonely countryside (030-015).

At the Cloven Mote, young people were paired or "mated" for the festivities — a custom of companionship during the gathering. Osberne was twice mated to a fair maiden at the [[cloven-motes|midwinter Cloven Mote]], a tradition that brought the young folk together (019-001, 019-003). The midsummer feast was similarly grand, celebrated as described in the chronicles of the Dales (036-001).

The gathering also served practical purposes: it was a time when news was shared, disputes were settled, and the community affirmed its bonds. The place of the Cloven Mote lay hard by the meeting point of the two Dales, a neutral ground (030-015).

## Significance

The Cloven Mote represents the social cohesion of the Dalesmen despite their scattered homesteads and the physical barrier of the Sundering Flood. It was the primary institution that maintained a shared identity among the Dalesfolk, functioning as both religious observance and civic assembly. For Osberne, the Cloven Mote marked important moments in his coming of age — it was there he first held a maiden's hand (015-006) and later found companionship (019-001).
