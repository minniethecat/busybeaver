[[Osberne]]'s journeys form the narrative backbone of The Sundering
Flood. From his first journey to [[Eastcheaping]] as a boy, through
the long campaigns alongside [[Sir Godrick]] to [[Longshaw]], to
the desperate ride across the [[Sundering Flood]] to find [[Elfhild]],
each journey marks a stage in his development from the boy of
[[Wethermel]] to the [[red-lad]] of legend.

## Major Journeys

### The Boy's First Journey: Wethermel to Eastcheaping

Osberne's first significant journey is with his guardian Master
Nicholas, who "would ride to Eastcheaping, and he took Osberne
with him" (020-001). This journey introduces the boy to the wider
world of markets, soldiers, and civic life beyond the isolated
steading.

### The Road to Longshaw

After proving himself at [[Eastcheaping]], Osberne rides south with
[[Sir Godrick]] to the fortress of [[Longshaw]]. The road passes
through dangerous borderlands where outlaws challenge them: "For
we know you, outlaws of Longshaw!" (040-009). This journey marks
Osberne's transition from local champion to regional commander.

### Campaigns Against the Black Skinners

Osberne rides with Sir Godrick's host into the wilderness to hunt
the [[Black Skinners]]. Through "the wood, which well they knew"
(044-003), they track and engage the marauders. These journeys
are military expeditions that test Osberne's developing skill in
war.

### The Three Years' War

For three years, "the warfare of the House of Longshaw, even for
those years while Osberne abode with Sir Godrick" (045-002), the
Red Lad wages a mobile war across the southern Dales. His journeys
range from Longshaw to the borders of [[wood-masterless|the Wood Masterless]].

### The Final Crossing

Osberne's culminating journey takes him across the Sundering Flood
itself — the river that separated him from Elfhild through all
the years of the story. This crossing represents the fulfillment
of his quest and the reunion of the sundered lovers.

## Significance

Osberne's journeys trace an archetypal hero's path: departure from
home, testing in the wider world, mastery in war, and return to
love. Each journey adds a layer to his identity — farm-boy,
merchant's companion, town defender, knight's ally, lord of war,
and finally, the lover who crosses the impassable barrier.
