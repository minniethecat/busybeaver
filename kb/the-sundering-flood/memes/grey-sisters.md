The Grey Sisters are a religious order whose house stands near the
great [[City of the Sundering Flood]]. They serve as a sanctuary
and waystation for travellers and refugees, offering protection
under religious authority. [[blue-knight]] promises [[Elfhild]]
that if danger threatens, she should "flee straightway to the
Grey Sisters" (060-006).

## Nature

A religious community of women — likely an order of nuns or
beguines — who maintain a house on the route between the
wilderness and the City. Their grey habit gives them their
name. The "House of the Grey Sisters" (062-001) is a recognized
institution, respected enough that armed men can be sent to
demand the release of guests (062-001).

## In the Story

### A Place of Refuge

The Grey Sisters' house serves as a safe haven on the dangerous
road to the City. The Blue Knight tells Elfhild that if he falls
in battle, she should flee "straightway to the Grey Sisters"
(060-003). The house is visible from the walls of the City,
making it a known landmark and a practical sanctuary — close
enough that a fugitive could reach it in a night's ride: "it
may be as far as the Grey Sisters, but tomorrow further"
(061-006).

### Political Role

The Grey Sisters' house is sufficiently important that military
forces can be dispatched there: "men-at-arms to the House of
the Grey Sisters" (062-001) are sent to demand the release of
guests. This suggests the Sisters possess enough institutional
authority — religious or customary — that even armed men
negotiate rather than simply storm their gates.

## Significance

The Grey Sisters represent the institutional Church's role in a
medieval world — providing sanctuary, mediating between armed
[[organizations-index|factions]], and offering a neutral ground where the vulnerable
can find protection. They are a brief but meaningful presence
in the novel, emblematic of the ordered world's institutions
that stand against the chaos represented by the [[Black Skinners]].
