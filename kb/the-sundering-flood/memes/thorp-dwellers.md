# Thorp-Dwellers

The **Thorp-Dwellers** are the folk of the small villages (thorps) in the settled lands between the Dale and the Wood Masterless. They are common husbandmen and villagers who are preyed upon by the Black Skinners until Osberne and Sir Godrick come to their rescue.

## The Rescue

Chapter 42 is titled "They Deliver the Thorp-Dwellers from the Black Skinners" [042-001]. As Osberne rides with Sir Godrick on their journey toward Longshaw, they encounter a region of thorps that has been suffering under the depredations of the Black Skinners — the slave-raiding company that supplies captives to the Red Skinners for sale.

Sir Godrick and Osberne, with their company, engage the Black Skinners and deliver the thorp-dwellers from their oppression. This action demonstrates the chivalric ideal of knighthood as protection of the weak — the strong fighting not for glory or conquest but for the defence of common folk.

## Way of Life

The thorp-dwellers are settled agricultural folk — more populous and less isolated than the Dalesmen of the upper reaches, but also more vulnerable to organised raiding. They lack the martial traditions of the Dalesmen and the walls of the City, making them dependent on the protection of lords and knights.

## Significance

The rescue of the thorp-dwellers is a key episode in Osberne's education as a knight. Under Sir Godrick's mentorship, he learns that the true purpose of martial skill is not personal glory but the defence of the helpless. This lesson distinguishes the heroes of Morris's tale — Sir Godrick, Osberne, the Blue Knight — from the predatory Barons and the slaving Skinners who use force only for self-enrichment.
