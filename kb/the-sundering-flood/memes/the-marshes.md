# The Marshes

The **Marshes** are marshy wetlands near Deepdale that serve as the site of the skirmish between Osberne's Dalesmen and the forces of the Baron of Deepdale. The difficult terrain plays to the Dalesmen's advantages of mobility and unconventional weaponry.

## Geography

The marshes lie in the lowlands near Deepdale, where the ground is waterlogged and treacherous. Such terrain is ill-suited to heavy cavalry — the feudal knight's primary mode of warfare — but manageable for lightly-equipped foot soldiers who know the ground. The marshes thus serve as a natural equaliser between the Dalesmen and the Baron's armoured forces.

## The Skirmish

Chapter 24 is titled "A Skirmish with the Baron of Deepdale in the Marshes" [024-001]. The Dalesmen, moving swiftly on foot with their long staves, use the marshy ground to their tactical advantage. The Baron's men-at-arms, encumbered by armour and reliant on cavalry, find the going difficult.

The skirmish demonstrates Osberne's talent for choosing terrain that favours his unconventional forces — a mark of the natural captain that he has become.

## Significance

The Marshes are more than a battlefield; they are a symbol of how the land itself can side with the righteous. In Morris's world, the free folk of the Dale fight on their own ground, using the landscape they know against invaders who are strangers to it. The marshes, like the staves, are part of the Dalesmen's home-field advantage — the natural world supporting natural justice.
