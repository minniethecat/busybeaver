The Cloven Motes were the great seasonal gatherings of the
[[Dalesmen]], held at the place called the [[Cloven Knoll]] on
Midsummer and midwinter. These assemblies combined the functions
of religious festival, communal council, market, and courtship
ritual. It was at a midwinter Cloven Mote that [[Osberne]] first
saw [[Elfhild]].

## Setting

The Motes were held at the Cloven Knoll, a natural amphitheatre
formed by the cleft hill where the East and West Dales meet. "The
East and West Dales held the Cloven Mote" (022-002) — the
gathering drew folk from across the entire Dale region.

## Key Occurrences

### Religious Observance

The Motes were tied to the Christian calendar: "they might go to
the Cloven Mote, and hear the Christmass in the church" (006-002)
at midwinter, and the "Midsummer Feast of the Cloven Mote" (036-001)
marked the summer solstice. The assembly combined folk tradition
with church ritual.

### Courtship and Community

The Motes served as the primary venue for courtship among the
widely-scattered Dalesfolk. Osberne was "mated to the Maid" —
paired with Elfhild — at successive Motes: "on the day of the
midwinter Cloven Mote, and he thought her fair" (019-001), and
again "to the midwinter Cloven Mote, and again was he mated to
the Maid" (019-003).

### Martial Display

The gatherings also featured martial contests and displays of
strength. Osberne held his rival Hardcastle's hand at "the Cloven
Mote last winter" (015-006), establishing a connection that
would later erupt into deadly conflict.

## Significance

The Cloven Motes were the binding institution of the Dales.
In a region of scattered steadings with no central government,
these twice-yearly assemblies provided the only regular forum
for collective decision-making, social bonding, and cultural
continuity. The name "Cloven Mote" reflects the cleft landscape
and the gathering ("moot") of the people.
