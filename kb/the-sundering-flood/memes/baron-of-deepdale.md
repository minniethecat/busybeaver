# Baron of Deepdale

A powerful robber-baron whose territory lay near [[Eastcheaping]], who waged a military campaign to subjugate the town and extend his dominion over the surrounding region, only to be captured by Osberne and Stephen the Eater and forced to sign a humiliating peace (021-002) (028-001).

## Role in the Eastcheaping War

The Baron of Deepdale was the lord of wide lands, with towns, tolls, and markets under him (028-001). In early May, he sent the [[porte-of-eastcheaping]] a formal challenge over a dispute concerning truage, initiating the [[Deepdale Conflict]] (021-002). By late spring he had waxed so mighty that he gathered a great host and came against Eastcheaping, demanding the town yield, batter down their walls, and accept his garrison and burgreve (025-004).

## Capture

During the siege, a fiddler who had spied out the land beyond Deepdale revealed to Stephen the Eater that the Baron was lodged in a Great Bastide with surprisingly few guards at night (025-008). Osberne, overhearing this, proposed a night raid: to go by night and cloud to the Great Bastide and carry off the Baron of Deepdale (025-011). The plan succeeded — Osberne and his companions infiltrated the camp, captured the Baron, and brought him to Sir Medard in the great tower of Eastcheaping (027-001).

## Aftermath

Following his capture, the Baron of Deepdale signed a deed of peace giving up to the Porte of Eastcheaping all that for which they had withstood him, and paid a substantial ransom for his own body — a free baron's ransom befitting a lord of wide lands (028-001). The stout men-at-arms of Deepdale flowed away from before Eastcheaping (027-017). The Baron, to his credit, bore no lasting grudge — he marvelled at Osberne and told him: "Well, lad, if ever thou art hard bestead, come thou to Deepdale, and we shall find somewhat for thee to do; and I bid thee thrive hale and well!" (027-017). Later, during the [[sundering-flood]] war, the Baron granted let-pass through his lands but would not allow [[sir-godrick]] to recruit men from his country (040-002).

## Character Assessment

The Baron of Deepdale is portrayed as a typical feudal aggressor — ambitious, powerful, and ultimately outmaneuvered. His willingness to offer Osberne future hospitality after being captured suggests a certain magnanimity or at least pragmatic respect for a worthy adversary. He serves as the primary antagonist of the Eastcheaping arc (Chapters 21–28), and his defeat cements Osberne's reputation as a formidable warrior beyond the borders of [[wethermel]] (027-017).
