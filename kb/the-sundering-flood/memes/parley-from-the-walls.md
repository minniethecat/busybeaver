# Parley from the Walls

The **Parley from the Walls** is a negotiation between the Dalesmen under Osberne and the forces defending Eastcheaping, described in Chapter 27: "They Parley from the Walls" [027-001]. It is the diplomatic turning-point of the Deepdale conflict.

## Context

After the skirmish in the marshes, Stephen the Eater's adventures in the foemen's camp, and the bringing of the Baron into Eastcheaping, both sides are ready to talk. The Dalesmen have proven their martial prowess; the City forces have seen that they face a determined and capable enemy. The parley is the moment when war turns toward peace.

## The Negotiation

Osberne and his Dalesmen stand before the walls of Eastcheaping and conduct the parley with the representatives of the City and the captive Baron. The terms of the negotiation are not described in extensive detail in the surviving corpus, but the outcome is clear: the Baron of Deepdale makes peace, and the immediate threat to Eastcheaping is lifted [028-001].

## Outcome

The parley succeeds in ending the Deepdale conflict without further bloodshed. The Baron of Deepdale "makes peace" [028-001], and the Dalesmen return to their homes — at least for a time. The parley also establishes Osberne as not merely a war-captain but a leader capable of statesmanship, able to wield words as well as swords.

## Significance

The Parley from the Walls represents the medieval ideal of conflict resolved through negotiation rather than annihilation. In Morris's world, right conduct in war includes knowing when to stop fighting. The parley is a demonstration that Osberne's wisdom matches his martial skill — a combination that will serve him well in the larger conflicts to come.
