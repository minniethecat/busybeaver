# Wulfgrimsson

**Wulfgrimsson** is one of Osberne's followers among the Eastdalers, mentioned as a companion during the military campaigns that the Dalesmen undertake under Osberne's captaincy.

## Role

Wulfgrimsson is one of the named Dalesmen who follow Osberne to war. His name — "son of Wulfgrim" — follows the Norse and Old English patronymic tradition common throughout the tale (compare "Osberne," "Wethermel"). He appears among the company of Dalesmen who march with Osberne during the campaigns against the Baron of Deepdale and later to the City.

## The Name

The name "Wulfgrimsson" carries the common Old English/Norse element "wulf" (wolf), linking him to the wild, martial character of the upper Dale. Names with wolf-elements are common in Germanic heroic tradition and in Morris's own naming conventions — they suggest fierceness and loyalty, the qualities of the wolf-pack.

## Significance

Wulfgrimsson is a minor figure, one of the many named followers who give texture and depth to the Dalesmen as a community. His presence in the tale, along with other named Eastdalers, reinforces that Osberne's leadership is not solitary — he commands real men with real names, families, and homes in the Dale.
