# Timeline of The Sundering Flood

The Sundering Flood follows the life of [[Osberne]] of Wethermel through three major arcs: his boyhood and the discovery of [[Elfhild]] across the great river (Chapters 1–20), the war with the [[Baron of Deepdale]] and the loss of Elfhild (Chapters 21–38), and the wider quest through [[Longshaw]] and the [[city-of-the-sundering-flood|City of the Sundering Flood]] culminating in reunion (Chapters 39–68).

## Act I: Wethermel and the Flood (Chapters 1–20)

Osberne grows up at [[Wethermel]], a homestead on the western bank of the [[Sundering Flood]]. He proves himself by slaying wolves that harry the flock (005-001), earns the respect of the [[Dalesmen]], and receives the sword [[Boardcleaver]] from the mysterious [[Steelhead]] (013-001). At the [[cloven-knoll|Bight of the Cloven Knoll]], he discovers [[Elfhild]] on the far bank and begins a long-distance courtship across the impassable river (010-001). They exchange gifts — Osberne shoots presents across the water with his bow (011-001). The act closes with [[hardcastle]]'s failed attempt to seize Wethermel and his slaying by Osberne (017-001), after which Osberne travels to [[Eastcheaping]] for the first time (020-001).

Key chapters:
- Ch. 1–5: Osberne's childhood and the wolf-slaying
- Ch. 6: The [[cloven-mote]] — community gathering of the Dalesmen
- Ch. 7–9: Steelhead arrives, the Bight of the Cloven Knoll
- Ch. 10–11: First contact with Elfhild across the Flood
- Ch. 12–14: Waywearer/Steelhead, the gift of Boardcleaver
- Ch. 15–17: Hardcastle's attack and death
- Ch. 18–20: Elfhild hears of the slaying, winter passes, Osberne goes to Eastcheaping

## Act II: The Eastcheaping War and Separation (Chapters 21–38)

Warriors from Eastcheaping ride into the Dale seeking aid against the [[Baron of Deepdale]] (021-002). Osberne takes leave of Elfhild (022-001) and is chosen Captain of the Dalesmen (023-001). The [[Deepdale Conflict]] unfolds through skirmishes (024-002), a siege of Eastcheaping (025-004), and Osberne's daring night raid to capture the Baron (025-011) (027-001). After the Baron's capture and the peace treaty (028-001), Osberne returns to Wethermel to find Elfhild gone — her kinswoman has died and she has vanished (033-001) (034-001). Osberne seeks counsel from Steelhead (035-001), arms the Dalesmen with staves (036-001), and departs Wethermel to seek his fortune (037-001), parting from [[stephen-eater|Stephen the Eater]] (038-001).

Key chapters:
- Ch. 21–22: Call to war, farewell to Elfhild
- Ch. 23–28: The Deepdale Conflict (skirmish, siege, capture, peace)
- Ch. 29–31: Return to Wethermel, [[tryst]]s through autumn and winter
- Ch. 32–34: Foemen among the Westdalers, Elfhild vanishes
- Ch. 35–38: Counsel of Steelhead, arming the Dalesmen, departure

## Act III: The Wider World and Reunion (Chapters 39–68)

Osberne enters the service of [[sir-godrick|Sir Godrick]] of Longshaw (039-001). They ride together, joust with the Knight of the Fish (041-001), deliver thorp-dwellers from the [[black-skinners|Black Skinners]] (042-001), and traverse the [[wood-masterless|Wood Masterless]] (043-001) to reach [[Longshaw]] (044-001). Osberne earns the name "[[red-lad|Red Lad]]" and scatters the host of the [[barons-league]] (045-001). He enters the [[city-of-the-sundering-flood|City of the Sundering Flood]] (046-001), fights in the Battle of the Square (047-001), and Sir Godrick is chosen Burgreve (048-001).

Meanwhile, Elfhild's story (revealed through the [[the-carline|Carline's]] tale, Ch. 54–64): she was sold by her kinswoman to a chapman, bought by the [[blue-knight|Blue Knight]] (055-001), taken to Brookside Castle (057-001), and eventually fled with the Carline to the [[grey-sisters|Grey Sisters]] (061-001), escaping chapmen through the Carline's wizardry (063-001).

Osberne, beguiled by felons (051-001), is ultimately reunited with Elfhild (052-001). They make themselves known to their people (065-001), return to the lip of the Sundering Flood (066-001), and with the help of a friend at need (067-001), the Knight of Longshaw gathers force for the final resolution (068-001).

Key chapters:
- Ch. 39–44: Journey with Sir Godrick to Longshaw
- Ch. 45–48: Red Lad's victories, entry to the City
- Ch. 49–53: The City King, reunion with Elfhild
- Ch. 54–64: The Carline's tale (Elfhild's journey)
- Ch. 65–68: Recognition, return to the Flood, final gathering

## Thematic Arc

The novel traces a circular journey: Osberne leaves the rural simplicity of Wethermel, proves himself in war and politics, and ultimately returns to the Sundering Flood with Elfhild. The river that once separated the lovers becomes the site of their reunion. This arc mirrors the medieval romance tradition of the knight-errant who must venture into the wider world before he can claim his place at home.
