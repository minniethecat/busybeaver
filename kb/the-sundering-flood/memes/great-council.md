# The Great Council of the City

The governing council of the [[city-of-the-sundering-flood|City of the Sundering Flood]], composed of the masters of the various [[eastcheaping-crafts|Crafts]] of the city. Together with the [[porte-of-eastcheaping|Porte]] and the Burgreve, the Great Council formed the civic government that eventually abolished the office of [[city-king|King]] and established full communal self-governance (048-007).

## Structure and Composition

The Great Council was constituted of the masters of the city's crafts and guilds. Under the terms of the peace brokered by [[sir-godrick|Sir Godrick of Longshaw]], the representation was reconfigured so that "so many of the masters of the Small Crafts should sit on the Great Council of the City, and that enough of them to make them of due weight in the Council" (048-007). This reform gave the [[eastcheaping-crafts|Lesser Crafts]] proportional power for the first time, shifting the balance of civic governance away from the wealthy great crafts and toward the broader citizenry.

## Powers and Functions

The Great Council held authority over the city's laws, trade, and defense. It worked in conjunction with the Porte, the executive body of the city, and the Burgreve, a military governor appointed to command the city's guard. The Burgreve was to be "chosen by all the Gilds of Craft, voting one with another, and not by the Great Council" (048-007) — a crucial provision that, given the numerical superiority of the Lesser Crafts, effectively placed the choice of Burgreve in their hands. [[sir-godrick|Sir Godrick of Longshaw]] was unanimously chosen as the first Burgreve under this new system (048-016).

## The Abolition of the Monarchy

The Great Council's most significant act was the abolition of the monarchy. After the [[city-king|City King]] fled to join the [[outland-king|Outland King]], a great Mote was called, and it was proposed that "since the City had a Porte and a Great Council, and a Burgreve under these, the office of King was little needed there" (049-007). The Council escheated the runaway King and decreed that all who had held of him should now hold of the Porte. "With little gainsaying, they did away with the office of King altogether, and most men felt the lighter-hearted therefor" (049-007).

## The Defense Against the Outland Threat

Under Sir Godrick's guidance, the Great Council approved the strategy for defending the city against the [[outland-king|Outland King]]'s invasion. When the two kings brought a great host the following year, Sir Godrick, "with the rede of the Great Council," allowed them to land twenty miles east of the city, then chose time and place for the decisive battle that destroyed the allied forces (049-010).

## Significance

The Great Council represents Morris's ideal of communal self-governance. Its evolution — from a body dominated by the great crafts to one representing all guilds, and ultimately to the institution that peacefully abolished monarchy — mirrors the historical development of medieval free cities and reflects Morris's socialist vision of democratic governance through guild organization.
