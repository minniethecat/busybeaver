# Hartshaw Knolls

A small stead on the western bank of the [[sundering-flood|Sundering Flood]], consisting of "two little knolls rising from the field, and betwixt them and about them a shaw of small wood" (005-016). It was the childhood home of [[elfhild|Elfhild]] and the site of her abduction by the [[black-skinners|Black Skinners]].

## Description

Hartshaw Knolls lay about seven miles down the water from [[wethermel|Wethermel]], on the western side of the Sundering Flood. As [[osberne|Osberne]] first beheld it on Yule morning, it appeared "friendly" to him — a premonition of his future connection to its inhabitant. The stead consisted of a house with surrounding garth, small and humble by the standards of the richer East Dale steads (005-017).

## Elfhild's Home

Hartshaw was the home of [[elfhild|Elfhild]], who lived there with her two aunts after the deaths of her parents. Her mother's sister, who died some years later, was buried at the church of [[allhallows|Allhallows]] the West, near the [[cloven-mote|Cloven Mote]] (031-012). Elfhild described the household as modest: "this little house which is hard hereby of Hartshaw Knolls" (034-010).

The proximity of Hartshaw to the Sundering Flood allowed Elfhild to make her secret journeys to the riverbank and to the cave where she met [[osberne|Osberne]] across the water. Her knowledge of the surrounding woods — "she knew so inwardly" (034-003) — was later crucial to her survival.

## The Raid and Destruction

During the raid of the [[black-skinners|Black Skinners]] on the West Dale, Hartshaw Knolls was attacked. The raiders fell upon the stead, and though the women of a neighboring stead at Longryggs escaped, at Hartshaw "the two women be missing" — Elfhild and her kinswoman the [[the-carline|Carline]] had disappeared (034-010). No bodies were found, and it was concluded that they had been carried off by the Skinners. The house and garth were left waste, and no newcomers came to dwell there afterward (035-001).

Osberne, returning from the battle, learned of the fate of Hartshaw with devastating grief. For years afterward, he searched for tidings of Elfhild, but Hartshaw remained desolate — a symbol of his loss.

## Symbolism

Hartshaw Knolls represents the fragile peace of the Dales before the incursion of outside forces. Its destruction by the Black Skinners marks the end of the idyllic childhood chapter of the narrative — the secret meetings across the Flood, the pipe and [[pipe-and-necklace|necklace]] gifts, the innocent love between Osberne and Elfhild. After Hartshaw falls, the story shifts from pastoral romance to the wider world of war and quest.
