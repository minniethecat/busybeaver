# The Pipe and Necklace

Two magical gifts bestowed upon [[elfhild|Elfhild]] by the [[dwarfs|Dwarf-folk]] who inhabited the cave in the cliffs above the [[sundering-flood|Sundering Flood]]. The gifts — a golden pipe and a jewelled necklace — were given in response to her earnest wishes and served both as her dearest possessions and as tokens of the supernatural forces that inhabited the land (007-009).

## The Pipe

The pipe was the first gift Elfhild received. One day, feeling lonely, she went into the cave and "fell to bewailing" that she had no music to comfort her. When she emerged onto the ledge, she found the pipe lying there:

> "there lay this fair thing on the ledge; and I took it up, and knew it for a pipe, for I had seen shepherds with such, and I set it to my lips and blew, and straightway it made the sweetest of music" (007-009)

The pipe's music had a magical quality. When Osberne first heard it drifting across the Sundering Flood, he found it "so sweet that he мог scarce forbear weeping" and felt compelled to follow the sound. The pipe's power was not merely aesthetic — its music seemed to have a calming effect on wild creatures and served as a ward against harm. Elfhild played it only when alone, hiding it from her aunts who would have "cried horror at them" (007-010), believing the cave-dwelling things brought evil upon the children of men.

## The Necklace

The second gift came when Elfhild wished for adornment: "I had nought to deck me with, neither of gold nor silver, as other maidens had" (007-010). She found a necklace of extraordinary beauty:

> "gold and emerald, gold and sapphire, gold and ruby; and it flashed in the sun, and Osberne thought it a fair toy indeed, but knew not that scarce a queen had got aught so fair in her treasure" (007-010)

The necklace represented both vanity and love — Elfhild decked herself with it when meeting Osberne, saying "it is a joy to show me unto thee so decked" (007-010). Like the pipe, she kept it hidden from her aunts, wearing it only in solitude or at her secret meetings with Osberne.

## Dwarf Origin

Both gifts were understood to be gifts of the [[dwarfs|Dwarf-folk]], the subterranean craftsmen whose [[dwarf-wrought-craft|Dwarf-wrought craft]] produced wonders like [[boardcleaver|Boardcleaver]]. The gifts appeared in response to wishes — a pattern suggesting that the Dwarfs, though feared by common folk, were beneficent toward those who approached them with innocent hearts. The cave itself was considered a place of peril by the Dale-dwellers, who warned of "the evil things that dwelt in the ness and all the ills they brought on the children of men" (007-010).

## Symbolic Significance

The pipe and necklace serve as symbols of the enchanted childhood world that Osberne and Elfhild inhabited before the wider world of war and quest intruded. When [[elfhild|Elfhild]] offers to give Osberne the pipe or necklace, he refuses — not from lack of desire, but from recognition that these gifts belong to her world and her protection. The gifts represent the special favor shown to Elfhild by the supernatural powers of the land, marking her as a chosen figure in the narrative.

After Hartshaw was raided by the [[black-skinners|Black Skinners]] and Elfhild was carried off, the fate of the pipe and necklace is not recorded — they vanished with her, becoming part of the mystery that Osberne spent years trying to unravel.
