# Brookside

Brookside is the chief manor and castle of the [[blue-knight|Blue Knight]] (Sir Mark), a fortified yet pleasant house situated east of the [[sundering-flood]]. It serves as a place of refuge for [[elfhild|Elfhild]] during the latter portion of the novel and becomes a site of reunion and preparation for the final conflict.

## Geography

Brookside lies east of the main theater of the Dales, "over-far" from certain strategic positions (064-005). Sir Godrick first points it out to Osberne from a distance, and when Osberne asks if it is Brookside, the Knight smiles and says: "Nay, said the Knight, it is a long way hence" (056-013). The castle is described as a "pleasant house" (060-003), surrounded by peaceful lands where "all seemed at peace about Brookside" (058-009).

## Role in the Story

### Elfhild's Refuge

Elfhild was brought to Brookside under the protection of the Blue Knight after her rescue from the [[black-skinners|Black Skinners]]. The Maiden was shown the castle from afar: "Lady, yonder is Brookside, my poor house, where I would have thee dwell" (057-009). She spent many merry days there in peace (058-009), and the household welcomed her arrival with great joy (061-002).

### The Blue Knight's Seat

Brookside was "the chiefest manor" of the Blue Knight (056-002). The castle maintained a garrison of "three score of men-at-arms under an ancient knight" (061-001), indicating its military significance. Despite being a lord of the [[barons-league|Barons' League]], the Blue Knight conducted his affairs from Brookside with honor, receiving a knight and two squires who rode to the castle as messengers (059-003).

### War and Reunion

When the Blue Knight determined to join the hosting of the Barons' League, he prepared to depart from Brookside (060-003). After the war's conclusion, Osberne came to Brookside and was reunited with Elfhild there — a moment of great joy and feasting at the castle (061-002).

The Lady of Brookside later sent "a half score of men-at-arms" to accompany Elfhild on her journey (062-001), demonstrating the castle's continued role as a base of support for the protagonists.
