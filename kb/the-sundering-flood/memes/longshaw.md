Longshaw is the great house and fortress of [[Sir Godrick]], located
"a long way south of the Dales" (039-007). It becomes a second home to
[[Osberne]] during the three years of the [[war-of-the-dales|Barons' War]], serving as the
military headquarters from which the [[dalesmen]] launch their campaigns
against the [[Barons' League]] and the [[Black Skinners]].

## Nature

A fortified manor-house surrounded by woodland — the "Shaw" from which
it takes its name. The great hall is large enough to host "all those
great men and war-farers" (045-011) at Yule feasts. The house is
defensible yet comfortable, a lordly seat rather than a grim castle.

## Geography

Longshaw lies south of the main Dales settlements, on the border of
hostile territory. "My chiefest house, which hight Longshaw, lieth a
long way south of the Dales" (039-007). The surrounding woodland, the
Shaw, gives cover and hunting grounds. The road to Longshaw passes
through contested marchlands where raiders and outlaws roam.

## History and Events

### Sir Godrick's Seat

Longshaw is the hereditary holding of Sir Godrick, who maintains it
as an independent power in the region. When Osberne first sees it, he
exclaims: "Lo, [[red-lad]], my House of Longshaw, and this is the Shaw
which we call ours" (044-003), presenting it with pride to his new
ally.

### Osberne's Sojourn

For "three years while Osberne abode with Sir Godrick" (045-002),
Longshaw is the base for the long war against the Barons. Osberne
becomes one of its commanders, and his cry of "The Red Lad for
Longshaw!" (046-007) becomes a battle-shout that rallies the men.

### The Barons' War

The hostile barons, outnumbering Longshaw's forces "three to Sir
Godrick's one" (045-008), attempt to besiege the fortress. Sir Godrick
declares "he will abide their coming to Longshaw" (045-006) and defeats
them through superior strategy. After the war, conquered barons "sent a
herald to Longshaw praying for peace" (045-010).

### Yule Feasts and Diplomacy

Longshaw hosts grand Yule celebrations where "noble Yule they kept at
Longshaw that year, with all those great men and war-farers" (045-011).
These feasts serve as both celebration and diplomatic council, binding
the Dales' allies together.
