Stephen the Eater is one of [[Osberne]]'s most loyal and enduring
companions. Named for his enormous capacity for food — "henceforth
is he called Stephen the Eater" (008-002) — he is a man of great
physical strength who fights at Osberne's side from the early
days at [[Wethermel]] through the wars at [[Eastcheaping]] and
[[Longshaw]].

## Identity

- **Name Origin**: Earned his epithet at his hiring feast, where
  he "played as good a part as if he had eaten nothing for three
  days" (008-004)
- **Role**: Osberne's personal retainer and man-at-arms
- **Loyalty**: Unwavering throughout the novel

## Biography

### Hiring at Wethermel

Stephen arrives at Wethermel as a hungry stranger and is taken
into service by Osberne. His first act — eating enormously at
supper — earns him his name, but Osberne sees his value: "he
was not rueing his bargain with Stephen the Eater" (008-006).

### Loyal Service

Stephen proves himself capable not just of eating but of
fighting and hard work. He helps Osberne with hunting, "wolves
and foxes" (020-001), and stands with him when Hardcastle
arrives to threaten Wethermel.

### The War at Eastcheaping

Stephen accompanies Osberne to [[Eastcheaping]] and fights in
the siege. His great voice cries out "from the marsh" (024-005)
during the battle. He is rewarded alongside Osberne after the
victory: "unto Osberne and Stephen the Eater in especial"
(028-002).

### Long Campaigns

Stephen follows Osberne to [[Longshaw]] and the southern wars.
He is among those who fight in the four corners of the field,
"and he lay long sick" (033-003) after a wound, but recovers
to continue serving.

### Friend and Herald

Beyond his martial role, Stephen serves as Osberne's
intelligence — "Stephen the Eater was somehow his friend in
more than meat and drink" (010-014). He brings urgent news:
"came Stephen the Eater hurrying into the hall at Wethermel"
(032-001).

## Relationships

- [[Osberne]] — his lord and friend
- [[Surly John]] — fellow retainer at Wethermel (contrast)
- [[Hardcastle]] — stood against him with Osberne
- [[Sir Medard]] — fought under his command at Eastcheaping
