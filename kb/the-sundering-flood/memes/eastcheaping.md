Eastcheaping is the commercial and civic heart of the Dales — "the
market town whither the Dalesmen had resort" (008-001). Governed by
[[porte-of-eastcheaping|the Porte]] and its council, the town hosts the wool market, maintains
a citizen militia, and serves as the gathering point for the
[[Dalesmen]] in times of war. It is here that [[Osberne]] first
proves himself as a warrior under [[Sir Medard]].

## Nature

A walled market town of considerable size and wealth. Eastcheaping
combines the functions of trade hub, civic center, and military
stronghold. Its walls are strong enough to withstand a Baron's siege,
and its market square serves as both commercial exchange and muster
ground.

## Geography

Eastcheaping sits at the crossroads of the Dales, accessible from
[[Wethermel]] in the north and [[Longshaw]] far to the south. The
town lies within a day or two's ride of the surrounding steadings,
making it the natural center for the widely-scattered Dales
settlements.

## History and Events

### The Wool Market

Dalesmen travel to Eastcheaping annually "to sell the autumn clip of
wool" (009-001). The market is the primary economic engine of the
Dales, drawing traders from the wider region. [[the-carline]] notes it
as "the market town whither the Dalesmen had resort" (008-001).

### Osberne's Early Visits

[[Osberne]] first visits Eastcheaping as a boy accompanying his
guardian Nicholas. Master Nicholas "would ride to Eastcheaping, and
he took Osberne with him" (020-001), exposing the young hero to the
wider world beyond Wethermel. These visits shape his growing
understanding of Dales politics and commerce.

### The Siege of Eastcheaping

The [[Barons' League]] lays siege to Eastcheaping. The town militia,
supplemented by hired captains and the Dalesmen volunteers, mounts
a defense. "Great cries of Eastcheaping! For the Porte! and A Medard,
A Medard!" (024-008) echo through the battle. Osberne distinguishes
himself in the fighting, earning the trust of [[Sir Medard]] and
the Porte.

### Alliance with the Dalesmen

After the siege is broken and the Barons retreat, "the men of the town
held a great feast" (027-017) to celebrate. The victory forges a
lasting alliance between Eastcheaping's citizen-soldiers and the
Dalesmen, establishing the military framework that later supports
[[Sir Godrick]]'s campaigns from Longshaw.

## Governance

The town is governed by the Porte, a civic council of leading
burgesses, and defended by the Captain of Eastcheaping. [[Sir Medard]]
serves as a key military leader and intermediary between the town
and the Dalesmen.
