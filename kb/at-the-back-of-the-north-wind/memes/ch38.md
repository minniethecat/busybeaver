CHAPTER XXXVIII. AT THE BACK OF THE NORTH WIND

[038-001]I DID not see [[diamond|Diamond]] for a week or so after this, and then he told me

[038-002]what I have now told you. I should have been astonished at his being

[038-003]able even to report such conversations as he said he had had with

[038-004][[north-wind|North Wind]], had I not known already that some children are profound in

[038-005]metaphysics. But a fear crosses me, lest, by telling so much about

[038-006]my friend, I should lead people to mistake him for one of those

[038-007]consequential, priggish little monsters, who are always trying to say

[038-008]clever things, and looking to see whether people appreciate them. When a

[038-009]child like that dies, instead of having a silly book written about him,

[038-010]he should be stuffed like one of those awful big-headed fishes you

[038-011]see in museums. But Diamond never troubled his head about what people

[038-012]thought of him. He never set up for knowing better than others. The

[038-013]wisest things he said came out when he wanted one to help him with some

[038-014]difficulty he was in. He was not even offended with [[nanny|Nanny]] and [[jim|Jim]] for

[038-015]calling him a silly. He supposed there was something in it, though he

[038-016]could not quite understand what. I suspect however that the other name

[038-017]they gave him, God's Baby, had some share in reconciling him to it.

[038-018]Happily for me, I was as much interested in metaphysics as Diamond

[038-019]himself, and therefore, while he recounted his conversations with North

[038-020]Wind, I did not find myself at all in a strange sea, although certainly

[038-021]I could not always feel the bottom, being indeed convinced that the

[038-022]bottom was miles away.

[038-023]“Could it be all [[dream|dreaming]], do you think, sir?” he asked anxiously.

[038-024]“I daren't say, Diamond,” I answered. “But at least there is one thing

[038-025]you may be sure of, that there is a still better love than that of the

[038-026]wonderful being you call North Wind. Even if she be a dream, the dream

[038-027]of such a beautiful creature could not come to you by chance.”

[038-028]“Yes, I know,” returned Diamond; “I know.”

[038-029]Then he was silent, but, I confess, appeared more thoughtful than

[038-030]satisfied.

[038-031]The next time I saw him, he looked paler than usual.

[038-032]“Have you seen your friend again?” I asked him.

[038-033]“Yes,” he answered, solemnly.

[038-034]“Did she take you out with her?”

[038-035]“No. She did not speak to me. I woke all at once, as I generally do when

[038-036]I am going to see her, and there she was against the door into the big

[038-037]room, sitting just as I saw her sit on her own doorstep, as white as

[038-038]snow, and her eyes as blue as the heart of an iceberg. She looked at me,

[038-039]but never moved or spoke.”

[038-040]“Weren't you afraid?” I asked.

[038-041]“No. Why should I have been?” he answered. “I only felt a little cold.”

[038-042]“Did she stay long?”

[038-043]“I don't know. I fell asleep again. I think I have been rather cold ever

[038-044]since though,” he added with a smile.

[038-045]I did not quite like this, but I said nothing.

[038-046]Four days after, I called again at the Mound. The maid who opened

[038-047]the door looked grave, but I suspected nothing. When I reached the

[038-048]drawing-room, I saw [[mrs-raymond|Mrs. Raymond]] had been crying.

[038-049]“Haven't you heard?” she said, seeing my questioning looks.

[038-050]“I've heard nothing,” I answered.

[038-051]“This morning we found our dear little Diamond lying on the floor of the

[038-052]big attic-room, just outside his own door--fast asleep, as we thought.

[038-053]But when we took him up, we did not think he was asleep. We saw

[038-054]that----”

[038-055]Here the kind-hearted lady broke out crying afresh.

[038-056]“May I go and see him?” I asked.

[038-057]“Yes,” she sobbed. “You know your way to the top of the tower.”

[038-058]I walked up the winding stair, and entered his room. A lovely figure, as

[038-059]white and almost as clear as alabaster, was lying on the bed. I saw at

[038-060]once how it was. They thought he was dead. I knew that he had gone to

[038-061]the back of the north wind.
