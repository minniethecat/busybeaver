## Overview

Mrs. Raymond is the wife of [[mr-raymond]], [[mr-raymond|the gentleman]] who becomes [[diamond|Diamond]]'s benefactor. Together with her husband, she provides a warm and welcoming home where [[diamond|Diamond]] spends his final years. The narrator notes the [[mr-raymond|Raymond]]s' hospitality: "From them I received the kindest attention" (035-090).

## Appearance and Character

Mrs. Raymond is portrayed through her actions rather than physical description, embodying maternal warmth and domestic devotion. Her character emerges through small, telling details — she confides that "she often rang her bell for [[diamond|Diamond]] to come and tell her something about her baby" (035-161), revealing both her reliance on the boy and the genuine [[love|affection]] she holds for him. She is a woman of feeling: when [[diamond|Diamond]] falls ill in the final chapter, the narrator observes that "Mrs. Raymond had been crying" (038-048), her tears testifying to the depth of her attachment. Unlike many adult characters in the novel who view [[diamond|Diamond]] with distant kindness, Mrs. Raymond treats him as part of the household, integrating him into the family's daily rhythms.

## Role in the Story

Mrs. Raymond appears in the novel's later chapters, after [[diamond|Diamond]] has come to live with the [[mr-raymond|Raymond]]s. She is portrayed as a caring and attentive presence who, along with her husband, ensures [[diamond|Diamond]] is well looked after. The narrator relates that "Mrs. Raymond confessed that she often rang her bell for [[diamond|Diamond]] to come and tell her something about her baby" (035-161), showing her fondness for the boy.

The [[mr-raymond|Raymond]]s' decision to take [[diamond|Diamond]] in comes after they "wanted to" care for him (035-020), a mutual decision between husband and wife that reflects their shared generosity. In the story's final chapter, when [[diamond|Diamond]] becomes ill, Mrs. Raymond is deeply affected: "I saw Mrs. Raymond had been crying" (038-048). Her grief at [[diamond|Diamond]]'s decline underscores the genuine [[love|affection]] the [[mr-raymond|Raymond]] household held for him.

## Relationships

Mrs. Raymond is married to [[mr-raymond]] and serves as a motherly figure to [[diamond]] during his time in their household. She is also connected to [[nanny]], who is cared for in the [[mr-raymond|Raymond]] home after her illness. Her relationship with [[diamond|Diamond]] is notably warm — she treats him not as a servant but as a cherished member of the household, and her grief at his final illness reveals the depth of the bond that formed between them.
