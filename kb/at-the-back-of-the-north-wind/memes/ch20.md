CHAPTER XX. DIAMOND LEARNS TO READ

[020-001]THE question of the tall gentleman as to whether [[diamond|Diamond]] could read or

[020-002]not set his father thinking it was high time he could; and as soon as

[020-003]old Diamond was suppered and bedded, he began the task that very [[darkness|night]].

[020-004]But it was not much of a task to Diamond, for his father took for his

[020-005]lesson-book those very rhymes his mother had picked up on [[the-sea|the sea-shore]];

[020-006]and as Diamond was not beginning too soon, he learned very fast indeed.

[020-007]Within a month he was able to spell out most of the verses for himself.

[020-008]But he had never come upon the poem he thought he had heard his mother

[020-009]read from it that day. He had looked through and through the book

[020-010]several times after he knew the letters and a few words, fancying he

[020-011]could tell the look of it, but had always failed to find one more like

[020-012]it than another. So he wisely gave up the search till he could really

[020-013]read. Then he resolved to begin at the beginning, and read them all

[020-014]straight through. This took him nearly a fortnight. When he had almost

[020-015]reached the end, he came upon the following verses, which took his fancy

[020-016]much, although they were certainly not very like those he was in search

[020-017]of.

[020-018]LITTLE BOY BLUE

[020-019]Little Boy Blue lost his way in a wood.

[020-020]Sing apples and cherries, roses and honey;

[020-021]He said, “I would not go back if I could,

[020-022]It's all so jolly and funny.”

[020-023]He sang, “This wood is all my own,

[020-024]Apples and cherries, roses and honey;

[020-025]So here I'll sit, like a king on my throne,

[020-026]All so jolly and funny.”

[020-027]A little snake crept out of the tree,

[020-028]Apples and cherries, roses and honey;

[020-029]“Lie down at my feet, little snake,” said he,

[020-030]All so jolly and funny.

[020-031]A little bird sang in the tree overhead,

[020-032]Apples and cherries, roses and honey;

[020-033]“Come and sing your [[music|song]] on my finger instead,

[020-034]All so jolly and funny.”

[020-035]The snake coiled up; and the bird flew down,

[020-036]And sang him the song of Birdie Brown.

[020-037]Little Boy Blue found it tiresome to sit,

[020-038]And he thought he had better walk on a bit.

[020-039]So up he got, his way to take,

[020-040]And he said, “Come along, little bird and snake.”

[020-041]And waves of snake o'er the damp leaves passed,

[020-042]And the snake went first and Birdie Brown last;

[020-043]By Boy Blue's head, with flutter and dart,

[020-044]Flew Birdie Brown with its song in its heart.

[020-045]He came where the apples grew red and sweet:

[020-046]“Tree, drop me an apple down at my feet.”

[020-047]He came where the cherries hung plump and red:

[020-048]“Come to my mouth, sweet kisses,” he said.

[020-049]And the boughs bow down, and the apples they dapple

[020-050]The grass, too many for him to grapple.

[020-051]And the cheeriest cherries, with never a miss,

[020-052]Fall to his mouth, each a full-grown kiss.

[020-053]He met a little brook [[song|singing]] a song.

[020-054]He said, “Little brook, you are going wrong.

[020-055]“You must follow me, follow me, follow, I say

[020-056]Do as I tell you, and come this way.”

[020-057]And the song-singing, sing-songing forest brook

[020-058]Leaped from its bed and after him took,

[020-059]Followed him, followed. And pale and wan,

[020-060]The dead leaves rustled as the water ran.

[020-061]And every bird high up on the bough,

[020-062]And every creature low down below,

[020-063]He called, and the creatures obeyed the call,

[020-064]Took their legs and their wings and followed him all;

[020-065]Squirrels that carried their tails like a sack,

[020-066]Each on his own little humpy brown back;

[020-067]Householder snails, and slugs all tails,

[020-068]And butterflies, flutterbies, ships all sails;

[020-069]And weasels, and ousels, and mice, and larks,

[020-070]And owls, and rere-mice, and harkydarks,

[020-071]All went running, and creeping, and flowing,

[020-072]After the merry boy fluttering and going;

[020-073]The dappled fawns fawning, the fallow-deer following,

[020-074]The swallows and flies, flying and swallowing;

[020-075]Cockchafers, henchafers, cockioli-birds,

[020-076]Cockroaches, henroaches, cuckoos in herds.

[020-077]The spider forgot and followed him spinning,

[020-078]And lost all his thread from end to beginning.

[020-079]The gay wasp forgot his rings and his waist,

[020-080]He never had made such undignified haste.

[020-081]The dragon-flies melted to mist with their hurrying.

[020-082]The mole in his moleskins left his barrowing burrowing.

[020-083]The bees went buzzing, so busy and beesy,

[020-084]And the midges in columns so upright and easy.

[020-085]But Little Boy Blue was not content,

[020-086]Calling for followers still as he went,

[020-087]Blowing his horn, and beating his drum,

[020-088]And crying aloud, “Come all of you, come!”

[020-089]He said to the shadows, “Come after me;”

[020-090]And the shadows began to flicker and flee,

[020-091]And they flew through the wood all flattering and fluttering,

[020-092]Over the dead leaves flickering and muttering.

[020-093]And he said to the wind, “Come, follow; come, follow,

[020-094]With whistle and pipe, and rustle and hollo.”

[020-095]And the wind wound round at his desire,

[020-096]As if he had been the gold cock on the spire.

[020-097]And the cock itself flew down from [[the-cathedral|the church]],

[020-098]And left the farmers all in the lurch.

[020-099]They run and they fly, they creep and they come,

[020-100]Everything, everything, all and some.

[020-101]The very trees they tugged at their roots,

[020-102]Only their feet were too fast in their boots,

[020-103]After him leaning and straining and bending,

[020-104]As on through their boles he kept walking and wending,

[020-105]Till out of the wood he burst on a lea,

[020-106]Shouting and calling, “Come after me!”

[020-107]And then they rose up with a leafy hiss,

[020-108]And stood as if nothing had been amiss.

[020-109]Little Boy Blue sat down on a stone,

[020-110]And the creatures came round him every one.

[020-111]And he said to the clouds, “I want you there.”

[020-112]And down they sank through the thin blue air.

[020-113]And he said to the sunset far in the West,

[020-114]“Come here; I want you; I know best.”

[020-115]And the sunset came and stood up on the wold,

[020-116]And burned and glowed in purple and gold.

[020-117]Then Little Boy Blue began to ponder:

[020-118]“What's to be done with them all, I wonder.”

[020-119]Then Little Boy Blue, he said, quite low,

[020-120]“What to do with you all I am sure I don't know.”

[020-121]Then the clouds clodded down till dismal it grew;

[020-122]The snake sneaked close; round Birdie Brown flew;

[020-123]The brook sat up like a snake on its tail;

[020-124]And the wind came up with a what-will-you wail;

[020-125]And all the creatures sat and stared;

[020-126]The mole opened his very eyes and glared;

[020-127]And for rats and bats and the world and his wife,

[020-128]Little Boy Blue was afraid of his life.

[020-129]Then Birdie Brown began to sing,

[020-130]And what he sang was the very thing:

[020-131]“You have brought us all hither, Little Boy Blue,

[020-132]Pray what do you want us all to do?”

[020-133]“Go away! go away!” said Little Boy Blue;

[020-134]“I'm sure I don't want you--get away--do.”

[020-135]“No, no; no, no; no, yes, and no, no,”

[020-136]Sang Birdie Brown, “it mustn't be so.

[020-137]“We cannot for nothing come here, and away.

[020-138]Give us some work, or else we stay.”

[020-139]“Oh dear! and oh dear!” with sob and with sigh,

[020-140]Said Little Boy Blue, and began to cry.

[020-141]But before he got far, he thought of a thing;

[020-142]And up he stood, and spoke like a king.

[020-143]“Why do you hustle and jostle and bother?

[020-144]Off with you all! Take me back to my mother.”

[020-145]The sunset stood at the gates of the west.

[020-146]“Follow me, follow me” came from Birdie Brown's breast.

[020-147]“I am going that way as fast as I can,”

[020-148]Said the brook, as it sank and turned and ran.

[020-149]Back to the woods fled the shadows like ghosts:

[020-150]“If we stay, we shall all be missed from our posts.”

[020-151]Said the wind with a voice that had changed its cheer,

[020-152]“I was just going there, when you brought me here.”

[020-153]“That's where I live,” said the sack-backed squirrel,

[020-154]And he turned his sack with a swing and a swirl.

[020-155]Said the cock of the spire, “His father's churchwarden.”

[020-156]Said the brook running faster, “I run through his garden.”

[020-157]Said the mole, “Two hundred worms--there I caught 'em

[020-158]Last year, and I'm going again next autumn.”

[020-159]Said they all, “If that's where you want us to steer for,

[020-160]What in earth or in water did you bring us here for?”

[020-161]“Never you mind,” said Little Boy Blue;

[020-162]“That's what I tell you. If that you won't do,

[020-163]“I'll get up at once, and go home without you.

[020-164]I think I will; I begin to doubt you.”

[020-165]He rose; and up rose the snake on its tail,

[020-166]And hissed three times, half a hiss, half a wail.

[020-167]Little Boy Blue he tried to go past him;

[020-168]But wherever he turned, sat the snake and faced him.

[020-169]“If you don't get out of my way,” he said,

[020-170]“I tell you, snake, I will break your head.”

[020-171]The snake he neither would go nor come;

[020-172]So he hit him hard with the stick of his drum.

[020-173]The snake fell down as if he were dead,

[020-174]And Little Boy Blue set his foot on his head.

[020-175]And all the creatures they marched before him,

[020-176]And marshalled him home with a high cockolorum.

[020-177]And Birdie Brown sang Twirrrr twitter twirrrr twee--

[020-178]Apples and cherries, roses and honey;

[020-179]Little Boy Blue has listened to me--

[020-180]All so jolly and funny.
