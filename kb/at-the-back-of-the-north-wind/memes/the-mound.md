# The Mound

**The Mound** is the country estate of [[mr-raymond|Mr. Raymond]], where [[diamond|Diamond]] serves as a page. The house stands upon an artificial hill, built originally as a hunting tower for Queen Elizabeth. It is the setting for the later chapters of *At the Back of [[north-wind|the North Wind]]*.

## Location and Architecture

The Mound stood upon a "little steep knoll, so smooth and symmetrical that it showed itself at once to be artificial" (035-002). It was built for Queen Elizabeth as a hunting tower — a place from the top of which one could "see the country for miles on all sides, and so be able to follow with your eyes the flying deer and the pursuing hounds and horsemen" (035-004).

The mound itself was constructed from materials dug from a nearby quarry-hole. The house was built of brick, with its foundations laid at the natural ground level, and the stones and earth of the mound heaped around and between them, "so that its great height should be well buttressed" (035-013).

## The Grounds

Near the house stood a cottage where [[diamonds-father|Joseph]] and his wife lived. It was a cottage "with a roof of thick thatch, which, in June and July, the wind sprinkled with the red and white petals it shook from the loose topmost sprays of the rose-trees climbing the walls" (035-016). [[diamond|Diamond]] initially lodged in a "nest under this thatch — a pretty little room with white muslin curtains" (035-019) before being installed as a page in the main house.

## Diamond's Room

When [[diamond|Diamond]] became [[mr-raymond|Mr. Raymond]]'s page, he was given a room at the top of one of the house's two towers. [[mrs-raymond|Mrs. Raymond]] led him "up and up the oval-winding stair" (035-033) to a tiny room with two windows commanding a view of the entire countryside. [[diamond|Diamond]] declared it "the grandest room in the house" because he would be "near the [[stars]], and yet not far from the tops of the trees" (035-038).

From his high window, [[diamond|Diamond]] looked down upon "a lake of green leaves, with glimpses of grass here and there at the bottom of it" (035-043). He once spotted a squirrel among the branches and called out, "my nest is built higher than yours" (035-046).

## Role in the Story

The Mound is where [[diamond|Diamond]] meets the narrator for the first time. The narrator, a tutor living on a neighbouring estate, finds [[diamond|Diamond]] sitting at the foot of "a great beech-tree" (035-057) reading a story by [[mr-raymond|Mr. Raymond]]. This meeting opens the final phase of [[diamond|Diamond]]'s story, in which his life at The Mound leads toward his final journey to the [[back-of-the-north-wind|back of the north wind]].
