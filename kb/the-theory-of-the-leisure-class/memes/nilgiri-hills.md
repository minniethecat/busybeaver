# Nilgiri Hills

The Nilgiri Hills are a mountain range in the Western Ghats of Tamil Nadu and Kerala, southern India. Historically inhabited by several indigenous tribal groups including the Todas, Kotas, Badagas, and Kurumbas.

## Role in Veblen's Analysis

Veblen referen[[christian-endeavor]]s the Nilgiri Hills in his opening ethnographic survey of cultural stages. The Todas, who inhabit this region, are cited as a prime example of the peaceable savage stage — small, sedentary communities without a defined leisure class, representing the earliest phase of cultural development in Veblen's evolutionary scheme. Veblen draws on 19th-century ethnographic accounts of the Todas to support his argument that [[leisure-class]] is not a universal feature of human society but an institution that emerges at a specific stage of cultural development. (001-006)
