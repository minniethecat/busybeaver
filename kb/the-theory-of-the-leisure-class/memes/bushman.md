# Bushman

The Bushman (San) groups of southern Africa are cited by Veblen, "more doubtfully," as possible instan[[christian-endeavor]]s of primitive savage culture — communities at the lowest rung of his cultural evolutionary scale, characterized by the absence of a leisure class and invidious class distinctions. Veblen's qualified citation reflects the methodological uncertainty in his ethnographic evidence. (001-006)

## Background

Veblen places the Bushman in the class of "savage groups" that represent the pre-barbarian cultural stage. His formulation — "more doubtfully, also some Bushman and Eskimo groups" — signals a double layer of caution: the groups themselves may be cases of degeneration from a higher cultural stage, and the ethnographic record for these specific groups was less reliable than for the Andaman Islanders and [[todas]]. Veblen's usage reflects late-19th-century anthropological categorizations, which grouped diverse African hunter-gatherer societies under broad racial-typological labels. (001-006)

The Bushman citation is notable for Veblen's admission of the fragility of the entire "primitive savagery" category. By flagging the Bushman as "more doubtfully" belonging to the class, Veblen implicitly acknowledges that his evolutionary schema rests on limited and potentially unreliable ethnographic data — yet proceeds to use the evidence instrumentally for his theoretical argument. (001-006)

## Role in Veblen's Analysis

The Bushman serve Veblen's argument by extending the geographical and cultural range of his primitive-savagery evidence. Their inclusion, however qualified, supports the cross-cultural breadth of his claim that societies without a leisure class existed across continents before the predatory-barbarian transition. The "more doubtfully" qualifier is analytically significant: it reveals that Veblen's evolutionary argument [[About]] [[leisure-class]] requires a baseline of pre-class, pre-predatory communities, and he is willing to marshal uncertain evidence to construct that baseline. (001-006) (001-008)
