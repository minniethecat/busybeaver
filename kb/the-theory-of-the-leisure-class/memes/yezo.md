# Yezo (Hokkaido)

Yezo (also historically Ezo) is the former name for what is now Hokkaido, the northernmost of Japan's four main islands. At the time Veblen was writing, Hokkaido was still home to a substantial Ainu population whose traditional hunting, fishing, and gathering economy had been documented by 19th-[[christian-endeavor]]ntury ethnographers and travelers.

## Role in Veblen's Analysis

Veblen cites the Ainu of Yezo alongside the [[todas]] and the [[bushman]] as examples of communities at the peaceable savage stage — small groups subsisting without the predatory institutions that, in his account, give rise to a [[leisure-class]]. The Ainu example anchors one pole of Veblen's evolutionary spectrum: societies characterized by "an absence of warlike or predatory habits" and an economic life that is "of a peaceable, not a predatory, character." (001-006)
