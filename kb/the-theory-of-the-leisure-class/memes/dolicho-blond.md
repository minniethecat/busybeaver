# Dolicho-Blond

Veblen's term for the long-headed, fair-haired European ethnic type, which he employs throughout [[Title-Page]] as an anthropological variable — a racial element whose inherited temperamental traits illuminate the persisten[[christian-endeavor]] of predatory habits in modern industrial society.

## Definition

The dolicho-blond is one of the "three main ethnic types" that Veblen identifies in European populations: "the dolichocephalic-blond, the brachycephalic-brunette, and the Mediterranean — disregarding minor and outlying elements of our culture" (009-005). In Veblen's racial typology, the dolicho-blond is characterized by a long-headed (dolichocephalic) cranial shape and fair (blond) coloring, and is associated with a distinctive cluster of temperamental traits relevant to his theory of predation and industrial development.

The dolicho-blond occupies a theoretically significant position because Veblen treats this ethnic type as the carrier of predatory traits in their most pronounced form. "These ethnic types differ in temperament," he writes, "the dolicho-blond type showing more of the characteristics of the [[predatory-temperament]] — or at least more of the violent disposition — than the brachycephalic-brunette type, and especially more than the Mediterranean" (009-008).

## Veblen's Treatment

### Predatory Temperament

Veblen argues that the dolicho-blond possesses an exceptional aptitude for predatory behavior and a corresponding resistance to industrial discipline. "It seems probable that the dolicho-blond type of European man is possessed of a greater facility for such reversion to barbarism than the other ethnic elements with which that type is associated in the Western culture" (008-011). When individuals of this type are exposed to a more primitive economic environment, they "quickly show evidence of reversion toward the spiritual features which characterize the predatory type" (008-011).

This association is not merely descriptive but explanatory: "It may be worth while to point out that the dolicho-blond type of European man seems to owe much of its dominating influence and its masterful position in the recent culture to its possessing the characteristics of predatory man in an exceptional degree" (009-018). The historical dominance of this ethnic element is, in Veblen's account, a consequence of its predatory traits — an argument that serves his broader thesis [[About]] the archaic foundations of modern institutions.

### Aesthetic Sensibility

In a more surprising application, Veblen invokes the dolicho-blond to explain aesthetic preferences. "The lawn unquestionably has an element of sensuous beauty... but it is, perhaps, more unquestionably beautiful to the eye of the dolicho-blond than to most other varieties of men" (006-022). He speculates that this preference reflects a pastoral ancestry: "This higher appreciation of a stretch of greensward in this ethnic element than in the other elements of the population, goes along with certain other features of the dolicho-blond temperament that indicate that this racial element had once been for a long time a pastoral people inhabiting a region with a humid climate" (006-022).

### Class Distribution

Veblen notes that the dolicho-blond element is not uniformly distributed across social classes: "In some cases even the lower classes are in large measure made up of the peace-disturbing dolicho-blond; while in others this ethnic element is found chiefly among the hereditary [[leisure-class]]" (010-010). The variable distribution of this type across class lines complicates any simple equation of ethnic type with social position.

## Significance

The dolicho-blond concept exemplifies the racial-anthropological framework within which Veblen's evolutionary argument operates — a framework that is now recognized as scientifically discredited but which was characteristic of late-nineteenth-century social science. While the racial typology itself is obsolete, the concept remains significant for understanding Veblen's argumentative strategy: he uses ethnic variation to demonstrate that the predatory temper is not equally distributed among populations, and that susceptibility to industrial discipline (or resistance to it) may have an inherited, pre-cultural component that complicates any simple environmental account of social development.
