# Martin

Martin is the ill-fated hired boy at [[green-gables|Green Gables]] whose unreliability and eventual desertion creates a pressing need for additional help on [[green-gables|the Cuthbert farm]] [002-019].

## Character Overview

Martin serves primarily as a plot device — his inadequacy as a farmhand sets in motion the events that bring [[davy-keith|Davy]] and [[dora-keith|Dora Keith]] to [[green-gables|Green Gables]]. [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]] describes him as "unreliable" [002-019], a judgment the narrative swiftly confirms when Martin departs [[humor|wit]]hout warning.

## Role in the Story

Martin's narrative function is to create the labor shortage at [[green-gables|Green Gables]] that prompts [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]] to consider taking in children for help. His unreliability is established from the first mention: "There's that unreliable Martin to look after them" [002-019]. His departure to "see the carnival" [003-102] leaves [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]] [[humor|wit]]hout assistance, forcing her to seek alternative arrangements — which leads directly to the arrival of the Keith twins.

## Relationships

Martin works for [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla Cuthbert]] at [[green-gables|Green Gables]]. His relationship [[humor|wit]]h his employer is characterized by frustration: "Martin has never come back yet. Perhaps he's too frightened to" [003-102]. [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]]'s observation that "Martin's just like all the rest of the boys" [003-110] reveals her dim view of hired help in general, [[humor|wit]]h Martin as the representative example.

## Key Scenes

- **The Assessment**: [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]] declares Martin unreliable as she contemplates needing help on the farm [002-019]
- **The Departure**: Martin leaves [[green-gables|Green Gables]] to attend a traveling carnival, abandoning his duties [003-102]
- **The Aftermath**: [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]] notes that Martin is "too frightened to come back" after his desertion [003-102]
- **The Judgment**: [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]] philosophically observes that "Martin's just like all the rest of them" [003-110], using him as a cautionary example

## Character Analysis

Martin is less a character than a narrative catalyst — his unreliability serves to justify the plot development that brings the Keith twins to [[green-gables|Green Gables]]. He represents a type that [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]] has learned to expect: the transient farm boy whose commitment to work dissolves at the first sign of entertainment. His brief presence in the novel underscores the practical challenges of rural farm life and [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]]'s need for dependable help.
