## Overview

Prince Edward Island is the maritime Canadian province where the entire [[anne-shirley|Anne]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]] narrative unfolds. The novel opens [[humor|wit]]h a description of an [[avonlea|Avonlea]] farmhouse on the Island [001-048], establishing the setting as intrinsic to the story's character. The Island's landscape — its red sandstone soil, rolling hills, sea breezes, and pastoral valleys — permeates every aspect of the narrative.

## Role in Story

The Island is not merely a backdrop but shapes the lives and identities of its inhabitants. [[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]], an orphan from Nova Scotia, finds her true [[belonging|home]] on Prince Edward Island. The novel explicitly notes that being "brought up elsewhere than in Prince Edward Island" carries a certain disadvantage of perspective [002-095].

The landscape actively participates in key moments: a crisp September day on the hills epitomizes the Island's seasonal beauty [007-021]. The climate itself becomes a point of local pride — certain weather phenomena are claimed to have "never been known in Prince Edward Island" [034-105], reflecting the inhabitants' deep identification [[humor|wit]]h their [[belonging|home]].

## Geographic Character

The Island's geography defines its communities: small farms nestled among rolling hills, villages connected by red dirt roads, and the ever-present proximity to the Gulf of St. Lawrence. This landscape fosters the tight-knit rural [[community|society]] that provides the novel's social fabric — a world where everyone knows their [[community|neighbors]]' business and the rhythms of farm life set the calendar.
