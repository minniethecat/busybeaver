# The Sunday School

The Sunday School at the [[avonlea-presbyterian-church|Avonlea Presbyterian Church]] is a central institution in the religious and moral [[teaching|education]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]'s children. It appears throughout *[[anne-shirley|Anne]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]* as the setting for [[davy-keith|Davy Keith]]'s memorable theological inquiries and as a marker of the [[neighbor|community]]'s commitment to raising its young in faith and discipline.

## Description

The Avonlea Sunday School operates as part of the Presbyterian Church's [[teaching|education]]al mission. Children attend weekly to learn hymns, catechism questions, Bible verses, and moral lessons under the supervision of adult volunteers from the congregation. The program serves all the children of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]'s Presbyterian families, from the youngest to those approaching confirmation age.

[[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla Cuthbert]] takes the religious [[teaching|education]] of [[davy-keith|Davy]] and [[dora-keith|Dora]] seriously, ensuring they attend Sunday School and learn their lessons at [[belonging|home]]: "[[davy-keith|Davy]] and [[dora-keith|Dora]] were taught a hymn, a catechism question, and two Bible verses every Sunday." The Sunday School thus extends [[avonlea-presbyterian-church|the church]]'s reach into the domestic sphere, [[humor|wit]]h parents and guardians reinforcing the week's lessons. [026-175]

## Activities

The Sunday School's primary activities include hymn singing, Scripture reading, catechism recitation, and moral [[teaching|instruction]]. The children are expected to behave [[humor|wit]]h appropriate solemnity — a standard that [[davy-keith|Davy Keith]] consistently tests. Willie White's complaint about wearing "patched pants to Sunday School all summer" reveals the social dimension of attendance: Sunday School is not only about religious [[teaching|education]] but also about [[neighbor|community]] visibility and the performance of respectability.

The Sunday School also serves as a disciplinary institution. When children misbehave during the week, the Sunday School provides a context for moral correction. [[davy-keith|Davy]]'s various "repentances" — or lack thereof — are calibrated against the standards he learns in Sunday School. The institution's emphasis on repentance and forgiveness becomes the framework through which [[davy-keith|Davy]]'s more outrageous exploits are interpreted and managed by the adults around him.

## Significance

The Sunday School is significant as an institution that bridges the sacred and the social. It provides children [[humor|wit]]h religious [[teaching|instruction]] while also serving as a site of [[neighbor|community]] identity and social regulation. For [[davy-keith|Davy Keith]], the Sunday School is the backdrop against which his un[[tradition|convention]]al theology plays out — his questions about repentance, punishment, and divine justice are filtered through the lens of what he has learned (and what he has resisted [[education|learning]]) in Sunday School.

The institution also reflects the broader role of [[avonlea-presbyterian-church|the church]] in [[avonlea|Avonlea]] life. The Presbyterian Church is not merely a [[belonging|place]] of worship but a comprehensive [[neighbor|community]] framework that educates the young, cares for the needy, and structures the moral life of the village. The Sunday School is [[avonlea-presbyterian-church|the church]]'s primary mechanism for transmitting values to the next generation.
