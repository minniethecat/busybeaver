# Mrs. Lynde

[[mrs-rachel-lynde|Mrs. Rachel Lynde]] is the Cuthberts' nearest neighbor and [[avonlea|Avonlea]]'s most dedicated observer of other people's affairs — a woman whose sharp tongue is matched by a fundamentally kind heart [001-112].

## Character Overview

Mrs. Lynde is a fixture of [[avonlea|Avonlea]] life, known for her insatiable curiosity about everyone's business and her willingness to share her opinions freely. [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla Cuthbert]] notes that "if you so much as loosened a shutter in the dead of the night and sneezed, Mrs. Lynde would ask you the next day how your cold was" [002-056]. Her role as [[neighbor|community]] watchdog is both resented and relied upon by the village.

## Role in the Story

Mrs. Lynde serves as a barometer of [[avonlea|Avonlea]] opinion and a catalyst for several plot developments. She is present at key moments: she [[humor|wit]]nesses the sale of the Millar farm [003-095], she comments on the Keith twins' arrival at [[green-gables|Green Gables]] [005-029], and she provides [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]] [[humor|wit]]h advice — whether solicited or not [007-001]. Her pronouncements carry weight in the [[neighbor|community]]; "Mrs. Lynde says" is a phrase that precedes many a village judgment [005-029], [007-063].

## Relationships

Mrs. Lynde's primary relationship is [[humor|wit]]h [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla Cuthbert]], her neighbor and frequent confidante — though [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]] is often the recipient rather than the source of information [003-095]. With [[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]], she shares a relationship of mutual appraisal: "Mrs. Lynde was complaining the other day that I wasn't growing up to be as pretty as she expected" [005-005]. [[anne-shirley|Anne]], in turn, occasionally fails to hold her tongue about the comparison, blurting out "So is Mrs. Lynde" [004-077] before [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]] sharply corrects her. Her marriage to [[thomas-lynde|Thomas Lynde]] [[belonging|place]]s her at the center of [[avonlea|Avonlea]] domestic life.

## Key Scenes

- **The Pig-in-a-Poke**: Mrs. Lynde investigates the prospect of [[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]] coming to [[green-gables|Green Gables]], interrogating [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]] about "taking a pig in a poke" [001-112]
- **The Kitchen Floor Report**: She tells [[anne-shirley|Anne]] that [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]] expects the kitchen floor to be scrubbed every second day [001-121]
- **The Millar Sale Witness**: "Mrs. Lynde had seen most of the transaction from her window" [003-096] as the events of the twins' arrival unfold
- **The [[avonlea-village-improvement-society|A.V.I.S.]] Observer**: Mrs. Lynde comes to give [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]] advice about the [[avonlea-village-improvement-society|Village Improvement Society]] [005-105], noting that she "doesn't know whether to approve" [007-063]

## Character Analysis

Mrs. Lynde embodies [[avonlea|Avonlea]]'s social conscience — the force of [[neighbor|community]] scrutiny that can be both oppressive and essential. Her character arc across *[[anne-shirley|Anne]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]* shows the softening of her sharp edges: though she remains the village's most vocal critic, her genuine care for her [[community|neighbors]] becomes increasingly evident. The narrative treats her [[humor|wit]]h [[love|affection]]ate [[laughter|humor]], recognizing that a Mrs. Lynde is necessary to the fabric of small-town life.
