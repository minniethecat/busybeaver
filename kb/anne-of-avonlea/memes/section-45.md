[045-001] Two hours later [[anne-shirley|Anne]] and [[charlotta-the-fourth|Charlotta the Fourth]] came down the lane again.

[045-002] [[gilbert-blythe|Gilbert]] had gone to [[grafton|West Grafton]] on an errand and [[diana-barry|Diana]] had to keep an

[045-003] engagement at [[belonging|home]]. Anne and Charlotta had come back to put things in

[045-004] order and lock up the little stone house. The garden was a pool of late

[045-005] golden sunshine, with butterflies hovering and bees booming; but the

[045-006] little house had already that indefinable air of desolation which

[045-007] always follows a festivity.

[045-008] “Oh dear me, don’t it look lonesome?” sniffed Charlotta the Fourth, who

[045-009] had been crying all the way home from the station. “A wedding ain’t

[045-010] much cheerfuller than a funeral after all, when it’s all over, Miss

[045-011] Shirley, ma’am.”

[045-012] A busy evening followed. The decorations had to be removed, the dishes

[045-013] washed, the uneaten delicacies packed into a basket for the delectation

[045-014] of Charlotta the Fourth’s young brothers at home. Anne would not rest

[045-015] until everything was in apple-pie order; after Charlotta had gone home

[045-016] with her plunder Anne went over the still rooms, feeling like one who

[045-017] trod alone some banquet hall deserted, and closed the blinds. Then she

[045-018] locked the door and sat down under the silver poplar to wait for

[045-019] Gilbert, feeling very tired but still unweariedly thinking “long, long

[045-020] thoughts.”

[045-021] “What are you thinking of, Anne?” asked Gilbert, coming down the walk.

[045-022] He had left his horse and buggy out at the road.

[045-023] “Of [[miss-lavendar-lewis|Miss Lavendar]] and [[stephen-irving|Mr. Irving]],” answered Anne dreamily. “Isn’t it

[045-024] beautiful to think how everything has turned out . . . how they have

[045-025] come together again after all the years of separation and

[045-026] misunderstanding?”

[045-027] “Yes, it’s beautiful,” said Gilbert, looking steadily down into Anne’s

[045-028] uplifted face, “but wouldn’t it have been more beautiful still, Anne,

[045-029] if there had been NO separation or misunderstanding . . . if they had

[045-030] come hand in hand all the way through life, with no memories behind

[045-031] them but those which belonged to each other?”

[045-032] For a moment Anne’s heart fluttered queerly and for the first time her

[045-033] eyes faltered under Gilbert’s gaze and a rosy flush stained the

[045-034] paleness of her face. It was as if a veil that had hung before her

[045-035] inner consciousness had been lifted, [[generosity|giving]] to her view a revelation of

[045-036] unsuspected feelings and realities. Perhaps, after all, romance did not

[045-037] come into one’s life with pomp and blare, like a gay knight riding

[045-038] down; perhaps it crept to one’s side like an old friend through quiet

[045-039] ways; perhaps it revealed itself in seeming prose, until some sudden

[045-040] shaft of illumination flung athwart its pages betrayed the rhythm and

[045-041] the music, perhaps . . . perhaps . . . [[romance|love]] unfolded naturally out of a

[045-042] beautiful friendship, as a golden-hearted rose slipping from its green

[045-043] sheath.

[045-044] Then the veil dropped again; but the Anne who walked up the dark lane

[045-045] was not quite the same Anne who had driven gaily down it the evening

[045-046] before. The page of girlhood had been turned, as by an unseen finger,

[045-047] and the page of womanhood was before her with all its charm and

[045-048] mystery, its pain and gladness.

[045-049] Gilbert wisely said nothing more; but in his silence he read the

[045-050] history of the next four years in the light of Anne’s remembered blush.

[045-051] Four years of earnest, happy work . . . and then the guerdon of a

[045-052] useful knowledge gained and a sweet heart won.

[045-053] Behind them in the garden the little stone house brooded among the

[045-054] shadows. It was lonely but not forsaken. It had not yet done with

[045-055] [[ambition|dreams]] and [[humor|laughter]] and the [[laughter|joy]] of life; there were to be future

[045-056] summers for the little stone house; meanwhile, it could wait. And over

[045-057] the river in purple durance the echoes bided their time.
