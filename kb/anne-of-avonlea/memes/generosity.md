# Generosity

The spirit of open-handed giving that Montgomery presents as a quiet virtue — captured in the observation that "she's so much 'before' that [[generous|generosity]] never catches up [[humor|wit]]h" (008-021) her, suggesting that [[generous|generosity]] can be outpaced by eagerness, but not diminished by it.

## Definition

Generosity in *[[anne-shirley|Anne]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]* is portrayed as a natural disposition rather than a calculated virtue. Montgomery does not spend long passages moralizing about giving; instead, [[generous|generosity]] manifests in small, concrete acts: a neighbor sharing preserves, a teacher giving extra time to a struggling pupil, a friend offering encouragement when it is most needed. The novel suggests that true [[generous|generosity]] is almost invisible — it does not announce itself.

The sole explicit mention — "she's so much 'before' that [[generous|generosity]] never catches up [[humor|wit]]h her" (008-021) — is characteristic of Montgomery's approach. Generosity is treated as something that follows naturally from a forward-looking, outward-facing personality. It is not a separate virtue to be cultivated but an expression of a life oriented toward others.

## Examples in the Text

**Generosity Outpaced (Chapter 8).** The observation that "she's so much 'before' that [[generous|generosity]] never catches up [[humor|wit]]h her" (008-021) frames [[generous|generosity]] not as a discrete act but as an orientation — the person who is always looking ahead, always thinking of what can be done for others, moves faster than [[generous|generosity]] can follow. This is Montgomery's way of suggesting that the spirit of giving exceeds any individual gift.

## Thematic Significance

Generosity in *[[anne-shirley|Anne]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]* is woven into the fabric of [[neighbor|community]] life rather than singled out for special attention. The novel depicts a world where giving is expected — not as an [[duty|obligation]] but as the natural expression of [[neighbor|neighborliness]]. [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s [[generous|generosity]] toward her pupils, [[mrs-lynde|Mrs. Lynde]]'s [[generous|generosity]] [[humor|wit]]h her time and opinion, [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]]'s [[generous|generosity]] in raising an orphan — these are not heroic acts but the ordinary texture of [[avonlea|Avonlea]] life.
