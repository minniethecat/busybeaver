# Orchard Slope

**Orchard Slope** is the [[barry-family|Barry family]] farm in [[avonlea|Avonlea]], on [[prince-edward-island|Prince Edward Island]]. Situated across the fields and through the [[haunted-wood|Haunted Wood]] from [[green-gables|Green Gables]], it is the [[belonging|home]] of [[diana-barry|Diana Barry]], [[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]]'s [[kindred-spirits|bosom friend]] — making it the second most significant domestic space in [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s world.

## Character Overview

Orchard Slope is, after [[green-gables|Green Gables]], the most frequently visited [[belonging|home]] in _[[anne-shirley|Anne]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]_. It represents warmth, welcome, and the particular intimacy of a neighbor's house that one enters [[humor|wit]]hout ceremony. [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s constant crossings — to tell news, to seek [[diana-barry|Diana]], to share an evening — map a geography of friendship onto the [[avonlea|Avonlea]] landscape.

## Key References

### The Path Between

> [016-171] Anne, on her way to Orchard Slope, met Diana, bound for Green Gables…

The rhythm of visits between the two [[belonging|home]]s is captured in this moment of symmetry: [[anne-shirley|Anne]] going to Orchard Slope meets [[diana-barry|Diana]] coming from it [016-171]. The path between the two farms is as worn [[humor|wit]]h use as any road in [[avonlea|Avonlea]], a physical trace of the friendship that defines [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s young womanhood.

### Carrying News

> [022-076] Anne ran across to Orchard Slope that evening to tell the news…

When [[anne-shirley|Anne]] has news — whether [[laughter|joy]]ful or burdensome — her first instinct is to cross to Orchard Slope [022-076]. The farm is the repository of her confidences, the [[belonging|place]] where she is most fully understood.

### The Evening Garden

> [024-156] to Orchard Slope.

A bare directional note — [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s movement toward Orchard Slope in the evening [024-156] — is characteristic of the novel's quiet anchoring of its emotional geography in physical crossings.

### The Final Visits

> [043-072] …evening when she went over to Orchard Slope by the…

In the novel's closing passages, [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s visits to Orchard Slope continue [043-072], a rhythm that will, the reader understands, persist as long as both [[anne-shirley|Anne]] and [[diana-barry|Diana]] call [[avonlea|Avonlea]] [[belonging|home]].

## Geography

Orchard Slope lies adjacent to [[green-gables|Green Gables]], separated by fields and [[haunted-wood|the Haunted Wood]]. The path between the two farms is the most traveled private route in the novel. The farm's name — Orchard Slope — suggests the gentle hillside orchards characteristic of [[prince-edward-island|Prince Edward Island]]'s rural landscape.

## Cultural Significance

Orchard Slope matters in Montgomery's fiction not for any dramatic event that occurs there, but for what it represents: the companionable domestic space where [[anne-shirley|Anne]] is not a teacher, not a caretaker, not a figure of moral guidance, but simply a friend. It is the architecture of intimacy, the [[belonging|home]]-that-is-not-one's-own where one is nonetheless entirely at [[belonging|home]].
