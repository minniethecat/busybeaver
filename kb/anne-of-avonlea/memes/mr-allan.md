# Mr. Allan

The Reverend Mr. Allan is the Presbyterian minister of [[avonlea|Avonlea]], serving the spiritual needs of the congregation alongside his wife [[mrs-allan|Mrs. Allan]]. His presence in the novel — and his eventual departure for a [[charlottetown|Charlottetown]] church — shape the religious life of the village [[neighbor|community]].

## Character Overview

Mr. Allan is the settled minister of the [[avonlea-presbyterian-church|Avonlea Presbyterian Church]], residing at the manse [[humor|wit]]h his wife and their new baby. His salary is a matter of [[neighbor|community]] discussion and occasional dispute, reflecting the practical challenges of supporting a minister in a rural parish. He is well-respected by the congregation and serves as a moral and spiritual authority in [[community|village life]]. [001-110]

## Role in the Story

Mr. Allan's primary narrative role is as the spiritual leader of the [[avonlea|Avonlea]] [[neighbor|community]]. During Sunday services, his prayers provide a backdrop for one of the novel's most memorable comic moments — when a series of piercing shrieks interrupts his prayer, caused by [[davy-keith|Davy Keith]]'s pet mouse escaping during the service. Mr. Allan's composure during this disruption speaks to his character: patient, long-suffering, and well-ac[[tradition|custom]]ed to the unpredictable [[nature-appreciation|nature]] of rural ministry. [010-133] [010-143]

He also serves as a source of wisdom and authority. When various [[neighbor|community]] issues arise — the [[avonlea-village-improvement-society|A.V.I.S.]] road controversy, matters of discipline and morality — the question "I wonder if Mr. Allan could do anything" is raised, reflecting the [[neighbor|community]]'s [[faith|trust]] in his judgment. [019-156]

His role culminates in his call to a [[charlottetown|Charlottetown]] church, a significant event that reshapes the [[avonlea|Avonlea]] congregation's leadership and sets the stage for the novel's closing chapters. [041-049]

Mr. Allan maintains his [[pride|dignity]] throughout the disruption, demonstrating the patience required of a rural minister. [010-133] [010-143]

**The Salary [[diana-barry|Di]]scussion**: The [[neighbor|community]] debates Mr. Allan's salary — whether it is adequate and whether the congregation is meeting its [[duty|obligation]]s. **The [[charlottetown|Charlottetown]] Call**: Mr. Allan receives and accepts a call to a church in [[charlottetown|Charlottetown]], a decision that prompts both celebration of his advancement and genuine sorrow at his departure from [[avonlea|Avonlea]]. [041-049]

## Character Analysis

Mr. Allan represents the institutional church in [[avonlea|Avonlea]] — not as a distant authority but as an integral and human presence. His departure for [[charlottetown|Charlottetown]] signals change: the end of an era for the congregation and a reminder that even the most settled institutions are subject to transition. Montgomery treats him [[humor|wit]]h consistent respect, presenting a clergyman who is neither sanctimonious nor remote but simply a good man doing his best in a demanding vocation.
