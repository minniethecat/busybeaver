## Character Overview

[[miss-lavendar-lewis|Miss Lavendar Lewis]] is a middle-aged spinster living in a quaint stone house called [[echo-lodge|Echo Lodge]] in [[grafton|Grafton]]. She is described as peculiar, romantic, and dreamy — "Sweet Miss Lavendar" to those who know her. Her appearance is youthful despite her age; she wears old-fashioned gowns of lavender and keeps her hair in girlish curls [030-092].

[[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]] first meets Miss Lavendar when she and [[diana-barry|Diana]] become lost on the road to [[spencervale|Spencervale]] and stumble upon [[echo-lodge|Echo Lodge]] [030-027]. The meeting proves fateful, as [[anne-shirley|Anne]] soon becomes a close confidante and ultimately the catalyst for reuniting Miss Lavendar [[humor|wit]]h her long-lost [[romance|love]].

## Role in Story

Miss Lavendar serves as a romantic counterpoint to [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla Cuthbert]]. While [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]] chose duty over [[romance|love]], Miss Lavendar's story demonstrates that [[love|romance]] can be reclaimed even after decades of waiting. Her [[romance|courtship]] [[humor|wit]]h [[stephen-irving|Stephen Irving]], rekindled after a twenty-five-year estrangement caused by a foolish quarrel [029-076], forms one of the novel's most poignant subplots.

[[anne-shirley|Anne]] plays a pivotal role when she recognizes [[stephen-irving|Stephen Irving]] as Miss Lavendar's former sweetheart and contrives to bring them together [030-053]. Miss Lavendar's eventual marriage and departure from [[echo-lodge|Echo Lodge]] mark a bittersweet transition — happiness found, but at the cost of leaving behind the enchanted world she built in [[loneliness|solitude]].

## Character Analysis

Miss Lavendar embodies the novel's theme that [[romance|romantic love]] need not be confined to youth. Her "spellbound" existence at [[echo-lodge|Echo Lodge]] — preserved in lavender and lace, waiting for a prince who seemed never to come [030-053] — reflects both the beauty and the limitation of living in imagination.

Unlike [[anne-shirley|Anne]], who channels her romanticism into ambition and growth, Miss Lavendar had allowed hers to become a form of arrested development. Her eventual marriage to [[stephen-irving|Stephen Irving]] represents not merely a happy ending but a release from the enchanted prison she had built for herself. The stone house at [[echo-lodge|Echo Lodge]], charming as it is, was also a [[belonging|place]] of waiting — and her departure from it signals her re-entry into the forward-moving current of life.
