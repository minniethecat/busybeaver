# Spencervale

**Spencervale** is a rural settlement on [[prince-edward-island|Prince Edward Island]], ten miles from [[avonlea|Avonlea]]. It appears in _[[anne-shirley|Anne]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]_ as the destination of a day-long expedition by [[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]] and [[diana-barry|Diana Barry]], undertaken in search of a coveted blue platter.

## Character Overview

Spencervale embodies the "far-away" [[belonging|place]] in Montgomery's geography — far enough for a journey to be an occasion, near enough to be reached and returned from in a day. Its role in the narrative is specific and functional: a destination that provides [[anne-shirley|Anne]] and [[diana-barry|Diana]] [[humor|wit]]h one of their last shared adventures before the responsibilities of adulthood fully claim them.

### The Blue Platter

[[anne-shirley|Anne]] learns that a blue platter — exactly the thing to delight [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]] — is to be found at [[wesley-keyson|Wesley Keyson]]'s farm in Spencervale [024-163]. Her immediate decision to go after it speaks to her characteristic blend of spontaneity and [[love|devotion]].

[[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s declaration [024-165] sets in motion a journey that becomes more than an errand: it is an expedition, a lark, a day of freedom on the open road [[humor|wit]]h her dearest friend.

### The Journey

> [025-002] …pedition. It was ten miles to Spencervale and the day was not promising…

The ten-mile journey [025-002] is undertaken despite an unpromising day, a measure of [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s determination and the significance she invests in the platter as a gift. The distance is rendered in Montgomery's precise geographical terms — ten miles, a specific and substantial distance for a horse-drawn excursion.

> [025-014] …isome drive the girls reached Spencervale and turned down the lane…

The arrival at Spencervale [025-014] is a small triumph — the girls have reached their destination, and the farm that holds the blue platter lies before them.

> [025-019] of a Spencervale farm came out to the fence or…

The farmers of Spencervale are sketched as curious, neighborly onlookers [025-019] — the rural world noting the passage of two young women on an unusual errand.

## Geography

Spencervale lies ten miles from [[avonlea|Avonlea]] by road. It is a farming settlement, close enough for a day trip but far enough to require a dedicated expedition. Its distance establishes it as one of the outermost points in [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s immediate geographical world.

## Cultural Significance

The Spencervale expedition is one of the last purely companionable adventures between [[anne-shirley|Anne]] and [[diana-barry|Diana]] before marriage and maturity reshape their friendship. Montgomery uses the journey — its distance, its uncertainty, its modest triumph — to capture a moment of transition: the girls are young women now, but still capable of setting off on a whim to retrieve a blue platter for the woman who raised one of them.
