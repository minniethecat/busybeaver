# Priscilla Grant

Priscilla Grant is a young teacher from the [[avonlea|Avonlea]] area whose appointment to the [[carmody|Carmody]] school becomes an early benchmark in [[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]]'s professional [[ambition|aspiration]]s — and who later reappears as a visitor at [[echo-lodge|Echo Lodge]] [002-145].

## Character Overview

Priscilla Grant enters the narrative as a figure of mild professional [[jealousy|envy]]: she has been given the [[carmody|Carmody]] school, a position [[anne-shirley|Anne]] had hoped to secure [002-145]. Her later appearance at [[echo-lodge|Echo Lodge]] shows her as "golden and fair in silk attire" [028-118], a polished young woman whose social graces contrast [[humor|wit]]h [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s more earnest demeanor.

## Role in the Story

Priscilla's primary narrative function is as a benchmark for [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s teaching career. When [[anne-shirley|Anne]] learns that the [[carmody|Carmody]] school has been given to Priscilla Grant, it crystallizes her disappointment and fuels her determination [002-145]. Her reappearance at [[echo-lodge|Echo Lodge]] — standing on the doorstep [[humor|wit]]h [[miss-lavendar-lewis|Miss Lavendar Lewis]] [028-118] — connects her to the novel's romantic subplot involving [[stephen-irving|Stephen Irving]]. The [[avonlea-village-improvement-society|A.V.I.S.]] improvers had "an eye on" the [[carmody|Carmody]] school grounds [021-032], showing that Priscilla's domain is a subject of village-wide interest.

Priscilla Grant's relationship [[humor|wit]]h [[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]] is one of indirect [[jealousy|rivalry]] — not personal animosity, but a shared ambition that [[belonging|place]]s them in competition.
