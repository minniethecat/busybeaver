## Character Analysis

Hester Gray exemplifies Montgomery's technique of layering history into landscape. Though dead for thirty years, she haunts the novel not as a ghost but as a memory embedded in the physical world — in her garden, her stone, her story. Through Hester, Montgomery suggests that [[avonlea|Avonlea]]'s beauty is deepened by the human lives that have passed through it, and that the past is never truly gone so long as someone remembers.
