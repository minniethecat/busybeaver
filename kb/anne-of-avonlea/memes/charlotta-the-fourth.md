# Charlotta the Fourth

[[miss-lavendar-lewis|Miss Lavendar Lewis]]'s devoted maid — a young woman whose "fearfully wide" smiles and fierce [[faith|loyalty]] to her "adored mistress" make her one of Montgomery's most memorable minor characters.

## Character Overview

Charlotta the Fourth is the latest in a line of Charlottas who have served at [[echo-lodge|Echo Lodge]], and her [[love|devotion]] to [[miss-lavendar|Miss Lavendar]] is absolute. She greets visitors [[humor|wit]]h "her very widest smile . . . and Charlotta's smiles *were* fearfully wide" — loving [[anne-shirley|Anne]] and [[diana-barry|Diana]] "for the sake of her adored mistress as well as for their own." Her comic enthusiasm is entirely genuine; there is no irony in Charlotta, only wholehearted [[love|devotion]].

She represents the [[pride|dignity]] of service freely given — a minor character who, in a few brief appearances, communicates the novel's belief that [[romance|love]] and [[faith|loyalty]] can flourish in any station.

## Role in the Story

Charlotta is primarily comic relief — her smiles and her [[love|devotion]] provide moments of warmth and [[laughter|humor]]. But she also serves a thematic function: her [[faith|loyalty]] to [[miss-lavendar|Miss Lavendar]] demonstrates that the lonely woman at [[echo-lodge|Echo Lodge]] is not un[[romance|love]]d, and her [[laughter|joy]] at [[miss-lavendar|Miss Lavendar]]'s eventual happiness is a measure of the [[romance|love]] [[miss-lavendar|Miss Lavendar]] inspires.

## Relationships

**[[miss-lavendar-lewis|Miss Lavendar Lewis]]** is Charlotta's "adored mistress" — the woman she serves [[humor|wit]]h wholehearted [[love|devotion]].

**[[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]]** and **[[diana-barry|Diana Barry]]** are visitors Charlotta [[romance|love]]s "for the sake of her adored mistress."

## Key Scenes

**The Widest Smile (Chapter 30).** Charlotta greets [[anne-shirley|Anne]] [[humor|wit]]h "her very widest smile" — a description whose italics convey both the magnitude and the endearing quality of her enthusiasm.

## Character Analysis

Charlotta the Fourth is a small gem of comic characterization. In a novel full of psychologically complex major characters, she reminds the reader that Montgomery's gifts extend to the briefest sketches — a maid whose smile defines her and whose [[faith|loyalty]] dignifies her.
