# Anne Shirley

The imaginative, red-haired orphan turned [[avonlea|Avonlea]] schoolteacher — now a young woman [[education|learning]] to balance her romantic sensibility [[humor|wit]]h the practical demands of adult [[duty|responsibility]], [[neighbor|community]] membership, and the care of others.

## Character Overview

Anne Shirley, now sixteen going on seventeen, has transformed from the talkative, dreamy orphan who arrived at [[green-gables|Green Gables]] by mistake into a certified teacher en[[faith|trust]]ed [[humor|wit]]h the [[teaching|education]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]'s children. Her defining trait remains her imagination — the faculty that enables her to see beauty in a dismal schoolroom, to empathize [[humor|wit]]h difficult [[community|neighbors]], and to recognize "[[friendship|kindred spirits]]" wherever they appear (001-186). But Anne of [[avonlea|Avonlea]] is a young woman [[humor|wit]]h new responsibilities: she must manage a classroom, help raise the orphaned twins [[davy-keith|Davy]] and [[dora-keith|Dora]], support [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]] as her eyesight weakens, and navigate her changing relationships [[humor|wit]]h [[gilbert-blythe|Gilbert Blythe]] and [[diana-barry|Diana Barry]].

Anne's first appearance in this novel establishes her mature voice — still romantic, still prone to vivid expression, but now tempered by experience and a growing awareness of duty (001-009).

## Role in the Story

Anne's primary role is as the [[avonlea|Avonlea]] schoolteacher, a position that tests her patience, her philosophy of kindness, and her capacity for firmness. Her campaign to win over the defiant [[anthony-pye|Anthony Pye]] — "[[patience|Patience]] and kindness will surely win him" (007-009) — occupies much of the early narrative and establishes the novel's central moral argument: that [[kindness|gentleness]] is more effective than severity.

Beyond the classroom, Anne serves as guardian to [[davy-keith|Davy]] and [[dora-keith|Dora Keith]] after their mother's death, a [[duty|responsibility]] she accepts [[humor|wit]]h characteristic determination: "it is our duty to take them" (009-140). The twins, particularly the mischievous [[davy-keith|Davy]], provide both comic relief and opportunities for Anne's moral [[teaching|education]].

Anne also catalyzes the [[avonlea-village-improvement-society|Avonlea Village Improvement Society]] (007-059), drives the [[love|romance]] plot between [[miss-lavendar|Miss Lavendar]] and [[stephen-irving|Stephen Irving]] (031-064), and serves as confidante to [[diana-barry|Diana Barry]] as her friend navigates first [[romance|love]] (043-118). In all these roles, Anne is the active center around which the novel's multiple plots orbit.

## Relationships

**[[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla Cuthbert]]** is Anne's adoptive mother and moral anchor. Their relationship has deepened since *Anne of [[green-gables|Green Gables]]* into one of mutual [[love|devotion]], [[humor|wit]]h Anne now increasingly the caretaker as [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]]'s health declines.

**[[gilbert-blythe|Gilbert Blythe]]** is Anne's intellectual companion and unacknowledged romantic future. Their relationship in this novel is characterized by shared professional experience — both are teachers — and mutual respect rather than overt [[love|romance]]. ## Character Analysis

Anne in *[[avonlea|Avonlea]]* is a character in transition — from girl to woman, from dreamer to doer, from orphan to [[neighbor|community]] pillar. Montgomery charts this development not as the abandonment of imagination but as its refinement into a practical and moral force. Anne's imagination now serves duty rather than escaping it: she uses it to forgive, to beautify, and to understand.

Her arc is one of integration — the romantic and the practical, the imaginative and the dutiful, the individual and the communal. By the novel's end, Anne has become a be[[romance|love]]d teacher, a [[faith|trust]]ed neighbor, and a young woman whose "wonderful ambitions" (041-088) are being fulfilled not in some distant future but in the daily labor of her [[avonlea|Avonlea]] life.
