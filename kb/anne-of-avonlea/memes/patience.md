## Definition

Patience in *[[anne-shirley|Anne]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]* is the capacity to endure difficulty, delay, or provocation [[humor|wit]]hout complaint. It is presented not as passive resignation but as an active virtue — a tool for shaping outcomes and a quality of character that distinguishes the mature from the impulsive.

[[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla Cuthbert]] articulates the practical approach to patience when confronting a stubborn situation: "Well, we'll see what patience and kindness can do" [009-016]. ## Thematic Significance

Patience functions as a bridge between the novel's competing values: romantic [[ambition|aspiration]] and practical duty. [[anne-shirley|Anne]] must learn patience in her teaching career, in waiting for her own future, and in dealing [[humor|wit]]h the irrepressible Keith twins. The virtue is never presented as easy — it must be actively cultivated and sometimes wisely abandoned.

The novel suggests that patience [[humor|wit]]hout kindness is mere endurance, while kindness [[humor|wit]]hout patience is sentimentality. Together they form the foundation of mature character.
