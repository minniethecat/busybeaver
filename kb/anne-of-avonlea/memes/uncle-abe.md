# Uncle Abe

Uncle Abe Andrews is [[avonlea|Avonlea]]'s self-styled weather prophet — a colorful elder whose predictions of rain and storm occasion much village amusement and occasional vindication [022-073].

## Character Overview

Uncle Abe is one of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]'s most memorable minor characters, known throughout the village for his confident weather predictions. His pronouncements are treated [[humor|wit]]h [[love|affection]]ate skepticism by the [[neighbor|community]]: "they just asked Uncle Abe what it was going to do to-morrow and expected the opposite" [018-138]. Despite his poor track record, he remains undaunted in his prophetic role [018-139].

## Role in the Story

Uncle Abe serves as a source of comic relief and local color in [[avonlea|Avonlea]] life. His weather predictions are a running thread of village conversation, [[humor|wit]]h characters frequently referencing what Uncle Abe has said about impending weather [022-073], [044-090]. His most notable moment comes when one of his predictions proves correct — a major storm that he had foretold materializes, [[generosity|giving]] him "his hour of triumph" [034-147]. The storm that made his reputation as a weather prophet [034-175] becomes a village landmark event, referenced weeks afterward [035-006].

## Relationships

Uncle Abe operates on the periphery of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]'s social circle. [[diana-barry|Diana Barry]] mentions him as a weather authority [044-090], and [[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]] acknowledges the [[neighbor|community]]'s ambivalent attitude toward his predictions [044-092], [044-093]. His relationship [[humor|wit]]h the broader village is one of gentle mockery mixed [[humor|wit]]h grudging respect — "folks won't laugh at Uncle Abe's predictions any more" after the great storm [034-135].

## Key Scenes

- **The Prophecy**: Uncle Abe predicts rain when [[anne-shirley|Anne]] hopes for fine weather for the school picnic [022-073]
- **The Great Storm**: His prediction of a major storm comes true, wading through floodwaters to the blacksmith's shop during his finest hour [034-042], [034-147]

- **Post-Storm Reputation**: Weeks after the storm, [[anne-shirley|Anne]] notes the changed weather as evidence of Uncle Abe's established credibility [035-006]

## Character Analysis

Uncle Abe represents [[avonlea|Avonlea]]'s folk wisdom and the village's tolerance for eccentricity. His persistence in the face of frequent inaccuracy speaks to a particular kind of small-town character — valued for entertainment as much as for genuine utility. The [[neighbor|community]]'s [[love|affection]]ate mockery ("Poor old Uncle Abe felt rather indignant" [033-126]) reveals [[avonlea|Avonlea]]'s capacity to accommodate individual quirkiness [[humor|wit]]hin its social fabric.
