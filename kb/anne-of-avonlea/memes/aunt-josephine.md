# Aunt Josephine

Aunt Josephine is [[diana-barry|Diana Barry]]'s great-aunt — a wealthy, somewhat intimidating elder whose visits to [[orchard-slope|Orchard Slope]] test the social graces of the younger generation and provide [[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]] [[humor|wit]]h opportunities for creative hospitality [024-164].

## Character Overview

Aunt Josephine represents the world beyond [[avonlea|Avonlea]] — [[charlottetown|Charlottetown]] [[community|society]], inherited wealth, and the expectations of an older generation. Her possession of a willow-ware platter [024-168] signals her access to finer domestic goods than those available in rural [[avonlea|Avonlea]]. [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s determination to "amuse your Aunt Josephine [[humor|wit]]h the 'strange eventful history'" [026-032] reveals both the pressure the matriarch's visits impose and [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s characteristic solution: storytelling.

## Role in the Story

Aunt Josephine's visits to [[orchard-slope|Orchard Slope]] create moments of social performance for the novel's young protagonists. Aunt Josephine is connected to [[avonlea|Avonlea]] through her great-niece [[diana-barry|Diana Barry]] and the Barry family at [[orchard-slope|Orchard Slope]].
