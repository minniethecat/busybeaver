# Mr. Spencer

Mr. Spencer is an [[avonlea|Avonlea]] farmer of some standing, known for his influential example in agricultural improvement and his conservative views on certain village innovations [009-029], [027-147].

## Character Overview

Mr. Spencer occupies a position of respect in [[avonlea|Avonlea]]'s agricultural [[neighbor|community]]. He is a farmer whose opinion carries weight — when he leads, "the other farmers follow Mr. Spencer's good example" [027-147]. His disapproval of certain of [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s teaching methods [009-029] shows him as a figure of traditional village authority, while his later role in agricultural improvement demonstrates his progressive side.

## Role in the Story

Mr. Spencer appears at two distinct points in the narrative, serving different functions. Early on, his disapproval of [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s teaching approach represents the conservative scrutiny under which she operates as a young teacher [009-029]. Later, he emerges as a positive force when his adoption of agricultural improvements inspires emulation: "the other farmers follow Mr. Spencer's good example" [027-147]. His relationship [[humor|wit]]h [[judson-parker|Judson Parker]] — "Parker knew that Mr. Spencer looked somewhat askance at him" [020-045] — hints at village social dynamics and the weight of Spencer's opinion.

## Relationships

Mr. Spencer's relationship [[humor|wit]]h [[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]] is one of evaluative oversight — as a [[neighbor|community]] elder, his approval or disapproval matters to her professional standing [009-029]. With [[judson-parker|Judson Parker]], he maintains a posture of disapproval [020-045], suggesting his role as a moral arbiter in village affairs. His influence over other farmers [027-147] marks him as an informal [[neighbor|community]] leader.

## Key Scenes

- **The [[judson-parker|Judson Parker]] Tension**: Mr. Spencer looks askance at [[judson-parker|Judson Parker]] [020-045], revealing village social dynamics
- **The Good Example**: When Mr. Spencer adopts agricultural improvements, "the other farmers follow Mr. Spencer's good example" [027-147]

## Character Analysis

Mr. Spencer embodies the dual [[nature-appreciation|nature]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]'s established figures — conservative in some matters (teaching methods), progressive in others (agricultural improvement). His influence flows not from official position but from personal standing, a pattern typical of small-[[neighbor|community]] leadership. The mention that Mr. Spencer's disapproval was considered important enough to relay to [[anne-shirley|Anne]] [009-029] illustrates the village's hierarchy of opinion and the social pressures operating on a young teacher.
