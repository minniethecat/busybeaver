# Charlottetown

Charlottetown is the provincial capital of [[prince-edward-island|Prince Edward Island]], referenced in *[[anne-shirley|Anne]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]* as the urban center beyond the rural world of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]. It represents commerce, publishing, ecclesiastical authority, and the broader world that lies outside the village's pastoral boundaries.

## Description

Charlottetown appears in the novel not through direct description — no character visits the city on-page — but through its influence as a distant center of authority and opportunity. It is the seat of the newspaper that publishes [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s short story, the location of the presbytery that calls [[mr-allan|Mr. Allan]] to a new position, and the destination to which [[mr-harrison|Mr. Harrison]] sends a cow by rail. The city's presence is felt in [[avonlea|Avonlea]] primarily through its newspapers, its ecclesiastical decisions, and its commercial reach. [018-137] [033-102]

## Location

Charlottetown is distant enough from [[avonlea|Avonlea]] to require travel by rail — [[mr-harrison|Mr. Harrison]] ships a cow there — yet close enough that its newspapers circulate in the village and its Presbyterian authorities oversee [[avonlea|Avonlea]]'s church affairs. The Charlottetown dailies arrive in [[avonlea|Avonlea]] and carry news, advertisements, and [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s early literary efforts. [001-086] [003-125] [023-080]

## Significance

Charlottetown functions as the symbolic "outside world" in a novel deeply rooted in rural [[community|village life]]. Its key significances include:

**Literary Aspiration**: [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s short story "Averil's Atonement" is published in the Charlottetown *Daily Enterprise*, marking her first literary success. The city's newspapers represent the gateway to a writing career beyond the village. [033-102]

**Ecclesiastical Authority**: The Presbyterian call to [[mr-allan|Mr. Allan]] comes from Charlottetown, demonstrating how decisions made in the city reshape [[avonlea|Avonlea]]'s [[neighbor|community]] life. The church hierarchy headquartered there exercises direct influence over the villagers' spiritual leadership. [041-049]

**Commerce and Movement**: [[mr-harrison|Mr. Harrison]] ships his cow to Charlottetown by rail, and the city's banks and businesses handle transactions for [[avonlea|Avonlea]] residents. The farm that becomes [[mr-harrison|Mr. Harrison]]'s was previously owned by someone who "moved to Charlottetown," highlighting the city as a destination for rural emigration. [001-086] [003-125]
