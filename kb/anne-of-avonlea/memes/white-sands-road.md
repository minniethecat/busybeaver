# White Sands Road

**White Sands Road** is the route branching from [[avonlea|Avonlea]] toward the settlement of [[white-sands|White Sands]] on [[prince-edward-island|Prince Edward Island]]. It appears in _[[anne-shirley|Anne]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]_ as a canvassing route for the [[avonlea-village-improvement-society|Avonlea Village Improvement Society]] and as the location of [[lorenzo-white|Lorenzo White]]'s farm.

## Character Overview

White Sands Road is one of several named roads that radiate from [[avonlea|Avonlea]] into the surrounding countryside — each road defining a direction, a [[neighbor|community]], and a distinct set of residents. In the novel, it is primarily associated [[humor|wit]]h the [[avonlea-village-improvement-society|A.V.I.S.]] subscription drive and [[humor|wit]]h [[lorenzo-white|Lorenzo White]]'s moment of paternal extravagance.

## Key References

### The Canvassing Route

> [007-118] for the White Sands road, and Jane and Gertie for the…

During the organization of the [[avonlea-village-improvement-society|A.V.I.S.]] canvassing effort, the White Sands road is assigned to one team while the [[newbridge|Newbridge]] road is given to [[anne-shirley|Anne]] and [[diana-barry|Diana]] [007-118]. Montgomery's careful mapping of who goes where reinforces the novel's sense of a thoroughly inhabited landscape — every road has its residents, its character, its canvassers.

### Lorenzo White's Farm

> [008-121] "But he lives on the White Sands road and it's quite a distance out of our way," protested Diana.

When [[anne-shirley|Anne]] insists on visiting [[lorenzo-white|Lorenzo White]] immediately — before his new-father euphoria wears off — [[diana-barry|Diana]] objects that he lives on the White Sands road, "quite a distance out of our way" [008-121]. [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s insistence pays off: the detour yields the largest single [[avonlea-village-improvement-society|A.V.I.S.]] subscription. The road, in this moment, is both obstacle and opportunity — a practical reality of rural canvassing that [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s strategic thinking overcomes.

## Geography

White Sands Road runs from the [[avonlea|Avonlea village]] center toward the settlement of [[white-sands|White Sands]]. It passes several Sloane [[belonging|home]]steads [008-109] and [[lorenzo-white|Lorenzo White]]'s farm [008-121]. Its distance from the main [[avonlea|Avonlea]] circuit makes it a commitment for canvassers, distinguishing it from the more convenient routes.

## Cultural Significance

In Montgomery's fiction, roads are not merely infrastructure — they are the armature of rural social life. White Sands Road, like the [[newbridge|Newbridge]] road, organizes the [[neighbor|community]]: it determines who canvasses where, who visits whom, whose farm is "out of the way." The road's modest narrative role reflects the quiet centrality of geographical thinking in [[avonlea|Avonlea]] life.
