# Mr. Bell

Mr. Bell is an [[avonlea|Avonlea]] farmer and neighbor of [[green-gables|Green Gables]] whose pasture lies beyond [[haunted-wood|the Haunted Wood]] [024-155], a practical man known for selling livestock and his notably lengthy sermons [028-074], [038-042].

## Character Overview

Mr. Bell is one of several [[avonlea|Avonlea]] farmers who populate the background of the novel's [[community|village life]]. He enters the narrative through two distinct channels: as a livestock dealer from whom [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]] purchases a calf [001-162], and as a figure of gentle ecclesiastical complaint — a man whose sermons test the patience of his congregation [028-074], [038-042].

## Role in the Story

Mr. Bell appears tangentially in the novel's fabric of [[community|village life]]. His farm connects geographically to [[green-gables|Green Gables]] via [[haunted-wood|the Haunted Wood]], making him part of the landscape [[anne-shirley|Anne]] traverses [024-155]. His role as a livestock seller is noted when [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]] buys a calf from him [001-162], later referenced as "the one he bought from Mr. Bell" [003-121].

## Relationships

Mr. Bell's most notable relationship [[humor|wit]]h the main cast is commercial — [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla Cuthbert]] purchases livestock from him [001-162], [003-121]. He is also referenced in connection [[humor|wit]]h [[mr-allan|Mr. Allan]]'s ministry, when "Mr. Bell says he's a minister of the old school" [038-042], suggesting Mr. Bell is among the [[avonlea-presbyterian-church|Presbyterian congregation]] and a man whose opinion on church matters carries some weight in the [[neighbor|community]].

## Key Scenes

- **The Calf Purchase**: [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]] buys a calf from Mr. Bell, a transaction noted in the novel's opening chapter [001-162]
- **The Pasture Crossing**: [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s route across Mr. Bell's pasture corner, through [[haunted-wood|the Haunted Wood]], is referenced as a familiar pathway [024-155]
- **The Sermon Worry**: [[anne-shirley|Anne]] "wishes devoutly that Mr. Bell were not quite so highly regarded as an elder" when contemplating the length of Sunday services [028-074]
- **The Old-School Ministry**: Mr. Bell offers his assessment of [[mr-allan|Mr. Allan]]'s preaching style [038-042]

## Character Analysis

Mr. Bell represents the solid, unremarkable [[avonlea|Avonlea]] citizen whose presence is felt through practical interactions rather than dramatic scenes. His dual appearance as farmer and church elder reflects the integration of agricultural and religious life in rural [[prince-edward-island|Prince Edward Island]]. The [[laughter|humor]] at his expense — wishing his sermons were shorter — is gentle and [[love|affection]]ate, characteristic of the novel's treatment of village characters.
