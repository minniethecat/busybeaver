## Character Overview

Gertie Pye is a schoolgirl in [[avonlea|Avonlea]] and a member of the Pye family, which includes her more prominent sister Josie Pye. The Pye family is a well-established [[avonlea|Avonlea]] household, and Gertie appears in several school and [[neighbor|community]] contexts throughout the narrative.

## Role in Story

Gertie Pye participates in the [[avonlea-village-improvement-society|Avonlea Village Improvement Society]], where she was appointed to a committee [007-111]. She is also noted for her fashionable appearance — on one occasion she "swept in, pompadoured," drawing attention [[humor|wit]]h her hairstyle [019-077]. The narrative remarks that "for once in her life Gertie Pye made all the sensation she desired" [019-086], suggesting an ongoing [[ambition|aspiration]] for social notice that typically went unfulfilled.

Among her schoolmates, Gertie is referenced as a peer whose style others compare themselves to. [[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]] mentions Gertie's and Julia Bell's approach to hair as an example when discussing personal appearance [021-123].
