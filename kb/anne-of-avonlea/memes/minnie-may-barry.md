# Minnie May Barry

**Minnie May Barry** is the youngest daughter of the [[barry-family|Barry family]] of [[orchard-slope|Orchard Slope]] in [[avonlea|Avonlea]], and the little sister of [[diana-barry|Diana Barry]], [[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]]'s [[kindred-spirits|bosom friend]].

## Character Overview

[[minnie-may|Minnie May]] appears only through the reported speech of other characters in _[[anne-shirley|Anne]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]_ — her own voice is never heard directly. She serves as a narrative device: her childish questions and concerns are relayed by [[diana-barry|Diana]] and [[davy-keith|Davy Keith]] to illuminate [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s evolving philosophy on faith, beauty, and goodness.

The glimpses we get of [[minnie-may|Minnie May]] reveal a child preoccupied [[humor|wit]]h the same questions that once consumed [[anne-shirley|Anne]] herself — what one wears in heaven, whether being pretty matters, and who she might marry when she grows up. Through these fragments, Montgomery sketches a younger generation of [[avonlea|Avonlea]] girls who look up to [[anne-shirley|Anne]] and [[diana-barry|Diana]] as models of young womanhood.

## Role in Story

[[minnie-may|Minnie May]] functions as a minor recurring presence — a child on the periphery of [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s world whose questions, reported by others, allow [[anne-shirley|Anne]] to articulate the values she has cultivated since her own girlhood. Like [[dora-keith|Dora Keith]], [[minnie-may|Minnie May]] represents the younger generation of [[avonlea|Avonlea]] children who are shaped by the example [[anne-shirley|Anne]] sets as their teacher.
