# Avonlea Presbyterian Church

The Presbyterian congregation that serves as [[avonlea|Avonlea]]'s spiritual center — a [[neighbor|community]] institution whose minister and his wife provide moral leadership and whose pews contain the full spectrum of village character.

## Overview

The Avonlea Presbyterian Church is the religious heart of the [[neighbor|community]]. Under the leadership of [[mr-allan|Mr. Allan]] and the quiet influence of his wife, the church provides not only Sunday services but a framework of moral and social cohesion for the village.

[[mrs-allan|Mrs. Allan]]'s conversations [[humor|wit]]h [[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]] — in which they discuss "[[ambition|dreams]] of friendship and ambition" (021-136) — exemplify the church's role as a space for genuine intellectual and spiritual exchange, not merely formal observance.

## Role in Avonlea

The church functions as both religious institution and social hub. It is where the [[neighbor|community]] gathers, where moral standards are articulated and modeled, and where relationships across age and social position are formed. The Allans represent a benevolent, intellectually engaged Christianity that harmonizes [[humor|wit]]h [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s imaginative sensibility.
