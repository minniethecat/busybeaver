## Character Overview

Mr. Harmon Andrews is an [[avonlea|Avonlea]] farmer and the father of [[jane-andrews|Jane Andrews]]. He is a respected member of the [[neighbor|community]] whose [[belonging|home]] serves as a meeting [[belonging|place]] for the [[avonlea-village-improvement-society|Avonlea Village Improvement Society]] [019-049]. His opinions carry weight in the neighborhood.

## Role in Story

Mr. Harmon Andrews hosts the [[avonlea-village-improvement-society|A.V.I.S.]] meeting at his [[belonging|home]], indicating his standing in the [[neighbor|community]] and willingness to support [[avonlea-village-improvement-society|civic improvement]] [019-049]. He offers advice to his daughter [[jane-andrews|Jane]], counseling her on practical matters [020-081]. His movements around the district are noted by observant [[community|neighbors]] like [[mrs-lynde|Mrs. Lynde]] [024-085], and he makes a dramatic late entrance to an event, "clattering up the hill" in his wagon [034-039].
