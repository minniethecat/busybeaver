# Mary Joe

Young Mary Joe is a household helper connected to the [[paul-irving|Paul Irving]] household who makes a memorable impression through [[davy-keith|Davy Keith]]'s comedic reports. Though she never appears directly in narrated scenes, her presence is vividly conveyed through dialogue and anecdote, making her one of the novel's more colorful minor figures.

Mary Joe is a young woman who helps at the Irving household near [[grafton|Grafton]]. ## Role in the Story

Mary Joe appears in the narrative solely through the reports of [[davy-keith|Davy]] and occasionally [[paul-irving|Paul Irving]]. Her role is to provide domestic presence at the Irving household, overseeing the boys when [[mrs-irving|Mrs. Irving]] is occupied. [[davy-keith|Davy]]'s accounts of interacting [[humor|wit]]h Mary Joe — negotiating for extra food, eavesdropping on her conversations, and testing the limits of her authority — provide some of the novel's lightest comedic moments. ## Character Analysis

Mary Joe functions as a background figure who enriches the novel's texture [[humor|wit]]hout driving the plot. Her primary narrative purpose is twofold: she provides a domestic anchor for the Irving household scenes, and she serves as a straight-man for [[davy-keith|Davy Keith]]'s comic adventures. Through [[davy-keith|Davy]]'s reports of her, Montgomery sketches a warm, slightly indulgent young woman — firm enough to enforce household rules, but soft-hearted enough to be won over by a charming little boy.
