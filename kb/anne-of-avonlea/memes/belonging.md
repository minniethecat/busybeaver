## Definition

Belonging in *[[anne-shirley|Anne]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]* is the experience of being accepted, rooted, and claimed by a place and its people. For [[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]], who arrived at [[green-gables|Green Gables]] as an orphan [[humor|wit]]h no history, the journey toward be[[envy|longing]] is the emotional core of her story, continuing even after her formal adoption into the Cuthbert household.

## Examples

The theme of be[[envy|longing]] appears in the novel's imagery and character arcs. [[anne-shirley|Anne]] imagines a dryad "be[[envy|longing]] to" a particular tree [027-033], expressing her own [[envy|longing]] through the language of myth and [[nature-appreciation|nature]] — every being, she feels, should have its proper place. The packing of "be[[envy|longing]]s" [036-117] becomes more than a logistical detail; it represents the material evidence of a life rooted in a [[neighbor|community]], the accumulation of objects that testify to being somewhere long enough to have possessions that matter.

## Thematic Significance

Belonging is the novel's most fundamental emotional question. [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s fear of not truly be[[envy|longing]] — of being an outsider despite her adoption — drives her ambition to prove herself worthy of [[green-gables|Green Gables]] and [[avonlea|Avonlea]]. Her teaching career, her friendships, and her role in the [[neighbor|community]] all serve to answer the question: does she belong here?

The novel answers affirmatively but subtly: be[[envy|longing]] is not achieved in a single moment but accumulated through years of small acts — teaching Sunday school, joining the Improvement Society, being known by one's [[community|neighbors]]. Belonging is less a status than a practice.

## Contrasting Perspectives

- **Belonging vs. Independence**: [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s desire to belong must coexist [[humor|wit]]h her fierce individuality
- **Belonging vs. Ownership**: The novel distinguishes between being claimed by a place and claiming it — the former is be[[envy|longing]], the latter possession
- **Belonging vs. Conformity**: True be[[envy|longing]] does not require [[anne-shirley|Anne]] to suppress her imagination or ambitions
