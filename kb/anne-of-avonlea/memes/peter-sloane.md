## Character Overview

Peter Sloane is a long-established resident of [[avonlea|Avonlea]] who serves the [[neighbor|community]] both as a landowner and as a local magistrate. His old house becomes the residence of [[mr-harrison|Mr. Harrison]], and his judicial capacity is called upon in matters of local dispute.

## Role in Story

Peter Sloane enters the narrative through his property — his old house is rented to [[mr-harrison|Mr. Harrison]], the new neighbour whose arrival causes considerable stir at [[green-gables|Green Gables]] and throughout [[avonlea|Avonlea]] [002-072]. The fact that "Peter has hired" a man to help [[humor|wit]]h farm work indicates his status as a landowner of some means [002-072].

His role as magistrate is referenced when a [[neighbor|community]] matter requires formal consultation [012-055]. This [[belonging|place]]s him among the respected figures of [[avonlea|Avonlea]] [[community|society]] — someone whose opinion carries legal weight.

Mrs. Peter Sloane appears separately in the narrative as a member of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]'s social circles, participating in gatherings alongside [[mrs-harmon-andrews|Mrs. Harmon Andrews]] and Mrs. William Bell [041-026]. Her characterization includes a "habit of sighing" over matters of emotional attachment [041-029].

## Relationships

### Mrs. Peter Sloane

Peter Sloane's wife is an active participant in [[avonlea|Avonlea]] social life, attending [[neighbor|community]] gatherings and sharing in the gossip and observations that define small-town sociability [041-026].

### Mr. Harrison

As the landlord of the house rented to [[mr-harrison|Mr. Harrison]] [002-072], Peter Sloane is indirectly involved in one of the novel's key neighbourly relationships — the evolving dynamic between [[mr-harrison|Mr. Harrison]] and [[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]].

### Oliver Sloane

[[oliver-sloane|Oliver Sloane]] is a fellow member of the extended Sloane family in [[avonlea|Avonlea]], active in the [[avonlea-village-improvement-society|Village Improvement Society]] and [[neighbor|community]] affairs.

### Anne Shirley

[[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s connection to Peter Sloane is through proximity — the [[mr-harrison|Harrison]] household, in Peter's former house, becomes a significant neighbourly presence at [[green-gables|Green Gables]] [002-072].

## Character Analysis

Peter Sloane represents the institutional backbone of rural [[avonlea|Avonlea]] — the landowner and magistrate whose authority is tacitly accepted and occasionally invoked. His characterization is notable for its indirectness: readers come to know him through his property, his wife's social appearances, and his formal role, rather than through direct action or dialogue.

The Sloane family, as depicted across multiple members (Peter, Oliver, Mrs. Sloane, and various children), constitutes one of the novel's extended [[avonlea|Avonlea]] clans — alongside the Pyes, the Andrews, and the Barrys. Together they form the dense social fabric that makes [[avonlea|Avonlea]] feel like a fully inhabited [[neighbor|community]] rather than a backdrop for [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s story alone.
