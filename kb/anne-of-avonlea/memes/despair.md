# Despair

The emotional counterforce to hope in [[anne-shirley|Anne]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]] — the crashing-down that follows ambition's flight, captured in [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s own words: "soaring up into the seventh heaven of imagination and then crashing down into despair because you don't get them" (024-044).

## Definition

In *[[anne-shirley|Anne]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]*, despair is not a permanent condition but a recurring visitor. Montgomery presents it as the natural shadow of high [[ambition|aspiration]] — those who hope most intensely are also capable of the deepest despair. Characters experience despair when their efforts fail, when they disappoint themselves or others, and when circumstances seem insurmountable.

Crucially, despair in Montgomery's world is always temporary. It is the trough between waves of hope, not the final destination. The novel's emotional architecture depends on this rhythm: hope rises, despair crashes, and hope upwells again (006-158). Without despair, hope would have no meaning; [[humor|wit]]hout hope, despair would have no resolution.

## Examples in the Text

**[[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]]'s Domestic Despair (Chapter 10).** [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla Cuthbert]], faced [[humor|wit]]h the chaos of the twin [[davy-keith|Davy]] and [[dora-keith|Dora]], is described as being "in despair" (010-007) — so frustrated she "finally threatened to whip [[davy-keith|Davy]]." Her despair is comically domestic, born of the gap between her orderly expectations and the reality of caring for a mischievous child.

**[[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s [[teaching|Teaching]] Desperation (Chapter 12).** [[anne-shirley|Anne]] appears as "despair personified" (012-011) at the yard gate, overcome by the challenges of her first months of teaching. [[anthony-pye|Anthony Pye]] has defeated her best efforts, and she feels herself a failure. This is professional despair — the fear that one's calling exceeds one's abilities.

**The Creative's Despair (Chapter 19).** In her story-writing efforts, [[anne-shirley|Anne]] experiences despair at the gap between her imaginative vision and her technical skill: "[[anne-shirley|Anne]], in despair, gave up trying to" (019-101) meet the standards of the baking-powder competition. This is the artist's despair — the recognition that craft must catch up to vision.

**The Gap Between Dreams and Reality (Chapter 24).** [[anne-shirley|Anne]] articulates the emotional arc of her imaginative life: "soaring up into the seventh heaven of imagination and then crashing down into despair because you don't get them" (024-044). This self-aware diagnosis captures the pattern of the novel's emotional rhythm.

## Thematic Significance

**Despair as Contrast.** Montgomery uses despair primarily to give weight to hope. Without the crashing-down, the soaring-up would feel weightless. Despair certifies the reality of a character's emotional investment — they care enough to despair when things go wrong.

**The Temporariness of Despair.** No character in the novel remains permanently in despair. It is always a passage, not a destination. This treatment reflects Montgomery's fundamentally optimistic worldview — despair is real but never final.

**Despair and Growth.** The novel suggests that surviving despair builds resilience. [[anne-shirley|Anne]] emerges from each bout of despair [[humor|wit]]h greater self-knowledge and emotional capacity. Despair, in this sense, is a teacher — though a harsh one.

## Contrasting Perspectives

**[[diana-barry|Diana]]'s Despairing Look.** [[diana-barry|Diana]], [[humor|wit]]h "a despairing look at her watch" (029-173), represents the milder, more practical form of despair — the frustration of plans foiled by time and circumstance. Her despair lacks [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s dramatic intensity but is no less real.

**Levi's Stubborn Despair.** Levi "won't have it taken down" (027-161) — his despair manifests as stubbornness, a refusal to accept help or change. Unlike [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s despair, which resolves into renewed effort, Levi's despair becomes a fixed position — and a cautionary example.
