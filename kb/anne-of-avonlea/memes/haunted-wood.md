# Haunted Wood

The **Haunted Wood** is a small wooded area in [[avonlea|Avonlea]], situated between [[green-gables|Green Gables]] and [[orchard-slope|Orchard Slope]]. Named by [[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]] in her imaginative girlhood, the wood appears throughout _[[anne-shirley|Anne]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]_ as a familiar pathway — no longer a source of terror for the grown [[anne-shirley|Anne]], but rich [[humor|wit]]h memory and the sig[[nature-appreciation|nature]] of Montgomery's landscape prose.

## Character Overview

The Haunted Wood exemplifies [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s transformative imagination: a perfectly ordinary patch of trees becomes, through the power of naming and storytelling, a [[belonging|place]] of significance. By the time of _[[anne-shirley|Anne]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]_, the wood no longer frightens its creator — [[anne-shirley|Anne]] walks through it matter-of-factly — yet the name endures, a monument to the child she was and the young woman she is becoming.

## Geography

The Haunted Wood lies along the path between [[green-gables|Green Gables]] and the Barry property at [[orchard-slope|Orchard Slope]]. It is adjacent to the [[birch-path|Birch Path]] and connects to the network of lanes, pastures, and orchards that define [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s [[avonlea|Avonlea]].

## Cultural Significance

The Haunted Wood is one of [[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]]'s most enduring acts of imaginative [[belonging|place]]-making. That Montgomery revisits it throughout the series — each time [[humor|wit]]h slightly diminished supernatural charge — traces [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s maturation from child dreamer to adult teacher while preserving the landscape that shaped her. The wood is where [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s imagination first took root; that it remains, named and known, is a quiet testament to the power of a child's vision.
