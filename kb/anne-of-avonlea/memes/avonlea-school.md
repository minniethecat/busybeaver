# Avonlea School

The one-room schoolhouse where [[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]] teaches — the primary setting for her professional life and the crucible in which her [[teaching|education]]al philosophy is tested

## Location and Setting

Avonlea School is situated in the rural landscape of [[prince-edward-island|Prince Edward Island]], part of the network of communities — [[avonlea|Avonlea]], [[carmody|Carmody]], [[white-sands|White Sands]], [[newbridge|Newbridge]], [[grafton|East Grafton]] — that form the geographic world of *[[anne-shirley|Anne]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]*. Montgomery's settings are rendered [[humor|wit]]h the [[love|affection]]ate precision of a writer who knew and [[romance|love]]d the [[prince-edward-island|Prince Edward Island]] countryside intimately.

## Role in the Story

Avonlea School serves as a significant setting in the novel, contributing to the richly textured rural world that shapes the characters' experiences and provides the backdrop for [[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]]'s young adulthood.
