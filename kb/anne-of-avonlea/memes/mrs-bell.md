# Mrs. Bell

Mrs. Bell is an [[avonlea|Avonlea]] matron and church member whose remarks on congregational affairs provide a window into the village's ecclesiastical gossip and social networks [041-038].

## Character Overview

Mrs. Bell functions as a voice of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]'s church [[neighbor|community]], dispensing opinions and news about ministers, presbytery decisions, and the ambitions of local youth. Her comments, though brief, illuminate the workings of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]'s religious social circuit — where news of a young person going to college [041-038] travels quickly through informal channels.

## Role in the Story

Mrs. Bell appears in a single concentrated scene of village conversation, where she delivers three pieces of news in rapid succession. She announces that "[[anne-shirley|Anne]] is going to college" [041-038], reports on Presbytery business regarding the congregation's minister [041-050], and offers a character assessment of a local boy: "He's a smart boy" [041-061]. These three remarks — [[teaching|education]], church politics, and personal appraisal — encapsulate the core concerns of [[avonlea|Avonlea]] social discourse.

## Relationships

Mrs. Bell is the wife of [[mr-bell|Mr. Bell]], the [[avonlea|Avonlea]] farmer whose pasture borders [[green-gables|Green Gables]]. Her connection to the Presbyterian church [[belonging|place]]s her in the same congregation as the Cuthberts and the Allans. Her comment about the Presbytery — "That means we'll be losing him" [041-050] — suggests an engagement [[humor|wit]]h church governance that marks her as a well-informed member of the congregation.
