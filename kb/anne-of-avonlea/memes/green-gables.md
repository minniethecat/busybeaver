# Green Gables

The Cuthbert [[belonging|home]]stead on [[prince-edward-island|Prince Edward Island]] — a white farmhouse surrounded by orchards, woods, and the famous [[haunted-wood|Haunted Wood]] — that has been [[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]]'s [[belonging|home]] since she arrived as an orphan and that remains the emotional and physical center of her world in *[[anne-shirley|Anne]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]*.

## Location and Setting

Green Gables is situated in the rural [[neighbor|community]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]] on [[prince-edward-island|Prince Edward Island]]. The property includes the main farmhouse, barns, orchards, and surrounding woodlands including the [[birch-path|Birch Path]] and [[haunted-wood|the Haunted Wood]]. Adjacent properties include [[mr-harrison|Mr. Harrison]]'s farm and the Barry family's [[orchard-slope|Orchard Slope]]. The farm is characterized by its pastoral beauty, which [[anne-shirley|Anne]] has named and celebrated: [[love|Love]]r's Lane, the [[willowmere|Willowmere]], and the Dryad's Bubble are all features of the Green Gables landscape.

## Inhabitants

**[[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]]** has lived at Green Gables since she was eleven, having arrived as an orphan mistakenly sent to Matthew and [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla Cuthbert]]. **[[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla Cuthbert]]** owns and manages the farm, now [[humor|wit]]h [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s increasing assistance as her eyesight weakens. **[[davy-keith|Davy]] and [[dora-keith|Dora Keith]]**, orphaned twins, join the household in the course of the novel (009-140). The late **Matthew Cuthbert**, [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]]'s brother, is a remembered and be[[romance|love]]d presence.

## Role in the Story

Green Gables is the novel's [[belonging|home]] base — the [[belonging|place]] [[anne-shirley|Anne]] leaves each morning for school and returns to each evening, the setting of domestic scenes [[humor|wit]]h [[marilla-cuthbert|Marilla]] and the twins, and the sanctuary to which she retreats from the demands of teaching and [[neighbor|community]] life. It is also the symbol of [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s be[[envy|longing]]: the orphan who belonged nowhere now belongs irrevocably to Green Gables.
