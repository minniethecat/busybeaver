## Character Overview

Mirabel Cotton is a ten-year-old pupil at [[avonlea|Avonlea]] school [029-084]. She becomes notable in the narrative primarily because of her uncle — a figure whose fearsome reputation spreads among the younger children and becomes a source of childhood terror.

## Role in Story

Mirabel first appears when [[dora-keith|Dora Keith]] is temporarily assigned to sit [[humor|wit]]h her at school [029-084]. It is through Mirabel that [[dora-keith|Dora]] and [[davy-keith|Davy]] learn about her uncle, who becomes a bogeyman figure in their imaginations. [[dora-keith|Dora]] tearfully asks about "Mirabel Cotton's uncle" [029-114], and Mirabel herself faces consequences — being kept in at recess — presumably for spreading tales about him [029-124].

The uncle's fearsome reputation lingers in the children's minds; [[davy-keith|Davy]] later asks anxiously whether someone else "isn't like Mirabel Cotton's uncle" [032-064], suggesting the story has taken firm root in [[avonlea|Avonlea]] schoolyard folklore.
