# Founding of the A.V.I.S.

[[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]]'s initiative to create a [[avonlea-village-improvement-society|Village Improvement Society]] in [[avonlea|Avonlea]], channeling her aesthetic vision into collective [[neighbor|community]] action.

## Overview

[[anne-shirley|Anne]] is "responsible for the [[avonlea-village-improvement-society|A.V.I.S.]], since I was the first to suggest it" (007-059). What begins as an idea — beautifying [[avonlea|Avonlea]] through collective effort — becomes a functioning organization that meets regularly, undertakes projects, and provides both comic relief and genuine [[neighbor|community]] benefit. "We had a meeting of the [[avonlea-village-improvement-society|A.V.I.S.]] last Friday night" (011-119) marks the transition from idea to institution.

## Significance

The founding of the [[avonlea-village-improvement-society|A.V.I.S.]] represents [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s integration into [[avonlea|Avonlea]]'s civic life. The orphan who arrived by mistake has become a [[neighbor|community]] organizer, and the Society is the institutional expression of her belief that "one ought to do something to improve" the [[belonging|place]] one lives. It also provides Montgomery [[humor|wit]]h opportunities for [[love|affection]]ate satire of small-town organizational life.
