# Redmond College

Redmond College is the institution of higher [[education|learning]] in Kingsport, Nova Scotia, to which [[anne-shirley|Anne Shirley]] and [[gilbert-blythe|Gilbert Blythe]] plan to matriculate after their teaching terms in [[avonlea|Avonlea]]. Though never visited on-page in *[[anne-shirley|Anne]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]*, Redmond functions as a symbol of intellectual [[ambition|aspiration]] and the wider world that awaits [[anne-shirley|Anne]] beyond the village.

## Description

Redmond College is a post-secondary institution located in Kingsport, Nova Scotia. It is introduced in the novel's final chapter as the destination toward which both [[anne-shirley|Anne]] and [[gilbert-blythe|Gilbert]] are headed — "[[anne-shirley|Anne]] and [[gilbert-blythe|Gilbert]] would leave for Redmond College." The college represents the culmination of the [[teaching|education]]al path that began in the [[avonlea|Avonlea]] schoolhouse, continued through Queen's Academy, and now points toward higher [[education|learning]] and professional opportunity. [044-004]

The college is never described physically in *[[anne-shirley|Anne]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]*, but its symbolic weight is considerable. For [[anne-shirley|Anne]], Redmond is the realization of her academic ambitions — proof that a girl from [[avonlea|Avonlea]] can aspire to and achieve a college [[teaching|education]]. For [[gilbert-blythe|Gilbert]], it represents professional training toward his goal of becoming a doctor. Together, their shared destination at Redmond foreshadows the deepening of their relationship in subsequent novels.

## Activities

Redmond's activities in the context of *[[anne-shirley|Anne]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]* are entirely prospective: the college is a future destination, not a present setting. [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s teaching at [[avonlea-school|Avonlea School]] and her evening studies constitute her preparation for Redmond. [[gilbert-blythe|Gilbert]], similarly, teaches at the [[white-sands|White Sands]] school while preparing for college. Their parallel trajectories — each working, saving, and studying toward the same goal — form one of the novel's quiet structural symmetries.

The college also features in the broader [[neighbor|community]] imagination. For [[avonlea|Avonlea]] residents, Redmond College carries prestige, and the fact that two of their own will attend it is a source of local pride.

## Significance

Redmond College occupies a crucial position in the novel's thematic architecture. Throughout *[[anne-shirley|Anne]] of [[avonlea|Avonlea]]*, [[anne-shirley|Anne]] grapples [[humor|wit]]h the tension between her [[romance|love]] for [[avonlea|Avonlea]] and her ambition for something more. Redmond represents the resolution of that tension: a future that extends beyond the village [[humor|wit]]hout rejecting it. [[anne-shirley|Anne]]'s plan to attend Redmond is not an escape from [[avonlea|Avonlea]] but an extension of the [[teaching|education]]al values [[avonlea|Avonlea]] has nurtured in her.

The college also represents the egalitarian promise of [[teaching|education]] in Montgomery's world. [[anne-shirley|Anne]], an orphan who might easily have remained a farm drudge, will attend college alongside students from more privileged backgrounds. Her journey from the asylum orphanage to Redmond College is the arc that gives the *[[anne-shirley|Anne]]* series its enduring power as a story of self-making through [[education|learning]].
