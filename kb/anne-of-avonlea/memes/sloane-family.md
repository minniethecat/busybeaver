The **Sloane family** is one of the most numerous families in [[avonlea|Avonlea]],
with several homesteads scattered through the village [008-109].
The Sloanes appear throughout the novel in various roles — as farmers,
[[avonlea-village-improvement-society|Improvement Society]] members,
schoolchildren, and local characters.

## The Sloane Homesteads

"Several Sloane homesteads" [008-109] are noted in the novel, suggesting the family's
longstanding presence and prosperity in the district. Peter Sloane's old house is rented
to newcomers [002-072], and his role as magistrate [012-055] indicates the family's
standing in the community.

## Role in the Community

The Sloanes feature prominently in Avonlea's civic and social life:

- Oliver Sloane moves the subscription plan at the Improvement Society meeting [007-107]
- Clifton Sloane handles dairy transport to Carmody [007-100]
- Mrs. Hiram Sloane provides sharp social commentary, calling the society a "Courting Club" [007-090]
- Peter Sloane serves as magistrate for legal matters [012-055]
- Multiple Sloane children attend the [[avonlea-school|Avonlea school]]

## Key Traits

The Sloanes are characterized by practicality and plain-spokenness. Mr. Peter Sloane's
famous attempt to define "octogenarian" — "he said he didn't know, but it must be something
like an octagon, and perhaps meant a person who had eight children" [003-106] — is one
of the novel's characteristic moments of rural humor [003-103–106].
