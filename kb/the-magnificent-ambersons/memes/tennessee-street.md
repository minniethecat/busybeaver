# Tennessee Street

Tennessee Street was one of the principal thoroughfares of Midland during the era of Amberson magnificence. Together with [[national-avenue|National Avenue]], it formed the axis of fashionable residential life in the town, hosting the sleigh races, carriage parades, and daily social rituals that defined the community's outward show of prosperity.

## The Axis of Fashionable Life

In the winter afternoons of Midland's golden age, trotters were out racing light sleighs on National Avenue and Tennessee Street (001-014). Everybody recognized both the trotters and the drivers, and knew them equally well on summer evenings, when slim buggies renewed the snow-time rivalry. The houses along Tennessee Street, like those facing [[military-square|Military Square]] and National Avenue, were built of brick and represented the solid prosperity of the town's leading families (001-072).

## Residential Character

Tennessee Street belonged to the core of old Midland, the quarter where the Ambersons and their peers had established their homes. The street was part of the neighborhood that the Amberson family had dominated since the Major made his fortune in 1873. Its houses, while not as grand as the [[amberson-mansion|Amberson Mansion]], were the residences of the town's substantial citizens who participated in the rituals of open houses, serenades, and social calls that defined the community's pre-automobile social order.

## The Changing Street

As the town grew into a smoke-filled industrial city, Tennessee Street lost its character as a fashionable address. The old families moved away or died out, and the street became part of a district that, by the final chapters of the Amberson story, bore little resemblance to the pristine neighborhood of sleigh bells and trotters. The transformation of Tennessee Street was a microcosm of the larger changes that swallowed the old order of Midland.
