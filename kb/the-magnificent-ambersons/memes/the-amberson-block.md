# The Amberson Block

The Amberson Block was a commercial property owned by [[major-amberson|Major Amberson]] in downtown Midland. It served as the headquarters for the [[friends-of-the-ace|Friends of the Ace]] boys' club and later became an emblem of the Amberson family's declining fortunes in the changing [[downtown-midland|Downtown Midland]] landscape.

## Structure and Location

The Amberson Block was an old-fashioned four-story brick warren of lawyers' offices, insurance offices, and real-estate offices, with a dry-goods store occupying the ground floor (004-159). When young George Amberson Minafer visited the building in his youth, he tied his horse to a telegraph pole and stood looking at the building critically. He thought it looked shabby, and believed his grandfather ought to replace it with a fourteen-story skyscraper, or even a higher one, such as he had lately seen in New York (004-163).

## The F.O.T.A. Headquarters

The fourth floor of the Amberson Block housed the secret meeting room of the [[friends-of-the-ace|Friends of the Ace]], the boys' club founded by George. The young Amberson heir obtained the room rent-free from his grandfather and secured furniture from the janitor. During club meetings, the boys performed elaborate rituals, including the ceremonial greeting "Welcome, Friend of the Ace" and formal proceedings under the gavel of the president. George also held the threat of reporting to his grandfather over the members if they did not comply with his wishes.

## Symbol of Decline

By the later years of the Amberson story, the Amberson Block had fallen into decay. George, now a struggling working man, drifted to the old Amberson Block in a darker period of his life, but the building was fallen into disrepair (045-032). Where the name "Amberson Block" had once been proudly displayed, there was now a long billboard sign that read "Doogan" (045-036). The transformation of the Amberson Block from a family asset to a derelict property bearing another name perfectly encapsulated the fall of the house of Amberson.
