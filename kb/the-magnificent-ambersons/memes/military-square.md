# Military Square

Military Square was one of the most fashionable residential locations in Midland during the era of Amberson splendor. To have a house facing Military Square was to be counted among the town's "prominent residents," and the Square served as an architectural and social marker of status in the community (001-071).

## A Mark of Prominence

In the narrator's description of Midland's social geography, houses facing Military Square are invoked as a shorthand for distinguished standing. The Square represented the established residential prestige of the older town, before the rise of new development such as [[amberson-addition|Amberson Addition]], which would eventually shift the center of fashionable life. As a landmark, Military Square anchored the traditional elite whose wealth was inherited rather than newly made.

## The Changing Town

Military Square belonged to the old Midland — the town of Isabel Amberson's youth, before the arrival of the automobile and the industrial fortunes that would transform the social landscape. As [[amberson-mansion|the Amberson Mansion]] on [[national-avenue|National Avenue]] and later developments like the [[amberson-addition|Amberson Addition]] came to define the town's newer elite geography, places like Military Square retained their quiet dignity but lost some of their cachet. The Square's gradual eclipse by newer addresses mirrored the larger transformation described throughout the novel, in which established wealth gave way to industrial fortunes and old landmarks lost their meaning.
