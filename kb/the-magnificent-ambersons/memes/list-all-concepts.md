# All Concepts

*The Magnificent Ambersons* is more than a family chronicle; it is a meditation on pride, change, and the cost of refusing to adapt. The concept pages in this wiki trace these themes through the novel's key passages, situating Tarkington's insights within their historical moment and drawing out their continuing relevance.

## Notes

- Concept pages are interpretive rather than descriptive — they make arguments about the novel's meaning, supported by PN-cited evidence.
- The most-referenced concepts (Pride and Arrogance, Fashion and Social Status) are central to the novel's thematic architecture.
