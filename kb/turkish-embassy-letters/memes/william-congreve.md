# William Congreve

## Overview

William Congreve (1670–1729) was the leading English comic playwright of the late 17th and early 18th centuries, celebrated for *The Way of the World* (1700) and *Love for Love* (1695). He was a close friend of Lady Mary Wortley Montagu, who mentions him several times in the Turkish Embassy Letters with genuine affection. Montagu was "firmly sensible of yours and Mr Congreve's" friendship and looked forward to reuniting with him upon her return to England.

## Biography

Congreve was born in Yorkshire in 1670 and raised in Ireland, where he attended Trinity College, Dublin alongside Jonathan Swift. His first play, *The Old Bachelor* (1693), was a triumph, and he rapidly established himself as the preeminent comic dramatist of his generation. *The Way of the World*, though now considered his masterpiece, was less successful on its initial production, and Congreve largely abandoned playwriting thereafter.

Congreve entered government service, holding minor political offices, and moved in the same Whig literary-political circles as Addison, Steele, and Pope. He was known for his wit, charm, and discretion — qualities that made him a valued friend to Montagu and others in her circle.

## Role in the Turkish Embassy Letters

Montagu mentions Congreve in Letter XXXVI with warmth: she is "firmly sensible of yours and Mr Congreve's" friendship and expresses her "desire to live in your memory" — the plural "your" linking Congreve with her correspondent as among those whose regard she most values (036-006).

In Letter LII, she anticipates seeing Congreve upon her return: "that I may see you and Mr Congreve, who render that ground classic ground" — an allusion to Congreve's literary stature and the pleasure of his company (052-002). The phrase "classic ground" is a literary compliment, acknowledging Congreve as a writer who has enriched English letters.

The summary of the letters' contents groups Congreve with Addison and Pope — the three principal literary figures in Montagu's correspondence — confirming his significance within her network (062-055).
