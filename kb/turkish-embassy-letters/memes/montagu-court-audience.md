# Montagu's Audience at the Ottoman Court

## Overview

During her residence in Constantinople (1717–1718), Lady Mary Wortley Montagu gained extraordinary access to the Ottoman imperial court — a privilege rarely granted to Europeans and virtually unheard of for European women. Her visit to the seraglio and audience with the sultan's declared favorite (*mosayp*) provided material for some of the most politically revealing passages in the Turkish Embassy Letters, demystifying the harem and revealing the political sophistication of Ottoman court women.

## The Visit

Montagu was permitted to visit "the declared favourite of the sultan" — a woman of significant political influence within the seraglio hierarchy (028-003). The visit was arranged through diplomatic channels and required navigating elaborate court protocol. Montagu was met at the door by a "black eunuch" who helped her from her coach and led her through a series of chambers, each more splendid than the last (033-003) (033-004).

The audience itself was conducted according to strict Ottoman ceremonial: "a private audience (according to ceremony) of half an hour" — a duration that signaled the significance of the meeting. Montagu's description of the favorite emphasizes her elegance, intelligence, and political acumen — qualities that challenged European stereotypes of harem women as passive or ignorant.

## Political Significance

Montagu's access to the seraglio was a diplomatic achievement in itself. European ambassadors — all male — could not enter the harem or speak directly with the sultan's women. Montagu's gender, which limited her in European contexts, became an advantage in the Ottoman court, opening doors that remained closed to male diplomats.

The visit also served Montagu's literary purposes. By describing the harem from the inside, she could challenge the orientalist fantasies that dominated European accounts of Ottoman court life. The women she met were not languishing odalisques but politically astute actors managing complex networks of influence.
