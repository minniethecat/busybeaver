# Mrs. T——

## Overview

"Mrs. T——" is the anonymized designation of a correspondent who received three letters from Lady Mary Wortley Montagu during her Ottoman embassy. The partial concealment of her identity — a convention of 18th-century published correspondence — reflects both the period's discretion about private relationships and Montagu's awareness that her letters might eventually reach a wider audience. Scholars have tentatively identified Mrs. T—— as Frances Thistlethwayte, a member of Montagu's English social circle.

## Biography

The identity of Mrs. T—— cannot be established with certainty from the published text of the Turkish Embassy Letters. Robert Halsband, in his definitive edition of Montagu's correspondence, suggested she was Frances Thistlethwayte (née Thistlethwayte), based on manuscript evidence and the pattern of Montagu's English correspondents. The Thistlethwayte family had connections to Montagu's social world in London.

The use of initials and dashes to conceal addressees' identities was standard practice in 18th-century published correspondence. Montagu's sister appears as the "Countess of ——," Lady Rich as "Lady R——," and Mrs. T—— follows this pattern of partial anonymity that allowed contemporary readers in the know to identify the recipients while preserving a veneer of discretion.

## Role in the Turkish Embassy Letters

The three letters to Mrs. T—— appear in the Turkish Embassy Letters at Letters XXXII and following. Their content suggests a correspondent of some intimacy — Montagu shares personal observations and reflections with a freedom that indicates a relationship of trust rather than mere acquaintance.

The decision to include letters to Mrs. T—— in the published collection, despite the anonymity, indicates that Montagu (or her editors) considered these letters to contain material of interest to readers beyond their original recipient. The letters to Mrs. T—— contribute to the collection's range, showing Montagu corresponding with a variety of English contacts.
