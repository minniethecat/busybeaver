# Memorial Tablet

The Memorial Tablet, or memorial service, is a Gloucester tradition honoring the fishermen lost at sea. The event takes place on Memorial Day and involves a public ceremony that includes a collection for the widows and orphans of deceased fishermen.

## The Memorial Day Observance

The memorial is referenced in the closing chapters of the narrative when Harvey Cheyne insists on staying in Gloucester to see the captain off, noting that "Monday's Memorial Day" [010-069]. Mr. Cheyne, newly arrived in the fishing town, inquires about "this memorial business" after hearing about it at the boardinghouse [010-070].

## Character and Purpose

Harvey describes the memorial as "a sort of song-and-dance act, whacked up for the summer boarders" — a performance-oriented ceremony designed partly for the tourists who visit Gloucester in season [010-070]. Despite this dismissive characterization, the event serves a genuine charitable purpose: the organizers "take up a collection for the widows and orphans" of fishermen lost at sea [010-070].

## Disko Troop's Independence

Disko Troop, the fiercely independent captain of the *We're Here*, takes a skeptical view of the memorial. He "don't think much of it," according to Harvey, precisely because it involves taking up a charitable collection — a form of dependence that runs counter to Disko's self-reliant character [010-070]. His attitude reflects the broader tension between the working fishermen's pride in self-sufficiency and the community's need to support the families of those who do not return from the Banks.
