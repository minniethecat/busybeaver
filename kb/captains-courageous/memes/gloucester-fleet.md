# Gloucester Fleet

The Gloucester fishing fleet is the backbone of the New England cod fishery in *Captains Courageous*. The schooner *We're Here*, captained by Disko Troop, is one of many vessels that sail from Gloucester to the Grand Banks each season, and the fleet's reputation for toughness and skill pervades the novel.

## The Fishing Fleet

Gloucester was the premier fishing port in late 19th-century New England. The bulk of vessels on the Grand Banks were from Gloucester:

> The bulk of them were mainly Gloucester boats, with a scattering from Provincetown, Harwich, Chatham, and some of the Maine ports. [005-038]

The Gloucester fleet operated under a strict code of maritime conduct. The Gloucester boats were known to "despise the Frenchmen" on the Banks, reflecting both the competitive nature of the international fishery and the Gloucestermen's confidence in their own methods [002-152].

## Gloucester Pride and Identity

To be a Gloucester man was a matter of fierce local pride. The worst insult one could hurl at a Gloucester fisherman was to mistake him for a Nova Scotian:

> To call a Gloucester man a Nova Scotian is not well received. [006-059]

This identity was forged in the dangerous work of the Banks fishery. The Gloucester fleet's crews preferred the risks and independence of fishing to the safer but less respected work of merchant shipping. As one crew member remarked:

> "There ain't money in Gloucester 'u'd hire me to ship on a regular trader." [003-157]

## The We're Here and Its Crew

Among the Gloucester fleet, the *We're Here* stood as a representative dory schooner. Her crew—Disko Troop, his son Dan, and the fishermen—embodied the skills and traditions of the Gloucester fishing community. The schooner hailed from Gloucester, and Disko Troop declared his origin with pride:

> "I'm Disko Troop o' the *We're Here* o' Gloucester, which you don't seem rightly to know." [001-065]

The crew's return to Gloucester at the end of the season was a homecoming shared by the entire fleet. As the schooners approached land:

> The low circle of hills round Gloucester Harbor, Ten Pound Island, the lighthouse—all the familiar landmarks came into view. [008-110]

## Gloucester's Role in the Fishery

The fleet's economic importance extended beyond fishing. When the catch came to market, Gloucester's fish dealers set prices that affected the entire coast:

> They did not want any of her fish in Gloucester. "Give us no price," the dealers would say. [006-040]

The fishermen's relationship with the Gloucester market was complex—they depended on it for their livelihood but often felt exploited by the middlemen who controlled prices.
