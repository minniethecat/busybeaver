# Georges Bank

A broad, shallow fishing ground lying east of Cape Cod and south of Nova Scotia, separated from the Grand Banks by the deep water of the Northeast Channel. Georges Bank is a secondary destination for the Gloucester fleet — warmer and more accessible than the Grand Banks, with its own populations of cod, halibut, and haddock.

## 地理位置

Georges Bank is an oval-shaped submerged plateau roughly 150 miles long and 75 miles wide, with depths ranging from 10 to 50 fathoms. It lies approximately 100 miles east of Cape Cod and is bounded by the Gulf of Maine to the north. Its shallower waters warm more quickly in spring, making it an attractive early-season alternative to the colder Grand Banks.

## 在书中的作用

Gloucester fishermen refer to Georges in the context of fishing strategy — where to go, when to move, which ground is producing. Disko Troop's knowledge extends to all the major Banks, and his decisions about where to anchor the *We're Here* reflect a lifetime of experience reading seasonal patterns (010-067).

## 航海/渔业特征

Georges is known for its strong tidal currents, which can make dory fishing dangerous. The bottom is predominantly sand and gravel, good holding ground for anchors. Cod and halibut are the primary target species, with haddock also present in significant numbers.
