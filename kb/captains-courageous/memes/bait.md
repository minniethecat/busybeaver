# Bait

**Bait** is the material used to attract and catch fish, a fundamental element of the cod-fishing life depicted in _Captains Courageous_. On the Grand Banks, fishermen use clams and fish offal to lure the cod onto their hooks.

## Baiting the Lines

Baiting is one of the first skills Harvey Cheyne must learn aboard the _We're Here_. Under Dan's instruction, Harvey struggles with "the mystery of baiting and heaving out the leads" while Dan's line is already in the water (003-027). The process requires speed and practice — a skilled fisherman baits and casts in one fluid motion.

## Types of Bait

The fishermen use several kinds of bait. Salt clams are a common choice — Dan "string[s] a salt clam on to the hook" and tells Harvey to "bait same's I do" (003-026). For trawl fishing, the crew use "selected offal of the cod" as bait, a practice that repurposes the waste from cleaned fish (003-155).

## Bait and Strategy

Bait selection reflects the captain's strategic knowledge. Disko Troop chooses his bait based on his reading of the fish and conditions. Dan explains that "Dad knows. Thet's why he's baitin' ez he is" (003-159). The Portuguese fishermen, who keep to themselves, are described as those who "bait big an' catch small" (003-074), suggesting that baiting strategy directly affects the size and quality of the catch.

## Baiting Trawl Lines

For trawl fishing, baiting is a large-scale operation. The boys are tasked with baiting "a tub or so o' trawl" — preparing hundreds of hooks on a long line (003-149). When a cod "had taken the bait right into his stomach," it signals the fish are hungry and the fishing will be good (003-133).
