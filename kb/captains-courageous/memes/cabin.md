# Cabin

The cabin of a fishing schooner is the ship's interior space — a cramped compartment below the quarter-deck where the crew eat, sleep, and take shelter from weather. Aboard the *We're Here*, the cabin is reached by a short ladder from the deck, and its roof — called the "house" — serves as the skipper's observation post (001-058). The cabin is a world apart from the wet, windy deck above: low-ceilinged, warm with the smell of cooking, and resonant with the voices of men telling stories around the table.

## 定义

The cabin of a seventy-ton schooner is a single compartment perhaps twelve feet long and eight feet wide, divided functionally rather than architecturally. One end houses the galley stove and food stores; the other contains bunks built into the hull, where the crew sleep in rotating watches. A central table, bolted to the deck, serves for meals, mending gear, and the endless card games that fill the hours between fishing (003-098). Above it, the cabin roof — the "house" — is covered with canvas and provides the only dry spot on deck.

## 具体细节

### The Cabin as Social Centre

When the day's fishing is done and the men come below, the cabin becomes the schooner's social heart. Disko Troop holds court at the table, dispensing judgments on gear, weather, and the day's catch. Uncle Salters reads his agricultural papers aloud, to the amusement of the fishermen. The cook works in the galley corner, and the cabin fills with the smells of salt pork, coffee, and fresh bread (002-111). It is in the cabin that Harvey Cheyne first feels himself drawn into the community of the crew.

### The Cabin Clock

A clock stands in the cabin, striking the hours and half-hours, regulating the ship's routine. At ten o'clock, the clock strikes and Penn creeps on deck for his night watch (002-177). The clock is one of the few mechanical objects aboard, and its regularity contrasts with the unpredictable rhythms of wind and fish.

### Disko's Cabin Authority

Disko Troop emerges from the cabin to settle disputes and give orders. When Uncle Salters makes a ruckus on deck, Disko comes "out of the cabin" and puts things right with a few quiet words (002-111). The cabin is his seat of command, and from it he runs the schooner with a blend of paternal authority and practical seamanship.
