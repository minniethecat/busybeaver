# Salt Cod

Split and salted Atlantic cod, packed in layers in the hold of a fishing schooner to cure during the weeks-long voyage from the Grand Banks to Gloucester. Salt cod is the economic engine of the entire fishery — every dory launched, every line hauled, every fish split at the dressing-down tables is in service of filling the hold with well-salted cod (002-124, 002-139).

## 定义

Salt cod is cod preserved by heavy salting after being gutted, beheaded, and split flat. On the Grand Banks, the process begins at the splitting tables on deck: each cod is opened, its backbone removed, and the flat fillets are packed in salt in the schooner's hold (002-147). The salt draws moisture from the fish, creating a brine that cures the flesh and prevents spoilage. By the time the schooner reaches Gloucester, the cod has transformed into a hard, pale, long-keeping product that can be stored for months and rehydrated for cooking.

Salt cod was one of the most important commodities in the Atlantic economy of the nineteenth century — a cheap, durable protein that fed slaves in the Caribbean, factory workers in Europe, and rural populations across the Americas. The Gloucester fleet, including the *We're Here*, was part of this global trade (002-139).

## 具体细节

### The Salting Process

After a cod is split, the two sides — now flat fillets connected at the tail — are passed to the salter. The fish is laid skin-side down in the hold, covered with a layer of coarse salt, then another layer of fish is laid on top, and so on (002-147). The weight of the upper layers presses the lower ones, helping to expel water and drive the salt into the flesh. The salt itself is coarse — fine salt would dissolve too quickly and the fish would spoil.

### The Smack

In Gloucester, the salt cod is offloaded and sold at the wharf. The crew is paid in shares — a percentage of the voyage's total catch value, not a fixed wage (001-103). A good voyage with a full hold of well-salted cod means good money for every man aboard. A poor voyage — light catch, spoiled fish, or falling market prices — means lean times. This share system ties every fisherman's fortune directly to the quality and quantity of salt cod he helps produce.

### Harvey's Education

For Harvey Cheyne, the concept of working for shares instead of a salary is baffling. Used to his father's millions, he cannot grasp why men would risk their lives for "a few old cod" (002-139). The economics of salt cod — the value of a fish measured in cents per pound, the difference between a ten-cent cod and a fifteen-cent cod — is one of the many lessons the *We're Here* teaches him about the real world.
