# Schooner

A fore-and-aft rigged sailing vessel carrying a crew of fishermen who launch small [[dory|dories]] daily to fish the [[grand-banks|Grand Banks]].

The [[schooner]] serves as mother ship, fish-processing platform, and floating home for weeks at sea. In *Captains Courageous*, the schooner *We're Here* of [[gloucester|Gloucester]] is the primary setting — the confined world where [[harvey-cheyne|Harvey Cheyne's]] transformation from spoiled millionaire's son to competent sailor takes place.

## 定义

A Grand Banks fishing schooner is a two-masted fore-and-aft rigged vessel designed to carry a stack of nested dories on deck and a crew of eight to twelve men (001-103). The schooner anchors at productive fishing grounds while her dories scatter across the surrounding water to set and haul trawl lines. Fish are brought back to the schooner for processing — gutting, splitting, and salting in the hold.

The typical voyage lasts several weeks, with the schooner remaining at sea until her hold is full of salted cod or the season ends (001-058). The deck layout includes a cabin-roof (called the "house"), a quarterdeck aft, a mainmast with a triangular riding-sail for stability at anchor, and pens on deck where the day's catch is dumped from returning dories (001-058).

Below deck are the crew's berths, the galley, and the fish hold where split and salted cod is packed in layers under Disko Troop's exacting supervision.

> The schooner, with a triangular riding-sail on the mainmast, played easily at anchor, and except for the man by the cabin-roof — "house" they call it — she was deserted.
> — (001-058)

## 具体细节

### The We're Here

The *We're Here* is a Gloucester fishing schooner captained by [[disko-troop|Disko Troop]]. She carries a crew that includes Disko's son [[dan-troop|Dan]], the Portuguese [[Manuel]], [[Long Jack]], [[uncle-salters|Uncle Salters]], [[penn|Penn]], and [[tom-platt|Tom Platt]] (001-103).

When Harvey Cheyne is rescued from the sea, he is taken aboard the *We're Here* and, finding no sympathy for his wealth or station, is put to work as a second boy at ten and a half dollars a month (001-103).

> "You are with me in the dory — Manuel my name, and I come from schooner *We're Here* of Gloucester. I find you on the water, an' I bring you aboard."
> — (001-043, 001-044)

### Daily Rhythm

The schooner's day revolves around the fishing cycle: at dawn the dories are launched, each with one or two men; throughout the day the little boats dot the horizon around the mother ship; at sunset the dories row back. Each schooner seems to pull her boats towards her by invisible strings (002-061).

The catch is forked into deck pens, then the crew works at the splitting tables — gutting, beheading, and splitting [[cod-fishing|cod]] — often late into the night when the pens are full.

> Each schooner in sight seemed to be pulling her dories towards her by invisible strings, and the little black figures in the tiny boats pulled like clockwork toys.
> — (002-061)

### The Fleet

The *We're Here* is one of many schooners working the Grand Banks. The schooners communicate by signal and recognize each other by their dories and paint schemes. When one schooner's dory has a problem — a lost anchor, damaged gear — other schooners recognize it and offer help.

This floating community operates by unwritten rules of mutual aid and professional respect, the foundation of what the fishermen call [[bank-law|Bank law]] — an unwritten code that governs all conduct on the fishing grounds.

### The Hold: Salt and Storage

Below the deck, the schooner's hold is a world of salt-bins and packed cod. Here [[disko-troop|Disko Troop]] and [[tom-platt|Tom Platt]] move among the layers, ensuring "good stowin's good saltin'" (002-164).

The hold is the schooner's economic heart — every fish caught, split, and salted here represents the voyage's profit. When the hold is full, the schooner heads for Gloucester and the market.

## 书中涉及场景

The schooner is the primary setting for the entire novel:

- **Harvey's rescue** (Chapter 1): Manuel brings Harvey from the dory to the *We're Here*, where he is given a hot drink, stripped of his wet clothes, and falls asleep in a bunk (001-043, 001-044).

- **First morning aboard** (Chapter 1): Harvey wakes to find himself in a world utterly alien to his luxury upbringing — the schooner's deck, the fishing fleet, and Disko Troop's matter-of-fact command (001-058).

- **The evening catch** (Chapter 2): As dories return, the deck becomes a frantic processing line — forking fish, sluicing pens, and working at the splitting tables (002-061, 002-073, 002-074).

- **Watches and discipline** (Chapter 3): Harvey and Dan stand night watches, responsible for the schooner's safety while the crew sleeps (003-077).
