# Jib

The **jib** is a triangular sail set forward of the foremast, carried on the jib-boom that extends from the bow of a sailing vessel. In _Captains Courageous_, the jib is one of the most frequently mentioned sails, reflecting its importance in the daily handling of a Grand Banks fishing schooner.

## Setting and Handling

Raising and lowering the jib is a routine deck operation. The command "Up jib and foresail" from Disko Troop sends the crew into action, with Long Jack "making fast the jib-sheet" while the others raise the heavy sail (003-077). When the schooner needs to slow or heave to, the order comes "Daown jib an' pole her!" (008-039). The jib is also dropped quickly when coming onto the fishing grounds: Harvey "let down the jib in a lump on the boom" while the lead was being cast (003-121).

## The Jib-Boom

The jib-boom is the spar extending forward from the bow to carry the jib. It is a prominent and sometimes vulnerable feature. The _We're Here_ surges ahead "ten feet beyond the surging jib-boom" (003-119), and the jib-boom is constantly in motion as the vessel works through the seas.

The jib-boom is also a place of both work and humor. The cook is memorably seen "riding the jib-boom and shouting Gaelic to a friend" on another vessel (008-008). At night in port, the "jib-boom solemnly poked at the low stars" (008-103). But the jib-boom could also be a point of vulnerability — some schooners have had their "jib-boom fished and spliced and bound" after damage (004-082), and one vessel's "jib-boom from port to starboard" gesticulation signals her wild motion (004-115).

## Identification at Sea

The jib serves as a means of identification between vessels. Experienced fishermen can recognize a schooner by the cut of her jib. Dan remarks on the _Carrie Pitman_: "She's the _Carrie Pitman_ from New Haven. Dad seen the patch in her foresail an' a new jib" (003-003). The sight of "a big jib with a wooden spar tied to it" suggests a particular type of rig (003-085).

## Under Jib Alone

In certain conditions the schooner sails under reduced canvas. "The _We're Here_, under jib and riding-sail, gave her no more canvas than was absolutely necessary" (006-064). This reduced sail plan is used in heavy weather or when maneuvering carefully near other vessels on the crowded fishing grounds.

## The Jib in the Sail Plan

The jib works in concert with the foresail and mainsail. The command "Up jib and fores'l" calls for the two headsails to be set together (010-142). Together with the foresail, the jib helps balance the schooner's sail plan, and "the setting of the jib and foresail" is a coordinated operation that requires the crew to work together on the pitching deck (004-097).
