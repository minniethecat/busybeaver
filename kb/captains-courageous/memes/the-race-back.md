# The Race Back to Gloucester

The final test of seamanship before the *We're Here* returns to port. With her hold full of salt cod and her crew eager for home, Disko Troop puts his schooner on a course for Gloucester — and, as is the custom among the fleet, the voyage becomes an informal race. Every captain wants to be first to market, and the run home is a test of everything a schooner and her crew can do (Chapters 8–10).

## 背景

The *We're Here* has been at sea for months. Her hold is full of well-salted cod — the measure of a successful voyage. The crew is tired, salt-crusted, and ready for the pleasures of shore: dry beds, hot food, families, and pay. The return voyage is the culmination of everything they have worked for (Chapters 8–10).

## 经过

Disko sets a course that takes advantage of wind, current, and his intimate knowledge of the route. Other schooners are doing the same, and the voyage becomes a contest — each captain trimming sail and pushing his vessel to be first into Gloucester Harbor. The crew works the sheets with practiced efficiency, the schooner heels to the wind, and the miles fall away. The *We're Here* races "neck and neck" against the *Parry Norman*, the Fleet betting tobacco on the outcome (008-100). For Harvey, who has learned to love the sea and his place in the crew, the race is exhilarating — proof that he belongs among these men.

## 结果与影响

The *We're Here* arrives in Gloucester, and Harvey's parents — who have traced him through the transatlantic telegraph — arrive to meet him. They find a son transformed: sunburned, callused, competent, and confident. The race home is the final chapter of Harvey's education, and his arrival marks the completion of his journey from boy to young man.
