# We're Here

The _We're Here_ is a Gloucester fishing schooner captained by [[Disko Troop]] that rescues Harvey Cheyne after he falls overboard from an ocean liner. For the next three months, the schooner serves as Harvey's floating school -- a cramped, odorous, and demanding classroom where the spoiled millionaire's son learns seamanship, labor, and human dignity. The vessel's name, spoken in the present tense, suggests both the fishermen's constant presence on the Banks and their matter-of-fact relationship with the sea. Manuel, the Portuguese doryman who first plucks Harvey from the water, declares simply: "I come from schooner We're Here of Gloucester. I live to Gloucester" (001-043).

## 地理位置与构造

The _We're Here_ is a two-masted fishing schooner out of Gloucester, Massachusetts, a seventy-ton vessel typical of the New England fishing fleet of the 1890s. Her interior, as Harvey discovers upon waking, is a "narrow, triangular cave" with a lamp hung against a "huge square beam" and a three-cornered table running from the bows to the foremast (001-045). The space is "packed as full of smells as a bale is of cotton" -- oilskins, fried fish, burnt grease, paint, pepper, stale tobacco, all "hooped together by one encircling smell of ship and salt water" (001-045). On deck, Harvey stumbles "aft, over a score of obstructions, to where a small, thickset, clean-shaven man with gray eyebrows sat on a step that led up to the quarterdeck" (001-058). The schooner, "with a triangular riding-sail on the mainmast, played easily at anchor" while the dories fished around her (001-058).

> The boat's motion was not that of a steamer. She was neither sliding nor rolling, but rather wriggling herself about in a silly, aimless way, like a colt at the end of a halter (001-045).

Below decks, the cabin contained a well-used Plymouth stove, a three-cornered table, and the dark double tier of bunks. When Harvey asked for milk with his coffee, Dan replied with the practical humor of the Banks: "Ner there ain't likely to be till 'baout mid-September. 'Tain't bad coffee. I made it" (001-048).

## 船员

The _We're Here_ carries a crew of eight fishermen -- each essential to the schooner's operation and each contributing to Harvey's education (001-091):

| [[Long Jack]] | Veteran fisherman |

Dan explains the crew's solidarity plainly: "We ain't any common wharf-end crowd by any manner o' means. We're fishermen, an' we've shipped together for six years an' more" (002-029). This close-knit company, bound by years of shared labor and danger, becomes the crucible in which Harvey's character is recast.

> "Not knowin' what your notions o' fun may be, I can't rightly say, young feller. But if I was you, I wouldn't call the boat which, under Providence, was the means o' savin' ye, names" -- [[Disko Troop]]'s rebuke when Harvey insults the schooner (001-065).

## 在书中的作用

### 救援与收容

The _We're Here_'s dory, manned by Manuel, finds Harvey half-drowned on the Grand Banks. The rescue comes in fog so thick Harvey hears "a gun and a horn and shouting" before "something bigger than the dory, but quite as lively, loomed alongside" (001-044). Disko Troop, who owns and commands the schooner, refuses to cut short the fishing season to return Harvey to land -- not out of cruelty but economic necessity: "There's eight of us on the _We're Here_, an' ef we went back naow -- it's more'n a thousand mile -- we'd lose the season" (001-091). Disko offers Harvey work instead: "Ten an' a ha'af fer second boy on the schooner -- an' all found -- fer to teach you an' fer the sake o' your health" (001-103).

The telegram that reaches Harvey's father months later summarizes the entire ordeal with telegraphic brevity: "Picked up by fishing schooner We're Here having fallen off boat great times on Banks fishing all well waiting Gloucester Mass care Disko Troop for money or orders" (009-007). Harvey has been transformed by then.

### Harvey's Transformation

Aboard the _We're Here_, Harvey undergoes a complete transformation. Disko offers him work at "ten an' a ha'af a month" -- a wage Harvey initially finds insulting. Yet the discipline of the schooner -- dressing down fish, standing watch, working the dories -- rebuilds Harvey's character. The moon witnesses the first night of Harvey's new life:

> The moon, who sees many strange things on the Banks, looked down on a slim youth in knickerbockers and a red jersey, staggering around the cluttered decks of a seventy-ton schooner, while behind him, waving a knotted rope, walked, after the manner of an executioner, a boy who yawned and nodded between the blows he dealt (002-176).

Dan Troop keeps Harvey awake through the night watch, "the words clotting on his tongue," speaking "of the beauty of watchfulness" as he slashes with the rope's end (002-177). It is a brutal education, but it takes hold.

### 渔业生活

The schooner's fishing routine structures the narrative: dories are launched at dawn, cod and halibut are hauled aboard, and the crew "dresses down" the catch on deck. "More schooners had crept up in the night, and the long blue seas were full of sails and dories" (003-002). Disko, studying the sea, "accepted the presence of the inquisitive schooners on the horizon as a compliment to his powers" (003-004). The _We're Here_'s rhythms -- the conch-shell horn and the discipline of the watch system -- form the backdrop of Harvey's education (002-176).

Manuel, ever the teacher, promises after supper to "show you a little schooner I make, with all her ropes. So we shall learn" (003-117). The model schooner becomes a classroom in miniature, with every line and block named and explained.

> Dad says everythin' on the Banks is signs, an' can be read wrong er right. Dad's deeper'n the Whale-hole -- Dan Troop on Disko's seamanship (003-043).

### 风暴与雾

The _We're Here_ proves her seaworthiness through the relentless weather of the Banks. On stormy days, "every square inch of the schooner singing its own tune," the black bulk of the cook balanced "behind the tiny galley over the glare of the stove, and the pots and pans in the pierced wooden board before it jarred and racketed to each plunge" (004-001). The foc'sle climbed and surged: "Up and up the foc'sle climbed, yearning and surging and quivering, and then, with a clear, sickle-like swoop, came down into the seas" (004-001).

After a blow, Disko would cry, "It's blown clear," and the _We're Here_ would slide "into long, sunk avenues and ditches which felt quite sheltered and homelike if they would only stay still; but they changed without rest or mercy, and flung up the schooner to crown one peak of a thousand gray hills" (004-070). At anchor, the little schooner would gambol "all around her anchor among the silver-tipped waves," pouncing on the cable "like a kitten" (004-113). The schooner's low freeboard meant she was "on most intimate terms with her surroundings," seeing "little of the horizon save when she topped a swell" (008-103).

> The little schooner was gambolling all around her anchor among the silver-tipped waves. Backing with a start of affected surprise at the sight of the strained cable, she pounced on it like a kitten, while the spray of her descent burst through the hawseholes with the report of a gun (004-113).

### 舰队生活

When the fog lifts, Harvey witnesses a sight he "will never forget" -- "the sun was just clear of the horizon they had not seen for nearly a week, and his low red light struck into the riding-sails of three fleets of anchored schooners. There must have been nearly a hundred of them, of every possible make and build" (008-001). The Bank fleet becomes a floating community, where dories gossip from boat to boat and "every schooner and nearly every man got it in turn" in the rough musical satire of the fishermen (008-023). After a gale claims lives across the fleet, a French bark that had traded tobacco with the We're Heres slips away one morning, and "Harvey saw the funeral through Disko's spyglass. It was only an oblong bundle slid overside" (008-043).

In the race home, "the We're Here was racing neck and neck for her last few loads against the Parry Norman; and so close was the struggle that the Fleet took side and betted tobacco" (008-100). Disko works them until they "fell asleep where they stood," but when the salt is all wetted and the flag hoisted, his homeward procession is a triumph: "Dan's accordion and Tom Platt's fiddle supplied the music of the magic verse you must not sing till all the salt is wet" (008-100).

### 告别

The schooner's power over Harvey is clearest at the moment of parting. Standing at Wouverman's wharf, he sees the _We're Here_ "fresh from the lumper's hands" and feels "a queer mixture of pride and sorrowfulness. Other people ... played about in catboats or looked at the sea from pier-heads; but he understood things from the inside -- more things than he could begin to think about. None the less, he could have sat down and howled because the little schooner was going off" (010-140). When Harvey finally casts off from the pier-head and "they slid her along the wharf-side with their hands," the jokes fall flat "in the presence of the two women, and it is hard to be funny with green harbour-water widening between good friends" (010-141).

The memorial service at Gloucester drives home the schooner's connection to loss. As the list of the drowned is read aloud, Harvey hears his own vessel's name: "May 10th. -- Schooner We're Here [the blood tingled all over him] Otto Svendson, 20, single, City, lost overboard" (010-127). The _We're Here_ has known death as intimately as it has known fish and fog.

## 命名与象征

The name "We're Here" operates on multiple levels. Practically, it identifies the vessel's presence on the fishing grounds -- a hail to other schooners in the fog, an announcement of position. Existentially, it asserts the fishermen's place in the world -- a declaration of being against the vast indifference of the Atlantic. The present-tense "we're" (rather than the past "we were") suggests continuity and endurance: the fishermen exist, persistently and matter-of-factly, in their element. The schooner is not merely a vehicle but a community, a school, and a home. When young Harvey asks, "Can a schooner like this go right across to Africa?" (006-044), the question reveals how thoroughly the vessel has captured his imagination -- it has become the measure of all possibility.
