# Provincetown

Provincetown is a historic fishing port located at the extreme tip of Cape Cod, Massachusetts. In *Captains Courageous*, it appears as one of the New England communities — alongside Gloucester, Harwich, and Chatham — whose fishermen make up the heterogeneous crews of the Grand Banks fishing fleet.

## In the Story

The Grand Banks fleet is described as a cosmopolitan gathering of vessels, with "the bulk of them mainly Gloucester boats, with a scattering from Provincetown, Harwich, Chatham, and some of the Maine ports" [005-038]. While Gloucester dominates the fleet, Provincetown contributes its share of boats and men to the seasonal fishing on the Banks.

The diversity of the fleet's origins is illustrated during the social gatherings that take place among the fishing vessels. When the *We're Here* hosts a breakfast during the fleet's sorting-out, "seven extra mouths sat down to breakfast: A Swede; a Chatham skipper; a boy from Hancock, Maine; one Duxbury, and three Provincetown men" [008-042]. The presence of three Provincetown men at a single gathering underscores the port's significant representation in the fleet and its role in the tight-knit fishing community of New England.

## Fishing Heritage

By the 1890s, Provincetown was already a well-established fishing center, its location at the tip of Cape Cod giving its fleet direct access to the Gulf of Maine and the broader North Atlantic fishing grounds. The town's fishermen, like their Gloucester counterparts, participated in the seasonal migration to the Grand Banks that forms the novel's backdrop. Provincetown's presence in the narrative reinforces the regional breadth of the fishing industry beyond Gloucester alone.
