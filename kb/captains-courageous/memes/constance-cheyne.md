# Constance Cheyne

Constance Cheyne, referred to throughout the novel as Mrs. Cheyne, is Harvey Cheyne's mother. Her name is revealed through the private rail car "Constance" — named for her — that carries the family across America [009-024]. Though she appears only in the final chapters, her maternal anguish is a constant background presence throughout Harvey's disappearance.

## 身份与背景

Mrs. Cheyne is the wife of the railroad magnate [[mr-cheyne|Mr. Cheyne]] and mother of Harvey Cheyne. Before Harvey's disappearance, she lived in constant anxiety about her son's safety: "Mrs. Cheyne lived in fear of breaking his nerves" [001-055]. Her overprotective parenting is cited as one reason Harvey became the "biggest nuisance aboard" the ocean liner [001-002].

## 外貌与性格

Mrs. Cheyne is portrayed through her emotional states rather than physical description. When news of Harvey's disappearance arrives, she collapses entirely — "Mrs. Cheyne lay still and moaned, or talked in broken sentences" [009-001]. Her grief is absolute and immobilizing, requiring constant medical attention. She is the emotional counterweight to her husband's decisive action.

## 主要经历

### The Collapse

When Harvey is reported drowned, Mrs. Cheyne is devastated. She becomes bedridden, attended by a doctor summoned at "Cheyne's imperious bidding" [009-008]. Her one repeated word — "hurry" — drives the frantic pace of the rescue mission [009-053].

> "Mrs. Cheyne she say you must come at once" — the summons that launches Cheyne's cross-country dash [009-014]

### The Journey East

Transported in the private car "Constance" — named for her — Mrs. Cheyne endures the punishing transcontinental journey in a state of near-collapse. Doctors apply "crushed ice to Mrs. Cheyne's neck" as they "toiled up the long grades" [009-047]. Her suffering is measured in the train's speed: "the speed was not enough for Mrs. Cheyne; and the heat, the remorseless August heat, was shaking her back to unconsciousness" [009-057].

### Reunion

Upon reaching Gloucester, Mrs. Cheyne is briefly mentioned as still fragile but recovering with the knowledge that Harvey is safe. Her final word to Harvey — asking if the fishermen "can't run the 'Constance' over to Gloucester" — shows her characteristic combination of wealth and maternal concern [009-084].

## 作用与意义

Mrs. Cheyne represents the domestic sphere that Harvey leaves behind — and to which he never fully returns. Her overprotective love, though well-intentioned, is shown as a contributing factor to Harvey's initial weakness. The novel suggests that her maternal anxiety, combined with her husband's wealth, created the "nuisance" Harvey was at the story's opening [001-002]. Her naming of the private car "Constance" — her own name — symbolizes how the Cheyne fortune is built around and for the family, yet ultimately cannot protect its members from the world.
