# East Gloucester

East Gloucester is the neighborhood of Gloucester, Massachusetts, where Disko Troop's family makes their home and where Harvey Cheyne returns to visit after his transformation in *Captains Courageous*. The area represents the rooted, working-class world of the fishing community that Harvey grows to respect.

## The Troop Family Home

After his months at sea aboard the *We're Here*, Harvey goes to East Gloucester to find Dan Troop's family. The visit is a key moment in Harvey's development—the wealthy boy from California seeks out the modest home of the fishing family who changed his life:

> "They went out on the trolley to East Gloucester, where they tramped through the woods to the Troop homestead." [008-125]

The trolley ride itself is a detail that grounds the story in late-19th-century Gloucester, when electric streetcars connected the city's neighborhoods.

## Harvey's Return

At the story's conclusion, Harvey comes back to East Gloucester to reconnect with the Troop family and share his plans for the future. His willingness to return repeatedly signals his genuine transformation from spoiled rich boy to someone who values the fishing community:

> "So Harvey came back to East Gloucester, and spent half a day explaining his plans to old Troop." [010-078]

The time Harvey spends in East Gloucester is marked by earnest conversation—he lays out his vision and seeks Disko Troop's counsel, demonstrating the respect he has earned through his months of labor on the Banks.

## Summer Visitors

East Gloucester also attracted summer boarders from the cities, a phenomenon that the year-round residents viewed with a mixture of amusement and disdain. Uncle Salters mentions them:

> "There's goin' to be a heap o' summer boarders araound East Gloucester this fall." [007-017]

The influx of tourists seeking the picturesque fishing port contrasts with the hard labor of the fishing families who call East Gloucester home. This tension between the working waterfront and the emerging tourist economy reflects broader changes in coastal New England at the turn of the century.
