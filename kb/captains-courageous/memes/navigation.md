# Navigation

Navigation at sea, as practiced by the fishermen of the Grand Banks fleet, is a practical skill acquired through experience rather than formal instruction. The captains of the Gloucester schooners navigate the North Atlantic using a combination of dead reckoning, knowledge of currents and weather patterns, and intimate familiarity with the fishing grounds.

## Navigation in the Fleet

Disko Troop, captain of the *We're Here*, exemplifies the practical navigator. His knowledge is gained through years at sea rather than book learning. When Harvey Cheyne attempts to display his formal education aboard the schooner, the crew's attitude toward such theoretical knowledge is dismissive — one deckhand remarks sarcastically that Harvey "warn't so cussed weak on navigation" [009-180], reflecting the fishermen's preference for hands-on seamanship over classroom instruction.

## Traditional Methods

The fishermen navigate by dead reckoning, reading the sea and sky. Disko Troop is known throughout the fleet for his ability to find fish where others cannot, a skill that combines navigational expertise with an uncanny sense of the cod's movements. The chart is a working tool — referenced in the doleful ballad of the lost fisherman — "Bring forth the chart, the doleful chart" — as the crucial instrument for locating position [006-036].
