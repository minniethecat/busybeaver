# Sixteenth Street

Sixteenth Street in Chicago serves as the critical railroad transfer point in the climactic cross-country dash near the end of *Captains Courageous*. It is where Harvey Cheyne Sr.'s private car, the *Constance*, switches from the western Santa Fe line to the eastern New York Limited for the final leg of the journey to Gloucester.

## Railroad Hub

The street functions as the junction between two major rail systems. Mr. Cheyne's urgent telegraph to his wife specifies the connection precisely:

> "Connect with New York Limited at Sixteenth Street, Chicago, Tuesday next." [009-024]

The Atchison, Topeka, and Santa Fe Railroad delivered the private car from the West Coast, and at Sixteenth Street responsibility passed to the eastern line. The transfer was a matter of carefully coordinated logistics:

> "Hand over my private car Constance from Santa Fe at Sixteenth Street next Tuesday p.m. on N.Y. Limited." [009-028]

## The Cross-Country Dash

The urgency of the Cheyne family's journey to reach Harvey in Gloucester centers on railroad logistics. The Santa Fe management in Chicago personally oversaw the transfer:

> "The Atchison, Topeka, and Santa Fe management, even into Chicago, made it their personal business to see the Constance through." [009-060]

For the Cheyne organization, the distance from San Diego to Chicago was a test of the railroad network's capabilities:

> "From San Diego to Sixteenth Street, Chicago, let the magic carpet work." [009-042]

The switching operations required precise timing and coordination across multiple railroad companies. The private car passed through Sixteenth Street in what was described as a blur, with the exhausted Mrs. Cheyne barely aware of the transfer:

> She was in a doze and did not notice the complex switching operations at Sixteenth Street. [009-058]
