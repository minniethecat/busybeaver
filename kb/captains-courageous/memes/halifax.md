# Halifax

Halifax, the capital of Nova Scotia, is a port city that supplies men to the Grand Banks fishing fleet. As a major center of the Canadian Maritime Provinces, Halifax and its surrounding communities contribute a significant portion of the fishermen who work alongside the Gloucester crews.

## Nova Scotian Fishermen

The Gloucester fleet draws crew from across the Maritime Provinces, and Nova Scotians form a distinctive presence among the fishermen. The men from Nova Scotia — including Halifax and its hinterlands — are known for their seamanship and their particular accent, described by the crew of the *We're Here* as coming from "the innards of Cape Breton" where "the farmers speak homemade Scotch" [002-039]. To call a Gloucester man a Nova Scotian is not well received, suggesting a rivalry between the Massachusetts and Canadian fishermen [005-002].

## The Maritime Connection

Halifax represents the broader Canadian maritime world that is tightly linked to the New England fishing industry. Nova Scotian vessels and crews share the Grand Banks with the Gloucester fleet, and the communities on both sides of the border are bound by common dangers. The list of the lost, read aloud at the Gloucester memorial, includes names from Nova Scotia: "Robert Beavon, 29, married, native of Pubnico, Nova Scotia" and "D. Hawkins, alias Williams, 34, married, Shelbourne, Nova Scotia" [010-099]. These names underscore that the sea takes its toll without regard for national borders.
