# Galley

The small, fiercely hot kitchen tucked below deck on a Grand Banks fishing schooner. Dominated by a cast-iron stove that glows red during meal preparation, the galley is both the source of the crew's sustenance and a social center — the one warm, dry place on a vessel otherwise exposed to the North Atlantic's cold and wet (004-001, 005-034).

## 定义

On a fishing schooner like the *We're Here*, the galley is a compact space — often no larger than a closet — equipped with a coal-fired stove, a small work surface, and storage for provisions. The cook, usually a crew member who has aged out of the physically demanding work of dory fishing, rules the galley as his domain (004-001). Meals are simple and hearty: salt pork, cod's tongues and sounds, ship's biscuit, coffee strong enough to float a iron spoon.

The galley stove serves double duty: it cooks the food and provides the only heat on the schooner. On cold, wet nights, crew members gravitate toward the galley, standing in the narrow companionway to dry their oilskins and warm their hands (005-034).

## 具体细节

### The Cook

The cook on the *We're Here* is an older man — "the cook balanced behind the tiny galley over the glare of the stove" — who has traded the physical demands of dory work for the different demands of feeding a hungry crew (004-001). He rises before the crew to start the coffee, works through the day preparing meals and cleaning, and is the last to sleep. The cook occupies an ambiguous position in the ship's hierarchy: not a fisherman, but essential; not an officer, but with authority over his galley.

### Meals at Sea

Food on the Banks is fuel, not cuisine. Breakfast might be fried salt pork and ship's biscuit; dinner (the midday meal) is the largest, often a stew of cod tongues and sounds mixed with pork scraps; supper is whatever can be quickly prepared after the exhausting work of dressing down (002-129). The galley stove burns coal, and when the galley is "red-hot as the fort's" during meal preparation, the crew knows food is coming (005-034).

### Social Hub

The galley is where crew members gather to talk, complain, tell stories, and escape the weather. It is a democracy of warmth: captain and cabin boy alike crowd into the narrow space, the hierarchy of the deck temporarily suspended. For Harvey, the galley is one of the few places on the schooner that feels like the civilized world he left behind — a place with a fire, hot food, and human company.
