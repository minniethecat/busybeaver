# Manuel

The Portuguese fisherman who pulls Harvey Cheyne from the North Atlantic and speaks the first words the boy hears in his new world: "You are with me in the dory — Manuel my name, and I come from schooner *We're Here* of Gloucester" (001-043). Manuel is a figure of almost supernatural vitality — he seems to have two pairs of hands and a head of cast-iron — and his brilliant smile becomes one of the reassuring constants of Harvey's life aboard the schooner (001-044).

## 身份与背景

Manuel is one of the many Portuguese fishermen who worked the Grand Banks out of Gloucester in the late nineteenth century. His English is accented but fluent, and he occupies a respected place among the *We're Here*'s crew. His skill with a splitting knife is unmatched — when the dories return, Manuel stands knee-deep among cod in the pen, his knife a blur, dispatching and splitting fish faster than any two other men (002-147).

## 外貌与性格

Manuel's defining characteristic is his energy. He blows the conch-shell horn with astonishing force, "swaying with the sway of the flat-bottomed dory" (001-044). He rows with effortless power. His smile — "a brilliant smile that Harvey learned to know well later" — is a beacon of warmth in the cold, hard world of the Banks (002-073).

## 主要经历

Manuel's rescue of Harvey is the novel's inciting incident. In the fog, he hears something that might be a cry, rows toward it, and pulls the drowning boy into his dory. He then blows his conch to summon the *We're Here* (001-043, 001-044). Throughout the voyage, Manuel remains a steady, smiling presence — never the center of attention, but always there, working, smiling, setting an example of cheerful competence.
