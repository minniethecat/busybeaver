# Cape Cod

Cape Cod is a prominent hook-shaped peninsula extending into the Atlantic Ocean from the southeastern corner of mainland Massachusetts. In *Captains Courageous*, it appears as both a geographic landmark familiar to New England fishermen and a reference point for the maritime storytelling tradition of the region.

## In the Story

Cape Cod appears first in the tragic tale of the *Active* of Portland, as recounted by Uncle Salters around the cabin of the *We're Here*. The abandoned vessel was found "leakin' off Cape Cod Light" during a terrible gale [004-037]. This somber story, set in the waters off Cape Cod, forms part of the oral tradition that the fishermen share during their long days at sea.

Tom Platt, the old man-o'-war's man who sailed on the USS *Ohio*, identifies as a Cape Cod man and listens to Salters's tale "in scornful silence," having heard the story more than twenty years prior [005-018]. His Cape Cod origin marks him as an insider to New England's fishing traditions and the region's deep maritime history. The Cape Cod reference situates the novel within the broader geography of New England seafaring, connecting the Grand Banks fishing grounds to the coastal communities that supply their crews.

## Geographic Significance

Cape Cod Light (also known as Highland Light) has been a critical navigational aid since 1797, marking the dangerous outer shore of the Cape for vessels approaching from the Grand Banks. For the Gloucester fishing fleet, rounding Cape Cod marked the transition from the open Atlantic to the more sheltered waters leading toward Massachusetts Bay and home port.
