# Catholic Faith

The Roman Catholic faith, as practiced by the Portuguese fishermen in the *Captains Courageous* narrative, represents a cultural and religious dimension distinct from the predominantly Protestant New England fishing community. The novel references Catholicism primarily through the character of Manuel, a Portuguese crew member aboard the *We're Here*.

## Portuguese Catholic Identity

Manuel's Catholicism is established early through direct dialogue. When asked, he confirms his religious identity with the simple statement, "You're a Roman Catholic, then?" — a question that draws an affirmative response [004-011]. His faith is woven into his identity as a Portuguese fisherman in a fleet dominated by Yankee Protestants from Gloucester and the surrounding Massachusetts ports.

## Cultural Tensions

The religious difference between the Portuguese Catholics and New England Protestants surfaces in conversation. Disko Troop, the pragmatic captain of the *We're Here*, frames the contrast between progress-oriented American Protestantism and the older traditions of Catholic Europe with a pointed rhetorical question: "How about progress and Catholic superstitions?" [008-097]. This remark captures the cultural distance between the two communities, even as they work side by side on the fishing grounds.

## Context in the Fleet

The Portuguese fishermen — Manuel and his countrymen — maintain their Catholic identity while fully integrated into the working life of the Gloucester fleet. Religious practice does not appear to be a source of active conflict aboard the *We're Here*; rather, it is noted as a fact of cultural difference within the diverse crew. The fleet includes men from Nova Scotia, Portugal, and various New England ports, each bringing distinct religious and cultural backgrounds to the shared work of fishing.
