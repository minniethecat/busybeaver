# Cook

The **cook** is an essential crew member aboard the fishing schooners of _Captains Courageous_, responsible for preparing meals that sustain the fishermen through their grueling work on the Grand Banks.

## The Cook of the _We're Here_

The _We're Here_ carries a memorable cook: "a huge, jet-black negro" (002-131) who moves "noiseless as a black shadow" (002-155) about the vessel. He is a man of few words — when addressed, "the cook nodded" (002-133). Despite his silence, his presence is felt throughout the ship's daily life.

## The Cook's Work

The cook's day begins early, collecting "the blood-ends" — the juicy fragments of fish — that the crew have saved overnight (003-001). He works in the cramped galley, "his shoulders against the locker" while preparing meals (004-003). His domain is the tiny galley where "the black bulk of the cook balanced behind the tiny galley" (004-001).

## The Cook's Status

While the cook is not a fisherman, he is a respected member of the crew. Dan notes approvingly that "Dad ships a good cook ef he do suffer with his brother" — Disko's brother being Salters, a farmer rather than a fisherman by trade (002-127). The cook's meals are a high point of the day: the crew need no call to dinner, as "the cook had no need to cry 'second half'" before the men came aft (002-129).

## Dan as Cook's Helper

Dan Troop serves as "cook's helper an' everything else that's boy's work aboard" the _We're Here_ (001-052), assisting the cook with his duties. This arrangement reflects the hierarchical structure of the fishing vessel, where boys perform supporting roles under the guidance of experienced crew members.
