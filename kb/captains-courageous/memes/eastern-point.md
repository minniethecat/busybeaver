# Eastern Point

Eastern Point is a prominent headland at the eastern entrance to Gloucester Harbor, Massachusetts. It serves as the primary navigational landmark for fishing vessels returning from the Grand Banks, marking the approach to home waters [001-074].

## Navigational Role

For the crew of the *We're Here*, sighting Eastern Point signals the end of a fishing season. Disko Troop tells Harvey that they may "see Eastern Point about September," indicating the duration of their voyage [001-074]. When the schooner races home in the final chapters, Dan identifies Eastern Point on approach, noting its appearance confirms they are "right on wind" for Gloucester [003-086], [005-040].

## In the Story

Eastern Point appears at key moments of arrival and departure. As the *We're Here* returns to Gloucester, Eastern Point "comes inter view," greeted with recognition by the crew [008-051]. The area around Eastern Point also hosts a boardinghouse where fishermen stay between voyages [010-003]. The landmark carries somber associations as well: a fisherman was "married, drowned in dory off Eastern Point," linking it to the dangers of the fishing life [010-123].
