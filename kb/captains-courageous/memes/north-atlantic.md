# North Atlantic

The North Atlantic Ocean is the opening setting of *Captains Courageous*. The story begins aboard a great transatlantic liner steaming through the North Atlantic fog, somewhere between Europe and the Grand Banks [001-001].

## The Opening Scene

The "weather door of the smoking-room had been left open to the North Atlantic fog" as the liner proceeds through heavy weather [001-001]. The fog is described as thick and disorienting—conditions that set the stage for Harvey Cheyne's fall overboard. The North Atlantic in this passage represents both the physical danger of the sea and the boundary between Harvey's pampered life aboard the liner and the harsh reality of the fishing world he is about to enter.

## In the Story

The North Atlantic fog is never merely weather; it is the narrative device that separates Harvey from his old life. When he is pulled from the water by the dory of the *We're Here*, he crosses from the transatlantic world of luxury liners into the working world of the Grand Banks fishing fleet [001-032]. The fog that opens the novel gives way to the clear air of the Banks, marking Harvey's moral and practical education.
