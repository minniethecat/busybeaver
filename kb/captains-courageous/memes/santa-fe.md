# Santa Fe Railroad

The Atchison, Topeka, and Santa Fe Railroad is the western rail line that carries Mr. Cheyne's private car, the *Constance*, from California to Chicago in the climactic cross-country dash of *Captains Courageous*. The line represents the reach of Cheyne's railroad empire and the speed of late-19th-century continental travel.

## The Cross-Country Journey

When Mr. Cheyne learns that his son Harvey has been rescued in Gloucester, he mobilizes his vast railroad network to reunite the family. The Santa Fe line handles the first and longest leg of the transcontinental journey:

> "Hand over my private car Constance from Santa Fe at Sixteenth Street next Tuesday p.m. on N.Y. Limited." [009-028]

The decision to route the private car via Santa Fe rather than another line was strategic:

> "Instead of handing him over to the Santa Fe straight through to Chicago," meaning the car would travel the entire western portion on Santa Fe rails [009-032].

## Cheyne's Railroad Empire

The Santa Fe line is a key component of Mr. Cheyne's extensive business interests. As a railroad magnate who built his fortune through aggressive consolidation, Cheyne commands deference from railroad management across the continent.

The Santa Fe managers treated the Constance's passage as a personal priority:

> "The Atchison, Topeka, and Santa Fe management, even into Chicago, made it their personal business to see the Constance through." [009-060]

After the journey, the Santa Fe men discussed the achievement with pride:

> "The Atchison, Topeka, and Santa Fe men, discussing life later, said the run from San Diego to Chicago was a record." [009-060]

The speed of the cross-country run—"sixty hours from here to Chicago"—demonstrated the capabilities of the American railroad network that Cheyne had helped build [009-026].
