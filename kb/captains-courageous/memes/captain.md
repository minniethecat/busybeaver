# Captain

The **captain** (also called **skipper**) is the master and commanding authority of a fishing schooner in _Captains Courageous_. The role demands deep knowledge of the sea, the fish, and the men under one's command.

## Disko Troop as Captain

Disko Troop, captain of the _We're Here_ of Gloucester, introduces himself to Harvey with quiet dignity: "I'm Disko Troop o' the _We're Here_ o' Gloucester" (001-065). He commands not only the vessel but the respect of the entire fishing fleet. As Dan explains, "Dad he knows the cod, an' the Fleet they know Dad knows" (003-003).

## The Captain's Knowledge

A captain's authority rests on knowledge. Disko Troop studies the fish with an almost mystical concentration — "he was studying the fish — pitting his knowledge and experience on the Banks against the roving cod in his own sea" (003-004). He thinks about weather, gales, currents, and food supplies from the perspective of the cod itself.

## The Skipper's Authority

The captain's authority on a fishing vessel is absolute. "An angry skipper makes an unhappy crew," the narrative observes (006-011). The crew defer to their skipper's judgment even when they disagree. Salters acknowledges Disko's authority plainly: "you bein' skipper here" (006-020). The captain of the _Carrie Pitman_, Nick Brady, is known as "the meanest man on the Banks" (003-073), showing how a captain's temperament defines the character of a vessel.

## Other Captains

The novel features several captains beyond Disko Troop. The French captain familiar with the Banks' fishing grounds tells Harvey about the waters they work (008-053). Other captains include those of tugs, water-boats, and ocean liners who form part of the maritime world Cheyne navigates in his search for his son (010-081).
