# Constance (Private Car)

The *Constance* is Mr. Cheyne's opulent private railroad car, named after his wife Constance Cheyne. It serves as the family's mobile residence during the cross-country journey from San Diego to Gloucester, Massachusetts, after Harvey Cheyne is found [009-022].

## Description and Features

The private car is described as a "great and gilded" vehicle, reflecting Cheyne's immense railroad wealth. It is attached to various railroad lines—including the Santa Fe, New York Central, and Hudson—as it crosses the continent [009-024], [009-042]. The car contains private quarters for the Cheyne family: Mr. Cheyne, Mrs. Cheyne (Constance), and their son Harvey [009-026].

## In the Story

The *Constance* plays a central role in Chapter 9, where Mr. Cheyne orchestrates a rapid cross-country journey to reach Harvey in Gloucester. Cheyne handles the logistics of the private car himself, treating it "as if she might have been a boat" [009-059]. The car is expedited across multiple railroad systems, with the Limited whisking it into Buffalo [009-061].

Mrs. Cheyne, unaccustomed to anything less than perfect service, is noted to be more demanding than her husband. Cheyne reflects that "Constance would never have allowed" the rough-and-ready arrangements that satisfied him [009-064]. Upon reaching the East Coast, Harvey asks if "they can't run the 'Constance' over to Gloucester," eager to reunite with his parents in familiar surroundings [009-084].
