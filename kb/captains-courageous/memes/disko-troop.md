# Disko Troop

Captain and owner of the Gloucester fishing schooner *We're Here*, Disko Troop is the moral and practical center of the novel. He knows the Grand Banks as a scholar knows his library — every rise and hollow of the sea floor, every current and cod ground, every sign of weather and fish. He treats Harvey Cheyne not as a millionaire's son but as a pair of hands that must earn their keep, and in doing so gives the boy the only thing his father's wealth never could: honest work at a fair wage (001-058, 002-163).

## 身份与背景

Disko Troop is a Gloucester fishing captain of the old school — he has worked the Banks since boyhood and owns his own schooner, a mark of success in the competitive world of salt cod fishing. He is married with a son, Dan, who sails with him as a full crew member. His authority aboard the *We're Here* is absolute but never arbitrary: he commands by competence, not by rank (001-058).

## 外貌与性格

Disko is described as a "small, thickset, clean-shaven man with gray eyebrows" who sits on the step leading to the quarterdeck, one eye always on the craft around him and the other on the telltale fly at the mainmast-head (001-058). He is a man of few words and dry humor — when Harvey first sees the cod piled in the pens and stares, Disko says simply: "These are cod. They ain't Damarskus figs" (002-163).

His defining trait is his absolute refusal to be impressed by wealth or status. When Harvey announces his father's millions, Disko's response is practical: here is a boy who needs to work, and here is work that needs doing. He offers ten and a half dollars a month — the standard rate for a second boy — and expects a full day's labor in return (001-103).

## 主要经历

### The Captain's Judgment

Disko's skill as a captain is demonstrated in countless small decisions: where to anchor, when to move, whether to hand-line or set trawl. He reads the water, the sky, and the behavior of fish with an intuition that comes from decades of experience. When the fog drops, Disko navigates by dead reckoning and instinct — he has known these waters since he was Harvey's age (002-175).

### Harvey's Education

Disko's treatment of Harvey is the engine of the boy's transformation. He does not coddle, does not explain, does not apologize. He assigns work and expects it done. When Harvey complains, Disko is unmoved; when Harvey succeeds, Disko offers no praise — only more work. This is not cruelty but pedagogy: the Banks teach themselves to those who will learn, and Disko's job is simply to put the boy in the way of the lesson.

## 作用与意义

Disko Troop represents a vanishing American ideal in Kipling's vision: the self-reliant, skilled working man whose authority derives from competence rather than capital. He is the counterweight to Harvey's father — one man builds railroads and millions, the other builds character and cod-pens, and the novel suggests that the latter work is the more essential.
