# Compass

The **compass** is a navigational instrument that indicates direction relative to magnetic north. In _Captains Courageous_, the compass is a vital tool aboard the fishing schooners working the Grand Banks, where fog, open sea, and the absence of landmarks make it the sole means of maintaining course and position.

## The Compass Aboard the We're Here

Disko Troop, the captain of the _We're Here_, relies on his knowledge of the Banks and the compass to navigate. He is described as "a master artist who knew the Banks blindfold," glancing at "the tiny compass in the window of the house" — the deckhouse where the wheel and navigational instruments are kept — to confirm his position (003-125). The compass sits in the small window of the deckhouse, always within sight of the helmsman.

## Practical Use at Sea

The compass on a fishing schooner is not merely an instrument but the foundation of daily work. Every course change, every decision to run before a gale or hold position on the Banks, depends on the compass reading. When Disko works the crew "mercilessly" and orders the hold restowed "into smaller compass, to make more room for the fish," the phrase "smaller compass" uses the compass as a metaphor for ordered, compact arrangement — a linguistic echo of the instrument's role in imposing order on the vast, formless sea (007-064).
