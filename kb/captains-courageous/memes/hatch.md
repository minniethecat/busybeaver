# Hatch

The hatches of the fishing schooner [[were-here|We're Here]] are the deck openings that provide access to the vessel's interior — the hold where the catch is stored and the crew quarters below. The main-hatch lies between the two masts, while the fore-hatch leads to the foc'sle where the men eat and sleep.

## The Main-Hatch

The main-hatch sits open between the two masts, serving as the primary access point for moving fish into the hold and for crew members climbing below. After a day's fishing, the men scramble down the hatch for meals, passing salt and stowing the day's catch.

> The darkness of the open main-hatch between the two masts. (002-051)

> Dan dropped into the hold, where Harvey heard him banging around. (002-124)

## Working the Hatches

During fishing operations, crew members move up and down the hatches constantly — carrying salt, stowing cod, and fetching gear. Long Jack roars down the hatch to summon the second half of the crew for their meals. At the end of a watch, the men slip below by twos and threes.

> Long Jack roared down the hatch, and the "second half" scrambled up. (002-144)

Commands travel through the hatches as naturally as the men themselves. Disko growls up the hatchway from the cabin, and they can hear him suck his pencil as he works out courses and soundings.

> Disko in the cabin growled up the hatchway, and they could hear him suck his pencil. (002-088)

## The Fore-Hatch and Foc'sle

The fore-hatch leads to the foc'sle (forecastle), the crew's living quarters in the bow. Below the fore-hatch are the bunks where the men sleep between watches, the three-cornered table running from bows to foremast, and the smells of oilskins, fried fish, and burnt grease that define life below decks.

Uncle Salters sits stiffly on the main-hatch nursing his stung hands after a wave catches him wrong.

> Uncle Salters sat stiffly on the main-hatch nursing his stung hands. (003-073)

## Rest on the Hatches

The hatches also serve as resting places. At the end of watch, the boys collapse on the main hatch, deeply asleep, and are rolled into their berths by the next man on deck.

> He found two boys in two tumbled heaps side by side on the main hatch, so deeply asleep that he actually rolled them to their berths. (002-177)
