# California

California is the Pacific-coast state that features prominently in the latter half of *Captains Courageous* as the hub of Harvey Cheyne Sr.'s vast railroad and lumber empire. From San Diego to Los Angeles and northward, the state's infrastructure and politics are entangled with the Cheyne family's fortunes.

## In the Story

Cheyne's business operations in the American West are under pressure as the novel reaches its climax. He faces "a devastating strike" in his Oregon lumber camps, and "the legislature of the State of California, which has no love for its makers, was preparing open war against him" [009-003]. This political hostility toward railroad magnates reflects the Gilded Age tensions between industry and state government.

When Harvey is found alive, the California rail network becomes the backbone of the rescue operation. "Los Angeles called to San Diego and Barstow that the Southern California engineers might know and be ready in their lonely roundhouses" [009-042], setting off a chain of communications that spans the continent from the Pacific to Chicago and eventually to Gloucester.

Later, as Cheyne and Harvey travel east together, they discuss the privileged sons of wealthy families. Cheyne remarks that "California's full of 'em" [010-037], contrasting the idle rich of the West Coast with Harvey's newfound competence and character, forged by his months at sea.

## Geographic and Economic Context

The California depicted in the novel is the booming post-Gold Rush state of the 1890s, dominated by railroad barons and industrialists like Cheyne. The Southern California railroad network centered on Los Angeles, San Diego, and Barstow, with connections eastward to the Atchison, Topeka, and Santa Fe line. This infrastructure enables the rapid cross-continental search that reunites the Cheyne family.
