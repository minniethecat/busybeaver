# Ship's Wheel

The ship's wheel is the steering control of the fishing schooner [[were-here|We're Here]]. Mounted aft, it is the station of Captain [[disko-troop|Disko Troop]], who steers the vessel through fog, gales, and the crowded waters of the Grand Banks fishing fleet. The wheel is lashed when not actively steered — a common practice on sailing vessels when hove-to or at anchor.

## The Captain's Station

The wheel is where Disko Troop exercises command. From the wheel he reads the wind, watches the sails, and calls orders to the crew. The galley clock hangs in plain sight of the wheel, allowing the captain to track the watches while steering.

> Troop, in the chocolate and yellow oilskins, stood at the wheel, and the galley clock hung in plain sight of the wheel. (002-034)

## Steering in Weather

In heavy seas, the wheel can kick and twitch as waves strike the rudder. The helmsman must anticipate the schooner's movement, easing the wheel at the right moment to keep the vessel steady. Disko lashes the wheel to his taste — setting it at a fixed position when the wind and current are steady enough.

> The lashed wheel groaned and kicked softly. (002-177)

> Disko did something to the wheel that checked the schooner's way. (003-121)

## The Wheel and the Night Watch

At night, in thick weather with rising wind, the elder men each in turn stump aft to look at the wheel. They check that the anchor holds, veer out cable against chafing, and glance at the dim anchor-light. The wheel groans and kicks softly in the shifts of the light wind — the soundtrack of the night watch.

> The lashed wheel groaned and kicked softly, the riding-sail slatted a little in the shifts of the light wind, the windlass creaked. (002-177)

The boys — Harvey and Dan — sleep deeply on the main hatch while the men above them work the wheel in rotation, each taking their share of the night's labor.

## Signals and Maneuvers

The wheel is also the instrument of precision at sea. When the schooner needs to check her way — picking up a dory, hailing another vessel, or avoiding a hazard — Disko works the wheel with small, expert movements. His face at such moments is blank, unreadable, the face of a man who has steered through a thousand nights on the Banks.

> Disko's face was as blank as the circle of the wheel. (003-091)
