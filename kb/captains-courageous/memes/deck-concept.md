# Deck

The deck of a fishing schooner was no leisure space. On the *We're Here*, it was a factory floor, a storm battleground, and a gathering place — often all within the same watch. Harvey Cheyne's first encounter with deck life comes when he "staggered over the wet decks to the nearest rail" [001-034] after being pulled from the sea.

## Workspace

The deck was where the day's catch was processed — "dressing down" — the cod split, salted, and stowed in the hold. The deck was perpetually wet with spray and fish slime. During a heavy catch, every man worked on deck until the fish were dressed.

## The Art of Dressing Down

Dressing down was the central ritual of deck work. When Disko called out, "Soon as we're through supper we git to dressing-down" [002-125], every man knew his station. Salt was scattered across the deck pens; cod were split with practiced strokes and pitched below: "Harvey pitched the fish by twos and threes down the hatch" [002-150].

The work was wet, cold, and ceaseless during a good strike. Even the fog could not stop it — "half an hour later, as they were dressing down, the Bank fog dropped on them, 'between fish and fish,' as they say. The men stopped dressing-down without a word" [003-077]. Only when the fog made further fishing impossible did the knives pause.

## Battlefield

In heavy weather, the deck became a dangerous place:

> "Fix yerself an' go on deck. Dad wants to see you" [001-052]

Five times in one storm watch, Harvey and Dan clung to the foregaff as waves swept the deck, "holding on for dear life" [008-042]. A man could be swept overboard, a dory smashed, or a wave could fill the deck faster than the pumps could drain her.

## Social Space

Between watches, the deck served as the crew's gathering place — for singing, storytelling, and the rare leisure hour. It was on deck that Harvey first heard the songs and stories that would shape his understanding of the fishing life.
