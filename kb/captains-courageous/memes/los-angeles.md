# Los Angeles

Los Angeles is a California city that features in the narrative as a node in the vast Cheyne business empire. It serves as the home port of the Cheyne family yacht *Constance* and as a communications hub during the frantic effort to reunite Harvey Cheyne with his parents.

## The Cheyne Connection

Mr. Cheyne's business interests extend across the American West, and Los Angeles is one of the cities where his presence is felt. A fellow passenger on the transatlantic liner tells Harvey that the elder Cheyne "built one place at San Diego... another at Los Angeles" — part of an empire that includes "half a dozen railroads" and "half the lumber on the Pacific slope" [001-015]. The city is linked to the Cheyne name through these industrial and real estate holdings.

## The Constance and the Telegraph

When Harvey is found alive on the East Coast, the telegram network springs into action across the continent. Los Angeles becomes a key relay point in the chain of communications that coordinates Harvey's return. K. H. Wade wires from Los Angeles to inquire about the *Constance* — "The 'Constance' is at Los Angeles, isn't she, Miss Kinzey?" [009-060] — and the reply sets in motion the mobilization of the private train. The telegraph operators in Los Angeles send "frantic clicks" demanding updates: "We want to know why-why-why? General uneasiness developed and spreading" [009-061].

## The Cross-Continental Railroad Chain

Los Angeles initiates the railroad chain that will carry Harvey home: "Los Angeles called to San Diego and Barstow that the Southern California engineers might know and be ready in their lonely roundhouses; Barstow passed the word to the Atlantic and Pacific; and Albuquerque flung it the whole length of the Atchinson, Topeka, and Santa Fe management, even into Chicago" [009-062]. From San Diego to Sixteenth Street, Chicago, the "magic carpet" of rail is laid down across two thousand three hundred and fifty miles.
