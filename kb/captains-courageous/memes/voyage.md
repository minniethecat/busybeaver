# Voyage

A **voyage** is a journey by sea, often of considerable duration and distance. In _Captains Courageous_, the voyage is more than a means of travel — it is the shaping experience of Harvey Cheyne's life. The fishing voyage to the Grand Banks structures the entire narrative, lasting from late spring through the summer fishing season.

## The Fishing Voyage as Transformative Experience

Harvey Cheyne's voyage begins accidentally. Swept overboard from a transatlantic liner, he is rescued by the fishing schooner _We're Here_ and thrust into the hard world of the Banks fishery. His initial thought is of his "father's wealth on the voyage home" and the tale of rescue he will tell (001-058). He does not yet understand that the voyage itself will change him more than any rescue story could convey.

The voyage transforms Harvey physically and mentally. By the end, Disko Troop proposes that Harvey "ship as boy for a voyage or two" before advancing further in the fishing trade (009-181). The notion of "a voyage or two" represents the standard unit of a seaman's experience — a complete seasonal cycle that tests and proves a sailor's worth.

## Voyages as Oral History

Aboard the _We're Here_, the older fishermen recount voyages past as a form of entertainment and education. They read from "an old leather-bound volume, smelling of a hundred voyages" — a book whose pages carry the accumulated wisdom of generations of seafarers (005-025). Disko Troop himself "spoke of whaling voyages in the Fifties; of great she-whales slain beside their young; of death agonies on the black tossing seas" (005-031). These stories of past voyages form the oral culture of the fishing fleet, binding the men together through shared knowledge of the sea's dangers and rewards.
