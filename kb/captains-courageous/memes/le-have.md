# Le Have

Le Have is a port and fishing bank located on the southern coast of Nova Scotia. It is part of the chain of banks — Le Have, Western, Banquereau, St. Pierre, Green, and Grand — that the Gloucester fleet works seasonally in pursuit of cod [002-050].

## The Fishing Bank

Le Have Bank is one of the grounds where the *We're Here* and other Gloucester schooners fish. Disko Troop, educating Harvey in the geography of the North Atlantic fisheries, traces with his pencil "from berth to berth over the whole string of banks — Le Have, Western, Banquereau, St. Pierre, Green, and Grand" [002-050]. The bank lies on the approach to the Bay of Fundy and the Nova Scotia coast, putting it within range of both Canadian and American fishing vessels.

## Incidents at Le Have

The waters off Le Have have seen their share of misfortune. Otto, a previous crew member of the *We're Here*, went overboard on Le Have — lost in a gale, never to return. His disappearance left a vacancy that Harvey Cheyne, by providential coincidence, fills [002-016]. Another incident off Le Have involved a misunderstanding with French sailors from Miquelon: Disko reminds the crew not to provoke the French boats by calling them insulting names "same's you did off Le Have" [005-019].

## Geography

Le Have itself is situated on the southern shore of Nova Scotia, west of Halifax. The fishing bank that bears its name lies offshore in the North Atlantic, part of the continental shelf that provides the rich feeding grounds for cod. The area is described as having a distinct character — "This is Le Have on a Sunday mornin'" becomes a saying aboard ship, evoking the quiet solemnity of the place [008-113].
