# Le Have Bank

A fishing bank lying off the southeastern coast of Nova Scotia, named for the LaHave River and the French settlement that once stood at its mouth. Le Have Bank is part of the broader Scotian Shelf fishery, fished by Gloucester schooners as part of their seasonal migration between the Gulf of Maine and the Grand Banks.

## 地理位置

Le Have Bank lies approximately 50 miles southeast of Halifax, Nova Scotia, at the edge of the Scotian Shelf. It is separated from Georges Bank by the Northeast Channel and from the Grand Banks by the Laurentian Channel. Its depths range from 30 to 60 fathoms.

## 在书中的作用

Le Have is part of the mental map that every Gloucester captain carries — one of the named grounds that define the geography of the North Atlantic fishery. It represents the vastness of the fishermen's world: they range from Cape Cod to Labrador, from Georges to the Grand Banks, following the cod through the seasons.

## 航海/渔业特征

Le Have Bank shares the cold, nutrient-rich waters of the Labrador Current with the Grand Banks, though it is somewhat warmer and closer to shore. Cod, halibut, and pollock are the primary target species.
