# Marblehead

Marblehead is a historic seaport on the Massachusetts coast, situated on a rocky peninsula north of Boston. In *Captains Courageous*, the town appears through the lens of the fishermen's storytelling tradition, providing the setting for the notorious tale of Ben Ireson — a cautionary story debated heatedly in the cabin of the *We're Here*.

## In the Story

The Ben Ireson story occupies a significant passage in the novel's oral narrative tradition. Disko Troop tells Harvey that the popular version of the tale — the one made famous by Whittier's poem — is "all dead wrong from start to finish," and insists on setting the record straight with his father's firsthand account [004-035]. Ireson, skipper of the *Betty*, was accused of refusing to rescue the crew of the foundering *Active* off Cape Cod during the War of 1812.

The Marblehead townsfolk, according to Disko's version, were "mad at him not runnin' the risk" [004-037]. When survivors of the *Active* were rescued by a Truro man and "come into Marblehead with their own tale to tell, sayin' how Ireson had shamed his town," the public sentiment turned violently against the skipper [004-037]. Disko clarifies that it was not the women who tarred and feathered Ireson — "Marblehead women don't act that way" — but rather "a passel o' men an' boys" who carted him around town in an old dory [004-037].

Beyond the Ireson tale, Marblehead appears in the narrative through Abishai, a solitary trawler who sails "the last o' the old Marblehead heel-tappers," a schooner nearly seventy years old with a distinctive quarterdeck design that, as Dan explains, "they don't make ... any more" [004-093]. This detail anchors the novel's sense of a vanishing maritime tradition, with Marblehead's shipbuilding history fading into memory.

## Maritime Significance

In the late nineteenth century, Marblehead was well past its Revolutionary-era peak as a fishing port, but it remained an important part of New England's maritime fabric. The "Marblehead heel-tappers" were a distinctive design of fishing schooner known for their shallow draft and maneuverability — qualities essential for working the rocky New England coastline. Abishai's ancient vessel, the last of its kind, serves as a living museum piece in the novel, a reminder of earlier eras of American shipbuilding.
