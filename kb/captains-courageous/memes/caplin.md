# Caplin

A small, silvery fish of the smelt family that appears in enormous schools on the Grand Banks, pursued by cod and, in turn, eagerly harvested by fishermen as bait. In *Captains Courageous*, the caplin run is a moment of abundance and excitement — a nighttime spectacle when the sea boils with fish and every dory on the Banks races to fill its bait barrels (003-077).

## 定义

Caplin (*Mallotus villosus*) are small forage fish, typically six to eight inches long, that migrate to coastal waters to spawn in vast numbers. On the Grand Banks, their arrival triggers a feeding frenzy among cod, halibut, and other predatory fish — and a corresponding frenzy among the fishermen who depend on caplin for bait (003-077). A "caplin run" is the sudden appearance of a school near the surface, darkening the water and attracting every fishing vessel in sight.

The caplin is central to the Grand Banks ecosystem: cod follow the caplin schools, and fishermen follow the cod. Without caplin for bait, the hand-line fishery would be far less productive. The schooners carry barrels specifically for storing caplin, and when the run is on, every available hand works to scoop them up (003-077).

## 具体细节

### The Nighttime Run

The caplin run in Chapter 3 is one of the novel's most vivid set pieces. The Bank fog drops on the crew while they are dressing down, and then someone spots the caplin — the water darkening with millions of small fish. Dan and Harvey are roused from their bunks to help. The sea seems to boil with fish; the dories are launched even in darkness; every fisherman works frantically to fill barrels with the silvery bait before the school moves on (003-077).

### Bait for the Banks

Caplin are the primary bait for cod hand-lining on the Grand Banks. Each dory carries a supply of caplin, which are hooked through the body and lowered to the sea floor on weighted lines (003-036). Fresh caplin are preferred, but they can be salted for longer storage. A good supply of caplin can make the difference between a full pen and an empty one — cod will not strike a bare hook, and artificial lures are unknown in this fishery.

### The Caplin as Cultural Marker

The caplin run is more than a fishing event — it is a social occasion. The entire fleet converges on the school, and for a few hours the usual rhythms of isolation and competition give way to collective effort. Men who normally compete for fishing ground work side by side, filling barrel after barrel. The run is talked about for days afterward, a shared memory that binds the fleet together (003-077).
