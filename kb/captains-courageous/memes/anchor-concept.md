# Anchor

In the world of *Captains Courageous*, the anchor is more than a piece of iron — it is a tool of survival, a marker of position, and a symbol of prudence at sea. The schooner *We're Here* rides "easily at anchor" [001-058] in fair weather, while in fog the anchor becomes a crucial safety device.

## The Anchor in Dory Fishing

When fishing from a dory in deep water, the anchor — or "kelleg" — held the small boat in position over the fishing grounds. Dan and Harvey, caught in a white fog, are advised by Disko: "Heave over the anchor, Harve, and we'll fish a piece till the thing lifts" [008-046]. In two-hundred-fathom water, even a three-pound lead was barely adequate.

## The Cost of Lost Gear

Anchors and their roding were not cheap, and losing one was a serious matter. Disko enforced strict accountability: "Lost two this trip a'ready — on sandy bottom too — an' Dad says next one he loses, sure's fishin', he'll give him the kelleg" [003-049].

A doryman who repeatedly lost anchors risked both his skipper's displeasure and his share of the voyage's profits. At voyage's end, Harvey carefully secured "Uncle Salters's sea-boots and Penn's dory-anchor" [010-141], a ritual of responsibility learned through months of hard experience.

## The Anchor-Light

At night, the anchor-light served as both a navigational aid and a signal of presence. Disko's instruction to "Rouse up an' see ef our anchor-light's bright an' shinin'" [002-173] was a routine but essential check — a darkened light could mean collision in the crowded anchorage of the fleet.

## Anchoring in Fog

In the dense Bank fog, the anchor became a survival tool. When visibility dropped to nothing, the safest course was simply to anchor where you were and wait: "when we git thirty fathom we'll turn in like little men" [006-026]. The anchor transformed a drifting, vulnerable vessel into a stationary, defensible position — one of the oldest and most reliable safety measures at sea.
