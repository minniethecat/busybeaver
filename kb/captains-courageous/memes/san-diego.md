# San Diego

San Diego, California, is the Cheyne family's primary residence and the headquarters of Mr. Cheyne's vast railroad and lumber empire [009-022]. It is depicted as a place of immense wealth, far removed from the fishing communities of Gloucester.

## The Cheyne Residence

Mr. Cheyne built "a raw new palace in San Diego," where his wife Constance and her relatives "occupied a wing of great price." The estate reflects the family's status as one of the wealthiest in America, with Cheyne conducting his business from a veranda-room connected by telegraph to his far-flung enterprises [009-022], [009-084].

## In the Story

San Diego serves as the starting point for the Cheyne family's cross-country journey to Gloucester after Harvey is found. Mr. Cheyne dispatches his private railroad car, the *Constance*, from San Diego, routing it across multiple rail lines to reach the East Coast [009-024]. The contrast between San Diego's luxury and Gloucester's working-fishing-port character underscores the cultural distance between Harvey's old life and his experiences aboard the *We're Here*.
