# Deck

The deck of a Grand Banks fishing schooner is the ship's working surface and the primary arena of daily life aboard. On the *We're Here*, the deck is a crowded, chaotic space: cluttered with trawl-lines and dory tackle, slippery with fish slime, and crossed constantly by men hauling, dressing, and stowing the catch. It is also the place where Harvey Cheyne learns to find his feet — literally and figuratively — among the schooner's crew (002-176).

## 定义

The deck of a seventy-ton fishing schooner like the *We're Here* is a relatively small working platform, perhaps sixty feet from bow to stern. Every square foot serves a purpose: the forward section houses the windlass and its tackle, amidships is the fish-pen where cod are thrown for dressing down, and aft is the quarter-deck and the cabin roof — called the "house" — where the skipper keeps watch (003-098). Unlike the clean decks of a pleasure yacht, a fishing schooner's deck is perpetually wet, cluttered with gear, and slick with the residue of thousands of fish (001-034).

## 具体细节

### The Cluttered Deck

Harvey's first impression of the deck is of barely controlled chaos. The deck is "at all free" in no place: trawl-lines are lashed to windward of the house, dory tackle lies heaped amidships, and the pen boards stand ready to receive fish. Harvey must learn to pick his way through this maze without tripping — a skill that takes him several weeks and many bruises (003-098). Tom Platt, with his Navy discipline, is particularly sharp about keeping gear stowed properly.

### The Quarter-Deck

The quarter-deck, just forward of the cabin, is the command post of the schooner. From here Disko Troop directs the fishing, watches the weather, and keeps an eye on the fleet. It is also where the clock stands — the ship's timekeeper that chimes the watches and marks the rounds of work and rest (002-058, 002-177).

### Harvey Learns the Deck

Harvey's physical education begins on the deck. He staggers "over the wet decks to the nearest rail" on his first morning, seasick and unsteady (001-034). By the end of his first fishing trip, he dances "over the deck like an eel at ebb-tide," dodging men and gear with the unconscious grace of an experienced hand (003-116). The deck is the measure of his transformation.
