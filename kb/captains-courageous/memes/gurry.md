# Gurry

The bloody slurry of cod heads, guts, bones, and offal that accumulates in a special pen on the schooner's deck during the evening dressing-down. Gurry is the unavoidable byproduct of the Grand Banks cod fishery — evidence of a good day's catch and, when dumped overboard, a powerful chum that draws more fish to the schooner's fishing ground (002-147, 003-036).

## 定义

In the Grand Banks fishing trade, gurry refers specifically to the waste produced by gutting, heading, and splitting cod. Each fish yields its liver (saved for oil), its tongue and sounds (saved as crew delicacies), and a mass of entrails and bones that go into the gurry pen — a designated area of the deck where the offal accumulates during the night's work (002-147). At the end of dressing-down, or when the pen is full, the gurry is shoveled over the side.

The gurry serves a practical purpose beyond waste disposal: dumped into the sea, it creates a slick of fish oil and blood that attracts more fish to the area. A schooner that has been fishing the same ground for several days builds up a "gurry ground" that can hold fish in the vicinity (003-036). But too much gurry can also attract dogfish and other pests.

## 具体细节

### The Dressing-Down

When the dories return and the catch is forked into the deck pens, the real work begins. Manuel stands knee-deep among cod, flourishing a drawn knife (002-147). Each fish is processed in a few practiced strokes: the throat is cut, the head tossed aside into the gurry, the belly slit, the liver dropped into a basket for oil, the backbone removed, and the split fish passed to the salter. The gurry pen fills rapidly — on a good day, the crew works until the gurry is ankle-deep and the pens are finally empty of whole fish (002-147, 002-149).

### Harvey's Initiation

For Harvey, the gurry is yet another shock. He has never seen fish processed — he has only seen them on marble slabs, clean and dead, in city markets (003-042). The smell of gurry, the blood, the casual violence of the splitting knives — it is a world away from his life of private schools and luxury liners. Learning to work amid the gurry, to swab the pens and not gag at the smell, is part of his transformation.

### The Gurry Ground

Experienced captains like Disko Troop understand the value of a good gurry ground. By dumping gurry in the same area night after night, a schooner can create a feeding station that holds cod in the vicinity. But the practice also attracts unwanted visitors: dogfish that tear the cod-lines, and sometimes even sharks. The balance between attracting cod and attracting pests is part of the captain's art (003-036).
