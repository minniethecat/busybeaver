# Hook

The **hook** is the essential terminal tackle of the cod fisherman in _Captains Courageous_. Whether on a hand-line or a trawl, the baited hook is what catches the fish, and the entire economy and daily rhythm of life aboard the _We're Here_ revolves around hooks in the water.

## Types of Hooks

The fishermen use different hooks for different fishing methods. For hand-line fishing, they employ "heavy leads and double cod-hooks, all neatly coiled on square reels" (003-014). The double cod-hook has two points to secure a better hold on the cod's mouth. For trawling, a "coiled line" carries "a big hook each few feet" along its length (003-155) — these hooks are larger and stouter, designed to sit on the bottom and wait for fish to find the bait.

Harvey learns early about the danger of certain hooks: "These hooks are made of soft metal, from the French boats, and they break easy. But the Gloucester hooks are hard, and they'd break a man's finger" (002-152). This distinction between soft French hooks and hard Gloucester hooks is important practical knowledge for any Banks fisherman.

## Baiting and Casting

Dan teaches Harvey the rhythm of baiting: stringing "a salt clam on to the hook" and casting the line (003-026). The skill lies in the speed — a good fisherman baits and casts in one motion while a novice fumbles. When the fish are biting well, they will even "bite on the bare hook" without bait, as happens during a frenzied spell of fishing (003-138).

## Hooking the Fish

The term "poke-hooked" describes a fish that has taken the hook deep: "It's a big un. Poke-hooked, too" (003-133). A poke-hooked cod requires careful handling to extract the hook. Dan uses "the short wooden stick he called a 'gob-stick'" to wrench out the hook (003-029). When the hook fouls in seaweed, the fisherman must patiently pick the weed free before he can fish again (003-031).

## Trawl Hooks

Trawl fishing involves hundreds of hooks set along a single line. The crew spends hours "cutting off the fish, rebaiting the hooks, and passing them back" (004-106). The freshly baited hooks attract fish even as they are being set: "fish bit at the newly baited hooks from which their brethren had just been taken" (004-108). There are rules too — the law "strictly forbids more than one hook on one line when the dories are on the Banks," though some fishermen use illegal "scrowgers" with five or six hooks (008-016).

## Handling Gear

The hook is part of a larger system of lines and tackle. The fishermen use a "long iron hook" to catch the dory's stern-rope when coming alongside (002-073), and the dory-tackle hooks are raced to when a squall threatens (003-166). The "great hook-block" is invoked as an oath by Manuel when the catch is exceptionally heavy (003-135).
