# Cod Fishing

The daily labor that defines life aboard the *We're Here* and every other [[schooner]] on the [[grand-banks|Grand Banks]].

[[harvey-cheyne|Harvey Cheyne's]] education begins in a [[dory]] with a hand-line and ends at the splitting tables, where [[Manuel]] and [[penn|Penn]] stand knee-deep in cod (002-147). The work is the novel's moral engine: a boy who has never done anything useful learns to haul, gut, split, and salt, earning his place among professionals who judge a man by how many fish he forks.

## 定义

Grand Banks cod fishing in the 1890s is a hand-line fishery: each fisherman works from a small dory, lowering weighted lines with baited double hooks to the sea floor. The cod are hauled up one at a time — no rods, no reels, just strong arms and leather nippers to protect the hands from the line cutting through at sixty fathoms (003-036).

When the dories return to the [[schooner]], the catch is forked into deck pens. Then the processing begins: throat cut, head removed, body split open, backbone and tail removed, flesh packed in salt in the hold (002-127, 002-147). A full pen means the crew works at the splitting tables until they are "splitting themselves instead of the fish" — so exhausted they can barely stand (002-127). The salt cuts into split knuckles, and the North Atlantic cold makes every motion an effort.

> Dan looked over the low bulwarks at half a dozen dories rowing towards them over the shining, silky sea.
> — (002-061)

## 具体细节

### The Daily Cycle

At dawn, the dories launch and the fishermen row to their chosen ground. Lines are baited with [[caplin]], squid, or other small fish. The leads are heaved over the side and allowed to sink to the bottom — often sixty to eighty fathoms down. When a cod takes the hook, the fisherman hauls hand-over-hand, the heavy lead and struggling fish burning through even woolen nippers (003-036).

When Harvey catches his first fish — a [[halibut]], not a cod — he battles it for twenty minutes, his knuckles raw, his face purple, his body half-blinded by staring at sunlit ripples (003-036, 003-040). It is the first thing he has ever earned by physical effort, and the crew's acknowledgment — grudging, professional — marks the beginning of his acceptance.

> Harvey had been too absorbed to notice the sea-fret drifting over the water. He straightened up, his body aching to the bone, and saw the fog.
> — (003-040, 003-048)

### Quality and Pride

The cod fishery runs on professional pride. Fishermen are judged by the size and quality of their catch. The evening count is a public ritual: Manuel calls out "Two hundred and thirty-one" as he forks his catch aboard (002-073); [[uncle-salters|Uncle Salters]] laboriously counts his notch by notch (002-113); [[long-jack|Long Jack]] howls his exasperation at Salters' slowness.

> "Mother av delight! He's forkin' them wan by wan," howled Long Jack, as Uncle Salters got to work laboriously.
> — (002-113)

### Salt Cod and the Market

Fresh cod are split and packed in salt in the schooner's hold, where they cure during the remainder of the voyage. At [[gloucester|Gloucester]], the salt cod is offloaded and sold — the primary product of the Grand Banks fishery, a staple food across the Atlantic world. The schooner stays at sea until her hold is full, which may take weeks depending on the fishing.

### The Splitting Tables

Standing at the tables, each man works with a drawn knife. Cod's livers drop into a basket for oil; heads are tossed aside for gurry; the split bodies go into the hold. [[Manuel]] flourishes his knife with professional speed; [[penn|Penn]] toils valiantly but weakly (002-147, 002-152). The work continues until the pens are empty — midnight, sometimes later.

> Manuel stood knee deep among cod in the pen, flourishing drawn knife.
> — (002-147)

## 书中涉及场景

Cod fishing is the engine of the entire narrative:

- **The evening pen** (Chapter 2): The pens are piled high with cod, and Disko asks Manuel which water he fished, judging the catch by where it came from (002-127).

- **Harvey's first fish** (Chapter 3): Harvey battles a halibut for twenty minutes — his initiation into the world of work (003-036, 003-040).

- **Counting the catch** (Chapter 2): The ritual of counting — Manuel's efficient forkfuls, Uncle Salters' painstaking notches, Long Jack's complaints (002-113).

- **The splitting tables** (Chapter 2): The crew processes fish into the night, the salt stinging their cut hands, exhaustion setting in (002-147, 002-149).
