# Newfoundland

Newfoundland is the large island province at Canada's easternmost edge, whose cold waters and rocky shores define the northern boundary of the Grand Banks fishery. In *Captains Courageous*, Newfoundland represents the remote, harsh edge of the maritime world — a place of "pitiless beaches" [004-102] and hardy fishing communities that the Gloucester fleet regards with a mixture of respect and wariness.

## The Newfoundland Shore

When the *We're Here* encounters a trawler working the coast, Dan explains to Harvey that the vessel "runs in fer bait all along the coast. Oh, no, not home, he don't go. He deals along the south an' east shore up yonder" [004-102], nodding toward the Newfoundland beaches. Dan adds that his father "won't never take me ashore there. They're a mighty tough crowd" [004-102], suggesting the rough-and-tumble character of the Newfoundland baiting ports.

## Baiting Ports

The Newfoundland coast serves as a crucial bait supply line for the North Atlantic fishery. Manuel tells "tales of queer dances or fights away in the cold Newfoundland baiting-ports" [005-037], painting a picture of isolated harbors where fishermen from many nations congregate and occasionally clash. These ports, scattered along Newfoundland's deeply indented coastline, were essential infrastructure for the Grand Banks fleet.

## The Virgin of Newfoundland

Aboard a French bark from the Newfoundland fleet, Harvey discovers her cabin "all stuck round with glaring coloured prints of the Virgin — the Virgin of Newfoundland, they called her" [005-057]. This folk religious figure, associated with the perilous Newfoundland fishery, represents the Catholic maritime culture of the French and Portuguese fishermen who worked those waters.

## Maritime Dangers

Newfoundland's waters are among the most dangerous in the North Atlantic. The memorial tablet in the Gloucester church records losses connected to Newfoundland: "Schooner *Harry Randolph* dismasted on the way home from Newfoundland; Asa Musie, married, 32, Main Street, City, lost overboard" [010-133]. The combination of cold water, sudden storms, and ice made the Newfoundland run particularly hazardous.
