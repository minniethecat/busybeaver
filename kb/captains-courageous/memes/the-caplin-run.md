# The Caplin Run

One of the novel's most vivid set pieces: the sudden appearance of an enormous school of caplin near the *We're Here* at night, transforming the dark water into a churning mass of silver and sending every fisherman in the fleet into a frenzy of activity. For Harvey Cheyne, roused from his bunk to help, it is a moment of pure, exhausting wonder — a glimpse of the abundance and urgency that define life on the Banks (003-077).

## 背景

Caplin are small forage fish that migrate in vast schools to spawn. On the Grand Banks, their arrival is eagerly anticipated — cod follow the caplin, and fishermen follow the cod. But the caplin themselves are also harvested: they are the primary bait for cod hand-lining, and a schooner that fills its bait barrels during a caplin run is well-supplied for days of productive fishing (003-077).

## 经过

The Bank fog drops on the crew while they are dressing down the day's catch — "between fish and fish," as Dan says. Then someone spots the caplin: the water darkening, silver flashes in the gloom. Within minutes, the dories are launched despite the darkness and fog. Every available hand — including Harvey and Dan, hauled from their bunks — works frantically with dip nets and buckets, scooping the thrashing fish from the water. The sea seems to boil with life (003-077).

The run lasts only as long as the school remains near the surface — perhaps an hour, perhaps less. The fleet converges, each schooner's dories racing to claim their share. The air is filled with shouting, splashing, and the occasional blast of a conch horn. It is chaos, but it is a productive, joyful chaos — the kind of moment that fishermen remember and retell for years.

## 结果与影响

The caplin run fills the *We're Here*'s bait barrels, ensuring good fishing for the days ahead. For Harvey, it is another lesson in the rhythms of the Banks: the sudden arrival of opportunity, the need for immediate, coordinated action, and the satisfaction of shared labor toward a common goal.
