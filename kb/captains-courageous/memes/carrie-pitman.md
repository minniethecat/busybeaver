# Carrie Pitman

The *Carrie Pitman* is a Gloucester fishing schooner that operates in the North Atlantic fishing fleet alongside the *We're Here*. She hails from West Chatham, Massachusetts, and carries an unsavory reputation among the fishing community.

## The Vessel

The *Carrie Pitman* is identified as sailing from West Chatham with a new sail and jib [003-003]. She is one of several schooners in the fleet familiar to Disko Troop and the crew of the *We're Here*, mentioned in the same breath as other working vessels on the Grand Banks [004-068].

## Reputation

The *Carrie Pitman* has an unsavory reputation among the fleet. She is described as "her own Jonah" — a ship that brings bad luck — with one fisherman remarking disparagingly that someone "ought to be on the Carrie Pitman" [004-067]. Her reputation for mishaps extends to her ground tackle: the fishermen make a running joke that "the Carrie Pitman's anchor doesn't hold her for a week" [008-023].

## Incidents at Sea

The *Carrie Pitman* is involved in several notable incidents. During one encounter, she nearly causes a collision, cutting her cable at the last moment as she bears down with "all the North Atlantic" behind her [006-064]. Another episode involves her cutting away a dory's tackle — an act blamed on her being "a law unto herself" [006-062]. The *We're Here* meets her on the Banks, exchanging shouted news of their catches as was customary [006-049].

## In Fishermen's Culture

The *Carrie Pitman* enters the oral culture of the fleet through chanteys and dock talk. A song sung aboard one vessel mocks her unreliable ground tackle: "The Carrie Pitman's anchor doesn't hold her for a week" [008-023]. She is referred to as "the irrepressible Carrie Pitman" — a wry acknowledgment of her persistent presence despite her flaws [007-045]. Some fishermen, discussing her poor reputation, call her boat "a mud-scow" in derision [008-040].
