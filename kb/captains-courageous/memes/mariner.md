# Mariner

A **mariner** is a person who navigates or assists in navigating a ship — a seafarer by trade. In _Captains Courageous_, Kipling uses the term with varying shades of meaning, from the ironic and humorous to the genuinely respectful, capturing the social hierarchy of the fishing fleet and Harvey Cheyne's transformation from landsman to seaman.

## Harvey Cheyne: From Passenger to Mariner

When Harvey Cheyne first comes aboard the _We're Here_, he is anything but a mariner. The contrast is drawn sharply: "His mother suffered agonies whenever he got his feet wet; but this mariner did not seem excited" — referring to the fisherman who pulls Harvey from the water without drama (001-060). The irony is deliberate: Harvey, the coddled millionaire's son, is rescued by a real mariner who treats near-drowning as routine.

As Harvey learns the ways of the schooner, the other fishermen mock his affectation of seamanship. Long Jack observes that "he consates himself he's a bowld mariner" — play-acting the role before he has truly earned it (005-012). Harvey adopts "one half of the ancient-mariner air" when he proudly announces the schooner's position after taking a sighting (005-005).

## The True Mariners

The genuine mariners of the fleet embody a different standard. Disko Troop, Long Jack, and the other fishermen have spent their lives at sea. After a successful season, the fleet's fishermen are depicted as "jovial mariners" who "swung off into the mist" singing a chorus — men at ease in their element (005-055).

By the end of his voyage, Harvey has earned a measure of belonging. When he returns to the _We're Here_ after months ashore, he "felt like the most ancient of mariners dropped into the old schooner among the battered dories" (010-141). The phrase that was once ironic has become earned.

## The Mariner's Qualities

Dan Troop's skill with the schooner reveals "what kind of mariner he was" — not through words but through action: the way he runs the vessel, handles the crew, and reads the sea (008-100). In the world of _Captains Courageous_, a mariner is defined by competence demonstrated, not status claimed.
