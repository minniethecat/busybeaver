# Little Penn

"Little Penn" is the familiar name given to Pennsylvania Pratt, a crew member of the Gloucester fishing schooner *We're Here*. Despite being known by a geographical nickname suggesting he comes from Pennsylvania, he is most often referred to by the diminutive "Little Penn" by his shipmates.

## Role Aboard the We're Here

Little Penn serves as a deckhand on the *We're Here*, working alongside Disko Troop, Tom Platt, Long Jack, Salters, Manuel, and Dan Troop. When Harvey Cheyne first encounters the crew, Little Penn is bent over his "square deep-sea reel and the tangled cod-lines" in the forecastle, absorbed in the tedious work of preparing fishing gear [002-041].

## Character and Labor

Little Penn toils valiantly at the grueling work of hand-line fishing, though it is "easy to see he was weak" compared to the more robust crew members [003-003]. During the intense rhythm of hauling cod, Manuel, himself working with machine-like efficiency, "found time to help him without breaking the chain of supplies" [003-003]. His dedication is unquestioned; even Uncle Salters, quick to criticize others, defends him when his deafness leads to misunderstanding, snapping that " 'Tweren't none o' his fault" before adding simply, "Penn's deef" [004-115].

## Deafness

Little Penn is deaf, a condition that shapes his interactions aboard the vessel. His shipmates have learned to accommodate this, calling him forward with voice and gesture. His deafness is treated matter-of-factly by the crew — it is a known fact, not a source of ridicule, and he is valued for the work he contributes despite his physical limitations [004-115].
