# Sable Island

Sable Island is a narrow sandbar island located approximately 160 kilometers southeast of Nova Scotia, in the North Atlantic Ocean. In the world of *Captains Courageous*, it is known as a place of treacherous shoals and a landmark that fishermen avoid when possible [001-082].

## In the Story

Sable Island appears in the narrative through the tale of old man Hasken of the *East Wind*, who, after a head injury, became delusional and "declared war on Sable Island because it was Bridish, an' the shoals run aout too far" [001-082]. This darkly comic story, told by Disko Troop, illustrates both the dangers of head injuries at sea and the island's fearsome reputation among the fishing fleet.

The *We're Here* comes within sight of Sable Island on its return voyage. After leaving the Grand Banks, they encounter a northeaster off Artimon Bank "that drove them within view of the East light of Sable Island" [008-105]. Disko Troop pointedly does not linger over the sight, a testament to the island's grim reputation among mariners.

The schooner *Marilla*, in another tale recalled by the crew, strayed disastrously close to Sable Island in a storm. The drunken crew "made out, betwix' that an' the chart an' the singin' in his head, that they was to the south'ard o' Sable Island, gettin' along glorious, but speakin' nothin'" [006-026], a near-disaster that highlights the perennial danger the island poses to unwary vessels.

## Maritime Significance

Sable Island's shifting sandbars extend far beyond its visible shoreline, making it one of the most dangerous navigational hazards in the North Atlantic. Since the sixteenth century, over 350 vessels have been wrecked on its shoals. In the novel, this danger is implicitly understood by all the fishermen, who give the island a wide berth whenever possible.
