# Ocean

The **ocean** — specifically the North Atlantic — is the defining setting of _Captains Courageous_. Nearly the entire novel takes place at sea, and the ocean serves as both workplace and proving ground for its characters.

## The Ocean as Setting

The story begins with Harvey Cheyne crossing the ocean aboard a luxury liner. Harvey himself admits he has a poor relationship with the sea: the German passenger notes that "he's 'most always sick on the ocean" (001-018). When Harvey brags about his father's wealth, he speaks as a first-time ocean traveler addressing "gen'elmen" (001-020).

## Mid-Ocean Isolation

The ocean's vastness creates the novel's central dramatic situation — the isolation of those who fall or are thrown into it. After Harvey is washed overboard, the passengers on the liner assume he is "drowned and dead in mid-ocean" (001-035). Later, the narrative reflects on the "grim edge of a fishing-dory in mid-ocean" as Harvey's new reality (003-036).

## Ocean and Character

The ocean reveals character. Disko Troop's wife "jest despises the ocean," according to her husband, contrasting with the fishermen who make their living upon it (009-191). The ocean is where men are tested — where young Olley was "hiked... outer the ocean" by Jason (007-060).

## Between Two Oceans

At the novel's close, Cheyne speaks of feeling "at loose ends between two oceans" — the Atlantic that took his son and the Pacific of his California home — and declares "it's time to connect again" (010-068), using the ocean as a metaphor for the gulf between his business life and his family.
