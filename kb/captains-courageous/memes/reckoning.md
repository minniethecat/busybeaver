# Dead Reckoning

Dead reckoning is the art of knowing where you are at sea without sight of land. On the Grand Banks schooners of the late 19th century, it is the captain's most essential skill — a daily calculation scratched with a nail onto a wooden panel in the cabin, combining the ship's course, estimated speed, and elapsed time to determine the vessel's position. Disko Troop practices it with the quiet confidence of a man who has navigated by it for decades.

## The Daily Ritual

Each day, Disko "scratches the reckoning and date with a nail on the rough pine table" in the *We're Here*'s cabin. This is not a precise science — there are no chronometers or sextant sightings involved in the daily mark — but an accumulated judgment born of experience. Disko factors in the set of currents, the leeway made by the wind, the schooner's known sailing qualities, and his intimate knowledge of the Banks' geography to place the vessel within a few miles of its true position. — (005-005)

The reckoning is not done in private. The cabin is the social center of the schooner, and the scratched table is visible to all. When Disko marks the day's position, he is also marking the crew's shared progress through the fishing season — how far they have come, how much ground remains to cover, how long until they turn for home.

## Reckoning vs. Challenge

The reckoning is also an assertion of authority. When Uncle Salters — "the agricultural person" turned fisherman — challenges Disko's methods, Disko's response reveals the stakes involved in navigation: "I've asked for my reckoning," he says, a phrase that carries the weight of accountability. The captain who makes the reckoning takes responsibility for the vessel's safety; those who question it had better have the skill to back their words. — (006-007)

In a broader sense, reckoning in the novel is not just navigation but judgment. Harvey's moral education is a kind of dead reckoning — starting from where he is, measuring his progress day by day, and adjusting his course based on the evidence of his own experience. The scratched nail-mark on the cabin table is a metaphor for the incremental, cumulative process of becoming a competent person.
