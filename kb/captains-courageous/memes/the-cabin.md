# The Cabin (We're Here)

The cabin of the schooner *We're Here* is the enclosed below-deck space where the crew eats, sleeps, and gathers during their months-long fishing voyage. It is a cramped but functional space, reflecting the austere conditions of the Grand Banks fishery [001-045].

## Physical Description

The cabin is a low-ceilinged space lined with bunks, a central table, and a small galley stove. It is lit by a single lamp and the glow from the stove. The space is described as smelling of fish, oilskins, tobacco, and salt—the accumulated odors of men living and working at sea for weeks on end [001-045], [002-030].

## Social Function

The cabin serves as the social center of the *We're Here*. Meals are eaten at the central table, where Disko Troop presides as captain and father. Stories are told, songs are sung, and disputes are settled within its confines. It is here that Harvey Cheyne first encounters the rough democracy of the fishing crew, and where he slowly earns his place among them [002-035], [003-010].

## In the Story

The cabin is where much of Harvey's moral education takes place. He learns to eat what is put before him without complaint, to listen rather than boast, and to respect the skills of the fishermen. The cabin is also where the crew gathers during storms and fog, reinforcing the bond between the men [004-020].
