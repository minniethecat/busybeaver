# Harvey Rescued

The rescue of Harvey Cheyne from the North Atlantic is the foundational event of *Captains Courageous* — the accident that transforms a spoiled millionaire's son into a Grand Banks fisherman. Washed overboard from the deck of a transatlantic liner, Harvey is plucked from the sea by Manuel, a Portuguese doryman from the Gloucester schooner *We're Here*.

## The Overboard

Harvey Cheyne, fifteen years old and seasick, is leaning over the rail of a crack Atlantic liner when a "swell" — a wave — catches him and carries him over the side. The liner steams on, oblivious to his loss. Harvey surfaces in the cold North Atlantic, choking and flailing, with nothing but fog and water around him. The narrative does not dramatize the moment of the fall; it is recounted in retrospect, through the fishermen's later conversations and Harvey's own fragmented memories.

## Manuel's Dory

Manuel, a Madeira-born doryman fishing from the *We're Here*, is the first to spot Harvey in the water. When Harvey comes to his senses, Manuel is already pulling him into the dory, speaking in his characteristic broken English: "You are with me in the dory — Manuel my name, and I come from schooner *We're Here* of Gloucester. I live to Gloucester. By-and-by we get supper. Eh, wha-at?" [001-092]. This simple introduction marks the beginning of Harvey's transformation.

## Aboard the *We're Here*

Manuel rows Harvey to the *We're Here*, where the crew hoists the dory aboard. Dan Troop hands Harvey a rope and iron hook, and Manuel "slipped it through a loop of rope at the dory's bow, caught Dan's tackle, hooked it to the stern-becket, and clambered into the schooner" [002-078]. Disko Troop, the skipper, accepts the rescued boy with the pragmatic philosophy of the Banks: a hand saved is a hand that can work.

The crew's reaction to Harvey's rescue becomes a point of pride and gossip across the fleet. Later, the men of other schooners call out: "They all seemed to know about Harvey's rescue, and asked if he were worth his salt yet" [008-034]. Harvey's story — the boy who fell from a liner and was picked up by a Gloucester fishing schooner — becomes part of the Banks' oral history for that season.

## Significance

The rescue sets the entire plot in motion. Harvey's fall strips him of his wealth, his entitlement, and his identity; his rescue gives him a new community, a new set of skills, and ultimately a new sense of self. The event also introduces the novel's central theme: the redemptive power of honest labor at sea.
