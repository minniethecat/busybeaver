# Sinjon Hotchkiss

Sinjon (St John) Hotchkiss is a young lawyer in Shaw's *Getting Married* whose professional expertise in contract law makes him the natural spokesman for a rationalized approach to marriage. Yet his own romantic entanglements reveal the limits of purely contractual thinking.

## Character Background

Hotchkiss is Edith's brother, making him the Bishop's brother-in-law. He is a lawyer by profession, described as "coming to the wedding" when the play opens [[049-627]]. His name — St John — carries ecclesiastical echoes that Shaw deploys ironically. He is entangled in a romantic triangle with Leo Bridgenorth: "I like Sinjon because he wants me" [[049-674]].

## Role in the Play

Hotchkiss arrives as the voice of legal rationality. He articulates the contractual reform Shaw advocates: "distinction between one civil contract and another" should be minimal [[049-1466]]. Yet his own irregular romantic arrangement dramatizes Shaw's point that the law lags behind life.

## Key Passages
