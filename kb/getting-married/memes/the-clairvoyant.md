# The Clairvoyant

A mysterious figure mentioned in Shaw's *Getting Married* — "she's a clairvoyant" — who represents Shaw's interest in non-institutional spirituality as an alternative to the Church he critiques [049-186].

## Character Background

The clairvoyant appears only in passing reference, but her presence signals Shaw's broader interest in spiritual experience outside ecclesiastical authority. Just as marriage needs reform, so too does religion — and the clairvoyant represents one direction reform might take.

## Symbolic Function

The clairvoyant serves as a counterpoint to the Bishop: where he represents institutional religion with its rigid doctrines on marriage, she represents direct spiritual experience unmediated by hierarchy. This is consistent with Shaw's "unborn religion" theme — a faith built from Western experience rather than Eastern importation.
