# Mrs Bridgenorth

This page covers the matriarchal dimension of Mrs Bridgenorth's role in *Getting Married*. For her character background and marriage crisis, see [[edith-bridgenorth]].

## Household Authority

Mrs Bridgenorth presides over the Bishop's kitchen — his "favorite room" — with the quiet competence of a woman who has made domestic management her profession [049-003]. She reads placidly while Collins counts; the household runs on her authority, not the Bishop's.

## The Mother's Role

As mother to Leo, Mrs Bridgenorth embodies the tension between maternal affection and institutional duty. She loves her son but must enforce the social conventions that his choices threaten. "How can I? Leo's coming" — her domestic obligations conflict with her maternal instincts [049-475].
