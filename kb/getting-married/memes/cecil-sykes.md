# Cecil Sykes

Cecil Sykes is a young man in Shaw's *Getting Married* whose marital prospects are shaped by economic calculation as much as romantic inclination. His situation illustrates Shaw's argument that marriage is a property transaction disguised as sentiment.

## Character Background

Cecil is among the younger generation whose marital futures are being negotiated during the play's crisis. His position — dependent on family approval for economic reasons — mirrors the structural dependency that Shaw argues distorts all marriage decisions.

## Role in the Play

Cecil's function is to show that even "normal" courtship is shaped by invisible economic constraints. He is not villainous or foolish; he is simply operating within a system that channels desire through property channels.

## Key Passages

Shaw embeds Cecil's situation in the broader marriage debate rather than giving him extended solo speeches — his presence is structural rather than rhetorical.
