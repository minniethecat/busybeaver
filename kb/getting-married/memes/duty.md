# Duty

Bernard Shaw weaponizes the Victorian language of duty in *Getting Married*, turning it against the institutions it was meant to defend. Where conventional morality speaks of the duty to remain married, Shaw speaks of "the duty to divorce when the marriage has lost its use" [[043-001]]. This inversion is central to his rhetorical strategy: he does not reject the concept of duty but redefines its object.

## Redefining Duty

Shaw's key move is to shift the referent of duty from the institution to the individuals within it. "It is a father's duty to prevent his son from making a disastrous marriage" — but it is equally the citizen's duty to reform the laws that make such disasters inevitable [[009-002]].

He extends this logic to divorce: "that is exactly what makes divorce a duty when the marriage has lost its use" [[043-001]]. Staying in a dead marriage is not virtuous endurance; it is a failure of moral responsibility to oneself and others.

## Duty to Children vs. Duty to Marriage

Shaw argues that the true duty is to children, not to the institution of marriage. "Having the parental duty performed by officials" is preferable to forcing incompatible parents to stay together "for the sake of the children" [[045-003]]. The duty to provide stable parenting is real; the duty to preserve a marriage that has failed is a sentimental fiction.

## Political Duty

Shaw extends the concept of duty to the political sphere: "the voter's duty is to take care that the Government consists of men who will do the work" [[013-001]]. This connects individual moral obligation to collective political action — a characteristically Fabian move. The duty to reform marriage is not merely personal but civic.

## Irony and Subversion

Shaw's treatment of duty is consistently ironic. He uses the word in its conventional Victorian sense while subverting its content. When a character says "I feel that I have a duty," Shaw invites the audience to ask: a duty to whom? Defined by what standard? In whose interest? These questions, persistently asked, unravel the moral fabric of the existing marriage system.

## Key Passages

> "That is exactly what makes divorce a duty when the marriage has lost its use" [[043-001]].

> "The voter's duty is to take care that the Government consists of men who will do the work" [[013-001]].

> "Having the parental duty performed by officials" as an alternative to forced marriage [[045-003]].
