## Statement

"Evil tendencies are early shown" is the explicit moral of The Blind Man and the Cub (042-002). A Blind Man, famous for identifying animals by touch alone, is handed a Wolf's Cub. He feels it, hesitates — he cannot name the species — but issues this verdict:

> Indeed, I am not sure whether it is a Wolf's Cub or a Fox's: but this I know — it would never do to trust it in a sheepfold. (042-001)

The Blind Man cannot see the Cub's form and cannot name it from its fur. But touch reveals what taxonomy conceals: the creature's **nature** — its orientation toward prey — is palpable. Species is uncertain; danger is not.

## Fables That Illustrate This Moral

### The Blind Man and the Cub (primary)

The fable is extraordinary among the Aesopica for its epistemology. The Blind Man is the metaphor for wisdom: he lacks sight but possesses touch — the most intimate sense — and his judgment transcends the visible. He knows that predators-to-be have a texture, a movement, a quality that the hand can read before the eye can classify (042-001).

### The Swallow and the Other Birds

A Swallow warns the other birds that the farmer is sowing hemp-seed — the raw material for nets. The birds mock her. Later, when the nets are woven and they are caught, they recognize too late that the early sign was the only one that mattered (039-001).

### The Mouse and the Weasel

A Mouse, who has been gnawing through a corn-bin, grows fat and cannot escape through the hole. A Weasel outside tells him that his tendency — to eat more than the exit permits — was always visible; only now does it become fatal (134-001).

## Mechanism

The Blind Man's method embodies Aesop's theory of character: what a creature **will do** is knowable before it does it. The Cub has not yet killed a sheep — it may never have seen one. But the inclination toward the sheepfold is in its bones, and the hand can detect what the eye would need a crime to confirm.

This contrasts with [[hypocrite-deceives-himself]], where evil is concealed behind a mask. The Cub's evil is not hidden — it is pre-conscious, not yet expressed, but fully present. The Cub acts consistently with its nature; it simply hasn't had the opport[[harmony|unity]] yet.
