# Oxford

The university town that was the cradle of the Oxford Movement and the spiritual forcing-house of Victorian religion. Strachey's Oxford of the 1830s is a place of intense intellectual and spiritual ferment — the Common Room of Oriel, the pulpit of St. Mary's, the quadrangles where young men discovered that the Christian religion, taken literally, demanded everything.

## In the Narrative

Oxford is the setting for the opening act of Manning's biography and the intellectual home of Newman, Keble, Pusey, and the other Tractarians. It is also the institution that rejected the Movement: "The University breathed such a sigh of relief as usually follows the difficult expulsion of a hard piece of matter from a living organism" when Newman departed (006-106). Arnold's Oxford — he was a Fellow of Oriel before becoming headmaster of Rugby — represents the Broad Church alternative that the Tractarians sought to defeat.
