# Catholic Emancipation (1829)

The Roman Catholic Relief Act of 1829 removed the legal barriers that had excluded Catholics from Parliament, the judiciary, and most public offices since the Reformation. It was the prerequisite for the Catholic revival that Manning would later lead — without Emancipation, there could be no restored hierarchy (1850), no Catholic archbishop of Westminster, no cardinal.

## In Strachey's Narrative

Strachey mentions Emancipation only in passing, but its effects are everywhere in his narrative. The elder Wilberforce, Manning's early patron, "living long enough to witness the victories of Liberalism, including the Catholic Emancipation Act" (001-085), is a symbolic figure: the old Evangelical order yielding to the new Catholic ascendancy that Manning would embody. The Act made possible everything Manning became.
