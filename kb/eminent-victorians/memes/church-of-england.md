# Church of England

The institution against which Manning and Newman rebelled. Strachey's pre-Tractarian Church was an institution of "comfortable" divines who "rode gaily to hounds" and "carried their two bottles" (002-080). It was the Oxford Movement's achievement — and, in Strachey's view, its irony — to transform this complacent establishment into something far more serious.

## Role in the Narrative

The Church of England appears in all four biographies as the background against which religious drama unfolds. Manning serves and then abandons it; Nightingale transcends its limitations through direct divine calling; Arnold attempts to reform it through Broad Church comprehensiveness; Gordon simply ignores it in favour of private mysticism.
