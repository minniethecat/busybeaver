# Rugby School

Dr. Arnold's Rugby was the laboratory in which the Victorian governing class was formed. Arnold's reforms — the prefect system, the chapel-centred moral curriculum, the cultivation of "earnestness" — created a template exported to public schools across England. Strachey's irony is that the product was "men admirably fitted to govern a colony or command a regiment — and to do little besides."

## Arnold's System

The prefect system gave senior boys authority over juniors, creating a hierarchy of responsibility that mirrored the social hierarchy they would later occupy. The classical curriculum connected them to Greece and Rome. The chapel provided moral formation without doctrinal specificity. The whole was designed to produce Christian gentlemen rather than scholars.
