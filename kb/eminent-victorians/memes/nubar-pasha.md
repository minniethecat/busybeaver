## Overview

Nubar Pasha (1825–1899) was an Egyptian statesman and diplomat of Armenian descent who served as Prime Minister of Egypt on three occasions. In Lytton Strachey's *Eminent Victorians*, he appears at a crucial juncture in the biography of [[general-gordon|General Charles Gordon]]: it was Nubar Pasha, the Egyptian minister, who offered Gordon the post that would ultimately lead him to [[khartoum|Khartoum]].

## The Fateful Offer

In 1874, Gordon was sent to represent Great Britain at an international commission in [[constantinople|Constantinople]]. There he chanced to meet Nubar Pasha, the Egyptian minister. Strachey recounts the pivotal moment:

> The Governorship of the Equatorial Provinces of the Sudan was about to fall vacant; and Nubar offered the post to Gordon, who accepted it. [034-157]

This encounter — seemingly accidental, in the diplomatic corridors of [[constantinople|Constantinople]] — set in motion the chain of events that would end with Gordon's death on the steps of the Governor's Palace at [[khartoum|Khartoum]] eleven years later.

## Gordon in Egyptian Service

It was under the aegis of the Egyptian Khedive, Ismail Pasha, and facilitated by Nubar Pasha's diplomatic initiative, that Gordon embarked on his extraordinary career as an imperial administrator in Africa. Gordon accepted the post with a characteristic blend of religious fatalism and ambition:

> 'For some wise design,' he wrote to his sister, 'God turns events one way or another.' [034-160]

## Political Career

Nubar Pasha served as Prime Minister of Egypt in 1878–1879, 1884–1888, and 1894–1895. His career spanned the critical period of Egypt's modernisation and its complex relationship with both the Ottoman Empire and European powers. He was known as a skilled negotiator and a modernising reformer who sought to balance Egyptian sovereignty with the realities of great-power politics.

## Legacy

In the context of the Gordon narrative, Nubar Pasha represents the contingent nature of historical destiny — the chance meeting, the casual offer, the decision that seemed minor at the time but that altered the course of a life and an empire. Without Nubar Pasha's offer at [[constantinople|Constantinople]], Gordon might never have gone to the Sudan, and the tragic drama of [[khartoum|Khartoum]] might never have been written.
