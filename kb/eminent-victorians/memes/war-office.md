# War Office

The embodiment of everything Nightingale fought against. The War Office that managed the Crimean campaign was a "gigantic machine for the prevention of effective action" — its supply chains broken, its medical service non-existent, its officers appointed by purchase rather than competence. Strachey portrays it as the institutional expression of the gentlemanly amateurism that Nightingale's whole career was dedicated to destroying.

## Nightingale's Campaign

After the war, Nightingale made the reform of the War Office her central project. Her Royal Commission exposed the systemic failures that had killed thousands; her recommendations — statistical, sanitary, architectural — transformed military medicine. The irony Strachey relishes is that a bedridden invalid, working through a confidential secretary, outmanoeuvred the entire masculine apparatus of the British state.
