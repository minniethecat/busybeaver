# Scutari

The Ottoman barracks hospital where Florence Nightingale arrived in November 1854 with thirty-eight nurses and found a medical catastrophe in progress. The Scutari hospitals — "the organisation of which had already given way under the stress of the war" (021-122) — became the laboratory in which Nightingale proved that sanitation, organisation, and trained nursing could save lives.

## Strachey's Scutari

Strachey's account of Scutari strips away the sentimental legend of the Lady with the Lamp to reveal the administrative chaos beneath. Wounded men lay on bare floors; medical stores were lost in the holds of ships in the harbour; the only nursing available was provided by untrained orderlies described as "a coarse old woman, always ignorant, usually dirty, often brutal" (019-046). Nightingale's achievement was not compassion but competence — the application of systematic organisation to a system designed for failure.
