# Rome

The city to which Manning's entire life was a pilgrimage. Strachey's Rome of the late 1860s, on the eve of the First Vatican Council, was "the proud and visible apex, the palpitating heart, the sacred sanctuary" of a unique fusion of spiritual and political power (014-049). The last hour of the Pope's temporal dominion had almost struck, and the city "arrayed herself to meet it in all her glory."

## Manning's Rome

Manning "flew to Rome in a whirlwind of Papal enthusiasm" as the Council approached. The Rome he found — seven hundred bishops, Zouaves in the colonnade, cardinals in their enormous carriages "like mysterious painted idols" — was the theatre in which he would play his greatest role.
