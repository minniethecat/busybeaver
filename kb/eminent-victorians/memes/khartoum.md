# Khartoum

The city that became Gordon's tomb. Founded in 1823 at the strategic meeting of the Blue and White Niles, Khartoum was the administrative centre of Egyptian rule in the Sudan — and the prize that the Mahdi's forces captured after a siege of nearly a year. Strachey's account of the siege transforms the city from a geographical location into a psychological space: the arena in which Gordon's contradictions played out to their fatal conclusion.

## Gordon's Khartoum

Gordon fortified the city with desperate energy, but the siege was unwinnable. His journals record the progressive deterioration of the defences, the dwindling of supplies, and the approach of the Mahdi's forces. When the city fell on January 26, 1885 — two days before the British relief force arrived — Gordon was killed on the palace steps. His head was displayed to the Mahdi as a trophy.
