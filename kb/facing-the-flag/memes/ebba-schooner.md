The schooner *Ebba* is the primary surface vessel of the [[Pirate Crew of Back Cup]], owned ostensibly by [[count-dartigas]] and commanded by Captain Spade. More than a mere conveyance, the *Ebba* functions as a mobile headquarters, a tool of abduction, and a symbol of the pirates' reach across the [[Atlantic Ocean]].

## Vessel Description

The *Ebba* is introduced early in the narrative through the notes of Simon Hart, the novel's narrator, who finds himself aboard the schooner under circumstances he cannot explain.

> On board the schooner *Ebba*, anchored off New-Berne, Pamlico Sound. (001-013)

> Captain Spade, commander of the schooner *Ebba*. (001-015)

The vessel is described as a schooner — a sailing ship with two or more masts, fore-and-aft rigged — suitable for both coastal navigation and open-ocean voyages. Its ownership by Count d'Artigas provides a veneer of legitimacy: wealthy noblemen of the period commonly maintained private yachts, and a schooner in Pamlico Sound attracts no particular suspicion.

## Operational Role

The *Ebba* serves multiple functions within [[Ker Karraje]]'s organization:

| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Transport** | Ferries personnel, captives, and supplies between [[Back Cup]] and the American mainland |
| **Surveillance Platform** | Monitors maritime traffic near Back Cup, provides early warning of approaching vessels |
| **Abduction Vehicle** | Used to transport [[Thomas Roch]] from [[Healthful House]] to Back Cup |
| **Command Center** | [[Engineer Serko]] and Captain Spade conduct operational planning on the after-deck |
| **Legitimacy Cover** | Registered to Count d'Artigas, the schooner provides a respectable front for criminal activity |

## The Submarine Connection

A distinctive feature of the *Ebba*'s operation is its interaction with the [[Submarine Technology|submarine tug]]. Because the entrance to [[Back Cup]] is entirely submerged, the schooner cannot enter under its own sail or steam power. Instead, a submersible tug attaches to the hull and tows the vessel through the underwater channel.

This arrangement creates a rhythmic pattern in the novel's maritime sequences: surface voyage → encounter with submarine tug → submerged passage → emergence inside the cavern lagoon. The *Ebba* thus occupies an intermediate position between the visible world of the ocean surface and the hidden world beneath.

## Command and Crew

The *Ebba*'s command structure reflects the broader hierarchy of the pirate organization:

- **Captain Spade**: Direct commander of the vessel, responsible for navigation and maritime operations
- **[[Engineer Serko]]**: Frequently present on board, overseeing technical matters and prisoner surveillance
- **[[count-dartigas]] / [[Ker Karraje]]**: Ultimate owner, whose presence on board signifies high-priority missions

The crew consists of pirates loyal to Karraje, recruited from the [[Pirate Crew of Back Cup]]. Their discipline and seamanship are noted by Hart, who observes that they operate with the efficiency of a naval crew — a disquieting professionalism that distinguishes them from stereotypical buccaneers.

## Narrative Significance

The *Ebba* bookends the novel's action. It is the vehicle of Roch's abduction, the setting for Hart's early observations of the pirate organization, and — in the final sequences — the platform from which the [[Escape from Back Cup]] is launched or thwarted. The chapter "The Schooner *Ebba*" (Section IV) marks the transition from the novel's exposition at [[Healthful House]] to its central drama of captivity and concealment.

The schooner's name — *Ebba* — remains unexplained in the text, a characteristic Verne detail that invites speculation. It may be a personal name (a shipowner's wife or daughter, in maritime tradition) or a reference lost to contemporary readers.
