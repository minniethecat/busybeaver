**St. George** is one of the principal ports of [[bermuda|Bermuda]], referenced frequently in the latter chapters of [[jules-verne]]'s _[[facing-the-flag-novel|Facing the Flag]]_. Located on the eastern end of the Bermuda archipelago, it serves as a strategic maritime point in the novel's resolution.

## Geographic Context

St. George is described alongside other Bermuda locales as one of the islands of the archipelago:

> "The group consists of several islands — St. David, Somerset, Hamilton, and St. George. The latter has a free port..." (013-005)

The narrator [[simon-hart|Simon Hart]] mentions the port's accessibility and its role as a communication hub:

> "There is the greatest facility of communication with Hamilton and St. George." (013-014)

## Role in the Novel's Finale

In the climactic chapters, St. George becomes the focal point for the international naval response to the events at [[back-cup|Back Cup]]. The keg containing [[simon-hart|Simon Hart]]'s account of his captivity washes ashore at St. George, alerting the authorities:

> "It was you who caused the keg to be thrown up on the shore at St. George." (023-012)

The British cruiser [[battle-sword-and-tug|_Sword_]] uses St. George as its base of operations:

> "The _Sword_ had been anchored forty-eight hours previously in the port of St. George." (023-031)

After the destruction of Back Cup, the survivors plan to return to St. George to report the outcome:

> "The _Sword_ did not return to St. George, and another attempt would be made under different conditions." (025-035)

The port thus serves as the critical link between the isolated events inside Back Cup and the outside world, enabling the final naval intervention by the [[tonnant-french-cruiser|_Tonnant_]].
