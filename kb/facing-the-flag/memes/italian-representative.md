The Italian Representative was among the unnamed foreign agents who approached [[Thomas Roch]] seeking to acquire his [[Fulgurator]] invention. Italy's involvement illustrates the widespread alarm and ambition that the weapon provoked among European powers.

## Italy's Strategic Interest

As a relatively young nation-state unified in 1861, Italy had both the strategic incentive to acquire advanced weapons and the industrial capability to produce them. A weapon of Roch's design — capable of devastating enemy fleets at range — would have been particularly attractive to a nation with extensive Mediterranean coastlines.

Like his counterparts from [[British Negotiator|Britain]], [[German Representative|Germany]], and [[Russian Representative|Russia]], the Italian Representative found that Roch's conditions made conventional acquisition impossible.
