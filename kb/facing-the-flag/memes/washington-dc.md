Washington, D.C., the capital of the United States, is the seat of the American government that assumed custody of [[Thomas Roch]] after his rejection by [[France]] and other European powers. Though never directly depicted in *Facing the Flag*, Washington is the source of the institutional decisions that shape the novel's events.

## Institutional Role

The American government's involvement with [[Thomas Roch]] began when the inventor, having been rejected by [[France]], Germany, and [[British Admiralty|Britain]], approached the United States. It was in Washington that the decision was made to place Roch at [[Healthful House]] under the supervision of the [[US Navy]] and the State Department.

After the [[kidnapping-of-roch|kidnapping]], Washington became the center of the American response. The [[American Secretary of the Navy|Secretary of the Navy]] ordered squadrons to search the [[Atlantic Ocean]], and diplomatic communications between Washington and European capitals intensified as the international dimensions of the crisis became clear.

## Symbolic Significance

Washington represents the institutional counterweight to [[Ker Karraje]]'s lawless domain at [[Back Cup]]. Where the pirate operates outside all national jurisdictions, Washington embodies the rule-bound, bureaucratic response — searches, patrols, diplomatic notes — that struggles to contend with an adversary who recognizes no such constraints.
