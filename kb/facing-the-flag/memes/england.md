## England

England is a major European power and one of the nations most directly involved in the events of Jules Verne's *Facing the Flag* (1896). As the colonial ruler of [[Bermuda]], the home country of [[Lieutenant Davon]], and a key participant in the international coalition against [[Ker Karraje]], England plays a significant background role in the novel's geopolitical framework.

## In the Novel

England's most direct connection to the narrative is through its possession of the [[Bermuda]] archipelago. The Bermuda group, which includes the island of [[Back Cup]] — the hidden lair of [[Ker Karraje]] — is a British colony. Simon Hart, the captive narrator, notes this fact with some irony as he realizes that the pirates have established their base within territory that nominally belongs to England:

> "The Bermuda group, which belongs to England." (011-020)

The British annexation of Bermuda is described in historical context, explaining how the islands came under English control:

> "It was because of its production of coffee, indigo, and arrowroot that England annexed the group — seized it, in other words." (013-004)

England is listed among the great powers threatened by [[Ker Karraje]]'s campaign of maritime piracy. After the pirate relocates from the [[Pacific Ocean|Pacific]] to the [[Atlantic Ocean|Atlantic]], his depredations create an international crisis affecting all the major naval powers:

> "A perfect reign of terror. England, France, Germany, Russia and America vainly dispatched their squadrons in pursuit." (016-054)

England's most tangible contribution to the anti-piracy effort comes through [[Lieutenant Davon]], a British naval officer who commands the crew that assists in the final confrontation at [[Back Cup]]. The narrative voice, in its closing passages, invokes England alongside the civilized nations:

> "Lieutenant Davon, gallant crew, may England, may the civilized nations, never forget what they owe you!" (025-002)

The novel's assessment of [[Ker Karraje]]'s threat suggests that England itself — despite its naval power — would struggle to confront the pirates in their hidden lair. After the destruction of [[Back Cup]], the narrative notes that no power, including England, would consider searching for survivors in the collapsed cavern:

> "No power, not even England, will think of seeking for them inside the cavern." (024-052)

## Connections

England connects to multiple key elements of the novel:

- **[[Bermuda]]**: British colony, home to the hidden pirate base of [[Back Cup]].
- **[[Back Cup]]**: Located within British territorial waters, an ironic detail given the pirate activity there.
- **[[Lieutenant Davon]]**: British naval officer who participates in the rescue operation.
- **[[Atlantic Ocean]]**: England is a major Atlantic naval power, implicated in hunting Karraje.
- **[[France]]**: Mentioned alongside England in the list of nations threatened by Karraje.
- **[[Ker Karraje|Count d'Artigas]]**: The pirate whose activities challenged English naval authority.

## Key Passages

England's colonial possession of Bermuda provides the geographical irony at the heart of the novel's setting:

> "The Bermuda group, which belongs to England." (011-020)

The historical annexation of Bermuda is noted with a hint of criticism:

> "It was because of its production of coffee, indigo, and arrowroot that England annexed the group — seized it, in other words." (013-004)

England's place in the international anti-piracy coalition is acknowledged in the novel's patriotic conclusion:

> "Lieutenant Davon, gallant crew, may England, may the civilized nations, never forget what they owe you!" (025-002)
