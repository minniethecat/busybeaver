## Fulgurator

[[roch-fulgurator-invention|the fulgurator]] is the revolutionary military weapon invented by [[thomas-roch|thomas roch]] in [[jules-verne|jules verne]]'s *Facing the Flag* (1896). Though its exact mechanism is never fully described, the fulgurator represents a weapon of unprecedented destructive power — a device so fearsome that multiple nations and a notorious pirate vie for control of its secret.

> "His genius as an inventor, however, remains intact; it has survived his moral decadence. If he is able to understand the business of ordinary life, he may be incapable of reasoning in a sound manner, except as regards matters relating to his invention." (003-015)

## In the Novel

The fulgurator drives every major plot development in *Facing the Flag*. It is Roch's obsession, the source of his mental deterioration, and the prize that motivates the novel's antagonists. The weapon's existence and Roch's refusal to part with its secret without "billions" in compensation create the central tension.

Roch's invention was first offered to the French government, which rejected it. He then approached Germany and Britain, both of which also refused his exorbitant demands. The United States, recognizing both the weapon's potential and Roch's deteriorating mental state, confined him at [[healthful-house|healthful house]] to protect the secret — a strategy that ultimately failed when [[ker-karraje|ker karraje]] kidnapped the inventor.

> "Thomas Roch's invention having been refused by the commission, steps ought to have been taken to prevent him from offering it elsewhere. Nothing of the kind was done, and there a great mistake was made." (002-001)

## Related Characters

- **Thomas Roch**: The inventor of the fulgurator. His entire identity is consumed by his creation, and his mental state oscillates between lucid genius and paranoid obsession.

- **Ker Karraje (Count d'Artigas)**: [[count-dartigas|the pirate]] who seeks to obtain the fulgurator's secret, believing it will make him invincible.

- **[[simon-hart|simon hart]]**: Roch's protector, who ultimately prevents the fulgurator from falling into [[pirate-crew|the pirates]]' hands.

- **[[engineer-serko|engineer serko]]**: The technical expert tasked with understanding and reverse-engineering Roch's invention.

## Key Passages

Roch's famous refrain expresses his fixation on the weapon's monetary value:

> "Billions! Billions!" (004-005)

The novel's title itself — *Facing the Flag* — refers to the climactic moment when Roch, in a flash of patriotic lucidity, turns his own weapon against the pirates rather than allowing it to be used against his homeland. The fulgurator is thus not merely a plot device but the symbolic axis around which the novel's themes of [[patriotism|patriotism]], genius, and moral responsibility revolve.
