The German Representative was one of the unnamed foreign agents who attempted to acquire [[Thomas Roch]]'s [[Fulgurator]] after the inventor's rejection by [[France]]. Germany was among the powers that Roch approached in his quest for payment and recognition.

> After France had refused to purchase his invention, he had offered it to Germany. (002-003)

## Role in the International Crisis

Germany's involvement in the Fulgurator affair reflects the geopolitical tensions of late nineteenth-century Europe. As a rising military and industrial power, Germany had both the resources to pay Roch's demanded price and the strategic interest in acquiring such a weapon. The prospect of the [[Fulgurator]] falling into German hands was particularly alarming for [[France]], still recovering from the 1870–71 Franco-Prussian War.

The German Representative's approach ultimately failed. Roch's insistence on payment before disclosure proved an insurmountable barrier for all governments that sought to acquire his secret through conventional diplomatic channels.
