The Exterior of [[Back Cup]] was the barren, unbroken outer surface of the volcanic islet that concealed the elaborate pirate base within. To passing vessels, the islet appeared uninhabited — a rocky outcrop of no interest or value, which was precisely the illusion that [[Ker Karraje]] depended upon for his security.

## Appearance

From the sea, [[Back Cup]] presented a forbidding aspect: steep volcanic walls rising from the [[Atlantic Ocean]], devoid of vegetation, with no visible entrance or habitation. The [[submarine-tunnel]] — the only access to the interior [[Back Cup Lagoon|lagoon]] — was entirely submerged and invisible from the surface. Even close approach revealed nothing to suggest the cavern, the dock, or the [[pirate-crew|pirate community]] within.

## The Deception's Success

The Exterior's deceptive barrenness was the foundation of [[Ker Karraje]]'s operational security. For years, naval patrols from multiple nations had searched for the pirate's base without success. The Exterior of [[Back Cup]] — unremarkable, uninviting, unexamined — was the reason. The base was hidden not in a remote corner of the world but in plain sight, protected by the assumption that nothing of value could exist within such an unpromising shell.
