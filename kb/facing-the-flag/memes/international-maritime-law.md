International maritime law is the legal framework that governs the conduct of nations and individuals at sea, and which [[Ker Karraje]] systematically violates in *Facing the Flag*. The novel explores the tension between lawless maritime violence and the institutional mechanisms designed to suppress it.

## The Legal Framework

The late nineteenth century saw the development of international maritime law through treaties, conventions, and customary practice. Piracy was recognized as a crime under international law — a hostis humani generis (enemy of all mankind) subject to the jurisdiction of any nation that captured the perpetrator. This principle underlies the [[international-coalition-formation|international coalition]]'s operation against [[Ker Karraje]]: any nation's warship had the legal authority to act against the pirate.

## Karraje's Exploitation of Legal Gaps

[[Ker Karraje]]'s operation exploited the gaps in maritime law enforcement. By concealing his base within the territorial waters of [[Bermuda]] (a British possession) while preying on international shipping, he created jurisdictional complications that delayed and complicated the response. His use of the [[submarine-tunnel]] further frustrated enforcement by making his base effectively invisible to surface patrols.
