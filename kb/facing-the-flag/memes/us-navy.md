## United States Navy

The United States Navy appears in [[jules-verne|jules verne]]'s *Facing the Flag* (1896) as the institutional force representing lawful authority in the Atlantic maritime domain. While not a major character, the Navy's presence frames the political stakes of the novel — particularly the American government's interest in [[thomas-roch|thomas roch]]'s [[fulgurator|fulgurator]] — and provides the military force that ultimately confronts [[ker-karraje|ker karraje]]'s pirate operation.

> "The Americans, being even more practical than the English, did not attempt to bargain for [[roch-fulgurator-invention|roch's fulgurator]], to which, in view of the French chemist's reputation, they attached exceptional importance." (002-007)

## In the Novel

The American government, acting through its naval and administrative apparatus, took custody of Thomas Roch after his rejections by [[french-government|france]], Germany, and Britain. Rather than negotiate for the fulgurator's secret, as the British had attempted, the Americans adopted a protective strategy: they confined Roch at [[healthful-house|healthful house]] to safeguard both the inventor and his dangerous knowledge.

This decision reflects a pragmatic recognition of two facts: that Roch's mental condition made negotiation impossible, and that his invention was too valuable — and too dangerous — to leave unprotected. The Navy's role is thus initially custodial rather than operational.

When Roch is kidnapped by Ker Karraje, the American government's protective strategy is exposed as insufficient. The Navy's subsequent pursuit — including the tug that engages Ker Karraje's submarine in the climactic battle — represents the restoration of lawful authority over the criminal exploitation of Roch's invention.

## Members

In the context of the novel, the Navy is represented institutionally rather than through named individual members. Its presence is felt through the administrative decisions that confine Roch and the operational forces that pursue his kidnappers.

- **The American Government**: Made the strategic decision to sequester Roch rather than bargain with him.
- **The Tug Crew**: The naval personnel who engage Ker Karraje's submarine in the climactic battle, representing the Navy's operational arm.

The Navy's involvement in *Facing the Flag* reflects the late-nineteenth-century context of American naval expansion and the growing recognition of the United States as an Atlantic maritime power with interests extending beyond its coastal waters.
