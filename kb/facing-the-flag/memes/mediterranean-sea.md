The Mediterranean Sea was the theater of [[Ker Karraje]]'s earlier pirate operations before he relocated to the [[Atlantic Ocean]] and established his base at [[Back Cup]]. References to the Mediterranean in *Facing the Flag* establish Karraje's history as an international criminal pursued by multiple naval powers.

## Karraje's Mediterranean Career

Before the events of the novel, [[Ker Karraje]] had operated as a pirate in the Mediterranean, preying on shipping and evading the navies of the European powers. His reputation from this period — as a ruthless and cunning adversary — precedes him in the narrative and explains the intensity of the international effort to locate and destroy him.

> Karraje, who had long been sought in vain by the navies of Europe. (001-009)

Karraje's eventual relocation from the Mediterranean to the [[Atlantic Ocean]] marked an escalation in his ambitions. The Mediterranean, bordered by the navies of [[France]], Italy, Britain, and the Ottoman Empire, had become too dangerous for sustained operations. The relative isolation of [[Bermuda]] offered a new base from which he could strike at Atlantic shipping lanes.

## Strategic Significance

The Mediterranean serves in the novel as a point of contrast with the [[Atlantic Ocean]]. Where the Mediterranean is a confined sea, heavily patrolled and diplomatically complex, the Atlantic offers space, anonymity, and the isolation of [[Back Cup]]. Karraje's move from one theater to the other reflects the adaptive strategy of a sophisticated criminal enterprise.
