Germany was one of the European great powers that [[Thomas Roch]] approached with his [[Fulgurator]] invention after his rejection by [[France]]. As a rising military and industrial power in the late nineteenth century, Germany represented both a potential buyer for Roch's weapon and a source of strategic anxiety for its European rivals.

## In the Novel

Germany's interest in the [[Fulgurator]] is mentioned early in the novel as part of the international competition for Roch's invention:

> After France had refused to purchase his invention, he had offered it to Germany. (002-003)

The German government, like those of [[British Admiralty|Britain]], [[Russian Representative|Russia]], and other powers, sent representatives to negotiate with Roch. All found the inventor intransigent — his demand for payment before disclosure was a condition no government would accept. Germany's failure to acquire the secret, like that of the other powers, contributed to the circumstances that ultimately delivered the weapon into [[Ker Karraje]]'s hands.

## Geopolitical Context

Verne's inclusion of Germany among the powers competing for Roch's invention reflects the tense international environment of the 1890s. The Franco-Prussian War of 1870–71 remained a living memory, and the prospect of a German military equipped with Roch's autonomous projectile would have resonated with Verne's French readership as a particularly alarming scenario.
