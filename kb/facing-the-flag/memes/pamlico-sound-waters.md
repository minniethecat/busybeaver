The Waters of [[Pamlico Sound]] form the estuarine system separating the North Carolina mainland from the Outer Banks barrier islands. In *Facing the Flag*, these waters were the route taken by the [[Ebba Schooner|Ebba]] as it departed from [[New Berne]] on its [[ebba-voyage-to-back-cup|voyage to Back Cup]], carrying the kidnapped [[Thomas Roch]] and [[Simon Hart]].

## The Night Passage

The [[Ebba Schooner|Ebba]] navigated the Sound's channels under cover of darkness, avoiding the surveillance that had been organized after the kidnapping. The shallow, shifting channels of the Sound required local knowledge — a resource that [[Captain Spade]] and his crew had acquired during their reconnaissance of the region in preparation for the operation.

> The schooner threaded the channels of Pamlico Sound with the confidence of those who knew every shoal and sandbar. (005-006)

## Strategic Importance

The Sound's geography — a vast, shallow, island-sheltered body of water — made it both a natural route for coastal traffic and a difficult area for pursuers to search. Once the [[Ebba Schooner|Ebba]] cleared the sound and entered the [[Atlantic Ocean]], the trail went cold. The American naval forces searching for the schooner had no way to determine which direction it had taken from the Sound's multiple outlets to the sea.
