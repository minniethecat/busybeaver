## North Carolina

North Carolina is one of the United States of America and serves as the initial geographical setting of Jules Verne's *Facing the Flag* (1896). It is the location of [[Healthful House]], the private sanatorium where [[Thomas Roch]] is confined, and the point from which the novel's central kidnapping occurs — setting in motion the entire chain of events that drive the narrative.

## In the Novel

The novel opens with a description of North Carolina and its capital, Raleigh, establishing the American setting that Verne chose for this story of international intrigue:

> "The capital of North Carolina — one of the forty-four states of the Union at this epoch — is the rather important city of Raleigh." (001-014)

North Carolina is the site of [[Healthful House]], located near New Berne on the Neuse River. The sanatorium was chosen by the United States government as a discreet and secure location to confine [[Thomas Roch]], the unstable French inventor of the [[Fulgurator]]. The state's geography — its proximity to [[Pamlico Sound]] and the Atlantic coast — proves to be a critical weakness in the government's security plan.

When [[Thomas Roch]] and his keeper [[Simon Hart]] are kidnapped by [[Captain Spade]] and the crew of the [[Ebba Schooner]], the abduction triggers a statewide response:

> "In every county of North Carolina a special surveillance was organized." (006-010)

The governor of North Carolina was personally informed of the incident:

> "The governor of North Carolina has been apprised that Healthful House has been the scene of a most mysterious outrage." (006-055)

Despite this mobilization, the kidnappers escape through [[Pamlico Sound]] to the open Atlantic, and North Carolina — the last place where [[Thomas Roch]] was under legitimate protection — recedes from the narrative as the action shifts to the hidden stronghold of [[Back Cup]].

## Connections

North Carolina connects to multiple key elements of the novel:

- **[[Healthful House]]**: Located near New Berne, North Carolina, the sanatorium is the state's most significant setting.
- **[[Thomas Roch]]**: Confined in North Carolina for eighteen months before his kidnapping.
- **[[Pamlico Sound]]**: The coastal waterway through which the kidnappers escape from North Carolina.
- **[[Ebba Schooner]]**: The pirate vessel that approached North Carolina waters to execute the kidnapping.
- **[[Atlantic Ocean]]**: North Carolina's coast opens onto the Atlantic, facilitating the pirates' escape route.
