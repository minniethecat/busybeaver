## France

France is the home country of both [[Simon Hart]] and [[Thomas Roch]], the two Frenchmen at the center of Jules Verne's *Facing the Flag* (1896). It is the first nation to which Roch offered his invention, the [[Fulgurator]], and the source of the patriotic motivation that drives Hart's resistance against [[Ker Karraje]] throughout his captivity.

## In the Novel

France's role in the narrative is primarily as the origin point of its protagonists and as the first nation entangled in the political struggle over the [[Fulgurator]]. Before the events of the novel, [[Thomas Roch]] had offered his devastating invention to the French government, making known the nature of the explosive and demanding an exorbitant price.

When France refused, Roch turned to other nations, setting in motion the international competition that ultimately placed him in American custody at [[Healthful House]].

> "He had offered the fulgurator to France, and made known the nature of the explosive." (001-033)

[[Simon Hart]]'s own backstory is tied to questions about his departure from France. A French engineer by training, Hart left his homeland under circumstances that the novel leaves partially ambiguous, though his patriotic motivations become clear as the narrative unfolds:

> "How this Frenchman came to quit France, what motive attracted him to the United States, why the Federal government had intrusted him with a mission of this importance — these were questions which the newspapers discussed with their usual exaggeration." (001-024)

Hart's French patriotism is the moral core of his resistance. Throughout his captivity at [[Back Cup]], he is driven not only by a desire to escape but by a determination to prevent [[Ker Karraje]] from using a French invention against France and other civilized nations:

> "Impelled by a spirit of patriotism, he desired to prevent a French invention from being used against his country." (002-016)

In the novel's climactic moments, Hart's identification with France becomes explicit and emotional. The approaching French warship is described not merely as a national vessel but as an extension of the homeland itself:

> "Frenchman, it is a very part of France that is approaching you! French territory advancing to meet you!" (028-015)

France is also mentioned among the great powers targeted by [[Ker Karraje]]'s campaign of maritime terror, alongside England, Germany, Russia, and America. The international coalition that ultimately confronts the pirate at [[Back Cup]] includes France among its participants.

## Connections

France connects to multiple key elements of the novel:

- **[[Simon Hart]]**: French engineer, the novel's narrator and protagonist.
- **[[Thomas Roch]]**: French inventor of the [[Fulgurator]], whose French origin is central to the story's patriotic themes.
- **[[Fulgurator]]**: First offered to France; the weapon's French origin is a recurring motif.
- **[[Ker Karraje|Count d'Artigas]]**: The pirate against whom Hart's French patriotism is mobilized.
- **[[England]]**: Mentioned alongside France among the powers threatened by Karraje.

## Key Passages

Roch's offer to France sets the political background of the entire novel:

> "He had offered the fulgurator to France, and made known the nature of the explosive." (001-033)

Hart's patriotism is the driving force of his resistance:

> "Impelled by a spirit of patriotism, he desired to prevent a French invention from being used against his country." (002-016)

The novel's emotional peak connects France directly to Hart's liberation:

> "Frenchman, it is a very part of France that is approaching you!" (028-015)
