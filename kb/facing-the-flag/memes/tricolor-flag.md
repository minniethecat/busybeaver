The Tricolor Flag (le drapeau tricolore) is the French national flag and the central symbolic object of *Facing the Flag*'s climactic scene. When [[Thomas Roch]], in the throes of his madness, recognizes the French tricolor approaching [[Back Cup]] aboard a French warship, his patriotic instinct overcomes his mercenary rage, triggering the novel's resolution.

## The Climactic Moment

The novel's title — *Facing the Flag* (French: *Face au drapeau*) — is fulfilled in the climactic passage where [[Thomas Roch]], who has been directing the [[Fulgurator]]'s destructive power on behalf of [[Ker Karraje]], sees the French warship approaching:

> The French flag was flying from the peak of the vessel. (028-012)

Roch's recognition of the flag triggers a transformation. The inventor who had sold his secret to pirates, who had demanded billions from the nations of the world, is suddenly recalled to his identity as a Frenchman. In that moment of recognition, he turns the [[Fulgurator]] against [[Back Cup]] itself, destroying the pirate base rather than allowing it to be used against his homeland.

## Symbolic Resonance

The flag functions in the novel as more than a national emblem — it is the physical manifestation of the [[patriotism]] that has been the submerged theme of [[Simon Hart]]'s resistance and the absent quality in [[Thomas Roch]]'s mercenary spiral. When Roch "faces the flag," he faces his own identity, his abandoned loyalties, and the nation that he had once sought to serve.
