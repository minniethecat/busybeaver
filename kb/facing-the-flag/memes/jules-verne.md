**Jules Gabriel Verne** (1828–1905) was a French novelist, poet, and playwright, best known for his _Voyages extraordinaires_ series of adventure and science fiction novels. He is the author of _Face au drapeau_ (1896), published in English as _[[facing-the-flag-novel|Facing the Flag]]_, which serves as the primary corpus for this wiki.

## The Novel

_Face au drapeau_ was written during Verne's later period, when his optimism about technological progress had grown more ambivalent. The novel explores themes of scientific secrecy, patriotism, and the destructive potential of advanced weaponry through the character of [[thomas-roch|Thomas Roch]], a French inventor who creates a devastating explosive device known as the [[fulgurator|Fulgurator]].

> "Facing the Flag by Jules Verne" (001-001)

The novel was first published in 1896 in France, with an anonymous English translation appearing in the United States in 1897 under the title _Facing the Flag_. A separate British translation by Mrs. Cashel Hoey was published by Sampson & Low under the title _For the Flag_.

## Literary Context

_Face au drapeau_ belongs to Verne's later period, alongside works such as _The Master of the World_ (1904) and _The Invasion of the Sea_ (1905). These later novels often feature isolated genius-inventors whose creations pose existential threats — a departure from the more optimistic technological visions of his earlier works like _[[wikipedia:Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Seas|Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea]]_ (1870).

The novel's concerns about advanced weaponry proved prescient: within two decades of its publication, World War I would demonstrate the devastating potential of modern military technology on an unprecedented scale.
