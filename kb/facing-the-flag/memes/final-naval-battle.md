The Final Naval Battle at [[Back Cup]] was the climactic confrontation between the international naval coalition and [[Ker Karraje]]'s pirate forces, ending with [[Thomas Roch]]'s patriotic recognition of the [[Tricolor Flag]] and the [[back-cup-explosion|destruction of Back Cup]].

## The Coalition's Approach

The battle began when warships from [[France]], [[British Admiralty|Britain]], and the [[US Navy|United States]], having finally identified [[Back Cup]]'s location, converged on the islet. The coalition's strategy combined surface blockade with direct assault, preventing escape through the [[submarine-tunnel]] while bringing naval gunfire to bear on the pirates' defensive positions.

The pirates, though outnumbered and outgunned, possessed one decisive advantage: [[Thomas Roch]]'s [[Fulgurator]], which had been mounted to defend the approaches to [[Back Cup]]. Under [[Ker Karraje]]'s direction, Roch used the weapon against the approaching warships, inflicting significant damage.

## Roch's Transformation

The battle's decisive moment came when [[Thomas Roch]] saw the French warship approaching [[Back Cup]] with the [[Tricolor Flag]] flying from its peak. The sight triggered his dormant [[patriotism]], transforming him from [[Ker Karraje]]'s weapon into his destroyer:

> The French flag was flying from the peak of the vessel. (028-012)

Roch redirected the [[Fulgurator]] against [[Back Cup]] itself, triggering the explosion that consumed the pirate base, its crew, and the invention that had been the object of so much scheming and bloodshed.

## Aftermath

The battle's outcome was total: [[Ker Karraje]]'s organization was destroyed, the [[Fulgurator]] was lost (presumably with its inventor), and the surviving prisoners, including [[Simon Hart]], were rescued by the French warship. The international threat that had driven the novel's plot was extinguished in a moment of patriotic redemption.
