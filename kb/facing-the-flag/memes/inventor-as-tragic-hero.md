The Inventor as Tragic Hero is a literary archetype embodied by [[Thomas Roch]] in *Facing the Flag*: a figure of exceptional intellectual gifts whose personal flaws — greed, paranoia, and pride — undermine his achievements and lead to his destruction. This archetype represents a darker vision of the scientist-inventor than the heroic model found in Verne's earlier works.

## Roch as Tragic Figure

[[Thomas Roch]] possesses the qualities of the classical tragic hero: greatness (his scientific genius), a fatal flaw (his obsessive greed and paranoia), and a reversal of fortune (from honored inventor to pirate captive). His tragedy is compounded by the irony that his greatest achievement — the [[Fulgurator]] — becomes the instrument of his destruction.

## The Dark Turn in Verne's Oeuvre

The tragic inventor archetype in *Facing the Flag* marks a departure from the more optimistic treatment of scientists in Verne's earlier novels. Where Captain Nemo's technological mastery is paired with ethical restraint, Roch's genius is unchecked by moral considerations. This darker vision reflects Verne's late-career pessimism about the relationship between technological progress and human wisdom.
