Effrondat serves as the boatswain aboard the schooner [[Ebba Schooner|Ebba]], the vessel operated by [[Ker Karraje]]'s pirate organization in Jules Verne's *Facing the Flag* (1896). As boatswain, he is responsible for deck operations, sail handling, and anchoring — the practical seamanship that keeps the schooner operational throughout the novel.

## Role Aboard the Ebba

Effrondat first appears during the abduction sequence, preparing the boat that will transport the kidnapped [[Thomas Roch]] and [[Simon Hart|Gaydon]] from the shore to the schooner.

> The boat was lowered, and Captain Spade and five sailors took their places in it. Four of the latter got out the oars. The boatswain, Effrondat, who was to remain in charge of the boat, went to the stern. (004-056)

When the kidnapping party returns with their captives, Effrondat has the boat ready for immediate departure.

> The party made for the boat, and found that Effrondat, the boatswain, had made all ready to receive them. (005-029)

Captain Spade's brusque command — "Hurry up, Effrondat, and cast off the painter" (005-031) — illustrates Effrondat's position in the chain of command: he is a skilled subordinate who executes orders with precision.

## Seamanship

Effrondat's competence as a sailor is demonstrated during the Ebba's departure and approach to [[Back Cup]]. During sail handling, his direction of the crew is noted for its efficiency.

> Captain Spade has also begun to take in sail, and the work, under the direction of the boatswain Effrondat, is executed with the same precision and promptness that struck the observer. (011-059)

When the Ebba arrives at Back Cup, Effrondat manages the anchoring operation.

> All the sailors are drawn up on deck, awaiting orders, and Boatswain Effrondat is making preparations to anchor. (012-047)

## Final Appearance

Effrondat is present at the novel's climax, listed among the pirates gathered at the passage of Back Cup during the final confrontation.

> All the pirates were making for the passage, in front of which were Ker Karraje, Engineer Serko, Captain Spade, Boatswain Effrondat, Engine-driver Gibson and the Count d'Artigas' big Malay attendant. (025-040)

His inclusion in this roll call of the pirate leadership confirms his status as a trusted member of Karraje's inner circle — not merely a common sailor but an officer of the crew.
