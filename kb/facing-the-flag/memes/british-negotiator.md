The British Negotiator was one of several unnamed international representatives who approached [[Thomas Roch]] in an attempt to acquire the secret of the [[Fulgurator]]. Operating on behalf of the [[British Admiralty]], he represents Great Britain's institutional interest in the weapon that Roch had first offered to [[France]].

> The British Admiralty had sent one of its agents to try and obtain the secret. (002-008)

## Role in the Novel

The British Negotiator's approach to [[Thomas Roch]] follows the pattern established by the other powers that Roch approached after his rejection by [[France]]. Each nation sent representatives who attempted to extract the secret of the [[Fulgurator]] from the unstable inventor.

The British representative, like his counterparts, found Roch intransigent. The inventor's paranoia, exacerbated by the French rejection, made him suspicious of all official approaches. He demanded payment before disclosure, a condition no government would accept for an unverified weapon.

> He demanded that the millions should be paid over to him before he would part with his secret. (002-009)

## Significance

The British Negotiator serves a narrative function rather than a characterological one. He is never named, never described, and appears only as an institutional function — the hand of the [[British Admiralty]] reaching toward dangerous knowledge. Together with representatives of [[France]], Germany, and other powers, he illustrates the international dimension of the Fulgurator crisis.
