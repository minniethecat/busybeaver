## Charleston

Charleston is a port city in South Carolina, United States, that plays a significant role in the latter part of Jules Verne's *Facing the Flag* (1896). It is the site of the public naval demonstration of the [[Fulgurator]], and its waters serve as a hunting ground for [[Ker Karraje|Count d'Artigas]]'s pirate operations after he relocates from the Pacific to the Atlantic.

## In the Novel

Charleston first appears in the narrative when Simon Hart, the captive engineer, speculates about the destination of the [[Ebba Schooner]] after his kidnapping from [[Healthful House]]. He considers Charleston among the possible ports the schooner might be heading toward along the Atlantic coast:

> "Savannah, Wilmington in North Carolina, and Charleston in South Carolina. What could be the destination of the schooner?" (002-030)

The city's most significant role comes in the backstory of [[Ker Karraje]]'s operations. After the pirate relocates his base of operations to [[Back Cup]] in the Atlantic, the waters off Charleston become one of his hunting grounds. It was here, four miles offshore in Charleston Bay, that the [[Fulgurator]]'s devastating power was demonstrated:

> "It was in the open sea, four miles off Charleston, in presence of several American warships and a large number of spectators, that the public experiment with the fulgurator was made." (017-007)

This demonstration was an elaborate deception orchestrated by [[Ker Karraje]] — posing as Count d'Artigas — to convince the world that the fulgurator's secret had been lost at sea, while he retained control of both the inventor and the weapon:

> "After the odious comedy in the bay of Charleston Ker Karraje operated in the Atlantic." (017-020)

The ruse was successful: the international community believed the fulgurator to be lying at the bottom of Charleston Bay, and the threat appeared neutralized. In reality, [[Ker Karraje]] continued refining the weapon from his hidden base at [[Back Cup]].

## Connections

Charleston is connected to several key narrative elements:

- **[[Fulgurator]]**: The weapon's public test took place in Charleston Bay, a carefully staged deception.
- **[[Ker Karraje|Count d'Artigas]]**: Used the Charleston demonstration as cover to continue his pirate operations undetected.
- **[[Ebba Schooner]]**: The pirate vessel was sighted in Charleston waters during the Atlantic phase of Karraje's campaign.
- **[[Atlantic Ocean]]**: Charleston lies on the Atlantic coast, the theater of Karraje's relocated operations.
- **[[North Carolina]]**: Charleston is considered alongside North Carolina ports as a possible destination for the Ebba.

## Key Passages

The public experiment at Charleston Bay was a pivotal moment in the narrative — a public spectacle designed to deceive:

> "It was in the open sea, four miles off Charleston, in presence of several American warships and a large number of spectators, that the public experiment with the fulgurator was made." (017-007)

The aftermath of the deception is summarized with bitter irony by the narrator:

> "After the odious comedy in the bay of Charleston Ker Karraje operated in the Atlantic, and there was reason to believe that the schooner Ebba was the instrument of his depredations." (017-020)
