The Russian Representative was one of the unnamed foreign agents who sought to acquire [[Thomas Roch]]'s [[Fulgurator]] after the inventor's rejection by [[France]]. Russia's interest reflected the strategic anxieties of a continental power with extensive coastlines to defend.

## The International Scramble

Like the [[British Negotiator]] and the [[German Representative]], the Russian agent found Roch unwilling to disclose his secret without prior payment. This intransigence, born of his experience with the [[French Government]]'s rejection, frustrated all attempts at conventional diplomatic acquisition of the [[Fulgurator]].

## Narrative Function

The Russian Representative serves to illustrate the global dimension of the Fulgurator crisis. Verne uses these shadowy international figures to suggest that Roch's invention threatened the entire international order, not merely the interests of any single nation.
