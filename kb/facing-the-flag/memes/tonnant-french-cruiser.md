The **_Tonnant_** is a French cruiser that appears in the climactic final chapters of Jules Verne's _[[facing-the-flag-novel|Facing the Flag]]_. As one of the international naval forces converging on Back Cup to confront [[count-dartigas|Count d'Artigas]] and his pirate operation, the _Tonnant_ plays a pivotal role in the story's resolution.

## In the Novel

Among the warships assembled to neutralize [[thomas-roch]] and his [[fulgurator|Fulgurator]] weapon, the _Tonnant_ is described as the swiftest of the squadron:

> "The swiftest of them, the _Tonnant_, a French cruiser, forged ahead..." (028-043)

The cruiser approaches [[back-cup|Back Cup]] at full speed, risking destruction from Roch's devastating weapon:

> "The _Tonnant_, at the risk of being blown to atoms, continued to steam full speed ahead." (028-044)

When the Fulgurator is deployed against the approaching warships, the _Tonnant_ sustains damage but survives the encounter:

> "The _Tonnant_ had not been seriously damaged by the explosion." (028-051)

## Rescue and Aftermath

After [[thomas-roch]]'s patriotic awakening — triggered by the sight of the French tricolor flying from the _Tonnant_ — the cruiser serves as the vessel of rescue. [[simon-hart|Simon Hart]], the narrator, and the inventor Roch are both taken aboard:

> "He was conveyed on board the _Tonnant_, where it was learned from his own lips the series of events..." (028-054)

The _Tonnant_'s arrival thus marks the turning point from tragedy to resolution in the novel's climax.
