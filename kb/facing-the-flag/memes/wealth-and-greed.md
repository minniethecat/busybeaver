Wealth and greed are powerful motivating forces in Jules Verne's *Facing the Flag*, driving both [[Thomas Roch]]'s intransigence and [[Ker Karraje]]'s criminal enterprise. The novel presents a world in which the pursuit of wealth distorts judgment, corrupts loyalty, and ultimately leads to destruction.

## Roch's Pecuniary Obsession

[[Thomas Roch]]'s demand for payment — "Billions! Billions!" — is the obsessive refrain that defines his madness. His fixation is specifically monetary: he will not part with his secret until the money is in his hands. This demand, repeated to every government that approaches him, transforms the [[Fulgurator]] from a technological marvel into a commodity:

> His secret was worth millions, and these millions he would not part with until they were counted into his hand. (002-005)

Roch's greed is presented as both a symptom of his [[madness-and-sanity|mental condition]] and a moral failure. The novel contrasts his mercenary attitude with the [[patriotism]] of [[Simon Hart]], for whom loyalty to [[France]] transcends any personal financial interest.

## Karraje's Plunder Economy

[[Ker Karraje]]'s motivation is more straightforwardly criminal: he seeks wealth through piracy, and the [[Fulgurator]] represents the ultimate tool for extracting it. His operation at [[Back Cup]] is an economic enterprise as much as a military one, funded by the plunder of seized vessels and directed toward the acquisition of ever greater riches.

The collision of these two forms of greed — Roch's obsessive demand for payment and Karraje's ambition to extract wealth through terror — creates the unstable alliance that drives the novel's second half. Neither man trusts the other; each sees the other as a means to wealth.

## Moral Framework

Verne's treatment of wealth in the novel reflects a nineteenth-century moral framework in which greed is a corrosive force. Roch's billions and Karraje's plunder are equally illusory — both are destroyed in the [[back-cup-explosion|explosion of Back Cup]], consumed by the very technology that was supposed to generate them.
