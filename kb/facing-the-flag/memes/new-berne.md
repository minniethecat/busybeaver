New Berne (also spelled New-Berne or Newbern) is a town in [[North Carolina]], located on the [[Neuse River]], that serves as the nearest settlement to [[Healthful House]] and the departure point for the [[Ebba Schooner|Ebba]]'s kidnapping voyage in Jules Verne's *Facing the Flag* (1896).

## Role in the Novel

New Berne is the gateway to [[Healthful House]]. Visitors to the sanatorium, including [[Ker Karraje|Count d'Artigas]], would have passed through the town on their way to the isolated facility. More critically, it is from the waters off New Berne that the [[Ebba Schooner|Ebba]] began its voyage to [[Back Cup]].

> The schooner was anchored off New-Berne, at the mouth of the Neuse. (005-003)

The town's position at the mouth of the [[Neuse River]] made it a natural point of departure for vessels entering [[Pamlico Sound]] and, beyond it, the [[Atlantic Ocean]]. The Ebba's presence at New Berne — a pleasure yacht in a modest coastal town — was itself a form of disguise, masking the vessel's true purpose.

## Geographical Context

New Berne sits at the confluence of the [[Neuse River]] and the Trent River, approximately thirty miles from the open waters of [[Pamlico Sound]]. In the novel's geography, this places it within the surveillance perimeter that the American government established after the kidnapping, though by then the [[Ebba Schooner|Ebba]] was already at sea.
