## Kidnapping of [[thomas-roch|thomas roch]]

The kidnapping of Thomas Roch is the inciting event of [[jules-verne|jules verne]]'s *Facing the Flag* (1896). Occurring on the night following [[ker-karraje|count d'artigas]]'s visit to [[healthful-house|healthful house]], the abduction removes both the inventor and his keeper [[simon-hart|simon hart]] from the safety of the sanatorium and sets the novel's main narrative in motion.

> "There was no help for it, however. The essential was to get possession of Roch. If they could kidnap Gaydon, too, in conformity with the intentions of the Count d'Artigas, so much the better. If not —" (005-008)

## In the Story

The kidnapping was carefully planned. During his daytime visit to Healthful House, Count d'Artigas had toured the facility and assessed the layout, the security arrangements, and the location of Roch's pavilion. [[captain-spade|captain spade]], who accompanied the Count, noted the position of the window to Roch's room and the path through the park to the waterfront.

That night, Captain Spade led a small raiding party that approached Healthful House under the cover of darkness. They crossed the grounds silently, reached the pavilion where Roch and his keeper Gaydon slept, and forced entry. Both Roch and Gaydon were overpowered, bound, gagged, and carried through the park to the shore of [[pamlico-sound|pamlico sound]], where a boat waited to ferry them to [[ebba-schooner|the schooner]] *Ebba*.

> "Captain Spade crept stealthily to the window, and standing on tiptoe, looked in. Through an aperture in the curtain he could see all over the room." (005-009)

## Participants

- **Captain Spade**: Led the raiding party and oversaw the extraction.
- **Count d'Artigas (Ker Karraje)**: Mastermind of the operation, though he did not personally participate in the raid.
- **Thomas Roch**: The primary target, taken against his will.
- **Simon Hart (Gaydon)**: Roch's keeper, taken as a secondary captive.

## Consequences

The kidnapping had far-reaching consequences. It removed Roch from the protection of the American government and delivered him into the hands of an international pirate. It placed Simon Hart in a position to observe Ker Karraje's operation from within, ultimately leading to [[pirate-crew|the pirates]]' downfall. And it demonstrated that even the most secure institutions of the civilized world were vulnerable to determined criminal enterprise.

The event transforms the novel's tone from institutional drama to maritime adventure, as Hart's narrative shifts from the enclosed world of Healthful House to the open sea and the hidden fortress of [[back-cup|back cup]].
