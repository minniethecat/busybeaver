The Gulf of Mexico is a body of water bordering the southern coast of the United States that features in the expanded naval search for [[Ker Karraje]] after the [[kidnapping-of-roch|kidnapping of Thomas Roch]]. Together with the [[Atlantic Ocean]] and the waters around [[Bermuda]], the Gulf was one of the regions where American and international naval forces searched for the pirate's hidden base.

## Role in the Search

After [[Thomas Roch]]'s disappearance from [[Healthful House]], the [[American Secretary of the Navy]] ordered a comprehensive search of the waters through which the [[Ebba Schooner|Ebba]] might have passed. While the primary search focused on the [[Atlantic Ocean]] and the [[Bermuda]] archipelago, the Gulf of Mexico was included as a precautionary measure — a recognition that [[Ker Karraje]]'s base could theoretically be located anywhere in the waters accessible from the American coast.

The Gulf's inclusion in the search perimeter illustrates the scale of the challenge facing the pursuing navies. Without intelligence about the [[submarine-tunnel]] at [[Back Cup]], the searchers had to cover an enormous area, from the Gulf of Mexico to the mid-Atlantic.

## Geographical Context

The Gulf of Mexico is bordered by the United States to the north, Mexico to the west and south, and Cuba to the southeast. Its connection to the [[Atlantic Ocean]] through the Straits of Florida made it a plausible — if ultimately incorrect — location for [[Ker Karraje]]'s hidden base.
