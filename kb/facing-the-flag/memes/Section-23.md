[023-001] "He cannot," I argued, "know that the [[count-d-artigas|Count d'Artigas]] is none other than [[count-dartigas|Ker Karraje]], the pirate. He cannot be aware that he has given up a part of his invention to such a bandit. I must open his eyes to the fact that he will never be able to enjoy his millions, that he is a prisoner in [[back-cup|Back Cup]], and will never be allowed to leave it, any more than I shall. Yes, I will make an appeal to his sentiments of humanity, and point out to him what frightful misfortunes he will be responsible for if he does not keep the secret of his deflagrator."

[023-002] All this I had said to myself, and was preparing to carry out my resolution, when I suddenly felt myself seized from behind.

[023-003] Two men held me by the arms, and another appeared in front of me.

[023-004] Before I had time to cry out the man exclaimed in English: "Hush! not a word! Are you not [[gaydon|Simon Hart]]?"

[023-005] "Yes, how did you know?"

[023-006] "I saw you come out of your cell."

[023-007] "Who are you, then?"

[023-008] "[[lieutenant-davon|Lieutenant Davon]], of the British Navy, of H.M.S. _Standard_, which is stationed at the [[bermuda|Bermudas]]."

[023-009] Emotion choked me so that it was impossible for me to utter a word.

[023-010] "We have come to rescue you from [[ker-karraje|Ker Karraje]], and also propose to carry off [[thomas-roch|Thomas Roch]]," he added.

[023-011] "Thomas Roch?" I stammered.

[023-012] "Yes, the document signed by you was found on the beach at St. George----" "In a keg, Lieutenant Davon, which I committed to the waters of the lagoon."

[023-013] "And which contained," went on the officer, "the notice by which we were apprised that the island of Back Cup served as a refuge for Ker Karraje and his band--Ker Karraje, this false Count d'Artigas, the author of the double [[kidnapping-of-roch|abduction]] from [[healthful-house|Healthful House]]."

[023-014] "Ah! Lieutenant Davon----" "Now we have not a moment to spare, we must profit by the obscurity."

[023-015] "One word, Lieutenant Davon, how did you penetrate to the interior of Back Cup?"

[023-016] "By means of the submarine boat _Sword_, with which we have been making experiments at St. George for six months past."

[023-017] "A submarine boat!"

[023-018] "Yes, it awaits us at the foot of the rocks. And now, Mr. Hart, where is Ker Karraje's tug?"

[023-019] "It has been away for three weeks."

[023-020] "Ker Karraje is not here, then?"

[023-021] "No, but we expect him back every day--every hour, I might say."

[023-022] "It matters little," replied Lieutenant Davon. "It is not after Ker Karraje, but Thomas Roch, we have come--and you also, Mr. Hart. The _Sword_ will not leave the lagoon till you are both on board. If she does not turn up at St. George again, they will know that I have failed--and they will try again."

[023-023] "Where is the _Sword_, Lieutenant?"

[023-024] "On this side, in the shadow of the bank, where it cannot be seen.

[023-025] Thanks to your directions, I and my crew were able to locate the tunnel. We came through all right, and ten minutes ago rose to the surface of the lake. Two men landed with me. I saw you issue from the cell marked on your plan. Do you know where Thomas Roch is?"

[023-026] "A few paces off. He has just passed me, on his way to his laboratory."

[023-027] "God be praised, Mr. Hart!"

[023-028] "Amen, Lieutenant Davon."

[023-029] The lieutenant, the two men and I took the path around the lagoon.

[023-030] We had not gone far when we perceived Thomas Roch in front of us. To throw ourselves upon him, gag him before he could utter a cry, bind him before he could offer any resistance, and bear him off to the place where the _Sword_ was moored was the work of a minute.

[023-031] The _Sword_ was a [[submarine-technology|submersible boat]] of only twelve tons, and consequently much inferior to the tug, both in respect of dimensions and power. Her screw was worked by a couple of dynamos fitted with accumulators that had been charged twelve hours previously in the port of St. George. However, the _Sword_ would suffice to take us out of this prison, to restore us to liberty--that liberty of which I had given up all hope. Thomas Roch was at last to be rescued from the clutches of Ker Karraje and [[engineer-serko|Engineer Serko]]. The rascals would not be able to utilize his invention, and nothing could prevent the warships from landing a storming party on the island, who would force the tunnel in the wall and secure [[pirate-crew|the pirates]]!

[023-032] We saw no one while the two men were conveying Thomas Roch to the _Sword_, and all got on board without incident. The lid was shut and secured, the water compartments filled, and the _Sword_ sank out of sight. We were saved!

[023-033] The _Sword_ was divided into three water-tight compartments. The after one contained the accumulators and machinery. The middle one, occupied by the pilot, was surmounted by a periscope fitted with lenticular portholes, through which an electric search-lamp lighted the way through the water. Forward, in the other compartment, Thomas Roch and I were shut in.

[023-034] My companion, though the gag which was choking him had been removed, was still bound, and, I thought, knew what was going on.

[023-035] But we were in a hurry to be off, and hoped to reach St. George that very night if no obstacle was encountered.

[023-036] I pushed open the door of the compartment and rejoined Lieutenant Davon, who was standing by the man at the wheel. In the after compartment three other men, including the engineer, awaited the lieutenant's orders to set the machinery in motion.

[023-037] "Lieutenant Davon," I said, "I do not think there is any particular reason why I should stay in there with Roch. If I can help you to get through the tunnel, pray command me."

[023-038] "Yes, I shall be glad to have you by me, Mr. Hart."

[023-039] It was then exactly thirty-seven minutes past eight.

[023-040] The search-lamp threw a vague light through the water ahead of the _Sword_. From where we were, we had to cross the lagoon through its entire length to get to the tunnel. It would be pretty difficult to fetch it, we knew, but, if necessary, we could hug the sides of the lake until we located it. Once outside the tunnel the _Sword_ would rise to the surface and make for St. George at full speed.

[023-041] "At what depth are we now?" I asked the lieutenant.

[023-042] "[[About]] a fathom."

[023-043] "It is not necessary to go any lower," I said. "From what I was able to observe during the equinoctial tides, I should think that we are in the axis of the tunnel."

[023-044] "All right," he replied.

[023-045] Yes, it was all right, and I felt that Providence was speaking by the mouth of the officer. Certainly Providence could not have chosen a better agent to work its will.

[023-046] In the light of the lamp I examined him. He was about thirty years of age, cool, phlegmatic, with resolute physiognomy--the English officer in all his native impassibility--no more disturbed than if he had been on board the _Standard_, operating with extraordinary _sang-froid,_ I might even say, with the precision of a machine.

[023-047] "On coming through the tunnel I estimated its length at about fifty yards," he remarked.

[023-048] "Yes, Lieutenant, about fifty yards from one extremity to the other."

[023-049] This calculation must have been pretty exact, since the new tunnel cut on a level with the coast is thirty-five feet in length.

[023-050] The order was given to go ahead, and the _Sword_ moved forward very slowly for fear of colliding against the rocky side.

[023-051] Sometimes we came near enough to it to distinguish a black mass ahead of it, but a turn of the wheel put us in the right direction again.

[023-052] Navigating a submarine boat in the open sea is difficult enough. How much more so in the confines of a lagoon!

[023-053] After five minutes' manoeuvring, the _Sword_, which was kept at about a fathom below the surface, had not succeeded in sighting the orifice.

[023-054] "Perhaps it would be better to return to the surface, Lieutenant," I said. "We should then be able to see where we are."

[023-055] "I think you are right, Mr. Hart, if you can point out just about where the tunnel is located."

[023-056] "I think I can."

[023-057] "Very well, then."

[023-058] As a precaution the light was turned off. The engineer set the pumps in motion, and, lightened of its water ballast, the boat slowly rose in the darkness to the surface.

[023-059] I remained at my post so that I could peer through the lookouts.

[023-060] At last the ascensional movement of the _Sword_ stopped, and the periscope emerged about a foot.

[023-061] On one side of me, lighted by the lamp by the shore, I could see the Beehive.

[023-062] "What is your opinion?" demanded the lieutenant.

[023-063] "We are too far north. The orifice is in the west side of the cavern."

[023-064] "Is anybody about?"

[023-065] "Not a soul."

[023-066] "Capital, Mr. Hart. Then we will keep on a level with the surface, and when we are in front of the tunnel, and you give the signal, we will sink."

[023-067] It was the best thing to be done. We moved off again and the pilot kept her head towards the tunnel.

[023-068] When we were about twelve yards off I gave the signal to stop. As soon as the current was turned off the _Sword_ stopped, opened her water tanks and slowly sank again.

[023-069] Then the light in the lookout was turned on again, and there in front of us was a black circle that did not reflect the lamp's rays.

[023-070] "There it is, there is the tunnel!" I cried.

[023-071] Was it not the door by which I was going to escape from my prison? Was not liberty awaiting me on the other side?

[023-072] Gently the _Sword_ moved towards the orifice.

[023-073] Oh! the horrible mischance! How have I survived it? How is it that my heart is not broken?

[023-074] A dim light appeared in the depth of the tunnel, about twenty-five yards in front of us. The advancing light could be none other than that, projected through the lookout of Ker Karraje's submarine boat.

[023-075] "The tug! The tug!" I exclaimed. "Lieutenant, here is the tug returning to Back Cup!"

[023-076] "Full speed astern," ordered the officer, and the _Sword_ drew back just as she was about to enter the tunnel.

[023-077] One chance remained. The lieutenant had swiftly turned off the light, and it was just possible that we had not been seen by the people in the tug. Perhaps, in the dark waters of the lagoon, we should escape notice, and when the oncoming boat had risen and moored to the jetty, we should be able to slip out unperceived.

[023-078] We had backed close in to the south side and the _Sword_ was about to stop, but alas, for our hopes! [[captain-spade|Captain Spade]] had seen that another submarine boat was about to issue through the tunnel, and he was making preparations to chase us. How could a frail craft like the _Sword_ defend itself against the attacks of Ker Karraje's powerful machine?

[023-079] Lieutenant Davon turned to me and said: "Go back to the compartment where Thomas Roch is and shut yourself in. I will close the after-door. There is just a chance that if the tug rams us the water-tight compartments will keep us up."

[023-080] After shaking hands with the lieutenant, who was as cool as though we were in no danger, I went forward and rejoined Thomas Roch. I closed the door and awaited the issue in profound darkness.

[023-081] Then I could feel the desperate efforts made by the _Sword_ to escape from or ram her enemy. I could feel her rushing, gyrating and plunging. Now she would twist to avoid a collision. Now she would rise to the surface, then sink to the bottom of the lagoon. Can any one conceive such a struggle as that in which, like two marine monsters, these machines were engaged in beneath the troubled waters of this inland lake?
