Simon Hart's Captivity at [[Back Cup]] was the extended period during which the novel's narrator, abducted alongside [[Thomas Roch]] during the [[kidnapping-of-roch|kidnapping]], lived as a prisoner within [[Ker Karraje]]'s hidden base. Under his alias as Gaydon, Hart navigated the dangers of his situation while gathering intelligence and awaiting an opportunity for rescue or escape.

## Conditions of Captivity

Hart's captivity was characterized by relative physical comfort combined with constant psychological pressure. He was housed within the [[back-cup-cavern|cavern]], given limited freedom of movement, and required to continue his duties as [[Thomas Roch]]'s attendant. His interactions with [[Engineer Serko]], [[Captain Spade]], and [[Ker Karraje]] were a continuous exercise in maintaining his cover while probing for weaknesses in his captors' operation.

## The Intelligence Mission

Throughout his captivity, Hart functioned as an involuntary intelligence agent, observing and recording the details of [[Back Cup]]'s layout, the composition of the [[pirate-crew]], the capabilities of the [[Fulgurator]], and the routines that governed life in the hidden base. This intelligence, though he had no means to transmit it, would prove valuable when the [[international-coalition-formation|international coalition]] finally located the base.
