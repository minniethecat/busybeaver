The **Fulgurator Demonstration** refers to the presentation of Thomas Roch's revolutionary explosive weapon, the Fulgurator, to a French government commission. This event, which took place prior to the main narrative of *Facing the Flag*, set in motion the chain of events that drives the novel's plot.

## Background

Thomas Roch, a forty-five-year-old French inventor, developed the Fulgurator — an explosive device of unprecedented power. Confident in its value, he offered it first to France. The French government convened a commission to evaluate the invention, consisting of prominent artillery and naval officers (001-030).

## The Demonstration and Rejection

Roch demanded an enormous sum for his invention — millions of francs — before he would fully disclose its workings (001-036). The French commission, skeptical of his claims and unwilling to meet his financial terms, broke off negotiations without reaching an agreement (001-038). The rejection had a devastating psychological effect on Roch, contributing to his mental deterioration.

## Aftermath

Following the French rejection, Roch's resentment grew. He subsequently offered the Fulgurator to Germany (002-003), raising alarm among French authorities. The international implications of the Fulgurator's potential acquisition by a rival power became a matter of serious concern (002-007). Other nations also attempted to negotiate for the invention, recognizing its potential to transform naval warfare (002-011).

## Significance

The failed demonstration and subsequent rejection represent a critical turning point. France's refusal to purchase the Fulgurator left Roch vulnerable to exploitation by the pirate Ker Karraje (Count d'Artigas), who orchestrated the kidnapping of the inventor in order to gain control of his weapon.
