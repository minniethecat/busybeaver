Simon Hart's Final Report is the implied narrative frame of *Facing the Flag*: the novel itself is presented as the official account that [[Simon Hart]] provided to the [[French Government]] after his rescue from [[Back Cup]], documenting his observations, actions, and the events leading to the destruction of [[Ker Karraje]]'s pirate empire.

## The Narrative Frame

The novel's first-person narration, with its detailed observations, precise chronology, and professional tone, is consistent with a government agent's official report. Hart's identity as a French engineer entrusted with a sensitive mission explains both his access to the events he describes and his narrative perspective — that of a trained observer and patriot.

## Documentary Function

As a report, the novel serves multiple documentary functions: it records the facts of [[Thomas Roch]]'s case for the historical record; it vindicates Hart's conduct during his captivity; it provides intelligence on [[Ker Karraje]]'s methods, organization, and vulnerabilities; and it offers a moral account of the events, identifying the institutional failures (France's rejection of Roch) and personal virtues (patriotism, duty) that shaped the outcome.
