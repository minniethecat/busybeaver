The Back Cup Lagoon was the internal body of water within [[Back Cup]]'s central cavern, accessible only through the [[submarine-tunnel]] that pierced the islet's outer wall. This hidden lake served as a natural harbor for the pirate vessels, including the [[Ebba Schooner|Ebba]] and the submarine tug, and was the operational heart of [[Ker Karraje]]'s maritime enterprise.

## Description

The Lagoon occupied the floor of [[Back Cup]]'s immense interior cavern, a space carved by volcanic forces and hidden from the outside world by the islet's unbroken exterior walls. The only access was through the [[submarine-tunnel]] — a submerged passage navigable only at certain tides and by vessels of shallow draft, a constraint that made the Lagoon an almost impregnable sanctuary.

The cavern's walls rose around the Lagoon, with terraces and chambers carved into the rock where [[Ker Karraje]]'s [[pirate-crew]] lived, worked, and stored their plunder. The [[Ebba Schooner|Ebba]] and the submarine tug were moored at a wooden dock constructed at the Lagoon's edge.

## Strategic Function

The Lagoon was the key to [[Ker Karraje]]'s operational security. No external observer could detect its existence — from the sea, [[Back Cup]] appeared to be a barren, uninhabited rock. The Lagoon allowed the pirates to maintain, repair, and conceal their vessels, emerging through the [[submarine-tunnel]] only when operational necessity demanded it.
