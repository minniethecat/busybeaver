Roch's Offer to Germany was one of the key events in the backstory of *Facing the Flag*, occurring after [[Thomas Roch]]'s rejection by [[France]] and before his eventual confinement at [[Healthful House]]. The offer was part of a pattern of international approaches that Roch made in his quest for recognition and payment.

## The Approach

After the [[French Government]] commission rejected his [[Fulgurator]], [[Thomas Roch]] turned to other European powers. Germany, as a rising military and industrial power, was a natural target for the inventor's approach:

> After France had refused to purchase his invention, he had offered it to Germany. (002-003)

The German government sent a [[German Representative|representative]] to negotiate with Roch, following the same pattern as the other powers. Like all the others, the German approach failed — Roch's demand for full payment before disclosure was a condition no government would meet for an unverified weapon.

## Consequences

Germany's rejection, following that of [[France]], deepened Roch's sense of grievance and paranoia. Each rejection reinforced his belief that the world's governments were conspiring to cheat him of his rightful reward. This progressive embitterment made him increasingly vulnerable to manipulation — a vulnerability that [[Ker Karraje]] would later exploit.
