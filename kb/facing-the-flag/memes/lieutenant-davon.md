Lieutenant Davon is a British naval officer who appears in the climactic sections of *Facing the Flag*. Serving aboard H.M.S. *St. George*, Davon represents the international naval force that converges on [[Back Cup]] in response to the threat posed by [[Ker Karraje]]'s possession of the [[Fulgurator]].

> "Lieutenant Davon, of the British Navy, of H.M.S. *St. George*—" (023-008)

## Role in the Climax

Davon's appearance comes late in the novel, during the [[Escape from Back Cup]] and the events leading to the [[back-cup-explosion]]. His presence signals the shift from individual action (Simon Hart's covert survival within the pirate stronghold) to collective international response. The British Navy, alongside other naval forces, has located [[Ker Karraje]]'s hidden base and is moving to neutralize the threat.

> "In a keg, Lieutenant Davon, which I committed to the waters of Pamlico Sound—" (023-012)

This passage, recounting how Simon Hart's message reached the outside world, connects Davon to the chain of communication that ultimately brings about the pirates' destruction. Hart's message in a keg, set adrift from [[Back Cup]] or the [[Ebba Schooner]], finds its way to the British Navy and triggers the international response.

## Character Significance

Lieutenant Davon is not a fully developed character — he appears briefly and serves primarily as a narrative device representing the arrival of legitimate naval authority. His function is symbolic: the British Navy officer representing the rule of law arriving to confront the lawless pirate enterprise. His brief role underscores the novel's theme of international order reasserting itself against criminal chaos.
