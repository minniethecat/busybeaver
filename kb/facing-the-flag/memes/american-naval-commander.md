The American Naval Commander was the unnamed officer who led the [[US Navy]] squadron ordered by the [[American Secretary of the Navy]] to search the waters around [[Bermuda]] for [[Ker Karraje]]'s hidden base. His operational command represented the military dimension of the American response to the [[kidnapping-of-roch|kidnapping of Thomas Roch]].

## The Search Operation

The Commander's squadron cruised the waters of the Bermuda archipelago, searching for any sign of the [[Ebba Schooner|Ebba]] or the hidden base from which [[Ker Karraje]] operated. The search was hampered by the pirate's use of the [[submarine-tunnel]] — a method of concealment that surface patrols could not detect.

## Coordination with International Forces

As the search narrowed and the [[international-coalition-formation|international coalition]] took shape, the Commander's squadron coordinated with warships from [[France]] and [[British Admiralty|Britain]]. This multilateral operation culminated in the [[final-naval-battle|final battle at Back Cup]].
