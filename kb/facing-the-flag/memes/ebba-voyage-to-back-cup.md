The **Ebba's Voyage to Back Cup** was the covert maritime journey of the schooner [[ebba-schooner|_Ebba_]] from [[pamlico-sound|Pamlico Sound]], North Carolina, to the pirate stronghold of [[back-cup|Back Cup]] in the Bermudas. The voyage transported the kidnapped inventor [[thomas-roch|Thomas Roch]] to the hidden base of the pirate [[count-dartigas|Count d'Artigas]] (Ker Karraje).

## Background

The schooner _Ebba_, commanded by [[captain-spade|Captain Spade]], was the personal vessel of Count d'Artigas. Ostensibly a pleasure yacht of Norwegian origin, the _Ebba_ was in reality a pirate ship used for covert operations along the American coast (002-023). Prior to the voyage, the crew had successfully abducted Thomas Roch from the Healthful House sanatorium in North Carolina and brought him aboard the schooner (004-045).

## Departure from Pamlico Sound

> "it only remained for the _Ebba_ to weigh anchor, descend the estuary and make her way out to sea through Pamlico Sound." (005-044)

The _Ebba_ began preparations for departure at ten o'clock on a night in late May. The schooner was anchored off New-Berne, near the mouth of the Neuse River, which empties into Pamlico Sound (001-014). Captain Spade directly inquired: "Shall I weigh anchor?" (006-003). The departure required navigating through Pamlico Sound and exiting via one of the narrow inlets between the barrier islands into the Atlantic (005-054).

The departure was complicated by increased scrutiny from American authorities. Following the kidnapping, the waters of Pamlico Sound were placed under observation, with patrol boats ordered to board and inspect all vessels (006-005). Despite the risk, the _Ebba_ calmly prepared to depart, and Count d'Artigas did not alter his plans (006-011).

## The Atlantic Crossing

Once clear of Pamlico Sound, the _Ebba_ entered the [[atlantic-ocean|Atlantic Ocean]] and set a course southeast toward Bermuda. The schooner was a swift vessel — described as having the qualities of a "splendid racing yacht," though built for longer voyages (005-052). Favorable weather conditions enabled the _Ebba_ to maintain good speed throughout the passage, the winds being "very favorable" for her heading (005-054).

The crossing lasted several days. When the _Ebba_ was observed weighing anchor and heading for the open sea, it attracted attention, as the vessel was known to make sudden departures without formal clearance (006-031). The crew maintained a deceptive outward appearance — the schooner flew the Norwegian flag, and Count d'Artigas posed as a wealthy yachtsman to avoid suspicion.

## Arrival at Back Cup

The voyage concluded when the _Ebba_ reached Back Cup, a small islet in the Bermudas whose distinctive dome-shaped silhouette resembled an inverted cup (012-056). Approaching from the west, the schooner navigated through the surrounding reefs before reaching the island's eastern side (014-010).

> "Yes, it is Back Cup, sure enough!" (013-001)

The interior of Back Cup concealed a vast cavern, accessible via a submerged tunnel, which Count d'Artigas had transformed into a hidden pirate base (014-032). Thomas Roch was transferred from the _Ebba_ into the cavern, where he was imprisoned and compelled to continue work on his explosive invention, the [[roch-fulgurator-invention|Fulgurator]].

## Significance

The _Ebba's_ voyage represents the transition between the kidnapping of Thomas Roch in North Carolina and his imprisonment inside Back Cup. The successful transit — evading American patrols and crossing the Atlantic undetected — enabled Ker Karraje's plan: to exploit Roch's invention for piracy on a global scale. The journey also established the operational range of the _Ebba_, demonstrating the pirate network's ability to move between the American coast and its Bermudan stronghold with impunity.
