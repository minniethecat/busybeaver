Roch's Offer to Britain was one of the events in the backstory of *Facing the Flag*, occurring in the period between [[Thomas Roch]]'s rejection by [[France]] and his confinement at [[Healthful House]]. The offer to the [[British Admiralty]] was part of Roch's campaign to sell his [[Fulgurator]] to the highest bidder.

## The British Approach

The [[British Admiralty]] dispatched an agent — the [[British Negotiator]] — to negotiate with Roch. The British approach was pragmatic, attempting to extract the secret through negotiation rather than outright purchase:

> The British Admiralty had sent one of its agents to try and obtain the secret. (002-008)

Like the representatives of [[France]], Germany, and other powers, the [[British Negotiator]] found Roch intransigent. The inventor's monetary obsession and deepening paranoia made any conventional negotiation impossible. The British government, unwilling to pay for an unverified weapon sight unseen, joined the list of nations that had failed to secure the [[Fulgurator]].

## Strategic Implications

Britain's failure to acquire the [[Fulgurator]] had significant strategic implications. As the world's preeminent naval power, Britain had perhaps the most to lose from the weapon falling into hostile hands. The British government's subsequent participation in the international naval coalition reflected its recognition that the threat could only be addressed through collective action.
