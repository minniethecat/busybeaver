Paris, the capital of [[France]], is the seat of the [[French Government]] that first evaluated and rejected [[Thomas Roch]]'s [[Fulgurator]]. Though never directly depicted in the novel, Paris looms as the symbolic center of French identity — the homeland to which [[Simon Hart]]'s patriotism is directed throughout his captivity.

## Symbolic Role

For [[Simon Hart]], Paris represents the civilization he is defending against the barbarism of [[Ker Karraje]]'s pirate empire. When Hart resists the attempts of [[Engineer Serko]] and [[Captain Spade]] to turn him, his loyalty is not merely to the French nation in the abstract but to the values — scientific, cultural, political — that Paris embodies.

The novel's climax reinforces this symbolism. The approach of the French warship to [[Back Cup]] is described not merely as a naval operation but as French territory itself advancing toward the captive:

> Frenchman, it is a very part of France that is approaching you! French territory advancing to meet you! (028-015)

## The French Government in Paris

The [[French Government]] commission that rejected Roch's invention sat in Paris. This rejection — characterized by the narrator as "a great mistake" — set in motion the chain of events that delivered the [[Fulgurator]] into [[Ker Karraje]]'s hands. Paris is thus both the origin of the crisis (through institutional shortsightedness) and its symbolic resolution (through the French warship's intervention).
