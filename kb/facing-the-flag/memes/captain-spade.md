Captain Spade is the maritime commander of [[Ker Karraje]]'s pirate organization and the captain of the schooner [[Ebba Schooner|Ebba]]. As a key member of the command triad alongside Karraje and [[Engineer Serko]], Spade executes the operational aspects of the pirates' criminal enterprise — including the pivotal kidnapping of [[Thomas Roch]] from [[Healthful House]].

## Command of the Ebba

Captain Spade's authority over the [[Ebba Schooner|Ebba]] is established from the earliest pages of Simon Hart's narrative. The schooner, ostensibly owned by [[count-dartigas]], is under Spade's direct command, and his seamanship is a critical asset to the pirate organization.

> Captain Spade, commander of the schooner Ebba. (001-015)

The schooner's operations — navigating the treacherous [[Atlantic Ocean]] waters, approaching the submerged entrance to [[Back Cup]], and maintaining the vessel's appearance of legitimacy — all depend on Spade's expertise. His crew, drawn from the [[Pirate Crew of Back Cup]], operates under his discipline with naval precision.

## Role in the Pirate Organization

Spade occupies a position of operational leadership within Karraje's hierarchy. The command structure can be understood as a functional division:

| Commander | Domain | Key Responsibilities |
|-----------|--------|---------------------|
| [[Ker Karraje]] ([[count-dartigas]]) | Strategy and diplomacy | Overall command, public persona, Roch management |
| [[Engineer Serko]] | Technical operations | Cavern infrastructure, weapons, surveillance |
| Captain Spade | Maritime operations | Navigation, the Ebba, tactical execution |

> With Engineer Serko and Captain Spade, Ker Karraje managed to seize a ship in the Pacific. (016-052)

This passage reveals that Spade has been with Karraje since the beginning of the pirate enterprise. He is not a hired hand but a founding member of the criminal organization — his loyalty tested over years of shared criminal activity.

## The Kidnapping of Thomas Roch

Captain Spade's most consequential action in the novel is his leadership of the nighttime raid on [[Healthful House]]. While [[count-dartigas]] provides the intelligence and the cover identity, Spade executes the tactical operation:

1. Navigating the [[Ebba Schooner|Ebba]] into position near the sanatorium
2. Leading the landing party that breaches the facility
3. Securing both [[Thomas Roch]] and his keeper Simon Hart
4. Returning the captives to the schooner for transport to [[Back Cup]]

The raid's success demonstrates Spade's competence in covert operations — a skill set more military than piratical, reflecting the organization's evolution beyond traditional maritime crime.

## Relationship with Engineer Serko

Spade and [[Engineer Serko]] form an operational partnership within the pirate hierarchy. They are frequently observed together on the after-deck of the [[Ebba Schooner|Ebba]], consulting in low voices about navigation, security, and the management of their prisoners.

> Captain Spade and Engineer Serko continue to converse in a low tone, and from the scraps of sentences that reach my ear I gather that they are discussing the probability of being sighted by some ship or another. (010-030)

Simon Hart's observations of the Spade-Serko partnership emphasize their complementary roles: Serko provides technical analysis while Spade contributes maritime judgment. Together, they form the executive arm of Karraje's command, translating strategic directives into operational reality.

> Captain Spade and Engineer Serko watch me. (010-033)

The constant surveillance of prisoners — a task shared between Spade and Serko — reinforces the atmosphere of captivity that pervades the novel's middle sections. Spade's silent watchfulness is as effective a deterrent as any physical restraint.

## Character Analysis

Captain Spade represents the professionalization of piracy in Verne's novel. Unlike the theatrical pirates of romantic fiction, Spade is characterized by:

1. **Discipline**: His crew operates with naval precision, not anarchic violence
2. **Competence**: His seamanship and tactical abilities are repeatedly demonstrated
3. **Loyalty**: His long service to Karraje suggests genuine commitment rather than mere mercenary interest
4. **Reserve**: Unlike the sardonic [[Engineer Serko]], Spade is described as largely silent, his thoughts concealed behind professional composure

This professionalism makes Spade more dangerous than any stereotypical buccaneer. He is not driven by greed or bloodlust but by the efficient execution of criminal operations — a modern sensibility that makes the Back Cup pirates a more credible threat than their romantic predecessors.

## Narrative Presence

Spade appears throughout the novel as a figure of quiet menace. He is present during key moments — the voyage from [[Pamlico Sound]], the approach to [[Back Cup]], the management of the prisoners within the cavern — but he rarely speaks at length. His authority is expressed through action rather than words.

This narrative restraint serves a literary purpose: Spade's silence makes [[Ker Karraje]] and [[Engineer Serko]] more vivid by contrast, while maintaining the sense that the pirate organization extends beyond its visible leadership. Spade represents the operational depth of the enterprise — the layer of competent execution that makes strategic ambition achievable.

## Fate

Like the rest of the Back Cup pirates, Captain Spade is presumably consumed in the [[back-cup-explosion]]. His fate is not individually described — he vanishes along with the schooner, the cavern, and the entire criminal enterprise. This collective annihilation reflects the novel's moral logic: organizations built on crime and coercion perish as a unit, their individual members indistinguishable in destruction.
