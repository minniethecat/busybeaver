The Deflagrator Negotiation was a critical sequence at [[Back Cup]] during which [[Ker Karraje]] and [[Engineer Serko]] attempted to extract from [[Thomas Roch]] the secret of the deflagrator — the triggering mechanism without which the [[Fulgurator]]'s explosive was useless.

## The Two Secrets

[[Thomas Roch]]'s invention consisted of two components. The explosive itself — the substance that could destroy a ship at range — was one secret. The deflagrator — the mechanism that detonated the explosive — was the other. Without the deflagrator, the explosive was inert:

> Without the deflagrator, the explosive was utterly valueless. (020-048)

Roch had sold the explosive formula to [[Ker Karraje]] but retained the deflagrator secret, maintaining leverage over his captors even in captivity. This strategic withholding demonstrated that beneath his [[madness-and-sanity|madness]], Roch retained a calculating intelligence.

## The Negotiation

The negotiation over the deflagrator was conducted primarily by [[Engineer Serko]], whose technical expertise allowed him to frame questions that might elicit information without alerting Roch to his captors' ignorance. Serko's approach combined flattery, technical curiosity, and veiled threats — a sophisticated interrogation that reflected the pirates' recognition that force alone would not extract the secret from a mind as disordered as Roch's.

The negotiation's failure (Roch never fully disclosed the deflagrator) contributed to the ultimate destruction of the [[Fulgurator]]. When Roch turned the weapon against [[Back Cup]], the incomplete knowledge of the pirates meant they could neither prevent nor replicate his act.
