# Idea

In Bergson's *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]*, the "idea" is not a neutral term for [[mind|thought]] but an **object of critique**. The philosophical tradition treats ideas as discrete, atomic units — mental representations that can be combined, associated, and causally linked. Bergson argues that this conception is a **spatialization** of [[mind|thought]]: in lived experience, ideas are not separate items but interpenetrating moments of a continuous mental life. The word "idea" it[[ego|self]], by naming a supposed mental atom, falsifies [[reality|the real]]ity it claims to describe.

## Bergson's Argument

### Ideas as Artificial Units

> [P02-001] …to establish between our ideas the same sharp and precise distinctions…

[[language|Language]] works by carving the continuous flow of [[mind|thought]] into **discrete units** — [[language|words]]. When we speak of an "idea," we are applying to [[mind|thought]] a schema borrowed from [[space]], where items are separate and external to one another. But in [[duration]], what we call an "idea" is not a thing but a **phase** of a continuous [[becoming|process]]:

> [001-012] …call up in both cases the same idea.

We think we are dealing with "the same idea" on two different occasions, but this is a linguistic illusion. The lived meaning of a [[mind|thought]] changes with the whole of one's mental life at each moment. "The same idea" is a **pr[[action|act]]ical fiction** that language imposes on the fluidity of [[mind|thought]].

### The Associationist Error

> [001-013] …ask ourselves in what does this idea consist…

[[association|Association]]ist psychology treats ideas as **atoms** that combine by laws of similarity and contiguity. Bergson's critique is that [[association|associationism]] takes the **product** of analysis (the idea as isolated by reflection) for the **[[reality|real]]ity** of mental life. In lived experience, ideas do not associate — they **interpenetrate**.

| View | Nature of Ideas | Relation Between Ideas |
|------|----------------|----------------------|
| [[association|Association]]ism | Discrete atoms | External (association by similarity/contiguity) |
| Bergson | Phases of continuous flow | Internal (interpenetration in [[duration]]) |

### Ideas, Sensations, and the Brightening Effect

> [001-028] …your sensations and all your ideas seem to brighten up: it is like…

Bergson observes that in moments of intense effort or [[feeling|emotion]], [[sensation]]s and ideas do not merely increase in number or intensity — they **[[quality|qualitative]]ly transform**. The "brightening" of ideas is not an addition of something to pre-existing mental atoms; it is a change in the whole of one's mental state.

## Key Distinctions

### Idea vs. Feeling

[[Feeling]] is not the opposite of idea but its **source**. Every idea, Bergson suggests, is an abstr[[action|act]]ion from a deeper, [[quality|qualitative]], felt whole. The [[intelligence|intellect]] extr[[action|act]]s ideas from the living texture of experience — and then treats these extr[[action|act]]s as if they were the original [[reality|real]]ity.

### Idea vs. Thought

[[Thought]], like idea, is a term that can refer either to the **living [[becoming|process]]** of [[thought|thinking]] (durational, [[quality|qualitative]]) or to its **spatialized product** (discrete propositions, logical relations). Bergson's critique of the "idea" is part of his broader critique of the spatialization of [[mind|thought]].

## Historical Context

Bergson's critique of ideas as discrete mental units targets the entire **associationist** tradition (Hume, [[mill|Mill]], [[spencer|Spencer]]) but also has deeper roots. **[[descartes|Descartes]]** treated "clear and distinct ideas" as the foundation of knowledge — Bergson would say that clarity and distinctness are signs of spatialization, not of truth. **[[kant|Kant]]** made ideas regulative principles of [[mind|reason]] — but still treated them as separate from [[intuition]]. Bergson's innovation is to argue that ideas are not separate from the flow of conscious life; they are **abstr[[action|act]]ions** from it.
