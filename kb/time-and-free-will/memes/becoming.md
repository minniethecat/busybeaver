# Becoming

Becoming (*devenir*) in Bergson's *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]* is not a property that things possess — it is **what things are**. [[reality|Reality]] is not a collection of static entities that occasionally change; [[reality|real]]ity is a continuous process of [[quality|qualitative]] transformation. [[Consciousness]], in particular, is in a "perpetual state of becoming" — its states are never fixed, never complete, always passing into new [[quality|qualitative]] configurations.

## Bergson's Argument

### Reality as Flux

Bergson's metaphysical starting point is that [[reality|real]]ity is a continuous process:

> [P01-006] …flux, a continuous process of becoming, to be grasped by intuition, by sympathetic insight.

This is the positive formulation of Bergson's method. [[reality|Reality]] is not to be reached by "elaborate construction of [[mind|thought]]" — by analysis, division, and synthesis of concepts. It is **given** in immediate experience as a flux. [[Intuition]] is the faculty that grasps this flux directly, without the intermediary of spatial symbols.

### Conscious States as Perpetual Becoming

Conscious states are not static items that can be named and catalogued. They are in continuous transformation:

> [003-306] …are in a constant state of becoming.

> [003-353] …they are in a constant state of becoming, and the same feeling, by the very fact of being repeated, is a new feeling…

A [[emotion|feeling]] of joy is not the same [[emotion|feeling]] from moment to moment. It continuously transforms — incorporating new elements, shedding old ones, changing its [[quality|qualitative]] char[[action|act]]er. What we call "the same [[emotion|feeling]]" is a pr[[action|act]]ical abbreviation for a continuous process of becoming.

> [003-448] …as living things, constantly becoming, as states not amenable to measure…

Conscious states are "living things" — they grow, transform, and interpenetrate. They are not amenable to [[rhythm|measure]] because [[rhythm|measure]]ment requires fixed, discrete units, and becoming admits of neither fixity nor discreteness.

### Becoming and the Self

The [[self]], for Bergson, is not a substance that endures through change. It **is** the change:

> [003-182] …which is in a perpetual state of becoming, with its permanent external…

The [[ego|self]]'s "permanent external" aspect — the social [[ego|self]], the named [[ego|self]] — appears fixed. But this is the [[self|superficial self]], the shadow in space. The [[self|deep self]] is becoming it[[ego|self]], a continuous process that never coincides with any fixed description.

### Becoming and the Inexpressible

The f[[action|act]] that [[consciousness|conscious states]] are in perpetual becoming explains why they resist [[language|linguistic expression]]:

> [003-463] …be expressed in words without becoming public property. How could they…

[[language|Language]] works with fixed terms — [[language|words]] that name stable concepts. But [[consciousness|conscious states]] are never stable. To name a [[emotion|feeling]] is to fix it, to arrest its becoming, to transform a process into a thing. The artist suggests becoming; the analyst kills it.

### Becoming and Free Will

The concept of becoming is essential to Bergson's solution of the [[free-will]] problem:

> [003-365] …being entangled in one another, becoming objective by this very entanglement…

When [[consciousness|conscious states]] interpenetrate and become "objective" — when they take on the apparent fixity of objects in space — we can then treat them as causes and effects. But this objectivity is an artif[[action|act]]. The states themselves, in their living becoming, are not causes that determine effects; they are the [[quality|qualitative]] unfolding of a free [[ego|self]].

### Becoming Unbearable

Bergson uses a striking example to illustrate [[quality|qualitative]] transformation:

> [003-235] …is altered to the point of becoming unbearable. The same does not…

A [[sensation]] that is pleasant at low intensity becomes unbearable — [[quality|qualitative]]ly different — as it occupies more and more of consciousness. This is not a [[quantity|quantitative]] increase crossing a threshold into a new quality; it is a continuous becoming in which each moment is [[quality|qualitative]]ly distinct from the last.

## Key Distinctions

### Becoming vs. Being

Traditional metaphysics opposes being (what is) to becoming (what changes). Bergson reverses the priority: becoming is primary, and being is a **projection** — the [[intelligence|intellect]]'s way of freezing the flux into manageable objects. What "is" in the strict sense is only the continuous process of becoming; fixed beings are abstr[[action|act]]ions.

### Becoming vs. Change

[[Change]] is often understood as a relation between two static states — state A at time T1, state B at time T2, with change being the difference between them. Bergson's becoming is not a relation between states but the **substance** of the states themselves. There are no static states between which change occurs; there is only the continuous flow of becoming.
