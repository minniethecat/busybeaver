# Deliberation

## Overview / Definition

Deliberation, for Bergson, is the conscious [[becoming|process]] through which a [[choice|decision]] takes shape over time. Against the deterministic picture of deliberation as a mechanical oscillation between pre-existing alternatives, Bergson argues that "all the time that the deliberation is going on, the [[ego|self]] is changing" [003-278]. Deliberation is thus not a calculation performed by a static [[ego|self]] upon fixed options, but a **[[evolution|continuous evolution]] of the [[ego|self]]** toward a free [[action|act]].

## Bergson's Argument

**Deliberation as [[ego|self]]-transformation.** The central Bergsonian insight is that deliberation is not about a static [[ego|self]] choosing between fixed options, but about the [[ego|self]] changing through the very [[becoming|process]] of deliberating. "All the time that the deliberation is going on, the [[ego|self]] is changing" [003-278]. By the time a [[choice|decision]] is reached, it is not the same [[ego|self]] that began the deliberation. This undermines the determinist's claim that the outcome could have been predicted from the initial conditions.

**Against the mechanical model.** Both determinists and libertarians "picture the deliberation under the form of an oscillation in space" [003-306], as if the [[ego|self]] swings between two fixed points like a pendulum. Bergson argues this spatial metaphor fundamentally misrepresents deliberation, which is a [[quality|qualitative]], temporal [[becoming|process]] of [[ego|self]]-formation [003-248].

**The formal deliberation critique.** Bergson questions whether we can "estimate beforehand by a formal deliberation" [003-250] what we will decide. The very attempt to formalize deliberation — to treat it as a logical procedure — strips it of the temporal [[dynamism]] that makes it what it is. "Why this deliberation?" [003-249] — the question it[[ego|self]] assumes a spatial framework that distorts the phenomenon.

**Deliberation and the free [[action|act]].** When "our deliberation is finished and our mind made up" [003-301], we have been "present at the deliberation of the [[ego|self]] in all its phases" [003-302]. The free [[action|act]] is not the output of deliberation but its culmination — the point at which the evolving [[ego|self]] crystallizes into [[action|act]]ion. The [[action|act]] is free because it expresses the [[self|whole self]] as it has become through the process of deliberation.

**Deliberation as evolution.** Bergson describes "a [[evolution|natural evolution]] to a free [[action|act]]" [003-278] — deliberation is an organic development, not a mechanical computation. Just as a living [[body|organism]] grows according to its own internal principle, the [[ego|self]] grows through deliberation toward a [[choice|decision]] that expresses its unique [[quality|qualitative]] history.

## Key Distinctions

| Aspect | [[mechanical|Mechanical]] Model | Bergson's View |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| [[self|Self]] during deliberation | Static, unchanging | Continuously evolving [003-278] |
| Options | Pre-existing, fixed | Emergent, shaped by the [[becoming|process]] |
| Process | Oscillation in space [003-306] | Qualitative evolution |
| Outcome | Determined by initial conditions | Free expression of the evolved [[ego|self]] |
| Temporal structure | Reversible (could go either way) | Irreversible ([[ego|self]] is cumulatively changed) |
