# Metaphysics

Metaphysics (*la méta[[body|physique]]*), in Bergson's *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]*, appears less as a positive doctrine than as a **cautionary case** — a discipline that, when it proceeds by abstr[[action|act]] speculation rather than by attending to the immediate data of [[consciousness]], reproduces the same spatializing errors as [[science]] and common sense. Bergson's critique of metaphysics is targeted: he does not reject metaphysical inquiry as such but rejects metaphysics that imports **ready-made spatial frameworks** into the study of duration and [[free-will]].

## Bergson's Argument

### The Common Problem of Metaphysics and Psychology

Bergson opens the book by situating his inquiry at the intersection of two disciplines:

> [P02-002] …Bergson is one which is common to metaphysics and psychology, the problem of freedom.

The problem of [[free-will|freedom]] is "common to metaphysics and psychology" — it belongs to both, and the failure of either discipline to resolve it stems from a shared methodological error: the importation of [[space]] into the domain of [[duration]]. Metaphysics, like psychology, has typically treated [[consciousness|conscious states]] as if they were **external objects** — discrete, measurable, and set side by side in a [[homogeneity|homogeneous medium]].

### Metaphysics as a Separate Domain

Bergson distinguishes the properly metaphysical question from the psychological one:

> [003-287] …another, belonging rather to metaphysics, which the determinists and their opponents solve a priori, the one by denying freedom, the other by affirming it.

The metaphysical approach to [[free-will|freedom]] — whether determinist or libertarian — "solves a priori." Both sides **presuppose** a spatial model of [[causality|causation]] and then argue over whether the [[ego|self]] is an exception to it. Bergson's method is to set aside these a priori metaphysical commitments and return to "the immediate data of consciousness" — the [[quality|qualitative]] feel of [[action|act]]ing freely.

### Metaphysics, Physics, and the Illusion of Necessity

Bergson argues that metaphysical systems like [[spinoza|Spinoza]]'s derive their apparent necessity not from the data of consciousness but from the **prestige of physical science**:

> [003-368] …Descartes' physics and Spinoza's metaphysics, but cannot bind future to present if we give up the spatial and quantitative conception of time…

"[[descartes|Descartes]]' physics and [[spinoza|Spinoza]]'s metaphysics" are treated as parallel cases — both project spatial determinism onto a [[reality|real]]ity that, when approached directly through consciousness, reveals it[[ego|self]] as **[[quality|qualitative]] and free**. The "binding" of future to present is an artif[[action|act]] of the spatial representation of [[time]], not a datum of experience.

> [003-371] …Cartesian physics, Spinozistic metaphysics, or the scientific theories of the nineteenth century…

Bergson groups "Cartesian physics, Spinozistic metaphysics, or the scientific theories of the nineteenth century" as equivalent expressions of the same spatializing tendency. Metaphysics, in this guise, is not an alternative to scientific determinism but its **abstr[[action|act]] [[extensity|extension]]** — the same spatial logic applied at a higher level of generality.

### The "Subtle Metaphysics" That Removes Legitimate Scruples

Bergson warns against metaphysics that explains away the genuine experience of [[free-will|freedom]]:

> [003-375] …mechanism or unless some subtle metaphysics removes our very legitimate scruples…

A "subtle metaphysics" can "remove our very legitimate scruples" — it can make us **doubt** what we directly experience. The "legitimate scruples" are the hesitations we feel when a deterministic theory conflicts with our lived sense of [[free-will|freedom]]. Metaphysics that explains away this conflict, rather than resolving it by returning to the data, is a **sophisticated form of deception**.

| Aspect of Metaphysics | Bergson's Critique | Alternative Method |
|-----------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| A priori [[reason|reasoning]] | Solves [[free-will|freedom]] before examining data | Return to immediate data of consciousness |
| Assimilation to physics | Copies spatial determinism at abstr[[action|act]] level | Recognize [[quality|qualitative]] nature of duration |
| "Subtle" explanation | Removes legitimate scruples about [[free-will|freedom]] | Take the experience of [[free-will|freedom]] seriously |
| Abstr[[action|act]] system-building | Projects spatial frameworks onto duration | Let the f[[action|act]]s of consciousness guide theory |

## Key Distinctions

### Metaphysics vs. Psychology

[[Psychology]], in Bergson's view, has the advantage of being closer to the data — it deals with [[consciousness|conscious states]] as they are **lived**. But psychology, too, falls into spatializing errors when it treats states as [[quantity|measurable magnitude]]s. Metaphysics is even more vulnerable: it operates at a level of abstr[[action|act]]ion where the **[[quality|qualitative]] texture** of experience is easily lost. The proper relation is not metaphysics above psychology but a **reformed metaphysics** grounded in psychological immediacy.

### Metaphysics and Determinism

[[Determinism]] is, for Bergson, a metaphysical doctrine par excellence — it is not discovered in experience but **imposed** on experience through the spatial representation of time. Libertarianism, in its typical form, is no better: it accepts the same spatial framework and merely asserts an exception. Bergson's project is to **dissolve** the metaphysical problem by showing that both sides share a false premise.

### Metaphysics and Science

[[Science]], particularly mathematical physics, provides the model that metaphysics often imitates: a system of necessary laws binding states to one another. Bergson's critique of metaphysics is, in part, a critique of **scientism** — the assumption that the methods successful in studying matter are adequate to studying consciousness.
