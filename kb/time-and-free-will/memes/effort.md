# Effort

Effort occupies a central place in Bergson's argument in *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]* because it sits at the **junction** between inner consciousness and outer [[action|act]]ion. The [[emotion|feeling]] of effort — particularly muscular effort — seems at first to offer psychophysics its strongest case: surely we can feel that one effort is "greater" than another? Bergson's answer is that the [[emotion|feeling]] of effort, properly analyzed, reveals the **[[quality|qualitative]] continuity** between the [[ego|self]] and its [[action|act]]ions — a continuity that the [[quantity|quantitative]] approach necessarily breaks apart.

## Bergson's Argument

### The Psychophysical View of Effort

> [001-002] …sensations, feelings, passions, efforts, are capable of growth and diminution…

The psychophysical tradition treats effort as a **magnitude** — a quantity of psychic force that can be more or less. Maine de Biran, one of Bergson's key predecessors, had already recognized effort as fundamental to the experience of [[ego|self]]hood. But psychophysics, in Bergson's view, corrupted this insight by trying to **[[rhythm|measure]]** effort as if it were a spatial magnitude.

### Muscular Effort and the Body

> [001-057] …e.g. in the case of muscular effort. Let us face this last phenomenon without any preconceived idea…

Muscular effort seems at first sight to be entirely [[quantity|quantitative]]. When we lift a heavier weight, we exert more effort. Bergson grants the apparent force of this argument, then deconstructs it:

> [001-058] Muscular effort seems at first sight to be quite…

When we lift a weight, Bergson argues, what we feel is not a **quantity** of effort but a **[[change|qualitative change]]** in our bodily state. More muscles are engaged, the rhythm of breathing changes, the whole [[matter|body]] participates differently. What we call "greater effort" is [[action|act]]ually a **different** bodily configuration — a [[interpenetration|qualitative multiplicity]] misdescribed as a [[quantity|quantitative]] increase:

> [001-028] …converting them into one single feeling of effort, of growing intensity, so it is always the whole personality which is present…

The [[emotion|feeling]] of growing effort is the [[emotion|feeling]] of more of the [[matter|body]] being **drawn into** the [[action|act]]ion. It is not that the same effort increases — it is that the effort changes in quality as it spreads through the [[matter|body]].

| View of Effort | What Is Measured | What Is Missed |
|---------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Psychophysics | [[quantity|Quantity]] of force exerted | Qualitative change in bodily involvement |
| Bergson | Whole-[[matter|body]] [[quality|qualitative]] state | (Nothing — this is the complete description) |

### Effort and the Self

> [001-024] The effort is accompanied by a muscular contraction…

Effort is not merely a bodily phenomenon. It is the felt experience of the **[[ego|self]]** passing into [[action|act]]ion. When we make an effort, we experience our own duration as a tension between what we are and what we will ourselves to become. This is why effort is central to the problem of [[freedom]]: the free [[action|act]] is precisely the [[action|act]] into which the [[self|whole self]] flows without resistance.

## Key Distinctions

### Effort vs. Action

[[Action]] is the completed outward [[motion|movement]]; effort is the felt **transition** toward [[action|act]]ion. The determinist model treats [[action|act]]ion as the effect of prior causes (motives); effort reveals the continuity between motive and [[action|act]]ion — they are not separate items but phases of one [[becoming|process]].

### Effort vs. Feeling

A [[feeling]] is a state that fills consciousness without necessarily involving [[action|act]]ion. Effort is the [[emotion|feeling]] of **transition** — the felt passage from an inner state to an outer [[motion|movement]]. The [[emotion|feeling]] of effort is uniquely revealing because it bridges the inner-outer divide.

### Effort vs. Will

The concept of [[will]] traditionally separates the mental [[action|act]] of [[choice|decision]] from the physical [[action|act]] of execution. Bergson's analysis of effort shows that this separation is artificial. In the experience of effort, [[choice|decision]] and execution are continuous — they are phases of a single [[quality|qualitative]] [[becoming|process]], not cause and effect.

## Historical Context

Bergson's analysis of effort engages with **Maine de Biran's** philosophy of effort as the primitive f[[action|act]] of [[ego|self]]-consciousness, **[[spencer|Herbert Spencer]]'s** evolutionary account of effort as nervous discharge, and **[[wundt|Wilhelm Wundt]]'s** experimental work on muscular [[sensation]]. Maine de Biran is the closest precursor: he argued that the experience of effort is the foundation of our awareness of [[ego|self]] as a causal agent. Bergson accepts this insight but radicalizes it — effort reveals not only the [[ego|self]] but the **durational** nature of the [[ego|self]].
