# Multiplicity

Multiplicity (*multiplicité*) in Bergson is not one concept but **two radically distinct kinds**: the **quantitative multiplicity** of objects in [[space]], and the **[[interpenetration|qualitative multiplicity]]** of [[consciousness|conscious states]] in [[duration]]. The failure to distinguish these two kinds is, Bergson argues, the source of the insoluble problems of [[free-will]], causality, and the mind-[[matter|body]] relation.

## Bergson's Argument

### Two Kinds of Multiplicity

Bergson's fundamental distinction:

> [P01-007] …if we relate conscious states to their multiplicity, he finds that there are two kinds of multiplicity…

**Quantitative multiplicity** is the multiplicity of things in space: distinct, countable, homogeneous units juxtaposed side by side. This is the multiplicity of **number** — to count items, we must retain each as a separate unit in a [[homogeneity|homogeneous medium]].

**Qualitative multiplicity** is the multiplicity of states in [[consciousness]]: interpenetrating, heterogeneous, incapable of being counted without distortion. A [[emotion|feeling]] of joy is not "one" unit next to a unit of hope; the two interpenetrate to form a **whole** that is different from the sum of its parts.

| Kind | Medium | Char[[action|act]]er | Example |
|------|--------|-----------|---------|
| Quantitative | [[space|Space]] | Juxtaposed, countable, homogeneous | Objects on a table |
| Qualitative | Duration | Interpenetrating, uncountable, heterogeneous | States of consciousness |

### Why the Distinction Matters

When we treat [[consciousness|conscious states]] as a quantitative multiplicity — as if they were separate items that could be compared by magnitude — we commit the error of **spatialization**:

> [001-249] …inapplicable to multiplicity of conscious states…

This error is the root of [[determinism]]: if [[consciousness|conscious states]] are discrete entities with measurable forces, then the "strongest" always wins. But in [[interpenetration|qualitative multiplicity]], states do not **compete** — they **interpenetrate**, each colored by the whole. The determinist's calculation has no object.

## Key Distinctions

### Qualitative Multiplicity vs. Interpenetration

[[Interpenetration]] is the **mode of [[simultaneity|coexistence]]** within a [[interpenetration|qualitative multiplicity]]. States in duration do not succeed one another like points on a line; they pervade one another, each retaining the past while opening onto the future. Qualitative multiplicity is the *structure*; interpenetration is the *dynamic*.

### Multiplicity and Free Will

The problem of [[free-will]] dissolves when we recognize that the [[ego|self]] is a [[interpenetration|qualitative multiplicity]]. The free [[action|act]] is not one state "winning" over others (as in [[quantity|quantitative]] [[causality|causation]]) but the expression of the **whole [[interpenetration|qualitative multiplicity]]** of the [[ego|self]] at a given moment. The [[ego|self]] changes continuously, and each [[action|act]] is the unique expression of that changing whole.
