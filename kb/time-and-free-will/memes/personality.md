# Personality

Personality (*la personnalité*), in Bergson's *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]*, is the **[[quality|qualitative]] unity** of the [[ego|self]] — not a collection of traits, [[tendency|disposition]]s, or mental states, but the whole person present in each of its expressions. A free [[action|act]] is one that "bears the mark of our personality"; a determined [[action|act]] is one in which the personality is bypassed. Personality is Bergson's term for the **[[self|deep self]]** considered as the source and char[[action|act]]er of [[action|act]]ion.

## Bergson's Argument

### The Whole Personality in Each State

> [003-266] …for the whole personality is in a single one of them…

Bergson's radical claim: "the whole personality is in a single one of them" — each state contains the entirety of the person. This is not hyperbole but a strict consequence of duration. In [[duration]], states **interpenetrate**; the past survives in the present; thus every state is colored by the whole history and char[[action|act]]er of the [[ego|self]]. A [[emotion|feeling]] of [[anger]] is not "anger in general" but the anger of **this particular person** with this particular history.

> [003-262] …this hatred reflects his whole personality. Language, however, denotes this state as an impersonal feeling…

Bergson notes a duality: "this hatred reflects his whole personality" but "language denotes this state as an impersonal [[emotion|feeling]]." [[Language]] names generic states — "hatred," "love," "anger" — as if they were identical tokens of a type. But the lived [[reality|real]]ity is that each person's hatred is **unique**, bearing the mark of their entire personality. [[language|Language]]'s impersonality is a pr[[action|act]]ical convenience that obscures the [[quality|qualitative]] [[reality|real]]ity.

### Personality and Freedom

> [003-281] …our acts spring from our whole personality, when they express it…

A free [[action|act]] is one that "springs from our whole personality, when they express it." Freedom is not the absence of determination but the **presence of personality** — when the [[action|act]] expresses not a single isolated motive but the entire [[ego|self]] concentrated in a [[choice|decision]]. The deeper the [[ego|self]] from which the [[action|act]] springs, the freer it is.

> [003-284] …that which bears the mark of our personality is truly free, for our self alone…

"That which bears the mark of our personality is truly free, for our [[ego|self]] alone will account for it." Bergson identifies [[free-will|freedom]] with the **authorship** of the whole personality. An [[action|act]] caused by a single motive, a reflex, or an external pressure is not free because it does not engage the [[self|whole self]]. Freedom is wholeness of expression.

### The Vibrating Personality

> [003-273] …run its course when our whole personality ought, so to speak, to vibrate…

The "whole personality ought to vibrate" — Bergson's metaphor of **vibration** captures the [[quality|qualitative]], resonant char[[action|act]]er of personality in [[action|act]]ion. Personality is not a substance but a **resonance** — how the [[self|whole self]] sounds through each of its [[action|act]]s. When the personality "vibrates," the [[action|act]] is singular and unmistakable; when it does not, the [[action|act]] is generic and determined.

### The Resistant Powers of Personality

> [001-044] …the resistant powers of our personality, and thus to bring us into a state of perfect responsiveness…

In [[aesthetic|aesthetic experience]], rhythm suspends "the resistant powers of our personality" — the habitual filters and defenses that normally shape perception. This suspension reveals the personality's **deeper receptivity** beneath its pr[[action|act]]ical armor. Personality has two aspects: the resistant surface that manages pr[[action|act]]ical life, and the deep resonance that is disclosed in aesthetic and free experience.

### Personality and Duration

> [003-289] …two different tendencies of my personality at successive moments of duration…

Bergson acknowledges that "two different tendencies of my personality" can appear at "successive moments of duration." Personality is not static; it **grows** and **changes** in duration. But this change is not the replacement of one state by another — it is the continuous reorganization of the whole. The personality at time t₂ is not a different personality from time t₁; it is the **same personality, grown**.

| Aspect of Personality | What It Reveals | Contrast |
|----------------------|-----------------|----------|
| Whole in each state | Qualitative unity of the [[ego|self]] | Aggregate of traits |
| Mark of the whole in free [[action|act]]s | Freedom as personal authorship | Freedom as indetermination |
| Vibration of the whole | Resonant expression of the [[self|deep self]] | [[mechanical|Mechanical]] response to stimuli |
| Resistant powers | Pr[[action|act]]ical surface of personality | Deep receptivity |
| Temporal change | Continuous growth of the whole | Replacement of states |

## Key Distinctions

### Personality vs. Ego

The [[ego]] is the [[ego|self]] as **object** — the [[ego|self]] that psychology studies, language names, and pr[[action|act]]ical life n[[self|ego]]tiates. Personality is the [[ego|self]] as **subject** — the living, growing, vibrating whole that cannot be objectified without distortion. Bergson's philosophy is a sustained effort to move from the [[self|ego]] to the personality.

### Personality and the Self

The [[self]] in Bergson has two levels: the [[self|superficial self]] (spatialized, linguistic, social) and the [[self|deep self]] (durational, [[quality|qualitative]], personal). Personality is not identical to the [[self|deep self]] — it is the [[self|deep self]] considered under the aspect of **expression** and **authorship**. Personality is the [[ego|self]] in the [[action|act]] of manifesting it[[ego|self]].

### Personality and Freedom

[[Freedom]] is personality in [[action|act]]ion. When the personality is fully engaged — when the [[self|whole self]] vibrates in the [[action|act]] — the [[action|act]] is free. When the personality is bypassed — when habit, reflex, or external pressure determine the [[action|act]] — the [[action|act]] is determined. Freedom and personality are two names for the same [[reality|real]]ity: the [[quality|qualitative]] unity of the [[ego|self]] expressing it[[ego|self]] without impediment.
