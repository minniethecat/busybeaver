# Ego

The [[self|ego]] (*moi*) in Bergson's *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]* is not a single entity but a **two-level [[reality|real]]ity**. The **superficial ego** is the self as it appears in social life and language — its states are separated, named, and arranged in [[space]]. The **deep ego** (*moi profond*) is the self as it lives in [[duration]] — its states interpenetrate, form an [[organic|organic whole]], and resist analysis into discrete parts. The con[[interpenetration|fusion]] of these two [[self|ego]]s is, Bergson argues, the root of the [[free-will]] problem.

## Bergson's Argument

### The Deep Ego in Duration

The deep ego is the self that lives **immediately** in duration, before language and social [[habit|convention]] impose spatial distinctions:

> [003-123] …conscious states assume when our ego lets itself live, when it refrains from separating its present state from its former states.

When the [[self|ego]] "lets itself live" — when it does not impose spatial cat[[self|ego]]ries on its own experience — its states form a continuous, [[quality|qualitative]] [[multiplicity]]. Each state is colored by the whole past, and the whole past is present in each state. This is the [[self|ego]] of **[[duration|real duration]]**.

### The Superficial Ego in Space

The superficial ego is the [[self|ego]] of **pr[[action|act]]ical life**. It projects its states onto a [[homogeneity|homogeneous medium]], separates them into distinct items, and names them with [[language|words]]:

> [003-266] …phantom self, the shadow of the ego projecting itself into space.

This "phantom self" is not an illusion — it is pr[[action|act]]ically necessary for [[action|act]]ion and communication. But it is a **shadow** of [[reality|the real]] [[self|ego]], a spatialized representation that mistakes the map for the territory.

> [003-273] …lodge on the surface of our ego and there get solidified in the form of independent and distinct states…

[[language|Language]] solidifies the fluid states of the deep ego into fixed, nameable items. Over time, we come to believe that these solidified states **are** the [[self|ego]], when they are only its spatial projection.

### The Ego and Time

The cont[[action|act]] between the [[self|ego]] and the external world reveals the nature of duration:

> [003-173] …real duration. In a word, our ego comes in contact with the external world…

The deep ego endures — its states are not external to one another but interpenetrate. The superficial ego lives in homogeneous [[time]], where states are arranged like points on a line. The difference between the two [[self|ego]]s is ultimately the difference between duration and space.

> [003-182] …the fleeting duration of our ego is fixed by its projection in homogeneous space…

### Why We Confuse the Two Egos

Bergson argues that social life and language compel us to live primarily in the superficial ego:

> [003-189] …the conventional ego, shows us under this appearance an impersonal ego…

The "[[habit|convention]]al [[self|ego]]" is the self we present to others and to ourselves in reflective [[mind|thought]]. Beneath it, the deep ego continues its [[quality|qualitative]] life, but we rarely attend to it directly. The task of philosophy is to **recover** the deep ego from beneath the layers of spatialized [[habit|convention]].

| Ego Level | Medium | States | Freedom |
|-----------|--------|--------|---------|
| Deep [[self|ego]] | Duration | Interpenetrating, [[quality|qualitative]] | **Free** (expresses the whole) |
| Superficial [[self|ego]] | [[space|Space]] | Juxtaposed, named, discrete | Determined (by isolated motives) |

## Key Distinctions

### Deep Ego vs. Superficial Ego

The deep ego is the self of **immediate experience** — the continuous, [[quality|qualitative]] [[becoming|flux]] that constitutes [[reality|real]] conscious life. The superficial ego is the self of **reflective analysis** — the self as an object of [[mind|thought]], divided into distinct [[emotion|feeling]]s, [[idea|ideas]], and motives. Both are [[reality|real]], but they are [[reality|real]] in different senses: the deep ego is metaphysically primary; the superficial ego is a pr[[action|act]]ical construction.

### Ego and the Free Act

The free [[action|act]], for Bergson, is the [[action|act]] that **expresses the whole deep ego**. When an [[action|act]]ion flows from the entirety of one's lived duration — not from an isolated motive or external pressure — it is free. The determinist's error is to treat the superficial ego (with its discrete, comparable motives) as the [[self|whole self]], and then to conclude that the "strongest motive" always wins.

## Historical Context

Bergson's two-[[self|ego]] model engages with **[[kant|Kant]]'s** distinction between the empirical [[self|ego]] (the self as it appears) and the transcendental [[self|ego]] (the self as condition of experience). But Bergson rejects [[kant|Kant]]'s conclusion that the [[self|deep self]] is unknowable — it is directly **lived**, even if language cannot capture it without distortion. The concept also anticipates **[[will|Will]]iam [[james|James]]'s** distinction between the "I" (self-as-subject) and the "me" (self-as-object), and influences later phenomenological accounts of the pre-reflective self.
