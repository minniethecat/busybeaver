# Endosmosis

Endosmosis (*l'endosmose*) is a term Bergson borrows from physics — where it denotes the dif[[interpenetration|fusion]] of a fluid through a semi-permeable membrane — to describe a **pervasive cognitive phenomenon**: the illicit intermingling of qualities that belong to distinct domains. In Bergson's usage, endosmosis is not a neutral [[becoming|process]] of exchange but a **source of philosophical error**. It is the [[mechanical|mechanism]] by which the spatial contaminates the temporal, the [[quantity|quantitative]] infiltrates the [[quality|qualitative]], and the language of [[determinism]] seeps into our [[mind|understanding]] of [[free-will]].

## Bergson's Argument

### Endosmosis Between Succession and Externality

The primary site of endosmosis is the relation between [[succession|temporal succession]] and spatial externality:

> [003-146] …between this succession without externality and this externality without succession, a kind of exchange takes place, very similar to what physicists call the phenomenon of endosmosis.

Bergson describes "succession without externality" — pure temporal order, the way [[consciousness|conscious states]] follow one another in [[duration]] — and "externality without succession" — pure spatial order, the way objects exist side by side in [[space]]. These are **conceptually distinct** domains, yet in ordinary [[mind|thought]], "a kind of exchange takes place": succession borrows the discreteness of spatial juxtaposition, and space borrows the ordering of [[succession|temporal succession]]. The result is not a synthesis but a **hybrid** — the spatialized idea of [[time]] as a measurable line.

### The Pendulum and Conscious Life

Bergson illustrates this endosmosis with the image of the pendulum:

> [003-146] As the successive phases of our conscious life, although interpenetrating, correspond individually to an oscillation of the pendulum which occurs at the same time…

The successive phases of conscious life "interpenetrate" — they form a [[quality|qualitative]] [[multiplicity]], each phase permeating the others. But each phase also "corresponds individually to an oscillation of the pendulum" — the physical, spatial event of the clock. The endosmosis occurs when we transfer the **discreteness** of the pendulum oscillations onto the interpenetrating phases of consciousness, treating them as if they, too, were separate and countable.

### Endosmosis Between Intensity and Extension

Bergson identifies a second site of endosmosis in our experience of motion:

> [003-152] But here again we meet with a case of endosmosis, an intermingling of the purely intensive sensation of mobility with the extensive representation of the space traversed.

The "purely intensive [[sensation]] of mobility" — the felt quality of moving, a state of [[consciousness]] — intermingles with "the extensive representation of the space traversed" — the measurable distance covered. We then "attribute to the motion the divisibility of the space" — we treat the [[quality|qualitative]] [[action|act]] of moving as if it were composed of the **infinitely divisible parts** of the space traversed. This is Zeno's error, reproduced at the level of perception.

### Endosmosis Between Freedom and Necessity

The most consequential endosmosis concerns the relation between free effort and causal necessity:

> [003-392] …an exchange, which again may be compared to the phenomenon of endosmosis, takes place between the dynamic idea of free effort and the mathematical concept of necessary determination.

The "dynamic idea of free effort" — the lived experience of [[action|act]]ing freely, a [[dynamic]] and [[quality|qualitative]] [[reality|real]]ity — exchanges properties with "the mathematical concept of necessary determination" — the abstr[[action|act]], spatial model of [[causality|causation]]. Through this endosmosis, [[free-will|freedom]] comes to be conceived as a **mysterious exception** to [[causality|causal law]], rather than as the [[quality|qualitative]] char[[action|act]]er of conscious [[action|act]]ion it[[ego|self]]. The very formulation of the free-will problem, Bergson suggests, is a product of endosmosis.

### The Mixed Idea of Measurable Time

Endosmosis reaches its culmination in the hybrid concept of [[time|measurable time]]:

> [003-433] Thus, by a real process of endosmosis we get the mixed idea of a measurable time, which is space in so far as it is homogeneity, and duration in so far as it is succession, that is to say, at bottom, the contradictory idea of succession in homogeneity.

"Measurable time" is a "mixed idea" — it is "space in so far as it is homogeneity, and duration in so far as it is succession." The result is "at bottom, the contradictory idea of succession in homogeneity" — a logical impossibility that we nevertheless live with because endosmosis makes the contradiction **invisible** to ordinary [[mind|thought]]. The philosopher's task is to reverse the endosmosis — to separate what has been illicitly mixed.

| Site of Endosmosis | Pure [[quality|Quality]] A | Pure [[quality|Quality]] B | Hybrid Result |
|-------------------|----------------|----------------|---------------|
| [[succession|Succession]] ↔ Externality | Temporal order without juxtaposition | Spatial order without succession | Measurable, linear time |
| Intensity ↔ [[extension|Extension]] | Felt quality of mobility | Divisibility of space traversed | [[motion|Motion]] as infinitely divisible |
| Freedom ↔ Necessity | [[dynamic|Dynamic]] idea of free effort | Mathematical causal determination | Freedom as exception to law |
| Duration ↔ [[homogeneity|Homogeneity]] | Qualitative succession | Homogeneous medium | The "contradictory idea" of [[time|measurable time]] |

## Key Distinctions

### Endosmosis vs. Interpenetration

[[Interpenetration]] is the **legitimate** mutual permeation of [[consciousness|conscious states]] within duration — states that "interpenetrate" without losing their [[quality|qualitative]] distinctness. Endosmosis, by contrast, is the **illegitimate** mixing of qualities from different domains. Interpenetration is a positive datum of consciousness; endosmosis is a cognitive error to be undone by philosophical analysis.

### Endosmosis vs. Synthesis

A genuine synthesis would integrate two elements into a new whole that preserves what is distinct in each. Endosmosis does not synthesize — it **confuses**. The hybrid concepts it produces ([[time|measurable time]], spatialized motion, necessary [[free-will|freedom]]) are not richer than their sources but **poorer** — they lose the [[quality|qualitative]] specificity of each pure term.

### Endosmosis and the Project of *Time and Free Will*

Bergson's entire method in *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]* can be understood as the systematic **reversal of endosmosis**. Each chapter separates what has been illicitly mixed: intensity from magnitude (Chapter I), duration from space (Chapter II), [[free-will|freedom]] from determinism (Chapter III). The method is not to construct new concepts but to **purify** existing ones by undoing the endosmosis that has corrupted them.
