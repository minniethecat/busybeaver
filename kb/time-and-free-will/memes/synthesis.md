# Synthesis

Synthesis (*synthèse*) in Bergson's *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]* is the **fundamental operation** of [[consciousness]]. It is the [[action|act]] by which the mind unifies a [[quality|qualitative]] [[multiplicity]] — successive positions into a perception of [[motion]], discrete units into a [[number]], and separate [[sensation]]s into a continuous [[quality|qualitative]] whole. Synthesis is what consciousness **does** in [[duration]]; analysis — the decomposition of the whole into parts — is what the [[intelligence|intellect]] does in [[space]].

## Bergson's Argument

### Synthesis as the Act of Consciousness

Bergson identifies synthesis as the defining [[action|act]]ivity of consciousness:

> [003-067] …speaking more exactly, as the synthesis of the one and the many.

Every conscious state is a synthesis: it unifies a multiplicity of elements into a single [[quality|qualitative]] whole. A [[motion|perception of motion]] synthesizes successive positions; a [[emotion|feeling]] of joy synthesizes innumerable [[sensation]]s, memories, and anticipations. The unity of consciousness is not a static oneness but an **[[action|act]]ive synthesis** — a unification that is continuously achieved and never completed.

### Number as Synthesis

[[number|Number]], for Bergson, is not a primitive given but a product of mental synthesis:

> [003-080] …a unit, in so far as it is a synthesis of the units which compose it…

To count is to synthesize: we retain each unit while adding the next, holding them together in a single [[action|act]] of consciousness. This synthesis is possible only because we project the units onto a **[[homogeneity|homogeneous medium]]** — [[space]]. Without this spatial projection, the units would interpenetrate [[quality|qualitative]]ly and could not be counted.

### Synthesis of Motion

The [[motion|perception of motion]] is a paradigm case of mental synthesis:

> [003-151] …point to another, is a mental synthesis, a psychic and therefore unextended process…

> [003-152] …successive positions and the synthesis of these positions. The first…

When we perceive a moving object, we do not perceive an infinity of positions. We synthesize the successive positions into a **single, indivisible [[action|act]]** of perception. The motion is not in space — it is in consciousness, as a synthesis. [[space|Space]] contains only positions; consciousness contains the synthesis of positions that we call motion.

### Synthesis and Extensity

Bergson uses the concept of synthesis to critique the empiricist account of space perception:

> [003-106] …extensity cannot result from synthesis of unextended sensations…

> [003-107] …supposed to result from their synthesis, as water from the combination of oxygen and hydrogen…

The empiricist claims that our perception of space is synthesized from unextended [[sensation]]s. Bergson replies that synthesis cannot produce extensity from non-extended elements. If the elements have no spatial char[[action|act]]er, no amount of synthesis will create space. Extensity must be **given** in the elements themselves, not produced by their combination.

### Synthesis in Duration

The synthesis performed by consciousness in duration is fundamentally different from [[intelligence|intellect]]ual synthesis:

> [003-173] …but, on the other hand, the synthesis carried out by our consciousness…

In duration, synthesis is **[[quality|qualitative]]**: it does not juxtapose elements but interpenetrates them. Each synthesized element retains its connection to the whole, and the whole is present in each element. This is the synthesis of the deep [[ego]], not the additive synthesis of the [[intelligence|intellect]].

### Synthesis and the Qualitative Transformation

When consciousness synthesizes new elements into an existing state, the state it[[ego|self]] **changes [[quality|qualitative]]ly**:

> [003-085] …state which results from their synthesis will have changed for this very reason…

The synthesis of a new [[sensation]] into a [[emotion|feeling]] of joy does not add to the joy — it **transforms** the joy into a different joy. Synthesis in duration is not additive but transformative. This is why psychic states cannot be [[rhythm|measure]]d: each synthesis produces a new quality, not a larger quantity.

## Key Distinctions

### Qualitative Synthesis vs. Quantitative Synthesis

Quantitative synthesis (in space) adds homogeneous units — 2 + 2 = 4, and the 4 is the same kind of thing as the 2. Qualitative synthesis (in duration) produces a new quality — joy + memory = a different joy, not "more" joy. The failure to distinguish these two syntheses is the root of [[psychophysics]].

### Synthesis vs. Analysis

[[analysis|Analysis]] decomposes a whole into parts; synthesis unifies parts into a whole. The [[intelligence|intellect]] **analyzes** — it breaks duration into discrete instants, the [[ego|self]] into separate motives. Consciousness **synthesizes** — it lives the interpenetration of states as a [[quality|qualitative]] whole. Philosophy's task is to recover the synthetic unity that analysis obscures.
