# Analysis

Analysis (*analyse*) in Bergson's philosophy is the **[[intelligence|intellect]]ual operation of decomposition** — the breaking of a whole into parts that are external to one another.

It is the default mode of conceptual [[thought|thinking]], indispensable for science and pr[[action|act]]ical life, but fundamentally incapable of grasping [[reality|the real]]ity of [[consciousness]]. Where analysis divides, [[reality|real]]ity interpenetrates; where analysis immobilizes, [[reality|real]]ity flows.

## Bergson's Argument

### Analysis as the Default Intellectual Mode

Bergson recognizes that analysis is the natural operation of the [[intelligence|intellect]]. We analyze a [[sensation]] into its components, an [[emotion]] into its elements, an [[idea]] into its constituent parts. This operation serves pr[[action|act]]ical needs — language, communication, prediction — but it systematically substitutes **spatial relations** for temporal ones:

> Concepts break up the continuous flow of reality into parts external to one another, they further the interests of language and social life and are useful primarily for practical purposes. But they give us nothing of the life and movement of reality… [P01-006]

The parts that analysis produces are not found in experience; they are **created** by the analytical [[action|act]] it[[ego|self]], which imposes a spatial grid on a temporal [[becoming|flux]].

### The Limits of Psychological Analysis

Bergson repeatedly demonstrates that psychological analysis reaches a limit beyond which it cannot go — not because of insufficient subtlety, but because the object of analysis is **[[quality|qualitative]]** and resists decomposition:

> …analysis often fails to discover in a work of art the elements which give rise to our emotion… [001-048]

The richer an [[feeling|emotion]]al state, the more completely it resists analytical decomposition. A great work of art produces a [[emotion|feeling]] that is **simple** in the sense of being indivisible — analysis can enumerate its conditions but never capture the [[emotion|feeling]] it[[ego|self]]. The artist **suggests** what analysis can only **dissect**.

### Analysis and the Idea of Number

Bergson's analysis of number reveals analysis at work on its own foundation:

> …our analysis of the idea of number could not but bring us to this conclusion… [003-102]

When we analyze the idea of number, we discover that it presupposes the [[intuition]] of space — a [[homogeneity|homogeneous medium]] in which identical units can be juxtaposed. Analysis thus reveals its own spatial presupposition. But this revelation is the limit of analysis: it cannot get "behind" space because spatialization is what analysis **does**.

### Analysis Distorts Its Object

Bergson insists that analysis is not a neutral instrument. It transforms what it touches:

> …the analysis which we have just attempted shows that time, as dealt with by the astronomer and the physicist, does indeed seem to be measurable and therefore homogeneous. [003-141]

When we analyze [[time]], we turn it into space — a line of discrete instants. When we analyze a [[sensation]] of pressure, we turn it into a collection of local [[sensation]]s. When we analyze the [[self]], we turn it into a bundle of discrete states. In every case, analysis **creates** the very discontinuities it claims to discover.

### The Role of Analysis in Bergson's Own Method

Bergson does not simply reject analysis — he **uses** it to reveal its own limits. Much of *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]* consists of careful psychological analysis that demonstrates where analysis breaks down:

> We do not propose to carry this analysis any further. The psychic state… [001-056]

The point is not to abandon analysis but to recognize that it must be **supplemented** by [[intuition]]. Analysis prepares the ground by clearing away spatial prejudices; [[intuition]] then grasps [[reality|the real]]ity that analysis has exposed but cannot capture.

## Key Distinctions

### Analysis vs. Intuition

| Aspect | Analysis | [[intuition|Intuition]] |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Operation | Divides, decomposes, immobilizes | Enters into, sympathizes, follows |
| Product | Parts external to one another | The whole in its [[motion|movement]] |
| Relation to space | Projects spatial relations | Turns away from space toward duration |
| Knowledge | Relative, symbolic, pr[[action|act]]ical | Absolute, direct, philosophical |

Analysis gives us **symbols** of [[reality|real]]ity; [[intuition]] gives us [[reality|real]]ity it[[ego|self]]. Both are necessary, but the error of traditional philosophy is to take the symbol for the thing.

### Analysis and Duration

[[Duration]] (*[[duration|durée]]*) is what analysis cannot reach. Duration is continuous, [[quality|qualitative]], interpenetrating — precisely the qualities that analysis, by its nature, must divide and immobilize. Every attempt to analyze duration turns it into space. The recognition of this limit is the starting point of Bergson's philosophy.

### Analysis and Sensation

Bergson's treatment of [[sensation]] demonstrates the analytical distortion in miniature.

When we analyze a [[sensation]] of pressure into "a greater number of peripheral [[sensation]]s" [001-070], we substitute a spatial description (many discrete [[sensation]]s spread over the [[matter|body]]) for a [[quality|qualitative]] experience (a pressure that has changed in kind). The analysis is useful for physiology but misses the conscious f[[action|act]].

### The Two Analyses

Bergson implicitly distinguishes two kinds of analysis:

| Kind | Method | Outcome |
|------|--------|---------|
| Spatial analysis | Decomposes into discrete units in a [[homogeneity|homogeneous medium]] | Useful for science; distorts consciousness |
| Reflexive analysis | Traces the genesis of spatial habits in consciousness | Reveals the limits of analysis it[[ego|self]] |

Bergson's own method is reflexive analysis — analysis turned back upon it[[ego|self]] to reveal the spatial assumptions that make it possible. This is the propaedeutic to [[intuition]].
