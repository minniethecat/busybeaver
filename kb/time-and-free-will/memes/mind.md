# Mind

In Bergson's *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]*, the mind (l'esprit) is not a neutral recorder of experience but an **[[action|act]]ive transformer** of it. The mind's fundamental operation is to **spatialize** — to break the continuous, [[quality|qualitative]] [[becoming|flux]] of [[duration]] into discrete, homogeneous, measurable units. This operation makes pr[[action|act]]ical life and science possible, but it systematically distorts our understanding of inner experience. The mind, for Bergson, is both our indispensable tool and our primary obstacle to grasping [[reality|real]]ity as it is.

## Bergson's Argument

### The Mind as Spatializing Faculty

> [001-015] …which occurs immediately to the mind, once it has entered upon this path…

Bergson argues that the mind has a deep and nearly irresistible tendency to treat **everything** as if it were in [[space]]. When we think about time, we imagine a line. When we think about [[emotion|feeling]]s, we imagine them as more or less intense — i.e., as occupying more or less "space" in consciousness. When we think about numbers, we imagine them as units arranged side by side. This spatializing habit is not a contingent error — it is **constitutive** of what the mind does:

> [001-060] We picture to our minds a psychic force imprisoned in the body…

The mind cannot think about inner states without representing them in spatial terms. This is why Bergson insists that the **language** of psychology is already compromised: every psychological term (intensity, magnitude, faculty, association) smuggles in a spatial schema.

### Mind and Unity

> [001-092] Act, of mind: all unity due to…

One of the mind's most important operations is the **[[synthesis|unification]]** of multiplicity. When we perceive a [[melody]], we do not hear isolated notes — we hear a whole. This [[synthesis|unification]] is the mind's **[[action|act]]**, not a property of the notes themselves:

> [003-067] …since it is brought before the mind by a simple intuition and is not constructed by an intellectual synthesis…

But Bergson distinguishes two kinds of unity: the **[[quality|qualitative]] unity** of duration (the interpenetration of [[consciousness|conscious states]]) and the **[[quantity|quantitative]] unity** of space (the juxtaposition of homogeneous units). The mind, by default, produces the second kind — it turns [[quality|qualitative]] wholes into [[quantity|quantitative]] aggregates.

| Mental Operation | What It Does | What It Loses |
|-----------------|-------------|---------------|
| Spatialization | Puts states "side by side" | The interpenetration of duration |
| Quantification | Assigns magnitudes | Qualities unique to each moment |
| Unification (spatial) | Groups under a concept | The lived continuity of experience |

### Mind and Intuition

> [003-081] …multiplicity of the act by which the mind perceives it. And there is no contradiction…

Bergson does not deny the mind its role. The mind is necessary for [[action|act]]ion, for science, for communication. But he insists that there is another mode of knowledge — **[[intuition]]** — that grasps [[reality|real]]ity without spatializing it. [[intuition|Intuition]] is not mind-less; it is mind operating in a different register — attending to the **[[quality|qualitative]]** rather than the [[quantity|quantitative]], the **continuous** rather than the discrete.

## Key Distinctions

### Mind vs. Consciousness

[[Consciousness]] is the immediate, [[quality|qualitative]] [[becoming|flux]] of experience. Mind is the faculty that **reflects on** consciousness, breaks it into parts, and relates those parts in space. Consciousness lives; mind analyzes. The error of traditional philosophy is to confuse the mind's analysis with consciousness it[[ego|self]].

### Mind vs. Intuition

[[Intuition]] is the mode of knowledge that grasps [[reality|real]]ity in its durational continuity. Mind, in its default mode, cannot do this — it spatializes. But mind can **discipline it[[ego|self]]** to attend differently, and this disciplined [[attention]] is [[intuition]]. [[intuition|Intuition]] is not the **absence** of mind but mind's [[ego|self]]-overcoming.

### Mind vs. Thought

[[Thought]] is the content of mental [[action|act]]ivity — [[idea|ideas]], concepts, judgments. Mind is the faculty that produces and relates thoughts. Bergson's concern is less with specific thoughts than with the **form** that the mind imposes on all thought — the spatial schema.

## Historical Context

Bergson's theory of mind engages most deeply with **[[kant|Immanuel Kant]]**. [[kant|Kant]] argued that the mind imposes spatial and temporal forms on experience — space and time are the "forms of [[intuition]]" through which we receive appearances. Bergson accepts that the mind imposes **space** on experience, but he rejects [[kant|Kant]]'s claim that **time** is also a form imposed by the mind. For Bergson, time (as duration) is the **[[reality|real]]** nature of inner experience, which the mind then spatializes into [[time|measurable time]]. Where [[kant|Kant]] saw two forms, Bergson sees one (space) and one [[reality|real]]ity (duration).
