# Intuition

Intuition (*intuition*) is Bergson's term for the **direct grasp of [[reality|real]]ity** — the sympathetic insight into the continuous [[becoming|flux]] of becoming that concepts, by their nature, divide and immobilize.

It is both a **philosophical method** and a **cognitive faculty**: the means by which we know [[duration]] from within, rather than reconstructing it from without through spatial symbols.

## Bergson's Argument

### Intuition as Philosophical Method

Bergson's method is announced from the outset as an alternative to the dominant conceptual tradition:

> For him reality is not to be reached by any elaborate construction of thought: it is given in immediate experience as a flux, a continuous process of becoming, to be grasped by intuition, by sympathetic insight. Concepts break up the continuous flow of reality into parts external to one another… [P01-006]

Concepts serve pr[[action|act]]ical life and social communication — they carve the world into manageable pieces. But they give us "nothing of the life and [[motion|movement]] of [[reality|real]]ity," substituting an artificial reconstruction of dead fragments for the living whole. Intuition is the effort to **enter into** the [[motion|movement]] rather than standing outside it.

### Intuition of Space and the Two Multiplicities

Bergson identifies an "intuition of space" that underlies [[quantity|quantitative]] [[thought|thinking]]:

> …quantitative or discrete multiplicity involves the intuition of space, but the multiplicity of conscious states is wholly qualitative. [P01-007]

There are thus **two kinds of intuition** at work in Bergson's analysis:

| Mode | Object | Char[[action|act]]er |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Intuition of space | Homogeneous medium, discrete units | Enables [[number|counting]], [[rhythm|measure]]ment, separation |
| Intuition of duration | Qualitative multiplicity, interpenetration | Grasps the continuous, the heterogeneous, the living |

The "intuition of space" is not Bergson's preferred mode — it is the [[intelligence|intellect]]ual habit that spatializes everything. True philosophical intuition turns toward the [[quality|qualitative]] [[becoming|flux]] of [[consciousness]] it[[ego|self]].

### The Simple Intuition of the Mind

In his analysis of number, Bergson describes how the mind grasps a multiplicity as a unity:

> …we master the whole of it by a simple and indivisible intuition of the mind; this unity thus includes a multiplicity, since it is the unity of a whole. [003-080]

> Every number is one, since it is brought before the mind by a simple intuition and is given a name… [003-067]

The simple intuition is the [[action|act]] by which the mind **synthesizes** a [[multiplicity|manifold]] into a single grasp. But this same [[action|act]], when applied to [[consciousness|conscious states]], is misleading: it treats the [[quality|qualitative]] interpenetration of [[duration]] as if it were a collection of discrete units waiting to be counted.

### The Intuition of Homogeneous Medium

Bergson identifies the fundamental spatial [[action|act]] as the intuition of an empty [[homogeneity|homogeneous medium]]:

> …it consists essentially in the intuition, or rather the conception, of an empty homogeneous medium. For it is scarcely possible to give any other definition of space: space is what enables us to distinguish a number of identical and simultaneous sensations from one another; it is thus a principle of differentiation other than that of qualitative differentiation… [003-098]

This intuition of [[homogeneity|homogeneous space]] is "perhaps peculiar to man and not shared by animals." It is the cognitive foundation of mathematics, [[rhythm|measure]]ment, and all [[quantity|quantitative]] [[thought|thinking]] — but it is also the source of the systematic distortion of psychic life.

### Intuition vs. Analysis

The contrast between intuition and analysis runs through Bergson's entire philosophy:

| Aspect | Intuition | [[analysis|Analysis]] |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Relation to object | Enters into it, sympathizes | Stands outside, decomposes |
| Product | The whole, grasped in its [[motion|movement]] | Parts, external to one another |
| Temporality | Respects duration and becoming | Freezes into instants and positions |
| Use | Philosophical knowledge of [[reality|the real]] | Pr[[action|act]]ical [[action|act]]ion and science |

[[analysis|Analysis]] is not wrong — it is **useful** for pr[[action|act]]ical purposes. But it cannot give us knowledge of [[reality|real]]ity because it substitutes spatial symbols for temporal becoming. The mistake of traditional metaphysics is to take the products of analysis for [[reality|the real]] it[[ego|self]].

## Key Distinctions

### Intuition and Consciousness

Intuition is not a special mystical faculty; it is **consciousness attending to it[[ego|self]]** without the intermediary of spatial symbols. When we cease to project our psychic states onto a [[homogeneity|homogeneous medium]] and attend to them as they are given — interpenetrating, [[quality|qualitative]], continuously transforming — we are intuiting.

### Intuition and Duration

[[Duration]] (*[[duration|durée]]*) is what intuition grasps. Duration is the form of conscious life: continuous, [[quality|qualitative]], heterogeneous. Concepts spatialize duration into a linear succession of discrete instants; intuition recovers the original interpenetration.

### Intuition and the Confused Intuition of Space

Much of Bergson's critical work consists in showing that what philosophers have called "intuition" is often the **confused intuition of space** — the implicit projection of spatial relations onto non-spatial [[reality|real]]ities:

> …the confused intuition of a relation between two extensive magnitudes… [001-013]

When we say one pain is "greater" than another, we are relying on a confused spatial intuition that places pains side by side for comparison. True intuition would reveal that they are different qualities, not different quantities.
