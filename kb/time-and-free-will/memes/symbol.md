# Symbol

## Overview / Definition

In *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]*, Bergson uses "symbol" (and "symbolic/symbolical representation") to denote the **spatial substitute** that consciousness employs when it tries to represent its own inner states. Instead of grasping duration directly, the [[intelligence|intellect]] replaces it with a material, homogeneous, spatial image — a symbol. This substitution is not merely a convenience; it fundamentally distorts the nature of what is being represented [003-077].

Bergson writes of "getting rid of the clumsy symbols round which we are fighting" [P02-001], positioning symbolic representation as the primary obstacle to an authentic [[intuition]] of duration. The symbol is "necessary for reckoning" [003-077] but it is not the thing it[[ego|self]] — it is a "symbolical interpretation of quality" [003-018] that reduces the [[quality|qualitative]] to the [[quantity|quantitative]].

## Bergson's Argument

Bergson's argument about symbols unfolds across three interrelated claims:

**Symbols are spatial.** When we attempt to represent psychic states, "we kept only the symbol which is necessary for reckoning" [003-077]. This symbol is invariably spatial — a line, a point, a [[homogeneity|homogeneous medium]] in which states can be laid side by side. "Our states are not countable unless symbolically represented in space" [003-090], because [[number|counting]] requires juxtaposition, and juxtaposition requires space.

**Symbols distort duration.** The [[action|act]] of symbolic representation transforms succession into simultaneity. "[[succession|Succession]] cannot be symbolized as a line without introducing the idea of space" [003-126]. The symbol of duration — a line — turns a [[interpenetration|qualitative multiplicity]] into a [[quantity|quantitative]] one, losing the interpenetration that char[[action|act]]erizes [[duration|real duration]] [003-132].

**Symbols are necessary yet misleading.** Bergson acknowledges that "some [[becoming|process]] of symbolical representation is necessary" [003-093] for pr[[action|act]]ical life and science. Without symbols, "our states are not countable" [001-311]. Yet this necessity is also the source of fundamental philosophical illusions: we mistake the symbol for [[reality|the real]]ity, the spatial representation for the temporal experience [003-102].

## Key Distinctions

| Aspect | Symbolic Representation | Direct [[intuition|Intuition]] |
|--------|------------------------|-------------------|
| Medium | Spatial, homogeneous | Temporal, heterogeneous |
| [[multiplicity|Multiplicity]] | Quantitative (juxtaposition) | Qualitative (interpenetration) |
| Access | Intellectual, reflective | Immediate, pre-reflective |
| Result | Distortion of duration | Grasp of duration it[[ego|self]] |

The "symbolical image of duration" [001-325] is precisely what Bergson wants us to see through — to recognize as an image rather than [[reality|the real]]ity.
