# Intelligence

Intelligence (*l'intelligence*), in Bergson's *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]*, is the **spatializing faculty** — the power of the mind to break continuous [[reality|real]]ity into discrete, analyzable parts. Intelligence is not, for Bergson, the whole of mind; it is a **pr[[action|act]]ical instrument** developed for [[action|act]]ion on [[matter]]. Its native domain is the homogeneous, the discontinuous, and the static. When intelligence turns inward and tries to grasp [[consciousness]] and [[duration]], it inevitably spatializes them — substituting symbols for [[reality|real]]ity.

## Bergson's Argument

### The Domain of Intelligence

> [003-196] …the organized and living intelligence, we shall witness the joining…

Bergson speaks of "the organized and living intelligence" — intelligence is not a cold logical machine but an **evolved capacity** embedded in the life of the [[body|organism]]. It has developed to meet pr[[action|act]]ical needs, to manipulate the material world, and to enable social coordination through [[language]]. Its limitations are not failures but its **constitution**: it is made for matter, not for mind.

> [003-309] …future antecedents, some higher intelligence would not be able to predict…

Even "some higher intelligence" — an intellect vastly superior to our own — "would not be able to predict" the free [[action|act]]. This is a decisive argument: prediction is not limited by the **degree** of intelligence but by its **nature**. Intelligence predicts by calculation from antecedents; but the free [[action|act]] is not calculable because it is not composed of discrete antecedents. A perfect intelligence would still fail at predicting [[free-will|freedom]] because intelligence operates by spatialization, and [[free-will|freedom]] belongs to duration.

### Intelligence and Instinct

> [003-048] L'homme et l'intelligence, p. 36.

Bergson contrasts intelligence with **instinct**. Intelligence operates on inert matter through analysis and construction; [[instinct]] operates on living wholes through sympathy and organization. Intelligence fabricates tools; instinct inhabits [[becoming|process]]es. The two are not opposed as higher to lower but as **different modes of engagement** — intelligence for the inorganic, instinct for the organic. The error is to treat intelligence as the only valid mode of knowing.

### The Geometrical Intelligence

> [004-034] A propos de l'évolution de l'intelligence géométrique…

Bergson traces "the evolution of geometrical intelligence" — the spatializing tendency that culminates in modern science and mathematics. Geometrical intelligence reduces the [[quality|qualitative]] to the [[quantity|quantitative]], the temporal to the spatial. It is enormously powerful for physics and engineering, but when applied to consciousness, it generates insoluble pseudo-problems — including the problem of [[freedom]].

### Intelligence, Analysis, and Synthesis

Intelligence proceeds by [[analysis]]: it breaks wholes into parts and reconstructs them from the parts. This method works perfectly for matter (which is genuinely divisible) but fails for consciousness (whose parts are abstr[[action|act]]ions). [[Intuition]], by contrast, proceeds by entering into the whole without decomposing it. Bergson does not reject intelligence but insists on its **jurisdictional limits** — intelligence is a tool, not the tribunal of all truth.

| Intelligence Char[[action|act]]eristic | Domain It Serves | Limitation |
|---------------------------|------------------|------------|
| Spatial analysis | [[matter|Matter]], physics, engineering | Cannot grasp duration |
| Discrete decomposition | Pr[[action|act]]ical [[action|act]]ion | Falsifies conscious continuity |
| Calculation from antecedents | Prediction in physical systems | Cannot predict free [[action|act]]s |
| Geometrical abstr[[action|act]]ion | Mathematics, science | Reduces quality to quantity |
| [[symbol|Symbol]]ic representation | [[language|Language]], social life | Substitutes symbol for [[reality|real]]ity |

## Key Distinctions

### Intelligence vs. Intuition

[[Intuition]] is the **supplement** to intelligence — the faculty that enters into duration without spatializing it. Intelligence grasps from without; [[intuition]] sympathizes from within. Bergson's philosophy is not anti-intellectual; it is a philosophy of **intellectual modesty** — recognizing what intelligence cannot do and turning to [[intuition]] for what intelligence distorts.

### Intelligence and Duration

Intelligence is constitutionally incapable of grasping [[duration]] because it operates by **arresting** flow — it must stop [[motion|movement]] to analyze it. But duration is pure [[motion|movement]]; to stop it is to lose it. The [[intuition]] of duration requires a different cognitive posture: not analysis but **sympathy**, not stopping but **entering into**.
