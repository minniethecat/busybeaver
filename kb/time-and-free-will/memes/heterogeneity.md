# Heterogeneity

Heterogeneity, in Bergson's *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]*, is the defining char[[action|act]]er of [[duration]] and conscious life. While [[homogeneity]] belongs to [[space]] — a medium in which parts are [[quality|qualitative]]ly identical and differ only in position — heterogeneity describes the **[[quality|qualitative]] diversity** of [[consciousness|conscious states]], where each moment is irreducibly unique and interpenetrates every other. Bergson's philosophy can be read as a sustained argument that [[reality|real]]ity is fundamentally heterogeneous and that philosophy's traditional errors arise from replacing this [[quality|qualitative]] diversity with spatial homogeneity.

## Bergson's Argument

### Heterogeneity as the Mark of the Qualitative

For Bergson, the [[quality|qualitative]] is the heterogeneous. A [[sensation]], an [[feeling|emotion]], a memory — each is not an instance of a general type but a **unique quality** with its own singular char[[action|act]]er:

> [001-149] …heterogeneity of, precludes return to former…

Unlike objects in space, which can be identical (two meters of string are the same thing), [[consciousness|conscious states]] are never identical. Each moment of experience bears the color of the entire past; it is what it is only because of everything that preceded it.

> [001-193] …assumed behind heterogeneity…

The [[intelligence|intellect]] habitually assumes a homogeneous substrate behind the heterogeneity of appearances. But Bergson insists that this assumption is unwarranted: heterogeneity is not an appearance covering a homogeneous [[reality|real]]ity; it is **[[reality|real]]ity it[[ego|self]]**.

### Heterogeneity vs. Homogeneity in Duration

The contrast between heterogeneity and homogeneity is the contrast between duration and space:

> [003-111] …what is given it as qualitative heterogeneity…

The [[intelligence|intellect]], shaped by pr[[action|act]]ical needs, takes the qualitative heterogeneity of immediate experience and translates it into [[homogeneity|extensive homogeneity]]. We replace the unique flavor of each moment with a position on a timeline. Bergson's method is to **reverse** this translation — to recover the original heterogeneity beneath the spatial schema.

> [003-112] …kind of reaction against that heterogeneity which is the very ground of our…

Our entire [[intelligence|intellect]]ual apparatus — language, mathematics, science — is a re[[action|act]]ion against heterogeneity, an attempt to domesticate it into manageable homogeneity. But this domestication comes at a price: it falsifies the nature of conscious life.

| Realm | Char[[action|act]]er | Parts | Relation | Knowledge |
|-------|----------|-------|----------|-----------|
| [[space|Space]] | Homogeneous | Identical, interchangeable | External (juxtaposition) | [[analysis|Analysis]] ([[intelligence|intellect]]) |
| Duration | Heterogeneous | Unique, unrepeatable | Internal (interpenetration) | [[intuition|Intuition]] |

### Pure Heterogeneity and Number

> [003-129] …with number: it would be pure heterogeneity.

[[number|Number]] requires homogeneity: to count, we must treat the counted items as identical units. Pure heterogeneity — the [[quality|qualitative]] diversity of duration — cannot be counted. This is why the attempt to [[rhythm|measure]] [[consciousness|conscious states]] (as in [[psychophysics]]) is fundamentally misguided.

> [003-167] …this distinctness, this heterogeneity contains number only potentially…

Heterogeneity contains number only potentially — that is, only after the [[intelligence|intellect]] has spatialized it. In its immediate [[reality|real]]ity, a heterogeneous multiplicity is not a sum of units but an **[[organic|organic whole]]** in which each part bears the mark of the totality.

## Key Distinctions

### Heterogeneity vs. Homogeneity

Heterogeneity is the principle of duration and consciousness; [[homogeneity]] is the principle of space and matter. The two are not complementary aspects of a single [[reality|real]]ity but **opposed** principles. Bergson's central argument is that philosophy must think the heterogeneous without reducing it to the homogeneous — difference in **kind** rather than difference in **degree**.

### Heterogeneity vs. Difference

Not all difference is heterogeneity. In a [[homogeneity|homogeneous medium]], parts differ only in **position** — they are different instances of the identical. Heterogeneity is **[[quality|qualitative]]** difference: difference in nature, not merely in location. The taste of coffee differs from the sound of a violin not in degree (more/less) but in kind.

### Heterogeneity and the Self

The [[ego|self]] in duration is a heterogeneous [[multiplicity]]: its states interpenetrate without losing their [[quality|qualitative]] uniqueness. This is Bergson's alternative to both the associationist [[ego|self]] (a collection of homogeneous atoms) and the [[kant|Kant]]ian [[ego|self]] (a formal unity imposed on a [[multiplicity|manifold]]).

> [003-365] …assuming, behind the heterogeneity of our sensations, a homogeneous…

The assumption of a homogeneous substrate behind heterogeneous [[sensation]]s is the **[[intelligence|intellect]]ualist fallacy** Bergson attributes to [[Kant]] and much of the philosophical tradition.

## Historical Context

### Bergson's Reaction to Associationism

The [[association|associationist psychology]] of [[Mill]], [[Spencer]], and others treated the mind as composed of discrete, homogeneous elements linked by [[association|laws of association]]. Bergson's emphasis on heterogeneity is a direct challenge to this picture: mental elements are never homogeneous atoms; they are always already permeated by the whole of the [[ego|self]].

### The Kantian Background

[[Kant]]'s transcendental aesthetic treats space and time as homogeneous forms of [[intuition]]. Bergson accepts that this describes the **[[intelligence|intellect]]ual representation** of experience, but he argues that it is not the original datum. The immediate experience of duration is heterogeneous; homogeneity is added by the [[intelligence|intellect]].
