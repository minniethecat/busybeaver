# Matter

In Bergson's *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]*, matter is not an independent substance but the **logical correlate** of the spatializing mind. When we strip concrete experience of its [[quality|qualitative difference]]s — its colors, sounds, textures, and felt intensities — what remains is homogeneous [[extensity|extension]]: matter. Matter is thus the **product** of abstr[[action|act]]ion, not a [[reality|real]]ity given in experience. Bergson's theory of matter is the bridge between his critique of [[quantity|quantitative]] psychology (Chapter 1) and his positive account of [[freedom]] (Chapter 3).

## Bergson's Argument

### Matter as the Product of Abstraction

> [001-060] If we consider the matter carefully, we shall see that…

Bergson's strategy is not to deny the existence of matter but to ask: what **kind** of existence does matter have? His answer is that matter is the concept we form when we systematically ignore the [[quality|qualitative]] dimension of experience. A piece of wax, to use [[descartes|Descartes]]' example, loses its scent, its color, its texture under heat — and we say that what remains is "matter." But what remains is precisely what is **homogeneous** — pure [[extension|spatial extension]] without quality:

> [001-146] …Kant's distinction between matter and form of consciousness…

[[kant|Kant]] distinguished between the **matter** of experience (the sensory content given in [[intuition]]) and the **form** of experience (the spatial-temporal framework imposed by the mind). Bergson agrees that matter, in [[kant|Kant]]'s sense, is what is given — but he denies that the matter of experience is it[[ego|self]] spatial. Spatiality is the **form** the mind imposes; the matter of experience (the [[quality|qualitative]] [[becoming|flux]]) is temporal — it is duration.

### Matter and Molecular Theory

> [001-143] …molecular theory of matter…

Nineteenth-century physics treated matter as composed of discrete molecules moving in space. Bergson does not contest the **utility** of this theory — it is enormously successful for prediction and control. But he insists that the molecular theory is a **representation** — a spatial model — not a description of [[reality|real]]ity as it is. The molecular theory treats matter as homogeneous particles in [[homogeneity|homogeneous space]]; but [[reality|real]] experience is never homogeneous. The [[heterogeneity|qualitative heterogeneity]] of experience is prior to the homogeneous matter of physics.

| Conception of Matter | Basis | Status |
|---------------------|-------|--------|
| Common sense | Solid, colored, textured things | Mixture of quality and abstr[[action|act]]ion |
| Physics (molecular) | Homogeneous particles in space | Useful representation, not [[reality|real]]ity |
| Bergson | What remains after abstr[[action|act]]ion | Logical product, not primitive given |

### Matter and Form

> [001-178] …Kant's distinction between matter and…

Following [[kant|Kant]], Bergson recognizes that the distinction between matter and form is fundamental to epistemology. But he reverses [[kant|Kant]]'s priority: for [[kant|Kant]], the form (space and time as a priori [[intuition]]s) is what the mind contributes; for Bergson, space is the form the mind imposes, but duration is not a form — it is the **[[reality|real]]** char[[action|act]]er of inner experience. Matter is thus doubly abstr[[action|act]]: it is what remains when we abstr[[action|act]] from quality, and it is what we get when we spatialize duration.

## Key Distinctions

### Matter vs. Body

The [[body]] is the lived, [[quality|qualitative]] object — my body as I experience it, with its pains, pleasures, and felt capacities. Matter is the body **despatialized** (or rather, spatialized in a different way) — the body as an object among objects, a configuration of homogeneous substance in [[homogeneity|homogeneous space]].

### Matter vs. Extension

[[Extension]] is the spatial char[[action|act]]er of experience — the f[[action|act]] that experience presents it[[ego|self]] as spread out in space. Matter is [[extensity|extension]] stripped of all [[quality|qualitative]] determination. [[extension|Extension]] is a feature of experience; matter is an abstr[[action|act]]ion from it.

### Matter vs. Quality

[[Quality]] is the concrete, lived char[[action|act]]er of experience — the redness of red, the warmth of warmth. Matter is quality **negated** — the residue when all qualities are removed. Bergson's point is that this residue is not more [[reality|real]] than quality; it is less [[reality|real]]. It is a useful fiction for physics, not a description of what is.

## Historical Context

Bergson's theory of matter engages with **[[descartes|Descartes]]'** dualism of mind and extended substance, **[[kant|Kant]]'s** matter-form distinction in the *Critique of Pure [[reason|Reason]]*, and **Lord Kelvin's** molecular theory of matter (which treated atoms as vortices in a homogeneous ether). [[descartes|Descartes]] made matter into a substance independent of mind; [[kant|Kant]] made matter into the sensory given formed by the mind's a priori [[intuition]]s; Bergson makes matter into the product of the mind's spatializing [[action|act]]ivity — not an illusion, but an abstr[[action|act]]ion whose **origin** we must understand if we are not to mistake it for [[reality|real]]ity.
