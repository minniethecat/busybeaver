# Dynamic

The dynamic (*le dynamique*), in Bergson's *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]*, names the **[[quality|qualitative]] mode of [[reality|real]]ity** that belongs to [[duration]] and [[consciousness]]. It is not a property that things possess but a **way of being** — the ongoing, irreversible [[becoming|process]] of becoming that char[[action|act]]erizes conscious life. The dynamic is Bergson's answer to the [[mechanical]]: where [[mechanical|mechanism]] freezes [[reality|real]]ity into static, spatial elements, the dynamic restores the **[[motion|movement]]**, the **progress**, the lived **tension** that is [[reality|the real]] texture of experience.

## Bergson's Argument

### The Dynamic Way of Looking at Things

Bergson introduces the dynamic as a methodological orientation:

> [001-028] But this wholly dynamic way of looking at things is repugnant to the reflective consciousness, because the latter delights in clean cut distinctions, which are easily expressed in words.

The "wholly dynamic way of looking at things" is "repugnant to the [[consciousness|reflective consciousness]]" — it resists the [[intelligence|intellect]]'s preference for "clean cut distinctions." The dynamic does not offer discrete elements to analyze; it presents a **continuous flow** that demands to be felt rather than dissected. The [[consciousness|reflective consciousness]], trained by [[language]] and pr[[action|act]]ical life, recoils from this fluidity and substitutes static, spatial representations.

### Inner Relations as Dynamic

Bergson contrasts the dynamic char[[action|act]]er of inner relations with the mathematical char[[action|act]]er of external relations:

> The relation of inner states is dynamic, not mathematical — it is a relation of qualitative interpenetration, not of quantitative equivalence.

Inner relations — the way one conscious state relates to another — are "dynamic." They cannot be expressed as equations or proportions because they involve **[[quality|qualitative]] transformation**: each new state modifies the whole, and the modified whole changes the quality of each state. This is the logic of [[interpenetration]], not of addition.

### The Dynamic Process in the Depths of the Soul

The dynamic is most vividly present in the [[action|act]] of free [[choice|decision]]:

> [003-169] …an organization of these units is going on in the depths of the soul, a wholly dynamic process, not unlike the purely qualitative way in which an anvil, if it could feel, would realize a series of blows from a hammer.

"An organization... is going on in the depths of the soul, a wholly dynamic process." The dynamic is not a static structure but an **ongoing organization** — an [[action|act]]ivity, not a state. Bergson's striking image — the anvil that "could feel" the blows of the hammer — captures the idea: the dynamic is not the measurable force of each blow but the **felt accumulation**, the [[quality|qualitative]] way each blow is absorbed into and transformed by the whole.

### The Dynamic Series of States

A free [[action|act]] emerges from a dynamic series:

> [003-278] A dynamic series of states is thus formed which permeate and strengthen one another, and which will lead by a natural evolution to a free act.

A "dynamic series of states" is not a sequence of discrete items but a **progressive interpenetration** — states "permeate and strengthen one another" and lead "by a [[evolution|natural evolution]] to a free [[action|act]]." The word "evolution" is crucial: the dynamic series is a genuine **development**, not a mechanical unfolding of predetermined elements. Each phase is [[quality|qualitative]]ly new.

### Dynamic Progress vs. Static Symbol

Bergson repeatedly contrasts the dynamic progress of consciousness with the static symbols that represent it:

> [003-300] But the figure merely gives the stereotyped memory of the process, and not the dynamic progress which issued in the act.

The "figure" — the spatial diagram of deliberation as an oscillation between alternatives — gives only "the stereotyped memory of the [[becoming|process]]," not "the dynamic progress which issued in the [[action|act]]." The dynamic progress is **what [[action|act]]ually happened**; the figure is a spatialized reconstruction that loses the [[quality|qualitative]] [[motion|movement]].

> [003-302] …how could it give us the least idea of the concrete movement, the dynamic progress by which the deliberation issued in the act?

The "concrete [[motion|movement]]" and the "dynamic progress" are one and the same — the lived, temporal [[becoming|process]] of deciding. Once we replace this with a spatial diagram, we lose what is essential: the **becoming** of the [[choice|decision]].

> [003-318] The second consists in substituting for the concrete reality or dynamic progress, which consciousness perceives, the material symbol of this progress when it has already reached its end…

The "second fundamental illusion of the [[consciousness|reflective consciousness]]" is "substituting for the concrete [[reality|real]]ity or dynamic progress... the material symbol of this progress." The dynamic progress is the **living [[becoming|process]]**; the material symbol is the **dead result**, projected backward in time as if it had been the cause.

### The Dynamic Conception of Causality

Bergson proposes a dynamic alternative to mechanical [[causality|causation]]:

> [003-388] …the dynamic conception of the causal relation ascribes to things a duration absolutely like our own, whatever may be the nature of this duration.

The "dynamic conception of the causal relation" — as opposed to the mathematical or [[mechanical|mechanical conception]] — "ascribes to things a duration absolutely like our own." This is not a theory of [[causality|causation]] but a **return to experience**: we understand [[causality|causation]] because we **live** it, in the effort by which our own dynamic progress issues in [[action|act]]ion. The dynamic relation is the prototype; mechanical [[causality|causation]] is its spatialized abstr[[action|act]]ion.

## Key Distinctions

### Dynamic vs. Mechanical

The [[mechanical]] reduces [[reality|real]]ity to static, spatial elements related by external determination. The dynamic restores the **internal relation** — the way each element is constituted by its place in a [[quality|qualitative]] whole. Mechanism explains by breaking down; the dynamic understands by **following** the [[quality|qualitative]] progress.

### Dynamic vs. Static

The static is the **product** — the [[action|act]] accomplished, the [[choice|decision]] made, the [[emotion|feeling]] named. The dynamic is the **production** — the [[action|act]]ing, the deciding, the [[emotion|feeling]] in its living mobility. Bergson's philosophy is an extended argument that philosophy and science have systematically mistaken the static for the dynamic, the product for the [[becoming|process]].

### Dynamic and Duration

[[Duration]] is the medium of the dynamic — the [[quality|qualitative]], temporal field in which dynamic progress occurs. To say that [[reality|real]]ity is dynamic is to say that it is **durational**: its being is becoming, its essence is temporal, its logic is [[quality|qualitative]] transformation rather than [[quantity|quantitative]] rearrangement.
