# Reflection

Reflection (*réflexion*), in Bergson's *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]*, is the **philosophical method** by which we suspend the spatial habits that obscure [[duration]] and return to immediate conscious experience. It is not abstr[[action|act]] contemplation or logical analysis but a **strenuous effort** — an [[action|act]] of [[attention]] that turns away from the pr[[action|act]]ical demands of life and the spatial schemas of language to grasp consciousness as it is given. Reflection is the antidote to habit, the discipline that makes [[intuition]] possible.

## Bergson's Argument

### The Strenuous Effort of Reflection

The culminating passage of *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]* names reflection as the [[action|act]] that opens access to [[duration|real duration]]:

> [003-450] …ver, by a strenuous effort of reflection, we turn our eyes from the sh…

The "strenuous effort of reflection" is Bergson's description of what philosophy requires: we must "turn our eyes from the shadow" — the spatial representation — to [[reality|the real]]ity — the temporal流动 it[[ego|self]]. This turning is not easy; it is a **strenuous effort** because every habit of [[mind|thought]], every structure of language, every demand of pr[[action|act]]ical life pulls us back toward spatialization.

### Reflection Reveals Quality, Not Quantity

> [003-013] …transition is a quantity, but reflection will show you that it is not…

Bergson repeatedly uses reflection as a **corrective**: common sense or science says X is a quantity (a measurable transition, a comparable intensity), but "reflection will show you" that it is in f[[action|act]] a quality. Reflection is the operation that **undoes** the [[quantity|quantitative]] misinterpretation and restores the [[quality|qualitative]] nature of the phenomenon.

> [001-071] …strength: here again further reflection will show you that this sensa…

In the analysis of [[intensity]], "further reflection" reveals that what we took to be a [[quantity|measurable magnitude]] is in f[[action|act]] a [[quality|qualitative]] state. Reflection is the method of Chapter I: take each apparent quantity, subject it to reflective scrutiny, and discover that it dissolves into [[interpenetration|qualitative multiplicity]].

### Reflection and the Colouring of the Self

> [003-414] …the colouring of the self the reflection of the frame in which we place…

This dense passage describes the relationship between the [[ego|self]] and the external world. The "colouring of the [[ego|self]]" — the [[quality|qualitative]] tone that permeates all our [[consciousness|conscious states]] — is "the reflection of the frame in which we place" our experiences. Reflection here has a double meaning: it is both the **philosophical [[action|act]]** by which we grasp this colouring and the **mirroring relation** by which the [[ego|self]] takes on the color of its circumstances without being determined by them.

### The Reflected Light of Other States

> [003-266] …hich comes to each of them by reflection from all the others, then the…

In [[duration]], each conscious state receives "by reflection" the light of all the others — each state is permeated by the whole of the [[ego|self]]'s history. This is not a causal relation but a **reflective** one: the past illuminates the present without determining it. The metaphor of light and reflection is Bergson's alternative to the mechanical metaphor of cause and effect.

### Reflection as Philosophical Discipline

Reflection is not a one-time [[action|act]] but a **discipline** — a pr[[action|act]]ice that must be sustained. Every page of *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]* en[[action|act]]s this discipline: Bergson takes a concept that seems clear (intensity, number, time, causality), subjects it to reflection, and shows that it dissolves into spatial con[[interpenetration|fusion]]. The book is a training manual in philosophical reflection.

## Key Distinctions

### Reflection vs. Analysis

[[Analysis]] breaks a phenomenon into parts; reflection **recovers the whole** from which analysis started. [[analysis|Analysis]] moves from the concrete to the abstr[[action|act]]; reflection moves from the abstr[[action|act]] (spatial concepts) back to the concrete (duration). [[analysis|Analysis]] is the natural operation of the [[intelligence|intellect]]; reflection is the artificial, strenuous effort of philosophy.

### Reflection vs. Judgment

[[Judgment]] compares and evaluates; reflection **suspends** comparison and evaluation to attend to the immediate. [[judgment|Judgment]] is always judgment of something as something — it imports cat[[self|ego]]ries. Reflection aims at a pre-cat[[self|ego]]rial grasp of experience. When Bergson says "reflection will show you," he means that reflection strips away the cat[[self|ego]]ries that judgment has imposed.

### Reflection vs. Intuition

[[Intuition]] is the **goal**; reflection is the **method**. We cannot simply intuit duration on command — the spatial habits are too strong. Reflection is the disciplined pr[[action|act]]ice that makes [[intuition]] possible: by repeatedly turning away from spatial representations, we prepare the mind for the direct grasp of duration. In later works (especially *Introduction to [[metaphysics|Metaphysics]]*), Bergson will make [[intuition]] the central concept; in *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]*, reflection is the bridge to it.

### Reflection and the Spatializing Tendency

The [[tendency]] to spatialize is constitutional — it belongs to the nature of mind. Reflection is the **counter-tendency** — the deliberate, effortful resistance to spatialization. This is why Bergson calls it "strenuous": it goes against the grain of our cognitive nature and the habits of daily life.
