# Quantity

Quantity, in Bergson's *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]*, is the cat[[self|ego]]ry of the **extended and measurable** — the domain where differences are differences of **more and less** along a homogeneous scale. Bergson's argument is not that quantity is un[[reality|real]], but that its domain is strictly limited to [[space]] and the material world. When we speak of a "greater" [[sensation]], a "stronger" desire, or a "larger" joy, we are projecting quantitative cat[[self|ego]]ries onto what is essentially [[quality]].

## Bergson's Argument

### The Proper Domain of Quantity

Quantity belongs to the external world — to objects distributed in [[space]]:

> [001-010] …distinction between two kinds of quantity: extensive and intensive magnitude…

Extensive quantities (length, area, volume) are directly measurable because their parts are **external to one another** in space. A meter contains ten decimeters; the parts are homogeneous and can be added. This is the legitimate domain of quantity.

> [001-011] …done, between two species of quantity, the first extensive and measurable…

But even within the cat[[self|ego]]ry of quantity, Bergson observes a critical distinction. Extensive quantity — the quantity of spatial objects — is clear and measurable. Intensive quantity — the supposed "quantity" of [[sensation]]s — is a **confused notion** that smuggles spatial properties into consciousness.

### The Illegitimate Extension to Consciousness

The central error Bergson diagnoses is the application of quantitative cat[[self|ego]]ries to [[consciousness|conscious states]]:

> [001-003] …thus setting up differences of quantity between purely internal states…

When we say one pain is "greater" than another, or one pleasure "more intense," we are using quantitative language for what is, in [[reality|real]]ity, a **[[change|qualitative change]]** in the whole of consciousness. A "more intense" [[sensation]] is not the same [[sensation]] plus something; it is a **different quality** of experience altogether.

> [001-012] …contains the other the greater quantity, why go on speaking of quantity…

If a larger space contains a smaller one, we can speak meaningfully of quantity. But [[consciousness|conscious states]] do not contain one another in space. When a stronger [[sensation]] replaces a weaker one, the original [[sensation]] has not been **added to**; it has been **transformed** into something [[quality|qualitative]]ly new.

### Quantity, Space, and Measurement

The possibility of quantity depends entirely on [[space]]:

> [001-020] …Why do we think of a greater quantity or a greater space?

We can [[rhythm|measure]] only what is homogeneous and extended — in short, what is spatial. Conscious states are neither homogeneous (each is unique) nor extended (they do not occupy space). Therefore, any attempt to [[rhythm|measure]] them quantitatively distorts their nature:

> [001-059] …consciousness under the form of quantity or at least of magnitude…

The [[intelligence|intellect]], shaped by the demands of [[action|act]]ion, habitually translates [[quality|qualitative difference]]s into quantitative ones because quantities can be **compared, calculated, and predicted**. This translation is pr[[action|act]]ically useful but philosophically misleading.

| Aspect | Quantity ([[space|Space]]) | [[quality|Quality]] (Consciousness) |
|--------|-----------------|------------------------|
| Nature | Homogeneous parts | Heterogeneous whole |
| Relation of parts | External (juxtaposed) | Internal (interpenetrating) |
| Measurement | Additive (1+1=2) | Non-additive ([[change|qualitative change]]) |
| [[change|Change]] | More or less of same | Transformation into something new |
| Domain | Material world | Conscious life |

### The Confusion of Quality and Quantity

The entire first chapter of *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]* is devoted to exposing the con[[interpenetration|fusion]] of [[quality]] with quantity:

> [P02-001] …the extended, of quality into quantity, has introduced contradiction…
> [P02-002] …simultaneity, of quality with quantity: this confusion once dispelled…

This con[[interpenetration|fusion]] is not accidental. It arises from the nature of **language** it[[ego|self]], which is built for the spatial world of distinct objects. Words separate what in consciousness is continuous; they make discrete what is interpenetrating. The very [[action|act]] of naming a [[sensation]] as "more intense" imposes a quantitative schema on a [[quality|qualitative]] [[reality|real]]ity.

## Key Distinctions

### Quantity vs. Quality

The quantity–quality distinction is fundamental to Bergson's entire project. Quantity admits of **degrees** along a homogeneous scale; quality involves **differences in kind**. A longer line is more of the same; a deeper blue is not "more blue" in the same sense — it is a different blue experience. Bergson argues that all so-called intensive quantities are [[action|act]]ually qualities:

> [001-071] …which is in truth a change of quantity; but, as your attention was called…

What we call an "increase" in intensity is [[action|act]]ually a **[[quality|qualitative]] transformation** of the whole conscious state, not the addition of more of the same.

### Quantity vs. Number

[[Number]] is the [[intelligence|intellect]]ual tool by which we handle quantity. But number it[[ego|self]], Bergson argues, presupposes [[space]]: to count is to juxtapose identical units in a [[homogeneity|homogeneous medium]]. The concept of number thus carries spatial assumptions into domains where they do not belong:

> [001-125] …compromise between quality and quantity…

When we count our "motives" or rank our "desires" by strength, we are applying a numerical-quantitative schema to what is essentially [[quality|qualitative]]. This is the error that makes determinism seem plausible.

### Quantity and Psychophysics

The [[psychophysics]] of [[Fechner]] and [[Weber]] claimed to establish a mathematical relationship between physical stimulus intensity and [[sensation]] intensity. Bergson argues that this whole project is built on the quantity–quality con[[interpenetration|fusion]]. A [[sensation]] has no measurable "quantity"; the psychophysical law [[rhythm|measure]]s only the **physical stimulus**, not the conscious state it[[ego|self]].

## Historical Context

### The Ancient Roots: Aristotle on Quantity

Aristotle distinguished between quantity (*poson*) and quality (*poion*) in his *Cat[[self|ego]]ries*, but he treated [[intensity|intensive magnitude]]s (like "more white") as a kind of quality, not as true quantity. Bergson revives and radicalizes this insight: [[intensity|intensive magnitude]] is not quantity at all; it is quality misdescribed.

### Descartes and the Mathematization of Nature

[[Descartes]]' program of reducing material nature to [[extensity|extension]] and motion made quantity the fundamental cat[[self|ego]]ry of physical science. Bergson does not oppose this within its proper domain (the material world), but he argues that [[descartes|Descartes]]' framework cannot be extended to consciousness without distortion.
