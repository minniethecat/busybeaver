# Interpenetration (interpénétration)

Interpenetration (*interpénétration*) is Bergson's term for the mutual pervasion of [[consciousness|conscious states]] in [[duration]]. Unlike objects in [[space]], which exist side by side in a relation of **juxtaposition**, [[consciousness|conscious states]] penetrate one another, each coloring and being colored by the whole. This concept is the logical endpoint of Bergson's analysis of [[intensity]] and the foundation of his theory of [[free-will]].

## Definition

Interpenetration char[[action|act]]erizes the mode of existence of [[consciousness|conscious states]] in duration. While spatial objects are external to one another (juxtaposed), [[consciousness|conscious states]] form an **[[organic|organic whole]]** where past, present, and future interpenetrate:

| Mode | Juxtaposition | Interpenetration |
|------|--------------|------------------|
| Domain | [[space|Space]] (external world) | Duration (consciousness) |
| Relation | External, separable | Internal, inseparable |
| [[multiplicity|Multiplicity]] | Quantitative (distinct units) | Qualitative (fused whole) |
| [[change|Change]] | Rearrangement of parts | Transformation of whole |
| Grasped by | [[analysis|Analysis]], [[rhythm|measure]]ment | [[intuition|Intuition]], immediate experience |

> (P01-007) …an interpenetration of elements so heterogeneous that…

This phrase from the editorial preface captures the essential idea: the elements of consciousness are not merely different from one another (heterogeneous) but also mutually pervasive (interpenetrating).

## Bergson's Usage

### Conscious States as Interpenetrating

Bergson's most important use of interpenetration is to describe the relationship among [[consciousness|conscious states]]. A [[emotion|feeling]], a memory, a perception — these are not separate atoms but moments of a single flowing whole:

> (001-149) …as interpenetration of conscious states…

> (001-197) …interpenetration of…

When we introspect honestly, we find that our states of [[consciousness]] do not follow one another like beads on a string. Each state permeates the others; the past lives on in the present, and the present is already colored by the anticipated future.

### Chapter III: The Free Act

In the final chapter, interpenetration becomes central to Bergson's argument for [[will|free will]]. The free [[action|act]] is one that expresses the **interpenetration** of the [[self|whole self]] — not a [[choice|decision]] among discrete options but the natural unfolding of a unified personality:

> (003-144) …a process of organization or interpenetration of conscious states is going on…

> (003-170) …without this interpenetration and this, so to speak, qualitative…

The determinist's error is to treat [[consciousness|conscious states]] as **juxtaposed** rather than interpenetrating. Once we recover [[reality|the real]]ity of interpenetration, the free [[action|act]] is seen not as a violation of causality but as the expression of the [[self|whole self]]:

> (003-190) …that interpenetration, which is the very essence of…

### The Vital Distinction

Bergson emphasizes that the distinction between juxtaposition and interpenetration is fundamental to his entire philosophy:

> (003-204) …of juxtaposition and that of interpenetration. Without this vital distinction…

Without grasping this distinction, we inevitably spatialize consciousness and fall into determinism. With it, we can recover the immediate data of consciousness and see [[free-will|freedom]] as a f[[action|act]] of experience.

## Key Distinctions

### Interpenetration vs. Juxtaposition

Juxtaposition is the spatial relation of external objects — they exist side by side, each external to the others. Interpenetration is the temporal relation of [[consciousness|conscious states]] — they exist **through** one another, each internal to the whole.

### Interpenetration vs. Fusion

Bergson sometimes uses "fusion" as a near-synonym, but interpenetration captures the key point: the states do not lose their distinctness (as in complete fusion) but rather pervade one another while retaining [[quality|qualitative difference]]s:

> (003-260) …and that of fusion or interpenetration. Such and such a feeling…

A [[emotion|feeling]] of joy is distinguishable from a [[emotion|feeling]] of hope, but they interpenetrate — the joy is hopeful, the hope is joyful.

### Interpenetration and Succession

Interpenetration transforms our [[mind|understanding]] of succession. [[succession|Succession]], in the spatial sense, implies one thing *after* another. But in duration, succession means interpenetration — the past enduring into the present:

> (003-433) …succession implies interpenetration, just as the pendulum of a clock…

### Interpenetration and the Self

The [[ego|self]], for Bergson, is the **continuity of interpenetration** — not a substance underlying states but the very [[becoming|process]] of states pervading one another:

> (003-437) …as they make up, by their interpenetration, the continuous evolution of…

> (003-445) …distinct ones in spite of their interpenetration.

The [[ego|self]] is not a thing; it is the interpenetration it[[ego|self]]. This is why the free [[action|act]] — the [[action|act]] that expresses the [[self|whole self]] — is possible.
