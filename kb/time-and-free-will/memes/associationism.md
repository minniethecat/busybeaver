# Associationism (associationnisme)

[[association|Association]]ism is the psychological theory that [[thought|mental life]] consists of discrete, atomistic elements — [[sensation]]s, images, [[idea|ideas]] — that combine through "[[association|laws of association]]" (contiguity, similarity, contrast). Bergson subjects this doctrine to sustained criticism throughout *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]*: [[association|associationism]], he argues, imposes a spatial framework on [[consciousness]] and is therefore constitutionally incapable of grasping [[duration|real duration]].

## Definition

[[association|Association]]ism treats [[consciousness|conscious states]] as **separable units** that can be decomposed and recomposed, like objects in [[space]]. Bergson's critique centers on the claim that [[consciousness|conscious states]] are never discrete entities but always interpenetrate one another in a [[multiplicity|qualitative multiplicity]]:

| Aspect | [[association|Association]]ism | Bergson's Critique |
|--------|---------------|-------------------|
| Basic unit | Atomistic [[sensation]]s/[[idea|ideas]] | Interpenetrating states |
| Combining principle | Laws of association (contiguity, similarity) | [[organic|Organic]] [[interpenetration|fusion]], not external linkage |
| Model | Spatial juxtaposition of objects | Musical [[melody]] — notes merge into a whole |
| Conception of the [[ego|self]] | A theatre where discrete states pass | A living duration whose states permeate one another |
| Result | Cannot explain [[free-will|free will]] or deep [[feeling|emotion]] | [[Consciousness]] is a continuous becoming |

## Bergson's Usage

### The Fundamental Confusion

Bergson argues in Chapter I that [[association|associationism]]'s root error is its **con[[interpenetration|fusion]] of two kinds of multiplicity**:

> (001-241) …associationism confuses the two kinds of, [173](…

The first kind is the [[quantity|quantitative]] multiplicity of objects in [[space]] — homogeneous, discrete, countable. The second is the [[quality|qualitative]] multiplicity of [[consciousness]] — heterogeneous, interpenetrating, unmeasurable. [[association|Association]]ism models the [[ego|self]] on the first kind of multiplicity, treating [[consciousness|conscious states]] as if they were objects laid out side by side.

### The Spatial Prejudice

The deeper problem, Bergson contends, is that [[association|associationism]] inherits a **spatial prejudice** built into language and [[thought|reflective thought]]:

> (001-197) …associationism fits superficial, [135](…

[[language|Language]] — with its discrete [[language|words]] and fixed cat[[self|ego]]ries — encou[[anger|rage]]s us to separate and spatialize what is [[action|act]]ually a continuous [[becoming|flux]]. [[association|Association]]ism, by building a psychology on the model of language (associating discrete "[[idea|ideas]]" as if they were [[language|words]] in a sentence), merely systematizes this everyday error.

### Associationism and Determinism

In Chapter III, Bergson links [[association|associationism]] to [[determinism]]:

> (003-192) …bly fall into the mistakes of associationism. We shall not insist on the l…

[[association|Association]]ism provides the psychological underpinning for determinism: if [[consciousness|conscious states]] are discrete atoms governed by [[association|laws of association]], then every successive state is mechanically determined by its predecessors. The [[free-will|free act]], which requires the whole [[ego|self]] to express it[[ego|self]] in a single undivided gesture, becomes impossible on associationist premises.

### The Inadequacy of Associationist Explanation

Bergson catalogs specific failures of [[association|associationism]]:

> (003-250) …adopting the point of view of associationism, it is difficult to maintain …

> (003-255) …position in the same way; and associationism would have distinguished the …

The doctrine cannot account for [[emotion|deep emotions]] that permeate the [[personality|whole personality]], for aesthetic [[emotion|feeling]], or for the [[free-will|experience of freedom]] — precisely because these phenomena involve the interpenetration of states that [[association|associationism]]'s atomistic model denies.

## Key Distinctions

### Associationism vs. Duration

The fundamental contrast. [[association|Association]]ism treats mental states as discrete, separable, and externally related; [[duration]] treats them as interpenetrating, fused, and organically related. The former models consciousness on [[space]]; the latter on [[time|real time]].

### Associationism vs. Psychophysics

Both are reductionist doctrines that Bergson criticizes, but from different angles. [[psychophysics|Psychophysics]] attempts to [[rhythm|measure]] [[consciousness|conscious states]] by their physical correlates, while [[association|associationism]] atomizes them into discrete units. Both commit the same root error: treating consciousness as a [[quantity|quantitative]] rather than [[quality|qualitative]] [[reality|real]]ity. See also [[fechner|Fechner's Law]] and the [[illusion]] of [[rhythm|measure]]ment.

### Associationism vs. Mechanism

[[mechanism|Mechanism]] is the metaphysical counterpart of [[association|associationism]] in psychology. As [[mechanical|mechanism]] treats the [[matter|physical world]] as composed of discrete parts governed by external laws, [[association|associationism]] treats the mental world the same way. Bergson's [[dynamism]] opposes [[mechanical|mechanism]]; his philosophy of [[duration]] opposes [[association|associationism]].
