# Aesthetic Feeling

Aesthetic [[emotion|feeling]] (*[[feeling|sentiment]] esthétique*) in Bergson's *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]* is the **paradigm case** of [[quality|qualitative]] psychic transformation. Unlike [[sensation]]s tied to external objects, [[feeling|aesthetic feeling]]s seem purely internal — "[[ego|self]]-sufficient" states of the [[soul]]. Their apparent increase in intensity is, upon analysis, a progression through [[quality|qualitative]]ly distinct stages. The [[feeling|aesthetic feeling]] is thus the strongest evidence for Bergson's thesis that psychic [[intensity]] is never [[quantity|quantitative]].

## Bergson's Argument

### The Aesthetic Feelings as Test Case

Bergson introduces [[feeling|aesthetic feeling]]s as the clearest example of [[quality|qualitative]] states:

> [001-036] The aesthetic feelings. Their increasing intensities…

> [001-037] The aesthetic feelings offer us a still more striking example…

Aesthetic [[emotion|feeling]] seems to vary in intensity — a "deeply moving" work vs. a "lightly pleasing" one. But Bergson argues that what varies is not a magnitude but the **nature** of the [[emotion|feeling]] it[[ego|self]]. Each degree of aesthetic intensity is a different [[emotion|feeling]], not the same [[emotion|feeling]] amplified.

> [001-037] …this progressive stepping in of new elements, which can be detected in the fundamental emotion and which seem to increase its magnitude, although in reality they do nothing more than alter its nature.

New elements — associations, resonances, perceptual nuances — enter the [[emotion|feeling]] and **transform its quality**. What we call "more intense" aesthetic experience is [[action|act]]ually a richer, more complex [[quality|qualitative]] state.

### Stages of Elevation

Bergson identifies distinct stages in the aesthetic emotion:

> [001-046] Stages in the aesthetic emotion.

> [001-047] …experienced by us will assume an aesthetic character, provided that it has been *suggested,* and not *caused.*

The [[feeling|aesthetic feeling]] progresses through **degrees of elevation**:
1. The [[emotion|feeling]] barely interrupts ordinary psychic flow
2. It draws [[attention]] but leaves awareness of surroundings int[[action|act]]
3. It completely engrosses consciousness — "puts it[[ego|self]] in place of all other states"

Each stage is not "more" of the same [[feeling|aesthetic feeling]] but a **different relation** between the [[emotion|feeling]] and the whole of consciousness.

### Suggestion, Not Causation

The distinction between suggestion and [[causality|causation]] is crucial to Bergson's aesthetics:

> [001-047] …the feeling of the beautiful is no specific feeling, but that every feeling experienced by us will assume an aesthetic character, provided that it has been *suggested,* and not *caused.*

A [[emotion|feeling]] that is **caused** — triggered by a physical stimulus or a pr[[action|act]]ical need — remains tied to its object. A [[emotion|feeling]] that is **suggested** — evoked by rhythm, form, or association — detaches from pr[[action|act]]ical concerns and becomes aesthetic. The artist does not cause [[emotion|feeling]]s; she suggests them, and the suggestion allows the [[emotion|feeling]] to take on an aesthetic char[[action|act]]er.

### The Rhythm of Self-Forgetfulness

Aesthetic experience involves a distinctive rhythm that lulls the [[ego|self]] into forgetting its separateness:

> [001-050] …successive intensities of the aesthetic feeling thus correspond to changes of state…

The regular rhythm of art — poetic meter, musical beat, visual pattern — induces a state in which the boundary between [[ego|self]] and world softens. The [[ego|self]] "forgets it[[ego|self]]" and merges with the [[quality|qualitative]] flow. This is not a loss of consciousness but a return to pure [[duration]], before the [[intelligence|intellect]] imposed spatial divisions.

### Aesthetic Feeling and Moral Sympathy

Bergson connects [[feeling|aesthetic feeling]] to moral experience:

> [001-040] …through its affinity with moral sympathy, the idea of which is vaguely suggested…

The [[feeling|aesthetic feeling]] of grace in motion passes over, through sympathy, into a moral [[emotion|feeling]]. The perception of ease in a dancer's [[motion|movement]] suggests the idea of [[moral|moral sympathy]] — a fellow-[[emotion|feeling]] that is not calculated but immediately felt. Aesthetic and moral experience share the same [[quality|qualitative]], non-calculative char[[action|act]]er.

## Key Distinctions

### Aesthetic vs. Ordinary Feeling

An ordinary [[emotion|feeling]] is embedded in pr[[action|act]]ical life — it serves a function, prompts an [[action|act]]ion, responds to a need. An [[feeling|aesthetic feeling]] is **detached** from pr[[action|act]]ical concerns. It is suggested, not caused; it is contemplated, not [[action|act]]ed upon. This detachment is what allows the [[emotion|feeling]]'s [[quality|qualitative]] nature to be revealed.

### Intensity vs. Elevation

Aesthetic [[emotion|feeling]]s admit of both **intensity** and **elevation**. Intensity refers to the degree to which the [[emotion|feeling]] monopolizes consciousness. Elevation refers to the [[emotion|feeling]]'s [[quality|qualitative]] char[[action|act]]er — from the merely agreeable to the sublime. Bergson treats both as [[quality|qualitative]] transformations, not [[quantity|quantitative]] scales.
