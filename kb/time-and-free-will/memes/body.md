# Body

The [[matter|body]] occupies a unique position in Bergson's *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]*: it is neither purely inner (like a [[feeling]]) nor purely outer (like a physical object). The [[matter|body]] is the **surface of cont[[action|act]]** between the [[quality|qualitative]] flow of [[consciousness]] and the [[quantity|quantitative]] order of [[space]]. Bergson's analysis of the [[matter|body]] serves two purposes: first, to explain why [[psychophysics|psychophysical measurement]]s seem to work (bodily changes are measurable); second, to show why they ultimately fail (the inner state is not reducible to its bodily expression).

## Bergson's Argument

### The Body as Surface

> [001-028] …increasing number of muscular contractions which take place on the surface of the body, thus converting them into one single feeling of effort…

Bergson's key insight is that every conscious state has a **bodily accompaniment** — muscular contr[[action|act]]ions, changes in breathing, adjustments in circulation. When a fear grows more intense, the [[matter|body]] trembles more; when a joy deepens, the [[matter|body]] relaxes. These bodily changes are what psychophysics **[[rhythm|measure]]s** — but it then mistakenly attributes the [[rhythm|measure]]ment to the inner state it[[ego|self]]:

> [001-065] …proportional to extent of our body affected.

The intensity of a [[sensation]] or [[emotion|feeling]] seems measurable only because we project the **spatial** magnitude of the bodily change onto the **[[quality|qualitative]]** change in consciousness. The [[matter|body]] is the **screen** on which inner states are projected in spatial form.

### The Whole Body Responds

> [001-072] Examine carefully somebody who is lifting heavier and heavier weights…

Bergson emphasizes that the [[matter|body]] does not respond in isolated parts. When effort increases, it is not that one muscle contr[[action|act]]s more — it is that **more of the [[matter|body]] is drawn into** the [[action|act]]ion. The whole [[matter|body]] reorganizes it[[ego|self]]:

> [001-071] …and then of all the rest of the body have taken part in the operation.

This "whole [[matter|body]]" response is Bergson's evidence that what appears as a [[quantity|quantitative]] increase is [[action|act]]ually a **[[change|qualitative change]]** in bodily configuration. The [[matter|body]]'s own mode of being is more durational than mechanical:

> [001-081] …the distended nostrils quiver. The whole body often trembles. The voice is strained…

| Aspect of Body | Measurable (Spatial) | Lived (Durational) |
|---------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Muscular tension | Pounds of force | [[feeling|Feeling]] of strain |
| Respiration | Breaths per minute | [[feeling|Feeling]] of breathlessness |
| Posture | Angles of joints | [[feeling|Feeling]] of stance |

### The Body and the Self

> [001-060] We picture to our minds a psychic force imprisoned in the body…

The traditional dualist picture — the mind is "inside" the [[matter|body]], directing it like a pilot in a ship — is precisely the spatializing error Bergson diagnoses. The [[ego|self]] is not **in** the [[matter|body]] in the way an object is in a container. The relation between [[ego|self]] and [[matter|body]] is that of **expression**: the [[matter|body]] expresses the [[quality|qualitative]] state of the [[ego|self]] in spatial form.

## Key Distinctions

### Body vs. Matter

[[Matter]] is the abstr[[action|act]], homogeneous [[extensity|extension]] that remains when all qualities are stripped away. The [[matter|body]], by contrast, is **lived** — it is the [[quality|qualitative]] object that suffers, [[action|act]]s, and expresses. The [[matter|body]] is matter **plus** quality — and the quality is not an addition but the [[matter|body]]'s [[reality|real]]ity.

### Body vs. Organism

The [[matter|body]] as analyzed by physiology is an **organism** — a system of organs with measurable functions. Bergson does not deny the validity of physiology; he denies that the physiological description captures what the [[matter|body]] **is** as lived. The lived [[matter|body]] is not an organism; the organism is the [[matter|body]] spatialized.

### Body vs. Sensation

A [[sensation]] is a [[quality|qualitative]] state that is relatively localizable in the [[matter|body]] — a pain in the foot, a warmth on the skin. The [[matter|body]] is the **totality** of possible [[sensation]]s — the lived framework within which [[sensation]]s arise and to which they refer. The [[matter|body]] is not the sum of its [[sensation]]s; it is their condition.

## Historical Context

Bergson's theory of the [[matter|body]] engages with **[[descartes|Descartes]]'** dualism (which made the [[matter|body]] a machine inhabited by an immaterial mind), **Maine de Biran's** philosophy of effort (which found the experience of [[ego|self]]hood in the [[matter|body]]'s resistance to the will), and the **psychophysical** tradition (which [[rhythm|measure]]d bodily changes as proxies for mental states). Maine de Biran is the crucial precursor: he argued that the experience of bodily effort is the foundation of the [[ego|self]]'s awareness of it[[ego|self]] as agent. Bergson generalizes this insight — the [[matter|body]] is not only the locus of effort but the **expressive surface** of all [[consciousness|conscious states]].
