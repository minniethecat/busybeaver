# Dynamism (dynamisme)

Dynamism is the [[metaphysics|metaphysical doctrine]] that the ultimate nature of [[matter|reality]] is **[[action|act]]ive force** rather than passive [[matter]]. In *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]*, Bergson positions dynamism as the necessary alternative to [[mechanism]], tracing both positions back to their origin in the [[voluntary|experience of voluntary effort]] — the primordial sense of our own causal [[action|agency]] from which we project our [[mind|understanding]] of [[causality]] outward.

## Definition

At its core, dynamism asserts that [[causality]] is not the external collision of inert particles but the internal experience of **force, tendency, and [[voluntary|will]]**. Bergson's innovation is to ground this metaphysical claim in the immediate data of [[consciousness]]:

| Aspect | Dynamism | Mechanism |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Ultimate [[reality|real]]ity | Force, [[action|act]]ivity, tendency | Inert matter, position, motion |
| Source of causality | Inner experience of [[voluntary|voluntary action]] | External observation of [[motion|billiard-ball collisions]] |
| Laws and f[[action|act]]s | F[[action|act]]s more [[reality|real]] than laws | Laws govern f[[action|act]]s |
| View of [[consciousness]] | Active, creative, free | Passive, determined, epiphenomenal |
| Philosophical ally | [[free-will|Freedom]] | [[determinism|Determinism]] |

## Bergson's Usage

### The Origin in Voluntary Action

Bergson traces both [[mechanical|mechanism]] and dynamism to a common source: the experience of **voluntary effort**. When we [[action|act]] voluntarily, we feel ourselves immediately as a **cause** — a force producing an effect through our own [[matter|body]]:

> (001-202) …dynamism derives, from voluntary activ…

This inner experience of causal efficacy is the prototype from which we derive all [[idea|ideas]] of causality. Dynamism stays faithful to this original experience; [[mechanical|mechanism]] abstr[[action|act]]s from it, replacing the felt force with mathematical relations between [[motion|moving bodies]].

### Dynamism and Free Will

In Chapter III, Bergson makes the connection between dynamism and [[free-will|freedom]] explicit:

> (003-207) FREE WILL Mechanism, dynamism and free will.

If the [[ego|self]] is, as dynamism maintains, a centre of [[action|activity]] and [[tendency]] — if f[[action|act]]s are more [[reality|real]] than laws — then [[free-will|free action]] is not an exception to natural law but the very fabric of [[reality|real]]ity as it appears in [[duration|immediate consciousness]]:

> (003-279) …ousness, which shows us inner dynamism as a fact.

### The Question of Simplicity

Mechanism and dynamism also diverge on the meaning of **simplicity** in nature:

> (003-211) …l be found that mechanism and dynamism take the word simplicity in t…

For [[mechanical|mechanism]], simplicity means reduction to the fewest possible elementary parts and laws. For dynamism, simplicity means fidelity to the immediate, undivided experience — the [[duration|simple act]] of consciousness that cannot be decomposed without losing its essence.

The [[mechanical|mechanism]]–dynamism debate runs through the entire modern period ([[descartes|Descartes]], [[leibniz|Leibniz]], [[kant|Kant]]) and Bergson's intervention is to relocate it from speculative metaphysics to the [[psychophysics|psychological]] evidence of [[duration|immediate experience]].

## Key Distinctions

### Dynamism vs. Mechanism

The fundamental opposition (see table above). Mechanism models nature on the [[space|spatial]] rearrangement of [[quantity|quantitatively]] equivalent parts; dynamism models nature on the [[time|temporal]] unfolding of [[quality|qualitatively]] distinct [[tendency|tendencies]]. See [[mechanism]] for the detailed contrast.

### Dynamism vs. Vitalism

Though Bergson is sometimes called a vitalist, dynamism in *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]* is not a biological doctrine. It concerns the metaphysics of causality and [[consciousness]], not the life-force of [[body|organism]]s. The connection to [[evolution|creative evolution]] belongs to Bergson's later work.

### Dynamism vs. Spiritualism

Dynamism is compatible with but not identical to [[soul|spirit]]ualism (the view that [[matter]] is derivative from [[consciousness|spirit]]). Bergson's dynamism is methodologically grounded in [[perception|psychological observation]], not in [[soul|spirit]]ualist metaphysics — it starts from the immediate data of [[consciousness]], not from a prior commitment to [[soul|spirit]] over [[matter]].
