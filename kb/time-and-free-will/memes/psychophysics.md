# The Rise of Psychophysics

The rise of psychophysics in the mid-19th century was a landmark event in the history of psychology — the first systematic attempt to apply **[[quantity|quantitative]] [[rhythm|measure]]ment** to mental phenomena. For Henri Bergson, psychophysics represents the paradigmatic case of the error he diagnoses throughout *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]*: the treatment of [[consciousness]] as if it could be [[rhythm|measure]]d like objects in [[space]].

## Background

### The Scientific Context

By the 1850s, the success of physics and chemistry in discovering [[quantity|quantitative]] laws of nature created pressure to extend the same methods to the mind. If the physical world could be [[rhythm|measure]]d, why not the psychical? This ambition was reinforced by the broader **positivist** climate — the conviction that genuine knowledge is [[quantity|quantitative]] knowledge.

### The Philosophical Context

[[Kant]] had argued that psychology could never become a proper science because mental phenomena occur in time (not space) and therefore cannot be mathematized. The psychophysicists set out to prove [[kant|Kant]] wrong:

> (001-003) …but even the opponents of psychophysics do not see any harm in speaking of…

Bergson notes that even those who rejected psychophysics accepted its vocabulary — speaking of "more or less intense" [[sensation]]s as if differences in [[intensity]] were [[quantity|quantitative]].

### Weber's Preliminary Work

The empirical foundation was laid by Ernst Heinrich [[Weber]], who discovered that the smallest perceptible difference between two stimuli is proportional to the magnitude of the stimuli. This "[[weber|Weber]]'s Law" was a purely empirical regularity — it was [[Fechner]] who transformed it into a comprehensive psychophysical theory.

## Development

### Fechner's Synthesis

In his *Elemente der Psychophysik* (1860), [[fechner|Fechner]] combined [[weber|Weber]]'s empirical findings with a theoretical postulate: that equal ratios of stimulus correspond to equal increments of [[sensation]]. By summing just-noticeable differences, [[fechner|Fechner]] claimed to derive a mathematical law — [[sensation]] intensity = k log(stimulus intensity):

> (002-069) Fechner's psychophysics. Weber's Law.

> (003-007) The novel feature in Fechner's treatment is that he did not…

### The Method of Minimum Differences

[[fechner|Fechner]]'s primary experimental method involved presenting subjects with pairs of stimuli and asking them to judge whether they were equal. By varying the difference systematically, he identified the "threshold" of perceptible difference:

> (003-006) Fechner's method of minimum differences…

> (002-067) …Delbœuf's psychophysics assumes a theoretical postulate…

The method was ingenious but, as Bergson would argue, it assumed what it needed to prove — that [[sensation]]s are homogeneous magnitudes that can be summed.

### Critical Reception

[[fechner|Fechner]]'s work generated intense debate. Supporters saw the dawn of a scientific psychology; critics charged that consciousness could not be [[rhythm|measure]]d:

> (003-016) …all psychophysics revolves in a vicious circle.

> (003-017) …method of mean gradations has set psychophysics on a new path…

The French mathematician Jules Tannery mounted a particularly acute critique, arguing on mathematical grounds that [[fechner|Fechner]]'s summation of thresholds was invalid.

## Consequences

### Bergson's Critique

Bergson devotes sustained [[attention]] to psychophysics across all three chapters of *[[time|Time]] and [[free-will|Free Will]]*. His critique operates at multiple levels:

1. **Conceptual**: Psychophysics confuses [[intensity]] ([[quality|qualitative]]) with magnitude ([[quantity|quantitative]]):
   > (001-280) …psychophysics attempts to measure…

2. **Phenomenological**: The "[[sensation]]" that psychophysics [[rhythm|measure]]s is not [[consciousness|immediate consciousness]] but a spatialized reconstruction:
   > (003-005) …the very thing which psychophysics retains and claims to measure…

3. **Logical**: The summation of thresholds is circular — it assumes [[sensation]]s are additive in order to prove they are additive:
   > (003-019) In a word, all psychophysics is condemned by its origin to…

4. **Metaphysical**: Psychophysics reinforces the deterministic worldview that makes [[free-will]] seem impossible:
   > (003-024) …such was the object of psychophysics. In this bold attempt…

### Legacy

Despite Bergson's critique, psychophysics laid the groundwork for experimental psychology. [[Wundt]] established the first psychology laboratory in 1879, extending psychophysical methods to re[[action|act]]ion time, [[attention]], and memory. The tension between [[quantity|quantitative]] and [[quality|qualitative]] approaches to consciousness — crystallized in Bergson's critique — remains a live issue in philosophy of mind.

## Bergson's Analysis

### The Psychophysical Postulate

Bergson identifies the "fundamental postulate" of psychophysics as the assumption that equal differences in stimulus correspond to equal differences in [[sensation]]:

> (001-271) …fundamental, of psychophysics…

> (002-068) …the postulate of Fechner's psychophysics, which we are going to examine…

This postulate is not empirically discovered; it is **imposed** by the commitment to [[quantity|quantitative]] method before any data is collected.

### The Vicious Circle

Bergson argues that psychophysics involves a vicious circle. To [[rhythm|measure]] [[sensation]]s, it must treat them as magnitudes; but to treat them as magnitudes, it must already have [[rhythm|measure]]d them:

> (003-022) In truth, psychophysics merely formulates with precision…

The very [[action|act]] of asking subjects to compare [[sensation]]s as "greater or less than" imposes a spatial framework that distorts the phenomenon being studied.

### The Symbolic Character

Bergson does not deny that psychophysics produces useful results. But he argues that these results are **symbolic** — they represent consciousness indirectly, through its bodily effects, not directly:

> (003-018) …it is a symbolical representation…

Psychophysics [[rhythm|measure]]s the [[matter|body]]'s response to stimuli, not consciousness it[[ego|self]]. The "[[sensation]]s" it quantifies are spatial surrogates for [[quality|qualitative]] experiences.
