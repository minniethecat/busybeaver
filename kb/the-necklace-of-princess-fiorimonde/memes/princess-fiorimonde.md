# Princess Fiorimonde

## Character Overview

Princess Fiorimonde is the central figure of the title story in Mary De Morgan's 1880 collection. She is renowned throughout the world for her extraordinary beauty, but her heart is cold and calculating. Rather than marry, she uses a magical golden necklace to transform each of her suitors into a bead, collecting them as trophies. She is a complex figure — both villain and protagonist — whose vanity and cruelty ultimately lead to her own destruction.

> But the Princess Fiorimonde was as cruel as she was beautiful, and she cared for no one but herself, and would not marry, though the King, her father, prayed her to choose a husband from among the princes who came to woo her.

## Role in the Story

As the title character, Fiorimonde drives the entire plot. When her father the King demands she choose a husband, she consults her old nurse, a witch who gives her a magical golden necklace. Each time a suitor proposes, she touches the necklace, speaking a charm that transforms the man into a bead which she strings onto the necklace.

> Then the Princess laughed, and the touch of the golden beads as they rattled together was like distant bells. "Twenty-one beads," she said; "twenty-one princes. That is a goodly necklace. I will wear it always."

She accumulates twenty-one beads — twenty-one princes — before her maid Yolande discovers the secret and, with Prince Florestan's help, breaks the necklace's spell. Her punishment is poetic: she herself is transformed into the final bead on the necklace.

> And as she spoke, she herself began to shrink and shrivel, and her form dwindled, and she became a bead upon the necklace, the two-and-twentieth bead, and there she must remain till the necklace is destroyed.

## Character Traits

Fiorimonde embodies vanity, selfishness, and the abuse of power. She is intelligent and resourceful but uses her gifts for destruction. Her refusal to love or be loved is her defining characteristic. She represents the dark side of beauty — how external perfection can mask internal corruption.

Her relationship with the old nurse (the witch) reveals her willing embrace of evil means to achieve her ends. She shows no remorse until the moment of her transformation.

## Key Relationships

- **The King** — Her father, who pressures her to marry but is ultimately helpless against her schemes
- **Yolande** — Her maid who discovers the necklace's secret and becomes instrumental in breaking the spell
- **Prince Florestan** — The suitor who, with Yolande's help, ultimately defeats her
- **The Old Nurse** — A witch who provides Fiorimonde with the magical necklace

## Significance

Fiorimonde is one of the most memorable villains in Victorian fairy tales. She inverts the traditional princess archetype — rather than waiting to be rescued, she actively destroys her suitors. Her story serves as a cautionary tale about the corruption of beauty and power, and the inevitable consequences of cruelty.
