# Music

Music serves as one of the most potent forces in Mary De Morgan's fairy tales — a power that can enchant, heal, and express what words cannot. From lutes to harps to the human voice, musical performance consistently bridges the ordinary and the magical.

## Arasmon's Lute

[[arasmon|Arasmon]], the wandering musician, wields music as his defining gift. His lute-playing is described as mesmerizing:

> When Arasmon played all who heard him were silent from wonder and admiration. [0351-P1]

His music, combined with [[chrysea|Chrysea]]'s singing, forms the emotional and narrative core of "The Wanderings of Arasmon." Their performances draw crowds wherever they travel.

## The Golden Harp

Music also appears in magical instruments. [[the-golden-harp|The Golden Harp]], a enchanted artifact, produces sounds of supernatural beauty, illustrating how the tales treat music as inherently connected to the magical realm.

## Music as Emotional Language

Throughout the stories, music communicates emotional states that prose cannot. When characters are overwhelmed by joy, grief, or wonder, the tales turn to musical metaphors — birdsong, instrumental performance, and the act of listening — to convey interior experience. The very birds fly down from trees to listen when the music is especially beautiful, suggesting that true art dissolves the boundaries between species and worlds.
