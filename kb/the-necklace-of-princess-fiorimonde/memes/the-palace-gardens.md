# The Palace Gardens

## Location and Context

The Palace Gardens lie within the grounds of [[the-palace|the royal palace]] where [[princess-fiorimonde|Princess Fiorimonde]] lived with her father the King (001-109). The gardens, accessible directly from [[the-royal-castle|the palace]], contain elm trees, beech trees, and cultivated flower beds, and extend to garden gates that open to the countryside beyond.

The gardens serve as the threshold between the splendor of court life and the darkness of [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]]'s [[magic-and-witchcraft|sorcery]] — a space of seeming innocence where her most calculated seductions unfold.

## Description

The gardens are lush and formally planted, with tall beech trees providing shade and elm trees standing as landmarks (001-131, 001-143). Roses feature prominently — [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] once wore a gown "the colour of the inside of a rose" to a feast before stepping into the garden (001-109). At sunrise, the gardens are bathed in "red, blue, and [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]]" light that [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] herself remarked upon, comparing the dawn colours to the bead on her enchanted necklace (001-124–125).

The gardens extend far enough to include secluded areas where conversations can be overheard — [[arasmon|Arasmon]] hid beneath a large beech tree and listened to courtiers plotting against him (001-143).

> "She walked on till she came beneath the window of Prince Hildebrandt's room, and here she paused and began to sing a little song as sweet and joyous as a lark's" (001-123).

## Key Events

### King Pierrot's Proposal

After the welcome feast, [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] stepped from her [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]]en chair and walked into the garden, standing under an elm tree in the moonlight. [[king-pierrot|King Pierrot]] followed and asked what [[wedding-as-ritual|wedding]] gift she desired. She answered: a necklace of the finest [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]] and jewels, exactly the length of [[the-gold-necklace|the gold cord]] around her throat — the very cord she would use to transform him into a bead (001-109–115).

### Prince Hildebrandt's Seduction

At sunrise the next morning, [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] rose early, dressed in a plain white gown with her hair loose, and crept into [[the-royal-castle|the palace]] gardens. She stood beneath [[prince-hildebrandt|Prince Hildebrandt]]'s window and sang "as sweet and [[joy-as-emotion|joy]]ous as a lark's" (001-123). The Prince, enchanted, hurried down to meet her. In the red-[[wealth-and-poverty|gold]] sunrise light, [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] drew his attention to the bead on her [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]]en cord, and shortly thereafter he too became a bead on her necklace (001-124–125).

### Arasmon's Discovery

Years later, [[arasmon|Arasmon]] sat in [[the-royal-castle|the palace]] gardens beneath a large beech tree and overheard two courtiers conspiring to steal [[the-golden-harp|his golden harp]] (001-143–145). Learning of their plan, he fled through the garden gates that same evening, taking the [[music-as-art|harp]] and disappearing into the countryside — a departure that set him on the path back to [[the-spellbound-village|the Spellbound Village]] and his eventual re[[marriage-and-union|union]] with [[chrysea|Chrysea]] (001-147).

## Narrative Function

The Palace Gardens function as a liminal space where nature's beauty masks human treachery. The red-[[wealth-and-poverty|gold]] sunrise that makes [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]]'s hair "look [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]], and her face rosy" is the same light in which she works her darkest magic. The garden is neither fully inside [[the-royal-castle|the palace]] (the realm of political power) nor fully outside it (the realm of freedom), making it the perfect stage for [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]]'s boundary-crossing [[magic-and-witchcraft|sorcery]].

For [[arasmon|Arasmon]], the gardens represent a different kind of threshold: the moment of overhearing transforms him from passive court musician to active fugitive, setting in motion [[the-heros-journey|the journey]] that leads back to [[chrysea|Chrysea]].
