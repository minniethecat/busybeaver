# The Magic Peas

The magic peas in Mary De Morgan's *[[the-necklace-of-princess-fiorimonde-story|The Necklace of Princess Fiorimonde]]* are the first link in the chain of enchantment that sustains Fiorimonde's power. Without them, she cannot reach the witch's mountain; without the witch, she cannot maintain her magic.

## The Nightly Ritual

Fiorimonde's escape from the palace begins with a handful of peas:

> "When night came, and everyone in the palace was fast asleep, the Princess went to her bedroom window and softly opened it. Then she took from her pocket a handful of peas and held them out of the window and chirruped low, and there flew down from the roof a small brown bird and sat upon her wrist and began to eat the peas. No sooner had it swallowed them than it began to grow and grow and grow till it was so big that the Princess could not hold it." [0045-P1]

The transformation is described with the repetition "grow and grow and grow" — the peas do not simply enlarge the bird; they produce a grotesque, exponential expansion. A small brown bird becomes "as large as an ostrich" in seconds.

## The Journey

Once transformed, the bird becomes Fiorimonde's transport:

> "Then the Princess climbed out of the window and seated herself on the bird's back, and at once it flew straight away over the tops of the trees till it came to the mountain where the old witch dwelt." [0045-P1]

The peas enable the pattern that sustains the entire plot: Fiorimonde's secret visits to the witch, where she learns the necklace spell and reports on her victims. Without the peas, the geography of evil — the palace, the mountain, the witch's hut — cannot be connected.

## Repeated Use

The peas are not a one-time enchantment. During the storm that rages on Gervaise's night at the palace:

> "Then she gave him a handful of seeds as before, and he grew and grew and grew till he was as large as an ostrich, and she sat upon his back and flew out through the air, laughing at the lightning and thunder which flashed and roared around her." [0277-P1]

The phrase "as before" indicates routine. The peas are a renewable resource — as long as Fiorimonde has them, she can reach the witch. Her laughter at the storm, while flying on an enchanted bird, captures the arrogance that her magic enables.

## The Counterpart

The peas are balanced by their opposite: [[the-magic-water|the magic water]] from a small black bottle, which shrinks the bird back to its natural size upon Fiorimonde's return. Together, the peas and the water form a complete system of transformation and reversal — the rhythm of Fiorimonde's double life.
