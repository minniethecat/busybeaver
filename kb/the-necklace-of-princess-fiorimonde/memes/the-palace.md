# The Palace

The Palace is the primary setting for the frame narrative of "[[the-necklace-of-princess-fiorimonde-story|The Necklace of Princess Fiorimonde]]." It is the seat of the unnamed King ([[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]]'s father) and the stage upon which the tragedy of the disappearing suitors unfolds.

## The Seat of Power

The palace re[[the-exchange-of-gifts|presents]] legitimate royal [[justice-and-injustice|authority]]. It is where the King "summoned his council" to decide that [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] should marry, where "heralds were sent to all the neighbouring kings and princes," and where each suitor arrives with pomp and ceremony.

## Key Locations Within

### Fiorimonde's Bedroom

The Princess's private chamber is where she keeps her secrets. She opens the window to summon the enchanted bird, and it is where [[yolande|Yolande]] watches her through the mirror as she combs her hair. Most critically, it is where [[gervaise|Gervaise]] and [[yolande|Yolande]] attempt their nighttime mission to see [[the-gold-necklace|the necklace]].

### The Banqueting Hall

Grand feasts are held here for arriving suitors. It is where [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] "looked lovelier than ever dressed in a red gown, the colour of the inside of a rose" — and where, after the feast, she slips away to [[the-palace-gardens|the garden]] with each [[fate-and-transformation|doom]]ed prince.

### The Palace Gardens

The garden serves as the setting for the critical encounters between [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] and her suitors. Here she stands "under an elm-tree looking at the shining moon" with [[king-pierrot|King Pierrot]], and here she lies "on the marble edge of a fountain, feeding the [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]] fish" when [[prince-florestan|Prince Florestan]] arrives.

### The Fountain

A marble fountain in [[the-palace-gardens|the garden]] where [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] sits "dabbling with her hands in the water, and playing with the fish." It is here that [[prince-florestan|Prince Florestan]] first sees her in person, and his fate is sealed.

## Contrast with the Witch's Hut

The palace stands in deliberate opposition to [[the-witchs-hut]]. The palace is public, ornate, and ostensibly virtuous — yet it harbours [[evil-as-deception|evil]] within. The hut is hidden, humble, and openly dark — yet its [[evil-as-deception|evil]] is honest. De Morgan uses this spatial opposition to underscore her theme that appearances deceive, and true [[evil-as-deception|wickedness]] may wear a crown.
