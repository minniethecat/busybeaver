# Magic and Witchcraft

Magic and Witchcraft permeates Mary De Morgan's 1880 collection. Nearly every story features magical transformation, enchanted objects, or supernatural beings, with magic functioning as both plot mechanism and moral test.

## Forms of Magic in the Collection

### Transformative Magic

The most prominent form is transformation — humans turned into objects or beings:
- Suitors transformed into beads on [[the-gold-necklace]]
- [[chrysea|Chrysea]] transformed into [[the-golden-harp]] in "[[the-wanderings-of-arasmon-story|The Wanderings of Arasmon]]"
- The necklace's victims restored to human form when the thread is cut
- [[princess-joan|Princess Joan]]'s stolen heart in "[[the-heart-of-princess-joan-story|The Heart of Princess Joan]]"

### Enchanted Objects

Magical items appear throughout:
- The unbreakable [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]] thread and [[the-gold-necklace|the necklace]]
- The witch's drum that summons supernatural guests
- The magic water that shrinks the giant bird
- The wizard's book of living portraits in "[[the-heart-of-princess-joan-story|The Heart of Princess Joan]]"

### Supernatural Beings

The collection is populated by magical creatures:
- The old witch and her community of dark elves, goblins, and wicked fairies
- The yellow fairy who steals [[princess-joan|Princess Joan]]'s heart
- The wizard in [[the-wizards-tower|the round tower]]
- The dark elves who torment the spellbound village

## Magic as Moral Test

De Morgan uses magic not as neutral power but as a moral crucible. Characters reveal their true nature through how they use or respond to magic:
- [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] uses magic for selfish cruelty and is ultimately destroyed by it
- [[chrysea|Chrysea]] uses her voice (itself a kind of magic) to free the spellbound village, even at great personal cost
- [[gervaise|Gervaise]] resists [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]]'s magical beauty to do what is right

## The Rules of Magic

De Morgan's magic follows consistent rules:
1. Magic has [[punishment-and-reward|consequences]] — [[the-old-witch|the witch]] warns [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] of the danger
2. Spells can be broken by specific counter-actions (cutting the thread, singing the elves' song)
3. Magical transformations preserve identity — the beads and [[music-as-art|harp]] retain their human essence
4. Magic cannot be hidden forever — [[yolande|Yolande]]'s observation ultimately leads to exposure
