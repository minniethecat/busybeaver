# King Adrian

King Adrian was one of the many royal suitors who came to woo [[princess-fiorimonde|Princess Fiorimonde]] and fell victim to her sorcery. He was transformed into a bead upon [[the-gold-necklace|her enchanted gold necklace]], alongside other kings and princes.

## Among Fiorimonde's Suitors

Adrian was among the first wave of suitors who answered the King's call for a husband for Princess Fiorimonde:

> The King agreed, and envoys were sent all the world over to bid any who would come and be the husband of Princess Fiorimonde. And come they did, kings and princes from south and north, east and west—King Adrian, Prince Sigbert, Prince Algar, and many more. [0147-P1]

Like all who preceded him, Adrian vanished on the wedding morning, transformed into a bead on the gold thread Fiorimonde wore around her neck.

## Named Among the Victims

[[yolande|Yolande]], Fiorimonde's maidservant, later revealed the truth to [[prince-florestan|Prince Florestan]], listing the victims by name:

> "This was Pierrot, and this Hildebrandt, and these are Adrian, and Sigbert, and Algar, and Cenred, and that is Pharamond, and that Raoul, and last of all here is your own master Prince Florestan." [0227-P1]

## Liberation

When [[gervaise|Gervaise]], the loyal servant of Prince Florestan, broke the gold thread, King Adrian was freed along with the other captive suitors:

> Gervaise had drawn off another bead, and King Hildebrandt appeared, and after him came Adrian, and Sigbert, and Algar, and Cenred, and Pharamond, and Raoul, and last of the princes, Gervaise's own dear master Florestan, and they all denounced Princess Fiorimonde and her wickedness. [0333-P1]
