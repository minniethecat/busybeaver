# The Wanderings of Arasmon (Story)

"The Wanderings of Arasmon" is the second tale in Mary De Morgan's 1880 collection *[[the-necklace-of-princess-fiorimonde-story|The Necklace of Princess Fiorimonde]] and Other Stories*. It follows a wandering musician's quest to find his wife [[chrysea|Chrysea]] after she is transformed by dark elves.

## Plot Summary

[[arasmon|Arasmon]] and [[chrysea|Chrysea]] are wandering musicians — he plays the [[music-as-art|lute]], she sings — whose music is so beautiful that "all who heard him were silent from wonder and admiration, but when [[chrysea|Chrysea]] sang they could not refrain from weeping." They are deeply in love and happy in their wandering life.

They arrive at a spellbound village where the inhabitants live in misery under a curse cast by "a terrible old wizard" served by "little dark elves." The elves steal food, destroy houses, and blight the land. [[chrysea|Chrysea]] learns that the spell can be broken if someone — who has never taken the elves' money — can hear their magical song and sing it back.

That night, [[chrysea|Chrysea]] goes alone to [[the-moorland|the moor]]. She hears the elves sing their spell and sings it back perfectly. The spell is broken, but the enraged elves transform her into "a bright [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]]en [[music-as-art|harp]]" hanging from a tree, with the curse that she shall remain so "until upon her strings someone shall play our tune, which she has dared to sing."

[[arasmon|Arasmon]] finds the [[music-as-art|harp]], not knowing it is [[chrysea|Chrysea]]. Whenever he plays it, the [[music-as-art|harp]] cries "[[arasmon|Arasmon]], [[arasmon|Arasmon]], I am here! It is I, [[chrysea|Chrysea]]" — but he cannot understand. He wanders the world for years, searching for [[chrysea|Chrysea]] while carrying the very [[music-as-art|harp]] that contains her.

## Key Characters

- **[[arasmon|Arasmon]]** — The wandering musician, devoted husband
- **[[chrysea|Chrysea]]** — His wife, a singer of unmatched beauty, transformed into a [[music-as-art|harp]]
- **The Wizard** — The malevolent magician who cursed the village
- **The Dark Elves** — The wizard's servants who torment the village

## Themes

Like the title story, "The Wanderings of Arasmon" explores transformation, [[love-and-loyalty|devotion]], and the tragic [[punishment-and-reward|consequences]] of encountering the supernatural. The [[music-as-art|harp]] motif — a transformed human who can only "speak" through music — echoes mythological traditions of [[fate-and-transformation|metamorphosis]] and unrequited recognition.
