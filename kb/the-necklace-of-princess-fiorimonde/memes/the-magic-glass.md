# The Magic Glass

The Magic Glass is a divinatory and [[truth-and-deception|truth]]-revealing object in Mary De Morgan's "[[the-heart-of-princess-joan-story|The Heart of Princess Joan]]." A gift from the sorcerer who sends [[prince-michael|Prince Michael]] on his quest, this small round piece of glass serves as both compass and protector — guiding [[prince-michael|Michael]] across the world by the light of a crimson star, and later revealing the true monstrous forms hidden beneath beautiful illusions within the enchanted [[the-royal-castle|castle]].

## Origin

The glass is given to [[prince-michael|Prince Michael]] by the old sorcerer — the same wizard who, seven years earlier, had stolen the heart of the infant [[princess-joan|Princess Joan]]. When [[prince-michael|Michael]] resolves to undertake [[the-heros-journey|the quest]] to recover it, the sorcerer provides the only aid he will offer:

> "Take this; it is all that I can give you, to help you, and through it you must look at the stars, and you will see that they are all of different colours — blue, green, red, and yellow; look for the one which is the deepest, brightest red, and follow it; it will lead you many miles both by land and sea, but follow steadily, and let nothing turn you from your course, and you will surely come to the castle wherein is imprisoned the heart of your Princess." [753-ST04]

[[prince-michael|Michael]] thanks the magician and takes the glass, beginning his journey immediately [755-ST04].

## Physical Description

The glass is described as "a small round piece of glass" carried in [[prince-michael|Michael]]'s bosom, close to his heart [753-ST04, 809-ST04]. Its small size allows it to be concealed, and [[prince-michael|Michael]] repeatedly draws it forth at critical moments. The glass is durable enough to survive years of hard labour — [[prince-michael|Michael]] consults it even after seven years of stone-hauling, when his clothes have become "mere rags" and his body bent with toil [813-ST04].

## Properties / Powers

The Magic Glass exhibits two distinct magical properties:

**Star-Vision.** When [[prince-michael|Michael]] first raises the glass to the night sky, the transformation is immediate and wondrous: "they looked wonderful. They were like jewels of all colours — green, blue, yellow, pink — and in the south was one of a deep glowing red, like a blood-red rose, and [[prince-michael|Michael]] knew that that was the star he must follow" [757-ST04]. The glass reveals the hidden colours of stars invisible to the naked eye, and the crimson star becomes [[prince-michael|Michael]]'s compass across strange lands by night [759-ST04]. Even after years of imprisonment on the serpent's island, [[prince-michael|Michael]] still consults the glass by night to confirm the star "still looked a bright crimson colour" [809-ST04].

**Illusion-Piercing.** Within the enchanted [[the-royal-castle|castle]], the glass's second power proves crucial. When the [[the-royal-castle|castle]]'s mistress appears as a stately, welcoming lady in black velvet, [[prince-michael|Michael]] remembers the glass: "drawing it forth when she was not looking, gazed through it at her, and then he beheld no finely-dressed lady, but a wizened old woman, robed in yellow, with an [[evil-as-deception|evil]] yellow face and [[evil-as-deception|evil]] yellow eyes" [835-ST04]. The glass reveals the [[truth-and-deception|truth]] behind magical disguises, exposing the yellow hag who controls the [[the-royal-castle|castle]]. This power is tested again when a beautiful child offers food, and a third time when the hag takes the form of [[princess-joan|Princess Joan]] herself — each time the glass reveals the same yellow hag beneath the illusion [845-ST04, 863-ST04]. On the final occasion, the glass literally saves [[prince-michael|Michael]]'s life: "when the cup struck against the magic glass in his bosom, and he drew it forth and looked at her, and he trembled with horror and disgust, for there he saw no lovely [[princess-joan|Princess Joan]], but the same yellow hag, who held in one skinny hand a goblet, formed from a skull" [863-ST04].

## Role in the Story

The Magic Glass serves triple narrative functions. As **compass**, it provides [[the-heros-journey|the quest]]'s direction, translating the sorcerer's cryptic instructions into a visible path across the heavens. As **protector**, it repeatedly saves [[prince-michael|Michael]] from the yellow hag's [[truth-and-deception|deception]]s within the [[the-royal-castle|castle]], where eating, drinking, or speaking any word other than his demand would mean failure and [[death-and-mortality|death]]. As **token of connection**, it reminds [[prince-michael|Michael]] of his purpose during the seven years of [[hope-as-resilience|hope]]less labour, the crimson star a persistent thread linking him to his distant home and to [[princess-joan|Joan]].

Without the glass, [[prince-michael|Michael]] would have been lost at three critical junctures: he could not have found the [[the-royal-castle|castle]], he would have been poisoned by the hag's banquet, and he would have drunk from the skull-goblet believing it was [[princess-joan|Joan]]'s loving [[the-exchange-of-gifts|offering]].

## Symbolic Significance

The Magic Glass embodies the theme of **seeing truly** that runs through "[[the-heart-of-princess-joan-story|The Heart of Princess Joan]]." While the story repeatedly emphasizes physical beauty — [[princess-joan|Joan]]'s portrait, the [[the-royal-castle|castle]]'s splendour, the hag's disguises — the glass re[[the-exchange-of-gifts|presents]] a deeper sight that penetrates surface appearances. Its placement "in his bosom," against [[prince-michael|Michael]]'s heart, suggests that true perception is a matter of the heart as much as the eyes.

The glass also functions as a **test of [[love-and-loyalty|fidelity]]**. [[prince-michael|Michael]] must remember to use it at each temptation; the glass only works when he actively chooses to look through it rather than trust appearances. In this sense, it parallels the [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]]en sword — both are [[the-exchange-of-gifts|gifts]] from mentor figures that require the hero's active agency to be effective.
