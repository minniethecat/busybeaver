# The Royal Castle

The royal castle — appearing variously as palace, court, and castle — is the primary setting across Mary De Morgan's 1880 collection *[[the-necklace-of-princess-fiorimonde-story|The Necklace of Princess Fiorimonde]]*. It is at once a place of splendour and a stage for [[the-wedding-feast|deception]], where [[the-wedding-feast|bridal feast]]s become disappearance scenes and beautiful chambers hide dark secrets.

## The Palace in "The Necklace of Princess Fiorimonde"

The primary palace belongs to the old King, father of [[princess-fiorimonde|Princess Fiorimonde]]. It is here that [[the-gold-necklace|the necklace]]'s deadly work unfolds. The palace is described through its activities rather than its architecture:

> "Great preparations were made for his arrival, and the Princess was decked in her finest array to greet him."

The palace hosts a sequence of [[the-wedding-feast|betrothal feast]]s, each ending with a vanished bridegroom. Its gardens become the site of [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]]'s encounters with her suitors — beneath an elm tree by moonlight, beside a fountain where she feeds [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]]fish — transforming the palace grounds from places of beauty into hunting grounds.

The palace's most significant space is [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]]'s bedchamber, where [[yolande|Yolande]] watches her mistress's secret [[joy-as-emotion|delight]] and where [[gervaise|Gervaise]] creeps by lantern-light to see [[the-gold-necklace|the necklace]] for himself — and loses [[yolande|Yolande]] to its power.

## The Palace in "The Heart of Princess Joan"

A different palace serves as the childhood home of [[princess-joan|Princess Joan]]. It is here that the yellow [[magic-and-witchcraft|fairy]] appears at [[princess-joan|Joan]]'s cradle, and here that [[princess-joan|Joan]] grows up beautiful but heartless. The palace gardens are where [[prince-michael|Prince Michael]], disguised as a peasant, first watches [[princess-joan|Joan]] and witnesses her coldness:

> "He walked near the palace gardens, and no one noticed him, and he saw a group of lovely ladies, who sat together on the grass."

Seven years later, the same palace is decorated for [[princess-joan|Joan]]'s [[wedding-as-ritual|wedding]] to [[king-lambert|King Lambert]] — "hung with flags, and arches of flowers were erected in front of it" — but the celebration is hollow, a [[marriage-and-union|marriage]] of convenience that the people do not support.

## The Tower of the Wizard

Outside [[prince-michael|Prince Michael]]'s palace stands a mysterious tower, made "of one huge stone" with "no joins in it at all." At its top dwells an ancient [[magic-and-witchcraft|wizard]] whom only [[prince-michael|Michael]] dares to visit. The tower's interior is a narrow, pitch-dark spiral staircase leading to a room "lit by a lamp which the old man himself had made, and in which no oil or wick was burning, but every day it was filled with sunbeams."

It is in this tower that [[prince-michael|Michael]] first sees [[princess-joan|Joan]]'s portrait in the wizard's enchanted book, and where he receives the magic glass that will guide and protect him on his quest.

## The Heart Castle

The most fantastical castle in the collection stands across a grey sea on a "flat, lonely shore, without trees or grass growing in sight":

> "It was built of black marble, and a more gloomy place could not be, for the windows were small and high up, and were all barred across, with heavy iron bars, and the castle had no spires or towers, but was one square black block, and looked more like a prison than a castle."

Guarded by a moat, a high wall, and a gray gnome with his serpent, the castle's exterior belies its interior. Inside, the yellow fairy creates illusions of luxury — "silken hangings, and [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]], and pearls, and flowers" — which vanish when [[prince-michael|Michael]] resists her temptations, revealing the [[truth-and-deception|truth]]: "a grim gray chamber like a vault."

## The Castle as Moral Space

De Morgan uses castle settings to map moral geography:

| Space | Function |
|-------|----------|
| Palace gardens | Encounters between suitor and princess — the threshold of danger |
| Princess's bedchamber | The hidden [[truth-and-deception|truth]] behind public performance |
| Banqueting hall | Public spectacle where disappearances are discovered |
| Prison tower | Where [[love-and-loyalty|true love]] proves itself through [[despair-and-endurance|endurance]] |
| Wizard's tower | The threshold between the natural and supernatural worlds |
