# The Witch's Hut

The Witch's Hut is a key location in "[[the-necklace-of-princess-fiorimonde-story|The Necklace of Princess Fiorimonde]]." It sits "on the side of a lonely mountain" and serves as the source of the [[magic-and-witchcraft|dark magic]] that drives the story's central conflict.

## Location and Secrecy

The hut is deliberately hidden from the world. De Morgan tells us that "no one but the King's daughter knew that she lived there." This secrecy is essential to the plot — [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]]'s power depends on no one discovering her magical tutelage, which is why she fears [[marriage-and-union|marriage]] and the scrutiny a husband would bring.

## Interior

When [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] first visits the hut in the story, she finds [[the-old-witch|the witch]] "in the middle of which, wrapped in a gray cloak which almost hid her." The witch keeps a "black bag" containing magical materials, including the [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]] thread. A drum and drumsticks sit in a corner, used to summon supernatural guests.

## The Underground Chamber

Beneath the hut lies a hidden space accessed through a trapdoor: "[[the-old-witch|the witch]] stopped drumming, and, going to the middle of the hut, stamped on the floor, and a trapdoor opened in the ground." This leads down "a narrow dark passage, to a large underground chamber" where [[the-old-witch|the witch]]'s supernatural gatherings take place — a subterranean ballroom for dark elves, goblins, witches, and wicked fairies.

## The Witch's Sabbath

The underground chamber hosts a terrifying supernatural dance. Guests include:
- "little dark elves with long tails"
- "goblins who chattered and laughed"
- "other witches who rode on broomsticks"
- "one wicked fairy in the form of a large cat, with bright green eyes"
- Another fairy "came sliding in like a long shining viper"

At cockcrow, all vanish instantly, and the gathering dissolves "with a whiff."

## Narrative Function

The hut functions as a classic fairy-tale threshold space — a place where the normal rules of the kingdom do not apply and where dark transformations are possible. It is the anti-palace: where [[the-royal-castle|the palace]] re[[the-exchange-of-gifts|presents]] legitimacy, order, and public life, the hut re[[the-exchange-of-gifts|presents]] secrecy, chaos, and hidden power.
