# The Transformation of King Pierrot

The Transformation of King Pierrot is the first and most dramatically detailed of the [[magic-and-witchcraft|enchantment]]s in "[[the-necklace-of-princess-fiorimonde-story|The Necklace of Princess Fiorimonde]]." It establishes the pattern that will repeat with variations for the next ten suitors, and reveals the full mechanism of [[the-gold-necklace|the necklace]]'s magic.

## Context

[[king-pierrot|King Pierrot]] is the first suitor chosen by [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]]'s father. He is described as "a young king... from over the sea" who ruled "a large rich country." He was "strong and handsome, with black hair, and eyes like sloes." The [[wedding-as-ritual|wedding]] is fully prepared, and all seems well until the evening before the ceremony.

## The Encounter

After a grand feast where [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] wears her red gown ("the colour of the inside of a rose"), she slips away to [[the-royal-castle|the palace]] garden. Under an elm tree, in the moonlight, [[king-pierrot|King Pierrot]] follows her. He asks what [[wedding-as-ritual|wedding]] gift she desires, and [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] requests "a necklace wrought of the finest [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]] and jewels." When [[king-pierrot|Pierrot]] asks about the plain [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]] cord she wears, she cunningly challenges him to test its strength.

## The Transformation

"[[king-pierrot|King Pierrot]] took the cord in both hands to pull it hard; but no sooner were his fingers closed around it than he vanished like a puff of smoke, and on the cord appeared a bright, beautiful bead — so bright and beautiful as was never bead before — clear as crystal, but shining with all colours — green, blue, and [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]]."

## Aftermath

[[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] returns to the banquet and feigns concern: "Pray, sire, send someone at once to find [[king-pierrot|King Pierrot]], for, as he was talking to me a minute ago, he suddenly left me." A search is mounted, but [[king-pierrot|King Pierrot]] is never found. [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] dons black mourning clothes and "begged to be allowed to live in seclusion for one month in which to grieve" — while privately laughing at her victim.

## Narrative Significance

This first transformation establishes the story's core mechanism and [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]]'s method. It demonstrates her cunning, her ability to manipulate, and her utter lack of remorse. The detailed description serves as a template the reader will recognize in each subsequent disappearance. The bead's beauty — "shining with all colours" — establishes the dark irony that victims become objects of adornment.
