# Fate

Fate in Mary De Morgan's collection is neither arbitrary nor external. It is the narrative machinery of moral consequence — a force that punishes the wicked and rewards the faithful, operating through the logic of the fairy tale rather than through divine intervention.

## The Fate of the Wicked

[[princess-fiorimonde|Princess Fiorimonde]]'s fate is sealed by the very instrument she created. The necklace that trapped her suitors becomes her own prison:

> "No sooner had she taken it in her hands than they fell with a rattle to the earth, and Fiorimonde herself was nowhere to be seen." [0321-P1]

Her fate is not externally imposed — it is the natural consequence of her own sorcery, a self-woven doom.

## Fate Recognized

The collection's characters often sense impending fate before it arrives. [[prince-florestan|Prince Florestan]]'s friend [[gervaise|Gervaise]] warns:

> "'There is an evil fate about the Princess Fiorimonde,' quoth he; 'many have gone to marry her, but where are they now?'" [0155-P1]

This recognition of pattern — the ability to read fate from precedent — is a form of wisdom the collection rewards.

## Public Acknowledgment of Fate

When Fiorimonde's fate is complete, it becomes a public monument:

> "So they lifted the golden thread with great care and hung it up outside the town-hall, and there the one bead glittered and gleamed in the sunlight, and all who saw it knew that it was the wicked Princess Fiorimonde who had justly met her fate." [0343-P1]

Fate, once fulfilled, becomes a visible warning — a bead on public display that teaches the community what happens to the wicked.

## Fate and Lament

Not all characters accept their fate quietly. The raven in "The Bread of Discontent" laments:

> "'Alas!' croaked the raven, 'my fate is indeed a hard one. But it serves me right for trusting a donkey who goes on his feet and cannot fly.'" [1059-P1]

The raven's self-awareness — "it serves me right" — reflects the collection's moral: fate is rarely undeserved. Even the complainer recognizes his own complicity.
