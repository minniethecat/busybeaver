# The Beads (Enchanted Necklace)

The beads are the central magical object of "[[the-necklace-of-princess-fiorimonde-story|The Necklace of Princess Fiorimonde]]," the title story of Mary De Morgan's 1880 collection. Each bead on the [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]]en cord is a living being — a king or prince transformed by [[magic-and-witchcraft|witchcraft]] — making [[the-gold-necklace|the necklace]] simultaneously a beautiful ornament and a prison for twelve souls.

## Creation and Properties

The necklace is fashioned by an old [[the-spellbound-village|witch]] who dwells in a hut on a lonely mountain. When [[princess-fiorimonde|Princess Fiorimonde]] complains that her father wishes her to marry, [[the-old-witch|the witch]] offers her a choice of [[magic-and-witchcraft|enchantment]]s:

> "'Would you like them to become dogs, to come at your call, or birds, to fly in the air, and sing of your beauty, or will you make them all into beads, the beads of such a necklace as never woman wore before, so that they may rest upon your neck, and you may take them with you always.'"

[[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] chooses [[the-gold-necklace|the necklace]]. The witch draws from a black bag "a long [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]] thread" whose ends are invisibly joined and which "however much you pulled, it would not break."

## The Transformation Mechanism

The necklace's power is activated by touch alone. When any person closes their fingers around [[the-gold-thread|the golden string]], they instantly vanish and become a bead:

> "No sooner were his fingers closed around it than he vanished like a puff of smoke, and on the cord appeared a bright, beautiful bead — so bright and beautiful as was never bead before — clear as crystal, but shining with all colours — green, blue, and gold."

The transformation requires no incantation, no potion — only that the victim grasps the cord. This simplicity is the source of [[the-gold-necklace|the necklace]]'s danger.

## The Witch's Warning

Crucially, [[the-gold-necklace|the necklace]] poses equal danger to its wearer:

> "'But this is a dangerous play,' quoth the witch, 'for, unless you are very careful, you yourself may become a bead and hang upon the string with the others, and there you will remain till someone cuts the string, and draws you off.'"

[[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] dismisses this warning: "Nay, never fear, I will be careful." Her overconfidence proves her undoing.

## The Bead Collection

Over many years, [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] systematically transforms eleven suitors:

| 2 | [[prince-hildebrandt|Prince Hildebrandt]] | "Tall, and fair, and strong, with flaxen hair and bright blue eyes" |
| 3 | King Adrian | — |
| 4 | Prince Sigbert | — |
| 5 | Prince Algar | — |
| 6 | Cenred | — |
| 7 | Pharamond | — |
| 8 | Baldwyn | — |
| 9 | Leofric | — |
| 10 | Raoul | — |
| 11 | [[prince-florestan|Prince Florestan]] | [[gervaise|Gervaise]]'s beloved master |
| 12 | [[yolande|Yolande]] | [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]]'s maid, accidentally transformed while trying to help [[gervaise|Gervaise]] |

When [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] wakes to find a twelfth bead she cannot account for, she is "much perplexed" but quickly rationalises: "At any rate, whoever it was, is fitly punished. My brave necklace, you can take care of yourself."

## The Necklace's Destruction

[[gervaise|Gervaise]], the loyal friend of [[prince-florestan|Prince Florestan]], devises a clever stratagem to destroy [[the-gold-necklace|the necklace]]. He tells [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] he has seen a woman more beautiful than she, and when she demands to see her, he offers his own crude necklace of acorns and haws in ex[[fate-and-transformation|change]] for hers. In her "rage and eagerness," [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] forgets [[the-old-witch|the witch]]'s warning:

> "She seized the string of beads to lift it from her neck, but no sooner had she taken it in her hands than they fell with a rattle to the earth, and Fiorimonde herself was nowhere to be seen."

The thirteenth bead — [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] herself — joins the string. [[gervaise|Gervaise]] then cuts [[the-gold-necklace|the golden thread]] with a dagger, releasing all twelve prisoners. The necklace is hung outside the town hall with only [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]]'s single bead remaining: "a warning to others who would do like her."

## Significance

The beads embody a triple symbolic function:
1. **Vanity punished**: Each bead is an object of beauty that hides a stolen life
2. **The predator becomes prey**: [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]], who used [[the-gold-necklace|the necklace]] to trap others, is ultimately trapped by it herself
3. **Justice through reversal**: The necklace's very mechanism of entrapment — closing one's fingers around the string — becomes the means of [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]]'s own undoing

The image of [[the-gold-necklace|the necklace]] with twelve bright beads and one "that glittered and gleamed in the sunlight" serves as the collection's most powerful visual emblem of [[evil-as-deception|wickedness]] exposed.
