# The Golden Harp

The Golden Harp is a magical musical instrument in Mary De Morgan's "[[the-wanderings-of-arasmon-story|The Wanderings of Arasmon]]," formed when the elf-folk of [[the-moorland|the moor]] transformed the singer [[chrysea|Chrysea]] into a small [[music-as-art|harp]] of pure [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]] as [[justice-and-injustice|punishment]] for singing their forbidden tune. It serves as both a prison and a vessel for [[chrysea|Chrysea]]'s enchanted voice, and its eventual playing by her husband [[arasmon|Arasmon]] becomes the instrument of her liberation.

## Origin

The [[music-as-art|harp]] was created on [[the-moorland|the moor]] where [[chrysea|Chrysea]] and her husband [[arasmon|Arasmon]], wandering musicians, had been separated. After [[arasmon|Arasmon]] left to find food, [[chrysea|Chrysea]] encountered [[the-moorland|the moor]]land elves and defiantly sang the magic tune they used to torment a nearby village. The chief elf decreed her [[justice-and-injustice|punishment]]:

> "Hear her, and let her have her will. Let us change her to no bird or beast, but to a bright golden harp, and thus shall she remain, until upon her strings someone shall play our tune, which she has dared to sing." [409-411-ST03]

The elves danced around [[chrysea|Chrysea]], and when their song ended, all that remained was "a little [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]] [[music-as-art|harp]] hanging upon the boughs of the tree" [411-ST03].

## Physical Description

The [[music-as-art|harp]] is described as "a beautiful little [[music-as-art|harp]], made of pure [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]]," with a distinctive feature at its crown: "at the top was a pair of [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]]en hands and arms clasped together" [425-ST03]. Unlike ordinary instruments, it remained "undulled, and untarnished" even after years of travel [501-ST03]. Its appearance was striking enough that even in a royal court glittering with [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]] and silver and crystal, the [[music-as-art|harp]] stood out as an object of wonder.

## Properties / Powers

The [[music-as-art|harp]] possesses several magical properties:

**The Enchanted Voice.** When its strings are touched, the [[music-as-art|harp]] produces music unlike any other instrument — "never before did [[music-as-art|harp]] give forth such music. It was like a woman's voice, and was most beautiful, but so sad that when [[arasmon|Arasmon]] heard it he felt inclined to cry" [423-ST03]. The voice within the strings belongs to [[chrysea|Chrysea]] herself, who calls out "[[arasmon|Arasmon]], [[arasmon|Arasmon]], I am here! It is I, [[chrysea|Chrysea]]" each time the strings are played — though no one but the blackbird can understand her words [423-ST03].

**Exclusive Player.** Only [[arasmon|Arasmon]] could draw the [[music-as-art|harp]]'s true music from its strings: "no one but [[arasmon|Arasmon]] could play upon his [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]]en [[music-as-art|harp]]. All the King's [[music-as-art|harp]]ers tried, and the King himself tried also, but when they touched the strings there came from them a strange, melancholy wailing, and no one but [[arasmon|Arasmon]] could bring out its beautiful notes" [509-ST03].

**The Breaking of the Spell.** The [[music-as-art|harp]]'s [[magic-and-witchcraft|enchantment]] could only be broken when someone played the specific elf-tune upon its strings — the very tune [[chrysea|Chrysea]] had sung that caused her transformation. When [[arasmon|Arasmon]] finally returned to the transformed moor and played the tune the blackbird had learned, "at once the [[music-as-art|harp]] slid from his hand, and [[chrysea|Chrysea]] stood beside him — [[chrysea|Chrysea]] dressed as of old, in shining white and [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]], with bright hair and eyes" [577-ST03].

**Nighttime Vigilance.** Even while [[arasmon|Arasmon]] slept with his head resting on the [[music-as-art|harp]], "if by chance one of its [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]]en threads was touched it would cry, '[[arasmon|Arasmon]], awake, I am here!'" — causing him to dream that [[chrysea|Chrysea]] was calling him [435-ST03].

## Role in the Story

The Golden Harp is central to the second half of "[[the-wanderings-of-arasmon-story|The Wanderings of Arasmon]]." After [[chrysea|Chrysea]]'s transformation, [[arasmon|Arasmon]] finds the [[music-as-art|harp]] hanging in a tree on the now-blooming moor and takes it with him on his quest to find his wife — unaware that she is imprisoned within it. The [[music-as-art|harp]] becomes his companion across "days and months and years through countries and villages which he had never known before" [435-ST03].

The [[music-as-art|harp]]'s extraordinary music wins [[arasmon|Arasmon]] a position as chief musician to a great King, arousing the jealousy of [[the-royal-castle|the court]] musicians. Throughout his wandering, [[arasmon|Arasmon]] asks everyone he meets, "Have you seen my wife [[chrysea|Chrysea]]?" — not realizing she travels with him always, her voice crying out from [[the-gold-thread|the golden string]]s he carries [431-ST03].

The [[music-as-art|harp]]'s final role is as the instrument of re[[marriage-and-union|union]]: when [[arasmon|Arasmon]] returns to [[the-moorland|the moor]] after many years, guided by the blackbird's song, he plays the elf-tune upon the [[music-as-art|harp]]'s strings, and [[chrysea|Chrysea]] is restored to human form [577-ST03].

## Symbolic Significance

The Golden Harp embodies several symbolic dimensions. As a **vessel of the silenced voice**, it re[[the-exchange-of-gifts|presents]] the paradox of artistic expression under [[magic-and-witchcraft|enchantment]] — [[chrysea|Chrysea]] can speak only through music, and only to those who know how to listen. The clasped [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]]en hands at the [[music-as-art|harp]]'s crown suggest both supplication and embrace, a frozen gesture of reaching out that mirrors [[chrysea|Chrysea]]'s trapped state.

The [[music-as-art|harp]] also functions as a **token of [[love-and-loyalty|faithful love]]**. [[arasmon|Arasmon]] carries it for years without knowing it holds his wife, his [[love-and-loyalty|devotion]] to finding her expressed through his care for the instrument. The fact that only [[arasmon|Arasmon]] can play its true music reinforces the idea that love is the key to recognition and release.

Finally, the [[music-as-art|harp]]'s creation by the elves as **[[justice-and-injustice|punishment]]-by-transformation** parallels other transformation motifs in De Morgan's collection — notably [[princess-fiorimonde|Princess Fiorimonde]]'s necklace, which also transforms living beings into decorative objects. Both items explore the tension between beauty and imprisonment.
