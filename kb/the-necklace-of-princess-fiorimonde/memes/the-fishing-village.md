# The Fishing Village

## Location and Context

The Fishing Village lies on "a lonely, rocky coast by the sea," reached by [[arasmon|Arasmon]] after walking far from the spellbound village and the transformed moorland (971-437). It is a small settlement perched on a rugged shoreline, perpetually vulnerable to the sea mists that roll in and endanger the fishing boats upon which the community depends. The village re[[the-exchange-of-gifts|presents]] a waypoint in [[arasmon|Arasmon]]'s long quest to find his lost wife [[chrysea|Chrysea]].

## Description

The village is small and exposed, situated directly on a rocky coast. Its most defining feature is the thick sea mist that frequently descends upon it — when [[arasmon|Arasmon]] arrives, "a thick mist had come up, and hung over the village, so that he could barely see the path before him as he walked" (971-437). A path leads from the village down to the beach where the fisherwomen gather. The village itself is modest, its life organized entirely around the rhythms of the sea and the fishing trade. There are no grand structures — only the practical dwellings of fishing families and the beach below, where boats launch and return.

> One day, towards night, when he had walked far, and was very tired, he came to a little village on a lonely, rocky coast by the sea, and he found that a thick mist had come up, and hung over the village, so that he could barely see the path before him as he walked. (971-437)

## Key Events

[[arasmon|Arasmon]] arrives at the village exhausted from his wanderings, still searching for [[chrysea|Chrysea]]. He finds the community in a state of anxious vigil — the fisherwomen stand on the beach straining to see through the mist, their husbands overdue from a fishing expedition. When [[arasmon|Arasmon]] asks about his wife, describing her [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]]en hair and white-and-[[wealth-and-poverty|gold]] gown, the women have seen no such person. [[arasmon|Arasmon]] joins their watch, but the boats' fate remains uncertain — he must continue his journey, his own search merging momentarily with the village's collective anxiety (971-437).

## Narrative Function

The Fishing Village serves as a mirror for [[arasmon|Arasmon]]'s quest. Just as the fisherwomen watch the sea for their missing husbands, [[arasmon|Arasmon]] searches the world for his lost wife. The mist that obscures the village and threatens the fishermen parallels the magical obfuscation that hides [[chrysea|Chrysea]]'s fate. The village's communal waiting — everyone watching together, sharing the same fear — contrasts with [[arasmon|Arasmon]]'s solitary wandering, emphasizing the isolation of his enchanted quest. Yet the brief connection with the fisherwomen offers a moment of shared humanity before he must continue alone.
