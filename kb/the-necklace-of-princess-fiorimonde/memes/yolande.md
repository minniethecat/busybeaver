# Yolande

Yolande is a key character in "[[the-necklace-of-princess-fiorimonde-story|The Necklace of Princess Fiorimonde]]," serving as the Princess's maid and ultimately as the catalyst for breaking [[the-gold-necklace|the necklace]]'s [[magic-and-witchcraft|enchantment]].

## Character Description

Yolande is introduced as [[princess-fiorimonde|Princess Fiorimonde]]'s "little maid." She is described as "a bright-faced girl with merry brown eyes, but she was not beautiful like [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]]." Significantly, De Morgan notes that Yolande "did not love her mistress, for she was afraid of her, and suspected her of her wicked ways."

## Observant and Perceptive

Yolande is the only person in [[the-royal-castle|the palace]] who notices the connection between the disappearing suitors and the growing number of beads on [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]]'s [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]] cord. She watches her mistress through the mirror as she combs her hair, observing [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]]'s secret laughter and the way she caresses the beads:

> "That is a wonderful bead on your Highness's cord," said Yolande, looking at its reflection in the mirror.

## Confronting Gervaise

When [[prince-florestan|Prince Florestan]]'s friend [[gervaise|Gervaise]] arrives, Yolande boldly tells him the [[truth-and-deception|truth]]:

> "She is a sorceress, and she wears the brave kings and princes who come to woo her, strung upon a cord round her neck."

She reveals she has been watching [[the-gold-necklace|the necklace]] grow: "first it was only an empty [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]] thread; then came [[king-pierrot|King Pierrot]]... then came [[prince-hildebrandt|Hildebrandt]]... ten beads hang upon the string, and tonight there will be eleven."

## The Plan

Yolande agrees to help [[gervaise|Gervaise]] see [[the-gold-necklace|the necklace]] by taking him to [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]]'s room while she sleeps. [[gervaise|Gervaise]] [[the-sacred-vow|promise]]s to marry her if they succeed in breaking the spell, and Yolande agrees: "That I will [[the-sacred-vow|promise]] gladly... but we must not speak or think of this till we have cut the cord from [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]]'s neck, and all her lovers are set free."

## Transformation

Tragically, when Yolande reaches to show [[gervaise|Gervaise]] the beads, her fingers accidentally close around [[the-gold-thread|the golden string]]. She is instantly transformed into the twelfth bead on [[the-gold-necklace|the necklace]], described as "brighter and clearer than the others, and of a warm red hue, like the red dress Yolande had worn."

## Heroic Role

Despite her transformation, Yolande's actions set in motion the events that ultimately lead to the breaking of the spell. Her courage in defying her terrifying mistress and her willingness to help [[gervaise|Gervaise]] make her one of De Morgan's most sympathetic and active female characters — a contrast to the passive princesses of conventional fairy tales.
