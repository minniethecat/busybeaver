# The Old Witch

The Old Witch is the hidden antagonist of the title story "[[the-necklace-of-princess-fiorimonde-story|The Necklace of Princess Fiorimonde]]," the source of [[princess-fiorimonde|Princess Fiorimonde]]'s sorcerous knowledge and the creator of the enchanted [[wealth-and-poverty|gold]] necklace.

## Character Overview

The witch dwells in isolation "in a hut on the side of a lonely mountain," unknown to anyone but [[princess-fiorimonde|Princess Fiorimonde]]. She is described in unequivocally negative terms:

> "This old witch was wicked and hideous, and no one but the King's daughter knew that she lived there."

She is the architect of [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]]'s beauty: "It was only the witch's arts which had made [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] so beautiful that there was no one like her in the world." In ex[[fate-and-transformation|change]], [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] assists the witch with "all her tricks" and guards the secret of her existence.

## Role in the Story

When [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] learns her father intends her to marry, she flies to the witch on an enchanted bird to seek help. She finds the witch wrapped in a gray cloak in her hut. The witch initially protests the intrusion — "Have I not told you I wished to be left alone for thirteen nights; why do you disturb me?" — but relents upon hearing [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]]'s plight.

The witch [[the-exchange-of-gifts|presents]] [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] with a choice of transformation methods for her suitors — dogs, birds, or beads — and [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] eagerly chooses [[the-gold-necklace|the necklace]]:

> "The necklace! the necklace! That will be best of all, to sling them upon a string and wear them around my throat."

Crucially, the witch issues a warning that foreshadows the story's climax:

> "But this is a dangerous play, for, unless you are very careful, you yourself may become a bead and hang upon the string with the others, and there you will remain till someone cuts the string, and draws you off."

## The Witch's Sabbath

Beyond providing [[the-gold-necklace|the necklace]], the witch leads dark rituals. [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] attends a witches' sabbath in the witch's underground chamber, accessed through a trapdoor in [[the-witchs-hut|the hut]] floor. The witch drums on an instrument, and "all her strange guests" — presumably other witches and dark creatures — dance and make merry "in a terrible way" until cockcrow.

Later, when [[gervaise|Gervaise]]'s challenge threatens [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]]'s composure, she again visits the witch for counsel. The witch urges her to act quickly: "Are you so silly or so vain as to be troubled because a Prince says idly what you know is not true? I tell you do not listen to him, but let him be slung to your chain as soon as may be."

## Key Relationships

- **[[princess-fiorimonde]]** — The witch's pupil, for whom she provides magical instruction, beauty enhancement, and the fatal necklace.
- **[[gervaise]]** — The hero who ultimately defeats [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]], fulfilling the witch's own prophecy about [[the-gold-necklace|the necklace]]'s danger.
- **The Dark Guests** — Unnamed supernatural beings who attend the witch's underground sabbaths.

## Significance

The Old Witch is the hidden wellspring of [[evil-as-deception|evil]] in the title story. She is never directly confronted or defeated — she exists as a force beyond the reach of the heroes, her influence flowing through [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]]. Her prophecy that [[princess-fiorimonde|Fiorimonde]] might herself become a bead establishes the story's moral logic: [[evil-as-deception|evil]] is self-consuming, and those who trap others may ultimately trap themselves. She re[[the-exchange-of-gifts|presents]] the archetype of the corrupting mentor, a dark inversion of the fairy godmother figure common in traditional fairy tales.
