# The Wizard's Tower

## Location and Context

The Wizard's Tower stands on a high hill in the kingdom governed by the King and Queen who were parents to [[prince-michael|Prince Michael]] (004-027–028). It is a landmark visible from the surrounding countryside, and its occupant — an ancient wizard of unknown age — was feared by the King and Queen, who never ventured near it (004-029).

Only [[prince-michael|Prince Michael]] knew the old sorcerer well and "did not fear him at all, but went up and down the tower as he chose" (004-029).

## Description

The tower is an architectural marvel and mystery: it is made of one huge stone, with no joins anywhere in its construction, and no one knew how it had been built (004-028). The tower has no windows — its interior stair is a narrow, dark, winding passageway, so narrow that only one person could walk in it at a time (004-030). This pitch-dark staircase leads straight up the centre to the single room at the top.

The wizard's chamber at the summit is brilliantly lit by a lamp of the old man's own invention — a lamp that burns no oil and holds no wick, but is filled each day with sunbeams that shine through the night after the sun has set (004-031). The light is intense: the room is "as light as day."

At its centre sits the wizard himself — a figure of pure white: white beard flowing nearly to his feet, white skin, white gown, and colourless eyes "as bright as two candles" (004-032). Beside him rests an enormous book of coloured pictures — living portraits of every man and woman in the world, each figure about three inches high, walking and moving across the page as though alive (004-033–035).

> "No man knew his age, for he had dwelt there for hundreds of years, and no one knew how the tower had been built, for it was made of one huge stone, and there were no joins in it at all" (004-028).

## Key Events

### Prince Michael's Visit

One bright moonlight night, [[prince-michael|Prince Michael]] saw a bright light shining from the tower top and resolved to visit the wizard. He entered through a little door at the base and climbed the dark winding stair to the chamber above (004-030).

### The Book of Living Portraits

The wizard showed [[prince-michael|Michael]] his great book — a volume filled with living, moving portraits of every person in the world. It was from this book that [[prince-michael|Michael]] later identified the fairy who had stolen [[princess-joan|Princess Joan]]'s heart, learning that it was hidden in an egg inside a crystal casket (004-035).

### After the Quest

When [[prince-michael|Michael]] returned victorious from recovering [[princess-joan|Princess Joan]]'s heart, messengers were sent to "his father and mother and the old wizard" to share the news, confirming the wizard's ongoing place in the kingdom's life as a trusted advisor and observer (004-059).

## Narrative Function

The Wizard's Tower functions as a threshold between the mundane and the magical. Its construction from a single jointless stone marks it as outside ordinary architecture; its sunbeam-powered lamp re[[the-exchange-of-gifts|presents]] the fusion of natural light with arcane knowledge. The tower is both sanctuary (for the ancient wizard's centuries of study) and resource (providing [[prince-michael|Michael]] with the knowledge essential to his quest).

The tower's spatial design — the narrow, dark ascent leading to a brilliantly lit chamber — mirrors [[the-heros-journey|the journey]] from ignorance to enlightenment, and from fear (the King and Queen's avoidance) to understanding ([[prince-michael|Michael]]'s friendship with the wizard).
