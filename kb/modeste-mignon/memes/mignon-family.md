# Mignon Family

The **Mignon family** consists of Charles Mignon, his wife Madame Mignon, and their daughter Modeste Mignon. They are the central family in Honoré de Balzac's novel *Modeste Mignon*.

## Overview

Charles Mignon, formerly the richest merchant in Le Havre and known as the Comte de La Bastie, built a celebrated villa in Ingouville. After his financial ruin and exile to China, the family was forced to sell the villa to Monsieur Vilquin and move to a modest chalet. The family's fall from fortune and subsequent restoration forms the economic backdrop of the novel.

## Thematic Significance

The Mignon family's trajectory — from wealth to ruin to restoration — embodies the novel's themes of [[fortune]], [[pride]], [[social-mobility]], and the tension between provincial life and Parisian society. Charles Mignon's return from China with a rebuilt fortune drives the plot's resolution.
