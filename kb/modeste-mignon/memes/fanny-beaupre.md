# Fanny Beaupré

Fanny Beaupré is a Parisian actress who appears as a passing reference in *Modeste Mignon*. Her presence in the novel is minimal — she is "supposed" to be someone of note (028-019) — but she recurs elsewhere in Balzac's fictional universe, appearing more substantially in *A Start in Life* (Un Début dans la vie).

## Role in the Novel

Fanny Beaupré is mentioned in passing within the novel's Parisian scenes, where she is referenced as "an actress who was supposed" to have some significance (028-019). Her function in the narrative is minimal — she is part of the Parisian backdrop against which the novel's central drama unfolds, a name dropped to evoke the world of the theatre and its demi-monde.

## Balzacian Context

Fanny Beaupré is one of Balzac's recurring characters — figures who drift through multiple novels of *La Comédie Humaine*, sometimes as central figures, sometimes as mere names. She appears most significantly in *A Start in Life* (1842), where she plays a more developed role. Her cameo in *Modeste Mignon* (1844) is typical of Balzac's technique of cross-referencing his own fictional universe, lending depth and verisimilitude to both works.
