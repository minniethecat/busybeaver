# Senator Wise

**Senator Wise** is a United States Senator mentioned in Owen Wister's *[[the-virginian|The Virginian]]*. He appears not as a character in the novel's action but as a figure invoked by the Virginian during a comic storytelling sequence aboard the eastbound train in Chapter XVI, where the Virginian uses the Senator's name as a conversational feint in his verbal sparring with [[trampas|Trampas]].

## Role in the Narrative

Senator Wise exists in the novel exclusively as a name in the Virginian's repertoire of anecdotes. During [[the-railroad|the train]] journey east, as the Virginian tells stories of his experiences in Tulare, California—a "dead county" where the only money was in a frog-farming venture [016-172]—he employs a deliberate narrative tactic. Pausing to eat, the Virginian makes "a feint at wandering to new fields of anecdote":

> "Talkin' of senators," he resumed, "Senator Wise—" [016-173]

Before he can deliver the Senator Wise story, he is interrupted by a question about wages from one of [[trampas|Trampas]]'s faction, then by [[trampas|Trampas]] himself attempting to reclaim the conversational ground. Each time the Virginian returns to his unfinished anecdote:

> "How much? Why, I never knew what the foreman got. The regular hands got a hundred. Senator Wise—" [016-175]

> "Why, it was wet an' muddy work, yu' see. A man risked rheumatism some. He risked it a good deal. Well, I was going to tell about Senator Wise. When Senator Wise was speaking of his visit to Alaska—" [016-177]

The Senator Wise story is never actually told; it remains permanently suspended as the Virginian's narrative weapon of misdirection. Each return to "Senator Wise" signals the Virginian's narrative control over the scene—he can resume or abandon a story at will, keeping [[trampas|Trampas]] and his allies off-balance.

## Narrative Function

The device of the permanently deferred anecdote serves several purposes in the chapter:

1. **Comic pacing**: [[the-virginian|The Virginian]]'s repeated attempts to begin the Senator Wise story, each time interrupted by the [[trampas|Trampas]] faction's attempts to deflect the conversation, creates a rhythmic comic pattern.

2. **Character revelation**: [[the-virginian|The Virginian]]'s skill at narrative manipulation—teasing an anecdote, setting it aside, returning to it—demonstrates the same tactical intelligence he displays in physical confrontations.

3. **Social commentary**: The invocation of "Senator Wise" suggests the Virginian's familiarity with political figures and Eastern society, countering any assumption that he is merely a provincial [[the-cowpuncher|cowboy]].

## Historical Context

The name "Senator Wise" may allude to a real or composite political figure of the late nineteenth-century American West. In the novel's fictional world, Senator Wise represents the intersection of Western frontier life with Eastern political power—a connection embodied in the Virginian himself, who moves between these worlds through his partnership with [[judge-henry|Judge Henry]] and his marriage to [[molly-stark-wood|Molly Wood]].
