# The Baby-Swapping Incident

The **baby-swapping incident** is one of the most celebrated comic episodes in Owen Wister's *[[the-virginian|The Virginian]]*. In the aftermath of the Goose Egg barbecue and dance, [[lin-mclean|Lin McLean]] — or rather, the Virginian acting through McLean — shuffles the sleeping infants of the [[bear-creek|Bear Creek]] families, creating a night of chaos that culminates in a dawn gathering of outraged parents and a confession delivered with characteristic Virginian flair.

## The Discovery

The incident unfolds through the experience of James and Lizzie Westfall, who return from the dance to find strangers in their children's blankets. Mrs. Westfall's outraged catalog of the offense — [[lin-mclean|Lin McLean]] has "mixed them up with other people's coughing, unhealthy brats" — is matched by her husband's irrepressible laughter. "As this crime against society loomed clear to [[james-westfall|James Westfall]]'s understanding, he sat down on the nearest piece of furniture, and heedless of his wife's tears and his exchanged children, broke into unregenerate laughter" (018-772).

## The Gathering at the Swintons'

By dawn, nearly every family on [[bear-creek|Bear Creek]] has discovered the substitution. The Taylors, the Carmodys, [[uncle-hughey|Uncle Hughey]] and his wife, Mr. Dow (whose wife is prone to fits), the Westfalls, the Thomases — all converge on the Swintons' in various states of outrage. "By sunrise, what with fathers and mothers and spectators and loud offspring, there was gathered such a meeting as has seldom been before among the generations of speaking men" (018-781).

The sorting of the infants requires a "ladies' caucus" from which the men are excluded, [[james-westfall|James Westfall]] observes that McLean had not merely "swapped the duds; he had shuffled the whole doggone deck" (018-783).

## The Virginian's Confession

The search party sent after [[lin-mclean|Lin McLean]] returns with a notice pegged to a tree reading "God bless our home!" — a mockery that inflames the mothers' desire for vengeance. It is at this moment that the Virginian makes his confession:

> "Yes," spoke the Virginian in their midst, "that wasn't sort o' right. Especially as I am the man you're huntin'."
> （018-791）

The confession, delivered with the timing of a cavalry charge, disarms the assembled fathers. The mothers remain less forgiving, but the men — "being as yet more their wives' lovers than their children's parents" — have begun "to see the mirthful side of the adventure."

## Significance

The baby-swapping episode serves several functions in the novel. It provides broad comedy after the intensity of the Goose Egg dance. It reveals the Virginian's capacity for mischief and his skill at managing people — his confession at the right moment transforms potential violence into laughter. And it showcases the [[bear-creek|Bear Creek]] community in its entirety, turning the scattered ranches into a temporary village united by shared outrage and shared humor.
