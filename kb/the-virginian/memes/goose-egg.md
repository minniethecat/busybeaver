# Goose Egg Ranch

**Goose Egg Ranch** (also called the "Goose Egg outfit") is the Stanton Brothers' cattle ranch on [[bear-creek|Bear Creek]] in Owen Wister's *[[the-virginian|The Virginian]]*. It serves as the setting for the novel's most memorable social event — the barbecue and dance of Chapter IX–X, where the [[the-cowpuncher|cowboy]]s of the region gather, where [[honey-wiggin|Honey Wiggin]] holds court as unofficial master of ceremonies, and where the Virginian's pursuit of [[molly-stark-wood|Molly Wood]] begins in earnest.

## The Barbecue

The Goose Egg barbecue is occasioned by the great cattle boom: steers have "jumped to seventy-five" dollars a head, and "on the strength of this vigorous price... the Stanton Brothers were giving a barbecue at the Goose Egg outfit, their ranch on [[bear-creek|Bear Creek]]" (009-002). The whole neighborhood is invited, and men come from forty miles around — the Virginian rides a hundred and eighteen miles to attend.

The barbecue is described with Wister's characteristic blend of the epic and the intimate: a steer roasting whole over an open fire, the "bed of flame under it" showing "steadily brighter against the dusk," while inside the Goose Egg kitchen "many small delicacies were preparing" (009-016). The scene captures the abundance and hospitality of the cattle country at its most prosperous and generous.

## The Dance

After the barbecue, the dance begins — and with it, the novel's most extended comic set-piece. [[honey-wiggin|Honey Wiggin]], "enjoying himself" with an audience of [[the-cowpuncher|cowboy]]s, systematically catalogs the failed romantic overtures of every man present toward the new schoolmarm. The Goose Egg dance is where the community's collective fascination with [[molly-stark-wood|Molly Wood]] is made explicit, and where the Virginian — arriving late, dressed in the "unnecessarily excellent" trousers and scarf he bought for the occasion — stakes his claim.

The dance also precipitates the baby-swapping prank that will occupy Chapter XVIII. The Goose Egg gathering, in other words, is the narrative catalyst for much of the novel's central comic and romantic action.

## The Goose Egg as Social Hub

The Goose Egg Ranch functions as the social center of the [[bear-creek|Bear Creek]] community. It is here that the scattered ranches' populations converge — not for business, as at the roundup, but for pleasure and courtship. The Stanton Brothers' hospitality makes the Goose Egg the natural gathering place, and the barbecue/dance combination — food, drink, music, and dancing — provides the setting in which the novel's romantic plot can properly launch.

## Approach and Departure

[[the-virginian|The Virginian]]'s approach to the Goose Egg is charged with anticipation: he has bought new clothes, he has been thinking about [[molly-stark-wood|the schoolmarm]] for months, and he is riding into a social situation that he knows will matter. His departure — with the promise of further meetings — sets the novel's central relationship in motion.
