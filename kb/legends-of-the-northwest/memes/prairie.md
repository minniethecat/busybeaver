# Prairie

The prairie landscape, home of the [[dakota|Dakotas]] and setting of Gordon [[legend|legends]].

## Cultural Role

Gordon's [[legend|legends]] are set in the world of the [[dakota|Dakota]] — a world of rivers, prairies, and the animals that sustained [[dakota|Dakota]] life. The prairie is woven into the fabric of the narratives.

## In the Book

References to prairies appear throughout *[[legend|Legend]]s of the Northwest*, reflecting their importance in [[dakota|Dakota]] daily life and oral tradition. Gordon's preface notes his study of [[dakota|Dakota]] [[tradition|customs]] and [[tradition|traditions]] (P01-007), grounded in the material culture of the prairie and its animals.

The [[legend|legends]] preserve a way of life centered on the prairie before "the day of the [[dakota|Dakotas]]" passed (P01-046).


Gordon describes the prairie as the ancestral home of the [[dakota|Dakotas]], the landscape that shaped their way of life. The [[legend|legends]] are set against this vast backdrop (P01-046).
