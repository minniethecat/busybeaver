# Wakan

Wakan is the fundamental [[dakota|Dakota]] concept of sacred or mysterious power, pervading all aspects of traditional [[dakota|Dakota]] [[soul|spirit]]uality and medicine.

## Definition

In [[dakota|Dakota]] belief, *Wakan* denotes that which is sacred, mysterious, or possessed of [[soul|spirit]]ual power. The term appears in many compound forms throughout the [[dakota|Dakota]] vocabulary:

- **Wakan Tanka** — the Great Spirit, the supreme sacred power. Gordon notes the [[dakota|Dakotas]] had no "Wakan Tanka" or "Wakan-péta" (fire [[soul|spirit]]) in their original belief system (018-002).
- **[[medicine-man|Wicasta Wakan]]** — "medicine man," a healer who casts out "unclean" [[spirit|spirits]] through sacred power (017-044).
- **Tahkoo Wakan** — "The Sacred Thing," the title of Riggs' [[dakota|Dakota]]-language gospel (008-200, P01-063).
- **[[wakan-teepee-site|Wakan Teepee]]** — "[[wakan-teepee|Sacred Lodge]]," a dwelling associated with sacred ceremonies (015-242).

## In the Book

Gordon's preface explains that he devoted years to studying [[dakota|Dakota]] language, history, [[tradition|traditions]], [[tradition|customs]], and [[superstition|superstitions]] (P01-007). The concept of *Wakan* underlies much of the [[soul|spirit]]ual dimension of the [[legend|legends]] he collected.

The missionaries who worked among the [[dakota|Dakotas]] imported the term "Wakan Tanka" as a translation for "Great Spirit" (017-018), adapting existing [[dakota|Dakota]] [[soul|spirit]]ual vocabulary to Christian concepts.
