# Medicine Pouch

The Medicine Pouch — "the magical pouch of the [[spirit|spirits]]" — is a sacred object in [[dakota|Dakota]] [[soul|spirit]]ual tradition, given by the deity [[unktéhee|Unktéhee]] to a chosen band of [[dakota|Dakota]] warriors as part of a covenant of sacred laws (008-170).

## Definition / Identity

The Medicine Pouch was a sacred bundle believed to contain [[soul|spirit]]ual power ([[wakan|Wakan]]). In Gordon's *[[legend|Legends of the Northwest]]*, it is presented alongside the "magical bone" as a gift from [[unktéhee|Unktéhee]], the [[dakota|Dakota]] water deity, to a select group of warriors (008-169).

- **Origin**: Gift from [[unktéhee|Unktéhee]], [[dakota|Dakota]] water deity
- **Language**: [[dakota|Dakota]]
- **Domain**: [[soul|spirit]]uality / ritual / sacred-object

## In the Book

The Medicine Pouch appears in *[[winona|Winona]]*, where [[unktéhee|Unktéhee]] appears to a band of chosen men and proclaims them "sons of [[unktéhee|Unktéhee]]" and "lords of the beasts and the birds, and the fishes that swim in the waters" (008-145). The deity then bestows two sacred objects — the magical bone and the magical pouch — along with a code of conduct.

### Role in the Legends

The giving of the Medicine Pouch marks a foundational moment in Gordon's depiction of [[dakota|Dakota]] [[soul|spirit]]uality. [[unktéhee|Unktéhee]] declares: "Now I give you the magical bone and the magical pouch of the [[spirit|spirits]]" (008-170). The pouch is not merely an object but carries binding [[soul|spirit]]ual laws: "Ye shall honor the pouch and the giver" (008-172). The commandments that follow — to walk as brothers, to protect the innocent, to hold sacred the wife of a brother — form a moral code anchored in the sacredness of the pouch.

The passage situates the Medicine Pouch within the broader [[dakota-mythology|Dakota Mythology]] framework, where [[unktéhee|Unktéhee]] is acknowledged as a giver of [[soul|spirit]]ual power and law.

### Representative Passages

> "Now I give you the magical bone and the magical pouch of the spirits. And these are the laws ye shall heed: Ye shall honor the pouch and the giver." — (008-170)
