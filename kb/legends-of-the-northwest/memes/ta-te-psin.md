# Ta-té-psin

An aged [[dakota|Dakota]] brave and the father of [[winona|Winona]], the heroine of Gordon's "[[winona|Winona]]" legend (Chapters VI–XII). Ta-té-psin is portrayed with dignity — a once-swift hunter now in his final years, whose love for his daughter and concern for her safety drive key moments in the legend.

## 身份与背景

- **Tribe / Nation**: [[dakota|Dakota]] ([[dakota|Sioux]])
- **Role**: Elder, father, provider
- **Family**: Daughter: [[winona|Winona]] (006-082)

## 在书中的角色

### 相关传奇

Ta-té-psin appears throughout the "[[winona|Winona]]" legend cycle as a grounding figure of paternal authority. His lodge is the domestic center of the early narrative, and his declining years add urgency to the question of [[winona|Winona]]'s future.

### 关键事件

[[winona|Winona]] was born in his teepee: "In the tee of Ta-té-psin was born [[winona|Winona]]" (006-082). She is described as "the pride of the aged Ta-té-psin" (006-107), and as "Ta-té-psin's compassionate daughter" (007-014).

Ta-té-psin is introduced as aging but still formidable: "Ta-té-psin, the aged brave, bends" (010-237), and the narrative notes "the days of Ta-té-psin are brief" (010-245). Yet in his youth he was swift: "in the lodge of swift footed Ta-té-psin" (010-265), and "ran the feet of Ta-té-psin untiring" (010-271).

As [[winona|Winona]] faces the choice between [[tamdoka|Tamdóka]] and DuLuth, Ta-té-psin's reaction is one of protective anger: "half angrily muttered Ta-té-psin" (011-035). He provides for his household: "In the tee of Ta-té-psin the store of wild-rice" (011-102). His mortality weighs on the narrative: "Ta-té-psin will die in his tee" (011-118).

Ta-té-psin's bow becomes a significant object: "The bow of Ta-té-psin" is taken by [[winona|Winona]] (011-130), and later "Ta-té-psin's bow lay at her feet" (011-156) — a symbolic transfer of agency from father to daughter. The legend notes that "Ta-té-psin's lodge laughed with abundance" (011-273) at a moment of seeming peace before the tragedy.

## 历史/文化背景

Ta-té-psin is a legendary figure. His name follows [[dakota|Dakota]] naming conventions. He represents the archetype of the aged father in [[dakota|Dakota]] oral tradition — the provider whose life is winding down, whose concern for his daughter's future drives the plot. His bow, passed to [[winona|Winona]], symbolizes both patriarchal protection and the transfer of agency to the next generation.
