The Holy Ghost Mission was a Jesuit mission established at La Pointe on Waug-a-bá-mé island in Cha-quam-egon Bay, on the shores of Lake Superior (018-099, 018-100). It was among the earliest Christian missions in the upper Great Lakes region.

## Foundation

The mission was founded by the Jesuits about the year 1660 (018-101). Its location on Waug-a-bá-mé — meaning "winding view" in the Ojibway language — overlooked the beautiful bay of Cha-quam-egon (018-100).

## Priests

Father René Menard was the first priest stationed at the mission (018-101). After Menard disappeared in the wilderness, Father Claude Allouëz permanently established the mission in 1665 (018-103). The celebrated Father Jacques Marquette succeeded Allouëz on September 13, 1669 (018-103).

## In Gordon's Poem

The poem describes a journey "to the Holy Ghost Mission" where "the Black Robes, the brave shepherds, fed their wild sheep on the isle Wau-ga-bá-mé" (010-185, 010-186). This poetic image captures the mission as a remote spiritual outpost on the island, serving the Native peoples of the Lake Superior region.
