# Dakota Maiden

The [[dakota|Dakota]] maidens — including the fifty maidens of the [[feast-of-the-virgins|Feast of the Virgins]] — who appear throughout the [[legend|legends]].

## Connection to the Legends

These figures appear in or contributed to the preservation of *[[legend|Legend]]s of the Northwest*. Gordon's years of study among the [[dakota|Dakotas]] (P01-007) informed his portrayal of [[dakota|Dakota]] and [[ojibway|Ojibway]] characters.

## In the Book

The [[legend|legends]] depict a world populated by warriors, maidens, chiefs, and editors whose combined efforts preserved these [[dakota|Dakota]] and [[ojibway|Ojibway]] [[tradition|traditions]] in English verse.


Fifty merry [[dakota|Dakota]] maidens appear in the [[feast-of-the-virgins|Feast of the Virgins]] (001-087). The maidens were central to the ceremonial [[dance|dances]] and courtship rituals (003-108). [[winona|Wiwaste]], the fairest [[dakota|Dakota]] maiden (001-151), is the most fully realized maiden character.
