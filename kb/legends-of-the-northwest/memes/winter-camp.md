# Winter Camp

The seasonal winter encampment of [[dakota|Dakota]] bands, a recurring event in the annual cycle.

The [[dakota|Dakota]] followed a seasonal round, with winter camps forming an important part of their yearly cycle. These gatherings were occasions for storytelling — the oral [[legend|legends]] that Gordon later collected (P01-007).

## In Gordon's Legends

Gordon's [[legend|legends]] capture these events within the broader narrative of [[dakota|Dakota]] life. His preface notes his study of [[dakota|Dakota]] [[tradition|customs]] (P01-007) and his debt to Riggs' ethnographic work (P01-063).

## Seasonal Life in the Winter Camp

The winter camp was a time of storytelling, when Dakota oral tradition passed from elders to the young. These gatherings preserved the legends — tales of [[heyoka|Heyóka]], [[unktéhee|Unktéhee]], and the great spirit-beings — that Gordon later collected and rendered into English verse (P01-007).

Long winter evenings, with families gathered around the camp-fire (009-269), were the schoolroom of Dakota culture. Winter was the season when [[wa-zi-ya|Wa-zí-ya]], the Winter-maker, held dominion, advancing southward from his frozen throne in the North (015-209–216).

The camp's survival through the "famine of winter" when "the wolf shall prowl" (004-169) depended on autumn provisions, hunting skill, and the spiritual guidance of the [[medicine-man|medicine men]].
