# Prophet's Vision

A visionary experience by a [[dakota|Dakota]] holy man, an event at the boundary of [[soul|spirit]]ual and historical.

[[dakota|Dakota]] holy men and prophets experienced visions that guided their people. These [[soul|spirit]]ual events were documented in the oral [[tradition|traditions]] Gordon collected (P01-007). The [[wakan|Wakan]] power manifested through such visionary experiences (017-044).

## In Gordon's Legends

Gordon's [[legend|legends]] capture these events within the broader narrative of [[dakota|Dakota]] life. His preface notes his study of [[dakota|Dakota]] [[tradition|customs]] (P01-007) and his debt to Riggs' ethnographic work (P01-063).

## Visions in Dakota Tradition

Visions and prophetic dreams held a central place in Dakota spiritual life. The [[medicine-man|medicine men]] interpreted visions and omens as messages from the spirit world (006-212). These interpretations could determine the course of war parties, hunting expeditions, and ceremonial timing.

In the [[winona|Winona]] legend cycle, prophetic visions foreshadow tragedy. The phantoms that flee from a maiden's vision (011-137) and the declarations of spirit-beings shape mortal decisions. For the Dakota, a vision was not a private psychological event — it was a public, consequential communication from the [[wakan|Wakan]] realm that demanded communal response.
