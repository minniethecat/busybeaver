# Bison

The American bison, central to [[dakota|Dakota]] subsistence and culture.

## Cultural Role

Gordon's [[legend|legends]] are set in the world of the [[dakota|Dakota]] — a world of rivers, prairies, and the animals that sustained [[dakota|Dakota]] life. The bison is woven into the fabric of the narratives.

## In the Book

References to bisons appear throughout *[[legend|Legend]]s of the Northwest*, reflecting their importance in [[dakota|Dakota]] daily life and oral tradition. Gordon's preface notes his study of [[dakota|Dakota]] [[tradition|customs]] and [[tradition|traditions]] (P01-007), grounded in the material culture of the prairie and its animals.

The [[legend|legends]] preserve a way of life centered on the bison before "the day of the [[dakota|Dakotas]]" passed (P01-046).


Hunters from the land of [[spirit|spirits]] seek the bison and the deer, connecting the animal to [[dakota|Dakota]] [[soul|spirit]]ual beliefs (001-047). Gordon's [[legend|legends]] preserve this world where the bison was central to survival.
