# Ojibway Raid

A surprise attack by [[ojibway|Ojibway]] warriors on a [[dakota|Dakota]] village — an event at the boundary of warfare and legend.

The crafty and creeping [[ojibway-nation|Ojibways]] launched raids on [[dakota|Dakota]] settlements (012-128). These raids drove the conflict that forms the backdrop of the [[winona|Winona]] legend cycle (005-109).

## In Gordon's Legends

Gordon's [[legend|legends]] capture these events within the broader narrative of [[dakota|Dakota]] life. His preface notes his study of [[dakota|Dakota]] [[tradition|customs]] (P01-007) and his debt to Riggs' ethnographic work (P01-063).

## The Dakota-Ojibway Conflict

The intertribal warfare between the [[dakota|Dakota]] and [[ojibway|Ojibway]] nations forms the historical backbone of many legends Gordon preserves. The Ojibway, armed earlier with European firearms through the [[fur-trade|fur trade]], pushed westward into Dakota lands, creating a century-long conflict that shaped the upper Mississippi region.

These raids were not merely military events — they became the dramatic setting for the [[winona|Winona]] legend cycle (005-109) and form the backdrop against which figures like [[wazi-kute|Wazí-kuté]] and [[tamdoka|Tamdóka]] emerge. Gordon captures the raiders as "creeping" through the landscape (012-128), reflecting the Dakota perspective on Ojibway stealth tactics.
