# Nicollet Island

Nicollet Island is an island in the [[mississippi-river|Mississippi River]] just above [[falls-of-st-anthony|the Falls]] of St. Anthony (013-051), known to the [[dakota|Dakota]] people as **Wi-ta Waste** ("Beautiful Island") (007-075) and also called Fairy Island (013-070). It appears prominently in the [[winona|Winona]] narrative (007-061–068) and the [[legend-of-the-falls|Legend of the Falls]] (013-010, 013-066–067).

## 地理位置

Nicollet Island lies in the [[mississippi-river|Mississippi River]] "just above [[falls-of-st-anthony|the Falls]]" of St. Anthony in present-day Minneapolis, [[minnesota|Minnesota]] (013-051). The [[dakota|Dakota]] called the island "Wi-ta Waste" — the Beautiful Island (007-075). Nearby [[spirit-island|Spirit Island]] (Wanagi-wita) sits "just below [[falls-of-st-anthony|the Falls]]" (007-073), and the two islands frame the cataract in [[dakota|Dakota]] geography.

- **[[dakota|Dakota]] Name**: Wi-ta Waste / Wee tah Wah-stay — "Beautiful Island" (007-075, 013-050)
- **Modern Name**: Nicollet Island
- **Region**: Minneapolis, [[minnesota|Minnesota]], on the [[mississippi-river|Mississippi River]]

## 在书中的角色

### 相关传说

Nicollet Island appears in two of Gordon's [[legend|legends]]:

**[[winona|Winona]]**: The island is the setting where [[winona|Winona]] and other [[dakota|Dakota]] maidens bathed and played in the river waters at the end of the day (007-061–068). The explorer [[daniel-duluth|Daniel DuLuth]] and his companions passed by the island on their river journey, singing French [[voyageurs|voyageur]] [[song|songs]] as they paddled (007-078–084).

**The [[legend-of-the-falls|Legend of the Falls]]**: After [[winona|Winona]] plunges over the falls, her [[soul|spirit]] and that of her child are said to haunt the island. Gordon describes a "snow white fawn and doe" walking "in the woods on Fairy Island" — [[spirit|spirits]] of the mother and child "sadly seeking evermore" (013-066–067). The island appears as "Wita-Wâstè" described as the "queen of fairy islands" (013-010).

### 叙事功能

Nicollet Island serves as a site of beauty and joy in the [[winona|Winona]] narrative — the place where [[dakota|Dakota]] maidens celebrate life — and transforms into a haunted, sorrowful location in the [[legend-of-the-falls|Legend of the Falls]], where the [[spirit|spirits]] of the dead wander under moonlight (013-071–074). The island thus bridges the two [[legend|legends]], marking the transition from [[winona|Winona]]'s life to her afterlife as a [[soul|spirit]].

## 历史与文化意义

The [[dakota|Dakota]] name "Wi-ta Waste" reflects the island's natural beauty as a wooded island in the [[mississippi-river|Mississippi River]]. The island's proximity to [[falls-of-st-anthony|the Falls]] of St. Anthony — a sacred site — and [[spirit-island|Spirit Island]] gave it a place within the [[dakota|Dakota]] sacred landscape. The area around the falls was known as a [[soul|spirit]]ual place where war-eagles nested and [[spirit|spirits]] dwelled (007-072–074).

The island's proximity to [[falls-of-st-anthony|the Falls]] of St. Anthony placed it near the sacred site of Wanagi-wita ([[spirit-island|Spirit Island]]), and the [[dakota|Dakota]] considered the area inhabited by [[spirit|spirits]] (013-064, 013-069).
