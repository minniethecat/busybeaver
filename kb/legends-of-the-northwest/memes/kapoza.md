# Kapóza

Kapóza was the principal village of the Light-foot, or Kapóza band of Dakotas, located near the Falls of St. Anthony (001-107)(015-235). It appears throughout Gordon's poetic cycle as an important Dakota settlement, standing alongside Mille Lacs and the Falls of St. Anthony as one of the notable places in Dakota country (018-139).

## Historical Significance

Kapóza was the home village of [[Little Crow]], the Dakota chief whose daughter Winona is the tragic heroine of the Winona legend (001-102)(001-152). The village served as the seat of the Kapóza band, whose chiefs were traditionally drawn from the "Little Crow" lineage (015-234).

A notable incident recorded in the corpus tells of a killing near Kapóza by the son of Cetan-Wakawa-mâni, suggesting the village and its surroundings were the scene of inter-band conflict (016-135).

## In the Legend Cycle

The village is woven throughout Gordon's narrative poems. In the Winona cycle, Kapóza is the home of the tall chief [[Wazi-kuté]] (007-246), and the village appears as a place from which warriors depart and to which they return (004-126)(007-220). The "tees of Kapóza" (teepees) are home to a maiden in the Sea-Gull legend (005-149).

Kapóza's chief, the "false Little Crow," figures in the tragic outcome of Winona's story — his broken promises contribute to her desperate leap from the cliff (005-200).

## Location

The village was situated near the Falls of St. Anthony on the Mississippi River, in what is now the Saint Paul, Minnesota area. The exact location places it at a strategic point along the river, where Dakota bands gathered and traded. It lay within easy reach of the hunting grounds referenced in the "Prelude" to Gordon's collection (001-107).
