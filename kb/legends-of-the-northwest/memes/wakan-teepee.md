The Wakan Teepee (Sacred Lodge) is the ceremonial sacred space of the [[dakota|Dakotas]] — both a physical structure for the Wakan Wacipee (Sacred [[dance|Dance]]) and, by extension, any place of concentrated [[soul|spirit]]ual power, including the cave at St. Paul known to the [[dakota|Dakotas]] as Wakan Teepee (018-031).

## Definition / Identity

Wakan Teepee translates as "sacred lodge" or "mystery dwelling" (016-148). It is the designated space for the most important [[dakota|Dakota]] ceremonies: "the Wakan Wacipee — or Sacred [[dance|Dance]] — is called the Wakan Teepee" (018-030), indicating that the dance and its sacred space are conceptually unified.

- **Origin**: [[dakota|Dakota]] ceremonial tradition; the concept predates European contact
- **Language**: [[dakota|Dakota]] ([[wakan|Wakan]] = sacred/mystery, Teepee = dwelling/lodge)
- **Domain**: ceremony / [[soul|spirit]]uality / sacred-space

The term extends to naturally sacred places: Carver's Cave at St. Paul was also called Wakan Teepee by the [[dakota|Dakotas]] (018-031). The Wakan Teepee is where sacred and social intersect — site of ceremonial dance, community gathering place, and burial ground for chiefs.

## In the Book

Gordon references the Wakan Teepee in several contexts. In "[[winona|Winona]]," the lights of the "Teepee Wakán" signal ceremonial gathering (008-113). The historical notes identify it as the burial site of chiefs and their kin (015-242), and the appendix documents the relationship between the Sacred [[dance|Dance]] and the Sacred Lodge (018-030, 018-031).

### Role in the Legends

In the poetic narrative, the Teepee Wakán appears as a beacon: "Lo the lights in the 'Teepee Wakán!'" (008-113) — a visible signal of ceremonial activity that draws the community.

The scholarly apparatus expands this into a broader cultural institution. The Wakan Teepee is a dance ground, a burial site, and a natural cave (Carver's Cave) recognized as inherently sacred (018-031). This layering of meanings — ceremonial, funerary, geographical — illustrates the density of the [[dakota|Dakota]] concept of [[wakan|Wakan]] (sacred power) as it attaches to specific places.

### Representative Passages

> "Lo the lights in the 'Teepee Wakán!'" (008-113)

> "They were both buried with their kindred near the 'Wakan Teepee,'" (015-242)

> "the Wakan Wacipee — or Sacred Dance — is called the Wakan Teepee" (018-030)
