# Anpétuwee

Anpétuwee is the [[dakota|Dakota]] sun deity — the more powerful of the twin sky brothers, worshipped as the father of life through the severe Sun-[[dance|Dance]] ceremony (017-087, 017-091).

## Definition / Identity

In [[dakota|Dakota]] cosmology, Anpétuwee is the day-sun, distinguished from his twin brother Han-yé-tuwee (the night-sun, or moon). The [[dakota|Dakotas]] believe the sun to be "beneficent and kind," yet they worship him through one of the most extreme ceremonies in their religious practice (017-091).

- **Origin**: [[dakota|Dakota]] cosmology — Anpétuwee and Han-yé-tuwee are twin brothers (017-087)
- **Language**: [[dakota|Dakota]] (*Anpétuwee* — "day sun")
- **Domain**: [[soul|spirit]]uality / deity / celestial

Anpétuwee is the more powerful of the twins: "Han-yé-tuwee receives his power from his brother and obeys him" (017-088). Every morning, "Anpétuwee issues from the lodge of Han-nan-na (the Morning) and begins his journey over the sky" (017-096–017-097). The moon watches over the earth while Anpétuwee sleeps (017-089).

## In the Book

Gordon invokes Anpétuwee in the poetic landscape of the [[winona|Winona]] narrative and provides scholarly documentation in his footnotes and appendix. The deity appears as both a celestial presence marking the passage of time and as an object of [[dakota|Dakota]] worship described with ethnographic precision.

### Role in the Legends

In the [[winona|Winona]] narrative, Anpétuwee rises as the morning light over the [[dakota|Dakota]] village: "Till Anpétuwee rose in the east" (008-206) and "Anpétuwee walked on his journey" (008-231). These appearances anchor the narrative in the [[dakota|Dakota]] cosmic order, where the sun's daily journey structures both ritual time and ordinary life.

The [[legend|legendary]] warrior's trophies are laid "at Anpétu's feet" (013-035), suggesting the sun as witness and arbiter of human deeds. The name Anpétu also occurs in the personal name [[anpetu-sapa|Anpétu-Sâpa]] ([[anpetu-sapa|Dark Sun]]), a female figure in the [[legend-of-the-falls|Legend of the Falls]] narrative (012-232, 016-107).

## The Sun-Dance

The [[dakota|Dakotas]] worship Anpétuwee through the Sun-[[dance|Dance]] — "usually held in the full of the moon" (017-094) — an ordeal of extreme severity. Gordon directs readers to [[george-catlin|George Catlin]] for further detail on this ceremony (017-091). The [[dakota|Dakota]] belief that the sun is beneficent yet worshipped through self-torture reflects a complex theology where cosmic kindness and ritual severity coexist.
