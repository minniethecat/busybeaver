# Falls of St. Anthony

The **Falls of St. Anthony** — *[[ha-ha|Ha-Ha]]* to the [[Dakota]] (017-174) — are the great waterfall on the [[Mississippi River]] at present-day Minneapolis. They are the subject of *The [[legend|Legend]] of the Falls* in [[Hanford Lennox Gordon|H. L. Gordon]]'s *[[Legend|Legends of the Northwest]]* and hold profound significance in [[dakota|Dakota]] cosmology.

## Geography

The Falls mark the only major waterfall on the [[mississippi-river|Mississippi River]]. [[Spirit Island]] — *Wanagi-wita* — sat just above the cataract:

> Island just above the Falls.
> (013-051)

A pillar of rock a few rods below the Falls was called by the [[dakota|Dakotas]] a sacred site (012-203).

## Dakota Names and Beliefs

### Ha-Ha

The [[dakota|Dakotas]] called the Falls *[[ha-ha|Ha-Ha]]* — an onomatopoeic name evoking the sound of rushing water:

> The Dakotas called the Falls of St. Anthony the Ha-Ha
> (017-174)

### The Unktéhee

[[dakota|Dakota]] [[legend|myth]]ology held that powerful subterranean [[spirit|spirits]], the [[unktéhee|Unktéhee]], dwelled in a vast cavern beneath the Falls:

> in a vast cavern under the Falls of St. Anthony. The Unktéhee
> (017-063)

These water [[spirit|spirits]] were among the most fearsome beings in [[dakota|Dakota]] cosmology.

### The Spirit of the Falls

A dark [[soul|spirit]] — [[anpetu-wee|Anpétu]]-sâpa — was believed to haunt the Falls:

> of Anpétu-sâpa that haunts the Falls of St. Anthony
> (016-107)

## The Legend of the Falls

Gordon's *[[legend|Legend]] of the Falls* (Sections 13–15) tells the story of a [[dakota|Dakota]] maiden who, like [[Winona]], plunged over the cataract:

> ...lo she plunges o'er the Falls.
> (013-058)

The legend connects the physical geography of the waterfall to the tragic love stories that permeate Gordon's collection.

## Cultural Crossroads

The Falls region was a gathering point for [[dakota|Dakota]] bands. The area from the Falls to Keóza (a [[dakota|Dakota]] settlement) was a route for ceremonial games:

> from the Falls to the Games at Keóza.
> (012-180)

The French explorer [[Louis Hennepin]] gave the Falls their European name, and the site later became central to the founding of Minneapolis.
