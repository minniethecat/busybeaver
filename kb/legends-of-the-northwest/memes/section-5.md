[004-001] Proud of their rites are the Virgins fair,

[004-002] For none but a Virgin may enter there.

[004-003] \'Tis a custom of old and a sacred thing;

[004-004] Nor rank nor beauty the warriors spare,

[004-005] If a tarnished maiden should enter there.

[004-006] And her that enters the Sacred Ring

[004-007] With a blot that is known or a secret stain

[004-008] The warrior who knows is bound to expose,

[004-009] And lead her forth from the ring again.

[004-010] And the word of the warrior is a sacred by law;

[004-011] For the Virgins\' Feast is a sacred thing.

[004-012] Aside with the mothers sat Hârpstinà:

[004-013] She durst not enter the virgins\' ring.

[004-014] Round and round to the merry song

[004-015] The maidens dance in their gay attire.

[004-016] While the loud \"Ho-Ho\'s\" of the tawny throng

[004-017] Their flying feet and their song inspire.

[004-018] They have finished the song and the sacred dance,

[004-019] And hand in hand to the feast advance\--

[004-020] To the polished bowls of the golden maize,

[004-021] And the sweet fawn meat in the polished trays.

[004-022] Then up from his seat in the silent crowd

[004-023] Rose the frowning, fierce-eyed, tall Red Cloud;

[004-024] Swift was his stride as the panther\'s spring,

[004-025] When he leaps on the fawn from his cavern lair;

[004-026] Wiwâstè he caught by her flowing hair,

[004-027] And dragged her forth from the Sacred Ring.

[004-028] She turned on the warrior. Her eyes flashed fire;

[004-029] Her proud lips quivered with queenly ire;

[004-030] Her hand to the Spirits she raised and said,

[004-031] And her sun browned cheeks were aflame with red:

[004-032] \"I am pure!\--I am pure as falling snow!

[004-033] Great Tâku-Skan-Skan \[51\] will testify!

[004-034] And dares the tall coward to say me no?\"

[004-035] But the sullen warrior made no reply.

[004-036] She turned to the chief with her frantic cries:

[004-037] \"Wakâwa\--my Father; he lies!\--he lies!

[004-038] Wiwâstè is pure as the faun unborn;

[004-039] Lead me back to the feast, or Wiwâstè dies!\"

[004-040] But the warriors uttered a cry of scorn,

[004-041] And he turned his face from her pleading eyes.

[004-042] Then the sullen warrior, the tall Red Cloud,

[004-043] Looked up and spoke and his voice was loud;

[004-044] But he held his wrath and spoke with care:

[004-045] \"Wiwâstè is young, she is proud and fair,

[004-046] But she may not boast of the virgin snows.

[004-047] The Virgins Feast is a Sacred thing:

[004-048] How durst she enter the Virgins ring?

[004-049] The warrior would fain, but he dares not spare;

[004-050] She is tarnished and only the Red Cloud knows.\"

[004-051] She clutched her hair in her clenched hand:

[004-052] She stood like statue bronzed and grand:

[004-053] Wakân-deè \[39\] flashed in her fiery eyes;

[004-054] Then, swift as the meteor cleaves the skies\--

[004-055] Nay, swift as the fiery Wakinyan\'s dart, \[32\]

[004-056] She snatch the knife from the warriors belt,

[004-057] And plunged it clean to the polished hilt\--

[004-058] With deadly cry\--in the villain\'s heart.

[004-059] Staggering he clutched the air and fell;

[004-060] His life-blood smoked on the trampled sand,

[004-061] And dripped from the knife in the virgin\'s hand.

[004-062] Then rose his kinsmen\'s savage yell.

[004-063] Swift as the doe\'s Wiwâstè\'s feet

[004-064] Fled away to the forest. The hunters fleet

[004-065] In vain pursue, and in vain they prowl,

[004-066] And lurk in the forest till dawn of day.

[004-067] They hear the hoot of the mottled owl;

[004-068] They hear the were-wolf\'s \[52\] winding howl;

[004-069] But the swift Wiwâstè is far away.

[004-070] They found no trace in the forest land,

[004-071] They found no trail in the dew-damp grass,

[004-072] They found no track in the river sand,

[004-073] Where they thought Wiwâstè would surely pass.

[004-074] The braves returned to the troubled chief;

[004-075] In his lodge he sat in his silent grief.

[004-076] \"Surely,\" they said, \"she has turned a spirit.

[004-077] No trail she left with her flying feet;

[004-078] No pathway leads to her far retreat.

[004-079] She flew in the air, and her wail\--we could hear it,

[004-080] As she upward rose to the shining stars;

[004-081] And we heard on the river, as we stood near it,

[004-082] The falling drops of Wiwâstè\'s tears.\"

[004-083] Wakâwa thought of his daughter\'s words

[004-084] Ere the south-wind came and the piping birds\--

[004-085] \"My Father, listen,\--my words are true,\"

[004-086] And sad was her voice as the whippowil

[004-087] When she mourns her mate by the moon-lit rill,

[004-088] \"Wiwâstè lingers alone with you;

[004-089] The rest are sleeping on yonder hill\--

[004-090] Save one\--and he an undutiful son,\--

[004-091] And you, my Father, will sit alone

[004-092] When Sisóka \[53\] sings and the snow is gone.\"

[004-093] His broad breast heaved on his troubled soul,

[004-094] The shadow of grief o\'er his visage stole

[004-095] Like a cloud on the face of the setting sun.

[004-096] \"She has followed the years that are gone,\" he said;

[004-097] \"The [[spirit|spirits]] the words of the witch fulfill;

[004-098] For I saw the ghost of my father dead,

[004-099] By the moon\'s dim light on the misty hill.

[004-100] He shook the plumes on his withered head,

[004-101] And the wind through his pale form whistled shrill.

[004-102] And a low, sad voice on the hill I heard.

[004-103] Like the mournful wail of a widowed bird.\"

[004-104] Then lo, as he looked from his lodge afar,

[004-105] He saw the glow of the Evening-star;

[004-106] \"And yonder,\" he said, \"is Wiwâstè\'s face;

[004-107] She looks from her lodge on our fading race.

[004-108] Devoured by famine, and fraud, and war,

[004-109] And chased and hounded from woe to woe,

[004-110] As the white wolves follow the buffalo.\"

[004-111] And he named the planet the _Virgin Star_. \[54\]

[004-112] \"Wakâwa,\" he muttered, \"the guilt is thine!

[004-113] She was pure,\--she was pure as the fawn unborn.

[004-114] O, why did I hark to the cry of scorn,

[004-115] Or the words of the lying libertine?

[004-116] Wakâwa, Wakâwa, the guilt is thine!

[004-117] The springs will return with the voice of birds,

[004-118] But the voice of my daughter will come no more.

[004-119] She wakened the woods with her musical words,

[004-120] And the sky-lark, ashamed of his voice, forbore.

[004-121] She called back the years that had passed, and long

[004-122] I heard their voice in her happy song.

[004-123] Her heart was the home of the sunbeam. Bright

[004-124] Poured the stream of her song on the starry night.

[004-125] O, why did the chief of the tall Hóhé

[004-126] His feet from Kapóza \[6\] so long delay?

[004-127] For his father sat at my father\'s feast,

[004-128] And he at Wakâwa\'s\--an honored guest.

[004-129] He is dead!\--he is slain on the Bloody Plain,

[004-130] By the hand of the treacherous Chippeway;

[004-131] And the face shall I never behold again

[004-132] Of my brave young brother\--the chief Chaskè.

[004-133] Death walks like a shadow among my kin;

[004-134] And swift are the feet of the flying years

[004-135] That cover Wakâwa with frost and tears,

[004-136] And leave their tracks on his wrinkled skin.

[004-137] Wakâwa, the voice of the years that are gone

[004-138] Will follow thy feet like the shadow of death,

[004-139] Till the paths of the forest and desert lone

[004-140] Shall forget thy footsteps. O living breath,

[004-141] Whence art thou, and whither so soon to fly?

[004-142] And whence are the years? Shall I overtake

[004-143] Their flying feet in the star-lit sky?

[004-144] From his last long sleep will the warrior wake?

[004-145] Will the morning break in Wakâwa\'s tomb,

[004-146] As it breaks and glows in the eastern skies?

[004-147] Is it true?\--will the spirits of kinsmen come

[004-148] And bid the bones of the brave arise?\"

[004-149] \"Wakâwa, Wakâwa, for thee the years

[004-150] Are red with blood and bitter with tears.

[004-151] Gone,\--brothers, and daughters, and wife,\--all gone

[004-152] That are kin to Wakâwa,\--but one\--but one\--

[004-153] Wakínyan Tanka\--undutiful son!

[004-154] And he estranged from his fathers tee,

[004-155] Will never return till the chief shall die.

[004-156] And what cares he for his father\'s grief?

[004-157] He will smile at my death,\--it will make him chief.

[004-158] Woe burns in my bosom. Ho, Warriors,\--Ho!

[004-159] Raise the song of red war; for your chief must go

[004-160] To drown his grief in the blood of the foe!

[004-161] I shall fall. Raise my mound on the sacred hill.

[004-162] Let my warriors the wish of their chief fulfill;

[004-163] For my fathers sleep in the sacred ground.

[004-164] The Autumn blasts o\'er Wakâwa\'s mound

[004-165] Shall chase the hair of the thistle\'s head,

[004-166] And the bare armed oak o\'er the silent dead.

[004-167] When the whirling snows from the north descend,

[004-168] Shall wail and moan in the midnight wind.

[004-169] In the famine of winter the wolf shall prowl,

[004-170] And scratch the snow from the heap of stones,

[004-171] And sit in the gathering storm and howl,

[004-172] On the frozen mound, for Wakâwa\'s bones.

[004-173] But the years that are gone shall return again.

[004-174] As the robin returns and the whippowil

[004-175] When my warriors stand on the sacred hill

[004-176] And remember the deeds of their brave chief slain.\"

[004-177] Beneath the glow of the Virgin Star

[004-178] They raised the song of the red war dance.

[004-179] At the break of dawn with the bow and lance

[004-180] They followed the chief on the path of war.

[004-181] To the north\--to the forests of fir and pine\--

[004-182] Led their stealthy steps on the winding trail,

[004-183] Till they saw the Lake of the Spirit \[55\] shine

[004-184] Through somber pines of the dusky dale.

[004-185] Then they heard the hoot of the mottled owl; \[56\]

[004-186] They heard the gray wolf\'s dismal howl;

[004-187] Then shrill and sudden the war whoop rose

[004-188] From an hundred throats of their swarthy foes,

[004-189] In ambush crouched in the tangled wood.

[004-190] Death shrieked in the twang of their deadly bows,

[004-191] And their hissing arrows drank brave men\'s blood.

[004-192] From rock, and thicket, and brush, and brakes,

[004-193] Gleamed the burning eyes of the forest snakes. \[57\]

[004-194] From brake, and thicket, and brush, and stone,

[004-195] The bow string hummed and the arrow hissed,

[004-196] And the lance of a crouching [[ojibway|Ojibway]] shone,

[004-197] Or the scalp-knife gleamed in a swarthy fist.

[004-198] Undaunted the braves of Wakâwa\'s band

[004-199] Jumped into the thicket with lance and knife,

[004-200] And grappled the Chippewas hand to hand;

[004-201] And foe with foe, in the deadly strife,

[004-202] Lay clutching the scalp of his foe and dead,

[004-203] With a [[tomahawk]] sunk in his ghastly head,

[004-204] Or his still heart sheathing a bloody blade.

[004-205] Like a bear in the battle Wakâwa raves,

[004-206] And cheers the hearts of his falling braves.

[004-207] But a panther crouches along his track,\--

[004-208] He springs with a yell on Wakâwa\'s back!

[004-209] The tall Chief, stabbed to the heart, lies low;

[004-210] But his left hand clutches his deadly foe,

[004-211] And his red right clenches the bloody hilt

[004-212] Of his knife in the heart of the slayer dyed.

[004-213] And thus was the life of Wakâwa spilt,

[004-214] And slain and slayer lay side by side.

[004-215] The unscalped corpse of their honored chief

[004-216] His warriors snatched from the yelling pack,

[004-217] And homeward fled on their forest track

[004-218] With their bloody burden and load of grief.

[004-219] The spirits the words of the brave fulfill,\--

[004-220] Wakâwa sleeps on the sacred hill,

[004-221] And Wakínyan Tânka, his son, is chief.

[004-222] Ah, soon shall the lips of men forget

[004-223] Wakâwa\'s name, and the mound of stone

[004-224] Will speak of the dead to the winds alone,

[004-225] And the winds will whistle their mock-regret.

[004-226] The speckled cones of the scarlet berries \[58\]

[004-227] Lie red and ripe in the prairie grass.

[004-228] The Sí-yo \[59\] clucks on the emerald prairies

[004-229] To her infant brood. From the wild morass,

[004-230] On the sapphire lakelet set within it,

[004-231] Magâ \[60\] sails forth with her wee ones daily.

[004-232] They ride on the dimpling waters gaily,

[004-233] Like a fleet of yachts and a man of war.

[004-234] The piping plover, the laughing linnet,

[004-235] And the swallow sail in the sunset skies.

[004-236] The whippowil from her cover hies,

[004-237] And trills her song on the amber air.

[004-238] Anon, to her loitering mate she cries

[004-239] \"Flip, O Will!\--trip, O Will!\--skip, O Will!\"

[004-240] And her merry mate from afar replies:

[004-241] \"Flip I will,\--skip I will,\--trip I will;\"

[004-242] And away on the wings of the wind he flies.

[004-243] And bright from her lodge in the skies afar

[004-244] Peeps the glowing face of the Virgin Star.

[004-245] The fox pups \[60\] creep from the mother\'s lair

[004-246] And leap in the light of the rising moon;

[004-247] And loud on the luminous moonlit lake

[004-248] Shrill the bugle notes of the lover loon;

[004-249] And woods and waters and welkin break

[004-250] Into jubilant song,\--it is joyful June.

[004-251] But where is Wiwâstè? O where is she\--

[004-252] The Virgin avenged\--the queenly queen\--

[004-253] The womanly woman\--the heroine?

[004-254] Has she gone to the spirits and can it be

[004-255] That her beautiful face is the Virgin Star

[004-256] Peeping out from the door of her lodge afar,

[004-257] Or upward sailing the silver sea.

[004-258] Star-beaconed and lit like an avenue,

[004-259] In the shining stern of her gold canoe?

[004-260] No tidings came\--nor the brave Chaskè:

[004-261] O, why did the lover so long delay?

[004-262] He promised to come with the robins in May,

[004-263] With the bridal gifts for the bridal day;

[004-264] But the fair May mornings have slipped away,

[004-265] And where is the lover\--the brave Chaskè?
