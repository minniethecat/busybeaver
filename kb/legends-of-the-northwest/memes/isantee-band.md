The Isantee Band was one of the principal divisions of the Dakota people. Their name appears in historical records as "Isantees" or "Izatys" — the latter being the form used by early French explorers and traders when referring to the band as a whole (018-133).

## Principal Village

The main village of the Isantees was Kathága, pronounced _Kah-thah-gah_, meaning "the place of waves and foam" (018-122). It stood at the Falls of St. Anthony, which the Dakotas called the Ha-ha — the loud, laughing waters (018-122).

The Dakotas believed the Falls marked the center of the earth, where the Great Unktéhee, the creator of the earth and man, dwelt (018-122). From this sacred place a path led to the Spirit-land.

## Historical Contact

The French explorer Daniel Greysolon DuLuth visited Kathága on July 2, 1679, and planted the French arms at the village (018-122). In his memoir of 1685, DuLuth recorded having reached "the great village of the Nadouecioux called Izatys, where never had a Frenchman been" (018-133).

The villages of the Dakotas were not permanent towns but camping grounds occupied at intervals (018-133).

## Leadership

When Father Louis Hennepin visited Mille Lacs in 1679–80, Wazi-kuté was the head chief, or Itâncan, of the Isantees (017-162). The flag of the Isantee chief flew over the country described in Gordon's poem (006-034).
