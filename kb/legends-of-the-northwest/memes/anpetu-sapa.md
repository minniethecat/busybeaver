# Anpétu-Sâpa

Anpétu-Sâpa (Dark Sun) is a [[soul|spirit]] being in [[dakota|Dakota]] belief who haunts the [[falls-of-st-anthony|Falls of St. Anthony]], appearing in Gordon's [[legend-of-the-falls|Legend of the Falls]] as a tragic female figure — "moon-faced, meek Anpétu-Sâpa" — who wins a hunter's heart (012-232, 016-107).

## Definition / Identity

Anpétu-Sâpa combines two [[dakota|Dakota]] words: *[[anpetu-wee|Anpétu]]* (sun / day) and *Sâpa* (dark / black), yielding "Dark Sun" — a [[soul|spirit]] of shadow associated with the cataract's mist and roar.

- **Origin**: [[dakota|Dakota]] folk belief — the [[soul|spirit]] of Anpétu-Sâpa that haunts [[falls-of-st-anthony|the Falls]] (016-107)
- **Language**: [[dakota|Dakota]] (*[[anpetu-wee|Anpétu]]* = sun, *Sâpa* = dark)
- **Domain**: [[soul|spirit]]uality / place-[[soul|spirit]] / legend

Gordon's footnote explicitly identifies the [[soul|spirit]]: "The [[soul|spirit]] of [[anpetu-wee|Anpétu]]-sâpa that haunts [[falls-of-st-anthony|the Falls]] of St. Anthony" (016-107). The [[dakota|Dakotas]] associated [[falls-of-st-anthony|the Falls]] with multiple [[soul|spirit]] beings — the subterranean [[unktéhee|Unktéhees]] dwelling beneath the cataract and Anpétu-Sâpa haunting the mist above, forming a layered [[soul|spirit]]ual geography.

## In the Book

### Role in the Legends

Anpétu-Sâpa appears in the [[legend-of-the-falls|Legend of the Falls]] narrative ("[[winona|Winona]]" and "The [[legend-of-the-falls|Legend of the Falls]]") as a female figure of both sorrow and beauty. In section 13 (the [[legend-of-the-falls|Legend of the Falls]]), she is introduced as "Moon-faced, meek Anpétu-Sâpa" who "won the hunter's heart and hand" (012-232), and later "Proud was fair Anpétu-Sâpa, and her eyes were glad with joy" (012-241).

In section 14, the mood shifts to tragedy: "And the sad [[anpetu-wee|Anpétu]]-sâpa spread the lodge with wonted care" (013-013), holding her sleeping child (013-018). The transformation from proud young bride to sorrowing mother mirrors [[falls-of-st-anthony|the Falls]]' dual nature — beauty and danger intertwined.

The [[dakota|Dakotas]] also identified the [[spirit-island|Spirit Island]] (*Wanagee We-ta*) just above the cataract, saying "the [[soul|spirit]] of Anpetu Sapa" dwelt there (012-204), connecting the dark sun [[soul|spirit]] to the sacred island site.
