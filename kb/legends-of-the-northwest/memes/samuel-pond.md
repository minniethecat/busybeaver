Rev. Samuel W. Pond was one of the first Protestant missionaries to the [[dakota|Dakota]] people, stationed at Shakopee in present-day [[minnesota|Minnesota]]. His long residence among the [[dakota|Dakotas]] made him a valuable linguistic and cultural authority, cited by [[hanford-gordon|Hanford Lennox Gordon]] in the footnotes of "[[legend|Legend]]s of the Northwest."

## 身份与背景

- **Tribe / Nation**: European-American
- **Role**: Protestant missionary, linguist, [[dakota|Dakota]] language authority
- **Location**: Shakopee, [[minnesota|Minnesota]]
- **Active Period**: Mid-to-late 19th century

## 在书中的角色

### 语言学来源

Gordon acknowledges Pond in his preface (P01-067) as "one of the first Protestant missionaries to these people, and himself the author of" works on the [[dakota|Dakota]] language. Pond's expertise in [[dakota|Dakota]] pronunciation and linguistics is cited when Gordon needs to clarify the proper rendering of [[dakota|Dakota]] words.

### 语言学贡献

In the footnotes (017-180), Gordon relies on Pond's authority to explain the pronunciation of [[dakota|Dakota]] words. For example, "[[ha-ha|I-ha-ha]]" is noted as being pronounced "E-rhah-rhah—lightly laughing" according to Pond, whose "long residence as a missionary among the [[dakota|Dakotas]] in this immediate" vicinity gave him intimate knowledge of the language.

## 历史/文化背景

Samuel Pond arrived in [[minnesota|Minnesota]] with his brother Gideon Pond in 1834, establishing one of the earliest Protestant missions in the [[dakota|Dakota]] territory. The Pond brothers created a written form of the [[dakota|Dakota]] language and produced some of the earliest [[dakota|Dakota]]-language publications, including a [[dakota|Dakota]] dictionary and grammar.

Their work paralleled that of [[stephen-riggs|Stephen R. Riggs]], who also served as a missionary-linguist among the [[dakota|Dakotas]] and produced major translations and linguistic studies. Together, these missionary-scholars documented the [[dakota|Dakota]] language at a critical period of cultural transition.
