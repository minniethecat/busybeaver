Little Crow (Ta-ó-ya-te-dú-ta, "His Red People") was a prominent [[dakota|Dakota]] chief of the Kapóza (Light-foot) band, appearing in Gordon's "[[legend|Legend]]s of the Northwest" as both a historical figure and a character in the [[winona|Winona]] narrative. He succeeded his father [[big-thunder|Big Thunder]] ([[big-thunder|Wakínyan Tânka]]) as chief and was the last of the Little Crow line.

## 身份与背景

- **Tribe / Nation**: [[dakota|Dakota]] ([[dakota|Sioux]]), Kapóza band
- **Role**: Chief, hereditary leader of the Light-foot band
- **Family**: Son of [[big-thunder|Wakínyan Tânka]] ([[big-thunder|Big Thunder]]); father of a daughter (the "star-eyed" maiden in [[winona|Winona]])

## 在书中的角色

### Winona 传奇

Little Crow figures prominently in the [[winona|Winona]] legend as the father of a beautiful daughter courted by suitors. In the poem, he is portrayed as a chief whose authority is challenged by the actions of other characters, including the false suitor and [[winona|Winona]]'s tragedy.

Gordon refers to "the star-eyed daughter of Little Crow" (001-102) and "the sweet-faced daughter of Little Crow" (001-152), introducing the chief through his daughter's beauty. The narrative voice of Little Crow appears in direct speech: "I heard the voice of the Little Crow" (003-075) and "But I heard the answer of Little Crow" (003-082), revealing him as an active participant in the council scenes.

### 与 Wiwásté 的关系

In the poem's later sections, Little Crow's position is directly challenged: "Thy father no more is the false Little Crow" (005-200), a pivotal line in the [[winona|Winona]] narrative where loyalties shift and the chief's authority is questioned.

## 历史/文化背景

Little Crow (Ta-ó-ya-te-dú-ta) was a real historical figure who led [[dakota|Dakota]] warriors during the [[minnesota|Minnesota]] outbreak of 1862–63. Gordon provides extensive historical notes (015-226–015-244) detailing the Little Crow lineage. The sobriquet "Little Crow" originated from the [[ojibway-nation|Chippewa]] name "Petit Corbeau," of which he was reportedly proud.

The name descended through three generations: the first Little Crow, his son [[big-thunder|Wakínyan Tânka]] ([[big-thunder|Big Thunder]]), and his grandson Ta-ó-ya-te-dú-ta, who became chief upon [[big-thunder|Big Thunder]]'s death. The last Little Crow was killed on July 3, 1863, near Hutchinson, [[minnesota|Minnesota]], by one Lamson.

The Little Crows were buried near the "[[wakan-teepee-site|Wakan Teepee]]" (sacred teepee), also known as Carver's Cave at St. Paul.
