# Spirit

**Spirits** are a central element of [[Dakota]] and [[Ojibway]] belief systems, appearing throughout the [[legend|legends]] collected by [[Hanford Lennox Gordon|H. L. Gordon]] as active forces in both the natural and human worlds.

The [[dakota|Dakota]] conceived of a world inhabited by spirits — hunters from the spirit-land who pursue the bison and the deer, day-spirits who rise with the dawn, and thunder-spirits who shake the sky.

## The Spirit-Land

In [[dakota|Dakota]] cosmology, the spirit-land (*Wanagi-wita*) is the destination of souls after death. Gordon describes it in poetic terms:

> But the land of the Spirits is fairer still,
> (002-139)

The journey to this realm follows the [[wanagee-tach-anku|Milky Way]] — the "Shining Path":

> ...the Shining Path [68] to the Spirit-land;
> (005-244)

## Spirit Beings in Gordon's Legends

### The Day-Spirit

The rising sun was personified as a [[soul|spirit]] being:

> Till the Day-Spirit [70] rose in the East
> (006-061)

### The Spider-Spirit

A malevolent [[soul|spirit]] figure appears in [[dakota|Dakota]] legend:

> ...they deemed him the black Spider-Spirit
> (006-166)

### Spirit of Thunder

The thunder was understood as a powerful [[soul|spirit]] entity:

> the Spirit of Thunder,
> (008-152)

### The Sacred Spirit

[[dakota|Dakota]] prayer invoked a supreme sacred [[soul|spirit]]:

> "Sacred Spirit, Father have pity on me always"
> (008-199)

## Spirit Island

[[spirit-island|Spirit Island]] (*Wanagi-wita*) — located just above the [[Falls of St. Anthony]] — held particular significance:

> in the oak on the Isle of the Spirit. [a]
> (007-059)

A [[soul|spirit]]-bird made her nest in an oak tree on this island:

> ...made her nest in an oak tree on Spirit island — Wanagi-wita just below the Falls
> (007-073)

The island was believed to be haunted until frightened away by approaching settlement.

## Spirit and Ceremony

The [[dakota|Dakota]] maiden [[Winona]] invoked spirits in her most dramatic moments:

> Her hand to the Spirits she raised and said,
> (004-030)

The sacred [[Dance|ceremonial dances]] were understood as communications with the [[soul|spirit]] world. The [[feast-of-the-virgins|Virgins' Feast]] included [[soul|spirit]] invocations as part of the ritual.

## Gordon's Documentation

Gordon's footnotes preserve the ethnographic record of [[dakota|Dakota]] [[soul|spirit]] beliefs, drawing heavily on [[Stephen R. Riggs|Riggs]]' documentation. The missionary concept of the "[[wakan|Great Spirit]]" (*[[wakan|Wakan Tanka]]*) was later imported into [[dakota|Dakota]] vocabulary (017-018), blending indigenous and Christian [[soul|spirit]]ual concepts.
