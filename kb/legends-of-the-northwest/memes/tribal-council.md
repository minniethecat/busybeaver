# Tribal Council

The council gatherings where [[dakota|Dakota]] leaders made decisions of war, peace, and alliance.

[[dakota|Dakota]] councils brought together the bands of the confederation for deliberation. DuLuth's historic council with the [[dakota|Dakota]] braves (009-110) exemplifies the diplomatic dimension of these gatherings.

## In Gordon's Legends

Gordon preserves these [[dakota|Dakota]] events through English verse, informed by his years of study and the work of missionaries like Riggs (P01-063). His representations aim to be faithful to the original [[tradition|traditions]] (P01-174).

## DuLuth's Council

One of the most significant councils in Gordon's work is [[daniel-duluth|Daniel DuLuth]]'s gathering with the Dakota braves (009-110). DuLuth, a French explorer and diplomat, convened the Dakota leaders at a critical moment in the intertribal wars of the upper Mississippi.

Council protocol was elaborate. The [[calumet|calumet]] ceremony opened proceedings, and formal silence was observed at great gatherings (018-110). Decisions required consensus among the band chiefs of the [[isantee-band|Isantee]] and the wider [[dakota-confederation|Dakota confederation]].

These councils deliberated matters of war, peace, hunting grounds, and alliance with the French. The [[dakota-council|Dakota council]] tradition, combining diplomatic deliberation with sacred ritual, was central to Dakota governance and inter-band relations.
