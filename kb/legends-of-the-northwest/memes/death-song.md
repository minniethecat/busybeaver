# Death Song

The death song is a recurring motif in [[dakota|Dakota]] warrior tradition — a ceremonial song sung by warriors facing death in battle.

## Cultural Significance

For [[dakota|Dakota]] warriors, the death song expressed courage in the face of mortality. Rather than lament, it affirmed the warrior's life and readiness to enter the [[soul|spirit]] world (001-047).

## In the Book

The death song appears at dramatic moments in *The [[feast-of-the-virgins|Feast of the Virgins]]* (003-108) and *[[winona|Winona]]*. Warriors facing death raise their voices, transforming defeat into [[soul|spirit]]ual transcendence.

Gordon's footnotes reference Riggs' documentation of this tradition (P01-063). The death song connects to [[superstition|Dakota beliefs]] about [[spirit|spirits]] and the afterlife (001-192).
