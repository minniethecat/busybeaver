The Cree (also Crees, Knisteneaux) are a major Algonquian-speaking Indigenous people of North America, referred to by the [[dakota|Dakotas]] as "Mastincapi" — Rabbits (009-050) — and appearing in Gordon's [[legend|Legends]] of the Northwest as allies of the [[ojibway|Ojibways]] and occasional enemies of the [[dakota|Dakotas]].

## Definition / Identity

The Cree are the Knisteneaux of Alexander Mackenzie's classification (016-139), one of the largest Indigenous groups in Canada and the northern United States. In [[dakota|Dakota]] terminology, they are called Mastincapi — "Rabbits" — a name reflecting their perceived timidity in warfare compared to the more aggressive [[dakota|Dakota]] warriors (009-050).

- **Origin**: Algonquian language family; the name "Cree" derives from the French "Cristinaux," a corruption of "Kenistenoag"
- **Language**: Algonquian (Cree-Montagnais-Naskapi dialect continuum)
- **Domain**: ethnography / intertribal-relations

The Cree appear in Gordon's poetic narratives as both adversaries and background figures, reflecting the complex intertribal dynamics of the Upper Midwest during the period when [[dakota|Dakota]] and [[ojibway|Ojibway]] territorial conflicts were intensifying.

## In the Book

Gordon references the Cree throughout his poems, most notably in "[[winona|Winona]]" and in his ethnographic footnotes.

### Role in the Legends

In "[[winona|Winona]]," a Cree warrior appears as part of a catalog of Indigenous maidens and their lovers — "A Huron, or a Cree, or a blooming Chippeway" (009-146). The Cree are also invoked in territorial framing: journeys go "to the land of the Crees or Dakotas" (007-184).

More dramatically, in "[[feast-of-the-virgins|The Feast of the Virgins]]," the Cree appear in a violent passage: "With their murder cries rode the bloody Cree" (003-039), emphasizing the intertribal conflicts that form the historical backdrop of Gordon's legends.

### Representative Passages

> "With their murder cries rode the bloody Cree" (003-039)

> "to the land of the Crees or Dakotas" (007-184)

> "A Huron, or a Cree, or a blooming Chippeway" (009-146)
