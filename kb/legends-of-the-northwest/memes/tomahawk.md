# Tomahawk

The tomahawk — a traditional war-club or lightweight axe — appears in Gordon's *[[legend|Legend]]s of the Northwest* as both a weapon of war and a cultural symbol.

## The Weapon

The tomahawk served as both a close-combat weapon and a throwing implement. In [[dakota|Dakota]] warfare, it was a primary weapon alongside the bow and lance (P01-007).

## In the Book

The tomahawk appears in battle scenes throughout the [[legend|legends]], particularly in *[[winona|Winona]]* where [[dakota|Dakota]] and [[ojibway|Ojibway]] warriors clash (009-153). The weapon symbolizes [[dakota|Dakota]] martial culture.

Gordon's ethnographic notes document [[dakota|Dakota]] weapons and warfare [[tradition|customs]] (P01-063). The inter-tribal conflicts portrayed in the [[legend|legends]] feature tomahawks prominently, reflecting the historical reality of [[dakota|Dakota]]-[[ojibway|Ojibway]] warfare.
