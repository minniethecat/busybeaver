# Lake Pepin

A natural widening of the [[mississippi-river|Mississippi River]], significant in the geography of Gordon's [[dakota|Dakota]] [[legend|legends]].

## Connection to the Legends

Gordon's *[[legend|Legend]]s of the Northwest* is grounded in the geography of the upper Mississippi region, which he studied alongside [[dakota|Dakota]] [[tradition|traditions]] (P01-007).

## In the Book

Lake Pepin appears in Gordon's poetic narratives. His [[legend|legends]] are set against the backdrop of this landscape. The place names reflect both [[dakota|Dakota]]/[[ojibway|Ojibway]] originals and European adaptations.


Gordon's *[[legend|Legend]]s of the Northwest* preserves this in the context of [[dakota|Dakota]] life (010-116).

Gordon acknowledges his debt to the Reverend S. R. Riggs, whose ethnographic work provided the foundation for his [[legend|legends]] (P01-063).

His preface claims fidelity to [[dakota|Dakota]] [[tradition|customs]], life, [[tradition|traditions]], and [[superstition|superstitions]] (P01-174).
