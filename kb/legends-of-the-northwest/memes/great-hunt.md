# The Great Hunt

The ceremonial buffalo hunt of the [[dakota|Dakota]] people, a central event in their subsistence and [[soul|spirit]]ual life.

## Background

Gordon's *[[legend|Legend]]s of the Northwest* preserves these events in poetic form, drawing on his study of [[dakota|Dakota]] [[tradition|traditions]] (P01-007) and the ethnographic work of missionaries like Riggs (P01-063).

## In the Book

The event is depicted in Gordon's [[legend|legends]] with attention to [[dakota|Dakota]] [[tradition|customs]]. His preface claims fidelity to his sources (P01-174).

## Significance

These events represent moments in [[dakota|Dakota]] history and culture that Gordon sought to preserve before "the day of the [[dakota|Dakotas]]" passed (P01-046).
