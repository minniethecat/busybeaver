# Tradition

The traditions of the [[dakota|Dakota]] people were among the primary subjects of H. L. Gordon's study. His preface explicitly names "traditions" as one of the five areas he investigated (P01-007).

## Gordon's Mission

Gordon's work aimed to capture [[dakota|Dakota]] traditions in English verse before they were lost. "The day of the [[dakota|Dakotas]] is done," he writes, and "the [[dakota|Dakotas]] of old are no more" (P01-046).

His footnotes reference the Reverend S. R. Riggs, who spent years documenting [[dakota|Dakota]] traditions as a missionary (P01-063). Gordon acknowledges his debt to Riggs' *Grammar and Dictionary of the [[dakota|Dakota]] Language* (P01-064).

## In the Book

The [[legend|legends]] represent Gordon's attempt to render [[dakota|Dakota]] oral traditions accessible to English readers. His preface claims fidelity to "[[dakota|Dakota]] customs, life, traditions, and [[superstition|superstitions]]" (P01-174).

The *[[feast-of-the-virgins|Feast of the Virgins]]* preserves ceremonial gathering traditions (003-108). *[[winona|Winona]]* and *The [[legend|Legend]] of [[falls-of-st-anthony|the Falls]]* each maintain specific [[dakota|Dakota]] traditions Gordon feared would vanish.
