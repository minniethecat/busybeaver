# Wanagee-Tach-anku

Wanagee-Tach-anku — the Pathway of the Spirits — is the [[dakota|Dakota]] name for the Milky Way, believed to be the celestial road over which the [[spirit|spirits]] of the dead pass to the [[spirit|Spirit]]-land (017-058–017-059).

## Definition / Identity

In [[dakota|Dakota]] cosmology, the Milky Way is not an astronomical object but a [[soul|spirit]]ual highway. Gordon's footnote records the [[dakota|Dakota]] belief with precision:

- **Origin**: [[dakota|Dakota]] cosmology — *Wanagee* = [[spirit|spirits]], *Tach-anku* = their path/road (017-058)
- **Language**: [[dakota|Dakota]] (*Wanagee-Tach-anku*, literally "[[spirit|Spirit]]s' Path")
- **Domain**: [[soul|spirit]]uality / cosmology / death-and-afterlife

The concept parallels the Latin *Via Lactea* (Milky Way) but carries a distinct theological meaning: rather than being spilled milk from a goddess, it is the road traveled by the dead. Gordon cites the missionary-linguist [[stephen-riggs|Stephen R. Riggs]]' *Tah-koo Wah-kan* (p. 101) as his ethnographic source (017-060), grounding this belief in documented [[dakota|Dakota]] oral tradition.

## In the Book

### Role in the Legends

The Pathway of the Spirits appears in Gordon's scholarly apparatus rather than in the verse itself, reflecting his practice of layering ethnographic footnotes beneath the poetic narrative. The reference sits within a cluster of footnotes in section 17 that systematically document [[dakota|Dakota]] beliefs about the afterlife, [[soul|spirit]] beings, and cosmic geography.

This cosmological detail enriches Gordon's poetic world: when characters die in the [[winona|Winona]] narrative or the [[sea-gull|Sea-Gull]] legend, the reader familiar with Gordon's notes understands that their [[spirit|spirits]] travel the Wanagee-Tach-anku to the [[spirit|Spirit]]-land — a journey grounded in authentic [[dakota|Dakota]] belief rather than literary invention.

### Representative Passage

> "The *Via Lactea* or Milky Way. The Dakotas call it *Wanagee-Tach-anku* — The path-way of the spirits and believe that over this path the spirits of the dead pass to the Spirit-land. See Riggs' Tah-koo Wah-kan, p. 101." (017-057–017-060)
