# Sea-Gull

A central figure in Gordon's "The [[legend|Legend]] of [[falls-of-st-anthony|the Falls]]" (Chapters XIII–XV). The Sea-Gull is the faithful first wife of [[panther|Panther]], mother of the [[raven|Raven]] ([[raven|Kâk-kâh-gè]]), and the tragic heroine whose patient endurance of betrayal and ultimate transformation gives the legend its emotional power and its name.

## 身份与背景

- **Tribe / Nation**: [[dakota|Dakota]] ([[dakota|Sioux]])
- **Role**: Wife, mother, the faithful spouse
- **Family**: Father: [[waub-ojeeg|Waub-Ojeeg]], the famous warrior (013-084); husband: [[panther|Panther]] (013-093); son: [[raven|Kâk-kâh-gè]], the [[raven|Raven]] (013-122); rival: the [[red-fox|Red Fox]] (014-098)

## 在书中的角色

### 相关传奇

The Sea-Gull is the co-protagonist of "The [[legend|Legend]] of [[falls-of-st-anthony|the Falls]]" (Chapters XIII–XV). The legend follows her marriage to [[panther|Panther]], her patient suffering through his infidelity with the [[red-fox|Red Fox]], and her supernatural transformation into the sea-gull bird — a metamorphosis that explains both the origin of [[falls-of-st-anthony|the Falls]] and the crying call of the sea-gull.

### 关键事件

The Sea-Gull's marriage begins joyfully: "When the [[panther|Panther]] wed the Sea-Gull" (013-093). She is a devoted wife, waiting faithfully in the wigwam: "Sat the Sea-Gull in the wigwam" (013-150), and ever loyal — "Found the faithful Sea-Gull waiting" (013-191). A son is born: "When the Sea-Gull wed the [[panther|Panther]]" she bears [[raven|Raven]] (013-230).

But trouble enters as [[panther|Panther]] grows cold. The Sea-Gull waits with sadness: "Sad at heart was Sea-Gull waiting" (013-256). She waits a full month: "One long moon the Sea-Gull waited" (013-276), aging with grief: "Old and wrinkled was the Sea-Gull" (013-291).

Despite her suffering, the Sea-Gull murmurs never (014-029) — she bears her pain in silence. She sits beside her rival in the wigwam: "Sat the Sea-Gull and the [[red-fox|Red Fox]]" (014-098). The [[red-fox|Red Fox]] bantered her (014-221), and the Sea-Gull privately resolves to act — "To herself said Sea-Gull — 'Surely'" (014-225).

The Sea-Gull's flight is swift and decisive: "Flew the Sea-Gull like a whirlwind" (014-236). But her voice is heard no more: "But no more the voice of Sea-Gull" (014-247). The [[red-fox|Red Fox]] notes her absence: "'Sea-Gull wanders late' said [[red-fox|Red Fox]]" (014-253).

The Sea-Gull's transformation is foretold: "If the Sea-Gull hear the wailing" (014-282). She has been robbed of her husband: "Robbed the Sea-Gull of her husband" (014-316). Yet in the legend's resolution, she reappears transformed: "Then again the snow-white Sea-Gull" (015-026). The final image shows domestic peace restored: "Long and happy with the [[panther|Panther]], Walked the Sea-Gull and the [[panther|Panther]]" (015-089, 015-095).

## 历史/文化背景

The Sea-Gull is a legendary figure whose transformation into a bird follows a widespread motif in [[dakota|Dakota]] and [[ojibway|Ojibway]] oral tradition — the metamorphosis of a wronged woman into a natural creature whose call echoes her grief. Gordon's rendering emphasizes the Sea-Gull's silent endurance and ultimate transcendence, making her the moral center of the legend. Her [[dakota|Dakota]] name, Waub-omeé-meé, connects her to her father [[waub-ojeeg|Waub-Ojeeg]], rooting her identity in the warrior lineage.
