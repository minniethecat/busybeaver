# Wazí-kuté

A tall [[dakota|Dakota]] chief in Gordon's "[[winona|Winona]]" legend (Chapters VI–XII). Wazí-kuté is the father of [[tamdoka|Tamdóka]], the crafty suitor of [[winona|Winona]]. His name — meaning "Winter" or associated with [[wa-zi-ya|Wazíya]], the god of winter — carries connotations of formidable power, and he is invoked as the standard of chiefly stature throughout the legend.

## 身份与背景

- **Tribe / Nation**: [[dakota|Dakota]] ([[dakota|Sioux]])
- **Role**: Chief, father
- **Family**: Son: [[tamdoka|Tamdóka]] (007-235, 008-238, 009-018)

## 在书中的角色

### 相关传奇

Wazí-kuté appears in the "[[winona|Winona]]" legend (Chapters VI–XII) primarily through his relationship to his son [[tamdoka|Tamdóka]]. He is referenced as a marker of lineage and status — [[tamdoka|Tamdóka]] is repeatedly identified as "son of tall Wazí-kuté" and "son of Chief Wazí-kuté," grounding the antagonist's identity in his father's renown.

### 关键事件

Wazí-kuté is first introduced by his title and stature: "The Itancan, tall Wazí-kuté" (007-209). The term Itâncan marks him as a chief or headman of his band. His physical height is emphasized — "tall Wazí-kuté" — making his stature both literal and figurative.

[[tamdoka|Tamdóka]]'s identity is repeatedly anchored to his father: "And the son of tall Wazí-kuté" (007-235). When [[tamdoka|Tamdóka]] acts, he does so "by the son of tall Wazí-kuté, [[tamdoka|Tamdóka]]" (008-238).

Even in hostile contexts, the lineage is invoked: "The son of Chief Wazí-kuté" (009-018). The repeated formula "son of tall/Chief Wazí-kuté" functions almost as an epithet, binding [[tamdoka|Tamdóka]]'s actions to his father's name.

Though Wazí-kuté never appears directly in the narrative action, his presence is felt through his son. The name itself carries weight — [[wa-zi-ya|Wazíya]] is the [[dakota|Dakota]] god of winter, a powerful and feared spirit, and the chief's name echoes this formidable association.

## 历史/文化背景

The name Wazí-kuté connects to Wazíya, the [[dakota|Dakota]] spirit of the North and god of winter — "Wazíya came down from the North" (016-000 context). In [[dakota|Dakota]] cosmology, Wazíya is one of the four winds or directional powers, associated with cold, ice, and the northern regions.

A chief bearing a name related to this spirit would carry formidable, even fearsome connotations. Gordon's emphasis on Wazí-kuté's height ("tall Wazí-kuté") reinforces this — he looms over the narrative as winter looms over the plains. The repeated epithet "son of tall Wazí-kuté" makes [[tamdoka|Tamdóka]]'s every action a reflection on his father's formidable reputation.
