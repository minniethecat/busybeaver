# Voyageurs

The **voyageurs** were French fur traders and explorers who traversed the waterways of the upper [[Mississippi River|Mississippi]] region in the 17th and 18th centuries. They were the first Europeans to make sustained contact with the [[Dakota]] people and feature prominently in the historical backdrop of [[Hanford Lennox Gordon|H. L. Gordon]]'s *[[Legend|Legends of the Northwest]]*.

## Origins

The French presence in the upper Mississippi dates to the period of early exploration. Gordon notes:

> ...the early French traders and voyageurs. "Dakota" signifies...
> (P01-010)

It was these traders who gave the [[dakota|Dakotas]] the name "[[dakota|Sioux]]" — a French abbreviation of the [[ojibway|Ojibway]] exonym *Nadowessioux*.

## Life of the Voyageur

Gordon's verse includes a rousing passage that captures the voyageur's life on the rivers:

> Merry is the life of the gay voyageur
> (009-133)

The refrain repeats through the poem (009-135, 009-141, 009-143, 009-149, 009-151), emphasizing the rhythm of paddling and the freedom of the trader's life.

Their [[song|songs]] became part of the cultural landscape of the region:

> ...favorite songs of the French voyageurs.
> (007-096)

## Travel and Trade Routes

The voyageurs moved by [[Canoe|canoe]] along the great waterways:

- The [[Mississippi River]] — the great artery of trade
- The [[St. Lawrence River]] — connecting to the Atlantic
- [[Lake Superior]] — the western gateway to [[ojibway|Ojibway]] territory
- The [[St. Louis River]] — route to the lake's western end

Their travel was physically demanding. Gordon describes the rhythmic labor:

> See! the voyageurs bend to their oars
> (009-227)

The voyageur's luggage was matched by the weight of expectation:

> is matched by the voyageur's luggage.
> (009-234)

## Homeward Journey

Gordon captures the voyageurs' return from the interior:

> of the voyageurs homeward returning,
> (009-123)

These returns brought furs, goods, and news — connecting the [[dakota|Dakota]] country to the broader French colonial world.

## Role in Gordon's Legends

### DuLuth

The most prominent voyageur figure in Gordon's [[legend|legends]] is [[Daniel DuLuth]], who appears as a diplomat mediating between the [[dakota|Dakota]] and [[Ojibway Nation|Ojibway]]. DuLuth addresses the [[dakota|Dakota]] braves at a feast in the [[Winona]] cycle (009-110).

### Documentation

French traders were often the first to witness and record [[dakota|Dakota]] ceremonies and [[tradition|customs]], including the [[Feast of the Virgins]] (003-108). Their accounts provided the historical foundation for Gordon's literary reconstructions.
