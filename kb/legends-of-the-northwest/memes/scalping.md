# Scalping

Scalping — the taking of an enemy's scalp as a trophy of war — appears in Gordon's *[[legend|Legends of the Northwest]]* as a recurring feature of [[dakota|Dakota]] and [[ojibway|Ojibway]] inter-tribal warfare.

## Definition / Identity

Scalping was a warfare practice among the [[dakota|Dakota]] and neighboring tribes, in which a warrior removed and preserved the scalp of a slain enemy as proof of victory and a mark of honor. Gordon portrays it as an integral element of the [[dakota|Dakota]] warrior code, particularly in conflicts with the [[ojibway|Ojibway]] ([[ojibway-nation|Chippewa]]) and Cree (003-037).

- **Origin**: Indigenous North American warfare custom
- **Domain**: warfare / custom
- **Related terms**: [[tomahawk|Tomahawk]], [[death-song|Death Song]]

## In the Book

The scalp appears throughout Gordon's poetic [[legend|legends]] as a symbol of martial honor and the brutality of inter-tribal conflict. Five distinct passages reference scalping, spanning both *[[feast-of-the-virgins|The Feast of the Virgins]]* and *[[legend-of-the-falls|The Legend of the Falls]]*.

### Role in the Legends

In *[[feast-of-the-virgins|The Feast of the Virgins]]*, scalping is mentioned in the context of the [[dakota|Dakota]] conflict with the Cree, where warriors took "the scalp of a warrior slain" as a war trophy (003-037). Gordon emphasizes that the honored [[dakota|Dakota]] warriors never took the scalps of women or children — "never the stain of an infant slain, or the blood of a mother that plead in vain" stained the plumes of the brave [[dakota|Dakota]] (003-040).

The [[dakota-ojibway-war|Dakota-Ojibway War]] passages describe hand-to-hand combat where the "scalp-knife gleamed in a swarthy fist" (004-197) and warriors fell "clutching the scalp of his foe and dead" (004-202). After Chief [[wakawa|Wakâwa]] fell in battle, his warriors snatched "the unscalped corpse of their honored chief" from the enemy, carrying him home with honor intact (004-215).

In *[[legend-of-the-falls|The Legend of the Falls]]*, the warrior Kenéw is described as having "scalps he won numbered seven by Kenéw-feathers" (013-254), indicating scalps were counted and recorded as marks of a warrior's achievements.

### Representative Passages

> "And each for the scalp of a warrior slain, when down on his camp from the northern plain, with their murder cries rode the bloody Cree." — (003-037)

> "Or the scalp-knife gleamed in a swarthy fist... Lay clutching the scalp of his foe and dead, with a [[tomahawk]] sunk in his ghastly head." — (004-197)
