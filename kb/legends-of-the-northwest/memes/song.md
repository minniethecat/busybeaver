# Song

**Song** was the vital medium of [[Dakota]] and [[Ojibway]] oral tradition — a way of preserving history, expressing emotion, conducting ceremony, and communicating with the [[Spirit|spirit world]].

Gordon's *[[Legend|Legends of the Northwest]]* are themselves literary adaptations of [[dakota|Dakota]] song [[tradition|traditions]], rendered into English verse.

## Ceremonial Songs

Song accompanied every major [[dakota|Dakota]] ceremony. The [[Feast of the Virgins]] was a particularly rich occasion for ceremonial singing:

> Round and round to the merry song
> (004-014)

> Their flying feet and their song inspire.
> (004-017)

The transition from song to dance marked the completion of ritual phases:

> They have finished the song and the sacred dance,
> (004-018)

## The War Song

The war song (*red war dance*) was raised before battle to prepare warriors:

> Raise the song of red war; for your chief must go;
> (004-159)

> They raised the song of the red war dance.
> (004-178)

These songs combined drum rhythms with vocal calls, creating a powerful psychological preparation for combat.

## The Voice as Instrument

Gordon's poetry frequently likens the human voice to song. The [[dakota|Dakota]] maiden's voice was compared to natural music:

> And her voice like the song of the mountain stream;
> (001-160)

The whippoorwill's call echoes the songs of the [[spirit|spirit-land]]:

> And forever the songs of the whippowils
> (002-141)

## Song and the Individual

Individual expression through song was celebrated in [[dakota|Dakota]] culture. A maiden might sing her joy:

> ...heard their voice in her happy song.
> (004-122)

Or pour her emotion into the night:

> Poured the stream of her song on the starry night.
> (004-124)

## Gordon's Musical Language

Gordon's verse continually references song and singing. His lexical field of music includes:

- The lark's song: "tripped from the tee like the song of a lark" (003-126)
- The seasonal song: "Shrilling his song in the azure seas" (003-211)
- The amber air: "And trills her song on the amber air" (004-237)
