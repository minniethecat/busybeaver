# Ojibway

The Ojibway (also called [[ojibway-nation|Ojibwa]] or [[ojibway-nation|Chippewa]]) are an [[algonkin|Algonkin]]-speaking Native American people who appear throughout Gordon's *[[legend|Legend]]s of the Northwest* as both rivals of the [[dakota|Dakotas]] and subjects of their own legend cycle.

## Identity

The [[ojibway-nation|Ojibways]] are a distinct people from the [[dakota|Dakotas]], speaking an [[algonkin|Algonkin]] language rather than the Siouan [[dakota|Dakota]] tongue. At the time of the book's setting, their territory included the shores of [[lake-superior|Lake Superior]] and the upper [[lake-superior|Great Lake]]s region — at that time "the home of the [[ojibway-nation|Ojibways]]" (009-153).

## Names and Language

- **Ojibway / [[ojibway-nation|Ojibwa]]**: The people's own name for themselves
- **[[ojibway-nation|Chippewa]]**: An alternate English rendering of the same name (009-153)
- **Language family**: [[algonkin|Algonkin]] ([[algonkin|Algonquian]])

The [[ojibway-nation|Ojibways]] gave their own names to major geographical features of the region:
- *Michesee Seebee* — "Big River," the Mississippi (010-116)
- *[[lake-superior|Michee Gumee]]* — "[[lake-superior|Great Lake]]," [[lake-superior|Lake Superior]] (010-118)

## In the Book

### Role in the Legends

The [[ojibway-nation|Ojibways]] appear as antagonists in the [[dakota|Dakota]] [[legend|legends]]. They are described as "crafty and creeping" (012-128) from the [[dakota|Dakota]] perspective, and the [[dakota|Dakota]] braves fear dying "by the cruel Ojibway's hand" (005-109).

### The Sea-Gull

The final legend cycle — *The [[sea-gull|Sea-Gull]]* — is explicitly subtitled "The [[ojibway-nation|Ojibwa]] [[legend|Legend]] of the [[pictured-rocks|Pictured Rocks]] of [[lake-superior|Lake Superior]]," shifting the narrative perspective from [[dakota|Dakota]] to Ojibway tradition.
