The Medicine-Man (Wicasta Wakan in [[dakota|Dakota]]) is the [[soul|spirit]]ual healer and magician of the [[dakota|Dakota]] people — a figure who bridges the human and [[soul|spirit]] worlds through secret knowledge and ritual power (017-044).

## Definition / Identity

The [[dakota|Dakota]] term Wicasta Wakan translates as "sacred man" or "mystery man" (017-152). The Medicine-Man's primary function is to cast out the "unclean [[soul|spirit]]" — to heal through [[soul|spirit]]ual intervention (017-044). The role encompasses both healing and magic, attributed to secret preparations and membership in exclusive ritual societies (018-028).

- **Origin**: [[dakota|Dakota]] [[soul|spirit]]ual tradition; the institution predates European contact
- **Language**: [[dakota|Dakota]] (Wicasta Wakan)
- **Domain**: [[soul|spirit]]uality / ceremony / social-role

Medicine-Men belong to a "secret order of 'Medicine-men,' or 'Sons of Unktéhee' — the [[spirit|Spirit]] of the Waters" (016-063). This order uses a preparation enabling them to handle fire and boiling water without injury (016-060, 016-061). Chiefs and leading warriors usually belong to this order (016-062), making it both [[soul|spirit]]ual and political.

## In the Book

Gordon references Medicine-Men primarily through his scholarly footnotes, documenting their practices with ethnographic precision. The Medicine-Men are connected to the most sacred ceremonies: the [[wakan-teepee|Wakan Wacipee]] (Sacred [[dance|Dance]]) performed in the [[wakan-teepee|Wakan Teepee]] (018-030), and the severe [[heyoka|Heyóka]] Wacípee ordeal (016-058).

### Role in the Legends

In the poetic narrative, Medicine-Men appear as background figures of [[soul|spirit]]ual authority. Gordon's footnotes reveal their central role in [[dakota|Dakota]] social structure. The chiefs and leading warriors are typically initiates (016-062), making the Medicine-Man order both a [[soul|spirit]]ual society and a political institution.

The ability to handle fire without harm (016-060, 016-061) is presented as evidence of genuine [[soul|spirit]]ual power recognized by the [[dakota|Dakota]] community. The footnote apparatus (016–018) provides detailed accounts of initiation rites, secret preparations, and the Medicine-Man's role in the most sacred ceremonies.
