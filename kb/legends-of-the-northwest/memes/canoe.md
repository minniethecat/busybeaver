# Canoe

The birch-bark canoe, essential to [[dakota|Dakota]] and [[ojibway|Ojibway]] transportation.

## Cultural Role

Gordon's [[legend|legends]] are set in the world of the [[dakota|Dakota]] — a world of rivers, prairies, and the animals that sustained [[dakota|Dakota]] life. The canoe is woven into the fabric of the narratives.

## In the Book

References to canoes appear throughout *[[legend|Legend]]s of the Northwest*, reflecting their importance in [[dakota|Dakota]] daily life and oral tradition. Gordon's preface notes his study of [[dakota|Dakota]] [[tradition|customs]] and [[tradition|traditions]] (P01-007), grounded in the material culture of the prairie and its animals.

The [[legend|legends]] preserve a way of life centered on the canoe before "the day of the [[dakota|Dakotas]]" passed (P01-046).


The [[voyageurs|voyageur]]s and [[voyageurs|French traders]] traveled these same waterways by canoe (P01-009). Gordon's *Prelude* invokes the Mississippi as the great water highway that shaped [[dakota|Dakota]] life (001-020).
