# Raven

The son of [[panther|Panther]] and the [[sea-gull|Sea-Gull]], known in [[dakota|Dakota]] as Kâk-kâh-gè. Raven is a central figure in the latter half of "The [[legend|Legend]] of [[falls-of-st-anthony|the Falls]]" (Chapters XIII–XV), mediating between his estranged parents and witnessing the supernatural events that explain the origin of [[falls-of-st-anthony|the Falls]].

## 身份与背景

- **Tribe / Nation**: [[dakota|Dakota]] ([[dakota|Sioux]])
- **Role**: Hunter, son, messenger between parents
- **Family**: Father: [[panther|Panther]]; Mother: the [[sea-gull|Sea-Gull]]; stepmother: the [[red-fox|Red Fox]]

## 在书中的角色

### 相关传奇

Raven appears in "The [[legend|Legend]] of [[falls-of-st-anthony|the Falls]]" (Chapters XIII–XV) as the child of [[panther|Panther]]'s first marriage. His role grows from a named infant to an active participant — watching, waiting, speaking to his father, and ultimately witnessing the transformative events.

### 关键事件

Raven is named at birth: "And they called the boy the Raven — Called him Kâk-kâh-gè — the Raven" (013-122, 013-123). Even as a child, he is cherished: [[panther|Panther]] "Took his blanket for the Raven" (013-133).

As a youth, Raven hunts alongside his father: "Forth went [[panther|Panther]] and the Raven" (014-257). He serves as a voice of conscience, speaking to [[panther|Panther]] about the domestic tension: "Then to [[panther|Panther]] spoke the Raven" (014-266), and his father responds: "Then spoke [[panther|Panther]] to the Raven" (014-279).

Raven keeps vigil: "So the Raven watched and waited" (014-285). He stays with the infant sibling: "Slumbered by the side of Raven" (014-288). His mother the [[sea-gull|Sea-Gull]] entrusts him with responsibility: "Spoke the mother to the Raven" (014-310), and gives him instructions: "Hither bring her, O my Raven" (014-334).

Raven ultimately witnesses and recounts the supernatural resolution: "Raven told his tale of wonder" (015-002). He is present at the final reconciliation: "Raven sat with [[sea-gull|Waub-omeé-meé]]" (015-022).

## 历史/文化背景

Raven is a legendary figure. The raven (Kâk-kâh-gè) holds symbolic significance in many Native American [[tradition|traditions]] as a messenger, trickster, or intermediary between worlds. In Gordon's poem, Raven serves as the bridge between the broken marriage of [[panther|Panther]] and [[sea-gull|Sea-Gull]] — the child who witnesses, speaks truth, and ultimately sees the family restored.
