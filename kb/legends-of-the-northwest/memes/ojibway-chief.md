# Ojibway Chief

The unnamed [[ojibway|Ojibway]] leaders who appear as antagonists in the [[dakota|Dakota]] legend cycles.

## Connection to the Legends

These figures appear in or contributed to the preservation of *[[legend|Legend]]s of the Northwest*. Gordon's years of study among the [[dakota|Dakotas]] (P01-007) informed his portrayal of [[dakota|Dakota]] and [[ojibway|Ojibway]] characters.

## In the Book

The [[legend|legends]] depict a world populated by warriors, maidens, chiefs, and editors whose combined efforts preserved these [[dakota|Dakota]] and [[ojibway|Ojibway]] [[tradition|traditions]] in English verse.


The [[ojibway-nation|Ojibways]] appear as the [[dakota|Dakota]]'s rivals, with their chiefs leading raids and battles (009-153). Gordon's *The [[sea-gull|Sea-Gull]]* presents the [[ojibway|Ojibway]] perspective, shifting from [[dakota|Dakota]] to [[ojibway|Ojibway]] oral tradition. The [[ojibway|Ojibway]] called [[lake-superior|Lake Superior]] [[lake-superior|Michee Gumee]] (010-118).
