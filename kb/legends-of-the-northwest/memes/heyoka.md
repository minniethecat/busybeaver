Heyóka is the sacred contrary [[soul|spirit]]-being in [[dakota|Dakota]] [[legend|myth]]ology — a "magical god" (002-003) whose nature reverses all normal order and who is honored through one of the most severe ceremonial ordeals known to the [[dakota|Dakotas]].

## Definition / Identity

Heyóka embodies the principle of sacred inversion in [[dakota|Dakota]] [[soul|spirit]]ual belief. The [[soul|spirit]] is the "reverse of nature in all things" (016-056): it feels cold in summer heat, suffers from heat in winter cold, cries when it laughs, and laughs when it cries (016-055).

- **Origin**: [[dakota|Dakota]] [[soul|spirit]]ual tradition; the name derives from the [[dakota|Dakota]] language
- **Language**: [[dakota|Dakota]]
- **Domain**: [[soul|spirit]]uality / ceremony / [[legend|myth]]ology

Heyóka is universally feared and reverenced by the [[dakota|Dakotas]] (016-057). The [[soul|spirit]]'s ceremonial dance — the Heyóka Wacípee — is one of the most grueling ordeals in [[dakota|Dakota]] religious practice, now rarely celebrated due to its severity (016-058, 016-059).

## In the Book

Gordon invokes Heyóka throughout the Prelude and "The [[feast-of-the-virgins|Feast of the Virgins]]," presenting the [[soul|spirit]] as a presiding supernatural force over the ceremonial gathering.

### Role in the Legends

In the Prelude, the "Sons of Heyóka" celebrate (001-197) and the braves dress for "the feast of Heyóka" (001-203), establishing the [[soul|spirit]] as the patron of the great ceremonial gathering.

In "The [[feast-of-the-virgins|Feast of the Virgins]]," Heyóka is addressed directly — "Great is Heyóka, the magical god" (002-003) — and the celebrants are "sons of Heyóka" (002-011). The [[soul|spirit]] crowns devotees with their "heart's desire" (002-014, 002-024).

In "[[winona|Winona]]," the young [[wanata|Wanâta]] is "stronger than Heyóka's giant form" (012-219). The footnote (016-057–016-059) provides the most detailed ethnographic account: the [[soul|spirit]]'s contrary nature, the severity of its dance, and its connection to the "Medicine-men" or "[[medicine-man|Sons of Unktéhee]]" (016-063).
