# Clan

The clan is the fundamental kinship-based social unit of the [[dakota|Dakota]] and [[ojibway|Ojibway]] peoples, appearing in Gordon's [[legend|legends]] as the framework through which chiefs and warriors organize their communities.

## Definition / Identity

A clan is a lineage-based social group tracing descent from a common ancestor, serving as the primary unit of political and social organization in traditional [[dakota|Dakota]] and [[ojibway|Ojibway]] societies:

- **Origin**: Indigenous to the [[dakota|Dakota]] and [[ojibway|Ojibway]] peoples
- **Language**: [[dakota|Dakota]] / [[ojibway|Ojibway]] (various terms including *doodem* in [[ojibway|Ojibway]])
- **Domain**: social-structure / kinship / political

In Gordon's poetry, the clan appears as a political and [[soul|spirit]]ual entity, with chiefs identified by their clan leadership. The "mystical clan" (007-227) suggests a group with [[soul|spirit]]ual or supernatural affiliations, while the "treacherous clan" (010-095) introduces political tension into the narrative.

## In the Book

Gordon references the clan as a social unit that defines a character's identity and allegiance. The chief is identified as "the chief of the clan" (008-115), and the clan's sons are portrayed as warrior elites blessed by [[soul|spirit]]ual favor.

### Role in the Legends

In the [[winona|Winona]] legend, the "chief of the mystical clan" (007-227) appears as a figure of [[soul|spirit]]ual authority. Gordon describes "the god-favored sons of the clan" (008-244), linking clan membership to divine blessing and warrior prestige.

In the [[minnetonka|Minnetonka]] story, a character is revealed as "the head of a treacherous clan" (010-095), introducing betrayal and political conflict into the narrative. The clan thus serves as both a source of identity and a vehicle for plot tension.

The clan system underlies much of Gordon's social world-building, even when not explicitly named. Characters' loyalties, marriages, and conflicts are shaped by clan affiliations that Gordon assumes his readers understand.

### Representative Passages

> "The chief of the mystical clan" (007-227)

> "the god-favored sons of the clan" (008-244)

> "But the head of a treacherous clan" (010-095)
