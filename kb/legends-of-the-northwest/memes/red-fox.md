# Red Fox

A crafty [[dakota|Dakota]] woman who becomes the second wife of [[panther|Panther]] in Gordon's "The [[legend|Legend]] of [[falls-of-st-anthony|the Falls]]" (Chapters XIII–XV). The Red Fox is the antagonist of the legend — her rivalry with the [[sea-gull|Sea-Gull]] and her scheming nature drive the domestic conflict at the story's heart.

## 身份与背景

- **Tribe / Nation**: [[dakota|Dakota]] ([[dakota|Sioux]])
- **Role**: Second wife, rival, antagonist
- **Family**: Husband: [[panther|Panther]] (014-013); co-wife and rival: the [[sea-gull|Sea-Gull]] (014-098)

## 在书中的角色

### 相关传奇

The Red Fox is the central antagonist of "The [[legend|Legend]] of [[falls-of-st-anthony|the Falls]]" (Chapters XIII–XV). Her entry into [[panther|Panther]]'s household as second wife precipitates the legend's crisis, and her crafty nature is contrasted with the [[sea-gull|Sea-Gull]]'s patient fidelity.

### 关键事件

The Red Fox enters the narrative walking from the island: "Walked the Red Fox from the island" (013-285). She immediately attracts the hunter's attention: "On the Red Fox smiled the hunter" (013-289). [[panther|Panther]] marries her: "So the [[panther|Panther]] wed the Red Fox" (014-013). As the new wife, she receives preferential treatment: "Choicest bits of all for Red Fox" (014-025).

The rivalry between the two wives is vividly rendered. The Red Fox and the [[sea-gull|Sea-Gull]] sit together in the wigwam in tense proximity: "Sat the [[sea-gull|Sea-Gull]] and the Red Fox" (014-098). The Red Fox is industrious — "Red Fox made a swing. She fastened" (014-214) — and provocative: she "bantered [[sea-gull|Sea-Gull]], saying" (014-221).

The Red Fox's challenge is direct: "Dares the Red Fox — dares my rival" (014-227). She is described as swift and dangerous: "Red Fox, swifter than red lightning" (014-237). When the [[sea-gull|Sea-Gull]] disappears, the Red Fox is found sitting in the wigwam (014-248), offering only the observation "'[[sea-gull|Sea-Gull]] wanders late' said Red Fox" (014-253).

The [[sea-gull|Sea-Gull]]'s perspective names her as the culprit: "For the Crafty Red Fox robbed us" (014-315). She is called "the Crafty Red Fox" — her defining epithet. [[panther|Panther]]'s feelings toward her sour: "Hateful to his heart was Red Fox" (015-007). The Red Fox remains in the wigwam (015-061), and a final confrontation occurs when "the Red Fox saw the [[sea-gull|Sea-Gull]]" (015-067) — recognizing her rival's transformed state.

## 历史/文化背景

The Red Fox is a legendary figure embodying the archetype of the cunning rival in [[dakota|Dakota]] oral tradition. Her characterization — crafty, swift, and disruptive — uses the fox as a natural symbol of cleverness and predation, common across Native American storytelling. Gordon's portrayal is nuanced: the Red Fox is not purely evil but ambitious and clever, a foil to the [[sea-gull|Sea-Gull]]'s quiet endurance. Her presence raises the legend's central moral question about fidelity, jealousy, and the consequences of favoring the new over the faithful.
