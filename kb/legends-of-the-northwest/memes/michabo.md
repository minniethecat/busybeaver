# Michabo

The [[wakan|Great Spirit]] of the [[algonkin|Algonkin]] peoples, smoking his pipe in [[ojibway|Ojibway]] legend.

## Connection to the Legends

Gordon's *[[legend|Legend]]s of the Northwest* references Michabo in the context of [[dakota|Dakota]] history and culture. His preface details his years of study (P01-007) and his debt to previous scholars and missionaries.

## In the Book

Gordon's footnotes and preface acknowledge multiple sources, including Riggs (P01-063), whose work among the [[dakota|Dakotas]] provided the ethnographic foundation for the [[legend|legends]].


Michabo — the Good, [[wakan|Great Spirit]] of the [[algonkin|Algonkin]]s (018-115) — is depicted in [[ojibway|Ojibway]] legend as smoking his pipe (010-226). Gordon distinguishes between [[dakota|Dakota]] and [[algonkin|Algonkin]] [[soul|spirit]]ual [[tradition|traditions]], with Michabo representing the latter.
