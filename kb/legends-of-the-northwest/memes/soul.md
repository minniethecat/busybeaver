# Soul

The soul, in the context of Gordon's [[legend|legends]], represents the immortal spiritual essence of a person — a core concept in [[dakota|Dakota]] and [[ojibway|Ojibway]] belief that shapes how characters face death, loss, and the afterlife.

## Definition / Identity

In traditional [[dakota|Dakota]] and [[ojibway|Ojibway]] spirituality, the soul is the indestructible spiritual core of a person that survives bodily death and journeys to the spirit world:

- **Origin**: Indigenous to the [[dakota|Dakota]] and [[ojibway|Ojibway]] peoples
- **Language**: [[dakota|Dakota]]: *wanagee* or *nagi*; [[ojibway|Ojibway]]: *ojichaag*
- **Domain**: spirituality / afterlife / belief

Gordon's appendix references the "Bridge of Souls" (019-024), a concept associated with the passage from the living world to the afterlife. The soul in Gordon's poetry is not abstract theology — it is felt in grief, longing, and the moment of death.

## In the Book

Gordon uses "soul" throughout his poems to convey inner emotional states and spiritual beliefs. The word appears in moments of grief, vengeance, love, and transcendence.

### Role in the Legends

The soul in Gordon's poetry often represents intense inner experience. A character "wrapped himself in his gloomy soul" (003-058) in isolation, while another "feasted his soul on the maiden's eyes" (003-149) in love. The soul can be "troubled" (004-093) or driven by "revenge in his savage soul" (005-003).

But Gordon also invokes the soul in its spiritual dimension. The belief that "the soul of the brave is immortal" (008-178) consoles the living after a warrior's death. In [[winona|Winona]]'s lament, a mother declares "She will die but her soul will rejoice" (011-164), framing death as release rather than extinction.

The most poignant use appears in the cry: "Behold, for the light of my soul is gone" (012-135, 012-151) — a figure of speech that equates the soul with life itself.

### Representative Passages

> "for the soul of the brave is immortal" (008-178)

> "She will die but her soul will rejoice" (011-164)

> "Behold, for the light of my soul is gone" (012-135)

> "And feasted his soul on the maiden's eyes" (003-149)
