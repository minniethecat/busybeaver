Colonel Josiah Snelling (1782–1828) was a United States Army officer who commanded the construction of [[fort-snelling|Fort Snelling]] at the confluence of the Mississippi and [[minnesota|Minnesota]] rivers. The fort, completed in 1825, became a crucial military and trading post in the Upper Midwest, and the surrounding region figures prominently in Gordon's "[[legend|Legend]]s of the Northwest." Snelling also authored "Tales of the Northwest," a collection of frontier stories that Gordon cited in his annotations.

## 在书中的角色

### Fort Snelling 作为地理锚点

[[fort-snelling|Fort Snelling]] serves as a key geographical reference in Gordon's "[[winona|Winona]]" narrative. The region around the fort — the confluence of the Mississippi and [[minnesota|Minnesota]] rivers — is where much of the [[dakota|Dakota]] action takes place:

> Mendota, properly Mdó-tè — meaning the outlet of lake or river into another, commonly applied to the region about Fort Snelling. (009-026)

Gordon uses [[fort-snelling|Fort Snelling]] as a fixed point in the landscape, anchoring the legendary geography of [[winona|Winona]]'s story in real-world cartography that his 19th-century readers would recognize.

### 作为 Gordon 的文献来源

Gordon cites Snelling's published work as an ethnographic reference. In a footnote discussing [[dakota|Dakota]] material culture, Gordon draws on Snelling's firsthand observations:

> (Footnote Snelling's) Tales of the Northwest, p. 21. Mackenzie's Travels. (016-050)

Gordon also references Snelling's work in connection with [[dakota|Dakota]] [[legend|legends]] and [[tradition|traditions]]:

> (Snelling's) "Tales of the Northwest," p. 286. note 15. (018-016)

This shows that Snelling, like other early frontier authors, served as an anthropological source for Gordon's literary project — a chain of knowledge transmission from military observer to poet.

## 历史/文化背景

Colonel Josiah Snelling served in the U.S. Army during the War of 1812 and was later assigned to oversee construction of the fort that would bear his name. [[fort-snelling|Fort Snelling]], situated on the bluff overlooking the confluence of the Mississippi and [[minnesota|Minnesota]] rivers, was strategically positioned to regulate the fur trade and manage relations between [[dakota|Dakota]] and [[ojibway|Ojibway]] peoples. The fort's presence marked the expanding reach of the United States into [[dakota|Dakota]] territory.

Snelling's "Tales of the Northwest" (1830) collected stories and observations from his frontier experiences. While less well-known today than Schoolcraft's ethnographic works or Longfellow's poetry, the book provided Gordon with regional color and firsthand detail for his footnotes.
