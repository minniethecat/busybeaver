# Lake Superior

**Lake Superior** — *Gitchee Gumee* in [[Ojibway]], *Medé Tânka* in [[Dakota]] (016-142) — is the largest of the Great Lakes and the setting of *The [[sea-gull|Sea-Gull]]*, the final legend in [[Hanford Lennox Gordon|H. L. Gordon]]'s *[[Legend|Legends of the Northwest]]*.

## Names

The lake bore different names across the peoples who knew it:

| People | Name | Meaning |
|--------|------|---------|
| [[ojibway|Ojibway]] | *Gitchee Gumee* | Great Sea / Great Lake |
| [[dakota|Dakota]] | *Medé Tânka* | Great Lake |
| French | *Lac Supérieur* | Upper Lake |

Gordon records the [[ojibway|Ojibway]] name:

> Gitchee Gumee — Great Lake, the Ojibway name of Lake Superior.
> (010-118)

The [[dakota|Dakotas]] had only one name for the lake, simply signifying its immense scale (016-142).

## The Ojibway Homeland

At the time of Gordon's [[legend|legends]], Lake Superior was the home of the [[Ojibway Nation]]:

> Lake Superior — at that time the home of the Ojibways. (Chippewas)
> (009-153)

The [[ojibway|Ojibway]] controlled the southern and western shores of the lake, and their territory extended along the [[St. Louis River]]:

> The Ojibways call the St. Louis River...
> (010-119)

## In Gordon's Legends

### The Sea-Gull

Gordon's *[[sea-gull|Sea-Gull]]* legend (Sections 16–17) is set on the shores and islands of Lake Superior. The story centers on an [[ojibway|Ojibway]] maiden transformed into a sea-gull, a legend that Schoolcraft later recorded:

> shore of Lake Superior many years ago. Schoolcraft may have...
> (018-153)

### The Pictured Rocks

The [[Pictured Rocks]] — dramatic sandstone cliffs on Lake Superior's southern shore — feature in the [[legend|legends]] as a landscape of [[soul|spirit]]ual significance.

## Fur Trade Routes

Lake Superior was a critical node in the [[Voyageurs|fur trade]] network. The French *[[voyageurs|voyageur]]s* traversed its waters, connecting the Great Lakes to the Mississippi watershed:

> Mud River to Lake Superior — was for centuries and still...
> (010-139)

The [[Dalles of the St. Louis|Dalles of the St. Louis River]] (010-121) marked a dramatic passage on the route to the lake.
