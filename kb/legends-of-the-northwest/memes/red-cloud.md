# Red Cloud

A tall, proud, and fierce [[dakota|Dakota]] warrior, central figure in "The [[feast-of-the-virgins|Feast of the Virgins]]" legend cycle (Chapters I–V). Known in [[dakota|Dakota]] as Mahpíya Dúta, the Red Cloud is the bravest warrior of his band — yet his pride and jealousy set the legend's tragic events in motion.

## 身份与背景

- **Tribe / Nation**: [[dakota|Dakota]] ([[dakota|Sioux]])
- **Role**: Warrior, suitor
- **Family**: Wife: the [[virgin-star|Virgin Star]] (promised) (002-151)

## 在书中的角色

### 相关传奇

Red Cloud is a principal character in "The [[feast-of-the-virgins|Feast of the Virgins]]" (Chapters I–V). He is the suitor of the fairest maiden at the feast, but his pride leads to suspicion and betrayal, making him both a romantic lead and a tragic antagonist.

### 关键事件

Red Cloud is introduced as a formidable figure: "Mahpíya Dúta the tall Red Cloud" (001-164), "brave and proud was the tall Red Cloud" (001-170). Among all warriors, "the bravest of all was the tall Red Cloud" (002-027).

At the [[feast-of-the-virgins|Feast of the Virgins]], Red Cloud speaks to the assembled braves: "Proud Red Cloud turned to the braves and said…" (002-037). He declares boldly that "Brave Red Cloud is only afraid of fear" (002-050).

The fairest maiden is pledged to him: "pledged me as wife to the tall Red Cloud" (002-151), and she accepts: "I will be wife to the fierce Red Cloud!" (002-156). Yet jealousy consumes him — "Dark was the visage of grim Red Cloud" (003-051). He suspects betrayal: "Does the tall Red Cloud for the false one sigh?" (003-067).

Red Cloud's pride turns vengeful: "The proud Red Cloud will revenge his wrong" (003-105). His appearance darkens throughout the legend: "The fierce Red Cloud with the flaunting feather" (003-186), "frowning, fierce-eyed, tall Red Cloud" (004-023). By the end, he is described as "the sullen warrior, the tall Red Cloud" (004-042), and the narrative compares him to "the serpent that tempted the proud Red Cloud" (005-002).

## 历史/文化背景

Red Cloud is a legendary figure, not the historical Oglala leader of the same name. Gordon's Red Cloud embodies the archetype of the proud warrior brought low by jealousy — a cautionary figure in the [[dakota|Dakota]] moral universe. The epithet "Mahpíya Dúta" (Red Cloud) carries symbolic weight: clouds that are red at sunset or dawn, beautiful but foreboding, mirroring the character's arc from pride to darkness.
