# Feast of the Virgins

The **Feast of the Virgins** — *[[wiwaste|Wiwâstè]]* in [[dakota|Dakota]] — is the major ceremonial gathering of the [[Dakota]] people and the central event of [[Hanford Lennox Gordon|H. L. Gordon]]'s first legend cycle, spanning Sections 2–5 of *[[Legend|Legends of the Northwest]]*.

## The Ceremony

The Feast was a multi-day gathering that combined feasting, [[Dance|ceremonial dance]], courtship, and social bonding. Gordon introduces it in the opening of the cycle:

> And the Feast of the Virgins is then to be;
> (003-108)

> The Feast of the Virgins is then to be!
> (003-109)

The refrain-like repetition in Gordon's verse emphasizes the central importance of the Feast to [[dakota|Dakota]] social life.

## Sacred Character

The Feast was explicitly sacred in nature. Gordon emphasizes this repeatedly:

> For the Virgins' Feast is a sacred thing.
> (004-011)

> The Virgins Feast is a Sacred thing:
> (004-047)

The ceremonial [[Song|songs]] and [[dance|dances]] performed at the Feast were not entertainment but ritual acts with [[soul|spirit]]ual significance.

## Winona at the Feast

The Feast of the Virgins is the setting for [[Winona]]'s first major appearance in the [[legend|legends]]. As the fairest [[dakota|Dakota]] maiden, she occupies a central role:

> Wiwâstè sings at the Virgins Feast;
> (003-251)

The Feast provides the social context in which the romantic tensions of the [[winona|Winona]] cycle unfold — the rivalry between [[dakota|Dakota]] and [[Ojibway]] suitors, and the first stirrings of the tragic conflict to come.

## Structure and Timing

The Feast followed a defined sequence of events:

- **Assembly**: [[dakota|Dakota]] bands gathered from across the territory
- **Sacred [[dance|Dance]]**: Maidens performed the [[virgins-dance|Virgins' Dance]] as the opening ritual
- **Feasting**: Communal meals marked the gathering's social dimension
- **Courtship**: The Feast provided a sanctioned setting for matchmaking
- **War Preparation**: Warriors raised the [[Song|war song]] as the Feast concluded

The timing of the Feast is marked in the narrative:

> Till the Feast of the Virgins I bade him wait
> (005-014)

> When the Feast of the Virgins is past," she said
> (005-156)

## French Documentation

French [[Voyageurs|traders and voyageurs]] witnessed and recorded the Feast of the Virgins during the early contact period:

> And the Feast of the Virgins is now to be. The early French traders...
> (003-214)

Gordon's footnote identifies the [[dakota|Dakota]] name: *[[wiwaste|Wiwâstè]]* — [[dance|Virgins Dance]] (or Feast) (015-177).
