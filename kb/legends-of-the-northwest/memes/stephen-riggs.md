# Stephen R. Riggs

**Stephen Return Riggs** was a missionary to the [[Dakota]] people and the most important ethnographic source for [[Hanford Lennox Gordon|H. L. Gordon]]'s *[[Legend|Legends of the Northwest]]*. His linguistic and cultural studies provided the scholarly foundation upon which Gordon built his legendary narratives.

## Missionary Work

Riggs spent forty years as a missionary among the [[dakota|Dakotas]], dedicating his life to understanding their language and [[tradition|customs]]:

> Riggs who, for forty years, has been a student of the Dakota language
> (017-143)

His firsthand experience gave him an authority that Gordon repeatedly acknowledged in his own work.

## Scholarly Contributions

### Grammar and Dictionary of the Dakota Language

Riggs authored the foundational reference works on the [[dakota|Dakota]] language, which Gordon consulted extensively:

> ...greatly aided by Rev. S. R. Riggs, author of the Grammar and Dictionary of the Dakota Language
> (P01-062)

### Tah-Koo Wah-Kan

Riggs also wrote *Tah-Koo [[wakan|Wah-Kan]]* (The Gospel Among the [[dakota|Dakotas]]), documenting [[dakota|Dakota]] religious beliefs and the missionary effort:

> Riggs' Tahkoo Wakan, p. 90.
> (008-200)

Gordon drew upon this work for his understanding of [[Wakan]] (sacred mystery) and [[dakota|Dakota]] [[soul|spirit]]ual concepts:

> from Rev. Stephen R. Riggs, author of the Dakota Grammar and Dictionary, and of "Tahkoo Wakán"
> (016-019)

## Influence on Gordon's Legends

Gordon's *[[legend|Legend]]s of the Northwest* is deeply indebted to Riggs' scholarship. Throughout the collection, Gordon cites Riggs as an authority on:

- **[[dakota|Dakota]] cosmology**: The [[Spirit|Spirit-land]] and journey of souls after death (017-059)
- **[[dakota|Dakota]] [[legend|myth]]ology**: The [[unktéhee|Unktéhee]] (subterranean [[spirit|spirits]]) dwelling under the [[Falls of St. Anthony]] (017-063)
- **[[dakota|Dakota]] ceremonies**: The Okee-pa ceremony and moon symbolism (017-093)
- **[[dakota|Dakota]] linguistics**: Place names, tribal names, and the meaning of "[[dakota|Dakota]]" itself (P01-014)

## Relationship with Other Scholars

Riggs' work connected with that of other scholars of the [[dakota|Dakota]] people:

- [[Edward D. Neill]] — whose *History of [[minnesota|Minnesota]]* also documented [[dakota|Dakota]] history (015-202)
- [[Stephen R. Riggs|Riggs' Mission]] — the missionary outpost among the [[dakota|Dakotas]]
