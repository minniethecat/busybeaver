# Unktéhee

Unktéhee (Oonk-táy-hee) is the Great Water Spirit in [[dakota|Dakota]] [[legend|myth]]ology — the creator of the earth and man, dwelling in subterranean waters, revered through feasts and [[dance|sacred dance]]s (017-061–017-063).

## Definition / Identity

Unktéhee occupies a foundational role in [[dakota|Dakota]] cosmology as the subterranean water deity who fashioned the earth and stands in perpetual opposition to [[thunder-bird|Wakínyan]], the sky-thunder power.

- **Origin**: [[dakota|Dakota]] cosmology — the Great Unktéhee created the earth and man (017-062)
- **Language**: [[dakota|Dakota]] (*Oonk-táy-hee*)
- **Domain**: [[soul|spirit]]uality / creation-[[legend|myth]]

Gordon's footnotes document both the singular Great Unktéhee (the creator) and the plural Unktéhees (his children): "There are many Unktéhees, children of the Great Unktéhee, who created the earth and man" (017-061–017-062). The Great Unktéhee formerly dwelt "in a vast cavern under the [[falls-of-st-anthony|Falls of St. Anthony]]" (017-063), and from there the Unktéhee [[spirit|spirits]] "sometimes issue to hold communion with the [[spirit|spirits]] of the [[dakota|Dakotas]]" (017-064).

## In the Book

Gordon weaves Unktéhee throughout the [[winona|Winona]] narrative, the [[legend-of-the-falls|Legend of the Falls]], and the scholarly footnotes. The entity appears in three distinct modes: as a cosmic creator, as a feared [[soul|spirit]] invoked in battle, and as the patron of the secret order of [[medicine-man|Medicine-Men]].

### Role in the Legends

In the [[winona|Winona]] narrative, Unktéhee is invoked in scenes of ritual and confrontation. The [[dakota|Dakota]] feast and dance to "the mighty Unktéhee" (008-203) expresses reverence, while on the battlefield, "Ye are [[medicine-man|Sons of Unktéhee]], forsooth!" (010-065) becomes a taunt hurled at defeated warriors. The cosmic struggle between Unktéhee and [[thunder-bird|Wakínyan]] — "When [[thunder-bird|Wakínyan]] flashed and thundered, when Unktéhee raved and roared" (012-221) — forms the elemental backdrop of the [[dakota|Dakota]] world.

Gordon's creation account describes Unktéhee fashioning the earth from mud brought up from the primordial waters (017-070), after which the [[thunder-bird|Thunder-Bird]]'s wing-flapping dried and shaped the land. This collaboration-turned-conflict established the enduring enmity between water-depth and sky-storm powers.

## The Sons of Unktéhee

The "[[medicine-man|Sons of Unktéhee]]" form a secret order of [[medicine-man|Medicine-Men]] (016-063). Chiefs and leading warriors usually belong to this order (016-062), making it both a [[soul|spirit]]ual and political institution. The order uses a preparation enabling members to handle fire and boiling water without injury (016-060–016-061). Their rites, ceremonies, and tricks are documented in Gordon's appendix (018-023).

The Unktéhees and the [[thunder-bird|Thunder-Birds]] are described as "perpetually at war" (017-078), and this opposition structures [[dakota|Dakota]] religious practice: the feast to Unktéhee (007-228) and the sacred Wacípee Wakán dance both honor the water powers while acknowledging the sky powers' rival claim.
