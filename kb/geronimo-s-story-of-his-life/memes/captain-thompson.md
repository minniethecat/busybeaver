**Captain Thompson** of the Fourth Cavalry served as the acting assistant adjutant general for the Department of Arizona during the final phase of the Apache Wars. He was present at Fort Bowie when [[geronimo]] (Geronimo) and [[naiche]] (Naiche) were held there following their surrender in 1886 (018-010).

## Role at Fort Bowie

According to Naiche's testimony, Captain Thompson spoke to the Apache prisoners at his house in Fort Bowie, reassuring them about their upcoming removal to Florida:

> Naiche said that Captain Thompson, who was the acting assistant adjutant general, Department of Arizona, told him at his house in Fort Bowie, "Don't be afraid; no harm shall come to you. You will go to your friends all right" [018-010].

Thompson also told the Apache prisoners that Fort Marion, their destination in Florida, was not a very large place and might not accommodate all of them. He suggested that they would probably be moved to a larger location within about six months where conditions would be better. He repeated these assurances when the Apache prisoners departed by train from Fort Bowie (018-010).

## Role in Surrender Context

Captain Thompson was present during the conversations between [[general-miles]] (General Miles) and the Apache leaders. The interpreter [[george-wratton]] (George Wratton) confirmed that he witnessed these conversations, and the Apache accounts specifically noted that Captain Thompson was present as well (018-010).
