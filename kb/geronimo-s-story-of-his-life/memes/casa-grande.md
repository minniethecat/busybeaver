## Overview

Casa Grande was a Mexican settlement in Sonora that appears repeatedly throughout Geronimo's narrative as both a destination for trade and raiding expeditions, and later as the site of a pivotal treaty and subsequent massacre that deepened Apache–Mexican hostilities (006-001, 011-010, 012-002).

## Geography

Casa Grande is located in Sonora, northern Mexico. The Sierra de Sahuaripa range lies not far east of the town (007-006), and Geronimo's accounts place it roughly forty miles east of a mountain village called "Crassanas" by the Apache (008-010). To the south of Casa Grande lies the road through a mountain pass at a place called "Gosoda" by the Apache, which served as a route for Mexican freighters (017-004).

## Role in the Narrative

Casa Grande figures prominently in Geronimo's account of the Apache wars in several key episodes:

- **First expedition (1858)**: Geronimo's entire Bedonkohe Apache tribe traveled through Sonora toward Casa Grande as their destination, but stopped at Kas-ki-yeh before reaching it (006-001).
- **Raiding base**: In multiple campaigns between 1861 and 1865, Geronimo's war parties used the mountains and settlements around Casa Grande as bases for raids and gathering horses (007-006, 008-010, 009-001).
- **Battle of 1880**: Geronimo's band was camped in the mountains south of Casa Grande when a company of Mexican troops attacked. The Apaches fought at close range and killed all twenty-four Mexican soldiers, but lost twelve warriors (011-008).
- **Treaty and massacre**: About four months later, the Apaches reassembled at Casa Grande to make a treaty of peace with the townspeople. After shaking hands and trading, the Mexicans gave the Apaches mescal, and soon nearly all the Indians were drunk. While they were intoxicated, two companies of Mexican troops from another town attacked, killing twenty Indians and capturing many more (011-010). Geronimo refers to this incident as "the treachery and massacre of Casa Grande," after which the Apaches did not reassemble for a long while (012-002).
- **Later campaigns (1884–1886)**: During the final phase of the Apache Wars, Geronimo's band again operated in the mountains west and south of Casa Grande, ambushing Mexican freighters along the Gosoda road (016-009, 016-011, 016-012, 017-004).
