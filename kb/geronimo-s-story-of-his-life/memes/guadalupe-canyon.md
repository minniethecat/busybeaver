## Overview

[[Guadalupe Canyon]] is a geographical feature in the Cajon Bonito Mountains near the Arizona–[[sonora|Sonora]] border. It appears in the late-stage dispatches of the [[geronimo|Geronimo]] campaign as a strategic position occupied by U.S. troops during the final pursuit of [[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s band in 1886.

## Geography

The canyon is located in the Cajon Bonito Mountains, a range straddling the Arizona–[[sonora|Sonora]] border region. The terrain in this area is rugged and mountainous, characteristic of the borderlands that Apache bands used for concealment and evasion during the decades of conflict. The canyon's position near the border made it tactically significant for the U.S. Army's effort to intercept [[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s movement between [[mexico|Mexican]] and American territories.

## Role in the Narrative

Guadalupe Canyon appears in the concluding phase of the [[geronimo|Geronimo]] campaign. According to military dispatches from 1886, Lieutenant Lockett with "an effective command" was positioned "near Guadalupe Canyon, in Cajon Bonito Mountains" (019-001) by August 20th as part of the coordinated effort to corner [[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s band. This deployment was part of a larger encirclement strategy that involved:

- Colonel Forsyth and Colonel Beaumont commanding the [[fort-huachuca|Huachuca]] and [[fort-bowie|Bowie]] districts
- [[captain-lawton|Lawton]]'s command positioned south of [[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s band
- Wilder with "G and M troops, Fourth Cavalry" moved to [[fronteraz]]
- Mexican authorities under [[governor-torres|Governor Torres]] acting "in concert with ours" (019-001)

The canyon represents one of the final geographic positions in the military cordon that contributed to [[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s eventual surrender — the conclusion of decades of Apache resistance.


> Lieutenant Lockett, with an effective command, will be in good position tomorrow, near Guadalupe Canyon, in Cajon Bonito Mountains. On the 11th I had a very satisfactory interview with Governor Torres. The Mexican officials are acting in concert with ours.
>
> (018-005)
