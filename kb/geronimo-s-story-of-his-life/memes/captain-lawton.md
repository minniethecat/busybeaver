## Overview

Captain Henry W. Lawton was a United States Army officer who commanded the field operations pursuing Geronimo and his band of Chiricahua Apaches during the summer of 1886. He led well-scouted troops that tracked the Apaches through the mountains of Sonora, Mexico, and served as the intermediary through whom surrender overtures were transmitted to General [[general-miles|Miles]]. The town of Lawton, Oklahoma, where Geronimo later dictated his autobiography, bears his name (P02-007)(P03-005).

## Life & Role

Captain Lawton was assigned to the Department of Arizona during the Geronimo campaign. After General [[general-miles|Miles]] was made commander of all western posts, troops under Captain Lawton trailed Geronimo's band continually, engaging in skirmishes almost every day (017-003). Lawton's scouts, described by Geronimo as "good scouts," were instrumental in maintaining pressure on the Apache band (017-003).

The climactic moment of Lawton's role came in late August 1886, when his camp surprised Geronimo's camp on the Yongi River in Sonora, capturing "all the Indian property, including hundreds of pounds of dried meat" (018-005). This loss of supplies was a decisive blow that helped force the Apaches toward surrender.

## Relationship to Geronimo

Captain Lawton was a pursuer and adversary, but also the officer through whom Geronimo's surrender was negotiated. Geronimo did not wish to treat directly with Lawton, stating that he "knew that General Miles was the chief of the American troops" and preferred to deal with him (017-008). Nevertheless, Lawton's scouts — including Kayitah, a [[chokonen-apache|Chokonen]] Apache, and Marteen, a [[nedni-apache|Nedni]] Apache — acted as messengers between the two sides (017-010).

After the surrender, Lawton's name became permanently linked to Geronimo's story through the town of Lawton, Oklahoma, where Geronimo was held as a prisoner of war and where he dictated his autobiography in 1905 (P03-005).

## Key Events

- **Yongi River Raid (August 1886)** — Lawton's troops surprised Geronimo's camp, capturing supplies and weakening the band's ability to resist (018-005)
- **Surrender Overtures (September 1886)** — Through Lieutenant [[gatewood|Gatewood]], Geronimo's messengers communicated surrender terms to Captain Lawton, who relayed them to General Miles (018-010)
- **Eleven-Day Parallel March** — Lawton's command moved north while Geronimo and [[naiche|Naiche]] moved parallel, frequently camping near the troops before the final surrender at Skeleton Canyon (018-010)
