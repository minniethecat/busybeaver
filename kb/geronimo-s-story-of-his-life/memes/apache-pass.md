# Apache Pass

Apache Pass was a strategic mountain passage in southeastern Arizona that served as a site of military occupation, treaty-making, and conflict between the Apaches and American forces — including the improper treatment of Apaches there that Geronimo lists among his grievances (016-006).

## Overview

Apache Pass appears throughout Geronimo's narrative as a locus of interaction — and often betrayal — between Apaches and Americans. It was where Mangus-Colorado and Cochise made treaties with the American military post (013-007), where Apaches received rations of beef (015-004), and where, according to Geronimo, his tribe suffered "improper treatment" that he remembered alongside the murder of Mangus-Colorado and his own unjust imprisonment (016-006).

## Role in the Narrative

- **Treaty site**: Cochise and Mangus-Colorado made treaties at the post stationed at Apache Pass (013-007)
- **Ration distribution**: Cochise received twelve steers for his tribe at Apache Pass (015-004)
- **Improper treatment**: Geronimo cites improper treatment at Apache Pass as one of his grievances motivating continued resistance (016-006)
