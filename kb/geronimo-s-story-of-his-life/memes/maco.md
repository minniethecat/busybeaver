**Maco** was the chief of the [[bedonkohe-apache]] (Bedonkohe Apache) band and the paternal grandfather of [[geronimo]] (Geronimo). Although Geronimo never met him, Maco's reputation as a formidable warrior was passed down through oral tradition within the family (005-001).

## Legacy and Memory

According to Geronimo's autobiography, his father often spoke of Maco's extraordinary physical qualities:

> My grandfather, Maco, had been our chief. I never saw him, but my father often told me of the great size, strength, and sagacity of this old warrior [005-001].

Maco's tenure as chief was defined by ongoing conflicts with Mexican settlements. The Bedonkohe Apaches under his leadership fought principal wars against the Mexicans and had intermittent conflicts with other Indian tribes as well. Geronimo noted that they "were seldom at peace for any great length of time with the Mexican towns" (005-001).

## Succession

Maco died when Geronimo's father was still a young warrior. After Maco's death, leadership of the Bedonkohe Apaches passed to [[mangus-colorado]] (Mangus-Colorado), who would become a prominent Apache leader during the mid-19th century. Geronimo's own father died when Geronimo was a small boy, ending the direct line of Maco's chieftaincy (005-002).
