## Overview

General Oliver Otis Howard was a senior United States Army officer who commanded the Division of the Pacific during the final phase of the Apache Wars. In Geronimo's narrative, Howard appears as the commander at Apache Pass (Fort Bowie) who negotiated a peace agreement with Geronimo's band, an arrangement that temporarily halted hostilities. Howard also submitted the official military report on the Geronimo campaign to the Adjutant General in Washington (018-016).

## Life & Role

General Howard commanded at [[fort-bowie]] (Apache Pass), a strategic location in Arizona Territory where several key encounters between the U.S. Army and the Apaches took place. When [[geronimo]] and his band came to Apache Pass, they found General Howard in command and negotiated peace terms (015-004). Geronimo records that he "found General Howard in command, and made peace with him" (015-004).

After Howard's departure from the post, conflict resumed. Geronimo notes that "some time after General Howard went away a band of our Indians went to the pass once more and had a fight with the soldiers" (015-005).

Howard later served as commander of the Division of the Pacific and submitted a comprehensive report on the Apache campaign dated September 17, 1886, documenting military operations and the condition of the division (018-016).

## Relationship to Geronimo

Unlike General [[general-crook|Crook]], whom Geronimo criticized harshly, General Howard is portrayed neutrally in Geronimo's account. Howard served as a peace broker rather than a pursuer. His departure from Apache Pass, however, was followed by renewed conflict — suggesting that the peace he negotiated was personal rather than institutional (015-005).

The editor S. M. Barrett records that a mutual acquaintance, [[gatewood|Gatewood]], described himself as "a friend of General Howard, whom I have heard speak well of the Apaches" (P03-003), indicating Howard's relatively sympathetic reputation among Apache-facing officers.

## Key Events

- **Apache Pass Peace Agreement** — Geronimo made peace with General Howard at Fort Bowie, temporarily halting hostilities (015-004)
- **Renewed Conflict After Departure** — After Howard left Apache Pass, fighting resumed between Apaches and soldiers (015-005)
- **Division of the Pacific Report (September 1886)** — Howard submitted an official military report detailing the Geronimo campaign and the condition of the Division of the Pacific (018-016)


> We never had so good a friend among the United States officers as General Howard. We could have lived forever at peace with him. If there is any pure, honest white man in the United States army, that man is General Howard. All the Indians respect him, and even to this day frequently talk of the happy times when General Howard was in command of our Post.
>
> (015-004)
