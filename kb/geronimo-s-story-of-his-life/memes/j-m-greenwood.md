**Dr. J. M. Greenwood** was the Superintendent of Schools at Kansas City, Missouri, and a facilitator of [[geronimo]] (Geronimo)'s 1906 autobiography. His personal introduction to the Apache leader in 1905 helped build the trust that made the book project possible (P03-003).

## Meeting with Geronimo

In the summer of 1905, Greenwood visited [[s-m-barrett]] (S. M. Barrett) at Lawton, Oklahoma, and Barrett took him to meet Geronimo at [[fort-sill]] (Fort Sill). The encounter began formally until Greenwood established common ground:

> Geronimo was quite formal and reserved until Dr. Greenwood said, "I am a friend of General Howard, whom I have heard speak of you." "Come," said Geronimo, and led the way to a shade, had seats brought for us, put on his war bonnet, and served watermelon [P03-003].

The mention of [[general-howard]] (General O. O. Howard) — who had known Geronimo from the 1886 surrender negotiations — was the key that opened the conversation. Geronimo's warm response to Greenwood set the stage for the trust that later enabled the book project.

## Relationship with Geronimo

After Greenwood returned to Kansas City, Geronimo came to see Barrett and asked about "my father" — a term of respect the Apache leader had adopted for Greenwood. When Barrett explained that Greenwood was a friend, not his father, Geronimo reflected on the importance of such friendships (P03-004). Geronimo's attachment to Greenwood, formed in a single meeting, illustrated the significance the Apache leader placed on personal relationships with those who treated him with dignity.

Barrett acknowledged Greenwood's "valuable suggestions" in the preface of the published autobiography, listing him alongside [[charles-w-taylor]] (Major Charles W. Taylor) and President David R. Boyd (P02-003).
