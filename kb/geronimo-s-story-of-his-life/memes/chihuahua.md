# Chihuahua

Chihuahua was an Apache warrior whose name appears among the close-knit band of fighters led by [[geronimo|Geronimo]] in the aftermath of the [[kas-ki-yeh-massacre|Kas-Ki-Yeh massacre]]. His name is listed in the brief, powerful roll call of Geronimo's warrior cohort.

## Warrior Band

In the chapter recounting the [[kas-ki-yeh-massacre|Kas-Ki-Yeh]] tragedy and its aftermath, Geronimo enumerates the warriors who formed his band:

> Geronimo, Nanne, Ozone, Chihuahua, Loco. (006-010)

This listing places Chihuahua within Geronimo's innermost fighting circle during one of the most intense periods of the Apache Wars — the years of retaliatory raiding that followed the massacre of Geronimo's family at [[kas-ki-yeh|Kas-Ki-Yeh]]. The five names, set apart in the narrative as a stark enumeration, represent the core of Geronimo's warrior band during this formative phase of his leadership.

## Name and Identity

Chihuahua shares his name with the Mexican state of Chihuahua, through which Geronimo's band frequently traveled during their raiding campaigns into Mexico. The Mexican state of Chihuahua appears separately in Geronimo's narrative as a geographical region — for example, "We entered Chihuahua and followed south on the east side of the Sierra Madre" (007-006) — but these geographical references are distinct from the warrior Chihuahua who appears in the roll call at 006-010.

The naming overlap between person and place reflects the deep entanglement of Apache life with the geography of northern Mexico, where many Apache bands operated and where personal names sometimes drew from the landscape, encounters, or events associated with particular regions.

## Significance in the Narrative

Like [[loco|Loco]] and other warriors who appear primarily in Geronimo's roll calls, Chihuahua is not a major figure in the autobiography. His significance lies in his membership in Geronimo's warrior band — the group of men who fought, raided, and survived together through the most dangerous years of the Apache resistance. Each name in the roll call at 006-010 represents a trusted comrade in a struggle that Geronimo describes as motivated by intense personal loss and the desire for revenge.
