**Governor Torres** was the Governor of [[sonora]] (Sonora), Mexico, during the final Apache Wars (Apache Wars) campaign of 1886. He played a key role in coordinating Mexican military operations with United States Army (United States Army) forces pursuing [[geronimo]] (Geronimo)'s band in the border region (018-005).

## Role in the 1886 Geronimo Campaign

In the summer of 1886, as U.S. forces under [[general-miles]] (General Nelson A. Miles) intensified operations against Geronimo's Apache band, Governor Torres committed Mexican troops to cooperate with the American pursuit. The joint effort was documented in U.S. military telegrams (018-005).

> Dispatches from Governor Torres, dated Hermosillo, Sonora, Mexico, from Colonels Forsyth and Beaumont confirmed that Geronimo with forty Indians was endeavoring to make terms of peace with Mexican authorities of the Fronteraz district [018-005].

On July 11, 1886, a U.S. military representative reported a "very satisfactory interview with Governor Torres," noting that "the Mexican officials are acting in concert with ours" (018-005).

## Coordination with U.S. Forces

Governor Torres's cooperation was critical to the encirclement of Geronimo's band. Mexican troops under his authority monitored the Sonora side of the border, while U.S. Army units under [[captain-lawton]] (Captain Lawton) operated from the north. This binational coordination closed off escape routes that the Apaches had traditionally used to evade pursuit (018-005).

The campaign culminated in Geronimo's surrender to General Miles at [[skeleton-canyon]] (Skeleton Canyon) in September 1886.
