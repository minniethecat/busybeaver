## Overview

Porico, whose name translates to White Horse, was the brother of [[geronimo|Geronimo]] and a member of his immediate family band. He appears in Geronimo's narrative as a trusted messenger sent on a critical diplomatic mission to Fort Bowie during the surrender negotiations of 1886. Porico is listed among Geronimo's closest kin in the account of his early life (003-003).

## Life & Role

Porico is first mentioned in Geronimo's account of his early family life, where he is listed alongside Geronimo, their sister, and their mother as the core family unit: "only myself, my brother, Porico (White Horse), and my sister, and my mother" (003-003). This intimate family grouping places Porico at the center of Geronimo's personal world.

Porico is later listed among the members of Geronimo's extended family band, appearing alongside other named relatives including "Klonnen, Nadeste, Na-ta-neal, Porico (White Horse)" (003-007). This listing confirms his continued presence within Geronimo's close circle.

During the 1886 campaign, Porico took on a diplomatic role. Geronimo records that "I sent my brother Porico (White Horse) with Mr. [[george-wratton|George Wratton]] on to Fort Bowie to see General [[general-miles|Miles]], and to tell him that we wished to return to Arizona" (017-010). This mission made Porico a key intermediary in the surrender negotiations.

Porico also appears as a scout in Geronimo's account of raiding activities, listed alongside other warriors including [[naiche|Natchie]], Goody, and John [[loco|Loco]] (008-006).

## Relationship to Geronimo

Porico was Geronimo's brother — one of the few biological relatives Geronimo names in his autobiography. The trust Geronimo placed in Porico is evident in the Fort Bowie mission: at the most consequential diplomatic moment of Geronimo's life, he chose his brother to carry his message of peace to the American commander.

## Key Events

- **Early Family Life** — Listed among Geronimo's immediate family: himself, Porico, their sister, and their mother (003-003)
- **Scout Service** — Served as a scout in raiding parties alongside Natchie, Goody, John Loco, and Jasen (008-006)
- **Fort Bowie Diplomatic Mission (August 1886)** — Sent by Geronimo with interpreter George Wratton to communicate surrender terms to General Miles (017-010)


> I was fourth in a family of eight children — four boys and four girls. Of that family, only myself, my brother, Porico (White Horse), and my sister, Nah-da-ste, are yet alive. We are held as prisoners of war in this Military Reservation (Fort Sill).
>
> (003-003)
