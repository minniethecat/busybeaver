**S. M. Barrett** was the Superintendent of Education in [[lawton]] (Lawton), Oklahoma, and the compiler of [[geronimo]] (Geronimo)'s 1906 autobiography, *Geronimo's Story of His Life*. Barrett's work preserved Geronimo's first-person account of Apache history and the Apache Wars (P02-001).

## Relationship with Geronimo

Barrett first met [[geronimo]] (Geronimo) in the summer of 1904 at [[fort-sill]] (Fort Sill), where he acted as interpreter for the Apache leader in selling a war bonnet (P03-001). Over subsequent visits, the two developed a working relationship that led to the autobiography project.

Barrett noted that Geronimo became a willing participant after learning that Barrett had been wounded by a Mexican — a shared experience that resonated with the Apache leader (P03-001).

> Barrett visited Geronimo at his teepee in the Fort Sill Military reservation, and the visits became reciprocal before the book project began [P03-002].

## Compilation of the Autobiography

In the summer of 1905, Barrett was visited by [[j-m-greenwood]] (Dr. J. M. Greenwood), Superintendent of Schools at Kansas City, Missouri. Barrett introduced Greenwood to Geronimo, and the meeting helped establish further trust between Barrett and the Apache leader (P03-003).

Barrett formally proposed the book project in late summer 1905. Geronimo initially objected but agreed after Barrett secured payment arrangements and obtained permission from military authorities (P03-005).

> Geronimo asked what the officers in charge had said about the plan. When Barrett replied that they had approved, Geronimo agreed to the book project [P03-005].

In October 1905, Barrett engaged [[asa-deklugie]] (Asa Deklugie), son of Whoa, chief of the Nedni Apaches, as interpreter. The compilation proceeded through daily storytelling sessions where Geronimo spoke without notes or stenographer (P03-006).

## Editorial Process and Publication

After the storytelling sessions concluded, Barrett submitted the manuscript to [[charles-w-taylor]] (Major Charles W. Taylor) of the Eighteenth Cavalry at Fort Sill for review (P03-010). Barrett then sent the manuscript to [[president-roosevelt]] (President Theodore Roosevelt), who encouraged publication (P03-011).

On June 2, 1906, Barrett transmitted the complete manuscript to the War Department. Despite some objections from the War Department regarding Geronimo's criticisms of U.S. military actions, the work was approved for publication (P03-013).

The book was published later in 1906. In his preface, Barrett dedicated the work to President Roosevelt and acknowledged assistance from Major Charles Taylor, Dr. J. M. Greenwood, and President David R. Boyd of the University of Oklahoma (P02-003).
