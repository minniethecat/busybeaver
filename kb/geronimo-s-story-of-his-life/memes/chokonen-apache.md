# Chokonen Apache

The Chokonen (Chiricahua) Apache band led by Cochise and later his son Naiche, who were the closest allies of Geronimo's Bedonkohe throughout the Apache Wars

## Overview

The Chokonen Apache band was one of the four allied Apache tribes — along with the other three bands — that formed the core of Apache military resistance against Mexican and American forces. Their territory, leadership, and relationship with Geronimo are documented throughout his autobiography.

## Identity & Territory

The four allied Apache tribes — Bedonkohe, Chokonen, Chihenne, and Nedni — were "fast friends in the days of freedom" (002-008). Each occupied distinct territory while sharing language, customs, and military cooperation.

## Role in the Apache Wars

The Chokonen Apache participated in the combined Apache campaigns against Mexico, fighting alongside the other bands under the leadership of their respective chiefs. They suffered the consequences of defeat alongside the other Apache bands, ultimately being confined to reservations as prisoners of war.
