## Overview

Fort Huachuca was a U.S. Army post in southern Arizona, established in 1877 near the Mexican border. During the final phase of the Geronimo campaign in 1886, it served as one of the command centers from which Colonel Forsyth directed operations to intercept Geronimo's band.

## Geography

Fort Huachuca is situated in Cochise County, southern Arizona, approximately 15 miles north of the Mexican border. Its location in the Huachuca Mountains placed it strategically near the traditional Apache travel corridors between Arizona and [[sonora]]. The fort's proximity to the border made it a critical base for coordinating cross-border pursuits.

## Role in the Narrative

Fort Huachuca appears in the final military dispatches of the Geronimo campaign. In August 1886, Colonel Forsyth, commanding the Huachuca district alongside Colonel Beaumont commanding the Bowie district, confirmed reports that "Geronimo with forty Indians is endeavoring to make terms of peace with Mexican authorities of [[fronteraz]] district" (019-001). The coordinated military operation involved troops from Fort Huachuca and [[fort-bowie]] working "in concert with" Mexican officials under [[governor-torres|Governor Torres]] of Sonora.

One of the scouts returning to Fort Huachuca from [[captain-lawton|Captain Lawton]]'s command encountered [[geronimo]], [[naiche]], and thirteen other Indians en route to Fronteraz, reporting they "looked worn and hungry" and that "Geronimo carried his right arm in a sling, bandaged" (019-001). This intelligence, relayed through Fort Huachuca, contributed to the tightening cordon that led to Geronimo's final surrender.

A military dispatch from the final campaign records the fort's role:

> Dispatches today from Governor Torres, dated Hermosillo, Sonora, Mexico, from Colonels Forsyth and Beaumont, commanding Huachuca and Bowie districts, confirms the following: Geronimo with forty Indians is endeavoring to make terms of peace with Mexican authorities of Fronteraz district.
>
> (018-005)
