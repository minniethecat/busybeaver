# Eagle Feathers

The eagle's feathers were the paramount symbol of chiefly authority among the Apaches, representing justice, wisdom, and power — worn by the first chief Apache as a gift from Usen and passed down through all subsequent chiefs as the insignia of legitimate leadership.

## Overview

When Usen taught the first man — named Apache — to prepare herbs for medicine, to hunt, and to fight, he appointed him the first chief of the Indians. As the sign of this office, Apache "wore the eagle's feathers as the sign of justice, wisdom, and power" (001-019). This symbolic connection between the eagle, chiefly authority, and the cardinal virtues of leadership established a visual language of governance that endured through Apache history.

## Description

### Divine Origin

The eagle's feathers were not merely decorative — they traced their authority to Usen himself. The first chief Apache received them as part of his appointment by the creator, establishing a direct link between divine authority, chiefly legitimacy, and the wearing of eagle feathers. This made eagle feathers a sacred insignia rather than a personal adornment.

### Meanings: Justice, Wisdom, and Power

The three qualities symbolized by the eagle's feathers — justice, wisdom, and power — encapsulated the Apache ideal of leadership. A chief was expected to exercise power (the ability to lead in war and peace), tempered by wisdom (the judgment to make sound decisions) and directed toward justice (fair treatment of the tribe). The eagle, as a creature of the sky that saw all things from above, embodied this comprehensive vision.

### Continuity Across Generations

The eagle feather tradition persisted across generations of Apache chiefs. Geronimo's mention of it in the opening chapter of his autobiography — describing the origin story of his people — places the symbol at the foundation of Apache identity and governance. Though he does not describe his own wearing of eagle feathers in detail, the symbol's presence in the origin narrative establishes its enduring significance.
