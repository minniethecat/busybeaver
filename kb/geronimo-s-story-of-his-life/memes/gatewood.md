# Lieutenant Gatewood

Lieutenant Charles B. Gatewood was an officer of the Sixth United States Cavalry who played a critical role in the final [[surrender-of-1886|surrender]] of [[geronimo|Geronimo]] in 1886. Gatewood was the intermediary through whom [[general-miles|General Nelson A. Miles]] communicated surrender terms to the Apache leader, making him one of the pivotal figures in the closing chapter of the Apache Wars.

## Role in the Surrender

In September 1886, as [[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s band was pursued through the Sierra Madre mountains of Mexico, the chain of communication that led to the final capitulation ran through Lieutenant Gatewood. [[general-miles|General Miles]] reported that the overtures of surrender came "through Lieutenant Gatewood, to Captain Lawton" (018-010). Gatewood served as the trusted intermediary who conveyed the terms between the military command and the Apache holdouts.

> The 6th of September General Miles reports the hostile Apaches made overtures of surrender, through Lieutenant Gatewood, to Captain Lawton. They desired certain terms and sent two messengers to me (Miles). They were informed that they must surrender as prisoners of war to troops in the field. (018-010)

Gatewood was specifically identified as belonging to the Sixth Cavalry in the official military correspondence documenting the surrender. His role is recorded in the formal capitulation report submitted by General Miles to the Adjutant General in Washington, D.C. on September 17, 1886 (018-016).

## Role in Geronimo's Narrative

Gatewood appears in Geronimo's story exclusively in the context of the surrender negotiations. Geronimo's account, as recorded by [[george-wratton|S. M. Barrett]], relies on the official military reports that document Gatewood's role. The fact that Gatewood is named in the formal reports — rather than being an anonymous intermediary — indicates the significance of his contribution to the successful conclusion of the campaign.

## Key Events

- **September 6, 1886** — Gatewood conveys Geronimo's surrender overtures to Captain Lawton, initiating the final negotiations (018-010)
- **September 17, 1886** — Gatewood's role is formally recorded in General Miles's report to the Adjutant General (018-016)
