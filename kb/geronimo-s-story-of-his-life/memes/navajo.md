## Overview

The Navajo (Diné) are repeatedly mentioned in [[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s narrative as neighbors of the [[apache-family|Apache]], sharing a territorial border. Although the two peoples were linguistically related — both speaking Athabaskan languages — Geronimo emphasizes their distinct identity, noting that the Apache "never held councils with the Navajo Indians" despite trading and occasional visiting (002-004).

## Identity & Territory

[[geronimo|Geronimo]] describes the Navajo as living north of the [[white-mountain-apache|White Mountain Apaches]], with their range "next to that of the Navajo Indians." He states explicitly that the Navajo were "not of the same blood as the Apaches," though both groups are part of the broader Athabaskan language family. Relationships were pragmatic rather than political: "We held councils with all Apache tribes, but never with the Navajo Indians. However, we traded with them and sometimes visited them" (002-004).

## Role in the Apache Wars

The Navajo were not direct participants in the Apache Wars against [[mexico|Mexico]] and the United States. Their interactions with the Apache were primarily economic and informational:

- **Trade**: Apache warriors traveled north to "trade for blankets from the Navajo Indians" (007-008).
- **Information**: The Apache learned the value of money from the Navajo. [[geronimo|Geronimo]] recounts that after trading with white men who gave them money, "we did not know the value of this money, but we kept it and later learned from the Navajo Indians that it was very valuable" (013-001).

## Notable Members

No specific Navajo individuals are named in [[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s narrative. The tribe is referenced only collectively.

## Relations with Other Tribes

The Navajo maintained peaceful trading relations with the Apache, particularly the White Mountain Apaches whose territory bordered theirs. However, Geronimo's account suggests the relationship was limited to commerce and informal visiting, without the formal council-based diplomacy that characterized relations among the Apache sub-tribes (002-004).
