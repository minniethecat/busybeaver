## Overview

[[apache-family|Apache]] burial customs reflected the tribe's reverence for warriors and the desire to protect the dead from disturbance. [[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s account of his father's funeral provides the most detailed description of these rites in the narrative, revealing a ceremony that combined honor, sacrifice, and seclusion.

## Description

When a warrior died, the watchers carefully closed his eyes, then "arrayed him in his best clothes, painted his face afresh, wrapped a rich blanket around him, saddled his favorite horse, bore his arms in front of him, and led his horse behind" as they carried the body to a cave in the mountain (005-002). The procession repeated the warrior's deeds of valor in "wailing tones."

After reaching the burial cave, the warrior's horses were slain, and all of his other property was given away, "as was customary in our tribe" (005-002). The body was deposited in the cave with his arms beside him. The grave was then hidden by piles of stone, ensuring that enemies could not find or desecrate it.

[[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s poetic summation captures the Apache ideal: "Wrapped in splendor he lies in seclusion, and the winds in the pines sing a low requiem over the dead warrior" (005-002).

Geronimo describes his father's funeral procession in detail:

> When he passed away, carefully the watchers closed his eyes, then they arrayed him in his best clothes, painted his face afresh, wrapped a rich blanket around him, saddled his favorite horse, bore his arms in front of him, and led his horse behind, repeating in wailing tones his deeds of valor as they carried his body to a cave in the mountain.
>
> (005-002)

## Widow Customs

After a warrior's death, his widow returned to her people and could be given away or sold by her father or brothers (005-003). A widow with children typically remained single for two or three years, while a childless widow remarried immediately. [[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s own mother chose to live with him and never remarried, deviating from the customary expectation.
