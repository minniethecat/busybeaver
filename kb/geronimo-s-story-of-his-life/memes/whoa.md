# Whoa

Whoa was the chief of the Nedni Apaches, the southernmost Apache band, who allied with Geronimo and Mangus-Colorado in the war against Mexico, provided refuge to Geronimo's band in Nedni territory, and was the father of Asa Deklugie — Geronimo's trusted interpreter.

## Overview

Whoa led the Nedni Apaches, whose territory lay south and west of the Bedonkohe homeland (002-007). The Nedni were "friends and kinsmen" of Geronimo's people, and Whoa personally joined Geronimo on expeditions into Mexico (011-002, 011-007). His son Asa Deklugie served as Geronimo's interpreter during the telling of his autobiography (P03-006). Whoa's death, sometime before the final campaigns, left Naiche as Geronimo's only remaining chiefly companion (016-011).

## Life & Role

### Leadership of the Nedni

The Nedni Apaches occupied territory south and west of the Bedonkohe. Whoa was their established chief, described by Geronimo as a reliable ally whose lands provided strategic depth for Apache operations (002-007). When Geronimo needed to escape pursuit, he could "go southward into the land of the Nedni Apaches" where Whoa would receive him (006-015).

### Alliance Against Mexico

Whoa committed Nedni warriors to the combined Apache force that fought Mexico. At the great gathering of Apache bands, the Nedni Apaches were led by Whoa alongside the Bedonkohe under Mangus-Colorado and the Chokonen under Cochise (006-018). Whoa personally accompanied Geronimo on expeditions: "The chief of the Nedni Apaches, Whoa, was with me" (011-002). The Nedni lands provided a base of operations — their territory was "the lands of the Nedni Apaches, our friends and kinsmen" (011-007).

### Later Years and Death

Whoa led the Nedni alongside Geronimo's Bedonkohe through multiple campaigns. However, by the time of the final period of resistance, "Whoa was dead, and Naiche was the only chief with me" (016-011). Geronimo's laconic mention of Whoa's death reflects the steady attrition of Apache leadership through the decades of warfare.

### Legacy Through Asa Deklugie

Whoa's son, Asa Deklugie, served as Geronimo's interpreter during the dictation of his autobiography to S.M. Barrett in 1905–1906. Asa is described as "the son of Whoa, chief of the Nedni Apaches" and "interpreter for Geronimo" (011-006). Through Asa, Whoa's lineage continued to serve Apache interests even after the wars had ended.

## Relationship to Geronimo

Whoa was a trusted ally and peer. Geronimo describes him as a chief who personally joined expeditions rather than merely authorizing them. The Nedni under Whoa provided refuge when Geronimo's band needed to escape pursuit, and their territory served as a base for operations against Mexico. Whoa's death was a significant loss — it left Naiche as Geronimo's only remaining chiefly companion.

## Key Events

- **Southern refuge** (006-015) — Received Geronimo when he sought refuge in Nedni territory after Kas-Ki-Yeh
- **Three-tribe alliance** (006-018) — Led Nedni forces alongside Mangus-Colorado (Bedonkohe) and Cochise (Chokonen)
- **Personal expedition with Geronimo** (011-002) — Accompanied Geronimo personally on a military expedition
- **Death** (016-011) — Died sometime before the final campaigns, leaving Naiche as Geronimo's sole remaining chief
