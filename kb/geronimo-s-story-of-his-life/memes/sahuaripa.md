## Overview

The Sierra de Sahuaripa is a mountain range in [[sonora|Sonora]], [[mexico|Mexico]], that figures prominently in [[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s narrative as one of the principal routes and camping grounds for Apache war parties crossing into Mexico. The range lies not far east of [[casa-grande|Casa Grande]] and provided the Apaches with familiar terrain for staging raids and evading pursuit (007-005, 007-006).

## Geography

The Sierra de Sahuaripa range runs through [[sonora|Sonora]], east of [[casa-grande|Casa Grande]] (007-006). [[geronimo|Geronimo]] describes entering the range from the Chokonen Apache territory to the north, crossing over from the east side of the Sierra Madre Mountains (007-004, 007-006). The southern part of the range was used as a camping and scouting base (008-001).

## Role in the Narrative

- **1861 expedition**: [[geronimo|Geronimo]] and twelve warriors crossed over to the Sierra de Sahuaripa range, not far east of [[casa-grande|Casa Grande]]. They rested one day and sent out scouts who reported pack trains five miles to the west — leading to a successful attack at daybreak (007-006).
- **1862 campaign**: The war party camped in the southern part of the Sierra de Sahuaripa range, again using it as a base for reconnaissance before raids (008-001).
- **1863 raid**: Geronimo and three warriors went south into [[sonora|Sonora]], camping in the Sierra de Sahuaripa Mountains, about forty miles west of [[casa-grande|Casa Grande]], where they planned an attack on the village of "Crassanas" (008-010).
- **Final campaigns (1884–1886)**: During the last phase of the Apache Wars, Geronimo's band again went south almost to [[casa-grande|Casa Grande]] and camped in the Sierra de Sahuaripa Mountains, ranging in the mountains of Old [[mexico|Mexico]] for about a year before returning to [[san-carlos-reservation|San Carlos]] (016-009).

## Notable Figures

- [[geronimo|Geronimo]] — led multiple expeditions through the Sahuaripa range between 1861 and 1886
- [[cochise|Cochise]] — associated with the Chokonen Apaches whose territory bordered the Sierra Madre

Geronimo describes entering the mountains at the start of his 1860 campaign:

> Soon after this (in the summer of 1860) I was again able to take the warpath against the Mexicans, this time with twenty-five warriors. We followed the trail of the Mexican troops last mentioned and entered the Sierra de Sahuaripa Mountains.
>
> (007-005)
