# Asa Deklugie

Asa Deklugie was the son of Whoa, chief of the Nedni Apaches, who served as [[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s official interpreter — translating between Apache and English — during the year-long dictation of his autobiography to Superintendent [[s-m-barrett|S.M. Barrett]] in 1905 and 1906.

## Overview

Asa Deklugie bridges two eras of [[apache-family|Apache]] history: he was the son of a war chief (Whoa of the Nedni Apaches) and the interpreter through whom [[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s life story reached the English-speaking world. Geronimo trusted him not only as a translator but as a cultural mediator — the sessions were held at Asa's own house and teepee as well as at Geronimo's (P03-007). His portrait appears among the photographic illustrations in the autobiography (011-006), and he is noted in the context of family gatherings (007-012) and ceremonial events (021-013).

## Life & Role

### Lineage

Asa was the son of Whoa, the noted chief of the Nedni Apaches who had been [[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s ally through the years of warfare against Mexico (P03-006, 002-007). This lineage gave him standing within [[apache-family|Apache]] society independent of his role as interpreter.

### The Autobiography Interpreter

When [[s-m-barrett|S.M. Barrett]], Superintendent of Education in [[lawton|Lawton]], Oklahoma, sought to record [[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s life story in 1904–1906, he faced a language barrier: Geronimo spoke Apache and some Spanish but limited English. The solution was "the services of an educated Indian, Asa Deklugie, son of Whoa" (P03-006). The interpretation chain ran: Geronimo spoke in Apache, Asa translated to English, and Barrett transcribed. The sessions were conducted at multiple locations — Geronimo's teepee, Asa's house, and "in some mountain dell" when Geronimo wished to speak in a natural setting (P03-007).

### Trusted Mediator

[[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s refusal to talk when a stenographer was present (P03-007) and his insistence on speaking only through trusted intermediaries underscore Asa's importance. He was not merely a translator but a gatekeeper — Geronimo would speak through him or not at all.

## Relationship to Geronimo

Asa served [[geronimo|Geronimo]] as a trusted interpreter and cultural mediator. The fact that Geronimo chose the son of his old ally Whoa for this role suggests both personal trust and a continuation of the [[bedonkohe-apache|Bedonkohe]]-Nedni alliance into the reservation era.
