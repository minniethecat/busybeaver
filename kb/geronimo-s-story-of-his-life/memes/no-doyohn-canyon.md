# No-doyohn Canyon

No-doyohn Canyon, in present-day Arizona, was the birthplace of [[geronimo|Geronimo]] in June 1829. The canyon lay within the traditional territory of the [[bedonkohe-apache|Bedonkohe Apache]] band, near the headwaters of the [[gila-river|Gila River]].

## Geronimo's Birthplace

Geronimo begins his autobiography with a simple, declarative statement of his origin:

> I was born in No-doyohn Canyon, Arizona, June, 1829. (003-001)

This opening line anchors the entire narrative in a specific geographical location — a canyon in the Arizona highlands that represented the heartland of the Bedonkohe people. The canyon's name, of Apache origin, is preserved only through Geronimo's account.

## The Bedonkohe Homeland

No-doyohn Canyon was situated within the broader territory of the [[bedonkohe-apache|Bedonkohe]] band. Geronimo describes this homeland in lyrical terms:

> This range was our fatherland; among these mountains our wigwams were hidden; the scattered valleys contained our fields; the boundless prairies, stretching away on every side, were our pastures; the rocky caverns were our burying places. (003-002)

The region, stretching from the headwaters of the [[gila-river|Gila River]], encompassed the mountains, valleys, and prairies that sustained Apache life — providing shelter, farmland, grazing land, and sacred burial sites.

## Significance

No-doyohn Canyon represents the starting point of Geronimo's life journey — a place of origin that became increasingly distant as decades of warfare, displacement, and imprisonment carried him far from his homeland. The canyon's exact location today is uncertain, known primarily through Geronimo's own words and the geographical context he provides.
