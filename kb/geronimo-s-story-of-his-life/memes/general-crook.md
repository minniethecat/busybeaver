## Overview

General George Crook was a United States Army officer who served as commander of the Department of Arizona during a critical period of Apache-white relations. In [[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s narrative, Crook appears as the officer in charge at [[san-carlos-reservation|San Carlos]] whose restrictive policies — including confiscating the Apaches' horses and weapons — prompted Geronimo and his followers to flee the reservation in 1885. Geronimo's manuscript contains pointed criticism of Crook's actions (P03-017).

## Life & Role

General Crook was assigned to command the [[san-carlos-reservation|San Carlos Apache reservation]] in Arizona Territory. His tenure was marked by strict enforcement of reservation rules that the [[apache-family|Apaches]] found oppressive. According to [[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s account, Crook "took the horses and cattle and would not let us go and hunt" (016-010), leaving the Apaches without their traditional means of subsistence.

Crook later led military operations into [[mexico|Mexico]] to pursue [[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s band after the breakout. His campaign achieved partial results: Geronimo records that Crook "had come down into Mexico with a large force of soldiers" and that negotiations followed (016-015). However, the terms offered by Crook were rejected by Geronimo, who described them as "such unjust orders" (017-002).

## Relationship to Geronimo

[[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s relationship with General Crook was adversarial. The manuscript singles out Crook for particular criticism, which the editor [[s-m-barrett|S. M. Barrett]] describes by noting that "in his original manuscript, Geronimo criticised General Crook" (P03-017). Geronimo blamed Crook's policies for the Apache breakout, arguing that it was "such unjust orders as those of General Crook" that "caused the outbreak of the Apaches" (017-002).

After the failed negotiations with Crook, [[geronimo|Geronimo]] agreed to return to the United States with General Crook but later fled again, ultimately coming under the pursuit of General [[general-miles|Miles]] and Captain [[captain-lawton|Lawton]].

## Key Events

- **San Carlos Reservation Command** — Crook enforced strict rules including confiscation of Apache horses and cattle, which [[geronimo|Geronimo]] cited as a cause of the outbreak (016-010)
- **Pursuit into Mexico** — Crook led a large force of soldiers into [[mexico|Mexico]] to negotiate with Geronimo's band after the 1885 breakout (016-015)
- **Failed Negotiations** — Geronimo rejected Crook's terms as "unjust," leading to continued hostilities (017-001), (017-002)


> General Crook said to me, "Why did you leave the reservation?" I said: "You told me that I might live in the reservation the same as white people lived. One year I raised a crop of corn, and gathered and stored it, and the next year I put in a crop of oats, and when the crop was almost ready to harvest, you told your soldiers to put me in prison, and if I resisted to kill me. If I had been let alone I would now have been in good circumstances, but instead of that you and the Mexicans are hunting me with soldiers."
>
> (016-015)
