**Lieutenant Manus** was a United States Army officer who commanded a battalion of Apache scouts during the final campaign against [[geronimo]] (Geronimo) in 1886. He was assigned to escort Geronimo's band from the Canyon of Los Embudos in Mexico to Fort Bowie, Arizona, following surrender negotiations with [[general-crook]] (General Crook) (018-014).

## The March from Los Embudos

After the assassination of [[crawford]] (Captain Crawford) by Mexican forces on January 11, 1886, the hostile Apache band requested talks with General Crook. A conference was held on March 25–27, 1886, in the Canyon of Los Embudos, approximately 25 miles south of San Bernardino, Mexico. At this conference, it was arranged that Lieutenant Manus and his battalion of scouts would escort the surrendered Apaches to Fort Bowie, Arizona (018-014).

The march began on the morning of March 28, 1886. However, on the night of March 29, Geronimo and [[naiche]] (Naiche), accompanied by twenty men, fourteen women, and two boys, became fearful of possible punishment and fled into the hills. Lieutenant Manus immediately mounted a pursuit, but was unable to recapture the escaping band (018-014).

## Aftermath

Geronimo's escape during Manus's escort mission prolonged the Apache campaign for several more months, ultimately leading to a second surrender in September 1886 under [[general-miles]] (General Miles) and the pursuit efforts of [[captain-lawton]] (Captain Lawton).
