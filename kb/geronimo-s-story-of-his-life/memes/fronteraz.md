## Overview

Fronteraz is a settlement in the Mexican state of [[sonora]], appearing in [[geronimo]]'s account of a raid during the Apache wars. The name appears in the context of a skirmish during one of Geronimo's expeditions into Mexican territory.

## Geography

Fronteraz is located in Sonora, the Mexican state directly south of Arizona. It lies within the corridor of Apache raiding routes that followed the mountain ranges and river courses southward from the border region. The surrounding terrain includes the arid highlands characteristic of northern Sonora.

## Role in the Narrative

Fronteraz appears as the site of a confrontation during an Apache raiding expedition into Mexican territory. In a raid described in Chapter XI, Geronimo's party "went south through Chihuahua as far as Santa Cruz, [[sonora]], then crossed over the [[sierra-madre|Sierra Madre]] Mountains" (011-001), passing through settlements including Fronteraz. The Mexican settlements in this region frequently mounted defenses against Apache incursions, and "Mexican troops were coming from the south to the north" (011-002) in pursuit of the raiders.

The settlement represents one of many small Mexican towns that became waypoints in the decades-long cycle of Apache raids and Mexican counterattacks that defined Geronimo's account of the border wars. The specific mention of Fronteraz places it among the string of locations — including Arispe, Nacozari, and Quitaco — that marked the Apache warpath through Sonora.

A military dispatch from the final campaign mentions Fronteraz as a negotiation site:

> One of our scouts, in returning to Fort Huachuca from Lawton's command, met him, Naiche, and thirteen other Indians on their way to Fronteraz; had a long conversation with them; they said they wanted to make peace, and looked worn and hungry. Geronimo carried his right arm in a sling, bandaged.
>
> (018-005)
