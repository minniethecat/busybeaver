## Overview

The Kiowa are a Plains Indian tribe who appear in [[geronimo]]'s narrative primarily in the context of his later life as a prisoner of war in Oklahoma. The Kiowa shared lands with the Comanche, and Geronimo's account mentions prominent Kiowa leaders as well as the tribe's participation in Apache social gatherings (012-010, 020-019, 023-003).

## Identity & Territory

The Kiowa were a Plains tribe whose traditional territory lay in what is now Oklahoma. In Geronimo's time, they were neighbors to the [[apache-family|Apache]] prisoners of war who were relocated to Fort Sill, Oklahoma. Geronimo notes that his people were "now held on Comanche and Kiowa lands, which are not suited to our needs" (023-003), indicating that the Apaches were placed on Kiowa territory rather than their own native land.

## Role in the Apache Wars

The Kiowa did not participate directly in the Apache Wars — they were a Plains tribe geographically and culturally distinct from the Apache. Their relevance to Geronimo's narrative comes from the post-war period, when the Apache prisoners of war were settled on lands shared with the Kiowa and Comanche in Oklahoma.

## Notable Members

- **Lone Wolf** — Chief of the Kiowa, photographed alongside Geronimo and described by Geronimo as "chief of Kiowas" (012-010, 017-014)
- **Gotebo** — War chief of the Kiowa, also photographed and described by Geronimo (017-014)

## Relations with Other Tribes

The Kiowa were close allies of the [[comanche|Comanche]], with whom they shared lands in Oklahoma (020-019, 023-003). During Eva Geronimo's coming-of-age dance at Medicine Creek, invitations were issued to "all Apaches, and many Comanches and Kiowas," showing cordial social relations between the tribes in the Fort Sill period (020-019).

> We are now held on Comanche and Kiowa lands, which are not suited to our needs—these lands and this climate are suited to the Indians who originally inhabited this country, of course, but our people are decreasing in numbers here, and will continue to decrease unless they are allowed to return to their native land.
>
> (023-003)
