## Overview

Fort Apache was a U.S. Army post established in eastern Arizona on the White Mountain Apache Reservation. It appears in Geronimo's narrative as one of the locations where Apache bands were concentrated under U.S. military supervision, and as the place where some [[chiricahua-apache|Chiricahua Apaches]] were held while [[geronimo]] was in Florida.

## Geography

Fort Apache is located in the White Mountain region of eastern Arizona, within the traditional territory of the [[white-mountain-apache|White Mountain Apaches]]. The post sits at an elevation of approximately 5,000 feet in ponderosa pine country, with the surrounding terrain of mountains and canyons characteristic of the Mogollon Rim region. Geronimo describes the White Mountain Apaches' range as being "next to that of the [[navajo|Navajo Indians]]" (002-003).

## Role in the Narrative

Fort Apache is referenced in the surrender negotiations between Geronimo and [[general-miles|General Miles]]. In his illustrated explanation of where the Apaches would be sent, the General placed stones on the ground: one representing Geronimo's band, another for "Chihuahua is with his band," and a third for "the Indians at Camp Apache" (019-010). The President's stated plan was to consolidate all Apache groups, including "the Indians at Camp Apache," at [[fort-marion]] in Florida.

The fort represented one node in the network of military posts — alongside [[fort-bowie]], [[fort-huachuca]], and [[fort-sill]] — that the U.S. Army used to control and relocate Apache populations during and after the Apache Wars. From Fort Apache, some Apache bands were eventually transferred to Fort Marion and later to Mount Vernon Barracks and Fort Sill.


> He picked up another stone and placed it a short distance from the first, and said, "This represents you, Geronimo." He then picked up a third stone and placed it a little distance from the others, and said, "This represents the Indians at Camp Apache. The President wants to take you and put you with Chihuahua."
>
> (018-013)
