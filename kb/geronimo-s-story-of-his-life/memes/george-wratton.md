## Overview

George Wratton was an interpreter who played a crucial communicative role between [[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s [[chiricahua-apache|Chiricahua Apaches]] and the United States authorities during the surrender negotiations of 1886 and the subsequent prisoner-of-war period at [[fort-sill|Fort Sill]], Oklahoma. His presence enabled direct communication between parties who shared no common language (017-010), (018-013).

## Life & Role

Wratton first appears in [[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s narrative during the critical surrender negotiations of late summer 1886. Geronimo sent his brother [[porico-white-horse|Porico]] (White Horse) "with Mr. George Wratton on to [[fort-bowie|Fort Bowie]] to see General Miles" to communicate the Apaches' wish to return to Arizona (017-010). Wratton's role as interpreter was essential — neither Geronimo nor most of his band spoke English fluently.

Wratton was officially listed among the personnel present at the surrender proceedings in [[skeleton-canyon|Skeleton Canyon]]. A military document from September 1886 records "George Wratton, the interpreter" alongside other officials involved in the capitulation (018-013).

After the surrender, Wratton continued his service at [[fort-sill|Fort Sill]], Oklahoma, where the Apache prisoners of war were held. [[geronimo|Geronimo]] notes that "then, as now, Mr. George Wratton superintended the Indians" (019-006), indicating Wratton's long-term administrative role overseeing the Apache prisoners.

## Relationship to Geronimo

Wratton occupied a unique position as a linguistic and cultural intermediary. He was trusted enough to accompany [[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s brother on a sensitive diplomatic mission to [[fort-bowie|Fort Bowie]], and he remained with the Apaches through their decades of imprisonment. Geronimo's description "then, as now" — written in 1905 — shows that Wratton's service spanned nearly twenty years (019-006).

## Key Events

- **Fort Bowie Mission (August 1886)** — Accompanied [[porico-white-horse|Porico]] (White Horse) to [[fort-bowie|Fort Bowie]] to communicate [[geronimo|Geronimo]]'s desire to return to Arizona and negotiate with General [[general-miles|Miles]] (017-010)
- **Skeleton Canyon Surrender (September 1886)** — Officially recorded as the interpreter at the Apache surrender proceedings in [[skeleton-canyon|Skeleton Canyon]] (018-013)
- **Fort Sill Superintendence (1886–1905+)** — Supervised the Apache prisoners of war at [[fort-sill|Fort Sill]] for nearly two decades (019-006)


> Then, as now, Mr. George Wratton superintended the Indians. He has always had trouble with the Indians, because he has mistreated them. One day an Indian, while drunk, stabbed Mr. Wratton with a little knife. The officer in charge took the part of Mr. Wratton and the Indian was sent to prison.
>
> (019-006)
