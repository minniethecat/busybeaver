## Overview

Kayitah was a Chokonen Apache who served as a scout for [[captain-lawton|Captain Lawton]]'s United States troops during the final campaign against Geronimo in 1886. Along with Marteen, a Nedni Apache scout, Kayitah delivered [[general-miles|General Miles]]'s message to Geronimo asking him to come in for a meeting — an encounter that directly led to Geronimo's surrender (017-010).

## Life & Role

Kayitah served as an Indian scout for Captain Henry Lawton during the summer of 1886, when Lawton's troops were pursuing Geronimo's band through the [[sierra-madre|Sierra Madre]] mountains. After Geronimo sent his brother Porico (White Horse) and George Wratton to [[fort-bowie]] to see General Miles, Kayitah and Marteen intercepted Geronimo's party before the messengers returned. They told Geronimo that General Miles had arrived and had sent them to ask Geronimo to meet him (017-010).

In response to this message, Geronimo went to the United States troops' camp and met directly with General Miles. At that meeting, Geronimo told Miles "how I had been wronged" and expressed his wish to return to the United States with his people to see their families who had been captured (017-010). This meeting initiated the final surrender negotiations that ended the Apache Wars.

## Relationship to Geronimo

Kayitah was a fellow Apache who served on the opposing side — as a scout for the U.S. Army — yet his role as messenger made him an intermediary rather than an enemy. Geronimo's narrative does not express resentment toward Kayitah; instead, the scout is presented as a neutral courier delivering General Miles's request for a meeting. Kayitah's message was the proximate cause of Geronimo's decision to walk into the Army camp and begin surrender talks.

## Key Events

- **Delivering General Miles's message (1886)** — Kayitah and Marteen found Geronimo's band and informed him that General Miles wished to meet, setting the surrender negotiations in motion (017-010)

> I sent my brother Porico (White Horse) with Mr. George Wratton on to Fort Bowie to see General Miles, and to tell him that we wished to return to Arizona; but before these messengers returned I met two Indian scouts—Kayitah, a Chokonen Apache, and Marteen, a Nedni Apache. They were serving as scouts for Captain Lawton.
>
> (017-010)
