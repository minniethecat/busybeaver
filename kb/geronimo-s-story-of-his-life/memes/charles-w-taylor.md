**Major Charles W. Taylor** was an officer of the Eighteenth Cavalry (Eighteenth Cavalry), United States Army (United States Army), and commandant at [[fort-sill]] (Fort Sill), Oklahoma, during the early 1900s. He played a role in the publication of [[geronimo]] (Geronimo)'s autobiography by reviewing the manuscript and providing editorial guidance (P03-010).

## Review of Geronimo's Autobiography

After [[s-m-barrett]] (S. M. Barrett) completed the draft manuscript of Geronimo's life story based on the Apache leader's oral accounts, he submitted it to Major Taylor for military review. Barrett acknowledged Taylor as one of the key contributors to the project:

> When he had finished his story I submitted the manuscript to Major Charles W. Taylor, Eighteenth Cavalry, commandant, Fort Sill, Oklahoma, who gave me some valuable suggestions as to additional related information which I asked Geronimo to give [P03-010].

Taylor's suggestions led Barrett to return to Geronimo for additional details. In most instances, Geronimo complied with these requests, enriching the final text with supplementary information (P03-010).

## Acknowledgments

In the published preface, Barrett formally acknowledged Taylor among the contributors whose advice improved the work. Taylor was listed alongside [[j-m-greenwood]] (Dr. J. M. Greenwood) of Kansas City and President David R. Boyd of the University of Oklahoma (P02-003).
